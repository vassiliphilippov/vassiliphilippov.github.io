renal vein

1. NOUN

/ ˈriː.nəl veɪn / (AmE), / ˈriː.nəl veɪn / (BrE)
Either of the veins that drain blood from the kidneys and carry it to the inferior vena cava.
vein draining blood from the kidney
medical

Examples

During the surgery, the doctor carefully avoided damaging the renal vein.
A blood clot in the renal vein can lead to serious complications.
Both kidneys are connected to the inferior vena cava by their respective renal veins.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: renal artery - the artery supplying blood to the kidney

Etymology

From Latin 'renalis' (pertaining to the kidneys) and 'vein'.
