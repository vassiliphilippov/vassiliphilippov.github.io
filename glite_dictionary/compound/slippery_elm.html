slippery elm

1. NOUN

/ ˈslɪpəri ɛlm / (AmE), / ˈslɪpəri ɛlm / (BrE)
A North American elm tree (Ulmus rubra) with rough, red-tinged young leaves and hard wood; its inner bark is sometimes used in herbal remedies.
a North American elm tree species

Examples

The inner bark of the slippery elm is often used in traditional medicine.
We planted a slippery elm in the backyard to provide shade during the summer.
Many North American forests once had abundant slippery elm trees, but their numbers have declined.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elm - a deciduous tree in the genus Ulmus with serrated leaves
