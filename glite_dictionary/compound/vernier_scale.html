vernier scale

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɜːrniər skeɪl / (AmE), / ˈvɜːniə skeɪl / (BrE)
A small movable scale that slides along a main scale, allowing for precise measurement of fractional divisions.
precision sliding measurement scale

Examples

He used a vernier scale to obtain more precise measurements than the main ruler could provide.
The technician checked the length with a caliper equipped with a vernier scale for accuracy.
Reading the vernier scale requires careful alignment of its marks with the main scale.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caliper - instrument with a vernier scale for measuring thickness or diameter

Etymology

Named after French mathematician Pierre Vernier (1580–1637), who invented it.
