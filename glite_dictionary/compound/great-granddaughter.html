great-granddaughter

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡreɪt ˈɡrænˌdɔtər / (AmE), / ˌɡreɪt ˈɡrænˌdɔːtə / (BrE)
the daughter of one's grandson or granddaughter
daughter of a grandchild

Examples

My great-granddaughter visited me during the summer holidays.
She often tells stories about her great-granddaughter’s achievements at school.
At the family reunion, four generations gathered, from grandmother to great-granddaughter.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: great-grandson - son of one's grandson or granddaughter
  • Degree Lower: granddaughter - daughter of one's son or daughter
