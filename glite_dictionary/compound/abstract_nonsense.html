abstract nonsense

1. NOUN

/ ˈæbˌstrækt ˈnɑnsɛns / (AmE), / ˈæbstrækt ˈnɒnsəns / (BrE)
language or ideas that are overly complex, theoretical, and considered meaningless or obscure
meaningless complex ideas or language

Examples

Many students complained that the lecture was filled with abstract nonsense and offered no practical insight.
She grew frustrated with the abstract nonsense in the philosophy book and put it down.
Rather than concrete proposals, the meeting dissolved into discussions of abstract nonsense.

Synset Examples

His theories were often dismissed as abstract nonsense.
Critics labeled the article as nothing more than abstract nonsense.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gibberish - unintelligible or meaningless speech or writing
  • Synonym: jargon - special words or expressions difficult for others to understand
