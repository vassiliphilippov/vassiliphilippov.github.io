Scotch whiskey

1. NOUN

/ skɑtʃ ˈwɪski / (AmE), / skɒtʃ ˈwɪski / (BrE)
Whiskey distilled in Scotland, especially that made from malted barley and often aged in oak barrels.
whiskey distilled in Scotland

Examples

He ordered a glass of Scotch whiskey to enjoy after dinner.
The distillery is renowned for producing some of the finest Scotch whiskey in the region.
Many collectors seek rare bottles of Scotch whiskey for their impressive flavor and history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whiskey - a distilled alcoholic beverage made from grain mash

Etymology

Scotch, meaning 'of Scotland,' combined with whiskey, from Irish uisce beatha 'water of life.'
