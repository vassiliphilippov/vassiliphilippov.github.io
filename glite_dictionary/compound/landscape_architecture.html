landscape architecture

1. NOUN

/ ˈlændˌskeɪp ˌɑrkəˈtɛktʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈlændskeɪp ˌɑːkɪˈtɛktʃə / (BrE)
the art and science of planning, designing, and managing land, including the design of outdoor spaces and large-scale constructed environments
the design and planning of outdoor environments

Examples

She studied landscape architecture at university before starting her own design firm.
The city's parks reflect innovative landscape architecture that integrates natural and urban spaces.
Modern landscape architecture addresses environmental sustainability alongside aesthetic concerns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: architecture - the art or practice of designing and constructing buildings

Etymology

From 'landscape' (Dutch: landschap) + 'architecture' (from Latin architectura).
