anamnestic response

1. NOUN

/ ˌænəmˈnɛstɪk rɪˈspɑns / (AmE), / ˌanəmˈnɛstɪk rɪˈspɒns / (BrE)
a renewed and accelerated production of antibodies upon a second or later exposure to the same antigen
rapid antibody production after repeat antigen exposure
medical

Examples

The anamnestic response enables the immune system to fight infections more efficiently upon subsequent exposures.
Doctors rely on the anamnestic response when administering booster vaccines.
An anamnestic response causes antibody levels to rise much faster than during the initial exposure.

Etymology

From Greek 'anamnēstikos' (reminding) and English 'response'.
