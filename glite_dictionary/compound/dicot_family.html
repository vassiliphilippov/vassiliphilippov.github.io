dicot family

1. NOUN

/ ˈdaɪˌkɑt ˈfæməli / (AmE), / ˈdaɪkɒt ˈfæmɪli / (BrE)
a family of flowering plants whose seeds typically have two embryonic leaves (cotyledons), usually visible at germination
group of plants with two seed leaves

Examples

The rose belongs to a dicot family known for its broad leaves and intricate flower structures.
Many common vegetables are part of a dicot family, including beans and tomatoes.
Botanists use the number of cotyledons to classify whether a plant is in a dicot family or not.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: monocot family - group of flowering plants with a single seed leaf (cotyledon)

Etymology

Dicot is short for dicotyledon, from Greek di- (two) + kotyledon (cup-shaped cavity).
