Lightning Hurler

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪtnɪŋ ˈhɜrlər / (AmE), / ˈlaɪtnɪŋ ˈhɜːlə / (BrE)
an epithet for Jupiter, the chief Roman god, emphasizing his role as the bringer of lightning
epithet for Jupiter, Roman god

Examples

In ancient texts, Jupiter is often called the Lightning Hurler because of his command over the storms.
The poet invoked the Lightning Hurler as a symbol of ultimate authority and power.
Roman mythology depicts the Lightning Hurler wielding thunderbolts from atop the heavens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Jupiter - chief Roman god; god of the sky and lightning
