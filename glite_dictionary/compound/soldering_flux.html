soldering flux

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɑdərɪŋ flʌks / (AmE), / ˈsɒdərɪŋ flʌks / (BrE)
A chemical substance applied to surfaces to be soldered, used to clean and prepare them and improve the bond during soldering.
chemical cleaner used in soldering

Examples

Before connecting the wires, the technician applied soldering flux to ensure a strong and clean joint.
Using soldering flux prevents oxidation and improves the quality of the soldered connection.
Always remove excess soldering flux after soldering to avoid potential corrosion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flux - chemical cleaning agent used in joining metals
