northern parula

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɔrðərn pəˈruːlə / (AmE), / ˈnɔːðən pəˈruːlə / (BrE)
a small gray-blue wood warbler with a yellow throat and breast, found in eastern North America
a North American species of wood warbler

Examples

We spotted a northern parula singing high in the maple tree this morning.
The migration of the northern parula is closely followed by bird enthusiasts in spring.
A bright yellow throat makes the northern parula easy to identify among other warblers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: warbler - a small songbird known for its warbling song

Etymology

From New Latin Parula, bird genus name; 'northern' refers to its range in North America.
