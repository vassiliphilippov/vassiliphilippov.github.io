genus Houttuynia

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌhaʊtˈtuː.i.ni.ə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌhaʊtˈtuː.i.ni.ə / (BrE)
A genus of east Asian low-growing plants that typically grow in wet places, containing a single species.
a genus of east Asian low-growing plants

Examples

The wetland garden featured several specimens of genus Houttuynia thriving near the pond.
Botanists studied the genus Houttuynia for its unique adaptation to marshy environments.
Medicinal uses have been reported for plants in the genus Houttuynia across East Asia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Houttuynia cordata - species of genus Houttuynia, known as fish mint or chameleon plant

Etymology

Named after Dutch botanist Martin Houttuyn (1720–1798).
