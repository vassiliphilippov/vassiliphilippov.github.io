great hundred

1. NOUN

/ ɡreɪt ˈhʌndrəd / (AmE), / ɡreɪt ˈhʌndrəd / (BrE)
an old term for the number 120, especially as used in trade and counting in Northern Europe and Britain
old term for 120 (ten times twelve)
archaic british

Examples

In medieval times, a great hundred meant 120 fish or furs, not 100 as some might expect.
The contract specified a delivery of two great hundreds of wool.
Historical records often mention the use of a great hundred in tallying goods in northern Europe.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: hundred - the number one hundred (100); short hundred

Etymology

From Old English, distinguishing a 'great hundred' (120) from a 'small or short hundred' (100), common in historical British and Northern European trade.
