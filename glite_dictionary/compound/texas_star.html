Texas star

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛksəs stɑr / (AmE), / ˈtɛksəs stɑː / (BrE)
A prairie herb native to Texas, known for its solitary lilac-colored, star-shaped flowers.
prairie herb with star-shaped lilac flowers

Examples

The Texas star blooms beautifully in the spring, adding color to the prairies.
Gardeners often choose the Texas star for its eye-catching, star-shaped flowers.
We spotted a rare Texas star while hiking through the grasslands.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɛksəs stɑr / (AmE), / ˈtɛksəs stɑː / (BrE)
A plant of the southern United States, notable for its large, showy, yellow, star-shaped flower heads and coarsely divided leaves; cultivated as an ornamental.
ornamental plant with large yellow flower heads

Examples

The Texas star is a popular addition to many southern gardens due to its bright blossoms.
Gardeners prize the Texas star for its ability to attract pollinators.
The leaves of the Texas star are deeply lobed, making the plant distinct even when not in flower.
