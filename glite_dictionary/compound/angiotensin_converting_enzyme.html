angiotensin converting enzyme

1. NOUN

/ ˌændʒi.oʊˌtɛn.sɪn kənˈvɝ.tɪŋ ˈɛn.zaɪm / (AmE), / ˌandʒi.əʊˌtɛn.sɪn kənˈvɜː.tɪŋ ˈɛn.zaɪm / (BrE)
a proteolytic enzyme that converts angiotensin I into angiotensin II, playing a key role in the regulation of blood pressure
enzyme converting angiotensin I to II
medical

Examples

Researchers studied the effects of the angiotensin converting enzyme on blood pressure regulation.
Certain medications inhibit the angiotensin converting enzyme to help treat hypertension.
The activity of angiotensin converting enzyme was measured in patient blood samples.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ACE inhibitor - a medication that inhibits angiotensin converting enzyme to lower blood pressure

Etymology

From 'angiotensin', based on its role in the angiotensin system, and 'converting enzyme', denoting its enzymatic function.
