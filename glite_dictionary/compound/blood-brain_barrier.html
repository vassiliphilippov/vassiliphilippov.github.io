blood-brain barrier

1. NOUN

/ blʌd breɪn ˈbæriɚ / (AmE), / blʌd breɪn ˈbæriə / (BrE)
A semipermeable membrane that separates circulating blood from brain tissue and protects the central nervous system from potentially harmful substances.
membrane protecting the brain from bloodborne substances
medical

Examples

Some medications cannot cross the blood-brain barrier, making neurological treatment more challenging.
Researchers are developing new ways to deliver drugs past the blood-brain barrier to treat brain diseases.
The blood-brain barrier helps protect neural tissue from toxins and pathogens in the bloodstream.

Synset Examples

the brain was protected from the large molecules of the virus by the blood-brain barrier

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barrier - a structure or object that blocks movement or access

Etymology

Formed from 'blood' + 'brain' + 'barrier', describing its function as a separator between blood and brain tissue.
