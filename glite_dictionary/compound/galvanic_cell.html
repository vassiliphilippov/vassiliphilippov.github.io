galvanic cell

1. NOUN

/ ɡælˈvænɪk sɛl / (AmE), / ɡælˈvanɪk sɛl / (BrE)
an electrochemical cell that produces electricity from a chemical reaction and cannot be recharged
electrochemical cell generating electricity

Examples

A galvanic cell converts chemical energy into electrical energy through spontaneous redox reactions.
The common battery is a type of galvanic cell used in many portable devices.
In a chemistry lab, students built a simple galvanic cell using copper and zinc electrodes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: voltaic cell - an electrochemical cell that uses a chemical reaction to produce electricity; another term for galvanic cell

Etymology

From the name of Italian scientist Luigi Galvani (1737–1798), whose work inspired the study of bioelectrical phenomena.
