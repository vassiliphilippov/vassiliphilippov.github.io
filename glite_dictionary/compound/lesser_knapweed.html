lesser knapweed

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛsər ˈnæpˌwid / (AmE), / ˈlɛsə ˈnapˌwiːd / (BrE)
a weedy perennial plant (Centaurea nigra) with tough wiry stems and purple flowers, native to Europe and widely naturalized elsewhere
European perennial plant with purple flowers

Examples

Lesser knapweed thrives in grasslands and attracts many species of insects.
The purple flower heads of lesser knapweed are a common sight in wild meadows each summer.
Many gardens include lesser knapweed for its ability to support pollinators.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: greater knapweed - a similar but usually larger knapweed species (Centaurea scabiosa)

Etymology

From 'lesser' (meaning smaller) and 'knapweed,' from Middle English, of uncertain origin.
