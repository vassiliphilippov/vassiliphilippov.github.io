genus Serranus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs səˈreɪnəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs səˈreɪnəs / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Serranidae, consisting mainly of small Pacific sea basses.
type genus of Serranidae (sea basses)
medical

Examples

The genus Serranus includes several species of colorful sea bass found in the Pacific Ocean.
Marine biologists often study the genus Serranus to understand the diversity of reef fish.
Within the family Serranidae, the genus Serranus serves as a model for evolutionary research.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Serranidae - family of fish known as sea basses and groupers

Etymology

From Latin 'serranus', a kind of fish, used in zoological nomenclature.
