angiospermous tree

1. NOUN

/ ˌændʒi.oʊˈspɝːməs ˈtriː / (AmE), / ˌæn.dʒɪ.əʊˈspɜː.məs ˈtriː / (BrE)
a tree whose seeds and ovules are contained within an ovary, characteristic of flowering plants
a tree with seeds enclosed in an ovary

Examples

The magnolia is an example of an angiospermous tree commonly found in southern gardens.
As an angiospermous tree, the apple tree produces flowers and fruit containing its seeds.
Unlike a pine, an angiospermous tree has seeds enclosed within a fruit.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: gymnospermous tree - a tree whose seeds are not enclosed in an ovary (such as a pine or fir)

Etymology

From 'angiosperm', from Greek angeion (vessel) + sperma (seed), referring to seeds enclosed in an ovary.
