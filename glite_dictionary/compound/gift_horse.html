gift horse

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɪft hɔrs / (AmE), / ˈɡɪft hɔːs / (BrE)
A gift, typically one of questionable or minor value, that should be accepted without criticism or inspection.
a gift that should not be criticized

Examples

He knew the old car was a gift horse, so he thanked his uncle and didn't complain.
When receiving a bonus, even if it's small, remember to accept the gift horse graciously.
She considered the free tickets a gift horse, despite the poor seats.

Synset Examples

it wasn't much, but don't look a gift horse in the mouth

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gift - something given voluntarily without payment

Etymology

From the proverb 'don't look a gift horse in the mouth,' alluding to inspecting a horse's age by its teeth instead of accepting it gratefully.
