willow-pattern

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪloʊ ˌpætərn / (AmE), / ˈwɪləʊ ˌpætn / (BrE)
a style of chinaware decorated with a blue-and-white Chinese-inspired design typically depicting a willow tree, a river, and often other landscape elements
blue-and-white chinaware design

Examples

She served tea in her finest willow-pattern cups every Sunday afternoon.
The antique shop had a collection of willow-pattern plates displayed in the window.
I inherited a beautiful willow-pattern teapot from my grandmother.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: china - high-quality white ceramic ware

Etymology

Named after the willow tree depicted in the distinctive pattern, first popularized in Britain in the late 18th century.
