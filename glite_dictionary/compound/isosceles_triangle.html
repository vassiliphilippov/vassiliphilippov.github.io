isosceles triangle

1. NOUN

/ aɪˈsɑːsəliːz ˈtraɪæŋɡəl / (AmE), / aɪˈsɒsɪliːz ˈtraɪæŋɡl / (BrE)
A triangle with exactly two sides of equal length.
triangle with two equal sides

Examples

The mathematician explained that an isosceles triangle always has two sides of the same length.
You can easily recognize an isosceles triangle by checking if two of its angles are equal.
She drew an isosceles triangle on the board to show the concept to her students.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: equilateral triangle - triangle with all sides equal
  • Antonym: scalene triangle - triangle with all sides of different lengths

Etymology

From Greek isoskelēs, from isos (equal) + skelos (leg).
