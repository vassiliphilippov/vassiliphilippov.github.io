order Laminariales

1. PROPN

/ ˈɔrdɚ læˌmɪnəˈraɪəliˌiz / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə læˌmɪnəˈraɪəliˌiːz / (BrE)
A taxonomic order of marine brown algae found in cold or polar seas, in some classifications the same as the family Laminariaceae.
taxonomic order of marine brown algae

Examples

The kelps included in the order Laminariales provide critical habitat for many marine species.
Researchers study the order Laminariales to understand the ecology of cold water ecosystems.
Several commercially harvested seaweeds belong to the order Laminariales.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Laminariaceae - family of brown algae, sometimes considered synonymous with Laminariales

Etymology

From Latin 'laminaria,' a genus of brown algae, with '-ales' denoting taxonomic order.
