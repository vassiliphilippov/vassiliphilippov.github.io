de facto

1. ADV

/ di ˈfæktoʊ / (AmE), / deɪ ˈfæktəʊ / (BrE)
in reality or in fact, although not officially established by law or by name
in practice; in reality (not officially)

Examples

Although he was never formally appointed, he acted as the de facto leader of the group.
Segregation in schools was banned, but de facto segregation continued for many years.
English is the de facto language of business in many countries.

Synset Examples

the result was, de facto, a one-party system

Related Terms

  • Antonym: de jure - by law; according to rightful entitlement or claim
  • Synonym: actually - in fact; really
  • Antonym: officially - formally or according to official rules

Etymology

From Latin 'de facto' meaning 'from the fact'.
