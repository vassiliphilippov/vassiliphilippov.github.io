Alaska fur seal

1. NOUN

/ əˈlæskə fɜr sil / (AmE), / əˈlæskə fɜː sɪəl / (BrE)
a species of fur seal (Callorhinus ursinus) found along the Pacific coast from Alaska to California, known for its dense underfur
species of fur seal from Alaska to California

Examples

Visitors to the Pribilof Islands can often spot the Alaska fur seal basking on rocky shores.
Strict regulations protect the population of the Alaska fur seal from overhunting.
Researchers are studying how climate change affects the migration of the Alaska fur seal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: northern fur seal - species of fur seal inhabiting the North Pacific, also known as Alaska fur seal
