thyroid gland

1. NOUN

/ ˈθaɪˌrɔɪd ɡlænd / (AmE), / ˈθaɪərɔɪd ɡlænd / (BrE)
A large, butterfly-shaped gland located at the base of the neck that produces hormones regulating metabolism and growth.
gland in neck controlling metabolism
medical

Examples

The thyroid gland regulates many metabolic processes in the body.
After the surgery, her thyroid gland needed regular monitoring for hormone levels.
An enlarged thyroid gland may indicate a medical condition such as goiter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: endocrine gland - a gland that secretes hormones directly into the bloodstream

Etymology

From Greek 'thyreoeidēs' (shield-shaped) + gland.
