line of defense

1. NOUN

/ laɪn əv dɪˈfɛns / (AmE), / laɪn əv dɪˈfɛns / (BrE)
a defensive structure or barrier that is used to protect against attack
defensive barrier or structure

Examples

The castle's outer line of defense was reinforced to withstand the enemy's attack.
Strong fortifications formed the first line of defense around the city.
Medical experts say vaccinations are the best line of defense against infectious diseases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: defense - protection against attack or harm
  • Synonym: barricade - a structure built as a defensive barrier

2. NOUN

/ laɪn əv dɪˈfɛns / (AmE), / laɪn əv dɪˈfɛns / (BrE)
an organization, system, or group that acts to protect against attack, danger, or threat
organization or system for protection

Examples

The local fire department is our first line of defense against house fires.
Vaccination programs serve as an important line of defense against infectious diseases.
For many people, savings accounts are their only line of defense in case of unexpected expenses.

Synset Examples

police are the major line of defense against crime

Related Terms

  • Synonym: defender - a person who defends something or someone
  • Antonym: line of attack - the primary force or direction in attacking
