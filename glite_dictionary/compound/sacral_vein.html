sacral vein

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪ.krəl veɪn / (AmE), / ˈsæ.krəl veɪn / (BrE)
a vein located in the sacral region at the lower part of the spine
vein in the sacral region
medical

Examples

During the surgery, the doctor carefully avoided damaging the sacral vein.
An injury to the sacral vein can lead to significant bleeding in the lower back area.
The anatomy textbook included a diagram illustrating the location of the sacral vein.

Etymology

From Latin sacer (sacred, relating to the sacrum) and English vein.
