secular games

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛkjələr ɡeɪmz / (AmE), / ˈsɛkjʊlə ɡeɪmz / (BrE)
the centennial religious rites and games held in ancient Rome to mark the beginning of a new generation, traditionally occurring every hundred years
centennial religious festivals of ancient Rome

Examples

The emperor presided over the secular games, celebrating Rome's new era with elaborate ceremonies.
Historical accounts describe the secular games as grand events drawing crowds from across the Roman Empire.
During the secular games, citizens witnessed both religious rituals and public entertainments.

Etymology

From Latin 'ludi saeculares' (games of an age or generation).
