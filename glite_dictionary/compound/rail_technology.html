rail technology

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪl tɛkˌnɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ˈreɪl tɛkˌnɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the field concerned with the design, construction, and operation of railway systems and infrastructure
the technology of designing and operating railroads

Examples

Advancements in rail technology have made high-speed trains more efficient and reliable.
Many universities now offer courses in rail technology to support the growing railway industry.
The implementation of digital systems has revolutionized rail technology worldwide.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: railway engineering - the branch of engineering dealing with railroads
