squaw man

1. NOUN

/ skwɑː mæn / (AmE), / skwɔː mæn / (BrE)
Offensive term for a white man married to a Native American woman.
offensive: white man married to a Native American woman
offensive american

Examples

The term squaw man is now considered highly offensive and outdated.
In early frontier literature, a squaw man was often depicted living at the margins of both communities.
He was labeled a squaw man because of his marriage to a Native American woman.

Etymology

From 'squaw' (now offensive term for Native American woman) and 'man'; originally American English, late 19th century.

2. NOUN

/ skwɑ mæn / (AmE), / skwɔː mæn / (BrE)
(dated, potentially offensive) A white man married to a Native American woman, especially as depicted in literature; sometimes used more broadly for men who advocate for women's issues or Native American rights.
white man married to Native woman (dated, offensive)
archaic offensive

Examples

The novel centers on a squaw man who is torn between two cultures.
Some critics argue that labeling him a squaw man overlooks the complexity of his identity.
He faced discrimination in the community as a squaw man, both for his marriage and his beliefs.
