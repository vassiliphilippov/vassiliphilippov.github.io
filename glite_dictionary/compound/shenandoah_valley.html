Shenandoah Valley

1. PROPN

/ ʃəˌnændoʊˈvæli / (AmE), / ʃəˌnændəʊˈvæli / (BrE)
a large valley in northern Virginia between the Allegheny and Blue Ridge Mountains, noted for its role in the American Civil War
valley in northern Virginia, USA

Examples

Many tourists visit the Shenandoah Valley each autumn to admire the vibrant fall foliage.
Several decisive battles of the Civil War took place in the Shenandoah Valley.
The Shenandoah Valley is famous for its picturesque landscapes and historic towns.

Etymology

From Shenandoah, the name of the river and region, possibly from an Iroquoian or Algonquian language.
