simple regression

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪmpəl rɪˈɡrɛʃən / (AmE), / ˈsɪmpəl rɪˈɡrɛʃən / (BrE)
a statistical method that examines the relationship between two variables, used to predict the value of one variable based on the other
statistical method for two-variable prediction

Examples

The professor explained how simple regression could predict outcomes from a single variable.
Economists often use simple regression to analyze the link between income and expenditure.
Our team utilized simple regression to estimate sales growth based on last year’s advertising budget.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: multiple regression - a statistical method examining the relationship between one dependent and two or more independent variables

Etymology

From Latin 'regressio' (a return, going back); 'simple' meaning 'single variable' in statistical context.
