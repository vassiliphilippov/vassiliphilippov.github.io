phonologic system

1. NOUN

/ ˌfoʊnəˈlɑdʒɪk ˈsɪstəm / (AmE), / ˌfəʊnəˈlɒdʒɪk ˈsɪstəm / (BrE)
the system of sounds (phones) used in a particular language; the phonological structure of a language
the sound system of a language
medical

Examples

Every language has a unique phonologic system that distinguishes its words and meanings.
Linguists study the phonologic system to understand how sounds function in communication.
A child's ability to learn the phonologic system of their native language develops over time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phonology - the study of the sound system of languages
  • Synonym: phonetics - the study of speech sounds and their production

Etymology

From Greek phōnē (sound, voice) and -logic (relating to study), via 'phonology'.
