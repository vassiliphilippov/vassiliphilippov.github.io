issuing house

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪʃuːɪŋ haʊs / (AmE), / ˈɪʃuːɪŋ haʊs / (BrE)
A bank or financial institution that arranges, registers, and sells securities for a company on the primary market.
bank that sells and registers securities

Examples

The company appointed an issuing house to handle its new share offering.
Several major issuing houses competed for the contract to distribute the bonds.
An issuing house plays a crucial role in bringing securities to the primary market.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: underwriter - a person or entity that assesses and assumes risk in financial transactions
