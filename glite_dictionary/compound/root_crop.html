root crop

1. NOUN

/ rut krɑp / (AmE), / ruːt krɒp / (BrE)
A plant grown mainly for its enlarged roots, which are harvested and used as food, such as beets, potatoes, or turnips.
crop grown for its edible roots

Examples

Farmers in this region often rotate root crops such as carrots and beets to maintain soil health.
The potato is a staple root crop in many countries around the world.
After harvesting the root crops, the soil must be prepared again for the next planting season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tuber - thickened underground part of a stem, like a potato
