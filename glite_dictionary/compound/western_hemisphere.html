western hemisphere

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɛstɚn ˈhɛməsˌfɪr / (AmE), / ˈwɛstən ˈhɛmɪsˌfɪə / (BrE)
the half of the Earth that includes North America and South America, typically defined as the hemisphere west of the Prime Meridian and east of the 180th meridian
the half of Earth with the Americas

Examples

Many countries in the western hemisphere celebrate similar holidays at different times of the year.
The western hemisphere has a diverse range of climates, from Arctic to tropical.
International treaties have helped preserve several wildlife species native to the western hemisphere.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: eastern hemisphere - the half of the Earth east of the Prime Meridian, including Europe, Asia, Africa, and Australia
