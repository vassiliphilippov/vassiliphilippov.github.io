banzai charge

1. NOUN

/ ˌbænˈzaɪ tʃɑrdʒ / (AmE), / ˌbanˈzʌɪ tʃɑːdʒ / (BrE)
a mass, often suicidal attack in which troops rush at the enemy, typically accompanied by cries of 'banzai'; especially associated with Japanese soldiers in World War II
mass military attack, Japanese WWII tactic

Examples

The Japanese soldiers launched a desperate banzai charge against the Allied positions at dawn.
Many survivors recalled the terrifying sound of the banzai charge during the battle.
A banzai charge was often seen as a final act of defiance when defeat seemed inevitable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: human wave attack - military tactic involving a mass assault of infantry

Etymology

From Japanese 'banzai' (ten thousand years, used as a cheer) and English 'charge'.
