environmental condition

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˌvaɪrənˈmɛntəl kənˈdɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˌvaɪrənˈmɛntl̩ kənˈdɪʃən / (BrE)
the physical, chemical, or biological state of the environment at a particular place or time, especially as it affects living organisms or processes.
state of the environment

Examples

The factory was forced to shut down due to poor environmental conditions.
Certain crops thrive best when environmental conditions are warm and humid.
Researchers monitor environmental conditions to assess the impact of pollution on local wildlife.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: climate - long-term weather patterns in an area
  • Synonym: weather - the state of the atmosphere at a place and time
