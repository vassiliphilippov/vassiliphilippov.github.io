Eccles cake

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛk.əlz keɪk / (AmE), / ˈɛk.lz keɪk / (BrE)
A small, round, flat cake made of flaky pastry filled with currants or other dried fruit.
pastry filled with dried fruit

Examples

I bought a fresh Eccles cake from the bakery this morning.
Would you like some tea and an Eccles cake with your afternoon snack?
Traditional Eccles cakes are filled with currants and topped with sugar.

Etymology

Named after Eccles, a town in Greater Manchester, England, where the cake originated.
