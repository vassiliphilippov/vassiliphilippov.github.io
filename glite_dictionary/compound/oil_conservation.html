oil conservation

1. NOUN

/ ɔɪl ˌkɑnsərˈveɪʃən / (AmE), / ɔɪl ˌkɒnsəˈveɪʃən / (BrE)
the protection and careful management of petroleum resources to prevent waste and ensure long-term availability
protection of petroleum resources

Examples

Recent government policies have promoted oil conservation to secure the country's future energy needs.
Companies are adopting new technologies aimed at oil conservation and reducing environmental damage.
Educating the public on oil conservation is crucial for sustainable development.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: energy conservation - the reduction in the use of energy resources
  • Antonym: oil waste - the excessive or careless use of petroleum resources
