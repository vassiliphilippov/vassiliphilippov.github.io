East Chadic

1. NOUN

/ ist ˈtʃædɪk / (AmE), / iːst ˈtʃadɪk / (BrE)
a group of Chadic languages spoken mainly in Chad and neighboring regions, forming part of the larger Afroasiatic language family
a group of Chadic languages in Chad

Examples

Several languages classified as East Chadic are spoken only in remote villages.
Linguists have studied the East Chadic languages to understand their relationship to other Afroasiatic tongues.
The diversity within the East Chadic group reflects the complex history of Central Africa.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: West Chadic - a group of Chadic languages spoken mainly in Nigeria and Niger

Etymology

Formed from 'East' and 'Chadic', the latter referencing the Lake Chad region.
