unobserved fire

1. NOUN

/ ʌnəbˈzɝvd ˈfaɪɚ / (AmE), / ʌnəbˈzɜːvd ˈfaɪə / (BrE)
artillery or gunfire whose point of impact cannot be directly seen or monitored by observers
artillery fire not directly observed

Examples

The artillery unit conducted unobserved fire due to heavy fog obscuring the target area.
In some situations, unobserved fire is necessary when direct observation is impossible.
The mission report noted significant risks associated with relying on unobserved fire during the operation.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: observed fire - artillery fire where the impact is directly observed
