phonograph recording

1. NOUN

/ ˈfoʊnəˌɡræf rɪˈkɔrdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfəʊnəɡrɑːf rɪˈkɔːdɪŋ / (BrE)
a sound recording in the form of a disk with a continuous groove, played using a rotating turntable and a needle
disk-shaped sound recording played by a turntable

Examples

He carefully placed the phonograph recording on the turntable and lowered the needle to start the music.
Many collectors seek rare phonograph recordings of jazz from the 1920s and 1930s.
The museum's exhibit includes an original phonograph recording of Caruso's performance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: record - a flat disc that stores sound, especially music
