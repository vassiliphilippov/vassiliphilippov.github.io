high frequency

1. NOUN

/ haɪ ˈfriːkwənsi / (AmE), / haɪ ˈfriːkwənsi / (BrE)
a pitch or sound with a greater number of vibrations per second, perceived as higher than other pitches
a pitch higher than others

Examples

Dogs can hear high frequencies that are inaudible to humans.
Some people find high frequency sounds irritating or uncomfortable.
The whistle emitted a high frequency tone to call the dog.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: low frequency - a pitch or sound perceived as lower than others

2. NOUN

/ haɪ ˈfriːkwənsi / (AmE), / haɪ ˈfriːkwənsi / (BrE)
A radio frequency band in the range of 3 to 30 megahertz, used in communications and broadcasting.
radio frequency band (3–30 MHz)

Examples

Ships often use high frequency radio waves to communicate over long distances.
Signals in the high frequency band can reflect off the ionosphere for global transmission.
Many amateur radio operators prefer working with high frequency ranges for international contacts.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: very high frequency - radio frequency band in the range of 30 to 300 megahertz
  • Degree Lower: medium frequency - radio frequency band in the range of 300 to 3000 kilohertz
