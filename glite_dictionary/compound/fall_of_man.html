Fall of man

1. NOUN

/ fɑl əv mæn / (AmE), / fɔːl əv mæn / (BrE)
In Christian theology, the event in which humankind fell from innocence into sin, traditionally as a result of Adam and Eve's disobedience in the Garden of Eden.
theological event of humankind's first sin

Examples

The doctrine of the Fall of man plays a central role in Christian theology.
Many writers have explored the consequences of the Fall of man in literature and philosophy.
After the Fall of man, human nature was believed to be forever changed.

Synset Examples

women have been blamed ever since the Fall

Related Terms

  • Synonym: original sin - the doctrine of inherited sinfulness based on Adam and Eve's actions
