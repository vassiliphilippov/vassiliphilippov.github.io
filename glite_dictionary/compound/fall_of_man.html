Fall of Man

1. NOUN

/ fɔl əv mæn / (AmE), / fɔːl əv mæn / (BrE)
(in Judeo-Christian tradition) the event in which Adam and Eve disobeyed God by eating the forbidden fruit, leading to the loss of innocence and humanity's exile from the Garden of Eden.
loss of innocence in Eden; Adam and Eve's disobedience

Examples

In Christian theology, the Fall of Man explains the origin of sin and human suffering.
Many works of art and literature have depicted the Fall of Man as a pivotal moment in human history.
The doctrine of original sin is closely linked to the concept of the Fall of Man.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: original sin - the doctrine that humanity inherits sin from Adam and Eve's transgression
