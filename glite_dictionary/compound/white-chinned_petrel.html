white-chinned petrel

1. NOUN

/ ˌwaɪt ˈtʃɪnd ˈpɛtrəl / (AmE), / ˌwaɪt ˈtʃɪnd ˈpɛtrəl / (BrE)
a large black seabird of the southern oceans, notable for a distinctive white patch on its chin
large black seabird with white chin

Examples

The white-chinned petrel breeds on remote islands in the subantarctic region.
Conservationists are concerned about declining white-chinned petrel populations due to fishing bycatch.
A white-chinned petrel was spotted gliding above the stormy southern seas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: petrel - a type of seabird of the family Procellariidae
