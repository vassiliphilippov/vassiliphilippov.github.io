eminence grise

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛmɪˈnɑns ˈɡriz / (AmE), / ˌɛ.mɪ.nəns ˈɡriːz / (BrE)
A person who has great influence or power, especially unofficially or behind the scenes, in a particular sphere.
person with unofficial influence or power

Examples

Many suspected that the CEO's assistant was the true eminence grise in the company.
Her role as an eminence grise in political circles was well known to insiders.
Although he held no official title, his position as eminence grise made him incredibly influential.

Synset Examples

the President's wife is an eminence grise in matters of education

Related Terms

  • Synonym: power behind the throne - a person who unofficially controls or influences those in authority

Etymology

French for 'grey eminence', originally referring to François Leclerc du Tremblay, advisor to Cardinal Richelieu.
