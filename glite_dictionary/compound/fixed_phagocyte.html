fixed phagocyte

1. NOUN

/ fɪkst fəˈɡoʊ.saɪt / (AmE), / fɪkst fəˈɡəʊ.saɪt / (BrE)
a type of phagocyte that remains within tissues such as the liver, spleen, or bone marrow, rather than circulating in the blood.
phagocyte residing in tissues (not blood)
medical

Examples

Kupffer cells in the liver are a classic example of a fixed phagocyte in the human body.
A fixed phagocyte remains stationed within certain tissues to defend against infection.
Unlike a wandering phagocyte, a fixed phagocyte does not migrate through the bloodstream.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: macrophage - a large phagocytic cell found in tissues
  • Antonym: wandering phagocyte - a phagocyte that moves freely through the blood or tissues

Etymology

From 'fixed' (stationary) and 'phagocyte', from Greek phagein (to eat) and kytos (cell).
