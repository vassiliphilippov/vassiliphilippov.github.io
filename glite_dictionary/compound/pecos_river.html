Pecos River

1. PROPN

/ ˈpeɪkəs ˈrɪvər / (AmE), / ˈpeɪkəs ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
a river in the southwestern United States, a tributary of the Rio Grande that flows from New Mexico through western Texas
tributary of the Rio Grande in the US

Examples

The Pecos River is famous for its scenic canyons and crystal-clear waters.
Several small towns are located along the Pecos River in Texas and New Mexico.
Farmers use water from the Pecos River to irrigate their crops.

Etymology

Named after the Pecos Pueblo, from the Spanish 'Pecos', the name for the local Native American people.
