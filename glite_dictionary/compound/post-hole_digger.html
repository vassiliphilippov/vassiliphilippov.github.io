post-hole digger

1. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊst hoʊl ˌdɪɡər / (AmE), / ˈpəʊst həʊl ˌdɪɡə / (BrE)
a tool with two blades or scoops used for digging narrow, deep holes for setting posts
tool for digging holes for posts

Examples

The farmer used a post-hole digger to install a new fence along the property line.
A post-hole digger makes the job of planting signposts much quicker and easier.
After borrowing a post-hole digger, he managed to set all the posts in one afternoon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shovel - tool with a broad blade for digging or moving materials
