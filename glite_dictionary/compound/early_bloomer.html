early bloomer

1. NOUN

/ ˈɝli ˈbluːmɚ / (AmE), / ˈɜːli ˈbluːmə / (BrE)
a person who develops abilities, skills, or talents at an earlier age than is typical among peers
person who develops abilities early

Examples

His son was an early bloomer who could read before starting kindergarten.
Being an early bloomer in sports gave her a head start on the competition.
Although he was an early bloomer, he continued to work hard throughout his life.

Synset Examples

The early bloomer surprised everyone with her talent.
As an early bloomer, he started his career young.
She was an early bloomer, excelling in academics.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: late bloomer - a person who develops abilities or talents later than peers
