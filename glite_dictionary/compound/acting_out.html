acting out

1. NOUN

/ ˈæktɪŋ aʊt / (AmE), / ˈæktɪŋ aʊt / (BrE)
an impulsive, uncontrollable outburst or disruptive behavior, especially by a troubled child or adult
impulsive or disruptive outburst

Examples

His constant acting out at school worried his teachers and parents.
The therapist believed her acting out was a response to family stress.
Nobody knew how to respond to his sudden acting out during the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tantrum - an uncontrolled outburst of anger and frustration, typically in a young child
  • Antonym: self-control - restraint exercised over one's own impulses, emotions, or desires

2. NOUN

/ ˈæktɪŋ aʊt / (AmE), / ˈæktɪŋ aʊt / (BrE)
(Psychology) The expression of previously restrained or inhibited emotions through actions rather than words, typically as a means of relieving emotional tension.
expressing emotions through actions (psychology)
medical

Examples

The therapist recognized her acting out as a way of coping with unresolved anger.
During periods of stress, children may resort to acting out at school or home.
His acting out helped him address feelings he couldn't otherwise express.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catharsis - the process of releasing strong or repressed emotions
