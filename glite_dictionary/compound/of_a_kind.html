of a kind

1. ADJ

/ əv ə kaɪnd / (AmE), / əv ə kaɪnd / (BrE)
used to describe something that is unique or belongs to a particular type, often suggesting similarity rather than exact sameness.
belonging to a particular or unique type

Examples

Her explanation was of a kind, but it didn't really clarify the situation.
Most of his stories are of a kind—amusing but not entirely believable.
The museum displays artifacts of a kind rarely found elsewhere.

Synset Examples

She's the only friend I have of a kind.
That restaurant serves dishes that are of a kind.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: of sorts - used to show something is not exactly the type described but similar in some way
