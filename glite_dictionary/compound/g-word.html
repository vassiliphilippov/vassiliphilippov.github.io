g-word

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiˌwɝd / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːˌwɜːd / (BrE)
a euphemism for a sensitive or offensive word beginning with the letter 'g', used to avoid saying the word itself
euphemism for a sensitive 'g' word

Examples

During the meeting, she carefully avoided mentioning the g-word to prevent controversy.
Some educators debate whether using the g-word helps or hinders open discussion.
He whispered the g-word, knowing it might upset the group.

Synset Examples

She avoided using the g-word during the discussion.
Using the g-word in public can create tension.
He finally said the g-word, shocking everyone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: n-word - a euphemism for a sensitive or offensive word beginning with 'n'
  • Synonym: f-word - a euphemism for a word beginning with 'f', usually 'fuck'

Etymology

Formed by analogy with euphemisms like 'n-word', using the initial letter of the sensitive term.
