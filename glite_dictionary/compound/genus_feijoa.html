genus Feijoa

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs feɪˈʒoʊ.ə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs feɪˈʒəʊ.ə / (BrE)
A genus of small South American shrubs or trees in the myrtle family, known for producing the edible fruit feijoa.
genus of South American shrubs or trees

Examples

The genus Feijoa includes several species native to South America.
Gardeners often cultivate plants from the genus Feijoa for their fragrant flowers and fruits.
The most widely cultivated member of the genus Feijoa is Feijoa sellowiana.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: feijoa - an edible green fruit from the genus Feijoa

Etymology

Named after João da Silva Feijó, Portuguese naturalist.
