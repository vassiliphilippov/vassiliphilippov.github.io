The Star-Spangled Banner

1. PROPN

/ ði stɑɹ ˈspæŋɡəld ˈbænɚ / (AmE), / ði stɑː ˈspæŋɡəld ˈbænə / (BrE)
the national anthem of the United States, originally a poem by Francis Scott Key set to music and adopted in 1931
U.S. national anthem
american

Examples

Before every major sports event in the U.S., The Star-Spangled Banner is sung or played.
Francis Scott Key wrote the words to The Star-Spangled Banner during the War of 1812.
Many Americans stand and remove their hats when The Star-Spangled Banner is performed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: national anthem - an official song of praise for a country

Etymology

The term 'star-spangled banner' refers to the flag of the United States, decorated with stars.
