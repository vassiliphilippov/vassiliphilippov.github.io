striking distance

1. NOUN

/ ˈstraɪkɪŋ ˌdɪstəns / (AmE), / ˈstraɪkɪŋ ˌdɪstəns / (BrE)
A distance close enough to reach, achieve, or affect something, often used metaphorically to mean being close to a goal or objective.
close enough to reach or achieve

Examples

The team was finally within striking distance of winning the championship.
Investors watched as the company's stock came within striking distance of its all-time high.
As soon as the hikers were in striking distance of the summit, the weather turned bad.

Synset Examples

The finish line is just a mile away, within striking distance.
We're within striking distance of our sales goal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: within reach - close enough to be achieved or obtained
  • Antonym: out of reach - too far to be achieved or obtained

Etymology

Originally a military/naval term, referring to being close enough to strike an enemy.
