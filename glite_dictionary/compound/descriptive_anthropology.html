descriptive anthropology

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈskrɪptɪv ænθrəˈpɑlədʒi / (AmE), / dɪˈskrɪptɪv ænθrəˈpɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the branch of anthropology concerned with providing scientific descriptions of individual human societies and cultures.
study of scientifically describing human societies

Examples

In descriptive anthropology, researchers focus on collecting detailed data about rituals and traditions of specific groups.
Her work in descriptive anthropology earned her recognition for accurately recording the lifestyles of remote tribes.
The museum's exhibit is based on findings from decades of descriptive anthropology in Southeast Asia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cultural anthropology - the study of cultural variation among humans
  • Synonym: ethnography - the scientific description of the customs of peoples and cultures

Etymology

From 'description' (Latin descriptio, a sketch or account) and 'anthropology' (Greek anthrōpos, human + -logia, the study of).
