Upper Tunguska

1. PROPN

/ ˈʌpɚ tʊŋˈguːskə / (AmE), / ˈʌpə tʊŋˈɡuːskə / (BrE)
a river in southeastern Siberia that flows northwest from Lake Baikal to join the Yenisei River
river in southeastern Siberia

Examples

The Upper Tunguska was a major route for traders in Siberia during the 18th century.
During the spring thaw, the Upper Tunguska often floods its banks.
Scientists conducted ecological studies along the Upper Tunguska last summer.
