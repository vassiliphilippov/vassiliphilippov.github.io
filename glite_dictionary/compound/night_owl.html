night owl

1. NOUN

/ ˈnaɪt ˌaʊl / (AmE), / ˈnaɪt ˌaʊl / (BrE)
a person who prefers to stay awake and be active late at night rather than in the morning
person active late at night

Examples

My brother is a true night owl who gets most of his work done after midnight.
Being a night owl, she often finds it hard to wake up early for work.
Many night owls enjoy the calm and quiet of late evening hours.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: early bird - a person who rises early in the morning

Etymology

From 'owl', a nocturnal bird, figuratively applied to people active at night.

2. NOUN

/ ˈnaɪt ˌaʊl / (AmE), / ˈnaɪt ˌaʊl / (BrE)
a person who habitually stays up late and is active during the night
person who stays up late

Examples

My sister is a night owl who loves to read until the early hours of the morning.
Although he tries to adjust to office hours, he's always been more of a night owl.
Being a night owl makes it hard for her to schedule early morning appointments.

Synset Examples

I'm more of a night owl than a day lark.
Being a night owl, she struggles to wake early.
He's a night owl who works best after dark.
As a night owl, he often misses morning meetings.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: morning person - a person who feels awake and energetic in the morning
  • Antonym: early bird - a person who gets up early and is most alert in the morning

Etymology

From the nocturnal habits of owls, known for being active at night.
