nuclear chemistry

1. NOUN

/ ˈnuː.kli.ɚ ˈkɛm.ɪ.stri / (AmE), / ˈnjuː.klɪ.ə ˈkɛm.ɪ.stri / (BrE)
The branch of chemistry concerned with radioactive substances and the chemical processes involving nuclear reactions.
chemistry of radioactive substances

Examples

She decided to specialize in nuclear chemistry for her graduate studies.
Advances in nuclear chemistry have led to new ways of generating energy.
Nuclear chemistry plays an important role in the treatment of certain cancers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: radiochemistry - chemistry dealing with radioactive materials

Etymology

From 'nuclear', relating to the nucleus of an atom, and 'chemistry', from Greek *khēmeia* (alchemy).
