music of the spheres

1. NOUN

/ ˈmjuː.zɪk əv ðə sfɪrz / (AmE), / ˈmjuː.zɪk ɒv ðə sfɪəz / (BrE)
an ancient philosophical concept that regards the movements of celestial bodies as producing a form of inaudible, harmonious music
ancient idea of celestial harmony

Examples

Many poets have referenced the music of the spheres in their descriptions of a harmonious universe.
Philosophers once believed that the music of the spheres could influence human moods and destinies.
She found the idea of the music of the spheres both mysterious and inspiring.

Etymology

Refers to the cosmological theory of Pythagoras and later philosophers; popularized in English from the 14th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmjuːzɪk əv ðə sfɪrz / (AmE), / ˈmjuːzɪk əv ðə sfɪəz / (BrE)
the philosophical concept that the proportions of celestial bodies create a form of harmonious music not heard by the human ear, symbolizing cosmic order and beauty
philosophical idea of celestial harmony

Examples

The poet described the harmony of nature as the music of the spheres.
For centuries, philosophers pondered the meaning of the music of the spheres.
Listening to the orchestra, she imagined the music of the spheres resonating through the heavens.

Synset Examples

Her poetry felt like the music of the spheres to my soul.
The astronomer described the universe as the music of the spheres.

Etymology

Originates from ancient Greek philosophy (Pythagoras and Plato), linking astronomy and music.
