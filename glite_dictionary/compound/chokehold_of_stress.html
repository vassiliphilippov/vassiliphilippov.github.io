chokehold of stress

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃoʊkˌhoʊld əv strɛs / (AmE), / ˈtʃəʊkˌhəʊld əv strɛs / (BrE)
a situation in which stress is so intense and overwhelming that it feels as if it is physically constraining or suffocating you
intense, overwhelming stress

Examples

During her divorce, he was caught in the chokehold of stress and struggled to focus at work.
The chokehold of stress left him exhausted and unable to sleep for weeks on end.
Many people experience the chokehold of stress when balancing demanding jobs and personal responsibilities.

Synset Examples

She felt the chokehold of stress during finals week.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chokehold - a firm grip around someone's neck or a controlling influence
