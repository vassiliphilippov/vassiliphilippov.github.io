recombinant DNA technology

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈkɑmbənənt ˌdiːˌɛnˈeɪ tɛkˈnɑlədʒi / (AmE), / rɪˈkɒmbɪnənt ˌdiːˌɛnˈeɪ tɛkˈnɒlədʒi / (BrE)
a set of techniques for joining DNA from different organisms in vitro, enabling the creation of new genetic combinations
techniques for combining DNA from different organisms

Examples

Recombinant DNA technology has revolutionized the production of human insulin for diabetes treatment.
Through recombinant DNA technology, scientists can insert beneficial genes into crops to improve yields.
The use of recombinant DNA technology has raised ethical questions about genetic modification and its impact on society.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: genetic engineering - the deliberate modification of characteristics of an organism by manipulating its genetic material

Etymology

From recombinant (from Latin 're-' + 'combinare', to combine) + DNA + technology.
