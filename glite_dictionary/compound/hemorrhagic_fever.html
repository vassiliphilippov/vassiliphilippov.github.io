hemorrhagic fever

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɛm.əˈrædʒ.ɪk ˈfiː.vɚ / (AmE), / ˌhɛm.əˈrædʒ.ɪk ˈfiː.və / (BrE)
Any of a group of viral diseases characterized by fever, bleeding, and other symptoms, commonly occurring in specific geographic areas.
viral disease causing fever and bleeding
medical

Examples

Several outbreaks of hemorrhagic fever have caused international concern due to their high mortality rates.
Ebola is a well-known form of hemorrhagic fever with severe symptoms.
The World Health Organization monitors regions at risk for hemorrhagic fever transmission.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: viral hemorrhagic fever - a disease caused by viruses leading to fever and bleeding

Etymology

From 'hemorrhagic' (relating to bleeding) and 'fever'.
