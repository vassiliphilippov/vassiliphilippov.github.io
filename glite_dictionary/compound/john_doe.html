John Doe

1. NOUN

/ dʒɑn doʊ / (AmE), / dʒɒn dəʊ / (BrE)
A name used to refer to an unidentified or generic man, especially in legal contexts or when the person's real name is unknown.
generic or unidentified man

Examples

The police listed the unidentified victim as John Doe in their report.
Hospitals often admit patients who cannot give their names as John Doe.
The lawsuit was filed against several John Does whose identities were not known.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Jane Doe - a name used for an unidentified or generic woman

Etymology

Originally used in English legal practice for an anonymous or hypothetical male party to a case, paired with 'Richard Roe'.

2. NOUN

/ dʒɑn doʊ / (AmE), / dʒɒn dəʊ / (BrE)
a name used in legal cases to refer to an unidentified or anonymous man, especially as a party to a lawsuit
unidentified man in legal cases
legal

Examples

The police were unable to identify the victim, so he was listed as John Doe in the report.
In the lawsuit, the defendant was referred to as John Doe until his real name became known.
The court documents named several John Does as possible witnesses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Jane Doe - unidentified female in legal cases

Etymology

Originated in English legal tradition as a placeholder name for an unknown male; often paired with 'Richard Roe'.
