Provisional IRA

1. PROPN

/ prəˈvɪʒənəl aɪ ɑr eɪ / (AmE), / prəˈvɪʒənəl aɪ ɑː ɑː / (BrE)
a militant organization of Irish nationalists active from 1969, seeking to remove British forces from Northern Ireland and unite Ireland, often through acts of terrorism and guerilla warfare
Irish nationalist militant organization (1969–)

Examples

The British government held talks with the Provisional IRA in hopes of ending the violence in Northern Ireland.
Several members of the Provisional IRA were arrested after a failed bombing attempt in Belfast.
The legacy of the Provisional IRA continues to influence politics in both Northern Ireland and the Republic of Ireland.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: IRA - Irish Republican Army, a paramilitary organization that fought for Irish independence

Etymology

Shortened from 'Provisional Irish Republican Army', formed 1969; 'provisional' distinguishes it from earlier IRA factions.
