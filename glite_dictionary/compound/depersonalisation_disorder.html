depersonalisation disorder

1. NOUN

/ diːˌpɜːrsənəˈleɪʒən dɪsˌɔrdər / (AmE), / diːˌpɜːsənəlaɪˈzeɪʃən dɪsˌɔːdə / (BrE)
A psychological disorder characterized by persistent or recurrent feelings of detachment from one's own thoughts, feelings, or body, often accompanied by a sense of unreality.
psychological disorder with feelings of detachment or unreality
medical

Examples

People with depersonalisation disorder often feel disconnected from their thoughts or bodies.
After experiencing trauma, she was diagnosed with depersonalisation disorder and began therapy.
It is common for those with depersonalisation disorder to report feeling like an outside observer of themselves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: derealisation disorder - a disorder marked by feelings that the external world is unreal

Etymology

From 'depersonalisation,' based on Latin 'de-' (removal) + 'persona' (person), and 'disorder.'
