relaxation time

1. NOUN

/ ˌriːlækˈseɪʃən taɪm / (AmE), / ˌriːlækˈseɪʃən taɪm / (BrE)
The time required for a system to return to equilibrium after a disturbance, typically measured as the time constant in an exponential process.
time for system to return to equilibrium

Examples

The relaxation time of a material determines how quickly it can adapt to changes in its environment.
After the magnetic field was removed, the relaxation time was measured to analyze the sample's properties.
Different tissues in the body have different relaxation times, which are important in MRI imaging.
