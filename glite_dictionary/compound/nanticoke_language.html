Nanticoke language

1. NOUN

/ ˈnæn.tɪˌkoʊk ˈlæŋ.ɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈnæn.tɪ.kəʊk ˈlaŋ.ɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Algonquian language historically spoken by the Nanticoke and Conoy peoples of the eastern United States
language of the Nanticoke and Conoy peoples

Examples

Few people today are fluent in the Nanticoke language due to its endangered status.
Researchers have compiled a dictionary to help preserve the Nanticoke language.
The Nanticoke language reflects the culture and traditions of the Nanticoke people.

Etymology

From Nanticoke, the name of a Native American people and their principal village, likely from an Algonquian root.
