Dalmatian iris

1. NOUN

/ dælˈmeɪʃən ˈaɪrɪs / (AmE), / dælˈmeɪʃən ˈaɪrɪs / (BrE)
A European species of iris with soft lilac-blue flowers, native to the Dalmatia region.
European iris with lilac-blue flowers

Examples

The Dalmatian iris is prized in gardens for its delicate lilac-blue petals.
She planted several Dalmatian irises along the pathway to add a splash of color.
Botanists have studied the Dalmatian iris to better understand its adaptation to rocky soils.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: iris - A genus of flowering plants with showy flowers

Etymology

Dalmatian from Dalmatia, a region on the Adriatic coast (now part of Croatia); iris from Greek 'iris', meaning rainbow.
