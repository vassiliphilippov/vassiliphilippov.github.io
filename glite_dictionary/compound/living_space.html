living space

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪvɪŋ speɪs / (AmE), / ˈlɪvɪŋ speɪs / (BrE)
(historical) Land or territory sought for occupation or expansion by a nation, especially regarded as necessary for its population or development.
land or territory sought for national expansion

Examples

In the 1930s, the regime justified its invasions by arguing the nation needed more living space.
The policy of acquiring living space led to aggressive expansion into neighboring countries.
Historians debate the consequences of seeking living space for national security and population growth.

Etymology

Calque of German 'Lebensraum' (living space).
