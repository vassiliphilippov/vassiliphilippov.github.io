Athyrium filix-femina

1. NOUN

/ əˈθɪriəm ˌfɪlɪks ˈfɛmɪnə / (AmE), / əˈθɪəriəm ˌfɪlɪks ˈfiːlɪks ˈfɛmɪnə / (BrE)
a species of fern, commonly known as the lady fern, valued for its delicate, feathery foliage and often cultivated in gardens
a species of fern with delicate foliage

Examples

The Athyrium filix-femina is a popular choice for shady garden borders due to its graceful fronds.
Botanists easily recognize Athyrium filix-femina by its light green, feathery leaves.
In spring, new fronds of Athyrium filix-femina unfurl elegantly in woodland gardens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lady fern - common name for Athyrium filix-femina

Etymology

From Latin: Athyrium (genus name); filix-femina meaning 'female fern' or 'lady fern' in Latin.
