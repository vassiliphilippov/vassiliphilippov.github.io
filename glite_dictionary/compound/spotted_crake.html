spotted crake

1. NOUN

/ ˈspɑtəd kreɪk / (AmE), / ˈspɒtɪd kreɪk / (BrE)
a small Eurasian rail (Porzana porzana) that inhabits swamps and marshes, noted for its spotted plumage
a small Eurasian marsh bird

Examples

The spotted crake is rarely seen due to its secretive habits and reedbed habitat.
During migration, birdwatchers in Europe search for the elusive spotted crake.
The distinctive call of the spotted crake can be heard in wetlands at dusk.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crake - a small rail, a type of bird found in marshes and wetlands
