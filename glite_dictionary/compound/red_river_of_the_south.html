Red River of the South

1. PROPN

/ ˌrɛd ˈrɪvɚ əv ðə ˈsaʊθ / (AmE), / ˌrɛd ˈrɪvə əv ðə ˈsaʊθ / (BrE)
a river in the south-central United States, a tributary of the Mississippi River that flows east from Texas along the southern border of Oklahoma and through Louisiana
river in southern U.S., Mississippi tributary

Examples

The Red River of the South forms part of the border between Texas and Oklahoma.
Early settlers followed the Red River of the South as they moved westward.
Flooding along the Red River of the South has shaped local communities for generations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mississippi River - major river in central United States
