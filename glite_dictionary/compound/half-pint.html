half-pint

1. NOUN

/ ˈhæfˌpaɪnt / (AmE), / ˈhɑːfˌpaɪnt / (BrE)
A unit of liquid measurement equal to half of a pint, especially used for beer or milk.
half a pint (liquid measure)

Examples

After a long walk, he enjoyed a half-pint of cold cider at the inn.
Children at school were each given a half-pint of milk with lunch.
She ordered a half-pint instead of a full pint, as she wasn't very thirsty.

Synset Examples

He ordered a half-pint of ale at the pub.
The kid drank his half-pint of milk quickly.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: pint - a unit of liquid or dry capacity; commonly used for beer, milk, etc.

Etymology

Formed from 'half' and 'pint'; 'pint' from Old French 'pinte'.
