tax benefit

1. NOUN

/ tæks ˈbɛnɪfɪt / (AmE), / tæks ˈbɛnɪfɪt / (BrE)
An allowance, deduction, or exemption in tax regulations designed to encourage certain types of commercial activity or provide financial relief.
allowance or deduction in tax regulations

Examples

The government introduced a new tax benefit for companies investing in renewable energy.
Families with children may be eligible for a tax benefit under the new legislation.
The tax benefit helped small businesses reduce their overall expenses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tax break - a special reduction in taxes
  • Synonym: tax deduction - an amount that can be subtracted from gross income to reduce taxable income
  • Antonym: tax penalty - an additional charge added due to violation of tax rules
