melodic line

1. NOUN

/ məˈlɑːdɪk laɪn / (AmE), / məˈlɒdɪk laɪn / (BrE)
a succession of musical notes arranged in a distinctive sequence, forming the main tune of a piece of music
main sequence of notes in music

Examples

The clarinet carried the melodic line throughout the second movement.
She practiced the melodic line until every note flowed smoothly into the next.
The composer wrote a new melodic line for the chorus.

Synset Examples

she was humming an air from Beethoven

Related Terms

  • Synonym: melody - a sequence of musical notes that are perceived as a single entity; the tune
