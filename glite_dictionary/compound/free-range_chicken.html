free-range chicken

1. NOUN

/ ˌfriˈreɪndʒ ˈtʃɪkɪn / (AmE), / ˌfriːˈreɪndʒ ˈtʃɪkɪn / (BrE)
A chicken that is raised in a natural environment with access to the outdoors, rather than being confined in cages or crowded spaces.
chicken raised with outdoor access

Examples

We always buy free-range chicken because we believe the meat is tastier and more humane.
The restaurant's menu highlights dishes made with locally sourced free-range chicken.
Raising free-range chickens can be more labor-intensive, but many farmers find it worthwhile.

Synset Examples

The farm raises free-range chickens for better quality meat.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: battery chicken - a chicken raised in cages or confined conditions
