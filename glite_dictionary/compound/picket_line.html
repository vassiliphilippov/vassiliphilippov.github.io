picket line

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪkɪt laɪn / (AmE), / ˈpɪkɪt laɪn / (BrE)
a line or group of people, often striking workers, standing or marching outside a place of work to protest or prevent others from entering during a dispute.
line of striking or protesting workers

Examples

The workers formed a picket line outside the factory gates to protest wage cuts.
Crossing the picket line during a strike is seen as controversial by many employees.
Police kept a close eye on the picket line to ensure the demonstration remained peaceful.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strike - an organized stoppage of work as a form of protest
  • Synonym: protest - the act of expressing disagreement or objection

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɪk.ɪt ˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈpɪk.ɪt ˌlaɪn / (BrE)
a line or group of people standing or marching outside a workplace or other location to protest or prevent others from entering during a strike or demonstration
line of demonstrators during a strike

Examples

The striking workers formed a picket line in front of the factory gate.
Police were called to maintain order near the picket line.
Crossing a picket line is often seen as breaking solidarity with fellow workers.

Synset Examples

She joined the picket line to support the union's cause.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strike - a work stoppage by employees as a form of protest

Etymology

From 'picket' (demonstrator or striker) + 'line' (row, queue), used since early 20th century in labor disputes.
