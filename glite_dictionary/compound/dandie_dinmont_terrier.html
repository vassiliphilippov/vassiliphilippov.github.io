Dandie Dinmont terrier

1. NOUN

/ ˈdændi ˈdɪnmɑnt ˈtɛriɚ / (AmE), / ˈdændi ˈdɪnmɒnt ˈtɛriə / (BrE)
a small breed of terrier with a long, wiry coat and drooping ears, known for its distinctive appearance and affectionate nature
a breed of small, wiry-coated terrier

Examples

The Dandie Dinmont terrier is known for its friendly personality and unique topknot of hair.
She adopted a Dandie Dinmont terrier from the animal shelter last year.
At the dog show, the Dandie Dinmont terrier caught everyone's attention with its distinctive look.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: terrier - a group of dog breeds originally bred to hunt vermin

Etymology

Named after the character Dandie Dinmont in Sir Walter Scott's novel 'Guy Mannering' (1815).
