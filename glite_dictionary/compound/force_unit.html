force unit

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔrs ˌjuː.nɪt / (AmE), / ˈfɔːs ˌjuː.nɪt / (BrE)
a unit used to measure physical force, such as the newton
unit for measuring force

Examples

In physics, the force unit most commonly used is the newton.
Engineers often convert one force unit to another for different measurement systems.
The pound-force is an older force unit still used in some countries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: newton - the SI unit of force, equal to the force that gives a mass of one kilogram an acceleration of one metre per second squared
