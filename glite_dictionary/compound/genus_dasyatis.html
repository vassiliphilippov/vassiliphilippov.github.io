genus Dasyatis

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˌdæsiˈætɪs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˌdæsɪˈatɪs / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Dasyatidae, comprising several species of stingrays commonly found in marine and freshwater environments.
type genus of stingray family Dasyatidae
medical

Examples

Several species classified under the genus Dasyatis inhabit both marine and freshwater environments.
Researchers studied the venomous spine of a species in the genus Dasyatis.
The genus Dasyatis includes some of the most widely distributed stingrays.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stingray - A group of rays, cartilaginous fish related to sharks, known for their flat bodies and barbed tails.

Etymology

From New Latin 'Dasyatis', from Greek 'dasys' (hairy) + 'batis' (a kind of fish or ray).
