ablative case

1. NOUN

/ ˈæbləˌtɪv keɪs / (AmE), / ˈablətɪv keɪs / (BrE)
(in grammar) a case of nouns, pronouns, and adjectives found in some languages, used to show movement away from something, or the means or manner by which an action is performed.
a grammatical case indicating movement away or means

Examples

Latin uses the ablative case to indicate motion away from a place.
In some languages, the ablative case can also express means or manner.
Students must learn the different endings for the ablative case in Latin grammar.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: genitive case - a grammatical case indicating possession or origin
  • Synonym: instrumental case - a grammatical case indicating the instrument used

Etymology

From Latin *ablativus*, from *ablatus* 'taken away', from *auferre* 'to carry away'.
