eastern coral snake

1. NOUN

/ ˈiːstɚn ˈkɔrəl sneɪk / (AmE), / ˈiːstən ˈkɒrəl sneɪk / (BrE)
a highly venomous snake (Micrurus fulvius) found in the southeastern United States and parts of Central America, recognized by its bands of red, yellow, and black
a venomous North American snake with vivid bands

Examples

The eastern coral snake has a potent neurotoxic venom that can be dangerous to humans.
Children are taught to recognize the red, yellow, and black bands of the eastern coral snake for safety.
Unlike many other snakes, the eastern coral snake is secretive and rarely seen during the day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coral snake - any venomous snake of the subfamily Elapinae with bright colored bands
