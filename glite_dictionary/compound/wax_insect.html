wax insect

1. NOUN

/ wæks ˈɪnˌsɛkt / (AmE), / waks ˈɪn.sɛkt / (BrE)
any insect that secretes a waxy substance, especially for protective or structural purposes.
insect that produces a waxy substance

Examples

The wax insect coats its body with a protective layer to prevent dehydration.
Certain species of wax insects play a significant role in the ecosystem by producing wax.
Farmers sometimes encounter wax insects while inspecting their crops for pests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wax scale - a type of insect from the family Coccidae known for producing wax
