press of sail

1. NOUN

/ prɛs əv seɪl / (AmE), / prɛs əv seɪl / (BrE)
the maximum amount of sail that a ship can carry safely under prevailing conditions
maximum safe amount of sail on a ship

Examples

The captain ordered the press of sail to be reduced as a storm approached.
With a full press of sail, the ship raced ahead of its pursuers.
The old clipper set a daring press of sail despite the heavy winds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sail - a piece of material extended on a ship to catch the wind
