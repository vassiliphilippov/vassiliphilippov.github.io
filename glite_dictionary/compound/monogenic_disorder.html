monogenic disorder

1. NOUN

/ ˌmoʊnəˈdʒɛnɪk dɪsˈɔrdər / (AmE), / ˌməʊnəˈdʒɛnɪk dɪsˈɔːdə / (BrE)
a genetic disorder caused by mutations in a single gene pair, inherited in a Mendelian manner
disorder caused by a single gene mutation
medical

Examples

Sickle cell anemia is a classic example of a monogenic disorder.
Researchers have identified over 7,000 rare monogenic disorders to date.
Treatments for monogenic disorder often target the faulty gene directly.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: polygenic disorder - a disorder controlled by multiple genes

Etymology

From mono- (one) and -genic (producing), referring to a single gene origin.
