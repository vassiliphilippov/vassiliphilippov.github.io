shittah tree

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃɪtə tri / (AmE), / ˈʃɪtə triː / (BrE)
A tree mentioned in the Bible, believed to refer to a species of acacia, valued for its durable wood.
Biblical acacia tree

Examples

According to the Old Testament, the Ark of the Covenant was made from shittah tree wood.
Botanists believe the shittah tree is a kind of acacia native to the Sinai region.
The shittah tree is valued in biblical texts for its strong, resilient timber.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acacia - A genus of trees and shrubs, many species found in warm climates

Etymology

From Hebrew *shittāh* (acacia tree), mentioned in the Bible.
