soapberry family

1. NOUN

/ ˈsoʊpˌbɛri ˈfæməli / (AmE), / ˈsəʊpˌbɛri ˈfæməli / (BrE)
a family of chiefly tropical and subtropical trees and shrubs (Sapindaceae) with leathery drupes; most plants produce toxic saponins
family of tropical trees and shrubs (Sapindaceae)

Examples

The lychee tree is a well-known member of the soapberry family.
Many plants in the soapberry family produce saponins, which can be toxic in large quantities.
Botanists grouped the new species with the soapberry family after extensive genetic studies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sapindaceae - the scientific name for the soapberry family of plants
