to dance the hucklebuck

1. VERB

/ dæns ðə ˈhʌkəlˌbʌk / (AmE), / dɑːns ðə ˈhʌkəlˌbʌk / (BrE)
to perform a lively dance characterized by energetic movements, popularized in the 1940s and 1950s, often associated with rhythm and blues music
perform the hucklebuck dance

Examples

At the wedding reception, everyone got up to dance the hucklebuck as the band played.
She taught the children how to dance the hucklebuck during their summer camp talent show.
Back in the day, my grandparents would dance the hucklebuck at every family gathering.

Synset Examples

They danced the hucklebuck all night at the party.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hucklebuck - a lively dance style or the music associated with it

Etymology

From the song "The Hucklebuck" (1949), which popularized the dance in American rhythm and blues culture.
