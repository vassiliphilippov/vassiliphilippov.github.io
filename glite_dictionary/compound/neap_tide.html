neap tide

1. NOUN

/ nip taɪd / (AmE), / niːp taɪd / (BrE)
A tide that occurs twice a month, when the difference between high and low water is least, happening at the first and third quarters of the moon.
lowest range of tides, twice monthly

Examples

During a neap tide, the difference between high and low water is at its smallest.
Sailors often prefer to dock their boats during a neap tide because the water level is more stable.
The tidal range was minimal yesterday due to the occurrence of a neap tide.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: spring tide - a tide with the greatest difference between high and low water, occurring at new and full moons

Etymology

From Old English *nēp* (scanty) + 'tide'.
