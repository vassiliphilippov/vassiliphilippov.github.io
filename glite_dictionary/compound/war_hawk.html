war hawk

1. NOUN

/ wɔr hɑk / (AmE), / wɔː hɒk / (BrE)
a person who strongly supports war or aggressive policies in international relations
advocate of aggressive foreign policy

Examples

The senator was criticized as a war hawk for supporting military intervention.
Many war hawks in Congress pushed for direct action.
His war hawk views were unpopular among pacifist voters.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dove - a person who opposes war or supports peaceful policies
  • Synonym: hawk - a person who favors aggressive or warlike policies, especially in foreign affairs

Etymology

Originally American English, formed by analogy with 'hawk' (a person who advocates war) in contrast to 'dove'.

2. NOUN

/ wɔr hɑk / (AmE), / wɔː hɒk / (BrE)
a person who strongly supports or advocates for the use of military force, especially in international relations or conflicts.
person advocating military intervention

Examples

The senator was labeled a war hawk after urging for military intervention in the crisis.
During the debate, her war hawk stance drew criticism from her colleagues.
Many voters were wary of electing a war hawk to handle diplomatic relations.

Synset Examples

The senator was known as a war hawk, always pushing for military action.
The war hawk's speeches stirred up support for intervention.
As a war hawk, he advocated for stronger defense budgets.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dove - a person who opposes war or promotes peaceful policies

Etymology

Metaphorical use of 'hawk' (a bird of prey) to indicate aggression, dating from early 19th-century American politics.
