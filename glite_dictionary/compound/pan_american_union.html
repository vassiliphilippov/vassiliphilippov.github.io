Pan American Union

1. NOUN

/ ˌpæn əˈmɛr.ɪ.kən ˈjuː.njən / (AmE), / ˌpan əˈmɛr.ɪ.kən ˈjuː.njən / (BrE)
the administrative agency that served as the predecessor to the Organization of American States, responsible for fostering cooperation among countries in the Americas
former agency of American States organization

Examples

The Pan American Union played a key role in fostering dialogue among member countries.
Historical documents from the Pan American Union are preserved in the OAS archives.
The Pan American Union ceased to exist when the Organization of American States was established.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Organization of American States - an international organization founded to promote solidarity and cooperation among American states

Etymology

From 'Pan American' (relating to all the countries of North, Central, and South America) and 'Union' (association or organization).
