Atlantic spiny dogfish

1. NOUN

/ ətˈlæntɪk ˈspaɪni ˈdɔɡˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ətˈlæntɪk ˈspaɪni ˈdɒɡˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
a small, slender shark (Squalus acanthias) found in Atlantic coastal waters of North America and Europe, noted for its sharp dorsal spines and common use in scientific dissection
small Atlantic dogfish shark species

Examples

The Atlantic spiny dogfish is often dissected in biology classes due to its distinctive anatomy.
Fishermen off the coast of New England sometimes catch Atlantic spiny dogfish in their nets.
Populations of the Atlantic spiny dogfish have declined in some areas due to overfishing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spiny dogfish - a small shark with sharp dorsal spines, found in temperate waters
