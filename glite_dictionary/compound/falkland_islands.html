Falkland Islands

1. PROPN

/ ˈfɔːk.lənd ˈaɪ.ləndz / (AmE), / ˈfɔːk.lənd ˈaɪ.ləndz / (BrE)
a group of over 100 islands in the South Atlantic Ocean, east of southern Argentina, governed as a British Overseas Territory
British territory of islands in South Atlantic
british

Examples

The sovereignty of the Falkland Islands has long been a subject of dispute.
Tourists visit the Falkland Islands for their unique wildlife and dramatic scenery.
She spent two years working as a teacher in the Falkland Islands.

Etymology

Named after Anthony Cary, 5th Viscount Falkland, who was the Treasurer of the Navy when the islands were first sighted by Europeans.
