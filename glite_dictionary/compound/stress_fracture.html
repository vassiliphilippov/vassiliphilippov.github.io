stress fracture

1. NOUN

/ stɹɛs ˈfræk.tʃɚ / (AmE), / stres ˈfræk.tʃə / (BrE)
a small crack in a bone caused by repeated stress or overuse, rather than by a single sudden injury
a bone crack caused by repeated stress
medical

Examples

The runner developed a stress fracture after increasing his mileage too quickly.
After weeks of pain, she was diagnosed with a stress fracture in her shin.
Athletes are at a higher risk of experiencing a stress fracture due to repetitive impact.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fracture - a break or crack in a bone
