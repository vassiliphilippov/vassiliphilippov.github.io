American dogwood

1. NOUN

/ əˈmɛr.ɪ.kən ˈdɔɡ.wʊd / (AmE), / əˈmɛr.ɪ.kən ˈdɒɡ.wʊd / (BrE)
A common North American shrub or small tree with reddish-purple twigs and clusters of white flowers, often grown for ornamental purposes.
North American shrub with white flowers

Examples

The American dogwood produces beautiful white blossoms every spring.
Gardeners often choose American dogwood for their landscapes because of its colorful twigs and flowers.
Birds are attracted to the berries of the American dogwood in autumn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dogwood - a type of tree or shrub with showy bracts
