elementary geometry

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛləˈmɛn.tə.ri dʒiˈɑː.mə.tri / (AmE), / ˌɛl.ɪˈmɛn.təri dʒiˈɒ.mə.tri / (BrE)
The branch of geometry dealing with the basic properties and relations of points, lines, angles, surfaces, and shapes, usually studied at school level and based on Euclid's axioms.
basic school-level geometry based on Euclidean principles

Examples

Most students are first introduced to elementary geometry in middle school mathematics classes.
Understanding elementary geometry is essential for studying more advanced fields like trigonometry and calculus.
He spent the afternoon reviewing elementary geometry concepts such as triangles and circles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Euclidean geometry - geometry based on Euclid's postulates

Etymology

From Latin 'geometria' (geometry) via Greek 'geōmetria', with 'elementary' from Latin 'elementarius' (rudimentary).
