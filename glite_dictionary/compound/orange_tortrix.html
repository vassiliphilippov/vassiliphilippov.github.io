orange tortrix

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔːrɪndʒ ˈtɔrtrɪks / (AmE), / ˈɒrɪndʒ ˈtɔːtrɪks / (BrE)
A species of moth (Argyrotaenia citrana) found in California, whose larvae feed on oranges and other fruit crops.
California moth whose larvae infest oranges

Examples

The orange tortrix can cause significant damage to citrus crops if not controlled.
Farmers monitor their orchards for signs of the orange tortrix larvae during the growing season.
Research is ongoing to find sustainable ways to manage orange tortrix infestations.
