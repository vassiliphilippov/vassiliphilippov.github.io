French rye

1. NOUN

/ frɛntʃ raɪ / (AmE), / frɛntʃ raɪ / (BrE)
a coarse perennial Eurasian grass, similar to oats, found on roadside verges, rough grassland, and hay meadows, and introduced in North America as a forage plant
a Eurasian grass used for forage

Examples

The farmer planted French rye in the meadow to improve soil quality and feed his livestock.
Clusters of French rye lined the roadside, giving the landscape a wild, rustic feel.
Researchers studied how French rye adapted to North American climates after introduction.
