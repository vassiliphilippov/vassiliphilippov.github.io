family Caeciliidae

1. NOUN

/ fæməli siːˌsɪliˈaɪdiː / (AmE), / fæməli siːˌsɪliːˈɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of legless amphibians, coextensive with the order Gymnophiona, commonly known as caecilians.
a family of legless amphibians

Examples

Members of the family Caeciliidae are known for their limbless, burrowing lifestyle in tropical regions.
Scientists study the distribution of the family Caeciliidae to better understand amphibian evolution.
The family Caeciliidae comprises several species of caecilians found mainly in South America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Gymnophiona - an order of legless, burrowing amphibians
  • Synonym: caecilian - common name for members of the family Caeciliidae

Etymology

From modern Latin Caeciliidae, based on Caecilia, type genus, from Latin caecus (blind), referring to their often reduced eyes.
