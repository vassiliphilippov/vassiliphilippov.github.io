so naff

1. ADJ

/ so næf / (AmE), / səʊ næf / (BrE)
Extremely poor in quality or unfashionable; very uncool or tacky, especially in British English.
extremely poor quality or unfashionable
informal slang british

Examples

I can't believe you bought that; it's so naff and out of style.
The decorations at the party were so naff, everyone was laughing at them.
Her playlist is so naff—nothing but cheesy old pop songs.

Synset Examples

That movie was so naff, I couldn't finish it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: naff - unfashionable or of poor quality (British slang)

Etymology

'Naff' is British slang, possibly from Polari (a form of slang used by British actors and the gay community).
