pilot engine

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪlət ˌɛn.dʒən / (AmE), / ˈpaɪlət ˌɛn.dʒɪn / (BrE)
a locomotive that runs ahead of a train to check the track for obstacles or dangers
locomotive sent ahead to check tracks

Examples

The railroad company sent a pilot engine ahead to ensure the track was clear after the storm.
A pilot engine led the main train through the mountainous region to check for landslides or obstructions.
Safety protocols required a pilot engine to precede passenger trains during harsh weather conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scout engine - a locomotive sent ahead to scout for dangers on the railway
