leopard's-bane

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛpərdzˌbeɪn / (AmE), / ˈlɛpədzˌbeɪn / (BrE)
any of several plants of the genus Doronicum, cultivated for their yellow, daisy-like flowers and often grown in gardens.
a plant of the genus Doronicum with yellow flowers

Examples

The bright yellow flowers of the leopard's-bane brightened up the garden in early spring.
Gardeners often choose leopard's-bane for its showy blossoms and long blooming period.
Despite its attractive appearance, leopard's-bane can be toxic to pets and wildlife if ingested.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arnica - a yellow-flowered plant sometimes also called 'leopard's-bane'

Etymology

Named for its supposed toxicity to leopards; 'bane' meaning something harmful.
