Anglican Catholic

1. NOUN

/ ˈæŋɡlɪkən ˈkæθlɪk / (AmE), / ˈæŋɡlɪkən ˈkæθlɪk / (BrE)
a member of the Anglican Church who emphasizes its Catholic traditions and practices
Anglican Church member with Catholic emphasis

Examples

As an Anglican Catholic, he cherishes both the liturgy and traditions of the early church.
Many Anglican Catholics attend services that closely resemble those of Roman Catholics.
The Anglican Catholic movement seeks to highlight the Catholic roots within Anglicanism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Anglican - a member of the Church of England or a church in communion with it
  • Antonym: Roman Catholic - a member of the Roman Catholic Church

Etymology

Formed from 'Anglican' (relating to the Church of England) and 'Catholic' (universal church).
