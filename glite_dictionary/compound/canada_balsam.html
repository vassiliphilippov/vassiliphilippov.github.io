Canada balsam

1. NOUN

/ kəˈnædə ˈbælsəm / (AmE), / kəˈnɑːdə ˈbælsəm / (BrE)
A medium-sized fir tree (Abies balsamea) of northeastern North America, whose aromatic resin is used in mounting microscope slides and whose wood is commonly used for pulpwood and Christmas trees.
medium-sized North American fir tree

Examples

The aromatic resin of the Canada balsam is prized for its use in optical instruments.
Every December, we buy a fresh Canada balsam for our living room Christmas tree.
The forest was filled with the scent of Canada balsam after the rain.

Etymology

Named for Canada and 'balsam', from Latin 'balsamum' (balsam, resin).

2. NOUN

/ kəˈnædə ˈbɔl.səm / (AmE), / kəˈnæd.ə ˈbæl.səm / (BrE)
a yellow, transparent resin obtained from the balsam fir, used as a cement in optical devices and as a mounting medium in microscopy
yellow resin from balsam fir used in optics and microscopy

Examples

The slides were permanently mounted using Canada balsam as the adhesive.
Optical lenses are sometimes cemented together with Canada balsam for clarity.
She prepared the microscope sample and sealed it with Canada balsam.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: balsam - a fragrant oily resin obtained from certain trees and used in medicine and perfumery

Etymology

Named for its Canadian origin and the balsam fir tree from which it is obtained.
