corn cob

1. NOUN

/ kɔrn kɑb / (AmE), / kɔːn kɒb / (BrE)
the hard, cylindrical central part of an ear of corn that holds the kernels
the core of an ear of corn

Examples

After eating the sweet kernels, he discarded the corn cob in the compost bin.
The recipe called for boiling the corn cob to make a flavorful broth.
Farmers sometimes use dried corn cobs as animal bedding or fuel.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: ear of corn - the entire seed head of a corn plant
