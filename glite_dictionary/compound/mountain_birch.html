mountain birch

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaʊn.tɪn bɝːtʃ / (AmE), / ˈmaʊn.tɪn bɜːtʃ / (BrE)
a birch tree native to the western United States, resembling the paper birch but with brownish bark
birch tree of western U.S. with brownish bark

Examples

The mountain birch thrives in higher elevations of the Rocky Mountains.
Unlike the paper birch, the mountain birch has distinctive brownish bark.
Hikers often spot mountain birch trees along the forest edge during their climb.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paper birch - birch tree with white, papery bark
