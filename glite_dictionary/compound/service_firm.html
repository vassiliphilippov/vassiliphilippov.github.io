service firm

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɝː.vɪs fɝːm / (AmE), / ˈsɜː.vɪs fɜːm / (BrE)
a business that provides services or facilities to clients for a fee, rather than producing goods
business providing services for a fee

Examples

The law office is a service firm specializing in corporate clients.
Many companies outsource IT functions to a service firm to save costs.
As a service firm, their main asset is the expertise of their employees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: service company - organization providing services to clients
  • Antonym: manufacturing firm - a business that produces goods rather than services
