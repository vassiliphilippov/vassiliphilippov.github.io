Brewer's spruce

1. NOUN

/ ˈbruːərz spruːs / (AmE), / ˈbruːəz spruːs / (BrE)
a medium-sized spruce tree native to California and Oregon, noted for its pendulous branches
spruce tree from California and Oregon

Examples

The Brewer's spruce grows best in the mountains of northern California and southern Oregon.
Botanists are studying how climate change affects the distribution of Brewer's spruce.
The pendulous branches of the Brewer's spruce give it a graceful appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spruce - a type of evergreen coniferous tree

Etymology

Named after the American botanist William Henry Brewer (1828–1910).
