fat cell

1. NOUN

/ fæt sɛl / (AmE), / fæt sɛl / (BrE)
A cell in the body that stores fat, found mainly in adipose tissue.
a cell that stores fat
medical

Examples

When you gain weight, your fat cells increase in size as they store more fat.
Researchers are studying how fat cells contribute to metabolic disorders.
Infants have a higher number of fat cells compared to adults.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adipocyte - another term for fat cell
