Okeechobee Waterway

1. PROPN

/ ˌoʊkiˈtʃoʊbi ˈwɔtərˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˌəʊkiˈtʃəʊbi ˈwɔːtəweɪ / (BrE)
a waterway in southern Florida connecting the Atlantic Ocean and the Gulf of Mexico via Lake Okeechobee and the Caloosahatchee River, used primarily by small boats
waterway in southern Florida linking Atlantic and Gulf

Examples

The Okeechobee Waterway allows vessels to cross Florida without navigating around the state.
After entering the Okeechobee Waterway, the yacht passed through several locks before reaching the Gulf of Mexico.
Boating enthusiasts often plan trips along the Okeechobee Waterway for its scenic views and access to both coasts.
