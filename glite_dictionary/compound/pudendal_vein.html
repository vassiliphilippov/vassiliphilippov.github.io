pudendal vein

1. NOUN

/ pjuːˈdɛndəl veɪn / (AmE), / pjuːˈdɛndəl veɪn / (BrE)
a vein that drains blood from the external genitalia (pudendum) and perineum
vein of the external genitalia
medical

Examples

The pudendal vein plays a crucial role in draining blood from the perineal region.
During the surgery, the doctor identified the pudendal vein to avoid accidental injury.
Blood clots in the pudendal vein are extremely rare but can cause significant discomfort.

Etymology

From Latin 'pudenda' meaning 'external genital organs' and Latin 'vena' meaning 'vein'.
