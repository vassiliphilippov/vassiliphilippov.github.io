throat infection

1. NOUN

/ ˈθroʊt ɪnˌfɛkʃən / (AmE), / ˈθrəʊt ɪnˌfɛkʃən / (BrE)
an infection of the throat, often involving the pharynx and tonsils, usually caused by bacteria or viruses
infection affecting the throat
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed her with a throat infection after she complained of pain and difficulty swallowing.
Children are particularly susceptible to throat infections during the winter months.
Antibiotics may be prescribed if your throat infection is caused by bacteria.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pharyngitis - inflammation of the pharynx, often called a sore throat
