metallographic sample

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛt̬l̩əˈɡræfɪk ˈsæmpəl / (AmE), / ˌmɛtələˈɡræfɪk ˈsæmpəl / (BrE)
a carefully prepared specimen of metal used for microscopic examination and analysis of its structure
prepared metal specimen for microscopic analysis

Examples

The technician prepared a metallographic sample to study the grain boundaries in the steel alloy.
Accurate grinding and polishing are crucial steps in creating a high-quality metallographic sample.
Each metallographic sample must be etched before microscopic examination to reveal structural features.

Synset Examples

The surface of a metallographic specimen is prepared by various methods of grinding, polishing, and etching.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: metallurgical specimen - a specimen of metal prepared for examination, similar context

Etymology

From 'metallography' (the study of the structure of metals) + 'sample.'
