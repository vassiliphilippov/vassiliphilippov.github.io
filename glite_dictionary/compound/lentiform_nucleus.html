lentiform nucleus

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛn.tɪ.fɔrm ˈnuː.kli.əs / (AmE), / ˈlɛn.tɪ.fɔːm ˈnjuː.kli.əs / (BrE)
a lens-shaped mass of grey matter in the basal ganglia of the brain, consisting of the putamen and the globus pallidus
lens-shaped basal ganglion in the brain
medical

Examples

The lentiform nucleus plays a key role in motor control within the basal ganglia.
An MRI scan can reveal abnormalities in the lentiform nucleus in certain neurological disorders.
Both the putamen and globus pallidus form the lentiform nucleus of the brain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: basal ganglia - group of nuclei in the brain linked to movement and coordination
  • Degree Lower: putamen - outer part of the lentiform nucleus
  • Degree Higher: globus pallidus - inner pale part of the lentiform nucleus

Etymology

From Latin 'lentiformis' (lentil-shaped) and 'nucleus' (kernel, core).
