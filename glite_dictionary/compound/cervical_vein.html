cervical vein

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɜrvɪkəl veɪn / (AmE), / ˈsɜːvɪkəl veɪn / (BrE)
a vein in the neck region that accompanies an artery with the same name
vein in the neck region
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that a blocked cervical vein could affect blood flow in the neck.
During surgery, care must be taken not to damage a cervical vein.
Ultrasound imaging can help identify issues with a cervical vein in some patients.

Etymology

From Latin 'cervix' meaning 'neck' and 'vena' meaning 'vein'.
