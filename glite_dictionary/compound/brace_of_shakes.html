brace of shakes

1. NOUN

/ breɪs əv ʃeɪks / (AmE), / breɪs əv ʃeɪks / (BrE)
A pair of milkshakes, typically enjoyed as a treat.
pair of milkshakes

Examples

After the game, they shared a brace of shakes at the local soda shop.
On hot afternoons, a brace of shakes is the perfect way to cool down.
He surprised his friend by bringing over a brace of shakes for their movie night.

Synset Examples

He ordered a brace of shakes at the diner.
The kids enjoyed a brace of shakes after dinner.
She treated herself to a brace of shakes on a hot day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: milkshake - a sweet drink made of milk, ice cream, and flavoring, blended together

Etymology

From 'brace' meaning 'pair' (from Old French 'brace'), and 'shakes', a shortening of 'milkshakes'.
