spectrographic analysis

1. NOUN

/ ˌspɛktroʊˈɡræfɪk əˈnæləsɪs / (AmE), / ˌspɛktrəˈɡræfɪk əˈnæləsɪs / (BrE)
the process of examining the spectra of substances using a spectroscope to determine their composition or properties
analysis of spectra using a spectroscope

Examples

The scientist used spectrographic analysis to determine the chemical composition of the star.
By employing spectrographic analysis, researchers identified traces of elements in the mineral sample.
Modern laboratories rely on spectrographic analysis for precise material characterization.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spectroscopy - the study of the interaction between matter and electromagnetic radiation

Etymology

From 'spectro-' (relating to spectra) and 'graphic' (pertaining to recording data); 'analysis' from Greek 'analusis' (a breaking up).
