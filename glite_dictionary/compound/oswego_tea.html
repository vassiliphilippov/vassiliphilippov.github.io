Oswego tea

1. NOUN

/ ɑzˈwiɡoʊ ti / (AmE), / ɒsˈwiːɡəʊ tiː / (BrE)
A perennial aromatic herb native to eastern North America, with tubular flowers in dense, showy heads, often used to make herbal tea.
aromatic North American herb (bee balm)

Examples

The garden was filled with Oswego tea, attracting bees and butterflies throughout the summer.
Many people brew Oswego tea for its pleasant aroma and flavor.
Native Americans traditionally used Oswego tea as a herbal remedy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bee balm - A common name for Oswego tea, a North American herb with aromatic leaves and showy flowers

Etymology

Named after Oswego, New York, where the local Native Americans were known to use it to make a tea substitute.
