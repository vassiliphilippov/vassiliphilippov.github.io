Ludian language

1. NOUN

/ ˈluːdiən ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈluːdɪən ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
a Baltic-Finnic language spoken by the Ludic people in northwestern Russia
Baltic-Finnic language of Russia

Examples

The Ludian language is primarily spoken in certain regions of northwestern Russia.
Linguists have studied the Ludian language to understand its relationship to Karelian and Veps.
Efforts are being made to preserve the endangered Ludian language for future generations.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Finnic language - a group of languages in the Finno-Ugric family
  • Synonym: Karelian language - a closely related Baltic-Finnic language

Etymology

From Ludic, the name of the ethnic group, + English 'language'.
