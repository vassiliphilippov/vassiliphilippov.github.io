family Xylariaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli zaɪˌlɛəriəˈsiːi / (AmE), / ˈfamɪli zaɪˌlɑːrɪəˈsiːiː / (BrE)
A family of fungi characterized by dark brown to black spores, including various wood-decaying species.
family of fungi with dark spores

Examples

Members of the family Xylariaceae are commonly found on decaying wood in forests.
The scientist identified the fungus as belonging to the family Xylariaceae based on spore characteristics.
Several economically important species are classified within the family Xylariaceae.

Etymology

From New Latin Xylariaceae, based on the genus Xylaria, from Greek xylon (wood).
