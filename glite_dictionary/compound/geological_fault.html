geological fault

1. NOUN

/ dʒiˈɑlədʒɪkəl fɔlt / (AmE), / dʒɪˈɒlədʒɪkəl fɔːlt / (BrE)
a crack in the Earth's crust along which movement has occurred, resulting in the displacement of rock layers
crack in Earth's crust with displacement

Examples

The city is located near a major geological fault, increasing the risk of earthquakes.
Scientists have mapped the geological fault running beneath the region to better understand seismic activity.
During the expedition, they discovered a previously unknown geological fault cutting through the mountains.

Synset Examples

they built it right over a geological fault
he studied the faulting of the earth's crust

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fault - crack in the Earth's crust with movement

Etymology

From Greek geō- (earth) and Latin fallere (to deceive, fail); here, 'fault' refers to geological breakage, not moral failing.
