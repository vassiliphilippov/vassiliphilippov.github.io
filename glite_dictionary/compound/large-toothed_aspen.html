large-toothed aspen

1. NOUN

/ lɑrdʒ tuθt ˈæspən / (AmE), / lɑːdʒ tuːθt ˈaspən / (BrE)
a species of aspen tree (Populus grandidentata) native to eastern North America, distinguished by its large, coarsely toothed leaves and narrow crown
tree with large, toothed leaves, North America

Examples

The large-toothed aspen is commonly found in the forests of eastern Canada and the United States.
Logging companies sometimes harvest large-toothed aspen for use in pulpwood and lumber.
The distinctive leaves of the large-toothed aspen make it easy to identify in the wild.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aspen - a type of poplar tree with rounded, toothed leaves
