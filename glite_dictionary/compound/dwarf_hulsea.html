dwarf hulsea

1. NOUN

/ dwɔrf hʌlˈsiːə / (AmE), / dwɔːf hʌlˈsiːə / (BrE)
a small perennial plant of the western United States, similar to but smaller than the alpine hulsea, with yellow daisylike flowers
a small North American flowering plant

Examples

The dwarf hulsea blooms in early summer on rocky slopes.
Botanists studied the habitat of the rare dwarf hulsea in California.
Unlike the alpine variety, the dwarf hulsea remains under ten inches tall.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: alpine hulsea - a similar, larger flowering plant found in mountainous regions
