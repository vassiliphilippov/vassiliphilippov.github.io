two-dimensional figure

1. NOUN

/ tu dɪˈmɛnʃənəl ˈfɪɡjər / (AmE), / tuː dɪˈmɛnʃənəl ˈfɪɡə / (BrE)
A shape or figure that exists in two dimensions, having only length and width but no depth.
shape with two dimensions

Examples

A square is a classic example of a two-dimensional figure in geometry.
In art class, we learned to draw and shade various two-dimensional figures.
The math teacher drew a triangle on the board and asked the students to identify the two-dimensional figure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plane figure - a flat, two-dimensional shape
  • Antonym: three-dimensional figure - a shape with three dimensions (solid geometry)
