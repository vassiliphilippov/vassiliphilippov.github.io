military police

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪlɪˌtɛri pəˈlis / (AmE), / ˈmɪlɪtəri pəˈliːs / (BrE)
the branch of a military force responsible for enforcing discipline, maintaining order, and guarding prisoners within the armed forces
disciplinary corps in the military

Examples

The military police were called in to restore order after the disturbance in the barracks.
During the exercise, the military police checked everyone's identification at the gates.
After the incident, the military police escorted the prisoners back to their cells.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: MP - abbreviation for military police
