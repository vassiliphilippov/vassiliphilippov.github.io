to handle with ease

1. VERB

/ ˈhændl wɪð iz / (AmE), / ˈhændl wɪð iːz / (BrE)
to manage or deal with something in a relaxed and confident way, without difficulty
manage or deal with something easily

Examples

Despite being new to the job, she was able to handle with ease every challenge that arose.
He seems to handle with ease situations that would stress most people out.
The students handled with ease the complex mathematics problem presented to them.

Synset Examples

As a pro, she handles tough situations with ease.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: handle effortlessly - manage or deal with something without needing any effort
  • Antonym: struggle with - have difficulty managing or dealing with something
