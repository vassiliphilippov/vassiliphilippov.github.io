privilege against self-incrimination

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɪvəlɪdʒ əˈɡɛnst sɛlf ɪnˌkrɪməˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈprɪvɪlɪdʒ əˈɡɛnst sɛlf ɪnˌkrɪmɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the legal right to refuse to answer questions or provide information that could be used to incriminate oneself, especially as protected by the Fifth Amendment in the United States
legal right to remain silent to avoid self-incrimination
legal american

Examples

The suspect invoked his privilege against self-incrimination during the interrogation.
In court, she relied on her privilege against self-incrimination to avoid answering certain questions.
Lawyers advised their clients to exercise the privilege against self-incrimination when questioned by authorities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: right to remain silent - the right of a person to refuse to answer questions from law enforcement
