freshwater clam

1. NOUN

/ ˈfrɛʃˌwɔtər klæm / (AmE), / ˈfrɛʃˌwɒtə klæm / (BrE)
a bivalve mollusk that lives in fresh water, especially those abundant in rivers of central United States.
bivalve mollusk living in fresh water

Examples

The river's sandy banks are home to the freshwater clam and many other aquatic species.
Scientists study the freshwater clam to understand changes in river ecosystems.
The freshwater clams filter water, helping to keep the river clean.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clam - a type of bivalve mollusk with a two-part shell
