orthopedic foot insole

1. NOUN

/ ɔrˈθoʊpɪdɪk fʊt ˈɪnˌsoʊl / (AmE), / ɔːˈθiːpɪdɪk fʊt ˈɪnsəʊl / (BrE)
a specially designed insole for shoes, intended to provide additional support or correct foot posture to relieve pain or discomfort
shoe insert for foot support or pain relief
medical

Examples

My doctor recommended I use orthopedic foot insoles to help with my arch pain.
After wearing orthopedic foot insoles for a few weeks, she noticed a significant reduction in discomfort.
Athletes often use orthopedic foot insoles to prevent injuries and improve comfort during training.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insole - removable inner sole of a shoe for comfort or support

Etymology

From Greek orthos (straight) + paideia (rearing of children), used in medical terms for correcting deformities, and Latin in- (in) + solum (sole of the foot).
