lymphocytic choriomeningitis virus

1. NOUN

/ lɪmfoʊˌsɪtɪk kōˌraɪoʊˌmɛnɪnˈdʒaɪtɪs ˈvaɪrəs / (AmE), / lɪmfəˌsɪtɪk kɒˌraɪəmɛnɪnˈdʒaɪtɪs ˈvʌɪrəs / (BrE)
an RNA virus that causes lymphocytic choriomeningitis, capable of infecting rodents, other animals, and humans
virus that causes lymphocytic choriomeningitis
medical

Examples

The lymphocytic choriomeningitis virus can be transmitted to humans through exposure to rodent urine or droppings.
Researchers are studying the lymphocytic choriomeningitis virus to better understand viral infections of the central nervous system.
Infection with lymphocytic choriomeningitis virus may cause flu-like symptoms or, in rare cases, neurological complications.

Etymology

From Greek 'lympha' (water, lymph), 'kytos' (cell), 'chorion' (membrane), 'meninx' (membrane), and Latin 'virus' (poison, slime).
