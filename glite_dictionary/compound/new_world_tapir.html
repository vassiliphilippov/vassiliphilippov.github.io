New World tapir

1. NOUN

/ nu ˈwɝːld ˈteɪpɚ / (AmE), / njuː ˈwɜːld ˈteɪpə / (BrE)
a tapir native to South America or Central America, belonging to genera Tapirus and distinctive from Old World tapirs
tapir of South or Central America

Examples

The New World tapir is often seen in rainforest habitats across Brazil and Colombia.
Unlike the Malayan tapir, the New World tapir has a uniform dark coloring.
Researchers study how the New World tapir adapts to different environments in Central America.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Old World tapir - a tapir species native to Southeast Asia (the Malayan tapir), distinct from those found in the Americas
