electrical energy

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈlɛktrɪkəl ˈɛnɚdʒi / (AmE), / ɪˈlɛktrɪkəl ˈɛnədʒi / (BrE)
energy produced by the movement of electric charge, typically used to power devices and systems
energy from moving electric charge

Examples

The factory is switching to solar panels to generate electrical energy.
A battery stores chemical energy and converts it into electrical energy when needed.
Most household appliances require electrical energy to operate efficiently.

Synset Examples

they built a car that runs on electricity
The power went out around midnight

Related Terms

  • Synonym: electricity - physical phenomena associated with electric charge; flow of charge in a conductor

Etymology

Formed from 'electrical' (from Greek ēlektron, 'amber') and 'energy' (from Greek energeia, 'activity').
