Carlina vulgaris

1. NOUN

/ kɑːrˈliːnə vʌlˈɡɛərɪs / (AmE), / kɑːˈliːnə vʌlˈɡɑːrɪs / (BrE)
a species of Eurasian thistle with spiny leaves and yellowish flower heads, growing in sandy and dry chalky soils
Eurasian thistle species

Examples

The spiny leaves of Carlina vulgaris can be seen along the edges of many European sand dunes.
Botanists identified several specimens of Carlina vulgaris during the field survey.
Due to its preference for dry, chalky soils, Carlina vulgaris thrives on limestone hills.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thistle - any of various prickly, flowering plants of the family Asteraceae

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Carlina' and species name 'vulgaris' meaning 'common'.
