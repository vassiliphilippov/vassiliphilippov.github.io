terra alba

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɛrə ˈælbə / (AmE), / ˌtɛrə ˈælbə / (BrE)
finely ground, white gypsum powder used especially as a pigment or filler in manufacturing and art
finely ground gypsum used as a pigment

Examples

The artist mixed terra alba with oils to achieve a brighter pigment.
Manufacturers use terra alba as a filler in making paper and paint.
Terra alba is valued in the food industry for its whiteness and purity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gypsum - a soft sulfate mineral used in construction and industry

Etymology

From Latin, literally 'white earth'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌtɛrə ˈælbə / (AmE), / ˌtɛrə ˈalba / (BrE)
a fine white clay or gypsum used in making tobacco pipes, pottery, and in whitening leather
fine white clay used in pottery and leather

Examples

The artisan shaped the delicate bowl from terra alba to achieve a flawless white finish.
Early tobacco pipes were often crafted from terra alba due to its smooth texture.
The tanner applied terra alba to the leather to whiten its surface during processing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gypsum - a soft sulfate mineral used in plaster and fertilizer

Etymology

From Latin 'terra alba', meaning 'white earth'.

3. NOUN

/ ˌtɛrə ˈælbə / (AmE), / ˌtɛrə ˈælbə / (BrE)
A fine, usually white clay formed by the weathering of aluminous minerals such as feldspar; used in ceramics, as an absorbent, and as a filler in products like paper.
white clay used in ceramics and industry

Examples

The pottery studio ordered several bags of terra alba for their glaze mixtures.
High-quality paper often uses terra alba as a filler to improve brightness and texture.
The absorbent nature of terra alba makes it valuable in various industrial processes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gypsum - a soft white mineral, calcium sulfate dihydrate, used in plaster and as a fertilizer

Etymology

From Latin, meaning 'white earth'.
