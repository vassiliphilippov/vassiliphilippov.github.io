North Temperate Zone

1. NOUN

/ nɔrθ ˈtɛmpərət zoʊn / (AmE), / nɔːθ ˈtɛmpərət zəʊn / (BrE)
the region of the Earth between the Arctic Circle and the Tropic of Cancer, characterized by temperate climate conditions
region between Arctic Circle and Tropic of Cancer

Examples

Summers in the North Temperate Zone can be warm but rarely reach tropical temperatures.
Many of the world’s most populous cities are located within the North Temperate Zone.
The changing seasons are a characteristic feature of the North Temperate Zone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: temperate zone - regions of the Earth between the tropics and the polar circles with moderate climates
