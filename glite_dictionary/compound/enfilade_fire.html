enfilade fire

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛn.fɪˈleɪd ˌfaɪər / (AmE), / ˌɛn.fɪˈlɑːd ˌfaɪə / (BrE)
Gunfire directed along the length of a formation, making it particularly effective and damaging.
gunfire along the length of a formation

Examples

The advancing troops were exposed to enfilade fire from the enemy positioned at the end of the trench.
To avoid enfilade fire, the soldiers took cover behind the hillside.
A well-planned defense can inflict devastating casualties by using enfilade fire along an attacking line.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enfilade - military formation alignment along a line

Etymology

From French 'enfiler' (to thread or string), from 'en-' (in) + 'file' (row or thread).
