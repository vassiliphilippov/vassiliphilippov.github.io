opera hood

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːpɚ hʊd / (AmE), / ˈɒpərə hʊd / (BrE)
a large, loose cloak, often with a hood, traditionally worn over evening clothes to attend the opera or other formal occasions
large cloak worn over evening clothes

Examples

She draped her opera hood over her shoulders before stepping out into the chilly night.
The audience in velvet opera hoods crowded the grand staircase of the old theater.
His grandmother's vintage opera hood was a family heirloom passed down for generations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cloak - a loose outer garment, typically sleeveless

Etymology

From 'opera' (public musical performance) and 'hood' (covering for the head), reflecting its use at operas.
