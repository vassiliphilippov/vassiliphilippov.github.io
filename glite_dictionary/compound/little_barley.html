little barley

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪt̬l ˈbɑr.li / (AmE), / ˈlɪt.əl ˈbɑː.li / (BrE)
An annual grass (Hordeum pusillum), native to western North America and common in the southern United States and tropical America, with short stiff awns, resembling barley.
a species of annual grass native to North America

Examples

Little barley is often found in disturbed soils along roadsides and fields.
Farmers sometimes struggle to control the spread of little barley in their pastures.
The botanist collected samples of little barley during her field research in Texas.
