squaw huckleberry

1. NOUN

/ skwɔː ˈhʌkəlˌbɛri / (AmE), / skwɔː ˈhʌkəlˌbɛri / (BrE)
a small branching blueberry shrub found in marshy areas of the eastern United States, with greenish or yellowish berries that are generally considered unpalatable to humans but are reportedly eaten by deer
a marshy eastern US blueberry shrub

Examples

The marsh was filled with squaw huckleberry shrubs and other wild plants.
Deer often eat the fruit of the squaw huckleberry, even though humans usually avoid it.
In early summer, the squaw huckleberry produces clusters of pale berries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blueberry - an edible blue or purple berry from plants of the genus Vaccinium
