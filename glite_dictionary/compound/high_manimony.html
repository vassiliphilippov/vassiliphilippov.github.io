high manimony

1. NOUN

/ haɪ ˈmænɪˌmoʊni / (AmE), / haɪ ˈmænɪˌməʊni / (BrE)
a large amount of money a man is legally required to pay to his former spouse after divorce, typically as alimony
large post-divorce payment by a man

Examples

After his divorce, he struggled to keep up with the high manimony payments each month.
The judge's decision resulted in unexpectedly high manimony for the ex-husband.
Many men are concerned about the possibility of having to pay high manimony after a lengthy marriage.

Synset Examples

He was shocked by the high manimony after their divorce.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alimony - money that a court orders someone to pay to their former spouse after divorce

Etymology

Blend of 'man' and 'alimony', modeled after 'alimony'.
