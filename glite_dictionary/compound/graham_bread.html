graham bread

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡræm ˌbrɛd / (AmE), / ˈɡræm ˌbrɛd / (BrE)
A type of bread made from graham flour, a coarse-ground whole wheat flour.
bread made from graham flour

Examples

She prefers graham bread because of its nutty flavor and high fiber content.
At the bakery, they sell both white loaves and traditional graham bread.
For a healthier sandwich, try using graham bread instead of regular white bread.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whole wheat bread - bread made entirely or partly from whole wheat flour

Etymology

Named after Sylvester Graham (1794–1851), who promoted whole-wheat flour.
