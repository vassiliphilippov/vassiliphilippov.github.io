genus Epiphyllum

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌɛp.ɪˈfɪl.əm / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌɛp.ɪˈfɪl.əm / (BrE)
A small genus of tropical American cacti, mainly found in Central America, known for their flat, leaflike stems and large flowers.
genus of tropical American cacti

Examples

The genus Epiphyllum includes several species prized for their spectacular night-blooming flowers.
Many gardeners enjoy cultivating members of the genus Epiphyllum because of their unique appearance.
The genus Epiphyllum is native to Central America and thrives in warm, humid conditions.

Etymology

From Latin 'epi-' (upon) and Greek 'phyllon' (leaf), referring to the plant's leaflike stems.
