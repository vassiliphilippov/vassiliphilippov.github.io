Windsor green

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪn.zər ɡrin / (AmE), / ˈwɪn.zə ɡriːn / (BrE)
a bright, light green pigment containing a form of chrome, used especially in painting and art materials
a bright light green pigment

Examples

The artist chose Windsor green for the leaves to create a vivid, natural look.
She mixed Windsor green with yellow to achieve a lighter tone for her landscape painting.
A tube of Windsor green lasted him through several projects in the studio.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chrome green - a green pigment made from chromium oxide

Etymology

Named after the English town of Windsor, often associated with artists' supplies and pigments.
