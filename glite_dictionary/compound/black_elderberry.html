black elderberry

1. NOUN

/ blæk ˈɛldɚˌbɛri / (AmE), / blak ˈɛldəˌbɛri / (BrE)
A shrub native to central and eastern North America, known for its clusters of purple-black berries used in wines and jellies.
a shrub with edible purple-black berries

Examples

Many people make syrup from black elderberry to boost their immune systems during winter.
The wine had a deep red color, thanks to the addition of black elderberry juice.
Birds flocked to the garden every autumn to feed on the ripe black elderberry clusters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elderberry - the berry of the elder tree, especially of the species Sambucus; edible and often used in cooking
