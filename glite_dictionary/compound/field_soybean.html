field soybean

1. NOUN

/ fiːld ˈsɔɪˌbin / (AmE), / fiːld ˈsɔɪˌbiːn / (BrE)
a variety of soybean grown primarily for use as livestock feed rather than for human consumption
soybean grown for animal feed

Examples

Farmers planted acres of field soybean to meet the demand for animal feed.
The nutritionist recommended adding field soybean to the cows' diet.
Bags of field soybean were delivered to the livestock barn last week.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soybean - an edible legume native to East Asia, widely cultivated for its protein-rich seeds
