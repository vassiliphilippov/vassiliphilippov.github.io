family Echeneidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ɛˌkiːnɪˈaɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli iːˌkiːnɪˈiːdaɪ / (BrE)
a family of fishes known as remoras or suckerfish, characterized by a sucking disk on the head for attaching to other fishes or ships
a fish family with a head suction disk (remoras)

Examples

The family Echeneidae includes species that are commonly known as remoras or suckerfish.
Many in the family Echeneidae use their suction disks to attach themselves to sharks or rays.
Biologists study the unique adaptations of the family Echeneidae for marine symbiosis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: remora - a fish that attaches itself to other sea creatures using a suction disk
  • Synonym: suckerfish - common name for fish of the family Echeneidae

Etymology

From New Latin ‘Echeneidae’, based on Greek ‘echein’ (to hold) and ‘naus’ (ship), referring to their habit of attaching themselves to ships.
