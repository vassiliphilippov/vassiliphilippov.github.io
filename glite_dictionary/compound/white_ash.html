white ash

1. NOUN

/ waɪt æʃ / (AmE), / waɪt æʃ / (BrE)
a small to medium-sized tree native to Australia and Tasmania, with smooth white to light-grey bark that sheds in patches or strips
Australian tree with white or grey shedding bark

Examples

The white ash is easily recognized by its distinctive light-grey peeling bark.
Several white ash trees grow along the nature reserve in Tasmania.
Botanists studied the white ash for its unique adaptation to the local soil.

2. NOUN

/ waɪt æʃ / (AmE), / waɪt æʃ / (BrE)
a North American ash tree (Fraxinus americana) with pale green or silvery leaves beneath and hard, brownish wood
a North American ash tree (Fraxinus americana)

Examples

The furniture was crafted from white ash for both its beauty and durability.
A row of white ash trees lined the street, providing plenty of shade in the summer.
Botanists are concerned about the decline of white ash due to invasive beetles.

  • Synonym: ash - a type of tree of the genus Fraxinus
