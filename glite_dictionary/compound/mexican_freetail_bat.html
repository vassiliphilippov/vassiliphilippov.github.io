Mexican freetail bat

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛksɪkən ˈfriˌteɪl bæt / (AmE), / ˈmɛksɪkən ˈfriːteɪl bæt / (BrE)
a species of bat common in the southern United States and Central America, known for its short velvety fur and southward migration in winter
species of bat found in southern U.S. and Central America

Examples

The Mexican freetail bat is famous for its massive evening emergence from cave roosts.
Researchers tracked the migration patterns of the Mexican freetail bat to better understand their winter habits.
Every summer, tourists gather to watch thousands of Mexican freetail bats fly out at dusk.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bat - a nocturnal, flying mammal
