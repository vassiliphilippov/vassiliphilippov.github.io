conchoid of Nicomedes

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːn.kɔɪd əv ˌnaɪ.kəˈmiː.diːz / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.kɔɪd ɒv ˌnɪ.kəˈmiː.diːz / (BrE)
a plane curve constructed by tracing the points obtained by marking a specific distance from a fixed point to a straight line, resulting in a characteristic shape; used in mathematics and geometry.
a specific geometric curve in mathematics

Examples

The ancient mathematicians studied the conchoid of Nicomedes to solve geometric problems.
By adjusting the distance parameter, the shape of the conchoid of Nicomedes changes significantly.
He drew a perfect conchoid of Nicomedes as part of his geometry assignment.

Synset Examples

the conchoid of a straight line with respect to a point not on the line.

Etymology

Named after the ancient Greek mathematician Nicomedes, who first described the curve.
