disability benefit

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈsæbɪlɪti ˈbɛnəfɪt / (AmE), / dɪˈsæbɪlɪti ˈbɛnɪfɪt / (BrE)
a payment made by the government or an insurance company to someone who is unable to work because of a disability
payment to people unable to work due to disability

Examples

After her accident, she applied for a disability benefit to help cover her living expenses.
Many people rely on disability benefit when they are unable to continue working due to illness or injury.
The government revised its disability benefit policy to make the application process easier.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: invalidity benefit - a former term for a similar payment
