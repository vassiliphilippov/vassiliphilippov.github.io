statistical regression

1. NOUN

/ stəˈtɪstɪkəl rɪˈɡrɛʃən / (AmE), / stəˈtɪstɪkəl rɪˈɡrɛʃən / (BrE)
a statistical analysis method that estimates the relationship between a dependent variable and one or more independent variables, allowing predictions of the dependent variable based on given values of the independent variables
method for predicting relationships between variables

Examples

The researcher used statistical regression to analyze the influence of age and diet on blood pressure.
One common application of statistical regression is in predicting housing prices based on several variables.
Understanding statistical regression helps scientists find meaningful relationships in complex data sets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: correlation - a measure of the strength and direction of association between two variables
  • Antonym: causation - the action of causing something, often contrasted with correlation in statistics

Etymology

From 'statistical' (related to statistics) and 'regression', from Latin 'regressio' (a return).
