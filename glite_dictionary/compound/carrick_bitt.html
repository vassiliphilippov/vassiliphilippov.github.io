carrick bitt

1. NOUN

/ ˈkærɪk bɪt / (AmE), / ˈkærɪk bɪt / (BrE)
either of a pair of strong posts on the deck of a ship that support a windlass
nautical: posts supporting a ship's windlass

Examples

The heavy anchor chain was wound securely around the carrick bitt during the storm.
The shipbuilder explained how the carrick bitts must be reinforced to support the new windlass.
During restoration, the crew found the original carrick bitt still intact after a century at sea.
