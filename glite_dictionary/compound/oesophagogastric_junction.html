oesophagogastric junction

1. NOUN

/ iˌsɑfəɡoʊˈɡæstrɪk ˈdʒʌŋkʃən / (AmE), / ɪˌsɒfəɡəʊˈɡæstrɪk ˈdʒʌŋkʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
the anatomical region where the esophagus meets the stomach, marking the transition between their epithelia
junction between esophagus and stomach
medical

Examples

The doctor identified a tumor located at the oesophagogastric junction during the endoscopy.
Inflammation at the oesophagogastric junction can cause symptoms of heartburn and difficulty swallowing.
Biopsies were taken from the oesophagogastric junction to check for abnormal cells.

Etymology

From Greek oesophagus (gullet) and gastric (of the stomach), with junction from Latin junctio (joining).
