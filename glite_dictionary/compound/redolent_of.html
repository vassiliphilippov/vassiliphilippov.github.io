redolent of

1. ADJ

/ ˈrɛdələnt ʌv / (AmE), / ˈrɛdələnt ɒv / (BrE)
having a strong smell of or strongly reminiscent of something, especially a particular quality or atmosphere
strongly smelling of or reminiscent of

Examples

The old library was redolent of leather-bound books and polished wood.
Her writing is redolent of childhood memories and nostalgia.
The air was redolent of spices from the nearby street market.

Synset Examples

Her perfume was redolent of jasmine and vanilla.
The bakery was redolent with the smell of fresh bread.
The garden was redolent with blooming flowers in spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: evocative - bringing strong images, memories, or feelings to mind

Etymology

From Latin redolens, redolentis (smelling of), from redolere (to emit a smell).
