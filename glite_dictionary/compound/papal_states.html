Papal States

1. PROPN

/ ˈpeɪpəl steɪts / (AmE), / ˈpeɪpəl steɪts / (BrE)
the territories in central Italy that were under the direct rule of the pope from the 8th century until 1870
historical papal territories in Italy

Examples

The unification of Italy in 1870 led to the end of the Papal States as a political entity.
Many European powers sought influence over the Papal States during the Renaissance.
The control of the Papal States allowed the pope significant power in both religious and political matters.

Etymology

From papal, relating to the pope, and states, political territories. The term refers to lands governed by the papacy.
