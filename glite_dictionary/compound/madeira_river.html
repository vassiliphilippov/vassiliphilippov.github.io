Madeira River

1. PROPN

/ məˈdɛrə ˈrɪvər / (AmE), / məˈdɪərə ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
a river in western Brazil, about 3,380 km long, which is a major tributary of the Amazon River
major tributary of the Amazon River in Brazil

Examples

The Madeira River flows through the heart of the Amazon rainforest, supporting diverse wildlife.
During the rainy season, the Madeira River often floods its banks.
Scientists are studying the impact of dams on the fish populations of the Madeira River.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Amazon River - the largest river in South America by discharge and basin area

Etymology

From Portuguese 'Madeira' (meaning 'wood'), the name given by Portuguese explorers.
