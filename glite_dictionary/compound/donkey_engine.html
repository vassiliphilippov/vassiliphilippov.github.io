donkey engine

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɑŋki ˌɛndʒɪn / (AmE), / ˈdɒŋki ˌɛndʒɪn / (BrE)
a small locomotive used for switching rolling stock in a railroad yard
small locomotive for switching rail cars

Examples

The old donkey engine chugged back and forth, rearranging the freight cars.
A donkey engine is essential for efficient operations in busy rail yards.
He spent his career operating a donkey engine in the city's main switching yard.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: switcher - a locomotive used for switching rail cars

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɑŋki ˌɛndʒɪn / (AmE), / ˈdɒŋki ˌɛndʒɪn / (BrE)
A small auxiliary engine, especially one used on ships to operate machinery such as a windlass.
small auxiliary engine, especially on ships

Examples

The old steamship still uses a donkey engine to raise the anchor.
They repaired the donkey engine before lifting the cargo onto the deck.
The crew relied on the donkey engine for operating various mechanical gear.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: auxiliary engine - an engine used to support the main engine, especially on ships

Etymology

From 'donkey' (animal symbolizing auxiliary work) + 'engine'. First used in the 19th century maritime industry.
