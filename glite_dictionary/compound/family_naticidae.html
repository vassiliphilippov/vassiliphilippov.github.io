family Naticidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli nəˈtɪsɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæməli nəˈtɪsɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of marine gastropod mollusks commonly known as moonshells, characterized by their smooth, rounded shells.
family of sea snails (moonshells)

Examples

Many collectors find shells from the family Naticidae particularly attractive due to their shiny, rounded appearance.
Predatory snails in the family Naticidae drill into the shells of other mollusks to feed.
The family Naticidae is distributed worldwide, inhabiting sandy marine environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moonshell - common name for gastropods in the family Naticidae

Etymology

From Latin 'Naticidae', based on 'Natica', the type genus of the family.
