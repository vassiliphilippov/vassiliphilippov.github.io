head linesperson

1. NOUN

/ hɛd ˈlaɪnzˌpɜrsən / (AmE), / hɛd ˈlaɪnzˌpɜːsən / (BrE)
an official in American football responsible for recording the yardage gained or lost during play and overseeing the chain crew
football official recording yardage

Examples

The head linesperson signaled that the play resulted in a first down.
She was appointed as the first female head linesperson in the league's history.
It's the head linesperson's responsibility to keep accurate track of the chains during the game.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: line judge - football official responsible for monitoring the line of scrimmage

Etymology

Derived from 'head linesman,' with 'person' used for gender neutral language; 'line' refers to the line of scrimmage in football.
