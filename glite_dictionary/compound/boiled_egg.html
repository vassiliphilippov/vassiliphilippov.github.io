boiled egg

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɔɪld ˌɛɡ / (AmE), / ˈbɔɪld ˌɛɡ / (BrE)
An egg that has been cooked in its shell in boiling water, typically until the white and yolk are firm or partially set.
egg cooked in its shell in boiling water

Examples

He ate a boiled egg for breakfast every morning.
Would you like your boiled egg soft or hard?
She peeled the boiled egg and sliced it over her salad.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poached egg - egg cooked out of the shell in simmering water
  • Synonym: scrambled egg - egg beaten and cooked while stirring
  • Synonym: fried egg - egg cooked by frying

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɔɪld ɛɡ / (AmE), / ˈbɔɪld ɛɡ / (BrE)
An egg that has been cooked in boiling water, with or without the shell removed after cooking.
egg cooked in boiling water

Examples

She had a boiled egg with toast for breakfast this morning.
Would you like your boiled egg soft or hard?
He peeled the boiled egg carefully before adding it to the salad.

Synset Examples

I prefer a boiled Egg for breakfast.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fried egg - egg cooked by frying, usually in a pan
  • Antonym: poached egg - egg cooked by poaching in simmering water
