paper plant

1. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪpɚ plænt / (AmE), / ˈpeɪpə plɑːnt / (BrE)
a tall plant of the Nile valley (Cyperus papyrus) whose fiber was used in ancient times for making paper and other items
Nile valley plant used for ancient paper

Examples

The ancient Egyptians cultivated the paper plant along the Nile to produce papyrus sheets.
Researchers discovered remnants of a paper plant in an ancient tomb, indicating its importance in early societies.
Modern botanists sometimes grow the paper plant in greenhouses for educational purposes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: papyrus - plant used to make ancient Egyptian paper
