Court of Saint James's

1. PROPN

/ kɔrt əv seɪnt ˈdʒeɪmzɪz / (AmE), / kɔːt əv seɪnt ˈdʒeɪmzɪz / (BrE)
the official royal court of the sovereign of the United Kingdom, to which ambassadors are accredited
the British royal court
british

Examples

The ambassador presented her credentials to the Court of Saint James's last week.
Diplomats from across the world are accredited to the Court of Saint James's.
It is traditional for foreign envoys to be received by the monarch at the Court of Saint James's.

Etymology

Named after St James's Palace in London, the official residence of the British sovereign.
