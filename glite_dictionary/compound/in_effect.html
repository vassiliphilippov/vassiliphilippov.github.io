in effect

1. ADV

/ ɪn ɪˈfɛkt / (AmE), / ɪn ɪˈfɛkt / (BrE)
(of a law, rule, or agreement) currently in force or having legal validity.
currently in force (of a law, rule, etc.)

Examples

The new tax regulations are now in effect and must be followed by all businesses.
Despite recent debates, the old policy is still in effect.
The curfew will remain in effect until further notice from the authorities.

Synset Examples

the law is still in effect

Related Terms

  • Synonym: in force - being legally operative or valid
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04816894-n
Synset POS: n

2. ADV

/ ɪn ɪˈfɛkt / (AmE), / ɪn ɪˈfɛkt / (BrE)
used to indicate that something is the case in practice or reality, even if not formally acknowledged
in actuality or reality; practically speaking

Examples

In effect, the agreement means we will be working together for another year.
His resignation was, in effect, forced by the board of directors.
In effect, she took control of the project, even though she wasn't officially in charge.

Synset Examples

she is effectively his wife
in effect, they had no choice

Related Terms

  • Synonym: in fact - used to emphasize the truth of a statement
  • Synonym: effectively - in a way that produces the intended result; in fact
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00060570-r
Synset POS: r
