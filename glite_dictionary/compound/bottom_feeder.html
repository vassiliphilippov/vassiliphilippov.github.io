bottom feeder

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑtəm ˌfidər / (AmE), / ˈbɒtəm ˌfiːdə / (BrE)
an opportunist who seeks to profit from the misfortunes or failures of others, often in an unscrupulous way
person profiting from others' misfortune

Examples

Some people see bankruptcy lawyers as bottom feeders taking advantage of others' problems.
The tabloids are full of bottom feeders who thrive on celebrity scandals.
Many consider ticket scalpers to be bottom feeders in the entertainment industry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: opportunist - a person who takes advantage of opportunities, often without regard to principles
  • Synonym: scavenger - a person who searches for and collects discarded items or profits from the misfortune of others

Etymology

Metaphorical use from 'bottom feeder', a fish that feeds on debris at the bottom of a body of water.
