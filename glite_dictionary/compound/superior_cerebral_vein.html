superior cerebral vein

1. NOUN

/ suːˈpɪriɚ səˈribəl veɪn / (AmE), / suːˈpɪəriə səˈriːbəl veɪn / (BrE)
a vein in the brain that drains blood from the upper part of the cerebral hemisphere and empties into the cavernous sinus
vein draining upper part of the brain
medical

Examples

Bleeding from a superior cerebral vein can result in serious complications if not treated promptly.
The surgeon identified the superior cerebral vein during the operation to avoid accidental damage.
Blockage of a superior cerebral vein may lead to increased intracranial pressure.

Etymology

From Latin 'superior' (higher), 'cerebrum' (brain) + 'vein'.

2. NOUN

/ suˈpɪriɚ səˈriːbrəl veɪn / (AmE), / suˈpɪəriə səˈriːbrəl veɪn / (BrE)
a cerebral vein that drains the upper surface (dorsal convexity) of the cerebral hemisphere and empties into the cavernous sinus
cerebral vein draining the upper hemisphere
medical

Examples

Blockage in a superior cerebral vein can lead to serious neurological symptoms.
During the procedure, the surgeon carefully avoided injuring the superior cerebral vein.
MRI imaging revealed a clot in the patient's superior cerebral vein.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: cerebral vein - any of the veins draining blood from the brain

Etymology

From Latin 'vena' (vein), 'cerebrum' (brain), and 'superior' (higher).
