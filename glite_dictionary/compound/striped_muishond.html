striped muishond

1. NOUN

/ straɪpt ˈmaɪshɒnd / (AmE), / straɪpt ˈmʌɪshɒnd / (BrE)
a small, ferret-sized carnivorous mammal of southern Africa, often tamed; also known as the striped polecat
ferret-sized African mammal, sometimes tamed

Examples

The striped muishond is often kept as a pet in some rural African communities.
A striped muishond has striking black and white fur and a bushy tail.
Farmers sometimes encounter a striped muishond near their fields at night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: striped polecat - ferret-sized African mammal, synonym for striped muishond

Etymology

From Afrikaans 'muishond' (literally 'mouse dog'), used for small, polecat-like animals in southern Africa.
