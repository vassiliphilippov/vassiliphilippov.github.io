shock therapy

1. NOUN

/ ʃɑk ˈθɛrəpi / (AmE), / ʃɒk ˈθɛrəpi / (BrE)
a treatment for certain mental disorders involving the administration of electric or chemical shocks that induce convulsions
treatment for mental disorders using induced shocks
medical

Examples

After years of medication, he was finally offered shock therapy as a last resort.
The psychiatrist explained the risks and benefits of shock therapy to the patient's family.
Some patients report significant improvements after undergoing shock therapy sessions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: electroconvulsive therapy - a psychiatric treatment using electrically induced seizures
