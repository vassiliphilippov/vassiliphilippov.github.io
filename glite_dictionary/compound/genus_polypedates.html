genus Polypedates

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˌpɒlɪˈpiːdeɪtiːz / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˌpɒlɪˈpiːdiːtiːz / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Polypedatidae, comprising certain species of tree frogs found in South and Southeast Asia.
type genus of Polypedatidae (tree frogs)

Examples

The genus Polypedates includes several species of tree frogs native to tropical Asia.
Scientists discovered a new species within the genus Polypedates in the rainforests of Borneo.
Members of the genus Polypedates are commonly called 'tree frogs' due to their arboreal habits.

Etymology

Polypedates: from Greek polus (many) + pedon (foot), referring to many-toed feet.
