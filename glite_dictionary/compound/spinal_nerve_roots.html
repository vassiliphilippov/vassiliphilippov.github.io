spinal nerve roots

1. NOUN

/ ˈspaɪnəl nɜːrv ruːts / (AmE), / ˈspaɪnəl nɜːv ruːts / (BrE)
either of the two nerve bundles (dorsal and ventral roots) that emerge from the spinal cord to form a spinal nerve
the two roots forming a spinal nerve
medical

Examples

Damage to the spinal nerve roots can result in loss of sensation or movement.
Inflammation of the spinal nerve roots is called radiculitis.
The surgeon identified the compressed spinal nerve roots during the procedure.
