experimental condition

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˌspɛr.əˈmɛn.t̬əl kənˈdɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / ɪkˌspɛr.ɪˈmen.təl kənˈdɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
a specific procedure or variable in an experiment that is deliberately changed to observe its effect, typically compared with a control condition
variable or procedure changed in an experiment

Examples

The participants in the experimental condition received the new medication, while the control group received a placebo.
Researchers measured the response rates under each experimental condition to determine the effect of temperature.
Every experimental condition was carefully monitored to ensure the validity of the results.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: control condition - the baseline or unchanged situation in an experiment for comparison with the experimental condition
