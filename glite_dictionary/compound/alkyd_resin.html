alkyd resin

1. NOUN

/ ˈæl.kɪd ˈrɛz.ɪn / (AmE), / ˈæl.kɪd ˈrɛz.ɪn / (BrE)
a synthetic resin made by heating polyhydric alcohols with polybasic acids, used especially in paints, varnishes, and adhesives for its durability
durable synthetic resin used in paints and adhesives

Examples

Modern artists often choose alkyd resin for its fast-drying properties in oil paints.
The new furniture finish uses alkyd resin to improve durability and gloss.
Many manufacturers rely on alkyd resin as a key ingredient in their industrial coatings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polyester resin - a synthetic resin made by polycondensation of polyhydric alcohols and polybasic acids

Etymology

From 'alkyd', a blend of 'alcohol' and 'acid', referring to the resin's chemical composition.
