ancient pine

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪnʃənt paɪn / (AmE), / ˈeɪnʃənt paɪn / (BrE)
a small, slow-growing pine tree of the western United States, closely related to the bristlecone pine, noted for its longevity and gnarled appearance
very long-lived pine tree of western US mountains

Examples

The ancient pine stood alone on the rocky crest, its branches twisted by centuries of wind.
Scientists study ancient pine groves to learn about climate patterns over thousands of years.
A grove of ancient pines near the summit has survived for millennia through harsh conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bristlecone pine - a long-lived pine tree of high-altitude western US
