frothy wave

1. NOUN

/ ˈfrɑːθi weɪv / (AmE), / ˈfrɒθi weɪv / (BrE)
A wave in the sea or ocean covered with bubbles or foam, usually created by agitation or breaking on the shore.
wave covered with bubbles or foam

Examples

Children ran along the beach, chasing the frothy waves as they retreated back into the sea.
The boat rocked gently on the frothy waves stirred up by the evening breeze.
Sunlight sparkled on the frothy waves, making the ocean look alive with bright silver.

Synset Examples

The frothy waves crashed against the shore.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foam - A mass of small bubbles formed on or in liquid, typically by agitation or fermentation
