extremely high frequency

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˈstriːmli haɪ ˈfriːkwənsi / (AmE), / ɪkˈstriːmli hɒɪ ˈfriːkwənsi / (BrE)
a radio frequency in the range of 30 to 300 gigahertz, used especially in microwave and satellite communications
radio frequency range (30–300 GHz)

Examples

Satellites often communicate using extremely high frequency bands to transmit large amounts of data.
The extremely high frequency range is considered ideal for certain types of radar systems.
Researchers are developing technology that utilizes extremely high frequency signals for faster wireless networks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: microwave - radio waves typically within 1–100 GHz frequencies
