hydroxide ion

1. NOUN

/ haɪˈdrɑksaɪd aɪˌɑn / (AmE), / haɪˈdrɒksaɪd aɪˌɒn / (BrE)
a negatively charged ion (OH−) consisting of one oxygen and one hydrogen atom, commonly found in alkaline solutions
negatively charged OH− ion in chemistry
medical

Examples

The presence of a hydroxide ion makes a solution alkaline.
When sodium dissolves in water, it creates a hydroxide ion and a sodium ion.
The reaction releases a hydroxide ion into the solution, raising the pH.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hydroxide - a compound containing the hydroxide ion
  • Antonym: proton - a subatomic particle with positive charge; opposite in charge to the hydroxide ion

Etymology

From 'hydroxide,' itself from 'hydro-' (water) and 'oxide' (compound with oxygen), plus 'ion' from Greek 'ion' (going).
