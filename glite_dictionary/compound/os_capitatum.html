os capitatum

1. NOUN

/ ɑs kæˈpɪtətəm / (AmE), / ɒs kəˈpɪtətəm / (BrE)
the largest carpal bone in the center of the wrist, having a rounded head that articulates with the third metacarpal
the largest wrist bone (capitate)
medical

Examples

A fracture of the os capitatum can result in reduced wrist mobility.
The surgeon carefully examined the os capitatum before proceeding with the operation.
Damage to the os capitatum may affect the articulation of the hand.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: capitate bone - the largest carpal bone in the wrist

Etymology

From Latin os (bone) + capitatum (having a head), referring to its rounded shape.
