sugar cane

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃuːɡər keɪn / (AmE), / ˈʃʊɡə keɪn / (BrE)
A tall tropical grass with thick, fibrous stalks, widely cultivated for its sweet sap, which is a primary source of sugar.
tropical grass used as a sugar source

Examples

The farmer planted acres of sugar cane to meet the growing demand for sweeteners.
Molasses is often produced as a byproduct when sugar cane is processed for sugar.
Freshly cut sugar cane juice is a popular drink in many tropical countries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sugar beet - a plant whose root contains a high concentration of sucrose; another major source of sugar

Etymology

From sugar + cane, cane from Old French 'cane', from Latin 'canna' (reed, cane).

2. NOUN

/ ˈʃʊɡɚ keɪn / (AmE), / ˈʃʊɡə keɪn / (BrE)
A tall tropical plant with thick, jointed stems that are a major source of sugar and molasses; the stems are sometimes chewed for their sweet juice.
tropical plant used as a source of sugar

Examples

In Brazil, large fields of sugar cane are harvested to produce ethanol fuel.
The vendor sold fresh sticks of sugar cane that customers chewed for the sweet juice.
After extracting the juice from sugar cane, the remaining fiber is used as animal feed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sugar beet - a plant whose root is a source of commercial sugar

Etymology

From sugar + cane, translating the Portuguese 'cana de açúcar'.
