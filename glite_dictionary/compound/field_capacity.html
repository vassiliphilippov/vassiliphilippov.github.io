field capacity

1. NOUN

/ ˈfiːld kəˌpæsəti / (AmE), / ˈfiːld kəˌpæsəti / (BrE)
the maximum amount of water that soil can hold after excess water has drained away and the rate of downward movement has decreased
maximum water soil can hold after draining

Examples

After heavy rainfall, the soil quickly reached its field capacity and could hold no more water.
Knowing the field capacity is essential for efficient irrigation management.
The laboratory measured the field capacity of different soil samples to assess water retention.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: wilting point - the minimal soil moisture at which a plant wilts
