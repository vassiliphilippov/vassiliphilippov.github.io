rag day

1. NOUN

/ ræɡ deɪ / (AmE), / ræɡ deɪ / (BrE)
a day, especially at a university, when students organize events or celebrations, often to raise money for charity
university fundraising celebration
british

Examples

The university organizes a rag day every spring to collect donations for local charities.
Students wore fancy dress costumes throughout rag day to entertain the town.
During rag day, the campus was filled with music and lively activities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rag week - a week of student events and fundraising, especially in universities

Etymology

From 'rag' meaning a boisterous student celebration or charitable fundraising event, especially in British universities.
