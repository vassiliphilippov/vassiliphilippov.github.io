genus Arnica

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ɑrˈnaɪ.kə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ɑːˈnɪ.kə / (BrE)
A large genus of perennial herbs found in north temperate and Arctic regions, known for their yellow flowers and use in herbal medicine.
a genus of medicinal herbs

Examples

genus Arnica includes several species used in traditional herbal remedies.
Some North American wildflowers belonging to the genus Arnica are cultivated for their medicinal properties.
The bright yellow blooms of the genus Arnica are a familiar sight in alpine meadows.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arnica - a plant of the genus Arnica, especially used medicinally

Etymology

From Latin *Arnica*, the name of the plant genus.
