double bind

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʌbəl ˌbaɪnd / (AmE), / ˈdʌbəl ˌbaɪnd / (BrE)
A situation in which a person is confronted with two or more conflicting demands, making it impossible to satisfy both and often causing stress or confusion, especially when imposed by someone in authority.
conflicting demands causing no-win situation

Examples

She found herself in a double bind, unable to please her boss without upsetting her colleagues.
Parents sometimes place their children in a double bind by demanding independent thinking but punishing disobedience.
The therapist explained how a double bind can lead to anxiety and confusion in relationships.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dilemma - A situation in which a difficult choice has to be made between two or more alternatives
  • Synonym: catch-22 - A difficult situation from which there is no escape due to mutually conflicting or dependent conditions
