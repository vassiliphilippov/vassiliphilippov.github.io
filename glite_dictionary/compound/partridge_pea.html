partridge pea

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑr.trɪdʒ pi / (AmE), / ˈpɑː.trɪdʒ piː / (BrE)
A tropical American plant (Chamaecrista fasciculata), related to the genus Cassia, with sensitive leaflets and yellow flowers, often found in prairies and open woods.
tropical American plant with sensitive leaflets

Examples

The partridge pea blooms in late summer with bright yellow flowers across the prairies.
Gardeners value the partridge pea for its ability to attract pollinators and enrich the soil.
We spotted several partridge pea plants along the hiking trail, their pods rattling in the breeze.
