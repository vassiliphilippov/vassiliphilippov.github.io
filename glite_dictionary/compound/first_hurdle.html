first hurdle

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɝst ˈhɝrdl̩ / (AmE), / ˈfɜːst ˈhɜːdl / (BrE)
the initial difficulty or obstacle that must be overcome in a process or activity
initial obstacle or challenge

Examples

Securing approval from the board was the first hurdle the team faced.
Learning the new software proved to be the first hurdle in the transition process.
Getting everyone to agree on the project’s goals can be a tough first hurdle.

Synset Examples

The first hurdle in the project was securing funding.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obstacle - something that blocks progress or makes it difficult
  • Synonym: barrier - a circumstance or obstacle that keeps people or things apart
