Manila hemp

1. NOUN

/ məˈnɪlə hɛmp / (AmE), / məˈnɪlə hɛmp / (BrE)
A plant native to the Philippines, known for its leafstalks which produce strong fiber used in making rope, paper, and textiles.
fiber-producing Philippine plant

Examples

The coarse fiber obtained from Manila hemp is highly valued for making ropes and marine cables.
Exports of Manila hemp have played a significant role in the economy of the Philippines.
Unlike regular hemp, Manila hemp comes from the abaca plant, a type of banana.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abaca - the plant from which Manila hemp is obtained

Etymology

Named after Manila, the capital city of the Philippines, where the fiber was historically exported.

2. NOUN

/ məˈnɪlə hɛmp / (AmE), / məˈnɪlə hɛmp / (BrE)
a strong, flexible fiber obtained from the abaca plant of the Philippines, used for making ropes, textiles, and other products
fiber from the abaca plant of the Philippines

Examples

The ropes on the old sailing vessel were made of Manila hemp, prized for its durability.
Many specialty papers use Manila hemp fibers because of their strength.
She bought a basket woven from Manila hemp at the local market.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abaca - a species of banana native to the Philippines, source of Manila hemp

Etymology

Named after Manila, the capital of the Philippines, from where the fiber was widely exported.
