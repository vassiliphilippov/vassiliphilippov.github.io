smooth muscle cell

1. NOUN

/ smuð ˈmʌsəl sɛl / (AmE), / smuːð ˈmʌsl sɛl / (BrE)
A type of elongated cell that makes up smooth muscle tissue, responsible for involuntary movements in organs and blood vessels.
cell forming smooth muscle tissue
medical

Examples

The walls of the intestine are lined with smooth muscle cells that contract to move food along.
Unlike skeletal muscle, smooth muscle cells are not under voluntary control.
Blood vessels rely on smooth muscle cells to regulate their diameter and blood flow.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: skeletal muscle cell - a cell found in skeletal (voluntary) muscle tissue
  • Degree Higher: cardiac muscle cell - a cell found in heart (cardiac) muscle tissue
