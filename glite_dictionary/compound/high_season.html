high season

1. NOUN

/ haɪ ˈsiːzn̩ / (AmE), / haɪ ˈsiːzn / (BrE)
the period during the year when travel to a particular destination is most popular and prices are highest
peak period for travel or tourism

Examples

Hotel prices nearly double during the high season in coastal towns.
It's often difficult to find available flights in high season unless you book months in advance.
We prefer to avoid the crowds by traveling just before the high season starts.

Synset Examples

they traveled to Europe in high season

Related Terms

  • Antonym: low season - the period when travel demand is lowest and prices are reduced
