family Tetraonidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli tɛˌtrioʊˈnaɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli tɛˌtrɪəʊˈnaɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of birds that includes grouse, known for their plump bodies and feathered legs, found mainly in cooler regions of the Northern Hemisphere.
family of birds including grouse

Examples

Scientists have long studied the migration patterns of the family Tetraonidae in northern climates.
Conservationists are working to protect several species within the family Tetraonidae that are now threatened.
The diet and habitat of the family Tetraonidae vary widely depending on the specific species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grouse - Any of various birds in the family Tetraonidae

Etymology

From New Latin 'Tetraonidae', based on 'Tetrao', the type genus, from Greek 'tetraōn' (a kind of game bird).
