symphonic music

1. NOUN

/ sɪmˈfɑnɪk ˈmjuːzɪk / (AmE), / sɪmˈfɒnɪk ˈmjuːzɪk / (BrE)
Music composed for or performed by a symphony orchestra, often characterized by complex structure and extended length.
music for a symphony orchestra

Examples

The orchestra performed symphonic music by Beethoven to a packed concert hall.
Many composers devoted much of their careers to writing symphonic music.
She discovered her passion for symphonic music after attending her first live performance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: symphony - an extended musical composition for orchestra

Etymology

Symphonic from Greek 'sumphōnikos' (harmonious); music from Greek 'mousikē'.
