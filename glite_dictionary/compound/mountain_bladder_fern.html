mountain bladder fern

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaʊn.tən ˈblæd.ɚ fɝn / (AmE), / ˈmaʊn.tɪn ˈblæd.ə fɜːn / (BrE)
a species of fern that grows in rocky mountainous regions, known for its bladder-like structures
fern found in rocky mountains

Examples

The mountain bladder fern thrives in the cool, moist crevices of alpine rocks.
Researchers discovered a rare mountain bladder fern growing at an unusually high altitude.
Gardeners value the mountain bladder fern for its delicate fronds and ability to withstand rocky soil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fern - a flowerless plant with feathery or leafy fronds
