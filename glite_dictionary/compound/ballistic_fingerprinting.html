ballistic fingerprinting

1. NOUN

/ bəˈlɪstɪk ˈfɪŋɡɚˌprɪntɪŋ / (AmE), / bəˈlɪstɪk ˈfɪŋɡəˌprɪntɪŋ / (BrE)
the identification of a specific firearm by examining the unique marks it leaves on bullets and shell casings it fires
firearm identification via bullet and casing marks

Examples

The investigators used ballistic fingerprinting to match the bullet to the suspect's gun.
Advances in ballistic fingerprinting have made it easier to link firearms to multiple crime scenes.
The police department invested in new ballistic fingerprinting technology to improve their forensic investigations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ballistics - the science of projectiles and firearms

Etymology

From 'ballistic', relating to projectiles, and 'fingerprinting', meaning identification by unique patterns.
