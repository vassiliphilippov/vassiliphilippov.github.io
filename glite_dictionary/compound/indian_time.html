Indian time

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪn.di.ən taɪm / (AmE), / ˈɪn.di.ən taɪm / (BrE)
(humorous) A flexible or relaxed attitude toward punctuality, especially as a cultural stereotype associated with people of Indian descent.
relaxed approach to punctuality (humorous)
informal

Examples

Our meeting was supposed to start at 3 PM, but on Indian time it began at 3:45.
She joked that her whole family operates on Indian time.
If you arrive a little late, don't worry—everyone here understands Indian time.

Synset Examples

Don't worry about being on time; it's Indian time here.
She laughed and said, 'Welcome to Indian time!' when guests arrived late.
He always arrives late, claiming it's just Indian time.
