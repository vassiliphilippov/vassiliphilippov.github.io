honey-flower

1. NOUN

/ ˈhʌni ˌflaʊ.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈhʌni ˌflaʊ.ə / (BrE)
An Australian shrub whose flowers produce abundant nectar, often used by bees to make honey.
Australian shrub with nectar-rich flowers

Examples

The honey-flower attracts a wide variety of bees in the spring.
Many Australian gardens feature the honey-flower because of its bright blooms and sweet nectar.
Local beekeepers often seek out areas rich in honey-flowers to produce high-quality honey.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhʌniˌflaʊ.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈhʌniˌflaʊ.ə / (BrE)
An erect bushy shrub native to eastern Australia, producing clusters of red flowers abundant in nectar.
Australian shrub with nectar-rich red flowers

Examples

The vibrant honey-flower attracts many birds and insects to the garden.
We planted a honey-flower next to the fence to enjoy its striking red blooms.
Throughout spring, the honey-flower provides a rich source of nectar for local bees.
