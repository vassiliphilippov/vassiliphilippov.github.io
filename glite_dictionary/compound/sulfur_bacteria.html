sulfur bacteria

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌlfər bækˈtɪəriə / (AmE), / ˈsʌlfə bækˈtɪəriə / (BrE)
any bacterium of the genus Thiobacillus, which obtains energy by oxidizing sulfur or sulfur compounds.
bacteria that oxidize sulfur for energy

Examples

Sulfur bacteria play an important role in the natural cycling of sulfur in the environment.
Scientists discovered sulfur bacteria living near the hydrothermal vents on the ocean floor.
Some types of sulfur bacteria are used in bioremediation to treat polluted water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Thiobacillus - genus of bacteria that oxidize sulfur
