Australian bonytongue

1. NOUN

/ əˈstreɪliən ˈboʊniˌtʌŋ / (AmE), / ɒˈstreɪliən ˈbəʊnitʌŋ / (BrE)
a large freshwater fish native to Australian rivers, noted for crescent-shaped reddish spots on each scale (Scleropages jardinii)
large Australian river fish with spotted scales

Examples

The Australian bonytongue is prized by aquarium enthusiasts for its unique spotted scales.
In northern Australia, the Australian bonytongue inhabits slow-moving rivers and billabongs.
Researchers are studying the habitat requirements of the Australian bonytongue to support conservation efforts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Scleropages jardinii - the scientific name for the Australian bonytongue
