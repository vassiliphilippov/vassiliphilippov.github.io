Swan River daisy

1. NOUN

/ swɑn ˈrɪvɚ ˈdeɪzi / (AmE), / swɒn ˈrɪvə ˈdeɪzi / (BrE)
A western Australian annual plant, widely cultivated for its flower heads with white, blue, violet, or variegated rays.
Australian annual flower with variegated rays

Examples

The vibrant Swan River daisy brought a splash of color to the garden beds.
Many gardeners choose the Swan River daisy for its delicate purple and blue blossoms.
He planted several Swan River daisies along the edge of the path to brighten the walkway.

Etymology

Named after the Swan River region in Western Australia, where it is native.
