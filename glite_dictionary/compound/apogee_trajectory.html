apogee trajectory

1. NOUN

/ ˈæpəˌdʒi trəˈdʒɛktəri / (AmE), / ˈapədʒi trəˈdʒɛktəri / (BrE)
the path followed by a spacecraft as it passes through the highest point in its orbit around a body
spacecraft's path at highest orbit point

Examples

The engineers spent weeks perfecting the apogee trajectory to ensure the probe reached its intended distance.
After launch, the satellite adjusted its engines to optimize its apogee trajectory.
Careful tracking of the apogee trajectory allowed the mission to collect valuable data from the outer atmosphere.

Synset Examples

We calculated the spacecraft's apogee trajectory.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: perigee trajectory - the path of a spacecraft's lowest point in orbit

Etymology

From 'apogee' (from Greek apogaion, farthest point from Earth) + 'trajectory' (from Latin trajectoria, path across).
