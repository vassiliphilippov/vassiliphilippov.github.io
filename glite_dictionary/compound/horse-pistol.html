horse-pistol

1. NOUN

/ hɔrs ˌpɪs.təl / (AmE), / hɔːs ˌpɪs.təl / (BrE)
a large pistol, typically carried in a holster by mounted soldiers or horsemen, used mainly in the past
large historical pistol for horsemen
archaic

Examples

The soldier drew his horse-pistol from its leather holster before charging into battle.
Antique collectors often seek out a well-preserved horse-pistol as a centerpiece for their collections.
The novel described the highwayman's horse-pistol shining in the moonlight as he blocked the road.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pistol - a small firearm designed to be held in one hand

Etymology

horse refers to mounted use; pistol from French *pistolet* (small gun).
