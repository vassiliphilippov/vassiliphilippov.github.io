Battle of Wake Island

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæt̬.əl əv weɪk ˈaɪ.lənd / (AmE), / ˈbæt.əl əv weɪk ˈaɪ.lənd / (BrE)
a battle in December 1941 during World War II, in which Japanese forces captured Wake Island after a determined defense by United States marines
World War II battle on Wake Island, 1941

Examples

The Battle of Wake Island was a significant early clash in the Pacific Theater of World War II.
Historians often cite the bravery shown during the Battle of Wake Island as an example of determination under siege.
Several documentaries detail the events leading up to and during the Battle of Wake Island.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Battle of Midway - a pivotal World War II battle fought near Midway Atoll in 1942
