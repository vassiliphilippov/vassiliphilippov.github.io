quick study

1. NOUN

/ kwɪk ˈstʌdi / (AmE), / kwɪk ˈstʌdi / (BrE)
a person who is able to learn new subjects or skills rapidly and easily
person who learns quickly

Examples

Although she had never tried painting before, Maria proved to be a quick study.
Thanks to being a quick study, he adapted to the new software in just one afternoon.
Even the coach was surprised at how much this quick study improved during the first week of training.

Synset Examples

she soaks up foreign languages like a sponge

Related Terms

  • Antonym: slow learner - person who takes longer than average to learn things
