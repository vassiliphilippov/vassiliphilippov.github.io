stage right

1. NOUN

/ steɪdʒ raɪt / (AmE), / steɪdʒ raɪt / (BrE)
The area of a stage that is to the right of an actor facing the audience.
right side of a stage from actor's view

Examples

The actor exited stage right after delivering his final line.
She waited patiently stage right before making her entrance.
All props needed for the scene should be placed stage right as instructed.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: stage left - the part of the stage on the actor's left as the actor faces the audience

2. NOUN

/ steɪdʒ raɪt / (AmE), / steɪdʒ raɪt / (BrE)
the right side of the stage as seen from the point of view of a performer facing the audience
right side of the stage (from actor's view)

Examples

The lead actress exited stage right after delivering her final line.
Please place the prop table stage right for the next scene.
He entered stage right, surprising the rest of the cast.

Synset Examples

She waved to the audience as she exited stage right.
The actor entered stage right, capturing everyone's attention.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: stage left - the left side of the stage from the actor's perspective
