Ravenna grass

1. NOUN

/ rəˈvɛnə ɡræs / (AmE), / rəˈvɛnə ɡrɑːs / (BrE)
a tall grass often grown for its long, white-ribbed leaves and large, feathery plumes resembling those of pampas grass
tall ornamental grass with plume-like flowers

Examples

Many gardeners plant Ravenna grass to create dramatic focal points in their landscapes.
The feathery plumes of Ravenna grass sway beautifully in the autumn breeze.
Unlike pampas grass, Ravenna grass can tolerate colder climates and poor soils.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pampas grass - a tall South American grass with large feathery plumes, often used in gardens
