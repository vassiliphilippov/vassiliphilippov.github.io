to see things differently

1. VERB

/ si ˈθɪŋz ˈdɪf.ɚ.ənt.li / (AmE), / siː ˈθɪŋz ˈdɪf.ər.ənt.li / (BrE)
to have a different opinion or perspective from others
have a different perspective

Examples

After traveling abroad, she began to see things differently than before.
Sometimes children see things differently from adults because of their limited experience.
It's important to listen to others who see things differently in a discussion.

Synset Examples

She tends to see things differently than the rest of us.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to disagree - to have a different opinion
  • Antonym: to see eye to eye - to agree completely
