genus Lycopersicum

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs laɪ.kəˈpɜr.sɪ.kəm / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs lɪ.kəˈpɜː.sɪ.kəm / (BrE)
a genus of flowering plants in the nightshade family (Solanaceae), including the cultivated tomato and its wild relatives
genus of plants including the tomato

Examples

The genus Lycopersicum contains several species, including the common tomato.
Botanists have reclassified many species once placed in genus Lycopersicum into Solanum.
The characteristics of genus Lycopersicum are widely studied in plant genetics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tomato - the red or yellow edible fruit of the plant Solanum lycopersicum

Etymology

From Latin 'Lycopersicum', meaning 'wolf peach', the historical name for the tomato; used in scientific classification.
