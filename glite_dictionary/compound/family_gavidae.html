family Gavidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ˌɡeɪvɪˌdi / (AmE), / ˈfæməli ˌɡævɪˌdiː / (BrE)
The scientific family of aquatic birds known as loons or divers, characterized by their streamlined bodies and exceptional diving ability.
the loon family of birds

Examples

The family Gavidae is best known for the haunting calls of its members across northern lakes.
Biologists observed that members of the family Gavidae are expert divers and fishers.
Loons, classified under the family Gavidae, breed in North America and northern Eurasia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loon - a large aquatic bird of the genus Gavia

Etymology

From New Latin Gavidae, based on the genus Gavia (loon).
