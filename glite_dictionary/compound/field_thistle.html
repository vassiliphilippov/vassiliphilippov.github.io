field thistle

1. NOUN

/ ˈfiːld ˌθɪsəl / (AmE), / ˈfiːld ˌθɪsl̩ / (BrE)
A stout North American thistle with purplish-pink flower heads, typically found in open fields and meadows.
a North American thistle with purplish-pink flowers

Examples

The field thistle blooms abundantly in the late summer, attracting butterflies and bees.
We spotted a patch of field thistle near the edge of the meadow.
Gardeners often remove field thistle to prevent it from spreading through open fields.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thistle - a plant with prickly leaves and typically purple flowers
