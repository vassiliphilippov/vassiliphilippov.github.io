Muscovy duck

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌskəvi dʌk / (AmE), / ˈmʌskəvi dʌk / (BrE)
a large wild duck native to Central and South America, often domesticated for its meat and eggs
large domesticated duck species

Examples

The farm keeps a flock of Muscovy ducks for their flavorful meat.
A single Muscovy duck glided across the pond, its crest raised.
Muscovy ducks are quieter than most other domestic duck breeds.

Etymology

The name may relate to 'Muscovy', the old name for Moscow, though the species does not originate from Russia.
