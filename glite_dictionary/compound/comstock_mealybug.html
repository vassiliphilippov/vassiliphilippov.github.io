Comstock mealybug

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑm.stɑk ˈmiː.li.bʌɡ / (AmE), / ˈkɒm.stɒk ˈmiː.li.bʌɡ / (BrE)
a species of Asiatic mealybug (Pseudococcus comstocki) introduced into the United States, known as a pest on citrus and apple trees
Asiatic pest insect of fruit trees

Examples

The Comstock mealybug has caused significant damage to citrus groves in California.
Orchard owners monitor their crops regularly to check for infestations of the Comstock mealybug.
Effective pesticides are used to control the spread of the Comstock mealybug among apple trees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mealybug - small, soft-bodied insect in the family Pseudococcidae, some are plant pests

Etymology

Named after American entomologist John Henry Comstock (1849–1931), with 'mealybug' describing the insect's waxy coating.
