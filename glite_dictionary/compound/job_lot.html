job lot

1. NOUN

/ dʒɑb lɑt / (AmE), / dʒɒb lɒt / (BrE)
a collection of miscellaneous items sold as a single group, often at a reduced price
miscellaneous items sold together

Examples

We bought a job lot of plates and glasses at the auction.
The shop is selling a job lot of clothing at a heavily discounted price.
He picked up a job lot of books from a secondhand dealer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: batch - a quantity of goods produced or handled together
  • Synonym: assortment - a mixture or collection of different things
