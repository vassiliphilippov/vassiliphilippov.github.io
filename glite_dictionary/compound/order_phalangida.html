order Phalangida

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdər fəˌlændʒɪdə / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə fəˌlændʒɪdə / (BrE)
A taxonomic order of arachnids commonly known as harvestmen, distinguished by their long legs and fused body regions.
order of arachnids; harvestmen

Examples

The order Phalangida includes many species commonly known as harvestmen or daddy longlegs.
Unlike spiders, members of the order Phalangida do not possess venom or silk glands.
Researchers discovered a new fossil species within the order Phalangida in ancient rock formations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: harvestman - a common name for members of order Phalangida
  • Antonym: spider - an arachnid of the order Araneae, distinct from harvestmen

Etymology

From New Latin 'Phalangida', based on 'phalanges' (fingers or limbs), inspired by the appendages of these arachnids.
