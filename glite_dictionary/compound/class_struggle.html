class struggle

1. NOUN

/ klæs ˈstrʌɡəl / (AmE), / klɑːs ˈstrʌɡl̩ / (BrE)
conflict or antagonism between different social or economic classes, especially between the proletariat and the capitalist class
conflict between social or economic classes

Examples

The idea of class struggle was central to Marxist theory.
Many historians argue that class struggle has shaped much of modern history.
Workers united in response to what they saw as class struggle against their employers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: class conflict - conflict between different social classes
  • Antonym: social harmony - absence of conflict or antagonism between social classes
