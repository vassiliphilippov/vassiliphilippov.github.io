Big Sioux River

1. PROPN

/ bɪɡ suː ˈrɪvɚ / (AmE), / bɪɡ suː ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
a river in the United States flowing south from northeastern South Dakota to join the Missouri River at Sioux City, Iowa
river in South Dakota and Iowa, USA

Examples

The Big Sioux River flooded several towns after heavy spring rains.
Fishing is a popular activity along the Big Sioux River during the summer months.
The Big Sioux River serves as a natural border between parts of Iowa and South Dakota.

Etymology

Named for the Sioux people and its relatively large size compared to nearby rivers.
