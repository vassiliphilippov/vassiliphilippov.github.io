major form class

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪdʒər fɔrm klæs / (AmE), / ˈmeɪdʒə fɔːm klɑːs / (BrE)
any of the main categories of words in traditional grammar, such as noun, verb, adjective, or adverb, that are based on grammatical function and form
main parts of speech in grammar

Examples

Nouns, verbs, adjectives, and adverbs are considered major form classes in English grammar.
Understanding the major form class of a word helps determine its role in a sentence.
Linguists often divide words into major form classes and minor form classes for analysis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: part of speech - a category into which words are classified based on their function, such as noun or verb
  • Antonym: minor form class - a less prominent category of words in grammar, often including prepositions, conjunctions, and interjections
