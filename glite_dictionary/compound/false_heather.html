false heather

1. NOUN

/ fɔls ˈhɛðər / (AmE), / fɔːls ˈhɛðə / (BrE)
A North American decumbent evergreen, heathlike plant with yellow flowers, commonly grown as a ground cover.
evergreen plant with yellow flowers

Examples

The garden bed is edged with false heather, adding a touch of vibrant yellow among the greenery.
Many landscapers recommend false heather for its low maintenance and year-round foliage.
After planting false heather, the backyard looked more colorful and inviting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cuphea - A genus of flowering plants, some species of which are called false heather
