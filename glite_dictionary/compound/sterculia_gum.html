sterculia gum

1. NOUN

/ stɝːˈkjuːliə ɡʌm / (AmE), / stɜːˈkjuːliə ɡʌm / (BrE)
a natural gum obtained from certain Asian Sterculia trees, used to thicken food, cosmetics, and in textile finishing
gum from Asian Sterculia trees, used as thickener

Examples

Sterculia gum is often used as a thickening agent in processed foods and pharmaceutical products.
The textile manufacturer added sterculia gum to their fabrics to improve smoothness and finish.
Cosmetic formulations sometimes include sterculia gum for its natural gelling properties.

Etymology

From the genus name Sterculia, named after the Roman god Sterculius.
