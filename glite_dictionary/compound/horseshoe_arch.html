horseshoe arch

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɔrsˌʃu ɑrtʃ / (AmE), / ˈhɔːsʃuː ɑːtʃ / (BrE)
an arch with a curve that extends wider than a semicircle before rounding off, resembling a horseshoe in shape; commonly found in Moorish and Islamic architecture.
arch with an outward-flaring curved shape

Examples

The old mosque features a stunning horseshoe arch above its main entrance.
Many medieval Spanish churches incorporated the horseshoe arch into their designs.
You can easily recognize the horseshoe arch by its distinctive, flared outline.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arch - a curved structure spanning an opening and supporting the weight above it
