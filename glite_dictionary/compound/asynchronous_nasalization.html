asynchronous nasalization

1. NOUN

/ əˈsɪŋ.krə.nəs ˌneɪ.zəl.ɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / əˈsɪŋ.krə.nəs ˌneɪ.zə.laɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
A type of nasalization in which an oral vowel is followed by a nasal semivowel, rather than both features occurring simultaneously.
nasalization where oral vowel precedes nasal semivowel
medical

Examples

The presence of asynchronous nasalization can affect the way certain vowels are pronounced in some languages.
Researchers found that asynchronous nasalization occurs primarily before fricative consonants in the studied dialect.
In Polish, word-final nasal vowels often exhibit asynchronous nasalization.

Synset Examples

It is only before a fricative (and for ‘ą’ in word-final position) that ‘ę’ and ‘ą’ are pronounced as asynchronous nasal vowels, that is, /eu/ and /ou/.
Before a fricative and in word-final position (in the case of ą) they are transcribed as an oral vowel /ɔ, ɛ/ followed by a nasal consonant /ɲ, ŋ/ or /j̃, w̃/

Related Terms

  • Antonym: synchronous nasalization - nasalization where oral and nasal features occur simultaneously

Etymology

From 'asynchronous' (not occurring at the same time) + 'nasalization'.
