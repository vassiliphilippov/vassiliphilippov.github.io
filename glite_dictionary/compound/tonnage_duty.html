tonnage duty

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʌnɪdʒ ˈduːti / (AmE), / ˈtʌnɪdʒ ˈdjuːti / (BrE)
A tax levied on ships entering a country, calculated based on the tonnage (carrying capacity) of the ship.
tax on ships based on tonnage
legal

Examples

The shipping company had to pay a substantial tonnage duty upon entering the American port.
Several nations abolished the tonnage duty to encourage more international trade.
Because their vessel was large, the crew faced higher tonnage duty charges.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tonnage - the capacity of a ship measured in tons
