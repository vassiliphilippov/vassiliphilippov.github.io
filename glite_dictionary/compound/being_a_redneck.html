being a redneck

1. NOUN

/ ˈbiːɪŋ ə ˈrɛdˌnɛk / (AmE), / ˈbiːɪŋ ə ˈrɛd.nɛk / (BrE)
The state or quality of identifying with rural, working-class culture in the United States, often characterized by traditional or rustic values; sometimes used humorously or pejoratively.
identifying with rural, working-class US culture
informal american

Examples

He's proud of being a redneck and celebrates his country lifestyle.
Some comedians make jokes about being a redneck to connect with their audience.
She never shied away from being a redneck and enjoyed living outside big cities.

Synset Examples

He joked about being a redneck at the family reunion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: redneck - A working-class white person from the rural southern United States, often seen as having unrefined or traditional views.
  • Synonym: hillbilly - A person from a rural mountainous area, especially in the southern US, often regarded as unsophisticated.
  • Antonym: urbane - Refined in manner, typically associated with city life; opposite of rustic or unsophisticated.

Etymology

Redneck: Originally referring to sunburned necks of rural laborers in the southern United States, now broadly denotes rural, working-class people.
