public service

1. NOUN

/ ˈpʌblɪk ˈsɝvɪs / (AmE), / ˈpʌblɪk ˈsɜːvɪs / (BrE)
employment or work within a government system, especially as part of the civil service.
work in government employment

Examples

After graduating, she decided to pursue a career in public service.
Many people enter public service to make a positive impact on their communities.
He spent most of his life working in public service at various government agencies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: civil service - permanent professional branches of a government's administration

2. NOUN

/ ˈpʌblɪk ˈsɝːvɪs / (AmE), / ˈpʌblɪk ˈsɜːvɪs / (BrE)
a service provided for the benefit of the community, especially by a government or official body
service provided for the public good

Examples

Many consider access to clean water a basic public service that should be protected.
The government is increasing its investment in public services like healthcare and education.
After retiring, she began volunteering with organizations dedicated to public service.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: civil service - the permanent professional branches of a government's administration

3. NOUN

/ ˈpʌblɪk ˈsɜrvɪs / (AmE), / ˈpʌblɪk ˈsɜːvɪs / (BrE)
A service provided by the government to benefit the community, such as education, health care, or public transportation.
government-provided community service

Examples

After the hurricane, many public services were interrupted, causing difficulties for residents.
Reliable public service is vital for a healthy, functioning society.
The government increased funding for public services such as healthcare and transportation.
