Ardennes counteroffensive

1. PROPN

/ ɑrˈdɛnz ˌkaʊntərəˈfɛnsɪv / (AmE), / ɑːˈdɛn ˌkaʊntərəˈfɛnsɪv / (BrE)
a major German counterattack against Allied forces in the Ardennes region of Belgium during World War II, launched in December 1944 and also known as the Battle of the Bulge
WWII German attack in Ardennes, 1944

Examples

Historians regard the Ardennes counteroffensive as one of the most significant battles of World War II.
Many Allied soldiers were caught off guard during the Ardennes counteroffensive in December 1944.
The Ardennes counteroffensive is often studied for its strategic surprise and impact on the war's outcome.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Battle of the Bulge - another name for the Ardennes counteroffensive
