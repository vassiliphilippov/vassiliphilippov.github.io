Peruvian cotton

1. NOUN

/ pəˈruːviən ˈkɑːtn̩ / (AmE), / pəˈruːviən ˈkɒtn̩ / (BrE)
A type of cotton characterized by long, rough, hairy fibers, typically grown in Peru.
type of cotton with long rough fibers

Examples

The fabric was made from Peruvian cotton, prized for its softness and durability.
Farmers in the region specialize in growing Peruvian cotton due to its superior quality.
Many luxury shirts are crafted using Peruvian cotton for its distinctive texture and strength.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cotton - soft white fibrous substance around seeds of the cotton plant
