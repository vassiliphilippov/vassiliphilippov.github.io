order Cetacea

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdər səˈteɪʃiə / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə sɪˈteɪʃɪə / (BrE)
the order of mammals that includes whales, dolphins, and porpoises
order of aquatic mammals (Cetacea)

Examples

The order Cetacea comprises a diverse range of aquatic mammals including both whales and dolphins.
Marine biologists study members of the order Cetacea to understand their migration patterns.
Evolutionary adaptations in the order Cetacea allow these animals to thrive in aquatic environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cetacean - a mammal of the order Cetacea, such as a whale, dolphin, or porpoise

Etymology

From Latin 'Cetacea', neuter plural of 'cētāceus' (pertaining to a whale), from 'cetus' (whale).
