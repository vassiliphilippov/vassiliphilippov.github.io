Protestant work ethic

1. NOUN

/ ˈproʊt̬əstənt wɜrk ˈɛθɪk / (AmE), / ˈprɒtɪstənt wɜːk ˈɛθɪk / (BrE)
The belief that hard work, discipline, and frugality are a moral benefit, especially as advocated by Protestant Christianity.
belief in hard work and discipline for moral good

Examples

Many historians argue that the Protestant work ethic played a significant role in the rise of capitalism.
She was raised with a strong Protestant work ethic that emphasized thrift and perseverance.
The company's culture is deeply influenced by the Protestant work ethic.

Synset Examples

The company thrives on the principles of the Protestant work ethic.
Her success is attributed to the Protestant work ethic.
He embodies the Protestant work ethic with his dedication.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: work ethic - the principle that hard work is intrinsically virtuous or worthy of reward

Etymology

From Protestant, a branch of Christianity, and 'work ethic', a principle that hard work is intrinsically virtuous.
