Great Britain

1. PROPN

/ ɡreɪt ˈbrɪtən / (AmE), / ɡreɪt ˈbrɪtn̩ / (BrE)
an island in the British Isles consisting of England, Scotland, and Wales
island comprising England, Scotland, and Wales

Examples

The mountains of Great Britain attract hikers from all over the world.
She traveled around Great Britain by train, visiting cities in England, Scotland, and Wales.
The climate of Great Britain is generally mild, with frequent rainfall throughout the year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: United Kingdom - the country consisting of Great Britain and Northern Ireland
  • Synonym: Britain - alternative informal name for Great Britain or the UK

Etymology

From Old English 'Bretan' (Britons), with 'Great' distinguishing it from Brittany in France.

2. PROPN

/ ɡreɪt ˈbrɪtən / (AmE), / ɡreɪt ˈbrɪt.ən / (BrE)
an island in northwestern Europe comprising the countries of England, Scotland, and Wales; sometimes used loosely to refer to the United Kingdom, which also includes Northern Ireland
the island of England, Scotland, and Wales

Examples

The Olympic team from Great Britain won several gold medals at the games.
Many tourists come to Great Britain to explore its rich history and picturesque landscapes.
The political relationship between Great Britain and Northern Ireland is often discussed in the news.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: United Kingdom - a country consisting of Great Britain and Northern Ireland
  • Synonym: England - country in southern Great Britain
  • Synonym: Scotland - country in northern Great Britain
  • Synonym: Wales - country in western Great Britain

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'Britannia Major' (Greater Britain) to distinguish from Brittany in France.
