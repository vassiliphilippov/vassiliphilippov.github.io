Chinese Revolution

1. NOUN

/ ˌtʃaɪˈniz ˌrɛvəˈluʃən / (AmE), / ˌtʃaɪˈniːz ˌrɛvəˈluːʃən / (BrE)
the revolution in China (1911–1912) that overthrew the Manchu dynasty and led to the establishment of the Republic of China
the 1911–1912 revolution overthrowing the Manchu dynasty in China

Examples

Many historians consider the Chinese Revolution a turning point in modern Asian history.
The Chinese Revolution of 1911 led to the collapse of the Qing dynasty.
Dr. Sun Yat-sen played a leading role in the Chinese Revolution.
