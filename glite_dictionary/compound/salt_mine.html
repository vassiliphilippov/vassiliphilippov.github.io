salt mine

1. NOUN

/ sɔlt maɪn / (AmE), / sɒlt maɪn / (BrE)
a mine from which salt is extracted
a mine for extracting salt

Examples

The ancient city was famous for its extensive salt mine operations.
Working in a salt mine can be both physically demanding and dangerous.
They toured the underground salt mine to learn about its history and geology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mine - an excavation in the earth for extracting minerals

2. NOUN

/ sɔlt maɪn / (AmE), / sɒlt maɪn / (BrE)
a place of work, especially one that is tedious, confining, or physically demanding; often used humorously or ironically.
tedious or confining workplace (figurative)

Examples

After the holiday break, it was back to the salt mine for most employees.
He jokingly refers to his office as the salt mine because of the endless paperwork.
She left the salt mine early today, relieved to finally have a night off.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drudgery - hard, menial, or dull work
  • Degree Higher: prison - a place of confinement; reference to being confined

Etymology

Originally referred to an actual mine where salt is extracted, later used figuratively for any difficult or dreary workplace.

3. NOUN

/ sɔlt maɪn / (AmE), / sɒlt maɪn / (BrE)
(informal, often humorous) A place where people have to do hard, monotonous, or tedious work, especially as a metaphor for a difficult or unpleasant job.
place of hard or tedious work (figurative)
informal

Examples

After the holidays, it's back to the salt mine for all the employees.
She referred to her daily commute and office job as the salt mine.
He escaped the salt mine for a week-long vacation at the beach.

Synset Examples

They described their office as a salt mine of stress.
He joked about his job feeling like a salt mine.

Etymology

Alluding to the historically harsh conditions of mining salt.
