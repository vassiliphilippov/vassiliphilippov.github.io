wedge bone

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɛdʒ boʊn / (AmE), / ˈwɛdʒ bəʊn / (BrE)
the part of the sirloin nearest the rump, often identified in butchery and meat preparation.
part of sirloin near the rump

Examples

The butcher carefully trimmed the wedge bone from the sirloin before slicing the steaks.
In traditional British butchery, the wedge bone cut is considered particularly flavorful.
Some recipes call for removing the wedge bone to make carving the roast easier.
