refined sugar

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈfaɪnd ˈʃʌɡɚ / (AmE), / rɪˈfaɪnd ˈʃʌɡə / (BrE)
White, purified sugar obtained through processing, commonly used as a sweetener in food and drinks.
processed white sugar used as sweetener

Examples

Nutritionists often recommend reducing refined sugar intake for better health.
The recipe calls for two cups of refined sugar to sweeten the cake.
Unlike honey or fruit, refined sugar provides no vitamins or minerals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: table sugar - white crystalline sugar used as a sweetener
