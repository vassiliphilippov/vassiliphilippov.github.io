trench knife

1. NOUN

/ trɛntʃ naɪf / (AmE), / trɛntʃ naɪf / (BrE)
A knife with a double-edged blade, originally designed for close-quarters combat, especially in trench warfare.
double-edged combat knife

Examples

He displayed a rare trench knife from World War I in his collection.
The soldier gripped his trench knife tightly, prepared for any close combat.
Collectors often seek original trench knives because of their unique historical significance.

Etymology

Named for its use in trench warfare, especially during World War I.
