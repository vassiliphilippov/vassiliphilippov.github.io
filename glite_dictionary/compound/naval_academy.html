naval academy

1. NOUN

/ ˈneɪvəl əˈkædəmi / (AmE), / ˈneɪvəl əˈkadəmi / (BrE)
An institution where people are educated and trained to become naval officers.
military school for training naval officers

Examples

He graduated from the naval academy and served for ten years in the navy.
The entrance exam for the naval academy is known to be very competitive.
Many aspiring officers hope to attend a prestigious naval academy to start their military careers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: military academy - an institution for training officers for the armed forces
