second-hand speech

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛkəndˌhænd ˈspiːtʃ / (AmE), / ˌsɛkəndˌhænd ˈspiːtʃ / (BrE)
conversation or dialogue that is overheard, particularly when someone inadvertently hears others' cellphone conversations without participating in them.
overheard conversation, especially on cellphones

Examples

I try to ignore second-hand speech on the train, but sometimes it's impossible to tune out loud conversations.
The rise of mobile phones has made second-hand speech a common annoyance in public spaces.
She found second-hand speech distracting, especially when people shared personal details in crowded cafes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to overhear - to hear someone else's conversation without their knowledge
