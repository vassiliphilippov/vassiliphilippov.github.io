night soil

1. NOUN

/ ˈnaɪt ˌsɔɪl / (AmE), / ˈnaɪt ˌsɔɪl / (BrE)
human excrement collected at night from cesspools, privies, etc., and used as fertilizer
human excreta used as fertilizer

Examples

Before modern sanitation systems, night soil was commonly spread on fields to enrich the soil.
The use of night soil as fertilizer posed serious health risks in many cities.
Collectors would remove night soil from urban homes under cover of darkness.

Etymology

From the practice of collecting human waste at night for use as fertilizer.
