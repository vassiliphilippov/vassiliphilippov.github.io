recipient role

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈsɪpiənt roʊl / (AmE), / rɪˈsɪpiənt rəʊl / (BrE)
In linguistics, the semantic role of the person or animal that receives something or is affected by the action expressed by the verb in a sentence.
linguistics: role of receiver in a sentence

Examples

In the sentence 'She gave the dog a treat,' the dog fulfils the recipient role.
Understanding the recipient role is important in analyzing sentence structure in linguistics.
Not every verb can assign a recipient role to an argument in a sentence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beneficiary role - the semantic role of someone benefiting from the action
  • Antonym: agent role - the semantic role of the doer of the action

Etymology

Formed from 'recipient' (from Latin recipiens, receiving) and 'role.'
