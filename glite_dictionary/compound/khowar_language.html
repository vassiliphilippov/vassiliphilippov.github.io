Khowar language

1. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊ.wɑr ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈkəʊ.wɑː ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
a Dardic language spoken primarily in northwestern Pakistan, especially in the Chitral district
Dardic language of northwestern Pakistan

Examples

Many people in Chitral speak Khowar language as their mother tongue.
The Khowar language has its own script and rich oral literature.
He is studying the grammar of the Khowar language for his research project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Dardic language - a subgroup of the Indo-Aryan languages spoken in northern Pakistan, India, and Afghanistan

Etymology

From 'Khowar', the native name of the language, meaning 'language of the Kho people'.
