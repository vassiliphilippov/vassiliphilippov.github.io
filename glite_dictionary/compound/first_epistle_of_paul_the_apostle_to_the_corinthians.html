First Epistle of Paul the Apostle to the Corinthians

1. PROPN

/ fɝst ɪˈpɪsəl ʌv pɔl ði əˈpɑsəl tu ðə kəˈrɪnθiənz / (AmE), / fɜːst ɪˈpɪsəl ɒv pɔːl ði əˈpɒsl tuː ðə kəˈrɪnθiənz / (BrE)
the first of two letters in the New Testament traditionally attributed to Saint Paul and addressed to the church at Corinth
New Testament letter from Saint Paul to Corinth

Examples

Many churches study passages from the First Epistle of Paul the Apostle to the Corinthians during their Bible lessons.
The First Epistle of Paul the Apostle to the Corinthians addresses issues facing the early Christian community in Corinth.
Scholars have written extensively about the teachings found in the First Epistle of Paul the Apostle to the Corinthians.
