poison oak

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɔɪzən oʊk / (AmE), / ˈpɔɪzən əʊk / (BrE)
a North American climbing or spreading plant with leaves that resemble oak leaves, producing an oil that causes skin irritation and rash upon contact
plant causing skin irritation

Examples

He developed a rash after brushing against poison oak on the hiking trail.
It is important to recognize poison oak to avoid skin irritation during outdoor activities.
The park ranger warned visitors about the presence of poison oak near the campsite.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poison ivy - North American plant with similar rash-causing oil

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɔɪzən oʊk / (AmE), / ˈpɔɪzən əʊk / (BrE)
A skin irritation or dermatitis caused by contact with the leaves or stems of the poison oak plant, which contain allergenic oils.
skin rash from poison oak plant
medical

Examples

Many hikers develop a painful rash after touching poison oak during their travels.
He had to see a doctor to treat his poison oak after clearing the backyard.
The camp counselor warned the children to avoid poison oak when walking through the woods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poison ivy - skin rash or plant causing rash, related toxic plant
  • Synonym: contact dermatitis - inflammation of the skin caused by contact with an allergen or irritant
