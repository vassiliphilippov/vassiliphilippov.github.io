as a plute

1. X

/ æz ə pluːt / (AmE), / æz ə pluːt / (BrE)
(informal) As someone who is wealthy or privileged; in the manner of a plutocrat.
in the manner of a wealthy or privileged person
informal

Examples

Ever since the promotion, he started acting as a plute and lost touch with his old friends.
She spoke as a plute when discussing luxury vacations most people couldn't afford.
Politicians who live as a plute often struggle to relate to ordinary voters.

Synset Examples

As a plute, he rarely understood the struggles of the average citizen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: as a plutocrat - as someone wealthy or a member of the ruling class
  • Antonym: as a pauper - as someone poor or disadvantaged

Etymology

'Plute' is an informal shortening of 'plutocrat', from Greek *ploutokratia* (rule by the wealthy).
