breech birth

1. NOUN

/ britʃ bɜrθ / (AmE), / briːtʃ bɜːθ / (BrE)
A birth in which a baby is delivered feet or buttocks first, rather than head first.
birth with feet or buttocks first
medical

Examples

The doctors were prepared for a breech birth when the baby's feet began to emerge first.
She needed a cesarean section due to the risks associated with a breech birth.
A breech birth can be more complicated than a head-first delivery.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cephalic birth - a birth in which the baby's head emerges first

Etymology

From 'breech' (buttocks) and 'birth', with 'breech' ultimately from Old English 'bryċ'.
