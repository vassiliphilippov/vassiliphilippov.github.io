strong verb

1. NOUN

/ strɔŋ vɝb / (AmE), / strɒŋ vɜːb / (BrE)
a verb that conveys vivid and powerful action, making writing more forceful and expressive
vivid, forceful action verb

Examples

Writers are encouraged to choose strong verbs to create more dynamic and engaging sentences.
Replacing generic verbs with strong verbs can greatly improve your prose.
Her editor suggested she use more strong verbs to strengthen the impact of her story.

Synset Examples

To improve writing, focus on strong verbs.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: weak verb - a verb that relies on auxiliary words or lacks vividness
