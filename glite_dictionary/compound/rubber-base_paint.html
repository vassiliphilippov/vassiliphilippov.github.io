rubber-base paint

1. NOUN

/ ˈrʌbər beɪs peɪnt / (AmE), / ˈrʌbə beɪs peɪnt / (BrE)
A type of water-based paint that uses a latex binder, providing flexibility and durability.
water-based paint with latex binder

Examples

We decided to use rubber-base paint for the kitchen walls because it dries quickly and is easy to clean.
Most modern houses are painted with rubber-base paint due to its resistance to moisture.
The painter recommended rubber-base paint for the bathroom to prevent mold and mildew.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: latex paint - water-based paint using latex as a binder
