Chippewaian language

1. NOUN

/ tʃɪpəˈweɪən ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / tʃɪpəˈweɪən ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Athabaskan language traditionally spoken by the Chipewyan people of northern Canada.
language of the Chipewyan people

Examples

The Chippewaian language is still spoken by elder members of the community in northern Canada.
Linguists documented several oral histories told in the Chippewaian language.
Efforts are underway to revitalize the Chippewaian language for future generations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Chipewyan - a member of the indigenous Dene people of northern Canada
  • Synonym: Athabaskan language - a family of indigenous North American languages including Chipewyan

Etymology

From 'Chipewyan', the name of the Dene-speaking indigenous people of northern Canada, with English 'language'.
