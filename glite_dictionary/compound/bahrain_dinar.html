Bahrain dinar

1. NOUN

/ bəˈreɪn dɪˈnɑr / (AmE), / bəˈreɪn dɪˈnɑː / (BrE)
the official currency and basic monetary unit of Bahrain, equal to 1,000 fils
currency unit of Bahrain

Examples

The price of the car is 7,500 Bahrain dinar including taxes and registration.
Tourists often exchange their dollars for Bahrain dinars at the airport.
He paid his monthly rent in Bahrain dinar as required by his landlord.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: fils - a subunit of currency used in several Arab countries including Bahrain

Etymology

From Bahrain (the country) + dinar, from Arabic dīnār, itself from Latin denarius.
