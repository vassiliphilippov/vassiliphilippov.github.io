air-to-surface

1. ADJ

/ ɛr tə ˈsɜːrfɪs / (AmE), / eə tə ˈsɜːfɪs / (BrE)
(of a weapon or missile) designed to be launched from aircraft at targets on the ground.
launched from aircraft at ground targets

Examples

The fighter jet was equipped with several air-to-surface missiles for the operation.
They tested a new air-to-surface weapon capable of targeting enemy installations.
The military invested heavily in developing air-to-surface technology during the conflict.

Synset Examples

air-to-surface missiles

Related Terms

  • Antonym: surface-to-air - (of a weapon or missile) launched from the ground at airborne targets

Etymology

Formed from 'air' and 'surface,' describing trajectory from the air to the ground; modelled on similar terms like 'surface-to-air.'
