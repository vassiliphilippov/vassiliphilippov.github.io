graylag goose

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡreɪˌlæɡ ɡus / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪˌlæɡ ɡuːs / (BrE)
a large, common grey wild goose native to Europe and western Asia, considered the ancestor of many domestic goose breeds
a common grey wild goose of Europe

Examples

Flocks of graylag goose can often be seen grazing near the wetlands in spring.
The graylag goose is thought to be the ancestor of most domesticated geese.
During migration, many graylag goose travel in characteristic V-shaped formations.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: goose - a large waterbird with a long neck, short legs, and webbed feet

Etymology

From 'grey' or 'gray' and 'lag' (late), referring to its late migration compared to other geese.
