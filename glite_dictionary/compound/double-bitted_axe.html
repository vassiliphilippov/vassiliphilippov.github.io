double-bitted axe

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʌb.əl ˈbɪt.ɪd æks / (AmE), / ˌdʌb.əl ˈbɪt.ɪd æks / (BrE)
An axe with cutting edges on both sides of the head.
axe with blades on both sides

Examples

The lumberjack preferred a double-bitted axe for its versatility when chopping and splitting wood.
She inherited a double-bitted axe from her grandfather, who used it for clearing forests.
Using a double-bitted axe allows the user to alternate between edges for improved efficiency.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: single-bitted axe - an axe with a cutting edge on one side only
