Klamath River

1. PROPN

/ ˈklæməθ ˈrɪvər / (AmE), / ˈklæməθ ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
a river in the western United States flowing southwest from southern Oregon through northern California to the Pacific Ocean
river in Oregon and northern California

Examples

Salmon spawning is a crucial part of the ecosystem along the Klamath River.
The historic Klamath River flows more than 250 miles before reaching the Pacific Ocean.
Conservation efforts have increased along the Klamath River in recent years.

Etymology

Named after the Klamath people, an Indigenous group of the region.
