business organization

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɪznɪs ˌɔːrɡənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈbɪznɪs ˌɔːɡənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
A company or other legal entity engaged in commercial, industrial, or professional activities, along with the people involved in it.
commercial or industrial company or entity

Examples

The tax laws changed the way a business organization could be structured for liability purposes.
She founded a successful business organization with more than 100 employees.
Choosing the right type of business organization is crucial for any entrepreneur.

Synset Examples

he bought his brother's business
a small mom-and-pop business
a racially integrated business concern

Related Terms

  • Synonym: company - a commercial business
  • Synonym: corporation - a large company or group authorized to act as a single entity
  • Synonym: partnership - a legal form of business operation between two or more individuals
