Ojibwa language

1. NOUN

/ oʊˈdʒɪbwə ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / əʊˈdʒɪbwə ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Algonquian language spoken by the Ojibwa people, native to areas in Canada and the northern United States
Algonquian language of the Ojibwa people

Examples

She is learning to speak Ojibwa language to connect with her heritage.
The Ojibwa language has a rich oral tradition that is still preserved today.
Linguists have documented the Ojibwa language to help keep it alive for future generations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ojibwe - the Ojibwa people, an indigenous group of North America

Etymology

From Ojibwa, the name of the indigenous people, plus 'language'.
