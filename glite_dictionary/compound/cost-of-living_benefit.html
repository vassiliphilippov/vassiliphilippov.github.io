cost-of-living benefit

1. NOUN

/ kɑst əv ˈlɪvɪŋ ˈbɛnɪfɪt / (AmE), / kɒst əv ˈlɪvɪŋ ˈbɛnɪfɪt / (BrE)
a benefit or payment that increases automatically in response to rising prices to help maintain purchasing power.
benefit that rises with price increases

Examples

The new contract includes a cost-of-living benefit to protect employees during periods of inflation.
Retirees welcomed the cost-of-living benefit that allows their pensions to keep pace with rising costs.
Some government programs offer a cost-of-living benefit that adjusts annually according to the consumer price index.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cost-of-living adjustment - an increase in income to match the rise in the cost of living
