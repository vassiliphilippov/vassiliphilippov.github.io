whitebark pine

1. NOUN

/ ˈwaɪtbɑrk ˌpaɪn / (AmE), / ˈwaɪtbɑːk ˌpaɪn / (BrE)
a small pine tree native to western North America, characterized by smooth gray-white bark and soft, brittle wood
a small western North American pine tree

Examples

The whitebark pine is often found growing at high elevations near the tree line.
Scientists are concerned about the decline of whitebark pine due to disease and climate change.
Hiking through the mountains, we saw many whitebark pine trees with their distinctive pale bark.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: limber pine - a similar pine species native to western North America
