dynamic electricity

1. NOUN

/ daɪˈnæmɪk ɪˌlɛkˈtrɪsɪti / (AmE), / daɪˈnæmɪk ɪˌlɛkˈtrɪsɪti / (BrE)
Electricity characterized by the movement of electric charge, typically as a current.
moving electric charge; electric current

Examples

The light bulb glows when dynamic electricity flows through its filament.
Young scientists learned the difference between static and dynamic electricity in class.
Batteries are common sources of dynamic electricity in portable devices.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: static electricity - electricity at rest; stationary electric charges
  • Synonym: electric current - a flow of electric charge in a conductor

Etymology

From Greek 'dynamis' (power, force) and 'electricity,' coined from 'elektron' (amber).
