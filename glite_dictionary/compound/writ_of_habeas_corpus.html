writ of habeas corpus

1. NOUN

/ rɪt əv ˌheɪ.bi.əs ˈkɔːr.pəs / (AmE), / rɪt əv ˌheɪ.bi.əs ˈkɔː.pəs / (BrE)
an official legal order requiring that a prisoner be brought before a court or judge, to determine if their detention is lawful
legal order to bring a prisoner before a judge
legal

Examples

The defense attorney filed a writ of habeas corpus to challenge the legality of his client's detention.
After several months in custody, she was released following a successful writ of habeas corpus.
A writ of habeas corpus was granted, requiring authorities to justify why the prisoner was being held.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: writ - a formal written order issued by a body with administrative or judicial jurisdiction
  • Synonym: subpoena - a writ ordering someone to attend a court

Etymology

From Latin 'habeas corpus' meaning 'you shall have the body'; a legal term in English law since the 14th century.
