kangaroo mouse

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæŋɡəˌruː maʊs / (AmE), / ˈkaŋɡəˌruː maʊs / (BrE)
a small, leaping rodent native to North American deserts, resembling a miniature kangaroo in its movements
a small leaping rodent of desert areas

Examples

The kangaroo mouse has adapted to survive in arid desert environments.
A kangaroo mouse can leap a surprising distance relative to its size.
Researchers observed several kangaroo mice foraging at night.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkæŋɡəˌruː maʊs / (AmE), / ˈkaŋɡəˌruː maʊs / (BrE)
A small, silky-haired rodent native to North America, related to but smaller than kangaroo rats, known for its external cheek pouches and long hind legs used for jumping.
small North American jumping rodent

Examples

The desert habitat is home to the rare kangaroo mouse and many other nocturnal creatures.
While hiking at night, we spotted a kangaroo mouse darting across the sand.
Unlike its larger cousin, the kangaroo mouse can survive long periods without drinking water.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: kangaroo rat - a larger related North American jumping rodent
