genus Ploceus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ploʊˈsiː.əs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs pləʊˈsiː.əs / (BrE)
the type genus of the family Ploceidae, comprising several species of weaver birds found in Africa and Asia
type genus of weaver birds (Ploceidae)

Examples

The genus Ploceus includes some of the most skilled nest-building birds in the world.
Birdwatchers were excited to observe a new species within the genus Ploceus during their trip to Kenya.
The intricate nests constructed by members of the genus Ploceus are a marvel of avian engineering.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weaverbird - a small bird noted for its intricately woven nest, member of the Ploceidae family

Etymology

From Latin 'genus' (kind, type) and New Latin 'Ploceus' (name given to the genus, based on Greek 'plokeus', meaning 'weaver').
