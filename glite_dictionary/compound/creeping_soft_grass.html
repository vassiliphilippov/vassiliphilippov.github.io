creeping soft grass

1. NOUN

/ ˈkripɪŋ sɑft ˌɡræs / (AmE), / ˈkriːpɪŋ sɒft ˌɡrɑːs / (BrE)
a European perennial grass with soft, velvety leaves, often found in meadows and woodlands
a European grass with velvety foliage

Examples

The meadow was dotted with creeping soft grass that felt like velvet underfoot.
Gardeners often plant creeping soft grass for its soft texture and ability to spread quickly.
After the rain, creeping soft grass thrived in the shaded parts of the woodland.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Holcus mollis - the scientific name for creeping soft grass
