Nentsi language

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɛntsi ˌlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈnɛntsi ˌlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
a Uralic language spoken by the Nenets people of northern Siberia
Uralic language of northern Siberia

Examples

Linguists have documented the phonology of the Nentsi language in several detailed studies.
The Nentsi language has survived despite pressures from dominant neighboring languages.
Few children today grow up speaking the Nentsi language at home, leading to concerns about its future.

Etymology

From Russian 'ненецкий язык' (Nenetskiy yazyk), referring to the language of the Nenets, an indigenous Samoyedic people.
