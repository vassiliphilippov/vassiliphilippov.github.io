Commonwealth of Australia

1. PROPN

/ ˈkɑːmənˌwɛlθ əv ɔːˈstreɪljə / (AmE), / ˈkɒmənwɛlθ əv ɔːˈstreɪliə / (BrE)
the official name of the country of Australia, occupying the entire Australian continent, established as a federation in 1901.
official name for Australia (country)

Examples

The Commonwealth of Australia was established as a federation in the early 20th century.
The Prime Minister represents the Commonwealth of Australia at international summits.
All states and territories are governed under the Commonwealth of Australia Constitution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Australia - country and continent in the Southern Hemisphere

Etymology

From 'commonwealth', meaning a political community, and 'Australia', derived from Latin 'australis' (southern).
