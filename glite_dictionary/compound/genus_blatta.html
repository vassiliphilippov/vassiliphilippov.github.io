genus Blatta

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˈblætə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈblætə / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Blattidae, comprising cockroaches that infest buildings worldwide.
type genus of cockroaches (Blattidae)

Examples

The genus Blatta includes several species of cockroaches commonly found in human dwellings.
Researchers studied the adaptability of insects in the genus Blatta to urban environments.
Infestations caused by members of the genus Blatta can be difficult to eradicate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cockroach - a nocturnal insect of the order Blattodea, typically infesting buildings

Etymology

From Latin 'blatta' (an insect, particularly a cockroach or moth).
