pleomorphic rhabdosarcoma

1. NOUN

/ pliː.oʊˈmɔːr.fɪk ræb.doʊˌsɑːrˈkoʊ.mə / (AmE), / pliː.əˈmɔː.fɪk ræb.dəʊˌsɑːˈkəʊ.mə / (BrE)
a rare type of rhabdomyosarcoma, affecting mainly the limb muscles of older adults, characterized by varied cell shapes
rare muscle cancer in older adults
medical

Examples

The oncologist explained that the tumor was a pleomorphic rhabdosarcoma, which required aggressive treatment.
Researchers are investigating new therapies for pleomorphic rhabdosarcoma in older patients.
Early detection of pleomorphic rhabdosarcoma can improve the chance of successful treatment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rhabdomyosarcoma - a malignant tumor of skeletal muscle origin
  • Degree Lower: sarcoma - a malignant tumor of connective or other non-epithelial tissue

Etymology

From Greek 'pleio-' (more/many) + 'morphē' (form) and 'rhabdo' (rod) + 'sarcoma' (fleshy tumor).
