varnish tree

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɑrnɪʃ tri / (AmE), / ˈvɑːnɪʃ triː / (BrE)
a small Asian tree that produces a poisonous sap used to make lacquer
Asian tree yielding lacquer sap

Examples

The sap of the varnish tree is carefully collected by skilled artisans in Asia.
Traditional Japanese lacquerware is made using resin from the varnish tree.
Contact with a varnish tree can cause severe skin irritation due to its toxic exudate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lacquer tree - another term for varnish tree, tree yielding lacquer-producing sap

2. NOUN

/ ˈvɑrnɪʃ tri / (AmE), / ˈvɑːnɪʃ triː / (BrE)
a large tree native to southeastern Asia, whose nuts yield an oil used in varnishes and whose kernels are sometimes used as candles
large Asian tree; source of varnish oil

Examples

The varnish tree produces nuts that are highly valued for their oil.
Local artisans extract oil from the varnish tree to make traditional paints and lacquers.
In some villages, the kernels of the varnish tree are strung together and used as candles.
