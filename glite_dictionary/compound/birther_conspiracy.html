birther conspiracy

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɜrθər kənˈspɪrəsi / (AmE), / ˈbɜːθə kənˈspɪrəsi / (BrE)
a political conspiracy theory alleging that a person, often a public figure, was not born in the country they claim, especially used in relation to the eligibility for public office
conspiracy theory about a person's birthplace
informal

Examples

Many people dismissed the birther conspiracy as unfounded and politically motivated.
The media covered the rise of the birther conspiracy and its impact on public opinion.
She debunked the birther conspiracy by presenting official documentation.

Synset Examples

The birther conspiracy continues to spark debate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conspiracy theory - a belief that some covert but influential organization is responsible for an event

Etymology

From 'birther' (one who questions someone's birth origin) + 'conspiracy'. 'Birther' emerged in the context of questioning U.S. President Barack Obama's birthplace.
