Turkestan Desert

1. NOUN

/ tɜrˌkɛstɑn ˈdɛzərt / (AmE), / tɜːˌkɛstæn ˈdɛzət / (BrE)
a desert located in Turkmenistan, south of the Aral Sea, known for its arid landscape
desert in Turkmenistan, south of Aral Sea

Examples

The Turkestan Desert is characterized by its vast stretches of sand and sparse vegetation.
Many migratory birds rest in oases scattered throughout the Turkestan Desert.
Environmental changes have further increased the aridity of the Turkestan Desert in recent decades.

Etymology

From 'Turkestan', a historical region of Central Asia, and 'desert', from Latin 'desertum' (a deserted place).
