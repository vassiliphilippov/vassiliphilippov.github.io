genus Haematopus

1. NOUN

/ ˌhiːməˈtoʊpəs / (AmE), / ˌhiːməˈtəʊpəs / (BrE)
A genus of wading birds known as oystercatchers, characterized by their strong bills used for opening shellfish.
genus of oystercatcher birds

Examples

The genus Haematopus includes all species of oystercatchers found worldwide.
Birds in the genus Haematopus are easily recognized by their bright orange bills.
Conservationists are studying the habitats of the genus Haematopus to protect declining populations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oystercatcher - a wading bird known for feeding on shellfish

Etymology

From Greek haima (blood) + pous (foot), referring to the birds' reddish legs.
