English ryegrass

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ ˈraɪˌɡræs / (AmE), / ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ ˈraɪɡrɑːs / (BrE)
a European perennial grass (Lolium perenne) often cultivated for pasture, hay, and lawns
a grass used for pasture, hay, and lawns

Examples

English ryegrass is widely sown by farmers to improve the quality of their grazing pastures.
The lawn was a vibrant green, thanks to the dense growth of English ryegrass.
Unlike some wild grasses, English ryegrass provides excellent fodder for cattle and sheep.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perennial ryegrass - a perennial ryegrass species, also Lolium perenne
