fourth ventricle

1. NOUN

/ fɔrθ ˈvɛntrɪkəl / (AmE), / fɔːθ ˈvɛntrɪkəl / (BrE)
a cavity in the brain located between the third ventricle and the central canal of the spinal cord, involved in the circulation of cerebrospinal fluid
cavity in the brain's ventricular system
medical

Examples

Researchers observed swelling near the fourth ventricle in the patient's MRI scan.
The cerebrospinal fluid flows through the fourth ventricle before entering the spinal canal.
Tumors in the fourth ventricle can cause severe neurological symptoms due to pressure on nearby structures.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: third ventricle - the ventricle of the brain situated between the two halves of the diencephalon
  • Degree Higher: central canal - a small canal that runs through the spinal cord, continuation of the ventricular system

Etymology

From Latin 'ventriculus' meaning 'little belly', used for chambers within organs.
