Northern Cross

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɔrðərn krɔs / (AmE), / ˈnɔːðən krɒs / (BrE)
a prominent group of five bright stars forming a cross shape in the constellation Cygnus
asterism of five stars in Cygnus

Examples

During the summer, the Northern Cross is clearly visible high in the night sky.
Many stargazers use the Northern Cross as a guide to finding the constellation Cygnus.
The bright star Deneb marks the top of the Northern Cross.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Southern Cross - an asterism of four bright stars forming a cross in the constellation Crux
