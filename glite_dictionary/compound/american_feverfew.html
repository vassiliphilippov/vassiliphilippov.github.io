American feverfew

1. NOUN

/ əˈmɛrɪkən ˈfiːvərˌfjuː / (AmE), / əˈmɛrɪkən ˈfiːvəˌfjuː / (BrE)
a stout perennial herb of eastern North America, bearing whitish flowers, whose leaves have been traditionally used by Catawba Indians to treat burns
a North American perennial herb with medicinal use

Examples

The American feverfew is valued in herbal medicine for its traditional use in treating burns.
Botanists studied the habitat preferences of American feverfew in southern Appalachia.
Many native remedies incorporated the leaves of American feverfew for their soothing properties.
