family Canidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæmɪli kəˈneɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli kaˈniːdiː / (BrE)
a family of carnivorous mammals that includes dogs, wolves, foxes, and jackals
group of mammals including dogs and foxes

Examples

The family Canidae includes both wild and domestic species such as wolves and dogs.
Foxes are a member of the family Canidae, sharing many traits with dogs.
Biologists study the evolutionary history of the family Canidae to understand canine diversity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: canine - relating to or resembling a dog or dogs

Etymology

From Latin 'Canidae', from 'canis' meaning 'dog'.
