pocket money

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑkɪt ˌmʌni / (AmE), / ˈpɒkɪt ˌmʌni / (BrE)
a small amount of money given regularly to a child by parents for minor personal expenses or spending as they wish
money for small personal expenses, esp. for children

Examples

Many parents give their children pocket money to teach them basic money management skills.
She saved her pocket money for several weeks to buy the new book she wanted.
As a child, my pocket money was just enough for a few sweets and a comic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: allowance - a sum of money given regularly, often for personal expenses

Etymology

From 'pocket' (meaning a small bag) and 'money'; refers to money kept in one's pocket for small expenses.
