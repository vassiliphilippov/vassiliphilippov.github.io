methamphetamine hydrochloride

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛθəmˌæfəˈmiːn haɪˌdrɒklaɪˈraɪd / (AmE), / ˌmɛθəmˌæfəˈmiːn hʌɪˌdrɒklʌɪˈraɪd / (BrE)
A synthetic stimulant drug, typically in the form of a crystalline hydrochloride, used medically to treat certain conditions but widely known for its potential for abuse.
stimulant drug; crystalline hydrochloride form
medical

Examples

The patient was prescribed methamphetamine hydrochloride to help manage attention deficit disorder.
Illegal production of methamphetamine hydrochloride has contributed to a rise in drug-related crimes.
Researchers are studying the effects of methamphetamine hydrochloride on the central nervous system.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: methamphetamine - a powerful, addictive stimulant drug

Etymology

From 'methamphetamine' (from methyl + amphetamine) and 'hydrochloride' (combination with hydrochloric acid).
