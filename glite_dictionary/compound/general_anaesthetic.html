general anaesthetic

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɛnərəl ænəsˈθɛtɪk / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛn(ə)rəl anaesˈθɛtɪk / (BrE)
A drug or agent that causes loss of consciousness and insensitivity to pain throughout the entire body, typically used during surgeries.
drug causing total body anaesthesia and unconsciousness
medical british

Examples

The patient was given a general anaesthetic before the surgery began.
She had never experienced a general anaesthetic and felt anxious about being unconscious.
Because of the complexity of the procedure, a general anaesthetic was necessary rather than a local one.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: local anaesthetic - drug that numbs sensation in a specific area of the body

Etymology

From 'general' (covering all or most parts) + 'anaesthetic', from Greek 'anaisthēsia' (insensibility).
