Saint Valentine's Day

1. NOUN

/ seɪnt ˈvælənˌtaɪnz deɪ / (AmE), / seɪnt ˈvaləntaɪnz deɪ / (BrE)
A holiday on February 14th celebrating love and affection, traditionally marked by the exchange of cards, gifts, and tokens of affection between partners, friends, or admirers.
holiday celebrating love and affection (Feb 14)

Examples

Every year on Saint Valentine's Day, couples exchange gifts and spend time together.
As a child, I enjoyed making cards for my classmates on Saint Valentine's Day.
Some people feel the pressure of expectations on Saint Valentine's Day, while others simply enjoy the occasion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Valentine - a card sent on Saint Valentine's Day

Etymology

Named after Saint Valentine, a Christian martyr, with the tradition dating back to the Middle Ages.
