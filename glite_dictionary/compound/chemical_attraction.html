chemical attraction

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɛmɪkəl əˈtrækʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɛmɪkəl əˈtrækʃən / (BrE)
the force that attracts atoms to each other and binds them together in a molecule
force binding atoms in a molecule

Examples

The chemical attraction between hydrogen and oxygen forms water molecules.
Understanding chemical attraction is fundamental in the study of chemistry.
The fabric dye relies on chemical attraction to adhere to natural fibers.

Synset Examples

basic dyes have an affinity for wool and silk

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chemical bond - a lasting attraction between atoms that enables the formation of chemical compounds
