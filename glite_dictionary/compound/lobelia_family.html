lobelia family

1. NOUN

/ loʊˈbiːliə ˈfæməli / (AmE), / ləʊˈbiːliə ˈfæmɪli / (BrE)
a family of flowering plants, sometimes considered a separate group or included within the Campanulaceae, comprising herbaceous plants with milky sap and often showy flowers
a family of flowering plants, sometimes called Campanulaceae

Examples

The lobelia family includes many popular garden plants known for their bright blue flowers.
Some taxonomists place the lobelia family within the broader Campanulaceae family.
Species in the lobelia family are often used in horticulture for their decorative blooms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Campanulaceae - the bellflower family; a family of flowering plants closely related to the lobelia family

Etymology

From the genus name Lobelia, named after Flemish botanist Matthias de Lobel (1538–1616).
