Petrarchan sonnet

1. NOUN

/ pəˈtrɑrkən ˈsʌnɪt / (AmE), / pɛˈtrɑːkən ˈsʌnɪt / (BrE)
A sonnet consisting of an octave with the rhyme scheme abbaabba, followed by a sestet with the rhyme scheme cdecde or cdcdcd.
sonnet form with specific rhyme scheme

Examples

Her latest poem is a Petrarchan sonnet exploring the theme of unrequited love.
Many students struggle to master the strict rhyme scheme of the Petrarchan sonnet.
The Petrarchan sonnet is distinguished by its clear division between octave and sestet.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Shakespearean sonnet - An English sonnet form with a different rhyme scheme (abab cdcd efef gg)
  • Degree Higher: sonnet - A poem of 14 lines using any of a number of formal rhyme schemes

Etymology

Named after the Italian poet Petrarch (Francesco Petrarca, 1304–1374), who popularized the form.
