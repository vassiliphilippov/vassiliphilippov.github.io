air-breathing

1. ADJ

/ ɛr ˈbriːðɪŋ / (AmE), / eə ˈbriːðɪŋ / (BrE)
(of an animal or machine) deriving oxygen from the air, as opposed to from water or another medium.
deriving oxygen from the air

Examples

Some dinosaurs were air-breathing, unlike modern fish that extract oxygen from water.
The invention of air-breathing engines revolutionized aircraft design.
Whales are air-breathing mammals that must surface regularly to breathe.

Synset Examples

he studied respiration in marine air-breathing vertebrates
large air-breathing ichthyosaurs had hydrofoils

Related Terms

  • Antonym: aquatic - living or operating in water
