Japanese stranglehold

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˈpæniz ˈstræŋˌhoʊld / (AmE), / dʒəˈpæniːz ˈstræŋɡˌhəʊld / (BrE)
A wrestling hold in which an opponent's arms are crossed in front of their own neck to exert pressure on their windpipe.
wrestling hold crossing opponent's arms before neck

Examples

The wrestler secured a Japanese stranglehold that brought his opponent to the mat.
She escaped from the Japanese stranglehold with a swift counter move.
Mastering the Japanese stranglehold requires both strength and precision.
