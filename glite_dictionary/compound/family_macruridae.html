family Macruridae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæmɪli ˌməˈrʊrɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli ˌməˈrʊərɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of deep-sea fishes known as grenadiers, characterized by elongated bodies and tapering tails.
a family of deep-sea fishes (grenadiers)

Examples

The family Macruridae includes many species of deep-sea grenadiers found in most oceans.
Researchers studied the distribution of family Macruridae in the North Atlantic.
Most members of the family Macruridae have large heads and slender, tapering tails.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grenadier - a type of deep-sea fish with a long, tapering tail

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek makros (long) + oura (tail), referencing the fishes’ long tails.
