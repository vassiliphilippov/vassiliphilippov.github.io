rezoning fee

1. NOUN

/ riˈzoʊ.nɪŋ fiː / (AmE), / riːˈzəʊ.nɪŋ fiː / (BrE)
a one-time fee charged, typically by a local authority or county, when a property owner requests to change the zoning classification of their property.
fee for property rezoning request

Examples

The county treasurer confirmed that the rezoning fee must be paid before the application is processed.
Many developers factor the rezoning fee into their overall project budget.
After learning about the high rezoning fee, she reconsidered altering her property's zoning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: application fee - fee paid when applying for a service or process
