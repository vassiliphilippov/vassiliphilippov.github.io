to totally forgot

1. VERB

/ ˈtoʊtəli fərˈɡɑt / (AmE), / ˈtəʊtəli fəˈɡɒt / (BrE)
Completely failed to remember something; did not remember at all.
completely failed to remember
informal

Examples

I totally forgot to bring my wallet to the store today.
She totally forgot his birthday, even though she'd marked it on her calendar.
Sorry I missed your call—I totally forgot to charge my phone last night.

Synset Examples

He totally forgot about our meeting yesterday.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forgot - failed to remember
  • Antonym: remembered - did not fail to recall
