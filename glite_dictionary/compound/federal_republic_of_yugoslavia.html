Federal Republic of Yugoslavia

1. PROPN

/ ˈfɛdərəl rɪˈpʌblɪk əv ˌjuːɡəˈslɑːviə / (AmE), / ˈfɛdərəl rɪˈpʌblɪk əv ˌjuːɡəˈslɑːvɪə / (BrE)
the official name used from 1992 to 2003 for the union of Serbia and Montenegro, a Balkan state in southeastern Europe
former Balkan state, Serbia and Montenegro, 1992–2003

Examples

The Federal Republic of Yugoslavia was formed after the breakup of the larger Socialist Federal Republic of Yugoslavia.
In 2003, the Federal Republic of Yugoslavia officially became the Union of Serbia and Montenegro.
Sanctions were imposed on the Federal Republic of Yugoslavia by the UN during the conflicts of the 1990s.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Union of Serbia and Montenegro - the name adopted by the same state after 2003, until 2006

Etymology

Formed from English 'federal', 'republic', and 'Yugoslavia', from the Serbo-Croatian 'Jugoslavija' (land of the southern Slavs).
