spick-and-span

1. ADJ

/ spɪk ən spæn / (AmE), / spɪk ən spæn / (BrE)
extremely clean, neat, and tidy; spotless.
completely neat and clean

Examples

She kept her house spick-and-span for guests at all times.
After a weekend of cleaning, the classroom looked spick-and-span.
His desk is always spick-and-span, with every item in its place.

Synset Examples

the apartment was immaculate
in her immaculate white uniform
a spick-and-span kitchen
their spic red-visored caps

Related Terms

  • Synonym: immaculate - perfectly clean, free from dirt or stain
  • Synonym: spotless - completely clean, with no dirt or stains
  • Antonym: dirty - covered or marked with an unclean substance

Etymology

From Middle English 'spikke' (a spike, nail) and 'span-new' (brand new), originally meaning 'as new as a newly-made nail.'
