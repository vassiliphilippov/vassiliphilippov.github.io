stinking bean trefoil

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɪŋkɪŋ biːn trɪˈfɔɪl / (AmE), / ˈstɪŋkɪŋ biːn trɪˈfɔɪl / (BrE)
a shrub with trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers, producing curved seed pods; its leaves emit a foul smell when crushed
shrub with foul-smelling leaves and yellow flowers

Examples

The stinking bean trefoil is native to marshy areas and can be identified by its distinctive odor.
Gardeners often avoid planting stinking bean trefoil because of the unpleasant smell released by its leaves.
When crushed, the leaves of stinking bean trefoil emit a strong, foul scent.
