carrying charge

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæriɪŋ tʃɑrdʒ / (AmE), / ˈkæriɪŋ tʃɑːdʒ / (BrE)
a fee charged for maintaining an account balance or for merchandise purchased on an installment plan
fee for maintaining an account or installment purchase

Examples

The store offered easy credit but applied a carrying charge to the unpaid balance each month.
Banks often impose a carrying charge when customers carry forward a credit card balance.
If you buy furniture on an installment plan, watch for any carrying charges that may apply.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interest - money paid regularly at a particular rate for the use of money lent

2. NOUN

/ ˈkæriɪŋ tʃɑrdʒ / (AmE), / ˈkæriɪŋ tʃɑːdʒ / (BrE)
the cost or expense of holding an asset or inventory over a period, often including interest, storage, insurance, and opportunity costs
cost of holding an asset or inventory

Examples

The warehouse company added a substantial carrying charge to account for insurance and storage fees.
Retailers must consider the carrying charge when holding unsold seasonal merchandise over to the next year.
Long-term inventory can increase your overall carrying charge and reduce profits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carrying cost - another term for carrying charge, often used interchangeably in finance and economics
