Byzantine rule

1. NOUN

/ baɪˈzæntin ruːl / (AmE), / bɪˈzæntaɪn ruːl / (BrE)
a set of rules or procedures that are extremely complicated, intricate, or involved, often to the point of being difficult to follow or understand
complex, intricate rules or procedures

Examples

Navigating the Byzantine rule of the tax system can intimidate even experienced accountants.
The university’s Byzantine rule for course registration left many students frustrated.
She managed to decipher the Byzantine rule that governed employee benefits in the company.

Synset Examples

He explained the Byzantine rules of the game in detail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: convoluted - (of an argument, story, etc.) extremely complex and difficult to follow
  • Antonym: simple rule - a rule that is straightforward and easy to understand or follow

Etymology

From 'Byzantine', referring to the bureaucracy of the Byzantine Empire, known for its complex and convoluted administrative systems.
