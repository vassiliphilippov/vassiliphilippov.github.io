sulphanilic acid

1. NOUN

/ sʌlfəˈnɪlɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / sʌl.fəˈnɪl.ɪk ˈæs.ɪd / (BrE)
a white crystalline acid, derived from aniline, used chiefly in making dyes and as a reagent
a crystalline acid used in dyes and tests

Examples

The laboratory technician prepared a solution containing sulphanilic acid for the dye synthesis experiment.
Sulphanilic acid is commonly used as a standard in analytical chemistry tests.
The presence of sulphanilic acid can be detected by its reaction with nitrites.

Etymology

From 'sulfanilic', combining 'sulfo-' (relating to sulfur) and 'aniline'.
