pyx chest

1. NOUN

/ pɪks tʃɛst / (AmE), / pɪks tʃɛst / (BrE)
a chest used to hold coins from the mint before they are tested for quality in an assay
chest for coins awaiting assay

Examples

The gold coins were locked in the pyx chest until the official assay took place.
Medieval records describe guards stationed by the pyx chest at the royal mint.
After minting, new sovereigns were sealed in the pyx chest pending inspection by the assayers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pyx - small box or container used in assay or religious context

Etymology

From Latin 'pyxis' (box), via Old French and Medieval Latin.
