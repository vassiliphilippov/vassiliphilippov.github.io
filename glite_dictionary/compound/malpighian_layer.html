Malpighian layer

1. NOUN

/ mælˈpɪɡiən ˈleɪər / (AmE), / malˈpɪɡiən ˈleɪə / (BrE)
the innermost living layer of the epidermis, consisting of cells that divide to form new skin cells
innermost living layer of the skin's epidermis
medical

Examples

The Malpighian layer is responsible for the continual regeneration of the skin.
Damage to the Malpighian layer can affect the skin's ability to heal from wounds.
The cells in the Malpighian layer are constantly dividing to produce new skin cells.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stratum basale - the basal layer of the epidermis, synonym for Malpighian layer

Etymology

Named after Marcello Malpighi, Italian anatomist (1628–1694), who studied the structure of skin.
