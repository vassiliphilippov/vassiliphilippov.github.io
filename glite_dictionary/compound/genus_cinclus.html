genus Cinclus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˈsɪŋkləs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈsɪŋkləs / (BrE)
The type genus of the bird family Cinclidae, which includes the dippers.
type genus of the Cinclidae (dippers)

Examples

The genus Cinclus is known for its aquatic habits and ability to dive underwater.
All five extant species within the genus Cinclus are commonly called dippers.
Researchers studied the genus Cinclus to understand the adaptations of birds to fast-flowing streams.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cinclidae - family of birds known as dippers
  • Synonym: dipper - a small aquatic songbird of the genus Cinclus

Etymology

From Latin 'cinclus', meaning a water ouzel or dipper.
