erythema nodosum leprosum

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛrɪˈθimə noʊˈdoʊsəm lɛˈproʊsəm / (AmE), / ˌɛrɪˈθiːmə nəʊˈdəʊsəm lɛˈprəʊsəm / (BrE)
an inflammatory complication of leprosy, characterized by painful skin lesions on the arms, legs, and face
leprosy complication with painful skin lesions
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed the patient with erythema nodosum leprosum after noticing large, painful nodules on her legs.
Treatment for erythema nodosum leprosum often involves corticosteroids to reduce inflammation.
Patients with erythema nodosum leprosum may experience recurring episodes of skin lesions during their illness.

Etymology

From Latin: 'erythema' (redness), 'nodosum' (knotty), and 'leprosum' (relating to leprosy).
