genus Jynx

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈdʒɪŋks / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈdʒɪŋks / (BrE)
A genus of small Old World woodpeckers known as wrynecks, characterized by their twisting neck movements.
genus of birds called wrynecks

Examples

The genus Jynx consists of birds commonly known as wrynecks, found across Europe, Asia, and Africa.
Biologists are studying the unique defense mechanisms of the genus Jynx in their natural habitats.
Unlike other woodpeckers, the genus Jynx lacks the ability to excavate wood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wryneck - a small bird of the genus Jynx with a twisting neck

Etymology

Genus name from Greek iynx, meaning 'wryneck'.
