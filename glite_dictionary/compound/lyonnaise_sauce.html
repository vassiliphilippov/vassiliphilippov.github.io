Lyonnaise sauce

1. NOUN

/ liˌoʊˈneɪz sɔs / (AmE), / liˌɒnˈeɪz sɔːs / (BrE)
a brown sauce made with sautéed chopped onions, parsley, and dry white wine or vinegar, typically served with meats or vegetables
brown sauce with onions and wine or vinegar

Examples

The chef topped the steak with Lyonnaise sauce to enhance its flavor.
A traditional Lyonnaise sauce is often paired with roasted chicken or beef.
You can make Lyonnaise sauce at home using onions, white wine, and fresh parsley.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: sauce - a liquid or semi-liquid substance served with food to add flavor

Etymology

French, from Lyonnaise meaning 'in the style of Lyon', named after Lyon, France.
