cargo liner

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑrɡoʊ ˈlaɪnər / (AmE), / ˈkɑːɡəʊ ˈlaɪnə / (BrE)
a large ship designed to carry cargo on a regular schedule along established routes
ship that regularly carries cargo

Examples

The port was busy as a cargo liner arrived to unload its shipments from overseas.
She spent her early career working on a cargo liner that sailed between Europe and South America.
The company expanded its fleet by acquiring a new cargo liner last year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: freighter - a ship that carries goods rather than passengers
  • Antonym: passenger liner - a liner that carries passengers on a regular schedule
