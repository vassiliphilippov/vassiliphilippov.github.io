Petasites vulgaris

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛtəˈsaɪtiːz vʌlˈgɛərɪs / (AmE), / ˌpiːtəˈsaɪtiːz vʌlˈɡɑːrɪs / (BrE)
A small Eurasian herb with broad leaves and lilac-pink, rayless flowers, typically found in moist areas.
a Eurasian herb with broad leaves

Examples

The broad leaves of Petasites vulgaris provide excellent ground cover in damp woodland areas.
Many gardeners plant Petasites vulgaris for its ability to thrive in moist, shaded gardens.
The lilac-pink flowers of Petasites vulgaris appear early in spring before the leaves fully emerge.

Etymology

From Latin, genus 'Petasites' (butterbur) and species 'vulgaris' (common).
