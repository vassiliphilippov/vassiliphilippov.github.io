big marigold

1. NOUN

/ bɪɡ ˈmærɪˌɡoʊld / (AmE), / bɪɡ ˈmærɪɡəʊld / (BrE)
A stout, branching annual plant native to Mexico and Central America, with large yellow to orange flower heads; commonly grown as an ornamental flower.
tall marigold with large yellow-orange flowers

Examples

She planted big marigold in her garden to add bright color throughout the summer.
A vibrant big marigold was the focal point of the floral arrangement.
The festival paraded streets lined with rows of big marigold blossoms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: African marigold - another common name for Tagetes erecta
  • Synonym: Tagetes erecta - the botanical name for big marigold
  • Degree Lower: French marigold - a related species with smaller flowers
