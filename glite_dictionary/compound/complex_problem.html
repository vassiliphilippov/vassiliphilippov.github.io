complex problem

1. NOUN

/ kəmˈplɛks ˈprɑbləm / (AmE), / kəmˈplɛks ˈprɒbləm / (BrE)
a problem that is difficult to analyze or solve because it involves many interconnected factors or variables
a difficult or multifaceted problem

Examples

She thrives on tackling complex problems at work, especially those requiring innovative solutions.
Climate change is a complex problem that demands cooperation across nations and disciplines.
Engineers spent months trying to find a fix for the complex problem in the software system.

Synset Examples

As a sysprog, she enjoys solving complex problems.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: simple problem - a problem that is easy to understand and solve
  • Synonym: difficult problem - a problem that is hard to solve
