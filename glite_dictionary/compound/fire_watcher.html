fire watcher

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪɚ ˈwɑtʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈfaɪə ˈwɒtʃə / (BrE)
(historical) A person whose job was to watch for and report fires caused by aerial bombing, especially during World War II in Britain.
person who watched for fires from air raids (WWII Britain)
british

Examples

During the Blitz, my grandfather served as a fire watcher on the rooftops of London.
Every night, volunteer fire watchers scanned the skies for incendiary bombs.
The role of a fire watcher was vital in minimizing the destruction caused by air raids.
