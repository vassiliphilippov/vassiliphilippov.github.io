laminar flow

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæmɪnər floʊ / (AmE), / ˈlæmɪnə fləʊ / (BrE)
the movement of a fluid in smooth parallel layers, with little or no mixing between them, as opposed to turbulent flow
flow of a fluid in smooth, parallel layers

Examples

The engineers designed the pipe to maintain laminar flow and reduce energy loss.
When water moves slowly through a narrow tube, it typically exhibits laminar flow.
Blood travels through small vessels in a laminar flow, ensuring efficient circulation.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: turbulent flow - fluid flow characterized by chaotic, irregular motion

Etymology

From Latin 'lamina' (thin plate or layer) + 'flow'.
