computer programme

1. NOUN

/ kəmˈpjuːtər ˈproʊɡræm / (AmE), / kəmˈpjuːtə ˈprəʊɡræm / (BrE)
a sequence of instructions written to be executed by a computer in order to perform a specific task or operation
sequence of executable instructions for a computer
british

Examples

The new computer programme helps doctors manage patient records efficiently.
She spent months writing a computer programme for her thesis project.
A simple computer programme can automate repetitive tasks in the office.

Synset Examples

the program required several hundred lines of code

Related Terms

  • Synonym: computer program - a sequence of executable instructions for a computer (American spelling)

Etymology

programme from French programme, from Greek programma (proclamation, edict); computer from Latin computare (to calculate).
