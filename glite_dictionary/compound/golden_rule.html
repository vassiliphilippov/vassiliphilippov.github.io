golden rule

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡoʊldən ˌruːl / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊldən ˌruːl / (BrE)
a basic principle that should always be followed in a particular situation or activity
basic guiding principle

Examples

The golden rule of writing is to know your audience.
Her golden rule for friendships is always to be honest.
One golden rule in baking is to measure your ingredients carefully.

Synset Examples

the golden rule of teaching is to be clear

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡoʊldən ruːl / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊldən ruːl / (BrE)
the principle that one should treat others as one would like others to treat oneself; often formulated as 'do unto others as you would have them do unto you'
ethical principle of treating others as oneself

Examples

Many religions and philosophies teach the golden rule as a guide to ethical behavior.
Applying the golden rule can help create a more respectful workplace environment.
Parents often emphasize the golden rule when teaching children about kindness and fairness.

Synset Examples

The golden rule is to treat others as you wish.
He always follows the golden rule in business.
Her life motto is based on the golden rule.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ethic of reciprocity - the principle of treating others as one is treated, another name for the golden rule
