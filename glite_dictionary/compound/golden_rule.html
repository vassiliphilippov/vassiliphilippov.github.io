Golden Rule

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡoʊldən ruːl / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊldən ruːl / (BrE)
the principle that you should treat other people as you would like them to treat you, based on Jesus' teaching in the Sermon on the Mount
ethical principle of treating others as oneself

Examples

Many parents teach their children to follow the Golden Rule from an early age.
In many cultures, the Golden Rule is considered a foundation of ethical behavior.
She reminded him of the Golden Rule when he was being unkind to his classmates.

Synset Examples

Whatsoever ye would that men do unto you, do you even so unto them

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ethic of reciprocity - the general moral concept of mutual treatment
