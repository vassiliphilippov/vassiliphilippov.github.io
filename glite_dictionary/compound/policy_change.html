policy change

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑləsi tʃeɪndʒ / (AmE), / ˈpɒləsi tʃeɪndʒ / (BrE)
a significant modification or shift in the principles, direction, or approach of an organization, government, or individual regarding policy matters
a significant shift in policy direction or approach

Examples

The new administration promised a policy change to address climate change more aggressively.
After years of opposition, the company finally announced a policy change in support of flexible working hours.
The country underwent a dramatic policy change following the election results.

Synset Examples

an about-face on foreign policy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reversal - a complete change in direction or decision
  • Antonym: continuity - the unbroken and consistent existence of something
