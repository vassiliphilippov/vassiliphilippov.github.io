chalk line

1. NOUN

/ tʃɑk laɪn / (AmE), / tʃɔːk laɪn / (BrE)
A string coated with chalk used in construction or carpentry to mark a straight line on a surface.
string for marking straight lines

Examples

The carpenter stretched a chalk line across the wall to mark where the shelves would go.
Before cutting the drywall, she snapped a chalk line to ensure a straight edge.
It's important to use a chalk line when installing tiles to keep everything aligned.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snap line - tool that marks straight lines by snapping a chalked string
