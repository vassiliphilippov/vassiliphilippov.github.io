Keystone State

1. PROPN

/ ˈkiːstoʊn steɪt / (AmE), / ˈkiːstəʊn steɪt / (BrE)
A nickname for the state of Pennsylvania in the United States, known for its central role in the founding of the nation.
nickname for Pennsylvania, USA

Examples

Pennsylvania is often called the Keystone State because of its pivotal role in American history.
The Keystone State boasts both vibrant cities and scenic rural landscapes.
Tourists flock to the Keystone State to visit historical sites such as Gettysburg.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Pennsylvania - A state in the northeastern United States, also called the Keystone State

Etymology

Refers to Pennsylvania's central ('keystone') position among the original 13 colonies.
