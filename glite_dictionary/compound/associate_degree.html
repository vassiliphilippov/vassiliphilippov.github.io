associate degree

1. NOUN

/ əˈsoʊsiˌeɪt dɪˈɡri / (AmE), / əˈsəʊsiət dɪˈɡriː / (BrE)
A degree awarded by a college or university upon completion of a course of study typically lasting two years, below the level of a bachelor's degree.
two-year academic degree

Examples

After completing her associate degree, she transferred to a university to pursue her bachelor's.
Many community colleges offer an associate degree in nursing or business administration.
Earning an associate degree can be a cost-effective way to start higher education.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: bachelor's degree - an academic degree awarded for a course of study that generally lasts three to four years
