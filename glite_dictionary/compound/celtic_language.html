Celtic language

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɛltɪk ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈkɛltɪk ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
A branch of the Indo-European language family once widespread in Europe before the Christian era, now represented by languages such as Irish, Welsh, and Breton.
branch of Indo-European languages

Examples

Scholars have traced the spread of the Celtic language across much of ancient Europe.
Welsh is an example of a living Celtic language still spoken today.
Many place names in France and Britain have roots in the Celtic language.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Indo-European language - A family of languages including most languages of Europe and parts of Asia
  • Synonym: Irish - A living Celtic language spoken in Ireland
  • Synonym: Welsh - A living Celtic language spoken in Wales

Etymology

From Latin 'Celtae' (the Celts) and 'language'.
