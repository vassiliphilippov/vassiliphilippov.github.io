bezoar goat

1. NOUN

/ ˈbiː.zɔr ɡoʊt / (AmE), / ˈbiː.zɔː ɡəʊt / (BrE)
a wild goat native to Iran and neighboring regions, recognized by its large horns and regarded as an ancestor of domestic goats
wild goat of Iran and adjacent areas

Examples

The bezoar goat is considered one of the primary ancestors of modern domestic goats.
Conservationists are working to protect the dwindling population of the bezoar goat in western Asia.
A magnificent pair of horns was found on an old bezoar goat living in the rocky mountains.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wild goat - a species of goat living in mountainous regions, ancestor of domesticated goats
  • Degree Lower: domestic goat - the common domesticated goat descended from wild goats

Etymology

Bezoar from Persian 'pād-zahr' meaning 'antidote', in reference to bezoar stones sometimes found in these goats.
