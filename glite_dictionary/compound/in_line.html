in line

1. ADJ

/ ɪn laɪn / (AmE), / ɪn laɪn / (BrE)
having a place in a sequence of succession or entitlement, especially for a position or reward
next in succession or entitlement

Examples

After the manager retires, Sarah is in line for the promotion.
Several candidates are in line to inherit the estate, but he is the favorite.
He was in line to become chairman until the board selected someone else.

Synset Examples

he was in line for the presidency

Related Terms

  • Synonym: next in line - following directly after someone else in a sequence of succession
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00129209-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADV

/ ɪn laɪn / (AmE), / ɪn laɪn / (BrE)
waiting one after another in a line or queue; in an orderly sequence
in a line or queue
american

Examples

We had to stand in line for an hour before entering the museum.
Children waited in line to buy ice cream from the truck.
At the airport, everyone formed in line to go through security.

Synset Examples

they waited in line for the tickets

Related Terms

  • Synonym: in a queue - waiting in a line of people or vehicles
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00166891-r
Synset POS: r
