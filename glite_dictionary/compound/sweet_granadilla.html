sweet granadilla

1. NOUN

/ swit ɡrænəˈdɪlə / (AmE), / swiːt ɡrænəˈdɪlə / (BrE)
a tropical fruit of the passionflower family (Passiflora ligularis), noted for its sweet, aromatic pulp; considered one of the best-tasting passionfruits
tropical fruit, Passiflora ligularis

Examples

The sweet granadilla is prized for its delicate flavor and juicy pulp.
Farmers in South America often export sweet granadillas to international markets.
She topped her dessert with slices of sweet granadilla for added sweetness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: passion fruit - fruit of several species in the Passiflora genus

Etymology

From Spanish 'granadilla' (little pomegranate), referring to the fruit's appearance.
