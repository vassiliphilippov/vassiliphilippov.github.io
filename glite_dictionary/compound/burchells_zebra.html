Burchell's zebra

1. NOUN

/ bɜrˈtʃɛlz ˈziːbrə / (AmE), / ˈbɜːtʃəlz ˈziːbrə / (BrE)
a subspecies of the plains zebra native to central and eastern Africa, characterized by its broad stripes and shadow stripes
subspecies of plains zebra in Africa

Examples

The Burchell's zebra is commonly found grazing across the savannas of southern Africa.
Researchers studied the migration patterns of Burchell's zebra during the dry season.
Unlike the mountain zebra, Burchell's zebra has less distinct striping on its legs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zebra - an African wild horse with black-and-white stripes

Etymology

Named after William John Burchell (1781–1863), English explorer and naturalist.
