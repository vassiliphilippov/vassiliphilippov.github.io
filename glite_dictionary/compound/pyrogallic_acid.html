pyrogallic acid

1. NOUN

/ ˌpaɪroʊˈɡælɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / ˌpʌɪrəˈɡalɪk ˈasɪd / (BrE)
a toxic white crystalline phenol used in the treatment of certain skin diseases and as a photographic developer
a toxic crystalline phenol chemical
medical

Examples

The dermatologist prescribed a mild concentration of pyrogallic acid cream for the patient’s psoriasis.
Early photographers often used pyrogallic acid as a developing agent for images.
Because of its toxicity, pyrogallic acid must be handled with care in laboratory settings.

Etymology

From 'pyro-' (fire) and 'gallic acid', referring to its production from gallic acid by heat.
