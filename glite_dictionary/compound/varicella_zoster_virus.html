varicella zoster virus

1. NOUN

/ ˌvær.əˈsɛl.ə ˈzɑs.tɚ ˈvaɪ.rəs / (AmE), / ˌvær.ɪˈsel.ə ˈzɒs.tə ˈvaɪ.rəs / (BrE)
a virus of the herpes family that causes chickenpox and shingles in humans
virus causing chickenpox and shingles
medical

Examples

The varicella zoster virus is best known for causing chickenpox in children.
After recovering from chickenpox, the varicella zoster virus can remain dormant in nerve tissue.
Doctors can diagnose shingles by identifying the presence of varicella zoster virus in a patient.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: herpesvirus - any virus belonging to the family Herpesviridae
  • Synonym: chickenpox - an infectious disease caused by the varicella zoster virus
  • Synonym: shingles - a reactivation disease caused by varicella zoster virus

Etymology

From Latin 'varicella' (chickenpox) and 'zoster' (belt, referring to shingles).
