elephant tree

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛl.ə.fənt triː / (AmE), / ˈɛl.ɪ.fənt triː / (BrE)
a small tree or shrub native to the southwestern United States, noted for its spicy odor, odd-pinnate leaves, and clusters of small white flowers.
aromatic shrub or tree of the southwestern U.S.

Examples

The elephant tree thrives in the arid regions of southern Arizona and northern Mexico.
We spotted an elephant tree along the rocky hillside during our hike.
The distinctive scent of the elephant tree made it easy to identify.
