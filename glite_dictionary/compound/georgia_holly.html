Georgia holly

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɔrdʒə ˈhɑli / (AmE), / ˈdʒɔːdʒə ˈhɒli / (BrE)
A North American holly shrub (Ilex longipes) native to the southeastern United States, particularly Georgia.
a North American holly shrub

Examples

The Georgia holly is prized for its bright red berries and glossy leaves.
We found a rare Georgia holly growing along the forest trail.
Many gardens in the South feature the Georgia holly for year-round greenery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: holly - evergreen shrub or small tree of the genus Ilex

Etymology

From Georgia (U.S. state), where the species is prevalent, and holly, the common name for the genus Ilex.
