latent hostility

1. NOUN

/ ˈleɪtənt hɑːˈstɪlɪti / (AmE), / ˈleɪtənt hɒˈstɪlɪti / (BrE)
Hostility or antagonism that exists but is not openly shown or expressed.
hidden or unexpressed hostility

Examples

He sensed a latent hostility in their polite conversation.
Despite their friendly exchanges, latent hostility simmered beneath the surface.
The merger talks were complicated by latent hostility between the two companies.

Synset Examples

he could sense her latent hostility to him
the diplomats' first concern was to reduce international tensions

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: hostility - strong dislike or antagonism; the state of being hostile

Etymology

From Latin 'latens' (lying hidden) + hostility.
