genus Calocarpum

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌkæ.ləˈkɑːr.pəm / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌkæ.ləˈkɑː.pəm / (BrE)
A genus of tropical American trees in the family Sapotaceae.
genus of tropical American trees

Examples

The genus Calocarpum contains several species of fruit-bearing trees found in Central and South America.
Botanists conducted a survey of the genus Calocarpum in the Amazon rainforest.
Members of the genus Calocarpum are valued for their edible fruit and timber.

Etymology

From Latin 'genus' (kind, type) and Neo-Latin 'Calocarpum' from Greek 'kalos' (beautiful) + 'karpos' (fruit).
