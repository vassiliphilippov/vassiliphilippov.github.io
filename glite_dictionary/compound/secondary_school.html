secondary school

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛkəndɛri skuːl / (AmE), / ˈsɛkəndəri skuːl / (BrE)
A school for students aged between about 11 and 18, intermediate between elementary school and college, typically including grades 9 to 12.
school between elementary and college

Examples

After graduating from secondary school, she decided to attend university.
Teachers at my secondary school encouraged students to think independently.
Many students participate in sports teams during their years at secondary school.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: high school - school for students typically aged 14 to 18 (grades 9-12)

Etymology

From Latin 'secundarius' (of the second rank) and 'school', indicating the stage following primary education.
