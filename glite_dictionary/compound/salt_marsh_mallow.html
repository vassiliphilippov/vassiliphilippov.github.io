salt marsh mallow

1. NOUN

/ sɔlt mɑrʃ ˈmæloʊ / (AmE), / sɒlt mɑːʃ ˈmæləʊ / (BrE)
a perennial plant native to the southeastern United States to New York, typically found in salt marshes and wetlands, with pink or white flowers.
wetland plant of eastern U.S.

Examples

The salt marsh mallow blooms along the coastal wetlands each summer.
Scientists studied the adaptation of the salt marsh mallow to saline environments.
We spotted a patch of salt marsh mallows near the edge of the estuary.
