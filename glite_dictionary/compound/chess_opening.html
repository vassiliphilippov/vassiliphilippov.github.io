chess opening

1. NOUN

/ tʃɛs ˈoʊpənɪŋ / (AmE), / tʃɛs ˈəʊpənɪŋ / (BrE)
a recognized sequence of moves played at the beginning of a game of chess, usually with established names and strategies
standard sequence of opening chess moves

Examples

He spent years studying the chess opening to improve his game.
The Sicilian Defense is one of the most popular chess openings among grandmasters.
Learning a new chess opening can give players a strategic advantage early in the match.

Synset Examples

he memorized all the important chess openings

Related Terms

  • Synonym: opening - the initial moves of a chess game
