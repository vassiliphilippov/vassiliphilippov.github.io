biauriculate heart

1. NOUN

/ baɪ.ɔːˈrɪk.jə.lət hɑrt / (AmE), / baɪ.ɔːˈrɪk.jʊ.lət hɑːt / (BrE)
a heart with two auricles, as found in mammals, birds, and reptiles
heart with two auricles
medical

Examples

The biauriculate heart is a key evolutionary adaptation in mammals and birds.
During the dissection, the teacher explained how the biauriculate heart differs from the hearts of fish.
Reptiles have a biauriculate heart, which allows for more efficient separation of oxygenated and deoxygenated blood.

Etymology

From Latin 'bi-' (two) and 'auricula' (little ear, referring to the atria of the heart).
