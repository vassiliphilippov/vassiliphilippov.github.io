vanishing point

1. NOUN

/ ˈvænɪʃɪŋ pɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˈvænɪʃɪŋ pɔɪnt / (BrE)
the point on the horizon in a perspective drawing or photograph at which parallel lines appear to converge.
point where parallel lines appear to meet

Examples

In this painting, all the railway tracks lead to a single vanishing point on the horizon.
The artist carefully positioned the vanishing point to create a strong sense of depth in the scene.
When drawing city streets, aligning the buildings toward a vanishing point helps achieve realistic perspective.

2. NOUN

/ ˈvænɪʃɪŋ pɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˈvænɪʃɪŋ pɔɪnt / (BrE)
the point at which something disappears or ceases to be visible, especially in art where parallel lines appear to converge in the distance
point where something disappears or converges

Examples

The road seemed endless as it stretched toward the vanishing point on the horizon.
Artists use the vanishing point to create depth and perspective in their drawings.
From the hilltop, the river narrowed until it reached its vanishing point in the distance.
