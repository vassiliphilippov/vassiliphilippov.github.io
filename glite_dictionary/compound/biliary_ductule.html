biliary ductule

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɪliˌɛri ˈdʌktjuːl / (AmE), / ˈbɪliːəri ˈdʌktjuːl / (BrE)
A small excretory duct in the liver that carries bile and empties into the hepatic ducts.
small bile duct in the liver
medical

Examples

The biliary ductule helps transport bile from the liver to the hepatic ducts.
Damage to a biliary ductule can interfere with the normal flow of bile.
Microscopic examination revealed inflammation in several biliary ductules within the liver tissue.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: hepatic duct - major duct that receives bile from liver

Etymology

From 'biliary' (relating to bile or the bile ducts) + 'ductule', diminutive of 'duct'.
