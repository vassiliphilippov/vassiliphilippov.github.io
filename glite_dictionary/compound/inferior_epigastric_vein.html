inferior epigastric vein

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈfɪriɚ ˌɛpɪˈɡæstrɪk veɪn / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɪəriə ˌepɪˈɡæstrɪk veɪn / (BrE)
a vein in the lower part of the anterior abdominal wall that drains into the external iliac vein
vein draining into the external iliac vein
medical

Examples

The inferior epigastric vein often needs to be identified during lower abdominal surgeries.
An enlarged inferior epigastric vein can be a sign of underlying vascular conditions.
Damage to the inferior epigastric vein may cause postoperative bleeding.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: external iliac vein - a large vein that receives blood from the lower limb and pelvis

Etymology

From Latin: 'epigastric' meaning 'above the stomach'; 'vein' from Latin 'vena'.
