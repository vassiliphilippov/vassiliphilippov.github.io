cape marigold

1. NOUN

/ keɪp ˈmærɪˌɡoʊld / (AmE), / keɪp ˈmærɪɡəʊld / (BrE)
a South African plant of the daisy family, cultivated for its profusion of usually yellow, daisylike flowers and aromatic foliage
flowering South African plant with yellow daisylike flowers

Examples

Many gardeners choose cape marigold for its vivid yellow blooms in the spring.
The cape marigold thrives in sunny, well-drained soil similar to its native habitat.
We planted a border of cape marigold along the walkway for a burst of color.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marigold - a plant with brightly colored, typically yellow or orange flowers

Etymology

Named after the Cape of Good Hope in South Africa, its region of origin, and 'marigold' for its flower similarity.
