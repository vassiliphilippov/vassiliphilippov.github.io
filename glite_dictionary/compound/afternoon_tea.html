afternoon tea

1. NOUN

/ ˌæftɚˌnun ˈti / (AmE), / ˌɑːftəˌnuːn ˈtiː / (BrE)
a light meal eaten in the afternoon, usually including tea and small cakes or sandwiches
a light midafternoon meal with tea and snacks
british

Examples

We enjoyed a traditional afternoon tea at the hotel, complete with scones and clotted cream.
She invited her friends over for afternoon tea in the garden on Sunday.
The café serves afternoon tea from three to five every day.

Synset Examples

an Englishman would interrupt a war to have his afternoon tea

Related Terms

  • Antonym: high tea - a more substantial evening meal, sometimes confused with afternoon tea

british
