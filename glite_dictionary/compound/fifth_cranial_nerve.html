fifth cranial nerve

1. NOUN

/ fɪfθ ˈkræniəl nɝv / (AmE), / fɪfθ ˈkræniəl nɜːv / (BrE)
the main sensory nerve of the face and the motor nerve for the muscles of mastication, also known as the trigeminal nerve
main sensory and motor nerve of the face
medical

Examples

The dentist explained that the fifth cranial nerve is involved in jaw movement and facial sensation.
Damage to the fifth cranial nerve can cause loss of sensation in parts of the face.
Neuralgia of the fifth cranial nerve can lead to intense facial pain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trigeminal nerve - the fifth cranial nerve responsible for facial sensation and chewing
