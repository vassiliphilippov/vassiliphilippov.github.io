Levite tribe

1. PROPN

/ ˈliːvaɪt traɪb / (AmE), / ˈliːvaɪt traɪb / (BrE)
one of the twelve tribes of ancient Israel, traditionally descended from Levi, noted for religious duties and service in the Temple
ancient Israelite tribe of religious service

Examples

He claimed descent from the Levite tribe through his father's line.
The Levite tribe played a central role in the religious life of ancient Israel.
Many biblical texts mention the special duties assigned to the Levite tribe.

Synset Examples

He traced his lineage back to the Levite tribe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Levite - a member of the tribe of Levi in ancient Israel

Etymology

From Hebrew Lewi (Levi), the son of Jacob, ancestor of the tribe.
