Bernoulli distribution

1. NOUN

/ bərˈnuːli dɪˌstrɪbjuˈʃən / (AmE), / bəːˈnuːli dɪsˌtrɪbjuːˈʃən / (BrE)
A probability distribution of a random variable which has exactly two possible outcomes, typically described as 'success' and 'failure,' with a fixed probability of success.
distribution with two possible outcomes, success or failure

Examples

The Bernoulli distribution is often used to model simple yes-or-no experiments.
If a coin is flipped once, the outcome can be described by a Bernoulli distribution.
Understanding the Bernoulli distribution is essential for studying more complex probability models.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: binomial distribution - probability distribution for the number of successes in several independent Bernoulli trials

Etymology

Named after Swiss mathematician Jacob Bernoulli (1654–1705).
