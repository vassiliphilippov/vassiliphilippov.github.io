duodenal ulcer

1. NOUN

/ duˈɑdənl ˈʌlsər / (AmE), / djuːˈiːdɪnəl ˈʌlsə / (BrE)
An ulcer that occurs in the lining of the duodenum, the first part of the small intestine.
ulcer in the duodenum
medical

Examples

Long-term use of certain painkillers can increase the risk of developing a duodenal ulcer.
The patient was diagnosed with a duodenal ulcer after experiencing severe abdominal pain.
A duodenal ulcer can sometimes cause bleeding or blockages if left untreated.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peptic ulcer - an ulcer occurring in the digestive tract, especially in the stomach or duodenum

Etymology

From Latin "duodenum" (part of the intestine) and Latin "ulcus" (sore).
