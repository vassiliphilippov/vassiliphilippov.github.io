colorimetric analysis

1. NOUN

/ kəˌlɝː.ɪˈmɛ.trɪk əˈnæl.ə.sɪs / (AmE), / kʌl.ər.ɪˌmɛt.rɪk əˈnæl.ɪ.sɪs / (BrE)
chemical analysis that determines the concentration of substances by measuring color intensity using a colorimeter
chemical analysis by color measurement
medical

Examples

Researchers used colorimetric analysis to determine the concentration of iron in the water samples.
The laboratory offers colorimetric analysis as a reliable method for measuring glucose in serum.
Accurate results depend on precise calibration of the equipment during colorimetric analysis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spectrophotometric analysis - analysis method using light absorption at various wavelengths

Etymology

From colorimetric, based on Latin 'color' (colour) and Greek 'metron' (measure), and analysis from Greek 'analusis' (a loosening, solution).
