American ginseng

1. NOUN

/ əˈmɛr.ɪ.kən ˈʤɪn.sɛŋ / (AmE), / əˈmɛr.ɪ.kən ˈdʒɪn.sɛŋ / (BrE)
a North American woodland herb (Panax quinquefolius) similar to Asian ginseng, valued for its medicinal root
a North American ginseng species (herb)

Examples

Many herbalists recommend American ginseng for its adaptogenic properties.
The export of American ginseng is regulated due to its high market value.
Traditional medicine often uses American ginseng as an alternative to Asian varieties.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Asian ginseng - an East Asian plant species, Panax ginseng, used in traditional medicine

Etymology

From American (referring to its native region) + ginseng (from Chinese rénshēn).
