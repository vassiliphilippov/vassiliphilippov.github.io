field balm

1. NOUN

/ ˈfiːld bɑːm / (AmE), / ˈfiːld bɑːm / (BrE)
a low-growing, strongly aromatic perennial herb of southern Europe and Great Britain, naturalized in the United States, also known as lemon balm (Melissa officinalis)
aromatic perennial herb; lemon balm

Examples

Many gardeners grow field balm for its lemon-scented leaves.
The tea made from field balm is said to have calming effects.
She planted field balm along the border of her herb garden.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lemon balm - common name for Melissa officinalis, an aromatic herb

2. NOUN

/ ˈfild bælm / (AmE), / ˈfiːld bɑːm / (BrE)
a trailing European aromatic plant of the mint family, with rounded leaves and small purplish flowers, often grown in hanging baskets; sometimes known as ground ivy and naturalized in North America
aromatic European plant, mint family

Examples

The gardener recommended planting field balm near the patio to attract bees and provide a pleasant scent.
In some herbal remedies, field balm is used for its mild medicinal properties.
The hanging basket was overflowing with lush green leaves and delicate purple flowers of field balm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ground ivy - another common name for field balm (Glechoma hederacea)
