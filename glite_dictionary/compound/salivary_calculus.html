salivary calculus

1. NOUN

/ ˈsæ.ləˌver.i ˈkæl.kjəl.əs / (AmE), / ˈsæl.ɪ.vər.i ˈkælk.jʊ.ləs / (BrE)
A hard stone-like deposit formed within a salivary gland or duct.
stone formed in a salivary gland
medical

Examples

The dentist discovered a salivary calculus blocking the patient's duct.
Surgery was required to remove the salivary calculus causing severe discomfort.
A large salivary calculus can lead to swelling and pain in the mouth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sialolith - a stone in a salivary gland or duct
  • Degree Lower: kidney stone - a hard stone in the kidney

Etymology

From Latin *salivarius* (of saliva) and *calculus* (small stone).
