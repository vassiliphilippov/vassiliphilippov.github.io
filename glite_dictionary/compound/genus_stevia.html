genus Stevia

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈstiː.vi.ə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈstiː.vi.ə / (BrE)
A genus of shrubs and herbs native to tropical and warm regions of the Americas, including several species valued for their sweet leaves.
genus of American shrubs and herbs

Examples

Plants belonging to the genus Stevia are known for their natural sweetening properties.
Many species in the genus Stevia are cultivated for herbal teas and sweeteners.
Botanists have classified over 200 species into the genus Stevia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stevia - the sweetener derived from plants of the genus Stevia

Etymology

Modern Latin, named after Spanish botanist Petrus Jacobus Stevus (Pedro Jaime Esteve).
