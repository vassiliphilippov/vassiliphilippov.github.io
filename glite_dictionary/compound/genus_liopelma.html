genus Liopelma

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs li.oʊˈpɛlmə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs li.əʊˈpɛlmə / (BrE)
The sole genus of the family Leiopelmatidae, comprising primitive New Zealand frogs.
genus of primitive New Zealand frogs

Examples

The New Zealand frog belonging to the genus Liopelma is considered one of the world's most primitive frogs.
Researchers have discovered new details about the evolutionary history of the genus Liopelma.
All living species in the genus Liopelma are found only in New Zealand.

Etymology

Liopelma from Greek 'leios' (smooth) + 'pelma' (sole of the foot); genus, from Latin 'genus' (kind, type).
