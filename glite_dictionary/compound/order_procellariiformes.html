order Procellariiformes

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdər proʊˌsɛlɛriˈɪfɔrˌmiːz / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə prəˌsɛləriˈɪfɔːmiːz / (BrE)
a group of seabirds including petrels, albatrosses, shearwaters, and diving petrels, classified together as an order in zoological taxonomy
a bird order including petrels and albatrosses

Examples

The order Procellariiformes comprises seabirds adapted to a life at sea, such as petrels and albatrosses.
Scientists study the migration patterns of birds in the order Procellariiformes to understand oceanic ecosystems.
The wingspans of some species within the order Procellariiformes are among the largest of all birds.

Etymology

From Latin 'procella' (storm) and 'iformes' (having the form of), referring to storm-associated birds.
