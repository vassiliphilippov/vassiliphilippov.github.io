field scabious

1. NOUN

/ ˈfiːld skəˈbɪəs / (AmE), / ˈfiːld ˈskeɪ.bi.əs / (BrE)
a perennial plant with bluish-lilac flowers, native to Europe and introduced to eastern North America, commonly found in meadows and fields.
a flowering perennial plant

Examples

The field scabious blooms beautifully throughout the summer in English meadows.
Gardeners often plant field scabious to attract bees and butterflies.
She picked a bunch of field scabious during her walk along the country lane.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scabious - any plant of the genus Scabiosa

Etymology

From the Latin 'scabiosus', referring to plants believed to cure scabies, and 'field' indicating its habitat.
