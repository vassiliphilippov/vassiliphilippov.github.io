red osier

1. NOUN

/ ɹɛd ˈoʊʒɚ / (AmE), / ɹɛd ˈəʊziə / (BrE)
a common North American shrub (Cornus sericea) with reddish-purple twigs and white flowers, often found in wetlands
North American shrub with red twigs and white flowers

Examples

The wetland was lined with red osier that added a splash of color even in winter.
Gardeners often plant red osier for its attractive stems and wildlife value.
In early summer, the red osier blooms with clusters of small white flowers.

Etymology

From 'red' (color of the twigs) and 'osier' (a term for willow-like plants), influenced by French 'osier' meaning 'willow'.

2. NOUN

/ rɛd ˈoʊʒər / (AmE), / rɛd ˈəʊziə / (BrE)
A Eurasian willow with reddish or purple twigs and bark high in tannin, often used in basketry.
a willow with reddish twigs and tannin-rich bark

Examples

The bright stems of the red osier stood out against the winter snow.
Many baskets were woven from the supple branches of the red osier.
The park's wetland restoration project included planting hundreds of red osier shrubs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: willow - a tree or shrub of the genus Salix, often with slender branches

Etymology

From 'red' referring to the color, and 'osier', from Old French *osier* (willow twig).
