rhesus monkey

1. NOUN

/ ˈrisəs ˈmʌŋki / (AmE), / ˈriːsəs ˈmʌŋki / (BrE)
A medium-sized monkey native to southern Asia, commonly used in medical research.
Asian monkey species, used in research

Examples

The rhesus monkey is frequently studied in neuroscience research.
Scientists used rhesus monkeys in their vaccine development trials.
A troop of rhesus monkeys was spotted near the river in India.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: macaque - a genus of Old World monkeys to which the rhesus monkey belongs

Etymology

From Latin 'rhesus,' the genus name.
