bird's nest fern

1. NOUN

/ bɝdz nɛst fɝn / (AmE), / bɜːdz nɛst fɜːn / (BrE)
a tropical Old World or Australian epiphytic fern (Asplenium nidus) that often grows in tree crotches and has broad, arching fronds forming a nest-like cluster
a tropical epiphytic fern with nest-like fronds

Examples

The bird's nest fern thrives in humid environments, making it a popular plant for bathrooms and greenhouses.
In the wild, the bird's nest fern often grows nestled in the branches of rainforest trees.
She placed a large bird's nest fern in the corner to add a touch of tropical greenery to her living room.
