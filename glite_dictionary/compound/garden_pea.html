garden pea

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɑrdn ˌpi / (AmE), / ˈɡɑːdn ˌpiː / (BrE)
the edible, multi-seeded pod of the common pea plant, typically flattened or cylindrical in shape
edible pod of the pea plant

Examples

She picked a fresh garden pea from the vine and ate it raw.
The chef added tender garden peas to the risotto for color and sweetness.
During summer, we often enjoy steamed garden peas with butter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pea - the small round seed or seed-pod of the plant Pisum sativum

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡɑrdən pi / (AmE), / ˈɡɑːdn̩ piː / (BrE)
a small, round, green seed eaten as a vegetable, or the plant on which it grows (Pisum sativum), commonly known as the pea used in cooking
edible green seed or plant (Pisum sativum)

Examples

She added a handful of garden peas to the risotto for extra color and flavor.
Fresh garden peas are in season during the spring and early summer.
The children enjoyed picking garden peas straight from the vine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pea - the small, round, edible seed or seed-pod of the plant Pisum sativum

3. NOUN

/ ˈɡɑrdən pi / (AmE), / ˈɡɑːdn piː / (BrE)
a plant (Pisum sativum) widely cultivated for its edible green seeds, usually eaten fresh rather than dried
plant yielding edible green peas

Examples

We planted garden peas in the backyard this spring.
Fresh garden pea pods are sweet and delicious when picked young.
The salad was topped with lightly steamed garden peas for extra flavor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pea - the round green seed of the garden pea plant
  • Degree Higher: snap pea - a type of pea eaten whole in the pod
