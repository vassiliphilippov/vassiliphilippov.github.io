Doctor of Public Health

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɑktər əv ˌpʌblɪk ˈhɛlθ / (AmE), / ˈdɒktə əv ˌpʌblɪk ˈhɛlθ / (BrE)
A doctoral degree awarded for advanced study and research in public health, focused on leadership and practice in preventive medicine and public health policy.
advanced doctoral degree in public health

Examples

After years of study, she was awarded the Doctor of Public Health degree from a leading university.
A Doctor of Public Health often leads health policy initiatives and public health programs.
Earning a Doctor of Public Health requires both academic coursework and practical field experience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: DrPH - abbreviation for Doctor of Public Health
