denticulate leaf

1. NOUN

/ dɛnˈtɪkjʊlət lif / (AmE), / dɛnˈtɪkjʊlət liːf / (BrE)
A leaf with a finely toothed margin, having very small, sharp projections along the edge.
leaf with finely toothed margin

Examples

The rose plant is known for its denticulate leaf margins, which are lined with tiny teeth.
Under a magnifying glass, the denticulate leaf clearly shows its minutely toothed edges.
This species can be identified by its denticulate leaf, unlike others with smooth margins.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: serrated leaf - leaf with saw-like, larger teeth along the margin
  • Antonym: entire leaf - leaf with a smooth, unnotched margin

Etymology

From Latin 'denticulatus' (having small teeth), diminutive of 'dens' (tooth).
