Grenada dollar

1. NOUN

/ ɡrəˈneɪdə ˈdɑlər / (AmE), / ɡrəˈnɑːdə ˈdɒlə / (BrE)
the basic unit of currency formerly used in Grenada before adoption of the East Caribbean dollar
former currency of Grenada

Examples

The museum displayed a rare Grenada dollar from the early twentieth century.
Before the East Caribbean dollar was introduced, people used the Grenada dollar for daily transactions.
Collectors often seek out old Grenada dollars as valuable pieces of numismatic history.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: East Caribbean dollar - the current basic unit of money in Grenada and other East Caribbean states
