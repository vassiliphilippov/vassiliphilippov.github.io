magnesium carbonate

1. NOUN

/ mæɡˌniʒiəm kɑrˈbɒneɪt / (AmE), / mæɡˌniːziəm kɑːˈbɒneɪt / (BrE)
a white crystalline salt, occurring naturally as magnesite or as part of dolomite, used in industry and as an antacid
white crystalline salt; occurs as magnesite or in dolomite

Examples

Many athletes use magnesium carbonate on their hands to improve grip during gymnastics or weightlifting.
Geologists found deposits of magnesium carbonate in the region, mainly in the form of magnesite.
Some antacid tablets contain magnesium carbonate to help neutralize stomach acid.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: magnesite - a mineral consisting largely of magnesium carbonate
