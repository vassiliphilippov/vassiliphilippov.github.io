lampshade

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæmpˌʃeɪd / (AmE), / ˈlæmpʃeɪd / (BrE)
A protective and decorative cover placed over a lamp to diffuse the light and reduce glare.
decorative cover for a lamp

Examples

She replaced the old lampshade with a new one to match her living room décor.
The bright light was too harsh until I added a lampshade to the fixture.
Dust had collected on the fabric lampshade over the years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shade - a device used to shield something from light or heat

2. NOUN

/ ˈlæmpˌʃeɪd / (AmE), / ˈlæmpʃeɪd / (BrE)
A cover that fits over a lamp to soften or direct its light.
cover diffusing lamp light

Examples

She replaced the old lampshade with a colorful new one to brighten the room.
The glass lampshade created beautiful patterns on the wall when the lamp was on.
Dust gathered quickly on top of the lampshade, so she cleaned it weekly.

Synset Examples

The quirky pet lamp-shade brightens up my study.
