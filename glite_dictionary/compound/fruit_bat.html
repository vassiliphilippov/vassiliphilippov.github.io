fruit bat

1. NOUN

/ ˈfruːt ˌbæt / (AmE), / ˈfruːt ˌbat / (BrE)
a large bat found in warm and tropical regions that feeds mainly on fruit
bat that eats fruit in tropical regions

Examples

A fruit bat soared quietly above the canopy, searching for ripe mangos.
The fruit bat has a keen sense of smell that helps it locate food at night.
Unlike insect-eating bats, the fruit bat rarely uses echolocation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flying fox - another name for certain large fruit bats
