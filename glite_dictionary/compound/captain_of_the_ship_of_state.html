captain of the ship of state

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæp.tən əv ðə ʃɪp əv steɪt / (AmE), / ˈkæp.tɪn əv ðə ʃɪp əv steɪt / (BrE)
a leader, especially the head of government or state, regarded as being responsible for guiding and governing a nation
leader or head guiding a nation

Examples

During the crisis, the prime minister acted as the captain of the ship of state, steering the country through uncertainty.
Many citizens expect their president to be a strong captain of the ship of state in times of change.
He was often referred to as the captain of the ship of state during his years in office.

Synset Examples

The president is the captain of the ship of state.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leader - a person who leads or commands a group, organization, or country
  • Synonym: president - the elected head of a republican state
  • Antonym: follower - someone who supports and is guided by another person

Etymology

Metaphor comparing leadership of a state to captaining a ship, a usage dating back to classical times.
