extrinsic fraud

1. NOUN

/ ɛkˈstrɪnsɪk frɔd / (AmE), / ɛkˈstrɪn.sɪk frɔːd / (BrE)
fraud that prevents a party from knowing their rights or from having a fair opportunity to present them at trial
fraud preventing fair legal process
legal

Examples

The defendant alleged extrinsic fraud occurred, depriving him of a chance to present his case.
The court may set aside a judgment if it was obtained through extrinsic fraud.
Unlike intrinsic fraud, extrinsic fraud involves deception that prevents a fair trial.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: intrinsic fraud - fraud relating to issues involved in the original action or proceeding itself

Etymology

From 'extrinsic' (from Latin extrinsecus, 'from outside') + 'fraud' (from Latin fraus, fraudis, 'deceit').
