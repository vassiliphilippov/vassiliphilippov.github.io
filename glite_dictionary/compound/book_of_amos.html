Book of Amos

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʊk əv ˈeɪ.məs / (AmE), / ˈbʊk əv ˈeɪ.mɒs / (BrE)
a book of the Old Testament containing the prophecies of Amos
Old Testament book of Amos's prophecies

Examples

The Book of Amos contains warnings about social justice and religious hypocrisy.
Many scholars study the historical context of the Book of Amos.
She quoted a passage from the Book of Amos during her sermon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Bible - the Christian scriptures consisting of the Old and New Testaments

Etymology

Amos is a Hebrew name; the book is named after the prophet whose words it records.
