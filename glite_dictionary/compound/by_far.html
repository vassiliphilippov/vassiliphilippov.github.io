by far

1. ADV

/ baɪ ˈfɑr / (AmE), / baɪ ˈfɑː / (BrE)
used to emphasize how much better, greater, or more important something is compared to others or to a standard
to a great degree; by a large margin

Examples

This is by far the most interesting book I’ve ever read.
Her performance was by far the best in the competition.
The restaurant is by far the most popular place to eat in town.

Synset Examples

she was by far the smartest student
it was far and away the best meal he had ever eaten

Related Terms

  • Synonym: far and away - used to emphasize the extent of difference

2. ADV

/ baɪ fɑr / (AmE), / baɪ fɑː / (BrE)
used to emphasize that something is much better, greater, or more important than anything else
to a great extent; significantly

Examples

This is by far the most challenging project I’ve ever worked on.
Jane is by far the most talented artist in the group.
The new model is by far more efficient than the previous one.

Synset Examples

This is by far my favorite restaurant in town.
That was by far the best movie I've seen.
He is by far the fastest runner in our team.
