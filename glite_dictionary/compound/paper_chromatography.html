paper chromatography

1. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪpɚ kroʊˌmætəˈɡræfi / (AmE), / ˈpeɪpə krəʊˌmætəˈɡræfi / (BrE)
a method of chromatography in which substances are separated by selective adsorption onto a strip of paper
chromatography using paper to separate substances

Examples

The scientists used paper chromatography to identify the pigments in the leaves.
In school, we learned how paper chromatography separates different ink colors.
Paper chromatography is a simple yet effective method for analyzing mixtures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chromatography - a laboratory technique for the separation of mixtures
