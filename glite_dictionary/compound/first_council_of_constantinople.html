First Council of Constantinople

1. PROPN

/ ˈfɝst ˌkaʊn.səl əv ˌkɑn.stæn.tɪˈnoʊ.pəl / (AmE), / ˈfɜːst ˌkaʊn.səl əv ˌkɒn.stæntɪˈnəʊ.pl̩ / (BrE)
the second ecumenical council of the Christian Church, held in 381, which expanded the Nicene Creed to address the Holy Spirit
second ecumenical council of Christianity, 381 AD

Examples

The First Council of Constantinople clarified the doctrine of the Holy Trinity for the Christian Church.
Historians study the significance of the First Council of Constantinople in shaping Christian beliefs.
At the First Council of Constantinople, new wording was added about the Holy Spirit to the Nicene Creed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Nicene Creed - statement of Christian faith formulated at the First Council of Nicaea
