central vowel

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛntrəl ˈvaʊəl / (AmE), / ˈsɛntrəl ˈvaʊəl / (BrE)
(linguistics) any vowel sound in which the tongue is positioned midway between a front vowel and a back vowel.
vowel with tongue positioned midway between front and back

Examples

The schwa is a common example of a central vowel in English.
In some languages, central vowels are distinguished by the degree of lip rounding.
A central vowel occurs in the middle position of the vowel chart, unlike front or back vowels.

Synset Examples

In practice, unrounded central vowels tend to be further forward and rounded central vowels further back.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: front vowel - vowel sound where tongue is positioned forward in the mouth
  • Antonym: back vowel - vowel sound where tongue is positioned toward the back of the mouth
