blue cohosh

1. NOUN

/ bluː ˈkoʊ.hɑːʃ / (AmE), / bluː ˈkəʊ.hɒʃ / (BrE)
a tall herb of eastern North America and Asia, with blue berrylike fruit and a thick knotty rootstock, formerly used medicinally
a medicinal plant with blue berries

Examples

The roots of blue cohosh were once used in traditional medicine to treat various ailments.
We spotted a patch of blue cohosh growing along the shady forest floor.
Herbalists sometimes caution against the use of blue cohosh due to its potential side effects.

Etymology

From 'blue' (color of the berry) and Algonquian 'cohosh' meaning 'root'.
