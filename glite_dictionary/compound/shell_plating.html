shell plating

1. NOUN

/ ʃɛl ˈpleɪ.tɪŋ / (AmE), / ʃɛl ˈpleɪ.tɪŋ / (BrE)
the plates of metal that cover the frame of a steel ship, forming its outer hull and corresponding to the planking of a wooden ship
metal plates forming a ship's outer hull

Examples

During the inspection, the engineer examined the shell plating for signs of corrosion or damage.
A breach in the shell plating can compromise the vessel's seaworthiness and safety.
Modern shipyards use advanced welding techniques to assemble the shell plating on large ocean liners.
