World Bank

1. PROPN

/ wɜrld bæŋk / (AmE), / wɜːld bæŋk / (BrE)
an international financial institution established by the United Nations to provide loans and technical assistance to developing countries, with member governments guaranteeing the loans
international financial institution belonging to the UN

Examples

The World Bank approved a new loan to help improve healthcare in the region.
Many developing countries rely on funding from the World Bank for infrastructure projects.
Reports from the World Bank often influence global economic policies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: International Monetary Fund - international organization promoting global monetary cooperation
