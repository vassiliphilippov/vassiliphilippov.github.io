mercy seat

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɝːsi siːt / (AmE), / ˈmɜːsi siːt / (BrE)
the throne of God, regarded especially in Judaism and Christianity as the place in the temple where God's presence appeared
the throne of God; place of divine presence

Examples

In the ancient temple, the mercy seat was seen as the place where God would meet Moses.
Many hymns refer to the mercy seat as a symbol of divine forgiveness.
He meditated on the meaning of the mercy seat in biblical tradition.

Etymology

From the Hebrew 'kapporet', usually rendered as 'mercy seat' in English translations of the Bible.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɝːsi siːt / (AmE), / ˈmɜːsi siːt / (BrE)
the golden lid of the Ark of the Covenant in the Hebrew Bible, regarded as the place where God's presence appeared and where atonement was made
golden lid of the Ark of the Covenant

Examples

In ancient Israelite worship, only the high priest was allowed to approach the mercy seat on the Day of Atonement.
The mercy seat was believed to symbolize the dwelling place of God among His people.
Scholars have debated the precise function of the mercy seat in biblical rituals.
