long-head coneflower

1. NOUN

/ lɔŋ hɛd ˈkoʊnˌflaʊɚ / (AmE), / lɒŋ hɛd ˈkəʊnˌflaʊə / (BrE)
a flowering plant of the genus Ratibida, resembling the Mexican hat coneflower, found from British Columbia to New Mexico.
a plant similar to the Mexican hat coneflower

Examples

The long-head coneflower thrives in wildflower meadows from British Columbia to New Mexico.
Gardeners often choose the long-head coneflower for its resilience and beautiful yellow petals.
We spotted several long-head coneflowers growing along the roadside during our trip.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mexican hat coneflower - a similar North American prairie wildflower
