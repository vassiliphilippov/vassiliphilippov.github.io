spray paint

1. NOUN

/ spreɪ peɪnt / (AmE), / spreɪ peɪnt / (BrE)
Paint that is dispersed in a fine mist and applied to surfaces using a spray gun or aerosol can.
paint applied in spray form

Examples

He used spray paint to create a colorful mural on the wall.
Vandals covered the sign in red spray paint overnight.
The artist prefers spray paint for its smooth, even coverage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aerosol paint - Paint dispensed from a pressurized can in a fine spray

2. VERB

/ spreɪ peɪnt / (AmE), / spreɪ peɪnt / (BrE)
to apply paint to a surface using an aerosol spray can
apply paint with an aerosol can

Examples

They decided to spray paint the old chairs to give them a fresh look.
Vandals spray painted graffiti on the wall last night.
He watched his neighbor spray paint the mailbox a bright red.

Synset Examples

They will spray paint the fence this weekend.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paint - to cover a surface with paint
  • Antonym: brush paint - to apply paint with a brush, not a spray
