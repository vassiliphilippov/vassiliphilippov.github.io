Nilo-Saharan language

1. NOUN

/ ˌnaɪloʊ səˈhɛrən ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌnaɪləʊ səˈhɑːrən ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
any of a family of languages spoken in East and Central Africa, primarily by Nilotic peoples from the Sahara to Kenya and Tanzania.
East African language family

Examples

The Maasai speak a Nilo-Saharan language that has deep historical roots in East Africa.
Scholars debate the classification of some Nilo-Saharan languages due to their complex linguistic features.
Linguistic studies of Nilo-Saharan language groups help trace ancient migration patterns in Africa.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Afro-Asiatic language - language family widely spoken in North Africa and the Middle East
  • Synonym: Niger-Congo language - largest language family in Africa by number of speakers

Etymology

From the names of the Nile and Sahara regions, indicating the geographic distribution of the language family.
