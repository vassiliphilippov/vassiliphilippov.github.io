rosebay willowherb

1. NOUN

/ ˈroʊzˌbeɪ ˈwɪloʊˌhɜrb / (AmE), / ˈrəʊzbeɪ ˈwɪləhɜːb / (BrE)
a tall perennial plant with narrow leaves and spikes of pinkish-purple flowers, often found in abundance in burned or cleared areas; also valued as a honey plant
tall perennial with pinkish-purple flowers, also called fireweed

Examples

After the forest fire, rosebay willowherb quickly covered the blackened ground with vibrant color.
Bees are drawn to rosebay willowherb, making it an important plant for honey production.
In early summer, the meadow was filled with tall spikes of rosebay willowherb swaying in the breeze.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fireweed - another name for rosebay willowherb, especially in North America
