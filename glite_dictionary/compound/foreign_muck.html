foreign muck

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔrən mʌk / (AmE), / ˈfɒrɪn mʌk / (BrE)
(informal, derogatory) Unwanted material or substances, especially debris, originating from another place or country.
unwanted material from elsewhere
informal

Examples

After the storm, my yard was filled with foreign muck washed in from the street.
He refused to eat what he called foreign muck, insisting on traditional home-cooked meals.
The pond was polluted by foreign muck carried in by the flooding river.

Synset Examples

He complained about the foreign muck in his garden.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: debris - scattered pieces of waste or remains
  • Synonym: rubbish - waste material; refuse or litter
