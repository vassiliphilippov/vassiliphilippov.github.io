Normandy landings

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɔr.mən.di ˈlænd.ɪŋz / (AmE), / ˈnɔː.mən.di ˈlænd.ɪŋz / (BrE)
the Allied military invasion of German-occupied Western Europe, which began on the coast of Normandy, France, on June 6, 1944, during World War II
WWII Allied invasion of Normandy, France

Examples

Many historians view the Normandy landings as the turning point in World War II.
The 75th anniversary of the Normandy landings was commemorated with ceremonies in France and around the world.
My grandfather fought in the Normandy landings and often shared stories of that pivotal day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: D-Day - the day on which the Normandy landings occurred (June 6, 1944)

Etymology

Normandy from the French region; 'landings' referring to military disembarkation.
