levy en masse

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɛvi ɑ̃ ˈmæs / (AmE), / ˌlɛvi ɒ̃ ˈmas / (BrE)
the act of conscripting or drafting large numbers of civilians into military service, especially during times of national emergency
mass conscription into military service

Examples

During the revolution, the government declared a levy en masse to defend the country.
The concept of levy en masse originated during the French Revolutionary Wars.
A sudden levy en masse was needed to respond to the invasion threat.

Etymology

From French 'levée en masse' (mass raising/recruitment).
