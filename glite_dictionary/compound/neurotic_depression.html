neurotic depression

1. NOUN

/ nʊˈɹɑː.tɪk dɪˈprɛʃ.ən / (AmE), / njʊəˈɹɒ.tɪk dɪˈprɛʃ.ən / (BrE)
a form of depression characterized by emotional distress without the presence of psychotic symptoms, such as delusions or hallucinations
depression without psychotic features
medical

Examples

Her doctor diagnosed her with neurotic depression after carefully evaluating her symptoms and medical history.
Unlike psychotic depression, neurotic depression does not include hallucinations or delusional thinking.
The patient responded well to therapy aimed at treating his neurotic depression.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: psychotic depression - depression accompanied by psychotic symptoms, such as delusions or hallucinations
  • Degree Higher: depressive disorder - a general term for all forms of depression or depressive illnesses

Etymology

From 'neurotic' (relating to neurosis) + 'depression'. 'Neurotic' is from Greek 'neuron' (nerve).
