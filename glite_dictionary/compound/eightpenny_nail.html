eightpenny nail

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪtpɛni neɪl / (AmE), / ˈeɪtpəni neɪl / (BrE)
A nail that is 2.5 inches (about 6.4 centimeters) long, commonly used in construction.
nail 2.5 inches long

Examples

The carpenter used an eightpenny nail to secure the boards together.
You will need an eightpenny nail for this part of the project.
She hammered an eightpenny nail into the frame to hold it in place.

Etymology

The term 'penny' in nail sizes derives from an old English system representing the price per hundred nails.
