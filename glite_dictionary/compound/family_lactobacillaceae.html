family Lactobacillaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ˌlæktoʊbæsɪˈleɪsiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli ˌlaktəʊbæsɪˈliːsiː / (BrE)
A family of bacteria that ferment carbohydrates mainly into lactic acid, including lactic acid bacteria and important pathogens.
group of lactic acid bacteria
medical

Examples

Many probiotic yogurts contain strains from the family Lactobacillaceae for their beneficial effects on digestion.
The family Lactobacillaceae plays a key role in the fermentation of dairy products.
Some members of the family Lactobacillaceae are used in food production, while others can act as pathogens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lactic acid bacteria - bacteria that produce lactic acid as a major metabolic end product

Etymology

From Lactobacillus (the genus name, from Latin 'lac' meaning milk and 'bacillus' meaning small rod) and scientific taxonomy family ending '-aceae'.
