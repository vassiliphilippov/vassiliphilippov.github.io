to break reality

1. VERB

/ breɪk riˈæləti / (AmE), / breɪk riˈalɪti / (BrE)
to challenge, distort, or disrupt ordinary perceptions or understanding of what is real, especially through art or media
challenge or distort real perceptions

Examples

The director uses special effects to break reality and create a dreamlike world.
Some writers aim to break reality with stories that blur the line between fact and fantasy.
Virtual reality technology can sometimes break reality for users, making them question what is real.

Synset Examples

The film aims to break reality with its stunning visuals.
Sometimes, art can break reality in unexpected ways.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: distort reality - to twist or alter the perception of what is real
  • Synonym: break - to make something separate into pieces forcibly
