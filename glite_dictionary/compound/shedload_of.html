shedload of

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃɛdˌloʊd əv / (AmE), / ˈʃɛdˌləʊd əv / (BrE)
an extremely large amount or quantity of something, typically used informally
a very large amount of something
informal british

Examples

Mark won a shedload of money in the lottery last year.
There's a shedload of paperwork to complete before the deadline.
We received a shedload of complaints about the new policy.

Synset Examples

He brought a shedload of snacks to the party.
She has a shedload of work to finish by Friday.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ton of - a very large amount of something (informal)
  • Synonym: load of - a lot of something; a large quantity (informal)
  • Degree Lower: bit of - a small amount of something

Etymology

British English slang; from 'shed' (a large outbuilding) + 'load', implying a load large enough to fill a shed.
