chicken Kiev

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɪk.ən kiˈɛv / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪk.ɪn kiˈɛv / (BrE)
A dish of boneless chicken breast pounded thin, filled with seasoned butter, rolled, breaded, and fried.
breaded chicken breast with herbed butter

Examples

I ordered the chicken Kiev at the restaurant because I love the combination of crispy chicken and melted butter.
Her homemade chicken Kiev was filled with garlic and parsley butter for extra flavor.
They served chicken Kiev with a side of mashed potatoes and steamed vegetables.

Etymology

Named after Kiev (Kyiv), the capital city of Ukraine, where the dish is believed to have originated or been popularized.
