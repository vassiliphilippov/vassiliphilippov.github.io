Independence Day

1. PROPN

/ ˌɪndəˈpɛndəns deɪ / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˈpɛndəns deɪ / (BrE)
The annual national holiday in the United States celebrated on July 4th, commemorating the adoption of the Declaration of Independence in 1776.
US national holiday on July 4th
american

Examples

Every year, Americans celebrate Independence Day with fireworks and parades.
Cities across the country hold special events to mark Independence Day.
On Independence Day, many people enjoy barbecues and family gatherings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Fourth of July - Another name for Independence Day in the US

Etymology

From 'independence' + 'day', marking the date the US declared independence from Britain.
