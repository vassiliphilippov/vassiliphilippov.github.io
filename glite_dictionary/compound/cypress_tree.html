cypress tree

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪprəs tri / (AmE), / ˈsʌɪprəs triː / (BrE)
An evergreen conifer of the genus Cupressus, native to northern temperate regions, with dark, scalelike leaves and rounded cones.
evergreen conifer of the genus Cupressus

Examples

The cypress tree stood tall by the ancient cemetery, its dark green needles contrasting with the white marble stones.
In warm climates, people often plant a cypress tree along garden pathways for shade and decoration.
Some species of the cypress tree are prized for their durable, aromatic wood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cypress - the tree or its wood; a tree of the genus Cupressus

Etymology

From Old French *cipres*, from Latin *cyparissus*, from Greek *kyparissos* (cypress).
