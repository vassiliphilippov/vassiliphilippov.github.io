Martin Luther King Day

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɑrtɪn ˈluːθɚ kɪŋ deɪ / (AmE), / ˌmɑːtɪn ˈluːθə kɪŋ deɪ / (BrE)
A public holiday in the United States observed on the third Monday in January to honor the civil rights leader Martin Luther King, Jr.
US holiday honoring Martin Luther King, Jr.
american

Examples

Many schools and offices are closed on Martin Luther King Day each year.
The parade on Martin Luther King Day attracts thousands of participants from across the city.
We spent Martin Luther King Day volunteering at a local community center.

Etymology

Named after Martin Luther King, Jr. (1929–1968), the American civil rights leader.
