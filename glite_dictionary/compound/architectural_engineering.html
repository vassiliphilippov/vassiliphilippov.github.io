architectural engineering

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑrkəˈtɛktʃərəl ˌɛn.dʒəˈnɪr.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌɑːkɪˈtɛktʃərəl ˌɛn.dʒɪˈnɪər.ɪŋ / (BrE)
The branch of engineering concerned with the design, construction, and operation of buildings, focusing on structural systems, building materials, and environmental control, as distinct from the artistic aspects of architecture.
engineering discipline for building construction

Examples

She decided to study architectural engineering because she was interested in both design and construction.
Many universities now offer specialized degrees in architectural engineering to meet the demands of the building industry.
Advances in architectural engineering have led to more energy-efficient and sustainable buildings.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: architecture - the art or practice of designing and constructing buildings

Etymology

architectural from 'architecture' and 'engineering', both from Latin roots; compound reflects the union of design and engineering principles.
