earth science

1. NOUN

/ ɜrθ ˈsaɪəns / (AmE), / ɜːθ ˈsaɪəns / (BrE)
the branch of science dealing with the physical constitution of the Earth and its atmosphere, including geology, meteorology, oceanography, and related fields
the study of the Earth's physical features and processes

Examples

She decided to major in earth science at university, focusing on geology and meteorology.
The television program explored recent discoveries in earth science and their impact on climate change research.
Field trips are an essential part of studying earth science, allowing students to observe rock formations and soil types firsthand.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: geology - the scientific study of the Earth's structure and substances
