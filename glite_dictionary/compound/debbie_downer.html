Debbie Downer

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛbi ˈdaʊnɚ / (AmE), / ˈdɛbi ˈdaʊnə / (BrE)
an informal term for a person who consistently adds negativity or pessimism to social situations
someone who habitually brings negativity
informal

Examples

Don't be a Debbie Downer; let's try to stay positive about the change.
Everyone was having fun until a Debbie Downer started complaining about the food.
No one likes it when a Debbie Downer ruins the mood of the group.

Synset Examples

Stop being a Debbie Downer and enjoy the party!
He's such a Debbie Downer when discussing future plans.
Don't be a Debbie Downer; let's focus on the positives.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: killjoy - a person who spoils the enjoyment of others
  • Synonym: wet blanket - a person who discourages enjoyment or enthusiasm

Etymology

From the female given name 'Debbie' and the adjective 'downer', popularized by a recurring sketch character on Saturday Night Live in the early 2000s.
