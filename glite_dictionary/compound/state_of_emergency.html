state of emergency

1. NOUN

/ steɪt əv ɪˈmɝːdʒənsi / (AmE), / steɪt əv ɪˈmɜːdʒənsi / (BrE)
a situation in which a government is empowered to take special measures to restore order or respond to a crisis
a time when emergency government powers are enacted

Examples

The president declared a state of emergency after the region was hit by severe flooding.
Schools were closed across the country during the state of emergency.
Many civil liberties may be restricted during a state of emergency.

Synset Examples

The government declared a state of emergency during the epidemic.
