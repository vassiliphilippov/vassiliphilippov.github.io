malacca cane

1. NOUN

/ məˈlækə keɪn / (AmE), / məˈlækə keɪn / (BrE)
a cane used as a walking stick, made from the stem of a rattan palm native to Southeast Asia
walking stick made from rattan palm

Examples

He strode confidently down the street, leaning on his malacca cane for support.
The antique shop displayed a beautifully polished malacca cane among its vintage walking sticks.
She inherited her grandfather's malacca cane, which had a silver handle.

Etymology

From Malacca, a region in Malaysia where the rattan palm is found, and 'cane' for a walking stick.
