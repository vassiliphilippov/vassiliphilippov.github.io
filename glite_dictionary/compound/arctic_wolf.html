Arctic wolf

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑrktɪk wʊlf / (AmE), / ˈɑːktɪk wʊlf / (BrE)
A subspecies of wolf native to Arctic North America, characterized by white fur and a black-tipped tail.
a white-furred wolf of Arctic North America

Examples

The Arctic wolf can survive in some of the coldest places on Earth.
Scientists have observed the hunting patterns of the Arctic wolf to learn about adaptation.
The thick white coat of the Arctic wolf helps it blend with the snowy landscape.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wolf - a large wild carnivorous mammal of the dog family
