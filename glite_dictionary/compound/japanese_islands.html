Japanese Islands

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˈpæniz ˈaɪləndz / (AmE), / dʒəˈpæniz ˈaɪləndz / (BrE)
the group of more than 3,000 islands belonging to Japan, situated to the east of Asia between the Sea of Japan and the Pacific Ocean
archipelago of Japan in East Asia

Examples

The Japanese Islands are prone to frequent earthquakes due to their location on the Pacific Ring of Fire.
Cherry blossoms bloom in spectacular fashion across the Japanese Islands each spring.
Many unique animal species are native to the Japanese Islands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: archipelago - a group or chain of islands
