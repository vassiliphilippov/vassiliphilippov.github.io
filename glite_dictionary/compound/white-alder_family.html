white-alder family

1. NOUN

/ waɪt ˈældɚ ˈfæmɪli / (AmE), / waɪt ˈældə ˈfæmɪli / (BrE)
A family of flowering plants that is coextensive with the genus Clethra, commonly known as the white-alders.
plant family of genus Clethra

Examples

The white-alder family is notable for its sweetly scented flowers in late summer.
Botanists classify Clethra alnifolia as a member of the white-alder family.
Research on the white-alder family helps improve understanding of plant evolution.
