hedge nettle

1. NOUN

/ hɛdʒ ˈnɛt̬l̩ / (AmE), / hɛdʒ ˈnɛtl̩ / (BrE)
a foul-smelling perennial plant of Eurasian origin with a green creeping rhizome, belonging to the genus Stachys
a Eurasian perennial plant (genus Stachys)

Examples

The leaves of the hedge nettle release a strong odor when crushed.
Gardeners often find hedge nettle spreading quickly due to its creeping rhizomes.
The hedge nettle is commonly found along woodland edges and roadside verges.

2. NOUN

/ hɛdʒ ˈnɛt̬əl / (AmE), / hɛdʒ ˈnɛtəl / (BrE)
A perennial herb with an odorless rhizome, found in moist areas in the Northern Hemisphere.
a perennial herb of moist places

Examples

The hedge nettle grows abundantly along the riverbanks throughout the summer.
Gardeners appreciate hedge nettle for its ability to thrive in shady, damp locations.
She identified the plant as a hedge nettle by its serrated leaves and upright stems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: woundwort - another name for hedge nettle, a perennial herb in moist places
