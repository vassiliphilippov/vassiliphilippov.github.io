engineer's chain

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈdʒɪnɪrz tʃeɪn / (AmE), / ɪnˈdʒɪnɪəz tʃeɪn / (BrE)
a unit of length equal to 100 feet, formerly used in surveying and engineering
unit of length (100 ft) used in surveying

Examples

The distance between the two markers was measured as one engineer's chain.
This old map uses engineer's chain as its unit of measurement.
Surveyors used an engineer's chain to establish property boundaries in the 19th century.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: chain - a unit of length equal to 66 feet (surveyors' chain)

Etymology

Named for its use by engineers in surveying; from 'engineer' + 'chain'.
