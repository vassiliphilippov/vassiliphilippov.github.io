classical mechanics

1. NOUN

/ ˈklæsɪkəl məˈkænɪks / (AmE), / ˈklæsɪkəl mɪˈkænɪks / (BrE)
the branch of physics that deals with the motion of bodies under the influence of forces, based on Newton's laws of motion, but not including quantum or relativistic effects
branch of physics based on Newton's laws

Examples

She studied classical mechanics in university before moving on to quantum theory.
The laws of classical mechanics break down at speeds close to the speed of light.
Many engineering problems can be solved efficiently using classical mechanics rather than more complex physics.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: quantum mechanics - branch of physics dealing with atomic and subatomic systems
  • Degree Higher: mechanics - branch of physics dealing with motion and forces
  • Degree Higher: relativistic mechanics - branch of mechanics taking into account the theory of relativity

Etymology

From 'classical' (relating to traditional or established forms) and 'mechanics' (from Greek mēkhanikē, the science of machines).
