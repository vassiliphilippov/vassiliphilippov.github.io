gray wolf

1. NOUN

/ ɡreɪ wʊlf / (AmE), / ɡreɪ wʊlf / (BrE)
a large wild wolf native to North America and Eurasia, known for its brindled grey coat and living mainly in forested northern regions
a large wolf with grey fur, native to northern regions

Examples

The gray wolf is one of the most widely distributed land mammals in the world.
Scientists have worked hard to reintroduce the gray wolf into its former habitats.
A pack of gray wolves roamed through the snowy forest in search of food.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wolf - a large wild carnivorous mammal of the dog family
