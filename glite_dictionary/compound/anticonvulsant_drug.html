anticonvulsant drug

1. NOUN

/ ˌæntiˌkənˈvʌlsənt drʌɡ / (AmE), / ˌæntiˌkɒnˈvʌlsənt drʌɡ / (BrE)
a drug used to treat or prevent convulsions, especially in epilepsy
medicine for preventing seizures
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed an anticonvulsant drug to help control her epilepsy.
After switching to a new anticonvulsant drug, his seizures became less frequent.
Research is ongoing to develop safer anticonvulsant drugs with fewer side effects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anticonvulsant - a medicine or substance that prevents or reduces convulsions
  • Synonym: antiepileptic drug - a drug used specifically to prevent epileptic seizures

Etymology

From anti- (against) + convulsant (from Latin *convulsio*, 'to pull violently').
