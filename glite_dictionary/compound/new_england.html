New England

1. PROPN

/ nuː ˈɪŋɡlənd / (AmE), / njuː ˈɪŋɡlənd / (BrE)
a region in the northeastern United States consisting of Maine, New Hampshire, Vermont, Massachusetts, Rhode Island, and Connecticut
region in the northeastern US

Examples

She grew up in New England, surrounded by stunning autumn landscapes.
Many colleges in New England have a long history and tradition.
The weather in New England is known for being unpredictable.

Etymology

Named by English explorer John Smith in 1616 for its similarity to England.
