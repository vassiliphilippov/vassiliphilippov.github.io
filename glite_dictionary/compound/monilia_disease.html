monilia disease

1. NOUN

/ moʊˈnɪliə dɪˈziz / (AmE), / məˈnɪliə dɪˈziːz / (BrE)
An infection caused by fungi of the genus Monilia or Candida, especially Candida albicans.
fungal infection by Monilia or Candida
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed her with monilia disease after noticing a white coating in her mouth.
Certain antibiotics can sometimes increase the risk of developing monilia disease.
Monilia disease is more likely to occur in people with weakened immune systems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: candidiasis - infection with Candida species (yeast infection)

Etymology

From New Latin Monilia (now synonymized with Candida), a genus of fungi.
