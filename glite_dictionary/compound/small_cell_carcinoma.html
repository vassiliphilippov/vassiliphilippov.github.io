small cell carcinoma

1. NOUN

/ smɔl sɛl kɑrˈsiːnoʊmə / (AmE), / smɔːl sɛl kɑːˈsiːnəmə / (BrE)
A highly malignant type of cancer composed of small round or oval cells with little cytoplasm, most commonly found in the lungs.
highly malignant cancer type, often in lungs
medical

Examples

The doctor confirmed the diagnosis of small cell carcinoma after reviewing the biopsy results.
Patients with small cell carcinoma of the lung often require aggressive chemotherapy.
Unlike other types of lung cancer, small cell carcinoma tends to grow and spread rapidly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oat cell carcinoma - another name for small cell carcinoma, especially in the lung
  • Antonym: non-small cell carcinoma - type of carcinoma that is not classified as small cell; broader group including adenocarcinoma and squamous cell carcinoma

Etymology

From 'carcinoma,' from Greek karkinōma (cancerous ulcer), with reference to the small, round cells characteristic of this cancer.
