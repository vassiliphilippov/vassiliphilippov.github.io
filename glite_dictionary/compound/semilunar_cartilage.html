semilunar cartilage

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛmɪˈluːnɚ ˈkɑrtɪlɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌsɛmɪˈluːnə ˈkɑːtɪlɪdʒ / (BrE)
a crescent-shaped cartilage in a joint, especially one of two in the knee, serving as a cushion between the ends of bones
crescent-shaped cartilage in a joint
medical

Examples

A torn semilunar cartilage can cause swelling and pain in the knee.
The surgeon repaired the damaged semilunar cartilage after the injury.
Athletes are at higher risk of injuring their semilunar cartilage during sports activities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meniscus - the curved cartilage structure in the knee joint

Etymology

From Latin 'semilunaris' (crescent-shaped) and 'cartilago' (cartilage).
