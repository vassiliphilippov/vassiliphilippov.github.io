musical instrument digital interface

1. NOUN

/ ˌmjuzɪkəl ˈɪnstrəmənt ˈdɪdʒɪtl ˈɪntərfeɪs / (AmE), / ˌmjuːzɪkəl ˈɪnstrəmənt ˈdɪdʒɪtl ˈɪntəfeɪs / (BrE)
a standard protocol that allows electronic musical instruments, computers, and other equipment to communicate and synchronize with each other
protocol for linking electronic musical instruments and computers

Examples

The musical instrument digital interface has revolutionized the way musicians record and produce electronic music.
Many synthesizers and computers connect using the musical instrument digital interface to transfer musical data.
Learning to use the musical instrument digital interface is essential for anyone interested in electronic music production.

Etymology

Formed as an acronym from the initial letters of 'Musical Instrument Digital Interface'.
