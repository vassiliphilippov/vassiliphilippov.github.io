Tupi-Guarani language

1. NOUN

/ ˌtuːpi ɡʊˈrɑːni ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌtuːpi ɡʊˈrɑːni ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
A family of closely related indigenous languages spoken in South America, especially in Brazil and surrounding regions.
family of South American indigenous languages

Examples

The Tupi-Guarani language family has influenced many place names in Brazil.
Researchers study the Tupi-Guarani language to learn about indigenous cultures of South America.
Several dialects of the Tupi-Guarani language are still spoken by native communities today.

Etymology

From Portuguese 'Tupi-Guarani', originally from the names of two major groups within the language family.
