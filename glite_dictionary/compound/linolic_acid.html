linolic acid

1. NOUN

/ lɪˈnoʊlɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / lɪˈnɒlɪk ˈasɪd / (BrE)
a liquid polyunsaturated fatty acid found in many plant oils, essential for human nutrition and used in making soaps and other products
an essential polyunsaturated fatty acid
medical

Examples

Foods such as sunflower oil are rich in linolic acid and other essential nutrients.
A deficiency in linolic acid can lead to various health problems.
Soap manufacturers often use linolic acid derived from vegetable oils in their products.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: linoleic acid - a related polyunsaturated fatty acid, often confused with linolic acid

Etymology

From Latin *linum* (flax) and the chemical suffix *-ic*.
