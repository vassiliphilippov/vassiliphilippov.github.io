spherical angle

1. NOUN

/ ˈsfɪrɪkəl ˈæŋɡəl / (AmE), / ˈsfɪərɪkəl ˈæŋɡl / (BrE)
an angle formed by the intersection of two arcs of great circles on a sphere, measured at their common point
angle formed by intersecting great circles on a sphere

Examples

The latitude and longitude of a location can be determined using a spherical angle.
In navigation, plotting a course often involves calculating the spherical angle between routes.
The astronomer explained how to measure a spherical angle using the intersection of two celestial circles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dihedral angle - angle formed between two intersecting planes

Etymology

From 'sphere' and 'angle'. Used in mathematics and navigation.
