candy cane

1. NOUN

/ ˈkændi keɪn / (AmE), / ˈkændi keɪn / (BrE)
a hard, sweet stick of candy, usually with red and white stripes and often flavored with peppermint, traditionally associated with Christmas
stick-shaped, striped candy, often for Christmas

Examples

Every Christmas, we hang a candy cane on the tree as a decoration.
She handed out a candy cane to each child in her class.
The red and white stripes of a candy cane are instantly recognizable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peppermint - a strong-flavored mint often used in candy canes
