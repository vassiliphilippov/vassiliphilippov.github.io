major diatonic scale

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪdʒər daɪəˈtɒnɪk skeɪl / (AmE), / ˈmeɪdʒə daɪəˈtɒnɪk skeɪl / (BrE)
A diatonic scale in which the notes are separated by whole tones except between the third and fourth, and the seventh and eighth degrees, which are separated by semitones. Often simply called a major scale.
a type of musical scale with a specific pattern of intervals

Examples

The major diatonic scale provides the foundation for much Western classical music.
She practiced running up and down the major diatonic scale on her violin every morning.
Understanding the major diatonic scale is essential for beginner music students.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: major scale - a scale with the same interval structure; another name for major diatonic scale
  • Antonym: minor scale - a scale with a different interval structure, often contrasted with the major scale

Etymology

From 'major' (Latin 'maior', meaning 'greater') and 'diatonic' (Greek 'diatonikos', relating to a scale of tones).
