ion exchange

1. NOUN

/ aɪˈɑn ɪksˌtʃeɪndʒ / (AmE), / aɪˈɒn ɪksˌtʃeɪndʒ / (BrE)
a process in which ions are exchanged between a solution and an insoluble solid, commonly used in industrial and laboratory settings for purification and separation
chemical process of exchanging ions

Examples

The water treatment plant uses ion exchange to remove calcium and magnesium ions.
In laboratories, ion exchange resins are used to purify chemicals.
Ion exchange is an essential step in the production of ultra-pure water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adsorption - the process by which atoms, ions, or molecules adhere to a surface

Etymology

From 'ion' (Greek ion, 'going') and 'exchange' (French échanger, from Latin ex- + cambiare, 'to exchange').
