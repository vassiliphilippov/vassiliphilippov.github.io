water bug

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔtɚ bʌɡ / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə bʌɡ / (BrE)
A small, light-brown cockroach originally from Europe, commonly found as a household pest in the United States.
a small cockroach household pest

Examples

Many apartment dwellers are familiar with water bugs skittering across the kitchen floor at night.
We called an exterminator after spotting several water bugs in the bathroom.
Unlike typical cockroaches, water bugs are smaller and light brown in color.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cockroach - a common household insect pest
  • Synonym: German cockroach - a small cockroach species often called a water bug

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɔt̬ɚ ˌbʌɡ / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə ˌbʌɡ / (BrE)
A large true bug that lives in or on the surface of water, adapted to aquatic life.
aquatic true bug

Examples

At the pond, I noticed a water bug gliding across the surface with its long legs.
The children were fascinated by the large water bug swimming just beneath the water.
Some species of water bug can deliver a painful bite if handled carelessly.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: giant water bug - a very large aquatic bug of the family Belostomatidae
  • Synonym: pond skater - an aquatic bug that skates on the surface of water (Gerridae)
