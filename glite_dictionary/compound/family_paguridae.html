family Paguridae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli pəˈɡjʊərɪdi / (AmE), / ˈfæməli pəˈɡjʊərɪdi / (BrE)
A family of crustaceans including most hermit crabs, typically found in marine environments and known for inhabiting empty mollusc shells.
taxonomic family of hermit crabs
medical

Examples

Many species within the family Paguridae exhibit the behavior of using empty shells for protection.
The family Paguridae is widely studied for its diverse adaptations to marine environments.
Shell selection is a key research focus in evolutionary studies of the family Paguridae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hermit crab - a marine crustacean that lives in empty shells for protection

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'Pagurus' (type genus) and '-idae' (family suffix).
