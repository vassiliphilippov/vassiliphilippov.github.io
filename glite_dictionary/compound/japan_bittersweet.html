Japan bittersweet

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˈpæn ˈbɪtərˌswit / (AmE), / dʒəˈpæn ˈbɪtəˌswiːt / (BrE)
An ornamental Asiatic climbing vine (Celastrus orbiculatus) with showy orange-yellow fruit containing a scarlet aril, often grown in gardens and naturalized in North America.
ornamental Asiatic vine with colorful fruit

Examples

The vibrant berries of Japan bittersweet are popular in autumn floral arrangements.
Gardeners are advised to prune Japan bittersweet regularly to prevent its aggressive growth.
Japan bittersweet has spread rapidly in some North American forests, outcompeting native plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bittersweet - a similar vine of the same genus
