tetrabasic acid

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɛtrəˈbeɪsɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / ˌtɛtrəˈbeɪsɪk ˈasɪd / (BrE)
an acid containing four replaceable hydrogen atoms in each molecule, often able to form four series of salts
acid with four replaceable hydrogens
medical

Examples

Phosphoric acid can act as a tetrabasic acid under certain conditions.
The chemist explained how a tetrabasic acid can form several types of salts.
Because it contains four replaceable hydrogen atoms, a tetrabasic acid can participate in multiple neutralization reactions.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: dibasic acid - an acid with two replaceable hydrogen atoms per molecule
  • Degree Lower: tribasic acid - an acid with three replaceable hydrogen atoms per molecule

Etymology

From Greek 'tetra-' meaning 'four' and 'basic', indicating the number of replaceable hydrogen atoms.
