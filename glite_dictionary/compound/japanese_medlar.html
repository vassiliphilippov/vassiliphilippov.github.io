Japanese medlar

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˈpæniz ˈmɛdlɑr / (AmE), / dʒəˈpæniːz ˈmɛdlɑː / (BrE)
An evergreen tree native to China and Japan, cultivated for its yellow, fuzzy, olive-sized fruit containing a large stone; also known as the loquat.
evergreen tree with edible yellow fruit

Examples

The Japanese medlar thrives in warm climates and produces fruit in early spring.
She planted a Japanese medlar in her garden for its ornamental flowers and edible fruit.
The Japanese medlar is often grown for both its fruit and its attractive foliage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loquat - another name for Japanese medlar or its fruit
