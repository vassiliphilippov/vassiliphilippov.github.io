Araguaya River

1. PROPN

/ ˌɑːrəˈɡwaɪ.ə ˈrɪvər / (AmE), / ˌarəˈɡwaɪ.ə ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
a river in central Brazil that flows generally northward, joining the Tocantins River, noted for its falls and natural beauty
river in central Brazil, tributary of the Tocantins

Examples

The Araguaya River is famous for its stunning waterfalls and rich biodiversity.
During their expedition, the team navigated several rapids on the Araguaya River.
Villages along the Araguaya River rely on fishing as an important source of livelihood.
