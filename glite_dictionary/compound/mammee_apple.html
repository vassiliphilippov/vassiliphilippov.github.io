mammee apple

1. NOUN

/ məˈmiː ˌæpəl / (AmE), / məˈmiː ˌapəl / (BrE)
A large, round or oval tropical fruit with a thick, rough brown skin and juicy yellow or reddish flesh.
tropical fruit with thick skin and juicy flesh

Examples

The mammee apple is a favorite fruit in many Caribbean countries.
He offered us slices of mammee apple fresh from his garden.
At the market, baskets of ripe mammee apples stood out among the tropical fruits.

Etymology

From Spanish 'mamey', from Taíno 'mami'.

2. NOUN

/ mæˈmiː ˌæpəl / (AmE), / məˈmiː ˌæpəl / (BrE)
A tropical American tree (Mammea americana) bearing large, edible fruits with a leathery rind and sweet, aromatic pulp.
tropical fruit tree with edible fruits

Examples

The market stall was selling ripe mammee apples fresh from the Caribbean.
She enjoyed the sweet, orange flesh of a mammee apple for dessert.
The tall mammee apple tree provided shade as well as delicious fruit.

Etymology

From Spanish ‘mamey’, of Taíno origin.
