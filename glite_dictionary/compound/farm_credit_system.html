Farm Credit System

1. NOUN

/ fɑrm ˈkrɛdɪt ˈsɪstəm / (AmE), / fɑːm ˈkrɛdɪt ˈsɪstəm / (BrE)
A nationwide cooperative system of banks and associations that provides credit to farmers and agricultural businesses, originally funded by the US federal government and now owned by its members.
US cooperative lending system for agriculture
american

Examples

The Farm Credit System plays a vital role in supporting rural communities and agricultural producers across the United States.
Many farmers rely on loans and financial services from the Farm Credit System to operate their businesses efficiently.
The Farm Credit System has its roots in early 20th-century policies aimed at stabilizing American agriculture.
