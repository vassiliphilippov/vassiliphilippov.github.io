family Lecanoraceae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ˌlɛkəˌnɔːˈreɪsiː / (AmE), / ˈfamɪli lɛkəˌnɔːˈreɪsiː / (BrE)
a family of fungi in the division Lichenes, known for including many common lichens
family of fungi (lichens)

Examples

The family Lecanoraceae includes many widely distributed lichen species.
Biologists have studied the family Lecanoraceae to better understand environmental indicators.
Some members of the family Lecanoraceae are important in ecological monitoring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Lecanora - a genus of lichens in the family Lecanoraceae

Etymology

From genus name Lecanora, from Greek lekanē (dish, basin), referencing the shape of the fruiting body, with the standard taxonomic suffix -aceae.
