thread-fish

1. NOUN

/ ˈθrɛd ˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈθrɛd ˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
a fish species characterized by greatly elongated front rays on its dorsal and anal fins, mainly found in warm coastal waters.
fish with long dorsal and anal fin rays

Examples

The thread-fish is easily recognized by its trailing fin rays.
Marine biologists studied the migration patterns of the thread-fish.
A thread-fish was spotted near the coral reef by divers.

Etymology

From 'thread' referring to the thin, threadlike fin rays, and 'fish'.
