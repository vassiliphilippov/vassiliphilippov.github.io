whole gale

1. NOUN

/ ˈhoʊl ɡeɪl / (AmE), / ˈhəʊl ɡeɪl / (BrE)
A wind with a speed of 55–63 knots (about 63–72 mph or 102–117 km/h), rated at number 10 on the Beaufort scale.
wind of 55–63 knots (Beaufort scale 10)

Examples

The fisherman stayed ashore when a whole gale was forecast for the evening.
Ships must be well-prepared to withstand a whole gale at sea.
During the night, a whole gale battered the coastline, causing widespread damage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: storm - violent weather with strong wind and precipitation
