leeward tide

1. NOUN

/ ˈliːwərd taɪd / (AmE), / ˈluːwəd taɪd / (BrE)
A tide that flows in the same direction as the wind is blowing.
tide moving with the wind

Examples

When sailing, a leeward tide can make steering more challenging for novice sailors.
The small fishing boat struggled against the leeward tide near the coast.
A sudden leeward tide swept the dinghy farther from shore than expected.

Synset Examples

a leeward tide is dangerous for small boats

Related Terms

  • Antonym: windward tide - a tide that runs in the opposite direction to the wind

Etymology

From 'leeward', meaning 'on or toward the side sheltered from the wind', and 'tide'.
