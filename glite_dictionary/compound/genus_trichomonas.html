genus Trichomonas

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs trɪˌkɑːməˈnæs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs trɪˌkɒməˈnæs / (BrE)
A group of flagellated protozoans that are parasitic in the digestive or genitourinary tracts of vertebrates and invertebrates, including humans.
a genus of parasitic flagellates
medical

Examples

The genus Trichomonas includes several species that can infect both humans and animals.
Researchers are studying how the genus Trichomonas adapts to different hosts.
Infections caused by the genus Trichomonas are treated with specific antiparasitic medications.

Etymology

From New Latin: 'Trichomonas' from Greek 'thrix' (hair) + 'monas' (single unit).
