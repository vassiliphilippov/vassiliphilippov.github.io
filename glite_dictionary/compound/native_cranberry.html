native cranberry

1. NOUN

/ ˈneɪ.tɪv ˈkrænˌbɛr.i / (AmE), / ˈneɪ.tɪv ˈkræn.bəri / (BrE)
a low-growing or spreading shrub with scarlet flowers and berry-like fruit resembling true cranberries, sometimes classified in the genus Styphelia
shrub with fruit resembling cranberries

Examples

The native cranberry is prized for its tart, edible berries in some Australian regions.
Gardeners often plant native cranberry as an ornamental shrub for its vibrant flowers.
Unlike true cranberries, the native cranberry thrives in drier, sandy soils.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: cranberry - a small, red, acidic fruit from the genus Vaccinium
