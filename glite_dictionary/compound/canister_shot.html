canister shot

1. NOUN

/ ˈkænɪstər ʃɑt / (AmE), / ˈkanɪstə ʃɒt / (BrE)
A type of ammunition consisting of a metal cylinder filled with small projectiles, designed to scatter them over a wide area when fired from artillery.
ammunition: metal cylinder with small projectiles

Examples

During the battle, the artillery fired canister shot to devastating effect against advancing infantry.
The museum displayed a rusted canister shot used in the nineteenth century.
Soldiers feared canister shot because it could inflict heavy casualties at close range.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grapeshot - type of ammunition with small metal balls for artillery
