cruise liner

1. NOUN

/ kruːz ˈlaɪnər / (AmE), / kruːz ˈlaɪnə / (BrE)
a large passenger ship used for pleasure voyages, where entertainment and amenities are provided on board
large passenger ship for pleasure cruises

Examples

They spent two weeks traveling the Caribbean on a luxurious cruise liner.
The new cruise liner boasts multiple swimming pools and restaurants.
Hundreds of passengers boarded the cruise liner for its maiden voyage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cruise ship - a ship used for pleasure voyages with accommodations and entertainment

Etymology

From 'cruise' (a voyage for pleasure) and 'liner' (a large passenger ship traveling on a regular route).
