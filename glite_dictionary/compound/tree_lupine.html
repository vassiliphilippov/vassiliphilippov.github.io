tree lupine

1. NOUN

/ tri ˈluːpaɪn / (AmE), / triː ˈluːpɪn / (BrE)
an evergreen shrub native to the Pacific coast of the United States, with showy yellow or blue flowers, often naturalized elsewhere
evergreen shrub with yellow or blue flowers

Examples

The tree lupine is admired for its vibrant yellow blossoms along the Californian coast.
Gardeners in Australia often cultivate tree lupine for its ornamental value and resilience.
With its blue and yellow flowers, the tree lupine brightens up many wild landscapes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lupine - a plant of the genus Lupinus noted for its tall, colorful flower spikes
