habitual abortion

1. NOUN

/ həˈbɪtʃuəl əˈbɔrʃən / (AmE), / həˈbɪtʃuəl əˈbɔːʃən / (BrE)
the occurrence of repeated spontaneous abortions, often for no identifiable reason
repeated spontaneous miscarriage
medical

Examples

After three consecutive pregnancies ended in habitual abortion, the couple sought medical advice.
Doctors monitor women with a history of habitual abortion for underlying health conditions.
Research into the causes of habitual abortion is ongoing in the field of reproductive medicine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: miscarriage - the spontaneous loss of a pregnancy before the fetus can survive independently

Etymology

From Latin 'habitus' (habit, condition) and 'abortio' (miscarriage).
