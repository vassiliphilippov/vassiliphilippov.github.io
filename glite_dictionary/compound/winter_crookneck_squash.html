winter crookneck squash

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪn.tɚ ˈkrʊk.nɛk skwɑʃ / (AmE), / ˈwɪn.tə ˈkrʊk.nɛk skwɒʃ / (BrE)
any of several varieties of winter squash characterized by hard rinds and long, curved necks
type of winter squash with curved neck

Examples

The farmer harvested several winter crookneck squash from his garden just before the first frost.
She roasted a winter crookneck squash with herbs for a hearty autumn dinner.
Winter crookneck squash stores well through the colder months due to its hard rind.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: squash - a vegetable from several plants of the genus Cucurbita

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɪntər ˈkrʊknɛk skwɑʃ / (AmE), / ˈwɪntə ˈkrʊknɛk skwɒʃ / (BrE)
A type of squash with a hard rind and an elongated curved neck, typically grown for use in winter.
type of hard-skinned squash with a curved neck

Examples

The farmer displayed a large winter crookneck squash at the harvest festival.
You can use winter crookneck squash in soups and stews during the colder months.
After harvesting, winter crookneck squash can be stored for several weeks.
