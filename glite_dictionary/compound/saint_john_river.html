Saint John River

1. PROPN

/ seɪnt dʒɑn ˈrɪvɚ / (AmE), / seɪnt dʒɒn ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
a river in northeastern North America that rises in Maine, flows through New Brunswick, and empties into the Bay of Fundy
river in Maine and New Brunswick

Examples

The Saint John River forms part of the border between Maine and New Brunswick.
Many communities rely on the Saint John River for their water supply and recreation.
Spring flooding along the Saint John River can cause significant damage to nearby towns.
