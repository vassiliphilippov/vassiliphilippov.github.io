voting age

1. NOUN

/ ˈvoʊtɪŋ eɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈvəʊtɪŋ eɪdʒ / (BrE)
the age at which a person is legally allowed to vote in public elections
legal age to vote

Examples

The government is considering lowering the voting age to 16.
Many countries have set the voting age at 18 years old.
Debates about the appropriate voting age occur during most election reforms.
