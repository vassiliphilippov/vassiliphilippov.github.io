Ofo language

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊfoʊ ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈəʊfəʊ ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
an extinct Siouan language formerly spoken by the Ofo people of the lower Mississippi Valley in the United States
extinct Siouan language of the Ofo people

Examples

The Ofo language became extinct in the early twentieth century after its last speakers died.
Linguists have studied the Ofo language to better understand Siouan language relationships.
Very few written records of the Ofo language have survived to the present day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Siouan language - a family of North American Indian languages, including Ofo, Dakota, Crow, and others
