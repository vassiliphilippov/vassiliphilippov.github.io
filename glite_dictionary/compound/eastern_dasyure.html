eastern dasyure

1. NOUN

/ ˈistɚn dæz.i.jʊr / (AmE), / ˈiːstən ˈdæzɪjʊə / (BrE)
A small carnivorous marsupial (Myoictis melas) native to New Guinea, distinguished by its striped back.
small carnivorous marsupial of New Guinea

Examples

The eastern dasyure hunts insects and small animals in the forests of New Guinea.
Researchers are studying the population dynamics of the eastern dasyure to aid conservation efforts.
A shy yet agile creature, the eastern dasyure is rarely seen in the wild during the day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dasyure - any of several small carnivorous marsupials found in Australia and New Guinea
