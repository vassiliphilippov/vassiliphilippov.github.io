chlorophyll d

1. NOUN

/ ˌklɔrəˌfɪl ˈdi / (AmE), / ˌklɒrəˌfɪl ˈdiː / (BrE)
a type of chlorophyll found, alongside chlorophyll a, in red algae, involved in photosynthesis
a pigment in red algae
medical

Examples

Red algae contain chlorophyll d in addition to other pigments for photosynthesis.
Scientists study chlorophyll d to understand how red algae absorb different wavelengths of light.
Unlike most land plants, some marine species have high concentrations of chlorophyll d.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chlorophyll a - the main form of chlorophyll in plants, algae, and cyanobacteria
  • Synonym: chlorophyll b - a form of chlorophyll found mainly in green plants and green algae

Etymology

From 'chlorophyll' (from Greek 'khlōros' meaning green + 'phullon' meaning leaf) and the letter 'd' denoting its classification.
