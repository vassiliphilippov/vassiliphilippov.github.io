cylinder head

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪlɪndɚ hɛd / (AmE), / ˈsɪlɪndə hɛd / (BrE)
a detachable cover that closes the top of a cylinder in an internal combustion engine or pump, often housing parts such as valves and spark plugs
detachable cover for engine or pump cylinder

Examples

The mechanic removed the cylinder head to inspect the pistons for any signs of damage.
Excessive heat can cause a cylinder head to warp, leading to engine problems.
He ordered a replacement cylinder head after finding cracks in the old one.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: valve cover - the cover over the valve mechanism in an internal combustion engine
