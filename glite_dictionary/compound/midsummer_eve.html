Midsummer Eve

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɪdˈsʌmɚ iːv / (AmE), / ˌmɪdˈsʌmə iːv / (BrE)
the evening or night before Midsummer Day, often celebrated with festivals and traditions in some cultures
night before Midsummer Day

Examples

On Midsummer Eve, villagers gather to light bonfires and dance around the maypole.
She wore a flower crown for the celebration on Midsummer Eve.
Many folktales speak of magical events occurring on Midsummer Eve.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Midsummer Day - the day celebrating the middle of summer, around the summer solstice

Etymology

From 'mid' + 'summer', with 'Eve' meaning the evening before a festival or event.
