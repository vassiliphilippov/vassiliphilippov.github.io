Second Council of Lyons

1. PROPN

/ ˈsɛkənd ˈkaʊnsəl əv liˈɑn / (AmE), / ˈsekənd ˈkaʊnsəl əv liˈɒ̃ / (BrE)
a church council held in 1274 that temporarily reunited the Greek Orthodox and Roman Catholic Churches
church council of 1274 restoring East-West union

Examples

The Second Council of Lyons was a turning point in the relationship between Eastern and Western Christianity.
At the Second Council of Lyons, delegates negotiated terms for the reunion of the churches.
The decisions made during the Second Council of Lyons had significant effects on ecclesiastical history.
