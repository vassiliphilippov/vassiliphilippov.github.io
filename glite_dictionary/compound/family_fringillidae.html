family Fringillidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli frɪnˌdʒɪˈlaɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli frɪnˌdʒɪˈliːdiː / (BrE)
a large family of small to medium-sized songbirds including finches, goldfinches, bullfinches, and related species
family of finches and related birds

Examples

The family Fringillidae includes familiar birds such as goldfinches, chaffinches, and siskins.
Many birdwatchers are fascinated by the diversity found within the family Fringillidae.
Species in the family Fringillidae are known for their conical beaks, which are adapted for eating seeds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: finch - a small songbird of the family Fringillidae

Etymology

From New Latin 'Fringillidae', from 'Fringilla', the type genus, meaning 'finch' in Latin.
