California romero

1. NOUN

/ kəˌlɪfɔrniə roʊˈmɛroʊ / (AmE), / kəˌlɪfɔːniə rəʊˈmɛrəʊ / (BrE)
a woolly-leaved aromatic plant native to southern California and Mexico, often used as a regional common name for certain sage species
aromatic plant native to southern California and Mexico

Examples

The hills were dotted with California romero, filling the air with their distinctive fragrance.
Local gardeners often plant California romero for its drought tolerance and aromatic leaves.
Wildlife in the region rely on California romero as an important food and shelter source.

Etymology

From California (region) and Spanish 'romero', meaning rosemary.
