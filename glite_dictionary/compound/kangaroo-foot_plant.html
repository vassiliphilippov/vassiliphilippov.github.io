kangaroo-foot plant

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæŋɡəˌru fʊt plænt / (AmE), / ˌkaŋɡəˌruː fʊt plɑːnt / (BrE)
an Australian herbaceous plant, chiefly of the genus Anigozanthos, with spring flowers covered in woolly hairs and shaped like a kangaroo's foot
Australian plant with kangaroo-foot-shaped flowers

Examples

The kangaroo-foot plant is popular in gardens for its unusual, fuzzy flowers.
Botanists studied the native kangaroo-foot plant to understand its pollination by birds.
She bought a kangaroo-foot plant at the nursery to add a touch of Australia to her backyard.

Etymology

Named for the resemblance of its flower clusters to the shape of a kangaroo's foot; originally from Australian English.
