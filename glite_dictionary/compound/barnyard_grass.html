barnyard grass

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑrnˌjɑrd ˌɡræs / (AmE), / ˈbɑːnˌjɑːd ˌɡrɑːs / (BrE)
a coarse annual grassy weed (Echinochloa crus-galli), widespread in many regions, often found in moist, cultivated ground; sometimes used for hay or grazing.
a common grassy weed, Echinochloa crus-galli

Examples

The farmer spent days pulling barnyard grass from his rice paddies to prevent it from choking his crops.
Barnyard grass thrives in wet, disturbed soils and can quickly overtake a field if not managed.
Some livestock will graze on barnyard grass when other forage is scarce.
