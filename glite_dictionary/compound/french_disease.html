French disease

1. NOUN

/ frɛntʃ dɪˈziz / (AmE), / frɛntʃ dɪˈziːz / (BrE)
an old euphemism for syphilis, used historically, especially in Europe
historical term for syphilis
archaic

Examples

Syphilis was commonly referred to as the French disease in European writings of the 16th century.
Doctors struggled to treat the French disease before the discovery of penicillin.
The term French disease reflects the historic practice of blaming foreign nations for epidemics.

Synset Examples

The term French disease was used to describe societal issues.
He joked that love was the French disease of his youth.
In the past, the French disease was a euphemism for syphilis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: syphilis - a sexually transmitted infection caused by the bacterium Treponema pallidum

Etymology

Named in the late 15th century, as different nations blamed the origin of syphilis on rivals (e.g., 'French disease' in England, 'Spanish disease' in France).
