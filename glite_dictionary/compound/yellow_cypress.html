yellow cypress

1. NOUN

/ ˈjɛloʊ ˈsaɪprəs / (AmE), / ˈjɛləʊ ˈsʌɪprəs / (BrE)
a tall evergreen tree of the Pacific coast of North America, often grown for its ornamental value; also known as Alaska cedar
a North American evergreen tree

Examples

The dense wood of the yellow cypress is highly valued for its durability and resistance to decay.
Several yellow cypress trees were planted along the coastal trail for shade and ornamentation.
Hikers admired the towering yellow cypress as they explored the old-growth forests of British Columbia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Alaska cedar - another name for yellow cypress
  • Degree Higher: cypress - any tree or shrub of the genus Cupressus or Chamaecyparis
