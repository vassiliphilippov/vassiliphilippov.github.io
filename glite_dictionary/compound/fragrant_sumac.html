fragrant sumac

1. NOUN

/ ˈfreɪɡrənt ˈsuːmæk / (AmE), / ˈfreɪɡrənt ˈsjuːmæk / (BrE)
a low, sweet-scented shrub native to eastern North America, with three-part leaves, yellowish-green flowers, and clusters of red hairy fruit
a sweet-scented shrub of eastern North America

Examples

The leaves of the fragrant sumac release a pleasant aroma when crushed.
We planted a fragrant sumac in the garden to attract birds and butterflies.
In autumn, the foliage of fragrant sumac turns a brilliant red.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sumac - any of various shrubs or small trees of the genus Rhus

Etymology

sumac from Old French *sumac*, from Arabic *summāq* (red).
