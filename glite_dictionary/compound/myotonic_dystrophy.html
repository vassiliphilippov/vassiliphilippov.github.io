myotonic dystrophy

1. NOUN

/ ˌmaɪ.əˈtɑ.nɪk dɪsˈtrə.fi / (AmE), / ˌmaɪ.əˈtɒn.ɪk dɪsˈtrɒf.i / (BrE)
A genetic disorder characterized by progressive muscle weakness and wasting, especially affecting the face, hands, feet, and neck, with possible speech difficulties and usually a slow progression.
genetic muscle-wasting disorder
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed him with myotonic dystrophy after noticing progressive weakness in his hands and facial muscles.
People with myotonic dystrophy often experience difficulty releasing their grip after shaking hands.
Research into treatments for myotonic dystrophy is ongoing, offering hope to those affected by the disease.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: muscular dystrophy - a group of inherited disorders characterized by muscle weakness and wasting

Etymology

From Greek 'mys' (muscle) + 'tonos' (tension) and 'dystrophy' from Greek 'dys-' (bad) + 'trophē' (nourishment).
