Kapsiki language

1. NOUN

/ kæpˈsiːki ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / kapˈsiːki ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
a Chadic language spoken mainly south of Lake Chad, especially in Adamawa State, Nigeria, and the Mandara mountains
Chadic language of Adamawa State, Nigeria

Examples

The Kapsiki language is primarily spoken in several villages in northeastern Nigeria.
Linguists are studying the grammar and vocabulary of the Kapsiki language to better understand Chadic languages.
She learned to greet elders in the Kapsiki language during her visit to the Mandara mountains.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Chadic language - a branch of the Afro-Asiatic language family spoken in parts of Central and West Africa
