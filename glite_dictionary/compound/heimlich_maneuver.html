Heimlich maneuver

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪmlɪk məˌnuːvɚ / (AmE), / ˈhaɪmlɪk məˌnjuːvə / (BrE)
an emergency procedure involving abdominal thrusts to expel an object blocking a person's airway
emergency procedure for choking

Examples

If someone is choking, you should perform the Heimlich maneuver immediately.
She learned how to do the Heimlich maneuver in her first aid training class.
The waiter used the Heimlich maneuver to save a customer's life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abdominal thrusts - forceful upward compressions of the abdomen to dislodge airway obstructions

Etymology

Named after Henry J. Heimlich, the American physician who first described the technique in 1974.
