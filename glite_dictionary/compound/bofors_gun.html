Bofors gun

1. NOUN

/ ˈboʊfɔːrz ɡʌn / (AmE), / ˈbəʊfɔːz ɡʌn / (BrE)
an automatic, usually double-barreled antiaircraft gun designed by the Swedish company Bofors and widely used in military defense systems
automatic antiaircraft gun

Examples

The navy ship was equipped with a Bofors gun for defense against aerial attacks.
During World War II, Allied forces relied heavily on the Bofors gun for antiaircraft protection.
A restored Bofors gun is displayed at the military museum as a piece of wartime history.

Etymology

Named after Bofors, the Swedish arms manufacturer that developed the gun.
