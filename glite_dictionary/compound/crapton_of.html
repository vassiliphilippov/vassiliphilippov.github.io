crapton of

1. NOUN

/ ˈkræp.tʌn ʌv / (AmE), / ˈkræp.tən ɒv / (BrE)
an extremely large amount of something; a great quantity (informal, often humorous or exaggerated)
a very large amount (informal)
informal slang

Examples

We still have a crapton of cleaning to do before the guests arrive.
There’s a crapton of traffic on the way to the airport this morning.
She brought a crapton of snacks to share at the meeting.

Synset Examples

I bought a crapton of groceries for the party.
He has a crapton of work to finish this week.
There’s a crapton of data to analyze for the project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ton of - a very large amount of something

Etymology

Formed humorously from "crap" (slang for a lot, nonsense, or excrement) + "ton" (a weight unit indicating large quantity).
