queen triggerfish

1. NOUN

/ kwiːn ˈtrɪɡərˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / kwiːn ˈtrɪɡəˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
a brightly colored tropical fish (Balistes vetula) found in the Atlantic Ocean, notable for its deep body and elongated dorsal fin spines
a tropical Atlantic fish species

Examples

The queen triggerfish is admired for its striking blue and yellow coloration.
Divers often spot queen triggerfish near coral reefs in the Caribbean Sea.
A queen triggerfish can reach a length of up to 60 centimeters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: triggerfish - any fish of the family Balistidae with first dorsal spine that can be locked erect
