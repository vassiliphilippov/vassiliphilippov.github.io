Oncidium papilio

1. NOUN

/ ɑnˈsɪdiəm pəˈpiliˌoʊ / (AmE), / ɒnˈsɪdiəm pəˈpɪliəʊ / (BrE)
A species of orchid from South America and Trinidad, known for its large yellow and reddish-brown flowers; sometimes classified within the genus Oncidium.
a South American orchid species

Examples

The Oncidium papilio is prized by collectors for its unusual butterfly-shaped blooms.
We saw an Oncidium papilio in full flower at the botanical garden in Trinidad.
Botanists debate whether Oncidium papilio should be classified in a separate genus or within Oncidium.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Oncidium' (the genus name), 'papilio' meaning 'butterfly', referring to the flower's butterfly-like appearance.
