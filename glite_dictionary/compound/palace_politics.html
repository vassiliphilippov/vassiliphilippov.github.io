palace politics

1. NOUN

/ ˈpæləs ˈpɑlətɪks / (AmE), / ˈpæləs ˈpɒlɪtɪks / (BrE)
the complex and often secretive maneuvering for power or influence within an organization, especially among those at the top
secretive internal maneuvering for power

Examples

She quickly learned to navigate the palace politics that dominated the executive suite.
Many talented employees quit because they couldn't handle the palace politics at headquarters.
Rumors and alliances fueled the palace politics surrounding the company's new leadership.

Synset Examples

Navigating palace politics requires both cunning and charm.
The merger involved a lot of palace politics among board members.
He was well-versed in palace politics, making him a key advisor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: office politics - rivalry and maneuvering for power within a workplace

Etymology

From 'palace', referencing royal courts, and 'politics', reflecting intrigue for power.
