sea gooseberry

1. NOUN

/ si ˈɡusˌbɛri / (AmE), / siː ˈɡuːsbəri / (BrE)
A small marine animal (ctenophore) with a rounded, transparent body and rows of cilia used for swimming.
marine ctenophore with cilia

Examples

The sea gooseberry glides gracefully through the water, propelled by its shimmering cilia.
You can often spot a sea gooseberry floating near the shore during the summer months.
The aquarium's touch pool lets visitors observe a sea gooseberry up close.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ctenophore - marine invertebrate with comb-like cilia, also called comb jelly
