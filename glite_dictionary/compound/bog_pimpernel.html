bog pimpernel

1. NOUN

/ bɑɡ ˈpɪmpərˌnɛl / (AmE), / bɒɡ ˈpɪmpənɛl / (BrE)
a small creeping European herb with delicate pink flowers, typically found in wet or marshy ground
small European herb with pink flowers

Examples

The bog pimpernel thrives in damp meadows and marshes throughout Europe.
We identified the delicate pink flowers of the bog pimpernel during our field trip.
Conservationists are working to protect habitats crucial for the bog pimpernel and other wetland plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pimpernel - any of several plants of the genus Anagallis

Etymology

From 'bog' (wetland) and 'pimpernel', from Middle French *pimpernelle* (a type of plant).
