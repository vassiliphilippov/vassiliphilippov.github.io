Papa Doc

1. PROPN

/ ˈpɑːpə ˌdɑk / (AmE), / ˈpæpə ˌdɒk / (BrE)
the nickname of François Duvalier (1907–1971), who was the oppressive dictator of Haiti from 1957 to 1971
nickname of Haitian dictator François Duvalier

Examples

Many Haitians lived in fear under Papa Doc's brutal regime.
The legacy of Papa Doc shaped Haiti's politics for decades.
Papa Doc maintained his power through violence and oppressive tactics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Duvalier - François Duvalier, dictator of Haiti (1907–1971)
  • Degree Lower: Baby Doc - nickname of Jean-Claude Duvalier, son and successor of Papa Doc as Haitian dictator

Etymology

From French 'papa' (father) and 'doc', short for 'docteur' (doctor); Duvalier was originally a physician.
