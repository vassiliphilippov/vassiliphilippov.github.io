dent corn

1. NOUN

/ dɛnt kɔrn / (AmE), / dɛnt kɔːn / (BrE)
a variety of corn with kernels that contain both hard and soft starch, causing an indentation at the crown when mature
corn variety with indented kernels

Examples

Most animal feed in the Midwest is made from dent corn rather than sweet corn.
The characteristic indentation of dent corn appears as the kernels dry on the cob.
Farmers prefer dent corn for making cornmeal due to its high starch content.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: flint corn - corn variety with hard, glassy kernels
