hyponitrous acid

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪpoʊˈnɪtrəs ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪpəʊˈnɪtrəs ˈasɪd / (BrE)
a weak, explosive, white crystalline acid with the chemical formula H₂N₂O₂, known for its instability and rarity in pure form
a weak, explosive crystalline acid (H₂N₂O₂)
medical

Examples

The laboratory handled hyponitrous acid with great care due to its explosive potential.
Chemists rarely encounter pure hyponitrous acid because of its instability.
Decomposition of hyponitrous acid can release nitrogen gas explosively under certain conditions.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: nitrous acid - a weak inorganic acid (HNO₂) involved in chemical reactions
  • Degree Higher: nitric acid - a strong mineral acid (HNO₃)

Etymology

From hypo- (meaning 'under' or 'less') and nitrous, relating to nitrogen compounds.
