grey lemming

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡreɪ ˈlɛmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪ ˈlɛmɪŋ / (BrE)
a species of small rodent found in parts of northern Europe and Asia, known for its grey fur; a type of Old World lemming
a species of small grey-furred lemming

Examples

The grey lemming inhabits the tundra regions of northern Europe and Asia.
We observed a small group of grey lemmings scurrying across the grasslands.
Unlike the Norway lemming, the grey lemming is noted for its softer, more uniform fur.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lemming - any of various small, short-tailed rodents of the Arctic tundra
