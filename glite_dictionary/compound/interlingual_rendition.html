interlingual rendition

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪntərˈlɪŋɡwəl rɛnˈdɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈlɪŋɡwəl rɛnˈdɪʃən / (BrE)
a written version of a text that expresses the same meaning in a different language from the original
translation into another language

Examples

The novelist insisted on reviewing each interlingual rendition of her work to ensure accuracy.
Academic journals sometimes require an interlingual rendition of abstracts for international readers.
The interlingual rendition captured the humor of the original text remarkably well.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: translation - the process or product of translating text from one language to another

Etymology

From Latin 'inter' (between) + 'lingua' (language) and 'rendition' (an act of rendering or translating).
