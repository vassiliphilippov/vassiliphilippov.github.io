poison dogwood

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɔɪzən ˈdɔɡˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈpɔɪzən ˈdɒɡˌwʊd / (BrE)
A smooth North American swamp shrub with pinnate leaves, greenish flowers, and greenish-white berries, known for yielding an irritating oil.
North American shrub yielding irritating oil

Examples

Hikers are warned to avoid poison dogwood, as its oil can cause severe skin irritation.
The berries of poison dogwood look attractive but are highly toxic if ingested.
Botanists often confuse poison dogwood with other swamp shrubs due to their similar appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poison sumac - A related shrub causing similar skin irritation
