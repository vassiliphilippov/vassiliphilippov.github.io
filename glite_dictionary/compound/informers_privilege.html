informer’s privilege

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈfɔrmɚz ˈprɪvəlɪdʒ / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɔːməz ˈprɪvɪlɪdʒ / (BrE)
the right of the government to refuse to disclose the identity of a confidential informant in legal proceedings
government right to keep informer’s identity secret
legal

Examples

The police asserted informer’s privilege to protect the source’s identity during the trial.
Because of informer’s privilege, the witness’s name was never disclosed in court.
Lawyers debated whether informer’s privilege applied in this particular investigation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: privilege - a special right or advantage granted to a particular person or group
