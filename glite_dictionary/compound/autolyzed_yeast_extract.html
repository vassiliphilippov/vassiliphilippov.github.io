autolyzed yeast extract

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔːtəˌlaɪzd jist ɪkˌstrækt / (AmE), / ˈɔːtəˌlaɪzd jiːst ɪkˌstrakt / (BrE)
a flavor enhancer made by breaking down yeast cells with their own enzymes, used in processed foods to add savory taste
flavor enhancer made from yeast

Examples

The label listed autolyzed yeast extract among the ingredients for added flavor.
Vegan chefs often use autolyzed yeast extract to create savory depth in dishes.
People sensitive to MSG sometimes also avoid products containing autolyzed yeast extract.

Synset Examples

Many snacks use autolyzed yeast extract as a seasoning.
She avoided foods with autolyzed yeast extract for health.
The soup contained autolyzed yeast extract for flavor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yeast extract - a food additive or spread made from the cells of yeast

Etymology

From 'autolyze' (to break down via self-produced enzymes) and 'yeast extract'.
