Pollyannish perspective

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑːliˈænɪʃ pərˈspɛktɪv / (AmE), / ˌpɒliˈænɪʃ pəˈspɛktɪv / (BrE)
an excessively or unrealistically optimistic viewpoint, often ignoring potential problems or challenges
overly optimistic viewpoint

Examples

Despite the team’s repeated failures, his Pollyannish perspective remained unchanged.
She was criticized for her Pollyannish perspective when discussing the company’s declining sales.
A Pollyannish perspective can sometimes prevent people from preparing for real difficulties.

Synset Examples

His Pollyannish perspective often overlooks real challenges.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: optimism - hopefulness and confidence about the future
  • Antonym: pessimism - a tendency to see the worst aspect of things

Etymology

From 'Pollyanna', the optimistic character in Eleanor H. Porter's 1913 novel, plus the suffix '-ish', and 'perspective'.
