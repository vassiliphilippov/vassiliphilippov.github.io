acid dye

1. NOUN

/ ˈæsɪd ˌdaɪ / (AmE), / ˈæsɪd ˌdaɪ / (BrE)
a synthetic dye in which the coloring component is part of a negatively charged ion, used chiefly for dyeing wool, silk, and other protein fibers
dye with colorant as a negative ion

Examples

Protein fibers such as wool are commonly colored using acid dye for vibrant results.
The lab stocked a wide array of acid dyes for research into textile coloration.
Silk scarves dyed with an acid dye tend to retain their brightness over time.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: basic dye - dye in which the chromophore is part of a positive ion
