order Foraminifera

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdɚ fəˌræməˈnɪfərə / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə fəˌræmɪˈnɪfərə / (BrE)
A large order of chiefly marine protozoans with perforated shells, known as foraminifers.
an order of shell-bearing protozoans

Examples

The order Foraminifera includes many species used in geological dating.
Scientists discovered several new genera within the order Foraminifera during the expedition.
Fossils from the order Foraminifera help reconstruct past environments and climate change.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foraminifer - any unicellular organism of the order Foraminifera

Etymology

From Latin 'foramen' (hole, opening), referring to the perforated shells.
