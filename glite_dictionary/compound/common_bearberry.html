common bearberry

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːmən ˈberˌberi / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən ˈbeəˌbɛri / (BrE)
an evergreen, mat-forming shrub found in North America and northern Eurasia; it has small white flowers, red berries, and leaves that turn red in autumn
a mat-forming evergreen shrub with red berries

Examples

The common bearberry thrives in rocky soils and is often found in alpine regions.
In autumn, the leaves of the common bearberry turn a striking red color.
Wildlife such as bears and birds are known to eat the berries of the common bearberry.
