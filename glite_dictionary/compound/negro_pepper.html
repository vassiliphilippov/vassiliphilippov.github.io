negro pepper

1. NOUN

/ ˈniɡroʊ ˈpɛpər / (AmE), / ˈniːɡrəʊ ˈpɛpə / (BrE)
a tropical West African evergreen tree (Xylopia aethiopica), whose pungent aromatic seeds are used as a spice and in traditional medicine
tropical African tree; aromatic seeds used as spice

Examples

Many Nigerian dishes are flavored with negro pepper for its unique aroma.
The seeds of negro pepper are sometimes used in traditional medicine to treat colds.
He added a pinch of ground negro pepper to the soup to enhance its flavor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Xylopia aethiopica - the scientific name of negro pepper
  • Synonym: guinea pepper - another common name for the seeds of this tree

Etymology

'Negro' from Spanish and Portuguese, ultimately from Latin 'niger' (black), referring to the color of the seeds.
