leather poisoning

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛðɚ ˈpɔɪzənɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlɛðə ˈpɔɪzənɪŋ / (BrE)
an illness caused by exposure to leather materials or chemicals used in leather processing, often resulting in toxic reactions
illness from exposure to leather or its chemicals
medical

Examples

Working in the factory for years increased her risk of leather poisoning.
Cases of leather poisoning have declined thanks to improved safety regulations.
Symptoms of leather poisoning may include skin irritation, nausea, and respiratory problems.

Synset Examples

He suffered from leather poisoning after handling the material.
Avoiding chemicals can prevent leather poisoning in workers.
