foxtail grass

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɑks.teɪl ˌɡræs / (AmE), / ˈfɒks.teɪl ˌɡrɑːs / (BrE)
A type of grass with a dense, bristly appearance, commonly found in sandy or moist areas along the eastern and southeastern coasts of the United States.
bristly grass found in US coastal regions

Examples

On our walk along the dunes, we saw plenty of foxtail grass swaying in the breeze.
The field was overgrown with foxtail grass after a wet spring season.
Gardeners often remove foxtail grass to prevent it from spreading to their lawns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foxtail - a plant with a bushy spike resembling a fox's tail

Etymology

Named for its bushy flower heads that resemble a fox's tail.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɑks.teɪl ɡræs / (AmE), / ˈfɒks.teɪl ɡrɑːs / (BrE)
any of various grasses, especially of the genera Alopecurus and Setaria, that have dense, brushlike flowering spikes resembling a fox's tail
grass with brushlike flowering spike

Examples

Several fields were overrun with foxtail grass after the rainy season.
Farmers try to eliminate foxtail grass because it can be harmful to livestock.
The dense spikes of foxtail grass catch the light beautifully at sunset.

Etymology

Named for the resemblance of its seed head to a fox's tail.
