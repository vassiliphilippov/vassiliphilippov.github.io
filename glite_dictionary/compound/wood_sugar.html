wood sugar

1. NOUN

/ wʊd ˈʃʌɡɚ / (AmE), / wʊd ˈʃʌɡə / (BrE)
a sugar obtained from wood or straw, especially xylose, used as a sweetener in foods for diabetics
sugar from wood or straw (xylose)

Examples

Foods sweetened with wood sugar are often marketed to people with diabetes.
Scientists extract wood sugar from straw for use in alternative sweeteners.
Some chewing gums contain wood sugar instead of regular table sugar.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: xylose - a sugar of the pentose class present in wood and straw
