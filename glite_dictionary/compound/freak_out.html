freak out

1. NOUN

/ ˈfriːk aʊt / (AmE), / ˈfriːk aʊt / (BrE)
A wild or intense hallucination or delusion, especially one induced by a hallucinogenic drug.
wild hallucination or delusion (drugs)
slang

Examples

The LSD gave him a freak out he would never forget.
She described her freak out as a terrifying but strangely enlightening experience.
Back in the 60s, freak outs were common among experimental drug users.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trip - an experience of hallucinatory effects due to drugs

Etymology

From 'freak' (slang for drug user or enthusiast) + 'out' (experience strongly); popularized in 1960s counterculture.
