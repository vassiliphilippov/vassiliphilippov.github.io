simple pistil

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪmpəl ˈpɪstl̩ / (AmE), / ˈsɪmpəl ˈpɪstl̩ / (BrE)
a pistil in a flower that consists of only one carpel
pistil with one carpel (botany)

Examples

A simple pistil can be found in many species of the buttercup family.
Each simple pistil develops from a single carpel, making it distinct from compound pistils.
The flower’s reproductive structure includes either a simple pistil or a compound pistil, depending on the species.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: compound pistil - a pistil formed from two or more fused carpels
