potassium hydrogen carbonate

1. NOUN

/ poʊˌtæsiəm haɪˌdrɑdʒən kɑrˈbɑneɪt / (AmE), / pəˌtæsiəm haɪˌdrɒdʒən kɑːˈbɒneɪt / (BrE)
a white crystalline salt (KHCO3) used in baking powder and as an antacid
a crystalline salt used in baking and medicine
medical

Examples

Potassium hydrogen carbonate is often used as a leavening agent in gluten-free baking recipes.
The doctor recommended potassium hydrogen carbonate tablets to help neutralize excess stomach acid.
Unlike sodium bicarbonate, potassium hydrogen carbonate does not add sodium to your diet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bicarbonate of soda - another term for sodium bicarbonate, a similar compound used in baking
  • Degree Lower: potassium carbonate - related chemical compound used as a drying agent and mild base

Etymology

Potassium from New Latin 'potassium', hydrogen from Greek 'hydro-', and carbonate from Latin 'carbo' (coal).
