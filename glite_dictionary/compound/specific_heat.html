specific heat

1. NOUN

/ spəˈsɪfɪk hiːt / (AmE), / spɪˈsɪfɪk hiːt / (BrE)
the amount of heat required to raise the temperature of one gram of a substance by one degree Celsius
amount of heat to raise temperature of a substance

Examples

Water has a high specific heat, allowing it to absorb lots of energy before its temperature rises.
The specific heat of copper is lower than that of aluminum.
Engineers must consider specific heat when designing cooling systems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heat capacity - the amount of heat needed to raise the temperature of a body by one degree Celsius

Etymology

From Latin specificus (constituting a kind or species) and English heat.
