test period

1. NOUN

/ tɛst ˈpɪriəd / (AmE), / tɛst ˈpɪəriəd / (BrE)
a period of time during which someone or something is tested to evaluate performance, suitability, or quality
a set time for testing someone or something

Examples

The new employee was hired on a test period to assess her suitability for the role.
Students are often given a test period to try out advanced classes.
After the test period, the company decided to permanently adopt the new software.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trial period - a period during which a person or thing is tested to assess suitability or quality
  • Synonym: probationary period - a period of testing a person's suitability for a job or role
