ash-heap

1. NOUN

/ ˈæʃˌhiːp / (AmE), / ˈæʃˌhiːp / (BrE)
A pile or mound of ashes, typically the residue left from burning.
a pile of ashes

Examples

After the fire, all that remained of the old barn was an ash-heap in the field.
He tossed the burned logs onto the ash-heap behind the cottage.
The historian described the forgotten empire as consigned to the ash-heap of history.

Synset Examples

The garden was littered with an ash-heap from the fire.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ash pile - A pile or heap of ashes
