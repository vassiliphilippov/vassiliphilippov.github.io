occupation licence

1. NOUN

/ əˈkeɪpɪʃən ˈlaɪsəns / (AmE), / əˈkjʊpəʃən ˈlaɪsəns / (BrE)
an official permit granting a person the legal right to work in a particular occupation
official permit to pursue a specific occupation
legal

Examples

You must obtain an occupation licence before working as a real estate agent.
The government revoked his occupation licence after several violations were discovered.
Many professions require an occupation licence to ensure public safety and standards.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: license - official permission or permit to do something
