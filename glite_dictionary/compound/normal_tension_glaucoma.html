normal tension glaucoma

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɔrml ˈtɛnʃən ɡloʊˈkoʊmə / (AmE), / ˈnɔːm(ə)l ˈtɛnʃən ɡləʊˈkəʊmə / (BrE)
A type of glaucoma in which optic nerve damage and vision loss occur despite normal intraocular pressure.
glaucoma with normal eye pressure
medical

Examples

Many patients with normal tension glaucoma experience progressive vision loss even when their eye pressure is within the normal range.
Early detection of normal tension glaucoma is challenging because it does not involve elevated intraocular pressure.
The doctor explained that normal tension glaucoma can still damage the optic nerve despite normal eye pressure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glaucoma - an eye disease marked by increased intraocular pressure
