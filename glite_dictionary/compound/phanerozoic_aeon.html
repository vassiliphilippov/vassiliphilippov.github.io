Phanerozoic aeon

1. NOUN

/ ˌfænəroʊˈzoʊɪk ˈiːɒn / (AmE), / ˌfænərəʊˈzəʊɪk ˈiːɒn / (BrE)
the geologic era from about 540 million years ago to the present, characterized by the abundance of animal and plant life
major geologic era of abundant life

Examples

The Phanerozoic aeon encompasses the majority of fossil records found by paleontologists.
During the Phanerozoic aeon, life on Earth diversified dramatically, including the rise of mammals and flowering plants.
Geologists divide the Phanerozoic aeon into three main eras: Paleozoic, Mesozoic, and Cenozoic.

Etymology

From Greek 'phaneros' (visible) + 'zoion' (animal), referring to visible life.
