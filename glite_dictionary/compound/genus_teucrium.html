genus Teucrium

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒiːnəs tuˈkriːəm / (AmE), / ˌdʒiːnəs tjuːˈkriːəm / (BrE)
a large, widely distributed genus of perennial herbs, shrubs, or subshrubs, native from the Mediterranean region to western Asia
a plant genus including herbs and shrubs

Examples

The genus Teucrium includes several plants commonly used in herbal medicine.
Researchers discovered a new species within the genus Teucrium in western Asia.
Members of the genus Teucrium are known for their aromatic leaves and diverse habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Teucrium - a plant within the genus Teucrium

Etymology

Genus name from Greek *teukrion* (a type of herb), Latinized to Teucrium.
