glibertarian approach

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡlɪbərˈtɛriən əˈproʊtʃ / (AmE), / ˌɡlɪbəˈtɛəriən əˈprəʊtʃ / (BrE)
A satirical or dismissive term for a political philosophy that superficially emphasizes liberty, often without serious consideration of the complexities involved.
superficial libertarian philosophy
informal slang

Examples

Some critics argue that the glibertarian approach ignores the realities of economic inequality.
The candidate’s glibertarian approach was popular among younger voters looking for simple solutions.
Many philosophers reject the glibertarian approach as lacking substance or real commitment to freedom.

Synset Examples

The glibertarian approach focuses on individual freedom.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: libertarian - a person who believes in free will and minimal government intervention

Etymology

Blend of 'glib' (meaning smooth or insincere) and 'libertarian.'
