aerial ladder

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛriəl ˈlædər / (AmE), / ˈeəriəl ˈlædə / (BrE)
a long, mechanically extendible ladder mounted on a fire truck, used to reach high places during firefighting or rescue operations
mechanically extendible ladder on fire truck

Examples

The firefighters quickly raised the aerial ladder to rescue people trapped on the third floor.
An aerial ladder allows crews to direct water streams from above during large building fires.
Not every fire truck is equipped with an aerial ladder, so mutual aid was requested.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fire truck - vehicle equipped to carry firefighters and firefighting equipment
  • Degree Lower: ladder - a structure for climbing, consisting of two side pieces joined by rungs

Etymology

Formed from 'aerial', relating to air or height, and 'ladder'.
