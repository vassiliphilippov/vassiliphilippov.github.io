observed fire

1. NOUN

/ əbˈzɝvd ˈfaɪɚ / (AmE), / əbˈzɜːvd ˈfaɪə / (BrE)
artillery or gunfire that is adjusted based on visual observation of where the shots land
artillery fire adjusted by observation

Examples

The commander ordered observed fire to correct the artillery's aim.
Using observed fire, the team was able to quickly adjust their targeting.
Safety protocols require that observed fire be used when friendly forces are near the target area.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unobserved fire - fire delivered without observation or adjustment
