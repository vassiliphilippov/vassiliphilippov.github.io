white tie

1. NOUN

/ ˈwaɪt taɪ / (AmE), / ˈwaɪt taɪ / (BrE)
a very formal style of evening dress for men, characterized by a white bow tie, tailcoat, and other formal accessories, worn at special occasions
formal men's evening dress with white bow tie

Examples

Guests are required to wear white tie for the gala dinner at the palace.
He looked distinguished in his white tie and tails at the charity ball.
Only the most prestigious events still call for a strict white tie dress code.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: black tie - a semiformal style of men's evening dress featuring a black bow tie and dinner jacket

2. NOUN

/ waɪt taɪ / (AmE), / waɪt taɪ / (BrE)
a formal dress code for evening events, requiring men to wear a black tailcoat, matching trousers, white formal shirt, white vest, and a white bow tie
the most formal evening dress code for men

Examples

The invitation specified white tie, so he rented a tailcoat and bought a crisp white bow tie.
Events at the royal palace usually require white tie attire for all male guests.
Few people today own a white tie ensemble, as such occasions are quite rare.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: black tie - a slightly less formal evening dress code
