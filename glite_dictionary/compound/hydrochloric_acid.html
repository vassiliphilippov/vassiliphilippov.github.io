hydrochloric acid

1. NOUN

/ haɪdroʊˈklɔrɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / hʌɪdrəʊˈklɒrɪk ˈasɪd / (BrE)
a strongly corrosive acid formed by dissolving hydrogen chloride gas in water, commonly used in chemistry and industry
a strong corrosive acid (HCl solution)
medical

Examples

Hydrochloric acid is commonly used in laboratories to adjust the pH of solutions.
The stomach produces hydrochloric acid to help digest food.
Accidental spills of hydrochloric acid must be handled with extreme care due to its corrosive nature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: muriatic acid - archaic name for hydrochloric acid
  • Degree Lower: sulfuric acid - another common strong acid, H2SO4

Etymology

From hydro- (water) and chloric (from chlorine).
