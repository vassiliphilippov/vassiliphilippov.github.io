beta test

1. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪ.tə tɛst / (AmE), / ˈbiː.tə tɛst / (BrE)
a trial of an experimental product, such as software, conducted by external users after internal testing and before its official release
trial test by external users before release

Examples

Our company offered a beta test of the new app to selected customers.
The feedback collected during the beta test led to many improvements before launch.
She participated in the beta test for the upcoming video game release.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: alpha test - the first internal testing phase of a product

Etymology

From the Greek letter beta (β), used in software development to denote the second stage of testing, following the 'alpha' phase.
