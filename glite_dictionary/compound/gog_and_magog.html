Gog and Magog

1. NOUN

/ ɡɑɡ ənd məˈɡɑɡ / (AmE), / ɡɒɡ ənd məˈɡɒɡ / (BrE)
(in the Bible) the names of enemies of God's people who, according to prophecy, will wage war against God at the end of the world.
biblical enemies of God's people

Examples

In Christian eschatology, Gog and Magog are seen as symbols of ultimate evil opposed to God.
The story of Gog and Magog appears both in the Book of Ezekiel and the Book of Revelation.
Many paintings depict Gog and Magog in dramatic scenes of battle at the end times.

Synset Examples

in the Book of Ezekiel, Gog is a ruler from the land of Magog but in the Book of Revelation Gog and Magog are nations under the rule of Satan

Etymology

From Hebrew 'Gog' (name of a leader) and 'Magog' (a land or people in biblical tradition).
