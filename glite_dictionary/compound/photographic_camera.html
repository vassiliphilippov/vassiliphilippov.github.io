photographic camera

1. NOUN

/ fəˌtoʊɡrəfɪk ˈkæmərə / (AmE), / fəˌtəʊɡrəfɪk ˈkæmərə / (BrE)
a device for taking photographs, typically consisting of a lightproof box with a lens and a film or sensor that records the image
device for taking photographs

Examples

My grandfather still owns a vintage photographic camera that uses film instead of digital storage.
The museum displayed an early photographic camera from the nineteenth century in its technology exhibit.
He brought his photographic camera to capture images of the wildlife during their safari.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: camera - a device for recording images, either on film or digitally

Etymology

From 'photographic' relating to photography and 'camera', from Latin 'camera' meaning 'chamber, vault'.
