gray polypody

1. NOUN

/ ɡreɪ ˈpɑlɪˌpoʊdi / (AmE), / ɡreɪ ˈpɒlɪˌpəʊdi / (BrE)
A fern that grows on rocks or tree trunks, with fronds that are greyish and scurfy underneath, found in the Americas and South Africa.
a fern with greyish, scurfy fronds

Examples

We spotted a gray polypody growing on the old stone wall near the river.
The underside of the gray polypody's fronds felt distinctly scurfy to the touch.
Botanists documented the spread of gray polypody across several regions in South America.
