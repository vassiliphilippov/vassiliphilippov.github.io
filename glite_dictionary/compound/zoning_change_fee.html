zoning change fee

1. NOUN

/ ˈzoʊ.nɪŋ tʃeɪndʒ fiː / (AmE), / ˈzəʊ.nɪŋ tʃeɪndʒ fiː / (BrE)
a one-time fee charged, typically by a local authority, for processing a property rezoning request
fee for property rezoning request

Examples

The developer paid a zoning change fee to have the land approved for commercial use.
A zoning change fee is required before your application can be reviewed by the county council.
Property owners sometimes object to the high zoning change fee imposed on rezoning requests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: filing fee - a fee paid when submitting a document for official purposes
