Niagara River

1. PROPN

/ naɪˈæɡrə ˈrɪvɚ / (AmE), / naɪˈæɡərə ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
a river that flows from Lake Erie to Lake Ontario, forming part of the border between Ontario, Canada, and New York, USA
river between Lake Erie and Lake Ontario

Examples

The Niagara River is famous for its stunning waterfalls and scenic beauty.
Many migratory birds follow the Niagara River during their seasonal journeys.
The border between Canada and the United States runs along the Niagara River in this region.

Etymology

Named after an Iroquoian word, likely meaning 'thunder of waters'.
