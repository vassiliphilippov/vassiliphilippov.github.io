smut fungus

1. NOUN

/ smʌt ˈfʌŋɡəs / (AmE), / smʌt ˈfʌŋɡəs / (BrE)
A fungus of the order Ustilaginales, typically parasitic on plants, causing smut disease, especially in cereal crops.
fungus parasitic on plants; causes smut disease

Examples

The corn crop was ruined by a smut fungus infection that spread rapidly through the field.
Researchers are studying the life cycle of smut fungus to develop resistant strains of wheat.
Black powdery spores, a sign of smut fungus, appeared on several stalks in the greenhouse.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rust fungus - a fungus causing rust disease in plants
