bitter-bark

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɪt̬ɚ ˌbɑrk / (AmE), / ˈbɪtə ˌbɑːk / (BrE)
an ornamental shrub or small tree native to swampy areas of the southwestern United States, with large pink or white sepals, also known for yielding Georgia bark used to treat fever
ornamental tree; source of Georgia bark

Examples

The bitter-bark is often planted in gardens for its attractive flowers.
Early settlers used bitter-bark as a remedy for fever and other ailments.
You can recognize bitter-bark by its distinctive large sepals and smooth bark.
