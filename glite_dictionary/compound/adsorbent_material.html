adsorbent material

1. NOUN

/ ædˈzɔrbənt məˈtɪriəl / (AmE), / adˈzɔːb(ə)nt məˈtɪəriəl / (BrE)
a material that has the ability to adsorb another substance onto its surface
material that can adsorb substances

Examples

Activated carbon is a commonly used adsorbent material in water purification systems.
The adsorbent material in the mask captures harmful particles from the air.
Scientists tested several adsorbent materials to remove contaminants from the chemical solution.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: absorbent material - material that absorbs rather than adsorbs

Etymology

From 'adsorbent,' based on Latin 'ad-' (to) and 'sorbere' (to suck in), and 'material' from Latin 'materialis'.
