English yew

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ juː / (AmE), / ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ juː / (BrE)
a species of yew tree native to Europe, known for its longevity and slow growth; Taxus baccata
a long-lived European yew tree species

Examples

The ancient English yew in the churchyard is believed to be over a thousand years old.
Many traditional longbows were crafted from English yew wood due to its strength and flexibility.
Botanists study the English yew for its remarkable resistance to disease and pests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yew - an evergreen tree with needle-like leaves and red berries
