mallee fowl

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæli faʊl / (AmE), / ˈmæli faʊl / (BrE)
a large Australian ground-dwelling bird (Leipoa ocellata) that builds large mounds of sand to incubate its eggs using natural heat
Australian bird that incubates eggs in sandy mounds

Examples

The mallee fowl constructs an enormous mound to incubate its eggs.
Scientists are monitoring mallee fowl populations to help conserve the species.
We spotted a mallee fowl scratching in the scrub during our outback trip.

Etymology

From Australian English 'mallee' (a type of eucalyptus scrub) and 'fowl'.
