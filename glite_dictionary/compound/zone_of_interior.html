zone of interior

1. NOUN

/ zoʊn əv ɪnˈtɪriər / (AmE), / zəʊn əv ɪnˈtɪəriə / (BrE)
the area within a theater of war that is not included in the theater of operations, typically used for support, administration, and logistics.
military term: part of war theater not on the front

Examples

Supplies were stored in the zone of interior before being transported to the battlefield.
The army maintained its headquarters in the zone of interior for better communication and support.
Reinforcements were dispatched from the zone of interior to the front lines as needed.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: theater of operations - the area where active combat operations take place in a war
