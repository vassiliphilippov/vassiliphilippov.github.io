bone-shaking fear

1. NOUN

/ ˈboʊn ˌʃeɪkɪŋ fɪr / (AmE), / ˈbəʊn ˌʃeɪkɪŋ fɪə / (BrE)
a feeling of extremely intense and overwhelming fear that seems to affect one physically
extremely intense fear

Examples

When the house shook during the earthquake, a bone-shaking fear gripped everyone inside.
He had faced danger before, but nothing compared to the bone-shaking fear he felt then.
The dark alley filled me with a sudden, bone-shaking fear I couldn't control.

Synset Examples

The news left us with bone-shaking fear.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: terror - a feeling of extreme fear
  • Synonym: dread - intense fear or apprehension
  • Degree Lower: anxiety - a feeling of worry, nervousness, or unease
