porous concrete

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɔːrəs kənˈkriːt / (AmE), / ˈpɔːrəs kɒnˈkriːt / (BrE)
a lightweight, precast concrete building material with a high volume of air voids, providing structural support as well as insulation and resistance to fire and mold
lightweight, insulating concrete material

Examples

The builders chose porous concrete for the new facility to improve insulation and reduce overall weight.
Using porous concrete helps manage stormwater runoff by allowing water to seep through its surface.
New regulations encourage the use of porous concrete in parking lots to minimize urban flooding.

Synset Examples

Better thermal efficiency of AAC makes it suitable for use in areas with extreme temperature as it eliminates need for separate materials for construction and insulation leading to faster construction and savings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aerated concrete - concrete with an expanded structure due to air or gas bubbles, also known as autoclaved aerated concrete
  • Antonym: normal concrete - standard dense concrete used for building structures
