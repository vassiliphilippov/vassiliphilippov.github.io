-miler

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪlər / (AmE), / ˈmaɪlə / (BrE)
used after a number to form a noun meaning something that is a specified number of miles in length, especially a race or journey
something with a specified number of miles (in combinations)

Examples

The charity run was a challenging 10-miler through the countryside.
He completed the Appalachian Trail section as a three-day 20-miler.
This weekend, the club is organizing a 100-miler for experienced cyclists.

Synset Examples

the race was a 30-miler

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mile - a unit of distance equal to 1,609 metres or 1,760 yards

Etymology

Formed from 'mile' + '-er' suffix, denoting something characterized by a specified number of miles.
