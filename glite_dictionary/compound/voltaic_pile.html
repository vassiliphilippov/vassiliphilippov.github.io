voltaic pile

1. NOUN

/ voʊlˈteɪ.ɪk paɪl / (AmE), / vɒlˈteɪ.ɪk paɪl / (BrE)
an early type of electric battery consisting of a series of voltaic cells stacked together, invented by Alessandro Volta
early battery of stacked cells

Examples

The invention of the voltaic pile marked a major breakthrough in the study of electricity.
Museum visitors can see a replica of the original voltaic pile built by Volta in 1800.
Unlike modern batteries, the voltaic pile used alternating discs of zinc and copper.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: battery - a device for storing and providing electricity

Etymology

Named after Alessandro Volta, Italian physicist, and the arrangement of stacked plates ('pile').
