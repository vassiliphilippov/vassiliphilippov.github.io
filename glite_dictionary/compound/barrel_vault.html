barrel vault

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛrəl vɔlt / (AmE), / ˈbærəl vɔːlt / (BrE)
an architectural element consisting of a single continuous arch, forming a semi-cylindrical ceiling or roof.
single continuous arched ceiling

Examples

The cathedral's nave was covered by a barrel vault, giving the space an impressive height.
Ancient Roman architects often used the barrel vault in their public buildings.
The barrel vault design efficiently distributes weight across the walls.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vault - an arched structure forming a ceiling or roof
