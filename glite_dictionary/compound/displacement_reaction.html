displacement reaction

1. NOUN

/ dɪsˈpleɪsmənt riˈækʃən / (AmE), / dɪsˈpleɪsmənt rɪˈækʃən / (BrE)
a chemical reaction in which a more reactive element displaces a less reactive element from its compound
type of chemical reaction
medical

Examples

In a displacement reaction, zinc metal replaces copper from copper sulfate solution.
The demonstration of a displacement reaction is a common experiment in high school chemistry.
A classic displacement reaction is when iron nails are placed in copper sulfate solution and copper is deposited.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: redox reaction - chemical reaction involving the transfer of electrons (oxidation and reduction)

Etymology

From 'displacement' + 'reaction'; 'displacement' from Latin 'dis-' (apart) + 'placēre' (to place).
