terrestrial guidance

1. NOUN

/ təˈrɛstriəl ˈɡaɪdəns / (AmE), / təˈrɛstriəl ˈɡaɪdəns / (BrE)
a method of controlling the flight of a missile using systems that respond to the strength and direction of the earth's gravitational field
missile guidance using Earth's gravity

Examples

Modern missiles often rely on terrestrial guidance combined with other advanced systems for high-precision targeting.
The effectiveness of terrestrial guidance depends on accurate measurement of the earth's gravitational field.
Engineers developed improved terrestrial guidance techniques to enhance missile reliability.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inertial guidance - a navigation technique using motion sensors and gyroscopes
