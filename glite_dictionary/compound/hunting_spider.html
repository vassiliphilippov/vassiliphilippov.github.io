hunting spider

1. NOUN

/ ˈhʌntɪŋ ˌspaɪdər / (AmE), / ˈhʌntɪŋ ˌspaɪdə / (BrE)
a spider that actively hunts and captures its prey instead of trapping it in a web
spider that hunts prey without a web

Examples

The hunting spider relies on its speed and agility to chase down its prey.
Unlike web-making spiders, the hunting spider roams the ground in search of insects.
Many people are surprised to learn that a hunting spider does not build a web at all.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wolf spider - a family of robust and agile hunting spiders
  • Antonym: web spider - a spider that constructs webs to catch prey
