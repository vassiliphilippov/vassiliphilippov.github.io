cotton wool

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑt̬(ə)n wʊl / (AmE), / ˈkɒt(ə)n wʊl / (BrE)
Soft, fluffy fibers from the cotton plant, usually in their raw or processed state, often used for medical, cosmetic, or cleaning purposes.
soft raw fibers from the cotton plant

Examples

The nurse cleaned the wound gently with a ball of cotton wool.
She packed some cotton wool into the box to protect the fragile vase.
Cotton wool can be used to remove makeup or apply lotion to the skin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cotton - soft white fiber from cotton plants, used as textile or material
  • Synonym: wool - soft curly or wavy hair forming the coat of a sheep or other animal
