Adi Granth

1. PROPN

/ ˌɑːdi ˈɡrʌnθ / (AmE), / ˌɑːdi ˈɡrʌnθ / (BrE)
The principal sacred scripture of Sikhism, comprising hymns, poetry, and teachings of the first five Sikh Gurus.
principal sacred text of Sikhism

Examples

The Adi Granth plays a central role in Sikh religious ceremonies and daily worship.
Many Sikhs visit the Golden Temple to hear recitations from the Adi Granth.
The teachings in the Adi Granth emphasize devotion, equality, and truthfulness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Guru Granth Sahib - the final and eternal Sikh Guru and scripture, expanded version of Adi Granth

Etymology

From Sanskrit: 'adi' (first, original) + 'granth' (book, text).
