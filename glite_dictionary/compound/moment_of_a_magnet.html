moment of a magnet

1. NOUN

/ ˈmoʊmənt əv ə ˈmægnət / (AmE), / ˈməʊmənt əv ə ˈmægnɪt / (BrE)
the torque that a magnet or magnetic dipole experiences in a magnetic field; a measure of the strength and orientation of a magnet's ability to produce a force or torque.
magnetic property indicating torque in a field

Examples

The moment of a magnet determines how it will align in an external magnetic field.
Scientists measured the moment of a magnet to analyze the properties of the new material.
If the moment of a magnet is large, the torque in the field will also be significant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: magnetic moment - the measure of the strength and orientation of a magnetic source
  • Synonym: torque - a force that causes rotation
