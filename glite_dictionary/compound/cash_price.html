cash price

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæʃ ˌpraɪs / (AmE), / ˈkæʃ ˌpraɪs / (BrE)
the amount of money required to purchase a commodity for immediate delivery in the spot market
price for immediate spot market delivery

Examples

The cash price of gold fluctuates daily based on market demand and supply.
She checked the cash price for wheat before making a purchase from the spot market.
The company's profits were impacted by the falling cash price of crude oil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spot price - the current market price for immediate delivery of a commodity
