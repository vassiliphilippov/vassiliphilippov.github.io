big hand

1. NOUN

/ bɪɡ hænd / (AmE), / bɪɡ hand / (BrE)
the longer hand on a clock or watch that points to the minutes
minute hand on a clock

Examples

Please move the big hand to the twelve when resetting the clock.
The big hand is pointing at the six, so it's half past the hour.
Children often learn to tell time by first recognizing the big hand and the small hand.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: minute hand - the hand on a clock that indicates the minutes
  • Antonym: small hand - the shorter hand on a clock that shows the hours

2. NOUN

/ bɪɡ hænd / (AmE), / bɪɡ hand / (BrE)
enthusiastic applause or praise, especially as a sign of approval for a performance
enthusiastic applause or praise

Examples

Let's give the band a big hand for their fantastic performance tonight!
The speaker received a big hand at the end of her inspiring lecture.
After the show, the children got a big hand from the entire audience.

Synset Examples

Give him a big hand for his amazing performance!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: round of applause - a period of applause from an audience
  • Synonym: ovation - an enthusiastic show of approval by clapping hands
