Chinese Shan

1. NOUN

/ ˌtʃaɪˈniz ʃæn / (AmE), / ˌtʃaɪˈniːz ʃæn / (BrE)
a branch of the Tai languages spoken in China, mainly in Southwest Yunnan Province and its surrounding areas
Tai language spoken in Southwest Yunnan, China

Examples

The Chinese Shan language is spoken by a minority population in Yunnan Province.
Linguists have studied the phonological features of Chinese Shan in comparison with other Tai languages.
Efforts are being made to preserve Chinese Shan among younger generations in Southwest China.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Shan - member of a Tai-speaking ethnic group in Southeast Asia, or their language
