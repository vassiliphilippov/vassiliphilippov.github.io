welfare Cadillac

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɛlˌfɛr ˈkædɪlæk / (AmE), / ˈwɛlˌfeə ˈkædɪlæk / (BrE)
A derogatory term for an expensive car, especially a Cadillac, allegedly owned by someone receiving welfare benefits, used to imply misuse or fraud of social assistance.
slang for luxury car owned by a welfare recipient
informal slang offensive american

Examples

The issue of the so-called welfare Cadillac is often brought up in debates about social spending.
Critics argue that stories about people buying a welfare Cadillac are exaggerated and misleading.
He dismissed the stereotype of the welfare Cadillac as harmful and untrue.

Synset Examples

She joked about the welfare Cadillac parked outside her house.
The term welfare Cadillac often stirs up heated debates.

Etymology

From 'welfare' (social assistance) + 'Cadillac' (a luxury car brand).
