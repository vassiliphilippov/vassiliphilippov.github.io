Loch Ness monster

1. NOUN

/ lɑk nɛs ˈmɑnstɚ / (AmE), / lɒx nɛs ˈmɒnstə / (BrE)
a large, legendary aquatic creature said to inhabit Loch Ness in Scotland, often depicted as resembling a plesiosaur or serpent
legendary creature of Loch Ness, Scotland

Examples

Many tourists visit Scotland in hopes of catching a glimpse of the Loch Ness monster.
Some scientists believe the Loch Ness monster is just a legend fueled by folklore and misidentification.
Stories about the Loch Ness monster have appeared in newspapers since the 1930s.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Nessie - popular nickname for the Loch Ness monster

Etymology

From Loch Ness, a lake in Scotland, and monster, meaning a large and frightening imaginary creature.
