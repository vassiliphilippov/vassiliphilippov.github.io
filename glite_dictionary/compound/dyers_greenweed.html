dyer's greenweed

1. NOUN

/ ˈdaɪɚz ˈɡrinˌwid / (AmE), / ˈdaɪəz ˈɡriːnwɪd / (BrE)
a small Eurasian shrub (Genista tinctoria) with clusters of yellow flowers, used for producing yellow dye and sometimes grown ornamentally
yellow-flowered shrub used for dye

Examples

Historically, dyer's greenweed was cultivated for its yellow dye in medieval Europe.
The bright yellow blooms of dyer's greenweed make it a popular choice for cottage gardens.
Invasive patches of dyer's greenweed have been reported in some regions of North America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: greenweed - any plant of the genus Genista, shrubs with yellow flowers

Etymology

The name refers to its traditional use by dyers; 'greenweed' is a common name for shrubs in the genus Genista.
