garrison cap

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡærəsən kæp / (AmE), / ˈɡærɪsən kap / (BrE)
A flat, foldable, wedge-shaped wool or cotton cap, typically worn as part of a military or service uniform.
foldable military cap

Examples

Each soldier wore a garrison cap tilted slightly to one side during the parade.
The museum display included several World War II uniforms with a matching garrison cap.
He tucked his garrison cap into his belt as he entered the mess hall.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: side cap - A similar type of foldable cap worn as part of military uniform
  • Degree Lower: beret - A soft, round, flat-crowned cap, also worn in military uniforms

Etymology

From 'garrison' (a military post), reflecting its use by soldiers.
