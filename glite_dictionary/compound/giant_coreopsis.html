giant coreopsis

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒaɪənt kɔːˈɹɑːpsɪs / (AmE), / ˈdʒaɪənt kɔːˈrɪpsɪs / (BrE)
a large shrub of coastal southern California, with feathery leaves and clusters of large yellow flower heads
large yellow-flowered Californian shrub

Examples

In spring, the giant coreopsis transforms coastal hills with its bright yellow blossoms.
Gardeners value the giant coreopsis for its unique feathery foliage and its drought resistance.
During their hike, they stopped to photograph the blooming giant coreopsis along the trail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coreopsis - a genus of plants with daisy-like yellow flowers

Etymology

From Latin 'coreopsis', referring to the genus name, with 'giant' for its size distinction.
