bissextile year

1. NOUN

/ bɪˈsɛkstaɪl jɪr / (AmE), / bɪˈsɛkstaɪl jɪə / (BrE)
A year in the calendar with an extra day added to February, making it 366 days long; a leap year.
leap year with extra day in February

Examples

The next bissextile year will occur in 2028, adding a day to February.
Traditionally, some cultures observe special customs on the extra day in a bissextile year.
A bissextile year helps synchronize our calendar with Earth's orbit around the sun.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leap year - a year with an extra day added to February; same as bissextile year

Etymology

From Latin 'bissextilis annus' (year having a sixth day twice), referring to the extra day in leap years in the Roman calendar.
