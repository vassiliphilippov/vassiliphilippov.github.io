wild licorice

1. NOUN

/ waɪld ˈlɪkərɪʃ / (AmE), / waɪld ˈlɪkərɪʃ / (BrE)
A North American plant resembling true licorice, with a root used for its flavor and medicinal properties.
North American plant similar to licorice

Examples

The root of wild licorice has been used in traditional Native American medicine.
I found wild licorice growing along the riverbank during my hike.
Herbalists often use wild licorice as a natural remedy for coughs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: licorice - sweet-tasting root used in flavoring, especially from Glycyrrhiza glabra

2. NOUN

/ waɪld lɪˈkɔrɪʃ / (AmE), / waɪld lɪˈkɒrɪʃ / (BrE)
a European perennial plant (Glycyrrhiza echinata) with roots that have a sweet, licorice-like flavor, related to cultivated licorice
European perennial plant, relative of licorice

Examples

The herbalist showed us a patch of wild licorice growing near the riverbank.
Farmers sometimes cultivate wild licorice for its medicinal root properties.
Unlike common licorice, wild licorice thrives in drier, more temperate regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: licorice - sweet-tasting root or its extract

3. NOUN

/ waɪld lɪˈkɒrɪʃ / (AmE), / waɪld lɪˈkɒrɪʃ / (BrE)
a type of bedstraw with sweet-tasting roots, used in some regions as a substitute for true licorice
bedstraw plant with sweet roots

Examples

Many herbal teas use wild licorice for its naturally sweet flavor.
The roots of wild licorice have been used in traditional remedies for centuries.
She found wild licorice growing along the edge of the forest.
