on paper

1. ADV

/ ɑn ˈpeɪ.pɚ / (AmE), / ɒn ˈpeɪ.pə / (BrE)
According to what is written or planned, rather than what actually exists or happens in practice.
in theory, not necessarily in reality

Examples

On paper, Sarah has all the qualifications needed for the job.
The project looked perfect on paper, but there were many difficulties during implementation.
On paper, their team should easily win the championship.

Synset Examples

on paper the candidate seems promising

Related Terms

  • Synonym: in theory - as opposed to in practice; theoretically
  • Antonym: in practice - in actual operation or reality
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00171356-r
Synset POS: r

2. ADV

/ ɑn ˈpeɪpɚ / (AmE), / ɒn ˈpeɪpə / (BrE)
according to what is written or printed, rather than what actually happens or is true in practice
as written or printed

Examples

On paper, the company looks very successful, but it has many hidden problems.
The plan seems perfect on paper, but it may not work in real life.
He qualified on paper, but lacked the necessary experience for the job.

Synset Examples

this is exactly what the composer had set down on paper

Related Terms

  • Synonym: in theory - in principle, but not necessarily in practice
