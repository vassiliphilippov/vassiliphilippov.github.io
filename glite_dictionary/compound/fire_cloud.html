fire cloud

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪɚ klaʊd / (AmE), / ˈfaɪə klaʊd / (BrE)
A large cloud of smoke and debris produced by a fire, especially following an explosion or major blaze.
cloud of smoke and debris from a fire

Examples

A massive fire cloud rose above the city after the chemical plant accident.
Witnesses described a dark fire cloud spreading over the hillside as the forest burned.
Helicopters dropped water on the fire cloud, but the smoke was still visible for miles.

Synset Examples

They noticed a fire cloud forming after the explosion.
The fire cloud loomed ominously over the forest.
A thick fire cloud rose into the sky during the blaze.
