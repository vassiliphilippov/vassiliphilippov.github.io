megavitamin therapy

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛɡəˈvaɪtəmɪn ˈθɛrəpi / (AmE), / ˌmɛɡəˈvɪtəmɪn ˈθɛrəpi / (BrE)
the practice of treating health conditions by taking very large doses of vitamins, often significantly higher than the recommended dietary allowance
therapy using high doses of vitamins
medical

Examples

Some patients seek out megavitamin therapy in hopes of boosting their immune systems.
The effectiveness of megavitamin therapy remains controversial among medical professionals.
A doctor warned her about the possible risks associated with megavitamin therapy.

Etymology

Formed from 'mega-' meaning very large and 'vitamin', referring to unusually high vitamin doses.
