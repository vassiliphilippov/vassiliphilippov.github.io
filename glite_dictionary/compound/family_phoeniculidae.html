family Phoeniculidae

1. NOUN

/ fəˌnɪkjəˈlaɪdi / (AmE), / fəʊˌnɪkjʊˈlɪːdɪ / (BrE)
A family of African birds known as wood hoopoes, characterized by their slender, curved bills and glossy plumage.
family of wood hoopoe birds

Examples

Scientists have observed that the family Phoeniculidae displays highly social behavior in the wild.
The distinctive calls of birds in the family Phoeniculidae can be heard throughout sub-Saharan Africa.
Members of the family Phoeniculidae are easily recognized by their iridescent plumage and long, downcurved bills.

Etymology

From New Latin Phoeniculidae, from the genus name Phoeniculus + -idae (family suffix in zoological taxonomy).
