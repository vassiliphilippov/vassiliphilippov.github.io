bush league

1. NOUN

/ bʊʃ liɡ / (AmE), / bʊʃ liːɡ / (BrE)
(in North American sports, especially baseball) a minor league or a league of teams not regarded as major league; also used figuratively to describe something as unsophisticated or of lower quality.
minor sports league; inferior category
american

Examples

He spent his first few years playing in the bush league before making it to the majors.
Some critics dismissed the new team as strictly bush league in their performance.
Using outdated equipment like that is really bush league for a professional organization.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: minor league - a league below the major league level, especially in baseball
  • Antonym: major league - the highest level of professional sports competition, especially in baseball

Etymology

Originates from North American slang, with 'bush' meaning rural or unsophisticated, referring to minor or less prestigious teams.

2. ADJ

/ bʊʃ liːɡ / (AmE), / bʊʃ liːɡ / (BrE)
Of inferior quality or amateurish, lacking professionalism or skill.
inferior or amateurish quality
informal american

Examples

His rude behavior at the meeting was considered bush league by his colleagues.
The software's constant crashes made it feel really bush league.
Many critics dismissed the film as bush league, citing its poor acting and direction.

Synset Examples

Their performance was so poor, it felt bush league.
He dismissed the idea as bush league and unoriginal.
The competition was fierce, not bush league at all.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amateurish - Lacking professional skill or expertise
  • Antonym: professional - Conforming to the standards expected of a profession; opposite of amateurish

Etymology

Originally from early 20th-century American baseball, referring to small-town or minor leagues (the "bush").
