inner circle

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪnɚ ˈsɝkəl / (AmE), / ˈɪnə ˈsɜːkəl / (BrE)
a small, exclusive group of people with power or influence within a larger group or organization
exclusive influential group

Examples

Only a few trusted advisors are allowed into the president’s inner circle.
Rumors quickly spread outside the company’s inner circle.
She worked hard to gain the trust of the organization’s inner circle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clique - a small, close-knit group of people, often with shared interests, who do not readily allow others to join

2. NOUN

/ ˈɪnɚ ˈsɝːkəl / (AmE), / ˈɪnə ˈsɜːkl / (BrE)
a small, closely connected group of trusted individuals who have special influence or access
close, trusted group of people

Examples

She relied on her inner circle for advice before making the announcement.
Only members of the inner circle were invited to the private meeting.
Rumors spread that someone from the president's inner circle had leaked the news.

Synset Examples

Trust is essential within the inner circle.
He only shares his secrets with his inner circle.
Her inner circle always supports her decisions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clique - a small close-knit group of people who do not readily allow others to join them
  • Antonym: outsider - a person who does not belong to a particular group
