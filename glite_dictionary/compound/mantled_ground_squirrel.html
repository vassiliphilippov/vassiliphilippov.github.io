mantled ground squirrel

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæntəld ɡraʊnd ˈskwɝl / (AmE), / ˈmæntld ɡraʊnd ˈskwɪrəl / (BrE)
a common black-striped, reddish-brown ground squirrel of western North America that resembles a large chipmunk
a North American ground squirrel species

Examples

We spotted a mantled ground squirrel darting across the hiking trail in the Rockies.
The mantled ground squirrel is often confused with the chipmunk due to its striped back.
Researchers observed several mantled ground squirrels gathering food before winter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chipmunk - a small, striped North American rodent related to squirrels
