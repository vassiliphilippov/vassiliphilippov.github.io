author mill

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔθɚ mɪl / (AmE), / ˈɔːθə mɪl / (BrE)
a business, organization, or system that produces a large number of authors or publications, often with little regard for quality
place or system producing many (low-quality) authors or works

Examples

The new publishing company was criticized for being an author mill rather than supporting genuine talent.
Aspiring writers quickly realized that joining an author mill would not lead to literary recognition.
Many bestsellers never pass through an author mill, relying instead on careful editing and promotion.

Synset Examples

The publishing industry feels like an author mill at times.
Many aspiring writers fear being trapped in an author mill.
The author mill churned out countless forgettable titles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vanity press - a publishing house that prints books at the author's expense
