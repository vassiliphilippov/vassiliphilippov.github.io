hedge violet

1. NOUN

/ hɛdʒ ˈvaɪ.əlɪt / (AmE), / hɛdʒ ˈvaɪ.əlɪt / (BrE)
A common European violet (Viola hirta) that grows in woods and hedgerows.
common European violet found in woods and hedgerows

Examples

The hedge violet adds a splash of purple to woodland paths in early spring.
Gardeners often admire the delicate appearance of the hedge violet in shaded areas.
Clusters of hedge violets thrive along the hedgerows bordering the old estate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: violet - a herbaceous plant with purple or white flowers
