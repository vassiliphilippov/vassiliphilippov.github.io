kerb crawler

1. NOUN

/ kɝb ˈkrɔlɚ / (AmE), / kɜːb ˈkrɔːlə / (BrE)
a person who drives slowly along the curb, especially at night, seeking to solicit sex from prostitutes
person seeking sex by driving along curb
british

Examples

The police arrested a kerb crawler during their late-night patrol through the city.
Local residents have complained about kerb crawlers disturbing their neighborhood.
Legislation was introduced to tackle the problem of kerb crawlers in urban areas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: john - a man who solicits sex from a prostitute

Etymology

From British English 'kerb' (curb) + 'crawler' (one who moves slowly), referring to the act of slowly driving by the curb.
