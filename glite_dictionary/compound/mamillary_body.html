mamillary body

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæməˌlɛri ˈbɑdi / (AmE), / ˈmamɪləri ˈbɒdi / (BrE)
either of two small round structures located on the underside of the brain, constituting the terminals of the anterior arches of the fornix and involved in memory functions
brain structure involved in memory
medical

Examples

Damage to the mamillary body can lead to memory disorders such as Wernicke-Korsakoff syndrome.
During the brain dissection, the professor pointed out the mamillary body as an important landmark.
MRI scans revealed abnormalities in the patient's mamillary bodies after the accident.

Etymology

From Latin mamillaris (nipple-shaped), referring to their rounded shape.
