compost heap

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmpoʊst hiːp / (AmE), / ˈkɒmpəʊst hiːp / (BrE)
a pile of organic waste, such as plant material and manure, left to decay to produce compost for use as fertilizer
pile for decaying organic waste

Examples

We toss vegetable scraps onto the compost heap to help create nutrient-rich soil.
The garden has a large compost heap tucked behind the greenhouse.
It takes several months for the materials in a compost heap to fully break down.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compost pile - a pile of organic material left to decompose for making compost
