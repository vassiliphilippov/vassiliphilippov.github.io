degree of freedom

1. NOUN

/ dəˈɡri əv ˈfridəm / (AmE), / dɪˈɡriː əv ˈfriːdəm / (BrE)
one of the minimum number of independent variables required to specify the state or configuration of a physical system, especially in mathematics, physics, or engineering
independent variable defining a system's state

Examples

In classical mechanics, each spatial coordinate adds a degree of freedom to the system.
A rigid body in three-dimensional space has six degrees of freedom.
The model's accuracy increases as the number of degrees of freedom rises.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: variable - quantity that can change or be varied

Etymology

Formed from 'degree' + 'of freedom', referring to the number of independent ways a system can move or be configured.

2. NOUN

/ dəˈɡri əv ˈfridəm / (AmE), / dɪˈɡriː əv ˈfriːdəm / (BrE)
a statistical parameter representing an independent value that can vary in an analysis, often used to describe the number of values in a calculation that are free to vary.
independent variable in statistics
medical

Examples

When calculating the variance, you need to consider the degrees of freedom involved.
In this model, each additional variable adds one degree of freedom.
The test's accuracy depends on the degree of freedom available in the data.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: statistic - a numerical value representing a property of a sample
