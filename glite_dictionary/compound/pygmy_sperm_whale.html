pygmy sperm whale

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪɡmi spɜrm weɪl / (AmE), / ˈpɪɡmi spɜːm weɪl / (BrE)
a small species of sperm whale (Kogia breviceps) found in warm waters around the world, especially near both coasts of North America
a small species of sperm whale

Examples

The stranded pygmy sperm whale was rescued by marine biologists and released into deeper water.
Unlike its larger cousin, the pygmy sperm whale is rarely seen at the ocean surface.
Scientists recently discovered new information about the diet of the elusive pygmy sperm whale.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: sperm whale - a large whale species (Physeter macrocephalus)

Etymology

From 'pygmy' (small) + 'sperm whale', referring to its smaller size compared to the common sperm whale.
