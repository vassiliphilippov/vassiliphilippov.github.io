Chilean cedar

1. NOUN

/ tʃɪˈliən ˈsɛdər / (AmE), / tʃɪˈliːən ˈsɛdə / (BrE)
a small evergreen tree of South America with coppery bark and attractive foliage, commonly grown for its wood
South American evergreen tree with coppery bark

Examples

The garden features a mature Chilean cedar near the pond, prized for its attractive foliage.
Carpenters value the wood of the Chilean cedar for its durability and color.
During our trip, we saw several Chilean cedars lining the mountain trail.
