ex vivo

1. ADJ

/ ɛks ˈviːvoʊ / (AmE), / ɛks ˈviːvəʊ / (BrE)
(of a biological process) taking place in an artificial environment outside the living organism.
outside the living organism (in artificial conditions)
medical

Examples

The researchers studied tissue regeneration ex vivo before attempting in vivo experiments.
This drug's effectiveness was first demonstrated ex vivo using isolated human cells.
An ex vivo perfusion system keeps organs viable for transplantation.

Synset Examples

in vitro fertilization

Related Terms

  • Synonym: in vitro - performed or taking place in a test tube or culture dish, outside a living organism
  • Antonym: in vivo - within the living organism

Etymology

From Latin 'ex' (out of) + 'vivo' (I live).
