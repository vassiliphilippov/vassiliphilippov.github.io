Jupiterian in scale

1. ADJ

/ ˌdʒuːpɪˈtɪriən ɪn skeɪl / (AmE), / ˌdʒuːpɪˈtɪəriən ɪn skeɪl / (BrE)
extremely large or grand in size; vast or immense, like the planet Jupiter.
extremely large; immense in size

Examples

The new hotel was Jupiterian in scale, dwarfing every other building in the city.
Her plans for the festival were Jupiterian in scale, with multiple stages and thousands of guests expected.
The sculpture was Jupiterian in scale, stretching across the entire public square.

Synset Examples

The artist's vision was truly Jupiterian in scale.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gigantic - very large; extremely big
  • Synonym: vast - immense, of very great size or extent

Etymology

From 'Jupiter', the largest planet in the solar system, used metaphorically.
