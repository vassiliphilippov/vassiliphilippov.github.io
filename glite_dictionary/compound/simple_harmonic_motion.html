simple harmonic motion

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪmpəl hɑrˈmɑnɪk ˈmoʊʃən / (AmE), / ˈsɪmpəl hɑːˈmɒnɪk ˈməʊʃən / (BrE)
periodic motion in which the restoring force is directly proportional to the displacement from an equilibrium position
periodic motion with force proportional to displacement

Examples

The motion of a mass attached to a spring is a classic example of simple harmonic motion.
In physics class, we learned how to calculate the period of simple harmonic motion.
The pendulum exhibits simple harmonic motion when its swings are small.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oscillation - movement back and forth at a regular speed
  • Degree Lower: damped harmonic motion - harmonic motion with decreasing amplitude over time
  • Degree Higher: forced harmonic motion - harmonic motion with an external driving force

Etymology

Formed from 'simple', 'harmonic', and 'motion'; 'harmonic' from Greek 'harmonikos' meaning 'skilled in music'.
