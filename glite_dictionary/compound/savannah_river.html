Savannah River

1. PROPN

/ səˈvænə ˈrɪvər / (AmE), / səˈvænə ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
a river in the southeastern United States forming most of the border between South Carolina and Georgia, and flowing southeast into the Atlantic Ocean
river forming border between South Carolina and Georgia

Examples

The Savannah River is vital for supplying water to both Georgia and South Carolina.
Many species of fish thrive in the Savannah River's diverse habitats.
The city of Savannah gets its name from the nearby Savannah River.
