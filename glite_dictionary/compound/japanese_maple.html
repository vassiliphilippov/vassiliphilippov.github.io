Japanese maple

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˈpæniz ˈmeɪpəl / (AmE), / dʒəˈpæniːz ˈmeɪpəl / (BrE)
a small deciduous tree native to Japan, valued for its deeply cut leaves that turn bright red or orange in autumn
tree with deeply cut red leaves in autumn

Examples

The Japanese maple in our garden displays stunning red foliage every autumn.
Bonsai enthusiasts often use the Japanese maple for its delicate leaves and elegant shape.
A mature Japanese maple can be a focal point in any landscape design.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maple - tree or shrub with lobed leaves and winged fruits

2. NOUN

/ dʒəˈpæniz ˈmeɪpəl / (AmE), / dʒəˈpɑːniːz ˈmeɪpəl / (BrE)
an ornamental shrub or small tree native to Japan and Korea, with deeply cut leaves and grown in many varieties
ornamental tree with deeply divided leaves

Examples

The garden features a beautiful Japanese maple with vibrant red leaves.
He planted a Japanese maple near the patio to provide a splash of color in autumn.
Many homeowners choose the Japanese maple for its elegant foliage and compact size.
