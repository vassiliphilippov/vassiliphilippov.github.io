system of macrophages

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪstəm əv ˈmækroʊˌfeɪdʒɪz / (AmE), / ˈsɪstəm əv ˈmakrəʊˌfeɪdʒɪz / (BrE)
A widely distributed network of free and fixed macrophages in the body, derived from bone marrow and involved in immune response.
network of immune system macrophages
medical

Examples

The system of macrophages plays a crucial role in defending the body against pathogens.
Damage to the system of macrophages can compromise immune function severely.
Researchers study the system of macrophages to understand how the body fights infections.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reticuloendothelial system - network of phagocytic cells including macrophages in organs and tissues
