prelatish manner

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɛlətɪʃ ˈmænər / (AmE), / ˈprɛlətɪʃ ˈmanə / (BrE)
behavior or views characteristic of a church leader or bishop
manner resembling a church leader

Examples

She spoke in a prelatish manner, her tone formal and authoritative.
His prelatish manner at the meeting seemed out of place among the casual attendees.
They objected to his prelatish manner, finding it pompous and old-fashioned.

Synset Examples

She dressed in a prelatish manner for the occasion.
His prelatish views were debated at the conference.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prelatical - relating to or characteristic of a prelate

Etymology

From 'prelatish' (relating to a prelate, church official), from Medieval Latin 'praelatus' (prelate), and 'manner' (way of behaving).
