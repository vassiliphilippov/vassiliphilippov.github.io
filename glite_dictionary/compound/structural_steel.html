structural steel

1. NOUN

/ ˈstrʌktʃərəl stil / (AmE), / ˈstrʌktʃərəl stiːl / (BrE)
a strong steel alloy specially formed and rolled into specific shapes for use in building construction and engineering structures
steel used in building construction

Examples

The new bridge was constructed entirely from structural steel for added durability and strength.
Engineers specified structural steel beams to support the weight of the skyscraper.
After the fire, inspectors checked the structural steel for signs of damage or warping.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: steel - a strong, hard metal made of iron and carbon
