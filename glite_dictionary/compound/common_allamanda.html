common allamanda

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmən əˈlɑːˌmændə / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən əˈlæməndə / (BrE)
a vigorous evergreen climbing plant of South America, known for its glossy, leathery leaves and golden yellow flowers
South American climbing plant with yellow flowers

Examples

The common allamanda adds vibrant color to many tropical gardens.
Gardeners appreciate the common allamanda for its rapid growth and striking blooms.
In Brazil, the common allamanda often climbs along fences and trellises.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Allamanda cathartica - the scientific name for common allamanda
