Seneka snakeroot

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛnɪkə ˈsneɪkˌruːt / (AmE), / ˈsɛnɪkə ˈsneɪkˌruːt / (BrE)
An eastern North American plant with a terminal cluster of small white flowers and roots that have been used medicinally.
North American medicinal plant

Examples

The herbalist recommended using Seneka snakeroot to treat the symptoms.
Early settlers learned about Seneka snakeroot from Indigenous peoples.
The white flowers of Seneka snakeroot bloom in late spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snakeroot - any of various plants with roots formerly used as a remedy for snakebite

Etymology

From the Seneca people, an Iroquois nation, combined with 'snakeroot' for the plant's historical medicinal use against snakebites.
