genus Tacca

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈtæk.ə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈtæk.ə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical plants with creeping rootstocks and small umbellate flowers, belonging to the family Dioscoreaceae.
genus of tropical plants with umbellate flowers

Examples

Several species within the genus Tacca are grown as ornamental plants for their unusual flowers.
The genus Tacca is distributed mainly in tropical regions of Southeast Asia and Africa.
Scientists recently discovered a new species in the genus Tacca during an expedition in Borneo.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: batflower - A common name for species Tacca chantrieri, known for its distinctive dark flowers

Etymology

From scientific Latin Tacca, the genus name; originally from Indonesian or Malay 'taka', a local name for these plants.
