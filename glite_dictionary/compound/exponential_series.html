exponential series

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˌspoʊnənʃəl ˈsɪriz / (AmE), / ɪkˌspəʊnənʃəl ˈsɪəriːz / (BrE)
a mathematical series in which each term is derived from the expansion of an exponential expression, typically of the form eˣ = 1 + x + x²/2! + x³/3! + ...
series from expansion of an exponential

Examples

The function eˣ can be represented as an exponential series for all real numbers x.
In calculus, the exponential series is a key example of an infinite power series.
Mathematicians use the exponential series to define and analyze the exponential function.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: power series - an infinite series of terms in powers of a variable

Etymology

From 'exponential' (from Latin 'exponere', meaning 'to put forth') and 'series' (from Latin 'series', meaning 'row, succession').
