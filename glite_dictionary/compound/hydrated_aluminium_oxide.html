hydrated aluminium oxide

1. NOUN

/ haɪˈdreɪtɪd əˌluːmɪnəm ˈɑksaɪd / (AmE), / haɪˈdreɪtɪd ˌaljʊˈmɪniəm ˈɒksaɪd / (BrE)
a white crystalline compound, Al(OH)₃, occurring naturally as the mineral gibbsite
white mineral compound, also known as gibbsite

Examples

Gibbsite is a naturally occurring form of hydrated aluminium oxide found in bauxite deposits.
To purify aluminium, hydrated aluminium oxide can be precipitated from solution before being reduced.
Laboratory samples of hydrated aluminium oxide are often used as a reference for mineralogical studies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gibbsite - the mineral form of hydrated aluminium oxide
