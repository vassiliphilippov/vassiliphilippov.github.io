common raccoon

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmən rəˈkun / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən rəˈkuːn / (BrE)
a North American mammal (Procyon lotor) noted for its black facial mask, ringed tail, and dexterous front paws
North American mammal with black facial mask

Examples

A common raccoon was rummaging through the trash bins behind our house last night.
The common raccoon is well adapted to urban environments across North America.
Biologists studied the diet of the common raccoon in several different habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: raccoon - medium-sized mammal native to North America, with a black facial mask and ringed tail
