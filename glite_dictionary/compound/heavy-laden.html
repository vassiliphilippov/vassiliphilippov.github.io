heavy-laden

1. ADJ

/ ˌhɛviˈleɪdən / (AmE), / ˌhɛviˈleɪdən / (BrE)
weighed down or burdened, especially with cares, troubles, or responsibilities
weighed down; burdened (often with cares)
archaic

Examples

After a long and difficult journey, she appeared heavy-laden with worry.
The heavy-laden travelers sought shelter from the storm.
He spoke in a heavy-laden voice, burdened by memories of the past.

Synset Examples

all ye that labor and are heavy-laden

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weighed down - feeling burdened or oppressed
  • Antonym: unburdened - not weighed down by cares or worries

Etymology

Formed from 'heavy' + 'laden' (past participle of 'lade', meaning to load).

2. ADJ

/ ˈhɛviˌleɪdən / (AmE), / ˈhɛviˌleɪdən / (BrE)
carrying a heavy load; weighed down with a physically heavy burden.
carrying a heavy load

Examples

The donkey climbed the steep hill, heavy-laden with sacks of grain.
After the storm, the tree branches were heavy-laden with snow.
The old truck, heavy-laden with bricks, struggled down the bumpy road.

Synset Examples

tree limbs burdened with ice
a heavy-laden cart
loaded down with packages

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burdened - carrying a heavy load or responsibility
  • Synonym: loaded - filled with as much as possible; heavily carrying something

Etymology

Formed from 'heavy' + 'laden' (past participle of 'lade', from Old English 'hladan', to load).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00871549-s
Synset POS: s
