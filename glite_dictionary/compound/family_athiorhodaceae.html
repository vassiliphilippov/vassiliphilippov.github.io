family Athiorhodaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˈæθioʊˌroʊdəˌsiː / (AmE), / ˈaθiəʊˌrəʊdəˌsiː / (BrE)
A family of small, motile sulfur bacteria.
a family of sulfur bacteria

Examples

Scientists have discovered new species within the family Athiorhodaceae in deep-sea vents.
The family Athiorhodaceae plays an important role in the sulfur cycle of aquatic environments.
Researchers are studying the genetic diversity of the family Athiorhodaceae to understand bacterial evolution.

Etymology

From New Latin Athiorhodaceae, formed from the genus Athiorhodus + -aceae (family suffix).
