macadamia nut tree

1. NOUN

/ məˌkædəˈmiːə nʌt triː / (AmE), / məkəˌdeɪmɪə nʌt triː / (BrE)
A small Australian tree with clusters of pink flowers, widely cultivated for its sweet, edible nuts.
Australian tree grown for edible nuts

Examples

The macadamia nut tree is native to Australia but is now grown in many warm regions worldwide.
She planted a macadamia nut tree in her backyard, hoping to harvest nuts in a few years.
Hawaii's economy benefits significantly from the cultivation of the macadamia nut tree.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: macadamia - a genus of trees producing edible nuts, native to Australia
  • Synonym: macadamia nut - the edible kernel of the macadamia nut tree

Etymology

Named after John Macadam, a Scottish-Australian chemist and politician.
