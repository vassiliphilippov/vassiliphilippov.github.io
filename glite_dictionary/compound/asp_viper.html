asp viper

1. NOUN

/ æsp ˈvaɪpəɹ / (AmE), / asp ˈvaɪpə / (BrE)
A venomous snake found in southern Europe, similar to but smaller than the adder (Vipera aspis).
a southern European venomous snake

Examples

The asp viper inhabits rocky hillsides in southern France and Italy.
Despite its small size, the asp viper has a potent venom.
Hikers are advised to watch for the asp viper during the warm summer months.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adder - a common name for several species of viper, mainly Vipera berus

Etymology

From Latin 'aspis', referring to a venomous snake, and 'viper'.
