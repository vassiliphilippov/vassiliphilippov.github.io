to double tongue

1. VERB

/ ˈdʌb.əl tʌŋ / (AmE), / ˈdʌb.əl tʌŋ / (BrE)
to play successive notes rapidly on a wind instrument by alternating movements of the tongue, producing a fast, clear articulation
play fast notes on a wind instrument using tongue technique

Examples

He learned to double tongue so he could play the passage at the required tempo.
The flutist practiced double tonguing to achieve clear and rapid notes.
It can be difficult for beginners to double tongue evenly across all registers.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: triple tongue - to articulate three rapid successive notes on a wind instrument using a special tonguing technique

Etymology

From the musical technique of using two different tongue positions to articulate notes rapidly.
