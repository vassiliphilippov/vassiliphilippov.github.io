Cooley's anaemia

1. NOUN

/ ˈkuːliz əˈniːmiə / (AmE), / ˈkuːliz əˈniːmiə / (BrE)
A severe, inherited form of thalassaemia (thalassemia major) characterized by the absence of normal hemoglobin, leading to severe anemia, skeletal deformations, and enlargement of the heart, spleen, and liver.
severe inherited form of thalassemia
medical british

Examples

Children suffering from Cooley's anaemia require regular blood transfusions throughout their lives.
Genetic screening can identify carriers of the genes responsible for Cooley's anaemia.
Advancements in medicine have improved the life expectancy of those diagnosed with Cooley's anaemia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thalassemia major - another term for Cooley's anaemia, severe inherited anemia
  • Synonym: sickle-cell anaemia - another inherited disorder causing abnormal hemoglobin

Etymology

Named after American pediatrician Thomas B. Cooley, who first described the condition in 1925; from anaemia (UK spelling of 'anemia').
