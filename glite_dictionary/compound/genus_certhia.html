genus Certhia

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈsɜːr.θi.ə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈsɜː.θɪ.ə / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Certhiidae, comprising the treecreepers, small songbirds known for their climbing behavior.
genus of treecreeper birds

Examples

The genus Certhia includes several species of treecreepers found across Eurasia and North America.
Ornithologists study the genus Certhia to understand treecreeper behavior and evolution.
Members of the genus Certhia are easily recognized by their slender, down-curved bills.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Certhiidae - family of small treecreeping birds

Etymology

From Latin 'Certhia,' the classical name for a kind of small bird.
