strong current

1. NOUN

/ strɔŋ ˈkɝːrənt / (AmE), / strɒŋ ˈkʌrənt / (BrE)
a powerful and fast-moving flow of water, usually in a river, sea, or other body of water
powerful water flow

Examples

Swimmers are advised to avoid this area because of the strong current.
The strong current swept the canoe downstream within minutes.
Despite warning signs, many people underestimate the danger of a strong current.

Synset Examples

He struggled to turn the boat against the strong current.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: undertow - a subsurface current that is moving offshore when waves are breaking on the shore
  • Synonym: rip current - a strong, narrow current flowing outward from the shore
