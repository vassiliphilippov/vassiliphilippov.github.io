Assumption of Mary

1. NOUN

/ əˈsʌmpʃən əv ˈmɛri / (AmE), / əˈsʌmpʃən ɒv ˈmeəri / (BrE)
(in the Roman Catholic Church) the celebration of the Virgin Mary's being taken up into heaven at the end of her earthly life; observed as a major feast day.
Virgin Mary's entry into heaven (Catholic feast)

Examples

Every year on August 15, Catholics celebrate the Assumption of Mary with special masses and processions.
The doctrine of the Assumption of Mary is a central belief in Roman Catholic theology.
Many churches across Europe hold festivals in honor of the Assumption of Mary.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Dormition - the Eastern Orthodox celebration of Mary's 'falling asleep' and entry into heaven

Etymology

From Latin assumptio Mariae, meaning 'the taking up of Mary.'
