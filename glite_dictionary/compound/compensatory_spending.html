compensatory spending

1. NOUN

/ kəmˈpɛnsəˌtɔri ˈspɛndɪŋ / (AmE), / kəmˈpɛnsətəri ˈspɛndɪŋ / (BrE)
government expenditure financed by borrowing, intended to stimulate the economy, especially during periods of recession or low growth
government borrowing to stimulate the economy

Examples

During a recession, the government often resorts to compensatory spending to increase demand.
Economists debated whether compensatory spending was effective in reducing unemployment rates.
The politician argued that compensatory spending would help jumpstart the sluggish economy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deficit spending - government spending in excess of income, typically financed by borrowing
