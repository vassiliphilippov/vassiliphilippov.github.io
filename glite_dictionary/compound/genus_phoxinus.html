genus Phoxinus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs foʊkˈsaɪ.nəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs fɒkˈsaɪ.nəs / (BrE)
A genus of small freshwater fish in the family Cyprinidae, commonly referred to as minnows.
a genus of small freshwater minnows

Examples

The genus Phoxinus includes several species found throughout Europe and Asia.
Researchers observed unique spawning behavior among members of the genus Phoxinus in the river.
Some anglers are interested in identifying different species within the genus Phoxinus for ecological studies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: minnow - a small freshwater fish of the family Cyprinidae

Etymology

From scientific Latin 'Phoxinus', the genus name for European minnows.
