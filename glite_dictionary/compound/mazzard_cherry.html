mazzard cherry

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæz.ərd ˈtʃɛr.i / (AmE), / ˈmæz.əd ˈtʃɛr.i / (BrE)
A wild or seedling form of the sweet cherry (Prunus avium), often used as rootstock for grafting cultivated cherries.
wild sweet cherry used as graft stock

Examples

Many orchardists use mazzard cherry as rootstock when grafting commercial cherry varieties.
The old garden was bordered with a row of mazzard cherry trees.
Experts recommend mazzard cherry roots for their resistance to certain soil conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sweet cherry - the cultivated cherry tree Prunus avium or its fruit

Etymology

From Cornish *masard* or *mazzard* (cherry), possibly of West Country origin.
