lead line

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛd laɪn / (AmE), / ˈlɛd laɪn / (BrE)
A line weighted with lead, used especially on ships to measure the depth of water.
weighted line for measuring water depth

Examples

Before entering the harbor, the captain ordered the crew to use the lead line to check the depth.
Sailors in the past relied on a lead line to avoid running aground in unfamiliar waters.
He carefully lowered the lead line over the side, waiting for it to touch the seabed.

Etymology

From the use of a lead weight attached to a line.
