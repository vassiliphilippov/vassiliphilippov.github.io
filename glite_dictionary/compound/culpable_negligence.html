culpable negligence

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌlpəbəl nəˈɡlɛdʒəns / (AmE), / ˈkʌlpəbəl nɪˈɡlɪdʒəns / (BrE)
(Law) Reckless failure to act with reasonable care, resulting in potential harm or risk to others.
reckless failure to use reasonable care (legal term)
legal

Examples

The driver was charged with culpable negligence after causing the accident.
In court, the lawyer argued that culpable negligence had resulted in serious injury to the patient.
Employers can be held liable for culpable negligence if they fail to provide a safe workplace.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gross negligence - extreme carelessness showing disregard for safety of others
  • Synonym: recklessness - disregard of risk or consequences
  • Degree Lower: ordinary negligence - failure to exercise reasonable care, less severe than culpable negligence

Etymology

From Latin 'culpabilis' (blameworthy) and 'negligentia' (neglect).
