dispersed particles

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈspɝst ˈpɑɹ.tɪ.kəlz / (AmE), / dɪˈspɜːst ˈpɑː.tɪ.klz / (BrE)
particles within a colloid that are distributed throughout another substance, forming the dispersed phase.
particles in a colloidal system

Examples

In milk, the dispersed particles are tiny fat droplets suspended in water.
The stability of a colloid depends on how well its dispersed particles resist coming together and settling out.
Under a microscope, the dispersed particles in the solution became visible as faint specks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: colloid - a mixture in which one substance is dispersed evenly throughout another
  • Antonym: dispersion medium - the substance in which dispersed particles are distributed, also part of a colloid
