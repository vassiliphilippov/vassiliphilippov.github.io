elfin safety

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛl.fɪn ˈseɪf.ti / (AmE), / ˈɛl.fɪn ˈseɪf.ti / (BrE)
Used humorously to refer to safety measures that are seen as overly delicate, whimsical, or unnecessarily careful, playing on the phrase 'health and safety.'
humorous term for exaggeratedly delicate safety
informal british

Examples

The company added a touch of elfin safety to its Christmas party, making sure the tinsel was non-flammable.
Some critics argue that modern playgrounds have become the victims of elfin safety concerns, robbing children of adventure.
He joked about the elfin safety guidelines attached to the fairy festival, saying even the pixies had helmets.

Synset Examples

She insisted on elfin safety when designing the event.
The elfin safety of our decorations is a top priority.
We should consider elfin safety when choosing materials.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: health and safety - regulations and precautions to prevent danger or injury in the workplace

Etymology

Playful alteration of 'health and safety,' with 'elfin' suggesting lightness or whimsy.
