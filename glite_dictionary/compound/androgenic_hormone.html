androgenic hormone

1. NOUN

/ ˌændɹəˈdʒɛnɪk ˈhɔːrmoʊn / (AmE), / ˌandrəˈdʒɛnɪk ˈhɔːməʊn / (BrE)
a male sex hormone produced in the testes or adrenal glands that is responsible for typical male sexual characteristics
male sex hormone promoting male traits
medical

Examples

Testosterone is the most well-known androgenic hormone in the human body.
Changes in muscle mass can result from increased production of androgenic hormone.
Certain medical conditions may require the regulation of androgenic hormone levels.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: testosterone - the main androgenic hormone in males
  • Antonym: estrogen - a female sex hormone promoting female characteristics

Etymology

From Greek andr- (man) + -genic (producing), and hormone (from Greek hormōn, to stimulate).
