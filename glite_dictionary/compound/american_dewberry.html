American dewberry

1. NOUN

/ əˈmɛr.ɪ.kən ˈduːˌbɛr.i / (AmE), / əˈmɛr.ɪ.kən ˈdjuːˌbɛr.i / (BrE)
A trailing bramble plant native to North America, with edible dark purple or black berries resembling blackberries.
North American bramble with edible berries

Examples

The American dewberry can be found growing wild along country roads.
She picked a handful of American dewberries to use in her homemade pie.
In spring, the American dewberry produces delicate white flowers before fruiting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dewberry - A trailing, bramblelike plant bearing edible berries

2. NOUN

/ əˈmɛr.ɪ.kən ˈduːˌbɛr.i / (AmE), / əˈmɛr.ɪ.kən ˈdjuːˌbɛr.i / (BrE)
a trailing bramble of eastern North America (Rubus flagellaris) that produces sweet, edible black berries similar to blackberries
trailing North American bramble; edible black fruit

Examples

The American dewberry can be found growing along roadsides and forest edges in the eastern United States.
Wildlife such as birds and small mammals often feed on the berries of the American dewberry.
She gathered a handful of ripe American dewberries for the homemade pie.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dewberry - a trailing bramble with edible berries
  • Synonym: blackberry - a dark, edible berry from a related plant
