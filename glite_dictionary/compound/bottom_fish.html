bottom fish

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑtəm fɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈbɒtəm fɪʃ / (BrE)
A fish that lives on or near the sea bottom, such as cod, haddock, or flounder, and often of commercial importance.
fish living on the sea bottom

Examples

Many commercial fisheries target bottom fish such as cod and haddock because of their economic value.
The trawl net is designed to catch bottom fish that live close to the ocean floor.
Because bottom fish are exposed to pollutants on the seabed, they can occasionally accumulate toxins.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: demersal fish - fish that live and feed on or near the bottom of seas or lakes
