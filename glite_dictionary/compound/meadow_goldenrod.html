meadow goldenrod

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛdoʊ ˈɡoʊldənˌrɑd / (AmE), / ˈmɛdəʊ ˈɡəʊldənˌrɒd / (BrE)
A large North American plant of the goldenrod family, with showy clusters of yellow flowers on arching branches, often considered a weed.
tall North American goldenrod with yellow flowers

Examples

The meadow goldenrod bloomed brightly across the open fields in late summer.
Many gardeners consider meadow goldenrod to be an attractive but invasive wildflower.
Bees and butterflies were drawn to the meadow goldenrod growing near the railway tracks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: goldenrod - plant with clusters of small yellow flowers
