beet armyworm

1. NOUN

/ bit ˈɑrmiˌwɚm / (AmE), / biːt ˈɑːmiwɜːm / (BrE)
the larva of a moth (Spodoptera exigua) that feeds on the leaves of beets and various other vegetables, often causing damage to crops
larva of a moth that damages vegetables

Examples

Farmers often lose significant crops to the beet armyworm each year.
The beet armyworm is resistant to several common pesticides, making it difficult to control.
Infestations of beet armyworm can devastate fields of spinach, lettuce, and other leafy greens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: armyworm - larva of various moth species that moves in large groups and damages crops
