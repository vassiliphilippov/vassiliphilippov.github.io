genus Pleuronectes

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌplʊəˌroʊˈnɛk.tiːz / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌplʊə.rəˈnɛk.tiːz / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Pleuronectidae, comprising certain species of flatfish, including the common European plaice.
type genus of flatfish family Pleuronectidae

Examples

The genus Pleuronectes includes several well-known species of flatfish found in European waters.
Marine biologists study the anatomy of fish in the genus Pleuronectes to understand their adaptation to life on the seafloor.
The European plaice is a prominent member of the genus Pleuronectes and is valued in commercial fishing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plaice - A type of flatfish in the genus Pleuronectes

Etymology

From Greek 'pleura' (side, rib) + 'nēktēs' (swimmer), relating to its laterally compressed body and swimming style; used in New Latin scientific classification.
