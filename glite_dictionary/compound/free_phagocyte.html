free phagocyte

1. NOUN

/ fri ˈfæɡəˌsaɪt / (AmE), / friː ˈfæɡəsaɪt / (BrE)
A type of phagocyte that circulates freely in the blood rather than being fixed in tissues.
phagocyte circulating in the blood
medical

Examples

The number of free phagocytes in the bloodstream increases during infection.
Unlike fixed phagocytes, free phagocytes move through the blood to areas of need.
Doctors study free phagocytes to better understand immune responses.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fixed phagocyte - a phagocyte that is stationary within tissues
