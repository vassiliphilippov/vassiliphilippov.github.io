minor tranquillizer

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪnɚ trænˈkwɪlaɪzɚ / (AmE), / ˈmaɪnə træŋˈkwɪlaɪzə / (BrE)
a drug used to relieve anxiety and reduce tension or irritability, typically less potent than major tranquillizers
anti-anxiety drug of milder potency
medical

Examples

After experiencing severe stress, the doctor prescribed a minor tranquillizer to help her relax.
Many people take minor tranquillizers to manage symptoms of anxiety disorders.
He was advised not to operate heavy machinery while taking a minor tranquillizer.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: major tranquillizer - a more potent tranquillizer used mainly to manage psychosis
  • Synonym: anxiolytic - a drug that reduces anxiety

Etymology

From Latin 'tranquillus' (quiet, calm) and English 'minor' (lesser).
