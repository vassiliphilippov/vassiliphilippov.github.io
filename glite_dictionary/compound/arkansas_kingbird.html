Arkansas kingbird

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːrkənsɔː ˈkɪŋbɜːrd / (AmE), / ˌɑːkənˌsɔː ˈkɪŋbɜːd / (BrE)
a North American kingbird species (Tyrannus verticalis) found in the western United States, with a pale grey head and back, yellowish breast, and black tail
North American kingbird species

Examples

The Arkansas kingbird is often spotted perched on power lines in the summer.
A pale grey back and yellowish breast help identify the Arkansas kingbird in the field.
Birdwatchers recorded several Arkansas kingbirds migrating through the valley last spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kingbird - a group of large, aggressive flycatchers of the Americas
