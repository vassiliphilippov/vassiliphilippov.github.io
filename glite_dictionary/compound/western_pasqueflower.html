Western pasqueflower

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɛstərn ˈpæskˌflaʊɚ / (AmE), / ˈwɛstən ˈpæskˌflaʊə / (BrE)
A perennial herbaceous plant (Anemone occidentalis, also known as Pulsatilla occidentalis) native to western North America, bearing large, hairy flowers, typically with pale or white petals.
plant species of western North America

Examples

We hiked along the trail and spotted a beautiful Western pasqueflower blooming near the snowmelt.
The Western pasqueflower is known for its striking, silky white petals and early spring blossoms.
Photographers travel to the mountains each year to capture fields of Western pasqueflower in full bloom.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pasqueflower - a plant of the genus Pulsatilla with showy flowers, often blooming in spring

Etymology

‘Pasque’ from Old French 'pasque' (Easter), referencing its spring flowering; 'Western' for its geographic distribution.
