mitigating circumstance

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪtəˌɡeɪtɪŋ ˈsɜrkəmˌstæns / (AmE), / ˈmɪtɪɡeɪtɪŋ ˈsɜːkəmˌstɑːns / (BrE)
(law) A fact or situation that does not justify or excuse an offence but reduces the severity or culpability of the penalty imposed.
fact reducing legal penalty
legal

Examples

The judge considered the mitigating circumstance of the defendant's difficult upbringing before determining the sentence.
Although the crime was serious, the presence of mitigating circumstances led to a reduced penalty.
Her remorse was taken as a mitigating circumstance during the trial.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: aggravating circumstance - (law) a circumstance that increases the severity or culpability of an offense
