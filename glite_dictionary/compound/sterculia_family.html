sterculia family

1. NOUN

/ stɚˈkjuːliə ˈfæməli / (AmE), / stəːˈkjuːliə ˈfamɪli / (BrE)
A large family of flowering plants in the order Malvales, including tropical trees and shrubs such as Sterculia, cacao, and kola.
family of tropical flowering plants

Examples

The sterculia family includes important tropical trees such as the source of cocoa.
Researchers studied genetic diversity within the sterculia family across Asia and Africa.
Several economically valuable plants belong to the sterculia family, including kola and cacao trees.

Etymology

Named after the genus Sterculia, from Latin 'Sterculius,' a Roman god of manure, due to the unpleasant smell of some species.
