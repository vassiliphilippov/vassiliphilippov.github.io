Tule tree

1. NOUN

/ ˈtuːli triː / (AmE), / ˈtuːli triː / (BrE)
A famous ancient Montezuma cypress in Santa María del Tule, Mexico, known for its enormous size and age.
giant Montezuma cypress tree in Mexico

Examples

Tourists from around the world come to see the Tule tree in Santa María del Tule.
The Tule tree is estimated to be more than 2,000 years old, making it one of the oldest living trees.
With its massive trunk, the Tule tree is considered a natural wonder of Mexico.

Synset Examples

some say the Tule tree is the world's largest single biomass

Etymology

Named after Santa María del Tule, the town where the tree is located; 'tule' from Nahuatl 'tōllin', meaning 'bulrush' or 'reed'.
