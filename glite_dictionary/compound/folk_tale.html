folk tale

1. NOUN

/ foʊk teɪl / (AmE), / fəʊk teɪl / (BrE)
A traditional story passed down orally among the people of a community or culture.
traditional story passed down orally

Examples

My grandmother used to tell me a fascinating folk tale about a clever rabbit and a hungry wolf.
Many cultures have a version of the Cinderella folk tale with unique local characters.
As a child, he was enchanted by every folk tale he heard around the campfire.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: myth - a traditional story, often involving supernatural beings or events
  • Synonym: legend - a traditional story sometimes regarded as historical but unauthenticated

Etymology

From 'folk' (people) + 'tale' (story).
