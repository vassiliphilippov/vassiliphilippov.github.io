speech bubble

1. NOUN

/ spiːtʃ ˈbʌb.əl / (AmE), / spiːtʃ ˈbʌb.əl / (BrE)
a rounded or elliptical outline in comics and cartoons containing the spoken words or thoughts of a character
outline showing words in comics or cartoons

Examples

The artist drew a speech bubble above the character’s head to show what she was saying.
Each speech bubble was filled with witty dialogue in the comic strip.
Children often add speech bubbles to their drawings to create conversations between their stick figures.

Synset Examples

Speech bubbles are used not only to include a character's words, but also emotions, voice inflections and unspecified language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thought bubble - outline in comics containing a character's unspoken thoughts
