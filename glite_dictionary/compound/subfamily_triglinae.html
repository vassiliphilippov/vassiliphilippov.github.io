subfamily Triglinae

1. NOUN

/ sʌbˌfæməli traɪˈɡlɪniː / (AmE), / sʌbˌfæmɪli traɪˈɡliːniː / (BrE)
A subfamily of fish within the family Triglidae, comprising the true searobins characterized by ordinary scales and lacking barbels.
a fish subfamily including true searobins

Examples

The subfamily Triglinae includes species commonly known as true searobins.
Recent studies have suggested revisions to the classification of the subfamily Triglinae.
Unlike other members of Triglidae, fish in the subfamily Triglinae lack barbels on their chins.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Triglidae - the fish family including searobins and related species

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'subfamily' and 'Triglinae' (the latter from the type genus Trigla).
