family Enterobacteriaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ɛntərboʊˌtɪriəˈsiː.iː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli ˌɛntəbakˌtɪərɪəˈsiː.iː / (BrE)
a large family of Gram-negative rod-shaped bacteria, including many important pathogens such as Escherichia coli and Salmonella
family of rod-shaped Gram-negative bacteria
medical

Examples

The family Enterobacteriaceae includes several bacteria that cause foodborne illnesses.
Medical students learn to identify members of the family Enterobacteriaceae in laboratory classes.
Many pathogens in the family Enterobacteriaceae are resistant to multiple antibiotics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Escherichia coli - a common species within the family Enterobacteriaceae

Etymology

From New Latin 'Enterobacterium' (a genus name) + '-aceae' (a standard suffix for biological family names).
