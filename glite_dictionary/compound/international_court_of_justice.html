International Court of Justice

1. PROPN

/ ˌɪntəˈnæʃənl kɔrt əv ˈdʒʌstɪs / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈnæʃənəl kɔːt əv ˈdʒʌstɪs / (BrE)
the principal judicial body of the United Nations, established to settle legal disputes between states and give advisory opinions on international legal issues
United Nations' principal judicial court

Examples

The International Court of Justice issued a ruling on the border dispute last year.
All member states are subject to the jurisdiction of the International Court of Justice.
Her research focuses on the procedures followed by the International Court of Justice.
