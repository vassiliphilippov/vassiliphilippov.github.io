child psychology

1. NOUN

/ tʃaɪld saɪˌkɑlədʒi / (AmE), / tʃaɪld saɪˈkɒlədʒi / (BrE)
The branch of psychology concerned with the social, emotional, and cognitive development of children.
the study of children's psychological development

Examples

She decided to specialize in child psychology during her university studies.
Understanding child psychology is crucial for effective teaching in early childhood education.
Books on child psychology often discuss how children form relationships and learn social skills.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: developmental psychology - the study of psychological growth and change throughout life
  • Degree Higher: psychology - the scientific study of the mind and behavior
