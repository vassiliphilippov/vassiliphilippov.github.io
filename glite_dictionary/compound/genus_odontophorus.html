genus Odontophorus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs oʊˌdɒn.təˈfɔːr.əs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs əʊˌdɒn.təˈfɔː.rəs / (BrE)
A genus of Central and South American crested partridges resembling quails, sometimes classified in a distinct subfamily or family.
genus of crested partridges in the Americas

Examples

The genus Odontophorus includes several species of crested partridges found in rainforests of Central and South America.
Recent studies suggest that the genus Odontophorus may need to be classified in its own family due to genetic differences.
Birdwatchers often search for unique species within the genus Odontophorus during expeditions in the Andes.

Etymology

From Greek 'odous, odont-' (tooth) + 'phoros' (bearing).
