cash grab

1. NOUN

/ kæʃ ɡræb / (AmE), / kæʃ ɡrab / (BrE)
A product, action, or effort mainly intended to make money quickly, often with little regard for quality or value.
product or action done just for profit
informal

Examples

Many fans accused the company of releasing the new game as a cash grab rather than to improve the experience.
Critics described the poorly made sequel as nothing more than a cash grab.
The lavish merchandise surrounding the film was widely seen as a cash grab by the studio.

Synset Examples

They criticized the movie as just a cash grab.
Many consider the merchandise a blatant cash grab.
The sequel felt like a cash grab to fans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: money-making scheme - an activity meant mainly to generate money
