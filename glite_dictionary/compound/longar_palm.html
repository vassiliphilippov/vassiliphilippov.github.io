longar palm

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɔŋɡər pɑm / (AmE), / ˈlɒŋɡə pɑːm / (BrE)
A tall fan palm of Africa, India, and Malaysia, yielding hard wood, sweet sap used for palm wine and sugar, and leaves used for thatching and weaving.
tall palm tree used for wood, sap, and leaves

Examples

The longar palm is commonly tapped for its sap, producing a sweet drink enjoyed in many villages.
Farmers use the leaves of the longar palm to make durable baskets and thatched roofs.
Because of its hard wood, the longar palm is valued in construction across rural areas.
