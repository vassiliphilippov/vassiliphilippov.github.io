rapid eye movement sleep

1. NOUN

/ ˈræpɪd aɪ ˈmuvmənt slip / (AmE), / ˈræpɪd aɪ ˈmuːvmənt sliːp / (BrE)
A stage of sleep characterized by rapid movement of the eyes, during which most dreaming occurs and brain activity is high.
stage of sleep with rapid eye movements and dreaming
medical

Examples

Scientists study rapid eye movement sleep to understand patterns of dreaming.
Most people experience several periods of rapid eye movement sleep each night.
Lack of sufficient rapid eye movement sleep may affect mood and memory.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: REM sleep - abbreviation for 'rapid eye movement sleep' referring to the same sleep stage
  • Antonym: non-REM sleep - the other main type of sleep, without rapid eye movement, usually dreamless
