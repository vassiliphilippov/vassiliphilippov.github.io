Kakatoe leadbeateri

1. NOUN

/ ˈkækəˌtoʊ liːdˈbiːtəri / (AmE), / ˈkækətəʊ liːdˈbiːtəri / (BrE)
A species of white Australian cockatoo with pink-tinged (roseate) plumage, also known as Major Mitchell's cockatoo.
a white Australian cockatoo with roseate plumage

Examples

The Kakatoe leadbeateri is easily recognizable by its striking pink crest and soft plumage.
Birdwatchers traveled to the outback to catch a glimpse of the elusive Kakatoe leadbeateri.
Conservation groups are working to protect the habitat of the Kakatoe leadbeateri in southern Australia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Major Mitchell's cockatoo - alternative common name for Kakatoe leadbeateri

Etymology

Named after Benjamin Leadbeater, a British naturalist.
