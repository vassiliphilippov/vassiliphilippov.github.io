infrared technology

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪnfrəˈrɛd tɛkˈnɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ˌɪnfrəˈrɛd tɛkˈnɒlədʒi / (BrE)
Technology that uses infrared radiation, especially for detecting or transmitting information based on heat patterns or energy.
technology using infrared radiation

Examples

Many security systems rely on infrared technology to monitor movement at night.
Researchers are developing new medical devices based on infrared technology.
Remote controls often use infrared technology to send signals to televisions.

Synset Examples

He uses infrared technology to detect heat patterns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thermal imaging - technique of forming images using infrared radiation

Etymology

From 'infrared' (from Latin 'infra' meaning 'below' and 'red', referring to wavelengths below visible red) and 'technology'.
