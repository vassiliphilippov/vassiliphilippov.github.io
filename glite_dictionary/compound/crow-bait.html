crow-bait

1. NOUN

/ ˈkroʊˌbeɪt / (AmE), / ˈkrəʊˌbeɪt / (BrE)
An extremely thin and weak horse, especially one considered near death and likely to become carrion.
emaciated horse near death

Examples

After years of hard labor, the once-proud stallion became little more than crow-bait.
The farmer sadly sold his old mare, calling her nothing but crow-bait now.
No one wanted to buy the crow-bait at the auction, as its ribs showed through its hide.

Etymology

From 'crow' (the bird, associated with carrion) + 'bait' (something that attracts).
