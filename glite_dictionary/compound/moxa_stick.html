moxa stick

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑksə stɪk / (AmE), / ˈmɒksə stɪk / (BrE)
A stick made from dried mugwort leaves, used in traditional East Asian medicine for moxibustion therapy by burning it near the skin.
stick for moxibustion therapy
medical

Examples

The practitioner heated the moxa stick and hovered it above the patient's acupuncture points.
During her session, a gentle warmth from the smoldering moxa stick was applied to help ease her pain.
Some people use a moxa stick at home as part of their alternative therapy routine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moxibustion - traditional therapy that burns moxa on the skin

Etymology

From Japanese 'moxa', via Dutch, from Chinese 'mógū' (mugwort).
