beach pea

1. NOUN

/ ˈbiːtʃ piː / (AmE), / ˈbiːtʃ piː / (BrE)
a wild leguminous plant of seashores in the northern temperate zone, with tough roots and purple flowers, often used to stabilize sand
a wild seaside plant with purple flowers

Examples

The beach pea grows along sandy coastal dunes, helping to prevent soil erosion.
We spotted several blooming beach peas during our walk by the shore.
Gardeners sometimes plant beach pea to stabilize dunes and attract pollinators.
