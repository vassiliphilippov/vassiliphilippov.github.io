mustard tree

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌstərd tri / (AmE), / ˈmʌstəd triː / (BrE)
an evergreen South American shrub, naturalized in the United States, that is sometimes responsible for poisoning livestock
a poisonous evergreen South American shrub

Examples

The farmer lost several cows after they ate leaves from the mustard tree.
Researchers are studying the spread of the mustard tree across southern states.
You should be careful not to let livestock graze near a mustard tree in that area.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmʌstərd triː / (AmE), / ˈmʌstəd triː / (BrE)
a small evergreen tree or shrub of the genus Salvadora, native to arid regions, whose fibrous branches are used for brushing teeth and whose shoots are used as fodder.
evergreen shrub/tree (genus Salvadora) used for brushing teeth

Examples

In some regions, the mustard tree is valued for its branches, which are used as natural toothbrushes.
The arid landscape was dotted with mustard trees providing shade and food for camels.
Ash from the burned mustard tree is sometimes collected and used as a source of salt.
