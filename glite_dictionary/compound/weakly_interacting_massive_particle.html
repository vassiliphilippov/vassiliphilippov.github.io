weakly interacting massive particle

1. NOUN

/ ˈwik.li ɪn.tɚˈækt.ɪŋ ˈmæs.ɪv ˈpɑr.tɪ.kəl / (AmE), / ˈwiːk.li ɪn.təˈræk.tɪŋ ˈmæs.ɪv ˈpɑː.tɪ.kəl / (BrE)
a hypothetical subatomic particle with large mass that interacts weakly with normal matter, thought to be a component of dark matter in the universe
hypothetical particle; candidate for dark matter

Examples

Scientists have proposed the existence of weakly interacting massive particles to explain the gravitational effects observed in galaxies.
The search for weakly interacting massive particles is a major focus in the study of dark matter.
Detecting a weakly interacting massive particle would revolutionize our understanding of the universe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: WIMP - abbreviation for 'weakly interacting massive particle'
  • Synonym: dark matter - matter hypothesized to account for gravitational effects but invisible to current instruments
