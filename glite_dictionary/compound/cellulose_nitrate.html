cellulose nitrate

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛljuˌloʊs ˈnaɪtreɪt / (AmE), / ˈsɛljʊˌləʊs ˈnaɪtreɪt / (BrE)
a highly flammable compound made by treating cellulose with nitric acid, used in making lacquers, films, and explosives
flammable compound used in lacquers and explosives

Examples

Early photographic films were made with cellulose nitrate, which proved to be highly flammable.
The furniture's glossy finish is due to a cellulose nitrate lacquer applied to the wood.
Due to its explosive properties, cellulose nitrate must be stored and handled with great care.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nitrocellulose - another term for cellulose nitrate, widely used synonym

Etymology

Formed from cellulose (from Latin 'cellula', small cell) and nitrate (from Latin 'nitras', salt of nitric acid).
