Racketeer Influenced and Corrupt Organizations Act

1. NOUN

/ ˌræ.kəˈtɪr ɪnˈfluːənst ənd kəˈrʌpt ˌɔːr.ɡən.aɪˈzeɪ.ʃənz ækt / (AmE), / ˌrak.ɪˈtɪə ɪnˈfluːənst ənd kəˈrʌpt ˌɔː.ɡən.aɪˈzeɪ.ʃənz ækt / (BrE)
a U.S. federal law enacted in 1970 to combat organized crime by providing extended penalties and forfeiture for acts performed as part of a criminal organization
U.S. law targeting organized crime (RICO Act)
legal american

Examples

The Racketeer Influenced and Corrupt Organizations Act was used to prosecute members of the criminal enterprise.
Lawyers discussed whether the Racketeer Influenced and Corrupt Organizations Act applied to the ongoing investigation.
Since its passage, the Racketeer Influenced and Corrupt Organizations Act has been a key tool in fighting organized crime.

Etymology

RICO is an acronym from the full name of the law, enacted by the U.S. Congress in 1970.
