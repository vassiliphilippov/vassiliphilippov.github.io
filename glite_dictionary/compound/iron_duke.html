Iron Duke

1. PROPN

/ ˈaɪrn ˌduːk / (AmE), / ˈaɪən ˌdjuːk / (BrE)
a nickname for Arthur Wellesley, 1st Duke of Wellington (1769–1852), British general and statesman best known for defeating Napoleon at Waterloo and later serving as Prime Minister.
nickname for the Duke of Wellington
british

Examples

The Iron Duke led his troops to victory at the Battle of Waterloo.
Many admire the determination and leadership shown by the Iron Duke during his career.
The house where the Iron Duke lived has become a historical site.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Duke of Wellington - Arthur Wellesley, British general and Prime Minister

Etymology

Refers to Arthur Wellesley, 1st Duke of Wellington, whose firmness in politics and battle earned him the epithet.
