B complex

1. NOUN

/ bi ˈkɑmplɛks / (AmE), / bi ˈkɒmplɛks / (BrE)
a group of water-soluble vitamins that were once thought to be a single vitamin but are now recognized as several distinct B vitamins important for metabolism
group of B vitamins

Examples

My doctor recommended taking a B complex supplement for increased energy.
This cereal is fortified with B complex vitamins essential for good health.
Deficiency in the B complex can lead to a variety of health problems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: B vitamin - any of several essential water-soluble vitamins formerly considered a single vitamin
