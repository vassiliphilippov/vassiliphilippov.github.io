cold storage

1. NOUN

/ koʊld ˈstɔɹ.ɪdʒ / (AmE), / kəʊld ˈstɔː.rɪdʒ / (BrE)
a place or system for keeping goods at low temperatures to preserve them, especially food or chemicals
storage at low temperatures for preservation

Examples

The vaccine must be kept in cold storage until it is ready to be distributed.
Meat is shipped in cold storage to prevent it from spoiling during transport.
Due to a malfunction, the cold storage unit at the warehouse needed to be repaired.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: refrigeration - the process of cooling something to preserve it

2. NOUN

/ koʊld ˈstɔɹ.ɪdʒ / (AmE), / kəʊld ˈstɔː.rɪdʒ / (BrE)
A state in which something is kept unused, inactive, or postponed, often temporarily.
state of being unused or postponed

Examples

After the merger talks failed, the project was put into cold storage indefinitely.
His proposal remained in cold storage until the company's priorities changed.
Many creative ideas end up in cold storage when funding runs out.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abeyance - temporary inactivity or suspension
  • Synonym: on hold - postponed or suspended temporarily

Etymology

From the literal sense of 'cold storage,' extended figuratively to mean postponement or inactivity.
