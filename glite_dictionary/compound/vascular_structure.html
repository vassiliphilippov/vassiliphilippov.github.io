vascular structure

1. NOUN

/ ˈvæs.kjə.lɚ ˈstrʌk.tʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈvæs.kjʊ.lə ˈstrʌk.tʃə / (BrE)
a tissue or organ made up of, or containing, blood vessels
tissue or organ with blood vessels
medical

Examples

The surgeon carefully avoided damaging any vascular structure during the operation.
Inflammation in a vascular structure can lead to serious health complications.
The retina is a highly vascular structure in the human eye.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blood vessel - a tubular structure carrying blood through the tissues and organs
  • Antonym: avascular - lacking blood vessels

Etymology

From Latin 'vasculum' (small vessel) and 'structure' (from Latin 'structura', a building).
