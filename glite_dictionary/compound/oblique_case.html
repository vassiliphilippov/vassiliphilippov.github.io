oblique case

1. NOUN

/ əˈblik keɪs / (AmE), / əˈbliːk keɪs / (BrE)
any grammatical case other than the nominative, used in some languages to indicate functions such as the object of a verb or a preposition
grammatical case other than the nominative

Examples

In Latin, the oblique case is used to mark objects and other grammatical roles besides the subject.
Hindi has an oblique case that governs how nouns change with certain postpositions.
Students had to identify which nouns were in the oblique case in the given sentence.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: nominative case - grammatical case marking the subject of a verb

Etymology

From Latin 'obliquus' (slanting, indirect) + English 'case' (grammatical category).
