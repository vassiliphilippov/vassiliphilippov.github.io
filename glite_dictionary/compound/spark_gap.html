spark gap

1. NOUN

/ spɑrk gæp / (AmE), / spɑːk ɡap / (BrE)
A device consisting of two electrodes separated by a gap, across which an electric spark can be passed, typically used in ignition systems or for controlling electrical discharges.
device with electrodes for electrical spark

Examples

The engineer inspected the spark gap to ensure it was clean for proper ignition.
Modern radios sometimes use a spark gap for high-voltage discharge protection.
A worn spark gap can cause misfiring in automobile engines.

2. NOUN

/ spɑrk ɡæp / (AmE), / spɑːk ɡap / (BrE)
A space between two electrical conductors where a spark can jump, typically used for controlling or measuring high voltages.
gap for a spark in electrical devices

Examples

When a spark gap is used, it allows excess voltage to safely discharge.
Engineers inspected the spark gap in the old radio to ensure proper function.
A lightning strike can jump across a spark gap and damage equipment if not properly grounded.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: electrode - a conductor through which electricity enters or leaves a medium
