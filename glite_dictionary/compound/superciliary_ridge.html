superciliary ridge

1. NOUN

/ ˌsuː.pɚˈsɪl.i.ˌɛr.i rɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌsuː.pəˈsɪl.i.ˌɛə.ri rɪdʒ / (BrE)
a ridge on the frontal bone located above the eye socket, forming the prominence of the brow
ridge above the eye socket (anatomy)
medical

Examples

The pronounced superciliary ridge is a distinguishing feature in the skulls of some early human ancestors.
In anatomy class, we learned to identify the superciliary ridge above the eye socket.
The injury left a bruise just below his superciliary ridge.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brow ridge - an alternative term for the ridge above the eye socket

Etymology

From Latin 'supercilium' (eyebrow) and English 'ridge'.
