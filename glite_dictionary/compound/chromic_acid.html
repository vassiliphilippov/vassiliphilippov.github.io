chromic acid

1. NOUN

/ ˈkroʊmɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / ˈkrəʊmɪk ˈasɪd / (BrE)
an unstable, strong acid known mainly in aqueous solution or in the form of its chromate salts, containing hexavalent chromium
an unstable, strong acid of hexavalent chromium
medical

Examples

Chromic acid is commonly used as a powerful oxidizing agent in laboratory settings.
Contact with chromic acid requires strict safety precautions due to its corrosive nature.
The cleaning solution was prepared by dissolving chromic acid in water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chromate - a salt or ester of chromic acid

Etymology

From 'chromic', relating to chromium, and 'acid'.
