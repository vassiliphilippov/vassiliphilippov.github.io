Bluegrass State

1. PROPN

/ ˈbluˌɡræs steɪt / (AmE), / ˈbluːˌɡrɑːs steɪt / (BrE)
a nickname for the state of Kentucky in the United States, known especially for horse breeding and bluegrass music
nickname for the state of Kentucky

Examples

Every spring, tourists flock to the Bluegrass State for the famous Kentucky Derby.
The Bluegrass State is celebrated for its rolling hills and thoroughbred horse farms.
He moved to the Bluegrass State to study music and enjoy the rural lifestyle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Kentucky - a state in the southeastern United States, commonly called the Bluegrass State

Etymology

Named after the bluegrass found in the region, and used as a popular nickname for Kentucky.
