at lightspeed

1. ADV

/ ət ˈlaɪtspiːd / (AmE), / ət ˈlaɪtsˌpiːd / (BrE)
very quickly; with great speed or rapidity
very quickly; extremely fast

Examples

The news of their engagement spread at lightspeed through social media.
Software updates are now released at lightspeed compared to a decade ago.
She typed the entire document at lightspeed, impressing her coworkers.

Synset Examples

She finished her report at lightspeed yesterday.
The project moved forward at lightspeed after the funding.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: at lightning speed - with great swiftness; very fast

Etymology

From scientific term 'lightspeed,' referring to the speed of light, used metaphorically for extreme speed.
