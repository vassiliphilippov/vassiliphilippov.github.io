flu epidemic

1. NOUN

/ flu ˌɛpɪˈdɛmɪk / (AmE), / flu ˌɛpɪˈdɛmɪk / (BrE)
a widespread outbreak of influenza affecting many people in a community, region, or population
widespread influenza outbreak

Examples

The city was hit hard by the flu epidemic last winter, with hospitals filled to capacity.
During the flu epidemic, schools and offices urged everyone to stay home if they felt unwell.
Researchers studied the spread of the flu epidemic to improve future vaccination strategies.

Synset Examples

The flu epidemic spread rapidly through the school.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: outbreak - a sudden occurrence of a disease in a community
  • Degree Higher: pandemic - an epidemic that has spread over multiple countries or continents
  • Degree Lower: case - an individual occurrence of a disease
