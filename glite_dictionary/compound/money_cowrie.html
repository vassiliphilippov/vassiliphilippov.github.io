money cowrie

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌni ˈkaʊri / (AmE), / ˈmʌni ˈkaʊəri / (BrE)
a cowrie shell that was used as money in parts of the southern Pacific and Africa
cowrie shell used as money

Examples

The ancient traders exchanged money cowries for goods such as salt and fabric.
A collection of money cowrie shells was uncovered at the archaeological site.
In some cultures, the money cowrie symbolized wealth and status.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cowrie - a small marine mollusk with a glossy, brightly patterned shell
