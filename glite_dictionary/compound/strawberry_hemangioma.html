strawberry hemangioma

1. NOUN

/ ˈstrɔːˌbɛri hɪˌmændʒiˈoʊmə / (AmE), / ˈstrɒˌbəri hɪˌmandʒiˈəʊmə / (BrE)
A congenital, bright red, raised vascular birthmark that resembles a strawberry and usually decreases in size during childhood.
red vascular birthmark in infants
medical

Examples

The pediatrician explained that the red mark on her arm was a strawberry hemangioma and would likely fade over time.
Many strawberry hemangiomas do not require treatment unless they interfere with vision or breathing.
Parents were relieved to learn that a strawberry hemangioma is generally harmless and tends to shrink as the child grows.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: birthmark - a benign irregularity on the skin present at birth

Etymology

From 'strawberry' due to its appearance, and medical term 'hemangioma' from Greek 'haima' (blood) + 'angeion' (vessel) + '-oma' (tumor).
