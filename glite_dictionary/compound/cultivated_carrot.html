cultivated carrot

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌltɪˌveɪtɪd ˈkæɹət / (AmE), / ˈkʌltɪˌveɪtɪd ˈkærət / (BrE)
A variety of carrot (Daucus carota subsp. sativus) widely grown for its long, orange edible root.
widely grown edible carrot plant

Examples

The cultivated carrot is an important vegetable crop in many countries.
Farmers grow cultivated carrot varieties with different shapes and flavors.
Unlike wild relatives, the cultivated carrot produces a sweet, large root ideal for eating raw or cooked.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carrot - a root vegetable, typically orange in color, eaten as a vegetable
