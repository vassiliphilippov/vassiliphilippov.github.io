sucker punch

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌkər pʌnt͡ʃ / (AmE), / ˈsʌkə pʌntʃ / (BrE)
A punch delivered unexpectedly, especially without warning or from behind.
an unexpected punch

Examples

He was knocked to the ground by a sucker punch during the argument.
The boxer received a warning after delivering a sucker punch to his opponent.
The disagreement escalated when someone threw a sucker punch without any provocation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ambush - an unexpected attack, often from a concealed position
  • Antonym: warning - advance notice of danger or attack

2. NOUN

/ ˈsʌkər pʌntʃ / (AmE), / ˈsʌkə pʌntʃ / (BrE)
an unexpected blow or attack, often delivered without warning and considered unfair, especially in a physical or metaphorical sense
unexpected or unfair blow or attack
informal american

Examples

The boxer caught his opponent off guard with a sucker punch in the final round.
The announcement of layoffs came as a sucker punch to the employees.
Her sarcastic remark landed like a sucker punch during the meeting.

Synset Examples

Her sudden criticism felt like a sucker punch.
He delivered a sucker punch with that last-minute change.
The unexpected news was a real sucker punch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blow - a hit with a hand, weapon, or hard object
  • Synonym: ambush - a surprise attack from a hidden position
  • Antonym: warning - advance notice or signal of something to come (opposite of surprise)

Etymology

From American slang: 'sucker' (a gullible person) + 'punch' (a blow with the fist).
