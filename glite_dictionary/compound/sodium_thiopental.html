sodium thiopental

1. NOUN

/ ˌsoʊdiəm θaɪˈoʊpɛntæl / (AmE), / ˌsəʊdiəm θaɪˈəʊpɛntal / (BrE)
a long-acting barbiturate drug used as a sedative and anesthetic, especially in medical procedures
barbiturate sedative and anesthetic
medical

Examples

The anesthesiologist administered sodium thiopental to induce unconsciousness before surgery.
In the past, sodium thiopental was commonly used for rapid anesthesia induction.
Because of its barbiturate effects, sodium thiopental must be carefully dosed by medical professionals.

Etymology

From 'sodium', the chemical element, and 'thiopental', derived from 'thio' (containing sulfur) and 'pental' (relating to five-carbon structure), indicating its chemical composition.
