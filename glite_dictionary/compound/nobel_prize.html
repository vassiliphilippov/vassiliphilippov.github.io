Nobel prize

1. NOUN

/ noʊˈbɛl praɪz / (AmE), / nəʊˈbɛl praɪz / (BrE)
An annual international award given for outstanding achievements in physics, chemistry, physiology or medicine, literature, economics, or for peace efforts.
international award for achievements in various fields

Examples

She was awarded the Nobel prize in literature for her profound and innovative writing.
Many scientists dream of winning the Nobel prize for their groundbreaking research.
The Nobel prize ceremony is held every year in December in Stockholm.

Etymology

Named after Alfred Nobel, Swedish inventor and philanthropist, who established the prizes in his will (1895).
