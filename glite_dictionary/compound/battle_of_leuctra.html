battle of Leuctra

1. PROPN

/ ˈbæt̬l əv ˈluːk.trə / (AmE), / ˈbætl əv ˈljuːktrə / (BrE)
a battle fought in 371 BC near Leuctra in which Thebes defeated Sparta, ending Sparta's military dominance in Greece
the 371 BC battle where Thebes defeated Sparta

Examples

The battle of Leuctra marked a turning point in ancient Greek history.
Historians often discuss the strategies employed during the battle of Leuctra.
After the battle of Leuctra, Thebes emerged as a dominant power in Greece.

Etymology

From Leuctra, the site in Boeotia, Greece, where the battle took place.
