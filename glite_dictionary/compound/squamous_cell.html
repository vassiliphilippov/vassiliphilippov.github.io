squamous cell

1. NOUN

/ ˈskweɪməs sɛl / (AmE), / ˈskweɪməs sɛl / (BrE)
an epithelial cell that is flat and plate-like, typically forming a single layer in certain types of epithelial tissue
a flat, plate-like epithelial cell
medical

Examples

The pathologist identified a cluster of abnormal squamous cells in the biopsy sample.
Damage to squamous cells can lead to various skin disorders and cancers.
A squamous cell carcinoma is a common form of skin cancer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: epithelial cell - a cell forming the covering or lining of body surfaces

Etymology

From Latin squamosus (scaly, scale-covered), referring to the flat, scale-like appearance of these cells.
