heavy metal

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛvi ˈmɛt̬l̩ / (AmE), / ˈhɛvi ˈmɛtl / (BrE)
a metallic element having a high density or high atomic weight, especially those that are toxic such as lead or mercury
dense, often toxic, metallic element

Examples

Many environmental regulations limit the release of heavy metal waste into rivers.
Some heavy metals, like cadmium and mercury, can accumulate in living organisms and cause health problems.
Exposure to heavy metal fumes in factories poses a significant risk to workers.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɛvi ˈmɛtəl / (AmE), / ˌhɛvi ˈmɛtəl / (BrE)
A type of loud and aggressive rock music characterized by strong rhythms, amplified distortion, and often dramatic lyrics about violence or fantasy.
genre of loud, amplified rock music

Examples

He has been a fan of heavy metal since high school, especially bands from the 1980s.
The concert featured several heavy metal groups performing to a crowd of enthusiastic fans.
Some people find heavy metal too loud, but others appreciate its energy and intensity.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: rock - A broad genre of popular music with a strong rhythmic component and use of electric guitars.
  • Degree Lower: punk rock - A fast, loud, and aggressive form of rock music characteristically anti-establishment in lyrics and style.
  • Degree Higher: thrash metal - A faster, more aggressive subgenre of heavy metal music.

Etymology

Originally used in the 1960s and 1970s, possibly from the phrase 'heavy metal thunder' in the song 'Born to be Wild' (1968), and earlier from chemistry referring to dense metallic elements.

3. NOUN

/ ˌhɛvi ˈmɛtəl / (AmE), / ˌhɛvi ˈmɛtəl / (BrE)
a type of loud, aggressive rock music characterized by amplified distortion, extended guitar solos, emphatic beats, and powerful vocals
loud, aggressive rock music genre

Examples

Liam's favorite band plays heavy metal with thunderous guitar riffs and powerful vocals.
After hearing a heavy metal concert live, she became a fan of the genre.
Not everyone enjoys the intensity of heavy metal, but its loyal fans are passionate.

Synset Examples

The band played heavy metal at the festival.
He loves listening to heavy metal music.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: hard rock - a style of rock music with heavy use of electric guitars but less intense than heavy metal

Etymology

From 'heavy' (intense) and 'metal,' adopted in the late 1960s to describe a new style of rock music.
