second moment

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛkənd ˈmoʊmənt / (AmE), / ˈsɛkənd ˈməʊmənt / (BrE)
In statistics, the expected value of the square of the deviations of a random variable from the origin, used to measure spread or variation.
expected value of the squared deviation from the origin (statistics)

Examples

The second moment of a probability distribution helps quantify its dispersion.
In physics, the second moment is often related to the moment of inertia.
Calculating the second moment about the origin is crucial in statistical analysis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: variance - expected value of the squared deviation from the mean
