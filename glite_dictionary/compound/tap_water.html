tap water

1. NOUN

/ ˈtæp ˌwɔtɚ / (AmE), / ˈtæp ˌwɔːtə / (BrE)
water that comes directly from a tap or faucet, typically used for drinking, cooking, or cleaning, as supplied by a public or private water system
water supplied from a tap or faucet

Examples

She filled her glass with tap water rather than using bottled water.
Many restaurants in Europe will serve you tap water if you ask for water with your meal.
Some people prefer to filter their tap water before drinking it.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: bottled water - water sold in bottles for drinking
  • Antonym: filtered water - water that has been passed through a filter to remove impurities
