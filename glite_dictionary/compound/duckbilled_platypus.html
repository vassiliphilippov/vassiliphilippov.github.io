duckbilled platypus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʌkˌbɪld ˈplætɪˌpʌs / (AmE), / ˈdʌkˌbɪld ˈplætɪpəs / (BrE)
a small, egg-laying mammal native to Australia and Tasmania, with dense fur, a broad, flat bill, a flat tail, and webbed feet; the only species in the family Ornithorhynchidae
Australian egg-laying mammal with a bill and webbed feet

Examples

The duckbilled platypus is one of the few mammals that lay eggs.
Researchers in Australia are studying the duckbilled platypus to better understand monotremes.
A duckbilled platypus uses its sensitive bill to hunt underwater for insects and small crustaceans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: platypus - the common name for the duckbilled platypus

Etymology

From 'duck-billed', referring to its bill, and Greek 'platypous' (flat-footed): 'platys' (broad) + 'pous' (foot).
