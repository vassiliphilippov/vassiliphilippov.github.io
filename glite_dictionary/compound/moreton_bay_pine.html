Moreton Bay pine

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɔːrtən ˈbeɪ paɪn / (AmE), / ˌmɔːtən ˈbeɪ paɪn / (BrE)
A large pine tree native to Australia and New Guinea, valued for its light, even-textured wood.
Australian/New Guinea pine tree

Examples

The Moreton Bay pine is commonly planted for its valuable timber.
Carpenters appreciate the light color and even texture of Moreton Bay pine wood.
In Queensland, vast plantations of Moreton Bay pine support the local timber industry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pine - any coniferous tree of the genus Pinus

Etymology

Named after Moreton Bay, a region in Queensland, Australia.
