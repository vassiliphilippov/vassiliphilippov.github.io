magnitude relation

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæɡ.nəˌtuːd rɪˈleɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˈmæɡ.nɪ.tjuːd rɪˈleɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
a relationship between two or more magnitudes, such as size, amount, or degree, often comparing their quantities or values
relationship between magnitudes

Examples

The magnitude relation between the two stars allows astronomers to determine their relative brightness.
Physicists often study the magnitude relation of forces acting on an object.
A clear understanding of the magnitude relation helps in comparing different quantities in mathematics.

Etymology

From Latin 'magnitudo' (greatness, size) and 'relatio' (a bringing back, relation).
