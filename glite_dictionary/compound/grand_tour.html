grand tour

1. NOUN

/ ɡrænd tʊr / (AmE), / ɡrænd tɔː / (BrE)
an organized sightseeing tour, especially of a building, institution, or notable location, often covering major features or attractions.
a sightseeing tour of a place

Examples

During the open house, visitors were given a grand tour of the newly renovated hospital.
Our guide took us on a grand tour of the historic college campus, highlighting its oldest buildings.
He arranged a grand tour of the theater before the performance began.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tour - a journey for pleasure in which several different places are visited

Etymology

Originally referred to an extended tour of Europe by young aristocrats, from French 'grand tour' (great journey).

2. NOUN

/ ɡrænd tʊr / (AmE), / ɡrænd tɔː / (BrE)
an extended journey through Europe, especially undertaken by wealthy young Englishmen in the 18th century as part of their education and cultural enrichment
historic European educational journey

Examples

After finishing university, Henry embarked on his grand tour of the European continent.
Many famous artists and writers were inspired by experiences during their grand tour in the 1700s.
The tradition of the grand tour influenced the development of art collections and museums in England.

Etymology

From French 'grand tour' (great journey), popularized in English during the 17th–18th centuries.
