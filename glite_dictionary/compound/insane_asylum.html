insane asylum

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈseɪn əˈsaɪləm / (AmE), / ɪnˈseɪn əˈsaɪləm / (BrE)
a hospital or institution for the care and treatment of people with severe mental disorders; now considered an outdated and often offensive term
psychiatric hospital (historical, outdated)
archaic offensive

Examples

During the 19th century, many people with mental illness were sent to the insane asylum.
The history of the insane asylum reveals much about changing attitudes toward mental health.
She researched the conditions in a local insane asylum for her novel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: psychiatric hospital - a modern hospital for treating mental illness

Etymology

From 'insane' (from Latin insanus, 'mad') + 'asylum' (from Greek asylon, 'refuge').
