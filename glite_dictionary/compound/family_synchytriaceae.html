family Synchytriaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli sɪŋˌkaɪtrɪˈeɪsiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli sɪŋˌkɪtrɪˈiːsiː / (BrE)
A family of fungi belonging to the order Chytridiales, comprising species known for their parasitic relationships with plants.
a family of fungi in the order Chytridiales

Examples

The family Synchytriaceae includes several species that infect potatoes and other crops.
Scientists study the family Synchytriaceae to better understand plant pathogens.
Members of the family Synchytriaceae are characterized by their unique reproductive structures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Chytridiales - an order of fungi including the Synchytriaceae family

Etymology

From scientific Latin: 'Synchytrium' (type genus) + '-aceae' (suffix denoting a family in botanical nomenclature).
