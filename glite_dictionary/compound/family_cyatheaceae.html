family Cyatheaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæmɪli saɪˌæθiˈeɪsiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli saɪˌæθiˈeɪsiː / (BrE)
a family of tropical tree ferns known for their large, arborescent forms, primarily found in warm regions
a family of tropical tree ferns

Examples

Many species in the family Cyatheaceae thrive in the humid forests of Southeast Asia.
The family Cyatheaceae includes some of the tallest tree ferns in the world.
Botanists have identified several new members of the family Cyatheaceae in recent years.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the genus name Cyathea, itself from Greek 'kyatheion' (little cup), referring to the shape of a part of the plant.
