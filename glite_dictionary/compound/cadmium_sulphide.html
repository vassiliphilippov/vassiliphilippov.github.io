cadmium sulphide

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæd.mi.əm ˈsʌl.faɪd / (AmE), / ˈkæd.mi.əm ˈsʌl.faɪd / (BrE)
a yellow crystalline compound of cadmium and sulfur, used as a pigment and in electronic devices
yellow compound of cadmium and sulfur

Examples

The artist used cadmium sulphide to create a bright yellow hue on the canvas.
Researchers studied the properties of cadmium sulphide crystals for use in solar cells.
Exposure to cadmium sulphide pigment should be limited due to its toxicity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cadmium - a soft, bluish-white metallic element used in batteries and pigments
  • Synonym: cadmium selenide - a compound of cadmium and selenium, used similarly as a pigment and semiconductor

Etymology

From cadmium + sulphide, with 'sulphide' from Latin 'sulphur' (sulfur).
