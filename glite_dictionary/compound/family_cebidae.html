family Cebidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ˈsiːbɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli ˈsiːbɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of New World monkeys, comprising all except marmosets and tamarins
family of New World monkeys (excluding marmosets and tamarins)

Examples

The family Cebidae includes capuchins, squirrel monkeys, and many others native to South and Central America.
Researchers have studied the social behavior of species belonging to the family Cebidae.
Unlike marmosets, monkeys of the family Cebidae have prehensile tails and complex social structures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cebidae - the scientific family of certain New World monkeys

Etymology

From Latin 'Cebidae', from Greek 'kēbos' (a long-tailed monkey).
