San Joaquin River

1. PROPN

/ sæn wɔːˈkiːn ˈrɪvər / (AmE), / san wɔːˈkiːn ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
a major river in central California that originates in the Sierra Nevada and flows northwest to join the Sacramento River, forming a large delta
river in central California, USA

Examples

The San Joaquin River provides vital water resources for California's Central Valley farms.
Flooding along the San Joaquin River can lead to significant agricultural and economic impacts.
Conservationists are working to restore salmon runs on the San Joaquin River.

Etymology

From Spanish 'San Joaquín', named after Saint Joachim.
