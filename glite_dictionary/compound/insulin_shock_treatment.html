insulin shock treatment

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪn.sə.lɪn ʃɑk ˈtrit.mənt / (AmE), / ˈɪn.sjʊ.lɪn ʃɒk ˈtriːt.mənt / (BrE)
a medical treatment in which large doses of insulin are administered to induce convulsions and coma, formerly used mainly for certain psychiatric disorders
inducing coma with insulin for medical treatment
medical

Examples

Insulin shock treatment was once used for patients with severe schizophrenia.
The risks associated with insulin shock treatment eventually led to its decline in use.
He studied the effects of insulin shock treatment on mental health disorders in the 1950s.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: electroconvulsive therapy - a treatment involving electric currents to induce controlled seizures in the brain

Etymology

From 'insulin', a hormone discovered in 1921, and 'shock treatment', a term for therapies inducing physiological shock.
