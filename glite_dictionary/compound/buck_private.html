buck private

1. NOUN

/ bʌk ˈpraɪvət / (AmE), / bʌk ˈpraɪvət / (BrE)
an enlisted man of the lowest rank in the U.S. Army or Marines
lowest enlisted rank in Army or Marines
american

Examples

After basic training, he served as a buck private in the infantry.
The buck private followed orders from his sergeant without question.
Many soldiers start their military careers as a buck private before rising through the ranks.

Synset Examples

our prisoner was just a private and knew nothing of value

Related Terms

  • Synonym: private - lowest enlisted rank in the Army or Marines

Etymology

Probably from 'buck,' meaning 'man' or 'fellow,' in American English slang, first attested in the late 19th century.
