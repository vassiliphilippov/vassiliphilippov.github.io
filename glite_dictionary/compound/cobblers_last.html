cobbler's last

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑblərz læst / (AmE), / ˈkɒbləz lɑːst / (BrE)
a solid form shaped like a human foot, used by a cobbler to make or repair shoes
shoe-shaped tool for shoemaking

Examples

The cobbler's last is essential for shaping leather into a proper shoe.
He inherited his grandfather's old cobbler's last and used it in his workshop.
A cobbler's last can come in various sizes to match different feet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: last - solid foot-shaped form used in shoemaking

Etymology

From 'cobbler' (shoemaker) and 'last' (Old English 'læste', meaning footprint or track).
