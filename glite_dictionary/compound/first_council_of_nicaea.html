First Council of Nicaea

1. PROPN

/ ˌfɝːst ˈkaʊnsəl əv naɪˈsiːə / (AmE), / ˌfɜːst ˈkaʊnsəl əv naɪˈsiːə / (BrE)
the first ecumenical council of the Christian Church, held in 325 AD, which produced the Nicene Creed and condemned Arianism as heresy
Christian council in 325 AD forming the Nicene Creed

Examples

The First Council of Nicaea played a crucial role in shaping early Christian doctrine.
Many historians study the decisions made at the First Council of Nicaea to understand the development of the Church.
The condemnation of Arianism was a major outcome of the First Council of Nicaea.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Nicene Creed - A statement of Christian faith formulated at the First Council of Nicaea

Etymology

From the Greek city name Nicaea (Νίκαια), where the council was held.
