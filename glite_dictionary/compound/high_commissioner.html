high commissioner

1. NOUN

/ haɪ kəˈmɪʃənɚ / (AmE), / haɪ kəˈmɪʃənə / (BrE)
a senior diplomatic official, usually serving as an ambassador between Commonwealth countries
senior diplomatic envoy between Commonwealth countries

Examples

The British high commissioner attended the ceremony in New Delhi.
Canada's high commissioner to Australia gave a speech on bilateral relations.
Several high commissioners gathered to discuss regional security issues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ambassador - an official representative sent by a country as its diplomatic envoy

Etymology

From 'high' (of great rank) and 'commissioner' (from commission), often used for diplomatic representatives within the Commonwealth.
