heavy weapon

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛvi ˈwɛpən / (AmE), / ˈhɛvi ˈwɛpən / (BrE)
a large but transportable weapon, such as artillery, mortars, or machine guns, used in military operations
large, transportable military weapon

Examples

The army deployed several heavy weapons to support the infantry units.
Carrying a heavy weapon requires a crew, not just a single soldier.
International treaties often regulate the use of heavy weapons in conflict zones.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: light weapon - a small, portable weapon easily carried by an individual
  • Synonym: artillery - large-caliber guns used in warfare on land
