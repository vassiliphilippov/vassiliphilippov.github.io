disc pack

1. NOUN

/ dɪsk pæk / (AmE), / dɪsk pæk / (BrE)
(computing) a portable unit consisting of multiple magnetic disks housed together for use in a disk storage device.
portable unit of magnetic disks in computing

Examples

The technician carefully inserted the disc pack into the storage unit for the nightly backup.
Older computers often required users to swap out one disc pack for another to access more data.
If the disc pack becomes damaged, all information stored on its magnetic disks can be lost.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hard disk - non-volatile storage device containing magnetic disks
