octadecanoic acid

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːktəˌdiːkəˈnoʊɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / ˌɒktəˌdiːkəˈnəʊɪk ˈasɪd / (BrE)
a saturated fatty acid found in animal and vegetable fats, commonly known as stearic acid
a saturated fatty acid (stearic acid)
medical

Examples

Octadecanoic acid is commonly found in both animal tallow and vegetable oils.
Researchers analyzed the octadecanoic acid content of various margarine samples.
Soap can be produced through the reaction of octadecanoic acid with a strong base.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stearic acid - another name for octadecanoic acid, a saturated fatty acid
  • Antonym: oleic acid - an unsaturated fatty acid found in fats and oils

Etymology

From Latin 'octo' (eight), 'decem' (ten), and '-anoic acid' (used in naming carboxylic acids).
