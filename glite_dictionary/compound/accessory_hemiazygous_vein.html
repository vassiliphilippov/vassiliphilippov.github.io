accessory hemiazygous vein

1. NOUN

/ əkˈsɛsəri ˌhɛmiˈeɪzɪɡəs veɪn / (AmE), / əkˈsɛsəri ˌhɛmiˈeɪzɪɡəs veɪn / (BrE)
a vein in the thorax formed by the union of the 4th to 7th posterior intercostal veins, which drains into the azygos vein
vein draining part of thorax into azygos vein
medical

Examples

The accessory hemiazygous vein collects blood from the upper left intercostal spaces.
A blockage in the accessory hemiazygous vein can affect circulation in the thoracic region.
Surgeons must carefully identify the accessory hemiazygous vein during thoracic procedures.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: azygos vein - main vein receiving blood from the thoracic wall
  • Synonym: hemiazygous vein - vein draining the lower left thoracic wall into the azygos vein

Etymology

From Latin 'accessorius' (additional), 'hemi-' (half), and 'azygos' (unpaired).
