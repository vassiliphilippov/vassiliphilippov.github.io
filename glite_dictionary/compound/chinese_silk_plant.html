Chinese silk plant

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃaɪniz sɪlk plænt / (AmE), / ˈtʃaɪniːz sɪlk plɑːnt / (BrE)
a tall perennial herb native to tropical Asia, cultivated for its woody stems that yield a fiber resembling flax
tall Asian plant grown for flax-like fiber

Examples

The Chinese silk plant is valued for its strong, flax-like fibers used in textiles.
Several Chinese silk plants were cultivated in the greenhouse for botanical research.
Farmers in tropical regions often grow the Chinese silk plant as an alternative fiber crop.
