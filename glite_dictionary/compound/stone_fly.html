stone fly

1. NOUN

/ ˈstoʊn flaɪ / (AmE), / ˈstəʊn flaɪ / (BrE)
a primitive winged insect with a flattened body, found near streams; its aquatic larvae have gills and live beneath stones; often used as bait by fishermen
winged insect; aquatic larvae; used as fishing bait

Examples

Fishers often use stone flies as bait due to their abundance in local streams.
The stone fly has gilled larvae that thrive beneath rocks in fast-flowing rivers.
During spring, the emergence of stone flies attracts many trout to the banks.
