equinoctial year

1. NOUN

/ ˌiːkwɪˈnɑkʃəl jɪr / (AmE), / ˌiːkwɪˈnɒkʃəl jɪə / (BrE)
The period of time taken by the Earth to complete one revolution around the sun, measured from one vernal equinox to the next, approximately 365.2424 days.
year measured between vernal equinoxes

Examples

The equinoctial year is slightly shorter than the sidereal year due to the precession of the equinoxes.
Astronomers consider the equinoctial year when defining calendars based on seasonal cycles.
The duration of the equinoctial year determines the timing of many cultural events tied to the vernal equinox.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tropical year - The time for the Earth to make one revolution relative to the vernal equinox

Etymology

From Latin aequinoctialis (of the equinox), from aequinoctium (equinox).
