pilot ladder

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪlət ˌlædər / (AmE), / ˈpaɪlət ˌladə / (BrE)
a rope or chain ladder with wooden or metal steps, used on ships for boarding from or to small boats, especially by pilots
nautical boarding ladder for ships

Examples

The pilot ladder hung down the side of the ship to allow easy boarding from the pilot boat.
Inspectors checked the condition of the pilot ladder before anyone attempted to climb it.
The captain waited until the harbor pilot had climbed the pilot ladder before greeting him on deck.
