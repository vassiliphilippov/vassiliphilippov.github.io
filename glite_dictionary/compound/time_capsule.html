time capsule

1. NOUN

/ taɪm ˈkæp.səl / (AmE), / taɪm ˈkæp.sjuːl / (BrE)
A container in which historical records or objects are placed to be preserved and discovered at a future date.
container for preserving items for the future

Examples

A time capsule was buried beneath the new school building to be opened in fifty years.
The team filled the time capsule with letters, photographs, and newspapers to share their story with future generations.
During the ceremony, the mayor placed a signed document into the time capsule.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: capsule - a small container or case
