Chilean bonito

1. NOUN

/ tʃɪˈliən bəˈniːtoʊ / (AmE), / tʃɪˈliːən bəˈniːtəʊ / (BrE)
A species of bonito fish found along the Pacific coast of the Americas, especially noted for its dark, oily flesh suitable for canning.
a Pacific bonito fish of the Americas

Examples

The fishermen brought in a large catch of Chilean bonito this morning.
Canned Chilean bonito is popular in many South American markets.
The rich flavor of Chilean bonito makes it ideal for certain traditional dishes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bonito - a type of medium-sized predatory fish related to the tuna
