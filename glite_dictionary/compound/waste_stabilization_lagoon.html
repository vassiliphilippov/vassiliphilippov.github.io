waste stabilization lagoon

1. NOUN

/ weɪst ˌstæbɪlɪˈzeɪʃən ləˈɡun / (AmE), / weɪst ˌstæbɪlʌɪˈzeɪʃən ləˈɡuːn / (BrE)
a large, shallow pond designed to treat wastewater through the interaction of sunlight, bacteria, and algae
pond for wastewater treatment

Examples

Many rural communities use waste stabilization lagoons to treat their sewage in an environmentally friendly way.
The waste stabilization lagoon relies on natural processes to break down organic matter in the water.
Designing a waste stabilization lagoon requires consideration of local climate and soil conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: waste stabilization pond - pond for treating wastewater with natural processes
