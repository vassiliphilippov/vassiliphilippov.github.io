atrial fibrillation

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪ.tri.əl ˌfɪb.rəˈleɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˈeɪ.tri.əl ˌfɪb.rɪˈleɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
an irregular and often rapid heartbeat caused by abnormal electrical impulses in the atria of the heart
irregular heartbeat of the heart's atria
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed him with atrial fibrillation after he complained of an irregular heartbeat.
People with atrial fibrillation are at increased risk of stroke and heart failure.
Her atrial fibrillation was effectively managed with medication and regular checkups.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arrhythmia - an abnormal rhythm of the heart

Etymology

From Latin 'atrium' (chamber, entrance) and 'fibrillatio' (twitching of fibers).
