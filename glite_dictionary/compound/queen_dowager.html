queen dowager

1. NOUN

/ kwiːn ˈdaʊ.ɪ.dʒɚ / (AmE), / kwiːn ˈdaʊ.ɪ.dʒə / (BrE)
the widow of a king, especially one who retains the title or rank of queen
widowed queen; widow of a king

Examples

After the king's death, she became the queen dowager and lived quietly at court.
The queen dowager maintained influence over political affairs even after her husband's passing.
A grand funeral was held for the beloved queen dowager attended by the entire royal family.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: queen consort - the wife of a reigning king

Etymology

From 'queen' and 'dowager', the latter from Old French *dowagere* (widow with a dowry).
