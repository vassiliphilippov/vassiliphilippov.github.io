cantaloup vine

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæn.təˌluːp vaɪn / (AmE), / ˈkæn.tə.luːp vaɪn / (BrE)
a variety of muskmelon plant that produces fruit with a tan rind and orange flesh, commonly known as cantaloupe
variety of muskmelon plant (cantaloupe vine)

Examples

The cantaloup vine thrives in warm climates with plenty of sunshine.
After planting the cantaloup vine, gardeners eagerly awaited its sweet fruit.
Pests can damage the leaves of the cantaloup vine before the melons are ready to harvest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: muskmelon - a melon of the species Cucumis melo, including various varieties

Etymology

Cantaloup from French, named after Cantalupo, Italy, where the melon was introduced to Europe.
