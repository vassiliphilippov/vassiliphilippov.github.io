Maximilian's sunflower

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæksɪˈmɪliənz ˈsʌnˌflaʊɚ / (AmE), / ˌmæksɪˈmɪliənz ˈsʌnˌflaʊə / (BrE)
A tall perennial plant native to central United States and Canada, with golden-yellow flowers, belonging to the species Helianthus maximiliani.
tall perennial North American sunflower

Examples

The garden was bright with Maximilian's sunflowers swaying in the autumn breeze.
Maximilian's sunflower attracts a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
She identified the tall, yellow blooms as Maximilian's sunflower during the nature walk.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sunflower - a tall plant with a large yellow flower and edible seeds

Etymology

Named after Prince Maximilian of Wied-Neuwied, a German explorer and naturalist.
