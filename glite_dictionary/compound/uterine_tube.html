uterine tube

1. NOUN

/ ˈjuːtɚɪn tuːb / (AmE), / ˈjuːtəriːn tjuːb / (BrE)
either of a pair of tubes that conduct eggs from the ovary to the uterus in female mammals; also known as a fallopian tube
tube carrying egg from ovary to uterus
medical

Examples

The egg travels through the uterine tube after ovulation.
A blockage in the uterine tube can cause infertility in women.
During surgery, the doctor examined both uterine tubes for abnormalities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fallopian tube - tube that carries eggs from the ovary to the uterus; synonym for uterine tube

Etymology

From Latin 'uterinus' (of the womb) + 'tube' (from Latin 'tubus', pipe or tube).
