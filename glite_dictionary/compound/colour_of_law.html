colour of law

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌlər əv lɔ / (AmE), / ˈkʌlə əv lɔː / (BrE)
(Law) The appearance or semblance of legal right, when in fact there is no law supporting the act; something done with apparent legal authority but in violation of the law.
apparent but illegitimate legal authority
legal british

Examples

The officer was charged for violating civil rights under the colour of law.
Actions justified under the colour of law may still be illegal if they exceed authority.
The organization investigated cases of abuse carried out under the colour of law.

Synset Examples

the plaintiff claimed that under color of law the officer had deprived him of his civil rights

Related Terms

  • Synonym: color of law - the American English spelling of 'colour of law'

Etymology

From legal use of ‘colour’ meaning 'appearance' or 'semblance,' and 'law' meaning legal authority.
