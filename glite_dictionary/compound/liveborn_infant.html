liveborn infant

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪvˌbɔrn ˈɪnfənt / (AmE), / ˈlaɪvbɔːn ˈɪnfənt / (BrE)
An infant who shows signs of life after birth, such as breathing, heartbeat, or movement.
infant alive after birth
medical

Examples

The hospital keeps detailed records of every liveborn infant to ensure proper care and documentation.
In legal terms, a liveborn infant must show evidence of breathing or heartbeat after delivery.
Advances in medicine have improved the survival rates of premature liveborn infants.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: stillborn - an infant born without signs of life
