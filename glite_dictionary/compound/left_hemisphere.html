left hemisphere

1. NOUN

/ lɛft ˈhɛmɪˌsfɪr / (AmE), / lɛft ˈhɛmɪsˌfɪə / (BrE)
the left half of the brain, responsible for controlling the right side of the body and involved in language and reasoning functions
left half of the brain
medical

Examples

Damage to the left hemisphere can affect a person's ability to speak clearly.
The left hemisphere is often associated with logical thinking and language skills.
Doctors studied the activity in the left hemisphere during the reading task.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: right hemisphere - the right half of the brain, controls left side of the body
