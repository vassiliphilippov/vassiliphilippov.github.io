Virginia cowslip

1. NOUN

/ vərˈdʒɪniə ˈkaʊslɪp / (AmE), / vəˈdʒɪnɪə ˈkaʊslɪp / (BrE)
a smooth, upright herb native to eastern North America, with broad leaves and clusters of blue flowers that are pink in bud
North American herb with blue flowers

Examples

The woods were filled with blooming Virginia cowslip in early spring.
Gardeners often plant Virginia cowslip for its attractive blue flowers.
You can spot Virginia cowslip growing in moist, shady areas along streams.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bluebell - other common name for Mertensia virginica
