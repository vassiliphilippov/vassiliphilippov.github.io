vitreous body

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪtriəs ˈbɑdi / (AmE), / ˈvɪtrɪəs ˈbɒdi / (BrE)
the clear, colorless, transparent gel that fills the space between the lens and the retina in the posterior chamber of the eyeball
transparent gel in posterior eye chamber
medical

Examples

The surgeon carefully removed part of the vitreous body to access the retina.
A detached vitreous body can lead to visual disturbances or even retinal tears.
With age, the vitreous body may liquefy, causing floaters in one's vision.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vitreous humor - the clear gel that fills the eye between lens and retina; alternative term

Etymology

From Latin vitreus (of glass), referring to its glass-like appearance.
