effects unit

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈfɛkts ˌjuːnɪt / (AmE), / ɪˈfɛkts ˌjuːnɪt / (BrE)
an electronic device that alters the sound of a musical instrument or other audio source, often used to produce various audio effects such as reverb, delay, or distortion.
device that modifies audio signals

Examples

Many guitarists use an effects unit to shape their unique sound during performances.
The studio purchased a new effects unit to enhance their audio mixing capabilities.
His pedalboard includes several effects units for different tonal options.

Synset Examples

Guitarists derive their signature sound or ‘tone’ from their choice of instrument, pickups, effects units, and guitar amp.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: effects pedal - a foot-operated type of effects unit used by musicians
  • Degree Lower: amplifier - an electronic device that increases the power of audio signals
