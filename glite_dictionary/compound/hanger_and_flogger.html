hanger and flogger

1. NOUN

/ ˈhæŋɚ ənd ˈflɑɡɚ / (AmE), / ˈhæŋə ənd ˈflɒɡə / (BrE)
a derogatory term for a person who criticizes or demands improvements excessively, often harming morale
person who excessively criticizes or demands
offensive

Examples

The team dreaded meetings because their new supervisor was a notorious hanger and flogger.
Calling him a hanger and flogger hurt his reputation in the company.
You don't have to be a hanger and flogger to get results from your team.

Synset Examples

He complained that the new manager was a real hanger and flogger.
The boss is a bit of a hanger and flogger, always pushing for more.
Being a hanger and flogger can hurt team morale.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: martinet - a person who is very strict and demands obedience
