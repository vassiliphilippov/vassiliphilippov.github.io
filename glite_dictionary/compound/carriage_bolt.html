carriage bolt

1. NOUN

/ ˈkærɪdʒ boʊlt / (AmE), / ˈkærɪdʒ bəʊlt / (BrE)
A bolt with a round head and a partially threaded shank, used in timber construction and inserted into pre-drilled holes.
roundheaded bolt used in timber construction

Examples

Tighten the carriage bolt securely to ensure the wooden beams are held together.
The table was assembled using several carriage bolts and nuts for stability.
A carriage bolt is ideal when you need a smooth, rounded surface on one side of the wood.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: hex bolt - a bolt with a hexagonal head
  • Degree Higher: lag bolt - a large wood screw with hexagonal head

Etymology

From the word 'carriage' (referring to carts or vehicles), as these bolts were originally used in wagon construction.
