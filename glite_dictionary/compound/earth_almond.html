earth almond

1. NOUN

/ ɜrθ ˈɑlmənd / (AmE), / ɜːθ ˈɑːlmənd / (BrE)
a European sedge (Cyperus esculentus) with small, edible, nutlike tubers, also known as tigernut
edible tuber of a sedge; tigernut

Examples

The creamy dessert was made with earth almonds instead of regular nuts.
Farmers in Spain cultivate earth almond tubers to produce horchata de chufa.
She found earth almonds at the market and used them in her granola recipe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tigernut - another common name for the same plant (Cyperus esculentus)
