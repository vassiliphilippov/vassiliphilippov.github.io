intensive care

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtɛnsɪv kɛr / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɛnsɪv keə / (BrE)
specialized medical treatment and close monitoring given to patients with severe or life-threatening conditions, typically in a dedicated hospital unit
specialized hospital care for critical patients
medical

Examples

After her accident, she was admitted to intensive care for close observation.
The hospital's intensive care unit is equipped with advanced life-support machines.
Doctors worked around the clock in intensive care to stabilize the patient's condition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: critical care - specialized medical care for patients with life-threatening conditions
