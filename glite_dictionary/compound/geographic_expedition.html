geographic expedition

1. NOUN

/ dʒiˈɑɡrəfɪk ˌɛkspəˈdɪʃən / (AmE), / dʒiˈɒɡrəfɪk ˌɛkspəˈdɪʃən / (BrE)
an organized journey undertaken to explore or discover unknown or little-known regions, especially for geographical research or mapping
organized journey for exploration or discovery

Examples

The team set out on a geographic expedition to chart the uninhabited islands of the Pacific.
Her research was greatly enriched by joining a geographic expedition through the Amazon rainforest.
Many important natural landmarks were discovered during the nineteenth-century geographic expeditions across Africa.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expedition - an organized journey with a particular purpose
  • Synonym: exploration - the action of traveling through an unfamiliar area to learn about it

Etymology

From 'geographic' (relating to geography) and 'expedition' (from Latin expeditionem, meaning 'a journey').
