cottage cheese

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːt̬ɪdʒ tʃiːz / (AmE), / ˈkɒtɪdʒ tʃiːz / (BrE)
A soft, mild, white cheese made from the curds of soured skim milk, often with a slightly lumpy texture.
soft, mild cheese made from curds

Examples

She spread cottage cheese on her toast for a healthy breakfast.
A bowl of fresh fruit topped with cottage cheese makes a light lunch.
He added cottage cheese to the salad for extra protein.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ricotta - Italian soft white cheese similar to cottage cheese

Etymology

Named for its simple, homemade production, often associated with rural cottages.
