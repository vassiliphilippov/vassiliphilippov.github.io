yellow colicroot

1. NOUN

/ ˈjɛloʊ ˈkɑlɪkˌruːt / (AmE), / ˈjɛləʊ ˈkɒlɪkˌruːt / (BrE)
A plant of the southeastern United States with yellow-bracted racemose flowers, smaller than Aletris farinosa.
plant with yellow-bracted flowers, southeastern US

Examples

The yellow colicroot thrives in the pine forests of the southeastern United States.
Botanists noted that yellow colicroot has smaller flowers compared to Aletris farinosa.
During our hike, we found several clusters of yellow colicroot along the sandy trail.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Aletris farinosa - A related colicroot plant, also known as white colicroot
