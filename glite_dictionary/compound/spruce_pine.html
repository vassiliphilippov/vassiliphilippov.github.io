spruce pine

1. NOUN

/ sprus paɪn / (AmE), / spruːs paɪn / (BrE)
A large pine tree native to the southeastern United States, having two needles per cluster and light, soft wood.
pine tree of southeastern US

Examples

The forest was dominated by tall spruce pines reaching toward the sky.
The light wood of spruce pine is often used in construction.
We identified several spruce pine specimens during our hike in Georgia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pine - any coniferous tree of the genus Pinus

/ sprus paɪn / (AmE), / spruːs paɪn / (BrE)

Examples

3. NOUN

/ ˈsprus ˌpaɪn / (AmE), / ˈspruːs ˌpaɪn / (BrE)
A shrubby pine tree native to the coastal northwestern United States, with two needles per bundle and red to yellow-brown bark fissured into small squares.
a pine tree species of the northwest U.S.

Examples

We spotted a tall spruce pine on the rocky shoreline during our hike.
The bark of the spruce pine peels away in small, square plates revealing its characteristic pattern.
Unlike most pines, the spruce pine has a shrubby form and is adapted to coastal conditions.

4. NOUN

/ sprus paɪn / (AmE), / spruːs paɪn / (BrE)
A small evergreen tree found in boggy areas of northeastern North America, with spreading branches and dense foliage; its wood is considered of lower quality.
small North American spruce tree; inferior wood

Examples

The dense foliage of the spruce pine provides excellent shelter for small animals.
Lumber from the spruce pine is often used for pulp rather than high-quality furniture.
We spotted a cluster of spruce pines growing along the edge of the wetland.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: black spruce - a North American spruce with similar habitat and characteristics
