pilot boat

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪlət ˌboʊt / (AmE), / ˈpaɪlət ˌbəʊt / (BrE)
a boat used to transport maritime pilots to and from large ships, typically to guide them in and out of port.
boat for carrying maritime pilots

Examples

The pilot boat approached the large tanker to bring the harbour pilot onboard.
In rough seas, the pilot boat maneuvered skillfully alongside the cargo ship.
Every morning, the pilot boat sets out early from the harbor to meet incoming vessels.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pilot - a person who guides ships through dangerous or congested waters
