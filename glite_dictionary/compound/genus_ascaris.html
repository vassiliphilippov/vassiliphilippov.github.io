genus Ascaris

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs æˈskɛr.ɪs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs æsˈkɑː.rɪs / (BrE)
the type genus of the family Ascaridae, consisting of roundworms with a three-lipped mouth
type genus of roundworms (Ascaridae)
medical

Examples

Many parasitic infections in humans are caused by worms of the genus Ascaris.
The genus Ascaris is characterized by having a three-lipped mouth structure.
Research into the genus Ascaris has contributed to our understanding of roundworm biology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ascaris lumbricoides - the large roundworm, a species in the genus Ascaris

Etymology

From Latin 'Ascaris', from Greek 'askaris' (intestinal worm).
