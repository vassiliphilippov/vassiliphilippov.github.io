individual retirement account

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪndɪˈvɪdʒuəl rɪˈtaɪərmənt əˈkaʊnt / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˈvɪdʒuəl rɪˈtaɪəmənt əˈkaʊnt / (BrE)
a U.S. retirement savings account that allows individuals to make annual contributions with tax-deferred growth on earnings until withdrawal
U.S. tax-advantaged retirement savings account
american

Examples

She opened an individual retirement account to start saving for her future.
Contributions to an individual retirement account are subject to annual limits set by the government.
An individual retirement account offers tax advantages for long-term retirement savings.
