mercuric chloride

1. NOUN

/ mɚˈkjʊrɪk ˈklɔːrˌaɪd / (AmE), / məˈkjʊərɪk ˈklɔːraɪd / (BrE)
a white, poisonous, soluble crystalline compound of mercury used as a pesticide, antiseptic, or wood preservative
poisonous mercury compound; used as pesticide/antiseptic
medical

Examples

The laboratory stored mercuric chloride in a locked cabinet because of its high toxicity.
Historically, mercuric chloride was often used as an antiseptic, despite its dangerous side effects.
Treating the wood with mercuric chloride prevented fungal growth but posed environmental risks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mercury(II) chloride - another name for mercuric chloride
  • Degree Lower: mercurous chloride - mercury(I) chloride; less toxic than mercuric chloride

Etymology

From 'mercuric' (containing mercury in the +2 oxidation state) and 'chloride' (compound of chlorine).
