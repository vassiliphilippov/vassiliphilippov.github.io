life cycle

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪf ˌsaɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈlaɪf ˌsaɪkl / (BrE)
the series of changes in the life of an organism, including reproduction and development from birth to maturity
the stages of growth and reproduction in an organism

Examples

A butterfly undergoes a fascinating life cycle from egg to adult.
Understanding the life cycle of a virus can help scientists develop effective treatments.
The company's report discussed the life cycle of its products, from design to end-of-life recycling.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: life span - the length of time an organism is expected to live

2. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪf ˌsaɪ.kəl / (AmE), / ˈlaɪf ˌsaɪ.kl̩ / (BrE)
the series of stages an organism goes through from the beginning of its life until it returns to the starting stage, often leading to new generations
series of stages in an organism's life

Examples

The butterfly's life cycle includes the stages of egg, larva, pupa, and adult.
Many insects have a short life cycle that allows them to reproduce quickly.
Understanding the life cycle of a frog helps students learn about metamorphosis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: life span - the length of time an organism is expected to live
