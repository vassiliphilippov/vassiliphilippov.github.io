narrow margin

1. NOUN

/ ˈnæroʊ ˈmɑrdʒɪn / (AmE), / ˈnærəʊ ˈmɑːdʒɪn / (BrE)
a very small difference between two totals, amounts, or scores, especially in a competition or election
small difference between totals or scores

Examples

The candidate won the election by a narrow margin of just a few hundred votes.
Their team lost the championship by a narrow margin, missing the final goal in the last minute.
Profits increased this quarter, but only by a narrow margin compared to last year.

Synset Examples

the president was not humbled by his narrow margin of victory
the landslide he had in the electoral college obscured the narrowness of a victory based on just 43% of the popular vote

Related Terms

  • Synonym: close call - a situation where the outcome is almost equal
  • Antonym: landslide - a victory or result with a very large margin

2. NOUN

/ ˈnæroʊ ˈmɑrdʒɪn / (AmE), / ˈnærəʊ ˈmɑːdʒɪn / (BrE)
a very small difference in the amount or number, especially between two competing amounts, votes, or results
a small difference in outcome or votes

Examples

She passed the final exam with a narrow margin, just one point above the cutoff.
The team managed to secure victory by a narrow margin after a tense match.
Profits increased this quarter, but only by a narrow margin.

Synset Examples

He won the race by a narrow margin.
The Democrat candidate won the election by a narrow margin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slim margin - a very small difference in result or votes
  • Antonym: wide margin - a large difference in result or votes
