Prix Goncourt

1. PROPN

/ pri ɡɒ̃ˈkuʁ / (AmE), / pri ˈɡɒ̃kʊʁ / (BrE)
An annual award given for the best and most imaginative prose work of the year in French literature.
French literary award

Examples

Winning the Prix Goncourt is considered a major achievement in the world of French literature.
The novel was awarded the Prix Goncourt in 2010, bringing its author international acclaim.
Several authors who have received the Prix Goncourt have later been translated into English.

Etymology

French, from 'prix' (prize) and 'Goncourt,' after the French literary brothers Edmond and Jules de Goncourt.
