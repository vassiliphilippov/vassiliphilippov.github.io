pine tar

1. NOUN

/ paɪn tɑr / (AmE), / paɪn tɑː / (BrE)
a dark, sticky substance produced by heating pine wood, used especially to waterproof wood and ropes or in medicinal ointments
dark resinous substance from heated pine wood

Examples

The baseball player rubbed pine tar onto his bat for a better grip.
Traditional wooden ships were coated with pine tar to prevent water damage.
He applied pine tar ointment to soothe his skin irritation.
