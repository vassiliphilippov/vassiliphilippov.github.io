right stage

1. NOUN

/ raɪt steɪdʒ / (AmE), / raɪt steɪdʒ / (BrE)
The part of a stage that is to the actor's right as they face the audience.
part of a stage (actor's right)

Examples

The main character entered from the right stage, drawing the audience's attention.
During the performance, all the props were hidden just off right stage.
The director asked the actor to move toward right stage for the next scene.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stage right - the right side of the stage from the perspective of an actor facing the audience
  • Antonym: left stage - the part of the stage on the actor's left as the actor faces the audience
