ballroom dance

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɔlˌrum dæns / (AmE), / ˈbɔːlruːm dans / (BrE)
a formal social dance performed by couples, such as the waltz, tango, or foxtrot, traditionally in a large hall
formal social dance for couples

Examples

She wore a sparkling gown to the ballroom dance on Saturday night.
Many teenagers take lessons to learn their favorite ballroom dance styles before prom.
The competition featured several couples performing an elegant ballroom dance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: waltz - a type of ballroom dance with a triple time signature
  • Synonym: tango - a ballroom dance of South American origin
