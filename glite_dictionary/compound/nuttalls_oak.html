Nuttall's oak

1. NOUN

/ ˈnʌtəlz oʊk / (AmE), / ˈnʌtəlz əʊk / (BrE)
a species of oak tree (Quercus texana), similar to the pin oak, that grows in damp areas of the Mississippi River basin
a species of oak tree (Quercus texana)

Examples

Many wildlife species rely on Nuttall's oak for both shelter and food in the floodplain forests.
The leaves of Nuttall's oak are sharply lobed, resembling those of the pin oak.
We planted several Nuttall's oak saplings along the riverbank to restore native habitat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pin oak - a North American oak tree with deeply lobed leaves, Quercus palustris

Etymology

Named after Thomas Nuttall (1786–1859), English botanist and naturalist.
