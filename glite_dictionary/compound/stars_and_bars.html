Stars and Bars

1. NOUN

/ stɑɹz ænd bɑɹz / (AmE), / stɑːz ənd bɑːz / (BrE)
the first official flag of the Confederate States of America, used from 1861 to 1863.
the first Confederate flag (1861–1863)

Examples

The Stars and Bars was replaced in 1863 due to confusion with the Union flag.
Historians often study the symbolism of the Stars and Bars in Civil War exhibitions.
An original Stars and Bars flag is preserved at the state museum.
