hot jazz

1. NOUN

/ hɑt dʒæz / (AmE), / hɒt dʒæz / (BrE)
a style of jazz music characterized by emotional intensity, strong rhythms, and improvisational playing, especially associated with early jazz
jazz style with strong rhythms and improvisation

Examples

Many musicians of the 1920s were pioneers of hot jazz in New Orleans.
The energetic rhythms of hot jazz got the entire audience up and dancing.
She enjoys listening to recordings of classic hot jazz bands from the early twentieth century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jazz - a style of music characterized by improvisation and strong rhythms
