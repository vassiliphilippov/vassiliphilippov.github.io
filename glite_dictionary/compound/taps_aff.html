taps aff

1. NOUN

/ tæps æf / (AmE), / taps af / (BrE)
(Scottish, informal) The act of taking off one's shirt, usually in warm weather or as a sign of celebration; also, a notice indicating water has been shut off.
removing one's shirt (Scottish), or water shut-off sign
informal british

Examples

When the sun comes out in Glasgow, it's always taps aff weather.
Locals joked that it was officially taps aff as soon as the temperature reached 15°C.
The festival was postponed when the taps aff warning was posted due to maintenance.

Synset Examples

He was upset when the taps aff sign was posted.
We left early because it was taps aff for the night.

Etymology

Scots dialect: 'tap' for top (shirt) and 'aff' for off.
