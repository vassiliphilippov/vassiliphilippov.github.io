Bradley method

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrædli ˌmɛθəd / (AmE), / ˈbrædli ˌmɛθəd / (BrE)
a method of preparing for natural childbirth through education, exercises, nutrition, and techniques of breathing and relaxation, usually with the father's assistance
natural childbirth preparation technique

Examples

They chose the Bradley method to help them prepare for a natural childbirth experience.
The Bradley method encourages partners to work together during labor for support and comfort.
Many parents attend classes in the Bradley method to learn about breathing and relaxation techniques.

Etymology

Named after Dr. Robert A. Bradley, who developed the method in the 1940s.
