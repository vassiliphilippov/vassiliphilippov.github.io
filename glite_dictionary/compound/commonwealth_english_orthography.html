Commonwealth English orthography

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑmənˌwɛlθ ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ ɔrˈθɑɡrəfi / (AmE), / ˌkɒmənˌwɛlθ ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ ɔːˈθɒɡrəfi / (BrE)
The system of English spelling as used in the United Kingdom, Ireland, and most Commonwealth countries, distinguished by variants such as 'analyse', 'centre', and 'useable'.
spelling system in UK and Commonwealth English

Examples

Some writers prefer to use Commonwealth English orthography when publishing international editions of their books.
The word 'colour' is spelled with a 'u' in Commonwealth English orthography but not in American spelling.
Teachers in Australia generally teach students to follow Commonwealth English orthography in their writing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: British English - the English language as used in Britain, including spelling, grammar, and vocabulary
  • Antonym: American English orthography - the system of English spelling used in the United States
