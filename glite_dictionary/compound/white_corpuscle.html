white corpuscle

1. NOUN

/ waɪt ˈkɔrpəsəl / (AmE), / waɪt ˈkɔːpʊsl̩ / (BrE)
a type of blood cell that helps defend the body by engulfing and digesting bacteria and fungi
a blood cell involved in immune defense
medical

Examples

During the infection, his white corpuscle count increased dramatically.
A white corpuscle helps protect the body by destroying harmful invaders.
Doctors measured her white corpuscle levels to assess immune function.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: white blood cell - a blood cell involved in the body's immune response
