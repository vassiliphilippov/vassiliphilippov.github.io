chrome alum

1. NOUN

/ kroʊm ˈæləm / (AmE), / krəʊm ˈaləm / (BrE)
A violet-colored chemical salt, usually potassium chromium sulfate, used in hide tanning and as a mordant in dyeing.
violet salt used in tanning and dyeing

Examples

The leather industry often uses chrome alum for tanning hides.
Dyers add chrome alum as a mordant to help colors set in fabrics.
The distinctive violet hue of chrome alum makes it easily recognizable in a laboratory.

Etymology

From 'chrome', relating to chromium, and 'alum', from Latin 'alumen' (alum).
