variola virus

1. NOUN

/ vəˈraɪə.lə ˈvaɪ.rəs / (AmE), / vəˈraɪ.ə.lə ˈvaɪ.rəs / (BrE)
the virus responsible for causing smallpox in humans; a member of the Orthopoxvirus genus
virus causing smallpox in humans
medical

Examples

The variola virus was declared eradicated by the World Health Organization in 1980.
Scientists study the variola virus to better understand historical pandemics.
There are concerns that the variola virus could be used as a bioweapon if samples are released.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smallpox - an infectious disease caused by the variola virus

Etymology

From Latin 'variola' (smallpox) and 'virus' (poison, slime).
