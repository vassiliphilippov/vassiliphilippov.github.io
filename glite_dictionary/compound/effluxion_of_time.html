effluxion of time

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈflʌkʃən əv taɪm / (AmE), / ɪˈflʌkʃən əv taɪm / (BrE)
The passage or gradual flow of time, especially as it leads to the ending of an agreement or a change in circumstances.
the gradual passage of time

Examples

With the effluxion of time, the old law became obsolete.
Many leases terminate by the effluxion of time rather than by notice.
He recognized the effluxion of time had brought many changes to his hometown.

Synset Examples

With the effluxion of time, memories fade away.
The effluxion of time has changed our perspectives.
He reflected on the effluxion of time during his retirement.

Etymology

From Latin 'effluxio' (a flowing out) + 'of time'.
