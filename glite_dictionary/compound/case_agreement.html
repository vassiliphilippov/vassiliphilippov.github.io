case agreement

1. NOUN

/ keɪs əˈɡriːmənt / (AmE), / keɪs əˈɡriːmənt / (BrE)
the correspondence in grammatical case between words, such as a noun and its modifier, within the same sentence structure
correspondence in grammatical case between words

Examples

In Latin, case agreement requires adjectives to match the case of the nouns they modify.
Teachers explained case agreement by showing how determiners change in different sentence roles.
The lack of case agreement in the translation led to grammatical errors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agreement - harmony or correspondence in grammatical features between words
