stemless carline thistle

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɛmlɪs ˈkɑrˌlaɪn ˈθɪsəl / (AmE), / ˈstɛmlɪs ˈkɑːˌlaɪn ˈθɪsəl / (BrE)
A perennial alpine plant (Carlina acaulis) of southern and eastern Europe, characterized by its large, stemless flowers with white or purple-brown florets, and a rosette of long, spiny leaves that are hairy beneath.
Alpine plant with stemless flowers, Europe

Examples

The fields in the Alps are dotted with the distinctive stemless carline thistle, lying close to the ground.
Hikers admire the beautiful stemless carline thistle for its striking, spiny leaves and large, silver flower heads.
Botanists often study the stemless carline thistle as an adaptation to harsh mountain climates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carline thistle - a group of thistle plants in the Carlina genus
  • Degree Higher: thistle - a prickly-leaved plant of the daisy family

Etymology

From 'carline thistle', ultimately from Latin 'Carlina', a genus name, and the descriptive 'stemless'.
