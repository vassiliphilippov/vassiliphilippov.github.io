barrel organ

1. NOUN

/ ˈbærəl ˌɔrɡən / (AmE), / ˈbærəl ˌɔːɡən / (BrE)
a mechanical musical instrument that produces music by turning a cylinder fitted with pegs or pins, which trigger notes as it rotates
mechanical musical instrument with a rotating cylinder

Examples

The street musician played a barrel organ while children danced around him.
In the museum, you can see a restored Victorian barrel organ on display.
He turned the handle of the barrel organ, filling the square with lively tunes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hurdy-gurdy - a mechanical stringed musical instrument played by turning a crank
