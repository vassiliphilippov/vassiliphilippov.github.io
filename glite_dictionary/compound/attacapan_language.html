Attacapan language

1. NOUN

/ ˌætəˈkɑːpən ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌætəˈkɑːpən ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
an extinct indigenous language formerly spoken by the Atakapa people of the Gulf Coast of Louisiana and Texas
an extinct Native American language of Louisiana and Texas

Examples

The Attacapan language became extinct in the early 20th century as fewer Atakapa people spoke it.
Linguists have studied remnants of the Attacapan language to understand its relationship with other Gulf Coast languages.
Much of what we know about the Attacapan language comes from the writings of early European explorers.

Etymology

From 'Atakapa', the name of the indigenous people who spoke the language.
