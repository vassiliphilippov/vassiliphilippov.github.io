genus Callicebus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒinəs ˌkælɪˈsiːbəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˌkælɪˈsiːbəs / (BrE)
A genus of New World monkeys known as titis, found in South America, characterized by their long, soft fur and bushy tails.
a genus of titi monkeys

Examples

The genus Callicebus includes several species of titi monkeys native to South America.
Recent studies focus on the social structure of the genus Callicebus in Amazonian forests.
Conservation efforts are crucial for some members of the genus Callicebus, which face habitat loss.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: titi monkey - common name for monkeys of the genus Callicebus

Etymology

From Greek 'kallos' (beauty) and 'kebos' (long-tailed monkey); used in scientific Latin nomenclature.
