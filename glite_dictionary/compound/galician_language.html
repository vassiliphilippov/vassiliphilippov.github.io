Galician language

1. NOUN

/ ɡəˈlɪʃən ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ɡəˈlɪʃən ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
A Romance language spoken in Galicia, an autonomous region of northwestern Spain.
language of Galicia, Spain

Examples

The Galician language has official status in the region alongside Spanish.
She decided to learn the Galician language before traveling to Santiago de Compostela.
Many traditional songs are performed in the Galician language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Portuguese language - A closely related Romance language spoken in Portugal and Brazil

Etymology

From Spanish 'gallego', from Latin 'Gallaecia', the Roman name for the region.
