financial ratio

1. NOUN

/ fəˈnænʃəl ˈreɪʃioʊ / (AmE), / faɪˈnænʃl ˈreɪʃɪəʊ / (BrE)
a numerical comparison between two financial values from an enterprise's financial statements, used to evaluate its financial performance or condition
comparison of financial statement figures

Examples

The company's debt-to-equity financial ratio improved significantly over the last year.
Investors often use a financial ratio to assess a firm's profitability and efficiency.
Before granting loans, banks analyze several financial ratios to determine creditworthiness.

Synset Examples

Financial ratios can be expressed as a decimal value, such as 0.10, or given as an equivalent percent value, such as 10%.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ratio - the quantitative relation between two amounts, showing the number of times one value contains or is contained within the other
