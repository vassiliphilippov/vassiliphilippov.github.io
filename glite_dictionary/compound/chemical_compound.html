chemical compound

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɛmɪkəl ˈkɑmpaʊnd / (AmE), / ˈkɛmɪkəl ˈkɒmpaʊnd / (BrE)
A substance formed from two or more elements or ingredients that are chemically united in fixed proportions.
substance made of chemically combined elements

Examples

Water is a chemical compound consisting of hydrogen and oxygen in a 2:1 ratio.
Carbon dioxide is another common chemical compound found in Earth's atmosphere.
Salt, or sodium chloride, is an essential chemical compound used in cooking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compound - A substance that is made from two or more elements joined chemically
