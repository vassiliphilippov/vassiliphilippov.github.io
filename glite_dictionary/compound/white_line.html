white line

1. NOUN

/ waɪt laɪn / (AmE), / waɪt laɪn / (BrE)
a white stripe painted on a road surface to mark traffic lanes or regulate vehicle movement
white road marking for lanes/traffic

Examples

You must not cross the white line in the center of the road when overtaking.
The white line on the edge of the highway helps drivers stay in their lanes at night.
Freshly painted white lines guided traffic through the newly constructed intersection.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: yellow line - a yellow stripe used for road markings
