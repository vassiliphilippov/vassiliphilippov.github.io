Christian Science

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɪstʃən ˈsaɪəns / (AmE), / ˈkrɪstʃən ˈsaɪəns / (BrE)
A Protestant religious denomination founded by Mary Baker Eddy in 1866, emphasizing spiritual healing and the teachings of Jesus Christ.
Protestant denomination founded by Mary Baker Eddy

Examples

She was raised in the Christian Science faith and attended services regularly.
The teachings of Christian Science place a strong emphasis on spiritual healing.
He decided to study Christian Science after reading works by Mary Baker Eddy.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkrɪs.tʃən ˈsaɪ.əns / (AmE), / ˈkrɪs.tʃən ˈsaɪ.əns / (BrE)
A religious system founded by Mary Baker Eddy, emphasizing spiritual healing and the power of prayer based on the teachings of the Bible.
religious system emphasizing spiritual healing

Examples

After her illness, she turned to Christian Science for guidance and spiritual healing.
The principles of Christian Science focus on the relationship between faith and health.
A number of churches practice Christian Science in cities around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Christian Scientist - a follower of Christian Science

Etymology

Named after the religious movement founded by Mary Baker Eddy in the United States in the 19th century.
