swamp hickory

1. NOUN

/ swɑmp ˈhɪkəri / (AmE), / swɒmp ˈhɪkəri / (BrE)
a hickory tree native to the eastern United States, distinguished by leaves with 7 or 9 leaflets and thin-shelled, very bitter nuts
a type of hickory tree with bitter nuts

Examples

The swamp hickory thrives in wet bottomland forests across the southeastern United States.
Squirrels tend to avoid the swamp hickory because its nuts are extremely bitter.
We identified the tree as a swamp hickory by counting the leaflets and examining the thin shell of its nuts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hickory - a type of tree in the genus Carya, known for hard wood and edible nuts
