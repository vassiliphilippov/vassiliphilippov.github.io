to double dog dare

1. VERB

/ ˈdʌbəl dɔg dɛr / (AmE), / ˈdʌbəl dɒg deə / (BrE)
to challenge someone in a forceful, playful way to do something, often by suggesting they cannot refuse
strong playful challenge
informal

Examples

I double dog dare you to eat that super spicy pepper.
He didn't want to go, but after a double dog dare, he felt he had to try.
She couldn't resist when her friend double dog dared her to sing in front of everyone.

Synset Examples

He accepted the double dog dare and ran a lap.
You can't back down now; I double dog dare you!
I double dog dare you to jump in the lake!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dare - to challenge someone to do something difficult, dangerous, or unusual
