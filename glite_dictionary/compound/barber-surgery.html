barber-surgery

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑrbər ˌsɜrdʒəri / (AmE), / ˈbɑːbə ˌsɜːdʒəri / (BrE)
the former practice or occupation of barbers performing surgical and dental procedures in addition to cutting hair
historical practice of barbers performing surgery
archaic

Examples

In medieval Europe, barber-surgery included bloodletting and tooth extractions.
The decline of barber-surgery coincided with the professionalization of medicine in the 18th century.
Historical texts often reference barber-surgery when discussing early medical practices.

Synset Examples

Paradoxically, formal recognition of master surgeons led to expansion of French barber-surgery because physicians supported the barbers in an attempt to mitigate the increasing influence of the surgeons of the long robe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barber - a person whose job is cutting hair
  • Synonym: surgeon - a medical practitioner qualified to practice surgery

Etymology

From ‘barber’ (a person who cuts hair) and ‘surgery’; reflects medieval European practice when barbers performed surgical tasks.
