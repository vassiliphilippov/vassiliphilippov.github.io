methacrylic acid

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛθ.əˈkrɪl.ɪk ˈæs.ɪd / (AmE), / ˌmɛθ.əˈkrɪl.ɪk ˈæs.ɪd / (BrE)
an unsaturated organic acid (C4H6O2), used in the manufacture of resins and plastics
unsaturated acid used to make plastics

Examples

Methacrylic acid is a key ingredient in the production of certain acrylic resins.
Many dental materials contain methacrylic acid derivatives for added durability.
Industrial chemists often work with methacrylic acid to create high-quality plastics.

Etymology

From 'meth-' (methyl) + 'acrylic acid', reflecting its chemical structure.
