Parry's pinyon

1. NOUN

/ ˈpæriːz ˈpɪnjən / (AmE), / ˈpæriːz ˈpɪnjɒn / (BrE)
A species of pinyon pine with mostly five needles per bundle, native to southern California and northern Baja California.
species of pinyon pine tree

Examples

The forest was dominated by Parry's pinyon and other native conifers.
Botanists studied the distribution of Parry's pinyon across southern California.
Birds depend on the seeds of Parry's pinyon as a vital food source.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pinyon pine - a group of North American pine trees with edible seeds

Etymology

Named after American botanist Charles C. Parry.
