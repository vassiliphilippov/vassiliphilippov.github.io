rose family

1. NOUN

/ roʊz ˈfæməli / (AmE), / rəʊz ˈfæməli / (BrE)
a large family of flowering plants (Rosaceae), including roses, apples, cherries, and many others, typically with alternate leaves and five-petaled flowers with numerous stamens
family of plants including roses and fruits

Examples

The rose family includes many well-known garden plants and fruit trees.
Apples, cherries, and strawberries all belong to the rose family.
The rose family is known for its five-petaled flowers and fragrant blooms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Rosaceae - the botanical name for the rose family
