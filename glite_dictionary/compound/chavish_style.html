chavish style

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃævɪʃ staɪl / (AmE), / ˈtʃævɪʃ staɪl / (BrE)
A style of dress and appearance associated with working-class British youth, typically featuring flashy or ostentatious clothing and accessories.
flashy British youth fashion
british slang

Examples

Her chavish style made her stand out at the formal party.
Some people judge others unfairly based on their chavish style of dress.
The magazine featured an article about the resurgence of chavish style in youth culture.

Synset Examples

The chavish style was all the rage among teenagers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chav - a young person in Britain perceived as behaving in a brash or loutish way

Etymology

From 'chav', British slang for a young person of a type characterized by brash and loutish behavior, plus English '-ish' and 'style'.
