Father of the Church

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɑðər əv ðə tʃɝːtʃ / (AmE), / ˈfɑːðə əv ðə tʃɜːtʃ / (BrE)
A prominent theologian and writer from the early Christian period (2nd to 7th century) whose teachings helped to define and establish official church doctrine.
early influential Christian theologian

Examples

Saint Augustine is widely regarded as a Father of the Church for his influential writings and theology.
Many Fathers of the Church wrote in either Latin or Greek, shaping Christian doctrine for centuries.
Gregory the Great, a renowned Father of the Church, contributed significantly to Western Christianity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Church Father - A synonym for Father of the Church, referring to early influential Christian theologians
