angle iron

1. NOUN

/ ˈæŋɡəl ˌaɪərn / (AmE), / ˈæŋɡl ˌaɪən / (BrE)
A length of iron or steel with an L-shaped cross-section, typically used in construction for support or reinforcement.
L-shaped metal bar

Examples

The shelves were supported by sturdy angle irons mounted on the wall.
To reinforce the frame, the builder welded pieces of angle iron at each corner.
We used a length of angle iron to repair the broken fence gate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bracket - a support projecting from a wall to hold a shelf or reinforce an angle
