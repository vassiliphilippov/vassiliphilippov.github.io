out of sight

1. ADV

/ aʊt əv saɪt / (AmE), / aʊt əv saɪt / (BrE)
no longer visible; unable to be seen any more
no longer visible

Examples

The children ran around the corner and were soon out of sight.
As the train pulled away from the station, it quickly moved out of sight.
She kept waving until the plane was completely out of sight.

Synset Examples

the ship disappeared behind the horizon and passed out of sight

Related Terms

  • Antonym: in sight - visible; able to be seen
Source: WordNet

2. ADJ

/ aʊt əv saɪt / (AmE), / aʊt ɒv saɪt / (BrE)
not visible; not within the range of vision.
not visible; hidden from view

Examples

The children quickly ran out of sight as soon as the game started.
She kept the birthday present out of sight until the party began.
The mountains were shrouded in fog and soon disappeared out of sight.

Synset Examples

concealed (or hidden) damage
in stormy weather the stars are out of sight

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hidden - kept out of sight; concealed
  • Antonym: visible - able to be seen
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ aʊt əv saɪt / (AmE), / aʊt əv saɪt / (BrE)
not visible; hidden from view or awareness
not visible; hidden

Examples

We kept the birthday presents out of sight until the party started.
Her fear of public speaking stays out of sight during most conversations.
Please place any confidential documents out of sight when visitors arrive.

Synset Examples

Keep the valuables out of sight when you’re traveling.
She prefers to keep her problems out of sight.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: in sight - visible; able to be seen
