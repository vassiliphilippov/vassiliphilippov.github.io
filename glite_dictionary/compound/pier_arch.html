pier arch

1. NOUN

/ pɪr ɑrtʃ / (AmE), / pɪə ɑːtʃ / (BrE)
An arch that is supported on piers rather than columns or walls.
arch supported on piers

Examples

The cathedral's nave is lined with a series of impressive pier arches.
Engineers inspected the pier arch for structural integrity after the earthquake.
The monument features a large central pier arch flanked by two smaller ones.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arch - A curved symmetrical structure spanning an opening

Etymology

From 'pier' (supporting structure) and 'arch' (curved structure), both from Old French origins.
