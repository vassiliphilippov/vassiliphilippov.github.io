Pali language

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːli ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈpɑːli ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
An ancient Indic language derived from Sanskrit, used as the liturgical and scriptural language of Theravada Buddhism.
language of early Buddhist scriptures

Examples

The sacred texts of Theravada Buddhism are written in Pali language.
She studied the Pali language in order to read ancient Buddhist scriptures in their original form.
Several universities offer courses in Pali language for students of religious studies.

Etymology

From Pali pāḷi (row, line, canonical text), adopted in English in the 19th century.
