original sin

1. NOUN

/ əˈrɪdʒənl sɪn / (AmE), / əˈrɪdʒɪnəl sɪn / (BrE)
(in Christian theology) the state of sin inherited by all human beings as a result of Adam's disobedience in the Garden of Eden.
inherited sin in Christian theology

Examples

The doctrine of original sin is central to many branches of Christianity.
Many theologians have debated the implications of original sin for human nature and salvation.
According to tradition, baptism washes away original sin in Christian believers.

Synset Examples

Adam and Eve committed the original sin when they ate the forbidden fruit in the Garden of Eden

Etymology

From Latin peccatum originale, with 'original' meaning 'originating' and 'sin' from Latin 'peccatum'.
