Dawson River salmon

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɔːsən ˈrɪvər ˈsæmən / (AmE), / ˈdɔːsən ˈrɪvə ˈsæmən / (BrE)
a large freshwater fish native to Australian rivers, especially the Dawson River, distinguished by a red or pink spot on each scale (Scleropages leichardti)
large Australian freshwater fish (Scleropages leichardti)

Examples

The Dawson River salmon is highly prized by anglers for its striking appearance and size.
Conservation efforts have increased the population of the rare Dawson River salmon in Queensland.
Unlike other native fish, the Dawson River salmon has a distinctive pink spot on every scale.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spotted barramundi - another common name for Scleropages leichardti

Etymology

Named after the Dawson River in Queensland, Australia, and 'salmon' for its superficial resemblance to salmon species.
