definite article

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛfɪnət ˈɑːrtɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈdɛfɪnət ˈɑːtɪkl / (BrE)
the word 'the' used before a noun to indicate that the identity of the noun is known to the reader or listener
the word 'the' indicating specificity

Examples

In the sentence 'The dog barked', definite article refers to the word 'the'.
English has only one definite article: 'the'.
She explained when to use a definite article in English grammar.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: indefinite article - the word 'a' or 'an' used to refer to a non-specific noun

Etymology

From Latin 'definitus' (defined) and 'articulus' (article).
