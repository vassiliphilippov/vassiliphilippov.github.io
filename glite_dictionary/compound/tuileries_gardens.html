Tuileries Gardens

1. PROPN

/ twiˈlɛriz ˈɡɑrdənz / (AmE), / twiːˈlɛəri ˈɡɑːdnz / (BrE)
the formal public gardens located next to the Louvre Museum in Paris, known for their historical significance and elegant design
formal gardens near the Louvre in Paris

Examples

Many tourists stroll through the Tuileries Gardens after visiting the Louvre Museum.
The Tuileries Gardens were originally created by Catherine de' Medici in the 16th century.
The sculptures and fountains in the Tuileries Gardens make it a favorite spot for locals and visitors alike.

Etymology

From French 'Jardin des Tuileries', named after the former tile (tuile) factories on the site.
