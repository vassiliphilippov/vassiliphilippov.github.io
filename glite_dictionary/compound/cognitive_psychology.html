cognitive psychology

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑɡnɪtɪv saɪˈkɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ˈkɒɡnɪtɪv saɪˈkɒlədʒi / (BrE)
a branch of psychology that focuses on the study of mental processes such as perception, memory, reasoning, and problem-solving
the study of mental processes in psychology

Examples

Many researchers in cognitive psychology study how people remember information and solve problems.
The field of cognitive psychology has advanced our understanding of language development.
She chose to specialize in cognitive psychology during her graduate studies.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: behaviorism - an approach in psychology emphasizing observable behavior

Etymology

From Latin 'cognitio' (knowledge) and 'psychologia' (study of the mind).
