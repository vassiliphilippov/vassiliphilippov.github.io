in for

1. ADJ

/ ɪn fɔɹ / (AmE), / ɪn fə / (BrE)
certain to be about to experience or receive something, especially something unpleasant or unexpected
about to experience (often unpleasant)

Examples

If you don't study for the test, you're in for a surprise.
The weather forecast says we're in for some heavy rain this weekend.
After missing the deadline, she knew she was in for some criticism from her boss.

Synset Examples

he knew he was in for a licking

Related Terms

  • Synonym: in store for - about to happen or be experienced by someone

2. ADJ

/ ɪn fər / (AmE), / ɪn fə / (BrE)
likely to experience or undergo something, often something unexpected or noteworthy
likely to experience (often unexpected)

Examples

With the storm approaching, we're in for a wild night.
You're in for a surprise if you think the test will be easy.
She knew she was in for trouble when she saw her boss waiting for her.

Synset Examples

I think you’re in for some interesting news.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: up for - willing or prepared to do something
