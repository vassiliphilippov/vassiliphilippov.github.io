elder stateswoman

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛldɚ ˈsteɪtsˌwʊmən / (AmE), / ˈɛldə ˈsteɪtsˌwʊmən / (BrE)
an experienced and respected older woman in politics or public affairs, whose advice is valued by government leaders
respected senior woman in public affairs

Examples

After her retirement, the party often consulted the elder stateswoman for guidance on difficult issues.
Widely admired as an elder stateswoman, she played a key role in advising the current president.
Her reputation as an elder stateswoman grew as younger politicians sought her counsel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elder statesman - an experienced and respected older man in politics whose advice is valued

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛldɚ ˈsteɪtsˌwʊmən / (AmE), / ˈɛldə ˈsteɪtsˌwʊmən / (BrE)
a senior woman, often in politics or public life, whose experience and advice are highly respected
respected senior female leader

Examples

The party turned to their elder stateswoman for advice during the leadership crisis.
As an elder stateswoman of the organization, she commanded respect from all members.
After retiring from active politics, she continued to be seen as an elder stateswoman whose counsel mattered.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elder statesman - a senior man, especially in politics, whose advice is respected

Etymology

Modelled on 'elder statesman'; 'stateswoman' is the feminine counterpart to 'statesman'.
