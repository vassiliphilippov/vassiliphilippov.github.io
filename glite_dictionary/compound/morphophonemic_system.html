morphophonemic system

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɔrfəfəˈniːmɪk ˈsɪstəm / (AmE), / ˌmɔːfəfəˈniːmɪk ˈsɪstəm / (BrE)
the system of rules governing the interaction between morphology and phonology in a particular language, especially how morphemes are pronounced in different environments
linguistic rules about morpheme pronunciation

Examples

The English morphophonemic system handles the plural endings in words like 'cats' and 'dogs' differently.
Researchers analyzed the morphophonemic system of Finnish to understand how suffixes affect pronunciation.
Learning the morphophonemic system can explain why some morphemes sound different in various contexts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: morphophonemics - the study of the interaction between morphological and phonological processes

Etymology

From 'morpho-' (form, shape) and 'phonemic' (relating to phonemes), combining morphology and phonology terminology.
