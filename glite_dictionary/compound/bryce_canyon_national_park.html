Bryce Canyon National Park

1. PROPN

/ braɪs ˈkænjən ˌnæʃənəl ˈpɑrk / (AmE), / braɪs ˈkænjən ˌnæʃənəl ˈpɑːk / (BrE)
a national park in southern Utah, United States, known for its distinctive multicolored rock formations and erosions
national park in Utah, USA, with unique rock formations

Examples

We watched the sunrise from Bryce Canyon National Park, where the rock formations glowed in the early light.
Millions of tourists visit Bryce Canyon National Park every year to hike among its famous hoodoos.
The unique landscape of Bryce Canyon National Park makes it a popular destination for photographers.

Etymology

Named after Ebenezer Bryce, a 19th-century settler in the area.
