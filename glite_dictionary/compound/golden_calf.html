golden calf

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡoʊldən ˈkæf / (AmE), / ˌɡəʊldən ˈkɑːf / (BrE)
(in the Bible) an idol made by Aaron for the Israelites to worship; more generally, anything that is worshipped or valued undeservedly or excessively.
idol or object of undeserved worship

Examples

Some people treat money as a golden calf, pursuing it above all else.
The film critic accused Hollywood of worshipping the box office as its new golden calf.
In his speech, the pastor warned against making technology a modern golden calf.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: idol - an image or representation worshipped as a god
  • Synonym: false god - a god that is not real; something wrongly valued as divine

Etymology

From the biblical account in Exodus, where the Israelites worshipped a golden idol in the form of a calf.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡoʊldən kæf / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊldən kɑːf / (BrE)
Something that is idolized or obsessively pursued but is unworthy or misguided, especially wealth or material gain.
object of misguided worship or devotion

Examples

In some cultures, celebrity has become a golden calf that people strive to emulate.
His pursuit of status turned into a golden calf that drove all his decisions.
The novel critiques society's worship of the golden calf of success.

Synset Examples

The company's obsession with profit became their golden calf.
He was criticized for worshipping the golden calf of greed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: idol - an image or object worshipped as a god
  • Synonym: false god - something worshipped in place of the true deity

Etymology

From the biblical story in Exodus, where the Israelites worship a golden calf as an idol.
