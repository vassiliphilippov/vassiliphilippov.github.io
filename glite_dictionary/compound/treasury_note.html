Treasury note

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrɛʒəri noʊt / (AmE), / ˈtrɛʒəri nəʊt / (BrE)
A government security with a maturity of 1 to 10 years, sold for cash or in exchange for maturing securities, often at auction.
government security with 1–10 year maturity

Examples

The government issued a new series of Treasury notes to help finance the budget deficit.
Many investors prefer Treasury notes because they offer a balance between yield and risk.
She sold her old Treasury note before it matured to reinvest in shorter-term securities.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Treasury bond - a government security with a maturity of more than 10 years
  • Degree Lower: Treasury bill - a government security with a maturity of less than one year
