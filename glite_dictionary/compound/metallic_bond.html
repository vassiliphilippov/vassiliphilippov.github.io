metallic bond

1. NOUN

/ məˈtælɪk bɑnd / (AmE), / mɪˈtælɪk bɒnd / (BrE)
a type of chemical bond in which electrons are shared among a lattice of metal atoms, allowing electronic conduction
type of chemical bond in metals
medical

Examples

Copper wires conduct electricity efficiently because of the presence of a metallic bond.
The metallic bond explains why metals are malleable and ductile.
In chemistry class, we learned that the metallic bond is responsible for metal's shiny appearance.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: covalent bond - a chemical bond formed by sharing electron pairs between atoms
  • Antonym: ionic bond - a chemical bond formed between oppositely charged ions
  • Degree Higher: chemical bond - an attraction between atoms that enables the formation of chemical substances

Etymology

From 'metallic' (relating to metal) and 'bond' (something that binds or holds together)
