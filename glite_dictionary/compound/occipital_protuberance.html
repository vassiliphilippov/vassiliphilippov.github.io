occipital protuberance

1. NOUN

/ əkˈsɪpɪtəl proʊˈtubərəns / (AmE), / ɒkˈsɪpɪtəl prəˈtjuːbərəns / (BrE)
a bony prominence on the outer surface of the occipital bone at the back of the skull
bony bump on the back of the skull
medical

Examples

The doctor located the occipital protuberance when examining the patient's skull.
Some hairstyles can accentuate the occipital protuberance at the back of the head.
A prominent occipital protuberance is sometimes visible beneath short hair.

Etymology

From Latin 'occipitalis' (of the back of the head) and 'protuberantia' (a swelling or bulge).
