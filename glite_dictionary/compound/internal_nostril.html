internal nostril

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtɝːnəl ˈnɑstrəl / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɜːnəl ˈnɒstrɪl / (BrE)
either of the two openings at the back of the nasal cavity that connect to the nasopharynx
posterior opening of the nasal cavity
medical

Examples

During the examination, the doctor used a light to inspect each internal nostril for any obstructions.
The air passes from the nasal cavity through the internal nostrils into the throat.
Certain animals have larger internal nostrils to facilitate more efficient breathing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: choana - one of the two openings at the back of the nasal cavity
