textile screw pine

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛkˌstaɪl skru paɪn / (AmE), / ˈtɛkstaɪl skruː paɪn / (BrE)
a tropical plant (Pandanus tectorius) found in Polynesia and other regions, known for its fibrous leaves used to make mats, hats, and other woven items
tropical plant used for weaving fibers

Examples

The leaves of the textile screw pine are traditionally woven into mats in many island communities.
After harvesting, she dried the textile screw pine leaves for basket weaving.
The Pacific region relies on the textile screw pine for a variety of everyday uses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: screw pine - a tropical plant of the genus Pandanus with long, narrow leaves
