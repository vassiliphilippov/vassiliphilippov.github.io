natural rubber

1. NOUN

/ ˈnætʃrəl ˈrʌbər / (AmE), / ˈnatʃ(ə)rəl ˈrʌbə / (BrE)
An elastic material obtained from the latex of certain trees, especially Hevea and Ficus, used in making a wide range of products.
elastic material from tree latex

Examples

Tires are commonly made from natural rubber due to its strength and flexibility.
Harvesting natural rubber from rubber trees is still a major industry in Southeast Asia.
Scientists have developed ways to recycle natural rubber to reduce environmental waste.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: synthetic rubber - artificially made elastic material used as a substitute for natural rubber
