order Trichoptera

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdər trɪˈkɒptərə / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə trɪˈkɒptərə / (BrE)
a taxonomic order of insects comprising the caddis flies, recognized by their hairy wings and aquatic larvae
insect order of caddis flies

Examples

Larvae from the order Trichoptera are often found building protective cases in streams and rivers.
Scientists collected specimens belonging to the order Trichoptera during their field study of the wetland.
Members of the order Trichoptera play an important role in freshwater ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caddis fly - a type of small moth-like insect in the order Trichoptera

Etymology

From Greek 'trichos' (hair) and 'ptera' (wings), referring to their hairy wings.
