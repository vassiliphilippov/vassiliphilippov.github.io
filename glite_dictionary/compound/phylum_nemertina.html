phylum Nemertina

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪləm nɛˌmɜrˈtaɪnə / (AmE), / ˈfʌɪləm nɛˌmɜːˈtiːnə / (BrE)
A taxonomic phylum comprising the proboscis worms, a group of mostly marine, elongated, unsegmented invertebrates known for their eversible proboscis.
a phylum of proboscis worms
medical

Examples

Researchers have discovered several new species within the phylum Nemertina in deep-sea ecosystems.
The defining feature of members of the phylum Nemertina is their unique eversible proboscis.
Courses in zoology often include coverage of the phylum Nemertina and its biological significance.

Etymology

From Greek 'nēmertēs', a name for ribbon worms, with 'phylum' from Greek 'phylon' (race, stock).
