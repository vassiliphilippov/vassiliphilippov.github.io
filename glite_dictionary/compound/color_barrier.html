color barrier

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌlər ˌbæri.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈkʌlə ˌbæri.ə / (BrE)
a social or legal barrier that prevents Black people or people of other races from participating equally in activities with white people, especially in the context of racial segregation
racial segregation barrier

Examples

Jackie Robinson was the first African American to break the color barrier in Major League Baseball.
Many athletes campaigned to end the color barrier in professional sports.
The Civil Rights Movement aimed to dismantle the color barrier in American society.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: segregation - the enforced separation of different racial groups
  • Antonym: integration - the act of bringing people of different racial groups together in society
