yak butter

1. NOUN

/ jæk ˈbʌt̬ər / (AmE), / jak ˈbʌtə / (BrE)
Butter made from the milk of yaks, commonly used in Tibetan and Himalayan cuisine.
butter from yak's milk

Examples

In the highland villages, locals serve tea mixed with yak butter to help residents stay warm.
She bought a small block of yak butter at the market during her trip to Tibet.
Many traditional Tibetan dishes rely on yak butter for their rich flavor and energy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: butter - a pale yellow edible fatty substance made by churning cream
