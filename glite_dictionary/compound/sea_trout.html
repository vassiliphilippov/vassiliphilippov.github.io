sea trout

1. NOUN

/ siː traʊt / (AmE), / siː traʊt / (BrE)
A silvery marine form of the brown trout that migrates from the sea to fresh water to spawn.
a migratory marine variety of brown trout

Examples

Anglers often seek out the sea trout during its spawning migration in early summer.
The sea trout is highly prized for both its fighting spirit and its delicate flavor.
After years at sea, the sea trout returns to the river where it was born to spawn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brown trout - a species of freshwater trout native to Europe and Asia

2. NOUN

/ ˈsi ˌtraʊt / (AmE), / ˈsiː ˌtraʊt / (BrE)
any of several species of fish found in North American coastal waters, belonging to the drum family (Sciaenidae), and resembling or called 'trout', such as the weakfish.
a North American coastal fish (Sciaenidae)

Examples

We spent the morning fishing for sea trout along the Atlantic coast.
The sea trout population has declined due to overfishing and habitat loss.
Grilled sea trout with lemon and herbs is a speciality at the seaside restaurant.

Etymology

From 'sea' + 'trout', named for its habitat and resemblance to trout.

3. NOUN

/ si ˈtraʊt / (AmE), / siː ˈtraʊt / (BrE)
The flesh of a marine trout that migrates from saltwater to freshwater, often used as food.
flesh of migratory marine trout

Examples

The chef prepared the sea trout with a lemon-butter sauce and fresh herbs.
She prefers the delicate flavor of sea trout over salmon when making fish cakes.
Fresh sea trout is often available at coastal markets during spring.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: trout - flesh of a freshwater or sea fish
