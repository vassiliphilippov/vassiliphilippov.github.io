Highland Scot

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪ.lənd skɑt / (AmE), / ˈhaɪ.lənd skɒt / (BrE)
a person native to or living in the Highlands of Scotland, typically associated with the culture and traditions of that region
native or resident of the Scottish Highlands

Examples

As a proud Highland Scot, he cherished the traditions passed down through generations.
The film features a Highland Scot who struggles to preserve his family's heritage.
Many Highland Scots still speak Gaelic and wear traditional dress at festivals.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Lowland Scot - a person native to or living in the Lowlands of Scotland
