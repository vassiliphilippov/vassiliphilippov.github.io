group O

1. NOUN

/ ɡruːp oʊ / (AmE), / ɡruːp əʊ / (BrE)
the blood group whose red cells lack both A and B antigens, making it the universal donor type
blood group lacking A and B antigens
medical

Examples

Group O blood is considered the universal donor in transfusion medicine.
Hospitals often keep extra supplies of group O blood for emergencies.
Because he has group O blood, he can donate to most patients in need.

Synset Examples

people with type O blood are universal donors

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: group A - blood group with A antigen on red cells
  • Degree Higher: group B - blood group with B antigen on red cells
  • Degree Higher: group AB - blood group with both A and B antigens

Etymology

From the letter 'O', used in the ABO blood group system to denote blood lacking A and B antigens.
