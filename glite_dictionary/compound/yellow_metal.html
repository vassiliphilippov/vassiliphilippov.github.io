yellow metal

1. NOUN

/ ˈjɛloʊ ˈmɛtəl / (AmE), / ˈjɛləʊ ˈmɛtəl / (BrE)
a type of brass containing a higher proportion of zinc, making it stronger than alpha brass; used for castings and hot-worked products
a strong type of brass alloy

Examples

The pipes were made from yellow metal to withstand high temperatures during industrial processes.
Shipbuilders often chose yellow metal for fittings because of its strength and resistance to corrosion.
The factory specializes in producing yellow metal castings for machinery and heavy equipment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brass - an alloy of copper and zinc
