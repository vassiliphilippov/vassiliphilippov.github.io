European hop

1. NOUN

/ jʊˈɹoʊpiən hɑp / (AmE), / jʊəˈɹəʊpiən hɒp / (BrE)
A climbing plant native to Europe, whose flowers are chiefly used to flavor beer and other malt liquors; also cultivated in other regions.
climbing plant used to flavor beer

Examples

The distinct aroma in this ale comes from the addition of European hop.
Brewers often prefer European hop for its delicate flavor profile.
Farmers in Oregon now cultivate European hop due to its popularity among craft breweries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hop - the plant whose flowers are used as a flavoring
