mosaic gold

1. NOUN

/ moʊˈzeɪ.ɪk ɡoʊld / (AmE), / məʊˈzeɪ.ɪk ɡəʊld / (BrE)
a bright yellow pigment, often made from tin(IV) sulfide, sometimes suspended in lacquer and used in gilding and decorative painting
a yellow pigment used in gilding

Examples

The artist used mosaic gold to highlight the ornate details of the frame.
Restorers carefully matched the original mosaic gold finish on the antique mirror.
She preferred mosaic gold over traditional gold leaf for its rich yellow hue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gold leaf - thin foil of gold used for gilding
  • Synonym: gilding - the process of applying gold or gold-like substances to a surface

Etymology

So named for its use in decorating mosaics and its gold-like appearance.
