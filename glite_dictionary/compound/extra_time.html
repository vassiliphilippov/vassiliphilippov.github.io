extra time

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛkstrə taɪm / (AmE), / ˈɛkstrə taɪm / (BrE)
additional time played at the end of a game when the score is tied, to determine the winner
additional playing time to break a tie in sports

Examples

After the teams were tied at the end of the match, the referee added extra time to decide the winner.
The players looked exhausted as they began extra time in the championship final.
If the game is still tied after extra time, it will go to a penalty shootout.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overtime - additional playing time in sports beyond regulation time
