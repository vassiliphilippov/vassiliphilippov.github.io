jack-o-lantern fungus

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒæk oʊ ˈlæntɚn ˌfʌŋɡəs / (AmE), / ˌdʒæk əʊ ˈlæntən ˌfʌŋɡəs / (BrE)
a large poisonous mushroom with orange caps and clustered narrow stalks, whose gills can glow in the dark
poisonous orange luminescent mushroom

Examples

The jack-o-lantern fungus is often mistaken for edible chanterelles by inexperienced foragers.
At night, the gills of a jack-o-lantern fungus can emit an eerie greenish glow.
You should never eat a jack-o-lantern fungus because it is highly poisonous.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Omphalotus olearius - scientific name for the jack-o-lantern fungus

Etymology

Named for its glowing gills, reminiscent of a jack-o'-lantern (from English folklore and pumpkin lanterns).
