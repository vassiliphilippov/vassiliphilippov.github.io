Republic of Equatorial Guinea

1. PROPN

/ rɪˈpʌblɪk əv ɪˌkwɛtəriəl ˈɡɪni / (AmE), / rɪˈpʌblɪk əv iːˌkwætəriəl ˈɡɪːni / (BrE)
a country in west central Africa, consisting of a mainland region and several islands in the Gulf of Guinea; independent from Spain since 1968.
country in west central Africa

Examples

Oil has transformed the Republic of Equatorial Guinea into one of Africa’s fastest-growing economies.
The Republic of Equatorial Guinea is known for its rich biodiversity and forested landscapes.
In 1968, the Republic of Equatorial Guinea gained its independence from Spain.
