genus Fulica

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs fjuːˈlaɪkə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs fjuːˈliːkə / (BrE)
A genus of aquatic birds in the rail family, commonly known as coots.
genus of aquatic birds; coots

Examples

The genus Fulica is found in wetlands across much of the world.
Many species within the genus Fulica are known simply as coots.
Researchers conducted a study on the breeding habits of birds in the genus Fulica.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coot - A medium-sized water bird with a white bill and forehead shield

Etymology

From Latin 'fulica', meaning 'coot'.
