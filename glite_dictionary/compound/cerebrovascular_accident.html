cerebrovascular accident

1. NOUN

/ səˌriːbroʊˈvæskjulɚ ˈæksɪdənt / (AmE), / sɛˌriːbrəʊˈvæskjʊlə ˈæksɪdənt / (BrE)
A sudden event in which blood supply to part of the brain is interrupted or reduced, causing loss of consciousness and potentially lasting brain damage; also known as a stroke.
medical term for stroke
medical

Examples

The patient suffered a cerebrovascular accident and was admitted to the hospital immediately.
Early intervention can greatly improve recovery after a cerebrovascular accident.
Doctors are trained to recognize the signs of a cerebrovascular accident as quickly as possible.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stroke - a sudden attack of weakness or paralysis affecting one side of the body, caused by an interruption of blood flow to the brain

Etymology

From Latin cerebrum (brain) + vasculum (vessel) + accident (unexpected event).
