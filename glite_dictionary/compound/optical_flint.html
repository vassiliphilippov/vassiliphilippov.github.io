optical flint

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑptɪkəl flɪnt / (AmE), / ˈɒptɪkəl flɪnt / (BrE)
a type of optical glass with high dispersion and a high refractive index, used in lenses and prisms
high-dispersion optical glass

Examples

Modern camera lenses often incorporate optical flint to correct for chromatic aberration.
Prisms made from optical flint provide superior color separation for scientific instruments.
The manufacturer supplied several types of optical flint for the telescope project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flint glass - a type of glass containing lead, giving it a high refractive index
