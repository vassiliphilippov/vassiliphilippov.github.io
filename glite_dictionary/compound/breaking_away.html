breaking away

1. NOUN

/ ˈbreɪkɪŋ əˈweɪ / (AmE), / ˈbreɪkɪŋ əˈweɪ / (BrE)
An act of departing quickly or escaping, especially in a sudden or unexpected manner.
a quick or sudden departure

Examples

The sudden breaking away of the prisoner caught the guards by surprise.
Her breaking away from the group was decisive and bold.
The event ended with a dramatic breaking away as the protesters dispersed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: escape - the act of getting free or avoiding something unwanted

2. NOUN

/ ˌbreɪkɪŋ əˈweɪ / (AmE), / ˌbreɪkɪŋ əˈweɪ / (BrE)
the act of separating from a group, organization, or tradition, often to form something new or act independently
act of separating from a group or tradition

Examples

The movement was marked by a breaking away from traditional values.
Her breaking away from the party shocked her supporters.
A sudden breaking away of several members led to the group's decline.

Synset Examples

there was a breakaway by the discontented members
a breaking away from family and neighborhood

Related Terms

  • Synonym: breakaway - an act or instance of breaking free; a noun form with similar meaning
