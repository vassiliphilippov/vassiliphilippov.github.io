baker's yeast

1. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪkərz jist / (AmE), / ˈbeɪkəz jiːst / (BrE)
A type of yeast used as a leavening agent in baking and brewing to make dough rise.
yeast used in baking and brewing

Examples

The recipe calls for one packet of baker's yeast to help the dough rise.
If you store baker's yeast in the refrigerator, it stays fresh longer.
Baker's yeast is essential for making fluffy bread and rolls.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yeast - a microscopic fungus used in fermentation
