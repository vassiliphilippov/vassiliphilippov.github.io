Tennessee walking horse

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɛnəˈsi ˈwɔkɪŋ hɔrs / (AmE), / ˌtɛnɪˈsiː ˈwɔːkɪŋ hɔːs / (BrE)
a breed of horse known for its smooth running walk and notable stamina, often used for riding and show
breed of horse known for smooth gait

Examples

She proudly rode her Tennessee walking horse in the parade down Main Street.
The Tennessee walking horse is famous for its gentle temperament and smooth gait.
Many riders choose a Tennessee walking horse for long trail rides because of its stamina.

Etymology

Named after the U.S. state of Tennessee, where the breed was developed, and for its distinctive walking gait.
