scarlet letter

1. NOUN

/ ˈskɑrlət ˈlɛtər / (AmE), / ˈskɑːlɪt ˈlɛtə / (BrE)
a red letter 'A' that adulterers were required to wear as a mark of shame, especially in Puritan society; by extension, a symbol of public disgrace
symbol of public shame for adultery

Examples

After the scandal, it felt as though she was forced to wear a scarlet letter everywhere she went.
The novel examines the consequences of carrying a scarlet letter in a strict society.
He described social media shaming as a modern version of the scarlet letter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stigma - a mark of disgrace associated with a particular circumstance, quality, or person

Etymology

Popularized by Nathaniel Hawthorne's novel 'The Scarlet Letter' (1850), based on historical Puritan practices.
