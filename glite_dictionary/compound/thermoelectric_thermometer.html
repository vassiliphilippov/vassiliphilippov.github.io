thermoelectric thermometer

1. NOUN

/ ˌθɜrmoʊɪˈlɛktrɪk θərˈmɑːmɪtər / (AmE), / ˌθɜːməʊɪˈlɛktrɪk θəˈmɒmɪtə / (BrE)
an instrument for measuring temperature based on the thermoelectric current produced by the junction of two different metals
thermometer using thermoelectric current
medical

Examples

The scientist calibrated the thermoelectric thermometer before starting the experiment.
A thermoelectric thermometer provides precise readings in environments with rapidly changing temperatures.
Engineers prefer a thermoelectric thermometer for monitoring temperature in industrial furnaces.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thermocouple - a device consisting of two different metals used for measuring temperature via thermoelectric effect
  • Degree Higher: thermometer - instrument for measuring temperature

Etymology

thermo- (relating to heat) + electric (relating to electricity), referring to the use of thermoelectric effects in measuring temperature.
