Wuhan pneumonia

1. NOUN

/ wuˈhɑn nuːˈmoʊn.jə / (AmE), / wuːˈhæn njʊˈməʊ.ni.ə / (BrE)
An early name for COVID-19, a viral respiratory disease caused by SARS-CoV-2, first identified in the city of Wuhan, China.
early name for COVID-19 disease

Examples

Many news outlets initially reported the outbreak as Wuhan pneumonia before it was named COVID-19.
Doctors struggled to treat patients during the early days of the Wuhan pneumonia outbreak.
The term Wuhan pneumonia has largely been replaced by COVID-19 in scientific literature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: COVID-19 - the disease caused by the novel coronavirus SARS-CoV-2
  • Synonym: SARS - severe acute respiratory syndrome, a related viral disease

Etymology

Named after Wuhan, China, where the disease was first identified, and 'pneumonia' for the lung inflammation it causes.
