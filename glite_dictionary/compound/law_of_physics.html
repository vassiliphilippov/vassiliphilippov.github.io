law of physics

1. NOUN

/ lɔ əv ˈfɪzɪks / (AmE), / lɔː əv ˈfɪzɪks / (BrE)
a statement describing a fundamental principle that governs natural phenomena in the physical world, usually expressed as a mathematical relationship
a principle governing physical phenomena

Examples

According to the law of physics, energy cannot be created or destroyed.
She always tried to explain every phenomenon using some law of physics.
New experiments occasionally challenge an established law of physics.

Synset Examples

They seem to have the law of physics on their side.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: physical law - a general statement or formula describing regularities in the physical world
