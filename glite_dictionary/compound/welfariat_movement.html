welfariat movement

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɛlˌfɛriət ˈmuːvmənt / (AmE), / ˈwɛl.fə.ri.ət ˈmuːv.mənt / (BrE)
A social movement focused on promoting or improving welfare policies and the rights of welfare recipients.
social movement for welfare issues

Examples

The welfariat movement gained momentum in response to proposed cuts to social benefits.
Activists within the welfariat movement organize protests and campaigns for policy reform.
She published a book examining the history of the welfariat movement in the 20th century.

Synset Examples

The welfariat movement aims to empower underprivileged communities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: welfare movement - social effort to promote welfare programs and rights

Etymology

Formed from 'welfariat' (from 'welfare' + -iat, as in proletariat) and 'movement'.
