toothless argument

1. NOUN

/ ˈtuθ.ləs ˈɑr.ɡjə.mənt / (AmE), / ˈtuːθ.ləs ˈɑː(ɡ).jʊ.mənt / (BrE)
an argument that lacks power, strength, or effectiveness
ineffective or weak argument

Examples

The lawyer's toothless argument failed to convince the jury.
Critics dismissed the proposal as a toothless argument lacking real substance.
She realized her case rested on a toothless argument with no factual support.

Synset Examples

His argument was toothless without any solid evidence.
Toothlessness in his argument led to its dismissal.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: strong argument - an argument that is convincing and persuasive
