herbal medicine

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɜrbəl ˈmɛdəsɪn / (AmE), / ˈhɜːbəl ˈmɛdəsɪn / (BrE)
A system of medicine that uses plants or plant extracts to prevent or treat diseases or to promote health.
medicine using plants

Examples

Many people turn to herbal medicine for relief from chronic conditions.
She studied the properties of plants in order to practice herbal medicine.
Some modern doctors integrate herbal medicine with conventional treatments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phytotherapy - the use of plant-derived medicines in the treatment and prevention of disease

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɝːbəl ˈmɛdɪsən / (AmE), / ˈhɜːbəl ˈmɛdɪsən / (BrE)
A substance or preparation made from plants and used to prevent or treat disease or to promote general health.
medicine made from plants

Examples

Many people use herbal medicine as a complementary therapy in addition to conventional treatments.
Research on the effectiveness of herbal medicine has increased significantly in recent years.
She visited an expert in herbal medicine to find natural remedies for her allergies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phytotherapy - the treatment of disease by the use of plant extracts
  • Antonym: conventional medicine - the standard medical practices using drugs and surgery
