probate will

1. NOUN

/ ˈproʊˌbeɪt wɪl / (AmE), / ˈprəʊˌbeɪt wɪl / (BrE)
An official certificate issued by a court confirming the validity of a will and authorizing executors to administer the estate.
legal certificate confirming a will
legal

Examples

The executor presented the probate will to the court to gain authority over the estate.
She was unable to access the assets until the probate will was officially granted.
A probate will ensures that the deceased's wishes are legally recognized and can be carried out.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: will - a legal document with a person's wishes for after death

Etymology

From Latin probatus (proved, tested) and will (Old English willa, wish, desire).
