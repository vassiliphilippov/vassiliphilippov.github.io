degenerative joint disease

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈdʒɛnərətɪv dʒɔɪnt dɪˈziz / (AmE), / dɪˈdʒɛnərətɪv dʒɔɪnt dɪˈziːz / (BrE)
a chronic condition characterized by the progressive breakdown of cartilage in the joints, commonly known as osteoarthritis
chronic cartilage breakdown in joints; osteoarthritis
medical

Examples

The x-rays revealed signs of degenerative joint disease in his knees.
Physical therapy can help manage the symptoms of degenerative joint disease.
She was diagnosed with degenerative joint disease after experiencing persistent joint pain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: osteoarthritis - a type of arthritis caused by degeneration of joint cartilage
  • Antonym: rheumatoid arthritis - a chronic inflammatory disorder affecting joints
