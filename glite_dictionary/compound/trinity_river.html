Trinity River

1. PROPN

/ ˈtrɪn.ə.ti ˈrɪv.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈtrɪn.ɪ.ti ˈrɪv.ə / (BrE)
a river in eastern Texas, United States, rising near Dallas and flowing southeast to Galveston Bay
river in eastern Texas, USA

Examples

The Trinity River runs through the heart of Dallas before emptying into Galveston Bay.
Flooding along the Trinity River prompted several road closures in the region.
Conservation groups are working to protect wildlife habitats along the Trinity River.

Etymology

Named by Spanish explorers in the 17th century as 'La Santísima Trinidad' (The Most Holy Trinity).
