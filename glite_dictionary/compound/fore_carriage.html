fore carriage

1. NOUN

/ fɔr ˈkærɪdʒ / (AmE), / fɔː ˈkærɪdʒ / (BrE)
a small wheeled carriage attached under the front end of a plow beam, used to support and guide the plow
front carriage on a plow

Examples

The farmer repaired the fore carriage to ensure the plow moved smoothly across the field.
Older plows often required a sturdy fore carriage for better stability and ease of use.
After years of use, the wooden wheels on the fore carriage needed to be replaced.
