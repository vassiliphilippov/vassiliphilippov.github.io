Eurasian kingfisher

1. NOUN

/ jʊˈreɪʒən ˈkɪŋˌfɪʃər / (AmE), / jʊəˈreɪʒən ˈkɪŋˌfɪʃə / (BrE)
a small brightly colored kingfisher (Alcedo atthis) with greenish-blue and orange plumage, found across Europe and Asia
small bird; kingfisher species of Europe and Asia

Examples

The Eurasian kingfisher is often seen perched above rivers waiting to catch fish.
With its vivid blue and orange feathers, the Eurasian kingfisher is easy to identify.
Birdwatchers travel to wetlands to catch a glimpse of the elusive Eurasian kingfisher.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kingfisher - a type of bird known for its bright colors and fishing behavior
