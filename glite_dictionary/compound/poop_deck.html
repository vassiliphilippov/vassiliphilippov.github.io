poop deck

1. NOUN

/ pup dɛk / (AmE), / puːp dɛk / (BrE)
a partial weather deck located at the stern of a ship, typically forming the roof of the cabin built in the rear superstructure
deck at the stern of a ship

Examples

The captain stood on the poop deck to address the crew assembled below.
During storms, waves often crashed over the poop deck, soaking everyone on watch.
From the poop deck, you could see a wide view of the ocean stretching to the horizon.

Etymology

From French 'poupe' (stern), from Latin 'puppis' (stern of a ship).
