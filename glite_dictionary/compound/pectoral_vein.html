pectoral vein

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛktərəl veɪn / (AmE), / ˈpɛktərəl veɪn / (BrE)
a vein that drains blood from the pectoral muscles and empties into the subclavian vein
vein draining the pectoral muscles
medical

Examples

The pectoral vein plays an important role in draining blood from the chest muscles.
Damage to the pectoral vein during surgery can lead to complications.
Anatomy students studied the path of the pectoral vein in their dissection class.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: subclavian vein - large vein beneath the clavicle that drains upper limb blood

Etymology

From Latin 'pectoralis' (of the chest) and 'vena' (vein).
