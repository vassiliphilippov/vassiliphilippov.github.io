Central Dravidian language

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛntrəl drəˈvɪdiən ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈsɛntrəl drəˈvɪdɪən ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
A Dravidian language or group of languages spoken mainly in central India.
Dravidian language of central India

Examples

The Gondi language is the best-known Central Dravidian language spoken by millions in central India.
Some linguists specialize in the study of Central Dravidian languages and their development.
Many villagers in Madhya Pradesh communicate in a Central Dravidian language as their mother tongue.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Dravidian language - member of the Dravidian family of languages spoken in southern India and Sri Lanka
