Pott's disease

1. NOUN

/ pɑts dɪˈziz / (AmE), / pɒts dɪˈziːz / (BrE)
Tuberculosis of the spine, characterized by the destruction of vertebrae resulting in spinal curvature.
tuberculosis of the spine
medical

Examples

The patient's severe back pain was ultimately diagnosed as Pott's disease.
In the 19th century, Pott's disease was a common cause of spinal deformity.
Effective antibiotics have made Pott's disease much less frequent in modern hospitals.

Etymology

Named after Sir Percivall Pott, the British surgeon who described the disease in the 18th century.
