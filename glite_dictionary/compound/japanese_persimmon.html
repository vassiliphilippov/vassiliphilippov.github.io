Japanese persimmon

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˈpænɪz pərˈsɪmən / (AmE), / dʒəˈpæn.iːz pəˈsɪm.ən / (BrE)
A small deciduous tree native to East Asia, bearing large red or orange edible fruit with an astringent taste.
East Asian tree with edible orange fruit

Examples

The Japanese persimmon is prized for its large, sweet fruit and ornamental value.
Farmers harvested the ripe Japanese persimmons late in the autumn for market.
My grandmother makes a delicious jam from Japanese persimmon each year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: persimmon - a sweet, edible orange fruit from several species of trees in the genus Diospyros
