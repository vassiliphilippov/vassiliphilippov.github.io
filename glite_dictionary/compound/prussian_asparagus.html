Prussian asparagus

1. NOUN

/ prʌʃən əˈspærəɡəs / (AmE), / ˈprʊʃ(ə)n əˈspærəɡəs / (BrE)
an Old World plant (star of Bethlehem) whose young shoots are edible, known botanically as Ornithogalum pyrenaicum
a plant with edible shoots (Ornithogalum pyrenaicum)

Examples

During the spring, locals gather Prussian asparagus from the wild as a seasonal delicacy.
The tender shoots of Prussian asparagus are prized in gourmet cuisine for their delicate flavor.
She sautéed Prussian asparagus with garlic and lemon for a simple side dish.
