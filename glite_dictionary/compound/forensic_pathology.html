forensic pathology

1. NOUN

/ fəˈrɛnsɪk pəˈθɑləʤi / (AmE), / fəˈrɛnsɪk pəˈθɒləʤi / (BrE)
the branch of medical science concerned with applying medical knowledge to legal investigations, especially in determining causes of death
medical branch for legal investigations
medical legal

Examples

He decided to specialize in forensic pathology after studying medicine.
Forensic pathology played a crucial role in solving the high-profile case.
Advances in forensic pathology have improved the accuracy of time-of-death estimates.

Synset Examples

forensic pathology provided the evidence that convicted the murderer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pathology - the scientific study of disease

Etymology

From Latin 'forensis' (of a forum or court of law) and Greek 'pathologia' (study of disease).
