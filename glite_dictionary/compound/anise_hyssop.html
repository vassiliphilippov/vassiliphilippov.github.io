anise hyssop

1. NOUN

/ ˈænɪs ˈhɪsəp / (AmE), / ˈænɪs ˈhɪsəp / (BrE)
A North American herb (Agastache foeniculum) of the mint family, having fragrant leaves with an anise-like scent, often used in herbal teas and gardens.
herb with anise-like scent, Agastache foeniculum

Examples

The tea made from anise hyssop leaves is both soothing and aromatic.
Many gardeners plant anise hyssop to attract bees and butterflies.
Dried anise hyssop can be used as a seasoning in various dishes.

Etymology

From 'anise' (derived from Latin anisum) and 'hyssop' (from Greek hyssōpos, a type of aromatic plant).
