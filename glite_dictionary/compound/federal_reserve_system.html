Federal Reserve System

1. PROPN

/ ˈfɛdərəl rɪˈzɝv ˈsɪstəm / (AmE), / ˈfɛdərəl rɪˈzɜːv ˈsɪstəm / (BrE)
the central banking system of the United States, responsible for regulating monetary policy and overseeing financial institutions
U.S. central banking system

Examples

The Federal Reserve System raised interest rates to curb inflation.
Banks across the U.S. are regulated by the Federal Reserve System.
The chairman of the Federal Reserve System gave a speech about economic recovery.

Synset Examples

the Fed seeks to control the United States economy by raising and lowering short-term interest rates and the money supply

Related Terms

  • Synonym: central bank - the national institution providing financial and monetary services for a country or group of countries
