order Alcyonaria

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdər ˌælsiəˈnɛəriə / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə ˌælsɪəˈnɛːrɪə / (BrE)
a taxonomic order of corals and sea anemones characterized by having eight-branched tentacles and eight septa.
order of corals with eight-branched tentacles

Examples

Soft corals from the order Alcyonaria are often found in tropical reefs.
Scientists classified the new species in the order Alcyonaria due to its eight-branched tentacles.
The diversity within the order Alcyonaria contributes to the complexity of coral reef ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: octocoral - any coral belonging to the subclass Octocorallia, having eight-fold symmetry

Etymology

From New Latin Alcyonaria, from Greek alkyon (kingfisher, also used for certain coral types).
