sieve tube

1. NOUN

/ siv tuːb / (AmE), / sɪv tjuːb / (BrE)
A tube formed by cells joined end-to-end, through which nutrients are transported in the phloem of flowering plants and some algae.
tube for nutrient flow in plant phloem

Examples

Sugars move through the phloem via sieve tubes to reach various parts of the plant.
Damage to the sieve tube can disrupt nutrient transport and harm the plant's growth.
Brown algae also contain specialized sieve tubes for conducting organic materials.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phloem - vascular tissue in plants that transports nutrients
