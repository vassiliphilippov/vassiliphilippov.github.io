proportional sampling

1. NOUN

/ pɹəˈpɔɹʃənl ˈsæmplɪŋ / (AmE), / pɹəˈpɔːʃənl ˈsæmplɪŋ / (BrE)
a sampling method in which the population is divided into subgroups, and random samples from each are selected in proportion to their size in the population
statistical method: sampling proportional to subgroups

Examples

Many researchers use proportional sampling to ensure that minority groups are adequately represented in surveys.
To obtain a more accurate picture, the company conducted proportional sampling across various age brackets.
With proportional sampling, each suburb's share of the total sample matches its share in the overall population.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stratified sampling - sampling method dividing population into strata, then sampling from each
