genus Borassus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs bəˈræsəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs bəˈræsəs / (BrE)
A genus of tall palms native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia, commonly known as palmyra palms, characterized by large fan-shaped leaves and edible fruit.
a genus of palmyra palms

Examples

The genus Borassus includes several species of palms valued for their fruit and timber.
Many rural communities in South Asia rely on the genus Borassus for food and construction materials.
Botanists have studied the genus Borassus to better understand palm diversity in tropical ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: palmyra palm - a tall tropical palm tree from the Borassus genus

Etymology

From New Latin 'Borassus', the genus name assigned by botanists.
