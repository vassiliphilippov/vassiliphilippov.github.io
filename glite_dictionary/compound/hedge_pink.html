hedge pink

1. NOUN

/ hɛdʒ pɪŋk / (AmE), / hɛdʒ pɪŋk / (BrE)
a European plant with pink or white flowers and leaves that yield a detergent when bruised, commonly known as soapwort (Saponaria officinalis)
European plant with detergent-yielding leaves

Examples

The hedge pink in her garden bloomed with vibrant pink flowers throughout the summer.
Villagers once used the crushed leaves of the hedge pink as a natural soap.
Botanists study the hedge pink for its unique detergent properties among European flora.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soapwort - plant with detergent properties, Saponaria officinalis
