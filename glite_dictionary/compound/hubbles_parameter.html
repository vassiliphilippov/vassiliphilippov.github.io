Hubble's parameter

1. NOUN

/ ˈhʌbəlz pəˌræmətɚ / (AmE), / ˈhʌbəlz pəˌraːmɪtə / (BrE)
(cosmology) the ratio of the speed at which a galaxy is receding from the observer to its distance from the observer, measuring the current rate of expansion of the universe.
rate of universal expansion in cosmology

Examples

Scientists use the Hubble's parameter to estimate the age of the universe.
An accurate value for Hubble's parameter helps astronomers understand how fast the universe is expanding today.
The measurement of Hubble's parameter is crucial for modern cosmological research.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hubble constant - the value of Hubble's parameter measured today

Etymology

Named after American astronomer Edwin Hubble (1889–1953).
