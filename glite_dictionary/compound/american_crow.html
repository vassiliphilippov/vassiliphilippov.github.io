American crow

1. NOUN

/ əˈmɛrɪkən kroʊ / (AmE), / əˈmɛrɪkən krəʊ / (BrE)
a large, glossy black bird of North America (Corvus brachyrhynchos) known for its intelligence and loud cawing call
common black crow of North America

Examples

The American crow is easily recognized by its all-black plumage and distinctive cawing sound.
Farmers sometimes regard the American crow as a pest because it feeds on crops.
In city parks, you can often see American crows scavenging for food.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crow - a large, black, common bird of the genus Corvus
