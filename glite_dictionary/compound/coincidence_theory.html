coincidence theory

1. NOUN

/ kəˈɪnsɪdəns ˈθɪri / (AmE), / kəʊˈɪnsɪdəns ˈθɪəri / (BrE)
the study of patterns or events that appear random but may reveal underlying connections or statistical regularities
study of seemingly random patterns

Examples

She became interested in coincidence theory after reading about statistical anomalies in nature.
The professor's research in coincidence theory revealed surprising connections between unrelated data sets.
Many believe that coincidence theory helps explain why random patterns are often misinterpreted as meaningful.
