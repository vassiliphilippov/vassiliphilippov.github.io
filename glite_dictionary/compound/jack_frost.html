Jack Frost

1. NOUN

/ dʒæk ˈfrɑst / (AmE), / dʒak ˈfrɒst / (BrE)
The personification of frost or winter weather, often depicted as a figure who brings cold weather.
personification of frost or winter

Examples

Children imagine that Jack Frost paints icy patterns on their windows overnight.
During the cold snap, it felt as though Jack Frost had come to visit the town.
Every winter, my grandmother tells us stories about Jack Frost nipping at our noses.

Etymology

Formed from the given name Jack (used for personifications) and frost.
