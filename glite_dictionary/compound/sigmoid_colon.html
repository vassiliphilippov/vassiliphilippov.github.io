sigmoid colon

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪɡmɔɪd ˈkoʊlɑn / (AmE), / ˈsɪɡmɔɪd ˈkəʊlɒn / (BrE)
the S-shaped section of the large intestine between the descending colon and the rectum
S-shaped section of the large intestine
medical

Examples

During a colonoscopy, the doctor carefully examined the sigmoid colon for any abnormalities.
Diverticulitis often affects the sigmoid colon due to its shape and location.
The surgeon removed a section of the sigmoid colon to treat the blockage.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: rectum - the final section of the large intestine, ending at the anus
  • Degree Lower: descending colon - the part of the colon before the sigmoid colon

Etymology

From Greek sigma (the letter S, referring to the shape) + colon (large intestine).
