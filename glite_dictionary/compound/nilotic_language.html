Nilotic language

1. NOUN

/ naɪˈlɑːtɪk ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / naɪˈlɒtɪk ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
a member of a group of languages spoken in East Africa, belonging to the Chari-Nile group
language group of East Africa

Examples

The Maasai people speak a Nilotic language known as Maa.
Researchers are studying the evolution of Nilotic languages in East Africa.
Dinka is an example of a Nilotic language spoken in South Sudan.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Nilo-Saharan - large language family including Nilotic languages

Etymology

From Nilotic, relating to the Nile, and language, referring to forms of speech.
