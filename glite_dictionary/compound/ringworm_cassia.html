ringworm cassia

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɪŋˌwɜrm ˈkæsiə / (AmE), / ˈrɪŋwɜːm ˈkæsiə / (BrE)
a tropical shrub, especially native to the Americas, with yellow flowers and large leaves, whose juice is used to treat ringworm and poisonous bites
tropical shrub used for medicinal purposes

Examples

Many herbalists recommend ringworm cassia for treating certain skin conditions due to its medicinal properties.
The large yellow blossoms of the ringworm cassia attract bees and butterflies in tropical gardens.
In traditional medicine, the leaves of ringworm cassia are often used to make a healing paste.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Senna alata - scientific name for ringworm cassia
