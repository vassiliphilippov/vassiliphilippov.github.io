Karen language

1. NOUN

/ kəˈrɛn ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / kəˈrɛn ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Tibeto-Burman language (or group of languages) spoken by the Karen people in the border regions of Myanmar (Burma) and Thailand.
a language spoken by the Karen people in Myanmar and Thailand

Examples

The Karen language is taught in some schools in refugee camps along the Thai-Myanmar border.
Linguists have documented several dialects of the Karen language.
She grew up speaking both Burmese and the Karen language at home.

Etymology

From Karen, the name of the ethnic group, + language.
