nitrous oxide

1. NOUN

/ ˈnaɪtrəs ˈɑksaɪd / (AmE), / ˈnaɪtrəs ˈɒksaɪd / (BrE)
a colourless gas (N₂O) with a sweetish odour, used as an anaesthetic, especially in dentistry, and as a propellant and oxidizer
a gas used as an anaesthetic and propellant
medical

Examples

Dentists often use nitrous oxide to help patients relax during procedures.
The use of nitrous oxide as an anaesthetic dates back to the 19th century.
Some racing cars use nitrous oxide to boost engine performance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: laughing gas - common nickname for nitrous oxide due to its euphoric effects

Etymology

From 'nitrous', relating to nitrogen, and 'oxide', from French 'oxyde' meaning an oxygen compound.
