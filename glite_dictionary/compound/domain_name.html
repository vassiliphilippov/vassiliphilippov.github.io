domain name

1. NOUN

/ doʊˈmeɪn neɪm / (AmE), / dəˈmeɪn neɪm / (BrE)
a unique address made up of letters and numbers, separated by periods, identifying websites or resources on the internet
internet address identifying websites or computers

Examples

Every business should register a domain name that matches its brand.
Type the domain name into your browser to visit the website.
There is a fee for renewing your domain name each year.

Synset Examples

domain names are organized hierarchically with the more generic parts to the right

Related Terms

  • Synonym: URL - uniform resource locator; the address of a web page
  • Antonym: IP address - numerical label assigned to each device on a computer network

Etymology

From 'domain' (an area of control or ownership) and 'name', reflecting its function as a unique identifier for an internet address.
