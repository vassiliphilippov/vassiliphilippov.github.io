countermortar fire

1. NOUN

/ ˌkaʊntərˈmɔrtər faɪr / (AmE), / ˌkaʊntəˈmɔːtə faɪə / (BrE)
mortar fire directed at destroying or neutralizing enemy mortars or weapons
military fire aimed at enemy mortars

Examples

The commanders ordered countermortar fire after detecting the enemy’s mortar position.
Our unit came under heavy countermortar fire while advancing across the field.
Intelligence provided accurate coordinates for launching countermortar fire against hostile positions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: counterbattery fire - artillery fire directed at enemy artillery

Etymology

From 'counter-' (against) and 'mortar fire'.
