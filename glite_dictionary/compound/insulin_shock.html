insulin shock

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪn.sə.lɪn ʃɑk / (AmE), / ˈɪn.sjʊ.lɪn ʃɒk / (BrE)
a condition caused by excessive insulin in the body leading to dangerously low blood sugar, often resulting in unconsciousness or coma
dangerous condition of low blood sugar from too much insulin
medical

Examples

The patient was rushed to the hospital after going into insulin shock during the night.
Managing diabetes includes knowing the signs of insulin shock and how to respond quickly.
He collapsed at work from insulin shock after accidentally injecting too much insulin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hypoglycemia - abnormally low level of sugar in the blood
  • Synonym: insulin coma - coma resulting from an overdose of insulin

2. NOUN

/ ˈɪn.sə.lɪn ʃɑk / (AmE), / ˈɪn.sjʊ.lɪn ʃɒk / (BrE)
a severe condition or treatment resulting from excessive insulin administration, causing convulsions and coma
coma or convulsions caused by excess insulin
medical

Examples

The patient was hospitalized after experiencing insulin shock as a result of an overdose.
Doctors once used insulin shock therapy to treat certain psychiatric conditions.
Early symptoms of insulin shock can include confusion, sweating, and rapid heartbeat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hypoglycemic shock - shock caused by dangerously low blood sugar levels
