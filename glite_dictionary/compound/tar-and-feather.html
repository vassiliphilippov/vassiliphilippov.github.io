to tar-and-feather

1. VERB

/ tɑɹ ən ˈfɛðɚ / (AmE), / tɑː ənd ˈfɛðə / (BrE)
to cover someone with tar and then with feathers as a form of public punishment or humiliation, especially in the past
cover someone with tar and feathers as punishment

Examples

In the 18th century, angry mobs would often tar-and-feather tax collectors as a warning to others.
The rebel leader threatened to tar-and-feather those who betrayed his cause.
He was tar-and-feathered for his unpopular opinion, although such punishments are now considered barbaric.

Synset Examples

The thief was tarred and feathered
