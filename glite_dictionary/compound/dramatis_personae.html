dramatis personae

1. NOUN

/ ˌdræmətɪs pərˈsoʊni / (AmE), / ˌdræmətɪs pəˈsəʊniː / (BrE)
the characters or actors in a play, especially as listed at the beginning of the script
the characters or actors in a play

Examples

The dramatis personae was listed on the first page of the program, giving the audience an overview of the characters.
Before rehearsals began, the director introduced the entire dramatis personae to the crew.
Modern productions sometimes update the dramatis personae to reflect contemporary settings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cast - the group of actors in a play, film, or show

Etymology

From Latin, meaning 'persons of the drama'.
