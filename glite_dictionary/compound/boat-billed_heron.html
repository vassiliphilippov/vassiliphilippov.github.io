boat-billed heron

1. NOUN

/ ˌboʊt bɪld ˈhɛr.ən / (AmE), / ˌbəʊt bɪld ˈher.ən / (BrE)
a tropical American heron (Cochlearius cochlearius) with a wide, boat-shaped bill, related to the night herons
tropical heron with a broad, boat-shaped bill

Examples

The boat-billed heron is often seen hunting for fish at dusk along mangrove rivers.
With its distinctively wide bill, the boat-billed heron stands out among other wading birds.
During our trip to Central America, we spotted a boat-billed heron perched silently on a branch.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: heron - a long-legged wading bird
  • Synonym: night heron - herons of the genus Nycticorax, active at night
