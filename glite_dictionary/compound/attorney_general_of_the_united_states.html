Attorney General of the United States

1. NOUN

/ əˈtɝːni dʒəˈnɝːəl əv ði ˌju ɛs / (AmE), / əˈtɜːni dʒəˈnɜːrəl ɒv ði ˌjuː ɛs / (BrE)
the head of the U.S. Department of Justice and chief law enforcement officer of the federal government
head of U.S. Justice Department
legal american

Examples

The Attorney General of the United States addressed Congress about new cybersecurity laws.
Merrick Garland was sworn in as Attorney General of the United States in 2021.
The responsibilities of the Attorney General of the United States include representing the federal government in legal matters.

Synset Examples

the post of Attorney General was created in 1789

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attorney general - the chief legal officer of a state or nation

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'attornatus' (appointed) and 'general' (from Latin 'generalis'); 'Attorney General' is a post-nominal construction meaning 'general attorney.'
