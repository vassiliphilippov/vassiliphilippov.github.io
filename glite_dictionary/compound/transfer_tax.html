transfer tax

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrænsfɚ ˌtæks / (AmE), / ˈtrɑːnsfə ˌtæks / (BrE)
a tax imposed on the passing of title to property from one person or entity to another
tax on passing property title

Examples

The buyer is responsible for paying the transfer tax when purchasing the house.
A transfer tax is often assessed when property changes ownership, such as in real estate sales.
Many states impose a transfer tax on the sale of automobiles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inheritance tax - tax paid on property or money inherited after someone dies
  • Synonym: gift tax - tax imposed on the transfer of property by one individual to another while receiving nothing in return or less than full value
