nautical chain

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɔtɪkəl tʃeɪn / (AmE), / ˈnɔːtɪkəl tʃeɪn / (BrE)
a unit of length used in nautical contexts, measuring 15 feet (about 4.57 meters)
nautical unit of length (15 ft)

Examples

The anchor was attached to thirty nautical chains of heavy steel cable.
Old maritime records often mention distances expressed in nautical chain units.
Navigators once measured harbor depths using a nautical chain as the standard.
