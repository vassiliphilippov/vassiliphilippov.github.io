positron emission tomography scanner

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑzɪˌtrɑn ɪˈmɪʃən təˌmɑɡrəfi ˈskænər / (AmE), / ˈpɒzɪˌtrɒn ɪˈmɪʃən təˈmɒɡrəfi ˈskænə / (BrE)
a medical imaging device that creates detailed cross-sectional images of metabolic processes in the body using positron emission tomography (PET) technology
medical imaging device for metabolic processes (PET)
medical

Examples

The hospital recently purchased a new positron emission tomography scanner to improve its cancer detection capabilities.
A radiologist explained how a positron emission tomography scanner reveals areas of high metabolic activity in the body.
With the help of a positron emission tomography scanner, doctors can monitor the effectiveness of treatments for neurological disorders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: PET scanner - device using positron emission tomography for medical imaging
  • Degree Lower: computed tomography scanner - medical imaging device that uses X-rays to create cross-sectional images (CT scan)

Etymology

Formed from 'positron' (the antimatter equivalent of the electron), 'emission', and 'tomography' (from Greek 'tomos' meaning slice and 'graphia' meaning writing), describing the imaging technique.
