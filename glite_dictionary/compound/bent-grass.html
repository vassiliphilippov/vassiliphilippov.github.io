bent-grass

1. NOUN

/ bɛnt ˌɡræs / (AmE), / bɛnt ˌɡrɑːs / (BrE)
A type of grass commonly used for lawns, pastures, and especially in bowling and putting greens.
type of grass for lawns and greens

Examples

The golf course features pristine bent-grass greens that are carefully maintained year-round.
Many landscapers recommend bent-grass for high-traffic lawns because of its resilience and fine texture.
He prefers to use bent-grass for his backyard putting green due to its smooth, dense growth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agrostis - genus of perennial grasses including bent-grass

Etymology

From 'bent' (an old term for certain grasses) and 'grass'.
