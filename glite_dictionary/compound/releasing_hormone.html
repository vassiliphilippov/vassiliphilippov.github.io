releasing hormone

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈlisɪŋ ˈhɔrˌmoʊn / (AmE), / rɪˈliːsɪŋ ˈhɔːməʊn / (BrE)
any of several hormones produced in the hypothalamus that stimulate the release of specific hormones from the anterior pituitary gland
hormone from hypothalamus that stimulates pituitary hormone release
medical

Examples

The hypothalamus produces releasing hormones that control the secretion of other hormones in the body.
A deficiency in a particular releasing hormone can affect growth and development.
Researchers are studying how different releasing hormones affect stress response in mammals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hormone - a regulatory substance produced in an organism to stimulate specific cells or tissues

2. NOUN

/ rɪˈlisɪŋ ˈhɔrˌmoʊn / (AmE), / rɪˈliːsɪŋ ˈhɔːməʊn / (BrE)
a hormone produced by the hypothalamus that stimulates the release of a specific hormone from the anterior pituitary gland
hormone that stimulates pituitary secretion
medical

Examples

The hypothalamus secretes a releasing hormone to stimulate the production of thyroid-stimulating hormone.
Corticotropin releasing hormone plays a crucial role in the body's stress response.
Researchers discovered a new type of releasing hormone involved in growth regulation.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inhibiting hormone - a hormone that inhibits the release of another hormone

Etymology

Formed from 'release' and 'hormone', following scientific nomenclature for hormones that trigger secretion of other hormones.
