Atomic Energy Commission

1. PROPN

/ əˈtɑː.mɪk ˈɛn.ɚ.dʒi kəˈmɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / əˈtɒ.mɪk ˈen.ə.dʒi kəˈmɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
a former United States government agency (1946–1974) responsible for overseeing atomic energy research and its civilian applications.
former US agency for atomic energy research
american

Examples

The Atomic Energy Commission played a vital role in overseeing the development of nuclear power in the 1950s.
After 1974, the responsibilities of the Atomic Energy Commission were transferred to other agencies.
The history of the Atomic Energy Commission provides insight into the challenges of regulating nuclear technology.
