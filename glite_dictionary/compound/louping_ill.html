louping ill

1. NOUN

/ ˈluːpɪŋ ˌɪl / (AmE), / ˈluːpɪŋ ˌɪl / (BrE)
An acute viral disease affecting the nervous system of sheep, caused by a virus transmitted by the tick Ixodes ricinus.
viral disease of sheep's nervous system
medical

Examples

The veterinarian diagnosed the sheep with louping ill after it displayed symptoms of tremors and incoordination.
Farmers in Scotland take precautions during tick season to prevent outbreaks of louping ill.
Vaccination is an effective measure to protect flocks from louping ill.

Etymology

From Scots loup (to leap), describing the leaping movements seen in affected animals, and 'ill' meaning sickness.
