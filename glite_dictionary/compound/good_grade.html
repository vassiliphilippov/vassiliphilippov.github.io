good grade

1. NOUN

/ ɡʊd ɡreɪd / (AmE), / ɡʊd ɡreɪd / (BrE)
a grade or score in school or college that is considered above average or high
high academic mark or score

Examples

If you want to get a good grade on the test, make sure you study in advance.
She was thrilled when she received a good grade in mathematics after working hard all semester.
Getting a good grade in English encouraged him to read more books.

Synset Examples

She always does her homework diligently to get good grades.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: excellent grade - a very high grade, often near the top of the scale
  • Degree Lower: passing grade - the minimum grade required to avoid failing
