gray flounder

1. NOUN

/ ɡreɪ ˈflaʊndɚ / (AmE), / ɡreɪ ˈflaʊndə / (BrE)
A species of flounder found along the Atlantic coast from North Carolina to Florida and in the eastern Gulf of Mexico.
a flounder species of the U.S. Atlantic and Gulf coasts

Examples

The fisherman was pleased to catch a large gray flounder near the Florida coast.
Some seafood restaurants in the southern United States feature gray flounder on their menus.
The gray flounder is commonly found in warm, shallow waters along the Atlantic coastline.
