Manchester terrier

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæn.tʃəstər ˈtɛr.i.ər / (AmE), / ˈman.tʃɪ.stə ˈtɛr.i.ə / (BrE)
a breed of small, short-haired dog with a black-and-tan coat, originally developed in Manchester, England for hunting rats
a small black-and-tan terrier breed

Examples

The Manchester terrier is known for its sleek black-and-tan coat and lively temperament.
She adopted a Manchester terrier from the local animal shelter.
A Manchester terrier can make a loyal and energetic companion for an active family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rat terrier - a breed of terrier developed to hunt vermin

Etymology

Named after Manchester, England, where the breed was developed.
