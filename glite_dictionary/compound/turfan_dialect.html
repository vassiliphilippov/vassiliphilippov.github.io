Turfan dialect

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʊrˌfæn ˈdaɪ.əˌlɛkt / (AmE), / ˈtɜː.fæn ˈdaɪ.ə.lɛkt / (BrE)
the variety of the Tocharian language that was spoken in the regions of Qarašähär and Turpan in present-day Xinjiang, China
Tocharian dialect of Qarašähär and Turpan

Examples

Many ancient manuscripts have been discovered in the Turfan dialect, shedding light on Silk Road history.
Linguists study the Turfan dialect to understand the development of Tocharian languages.
The Turfan dialect differs significantly from the Tocharian A dialect found in other regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Tocharian - an extinct branch of the Indo-European language family formerly spoken in Central Asia

Etymology

Named after Turfan (Turpan), a city in Xinjiang, China; 'dialect' from Greek dialektos (discourse, language).
