spina bifida

1. NOUN

/ ˌspaɪnə ˈbɪfɪdə / (AmE), / ˌspaɪnə ˈbɪfɪdə / (BrE)
a congenital defect in which the spinal column is incomplete, causing the spinal cord or its coverings to protrude outside the body
congenital defect of the spinal column
medical

Examples

The doctors confirmed the diagnosis of spina bifida after the ultrasound examination.
Children born with spina bifida may need specialized medical care and physical therapy.
Advances in prenatal testing have improved early detection of spina bifida.

Etymology

From Latin, meaning 'split spine'; 'spina' (spine) and 'bifida' (split).
