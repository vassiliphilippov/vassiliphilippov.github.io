fore plane

1. NOUN

/ fɔr pleɪn / (AmE), / fɔː pleɪn / (BrE)
a carpenter's plane that is intermediate in length and function between a jack plane and a jointer plane, used to flatten and smooth wood surfaces
carpenter's plane between jack and jointer

Examples

The carpenter used a fore plane to flatten the rough surface before finishing the board.
After the jack plane, the fore plane helps bring the wood closer to its final shape.
Traditional woodworkers often rely on a fore plane for preparing large panels by hand.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: jack plane - a carpenter's plane, typically shorter, used for rough shaping of wood
  • Degree Higher: jointer plane - a long carpenter's plane used for final smoothing and truing of boards

Etymology

From 'fore' meaning 'before' and 'plane' (tool for smoothing surfaces), indicating its use prior to the final smoothing.
