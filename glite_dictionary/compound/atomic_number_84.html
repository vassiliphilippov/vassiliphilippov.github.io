atomic number 84

1. NOUN

/ əˌtɑmɪk ˈnʌmbɚ ˈeɪti ˈfɔr / (AmE), / əˌtɒmɪk ˈnʌmbə ˈeɪti ˈfɔː / (BrE)
the chemical element polonium; a radioactive metallic element similar to tellurium and bismuth, found in uranium ores or produced by bombarding bismuth with neutrons
the chemical element polonium (atomic number 84)

Examples

Marie Curie discovered atomic number 84, which she named polonium, during her research into radioactive elements.
Exposure to atomic number 84 can be extremely hazardous due to its high radioactivity.
Scientists use atomic number 84 in some industrial devices for its ability to release alpha particles.
