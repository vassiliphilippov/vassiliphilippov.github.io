Atlantic halibut

1. NOUN

/ əˈtlæntɪk ˈhæləˌbət / (AmE), / ətˈlæntɪk ˈhælɪbət / (BrE)
a large species of flatfish (Hippoglossus hippoglossus) found in the North Atlantic, valued for its edible flesh
large North Atlantic flatfish

Examples

The Atlantic halibut is highly prized by commercial fishermen for its firm, white flesh.
Overfishing has caused a significant decline in wild Atlantic halibut populations.
A record-breaking Atlantic halibut was caught off the coast of Norway, weighing over 200 kilograms.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: halibut - any of various large flatfishes, especially of the genus Hippoglossus
