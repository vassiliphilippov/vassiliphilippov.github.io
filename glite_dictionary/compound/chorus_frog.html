chorus frog

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔːrəs frɔɡ / (AmE), / ˈkɔːrəs frɒɡ / (BrE)
a small North American frog, known for its loud, musical call, belonging to the genus Pseudacris
small North American frog with a loud call

Examples

During the spring, the chorus frog can be heard calling in wetlands across the region.
We spotted a small chorus frog camouflaged among the reeds near the pond.
Biologists recorded the population trends of the chorus frog over several years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spring peeper - a small chorus frog notable for its high-pitched call, Pseudacris crucifer
