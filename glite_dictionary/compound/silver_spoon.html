silver spoon

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪlvɚ spun / (AmE), / ˈsɪlvə spuːn / (BrE)
A situation of inherited wealth and privilege, typically from being born into an established upper-class family.
inherited wealth and privilege

Examples

Growing up with a silver spoon, she never had to worry about money.
He dislikes being judged just because he was born with a silver spoon.
Many politicians come from silver spoon backgrounds, which can distance them from average voters.

Synset Examples

he was born with a silver spoon in his mouth
she is the daughter of old money from Massachusetts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: old money - wealth inherited across generations; established wealth
  • Antonym: nouveau riche - people who have recently acquired wealth, contrasted with inherited wealth

Etymology

Refers to the tradition of giving silver spoons as christening gifts to symbolize wealth; figurative usage dates back to the 18th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɪlvɚ spuːn / (AmE), / ˈsɪlvə spuːn / (BrE)
a situation of being born into wealth and privilege; used to refer to someone who benefits from a wealthy or privileged upbringing
born into wealth or privilege

Examples

She was accused of having a silver spoon and not understanding real hardship.
Many people believe that success comes more easily to those born with a silver spoon.
Despite his silver spoon upbringing, he worked hard for everything he achieved.

Synset Examples

She stirred her soup with a shiny silver spoon.
She never had to struggle, growing up with a silver spoon.
Being born with a silver spoon in his mouth made life easier.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: privileged - having special rights, advantages, or immunities
  • Antonym: underprivileged - not enjoying the same standard of living or rights as the majority of people

Etymology

Refers to the old tradition of giving silver spoons as gifts to wealthy newborns; 'born with a silver spoon in one's mouth' dates back to the 18th century.
