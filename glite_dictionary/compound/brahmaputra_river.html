Brahmaputra River

1. PROPN

/ ˌbrɑːməˈputrə ˈrɪvər / (AmE), / ˌbrɑːməˈpuːtrə ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
a major river in South Asia that flows from Tibet through India and Bangladesh, entering the Bay of Bengal
river in South Asia flowing into the Bay of Bengal

Examples

The Brahmaputra River often floods during the monsoon season, affecting millions of people.
Many diverse cultures have flourished along the banks of the Brahmaputra River.
The journey of the Brahmaputra River through India and Bangladesh is both scenic and vital to the region.

Etymology

From Sanskrit 'Brahmaputra', meaning 'son of Brahma' (a Hindu god).
