white potato

1. NOUN

/ waɪt pəˈteɪtoʊ / (AmE), / waɪt pəˈteɪtəʊ / (BrE)
A common variety of potato with white flesh, grown as a starchy food crop.
common white-fleshed potato

Examples

She boiled a white potato and mashed it with butter for dinner.
Many gardeners prefer to grow white potatoes because of their creamy texture.
The recipe called for one large white potato, peeled and diced.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: potato - the starchy tuber of a widely cultivated plant, Solanum tuberosum
