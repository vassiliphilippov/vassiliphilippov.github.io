right of search

1. NOUN

/ raɪt əv sɜrtʃ / (AmE), / raɪt əv sɜːtʃ / (BrE)
the right of a belligerent nation to stop and search neutral ships on the high seas during wartime
legal right to search neutral ships in war
legal

Examples

The admiral invoked the right of search to inspect merchant vessels passing through the blockade.
During the 19th century, disputes over the right of search often led to diplomatic tensions between nations.
Many neutral countries protested when their ships were intercepted under the right of search.
