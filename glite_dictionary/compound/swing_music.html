swing music

1. NOUN

/ swɪŋ ˈmjuzɪk / (AmE), / swɪŋ ˈmjuːzɪk / (BrE)
A style of jazz characterized by a strong rhythmical drive, performed by big bands and popular in the 1930s and 1940s.
style of jazz music from 1930s–40s

Examples

Many dancers still enjoy performing to swing music at retro-themed events.
The golden age of swing music saw bands led by Duke Ellington and Benny Goodman.
Her grandfather introduced her to swing music from his impressive vinyl collection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jazz - a style of music with syncopated rhythm and improvisation
