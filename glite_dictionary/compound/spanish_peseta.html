Spanish peseta

1. NOUN

/ spænɪʃ pəˈseɪtə / (AmE), / ˈspænɪʃ pəˈsiːtə / (BrE)
the former basic unit of currency in Spain, equal to 100 centimos, used before the adoption of the euro
former currency unit of Spain

Examples

Before the euro, people in Spain used the Spanish peseta for all their daily transactions.
He still has some old Spanish peseta coins in his collection.
The Spanish peseta was replaced by the euro in 2002.

Etymology

From Spanish 'peseta', diminutive of 'peso' (weight, coin).
