snake god

1. NOUN

/ sneɪk ɡɑd / (AmE), / sneɪk ɡɒd / (BrE)
a deity worshipped in certain voodoo and African-derived religions, often associated with snakes, especially in the West Indies
deity in voodoo and snake-centered religions

Examples

The worshippers gathered in the temple to pay homage to the snake god.
Legends about the snake god are still told in villages across the Caribbean.
Art depicting the snake god often features vibrant colors and intricate serpent designs.
