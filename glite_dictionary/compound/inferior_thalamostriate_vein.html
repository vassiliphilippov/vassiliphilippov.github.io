inferior thalamostriate vein

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈfɪriɚ ˌθæləmoʊˈstraɪ.eɪt veɪn / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɪəriə ˌθæləməʊˈstraɪ.eɪt veɪn / (BrE)
a vein in the brain that acts as a tributary to the basal vein
a brain vein, tributary to the basal vein
medical

Examples

The inferior thalamostriate vein drains blood from parts of the thalamus and striatum.
During neuroanatomy class, we examined the inferior thalamostriate vein and its connection to the basal vein.
Damage to the inferior thalamostriate vein can sometimes complicate brain surgery.

Etymology

From Latin: 'thalamus' (chamber, referring to a part of the brain) + 'striate' (striped), indicating location and appearance; 'vein' from Latin 'vena'.
