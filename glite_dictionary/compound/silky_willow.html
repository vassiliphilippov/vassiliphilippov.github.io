silky willow

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪlki ˈwɪloʊ / (AmE), / ˈsɪlki ˈwɪləʊ / (BrE)
a North American willow tree (Salix sericea) with greyish, silky, hairy leaves that often turn black when dried
North American willow with silky leaves

Examples

The silky willow thrives in wetlands along streams and rivers.
Botanists collected leaves from a silky willow for further study.
Many birds nest in the branches of the silky willow each spring.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɪlki ˈwɪloʊ / (AmE), / ˈsɪlki ˈwɪləʊ / (BrE)
a small shrubby tree native to western North America, particularly from Alaska to Oregon, known for its silky leaves
shrubby North American willow tree

Examples

The silky willow thrives along riverbanks from Alaska to Oregon.
Naturalists spotted a rare silky willow in the wetland preserve.
The leaves of the silky willow have a distinctive silky texture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: willow - a tree or shrub of the genus Salix, typically with narrow leaves and flexible branches
