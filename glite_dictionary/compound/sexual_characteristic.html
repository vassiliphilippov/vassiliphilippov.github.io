sexual characteristic

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛkʃuəl ˌkæɹəktəˈɪstɪk / (AmE), / ˈsɛkʃuəl ˌkærɪktəˈɪstɪk / (BrE)
a physical or psychological trait that is strongly associated with one sex compared to the other, such as body structure or certain behaviors
trait linked to one sex; male or female quality

Examples

Development of sexual characteristics often occurs during puberty.
Certain sexual characteristics distinguish male birds from females.
Doctors assess sexual characteristics to help understand genetic or hormonal conditions.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: secondary sexual characteristic - a trait that appears at puberty and distinguishes the sexes, but is not directly involved in reproduction
