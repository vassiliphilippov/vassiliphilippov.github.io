jumping mouse

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒʌmpɪŋ maʊs / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌmpɪŋ maʊs / (BrE)
a small rodent with long hind legs and no cheek pouches, found in woodlands of Eurasia and North America, known for its jumping ability
small woodland rodent with long hind legs

Examples

The jumping mouse escaped quickly by leaping through the grass.
Scientists study the hibernation patterns of the jumping mouse every winter.
A jumping mouse can cover surprisingly long distances with its powerful jumps.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rodent - a small mammal characterized by continuously growing incisors
