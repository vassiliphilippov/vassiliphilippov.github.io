to check your pulse

1. VERB

/ tʃɛk jʊr pʌls / (AmE), / tʃɛk jɔː pʌls / (BrE)
to measure the rate at which your heart is beating by feeling your pulse, typically by placing fingers on the wrist or neck
measure your heart rate by feeling your pulse

Examples

It's a good idea to check your pulse after exercising to monitor your heart rate.
If you feel dizzy, you should check your pulse to make sure it's regular.
Doctors often check your pulse during a physical examination.

Synset Examples

Checking your pulse is important during exercise.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: take your pulse - to measure the heart rate by feeling the pulse
