First of October Anti-Fascist Resistance Groups

1. PROPN

/ ˈfɜrst əv ɑkˈtoʊbɚ ˈænti ˈfæʃɪst rɪˈzɪstəns ˈɡruːps / (AmE), / ˈfɜːst əv ɒkˈtəʊbə ˈænti ˈfæʃɪst rɪˈzɪstəns ˈɡruːps / (BrE)
a former Spanish armed revolutionary group, active mainly in the late 20th century, which sought to overthrow the Spanish government and establish a Marxist-Leninist regime
Spanish Marxist-Leninist armed group (GRAPO)

Examples

The police arrested several members of the First of October Anti-Fascist Resistance Groups in a large operation in Madrid.
The First of October Anti-Fascist Resistance Groups were responsible for a series of high-profile actions in the late 1970s.
Historians continue to debate the long-term impact of the First of October Anti-Fascist Resistance Groups on Spanish politics.

Synset Examples

GRAPO is vehemently opposed to the United States

Etymology

From Spanish 'Grupos de Resistencia Antifascista Primero de Octubre' (GRAPO), referring to the date of their first major action.
