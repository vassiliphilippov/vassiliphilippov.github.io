sensory hair

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛn.sɚ.i heɚ / (AmE), / ˈsɛn.sə.ri heə / (BrE)
a long, stiff hair found on the snout or brow of most mammals, used for sensing the environment
long, stiff, sensory hair of mammals

Examples

Cats use their sensory hairs to judge whether they can fit through tight spaces.
The scientist studied the sensory hairs on a mouse's snout to learn how they detect movement.
Many mammals rely on sensory hair to navigate their environment in the dark.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whisker - a long projecting hair or bristle growing from the face of some animals
