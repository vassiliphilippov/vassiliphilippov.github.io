coal oil

1. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊl ɔɪl / (AmE), / ˈkəʊl ɔɪl / (BrE)
a flammable oil distilled from coal and used as fuel, especially in lamps and heaters
flammable oil from coal, used as fuel

Examples

Before electricity, people used coal oil lamps to light their homes.
The smell of coal oil lingered in the old cabin after lighting the heater.
She poured coal oil into the lamp and struck a match to ignite it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kerosene - a light fuel oil obtained by distilling petroleum, used especially in jet engines and lamps

Etymology

From 'coal' + 'oil', referring to oil derived from the distillation of coal.
