Jersey elm

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɜrzi ɛlm / (AmE), / ˈdʒɜːzi ɛlm / (BrE)
a cultivated variety of English elm with upright branches and broader leaves, distinguished among elm species
a type of English elm tree

Examples

The leaves of the Jersey elm are noticeably broader than those of other elms.
Many old parks in the region feature stately Jersey elm trees lining the avenues.
The Jersey elm is prized for its resistance to certain diseases affecting other elm varieties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: English elm - a species of elm tree native to Europe, especially England
