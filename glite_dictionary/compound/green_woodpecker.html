green woodpecker

1. NOUN

/ ɡrin ˈwʊdˌpɛkər / (AmE), / ɡriːn ˈwʊdˌpɛkə / (BrE)
a woodpecker native to Europe and western Asia, noted for its greenish plumage and distinctive call
European and western Asian woodpecker species

Examples

The green woodpecker can often be seen feeding on ants in open grassy areas.
We heard the distinctive laughing call of a green woodpecker during our walk in the woods.
Unlike other species, the green woodpecker rarely drums on trees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: woodpecker - a bird with a strong bill that pecks at wood to find insects
