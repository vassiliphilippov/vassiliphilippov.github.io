canine distemper

1. NOUN

/ ˈkeɪnaɪn dɪˈstɛmpər / (AmE), / ˈkeɪnaɪn dɪˈstɛmpə / (BrE)
a contagious viral disease affecting dogs, especially puppies, characterized by high fever, respiratory inflammation, and often neurological symptoms
infectious viral disease of dogs
medical

Examples

The veterinarian diagnosed the puppy with canine distemper after noticing its persistent cough and high fever.
Vaccinating your dog is the best way to prevent canine distemper.
Shelters often screen new arrivals for canine distemper to prevent outbreaks.

Etymology

From Latin 'caninus' (dog) and Old French 'destemprer' (to disturb).
