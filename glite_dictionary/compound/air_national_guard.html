Air National Guard

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛr ˌnæʃənəl ˈɡɑrd / (AmE), / ˌeə ˌnæʃənəl ˈɡɑːd / (BrE)
a reserve military force composed of civilian volunteers who support the United States Air Force, providing rapid mobilization during war and assistance during national emergencies
U.S. Air Force reserve component
american

Examples

The Air National Guard was called upon to assist after the hurricane devastated the region.
Many pilots in the Air National Guard also have civilian careers outside of their military service.
During wartime, the Air National Guard can be mobilized to support active-duty Air Force missions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: National Guard - a reserve military force; the collective of Army and Air National Guard units in the U.S.
