libertopian manifesto

1. NOUN

/ lɪbərˈtoʊpiən mænəˈfəstoʊ / (AmE), / lɪbəˈtəʊpiən mænɪˈfɛstəʊ / (BrE)
a political document that promotes or outlines the core beliefs and principles of libertarianism, especially as envisioned by the 'Libertopia' movement or ideology
document promoting libertarian principles

Examples

The libertopian manifesto inspired many young activists.
Reading the libertopian manifesto gave her a new perspective on personal freedom and government.
Several organizations referenced the libertopian manifesto in their campaigns for policy reform.

Synset Examples

The libertopian manifesto inspired many young activists.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: manifesto - a public declaration of policy and aims
  • Synonym: libertarianism - a political philosophy advocating minimal state intervention

Etymology

Compound of 'Libertopia' (referring to an ideal libertarian society) and 'manifesto' (from Italian, 'manifesto', meaning a public declaration).
