genus Apus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈeɪ.pəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈeɪ.pʊs / (BrE)
A genus of birds comprising the swifts, which are characterized by their slender bodies, long wings, and fast flight.
genus of birds including swifts

Examples

The genus Apus is well known for the aerial abilities of its members.
Scientists identified the fossil as belonging to the genus Apus after extensive analysis.
Birdwatchers are often fascinated by the migratory patterns of the genus Apus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swift - A small fast-flying bird in the Apodidae family

Etymology

From Latin 'apus', meaning swift (bird), originally from Greek 'apous', meaning 'without feet'.
