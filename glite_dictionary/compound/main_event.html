main event

1. NOUN

/ meɪn ɪˈvɛnt / (AmE), / meɪn ɪˈvɛnt / (BrE)
the most important or central event in a series of events, especially in entertainment, sports, or public gatherings
most important or central event

Examples

The crowd eagerly awaited the main event of the wrestling match.
After several opening acts, the band finally took the stage for the main event.
Tickets for the main event sold out in just a few hours.

Synset Examples

The main event of the evening was a surprise concert.
The main event drew a large crowd of enthusiastic fans.
The main event was great, but the sideshow was unforgettable.
They saved the main event for last to keep everyone engaged.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: highlight - the most outstanding part or event
  • Antonym: side event - a less important or secondary event
