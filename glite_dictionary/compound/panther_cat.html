panther cat

1. NOUN

/ ˈpænθɚ kæt / (AmE), / ˈpænθə kat / (BrE)
A nocturnal wildcat of Central America and South America with a dark-spotted buff-brown coat.
spotted wildcat of Central/South America

Examples

The panther cat is well known for its agility and stealth in the dense rainforests.
Researchers have tracked several panther cats to study their nocturnal hunting habits.
The unique coat pattern of the panther cat helps it blend into the forest underbrush.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ocelot - a medium-sized spotted wildcat native to Central and South America
