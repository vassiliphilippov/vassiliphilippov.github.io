kauri gum

1. NOUN

/ ˈkaʊri ɡʌm / (AmE), / ˈkɑːri ɡʌm / (BrE)
a fossilized resin from kauri trees of New Zealand, used especially in making varnishes and linoleum
fossil resin from New Zealand kauri trees

Examples

Early settlers in New Zealand collected kauri gum from the forests for export to Europe.
The manufacture of high-quality varnish often relied on kauri gum as a primary ingredient.
We visited a museum that displayed ancient pieces of kauri gum found buried in the ground.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amber - fossilized tree resin used as a gemstone

Etymology

From Māori 'kauri' (the tree species) and English 'gum' (resin).
