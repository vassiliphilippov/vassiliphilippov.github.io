plea bargain

1. NOUN

/ pli ˈbɑrɡən / (AmE), / pliː ˈbɑːɡən / (BrE)
an agreement in a criminal case where the defendant pleads guilty to a lesser charge in exchange for a lighter sentence or the dismissal of other charges
agreement to plead guilty to a lesser charge
legal

Examples

After lengthy discussions, he accepted a plea bargain from the prosecutors.
The defendant's lawyer recommended considering a plea bargain to avoid a lengthy trial.
Many criminal cases are resolved by reaching a plea bargain rather than going to court.

Synset Examples

his admission was part of a plea bargain with the prosecutor
plea bargaining helps to stop the courts becoming congested

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plea bargaining - the process of negotiating in a criminal case for a reduced charge or sentence
  • Antonym: trial - a formal examination of evidence in court
