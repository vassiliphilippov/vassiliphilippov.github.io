tonic key

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɑːnɪk kiː / (AmE), / ˈtɒnɪk kiː / (BrE)
the principal key in which a piece of music is composed, serving as its tonal center.
main key of a musical composition

Examples

The symphony was written in the key of G major, making G its tonic key.
During the sonata, the music repeatedly returns to the tonic key after exploring others.
Musicians must first determine the tonic key to understand the structure of the composition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: key - the system of notes based on a particular tonic in music
