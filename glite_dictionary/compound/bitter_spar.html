bitter spar

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɪt̬ɚ ˌspɑr / (AmE), / ˈbɪtə ˌspɑː / (BrE)
A light-colored mineral composed of calcium magnesium carbonate, used as a source of magnesium, in ceramics, and as fertilizer.
calcium magnesium carbonate mineral

Examples

Geologists identified the pale rock as bitter spar during the field survey.
The factory imports bitter spar to use as a source of magnesium.
Farmers sometimes add bitter spar to soil to improve its nutrient content.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dolomite - A mineral composed of calcium magnesium carbonate; bitter spar is another name for this mineral.

Etymology

From 'bitter' (referring to taste) and 'spar' (a term for non-metallic minerals easy to cleave).
