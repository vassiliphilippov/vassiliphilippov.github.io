fire-bush

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪɚ ˌbʊʃ / (AmE), / ˈfaɪə ˌbʊʃ / (BrE)
A densely branched Eurasian shrub whose foliage turns purple-red in autumn.
shrub with autumn red foliage

Examples

The fire-bush in their garden turned a deep red as autumn arrived.
Gardeners often plant fire-bush for its vibrant foliage in the fall.
A mature fire-bush can provide striking color in landscaping projects.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪərˌbʊʃ / (AmE), / ˈfaɪəˌbʊʃ / (BrE)
an evergreen South American shrub with showy, trumpet-shaped orange flowers, often grown as an ornamental or houseplant
ornamental South American shrub with orange flowers

Examples

The fire-bush added vibrant color to their tropical garden.
She keeps a fire-bush in her sunlit living room as a houseplant.
Gardeners prize the fire-bush for its striking orange blossoms and evergreen foliage.
