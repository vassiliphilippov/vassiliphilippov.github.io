icing the puck

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪ.sɪŋ ðə pʌk / (AmE), / ˈaɪ.sɪŋ ðə pʌk / (BrE)
In ice hockey, the act of shooting the puck from within your own defensive zone across both the center red line and the opposing team's goal line, without the puck being touched, resulting in a stoppage of play.
ice hockey: shooting puck across rink illegally

Examples

The team was penalized for icing the puck in the final minutes of the period.
After icing the puck, the defensive players were not allowed to make a line change.
The coach warned the players to avoid icing the puck under pressure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: icing - shooting the puck across the ice to the opponent’s goal line without being touched (ice hockey rule)
