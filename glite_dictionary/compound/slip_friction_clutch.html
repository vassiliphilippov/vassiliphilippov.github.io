slip friction clutch

1. NOUN

/ slɪp ˈfrɪkʃən klʌtʃ / (AmE), / slɪp ˈfrɪkʃən klʌtʃ / (BrE)
a friction clutch designed to allow slipping when the applied torque exceeds a set limit, preventing damage to machinery
type of friction clutch that allows slipping

Examples

The slip friction clutch prevents engine damage by disengaging under excessive load.
Many machines use a slip friction clutch to protect gears from sudden shocks.
If the slip friction clutch wears out, it may not function properly and allow too much slippage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: friction clutch - a clutch using friction to transmit power between surfaces
