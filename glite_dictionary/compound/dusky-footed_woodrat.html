dusky-footed woodrat

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʌski ˈfʊtɪd ˈwʊdˌræt / (AmE), / ˈdʌski ˈfʊtɪd ˈwʊdˌræt / (BrE)
a large, nocturnal rodent (Neotoma fuscipes) native to western North America, characterized by grayish-brown fur and pale feet
a rodent species native to western North America

Examples

The dusky-footed woodrat collects leaves and twigs to build its distinctive stick nests.
Researchers have studied the dusky-footed woodrat to better understand its role in the forest ecosystem.
Tick populations in some regions are influenced by the presence of the dusky-footed woodrat.
