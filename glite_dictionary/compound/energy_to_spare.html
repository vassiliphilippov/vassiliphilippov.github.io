energy to spare

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛnɚdʒi tə spɛr / (AmE), / ˈɛnədʒi tə speə / (BrE)
more energy than is needed for a particular activity; excess energy available
more energy than needed

Examples

After running the marathon, she somehow still had energy to spare.
The children played outside for hours and returned home with energy to spare.
He finished his shift with energy to spare, eager to start his evening activities.

Synset Examples

He had energy to spare after the workout.
