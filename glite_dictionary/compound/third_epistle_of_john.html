Third Epistle of John

1. PROPN

/ θɜrd ɪˈpɪsəl ʌv dʒɑn / (AmE), / θɜːd ɪˈpɪsl ɒv dʒɒn / (BrE)
the third letter in the New Testament traditionally attributed to Saint John the Apostle, addressed to Gaius and concerning church matters
a New Testament epistle by Saint John

Examples

The Third Epistle of John addresses issues of hospitality among early Christians.
Many scholars study the Third Epistle of John for its insight into early church leadership.
She found a passage in the Third Epistle of John that resonated with her values.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Second Epistle of John - the second New Testament letter attributed to Saint John
  • Synonym: First Epistle of John - the first New Testament letter attributed to Saint John

Etymology

The title derives from Greek 'Epistolē Iōannou' (Epistle of John).

2. PROPN

/ θɜrd ɪˈpɪsəl əv dʒɑn / (AmE), / θɜːd ɪˈpɪsl əv dʒɒn / (BrE)
the third letter in the New Testament traditionally attributed to Saint John the Apostle; a short epistle addressed to Gaius
third letter of John in the New Testament

Examples

The Third Epistle of John is addressed to a man named Gaius, praising his hospitality.
Many scholars debate the authorship of the Third Epistle of John and its similarities to other Johannine letters.
During Bible study, we discussed lessons from the Third Epistle of John.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Second Epistle of John - the second letter attributed to Saint John
  • Synonym: First Epistle of John - the first letter attributed to Saint John

Etymology

Epistle from Latin 'epistula' (letter), John from Hebrew 'Yochanan' (God is gracious).
