magnesium sulfate

1. NOUN

/ mæɡˌnizɪəm ˈsʌlfeɪt / (AmE), / mæɡˌniːziəm ˈsʌlfeɪt / (BrE)
a white, crystalline salt used in medicine and industry, especially as a laxative and in bath salts
a salt used in medicine and industry

Examples

The doctor prescribed magnesium sulfate to help relieve her muscle cramps.
They dissolved magnesium sulfate in water to create a soothing bath.
Farmers sometimes use magnesium sulfate as a fertilizer to improve soil quality.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Epsom salt - common name for magnesium sulfate, especially in its heptahydrate form

Etymology

From magnesium (element name, from Magnesia, a region in Greece) and sulfate (from Latin 'sulfas', sulfatis).
