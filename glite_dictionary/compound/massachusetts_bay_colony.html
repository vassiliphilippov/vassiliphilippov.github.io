Massachusetts Bay Colony

1. PROPN

/ ˌmæsətʃuːsɪts beɪ ˈkɑːləni / (AmE), / ˌmæsətʃuːsɪts beɪ ˈkɒləni / (BrE)
a British colonial settlement in North America, established in the early 17th century and centered in present-day Massachusetts, with capitals including Salem, Charlestown, and Boston
historical British colony in Massachusetts

Examples

The Massachusetts Bay Colony was founded by English Puritans seeking religious freedom in the early 1600s.
Boston quickly became the most important city in the Massachusetts Bay Colony.
Many early American traditions can be traced back to the settlers of the Massachusetts Bay Colony.

Etymology

Named after the Massachusett people and Massachusetts Bay; from Latin 'colonia' (settlement).
