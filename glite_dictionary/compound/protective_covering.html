protective covering

1. NOUN

/ prəˈtɛktɪv ˈkʌvərɪŋ / (AmE), / prəˈtɛktɪv ˈkʌvərɪŋ / (BrE)
A covering used to shield something from damage or injury.
a covering that provides protection

Examples

The workers wore a protective covering over their uniforms to prevent contamination.
Always use a protective covering when handling chemicals in the laboratory.
The table was shielded with a protective covering to avoid scratches during the move.

Synset Examples

they had no protection from the fallout
wax provided protection for the floors

Related Terms

  • Synonym: covering - something that covers or overlays another thing
  • Synonym: protection - the act of protecting or state of being protected

2. NOUN

/ prəˈtɛktɪv ˈkʌvərɪŋ / (AmE), / prəˈtɛktɪv ˈkʌvərɪŋ / (BrE)
the tough natural outer layer that shields or protects an organism from injury, infection, or adverse conditions
tough natural outer layer of an organism

Examples

The turtle's protective covering shields it from predators and harsh environments.
Cacti have a thick protective covering to reduce water loss in desert climates.
The beetle's shell acts as a protective covering against damage and dehydration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shell - the hard outer covering of some animals, such as mollusks or eggs
  • Synonym: skin - the outer protective layer of a living organism, especially a vertebrate
  • Synonym: membrane - a thin, flexible layer acting as a boundary, lining, or protection in organisms
