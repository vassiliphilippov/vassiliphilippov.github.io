dynamic microphone

1. NOUN

/ daɪˈnæmɪk ˈmaɪkroʊˌfoʊn / (AmE), / daɪˈnæmɪk ˈmaɪkrəˌfəʊn / (BrE)
A type of microphone that converts sound into an electrical signal using electromagnetic induction, commonly used in live sound and recording due to its durability and reliability.
microphone using electromagnetic induction

Examples

He prefers using a dynamic microphone on stage because it's less sensitive to background noise.
The studio engineer set up a dynamic microphone to record the band's vocals.
A dynamic microphone is ideal for capturing loud instruments like drums and guitar amplifiers.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: condenser microphone - a microphone using a capacitor to convert sound into an electrical signal

Etymology

From 'dynamic', meaning relating to force or power, and 'microphone', from Greek 'mikros' (small) and 'phōnē' (sound).
