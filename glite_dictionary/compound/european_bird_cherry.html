European bird cherry

1. NOUN

/ jʊˈroʊpiən bɝd ˈtʃɛri / (AmE), / jʊəˈrəʊpiən bɜːd ˈtʃɛri / (BrE)
a small European cherry tree (Prunus padus) closely resembling the American chokecherry, known for its clusters of white flowers and small black fruit
a European cherry tree, Prunus padus

Examples

The European bird cherry produces fragrant white blooms in late spring.
Gardeners sometimes plant the European bird cherry for its decorative flowers and fruit.
The bark of the European bird cherry was traditionally used in herbal medicine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chokecherry - a North American cherry tree with astringent fruit
