genus Bison

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈbaɪ.sən / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈbɪ.sən / (BrE)
A genus of large, hairy, hump-shouldered bovine mammals, including the American and European bison, sometimes considered a subgenus of genus Bos.
biological genus of bison species

Examples

The genus Bison includes both the American bison and the European bison.
Scientists debated whether to classify the American buffalo as part of the genus Bison or as a member of Bos.
Research into the genus Bison has provided new insights into the evolution of large grazing mammals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bison - a large, shaggy-haired wild ox native to North America and Europe
  • Degree Higher: genus Bos - the genus including domestic cattle and their close relatives

Etymology

From Latin 'bison', meaning wild ox, used in zoological taxonomy.
