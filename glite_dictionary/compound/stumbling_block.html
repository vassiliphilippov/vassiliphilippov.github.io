stumbling block

1. NOUN

/ ˈstʌmblɪŋ ˌblɑk / (AmE), / ˈstʌmblɪŋ ˌblɒk / (BrE)
a circumstance, problem, or factor that prevents progress or makes things more difficult
obstacle; hindrance to progress

Examples

Lack of funding has been a major stumbling block for the project.
Language differences can serve as a stumbling block in international business.
The committee identified several stumbling blocks to reaching an agreement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obstacle - something that blocks progress
  • Synonym: impediment - something that hinders progress

2. NOUN

/ ˈstʌmblɪŋ ˌblɑk / (AmE), / ˈstʌmblɪŋ ˌblɒk / (BrE)
A difficulty or obstacle that prevents progress or makes success difficult.
obstacle or hindrance to progress

Examples

The lack of experience proved to be a serious stumbling block for the young team.
Regulatory approval has become a stumbling block for the drug's release.
Financial difficulties were the main stumbling block to launching their new business.

Synset Examples

Lack of funding was a major stumbling block for the project.
The language barrier was a stumbling block during negotiations.
Her fear of public speaking became a stumbling block in her career.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obstacle - something that blocks one's way or hinders progress
  • Synonym: hindrance - a thing that provides resistance, delay, or obstruction

Etymology

From the literal image of something in one's path that causes one to stumble; attested in English since the 14th century.
