past-tense morpheme

1. NOUN

/ ˈpæst ˌtɛns ˈmɔːrfiːm / (AmE), / ˈpɑːst ˌtɛns ˈmɔːfiːm / (BrE)
A morpheme that indicates that a verb is in the past tense.
morpheme marking past tense

Examples

In English, the '-ed' ending in 'walked' is a past-tense morpheme.
Some languages use an internal vowel change as a past-tense morpheme.
The word 'talked' contains the past-tense morpheme '-ed'.

Synset Examples

In English, The most popular past tense morpheme is indicated by the suffix ‘-ed’ added to regular verbs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: morpheme - smallest meaningful unit in a language
  • Degree Lower: suffix - an affix added at the end of a word

Etymology

Formed from 'past tense' (referring to the grammatical tense) and 'morpheme' (from French morphème, from Greek morphē 'form').
