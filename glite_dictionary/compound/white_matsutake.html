white matsutake

1. NOUN

/ waɪt ˌmɑːtsuːˈtɑːkeɪ / (AmE), / waɪt ˌmætsuːˈtæki / (BrE)
a large white edible mushroom with white gills and a spicy, aromatic odor, which develops brown stains as it ages; collected commercially for Asian cooking, especially in the Pacific Northwest
a large white edible mushroom used in Asian cuisine

Examples

Chefs prize the white matsutake for its unique aroma and flavor.
Harvesters in Oregon collect white matsutake mushrooms for export to Asian markets.
The stir-fry included sliced white matsutake as the star ingredient.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: matsutake - an aromatic edible mushroom prized in Japanese cuisine

Etymology

Japanese 'matsutake' (松茸), meaning 'pine mushroom,' adopted into English for the species Tricholoma magnivelare.
