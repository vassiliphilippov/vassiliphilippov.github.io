Great Attractor

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡreɪt əˈtræktər / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪt əˈtræktə / (BrE)
a massive concentration of galaxies in the direction of Centaurus and Hydra, whose gravitational pull is believed to influence the movements of other galaxies
astronomical region exerting strong gravitational pull

Examples

Scientists continue to study the Great Attractor to understand its effect on the motion of local galaxies.
The Milky Way is one of many galaxies being drawn toward the mysterious Great Attractor.
Astronomers first inferred the existence of the Great Attractor by observing peculiar velocities in surrounding galaxies.
