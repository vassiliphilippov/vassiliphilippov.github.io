cinchona bark

1. NOUN

/ sɪnˈtʃoʊnə bɑrk / (AmE), / sɪnˈtʃəʊnə bɑːk / (BrE)
the dried bark of cinchona trees, used in medicine as a source of quinine and quinidine
medicinal bark of cinchona tree

Examples

Extracts from cinchona bark were once the primary treatment for malaria.
The pharmacist showed him a jar containing dried cinchona bark.
Historically, cinchona bark played a crucial role in combating tropical diseases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quinine - a bitter medicinal compound extracted from cinchona bark, used to treat malaria

Etymology

From Spanish *cinchona*, named after the Countess of Chinchón; 'bark' from Old English *beorc*.
