hare wallaby

1. NOUN

/ hɛr ˈwæl.ə.bi / (AmE), / heə ˈwæl.ə.bi / (BrE)
A small Australian wallaby that resembles a hare in appearance and has persistent teeth, belonging to certain genera such as Lagorchestes.
small Australian wallaby resembling a hare

Examples

The hare wallaby is known for its speed and agility in the Australian outback.
Researchers studied the habitat preferences of the endangered hare wallaby.
Unlike other wallabies, the hare wallaby has long ears and a slender build.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wallaby - a small or medium-sized kangaroo, especially any of the smaller species native to Australia and New Guinea
