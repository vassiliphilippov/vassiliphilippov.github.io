Bertillon system

1. NOUN

/ ˌbɜːrtɪˈjɑːn ˈsɪstəm / (AmE), / ˌbɛːtɪˈjɒ̃ ˈsɪstəm / (BrE)
a system for identifying individuals based on detailed physical measurements, photographs, and records of distinguishing features, developed by Alphonse Bertillon in the late 19th century
system of identification using physical measurements

Examples

The police department adopted the Bertillon system to improve their criminal identification procedures.
Before fingerprinting became standard, many countries relied on the Bertillon system to catalog suspected criminals.
Alphonse Bertillon's Bertillon system revolutionized forensic science in the late 1800s.

Etymology

Named after French criminologist Alphonse Bertillon (1853–1914), who developed the system.
