generally accepted accounting principles

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒɛnərəli əkˈsɛptɪd əˈkaʊntɪŋ ˈprɪnsəpəlz / (AmE), / ˌdʒɛnərəli əkˈsɛptɪd əˈkaʊntɪŋ ˈprɪnsɪplz / (BrE)
a set of rules, standards, and procedures that define accepted accounting practices and guide the preparation of financial statements
rules and standards for accounting practice

Examples

All publicly traded companies in the US must follow generally accepted accounting principles when preparing their financial reports.
The new accountant ensured that every transaction was recorded in accordance with generally accepted accounting principles.
Understanding generally accepted accounting principles is essential for anyone working in corporate finance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: GAAP - abbreviation for generally accepted accounting principles
