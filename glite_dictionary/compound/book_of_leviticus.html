Book of Leviticus

1. PROPN

/ bʊk əv ləˈvɪtɪkəs / (AmE), / bʊk əv ləˈvɪtɪkəs / (BrE)
the third book of the Old Testament, outlining Levitical law and ritual instructions for priests and the Israelites
the third book of the Old Testament

Examples

Many ancient rituals described in the Book of Leviticus are still studied by scholars today.
She quoted a passage from the Book of Leviticus during the religious discussion.
The Book of Leviticus outlines dietary laws and priestly duties for the Israelites.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Pentateuch - the first five books of the Old Testament
  • Degree Lower: Book of Genesis - the first book of the Old Testament
  • Degree Lower: Book of Exodus - the second book of the Old Testament
  • Degree Higher: Book of Numbers - the fourth book of the Old Testament
  • Degree Higher: Book of Deuteronomy - the fifth book of the Old Testament

Etymology

From Latin 'Leviticus', from Greek 'Leuitikon' (relating to the Levites), referring to the priestly tribe of Israel.
