high alert

1. NOUN

/ haɪ əˈlɝt / (AmE), / haɪ əˈlɜːt / (BrE)
a state of heightened readiness or vigilance, especially in response to a perceived threat or emergency
state of heightened vigilance

Examples

The security team remained on high alert throughout the night after the alarm was triggered.
Hospitals were placed on high alert during the flu outbreak to manage patient flow.
As the storm approached, emergency services were put on high alert.

Synset Examples

His weirdar was on high alert during the strange meeting.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: red alert - the highest state of alert, often military or security related
  • Degree Lower: alert - a state of being watchful and ready to respond
