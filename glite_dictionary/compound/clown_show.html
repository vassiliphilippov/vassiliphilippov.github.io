clown show

1. NOUN

/ klaʊn ʃoʊ / (AmE), / klaʊn ʃəʊ / (BrE)
a situation that is chaotic, disorganized, or farcical, often due to poor management or execution
chaotic or farcical situation
informal

Examples

After the changes in management, the entire department turned into a clown show.
The press conference was such a clown show that reporters struggled to take it seriously.
Don't invite them—they turned last year's event into a clown show.

Synset Examples

The meeting felt like a clown show with everyone talking over each other.
The debate turned into a complete clown show last night.
His presentation was such a clown show that no one took him seriously.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fiasco - a complete and ignominious failure
  • Synonym: debacle - a sudden and ignominious failure; a fiasco
  • Antonym: success - the accomplishment of an aim or purpose
