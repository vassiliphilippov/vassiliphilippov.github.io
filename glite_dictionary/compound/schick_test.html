Schick test

1. NOUN

/ ʃɪk tɛst / (AmE), / ʃɪk tɛst / (BrE)
a skin test used to determine an individual's immunity to diphtheria by injecting diphtheria toxin intradermally and observing the reaction
skin test for diphtheria immunity
medical

Examples

The doctor administered a Schick test to determine if the child was immune to diphtheria.
After the Schick test, it was clear he needed a vaccine booster.
During the early 20th century, the Schick test was widely used in public health campaigns.

Etymology

Named after Béla Schick (1877–1967), the Austrian-American pediatrician who developed the test.
