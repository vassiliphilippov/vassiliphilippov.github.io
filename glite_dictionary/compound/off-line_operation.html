off-line operation

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔfˌlaɪn ˌɑpəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈɒfˌlaɪn ˌɒpəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
an operation performed by equipment that is not controlled by the central processing unit and is not directly connected to the main computer system
operation outside central processing unit control

Examples

The backup process is often an off-line operation, performed when the computer is not in use.
Data conversion was carried out as an off-line operation before being uploaded to the mainframe.
Some maintenance tasks require an off-line operation to avoid disrupting system performance.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: on-line operation - operation performed under control of the central processing unit

Etymology

From 'off-line' (not connected to a central system) + 'operation'.
