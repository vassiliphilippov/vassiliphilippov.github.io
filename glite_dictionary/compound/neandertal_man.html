Neandertal man

1. NOUN

/ niˈæn.dərˌtæl mæn / (AmE), / niˈɑːn.dəˌtɑːl mæn / (BrE)
an extinct species of robust humans (Homo neanderthalensis) who lived in Europe and western Asia during the Middle Paleolithic period
extinct species of early human

Examples

The discovery of the Neandertal man provided key insights into human evolution.
Scientists believe that Neandertal man coexisted with early modern humans for thousands of years.
Fossilized remains of Neandertal man have been found in several caves across Europe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Neanderthal - an extinct species or subspecies of archaic humans
  • Antonym: Homo sapiens - the modern species of human beings

Etymology

Named after the Neander Valley (Neandertal) in Germany, where remains were first discovered in 1856; from German 'Neanderthal'.
