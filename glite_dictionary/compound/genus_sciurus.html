genus Sciurus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs saɪˈjʊərəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs sɪˈjʊərəs / (BrE)
the principal genus of the family Sciuridae, including the typical moderate-sized arboreal squirrels found in many parts of the world
genus of typical tree squirrels

Examples

The eastern gray squirrel belongs to the genus Sciurus and is common in North America.
Scientists conducted a study on various species within the genus Sciurus to understand their behavior.
Members of the genus Sciurus are well-adapted to living in trees and are found worldwide.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: squirrel - a small rodent with a long bushy tail, family Sciuridae

Etymology

From Latin *Sciurus*, from Ancient Greek *skiouros* (squirrel); 'genus' from Latin (kind, sort).
