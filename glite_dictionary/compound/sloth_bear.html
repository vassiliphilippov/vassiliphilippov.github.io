sloth bear

1. NOUN

/ sloʊθ bɛr / (AmE), / sləʊθ beə / (BrE)
a coarse-haired, long-snouted bear native to south-central Asia, known for its shaggy fur and insect diet
a bear species from south-central Asia

Examples

The sloth bear feeds primarily on termites and ants in its native forests.
Unlike other bears, the sloth bear has a long, shaggy coat and a distinctive snout.
Zoologists observed a sloth bear digging through a termite mound in the wild.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bear - large, heavy mammal with thick fur and a short tail
