banana oil

1. NOUN

/ bəˈnænə ɔɪl / (AmE), / bəˈnænə ɔɪl / (BrE)
A colorless liquid ester with a strong odor of bananas, commonly used as a solvent and in artificial flavoring.
liquid ester with banana odor

Examples

Laboratory workers often use banana oil as a solvent due to its effectiveness and pleasant smell.
The flavor of artificial banana candy comes from a compound called banana oil.
When the technician uncapped the jar, the scent of banana oil quickly filled the room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: isoamyl acetate - An ester of isoamyl alcohol and acetic acid, also known as banana oil

Etymology

Named for its banana-like scent; the common name for isoamyl acetate, derived from amyl alcohol.
