yellow honeysuckle

1. NOUN

/ ˈjɛl.oʊ ˈhʌn.iˌsʌk.əl / (AmE), / ˈjɛl.əʊ ˈhʌn.iˌsʌk.əl / (BrE)
A climbing deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States, with fragrant yellow flowers that later turn orange, borne in terminal whorls.
a climbing shrub with yellow-orange fragrant flowers

Examples

The yellow honeysuckle climbed up the garden fence, spreading its sweet scent in early summer.
We admired the clusters of fragrant flowers on the yellow honeysuckle during our hike in Georgia.
Many gardeners choose yellow honeysuckle for its vibrant color and ability to attract hummingbirds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: honeysuckle - any of various shrubs or vines with fragrant tubular flowers

2. NOUN

/ ˈjɛloʊ ˈhʌniˌsʌkəl / (AmE), / ˈjɛləʊ ˈhʌniˌsʌkl̩ / (BrE)
A twining deciduous shrub native to northeastern America, with clusters of purple-tinged yellow-green flowers.
a North American shrub with yellowish flowers

Examples

The yellow honeysuckle produces delicate clusters of flowers in early summer.
Gardeners appreciate the fragrant blooms of the yellow honeysuckle along fences and trellises.
Native birds are attracted to the nectar from the yellow honeysuckle's yellow-green blossoms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: honeysuckle - a group of climbing shrubs with sweet-smelling flowers
