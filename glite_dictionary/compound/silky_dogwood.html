silky dogwood

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪlki ˈdɔɡˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈsɪlki ˈdɒɡˌwʊd / (BrE)
a deciduous shrub of eastern North America with purplish stems and blue fruit
shrub with purplish stems and blue fruit

Examples

The silky dogwood attracts many bird species with its clusters of blue berries.
We planted a silky dogwood along the stream to help control erosion.
In the autumn, the purplish stems of the silky dogwood become especially striking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dogwood - any shrub or tree of the genus Cornus

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɪlki ˈdɔɡˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈsɪlki ˈdɒɡˌwʊd / (BrE)
a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America, with silky-haired stems and leaves, often found near water
a North American shrub with silky stems and leaves

Examples

The wetlands were dotted with clusters of silky dogwood in full bloom.
Gardeners value silky dogwood for its attractive, silvery foliage and white flowers.
Wild birds often nest in the dense branches of silky dogwood shrubs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dogwood - a type of shrub or small tree in the genus Cornus
