potato tree

1. NOUN

/ pəˈteɪtoʊ triː / (AmE), / pəˈteɪtəʊ triː / (BrE)
a hardy climbing shrub of Chile grown as an ornamental for its fragrant flowers; not a true potato
Chilean ornamental climbing shrub

Examples

The potato tree is valued for its beautiful and fragrant purple flowers.
Despite its name, the potato tree is not related to the edible potato.
Gardeners in mild climates often plant the potato tree as an ornamental vine.

2. NOUN

/ pəˈteɪtoʊ ˌtriː / (AmE), / pəˈteɪtəʊ ˌtriː / (BrE)
A South American shrub or small tree (Solanum erianthum or Solanum macranthum) widely grown in tropical regions, not related to the edible potato.
South American shrub or tree, not a true potato

Examples

The garden was dominated by a tall potato tree covered in purple flowers.
Many tropical landscapes feature the potato tree for its ornamental value.
Despite its name, the potato tree is not related to the common vegetable.
