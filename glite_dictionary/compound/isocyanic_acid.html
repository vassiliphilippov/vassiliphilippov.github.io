isocyanic acid

1. NOUN

/ ˌaɪsoʊkaɪˌænɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / ˌaɪsəʊkaɪˌanɪk ˈasɪd / (BrE)
a chemical compound, HNCO, known only in the form of its esters; a weak and unstable acid in the cyanate family
an unstable chemical acid, HNCO

Examples

Isocyanic acid is rarely found in pure form due to its instability and tendency to polymerize.
Chemists often study isocyanic acid through its esters rather than its isolated form.
The reaction involves the formation of isocyanic acid as an intermediate product.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cyanic acid - a related acid, HOCN, also known in chemical compounds

Etymology

From iso- (meaning equal) + 'cyanic' (relating to cyanide compounds or the cyanogen group).
