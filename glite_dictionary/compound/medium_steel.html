medium steel

1. NOUN

/ ˈmiːdiəm stil / (AmE), / ˈmiːdiəm stiːl / (BrE)
steel containing 0.15–0.3% carbon, used for applications requiring a balance of strength and ductility
steel with moderate carbon content

Examples

The construction project specified medium steel for its beams to balance strength and flexibility.
Automotive components are often made from medium steel due to its durability.
Welders must adjust their technique when working with medium steel to ensure optimal joint strength.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: low steel - steel with low carbon content (generally less than 0.15%)
  • Degree Higher: high steel - steel with high carbon content (generally above 0.3%)
