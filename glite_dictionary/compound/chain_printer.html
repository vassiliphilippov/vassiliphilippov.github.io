chain printer

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃeɪn ˌprɪntər / (AmE), / ˈtʃeɪn ˌprɪntə / (BrE)
an impact printer in which type slugs are mounted on links of a rotating chain, striking paper to produce characters.
type of impact printer with type on a chain

Examples

The old computer lab still uses a chain printer for bulk report printing.
A chain printer can print lines rapidly by striking type slugs onto paper via an inked ribbon.
Unlike modern printers, the chain printer is noisy but reliable for large print jobs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: line printer - a high-speed printer that prints an entire line at once
  • Antonym: dot matrix printer - an impact printer that forms characters as a series of dots
