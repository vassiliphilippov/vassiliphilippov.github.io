sickle cell

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪkəl sɛl / (AmE), / ˈsɪkəl sɛl / (BrE)
an abnormal red blood cell with a crescent shape and an atypical form of hemoglobin, commonly found in people with sickle cell disease
abnormal crescent-shaped red blood cell
medical

Examples

Microscopic examination revealed numerous sickle cells among the red blood cells.
The presence of sickle cells can cause blockages in small blood vessels.
Doctors diagnose sickle cell disease by identifying sickle cells in a blood sample.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sickle cell anemia - hereditary disease characterized by sickle cells

Etymology

From 'sickle', referring to its curved shape, and 'cell', from Latin 'cella' (small room, chamber).
