whiz-kid

1. NOUN

/ wɪz kɪd / (AmE), / wɪz kɪd / (BrE)
a young person who is very skilled or successful, especially in business or a particular profession
exceptionally talented or successful young person
informal american

Examples

The company's new CEO is a whiz-kid who rose to the top at just 28.
As a tech whiz-kid, she developed several popular apps before finishing college.
Many investors were eager to hear the whiz-kid's strategy for success.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prodigy - a young person with exceptional talent or ability
  • Synonym: genius - a person with extraordinary intellectual or creative power

Etymology

Formed from 'whiz' (a person unusually skilled or talented) and 'kid' (child or young person), popularized in 20th-century U.S. English.
