round whitefish

1. NOUN

/ raʊnd ˈwaɪtˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / raʊnd ˈwaɪtˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
a freshwater fish (Prosopium cylindraceum) of northern North America and Siberia, having a bronze back and white underside
a freshwater fish of northern North America and Siberia

Examples

The round whitefish is commonly found in cold northern lakes and rivers.
Commercial fishermen sometimes catch round whitefish alongside other species.
Researchers studied the habitat preferences of the round whitefish in Lake Superior.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whitefish - a general term for various freshwater fish of the subfamily Coregoninae
