Kosher Nostra

1. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊʃər ˈnɑstrə / (AmE), / ˈkəʊʃə ˈnɒstrə / (BrE)
A term referring to organized crime groups composed of Jewish Americans, especially in the early to mid-20th century.
Jewish-American organized crime groups

Examples

The documentary traced the history of the Kosher Nostra in New York during the 1920s.
Some historians argue that the influence of the Kosher Nostra has often been exaggerated in popular culture.
She was researching figures connected to the Kosher Nostra for her latest crime novel.

Synset Examples

The article explores the myth of the Kosher Nostra.
They joked about the Kosher Nostra while discussing mob movies.
The Kosher Nostra is a term used to describe organized crime.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cosa Nostra - Italian-American Mafia organized crime syndicate
  • Synonym: Mafia - A general term for organized crime syndicates, especially of Italian origin

Etymology

Formed by analogy with 'Cosa Nostra' (Italian Mafia), combining 'kosher' (referring to Jewish dietary law) with 'Nostra'.
