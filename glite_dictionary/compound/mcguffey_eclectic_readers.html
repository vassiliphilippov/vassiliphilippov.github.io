McGuffey Eclectic Readers

1. NOUN

/ məˈɡʌfi ɪˈklɛktɪk ˈridɚz / (AmE), / məˈɡʌfi ɪˈklɛktɪk ˈriːdəz / (BrE)
a series of 19th-century American school textbooks for teaching reading, known for combining reading instruction with moral lessons
series of American school readers with moral lessons

Examples

Many American students in the 19th century learned to read using the McGuffey Eclectic Readers.
The McGuffey Eclectic Readers were known for their emphasis on moral instruction as well as literacy.
Collectors often seek original editions of the McGuffey Eclectic Readers for their historical significance.

Etymology

Named after William Holmes McGuffey (1800–1873), the American educator who compiled the series.
