to have on board

1. VERB

/ hæv ɑn bɔrd / (AmE), / hæv ɒn bɔːd / (BrE)
to include or involve (someone) as part of a group, organization, or team
include or involve (someone) in a group

Examples

We're thrilled to have you on board for this project.
The company now has her on board as a senior consultant.
They were eager to have more experts on board to advise them.

Synset Examples

The committee is excited to have you on board.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: welcome - to greet someone gladly or hospitably
  • Antonym: to exclude - to deliberately not include someone
