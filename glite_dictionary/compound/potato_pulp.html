potato pulp

1. NOUN

/ pəˈteɪtoʊ pʌlp / (AmE), / pəˈteɪtəʊ pʌlp / (BrE)
the fibrous byproduct remaining after starch is washed from mashed potatoes, often used as animal feed
byproduct of potato starch extraction

Examples

The factory processes tons of potato pulp each day for use in livestock feed.
After extracting the starch, the leftover potato pulp can be dried and stored easily.
Farmers often buy potato pulp as a cost-effective supplement for animal diets.

Synset Examples

Potato pulp is often dried and dehydrated since it contains a lot of water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: potato starch - the starch extracted from potatoes
