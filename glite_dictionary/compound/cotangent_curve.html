cotangent curve

1. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊˌtændʒənt kɜːrv / (AmE), / ˈkəʊˌtændʒənt kɜːv / (BrE)
the graph of the cotangent function, represented in Cartesian coordinates as y = a cot x, which is periodic and shows the values of the cotangent across its domain
graph of the cotangent function

Examples

The cotangent curve rapidly decreases and increases as it approaches its vertical asymptotes.
Graphing a cotangent curve helps visualize how the cotangent function behaves across its period.
The cotangent curve repeats itself every π units on the x-axis.

Synset Examples

Note that the cotangent curve, like all trigonometric graphs, is periodic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sine curve - graph of the sine function
  • Synonym: tangent curve - graph of the tangent function
  • Synonym: secant curve - graph of the secant function

Etymology

From 'cotangent', a trigonometric function (from Latin 'tangens', meaning 'touching'), and 'curve' (from Latin 'curvus', meaning 'bent').
