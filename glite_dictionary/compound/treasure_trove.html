treasure trove

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrɛʒər troʊv / (AmE), / ˈtrɛʒə trəʊv / (BrE)
a valuable or richly diverse collection of things, especially one discovered unexpectedly
valuable or rich collection of things

Examples

The ancient library was a treasure trove of historical documents.
Her grandmother's jewelry box turned out to be a treasure trove of family heirlooms.
Online archives have become a treasure trove for researchers searching for rare manuscripts.

Synset Examples

her book was a treasure trove of new ideas
mother's attic was a treasure trove when we were looking for antiques

Related Terms

  • Synonym: treasure - wealth or riches stored or accumulated
  • Synonym: gold mine - a rich source of something valuable or useful

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman *tresor trové* (found treasure).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtrɛʒər troʊv / (AmE), / ˈtrɛʒə trəʊv / (BrE)
a valuable collection, especially of money, gold, or jewels, found hidden and whose ownership is not known
hidden valuable collection or find

Examples

The archaeologists discovered a treasure trove of ancient coins beneath the old temple.
The attic turned out to be a treasure trove of vintage memorabilia and forgotten photographs.
The island legend spoke of a pirate's treasure trove hidden deep within the caves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hoard - a stock or store of money or valued objects, typically one that is secret or carefully guarded

Etymology

From Old French *tresor trové* meaning 'found treasure'.
