false saber-toothed tiger

1. NOUN

/ fɔls ˈseɪbər tuːθ ˈtaɪɡər / (AmE), / fɔːls ˈseɪbə tuːθ ˈtaɪɡə / (BrE)
an extinct North American cat of the Miocene and Pliocene epochs that resembled true saber-toothed cats but was less specialized, belonging to primitive lineages earlier than Smilodon
extinct North American prehistoric cat

Examples

Fossils of the false saber-toothed tiger have been found throughout North America.
The false saber-toothed tiger was less specialized than its famous relative, the Smilodon.
Paleontologists study the false saber-toothed tiger to understand early feline evolution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: saber-toothed tiger - an extinct prehistoric cat with large saber-shaped canine teeth
  • Degree Higher: Smilodon - a genus of true saber-toothed cats from the Pleistocene era
