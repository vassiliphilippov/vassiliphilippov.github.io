grass-of-Parnassus

1. NOUN

/ ˌgræs əv pɑːrˈnæsəs / (AmE), / ˌɡrɑːs əv pɑːˈnasəs / (BrE)
A perennial bog plant of the genus Parnassia, with broad smooth basal leaves and solitary pale, buttercup-like flowers, found in wet habitats.
bog plant with pale, buttercup-like flower

Examples

The delicate grass-of-Parnassus brightened the damp meadows with its pale blooms.
Botanists discovered a rare species of grass-of-Parnassus thriving in the northern bog.
She collected several grass-of-Parnassus specimens for her wildflower project.

Etymology

Named after Mount Parnassus in Greece, where the plant was formerly abundant.
