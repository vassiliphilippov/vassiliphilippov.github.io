fennel flower

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɛnəl ˌflaʊər / (AmE), / ˈfɛnəl ˌflaʊə / (BrE)
A plant of the buttercup family (Nigella), native to southern Europe, known for its feathery foliage and blue or white flowers.
Mediterranean Nigella plant with blue flowers

Examples

The fennel flower adds a delicate touch to cottage gardens with its feathery leaves.
She used seeds from the fennel flower in her baking recipes.
Wild fennel flowers grow abundantly in southern France's countryside.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nigella - A genus of flowering plants in the buttercup family, including Nigella sativa and Nigella damascena.

Etymology

From 'fennel' (due to resemblance in foliage) + 'flower'.
