genus Argyreia

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ɑːrˈdʒaɪ.ri.ə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ɑːˈdʒɪə.ri.ə / (BrE)
a genus of woody climbing plants native to tropical Asia and Australia, belonging to the family Convolvulaceae
genus of tropical Asian and Australian woody climbers

Examples

The genus Argyreia includes several species known for their ornamental value.
Researchers studied the medicinal properties of plants in the genus Argyreia.
Members of the genus Argyreia are often found climbing in tropical forests.

Etymology

From New Latin, after Greek 'argyros' meaning 'silver', referring to the silvery leaves of some species.
