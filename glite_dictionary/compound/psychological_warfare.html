psychological warfare

1. NOUN

/ saɪˌka.ləˈdʒɪ.kəl ˈwɔːr.fɛr / (AmE), / saɪˌkɒ.ləˈdʒɪ.kəl ˈwɔː.feə / (BrE)
The use of psychological tactics, such as propaganda or intimidation, to weaken an opponent's morale or will to fight.
use of psychological tactics in conflict

Examples

The army engaged in psychological warfare to undermine the enemy’s confidence.
During the Cold War, both sides practiced psychological warfare through misinformation campaigns.
Effective psychological warfare can often do more damage than physical attacks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: propaganda - information spread to influence opinion, often misleading

2. NOUN

/ ˌsaɪkəˈlɑdʒɪkəl ˈwɔrˌfɛr / (AmE), / ˌsaɪkəˈlɒdʒɪkəl ˈwɔːˌfeə / (BrE)
the use of tactics intended to influence or manipulate the thoughts, feelings, or behavior of opponents, especially in military or political contexts
tactics to influence opponents' minds

Examples

The army employed psychological warfare to weaken the enemy's morale before launching an attack.
Rumors and disinformation are common tools in psychological warfare.
During the Cold War, both sides engaged in intensive psychological warfare against each other.

Synset Examples

They used psychological warfare during the negotiations.
The film depicted the horrors of psychological warfare.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: propaganda - information spread to influence public opinion
  • Synonym: psychological operations - military operations aimed at influencing emotions and behavior
