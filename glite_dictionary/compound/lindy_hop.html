lindy hop

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪn.di hɑp / (AmE), / ˈlɪn.di hɒp / (BrE)
an energetic and lively American swing dance, originating in the 1920s and 1930s, characterized by fast-paced, improvisational partner movements
energetic American swing dance from the 1930s

Examples

We learned the lindy hop at a vintage dance class last summer.
The dance floor came alive when the band started playing music perfect for the lindy hop.
She watched in awe as the talented couple performed a flawless lindy hop routine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jitterbug - a fast dance style closely related to swing dances like the lindy hop

Etymology

Probably named after Charles Lindbergh ('Lucky Lindy'), the famous aviator, due to the dance's 'jumping' style.
