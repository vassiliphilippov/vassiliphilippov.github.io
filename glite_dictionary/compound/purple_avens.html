purple avens

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɝːpəl ˈævənz / (AmE), / ˈpɜːpl ˈævənz / (BrE)
A perennial herb native to northern temperate regions, with pinnate leaves and nodding flowers featuring a brown-purple calyx and orange and pink petals.
perennial herb with nodding purple-backed flowers

Examples

The purple avens thrives in moist meadows across northern Europe and North America.
Botanists studied the distinctive nodding flowers of the purple avens for their unique coloration.
Gardeners often plant purple avens to attract pollinators with its showy petals.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɝːpəl ˈævənz / (AmE), / ˈpɜːpl ˈævənz / (BrE)
A North American perennial plant with hairy basal pinnate leaves, purple flowers, and plume-tipped fruits.
North American perennial plant with purple flowers

Examples

The meadow was dotted with bright purple avens swaying gently in the breeze.
Gardeners often plant purple avens to attract pollinators during the spring.
She recognized the distinctive leaves and blossoms of the purple avens along the hiking trail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: avens - any plant of the genus Geum, with lobed leaves and five-petaled flowers
