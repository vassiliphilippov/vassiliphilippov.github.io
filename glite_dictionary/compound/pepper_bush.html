pepper bush

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛpər bʊʃ / (AmE), / ˈpɛpə bʊʃ / (BrE)
A shrub native to the eastern and southern coastal United States, known for its fragrant white flower spikes.
fragrant-flowered American shrub

Examples

The pepper bush blooms in late summer, filling the air with a sweet, spicy fragrance.
Gardeners often plant pepper bush for its attractive white flowers and its ability to attract pollinators.
We spotted a pepper bush growing along the marshy edge of the pond.
