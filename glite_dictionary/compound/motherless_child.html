motherless child

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌðərləs tʃaɪld / (AmE), / ˈmʌðələs tʃaɪld / (BrE)
a child whose mother has died, is absent, or is otherwise not in their life
child without a mother

Examples

The story focused on a motherless child struggling to find her place in the world.
After his mother passed away, he became a motherless child at the age of six.
The teacher extended special care to the motherless child in her class.

Synset Examples

That motherless child needs some love and support.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: orphan - a child whose parents are dead
