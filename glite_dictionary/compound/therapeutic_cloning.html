therapeutic cloning

1. NOUN

/ ˌθɛrəˈpjutɪk ˈkloʊnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌθɛrəˈpjuːtɪk ˈkləʊnɪŋ / (BrE)
the technique of creating tissue or organs from a person's own cells through nuclear transplantation, to produce immune-compatible cells for transplant, especially stem cells
creation of cells/organs for transplant using a person's own cells
medical

Examples

Scientists hope that therapeutic cloning could provide replacement tissues without the risk of rejection.
The ethical debate over therapeutic cloning centers around the use of embryonic stem cells.
Advancements in therapeutic cloning may lead to new treatments for conditions like Parkinson's disease.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: reproductive cloning - production of a genetically identical organism by cloning
  • Synonym: stem cell - an undifferentiated cell capable of giving rise to specialized cell types

Etymology

From therapeutic (relating to treatment of disease) + cloning (making a genetically identical copy).
