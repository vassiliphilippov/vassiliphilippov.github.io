multiplicative inverse

1. NOUN

/ mʌltəpləˌkeɪtɪv ɪnˈvɜrs / (AmE), / mʌltɪplɪkətɪv ɪnˈvɜːs / (BrE)
(Mathematics) A number which, when multiplied by a given number, results in 1; also called the reciprocal.
a number which, when multiplied by another, gives 1
medical

Examples

The multiplicative inverse of 5 is 1/5, since 5 × 1/5 equals 1.
To solve the equation, she multiplied both sides by the multiplicative inverse of the coefficient.
Every nonzero real number has a multiplicative inverse.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reciprocal - mathematical term for a number that, multiplied by a given number, yields 1

Etymology

From Latin multiplicare (to multiply) and inversus (turned upside down).
