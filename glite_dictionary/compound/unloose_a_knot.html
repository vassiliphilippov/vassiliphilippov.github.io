to unloose (a) knot

1. VERB

/ ʌnˈluːs nɒt / (AmE), / ʌnˈluːs nɒt / (BrE)
to untie or free a knot by loosening it
to untie a knot
archaic

Examples

It took him several minutes to unloose the knot in the shoelace.
After the hike, she tried to unloose the knot in the rope.
The sailor carefully unloosed the knot to free the anchor.

Synset Examples

They needed to unloose the knots on the line.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: untie a knot - to release a knot by loosening the rope or string

Etymology

From 'unloose' (Middle English, from un- + loose), meaning to make loose or release, and 'knot' (Old English cnotta).
