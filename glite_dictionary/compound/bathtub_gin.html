bathtub gin

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæθˌtʌb dʒɪn / (AmE), / ˈbɑːθˌtʌb dʒɪn / (BrE)
homemade gin, especially that made illegally and often under poor conditions
illegally homemade gin

Examples

During Prohibition, people made bathtub gin at home to get around the alcohol ban.
The quality of bathtub gin was often questionable and sometimes dangerous to drink.
She learned about bathtub gin while researching American history for her novel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moonshine - illegally distilled or smuggled liquor, especially corn whiskey
  • Degree Higher: gin - a clear alcoholic spirit distilled from grain or malt and flavored with juniper berries

Etymology

American English, from making gin in bathtubs during Prohibition (1920s USA).

2. NOUN

/ ˈbætˌtʌb dʒɪn / (AmE), / ˈbætˌtʌb dʒɪn / (BrE)
illegally made or homemade alcoholic liquor, especially of poor quality, produced during the Prohibition era in the United States
illicit homemade alcohol, especially during Prohibition

Examples

During the Roaring Twenties, many people secretly drank bathtub gin in underground bars.
The homemade bathtub gin burned his throat but was all they could get during Prohibition.
She recounted stories of her grandmother making bathtub gin in the kitchen sink.

Synset Examples

They laughed about the old days of bathtub gin parties.
During Prohibition, people made bathtub gin at home.
He claimed his bathtub gin was the best in town.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moonshine - illegally made distilled alcohol, especially in the US
  • Synonym: bootleg - illegally made, distributed, or sold alcohol

Etymology

From the practice of making gin in bathtubs during U.S. Prohibition (1920–1933).
