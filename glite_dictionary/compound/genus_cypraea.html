genus Cypraea

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs saɪˈpriː.ə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs sɪˈpriː.ə / (BrE)
the type genus of the family Cypraeidae, comprising the typical cowries
genus of typical cowries

Examples

The genus Cypraea includes some of the most beautiful cowry shells found in tropical oceans.
Researchers recently discovered a rare species within the genus Cypraea off the coast of Madagascar.
Collecting specimens of the genus Cypraea has long fascinated marine biologists and shell enthusiasts alike.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cowrie - a marine mollusk with a glossy, brightly patterned shell

Etymology

From Latin 'Cypraea', the name applied to cowry shells, itself from Greek 'kupria', referencing Cyprus, where such shells were found.
