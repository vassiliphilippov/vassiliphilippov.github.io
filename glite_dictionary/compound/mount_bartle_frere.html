Mount Bartle Frere

1. PROPN

/ maʊnt ˌbɑrtəl ˈfrɪər / (AmE), / maʊnt ˌbɑːtəl ˈfrɪə / (BrE)
the highest mountain peak in Queensland, Australia, located in the Bellenden Ker Range within the Wet Tropics World Heritage Area
the highest mountain peak in Queensland, Australia

Examples

We hiked to the summit of Mount Bartle Frere during our visit to Queensland.
Clouds often cover the peak of Mount Bartle Frere in the rainy season.
Mount Bartle Frere offers breathtaking views of the surrounding rainforest.

Etymology

Named after Sir Henry Bartle Frere, a British colonial administrator.
