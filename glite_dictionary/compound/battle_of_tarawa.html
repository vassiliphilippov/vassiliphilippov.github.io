Battle of Tarawa

1. PROPN

/ ˈbæt̬.əl əv təˈrɑː.wə / (AmE), / ˈbæt.əl əv təˈrɑː.wə / (BrE)
a battle in the Pacific during World War II (November 1943) in which United States Marines captured Tarawa Atoll from Japanese forces after intense fighting
World War II battle in the Pacific (1943)

Examples

The Battle of Tarawa marked one of the bloodiest days for the Marines in the Pacific campaign.
Many historians study the tactics used during the Battle of Tarawa to understand amphibious assaults.
A memorial commemorates those who fell during the fierce fighting at the Battle of Tarawa.
