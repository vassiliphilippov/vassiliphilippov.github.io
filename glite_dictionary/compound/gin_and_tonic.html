gin and tonic

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒɪn ən ˈtɑnɪk / (AmE), / ˌdʒɪn ən ˈtɒnɪk / (BrE)
A drink consisting of gin mixed with tonic water, usually served with ice and a slice of lemon or lime.
a mixed drink of gin and tonic water

Examples

She ordered a gin and tonic at the bar before dinner.
On summer evenings, he enjoys a cold gin and tonic in the garden.
Would you like a slice of lime in your gin and tonic?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gin - a clear alcoholic spirit distilled from grain or malt and flavored with juniper berries
