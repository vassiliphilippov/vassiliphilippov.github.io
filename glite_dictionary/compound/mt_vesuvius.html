Mt. Vesuvius

1. PROPN

/ ˌmaʊnt vəˈsuːviəs / (AmE), / ˌmaʊnt vɪˈsuːvɪəs / (BrE)
a volcano in southwestern Italy, famous for its eruption in AD 79 that destroyed the Roman cities of Pompeii and Herculaneum
volcano in southwestern Italy

Examples

The eruption of Mt. Vesuvius in AD 79 buried Pompeii under volcanic ash.
Tourists often hike up Mt. Vesuvius to see the famous crater.
Scientists continue to monitor Mt. Vesuvius due to its potential for future eruptions.

Etymology

From Latin Vesuvius (the name of the mountain).
