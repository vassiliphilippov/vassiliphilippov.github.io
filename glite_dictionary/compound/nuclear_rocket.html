nuclear rocket

1. NOUN

/ ˈnuːkliɚ ˈrɑːkɪt / (AmE), / ˈnjuːkliə ˈrɒkɪt / (BrE)
a rocket engine in which a nuclear reactor is used to heat a propellant, providing thrust for space travel
rocket engine heated by a nuclear reactor

Examples

The engineers discussed the potential advantages of a nuclear rocket for interplanetary missions.
A nuclear rocket could provide higher efficiency compared to traditional chemical propulsion systems.
Research into nuclear rocket technology began in the mid-twentieth century as part of ambitious space programs.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: chemical rocket - a rocket powered by chemical reactions
