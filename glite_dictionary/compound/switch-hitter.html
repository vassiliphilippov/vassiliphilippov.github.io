switch-hitter

1. NOUN

/ ˈswɪtʃˌhɪtər / (AmE), / ˈswɪtʃˌhɪtə / (BrE)
a baseball player who can bat both right-handed and left-handed
baseball player who bats both ways

Examples

As a switch-hitter, he gives his team more options against both left- and right-handed pitchers.
The team's only switch-hitter homered in last night's crucial playoff game.
Many scouts look for a switch-hitter when recruiting new baseball talent.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: left-handed batter - a batter who bats only left-handed
  • Antonym: right-handed batter - a batter who bats only right-handed

Etymology

From 'switch', meaning to change, and 'hitter', meaning batter; coined in early 20th-century American baseball.
