eggheaded approach

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛɡˌhɛdɪd əˈproʊtʃ / (AmE), / ˈɛɡˌhɛdɪd əˈprəʊtʃ / (BrE)
An excessively academic or intellectual method or way of dealing with something, often seen as impractical or out of touch with everyday concerns.
overly academic or intellectual method
informal

Examples

The manager's eggheaded approach was criticized for being out of touch with industry realities.
Sometimes an eggheaded approach leads to theories that are too complex for practical application.
Her proposal was rejected because it was based on an eggheaded approach rather than practical experience.

Synset Examples

His eggheaded approach to the project slowed things down.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: academic - related to scholarly or scholarly-minded methods
  • Antonym: practical approach - a method prioritizing actual results and usefulness

Etymology

From 'egghead', US slang (mid-20th century) for an intellectual, combined with 'approach'.
