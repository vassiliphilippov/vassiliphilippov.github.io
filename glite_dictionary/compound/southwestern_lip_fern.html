southwestern lip fern

1. NOUN

/ ˌsaʊθˈwɛstərn lɪp fɜrn / (AmE), / ˌsaʊθˈwɛstən lɪp fɜːn / (BrE)
a species of lip fern native to Texas, Oklahoma, Colorado, Arizona, and Mexico, characterized by tall, erect, tufted fronds
fern species native to southwestern US and Mexico

Examples

The southwestern lip fern thrives in rocky, sunlit areas across Arizona and New Mexico.
During our hike, we spotted a cluster of southwestern lip fern near the canyon's edge.
Gardeners appreciate the drought tolerance of the southwestern lip fern for xeriscaping projects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lip fern - any fern of the genus Cheilanthes, known for having membranous leaf margins
