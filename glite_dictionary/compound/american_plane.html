American plane

1. NOUN

/ əˈmɛrɪkən pleɪn / (AmE), / əˈmɛrɪkən pleɪn / (BrE)
a very large tree native to eastern and central North America, with broad spreading branches and distinctive peeling bark; also called sycamore or buttonwood
large North American plane tree

Examples

The massive American plane in the park provides shade for dozens of people each summer.
Many city streets are lined with American plane trees, prized for their resilience and beauty.
Fallen leaves from the American plane carpet the ground in autumn with gold and brown hues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sycamore - another common name for the American plane
