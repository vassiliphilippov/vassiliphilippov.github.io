place-worship

1. NOUN

/ ˈpleɪs ˌwɜrʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈpleɪs ˌwɜːʃɪp / (BrE)
The practice of worshipping or veneration of specific places believed to have religious or spiritual significance.
worship of specific places

Examples

Many ancient cultures practiced place-worship at sacred groves and mountains.
The old temple became a center for place-worship after miraculous events were reported there.
Anthropologists study place-worship as part of their research on religious traditions.
