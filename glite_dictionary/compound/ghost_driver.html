ghost driver

1. NOUN

/ ɡoʊst ˈdraɪvər / (AmE), / ɡəʊst ˈdraɪvə / (BrE)
a person or system that operates a vehicle autonomously or without being detected
autonomous or unnoticed vehicle operator

Examples

After the upgrade, the car could function as a ghost driver on the highway at night.
Sometimes it felt like a ghost driver was steering her life from behind the scenes.
The new system allows for a ghost driver to take over in emergencies.

Synset Examples

The software update turned my old car into a ghost driver.
He felt like a ghost driver, unseen yet in control.
The car operated smoothly, like a ghost driver guiding it.

Etymology

Formed from 'ghost' (unseen or hidden) and 'driver' (person who operates a vehicle).
