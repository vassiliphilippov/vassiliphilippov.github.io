pearly razorfish

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɜrli ˈreɪzərˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈpɜːli ˈreɪzəˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
A species of small marine fish with a slender body and pearly coloration, found in sandy coastal waters.
a small pearly colored marine fish

Examples

The pearly razorfish buries itself in the sand when threatened.
Divers often spot the colorful pearly razorfish near coral reefs.
A pearly razorfish can change its coloration to blend with its surroundings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: razorfish - a type of slender marine fish with a sharp-edged snout
