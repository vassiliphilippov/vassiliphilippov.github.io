bull terrier

1. NOUN

/ bʊl ˈtɛriər / (AmE), / bʊl ˈtɛriə / (BrE)
A powerful, short-haired breed of dog that originated in England by crossing bulldogs with various terriers, known for its muscular build and distinct egg-shaped head.
breed of strong English terrier dog

Examples

The bull terrier is recognized for its unique egg-shaped head and lively temperament.
She adopted a bull terrier from the animal shelter and quickly became fond of its loyalty.
Many bull terriers excel at agility and obedience competitions, surprising those unfamiliar with the breed.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: terrier - any of several breeds of small, energetic dogs originally bred for hunting
  • Degree Higher: bulldog - a muscular, heavyset dog breed with a wrinkled face and distinctive pushed-in nose

Etymology

From 'bull' (as in bulldog) and 'terrier', reflecting its crossbred origins in England.
