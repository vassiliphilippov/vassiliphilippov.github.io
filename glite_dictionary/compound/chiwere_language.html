Chiwere language

1. NOUN

/ tʃaɪˈwɛr i ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / tʃaɪˈwɛə ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Siouan language traditionally spoken by the Iowa, Otoe, and Missouria peoples
Native American Siouan language

Examples

The Chiwere language is critically endangered, with only a few fluent speakers remaining.
Researchers have documented traditional stories in the Chiwere language for preservation purposes.
Her grandmother taught her basic phrases in the Chiwere language to help keep their heritage alive.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Siouan - referring to the Siouan language family

Etymology

From the name 'Chiwere,' an endonym used by the speakers, plus 'language.'
