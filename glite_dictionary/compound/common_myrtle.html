common myrtle

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmən ˈmɜrtl̩ / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən ˈmɜːt(ə)l / (BrE)
a Mediterranean evergreen shrub (Myrtus communis) with aromatic leaves, white or pinkish flowers, and black berries
Mediterranean aromatic shrub

Examples

The common myrtle is often cultivated in gardens for its fragrant blossoms.
In Mediterranean climates, the common myrtle thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight.
Traditional herbal medicine sometimes uses parts of the common myrtle for their aromatic properties.
