triumphal arch

1. NOUN

/ traɪˈʌmfəl ɑːrtʃ / (AmE), / traɪˈʌmfəl ɑːtʃ / (BrE)
a monumental arched structure built to commemorate a notable victory or event, often serving as an entrance or symbolic gateway
monumental arch commemorating a victory

Examples

Visitors to Paris often admire the triumphal arch known as the Arc de Triomphe.
A splendid triumphal arch was erected to celebrate the king's return from battle.
The Romans popularized the construction of the triumphal arch throughout their empire.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arch - a curved structure spanning an opening

Etymology

From Latin 'arcus triumphalis', meaning 'arch of triumph'.
