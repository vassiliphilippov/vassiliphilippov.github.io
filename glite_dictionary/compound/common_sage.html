common sage

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmən seɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən seɪdʒ / (BrE)
A shrubby plant (Salvia officinalis) with aromatic greyish-green leaves, used as a cooking herb.
aromatic herb; Salvia officinalis

Examples

Fresh common sage adds a robust flavor to roasted meats and vegetables.
Many gardeners grow common sage for its attractive foliage and culinary uses.
The recipe calls for a teaspoon of chopped common sage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sage - Aromatic culinary herb; Salvia genus
