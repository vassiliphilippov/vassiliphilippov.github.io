bone marrow

1. NOUN

/ boʊn ˈmæroʊ / (AmE), / bəʊn ˈmærəʊ / (BrE)
Very soft, nutritious tissue found inside certain bones, used as food and considered a delicacy in some cuisines.
soft, edible tissue inside bones

Examples

Many gourmet restaurants serve bone marrow roasted and spread on toast.
Traditional beef stew is sometimes enriched with bone marrow for added flavor and nutrition.
He scooped out the bone marrow and described its taste as rich and buttery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marrowbone - a bone containing edible marrow

2. NOUN

/ boʊn ˈmæroʊ / (AmE), / bəʊn ˈmærəʊ / (BrE)
the soft, fatty tissue inside bones that produces blood cells and fills the cavities of most bones in humans and animals
soft tissue inside bones; blood cell producer

Examples

Chemotherapy can damage the bone marrow and reduce blood cell production.
A transplant of bone marrow was necessary to treat her leukemia.
Doctors extracted bone marrow from his hip for analysis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marrow - the soft, fatty tissue found in bone cavities
