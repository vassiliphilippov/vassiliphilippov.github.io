Rocky-mountain maple

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɑki ˈmaʊntn ˈmeɪpəl / (AmE), / ˌrɒki ˈmaʊntɪn ˈmeɪpəl / (BrE)
A small species of maple tree native to the northwestern regions of North America, known for its shrubby growth and distinctive leaves.
small maple tree of northwestern North America

Examples

The Rocky-mountain maple is often found growing along streams in the Pacific Northwest.
Gardeners appreciate the Rocky-mountain maple for its hardiness and attractive foliage.
In autumn, the leaves of the Rocky-mountain maple turn brilliant shades of red and orange.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maple - a tree or shrub of the genus Acer, known for lobed leaves and winged fruit
