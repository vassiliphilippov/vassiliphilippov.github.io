active voice

1. NOUN

/ ˈæktɪv vɔɪs / (AmE), / ˈæktɪv vɔɪs / (BrE)
a grammatical construction in which the subject of the sentence performs the action expressed by the verb
grammatical voice with subject performing the action

Examples

In the sentence 'She wrote the letter,' the active voice is used.
Writers are often encouraged to use the active voice to make their sentences clearer.
The active voice typically makes writing more direct and concise.

Synset Examples

‘The boy threw the ball’ uses the active voice

Related Terms

  • Antonym: passive voice - grammatical voice where the subject receives the action

Etymology

From Latin 'activus' (active) and Old French 'vois' (voice) via Middle English.
