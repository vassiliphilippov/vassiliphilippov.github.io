coronoid process of the mandible

1. NOUN

/ kəˈroʊnɔɪd ˈprɑsɛs əv ðə mænˈdɪbəl / (AmE), / kəˈrəʊnɔɪd ˈprɒsɛs əv ðə mænˈdɪbəl / (BrE)
the thin, triangular projection at the upper front part of the mandible that provides attachment for the temporalis muscle
projection on the upper mandible for muscle attachment
medical

Examples

The coronoid process of the mandible serves as a crucial attachment site for chewing muscles.
During the operation, the surgeon avoided damaging the coronoid process of the mandible.
X-ray images revealed a fracture near the coronoid process of the mandible.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mandible - the lower jawbone

Etymology

From Greek korōnē (hooked, curved) and Latin mandibula (jaw).
