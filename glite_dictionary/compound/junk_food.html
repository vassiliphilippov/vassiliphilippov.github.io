junk food

1. NOUN

/ dʒʌŋk fuːd / (AmE), / dʒʌŋk fuːd / (BrE)
Food that is tasty but high in calories and low in nutritional value, often considered unhealthy.
unhealthy, low-nutrition food

Examples

Eating too much junk food can lead to health problems like obesity.
The vending machine was stocked with junk food such as chips and candy bars.
She tries to avoid junk food and eat balanced meals instead.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fast food - easily prepared processed food served quickly
  • Antonym: health food - food believed to be very good for your health

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʒʌŋk fuːd / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌŋk fuːd / (BrE)
Food that is high in calories but low in nutritional value, often containing a lot of sugar, fat, or salt.
unhealthy, low-nutrient food

Examples

Many people find it hard to resist eating junk food when they are stressed.
Parents are encouraged to limit their children's consumption of junk food.
The vending machine in the office only sells junk food like chips and candy.

Synset Examples

Junk food news often highlights the newest fast food items.
Her consumption of junk food is concerning.
He got sick after eating too much junk food, like ralphie.
He needed to ungage from junk food for his diet.
She prefers healthy snacks over junk food.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fast food - food that can be prepared and served quickly, often considered unhealthy
  • Antonym: health food - food considered to be beneficial to health and especially low in unhealthy substances

Etymology

Formed from 'junk' (worthless) and 'food'; common in American English since the 1970s.
