bronchial artery

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrɑːŋkiəl ˈɑːrtəri / (AmE), / ˈbrɒŋkɪəl ˈɑːtəri / (BrE)
a small artery that accompanies and supplies blood to the bronchi and other tissues in the lungs
artery supplying blood to the bronchi
medical

Examples

The surgeon carefully isolated the bronchial artery before proceeding with the procedure.
A blockage in a bronchial artery can reduce blood supply to lung tissues.
The anatomy textbook included a detailed diagram showing the bronchial artery and its branches.

Etymology

From 'bronchial,' relating to the bronchi (from Greek 'bronkhos'), and 'artery,' from Greek 'artēria' (windpipe, artery).
