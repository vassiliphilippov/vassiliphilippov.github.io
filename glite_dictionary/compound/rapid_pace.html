rapid pace

1. NOUN

/ ˈræpɪd peɪs / (AmE), / ˈræpɪd peɪs / (BrE)
a speed of progress or movement that is much faster than usual or expected
very fast rate of progress

Examples

The economy is changing at a rapid pace due to technological innovation.
She had to learn new skills at a rapid pace to keep up with her job requirements.
Construction on the new highway proceeded at a rapid pace throughout the summer.

Synset Examples

The project moved at a rapid pace to meet the deadline.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fast pace - quick speed of progress or movement
  • Antonym: slow pace - moving at a low speed
