passionflower vine

1. NOUN

/ ˈpæʃ.ənˌflaʊ.ər vaɪn / (AmE), / ˈpæʃ.ənˌflaʊ.ə vaɪn / (BrE)
A vine belonging to the genus Passiflora, chiefly found in tropical America, some species of which bear edible fruit.
a tropical vine of the genus Passiflora

Examples

The passionflower vine climbed the trellis, displaying its striking purple flowers by midsummer.
Some species of passionflower vine produce fruit known as passion fruit, which is both sweet and aromatic.
Gardeners often grow passionflower vines for their fast growth and exotic flowers.

Etymology

From 'passionflower', named by 16th-century Spanish missionaries who associated its floral structure with the Passion of Christ, and 'vine'.
