nota bene

1. NOUN

/ ˌnoʊtə ˈbeɪni / (AmE), / ˌnəʊtə ˈbeːneɪ / (BrE)
a phrase meaning 'note well'; used to draw special attention to a statement or important point
phrase meaning 'note well'; pay special attention

Examples

Please nota bene that the office will be closed for renovations next week.
He added a nota bene in the margin to highlight the key rule.
The instructions included a nota bene about the strict deadline.

Synset Examples

the margins of his book were generously supplied with pencilled NBs

Etymology

From Latin 'nota bene' meaning 'mark well' or 'note well'.
