emergency medicine

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈmɝːdʒənsi ˈmɛdəsən / (AmE), / ɪˈmɜːdʒənsi ˈmɛdɪsɪn / (BrE)
the branch of medicine concerned with the immediate diagnosis and treatment of acute illnesses or injuries
medical specialty for acute care
medical

Examples

He decided to specialize in emergency medicine because he enjoys working in fast-paced environments.
Hospitals rely on highly trained professionals in emergency medicine to handle critical situations.
After finishing her residency, she began working in emergency medicine at a busy urban hospital.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trauma medicine - medical specialty focused on the treatment of traumatic injuries
