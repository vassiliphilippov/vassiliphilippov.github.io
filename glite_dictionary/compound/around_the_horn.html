around the Horn

1. X

/ əˈraʊnd ðə hɔrn / (AmE), / əˈraʊnd ðə hɔːn / (BrE)
(of a ship or sailor) to sail around Cape Horn, the southern tip of South America, often regarded as a difficult and dangerous passage.
navigating around Cape Horn

Examples

The clipper ship was damaged while sailing around the Horn in stormy weather.
Many sailors dreaded the treacherous journey around the Horn due to its fierce winds.
In the nineteenth century, merchants often went around the Horn to reach the Pacific.

Synset Examples

The ship made it successfully around the Horn.
They sailed around the Horn during their adventure.
Sailing around the Horn requires skilled navigators.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cape Horn - the southernmost point of South America
