fresh hell

1. NOUN

/ frɛʃ hɛl / (AmE), / frɛʃ hɛl / (BrE)
a new and particularly difficult or distressing problem or situation, especially one that follows closely after previous troubles
a new and intense difficulty or misery

Examples

Every time I think things can't get worse, a fresh hell appears on my desk.
Waking up to a flooded kitchen was a fresh hell I wasn't prepared for.
He described paperwork delays as a fresh hell every month.

Synset Examples

This new project feels like a fresh hell every day.
The traffic jam turned my commute into a fresh hell.
Working through the weekend felt like fresh hell.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nightmare - an extremely unpleasant or difficult experience
  • Synonym: torment - severe physical or mental suffering

Etymology

Popularized by American writer Dorothy Parker in the early 20th century, reflecting the idea of recurring new troubles.
