ileal artery

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪliəl ˈɑːr.t̬i / (AmE), / ˈɪliːəl ˈɑː.təri / (BrE)
a branch of the superior mesenteric artery that supplies blood to the ileum in the small intestine
artery supplying the ileum
medical

Examples

During surgery, care must be taken not to damage the ileal artery when operating near the small intestine.
A blockage in the ileal artery can reduce blood supply to the ileum, causing digestive problems.
The ileal artery branches off the superior mesenteric artery to deliver oxygenated blood to the lower part of the small intestine.

Etymology

From Latin *ileum* (the last part of the small intestine) and *arteria* (artery).
