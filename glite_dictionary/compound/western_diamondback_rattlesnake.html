Western diamondback rattlesnake

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɛstɚn ˈdaɪəməndˌbæk ˈræt̬lˌsneɪk / (AmE), / ˈwɛstən ˈdaɪəməndˌbæk ˈrætlˌsneɪk / (BrE)
a large and highly venomous rattlesnake (Crotalus atrox) found in the southwestern United States and Mexico, known for the distinctive diamond pattern on its back.
venomous snake of southwestern US and Mexico

Examples

The Western diamondback rattlesnake is often encountered in the deserts of Arizona and Texas.
A bite from a Western diamondback rattlesnake can be life-threatening without prompt medical treatment.
Researchers tracked the movements of several Western diamondback rattlesnakes to better understand their behavior.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rattlesnake - a venomous American snake that makes a rattling sound with its tail
