vaulting horse

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɔltɪŋ hɔrs / (AmE), / ˈvɔːltɪŋ hɔːs / (BrE)
a solid apparatus with one elongated end and no pommels, used lengthwise in gymnastics for vaulting exercises
apparatus used in gymnastics for vaulting

Examples

The gymnast sprinted towards the vaulting horse and launched herself into the air.
During practice, he struggled to clear the vaulting horse cleanly.
The vaulting horse was adjusted to accommodate athletes of different heights.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pommel horse - gymnastic apparatus with two handles (pommels), used for swinging exercises

Etymology

From 'vault', meaning to leap, and 'horse', originally referring to a padded frame shaped to resemble a horse.
