Orange River

1. PROPN

/ ˈɔrɪndʒ ˈrɪvər / (AmE), / ˈɒrɪndʒ ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
the longest river in South Africa, flowing westward to the Atlantic Ocean
river in South Africa

Examples

The Orange River forms part of the border between South Africa and Namibia.
Many farms along the Orange River rely on its water for irrigation.
The source of the Orange River is in the Drakensberg mountains.

Etymology

Named after the Dutch House of Orange.

2. PROPN

/ ˈɔrɪndʒ ˈrɪvər / (AmE), / ˈɒrɪndʒ ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
a river in southern Africa that flows westward across South Africa to the Atlantic Ocean, forming part of the border with Namibia
major river in southern Africa

Examples

The Orange River forms a natural boundary between South Africa and Namibia.
Farmers rely on water from the Orange River to irrigate their crops in the arid region.
The team followed the Orange River all the way to its mouth at the Atlantic Ocean.

Etymology

Named after the Dutch House of Orange.
