nontricyclic antidepressant drug

1. NOUN

/ ˌnɑːn.trɪˈsaɪklɪk ˌæn.ti.dɪˈprɛsənt drʌɡ / (AmE), / ˌnɒn.trɪˈsʌɪklɪk ˌantɪdɪˈprɛsənt drʌɡ / (BrE)
an antidepressant drug that does not belong to the tricyclic class and does not work by inhibiting monoamine oxidase (MAO)
antidepressant not of tricyclic class or MAO inhibitors
medical

Examples

Doctors may prescribe a nontricyclic antidepressant drug if a patient cannot tolerate tricyclics.
Newer nontricyclic antidepressant drugs tend to have fewer side effects than older medications.
Some people respond better to a nontricyclic antidepressant drug compared to traditional options.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: tricyclic antidepressant - antidepressant drug with a three-ring chemical structure
  • Degree Lower: antidepressant - any drug used to treat depressive disorders

Etymology

Formed from 'non-' (not) + 'tricyclic antidepressant' (antidepressant with a three-ring structure).
