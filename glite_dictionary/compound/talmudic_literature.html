Talmudic literature

1. NOUN

/ tɑlˈmudɪk ˈlɪtərətʃər / (AmE), / tælˈmjuːdɪk ˈlɪtrətʃə / (BrE)
the body of ancient rabbinical writings that form an important part of Jewish tradition, including the Talmud and related texts
ancient rabbinical Jewish writings

Examples

Many scholars devote their lives to the study of Talmudic literature.
Courses on Talmudic literature are offered at several major universities.
Understanding Talmudic literature provides insight into Jewish law and tradition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Talmud - the central text of Rabbinic Judaism consisting of the Mishnah and Gemara

Etymology

Talmudic from Hebrew 'Talmudh' (learning, instruction) + literature.
