dermoid cyst

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɜːrˌmɔɪd sɪst / (AmE), / ˈdɜːmɔɪd sɪst / (BrE)
A benign cystic tumor with a wall lined by epithelium, containing tissues or material such as hair, skin, or teeth.
benign cystic tumor with specialized tissue
medical

Examples

The surgeon removed a dermoid cyst from the patient's ovary during the operation.
A dermoid cyst can occasionally be found near the eye or other parts of the body.
Dermatologists use imaging techniques to diagnose a dermoid cyst accurately.

Etymology

From Greek 'derma' (skin) and 'eidos' (form), referring to cysts with skin-like tissue.
