liquid gold

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪkwɪd ˈɡoʊld / (AmE), / ˈlɪkwɪd ˈɡəʊld / (BrE)
a substance that is highly valuable, especially liquid forms such as oil or honey
something extremely valuable, especially a liquid

Examples

The locals refer to their unique olive oil as liquid gold because of its quality and value.
After the price surge, crude oil was often called liquid gold by investors.
Bee farmers cherish their honey, sometimes describing it as liquid gold due to its rarity and sweetness.

Synset Examples

They call the honey produced here liquid gold.
The oil is considered liquid gold in this region.
With its rich flavor, the olive oil is like liquid gold.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: treasure - a collection of valuable things; great wealth
  • Antonym: worthless - having no value or use
