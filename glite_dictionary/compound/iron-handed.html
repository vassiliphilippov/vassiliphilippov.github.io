iron-handed

1. ADJ

/ ˌaɪrn ˈhændɪd / (AmE), / ˌaɪən ˈhændɪd / (BrE)
Dealing with authority or enforcement in a harsh and strict manner; showing no leniency or mercy.
strict and harsh in authority

Examples

The new manager was iron-handed with those who broke company policies.
Many citizens resented the iron-handed tactics used by the police during the protests.
His iron-handed leadership style left little room for negotiation or dissent.

Synset Examples

The iron-handed enforcement of rules created tension.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: iron-fisted - ruling or enforcing harshly and strictly
  • Antonym: lenient - showing tolerance or mercy; not strict

Etymology

From the metaphor of wielding power with a hand of iron, combining 'iron' (symbolizing strength and rigidity) + 'handed'.
