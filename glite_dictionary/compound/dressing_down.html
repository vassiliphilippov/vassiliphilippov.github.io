dressing down

1. NOUN

/ ˈdrɛsɪŋ ˌdaʊn / (AmE), / ˈdrɛsɪŋ ˌdaʊn / (BrE)
a severe reprimand or scolding, often delivered in a formal or emphatic manner
severe scolding or reprimand

Examples

The manager gave him a dressing down in front of the entire staff.
After the mistake, she received a stern dressing down from her coach.
He was upset after getting a public dressing down at the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reprimand - an official rebuke or criticism
  • Synonym: scolding - angrily criticizing someone, especially for bad behavior

Etymology

From the verbal phrase 'dress down', meaning to reprimand; 'dress' in the sense of 'prepare, deal with'.
