spark plug

1. NOUN

/ spɑrk ˌplʌɡ / (AmE), / spɑːk ˌplʌɡ / (BrE)
A device fitted into the cylinder head of an internal-combustion engine that ignites the fuel-air mixture by means of an electric spark.
engine device that ignites fuel-air mixture

Examples

The mechanic replaced the old spark plug to improve the car’s performance.
A dirty spark plug can cause an engine to misfire or run poorly.
He learned how to clean and install a new spark plug during his automotive class.
