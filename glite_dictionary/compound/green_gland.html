green gland

1. NOUN

/ ɡrin ɡlænd / (AmE), / ɡriːn ɡlænd / (BrE)
a pair of excretory glands located near the base of the large antennae in some crustaceans
excretory organ in some crustaceans

Examples

The green gland in crayfish helps regulate water and salt balance in their bodies.
Scientists observed that the green gland was vital for removing waste from the lobster's system.
Damage to the green gland can disrupt the excretory process in many crustaceans.
