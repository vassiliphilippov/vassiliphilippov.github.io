superorder Acanthopterygii

1. NOUN

/ ˌsuː.pɚˈɔr.dɚ əˌkænθ.pə.təˈrɪdʒ.i.aɪ / (AmE), / ˌsuː.pəˈɔː.də əˌkanθ.pə.təˈrɪdʒ.i.aɪ / (BrE)
A large group of teleost fishes characterized by fins with sharp, bony rays.
group of fishes with spiny-finned rays

Examples

The superorder Acanthopterygii includes many familiar fish such as perch and tuna.
Scientists studied the evolutionary history of the superorder Acanthopterygii to understand fin development.
Most members of the superorder Acanthopterygii are known for their spiny dorsal fins.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Acanthopterygii - group of spiny-finned fishes

Etymology

From Greek 'akantha' (thorn, spine) and 'pteryx' (fin, wing), combined in taxonomic Latin.
