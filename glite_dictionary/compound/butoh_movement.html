butoh movement

1. NOUN

/ ˈbuːtoʊ ˈmuːvmənt / (AmE), / ˈbuːtəʊ ˈmuːvmənt / (BrE)
a form of avant-garde Japanese dance theater characterized by slow, controlled movements and experimental themes
avant-garde Japanese dance theater

Examples

The performance incorporated traditional elements with contemporary butoh movement to create a powerful statement.
Many artists have been inspired by the intensity of butoh movement in their own choreography.
She studied butoh movement in Tokyo before bringing its unique style to the local theater scene.

Synset Examples

The dance performance featured haunting butoh movements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: butoh - a style of Japanese dance theater with similar characteristics

Etymology

From Japanese 'butō' (dance) and English 'movement'.
