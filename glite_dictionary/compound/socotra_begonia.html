Socotra begonia

1. NOUN

/ soʊˈkoʊtrə bɪˈgoʊniə / (AmE), / səˈkəʊtrə bɪˈɡəʊniə / (BrE)
a semi-tuberous species of begonia with peltate leaves and rose-pink flowers, native to Yemen's Socotra Island
a begonia plant species from Socotra, Yemen

Examples

The Socotra begonia thrives in the unique climate of the island.
Botanists admire the unusual peltate leaves of the Socotra begonia.
Gardeners value the Socotra begonia for its showy, rose-pink flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: begonia - a genus of flowering plants with colorful flowers and foliage

Etymology

Named after Socotra Island (Yemen) and the genus Begonia.
