emergency brake

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈmɝːdʒənsi breɪk / (AmE), / ɪˈmɜːdʒənsi breɪk / (BrE)
a brake in a motor vehicle operated manually, typically used to keep the vehicle stationary when parked or in emergencies
manual brake for vehicle emergencies or parking

Examples

He pulled the emergency brake when the car started rolling down the hill.
Always set the emergency brake before leaving your vehicle parked on a slope.
The emergency brake failed during the safety inspection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parking brake - brake used to keep a parked vehicle stationary

2. NOUN

/ ɪˈmɝːdʒənsi breɪk / (AmE), / ɪˈmɜːdʒənsi breɪk / (BrE)
A brake in a vehicle that is operated by hand and usually functions through a mechanical linkage, used especially when the main brakes fail or to keep the vehicle stationary.
hand-operated vehicle brake

Examples

He pulled the emergency brake when the car started rolling down the hill.
Always set the emergency brake before leaving your vehicle parked on a slope.
The mechanic explained how to properly use the emergency brake in case of brake failure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parking brake - a brake used to prevent a parked vehicle from moving
