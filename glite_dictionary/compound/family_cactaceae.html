family Cactaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli kækˈteɪsiːˌiː / (AmE), / ˈfæməli kækˈtæsɪiː / (BrE)
the botanical family comprising the cacti, characterized by succulent stems and often spines, largely native to the Americas
botanical family of cacti

Examples

Many species within the family Cactaceae are adapted to survive in arid desert environments.
The botanical garden features an extensive collection from the family Cactaceae, including rare flowering varieties.
Research on drought resistance often focuses on plants in the family Cactaceae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cactus - a succulent plant with thick, fleshy stems

Etymology

From Latin 'Cactaceae', from 'cactus' (cactus plant) + '-aceae' (family suffix in botany).
