Lake Keuka

1. PROPN

/ leɪk ˈkjuːkə / (AmE), / leɪk ˈkjuːkə / (BrE)
A glacial lake in central New York State, distinguished as the only Y-shaped lake among the Finger Lakes.
Y-shaped glacial lake in New York State

Examples

Every summer, families gather to vacation at Lake Keuka and enjoy its scenic views.
The unique Y-shape of Lake Keuka sets it apart from the other Finger Lakes.
Boating and fishing are popular activities on Lake Keuka throughout the warmer months.

Etymology

From a Native American (Seneca) word meaning 'canoe landing' or 'lake with an elbow'.
