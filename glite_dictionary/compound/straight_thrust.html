straight thrust

1. NOUN

/ streɪt θrʌst / (AmE), / streɪt θrʌst / (BrE)
(in fencing) an attacking move in which the fencer lunges forward with one foot, keeping the back leg straight and the sword arm outstretched.
fencing attack with one foot forward, back leg straight

Examples

The fencer scored a point with a well-timed straight thrust to his opponent's chest.
She practiced her straight thrust repeatedly to improve her accuracy and speed.
A classic straight thrust is one of the most fundamental attacks taught to beginners.
