choroid coat

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔːrɔɪd koʊt / (AmE), / ˈkɔːrɔɪd kəʊt / (BrE)
a vascular layer of the eye situated between the retina and the sclera, containing dark pigment to reduce scattering of light inside the eye
vascular membrane in the eye
medical

Examples

The choroid coat supplies blood and nutrients to the outer layers of the retina.
Damage to the choroid coat can impair vision and cause eye disorders.
The dark pigment in the choroid coat helps to prevent light from scattering within the eye.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: choroid - vascular layer of the eye between retina and sclera

Etymology

From Greek 'choroeidēs' (resembling a membrane) and 'coat' (covering or layer).
