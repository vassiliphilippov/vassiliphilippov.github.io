death penalty

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛθ ˌpɛnəlti / (AmE), / ˈdɛθ ˌpɛnəlti / (BrE)
the legal punishment of death imposed by a court for a serious crime
execution as legal punishment

Examples

Many countries have abolished the death penalty due to concerns about human rights.
The jury found him guilty, but they decided not to recommend the death penalty.
Some people believe the death penalty serves as a deterrent to serious crimes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: capital punishment - legal infliction of death as a penalty for a crime
