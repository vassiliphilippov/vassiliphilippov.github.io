phonetic symbol

1. NOUN

/ fəˈnɛtɪk ˈsɪmbəl / (AmE), / fəˈnɛtɪk ˈsɪmbəl / (BrE)
a written character used in phonetic transcription to represent a particular speech sound
character representing a speech sound

Examples

Linguists use special phonetic symbols to record the precise pronunciation of words.
The International Phonetic Alphabet consists of many different phonetic symbols.
Learning the basic phonetic symbols helped her improve her English pronunciation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grapheme - a written symbol that represents a sound or phoneme
  • Degree Lower: phoneme - distinct unit of sound in a language
