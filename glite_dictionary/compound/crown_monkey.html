crown monkey

1. NOUN

/ kraʊn ˈmʌŋki / (AmE), / kraʊn ˈmʌŋki / (BrE)
An Indian macaque distinguished by a bonnet-like tuft of hair on its head.
Indian macaque with bonnet-shaped hair tuft

Examples

The crown monkey is easily recognized by the unique tuft of hair resembling a crown on its head.
During the safari, we saw a group of crown monkeys playing in the trees.
Researchers study the behavior of the crown monkey to better understand primate social structures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bonnet macaque - a macaque species found in southern India with a tuft or 'bonnet' of hair
