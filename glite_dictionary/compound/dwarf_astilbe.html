dwarf astilbe

1. NOUN

/ dwɔrf əˈstɪlbi / (AmE), / dwɔːf əˈstɪlbi / (BrE)
A mat-forming evergreen plant native to Asia, with finely divided leaves and small pink to burgundy flowers, often grown as ground cover in gardens.
mat-forming evergreen Asian ground cover plant

Examples

We planted dwarf astilbe along the garden path to create a dense, colorful ground cover.
The dwarf astilbe produces delicate ferns and tiny burgundy flowers in early summer.
Gardeners appreciate dwarf astilbe for its ability to thrive in shady, moist areas.

Etymology

From 'dwarf', describing its small size, and astilbe, from Greek 'a-' (without) and 'stilbe' (brightness), referring to the genus of flowering plants.
