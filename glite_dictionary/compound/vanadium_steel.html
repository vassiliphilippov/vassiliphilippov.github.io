vanadium steel

1. NOUN

/ vəˈneɪdiəm stil / (AmE), / vəˈneɪdiəm stiːl / (BrE)
a type of steel alloyed with vanadium to increase strength and stability at high temperatures
steel alloyed with vanadium for strength

Examples

The use of vanadium steel in tools provides greater resistance to wear and fatigue.
Engine parts made from vanadium steel can withstand higher operating temperatures.
Manufacturers often select vanadium steel for applications requiring exceptional strength.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alloy steel - steel made by adding other elements for specific properties

Etymology

From vanadium, a chemical element, and steel; 'vanadium' from Swedish Vanadis, a goddess name.
