Iris virginica

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪrɪs vərˈdʒɪnɪkə / (AmE), / ˈaɪrɪs vəˈdʒɪnɪkə / (BrE)
A species of iris with blue or violet flowers, native to the eastern United States, also known as the Southern blue flag.
a species of iris native to eastern US

Examples

The wetlands were filled with blooming Iris virginica in early summer.
Botanists observed that Iris virginica thrives in moist, open habitats.
Gardeners prize Iris virginica for its vivid blue flowers and easy maintenance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blue flag - a common name for similar North American irises

Etymology

From New Latin, combining genus name Iris and specific epithet virginica, meaning 'of Virginia'.
