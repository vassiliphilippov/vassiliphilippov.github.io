U.S. government

1. NOUN

/ ˌjuː ˈɛs ˈɡʌvərnmənt / (AmE), / ˌjuː ˈɛs ˈɡʌvənmənt / (BrE)
the federal government of the United States, consisting of the executive, legislative, and judicial branches
federal government of the United States

Examples

She works as an analyst for the U.S. government in Washington, D.C.
Debates about healthcare often involve disagreements over the role of the U.S. government in providing services.
