Tunisian dinar

1. NOUN

/ tuːˈnɪʒən dɪˈnɑr / (AmE), / tjuːˈnɪʒən dɪˈnɑː / (BrE)
the official currency and basic unit of money in Tunisia
currency of Tunisia

Examples

The price of the souvenir was twenty Tunisian dinars.
She exchanged her euros for Tunisian dinars at the airport.
The Tunisian dinar has a fixed exchange rate with several major foreign currencies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dinar - a unit of currency used in several countries, including Algeria and Jordan

Etymology

From 'Tunisian', referring to Tunisia, and Arabic 'dīnār', from Latin 'denarius', an ancient Roman coin.
