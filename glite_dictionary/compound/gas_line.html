gas line

1. NOUN

/ ɡæs laɪn / (AmE), / ɡæs laɪn / (BrE)
A pipe or system of pipes used to transport natural gas from one place to another.
pipeline for transporting natural gas

Examples

A leak in the gas line forced the evacuation of the building.
Before digging, you should always check for a gas line in the area.
The plumber repaired the cracked gas line behind the stove.

Synset Examples

the workmen broke through the gas line

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pipeline - a long pipe, typically underground, for conveying gas, oil, etc. over long distances

2. NOUN

/ ɡæs laɪn / (AmE), / ɡas laɪn / (BrE)
a pipe that carries gasoline from a fuel tank to a gasoline engine
pipe carrying gasoline to engine

Examples

The mechanic discovered a leak in the gas line that needed urgent repair.
He replaced the clogged gas line to restore the car's performance.
Always check the gas line for cracks before starting the engine after long storage.

Synset Examples

the car wouldn't start because dirt clogged the gas line

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fuel line - a pipe that carries fuel from a tank to an engine
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03406415-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ɡæs laɪn / (AmE), / ɡas laɪn / (BrE)
a line of vehicles waiting to buy gasoline, especially at a filling station during a fuel shortage
queue of vehicles for gasoline

Examples

During the fuel crisis, we had to wait in a gas line for over two hours.
The gas line stretched around the block as drivers tried to fill their tanks before the shortage worsened.
Local news reported on the frustration felt by those stuck in long gas lines last weekend.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: queue - a line of people or vehicles awaiting their turn
