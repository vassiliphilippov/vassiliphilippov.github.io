Spanish bayonet

1. NOUN

/ ˈspænɪʃ ˈbaɪənɛt / (AmE), / ˈspænɪʃ ˈbeɪənɪt / (BrE)
A tall yucca plant native to the southwestern United States and Mexico, characterized by a woody stem, stiff, sword-like pointed leaves, and large clusters of white flowers.
a tall yucca plant with sword-like leaves

Examples

The Spanish bayonet thrives in dry, sandy soils across the southwestern deserts.
Gardeners admire the Spanish bayonet for its striking appearance and resilience to drought.
She planted a Spanish bayonet near the fence for its sharp leaves as a natural barrier.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yucca - a genus of plants with stiff, sword-shaped leaves and white flowers

Etymology

Named for its sharp, bayonet-like leaves; 'Spanish' refers to its prevalence in regions of former Spanish territory.

2. NOUN

/ ˈspænɪʃ baɪˌɑnɪt / (AmE), / ˈspænɪʃ baɪˌɒnɪt / (BrE)
a stiff, short-trunked yucca plant native to the southern United States and tropical America, with rigid, spine-tipped leaves and clusters of white flowers
a spiky-leaved yucca plant of the Americas

Examples

The Spanish bayonet in our front yard blooms with clusters of large white flowers every spring.
Gardeners should be cautious when planting Spanish bayonet due to its sharp, stiff leaves.
During our hike in Texas, we saw several Spanish bayonet plants growing along the trail.
