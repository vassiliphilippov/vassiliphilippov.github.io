Colorado four o'clock

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːləˈrædoʊ fɔr əˈklɑːk / (AmE), / ˌkɒləˈrædəʊ fɔːr əˈklɒk / (BrE)
A wildflower with vibrant deep pink, tubular, evening-blooming flowers, native to sandy and desert areas from southern California to southern Colorado and into Mexico.
desert wildflower with deep pink evening blooms

Examples

The Colorado four o'clock produces striking pink flowers that bloom in the coolness of the evening.
We spotted several Colorado four o'clock plants while hiking through the sandy Mojave Desert.
Gardeners appreciate the Colorado four o'clock for its drought resistance and vibrant blossoms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: four o'clock - a plant of the Mirabilis genus, often blooming in late afternoon or evening
