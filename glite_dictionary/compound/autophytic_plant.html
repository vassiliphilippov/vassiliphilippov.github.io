autophytic plant

1. NOUN

/ ˌɔːtəˈfɪtɪk plænt / (AmE), / ˌɔːtəʊˈfɪtɪk plɑːnt / (BrE)
A plant capable of producing its own food from simple inorganic substances, typically through photosynthesis.
plant making its own food (photosynthesis)

Examples

An autophytic plant creates energy by converting sunlight, water, and carbon dioxide into sugars.
Unlike parasitic flora, the autophytic plant does not depend on other organisms for nutrition.
Most green plants are classified as autophytic plants because of their ability to photosynthesize.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: autotroph - an organism that produces its own food from inorganic substances
  • Antonym: heterotrophic plant - a plant that cannot synthesize its own food and relies on other sources

Etymology

From Greek auto- (self) and -phyte (plant).
