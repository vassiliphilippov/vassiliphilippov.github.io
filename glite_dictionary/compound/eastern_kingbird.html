eastern kingbird

1. NOUN

/ ˈistərn ˈkɪŋbɝd / (AmE), / ˈiːstən ˈkɪŋbɜːd / (BrE)
a North American kingbird species (Tyrannus tyrannus) known for its distinctive white-tipped tail and migratory habits
North American kingbird species

Examples

The eastern kingbird is easily identified by the bold white band at the tip of its tail.
I spotted an eastern kingbird perched on the fence, surveying the field for insects.
Every spring, the eastern kingbird returns to our region to nest and raise its young.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kingbird - a type of large flycatcher bird, genus Tyrannus
