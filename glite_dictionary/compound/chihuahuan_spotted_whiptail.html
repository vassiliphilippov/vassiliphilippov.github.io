Chihuahuan spotted whiptail

1. NOUN

/ tʃɪˈwɑwən ˈspɑtɪd ˈwɪptɛɪl / (AmE), / tʃɪˈwɑːwən ˈspɒtɪd ˈwɪpteɪl / (BrE)
a species of lizard (Aspidoscelis exsanguis) found in the southwestern United States and Mexico, recognized by its longitudinal stripes and light spots
a species of striped-and-spotted lizard

Examples

The Chihuahuan spotted whiptail is often seen darting quickly across rocky landscapes in southern New Mexico.
Researchers studied the diet of the Chihuahuan spotted whiptail to learn more about its role in the ecosystem.
We spotted a group of Chihuahuan spotted whiptails while hiking in the desert.

Etymology

From Chihuahuan, referring to the Chihuahuan Desert region, and whiptail, describing the lizard's long, slender tail.
