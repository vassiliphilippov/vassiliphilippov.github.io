two-toed sloth

1. NOUN

/ ˌtuː ˈtoʊd sloʊθ / (AmE), / ˌtuː ˈtəʊd sləʊθ / (BrE)
a sloth native to Central and South America with two long claws on each forefoot and three on each hindfoot.
Central/South American sloth with two forefoot claws

Examples

The two-toed sloth spends most of its life hanging upside down in the rainforest canopy.
Unlike its three-toed relative, the two-toed sloth is more active at night.
Researchers observed a two-toed sloth slowly making its way across a branch.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: three-toed sloth - a sloth species with three claws on each foot

2. NOUN

/ tuː toʊd sloʊθ / (AmE), / tuː təʊd slɒθ / (BrE)
A relatively small, fast-moving species of sloth with two long claws on each front foot, native to Central and South America.
species of sloth with two front claws

Examples

The two-toed sloth moved surprisingly quickly among the dense rainforest branches.
Unlike its cousin, the two-toed sloth is more active during the night.
Researchers observed a two-toed sloth hanging upside down while searching for leaves to eat.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: three-toed sloth - another species of sloth, has three toes on each front foot
