temperate rain forest

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛmpərət ˈreɪn ˌfɔrɪst / (AmE), / ˈtɛmpərət ˈreɪn ˌfɒrɪst / (BrE)
A type of rain forest that occurs in temperate regions, characterized by high rainfall and moderate temperatures.
rain forest in a temperate region

Examples

The Pacific Northwest is home to a temperate rain forest with lush, green vegetation year-round.
Scientists study the unique biodiversity found in a temperate rain forest.
A temperate rain forest typically receives more than 200 centimeters of rain annually.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: tropical rain forest - a rain forest occurring in tropical regions
