pagan religion

1. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪɡən rɪˈlɪdʒən / (AmE), / ˈpeɪɡən rɪˈlɪdʒən / (BrE)
any religion other than Christianity, Judaism, or Islam, especially those involving polytheism or nature worship.
a non-Abrahamic religion

Examples

Many ancient European cultures practiced some form of pagan religion.
The rituals of the pagan religion were often connected to the cycles of nature.
Scholars have studied how Christianity interacted with local pagan religion during the Middle Ages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polytheism - the belief in or worship of more than one god
  • Antonym: monotheism - the belief in only one God

Etymology

From Latin 'paganus' (country-dweller, heathen) and 'religio' (religion).
