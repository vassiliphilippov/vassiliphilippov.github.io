competition wallah

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːmpəˈtɪʃən ˈwɑːlə / (AmE), / ˌkɒmpɪˈtɪʃən ˈwɒlə / (BrE)
(historical, British-Indian) A person who secures a post, especially in the civil service, by means of competitive examination, or, more generally, someone who thrives in competitive business or trade.
person who succeeds through competition
archaic british

Examples

The competition wallah always knows the latest market trends.
As a competition wallah, she thrives on outsmarting her rivals.
During the British Raj, a competition wallah was someone who earned a civil service post through examination.

Synset Examples

Etymology

From 'competition' + Hindi/Urdu 'wallah' (meaning person involved with or associated with something).
