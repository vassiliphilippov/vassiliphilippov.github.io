panchromatic film

1. NOUN

/ ˌpæn.kɹoʊˈmæt.ɪk fɪlm / (AmE), / ˌpan.krəʊˈmat.ɪk fɪlm / (BrE)
Photographic film that is sensitive to light of all visible colors, including red.
film sensitive to all visible colors

Examples

Early photographers favored panchromatic film for its accurate color rendering in black-and-white prints.
Because panchromatic film captures a full spectrum of light, it is ideal for detailed landscape photography.
Many classic movies were shot on panchromatic film to achieve balanced grayscale tones.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: orthochromatic film - photographic film insensitive to red light

Etymology

From Greek 'pan' (all) + 'chrōma' (color), indicating sensitivity to all colors.
