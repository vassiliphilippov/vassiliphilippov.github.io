irrational motive

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌræʃənəl ˈmoʊtɪv / (AmE), / ɪˌræʃənəl ˈməʊtɪv / (BrE)
a reason or motivation for doing something that does not follow logical or reasonable thought
motivation inconsistent with logic or reason

Examples

Jealousy is often an irrational motive behind people's actions.
She acted out of an irrational motive, not considering the logical consequences.
Courts try to determine whether behavior is driven by an irrational motive or a reasonable cause.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: rational motive - a motivation consistent with logic or reason
