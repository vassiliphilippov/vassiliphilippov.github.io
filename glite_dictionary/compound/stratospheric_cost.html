stratospheric cost

1. NOUN

/ ˌstræt̬əˈsfɪrɪk kɑst / (AmE), / ˌstrætəˈsfɪərɪk kɒst / (BrE)
an extremely high or exorbitant cost
very high cost

Examples

The company refused to pay the stratospheric cost for the luxury event venue.
Buying a new car can come with a stratospheric cost these days.
Many families are leaving the city due to the stratospheric cost of housing.

Synset Examples

The costs of living here are stratospheric.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sky-high price - an extremely high price

Etymology

From 'stratospheric' (relating to the stratosphere), used figuratively for extreme height, and 'cost'.
