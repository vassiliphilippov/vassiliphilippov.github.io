genus Tussilago

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs tʌsɪˈleɪɡoʊ / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs tʌsɪˈleɪɡəʊ / (BrE)
A genus of low, creeping yellow-flowered perennial herbs of north temperate regions, known as coltsfoots; sometimes includes related species classified in other genera.
genus of perennial herbs (coltsfoots)

Examples

The genus Tussilago is best known for its only widespread species, coltsfoot.
Botanists sometimes debate whether certain plants should be included in the genus Tussilago or classified separately.
The flowers of the genus Tussilago appear before their leaves in early spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coltsfoot - a plant of the genus Tussilago, especially Tussilago farfara

Etymology

From Latin 'tussilago', the type genus, derived from 'tussis' (cough).
