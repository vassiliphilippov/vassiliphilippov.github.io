peregrine falcon

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛr.ə.ɡrɪn ˈfæl.kən / (AmE), / ˈpɛr.ɪ.ɡrɪn ˈfɔːl.kən / (BrE)
a large, swift species of falcon, Falco peregrinus, found worldwide and noted for its use in falconry
a large, swift falcon species

Examples

The peregrine falcon is renowned for its incredible diving speed when hunting prey.
Conservation efforts have helped the peregrine falcon recover from near extinction in many areas.
A peregrine falcon nested on the skyscraper's ledge in the city center.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: falcon - a bird of prey of the family Falconidae

Etymology

From Latin 'peregrinus' (foreigner, wanderer), referring to its wide migration.
