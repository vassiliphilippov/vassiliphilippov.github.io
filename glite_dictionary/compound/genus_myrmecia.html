genus Myrmecia

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒiːnəs mərˈmiːʃiə / (AmE), / ˌdʒiːnəs mɜːˈmiːsɪə / (BrE)
A genus of large, aggressive ants native to Australia, commonly known as bulldog ants, known for their powerful stings and strong mandibles.
a genus of large, aggressive Australian ants

Examples

The genus Myrmecia is known for species with extremely painful stings.
Scientists studying Australian fauna have documented over 90 species within the genus Myrmecia.
Members of the genus Myrmecia are among the most primitive living ants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bulldog ant - common name for ants of the genus Myrmecia

Etymology

From Greek 'myrmex' (ant), used in scientific Latin.
