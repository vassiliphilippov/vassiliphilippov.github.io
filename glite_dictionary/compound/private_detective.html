private detective

1. NOUN

/ ˈpraɪvət dɪˈtɛktɪv / (AmE), / ˈpraɪvət dɪˈtɛktɪv / (BrE)
a person not employed by the police who is hired to investigate crimes or find information, often working independently or for a private agency
independent investigator for hire

Examples

The private detective uncovered evidence that the police had missed.
She hired a private detective to follow her husband.
Many novels feature a hard-boiled private detective as the main character.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: detective - a person, especially a police officer, whose job is to investigate crimes and catch criminals
