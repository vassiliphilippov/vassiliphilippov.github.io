logical proof

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɑːdʒɪkəl pruːf / (AmE), / ˈlɒdʒɪkəl pruːf / (BrE)
a demonstration using logical reasoning to show that a theorem or statement is necessarily true
demonstration using logic to prove a statement

Examples

The mathematician presented a logical proof to establish the validity of the new theorem.
Constructing a logical proof often requires breaking down a problem into smaller, manageable steps.
Students learned how to write a logical proof during the introductory logic course.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: proof - evidence or argument establishing a fact or the truth of a statement
