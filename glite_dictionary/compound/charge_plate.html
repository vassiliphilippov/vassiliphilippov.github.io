charge plate

1. NOUN

/ tʃɑrdʒ pleɪt / (AmE), / tʃɑːdʒ pleɪt / (BrE)
an early form of credit card, typically a small metal or plastic plate, used to assure payment for goods and services
early metal or plastic credit card

Examples

In the 1950s, my grandmother paid for groceries with a charge plate issued by the department store.
Few people today remember using a charge plate before credit cards became widespread.
The old charge plate in the museum looked surprisingly modern for its time.

Synset Examples

do you take plastic?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: credit card - a plastic card used to make purchases by credit

Etymology

Formed from 'charge' (to bill for payment) and 'plate' (referring to the physical metal or plastic plate used).
