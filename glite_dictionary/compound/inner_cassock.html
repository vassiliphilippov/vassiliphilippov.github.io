inner cassock

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪnər ˈkæsək / (AmE), / ˈɪnə ˈkæsək / (BrE)
a long, collared garment reaching the feet, with narrow, tapered sleeves, worn by clergy, monastics, and sometimes male seminarians, especially in Eastern Orthodox and Eastern Catholic traditions
long clerical garment worn by clergy

Examples

The priest wore his inner cassock beneath the more ornate vestments during the service.
Monks are often seen in a plain black inner cassock as they perform their daily duties.
At the seminary, students were required to put on an inner cassock for morning prayers.

Synset Examples

The inner rason is the basic garment, and is worn at all times, even when working.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cassock - a long robe worn by clergy in many Christian traditions
