poor man's shilling

1. NOUN

/ pʊr mænz ˈʃɪlɪŋ / (AmE), / pɔː mænz ˈʃɪlɪŋ / (BrE)
a low-cost investment, especially one regarded as minimal or of lower quality
low-cost or minimal investment

Examples

Investing in old, small apartment buildings was considered a poor man's shilling by many experts.
Some see buying inexpensive collectibles as a poor man's shilling in hopes of future profit.
He made his start in finance with what he called his poor man's shilling: a handful of penny stocks.

Synset Examples

Investing in small startups can be seen as poor man's shilling.
In the stock market, penny stocks are considered poor man's shilling.
He compared his small investments to a poor man's shilling.
