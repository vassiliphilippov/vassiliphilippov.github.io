ocellated turkey

1. NOUN

/ oʊˈsɛl.eɪ.tɪd ˈtɝː.ki / (AmE), / əʊˈsɛl.ɪ.eɪ.tɪd ˈtɜː.ki / (BrE)
a wild turkey species native to Central America and northern South America, known for its iridescent plumage and eye-like spots on its tail feathers
wild turkey of Central/South America

Examples

The ocellated turkey is easily recognized by the vivid spots on its tail feathers.
During their trip to Guatemala, the group spotted a rare ocellated turkey in the dense forest.
Conservationists are working to protect the habitat of the endangered ocellated turkey.

Etymology

From Latin ocellatus, meaning 'having small eyes,' referring to the eye-like spots on its feathers.
