common apricot

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmən ˈæprɪˌkɑt / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən ˈæprɪkɒt / (BrE)
a small, deciduous tree of temperate regions, Prunus armeniaca, cultivated for its downy yellow or rosy edible fruits
temperate fruit tree, Prunus armeniaca

Examples

The common apricot is widely grown in orchards across southern Europe.
Farmers carefully selected the best common apricot trees for their fruit quality.
The leaves of the common apricot are broad and rounded, making them easy to identify.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apricot - the fruit of the apricot tree
