variable resistor

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɛriəbəl rɪˈzɪstər / (AmE), / ˈveəriəbl rɪˈzɪstə / (BrE)
an electronic component whose resistance can be adjusted to control the flow of electric current in a circuit
an adjustable electrical resistor

Examples

A variable resistor allows you to fine-tune the brightness of the lamp.
By adjusting the variable resistor, the technician controlled the speed of the motor.
The circuit diagram shows a variable resistor labeled as VR1.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: potentiometer - a type of variable resistor used to control voltage
  • Antonym: fixed resistor - a resistor with a resistance value that cannot be adjusted
