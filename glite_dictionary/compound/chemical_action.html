chemical action

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɛmɪkəl ˈækʃən / (AmE), / ˈkɛmɪkəl ˈækʃən / (BrE)
a process involving chemical change, determined by the atomic or molecular composition and structure of substances involved
process involving chemical change

Examples

The rusting of iron is a classic example of chemical action.
Acids and bases undergo chemical action when mixed, resulting in new compounds.
Cooking involves many types of chemical action that change the flavor and texture of food.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chemical reaction - a process in which substances interact to form new substances
