petty officer

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛt̬i ˌɑːfɪsər / (AmE), / ˈpɛti ˌɒfɪsə / (BrE)
A noncommissioned officer in the Navy or Coast Guard, equivalent in rank to a sergeant in the Army.
Navy/Coast Guard noncommissioned officer

Examples

After serving for eight years, she was promoted to petty officer in the Navy.
The petty officer organized the daily duties for the sailors on deck.
As a petty officer, he supervised training sessions for new recruits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sergeant - Noncommissioned army officer of similar rank to a petty officer

Etymology

From petty (meaning 'minor, subordinate') and officer, denoting a lower-ranking officer; in use since the 16th century.
