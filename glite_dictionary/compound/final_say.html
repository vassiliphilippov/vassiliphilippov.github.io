final say

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪnəl seɪ / (AmE), / ˈfaɪnəl seɪ / (BrE)
the authority to make the ultimate decision in a matter
the ultimate decision or authority

Examples

In most families, the parents have the final say over important rules.
The judge's decision is the final say in the case.
Even though the team discussed many ideas, the coach had the final say.

Synset Examples

He insisted that he should have the final say.
In the end, she made the final say on the project.
The manager has the final say on all hiring decisions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: last word - the final decision or statement in a matter
