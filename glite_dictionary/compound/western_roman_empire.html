Western Roman Empire

1. PROPN

/ ˈwɛstərn ˈroʊmən ˈɛmpaɪər / (AmE), / ˈwɛstən ˈrəʊmən ˈɛmpaɪə / (BrE)
the western half of the Roman Empire after its division in 395 AD, lasting until its fall in 476 AD
the western half of the Roman Empire

Examples

The collapse of the Western Roman Empire marked the end of ancient Rome.
Historians debate the reasons for the fall of the Western Roman Empire.
After the Western Roman Empire fell, the Middle Ages began in Europe.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Eastern Roman Empire - the eastern half of the Roman Empire after the division, also called the Byzantine Empire
