military capability

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɪlɪˌtɛri kəˈpeɪbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ˌmɪlɪtəˌri kəˈpeɪbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the ability of a country's armed forces in terms of personnel, equipment, and readiness to conduct military operations or fight a war
armed forces' capacity for military action

Examples

The country's military capability has increased significantly due to new investments in defense.
Assessing an enemy's military capability is crucial before entering a conflict.
Disagreements arose over the government's plan to expand its military capability.

Synset Examples

we faced an army of great strength
politicians have neglected our military posture

Related Terms

  • Synonym: military strength - the total power or effectiveness of a country's armed forces
  • Antonym: military weakness - the inadequate capability of a country's armed forces
