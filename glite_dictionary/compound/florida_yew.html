Florida yew

1. NOUN

/ flɔːˈrɪdə juː / (AmE), / fləˈrɪdə juː / (BrE)
a small, bushy yew tree native to northern Florida, characterized by spreading branches and very narrow leaves
small yew tree of northern Florida

Examples

The Florida yew is a rare species found only in a small region of northern Florida.
Because of habitat loss, the Florida yew is listed as an endangered plant.
Gardeners value the Florida yew for its attractive, slender leaves and dense branches.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yew - an evergreen tree or shrub with flat needle-like leaves
