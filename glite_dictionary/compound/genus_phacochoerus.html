genus Phacochoerus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˌfækəˈkwiːrəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˌfækəˈkwɪərəs / (BrE)
A genus of wild pigs native to Africa, known commonly as warthogs, characterized by their large facial warts and curved tusks.
warthog genus of wild pigs

Examples

The genus Phacochoerus consists of two species commonly known as warthogs.
Researchers have studied the behavior of animals within the genus Phacochoerus in their natural habitat.
Unlike other wild pigs, members of the genus Phacochoerus are adapted to dry savanna environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: warthog - common name for animals of genus Phacochoerus

Etymology

From Greek *phakos* (wart) + *choiros* (pig), alluding to the animal's appearance.
