gum dammar

1. NOUN

/ ɡʌm ˈdæmɑr / (AmE), / ɡʌm ˈdæmə / (BrE)
a hard, pale resin obtained from certain trees, especially the amboyna pine, used in varnishes, incense, and as a glazing agent
hard resin from certain tropical trees

Examples

The artist added a small amount of gum dammar to her oil paints for extra gloss.
Restorers often use gum dammar in traditional picture varnishes.
The fragrant smoke from burning gum dammar filled the air during the ceremony.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dammar - a resin from certain trees used in varnish and incense

Etymology

From Malay 'damar', meaning 'resin' or 'torch'.
