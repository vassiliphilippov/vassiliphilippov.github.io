pignut hickory

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪɡˌnʌt ˈhɪkəri / (AmE), / ˈpɪɡˌnʌt ˈhɪkəri / (BrE)
An American hickory tree (Carya glabra) with bitter, hard-shelled nuts.
species of American hickory tree

Examples

The forest was dominated by pignut hickory and oak trees.
Wildlife in the region often feed on the nuts of the pignut hickory.
Many parks feature mature pignut hickory trees known for their strong wood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shagbark hickory - American hickory tree with shaggy bark, Carya ovata

Etymology

The name refers to the nuts sometimes eaten by pigs; 'hickory' from an Algonquian word (possibly Powhatan 'pawcohiccora').
