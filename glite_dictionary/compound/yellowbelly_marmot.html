yellowbelly marmot

1. NOUN

/ ˈjɛloʊˌbɛli ˈmɑːrmət / (AmE), / ˈjɛləʊˌbɛli ˈmɑːmət / (BrE)
a heavy-bodied yellowish-brown marmot native to rocky areas of western North America
North American marmot species

Examples

The yellowbelly marmot is often seen basking on rocks in the mountainous regions of Colorado.
Researchers tracked the movements of several yellowbelly marmots to study their hibernation patterns.
Predators like coyotes and eagles frequently prey on the yellowbelly marmot in the wild.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marmot - a burrowing rodent related to squirrels
