cardiac muscle

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑr.di.æk ˈmʌs.əl / (AmE), / ˈkɑː.di.æk ˈmʌs.əl / (BrE)
the specialized muscle tissue found in the heart, adapted for continuous rhythmic contraction
the muscle tissue of the heart
medical

Examples

The cardiac muscle contracts automatically to pump blood throughout the body.
Damage to the cardiac muscle can occur during a heart attack.
Unlike other muscles, cardiac muscle cells are adapted for continuous rhythmic contraction.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: smooth muscle - muscle tissue found in walls of internal organs other than the heart
  • Antonym: skeletal muscle - muscle tissue attached to bones and responsible for voluntary movement

Etymology

From Latin 'cardiacus' (pertaining to the heart) and Latin 'musculus' (muscle).
