water nymph

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔtər nɪmf / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə nɪmf / (BrE)
(in Greek and Roman mythology) a female spirit or minor deity associated with bodies of water, such as rivers, lakes, or springs.
female mythological spirit of water

Examples

In the myth, a water nymph lived in the sparkling stream, enchanting travelers who passed by.
The artist painted a water nymph emerging gracefully from the lake at dawn.
Stories of water nymphs have inspired poets and writers for centuries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: naiad - a type of water nymph in Greek mythology

3. NOUN

/ ˈwɔtɚ nɪmf / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə nɪmf / (BrE)
(in mythology and literature) a female supernatural being or spirit associated with bodies of water, such as rivers, lakes, or springs.
female water spirit in mythology

Examples

In Greek myths, a water nymph might protect a sacred spring or lake.
The poet described the shimmering water nymph dancing on the riverbank at dawn.
Legends say that a water nymph can lure travelers into the depths with her enchanting song.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nymph - mythological female spirit of nature

Etymology

From Greek mythology, where 'nymph' (Greek 'nymphē') referred to a minor nature goddess, often associated with a particular natural feature.
