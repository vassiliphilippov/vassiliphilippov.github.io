masculine rhyme

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæskjəlɪn raɪm / (AmE), / ˈmæskjʊlɪn raɪm / (BrE)
a rhyme in which the repeated accented vowel sound is in the final syllable of the words, as in 'disdain' and 'complain'
poetic rhyme ending with stressed syllable

Examples

The poet uses masculine rhyme to create a forceful ending to each line.
Most English sonnets contain lines that end with masculine rhyme.
Words like 'plan' and 'fan' are examples of masculine rhyme in poetry.

Synset Examples

In English-language poetry, especially serious verse, masculine rhymes comprise a majority of all rhymes.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: feminine rhyme - a rhyme in which the repeated vowel is followed by one or more unstressed syllables

Etymology

From 'masculine' (relating to male), here meaning 'strong' in contrast to 'feminine rhyme'.
