Hakka dialect

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑːkə ˈdaɪəˌlɛkt / (AmE), / ˈhækə ˈdaɪəlɛkt / (BrE)
a Chinese dialect spoken in southeastern China and among the Hakka people in various regions
a Chinese dialect spoken by the Hakka

Examples

Many elderly people in the village still speak the Hakka dialect at home.
Linguists have studied the unique tones and vocabulary of the Hakka dialect.
He grew up learning both Mandarin and the Hakka dialect from his parents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mandarin - the most widely spoken Chinese dialect/language
  • Synonym: Cantonese - a major Chinese dialect spoken in Guangdong and Hong Kong

Etymology

From Hakka (a Chinese ethnolinguistic group) + dialect; 'Hakka' comes from the Chinese 客家 (Kèjiā), meaning 'guest families'.
