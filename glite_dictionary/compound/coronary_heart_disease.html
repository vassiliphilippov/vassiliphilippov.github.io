coronary heart disease

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔrəˌneri hɑrt dɪˈziz / (AmE), / ˈkɒrənəri hɑːt dɪˈziːz / (BrE)
A condition in which the arteries supplying blood to the heart become narrowed or blocked, leading to reduced blood flow and increased risk of heart attack.
disease of the heart's blood vessels
medical

Examples

After his diagnosis with coronary heart disease, he changed his diet and began exercising regularly.
Smoking is a major risk factor for developing coronary heart disease in adults.
The doctor explained that coronary heart disease occurs when the arteries to the heart become narrowed or blocked.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coronary artery disease - damage or disease in the heart's major blood vessels
  • Degree Higher: myocardial infarction - heart attack caused by blocked blood flow to the heart muscle
