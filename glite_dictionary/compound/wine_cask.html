wine cask

1. NOUN

/ waɪn kæsk / (AmE), / waɪn kɑːsk / (BrE)
a large barrel or container made of wood, used for storing or aging wine
barrel for storing wine

Examples

The chateau stores its best vintage in an old wine cask deep in the cellar.
After fermentation, the wine is transferred to a wine cask for aging.
They opened a massive wine cask during the harvest festival celebration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barrel - a large, cylindrical container
  • Synonym: wine barrel - a barrel specifically used for wine
