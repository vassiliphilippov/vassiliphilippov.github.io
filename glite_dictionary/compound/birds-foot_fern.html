bird's-foot fern

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɝdz ˌfʊt fɝn / (AmE), / ˈbɜːdz ˌfʊt fɜːn / (BrE)
A species of fern native to California and Baja California, having purple-brown leafstalks and resembling a bird's foot in shape.
fern species native to California and Baja California

Examples

We spotted a rare bird's-foot fern growing among the rocks during our hike.
The bird's-foot fern is easily recognized by its purple-brown leafstalks and divided leaves.
Botanists study the bird's-foot fern to understand how it adapts to the dry cliffs of California.
