jook joint

1. NOUN

/ dʒuːk dʒɔɪnt / (AmE), / dʒuːk dʒɔɪnt / (BrE)
a small, often rustic establishment in the southeastern United States offering food, drink, music, and dancing, typically featuring a jukebox
roadside US venue with food, drink, and music
american

Examples

The band played late into the night at the local jook joint.
During their road trip through Mississippi, they stopped at a famous jook joint for dinner and dancing.
Many blues musicians got their start performing at jook joints in the South.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: juke joint - small US bar with music, food, and dancing

Etymology

Variant of 'juke joint', from Gullah 'juke' (disorderly, wicked) of West African origin.
