piece rate

1. NOUN

/ pis reɪt / (AmE), / piːs reɪt / (BrE)
a fixed payment made for each unit of work produced or completed, rather than for hours worked
payment per item produced

Examples

Workers are often paid a piece rate to incentivize higher productivity.
The company switched from an hourly wage to a piece rate system last year.
Earning a piece rate means your pay depends on how many items you finish.

Synset Examples

Factories today may receive the label ‘sweatshop’ more because they have the long hours and poor working conditions, even if they pay an hourly or daily wage instead of a piece rate.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hourly wage - payment based on hours worked
