blue state

1. NOUN

/ bluː steɪt / (AmE), / bluː steɪt / (BrE)
(US politics) A U.S. state that predominantly supports or votes for the Democratic Party in elections.
U.S. state favoring Democrats
american

Examples

California has long been known as a blue state due to its voting patterns.
Many urban centers are located in blue states and tend to vote Democrat.
After the election, the map showed the Northeast as mostly blue states.

Synset Examples

The blue state trend continues in many urban areas.
New York consistently votes as a blue state.
California is often considered a blue state in elections.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: red state - U.S. state that typically votes for the Republican Party

Etymology

From the association of the color blue with the Democratic Party in U.S. political maps since the early 2000s.
