penny wedding

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛni ˈwɛdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpɛni ˈwɛdɪŋ / (BrE)
a simple and inexpensive wedding where guests contribute small amounts or modest gifts, often in a communal spirit
simple, inexpensive wedding

Examples

They couldn't afford a lavish ceremony, so they had a penny wedding attended by only close friends and family.
The tradition of the penny wedding was once common in rural Scottish communities.
Guests at a penny wedding often brought homemade food and small tokens instead of expensive gifts.

Synset Examples

At their penny wedding, guests brought small gifts.
They organized a penny wedding to keep costs low.
The couple opted for a penny wedding to celebrate modestly.

Etymology

From 'penny' (referring to a small sum of money) and 'wedding', describing the low-cost nature of the ceremony.
