to clean the floor

1. VERB

/ klin ðə flɔr / (AmE), / kliːn ðə flɔː / (BrE)
to remove dirt, dust, or stains from the floor, making it tidy and hygienic
remove dirt or stains from a floor

Examples

Please clean the floor after finishing the cooking to keep the kitchen tidy.
He forgot to clean the floor in his room, so it was still dusty when his parents visited.
After the dog tracked in mud, she had to clean the floor twice in one day.

Synset Examples

She cleaned the floor before the guests arrived.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wash the floor - wipe or scrub the floor using water or a cleaning solution
  • Degree Lower: to sweep the floor - remove dust or debris from the floor, usually with a broom
