cacodyl group

1. NOUN

/ ˈkækəˌdɪl ˌɡruːp / (AmE), / ˈkækədɪl ˌɡruːp / (BrE)
A univalent chemical group, (CH3)2As–, derived from cacodyl or arsine and found in various organoarsenic compounds.
univalent group (CH3)2As– in chemistry
medical

Examples

The cacodyl group is present in several organoarsenic compounds.
Chemists identified the cacodyl group as a key functional group in the reaction.
Research on the toxicity of arsenic compounds often considers the cacodyl group.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arsinyl group - the univalent group derived from arsine, formula: AsH2–

Etymology

From cacodyl (from Greek kakōdēs 'evil-smelling') and 'group' for chemical grouping.
