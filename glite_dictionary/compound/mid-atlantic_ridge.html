Mid-Atlantic Ridge

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɪd əˈtlæntɪk rɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌmɪd əˈtlæntɪk rɪdʒ / (BrE)
a long, continuous underwater mountain range in the Atlantic Ocean, extending from Iceland in the north to near Bouvet Island in the south
underwater mountain range in the Atlantic Ocean

Examples

The Mid-Atlantic Ridge is the longest mountain range on Earth, though most of it lies underwater.
Scientists have discovered new species living near the hydrothermal vents of the Mid-Atlantic Ridge.
The spreading of the plates at the Mid-Atlantic Ridge provides evidence for the theory of plate tectonics.
