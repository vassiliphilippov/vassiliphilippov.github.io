yellow bugle

1. NOUN

/ ˈjɛloʊ ˈbjuːɡəl / (AmE), / ˈjɛləʊ ˈbjuːɡl / (BrE)
a low-growing annual plant with yellow, red-dotted flowers, faintly aromatic of pine resin, native to Europe, the British Isles, and North Africa
low-growing plant with yellow, red-dotted flowers

Examples

Clusters of yellow bugle can be seen blooming along the woodland paths in spring.
The yellow bugle is known for its pine resin scent and attractive flowers.
Gardeners appreciate yellow bugle for its ability to thrive in shaded, moist habitats.
