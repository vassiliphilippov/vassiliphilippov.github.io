element of a cone

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛləmənt əv ə koʊn / (AmE), / ˈɛlɪmɛnt əv ə kəʊn / (BrE)
a straight line joining the apex of a cone to a point on its base
straight line from apex to base of a cone

Examples

In geometry, the length of an element of a cone is often called the slant height.
Each element of a cone is equal in length if the base is a circle.
The surface of the cone can be generated by rotating an element of a cone around its axis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slant height - the length measured along the side of a cone from apex to base
