case of crim. con.

1. NOUN

/ keɪs ʌv krɪm kɒn / (AmE), / keɪs əv krɪm kɒn / (BrE)
A legal action for adultery or infidelity, historically brought by a husband against a man alleged to have had sexual relations with his wife.
legal action for adultery (historical)
legal archaic british

Examples

The judge dismissed the case of crim. con. due to insufficient evidence.
In Victorian England, a case of crim. con. could ruin a family's reputation.
He filed a case of crim. con. against his wife's alleged lover.

Synset Examples

The couple's separation was due to a case of crim. con.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: criminal conversation - legal term for adultery under historical common law
  • Synonym: adultery - voluntary sexual intercourse between a married person and someone who is not their spouse

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'criminal conversation', a legal term from English common law.
