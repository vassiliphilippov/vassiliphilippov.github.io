rooting reflex

1. NOUN

/ ˈruːtɪŋ rɪˌflɛks / (AmE), / ˈruːtɪŋ rɪˌflɛks / (BrE)
a reflex action in newborn babies in which the head turns and the mouth makes sucking movements when the cheek or mouth is gently stroked
infant reflex turning head and sucking
medical

Examples

The rooting reflex helps newborns find the nipple when they are hungry.
If you touch a baby's cheek, the rooting reflex will cause them to turn toward your hand.
The pediatrician checked the infant's rooting reflex during the examination.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sucking reflex - a reflex in infants to suck on objects placed in the mouth

Etymology

From 'root', meaning to turn or search (as for food), and 'reflex', from Latin 'reflexus' (bent back).
