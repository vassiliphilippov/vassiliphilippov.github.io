to go awry

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ əˈraɪ / (AmE), / ɡəʊ əˈraɪ / (BrE)
to fail to happen as planned or expected; to go wrong
to go wrong or unexpectedly

Examples

Our holiday plans went awry when the airline lost our luggage.
The experiment went awry because an essential component was missing.
His speech went awry after he forgot his lines.

Synset Examples

When the project went awry, we had to regroup.
The picnic plans went awry due to the sudden rain.
She acted as an aborter when the plans went awry.
Her plans went awry, leaving her feeling like a cat in the pan.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to go wrong - to not proceed as planned or expected
  • Antonym: to succeed - to achieve a desired result or occur as planned

Etymology

From 'awry,' meaning 'away from the proper direction,' Middle English from Old English 'on wry.'
