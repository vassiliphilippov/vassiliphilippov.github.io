intravenous feeding

1. NOUN

/ ɪntrəˈvinəs ˈfidɪŋ / (AmE), / ɪntrəˈviːnəs ˈfiːdɪŋ / (BrE)
the administration of nutrients directly into a vein, usually when a person is unable to eat or absorb food normally
administration of nutrients via a vein
medical

Examples

The patient survived thanks to intravenous feeding when he was unable to swallow.
Many infants in intensive care rely on intravenous feeding for essential nutrients.
Doctors opted for intravenous feeding after complications from gastrointestinal surgery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parenteral nutrition - nutrition provided by means other than the digestive tract, often intravenously
