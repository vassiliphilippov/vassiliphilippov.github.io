Eton collar

1. NOUN

/ ˈiːtən ˈkɑlər / (AmE), / ˈiːtən ˈkɒlə / (BrE)
a broad, stiff white collar that lies flat over the lapels of a jacket, formerly worn by boys at Eton College and elsewhere
broad, flat white collar for jackets
british

Examples

The young boy wore an Eton collar with his navy blazer for the school portrait.
In Victorian England, the Eton collar was a symbol of upper-class education.
The costume designer added an Eton collar to the actor’s outfit to evoke the Edwardian era.

Etymology

Named after Eton College, a famous English boys' school, where it was part of the uniform.
