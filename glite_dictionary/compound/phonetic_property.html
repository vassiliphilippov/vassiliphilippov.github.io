phonetic property

1. NOUN

/ fəˈnɛtɪk ˈprɑpɚti / (AmE), / fəˈnɛtɪk ˈprɒpəti / (BrE)
An articulatory or acoustic characteristic of a speech sound, such as voicing, place of articulation, or pitch.
feature of speech sound

Examples

Voicing is an example of a phonetic property that distinguishes certain English consonants.
The phonetic property of aspiration affects how some sounds in English are pronounced.
Researchers analyzed each phonetic property to understand how children learn to differentiate speech sounds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phonetic feature - any distinctive characteristic of a speech sound used in phonology

Etymology

From Greek phōnētikos (relating to sound) + Middle English proprete (property), from Latin proprietas.
