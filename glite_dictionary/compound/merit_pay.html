merit pay

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛrɪt peɪ / (AmE), / ˈmɛrɪt peɪ / (BrE)
extra pay given to an employee based on job performance or achievement, especially in education.
extra pay based on performance

Examples

Many schools have introduced merit pay to reward high-performing teachers.
The company is considering merit pay as an incentive for increased productivity.
Debate continues over whether merit pay improves educational outcomes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: performance-related pay - pay system where compensation depends on measured job performance
