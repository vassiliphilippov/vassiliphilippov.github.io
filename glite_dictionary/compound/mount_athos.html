Mount Athos

1. PROPN

/ maʊnt ˈæθɒs / (AmE), / maʊnt ˈæθɒs / (BrE)
an autonomous peninsula in northeastern Greece, known for its many Greek Orthodox monasteries and spiritual heritage
autonomous monastic region in Greece

Examples

Pilgrims travel from all over the world to visit Mount Athos and its ancient monasteries.
Entry to Mount Athos is strictly regulated to preserve its religious traditions.
The spiritual communities of Mount Athos have existed for more than a thousand years.

Etymology

From Greek Ἄθως (Athos), the name of the mountain and peninsula.
