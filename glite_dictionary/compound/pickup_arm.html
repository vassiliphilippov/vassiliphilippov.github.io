pickup arm

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪkˌʌp ɑrm / (AmE), / ˈpɪkʌp ɑːm / (BrE)
the balanced, movable arm on a record player that holds the cartridge and stylus, allowing it to track the grooves of a disc
movable arm on turntable for cartridge/stylus

Examples

Be careful not to touch the pickup arm while the record is playing.
The pickup arm should be balanced correctly for the best sound quality.
He replaced the old pickup arm with a high-end model to improve playback.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tonearm - the arm on a turntable that supports the cartridge and stylus
