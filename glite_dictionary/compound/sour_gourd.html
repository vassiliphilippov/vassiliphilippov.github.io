sour gourd

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaʊɚ ɡɔrd / (AmE), / ˈsaʊə ɡɔːd / (BrE)
an African gourd-like fruit with edible sour-tasting pulp, used as food in some cultures
African fruit with edible sour pulp

Examples

The villagers gathered sour gourds from the fields for the evening meal.
She made a tangy sauce using the pulp of sour gourd.
Adding chopped sour gourd to the curry gave it a unique, tart flavor.

2. NOUN

/ saʊr ɡɔrd / (AmE), / saʊə ɡɔːd / (BrE)
an acid-tasting Australian gourd-like fruit with a woody rind and large seeds
Australian acidic gourd-like fruit

Examples

The sour gourd is often harvested in late summer when the fruit is fully ripe.
Local recipes sometimes include pickled sour gourd to add a tangy flavor.
Early settlers learned to crack open the hard shell of the sour gourd for its edible seeds.

3. NOUN

/ saʊɚ ɡɔrd / (AmE), / saʊə ɡɔːd / (BrE)
an Australian tree bearing an agreeable, acid-tasting fruit that resembles a gourd
Australian tree with acid fruit

Examples

The fruit of the sour gourd is often used in traditional Australian recipes.
Many birds are attracted to the sour gourd tree because of its acidic fruit.
Botanists studied the sour gourd to better understand its adaptation to the Australian climate.
