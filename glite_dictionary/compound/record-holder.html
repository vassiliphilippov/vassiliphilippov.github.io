record-holder

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛkərd ˌhoʊldər / (AmE), / ˈrɛkɔːd ˌhəʊldə / (BrE)
a person or organization who holds the best performance or achievement in a particular activity or category, as officially recognized
person holding an officially recognized record

Examples

She became the new record-holder after completing the marathon in under two hours.
The record-holder for the most Olympic gold medals is Michael Phelps.
As the record-holder, he was invited to appear on national television.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: champion - person who has won first place in a competition
