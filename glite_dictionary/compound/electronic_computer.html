electronic computer

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌlɛkˈtrɑnɪk kəmˈpjuːtɚ / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛkˈtrɒnɪk kəmˈpjuːtə / (BrE)
A machine that processes data and performs calculations automatically using electronic circuits.
automated data-processing machine

Examples

The first electronic computer was developed during World War II for codebreaking.
Modern society depends heavily on the processing power of the electronic computer.
She programmed the electronic computer to solve complex mathematical equations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: computer - A device for storing and processing data

Etymology

From 'electronic', relating to electrons or electronics, and 'computer', from Latin 'computare' (to calculate).
