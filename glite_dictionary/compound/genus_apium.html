genus Apium

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈeɪ.pi.əm / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈæp.i.əm / (BrE)
A genus of plants in the parsley family (Apiaceae), which includes celery and related species.
plant genus including celery

Examples

The genus Apium contains several edible and medicinal species.
Celery is perhaps the most well-known member of the genus Apium.
Botanists often study the genus Apium for its culinary and ecological importance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: celery - an edible plant of the species Apium graveolens

Etymology

From Latin *apium* (parsley or celery).
