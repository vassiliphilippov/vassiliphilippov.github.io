Darwin Award

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɑrwɪn əˌwɔrd / (AmE), / ˈdɑːwɪn əˌwɔːd / (BrE)
A tongue-in-cheek award given posthumously or humorously for exceptionally foolish actions resulting in death or serious injury, suggesting the recipient has removed themselves from the gene pool and thus contributed to human evolution.
humorous 'award' for fatal foolishness

Examples

After his ill-advised attempt to pet a wild lion, people joked that he deserved a Darwin Award.
Each year, bizarre accident stories are posted online as contenders for the Darwin Award.
She laughed when reading about someone who won a Darwin Award for trying to repair a live electrical wire with wet hands.

Synset Examples

He earned a Darwin Award for his reckless stunt.
Winning a Darwin Award is a dubious honor.
The news reported several Darwin Awards this year.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: ignominious - deserving or causing public disgrace or shame

Etymology

Named after Charles Darwin, from his theory of evolution by natural selection; originated in internet culture in the 1990s.
