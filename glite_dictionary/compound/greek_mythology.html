Greek mythology

1. NOUN

/ ɡrik mɪˈθɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ɡriːk mɪˈθɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the traditional stories and legends comprising the religious beliefs and practices of the ancient Greeks, often involving gods, heroes, and mythical creatures
myths and legends of ancient Greece

Examples

Many classic works of literature are inspired by Greek mythology.
The Olympian gods are central figures in Greek mythology.
She has always been fascinated by the stories of heroes and monsters in Greek mythology.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Roman mythology - the myths and legends of ancient Rome
  • Degree Lower: mythology - a collection of myths, especially those belonging to a particular religious or cultural tradition

2. NOUN

/ ɡrik mɪˈθɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ɡriːk mɪˈθɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the body of myths and legends belonging to ancient Greece, concerning their gods, heroes, and the nature of the world
myths and legends of ancient Greece

Examples

She developed an interest in Greek mythology after reading stories about Zeus and Athena.
Many modern books and films draw inspiration from Greek mythology.
The art museum has a special exhibit on Greek mythology this month.
