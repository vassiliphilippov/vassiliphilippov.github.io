wood engraving

1. NOUN

/ wʊd ɪnˈɡreɪvɪŋ / (AmE), / wʊd ɪnˈɡreɪvɪŋ / (BrE)
a print made by pressing paper onto an engraved wood block, especially used for illustrations in books and art.
print made from an engraved wood block

Examples

The artist created a beautiful wood engraving for the book's frontispiece.
I bought an antique wood engraving at the market last weekend.
Her collection includes several rare nineteenth-century wood engravings of landscapes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: woodcut - a print made from a design cut in a block of wood

2. NOUN

/ wʊd ɛnˈɡreɪvɪŋ / (AmE), / wʊd ɪnˈɡreɪvɪŋ / (BrE)
A print made from a design cut into the end grain of a block of wood, or the technique of producing such prints.
engraved print or block made from wood

Examples

The museum's collection features a rare wood engraving from the nineteenth century.
She learned the delicate technique of wood engraving during her art studies.
Each wood engraving captures intricate detail impossible to achieve with other methods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: etching - a print made by etching a design on a metal plate
  • Synonym: lithograph - a print made by lithography

Etymology

Engraving made from a wood block; 'wood' from Old English 'wudu', 'engraving' from French 'engravir'.
