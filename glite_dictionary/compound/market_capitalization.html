market capitalization

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑrkət ˌkæpɪtəlaɪˌzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈmɑːkɪt ˌkæpɪtələˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
The total value of a company's outstanding shares, calculated by multiplying the current share price by the number of shares.
total value of a company's shares

Examples

The company's market capitalization soared to over $50 billion after the successful product launch.
Many investors use market capitalization to classify companies as large-cap, mid-cap, or small-cap.
Recent fluctuations in stock price have significantly affected the firm's market capitalization.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: market cap - abbreviated form of market capitalization
