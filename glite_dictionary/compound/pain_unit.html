pain unit

1. NOUN

/ peɪn ˈjuː.nɪt / (AmE), / peɪn ˈjuː.nɪt / (BrE)
a unit used to measure the intensity of pain, often in medical or scientific contexts.
unit for measuring pain intensity
medical

Examples

The researchers used a standardized pain unit to compare results from different patients.
In some studies, ten pain units is considered the maximum possible discomfort.
Assigning a numeric value to a pain unit allows doctors to track a patient's progress objectively.
