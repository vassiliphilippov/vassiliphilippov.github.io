accessory hemiazygos vein

1. NOUN

/ əkˈsɛsəri ˌhɛmiˈæzɪˌɡɒs veɪn / (AmE), / əkˈsɛsəri ˌhɛmiˈæzɪɡɒs veɪn / (BrE)
a vein formed by the union of the 4th to 7th posterior intercostal veins, which empties into the azygos vein
vein in the thoracic cavity draining into the azygos vein
medical

Examples

The accessory hemiazygos vein is prominent in anatomical illustrations of the thoracic cavity.
Surgical procedures near the spine must take care to avoid injuring the accessory hemiazygos vein.
The doctor explained that the accessory hemiazygos vein drains blood from the posterior intercostal spaces.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: azygos vein - a vein running up the right side of the thoracic vertebral column draining into the superior vena cava

Etymology

From Latin 'accessorius' (additional) and Greek 'haima' (blood) + 'zygon' (yoke, joining).
