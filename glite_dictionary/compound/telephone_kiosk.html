telephone kiosk

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛləˌfoʊn ˈkiːˌɑsk / (AmE), / ˈtɛlɪfəʊn ˈkiːɒsk / (BrE)
a small building or booth where a public telephone is installed for people to use
booth for using a public telephone

Examples

He stepped into the telephone kiosk to make a quick call during the rain.
Red telephone kiosks are a well-known sight in British cities.
The telephone kiosk on the corner has not been used in years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phone booth - enclosed booth housing a public telephone

Etymology

From 'telephone' (from Greek 'tēle' meaning 'far' and 'phone' meaning 'voice') + 'kiosk' (from Turkish 'kiöşk', meaning 'pavilion').
