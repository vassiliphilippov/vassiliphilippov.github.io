sulcus lateralis cerebri

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌlkəs ləˈtɛɹi.ælɪs səˈriːbraɪ / (AmE), / ˈsʌlkəs ləˈtɪə.ɹælɪs səˈriːbriː / (BrE)
the deep groove in each cerebral hemisphere that separates the frontal lobe from the temporal lobe; also known as the lateral sulcus or Sylvian fissure
deep brain groove separating lobes
medical

Examples

The sulcus lateralis cerebri is a key landmark in brain anatomy, separating major lobes.
During surgery, care must be taken near the sulcus lateralis cerebri to prevent damage to surrounding tissue.
The MRI scan revealed an abnormality close to the sulcus lateralis cerebri on the patient's left hemisphere.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lateral sulcus - another common name for sulcus lateralis cerebri
  • Synonym: Sylvian fissure - another name for the lateral sulcus of the brain

Etymology

From Latin: 'sulcus' meaning 'groove', 'lateralis' meaning 'lateral', and 'cerebri' meaning 'of the brain'.
