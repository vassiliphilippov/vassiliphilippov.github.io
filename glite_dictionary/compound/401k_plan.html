401(k) plan

1. NOUN

/ fɔr oʊ wʌn keɪ plæn / (AmE), / fɔː ɔː wʌn keɪ plæn / (BrE)
A retirement savings plan in the United States where employees contribute a portion of their wages pre-tax, often with matching contributions from employers; funds grow tax-deferred until withdrawal.
US retirement savings plan
american

Examples

She started contributing to her 401(k) plan as soon as she began her first job.
Many companies offer a 401(k) plan with matching contributions to encourage employees to save for retirement.
When he changed jobs, he rolled over his 401(k) plan into an IRA.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: IRA - Individual Retirement Account; another type of US retirement savings plan

Etymology

Named after section 401(k) of the United States Internal Revenue Code.
