genus Pristis

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˈprɪstɪs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈprɪstɪs / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Pristidae, which comprises the sawfishes.
sawfish genus in Pristidae family

Examples

The genus Pristis includes several species of sawfish found in tropical and subtropical waters.
Conservationists are concerned about the critically endangered status of many members of the genus Pristis.
In scientific classification, genus Pristis is distinguished by its elongated, saw-like snout.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sawfish - a large tropical fish with a long, flattened snout edged with teeth

Etymology

From Latin 'genus' (kind) and Greek 'pristis' (sawfish).
