pink paper daisy

1. NOUN

/ pɪŋk ˈpeɪpɚ ˈdeɪzi / (AmE), / pɪŋk ˈpeɪpə ˈdeɪzi / (BrE)
a flowering plant of southwestern Australia, noted for its bright pink, papery, daisy-like flowers that are often grown for drying
Australian daisy with bright pink papery flowers

Examples

The pink paper daisy is a favorite among gardeners for its vibrant color and long-lasting blooms.
She arranged a bouquet using dried pink paper daisy flowers for decoration.
In southwestern Australia, the fields of pink paper daisy attract many visitors each spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: everlasting - a flower that retains its shape and color when dried, often used in dried floral arrangements
