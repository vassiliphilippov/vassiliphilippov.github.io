capulin tree

1. NOUN

/ kəˈpuːlɪn ˌtriː / (AmE), / kəˈpjuːlɪn ˌtriː / (BrE)
A Mexican black cherry tree (Prunus salicifolia) that bears edible fruit.
Mexican black cherry tree with edible fruit

Examples

The capulin tree thrives in the highlands of Central America.
Many locals harvest the small, dark cherries from the capulin tree in late summer.
A mature capulin tree can produce a plentiful crop of sweet, edible fruit each year.

Etymology

From Spanish 'capulín', the indigenous name for the cherry in Mexico.
