order Mysidacea

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdər ˌmaɪsɪˈdeɪsiə / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə ˌmʌɪsɪˈdeɪsɪə / (BrE)
a taxonomic order of small, shrimp-like crustaceans commonly known as opossum shrimps, found in marine and freshwater environments.
order of small shrimp-like crustaceans

Examples

Marine biologists discovered a new species belonging to the order Mysidacea in the deep sea trench.
The order Mysidacea is distinguished by the presence of a brood pouch in females.
Students examined specimens from the order Mysidacea under the microscope during their zoology class.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: opossum shrimp - common name for shrimp-like crustaceans of the order Mysidacea

Etymology

From modern Latin Mysidacea, based on genus Mysid- (from Greek mysis, a kind of shrimp).
