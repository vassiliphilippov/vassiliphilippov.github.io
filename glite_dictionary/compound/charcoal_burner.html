charcoal burner

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɑːrˌkoʊl ˈbɝːnər / (AmE), / ˈtʃɑːkəʊl ˈbɜːnə / (BrE)
a stove or device that burns charcoal as a source of heat or fuel
stove for burning charcoal

Examples

The old cottage was heated by a charcoal burner during the winter months.
She bought a portable charcoal burner for outdoor cooking at the campsite.
A traditional charcoal burner requires proper ventilation to ensure safety.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stove - an apparatus for heating or cooking

2. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɑːrkoʊl ˈbɜrnər / (AmE), / ˈtʃɑːkəʊl ˈbɜːnə / (BrE)
a person whose occupation is making charcoal, typically by burning wood in a controlled manner
person who makes charcoal as a job

Examples

The old forest was once home to a skilled charcoal burner who supplied fuel to the blacksmiths.
As a charcoal burner, he spent days watching over the slow-burning wood piles.
In history lessons, we learned how the charcoal burner played a vital role in early industrial economies.
