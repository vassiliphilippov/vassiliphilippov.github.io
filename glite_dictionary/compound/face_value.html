face value

1. NOUN

/ feɪs ˈvælju / (AmE), / feɪs ˈvæljuː / (BrE)
the nominal value shown on a security or bond, as stated by the issuer, not reflecting its market value
nominal value stated on a security or bond

Examples

The face value of the bond is $1,000, regardless of its current trading price.
Shares were sold at their face value rather than their market value.
The certificate's face value does not always reflect its true worth.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: market value - the price at which a security is traded in the market

2. NOUN

/ feɪs ˈvæl.juː / (AmE), / feɪs ˈvæl.juː / (BrE)
the apparent worth or value of something as stated or shown, not necessarily its true or real value
apparent or stated value

Examples

You shouldn't always take what politicians say at face value—consider their motives.
Although the ticket's face value was $50, it sold for much more on the resale market.
She accepted his promise at face value, but later realized he was not sincere.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nominal value - the value stated on the face of a financial instrument
  • Antonym: real value - the actual value, taking factors such as inflation or true worth into account

Etymology

From 'face' referring to the front shown, and 'value'. Originally from finance, denoting the value printed on coins, notes, or stamps.
