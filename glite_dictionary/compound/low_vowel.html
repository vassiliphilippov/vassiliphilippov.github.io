low vowel

1. NOUN

/ loʊ ˈvaʊəl / (AmE), / ləʊ ˈvaʊəl / (BrE)
A vowel pronounced with the tongue positioned as low as possible in the mouth, such as the 'a' in 'cat'.
vowel pronounced with low tongue position

Examples

In American English, the word 'cat' contains a low vowel sound.
Children often find it easier to distinguish a low vowel from a high vowel when learning to read.
The linguistics professor explained how the tongue drops for a low vowel.

Synset Examples

There is an addictive musicality to the language throughout the collection, with its combination of consonant-heavy Germanic monosyllables and the open vowels of Spanish.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: high vowel - a vowel pronounced with the tongue raised high in the mouth

Etymology

Formed from 'low' and 'vowel', referring to tongue position in phonetics.
