tinea capitis

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɪni.ə kəˈpɪtɪs / (AmE), / ˈtɪniːə kəˈpɪtɪs / (BrE)
a fungal infection of the scalp characterized by patches of hair loss and scaling
fungal scalp infection causing hair loss
medical

Examples

The child was diagnosed with tinea capitis after developing scaly bald spots on his scalp.
Proper hygiene can help prevent the spread of tinea capitis among schoolchildren.
Treatment for tinea capitis typically involves oral antifungal medications.

Etymology

From Latin tinea (worm, ringworm) + capitis (of the head).
