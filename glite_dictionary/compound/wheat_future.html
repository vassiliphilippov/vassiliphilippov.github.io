wheat future

1. NOUN

/ wiːt ˈfjʊtʃər / (AmE), / wiːt ˈfjuːtʃə / (BrE)
a contract to buy or sell wheat at an agreed price to be delivered at a specified future date
contract for future delivery of wheat

Examples

Many investors buy wheat futures to hedge against the risk of price fluctuations in the grain market.
The price of wheat futures rose sharply after reports of poor harvests abroad.
Traders monitor weather reports closely when dealing in wheat futures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: futures contract - an agreement to buy or sell an asset at a predetermined future date and price
