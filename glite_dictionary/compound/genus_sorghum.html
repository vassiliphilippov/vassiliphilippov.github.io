genus Sorghum

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˈsɔːrɡəm / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈsɔːɡəm / (BrE)
A genus of annual and perennial tropical and subtropical grasses that includes species cultivated for their cereal grains, known as sorghum.
a genus of cereal grasses including sorghum

Examples

Research on genus Sorghum has led to the development of drought-resistant crops.
Several species within the genus Sorghum are important for food and biofuel production.
The genus Sorghum is native to tropical and subtropical regions around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sorghum - a cereal grass and its edible grain

Etymology

From Latin 'genus' (kind, type) and modern Latin 'Sorghum', probably from Italian 'sorgo' (sorghum).
