economic strangulation

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈkɑnəmɪk ˌstræŋɡjəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪˈkɒnəmɪk ˌstræŋɡjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
The punishment or suppression of a group or nation by cutting off trade or commercial interactions with them.
cutting off trade to punish or suppress

Examples

The government threatened economic strangulation if the neighboring state did not comply with its demands.
The policy resulted in economic strangulation of the entire region, leading to severe hardship.
During the war, economic strangulation was used as a tool to weaken the enemy's resolve.

Synset Examples

the economic strangulation of the Jews by the Nazi Party

Related Terms

  • Synonym: embargo - an official ban on trade with a particular country
  • Synonym: blockade - an act of sealing off a place to prevent goods or people from entering or leaving
