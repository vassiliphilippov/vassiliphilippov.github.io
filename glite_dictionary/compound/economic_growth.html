economic growth

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛkəˈnɑmɪk ˈɡroʊθ, ˌikə- / (AmE), / ˌiːkəˈnɒmɪk ˈɡrəʊθ, ˌɛkə- / (BrE)
an increase in the amount of goods and services produced by an economy over time, leading to a rise in national income
increase in a country's economic output

Examples

Many governments prioritize economic growth to improve living standards for their citizens.
High levels of investment often lead to sustained economic growth over time.
Environmental policies sometimes conflict with rapid economic growth in developing countries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: economic development - the process of improving the economic well-being and quality of life of a nation or region
  • Antonym: recession - a significant decline in economic activity across the economy
