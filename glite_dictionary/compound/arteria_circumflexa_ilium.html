arteria circumflexa ilium

1. NOUN

/ ɑrˈtɪriə sɜrkəmˈflɛksə ˈɪliəm / (AmE), / ɑːˈtɪəriə ˌsɜːkəmˈflɛksə ˈɪliəm / (BrE)
an artery that supplies blood to the lower abdominal wall, skin, and the sartorius muscle
artery of the lower abdominal wall
medical

Examples

The arteria circumflexa ilium plays a crucial role in supplying blood to the abdominal wall.
Damage to the arteria circumflexa ilium can result in poor blood flow to the sartorius muscle.
Surgeons must be cautious not to sever the arteria circumflexa ilium during procedures involving the lower abdomen.

Etymology

From Latin: 'arteria' (artery), 'circumflexa' (bending around), 'ilium' (part of the pelvis).
