genus Phyllium

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs fɪˈliːəm / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs fɪˈliːəm / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Phylliidae, consisting of leaf insects known for their remarkable plant mimicry.
type genus of leaf insects

Examples

The genus Phyllium includes several species of leaf insects notable for their camouflage.
Researchers have discovered a new member of the genus Phyllium in Southeast Asia.
Members of the genus Phyllium are masters of mimicry, often mistaken for leaves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Phylliidae - the family of leaf insects

Etymology

From New Latin, from Greek phyllon (leaf), referencing the insects' leaf-like appearance.
