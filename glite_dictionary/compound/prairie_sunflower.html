prairie sunflower

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɛri ˈsʌnˌflaʊər / (AmE), / ˈpreəri ˈsʌnˌflaʊə / (BrE)
a species of sunflower with slender, usually branching stems, native to the central United States
a sunflower species native to central U.S.

Examples

The prairie sunflower blooms in late summer across the grasslands of Kansas.
Wildlife in the Midwest often feed on the seeds of the prairie sunflower.
Gardeners appreciate the prairie sunflower for its drought resistance and vibrant yellow petals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sunflower - a tall plant with a large yellow flower head
