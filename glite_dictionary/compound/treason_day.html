Treason Day

1. NOUN

/ ˈtriːzən deɪ / (AmE), / ˈtriːzən deɪ / (BrE)
a day set aside to commemorate acts of treason or significant historical political events, often marked by reflection, debate, or protest
commemorative day for acts of treason or political events

Examples

On Treason Day, many people attend public lectures about the country's revolutionary history.
The annual Treason Day parade draws crowds from all over the region.
Schoolchildren often perform plays reenacting historic events on Treason Day.

Synset Examples

On Treason Day, the nation reflects on its history.
The events of Treason Day are still debated today.
Treason Day is marked by protests and remembrance.
