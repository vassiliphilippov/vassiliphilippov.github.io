press box

1. NOUN

/ prɛs bɑks / (AmE), / prɛs bɒks / (BrE)
an area or enclosure at a sports stadium, arena, or similar venue that is reserved for reporters, broadcasters, and other members of the media covering the event
box or area reserved for reporters at an event

Examples

The reporters filed into the press box to cover the big game.
She waved to her colleagues in the press box before taking her seat.
From the press box, journalists had a perfect view of the field.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: media box - area reserved for the media at events
