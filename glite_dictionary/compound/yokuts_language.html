Yokuts language

1. NOUN

/ joʊˈkʌts ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / jəʊˈkʌts ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Penutian language traditionally spoken by the Yokuts people of the San Joaquin Valley, California
Penutian language of the Yokuts people

Examples

Few people still speak the Yokuts language in California today.
Scholars have compiled dictionaries to help preserve the Yokuts language.
The Yokuts language contains many words describing the natural environment of the San Joaquin Valley.

Etymology

From Yokuts, the ethnonym of the indigenous people, and 'language'.
