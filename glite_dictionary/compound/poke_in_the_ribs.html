to poke in the ribs

1. VERB

/ poʊk ɪn ðə rɪbz / (AmE), / pəʊk ɪn ðə rɪbz / (BrE)
to nudge or prod someone in the side of the body, usually as a playful gesture or to attract attention
playfully prod someone's side

Examples

He tried to get my attention by poking me in the ribs during the meeting.
Whenever she wanted him to laugh, she would poke him in the ribs.
Tom poked his friend in the ribs to signal it was time to leave.

Synset Examples

She decided to poke him in the ribs to wake him up.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nudge - to push someone gently, usually with an elbow, to attract attention
  • Synonym: prod - to poke someone or something with a finger or pointed object
