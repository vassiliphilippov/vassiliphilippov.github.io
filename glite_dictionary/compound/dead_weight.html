dead weight

1. NOUN

/ dɛd weɪt / (AmE), / dɛd weɪt / (BrE)
A heavy burden or responsibility that slows progress or is difficult to carry or support.
a heavy or oppressive burden

Examples

The outdated policies are a real dead weight on company growth.
She felt like a dead weight in the team after her injury.
Carrying unnecessary baggage can become a dead weight on your journey.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burden - something that is carried, a load or responsibility

2. NOUN

/ dɛd weɪt / (AmE), / dɛd weɪt / (BrE)
A heavy, motionless mass that is difficult to move or carry; something or someone that is a burden and does not contribute.
a heavy, motionless burden

Examples

He struggled to lift the dead weight of the fallen tree branch.
Carrying her brother felt like carrying a dead weight up the stairs.
The company saw him as a dead weight after years of little contribution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burden - something heavy that must be carried or endured

3. NOUN

/ dɛd weɪt / (AmE), / dɛd weɪt / (BrE)
A person or thing that makes progress difficult by being a burden or hindrance.
a heavy burden or hindrance

Examples

She felt like a dead weight during the marathon when she couldn't keep up with her friends.
The company struggled for years because of a dead weight in upper management.
Carrying unnecessary expenses has become a dead weight on the organization's finances.
