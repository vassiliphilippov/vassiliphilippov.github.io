cell organelle

1. NOUN

/ sɛl ɔːrɡəˈnɛl / (AmE), / sɛl ɔːɡəˈnɛl / (BrE)
a specialized structure within a cell that performs a specific function, similar to an organ in the body
specialized structure within a cell

Examples

The mitochondrion is a cell organelle responsible for producing energy in the form of ATP.
Under the microscope, each cell organelle can be identified by its unique shape and position.
Biologists discovered that damage to a cell organelle can affect the function of the whole cell.

Synset Examples

the first organelle to be identified was the nucleus

Related Terms

  • Synonym: organelle - a specialized structure within a living cell

Etymology

From Latin 'organum' (tool, instrument), with '-elle' as a diminutive, indicating 'small organ' within a cell.
