internal auditory artery

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtɜrnəl ɔːˈdɪtɔri ˈɑrˌtɛri / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɜːnəl ɔːˈdɪtəri ˈɑːtəri / (BrE)
an artery branching from the basilar artery that supplies blood to the inner ear (labyrinth)
artery supplying the inner ear
medical

Examples

The internal auditory artery can be visualized during advanced MRI scans of the brainstem region.
Compromise of the internal auditory artery may lead to hearing loss and balance disturbances.
During the operation, the surgeon carefully avoided the internal auditory artery to prevent damaging the patient's inner ear.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: basilar artery - major artery at the base of the brain
  • Synonym: labyrinthine artery - alternative name for the internal auditory artery
