maple syrup

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪpəl ˌsɪrəp / (AmE), / ˈmeɪpəl ˌsɪrəp / (BrE)
a sweet, thick syrup made by boiling down the sap of sugar maple trees, often used as a topping for food such as pancakes and waffles
sweet syrup from sugar maple sap

Examples

She poured maple syrup over her pancakes for breakfast.
The recipe calls for two tablespoons of maple syrup to sweeten the glaze.
Authentic maple syrup is produced in Canada and parts of the United States.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: honey - a sweet, sticky substance made by bees
  • Synonym: molasses - a thick, dark syrup produced from sugar cane or sugar beet

Etymology

From 'maple' (the type of tree) and 'syrup' (from Old French 'sirop')
