spastic abasia

1. NOUN

/ ˈspæstɪk əˈbeɪziə / (AmE), / ˈspæstɪk əˈbeɪzɪə / (BrE)
Inability to walk caused by spastic contractions of the leg muscles.
inability to walk due to muscle spasms
medical

Examples

The patient's diagnosis of spastic abasia explained his difficulty in walking.
After her stroke, she developed spastic abasia and needed physical therapy.
Neurologists observed symptoms consistent with spastic abasia during the examination.

Etymology

From Latin 'spasticus' (drawing, convulsive) and Greek 'abasis' (inability to walk).
