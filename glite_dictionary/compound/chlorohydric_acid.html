chlorohydric acid

1. NOUN

/ ˌklɔːroʊˈhaɪdrɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / ˌklɔːrəʊˈhaɪdrɪk ˈæsɪd / (BrE)
A strongly corrosive acid consisting of an aqueous solution of hydrogen chloride; also known as hydrochloric acid.
aqueous solution of hydrogen chloride (acid)
medical

Examples

Laboratory safety guidelines require careful handling of chlorohydric acid due to its highly corrosive nature.
Some industrial processes use diluted chlorohydric acid for cleaning metal surfaces.
Exposure to chlorohydric acid fumes can cause severe irritation of the respiratory tract.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hydrochloric acid - another name for chlorohydric acid
  • Degree Lower: sulfuric acid - a different strong mineral acid (H₂SO₄)

Etymology

From French 'chlorhydrique', based on 'chlore' (chlorine) and 'hydrique' (hydrogen).
