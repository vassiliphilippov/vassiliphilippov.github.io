genus Porphyrio

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs pɔːrˈfɪr.i.oʊ / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs pɔːˈfɪə.rɪ.əʊ / (BrE)
A genus of birds in the rail family, known as Old World purple gallinules, found in Africa, Asia, Australia, and Europe.
a genus of purple gallinule birds

Examples

The genus Porphyrio includes several colorful species known as swamphens.
Biologists observed a new breeding behavior among birds of the genus Porphyrio.
Many members of the genus Porphyrio inhabit wetlands throughout southern Europe and Asia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swamphen - common name for various species in the genus Porphyrio

Etymology

From Latin 'porphyrio' (swamphen), from Greek 'porphuríōn' (purple, violet).
