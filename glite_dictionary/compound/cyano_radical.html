cyano radical

1. NOUN

/ saɪˈænoʊ ˈrædɪkəl / (AmE), / saɪˈanəʊ ˈrædɪkəl / (BrE)
the univalent chemical group –CN found in many organic compounds, especially nitriles
the –CN (cyano) group in chemistry
medical

Examples

The cyano radical is highly reactive and present in many important organic reactions.
Exposure to compounds containing the cyano radical can be dangerous due to their toxicity.
Chemists often use the cyano radical to synthesize new pharmaceuticals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cyanide - a chemical compound containing the –CN group
  • Synonym: nitrile - an organic compound containing the –CN group

Etymology

From 'cyano-' meaning 'blue' (Greek 'kyanos') and 'radical' in the chemical sense of an atom or group of atoms.
