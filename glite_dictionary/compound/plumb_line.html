plumb line

1. NOUN

/ plʌm laɪn / (AmE), / plʌm laɪn / (BrE)
A cord with a metal weight attached to one end, used to find the vertical direction or establish a straight vertical line.
tool for finding the vertical line

Examples

The mason used a plumb line to ensure the wall was perfectly straight.
Before hanging the picture, she checked the alignment with a plumb line.
Engineers often rely on a plumb line to verify the true vertical at a construction site.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spirit level - tool for determining if a surface is horizontal or vertical

Etymology

From Latin 'plumbum' meaning 'lead', referring to the weight (traditionally made of lead) at the end of the cord.
