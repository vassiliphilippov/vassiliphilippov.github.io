dog grass

1. NOUN

/ dɔɡ ɡræs / (AmE), / dɒɡ ɡrɑːs / (BrE)
a European grass that spreads rapidly by creeping underground stems and is considered a weed in North America
a fast-spreading weedy European grass

Examples

Dog grass often invades gardens, choking out other desirable plants with its fast-growing roots.
Some farmers struggle to eradicate dog grass from their fields because of its persistent rhizomes.
The park's meadows were dotted with patches of dog grass thriving among the native flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: couch grass - Alternative common name for Elymus repens
  • Degree Lower: weed - An unwanted plant, typically in gardens or farms
  • Degree Higher: invasive species - A species that spreads widely and causes ecological harm
