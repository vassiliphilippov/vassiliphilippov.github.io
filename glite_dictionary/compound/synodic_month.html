synodic month

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈnɑdɪk mʌnθ / (AmE), / sɪˈnɒdɪk mʌnθ / (BrE)
the period between successive new moons, averaging about 29.5 days
lunar cycle between new moons

Examples

A synodic month is used to calculate the dates of many religious lunar calendars.
During each synodic month, the Moon completes its cycle of phases from new to full and back to new again.
The length of a synodic month is about 29.5 days, slightly longer than a sidereal month.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sidereal month - the time it takes the Moon to return to the same position among the stars

Etymology

From Greek 'synodos' (meeting, conjunction), referring to the alignment of the Sun and Moon.
