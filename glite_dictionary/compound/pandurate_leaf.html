pandurate leaf

1. NOUN

/ ˈpændjʊˌreɪt lif / (AmE), / ˈpændjʊˌreɪt liːf / (BrE)
A leaf that is shaped like a fiddle, with a broad upper and lower end and a constricted middle.
leaf shaped like a fiddle

Examples

The violet plant is known for its pandurate leaf that resembles the shape of a violin.
Botanists examined the pandurate leaf to distinguish this species from others in the same genus.
A pandurate leaf is a key identifying feature in some wildflower identification guides.

Etymology

From Latin 'panduratus' meaning 'fiddle-shaped'.
