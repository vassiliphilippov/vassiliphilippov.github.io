holding pattern

1. NOUN

/ ˈhoʊldɪŋ ˌpætɚn / (AmE), / ˈhəʊldɪŋ ˌpætn̩ / (BrE)
a flight path, typically a circular or oval route, that an aircraft maintains while waiting for permission to land
flight path aircraft follows while awaiting landing clearance

Examples

The plane entered a holding pattern above the airport due to heavy air traffic.
Fog forced several pilots to circle in a holding pattern until visibility improved.
After forty minutes in the holding pattern, the flight was finally cleared to land.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flight path - the route taken by an aircraft through the air

2. NOUN

/ ˈhoʊldɪŋ ˌpætərn / (AmE), / ˈhəʊldɪŋ ˌpætn / (BrE)
A state or situation in which no progress is made and no decisions are taken, often while waiting for something to happen.
state of inaction or waiting

Examples

After the merger talks stalled, the company entered a holding pattern for several months.
We've been in a holding pattern waiting for the permits to be approved.
Negotiations are in a holding pattern until both sides are ready to compromise.

Synset Examples

you should go into a holding pattern until he gets over his disappointment

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stalemate - a situation with no progress possible
  • Synonym: impasse - a situation in which no progress is possible

Etymology

Originally from aviation, where it refers to an aircraft flying in a set path while awaiting permission to land.

3. NOUN

/ ˈhoʊldɪŋ ˌpætərn / (AmE), / ˈhəʊldɪŋ ˌpætn̩ / (BrE)
A usually temporary state in which progress or action is delayed, especially while waiting for further instructions or developments; originally, a flight path that keeps an aircraft in the air while awaiting clearance to land.
a temporary pause in progress or flight path

Examples

The project remains in a holding pattern while management reviews the next steps.
Bad weather forced the flight into a holding pattern above the airport for forty minutes.
We're in a holding pattern until the client responds to our proposal.

Synset Examples

We’re in a holding pattern until the new policy is approved.
The plane entered a holding pattern due to bad weather.
The project is in a holding pattern until we secure funding.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pause - a temporary stop or break
  • Synonym: delay - the act of postponement or putting off
  • Antonym: advance - moving forward or making progress
