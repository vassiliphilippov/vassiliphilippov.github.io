family Elateridae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ɪˌlætəˈraɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli ɪˌlætəˈraɪdiː / (BrE)
a large family of beetles known as click beetles, some of which include certain fireflies
a beetle family: click beetles, some fireflies

Examples

The family Elateridae includes hundreds of species commonly referred to as click beetles.
Some members of the family Elateridae produce light and are mistaken for fireflies.
Scientists have studied the jumping mechanism unique to the family Elateridae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: click beetle - a beetle of the family Elateridae, noted for its clicking mechanism

Etymology

From New Latin 'Elateridae', based on Greek 'elatēr' (driver), used in taxonomy to describe click beetles.
