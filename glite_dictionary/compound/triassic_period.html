Triassic period

1. NOUN

/ traɪˈæsɪk ˈpɪriəd / (AmE), / traɪˈasɪk ˈpɪərɪəd / (BrE)
The geological period between about 252 and 201 million years ago, marked by the appearance of dinosaurs, marine reptiles, and significant volcanic activity.
early dinosaur era in Earth's history

Examples

The Triassic period marked the beginning of the age of dinosaurs on Earth.
During the Triassic period, Pangaea was the single large landmass covering our planet.
Many scientists study rocks and fossils from the Triassic period to understand prehistoric life.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Jurassic period - the geological period following the Triassic, known for dinosaur dominance
  • Degree Lower: Permian period - the geological period before the Triassic, ending with a mass extinction

Etymology

From Latin 'trias' (meaning 'triad', referring to three rock layers) and Greek 'periodos' (circuit, period).
