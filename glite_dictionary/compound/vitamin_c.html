vitamin C

1. NOUN

/ ˌvaɪtəmɪn ˈsi / (AmE), / ˌvɪtəmɪn ˈsi / (BrE)
a water-soluble vitamin found in various fruits and vegetables, essential for maintaining healthy skin, blood vessels, and immune function, and preventing scurvy
essential nutrient, also called ascorbic acid

Examples

Oranges are a great source of vitamin C and can help boost your immune system.
Doctors recommend getting enough vitamin C to prevent deficiencies like scurvy.
Many people take vitamin C supplements during cold and flu season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ascorbic acid - the chemical name for vitamin C

Etymology

Named using 'vitamin' (from Latin 'vita' meaning life and 'amine') plus 'C', designating its scientific classification.
