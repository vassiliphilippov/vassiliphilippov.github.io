Japanese yew

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˈpæniz juː / (AmE), / dʒəˈpænɪz juː / (BrE)
a shrubby, hardy evergreen tree or shrub native to China and Japan, with glossy dark green leaves, often cultivated as an ornamental plant
ornamental evergreen shrub or tree from East Asia

Examples

The Japanese yew is commonly planted in gardens for its dense foliage and attractive appearance.
Many landscapers recommend the Japanese yew for hedges because it is easy to shape and maintain.
Be careful when handling Japanese yew plants, as all parts are toxic if ingested.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yew - an evergreen tree or shrub with needle-like leaves and red berry-like fruits, genus Taxus
