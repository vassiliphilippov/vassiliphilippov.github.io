monoblastic leukemia

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɑːnəˈblæstɪk lʊˈkiːmiə / (AmE), / ˌmɒnəˈblæstɪk ljuːˈkiːmiə / (BrE)
A type of leukemia marked by the abnormal increase of monocytes and monoblasts in the blood.
leukemia with proliferation of monocytes and monoblasts
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with monoblastic leukemia after presenting with a high white blood cell count.
Treatment for monoblastic leukemia typically involves chemotherapy and frequent monitoring.
Researchers are studying new therapies to improve outcomes for those with monoblastic leukemia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leukemia - cancer of blood-forming tissues, hindering body's ability to fight infection

Etymology

From Greek 'mono-' (single), 'blastós' (germ/sprout), and 'leukaimia' (white blood, leukemia).
