electrical disturbance

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌlɛktrɪkəl dɪsˈtɝbəns / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛktrɪkəl dɪsˈtɜːb(ə)ns / (BrE)
unwanted electrical signals or noise, often produced by external sources, that interfere with the normal operation of electrical or electronic devices
unwanted signals interfering electrically

Examples

A sudden electrical disturbance caused the radio to emit static for several seconds.
Lightning strikes often create electrical disturbances that disrupt satellite communications.
Engineers are working to minimize electrical disturbance in sensitive laboratory equipment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interference - unwanted disturbance in a signal
  • Synonym: noise - unwanted random addition to a signal
