common booklouse

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmən ˈbʊkˌlaʊs / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən ˈbʊkˌlaʊs / (BrE)
a small insect, typically found in damp indoor areas, that feeds on mold and starchy materials such as book bindings
a small indoor insect that feeds on mold and starchy materials

Examples

The common booklouse thrives in damp, humid environments such as basements and old libraries.
If you spot a common booklouse in your books, it's a sign the area may be too moist.
Homeowners are often surprised to discover that the common booklouse can cause minor damage to wallpaper and stored papers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: booklouse - a small insect that feeds on mold and book bindings
