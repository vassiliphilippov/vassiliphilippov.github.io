battered sav

1. NOUN

/ ˈbætərd sæv / (AmE), / ˈbætəd sav / (BrE)
A sausage dipped in batter and deep-fried, typically sold in fish and chip shops.
battered and deep-fried sausage
british

Examples

I bought a battered sav from the chip shop for lunch.
She prefers her battered sav with plenty of tomato sauce.
At the fair, kids lined up to get a hot, crunchy battered sav.

Synset Examples

The battered sav was crispy and delicious.
He ordered a battered sav at the local chip shop.
After a long day, a battered sav hit the spot.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corndog - a sausage coated in cornmeal batter and deep-fried
  • Synonym: saveloy - a highly seasoned red sausage, used for making battered savs

Etymology

'Sav' is short for 'saveloy', a type of seasoned sausage; the term is chiefly used in Australia.
