zebra-tailed lizard

1. NOUN

/ ˈziːbrə teɪld ˈlɪzərd / (AmE), / ˈzɛbrə teɪld ˈlɪzəd / (BrE)
a swift lizard with a long, black-banded tail and long legs, found in the deserts of the United States and Mexico
desert lizard with black-banded tail

Examples

The zebra-tailed lizard darted across the hot desert sand, using its banded tail for balance.
Zoologists observed a group of zebra-tailed lizards basking on rocky outcrops in the Mojave Desert.
Despite the harsh midday heat, the zebra-tailed lizard was active and quick to evade predators.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lizard - a reptile with a long body and tail and movable eyelids
