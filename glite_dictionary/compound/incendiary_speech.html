incendiary speech

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈsɛnd.iˌɛri spiːtʃ / (AmE), / ɪnˈsɛn.di.ə.ri spiːtʃ / (BrE)
a speech intended to provoke strong feelings or reactions, often anger or unrest
speech meant to provoke strong reactions

Examples

The politician's incendiary speech rallied his supporters but angered many others in the audience.
Her incendiary speech at the rally led to heated debates across the country.
The CEO gave an incendiary speech that exposed controversial company practices.

Synset Examples

He delivered an incendiary speech that sparked outrage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: provocative speech - speech meant to provoke thought or reaction
  • Antonym: conciliatory speech - speech intended to calm or pacify

Etymology

From Latin 'incendīārius' (setting on fire), used metaphorically for words that stir up emotion.
