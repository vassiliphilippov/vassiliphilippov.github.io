family Rhamnaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˈræm.nəˌsiː.iː / (AmE), / ˈram.nəˌsiː.iː / (BrE)
a family of usually thorny trees and shrubs bearing drupaceous fruit, many of which have medicinal value
a plant family of thorny medicinal trees and shrubs

Examples

The family Rhamnaceae includes plants that are valued in traditional medicine.
Many shrubs belonging to the family Rhamnaceae are known for their thorny branches.
Botanists have identified several new species within the family Rhamnaceae in recent years.

Etymology

From modern Latin 'Rhamnaceae', from 'Rhamnus', a genus in the family, plus the suffix '-aceae' used for plant families.
