horse manure

1. NOUN

/ hɔrs məˈnʊr / (AmE), / hɔːs məˈnjʊə / (BrE)
Manure consisting of the excrement of horses, commonly used as a fertilizer for soil.
manure from horses used as fertilizer

Examples

Many gardeners prefer horse manure because it is less likely to burn plants than other fertilizers.
During spring, the farm smelled strongly of freshly spread horse manure.
Composted horse manure can significantly improve soil structure and fertility.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: manure - organic matter used as fertilizer in agriculture
