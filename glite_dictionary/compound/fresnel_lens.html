Fresnel lens

1. NOUN

/ frɛˈnɛl lɛnz / (AmE), / freɪˈnɛl lɛnz / (BrE)
a type of lens made up of a series of concentric rings, designed to be much thinner and lighter than a conventional lens of similar size, often used in lighthouses and projection systems
thin lens of concentric rings for focusing light

Examples

The lighthouse beacon used a powerful Fresnel lens to focus its light miles out to sea.
A Fresnel lens is often found in overhead projectors for its ability to concentrate light efficiently.
Photographers sometimes use a portable Fresnel lens to shape and intensify studio lighting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lens - a piece of glass or plastic used to focus or disperse light

Etymology

Named after French physicist Augustin-Jean Fresnel (1788–1827), who invented it.
