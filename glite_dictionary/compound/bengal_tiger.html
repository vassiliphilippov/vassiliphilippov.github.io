Bengal tiger

1. NOUN

/ bɛnˈɡɔl ˈtaɪɡɚ / (AmE), / bɛnˈɡɔːl ˈtaɪɡə / (BrE)
a large subspecies of tiger (Panthera tigris tigris) native to the Indian subcontinent, known for its distinctive orange fur with black stripes
subspecies of tiger native to South Asia

Examples

The Bengal tiger is the most numerous tiger subspecies, primarily found in India.
Conservation efforts have helped increase the Bengal tiger population in recent years.
A fully grown Bengal tiger can weigh up to 220 kilograms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tiger - a large wild cat species with a striped coat

Etymology

Named after Bengal, a region in South Asia where the subspecies is found.
