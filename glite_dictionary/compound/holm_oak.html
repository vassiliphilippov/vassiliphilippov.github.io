holm oak

1. NOUN

/ hoʊm oʊk / (AmE), / həʊm əʊk / (BrE)
An evergreen oak tree of southern Europe with leaves resembling those of holly, producing hard wood.
a Mediterranean evergreen oak tree

Examples

The holm oak is common in Mediterranean woodlands.
A grove of holm oak trees provided shade during our hike in Spain.
Craftsmen value holm oak for its exceptionally hard wood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oak - a large tree that bears acorns and has lobed deciduous leaves

Etymology

Holm from Old English 'holegn' (holly), referring to the resemblance of its leaves to holly.
