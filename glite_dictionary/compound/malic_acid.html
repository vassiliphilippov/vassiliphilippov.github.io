malic acid

1. NOUN

/ ˈmælɪk ˌæsɪd / (AmE), / ˈmælɪk ˌasɪd / (BrE)
an organic acid found in many fruits and used as a food additive to add sourness
organic acid in fruits, food additive

Examples

Green apples get their tart flavor from a high concentration of malic acid.
Malic acid is commonly added to candy to give it a sour taste.
During winemaking, malic acid can be converted into lactic acid to soften the wine's flavor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: citric acid - a weak organic acid found in citrus fruits
  • Synonym: tartaric acid - a dicarboxylic acid found in many plants, particularly grapes

Etymology

From Latin 'malum' (apple), as it was first isolated from apple juice.
