devil's cigar

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛvəlz sɪˈɡɑr / (AmE), / ˈdɛvəlz sɪˈɡɑː / (BrE)
An unusual fungus (Chorioactis geaster) found in Texas and Japan, noted for its cigar-shaped fruiting body that splits open to release spores.
cigar-shaped rare fungus

Examples

The rare devil's cigar is found only in Texas and parts of Japan.
Mycologists were excited to discover a devil's cigar near the riverbank last spring.
The unique splitting sound of the devil's cigar when it opens earned it its name.
