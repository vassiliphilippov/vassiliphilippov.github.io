cold-water flat

1. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊld ˌwɔtɚ flæt / (AmE), / ˈkəʊld ˌwɔːtə flæt / (BrE)
an apartment, especially in an older building, that lacks modern conveniences such as central heating or hot running water
apartment lacking modern amenities

Examples

They lived in a cold-water flat in the city for several years.
The old building contains several cold-water flats that have not been renovated.
Growing up in a cold-water flat shaped his understanding of hardship.

Etymology

From the practice of describing apartments by water supply; 'cold-water' indicating only cold tap water was available.
