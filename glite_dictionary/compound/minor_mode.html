minor mode

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪnɚ moʊd / (AmE), / ˈmaɪnə məʊd / (BrE)
A musical key or tonality based on a minor scale, typically having a sadder or more somber sound than major modes.
musical key based on a minor scale

Examples

The piece is written entirely in the minor mode, giving it a melancholic feel.
Many folk songs make use of the minor mode to convey deep emotion.
Switching from major to minor mode can dramatically alter the mood of a melody.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: major mode - a musical key based on the major scale
