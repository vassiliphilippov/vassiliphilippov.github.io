basket ash

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæskɪt æʃ / (AmE), / ˈbɑːskɪt æʃ / (BrE)
A vigorous North American tree with dark brown heavy wood, whose leaves turn gold in autumn; often used in basket-making.
North American ash tree, used in basketry

Examples

The basket ash is prized for its strong, flexible wood ideal for weaving baskets.
In autumn, the leaves of the basket ash turn a brilliant golden color along riverbanks.
Many Native American tribes traditionally used basket ash to craft sturdy baskets and tools.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: green ash - North American ash tree with green leaves, closely related species (Fraxinus pennsylvanica)

Etymology

Named for its use in basket-making; 'ash' from Old English 'æsc' (ash tree).
