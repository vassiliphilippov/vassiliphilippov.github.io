Tatahumara language

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɑːtə(h)ˈhuːmərə ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌtætə(h)ˈhuːmərə ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Uto-Aztecan language spoken by the Tatahumara people of northern Mexico
language of the Tatahumara people

Examples

Linguists have studied the Tatahumara language to understand its relationship to other Uto-Aztecan languages.
Many cultural traditions of the Tatahumara are preserved through the Tatahumara language.
Efforts are being made to document and revitalize the Tatahumara language for future generations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Tarahumara language - the Uto-Aztecan language of the Tarahumara people (variant spelling)

Etymology

From the name of the Tatahumara people, also known as the Tarahumara, and 'language'.
