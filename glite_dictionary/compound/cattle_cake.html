cattle cake

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæt̬l keɪk / (AmE), / ˈkætl keɪk / (BrE)
a concentrated artificial feed for cattle, typically processed and formed into solid blocks or cakes
concentrated block-form cattle feed

Examples

The farmer ordered several tons of cattle cake to supplement the herd's winter diet.
Feeding the cows with cattle cake improved their milk production considerably.
After harvest, the cattle cake was stored in a dry shed to keep it fresh.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cattle feed - food given to cattle, often including hay, silage, or compound feeds
