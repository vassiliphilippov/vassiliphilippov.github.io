glaucous bristlegrass

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡlaʊkəs ˈbrɪsəlˌɡræs / (AmE), / ˈɡlɔːkəs ˈbrɪsəlˌɡrɑːs / (BrE)
a common weedy grass with bristly seed heads, found widely in temperate regions
a common bristly temperate grass

Examples

Glaucous bristlegrass often takes over roadsides and disturbed areas throughout the summer.
Farmers try to control glaucous bristlegrass because it competes with crops for nutrients.
The bristly seed heads are a key feature in identifying glaucous bristlegrass in the field.

Etymology

From Latin 'glaucus' meaning bluish-green or gray, and 'bristlegrass' describing its bristly seed heads.
