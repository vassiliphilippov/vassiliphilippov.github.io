stinking iris

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɪŋkɪŋ ˈaɪrɪs / (AmE), / ˈstɪŋkɪŋ ˈaɪərɪs / (BrE)
a perennial plant with purple flowers and foul-smelling leaves, native to southern and western Europe and North Africa
iris species with smelly leaves

Examples

The stinking iris is often found growing in shaded woodlands in western Europe.
Gardeners may avoid planting stinking iris because of the unpleasant smell of its leaves.
Despite its name, the stinking iris produces attractive purple flowers in early summer.
