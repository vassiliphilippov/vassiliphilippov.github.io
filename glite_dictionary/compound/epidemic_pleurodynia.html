epidemic pleurodynia

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛpɪˈdɛmɪk pliːˌɔːrˈdaɪniə / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈdɛmɪk pljuːˌɔːrˈdɪnɪə / (BrE)
An acute infectious disease that occurs in outbreaks and is characterized by sudden attacks of chest pain.
acute infectious disease with chest pain
medical

Examples

The outbreak of epidemic pleurodynia in the community led to several hospital visits due to severe chest pain.
Doctors quickly identified the severe chest pains as symptoms of epidemic pleurodynia during the local epidemic.
Children are often more susceptible to epidemic pleurodynia, especially during seasonal increases in viral infections.

Etymology

From Greek 'pleura' (side, rib) and 'odynē' (pain), with 'epidemic' from Greek 'epi' (upon) + 'dēmos' (people).
