satin stitch

1. NOUN

/ ˈsætn̩ stɪtʃ / (AmE), / ˈsætɪn stɪtʃ / (BrE)
A flat embroidery stitch made by working closely spaced stitches side by side to cover a section of fabric, creating a smooth, satiny appearance.
embroidery stitch for smooth, satiny surfaces

Examples

She used a satin stitch to fill in the petals of the embroidered flower.
Carefully working the satin stitch, he achieved a flawless, glossy finish on the design.
The edges of the leaves were outlined in satin stitch for added texture.

Etymology

From 'satin' (the smooth, shiny fabric) and 'stitch' (a loop or link made with needle and thread).
