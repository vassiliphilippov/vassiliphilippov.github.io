genus Lanius

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˈleɪni.əs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈleɪnɪ.əs / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Laniidae, comprising the typical shrikes.
type genus of the shrikes (Laniidae)

Examples

The genus Lanius includes many species of shrikes found across Eurasia and Africa.
Scientists have recently identified a new species within the genus Lanius.
Members of the genus Lanius are known for their distinctive hooked bills and predatory behavior.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shrike - a medium-sized predatory songbird of the family Laniidae

Etymology

From Latin 'Lanius' meaning 'butcher', referring to the shrikes' feeding habits.
