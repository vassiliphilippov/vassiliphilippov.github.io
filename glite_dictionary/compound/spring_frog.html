spring frog

1. NOUN

/ sprɪŋ frɔɡ / (AmE), / sprɪŋ frɒɡ / (BrE)
a North American frog found in or near marshes and ponds, similar to the bullfrog
North American marsh-dwelling frog

Examples

We spotted a spring frog resting near the edge of the pond.
The croaking of the spring frog can be heard every year after the snow melts.
Many wildlife photographers come here to observe the rare spring frog.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bullfrog - a large American frog known for its loud call

2. NOUN

/ sprɪŋ frɑɡ / (AmE), / sprɪŋ frɒɡ / (BrE)
a common North American green or brownish frog, typically marked with white-edged dark oval spots
North American frog with dark spotted markings

Examples

We found a spring frog resting on a mossy log near the stream.
The white-edged spots helped us identify the amphibian as a spring frog.
Many children enjoy listening to the calls of the spring frog in the early evening.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pickerel frog - a North American frog with similar markings, often confused with the spring frog
