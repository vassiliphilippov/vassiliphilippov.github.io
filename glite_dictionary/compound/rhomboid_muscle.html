rhomboid muscle

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɑːm.bɔɪd ˈmʌs.əl / (AmE), / ˈrɒm.bɔɪd ˈmʌs.əl / (BrE)
A muscle of the upper back, located between the spine and the shoulder blade, that helps move and stabilize the scapula.
upper back muscle that moves the shoulder blade
medical

Examples

Physical therapists often target the rhomboid muscle to relieve upper back pain.
Injury to the rhomboid muscle can cause discomfort when moving the shoulders.
Strengthening your rhomboid muscle may improve posture and reduce the risk of shoulder problems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scapula - shoulder blade bone

Etymology

From Greek *rhombos* (an equilateral parallelogram) due to the muscle's shape.
