concerto grosso

1. NOUN

/ kənˌtʃɛrtoʊ ˈɡroʊsoʊ / (AmE), / kənˌtʃɛːtəʊ ˈɡrɒsəʊ / (BrE)
A Baroque musical composition for an orchestra and a small group of solo instruments, typically featuring contrast between the groups.
Baroque composition for orchestra and solo group

Examples

The orchestra performed a famous concerto grosso by Handel to open the concert.
A concerto grosso typically features a small group of soloists set against the full ensemble.
During the Baroque period, composers like Corelli and Bach wrote many concerto grosso pieces.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: concerto - musical composition for solo instrument(s) and orchestra

Etymology

Italian, literally 'big concerto', from 'concerto' (concert) and 'grosso' (big).
