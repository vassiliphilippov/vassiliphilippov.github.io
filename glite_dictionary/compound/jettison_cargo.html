to jettison cargo

1. VERB

/ ˈdʒɛt.ɪ.sən ˈkɑr.goʊ / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛt.ɪ.sən ˈkɑː.ɡəʊ / (BrE)
to throw goods overboard or out of a vehicle, especially to lighten the load in an emergency
throw goods away to lighten a load

Examples

During the storm, the captain had to jettison cargo to keep the ship afloat.
The crew decided to jettison cargo after the engine failure to reduce weight.
If the plane is overloaded, we may need to jettison cargo for a safe landing.

Synset Examples

The crew had to jettison cargo during the emergency landing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to jettison - to throw or drop something from a ship or aircraft

Etymology

From 'jettison', from Middle English, via Old French 'getteson', from Latin 'iactatio' (act of throwing), combined with 'cargo'.
