judicial principle

1. NOUN

/ dʒuˈdɪʃəl ˈprɪnsəpəl / (AmE), / dʒuˈdɪʃəl ˈprɪnsɪpl / (BrE)
A fundamental rule or norm that forms the basis for the development of legal decisions and jurisprudence.
fundamental rule underlying legal judgments
legal

Examples

The case was decided according to an established judicial principle regarding fair treatment.
One important judicial principle is the presumption of innocence in criminal law.
The Supreme Court elaborated on the judicial principle of equal protection under the law.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: legal principle - a basic rule or doctrine that guides the law

Etymology

From Latin 'judicialis' (of judgment, judicial) and 'principium' (beginning, foundation).
