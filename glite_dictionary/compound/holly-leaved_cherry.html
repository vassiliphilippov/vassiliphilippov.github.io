holly-leaved cherry

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑli livd ˈt͡ʃɛri / (AmE), / ˈhɒli liːvd ˈtʃɛri / (BrE)
A California evergreen wild shrub or small tree (Prunus ilicifolia) with spiny, leathery leaves and white flowers.
California wild shrub with spiny leaves

Examples

The holly-leaved cherry thrives in the chaparral regions of California and provides food for local wildlife.
Its dense foliage makes the holly-leaved cherry a popular choice for natural hedges.
Native Americans traditionally used the fruit of the holly-leaved cherry in their diet.
