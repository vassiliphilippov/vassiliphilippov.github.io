universal solvent

1. NOUN

/ juːˈnɪvɚsəl ˈsɑlvənt / (AmE), / juːˈnɪvəsəl ˈsɒlvənt / (BrE)
a hypothetical substance capable of dissolving all other substances, originally sought by alchemists
hypothetical solvent dissolving anything

Examples

Medieval alchemists spent centuries in pursuit of the universal solvent.
The concept of a universal solvent is more a philosophical curiosity than a scientific possibility.
If a universal solvent truly existed, it would be impossible to contain it in any vessel.

Etymology

From Latin 'universalis' (universal) + 'solvens' (solvent).
