tall story

1. NOUN

/ tɔl ˈstɔri / (AmE), / tɔːl ˈstɔːri / (BrE)
an exaggerated or unlikely story that is difficult to believe
exaggerated or unlikely tale

Examples

He told a tall story about single-handedly fighting off a bear in the woods.
Her grandfather's tall stories always made the children laugh and wonder.
Everyone thought it was just a tall story until the evidence proved him right.

Synset Examples

She loves sharing her tall story from childhood.
That sounds like a tall story to me.
He told a tall story about his travels.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tall tale - a story that is very exaggerated and hard to believe
  • Antonym: truth - the true facts or reality of a situation
