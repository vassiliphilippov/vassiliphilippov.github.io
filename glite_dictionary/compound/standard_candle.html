standard candle

1. NOUN

/ ˈstændɚd ˈkændl̩ / (AmE), / ˈstændəd ˈkændl / (BrE)
a unit of luminous intensity equal to 1/60 of the luminous intensity per square centimeter of a black body at 2,046 degrees Kelvin, used under the Systeme International d'Unites
unit of luminous intensity (physics)

Examples

The brightness of the lamp was once measured using a standard candle as a reference.
In early photometry, a standard candle defined the unit for luminous intensity.
Scientists replaced the traditional standard candle with more precise measurements for defining the candela.
