SWAT squad

1. NOUN

/ swɑt skwɑd / (AmE), / swɒt skwɒd / (BrE)
A specialized unit of police officers trained to handle violent and dangerous situations, often involving firearms or hostage crises.
specialized police tactical unit

Examples

The SWAT squad was called in to handle the armed standoff.
When the situation escalated, a SWAT squad arrived to negotiate with the suspect.
Our city recently established a new SWAT squad to respond to high-risk incidents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: SWAT team - A police unit with special training and equipment for dangerous operations; sometimes used interchangeably with 'SWAT squad'.

Etymology

Acronym SWAT from 'Special Weapons and Tactics', developed in the United States in the 1960s.
