bird of paradise

1. NOUN

/ bɝd əv ˈpærəˌdaɪs / (AmE), / bɜːd əv ˈpærədaɪs / (BrE)
a tropical ornamental plant with striking orange and blue flowers that resemble the shape of a bird, native to South Africa and South America
tropical plant with bird-like flowers

Examples

The bird of paradise is prized for its brightly colored flowers that resemble a tropical bird.
She planted a bird of paradise in her garden to add a touch of the exotic.
The vibrant bird of paradise attracted visitors at the botanical garden.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strelitzia - the genus of plants known as bird of paradise

Etymology

So named because the flower's shape and color resemble an exotic bird.

2. NOUN

/ bɝd əv ˈpærəˌdaɪs / (AmE), / bɜːd əv ˈpærədaɪs / (BrE)
a brilliantly colored bird native to New Guinea and nearby islands, noted for its striking plumage and elaborate courtship displays
brightly colored New Guinea bird

Examples

The male bird of paradise flashed its brilliant feathers during an elaborate courtship display.
Some species of bird of paradise are threatened by habitat loss in New Guinea's forests.
Photographers travel from around the world to capture the beauty of the bird of paradise.

Etymology

Named for their spectacular appearance, comparable to something from paradise.

3. NOUN

/ ˈbɝːd əv ˈpærəˌdaɪs / (AmE), / ˈbɜːd əv ˈpærədaɪs / (BrE)
a tropical flowering plant with bright orange or red flowers, sometimes classified in the genus Poinciana
tropical flowering plant with bright flowers

Examples

The vivid colors of the bird of paradise make it a popular choice in tropical gardens.
She planted a bird of paradise near the patio for its striking blooms.
Many florists use the bird of paradise in their exotic floral arrangements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Strelitzia - a genus of plants commonly known as bird of paradise

Etymology

Named for its resemblance to the plumage of the bird-of-paradise.
