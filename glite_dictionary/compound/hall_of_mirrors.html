hall of mirrors

1. NOUN

/ hɔl əv ˈmɪrɚz / (AmE), / hɔːl əv ˈmɪərəz / (BrE)
a room or structure filled with mirrors arranged so as to distort reflections, often found in amusement parks or funhouses
room filled with distorting mirrors

Examples

The children got lost in the hall of mirrors during their first trip to the amusement park.
We laughed at our funny shapes inside the hall of mirrors at the carnival.
Navigating the hall of mirrors was more challenging than we expected.

Synset Examples

The funhouse featured a hall of mirrors that confused everyone.
