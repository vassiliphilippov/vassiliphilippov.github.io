wild chervil

1. NOUN

/ waɪld ˈtʃɜrvɪl / (AmE), / waɪld ˈtʃɜːvɪl / (BrE)
a tall, coarse Eurasian biennial herb (Anthriscus sylvestris), with fern-like leaves and umbrella-shaped clusters of small white flowers, now widely naturalized as a weed in parts of North America.
a Eurasian weed with fern-like leaves

Examples

Wild chervil can quickly invade fields and roadsides if left unchecked.
The tall stems and white flower clusters of wild chervil make it easy to identify.
Many gardeners struggle to remove wild chervil from their lawns due to its fast growth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chervil - a delicate annual herb (Anthriscus cerefolium) used as a culinary herb
