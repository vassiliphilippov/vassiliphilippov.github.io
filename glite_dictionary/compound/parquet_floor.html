parquet floor

1. NOUN

/ pɑːrˈkeɪt flɔːr / (AmE), / ˈpɑː.keɪ flɔː / (BrE)
A floor made of small pieces of wood arranged in a decorative and geometric pattern.
floor of patterned wood pieces

Examples

The dining room featured a beautiful parquet floor with intricate designs.
After years of wear, the parquet floor was polished and restored to its original shine.
She admired the way the sunlight highlighted the pattern of the parquet floor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parquetry - decorative wooden flooring technique

Etymology

From French 'parquet', diminutive of 'parc' (enclosure) + English 'floor'.
