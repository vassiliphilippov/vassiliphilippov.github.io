Grand Inquisitor

1. NOUN

/ ɡrænd ɪnˈkwɪzɪtər / (AmE), / ɡrænd ɪnˈkwɪzɪtə / (BrE)
the chief official in charge of the Inquisition, especially in Spain or Portugal
chief official of the Inquisition

Examples

The Grand Inquisitor wielded immense power during the Spanish Inquisition.
As Grand Inquisitor, he oversaw the trials of suspected heretics.
The history books describe the fear inspired by the Grand Inquisitor in medieval Portugal.

Etymology

From grand (meaning 'great') + inquisitor (from Latin *inquisitor*, 'examiner').
