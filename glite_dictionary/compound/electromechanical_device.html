electromechanical device

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌlɛktroʊməˈkænɪkəl dɪˈvaɪs / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛktrəʊməˈkænɪkəl dɪˈvʌɪs / (BrE)
A mechanism or machine that operates using both electrical and mechanical components.
device operated by electricity and mechanical parts

Examples

The elevator is an example of an electromechanical device used in everyday life.
An electromechanical device can convert electrical energy into mechanical motion.
Engineers are developing new electromechanical devices for use in robotics.

Etymology

Formed from 'electro-', meaning electricity, and 'mechanical', from Greek 'mekhane' (machine), plus 'device'.
