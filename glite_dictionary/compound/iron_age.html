Iron Age

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪ.ɚn eɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈaɪ.ən eɪdʒ / (BrE)
the period in human history following the Bronze Age, marked by the widespread use of iron for tools and weapons
historical period when iron was in widespread use

Examples

Many ancient civilizations reached new heights during the Iron Age.
Archaeologists discovered iron tools dating back to the early Iron Age.
The transition to the Iron Age marked a major advance in human technology.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Bronze Age - the period before the Iron Age, characterized by the use of bronze tools
  • Degree Lower: Stone Age - the earliest period of human culture, marked by the use of stone tools

Etymology

Named for the predominant use of iron during this era, following the Stone Age and Bronze Age.

2. NOUN

/ ˈaɪərn eɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈaɪən eɪdʒ / (BrE)
In classical mythology, the last and most corrupt age of the world, marked by decline and suffering.
last, worst age in classical mythology

Examples

According to Ovid, humanity entered the Iron Age when justice and virtue had vanished.
Poets often contrast the Iron Age with the earlier Golden Age to highlight moral decline.
The Iron Age in mythology is a period of violence and hardship, unlike the peace of previous ages.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Golden Age - the first and best age in classical mythology, a time of peace and prosperity

Etymology

From classical mythology; named after iron, symbolizing hardness and corruption.
