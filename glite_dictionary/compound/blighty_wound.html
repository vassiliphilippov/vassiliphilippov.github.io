blighty wound

1. NOUN

/ ˈblaɪti wund / (AmE), / ˈblaɪti wuːnd / (BrE)
A wound serious enough to require a British soldier to be sent home from military service abroad, especially during World War I.
wound sending a British soldier home
british

Examples

The soldier was relieved to receive a blighty wound that ended his time at the front.
Stories from World War I often recount men hoping for a blighty wound to escape the trenches.
A blighty wound was viewed by many as a ticket safely back to England.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wound - an injury to body tissue

Etymology

From British slang 'Blighty' (meaning Britain, especially home) plus 'wound'; 'Blighty' derives from Hindi 'vilāyatī' (foreign, British).
