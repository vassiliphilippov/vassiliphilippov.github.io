valerian family

1. NOUN

/ vəˈlɛriən ˈfæməli / (AmE), / vəˈlɪəriən ˈfæmɪli / (BrE)
A family of mostly herbaceous plants, typically having a strong, unpleasant odor; botanically known as Valerianaceae.
family of odoriferous herbaceous plants

Examples

The valerian family includes herbs known for their distinctively strong smell.
Botanists study the valerian family to understand the medicinal uses of certain species.
Some members of the valerian family are prized for their sedative properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Valerianaceae - Botanical family name for the valerian family
  • Synonym: valerian - a plant of the genus Valeriana, especially Valeriana officinalis

Etymology

From the genus name Valeriana, from Latin 'valere' (to be strong), referencing the plant's smell.
