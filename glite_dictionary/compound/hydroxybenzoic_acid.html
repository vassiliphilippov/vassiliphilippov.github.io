hydroxybenzoic acid

1. NOUN

/ haɪˌdrɑksiˌbɛnˈzoʊɪk ˌæsɪd / (AmE), / haɪˌdrɒksiˌbɛnˈzəʊɪk ˌæsɪd / (BrE)
a crystalline compound derived from benzoic acid by replacing one hydrogen atom with a hydroxyl group, used in organic chemistry and found in some plants
a derivative of benzoic acid with a hydroxyl group
medical

Examples

Hydroxybenzoic acid is commonly found in certain fruits and used as a preservative in food products.
Researchers measured the concentration of hydroxybenzoic acid in herbal extracts to study their antioxidant properties.
The presence of hydroxybenzoic acid in urine may indicate the consumption of foods rich in polyphenols.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: benzoic acid - a simple aromatic carboxylic acid

Etymology

hydroxy- (from Greek 'hydor' meaning water, relating to the hydroxyl group) + benzoic acid (from benzoin, a resin, + -ic).
