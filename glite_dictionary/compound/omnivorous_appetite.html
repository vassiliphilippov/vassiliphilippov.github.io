omnivorous appetite

1. NOUN

/ ɑmˈnɪvərəs ˈæpəˌtaɪt / (AmE), / ɒmˈnɪvərəs ˈæpɪtaɪt / (BrE)
a desire or willingness to eat many different kinds of food
desire to eat various foods

Examples

Her omnivorous appetite made her the perfect travel companion, eager to try any local specialty.
With his omnivorous appetite, he enjoyed everything from sushi to traditional French cuisine.
The chef's omnivorous appetite inspired a menu featuring flavors from every continent.

Synset Examples

He has an omnivorous appetite for all types of cuisine.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: appetite - a natural desire to satisfy a bodily need, especially for food
  • Degree Higher: voracious appetite - an extremely eager or insatiable desire for food

Etymology

Omnivorous from Latin 'omnivorus', meaning 'all-eating'; appetite from Latin 'appetitus', meaning 'desire for'.
