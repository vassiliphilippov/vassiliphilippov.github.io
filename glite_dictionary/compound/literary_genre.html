literary genre

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɪtəˌrɛri ˈʒɑnrə / (AmE), / ˌlɪtərəri ˈʒɒnrə / (BrE)
A category of literary composition characterized by a particular style, form, or content.
category of literary composition

Examples

She prefers the mystery literary genre above all others for its suspenseful plots.
Science fiction is a popular literary genre that explores futuristic concepts and technology.
The novel blends elements from more than one literary genre to create a unique narrative style.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: genre - a category or style, especially of art or literature

Etymology

From French 'genre' (kind, sort), itself from Latin 'genus' (kind, type); 'literary' from Latin 'litterarius'.
