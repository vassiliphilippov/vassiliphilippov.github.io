self-proclaimed

1. ADJ

/ ˌsɛlf prəˈkleɪmd / (AmE), / ˌsɛlf prəˈkleɪmd / (BrE)
(of a person or their title/status) having claimed a particular identity, title, or status for oneself, often without external recognition.
claiming a title or status oneself

Examples

He is a self-proclaimed expert on Italian cuisine, though he has never visited Italy.
The city hosted a festival organized by a self-proclaimed art critic.
Many self-proclaimed gurus advertise their seminars online.

Synset Examples

The film featured a character who was a self-proclaimed masturbator.
He's a self-proclaimed sports junkie who never misses a game.
Every time we argue, he acts like a self-proclaimed dickologist.
The party was hosted by the self-proclaimed gaylord.
As a self-proclaimed hamburgerologist, he knows all the best joints.
She condemned the actions of self-proclaimed muslimonazis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: self-styled - having given oneself a particular name or title, often without authority

Etymology

Formed from 'self-' (referring to oneself) + 'proclaimed' (from Latin proclamare, to cry out publicly).
