Chinese alligator

1. NOUN

/ tʃaɪˈniːz ˈæləˌɡeɪtɚ / (AmE), / tʃaɪˈniːz ˈælɪˌɡeɪtə / (BrE)
a small species of alligator found in the Yangtze River valley in China, distinguished by its relatively unwebbed digits
small alligator species native to China

Examples

The Chinese alligator is critically endangered due to habitat loss and fragmentation.
Unlike its American cousin, the Chinese alligator has unwebbed digits and is much smaller in size.
Researchers have launched conservation programs to help the remaining Chinese alligator population survive.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alligator - large reptile with a broad snout, found in the Americas and China
