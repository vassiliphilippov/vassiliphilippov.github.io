family Oriolidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ɔːˌraɪ.əˈliː.deɪ / (AmE), / ˈfæməli ɔːˌrɪəˈliː.diː / (BrE)
A family of birds known as Old World orioles, found in Africa, Europe, Asia, and Australia.
taxonomic family of Old World orioles

Examples

The family Oriolidae includes many brightly colored birds native to the Old World.
Scientists studied the migratory patterns of the family Oriolidae across Europe.
The golden oriole is a well-known member of the family Oriolidae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oriole - a colorful bird of the family Oriolidae or Icteridae

Etymology

From New Latin Oriolidae, from genus name Oriolus + suffix -idae (denoting families in zoology).
