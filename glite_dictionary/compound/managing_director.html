managing director

1. NOUN

/ ˈmænɪdʒɪŋ daɪˈrɛktər / (AmE), / ˈmænɪdʒɪŋ daɪˈrɛktə / (BrE)
the person with the highest level of managerial responsibility in a company, responsible for overseeing operations and implementing policies
chief executive in a company

Examples

After ten years with the company, she was promoted to managing director last year.
The managing director announced a new strategy to increase profitability across all divisions.
Every major decision must be approved by the managing director before being implemented.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chief executive officer - the highest-ranking person in a company, responsible for overall management
  • Degree Lower: director - a member of the board of people that manages or oversees a company or organization
