methyl alcohol

1. NOUN

/ ˈmiːθəl ˈæl.kəˌhɑl / (AmE), / ˈmiːθəl ˈæl.kəˌhɒl / (BrE)
a poisonous, colorless, flammable liquid used as an antifreeze, solvent, fuel, and as a denaturant for ethyl alcohol
poisonous liquid alcohol (methanol)

Examples

The laboratory ordered several liters of methyl alcohol for their experiments.
Improper handling of methyl alcohol can result in severe poisoning and even death.
Car radiators in cold climates often use methyl alcohol as an antifreeze.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: methanol - the same compound as methyl alcohol; a synonym

Etymology

From 'methyl', a chemical prefix (from Greek 'methy' for wine and 'hyle' for wood), and 'alcohol'.
