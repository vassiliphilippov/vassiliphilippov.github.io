paramilitary organization

1. NOUN

/ ˌpærəˈmɪlɪˌtɛri ˌɔːrɡənəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌpærəˈmɪlɪtəri ˌɔːɡənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
a group of civilians organized along military lines, especially to function in place of or to support regular armed forces
group of civilians organized like an army

Examples

The government accused the paramilitary organization of instigating violence in the region.
A paramilitary organization often recruits members from local communities to assist in operations.
Several countries have relied on a paramilitary organization to support their armed forces during times of war.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: militia - a military force raised from the civil population
  • Antonym: military - the armed forces of a country

Etymology

Formed from 'paramilitary', from Greek ‘para-’ (beside) + 'military'.
