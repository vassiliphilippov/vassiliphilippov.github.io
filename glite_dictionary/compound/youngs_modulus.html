Young's modulus

1. NOUN

/ ˈjʌŋz ˈmɑːdʒələs / (AmE), / ˈjʌŋz ˈmɒdjʊləs / (BrE)
A measure of the elasticity of a material, defined as the ratio of stress to strain when a wire or rod is stretched within its elastic limit.
measure of material elasticity

Examples

The steel cable's Young's modulus is much higher than that of rubber, making it less likely to stretch.
Engineers use Young's modulus to determine how much a material will deform under stress.
Accurate measurement of Young's modulus is essential in designing safe bridges and buildings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elastic modulus - general measure of a material's resistance to deformation

Etymology

Named after British scientist Thomas Young (1773–1829).
