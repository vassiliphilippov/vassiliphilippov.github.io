white bryony

1. NOUN

/ waɪt ˈbraɪəni / (AmE), / waɪt ˈbraɪəni / (BrE)
A climbing vine with white flowers, thick roots, and small black berries, native to Europe and extending to Iran.
a vine with white flowers and black berries

Examples

White bryony climbs along the garden fence, its tendrils wrapping tightly around the posts.
The berries of white bryony should not be eaten, as they are considered poisonous.
During their hike through rural France, they spotted white bryony growing wild among the hedgerows.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bryony - a genus of climbing plants with tendrils and berries
