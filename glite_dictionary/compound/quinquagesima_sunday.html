Quinquagesima Sunday

1. NOUN

/ kwɪŋkwəˈdʒɛsəmə ˈsʌndeɪ / (AmE), / kwɪŋkwəˈdʒɛsɪmə ˈsʌndeɪ / (BrE)
The Sunday before Ash Wednesday, marking the last Sunday before the start of Lent in the Christian calendar.
Sunday before Ash Wednesday, start of Lent

Examples

Many Christians attend a special service on Quinquagesima Sunday to prepare for Lent.
The tradition of observing Quinquagesima Sunday dates back to the early centuries of the Church.
On Quinquagesima Sunday, the congregation reflected on themes of forgiveness and self-denial.

Etymology

From Latin 'quinquagesima' (fiftieth), referring to fifty days before Easter.
