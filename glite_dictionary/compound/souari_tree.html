souari tree

1. NOUN

/ suˈɑːri tri / (AmE), / suˈɑːri triː / (BrE)
a large evergreen tree native to South America, with trifoliate leaves and drupes that produce edible nutlike seeds used for food and cooking oil
South American evergreen tree; edible nut seeds

Examples

The nuts of the souari tree are commonly eaten in parts of Brazil.
Farmers harvest oil from the seeds of the souari tree for cooking purposes.
A towering souari tree provides ample shade in the tropical forest.

Etymology

From French 'souari', from a native South American language.
