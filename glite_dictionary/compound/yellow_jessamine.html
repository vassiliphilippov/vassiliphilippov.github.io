yellow jessamine

1. NOUN

/ ˈjɛloʊ ˈdʒɛsəˌmin / (AmE), / ˈjɛləʊ ˈdʒɛsəˌmiːn / (BrE)
a poisonous evergreen vine of the southeastern United States, with fragrant yellow funnel-shaped flowers
poisonous vine with yellow flowers (US)

Examples

The sweet scent of yellow jessamine filled the southern garden in early spring.
Gardeners warn that yellow jessamine is toxic if ingested by humans or pets.
Along the fence, yellow jessamine vines climbed, brightening the landscape with their golden blossoms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Carolina jessamine - another common name for the same species (Gelsemium sempervirens)
