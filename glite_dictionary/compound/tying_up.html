tying up

1. NOUN

/ ˈtaɪɪŋ ˌʌp / (AmE), / ˈtaɪɪŋ ˌʌp / (BrE)
the act of securing a boat or ship to a dock or mooring using ropes
securing a vessel with ropes

Examples

The crew completed the tying up of the ship just before the storm hit.
After a long journey, the tying up at the harbor felt like a relief for the tired sailors.
Proper tying up is essential to prevent the boat from drifting away overnight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mooring - the act of securing a vessel with cables, anchors, or lines
