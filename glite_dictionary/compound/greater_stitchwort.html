greater stitchwort

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡreɪtər ˈstɪtʃˌwɝt / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪtə ˈstɪtʃˌwɜːt / (BrE)
a low-growing plant of the pink family found in Europe and temperate Asia, with slender stems and small white star-shaped flowers (Stellaria holostea)
a flowering plant with white star-shaped flowers

Examples

Every spring, the woodland edges are dotted with greater stitchwort in bloom.
She pointed out the delicate flowers of greater stitchwort growing among the grass.
A patch of greater stitchwort brightened the shady side of the hedgerow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stitchwort - general name for several plants in the genus Stellaria

Etymology

stitchwort from 'stitch' (a sharp pain) + 'wort' (Old English for plant), named for its folk use in treating side pain.
