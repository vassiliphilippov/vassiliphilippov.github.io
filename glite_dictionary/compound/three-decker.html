three-decker

1. NOUN

/ ˈθriːˌdɛkər / (AmE), / ˈθriːˌdɛkə / (BrE)
a warship equipped with guns on three separate decks, especially used in the 17th to 19th centuries
warship with three gun decks

Examples

The British Navy launched a three-decker that dominated naval battles in the 18th century.
Many three-decker ships were the pride of European fleets.
The famous HMS Victory was a celebrated three-decker during the Napoleonic Wars.

2. NOUN

/ θriˈdɛkər / (AmE), / θriːˈdɛkə / (BrE)
a ship that has three complete decks, especially those used in navy fleets between the 17th and 19th centuries
ship with three complete decks

Examples

The British Navy's most powerful vessels were the formidable three-decker ships of the line.
During the battle, the admiral commanded his fleet from the massive three-decker.
Models of three-decker warships are popular among maritime historians.
