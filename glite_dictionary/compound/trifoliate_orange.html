trifoliate orange

1. NOUN

/ traɪˈfoʊliət ˈɔrɪndʒ / (AmE), / traɪˈfəʊlɪət ˈɒrɪndʒ / (BrE)
a small, fast-growing, spiny Chinese orange tree (Poncirus trifoliata) with fragrant flowers and decorative but inedible fruit, often used as rootstock for grafting or in hedges
a spiny Chinese orange tree with inedible fruit

Examples

The trifoliate orange is often grown as a hedge because of its dense, thorny branches.
Gardeners frequently use trifoliate orange as rootstock for cultivated citrus trees.
Although the trifoliate orange produces attractive fruit, it is generally considered inedible.

Etymology

From Latin 'tri-' (three) + 'folium' (leaf), referring to its three-part leaves; 'orange' after its fruit.
