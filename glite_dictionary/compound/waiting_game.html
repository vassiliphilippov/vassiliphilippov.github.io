waiting game

1. NOUN

/ ˈweɪtɪŋ ɡeɪm / (AmE), / ˈweɪtɪŋ ɡeɪm / (BrE)
a strategy of deliberately delaying action in order to gain an advantage or force an opponent to act first
strategy of deliberate delay

Examples

Both companies are playing a waiting game to see who will lower their prices first.
Negotiations have stalled, and it's turned into a waiting game between the two sides.
Sometimes patience is necessary, and you have to play the waiting game to achieve the best result.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: delay tactic - a strategy intended to postpone or slow down an outcome
Source: WordNet

2. NOUN

/ ˈweɪtɪŋ ɡeɪm / (AmE), / ˈweɪtɪŋ ɡeɪm / (BrE)
a situation in which someone must wait patiently for an outcome or for something to happen, often because action is not possible or advantageous at the moment
situation requiring patient waiting

Examples

Launching the new product has turned into a waiting game as we await approval.
Negotiations became a waiting game, with neither side willing to make the first move.
Sometimes finding the right job is just a waiting game that takes perseverance.

Synset Examples

The project is a waiting game until the client responds.
It's a waiting game now until we hear back.
Life can feel like a waiting game sometimes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: patience - the capacity to accept or tolerate delay without becoming annoyed
  • Antonym: rush - to act with great haste or urgency
