plectognath fish

1. NOUN

/ plɛkˈtɑɡ.næθ fɪʃ / (AmE), / plɛkˈtɒɡ.næθ fɪʃ / (BrE)
A tropical marine fish with teeth fused into a beak and thick skin covered with bony plates or spines, belonging to the order Tetraodontiformes, such as triggerfish and pufferfish.
tropical fish with beak-like teeth and bony skin plates

Examples

The coral reef was teeming with plectognath fish like triggerfish and boxfish.
Some species of plectognath fish are popular among aquarium enthusiasts for their unique appearance.
Marine biologists study plectognath fish to understand how their fused beak-like teeth help them feed on hard-shelled prey.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: triggerfish - a type of plectognath fish known for its tough skin and trigger-like dorsal fin
  • Synonym: pufferfish - another member of the plectognath group with the ability to inflate its body

Etymology

From Greek 'plektos' (twisted) + 'gnathos' (jaw).
