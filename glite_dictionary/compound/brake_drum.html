brake drum

1. NOUN

/ ˈbreɪk ˌdrʌm / (AmE), / ˈbreɪk ˌdrʌm / (BrE)
a hollow metal cylinder attached to a wheel, inside which the brake shoes press to slow or stop the vehicle
part of a vehicle brake system

Examples

The mechanic needs to replace the worn brake drum before the car is safe to drive.
Older vehicles often use a brake drum rather than a disc brake on the rear wheels.
A damaged brake drum can cause vibration and a decrease in braking efficiency.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: disc brake - type of brake using a disk and calipers
