wild morning-glory

1. NOUN

/ waɪld ˈmɔrnɪŋ ˌɡlɔri / (AmE), / waɪld ˈmɔːnɪŋ ˌɡlɒri / (BrE)
a common Eurasian and American climbing plant with pink flowers, sometimes classified under the genus Convolvulus
a wild climbing plant with pink flowers

Examples

Wild morning-glory can quickly cover fences and other plants in the garden.
The pink flowers of the wild morning-glory brighten up the roadside in early summer.
We pulled up several wild morning-glories that were choking the vegetable patch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bindweed - another name for plants in the same family, with twining stems and trumpet-shaped flowers
  • Synonym: morning glory - any of various related plants with showy trumpet-shaped flowers, especially cultivated types

2. NOUN

/ waɪld ˈmɔrnɪŋ ˌɡlɔri / (AmE), / waɪld ˈmɔːnɪŋ ˌɡlɒri / (BrE)
A weakly climbing perennial plant, native to Europe but naturalized in North America, with white or pink funnel-shaped flowers, often regarded as an invasive weed.
European climbing perennial plant with pink or white flowers

Examples

The wild morning-glory quickly covered the fence with its twisting vines.
Gardeners often struggle to control wild morning-glory because it spreads so rapidly.
I spotted delicate pink blooms of wild morning-glory along the roadside.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bindweed - another common name for plants in the same genus, sometimes used interchangeably
