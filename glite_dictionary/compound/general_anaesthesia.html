general anaesthesia

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɛnərəl ænəsˈθiʒə / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛnərəl ˌænəsˈθiːziə / (BrE)
A medically induced state of total unconsciousness used to prevent pain during surgery or other medical procedures.
medically induced unconsciousness
medical british

Examples

The patient was placed under general anaesthesia before the operation began.
Children may feel groggy after waking up from general anaesthesia.
Advances in general anaesthesia have made complex surgeries much safer.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: local anaesthesia - loss of sensation in a specific body part
  • Synonym: general anesthesia - American spelling of 'general anaesthesia'

Etymology

From Modern Latin 'anaesthesia', from Greek 'anaisthetos' (insensible), combining 'an-' (without) and 'aisthēsis' (sensation).
