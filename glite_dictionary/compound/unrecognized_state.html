unrecognized state

1. NOUN

/ ˌʌnˈrɛkəgˌnaɪzd steɪt / (AmE), / ˌʌnˈrɛkəgnaɪzd steɪt / (BrE)
A territory that has established de facto independence and governs itself, but is not officially recognized as a sovereign state by the international community.
self-governing territory lacking official recognition

Examples

Somaliland is often cited as an unrecognized state in the Horn of Africa.
After declaring independence, they functioned as an unrecognized state for many years.
Many unrecognized states issue their own currency and passports despite international isolation.
