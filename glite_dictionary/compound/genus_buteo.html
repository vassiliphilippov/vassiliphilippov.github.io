genus Buteo

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈbjuː.tioʊ / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈbjuː.tɪəʊ / (BrE)
A genus of broad-winged, soaring hawks found in many parts of the world, including species such as buzzards and hawks.
genus of broad-winged, soaring hawks

Examples

The genus Buteo includes many of the familiar buzzards found in Europe and America.
Researchers observed several members of the genus Buteo soaring over the open fields.
Characteristics of the genus Buteo include broad wings and a rounded tail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Buteo - a genus of medium to large, broad-winged raptors
  • Synonym: hawk - a bird of prey known for its sharp vision and hunting ability

Etymology

From Latin 'buteo', meaning 'buzzard'.
