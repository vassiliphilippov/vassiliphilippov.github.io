Komodo lizard

1. NOUN

/ kəˈmoʊdoʊ ˈlɪzərd / (AmE), / kəˈməʊdəʊ ˈlɪzəd / (BrE)
a very large species of lizard, native to certain Indonesian islands, known for being the largest living lizard
very large lizard from Indonesia

Examples

The Komodo lizard can grow up to three meters long and has a powerful bite.
Scientists travel to the Indonesian islands to study the rare Komodo lizard.
A Komodo lizard can detect its prey from several kilometers away using its keen sense of smell.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Komodo dragon - another name for the Komodo lizard

Etymology

From Komodo, the Indonesian island where it is found, combined with 'lizard'.
