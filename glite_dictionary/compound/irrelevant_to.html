irrelevant to

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈrɛl.ə.vənt tuː / (AmE), / ɪˈrel.ɪ.vənt tə / (BrE)
Not applicable or related to a particular subject, situation, or matter.
not related or applicable to

Examples

Her previous experience is irrelevant to the new project requirements.
This detail is completely irrelevant to our decision-making process.
The witness's statement was ruled irrelevant to the case by the judge.

Synset Examples

That information is irrelevant to our current task.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: relevant to - closely connected or appropriate to the subject

Etymology

From 'irrelevant' (from French 'irrélevant' or Late Latin 'irrelevans', not raising up) + 'to'.
