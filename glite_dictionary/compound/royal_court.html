royal court

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɔɪ.əl kɔrt / (AmE), / ˈrɔɪ.əl kɔːt / (BrE)
The sovereign and their advisers who form the governing authority of a monarchy.
monarch's governing body and advisers

Examples

The king's royal court convened to discuss important matters of state.
Members of the royal court played a key role in shaping political decisions.
During the ceremony, the royal court stood behind the queen in solemn support.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: court - the official residence or establishment of a sovereign or dignitary
  • Synonym: monarchy - a system of government by a king or queen
  • Synonym: government - the governing body of a nation, state, or community

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɔɪ.əl kɔrt / (AmE), / ˈrɔɪ.əl kɔːt / (BrE)
The household, family, and close attendants surrounding a reigning monarch or prince, often including advisers, servants, and nobility.
household and entourage of a monarch

Examples

The royal court gathered in the palace to celebrate the king's birthday.
Diplomats from other countries were often received by the royal court with elaborate ceremonies.
Artists and musicians were regularly invited to perform for the royal court during the Renaissance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: court - the retinue or household of a sovereign
  • Synonym: royal family - the immediate family of a reigning monarch
