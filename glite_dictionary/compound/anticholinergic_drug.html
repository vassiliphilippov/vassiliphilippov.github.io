anticholinergic drug

1. NOUN

/ ˌæn.tiˌkoʊ.ləˈnɜr.dʒɪk ˌdrʌɡ / (AmE), / ˌæn.tiˌkəʊ.lɪˈnɜː.dʒɪk ˌdrʌɡ / (BrE)
a drug that blocks or inhibits the action of acetylcholine in the nervous system, often used to treat conditions such as asthma, gastrointestinal disorders, and certain types of poisoning
drug that blocks acetylcholine
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed an anticholinergic drug to help reduce her asthma symptoms.
Some side effects of an anticholinergic drug can include dry mouth and blurred vision.
When treating certain types of poisoning, an anticholinergic drug may be administered as an antidote.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cholinergic drug - a drug that mimics or enhances the action of acetylcholine

Etymology

From anti- (against) + cholinergic (relating to acetylcholine), based on Greek 'chole' (bile) and 'ergon' (work).
