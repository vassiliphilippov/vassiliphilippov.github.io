hearty tucker

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑrti ˈtʌkər / (AmE), / ˈhɑːti ˈtʌkə / (BrE)
A substantial and satisfying meal, often eaten after physical activity or when very hungry, chiefly in Australian or British English.
substantial meal (Australian/British, informal)
informal british

Examples

After a tough day at work, nothing beats a plate of hearty tucker.
They arrived at the campsite starving and ready for some hearty tucker.
The pub is famous for serving traditional hearty tucker at reasonable prices.

Synset Examples

After a long hike, we enjoyed a hearty tucker.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tucker - food; a meal, especially in Australian and British English

Etymology

From 'hearty' (full of heart, robust) + Australian slang 'tucker' (food, probably from English dialect).
