stylomastoid vein

1. NOUN

/ staɪloʊˌmæstɔɪd veɪn / (AmE), / staɪləʊˌmæstɔɪd veɪn / (BrE)
a vein that drains blood from the tympanic cavity and empties into the retromandibular vein
vein draining the tympanic cavity
medical

Examples

The stylomastoid vein is responsible for draining blood from the tympanic cavity.
Surgeons must be cautious to avoid damaging the stylomastoid vein during ear operations.
Blockage of the stylomastoid vein can lead to complications in the drainage of the middle ear.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: retromandibular vein - a vein into which the stylomastoid vein empties

Etymology

From 'stylo-' (relating to the styloid process) and 'mastoid' (relating to the mastoid process), anatomical features of the skull.
