macaroni wheat

1. NOUN

/ ˌmækəˈroʊni wiːt / (AmE), / ˌmakəˈrəʊni wiːt / (BrE)
a variety of hard wheat with dark-colored kernels, high in gluten, used especially for making pasta and bread
variety of hard wheat for pasta and bread

Examples

The best pasta is often made from macaroni wheat due to its high gluten content.
Farmers in North Dakota commonly grow macaroni wheat for both local use and export.
The bakery uses macaroni wheat to produce their specialty breads.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: durum wheat - hard wheat used for pasta and semolina, scientific name Triticum durum

Etymology

From Italian 'maccherone' (macaroni) + wheat, referring to its primary use in pasta production.
