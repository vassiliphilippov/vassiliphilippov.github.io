fellow traveller

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɛloʊ ˈtrævələr / (AmE), / ˈfeləʊ ˈtrævələ / (BrE)
a person who accompanies you on a journey
person accompanying you on a journey

Examples

One of my fellow travellers told the group a fascinating story about India.
During the long train ride, I became friends with a fellow traveller.
She shared snacks with her fellow travellers while waiting for the delayed flight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: companion - person who accompanies another

Etymology

From 'fellow' (companion; from Old English 'feolaga') + 'traveller' (one who travels; from Middle English 'travailen').

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɛloʊ ˈtrævələr / (AmE), / ˈfɛləʊ ˈtrævələ / (BrE)
a person who sympathizes with the ideals of communism or a political movement but is not an official member of the associated party
communist sympathizer (not party member)

Examples

Though never a member of the party, he was often labeled a fellow traveller due to his outspoken support for leftist causes.
During the 1950s, many artists and writers were accused of being fellow travellers in the United States.
She insisted she was just a fellow traveller, not a card-carrying party member.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sympathizer - a person who supports a cause or movement without being a formal member

Etymology

Originally used in 1920s Russia as 'poputchik' for non-party supporters of Bolshevism; adopted into English in the same context.
