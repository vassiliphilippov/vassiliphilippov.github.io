tactual sensation

1. NOUN

/ ˈtæk.tʃu.əl sɛnˈseɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˈtæk.tʃʊ.əl sɛnˈseɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
a sensation received by touch or pressure through receptors in the skin
sensation of touch or pressure

Examples

The soft fabric provided a pleasing tactual sensation against her skin.
Some animals rely heavily on tactual sensation to explore their environment.
A light breeze can trigger a subtle tactual sensation on exposed skin.

Synset Examples

she likes the touch of silk on her skin
the surface had a greasy feeling

Related Terms

  • Synonym: touch - the sense by which physical contact is perceived
  • Synonym: haptic - relating to the sense of touch

Etymology

From Latin 'tactus' (touch) + 'sensation' (perception).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtæktʃuəl sɛnˈseɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈtæktʃʊəl sɛnˈseɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
the ability to perceive stimuli such as pressure, heat, or pain through the sense of touch via the skin
perception of stimuli by touch
medical

Examples

The loss of tactual sensation in his fingertips made it difficult for him to play the piano.
Nerves in the skin are responsible for tactual sensation such as warmth, cold, or pain.
Scientists study the brain regions involved in tactual sensation to better understand how we perceive touch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tactile sensation - the sensation received by touch

Etymology

From Latin 'tactus' (touch) and English 'sensation'.
