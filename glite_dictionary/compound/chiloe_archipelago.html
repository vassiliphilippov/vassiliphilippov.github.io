Chiloe Archipelago

1. PROPN

/ ˌtʃiːloʊ ˌɑːrkəˈpɛləˌɡoʊ / (AmE), / ˌtʃiːləʊ ˌɑːkɪˈpeləɡəʊ / (BrE)
a group of islands off the south-central coast of Chile, notable for its main island, Chiloé, and its historical and cultural significance
group of islands off south-central Chile

Examples

Many travelers visit the Chiloe Archipelago to experience its rich folklore and wooden churches.
The Chiloe Archipelago is famous for its unique architecture and lush, rainy landscapes.
Fishing and agriculture are central to life in the Chiloe Archipelago.

Etymology

From Spanish 'Chiloé', the name of the main island, itself possibly from Mapuche 'chilhué' (place of seagulls) + 'archipiélago' (archipelago).
