artichoke plant

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːrtɪˌtʃoʊk plænt / (AmE), / ˈɑːtɪtʃəʊk plɑːnt / (BrE)
A Mediterranean thistlelike plant, widely cultivated for its large edible flower head.
plant cultivated for edible flower head

Examples

The artichoke plant requires a mild climate to thrive and produce large flower heads.
Farmers are expanding their fields to include more artichoke plants this season.
She harvested several artichoke plants from her garden for tonight's dinner.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: artichoke - the edible flower bud of the artichoke plant

Etymology

From Italian 'articiocco,' from Spanish 'alcachofa,' from Arabic 'al-ḵuršūf.'
