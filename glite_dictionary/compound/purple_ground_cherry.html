purple ground cherry

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɝpəl ɡraʊnd ˈtʃɛri / (AmE), / ˈpɜːpl ɡraʊnd ˈtʃɛri / (BrE)
An annual plant from tropical South America, bearing edible purple fruits.
South American annual plant with purple edible fruit

Examples

The purple ground cherry thrives in warm, tropical climates and is popular for its sweet fruit.
Some farmers in Brazil grow purple ground cherry for both local consumption and export.
You can eat the purple ground cherry raw or use it to make jams and desserts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ground cherry - A plant of the genus Physalis, bearing edible fruits encased in a papery husk

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɝː.pəl ɡraʊnd ˈtʃɛr.i / (AmE), / ˈpɜː.pəl ɡraʊnd ˈtʃɛr.i / (BrE)
A Mexican annual plant naturalized in eastern North America, bearing yellow to purple edible fruit resembling small tomatoes.
plant with edible yellow to purple fruit

Examples

The purple ground cherry thrives in gardens with full sun and well-drained soil.
She harvested a basket of ripe purple ground cherry fruit from her backyard.
Locals enjoy making jam from the sweet and tangy purple ground cherry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ground cherry - plant genus Physalis with small edible fruits enclosed in a husk
