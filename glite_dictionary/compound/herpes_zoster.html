herpes zoster

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɜːrpiːz ˈzoʊstər / (AmE), / ˌhɜːpiːz ˈzɒstə / (BrE)
a viral infection characterized by painful skin eruptions along a nerve path, often accompanied by severe neuralgia; also known as shingles
viral infection causing painful skin rash (shingles)
medical

Examples

She was diagnosed with herpes zoster after developing a painful rash on her side.
Treatment for herpes zoster can help reduce the risk of complications.
The doctor explained that the nerve pain from herpes zoster can persist even after the rash disappears.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shingles - common name for herpes zoster

Etymology

From Latin *herpes* (a creeping eruption) and Greek *zōstēr* (girdle, referring to the band-like rash).

2. NOUN

/ ˌhɜːrpiːz ˈzoʊstər / (AmE), / ˌhɜːpiːz ˈzɒstə / (BrE)
a herpesvirus that causes shingles, characterized by a painful skin rash and blisters
virus that causes shingles
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed her with herpes zoster, explaining that it was the cause of her painful rash.
Vaccination can help prevent herpes zoster and reduce the risk of developing shingles later in life.
Older adults are more susceptible to herpes zoster due to a weakened immune system.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shingles - an acute, painful inflammation of the nerve ganglia, with a skin eruption often forming a girdle around the middle of the body, caused by herpes zoster

Etymology

From Greek 'herpēs' (creeping) and Latin 'zoster' (girdle or belt), referencing the pattern of the rash.
