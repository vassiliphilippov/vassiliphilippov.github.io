warren of streets

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔr.ən əv strits / (AmE), / ˈwɒr.ən əv striːts / (BrE)
a complex and confusing network of narrow, winding streets, resembling a rabbit warren
maze-like network of narrow streets

Examples

After wandering for hours, we realized we were lost in the warren of streets in the old city.
The detective navigated the warren of streets to track down his suspect.
Tourists often find the warren of streets in this medieval town both charming and confusing.

Synset Examples

He felt lost in the warren of city streets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maze - a complex network of paths or passages
  • Synonym: labyrinth - a complicated irregular network of passages or paths

Etymology

Figurative use of 'warren,' originally meaning a network of rabbit burrows, applied to urban environments.
