fairy story

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɛri ˌstɔri / (AmE), / ˈfeəri ˌstɔːri / (BrE)
A story involving fairies and magical beings, typically told to entertain children.
children's story about fairies

Examples

As a child, she begged her grandmother to tell her a fairy story every night.
Many fairy stories feature magical forests and enchanted castles.
He dismissed the rumors as nothing but an old fairy story.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fairy tale - a story involving magical beings or fantastical events, often for children

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɛri ˌstɔri / (AmE), / ˈfeəri ˌstɔːri / (BrE)
an interesting but highly improbable story, often told as an excuse or to deceive
implausible or deceptive story

Examples

No one believed his fairy story about why he was late to the meeting.
She dismissed the rumor as just another fairy story with no evidence.
He tried to get out of trouble with a complicated fairy story about lost documents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fairy tale - an implausible story, sometimes used as an excuse or lie
  • Antonym: truth - the state or quality of being true; factual
  • Synonym: lie - a false statement made with deliberate intent to deceive

Etymology

From 'fairy', referring to mythical beings from folklore, and 'story'.
