antihypertensive drug

1. NOUN

/ ˌæntiˌhaɪpərˈtɛnsɪv drʌɡ / (AmE), / ˌæntiˌhʌɪpəˈtɛnsɪv drʌɡ / (BrE)
a drug used to lower high blood pressure
drug that lowers high blood pressure
medical

Examples

My doctor prescribed an antihypertensive drug to help control my blood pressure.
Some patients may need more than one antihypertensive drug to achieve desired results.
The new antihypertensive drug was shown to reduce complications in people with severe hypertension.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blood pressure medication - a drug for treating high blood pressure

Etymology

From anti- (against) + hypertensive (causing high blood pressure), itself from hyper- (over) + tension.
