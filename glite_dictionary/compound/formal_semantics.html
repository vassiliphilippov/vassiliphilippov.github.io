formal semantics

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔrməl səˈmæntɪks / (AmE), / ˈfɔːməl sɪˈmæn.tɪks / (BrE)
the branch of linguistics that studies meaning by applying formal logic and mathematical principles to language
study of meaning using logic in linguistics

Examples

The professor specializes in formal semantics, focusing on how logical systems represent linguistic meaning.
Many linguists consider formal semantics crucial for understanding the structure of complex sentences.
Her research in formal semantics explores how mathematical logic applies to natural language interpretation.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: semantics - the study of meaning in language
  • Antonym: pragmatics - the study of how context influences meaning in language
