just peachy

1. ADJ

/ dʒʌst ˈpiːtʃi / (AmE), / dʒʌst ˈpiːtʃi / (BrE)
Very good or pleasant; excellent, often used with irony to mean the opposite.
very good or pleasant (often ironic)
informal american

Examples

Everything is just peachy now that the problem has been resolved.
She told me her day was just peachy, but I could tell she was upset.
"Oh, that's just peachy," he said sarcastically after hearing the bad news.

Synset Examples

The weather was just peachy for our picnic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peachy - informal, excellent or very attractive

Etymology

Peachy is used informally in American English to mean 'excellent', from the fruit 'peach'.
