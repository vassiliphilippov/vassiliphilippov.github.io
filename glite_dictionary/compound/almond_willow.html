almond willow

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːlmənd ˈwɪloʊ / (AmE), / ˈɑːlmənd ˈwɪləʊ / (BrE)
An Old World species of willow (Salix triandra) with light green leaves, cultivated especially for use in basketry.
species of willow used in basketry

Examples

The almond willow is often chosen by artisans for weaving baskets because of its flexible stems.
In early spring, the leaves of the almond willow turn a delicate light green.
Traditional basket makers prefer almond willow for its durability and workability.
