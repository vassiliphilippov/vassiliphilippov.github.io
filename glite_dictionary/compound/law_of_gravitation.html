law of gravitation

1. NOUN

/ lɔ əv ˌɡrævəˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / lɔː əv ˌɡravɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the principle in physics that states any two bodies attract each other with a force directly proportional to the product of their masses and inversely proportional to the square of the distance between them
physical law of gravitational attraction

Examples

According to the law of gravitation, all objects in the universe attract each other regardless of their size.
Isaac Newton formulated the law of gravitation in the seventeenth century, revolutionizing physics.
The movement of the planets can be explained using the law of gravitation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Newton's law of universal gravitation - Newton's formulation of the law of gravitation
  • Antonym: law of electromagnetism - physical law about electromagnetic forces
