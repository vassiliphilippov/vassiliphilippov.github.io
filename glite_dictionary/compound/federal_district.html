federal district

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɛdərəl ˈdɪstrɪkt / (AmE), / ˈfɛdərəl ˈdɪstrɪkt / (BrE)
A district set aside as the official seat of government of a federation, often not part of any state or province.
district as federation's government seat

Examples

Washington, D.C., is the most well-known federal district in the United States.
The capital city is located within a separate federal district to ensure political neutrality.
Many countries have established a federal district to house their central government offices.
