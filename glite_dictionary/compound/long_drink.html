long drink

1. NOUN

/ lɔŋ drɪŋk / (AmE), / lɒŋ drɪŋk / (BrE)
A drink served in a tall glass, typically consisting of a larger quantity of liquid than a standard serving.
a large drink served in a tall glass

Examples

He ordered a long drink of lemonade to quench his thirst.
Some cocktails are best enjoyed as a long drink over ice.
After the game, she sat down with a refreshing long drink.

Synset Examples

He enjoyed a long drink while relaxing by the pool.
She ordered a long drink to cool off after the hike.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: short drink - a small serving of a beverage, typically in a short glass
