Mameluke dynasty

1. PROPN

/ ˈmæməˌluk ˈdaɪnəsti / (AmE), / ˈmaməljuːk ˈdɪnəsti / (BrE)
a dynasty of former slave soldiers who ruled Egypt and Syria from the 13th to early 16th centuries
historical ruling dynasty of Egypt and Syria

Examples

The Mameluke dynasty played a key role in defending the region from Mongol invasions.
Cairo flourished artistically and economically under the Mameluke dynasty.
After the fall of the Mameluke dynasty, Egypt became part of the Ottoman Empire.

Synset Examples

She read about the Mameluke dynasty in her history class.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Ottoman Empire - empire that succeeded Mameluke rule in Egypt

Etymology

From Arabic mamlūk (owned, possessed, slave); refers to the slave origins of its rulers.
