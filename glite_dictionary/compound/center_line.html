center line

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛn.tɚ laɪn / (AmE), / ˈsɛn.tə laɪn / (BrE)
A line that divides a plane figure or object into two equal or symmetrical parts.
line dividing a plane figure equally

Examples

The artist used a center line to ensure both halves of the design were symmetrical.
Engineers often draw a center line to mark the exact middle of a component.
Each page was folded along its center line before being cut.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: axis - an imaginary line about which a body rotates or is symmetrically arranged
  • Synonym: diameter - a straight line passing through the center of a circle or sphere
