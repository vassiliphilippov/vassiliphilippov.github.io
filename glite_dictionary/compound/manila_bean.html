Manila bean

1. NOUN

/ məˈnɪlə biːn / (AmE), / məˈnɪlə biːn / (BrE)
a tropical twining annual vine (Psophocarpus tetragonolobus) with tuberous roots, clusters of purplish flowers, and pods with four jagged wings, native to the Old World tropics
a tropical climbing bean plant

Examples

Farmers in Southeast Asia cultivate Manila bean for its nutritious pods and roots.
The Manila bean thrives in warm, humid climates and is valued for its versatility.
Chefs use the young pods of the Manila bean in stir-fried dishes and salads.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: winged bean - a tropical bean plant with edible pods and tubers, also known as Psophocarpus tetragonolobus

Etymology

Named after Manila, the capital of the Philippines, and 'bean' referring to its edible pods.
