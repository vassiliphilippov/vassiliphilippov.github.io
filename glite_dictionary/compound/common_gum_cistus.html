common gum cistus

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmən ɡʌm ˈsɪstəs / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən ɡʌm ˈsɪstəs / (BrE)
a Mediterranean shrub (Cistus ladanifer) with white flowers and sticky stems and leaves that produce a fragrant resin used in perfumery, especially as a fixative
Mediterranean shrub with fragrant resin

Examples

The common gum cistus is prized by gardeners for its attractive white flowers and aromatic resin.
Perfume makers use the resin from the common gum cistus to create long-lasting fragrances.
In its native habitat, the common gum cistus thrives in dry, rocky soils under the Mediterranean sun.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: labdanum - a resin obtained from Cistus shrubs, used in perfumery
