Garonne River

1. PROPN

/ ɡəˈrɔn ˈrɪvər / (AmE), / ɡəˈrɒn ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
a river in southwestern France that rises in the Pyrenees and flows northwest to the Bay of Biscay
river in southwestern France

Examples

The Garonne River is essential for irrigation and transportation in southwestern France.
Many picturesque towns are situated along the Garonne River.
In the spring, the Garonne River often swells with melted snow from the Pyrenees.

Etymology

From French 'Garonne', from Latin 'Garumna'.
