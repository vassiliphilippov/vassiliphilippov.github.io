amniotic sac

1. NOUN

/ æmˈniːɑtɪk sæk / (AmE), / æmˈniːətɪk sæk / (BrE)
the thin, innermost membranous sac that surrounds and protects the developing embryo or fetus of reptiles, birds, and mammals
membranous sac enclosing an embryo or fetus
medical

Examples

The baby develops inside the amniotic sac, which cushions and protects it during pregnancy.
When the amniotic sac breaks, it is often referred to as a woman's water breaking.
Doctors monitor the amniotic sac for any signs of rupture or infection during prenatal care.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amnion - the innermost membrane that encloses the embryo in higher vertebrates

Etymology

From 'amnion' (Greek 'amnion', meaning 'membrane') + 'sac'.
