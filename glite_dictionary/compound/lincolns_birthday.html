Lincoln's Birthday

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪŋkənz ˈbɝːθ.deɪ / (AmE), / ˈlɪŋkənz ˈbɜːθ.deɪ / (BrE)
A day observed in honor of President Abraham Lincoln, traditionally celebrated on February 12th in the United States.
U.S. holiday commemorating Abraham Lincoln

Examples

Many schools close in observance of Lincoln's Birthday each February.
Some states no longer consider Lincoln's Birthday an official public holiday.
Parades and ceremonies are held to celebrate Lincoln's Birthday across the country.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Presidents' Day - US holiday honoring presidents, especially Washington and Lincoln
  • Synonym: Washington's Birthday - US holiday commemorating George Washington's birthday
