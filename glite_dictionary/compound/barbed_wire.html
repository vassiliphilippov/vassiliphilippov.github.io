barbed wire

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑrbd ˌwaɪr / (AmE), / ˌbɑːbd ˈwaɪə / (BrE)
strong wire with sharp points spaced at intervals, used to prevent passage by people or animals
wire with sharp barbs for fencing

Examples

The farmer installed barbed wire along the fence to keep the cattle from escaping.
The prison was surrounded by barbed wire to prevent inmates from climbing over the walls.
She scratched her arm on a strand of barbed wire while crossing the field.
