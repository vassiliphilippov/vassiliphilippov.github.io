nuclear warhead

1. NOUN

/ ˈnuːkliɚ ˈwɔrˌhɛd / (AmE), / ˈnjuːkliə ˈwɔːhɛd / (BrE)
A warhead designed to deliver a nuclear explosive, typically as part of a missile or bomb.
explosive device in missiles or bombs using nuclear energy

Examples

The missile was equipped with a nuclear warhead capable of devastating an entire city.
International treaties aim to limit the number of nuclear warheads every country can possess.
The discovery of a stolen nuclear warhead caused a global security alert.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: conventional warhead - a warhead using conventional explosives rather than nuclear material
