very well

1. ADV

/ ˌvɛri ˈwɛl / (AmE), / ˌvɛri ˈwɛl / (BrE)
in a way that is quite good or satisfactory; fairly well
quite well; satisfactorily

Examples

Despite the rain, the event went very well and everyone had a good time.
She performed very well on her exam, scoring among the top students.
Although he was nervous, he spoke very well during the presentation.

Synset Examples

she doesn't feel first-rate today

Related Terms

  • Synonym: well - in a good or satisfactory way

2. ADV

/ ˌvɛri ˈwɛl / (AmE), / ˌvɛri ˈwɛl / (BrE)
used to express agreement or acceptance, especially to concede a point or to signify readiness to comply, often at the beginning of a sentence.
expression of agreement or acceptance

Examples

Very well, if you insist, we will start the meeting now.
'You may go.' 'Very well,' she replied, gathering her things.
Very well, I will accompany you to the station.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: all right - expression of agreement or acceptance
  • Antonym: no way - expression of refusal or disagreement
