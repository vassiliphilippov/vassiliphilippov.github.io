broom snakeweed

1. NOUN

/ bruːm ˈsneɪkˌwiːd / (AmE), / bruːm ˈsneɪkwiːd / (BrE)
a low-growing sticky subshrub of the southwestern United States, with slender branches, narrow leaves, and hundreds of tiny yellow flower heads
a yellow-flowered southwestern US subshrub

Examples

The dry landscape was dotted with clusters of broom snakeweed in full yellow bloom.
Ranchers monitor the spread of broom snakeweed as it can affect grazing livestock.
Botanists collected samples of broom snakeweed to study its effects on the local ecosystem.
