dry wash

1. NOUN

/ ˈdraɪ ˌwɑʃ / (AmE), / ˈdraɪ ˌwɒʃ / (BrE)
a dry stream bed or channel that temporarily fills with water after heavy rain, especially found in arid regions
dry bed of an intermittent stream

Examples

The hikers followed the dry wash through the canyon, searching for signs of wildlife.
Flash floods can quickly transform a dry wash into a rushing torrent.
Many desert plants thrive along the edge of a dry wash where water occasionally collects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arroyo - a steep-sided gully formed by fast-flowing water, chiefly in arid regions
