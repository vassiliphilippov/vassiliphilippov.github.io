pepper-and-salt

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛpər ənd sælt / (AmE), / ˌpɛpə ənd sɒlt / (BrE)
A fabric or cloth woven with a mixture of light and dark threads, creating a flecked or mottled appearance.
fabric with light and dark flecks

Examples

His favorite jacket was made of pepper-and-salt tweed, giving it a timeless, classic look.
The tailor recommended a pepper-and-salt fabric for a subtle yet distinguished suit.
She admired the pepper-and-salt pattern of the upholstery in the vintage armchair.

Etymology

Allusive, from the resemblance to the mixture of black pepper and salt.
