Marumi kumquat

1. NOUN

/ məˈruːmi ˈkʌmˌkwɑt / (AmE), / məˈruːmi ˈkʌmˌkwɒt / (BrE)
A small evergreen shrub (Fortunella japonica) that bears round, edible yellow-orange kumquats.
shrub with round edible kumquats

Examples

The Marumi kumquat is popular for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruit.
I planted a Marumi kumquat in my backyard to enjoy fresh citrus all year round.
The sweet, tart flavor of the Marumi kumquat makes it ideal for marmalades and preserves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kumquat - small citrus fruit; also the plant bearing it

Etymology

Marumi from Japanese, referring to its round shape; 'kumquat' from Cantonese gam1 gwat1 (golden orange).
