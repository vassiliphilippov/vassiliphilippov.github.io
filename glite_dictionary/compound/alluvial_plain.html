alluvial plain

1. NOUN

/ əˈluː.vi.əl pleɪn / (AmE), / əˈljuː.vi.əl pleɪn / (BrE)
A largely flat area of land formed by the deposition of sediment carried by running water, typically found adjacent to rivers and streams.
flat land formed by river deposits

Examples

The fertile alluvial plain supported intensive agriculture for centuries.
Many cities have developed on alluvial plains due to their rich soil and flat terrain.
The annual flooding helped to renew the alluvial plain with fresh layers of silt.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: floodplain - low-lying ground adjacent to a river, subject to flooding

Etymology

From Latin 'alluvius' (washed against) and 'planum' (flat surface).
