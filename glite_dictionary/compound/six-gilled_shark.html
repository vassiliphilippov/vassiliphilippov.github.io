six-gilled shark

1. NOUN

/ sɪks ɡɪld ʃɑrk / (AmE), / sɪks ɡɪld ʃɑːk / (BrE)
A large primitive shark with six gill slits, widely distributed in warm seas.
a large shark with six gill slits

Examples

The six-gilled shark can be found in deep waters across the world's oceans.
Scientists were excited to observe a rare six-gilled shark near the underwater canyon.
Unlike most sharks, the six-gilled shark has a more primitive appearance and six distinct gill slits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shark - large predatory fish with cartilaginous skeleton
