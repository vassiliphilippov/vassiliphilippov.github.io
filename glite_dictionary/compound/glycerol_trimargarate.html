glycerol trimargarate

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡlɪsəˌrɔl traɪˈmɑrɡəˌreɪt / (AmE), / ˈɡlɪsərɒl traɪˈmɑːɡəreɪt / (BrE)
a chemical compound formed by the esterification of glycerol with three margaric acid molecules
a glyceryl ester of margaric acid
medical

Examples

Glycerol trimargarate is used in laboratory studies to explore fat metabolism in organisms.
The structure of glycerol trimargarate consists of a glycerol backbone bonded to three margaric acid residues.
Researchers measured the melting point of glycerol trimargarate to compare it with other triglycerides.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: triglyceride - a compound formed from glycerol and three fatty acids

Etymology

From 'glycerol' (a simple polyol compound) and 'trimargarate' (indicating three margaric acid groups).
