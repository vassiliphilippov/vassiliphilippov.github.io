superorder Malacopterygii

1. NOUN

/ suː.pɚˌɔːr.dɚ ˌmæl.əˌkɒp.təˈrɪdʒ.i.aɪ / (AmE), / ˌsuː.pərˌɔː.də ˌmæl.əˌkɒp.təˈrɪdʒ.ɪ.aɪ / (BrE)
a taxonomic group of teleost fishes characterized by fins supported by flexible, cartilaginous rays.
group of fishes with soft-rayed fins

Examples

The herring belongs to the superorder Malacopterygii, which includes many soft-finned fishes.
Fins in the superorder Malacopterygii are notable for their flexible, cartilaginous rays.
Scientists debated the classification of certain species within the superorder Malacopterygii due to overlapping characteristics.

Etymology

From Greek 'malakos' (soft) and 'pteryx' (fin, wing).
