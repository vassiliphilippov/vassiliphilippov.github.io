blue-rinse brigade

1. NOUN

/ bluː rɪns brɪˈɡeɪd / (AmE), / bluː rɪns brɪˈɡeɪd / (BrE)
a group of elderly women, especially those who dye their hair bluish-grey and are seen as traditional or fond of gossip and social gatherings
group of elderly women with dyed hair
british informal

Examples

The blue-rinse brigade arrived at the café early for their weekly meeting.
She never thought she'd join the blue-rinse brigade, but she loves their company now.
The local blue-rinse brigade is famous for organizing charity bake sales.

Synset Examples

The blue-rinse brigade loves to gossip about the latest news.
She joked about becoming part of the blue-rinse brigade.
The blue-rinse brigade meets every Thursday for tea.

Etymology

Refers to the blue rinse, a hair dye used by older women, and 'brigade' as a collective term.
