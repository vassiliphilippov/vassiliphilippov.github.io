cold war

1. NOUN

/ koʊld wɔr / (AmE), / kəʊld wɔː / (BrE)
a state of political tension and hostility between nations that stops short of open armed conflict, typically involving threats, propaganda, and other measures
political hostility without armed conflict

Examples

The decades-long cold war between the United States and the Soviet Union shaped global politics.
Many historians study how the cold war influenced economic and technological development.
During the cold war, both sides invested heavily in espionage and propaganda.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hot war - a war involving active fighting and direct armed conflict

Etymology

First used after World War II to describe the political situation between the US and the Soviet Union.
