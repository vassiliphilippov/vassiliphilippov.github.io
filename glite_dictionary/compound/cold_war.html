Cold War

1. NOUN

/ koʊld wɔr / (AmE), / kəʊld wɔː / (BrE)
The period of intense political and military tension from 1945 to 1990 between the United States and its allies and the Soviet Union and its allies, without direct armed conflict between the major powers.
state of tension between US and Soviet Union, 1945–1990

Examples

Many historians believe that the Cold War profoundly shaped global politics in the twentieth century.
During the Cold War, the threat of nuclear conflict loomed large over international relations.
The Cold War ended with the dissolution of the Soviet Union in 1991.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hot war - a conflict involving direct military combat

Etymology

From the phrase 'cold war', first used in English in the late 1940s, indicating conflict without direct warfare.
