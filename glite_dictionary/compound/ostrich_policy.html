ostrich policy

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːstrɪtʃ ˈpɑːləsi / (AmE), / ˈɒstrɪtʃ ˈpɒləsi / (BrE)
a practice or policy of deliberately ignoring or denying problems rather than confronting them
policy of ignoring problems

Examples

Management's ostrich policy toward financial problems only made the crisis worse.
The country's continued ostrich policy in response to environmental threats drew criticism from experts.
You can't adopt an ostrich policy every time a difficult issue arises.

Synset Examples

The team's ostrich policy led to missed deadlines.
His ostrich policy regarding climate change is concerning.
Adopting an ostrich policy won't solve our funding issues.
Her approach to ostrich politics is frustrating the team.
We need to stop engaging in ostrich politics and act.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bury your head in the sand - to avoid facing up to difficulties or unpleasant facts
  • Antonym: to face the music - to confront an unpleasant situation

Etymology

Alludes to the (incorrect) belief that ostriches bury their heads in the sand to avoid danger.
