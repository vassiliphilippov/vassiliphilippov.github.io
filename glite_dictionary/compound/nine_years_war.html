Nine Years' War

1. NOUN

/ naɪn jɪrz wɔr / (AmE), / naɪn jɪəz wɔː / (BrE)
a major European conflict (1689–1697) fought primarily between France, led by Louis XIV, and a coalition including Spain, the Holy Roman Empire, England, and the Dutch Republic.
European war, 1689–1697, France vs. Grand Alliance

Examples

The Nine Years' War significantly altered the balance of power in Europe.
Historians often debate the causes and consequences of the Nine Years' War.
France suffered heavy losses during the Nine Years' War against the Grand Alliance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: War of the Grand Alliance - alternative name for the Nine Years' War
  • Degree Higher: War of the Spanish Succession - major European conflict directly following the Nine Years' War
