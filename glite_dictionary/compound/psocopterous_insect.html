psocopterous insect

1. NOUN

/ soʊˈkɑptərəs ˈɪnˌsɛkt / (AmE), / səʊˈkɒptərəs ˈɪnsekt / (BrE)
A small, soft-bodied insect with chewing mouthparts, belonging to the order Psocoptera, often wingless or with two pairs of wings.
insect of the order Psocoptera

Examples

The psocopterous insect was found living under the damp bark of the old tree.
Scientists collected several species of psocopterous insects during their field study of forest microfauna.
A psocopterous insect can sometimes be mistaken for a tiny mite or aphid when observed closely.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: booklouse - a common name for small psocopterous insects found in books or damp areas
  • Synonym: barklouse - a common name for psocopterous insects living on bark

Etymology

From modern Latin Psocoptera (order name), from Greek psōkhein (to rub, grind).
