remittance woman

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈmɪtəns ˈwʊmən / (AmE), / rɪˈmɪtəns ˈwʊmən / (BrE)
A woman, typically living abroad, who is financially supported by regular payments sent from her family or home country, often because her presence at home is considered undesirable.
exiled woman living on money sent from home
archaic

Examples

In colonial Australia, a remittance woman might find herself isolated from both her homeland and local society.
The novel tells the story of a remittance woman surviving on funds from her disapproving family in England.
Rumors circulated about the quiet remittance woman who rarely spoke of her past.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: remittance man - a man living abroad on money sent from home, often due to family disgrace

Etymology

Patterned after 'remittance man'; 'remittance' from Latin 'remittere' (to send back).
