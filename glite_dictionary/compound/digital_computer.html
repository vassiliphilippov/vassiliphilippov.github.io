digital computer

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɪdʒɪt̬əl kəmˈpjuːtɚ / (AmE), / ˈdɪdʒɪtəl kəmˈpjuːtə / (BrE)
A computer that processes information by representing data as numerical (typically binary) digits.
computer using binary digits to process data

Examples

The invention of the digital computer revolutionized the way we process and store information.
Most modern devices, from smartphones to servers, rely on digital computers to perform calculations.
Unlike its analog predecessor, a digital computer stores data in binary form.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: analog computer - a computer that processes data represented by continuously variable quantities
