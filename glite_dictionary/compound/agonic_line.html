agonic line

1. NOUN

/ əˈɡɑː.nɪk laɪn / (AmE), / əˈɡɒn.ɪk laɪn / (BrE)
An imaginary line on the Earth's surface connecting points where the magnetic declination is zero.
line with zero magnetic declination

Examples

The location of the agonic line shifts over time due to changes in the Earth's magnetic field.
Navigators use charts that mark the agonic line to correct their compass readings.
On this map, the agonic line is shown passing through parts of South America and the Atlantic Ocean.

Etymology

From Greek 'a-' (not) + 'gonia' (angle), referring to places of no magnetic angle.
