proboscis flower

1. NOUN

/ proʊˈbɑsɪs ˈflaʊər / (AmE), / prəˈbɒsɪs ˈflaʊə / (BrE)
an annual plant native to the southern United States and Mexico, with large whitish or yellowish mottled flowers and a long curving beak-like structure
annual plant with beaked flowers

Examples

The proboscis flower is known for its unusual, beak-shaped blossoms.
Gardeners in Texas often admire the proboscis flower for its striking colors and unique form.
Pollinators are attracted to the proboscis flower because of its prominent, curved floral structure.
