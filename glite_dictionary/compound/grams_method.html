Gram's method

1. NOUN

/ ɡræmz ˈmɛθəd / (AmE), / ɡræmz ˈmɛθəd / (BrE)
a staining technique used in microbiology to classify bacteria as Gram-positive or Gram-negative, involving gentian violet and other reagents
staining technique for classifying bacteria

Examples

The microbiologist used Gram's method to quickly differentiate between Gram-positive and Gram-negative bacteria.
Understanding Gram's method is essential for anyone studying clinical microbiology.
After performing Gram's method, the cells appeared purple, indicating they were Gram-positive.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Gram stain - the process or result of applying Gram's method to bacteria

Etymology

Named after Hans Christian Gram, the Danish bacteriologist who developed the method in 1884.
