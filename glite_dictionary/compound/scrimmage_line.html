scrimmage line

1. NOUN

/ ˈskrɪmɪdʒ laɪn / (AmE), / ˈskrɪmɪdʒ laɪn / (BrE)
in American football, the imaginary line parallel to the goal lines where players line up at the start of each play
line in American football indicating play start
american

Examples

After each down, the referee marks the new scrimmage line on the field.
The quarterback stood just behind the scrimmage line before calling the play.
Defenders must stay behind the scrimmage line until the ball is snapped.

Synset Examples

the runner was tackled at the line of scrimmage

Related Terms

  • Synonym: line of scrimmage - the same concept, often used interchangeably
