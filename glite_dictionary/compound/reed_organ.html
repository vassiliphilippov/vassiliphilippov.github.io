reed organ

1. NOUN

/ rid ˈɔːrɡən / (AmE), / riːd ˈɔːɡən / (BrE)
A keyboard instrument in which sound is produced by air being forced through free metal reeds, typically using bellows.
keyboard instrument using air-driven reeds

Examples

The old church still has a functioning reed organ that is used during special ceremonies.
She learned to play hymns on her grandmother's antique reed organ.
A skilled technician restored the broken bellows of the reed organ to make it playable again.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: harmonium - a similar keyboard instrument with free reeds and bellows
  • Synonym: pump organ - another term for reed organ, especially in North America
  • Antonym: pipe organ - keyboard instrument producing sound by air through pipes rather than reeds
