the only way out

1. NOUN

/ ði ˈoʊnli weɪ aʊt / (AmE), / ði ˈəʊnli weɪ aʊt / (BrE)
the sole method or possibility for escaping from a place or situation
sole means of escape

Examples

In the dark tunnel, the only way out was through a narrow door at the far end.
Sometimes, asking for help is the only way out of a difficult situation.
For the hostages, the only way out was to wait for the police to negotiate their release.

Synset Examples

The emergency exit was the only way out.
