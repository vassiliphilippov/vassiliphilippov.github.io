sea purse

1. NOUN

/ siː pɝs / (AmE), / siː pɜːs / (BrE)
the strong outgoing undercurrent moving seaward after waves break on the shore
seaward undercurrent after waves break

Examples

Surfers should beware of the sea purse after each wave crashes onto the beach.
Children playing near the shore need to be warned about the strength of the sea purse.
The sea purse can catch swimmers off guard and pull them away from land.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: undertow - a strong current below the surface moving away from shore
