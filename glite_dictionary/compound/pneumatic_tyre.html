pneumatic tyre

1. NOUN

/ nuˈmæt̬ɪk ˈtaɪɚ / (AmE), / njuˈmætɪk ˈtaɪə / (BrE)
A tire made of reinforced rubber and filled with compressed air, used on motor vehicles, bicycles, and similar vehicles.
air-filled rubber tire

Examples

The invention of the pneumatic tyre revolutionized the comfort of automobile travel.
Cyclists prefer a pneumatic tyre for its ability to absorb shocks from rough roads.
He noticed that his bike’s pneumatic tyre had gone flat after running over a nail.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: solid tyre - tire made of solid rubber, not filled with air

Etymology

From Greek pneuma (air, breath), referring to air-filled design.
