Hubbard squash

1. NOUN

/ ˈhʌbərd skwɑʃ / (AmE), / ˈhʌbəd skwɒʃ / (BrE)
a large winter squash with a warty, grey-green rind and sweet orange flesh, typically shaped like a football
a type of large winter squash

Examples

I roasted a Hubbard squash for dinner and served it with brown butter.
The Hubbard squash at the farmers' market was the biggest winter squash I had ever seen.
Soup made from Hubbard squash has a rich, sweet flavor perfect for autumn meals.

Etymology

Named after Elizabeth Hubbard, who introduced the squash in the 19th century, United States.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhʌbərd skwɑʃ / (AmE), / ˈhʌbəd skwɒʃ / (BrE)
a variety of large winter squash with a rough, warty, greyish-green rind and sweet orange flesh
a type of large winter squash

Examples

Many recipes call for roasted Hubbard squash due to its sweet, dense flesh.
She bought a large Hubbard squash at the farmers' market for soup.
The rough, warty skin of Hubbard squash can be challenging to peel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: squash - an edible gourd, vegetable, or the plant that produces it

Etymology

Named after Elizabeth Hubbard, an early grower and promoter in the United States in the mid-19th century.
