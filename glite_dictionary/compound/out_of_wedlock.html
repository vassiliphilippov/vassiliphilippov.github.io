out of wedlock

1. ADV

/ aʊt əv ˈwɛd.lɑk / (AmE), / aʊt əv ˈwɛd.lɒk / (BrE)
(of a child) born to parents who are not married to each other.
born to unmarried parents

Examples

She became pregnant and had her child out of wedlock.
In some cultures, being born out of wedlock once carried a heavy social stigma.
The report shows an increase in children born out of wedlock over the past decade.

Synset Examples

this child was born illegitimately

Related Terms

  • Synonym: illegitimate - (of a child) born to parents not lawfully married
  • Antonym: legitimate - (of a child) born to parents lawfully married

Etymology

From 'wedlock' (Middle English wedlok, Old English weddlāc, meaning marriage) with the prepositional phrase 'out of'.
