ion beam

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪɑn bim / (AmE), / ˈaɪɒn biːm / (BrE)
a stream of electrically charged atoms or molecules (ions) moving together in the same direction, typically at high speed
stream of moving ions

Examples

The accelerator produced a powerful ion beam used for medical research.
Scientists focused the ion beam to modify the surface of the material.
During the experiment, the ion beam was carefully controlled to achieve accurate results.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: particle beam - a stream of charged or neutral particles moving in the same direction
