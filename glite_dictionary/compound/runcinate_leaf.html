runcinate leaf

1. NOUN

/ ˈrʌnsɪˌneɪt liːf / (AmE), / ˈrʌnsɪˌneɪt liːf / (BrE)
A leaf with deeply incised margins where the lobes or teeth curve toward the base, as seen in a dandelion leaf.
leaf with backward-curved lobes
medical

Examples

The dandelion is well known for its runcinate leaf shape, which helps distinguish it from other plants.
A botanist identified the specimen by noting its runcinate leaf margins curving toward the base.
Many wild salads contain plants with a runcinate leaf, adding both visual interest and texture.

Etymology

From Latin 'runcinatus', meaning 'sawed off'.
