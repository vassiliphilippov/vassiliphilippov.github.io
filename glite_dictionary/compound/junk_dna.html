junk DNA

1. NOUN

/ dʒʌŋk diː ɛn eɪ / (AmE), / dʒʌŋk diː ɛn eɪ / (BrE)
DNA sequences that do not code for proteins or functional genes and whose function, if any, is largely unknown.
non-coding DNA sequences
medical

Examples

Scientists once believed that junk DNA served no purpose in the genome.
Only a small fraction of our DNA contains genes, while the rest is mostly junk DNA.
Recent studies suggest that some junk DNA may play a regulatory role.

Synset Examples

most of the genome consists of junk DNA
