sovok aesthetic

1. NOUN

/ səˈvoʊk ɛsˈθɛtɪk / (AmE), / səˈvɒk ɪsˈθetɪk / (BrE)
a style of design or visual appearance characterized by features typical of the Soviet era, often seen as utilitarian, outdated, or drab.
Soviet-era design style or look

Examples

The apartment's sovok aesthetic reminded him of his childhood in Moscow.
Many young artists are reclaiming the sovok aesthetic in their modern installations.
She criticized the hotel's lobby for its outdated sovok aesthetic.

Synset Examples

She couldn't stand the sovok aesthetic of the old cafe.

Etymology

From Russian 'sovok' (slang for 'Soviet' or associated with the Soviet Union) and 'aesthetic' (concerned with beauty or appreciation of art).
