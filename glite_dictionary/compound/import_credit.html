import credit

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪmpɔrt ˈkrɛdɪt / (AmE), / ˈɪmpɔːt ˈkrɛdɪt / (BrE)
a line of credit provided by a bank to an importer, allowing an exporter to draw funds for goods shipped
bank credit for import transactions

Examples

The company secured an import credit to finance the purchase of machinery from overseas suppliers.
An import credit can reduce risk for both the importer and exporter in international trade.
Many banks offer import credit facilities to support clients engaged in global commerce.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: letter of credit - a letter issued by a bank guaranteeing payment to a seller
