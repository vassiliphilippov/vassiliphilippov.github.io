kangaroo rat

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæŋɡəˌru ˌræt / (AmE), / ˈkaŋɡəˌruː ræt / (BrE)
A small, rabbit-sized Australian marsupial resembling a rat but related to kangaroos.
Australian marsupial resembling a rat

Examples

The kangaroo rat moves quickly across the Australian outback in search of food.
Unlike true rats, the kangaroo rat is actually a marsupial related to kangaroos.
Scientists observed the kangaroo rat hopping on its strong hind legs.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkæŋɡəˌru ˌræt / (AmE), / ˈkaŋɡəˌruː ˌræt / (BrE)
a small, jumping rodent native to desert regions of North America and Mexico, known for its long hind legs and tail
small desert-jumping rodent

Examples

The kangaroo rat can survive in the desert without ever drinking water directly.
Researchers observed the kangaroo rat hopping quickly to escape predators.
The long tail of a kangaroo rat helps it balance when leaping across the sand.

Etymology

Named for its kangaroo-like leaping movement and long hind legs, though not closely related to true kangaroos.
