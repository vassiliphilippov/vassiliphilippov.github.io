Sonoran lyre snake

1. NOUN

/ səˈnɔrən ˈlaɪər sneɪk / (AmE), / səˈnɔːrən ˈlaɪə sneɪk / (BrE)
A species of snake (Trimorphodon lambda) native to desert regions of southwestern North America, known for its lyre-shaped head markings.
species of desert snake native to southwestern North America

Examples

The Sonoran lyre snake is most active at night during the hottest months of the year.
Unlike other desert snakes, the Sonoran lyre snake is skilled at climbing rocky slopes and cliffs.
You can identify a Sonoran lyre snake by the distinctive lyre-shaped pattern on its head.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lyre snake - snake species known for lyre-shaped markings on the head
