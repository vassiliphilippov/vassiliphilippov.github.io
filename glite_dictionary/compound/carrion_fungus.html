carrion fungus

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæriən ˈfʌŋɡəs / (AmE), / ˈkæriən ˈfʌŋɡəs / (BrE)
Any of various ill-smelling brown-capped fungi of the order Phallales, typically emitting a rotting odor to attract insects.
ill-smelling fungus of the order Phallales

Examples

The carrion fungus released a foul odor to attract flies for spore dispersal.
Naturalists discovered a rare carrion fungus growing in the damp forest soil.
The dog was drawn to the smell of a carrion fungus hidden among the leaves.

Synset Examples

the foul smell of the stinkhorn attracts insects that carry the spores away on their feet

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stinkhorn - a type of foul-smelling fungus in the order Phallales

Etymology

From 'carrion' (decaying flesh) and 'fungus'; fungus so named for its flesh-like odor.
