vernier caliper

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɝːniɚ ˈkælɪpɚ / (AmE), / ˈvɜːnɪə ˈkælɪpə / (BrE)
an instrument for making precise linear measurements, consisting of a main scale and a sliding secondary scale (vernier) for increased accuracy
precision measuring tool with a vernier scale

Examples

The technician measured the shaft diameter using a vernier caliper for greater precision.
Before starting the experiment, she checked the thickness of the sheet metal with a vernier caliper.
A vernier caliper allows you to read measurements to a fraction of a millimeter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: micrometer - instrument for precise measurement of small distances

Etymology

Named after Pierre Vernier (1580–1637), French mathematician and inventor of the vernier scale; 'caliper' probably from French 'calibre' or Medieval Latin 'caliperium'.
