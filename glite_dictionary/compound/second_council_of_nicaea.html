Second Council of Nicaea

1. PROPN

/ ˈsɛkənd ˈkaʊnsəl əv naɪˈsiːə / (AmE), / ˈsɛkənd ˈkaʊnsəl əv nɪˈsiːə / (BrE)
The seventh ecumenical council held in 787, which condemned iconoclasm and established rules for the veneration of holy images in the Christian Church.
ecumenical council of 787 rejecting iconoclasm

Examples

The Second Council of Nicaea played a pivotal role in ending the first period of iconoclasm.
Historians often discuss the theological implications of the Second Council of Nicaea.
The decisions made at the Second Council of Nicaea shaped Christian attitudes toward religious images for centuries.

Etymology

From the Latin 'concilium Nicaenum', referencing the city of Nicaea (now İznik, Turkey), where the council was held.
