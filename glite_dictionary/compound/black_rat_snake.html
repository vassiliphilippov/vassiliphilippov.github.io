black rat snake

1. NOUN

/ blæk ræt sneɪk / (AmE), / blæk ræt sneɪk / (BrE)
a large, harmless, shiny black snake native to North America, often found in forests and fields
large shiny black North American snake

Examples

The black rat snake is known for its ability to climb trees in search of prey.
Farmers often welcome the presence of a black rat snake because it helps control rodent populations.
Despite its size and appearance, the black rat snake is not venomous and poses no threat to humans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rat snake - a group of nonvenomous snakes that feed on rodents
