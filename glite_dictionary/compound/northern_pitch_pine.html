northern pitch pine

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɔrðərn pɪtʃ paɪn / (AmE), / ˈnɔːðən pɪtʃ paɪn / (BrE)
a large pine tree native to eastern North America, having needles in clusters of three and rough, thick bark
a pine tree native to eastern North America

Examples

The northern pitch pine thrives in poor, sandy soils where few other trees can survive.
Foresters conducted a survey of the local northern pitch pine population after the wildfire.
The cones of the northern pitch pine require high heat to open and release their seeds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pitch pine - a pine tree native to eastern North America, noted for resinous wood
