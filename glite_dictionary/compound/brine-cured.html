brine-cured

1. ADJ

/ ˈbraɪn ˌkɜrd / (AmE), / ˈbraɪn ˌkɜːd / (BrE)
(of food, especially meat) preserved by soaking in a solution of salt and water.
preserved in saltwater (esp. meat)

Examples

The chef recommended using brine-cured ham for added flavor in the recipe.
Authentic pastrami is often made from brine-cured beef brisket.
She preferred brine-cured olives for their intense, salty taste.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: salt-cured - preserved by treatment with salt
