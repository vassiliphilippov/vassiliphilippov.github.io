Baroque era

1. NOUN

/ bəˈroʊk ˈɛrə / (AmE), / bəˈrəʊk ˈɛərə / (BrE)
The period in European history from about 1600 to 1750, marked by the flourishing of the baroque style in art, architecture, and music.
artistic period 1600–1750 in Europe

Examples

The Baroque era produced some of the most famous music by composers like Bach and Handel.
Many cathedrals built during the Baroque era are known for their elaborate decorations.
The Baroque era transformed European art with its emphasis on drama and grandeur.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Baroque - the artistic style characterized by ornate detail
  • Degree Lower: Renaissance - the period in Europe preceding the Baroque era, from the 14th to the 17th century
  • Degree Higher: Classical era - the period following the Baroque era in European music and art

Etymology

From French 'baroque' (irregular, extravagant) and era (period of time).
