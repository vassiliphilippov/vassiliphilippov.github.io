coin silver

1. NOUN

/ kɔɪn ˈsɪlvɚ / (AmE), / kɔɪn ˈsɪlvə / (BrE)
Silver of the standard purity used for making legal silver coins, typically containing about 90% pure silver.
silver alloy used for coins

Examples

Early American spoons were often made of coin silver rather than sterling.
He collects antique forks crafted from coin silver.
The vase, marked 'pure coin silver', dates back to the 19th century.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: sterling silver - a higher-purity silver alloy (92.5% silver) used for jewelry and tableware

Etymology

From 'coin' relating to currency and 'silver', denoting the metal. Historically, coin silver in the U.S. was usually .900 fine (90% silver, 10% copper).
