Scandinavian nation

1. NOUN

/ ˌskændəˈneɪviən ˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌskandɪˈneɪvɪən ˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
a country that is part of Scandinavia, typically Denmark, Norway, or Sweden
a country in Scandinavia

Examples

Denmark is considered a Scandinavian nation because of its location and cultural ties.
The three main Scandinavian nations are Norway, Sweden, and Denmark.
Each Scandinavian nation has its own language and unique traditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Scandinavia - a region in Northern Europe including Denmark, Norway, and Sweden
  • Degree Higher: Nordic country - any of the countries in the broader Nordic region

Etymology

Scandinavian from Scandinavia (a region in Northern Europe), + nation from Latin natio (nation, people).
