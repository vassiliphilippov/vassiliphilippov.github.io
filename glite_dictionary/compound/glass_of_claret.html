glass of claret

1. NOUN

/ ɡlæs əv ˈklærət / (AmE), / ɡlɑːs əv ˈklærɪt / (BrE)
A glass containing claret, a type of red wine, especially as served with a meal.
glass of red wine (claret)
british

Examples

Would you like a glass of claret with your dinner?
He leisurely sipped his glass of claret by the fireplace.
During the meal, she offered her guest a glass of claret.

Synset Examples

He poured a glass of claret for dinner.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: claret - a type of dry red wine from Bordeaux, France
  • Synonym: glass of wine - a serving of wine in a glass

Etymology

From 'claret', a British term for red Bordeaux wine, derived from French 'clairet' (pale wine).
