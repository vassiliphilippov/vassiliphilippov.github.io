gaining control

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡeɪnɪŋ kənˈtroʊl / (AmE), / ˈɡeɪnɪŋ kənˈtrəʊl / (BrE)
the act of taking possession of property or a situation, especially by force or without the owner's consent
the act of taking possession, often by force

Examples

The company's gaining control of the land sparked protests from the local community.
After the revolution, the army's gaining control of government buildings was swift and decisive.
The gaining control of assets by force is often condemned in international law.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seizure - the act of taking control of something by force or legal right
  • Antonym: loss - the act or process of losing possession or control
