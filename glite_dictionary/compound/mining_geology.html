mining geology

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪ.nɪŋ dʒiˈɑ.lə.dʒi / (AmE), / ˈmaɪ.nɪŋ dʒiˈɒl.ə.dʒi / (BrE)
the branch of economic geology concerned with applying geological knowledge to the exploration and extraction of mineral resources
geology relating to mining

Examples

She decided to specialize in mining geology during her postgraduate studies.
Advances in mining geology have made it easier to locate valuable mineral deposits.
Many universities offer courses in mining geology for aspiring geologists and engineers.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: economic geology - the branch of geology that deals with materials of economic value, such as minerals and fuels
