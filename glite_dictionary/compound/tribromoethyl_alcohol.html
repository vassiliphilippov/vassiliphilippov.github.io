tribromoethyl alcohol

1. NOUN

/ traɪˌbroʊmoʊˈɛθɪl ˈæl.kəˌhɑl / (AmE), / traɪˌbrəʊməʊˈɛθɪl ˈæl.kəˌhɒl / (BrE)
a chemical compound formerly used as an intravenous anesthetic
a chemical anesthetic compound
medical

Examples

The use of tribromoethyl alcohol as an anesthetic has declined due to its side effects.
Researchers studied the effects of tribromoethyl alcohol in laboratory animals.
Because of safety concerns, tribromoethyl alcohol was replaced by newer anesthetic agents.

Etymology

From 'tri-' (three), 'bromo' (bromine), and 'ethyl alcohol' (ethanol), indicating its molecular structure.
