infectious hepatitis

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈfɛkʃəs ˌhɛpəˈtaɪtɪs / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɛkʃəs ˌhɛpəˈtaɪtɪs / (BrE)
an acute and usually mild viral infection of the liver, most often transmitted by contaminated food or water, and caused by the hepatitis A virus
mild form of viral hepatitis (hepatitis A)
medical

Examples

Outbreaks of infectious hepatitis are often associated with contaminated water supplies.
She contracted infectious hepatitis after eating food from a street vendor during her travels.
Vaccination can help prevent infectious hepatitis in countries where sanitation is a concern.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hepatitis A - acute viral liver infection caused by hepatitis A virus
  • Antonym: serum hepatitis - hepatitis B, a more serious, blood-borne form of hepatitis
