tetrachoric correlation

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɛtrəˈkɔrɪk kəˌrɛˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌtɛtrəˈkɒrɪk kəˌrɛlˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
a statistical measure estimating the correlation between two normally distributed variables, each divided into two categories (dichotomized)
statistical measure for dichotomized variables

Examples

The tetrachoric correlation is often used to estimate the relationship between two artificially dichotomized variables.
Statisticians calculated a tetrachoric correlation to assess the association in the survey responses.
Understanding the tetrachoric correlation can be helpful when analyzing psychological test data.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: correlation coefficient - a number representing the degree of correlation between two variables
  • Degree Lower: phi coefficient - measure of association for two binary variables

Etymology

From Greek 'tetra-' (four) + 'choros' (space, place) and 'correlation', referring to classification into four groups.
