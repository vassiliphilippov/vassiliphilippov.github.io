love-in-idleness

1. NOUN

/ ˌlʌv ɪn ˈaɪdlnɪs / (AmE), / ˌlʌv ɪn ˈaɪdlnɪs / (BrE)
a common European wildflower (Viola tricolor) with purple, yellow, and white flowers, from which most common garden pansies are derived
a European wildflower; ancestor of garden pansies

Examples

The old cottage garden was sprinkled with love-in-idleness beside the roses and foxgloves.
Shakespeare mentioned love-in-idleness in 'A Midsummer Night's Dream' as a magical flower.
Many cultivated pansies trace their origins to the wildflower known as love-in-idleness.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: pansy - a popular cultivated garden plant with brightly colored flowers; derived from Viola tricolor

Etymology

Refers to Viola tricolor; the name was popularized by Shakespeare's 'A Midsummer Night's Dream'.
