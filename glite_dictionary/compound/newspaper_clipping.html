newspaper clipping

1. NOUN

/ ˈnuːzˌpeɪpər ˈklɪpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈnjuːzˌpeɪpə ˈklɪpɪŋ / (BrE)
an article, excerpt, or advertisement cut out from a newspaper or magazine, usually to keep for reference or as a memento
article or excerpt cut from a newspaper

Examples

She saved every newspaper clipping about her son's achievements.
He found an old newspaper clipping announcing the opening of the local library.
The biography included several rare newspaper clippings from the 1920s.

Synset Examples

he searched through piles of letters and clippings

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clipping - an item cut from a publication for reference or collection
