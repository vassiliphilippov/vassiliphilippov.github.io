Wilderness Campaign

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪldərnəs kæmˌpeɪn / (AmE), / ˈwɪldənəs kamˌpeɪn / (BrE)
a series of battles fought in 1864 during the American Civil War between General Grant's and General Lee's armies, marked by heavy casualties and indecisive outcomes
series of 1864 Civil War battles between Grant and Lee

Examples

The Wilderness Campaign marked the beginning of Grant's relentless pursuit of Lee's army.
During the Wilderness Campaign, thick forests made conventional tactics nearly impossible.
Both armies endured heavy losses in the bloody battles of the Wilderness Campaign.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Battle of the Wilderness - major battle within the Wilderness Campaign
