Holy Sepulcher

1. PROPN

/ ˈhoʊli ˈsɛpəlkər / (AmE), / ˈhəʊli ˈsɛp(ə)lkə / (BrE)
the tomb in which the body of Jesus Christ was laid after his crucifixion and before his resurrection
tomb of Christ after crucifixion

Examples

Thousands of pilgrims visit the Holy Sepulcher in Jerusalem each year.
The Church of the Holy Sepulcher is considered one of Christianity's most sacred sites.
Legends surround the exact location of the Holy Sepulcher within the church.

Etymology

From Latin 'sepulcrum' (tomb, grave), via Old French 'sepulcre'.
