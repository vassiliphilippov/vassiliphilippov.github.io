Chilean strawberry

1. NOUN

/ tʃɪˈli.ən ˈstrɔːˌbɛri / (AmE), / tʃɪˈliː.ən ˈstrɒb.əri / (BrE)
A species of wild strawberry native to western North America and South America, known as a parent species of many cultivated strawberries.
wild strawberry species; parent of cultivated strawberries

Examples

The Chilean strawberry is valued for its role in the development of modern strawberry varieties.
Gardeners grow the Chilean strawberry both for its fruit and its ornamental qualities.
The fruit of the Chilean strawberry tends to be larger and paler than that of other wild strawberries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strawberry - a sweet soft red fruit with a seed-studded surface
