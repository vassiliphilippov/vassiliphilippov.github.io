order Sphagnales

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdɚ sfæɡˈneɪliːz / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə sfæɡˈneɪliːz / (BrE)
A taxonomic order of mosses consisting of or coextensive with the genus Sphagnum, sometimes placed in a separate subclass in some classifications.
a moss order including Sphagnum

Examples

The order Sphagnales consists almost exclusively of peat mosses found in wetlands.
Some botanists place the order Sphagnales in its own subclass due to its unique characteristics.
Researchers study the ecological role of order Sphagnales in carbon storage within peat bogs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sphagnum - a genus of mosses known as peat moss

Etymology

From New Latin 'Sphagnales', from 'Sphagnum' (a genus of mosses) plus the taxonomic suffix '-ales'.
