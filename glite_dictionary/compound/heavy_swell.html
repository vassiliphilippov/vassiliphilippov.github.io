heavy swell

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛvi swɛl / (AmE), / ˈhɛvi swɛl / (BrE)
a broad and deep undulating movement of the sea, marked by large, rolling waves
large, deep ocean waves

Examples

The ship rocked violently in the heavy swell that followed the storm.
Surfers enjoyed the heavy swell along the coastline after several days of strong winds.
The crew struggled to launch the lifeboats in the heavy swell near the harbor gates.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: surf - waves on the sea that break on the shore
  • Degree Higher: storm surge - an abnormal rise of water generated by a storm
