white honeysuckle

1. NOUN

/ waɪt ˈhʌniˌsʌkəl / (AmE), / waɪt ˈhʌniˌsʌkl̩ / (BrE)
A bushy honeysuckle with twining branches and white or yellowish-white flowers, native to the southern United States.
bushy honeysuckle with white flowers, US South

Examples

The white honeysuckle in her garden filled the air with a sweet fragrance every evening.
They planted white honeysuckle along the fence to create a beautiful and aromatic border.
In the southern states, white honeysuckle is a common sight in woodlands and backyards.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: honeysuckle - a climbing plant with sweet-scented flowers

2. NOUN

/ waɪt ˈhʌniˌsʌkəl / (AmE), / waɪt ˈhʌnɪˌsʌkl / (BrE)
A shrub native to swamps in the eastern United States, with small white to pinkish flowers that resemble those of true honeysuckle.
North American shrub with honeysuckle-like flowers

Examples

The swamps were filled with white honeysuckle, adding a sweet fragrance to the air.
Gardeners often plant white honeysuckle for its attractive flowers and hardy nature.
She recognized the white honeysuckle by its pale blooms and delicate scent.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: honeysuckle - a climbing plant with tubular, sweet-smelling flowers
