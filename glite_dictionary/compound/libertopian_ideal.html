libertopian ideal

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɪbərˈtoʊpiən aɪˈdiəl / (AmE), / ˌlɪbəˈtəʊpiən aɪˈdiːəl / (BrE)
A set of principles advocating for personal freedom and minimal government intervention, often held as an aspiration by supporters of libertarian ideals.
principles favoring personal freedom and minimal government

Examples

Many political debates center around the merits of a libertopian ideal versus greater state involvement.
She argued passionately for the libertopian ideal of maximum individual autonomy.
His novel is set in a society founded on libertopian ideals where government interference is virtually nonexistent.

Synset Examples

His libertopian ideals were evident in his speeches.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: libertarianism - the belief in individual liberty and minimal government
  • Antonym: authoritarianism - advocacy of strict obedience to authority at the expense of personal freedom

Etymology

Blend of 'libertarian' and 'utopian', with 'ideal' from Latin 'idealis' (existing in idea).
