geographical point

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒiːəˈɡræfɪkəl pɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˌdʒiːəˈɡræfɪkəl pɔɪnt / (BrE)
A precise location on the surface of the Earth, defined by geographical coordinates.
precise location on Earth's surface

Examples

The GPS device can identify your exact geographical point anywhere in the world.
Cartographers marked every significant geographical point on their new map.
Researchers visited the remote geographical point to collect soil samples.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: location - A place or position

Etymology

From geography (Greek geōgraphia: earth-writing) and point (Old French, from Latin punctum: a point).
