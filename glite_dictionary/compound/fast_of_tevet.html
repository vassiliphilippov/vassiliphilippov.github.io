Fast of Tevet

1. NOUN

/ fæst əv təˈvɛt / (AmE), / fɑːst əv tɛˈvɛt / (BrE)
(in Judaism) a minor fast day observed on the tenth day of Tevet to commemorate the beginning of the siege of Jerusalem and, in some communities, as a memorial day for Holocaust victims.
Jewish minor fast day on Tevet 10

Examples

Many Jews observe the Fast of Tevet by refraining from food and drink from sunrise to nightfall.
The synagogue announced a special service for the Fast of Tevet to remember the victims of the Holocaust.
During the Fast of Tevet, some communities read special prayers commemorating the siege of Jerusalem.

Etymology

From Hebrew 'Asarah B'Tevet', meaning 'the tenth of Tevet'.
