second hand

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛkənd ˌhænd / (AmE), / ˈsɛkənd ˌhænd / (BrE)
the hand on a clock or watch that indicates the passing of seconds.
clock or watch hand showing seconds

Examples

The second hand on my watch moves smoothly instead of ticking.
I waited until the second hand reached the twelve before starting the timer.
She watched the second hand sweep around the clock as the minutes passed.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: minute hand - the hand on a timepiece that indicates the minutes
  • Degree Lower: hour hand - the hand on a timepiece that indicates the hours
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04171818-n
Synset POS: n

2. ADV

/ ˈsɛkənd ˌhænd / (AmE), / ˈsekənd ˌhænd / (BrE)
(of goods) by buying or acquiring from a previous owner rather than new.
from a previous owner, not new

Examples

She always buys her books second hand to save money.
Most of the furniture in their house was purchased second hand.
He prefers to get his clothes second hand from thrift stores.

Synset Examples

I prefer to buy second hand

Related Terms

  • Synonym: used - pre-owned, not new (of goods)
  • Antonym: new - not previously owned or used
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00506948-r
Synset POS: r

3. NOUN

/ ˈsɛk.ənd hænd / (AmE), / ˈsek.ənd hand / (BrE)
by means of an intermediate person or source rather than directly; not firsthand.
by means of an intermediate source

Examples

She heard about the news only second hand through a mutual friend.
Much of the information was available second hand, not from those who witnessed the events.
He preferred not to judge people based on second hand accounts.

Synset Examples

he could learn at second hand from books

Related Terms

  • Antonym: firsthand - coming directly from personal experience or source

Synset POS: n
