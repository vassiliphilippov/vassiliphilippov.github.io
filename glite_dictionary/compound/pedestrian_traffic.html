pedestrian traffic

1. NOUN

/ pəˈdɛstriən ˈtræfɪk / (AmE), / pɪˈdɛstriən ˈtræfɪk / (BrE)
people moving by foot, especially as a flow or group in public spaces or along streets
people walking in public spaces

Examples

There was heavy pedestrian traffic downtown during the afternoon rush hour.
The city installed more crosswalks to ensure the safety of pedestrian traffic.
Construction on the main street redirected pedestrian traffic to temporary walkways.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foot traffic - the movement of people walking in a particular area
