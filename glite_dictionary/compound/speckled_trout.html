speckled trout

1. NOUN

/ ˈspɛkəld traʊt / (AmE), / ˈspɛkəld traʊt / (BrE)
a species of freshwater trout with a spotted body, valued as a food fish
freshwater trout with spotted body

Examples

The speckled trout is prized by anglers for its fighting spirit and delicate flavor.
We grilled a freshly-caught speckled trout over the campfire last night.
Her photo of a speckled trout won first prize in the nature photography contest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brook trout - a type of freshwater trout native to eastern North America
  • Synonym: rainbow trout - a species of trout known for its colorful markings

2. NOUN

/ ˈspɛkəld traʊt / (AmE), / ˈspɛkəld traʊt / (BrE)
a North American freshwater trout, known for its spotted appearance, that has been introduced in Europe
a North American freshwater trout with spots

Examples

The angler proudly displayed the speckled trout he caught from the mountain stream.
Conservation programs have helped increase the population of speckled trout in some areas.
Many fly fishers travel to Canada for the chance to catch wild speckled trout.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brook trout - a small, speckled North American freshwater trout

Etymology

From 'speckled' (spotted) + 'trout', describing the appearance of the fish.
