European beggar-ticks

1. NOUN

/ jʊˈroʊpiən ˈbɛɡər tɪks / (AmE), / jʊəˈrəʊpiən ˈbɛɡə tɪks / (BrE)
A species of bur marigold native to temperate Eurasia, characterized by its yellow flowers and barbed seeds that attach to animals and clothing.
Eurasian plant; type of bur marigold

Examples

The fields were filled with European beggar-ticks in late summer, their barbed seeds clinging to passing animals.
Hikers often find European beggar-ticks stuck to their clothes after walking through meadows.
Researchers study the spread of European beggar-ticks as part of Eurasian plant ecology projects.
