Milesian philosophy

1. NOUN

/ maɪˈliʒən fəˈlɑsəfi / (AmE), / mɪˈliːzɪən fəˈlɒsəfi / (BrE)
the school of philosophy founded in ancient Ionia, focusing on explanations of nature and the cosmos, associated with thinkers such as Thales, Anaximander, and Anaximenes
ancient Ionian school of philosophy

Examples

Thales is a prominent figure in Milesian philosophy.
Students of ancient Greece often study Milesian philosophy as the foundation of Western science.
Milesian philosophy emphasized natural explanations over mythological ones.

Synset Examples

Thales is a prominent figure in Milesian philosophy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Pre-Socratic philosophy - philosophical thought predating Socrates

Etymology

Milesian: from Miletus, an ancient Greek city in Ionia; philosophy: from Greek philosophia (love of wisdom).
