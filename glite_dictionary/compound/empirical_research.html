empirical research

1. NOUN

/ ɪmˈpɪrɪkəl rɪˈsɝtʃ / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɪərɪkəl rɪˈsɜːtʃ / (BrE)
research based on observation, experience, or experiment rather than theory or pure logic
research based on observed or experimental evidence

Examples

Scientists rely on empirical research to validate their hypotheses and theories.
The findings were based on empirical research rather than speculation.
Her dissertation includes several case studies as examples of empirical research.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: theoretical research - research based mainly on theory rather than observation or experiment

Etymology

Empirical from Greek 'empeirikos' (experienced), and research from Old French 'recercher' (to seek).
