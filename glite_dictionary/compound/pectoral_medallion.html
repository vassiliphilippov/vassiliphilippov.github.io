pectoral medallion

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛktərəl məˈdæliən / (AmE), / ˈpɛktərəl mɪˈdalɪən / (BrE)
a large decorative ornament or medal worn on the chest, especially as part of ceremonial or religious dress
large ceremonial ornament worn on the chest

Examples

The priest wore a golden pectoral medallion during the ceremony.
Ancient Egyptian royalty often displayed elaborate pectoral medallions as symbols of their status.
Her costume was finished with a jeweled pectoral medallion that sparkled in the light.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: medallion - a large medal, typically worn as a decoration

Etymology

From Latin 'pectoralis' (of the chest) and 'medallion' (large medal).
