hard currency

1. NOUN

/ hɑrd ˈkɜrənsi / (AmE), / hɑːd ˈkʌrənsi / (BrE)
A currency that is widely accepted around the world, stable in value, and unlikely to depreciate suddenly.
stable, widely accepted currency

Examples

Many investors prefer to keep their assets in hard currency during times of economic instability.
After the political crisis, the value of their local money plummeted, and hard currency became essential for imports.
The contract required all payments to be made in hard currency to avoid exchange rate risks.

Synset Examples

the countries agreed to conduct their bilateral trade in hard currency, replacing previous barter arrangements
Germany once had a solid economy, good fiscal and monetary policies, and a hard currency

Related Terms

  • Antonym: soft currency - a currency that is likely to depreciate or is not widely accepted in international trade

2. NOUN

/ hɑrd ˈkɝːrənsi / (AmE), / hɑːd ˈkʌrənsi / (BrE)
money in the form of bills or coins, as opposed to checks or electronic payments
money in bills or coins

Examples

Many vendors will only accept hard currency rather than checks or credit cards.
Tourists are advised to bring hard currency when traveling to remote areas.
The country faces a shortage of hard currency due to economic sanctions.

Synset Examples

there is a desperate shortage of hard cash

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cash - money in coins or notes, as distinct from checks or credit
