ivory-pounder

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪvri ˌpaʊndər / (AmE), / ˈaɪvəri ˌpaʊndə / (BrE)
an informal term for a skilled pianist, especially one associated with classical music
informal term for a pianist
informal

Examples

Many jazz clubs have featured a resident ivory-pounder to entertain their guests.
The young ivory-pounder wowed the audience with her technical brilliance.
Though self-taught, he soon became the most talented ivory-pounder in the city.

Synset Examples

She’s an ivory-pounder, known for her emotive piano playing.
As an ivory-pounder, he was celebrated for his classical compositions.
The ivory-pounder delivered an impressive performance at the gala.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pianist - a person who plays the piano, especially professionally

Etymology

From 'ivory' (referring to piano keys, traditionally made from ivory) + 'pounder' (one who strikes repeatedly).
