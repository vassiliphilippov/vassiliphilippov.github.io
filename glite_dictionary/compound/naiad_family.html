naiad family

1. NOUN

/ ˈnaɪˌæd ˈfæməli / (AmE), / ˈneɪˌad ˈfæmɪli / (BrE)
A monotypic family of aquatic plants (Najadaceae) characterized by slender leaves and small flowers.
aquatic plant family (Najadaceae)

Examples

The naiad family includes delicate aquatic plants found in freshwater lakes and ponds.
Botanists studied the leaf structure of several species in the naiad family.
Members of the naiad family are often important for maintaining water quality in their habitats.
