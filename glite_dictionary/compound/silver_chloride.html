silver chloride

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪlvər klaɪˌraɪd / (AmE), / ˈsɪlvə ˈklɔːraɪd / (BrE)
A white, insoluble chemical compound (AgCl) used mainly in photographic materials, electrochemistry, and some medicines.
a white chemical compound (AgCl) used in photography
medical

Examples

Silver chloride is sensitive to light and turns violet when exposed to sunlight.
Photographers once relied on silver chloride paper to produce high-quality prints.
Chemists prepared a solution containing silver chloride for the classroom experiment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silver bromide - a photographic chemical similar to silver chloride

Etymology

From 'silver', referring to the element, and 'chloride', from Latin 'chloridum', meaning a compound with chlorine.
