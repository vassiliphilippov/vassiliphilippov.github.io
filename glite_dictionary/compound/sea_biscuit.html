sea biscuit

1. NOUN

/ ˈsiː ˌbɪskɪt / (AmE), / ˈsiː ˌbɪskɪt / (BrE)
A very hard, unsalted biscuit or bread, formerly used as a staple food by sailors.
hard unsalted sailor's biscuit

Examples

During the long voyage, sailors often survived on sea biscuit and dried meat.
The museum displayed a sea biscuit recovered from a nineteenth-century shipwreck.
He broke a tooth trying to eat an old sea biscuit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hardtack - hard bread or biscuit used as ship's staple
