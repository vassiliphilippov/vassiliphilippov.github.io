religious doctrine

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈlɪdʒəs ˈdɑktrɪn / (AmE), / rɪˈlɪdʒəs ˈdɒktrɪn / (BrE)
a set of beliefs or teachings officially accepted and taught by a religious group
official beliefs of a religion

Examples

Many conflicts have arisen over interpretations of religious doctrine.
The church updated its religious doctrine to reflect modern values.
She spent years studying the religious doctrine of several faiths.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dogma - an official system of principles or beliefs, especially in religion
