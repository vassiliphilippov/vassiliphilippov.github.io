British Crown

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrɪtɪʃ kraʊn / (AmE), / ˈbrɪtɪʃ kraʊn / (BrE)
the symbol of the power and authority of the British monarchy, often used to represent the legal and governmental authority exercised in the monarch's name
symbol of British monarchy's authority
british

Examples

The government acted on behalf of the British Crown in negotiating the treaty.
He was prosecuted for crimes against the British Crown.
Throughout history, many colonies owed allegiance to the British Crown.

Synset Examples

members of the British Commonwealth owe allegiance to the British Crown

Related Terms

  • Synonym: the Crown - the monarchy or reigning monarch as a legal authority
