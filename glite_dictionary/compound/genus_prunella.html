genus Prunella

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs pruːˈnɛlə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs pruːˈnɛlə / (BrE)
The type genus of the bird family Prunellidae, which includes the accentors.
type genus of the accentor bird family

Examples

The genus Prunella includes several species of small passerine birds known as accentors.
Researchers have observed unique mating behaviors among birds in the genus Prunella.
The genus Prunella is primarily found in Eurasia, with species adapted to mountain habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Prunellidae - The bird family containing the accentors

Etymology

Prunella from New Latin, based on Latin 'pruna' (plum), originally applied to birds believed to eat plums.
