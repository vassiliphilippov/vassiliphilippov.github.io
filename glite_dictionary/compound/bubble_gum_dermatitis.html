bubble gum dermatitis

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌb.əl ɡʌm ˌdɜɹ.məˈtaɪ.tɪs / (AmE), / ˈbʌb.əl ɡʌm ˌdɜː.məˈtaɪ.tɪs / (BrE)
A form of allergic contact dermatitis occurring around the lips, especially in children, after chewing bubble gum.
skin allergy from chewing bubble gum
medical

Examples

The pediatrician diagnosed him with bubble gum dermatitis after examining the rash around his mouth.
Some children may develop bubble gum dermatitis due to allergies to certain flavorings in gum.
Her parents noticed recurring bubble gum dermatitis whenever she chewed gum with artificial coloring.
