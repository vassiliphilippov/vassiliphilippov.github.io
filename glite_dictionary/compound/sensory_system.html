sensory system

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛn.sə.ri ˈsɪs.təm / (AmE), / ˈsɛns.ər.i ˈsɪs.təm / (BrE)
a system in the body that receives and processes sensory information, such as sight, hearing, or touch
system for processing sensory input

Examples

The human body contains several sensory systems, including vision, hearing, taste, smell, and touch.
Damage to the sensory system can result in loss of sensation or altered perception.
Scientists study how each sensory system processes external stimuli differently.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nervous system - the network of nerve cells and fibers transmitting signals between parts of the body

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɛn.sə.ri ˈsɪs.təm / (AmE), / ˈsɛn.səri ˈsɪs.təm / (BrE)
the parts of the body, including sense organs and associated nerves, responsible for detecting and processing sensory information such as sight, sound, taste, smell, and touch.
body's system of sense organs

Examples

The human sensory system includes organs such as the eyes, ears, nose, tongue, and skin.
Researchers studied how the sensory system responds to different types of stimuli.
Damage to the sensory system can result in a loss of vision, hearing, or touch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nervous system - the network of nerve cells and fibers transmitting signals in the body
  • Antonym: motor system - the part of the nervous system that controls movement

Etymology

From Latin "sensus" (feeling, perception) and "systema" (organized whole).
