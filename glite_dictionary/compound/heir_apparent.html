heir apparent

1. NOUN

/ ɛr əˈpærənt / (AmE), / eə(r) əˈpærənt / (BrE)
A person who is first in line to inherit a title, throne, or estate and whose right to inherit cannot be displaced by the birth of another heir.
person guaranteed to inherit title or estate

Examples

As the heir apparent, Prince Charles was expected to succeed Queen Elizabeth II.
Despite being named heir apparent, he worked hard to earn the respect of his future subjects.
The law recognized her as the heir apparent to the family estate.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: heir presumptive - an heir whose claim can be displaced by the birth of a more eligible heir

Etymology

From Old French 'heir' (successor) and late Latin 'apparens' (appearing, evident).
