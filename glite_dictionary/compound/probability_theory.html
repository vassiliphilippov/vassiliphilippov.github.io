probability theory

1. NOUN

/ prəˌbæbɪlɪti ˈθɪri / (AmE), / prɒbəˈbɪlɪti ˈθɪəri / (BrE)
the branch of mathematics concerned with the analysis and calculation of probabilities and random events
branch of mathematics about probabilities

Examples

She studied probability theory to better understand statistical analysis in her research.
Probability theory is fundamental for modeling random processes in science and engineering.
Many card games rely on concepts from probability theory to calculate odds and make strategic decisions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: statistics - the branch of mathematics dealing with data collection, analysis, interpretation, and presentation

Etymology

Formed from 'probability' (from Latin probabilitas, likelihood) and 'theory' (from Greek theoria, contemplation).
