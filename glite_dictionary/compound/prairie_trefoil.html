prairie trefoil

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɛri ˈtrɛfɔɪl / (AmE), / ˈpreəri ˈtrɛfɔɪl / (BrE)
A North American annual plant with red or rose-colored flowers, commonly found in prairies.
North American plant with red or pink flowers

Examples

The fields were dotted with blooming prairie trefoil during late spring.
Botanists recorded the spread of prairie trefoil across the restored grasslands.
She planted prairie trefoil to attract pollinators to her garden.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trefoil - any plant of the genus Trifolium, with three-lobed leaves

Etymology

From 'prairie' (French, meaning meadow or grassland) and 'trefoil' (from Latin trifolium, three-leaved plant).
