shagbark hickory

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃæɡˌbɑrk ˈhɪkəri / (AmE), / ˈʃaɡˌbɑːk ˈhɪkəri / (BrE)
A North American hickory tree (Carya ovata) with loose, shaggy grey bark and edible nuts.
North American tree with shaggy bark and edible nuts

Examples

The shagbark hickory is easily recognized by its distinctive peeling bark.
Native Americans traditionally used shagbark hickory nuts as a food source.
Our property is shaded by several mature shagbark hickories.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hickory - a type of North American tree of the genus Carya
