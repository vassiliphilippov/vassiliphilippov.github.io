family Pelobatidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ˌpɛloʊˈbætɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli ˌpɛləʊˈbatɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of amphibians commonly known as spadefoot toads, characterized by their burrowing habits and distinctive spade-like projections on their hind feet
family of spadefoot toads (amphibians)

Examples

The family Pelobatidae includes several species adapted to a burrowing lifestyle.
Biologists study the family Pelobatidae to learn more about amphibian evolution.
Several European and North American toads belong to the family Pelobatidae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spadefoot toad - toad belonging to the family Pelobatidae, known for burrowing

Etymology

From modern Latin Pelobatidae, based on genus Pelobates, from Greek pelos (mud) and bates (one who treads).
