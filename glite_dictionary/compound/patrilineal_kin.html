patrilineal kin

1. NOUN

/ ˌpætrəˈlɪniəl kɪn / (AmE), / ˌpætrɪˈlɪniəl kɪn / (BrE)
a relative who is connected to a person through the father's line of descent
relative on the father's side

Examples

Inheritance was passed down through the patrilineal kin in the ancient tribe.
Family gatherings included both maternal and patrilineal kin each year.
Many societies trace clan membership through the patrilineal kin rather than the maternal line.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: matrilineal kin - relative on the mother's side

Etymology

From Latin 'pater' (father) + 'linea' (line) and English 'kin'.
