all due respect

1. NOUN

/ ɔl du rɪˈspɛkt / (AmE), / ɔːl djuː rɪˈspɛkt / (BrE)
used to politely express disagreement or criticism, while showing that respect is intended for the person addressed
polite phrase used before disagreeing

Examples

All due respect, I think your proposal has some serious flaws.
With all due respect, I must disagree with your assessment.
He started his reply with all due respect before offering his critique.

Synset Examples

She expressed her concerns with all due respect for the committee.
He spoke with all due respect to the judge's decision.
With all due respect, I disagree with your opinion.
