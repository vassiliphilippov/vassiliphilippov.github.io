print run

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɪnt ˌrʌn / (AmE), / ˈprɪnt ˌrʌn / (BrE)
the period or quantity of printing for a particular edition of a publication, such as a newspaper, magazine, or book.
period or quantity of publication printing

Examples

The first print run of the novel sold out within days of its release.
Due to high demand, the magazine increased its print run for the next issue.
The newspaper's print run starts early in the morning to meet delivery deadlines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: edition - a particular version or release of a published text
