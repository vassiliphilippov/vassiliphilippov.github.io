woolly bear moth

1. NOUN

/ ˈwʊli bɛr mɑθ / (AmE), / ˈwʊli beə mɒθ / (BrE)
a caterpillar with a thick, bristly coat of hair, especially the larva of a moth in the family Arctiidae, known for its fuzzy appearance
hairy caterpillar of family Arctiidae

Examples

Every fall, children search the garden for the distinctive woolly bear moth caterpillar.
The woolly bear moth is known for its fuzzy, banded appearance.
Scientists study the woolly bear moth to understand how it survives harsh winter temperatures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: woolly bear - hairy caterpillar, especially of the family Arctiidae
