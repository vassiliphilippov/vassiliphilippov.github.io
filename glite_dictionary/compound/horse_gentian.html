horse gentian

1. NOUN

/ hɔrs ˈdʒɛnʃən / (AmE), / hɔːs ˈdʒɛnʃən / (BrE)
A coarse, weedy perennial herb native to America, having large usually perfoliate leaves and purple or dull red flowers.
North American perennial herb with purple or red flowers

Examples

The garden's wild section features several horse gentian plants blooming in late summer.
Botanists documented the spread of horse gentian across the newly cleared field.
Herbalists once used horse gentian roots for traditional remedies.
