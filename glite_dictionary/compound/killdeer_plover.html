killdeer plover

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɪlˌdɪr ˈplʌvər / (AmE), / ˈkɪlˌdɪə ˈplʌvə / (BrE)
A North American plover with a distinctive call, commonly found around inland waters and fields.
North American plover with a loud call

Examples

We spotted a killdeer plover running along the edge of the pond.
The distinctive cry of the killdeer plover echoed across the open field.
A killdeer plover often fakes a broken wing to distract predators from its nest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plover - any of a family of wading birds with a short bill

Etymology

From 'killdeer' (imitative of its cry) + 'plover' (from Old French 'plovier').
