violet wood sorrel

1. NOUN

/ ˈvaɪələt wʊd ˈsɔːrəl / (AmE), / ˈvaɪələt wʊd ˈsɒrəl / (BrE)
A perennial herb native to eastern North America, characterized by palmately compound leaves and usually rose-purple flowers.
a perennial North American herb with rose-purple flowers

Examples

The violet wood sorrel blooms beautifully in the spring along forest edges.
Gardeners appreciate the delicate appearance of violet wood sorrel in shaded areas.
We spotted a patch of violet wood sorrel with its distinctive rose-purple flowers by the trail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wood sorrel - a group of plants in the genus Oxalis with clover-like leaves and small flowers
