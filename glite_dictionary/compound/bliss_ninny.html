bliss ninny

1. NOUN

/ blɪs ˈnɪni / (AmE), / blɪs ˈnɪni / (BrE)
a person who is foolishly optimistic or naive, especially someone who remains happy and positive regardless of negative reality
foolishly optimistic or naive person
informal

Examples

Only a bliss ninny would ignore the growing list of problems.
Her friends called her a bliss ninny for refusing to worry about the bad news.
Don't be such a bliss ninny; sometimes it's wise to be cautious.

Synset Examples

Only a bliss ninny would think everything will be fine.
Stop being a bliss ninny and face the facts.
He acted like a bliss ninny, ignoring all the warnings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pollyanna - a person with an excessively or blindly optimistic attitude

Etymology

Blend of 'bliss' (extreme happiness) and 'ninny' (foolish person).
