kei apple bush

1. NOUN

/ keɪ ˈæpəl bʊʃ / (AmE), / keɪ ˈæpəl bʊʃ / (BrE)
a vigorous spiny shrub native to South Africa, cultivated for its round yellow juicy edible fruits
South African spiny shrub with edible yellow fruit

Examples

The kei apple bush thrives in warm, sunny climates and produces fruit in abundance.
Farmers along the river have long cultivated the kei apple bush for its tart, edible berries.
The thick, spiny branches of the kei apple bush can also be used to form natural hedges.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kei apple - the edible fruit of the kei apple bush

Etymology

From the Kei River in South Africa, where the species is found, and 'apple', referring to its apple-like fruit.
