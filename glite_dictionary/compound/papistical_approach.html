papistical approach

1. NOUN

/ pəˈpɪstɪkəl əˈproʊtʃ / (AmE), / pəˈpɪstɪkəl əˈprəʊtʃ / (BrE)
A method or style of governance or policy making that resembles that of the Pope or the papacy, often implying strong central authority and religious influence.
Pope-like style of governance
archaic

Examples

The new leader's papistical approach alienated local communities seeking more autonomy.
Historians argue that a papistical approach stifles dissent within religious organizations.
Critics accused the administration of adopting a papistical approach to education reform.

Synset Examples

The papistical approach to governance faced backlash.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: papal - Relating to the Pope or the papacy

Etymology

From 'papistical' (pertaining to the Pope or the papacy) and 'approach' (method or way of doing something).
