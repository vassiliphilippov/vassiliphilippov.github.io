genu valgum

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒɛnju ˈvælɡəm / (AmE), / ˌdʒɛnjuː ˈvalɡəm / (BrE)
A condition in which the knees angle in and touch each other when the legs are straightened, causing an inward slant of the thighs; commonly known as knock-knee.
inward-angled knees (knock-knee)
medical

Examples

The orthopedist diagnosed her with genu valgum after a careful examination of her gait.
Children with genu valgum often outgrow the condition as their bones mature.
Treatment for genu valgum may include physical therapy or, in severe cases, surgery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: knock-knee - a condition where the knees touch but the ankles do not

Etymology

From Latin genu (knee) + valgum (knock-kneed).
