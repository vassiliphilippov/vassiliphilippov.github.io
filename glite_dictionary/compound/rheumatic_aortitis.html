rheumatic aortitis

1. NOUN

/ ruˈmætɪk ɔrˈtaɪtɪs / (AmE), / ruːˈmatɪk ɔːˈtaɪtɪs / (BrE)
Inflammation of the aorta occurring as a complication of rheumatic fever.
aortic inflammation from rheumatic fever
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with rheumatic aortitis following a prolonged case of rheumatic fever.
Complications of rheumatic aortitis can include aortic valve insufficiency.
Early detection of rheumatic aortitis is crucial for effective treatment and prevention of heart damage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aortitis - inflammation of the aorta

Etymology

From 'rheumatic' relating to rheumatic fever and 'aortitis' (inflammation of the aorta).
