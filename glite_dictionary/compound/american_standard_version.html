American Standard Version

1. NOUN

/ əˈmɛrɪkən ˈstændərd vɜrʒən / (AmE), / əˈmɛrɪkən ˈstændəd ˈvɜːʃən / (BrE)
A revised English translation of the Bible, based on the King James Version and first published in the United States in 1901.
revised English Bible translation (1901)

Examples

Many scholars prefer the American Standard Version for its accuracy and literary style.
She owns a rare 1901 edition of the American Standard Version of the Bible.
For comparison, he read passages from both the King James and American Standard Version.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: King James Version - An earlier English translation of the Bible published in 1611

Etymology

Named for its origin and the translation committee's aim for an American English text based on the King James Version.
