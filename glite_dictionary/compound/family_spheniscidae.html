family Spheniscidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæmɪli sfɛˈnɪsɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli sfɛnɪˈsɪdiː / (BrE)
the biological family comprising all living species of penguins
taxonomic group of all penguins

Examples

All living penguin species are classified within the family Spheniscidae.
The evolutionary history of the family Spheniscidae is the subject of much scientific research.
Fossil records help trace the origins of the family Spheniscidae back millions of years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: penguin - flightless seabird of the southern hemisphere

Etymology

From Modern Latin *Spheniscidae*, based on Greek *sphēniskos* (small wedge), referring to the penguin's body shape.
