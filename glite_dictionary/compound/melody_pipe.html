melody pipe

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛlədi paɪp / (AmE), / ˈmɛlədi paɪp / (BrE)
a reed pipe with finger holes on which the melody is played, typically in an organ or similar instrument
reed pipe with finger holes for melody

Examples

The organ builder carefully adjusted each melody pipe before the concert.
In a bagpipe, the melody pipe is known as the chanter, which the player uses to create tunes.
Replacing a cracked melody pipe improved the instrument's overall sound quality.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: drone pipe - a pipe in a musical instrument that produces a constant tone
