marriage of convenience

1. NOUN

/ ˈmærɪdʒ əv kənˈviːniəns / (AmE), / ˈmærɪdʒ əv kənˈviːniəns / (BrE)
a marriage entered into for practical or political reasons, rather than for love or personal affection
a marriage for practical or political reasons

Examples

They entered into a marriage of convenience to secure their families' business interests.
The novel explores the challenges faced in a marriage of convenience compared to a marriage for love.
His citizenship was granted after his marriage of convenience with a local resident.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marriage - the legal union of two people as partners in a personal relationship
