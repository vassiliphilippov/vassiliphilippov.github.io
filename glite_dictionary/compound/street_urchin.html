street urchin

1. NOUN

/ strit ˈɝːtʃɪn / (AmE), / striːt ˈɜːtʃɪn / (BrE)
a poor child who spends most of their time in the streets, especially in slum areas
a homeless or neglected child living on the streets

Examples

The novel tells the story of a street urchin surviving in Victorian London.
A group of street urchins played soccer in the alley despite the rain.
He grew up as a street urchin, fending for himself in the big city.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: waif - a homeless, neglected, or abandoned child
  • Synonym: ragamuffin - a child in ragged, dirty clothes

Etymology

Urchin from Middle English 'urchon', meaning hedgehog, later used to describe mischievous or ragged children.
