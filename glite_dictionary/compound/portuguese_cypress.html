Portuguese cypress

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɔrtʃəˌgiːz ˈsaɪprəs / (AmE), / ˌpɔːtʃəˌgiːz ˈsʌɪprəs / (BrE)
a tall, spreading evergreen tree native to Mexico, known for its drooping branches and introduced into Portugal from Goa
a tall spreading evergreen tree

Examples

The Portuguese cypress is often seen in formal gardens due to its elegant, drooping branches.
After visiting Portugal, she planted a Portuguese cypress in her backyard as a reminder of her trip.
Experts believe the Portuguese cypress was first introduced from Goa and has since become widespread.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Monterey cypress - a cypress native to California with similar appearance
