mesne lord

1. NOUN

/ miːn lɔrd / (AmE), / miːn lɔːd / (BrE)
a feudal lord who holds land from a superior lord while acting as lord to his own tenants
intermediate feudal lord
legal

Examples

The mesne lord collected rent from his tenants and paid homage to the overlord.
Under the feudal system, a mesne lord acted as both tenant and landlord.
Disputes sometimes arose between the mesne lord and those above or below him in the hierarchy.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: overlord - a lord who has authority over other lords
  • Degree Lower: tenant - one who holds or possesses land or property by any kind of right or title

Etymology

From Anglo-French *mesne* (middle, intermediate) and *lord*.
