anguid lizard

1. NOUN

/ ˈæŋɡwɪd ˈlɪzərd / (AmE), / ˈæŋɡwɪd ˈlɪzəd / (BrE)
a lizard belonging to the family Anguidae, found in warm regions, harmless and often beneficial as a predator of slugs and insects
lizard of the Anguidae family

Examples

The anguid lizard is often found in gardens where it feeds on pests like slugs.
Glass lizards are a type of anguid lizard with a snake-like appearance.
Many people mistake the anguid lizard for being dangerous, but they are completely harmless.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glass lizard - a legless lizard from the Anguidae family

Etymology

From Latin 'anguis' (snake), referencing the snake-like appearance of some members of the family.
