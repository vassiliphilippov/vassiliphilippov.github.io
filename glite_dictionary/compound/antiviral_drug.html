antiviral drug

1. NOUN

/ ˌæntiˈvaɪrəl drʌɡ / (AmE), / ˌæntɪˈvaɪərəl drʌɡ / (BrE)
a drug used to treat viral infections by inhibiting the development or replication of viruses
drug treating viral infections
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed an antiviral drug to help her recover from the flu more quickly.
Research into new antiviral drugs has increased during recent virus outbreaks.
Patients with certain chronic infections may require ongoing treatment with an antiviral drug.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: antibiotic - a drug used to treat bacterial infections
  • Synonym: antiviral - acting to inhibit the development or replication of viruses
