house sparrow

1. NOUN

/ haʊs ˈspɛroʊ / (AmE), / haʊs ˈspærəʊ / (BrE)
a small, hardy brown-and-grey bird (Passer domesticus) native to Europe and widely introduced elsewhere
a small brown-and-grey European bird

Examples

A house sparrow perched on the garden fence, chirping loudly in the early morning.
In many cities, the house sparrow has adapted to living close to humans.
The children watched as a flock of house sparrows pecked at crumbs on the sidewalk.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sparrow - a small bird of the family Passeridae
