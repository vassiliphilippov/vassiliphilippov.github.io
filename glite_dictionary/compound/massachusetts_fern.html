Massachusetts fern

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæsəˈtʃuːsɪts fɜrn / (AmE), / ˌmæsəˈtʃuːsɪts fɜːn / (BrE)
A delicate, feathery shield fern native to the eastern United States, sometimes classified in the genus Thelypteris.
a feathery shield fern of eastern US

Examples

We found a cluster of Massachusetts ferns growing along the shaded stream bank.
The Massachusetts fern is prized for its lacy foliage in woodland gardens.
Botanists occasionally debate whether the Massachusetts fern belongs in the genus Thelypteris.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fern - a flowerless plant with feathery or leafy fronds
