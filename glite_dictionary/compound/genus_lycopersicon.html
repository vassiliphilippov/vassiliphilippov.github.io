genus Lycopersicon

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs laɪˌkɒ.pərˈsaɪ.kən / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs lɪˌkɒ.pəˈsaɪ.kən / (BrE)
a former botanical genus that included the tomato and several related species, now usually classified under the genus Solanum
former genus for tomato plants

Examples

The species once classified under genus Lycopersicon are now typically placed in Solanum.
Many botanical texts still reference genus Lycopersicon in discussions of tomato cultivation.
Gardeners sometimes encounter the name genus Lycopersicon on old seed packets or plant labels.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tomato - the edible fruit or plant commonly associated with Lycopersicon

Etymology

From Greek 'lykopersikon', meaning 'wolf peach'.
