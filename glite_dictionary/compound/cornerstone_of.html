cornerstone of

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔrnərstoʊn əv / (AmE), / ˈkɔːnəstəʊn ɒv / (BrE)
an essential, fundamental principle or base on which something is built or depends
key foundational principle or basis

Examples

Honesty is the cornerstone of her business philosophy.
Education is often regarded as the cornerstone of a thriving democracy.
Respect and communication form the cornerstone of a healthy marriage.

Synset Examples

Honesty is the cornerstone of her business philosophy.
Education is the cornerstone of a successful society.
Trust is the cornerstone of any good relationship.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foundation of - underlying basis or principle supporting something
  • Synonym: bedrock of - basic, underlying principle or element

Etymology

From 'cornerstone', meaning the first stone set in the construction of a masonry foundation, symbolizing a fundamental element.
