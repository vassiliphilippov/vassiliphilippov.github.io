Spielmeyer-Vogt disease

1. NOUN

/ ˌspiːlmaɪər ˈfɔːkt dɪˌziz / (AmE), / ˌspiːlmaɪə ˈvɒkt dɪˌziːz / (BrE)
A rare inherited disorder of lipid metabolism that begins in early childhood and causes progressive blindness, dementia, and early death.
rare inherited brain disorder in children
medical

Examples

Doctors diagnosed the child with Spielmeyer-Vogt disease after he began to lose his vision and cognitive abilities.
Because Spielmeyer-Vogt disease is inherited, genetic counseling was recommended for the affected family.
Research into treatments for Spielmeyer-Vogt disease is ongoing due to its severity and early onset.

Etymology

Named after German neurologists Heinrich Spielmeyer and Walter Vogt.
