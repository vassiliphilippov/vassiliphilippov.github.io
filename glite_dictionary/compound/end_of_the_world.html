end of the world

1. NOUN

/ ɛnd əv ðə wɝːld / (AmE), / ɛnd əv ðə wɜːld / (BrE)
(in Christian eschatology) the final event in the world’s history, when God is believed to judge all human beings, determining their eternal fate.
final judgment and end of earthly life

Examples

Many people throughout history have predicted the end of the world would occur in their lifetime.
Religious texts often describe the end of the world as a time of judgment.
Some interpret natural disasters as signs pointing toward the end of the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Judgment Day - the day at the end of time when God judges all humans

2. NOUN

/ ˌɛnd əv ðə ˈwɝːld / (AmE), / ˌɛnd əv ðə ˈwɜːld / (BrE)
an imagined or perceived disastrous event or situation, often used to describe something that feels catastrophic but is not truly so
an imagined disastrous situation

Examples

For her, not getting the job felt like the end of the world at the time.
When Tom forgot his lines on stage, he thought it was the end of the world.
Missing one homework assignment isn’t the end of the world, so don’t stress too much.

Synset Examples

It felt like the end of the world after the breakup.
They joked about it being the end of the world.
Losing the game was disappointing, but not the end of the world.
Missing the bus isn’t the end of the world.
If you fail, it’s not the end of the world.
Some people act like every mistake is the end of the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catastrophe - an event causing great and often sudden damage or suffering
  • Synonym: crisis - a time of intense difficulty, trouble, or danger
