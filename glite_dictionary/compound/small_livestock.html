small livestock

1. NOUN

/ smɔl ˈlaɪvˌstɑk / (AmE), / smɔːl ˈlaɪvstɒk / (BrE)
Domesticated animals of relatively small size, such as chickens, ducks, rabbits, and similar animals, kept for use or profit.
small domesticated farm animals

Examples

The farm specializes in raising small livestock such as rabbits and chickens.
Many homesteaders keep small livestock for eggs, meat, and additional income.
A shed was built to shelter the small livestock during the winter months.
