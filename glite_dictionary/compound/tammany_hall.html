Tammany Hall

1. NOUN

/ ˌtæməni ˈhɔl / (AmE), / ˌtæməni ˈhɔːl / (BrE)
a powerful political organization within the Democratic Party in New York City, active primarily in the late 19th and early 20th centuries, known for its influence and corruption
New York City political organization, historic, often corrupt
american

Examples

Many reformers in the late 19th century campaigned against Tammany Hall and its corrupt practices.
The influence of Tammany Hall was felt in every aspect of New York City's government for decades.
Boss Tweed was one of the most notorious leaders associated with Tammany Hall.

Etymology

Named after Tamanend, a respected 17th-century Native American leader, whose name was used symbolically by the organization.
