clinid fish

1. NOUN

/ ˈklaɪnɪd fɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈklɪnɪd fɪʃ / (BrE)
A small, usually elongated marine fish found mainly on coral reefs and seagrass beds, belonging to the family Clinidae.
a small marine fish of the family Clinidae

Examples

The vibrant colors of the clinid fish help it camouflage among coral reefs.
A clinid fish can often be found hiding in the crevices of seagrass beds.
Marine biologists studied several clinid fish to learn more about their unique behaviors.

Etymology

From New Latin Clinidae (the family name), based on the genus Clinus.
