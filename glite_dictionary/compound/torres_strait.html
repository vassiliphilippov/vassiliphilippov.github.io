Torres Strait

1. PROPN

/ ˈtɔːrəz streɪt / (AmE), / ˈtɒrɪz streɪt / (BrE)
a strait between northeastern Australia and southern New Guinea, connecting the Coral Sea with the Arafura Sea
strait between Australia and New Guinea

Examples

Many ships pass through the Torres Strait on their way between Australia and Asia.
The Torres Strait is home to a diverse range of marine life.
The islands in the Torres Strait are inhabited by Indigenous Torres Strait Islanders.

Etymology

Named after Luis Váez de Torres, the Spanish navigator who sailed through it in 1606.
