bells of Ireland

1. NOUN

/ bɛlz əv ˈaɪərlənd / (AmE), / bɛlz əv ˈaɪələnd / (BrE)
an annual plant (Moluccella laevis) with tall stems and small white flowers enclosed in large, bell-shaped green calyces, commonly grown for ornamental use
ornamental plant with bell-shaped green calyces

Examples

We planted bells of Ireland in the border to add some height and green contrast to the flower bed.
Florists often use bells of Ireland in arrangements because of their striking color and shape.
The tall stems of bells of Ireland make them an excellent choice for cut flower displays.

Etymology

The name refers to the shape and color of the plant's green calyces, resembling bells, and its resemblance to the color associated with Ireland. Native to the eastern Mediterranean.
