parlor car

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑrlɚ kɑr / (AmE), / ˈpɑːlə kɑː / (BrE)
a railroad passenger car offering comfortable individual seats, usually requiring an extra fare, for daytime travel
railroad car with individual chairs for extra fare

Examples

She paid extra to ride in the parlor car for a more comfortable trip.
The train's parlor car offered wide, reclining seats and large windows.
Only first-class passengers were allowed access to the parlor car during the journey.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Pullman car - luxurious railroad passenger car, often with sleeping accommodations
