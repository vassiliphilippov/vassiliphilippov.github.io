Brewer's mole

1. NOUN

/ ˈbruːərz moʊl / (AmE), / ˈbruːəz məʊl / (BrE)
a species of mole native to eastern North America, known for its velvety fur and subterranean lifestyle
mole species from eastern North America

Examples

The Brewer's mole is rarely seen above ground, as it spends most of its life tunneling underground.
Due to habitat loss, the population of Brewer's moles has declined in some areas.
Researchers studied the diet of the Brewer's mole to better understand its ecological role.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mole - a small burrowing mammal with velvety fur

Etymology

Named after Thomas Mayo Brewer, an American naturalist.
