family Potamogalidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌfæməli poʊˌtæmoʊɡəˈlaɪdi / (AmE), / ˌfæmɪli pəʊˌtæməʊɡəˈlaɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of small, semi-aquatic mammals known as otter shrews, native to central and western Africa.
a family of mammals (otter shrews)

Examples

The family Potamogalidae includes the three species of otter shrews found in Africa.
Researchers have studied the family Potamogalidae to better understand the evolution of semi-aquatic mammals.
Unlike true otters, members of the family Potamogalidae are more closely related to tenrecs.

Etymology

From scientific Latin Potamogalidae, based on Greek potamos (river) and galē (weasel).
