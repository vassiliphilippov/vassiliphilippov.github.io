to face vitriol

1. VERB

/ feɪs ˈvɪtriəl / (AmE), / feɪs ˈvɪtrɪɒl / (BrE)
to experience harsh and abusive criticism, especially in public or online settings
experience harsh criticism or abuse

Examples

After publishing her article, the journalist began to face vitriol from readers in the comments section.
Public figures often face vitriol for expressing controversial opinions online.
He was prepared to face vitriol from critics after releasing the new policy proposal.

Synset Examples

He faced vitriol online after sharing his opinion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: receive criticism - to be the target of criticism
  • Antonym: face praise - to receive praise (opposite of being criticized)
