beggar-my-neighbour policy

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛɡər maɪ ˈneɪbər ˈpɑːləsi / (AmE), / ˈbɛɡə maɪ ˈneɪbə ˈpɒləsi / (BrE)
A government or national policy that seeks to improve its own economic situation at the expense of other countries, often by means such as tariffs or competitive devaluation.
national policy of self-benefit at others' expense

Examples

During the Great Depression, many countries adopted a beggar-my-neighbour policy to protect their own economies.
A beggar-my-neighbour policy can lead to trade wars and increased tensions between nations.
Experts argue that a global recession is worsened by each country’s reliance on a beggar-my-neighbour policy.

Synset Examples

the United States has pursued a beggar-my-neighbor policy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: protectionism - the policy of restricting imports to protect domestic industries
  • Antonym: free trade - unrestricted international exchange of goods and services

Etymology

From the card game 'beggar-my-neighbour', referring metaphorically to a policy benefiting one party while harming others.
