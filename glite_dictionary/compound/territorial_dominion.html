territorial dominion

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɛrəˈtɔriəl dəˈmɪnjən / (AmE), / ˌtɛrɪˈtɔːrɪəl dəˈmɪnjən / (BrE)
An area of land under the control or authority of a particular ruler, government, or administrative entity.
area under authority or control

Examples

The country expanded its territorial dominion by annexing neighboring lands.
Each colonial power maintained strict control over its territorial dominion in the region.
Disputes over territorial dominion have led to conflicts throughout history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: territory - An area of land under the jurisdiction of a ruler or state
  • Synonym: dominion - Sovereignty or control; the power or right of governing and controlling

Etymology

From Latin 'territorium' (land, territory) and 'dominium' (ownership, rule).
