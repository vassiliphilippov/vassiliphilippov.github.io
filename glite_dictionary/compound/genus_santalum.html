genus Santalum

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs sænˈteɪləm / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈsæntələm / (BrE)
A genus of parasitic trees found in Indonesia and Malaysia, including sandalwood species.
genus of parasitic Indonesian/Malaysian trees

Examples

The genus Santalum includes species well-known for their fragrant heartwood.
Ecologists have studied the unique parasitic nature of trees in the genus Santalum.
Many perfumes use oils extracted from members of the genus Santalum.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sandalwood - A fragrant wood from trees of the genus Santalum

Etymology

From Latin 'santalum', from Greek 'santalon', referring to sandalwood.
