family Pandionidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌfæməli pænˈdaɪ.əˌniːdeɪ / (AmE), / ˌfæmɪli pænˈdɪəʊnɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of large fish-eating birds of prey, comprising the ospreys.
osprey bird family (Pandionidae)

Examples

The family Pandionidae consists of only one living species, the osprey.
Birdwatchers were excited to spot a member of the family Pandionidae near the river.
Taxonomists classify ospreys under the family Pandionidae due to their unique characteristics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: osprey - large fish-eating bird of prey

Etymology

From scientific Latin Pandionidae, based on Pandion (the genus name for ospreys) + the family suffix -idae.
