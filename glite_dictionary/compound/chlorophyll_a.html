chlorophyll a

1. NOUN

/ klɔːrəˌfɪl ˈeɪ / (AmE), / klɔːrəˌfɪl ˈeɪ / (BrE)
the principal form of chlorophyll in higher plants, algae, and cyanobacteria, with a blue-black color in pure form and blue-green in solution
principal plant pigment type
medical

Examples

Chlorophyll a absorbs light most efficiently in the blue-violet and red parts of the spectrum.
Levels of chlorophyll a are commonly used to estimate the amount of algae in water samples.
Scientists analyzed the chlorophyll a content to assess plant health.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chlorophyll - the green pigment found in plants
  • Degree Lower: chlorophyll b - a yellow-green plant pigment, an accessory to chlorophyll a

Etymology

Formed from 'chlorophyll', from Greek 'khlōros' (green) + 'phyllon' (leaf), plus 'a' denoting the primary type.
