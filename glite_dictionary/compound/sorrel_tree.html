sorrel tree

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɔr.əl tri / (AmE), / ˈsɒr.əl triː / (BrE)
an Australian tree known for its acid-tasting foliage
Australian tree with acidic leaves

Examples

The leaves of the sorrel tree are often used in traditional remedies for their tart flavor.
Many gardeners admire the sorrel tree for its unique foliage and decorative value.
Certain animals avoid the sorrel tree because of its acidic leaves.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɔrəl tri / (AmE), / ˈsɒrəl triː / (BrE)
A deciduous tree of eastern North America with deeply fissured bark, sprays of small fragrant white flowers, and sour-tasting leaves.
deciduous tree of eastern North America

Examples

The sorrel tree is known for its clusters of fragrant white flowers that bloom in late summer.
Leaves from the sorrel tree have a distinctly sour taste that gives the tree its common name.
Hikers in the Appalachian region often encounter the sorrel tree growing along wooded slopes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sourwood - another common name for the sorrel tree (Oxydendrum arboreum)
