Nuttall oak

1. NOUN

/ ˈnʌtəl oʊk / (AmE), / ˈnʌtl əʊk / (BrE)
a species of oak tree native to the Mississippi River basin, with similarities to the pin oak, typically found in wet or damp sites
a species of oak tree in the Mississippi basin

Examples

The Nuttall oak is well adapted to wet soils in the southern United States.
Many wildlife species depend on Nuttall oak acorns as a food source.
The distinct leaf shape helps botanists identify the Nuttall oak among other oaks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pin oak - a species of oak tree with deeply lobed leaves and slender branches

Etymology

Named after Thomas Nuttall, English botanist and zoologist (1786–1859).
