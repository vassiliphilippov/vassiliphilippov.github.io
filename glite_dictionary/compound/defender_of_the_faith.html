Defender of the Faith

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈfɛndər əv ðə feɪθ / (AmE), / dɪˈfɛndə əv ðə feɪθ / (BrE)
a title given to English and later British sovereigns, originally granted to King Henry VIII by Pope Leo X and now used as part of the monarch's official style
royal title of English/British monarchs

Examples

Queen Elizabeth II held the official title Defender of the Faith throughout her reign.
The words Defender of the Faith are inscribed on British coins.
As Defender of the Faith, the monarch is seen as a protector of the Church of England.

Etymology

From Latin 'Fidei Defensor', title conferred by Pope Leo X on Henry VIII in 1521.
