metabolic acidosis

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛtəˈbɑlɪk əˈsɪdoʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌmɛtəˈbɒlɪk əˈsɪdəʊsɪs / (BrE)
A condition characterized by increased acidity in the body fluids due to accumulation of acids or abnormal loss of bases, often caused by kidney disease or severe diarrhea.
excess body acid due to metabolic causes
medical

Examples

The blood tests revealed that the patient was suffering from metabolic acidosis caused by uncontrolled diabetes.
In cases of kidney failure, metabolic acidosis can develop due to impaired acid excretion.
Severe diarrhea is a common cause of metabolic acidosis in both adults and children.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: alkalosis - a condition of excess base (alkali) in the body fluids

Etymology

From 'metabolic', relating to metabolism, and 'acidosis', from acid + -osis (condition).
