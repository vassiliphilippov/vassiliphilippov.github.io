Green Beret

1. NOUN

/ ɡrin bəˈreɪ / (AmE), / ɡriːn bəˈreɪ / (BrE)
a member of the United States Army Special Forces, known for specialized and often covert military operations
U.S. Army Special Forces soldier
american

Examples

He proudly served as a Green Beret during some of the most challenging missions overseas.
Becoming a Green Beret requires intense training and exceptional determination.
The Green Beret led his team through hostile territory with remarkable skill.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: commando - a soldier specially trained for carrying out raids

Etymology

From the green beret hat worn as part of the official uniform of the U.S. Army Special Forces, established 1952.
