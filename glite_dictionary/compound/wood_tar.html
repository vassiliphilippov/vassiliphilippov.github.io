wood tar

1. NOUN

/ wʊd tɑr / (AmE), / wʊd tɑː / (BrE)
a dark, viscous liquid obtained by the destructive distillation of wood, used as a preservative or in making chemicals
tar distilled from wood

Examples

Traditional boats were often sealed with wood tar to protect them from water damage.
The pungent smell of wood tar lingered in the air after the process was complete.
Old medical remedies sometimes included small amounts of wood tar as an ingredient.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: coal tar - tar obtained from the destructive distillation of coal
