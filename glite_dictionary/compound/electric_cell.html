electric cell

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈlɛktrɪk sɛl / (AmE), / ɪˈlɛktrɪk sɛl / (BrE)
A device that produces an electric current as a result of a chemical reaction.
device producing electric current by chemical reaction

Examples

A single electric cell can power a small flashlight for several hours.
The chemistry class demonstrated how an electric cell generates electricity through a reaction.
He replaced the exhausted electric cell in his handheld radio.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: battery - a device consisting of one or more electric cells
