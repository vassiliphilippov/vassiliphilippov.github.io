Protestant Reformation

1. NOUN

/ ˌproʊ.t̬ə.stənt ˌrɛf.ərˈmeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌprɒt.ɪ.stənt ˌrɛf.əˈmeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the 16th-century religious movement that sought to reform the Roman Catholic Church, leading to the establishment of Protestant churches
16th-century movement reforming the Catholic Church

Examples

Many historians consider the Protestant Reformation one of the most significant events in European history.
Martin Luther’s 95 Theses are often cited as the starting point of the Protestant Reformation.
The Protestant Reformation led to major changes in religious beliefs and practices across Western Europe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Reformation - the action or process of reforming an institution or practice; often refers specifically to the same historical event

Etymology

From Latin 'reformatio' (restoration, renewal), with 'Protestant' referring to those who protested against certain Catholic practices.
