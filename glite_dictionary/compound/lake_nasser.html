Lake Nasser

1. PROPN

/ leɪk ˈnæsər / (AmE), / leɪk ˈnæsə / (BrE)
a large artificial lake in southern Egypt, formed by the building of the Aswan High Dam on the Nile River
artificial lake in southern Egypt

Examples

Tourists often visit Lake Nasser to enjoy fishing and see the surrounding monuments.
The creation of Lake Nasser has had a significant impact on agriculture in the region.
Crocodiles are sometimes spotted along the shores of Lake Nasser.

Etymology

Named after Gamal Abdel Nasser, president of Egypt (1956–1970).
