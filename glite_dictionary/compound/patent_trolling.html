patent trolling

1. NOUN

/ ˈpætnt ˌtroʊlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpætnt ˌtrəʊlɪŋ / (BrE)
the practice of obtaining or enforcing patents primarily to demand licensing fees or settlements, often without intending to produce or market the patented invention
abusive enforcement of patents for profit
legal

Examples

New legislation aims to reduce patent trolling in the software industry.
Some companies have gone out of business due to aggressive patent trolling lawsuits.
The debate over patent trolling continues among policymakers and technology firms.

Synset Examples

The government is considering laws against patent trolling.
Patent trolling has become a significant issue for tech firms.
They accused him of engaging in patent trolling practices.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: patent troll - a person or company that engages in patent trolling

Etymology

From 'patent' + 'trolling', with 'troll' meaning to provoke or harass for personal gain.
