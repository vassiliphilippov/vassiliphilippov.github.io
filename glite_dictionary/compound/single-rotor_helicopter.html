single-rotor helicopter

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪŋɡəl ˈroʊtər ˈhɛlɪˌkɑptər / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋɡl ˈrəʊtə ˈhɛlɪˌkɒptə / (BrE)
A helicopter with one main rotating blade assembly used for lift and control, typically with a tail rotor for stability.
helicopter with one main rotor

Examples

The single-rotor helicopter took off smoothly from the landing pad.
A single-rotor helicopter usually requires a tail rotor to counteract torque.
Most modern air ambulances use a single-rotor helicopter design for efficiency.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: helicopter - an aircraft with horizontally spinning rotors
