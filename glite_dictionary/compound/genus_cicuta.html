genus Cicuta

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs sɪˈkjuː.tə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs sɪˈkjuː.tə / (BrE)
A small genus of perennial herbs known as water hemlock, with deadly poisonous tuberous roots.
a genus of poisonous plants (water hemlock)

Examples

The roots of genus Cicuta contain compounds that can be fatal if ingested.
Botanists recently discovered a new population belonging to the genus Cicuta near the marshlands.
Children should be warned never to touch plants from the genus Cicuta due to their toxicity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hemlock - a highly poisonous plant of the same family

Etymology

From Latin 'Cicuta' (hemlock).
