bighorn sheep

1. NOUN

/ ˈbaɪˌhɔrn ʃiːp / (AmE), / ˈbaɪˌhɔːn ʃiːp / (BrE)
a wild sheep of mountainous regions in western North America, noted for its large, curved horns in the male
North American wild sheep with curved horns

Examples

The bighorn sheep climbed effortlessly up the steep rocky cliffs.
During our hike, we spotted a herd of bighorn sheep grazing on the plateau.
A male bighorn sheep used its massive horns to battle a rival during mating season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sheep - domesticated ruminant mammal kept for wool or meat
