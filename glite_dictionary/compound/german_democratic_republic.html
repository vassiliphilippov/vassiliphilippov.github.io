German Democratic Republic

1. PROPN

/ ˈdʒɜrmən ˌdɛm.əˈkrætɪk rɪˈpʌblɪk / (AmE), / ˈdʒɜːmən ˌdem.əˈkrætɪk rɪˈpʌblɪk / (BrE)
the former socialist republic in north-central Europe, established in 1949 in the Soviet-occupied zone of Germany and reunified with West Germany in 1990
former East German state (1949–1990)

Examples

Many people fled the German Democratic Republic before the Berlin Wall was built.
The German Democratic Republic was reunified with West Germany in 1990 after decades of division.
Life in the German Democratic Republic was shaped by Soviet influence and socialist policies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: East Germany - common name for the German Democratic Republic
