alder-leaved serviceberry

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔːldər liːvd ˈsɜːrvɪsˌber.i / (AmE), / ˈɔːldə liːvd ˈsɜːvɪsbɛri / (BrE)
a shrub or small tree of northwestern North America with fragrant creamy white flowers and small waxy purple-red fruits
shrub or small tree native to NW North America

Examples

The alder-leaved serviceberry blooms in early spring with clusters of creamy white flowers.
Birds are attracted to the purple-red fruits of the alder-leaved serviceberry during the summer.
Gardeners value the alder-leaved serviceberry for its attractive foliage and ornamental berries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: serviceberry - a shrub or small tree of the genus Amelanchier
