column chromatography

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːləm kɹoʊˌmætəˈɡɹæfi / (AmE), / ˈkɒləm krəʊˌmætəˈɡræfi / (BrE)
A method of chromatography in which mixtures are separated based on selective adsorption as they pass through a column packed with a solid adsorbent.
separation technique using an adsorbent column

Examples

The laboratory used column chromatography to separate the different compounds in the sample.
She improved her results by optimizing the solvent system for column chromatography.
A glass tube filled with silica gel is typically used in column chromatography experiments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chromatography - technique for separating mixtures based on differential adsorption

Etymology

From 'column', a cylindrical container, and 'chromatography', from Greek 'khrōma' (color) + 'graphein' (to write).
