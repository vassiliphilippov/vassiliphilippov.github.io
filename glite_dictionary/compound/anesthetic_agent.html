anesthetic agent

1. NOUN

/ ˌænəsˈθɛtɪk ˈeɪdʒənt / (AmE), / ˌænɪsˈθetɪk ˈeɪdʒənt / (BrE)
a drug that causes temporary loss of sensation in the body, often used to prevent pain during medical procedures
drug causing temporary loss of sensation
medical

Examples

The dentist used an anesthetic agent to numb my gums before the procedure.
Surgeons carefully select the appropriate anesthetic agent for each operation.
Allergic reactions to an anesthetic agent can be serious and require immediate attention.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anesthetic - a substance that induces insensitivity to pain
  • Degree Lower: analgesic - a drug that relieves pain without causing loss of sensation

Etymology

From 'anesthetic,' from Greek 'anaisthēsia' (insensibility), and 'agent,' from Latin 'agentem' (one who acts).
