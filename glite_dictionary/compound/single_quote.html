single quote

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪŋɡəl ˌkwoʊt / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋɡl ˌkwəʊt / (BrE)
a punctuation mark used as a single quotation mark (‘ or ’), often to indicate speech or quotations within quotations
the single quotation mark (‘ or ’)

Examples

To indicate the phrase ‘case closed,’ use the single quote mark on each side.
In British English, it is usual to start quotations with a single quote rather than a double one.
You can type a single quote by pressing the apostrophe key on your keyboard.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: double quote - the symbol (“ or ”) used as a double quotation mark
