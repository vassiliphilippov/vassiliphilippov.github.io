canoe cedar

1. NOUN

/ kəˈnu ˈsiːdər / (AmE), / kəˈnuː ˈsiːdə / (BrE)
A large and valuable species of arborvitae (Thuja plicata), native to the northwestern United States and widely used by Indigenous peoples for making canoes.
large arborvitae tree native to northwestern US

Examples

The native peoples crafted their vessels from the sturdy wood of the canoe cedar.
The forests of the Pacific Northwest are home to the majestic canoe cedar.
A towering canoe cedar grew near the river, its bark valued for traditional uses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: western red cedar - common name for Thuja plicata, a large coniferous tree

Etymology

Named for its traditional use by Indigenous peoples of the Pacific Northwest in building canoes.
