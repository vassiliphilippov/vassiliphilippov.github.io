.45 calibre

1. ADJ

/ ˌfɔːr.ti ˈfaɪv ˈkæl.ɪ.bɚ / (AmE), / ˌfɔː.ti ˈfaɪv ˈkæl.ɪ.bə / (BrE)
of or relating to a gun bore or ammunition with a diameter of 0.45 inches (about 11.43 mm)
relating to a 0.45-inch gun bore or ammo

Examples

The officer carried a .45 calibre pistol as his sidearm.
Collectors often seek original .45 calibre revolvers from the early twentieth century.
She purchased ammunition that was compatible with her .45 calibre handgun.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: .38 calibre - relating to a gun bore or ammunition with a diameter of 0.38 inches
  • Degree Higher: .50 calibre - relating to a gun bore or ammunition with a diameter of 0.50 inches

Etymology

From the numerical value '.45' meaning 0.45 inches, and 'calibre,' from French 'calibre', referring to the diameter of a gun barrel.
