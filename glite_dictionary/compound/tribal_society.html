tribal society

1. NOUN

/ ˈtraɪbəl səˈsaɪəti / (AmE), / ˈtraɪb(ə)l səˈsaɪəti / (BrE)
a society organized chiefly on the basis of kinship, clans, or tribes, often characterized by traditional social structures and customs
society organized as a tribe

Examples

Anthropologists study how a tribal society governs itself and preserves its cultural traditions.
In a tribal society, decisions are often made by a council of elders.
The introduction of modern technology changed many aspects of the traditional tribal society.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tribe - a social group consisting of people related by kinship or custom
