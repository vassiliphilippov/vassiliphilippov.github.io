soft tick

1. NOUN

/ sɔft tɪk / (AmE), / sɒft tɪk / (BrE)
a tick of the family Argasidae that lacks a hard dorsal shield and has mouthparts located on the underside of the head
a type of tick without a hard dorsal shield
medical

Examples

Researchers found several soft ticks on the birds nesting in the abandoned building.
The bite of a soft tick can sometimes transmit diseases to humans and animals.
Unlike hard ticks, a soft tick does not have a rigid shield on its back.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hard tick - tick with a hard dorsal shield (family Ixodidae)
