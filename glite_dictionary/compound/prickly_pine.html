prickly pine

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɪkli paɪn / (AmE), / ˈprɪkli paɪn / (BrE)
a small pine tree native to the eastern United States, especially the Appalachians, characterized by two needles per bundle, dark brown flaking bark, and cones with thorn-tipped scales
a small pine tree with thorn-tipped cones

Examples

The forest was filled with prickly pines whose sharp cones made walking barefoot impossible.
Scientists studied the unique bark of the prickly pine to understand its resilience to pests.
The Appalachian landscape is home to several species, including the prickly pine.
