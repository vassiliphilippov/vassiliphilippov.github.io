Indian mustard

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪndiən ˈmʌstərd / (AmE), / ˈɪndiən ˈmʌstəd / (BrE)
a plant (Brassica juncea) whose leaves are eaten as cooked greens and seeds are used as a spice and for oil; an edible leafy green vegetable.
an edible leafy green plant; Brassica juncea

Examples

She cooked Indian mustard with garlic and served it over rice.
Many Asian recipes call for Indian mustard as a key ingredient in stir-fries.
The farmer's market sells fresh Indian mustard during the spring season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mustard greens - the edible leaves of various mustard plants, including Indian mustard

2. NOUN

/ ˈɪndiən ˈmʌstərd / (AmE), / ˈɪndiən ˈmʌstəd / (BrE)
a plant of Asian origin (Brassica juncea) whose leaves and seeds are used as a potherb and for making mustard
Asian plant used as a potherb

Examples

The spicy leaves of Indian mustard are a popular ingredient in many Asian dishes.
Farmers planted rows of Indian mustard to harvest both the seeds and the tender greens.
Many home gardeners value Indian mustard for its fast growth and rich flavor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mustard - a yellow-flowered plant grown for its seeds and leaves
