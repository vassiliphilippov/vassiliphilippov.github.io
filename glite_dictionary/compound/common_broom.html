common broom

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmən ˌbrum / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən ˌbruːm / (BrE)
a deciduous shrub native to western Europe, grown for its bright yellow flowers and often used as an ornamental plant
yellow-flowered European shrub

Examples

The common broom is easily recognized by its vibrant yellow blossoms in late spring.
They planted common broom along the hillside for both beauty and erosion control.
Gardeners prize common broom for its hardiness and colorful appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: broom - a shrub with slender green stems and small yellow flowers
