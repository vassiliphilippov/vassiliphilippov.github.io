vital capacity

1. NOUN

/ ˈvaɪt̬l kəˈpæsəti / (AmE), / ˈvaɪtəl kəˈpæsəti / (BrE)
the maximum amount of air that can be exhaled after fully inhaling, used as an indicator of lung health
maximum exhaled air after full inhalation
medical

Examples

The doctor measured my vital capacity to assess my lung function after my illness.
Athletes often have a higher vital capacity due to their rigorous training.
Smoking can significantly reduce a person's vital capacity over time.

Etymology

From Latin 'vita' (life) and 'capacity' from Latin 'capacitas' (ability to hold).
