Pahlavi language

1. NOUN

/ pɑːˈlɑːvi ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / pɑːˈlɑːvi ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Iranian language used in Zoroastrian literature from the 3rd to 10th centuries CE
historical Iranian language (Zoroastrian texts)

Examples

Scholars have translated many ancient texts from the Pahlavi language into modern Persian.
The Pahlavi language is essential for the study of early Zoroastrian scriptures.
Few people today can read the Pahlavi language fluently.

Etymology

From Persian 'Pahlavi', referring to Parthia, a historical region in Iran.
