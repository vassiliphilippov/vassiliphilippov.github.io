possum haw

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑsəm hɑ / (AmE), / ˈpɒsəm hɔː / (BrE)
A deciduous shrub native to the southeastern and central United States, noted for its bright red berries and adaptability to wet soils.
a deciduous shrub of the southeastern and central US

Examples

In late autumn, the possum haw is covered with clusters of bright red berries.
Gardeners often plant possum haw to attract birds to their yards.
The leaves of the possum haw turn yellow before falling in the winter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deciduous holly - a synonym for possum haw, another common term for the same plant
