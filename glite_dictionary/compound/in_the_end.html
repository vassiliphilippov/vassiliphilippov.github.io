in the end

1. ADV

/ ɪn ði ɛnd / (AmE), / ɪn ðiː end / (BrE)
after everything has been considered; as the final result or outcome of a process or series of events.
finally; as the final outcome

Examples

In the end, they decided to move to the countryside for a quieter life.
Despite many arguments, in the end they reached a compromise.
You might not understand now, but in the end, it will all make sense.

Synset Examples

ultimately he had to give in
at long last the winter was over

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ultimately - finally; in the end
  • Synonym: eventually - at some later time; in the end
  • Synonym: at last - after a long time; finally

2. ADV

/ ɪn ði ˈɛnd / (AmE), / ɪn ði ˈend / (BrE)
after everything has been considered; finally or eventually
finally; eventually, after a long time

Examples

She worked hard and, in the end, passed all her exams.
They argued for hours but in the end reached a compromise.
You may not agree with him now, but in the end he was right.

Synset Examples

she will succeed in the end

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eventually - in the end, especially after a long delay
  • Synonym: ultimately - in the most important or final way
  • Antonym: at first - at the beginning, before other things happened
