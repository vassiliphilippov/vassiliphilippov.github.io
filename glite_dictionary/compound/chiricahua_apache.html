Chiricahua Apache

1. NOUN

/ ˌtʃɪrɪˈkɑːwə əˈpætʃi / (AmE), / ˌtʃɪrɪˈkɑːwə əˈpætʃi / (BrE)
a variety of the Apache language spoken by the Chiricahua people, primarily in New Mexico, the Mescalero Reservation, and Fort Sill, Oklahoma.
an Apache language variety

Examples

Only a few elders still speak Chiricahua Apache fluently.
A linguist recorded several traditional stories in Chiricahua Apache.
Efforts are underway to teach Chiricahua Apache to younger generations.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Apache - any of the Athabaskan languages spoken by the Apache people

Etymology

Chiricahua from the name of the Chiricahua tribe; Apache from Zuni *ʔa·paču* (enemy).
