grape phylloxera

1. NOUN

/ ɡreɪp fɪˈlɑksərə / (AmE), / ɡreɪp fɪˈlɒksərə / (BrE)
a small, sap-sucking insect (Daktulosphaira vitifoliae) that is highly destructive to grapevines, causing damage to roots and leaves
insect pest of grapevines

Examples

The destruction of vineyards in Europe was largely caused by grape phylloxera in the 19th century.
Many modern grape varieties have been bred to resist grape phylloxera infestations.
Quarantine measures are vital to prevent the spread of grape phylloxera to new wine-growing regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phylloxera - an insect pest of plants that feeds on roots and leaves

Etymology

Phylloxera from Greek phyllon (leaf) and xeros (dry).
