War between the States

1. NOUN

/ wɔɹ bəˈtwin ðə steɪts / (AmE), / wɔː bəˈtwiːn ðə steɪts / (BrE)
Another name for the American Civil War (1861–1865), fought between the northern and southern states of the United States.
alternative term for American Civil War

Examples

Many history books still refer to the conflict as the War between the States.
Some descendants of Confederate soldiers prefer the term War between the States over Civil War.
The War between the States had a profound impact on American society and politics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: American Civil War - The civil war in the United States from 1861 to 1865

Etymology

An American English phrase that arose in the late 19th century as a regional or alternative way of referring to the Civil War, emphasizing a conflict between states rather than a rebellion.
