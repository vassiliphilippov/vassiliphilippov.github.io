American cockroach

1. NOUN

/ əˈmɛrɪkən ˈkɑkˌroʊtʃ / (AmE), / əˈmɛrɪkən ˈkɒkˌrəʊtʃ / (BrE)
a large reddish-brown cockroach, originally native to the southern United States but now found worldwide, known for its ability to fly short distances
large reddish-brown species of cockroach

Examples

The American cockroach is often found in warm, humid environments like basements and kitchens.
Scientists discovered an American cockroach colony in the subway system.
The American cockroach can grow up to 4 centimeters in length.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cockroach - a flat, brown or black insect sometimes found in buildings
