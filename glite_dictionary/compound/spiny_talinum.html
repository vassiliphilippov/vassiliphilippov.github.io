spiny talinum

1. NOUN

/ ˈspaɪni təˈlaɪnəm / (AmE), / ˈspaɪni təˈlaɪnəm / (BrE)
A low, cushion-forming plant native to the southwestern United States, with rose to crimson-magenta flowers and leaf midribs that persist as spines after the leaves die.
cushion-forming plant of the US Southwest

Examples

The spiny talinum is often found in arid regions where its persistent spines deter herbivores.
Botanists were excited to document a large patch of spiny talinum during their field survey.
After the leaves fall off, the spines of spiny talinum remain, giving the plant its characteristic appearance.

Etymology

Talinum is the genus name, from Latinized form of a Dahomey (Benin) name; 'spiny' refers to its spine-like leaf midribs.
