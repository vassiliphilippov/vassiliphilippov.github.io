porcine laughter

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɔr.saɪn ˈlæf.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈpɔː.saɪn ˈlɑːf.tə / (BrE)
Laughter that is hearty, loud, and reminiscent of the sounds made by pigs; boisterous, snorting or grunting laughter.
loud, pig-like laughter

Examples

Sarah's porcine laughter echoed through the small café, drawing smiles from everyone nearby.
His porcine laughter made the children giggle even more at his silly jokes.
The comedian's joke was met with a burst of porcine laughter from the audience.

Synset Examples

Her porcine laughter filled the room with joy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boisterous laughter - loud and energetic laughter
  • Synonym: guffaw - a loud and hearty laugh

Etymology

From Latin 'porcinus' (of or like a pig) + English 'laughter'.
