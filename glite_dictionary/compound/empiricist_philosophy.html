empiricist philosophy

1. NOUN

/ ɪmˈpɪrɪsɪst fəˈlɑːsəfi / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɪrɪsɪst fəˈlɒsəfi / (BrE)
The philosophical doctrine that knowledge is primarily derived from sensory experience.
the view that knowledge comes from experience

Examples

Many scientists are influenced by empiricist philosophy in their approach to understanding the world.
The debate between rationalism and empiricist philosophy has shaped modern epistemology.
She wrote her thesis on the impact of empiricist philosophy on eighteenth-century thought.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: empiricism - the theory that all knowledge is derived from sense-experience
  • Antonym: rationalism - the doctrine that knowledge comes from reason rather than experience

Etymology

From 'empiricist' (a person adhering to empiricism) and 'philosophy,' both ultimately from Greek roots.
