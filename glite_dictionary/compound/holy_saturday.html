Holy Saturday

1. NOUN

/ ˌhoʊli ˈsætɚdeɪ / (AmE), / ˌhəʊli ˈsætədeɪ / (BrE)
the Saturday before Easter, observed as the last day of Lent in the Christian calendar
the Saturday before Easter

Examples

Christians around the world observe Holy Saturday as a time of reflection and expectation.
The church remained silent and dimly lit during the Holy Saturday vigil.
Many people prepare for Easter Sunday by attending services on Holy Saturday.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Easter - the festival celebrating the resurrection of Christ
