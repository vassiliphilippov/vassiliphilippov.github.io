genus Suksdorfia

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs sʌksˈdɔːrfiə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs sʌksˈdɔːfɪə / (BrE)
A small genus of rhizomatous herbs native to northwestern America and South America.
genus of herbs native to the Americas

Examples

Botanists discovered a new species within the genus Suksdorfia during their South American expedition.
The genus Suksdorfia is known for its rhizomatous growth and distinctive flowers.
Many wildflowers of the genus Suksdorfia thrive in the moist habitats of northwestern America.

Etymology

Named after Wilhelm Nikolaus Suksdorf, a German-American botanist, combined with the Latin genus.
