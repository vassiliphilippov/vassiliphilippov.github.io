postmature infant

1. NOUN

/ ˌpoʊst.məˈtjʊr ˈɪn.fənt / (AmE), / ˌpəʊst.məˈtjʊə ˈɪn.fənt / (BrE)
an infant born after 42 weeks of gestation, often showing signs of placental insufficiency
baby born after 42 weeks gestation
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that a postmature infant might require special monitoring due to prolonged gestation.
A postmature infant can sometimes have dry, peeling skin as a result of their extended time in the womb.
Research suggests that postmature infants may need different care compared to those born at term.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: premature infant - an infant born before 37 weeks of gestation

Etymology

From 'post-' (after) + 'mature' (fully developed) + 'infant' (child).
