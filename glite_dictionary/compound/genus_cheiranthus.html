genus Cheiranthus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs kaɪˈræn.θəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs kɪəˈran.θəs / (BrE)
A genus of Old World perennial plants known for their showy flowers, typically referred to as wallflowers.
genus of flowering perennial plants

Examples

Many gardeners plant genus Cheiranthus members to add vibrant color to their flower beds.
The genus Cheiranthus is well known for its resilient, fragrant blossoms.
Botanists are interested in the adaptability of species within the genus Cheiranthus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wallflower - species of flowering plant in the genus Cheiranthus

Etymology

From New Latin Cheiranthus, from Greek kheir 'hand' and anthos 'flower'.
