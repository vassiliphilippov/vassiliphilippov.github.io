genus Ananas

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs æˈnæn.əs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs əˈnæn.əs / (BrE)
A genus of tropical American plants with sword-shaped leaves and fleshy compound fruits, most notably the pineapple.
pineapple plant genus

Examples

The genus Ananas includes several species, but the pineapple is the most widely cultivated.
Botanists classified the plant as belonging to the genus Ananas due to its distinctive fruit.
Research on the genus Ananas has led to improved varieties of pineapple for commercial farming.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pineapple - the best-known species in the genus Ananas, a tropical fruit

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the Tupi word 'nanas' meaning 'pineapple'.
