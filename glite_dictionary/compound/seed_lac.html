seed lac

1. NOUN

/ sid læk / (AmE), / siːd læk / (BrE)
granular lac material produced by crushing and washing stick lac, used in the preparation of shellac and other products
granular lac material from processed stick lac

Examples

The factory imports seed lac and processes it into pure shellac for the market.
After collecting the stick lac, workers crush and wash it to produce seed lac.
High-quality varnish requires purified seed lac before it can be mixed with alcohol.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: stick lac - uncleaned lac resin as gathered from trees
  • Degree Higher: shellac - a refined product made from processed lac

Etymology

From 'seed' (owing to the granule shape) and 'lac', from Persian 'lak' (resinous substance produced by insects).
