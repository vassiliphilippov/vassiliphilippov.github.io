water pipe

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔtɚ ˌpaɪp / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə ˌpaɪp / (BrE)
an oriental smoking device consisting of a container of water through which smoke is drawn and cooled before being inhaled, typically using a long flexible tube; also known as a hookah or narghile.
tobacco pipe with water cooling (hookah)

Examples

The café offered traditional water pipes for guests to enjoy flavored tobacco.
He spent the evening with friends, sharing stories and passing around a water pipe.
In many Middle Eastern cultures, smoking a water pipe is a social tradition.

Synset Examples

a bipolar world with the hookah and Turkish coffee versus hamburgers and Coca Cola

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hookah - an oriental smoking pipe with water for cooling smoke
  • Synonym: narghile - variant of water pipe used for smoking

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɔtər paɪp / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə paɪp / (BrE)
a pipe through which water is conveyed, especially as part of a plumbing or irrigation system
pipe used for carrying water

Examples

The plumber replaced the old water pipe to prevent future leaks.
A broken water pipe caused flooding in the basement.
They installed a new water pipe to connect the house to the main supply.

Synset Examples

He discovered a leak in the water pipe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pipe - a tube through which liquids or gases can flow
