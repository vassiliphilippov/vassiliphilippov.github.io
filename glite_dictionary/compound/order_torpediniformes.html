order Torpediniformes

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdər tɔˌpɛdɪnɪˈfɔrmiːz / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə tɔːˌpiːdɪnɪˈfɔːmiːz / (BrE)
a taxonomic order of rays with torpedo-shaped bodies, including electric rays
an order of torpedo-shaped rays (fish)

Examples

Scientists discovered a new species in the order Torpediniformes along the coast of Brazil.
Members of the order Torpediniformes are known for their ability to generate electric shocks.
The order Torpediniformes includes several varieties of electric rays found in tropical seas.

Etymology

From Latin 'torpedo' (electric ray, numbness) and the taxonomic suffix '-iformes' (denoting orders).
