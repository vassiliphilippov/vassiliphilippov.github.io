johnny cake

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɑni ˌkeɪk / (AmE), / ˈdʒɒni ˌkeɪk / (BrE)
A flat cake made from cornmeal, usually cooked pancake-style on a griddle; especially common in New England and parts of the Caribbean.
cornmeal griddle cake, regional food

Examples

In New England, johnny cake is a beloved breakfast staple served with butter and syrup.
The chef demonstrated how to make traditional johnny cake on a cast-iron griddle.
He enjoyed a warm johnny cake with his morning coffee, just like his grandmother used to make.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cornbread - bread made from cornmeal; staple food in American cuisine

Etymology

Probably an alteration of 'journey cake', so called because it could be easily carried on journeys.
