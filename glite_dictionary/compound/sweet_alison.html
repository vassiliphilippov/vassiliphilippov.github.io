sweet alison

1. NOUN

/ swit ˈælɪsən / (AmE), / swiːt ˈælɪsən / (BrE)
a perennial European plant (Lobularia maritima) with clusters of small, fragrant, usually white flowers, often grown in gardens
flowering garden plant with white fragrant blooms

Examples

The garden beds were filled with sweet alison to create a fragrant border along the path.
Every spring, she planted sweet alison to attract bees and butterflies to her flowerbeds.
Clusters of sweet alison added a delicate touch of white to the rock garden.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alyssum - a plant of the same genus with similar small fragrant flowers
