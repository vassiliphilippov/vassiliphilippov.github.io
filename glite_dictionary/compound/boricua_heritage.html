boricua heritage

1. NOUN

/ bɔˈriːkwə ˈhɛrɪtɪdʒ / (AmE), / bɔːˈriːkwə ˈhɛrɪtɪdʒ / (BrE)
the cultural traditions, history, and identity associated with people of Puerto Rican descent
Puerto Rican cultural heritage

Examples

She takes great pride in her boricua heritage and often shares stories of her ancestors.
Many festivals in New York highlight boricua heritage through music and traditional foods.
The museum curated an exhibit dedicated to the art and boricua heritage of the island.

Synset Examples

He played traditional music to celebrate his boricua heritage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Puerto Rican heritage - the cultural heritage of Puerto Ricans

Etymology

‘Boricua’ derives from the indigenous Taíno name for the island of Puerto Rico, ‘Borikén’ or ‘Borinquen’.
