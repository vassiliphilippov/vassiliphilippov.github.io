alpha foetoprotein

1. NOUN

/ ˈælfə ˌfoʊtəˈproʊtin / (AmE), / ˈælfə ˌfəʊtəˈprəʊtiːn / (BrE)
a protein produced by the fetus, present in fetal serum and amniotic fluid, whose abnormal levels may indicate certain developmental abnormalities such as neural tube defects or some cancers
fetal protein tested in prenatal screening
medical

Examples

Doctors measure alpha foetoprotein levels during pregnancy to screen for certain birth defects.
An elevated alpha foetoprotein result may require further diagnostic testing.
Some liver cancers can cause an increase in alpha foetoprotein in adults.

Etymology

From Greek 'alpha' (first letter of Greek alphabet) and 'foetoprotein', combining 'foetus' (Latin for 'offspring') and 'protein'.
