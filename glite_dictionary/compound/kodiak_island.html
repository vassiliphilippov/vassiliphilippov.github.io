Kodiak Island

1. PROPN

/ ˈkoʊdiˌæk ˈaɪlənd / (AmE), / ˈkəʊdiˌæk ˈaɪlənd / (BrE)
an island in the Gulf of Alaska off southern Alaska, known for its historical Russian settlement in 1784
island in the Gulf of Alaska, southern Alaska

Examples

The brown bears native to Kodiak Island are some of the largest in the world.
Russian settlers established a fur trading post on Kodiak Island in the late 18th century.
Environmental scientists continue to study the pristine ecosystems found on Kodiak Island.
