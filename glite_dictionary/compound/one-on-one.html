one-on-one

1. ADJ

/ ˌwʌn ən ˈwʌn / (AmE), / ˌwʌn ɒn ˈwʌn / (BrE)
involving or taking place directly between two individuals, often with no others participating
directly between two individuals

Examples

The company offers one-on-one coaching sessions for its employees.
He prefers one-on-one meetings where he can get to know his client better.
The teacher provided one-on-one instruction to help the student improve.

Synset Examples

one-on-one instruction

Related Terms

  • Synonym: face-to-face - direct meeting or interaction between two people

WordNet ID: oewn-01866405-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADV

/ ˌwʌn ən ˈwʌn / (AmE), / ˌwʌn ɒn ˈwʌn / (BrE)
Between two people directly and without others involved.
directly between two people

Examples

The two managers decided to discuss the issue one-on-one rather than involve the entire team.
She prefers to coach her clients one-on-one for more personalized attention.
They met one-on-one to resolve their differences privately.

Synset Examples

preferred to settle the matter one-on-one
interviewed her person-to-person

Related Terms

  • Synonym: face-to-face - direct, personal contact or communication
