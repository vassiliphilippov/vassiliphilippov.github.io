common horsetail

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmən ˈhɔrˌsteɪl / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən ˈhɔːsteɪl / (BrE)
a widespread species of horsetail plant (Equisetum arvense) found in Eurasia, Greenland, and North America, recognized for its jointed, reed-like stems
a common species of horsetail plant

Examples

The common horsetail is often found near streams and wet meadows.
Gardeners sometimes struggle to remove common horsetail from their flower beds.
Ancient cultures used common horsetail for its medicinal properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horsetail - a plant of the genus Equisetum with hollow, jointed stems
