cocked hat

1. NOUN

/ kɑkt hæt / (AmE), / kɒkt hæt / (BrE)
a hat with sides turned up and fastened to form points, often worn as part of historic or ceremonial dress
hat with brims turned up to form points

Examples

The general wore a distinctive cocked hat as part of his uniform.
A cocked hat was a fashionable accessory in the 18th century.
She admired the antique cocked hat displayed in the museum.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tricorne - three-cornered hat often worn in the 18th century
