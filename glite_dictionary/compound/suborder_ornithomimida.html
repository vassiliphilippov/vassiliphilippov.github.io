suborder Ornithomimida

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌbˌɔrdər ɔrˌnɪθəˈmɪmədə / (AmE), / ˈsʌbˌɔːdə ɔːˌnɪθəˈmɪmɪdə / (BrE)
a group of lightly built, medium-sized theropod dinosaurs resembling ostriches, belonging to the suborder Ornithomimida
a suborder of ostrich-like theropod dinosaurs

Examples

Fossils of the suborder Ornithomimida have been discovered on several continents.
The suborder Ornithomimida was characterized by its long legs and toothless beak.
Paleontologists study members of the suborder Ornithomimida to understand the evolution of bird-like dinosaurs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: theropod - a group of mainly carnivorous dinosaurs

Etymology

From Greek 'ornithos' (bird) and 'mimos' (mimic), referring to bird-mimicking dinosaurs.
