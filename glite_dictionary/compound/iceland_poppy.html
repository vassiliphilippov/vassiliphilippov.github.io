Iceland poppy

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪslənd ˌpɑpi / (AmE), / ˈaɪslənd ˌpɒpi / (BrE)
a hardy perennial poppy, native to Europe and Asia, with brightly colored white, yellow, or orange flowers often grown as an ornamental plant
a perennial poppy with brightly colored flowers

Examples

The Iceland poppy adds a burst of color to spring gardens with its delicate petals.
Many gardeners choose the Iceland poppy for its ability to thrive in cooler climates.
Orange and yellow Iceland poppies waved gently in the mountain breeze.

2. NOUN

/ ˈaɪslənd ˈpɑpi / (AmE), / ˈaɪslənd ˈpɒpi / (BrE)
a hardy perennial poppy (Papaver nudicaule) of northern regions, having fragrant white, yellow, orange, or peach flowers
a perennial poppy with colorful flowers

Examples

The Iceland poppy blooms beautifully in cool climates, adding vibrant colors to spring gardens.
She planted several Iceland poppies along the walkway for a cheerful display.
Botanists admire the delicate petals of the Iceland poppy for their unique shades and fragrance.
