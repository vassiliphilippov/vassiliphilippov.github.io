to rest on someone's shoulder

1. VERB

/ rɛst ɑn ˈsʌmwʌnz ˈʃoʊldəɹ / (AmE), / rɛst ɒn ˈsʌmwʌnz ˈʃəʊldə / (BrE)
to lean against or place one's head or body on another person's shoulder, often for comfort or support
lean or place head/body on another's shoulder

Examples

After a long day, she liked to rest on his shoulder while they watched television.
The child quietly rested on her shoulder and fell asleep during the train ride.
He offered comfort by letting his friend rest on his shoulder as she cried.

Synset Examples

He rested his head on her shoulder during the movie.
