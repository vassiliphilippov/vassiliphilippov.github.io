poodle shooter

1. NOUN

/ ˈpuːdəl ˌʃuːtɚ / (AmE), / ˈpuːdəl ˌʃuːtə / (BrE)
A derogatory term for a small, usually lightweight firearm regarded as ineffective or suitable only for shooting small game.
derogatory term for a small, weak firearm
informal

Examples

Many enthusiasts dismissed the new rifle as just another poodle shooter.
He joked that bringing a poodle shooter to the competition would only embarrass him.
Some hunters carry a poodle shooter for targeting small animals or for beginner practice.

Synset Examples

He called his lightweight gun a poodle shooter.
The poodle shooter was perfect for small game hunting.
He joked about his rifle being a poodle shooter.

Etymology

Modelled after the perceived suitability of the firearm only for hunting small dogs, such as poodles; popularized in US gun culture in the late 20th century.
