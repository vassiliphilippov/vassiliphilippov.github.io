Australian reed grass

1. NOUN

/ əˈstreɪljən ɹid ɡɹæs / (AmE), / ɒˈstreɪliən ɹiːd ɡrɑːs / (BrE)
a tall Australian grass with reedlike appearance, sometimes used for hay
tall Australian reedlike grass

Examples

The wetland was dominated by Australian reed grass, providing shelter for waterbirds.
Farmers in the region sometimes harvest Australian reed grass and use it for hay.
Efforts to restore the riverbanks included planting native species such as Australian reed grass.
