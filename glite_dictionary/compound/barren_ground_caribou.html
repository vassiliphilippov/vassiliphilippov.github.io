barren ground caribou

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛrən ɡraʊnd kəˈriːbuː / (AmE), / ˈbærən ɡraʊnd kəˈriːbuː / (BrE)
a subspecies of caribou (Rangifer tarandus groenlandicus) native to the tundra regions of northern Canada, notable for its long migrations
a caribou subspecies from northern Canada

Examples

The barren ground caribou migrate hundreds of miles across the Arctic tundra each year.
Scientists track the barren ground caribou to study the impact of climate change on their habitat.
Indigenous communities have long relied on the barren ground caribou for food and cultural practices.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caribou - a large North American reindeer
