genus Clupea

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs kluˈpiː.ə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs kluːˈpiː.ə / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Clupeidae, comprising the typical herrings.
type genus of herrings

Examples

Species of the genus Clupea are widely used in commercial fisheries.
The Atlantic herring belongs to the genus Clupea within the family Clupeidae.
Morphological studies of the genus Clupea have provided important insights into fish evolution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: herring - a small, silvery fish of the family Clupeidae
  • Synonym: Clupeidae - the biological family comprising herrings, shads, sardines, and menhadens

Etymology

From Latin 'Clupea', a name for herring, used in taxonomy.
