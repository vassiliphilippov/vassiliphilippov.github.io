tree toad

1. NOUN

/ ˈtriˌtoʊd / (AmE), / ˈtriː təʊd / (BrE)
A small arboreal (tree-dwelling) toad, typically with adhesive pads on its toes, found in Southeast Asia, Australia, and the Americas.
tree-dwelling amphibian with adhesive toes

Examples

A tree toad clung to the windowpane throughout the summer rainstorm.
Biologists discovered a new species of tree toad in the rainforest canopy.
Children watched in fascination as a green tree toad leapt from branch to branch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tree frog - an arboreal frog, especially those with adhesive toe pads
