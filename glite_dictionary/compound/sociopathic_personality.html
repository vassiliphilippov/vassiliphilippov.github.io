sociopathic personality

1. NOUN

/ ˌsoʊsiəˈpæθɪk pərˌsɑnəˈlɪti / (AmE), / ˌsəʊsiəˈpæθɪk pəˌsɒnəˈlɪti / (BrE)
A personality disorder characterized by a lack of moral responsibility or social conscience and emotional detachment, often associated with impulsive or violent behavior without guilt.
personality disorder marked by lack of conscience or remorse
medical

Examples

The therapist diagnosed him with a sociopathic personality after a series of psychological evaluations.
A person with a sociopathic personality often disregards the feelings and rights of others without remorse.
Many criminal cases have involved individuals later identified as having a sociopathic personality.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antisocial personality disorder - a personality disorder marked by pervasive disregard for social norms and rights of others
  • Synonym: psychopathic personality - a personality disorder with similar traits; historical equivalent term

Etymology

From 'sociopathic' (from Latin 'socius', companion, and Greek 'pathos', suffering) + 'personality'.
