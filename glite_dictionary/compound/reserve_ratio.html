reserve ratio

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈzɜrv ˈreɪʃioʊ / (AmE), / rɪˈzɜːv ˈreɪʃɪəʊ / (BrE)
the percentage of a bank's total deposits that must be held in reserve and not lent out, in order to ensure the bank can meet withdrawal demands
required percentage of bank deposits held in reserve

Examples

The central bank decided to increase the reserve ratio to control inflation.
A lower reserve ratio allows banks to lend out more money, stimulating the economy.
Changes in the reserve ratio can significantly influence a country's banking system.

Synset Examples

The required reserve ratio is sometimes used as a tool in monetary policy, influencing the country's borrowing and interest rates by changing the amount of funds available for banks to make loans with.
