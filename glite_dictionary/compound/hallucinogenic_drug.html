hallucinogenic drug

1. NOUN

/ həˌluːsɪnəˈdʒɛnɪk drʌɡ / (AmE), / həˌluːsɪnəˈdʒɛnɪk drʌɡ / (BrE)
a psychoactive substance that causes hallucinations or altered sensory perceptions
drug causing hallucinations

Examples

The use of a hallucinogenic drug can cause vivid sensory distortions and changes in perception.
Some indigenous cultures have used hallucinogenic drugs for spiritual and ceremonial purposes.
The scientist studied the effects of a new hallucinogenic drug on the brain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: psychedelic - drug producing mind-altering effects and hallucinations
  • Antonym: depressant - drug reducing nervous activity or bodily functions
  • Antonym: stimulant - drug increasing physiological activity and alertness

Etymology

From 'hallucinogenic' (from Latin *alucinari*, to wander in mind, and Greek *-genic*, producing), and 'drug' (from Old French *drogue*).
