Newgate Prison

1. PROPN

/ ˈnuːˌɡeɪt ˈprɪzən / (AmE), / ˈnjuːˌɡeɪt ˈprɪzən / (BrE)
a former prison in London, notorious for its harsh conditions and historical significance, which was closed and demolished in 1902
historic prison in London, England
british

Examples

Many famous criminals were incarcerated at Newgate Prison during the eighteenth and nineteenth centuries.
In Dickens’s novels, Newgate Prison often appears as a symbol of despair and injustice.
The notorious Newgate Prison riots of 1780 resulted in the building being severely damaged by fire.

Etymology

Named after Newgate, a gate in the old London Wall near which the prison was built.
