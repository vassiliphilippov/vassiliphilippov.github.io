zinc sulfate

1. NOUN

/ zɪŋk ˈsʌlˌfeɪt / (AmE), / zɪŋk ˈsʌl.feɪt / (BrE)
a colorless, water-soluble powder used in dyeing, wood preservation, and for electrodepositing zinc
a chemical compound used in industry and dyeing

Examples

Industrial processes often use zinc sulfate as a mordant in textile dyeing.
Exposure to zinc sulfate can occur when treating wood to prevent decay.
The laboratory ordered several kilograms of zinc sulfate for electroplating experiments.

Etymology

From 'zinc' (the metallic element) and 'sulfate', from Latin 'sulfas' (a salt of sulfuric acid).
