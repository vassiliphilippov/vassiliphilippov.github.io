Airedale terrier

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛrˌdeɪl ˈtɛriər / (AmE), / ˈɛədeɪl ˈtɛriə / (BrE)
a large terrier breed with a wiry coat, originally bred in Yorkshire, England
large wiry-coated terrier breed

Examples

The Airedale terrier is often called the 'King of Terriers' because of its size.
Their family adopted an Airedale terrier from a local shelter last spring.
I saw an excited Airedale terrier chasing after a ball in the park.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: terrier - a type of dog originally bred for hunting and digging out vermin

Etymology

Named after the Airedale valley in Yorkshire, England, where the breed originated.
