the Great Hunger

1. NOUN

/ ðə ɡreɪt ˈhʌŋɚ / (AmE), / ðə ɡreɪt ˈhʌŋɡə / (BrE)
the famine that occurred in Ireland between 1846 and 1851, caused by a potato blight, leading to widespread starvation and mass emigration
Irish famine of 1846–1851

Examples

Books and documentaries often explore the causes and consequences of the Great Hunger.
Many families emigrated to America during the Great Hunger in search of a better life.
The devastating impact of the Great Hunger is still felt in Irish society today.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: the Irish Potato Famine - the major potato blight famine in Ireland in the mid-19th century

Etymology

Refers to the severe Irish famine known in Irish as 'An Gorta Mór' (The Great Hunger), from Irish Gaelic.
