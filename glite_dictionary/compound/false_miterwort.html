false miterwort

1. NOUN

/ fɔls ˈmaɪtɚwɝt / (AmE), / fɔːls ˈmaɪtəwɜːt / (BrE)
a plant with tiny white flowers hanging in loose clusters on leafy stems, found in moist woods from Alaska to central California and east to Montana
a woodland plant with small white clustered flowers

Examples

We spotted several false miterwort plants growing along the damp forest path.
The delicate flowers of the false miterwort are easily overlooked by casual hikers.
In early spring, false miterwort blooms quietly among the moss and fallen leaves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: miterwort - a related plant in the same genus, often with similar flowers

2. NOUN

/ fɔls ˈmaɪtɚˌwɝt / (AmE), / fɔːls ˈmaɪtəˌwɜːt / (BrE)
A woodland plant with stolons and white flowers that blooms in spring, resembling the true miterwort.
spring-blooming woodland plant with white flowers

Examples

We found a patch of false miterwort blooming along the shaded woodland trail.
The delicate white flowers of the false miterwort catch the morning light each spring.
Unlike true miterwort, false miterwort spreads by stolons through the forest understory.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: miterwort - a woodland plant with similar appearance, also called bishop's cap

Etymology

From the resemblance to miterwort, with 'false' indicating it is not the true species.
