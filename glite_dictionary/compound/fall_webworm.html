fall webworm

1. NOUN

/ fɔl ˈwɛbˌwɝm / (AmE), / fɔːl ˈwɛbˌwɜːm / (BrE)
a moth caterpillar (Hyphantria cunea) whose larvae spin large webs on deciduous trees in late summer and fall, often causing defoliation
caterpillar pest that forms webs on trees

Examples

The fall webworm is often seen covering tree branches with its distinctive webs in late summer.
Many homeowners struggle to control infestations of the fall webworm in their gardens each year.
If left untreated, a fall webworm infestation can cause significant damage to ornamental trees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: webworm - larvae of several moth species that spin webs on trees or plants
