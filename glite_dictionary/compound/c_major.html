C major

1. NOUN

/ si ˈmeɪdʒɚ / (AmE), / si ˈmeɪdʒə / (BrE)
The major scale whose key signature has no sharps or flats, with C as its tonic.
the major scale with no sharps or flats

Examples

Many beginner pianists start learning with C major because it uses only the white keys.
The symphony is composed in C major, giving it a bright and cheerful sound.
He played a simple melody in C major during his music lesson.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: C minor - the minor scale with C as its tonic
  • Degree Higher: G major - the major scale with one sharp, G as tonic
  • Degree Higher: F major - the major scale with one flat, F as tonic
