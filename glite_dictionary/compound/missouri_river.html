Missouri River

1. PROPN

/ mɪˈzʊri ˈrɪvər / (AmE), / mɪˈzʊəri ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
the longest river in the United States, flowing southeast from Montana to join the Mississippi River near St. Louis
the longest river in the United States

Examples

The Missouri River flows over 2,300 miles before joining the Mississippi near St. Louis.
Lewis and Clark traveled up the Missouri River during their famous expedition.
Major cities like Kansas City are located on the banks of the Missouri River.

Synset Examples

The Missouri and Mississippi Rivers together form the third longest river in the world

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mississippi River - major North American river and tributary system, into which the Missouri River flows

Etymology

Named after the Missouri people, from an Algonquian language meaning 'people with wooden canoes'.
