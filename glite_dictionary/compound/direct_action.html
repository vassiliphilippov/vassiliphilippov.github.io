direct action

1. NOUN

/ dəˈrɛkt ˈækʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈrɛkt ˈakʃən / (BrE)
a protest taken by individuals or groups, especially labor or minority groups, to achieve their aims by immediate and direct means rather than through legal or political processes
immediate protest to achieve demands

Examples

The workers decided to take direct action by staging a sit-in at the factory.
Environmental activists used direct action to block the construction of the oil pipeline.
Many social movements have relied on direct action to draw attention to their causes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: protest - an organized public demonstration expressing strong objection
  • Synonym: civil disobedience - refusal to obey certain laws as a form of peaceful political protest
