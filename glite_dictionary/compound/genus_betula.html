genus Betula

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs bəˈtuː.lə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs bɛˈtjuː.lə / (BrE)
A genus of deciduous trees and shrubs in the family Betulaceae, commonly known as birches.
genus of birch trees/shrubs

Examples

The genus Betula includes many species commonly known as birches.
Some members of the genus Betula are valued for their ornamental bark and delicate leaves.
The paper birch is a well-known species within the genus Betula.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: birch - a tree of the genus Betula, with thin bark and slender branches

Etymology

From Latin *Betula* (birch tree), used in scientific classification.
