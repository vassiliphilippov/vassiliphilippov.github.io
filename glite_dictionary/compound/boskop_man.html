Boskop man

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑːs.kɑːp mæn / (AmE), / ˈbɒs.kɒp man / (BrE)
a possible early Homo sapiens, represented by a cranium found in the Transvaal, formerly considered a separate species
possible early Homo sapiens fossil

Examples

The discovery of the Boskop man skull led to early theories about human evolution in Africa.
Some scientists now believe that Boskop man belonged to early Homo sapiens, not a separate species.
The Boskop man fossils were initially thought to show a unique human lineage.

Etymology

Named after Boskop, a location in South Africa where the skull was found.
