alpha methyl dopa

1. NOUN

/ ˌælfə ˌmiː.θəl ˈdoʊ.pə / (AmE), / ˌælfə ˌmiː.θəl ˈdəʊ.pə / (BrE)
an antihypertensive drug used in the treatment of high blood pressure, often sold under the trade name Aldomet
antihypertensive drug
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed alpha methyl dopa to help control her high blood pressure.
Patients taking alpha methyl dopa should have their liver function monitored regularly.
Alpha methyl dopa is especially useful in managing hypertension during pregnancy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: methyldopa - antihypertensive drug also known as alpha methyl dopa

Etymology

From 'alpha', indicating a position on the carbon chain, and 'methyl dopa', combining 'methyl' (a chemical group) and 'dopa' (dihydroxyphenylalanine).
