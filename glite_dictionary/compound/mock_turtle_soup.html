mock turtle soup

1. NOUN

/ mɑk ˈtɝt̬l̩ suːp / (AmE), / mɒk ˈtɜːtl̩ suːp / (BrE)
a soup made from a calf's head or other meat as a substitute for green turtle soup
soup imitating green turtle soup

Examples

Many Victorian recipes for mock turtle soup require a calf's head as the main ingredient.
He served mock turtle soup at the dinner party to evoke traditional British cuisine.
After learning about green turtle soup, I decided to try making mock turtle soup at home.

Etymology

First recorded in the 18th century, named as an imitation ('mock') of green turtle soup, which was a delicacy in England.
