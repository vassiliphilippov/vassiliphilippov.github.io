Chinese whisper

1. NOUN

/ ˌtʃaɪ.niz ˈwɪs.pɚ / (AmE), / ˌtʃaɪ.niːz ˈwɪs.pə / (BrE)
a game in which a message is passed along a line of people by whispering, with each person only hearing it once, often resulting in the message becoming increasingly distorted
a game where a message is distorted by repetition
british

Examples

We played Chinese whisper at the party and ended up with a completely different message.
Rumours about the manager’s decision spread through the office like Chinese whisper.
What began as a simple instruction became a confusing message after a round of Chinese whisper.

Synset Examples

What started as a joke turned into Chinese whispers.
The story changed entirely through Chinese whispers.
The news spread like Chinese whispers in the office.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: telephone game - a game in which a message becomes distorted as it is passed on

Etymology

Refers to the supposed difficulty of accurately transmitting messages through a chain; first recorded in late 19th century Britain.
