vindictive protectiveness

1. NOUN

/ vɪnˈdɪktɪv prəˈtɛktɪvnəs / (AmE), / vɪnˈdɪktɪv prəˈtɛktɪvnəs / (BrE)
a form of protective behavior characterized by spitefulness or hostility, especially in defending family members or close associates
spiteful or hostile protective behavior

Examples

Her vindictive protectiveness made it difficult for anyone to criticize her brother.
The manager's vindictive protectiveness towards his team often escalated minor issues into heated conflicts.
Instead of calming the situation, his vindictive protectiveness only increased the tension among friends.

Synset Examples

She often showed vindictive protectiveness towards her siblings.
Her vindictive protectiveness led to constant arguments.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: protectiveness - the quality of wanting to protect someone
  • Degree Higher: vindictiveness - having a strong or unreasoning desire for revenge
