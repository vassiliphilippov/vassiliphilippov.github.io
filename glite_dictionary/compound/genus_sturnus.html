genus Sturnus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˈstɜrnəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈstɜːnəs / (BrE)
The type genus of the Sturnidae family, comprising the common starlings and related birds.
type genus of the starling family (Sturnidae)

Examples

The genus Sturnus includes the common starling found throughout Europe and Asia.
Birds classified under the genus Sturnus are known for their adaptability to urban environments.
Research on the genus Sturnus has provided insights into flocking behavior in birds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: starling - a small to medium-sized passerine bird of the family Sturnidae

Etymology

From Latin 'Sturnus', meaning 'starling'.
