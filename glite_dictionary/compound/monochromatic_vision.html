monochromatic vision

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɑːnəkroʊˈmætɪk ˈvɪʒən / (AmE), / ˌmɒnəkrəˈmætɪk ˈvɪʒən / (BrE)
A condition of complete color blindness in which colors can only be distinguished by their brightness.
complete color blindness
medical

Examples

People with monochromatic vision perceive the world only in shades of gray.
Doctors diagnosed him with monochromatic vision after a series of visual tests.
Monochromatic vision makes it difficult for affected individuals to distinguish ripe fruit from unripe ones.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: achromatopsia - complete inability to perceive color; total color blindness

Etymology

From Greek 'monos' (single) + 'chrōma' (color) + 'vision'.
