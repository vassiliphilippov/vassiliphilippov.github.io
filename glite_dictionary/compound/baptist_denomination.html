Baptist denomination

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæp.tɪst ˌdɛn.əˈmeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˈbæp.tɪst ˌdɛn.əˈmeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
a distinct group or branch within the Baptist Christian tradition, typically with its own organizational structure and beliefs
branch of the Baptist Christian tradition

Examples

The Southern Baptist Convention is the largest Baptist denomination in the United States.
Each Baptist denomination may have its own distinctive practices and beliefs about baptism.
She grew up attending a church that belonged to a small Baptist denomination.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: denomination - a recognized autonomous branch of the Christian Church
