genus Moneses

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs moʊˈniːsiz / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs məʊˈniːsiːz / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the family Ericaceae, containing a single species commonly known as one-flowered wintergreen, sometimes included in the genus Pyrola.
a plant genus in the Ericaceae family

Examples

The genus Moneses contains only a single species native to temperate regions.
Some botanists classify the one-flowered wintergreen under the genus Moneses rather than Pyrola.
Research into the genus Moneses has informed our understanding of plant evolution in the Ericaceae family.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Pyrola - a genus of evergreen herbs closely related to Moneses

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'genus' and 'Moneses' (from Greek 'monēsēs', meaning 'solitary').
