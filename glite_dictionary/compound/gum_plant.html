gum plant

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡʌm ˌplænt / (AmE), / ˈɡʌm ˌplɑːnt / (BrE)
any of several western American plants of the genus Grindelia, with sticky, resinous leaves and stems, sometimes used medicinally and often poisonous to livestock
western American Grindelia plant; resinous and sometimes toxic

Examples

The dried leaves of the gum plant were historically used in medicinal remedies.
Livestock are kept away from fields of gum plant because of its toxic effects.
I spotted several gum plants growing near the river in California.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Grindelia - the botanical genus that includes gum plants
