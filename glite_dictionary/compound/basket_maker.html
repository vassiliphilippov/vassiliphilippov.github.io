Basket Maker

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæskɪt ˌmeɪkər / (AmE), / ˈbɑːskɪt ˌmeɪkə / (BrE)
a member of a prehistoric group of Native Americans related to the Pueblo peoples, known for their skill in making baskets
prehistoric Native American basket-makers

Examples

The Basket Maker culture is considered the forerunner of the ancient Pueblo peoples.
Archaeologists discovered beautifully woven artifacts made by the Basket Makers in the caves of the Southwest.
Many Basket Maker sites reveal intricate techniques in early basketry and weaving.

Etymology

Refers to the basket-making traditions of these prehistoric peoples identified by archaeologists.
