bird pepper

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɝːd ˌpɛpər / (AmE), / ˈbɜːd ˌpɛpə / (BrE)
A small plant native to tropical America, bearing very small, extremely hot red fruits; believed to be an ancestor of sweet and many hot peppers.
very hot, small red pepper plant

Examples

The bird pepper is prized by chefs for its intense heat and distinct flavor.
Wild bird peppers can still be found growing in parts of Central America.
Scientists speculate that the bird pepper is ancestral to many cultivated hot peppers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chili pepper - any of various peppers with spicy fruits
