genus Equus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˈiːkwəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈiːkwəs / (BrE)
A genus of mammals in the family Equidae, including horses, zebras, and donkeys, and the only surviving genus of the family.
biological genus of horses, zebras, and donkeys

Examples

The genus Equus includes modern horses, zebras, and donkeys.
Scientists believe that early species of the genus Equus evolved in North America.
Members of the genus Equus are known for their single-toed hooves.

Etymology

From Latin 'equus' meaning 'horse'.
