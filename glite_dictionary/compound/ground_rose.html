ground rose

1. NOUN

/ ɡraʊnd roʊz / (AmE), / ɡraʊnd rəʊz / (BrE)
A low-growing bristly shrub of southern Oregon and California, with creeping rootstocks and usually bearing corymbose clusters of flowers.
low bristly shrub with creeping roots, native to western US

Examples

The ground rose spreads quickly across sandy soils with its creeping roots.
We spotted several ground roses blooming along the California trail.
Gardeners value the ground rose for its resilience in dry climates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rose - a prickly bush or shrub with fragrant flowers, usually of the genus Rosa
