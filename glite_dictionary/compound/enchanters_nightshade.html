enchanter's nightshade

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtʃæntərz ˈnaɪtʃeɪdʒ / (AmE), / ɪnˈtʃɑːntəz ˈnaɪtʃeɪdʒ / (BrE)
Any of several perennial herbs of the genus Circaea, found in the Northern Hemisphere, with small white flowers that typically bloom at dawn.
shade-loving perennial herb, genus Circaea

Examples

The woodland floor was dotted with the delicate white flowers of enchanter's nightshade.
Many gardeners appreciate enchanter's nightshade for its subtle beauty in shaded areas.
While hiking in Europe, we encountered patches of enchanter's nightshade beneath the forest canopy.

Etymology

The common name refers to the genus Circaea, named after the enchantress Circe in Greek mythology.
