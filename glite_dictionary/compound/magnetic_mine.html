magnetic mine

1. NOUN

/ mægˈnɛtɪk maɪn / (AmE), / mæɡˈnɛtɪk maɪn / (BrE)
a type of underwater explosive device that detonates when it detects the magnetic field of a nearby vessel, such as the steel hull of a ship
underwater mine detonated by magnetic field

Examples

The navy deployed several magnetic mines in the harbor to deter enemy ships.
During the war, many vessels were damaged by undetected magnetic mines.
Modern minesweepers are equipped to locate and disarm magnetic mines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mine - an explosive device placed in the ground or water to destroy vehicles or ships
