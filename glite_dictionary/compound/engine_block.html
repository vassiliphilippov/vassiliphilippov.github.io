engine block

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛn.dʒən blɑk / (AmE), / ˈɛn.dʒɪn blɒk / (BrE)
the main metal body of an engine, containing the cylinders and cooling passages
main metal part of an engine

Examples

The mechanic discovered a crack in the engine block during the inspection.
Replacing an engine block can be costly, so regular maintenance is important.
The engine block is usually made of cast iron or aluminum alloy.

Synset Examples

the engine had to be replaced because the block was cracked

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cylinder block - the casting that holds the cylinders in an internal combustion engine
