subfamily Caesalpinioideae

1. NOUN

/ sʌbˌfæmɪli siːˌsælpɪniˈoʊɪdiː / (AmE), / sʌbˌfæmɪli siːˌsælpɪnɪˈəʊɪdiː / (BrE)
A subfamily of flowering plants within the legume family, sometimes considered as the family Caesalpiniaceae in some classification systems.
subfamily of legumes (botany)

Examples

Many ornamental trees belong to the subfamily Caesalpinioideae within the legume family.
Recent taxonomic revisions have placed certain species under the subfamily Caesalpinioideae rather than as a separate family.
The subfamily Caesalpinioideae includes several genera important to tropical agriculture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Caesalpiniaceae - the former family name now often considered a subfamily, Caesalpinioideae

Etymology

From Latin 'Caesalpinia' (a genus in the group) and Greek '-oideae', a botanical subfamily suffix.
