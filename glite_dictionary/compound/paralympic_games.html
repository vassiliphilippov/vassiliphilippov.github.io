Paralympic Games

1. NOUN

/ ˌpær.əˈlɪm.pɪk ˈɡeɪmz / (AmE), / ˌpar.əˈlɪm.pɪk ˈɡeɪmz / (BrE)
an international multi-sport event held for athletes with disabilities, typically occurring shortly after the Olympic Games in the same host city
international sporting event for athletes with disabilities

Examples

The Paralympic Games showcase the abilities of athletes from around the world with various disabilities.
After winning a gold medal at the Paralympic Games, she became an inspiration to many.
Tokyo hosted both the Olympic and Paralympic Games in 2021, drawing global attention to adaptive sports.

Synset Examples

One of them is the channels that NBC as the broadcasting rights owner for the United States will use to air the Paralympic Games on.
McKenzie, a silver and bronze Paralympic medalist in wheelchair basketball, retired from the game after the 2012 Summer Paralympics in London
Wikinews interviews academic Simon Ličen about attitudes towards US Paralympics

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Olympic Games - international multi-sport event for able-bodied athletes

Etymology

From 'Paralympic,' combining 'para-' (beside, parallel to) and 'Olympic', indicating the Games run parallel to the Olympics.
