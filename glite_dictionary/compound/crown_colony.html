Crown Colony

1. NOUN

/ kraʊn ˈkɑl.ə.ni / (AmE), / kraʊn ˈkɒl.ə.ni / (BrE)
A British colony governed directly by the British Crown, typically administered by a governor appointed by the monarch.
British colony governed by the Crown
british

Examples

Hong Kong was a Crown Colony until it was handed back to China in 1997.
The governor of the Crown Colony exercised powers on behalf of the British monarch.
Many laws in former Crown Colonies still reflect British legal traditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: colony - a country or area under the full or partial political control of another country

Etymology

From 'crown' referring to the British monarchy and 'colony' from Latin 'colonia' (settlement).
