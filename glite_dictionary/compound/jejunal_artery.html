jejunal artery

1. NOUN

/ dʒɪˈdʒunəl ˈɑːrtəri / (AmE), / dʒɪˈdʒuːnəl ˈɑːtəri / (BrE)
an artery branching from the superior mesenteric artery that supplies blood to the jejunum
branch artery supplying the jejunum
medical

Examples

The surgeon carefully identified the jejunal artery before proceeding with the operation.
Damage to the jejunal artery can lead to insufficient blood supply to the small intestine.
Medical imaging revealed a blockage in the jejunal artery of the patient.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: superior mesenteric artery - the main artery that supplies the midgut including the jejunum

Etymology

From Latin 'jejunum' (fasting, the middle section of the small intestine) and 'artery' from Greek 'arteria'.
