yolk sac

1. NOUN

/ joʊk sæk / (AmE), / jəʊk sæk / (BrE)
a membranous structure that encloses the yolk in the eggs of birds, reptiles, marsupials, and some fishes, serving to circulate nutrients to the developing embryo
membranous nutrient structure in embryos

Examples

In chicks, the yolk sac provides essential nutrients during the early stages of development.
The yolk sac is often visible during an ultrasound of early pregnancy in mammals.
Researchers studied how the yolk sac functions in the embryos of various fish species.

2. NOUN

/ joʊk sæk / (AmE), / jəʊk sæk / (BrE)
a membranous structure in vertebrate embryos that serves as a primary site for blood formation and nutrient circulation before the heart develops
membranous embryonic structure for early circulation
medical

Examples

The yolk sac provides early nourishment to the developing embryo.
Cells in the yolk sac are responsible for forming the embryo’s first blood vessels.
Doctors observed abnormalities in the yolk sac during the ultrasound examination.

Etymology

From 'yolk' (the yellow internal part of an egg) and 'sac' (a pouch-like structure).
