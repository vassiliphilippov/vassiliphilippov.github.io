kaiser roll

1. NOUN

/ ˈkaɪzər roʊl / (AmE), / ˈkaɪzə rəʊl / (BrE)
a round, crusty bread roll with a crisp, often poppy-seeded top, traditionally made by folding the corners of a square piece of dough into the center
round crusty bread roll, often with poppy seeds

Examples

I ordered a sandwich on a kaiser roll with turkey and Swiss cheese.
The bakery makes fresh kaiser rolls every morning for breakfast crowds.
He prefers his burger on a toasted kaiser roll rather than a plain bun.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bread roll - small, individually baked piece of bread

Etymology

From German Kaiser ('emperor') + Roll ('roll'), named after Emperor Franz Joseph I of Austria.
