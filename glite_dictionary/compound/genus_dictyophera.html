genus Dictyophera

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɪk.ti.əˌfɪr.ə / (AmE), / ˈdɪk.ti.əˌfɪə.rə / (BrE)
A genus of fungi closely related to the genus Phallus, distinguished by an indusium hanging from below the cap like a skirt.
a genus of fungi with a skirt-like veil

Examples

Members of the genus Dictyophera are known for their remarkable lacy veils.
The genus Dictyophera includes species often called 'veiled stinkhorns.'
Researchers discovered a new species within the genus Dictyophera in Southeast Asia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: genus Phallus - a related genus of fungi known for its phallic shape

Etymology

From Greek diktyon (net) + pherein (to bear), referring to the net-like skirt (indusium) of the fungi.
