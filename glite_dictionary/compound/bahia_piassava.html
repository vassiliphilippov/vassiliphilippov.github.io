Bahia piassava

1. NOUN

/ bəˈhiə pi.əˈsɑːvə / (AmE), / bəˈhiːə pi.əˈsɑːvə / (BrE)
A Brazilian palm tree, Attalea funifera, whose stiff fibers are used in making ropes, mats, and brushes.
a Brazilian palm yielding useful fibers

Examples

The stiff fibers of the Bahia piassava are ideal for making durable brooms and brushes.
Traditional craftsmen in Brazil still harvest Bahia piassava by hand for local industries.
Exports of Bahia piassava have become a significant part of the regional economy in eastern Brazil.

Etymology

From Portuguese 'Bahia', a state in Brazil, and 'piassava', from Tupi 'piaçaba' (plant fiber).
