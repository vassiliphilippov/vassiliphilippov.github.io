plant fibre

1. NOUN

/ plænt ˈfaɪbɚ / (AmE), / plɑːnt ˈfaɪbə / (BrE)
a natural fiber obtained from plants and used in textiles, ropes, or as dietary fiber
fiber derived from plants

Examples

Cotton is a type of plant fibre that is widely used to make clothing.
Many environmentally friendly products are now made from plant fibre instead of plastic.
She added more plant fibre to her diet to improve digestion.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: animal fibre - fiber obtained from animals, such as wool or silk
  • Antonym: synthetic fibre - fiber made from synthetic materials rather than natural sources
  • Synonym: fiber - thread or filament from which a material is formed
