pair of virginals

1. NOUN

/ pɛr əv ˈvɝːdʒənlz / (AmE), / peə əv ˈvɜːdʒɪnəlz / (BrE)
A legless, rectangular keyboard instrument similar to a harpsichord, played mainly in the 16th and 17th centuries, commonly by women.
historical keyboard instrument like a small harpsichord

Examples

A beautiful pair of virginals stood in the corner of the drawing room, admired by the guests.
During the Renaissance, a pair of virginals was a popular instrument among upper-class women.
The museum's collection includes a rare pair of virginals from early 17th-century England.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: virginals - a small, legless harpsichord from the Renaissance and Baroque periods
  • Degree Higher: harpsichord - a keyboard instrument where strings are plucked rather than struck

Etymology

From Old English 'pair' + 'virginals', possibly from Latin 'virginalis' (virginal, maidenly), reflecting its association with young women.
