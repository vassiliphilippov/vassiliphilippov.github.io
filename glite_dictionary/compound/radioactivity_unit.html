radioactivity unit

1. NOUN

/ ˌreɪdiˌoʊˈæktɪvɪti ˈjuːnɪt / (AmE), / ˌreɪdiəʊˈæktɪvɪti ˈjuːnɪt / (BrE)
a unit used to measure the intensity of radioactivity
unit for measuring radioactivity

Examples

The radioactivity unit used in most countries is the becquerel.
Safety standards specify maximum levels in each radioactivity unit.
The scientist explained how a radioactivity unit quantifies decay activity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: becquerel - the SI unit of radioactivity (one disintegration per second)
  • Synonym: curie - a non-SI unit of radioactivity (equal to 3.7 × 10¹⁰ disintegrations per second)
