alimentary tract smear

1. NOUN

/ ˌæl.əˈmɛn.tə.ri trækt smɪr / (AmE), / ˌæl.ɪˈmɛn.tər.i trækt smɪə / (BrE)
a cytologic smear obtained from a part of the alimentary tract using specialized techniques, mainly to diagnose diseases such as cancer
smear test of the digestive tract lining
medical

Examples

A biopsy and an alimentary tract smear were both performed to aid in the diagnosis.
The doctor recommended an alimentary tract smear to check for abnormal cells in the esophagus.
Detection of early cancer is possible through regular alimentary tract smear screenings.
