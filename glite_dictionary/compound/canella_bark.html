canella bark

1. NOUN

/ kəˈnɛlə bɑːrk / (AmE), / kəˈnɛlə bɑːk / (BrE)
The aromatic inner bark of the Canella winterana tree, used as a condiment and tonic.
aromatic bark used as spice or tonic

Examples

The chef added a stick of canella bark to infuse the dessert with a spicy aroma.
In traditional remedies, canella bark was valued for its tonic properties.
Dried canella bark is sometimes mistaken for cinnamon in the spice trade.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cinnamon - aromatic spice made from the inner bark of certain trees

Etymology

From the genus name Canella, derived from Latin 'canna' (reed, cane).
