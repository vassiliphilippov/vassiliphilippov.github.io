Oriental arborvitae

1. NOUN

/ ɔːrˈiː.ən.tl ˌɑːr.bərˈviː.taɪ / (AmE), / ɔːˈrɪ.ənt(ə)l ˌɑː.bəˈviː.taɪ / (BrE)
an evergreen shrub or small tree native to Asia, widely cultivated for ornamental use in the United States and Europe
evergreen Asian shrub or small tree

Examples

The Oriental arborvitae is often used as a hedge due to its dense foliage and attractive appearance.
Many gardeners in Europe prefer the Oriental arborvitae for its hardiness and unique, upright form.
We planted an Oriental arborvitae near the entrance to add year-round greenery to the garden.

Etymology

From Latin 'arbor vitae' meaning 'tree of life', combined with 'Oriental' referring to its Asian origin.
