gamma aminobutyric acid

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡæmə əˌminoʊbjuːˈtɪrɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / ˌɡæmə əˌmiːnəʊbjuːˈtɪrɪk ˈæsɪd / (BrE)
an amino acid found in the central nervous system that acts as an inhibitory neurotransmitter, reducing nerve activity
inhibitory neurotransmitter in the brain
medical

Examples

Gamma aminobutyric acid levels in the brain are linked to anxiety and sleep disorders.
Doctors studied the effects of increased gamma aminobutyric acid on neural activity.
The drug works by enhancing gamma aminobutyric acid transmission, calming overactive neurons.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: GABA - abbreviation for gamma aminobutyric acid, the same compound

Etymology

Named for the position of the amino group (gamma carbon) and from 'aminobutyric acid', reflecting its structure.
