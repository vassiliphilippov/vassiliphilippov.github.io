to barely hear

1. VERB

/ ˈbɛrli hɪr / (AmE), / ˈbeəli hɪə / (BrE)
to hear something with great difficulty, almost not hearing it at all
hear with great difficulty

Examples

It was raining so hard I could barely hear the announcer.
She spoke so softly I could barely hear her across the room.
He turned the volume up because he could barely hear the dialogue in the movie.

Synset Examples

He could barely hear her whisper over the crowd.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: almost hear - to hear something nearly but not quite
