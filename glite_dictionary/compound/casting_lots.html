casting lots

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæs.tɪŋ lɑts / (AmE), / ˈkɑːst.ɪŋ lɒts / (BrE)
the act of making a decision or choice by drawing or throwing lots (such as straws, stones, or similar objects) to produce a random outcome
deciding by drawing lots

Examples

In ancient times, important decisions were often made by casting lots to ensure fairness.
The group could not agree, so they settled the matter by casting lots.
According to tradition, the soldiers decided who would receive the garment by casting lots.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drawing lots - the act of choosing by taking lots at random

Etymology

From the verb 'cast' (to throw) and 'lot' (an object drawn randomly); widely attested in Biblical and ancient sources.
