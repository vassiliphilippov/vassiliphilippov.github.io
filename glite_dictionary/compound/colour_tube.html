colour tube

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌlər tuːb / (AmE), / ˈkʌlə tjuːb / (BrE)
a television picture tube that displays images in full color using red, green, and blue phosphors
television tube for full-color images

Examples

The old television set still works, but the colour tube takes a while to warm up.
Technicians replaced the faulty colour tube to restore vibrant images on the screen.
Manufacturers stopped producing colour tubes as flat-screen technology became popular.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: picture tube - the cathode ray tube in a television or monitor that displays the picture
