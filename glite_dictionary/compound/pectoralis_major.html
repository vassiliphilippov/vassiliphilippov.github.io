pectoralis major

1. NOUN

/ ˌpek.təˈreɪ.lɪs ˈmeɪ.dʒɚ / (AmE), / ˌpek.təˈreɪ.lɪs ˈmeɪ.dʒə / (BrE)
the thick, fan-shaped muscle located at the chest that helps move the arm by adducting and rotating it
a large chest muscle
medical

Examples

The pectoralis major muscle is crucial for movements like pushing or lifting objects above the head.
During the bench press, the pectoralis major is one of the primary muscles activated.
Injury to the pectoralis major can significantly reduce strength and mobility in the arm.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: pectoralis minor - a smaller chest muscle located beneath the pectoralis major

Etymology

From Latin 'pectoralis' (of the chest) and 'major' (greater), referring to its position and size.
