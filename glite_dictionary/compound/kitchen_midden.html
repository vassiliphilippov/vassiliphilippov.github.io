kitchen midden

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɪtʃən ˈmɪdən / (AmE), / ˈkɪtʃən ˈmɪdən / (BrE)
An archaeological mound consisting of accumulated domestic waste such as shells, animal bones, and other refuse, marking the site of a prehistoric human settlement.
prehistoric refuse heap found in archaeology

Examples

Archaeologists uncovered a large kitchen midden near the ancient coastal settlement.
The kitchen midden contained thousands of oyster shells and fragments of pottery.
Researchers studied the layers of the kitchen midden to learn about the diet of prehistoric people.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: midden - a dunghill or refuse heap

Etymology

From 'kitchen' (meaning cooking area) and Danish 'mødding' (dung heap, refuse heap).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɪtʃən ˈmɪdən / (AmE), / ˈkɪtʃɪn ˈmɪd.ən / (BrE)
An ancient mound or deposit composed mainly of shells, animal bones, and other domestic refuse, marking the site of a prehistoric or ancient settlement.
prehistoric refuse heap

Examples

Archaeologists discovered a kitchen midden containing shellfish remains dating back thousands of years.
The presence of a kitchen midden suggests that people inhabited the area during the Neolithic era.
By studying a kitchen midden, researchers can learn about ancient diets and daily life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: midden - a pile of domestic waste, or refuse heap

Etymology

From Danish 'mødding' meaning 'dung heap' or 'refuse heap'.
