tin can

1. NOUN

/ tɪn kæn / (AmE), / tɪn kæn / (BrE)
An informal term for a destroyer, a fast, lightly armored warship.
informal term for a destroyer warship
informal

Examples

The fleet included six tin cans that led the way in the attack.
Veterans who served on tin cans often spoke of the dangers they faced at sea.
He proudly recalled his days as an officer aboard a tin can.

2. NOUN

/ tɪn kæn / (AmE), / tɪn kæn / (BrE)
A small, airtight metal container, typically used for storing food, drinks, or other substances such as paint.
airtight metal food or drink container

Examples

She opened a tin can of soup for lunch.
Recycling centers collect empty tin cans from households every week.
The paint was stored in a large tin can in the garage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: can - a container for holding liquids or food, often metal

Etymology

From 'tin' (the metal used in plating) + 'can', reflecting the original material of manufacture.
