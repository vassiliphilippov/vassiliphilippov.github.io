marcescent flower

1. NOUN

/ mɑrˈsɛsənt ˈflaʊɚ / (AmE), / mɑːˈsɛsənt ˈflaʊə / (BrE)
a flower that withers but remains attached to the plant instead of falling off
withered flower remaining on plant

Examples

The marcescent flower stayed attached to the branch even after the petals faded.
Gardeners noted the presence of marcescent flowers on several of the older shrubs that year.
Unlike most blossoms, the marcescent flower remains on the stem long after it has wilted.

Synset Examples

The marcescent flowers were a sad reminder of summer.
The marcescent leaves clung to the branches all winter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marcescent - withering but not falling off (applies to leaves, flowers, etc.)
  • Antonym: deciduous flower - a flower that falls off after withering

Etymology

From Latin 'marcescens', present participle of 'marcescere' (to wither).
