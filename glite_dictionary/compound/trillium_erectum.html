Trillium erectum

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrɪliəm ɪˈrɛktəm / (AmE), / ˈtrɪlɪəm ɪˈrɛktəm / (BrE)
A species of trillium native to eastern North America, known for its malodorous pink to purple flowers and an astringent root traditionally used in folk medicine.
a species of trillium with malodorous flowers

Examples

The roots of Trillium erectum were once widely used in folk medicine.
You can identify Trillium erectum by its three broad leaves and dark purple flower.
Trillium erectum is commonly found in moist woodlands in eastern North America.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Trillium' (genus name), 'erectum' meaning 'upright.'
