kitchen cabinet

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɪtʃən ˈkæbənət / (AmE), / ˈkɪtʃ(ə)n ˈkæbɪnət / (BrE)
A group of unofficial advisers to the head of a government, especially a president or prime minister.
informal government advisers

Examples

The president often consulted his kitchen cabinet before making decisions on foreign policy.
Many believed that the real power was held by the kitchen cabinet, not the official ministers.
She was considered part of the mayor's kitchen cabinet due to her strong personal influence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adviser - a person who gives advice, especially professionally
  • Degree Higher: cabinet - the official group of senior government ministers

Etymology

Named after U.S. President Andrew Jackson's use of informal advisers who reportedly met in the White House kitchen.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɪtʃən ˈkæbɪnɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɪtʃɪn ˈkæbɪnɪt / (BrE)
a cupboard or set of cupboards in a kitchen used for storing food, dishes, utensils, or other kitchen items
cupboard for storing kitchen items

Examples

She cleaned every shelf in the kitchen cabinet before putting away the groceries.
We installed new kitchen cabinets during the renovation last spring.
He reached into the kitchen cabinet to grab a mug.

Synset Examples

She organized the dishes in the kitchen cabinet.
