marathon runner

1. NOUN

/ ˈmærəˌθɑn ˌrʌnər / (AmE), / ˈmærəθən ˌrʌnə / (BrE)
a person who takes part in marathon races, especially long-distance running events of 26.2 miles (42.195 km)
person who runs marathons

Examples

He trained for months to become a successful marathon runner.
The city honored its oldest marathon runner after she completed her twentieth race.
Many aspiring athletes dream of being a marathon runner and finishing the famous Boston Marathon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: runner - a person who runs, especially in races

Etymology

From 'marathon' (referring to the legendary Greek battle at Marathon and the subsequent race) + 'runner'.
