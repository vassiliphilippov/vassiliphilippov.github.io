great bustard

1. NOUN

/ ɡreɪt ˈbʌstərd / (AmE), / ɡreɪt ˈbʌstɑːd / (BrE)
a very large bird native to Europe and Asia, noted as the heaviest flying bird species in Europe.
large European and Asian bird species

Examples

The great bustard is considered the heaviest flying bird in Europe.
Conservationists are working to protect the habitat of the great bustard.
We spotted a great bustard during our trip to the grasslands of Spain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bustard - large, terrestrial birds of open country

Etymology

From 'great' (large, significant) + 'bustard' (from Old French 'bistarde', Latin 'otis tarda').
