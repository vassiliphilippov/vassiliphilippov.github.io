Fifth Amendment

1. PROPN

/ fɪfθ əˈmɛndmənt / (AmE), / fɪfθ əˈmɛndmənt / (BrE)
The amendment to the United States Constitution that guarantees due process of law, prohibits self-incrimination and double jeopardy, and requires just compensation for property taken for public use.
US constitutional guarantee of rights for accused persons
legal american

Examples

The suspect invoked the Fifth Amendment during the trial to avoid self-incrimination.
Many people know that the Fifth Amendment protects their right to remain silent.
The government must provide compensation under the Fifth Amendment if it takes private property for public use.
