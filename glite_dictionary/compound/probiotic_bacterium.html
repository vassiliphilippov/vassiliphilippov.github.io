probiotic bacterium

1. NOUN

/ proʊˌbaɪˈɑtɪk bæˈktɪriəm / (AmE), / prəʊˌbʌɪˈɒtɪk bækˈtɪəriəm / (BrE)
a beneficial bacterium that lives in the digestive tract and promotes healthy gut function
beneficial gut bacterium
medical

Examples

Yogurt is often promoted as a source of live probiotic bacterium that can aid digestion.
Taking a probiotic bacterium supplement may help restore gut balance after antibiotic use.
Scientists are studying how different probiotic bacterium strains affect human health.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lactic acid bacterium - a type of bacteria, often probiotic, that produces lactic acid
  • Antonym: pathogenic bacterium - a bacterium that can cause disease

Etymology

From 'probiotic' (from Greek 'pro' meaning 'for' + 'biotic' from 'bios' meaning 'life') and Latin 'bacterium' (small rod).
