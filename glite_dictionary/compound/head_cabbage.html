head cabbage

1. NOUN

/ hɛd ˈkæbɪdʒ / (AmE), / hɛd ˈkabɪdʒ / (BrE)
A cultivated cabbage plant with a short, thick stalk and a large, compact head of edible, usually green leaves.
cabbage with a compact head of leaves

Examples

She chopped the head cabbage into thin strips for the salad.
Farmers harvested rows of head cabbage in the early morning sun.
A recipe called for one medium head cabbage, minced and sautéed with onions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cabbage - a leafy garden plant eaten as a vegetable

2. NOUN

/ hɛd ˈkæb.ɪdʒ / (AmE), / hɛd ˈkæb.ɪdʒ / (BrE)
A variety of cabbage with a large, firm, and compact globular head, commonly used in cooking and salads.
cabbage with a dense, round head

Examples

She shredded the head cabbage to make a fresh coleslaw for the picnic.
Stir-fried head cabbage with garlic is a popular side dish in many Asian cuisines.
The market had piles of green head cabbage ready for winter storage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cabbage - a leafy vegetable grown for its edible head
  • Degree Lower: savoy cabbage - cabbage variety with crinkled leaves
