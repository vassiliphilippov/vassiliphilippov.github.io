Tarzan of the Apes

1. PROPN

/ ˈtɑːrˌzæn əv ði eɪps / (AmE), / ˈtɑːz(ə)n əv ðiː eɪps / (BrE)
the fictional man raised by apes who is the hero of a series of adventure novels by Edgar Rice Burroughs
fictional hero raised by apes in Burroughs' novels

Examples

Many film adaptations have brought Tarzan of the Apes to life on the big screen.
As a child, she was fascinated by the story of Tarzan of the Apes and his jungle adventures.
Tarzan of the Apes first appeared in print in 1912 and has inspired generations of readers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Tarzan - legendary character raised by apes in the jungle

Etymology

Tarzan is a name invented by Edgar Rice Burroughs for his character, first appearing in 'Tarzan of the Apes' (1912).
