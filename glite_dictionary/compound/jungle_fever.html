jungle fever

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒʌŋɡəl ˈfiːvər / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌŋɡl ˈfiːvə / (BrE)
a severe form of malaria that occurs in tropical regions
severe malaria in tropical regions
medical

Examples

During the expedition, several members of the team fell ill with jungle fever.
Doctors struggled to control the spread of jungle fever in the remote village.
He wrote about his frightening battle with jungle fever in his memoir.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: malaria - a disease transmitted by mosquitoes, causing fever and chills

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʒʌŋɡəl ˌfiːvər / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌŋɡl ˌfiːvə / (BrE)
an intense desire for adventure or excitement, especially relating to travel to exotic or natural places
strong urge for exotic adventure or travel

Examples

Ever since she read about the Amazon, she's had jungle fever and dreams of exploring rainforests.
His jungle fever pushed him to sign up for a trekking adventure in Southeast Asia.
Travel magazines often fuel readers' jungle fever with stories of wild, untouched landscapes.

Synset Examples
