Meibomian gland

1. NOUN

/ maɪˈboʊmiən ɡlænd / (AmE), / maɪˈbəʊmiən ɡlænd / (BrE)
a long sebaceous gland located in the eyelids, responsible for secreting oils that lubricate the surface of the eye
sebaceous gland in the eyelids
medical

Examples

A blockage in a Meibomian gland may cause irritation and swelling of the eyelid.
Doctors examined her eyelid for signs of a Meibomian gland dysfunction.
Proper eyelid hygiene can help maintain healthy Meibomian glands.

Synset Examples

bacterial infection of a Meibomian gland produces a stye

Etymology

Named after German physician Heinrich Meibom (1638–1700).
