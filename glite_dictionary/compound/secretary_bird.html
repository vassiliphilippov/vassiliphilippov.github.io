secretary bird

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈkrɛtɛri bɝd / (AmE), / sɪˈkrɛtəri bɜːd / (BrE)
a large, long-legged African bird of prey (Sagittarius serpentarius) known for its distinctive crest and for hunting snakes and other reptiles on the ground
large African bird of prey

Examples

The secretary bird is famous for its impressive technique in hunting snakes in the African savanna.
We spotted a rare secretary bird striding gracefully through the tall grass during our safari.
Unlike most birds of prey, the secretary bird hunts on foot rather than from the air.

Etymology

Named from its crest of long feathers thought to resemble a secretary's quill pens behind the ear.
