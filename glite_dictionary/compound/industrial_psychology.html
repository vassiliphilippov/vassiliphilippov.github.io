industrial psychology

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈdʌstriəl saɪˈkɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ɪnˈdʌstriəl saɪˈkɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the scientific study of human behavior in workplaces and the application of psychological principles to solve problems in industry and business
application of psychology in workplaces

Examples

She decided to pursue a career in industrial psychology after noticing the importance of employee well-being.
Many companies consult experts in industrial psychology to improve workplace productivity.
His graduate thesis focused on the impact of leadership styles, a key topic in industrial psychology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: organizational psychology - the branch of psychology focusing on behavior within organizations
