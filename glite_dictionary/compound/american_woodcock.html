American woodcock

1. NOUN

/ əˈmɛrɪkən ˈwʊdkɑk / (AmE), / əˈmɛrɪkən ˈwʊdkɒk / (BrE)
a small North American bird with a long bill, prized as a game bird
small North American long-billed game bird

Examples

We spotted an American woodcock near the edge of the wetland at dawn.
The American woodcock is sought after by bird hunters for its elusive nature.
Each spring, the unique courtship display of the American woodcock attracts birdwatchers from across the country.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: woodcock - a bird of the genus Scolopax, known for its long bill and woodland habitat
