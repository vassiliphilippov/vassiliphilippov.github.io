stereoscopic vision

1. NOUN

/ ˌstɛriəˈskɑpɪk ˈvɪʒən / (AmE), / ˌstɪəriəˈskɒpɪk ˈvɪʒən / (BrE)
the ability to perceive depth and three-dimensional structure as a result of combining two slightly different images from each eye
three-dimensional vision from both eyes
medical

Examples

Owls have remarkable stereoscopic vision, allowing them to judge distances when hunting prey at night.
Virtual reality headsets create an illusion of depth by giving each eye a separate image, simulating stereoscopic vision.
The loss of one eye significantly impairs stereoscopic vision and depth perception.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: binocular vision - the ability to use both eyes to perceive a single three-dimensional image

Etymology

From stereo- (Greek 'solid') + 'scopic' ('seeing, viewing'); refers to viewing with two eyes.
