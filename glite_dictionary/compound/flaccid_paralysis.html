flaccid paralysis

1. NOUN

/ ˈflæsɪd pəˈræləsɪs / (AmE), / ˈflæsɪd pəˈralɪsɪs / (BrE)
A loss or weakness of muscle tone caused by injury or disease affecting the nerves that supply muscles, resulting in limp and weak muscles.
loss of muscle tone due to nerve damage
medical

Examples

Polio can cause flaccid paralysis in affected limbs, leaving muscles limp and unresponsive.
The patient developed flaccid paralysis after the severe nerve injury to his arm.
Unlike spastic paralysis, flaccid paralysis results in weak, hypotonic muscles without spasms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paralysis - loss of ability to move part or most of the body

Etymology

From Latin 'flaccidus' (flabby) and Greek 'parálusis' (paralysis).
