Sachs disease

1. NOUN

/ sæks dɪˈziːz / (AmE), / sæks dɪˈziːz / (BrE)
A rare hereditary disorder of lipid metabolism, most common among people of Eastern European Jewish descent, which leads to the accumulation of lipids in nerve cells and typically results in death in early childhood.
hereditary lipid metabolism disorder, fatal in early childhood
medical

Examples

Sachs disease is most commonly found among individuals of Ashkenazi Jewish ancestry.
Doctors may recommend genetic screening for Sachs disease before starting a family in high-risk populations.
Children affected by Sachs disease often show neurological symptoms within the first few months of life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Tay-Sachs disease - A hereditary metabolic disorder causing lipid accumulation in nerve cells, fatal in children

Etymology

Named after Warren Tay and Bernard Sachs, physicians who described the disease in the 19th century.
