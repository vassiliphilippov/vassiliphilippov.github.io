ground state

1. NOUN

/ ɡraʊnd steɪt / (AmE), / ɡraʊnd steɪt / (BrE)
(Physics) The lowest energy state of an atom or other particle.
lowest energy state of an atom or particle

Examples

An atom in its ground state is stable and emits no radiation.
The transition from an excited state to the ground state releases energy.
At absolute zero, all particles occupy their ground state.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: excited state - a state of higher energy than the ground state
