Chattahoochee River

1. PROPN

/ ˌtʃætəˈhuːtʃi ˈrɪvər / (AmE), / ˌtʃætəˈhuːtʃi ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
A river in the southeastern United States, rising in northern Georgia and flowing southwest and south to join the Flint River at the Florida border, forming the Apalachicola River.
river in Georgia, USA

Examples

The Chattahoochee River provides water to millions of residents in the Atlanta area.
We spent the weekend kayaking along the scenic Chattahoochee River.
The Chattahoochee River marks part of the border between Georgia and Alabama.

Etymology

From the Creek (Muskogean) language, likely meaning 'painted rocks.'
