tabasco plant

1. NOUN

/ təˈbæskoʊ plænt / (AmE), / təˈbæskəʊ plɑːnt / (BrE)
a plant bearing very hot, medium-sized, oblong red peppers, cultivated mainly in the Gulf Coast states for making hot sauce.
pepper plant used for hot sauce

Examples

The tabasco plant thrives in warm climates and is prized for its spicy peppers.
Farmers in Louisiana often cultivate the tabasco plant to supply hot sauce manufacturers.
She grew a tabasco plant in her backyard to harvest her own chili peppers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Capsicum frutescens - the scientific name of the tabasco pepper plant
  • Degree Lower: jalapeño - a different variety of hot pepper plant
  • Degree Higher: habanero - a hotter variety of pepper plant

Etymology

From Mexican Spanish 'Tabasco', a region in Mexico where the pepper originated.
