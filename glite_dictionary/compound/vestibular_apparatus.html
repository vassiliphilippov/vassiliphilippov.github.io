vestibular apparatus

1. NOUN

/ vɛˈstɪbjələr əˈpærətəs / (AmE), / vɛˈstɪbjələr əˈparətəs / (BrE)
the system of organs in the inner ear that are responsible for balance and spatial orientation
organs in inner ear for balance
medical

Examples

Disorders of the vestibular apparatus can lead to dizziness and problems with balance.
The vestibular apparatus helps us detect changes in head position and movement.
Damage to the vestibular apparatus may result in vertigo and loss of equilibrium.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inner ear - part of the ear containing the organs for balance and hearing

Etymology

From Latin 'vestibulum' (entrance hall) + apparatus (equipment),
