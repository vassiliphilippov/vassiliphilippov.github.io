surgical suction pump

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɝdʒɪkəl ˈsʌkʃən pʌmp / (AmE), / ˈsɜːdʒɪkəl ˈsʌkʃən pʌmp / (BrE)
A medical device used during and after surgical procedures to remove fluids, tissues, or gases from the body.
device for removing fluids during surgery
medical

Examples

The surgeon used a surgical suction pump to clear blood from the operating field.
Before closing the incision, the nurse activated the surgical suction pump to remove excess fluids.
Modern surgical suction pumps are essential equipment in operating rooms around the world.

Synset Examples

Surgical vacuum pumps are being used not only for general surgery, but also for liposuction and endoscopy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aspirator - a device used for removing fluids or gases by suction
