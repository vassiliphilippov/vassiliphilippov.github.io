alkyl radical

1. NOUN

/ ˈæl.kɪl ˈræd.ɪ.kəl / (AmE), / ˈæl.kɪl ˈræd.ɪ.kəl / (BrE)
a univalent chemical group with the general formula CnH2n+1, derived from aliphatic hydrocarbons
univalent group from aliphatic hydrocarbons
medical

Examples

The methyl group is the simplest alkyl radical and is commonly found in organic compounds.
Researchers studied the reactivity of a newly synthesized alkyl radical in the laboratory.
Combustion reactions often involve the formation and breakdown of alkyl radicals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alkyl group - a univalent group derived from alkanes by removal of a hydrogen atom

Etymology

From 'alkyl', based on 'alkane' and 'yl', and 'radical' from Latin 'radix' (root).
