South Island

1. PROPN

/ saʊθ ˈaɪ.lənd / (AmE), / saʊθ ˈaɪ.lənd / (BrE)
the larger but less populous of the two main islands of New Zealand, separated from the North Island by Cook Strait.
main island of New Zealand

Examples

Many tourists travel to the South Island to experience its stunning mountains and lakes.
The South Island was hit by a strong earthquake last year.
Queenstown, on the South Island, is known for its adventure sports.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: North Island - the smaller but more populous of the two main islands of New Zealand
