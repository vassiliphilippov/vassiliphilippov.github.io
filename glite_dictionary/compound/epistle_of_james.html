Epistle of James

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈpɪsəl əv ˈdʒeɪmz / (AmE), / ɪˈpɪsəl əv ˈdʒeɪmz / (BrE)
a book of the New Testament attributed to Saint James the Apostle, containing teachings on faith and conduct
New Testament book by Saint James

Examples

The Epistle of James emphasizes the relationship between faith and deeds in Christian life.
Many scholars debate the authorship and audience of the Epistle of James.
My church group is currently studying the teachings found in the Epistle of James.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: James - the apostle who is traditionally identified as the author of the Epistle
  • Synonym: Epistle - a letter, especially a formal or didactic one; often used for books in the New Testament

Etymology

From Latin epistola (letter) and James, after the apostle Jacobus in Latin.
