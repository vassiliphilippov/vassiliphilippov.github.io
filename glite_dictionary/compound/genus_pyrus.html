genus Pyrus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˈpaɪrəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈpʌɪrəs / (BrE)
A genus of deciduous fruit trees native to Europe and Asia, comprising the pears.
genus of pear trees

Examples

The genus Pyrus includes several species that are widely cultivated for their fruit.
Botanists classify the European pear as Pyrus communis within the genus Pyrus.
Many ornamental varieties in the genus Pyrus are valued for their beautiful blossoms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pear - an edible fruit produced by trees in the genus Pyrus

Etymology

From Latin 'pyrus' (pear) used in botanical naming.
