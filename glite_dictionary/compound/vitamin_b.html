vitamin B

1. NOUN

/ ˈvaɪtəmɪn ˈbi / (AmE), / ˈvɪtəmɪn ˈbi / (BrE)
the group of water-soluble vitamins known as the B complex, which were once believed to be a single vitamin but are now recognized as several distinct B vitamins
group of B-complex vitamins

Examples

Doctors often recommend increasing your vitamin B intake for better energy levels.
The term vitamin B was once used before scientists identified several separate B vitamins.
Leafy greens and whole grains are rich sources of vitamin B.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: B vitamin - any individual vitamin in the B complex
  • Antonym: vitamin C - a different water-soluble vitamin essential for health
