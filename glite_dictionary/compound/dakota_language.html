Dakota language

1. NOUN

/ dəˈkoʊtə ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / dəˈkəʊtə ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Siouan language spoken by the Dakota people, primarily in North and South Dakota and surrounding regions.
the Siouan language of the Dakota people

Examples

Many efforts are underway to revitalize the Dakota language among younger generations.
He studied the Dakota language to better understand the history and culture of the Plains tribes.
A dictionary of the Dakota language was published by missionaries in the 19th century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Siouan - a family of North American Indian languages including Dakota

Etymology

From Dakota, the name of the Native American tribe, and language, itself.
