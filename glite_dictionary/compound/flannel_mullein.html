flannel mullein

1. NOUN

/ ˈflæn.əl ˈmʌl.ɪn / (AmE), / ˈflæn.əl ˈmʌl.ɪn / (BrE)
a tall, woolly plant with dense spikes of yellow flowers (Verbascum thapsus), formerly used for torches in ancient times
tall woolly herb with yellow flowers

Examples

The flannel mullein stood out in the field with its tall, woolly stalks topped with yellow blossoms.
Ancient Greeks once used flannel mullein dipped in tallow as torches for ceremonies.
Gardeners sometimes plant flannel mullein for its attractive flowers and drought resistance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mullein - a genus of tall, woolly plants with spikes of yellow flowers
