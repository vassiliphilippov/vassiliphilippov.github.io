straight-fluted drill

1. NOUN

/ streɪt ˈfluːtɪd drɪl / (AmE), / streɪt ˈfluːtɪd drɪl / (BrE)
A drill used for rock or masonry with straight, non-spiral grooves (flutes) along its shaft to provide channels for debris removal.
rock drill with straight grooves

Examples

The engineer selected a straight-fluted drill to reduce the risk of the bit wandering during the initial cut.
Unlike spiral drills, a straight-fluted drill is ideal for working with brittle materials like masonry.
The technician used a straight-fluted drill to create clean holes in the hard rock surface.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: twist drill - a drill with helical (spiral) flutes
