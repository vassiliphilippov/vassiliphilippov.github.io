center field

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛntɚ fiːld / (AmE), / ˈsɛntə fiːld / (BrE)
the area of the outfield in baseball directly behind second base and ahead of the catcher, between left field and right field
the middle part of the outfield in baseball

Examples

The batter hit a sharp line drive into center field.
A speedy player should cover a lot of ground in center field.
The outfielder caught the fly ball deep in center field.

Synset Examples

he hit the ball to deep center

Related Terms

  • Synonym: left field - the area of the outfield to the left as facing out from home plate
  • Synonym: right field - the area of the outfield to the right as facing out from home plate

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɛn.tɚ fiːld / (AmE), / ˈsɛn.tə fiːld / (BrE)
the position in baseball in which the player fields balls hit to the central area of the outfield
central outfield position in baseball

Examples

He plays center field for the local baseball team.
A long fly ball was caught in center field during the seventh inning.
She has excelled at center field since joining the varsity squad.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: outfield - the area of a baseball field beyond the infield
