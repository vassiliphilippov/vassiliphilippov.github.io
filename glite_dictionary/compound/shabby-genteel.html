shabby-genteel

1. ADJ

/ ˌʃæbi dʒɛnˈtiːl / (AmE), / ˌʃæbi dʒɛnˈtiːl / (BrE)
having an appearance of faded elegance or gentility, trying to maintain dignity and self-respect despite poverty or shabbiness
trying to appear respectable despite shabbiness

Examples

Their shabby-genteel home told a story of former prosperity now faded.
She maintained a shabby-genteel air, wearing her old clothes with pride.
The city's shabby-genteel neighborhoods were once grand but now showed signs of decline.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: genteel - polite, refined, or respectable, often in an affected or ostentatious way
  • Degree Lower: shabby - in poor condition through long use or lack of care

Etymology

From 'shabby' (worn or faded) + 'genteel' (refined, polite); used since the early 19th century.
