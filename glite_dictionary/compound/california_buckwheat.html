California buckwheat

1. NOUN

/ kælɪˈfɔrniə ˈbʌkˌwiːt / (AmE), / kælɪˈfɔːniə ˈbʌkwiːt / (BrE)
a low-growing shrub of western United States deserts, with spreading branches and clusters of small flowers, commonly found from California to Utah
a desert shrub native to western US

Examples

The sandy hillside was dotted with California buckwheat, its white blossoms attracting native bees.
Hikers in the Mojave often spot California buckwheat thriving alongside creosote bush and sage.
Native Americans traditionally used California buckwheat for medicinal purposes and as a food source.
