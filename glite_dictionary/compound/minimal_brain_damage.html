minimal brain damage

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪn.ɪ.məl breɪn ˈdæm.ɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈmɪn.ɪ.məl breɪn ˈdam.ɪdʒ / (BrE)
a condition, primarily in boys, characterized by behavioral and learning disorders thought to result from mild brain dysfunction
mild brain dysfunction with behavioral and learning disorders
medical

Examples

Some experts believe that minimal brain damage may explain certain learning difficulties in children.
In the 1970s, the term minimal brain damage was commonly used to describe hyperactive behavior.
The diagnosis of minimal brain damage is now considered outdated and has been largely replaced by newer terms.
