final clause

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪnəl klɔz / (AmE), / ˈfaɪnəl klɑːz / (BrE)
A subordinate clause that expresses purpose or intention, often introduced by phrases like 'in order that' or 'lest'.
subordinate clause expressing purpose

Examples

The teacher explained how to use a final clause to show the purpose of an action.
In the sentence 'He whispered so that no one would hear,' the words after 'so that' form a final clause.
Many languages use a specific structure for a final clause to express intention or aim.

Synset Examples

Final clauses that refer to the same subject as the main clause of the sentence can be expressed with to, in order to, so as to, for fear of, et cetera.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: purpose clause - subordinate clause stating the purpose of the main clause

Etymology

From Latin 'finalis' (of or related to an end) and 'clause' (from Latin 'clausula', a closing).
