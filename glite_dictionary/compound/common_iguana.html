common iguana

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːmən ɪˈɡwɑːnə / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən ɪˈɡwɑːnə / (BrE)
a large, herbivorous lizard native to Central and South America, with a spiny crest along its back, commonly found in trees and sometimes used as food
large tropical arboreal lizard of the Americas

Examples

The common iguana is often seen basking on tree branches near rivers in Central America.
Local farmers sometimes catch the common iguana for food.
The vivid green color helps young common iguanas blend in with the rainforest foliage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: green iguana - the same species, alternate common name
