Fucus serratus

1. NOUN

/ ˈfjuːkəs səˈreɪtəs / (AmE), / ˈfjuːkəs səˈrɑːtəs / (BrE)
A species of brown seaweed with serrated edges, commonly found on rocky seashores in the North Atlantic.
species of brown seaweed with serrated edges

Examples

The rocky tide pools were covered in Fucus serratus, with its characteristic jagged edges.
Researchers studied the growth patterns of Fucus serratus along the Atlantic coast.
Harvesters collect Fucus serratus for use in various cosmetic and food products.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Fucus' (seaweed) and 'serratus' (saw-toothed, serrated).
