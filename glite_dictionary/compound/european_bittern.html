European bittern

1. NOUN

/ jʊˈroʊpiən ˈbɪtərn / (AmE), / jʊəˈrəʊpiən ˈbɪtən / (BrE)
a large brown wading bird (Botaurus stellaris) found in marshes across Europe, known for its booming call
large marsh bird of Europe

Examples

The European bittern is known for its distinctive booming call during the breeding season.
Conservation efforts have helped increase the population of the European bittern in several countries.
We spotted a European bittern hiding among the reeds at the nature reserve.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bittern - a wading bird of the heron family with camouflaged plumage
