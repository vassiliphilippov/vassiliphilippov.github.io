Western rattlesnake

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɛstɚn ˈrætl̩ˌsneɪk / (AmE), / ˈwɛstən ˈræt(ə)lˌsneɪk / (BrE)
a venomous rattlesnake (Crotalus viridis) native to North America, widely distributed between the Mississippi River and the Rocky Mountains
a venomous North American rattlesnake species

Examples

Hikers in the region are warned to watch for the Western rattlesnake along rocky trails.
The Western rattlesnake relies on its camouflage to avoid predators and surprise prey.
Scientists are tracking the migration patterns of the Western rattlesnake across its native range.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rattlesnake - any of various venomous snakes of the genera Crotalus or Sistrurus
