easy street

1. NOUN

/ ˈizi ˌstrit / (AmE), / ˈiːzi ˌstriːt / (BrE)
a situation in which someone is financially secure and free from worry about money
state of comfortable financial security

Examples

After winning the lottery, Mark found himself on easy street for the rest of his life.
Many people dream of retiring to easy street after years of hard work.
She thought marrying a millionaire would put her on easy street, but it wasn’t so simple.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: poverty - the state of having little or no money or possessions
  • Synonym: affluence - the state of having a great deal of money; wealth
