trade policy

1. NOUN

/ treɪd ˈpɑləsi / (AmE), / treɪd ˈpɒləsi / (BrE)
A government's official policy regarding the regulation and control of foreign trade.
government policy on foreign trade

Examples

The government's trade policy aims to promote exports and protect local industries.
Changes in trade policy can have significant effects on the global economy.
The minister announced a new trade policy to attract foreign investors.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: protectionism - government policy restricting imports to protect domestic industries
  • Degree Higher: free trade - policy promoting unrestricted international trade
