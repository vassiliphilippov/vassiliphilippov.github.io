stolen valor

1. NOUN

/ ˈstoʊlən ˈvælər / (AmE), / ˈstəʊlən ˈvælə / (BrE)
the act of falsely claiming to have served in the military or to have received military honors, usually to gain respect, benefits, or recognition
false claims of military service or honors

Examples

The internet frequently exposes cases of stolen valor involving people who wear unearned medals.
Legislation has made stolen valor a prosecutable offense in several countries.
He was confronted by veterans after his stolen valor became public knowledge.

Synset Examples

Her story turned out to be a case of stolen valor.
He was exposed for his stolen valor at the ceremony.
The police investigated the claims of stolen valor.

Etymology

From 'stolen' (past participle of 'steal') and 'valor' (from Latin 'valorem', meaning worth or valor), referring to the theft of recognition or honor.
