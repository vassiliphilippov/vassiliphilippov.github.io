holiday season

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑːlɪdeɪ ˈsiːzən / (AmE), / ˈhɒlɪdeɪ ˈsiːzn̩ / (BrE)
the period of the year, often around late December, when many people take holidays and celebrate various festivals
period when many people take holidays

Examples

The holiday season is always a busy time in retail stores.
Many families travel long distances to visit relatives during the holiday season.
People often feel both joy and stress in the holiday season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Christmas season - the period of time leading up to and including Christmas
  • Synonym: holiday - a period of time when people do not have to go to work or school and can rest or travel

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɑlɪˌdeɪ ˈsizn̩ / (AmE), / ˈhɒlɪˌdeɪ ˈsiːzn̩ / (BrE)
the period around major holidays, typically characterized by increased activity, celebrations, and often higher retail sales
period around major holidays

Examples

Many companies offer special promotions during the holiday season to attract more customers.
The airport is always crowded during the holiday season as people travel to visit family.
Planning ahead is essential if you want to avoid stress in the holiday season.

Synset Examples

The sales spiked during the holiday season.
Retail sales surged during the holiday season.
The holiday season is always so hectic for retailers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: festive season - another term for the period around major holidays
