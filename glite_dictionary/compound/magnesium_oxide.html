magnesium oxide

1. NOUN

/ mægˈniːziəm ˈɑksaɪd / (AmE), / mæɡˈniːziəm ˈɒksaɪd / (BrE)
a white, odorless solid mineral (MgO) that occurs naturally as periclase and is used as a source of magnesium and in various industrial applications
white mineral compound (MgO)

Examples

The refractory bricks are made using magnesium oxide for its high melting point.
Doctors may prescribe magnesium oxide as a dietary supplement to treat magnesium deficiency.
Industrial processes often use magnesium oxide to remove sulfur from flue gases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: periclase - the natural mineral form of magnesium oxide

Etymology

From 'magnesium', named after Magnesia in Greece, and 'oxide', from French 'oxyde', from Greek 'oxys' (sharp, acid) and 'hidas' (form).
