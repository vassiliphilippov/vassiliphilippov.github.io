internal control

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtɝnəl kənˈtroʊl / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɜːnəl kənˈtrəʊl / (BrE)
An accounting procedure or system put in place within an organization to ensure efficiency, implement policies, protect assets, and prevent fraud or error.
system to promote efficiency and prevent fraud in organizations

Examples

The company improved its internal control procedures to reduce the risk of financial fraud.
Strong internal control systems help organizations detect errors before they become serious issues.
Auditors reviewed the firm's internal controls to ensure compliance with regulations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: audit - official examination of accounts and records
