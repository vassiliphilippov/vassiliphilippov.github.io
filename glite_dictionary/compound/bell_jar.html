bell jar

1. NOUN

/ bɛl dʒɑɹ / (AmE), / bɛl dʒɑː / (BrE)
A bell-shaped glass cover, typically used to protect delicate objects, cover scientific apparatus, or contain gases.
bell-shaped glass cover for protection or science

Examples

The scientist placed the specimen under a bell jar to prevent contamination.
Antique clocks are often displayed under a bell jar to protect them from dust.
The experiment was conducted inside a bell jar to observe the reaction in a controlled environment.
