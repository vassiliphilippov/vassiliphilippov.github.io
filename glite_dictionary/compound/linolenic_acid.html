linolenic acid

1. NOUN

/ lɪˌnoʊˈlɛnɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / lɪˌnɒˈlɛnɪk ˈasɪd / (BrE)
a polyunsaturated fatty acid found in some plant oils, essential in the human diet
an essential polyunsaturated fatty acid
medical

Examples

Flaxseed oil is a rich source of linolenic acid, which is important for human health.
Dietitians often recommend foods high in linolenic acid to support heart function.
The nutritional label listed linolenic acid among the beneficial fatty acids present.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: linoleic acid - another essential polyunsaturated fatty acid found in plant oils

Etymology

From linolenic, derived from linseed and the chemical suffix -ene, plus acid.
