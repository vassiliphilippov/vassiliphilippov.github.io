controlling interest

1. NOUN

/ kənˈtroʊlɪŋ ˈɪntrəst / (AmE), / kənˈtrəʊlɪŋ ˈɪntrəst / (BrE)
Ownership of more than 50% of a company's voting shares, giving the holder control over company decisions.
majority ownership of a company

Examples

The company acquired a controlling interest in its main competitor last year.
He sold enough shares to lose his controlling interest in the corporation.
Investors are seeking a controlling interest to influence management decisions.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: minority interest - ownership of less than 50% of a company's voting shares
