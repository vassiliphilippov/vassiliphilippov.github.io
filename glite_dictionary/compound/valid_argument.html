valid argument

1. NOUN

/ ˈvælɪd ˈɑːrɡjəmənt / (AmE), / ˈvælɪd ˈɑːɡjʊmənt / (BrE)
a point or line of reasoning that is logically sound and reasonable
logically sound reasoning

Examples

She presented a valid argument for implementing the new policy.
We should always consider every valid argument before making a decision.
The professor praised the student's valid argument during the debate.

Synset Examples

This phrase dismisses valid arguments based on race.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: invalid argument - an argument with faulty or illogical reasoning
  • Synonym: sound argument - an argument with both valid reasoning and true premises
