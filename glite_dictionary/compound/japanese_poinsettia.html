Japanese poinsettia

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˈpænɪs pɔɪnˈsɛtiə / (AmE), / dʒəˈpænɪs pɔɪnˈsɛtɪə / (BrE)
A showy flowering plant found from the southern United States to Peru, known for its bright bracts and resemblance to the common poinsettia.
showy flowering plant related to poinsettia

Examples

The Japanese poinsettia thrives in warm climates and produces vibrant bracts in the summer.
Gardeners often plant Japanese poinsettia for its unique appearance and colorful foliage.
I mistook a Japanese poinsettia for its more common relative during my trip to Florida.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poinsettia - A plant with bright red and green foliage, popular as a Christmas decoration
