winter heliotrope

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪntər ˈhiːliəˌtroʊp / (AmE), / ˈwɪntə ˈhiːliəˌtrəʊp / (BrE)
a European herb (Petasites fragrans) with vanilla-scented white-pink flowers, often found in damp places and blooming in winter
European herb with vanilla-scented white-pink flowers

Examples

The winter heliotrope spreads rapidly along riverbanks, adding a splash of color to the landscape during colder months.
Gardeners appreciate winter heliotrope for its fragrant flowers that bloom when little else is in flower.
I recognized the sweet scent of winter heliotrope as I walked through the park in January.
