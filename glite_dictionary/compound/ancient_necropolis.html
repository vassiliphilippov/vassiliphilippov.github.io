ancient necropolis

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪnʃənt nəˈkrɑːpəlɪs / (AmE), / ˈeɪnʃənt nɛˈkrɒpəlɪs / (BrE)
a large burial ground or cemetery from ancient times, especially one belonging to an ancient city
burial ground from ancient times

Examples

The discovery of an ancient necropolis shed new light on early Roman burial customs.
Archaeologists have spent years carefully excavating the ancient necropolis outside the city walls.
Tourists are often fascinated by the elaborate tombs in the ancient necropolis near Luxor.

Synset Examples

The ancient necropolis was a haunting sight at dusk.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cemetery - a place where the dead are buried
  • Synonym: burial ground - place where people are buried

Etymology

From Greek nekropolis, meaning 'city of the dead'.
