peer of the realm

1. NOUN

/ pɪr əv ðə ˈrɛlm / (AmE), / pɪə əv ðə ˈrɛlm / (BrE)
a member of the British nobility who has the right to sit in the House of Lords
British noble entitled to sit in the House of Lords
british

Examples

As a peer of the realm, he attended debates in the House of Lords.
She became a peer of the realm after inheriting her father's title.
Historically, a peer of the realm enjoyed significant privileges in British society.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peer - a member of the British nobility

Etymology

From 'peer' meaning 'equal' (from Latin 'par') and 'realm' (from Old French 'reaume', kingdom).
