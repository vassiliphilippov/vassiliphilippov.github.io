chronic gastritis

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɑː.nɪk ɡæˈstraɪ.tɪs / (AmE), / ˈkrɒ.nɪk ɡæˈstraɪ.tɪs / (BrE)
persistent inflammation of the lining of the stomach, often associated with underlying medical conditions or infections
long-term stomach lining inflammation
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with chronic gastritis after several months of persistent stomach discomfort.
Long-term use of certain medications can contribute to the development of chronic gastritis.
Doctors often monitor individuals with chronic gastritis for additional complications such as ulcers or anemia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gastritis - inflammation of the lining of the stomach

Etymology

From Greek *khronikos* (long-lasting) + *gastritis* (inflammation of the stomach), with gastritis from Greek *gastēr* (stomach) + *-itis* (inflammation).
