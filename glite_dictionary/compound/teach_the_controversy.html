to teach the controversy

1. VERB

/ titʃ ðə kənˈtrɒvɝsi / (AmE), / tiːtʃ ðə kənˈtrɒvəsi / (BrE)
to present or advocate for discussing opposing viewpoints on a controversial topic, especially in education
present opposing viewpoints in a debate

Examples

Some educators believe it's important to teach the controversy on topics like evolution.
The school board voted to teach the controversy in the science curriculum.
Critics argue that to teach the controversy is to give undue weight to pseudoscientific views.

Synset Examples

They aim to teach the controversy to encourage critical thinking.
We need to teach the controversy in history class.
He believes in teaching the controversy regarding climate change.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: present both sides - to show all main arguments in a debate or issue
