wheatfield

1. NOUN

/ ˈwiːtˌfiːld / (AmE), / ˈwiːtfiːld / (BrE)
A field in which wheat is grown.
field planted with wheat

Examples

In summer, the wheatfield glowed a golden yellow under the afternoon sun.
An old barn stood at the edge of the vast wheatfield.
After the harvest, only stubble remained in the empty wheatfield.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cornfield - a field planted with corn

Etymology

From 'wheat' + 'field', both Old English in origin.
