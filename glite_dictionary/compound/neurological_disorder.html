neurological disorder

1. NOUN

/ nuˌɹɑːləˈdʒɪkəl dɪsˈɔɹdɚ / (AmE), / njuːˌɹɒlɪˈdʒɪkəl dɪsˈɔːdə / (BrE)
A disorder or disease affecting the nervous system, including the brain, spinal cord, or nerves.
disorder of the nervous system
medical

Examples

Epilepsy is a well-known neurological disorder that affects millions of people worldwide.
The doctor specializes in treating rare neurological disorders such as multiple sclerosis.
Early detection of a neurological disorder can improve the chances of effective treatment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nervous system - the network of nerves and cells that carry messages to and from the brain and spinal cord to various parts of the body

Etymology

From Greek 'neuron' (nerve) and Latin 'disordinare' (to put out of order), via 'neurological' and 'disorder'.
