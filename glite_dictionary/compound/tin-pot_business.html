tin-pot business

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɪn ˌpɑt ˈbɪznəs / (AmE), / ˈtɪn ˌpɒt ˈbɪznəs / (BrE)
A business that is considered small, unimportant, or poorly run, often lacking professionalism or resources.
small or poorly run business

Examples

Many considered their tin-pot business a joke compared to established firms in the industry.
Despite starting as a tin-pot business, they eventually grew into a respected company.
He was tired of working for a tin-pot business with no real chance for advancement.

Synset Examples

They ran a tin-pot business that lacked professionalism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mom-and-pop - a small, family-run business
  • Antonym: establishment - a business organization or premises

Etymology

From 'tin-pot', originally referring to something cheap or of poor quality, combining 'tin' and 'pot' to suggest lack of worth or seriousness.
