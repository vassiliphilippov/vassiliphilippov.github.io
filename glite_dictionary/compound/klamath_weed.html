Klamath weed

1. NOUN

/ ˈklæməθ wiːd / (AmE), / ˈklæməθ wiːd / (BrE)
a yellow-flowered perennial plant (Hypericum perforatum), common in fields and waste places, often considered a weed in rangelands
yellow-flowered perennial weed

Examples

Farmers in the area are concerned by the rapid spread of Klamath weed in their pastures.
The yellow blooms of Klamath weed stand out among the native grasses during early summer.
Efforts to control Klamath weed have included introducing beetles that feed on the plant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: St John's wort - common name for several plants of the genus Hypericum, especially Hypericum perforatum

Etymology

Named after the Klamath region in the western United States; also commonly known as St John's wort.
