Knight of the Round Table

1. NOUN

/ naɪt əv ðə raʊnd ˈteɪbəl / (AmE), / naɪt əv ðə raʊnd ˈteɪbl̩ / (BrE)
(in Arthurian legend) a member of King Arthur's court, famed for chivalry and heroic deeds; one of the legendary knights who sat at the Round Table.
Arthurian legendary knight

Examples

Sir Lancelot was perhaps the most famous Knight of the Round Table.
Each Knight of the Round Table swore an oath to uphold chivalry and justice.
Legends tell of the heroic deeds performed by every Knight of the Round Table in King Arthur's service.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: knight - a man awarded a non-hereditary title by a monarch or other political leader for service to the monarch or country
