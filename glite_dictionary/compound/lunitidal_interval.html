lunitidal interval

1. NOUN

/ ˌluːnɪˈtaɪdəl ˈɪntɚvəl / (AmE), / ˌluːnɪˈtaɪdəl ˈɪntəvəl / (BrE)
the time period between the moon's transit over a particular meridian and the following high tide at that location
time between moon's crossing and next local high tide

Examples

The lunitidal interval is used in tide tables to predict the timing of local high tides.
Scientists measured the lunitidal interval to better understand the bay's tidal patterns.
Because the lunitidal interval varies by location, local data is essential for accurate tide predictions.

Etymology

From Latin 'luna' (moon) + 'tidal' + 'interval'.
