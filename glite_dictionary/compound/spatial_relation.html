spatial relation

1. NOUN

/ ˈspeɪʃəl rɪˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈspeɪʃəl rɪˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the arrangement or positioning of objects in space relative to one another
arrangement of objects in space

Examples

The designer considered the spatial relation between the sofa and the windows.
Children learn about spatial relations by building with blocks.
When arranging photographs on the wall, their spatial relation can affect the overall look of the room.

Synset Examples

the position of the hands on the clock
he specified the spatial relations of every piece of furniture on the stage

Related Terms

  • Synonym: position - the location of something in relation to other things
  • Synonym: orientation - the direction or alignment of something in space

Etymology

From 'spatial', relating to space (from Latin spatium, space), and 'relation', from Latin relatio (bringing back, relation).
