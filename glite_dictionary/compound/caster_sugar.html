caster sugar

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæstər ˈʃʊɡər / (AmE), / ˈkɑːstə ˈʃʊɡə / (BrE)
Sugar ground to a very fine granule, used especially in baking and for sweetening drinks.
very finely granulated sugar

Examples

The recipe calls for caster sugar because it dissolves quickly in batter.
She sprinkled caster sugar on top of the freshly baked sponge cake.
If you can't find caster sugar, you can grind regular sugar to a finer texture.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: granulated sugar - sugar with standard-sized crystals
  • Degree Higher: powdered sugar - sugar ground to a powder, also called confectioner's sugar

Etymology

From 'caster', a container with a perforated top used to sprinkle fine powders, from the practice of sprinkling this sugar from a castor.
