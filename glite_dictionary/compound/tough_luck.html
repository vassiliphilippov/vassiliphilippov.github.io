tough luck

1. NOUN

/ tʌf lʌk / (AmE), / tʌf lʌk / (BrE)
used to express sympathy or, more often, a lack of sympathy for someone’s misfortune; bad luck or unfortunate circumstances.
bad luck; expression of sympathy or indifference

Examples

After all his hard work, losing the contract was just tough luck.
You forgot your homework again? Well, tough luck—the deadline was yesterday.
If it rains during the picnic, that’s tough luck for everyone.

Synset Examples

It’s tough luck if you don’t plan ahead.
That’s just tough luck; you’ll have to try again.
You missed the bus? That's tough luck!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bad luck - unfortunate circumstances; misfortune
