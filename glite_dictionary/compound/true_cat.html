true cat

1. NOUN

/ truː kæt / (AmE), / truː kæt / (BrE)
A mammal of the family Felidae that typically has thick, soft fur and cannot roar; includes domestic cats and wildcats but excludes big cats like lions and tigers.
feline mammal that can't roar

Examples

The domestic cat is classified as a true cat because it cannot roar.
Unlike lions or tigers, true cats are known for their inability to roar and for their agile movements.
Wildcats, such as the European wildcat, are examples of true cats living in the wild.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: big cat - large felines that can roar, like lions and tigers
  • Synonym: domestic cat - small domesticated member of the cat family
  • Synonym: wildcat - small, non-domesticated member of the cat family
