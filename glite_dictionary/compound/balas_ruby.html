balas ruby

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæləs ˈruːbi / (AmE), / ˈbæləs ˈruːbi / (BrE)
a pale rose-colored variety of spinel, formerly mistaken for a true ruby and valued as a gemstone
pale rose spinel gemstone variety

Examples

The crown was adorned with a large balas ruby set among diamonds.
Collectors prize the distinctive pale hue of a genuine balas ruby.
Historically, many famous 'rubies' in royal regalia were actually balas rubies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ruby - a precious stone consisting of corundum in a shade of red

Etymology

From Medieval Latin balasius, from Arabic balakhsh, ultimately from Badakhshan, a region in present-day Afghanistan known for spinel mines.
