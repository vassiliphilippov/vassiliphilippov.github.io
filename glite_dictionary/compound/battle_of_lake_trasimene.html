Battle of Lake Trasimene

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæt̬əl əv ˌleɪk træˈsɪmiːn / (AmE), / ˈbæt.əl əv ˌleɪk træˈsɪmiːn / (BrE)
a battle in central Italy in 217 BC, where Hannibal's Carthaginian forces defeated the Romans under Flaminius during the Second Punic War
battle in Italy where Hannibal defeated the Romans, 217 BC

Examples

The Battle of Lake Trasimene marked one of Hannibal's most significant victories against Rome.
Many historians regard the Battle of Lake Trasimene as a turning point in the Second Punic War.
Flaminius underestimated Hannibal's tactics, leading to disaster at the Battle of Lake Trasimene.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Battle of Cannae - a major battle where Hannibal decisively defeated the Romans in 216 BC
  • Synonym: Second Punic War - the war between Carthage and Rome in which the Battle of Lake Trasimene occurred

2. PROPN

/ ˈbæt̬l əv ˌleɪk træˈsɪməni / (AmE), / ˈbætl əv ˌleɪk træˈsɪməni / (BrE)
a battle fought in 217 BC during the Second Punic War, in which Hannibal ambushed and defeated a Roman army led by Flaminius near Lake Trasimene in Italy
battle in 217 BC between Hannibal and Rome

Examples

The Battle of Lake Trasimene was one of Hannibal's most famous victories against the Romans.
Many historians regard the Battle of Lake Trasimene as a brilliant example of military ambush tactics.
The unexpected defeat at the Battle of Lake Trasimene shocked the Roman Republic and changed the course of the war.
