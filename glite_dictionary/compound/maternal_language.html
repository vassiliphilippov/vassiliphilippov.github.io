maternal language

1. NOUN

/ məˈtɝːnəl ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / məˈtɜːnəl ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the language that a person learns from birth and speaks as their native language, typically passed down from parents
native language; first language

Examples

Although she speaks several languages, Spanish remains her maternal language.
In their new country, parents struggled to ensure their children did not forget their maternal language.
Preserving one's maternal language helps maintain a strong connection to cultural identity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mother tongue - the language that a person has spoken from earliest childhood
  • Synonym: native language - the first language learned by a person, typically from birth

Etymology

From Latin 'mater' (mother) and Latin 'lingua' (language, tongue); 'maternal' means relating to a mother.
