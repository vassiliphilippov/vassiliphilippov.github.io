ground wave

1. NOUN

/ ɡraʊnd weɪv / (AmE), / ɡraʊnd weɪv / (BrE)
a radio wave that travels along or near the Earth's surface, rather than through the atmosphere or space
radio wave traveling near Earth's surface

Examples

Many AM radio stations rely on ground wave propagation during the day for long-distance transmission.
A ground wave can be affected by terrain, especially hills and bodies of water.
Unlike sky waves, a ground wave follows the curvature of the Earth.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sky wave - a radio wave that is reflected back to Earth from the ionosphere
  • Antonym: direct wave - a radio wave that travels directly through the atmosphere without following the ground or being reflected
