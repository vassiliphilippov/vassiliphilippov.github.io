yellow bass

1. NOUN

/ ˈjɛloʊ bæs / (AmE), / ˈjɛləʊ bas / (BrE)
a small North American freshwater bass (Morone mississippiensis) with yellowish coloration, resembling the larger striped bass
a North American freshwater fish species

Examples

The angler proudly displayed the yellow bass he caught at the lake.
Yellow bass are known for their lively fight when hooked.
Unlike striped bass, yellow bass are smaller and usually found in inland waters.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: striped bass - a larger species of bass with horizontal stripes
  • Synonym: white bass - a closely related freshwater bass species
