buzz of the crowd

1. NOUN

/ bʌz əv ðə kraʊd / (AmE), / bʌz əv ðə kraʊd / (BrE)
the feeling of excitement and energy generated by a large group of people, especially an audience
excitement and energy from a crowd

Examples

There's nothing like the buzz of the crowd before a big game starts.
She thrives on the buzz of the crowd when performing on stage.
The buzz of the crowd could be felt from outside the arena.

Synset Examples

He loves the buzz of the crowd at concerts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: atmosphere - the mood or feeling in a place or situation
  • Antonym: silence - absence of noise or excitement in a crowd
