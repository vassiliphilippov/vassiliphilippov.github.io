Admiralty Island

1. PROPN

/ ˌæd.mɪr.əl.ti ˈaɪ.lənd / (AmE), / ˌæd.mɪr.əl.ti ˈaɪ.lənd / (BrE)
an island in the Alexander Archipelago of Alaska, located near Juneau, known for its large population of brown bears and dense forests.
island in Alaska's Alexander Archipelago

Examples

We went hiking on Admiralty Island to see the brown bears in their natural habitat.
Much of Admiralty Island is protected as part of a national monument.
The dense forests of Admiralty Island are home to a variety of wildlife.

Etymology

Named after the British Admiralty in the 18th century.
