sick berth

1. NOUN

/ sɪk bɜrθ / (AmE), / sɪk bɜːθ / (BrE)
(Nautical) A room or area on a ship designated for the treatment of sick or injured people.
ship's medical room or infirmary

Examples

During his time in the navy, he spent several days in the sick berth recovering from an illness.
The ship's sick berth was well-equipped to handle minor injuries and illnesses.
After the accident on deck, the wounded sailor was taken directly to the sick berth for treatment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sickbay - ship's medical room or hospital; alternative modern term
