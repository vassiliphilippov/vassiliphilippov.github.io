anaphylactic antibody

1. NOUN

/ ˌænəfəˈlæktɪk ˈæn.t̬iˌbɑː.di / (AmE), / ˌan.ə.fɪˈlak.tɪk ˈæn.tɪˌbɒd.i / (BrE)
an antibody that binds to certain cells because of properties of its Fc region, and is involved in allergic immune responses.
antibody involved in allergic reactions
medical

Examples

Exposure to the allergen triggered the release of anaphylactic antibody in his immune system.
Scientists study how the anaphylactic antibody binds to specific cells in allergic reactions.
The presence of anaphylactic antibodies can lead to symptoms such as swelling and difficulty breathing.

Synset Examples

Cytophilic antibodies are responsible for the allergic immunological response of the organism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cytophilic antibody - an antibody with a strong affinity for certain cell types
  • Degree Lower: antibody - a protein produced by the immune system to recognize and neutralize foreign substances

Etymology

From 'anaphylactic' (relating to anaphylaxis, a severe allergic reaction) and 'antibody'.
