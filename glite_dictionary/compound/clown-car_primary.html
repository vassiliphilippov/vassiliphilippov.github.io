clown-car primary

1. NOUN

/ ˈklaʊn kɑːr ˈpraɪˌmɛri / (AmE), / ˈklaʊn kɑː ˈprɪˌməri / (BrE)
A primary election characterized by an excessive and chaotic number of candidates.
chaotic primary with many candidates
informal

Examples

Some analysts called the 2016 Republican contest a clown-car primary because of the sheer number of hopefuls.
The clown-car primary made it hard for voters to distinguish serious candidates from the rest.
After the clown-car primary, party leaders pushed for electoral reforms to reduce such confusion.

Synset Examples

The media labeled it a clown-car primary due to the absurdity.
Voters were confused by the chaos of the clown-car primary.
The election felt like a clown-car primary with too many candidates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crowded field - an election with many candidates
  • Antonym: runaway race - an election where one candidate dominates early

Etymology

Formed by analogy to 'clown car', a circus car from which an improbably large number of clowns emerge.
