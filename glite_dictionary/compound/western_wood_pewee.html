western wood pewee

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɛstərn wʊd ˈpiːwi / (AmE), / ˈwɛstən wʊd ˈpiːwi / (BrE)
a small flycatcher (Contopus sordidulus) found in western North America, recognized for its plain grayish color and melodious call
a small western North American flycatcher

Examples

We spotted a western wood pewee perched quietly on a fence post during our hike.
The call of the western wood pewee is a familiar sound in mountain forests throughout the summer.
Unlike its eastern counterpart, the western wood pewee prefers open woodlands and forest edges.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: eastern wood pewee - a similar flycatcher species found in eastern North America
