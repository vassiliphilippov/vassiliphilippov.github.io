true equality

1. NOUN

/ tru ɪˈkwɑlɪti / (AmE), / truː ɪˈkwɒlɪti / (BrE)
complete and genuine fairness and equal rights, without exceptions or limitations
genuine and complete fairness and equal rights

Examples

Many activists continue to fight for true equality in the workplace.
Only with true equality can a society reach its full potential.
Laws alone do not guarantee true equality; social attitudes must also change.

Synset Examples

Challenges of genderism must be addressed for true equality.
