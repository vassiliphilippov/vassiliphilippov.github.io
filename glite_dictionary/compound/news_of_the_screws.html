News of the Screws

1. PROPN

/ nuz əv ðə skruz / (AmE), / njuːz əv ðə skruːz / (BrE)
an informal, often derogatory, nickname for the British tabloid newspaper 'News of the World'
British tabloid newspaper nickname
informal british

Examples

He's always quoting headlines from the News of the Screws at work.
The scandal was splashed all over the News of the Screws last Sunday.
Journalists from the News of the Screws were caught in a phone-hacking controversy.

Synset Examples

The tabloids are full of News of the Screws lately.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: News of the World - British Sunday tabloid newspaper (1843–2011)

Etymology

Informal play on the title 'News of the World', using 'screws' as a pun suggesting scandal or sensationalism.
