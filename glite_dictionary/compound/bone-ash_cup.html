bone-ash cup

1. NOUN

/ ˈboʊnˌæʃ kʌp / (AmE), / ˈbəʊnˌæʃ kʌp / (BrE)
A small, porous bowl made from bone ash, used in assaying to separate precious metals from other substances such as lead.
porous bowl used in assaying

Examples

The metallurgist placed the sample in a bone-ash cup before heating it in the furnace.
During the assaying process, bone-ash cups help separate precious metals from base materials.
A new shipment of bone-ash cups arrived for use in the laboratory.

Etymology

From 'bone ash', the ash obtained by calcining bones, and 'cup', referring to its shape and function in metallurgy.
