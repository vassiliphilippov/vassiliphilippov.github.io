genus Passer

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈpæs.ər / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈpæs.ə / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Passeridae, which includes the true sparrows found in Eurasia and Africa.
type genus of the sparrow family Passeridae

Examples

The genus Passer includes some of the world’s most familiar small birds, such as the house sparrow.
Biologists have identified several species within the genus Passer across Europe, Asia, and Africa.
Research on the genus Passer helps scientists understand avian adaptation in urban environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Passeridae - the sparrow family in zoological taxonomy

Etymology

From Latin 'passer', meaning sparrow.
