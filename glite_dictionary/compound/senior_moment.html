senior moment

1. NOUN

/ ˈsiːniɚ ˈmoʊmənt / (AmE), / ˈsiːniə ˈməʊmənt / (BrE)
a brief period when someone, especially an older person, temporarily forgets something or becomes confused
temporary lapse of memory, often in older people
informal

Examples

I had a senior moment and completely forgot where I left my keys.
During the meeting, he paused with a senior moment before recalling the details.
She laughed off her senior moment when she called her grandson by the dog's name.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: memory lapse - a temporary failure to remember something

Etymology

Formed from 'senior', meaning older person, and 'moment', referring to a short period; originated in late 20th-century American English.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsinjɚ ˈmoʊmənt / (AmE), / ˈsiːniə ˈməʊmənt / (BrE)
a temporary and often minor lapse in memory or judgment, typically attributed to the effects of aging
minor lapse in memory linked to aging
informal

Examples

I had a senior moment and left my keys in the refrigerator.
He laughed off his senior moment when he forgot his neighbor's name.
We all have the occasional senior moment, regardless of our age.
