club-moss

1. NOUN

/ ˈklʌb ˌmɑs / (AmE), / ˈklʌb ˌmɒs / (BrE)
A primitive evergreen plant resembling moss, with spores produced in club-shaped cones (strobiles), belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.
primitive evergreen plant; Lycopodiaceae

Examples

The forest floor was thick with club-moss and ferns.
Many species of club-moss have medical uses dating back centuries.
We found a rare variety of club-moss during our hike in the mountains.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lycopodium - type of club-moss plant; common genus in Lycopodiaceae

Etymology

From 'club' (referring to shape of the spore cones) + 'moss'.
