Sino-Japanese War

1. PROPN

/ ˌsaɪnoʊ dʒəˈpæniz wɔr / (AmE), / ˌsʌɪnəʊ dʒəˈpaniːz wɔː / (BrE)
The war fought between China and Japan from 1894 to 1895, primarily over control of the Korean Peninsula, resulting in a decisive Japanese victory.
1894–1895 war between China and Japan

Examples

The Sino-Japanese War marked a significant turning point in East Asian history.
During the Sino-Japanese War, Japan established dominance in the region.
The consequences of the Sino-Japanese War were felt for decades in both China and Korea.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Russo-Japanese War - The war between Russia and Japan (1904–1905) over imperial ambitions in East Asia.

Etymology

From 'Sino-', meaning 'Chinese', and 'Japanese', denoting the nations involved.
