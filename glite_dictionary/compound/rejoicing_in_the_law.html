Rejoicing in the Law

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈdʒɔɪsɪŋ ɪn ðə lɔ / (AmE), / rɪˈdʒɔɪsɪŋ ɪn ðə lɔː / (BrE)
(Judaism) A Jewish holy day celebrated on the 22nd or 23rd of Tishri, marking the completion of the annual cycle of Torah readings.
Jewish festival marking end of Torah reading cycle

Examples

The community gathered to celebrate Rejoicing in the Law with dancing and singing.
During Rejoicing in the Law, the Torah scrolls are carried around the synagogue in joyful procession.
As part of Rejoicing in the Law, children often receive sweets and join in the festivities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Simchat Torah - Jewish festival celebrating completion of annual Torah reading
