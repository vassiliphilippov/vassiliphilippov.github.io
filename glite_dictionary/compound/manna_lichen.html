manna lichen

1. NOUN

/ ˈmænə ˌlaɪkən / (AmE), / ˈmanə ˌlɪkən / (BrE)
A type of crusty or bushy lichen found in African and Arabian deserts, known for rolling in the wind and being used as food by people and animals.
desert lichen used as food

Examples

Nomads in the desert collect manna lichen as a source of nourishment during dry seasons.
Animals can survive harsh conditions by feeding on manna lichen blown across the sand dunes.
In some regions, traditional recipes incorporate manna lichen for its unique texture and nutritional value.
