Trinidad and Tobago

1. PROPN

/ ˌtrɪnɪˌdæd ənd təˈbeɪgoʊ / (AmE), / ˌtrɪnɪˌdæd ən təˈbeɪɡəʊ / (BrE)
an island country in the Caribbean, comprising the islands of Trinidad and Tobago, situated off the northeastern coast of Venezuela
island country in the Caribbean

Examples

Tourists are drawn to Trinidad and Tobago for its vibrant festivals and beautiful beaches.
After gaining independence in 1962, Trinidad and Tobago developed a strong cultural identity.
The cuisine of Trinidad and Tobago reflects its diverse ethnic background.

Etymology

From the Spanish 'Trinidad' (Holy Trinity) and the native name 'Tobago'.
