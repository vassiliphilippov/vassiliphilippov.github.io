bus topology

1. NOUN

/ bʌs təˈpɑlədʒi / (AmE), / bʌs təˈpɒlədʒi / (BrE)
A network layout in which all devices are connected to a single central cable, called a bus or busbar.
network design with a single central cable

Examples

Our old office network used a bus topology, which made troubleshooting cable faults relatively straightforward.
A bus topology is easy and inexpensive to set up, but a failure in the main cable can bring down the whole network.
Modern networks rarely use bus topology because it is less reliable and scalable than newer designs.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: star topology - network design in which devices connect to a central hub
  • Antonym: ring topology - network design in which each device connects to two others, forming a ring
  • Antonym: mesh topology - network where devices are interconnected directly

Etymology

bus refers to the central cable in a network, from Latin 'omnibus' (for all), and topology from Greek 'topos' (place) + 'logia' (study).
