Karelian Isthmus

1. PROPN

/ kəˈriːliən ˈɪsθməs / (AmE), / kəˈrɪəliən ˈɪsθməs / (BrE)
a narrow strip of land between the Gulf of Finland and Lake Ladoga, connecting Finland and Russia
isthmus between Gulf of Finland and Lake Ladoga

Examples

The Karelian Isthmus played a significant role in several battles during World War II.
Many people visit the Karelian Isthmus for its beautiful lakes and forests.
Our geography class studied the unique position of the Karelian Isthmus between Finland and Russia.

Etymology

From Karelia, the name of the region, combined with 'isthmus'.
