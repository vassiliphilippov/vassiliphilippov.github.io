water supply

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔtɚ səˌplaɪ / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə səˌplaɪ / (BrE)
a system or facility that provides water, especially to a community, building, or region
system providing water

Examples

The old hospital relies on an outdated water supply system.
After the drought, the city's water supply was severely depleted.
Routine testing ensures that the water supply remains safe for residents.

Synset Examples

the town debated the purification of the water supply
first you have to cut off the water

Related Terms

  • Synonym: waterworks - a facility for treating and supplying water

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɔtər səˌplaɪ / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə səˌplaɪ / (BrE)
a system or source of water provided for a community or building for drinking, washing, etc.
source or system providing water to a community

Examples

The city upgraded its water supply to meet the growing population's needs.
A burst pipe disrupted the water supply for several hours.
Access to a clean water supply is essential for good health.

Synset Examples

Don't let the chemicals contaminate the water supply.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: water source - a place from which water originates, such as a river or reservoir
