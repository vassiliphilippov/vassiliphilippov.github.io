bathyal zone

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæθ.i.əl zoʊn / (AmE), / ˈbæθ.i.əl zəʊn / (BrE)
the part of the ocean between the continental shelf and the abyssal zone, typically ranging from 200 to 2000 meters in depth
deep ocean region below the continental shelf

Examples

Many unique species inhabit the bathyal zone due to its specific environmental conditions.
Research submarines have begun to explore the mysteries of the bathyal zone for new life forms.
The transition from the continental shelf to the bathyal zone is marked by a steep drop in ocean depth.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: abyssal zone - region of the deep ocean below the bathyal zone
  • Degree Lower: continental shelf - the extended perimeter of each continent, which is submerged under relatively shallow seas

Etymology

From Greek bathys (deep) + -al (relating to) + zone (belt, region).
