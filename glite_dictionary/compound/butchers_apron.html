butcher's apron

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʊtʃərz ˈeɪprən / (AmE), / ˈbʊtʃəz ˈeɪprən / (BrE)
a protective garment, typically made of heavy cloth or plastic, worn by butchers to protect their clothing while working
protective garment worn by butchers

Examples

He slipped on his butcher's apron before preparing the day's cuts of meat.
The old butcher's apron was splattered with the signs of a busy morning.
After work, she folded her butcher's apron and placed it in her locker.

Synset Examples

The chef's butcher's apron was covered in spices and herbs.
She hung her butcher's apron by the door after a long day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apron - a protective garment worn over the front of one's clothes
