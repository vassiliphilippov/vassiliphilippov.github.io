American Sign Language

1. NOUN

/ əˈmɛrɪkən saɪn ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / əˈmɛrɪkən saɪn ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the primary sign language used by deaf and hard-of-hearing people in the United States and parts of Canada
the main sign language in the United States

Examples

Many universities now offer courses in American Sign Language as part of their language programs.
She communicates with her deaf sister using American Sign Language.
Interpreters fluent in American Sign Language are in high demand at public events.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sign language - a language that uses manual communication instead of sound
