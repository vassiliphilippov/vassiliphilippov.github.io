ice needle

1. NOUN

/ aɪs ˈniːdəl / (AmE), / aɪs ˈniːdl̩ / (BrE)
A slender, elongated crystal of ice, typically formed in the atmosphere or on surfaces under certain conditions.
slender crystal of ice

Examples

The frost on the window was made up of countless ice needles sparkling in the morning sun.
Scientists observed ice needles forming on the cold metal surface during the experiment.
When the temperature dropped suddenly, ice needles began to grow from the damp ground.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ice crystal - a tiny crystal of ice found in clouds or as frost
