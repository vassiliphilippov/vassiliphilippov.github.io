sweetheart deal

1. NOUN

/ ˈswiːthɑrt ˌdiːl / (AmE), / ˈswiːthɑːt ˌdiːl / (BrE)
an agreement that is very favorable to one party, often due to favoritism or secret arrangements
an unusually favorable deal (often from favoritism)

Examples

The company secured a sweetheart deal with local officials, sparking public outrage.
Some critics claim the tax incentives were just a sweetheart deal for big corporations.
After months of negotiations, they walked away with a real sweetheart deal.

Synset Examples

The senator's sweetheart deal raised eyebrows.
Many criticized the company for its sweetheart deal with the government.
They got a sweetheart deal on that property.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kickback - a payment made to someone for facilitating a transaction, often illicitly
  • Synonym: favoritism - the practice of giving unfair preferential treatment
