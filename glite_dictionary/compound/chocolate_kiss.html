chocolate kiss

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɑklət kɪs / (AmE), / ˈtʃɒklət kɪs / (BrE)
a bite-sized, conical piece of chocolate, typically individually wrapped, often known as a Hershey's Kiss.
conical bite-sized chocolate candy

Examples

She handed me a chocolate kiss as a sweet treat after lunch.
I bought a bag of chocolate kisses to share with my friends.
Each chocolate kiss was wrapped in shiny foil, making it look extra special.

Etymology

From 'chocolate' + 'kiss'; popularized by the Hershey's Kiss, a chocolate candy introduced in 1907.
