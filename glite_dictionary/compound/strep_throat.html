strep throat

1. NOUN

/ stɹɛp θɹoʊt / (AmE), / stɹɛp θɹəʊt / (BrE)
A sore throat caused by infection with streptococcus bacteria, often accompanied by fever and swollen tonsils.
sore throat caused by strep infection
medical

Examples

My doctor said I have strep throat and prescribed antibiotics.
Children are especially likely to get strep throat during the winter months.
He stayed home from school because his strep throat was very painful.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sore throat - pain or irritation in the throat

Etymology

Shortened from 'streptococcal' (from Streptococcus) plus 'throat'.
