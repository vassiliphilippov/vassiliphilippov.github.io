ancestor worship

1. NOUN

/ ˈæn.sɛs.tɚ ˌwɜːr.ʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈæn.ses.tə ˌwɜː.ʃɪp / (BrE)
the religious practice of honoring and praying to one's ancestors, often believed to influence the fortune and fate of their descendants
religious practice of honoring ancestors

Examples

Many Asian cultures practice ancestor worship as part of their traditional beliefs.
Archaeologists found evidence of ancestor worship in ancient burial sites.
He learned about ancestor worship during his studies of world religions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ancestor veneration - reverence or respect for ancestors without necessarily involving worship

2. NOUN

/ ˈæn.sɛs.tɚ ˈwɜr.ʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈæn.ses.tə ˈwɜː.ʃɪp / (BrE)
the practice of showing respect or reverence to deceased ancestors, often involving rituals or offerings, based on the belief that their spirits can influence the living
ritual veneration of deceased ancestors

Examples

Many East Asian cultures have traditions of ancestor worship that date back thousands of years.
He researched the role of ancestor worship in shaping family and community values.
Ancestor worship ceremonies often involve offering food and incense at family altars.

Synset Examples

Most of the historically known practices of ancestor worship in Japan are adaptations of Chinese customs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ancestor veneration - the act of honoring ancestors without religious worship
