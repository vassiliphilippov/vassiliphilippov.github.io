circle of Willis

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɝː.kəl əv ˈwɪl.ɪs / (AmE), / ˈsɜː.kəl əv ˈwɪl.ɪs / (BrE)
a circular arrangement of arteries located at the base of the brain that supplies blood to the brain and surrounding structures
arterial ring at the base of the brain
medical

Examples

The circle of Willis is crucial for maintaining blood flow to the brain if one artery becomes blocked.
Damage or variation in the circle of Willis can increase the risk of stroke.
Medical imaging was used to examine the patient's circle of Willis for any abnormalities.

Etymology

Named after English physician Thomas Willis (1621–1675), who described the structure.
