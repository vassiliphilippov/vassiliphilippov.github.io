polar hare

1. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊlər hɛr / (AmE), / ˈpəʊlə hɛə / (BrE)
a large species of hare native to northern North America, turning nearly completely white in winter
large North American hare with white winter fur

Examples

The polar hare has adapted to survive in the freezing climate of the Arctic tundra.
In winter, the polar hare becomes almost invisible against the snow due to its white fur.
Researchers spotted several polar hares feeding on shrubs near their campsite.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Arctic hare - another name for the polar hare
