diagnostic assay

1. NOUN

/ daɪəɡˈnɑstɪk ˈæseɪ / (AmE), / daɪəɡˈnɒstɪk ˈæseɪ / (BrE)
A scientific test or procedure performed to identify or confirm a medical condition or disease.
test for diagnosing disease
medical

Examples

The laboratory used a diagnostic assay to confirm the presence of the virus.
Recent advances in diagnostic assays have improved early detection of cancer.
The accuracy of a diagnostic assay is essential for the correct treatment of patients.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assay - a test or analysis, especially for the presence or amount of a substance

Etymology

From Ancient Greek 'diagnōstikos' (able to distinguish) and French 'essai' (trial, test).
