Rhode Island bent

1. NOUN

/ ˌroʊd ˈaɪlənd bɛnt / (AmE), / ˌrəʊd ˈaɪlənd bɛnt / (BrE)
A type of common grass with slender stems and narrow leaves, used in lawns and golf courses, especially in the northeastern United States.
type of slender-stemmed grass

Examples

The golf course was renowned for its lush Rhode Island bent, which provided a smooth putting surface.
Gardeners often choose Rhode Island bent for its resilience and fine texture in lawns.
A patch of Rhode Island bent grew wild near the edge of the old field.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bentgrass - any grass of the genus Agrostis, especially those used for lawns and turf
