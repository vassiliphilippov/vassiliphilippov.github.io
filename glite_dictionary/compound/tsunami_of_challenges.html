tsunami of challenges

1. NOUN

/ tsuˈnɑmi ʌv ˈtʃælɪndʒɪz / (AmE), / tsuˈnɑːmi ɒv ˈtʃælɪndʒɪz / (BrE)
a large and overwhelming number of difficulties arising at once
overwhelming amount of difficulties

Examples

After the merger, the company was hit by a tsunami of challenges that threatened its survival.
She managed to stay positive despite facing a tsunami of challenges during her studies.
When the project began, no one expected the tsunami of challenges that would soon follow.

Synset Examples

He faced a tsunami of challenges at work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flood of problems - a sudden large number of difficulties
  • Synonym: wave of obstacles - a sudden increase in difficulties or barriers

Etymology

Tsunami is from Japanese, meaning 'harbor wave', here used figuratively to describe a sudden, overwhelming force.
