-sounding

1. ADJ

/ ˈsaʊndɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsaʊndɪŋ / (BrE)
(in compounds) having a sound of the type specified; used after adjectives to form compounds describing the quality of something's sound.
having a specified type of sound (in compounds)

Examples

The violin produced a rich-sounding tone that captivated the audience.
She has a sweet-sounding voice that soothes everyone who hears it.
They were startled by the harsh-sounding alarm early in the morning.

Synset Examples

harsh-sounding

Related Terms

  • Synonym: -looking - having a specified appearance (in compounds)

2. ADJ

/ ˈsaʊndɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsaʊndɪŋ / (BrE)
(in combination) appearing to have the qualities or characteristics described by the preceding word, especially in terms of sound or impression given.
appearing as described (in compounds)

Examples

His speech was full of fine-sounding promises but lacked substance.
They were impressed by his high-sounding titles, unaware of their actual meaning.
The proposal may be good-sounding but is not feasible in practice.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: -looking - (in compounds) appearing as described visually

Etymology

From the verb 'sound' (to seem, to give a certain impression) plus the present participle -ing.
