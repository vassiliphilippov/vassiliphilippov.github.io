bull pine

1. NOUN

/ bʊl paɪn / (AmE), / bʊl paɪn / (BrE)
a tall pine tree (Pinus ponderosa) of western North America, having dark green needles clustered in twos to fives and thick, dark brown bark when mature
a tall pine tree of western North America

Examples

The bull pine is a prominent feature of forests in the American West.
We hiked through a grove filled with ancient bull pine towering above us.
The dark, thick bark of the mature bull pine helps protect it from wildfires.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ponderosa pine - another common name for the bull pine (Pinus ponderosa)
