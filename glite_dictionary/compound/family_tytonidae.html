family Tytonidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli taɪˈtɑnɪˌdi / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli tɪˈtəʊnɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of birds comprising only the barn owls.
family of barn owls

Examples

All members of the family Tytonidae are known for their distinctive heart-shaped facial discs.
The family Tytonidae is separate from other owl families due to unique anatomical features.
Research on the family Tytonidae has revealed much about the evolution of nocturnal birds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barn owl - a medium-sized, pale-coloured owl found worldwide, belonging to this family

Etymology

From New Latin Tytonidae, from genus Tyto (barn owl) + -idae (family suffix).
