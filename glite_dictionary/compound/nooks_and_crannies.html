nooks and crannies

1. NOUN

/ nʊks ənd ˈkræniz / (AmE), / nʊks ənd ˈkræniz / (BrE)
very small, hidden, or remote parts or places, especially those that are difficult to find or reach
hidden or remote places; corners

Examples

She searched every nook and cranny of the attic for her old diary.
The explorers investigated the nooks and crannies of the ancient ruins.
He loves cleaning and makes sure to reach all the nooks and crannies in the house.

Synset Examples

he explored every nook and cranny of science

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corner - an interior angle formed by two meeting walls
