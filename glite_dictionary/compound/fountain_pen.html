fountain pen

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaʊntn̩ pɛn / (AmE), / ˈfaʊntən pɛn / (BrE)
a pen with an internal reservoir for ink that flows to the nib for writing
pen with internal ink reservoir

Examples

She signed the contract with her favorite fountain pen.
The collector displayed a rare vintage fountain pen from the early 1900s.
Using a fountain pen gives handwriting a smooth and elegant appearance.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ballpoint pen - a pen that dispenses ink over a metal ball at its point
  • Antonym: dip pen - a pen that must be dipped into ink for writing

Etymology

From 'fountain' (referring to the reservoir) + 'pen', first used in 19th-century English.
