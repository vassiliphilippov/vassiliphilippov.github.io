people of colour

1. NOUN

/ ˈpipəl əv ˈkʌlɚ / (AmE), / ˈpiːpl əv ˈkʌlə / (BrE)
persons belonging to racial groups with skin pigmentation different from that of white people, especially Black, Asian, and other non-white groups
non-white racial or ethnic groups

Examples

The organization is committed to increasing representation for people of colour in its leadership roles.
Many people of colour face systemic barriers in education and employment.
Events highlighting the experiences of people of colour are held throughout the year.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: white - persons of the racial group with light skin pigmentation
  • Synonym: person of colour - an individual belonging to a non-white racial or ethnic group

Etymology

From colour (as a reference to skin tone), used in social and political contexts since the late 18th century; alternative spelling 'people of color' used especially in American English.
