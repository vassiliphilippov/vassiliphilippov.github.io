methylene blue

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛθəˌlin blu / (AmE), / ˈmiːθəliːn bluː / (BrE)
a dark green crystalline dye used as a stain in microscopy, an antiseptic, a chemical indicator, and an antidote for cyanide poisoning
chemical dye and medical antidote
medical

Examples

The doctor administered methylene blue as an antidote for cyanide poisoning.
In the laboratory, methylene blue is often used to stain cells for microscopic examination.
The solution turned blue after adding methylene blue as a chemical indicator.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dye - a substance used to color materials
  • Synonym: stain - a substance used in microscopy to color specimens

Etymology

From 'methylene,' referring to the chemical group, and 'blue' for its color.
