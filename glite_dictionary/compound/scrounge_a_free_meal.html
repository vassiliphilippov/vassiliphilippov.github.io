to scrounge a free meal

1. VERB

/ skraʊndʒ ə friː miːl / (AmE), / skraʊndʒ ə friː miːl / (BrE)
to obtain a meal without paying for it, typically by seeking opportunities or relying on others' generosity or lack of attention
get a meal for free by opportunism

Examples

Whenever Tom visits his friends, he tries to scrounge a free meal by dropping hints about being hungry.
She often managed to scrounge a free meal at work by showing up near lunchtime.
At college, it was common to scrounge a free meal by attending club meetings with free food.

Synset Examples

He always tries to scrounge a free meal at parties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to beg - to ask for food or money as a favor or charity
  • Synonym: mooch - to get something, especially food or money, without paying for it

Etymology

From 'scrounge' (to seek or obtain by irregular means), British slang origin, possibly related to dialectal 'scrunge' (to steal).
