Tennessee Walker

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɛnəsi ˈwɑkər / (AmE), / ˌtɛnəsi ˈwɔːkə / (BrE)
A breed of horse known for its stamina and a distinctive, fast running walk, often used in riding and shows.
breed of horse with running walk gait

Examples

Her new Tennessee Walker impressed everyone at the riding competition with its smooth gaits.
The Tennessee Walker is especially famous for its comfortable running walk, making it a favorite among long-distance riders.
He learned to ride on a gentle Tennessee Walker at his cousin’s farm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: American Saddlebred - a breed of horse developed in the United States, known for its smooth gaits

Etymology

Named after the U.S. state of Tennessee, where the breed was developed, and its characteristic gait.
