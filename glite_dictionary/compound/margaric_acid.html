margaric acid

1. NOUN

/ mɑrˈɡærɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / mɑːˈɡarɪk ˈasɪd / (BrE)
a colorless, crystalline synthetic fatty acid used in laboratory and industrial processes
a synthetic crystalline fatty acid

Examples

The laboratory synthesized margaric acid as part of its research into fatty acids.
Pure margaric acid appears as colorless crystals under the microscope.
Industrial applications sometimes require the use of margaric acid in chemical processes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heptadecanoic acid - the systematic chemical name for margaric acid

Etymology

From French *margarique*, from Greek *margaron* (pearl), referring to its pearly appearance.
