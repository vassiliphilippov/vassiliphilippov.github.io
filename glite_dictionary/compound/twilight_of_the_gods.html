Twilight of the Gods

1. NOUN

/ ˈtwaɪˌlaɪt ʌv ðə ɡɑdz / (AmE), / ˈtwaɪlaɪt əv ðə ɡɒdz / (BrE)
in Norse mythology, the myth about the ultimate destruction of the gods in a final battle with evil, also known as Ragnarök
end-of-world myth in Norse mythology

Examples

The ancient legend of the Twilight of the Gods has inspired countless works of art and literature.
Many scholars have compared the story of the Twilight of the Gods to other apocalyptic myths.
Wagner's famous opera, Twilight of the Gods, dramatizes the downfall of the Norse deities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ragnarök - the prophesied end of the world in Norse mythology

Etymology

From English 'twilight' (dusk) and 'of the gods'; a common translation of German 'Götterdämmerung', the title of Wagner's opera based on Norse myth.
