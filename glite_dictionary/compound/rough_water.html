rough water

1. NOUN

/ rʌf ˈwɑtər / (AmE), / rʌf ˈwɔːtə / (BrE)
Water, especially at sea, that is agitated, choppy, or difficult to navigate, typically due to wind or stormy weather.
agitated or choppy water (esp. at sea)

Examples

The ferry was canceled because of rough water in the bay.
Sailors must be experienced to navigate rough water safely.
Strong winds whipped the sea into rough water overnight.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: calm water - water that is smooth, not disturbed by wind or waves
