Torrey tree

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɔːri triː / (AmE), / ˈtɒri triː / (BrE)
a rare small evergreen tree native to northern Florida, known for its glossy green leaves that emit a fetid smell when crushed
rare evergreen tree of northern Florida

Examples

The Torrey tree is found only in a few locations in northern Florida.
The leaves of the Torrey tree release an unpleasant odor when crushed.
Botanists are working to conserve the endangered Torrey tree population.

Etymology

Named after American botanist John Torrey (1796–1873).
