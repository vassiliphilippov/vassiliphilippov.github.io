retrograde amnesia

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛtroʊˌɡreɪd æmˈniːʒə / (AmE), / ˈrɛtrəʊˌɡreɪd amˈniːzɪə / (BrE)
A loss of memory for events that occurred immediately before a trauma or injury.
loss of memory before trauma
medical

Examples

After the accident, she suffered from retrograde amnesia and could not recall the moments before the crash.
The patient’s retrograde amnesia made it difficult for doctors to determine how the injury occurred.
Films sometimes depict retrograde amnesia when a character cannot remember events leading up to a significant incident.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: anterograde amnesia - loss of memory for events after a trauma

Etymology

From Latin retrogradus (moving backward) + Greek amnesia (forgetfulness).
