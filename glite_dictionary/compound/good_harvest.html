good harvest

1. NOUN

/ ɡʊd ˈhɑrvəst / (AmE), / ɡʊd ˈhɑːvɪst / (BrE)
a harvest in which crops are plentiful and yields are high
a plentiful crop yield

Examples

After months of hard work, the farmers finally enjoyed a good harvest this year.
Rain at the right time can make the difference between a poor and a good harvest.
The festival was held to celebrate the good harvest that fed the entire village.

Synset Examples

Farmers need to sow early to ensure a good harvest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bumper crop - an unusually large harvest
  • Antonym: poor harvest - a harvest with low yields
