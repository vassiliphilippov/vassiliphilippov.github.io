Fuji cherry

1. NOUN

/ ˈfuːdʒi ˈtʃɛri / (AmE), / ˈfuːdʒi ˈtʃɛri / (BrE)
a shrubby Japanese cherry tree with pale pink blossoms, grown as an ornamental plant
a Japanese cherry tree with pale pink blossoms

Examples

The Fuji cherry is admired for its delicate, pale pink flowers in early spring.
Gardeners often plant the Fuji cherry to brighten up their yards with seasonal color.
During the festival, the Fuji cherry trees attract many visitors who wish to see them in full bloom.

Etymology

From Japanese 'Fuji,' referring to the region or Mount Fuji, combined with 'cherry.'
