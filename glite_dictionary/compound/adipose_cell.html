adipose cell

1. NOUN

/ ˈædɪˌpoʊs sɛl / (AmE), / ˈadɪpəʊs sɛl / (BrE)
a cell specialized for the storage of fat, found in connective tissue
cell that stores fat
medical

Examples

The body stores surplus energy in adipose cells beneath the skin.
Researchers studied how adipose cells react to different diets.
Excess calories can cause adipose cells to increase in size, leading to weight gain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fat cell - cell specialized in storing fat

Etymology

From Latin 'adeps' (fat) + 'cellula' (small room, cell).
