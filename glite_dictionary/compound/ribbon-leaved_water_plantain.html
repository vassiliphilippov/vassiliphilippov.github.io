ribbon-leaved water plantain

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɪb.ən liːvd ˈwɔ.tɚ ˌplæn.tɪn / (AmE), / ˈrɪb.ən liːvd ˈwɔː.tə ˌplæn.tɪn / (BrE)
A species of aquatic plant (Alisma gramineum) with narrow, ribbon-like leaves, commonly found in shallow water habitats.
aquatic plant with narrow leaves

Examples

The ribbon-leaved water plantain thrives in shallow ponds and slow-moving streams.
Botanists identified the aquatic species as ribbon-leaved water plantain during their field survey.
You can spot ribbon-leaved water plantain by its long, slender leaves floating on the water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: water plantain - Aquatic plants of the genus Alisma
