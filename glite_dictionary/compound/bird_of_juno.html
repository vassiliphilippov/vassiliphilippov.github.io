bird of Juno

1. NOUN

/ bɝd əv ˈdʒuːnoʊ / (AmE), / bɜːd əv ˈdʒuːnəʊ / (BrE)
a large southeast Asian pheasant, often raised for its ornamental appearance
large ornamental southeast Asian pheasant

Examples

The bird of Juno is prized by collectors for its vivid plumage and grand tail feathers.
Many aviaries display the bird of Juno as their centerpiece attraction.
Farmers in Southeast Asia sometimes keep the bird of Juno for both beauty and cultural significance.

Etymology

Named for the Roman goddess Juno, in reference to the bird's beauty.
