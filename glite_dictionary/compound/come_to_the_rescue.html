to come to the rescue

1. VERB

/ kʌm tə ðə rɪˈskju / (AmE), / kʌm tə ðə rɪˈskjuː / (BrE)
to arrive or intervene to help someone who is in trouble or needs assistance
intervene to help someone in trouble

Examples

The firefighters came to the rescue when the building caught fire.
Just when we thought we were lost, a local man came to the rescue.
Whenever someone needs help, she never hesitates to come to the rescue.

Synset Examples

The lifeguard quickly came to her rescue at the beach.
He knew he had to come to her rescue that night.
He always comes to the rescue like the Lone Ranger.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: save - to help someone out of danger or trouble
  • Antonym: abandon - to leave someone in trouble without help
