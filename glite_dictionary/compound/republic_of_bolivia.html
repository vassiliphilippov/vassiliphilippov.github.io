Republic of Bolivia

1. PROPN

/ rɪˈpʌblɪk əv bəˈlɪviə / (AmE), / rɪˈpʌblɪk əv bəˈlɪviə / (BrE)
A landlocked country in central South America, established in 1825 after gaining independence from Spain.
country in central South America

Examples

The Republic of Bolivia is bordered by Brazil to the north and east.
After the revolution, the Republic of Bolivia adopted a new constitution.
Climate varies widely across the Republic of Bolivia, from tropical lowlands to Andean highlands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Bolivia - The short form of the country's name

Etymology

Named after Simón Bolívar, a South American independence leader.
