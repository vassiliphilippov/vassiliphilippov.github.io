Word of God

1. NOUN

/ ˌwɝːd əv ɡɑd / (AmE), / ˌwɜːd əv ɡɒd / (BrE)
the message or teachings of the Christian gospel, regarded as divinely inspired or authoritative
Christian Gospel message; divine teachings

Examples

Christians believe the Word of God offers guidance for leading a moral life.
The preacher delivered a sermon based on the Word of God.
Many people find comfort and strength in reading the Word of God.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Gospel - the teaching or revelation of Christ

2. NOUN

/ wɝd əv ɡɑd / (AmE), / wɜːd əv ɡɒd / (BrE)
a manifestation or communication of the mind and will of God, often regarded as sacred or authoritative in religious traditions
divine revelation or message

Examples

Many believers study the Word of God daily to seek guidance and inspiration.
He regarded the ancient text as the true Word of God and followed its teachings strictly.
For centuries, people have debated the interpretation of the Word of God.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: revelation - the disclosure of divine or sacred truth, especially through supernatural means
  • Synonym: Scripture - the sacred writings of Christianity contained in the Bible
