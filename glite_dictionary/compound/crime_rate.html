crime rate

1. NOUN

/ ˈkraɪm ˌreɪt / (AmE), / ˈkraɪm ˌreɪt / (BrE)
the number of crimes committed in a particular area, expressed as a proportion of the population, usually per 1,000 people per year
ratio of crimes to population in an area

Examples

The city has seen a sharp drop in its crime rate over the past decade.
Police officials are concerned about the rising crime rate in rural communities.
A low crime rate is often cited as a benefit of living in this area.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incidence - the frequency or rate at which something happens, especially something undesirable
