ship biscuit

1. NOUN

/ ʃɪp ˈbɪskɪt / (AmE), / ʃɪp ˈbɪskɪt / (BrE)
A very hard, unsalted biscuit or bread once commonly used as a staple food on ships.
hard, unsalted bread for ships

Examples

During the long voyage, sailors survived mainly on ship biscuit and salted meat.
The ship biscuit was so hard it had to be soaked in water before eating.
Historians found fragments of ship biscuit in shipwrecks dating back to the 18th century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hardtack - very hard, dry biscuit or bread; long-lasting ship's food
