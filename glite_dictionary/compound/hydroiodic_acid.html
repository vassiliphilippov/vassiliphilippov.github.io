hydroiodic acid

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪdroʊ.aɪˈɑːdɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / ˌhaɪdrəʊ.aɪˈɒdɪk ˈæsɪd / (BrE)
A strong acid formed by dissolving hydrogen iodide in water.
a strong acid (hydrogen iodide solution)
medical

Examples

Concentrated hydroiodic acid must be stored carefully because it can react with air and light.
The reduction of some organic compounds can be achieved using hydroiodic acid as a reagent.
Exposure to hydroiodic acid vapors may cause irritation to the respiratory system.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hydrochloric acid - a strong acid formed by hydrogen chloride in water

Etymology

From 'hydro-' (referring to hydrogen) + 'iodic' (from iodine).
