Teton Dakota

1. NOUN

/ ˈtiːtɑn dəˈkoʊtə / (AmE), / ˈtiːtɒn dəˈkəʊtə / (BrE)
a member of the large western branch of the Sioux people, consisting of several groups that traditionally lived on the Great Plains of North America
western branch of the Sioux people

Examples

Many Teton Dakota settled in the Dakotas after the westward expansion of the United States.
The culture of the Teton Dakota included bison hunting and living in tipis on the plains.
He learned about the history and traditions of the Teton Dakota during his anthropology research.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sioux - a group of Native American peoples and their language family

Etymology

From Teton, the name of the group, and Dakota, a term used for one of the largest divisions of the Sioux family.
