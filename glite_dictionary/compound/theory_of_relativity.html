theory of relativity

1. NOUN

/ ˈθɪəri əv rɪˈlætɪvəti / (AmE), / ˈθɪəri əv rɪˈlatɪvɪti / (BrE)
The scientific theory proposed by Albert Einstein that space and time are relative and not absolute, especially as described in the special and general theories of relativity.
Einstein's theory that space and time are relative

Examples

Einstein's theory of relativity revolutionized the way scientists understand the universe.
Many modern technologies, such as GPS, rely on corrections predicted by the theory of relativity.
The theory of relativity explains how space and time are interconnected.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: special relativity - The part of relativity dealing with uniform motion, proposed by Einstein in 1905
  • Degree Higher: general relativity - Einstein's theory including gravitation and acceleration

Etymology

From theory (from Greek theōria) + relativity, popularized after Einstein's work in the early 20th century.
