law degree

1. NOUN

/ lɔ dɪˈɡri / (AmE), / lɔː dɪˈɡriː / (BrE)
an academic degree awarded to someone who has completed studies at a law school
academic qualification in law

Examples

After years of study, she finally received her law degree from Harvard.
Many politicians started their careers by earning a law degree.
He decided to pursue a law degree after working as a paralegal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Juris Doctor - basic professional law degree in the US
  • Synonym: Bachelor of Laws - undergraduate law degree in some countries
