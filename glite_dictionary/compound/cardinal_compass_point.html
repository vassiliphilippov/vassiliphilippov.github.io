cardinal compass point

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑrdɪnəl ˈkʌmpəs pɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˈkɑːdɪnəl ˈkʌmpəs pɔɪnt / (BrE)
one of the four main compass directions: north, east, south, or west.
one of the main compass directions

Examples

The ship changed course to head directly toward the cardinal compass point of north.
Each cardinal compass point is represented by a large letter on the map.
Explorers relied on the cardinal compass points to determine which direction to travel.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: intercardinal compass point - one of the four directions halfway between cardinal points (northeast, southeast, etc.)

Etymology

From Latin cardinalis (principal, chief) and Old French compas (circle, compass).
