saprophytic organism

1. NOUN

/ ˌsæprəˈfɪtɪk ˈɔːrɡənɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌsæprəˈfɪtɪk ˈɔːɡənɪzəm / (BrE)
an organism, such as a fungus or bacterium, that obtains its nutrients from decaying organic matter
organism feeding on decaying matter
medical

Examples

A saprophytic organism plays an essential role in breaking down dead leaves in the forest.
Many mushrooms are classified as a saprophytic organism because they feed on decaying wood.
After an animal dies, a saprophytic organism helps decompose its remains.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decomposer - an organism that breaks down dead or decaying matter

Etymology

From Greek sapros (rotten) + phyton (plant) + organism.
