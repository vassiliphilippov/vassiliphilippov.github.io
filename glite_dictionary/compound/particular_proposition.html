particular proposition

1. NOUN

/ pərˈtɪkjələr ˌprɑːpəˈzɪʃən / (AmE), / pəˈtɪkjʊlə ˌprɒpəˈzɪʃən / (BrE)
(Logic) A proposition that asserts something about some, but not all, members of a class.
logic: claim about some members of a class

Examples

The statement 'Some birds can fly' is a particular proposition because it refers to only some birds.
Philosophers often distinguish between a particular proposition and a universal proposition.
In syllogistic logic, a particular proposition makes a claim that does not apply to every member of a group.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: universal proposition - a proposition that asserts something about all members of a class
