zero-sum game

1. NOUN

/ ˌziːroʊ ˈsʌm ɡeɪm / (AmE), / ˌzɪərəʊ ˈsʌm ɡeɪm / (BrE)
a situation or competition in which the total gain and loss among all participants is equal, so that one person's gain is exactly balanced by another's loss
situation where one's gain equals another's loss

Examples

Negotiating a salary can often feel like a zero-sum game, but sometimes creative solutions benefit everyone.
In chess, the outcome is a classic example of a zero-sum game, where one player's win is the other's loss.
They treated the project as a zero-sum game, competing for credit instead of working together.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: win-win - a situation where all parties benefit
  • Antonym: non-zero-sum game - a scenario where participants' gains and losses are not necessarily balanced

Etymology

From 'zero-sum', a term used in game theory, where the total amount to be gained or lost is zero.
