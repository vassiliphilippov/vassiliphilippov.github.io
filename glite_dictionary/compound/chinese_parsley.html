Chinese parsley

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃaɪniz ˈpɑr.sli / (AmE), / ˈtʃaɪniːz ˈpɑːs.li / (BrE)
an aromatic herb, also known as coriander, with edible leaves and seeds that resemble those of parsley
coriander; aromatic herb used in cooking

Examples

She sprinkled chopped Chinese parsley over the soup for extra flavor.
Many Asian dishes use Chinese parsley as a fresh garnish.
The recipe calls for a handful of Chinese parsley leaves, also known as coriander.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coriander - an aromatic herb with edible leaves and seeds
  • Synonym: cilantro - the leaves of the coriander plant, used as an herb

2. NOUN

/ ˈtʃaɪˌniz ˈpɑr.sli / (AmE), / ˈtʃaɪniːz ˈpɑː.sli / (BrE)
another name for cilantro; the leaves of the coriander plant, used as a seasoning or garnish
cilantro; leaves of the coriander plant

Examples

Fresh Chinese parsley is often sprinkled on soups and curries in Asian cuisine.
She chopped some Chinese parsley and added it to the salsa for extra flavor.
The market was out of Chinese parsley, so I used regular parsley instead.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cilantro - the leaves of the coriander plant, used in cooking
  • Synonym: coriander - the plant whose leaves and seeds are used as culinary herbs and spices
