branchiopod crustacean

1. NOUN

/ ˈbræn.ki.oʊ.pɑd krʌˈsteɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˈbræŋ.kɪ.ə.pɒd krʌˈsteɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
an aquatic crustacean typically having a carapace and many pairs of leaflike appendages used for swimming, respiration, and feeding
type of aquatic crustacean with leaflike appendages

Examples

The fairy shrimp is a well-known branchiopod crustacean found in temporary ponds.
Scientists study branchiopod crustaceans to understand adaptations to freshwater habitats.
The carapace and leaflike appendages distinguish a branchiopod crustacean from other aquatic species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crustacean - aquatic arthropod group including crabs, lobsters, and shrimp

Etymology

From Greek 'branchia' (gills) + 'pous, podos' (foot), referring to their gill-footed appendages.
