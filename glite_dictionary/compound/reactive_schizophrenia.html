reactive schizophrenia

1. NOUN

/ riˈæktɪv skɪtsəˈfriːniə / (AmE), / riˈæktɪv skɪtsəˈfriːniə / (BrE)
a type of schizophrenia that begins suddenly, often in response to stressful events, and typically lasts for a relatively short time
sudden-onset, brief schizophrenia
medical

Examples

Doctors diagnosed her with reactive schizophrenia after a period of intense and sudden stress.
Unlike chronic cases, reactive schizophrenia may resolve within a few weeks or months.
He received treatment for reactive schizophrenia following a traumatic life event.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: chronic schizophrenia - a long-lasting, slowly developing form of schizophrenia

Etymology

From 'reactive' (responding to stimuli) + 'schizophrenia' (Greek 'schizein', to split, and 'phren', mind).
