First Battle of Bull Run

1. PROPN

/ ˌfɝst ˈbætəl əv bʊl ˈrʌn / (AmE), / ˌfɜːst ˈbætl əv bʊl ˈrʌn / (BrE)
the first major battle of the American Civil War, fought in July 1861 near Manassas, Virginia, resulting in a Confederate victory
the first major Civil War battle (1861)

Examples

Many historians consider the First Battle of Bull Run as a turning point in early Civil War morale.
Tourists often visit the site where the First Battle of Bull Run took place in 1861.
The aftermath of the First Battle of Bull Run shocked both Northern and Southern citizens.
