malicious mischief

1. NOUN

/ məˈlɪʃəs ˈmɪstʃɪf / (AmE), / məˈlɪʃəs ˈmɪstʃɪf / (BrE)
the willful and deliberate destruction of another person's property, especially as a criminal offense
willful destruction of property (legal term)
legal

Examples

The teenager was charged with malicious mischief after vandalizing the school.
Local law enforcement is investigating several cases of malicious mischief in the neighborhood.
He was held responsible for malicious mischief after damaging his ex-employer’s vehicle.
