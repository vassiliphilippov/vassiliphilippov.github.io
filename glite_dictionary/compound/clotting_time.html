clotting time

1. NOUN

/ ˈklɑtɪŋ taɪm / (AmE), / ˈklɒtɪŋ taɪm / (BrE)
the period taken for a sample of blood to form a clot, used to assess blood's ability to coagulate
time for blood to clot (medical term)
medical

Examples

The laboratory measured his clotting time to check for possible bleeding disorders.
A prolonged clotting time can indicate a deficiency in certain coagulation factors.
Doctors may use clotting time as part of the evaluation for unexplained bruising.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bleeding time - the time taken for bleeding to stop after a standardized skin incision; related hemostatic test
