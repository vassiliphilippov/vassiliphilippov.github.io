order Tubulidentata

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdər tjuːˌbjuːlɪˈdɛntətə / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə tjuːˌbjuːlɪˈdentətə / (BrE)
A taxonomic order of placental mammals containing the aardvark as its only living species.
taxonomic order including the aardvark

Examples

The order Tubulidentata is notable for having only one living species, the aardvark.
Researchers study the order Tubulidentata to learn about the evolutionary history of placental mammals.
Fossil records indicate that the order Tubulidentata once included several extinct relatives of the aardvark.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aardvark - an African mammal with a tubular snout, the only living member of Tubulidentata

Etymology

From Latin 'tubulus' (small tube) + 'dentatus' (toothed), referring to the unique tubular teeth of members of this group.
