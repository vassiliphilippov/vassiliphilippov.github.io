spiny anteater

1. NOUN

/ ˈspaɪni ænˌtidər / (AmE), / ˈspaɪni ˈæntiːtə / (BrE)
a burrowing egg-laying mammal covered with spines, having a long snout and claws for eating ants and termites; native to Australia and New Guinea
burrowing spiny mammal of Australia/New Guinea

Examples

The spiny anteater uses its long tongue to catch ants deep inside logs.
Unlike a true anteater, the spiny anteater lays eggs and has spines for protection.
Many tourists hope to spot a spiny anteater while visiting the Australian outback.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: echidna - egg-laying spiny mammal native to Australia and New Guinea

2. NOUN

/ ˈspaɪni ænˌtiːtər / (AmE), / ˈspaɪni ˈænˌtiːtə / (BrE)
a burrowing mammal with spines, a long snout, and claws, known for eating ants and termites; commonly known as an echidna
a burrowing spiny mammal (echidna)

Examples

The spiny anteater is one of the only egg-laying mammals in the world.
While exploring the forest, we spotted a spiny anteater digging for ants.
Unlike true anteaters, the spiny anteater is covered with sharp spines for protection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: echidna - another name for the spiny anteater, a monotreme mammal
