mystery play

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪstəri pleɪ / (AmE), / ˈmɪstəri pleɪ / (BrE)
a type of medieval drama based on biblical stories, especially episodes from the life of Christ, performed in churches or public places
medieval biblical drama

Examples

The town annually re-enacts a mystery play to celebrate its medieval heritage.
Scholars study mystery plays to understand how religion and theater intersected in early Europe.
Performing a mystery play required elaborate costumes and coordination among local guilds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: morality play - an allegorical play in which characters represent moral qualities, popular in the Middle Ages

Etymology

From Middle English 'mysterie' (manual occupation, craft) referring to guilds that performed these plays.
