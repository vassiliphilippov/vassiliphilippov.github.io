American wisteria

1. NOUN

/ əˈmɛɹɪkən wɪˈstɪɹiə / (AmE), / əˈmɛɹɪkən wɪˈstɪəriə / (BrE)
A climbing vine native to the eastern United States, similar to Japanese wisteria, with pale purple-lilac flowers.
climbing vine with pale purple-lilac flowers

Examples

The American wisteria in our garden bloomed beautifully this spring.
Unlike its Asian relatives, American wisteria grows well in the southeastern United States.
Gardeners admire American wisteria for its delicate, pale purple flowers.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Japanese wisteria - climbing vine native to Japan with violet flowers
  • Synonym: wisteria - genus of climbing vines with cascades of bluish flowers
