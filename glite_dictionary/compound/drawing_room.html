drawing room

1. NOUN

/ ˈdrɔɪŋ ˌruːm / (AmE), / ˈdrɔː(r).ɪŋ ˌruːm / (BrE)
a private compartment on a sleeper rail car, typically containing three bunks and a toilet
private sleeper compartment on a train

Examples

The drawing room offered more privacy and comfort than the standard sleeper car.
She reserved a drawing room for her family's overnight journey across the country.
Each drawing room comes with its own toilet and sleeping berths, making long train trips much more convenient.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdrɔɪŋ ruːm / (AmE), / ˈdrɔːɪŋ ruːm / (BrE)
a formal room in a house where visitors are received and entertained
formal entertaining room in a house

Examples

Guests were shown into the drawing room for tea and conversation.
The manor's drawing room was elegantly decorated with antique furniture.
After dinner, the ladies retired to the drawing room while the men remained at the table.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: living room - a room in a house for general and informal everyday use
  • Synonym: parlor - a sitting room in a private house, especially in older usage

Etymology

From 'withdrawing room,' referring to a room to which ladies withdrew after dinner (17th century English).
