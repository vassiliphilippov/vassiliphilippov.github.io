touch perception

1. NOUN

/ tʌt͡ʃ pərˈsɛpʃən / (AmE), / tʌt͡ʃ pəˈsɛpʃən / (BrE)
the ability to sense pressure, heat, pain, or other stimuli through the skin
faculty of sensing via the skin
medical

Examples

The patient's touch perception was tested using a soft brush against her skin.
Damage to the nerves can result in a loss of touch perception in the affected area.
Children develop touch perception as their nervous systems mature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tactile perception - the perception of touch sensations
