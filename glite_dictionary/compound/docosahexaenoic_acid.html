docosahexaenoic acid

1. NOUN

/ doʊˌkoʊsəˌhɛksiˈənoʊ.ɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / dəʊˌkəʊsəˌhɛksɪˈiːnəʊɪk ˈasɪd / (BrE)
an omega-3 fatty acid with 22 carbon atoms, important for brain and eye development, found especially in fish such as tuna and bluefish
an omega-3 fatty acid found in fish
medical

Examples

Salmon and mackerel are rich sources of docosahexaenoic acid (DHA), crucial for brain health.
Many infant formulas are now fortified with docosahexaenoic acid to support early development.
Researchers are studying how docosahexaenoic acid may help reduce the risk of certain chronic diseases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: omega-3 fatty acid - a type of polyunsaturated fatty acid important in diet

Etymology

From Greek 'dokos' (beam) + 'hexa' (six) + 'enoic', relating to unsaturated carboxylic acids.
