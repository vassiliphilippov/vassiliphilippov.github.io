yellow cedar

1. NOUN

/ ˈjɛloʊ ˈsiːdər / (AmE), / ˈjɛləʊ ˈsiːdə / (BrE)
a tall evergreen conifer of the Pacific coast of North America, often cultivated as an ornamental tree
evergreen tree of Northwest North America

Examples

The wood of yellow cedar is highly valued for its durability and resistance to decay.
Many parks in British Columbia feature majestic yellow cedar trees lining the trails.
Gardeners often plant yellow cedar for its attractive foliage and graceful shape.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cedar - any of several coniferous trees with aromatic wood
