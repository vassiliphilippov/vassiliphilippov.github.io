John Barleycorn

1. NOUN

/ dʒɑn ˈbɑːrliˌkɔrn / (AmE), / dʒɒn ˈbɑːliˌkɔːn / (BrE)
A personification or literary term for strong alcoholic spirits, especially distilled drinks such as whisky; sometimes used to refer to alcohol in general.
personification of strong alcoholic spirits

Examples

Many poets have written about John Barleycorn and his seductive power over people.
He finally gave up John Barleycorn after years of heavy drinking.
The tale tells of John Barleycorn tempting travelers in the dead of night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whisky - a distilled alcoholic beverage made from fermented grain mash
  • Synonym: alcohol - a colorless volatile flammable liquid which is the intoxicating constituent of wine, beer, spirits, and other drinks

Etymology

A personification, from the name John combined with 'barleycorn' (the grain used in making alcohol), popularized by folk songs and literature.
