head gasket

1. NOUN

/ hɛd ˈɡæskɪt / (AmE), / hɛd ˈɡɑːskɪt / (BrE)
A gasket placed between an engine block and cylinder head to seal the combustion chamber and prevent leaks.
gasket sealing engine cylinder head

Examples

The mechanic replaced the blown head gasket to fix the engine's overheating problem.
A leaking head gasket can cause coolant and oil to mix, leading to serious engine damage.
If your head gasket fails, you might notice white smoke coming from the exhaust.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gasket - a mechanical seal that fills the space between two or more mating surfaces
