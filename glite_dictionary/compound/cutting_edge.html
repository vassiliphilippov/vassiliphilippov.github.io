cutting edge

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌtɪŋ ˌɛdʒ / (AmE), / ˈkʌtɪŋ ˌɛdʒ / (BrE)
the sharp, cutting side of a blade such as that of a knife or other cutting instrument
sharp side of a blade

Examples

Be careful when you handle the knife; its cutting edge is extremely sharp.
The chef honed the cutting edge of his favorite cleaver before starting to cook.
The scissors wouldn't cut because their cutting edge had become dull over time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blade - the flat, sharp-edged part of a cutting instrument

2. NOUN

/ ˈkʌtɪŋ ɛdʒ / (AmE), / ˈkʌtɪŋ ɛdʒ / (BrE)
the most advanced or innovative position in a particular field; the forefront of progress or development
foremost position in progress or innovation

Examples

This company is at the cutting edge of medical technology and innovation.
His research puts him on the cutting edge of renewable energy development.
The new smartphone features several cutting edge improvements over last year's model.

Synset Examples

the Cotswolds were once at the forefront of woollen manufacturing in England

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forefront - the leading or most important position
