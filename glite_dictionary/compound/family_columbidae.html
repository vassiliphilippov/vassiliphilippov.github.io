family Columbidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli kəˈlʌmbɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli kɒˈlʌmbɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of birds in the order Columbiformes, including all doves and pigeons.
bird family: doves and pigeons

Examples

The family Columbidae comprises more than 300 species, including both doves and pigeons.
During the lecture, the professor discussed the evolutionary traits unique to the family Columbidae.
All members of the family Columbidae share similar nesting and feeding habits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dove - A bird in the family Columbidae, typically with a small body and a soft cooing call
  • Synonym: pigeon - A stout-bodied bird in the family Columbidae, commonly found in towns and cities

Etymology

From Latin 'Columba' (dove, pigeon), used in scientific naming conventions.
