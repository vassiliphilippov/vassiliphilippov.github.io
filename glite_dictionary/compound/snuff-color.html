snuff-color

1. NOUN

/ snʌf ˈkʌlɚ / (AmE), / snʌf ˈkʌlə / (BrE)
a dark yellowish-brown color, resembling the color of powdered tobacco (snuff)
dark yellowish-brown color

Examples

The cloth was dyed a rich snuff-color, lending it an antique appearance.
She wore an old coat of snuff-color that matched the autumn leaves.
The artist carefully mixed paints to achieve the perfect snuff-color for the landscape.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: brown - a color produced by mixing red, yellow, and blue, often associated with earth or wood
  • Degree Lower: ochre - a moderate yellow-orange to orange color, often earth-like

Etymology

Derived from 'snuff', meaning powdered tobacco, referring to the typical color of snuff, and 'color'.
