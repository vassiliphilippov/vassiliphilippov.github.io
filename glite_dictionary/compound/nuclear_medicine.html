nuclear medicine

1. NOUN

/ ˈnuːkliɚ ˈmɛdɪsən / (AmE), / ˈnjuːkliə ˈmɛdɪsən / (BrE)
a branch of medicine that uses radioactive substances for diagnosis and treatment of diseases
medical imaging and treatment using radioactivity
medical

Examples

The hospital recently introduced nuclear medicine techniques to improve cancer diagnosis.
Specialists in nuclear medicine often work with PET and SPECT scanners.
He chose to pursue a career in nuclear medicine after medical school.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: radiology - the scientific discipline of medical imaging

Etymology

From 'nuclear' relating to the nucleus of an atom, and 'medicine'.
