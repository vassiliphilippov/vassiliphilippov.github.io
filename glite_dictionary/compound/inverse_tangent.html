inverse tangent

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˌvɝs ˈtæn.dʒənt / (AmE), / ɪnˌvɜːs ˈtæn.dʒənt / (BrE)
the function that gives the angle whose tangent is a given number; also known as arctangent.
mathematical function; arctangent

Examples

The inverse tangent of 1 is 45 degrees, or pi over 4 radians.
To find the angle, calculate the inverse tangent of the opposite side divided by the adjacent side.
The calculator can compute the inverse tangent for both positive and negative values.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arctangent - the angle whose tangent is a given number

Etymology

From 'inverse', meaning reversed in order or effect, and 'tangent', from Latin 'tangens' (touching).
