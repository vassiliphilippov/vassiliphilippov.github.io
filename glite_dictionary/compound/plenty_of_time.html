plenty of time

1. NOUN

/ ˈplɛnti əv taɪm / (AmE), / ˈplɛnti əv taɪm / (BrE)
a period of time that is more than sufficient for a particular purpose
more than enough time

Examples

Don't rush, we have plenty of time to get ready.
He arrived early because he thought there wasn't plenty of time left.
With plenty of time before the meeting, she relaxed in the café.

Synset Examples

We have plenty of time to finish the report.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: enough time - as much time as needed
