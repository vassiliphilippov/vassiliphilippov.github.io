reich-wing

1. NOUN

/ raɪk wɪŋ / (AmE), / raɪk wɪŋ / (BrE)
A derogatory term for extreme or authoritarian right-wing political ideology or views, often with implied associations to fascism or Nazism.
pejorative for extreme right-wing views
slang offensive

Examples

Many criticized the reich-wing rhetoric for promoting authoritarian policies.
The newspaper accused the new government of having reich-wing tendencies.
His reich-wing comments on immigration sparked a strong backlash online.

Synset Examples

Many criticized the reich-wing policies for being too extreme.
The reich-wing party gained support in the last election.
His reich-wing views often sparked heated debates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: right-wing - conservative or reactionary political views
  • Synonym: far-right - ultra-conservative/extreme right-wing politics
  • Antonym: left-wing - liberal or socialist political views

Etymology

Blend of 'Reich' (German for 'empire', referencing the Third Reich) and 'right-wing'.
