family Scyliorhinidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ˌsaɪliɔːrˈhɪnɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli ˌsaɪlɪəˈrɪnɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of small bottom-dwelling sharks, commonly known as catsharks, found in temperate and tropical seas.
a family of small bottom-dwelling sharks

Examples

The family Scyliorhinidae includes more than a hundred species of catsharks.
Marine biologists study members of the family Scyliorhinidae to understand biodiversity on the ocean floor.
The elongated bodies and mottled patterns are characteristic of the family Scyliorhinidae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catshark - a small shark from the family Scyliorhinidae

Etymology

From New Latin 'Scyliorhinidae', from Greek 'skullion' (dogfish) and 'rhinos' (nose).
