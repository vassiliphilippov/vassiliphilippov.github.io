wireless fidelity

1. NOUN

/ ˈwaɪrlɪs fɪˈdɛlɪti / (AmE), / ˈwaɪələs fɪˈdɛlɪti / (BrE)
A system for wireless local area networking that uses high-frequency radio signals to transmit and receive data over short distances, commonly known as Wi-Fi.
wireless networking technology (Wi-Fi)

Examples

Many public places now offer free wireless fidelity so people can connect their devices to the internet.
The university upgraded its wireless fidelity system to improve coverage for students and faculty.
Before installing wireless fidelity at home, make sure your router is compatible with your devices.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Wi-Fi - A technology for wireless local area networking based on the IEEE 802.11 standards.

Etymology

Formed from 'wireless' and 'fidelity', later abbreviated as 'Wi-Fi'.
