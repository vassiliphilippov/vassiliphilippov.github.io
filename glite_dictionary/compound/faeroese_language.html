Faeroese language

1. NOUN

/ ˌfɛəroʊˈiːz ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌfeərəʊˈiːz ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the North Germanic language, closely related to Icelandic, spoken by the inhabitants of the Faroe Islands
North Germanic language of the Faroe Islands

Examples

He is fluent in both Icelandic and Faeroese language.
The Faeroese language has preserved many Old Norse features.
They offer classes in the Faeroese language for new immigrants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Icelandic - language spoken in Iceland, closely related to Faeroese
  • Synonym: Faroese - the language of the Faroe Islands (alternative spelling)

Etymology

From Faroese 'føroyskt mál' (language of the Faroes); 'Faeroe' from Danish 'Færøerne' (Faroe Islands).
