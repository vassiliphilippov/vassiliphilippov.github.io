cold wave

1. NOUN

/ koʊld weɪv / (AmE), / kəʊld weɪv / (BrE)
a prolonged period of unusually cold weather, often affecting a large geographical area
prolonged period of very cold weather

Examples

The city braced itself for the incoming cold wave predicted by weather forecasters.
Last January, a severe cold wave swept across northern Europe, causing widespread disruption.
Farmers suffered heavy losses when an unexpected cold wave damaged the crops.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: heat wave - a prolonged period of excessively hot weather
