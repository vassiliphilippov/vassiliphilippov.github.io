birther movement

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɝːðɚ muːv.mənt / (AmE), / ˈbɜːðə ˈmuːv.mənt / (BrE)
a political campaign or group asserting that a public figure, especially a president, was not born in the country they serve, thereby questioning their eligibility for office
political group questioning someone's birthplace

Examples

The birther movement gained considerable attention during the 2008 U.S. presidential campaign.
Many news outlets debunked the claims made by members of the birther movement.
Historians will likely study the impact of the birther movement for years to come.

Synset Examples

The birther movement gained traction during the election.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: birther - someone who questions the birthplace and eligibility of a public figure

Etymology

From 'birther' (those who question birth certificates and eligibility) and 'movement'; popularized in U.S. politics in the 2000s.
