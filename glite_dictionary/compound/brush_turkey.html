brush turkey

1. NOUN

/ brʌʃ ˈtɝː.ki / (AmE), / brʌʃ ˈtɜː.ki / (BrE)
a large, black megapode bird native to wooded regions of Australia and New Guinea, known for building large nesting mounds
Australian-New Guinean megapode bird

Examples

We spotted a brush turkey scratching through the undergrowth in the national park.
The brush turkey uses its powerful feet to build massive nesting mounds.
In Australia, the brush turkey is often seen in suburban gardens searching for food.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: megapode - a family of ground-dwelling birds that build large nesting mounds
