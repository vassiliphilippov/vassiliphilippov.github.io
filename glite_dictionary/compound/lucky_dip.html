lucky dip

1. NOUN

/ ˈlʌki dɪp / (AmE), / ˈlʌki dɪp / (BrE)
A game, typically at fairs or parties, where small prizes are hidden in a container and participants pay to pick one out at random.
game where prizes are drawn at random
british

Examples

Children lined up to try their luck at the lucky dip during the village fete.
For just fifty pence, you could have a go at the lucky dip and win a surprise gift.
She won a small toy car from the lucky dip at the school fair.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlʌki dɪp / (AmE), / ˈlʌki dɪp / (BrE)
a selection or choice made purely at random, often for amusement or as part of a game; also used figuratively to refer to a process where outcomes are decided by chance
random selection or choice
british

Examples

The company’s new recruitment process is such a lucky dip that you never know who will get in.
At the school fair, children queued up to try their luck at the lucky dip stall.
Choosing a book from that shelf is a real lucky dip—it could be anything!

Synset Examples

their system of hiring people seemed to be a sort of lucky dip

Related Terms

  • Synonym: raffle - a means of choosing winners by chance, usually at an event
  • Synonym: lottery - a system of choosing people or things by chance

Etymology

Originally British English; refers to the practice at fairs where participants draw prizes from a container without knowing their contents.
