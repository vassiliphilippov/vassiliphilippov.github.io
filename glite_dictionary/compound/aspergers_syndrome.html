Asperger's syndrome

1. NOUN

/ ˈæspərdʒərz ˌsɪnˌdroʊm / (AmE), / ˈaspɜːgəz ˌsɪndrəʊm / (BrE)
A developmental disorder affecting the ability to effectively socialize and communicate, characterized by difficulties in social interaction and repetitive patterns of behavior.
developmental disorder affecting social interaction
medical

Examples

Her son was diagnosed with Asperger's syndrome at the age of seven.
Many people with Asperger's syndrome excel in fields that require attention to detail.
Public awareness of Asperger's syndrome has increased significantly in recent years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: autism spectrum disorder - a range of neurodevelopmental conditions including autism and Asperger's syndrome

Etymology

Named after Hans Asperger (1906–1980), Austrian pediatrician, who first described the condition.
