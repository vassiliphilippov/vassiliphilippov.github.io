Star-Spangled Banner

1. NOUN

/ stɑr ˈspæŋɡəld ˈbænɚ / (AmE), / stɑː ˈspæŋɡəld ˈbænə / (BrE)
the national flag of the United States of America, often referred to in a patriotic context
U.S. national flag

Examples

The Star-Spangled Banner was raised over Fort McHenry after the battle.
Many Americans wave the Star-Spangled Banner during Fourth of July parades.
The Star-Spangled Banner is a symbol of freedom and national pride throughout the United States.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Stars and Stripes - another name for the national flag of the United States

Etymology

From the title of the 1814 poem by Francis Scott Key, later adopted as the U.S. national anthem.
