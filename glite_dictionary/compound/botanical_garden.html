botanical garden

1. NOUN

/ bəˈtænɪkəl ˈɡɑrdn̩ / (AmE), / bəˈtænɪkəl ˈɡɑːdn̩ / (BrE)
A garden dedicated to the cultivation, display, and study of a wide variety of plants, often including trees and shrubs, for scientific, educational, and ornamental purposes.
garden for cultivated plant display and study

Examples

The city’s botanical garden attracts visitors from around the world with its rare plant collections.
She volunteers at the local botanical garden every Saturday, helping to maintain the greenhouse.
Over the weekend, we enjoyed a peaceful stroll through the botanical garden to admire the spring flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arboretum - a place where trees and shrubs are grown for study and display

2. NOUN

/ bəˈtænɪkəl ˈɡɑrdən / (AmE), / bəˈtænɪkəl ˈɡɑːdn̩ / (BrE)
A garden where a wide variety of plants are grown for scientific study, conservation, display, and education.
public garden for plant collections

Examples

She visited the botanical garden to see the new orchid collection in bloom.
The city’s botanical garden offers guided tours and educational programs for children.
Many rare and endangered species are conserved at the local botanical garden.

Synset Examples

He attended a lecture on treelogy at the botanical garden.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arboretum - a place where trees and shrubs are cultivated for scientific or educational purposes

Etymology

From 'botanical', relating to plants, and 'garden'. 'Botanical' derives from Late Latin 'botanicus' (of herbs).
