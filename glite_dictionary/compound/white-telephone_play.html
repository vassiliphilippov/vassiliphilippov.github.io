white-telephone play

1. NOUN

/ ˈwaɪt tɛlɪˌfoʊn pleɪ / (AmE), / ˈwaɪt tɛlɪfəʊn pleɪ / (BrE)
A type of theatrical play, especially from early 20th-century Italy, characterized by melodrama, sentimentality, and the depiction of wealthy, upper-class settings.
melodramatic, sentimental play depicting upper-class life

Examples

Her performance in that white-telephone play was outstanding.
The set design of the white-telephone play featured elegant furniture and lavish decor.
Many early Italian directors specialized in the melodramatic style of the white-telephone play.

Synset Examples

Her performance in that white-telephone play was outstanding.

Etymology

From the symbolic use of white telephones, which represented wealth and modernity in Italian cinema and theatre in the 1930s and 1940s.
