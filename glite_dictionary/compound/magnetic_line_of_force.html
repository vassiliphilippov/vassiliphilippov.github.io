magnetic line of force

1. NOUN

/ mæɡˈnɛtɪk laɪn əv ˈfɔrs / (AmE), / mæɡˈnɛtɪk laɪn əv ˈfɔːs / (BrE)
an imaginary line in a magnetic field, the tangent to which at any point gives the direction of the magnetic force at that point
imaginary line indicating magnetic field direction

Examples

Iron filings arrange themselves along magnetic lines of force when sprinkled near a magnet.
The picture showed the curved magnetic lines of force around the bar magnet.
Earth’s magnetic lines of force extend from the magnetic north pole to the south pole.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: magnetic field - the region around a magnet in which magnetic force is exerted
