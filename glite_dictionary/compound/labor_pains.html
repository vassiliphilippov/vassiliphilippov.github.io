labor pains

1. NOUN

/ ˈleɪ.bɚ ˌpeɪnz / (AmE), / ˈleɪ.bə ˌpeɪnz / (BrE)
intermittent pain experienced by a woman during childbirth, caused by contractions of the uterus
childbirth contractions pain
medical

Examples

The doctor assured her that the labor pains she was experiencing were normal and a sign of progress.
As the labor pains intensified, she gripped her partner's hand for support.
Most women feel labor pains begin as rhythmic cramps that gradually grow stronger and closer together.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contraction - a tightening of the muscles of the uterus during labor
