genetic psychology

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˈnɛtɪk saɪˈkɑlədʒi / (AmE), / dʒɪˈnɛtɪk saɪˈkɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the branch of psychology concerned with the study of the social and mental development of children
study of children's psychological development

Examples

She decided to specialize in genetic psychology to better understand how children develop over time.
Many classic theories in genetic psychology originated from observations of children in classroom settings.
The university offers a course in genetic psychology for students interested in childhood mental development.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: developmental psychology - the scientific study of how and why human beings change over the course of their life

Etymology

From 'genetic' (relating to origin or development) and 'psychology'.
