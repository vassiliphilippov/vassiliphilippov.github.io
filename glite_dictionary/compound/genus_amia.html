genus Amia

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˈeɪmiə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈæmɪə / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Amiidae, consisting of North American freshwater fish known as bowfins.
type genus of Amiidae (bowfins)

Examples

The genus Amia contains only one living species, the bowfin.
Fossils of the genus Amia have been found dating back to the Jurassic period.
Researchers are studying the evolutionary history of the genus Amia within North American freshwater ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Amiidae - the family of freshwater fish known as bowfins

Etymology

From Latin 'genus' (kind, type) + scientific name 'Amia', from Greek 'amía' (a kind of fish).
