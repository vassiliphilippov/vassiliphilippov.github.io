Paget's disease

1. NOUN

/ ˈpædʒɪts dɪˌziz / (AmE), / ˈpædʒɪts dɪˌziːz / (BrE)
A chronic condition in which abnormal bone destruction and regrowth occur, leading to deformed or enlarged bones, often causing pain, fractures, and skeletal deformities, most commonly in middle-aged and elderly people.
disease causing abnormal bone growth and deformities
medical

Examples

Paget's disease is most commonly diagnosed in older adults experiencing unexplained bone pain.
The X-ray revealed that the patient's leg deformity was the result of Paget's disease.
Treatments for Paget's disease often focus on managing symptoms and slowing bone changes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: osteitis deformans - another name for Paget's disease

Etymology

Named after Sir James Paget (1814–1899), English surgeon and pathologist, who first described the disease.
