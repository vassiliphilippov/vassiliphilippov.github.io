Richardson vole

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɪtʃɚdsən voʊl / (AmE), / ˈrɪtʃədsən vəʊl / (BrE)
A large vole species native to western North America, identified scientifically as Microtus richardsoni.
large vole of western North America

Examples

The Richardson vole thrives in high-altitude meadows across the Rocky Mountains.
Researchers studied the habitat preferences of the Richardson vole over several years.
A Richardson vole was spotted gathering grass near its burrow at dusk.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vole - A small, mouselike rodent with a stout body and short tail

Etymology

Named after Sir John Richardson (1787–1865), Scottish naturalist and explorer.
