hydraulic brake

1. NOUN

/ haɪˈdrɑːlɪk breɪk / (AmE), / haɪˈdrɒlɪk breɪk / (BrE)
a brake system in which the pressure from brake fluid transmits force from the pedal to the brake pads or shoes
brake using fluid pressure

Examples

The new car features a hydraulic brake system for smoother and safer stops.
Most modern vehicles rely on hydraulic brakes instead of mechanical ones.
When the hydraulic brake failed, the mechanic found a leak in the brake line.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disc brake - a brake that uses calipers to squeeze pairs of pads against a disc
  • Synonym: drum brake - a brake in which shoes press against a rotating drum

Etymology

From 'hydraulic', relating to liquid in motion (Greek: hydraulikos), and 'brake', device for slowing or stopping.
