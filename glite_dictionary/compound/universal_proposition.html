universal proposition

1. NOUN

/ juːˈnɪvɚsəl ˌprɑːpəˈzɪʃən / (AmE), / juːˈnɪvəsəl ˌprɒpəˈzɪʃən / (BrE)
(logic) A proposition that asserts that something is true for all members of a given class.
statement true for all case in a class (logic)

Examples

The statement 'All mammals are warm-blooded' is a universal proposition in biology.
In logic, a universal proposition can often be symbolized using the universal quantifier.
She debated whether the claim was truly a universal proposition or if exceptions might exist.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: existential proposition - a proposition asserting that something exists in a class (logic)

Etymology

Formed from 'universal' (from Latin universalis, 'of or belonging to all') and 'proposition' (from Latin propositio, 'a statement or assertion').
