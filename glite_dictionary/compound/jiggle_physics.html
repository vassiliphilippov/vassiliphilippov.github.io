jiggle physics

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɪɡəl ˈfɪzɪks / (AmE), / ˈdʒɪɡl ˈfɪzɪks / (BrE)
animated simulation of the way soft body parts or objects move and bounce, used in video games or animation to create visually appealing motion effects
simulated bouncing motion in animation or games

Examples

Many modern fighting games use jiggle physics to make animated characters move more realistically.
The developer toned down the jiggle physics in the new update after receiving feedback from players.
Some players find excessive jiggle physics distracting during gameplay.

Synset Examples

The game uses jiggle physics for realistic movement.
Her dress had jiggle physics that made it entertaining.
I love how jiggle physics adds fun to animations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ragdoll physics - simulation technique for limp, articulated bodies in games or animation

Etymology

From 'jiggle' meaning a quick, light shaking movement, and 'physics,' referring to the simulation of physical movement in computer graphics.
