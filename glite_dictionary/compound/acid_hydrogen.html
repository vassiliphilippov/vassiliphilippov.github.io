acid hydrogen

1. NOUN

/ ˈæsɪd ˈhaɪdrədʒən / (AmE), / ˈæsɪd ˈhʌɪdrɪdʒən / (BrE)
a hydrogen atom in an acid molecule that can dissociate as a positive ion (proton) during a chemical reaction
hydrogen atom in an acid that can dissociate as H⁺
medical

Examples

The acid hydrogen is released as a proton during the dissociation of hydrochloric acid in water.
In sulfuric acid, each molecule contains two acid hydrogens that can dissociate sequentially.
The presence of acid hydrogen determines the strength and behavior of an acid in solution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: proton - a positively charged subatomic particle; in this context, a hydrogen ion
