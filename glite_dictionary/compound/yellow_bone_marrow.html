yellow bone marrow

1. NOUN

/ ˈjɛloʊ bəʊn ˈmær.oʊ / (AmE), / ˈjɛləʊ bəʊn ˈmær.əʊ / (BrE)
A type of bone marrow that is yellow in color due to a high fat content, found mainly in the cavities of long bones in adults.
fatty bone marrow in long bones
medical

Examples

With age, much of the red marrow is replaced by yellow bone marrow in the long bones.
Doctors examined the patient's yellow bone marrow to assess the impact of the treatment.
Yellow bone marrow primarily functions as a fat store rather than in blood cell formation.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: red bone marrow - form of bone marrow involved in blood cell production
