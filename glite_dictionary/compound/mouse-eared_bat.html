mouse-eared bat

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaʊs ɪrd bæt / (AmE), / ˈmaʊs ɪəd bat / (BrE)
a bat of the genus Myotis, distinguished by ears that resemble those of a mouse
bat of the Myotis genus with mouse-like ears

Examples

The mouse-eared bat is common in caves throughout Europe and Asia.
Researchers tagged several mouse-eared bats to study their nightly hunting patterns.
A young mouse-eared bat was found roosting in the attic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bat - flying mammal

Etymology

Formed from 'mouse' and 'eared,' referring to the bat's ear shape resembling that of a mouse.
