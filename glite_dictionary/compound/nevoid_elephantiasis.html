nevoid elephantiasis

1. NOUN

/ ˈniː.vɔɪd ˌɛl.ə.fənˈtaɪ.əsɪs / (AmE), / ˈniː.vɔɪd ˌɛl.ɪ.fənˈtaɪ.əsɪs / (BrE)
A congenital condition characterized by unilateral thickening of the skin on an extremity due to enlargement and obstruction of lymph vessels.
congenital thickening of skin from lymph vessel issues
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with nevoid elephantiasis after exhibiting unilateral swelling of the lower leg.
Congenital lymph vessel malformation is the primary cause of nevoid elephantiasis in affected individuals.
Surgical intervention may be required to manage advanced cases of nevoid elephantiasis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elephantiasis - chronic swelling and thickening of skin due to lymphatic obstruction

Etymology

From 'nevus' (Latin for birthmark) and 'elephantiasis' (Greek, meaning condition resembling an elephant), referring to the skin's appearance.
