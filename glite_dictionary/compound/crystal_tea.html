crystal tea

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɪstl ti / (AmE), / ˈkrɪstl tiː / (BrE)
an evergreen shrub of eastern North America with white or creamy bell-shaped flowers and hairy dark green leaves, which were used to make tea during the American Revolution
shrub used for tea in colonial America

Examples

During the American Revolution, colonists brewed crystal tea as a substitute for imported tea.
The leaves of crystal tea have a slightly astringent flavor reminiscent of black tea.
Botanists often study crystal tea as an example of plants used in traditional herbal remedies.
