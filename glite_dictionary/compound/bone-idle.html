bone-idle

1. ADJ

/ boʊn ˈaɪdl̩ / (AmE), / bəʊn ˈaɪdl / (BrE)
Extremely lazy or unwilling to work; habitually idle.
extremely lazy or idle
british

Examples

No matter how much I encourage him, he's still bone-idle and won't lift a finger around the house.
Her bone-idle attitude is frustrating her manager at work.
Some people claim teenagers are bone-idle, but that's just an unfair stereotype.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lazy - unwilling to work or put in effort
  • Antonym: hardworking - diligent and industrious; opposite of lazy

Etymology

Probably from 'idle' with 'bone' as an intensifier; recorded since the 19th century in British English.
