American parsley fern

1. NOUN

/ əˈmɛrɪkən ˈpɑrsli fɜrn / (AmE), / əˈmɛrɪkən ˈpɑːsli fɜːn / (BrE)
A rock-inhabiting fern native to northern North America, forming thick tufts and having fronds that resemble parsley.
fern with parsley-like fronds from North America

Examples

The American parsley fern thrives in rocky crevices across Canada and parts of the northern United States.
Botanists discovered a new population of American parsley fern in a remote mountain region.
With its fronds shaped like parsley leaves, the American parsley fern is easily recognizable in the wild.
