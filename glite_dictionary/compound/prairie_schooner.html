prairie schooner

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɛri ˈskuːnər / (AmE), / ˈprɛːri ˈskuːnə / (BrE)
a large covered wagon with broad wheels, used by United States pioneers to cross the prairies in the 19th century
19th-century covered wagon

Examples

The family loaded their belongings into a prairie schooner and set out for the West.
Several prairie schooners could be seen on the horizon, kicked up by clouds of dust.
The canvas top of the old prairie schooner flapped in the strong prairie wind.

Etymology

The term combines 'prairie' (from French 'prairie', meaning meadow) and 'schooner' (originally a type of sailing ship), likening the wagon's canvas top to a ship's sails.
