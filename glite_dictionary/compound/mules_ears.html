mule's ears

1. NOUN

/ ˈmjulz ˌɪrz / (AmE), / ˈmjuːlz ˌɪəz / (BrE)
A balsamic-resinous herb native to the northwestern United States, with clumps of lance-shaped leaves and tall stems ending in large deep yellow flowers.
herb with lance-shaped leaves and yellow flowers (NW US)

Examples

We spotted mule's ears growing along the hiking trail in the mountains.
The leaves of mule's ears are long and pointed, much like the ears of a mule.
In spring, mule's ears bloom with bright yellow flowers that attract bees and butterflies.

Etymology

Named for the shape of its leaves, which resemble a mule's ears.
