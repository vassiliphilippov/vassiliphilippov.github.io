geometric progression

1. NOUN

/ dʒiəˈmɛtrɪk prəˈɡrɛʃən / (AmE), / ˌdʒiːəˌmɛtrɪk prəˈɡrɛʃən / (BrE)
(Mathematics) A sequence of numbers in which each term is obtained by multiplying the previous term by a fixed, nonzero constant.
a mathematical sequence; each term multiplied by a constant

Examples

The population of the bacteria increased according to a geometric progression every hour.
If you double your investment each year, your gains will follow a geometric progression.
1, 2, 4, 8, 16 is an example of a geometric progression.

Synset Examples

1-4-16-64-256- is the start of a geometric progression

Related Terms

  • Antonym: arithmetic progression - a sequence in which the difference between any two consecutive terms is constant

Etymology

From geometric, relating to geometry, and progression from Latin progressio (advance, forward movement).
