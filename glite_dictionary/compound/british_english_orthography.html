British English orthography

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrɪtɪʃ ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ ɔrˈθɑɡrəfi / (AmE), / ˈbrɪtɪʃ ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ ɔːˈθɒɡrəfi / (BrE)
the conventional spelling system and rules of the English language as used in the United Kingdom and most Commonwealth countries, distinct from American English spelling
spelling system used in the UK and most Commonwealth countries
british

Examples

Many academic publications follow British English orthography rather than American spelling conventions.
All government documents in Australia use British English orthography by default.
If you write for a UK audience, it's important to use British English orthography throughout your work.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: American English orthography - English spelling conventions used in the United States
