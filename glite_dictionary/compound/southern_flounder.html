southern flounder

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌðɚn ˈflaʊndɚ / (AmE), / ˈsʌðən ˈflaʊndə / (BrE)
a species of flatfish native to the coastal waters of the southern United States, noted for its ability to camouflage with the sea floor
a flatfish found in the southern US

Examples

Fishermen along the Gulf Coast often catch southern flounder during the fall migration.
The southern flounder can change its coloration to blend with the ocean floor.
Populations of southern flounder have declined due to overfishing and habitat loss.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flounder - a type of flatfish found in coastal waters
