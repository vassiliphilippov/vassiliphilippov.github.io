oboe d'amore

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊboʊ də ˈmɔːreɪ / (AmE), / ˌəʊbəʊ dɑːˈmɔːreɪ / (BrE)
an oboe pitched a minor third lower than the standard oboe, used especially in baroque music
a lower-pitched type of oboe

Examples

The concerto was written specifically for the oboe d'amore, giving it a warm and mellow tone.
Baroque composers often featured the oboe d'amore for its distinct timbre.
She played a solo on the oboe d'amore during the orchestral performance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oboe - a woodwind instrument with a double reed

Etymology

From Italian, meaning 'oboe of love'.
