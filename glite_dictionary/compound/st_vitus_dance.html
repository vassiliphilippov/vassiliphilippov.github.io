St. Vitus dance

1. NOUN

/ seɪnt ˈvaɪtəs dæns / (AmE), / sənt ˈvaɪtəs dɑːns / (BrE)
A disorder characterized by involuntary, rapid, irregular movements, occurring mainly in children and associated with rheumatic fever; also known as Sydenham's chorea.
chorea linked to rheumatic fever, mainly in children
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed the child with St. Vitus dance after observing her uncontrollable limb movements.
Cases of St. Vitus dance are now rare due to advances in treating rheumatic fever.
Historically, St. Vitus dance terrified families because of its sudden, jerky motions in children.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chorea - disorder marked by involuntary movements

Etymology

Named after St. Vitus, the patron saint of dancers, and associated with medieval dancing outbreaks; 'dance' refers to the jerky movements.
