Ebola virus

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈboʊlə ˈvaɪrəs / (AmE), / iˈbəʊlə ˈvaɪrəs / (BrE)
A highly infectious filovirus that causes Ebola hemorrhagic fever in humans and animals.
virus causing Ebola hemorrhagic fever
medical

Examples

The Ebola virus outbreak in West Africa in 2014 drew international concern.
Scientists are studying how the Ebola virus spreads between animals and humans.
Strict safety protocols are required when handling samples containing the Ebola virus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Marburg virus - a related filovirus causing similar hemorrhagic fevers

Etymology

Named after the Ebola River in the Democratic Republic of the Congo, where the virus was first identified in 1976.
