secondary diagonal

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛk.ənˌdɛr.i daɪˈæɡ.ən.əl / (AmE), / ˈsɛk.əndəri dʌɪˈaɪ.ə.ɡən.əl / (BrE)
in a square matrix, the diagonal running from the bottom-left corner to the top-right corner
diagonal from bottom-left to top-right in a matrix

Examples

In this 3x3 matrix, the secondary diagonal contains the elements 3, 5, and 7.
The sum of the secondary diagonal is often used in matrix computations.
She highlighted both the main and secondary diagonal for clarity during her lecture.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: main diagonal - the diagonal of a square matrix running from the upper left to the lower right
