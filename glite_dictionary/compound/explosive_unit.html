explosive unit

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˈsploʊsɪv ˈjuːnɪt / (AmE), / ɪkˈspləʊsɪv ˈjuːnɪt / (BrE)
a unit used for measuring the force generated by an explosion
unit for measuring explosion force

Examples

The impact of the blast was calculated using a standard explosive unit.
Researchers compared different materials by assessing their energy release in explosive units.
The safety regulations specify limits expressed in explosive units to prevent structural damage.
