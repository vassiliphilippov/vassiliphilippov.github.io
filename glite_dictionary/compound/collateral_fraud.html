collateral fraud

1. NOUN

/ kəˈlæt̬.ər.əl frɑːd / (AmE), / kəˈlæt.ər.əl frɔːd / (BrE)
fraud that prevents a person from knowing their rights or having a fair opportunity to present their case at trial
legal fraud blocking fair trial
legal

Examples

The defendant alleged collateral fraud in the contract negotiations, claiming he was misled about his legal options.
Because of the uncovered collateral fraud, the court declared the original verdict invalid and ordered a new trial.
If proven, collateral fraud could lead to the reversal of the judgment due to the deprivation of a fair hearing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fraud - wrongful or criminal deception intended to result in financial or personal gain
