hangar queen

1. NOUN

/ ˈhæŋɚ kwiːn / (AmE), / ˈhæŋə kwiːn / (BrE)
an aircraft that is frequently kept out of service because of repeated or persistent maintenance problems
aircraft often grounded due to maintenance issues
informal

Examples

That old jet has become a hangar queen, spending more time on the ground than in the air.
Our squadron is frustrated with the hangar queen constantly waiting for parts or repairs.
They finally retired the hangar queen after years of mechanical issues.

Etymology

Aviation slang, from 'hangar' (aircraft storage building) and 'queen' (something occupying a role but inactive).

2. NOUN

/ ˈhæŋɚ kwiːn / (AmE), / ˈhæŋə kwiːn / (BrE)
an aircraft that is seldom flown or is frequently grounded due to maintenance or repair issues
aircraft often out of service

Examples

The aging bomber became a hangar queen after a series of mechanical failures.
Maintenance crews worked tirelessly to get the hangar queen back in the air.
Because of constant repairs, the fighter jet earned a reputation as a hangar queen.
