St John's wort

1. NOUN

/ sənt ˈdʒɑnz wɔrt / (AmE), / sənt ˈdʒɒnz wɔːt / (BrE)
any of several plants of the genus Hypericum with yellow flowers, especially Hypericum perforatum, traditionally used in herbal medicine and folklore to ward off evil.
plant of the genus Hypericum, yellow-flowered herb

Examples

Many people use St John's wort as a natural remedy for mild depression.
The bright yellow flowers of St John's wort bloom in midsummer across the countryside.
Legend says St John's wort was hung over doorways to ward off evil spirits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hypericum - the genus of flowering plants including St John's wort

Etymology

Named after St John the Baptist, as the plant was traditionally harvested on his feast day (June 24). From Middle English wort (plant or herb).
