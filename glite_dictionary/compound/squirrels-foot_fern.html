squirrel's-foot fern

1. NOUN

/ ˈskwɝːrəlz fʊt fɝn / (AmE), / ˈskwɪrəlz fʊt fɜːn / (BrE)
a feathery fern native to tropical Asia and Malaysia, known for its soft, furry rhizomes resembling a squirrel's foot
feathery fern of tropical Asia and Malaysia

Examples

The squirrel's-foot fern thrives in humid, shaded gardens where its delicate fronds can spread gracefully.
Botanists admire the unique rhizomes of the squirrel's-foot fern for their resemblance to small animal paws.
I added a squirrel's-foot fern to my collection of tropical plants for its lush foliage and unusual root structure.

Etymology

Named for the furry, foot-like appearance of the plant's rhizomes, which resemble a squirrel's foot.
