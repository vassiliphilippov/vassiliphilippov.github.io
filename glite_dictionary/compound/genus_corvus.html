genus Corvus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˈkɔːrvəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈkɔːvəs / (BrE)
The principal genus of the family Corvidae, comprising crows, ravens, and related birds.
genus of crows and ravens

Examples

The genus Corvus includes both the common raven and several species of crows.
Scientists debate how many species belong to the genus Corvus.
Birdwatchers often look for members of the genus Corvus in rural and urban environments alike.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corvid - a bird of the crow family

Etymology

From Latin 'Corvus' meaning 'crow'.
