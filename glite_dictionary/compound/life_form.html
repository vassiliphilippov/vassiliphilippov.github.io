life form

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪf fɔrm / (AmE), / ˈlaɪf fɔːm / (BrE)
the characteristic physical structure of a mature living organism.
physical form of a living organism

Examples

The tree's life form is adapted to survive in arid environments.
Scientists study the life form of the frog to understand its unique adaptations.
Cacti exhibit a life form characterized by thick stems and spines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: organism - an individual living thing, such as an animal, plant, or bacterium
