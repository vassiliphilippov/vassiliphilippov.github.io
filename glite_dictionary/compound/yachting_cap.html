yachting cap

1. NOUN

/ ˈjɑːtɪŋ kæp / (AmE), / ˈjɒtɪŋ kæp / (BrE)
a cap with a flat, circular top and a visor, traditionally worn for yachting or sailing
sailor's cap with flat top and visor

Examples

The captain wore his yachting cap proudly during the regatta.
She bought a classic white yachting cap as a souvenir from the marina.
Many boaters consider a yachting cap an essential part of their sailing attire.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sailor cap - a round, flat-topped cap worn by sailors

Etymology

From 'yachting' (related to yachts) and 'cap'.
