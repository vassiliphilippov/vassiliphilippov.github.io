South Korean won

1. NOUN

/ saʊθ kəˈriːən wɑn / (AmE), / saʊθ kəˈriːən wɒn / (BrE)
the official currency of South Korea, subdivided into 100 jeon
currency of South Korea

Examples

Tourists can exchange dollars for South Korean won at most international airports.
The value of the South Korean won has fluctuated against the US dollar this year.
She saved a few South Korean won bills as souvenirs from her trip to Seoul.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: won - the currency unit of North or South Korea

Etymology

From Korean 원 (won), itself from Chinese 圓 (yuán, meaning 'round; coin').
