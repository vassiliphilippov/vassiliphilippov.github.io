horn of plenty

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɔrn əv ˈplɛnti / (AmE), / ˌhɔːn əv ˈplɛnti / (BrE)
A symbol of abundance and nourishment, typically depicted as a goat's horn overflowing with grain, flowers, and fruit.
symbol of abundance (cornucopia)

Examples

The Thanksgiving table was decorated with a horn of plenty overflowing with fruits and vegetables.
Artists often use the horn of plenty as a motif to represent prosperity in their paintings.
During the festival, a large horn of plenty was placed at the entrance as a sign of good fortune.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cornucopia - a symbol of abundance, often represented as a horn overflowing with produce
