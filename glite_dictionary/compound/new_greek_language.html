New Greek language

1. NOUN

/ nu ˈɡrik ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / njuː ˈɡriːk ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Greek language as spoken and written from the late medieval period to the present day, also known as Modern Greek
modern form of the Greek language

Examples

She studied the New Greek language at university to better understand modern Greek culture.
The New Greek language has evolved considerably from its ancient predecessor.
Courses in the New Greek language are now widely available online.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Ancient Greek - the Greek language as spoken and written in classical antiquity
  • Degree Lower: Medieval Greek - the Greek language as spoken and written from the 6th to the 15th century CE

Etymology

New Greek, or Modern Greek, distinguishes forms of Greek since the 15th century CE, following Medieval and Ancient Greek.
