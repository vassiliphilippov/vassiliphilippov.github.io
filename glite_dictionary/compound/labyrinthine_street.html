labyrinthine street

1. NOUN

/ ˌlæb.əˈrɪn.θin strit / (AmE), / ˌlæb.ɪˈrɪn.θiːn striːt / (BrE)
a street or network of streets that is highly complex and winding, making navigation difficult
complex, winding street(s)

Examples

The old quarter was a maze of labyrinthine streets that confused even the locals.
Tourists often get lost in the labyrinthine streets that wind through the ancient capital.
After dark, the labyrinthine street became eerily quiet and deserted.

Synset Examples

They navigated the labyrinthine streets of the old city.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maze - a complicated network of paths or passages

Etymology

Labyrinthine from Greek 'labyrinthos' (maze), meaning maze-like.
