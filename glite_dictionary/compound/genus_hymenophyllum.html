genus Hymenophyllum

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˌhaɪmɪnəˈfɪləm / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˌhɪmɪnəˈfɪləm / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Hymenophyllaceae, comprising delicate filmy ferns.
type genus of filmy ferns

Examples

Some rainforest epiphytes belong to the genus Hymenophyllum, known for their thin, translucent leaves.
The genus Hymenophyllum includes species commonly called filmy ferns due to their delicate fronds.
Botanists discovered a new species in the genus Hymenophyllum growing on a shaded cliff.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hymenophyllaceae - A family of delicate, often translucent ferns

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, combining Greek humnē (membrane) and phullon (leaf).
