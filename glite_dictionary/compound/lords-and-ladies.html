lords-and-ladies

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɔrdz ənd ˈleɪdiz / (AmE), / ˌlɔːdz ənd ˈleɪdiz / (BrE)
A common European woodland plant (Arum maculatum) with arrow-shaped leaves, a pale hood-like spathe, and a short purple spadix, appearing in early spring.
a European woodland plant (Arum maculatum)

Examples

We spotted lords-and-ladies growing in the shady corners of the woodland.
The arrow-shaped leaves of lords-and-ladies appear in early spring before most other plants.
Children are often warned not to touch lords-and-ladies because its berries are poisonous.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cuckoo-pint - another name for Arum maculatum
  • Degree Higher: arum - plant genus including lords-and-ladies and related species

Etymology

The name likely refers to the plant's distinctive spadix and spathe, which were fancifully likened to a lord and lady.
