sterling silver

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɜrlɪŋ ˈsɪlvər / (AmE), / ˈstɜːlɪŋ ˈsɪlvə / (BrE)
a high-quality silver alloy containing 92.5% silver and 7.5% other metals, usually copper, used for coins, jewelry, and tableware
high-quality silver alloy (92.5% silver)

Examples

The necklace is made of sterling silver, ensuring both beauty and durability.
He received a set of sterling silver cutlery as a wedding gift.
Many antique coins are composed of sterling silver rather than pure silver.

Etymology

From 'sterling' (Middle English, meaning 'of high quality') and 'silver'.
