spear carrier

1. NOUN

/ ˈspɪr ˌkæriər / (AmE), / ˈspɪə ˌkæriə / (BrE)
a minor actor in a play, movie, or opera, especially one who has little or no speaking part
minor actor with a nonspeaking role

Examples

After years of being a spear carrier, she finally landed a leading role in the theater company.
He started his movie career as a spear carrier in historical dramas.
Most of the cast at the back were just spear carriers with no lines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: extra - a minor actor with no speaking part in a film or play

Etymology

Originally from theater productions where minor soldiers held spears and had no lines.

2. NOUN

/ spɪr ˈkæriər / (AmE), / spɪə ˈkæriə / (BrE)
a person who has a very minor or insignificant role, especially in a play, film, or group
minor or insignificant role

Examples

Although he was just a spear carrier in the production, he enjoyed being part of the play.
She grew tired of always being a spear carrier in group projects instead of leading.
Even in her first job, she was more than a spear carrier and quickly took on greater responsibility.

Synset Examples

She felt like a spear carrier in the large ensemble cast.
Even as a spear carrier, he stole the scene.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: extra - an actor with a non-speaking, usually background role

Etymology

Originally from stage and opera, where extras would carry spears in crowd scenes.
