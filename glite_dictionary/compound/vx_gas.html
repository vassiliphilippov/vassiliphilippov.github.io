VX gas

1. NOUN

/ viː ˈɛks ɡæs / (AmE), / viː ˈɛks ɡæs / (BrE)
a highly toxic chemical nerve agent used in chemical warfare, capable of causing fatal disruption of the nervous system through skin contact or inhalation
highly toxic nerve agent (chemical warfare)

Examples

The military conducted drills to simulate exposure to VX gas and train emergency responders.
Even a tiny drop of VX gas on the skin can be fatal within minutes.
International treaties strictly prohibit the production and use of VX gas.

Synset Examples

VX gas is one of those things we wish we could disinvent

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nerve agent - a substance that disrupts the nervous system, used as a chemical weapon

Etymology

From the designation 'VX', a code for 'venomous agent X' used by the U.S. Army.
