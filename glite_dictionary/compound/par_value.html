par value

1. NOUN

/ pɑr ˈvæl.ju / (AmE), / pɑː ˈvæl.juː / (BrE)
the nominal or face value of a security as stated by the issuing company, not necessarily its market value
nominal value of a security

Examples

The company's bonds were issued at a par value of $1,000 each.
Shares may have a par value as low as one cent or no par value at all.
Dividends are often calculated as a percentage of the par value of preferred stock.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: face value - the stated value printed on a security or coin
  • Antonym: market value - the price at which an asset would trade in a competitive auction setting

Etymology

From Latin par (equal) and value (worth).
