candelilla wax

1. NOUN

/ ˌkændəˈliːjə wæks / (AmE), / ˌkændɪˈlɪlə wæks / (BrE)
a hard, brownish vegetable wax obtained from the leaves of the candelilla shrub, used in cosmetics, food, and polishes
wax from candelilla shrub

Examples

Many natural lip balms use candelilla wax as a vegan alternative to beeswax.
The shine on the furniture comes from a polish containing candelilla wax.
Chewing gum makers sometimes add candelilla wax to improve the gum's texture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carnauba wax - a hard wax obtained from the leaves of the carnauba palm

Etymology

From Spanish 'candelilla' (diminutive of 'candela', meaning 'candle').
