Lot's wife

1. NOUN

/ lɑts waɪf / (AmE), / lɒts waɪf / (BrE)
a woman in the Old Testament who was turned into a pillar of salt after looking back at the destruction of Sodom and Gomorrah, contrary to God's command
woman from the Bible turned into a pillar of salt

Examples

The story of Lot's wife is a warning against disobedience in religious teachings.
Some sculptures depict Lot's wife as a solitary figure gazing backward.
In literature, 'turning into Lot's wife' is sometimes used as a metaphor for being unable to let go of the past.

2. NOUN

/ lɑts waɪf / (AmE), / lɒts waɪf / (BrE)
a person who looks back with regret or hesitates to move forward, alluding to the Biblical story of Lot's wife turning into a pillar of salt for looking back at Sodom
person who looks back or regrets (Biblical allusion)

Examples

She warned me not to be a Lot's wife and dwell on missed opportunities.
After the merger, some employees became like Lot's wife, unable to let go of the old ways.
He acted like Lot's wife, constantly regretting his decision to move abroad.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: regret - feel sad or disappointed about something that has happened
  • Degree Lower: nostalgia - a sentimental longing for the past

Etymology

From the Biblical story in Genesis, where Lot's wife was turned into a pillar of salt for looking back at the destruction of Sodom.
