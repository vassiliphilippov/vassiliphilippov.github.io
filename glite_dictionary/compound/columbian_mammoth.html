Columbian mammoth

1. NOUN

/ kəˈlʌmbiən ˈmæməθ / (AmE), / kəˈlʌmbiən ˈmaməθ / (BrE)
an extinct species of large mammoth that lived in North America during the Pleistocene epoch
extinct North American mammoth species

Examples

Fossils of the Columbian mammoth have been discovered in several parts of North America.
The Columbian mammoth was one of the largest mammals to roam the ancient plains.
Scientists study the bones of the Columbian mammoth to learn about prehistoric environments.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: woolly mammoth - extinct mammoth species with a thick coat, native to Eurasia and North America

Etymology

Named after Christopher Columbus; 'mammoth' from Russian 'mamont'.
