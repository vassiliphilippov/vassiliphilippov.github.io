Hessian fly

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛʃən flaɪ / (AmE), / ˈhɛsɪən flaɪ / (BrE)
a small fly (Mayetiola destructor) whose larvae infest and damage wheat and other cereal crops
small fly pest of grains

Examples

The Hessian fly caused severe damage to wheat crops across the Midwest last year.
Farmers monitor their fields for signs of Hessian fly infestations to prevent serious yield loss.
Pesticides are sometimes used to control Hessian fly populations in vulnerable regions.

Etymology

Named after Hessian soldiers, who were wrongly believed to have introduced it to North America during the American Revolution.
