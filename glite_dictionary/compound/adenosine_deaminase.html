adenosine deaminase

1. NOUN

/ əˌdiːnoʊˌsiːn diːˈæmɪˌneɪs / (AmE), / əˌdiːnəʊˌsiːn diːˈæmɪˌneɪs / (BrE)
an enzyme found in mammals that catalyzes the removal of an amino group from adenosine, producing inosine and ammonia
enzyme that deaminates adenosine
medical

Examples

Adenosine deaminase deficiency can lead to severe combined immunodeficiency in humans.
The doctor ordered a test to measure adenosine deaminase levels in the patient's blood sample.
Researchers are studying the role of adenosine deaminase in immune system regulation.

Synset Examples

ADA deficiency can lead to one form of severe combined immunodeficiency disease
the gene encoding ADA was one of the earlier human genes to be isolated and cloned for study

Etymology

From adenosine (a nucleoside) + deaminase (an enzyme that removes amino groups).
