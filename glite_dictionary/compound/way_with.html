way with

1. NOUN

/ weɪ wɪð / (AmE), / weɪ wɪð / (BrE)
a natural ability or talent for handling or using someone or something effectively
natural talent for handling something

Examples

She has a remarkable way with children, always able to calm them down quickly.
He really has a way with words, persuading everyone with his speeches.
People say she has a way with animals and can train even the most stubborn dogs.

Synset Examples

Artists often have a way with colors and shapes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: knack - an acquired or natural skill at performing a task
  • Synonym: talent - a natural aptitude or skill
