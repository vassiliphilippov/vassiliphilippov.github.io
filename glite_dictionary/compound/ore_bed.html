ore bed

1. NOUN

/ ɔr bɛd / (AmE), / ɔː bɛd / (BrE)
A layer or stratum of ore found within the earth, typically forming a distinct band or deposit within rock.
layer of ore within rock

Examples

The miners discovered a rich ore bed deep beneath the mountain.
Geologists mapped the location of each ore bed before mining operations began.
This region is famous for its extensive ore beds containing valuable minerals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vein - a distinct sheetlike body of crystallized minerals within a rock
  • Synonym: lode - a rich source or supply, especially of a mineral deposit
