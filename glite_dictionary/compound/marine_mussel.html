marine mussel

1. NOUN

/ məˈriːn ˈmʌsəl / (AmE), / məˈriːn ˈmʌsəl / (BrE)
A type of bivalve mollusk found in saltwater environments, with a dark, elongated shell, typically attached to rocks or other hard surfaces in intertidal zones.
saltwater bivalve mollusk

Examples

The rocky coastline was covered in clusters of marine mussels clinging tightly to the stones.
Harvesting marine mussels at low tide is a common practice in many coastal communities.
Many seabirds feed on marine mussels by dropping them from heights to crack their shells.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: freshwater mussel - bivalve mollusk found in fresh water
  • Synonym: mussel - general term for bivalve mollusks of several families
