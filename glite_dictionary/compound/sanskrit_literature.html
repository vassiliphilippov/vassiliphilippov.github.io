Sanskrit literature

1. NOUN

/ ˈsænˌskrɪt ˈlɪt̬ərətʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈsænˌskrɪt ˈlɪtərətʃə / (BrE)
the body of literary works written in the Sanskrit language, especially those associated with Hindu traditions
literary works in Sanskrit, often Hindu

Examples

He devoted his life to the study of Sanskrit literature and its historical context.
Many philosophical texts in Sanskrit literature have influenced Hinduism and Buddhism.
Universities around the world offer courses on Sanskrit literature and classical poetry.
