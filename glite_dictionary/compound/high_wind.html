high wind

1. NOUN

/ haɪ wɪnd / (AmE), / haɪ wɪnd / (BrE)
a wind of great force or speed; a very strong wind
very strong wind

Examples

The high wind knocked out power lines across the city.
Sailing was cancelled due to the risk of high wind and rough waters.
During the storm, high wind made driving conditions extremely dangerous.

Synset Examples

rain and high winds covered the region

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gale - a very strong wind, typically 34–40 knots
