volume unit

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɑljum ˌjunɪt / (AmE), / ˈvɒljuːm ˌjuːnɪt / (BrE)
a unit used to measure the amount of space occupied by a substance or object
unit of measurement for volume

Examples

The scientist recorded the results in volume units to track changes in the liquid's capacity.
Many recipes list ingredients in volume units such as cups or milliliters.
The report converted all measurements to standard volume units for easier comparison.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: liter - a metric unit of capacity, equal to one cubic decimeter
  • Synonym: cubic meter - the SI unit of volume, equal to a cube with sides of one meter
