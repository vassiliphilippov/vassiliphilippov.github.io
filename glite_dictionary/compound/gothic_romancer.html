Gothic romancer

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɑːθɪk roʊˈmænsər / (AmE), / ˈɡɒθɪk rəʊˈmɑːnsə / (BrE)
a writer of Gothic romances, especially novels featuring mystery and the supernatural typical of the Gothic genre
writer of Gothic romances

Examples

Ann Radcliffe is considered a pioneering Gothic romancer whose novels set the standard for the genre.
The Gothic romancer skillfully wove suspense and supernatural elements into her stories.
In the 19th century, being a successful Gothic romancer often meant exploring themes of terror and decay.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: novelist - a writer of novels
  • Synonym: Gothic - related to the Gothic literary genre

Etymology

From 'Gothic romance', a literary genre popular in the 18th and 19th centuries, and 'romancer' meaning one who writes romances.
