brown sugar

1. NOUN

/ ˌbraʊn ˈʃʊɡər / (AmE), / ˌbraʊn ˈʃʊɡə / (BrE)
Sugar that is unrefined or only partly refined, retaining some molasses and typically brown in color.
unrefined or partly refined sugar with molasses

Examples

She sprinkled a little brown sugar on her oatmeal before eating breakfast.
The recipe calls for two cups of brown sugar to add moisture and flavor.
You can substitute brown sugar for white sugar in many baked goods for a richer taste.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: white sugar - sugar that has been fully refined and is white in color
