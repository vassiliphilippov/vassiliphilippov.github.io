bacillary dysentery

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæsəˌlɛri dɪsˈɛn.tə.ri / (AmE), / ˈbæsɪləri dɪsˈɛntəri / (BrE)
an acute intestinal infection caused by Shigella bacteria, marked by diarrhea, fever, and abdominal pain
intestinal infection caused by Shigella bacteria
medical

Examples

Bacillary dysentery is common in areas with poor sanitation and contaminated water supplies.
The doctor diagnosed the patient with bacillary dysentery after reviewing the test results.
Outbreaks of bacillary dysentery often lead to severe health problems in refugee camps.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: dysentery - general term for diseases causing severe diarrhea with blood, often due to infection

Etymology

Bacillary from Latin 'bacillus' (little rod) referring to rod-shaped bacteria; dysentery from Greek 'dusenteria' (dys- 'bad' + enteron 'intestine').
