high-risk

1. ADJ

/ haɪ rɪsk / (AmE), / haɪ rɪsk / (BrE)
involving a high likelihood of loss, failure, or danger, especially in financial or business contexts
involving a high chance of loss or danger

Examples

Investing in high-risk stocks can lead to both big gains and major losses.
Many insurance companies refuse to cover high-risk drivers because they are more likely to have accidents.
The company was criticized for making high-risk loans during the housing boom.

Synset Examples

a bad investment
high risk investments
anything that promises to pay too much can't help being risky
speculative business enterprises

Related Terms

  • Synonym: risky - involving the possibility of failure or loss
  • Antonym: safe - free from risk or danger
