anti-racketeering law

1. NOUN

/ ˌæn.tiː ræk.əˈtɪr.ɪŋ lɔ / (AmE), / ˌæn.tiː ræk.ɪˈtɪə.rɪŋ lɔː / (BrE)
A law enacted to combat organized crime by imposing strict penalties and allowing the seizure of assets gained through illegal activities.
law against organized crime and racketeering
legal

Examples

The government passed a new anti-racketeering law to cripple criminal organizations.
Many mobsters were prosecuted under the federal anti-racketeering law.
Lawyers debated the effectiveness of the anti-racketeering law in deterring white-collar crime.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: RICO Act - U.S. law targeting racketeering and organized crime

Etymology

Formed from 'anti-' (against) and 'racketeering' (engaging in organized illegal activities).
