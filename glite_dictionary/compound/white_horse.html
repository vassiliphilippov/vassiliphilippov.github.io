white horse

1. NOUN

/ waɪt hɔrs / (AmE), / waɪt hɔːs / (BrE)
a wave in the sea or ocean whose crest has been blown by the wind, causing it to break and appear white
wave with a white, breaking crest

Examples

The strong wind whipped up white horses on the surface of the sea.
As we sailed into open water, white horses danced along the horizon.
Children watched the white horses rolling in and crashing on the beach.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whitecap - a wave with a white, foamy crest
