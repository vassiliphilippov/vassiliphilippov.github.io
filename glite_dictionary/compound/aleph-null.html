aleph-null

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːlɛf ˈnʌl / (AmE), / ˌælef ˈnʌl / (BrE)
the cardinal number representing the smallest infinity; the cardinality of the set of natural numbers
smallest infinite cardinal number in mathematics

Examples

The set of natural numbers has cardinality aleph-null in set theory.
Mathematicians often use aleph-null to describe the size of infinite countable sets.
You cannot reach aleph-null by simply adding one to a finite number.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: aleph-one - the next higher cardinal number after aleph-null

Etymology

From Hebrew 'aleph' (the first letter of the Hebrew alphabet) and English 'null'.
