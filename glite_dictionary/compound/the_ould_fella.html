the ould fella

1. NOUN

/ ði oʊld ˈfɛlə / (AmE), / ði əʊld ˈfɛlə / (BrE)
(informal, chiefly Irish English) an old man, especially used affectionately to refer to one's father or an elderly male.
affectionate term for an old man (Irish English)
informal british

Examples

Whenever I needed advice, I'd go to the ould fella for a chat.
The ould fella always had a witty saying ready for any situation.
Last weekend, my brother and I took the ould fella fishing.

Synset Examples

The ould fella shared stories of his youth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: the old man - an affectionate term for one's father or an old man

Etymology

Irish English; 'ould' is a regional spelling of 'old'.
