Plott hound

1. NOUN

/ plɑt haʊnd / (AmE), / plɒt haʊnd / (BrE)
An American breed of hunting dog with a brindle coat, originally bred for hunting bears and wild boars.
American brindle hunting dog breed

Examples

The Plott hound is known for its determination and courage when hunting large game.
Many hunters in the southern United States rely on their Plott hound to track wild boar.
A brindle-coated Plott hound led the search party through the dense forest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hound - a type of dog bred for hunting

Etymology

Named after the Plott family, who developed the breed in North Carolina, United States.
