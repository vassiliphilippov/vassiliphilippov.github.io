naphthalene poisoning

1. NOUN

/ næfˈθælin ˈpɔɪzənɪŋ / (AmE), / næfˈθæliːn ˈpɔɪzənɪŋ / (BrE)
A toxic condition caused by inhaling or ingesting naphthalene, often resulting in symptoms like nausea, headache, and anemia.
toxic effect of naphthalene exposure
medical

Examples

Children are at risk of naphthalene poisoning if they accidentally swallow mothballs.
Chronic exposure to mothball fumes can lead to naphthalene poisoning.
The doctor suspected naphthalene poisoning after hearing about the patient's contact with mothballs.
