antipersonnel bomb

1. NOUN

/ ˌæn.tiˌpɝː.səˈnɛl bɑm / (AmE), / ˌæn.tiˌpɜː.səˈnɛl bɒm / (BrE)
a bomb designed to injure or kill people by producing many small, high-velocity fragments upon explosion
bomb intended to harm personnel with fragments

Examples

The military deployed an antipersonnel bomb to target enemy troops in the open field.
Fragments from the antipersonnel bomb spread over a wide area, causing extensive injuries.
International treaties have sought to restrict the use of the antipersonnel bomb because of its effects on civilians.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antipersonnel mine - a landmine intended to injure or kill people rather than vehicles

Etymology

From anti- (against) + personnel (people employed in an organization) + bomb.
