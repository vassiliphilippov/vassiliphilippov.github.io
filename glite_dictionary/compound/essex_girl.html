Essex girl

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛsɛks ɡɜrl / (AmE), / ˈɛsɪks ɡɜːl / (BrE)
A stereotype of a young woman from Essex, England, typically characterized as brash, materialistic, and lacking in intelligence or sophistication.
stereotypical young woman from Essex, England
british

Examples

The media often portrays the Essex girl as loud and flashy, but many find the stereotype unfair.
She laughed off the insult, refusing to be defined by the Essex girl label.
At the party, some guests joked about her accent in a playful nod to the Essex girl stereotype.

Synset Examples

The stereotype of an Essex girl often involves flashy fashion.
She embraced the Essex girl persona with confidence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Valley girl - stereotypical image of an affluent, superficial young woman from California's San Fernando Valley

Etymology

Named after Essex, a county in southeastern England.
