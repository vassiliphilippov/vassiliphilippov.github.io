conducting wire

1. NOUN

/ kənˈdʌktɪŋ waɪɚ / (AmE), / kənˈdʌktɪŋ waɪə / (BrE)
a wire made of metal that is used to carry electricity from one place to another
metal wire carrying electricity

Examples

The electrician replaced the old conducting wire to prevent power loss.
Aluminum is sometimes used as a conducting wire in power transmission lines.
Exposed conducting wire can be dangerous if touched.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conductor - material or device that allows electricity to flow through it
  • Antonym: insulated wire - wire with a non-conducting covering to prevent electrical contact
