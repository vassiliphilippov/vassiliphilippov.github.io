beat combo

1. NOUN

/ bit ˈkɑmˌboʊ / (AmE), / biːt ˈkɒm.bəʊ / (BrE)
a small musical group or band, especially one focused on rhythm and dance music, commonly associated with 1960s British popular music
1960s rhythm-focused pop group
british

Examples

The new beat combo attracted a large following among local teenagers.
Fans crowded the club to see the renowned beat combo on Saturday night.
In the 1960s, every town seemed to have its own beat combo.

Synset Examples

The popular beat combo performed at the local festival.
Everyone danced when the popular beat combo took the stage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: band - a group of musicians who play together
  • Synonym: ensemble - a group of musicians who perform together

Etymology

From 'beat' referring to rhythm-driven popular music styles of the 1960s, and 'combo', short for combination (meaning small band or group).
