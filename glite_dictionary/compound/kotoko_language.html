Kotoko language

1. NOUN

/ koʊˈtoʊkoʊ ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / kəʊˈtəʊkəʊ ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
a Chadic language spoken by the Kotoko people in areas south of Lake Chad, particularly along the lower Chari and Logone rivers
Chadic language of the Kotoko people

Examples

Linguists have documented the Kotoko language as part of the wider Chadic family.
Several villages in Cameroon and Chad use the Kotoko language as their primary means of communication.
Efforts are being made to preserve the Kotoko language among younger generations.
