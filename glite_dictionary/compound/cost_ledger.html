cost ledger

1. NOUN

/ kɑst ˈlɛdʒər / (AmE), / kɒst ˈlɛdʒə / (BrE)
a book or record in which a business's accumulated costs are classified and recorded in various categories
book recording classified business costs

Examples

The company maintained a detailed cost ledger to monitor all manufacturing expenses by department.
Auditors reviewed the cost ledger to ensure that indirect costs had been properly allocated.
Every entry in the cost ledger must be supported by appropriate documentation and receipts.
