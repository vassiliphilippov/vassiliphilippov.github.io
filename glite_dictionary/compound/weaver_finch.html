weaver finch

1. NOUN

/ ˈwiːvər ˌfɪntʃ / (AmE), / ˈwiːvə ˌfɪntʃ / (BrE)
A finch-like African or Asian bird that builds intricately woven nests and often lives in colonies.
African or Asian bird known for woven nests

Examples

The weaver finch constructs an intricate nest using blades of grass and twigs.
Large colonies of weaver finches can often be seen hanging from savanna trees.
Many ornithologists study the weaver finch because of its remarkable nest-building skills.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weaverbird - Any of several small passerine birds that weave elaborate nests
