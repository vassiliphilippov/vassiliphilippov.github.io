Hebrew Bible

1. NOUN

/ ˈhiːbru ˈbaɪbəl / (AmE), / ˈhiːbruː ˈbaɪbəl / (BrE)
the Jewish scriptures consisting of three divisions — the Torah, the Prophets, and the Writings
the Jewish scriptures (Torah, Prophets, Writings)

Examples

Many scholars study the Hebrew Bible to understand ancient Israelite history and religion.
The Hebrew Bible is foundational to both Judaism and Christianity.
Museum exhibits often include ancient manuscripts of the Hebrew Bible.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Tanakh - the canonical collection of Jewish texts, also known as the Hebrew Bible

Etymology

'Hebrew' from Old French *ebreu*, from Latin *Hebraeus*; 'Bible' from Greek *biblia* (books).
