state secret

1. NOUN

/ steɪt ˈsiːkrət / (AmE), / steɪt ˈsiːkrɪt / (BrE)
information that a government classifies as confidential for reasons of national security or public interest
government-classified confidential information

Examples

Revealing a state secret can have serious legal consequences.
She was accused of passing a state secret to foreign agents.
Unauthorized disclosure of a state secret is considered a major crime in many countries.

Synset Examples

Revealing a state secret can lead to severe consequences.
He was charged with leaking a state secret.
The documents contained a serious state secret.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: classified information - information officially designated as secret or confidential
  • Antonym: public knowledge - information that is not secret and is known by the public
