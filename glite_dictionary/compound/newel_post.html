newel post

1. NOUN

/ ˈnuːəl poʊst / (AmE), / ˈnjuːəl pəʊst / (BrE)
the upright post at the top or bottom of a staircase that supports the handrail
staircase post supporting the handrail

Examples

She gripped the newel post for support as she turned to descend the stairs.
The elegant newel post at the bottom of the staircase was carved from oak.
A child's toy was found tucked behind the newel post after years of being lost.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: baluster - a short pillar supporting the rail of a staircase
  • Synonym: handrail - a rail to be grasped as a support on a stairway

Etymology

From Old French *noel* (pole, post), ultimately from Latin *nodus* (knot, node).
