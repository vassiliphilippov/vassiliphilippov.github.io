capacitor mike

1. NOUN

/ kəˈpæsɪtɚ maɪk / (AmE), / kəˈpæsɪtə maɪk / (BrE)
A microphone that uses a capacitor with one fixed plate and one diaphragm that moves in response to sound waves.
microphone using a capacitor and diaphragm

Examples

The studio uses a capacitor mike to achieve high-fidelity recordings of vocals.
Many podcasters prefer a capacitor mike due to its sensitivity to subtle sounds.
He replaced the old dynamic mic with a new capacitor mike for better clarity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: condenser microphone - microphone using a capacitor principle

Etymology

From 'capacitor', an electrical component, and 'mike', informal for 'microphone'.
