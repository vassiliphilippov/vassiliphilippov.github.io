Job's comforter

1. NOUN

/ dʒoʊbz ˈkʌmfərtər / (AmE), / dʒəʊbz ˈkʌmfətə / (BrE)
a person who tries to offer consolation but instead makes the recipient feel worse
someone whose consolation causes more distress

Examples

Whenever I was feeling low, Mark acted like a Job's comforter instead of helping.
Instead of cheering me up, her comments made her sound like a real Job's comforter.
Everyone needs support, but nobody wants a Job's comforter in their time of need.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: false friend - a person who pretends to be comforting but is not truly helpful

Etymology

From the biblical story of Job, whose friends' attempts to comfort him only deepened his misery.
