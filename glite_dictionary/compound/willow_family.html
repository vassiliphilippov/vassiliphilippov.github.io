willow family

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪloʊ ˈfæməli / (AmE), / ˈwɪləʊ ˈfæmɪli / (BrE)
a family of trees and shrubs (Salicaceae) that includes willows (Salix) and poplars (Populus), characterized by hairy catkins
family of trees including willows and poplars

Examples

The willow family includes both willows and poplars, which are found throughout the Northern Hemisphere.
Many members of the willow family thrive in wet soils near rivers and streams.
Researchers studied the medicinal properties of plants in the willow family for pain relief.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Salicaceae - the botanical name for the family of willows and poplars
  • Antonym: rose family - a different family of flowering plants, the Rosaceae
