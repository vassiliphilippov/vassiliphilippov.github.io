rugger bugger

1. NOUN

/ ˈrʌɡər ˈbʌɡər / (AmE), / ˈrʌɡə ˈbʌɡə / (BrE)
A person who is passionately enthusiastic about rugby, especially as a player or devoted fan; often used informally and sometimes humorously.
enthusiastic rugby fan or player
informal slang british

Examples

My brother is such a rugger bugger—he travels to every away game with his team.
You can always spot a rugger bugger at the pub after a big match.
The university’s rugby team is full of proud rugger buggers who train together year-round.

Synset Examples

He's a true rugger bugger, always playing on weekends.
The rugger bugger in our group never misses a match.
Being a rugger bugger means you love the sport.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rugby fan - a person who supports or enjoys rugby
  • Synonym: rugger - slang term for rugby or a rugby player

Etymology

Formed from 'rugger' (British slang for rugby) and 'bugger' (British slang meaning chap or fellow, originally a vulgar term).
