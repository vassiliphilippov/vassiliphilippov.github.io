pied lemming

1. NOUN

/ paɪd ˈlɛmɪŋ / (AmE), / paɪd ˈlɛmɪŋ / (BrE)
A North American species of lemming with a white winter coat and greatly enlarged claws on some digits.
North American lemming with white winter coat

Examples

The pied lemming develops a striking white coat during the harsh Arctic winter.
Enlarged claws help the pied lemming dig through snow in search of food.
Scientists observed changes in the population of pied lemmings after a particularly severe winter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lemming - a small rodent native to Arctic regions, known for cyclic population booms

Etymology

From 'pied', meaning multicolored or variegated, and 'lemming', the name for small Arctic rodents.
