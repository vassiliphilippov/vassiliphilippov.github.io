glenoid fossa

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡlɛ.nɔɪd ˈfɑ.sə / (AmE), / ˈɡliː.nɔɪd ˈfɒs.ə / (BrE)
the shallow depression in the scapula that articulates with the head of the humerus to form the shoulder joint
depression in scapula forming the shoulder joint
medical

Examples

The surgeon carefully examined the glenoid fossa for signs of erosion before proceeding with the shoulder replacement.
A dislocated shoulder can occur when the head of the humerus slips out of the glenoid fossa.
Damage to the glenoid fossa can significantly affect the stability and motion of the shoulder joint.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scapula - the shoulder blade bone
  • Synonym: humerus - the bone of the upper arm or forelimb

Etymology

From Greek 'glenoidēs' (socket-like) and Latin 'fossa' (ditch, trench).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡliː.nɔɪd ˈfɑː.sə / (AmE), / ˈɡliː.nɔɪd ˈfɒs.ə / (BrE)
a deep hollow in the temporal bone of the skull that receives the condyle of the mandible, forming part of the jaw joint
depression in the skull for the jaw joint
medical

Examples

The dentist explained that pain near the glenoid fossa could indicate a temporomandibular joint disorder.
A fracture involving the glenoid fossa may affect jaw movement and require specialized treatment.
Detailed imaging of the glenoid fossa helped surgeons plan the reconstructive procedure.

Etymology

From Greek *glenoeides* (like a socket) and Latin *fossa* (ditch, trench).
