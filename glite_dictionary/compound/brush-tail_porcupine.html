brush-tail porcupine

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrʌʃ teɪl ˈpɔːrkjupaɪn / (AmE), / ˈbrʌʃ teɪl ˈpɔːkjʊpaɪn / (BrE)
a species of porcupine distinguished by a tuft of large, bristly hairs at the end of its tail
porcupine with a bristly tail tuft

Examples

The brush-tail porcupine is native to parts of Africa and Asia.
Researchers observed a brush-tail porcupine using its bristled tail to ward off predators.
Unlike other porcupines, the brush-tail porcupine has distinctive beaded bristles on its tail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: porcupine - a large rodent with spines or quills on its body
