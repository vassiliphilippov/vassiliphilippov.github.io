water clover

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔt̮ɚ ˈkloʊvɚ / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə ˈkləʊvə / (BrE)
A type of aquatic fern found in Europe, Asia, and eastern North America, characterized by leaves with four clover-like leaflets.
aquatic fern with clover-like leaves

Examples

The pond is home to several species, including the distinctive water clover.
Botanists collected samples of water clover from the marsh for study.
The leaves of a water clover plant float delicately on the surface.
