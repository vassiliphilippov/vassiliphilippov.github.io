managed economy

1. NOUN

/ ˈmænɪdʒd ɪˈkɑːnəmi / (AmE), / ˈmænɪdʒd ɪˈkɒnəmi / (BrE)
an economy in which the government plays a major role in allocating goods and resources and determining prices, rather than relying solely on market forces
economy with significant government intervention

Examples

Under a managed economy, the government decides which industries receive major investments and sets price controls.
The shift from a managed economy to a market-based system caused significant economic upheaval.
Many socialist countries have historically operated under a managed economy model.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: market economy - an economy in which market forces determine the allocation of resources and prices
