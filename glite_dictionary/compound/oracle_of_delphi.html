oracle of Delphi

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrəkəl əv ˈdɛlfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈɒrəkəl əv ˈdɛlfaɪ / (BrE)
in ancient Greek mythology, the shrine at Delphi where a priestess delivered cryptic messages and prophecies from Apollo to those seeking guidance
ancient Greek shrine of prophecy

Examples

Many ancient leaders visited the oracle of Delphi to receive guidance before important decisions.
The pronouncements from the oracle of Delphi were often enigmatic and required interpretation.
Historians continue to study the cultural impact of the oracle of Delphi in the Greek world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oracle - person or place regarded as a source of wise counsel or prophecy

Etymology

From Greek Δελφοί (Delphoi), the ancient sanctuary, and Latin oraculum (oracle).
