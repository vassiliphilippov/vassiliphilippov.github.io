alloy steel

1. NOUN

/ ˈæˌlɔɪ stil / (AmE), / ˈalɔɪ stiːl / (BrE)
A type of steel whose properties are determined by the presence of additional elements, such as chromium, nickel, or molybdenum, besides carbon.
steel with added elements for specific properties

Examples

Many modern bridges are built using alloy steel for enhanced strength and resistance to corrosion.
The addition of chromium makes alloy steel suitable for high-temperature environments.
Engine parts often use alloy steel because of its superior durability.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: carbon steel - steel that contains only carbon as the main alloying element
