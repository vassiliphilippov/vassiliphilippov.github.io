positive degree

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑzətɪv dɪˈɡri / (AmE), / ˈpɒzətɪv dɪˈɡriː / (BrE)
the base form of an adjective or adverb, expressing a quality without indicating comparison or intensity
basic adjective/adverb form; non-comparative

Examples

In the sentence 'He is tall,' the adjective 'tall' is in the positive degree.
Most English adjectives, such as 'happy,' appear first in their positive degree before forming comparatives and superlatives.
Understanding the positive degree is essential for constructing simple descriptive sentences.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: comparative degree - form of an adjective/adverb showing greater or lesser degree
  • Degree Higher: superlative degree - form showing the highest or lowest degree

Etymology

From Latin 'positivus' (settled, laid down) and 'gradus' (step, degree).
