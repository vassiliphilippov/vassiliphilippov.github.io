South American Indian

1. NOUN

/ saʊθ əˈmɛrɪkən ˈɪndiən / (AmE), / saʊθ əˈmɛrɪkən ˈɪndiən / (BrE)
a member of an indigenous people native to South America, especially before European colonization
indigenous person of South America

Examples

Many South American Indians have maintained their traditional ways of life despite external pressures.
The museum features exhibits about the art and history of South American Indians.
Anthropologists study the languages spoken by various South American Indian groups.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Native American - a member of any of the indigenous peoples of the Americas
  • Degree Lower: North American Indian - a member of any of the indigenous peoples of North America
