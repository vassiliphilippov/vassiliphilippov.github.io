Taklimakan Desert

1. PROPN

/ ˌtɑːklɪˈmɑːn ˈdɛzərt / (AmE), / ˌtæklɪˈmækən ˈdɛzət / (BrE)
a vast desert in western China, known for its dry climate and shifting sand dunes
a desert in western China

Examples

The Taklimakan Desert is one of the largest sandy deserts in the world.
Scientists have conducted climate research in the Taklimakan Desert to study its unique ecosystem.
Caravans once crossed the perilous Taklimakan Desert along the Silk Road route.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Gobi Desert - a large desert in northern China and southern Mongolia

Etymology

From Uyghur "Taklimakan" (the name of the desert), possibly meaning 'place of ruins' or 'once you go in, you won't come out.'
