epoxy resin

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈpɑksi ˈrɛzən / (AmE), / ɪˈpɒksi ˈrɛzɪn / (BrE)
a synthetic thermosetting polymer used mainly in strong adhesives, coatings, and laminates
synthetic thermosetting polymer

Examples

The table was coated with a layer of epoxy resin to give it a glossy finish.
Some artists use epoxy resin to create jewelry and decorative pieces.
The strength of epoxy resin makes it popular for repairing boat hulls and surfboards.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: epoxy - a type of adhesive or material based on epoxide resins
  • Antonym: polyester resin - a different type of synthetic resin used in plastics and composites

Etymology

From 'epoxy,' referring to the epoxide group, and 'resin,' from Latin 'resina' (resin).
