joint venture

1. NOUN

/ dʒɔɪnt ˈvɛntʃər / (AmE), / dʒɔɪnt ˈventʃə / (BrE)
A business arrangement in which two or more parties collaborate on a specific project, sharing resources, risks, and profits.
business project shared by partners

Examples

The two tech companies established a joint venture to develop new software for smart devices.
By forming a joint venture, both firms aimed to reduce risks while entering the foreign market.
Their joint venture with a local manufacturer allowed them to expand production quickly.

Synset Examples

a joint venture between the film companies to produce TV shows

Related Terms

  • Synonym: partnership - a business arrangement where parties manage and operate a business together

Etymology

From 'joint' (shared, combined) and 'venture' (business undertaking); first used in business contexts in the 20th century.
