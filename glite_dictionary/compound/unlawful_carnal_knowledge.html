unlawful carnal knowledge

1. NOUN

/ ʌnˈlɔfəl ˈkɑrnəl ˈnɑlɪdʒ / (AmE), / ʌnˈlɔːfəl ˈkɑːnəl ˈnɒlɪdʒ / (BrE)
illegal or forbidden sexual intercourse, especially as defined by law or social custom.
illegal or forbidden sexual intercourse
legal archaic

Examples

In the nineteenth century, people were sometimes prosecuted for unlawful carnal knowledge.
The book discussed cases involving unlawful carnal knowledge under Victorian law.
He was charged with unlawful carnal knowledge as defined by the court documents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fornication - voluntary sexual intercourse between people not married to each other
  • Synonym: adultery - voluntary sexual intercourse between a married person and someone who is not their spouse

Etymology

From Middle English 'carnal', from Latin 'carnalis' (fleshly); 'knowledge' as a euphemism for sexual intercourse.
