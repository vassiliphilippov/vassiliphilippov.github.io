paper nautilus

1. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪpər ˈnɔːtɪləs / (AmE), / ˈpeɪpə ˈnɔːtɪləs / (BrE)
A cephalopod mollusk of warm seas, the female of which produces a delicate, papery spiral shell.
marine mollusk with papery spiral shell

Examples

The paper nautilus is known for its beautiful, delicate shell that floats on the ocean surface.
Scientists have long studied the paper nautilus to understand how its shell is formed.
A paper nautilus was washed ashore during last night's storm, amazing beachgoers with its intricate design.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Argonaut - genus of pelagic octopuses whose females produce papery shell
