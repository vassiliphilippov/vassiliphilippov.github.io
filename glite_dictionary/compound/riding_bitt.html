riding bitt

1. NOUN

/ ˈraɪdɪŋ bɪt / (AmE), / ˈraɪdɪŋ bɪt / (BrE)
one of the large sturdy posts on a ship's deck used to secure the cable of a dropped anchor
large post for securing an anchor cable

Examples

The crew quickly wrapped the anchor cable around the riding bitt to secure the ship.
During heavy storms, the strength of the riding bitt was crucial for keeping the vessel steady.
Maintenance of the riding bitt is a regular part of the ship’s safety checks.
