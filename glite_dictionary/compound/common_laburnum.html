common laburnum

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːmən ləˈbɝːnəm / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən ləˈbɜːnəm / (BrE)
A small ornamental tree or shrub (Laburnum anagyroides) with hanging clusters of yellow flowers, often grown in gardens and parks.
ornamental tree with yellow flowers

Examples

The golden blossoms of the common laburnum lit up the garden in spring.
Many people admire the common laburnum for its long, hanging clusters of yellow flowers.
Caution is required when planting common laburnum because all parts are poisonous if ingested.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: laburnum - any tree or shrub of the genus Laburnum

Etymology

From the genus name Laburnum, Latin, plus 'common' for the typical species.
