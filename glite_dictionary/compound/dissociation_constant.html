dissociation constant

1. NOUN

/ dɪˌsoʊsiˈeɪʃən ˈkɑnstənt / (AmE), / dɪˌsəʊsɪˈeɪʃən ˈkɒnstənt / (BrE)
a measure of the equilibrium between a compound and its dissociated ions in solution, given as a constant value
chemistry: equilibrium constant for dissociation
medical

Examples

The dissociation constant of acetic acid in water determines its strength as a weak acid.
Scientists measured the dissociation constant to understand how tightly the drug binds to the receptor.
A low dissociation constant indicates strong binding between a ligand and a protein.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: association constant - equilibrium constant for association reactions

Etymology

From 'dissociation' + 'constant', used in chemistry since the late 19th century.
