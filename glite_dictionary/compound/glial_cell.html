glial cell

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡlaɪəl sɛl / (AmE), / ˈɡliː.əl sɛl / (BrE)
a type of cell in the nervous system that supports, protects, and maintains the function of neurons
supporting cell in the nervous system
medical

Examples

Scientists have discovered that glial cells play a key role in supporting neurons in the brain.
Damage to glial cells can lead to various neurological disorders.
Recent studies show that glial cells are involved in learning and memory processes.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: neuron - a nerve cell that transmits electrical and chemical signals in the nervous system

Etymology

From Greek 'glia' meaning glue, reflecting their supportive role.
