common American shad

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmən əˈmɛrɪkən ʃæd / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən əˈmɛrɪkən ʃæd / (BrE)
A shad species (Alosa sapidissima) native to the Atlantic coast of North America and introduced to the Pacific coast, valued as a food fish.
North American species of shad fish

Examples

The common American shad is prized by anglers during its annual spawning migrations.
Commercial fisheries along the East Coast rely heavily on the common American shad.
Scientists are studying the migration patterns of the common American shad to support conservation efforts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: American shad - A species of shad native to North America (Alosa sapidissima)
