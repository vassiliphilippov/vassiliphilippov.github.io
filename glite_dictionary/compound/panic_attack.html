panic attack

1. NOUN

/ ˈpænɪk əˌtæk / (AmE), / ˈpænɪk əˌtæk / (BrE)
A sudden period of intense fear or anxiety that usually occurs without warning and often causes physical symptoms such as rapid heartbeat or shortness of breath.
sudden episode of intense fear or anxiety
medical

Examples

She experienced a panic attack before giving her presentation at work.
The symptoms of a panic attack can be frightening and confusing.
After his first panic attack, he sought help from a therapist.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anxiety attack - a sudden episode of intense anxiety
