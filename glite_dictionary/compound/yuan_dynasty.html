Yuan dynasty

1. NOUN

/ juˈɑːn ˈdaɪˌnæsti / (AmE), / juːˈæn ˈdɪnəsti / (BrE)
the imperial dynasty of China that ruled from 1279 to 1368, established by the Mongols under Kublai Khan
imperial Chinese dynasty, 1279–1368

Examples

The Yuan dynasty was established by Kublai Khan after the conquest of the Southern Song.
During the Yuan dynasty, the capital of China was moved to present-day Beijing.
Many important technological advancements were made during the Yuan dynasty.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Ming dynasty - the dynasty that succeeded the Yuan dynasty in China
  • Degree Lower: Song dynasty - the Chinese dynasty preceding the Yuan

Etymology

From Mandarin Chinese 元朝 (Yuán Cháo), meaning 'origin' or 'beginning' dynasty.
