chicken on a stick

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɪkən ɑn ə stɪk / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪkɪn ɒn ə stɪk / (BrE)
grilled or cooked pieces of chicken served on a skewer, often sold as a snack at fairs, festivals, or food stalls
grilled chicken served on a skewer

Examples

At the street market, I bought a chicken on a stick for a quick lunch.
Many food stalls sell chicken on a stick at county fairs and sporting events.
She prefers chicken on a stick over other grilled foods because it's easy to eat on the go.

Synset Examples

They served the grilled chicken on a stick at the fair.
