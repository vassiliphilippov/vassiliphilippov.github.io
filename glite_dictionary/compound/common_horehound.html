common horehound

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːmən ˈhɔːrˌhaʊnd / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən ˈhɔːhaʊnd / (BrE)
a European herb (Marrubium vulgare) with hairy leaves and small white flowers, used in medicine and as a flavoring
European herb used in medicine and flavoring

Examples

She brewed a tea from common horehound to soothe her sore throat.
The garden bed was filled with aromatic herbs, including common horehound and mint.
Old remedies often featured extracts of common horehound as a cough suppressant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horehound - a plant of the genus Marrubium, especially the common horehound

Etymology

From Old English 'hārhūne', probably from 'hār' (hoary/grey) + 'hūne' (a plant); 'common' used for distinction.
