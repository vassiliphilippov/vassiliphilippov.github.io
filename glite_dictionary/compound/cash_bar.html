cash bar

1. NOUN

/ kæʃ bɑr / (AmE), / kæʃ bɑː / (BrE)
a bar at a party or event where guests pay for their drinks individually, rather than being provided drinks for free
bar at event where drinks must be paid for

Examples

The wedding reception featured a cash bar instead of complimentary drinks.
Guests were disappointed to find out there would be a cash bar at the company party.
If you plan on drinking, bring some money for the cash bar at the gala.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: open bar - a bar at an event where drinks are provided free to guests
