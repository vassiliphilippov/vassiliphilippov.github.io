valve-in-head engine

1. NOUN

/ ˈvælv ɪn ˈhɛd ˈɛn.dʒɪn / (AmE), / ˈvælv ɪn ˈhɛd ˈɛn.dʒɪn / (BrE)
an internal combustion engine with both the intake and exhaust valves located in the cylinder head
engine with valves in cylinder head

Examples

The mechanic explained that the car used a valve-in-head engine for better efficiency.
Many classic trucks from the 1950s were equipped with a valve-in-head engine.
When restoring old motorcycles, I often encounter the term valve-in-head engine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overhead valve engine - internal combustion engine with valves in the cylinder head, also known as OHV engine
