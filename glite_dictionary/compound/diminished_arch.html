diminished arch

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈmɪnɪʃt ɑrtʃ / (AmE), / dɪˈmɪnɪʃt ɑːtʃ / (BrE)
An arch in which the height is less than half its width, typically found in architectural structures.
an arch with height less than half its width

Examples

The old bridge featured a diminished arch that made its silhouette unique among river crossings.
In classical architecture, a diminished arch is less common than the semicircular or pointed forms.
The restoration involved carefully reconstructing the fallen stones of the diminished arch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arch - a curved symmetrical structure spanning an opening
