nickel-iron accumulator

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɪkəl ˈaɪərn əˌkjuːmjəˈleɪtər / (AmE), / ˈnɪkəl ˈaɪən əˌkjuːmjʊˈleɪtə / (BrE)
a rechargeable battery with a nickel oxide cathode, iron anode, and potassium hydroxide electrolyte, typically supplying about 1.2 volts per cell
type of rechargeable battery with nickel and iron electrodes

Examples

The nickel-iron accumulator was often used in early railway and mining applications due to its durability.
Although heavy, a nickel-iron accumulator can endure more charge cycles than many other battery types.
He replaced the faulty cell in the nickel-iron accumulator to restore power to the off-grid cabin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: storage battery - a device that stores electrical energy for later use
