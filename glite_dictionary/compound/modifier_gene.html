modifier gene

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑdɪˌfaɪɚ dʒin / (AmE), / ˈmɒdɪˌfaɪə dʒiːn / (BrE)
a gene that influences or alters the effect produced by another gene
gene altering another gene's effect

Examples

The presence of a modifier gene can reduce the severity of certain genetic disorders.
Scientists are researching how modifier genes may affect resistance to diseases.
A single modifier gene can change the way a trait appears in an organism.
