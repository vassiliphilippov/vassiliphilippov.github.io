country dancing

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌntri ˌdænsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkʌntri ˌdɑːnsɪŋ / (BrE)
a traditional folk dance style in which couples are arranged in sets or lines facing each other, performing coordinated steps
traditional folk dance with couples in sets or lines

Examples

They attended a country dancing class every Thursday evening at the village hall.
Country dancing was a popular social activity in 18th-century England.
The festival featured live music and traditional country dancing for everyone to join.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contra dance - a type of country dance with couples in two lines

Etymology

Combination of 'country' (referring to rural folk) and 'dancing'.
