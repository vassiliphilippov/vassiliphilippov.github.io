Oregon larch

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔr.ɪ.ɡən lɑrtʃ / (AmE), / ˈɒr.ɪ.ɡən lɑːtʃ / (BrE)
a tall tree of western North America with pale green, sharply pointed leaves and oblong cones, valued for its timber
tall timber tree of western North America

Examples

The Oregon larch is known for its straight trunk and high-quality wood.
Hikers in the Pacific Northwest often admire the unique foliage of the Oregon larch.
Several Oregon larch trees were planted to restore the damaged forest ecosystem.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: larch - a type of tree with needle-like leaves and cones
