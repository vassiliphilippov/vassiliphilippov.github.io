lesser hemlock

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛsər ˈhɛmlɑk / (AmE), / ˈlɛsə ˈhɛmlɒk / (BrE)
a European plant (Aethusa cynapium) resembling parsley, naturalized in America, that may cause nausea and poisoning if eaten
poisonous plant resembling parsley

Examples

The field was scattered with lesser hemlock, which some people easily mistake for wild parsley.
Ingesting even a small amount of lesser hemlock can lead to symptoms of poisoning.
Gardeners should take care to remove lesser hemlock from areas where children or pets play.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: hemlock - highly poisonous plant of the parsley family (Conium maculatum)
  • Synonym: fool's parsley - another name for Aethusa cynapium

Etymology

Named for its resemblance to the more poisonous hemlock; 'lesser' referring to its smaller size or lesser toxicity.
