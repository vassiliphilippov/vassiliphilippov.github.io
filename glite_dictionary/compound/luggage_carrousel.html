luggage carrousel

1. NOUN

/ ˈlʌɡɪdʒ kəˈruːsəl / (AmE), / ˈlʌɡɪdʒ kəˈruːsəl / (BrE)
a moving conveyor belt at an airport from which passengers collect their checked luggage after a flight
conveyor belt for baggage claim at airports

Examples

Passengers waited by the luggage carrousel for their suitcases to appear.
She realized her bag was missing when it failed to show up on the luggage carrousel.
Airport staff quickly removed an unclaimed bag from the luggage carrousel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: baggage claim - area where passengers collect luggage after a flight

Etymology

From French 'carrousel', meaning 'merry-go-round', referring to the rotating motion.
