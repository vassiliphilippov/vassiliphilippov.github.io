seismic shift

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪz.mɪk ʃɪft / (AmE), / ˈsaɪz.mɪk ʃɪft / (BrE)
A sudden, significant, or dramatic change, especially in ideas, beliefs, policies, or circumstances.
dramatic or fundamental change

Examples

The election represented a seismic shift in the country's political landscape.
There has been a seismic shift in public opinion on climate change over the last decade.
The merger brought a seismic shift in the structure of the entire industry.

Synset Examples

That discovery caused a seismic shift in our understanding.
Her promotion marked a seismic shift in team dynamics.
The company experienced a seismic shift in its strategy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: turning point - a time at which a decisive change takes place
  • Antonym: status quo - the existing state of affairs, especially regarding social or political issues

Etymology

Formed by analogy to geological seismic shifts (earthquakes), extending the meaning metaphorically to major changes.
