Captain Hindsight

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæp.tən ˈhaɪnd.saɪt / (AmE), / ˈkæp.tɪn ˈhaɪnd.sʌɪt / (BrE)
A person who points out mistakes or criticizes decisions only after the outcome is known.
one who criticizes with hindsight
informal

Examples

It's easy to be Captain Hindsight after the decision has already been made.
She didn't want to sound like Captain Hindsight, but she thought they should have planned ahead.
He acted as Captain Hindsight, blaming everyone after the project failed.

Synset Examples

Everyone loves to be Captain Hindsight during meetings, pointing out mistakes.
After the game, he became Captain Hindsight, criticizing the coach's decisions.
She played Captain Hindsight when she said we should have prepared better.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Monday morning quarterback - a person who criticizes or judges something after the event

Etymology

Popularized by the animated TV show 'South Park' (2010), playing on 'hindsight' and the superhero naming convention.
