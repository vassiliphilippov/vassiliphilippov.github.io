essential hypertension

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈsɛnʃəl haɪpərˈtɛnʃən / (AmE), / ɪˈsɛnʃəl haɪpəˈtɛnʃən / (BrE)
a form of high blood pressure that is persistent, has no identifiable cause, and is considered chronic and pathological
persistent high blood pressure with no known cause
medical

Examples

Patients diagnosed with essential hypertension often require long-term management to control their blood pressure.
Unlike secondary hypertension, essential hypertension develops without any clear underlying cause.
Doctors frequently encounter cases of essential hypertension in adults during routine check-ups.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: secondary hypertension - high blood pressure caused by an identifiable underlying condition
  • Synonym: hypertension - abnormally high blood pressure

Etymology

From Latin 'essentia' (being, essence) and Greek 'hyper-' (over) + 'tension' (pressure).
