map projection

1. NOUN

/ mæp prəˈdʒɛkʃən / (AmE), / mæp prəˈdʒɛkʃən / (BrE)
a method of depicting the curved surface of the Earth or another celestial body on a flat map using a systematic arrangement of lines of latitude and longitude
representation of a globe on a flat map

Examples

The Mercator map projection is commonly used for navigation because it preserves angles.
Each map projection distorts the Earth's surface in a different way.
Understanding map projection is essential for anyone studying cartography.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: projection - the process of representing a curved surface on a plane
