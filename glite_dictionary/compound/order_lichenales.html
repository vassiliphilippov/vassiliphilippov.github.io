order Lichenales

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdər laɪkəˈneɪliːz / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə laɪkəˈneɪliːz / (BrE)
a former taxonomic order used for certain lichen-forming organisms, now classified under the division Lichenes
historical taxonomic order of lichens

Examples

Many 19th-century botanists referred to certain lichen species as belonging to the order Lichenales.
Modern classifications have largely replaced the order Lichenales with the division Lichenes.
The term order Lichenales is now mainly encountered in historical texts on lichen taxonomy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Lichenes - division of lichen-forming organisms

Etymology

From New Latin: 'order' (taxonomic rank) + 'Lichenales' (from 'lichen', referring to the group of organisms).
