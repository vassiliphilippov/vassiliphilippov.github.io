catalyst for action

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæt̬.ə.lɪst fər ˈæk.ʃən / (AmE), / ˈkæt.ə.lɪst fə ˈæk.ʃən / (BrE)
an agent or event that provokes or speeds up activity, change, or progress in a particular situation
something that provokes change or activity

Examples

Her rousing appeal became a catalyst for action within the community.
The policy changes acted as a catalyst for action in the environmental movement.
Hope can be a powerful catalyst for action during challenging times.

Synset Examples

Her speech served as a catalyst for action among the crowd.
The new technology was a catalyst for economic development.
Education acts as a catalyst for social change.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catalyst - an agent that provokes or speeds significant change or action

Etymology

Figurative use of 'catalyst' (from chemistry, meaning a substance that speeds up a reaction) plus 'action.'
