small intestine

1. NOUN

/ smɔl ɪnˈtɛstɪn / (AmE), / smɔːl ɪnˈtɛstɪn / (BrE)
the long, narrow part of the digestive tract where most digestion and absorption of food occurs, between the stomach and the large intestine
part of the digestive tract between stomach and large intestine
medical

Examples

The small intestine is responsible for absorbing most of the nutrients from the food we eat.
After surgery, her small intestine needed time to recover before she could eat normally again.
The small intestine connects the stomach to the large intestine in the human digestive system.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: large intestine - the wider, lower part of the intestine leading to the anus

Etymology

From Latin 'intestinum','intestine' and Middle English 'smal', meaning 'narrow, not large'.
