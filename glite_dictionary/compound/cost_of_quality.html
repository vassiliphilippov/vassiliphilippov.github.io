cost of quality

1. NOUN

/ kɑst əv ˈkwɑləti / (AmE), / kɒst əv ˈkwɒləti / (BrE)
the total costs associated with ensuring and measuring product quality, including the costs of achieving good quality and the costs resulting from failing to achieve it
total quality-related costs

Examples

A detailed report will analyze the cost of quality for every department in the company.
Investing in prevention measures may actually reduce the overall cost of quality over time.
The manager explained how the hidden cost of quality can impact profitability.
