lemon balm

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛmən bɑm / (AmE), / ˈlɛmən bɑːm / (BrE)
a herb with lemon-scented leaves, used in teas, cooking, and as a flavoring
aromatic herb with lemon-scented leaves

Examples

She brewed a cup of lemon balm tea to help her relax before bed.
Chopped lemon balm adds a fresh flavor to summer fruit salads.
The garden was full of the fragrant scent of lemon balm after the rain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Melissa officinalis - scientific name for lemon balm

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɛmən bɑm / (AmE), / ˈlɛmən bɑːm / (BrE)
a bushy perennial herb of the mint family with small white or yellowish flowers and fragrant lemon-scented leaves, often grown in gardens and used for culinary or medicinal purposes
herb with lemon-scented leaves

Examples

He added a sprig of lemon balm to the tea for a refreshing citrus flavor.
Lemon balm grows easily in most gardens and attracts pollinators with its fragrant leaves.
Some people use lemon balm to flavor desserts or prepare soothing herbal infusions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Melissa officinalis - scientific name for lemon balm
  • Degree Lower: mint - aromatic herb in same family as lemon balm

Etymology

From 'lemon' (the fruit) and 'balm' (from Old French 'basme', from Latin 'balsamum', meaning aromatic resin).
