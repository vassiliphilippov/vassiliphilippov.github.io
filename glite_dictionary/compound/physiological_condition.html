physiological condition

1. NOUN

/ ˌfɪziəˌlɑdʒɪkəl kənˈdɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌfɪziəˌlɒdʒɪkəl kənˈdɪʃən / (BrE)
the state of the body or the way in which bodily functions are operating, especially with regard to health or normal functioning
state of the body or its functions

Examples

Regular exercise has a positive effect on your overall physiological condition.
Certain diseases can drastically alter the physiological condition of patients.
The study focused on how altitude impacts the physiological condition of athletes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: health - the general condition of a person's body or mind
  • Antonym: disease - an abnormal condition affecting the body

Etymology

From Greek 'physis' (nature) and Latin 'condicio' (condition, situation).
