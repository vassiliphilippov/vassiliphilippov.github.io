Grand River

1. PROPN

/ ɡrænd ˈrɪvər / (AmE), / ɡrænd ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
a river in southern Michigan, United States, which flows northwest to empty into Lake Michigan.
river in southern Michigan, US

Examples

The Grand River is the longest river in Michigan, stretching over 250 miles.
Many communities along the Grand River rely on it for recreation and transportation.
In spring, the banks of the Grand River are lined with wildflowers and migrating birds.
