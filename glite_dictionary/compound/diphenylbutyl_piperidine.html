diphenylbutyl piperidine

1. NOUN

/ ˌdaɪfəˌnilˈbjutɪl pɪˈpɛrəˌdin / (AmE), / ˌdɪfiːˌniːlˈbjuːtɪl pɪˈpɪrɪˌdiːn / (BrE)
a group of antipsychotic drugs chiefly used in the treatment of schizophrenia
antipsychotic drug group for schizophrenia
medical

Examples

Some potent antipsychotics belong to the diphenylbutyl piperidine class.
Researchers are studying the side effects of diphenylbutyl piperidine drugs.
Haloperidol is not a diphenylbutyl piperidine, but another drug group member may be prescribed.

Etymology

Formed from 'diphenyl' (two phenyl groups), 'butyl' (a four-carbon chain), and 'piperidine' (an organic chemical derived from piperidine).
