thrift institution

1. NOUN

/ θrɪft ˌɪn.stɪˈtuː.ʃən / (AmE), / θrɪft ˌɪn.stɪˈtjuː.ʃən / (BrE)
a financial institution, such as a savings bank or credit union, that encourages personal savings and home buying
financial institution for savings and mortgages

Examples

The new law tightened regulations on thrift institutions to protect consumers' savings.
She opened an account with a local thrift institution to save for her first home.
Many thrift institutions offer attractive interest rates for long-term savers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: savings bank - a bank that receives savings deposits and pays interest
  • Synonym: credit union - a cooperative financial institution owned by its members
