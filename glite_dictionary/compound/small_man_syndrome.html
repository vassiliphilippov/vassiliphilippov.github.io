small man syndrome

1. NOUN

/ smɔl mæn ˈsɪnˌdroʊm / (AmE), / smɔːl mæn ˈsɪnˌdrəʊm / (BrE)
a tendency for a man, especially one of shorter stature, to exhibit overly aggressive or domineering behavior as a way of compensating for perceived inferiority
aggressive behavior due to insecurity about short stature

Examples

Many believed the CEO's demanding management style was just small man syndrome in action.
He was accused of small man syndrome after losing his temper over minor issues.
Journalists often debate whether his assertiveness stems from small man syndrome or genuine confidence.

Synset Examples

She said his behavior was typical small man syndrome.
His aggressive attitude was a classic small man syndrome.
He often faced criticism for his small man syndrome.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Napoleon complex - similar concept describing compensatory behavior allegedly due to short stature
