family Sparganiaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌfæməli spɑːrˌɡæniˈeɪsiˌaɪ / (AmE), / ˌfæmɪli spɑːˌɡæniˈeɪsɪˌiː / (BrE)
A family of aquatic or marsh plants, coextensive with the genus Sparganium.
family of aquatic plants (Sparganium)

Examples

The family Sparganiaceae includes the aquatic plants known as bur-reeds.
Botanists previously classified Sparganium within the family Sparganiaceae before molecular studies.
Wetlands often contain representatives of the family Sparganiaceae thriving in shallow waters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sparganium - genus of aquatic plants known as bur-reeds

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'Sparganium' (the genus name) and '-aceae' (a standard suffix for plant families).
