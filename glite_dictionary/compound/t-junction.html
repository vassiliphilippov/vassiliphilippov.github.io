T-junction

1. NOUN

/ ˈti ˌdʒʌŋkʃən / (AmE), / ˈtiː ˌdʒʌŋkʃən / (BrE)
A place where one road or pipe meets another at right angles, forming the shape of the letter 'T'.
junction shaped like a 'T'

Examples

Turn left at the T-junction and follow the road for two miles.
The water pipe makes a sharp turn at the T-junction near the main valve.
There was a stop sign at the T-junction in the middle of the village.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: crossroads - a place where two or more roads intersect

Etymology

From the resemblance to the letter 'T', combined with 'junction' from Latin 'iunctio' (a joining).
