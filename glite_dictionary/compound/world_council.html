world council

1. NOUN

/ wɝːld ˈkaʊn.səl / (AmE), / wɜːld ˈkaʊn.səl / (BrE)
an international council composed of representatives from different nations, often to discuss or coordinate global issues
international council of representatives

Examples

The United Nations is often considered the most prominent world council addressing international concerns.
A new world council was established to coordinate efforts against climate change.
Membership in the world council provided the country with a voice in global policy decisions.
