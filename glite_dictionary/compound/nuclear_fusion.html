nuclear fusion

1. NOUN

/ ˈnuːkliɚ ˈfjuːʒən / (AmE), / ˈnjuːkliə ˈfjʊʒn̩ / (BrE)
A nuclear reaction in which two or more atomic nuclei combine to form a heavier nucleus, releasing energy in the process.
combining nuclei to release energy
medical

Examples

The process of nuclear fusion powers the sun and other stars.
Scientists are experimenting with nuclear fusion as a potential source of clean energy.
Achieving controlled nuclear fusion on Earth could revolutionize how we generate electricity.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: nuclear fission - A nuclear reaction in which a nucleus splits into lighter nuclei, releasing energy

Etymology

From Latin nucleus (kernel, core) and fusion (from Latin fusio, a pouring or melting).
