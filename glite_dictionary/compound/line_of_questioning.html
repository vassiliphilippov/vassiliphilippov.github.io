line of questioning

1. NOUN

/ laɪn əv ˈkwɛstʃənɪŋ / (AmE), / laɪn əv ˈkwɛstʃənɪŋ / (BrE)
a series of related questions intended to pursue or support a particular argument or topic
series of related questions with specific intent

Examples

The lawyer pursued a new line of questioning to expose inconsistencies in the witness's story.
During the interview, the reporter's line of questioning made the CEO visibly uncomfortable.
The judge asked the prosecutor to explain the relevance of her line of questioning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interrogation - the act of questioning, often formally
  • Synonym: cross-examination - formal questioning, especially in court, to challenge testimony
