ax head

1. NOUN

/ ˈæks hɛd / (AmE), / ˈæks hɛd / (BrE)
the metal cutting part at the end of an ax, used for chopping or splitting
metal cutting part of an ax

Examples

The blacksmith repaired the broken ax head for the lumberjack.
After years of use, the ax head became dull and needed sharpening.
She replaced the wooden handle but kept the original ax head.
