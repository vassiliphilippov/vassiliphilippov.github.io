Polaroid camera

1. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊləˌrɔɪd ˈkæmərə / (AmE), / ˈpəʊləˌrɔɪd ˈkæmərə / (BrE)
A type of camera that develops and produces a finished print of a photograph within seconds of taking the picture.
instant camera producing prints rapidly

Examples

She took a picture with her Polaroid camera and handed out instant prints to her friends.
Vintage Polaroid cameras have become popular collectibles among photography enthusiasts.
The Polaroid camera instantly produced a photo, capturing the moment perfectly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: instant camera - a camera that produces finished photographs within seconds after exposure

Etymology

From Polaroid, the trademark of the company that invented the instant camera, combined with camera.
