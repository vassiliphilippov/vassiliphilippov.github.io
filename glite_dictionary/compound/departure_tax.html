departure tax

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈpɑrtʃər tæks / (AmE), / dɪˈpɑːtʃə tæks / (BrE)
a tax imposed on travelers when leaving a country by land, sea, or air
tax charged when leaving a country

Examples

Many airports around the world charge a departure tax that is not always included in the ticket price.
Travelers should check if a departure tax must be paid in cash before leaving the country.
The government increased the departure tax to boost tourism revenue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exit tax - a tax levied on individuals or companies when leaving a country or jurisdiction
