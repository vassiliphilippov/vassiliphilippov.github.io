auditory meatus

1. NOUN

/ əˌdɔːdəri miˈeɪtəs / (AmE), / ɔːˌdɪtəri miˈeɪtəs / (BrE)
either of the passages in the outer ear extending from the auricle to the eardrum
passage in the outer ear
medical

Examples

The doctor examined the auditory meatus to check for signs of infection.
A build-up of earwax can block the auditory meatus and affect hearing.
Tumors in the auditory meatus are rare but can impact auditory function.

Etymology

From Latin 'auditorius' (of hearing) and 'meatus' (passage).
