genus Galium

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˈɡeɪliəm / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈɡeɪliəm / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the family Rubiaceae, commonly known as bedstraws and cleavers, comprising annual or perennial herbs.
genus of plants: bedstraws and cleavers

Examples

The genus Galium includes a wide variety of annual and perennial herbs found across different continents.
Many species within the genus Galium are known for their tiny, star-shaped flowers and whorled leaves.
Botanists have classified cleavers and bedstraw under the genus Galium due to their similar characteristics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bedstraw - Common name for various species in Galium genus
  • Synonym: cleavers - Common name for Galium aparine, a species in this genus

Etymology

From Latin 'Galium', the genus name, from Ancient Greek 'gala' (milk), because some species were used to curdle milk.
