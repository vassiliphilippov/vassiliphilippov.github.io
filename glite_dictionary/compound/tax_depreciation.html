tax depreciation

1. NOUN

/ tæks ˌdiːprɪˈʃieɪʃən / (AmE), / tæks ˌdiːpriˈʃeɪʃən / (BrE)
an amount allowed as a tax deduction to recover the cost of business assets over time
deduction of asset cost for tax purposes

Examples

The company claimed tax depreciation on its new equipment to reduce its taxable income.
You can use tax depreciation to spread the cost of major assets over several years.
Proper calculation of tax depreciation is essential for accurate financial reporting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: depreciation - reduction in the value of an asset over time
