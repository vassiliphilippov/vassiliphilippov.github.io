protozoal infection

1. NOUN

/ ˌproʊtəˈzoʊəl ɪnˈfɛkʃən / (AmE), / ˌprəʊtəʊˈzəʊəl ɪnˈfɛkʃən / (BrE)
an infection caused by protozoan organisms, typically single-celled parasites
infection caused by protozoa
medical

Examples

Malaria is a well-known example of a protozoal infection affecting millions worldwide.
Certain protozoal infections can cause severe gastrointestinal symptoms in humans.
The doctor suspected a protozoal infection after reviewing the patient's travel history and symptoms.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: bacterial infection - an infection caused by bacteria
  • Antonym: viral infection - an infection caused by a virus

Etymology

protozoal: from Greek protos (first) + zoon (animal); infection: from Latin infectio (pollution, contagion).
