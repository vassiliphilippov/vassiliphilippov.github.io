fig moth

1. NOUN

/ fɪɡ mɔθ / (AmE), / fɪɡ mɒθ / (BrE)
a small moth whose larvae feed on and create webbing in various stored vegetable products, including dried figs and fruits
a moth whose larvae infest stored vegetable products

Examples

The dried fruits in the pantry were ruined by an infestation of fig moth larvae.
Warehouse managers often inspect shipments carefully to prevent fig moth contamination.
Proper storage methods can reduce the risk of fig moth infestations in food supplies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meal moth - a moth whose larvae infest stored grain products
