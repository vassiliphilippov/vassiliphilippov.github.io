marsh hen

1. NOUN

/ mɑrʃ hɛn / (AmE), / mɑːʃ hɛn / (BrE)
A common name for certain marsh-dwelling birds, especially the American coot, found in North America.
North American marsh-dwelling bird, especially the coot

Examples

We spotted a marsh hen gliding quietly through the reeds.
The wetlands provide an ideal habitat for the elusive marsh hen.
Birdwatchers often hope to catch a glimpse of a marsh hen during their spring migration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coot - A medium-sized water bird with a black plumage and white bill, related to the rail family

2. NOUN

/ mɑrʃ hɛn / (AmE), / mɑːʃ hɛn / (BrE)
A small aquatic bird of the genus Gallinula, distinguished from rails by a frontal shield and a resemblance to domestic hens.
small aquatic bird with a frontal shield

Examples

A marsh hen darted between the reeds, its bright shield visible in the sunlight.
Many birdwatchers visit the wetlands each spring in hopes of spotting a rare marsh hen.
The distinctive call of the marsh hen echoed across the quiet marsh.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moorhen - another common name for Gallinula; a synonym
