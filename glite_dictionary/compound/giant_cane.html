giant cane

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒaɪənt keɪn / (AmE), / ˈdʒaɪənt keɪn / (BrE)
a tall grass (Arundinaria gigantea) native to the southern United States, often growing in dense thickets
tall grass of the southern United States

Examples

The riverbank was lined with thick stands of giant cane that provided cover for wildlife.
Historically, Native Americans used giant cane for weaving baskets and building shelters.
Many Southern wetlands feature giant cane as an important part of the ecosystem.
