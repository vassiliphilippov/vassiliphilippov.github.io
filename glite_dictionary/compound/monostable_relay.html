monostable relay

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɑnoʊˈsteɪbəl rɪˈleɪ / (AmE), / ˌmɒnəˈsteɪbəl rɪˈleɪ / (BrE)
an electrical relay that returns to its default position when the current is turned off
type of electrical relay that resets automatically

Examples

When the power is removed, the monostable relay returns to its normal position.
In the circuit diagram, a monostable relay is used for automatic reset.
The technician recommended using a monostable relay for safety reasons.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: bistable relay - a relay with two stable states

Etymology

From 'mono-' meaning 'single' and 'stable', reflecting its single stable state.
