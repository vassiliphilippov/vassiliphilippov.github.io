field artillery

1. NOUN

/ fiːld ɑrˈtɪləri / (AmE), / fiːld ɑːˈtɪləri / (BrE)
large, movable guns or howitzers used by armies on the battlefield, typically to support front-line troops with firepower, excluding antiaircraft weapons.
military guns used to support troops in the field

Examples

The general ordered the field artillery to target enemy fortifications.
During the battle, field artillery provided crucial support to the advancing infantry.
Modern field artillery units are equipped with highly mobile and accurate weaponry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: artillery - large-calibre guns used in warfare on land

Etymology

field (battle area) + artillery (from Old French artillerie, meaning 'equipment of war').
