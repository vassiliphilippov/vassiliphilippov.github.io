Nova lox

1. NOUN

/ ˈnoʊ.və lɑks / (AmE), / ˈnəʊ.və lɒks / (BrE)
Brine-cured salmon that is less salty than traditional lox, sometimes cured with sugar; commonly served on bagels.
mildly brined salmon used as a delicacy

Examples

He ordered a bagel with Nova lox and cream cheese for breakfast.
The deli is famous for its fresh Nova lox sandwiches.
Unlike traditional lox, Nova lox has a milder, less salty flavor.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: lox - salt-cured salmon, typically more salty than Nova lox

Etymology

'Nova' refers to Nova Scotia, where this style of cured salmon originated.
