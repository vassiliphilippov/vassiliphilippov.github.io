cownose ray

1. NOUN

/ ˈkaʊˌnoʊz reɪ / (AmE), / ˈkaʊnəʊz reɪ / (BrE)
a large species of ray (Rhinoptera bonasus) found in the western Atlantic Ocean, especially along the eastern coast of North America
a large ray found off eastern North America

Examples

We spotted a cownose ray gliding gracefully near the surface of the water.
The distinctive snout of the cownose ray helps distinguish it from other species of rays.
Fishermen must be cautious, as the barb on a cownose ray can cause a painful injury.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ray - a type of cartilaginous fish with flattened bodies and enlarged pectoral fins

Etymology

Named for the distinctive cow-like shape of its snout.
