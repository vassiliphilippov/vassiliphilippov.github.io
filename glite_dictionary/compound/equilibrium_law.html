equilibrium law

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌkwɪlɪˈbrɪəm lɔ / (AmE), / ɪˌkwiːlɪˈbrɪəm lɔː / (BrE)
(Chemistry) The principle that, at chemical equilibrium in a reversible reaction, the ratio of the rate of the forward reaction to the rate of the reverse reaction is constant for that reaction.
chemical principle about rates at equilibrium
medical

Examples

The equilibrium law helps chemists predict the concentrations of substances in a reaction at equilibrium.
According to the equilibrium law, the reaction rates become balanced at a constant ratio.
Understanding the equilibrium law is essential for calculating equilibrium constants in chemistry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: law of mass action - Principle stating the rate of a chemical reaction is proportional to the concentration of reactants
