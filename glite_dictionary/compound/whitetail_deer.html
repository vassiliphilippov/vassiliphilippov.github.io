whitetail deer

1. NOUN

/ ˈwaɪtˌteɪl dɪr / (AmE), / ˈwaɪtteɪl dɪə / (BrE)
a common North American deer (Odocoileus virginianus) with a tail that has a white underside, raised when alarmed
common North American deer with white tail underside

Examples

A whitetail deer darted across the road just as we were driving through the forest.
Hunters often search for whitetail deer tracks in the early morning after a fresh snowfall.
The whitetail deer is easily recognized by its distinctive tail and graceful movement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mule deer - a large deer of western North America with large ears
