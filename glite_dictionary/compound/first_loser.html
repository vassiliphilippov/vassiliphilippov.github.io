first loser

1. NOUN

/ fɜrst ˈluːzər / (AmE), / fɜːst ˈluːzə / (BrE)
(informal, humorous) The person or team that finishes second in a competition; the runner-up.
humorous term for runner-up
informal

Examples

After losing the championship match, he was jokingly called the first loser by his friends.
She received a silver medal and laughed about being the first loser.
While disappointed not to win, he still preferred being the first loser to finishing last.

Synset Examples

She joked that being runner-up makes you the first loser.
He felt like the first loser after missing out on the promotion.
In sports, they say second place is the first loser.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: runner-up - a person or team taking second place in a competition
