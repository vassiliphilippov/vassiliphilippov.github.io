family Mucoraceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌfæməli mjuːkɔːˈreɪsiː / (AmE), / ˌfæmɪli mjuːkɔːˈreɪsiː / (BrE)
A large family of mainly saprophytic fungi, including many molds that are destructive to food products.
family of common saprophytic fungi
medical

Examples

The family Mucoraceae includes many species that grow on decaying organic matter.
Several molds in the family Mucoraceae are notorious for spoiling stored grains and bread.
Researchers are studying the family Mucoraceae due to its economic impact on food preservation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mucor - a genus of molds within the family Mucoraceae

Etymology

From New Latin 'Mucoraceae', based on 'Mucor' (a genus of molds) plus '-aceae' (a family suffix in taxonomy).
