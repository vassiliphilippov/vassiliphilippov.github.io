red dogwood

1. NOUN

/ rɛd ˈdɔɡˌwʊd / (AmE), / rɛd ˈdɒɡˌwʊd / (BrE)
A European deciduous shrub with leaves that turn red in autumn and dull white flowers, commonly grown for ornamental purposes.
European shrub with red leaves in autumn

Examples

The leaves of the red dogwood turn a brilliant crimson every autumn.
Gardeners often plant red dogwood for its colorful stems and pretty spring blossoms.
A hedge of red dogwood lined the walkway, attracting birds with its berries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dogwood - A tree or shrub of the genus Cornus, with decorative flowers and brightly colored stems or berries

2. NOUN

/ rɛd ˈdɔɡˌwʊd / (AmE), / rɛd ˈdɒɡˌwʊd / (BrE)

North American shrub with reddish twigs and white flowers

Examples

The red dogwood in her yard blooms beautifully every spring.
Gardeners value the red dogwood for its striking winter twigs and attractive flowers.
We planted a red dogwood near the fence to add color to our landscape.
