lens cortex

1. NOUN

/ lɛnz ˈkɔrtɛks / (AmE), / lɛnz ˈkɔːtɛks / (BrE)
the outer layer of tissue surrounding the nucleus of the lens in the eye
outer tissue of the eye's lens
medical

Examples

A cataract can develop in the lens cortex, causing vision to become blurred.
Research has shown that the lens cortex becomes less flexible with age.
Swelling of the lens cortex is often seen in certain types of eye trauma.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: lens nucleus - the central core of the lens in the eye

Etymology

From Latin 'lens' (a lentil; the eye lens) and 'cortex' (bark, outer covering).
