black knapweed

1. NOUN

/ blæk ˈnæpˌwid / (AmE), / blæk ˈnapˌwiːd / (BrE)
a perennial plant with tough, wiry stems and purple flower heads, native to Europe but now widespread in many areas
a European perennial plant with purple flowers

Examples

We found black knapweed growing wild along the country lane.
The field was dotted with the bright purple blooms of black knapweed.
Gardeners often remove black knapweed because it can become invasive.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: knapweed - any plant of the genus Centaurea

Etymology

From 'black' (color) and 'knapweed', an Old English term for several plants of the Centaurea genus.
