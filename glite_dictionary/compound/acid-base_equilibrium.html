acid-base equilibrium

1. NOUN

/ ˈæsɪd beɪs ˌiːkwəˈlɪbriəm / (AmE), / ˈæsɪd beɪs ˌiːkwɪˈlɪbrɪəm / (BrE)
the physiological balance between acids and bases in the body, essential for maintaining normal bodily functions
balance between acids and bases in the body
medical

Examples

Disturbances in acid-base equilibrium can lead to serious health problems.
The kidneys play a key role in regulating acid-base equilibrium in the body.
Doctors monitor acid-base equilibrium closely in patients with respiratory disorders.

Synset Examples

with a normal acid-base balance in the body the blood is slightly alkaline

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acid-base balance - the maintenance of the proper proportions of acids and bases in the body

Etymology

Formed from 'acid', 'base', and 'equilibrium', from Latin 'acidus' (sour), Middle English 'base' (alkali), and Latin 'aequilibrium' (balance).
