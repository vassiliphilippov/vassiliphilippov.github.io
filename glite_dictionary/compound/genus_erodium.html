genus Erodium

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ɪˈroʊ.di.əm / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ɪˈrəʊ.di.əm / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants, known as heron's-bills, native to Europe, South America, and Australia, especially in mountainous regions.
a genus of flowering plants (heron's-bills)

Examples

The genus Erodium includes several species commonly found in rocky alpine habitats.
Botanists recently discovered a new member of the genus Erodium in Patagonia.
Many gardeners appreciate the delicate flowers of plants in the genus Erodium.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Erodium - the genus of plants known as heron's-bill
  • Degree Higher: geranium - closely related genus of flowering plants (cranesbill)
  • Synonym: Pelargonium - another related genus within the Geraniaceae family

Etymology

From Greek *erōdios* (heron), referring to the beak-like shape of the fruit.
