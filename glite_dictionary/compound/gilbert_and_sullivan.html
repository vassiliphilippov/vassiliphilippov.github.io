Gilbert and Sullivan

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɪlbɚt ənd ˈsʌlɪvən / (AmE), / ˈɡɪlbət ənd ˈsʌlɪvən / (BrE)
the collaborative musical works, especially comic operas, created by the English dramatist W. S. Gilbert and composer Arthur Sullivan
collaborative works of Gilbert and Sullivan

Examples

She grew up listening to Gilbert and Sullivan on the radio every Sunday.
Many schools perform Gilbert and Sullivan operettas because they are light and comic.
He has a complete collection of Gilbert and Sullivan scores in his library.

Synset Examples

he could sing all of Gilbert and Sullivan

Etymology

Named after W. S. Gilbert (1836–1911) and Arthur Sullivan (1842–1900), English dramatist and composer.
