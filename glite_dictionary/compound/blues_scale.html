blues scale

1. NOUN

/ bluːz skeɪl / (AmE), / bluːz skeɪl / (BrE)
a musical scale characterized by intervals that alternate between major and minor, commonly used in jazz and blues music
musical scale used in blues and jazz

Examples

Many guitarists learn the blues scale early on to create expressive solos.
The pianist improvised using the blues scale during his performance.
Jazz musicians often rely on the blues scale for a distinctive sound.

Synset Examples

There are no notes in the minor pentatonic that are not in blues scale for the same scale, but a true blues scale contains a flatted fifth.

Related Terms
