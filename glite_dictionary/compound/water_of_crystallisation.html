water of crystallisation

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔtɚ əv ˌkrɪstəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə əv ˌkrɪstəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
water molecules that are an integral part of the structure of a crystalline compound, typically present in a definite proportion
water bound in crystals of compounds
medical

Examples

The blue color of copper sulfate crystals is due to the water of crystallisation present in the compound.
Heating removes the water of crystallisation, causing the salt to lose its crystalline appearance.
Alum loses its water of crystallisation on exposure to dry air, becoming powdery.

Etymology

From the process noun 'crystallisation', based on 'crystal' from Greek 'krustallos' (ice, crystal).
