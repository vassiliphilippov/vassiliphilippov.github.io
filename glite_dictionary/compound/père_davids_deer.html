Père David's deer

1. NOUN

/ pɛr dəˈvidz dɪr / (AmE), / pɛə dəˈviːdz dɪə / (BrE)
a large species of deer from China, now surviving only in captivity, known for its unique antlers and named after the French missionary and naturalist Armand David
large Chinese deer species, now only in captivity

Examples

The Père David's deer once roamed the wetlands of China but today lives only in managed reserves.
Conservationists are working to reintroduce Père David's deer into its native habitat.
A herd of Père David's deer grazed peacefully by the lakeshore at the wildlife park.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deer - hoofed ruminant mammals of the family Cervidae

Etymology

Named after Père (Father) Armand David (1826–1900), a French missionary and naturalist who first described the species.
