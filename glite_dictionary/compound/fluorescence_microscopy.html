fluorescence microscopy

1. NOUN

/ flʊˈrɛsəns maɪˈkrɑːskəpi / (AmE), / flʊəˈrɛsəns mʌɪˈkrɒskəpi / (BrE)
a method of light microscopy in which a specimen is illuminated with specific wavelengths to excite fluorescent dyes, allowing the visualization of structures by their emitted light
microscopy using fluorescent dyes and light
medical

Examples

Fluorescence microscopy allows scientists to study the distribution of proteins within living cells.
Advances in fluorescence microscopy have revolutionized our understanding of cellular processes.
To observe the labeled antibodies, researchers used fluorescence microscopy for visualization.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: confocal microscopy - a type of optical imaging increasing resolution by using point illumination and spatial pinhole detection

Etymology

From 'fluorescence' (from Latin 'fluor', a mineral, and 'escence', indicating process) and 'microscopy' (from Greek 'mikros' meaning small, and 'skopein' meaning to look at).
