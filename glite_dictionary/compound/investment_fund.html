investment fund

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈvɛstmənt fʌnd / (AmE), / ɪnˈvɛstmənt fʌnd / (BrE)
a supply of money pooled by investors and managed collectively to earn a profit, often by investing in stocks, bonds, or other assets
pooled money for collective investment

Examples

Many people choose to put their savings into investment funds for potential growth.
The government encourages the creation of investment funds to support small businesses.
Global investment funds are attracted to emerging markets for higher returns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mutual fund - an investment fund that pools money from many investors to purchase securities
  • Degree Higher: hedge fund - an investment fund that employs complex strategies to maximize returns

Etymology

From 'investment,' from Latin investire (to clothe, dress), and 'fund,' from Latin fundus (bottom, foundation).
