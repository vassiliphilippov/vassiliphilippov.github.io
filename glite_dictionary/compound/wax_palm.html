wax palm

1. NOUN

/ wæks pɑm / (AmE), / waks pɑːm / (BrE)
a South American palm tree whose trunk yields a wax similar to carnauba wax
South American palm yielding wax

Examples

The wax palm towers over the Andean landscape, reaching impressive heights.
Harvesters collect the wax coating from the trunk of the wax palm to make polishes.
The wax palms in Colombia are protected due to their ecological importance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carnauba palm - a Brazilian palm that produces carnauba wax

2. NOUN

/ wæks pɑm / (AmE), / waks pɑːm / (BrE)
A tall palm tree native to the Andes, known for producing a resinous wax used in making candles.
Andean palm tree yielding resinous wax

Examples

The wax palm is the national tree of Colombia.
Local communities harvest wax from the trunk of the wax palm to make candles.
The towering wax palm stands out against the Andes mountains.

3. NOUN

/ wæks pɑm / (AmE), / wæks pɑːm / (BrE)
A Brazilian palm tree with edible roots, used as a source of leaf fiber and a brittle yellowish wax.
Brazilian palm yielding fiber and wax

Examples

The wax palm is prized for the valuable fiber extracted from its leaves.
Local farmers harvest the yellowish wax produced by the wax palm for industrial use.
The wax palm thrives in certain regions of Brazil and supports several small economies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carnauba palm - Brazilian palm tree also known for producing carnauba wax
