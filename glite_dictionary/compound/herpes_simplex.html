herpes simplex

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɝːpiːz ˈsɪm.plɛks / (AmE), / ˈhɜː.piːz ˈsɪm.pleks / (BrE)
a virus from the herpesvirus family that causes skin and nerve infections such as cold sores or genital herpes
herpes virus causing skin and nerve infection
medical

Examples

Many people are infected with herpes simplex without ever showing symptoms.
Cold sores are usually caused by the herpes simplex virus type 1.
Doctors can prescribe antiviral medication to help control herpes simplex outbreaks.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: herpes zoster - a herpesvirus causing shingles, also known as shingles virus

Etymology

From Greek 'herpēs' (to creep), referring to spreading nature, and Latin 'simplex' (simple, single).

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɝːpiːz ˈsɪmˌplɛks / (AmE), / ˈhɜːpiːz ˈsɪmplɛks / (BrE)
A viral infection caused by the herpes simplex virus, characterized by small, often painful blisters on the skin or mucous membranes.
viral infection causing skin blisters
medical

Examples

The doctor confirmed that the painful blisters were caused by herpes simplex.
Many people are unaware that herpes simplex can also infect the eyes.
Antiviral medication can help reduce the frequency of herpes simplex outbreaks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cold sore - A blister on or near the lips caused by the herpes simplex virus
  • Antonym: herpes zoster - A viral infection causing shingles, different from herpes simplex

Etymology

From Greek 'herpēs' (creeping) and Latin 'simplex' (simple, single).
