Pearson product-moment correlation coefficient

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪrsən ˌprɑdʌkt ˈmoʊmənt kəˌrɔləˈʃən koʊˈɪfɪʃənt / (AmE), / ˈpɜːsən ˌprɒdʌkt ˈməʊmənt kəˌrɛlɪˈeɪʃən kəʊˈɪfɪʃənt / (BrE)
a statistical measure of the strength and direction of a linear relationship between two variables
statistical measure of linear correlation

Examples

Researchers calculated the Pearson product-moment correlation coefficient to examine the relationship between test scores and hours studied.
When the Pearson product-moment correlation coefficient is close to 1, it suggests a strong positive linear association.
You can use the Pearson product-moment correlation coefficient to analyze whether two variables are linearly connected.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: correlation coefficient - a statistical measure that describes the strength and direction of a relationship between two variables

Etymology

Named after English statistician Karl Pearson (1857–1936).
