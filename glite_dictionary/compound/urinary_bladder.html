urinary bladder

1. NOUN

/ ˈjʊrəˌnɛri ˈblædər / (AmE), / ˈjʊərɪnəri ˈblædə / (BrE)
a membranous sac in which urine is temporarily stored before being discharged from the body
organ storing urine temporarily
medical

Examples

The urinary bladder can expand significantly to accommodate increasing amounts of urine.
Infections of the urinary bladder are common, especially in women.
During surgery, the doctor carefully examined the patient's urinary bladder for any abnormalities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bladder - a sac-shaped organ for holding fluids in the body

Etymology

From Latin 'urinarius' (of urine) and Old English 'blædre' (bladder).
