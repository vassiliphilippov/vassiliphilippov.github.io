rough fish

1. NOUN

/ rʌf fɪʃ / (AmE), / rʌf fɪʃ / (BrE)
A fish regarded as undesirable for food, sport, or bait, especially in North America.
fish undesirable for food or sport

Examples

Many anglers ignore rough fish while targeting more popular game species.
The lake is home to several types of rough fish that are not commonly eaten.
Conservationists debate the management of native rough fish populations.
