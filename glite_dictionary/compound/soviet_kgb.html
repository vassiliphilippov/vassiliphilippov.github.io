Soviet KGB

1. PROPN

/ ˈsoʊvi.ɪt ˌkeɪ dʒiː ˈbiː / (AmE), / ˈsəʊvɪət ˌkeɪ dʒiː ˈbiː / (BrE)
the main security and intelligence agency of the former Soviet Union, responsible for state security, intelligence, and secret police activities
security agency of the former Soviet Union

Examples

During the Cold War, the Soviet KGB was notorious for conducting espionage activities worldwide.
The Soviet KGB played a crucial role in maintaining internal security within the USSR.
Former agents of the Soviet KGB have published memoirs revealing the agency's secret operations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: secret police - a police force operating in secret to control or suppress dissent

Etymology

KGB from Russian 'Komitet Gosudarstvennoy Bezopasnosti' meaning 'Committee for State Security'.
