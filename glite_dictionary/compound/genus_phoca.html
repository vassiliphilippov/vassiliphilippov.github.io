genus Phoca

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈfoʊ.kə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈfəʊ.kə / (BrE)
The type genus of the Phocidae family, comprising the true earless seals found in the Northern Hemisphere.
the genus of earless or true seals

Examples

The harbor seal is a member of the genus Phoca, which includes several northern hemisphere species.
Marine biologists study various animals within the genus Phoca to understand seal evolution.
Species in the genus Phoca are commonly known as true seals or earless seals.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Phocidae - the family of earless seals to which genus Phoca belongs
  • Synonym: seal - common name for marine mammals of the Phocidae family

Etymology

From Latin 'Phoca', from Greek 'phōkē' meaning 'seal'.
