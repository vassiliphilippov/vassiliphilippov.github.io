ultrasound scanner

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌltrəˌsaʊnd ˈskænər / (AmE), / ˈʌltrəsaund ˈskanə / (BrE)
A device that uses ultrasonic waves to produce images of internal body structures, often used to monitor pregnancies or diagnose medical conditions.
device producing images with ultrasonic waves
medical

Examples

The radiologist used an ultrasound scanner to capture detailed images of the patient's liver.
Modern ultrasound scanners can provide three-dimensional images of an unborn baby.
Before the surgery, doctors checked the area with an ultrasound scanner for any abnormalities.

Synset Examples

Images from the ultrasound scanner are transferred and displayed using the DICOM standard.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ultrasound - sound waves with frequencies above the audible range, used in medical imaging
  • Synonym: sonogram - an image produced by ultrasound scanning
