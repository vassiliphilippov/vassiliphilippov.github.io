grass snake

1. NOUN

/ ɡræs sneɪk / (AmE), / ɡrɑːs sneɪk / (BrE)
a harmless European snake (Natrix natrix) with a yellow collar, commonly found in grassy areas in England and elsewhere in Europe
non-venomous European snake with a yellow collar

Examples

We spotted a grass snake slithering quietly through the reeds by the pond.
The grass snake is easily recognized by the yellow collar around its neck.
Unlike vipers, the grass snake is harmless and does not pose a threat to humans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: garter snake - a common North American non-venomous snake

2. NOUN

/ ɡræs sneɪk / (AmE), / ɡrɑːs sneɪk / (BrE)
a small, non-venomous green snake found in North America, primarily feeding on insects
North American green insect-eating snake

Examples

We spotted a grass snake slithering quietly through the tall weeds behind the barn.
The grass snake is harmless to humans and feeds mainly on insects and small amphibians.
Children at the nature camp learned how to identify a grass snake by its bright green color.

3. NOUN

/ ɡræs sneɪk / (AmE), / ɡrɑːs sneɪk / (BrE)
A harmless, nonvenomous snake, typically with longitudinal stripes, found in North and Central America.
nonvenomous striped snake

Examples

The grass snake basked in the morning sun near the edge of the pond.
Many gardeners welcome the presence of a grass snake because it keeps pests away.
Children were excited to spot a few grass snakes slithering through the tall grass in the field.
