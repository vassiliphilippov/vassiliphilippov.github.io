two-sided argument

1. NOUN

/ tuː ˈsaɪdɪd ˈɑːrɡjəmənt / (AmE), / tuː ˈsaɪdɪd ˈɑːɡjʊmənt / (BrE)
a discussion or debate in which two opposing viewpoints are presented
debate with two opposing views

Examples

The meeting quickly turned into a two-sided argument over how to spend the extra budget.
A two-sided argument allows people to consider both the benefits and drawbacks of a proposal.
During the election campaign, every major issue sparked a two-sided argument on television.

Synset Examples

They had a two-sided argument about the new policy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: debate - a formal discussion on a particular topic with opposing arguments
  • Synonym: discussion - the process of talking about something with others
  • Antonym: one-sided argument - an argument that presents only one point of view
