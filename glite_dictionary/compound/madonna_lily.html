Madonna lily

1. NOUN

/ məˈdɑnə ˈlɪli / (AmE), / məˈdɒnə ˈlɪli / (BrE)
A species of lily native to the eastern Mediterranean and the Balkans, known for its broad, funnel-shaped white flowers.
a white-flowered lily species native to the eastern Mediterranean

Examples

The Madonna lily is prized for its pure white blossoms and elegant shape.
Gardeners often plant the Madonna lily in spring for summer blooms.
Legends say the Madonna lily was associated with the Virgin Mary due to its beauty and symbolism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lily - a plant of the genus Lilium with large, prominent flowers

Etymology

From 'Madonna', a title of the Virgin Mary, and 'lily'; the plant's white flowers symbolize purity.
