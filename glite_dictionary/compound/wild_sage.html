wild sage

1. NOUN

/ waɪld seɪdʒ / (AmE), / waɪld seɪdʒ / (BrE)
A Eurasian perennial plant with blue flowers and leaves resembling those of verbena, especially found naturalized in the United States; commonly refers to several wild-growing species of sage.
Eurasian plant with blue flowers, similar to verbena

Examples

The garden was bordered by a row of wild sage with striking blue flowers.
Botanists have observed that wild sage thrives in the region's dry, open fields.
She picked a few leaves of wild sage to add a pleasant aroma to her bouquet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sage - a plant of the genus Salvia, especially used as a culinary herb
