radio dispatch

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪdioʊ dɪˈspætʃ / (AmE), / ˈreɪdiəʊ dɪˈspætʃ / (BrE)
A message or report transmitted by radio, especially as sent from a location to a central office or to the public.
message sent via radio transmission

Examples

The officer immediately responded to the radio dispatch about the robbery in progress.
She listened to the breaking news radio dispatch while driving to work.
A radio dispatch alerted all nearby units to the emergency situation.

Synset Examples

A radio dispatch gets its veracity from the authentic sound of the reporter being in the scene — even interacting with others in the scene.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dispatch - an official report or message

Etymology

Formed from 'radio', from Latin 'radius' (ray, beam), and 'dispatch', from Italian 'dispacciare'.
