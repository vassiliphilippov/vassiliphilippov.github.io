calcium chloride

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæl.si.əm ˈklɔːr.aɪd / (AmE), / ˌkælsiəm ˈklɔːraɪd / (BrE)
a white, deliquescent salt (CaCl₂) used for de-icing roads and as a drying agent
a salt used for de-icing and drying

Examples

The maintenance crew used calcium chloride to melt the ice on the sidewalks.
To speed up the drying process, calcium chloride was placed in the storage room.
Calcium chloride is often added to road salt to improve its effectiveness during harsh winters.

Etymology

From 'calcium', from Latin 'calx' (lime), and 'chloride', from Greek 'chloros' (green) referencing chlorine.
