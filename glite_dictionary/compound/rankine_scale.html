Rankine scale

1. NOUN

/ ˈræŋ.kɪn skeɪl / (AmE), / ˈræŋ.kɪn skeɪl / (BrE)
a scale of absolute temperature in which degrees are equal in size to Fahrenheit degrees, with zero set at absolute zero
absolute temperature scale using Fahrenheit degrees

Examples

Engineers sometimes use the Rankine scale when dealing with thermodynamic calculations in the United States.
At zero on the Rankine scale, all molecular motion theoretically stops, as it is absolute zero.
The Rankine scale is primarily used in engineering systems where heat computations are done using Fahrenheit degrees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Kelvin scale - absolute temperature scale using Celsius-sized degrees
  • Degree Lower: Celsius scale - temperature scale with water freezing at 0°C and boiling at 100°C

Etymology

Named after Scottish engineer William John Macquorn Rankine (1820–1872).
