tarot card

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛɹ.oʊ ˌkɑɹd / (AmE), / ˈtæɹ.əʊ ˌkɑːd / (BrE)
one of a set of cards used for fortune-telling or in certain games, typically featuring symbolic imagery and divided into the Major and Minor Arcana
card from a set used in divination or games

Examples

She drew a tarot card to gain insight into her future.
The magician carefully shuffled the tarot cards before the reading began.
Each tarot card has a unique image and symbolic meaning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: playing card - piece of specially prepared card used in games

Etymology

From French 'tarot', of uncertain origin.
