bead fern

1. NOUN

/ bid fɜrn / (AmE), / biːd fɜːn / (BrE)
a spreading fern found in eastern North America and eastern Asia, with fronds divided into beadlike lobes and sori forming rounded clusters
a fern with beadlike lobes on its fronds

Examples

The bead fern thrives in shaded, moist woodland gardens.
When touched, the fronds of the bead fern may slightly fold in response.
Gardeners appreciate how bead fern adds unique texture to shady landscapes.
