pulmonary circulation

1. NOUN

/ ˈpʌlməˌnɛri ˌsɝːkjəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈpʌlmənəri ˌsɜːkjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the passage of blood from the heart to the lungs and back to the heart, where it is oxygenated
circulation of blood between heart and lungs
medical

Examples

The pulmonary circulation carries deoxygenated blood from the heart to the lungs for oxygenation.
Damage to the pulmonary circulation can result in serious respiratory problems.
Medical students study the pulmonary circulation to understand how the body exchanges gases.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: systemic circulation - circulation of blood between the heart and the rest of the body

Etymology

From Latin 'pulmonarius' (of the lungs) and 'circulatio' (a going round).
