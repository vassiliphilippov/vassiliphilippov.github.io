Authorized Version

1. PROPN

/ ˈɔθəˌraɪzd vɚˈʒən / (AmE), / ˈɔːθəraɪzd ˈvɜːʃən / (BrE)
the English translation of the Bible first published in 1611, also known as the King James Version
English translation of the Bible (1611)

Examples

Many scholars prefer to study the Authorized Version because of its historical significance.
The language of the Authorized Version has influenced English literature for centuries.
Some older churches still use readings from the Authorized Version in their services.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: King James Version - Another name for the Authorized Version of the Bible

Etymology

Refers to the official authorization by King James I of England in 1604.
