deltoid leaf

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛl.tɔɪd lif / (AmE), / ˈdɛl.tɔɪd liːf / (BrE)
A simple leaf shaped like the Greek capital letter delta (Δ), typically triangular with a broad base and pointed tip.
triangular, delta-shaped leaf

Examples

The cottonwood tree is easily identified by its deltoid leaf shape.
Botanists often describe leaves as ovate, lanceolate, or deltoid leaf depending on their form.
The deltoid leaf of the sycamore is both broad and sharply pointed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: triangular leaf - a leaf shaped like a triangle
  • Degree Lower: ovate leaf - an egg-shaped leaf

Etymology

From Greek delta (Δ), referring to its shape, and the English word 'leaf'.
