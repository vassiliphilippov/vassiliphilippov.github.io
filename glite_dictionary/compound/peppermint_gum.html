peppermint gum

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛpərˌmɪnt ɡʌm / (AmE), / ˈpɛpəmənt ɡʌm / (BrE)
a species of eucalyptus tree native to Tasmania, known for its aromatic leaves with a peppermint scent
Tasmanian eucalyptus tree with peppermint aroma

Examples

The leaves of the peppermint gum are often used to produce essential oils.
Many gardens in Tasmania feature the peppermint gum for its beauty and fragrance.
Koalas may feed on the foliage of the peppermint gum in their natural habitat.

Etymology

Named for the peppermint-like aroma of its leaves; 'gum' refers to the eucalyptus group of trees.
