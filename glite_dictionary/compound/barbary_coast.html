Barbary Coast

1. PROPN

/ ˈbɑːrbəri koʊst / (AmE), / ˈbɑːbəri kəʊst / (BrE)
a historical region of northern Africa along the Mediterranean, between Egypt and Gibraltar, formerly known for piracy from the 16th to 19th centuries
historic North African Mediterranean region

Examples

The notorious pirates of the Barbary Coast attacked ships sailing in the Mediterranean Sea.
European powers often negotiated with the rulers of the Barbary Coast to protect their merchant fleets.
Stories of captivity along the Barbary Coast circulated widely throughout 18th-century Europe.

Etymology

From 'Barbary,' an English exonym for the Berber-speaking regions of North Africa, and 'coast'.

2. PROPN

/ ˈbɑːrbəri koʊst / (AmE), / ˈbɑːbəri kəʊst / (BrE)
the Mediterranean coast of northern Africa, historically known for Moorish pirates who operated there from the 16th to 19th centuries
Mediterranean coast of North Africa, famed for pirates

Examples

European ships often avoided the Barbary Coast to escape pirate attacks.
Stories of the Barbary Coast have inspired many adventure novels and films.
Trade in the Mediterranean was frequently disrupted by raids from the Barbary Coast.

Etymology

From 'Barbary', referring to the Berber people of North Africa, combined with 'coast'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈbɑːrbɛri ˈkoʊst / (AmE), / ˈbɑːbəri ˈkəʊst / (BrE)
An area of a city, especially San Francisco's waterfront in the late 19th century, known for its gambling, brothels, saloons, and lawlessness.
notorious district known for vice and nightlife

Examples

After dark, the Barbary Coast was alive with music, laughter, and danger.
City leaders vowed to clean up the Barbary Coast and restore order to the waterfront.
The novel is set in the days when the Barbary Coast was infamous for its wild nightlife.

Synset Examples

we'll tolerate no Barbary Coast in this city!

Etymology

Named after the Barbary Coast region of North Africa, associated with piracy; originally applied to San Francisco's lawless waterfront after the 1849 gold rush.
