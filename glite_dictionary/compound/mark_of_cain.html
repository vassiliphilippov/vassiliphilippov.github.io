mark of Cain

1. NOUN

/ mɑrk əv keɪn / (AmE), / mɑːk əv keɪn / (BrE)
a sign or stigma indicating a person's guilt, sinful nature, or social outcast status, derived from the Biblical story of Cain
sign of guilt or disgrace

Examples

Some believed the criminal bore the mark of Cain, forever separated from decent society.
The story explores how a family can be branded with a mark of Cain for generations.
She felt as if a mark of Cain followed her, no matter how much she tried to change.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stigma - a mark of disgrace associated with a particular circumstance, quality, or person

Etymology

From the Biblical story of Cain in the Book of Genesis, where God set a mark on Cain after he killed his brother Abel.
