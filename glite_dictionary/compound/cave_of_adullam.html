Cave of Adullam

1. PROPN

/ ˈkeɪv əv əˈdʌləm / (AmE), / ˈkeɪv əv əˈdʌləm / (BrE)
A place of refuge or sanctuary, especially for those seeking solace or support; often used metaphorically referring to a safe haven for the marginalized or distressed.
metaphorical place of refuge or sanctuary

Examples

The activists described their meeting place as a Cave of Adullam for those fighting injustice.
After his failure, he retreated to his own Cave of Adullam to recover.
Literature often refers to the Cave of Adullam as a source of strength for the downtrodden.

Synset Examples

The Cave of Adullam became a sanctuary for lost souls.
The group met weekly at the Cave of Adullam for support.
He found solace in the teachings from the Cave of Adullam.

Etymology

From the biblical Cave of Adullam, where David found refuge (see 1 Samuel 22:1-2 in the Old Testament).
