kinetic sculpture

1. NOUN

/ kɪˈnɛtɪk ˈskʌlptʃər / (AmE), / kɪˈnɛtɪk ˈskʌlptʃə / (BrE)
A work of art designed to move, either mechanically or by natural forces such as wind.
sculpture that incorporates movement

Examples

The museum featured a fascinating kinetic sculpture that spun slowly in the sunlight.
A gentle breeze set the kinetic sculpture in the garden into graceful motion.
He decided to create a kinetic sculpture powered entirely by solar energy.

Synset Examples

He loved watching the kinetic sculptures move.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: mobile - a type of kinetic sculpture suspended so it moves freely
  • Antonym: stabile - a type of sculpture that does not incorporate motion

Etymology

From 'kinetic,' from Greek *kinētikos* (of motion), and 'sculpture,' from Latin *sculptura* (carving).
