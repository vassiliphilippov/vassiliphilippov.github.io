family Haemoproteidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli hiːˌmoʊproʊˈtiːɪˌdaɪ / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli hɪˌməʊprəʊˈtiːɪˌdiː / (BrE)
a family of protozoan parasites that infect birds, belonging to the order Haemosporida.
a family of bird-infecting protozoan parasites

Examples

Species within the family Haemoproteidae are known to cause malaria-like infections in birds.
Veterinarians often screen wild birds for parasites from the family Haemoproteidae.
The family Haemoproteidae includes several genera known for their distinctive crescent-shaped parasites.

Etymology

From New Latin genus name Haemoproteus, from Greek haima (blood) + proteus (first).
