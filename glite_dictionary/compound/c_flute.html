C flute

1. NOUN

/ si fluːt / (AmE), / si fluːt / (BrE)
the standard Western concert flute, pitched in C, and commonly used in orchestras and bands
standard Western concert flute

Examples

She played the C flute in the school orchestra throughout her teenage years.
Most beginners start lessons on the C flute before trying other types like the piccolo or alto flute.
The symphony's first movement featured a beautiful C flute solo.

Synset Examples

The dimensions and key system of the modern western concert flute and its close relatives are almost completely the work of the great flautist, composer, acoustician and silversmith, Theobald Boehm, who patented his system in 1847.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: concert flute - standard side-blown flute used in Western music, pitched in C
  • Degree Lower: alto flute - larger, lower-pitched flute in the key of G
  • Degree Higher: piccolo - smaller, higher-pitched flute
