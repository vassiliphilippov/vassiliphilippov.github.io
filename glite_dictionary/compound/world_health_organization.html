World Health Organization

1. PROPN

/ wɝld hɛlθ ˌɔrɡənɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / wɜːld hɛlθ ˌɔːɡənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
an international agency of the United Nations that coordinates global health activities and assists countries in improving health services
United Nations global health agency

Examples

The World Health Organization sets international standards for public health.
During the pandemic, the World Health Organization provided crucial guidance to countries worldwide.
Many governments collaborate closely with the World Health Organization to tackle diseases and health crises.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: WHO - acronym for World Health Organization

Etymology

Founded in 1948 as a specialized agency of the United Nations.
