capped macaque

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæpt məˈkæk / (AmE), / ˈkæpt məˈkɑːk / (BrE)
a species of macaque found in India, characterized by a bonnet-like tuft of hair on its head
a species of Indian macaque with a tufted crown

Examples

The capped macaque is native to the forests of northeastern India.
Researchers studied the behavior of several capped macaques in their natural habitat.
The capped macaque is easily recognized by the tuft of hair on its head.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bonnet macaque - a closely related species of Indian macaque distinguished by a different hair tuft
