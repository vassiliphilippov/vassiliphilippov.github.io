drill rod

1. NOUN

/ drɪl rɑd / (AmE), / drɪl rɒd / (BrE)
A type of high-quality carbon steel used for making rock drills, dowels, and machine parts requiring high strength and durability.
carbon steel for drills and dowels

Examples

The engineer selected a drill rod that could withstand the intense pressure of deep-earth drilling.
After frequent use, the drill rod showed signs of wear and needed to be replaced.
Manufacturers recommend using a hardened drill rod for the most demanding construction projects.
