cat's-ear

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæts ɪr / (AmE), / ˈkæts ɪə / (BrE)
any of several plants of the genus Calochortus, noted for their flowers with petals shaped like a cat's ear
plant with cat's ear-shaped petals

Examples

The cat's-ear bloomed in our garden, its delicate petals resembling the ears of a cat.
Botanists studied the unique structure of several cat's-ear species in the wild.
She picked a cat's-ear and sketched its furry-looking petals in her notebook.

Etymology

Named for the resemblance of the petals to a cat’s ear.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkæts ɪr / (AmE), / ˈkats ɪə / (BrE)
a European plant with yellow flower heads and leaves that resemble a cat's ears, widely naturalized in North America
a plant with yellow flowers and ear-shaped leaves

Examples

The field was dotted with bright yellow cat's-ear blooming under the summer sun.
She mistook the cat's-ear for a dandelion because of its similar flower.
Gardeners often remove cat's-ear from lawns as it spreads quickly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dandelion - a similar-looking yellow-flowered weed often confused with cat's-ear

Etymology

Named for the shape of its leaves, which are thought to resemble a cat's ears.
