Hooke's law

1. NOUN

/ hʊks lɔ / (AmE), / hʊks lɔː / (BrE)
The principle in physics stating that, within the elastic limit, the stress applied to a solid is proportional to the strain it produces.
law in physics relating stress and strain

Examples

According to Hooke's law, a spring's extension is directly proportional to the force applied to it.
Engineers use Hooke's law to calculate how materials will deform under stress.
The experiment demonstrated the limits of Hooke's law when the material was stretched beyond its elastic region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elasticity - the ability of a material to return to its original shape after being stretched or compressed

Etymology

Named after English scientist Robert Hooke (1635–1703) who formulated the law.
