brain damage

1. NOUN

/ ˈbreɪn ˌdæmɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈbreɪn ˌdamɪdʒ / (BrE)
Injury to the brain that impairs its functions, often permanently, and may be caused by trauma, infection, lack of oxygen, or other factors.
injury impairing brain function
medical

Examples

After the accident, he suffered severe brain damage and lost much of his memory.
Certain infections can cause brain damage if not treated promptly.
Doctors are researching new ways to repair brain damage caused by strokes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brain injury - damage to the brain caused by external force
  • Degree Lower: concussion - temporary unconsciousness or confusion caused by a blow to the head

2. NOUN

/ ˈbreɪn ˌdæmɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈbreɪn ˌdamɪdʒ / (BrE)
Injury to the brain that impairs its normal function, often causing lasting physical or mental effects.
injury impairing brain function
medical

Examples

After the stroke, he suffered from brain damage that affected his memory.
Doctors warned that lack of oxygen could result in permanent brain damage.
Wearing a helmet while cycling helps prevent brain damage in the event of an accident.

Synset Examples

The accident left him with minor brain damage.
Too much stress can lead to lasting brain damage.
He worried that his late-night habits would cause brain damage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brain injury - physical trauma to the brain
  • Degree Lower: concussion - temporary unconsciousness or confusion from a blow to the head
  • Degree Higher: brain death - irreversible loss of all brain function
