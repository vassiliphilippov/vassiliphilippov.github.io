pudendal artery

1. NOUN

/ pjuˈdɛndəl ˈɑːrtəri / (AmE), / pjuˈdɛndəl ˈɑːtəri / (BrE)
an artery that supplies blood to the external genital organs and perineum in humans
artery supplying the external genital organs
medical

Examples

During surgery, the pudendal artery must be carefully identified to avoid excessive bleeding.
A blockage in the pudendal artery can lead to numbness in the genital area.
The pudendal artery plays a crucial role in the blood supply to the perineum.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: internal pudendal artery - the main branch of the pudendal arteries that supplies the perineum and external genitalia

Etymology

From Latin 'pudendus' (modest, shameful), referring to the genital region.
