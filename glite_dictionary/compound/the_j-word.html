the j-word

1. NOUN

/ ði ˈdʒeɪ wɝd / (AmE), / ðə ˈdʒeɪ wɜːd / (BrE)
A euphemism used to refer to a word beginning with 'j' that is regarded as sensitive, controversial, or potentially offensive.
euphemism for a sensitive 'j'-word

Examples

We should be careful not to say the j-word during this presentation.
Some newspapers avoid printing the j-word to prevent stirring controversy.
He was criticized for using the j-word publicly, even out of context.

Synset Examples

Let’s avoid the j-word and focus on solutions.
Don’t use the j-word in this discussion, please.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: the n-word - euphemism for a taboo word starting with 'n'
  • Synonym: the f-word - euphemism for a taboo word starting with 'f'

Etymology

Patterned after 'the n-word,' using the letter 'j' to euphemistically avoid direct mention of a controversial term.
