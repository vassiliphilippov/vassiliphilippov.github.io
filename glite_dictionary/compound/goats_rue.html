goat's rue

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡoʊts ruː / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊts ruː / (BrE)
A tall bushy European perennial plant (Galega officinalis) with pinnate leaves and slender spikes of blue flowers, sometimes used medicinally.
a European perennial plant with blue flowers

Examples

The herbalist recommended goat's rue as a traditional remedy for boosting milk production.
Clusters of blue flowers covered the goat's rue growing at the edge of the meadow.
Although not commonly cultivated, goat's rue is occasionally found in botanical gardens.

Etymology

From the plant's folk use as a remedy for goats; from Old French 'rue' (herb of grace).

2. NOUN

/ ɡoʊts ru / (AmE), / ɡəʊts ruː / (BrE)
A perennial subshrub native to eastern North America, with downy leaves and yellowish or rose flowers, known as a source of rotenone.
North American subshrub; source of rotenone

Examples

The forest floor was dotted with patches of goat's rue in full bloom.
Farmers once used goat's rue as a natural insecticide due to its rotenone content.
He identified the unusual plant as goat's rue by its soft, downy leaves.
