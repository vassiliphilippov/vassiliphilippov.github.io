local option

1. NOUN

/ ˈloʊ.kəl ˈɑp.ʃən / (AmE), / ˈləʊ.kəl ˈɒp.ʃən / (BrE)
the right or freedom of a local government area to adopt or reject certain laws, especially by popular vote; commonly used in reference to regulation of alcohol sales or other controversial statutes.
freedom for local area to decide laws by vote
legal

Examples

The county held a referendum to decide the local option on the sale of alcoholic beverages.
Some states allow each municipality to exercise a local option regarding gambling laws.
Debate over the local option divided the community for months before the vote.

Etymology

From 'local', meaning 'relating to a particular region', and 'option', from Latin 'optio' (choice).
