Golden Gate Bridge

1. PROPN

/ ˈɡoʊldən ɡeɪt brɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊldən ɡeɪt brɪdʒ / (BrE)
a famous suspension bridge spanning the Golden Gate Strait, connecting San Francisco to Marin County in California
iconic bridge in San Francisco

Examples

The Golden Gate Bridge is often shrouded in morning fog, creating a mysterious and beautiful scene.
Many tourists stroll or bike across the Golden Gate Bridge to admire the views of San Francisco Bay.
The distinctive red color of the Golden Gate Bridge makes it one of the most photographed structures in the world.

Etymology

Named after the Golden Gate Strait, which the bridge spans.

2. PROPN

/ ˌgoʊldən ˈɡeɪt ˌbrɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌɡəʊldən ˈɡeɪt ˌbrɪdʒ / (BrE)
a famous suspension bridge spanning the Golden Gate Strait in San Francisco, California
iconic suspension bridge in San Francisco

Examples

Tourists flock to San Francisco to see the Golden Gate Bridge in person.
The fog often rolls over the Golden Gate Bridge, creating a breathtaking scene.
The Golden Gate Bridge was once the longest suspension bridge in the world.

Synset Examples

San Francisco is known for its iconic Golden Gate Bridge.

Etymology

Named after the Golden Gate Strait, which it spans, and 'bridge' from Old English 'brycg'.
