vice admiral

1. NOUN

/ ˈvaɪs ˌæd.mɪ.rəl / (AmE), / ˈvaɪs ˌæd.mɪ.rəl / (BrE)
A naval officer of high rank, ranking below a full admiral and above a rear admiral.
naval officer below admiral

Examples

After years of service, he was promoted to vice admiral in the navy.
The vice admiral oversaw several fleets during the operation.
She was the first woman to achieve the rank of vice admiral in her country's history.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: admiral - the highest naval officer rank
  • Degree Lower: rear admiral - a naval officer ranking below a vice admiral

Etymology

From Latin 'vice' (in place of) and 'admiral' (from Arabic 'amīr al-').
