rutilant glow

1. NOUN

/ ˈruː.tɪ.lənt ɡloʊ / (AmE), / ˈruː.tɪ.lənt ɡləʊ / (BrE)
a reddish or golden light, often used in a poetic or literary context to describe a warm luminescence
reddish or golden light

Examples

The ancient lamp filled the hall with a rutilant glow that danced on the walls.
After the fire was lit, a rutilant glow spread across the faces gathered around.
Even through the storm, the lighthouse sent out a rutilant glow to guide the ships safely in.

Synset Examples

The sunset cast a rutilant glow over the horizon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glow - a steady light, especially in the dark

Etymology

From Latin 'rutilans', present participle of 'rutilare' (to be red or golden).
