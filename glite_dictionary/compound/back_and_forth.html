back and forth

1. ADV

/ bæk ənd fɔrθ / (AmE), / bæk ənd fɔːθ / (BrE)
in one direction and then in the opposite direction, often repeatedly
moving in opposite directions repeatedly

Examples

The children ran back and forth across the playground all afternoon.
She paced back and forth while waiting for the important phone call.
The pendulum swung back and forth in a steady rhythm.

Synset Examples

he traveled back and forth between Los Angeles and New York
the treetops whipped to and fro in a frightening manner
the old man just sat on the porch and rocked back and forth all day

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to and fro - back and forward repeatedly
