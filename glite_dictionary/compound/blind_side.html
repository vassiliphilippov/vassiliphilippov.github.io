blind side

1. NOUN

/ blaɪnd saɪd / (AmE), / blaɪnd saɪd / (BrE)
the side where a person's vision is obstructed or limited, making it vulnerable or less visible to them
side where vision is obstructed

Examples

Always check your blind side before changing lanes on the highway.
The defender sneaked up on the quarterback's blind side and caused a fumble.
She failed to see the cyclist coming from her blind side as she turned.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weak side - the less dominant or less visible side, especially in sports
  • Antonym: sighted side - the side that is clearly visible to someone
