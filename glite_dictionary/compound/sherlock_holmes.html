Sherlock Holmes

1. PROPN

/ ˈʃɝːlɑk ˈhoʊmz / (AmE), / ˈʃɜːlɒk ˈhəʊmz / (BrE)
a fictitious detective created by Sir Arthur Conan Doyle, known for his keen observation and logical reasoning
famous fictional detective by Conan Doyle

Examples

Many readers admire Sherlock Holmes for his remarkable powers of deduction and analytical thinking.
The character of Sherlock Holmes has been portrayed by numerous actors in film and television adaptations.
Whenever a mystery arises, someone inevitably jokingly refers to themselves as Sherlock Holmes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: detective - a person, especially a police officer, whose job is to investigate crimes and catch criminals

Etymology

First appeared in A. Conan Doyle's novel 'A Study in Scarlet' (1887).
