Fourteenth Amendment

1. PROPN

/ fɔrˈtinθ əˈmɛndmənt / (AmE), / ˌfɔːˈtiːnθ əˈmɛndmənt / (BrE)
an amendment to the United States Constitution adopted in 1868, granting citizenship and equal protection under the law to all persons born or naturalized in the U.S., and extending the guarantees of the Bill of Rights to the states
U.S. constitutional amendment on rights and equality
legal american

Examples

The Fourteenth Amendment guarantees equal protection of the laws to all citizens.
Many landmark Supreme Court decisions are based on the Fourteenth Amendment.
The rights protected by the Fourteenth Amendment have had a profound impact on American history.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Fifteenth Amendment - an amendment granting voting rights regardless of race
  • Degree Lower: Thirteenth Amendment - an amendment abolishing slavery in the U.S.

2. NOUN

/ ˌfɔrˈtinθ əˈmɛndmənt / (AmE), / ˌfɔːˈtiːnθ əˈmɛndmənt / (BrE)
an amendment to the U.S. Constitution, adopted in 1868, that grants citizenship to all persons born or naturalized in the United States and guarantees equal protection of the laws.
U.S. constitutional amendment on equal protection and citizenship
legal american

Examples

The Fourteenth Amendment is frequently cited in court cases involving civil rights.
After the Civil War, the Fourteenth Amendment established the principle of equal protection under the law.
Debates about due process often involve interpretation of the Fourteenth Amendment.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Fifteenth Amendment - U.S. constitutional amendment addressing voting rights and race, adopted after the Fourteenth Amendment
  • Degree Lower: Thirteenth Amendment - U.S. constitutional amendment abolishing slavery, preceding the Fourteenth Amendment
