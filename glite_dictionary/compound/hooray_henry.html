Hooray Henry

1. NOUN

/ həˌreɪ ˈhɛnri / (AmE), / həˌreɪ ˈhɛnri / (BrE)
a young man from an upper-class background who is lively, loud, and socially privileged, but considered arrogant or ineffectual
loud upper-class young man (UK)
british

Examples

The club was full of Hooray Henrys boasting about their holidays and expensive cars.
I'm tired of those Hooray Henrys who think they own the place.
He was dismissed as just another Hooray Henry with more money than sense.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: toff - a wealthy or upper-class person, often perceived as snobbish

Etymology

British English, from 'hooray' (exclamation of excitement) + the given name Henry, used generically.

2. NOUN

/ həˈreɪ ˈhɛnri / (AmE), / hʊˈreɪ ˈhɛnri / (BrE)
an upper-class or wealthy young man who is loud, boastful, and ostentatious, often seen as self-indulgent or arrogant
a wealthy, flamboyant, loud young man
british informal

Examples

The club was filled with Hooray Henrys flaunting their expensive watches and talking loudly.
She didn't want to be seen dating another Hooray Henry from the exclusive rugby club.
At the racing event, several Hooray Henrys were making a spectacle of themselves.

Synset Examples

Every Hooray Henry knows how to have fun.
He acted like a typical Hooray Henry all night.
The Hooray Henrys at the party were so loud.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: toff - a wealthy or upper-class person, typically arrogant
  • Antonym: yob - a loutish, uncouth young man

Etymology

Originated in Britain, likely from the phrase 'hooray' used as a cheer, combined with 'Henry' as a typical upper-class male name.
