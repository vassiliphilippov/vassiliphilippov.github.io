bone black

1. NOUN

/ boʊn blæk / (AmE), / bəʊn blæk / (BrE)
a black pigment consisting of charred animal bones, used in paints, inks, and as a colouring agent
pigment made from carbonized bones

Examples

The artist chose bone black to achieve a deep, matte finish in her painting.
Historic printmakers often used bone black as a primary pigment in their inks.
A trace of bone black can sometimes be detected in the analysis of old frescoes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ivory black - a fine black pigment made by charring ivory, similar in use to bone black
