Woolworth gun

1. NOUN

/ ˈwʊl.wɝθ ɡʌn / (AmE), / ˈwʊl.wəθ ɡʌn / (BrE)
A toy gun, typically inexpensive, that was sold by Woolworth's stores.
toy gun sold by Woolworth's stores

Examples

As a boy, he loved running around the garden with his Woolworth gun pretending to be a cowboy.
The Woolworth gun was a popular choice among children in the 1950s and 60s.
She discovered an old Woolworth gun in her attic while sorting through childhood toys.

Synset Examples

She cherished her childhood memories of playing with a Woolworth gun.
He bought his first toy with a Woolworth gun at the store.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: toy gun - a children's imitation of a firearm used for play

Etymology

Woolworth from the retail chain Frank W. Woolworth Co., founded in the U.S. in 1879; gun as in toy firearm.
