genus Coccyzus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs kɒkˈsaɪzəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs kɒkˈsaɪzəs / (BrE)
A genus of birds in the cuckoo family (Cuculidae) containing American cuckoo species.
genus of American cuckoos

Examples

Many cuckoo species in North America belong to the genus Coccyzus.
Researchers studied migration patterns in birds from the genus Coccyzus.
The yellow-billed cuckoo is a well-known member of the genus Coccyzus.

Etymology

From Greek kokkyzō (to cry cuckoo), also the type genus name in Linnaean taxonomy.
