Heidelberg man

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪdəlˌbɝɡ mæn / (AmE), / ˈhaɪd(ə)lˌbɜːɡ mæn / (BrE)
an extinct species of early human (Homo heidelbergensis) who lived in Europe during the Middle Pleistocene period
an early human species found in Europe

Examples

The discovery of the Heidelberg man fossils provided new insights into human evolution.
Scientists believe that Heidelberg man was a common ancestor of both Neanderthals and modern humans.
Archaeological sites in Europe have revealed tools made by Heidelberg man.

Etymology

Named after Heidelberg, Germany, where fossils were first discovered in 1907.
