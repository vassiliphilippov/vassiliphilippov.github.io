Muphry's law

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌfɹiz lɑ / (AmE), / ˈmʌfɹiz lɔː / (BrE)
the principle that any piece of writing about proofreading or editing is likely to contain typographical or language errors, especially in corrections
errors in proofreading text about proofreading

Examples

Every time I send out a newsletter about typos, Muphry's law ensures I make one myself.
The revised document fell victim to Muphry's law when an obvious error slipped through.
Editors laughed as Muphry's law appeared in the correction column.

Synset Examples

He joked about Muphry's law ruining their report.
Muphry's law struck again in my presentation.
She experienced Muphry's law during her thesis defense.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Murphy's law - the adage that anything that can go wrong will go wrong

Etymology

A deliberate misspelling of Murphy's Law, coined by John Bangsund (Australian editor) in the 1990s.
