family Dendrocolaptidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæmɪli ˌdɛndroʊkoʊˈlæptɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli ˌdɛndrəkəʊˈlæptɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of birds known as woodcreepers, native to Central and South America, characterized by arboreal habits and climbing specializations.
family of woodcreeper birds

Examples

The family Dendrocolaptidae contains nearly sixty species of woodcreepers found in Central and South America.
Scientists study the evolutionary traits unique to the family Dendrocolaptidae.
Many ornithologists are fascinated by the adaptive behaviors of the family Dendrocolaptidae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: woodcreeper - bird of the family Dendrocolaptidae

Etymology

From New Latin *Dendrocolaptidae*, based on genus *Dendrocolaptes* and Greek *dendron* (tree) + *kolapto* (to peck).
