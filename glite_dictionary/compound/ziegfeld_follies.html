Ziegfeld Follies

1. PROPN

/ ˈziɡ.fɛld ˈfɑ.liz / (AmE), / ˈziːɡ.fɛld ˈfɒl.iːz / (BrE)
a series of lavish theatrical revues produced by Florenz Ziegfeld in New York City, famed for elaborate costumes, sets, and performances, particularly during the early 20th century
iconic series of theatrical revues (early 1900s NYC)

Examples

The Ziegfeld Follies set a new standard for Broadway entertainment in the early 20th century.
Many famous performers launched their careers in the Ziegfeld Follies.
Lavish costumes and grand musical numbers defined the signature style of the Ziegfeld Follies.

Etymology

Named after Florenz Ziegfeld (1867–1932), American theatrical producer, and taken from the French word "folie" meaning "madness" or "extravaganza".
