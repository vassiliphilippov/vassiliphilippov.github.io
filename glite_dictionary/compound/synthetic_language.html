synthetic language

1. NOUN

/ sɪnˈθɛtɪk ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / sɪnˈθɛtɪk ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
A language in which grammatical relationships are expressed by the use of inflectional endings or affixes.
language with inflectional endings

Examples

Latin is a classic example of a synthetic language due to its complex system of inflections.
The distinction between analytic and synthetic language types is important in linguistic studies.
Old Russian was a synthetic language with many case endings and verb forms.

Synset Examples

English is a mildly synthetic language, while older Indo-European languages, like Latin, Greek and Sanskrit, are highly synthetic.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: analytic language - A language that primarily uses word order and auxiliary words instead of inflections.

Etymology

From 'synthetic' (from Greek sunthētikos, arranging together) + 'language'.
