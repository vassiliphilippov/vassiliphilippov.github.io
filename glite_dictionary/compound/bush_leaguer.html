bush leaguer

1. NOUN

/ bʊʃ ˈliːɡɚ / (AmE), / bʊʃ ˈliːɡə / (BrE)
a player who is a member of a minor-league baseball team, especially in the United States
minor-league baseball player
american

Examples

The bush leaguer hoped to get noticed by the scouts and make it to the major leagues.
He spent years as a bush leaguer before finally signing with a top-tier team.
Many bush leaguers dream of one day playing at Yankee Stadium.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: minor leaguer - a player in a minor-league team
  • Antonym: major leaguer - a player in a major-league baseball team

Etymology

From American English "bush league" (minor league or less prestigious league), itself from "bush" meaning rural or small-town.
