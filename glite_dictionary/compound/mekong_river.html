Mekong River

1. PROPN

/ ˈmeɪ.kɔŋ ˈrɪv.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈmiː.kɒŋ ˈrɪv.ə / (BrE)
a major river in Southeast Asia, flowing from Tibet through China, Myanmar, Laos, Thailand, Cambodia, and Vietnam, and into the South China Sea
large river in Southeast Asia

Examples

The Mekong River is vital for transportation and agriculture in many Southeast Asian countries.
Fishermen depend on the Mekong River for their daily livelihood.
The Mekong River delta is home to a rich variety of plant and animal species.

Etymology

From Khmer Mékông, probably from Sanskrit mahat (great) and gaṅgā (Ganges).
