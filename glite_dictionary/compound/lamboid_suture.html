lamboid suture

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæm.bɔɪd ˈsu.tʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈlam.bɔɪd ˈsjuː.tʃə / (BrE)
the serrated joint between the occipital and parietal bones of the skull
skull joint between occipital and parietal bones
medical

Examples

The lamboid suture is easily visible in cranial X-rays of children.
Premature closure of the lamboid suture can lead to skull deformities.
The anatomy professor pointed to the lamboid suture on the model of the human skull.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sagittal suture - the suture between the two parietal bones of the skull
  • Synonym: coronal suture - the suture between the parietal and frontal bones of the skull

Etymology

From Greek lamboeidēs (lambda-shaped), referring to its resemblance to the Greek letter lambda (Λ).
