family Urticaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ˌɜːrtɪˈkeɪsiː / (AmE), / ˈfamɪli ˌɜːtɪˈkeɪsiː / (BrE)
a family of flowering plants, including many nettle species with stinging hairs, classified in the order Urticales
a family of nettle plants

Examples

The family Urticaceae includes the common stinging nettle, which is found worldwide.
Some members of the family Urticaceae cause skin irritation due to their hairs.
Many botanists study the diversity within the family Urticaceae when researching flowering plants.

Etymology

From Latin 'Urtica' (nettle), the type genus of the family, with '-aceae' denoting a plant family.
