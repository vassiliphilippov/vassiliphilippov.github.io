spotted sunfish

1. NOUN

/ ˈspɑːtɪd ˈsʌnˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈspɒtɪd ˈsʌnˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
a small freshwater fish (Lepomis punctatus) of the sunfish family, native to streams in the southeastern United States, notable for its dark spots and valued as a panfish
a type of small freshwater fish of southeastern U.S.

Examples

The spotted sunfish is often found in clear, slow-moving streams throughout the southeastern United States.
Anglers appreciate the fight of the spotted sunfish despite its small size.
We caught several spotted sunfish during our fishing trip near the Florida-Georgia border.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sunfish - a common name for several species of small, freshwater fish in the family Centrarchidae
