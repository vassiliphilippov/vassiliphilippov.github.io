true satori

1. NOUN

/ tru səˈtɔri / (AmE), / tru səˈtɔːri / (BrE)
a sudden and genuine moment of enlightenment or realization, especially as understood in Zen Buddhism
genuine moment of enlightenment

Examples

After years of searching, meditation finally brought him true satori.
The poem captures the moment of true satori the author experienced in nature.
Her insight during the retreat felt like true satori to her companions.

Synset Examples

Finding that book was a true satori for his understanding.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enlightenment - a state of gaining spiritual knowledge or insight
  • Synonym: epiphany - a sudden realization or understanding

Etymology

From Japanese satori (understanding, enlightenment), with 'true' added for emphasis.
