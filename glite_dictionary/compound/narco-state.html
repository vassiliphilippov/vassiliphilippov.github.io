narco-state

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɑːrkoʊ steɪt / (AmE), / ˈnɑːkəʊ steɪt / (BrE)
A country or region whose government is heavily influenced or controlled by drug cartels, resulting in widespread corruption and lack of effective law enforcement.
state controlled by drug cartels

Examples

The collapse of the police force has turned the region into a narco-state run by criminal gangs.
Journalists were banned from reporting in the narco-state due to threats from drug lords.
Many international organizations refuse to negotiate with a narco-state because of its ties to drug trafficking.

Synset Examples

this Mexican town has become a narco-state that ships 100 pounds of cocaine to the United States every day

Related Terms

  • Synonym: failed state - country with ineffective government and loss of control

Etymology

Formed from 'narco-' (relating to narcotics) and 'state'; modeled after terms like 'city-state'.
