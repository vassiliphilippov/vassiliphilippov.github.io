middle term

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪd(ə)l tɜrm / (AmE), / ˈmɪd(ə)l tɜːm / (BrE)
the term in a syllogism that appears in both premises but not in the conclusion
logical term in syllogism

Examples

In a classic syllogism, the middle term connects the major and minor premises.
The middle term never appears in the conclusion of the argument.
Logicians analyze how the middle term relates the two premises in deductive reasoning.
