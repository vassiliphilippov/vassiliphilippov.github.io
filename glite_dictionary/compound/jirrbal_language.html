Jirrbal language

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɪrbəl ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈdʒɪəbəl ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
an Australian Aboriginal language traditionally spoken in northeast Queensland.
Australian Aboriginal language of northeast Queensland

Examples

Researchers have documented the grammar of the Jirrbal language in several academic papers.
Many elders in the region are fluent in the Jirrbal language and work to pass it on to younger generations.
Efforts are underway to revitalize the Jirrbal language through community workshops and school programs.

Etymology

From the name 'Jirrbal,' the people who speak the language, an Aboriginal word.
