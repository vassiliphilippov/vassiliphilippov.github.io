equinoctial storm

1. NOUN

/ ˌekwəˈnɑkʃəl stɔrm / (AmE), / ˌekwɪˈnɒkʃəl stɔːm / (BrE)
A violent rainstorm that typically occurs around the time of an equinox.
violent storm near an equinox

Examples

The sailors were caught in an unexpected equinoctial storm while crossing the Atlantic.
Each year, the region braces itself for a potential equinoctial storm in September.
The damage caused by the equinoctial storm was severe, flooding homes and uprooting trees.

Etymology

From Latin 'aequinoctialis' (of the equinox) + 'storm'.
