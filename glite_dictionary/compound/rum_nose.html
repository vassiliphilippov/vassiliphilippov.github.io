rum nose

1. NOUN

/ rʌm noʊz / (AmE), / rʌm nəʊz / (BrE)
a reddish, enlarged nose with visible blood vessels, often linked to excessive alcohol consumption
red, enlarged nose from alcohol abuse
informal medical

Examples

His rum nose was a telltale sign of years spent drinking at the local pub.
People used to believe that a rum nose meant someone was a heavy drinker.
Doctors now know a rum nose can result from rosacea, not just alcohol abuse.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rhinophyma - severe form of rosacea causing a large, bulbous nose with thickened skin

Etymology

From 'rum' (a type of alcohol) and 'nose,' reflecting the association with heavy drinking.
