order Anura

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdər æˈnjʊrə / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə æˈnjʊərə / (BrE)
A taxonomic order of amphibians that includes all frogs and toads.
taxonomic order of frogs and toads
medical

Examples

The order Anura encompasses more than 7,000 species of frogs and toads worldwide.
Scientists study the order Anura to understand amphibian evolution.
Unlike newts or salamanders, all members of the order Anura lack tails as adults.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frog - common name for members of order Anura
  • Synonym: toad - common name for certain members of order Anura

Etymology

From Neo-Latin 'Anura' meaning 'without tail', from Greek 'an-' (without) + 'oura' (tail).
