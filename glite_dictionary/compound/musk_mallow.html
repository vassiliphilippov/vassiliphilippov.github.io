musk mallow

1. NOUN

/ mʌsk ˈmæloʊ / (AmE), / mʌsk ˈmæləʊ / (BrE)
a bushy tropical Asian herb grown for its hibiscus-like yellow, pink, or scarlet flowers
tropical herb with hibiscus-like flowers

Examples

The musk mallow adds a splash of color to my garden borders every summer.
Some perfumes use oil extracted from the seeds of musk mallow.
The vibrant blooms of the musk mallow attract bees and butterflies.

Etymology

From 'musk' for its scent and 'mallow' (from Old English 'malwe').

2. NOUN

/ mʌsk ˈmæloʊ / (AmE), / mʌsk ˈmaləʊ / (BrE)
an erect perennial plant native to Europe and Asia, with faintly musk-scented leaves and white or pink flowers, sometimes found in North America.
a perennial plant with musk-scented leaves and pink or white flowers

Examples

The musk mallow blooms with delicate pink flowers in the summer garden.
Gardeners appreciate musk mallow for its subtle fragrance and attractive blossoms.
He planted musk mallow along the fence to add color and scent to the yard.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mallow - a family of flowering plants with soft, hairy leaves and showy flowers
