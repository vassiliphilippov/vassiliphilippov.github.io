semidetached house

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛmi.dɪˈtætʃt haʊs / (AmE), / ˌsɛmi.dɪˈtætʃt haʊs / (BrE)
A house that is joined to another house on one side by a shared wall, forming one of two attached homes.
house joined to another by one wall

Examples

They recently bought a semidetached house in the suburbs for their growing family.
A semidetached house offers more privacy than an apartment but shares a wall with neighbors.
Many British families dream of owning their own semidetached house with a small garden.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: detached house - a house standing alone, not attached to others
  • Antonym: terraced house - a house that is part of a continuous row of similar houses joined together
  • Synonym: duplex - a building divided into two separate homes, often on separate floors or sides

Etymology

From 'semi-' meaning 'half, partly' (Latin) + 'detached', referring to houses partly attached to another unit.
