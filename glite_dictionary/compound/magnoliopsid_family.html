magnoliopsid family

1. NOUN

/ mægˌnoʊliˈɑːpsɪd ˈfæməli / (AmE), / maɡˌnəʊliˈɒpsɪd ˈfæmɪli / (BrE)
a family of flowering plants that have two cotyledons (embryonic leaves) in the seed, typically appearing during germination
plant family with two seed leaves
medical

Examples

The rose belongs to a different magnoliopsid family than the magnolia.
Botanists study the diversity of each magnoliopsid family to understand evolutionary relationships.
Members of this magnoliopsid family are widespread in temperate regions.

Etymology

From modern Latin Magnoliopsida (class of plants named after Pierre Magnol) + 'family'.
