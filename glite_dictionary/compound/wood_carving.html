wood carving

1. NOUN

/ wʊd ˈkɑrvɪŋ / (AmE), / wʊd ˈkɑːvɪŋ / (BrE)
the art or act of shaping wood with cutting tools to produce figures, designs, or ornamentation
the art of shaping wood with tools

Examples

The museum displayed an ancient wood carving depicting a mythical animal.
She enrolled in a workshop to improve her wood carving techniques.
Intricate wood carving adorned the doors of the historic church.

Synset Examples

Some of the finest extant examples of early European wood carving are from the Middle Ages in Germany, Russia, Italy and France, where the typical themes of that era were Christian iconography.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sculpture - the art of making figures or designs in three dimensions
  • Synonym: woodwork - the activity or skill of making things from wood
