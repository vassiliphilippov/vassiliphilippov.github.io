extreme right-winger

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˈstriːm ˈraɪt ˌwɪŋər / (AmE), / ɪkˈstriːm ˈraɪt ˌwɪŋə / (BrE)
A person with extreme conservative views who strongly opposes progress or liberalism.
person with extreme conservative views

Examples

The senator has often been called an extreme right-winger due to his opposition to social reforms.
As an extreme right-winger, she disagreed with any form of government intervention in the economy.
Many extreme right-wingers protested against the changes, fearing a shift towards liberal policies.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: conservative - a person who is averse to change and holds to traditional values and attitudes
  • Synonym: reactionary - a person who opposed political or social progress or reform
