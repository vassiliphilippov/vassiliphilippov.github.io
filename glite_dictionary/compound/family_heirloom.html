family heirloom

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæmɪli ˈɛrluːm / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli ˈeəluːm / (BrE)
an object of value that has been passed down from one generation to another within a family
valuable item passed down in a family

Examples

The ring she wore was a cherished family heirloom passed down from her grandmother.
After her father passed away, she inherited a family heirloom that had been in the family for centuries.
Each generation is responsible for caring for the family heirloom and ensuring its legacy continues.

Synset Examples

The antique clock was a family heirloom.
The vintage teacup and saucer set was a family heirloom.
The necklace was a précieuse family heirloom.
She discovered family heirlooms at rack and manger.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heirloom - a valuable object that has belonged to a family for several generations

Etymology

From family + heirloom; 'heirloom' from Middle English, from heir + loom (legacy, implement).
