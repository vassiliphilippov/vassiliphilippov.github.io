West Coast of the United States

1. PROPN

/ wɛst koʊst əv ðə ˌjuːnaɪtɪd steɪts / (AmE), / wɛst kəʊst əv ðə ˌjuːnaɪtɪd steɪts / (BrE)
the western coastal region of the United States, stretching from Washington to southern California
western coastal region of the U.S.

Examples

Many tech companies are headquartered on the West Coast of the United States.
The climate along the West Coast of the United States is generally mild and influenced by the Pacific Ocean.
Tourists often visit the West Coast of the United States to see cities like Los Angeles, San Francisco, and Seattle.
