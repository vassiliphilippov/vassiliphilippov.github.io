Gota Canal

1. PROPN

/ ˈɡoʊtə kəˈnæl / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊtə kəˈnæl / (BrE)
a canal in Sweden connecting the Baltic Sea with Lake Vänern, enabling small oceangoing ships to cross the country
Swedish canal connecting Baltic Sea and Lake Vänern

Examples

The Gota Canal played a crucial role in Sweden's industrial development in the 19th century.
Many tourists enjoy boat trips along the scenic Gota Canal during the summer.
The engineer was proud to have worked on the restoration of the historic Gota Canal.

Etymology

From Swedish Göta kanal, named after the river Göta älv and the Latin 'canalis'.
