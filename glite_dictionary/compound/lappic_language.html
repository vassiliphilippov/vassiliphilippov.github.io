Lappic language

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæpɪk ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈlapɪk ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
any of the languages spoken by the Lapps (Sami people), generally classified as Uralic languages, mainly in northern parts of Europe
a Sami (Lappic) language of the Uralic family

Examples

Northern Sami is the most widely spoken Lappic language in Scandinavia.
The preservation of Lappic languages is important for maintaining indigenous culture.
Some linguists study the similarities among various Lappic languages and other Uralic tongues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sami language - language of the Sami people in northern Europe

Etymology

From Lapp, an old term for Sami people, plus ‘-ic’ and ‘language’.
