public treasury

1. NOUN

/ ˈpʌblɪk ˈtrɛʒəri / (AmE), / ˈpʌblɪk ˈtrɛʒəri / (BrE)
the funds of a government or the department responsible for receiving, storing, and disbursing public money
government funds or their managing department

Examples

The new budget allocated an additional $2 billion to the public treasury.
Instances of corruption can have a devastating effect on the nation’s public treasury.
All taxes collected are deposited into the public treasury before they are redistributed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: treasury - the government department in charge of finances
