Battle of the Ardennes Bulge

1. PROPN

/ ˈbæt̬əl əv ði ɑrˈdɛnz bʌldʒ / (AmE), / ˈbæt.əl əv ði ɑːˈdɛn bʌldʒ / (BrE)
a major battle during World War II, fought in December 1944 when German forces launched a surprise counteroffensive in the Ardennes forest against the Allies
World War II battle in Ardennes, Dec 1944

Examples

Many historians consider the Battle of the Ardennes Bulge a turning point in World War II.
During the Battle of the Ardennes Bulge, Allied forces faced harsh winter conditions and fierce resistance.
The museum displays several artifacts from the Battle of the Ardennes Bulge and tells the stories of those who fought there.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Battle of the Bulge - the common name for the same World War II battle in the Ardennes

Etymology

From the location Ardennes (a region in Belgium, Luxembourg, and France) and 'bulge' indicating the outward curve made in Allied lines during the battle.
