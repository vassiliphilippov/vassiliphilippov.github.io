hell from above

1. NOUN

/ hɛl frəm əˈbʌv / (AmE), / hɛl frəm əˈbʌv / (BrE)
A situation involving extreme chaos or destruction originating from the sky, often referring to disasters, attacks, or severe weather.
destructive/chaotic force from the sky

Examples

During the air raid, the city endured hell from above as bombs rained down.
The hurricane unleashed hell from above on the coastal village.
Lightning and hail brought hell from above to the unsuspecting town.

Synset Examples

We watched as hell from above battered the rooftops.
The thunderstorm brought hell from above last night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hell - extreme trouble or chaos
