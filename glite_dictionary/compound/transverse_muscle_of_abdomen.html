transverse muscle of abdomen

1. NOUN

/ trænzˈvɜrs ˈmʌsəl əv æbˈdɑmən / (AmE), / trænsˈvɜːs ˈmʌsl əv æbˈdɒmən / (BrE)
a flat muscle with transverse fibers that forms the anterior and lateral walls of the abdominal cavity
a muscle in the abdominal wall with transverse fibers
medical

Examples

The transverse muscle of abdomen plays a crucial role in maintaining core stability.
During abdominal exercises, the transverse muscle of abdomen helps compress the abdominal contents.
Injury to the transverse muscle of abdomen can result in a weakened abdominal wall.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: rectus abdominis - a paired muscle running vertically on each side of the anterior abdominal wall
  • Degree Higher: external oblique muscle - the largest and outermost muscle of the three flat muscles of the lateral anterior abdomen

Etymology

From Latin transversus (turned across) + musculus abdominis (muscle of the abdomen).
