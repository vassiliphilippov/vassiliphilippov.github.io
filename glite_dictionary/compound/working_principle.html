working principle

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɝːkɪŋ ˈprɪnsəpəl / (AmE), / ˈwɜːkɪŋ ˈprɪnsɪpl / (BrE)
a fundamental rule or mechanism that explains how something operates or functions, especially in technical or scientific contexts
functional rule or mechanism

Examples

The working principle of a refrigerator is based on the transfer of heat.
Before using the new machine, it's essential to understand its working principle.
Einstein explained the working principle behind the photoelectric effect in his 1905 paper.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: principle - a basic truth or law that underlies the way something works
