flying cat

1. NOUN

/ ˈflaɪɪŋ kæt / (AmE), / ˈflaɪɪŋ kat / (BrE)
an arboreal nocturnal mammal found in Southeast Asia and the Philippines, resembling a lemur and capable of gliding long distances by spreading a fold of skin from neck to tail; also called a colugo
tree-dwelling gliding mammal (colugo) of Southeast Asia

Examples

The flying cat glides gracefully through the forest canopy, searching for fruit and young leaves.
Researchers observed a flying cat make an impressive leap between two distant trees.
Despite its name, the flying cat is not a feline but is more closely related to primates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: colugo - another name for the flying cat; gliding mammal of Southeast Asia
