box Kodak

1. NOUN

/ bɑks ˈkoʊdæk / (AmE), / bɒks ˈkəʊdæk / (BrE)
an early simple camera manufactured by Kodak, shaped like a rectangular box, widely used in the late 19th and early 20th centuries
early simple box-shaped Kodak camera

Examples

My grandmother took family portraits with a box Kodak in the 1920s.
Collectors prize the box Kodak for its iconic design and historical value.
The box Kodak revolutionized amateur photography by making it simple and affordable.

Etymology

From 'box' (shape) and Kodak, the trademark of the George Eastman company that popularized the camera.
