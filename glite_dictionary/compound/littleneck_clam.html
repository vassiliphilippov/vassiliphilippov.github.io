littleneck clam

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪtəlˌnɛk klæm / (AmE), / ˈlɪtəlˌnɛk klæm / (BrE)
a small young quahog, especially valued as a raw shellfish in cooking; popular as a food, particularly in New York
small edible quahog clam, eaten raw
american

Examples

She ordered a dozen littleneck clams on the half shell for the appetizer.
The restaurant is famous for its fresh littleneck clams harvested daily from local waters.
Many seafood lovers enjoy littleneck clams raw with a squeeze of lemon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quahog - an edible clam of the species Mercenaria mercenaria

Etymology

From 'littleneck', referring to a size classification, and 'clam', an edible bivalve mollusk.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɪt̬.nɛk klæm / (AmE), / ˈlɪt.əl.nɛk klæm / (BrE)
a small, young quahog clam, often prized for its tender meat and typically eaten raw or cooked
a small, young quahog clam

Examples

The seafood platter included fresh littleneck clams served on crushed ice with lemon wedges.
He prefers to steam littleneck clams with white wine and garlic for a light appetizer.
We harvested several dozen littleneck clams from the bay during low tide.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quahog - an edible clam native to the eastern coast of North America

Etymology

From 'little' + 'neck', possibly referring to the clam's small size and the shape of its shell; 'clam' from earlier English.
