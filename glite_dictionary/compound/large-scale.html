large-scale

1. ADJ

/ ˈlɑrdʒˌskeɪl / (AmE), / ˈlɑːdʒˌskeɪl / (BrE)
Involving considerable size, scope, or extent; covering a wide area or affecting many people or things.
involving great size or extent

Examples

The government announced a large-scale investigation into environmental violations.
They are planning a large-scale renovation of the entire campus next year.
The company faced losses after a large-scale cyberattack affected its core systems.

Synset Examples

a large-scale attack on AIDS is needed

Related Terms

  • Antonym: small-scale - involving only a limited area, number, or size
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01391352-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ lɑrdʒ skeɪl / (AmE), / lɑːdʒ skeɪl / (BrE)
Constructed, drawn, or made at a relatively large scale, especially for detailed examination or display, as with maps or plans.
constructed or drawn at a big scale

Examples

The engineer used a large-scale map to plan the new highway route.
A large-scale blueprint allows for more precise adjustments and modifications.
They created a large-scale model of the building for the presentation.

Synset Examples

large-scale maps

Related Terms

  • Antonym: small-scale - constructed or drawn at a small scale, showing less detail

Synset POS: s
