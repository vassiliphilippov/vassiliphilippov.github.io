genus Rhynia

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˈraɪniə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈraɪnɪə / (BrE)
A type genus of the family Rhyniaceae, consisting of small, leafless, dichotomously branching fossil plants with terminal sporangia and smooth branching rhizomes, found in Early Devonian deposits.
fossil genus of ancient leafless plants

Examples

The discovery of genus Rhynia fossils provided crucial insights into the evolution of early land plants.
Paleobotanists often cite genus Rhynia as one of the earliest examples of vascular plants.
Specimens belonging to genus Rhynia have been found in the Rhynie Chert of Scotland.

Etymology

From New Latin *Rhynia*, named after Rhynie, a village in Scotland where its fossils were discovered.
