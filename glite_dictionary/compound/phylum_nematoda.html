phylum Nematoda

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪləm ˌnɛməˈtoʊdə / (AmE), / ˈfʌɪləm ˌniːməˈtəʊdə / (BrE)
a large phylum of unsegmented, cylindrical worms that includes roundworms, threadworms, and eelworms
a phylum of unsegmented roundworms
medical

Examples

The phylum Nematoda consists of thousands of species found in nearly every environment on Earth.
Many parasites that affect plants and animals belong to the phylum Nematoda.
Researchers are studying the role of the phylum Nematoda in soil health and nutrient cycling.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nematode - any worm of the phylum Nematoda

Etymology

From Greek 'nēmatōdēs' (threadlike), from 'nēma' (thread).
