primary atypical pneumonia

1. NOUN

/ ˈpraɪˌmɛri ˌeɪˈtɪpɪkəl nuˈmoʊniə / (AmE), / ˈprʌɪməri ˌeɪˈtɪpɪkəl njuːˈməʊnɪə / (BrE)
A form of pneumonia, mainly affecting children and young adults, characterized by high fever and coughing, and caused by mycoplasma bacteria.
mild form of pneumonia caused by mycoplasma
medical

Examples

Many cases of primary atypical pneumonia occur in school-aged children during the winter months.
Treatment for primary atypical pneumonia usually involves antibiotics effective against mycoplasma bacteria.
He recovered from primary atypical pneumonia without being hospitalized, thanks to early detection and care.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: walking pneumonia - mild type of pneumonia with similar symptoms, often caused by mycoplasma
  • Degree Higher: pneumonia - inflammation of the lungs usually caused by infection

Etymology

From Greek 'pneumōn' (lung) and 'mycoplasma' (referring to the causative agent).
