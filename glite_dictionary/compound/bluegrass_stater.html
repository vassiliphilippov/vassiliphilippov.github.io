Bluegrass Stater

1. NOUN

/ bluːˌɡræs ˈsteɪtər / (AmE), / bluːˌɡrɑːs ˈsteɪtə / (BrE)
a native or resident of Kentucky, especially in reference to the state's nickname, the Bluegrass State
person from Kentucky

Examples

As a proud Bluegrass Stater, she always cheered for the University of Kentucky Wildcats.
Several Bluegrass Staters attended the annual Kentucky Derby this year.
Growing up as a Bluegrass Stater meant spending summers exploring the rolling hills of the countryside.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Kentuckian - a native or inhabitant of Kentucky

Etymology

Formed from Kentucky’s nickname ‘the Bluegrass State’ and the demonymic suffix ‘-er’.
