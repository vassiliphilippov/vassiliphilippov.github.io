genus Quercus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˈkwɜːrkəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈkwɜːkəs / (BrE)
A genus of deciduous or evergreen trees comprising all species of oaks.
the botanical genus of oaks

Examples

The genus Quercus includes hundreds of species found across the Northern Hemisphere.
Researchers studied several rare members of the genus Quercus in the forest.
Acorns are the fruit produced by trees in the genus Quercus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oak - a tree or shrub belonging to the genus Quercus

Etymology

From Latin 'quercus' meaning 'oak'.
