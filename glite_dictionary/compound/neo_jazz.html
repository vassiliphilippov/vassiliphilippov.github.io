neo jazz

1. NOUN

/ ˈniː.oʊ dʒæz / (AmE), / ˈniː.əʊ dʒæz / (BrE)
a style of jazz music that developed after 1940, often blending traditional jazz elements with modern styles or influences
modern style of jazz music after 1940

Examples

In recent years, neo jazz artists have gained popularity by blending electronic sounds with traditional jazz instruments.
The festival features a lineup of neo jazz performers pushing the boundaries of the genre.
Many young musicians are drawn to neo jazz for its innovative approach and fusion of musical influences.

Etymology

From Greek 'neo-' meaning 'new' and 'jazz'.
