Byzantine Greek

1. NOUN

/ baɪˌzæntiːn ˈɡrik / (AmE), / bɪˌzantɪn ˈɡriːk / (BrE)
the form of the Greek language used approximately from AD 600 to 1200, during the Byzantine Empire period
Greek language used AD 600-1200

Examples

Scholars often study Byzantine Greek to understand the evolution of religious texts written during the Middle Ages.
The liturgy in the Greek Orthodox Church is still influenced by Byzantine Greek.
Her research focuses on the grammatical structures unique to Byzantine Greek manuscripts.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Classical Greek - the form of Greek used from roughly the 9th to 4th centuries BC
  • Degree Higher: Modern Greek - the Greek language as used from the late medieval period to the present

Etymology

Byzantine from Byzantium (later Constantinople, now Istanbul), combined with Greek.
