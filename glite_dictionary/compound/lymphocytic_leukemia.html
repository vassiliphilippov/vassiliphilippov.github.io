lymphocytic leukemia

1. NOUN

/ lɪmˌfoʊˈsɪtɪk luˈkiːmiə / (AmE), / lɪmˌfəˈsɪtɪk luːˈkiːmiə / (BrE)
A type of leukemia marked by an abnormal increase of lymphocytic cells in the blood and enlargement of lymphoid tissues.
leukemia with excess lymphocytic cells
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed him with lymphocytic leukemia after analyzing his blood samples.
Treatment options for lymphocytic leukemia have improved significantly over the past decade.
She lost her brother to lymphocytic leukemia when he was just twelve years old.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leukemia - a malignant disease of the blood-forming tissues
  • Degree Lower: lymphoma - cancer of the lymphatic system

Etymology

From lymphocytic (relating to lymphocytes) + leukemia (from Greek 'leukos' meaning 'white' and 'haima' meaning 'blood').
