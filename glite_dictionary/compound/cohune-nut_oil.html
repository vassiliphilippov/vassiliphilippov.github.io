cohune-nut oil

1. NOUN

/ koʊˈhuːn nʌt ɔɪl / (AmE), / kəˈhuːn nʌt ɔɪl / (BrE)
a semisolid fat obtained from the nuts of the cohune palm, used especially in cooking and soap making
oil from cohune palm nuts

Examples

In traditional recipes, cohune-nut oil is valued for its rich flavor and high smoke point.
Soap manufacturers sometimes prefer cohune-nut oil for its moisturizing properties.
The villagers harvested cohune nuts and extracted cohune-nut oil for cooking and lighting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: palm oil - an edible oil obtained from the fruit of the oil palm

Etymology

From 'cohune', the name of the palm tree native to Central America, and 'nut oil'.
