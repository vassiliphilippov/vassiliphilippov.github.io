ethmoidal vein

1. NOUN

/ ɛθˈmɔɪdəl veɪn / (AmE), / ɛθˈmɔɪdəl veɪn / (BrE)
a vein that drains blood from the ethmoidal sinuses and empties into the superior ophthalmic vein
vein draining the ethmoidal sinuses
medical

Examples

The ethmoidal vein is important in draining blood from the sinuses above the nasal cavity.
Inflammation in the sinuses can sometimes affect the ethmoidal vein and surrounding structures.
During surgery, the surgeon carefully isolated the ethmoidal vein to minimize bleeding.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: superior ophthalmic vein - a vein into which the ethmoidal vein drains

Etymology

From 'ethmoid', referring to the ethmoid bone, plus 'vein'.
