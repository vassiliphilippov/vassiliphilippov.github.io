British East Africa

1. PROPN

/ ˌbrɪtɪʃ iːst ˈæf.rɪ.kə / (AmE), / ˌbrɪtɪʃ iːst ˈæf.rɪ.kə / (BrE)
a former group of British territories in eastern Africa, including Kenya, Tanganyika, Uganda, and Zanzibar, existing mainly from the late 19th to the mid-20th century
former British territories in eastern Africa
british

Examples

The railway played a crucial role in the development of British East Africa.
Trade routes through British East Africa connected the interior with the Indian Ocean ports.
Many historical documents detail the governance of British East Africa by the British Empire.

Etymology

Named for the eastern region of Africa held as British colonies during the colonial era.
