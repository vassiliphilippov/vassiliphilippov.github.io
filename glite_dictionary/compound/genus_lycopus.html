genus Lycopus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs laɪˈkoʊpəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs lɪˈkəʊpəs / (BrE)
A small genus of herbs in the mint family (Lamiaceae), commonly known as water horehounds.
genus of mint family herbs

Examples

The genus Lycopus includes several species that thrive in wetlands and marshy environments.
Researchers studied the distribution of genus Lycopus throughout North America and Europe.
Certain members of the genus Lycopus have historically been used in herbal medicine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: water horehound - common name for plants of the genus Lycopus

Etymology

From Latin 'Lycopus', from Greek 'lykos' (wolf) + 'pous' (foot), said to refer to the shape of the leaves.
