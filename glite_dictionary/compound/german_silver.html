German silver

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɜrmən ˈsɪlvɚ / (AmE), / ˈdʒɜːmən ˈsɪlvə / (BrE)
a silver-white alloy consisting mainly of copper, zinc, and nickel, but containing no actual silver
a copper-zinc-nickel alloy, no real silver

Examples

The candlesticks were made of German silver, giving them a bright, silvery sheen.
Cutlery once commonly used German silver because it was durable and attractive.
Although called German silver, the alloy does not actually contain any real silver.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nickel silver - a synonym for German silver, a copper-zinc-nickel alloy

Etymology

Named for its development by German metalworkers in the early 19th century to imitate silver.
