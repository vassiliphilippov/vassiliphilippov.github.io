war party

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔr ˌpɑrti / (AmE), / ˈwɔː ˌpɑːti / (BrE)
a political party or group that supports war or advocates for military action
political group supporting war

Examples

The war party in Congress pushed for immediate military intervention.
He was criticized for aligning himself with the war party during the conflict.
Debates between the war party and its opposition often became heated.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: peace party - a political party or group that opposes war or advocates for peace

2. NOUN

/ wɔr ˈpɑrti / (AmE), / wɔː ˈpɑːti / (BrE)
a group of people, especially Native Americans, who set out together to conduct a raid or attack against an enemy
group organized to wage or raid an enemy

Examples

A war party set out before dawn to ambush the opposing tribe's camp.
The chief led the war party across the river under the cover of darkness.
Historians have documented several notable war parties in pre-colonial North America.
