War of the Austrian Succession

1. NOUN

/ wɔr əv ði ɔːstriən səkˈsɛʃən / (AmE), / wɔːr əv ði ˈɒstriən səkˈsɛʃn / (BrE)
the war fought from 1740 to 1748, primarily over the succession to the Austrian throne, involving Prussia, Austria, and most major European powers
major European war (1740–1748) over Austrian succession

Examples

The War of the Austrian Succession dramatically altered the political landscape of eighteenth-century Europe.
Historians still debate the underlying causes of the War of the Austrian Succession.
The Treaty of Aix-la-Chapelle ended the War of the Austrian Succession in 1748.
