black-necked grebe

1. NOUN

/ ˈblækˌnɛkt ɡrib / (AmE), / ˈblækˌnɛkt ɡriːb / (BrE)
a small water bird with yellow ear tufts and a black neck, found in Eurasia, southern Africa, and western United States
species of grebe with a black neck and yellow ear tufts

Examples

The black-necked grebe is known for its striking black neck and bright yellow ear tufts during the breeding season.
We spotted several black-necked grebes swimming together on the lake in southern Africa.
During migration, the black-necked grebe often forms large flocks and can be seen on salt lakes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eared grebe - a North American name for the same species (Podiceps nigricollis)
