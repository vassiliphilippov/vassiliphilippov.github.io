I-beam

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪ ˌbiːm / (AmE), / ˈaɪ ˌbiːm / (BrE)
A steel or iron beam with a cross-section shaped like the letter 'I', commonly used in construction for structural support.
beam shaped like an 'I' in cross-section

Examples

The steel I-beam provided essential support for the bridge's deck.
Construction workers hoisted a massive I-beam into place on the building's frame.
Modern skyscrapers rely on I-beams for their strength and stability.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: girder - a large beam used to support structures

Etymology

Named for its resemblance in cross-section to the capital letter 'I'.
