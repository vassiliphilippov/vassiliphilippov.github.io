darning needle

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɑrnɪŋ ˌnidəl / (AmE), / ˈdɑːnɪŋ ˌniːdl̩ / (BrE)
A slender-bodied insect with iridescent wings, commonly known as a dragonfly, whose adults and nymphs feed on mosquitoes and other small insects.
a dragonfly; slender-winged insect

Examples

We watched a darning needle hover over the pond, its wings catching the sunlight.
Children used to believe that a darning needle could sew their lips shut if they told lies.
A darning needle darted swiftly through the air, hunting for mosquitoes near the water's edge.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dragonfly - a large, slender, iridescent-winged insect; another common name for darning needle

Etymology

Alludes to the resemblance of the insect's body to a sewing needle.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɑrnɪŋ ˌniːdəl / (AmE), / ˈdɑːnɪŋ ˌniːdl̩ / (BrE)
A long needle with a large eye, used for sewing with heavy thread or for embroidery.
large-eyed sewing needle

Examples

She threaded the darning needle carefully to repair the hole in her sweater.
A darning needle is essential when you're working with thick yarn or thread.
The old sewing box contained a silver darning needle passed down from her grandmother.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: embroidery needle - needle designed for embroidery, with a large eye

Etymology

From 'darn' (to mend) + 'needle'.
