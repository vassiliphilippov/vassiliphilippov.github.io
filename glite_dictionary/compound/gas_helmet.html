gas helmet

1. NOUN

/ ɡæs ˈhɛlmət / (AmE), / ɡæs ˈhɛlmɪt / (BrE)
a type of protective mask with a filter, designed to protect the face and lungs from poisonous gases, used especially in early chemical warfare
protective mask against poisonous gases

Examples

During World War I, soldiers were issued a gas helmet to protect themselves from chemical attacks.
The museum exhibits an original gas helmet used in early chemical warfare.
Unlike modern masks, the gas helmet covered the entire head and was less effective against certain gases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gas mask - a protective mask used to filter out poisonous gases; modern term
