to be a churl

1. VERB

/ bi ə tʃɜrl / (AmE), / bi ə tʃɜːl / (BrE)
to act in a rude or ill-mannered way
to behave rudely or without manners
archaic

Examples

Please don't be a churl when guests arrive at your home.
He was told not to be a churl in front of his grandmother.
It's easy to be a churl when you're having a bad day, but try to be polite.

Synset Examples

Don't be a churl; offer your seat to the elderly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: churlish - having a rude or surly disposition

Etymology

From Old English 'ceorl' (man of low status), later meaning a rude or surly person.
