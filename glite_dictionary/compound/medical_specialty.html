medical specialty

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛdɪkəl ˈspɛʃəlti / (AmE), / ˈmɛdɪkəl ˈspɛʃ(ə)lti / (BrE)
A branch of medical science concerned with a specific area of medicine, especially focusing on nonsurgical techniques.
branch of medicine (nonsurgical)
medical

Examples

She decided to pursue cardiology as her medical specialty after graduating from medical school.
There are numerous medical specialties within internal medicine, such as endocrinology and rheumatology.
Choosing a medical specialty requires considering one's interests and long-term career goals.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: surgical specialty - branch of medicine focused on surgical techniques

Etymology

From Latin 'medicus' (physician) and Late Latin 'specialitas' (a particular sort or kind).
