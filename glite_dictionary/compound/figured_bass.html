figured bass

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪɡjɚd ˌbeɪs / (AmE), / ˈfɪɡjəd ˌbeɪs / (BrE)
A bass part in music that is written out in full and accompanied by numbers indicating the chords to be played above it.
musical notation indicating chords above bass

Examples

The composer provided a figured bass for the harpsichord player to interpret during the performance.
Students learned how to realize a figured bass in their Baroque music class.
The cellist followed the figured bass while the keyboardist improvised harmonies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: basso continuo - a form of musical accompaniment using figured bass
