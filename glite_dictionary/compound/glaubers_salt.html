Glauber's salt

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡlaʊbərz sɑlt / (AmE), / ˈɡlaʊbəz sɒlt / (BrE)
A colorless crystalline form of sodium sulfate, used as a laxative and in various industrial processes.
hydrated sodium sulfate, Na2SO4·10H2O
medical

Examples

The laboratory ordered more Glauber's salt to use in their experiments.
Historically, Glauber's salt was administered as a laxative for digestive disorders.
Industrial processes sometimes utilize Glauber's salt for its dehydrating properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sodium sulfate - a white crystalline salt, Na2SO4, used in various industries

Etymology

Named after Johann Rudolf Glauber, a German-Dutch chemist who discovered it in the 17th century.
