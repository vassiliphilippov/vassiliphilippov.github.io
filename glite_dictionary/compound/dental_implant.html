dental implant

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛnt̬l ɪmˌplænt / (AmE), / ˈdɛntəl ɪmˌplɑːnt / (BrE)
an artificial device inserted into the jaw to replace a missing natural tooth
artificial device replacing a tooth
medical

Examples

The surgeon explained that a dental implant could restore function and appearance after tooth loss.
It can take several months for a dental implant to fully integrate with the jawbone.
Many patients choose a dental implant instead of traditional dentures for a more permanent solution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: implant - an object inserted into the body, especially by surgery

Etymology

From Latin 'dens' (tooth) and Latin 'implanto' (to insert or plant in).
