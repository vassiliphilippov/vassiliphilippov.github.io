chicken Marengo

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɪk.ɪn məˈrɛŋ.goʊ / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪk.ɪn məˈrɛŋ.gəʊ / (BrE)
a dish of braised chicken cooked with onions and mushrooms in a wine and tomato sauce
braised chicken dish with wine and tomato sauce

Examples

The chef prepared chicken Marengo for the dinner party, impressing all the guests with its rich flavors.
Have you ever tried making chicken Marengo at home using fresh tomatoes and a good wine?
She ordered the chicken Marengo at the French restaurant, eager to taste the classic dish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coq au vin - French chicken dish cooked in wine, lardons, mushrooms, and garlic

Etymology

Named after the Battle of Marengo, where the dish is said to have originated for Napoleon.
