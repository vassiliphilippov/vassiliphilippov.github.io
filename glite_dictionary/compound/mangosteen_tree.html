mangosteen tree

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæŋɡəˌstin ˌtri / (AmE), / ˈmæŋɡəstiːn ˌtriː / (BrE)
An evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia, known for its thick, leathery leaves and sweet, edible purple fruit called the mangosteen.
tropical fruit tree of Southeast Asia

Examples

The mangosteen tree thrives in warm, humid climates with plenty of rainfall.
Farmers in Indonesia cultivate the mangosteen tree for its prized fruit.
The leaves of the mangosteen tree are thick, glossy, and leathery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mangosteen - the sweet, edible purple fruit of the mangosteen tree

Etymology

From 'mangosteen', ultimately from Malay 'manggis', plus 'tree'.
