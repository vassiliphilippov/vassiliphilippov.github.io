controversial ideology

1. NOUN

/ kənˌtroʊvɚʃəl ˌaɪdiˈɑlədʒi / (AmE), / kɒnˌtrɒvɜːʃəl ˌaɪdiˈɒlədʒi / (BrE)
a set of ideas or beliefs that provokes disagreement, debate, or strong opposition
ideas provoking strong disagreement

Examples

The politician was criticized for endorsing a controversial ideology during the debate.
Universities sometimes struggle with how to address speakers who promote controversial ideologies.
History has shown that a controversial ideology can deeply divide societies.

Synset Examples

The Hitler Channel is known for spreading controversial ideologies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ideology - a system of ideas and ideals forming the basis of a theory or policy
