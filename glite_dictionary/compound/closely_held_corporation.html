closely held corporation

1. NOUN

/ ˈkloʊsli hɛld ˌkɔːrpəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈkləʊsli hɛld ˌkɒrpəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
A corporation whose shares are owned by a small number of shareholders, with limited public trading and no intention to sell major holdings.
company with shares held by few owners

Examples

The Smith family still controls the closely held corporation they founded a century ago.
As a closely held corporation, its shares rarely change hands on the market.
Regulations for a closely held corporation may differ from those for publicly traded companies.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: publicly traded company - A company whose shares are traded freely on a stock exchange
  • Degree Lower: private company - A business whose shares are not offered for public sale
