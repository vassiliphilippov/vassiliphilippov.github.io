safety belt

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪfti bɛlt / (AmE), / ˈseɪfti bɛlt / (BrE)
A belt designed to secure a person to a seat or object as a protective restraint to prevent injury, especially in vehicles or when working at a height.
protective belt to prevent injury

Examples

Please fasten your safety belt before the plane takes off.
The technician wore a safety belt while working on the high scaffolding.
New laws require all car passengers to wear a safety belt at all times.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seat belt - a belt securing someone in a seat, especially in a vehicle
