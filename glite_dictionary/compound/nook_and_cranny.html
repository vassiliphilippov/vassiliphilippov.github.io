nook and cranny

1. NOUN

/ nʊk ənd ˈkræni / (AmE), / nʊk ənd ˈkræni / (BrE)
every small or hidden part of a place or thing, especially when emphasizing thoroughness in searching or exploring.
every small or hidden place or part

Examples

The detective searched every nook and cranny of the old mansion for clues.
Dust had gathered in all the nooks and crannies of the bookshelf by the window.
She explored all the nooks and crannies of the city during her trip.

Synset Examples

he explored every nook and cranny of science

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corner - a place or angle where two sides meet; a remote or hidden part
  • Synonym: crevice - a narrow opening or fissure, especially in a rock or wall
