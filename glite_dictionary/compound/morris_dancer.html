morris dancer

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɔrɪs ˌdænsər / (AmE), / ˈmɒrɪs ˌdɑːnsə / (BrE)
a person who performs a traditional English folk dance known as morris dancing, often wearing costumes and using props such as sticks or handkerchiefs
person who performs morris dancing
british

Examples

Every summer, the local morris dancer troupe performs at the village festival.
He became a skilled morris dancer after years of practice and dedication to the tradition.
Tourists gathered to watch the colorful costumes and energetic moves of each morris dancer in the parade.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: morris dancing - the traditional English folk dance performed

Etymology

From 'Morris', possibly from 'Moorish,' referring to the old English folk dance style; plus 'dancer.'
