genetic lottery

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˈnɛtɪk ˈlɑːtəri / (AmE), / dʒɪˈnɛtɪk ˈlɒtəri / (BrE)
the random distribution of inherited genetic traits, which may result in advantages or disadvantages for an individual
random chance in inherited genetic traits

Examples

Some believe their height or athleticism is simply the result of the genetic lottery.
Winning the genetic lottery can sometimes mean fewer health problems later in life.
Researchers study how the genetic lottery contributes to differences in intelligence and personality.
