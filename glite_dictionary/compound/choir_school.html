choir school

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwaɪɚ skuːl / (AmE), / ˈkwaɪə skuːl / (BrE)
A school, often attached to a cathedral or monastery, where children with singing ability are given a general education and trained for singing in choirs.
school for training choir singers

Examples

He attended a choir school attached to the city's main cathedral.
Many famous singers began their training at a choir school.
The choir school provides both musical and academic education for its students.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cathedral school - a school attached to a cathedral, providing education often including choir training
