assault rifle

1. NOUN

/ əˈsɔlt ˌraɪfəl / (AmE), / əˈsɔːlt ˌraɪfəl / (BrE)
a rapid-fire rifle with a detachable magazine, capable of automatic or semiautomatic fire, designed for military use
military automatic or semiautomatic rifle

Examples

The soldier carried an assault rifle during the training exercise.
The ban on assault rifles sparked a nationwide debate on gun control.
Modern assault rifle designs often feature modular components for different missions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: machine gun - fully automatic gun, often used in combat
  • Degree Lower: submachine gun - lightweight automatic gun firing pistol ammunition

Etymology

From 'assault', meaning attack, and 'rifle', from French 'rifle' (grooved firearm).

2. NOUN

/ əˈsɔlt ˌraɪfəl / (AmE), / əˈsɔːlt ˌraɪfəl / (BrE)
A rapid-fire, magazine-fed military firearm designed for use by soldiers.
rapid-fire military firearm

Examples

The soldier carried an assault rifle during the operation.
Strict regulations govern the ownership of an assault rifle in many countries.
An assault rifle is standard issue for infantry in modern armies.

Synset Examples

The military used an assault rifle in training.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: rifle - a gun with a long barrel
  • Degree Higher: machine gun - an automatic gun that fires bullets rapidly

Etymology

From 'assault' (a violent attack) + 'rifle', referring to use in military assaults.
