sulfanilic acid

1. NOUN

/ sʌl.fəˈnɪl.ɪk ˌæs.ɪd / (AmE), / sʌl.fəˈnɪl.ɪk ˌæs.ɪd / (BrE)
a crystalline organic acid derived from aniline, used chiefly in the manufacture of dyes and other chemicals
a crystalline acid used in dye manufacture
medical

Examples

The laboratory ordered a fresh supply of sulfanilic acid for the synthesis experiments.
Sulfanilic acid is an important intermediate in the production of azo dyes.
Proper handling of sulfanilic acid is necessary due to its chemical properties.

Etymology

From 'sulfonic' and 'aniline,' indicating its chemical origin as a sulfonated derivative of aniline.
