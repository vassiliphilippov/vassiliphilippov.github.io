fly-by-night

1. ADJ

/ ˈflaɪ baɪ ˌnaɪt / (AmE), / ˈflaɪ baɪ ˌnaɪt / (BrE)
(of a person or business) unreliable or dishonest and likely to disappear suddenly, especially to avoid paying debts or being held accountable.
unreliable or dishonest (esp. business/person)

Examples

Consumers are often warned about fly-by-night operations that offer deals too good to be true.
The fly-by-night builder vanished with their deposit before any work was started.
She lost her savings to a fly-by-night investment firm.

Synset Examples

They advised against investing in fly-by-night schemes.
He only uses fly-by-night contractors for his projects.
That fly-by-night company disappeared after a week.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: trustworthy - reliable and deserving of trust
  • Antonym: reputable - having a good reputation; trustworthy, reliable

Etymology

Originally referred to a person who flits from place to place at night to avoid creditors; first recorded in the 19th century.
