organ of Corti

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrɡən əv ˈkɔrti / (AmE), / ˈɔːɡən əv ˈkɔːti / (BrE)
a structure in the cochlea of the inner ear that contains the sensory receptors for hearing
sensory organ for hearing in the inner ear
medical

Examples

The organ of Corti converts sound vibrations into nerve signals sent to the brain.
Damage to the organ of Corti can result in permanent hearing loss.
Scientists are studying how the organ of Corti repairs itself after injury.

Etymology

Named after Italian anatomist Alfonso Corti, who described it in 1851.
