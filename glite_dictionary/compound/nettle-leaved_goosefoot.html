nettle-leaved goosefoot

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɛt̬l livd ˈɡusˌfʊt / (AmE), / ˈnɛtl liːvd ˈɡuːsfʊt / (BrE)
A European annual plant, Chenopodium murale, with coarsely toothed leaves, found widely in the United States and southern Canada.
a European annual plant with toothed leaves

Examples

The field was full of nettle-leaved goosefoot, easily recognized by its jagged-edged leaves.
Gardeners often remove nettle-leaved goosefoot as it can become an invasive weed.
You can find nettle-leaved goosefoot growing in disturbed soils across North America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Chenopodium murale - scientific name for nettle-leaved goosefoot
