amyl alcohol

1. NOUN

/ ˈæmɪl ˈælkəˌhɔl / (AmE), / ˈæmɪl ˈælkəhɒl / (BrE)
A colorless liquid mixture of isomeric alcohols, used as a solvent and in organic synthesis.
chemical solvent; mixture of alcohols
medical

Examples

Amyl alcohol is commonly used as a solvent in the laboratory for organic reactions.
The production process involves the extraction and purification of amyl alcohol from fusel oil.
Because of its distinctive smell, amyl alcohol can be easily identified in industrial settings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pentanol - an alcohol with five carbon atoms; the main component of amyl alcohol

Etymology

From 'amyl', relating to the pentyl group (from Latin 'amylum' for starch), and 'alcohol'.
