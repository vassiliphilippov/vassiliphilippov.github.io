sweet bay

1. NOUN

/ swit beɪ / (AmE), / swiːt beɪ / (BrE)
A North American shrub or small tree (Magnolia virginiana) with fragrant white flowers, native to the southeastern United States.
North American fragrant shrub or tree

Examples

The sweet bay is often used in landscaping for its attractive, glossy leaves.
Wildlife is attracted to the sweet bay because of its aromatic flowers.
In spring, the sweet bay blooms with a profusion of creamy white blossoms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bay tree - an evergreen tree (Laurus nobilis) whose aromatic leaves are used as a herb
