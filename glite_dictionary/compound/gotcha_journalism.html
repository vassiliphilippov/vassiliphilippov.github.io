gotcha journalism

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɑtʃə ˈdʒɜrnəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈɡɒtʃə ˈdʒɜːnəlɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
a style of journalism that seeks to catch public figures off guard or expose their mistakes, often in an aggressive or confrontational manner
aggressive journalism seeking to expose mistakes

Examples

Some critics argue that gotcha journalism undermines serious political discussion.
She accused the reporter of practicing gotcha journalism instead of focusing on the issues.
Candidates often prepare for interviews to avoid falling victim to gotcha journalism.

Synset Examples

His interview style is often criticized for gotcha journalism.
She avoided gotcha journalism in her reporting.
The debate turned into a gotcha journalism free-for-all.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ambush journalism - aggressive journalism involving unexpected or confrontational questioning

Etymology

From the colloquial 'gotcha', meaning 'got you', combined with 'journalism'.
