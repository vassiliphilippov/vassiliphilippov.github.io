royal purple

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɔɪəl ˈpɝːpəl / (AmE), / ˈrɔɪəl ˈpɜːpl̩ / (BrE)
a deep shade of purple with a red undertone, traditionally associated with royalty.
a deep reddish-purple color

Examples

The curtains in the palace were dyed a rich royal purple to signify wealth and status.
She wore a gown of royal purple at the gala, making a dramatic entrance.
Artists often use royal purple to depict luxury and regality in their paintings.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: purple - a color intermediate between red and blue
  • Degree Higher: violet - a bluish-purple color, less red than purple
