brown sauce

1. NOUN

/ ˈbraʊn sɔs / (AmE), / ˈbraʊn sɔːs / (BrE)
A sauce made with soy sauce as a base, often used in Chinese and other East Asian cuisines.
soy sauce-based sauce in East Asian cooking

Examples

Many Chinese takeout dishes are served with brown sauce that adds a rich flavor to vegetables and meats.
She tried to replicate the restaurant's brown sauce for her homemade stir-fry.
The chef recommended using oyster sauce if you don't have brown sauce available.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbraʊn sɔs / (AmE), / ˈbraʊn sɔːs / (BrE)
A savory sauce made from beef or veal stock thickened with a butter and flour roux, often seasoned with herbs or Worcestershire sauce.
savory sauce thickened with roux and stock

Examples

She poured some brown sauce over her steak to add extra flavor.
The chef prepared a rich, flavorful brown sauce to accompany the roast beef.
You can make a basic brown sauce by combining beef stock with a roux and herbs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gravy - a sauce made from meat juices, thickened with flour
