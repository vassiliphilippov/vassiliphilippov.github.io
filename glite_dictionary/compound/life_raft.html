life raft

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪf ræft / (AmE), / ˈlaɪf rɑːft / (BrE)
a small, inflatable or rigid raft used for emergency evacuation at sea when a ship must be abandoned
emergency raft for abandoning ship

Examples

The crew inflated the life raft and climbed aboard after abandoning the vessel.
During the drill, every passenger practiced how to board a life raft quickly and safely.
The life raft was spotted by rescuers floating near the wreckage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lifeboat - a sturdy boat for rescuing people at sea

2. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪf ræft / (AmE), / ˈlaɪf rɑːft / (BrE)
something that provides support or relief in a difficult or dangerous situation.
source of support in trouble

Examples

During the economic crisis, the government assistance program became a much-needed life raft for thousands.
For many people, therapy acts as a life raft during periods of emotional distress.
The unexpected inheritance was a financial life raft that saved the family from bankruptcy.

Synset Examples

The charity event was a life raft for struggling families.
Education can serve as a life raft for many.
He saw the new job offer as a life raft during tough times.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lifeline - a thing on which someone or something depends or which provides a means of escape from a difficult situation

Etymology

Modelled after the literal sense of 'life raft', a raft used in emergencies at sea.
