promissory note

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɑː.məˌsɔːr.i noʊt / (AmE), / ˈprɒ.mɪ.səri nəʊt / (BrE)
a signed written promise to pay a specified sum of money, either on demand or at a fixed future time, to a named person or to the bearer
written promise to pay money
legal

Examples

The bank required him to sign a promissory note before granting the loan.
She defaulted on her promissory note and faced legal action as a result.
A promissory note is often used in business dealings as a formal evidence of debt.

Synset Examples

I had to co-sign his note at the bank

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: IOU - an informal written acknowledgment of debt
  • Synonym: bill of exchange - a written order to pay a specified sum to a named person or entity

Etymology

From Latin 'promissorius' (containing a promise) + English 'note'.
