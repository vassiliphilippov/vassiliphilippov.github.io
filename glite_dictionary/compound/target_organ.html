target organ

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɑrɡɪt ˈɔrɡən / (AmE), / ˈtɑːɡɪt ˈɔːɡən / (BrE)
an organ intended to receive a therapeutic dose of a radioactive substance, especially in radiology or nuclear medicine
organ targeted in radiation therapy
medical

Examples

The doctor selected the liver as the target organ for the radioactive treatment.
Damage to the target organ can sometimes be minimized by adjusting the dosage.
In cancer therapy, identifying the correct target organ is crucial for effective treatment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: target tissue - tissue intended to receive treatment, often by drugs or radiation

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɑrɡət ˈɔrɡən / (AmE), / ˈtɑːɡɪt ˈɔːɡən / (BrE)
an organ that is primarily affected by a particular hormone or drug, responding specifically to its action
organ mainly affected by a hormone or drug
medical

Examples

The thyroid gland is the target organ for thyroid-stimulating hormone.
After administration of the drug, physicians monitor its impact on the target organ.
Insulin acts primarily on the liver as its main target organ.
