Jamaica dogwood

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˈmeɪ.kə ˈdɔːɡ.wʊd / (AmE), / dʒəˈmeɪ.kə ˈdɒɡ.wʊd / (BrE)
a small tree native to the West Indies and Florida, known for its large compound leaves, showy flowers, and use as a source of fish poison
a small tropical tree used for fish poisoning

Examples

The Jamaica dogwood is often planted for its attractive flowers and unusual seedpods.
Traditional fishermen used the bark of the Jamaica dogwood to stun fish in the rivers.
Bees are attracted to the sweet-smelling blooms of the Jamaica dogwood during spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Piscidia piscipula - scientific name for Jamaica dogwood
