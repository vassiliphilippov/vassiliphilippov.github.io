American liquorice

1. NOUN

/ əˈmɛrɪkən ˈlɪkərɪʃ / (AmE), / əˈmɛrɪkən ˈlɪk.ər.ɪs / (BrE)
A North American plant similar to true licorice, with a root possessing similar properties.
North American licorice-like plant

Examples

Many Native American tribes traditionally used American liquorice for medicinal purposes.
You can find American liquorice growing in prairies and along riverbanks.
The root of American liquorice is sometimes used as a natural sweetener.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: liquorice - a sweet, black substance obtained from the root of a Mediterranean plant or the plant itself
