Mujahidin-e Khalq Organization

1. NOUN

/ muːˌdʒɑːhɪˈdiːn eɪ khælˈk ɔːrɡənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / muːˌdʒɑːhɪˈdiːn eɪ khælˈk ɔːɡənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
a militant Iranian political organization founded in the 1960s, originally opposed to the Shah and later to the Islamic government, combining Marxist and Islamic ideologies; designated as a terrorist group by some countries
Iranian militant political organization

Examples

The Mujahidin-e Khalq Organization was founded to oppose the Shah's regime in Iran.
Several countries have designated the Mujahidin-e Khalq Organization as a terrorist group due to its activities.
The Mujahidin-e Khalq Organization has both an armed and a political wing operating in exile.

Etymology

From Persian, 'Mujāhidīn-e Khalq' meaning 'Holy Warriors of the People'.
