bubble jet printer

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌbəl dʒɛt ˈprɪnɾɚ / (AmE), / ˈbʌbəl dʒɛt ˈprɪntə / (BrE)
a type of ink-jet printer that uses tiny heated elements to produce bubbles of ink, which are then ejected onto paper to form images or text
type of ink-jet printer using heated bubbles

Examples

The company decided to replace all the old laser printers with bubble jet printers for better photo quality.
With a bubble jet printer, you can produce vivid color documents at home.
She bought a new bubble jet printer because it's quieter and more affordable than some other models.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ink-jet printer - a printer that sprays ink onto paper to create images

Etymology

Formed from 'bubble', 'jet', and 'printer'; 'bubble jet' is a trademarked term introduced by Canon in the 1980s.
