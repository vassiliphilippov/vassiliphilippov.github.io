soft-finned fish

1. NOUN

/ sɔft fɪnd fɪʃ / (AmE), / sɒft fɪnd fɪʃ / (BrE)
A fish of the superorder Malacopterygii, characterized by having soft, flexible fin rays rather than spiny ones.
fish with soft, flexible fin rays (Malacopterygii)

Examples

The salmon is a well-known example of a soft-finned fish found in many rivers and oceans.
Researchers studied the unique bone structure of several soft-finned fish species collected from deep lakes.
Some aquarium enthusiasts prefer keeping soft-finned fish because of their elegant swimming style.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: spiny-finned fish - fish with fins supported by hard, spiny rays (Acanthopterygii)
