narrow-leaved flame flower

1. NOUN

/ ˈnæroʊ liːvd fleɪm ˌflaʊər / (AmE), / ˈnærəʊ liːvd fleɪm ˌflaʊə / (BrE)
A perennial plant native to the southwestern United States, similar to Talinum aurantiacum but with narrower leaves and yellow-orange flowers.
a plant with narrow leaves and yellow-orange flowers

Examples

The narrow-leaved flame flower thrives in dry, rocky soils throughout the southwestern United States.
Botanists distinguish the narrow-leaved flame flower from related species by its slender leaves and vivid blooms.
We admired the striking yellow-orange blossoms of the narrow-leaved flame flower during our hike.
