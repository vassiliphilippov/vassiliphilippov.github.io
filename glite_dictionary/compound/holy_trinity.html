Holy Trinity

1. NOUN

/ ˈhoʊli ˈtrɪn.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˈhəʊli ˈtrɪn.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
In Christian doctrine, the unity of the Father, the Son, and the Holy Spirit as three persons in one Godhead.
the three persons of the Christian Godhead

Examples

Many Christians pray in the name of the Holy Trinity.
The doctrine of the Holy Trinity is a foundation of mainstream Christian beliefs.
Churches often have artwork depicting the Holy Trinity in various forms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Trinity - the state of being three; a group of three closely related members

Etymology

From Latin 'trinitas' (triad, threefold).
