electric dipole

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈlɛktrɪk ˈdaɪˌpoʊl / (AmE), / ɪˈlɛktrɪk ˈdʌɪpəʊl / (BrE)
a pair of equal and opposite electric charges separated by a small distance, considered as a unit
pair of opposite electric charges

Examples

A water molecule can be modeled as an electric dipole due to its charge separation.
When placed in an electric field, an electric dipole experiences a torque that tries to align it with the field.
The strength of an electric dipole is measured by its dipole moment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dipole - a pair of equal and opposite charges or magnetic poles
