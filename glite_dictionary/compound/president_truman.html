President Truman

1. PROPN

/ ˈprɛzɪdənt ˈtruːmən / (AmE), / ˈprɛzɪdənt ˈtruːmən / (BrE)
the 33rd President of the United States (1945–1953), known for authorizing the use of atomic bombs against Japan and succeeding Franklin D. Roosevelt.
33rd U.S. President, authorized atomic bombs in WWII

Examples

President Truman made the difficult decision to authorize the use of atomic bombs against Japan.
Many historians debate whether President Truman's policies shaped the early years of the Cold War.
When Franklin D. Roosevelt died, President Truman assumed office during a crucial period of World War II.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Harry S. Truman - full name of President Truman, 33rd President of the United States
