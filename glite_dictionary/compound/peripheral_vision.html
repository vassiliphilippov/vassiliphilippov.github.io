peripheral vision

1. NOUN

/ pəˈrɪfərəl ˈvɪʒən / (AmE), / pəˈrɪfərəl ˈvɪʒən / (BrE)
the ability to see objects outside the direct line of vision, at the edges of the visual field
ability to see at the edges of vision

Examples

Athletes often rely on their peripheral vision to detect movement from the sides while focusing straight ahead.
Driving at night requires good peripheral vision to notice hazards coming from the edges of the road.
Doctors test peripheral vision to check for eye conditions such as glaucoma.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: central vision - vision in the center part of the visual field

Etymology

From 'peripheral,' meaning 'relating to the edge,' and 'vision.' 'Peripheral' is from Greek 'periphereia' meaning 'circumference.'
