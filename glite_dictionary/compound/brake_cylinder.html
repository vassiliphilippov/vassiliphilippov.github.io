brake cylinder

1. NOUN

/ breɪk ˈsɪlɪndɚ / (AmE), / breɪk ˈsɪlɪndə / (BrE)
A cylinder in a vehicle's braking system that holds and applies pressure to brake fluid when a piston moves, causing the brakes to engage.
cylinder applying pressure in a brake system

Examples

The mechanic discovered that the brake cylinder was leaking, affecting the car's stopping power.
If the brake cylinder fails, it can result in reduced braking efficiency.
Many vehicles need periodic inspection of the brake cylinder to ensure safe operation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: master cylinder - main hydraulic cylinder controlling brake pressure
