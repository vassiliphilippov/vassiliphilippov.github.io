eleostearic acid

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛli.oʊˈstɪərɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / ˌɛli.əʊˈstɪərɪk ˈasɪd / (BrE)
a crystalline unsaturated fatty acid, typically found in certain plant oils
a crystalline unsaturated fatty acid

Examples

Vernonia oil is a rich source of eleostearic acid, a valuable industrial compound.
Researchers studied the polymerization behavior of eleostearic acid in various conditions.
Linseed oil contains a different structure compared to eleostearic acid, affecting its drying properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fatty acid - a carboxylic acid with a long aliphatic chain, which can be saturated or unsaturated

Etymology

From Greek 'elaion' (oil) + 'stear' (tallow, fat), referring to its occurrence in certain oils.
