sickle lucerne

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪkəl lʊˈsɜrn / (AmE), / ˈsɪkəl ljuːˈsɜːn / (BrE)
a European plant of the legume family, naturalized in North America, with yellow flowers and sickle-shaped seed pods
a plant with yellow flowers and curved seed pods

Examples

The farmer planted sickle lucerne to improve the soil quality in his pasture.
You can identify sickle lucerne by its bright yellow flowers and distinctive curved seed pods.
In some regions, sickle lucerne is valued as both a forage crop and a cover plant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Medicago falcata - scientific name for sickle lucerne
