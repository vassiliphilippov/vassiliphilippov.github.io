dog stinkhorn

1. NOUN

/ dɔg ˈstɪŋˌhɔrn / (AmE), / dɒg ˈstɪŋˌhɔːn / (BrE)
A type of stinkhorn fungus with a stalk lacking a cap and a slimy gleba near the apex that attracts insects.
a stinkhorn fungus lacking a cap

Examples

The dog stinkhorn is known for its unpleasant odor, which attracts flies to aid in spore dispersal.
Botanists discovered several dog stinkhorns growing in the damp woodland after the rain.
Children were curious about the strange appearance of the dog stinkhorn in their backyard.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stinkhorn - a group of foul-smelling fungi whose fruiting bodies attract insects
