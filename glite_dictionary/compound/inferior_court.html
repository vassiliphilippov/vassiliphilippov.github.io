inferior court

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈfɪriɚ kɔrt / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɪəriə kɔːt / (BrE)
a court whose decisions may be reviewed and reversed by a higher court
court below a higher appellate court
legal

Examples

The district court is an inferior court whose rulings can be appealed to the high court.
Cases from an inferior court are often reviewed by a superior court.
In the judicial hierarchy, an inferior court does not set binding precedent over other courts.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: superior court - a court that hears appeals from lower courts

Etymology

From Latin 'inferior' (lower) and 'court' (legal tribunal).
