wood coal

1. NOUN

/ wʊd koʊl / (AmE), / wʊd kəʊl / (BrE)
a carbon-rich material obtained by heating wood or other organic matter in the absence of air; charcoal
charcoal made from wood

Examples

The blacksmith used wood coal to heat the metal in the forge.
Traditional water filters often contain wood coal to trap impurities.
Early artists sometimes drew with sticks of wood coal for bold, dark lines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: charcoal - a black, porous form of carbon made from burning wood without air

2. NOUN

/ wʊd koʊl / (AmE), / wʊd kəʊl / (BrE)
A type of coal that is intermediate in properties between peat and bituminous coal, often identified with lignite.
type of coal between peat and bituminous coal

Examples

Some power plants use wood coal as a fuel source due to its abundance in certain regions.
Geologists found large deposits of wood coal near the river delta.
Compared to bituminous coal, wood coal produces less energy when burned.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lignite - a soft brownish-black coal showing traces of plant structure, intermediate between bituminous coal and peat
  • Degree Lower: peat - partially decayed plant matter found in bogs, used as fuel, and the lowest rank of coal
  • Degree Higher: bituminous coal - a type of coal with higher carbon content than lignite, used as fuel and in steelmaking
