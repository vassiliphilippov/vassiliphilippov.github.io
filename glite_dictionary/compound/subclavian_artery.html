subclavian artery

1. NOUN

/ sʌbˈkleɪviən ˈɑːrˌtɛri / (AmE), / sʌbˈkleɪvɪən ˈɑːtəri / (BrE)
either of two major arteries that carry blood to the neck, shoulders, and arms from the heart
artery supplying neck and arms
medical

Examples

A blockage in the subclavian artery can lead to reduced blood flow to the arm.
Doctors repaired damage to the patient's subclavian artery after the accident.
The left subclavian artery branches off the aortic arch.

Etymology

From Latin 'subclavius', meaning 'under the clavicle' (collarbone), referring to its anatomical location.
