pediatric medicine

1. NOUN

/ ˌpiːdiˈætrɪk ˈmɛdəsən / (AmE), / ˌpiːdɪˈatrɪk ˈmɛdɪsɪn / (BrE)
The branch of medicine concerned with the medical care and treatment of infants, children, and adolescents.
medical care of children
medical

Examples

After graduating from medical school, she decided to specialize in pediatric medicine.
Advancements in pediatric medicine have significantly increased the survival rates of premature infants.
The hospital is renowned for its contributions to pediatric medicine and childhood health.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pediatrics - the branch of medicine concerned with children and their diseases

Etymology

From Greek pais, paidos (child) and Latin medicina (medicine).
