false imprisonment

1. NOUN

/ fɔls ɪmˈprɪzənmənt / (AmE), / fɔːls ɪmˈprɪzənmənt / (BrE)
(law) the illegal confinement of a person without legal authority or justification.
illegal confinement without legal authority
legal

Examples

The court awarded damages to the plaintiff for false imprisonment by the security guards.
Being detained in a locked room without cause is a form of false imprisonment.
She filed a lawsuit alleging false imprisonment after being held by the police without charge.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kidnapping - the unlawful seizure and carrying away of a person by force
  • Degree Lower: imprisonment - the state of being confined in prison
  • Degree Lower: arrest - the act of detaining someone legally under authority
