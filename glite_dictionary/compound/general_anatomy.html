general anatomy

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɛn.ɚ.əl əˈnæt.ə.mi / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛn.ər.əl əˈnæt.ə.mi / (BrE)
the branch of biology concerned with the structure and organization of animals.
study of animal structure
medical

Examples

The course in general anatomy covers the basic structure of all major animal groups.
Medical students must master general anatomy before moving on to specialized subjects.
Her research in general anatomy helped clarify several complex physiological processes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anatomy - the study of the bodily structure of living organisms

Etymology

From Latin 'anatomia', meaning dissection; 'general' denotes a broad, overall study.
