alkane series

1. NOUN

/ ˈæl.keɪn ˈsɪr.iːz / (AmE), / ˈæl.keɪn ˈsɪə.riːz / (BrE)
a group of non-aromatic saturated hydrocarbons with the general formula CnH(2n+2), including methane, ethane, propane, and others.
group of saturated hydrocarbons in chemistry

Examples

The alkane series includes methane, ethane, propane, and other saturated hydrocarbons.
Students are required to memorize the structural formulas of the alkane series in organic chemistry class.
Petroleum is an important source of compounds belonging to the alkane series.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alkane - a saturated hydrocarbon with single bonds

Etymology

From 'alkane' (chemical term) and 'series' (sequence), alkane from 'alkyl' and '-ane'.
