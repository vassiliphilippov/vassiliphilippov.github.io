to kena sai

1. VERB

/ kəˈnɑ saɪ / (AmE), / kəˈnɑː saɪ / (BrE)
(slang) To be embarrassed, caught in a bad situation, or to make a mistake or blunder.
to be embarrassed or make a blunder (slang)
slang

Examples

She tried to play it cool at the presentation but ended up kena sai in front of everyone.
If you don't double-check your work, you might kena sai like last time.
He really kena sai when his secret was revealed to the whole office.

Synset Examples

She felt embarrassed after she kena sai in public.
If you’re not careful, you might kena sai like I did.
He made a mistake and kena sai during the meeting.

Etymology

From Malay 'kena' (to get; to suffer) and Hokkien 'sai' (excrement), originally meaning 'to get into trouble.'
