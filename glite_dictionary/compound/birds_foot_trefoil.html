bird's foot trefoil

1. NOUN

/ bɝdz fʊt ˈtriːfɔɪl / (AmE), / bɜːdz fʊt ˈtriːfɔɪl / (BrE)
a small flowering plant of the pea family with yellow flowers and slender seed pods that resemble a bird's foot, native to Europe and Asia
a yellow-flowered legume plant

Examples

The meadow was dotted with bird's foot trefoil, adding splashes of yellow among the grass.
Conservationists often plant bird's foot trefoil to improve habitats for butterflies and bees.
We identified several wildflowers, including bird's foot trefoil, along the roadside.

Etymology

So named because the seed pods resemble a bird's foot; 'trefoil' refers to the three-lobed leaves, from Old French 'trefueille', from Latin 'trifolium'.

2. NOUN

/ bɝːdz fʊt ˈtriːfɔɪl / (AmE), / bɜːdz fʊt ˈtriːfɔɪl / (BrE)
a European plant (Lotus corniculatus) of the pea family with yellow flowers and claw-shaped seed pods, cultivated for forage and introduced in America
a European forage plant with claw-shaped pods

Examples

The meadow was covered in bright yellow bird's foot trefoil during the spring.
Farmers often grow bird's foot trefoil as a forage crop for their livestock.
You can identify bird's foot trefoil by its unique claw-shaped seed pods.

Etymology

So named for the shape of its seed pods, which resemble a bird's foot; 'trefoil' from Latin 'trifolium' (three-leaved).
