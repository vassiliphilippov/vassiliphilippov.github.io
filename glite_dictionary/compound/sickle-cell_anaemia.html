sickle-cell anaemia

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪkəl sɛl əˈniːmiə / (AmE), / ˈsɪkəl sɛl əˈniːmiə / (BrE)
A hereditary blood disorder most common among people of African descent, characterized by red blood cells that assume a crescent or sickle shape, leading to anemia and related health problems.
hereditary blood disorder with sickle-shaped cells
medical british

Examples

People living with sickle-cell anaemia frequently experience episodes of pain known as sickle cell crises.
New treatments have improved the life expectancy of children born with sickle-cell anaemia.
She was diagnosed with sickle-cell anaemia after a series of unexplained illnesses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anemia - a condition marked by a deficiency of red blood cells or hemoglobin
  • Synonym: sickle cell disease - collective term for disorders involving sickle-shaped red blood cells

Etymology

From 'sickle' (due to cell shape) and 'anaemia', itself from Greek 'anaimia' (lack of blood).
