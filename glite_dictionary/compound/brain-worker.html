brain-worker

1. NOUN

/ ˈbreɪn ˌwɜrkər / (AmE), / ˈbreɪn ˌwɜːkə / (BrE)
A person whose job involves using mental rather than physical effort, especially to solve problems or create ideas.
person who works primarily with their intellect

Examples

As a brain-worker, she spends most of her day analyzing data and drafting reports.
Many brain-workers struggle with stress caused by intense mental effort.
The modern economy increasingly values the contributions of the brain-worker over manual labor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intellectual - a person possessing a highly developed intellect
  • Antonym: manual worker - a person who does physical work
