suborder Myomorpha

1. NOUN

/ ˌsʌbˈɔrdər ˌmaɪəˈmɔrfə / (AmE), / ˌsʌbˈɔːdə ˌmaɪəˈmɔːfə / (BrE)
A taxonomic suborder of rodents that includes true rats, mice, and related species.
rodent suborder including rats and mice

Examples

Species within the suborder Myomorpha are found on every continent except Antarctica.
The suborder Myomorpha contains many of the small rodents commonly kept as laboratory animals.
Studies of the suborder Myomorpha help scientists understand the evolution of rodents.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Rodentia - the order of mammals that includes all rodents

Etymology

From Greek 'mys' (mouse) and 'morphē' (form or shape).
