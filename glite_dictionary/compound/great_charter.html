Great Charter

1. PROPN

/ ɡreɪt ˈtʃɑːrtər / (AmE), / ɡreɪt ˈtʃɑːtə / (BrE)
the royal charter of political rights granted to rebellious English barons by King John in 1215, known as the Magna Carta
historic English royal charter (Magna Carta)

Examples

The principles of the Great Charter have influenced many modern constitutions.
Many historians consider the Great Charter a foundation of English liberties.
King John was forced to seal the Great Charter at Runnymede in 1215.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Magna Carta - another name for the Great Charter of 1215

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'Magna Carta', meaning 'Great Charter'.
