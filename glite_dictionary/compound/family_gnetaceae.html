family Gnetaceae

1. NOUN

/ fæməli dʒnɛˈteɪsiː / (AmE), / fæmɪli dʒnɛˈteɪsiː / (BrE)
A botanical family of plants with small unisexual flowers and fleshy or winged fruits, including the genus Gnetum and sometimes Ephedra and Welwitschia.
a botanical family of seed plants

Examples

The family Gnetaceae contains several species that are important in ecological studies.
Scientists still debate which plants should be classified in the family Gnetaceae.
Members of the family Gnetaceae are known for their distinctive reproductive structures.

Etymology

From modern Latin Gnetaceae, formed from Gnetum (type genus) + -aceae (family suffix).
