fishing worm

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪʃɪŋ wɜrm / (AmE), / ˈfɪʃɪŋ wɜːm / (BrE)
A worm that is used as bait by anglers when fishing, typically a terrestrial species that burrows in soil and helps aerate it.
worm used as fishing bait

Examples

He dug through the garden to find a fishing worm for his morning trip to the lake.
Many anglers prefer using a live fishing worm over artificial lures.
After the rain, the sidewalk was littered with fishing worms ready to be collected.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: earthworm - a common terrestrial worm often used as fishing bait
