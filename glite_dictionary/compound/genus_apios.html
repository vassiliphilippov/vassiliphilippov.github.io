genus Apios

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˈeɪ.pi.ɑs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈeɪ.pɪ.ɒs / (BrE)
A genus of twining perennial plants in the pea family, native to North America, including species like the groundnut (Apios americana).
a genus of North American climbing plants

Examples

The tubers of genus Apios were an important food source for Native Americans.
Botanists studied the adaptability of genus Apios to various soil types.
One well-known member of genus Apios is the groundnut, valued for its edible roots.

Etymology

From Greek apios (pear), used as a plant name by Linnaeus.
