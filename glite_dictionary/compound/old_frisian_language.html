Old Frisian language

1. NOUN

/ oʊld ˈfrɪʒən ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / əʊld ˈfrɪzɪən ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the West Germanic language spoken in Frisia until the 16th century, ancestral to modern Frisian
historic West Germanic language of Frisia

Examples

Scholars use texts written in Old Frisian language to study the early history of the Frisians.
The Old Frisian language was spoken from the 8th to the 16th centuries in coastal regions of the Netherlands and Germany.
Legal codes in the Old Frisian language reveal much about medieval society in Frisia.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Frisian language - a West Germanic language spoken in the Friesland region

Etymology

Old Frisian refers to the historical stage of the Frisian language, from medieval Frisia (now parts of the Netherlands and Germany).
