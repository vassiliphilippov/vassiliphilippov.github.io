nest egg

1. NOUN

/ nɛst ɛɡ / (AmE), / nɛst ɛɡ / (BrE)
An artificial egg placed in a bird's nest, especially in a henhouse, to encourage hens to lay their eggs in that spot.
artificial egg used to encourage hens to lay

Examples

The farmer placed a nest egg in each coop to make the hens feel more comfortable.
We found a glass nest egg in the chicken house, used to guide the hens.
Without a nest egg, the hens sometimes wander off to lay their eggs elsewhere.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decoy - object used to lure animals

2. NOUN

/ nɛst ɛg / (AmE), / nɛst ɛg / (BrE)
a sum of money saved and set aside for the future, especially for retirement or an emergency
money saved for the future

Examples

She kept a nest egg in case of emergencies.
After years of careful saving, they built up a comfortable nest egg for retirement.
He used part of his nest egg to start a small business.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: savings - money that has been saved, especially in a bank
  • Synonym: reserve - a supply of something set aside for future use

Etymology

Originally referred to an artificial egg placed in a nest to encourage hens to lay; later used figuratively for money reserved to encourage further saving.

3. NOUN

/ nɛst ɛɡ / (AmE), / nɛst ɛɡ / (BrE)
A sum of money saved for the future, especially for retirement or emergencies.
savings set aside for future use

Examples

After years of hard work, they managed to build a substantial nest egg for retirement.
She dipped into her nest egg to pay for her daughter's college education.
It's important to have a nest egg in case of unexpected emergencies.

Synset Examples

Investing wisely can grow your nest egg significantly.
They saved a nice nest egg for their retirement.
Her nest egg helped her buy a house.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: savings - money that has been saved, especially for the future

Etymology

Originally referring to a real or artificial egg put in a nest to encourage hens to lay more eggs; later, metaphorically applied to money set aside for future use.
