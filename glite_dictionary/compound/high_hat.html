high hat

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪ hæt / (AmE), / ˈhaɪ hæt / (BrE)
A man's formal hat with a tall, flat crown, usually covered with silk or beaver fur; also called a top hat.
man's formal hat with tall crown

Examples

He wore a black high hat to the opera, giving him a remarkably distinguished appearance.
In Victorian times, the high hat was a symbol of social status and formality.
The antique shop displayed a collection of vintage high hats from the 19th century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: top hat - a man's tall, flat-crowned, formal hat

2. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪ ˌhæt / (AmE), / ˈhaɪ ˌhæt / (BrE)
A pair of cymbals mounted on a stand and operated by a foot pedal, used as part of a drum kit.
cymbals operated by foot pedal in a drum kit

Examples

The drummer kept time using the high hat throughout the song.
He adjusted the tension on the high hat to get a crisper sound.
During the solo, she played an intricate rhythm on the high hat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hi-hat - cymbals in a drum kit operated by a foot pedal
