master class

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæs.tɚ klæs / (AmE), / ˈmɑː.stə klɑːs / (BrE)
A class, especially in music or the arts, given to talented students by a distinguished expert to improve their skills.
advanced class taught by an expert

Examples

The renowned pianist held a master class for the music students at the conservatory.
Writers from across the country attended her master class in creative nonfiction.
Taking a master class with a world-class violinist was a once-in-a-lifetime opportunity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: workshop - a brief intensive course emphasizing interaction and practical work

2. NOUN

/ ˈmæs.tɚ klæs / (AmE), / ˈmɑː.stə klɑːs / (BrE)
An advanced lesson or course taught by an expert, especially for highly proficient students in a particular subject or skill.
advanced course by an expert

Examples

The chef’s master class offered advanced tips for experienced cooks.
This weekend there will be a writing master class at the community center.
