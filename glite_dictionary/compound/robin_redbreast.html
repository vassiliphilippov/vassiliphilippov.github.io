robin redbreast

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɑbɪn ˈrɛdˌbrɛst / (AmE), / ˈrɒbɪn ˈrɛdˌbrɛst / (BrE)
a small European songbird (Erithacus rubecula) with a reddish breast, commonly found in gardens and woodlands
European songbird with a reddish breast

Examples

A robin redbreast perched on the garden fence, singing cheerfully in the early morning.
Many poets have written about the cheerful appearance of the robin redbreast in winter.
Children in Britain often recognize the robin redbreast as a symbol of Christmas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: robin - small songbird, especially the European Robin or the American Robin

Etymology

From the given name Robin and the description 'redbreast' referring to its coloring; name dates from the 16th century in English.
