California sagebrush

1. NOUN

/ kælɪˈfɔrniə ˈseɪdʒˌbrʌʃ / (AmE), / kælɪˈfɔːniə ˈseɪdʒˌbrʌʃ / (BrE)
A low, ashy-grey shrub native to California, commonly found in coastal and chaparral regions.
a low, grey California shrub

Examples

The trail was lined with California sagebrush, its scent strong in the dry summer air.
Gardeners in coastal areas often plant California sagebrush for its drought tolerance.
Hikers sometimes rub the leaves of California sagebrush between their fingers to release its distinctive fragrance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sagebrush - a shrub of the genus Artemisia found in western North America
