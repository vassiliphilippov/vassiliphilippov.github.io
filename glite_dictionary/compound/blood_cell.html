blood cell

1. NOUN

/ ˈblʌd sɛl / (AmE), / ˈblʌd sɛl / (BrE)
A cell normally present in blood, such as a red blood cell (erythrocyte) or white blood cell (leukocyte), and sometimes including platelets.
a cell found in blood

Examples

The doctor examined the patient's blood cells under a microscope for signs of infection.
Red and white blood cells have different functions in the immune system.
A decrease in blood cells can indicate anemia or other medical conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: erythrocyte - a red blood cell
  • Synonym: leukocyte - a white blood cell
