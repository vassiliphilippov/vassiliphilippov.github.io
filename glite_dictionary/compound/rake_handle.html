rake handle

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪk ˌhændl̩ / (AmE), / ˈreɪk ˌhændl / (BrE)
the long stick or shaft by which a rake is held and operated
the handle of a rake

Examples

The old rake handle splintered when I tried to move the heavy leaves.
He replaced the broken rake handle with a sturdy wooden one from the hardware store.
Cleaning the garden shed, she found an extra rake handle propped in the corner.
