Chinese chive

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃaɪ.niz tʃaɪv / (AmE), / ˈtʃaɪ.niːz tʃaɪv / (BrE)
a perennial herb native to eastern Asia, with flat, grass-like leaves, used as a vegetable or seasoning in cooking; larger than common chives.
Asian herb used as seasoning; larger than chives

Examples

Fresh Chinese chive is a key ingredient in many Asian dumplings and stir-fries.
She added chopped Chinese chive to the omelette for extra flavor.
The market sells both regular chives and Chinese chive by the bunch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: garlic chive - another name for Chinese chive (Allium tuberosum)
  • Degree Lower: chive - a related herb with slender leaves (Allium schoenoprasum)
