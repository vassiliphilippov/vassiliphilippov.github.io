gray poplar

1. NOUN

/ ɡreɪ ˈpɑːplɚ / (AmE), / ɡreɪ ˈpɒplə / (BrE)
a large, fast-growing poplar tree (Populus × canescens) with slightly lobed, toothed leaves that are grayish underneath, native to Europe but naturalized in other regions
a species of European poplar tree

Examples

The gray poplar is often planted in parks for its rapid growth and attractive foliage.
Botanists have noted that the gray poplar can easily hybridize with other poplar species.
The leaves of the gray poplar are green on top and grayish underneath, making the tree easy to identify.
