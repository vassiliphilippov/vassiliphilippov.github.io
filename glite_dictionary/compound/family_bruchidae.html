family Bruchidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli bruːˈkɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli bruːˈkɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of beetles known as seed beetles, whose larvae typically infest and consume seeds, especially those of legumes.
family of seed beetles in entomology

Examples

Many species in the family Bruchidae are notorious agricultural pests, causing significant damage to stored legumes.
Researchers are studying the life cycle of the family Bruchidae to find better pest control methods.
The museum's collection includes rare specimens from the family Bruchidae collected over a century ago.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seed beetle - common name for beetles of the family Bruchidae

Etymology

From New Latin Bruchidae, named after genus Bruchus, from Greek bruō (to swell, grow).
