siege of Orleans

1. NOUN

/ sidʒ əv ɔrˈliənz / (AmE), / siːdʒ əv ɔːˈliːɒ̃ / (BrE)
the military blockade of Orléans by English forces during the Hundred Years' War, relieved by Joan of Arc in 1429
military siege of Orléans, 1428–29

Examples

The siege of Orleans marked a turning point in the Hundred Years’ War.
Joan of Arc led French forces to break the siege of Orleans in 1429.
Many historians regard the siege of Orleans as one of Joan of Arc’s most important victories.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hundred Years' War - the long conflict between England and France (1337–1453)
  • Synonym: Joan of Arc - French heroine who led the relief of Orléans
