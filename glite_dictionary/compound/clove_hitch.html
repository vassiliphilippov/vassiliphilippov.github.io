clove hitch

1. NOUN

/ kloʊv hɪtʃ / (AmE), / kləʊv hɪtʃ / (BrE)
a knot used to secure a rope temporarily to a post, spar, or similar object
knot for fastening rope to a post

Examples

The clove hitch is often used when you need a knot that can be tied quickly and untied easily.
To secure the tent to the pole, he tied a clove hitch around it.
Sailors rely on the clove hitch for fastening ropes to rails or posts on a ship.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bowline - a type of knot that creates a fixed loop at the end of a rope

Etymology

From the word 'clove' (past tense of 'cleave') and 'hitch' (a knot or fastening).
