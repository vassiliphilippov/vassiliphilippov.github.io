hoary alyssum

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɔri əˈlɪsəm / (AmE), / ˈhɔːri əˈlɪsəm / (BrE)
A European annual plant (Berteroa incana) with grey-green downy leaves and dense clusters of small white flowers, often considered a weed in North America.
a European annual plant with white flowers

Examples

The pasture was invaded by hoary alyssum, making it unsafe for grazing horses.
Gardeners often struggle to remove hoary alyssum because it spreads so quickly.
Hoary alyssum is easily identified by its grey-green leaves and hairy seed pods.

Etymology

From 'hoary' (greyish-white) and 'alyssum', a genus name from Greek 'alysson' (a plant believed to cure madness).
