automatic choke

1. NOUN

/ ˌɔtəˈmætɪk tʃoʊk / (AmE), / ˌɔːtəˈmætɪk tʃəʊk / (BrE)
A device in an internal combustion engine that automatically regulates the air-fuel mixture by controlling the air flow to the carburetor, usually to facilitate starting the engine.
device that automatically regulates carburetor air flow

Examples

The old car wouldn't start until the automatic choke adjusted the air flow.
Modern vehicles are usually equipped with an automatic choke to make cold starting easier.
He discovered the problem was caused by a faulty automatic choke in the carburetor.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: manual choke - a choke operated by hand rather than automatically
