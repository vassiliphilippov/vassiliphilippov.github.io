brazil nut

1. NOUN

/ brəˈzɪl nʌt / (AmE), / brəˈzɪl nʌt / (BrE)
A large, three-sided edible nut with a hard brown shell and creamy white oily flesh, native to South America.
large edible South American nut

Examples

She cracked open a brazil nut as a snack during the movie.
A bowl of mixed nuts usually contains at least one brazil nut.
The brazil nut is valued for its rich, buttery flavor and high selenium content.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nut - an edible fruit consisting of a hard shell and a seed

Etymology

Named after Brazil, the country of origin for the trees producing these nuts.
