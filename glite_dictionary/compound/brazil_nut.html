Brazil nut

1. NOUN

/ brəˈzɪl nʌt / (AmE), / brəˈzɪl nʌt / (BrE)
A tall South American tree (Bertholletia excelsa) of the Amazon rainforest that produces edible seeds known as Brazil nuts.
South American tree producing edible nuts

Examples

The Brazil nut is an essential species in the Amazon rainforest ecosystem.
Conservationists are working to protect the Brazil nut from deforestation.
The towering Brazil nut can live for hundreds of years in its native habitat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Brazil nut - the edible seed of the Brazil nut tree

Etymology

Named after Brazil, the country where the tree is native.

2. NOUN

/ brəˈzɪl nʌt / (AmE), / brəˈzɪl nʌt / (BrE)
A large, three-sided edible nut from a South American tree, with white oily meat and a hard brown shell.
large edible nut from South America

Examples

She cracked open a Brazil nut and offered it to her friend.
The trail mix included almonds, walnuts, and pieces of Brazil nut.
Harvesting Brazil nuts plays a crucial role in supporting local economies in the Amazon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: macadamia nut - round, edible nut native to Australia
  • Synonym: cashew nut - kidney-shaped edible nut from a tropical tree
  • Synonym: hazelnut - round, edible nut from a hazel tree

Etymology

Named after Brazil, the country where the nut trees are native.
