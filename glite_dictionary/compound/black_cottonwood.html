black cottonwood

1. NOUN

/ blæk ˈkɑtnˌwʊd / (AmE), / blæk ˈkɒtnˌwʊd / (BrE)
A North American poplar tree with large rounded, scalloped leaves and brownish bark and wood.
a North American poplar tree

Examples

The black cottonwood is commonly found along river banks in the Pacific Northwest.
Falling leaves from the black cottonwood carpeted the hiking trail in late autumn.
Researchers studied the growth patterns of black cottonwood for climate change analysis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poplar - a type of fast-growing deciduous tree in the genus Populus

2. NOUN

/ blæk ˈkɑt̬.nˌwʊd / (AmE), / blæk ˈkɒt.nwʊd / (BrE)
A tall poplar tree native to western North America, with dark green, shiny leaves and rusty or silvery undersides.
a North American poplar tree species

Examples

The black cottonwood is common along riverbanks in the Pacific Northwest.
A row of black cottonwoods provided shade for the hikers.
Researchers studied the rapid growth rate of the black cottonwood in different soil conditions.

Related Terms
