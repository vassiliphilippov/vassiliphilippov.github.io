internal cerebral vein

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtɜrnəl səˈribəl veɪn / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɜːnəl səˈriːbrəl veɪn / (BrE)
either of two veins in the brain that run near the midline and join to form the great cerebral vein, responsible for draining blood from internal brain structures
a vein in the brain that drains internal structures
medical

Examples

The internal cerebral vein plays a key role in the venous drainage of deep brain structures.
Damage to an internal cerebral vein can result in significant neurological deficits.
The two internal cerebral veins unite to form the great cerebral vein, also known as the vein of Galen.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: great cerebral vein - a large vein formed by the union of the internal cerebral veins

Etymology

From Latin cerebrum (brain) and vena (vein).
