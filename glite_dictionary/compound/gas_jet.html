gas jet

1. NOUN

/ ɡæs dʒɛt / (AmE), / ɡæs dʒɛt / (BrE)
a device in which combustible gas is released through a nozzle to produce a steady flame, often used for heating or lighting
device emitting a flame from combustible gas

Examples

The laboratory technician carefully lit the gas jet to heat the chemical solution.
Victorian homes often relied on a gas jet for lighting before electricity became widespread.
Please be sure the gas jet is turned off after you finish using the stove.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Bunsen burner - a common laboratory gas burner with adjustable air supply
