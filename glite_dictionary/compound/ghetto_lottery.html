ghetto lottery

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɛtoʊ ˈlɑːtəri / (AmE), / ˈɡɛtəʊ ˈlɒtəri / (BrE)
an unexpected stroke of good fortune, especially occurring in a disadvantaged or impoverished environment; often used humorously or ironically
unexpected luck in a disadvantaged context
informal

Examples

After finding a hundred dollars on the street, he joked that he'd won the ghetto lottery.
When the old car finally started without trouble, she felt like she'd hit the ghetto lottery that day.
He considered the insurance payout his family's ghetto lottery, even though the situation was bittersweet.

Synset Examples

The ghetto lottery is a metaphor for unexpected luck.
Winning the ghetto lottery changed his life overnight.
She joked about hitting the ghetto lottery with her luck.
