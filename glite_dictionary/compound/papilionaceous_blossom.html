papilionaceous blossom

1. NOUN

/ pəˌpɪlioʊˈneɪʃəs ˈblɑsəm / (AmE), / pəˌpɪlɪəˈneɪʃəs ˈblɒsəm / (BrE)
a blossom having petals arranged in a way that resembles a butterfly, typically found in plants of the pea family
butterfly-shaped flower petal arrangement

Examples

The garden was filled with papilionaceous blossoms, adding vibrant color to the landscape.
Many legumes bear papilionaceous blossoms that appeal to pollinators.
He studied the structure of a papilionaceous blossom for his botany class.

Synset Examples

She admired the papilionaceous blossoms in the garden.
The plant features papilionaceous petals that attract bees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pea flower - flower type typical of plants in the pea family (Fabaceae)

Etymology

From Latin 'papilio' meaning 'butterfly', referring to the resemblance of the petals to a butterfly's wings.
