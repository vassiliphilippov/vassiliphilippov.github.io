Boston lettuce

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑstən ˈlɛtɪs / (AmE), / ˈbɒstən ˈlɛtɪs / (BrE)
a type of butterhead lettuce with relatively soft, tender leaves, often used in salads
variety of soft-leaf lettuce

Examples

She added fresh Boston lettuce to the salad for a soft texture.
At the market, he chose a head of Boston lettuce for tonight's dinner.
Boston lettuce is often preferred in sandwiches because of its mild flavor and tender leaves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: butterhead lettuce - a type of lettuce with soft, buttery leaves

Etymology

Named after the city of Boston, where this variety became popular in the United States.
