red-breasted sapsucker

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɛdˈbrɛstɪd ˈsæpˌsʌkər / (AmE), / ˌrɛdˈbrɛstɪd ˈsæpˌsʌkə / (BrE)
A species of North American woodpecker (Sphyrapicus ruber) with a distinctive red breast, found primarily in western forests.
North American woodpecker species

Examples

We spotted a red-breasted sapsucker pecking at the tree while hiking in Oregon.
The red-breasted sapsucker is known for drilling neat rows of holes in bark to feed on sap.
Unlike other woodpeckers, the red-breasted sapsucker prefers coniferous forests along the Pacific coast.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sapsucker - a type of woodpecker that feeds on sap
