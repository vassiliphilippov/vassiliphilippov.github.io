trading stamp

1. NOUN

/ ˈtreɪdɪŋ stæmp / (AmE), / ˈtreɪdɪŋ stamp / (BrE)
a small printed token given by retailers to customers as a reward for purchases, which can be collected and redeemed for products from a catalogue or list
redeemable retail reward token

Examples

Many stores in the 1960s gave out trading stamps with every purchase.
She collected enough trading stamps to exchange for a new toaster.
The popularity of trading stamps declined as loyalty programs became more digital.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coupon - a voucher entitling the holder to a discount or exchange for goods
