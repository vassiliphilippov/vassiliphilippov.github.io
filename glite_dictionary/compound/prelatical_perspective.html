prelatical perspective

1. NOUN

/ ˌprɛˈlætɪkəl pɚˈspɛktɪv / (AmE), / ˌprɛˈlætɪkəl pəˈspɛktɪv / (BrE)
a viewpoint or attitude that supports or is characteristic of the authority and practices of bishops or high-ranking clergy
viewpoint favoring bishops/clergy

Examples

The reforms were criticized for being shaped by a prelatical perspective rather than by lay interests.
In the debate, she challenged the prelatical perspective that dominated the conversation.
Historians often analyze church policy from either a prelatical perspective or a more secular one.

Synset Examples

His writings often reflect a prelatical perspective.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prelatical - relating to or characteristic of prelates (high-ranking clergy)
  • Synonym: episcopal - relating to bishops

Etymology

From 'prelatical', relating to prelates (high-ranking clergy), ultimately from Latin 'praelatus' (preferred, set above).
