epidemic parotitis

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛpɪˈdɛmɪk pəˈrɑtətɪs / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈdɛmɪk pəˈrɒtɪtɪs / (BrE)
an acute, contagious viral disease characterized by fever and swelling of the parotid glands; also known as mumps
acute contagious viral disease; mumps
medical

Examples

An outbreak of epidemic parotitis forced the local school to close for a week.
Measles, rubella, and epidemic parotitis are all preventable with vaccination.
Physicians recorded several cases of epidemic parotitis in the community this year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mumps - common name for epidemic parotitis, a viral infection causing swelling of the salivary glands

Etymology

From Greek 'parotis' (near the ear) and 'epidemic' (widespread occurrence of a disease).
