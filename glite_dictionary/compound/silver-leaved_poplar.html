silver-leaved poplar

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪlvɚ ˌlivd ˈpɑplɚ / (AmE), / ˈsɪlvə ˌliːvd ˈpɒplə / (BrE)
a species of poplar tree (Populus alba) with distinctive white bark and leaves that have whitish undersides, widely cultivated for ornamental purposes
a white-barked poplar tree with silvery leaves

Examples

The silver-leaved poplar is often planted along city streets for its attractive foliage.
A row of silver-leaved poplars provided shade along the riverbank.
She admired the shimmering leaves of the silver-leaved poplar as the wind blew.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: white poplar - a common name for Populus alba, another name for silver-leaved poplar
