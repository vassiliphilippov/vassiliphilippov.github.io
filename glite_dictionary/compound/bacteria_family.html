bacteria family

1. NOUN

/ bækˈtɪəriə ˈfæməli / (AmE), / bækˈtɪəriə ˈfæməli / (BrE)
A taxonomic rank in the classification of bacteria, grouping together related genera.
taxonomic rank grouping bacterial genera

Examples

The bacteria family Lactobacillaceae includes many species used in food fermentation.
Scientists discovered a new bacteria family in the deep-sea hydrothermal vents.
Each bacteria family is distinguished by unique genetic characteristics.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: genus - a taxonomic category below a family and above a species
  • Degree Higher: order - a taxonomic rank above family

Etymology

From 'bacteria', modern Latin from Greek bakterion (rod) and 'family', Latin familia.
