genus Mycoplasma

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˌmaɪkoʊˈplæzmə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˌmaɪkəʊˈplazmə / (BrE)
the only genus of the family Mycoplasmataceae, comprising bacteria lacking cell walls, some of which are pathogenic in humans and animals
bacterial genus in Mycoplasmataceae family
medical

Examples

Researchers isolated a new species within the genus Mycoplasma from contaminated cell cultures.
The genus Mycoplasma includes several pathogens responsible for respiratory infections in humans.
Members of the genus Mycoplasma lack a cell wall, making them resistant to many common antibiotics.

Etymology

From Greek 'mykes' (fungus) and 'plasma' (something molded), referring to the organisms’ plasticity and shape.
