Rocky mountain pinon

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɑki ˈmaʊntn ˈpiːnjoʊn / (AmE), / ˈrɒki ˈmaʊntɪn ˈpiːnjɒn / (BrE)
a small, compact pine tree of the southwestern United States with two needles, valued for its edible nuts
a pine tree of the southwestern US, valued for its nuts

Examples

Many Native American tribes harvested Rocky mountain pinon nuts as an important seasonal food.
The Rocky mountain pinon typically grows on dry, rocky slopes at higher elevations.
Wildlife such as birds and squirrels rely on Rocky mountain pinon seeds for nutrition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pinyon pine - a group of North American pine trees known for edible seeds
