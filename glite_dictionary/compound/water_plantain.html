water plantain

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔtər ˌplæn.tɪn / (AmE), / ˈwɔː.tə ˌplæn.tɪn / (BrE)
a marsh plant with clusters of small white or pinkish flowers and broad pointed or rounded leaves, typically found in wet environments
marsh plant with white or pinkish flowers

Examples

The marsh was filled with water plantain growing along the edges of the pond.
She identified the broad leaves and delicate white flowers as those of water plantain.
Water plantain often provides important habitat for aquatic wildlife.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Alisma - genus of aquatic plants known as water plantain
