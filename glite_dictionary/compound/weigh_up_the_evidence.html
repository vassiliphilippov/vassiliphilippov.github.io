to weigh up the evidence

1. VERB

/ weɪ ʌp ði ˈɛvədəns / (AmE), / weɪ ʌp ði ˈɛvɪdəns / (BrE)
to carefully consider all the available evidence before making a judgment or decision
consider all the evidence carefully

Examples

The jury must weigh up the evidence before reaching a verdict.
Before deciding, you should weigh up the evidence from both sides.
He took his time to weigh up the evidence presented during the trial.

Synset Examples

We need to weigh up the evidence before concluding.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to evaluate - to assess or judge something carefully
  • Synonym: judge - to form an opinion after consideration
