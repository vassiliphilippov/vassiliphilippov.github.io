electric light

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈlɛktrɪk laɪt / (AmE), / ɪˈlɛktrɪk laɪt / (BrE)
A device that produces light using electricity, typically consisting of a glass enclosure and a heated wire filament.
artificial light powered by electricity

Examples

The room was brightly lit by a single electric light hanging from the ceiling.
After sunset, the village square is illuminated by new electric lights.
He was fascinated by the invention of the electric light and its impact on daily life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lamp - a device for giving light, especially one that has a covering or is contained within something
  • Antonym: candle - stick of wax with a wick for burning to produce light
