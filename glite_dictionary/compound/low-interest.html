low-interest

1. ADJ

/ ˈloʊ ˌɪntrəst / (AmE), / ˈləʊ ˌɪntrəst / (BrE)
(of a loan or other financial product) charging or involving a relatively small percentage as interest.
charging a small interest rate

Examples

She managed to secure a low-interest loan to buy her first home.
Many banks are now offering low-interest credit cards to attract new customers.
Students often look for low-interest options to finance their education.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: high-interest - charging a relatively high percentage of interest on a loan or financial product
