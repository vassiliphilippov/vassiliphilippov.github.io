seismic disturbance

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪzmɪk dɪˈstɝːbəns / (AmE), / ˈsaɪzmɪk dɪˈstɜːbəns / (BrE)
a sudden shaking, movement, or agitation of the earth's crust, often caused by natural forces such as an earthquake or volcanic activity.
earth's crust agitation (geology)

Examples

A minor seismic disturbance was recorded near the volcano late last night.
The scientists analyzed data from the seismic disturbance to predict future activity.
Residents reported feeling a seismic disturbance just before the main earthquake struck.

Synset Examples

the first shock of the earthquake came shortly after noon while workers were at lunch

Related Terms

  • Synonym: earthquake - a sudden and violent shaking of the ground, often causing destruction

Etymology

From 'seismic', from Greek 'seismos' (a shaking, earthquake), plus 'disturbance'.
