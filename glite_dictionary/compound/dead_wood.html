dead wood

1. NOUN

/ dɛd wʊd / (AmE), / dɛd wʊd / (BrE)
People or things within an organization that are unproductive, unnecessary, or no longer useful.
unproductive or unnecessary people or things

Examples

The new manager isn't afraid to cut dead wood from the team if necessary.
After the merger, the company got rid of a lot of dead wood in upper management.
It's important to regularly assess staff performance to avoid accumulating too much dead wood.

Synset Examples

Eliminating dead wood will streamline our processes.
His presence is just dead wood in this project.
We need to remove the dead wood from our organization.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: liability - someone or something that causes problems or is a burden
  • Antonym: asset - a useful or valuable person or thing
