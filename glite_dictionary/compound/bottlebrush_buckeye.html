bottlebrush buckeye

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑːt̬.əlˌbrʌʃ ˈbʌk.aɪ / (AmE), / ˈbɒt.əlˌbrʌʃ ˈbʌk.aɪ / (BrE)
a spreading shrub native to the southeastern United States, having showy spikes of pink or white flowers resembling a bottlebrush
spreading shrub with bottlebrush-shaped flowers

Examples

The bottlebrush buckeye is prized for its dramatic summer blooms in southern gardens.
A mature bottlebrush buckeye can spread over ten feet wide and provide excellent shade.
Gardeners appreciate the bottlebrush buckeye for attracting pollinators with its vibrant flowers.

Etymology

Named for its flower spikes, which resemble a bottlebrush, and its resemblance to the buckeye tree.
