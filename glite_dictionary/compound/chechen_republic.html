Chechen Republic

1. PROPN

/ ˈʧɛʧən rɪˈpʌblɪk / (AmE), / ˈʧɛʧɛn rɪˈpʌblɪk / (BrE)
an autonomous republic in southwestern Russia, located in the northern Caucasus Mountains and bordering Georgia, known for declaring independence in 1991 and subsequent conflict with Russia.
autonomous republic in southwestern Russia

Examples

The Chechen Republic has experienced prolonged conflict in the post-Soviet era.
Many refugees from the Chechen Republic have resettled in neighboring countries.
Diplomatic relations with the Chechen Republic remain a sensitive issue for Russia.
