American beech

1. NOUN

/ əˈmɛrɪkən ˈbiːtʃ / (AmE), / əˈmɛrɪkən ˈbiːtʃ / (BrE)
A North American deciduous tree (Fagus grandifolia) with smooth gray bark, light green leaves, and edible nuts.
North American deciduous tree with edible nuts

Examples

The woods behind our house are filled with towering American beech trees.
Squirrels gathered the nuts that had fallen from the American beech in autumn.
The smooth, silvery bark of the American beech is easy to recognize in the forest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beech - A large deciduous tree with smooth gray bark and small edible nuts
