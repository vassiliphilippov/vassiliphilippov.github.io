dwarf phlox

1. NOUN

/ dwɔrf flɑks / (AmE), / dwɔːf flɒks / (BrE)
A low-growing, tufted perennial plant of the phlox family, with needlelike evergreen leaves and pink or white flowers, native to the United States and often grown as a ground cover.
low perennial plant with pink or white flowers

Examples

The garden bed was bordered with a colorful display of dwarf phlox.
She planted dwarf phlox along the pathway for year-round greenery and spring blossoms.
Dwarf phlox thrives best in well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phlox - A genus of flowering plants native to North America, known for their colorful blooms
