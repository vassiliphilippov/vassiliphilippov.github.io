quartz glass

1. NOUN

/ kwɔrts ɡlæs / (AmE), / kwɔːts ɡlɑːs / (BrE)
A colorless, transparent glass made almost entirely of pure silica, valued for its high resistance to heat and chemical corrosion.
glass made from pure silica

Examples

Laboratory equipment often uses quartz glass because it can withstand high temperatures.
The telescope's lens was made from quartz glass to ensure clarity and durability.
Optical fibers manufactured from quartz glass offer excellent transmission quality for telecommunications.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fused silica - glass made by melting pure silica
  • Degree Lower: glass - hard, brittle substance made by fusing sand with soda, lime, and sometimes other ingredients
