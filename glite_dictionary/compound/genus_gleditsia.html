genus Gleditsia

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ɡlɛˈdɪtsi.ə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ɡlɛˈdɪtsi.ə / (BrE)
A genus of deciduous trees in the pea family, including the honey locust, native to North America and Asia.
tree genus including honey locusts

Examples

The genus Gleditsia is best known for the honey locust, a tree valued for its shade and pods.
Several species within the genus Gleditsia are cultivated as ornamental trees in parks and gardens.
Botanists studied the leaf patterns of the genus Gleditsia to understand their adaptation to different climates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: honey locust - A species of tree in the genus Gleditsia known for its sweet-tasting pods

Etymology

Named after Johann Gottlieb Gleditsch, an 18th-century German botanist.
