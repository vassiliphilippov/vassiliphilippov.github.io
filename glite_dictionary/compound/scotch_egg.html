Scotch egg

1. NOUN

/ skɑtʃ ɛɡ / (AmE), / skɒtʃ ɛɡ / (BrE)
a hard-boiled egg wrapped in seasoned sausage meat, coated in breadcrumbs, and deep-fried or baked, often served cold as a snack or picnic food
a sausage-wrapped, breaded hard-boiled egg snack
british

Examples

He picked up a Scotch egg at the deli as a quick lunch.
The picnic basket included sandwiches, fruit, and a homemade Scotch egg.
At the pub, I always order a Scotch egg as an appetizer.

Etymology

First recorded in early 19th-century Britain; possibly named after a London department store (Fortnum & Mason) or relating to 'scotched' (minced meat).
