to point-and-drool

1. VERB

/ pɔɪnt ənd drul / (AmE), / pɔɪnt ənd druːl / (BrE)
(informal) To express excessive admiration or desire, often in a conspicuous or exaggerated way.
show excessive admiration or desire
informal

Examples

Whenever the new phones are released, tech fans point-and-drool over every feature.
As soon as the rare sports car drove by, he couldn't help but point-and-drool.
She tends to point-and-drool over every designer shoe she sees in the store.

Synset Examples
