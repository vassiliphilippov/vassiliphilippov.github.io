pitch black

1. NOUN

/ pɪtʃ blæk / (AmE), / pɪtʃ blæk / (BrE)
completely dark; having no light at all; very black.
completely dark; very black

Examples

At midnight, the room was pitch black and silent.
We couldn't see anything because the cave was pitch black inside.
The power outage left the entire neighborhood pitch black.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jet black - completely black; very dark
  • Synonym: coal black - very black, like coal
  • Synonym: pitch dark - completely dark; without any light

Etymology

From 'pitch' (a sticky, black substance) used figuratively to intensify 'black'.
