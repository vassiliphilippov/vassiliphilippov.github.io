genus Calophyllum

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌkæ.ləˈfɪl.əm / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌkæl.əˈfɪl.əm / (BrE)
A genus of tropical evergreen trees, including several species often valued for their timber and ornamental use.
a genus of tropical evergreen trees

Examples

Many species in the genus Calophyllum are known for their attractive glossy leaves and large, fragrant flowers.
The timber from certain trees in the genus Calophyllum is highly prized in tropical regions.
Researchers discovered a new species belonging to the genus Calophyllum in the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

Etymology

From Latin 'calophyllum', from Greek 'kalos' (beautiful) + 'phyllon' (leaf).
