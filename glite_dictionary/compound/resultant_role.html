resultant role

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈzʌltənt roʊl / (AmE), / rɪˈzʌltənt rəʊl / (BrE)
(Linguistics) The semantic role of a noun phrase whose referent comes into existence as a result of the action denoted by the verb in the clause.
semantic role created by a verb's action

Examples

In the sentence 'She built a house,' 'house' fulfills the resultant role because it comes into existence through building.
The resultant role is essential in analyzing sentences where something is created or produced.
A noun phrase serving a resultant role typically refers to something that did not exist before the action took place.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: patient - the semantic role of a noun phrase affected by the action
  • Antonym: agent - the semantic role of the 'doer' of the action

Etymology

From 'resultant' (from Latin resultans) + 'role' (from French rôle, meaning 'part played').
