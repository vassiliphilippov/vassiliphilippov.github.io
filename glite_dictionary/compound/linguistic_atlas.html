linguistic atlas

1. NOUN

/ lɪŋˈɡwɪstɪk ˈætləs / (AmE), / lɪŋˈɡwɪstɪk ˈætləs / (BrE)
An atlas that illustrates the geographical distribution of distinctive linguistic features, such as dialects or language usages, across regions.
atlas of language features by region

Examples

The linguistic atlas provides an overview of regional dialects throughout the country.
Scholars used the linguistic atlas to compare variations in word usage across Europe.
Creating a linguistic atlas requires extensive fieldwork and data collection from local speakers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dialect map - a map charting dialects by region

Etymology

From 'linguistic', relating to language, and 'atlas', from the name of the Titan Atlas (in reference to map collections).
