dappled-gray

1. NOUN

/ ˈdæpəld ˈɡreɪ / (AmE), / ˈdæpəld ˈɡreɪ / (BrE)
A gray color, especially of a horse, characterized by a mottled pattern of darker gray spots or markings.
gray color with mottled darker markings

Examples

The magnificent stallion was a dappled-gray with striking dark spots across his back.
A dappled-gray mare won the prize at the horse show this year.
Among the herd was a young dappled-gray foal, easily identifiable by its unique markings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gray - a color between black and white

Etymology

From 'dappled' (spotted) and 'gray,' describing the coat pattern of certain animals, especially horses.
