George Washington Bridge

1. PROPN

/ ˌdʒɔrdʒ ˈwɑʃɪŋtən ˈbrɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌdʒɔːdʒ ˈwɒʃɪŋtən ˈbrɪdʒ / (BrE)
a famous suspension bridge spanning the Hudson River between New York City and New Jersey
notable bridge over the Hudson River in New York

Examples

Traffic was backed up for miles on the George Washington Bridge during the morning rush hour.
The George Washington Bridge connects Manhattan to Fort Lee, New Jersey.
He admired the city skyline as he drove over the George Washington Bridge at sunset.

Etymology

Named after George Washington, the first President of the United States.
