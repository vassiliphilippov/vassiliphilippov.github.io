mercenary role

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɝː.səˌner.i roʊl / (AmE), / ˈmɜː.sən.əri rəʊl / (BrE)
A position or function assumed primarily for money or personal gain rather than loyalty or principle.
profit-motivated position or function

Examples

He accepted a mercenary role at the company, caring only about the paycheck.
Her decision to play a mercenary role in the negotiations angered her former allies.
Taking a mercenary role in politics can undermine one's credibility.

Synset Examples

He took on a mercenary role in the conflict zone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mercenary - a person primarily motivated by money rather than ethics or loyalty
