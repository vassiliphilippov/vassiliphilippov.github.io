Old Line State

1. PROPN

/ oʊld laɪn steɪt / (AmE), / əʊld laɪn steɪt / (BrE)
A nickname for the U.S. state of Maryland, one of the original thirteen colonies with its capital at Annapolis.
nickname for Maryland, U.S. state

Examples

Tourists often visit the Old Line State to see its historic cities and beautiful Chesapeake Bay.
She's moving to the Old Line State for a new job in Baltimore.
The Old Line State played a significant role during the Revolutionary War.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Maryland - a state in the Mid-Atlantic region of the United States

Etymology

The nickname refers to the Maryland Line, Maryland's regular line troops who served with distinction in the American Revolutionary War.
