Zanzibar copal

1. NOUN

/ ˈzænzəˌbɑr koʊˈpæl / (AmE), / ˈzænzəbɑː ˈkəʊpæl / (BrE)
a hard, yellowish resin obtained from trees native to East Africa, used chiefly in making varnishes and lacquers
East African tree resin used in varnish

Examples

The finest varnishes were often made using Zanzibar copal as a key ingredient.
Collectors prize carvings made from Zanzibar copal for their translucent amber appearance.
Exports of Zanzibar copal played a significant role in the island's economy in the nineteenth century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: copal - a hard resin used in varnishes, from various tropical trees

Etymology

Zanzibar, from the island off East Africa where copal is exported; copal from Spanish 'copalli', from Nahuatl.
