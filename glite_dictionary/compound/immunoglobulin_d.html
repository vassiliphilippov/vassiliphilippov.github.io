immunoglobulin D

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌmjunəˌɡlɑbjəˈlɪn di / (AmE), / ɪˌmjuːnəʊˌɡlɒbjʊˈlɪn diː / (BrE)
A class of antibody present in blood serum in small amounts, one of the five main types of immunoglobulins in the immune system.
a class of antibody in blood serum
medical

Examples

Doctors often measure immunoglobulin D levels to help diagnose certain immune disorders.
Immunoglobulin D is found in very small amounts compared to the other antibody classes.
Researchers are still studying the function of immunoglobulin D in the human body.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: immunoglobulin G - the most common antibody class in blood
  • Degree Higher: immunoglobulin M - an antibody class with higher blood levels than IgD

Etymology

From immuno- (relating to immunity) + globulin (a group of proteins) + D (letter distinguishing the antibody class).
