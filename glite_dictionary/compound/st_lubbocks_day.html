St Lubbock's Day

1. PROPN

/ seɪnt ˈlʌbəkz deɪ / (AmE), / sənt ˈlʌbəkz deɪ / (BrE)
a former name for a public holiday in the United Kingdom, originally designated as a bank holiday; now historical and not officially observed
former UK public holiday; historical term
archaic british

Examples

Many businesses used to close on St Lubbock's Day as it was considered a bank holiday.
The tradition of celebrating St Lubbock's Day has faded over time.
Historical records mention lavish parades on St Lubbock's Day in Victorian England.

Synset Examples

St. Lubbock's Day is marked with great enthusiasm.
We always have a party for St. Lubbock's Day.

Etymology

Named after Sir John Lubbock, who promoted the Bank Holidays Act of 1871 in the UK.
