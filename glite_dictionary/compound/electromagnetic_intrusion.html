electromagnetic intrusion

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌlɛk.troʊˌmæɡˈnɛt.ɪk ɪnˈtru.ʒən / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛk.trəʊˌmæɡˈnɛt.ɪk ɪnˈtruː.ʒən / (BrE)
the deliberate introduction of electromagnetic energy into communication channels to confuse or deceive operators
deliberate disruption of communication using electromagnetic energy

Examples

Military units used electromagnetic intrusion to disrupt enemy communications during the operation.
The security team investigated suspicious electromagnetic intrusion affecting the network's integrity.
Modern defense systems include safeguards against electromagnetic intrusion by hostile actors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jamming - deliberately preventing or interfering with radio communications
  • Synonym: electronic warfare - military action involving the use of electromagnetic energy to control or attack communication systems
