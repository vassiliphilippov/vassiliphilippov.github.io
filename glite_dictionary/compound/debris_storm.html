debris storm

1. NOUN

/ dəˈbri stɔrm / (AmE), / dɪˈbriː stɔːm / (BrE)
a sudden spread or cloud of dust and debris, especially from a collapsing structure or building
sudden cloud of dust and debris

Examples

The explosion caused a massive debris storm that blanketed the entire street.
Rescue workers struggled to see through the debris storm following the building's sudden collapse.
After the earthquake, a debris storm filled the air, making it difficult for anyone nearby to breathe.

Synset Examples

the destruction of the building produced an enormous debris surge

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dust cloud - a cloud of dust in the air, especially from destruction or collapse
