spiny lizard

1. NOUN

/ ˈspaɪni ˈlɪzərd / (AmE), / ˈspaɪni ˈlɪzəd / (BrE)
A type of lizard, typically found in deserts, characterized by spiny scales and commonly feeding on ants.
a desert lizard with spiny scales

Examples

The desert spiny lizard is known for its habit of basking on warm rocks.
You might spot a spiny lizard darting across the sand in the midday sun.
Many spiny lizards feed primarily on ants and other small insects.

2. NOUN

/ ˈspaɪni ˈlɪzərd / (AmE), / ˈspaɪni ˈlɪzəd / (BrE)
any of various lizards of North and Central America, characterized by overlapping ridged, pointed scales
North/Central American lizard with spiny scales

Examples

The desert is home to the spiny lizard, which basks on rocks in the sunlight.
We spotted a large spiny lizard climbing the fence outside our cabin.
Researchers observed several spiny lizards displaying territorial behavior during the summer months.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lizard - any reptile of the order Lacertilia
