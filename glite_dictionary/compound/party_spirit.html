party spirit

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːrti ˈspɪrɪt / (AmE), / ˈpɑːti ˈspɪrɪt / (BrE)
Strong loyalty or devotion to a particular political party, often accompanied by partisan feeling and bias.
strong loyalty to a political party

Examples

Many important decisions were delayed by excessive party spirit in the legislature.
She urged her colleagues to put aside party spirit for the good of the country.
The debate turned heated as party spirit began to override objective discussion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: partisanship - strong support for a specific party, group, or cause
  • Antonym: neutrality - the state of not supporting or helping either side in a conflict or dispute
