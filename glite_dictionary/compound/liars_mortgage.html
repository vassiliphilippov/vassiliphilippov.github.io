liar's mortgage

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪɚz ˈmɔrɡɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈlaɪəz ˈmɔːɡɪdʒ / (BrE)
a mortgage arrangement involving false or misleading information, often provided by the borrower, to qualify for a loan that they may not otherwise receive
fraudulent or deceptive mortgage arrangement

Examples

The bank is reviewing all accounts to find any liar's mortgage cases from recent years.
After the financial crash, the prevalence of the liar's mortgage became widely known.
Lawmakers introduced stricter lending standards to prevent another liar's mortgage scandal.

Synset Examples

He was caught in a liar's mortgage scheme last year.
The bank tightened regulations after the liar's mortgage crisis.
Many families lost their homes due to liar's mortgages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fraudulent mortgage - a mortgage obtained by lying or using false information
  • Degree Lower: subprime mortgage - a mortgage offered to borrowers with lower credit ratings, often riskier

Etymology

From liar + mortgage; popularized in the US housing crisis of the 2000s.
