genus Parus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˈpɛərəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈpɑːrəs / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Paridae, comprising various species of tits and chickadees.
type genus of the Paridae (tits, chickadees)

Examples

The genus Parus includes well-known species such as the great tit and blue tit.
Several members of the genus Parus can be commonly found in European woodlands.
Research on the genus Parus has contributed to our understanding of avian behavior.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Paridae - the family of birds including tits and chickadees

Etymology

From Latin 'parus' (a kind of titmouse or small bird), used in zoological taxonomy.
