Aramaic language

1. NOUN

/ ˌærəˈmeɪɪk ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌærəˈmeɪɪk ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
a Semitic language originally spoken by the ancient Arameans and still used by some communities in southwestern Asia
ancient Semitic language of the Arameans

Examples

The Aramaic language was widely used in the Middle East during ancient times.
Many ancient texts, including portions of the Bible, are written in Aramaic language.
Some communities in Syria and Iraq still speak varieties of the Aramaic language today.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Semitic language - a family of languages including Hebrew, Arabic, and Aramaic

Etymology

From Latin Aramaicus, from Ancient Greek Aramaïkos, relating to Aram (ancient region in Syria).
