Four Horsemen

1. NOUN

/ fɔr ˈhɔrs.mən / (AmE), / fɔː ˈhɔːs.mən / (BrE)
(in the New Testament) the four riders symbolizing conquest, war, famine, and plague who are prophesied to appear at the end of the world.
the four riders of the Apocalypse in the New Testament

Examples

During the discussion, the historian referenced the Four Horsemen as symbols of catastrophe and change.
Many paintings depict the Four Horsemen riding together, each wielding power over a separate calamity.
People often use the expression 'the Four Horsemen' when talking about widespread disasters or end-time scenarios.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Four Horsemen of the Apocalypse - the full phrase for the symbolic horsemen in Revelation
  • Synonym: apocalypse - the end of the world, especially as described in the Bible

Etymology

From the Book of Revelation in the New Testament of the Bible, referring to the four riders representing major calamities.
