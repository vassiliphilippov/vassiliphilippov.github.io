ready reckoner

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛdi ˈrɛkənər / (AmE), / ˈrɛdi ˈrɛkənə / (BrE)
a handbook or manual containing tables or charts for making quick calculations, especially of prices, weights, or interest.
handbook of computation tables

Examples

The accountant reached for his ready reckoner to check the latest conversion rates.
Before calculators, a ready reckoner was essential for shopkeepers working out prices.
She found an old ready reckoner among her grandfather's business books.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: table book - a book of tables for calculation or reference

Etymology

From 'ready' (prepared, available) + 'reckoner' (one who reckons; calculator); common in British English from the 18th century.
