order Tuberales

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdər tuːbəˈreɪliːz / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə tjuːbəˈreɪliːz / (BrE)
A small order of fungi in the subdivision Ascomycota, characterized by closed underground fruiting bodies (ascocarps).
an order of fungi with underground fruiting bodies
medical

Examples

The order Tuberales includes fungi that produce prized truffles.
Scientists studied the ecological impact of members of the order Tuberales in Mediterranean forests.
Only a few species of the order Tuberales are collected for culinary use.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: truffle - an edible subterranean fungus from this order

Etymology

From Latin 'tuber' (swelling, truffle), name given for truffle-like forms in this order.
