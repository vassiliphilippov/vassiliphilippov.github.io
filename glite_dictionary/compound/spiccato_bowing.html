spiccato bowing

1. NOUN

/ spɪˈkɑtoʊ ˈboʊɪŋ / (AmE), / spɪˈkɑːtəʊ ˈbəʊɪŋ / (BrE)
A technique of bowing in which the bow bounces lightly off the strings, producing short, detached notes.
type of violin bowing with bouncing bow

Examples

The violinist used spiccato bowing to create a lively, bouncing rhythm in the scherzo.
Learning spiccato bowing can be challenging for beginner string players.
In this passage, the composer marks spiccato bowing to achieve a crisp, articulate sound.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: legato bowing - bowing technique that produces smooth, connected notes

Etymology

From Italian 'spiccato' (detached), past participle of 'spiccare' (to detach).
