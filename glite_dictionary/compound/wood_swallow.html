wood swallow

1. NOUN

/ wʊd ˈswɑːloʊ / (AmE), / wʊd ˈswɒləʊ / (BrE)
An Australasian and Asiatic bird of the family Artamidae, related to the shrikes and resembling a swallow in appearance.
Australasian and Asiatic bird, resembles a swallow

Examples

A flock of wood swallows perched high in the eucalyptus trees, chattering among themselves.
The wood swallow swooped gracefully over the meadow, hawking insects in midair.
In parts of Southeast Asia, the wood swallow is a common sight near forest edges and open country.
