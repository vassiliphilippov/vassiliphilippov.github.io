family Arcidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ɑːrˈsɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli ɑːˈsɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of marine bivalve mollusks known as ark shells, characterized by their thick, often rectangular shells.
a family of marine bivalve mollusks (ark shells)

Examples

The family Arcidae includes species that are commonly found in warm, shallow marine waters.
Many shells from the family Arcidae are collected for their attractive patterns and sturdy form.
Zoologists studied the family Arcidae to understand their adaptation to different marine environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ark shell - a common name for species in the family Arcidae

Etymology

From New Latin 'Arcidae', based on the type genus 'Arca'.
