quick deflection

1. NOUN

/ kwɪk dɪˈflɛkʃən / (AmE), / kwɪk dɪˈflɛkʃən / (BrE)
a rapid or abrupt response intended to evade or avoid addressing an issue or question
rapid evasive response or reaction

Examples

She gave a quick deflection when asked about the controversial decision.
The politician's quick deflection kept the press from pressing further on the topic.
With a quick deflection, he changed the subject before things got awkward.

Synset Examples

He made a quick deflection to avoid the question.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: evasion - the act of avoiding something
  • Synonym: deflection - the act of turning aside or deviating
