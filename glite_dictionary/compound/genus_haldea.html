genus Haldea

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs hælˈdiː.ə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs halˈdiː.ə / (BrE)
A genus of small ground-dwelling snakes found in North America.
genus of North American ground snakes

Examples

The genus Haldea includes small snakes commonly found in leaf litter across the southern United States.
Biologists recently reclassified certain species formerly placed in the genus Haldea.
Specimens of the genus Haldea are nonvenomous and play a role in controlling insect populations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ground snake - any of various small terrestrial snakes

Etymology

From New Latin 'Haldea', assigned in zoological nomenclature.
