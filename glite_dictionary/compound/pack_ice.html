pack ice

1. NOUN

/ pæk aɪs / (AmE), / pak aɪs / (BrE)
A large area of floating ice masses that have been driven together by wind and currents, typically found in polar seas.
large area of floating ice masses

Examples

The ship became trapped in the pack ice and was unable to move any further north.
Satellite imagery showed the pack ice extending farther south than usual this year.
Polar bears are often seen hunting on drifting pack ice during the spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sea ice - ice that forms from seawater and floats on the ocean surface
  • Degree Lower: drift ice - floating fragments of ice not attached to land
