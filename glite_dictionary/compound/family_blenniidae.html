family Blenniidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli bləˈnɪɪˌdeɪ / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli blɛnˈɪɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of small marine and freshwater fish known as combtooth blennies, distinguished by elongated bodies and comb-like teeth.
taxonomic family of combtooth blennies

Examples

The family Blenniidae includes many species of colorful marine fish found in coral reefs.
Members of the family Blenniidae are characterized by their comb-shaped teeth and scaleless bodies.
Scientists discovered a new species within the family Blenniidae along the Pacific coastline.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blenny - a small sea fish of the family Blenniidae, with elongated body and comb-like teeth

Etymology

From New Latin 'Blenniidae', based on genus 'Blennius'.
