present perfect tense

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɛzənt ˈpɜrfɪkt tɛns / (AmE), / ˈprɛznt ˈpɜːfɪkt tɛns / (BrE)
a verb tense used to express an action that has been completed at the present time or has relevance to the present
a verb tense indicating completed action connected to the present

Examples

In English, we use the present perfect tense to talk about experiences up to now.
The sentence 'She has gone home' is in the present perfect tense.
Students sometimes confuse the present perfect tense with the simple past.

Synset Examples

‘I have finished’ is an example of the present perfect

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: past perfect tense - a verb tense expressing an action completed before a certain point in the past
  • Degree Higher: future perfect tense - a verb tense expressing an action that will be completed before a specified time in the future

Etymology

Formed from Latin *praesens* (present) and *perfectum* (completed).
