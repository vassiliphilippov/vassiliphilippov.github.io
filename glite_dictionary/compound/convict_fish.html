convict fish

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑn.vɪkt fɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.vɪkt fɪʃ / (BrE)
a greenling fish with a whitish body marked with black bands
fish with black-banded whitish body

Examples

The convict fish is easily recognized by its distinct black bands running along its pale body.
Marine biologists studied the habitat of the convict fish along the rocky reefs.
We spotted a school of convict fish swimming near the coral during our dive.
