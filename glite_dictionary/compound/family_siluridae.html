family Siluridae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli sɪˈljʊrəˌdi / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli sɪˈljʊərəˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of freshwater catfishes found in Europe and Asia, known as the Old World catfishes.
a family of Old World catfishes

Examples

The family Siluridae includes many species of large, freshwater catfish native to Asia and Europe.
Ichthyologists often study the unique adaptations found within the family Siluridae.
Members of the family Siluridae are distinguished by their elongated bodies and long barbels.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catfish - a type of freshwater fish with whisker-like barbels

Etymology

From Latin 'Silurus' (a kind of fish) plus '-idae', a standard suffix for animal family names in taxonomy.
