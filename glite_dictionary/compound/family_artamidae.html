family Artamidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ɑːrˈtæmədiː / (AmE), / ˈfæməli ɑːˈtamɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of passerine birds commonly known as wood swallows, native to Australasia and Southeast Asia.
a family of birds (wood swallows)
medical

Examples

The family Artamidae includes several species renowned for their aerial skills and social behavior.
Scientists are studying the migration patterns of the family Artamidae across the Australasian region.
Many birds in the family Artamidae have adapted to diverse habitats, ranging from forests to urban areas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: woodswallow - any bird of the family Artamidae

Etymology

From New Latin Artamidae, based on the genus name Artamus, from Greek artamos (butcher bird).
