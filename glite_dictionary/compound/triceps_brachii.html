triceps brachii

1. NOUN

/ ˈtraɪsɛps ˈbreɪkiaɪ / (AmE), / ˈtraɪsɛps ˈbræki.aɪ / (BrE)
the large muscle on the back of the upper arm, responsible for extending the forearm at the elbow
muscle on the back of the upper arm
medical

Examples

When you straighten your arm, the triceps brachii contracts to extend the elbow.
Strengthening your triceps brachii can improve your performance in push-ups and bench presses.
An injury to the triceps brachii may limit your ability to extend your arm.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: biceps brachii - the muscle on the front of the upper arm, responsible for flexing the forearm

Etymology

From Latin 'triceps' (three-headed) + 'brachii' (of the arm)
