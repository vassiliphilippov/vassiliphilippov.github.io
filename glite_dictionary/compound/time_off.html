time off

1. NOUN

/ taɪm ɔf / (AmE), / taɪm ɒf / (BrE)
a period when someone is not required to work, especially as an authorized absence from employment
authorized period away from work

Examples

The manager granted her time off so she could recover from her illness.
He usually takes a few days of time off during the holidays.
Whenever I need time off, I just fill out a request form.

Synset Examples

he requested time off to attend his grandmother's funeral

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leave - authorized period of absence from work
  • Synonym: vacation - period of time spent away from work, often for rest or travel

2. NOUN

/ taɪm ɑf / (AmE), / taɪm ɒf / (BrE)
a period when you do not work, usually taken as holiday, leave, or rest from employment or duties
period away from work or duties

Examples

I'm planning to take some time off next month to visit my family.
The company encourages employees to use their time off to maintain a healthy work-life balance.
After working nonstop for months, he finally got a few days of time off.

Synset Examples

He requested time off to recharge after a hectic week.
Use your time off wisely to relax and unwind.
She enjoys taking time off to travel every summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leave - officially sanctioned absence from work or duty
  • Synonym: holiday - a period when regular work is suspended, often for relaxation
  • Antonym: work - activity involving mental or physical effort done in order to achieve a purpose or result
