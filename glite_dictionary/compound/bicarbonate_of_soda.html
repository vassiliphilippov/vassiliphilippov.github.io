bicarbonate of soda

1. NOUN

/ baɪˈkɑɹbəˌneɪt əv ˈsoʊdə / (AmE), / baɪˌkɑːbəneɪt əv ˈsəʊdə / (BrE)
a white, soluble crystalline compound (sodium bicarbonate, NaHCO₃), used in baking, as an antacid, and in effervescent drinks
sodium bicarbonate; used in baking and as an antacid

Examples

To make the cake rise, add a teaspoon of bicarbonate of soda to the batter.
My grandmother always keeps bicarbonate of soda in the kitchen for cleaning and baking.
If you have indigestion, a little bicarbonate of soda in water can help settle your stomach.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: baking soda - another common name for sodium bicarbonate

Etymology

From 'bicarbonate', a chemical term, and 'soda' referring to sodium compounds.
