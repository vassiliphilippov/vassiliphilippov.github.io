Black and Tan

1. NOUN

/ ˌblæk ən ˈtæn / (AmE), / ˌblæk ən ˈtæn / (BrE)
A member of an English force recruited to assist the Royal Irish Constabulary in suppressing the Irish independence movement between 1919 and 1921, distinguished by their black and tan uniforms.
English constable in Ireland (1919–21)

Examples

During the Irish War of Independence, the Black and Tan forces were infamous for their harsh tactics.
His grandfather often told stories about confrontations with the Black and Tan squads in his village.
The legacy of the Black and Tan era still lingers in Irish collective memory.

Etymology

Refers to the colors of the improvised uniforms worn by recruits—black (from Royal Irish Constabulary uniforms) and tan (from British army khaki).
