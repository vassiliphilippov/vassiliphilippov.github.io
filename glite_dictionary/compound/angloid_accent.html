angloid accent

1. NOUN

/ ˈæŋ.lɔɪd ˈæk.sənt / (AmE), / ˈæŋ.lɔɪd ˈæk.sənt / (BrE)
an accent that resembles the speech of English speakers, especially when adopted by non-native speakers
accent resembling English speakers

Examples

Many actors struggle to master an angloid accent for international roles.
The film features a villain whose angloid accent adds to his mysterious persona.
She was praised for her perfect imitation of an angloid accent in the play.

Synset Examples

He speaks with an angloid accent that hints at his background.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: English accent - the accent of native English speakers

Etymology

Formed from 'Anglo-' (relating to English) and '-oid' (resembling); 'accent' from Latin 'accentus'.
