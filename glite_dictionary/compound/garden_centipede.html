garden centipede

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɑrdn ˈsɛntɪˌpid / (AmE), / ˈɡɑːdn ˈsɛntɪpiːd / (BrE)
a minute arthropod, often found infesting the underground parts of vegetable and greenhouse crops
small soil-dwelling arthropod pest

Examples

The garden centipede can cause significant damage to the roots of young tomato plants.
Gardeners often check the soil for garden centipedes when their crops show signs of wilting.
Applying organic mulch may help deter garden centipede infestations in the vegetable patch.
