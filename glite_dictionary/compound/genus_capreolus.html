genus Capreolus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs kæpˈriː.oʊ.ləs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs kæpˈriː.ə.ləs / (BrE)
A genus of deer that includes the roe deer, native to Europe and Asia.
genus of deer (roe deer)

Examples

Scientists studied the population trends of the genus Capreolus in various European forests.
The genus Capreolus is characterized by its small size and short antlers.
Fossil records show the ancient presence of the genus Capreolus across Eurasia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: roe deer - a small, graceful species of deer in Europe and Asia

Etymology

From Latin 'Capreolus' meaning 'a little goat' or 'roe deer'.
