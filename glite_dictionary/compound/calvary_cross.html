Calvary cross

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæl.vɛri krɑs / (AmE), / ˈkæl.vəri krɒs / (BrE)
A representation of a Latin cross set on three steps, often used in Christian art and symbolism.
Latin cross on three steps

Examples

The church altar was adorned with a large Calvary cross made of wood.
In heraldry, the Calvary cross symbolizes the steps leading up to Calvary.
Several gravestones in the cemetery were engraved with a Calvary cross.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Latin cross - A cross with a longer descending arm

Etymology

From Calvary, the hill near Jerusalem where Jesus was crucified, combined with 'cross'.
