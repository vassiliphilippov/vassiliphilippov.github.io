family Tethyidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli tɛˈθɪɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈfamɪli tɛˈθɪɪˌdiː / (BrE)
a family of sea hares, marine gastropod mollusks in the order Anaspidea, known for their soft bodies and internal shells
a family of sea hares (marine mollusks)

Examples

Species belonging to the family Tethyidae are often found in shallow coastal waters.
Marine biologists discovered a new member of the family Tethyidae near the Mediterranean coast.
The family Tethyidae is known for its distinctive sea hare species that release ink when threatened.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sea hare - a marine mollusk with a soft body and a small internal shell

Etymology

From New Latin *Tethyidae*, derived from the genus *Tethys*, named after a Greek sea goddess.
