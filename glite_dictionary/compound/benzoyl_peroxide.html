benzoyl peroxide

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛnzoʊɪl pəˈrɑːksaɪd / (AmE), / ˈben.zɔɪl pəˈrɒk.saɪd / (BrE)
a white crystalline chemical used as a bleaching agent and as a catalyst in chemical reactions, especially in the treatment of acne
crystalline peroxide used in bleaching and acne treatment

Examples

The dermatologist recommended benzoyl peroxide cream to help clear up my acne.
Benzoyl peroxide is often used in laboratories as a catalyst for certain polymerization reactions.
Many over-the-counter skin cleansers contain benzoyl peroxide as an active ingredient.

Etymology

From 'benzoyl', relating to the benzene ring, and 'peroxide', from Greek 'oxys' (sharp, acid) via French and German.
