run-on sentence

1. NOUN

/ ˈrʌn ɑn ˈsɛntəns / (AmE), / ˈrʌn ɒn ˈsɛntəns / (BrE)
An ungrammatical sentence in which two or more independent clauses are joined without an appropriate conjunction or punctuation.
ungrammatical sentence lacking proper conjunctions or punctuation

Examples

Please revise this run-on sentence to separate the ideas with proper punctuation.
A run-on sentence can make your writing unclear and confusing to readers.
She wrote a run-on sentence that combined three thoughts without a single period.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fragment - an incomplete sentence, often lacking a subject or verb
