American angelica tree

1. NOUN

/ əˈmɛrɪkən ænˈdʒɛlɪkə triː / (AmE), / əˈmɛrɪkən ænˈdʒɛlɪkə triː / (BrE)
a small deciduous tree or shrub native to the eastern United States, known for its spiny stems and clusters of white flowers
small deciduous tree/shrub of eastern US

Examples

The American angelica tree is often found along woodland edges in the southeastern United States.
Gardeners appreciate the American angelica tree for its striking white flower clusters in late summer.
Despite its thorns, the American angelica tree attracts many pollinators when in bloom.
