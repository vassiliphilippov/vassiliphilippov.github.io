hypothalamic releasing hormone

1. NOUN

/ haɪ.poʊ.θəˈlæ.mɪk rɪˈlisɪŋ ˈhɔrˌmoʊn / (AmE), / haɪ.pəʊ.θəˈlæ.mɪk rɪˈliːsɪŋ ˈhɔː.məʊn / (BrE)
Any of several hormones produced by the hypothalamus that are carried to the anterior pituitary gland, stimulating the release of specific pituitary hormones.
hormone from hypothalamus that stimulates pituitary
medical

Examples

The doctor explained how a hypothalamic releasing hormone triggers the pituitary to secrete growth hormone.
Researchers discovered a new hypothalamic releasing hormone affecting thyroid function.
Disorders in hypothalamic releasing hormone production can disrupt the entire endocrine system.

Etymology

From hypothalamus (part of the brain) and hormone; 'releasing' refers to their action of triggering release of other hormones.
