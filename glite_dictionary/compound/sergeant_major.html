sergeant major

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɑrdʒənt ˈmeɪdʒər / (AmE), / ˌsɑːdʒənt ˈmeɪdʒə / (BrE)
a large blue-grey damselfish with black vertical stripes, found nearly worldwide in shallow marine waters
blue-grey striped damselfish

Examples

A school of sergeant major fish darted around the coral reef.
The distinctive stripes of the sergeant major make it easy to identify for snorkelers.
Sergeant majors are commonly seen near the surface in tropical oceans.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɝdʒənt ˈmeɪdʒɚ / (AmE), / ˈsɑːdʒənt ˈmeɪdʒə / (BrE)
a noncommissioned officer serving as the chief administrative officer in an army headquarters unit; also used for the highest-ranking noncommissioned officer in some armed forces
chief noncommissioned officer in army unit

Examples

The sergeant major called the company to attention before the inspection began.
After twenty years of service, he retired as a sergeant major in the army.
The sergeant major is responsible for maintaining discipline among the troops.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: sergeant - a noncommissioned officer above corporal and below staff sergeant
  • Degree Higher: warrant officer - a rank above sergeant major in some military organizations

Etymology

From French 'sergent-major', originally referring to a senior NCO in European armies.
