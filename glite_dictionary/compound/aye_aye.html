aye aye

1. INTJ

/ aɪ aɪ / (AmE), / aɪ aɪ / (BrE)
a naval response indicating understanding or compliance, used especially to acknowledge an order
naval response meaning 'I understand'

Examples

When given instructions by the captain, the sailor replied smartly, 'aye aye, sir.'
'All hands to deck!' commanded the officer, and a chorus of 'aye aye' echoed in response.
On receiving the orders, she acknowledged them with a firm 'aye aye' before moving to her post.

Synset Examples

With a salute, he said, 'Aye aye, sir!'

Related Terms

  • Synonym: roger - used in radio communication to indicate a message is received and understood
  • Synonym: yes - word expressing agreement or affirmation
  • Antonym: negative - word expressing refusal or disagreement, especially in radio communication

Etymology

Reduplication of 'aye' (meaning 'yes'), used in naval contexts to affirm compliance.
