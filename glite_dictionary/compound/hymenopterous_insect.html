hymenopterous insect

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪmɪˈnɑptəɹəs ˈɪnˌsɛkt / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪmɪˈnɒptərəs ˈɪnˌsɛkt / (BrE)
an insect belonging to the order Hymenoptera, such as bees, wasps, and ants, typically having two pairs of membranous wings and an ovipositor often adapted for stinging or piercing
insect of the Hymenoptera order (bee, wasp, ant etc.)

Examples

The honeybee is a well-known hymenopterous insect vital for pollination.
Some species of hymenopterous insect have developed complex social structures, like ants and wasps.
A scientist may collect various hymenopterous insects for research on insect behavior.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hymenoptera - order of insects including bees, wasps, and ants

Etymology

From Greek hymen (membrane) + pteron (wing), referring to the membranous wings.
