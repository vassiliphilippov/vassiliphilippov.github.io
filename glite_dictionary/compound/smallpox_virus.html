smallpox virus

1. NOUN

/ ˈsmɔlˌpɑks ˈvaɪrəs / (AmE), / ˈsmɔːlpɒks ˈvaɪərəs / (BrE)
the virus (Variola virus) that causes smallpox in humans, sometimes considered a potential bioweapon
virus causing smallpox in humans
medical

Examples

The smallpox virus was responsible for millions of deaths before its eradication.
Scientists have stored samples of the smallpox virus in high-security laboratories.
There are concerns that the smallpox virus could be used as a biological weapon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smallpox - a serious infectious disease eradicated in the 20th century
