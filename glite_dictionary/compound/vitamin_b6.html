vitamin B6

1. NOUN

/ ˌvaɪtəmɪn ˌbi sɪks / (AmE), / ˌvɪtəmɪn ˌbiː sɪks / (BrE)
a B vitamin essential for the metabolism of amino acids and starch in the body
essential B vitamin for metabolism
medical

Examples

Foods such as bananas and avocados are good sources of vitamin B6.
A deficiency in vitamin B6 can lead to anemia and other health problems.
The supplement contains a daily dose of vitamin B6 to support energy metabolism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pyridoxine - another name for vitamin B6
  • Degree Higher: vitamin B12 - another B vitamin important for metabolism
  • Degree Lower: vitamin B1 - another B vitamin with a different role
