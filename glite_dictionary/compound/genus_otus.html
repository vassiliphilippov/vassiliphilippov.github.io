genus Otus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈoʊ.təs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈəʊ.təs / (BrE)
A genus of owls in the family Strigidae, including the Scops owl and related species.
a genus of owls including the Scops owl

Examples

The genus Otus includes several species of small, nocturnal owls.
Scientists recently discovered a new species within the genus Otus in Southeast Asia.
The Scops owl is perhaps the most well-known representative of the genus Otus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Scops owl - a small owl, member of the genus Otus

Etymology

From Latin 'otus', meaning 'horned owl'.
