heterophil antibody

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɛtəroʊˌfɪl ˈæntɪˌbɑdi / (AmE), / ˌhɛtərəʊˌfɪl ˈæntɪˌbɒdi / (BrE)
an antibody present in the blood of individuals with infectious mononucleosis, used as a diagnostic marker for the disease
antibody found in mono patients
medical

Examples

The presence of heterophil antibody in the blood confirmed the doctor's suspicion of infectious mononucleosis.
A positive test for heterophil antibody is often used to help diagnose cases of glandular fever.
Researchers are studying the accuracy of heterophil antibody detection in adolescent patients.

Etymology

From 'heterophil', meaning reacting with antigens from a different species, and 'antibody'.
