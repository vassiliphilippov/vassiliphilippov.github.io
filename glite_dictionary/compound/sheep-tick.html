sheep-tick

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃiːp tɪk / (AmE), / ˈʃiːp tɪk / (BrE)
A wingless parasitic fly that feeds on the blood of sheep and cattle.
parasitic insect on sheep and cattle

Examples

The farmer inspected the flock for any sign of sheep-tick infestations.
Regular shearing helps reduce the risk of sheep-tick attacks on livestock.
Chemical dips are often used to protect sheep from sheep-tick parasites.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tick - small parasitic arachnid that feeds on the blood of mammals and birds

2. NOUN

/ ʃip tɪk / (AmE), / ʃiːp tɪk / (BrE)
a tick that parasitizes sheep and other animals, sometimes transmitting diseases such as louping ill and Lyme disease
tick parasitic on sheep, disease vector

Examples

The farmer found a sheep-tick attached to one of his lambs during routine inspection.
Veterinarians warn that sheep-ticks can transmit dangerous diseases to livestock and humans.
A single bite from a sheep-tick can result in serious illness if not treated promptly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tick - a small arachnid parasite that feeds on blood
