kilowatt hour

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɪl.oʊˌwɑt ˌaʊɚ / (AmE), / ˈkɪl.əwɒt ˌaʊə / (BrE)
a unit of energy equal to the work done by a power of 1,000 watts operating for one hour
unit of energy (1,000 watts for one hour)

Examples

Our electricity bill is calculated based on the number of kilowatt hours we use.
Charging an electric car can take about 40 kilowatt hours of energy.
The appliance consumes one kilowatt hour when used for an hour.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kilojoule - a metric unit of energy equal to 1,000 joules
  • Degree Higher: megawatt hour - a unit of energy equal to one million watt hours

Etymology

Formed from 'kilowatt' (from 'kilo-' meaning thousand and 'watt', the unit of power) and 'hour'.
