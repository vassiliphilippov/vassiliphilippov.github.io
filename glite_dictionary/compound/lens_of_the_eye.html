lens of the eye

1. NOUN

/ lɛnz əv ði aɪ / (AmE), / lɛnz əv ði aɪ / (BrE)
the transparent, biconvex structure behind the iris of the eye that focuses light onto the retina
transparent eye structure focusing light
medical

Examples

The lens of the eye becomes cloudy in patients with cataracts.
As we age, the lens of the eye may lose its flexibility, causing presbyopia.
Light passes through the cornea and the lens of the eye before reaching the retina.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cornea - transparent front part of the eye covering the iris and pupil
  • Synonym: retina - light-sensitive layer at the back of the eye
