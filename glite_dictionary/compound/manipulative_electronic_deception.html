manipulative electronic deception

1. NOUN

/ məˈnɪp.jə.lə.tɪv ɪˌlɛk.trɒ.nɪk dɪˈsɛp.ʃən / (AmE), / məˈnɪp.jʊ.lə.tɪv ɪˌlɛk.trɒ.nɪk dɪˈsɛp.ʃən / (BrE)
actions taken to hide or alter electronic signals in order to mislead an enemy or prevent the enemy from detecting sensitive operations
use of electronic means to mislead or conceal information

Examples

As part of their strategy, the unit employed manipulative electronic deception to confuse enemy radars.
The success of the operation depended on effective manipulative electronic deception to mask their movement.
Advances in technology have made manipulative electronic deception more sophisticated and harder to counter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: electronic countermeasure - techniques used to deceive or interfere with enemy electronic systems
