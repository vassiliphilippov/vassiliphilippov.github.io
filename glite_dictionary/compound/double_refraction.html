double refraction

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʌbəl rɪˈfrækʃən / (AmE), / ˈdʌbəl rɪˈfrækʃən / (BrE)
the splitting of a single light ray into two separate rays that are polarized at right angles when passing through certain materials, such as calcite
splitting of light into two rays in some materials

Examples

Calcite crystals are well known for their ability to exhibit double refraction.
The phenomenon of double refraction is essential in the study of polarized light.
When a beam of light enters certain crystals, double refraction occurs, producing two distinct rays.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: birefringence - the property of a material causing double refraction
