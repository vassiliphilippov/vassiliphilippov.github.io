deck-house

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛkˌhaʊs / (AmE), / ˈdɛkˌhaʊs / (BrE)
A structure built on the upper deck of a ship, often used as shelter or for accommodation.
superstructure on upper deck of a ship

Examples

The captain emerged from the deck-house to inspect the ship's progress.
Strong winds rattled the windows of the deck-house during the storm.
The crew stored their navigation instruments inside the deck-house for safekeeping.

Etymology

From 'deck' (the floor-like surface covering a ship's hull) and 'house' (shelter or building).
