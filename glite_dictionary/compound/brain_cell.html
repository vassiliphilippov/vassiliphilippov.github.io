brain cell

1. NOUN

/ ˈbreɪn sɛl / (AmE), / ˈbreɪn sɛl / (BrE)
a cell in the brain, typically a neuron, that transmits and processes information
nerve cell found in the brain

Examples

Scientists are studying how brain cells communicate with each other.
Heavy drinking over many years can damage brain cells.
A stroke can cause certain brain cells to die, leading to loss of function.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neuron - a specialized cell transmitting nerve impulses; a nerve cell
  • Degree Lower: glial cell - a cell in the brain that supports and protects neurons
