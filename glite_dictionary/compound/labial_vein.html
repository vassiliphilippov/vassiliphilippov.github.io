labial vein

1. NOUN

/ ˈleɪ.bi.əl veɪn / (AmE), / ˈleɪ.bi.əl veɪn / (BrE)
a vein that drains blood from the lips of the vulva
vein draining the lips of the vulva
medical

Examples

The labial vein can become more prominent during pregnancy due to increased blood flow.
Infections in the vulva may spread via the labial vein to other pelvic veins.
A detailed anatomy class included diagrams highlighting the labial vein and surrounding structures.

Etymology

From Latin 'labium' meaning 'lip' and 'vein'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈleɪ.bi.əl veɪn / (AmE), / ˈleɪ.bi.əl veɪn / (BrE)
a vein that drains blood from the lips of the mouth
vein that drains the lips
medical

Examples

The surgeon carefully avoided the labial vein during the procedure to minimize bleeding.
Swelling of the labial vein can occur following trauma to the mouth.
During the anatomy lesson, we observed the labial vein in a cross-section of the lip.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vein - a blood vessel that carries blood toward the heart

Etymology

From Latin 'labium' (lip) and 'vein', referring to veins of the lips.
