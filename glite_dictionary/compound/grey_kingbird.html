grey kingbird

1. NOUN

/ ɡreɪ ˈkɪŋˌbɝd / (AmE), / ɡreɪ ˈkɪŋˌbɜːd / (BrE)
a large kingbird (Tyrannus dominicensis) native to the southeastern United States and the Caribbean, known for its gray plumage and aggressive behavior
a large gray kingbird found in the Americas

Examples

The grey kingbird is often seen perched on telephone wires in southern Florida.
During migration season, we spotted several grey kingbirds along the coast.
Unlike the eastern kingbird, the grey kingbird has a stouter beak and a more robust body.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: eastern kingbird - a related but smaller species of kingbird with dark upperparts and white underparts
