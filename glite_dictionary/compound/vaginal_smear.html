vaginal smear

1. NOUN

/ vəˈdʒaɪ.nəl smɪr / (AmE), / vəˈdʒaɪ.nəl smɪə / (BrE)
a sample of cells taken from the vaginal lining for examination, typically to screen for infections or abnormal cells
sample of vaginal cells for examination
medical

Examples

The doctor recommended a vaginal smear as part of the routine gynecological check-up.
Laboratory analysis of the vaginal smear showed no signs of infection.
A vaginal smear can help detect abnormal cervical cells early on.
