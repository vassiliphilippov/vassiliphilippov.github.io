European bean

1. NOUN

/ jʊˈroʊpiən biːn / (AmE), / jʊəˈrəʊpiən biːn / (BrE)
an upright plant native to Europe, grown mainly for its large, flat edible seeds and also used as animal fodder
Old World bean plant with edible seeds

Examples

Farmers cultivate the European bean for both its seeds and to feed their livestock.
Dishes featuring European bean are popular in Mediterranean cuisine.
The European bean thrives in the temperate climates of Northern Europe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: broad bean - another name for the large-seeded European bean plant
  • Synonym: fava bean - common North American term for the same plant
