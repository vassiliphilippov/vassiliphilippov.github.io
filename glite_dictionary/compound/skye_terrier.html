Skye terrier

1. NOUN

/ skaɪ təˈrɪr / (AmE), / skaɪ ˈtɛrɪə / (BrE)
a Scottish breed of terrier with a long, low body, short legs, and a dense, shaggy coat, originally from the Isle of Skye
Scottish breed of terrier dog

Examples

The Skye terrier is known for its loyalty and elegant, flowing coat.
Queen Victoria was a notable enthusiast of the Skye terrier breed.
Their Skye terrier quickly became the favorite pet of the family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: terrier - a type of small, energetic dog, often bred for hunting

Etymology

From the Isle of Skye in Scotland, where the breed originated, and 'terrier' from Old French 'chien terrier' (earth dog).
