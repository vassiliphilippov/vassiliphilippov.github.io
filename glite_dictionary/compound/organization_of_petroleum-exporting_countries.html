Organization of Petroleum-Exporting Countries

1. PROPN

/ ɔːrɡənaɪˈzeɪʃən əv ˌpetroʊliəm ɪksˈpɔːrtɪŋ ˈkʌntriz / (AmE), / ɔːɡənaɪˈzeɪʃ(ə)n əv ˌpɛtrəʊliəm ɪksˈpɔːtɪŋ ˈkʌntriz / (BrE)
an international organization formed in 1961 by major oil-producing countries to coordinate petroleum production and pricing policies
global alliance of oil-producing countries

Examples

The Organization of Petroleum-Exporting Countries announced a reduction in oil production to stabilize prices.
Many governments monitor decisions made by the Organization of Petroleum-Exporting Countries because they impact global energy markets.
Indonesia was once a member of the Organization of Petroleum-Exporting Countries but later suspended its membership.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: OPEC - abbreviation for Organization of Petroleum-Exporting Countries
