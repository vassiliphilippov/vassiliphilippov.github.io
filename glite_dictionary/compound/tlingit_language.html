Tlingit language

1. NOUN

/ ˈtlɪŋɡɪt ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈtlɪŋɡɪt ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the native Na-Dene language spoken by the Tlingit people of southeastern Alaska and western Canada
language of the Tlingit people

Examples

Few people today are fluent in the Tlingit language outside of Alaska.
She is taking a course to learn the Tlingit language and preserve her heritage.
There are newly published books and audio recordings in the Tlingit language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Na-Dene - a language family including Tlingit and other languages

Etymology

From Tlingit (self-designation), meaning 'people of the tides'.
