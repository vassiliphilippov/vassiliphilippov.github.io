Pollyannaish belief

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑːliˈænəɪʃ bəˈliːf / (AmE), / ˌpɒliˈænəɪʃ bɪˈliːf / (BrE)
An unrealistically or excessively optimistic outlook, often ignoring potential problems or difficulties.
unrealistically optimistic outlook

Examples

Despite the setbacks, she clung to her Pollyannaish belief that success was inevitable.
His Pollyannaish belief in people's goodness sometimes left him disappointed.
Some critics argue that a Pollyannaish belief in technology ignores potential risks.

Synset Examples

He maintained a Pollyannaish belief that everything would work out.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: optimism - hopefulness and confidence about the future
  • Antonym: pessimism - tendency to see the worst aspect of things

Etymology

From the character Pollyanna in the 1913 novel by Eleanor H. Porter, known for her excessive optimism, combined with 'belief'.
