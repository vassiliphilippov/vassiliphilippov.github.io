coat-of-mail shell

1. NOUN

/ ˌkoʊt əv ˈmeɪl ʃɛl / (AmE), / ˌkəʊt əv ˈmeɪl ʃɛl / (BrE)
A primitive marine mollusk with a body covered by eight overlapping calcareous plates, also known as a chiton.
marine mollusk with eight shell plates

Examples

The coat-of-mail shell protected the chiton from predators as it clung to the rocky shore.
Scientists collected several coat-of-mail shells during their marine survey of the region.
The coat-of-mail shell is remarkable for its flexible, armored appearance reminiscent of medieval armor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chiton - A marine mollusk with a shell composed of eight plates

Etymology

The name refers to the resemblance of the mollusk's shell to a suit of chain mail armor.
