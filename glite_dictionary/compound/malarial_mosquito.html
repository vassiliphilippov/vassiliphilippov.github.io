malarial mosquito

1. NOUN

/ məˈlɛriəl məˈskiːtoʊ / (AmE), / məˈleəriəl məˈskiːtəʊ / (BrE)
a mosquito of the genus Anopheles that transmits the parasite causing malaria
mosquito that transmits malaria
medical

Examples

The spread of malaria depends on the presence of the malarial mosquito in tropical regions.
Scientists are studying how the malarial mosquito transmits the deadly parasite to humans.
Efforts to eradicate the malarial mosquito have significantly reduced malaria cases in some countries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Anopheles - a genus of mosquito that transmits malaria
