Welsh rabbit

1. NOUN

/ wɛlʃ ˈræbɪt / (AmE), / wɛlʃ ˈræbɪt / (BrE)
A dish consisting of melted cheese, often combined with ale or beer, served hot over slices of toast.
melted cheese on toast, sometimes with ale or beer

Examples

For supper, he prepared Welsh rabbit with extra sharp cheddar melted over thick slices of bread.
Many British pubs serve Welsh rabbit as a traditional comfort food.
Adding a dash of mustard gives Welsh rabbit an extra kick of flavor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rarebit - an alternative name for the dish Welsh rabbit

Etymology

The term dates from the 18th century, possibly with 'rabbit' humorously used instead of 'rarebit' to mock the absence of meat.
