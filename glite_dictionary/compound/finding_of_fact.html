finding of fact

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪndɪŋ əv ˈfækt / (AmE), / ˈfaɪndɪŋ əv ˈfakt / (BrE)
(Law) A decision made by a court or jury regarding the facts of a case, which may be used as the basis for a legal judgment.
court or jury's decision on facts
legal

Examples

The jury's finding of fact was crucial in determining the outcome of the trial.
The judge relied on the finding of fact when handing down the sentence.
The appeal was denied because the court found no error in the original finding of fact.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: finding - a conclusion reached as the result of investigation or examination
