howler monkey

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaʊlər ˌmʌŋki / (AmE), / ˈhaʊlə ˌmʌŋki / (BrE)
a large monkey of the tropical forests of Central and South America, known for its loud howling cry
a loud-voiced South American monkey

Examples

The howler monkey woke the entire village with its powerful calls at dawn.
Tourists in the rainforest often hear the distant roar of a howler monkey.
Researchers observed a group of howler monkeys moving through the treetops.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: monkey - a primate of the group that includes Old World monkeys and New World monkeys
