rabbit brush

1. NOUN

/ ˈræbɪt ˌbrʌʃ / (AmE), / ˈræbɪt ˌbrʌʃ / (BrE)
a pleasantly aromatic North American shrub with slender, hairy branches and clusters of small yellow flowers, found on western plains; used as a source of yellow dye
shrubby plant of western North America

Examples

In late summer, the rabbit brush blooms, transforming the desert landscape with its bright yellow flowers.
The Navajo traditionally use rabbit brush to make a yellow dye for weaving.
Jackrabbits often find shelter under the dense branches of the rabbit brush.
