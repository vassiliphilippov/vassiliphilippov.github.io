dolce far niente

1. NOUN

/ ˌdoʊltʃeɪ fɑr ˈnjɛnteɪ / (AmE), / ˌdɒltʃeɪ fɑː ˈnjɛnteɪ / (BrE)
a feeling of pleasant idleness and carefree relaxation; the sweetness of doing nothing
pleasant, carefree idleness

Examples

During their vacation, they enjoyed the dolce far niente of sunny afternoons by the sea.
She longed for a weekend of dolce far niente after months of hectic schedules.
He described his retirement as a period of dolce far niente, filled with books and naps.

Etymology

From Italian, literally ‘sweet doing nothing’.
