genus Luffa

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈluː.fə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈlʌf.ə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical and subtropical vines, known as dishcloth gourds, whose fibrous fruit is often used as a scrubbing sponge.
a genus of tropical vines (dishcloth gourds)

Examples

The genus Luffa includes species commonly grown for their sponge-like fruit.
Gardeners cultivate plants from the genus Luffa to produce natural bath sponges.
Research into the genus Luffa has revealed potential uses of its fibers in eco-friendly materials.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loofah - the fibrous, sponge-like fruit of Luffa plants used as a scrubber

Etymology

From Arabic *lūfa* (loofah gourd), adopted into New Latin in botanical nomenclature.
