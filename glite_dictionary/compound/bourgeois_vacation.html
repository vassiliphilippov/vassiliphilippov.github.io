bourgeois vacation

1. NOUN

/ bʊrˌʒwɑ vəˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈbʊəʒwɑː vəˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
a holiday characterized by luxury, affluence, and middle-class comfort, often seen as typical of the bourgeoisie
luxurious, affluent holiday experience

Examples

They saved up for a bourgeois vacation in the south of France.
Her friends teased her about her taste for a bourgeois vacation.
After years of backpacking, they decided to try a truly bourgeois vacation instead.

Synset Examples

They enjoyed a bourgeois vacation in a five-star resort.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: luxury holiday - a holiday marked by great comfort and elegance
  • Antonym: budget vacation - a holiday involving minimal expense or luxury

Etymology

From French 'bourgeois' (middle class); 'vacation' from Latin 'vacare' (to be empty, free).
