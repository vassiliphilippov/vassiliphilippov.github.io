safety net

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪfti nɛt / (AmE), / ˈseɪfti nɛt / (BrE)
a large, strong net placed below acrobats or aerialists to catch them if they fall during a performance
net to catch acrobats in circus

Examples

The circus ensured that a safety net was in place before the acrobats performed their daring stunts.
After losing her balance on the trapeze, the gymnast landed safely in the safety net.
Inspectors examined the safety net to make sure it was secure and free from holes.

2. NOUN

/ ˈseɪfti nɛt / (AmE), / ˈseɪfti nɛt / (BrE)
something that provides security or protection against financial or professional hardship or failure
guarantee of protection against hardship

Examples

After losing his job, the unemployment benefit acted as a safety net for his family.
Many people rely on social security as a financial safety net in retirement.
Without a safety net, entrepreneurs risk losing everything if their ventures fail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lifeline - a thing on which someone or something depends or which provides a means of escape from a difficult situation
  • Synonym: buffer - something that lessens or absorbs the shock of an impact or setback
  • Antonym: risk - exposure to danger, harm, or loss

Etymology

Originally referred to a net below circus performers to prevent injury, later used figuratively for financial or professional protection.

3. NOUN

/ ˈseɪfti nɛt / (AmE), / ˈseɪfti nɛt / (BrE)
a system or arrangement that provides support or protection against hardship, especially in times of financial or social need
system providing protection against hardship

Examples

The government established a safety net for those affected by the economic crisis.
She relied on her family's safety net after losing her job.
Without a financial safety net, many people face serious difficulties.

Synset Examples

Unemployment benefits serve as a safety net for workers.
The program acts as a safety net for vulnerable families.
They needed a safety net before starting their business.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: buffer - something that lessens or absorbs impact or hardship
  • Synonym: protection - the action of keeping someone or something safe

Etymology

Originally referred to the literal net under circus performers; used figuratively from early 20th century.
