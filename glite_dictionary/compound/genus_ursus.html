genus Ursus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒinəs ˈɜrsəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈɜːsəs / (BrE)
the type genus of the bear family Ursidae, including the brown bear and, in some classifications, all bears
bear genus in biological classification

Examples

The genus Ursus includes well-known species such as the brown bear and the polar bear.
Modern taxonomists debate which bears to assign to the genus Ursus.
Fossils from the genus Ursus provide insight into the evolution of present-day bears.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bear - large heavy mammal of the family Ursidae
  • Synonym: Ursidae - the bear family in biological taxonomy

Etymology

From Latin 'Ursus' meaning 'bear'
