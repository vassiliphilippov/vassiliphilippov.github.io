coping stone

1. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊpɪŋ stoʊn / (AmE), / ˈkəʊpɪŋ stəʊn / (BrE)
a stone set on top of a wall to form a protective or finishing cap
top protective stone on a wall

Examples

The mason expertly placed each coping stone along the length of the garden wall.
After years of weathering, several coping stones needed to be replaced on the old bridge.
A missing coping stone can allow water to seep into the wall, causing long-term damage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: capstone - a stone fixed on top of something, especially a wall

Etymology

From 'coping' (the top layer or covering of a wall) + 'stone'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊpɪŋ stoʊn / (AmE), / ˈkəʊpɪŋ stəʊn / (BrE)
a stone, usually shaped and sometimes ornamental, used to form the top layer of a wall to protect it from weather and complete its appearance
protective top stone on a wall

Examples

The builder placed a coping stone at each end of the boundary wall.
After the storm, several coping stones had fallen from the old churchyard wall.
A decorative coping stone was added to enhance the garden wall’s appearance.

Synset Examples

The architect chose a sleek coping stone for the new building.
He replaced the damaged coping stone on the garden wall.
The wall was finished with a beautiful coping stone.

Etymology

From 'cope' (to cover) + 'stone'; 'cope' from Middle French 'coupe' and Late Latin 'copa' (cowl, covering).
