object glass

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑbdʒɛkt ˌɡlæs / (AmE), / ˈɒbdʒɪkt ˌɡlɑːs / (BrE)
the lens or system of lenses in a telescope or microscope that is closest to the object being viewed
lens nearest object in optical device

Examples

The object glass of the microscope must be perfectly clean for precise observations.
Over time, the object glass in an old telescope may become scratched or cloudy.
He carefully adjusted the object glass to bring the specimen into sharp focus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: objective lens - the lens in a microscope or telescope nearest to the object being viewed

Etymology

From 'object' (Latin 'objectum', something thrown before) + 'glass', referring to a lens.
