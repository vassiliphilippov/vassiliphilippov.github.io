ruffed grouse

1. NOUN

/ ˈrʌft ˌɡraʊs / (AmE), / ˈrʌft ˌɡraʊs / (BrE)
a medium-sized North American game bird with a fan-shaped tail and a ruff of feathers around its neck, found in forests of the eastern United States and Canada
North American game bird with a ruff of neck feathers

Examples

We spotted a ruffed grouse while hiking in the forest early this morning.
The ruffed grouse is known for its drumming sound during mating season.
Hunters consider the ruffed grouse one of the most challenging game birds in North America.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: grouse - a bird of the pheasant family, often hunted as game

Etymology

From 'ruffed', referring to its distinctive feathered ruff, and 'grouse', a family of birds.
