madder family

1. NOUN

/ ˈmædər ˈfæməli / (AmE), / ˈmadə ˈfamɪli / (BrE)
A family of mostly tropical trees, shrubs, and herbs (Rubiaceae), including coffee, chinchona, gardenia, madder, bedstraws, and partridgeberry.
family of tropical plants (Rubiaceae)

Examples

The madder family includes economically important plants such as coffee and quinine-yielding trees.
Gardenias, which belong to the madder family, are popular ornamental shrubs in many gardens.
Researchers studied the medicinal properties of several herbs in the madder family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Rubiaceae - the botanical family to which the madder family belongs

Etymology

Named after 'madder,' a plant in the family; from Old English 'madder' (a type of dye plant).
