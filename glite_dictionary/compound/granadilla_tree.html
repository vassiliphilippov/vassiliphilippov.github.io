granadilla tree

1. NOUN

/ ɡrəˈnædʒələ tri / (AmE), / ɡrəˈnadɪlə triː / (BrE)
a West Indian tree (Brya ebenus) that yields a high-quality green ebony wood
West Indian tree yielding green ebony

Examples

The wood of the granadilla tree is highly prized for making musical instruments.
Carpenters in the Caribbean often seek out the granadilla tree for its durable timber.
Conservationists are concerned about the dwindling population of granadilla trees in the wild.
