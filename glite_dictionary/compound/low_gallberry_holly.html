low gallberry holly

1. NOUN

/ loʊ ˈɡælˌbɛri ˈhɑli / (AmE), / ləʊ ˈɡælˌbɛri ˈhɒli / (BrE)
an evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States, also known as inkberry, with glossy leaves and black berries
evergreen shrub, also called inkberry

Examples

The low gallberry holly thrives in wet, sandy soils along the coastal plains of the southeastern United States.
Gardeners often plant low gallberry holly for its attractive evergreen foliage and wildlife benefits.
The berries of the low gallberry holly provide food for birds throughout the winter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inkberry - an evergreen shrub with black berries, another name for low gallberry holly
