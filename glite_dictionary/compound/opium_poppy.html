opium poppy

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊpiəm ˌpɑpi / (AmE), / ˈəʊpiəm ˌpɒpi / (BrE)
a plant species of southwestern Asia with greyish leaves and white or reddish flowers, cultivated as the source of opium
plant used as source of opium

Examples

The opium poppy has been cultivated for centuries for both medicinal and illegal purposes.
Scientists study the opium poppy to better understand how pain-relieving compounds are produced.
Many traditional remedies were derived from the sap of the opium poppy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Papaver somniferum - scientific name for the opium poppy

Etymology

From Latin 'opium' (from Greek 'opion', poppy juice) and English 'poppy'.
