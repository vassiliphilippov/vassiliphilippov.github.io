tau neutrino

1. NOUN

/ taʊ nuˈtrinoʊ / (AmE), / taʊ njuːˈtriːnəʊ / (BrE)
A type of neutrino associated with the tau lepton, forming one of the three known neutrino flavors in particle physics.
subatomic particle; neutrino type

Examples

The discovery of the tau neutrino confirmed the existence of the third type of neutrino predicted by particle physics theory.
Scientists at Fermilab detected the elusive tau neutrino after years of careful experimentation.
The tau neutrino interacts only via the weak nuclear force, making it extremely difficult to observe directly.

Synset Examples

When the third type of lepton, the tau, was discovered in 1975 at the Stanford Linear Accelerator Center, it too was expected to have an associated neutrino (the tau neutrino).

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: electron neutrino - the neutrino associated with the electron lepton
  • Degree Lower: muon neutrino - the neutrino associated with the muon lepton

Etymology

From Greek letter tau (for third lepton type) + 'neutrino' (Italian diminutive of 'neutro', neutral).
