Confederate rose mallow

1. NOUN

/ kənˈfɛdərət roʊz ˈmæloʊ / (AmE), / kənˈfɛdərət rəʊz ˈmæləʊ / (BrE)
A Chinese shrub or small tree (Hibiscus mutabilis) grown for its large white or pink flowers, which turn deep red by evening, commonly cultivated and naturalized in the southeastern United States.
Chinese shrub with color-changing flowers

Examples

The Confederate rose mallow is admired for its dramatic transformation from white to crimson within a single day.
Gardeners in the southern United States prize the Confederate rose mallow for its striking late-season blooms.
She planted a Confederate rose mallow in her backyard to add vibrant color to her autumn garden.

Etymology

From the common name 'Confederate rose' and 'mallow', reflecting its association with the mallow family and the American South.
