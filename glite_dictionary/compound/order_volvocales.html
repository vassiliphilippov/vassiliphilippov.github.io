order Volvocales

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdɚ ˌvɑːlvoʊˈkeɪliːz / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə ˌvɒlvəʊˈkeɪliːz / (BrE)
a taxonomic order of chiefly freshwater green algae, which may be solitary or form colonies
a group of green algae, often colonial

Examples

Many species in the order Volvocales form spherical colonies that swim together in freshwater habitats.
Research on the order Volvocales has greatly increased our understanding of multicellularity in algae.
The order Volvocales includes both solitary cells and complex colonies such as those seen in Volvox.

Etymology

From Latin 'volvo' (to roll), reflecting the rolling motion of some colonies, and modern botanical taxonomy.
