Hooker's green

1. NOUN

/ ˈhʊkərz ɡriːn / (AmE), / ˈhʊkəz ɡriːn / (BrE)
a green pigment made by mixing Prussian blue and gamboge, commonly used by artists
a green pigment made from Prussian blue and gamboge

Examples

The painter chose Hooker's green to capture the lush foliage of the landscape.
Many watercolor artists prefer Hooker's green for its vibrant yet natural hue.
She mixed a touch of Hooker's green into her palette to deepen the shadows in the trees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: viridian - a transparent, bluish-green pigment

Etymology

Named after English botanical artist William Hooker (1779–1832), who popularized its use.
