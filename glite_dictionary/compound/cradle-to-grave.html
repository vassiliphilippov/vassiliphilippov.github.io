cradle-to-grave

1. ADJ

/ ˌkreɪ.dl tə ˈɡreɪv / (AmE), / ˌkreɪ.dl tə ˈɡreɪv / (BrE)
lasting or applying throughout the entire life of a person or the full duration of something, from beginning to end.
from birth to death; throughout a whole lifespan or process

Examples

The company offers cradle-to-grave support to ensure customer satisfaction at every stage.
The new policy provides cradle-to-grave healthcare for all citizens.
A cradle-to-grave analysis helps companies assess the full environmental impact of their products.

Synset Examples

Their cradle-to-grave approach ensures no detail is overlooked.
He appreciated the cradle-to-grave care provided by the hospital.
The company offers cradle-to-grave services for its clients.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lifecycle - the series of stages through which something (such as an individual or a product) passes during its lifetime
  • Synonym: comprehensive - including all or nearly all elements or aspects of something

Etymology

Combination of 'cradle' (symbolizing birth) and 'grave' (symbolizing death), first widely used in the 20th century.
