Armenian alphabet

1. NOUN

/ ɑrˈminiən ˈæl.fə.bɛt / (AmE), / ɑːˈmiːniən ˈæl.fə.bɛt / (BrE)
the script of 38 letters used to write the Armenian language
script for the Armenian language

Examples

The ancient manuscript was written in the Armenian alphabet, which is still used today.
Students in Armenia first learn the Armenian alphabet before reading and writing in their native language.
She displayed a chart of the Armenian alphabet in her classroom to help new learners.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cyrillic alphabet - the script used for Russian and some other languages
  • Synonym: Greek alphabet - the script used for writing Greek

Etymology

Armenian from the name of Armenia, the alphabet was created in the early 5th century by Mesrop Mashtots.
