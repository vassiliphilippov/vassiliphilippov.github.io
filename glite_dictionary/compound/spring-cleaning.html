spring-cleaning

1. NOUN

/ sprɪŋ ˈkliːnɪŋ / (AmE), / sprɪŋ ˈkliːnɪŋ / (BrE)
a thorough cleaning of a house, typically carried out at the end of winter or in early spring
thorough house cleaning after winter

Examples

Every year, we dedicate one weekend in March to spring-cleaning the entire house.
She found old photographs while doing her annual spring-cleaning last April.
After a thorough spring-cleaning, the house looked bright and refreshed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cleaning - the action of making something clean

Etymology

From 'spring' (the season) + 'cleaning' (the act of cleaning), referring to the tradition of deep cleaning at the start of spring.
