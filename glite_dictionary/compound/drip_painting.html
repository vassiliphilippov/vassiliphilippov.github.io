drip painting

1. NOUN

/ drɪp ˈpeɪntɪŋ / (AmE), / drɪp ˈpeɪntɪŋ / (BrE)
a style of abstract painting in which paint is dripped or poured onto the canvas, rather than being carefully applied
painting style where paint is dripped onto the canvas

Examples

Jackson Pollock is famous for pioneering the drip painting technique in modern art.
The museum's new exhibit features several striking drip paintings from the 1940s.
She created a drip painting by carefully pouring layers of colored paint on the canvas.

Synset Examples

Pollock found drip painting to his liking; later using the technique almost exclusively, he would make use of such unconventional tools as sticks, hardened brushes and even basting syringes to create large and energetic abstract works.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: action painting - a technique of painting involving spontaneous dribbling, splashing or smearing of paint
