heat energy

1. NOUN

/ hit ˈɛnɚdʒi / (AmE), / hiːt ˈɛnədʒi / (BrE)
energy transferred from one body to another as a result of a temperature difference
energy transferred by temperature difference

Examples

The sun provides heat energy that warms the Earth's surface.
When wood burns, it releases light and heat energy.
Insulation helps reduce the loss of heat energy from your home.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thermal energy - energy possessed by an object due to the movement of its particles
