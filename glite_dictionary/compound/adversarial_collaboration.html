adversarial collaboration

1. NOUN

/ ˌæd.vɚˈsɛr.i.əl kəˌlæb.əˈreɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌæd.vəˈsɛə.ri.əl kəˌlæb.əˈreɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
a method or practice in which individuals or groups with opposing viewpoints work together to achieve a common goal or to resolve disputes objectively
cooperation between opposing parties

Examples

The research paper was the result of an adversarial collaboration between scientists with differing theories.
Through adversarial collaboration, the legal and medical teams developed a comprehensive policy.
Their adversarial collaboration produced a balanced report that addressed both sides of the debate.

Synset Examples

They engaged in adversarial collaboration to tackle the contentious issue.
Adversarial collaboration can yield innovative solutions to complex problems.
The project succeeded due to adversarial collaboration between rival teams.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: collaboration - working together to produce or create something
  • Synonym: cooperation - the process of working together for a common goal
  • Degree Lower: adversarial - involving opposition or conflict

Etymology

From 'adversarial' (relating to opponents) and 'collaboration' (working together).
