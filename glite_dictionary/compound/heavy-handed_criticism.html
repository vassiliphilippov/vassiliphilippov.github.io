heavy-handed criticism

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɛvi ˈhændɪd ˈkrɪtɪsɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌhɛvi ˈhændɪd ˈkrɪtɪsɪzəm / (BrE)
criticism that is excessively forceful or harsh, lacking subtlety or sensitivity
overly harsh criticism

Examples

Her manager's heavy-handed criticism left her feeling demoralized rather than motivated to improve.
Despite his good intentions, his heavy-handed criticism often alienates his colleagues.
The film's director was notorious for giving heavy-handed criticism during rehearsals.

Synset Examples

She found his heavy-handed criticism hard to accept.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: constructive criticism - helpful feedback intended to improve someone’s work
  • Degree Lower: gentle criticism - mild or tactful feedback
  • Degree Higher: scathing criticism - severely harsh or damaging feedback
