striated muscle

1. NOUN

/ ˈstraɪ.eɪ.tɪd ˈmʌs.əl / (AmE), / ˈstraɪ.eɪ.tɪd ˈmʌs.əl / (BrE)
A muscle tissue marked by transverse stripes, usually attached to bones and responsible for voluntary movement in the skeleton.
muscle tissue with stripes, moves skeleton
medical

Examples

The biceps is a type of striated muscle responsible for flexing the arm.
Damage to striated muscle can impair voluntary movement and strength.
Microscopic examination revealed that the tissue was clearly striated muscle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skeletal muscle - muscle attached to the skeleton and involved in voluntary movement
  • Antonym: smooth muscle - muscle tissue not under voluntary control, not striated
  • Synonym: cardiac muscle - specialized striated muscle of the heart

Etymology

From Latin 'striatus' (furrowed, striped) + 'musculus' (little mouse, muscle).
