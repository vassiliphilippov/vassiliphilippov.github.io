to shift someone's investments

1. VERB

/ ʃɪft ˈsʌmwʌnz ɪnˈvɛstmənts / (AmE), / ʃɪft ˈsʌmwʌnz ɪnˈvɛstmənts / (BrE)
to move financial assets from one type of investment to another, often to adjust risk or seek better returns
move assets from one investment to another

Examples

After considering market trends, she decided to shift her investments from stocks to bonds.
Many retirees choose to shift their investments into lower-risk funds.
Economic uncertainty may prompt some to shift their investments toward real estate.

Synset Examples

He decided to shift his investments into safer options.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to reallocate - to assign or distribute something again, especially resources or investments
