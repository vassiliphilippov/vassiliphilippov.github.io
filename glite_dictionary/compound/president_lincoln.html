President Lincoln

1. PROPN

/ ˈprɛzɪdənt ˈlɪŋkən / (AmE), / ˈprɛzɪdənt ˈlɪŋkən / (BrE)
The 16th President of the United States (1861–1865), who led the nation during the Civil War and issued the Emancipation Proclamation.
16th President of the United States, Abraham Lincoln

Examples

Many historians consider President Lincoln one of the greatest leaders in American history.
President Lincoln delivered the Gettysburg Address in 1863.
The Emancipation Proclamation was signed by President Lincoln during the Civil War.
