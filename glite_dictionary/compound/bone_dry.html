bone dry

1. ADJ

/ boʊn ˈdraɪ / (AmE), / bəʊn ˈdraɪ / (BrE)
completely dry; without any moisture at all.
completely dry; no moisture

Examples

The towels came out of the dryer bone dry and ready to be folded.
After weeks without rain, the ground was bone dry and cracked.
Make sure your hands are bone dry before handling electronic equipment.

Synset Examples

bone-dry leaves are a fire hazard
a drier to get the clothes bone dry

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parched - extremely dry, especially due to heat
  • Antonym: soaked - completely wet; saturated with liquid

Etymology

From the literal comparison to a weathered bone, which is extremely dry.
