heavy metal music

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛvi ˈmɛtəl ˈmjuːzɪk / (AmE), / ˈhɛvi ˈmɛtəl ˈmjuːzɪk / (BrE)
A style of loud and aggressive rock music characterized by amplified distortion, emphatic rhythms, and often violent or fantastical lyrics.
loud, aggressive rock music genre

Examples

He has been a fan of heavy metal music since high school.
Many people associate heavy metal music with powerful guitar riffs and energetic drumming.
The festival featured several bands known for their unique style of heavy metal music.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: rock music - a genre of popular music with a strong rhythm, developed from rock and roll

Etymology

Formed in the late 1960s and early 1970s. The term 'heavy metal' may derive from 1960s counterculture slang, influenced by the phrase in Steppenwolf's song 'Born to Be Wild'.
