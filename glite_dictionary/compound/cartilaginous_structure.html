cartilaginous structure

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑrtl̩ˈædʒənəs ˈstrʌktʃər / (AmE), / ˌkɑːtɪˈlædʒɪnəs ˈstrʌktʃə / (BrE)
an anatomical body part that is shaped or supported by cartilage rather than bone
anatomical structure made of cartilage
medical

Examples

Sharks have a cartilaginous structure instead of a bony skeleton.
The human ear is a good example of a cartilaginous structure in the body.
In embryos, many bones start as a cartilaginous structure before hardening.

Etymology

From 'cartilaginous', derived from Latin cartilago (gristle), and 'structure', from Latin structura (a building, arrangement).
