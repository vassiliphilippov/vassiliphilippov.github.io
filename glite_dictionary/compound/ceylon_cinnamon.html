Ceylon cinnamon

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈlɑn ˈsɪnəmən / (AmE), / sɪˈlɒn ˈsɪnəmɪn / (BrE)
A tropical Asian tree (Cinnamomum verum) with aromatic yellowish-brown bark, used as the source of true cinnamon.
tree producing true cinnamon

Examples

Many bakers prefer Ceylon cinnamon for its mild, complex flavor in pastries and desserts.
The bark of Ceylon cinnamon peels off in thin layers and is often more expensive than cassia.
Sri Lanka is the leading exporter of Ceylon cinnamon worldwide.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: cassia - a related species used as a cinnamon substitute
  • Synonym: cinnamon - the general spice derived from the bark of certain trees

Etymology

Ceylon, former name of Sri Lanka, where the tree is native, plus cinnamon (from Latin cinnamomum).
