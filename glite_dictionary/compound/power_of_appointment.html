power of appointment

1. NOUN

/ ˌpaʊ.ɚ əv əˈpɔɪnt.mənt / (AmE), / ˌpaʊ.ə əv əˈpɔɪnt.mənt / (BrE)
the legal authority given, usually in a will or deed, to designate who shall receive certain property or benefit
authority to designate beneficiaries of property
legal

Examples

Under the trust, she holds a power of appointment to decide which of the children receives the inheritance.
The will granted him a power of appointment over the family estate.
Exercising his power of appointment, the trustee distributed the assets among the beneficiaries.

Etymology

Legal term; 'appointment' from Old French 'apointer', from Latin 'appunctare'.
