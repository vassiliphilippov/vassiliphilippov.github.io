genus Homarus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs hoʊˈmɛrəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs həʊˈmɑːrəs / (BrE)
The genus of large edible lobsters, especially the European and American lobsters, within the family Homaridae.
genus of edible lobsters

Examples

The genus Homarus includes both the American lobster and the European lobster.
Scientists study the evolutionary differences within the genus Homarus to understand lobster diversity.
Commercial fisheries mainly target species belonging to the genus Homarus for their economic value.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lobster - large marine crustacean of the family Homaridae

Etymology

From Latin 'genus' (kind) and 'Homarus', the lobster genus name from Greek 'homaros' (lobster).
