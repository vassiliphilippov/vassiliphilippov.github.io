theory of preformation

1. NOUN

/ ˈθɪəri əv ˌpriːfɔrˈmeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈθɪəri əv ˌpriːfɔːˈmeɪʃən / (BrE)
the now-discredited scientific theory that an individual develops by the simple enlargement of a tiny fully formed organism (a homunculus) present in the germ cell
discredited theory of organism development

Examples

The theory of preformation was widely accepted before modern genetics disproved it.
According to the theory of preformation, all traits were thought to be present in miniature from conception.
Many 18th-century scientists subscribed to the theory of preformation despite a lack of empirical evidence.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: epigenesis - the theory that organisms develop from the successive differentiation of an originally undifferentiated structure

Etymology

From Latin praeformatio (a shaping beforehand); 'preformation' refers to the prior formation of the organism.
