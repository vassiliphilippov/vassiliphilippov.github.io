double shot

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʌbəl ʃɑt / (AmE), / ˈdʌb(ə)l ʃɒt / (BrE)
A serving of liquor equal to twice the standard measure, typically 100 mL, drunk from a shot glass.
twice the standard shot of liquor

Examples

She asked the bartender for a double shot of espresso to wake herself up.
He poured himself a double shot of whiskey after a long day at work.
The cocktail recipe calls for a double shot of rum and fresh lime juice.

Synset Examples

He ordered a double shot of vodka and swallowed it hastily.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: shot - a small measure of spirits or liquor served in a glass
