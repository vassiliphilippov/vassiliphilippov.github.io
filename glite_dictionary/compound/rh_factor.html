Rh factor

1. NOUN

/ ɑr eɪ ˈfæktər / (AmE), / ɑː ɪtʃ ˈfæktə / (BrE)
a protein present on the surface of red blood cells in most people, used to determine blood type compatibility
a protein determining blood type compatibility
medical

Examples

She learned during pregnancy that her Rh factor was negative.
Doctors must check a patient's Rh factor before a blood transfusion.
Differences in Rh factor can complicate childbirth if not properly managed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blood type - a classification of blood based on the presence or absence of antibodies and inherited antigenic substances
  • Synonym: ABO system - the major system of blood-group categorization

Etymology

Named after the Rhesus monkey, in which the factor was first discovered.
