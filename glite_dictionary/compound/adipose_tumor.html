adipose tumor

1. NOUN

/ ˈædɪˌpoʊs ˈtuːmər / (AmE), / ˈædɪpəʊs ˈtjuːmə / (BrE)
a tumor composed of fatty tissue, typically benign and also known as a lipoma
benign fatty tissue tumor
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed the lump as an adipose tumor after a physical examination.
Surgical removal of an adipose tumor is sometimes recommended if it causes discomfort.
An adipose tumor usually grows slowly and is not cancerous.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lipoma - a benign tumor made of fatty tissue

Etymology

From Latin adiposus (fatty) and tumor (swelling).
