Friendly Islands

1. PROPN

/ ˈfrɛndli ˈaɪləndz / (AmE), / ˈfrɛndli ˈaɪləndz / (BrE)
an alternative historical name for Tonga, a Polynesian monarchy in the South Pacific
historical name for Tonga

Examples

During his voyage, Captain Cook landed on the Friendly Islands and was welcomed by the local people.
The Friendly Islands gained independence from British rule in 1970 and are now known as Tonga.
Many 19th-century explorers wrote about their visits to the Friendly Islands in their travel journals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Tonga - a Polynesian monarchy in the South Pacific Ocean

Etymology

Named 'Friendly Islands' by Captain James Cook in 1773, due to the warm reception he received.
