white snakeroot

1. NOUN

/ waɪt ˈsneɪkˌruːt / (AmE), / waɪt ˈsneɪkˌruːt / (BrE)
A North American herbaceous plant with flat-topped clusters of small white flowers, reputed to cause trembles and milk sickness in livestock and humans if ingested.
North American plant causing milk sickness

Examples

Farmers were once wary of letting cows graze where white snakeroot grew, fearing their milk would become poisonous.
The field was filled with white snakeroot, its tiny blooms brightening the late summer landscape.
Researchers studied the toxic effects of white snakeroot on livestock in the region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Eupatorium - a genus of plants sometimes including white snakeroot
