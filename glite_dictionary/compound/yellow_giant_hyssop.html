yellow giant hyssop

1. NOUN

/ ˈjɛloʊ ˈdʒaɪənt ˈhɪsəp / (AmE), / ˈjɛləʊ ˈdʒaɪənt ˈhɪsəp / (BrE)
a tall perennial plant with sturdy stems and yellow-green flowers, native to southern Canada and the southeastern United States
a tall perennial plant with yellow-green flowers

Examples

The yellow giant hyssop is often found in prairie grasslands in the Midwest.
Gardeners appreciate the yellow giant hyssop for its attractive flowers and upright growth.
Pollinators such as bees are drawn to the yellow giant hyssop during its summer bloom.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: giant hyssop - a group of tall plants in the genus Agastache
