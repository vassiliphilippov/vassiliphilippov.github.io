civil wrong

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪvəl rɔŋ / (AmE), / ˈsɪvəl rɒŋ / (BrE)
(Law) An act or omission that causes harm or loss to another person and for which a legal action for damages may be brought in civil court.
act causing harm actionable in civil court
legal

Examples

The company was found liable for a civil wrong after polluting the river.
Defamation is considered a civil wrong and may result in a lawsuit for damages.
The court determines if the defendant has committed a civil wrong against the plaintiff.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tort - a wrongful act leading to civil legal liability
  • Antonym: crime - an act punishable by law, usually considered an offense against the state
