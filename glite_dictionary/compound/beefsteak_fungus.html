beefsteak fungus

1. NOUN

/ ˈbifˌsteɪk ˈfʌŋɡəs / (AmE), / ˈbiːfsteɪk ˈfʌŋɡəs / (BrE)
a type of edible fungus with a reddish, meat-like cap, commonly found in some regions and known for its resemblance to raw beef
a reddish edible fungus resembling raw meat

Examples

We found a large beefsteak fungus growing on the old oak tree in the woods.
The chef prepared a vegetarian dish using slices of beefsteak fungus as a meat substitute.
Because of its texture and flavor, many foragers prize the beefsteak fungus during the late summer months.

Etymology

Named for its resemblance in color and texture to raw beefsteak.
