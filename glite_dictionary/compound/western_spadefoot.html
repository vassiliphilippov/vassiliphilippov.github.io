western spadefoot

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɛstərn ˈspeɪdˌfʊt / (AmE), / ˈwɛstən ˈspeɪdfʊt / (BrE)
a species of spadefoot toad (Spea hammondii) native to California, known for its burrowing habits and distinctive spade-like projections on its feet
species of spadefoot toad native to California

Examples

The western spadefoot is rarely seen above ground except during rainy seasons.
After heavy rainfall, western spadefoots emerge in large numbers to breed in temporary pools.
Conservationists are working to protect the western spadefoot from habitat loss.
