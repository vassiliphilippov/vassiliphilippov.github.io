sublingual salivary gland

1. NOUN

/ sʌbˈlɪŋɡwəl ˈsæləˌvɛri ɡlænd / (AmE), / sʌbˈlɪŋɡwəl ˈsælɪvəri ɡlænd / (BrE)
A small salivary gland located under the tongue on each side of the mouth, which produces mucin, a component of saliva.
salivary gland under the tongue
medical

Examples

The sublingual salivary gland helps keep the mouth moist by secreting mucus-rich saliva.
Surgery near the tongue may risk damaging the sublingual salivary gland.
Enlargement of a sublingual salivary gland can cause discomfort or swelling under the tongue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parotid gland - largest salivary gland, located near the ear
  • Synonym: submandibular gland - salivary gland located beneath the lower jaw

Etymology

From Latin sub- (under) + lingua (tongue) + gland (gland).
