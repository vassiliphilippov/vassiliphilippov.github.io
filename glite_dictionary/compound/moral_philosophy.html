moral philosophy

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɔrəl fəˈlɑsəfi / (AmE), / ˈmɒrəl fəˈlɒsəfi / (BrE)
the branch of philosophy concerned with the study of moral values, principles, and rules of right and wrong conduct
study of moral values and rules

Examples

She decided to major in moral philosophy at university because she was interested in questions of right and wrong.
The debate drew on concepts from moral philosophy to explore the nature of justice and fairness.
Many classic texts in moral philosophy address the conflict between individual interests and societal good.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ethics - the philosophical study of moral principles

Etymology

From Latin 'moralis' (moral) + Greek 'philosophia' (love of wisdom).
