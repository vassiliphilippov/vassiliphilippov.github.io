genus Phallus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈfæl.əs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈfal.əs / (BrE)
A genus of fungi characterized by a cap or pileus that hangs free around the stem, including several well-known stinkhorn species.
genus of fungi with hanging cap

Examples

The genus Phallus includes several species commonly known as stinkhorns.
Botanists discovered a rare member of the genus Phallus in the damp woodland.
Many species in the genus Phallus emit a strong odor to attract insects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stinkhorn - a foul-smelling fungus in the same family as genus Phallus

Etymology

From Latin 'phallus', referring to the phallic shape of the fruiting bodies.
