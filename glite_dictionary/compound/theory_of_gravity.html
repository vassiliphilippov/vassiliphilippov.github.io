theory of gravity

1. NOUN

/ ˈθiəri əv ˈɡrævəti / (AmE), / ˈθɪəri əv ˈɡravɪti / (BrE)
the scientific theory that any two objects in the universe attract each other with a force proportional to their masses and inversely proportional to the square of the distance between them
scientific theory of universal attraction between masses

Examples

Isaac Newton formulated the theory of gravity after observing an apple fall from a tree.
The theory of gravity explains why planets orbit the Sun in predictable paths.
Scientists continue to test and refine the theory of gravity with new astronomical observations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gravitation - the force by which objects attract each other
  • Synonym: law of universal gravitation - Newton's formulation of gravity's force between masses
