to jade someone's perspective

1. VERB

/ dʒeɪd ˈsʌmwʌnz pərˈspɛktɪv / (AmE), / dʒeɪd ˈsʌmwənz pəˈspɛktɪv / (BrE)
to make someone cynical, weary, or less open-minded due to experience or overexposure
make someone cynical or weary

Examples

Years of bitter negotiations eventually jaded his perspective on international cooperation.
Constant disappointment in the workplace can jade your perspective over time.
It's important not to let negative experiences jade your perspective on future opportunities.

Synset Examples

Traveling can jade your perspective if you're not careful.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cynicize - to make someone cynical
  • Antonym: invigorate someone's perspective - to make someone more energetic or open-minded

Etymology

From the verb 'jade', meaning to tire or weary, from earlier meaning 'to wear out' (possibly from obsolete jade 'a worn-out horse').
