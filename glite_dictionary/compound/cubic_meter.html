cubic meter

1. NOUN

/ ˈkjuːbɪk ˈmiːtər / (AmE), / ˈkjuːbɪk ˈmiːtə / (BrE)
a metric unit of volume equal to 1,000 liters, used for measuring large amounts of liquid or space
metric unit of volume (1,000 liters)

Examples

The pool holds nearly fifty cubic meters of water when it is full.
A shipping container might have a capacity of thirty cubic meters.
One cubic meter of concrete weighs more than two tons.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: liter - metric unit of volume equal to 1,000 cubic centimeters
  • Degree Lower: cubic centimeter - metric unit of volume equal to one millionth of a cubic meter

Etymology

From Latin "cubus" (cube) and Greek "metron" (measure), reflecting measurement of space in cubes of 1 meter per side.
