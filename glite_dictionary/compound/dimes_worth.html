dime's worth

1. NOUN

/ ˈdaɪmz wɜrθ / (AmE), / ˈdaɪmz wɜːθ / (BrE)
An amount or value regarded as very small or insignificant.
very small amount or value
american

Examples

He didn't care a dime's worth about the outcome of the game.
Her explanation wasn't worth a dime's worth of my time.
You won't get a dime's worth of information from that report.

Synset Examples

I wouldn't give a dime's worth for that opinion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pittance - a very small or inadequate amount of money
  • Antonym: fortune - a large amount of money or value

Etymology

From 'dime', an American coin worth ten cents, used to indicate a small sum.
