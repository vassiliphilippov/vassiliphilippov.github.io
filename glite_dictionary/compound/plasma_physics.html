plasma physics

1. NOUN

/ ˈplæz.mə ˈfɪz.ɪks / (AmE), / ˈplæz.mə ˈfɪz.ɪks / (BrE)
the branch of physics that studies the properties and behavior of matter in the plasma state
study of matter in the plasma state

Examples

Many scientists are attracted to plasma physics because of its importance in fusion energy research.
Universities often offer advanced courses in plasma physics at the graduate level.
New discoveries in plasma physics have improved our understanding of both space and laboratory plasmas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plasma - ionized gas consisting of positive ions and free electrons

Etymology

From 'plasma', coined in the late 19th century from Greek 'plasma' (something molded), and 'physics', from Greek 'physis' (nature).
