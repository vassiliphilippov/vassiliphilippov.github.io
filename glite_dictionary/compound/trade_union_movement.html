trade union movement

1. NOUN

/ treɪd ˈjuːniən ˈmuːvmənt / (AmE), / treɪd ˈjuːniən ˈmuːvmənt / (BrE)
An organized effort by workers, often through labor unions, to improve their working conditions, rights, and status through collective action.
organized workers' movement for collective rights

Examples

The trade union movement played a key role in securing better wages for workers in the 20th century.
There has been a renewed interest in the trade union movement among young employees recently.
Leaders of the trade union movement met with government officials to discuss labor reforms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: labor movement - movement by workers to improve rights and conditions
