oral cavity

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrəl ˈkævəti / (AmE), / ˈɔːrəl ˈkævɪti / (BrE)
the opening in the mouth bounded by the lips, within which food is taken in and speech sounds are formed
the mouth opening (anatomical term)
medical

Examples

The dentist examined the patient's oral cavity for any signs of infection.
Proper hygiene of the oral cavity is essential for preventing dental diseases.
The surgeon noted redness and swelling inside the oral cavity.

Synset Examples

he stuffed his mouth with candy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mouth - the opening in the face used for eating and speaking

Etymology

From Latin 'os' (mouth) and 'cavitas' (cavity).
