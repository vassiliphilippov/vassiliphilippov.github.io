Benjamin Franklin Bridge

1. PROPN

/ ˌbɛndʒəmɪn ˈfræŋklɪn brɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌbɛndʒəmɪn ˈfræŋklɪn brɪdʒ / (BrE)
a suspension bridge spanning the Delaware River between Philadelphia, Pennsylvania, and Camden, New Jersey.
suspension bridge over Delaware River, USA

Examples

The Benjamin Franklin Bridge offers a stunning view of the Philadelphia skyline.
Traffic was heavy on the Benjamin Franklin Bridge during the holiday weekend.
Cyclists often use the dedicated path on the Benjamin Franklin Bridge to cross the river.

Etymology

Named after Benjamin Franklin (1706–1790), American statesman and inventor.
