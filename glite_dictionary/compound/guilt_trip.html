guilt trip

1. NOUN

/ ɡɪlt trɪp / (AmE), / ɡɪlt trɪp / (BrE)
a feeling of guilt or remorse, especially one induced deliberately by someone else to influence another person's behavior
induced feeling of guilt or remorse

Examples

Her mother laid a guilt trip on her for not calling more often.
Don't let him put a guilt trip on you about missing the meeting.
She went on a guilt trip after forgetting her friend's birthday.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: remorse - deep regret or guilt for a wrong committed
  • Synonym: shame - a painful feeling caused by consciousness of guilt or shortcoming
