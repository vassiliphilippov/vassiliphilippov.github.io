lepidopterous insect

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɛpɪˈdɑptərəs ˈɪnsɛkt / (AmE), / ˌlɛpɪˈdɒptərəs ˈɪnsɛkt / (BrE)
an insect belonging to the order Lepidoptera, characterized by two pairs of wings covered with tiny scales, such as butterflies and moths
insect with scaly wings (butterflies, moths)

Examples

The museum's collection includes many rare lepidopterous insects from tropical regions.
A lepidopterous insect can be easily identified by the fine scales that cover its wings.
Researchers are studying how climate change affects the distribution of lepidopterous insects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: butterfly - an insect with brightly colored wings, belonging to Lepidoptera
  • Synonym: moth - an insect with feathery or stout bodies, mostly nocturnal, belonging to Lepidoptera

Etymology

From Greek 'lepidos' (scale) + 'pteron' (wing) via scientific Latin 'Lepidoptera'.
