lobate foot

1. NOUN

/ ˈloʊ.beɪt fʊt / (AmE), / ˈləʊ.beɪt fʊt / (BrE)
A bird's foot in which the individual toes have membranous flaps or lobes along their sides, aiding in swimming.
bird's foot with flaps on toes

Examples

The grebe's lobate foot allows it to swim efficiently in water.
Unlike webbed feet, a lobate foot has lobes on each toe instead of connecting membranes.
Some aquatic birds evolved a lobate foot to help them maneuver through dense vegetation.

Etymology

From Latin 'lobatus' (having lobes) and 'foot'.
