isometric process

1. NOUN

/ aɪ.soʊˈmɛ.trɪk ˈprɑː.sɛs / (AmE), / aɪ.səˈmɛ.trɪk ˈprəʊ.sɛs / (BrE)
a thermodynamic process in which the volume of the system remains constant while other properties, such as pressure or temperature, may change.
thermodynamic process with constant volume

Examples

In an isometric process, the volume of the gas does not change despite variations in pressure.
During the experiment, the scientist observed an isometric process by sealing the system at a fixed volume.
Heating a gas in a rigid container is a classic example of an isometric process.

Synset Examples

In practice, a gas that receives heat in an isochoric process is a constant volume heating and at the same time it is a constant volume pressurization; a gas that looses heat in an isochoric process is a constant volume cooling and at the same time it is a constant volume depressurization.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: isochoric process - thermodynamic process at constant volume

Etymology

From Greek 'isos' (equal) + 'metron' (measure) and Latin 'processus' (going forward, process).
