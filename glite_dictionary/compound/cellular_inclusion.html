cellular inclusion

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛljələr ɪnˈkluʒən / (AmE), / ˈsɛljʊlə ɪnˈkluːʒən / (BrE)
a small body or structure found within the cytoplasm of a cell, often associated with certain diseases or cellular processes
small intracellular body within a cell
medical

Examples

The pathologist identified several cellular inclusions in the liver tissue sample.
Some viral infections are diagnosed by the presence of distinct cellular inclusions in the affected cells.
Under the microscope, the cellular inclusion was visible as a dark spot inside the neuron.

Synset Examples

an inclusion in the cytoplasm of the cell

Etymology

From 'cellular' (relating to cells) and Latin 'inclusio' (enclosure, inclusion).
