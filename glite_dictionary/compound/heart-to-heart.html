heart-to-heart

1. ADJ

/ ˌhɑrt tə ˈhɑrt / (AmE), / ˌhɑːt tə ˈhɑːt / (BrE)
(of a conversation) characterized by openness and sincerity; frank and intimate.
open, sincere conversation

Examples

They had a heart-to-heart conversation about their future together.
Sometimes you just need a heart-to-heart with a close friend.
After weeks of tension, they finally sat down for a heart-to-heart.

Synset Examples

his candid eyes
an open and trusting nature
a heart-to-heart talk

Related Terms

  • Synonym: candid - truthful and straightforward, frank
  • Synonym: frank - open, honest, and direct in speech or writing
  • Antonym: guarded - careful not to give too much information or show feelings

Etymology

From the metaphorical use of 'heart' to mean the center of emotion and feeling.

2. NOUN

/ ˌhɑrt tə ˈhɑrt / (AmE), / ˌhɑːt tə ˈhɑːt / (BrE)
a serious and intimate conversation held in private, typically to discuss personal feelings or problems
private intimate conversation

Examples

They decided to have a heart-to-heart about their relationship problems.
After the argument, she felt they needed a heart-to-heart to clear the air.
A late-night heart-to-heart brought them closer together.

Synset Examples

he took me aside for a little heart-to-heart

Related Terms

  • Synonym: confession - the act of admitting or discussing something personal with someone
  • Degree Lower: chat - an informal conversation
  • Degree Higher: confrontation - a direct and sometimes tense discussion about feelings or problems

Etymology

From 'heart' (the seat of emotions) and 'to heart', expressing direct openness.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07151023-n
Synset POS: n
