church property

1. NOUN

/ tʃɝːtʃ ˈprɑːpɚti / (AmE), / tʃɜːtʃ ˈprɒpəti / (BrE)
land, buildings, or assets owned by a church, including property used for religious and related purposes
property owned by a church

Examples

The city council debated whether the old church property could be converted into a community center.
After the merger, all church property was reassessed for insurance purposes.
Donations helped the parish expand its church property to build a new hall.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: church land - land owned specifically by a church
