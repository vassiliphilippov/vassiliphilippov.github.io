liposomal delivery vector

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɪpəˈsoʊməl dɪˈlɪvəri ˈvɛktɚ / (AmE), / ˌlɪpəˈsəʊməl dɪˈlɪvəri ˈvɛktə / (BrE)
a method of transferring genetic material into cells using liposomes as carriers
biotech gene transfer using liposomes
medical

Examples

The liposomal delivery vector efficiently introduced the therapeutic gene into target cells.
Researchers opted for a liposomal delivery vector to minimize immune response during gene therapy.
Recent advances have improved the stability of the liposomal delivery vector in vivo.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: viral vector - a vehicle for gene delivery using viruses

Etymology

From 'liposome', coined from Greek 'lipos' (fat) + 'soma' (body), and 'vector' from Latin 'vehere' (to carry).
