grey body

1. NOUN

/ ɡreɪ ˈbɑdi / (AmE), / ɡreɪ ˈbɒdi / (BrE)
a body that emits thermal radiation at every wavelength in constant proportion to the emission of a black body at the same temperature, but with an emissivity less than one
object emitting radiation proportionally less than a black body

Examples

A grey body does not emit as much energy as a black body at the same temperature.
Scientists model planetary surfaces as a grey body when analyzing heat emission.
Unlike a perfect emitter, a grey body's emissivity remains constant across all wavelengths.

Synset Examples

A source with lower emissivity independent of frequency often is referred to as a gray body.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: black body - a theoretical object that absorbs all incident electromagnetic radiation and emits the maximum possible radiation at each wavelength
