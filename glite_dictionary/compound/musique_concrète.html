musique concrète

1. NOUN

/ muˌzik kɑnˈkrɛt / (AmE), / myˌziːk kɒŋˈkrɛt / (BrE)
A type of music composition where recorded natural and artificial sounds are arranged and manipulated electronically to create a musical work.
composition using recorded, arranged sounds

Examples

The composer specialized in musique concrète, blending city noises with traditional instruments.
Some of the earliest pieces of musique concrète were created in Paris studios in the 1940s.
He was fascinated by musique concrète after hearing a composition built from recorded train sounds.

Synset Examples

title Composition Concrete refers in part to the musique concrete of the French-American composer Edgar Varese, who taped sounds and then edited and reorganized them, and in part to Davis's own style.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: electroacoustic music - music involving electronic sound production or manipulation

Etymology

French, meaning 'concrete music', coined by Pierre Schaeffer in the 1940s.
