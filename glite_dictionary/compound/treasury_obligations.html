Treasury obligations

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrɛʒəri ˌɑbləˈɡeɪʃənz / (AmE), / ˈtrɛʒəri ˌɒblɪˈɡeɪʃənz / (BrE)
Negotiable debt securities issued by the United States government, guaranteeing timely payment of interest and principal.
U.S. government debt securities

Examples

Investors often choose Treasury obligations for the safety and stability they provide.
The central bank increased its purchases of Treasury obligations to support the bond market.
Many retirement portfolios include Treasury obligations to reduce overall risk.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Treasury bonds - long-term fixed-interest U.S. government debt securities
  • Synonym: Treasury bills - short-term U.S. government debt securities
  • Synonym: Treasury notes - medium-term U.S. government debt securities
