Old Irish

1. NOUN

/ oʊld ˈaɪrɪʃ / (AmE), / əʊld ˈaɪərɪʃ / (BrE)
the earliest form of the Irish Gaelic language, spoken and written in Ireland and parts of Scotland from around the 6th to the 12th centuries
early form of Irish Gaelic (c. 600–1100)

Examples

Many ancient manuscripts are written in Old Irish.
Scholars study Old Irish to understand the origins of Celtic languages.
Learning Old Irish is important for medieval Irish literature research.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Middle Irish - the stage of the Irish language following Old Irish, spoken from the 10th to the 12th century
