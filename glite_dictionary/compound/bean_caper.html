bean caper

1. NOUN

/ bin ˈkeɪ.pɚ / (AmE), / biːn ˈkeɪ.pə / (BrE)
a perennial shrub of the eastern Mediterranean and southwestern Asia, whose flower buds are used as capers in cooking
Mediterranean shrub with edible buds (capers)

Examples

The bean caper is commonly found in Mediterranean gardens for its culinary uses.
Pickled bean caper buds add a tangy flavor to many dishes.
She planted a bean caper in her backyard to harvest fresh capers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caper - the pickled flower bud of the caper bush, used as a seasoning or garnish
