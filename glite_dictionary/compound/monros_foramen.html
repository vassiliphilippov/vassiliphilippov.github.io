Monro's foramen

1. NOUN

/ mənˈroʊz fəˈreɪmən / (AmE), / mənˈrəʊz fəˈreɪmən / (BrE)
the small opening that connects each lateral ventricle of the brain with the third ventricle, allowing the passage of cerebrospinal fluid
opening connecting brain ventricles
medical

Examples

The surgeon carefully identified the Monro's foramen during the ventricular procedure.
Blockage of the Monro's foramen can result in hydrocephalus due to impaired fluid flow.
MRI scans revealed a lesion near the patient's Monro's foramen.

Etymology

Named after Scottish anatomist Alexander Monro (1733–1817), who described its function.
