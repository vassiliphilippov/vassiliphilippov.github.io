puritan ethic

1. NOUN

/ ˈpjʊr.ɪ.tən ˈɛθ.ɪk / (AmE), / ˈpjʊə.rɪ.tən ˈɛθ.ɪk / (BrE)
the strict moral code and self-discipline associated with the beliefs and practices of the Puritans, often emphasizing hard work and avoidance of pleasure
strict moral code linked to Puritanism

Examples

The town was shaped by the puritan ethic of its early settlers, who forbade dancing and drinking.
Her parents instilled a puritan ethic in her, expecting honesty and hard work above all else.
Critics argue that the puritan ethic can lead to an overly restrictive society.

Synset Examples

The community's puritan ethics shaped its strict rules.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: work ethic - belief in the moral value of work and diligence

Etymology

From Puritan (a member of a 16th–17th century English Protestant group) + ethic (moral principles).
