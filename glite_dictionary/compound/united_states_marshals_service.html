United States Marshals Service

1. PROPN

/ ˌjuːnaɪtɪd ˈsteɪts ˈmɑrʃəlz ˈsɜrvɪs / (AmE), / ˌjuːnaɪtɪd ˈsteɪts ˈmɑːʃəlz ˈsɜːvɪs / (BrE)
the federal law enforcement agency in the United States responsible for protecting the federal judiciary, transporting federal prisoners, protecting federal witnesses, and managing seized criminal assets
U.S. federal law enforcement agency
american

Examples

After his testimony, the United States Marshals Service provided round-the-clock protection to the witness.
The United States Marshals Service is responsible for apprehending fugitives and managing assets seized from criminals.
A career with the United States Marshals Service requires rigorous training in federal law enforcement.
