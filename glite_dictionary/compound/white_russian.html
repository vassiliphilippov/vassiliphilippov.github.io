White Russian

1. NOUN

/ waɪt ˈrʌʃən / (AmE), / waɪt ˈrʌʃən / (BrE)
an old term for the Belarusian language, a Slavic language spoken in Belarus
old term for Belarusian language

Examples

The traditional poetry was recited in White Russian at the festival.
He studied White Russian to better understand historical documents from Belarus.
The book was originally written in White Russian before being translated into English.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Belarusian - the official Slavic language spoken in Belarus

Etymology

From historical use of 'White Russia' to refer to Belarus; 'Russian' here means 'of Russia' in a broad regional sense.

2. NOUN

/ ˌwaɪt ˈrʌʃən / (AmE), / ˌwaɪt ˈrʌʃən / (BrE)
a cocktail consisting of vodka, coffee liqueur, and milk or cream, usually served over ice
a cocktail with vodka, coffee liqueur, and cream

Examples

He ordered a White Russian at the bar and sipped it slowly.
The recipe for a classic White Russian calls for vodka, Kahlúa, and cream.
She brought a tray of White Russians to the guests during the party.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Black Russian - a cocktail made with vodka and coffee liqueur, but without cream
