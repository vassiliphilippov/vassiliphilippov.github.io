lesser galangal

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛsər ɡəˈlæŋɡəl / (AmE), / ˈlɛsə ɡəˈlæŋɡəl / (BrE)
A Chinese perennial plant (Alpinia officinarum) with white flowers and aromatic, pungent roots used in medicine and as a flavoring.
aromatic root plant used in cooking and medicine

Examples

Traditional Chinese medicine often prescribes lesser galangal for digestive issues.
The chef added a slice of lesser galangal to enhance the fragrance of the soup.
Dried lesser galangal roots are available in many Asian grocery stores.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: greater galangal - a related tropical plant with larger rhizomes, also used as spice and medicine

Etymology

'Galangal' from Arabic *khalanjan*, from Chinese *gao liang jiang*; 'lesser' distinguishes it from 'greater galangal'.
