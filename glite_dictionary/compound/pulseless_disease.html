pulseless disease

1. NOUN

/ ˈpʌl.sləs dɪˈziz / (AmE), / ˈpʌl.sləs dɪˈziːz / (BrE)
a rare inflammatory disease of the large arteries, particularly the aorta and its branches, resulting in the absence of a detectable pulse in the arms or carotid arteries
arterial disease causing absent arm pulse
medical

Examples

The doctor suspected pulseless disease due to the absence of pulses in her arms.
Patients with pulseless disease may experience fatigue and dizziness from poor blood circulation.
A diagnosis of pulseless disease requires careful vascular examination and imaging studies.
