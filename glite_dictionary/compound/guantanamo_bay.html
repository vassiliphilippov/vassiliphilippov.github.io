Guantanamo Bay

1. PROPN

/ ɡwɑnˌtɑːnəˌmoʊ beɪ / (AmE), / ɡwænˌtænəməʊ beɪ / (BrE)
an inlet of the Caribbean Sea in southeastern Cuba, noted for the United States naval base established there in 1903
inlet in Cuba, site of US naval base

Examples

The detention center at Guantanamo Bay has been the subject of intense debate.
Many politicians have called for the closure of Guantanamo Bay.
She studied the history of the US naval base at Guantanamo Bay for her thesis.

Etymology

From Spanish Guantánamo, the name of the region in Cuba.
