fish filet

1. NOUN

/ fɪʃ fɪˈleɪ / (AmE), / fɪʃ ˈfɪlɪt / (BrE)
a boneless slice or side of a fish, usually cut lengthwise for cooking or serving
boneless slice or side of a fish

Examples

She ordered a grilled fish filet with lemon sauce at the restaurant.
The recipe calls for a fresh fish filet, seasoned with herbs and olive oil.
Carefully remove the bones to ensure a clean fish filet for cooking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fillet - a boneless strip of meat or fish

Etymology

Filet from French 'filet' meaning 'thread' or 'strip', used in English for a boneless slice of meat or fish.
