nonrapid eye movement

1. NOUN

/ ˌnɑnˌræpɪd ˈaɪ ˌmuːvmənt / (AmE), / ˌnɒnˌræpɪd ˈaɪ ˌmuːvmənt / (BrE)
the part of sleep during which rapid eye movements and dreaming do not occur, comprising the majority of typical sleep
stage of sleep without rapid eye movements
medical

Examples

Sleep researchers closely monitor nonrapid eye movement phases to study sleep disorders.
Most of your night's rest is spent in nonrapid eye movement sleep rather than dreaming.
Infants experience longer periods of nonrapid eye movement compared to adults.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: rapid eye movement - stage of sleep characterized by quick eye movements and dreaming
