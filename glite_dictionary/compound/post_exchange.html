post exchange

1. NOUN

/ poʊst ɪksˈtʃeɪndʒ / (AmE), / pəʊst ɪksˈtʃeɪndʒ / (BrE)
a store or commissary on a United States Army post where soldiers and their families can buy goods
Army base commissary/store
american

Examples

After training, the soldiers stopped by the post exchange to buy snacks and toiletries.
The post exchange offers a variety of goods at discounted prices to military members.
Families living on the base often depend on the post exchange for everyday necessities.

Etymology

From 'post', meaning military base, and 'exchange', from the store's function as a retail exchange for goods.
