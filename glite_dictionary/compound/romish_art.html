Romish art

1. NOUN

/ ˈroʊmɪʃ ɑːrt / (AmE), / ˈrəʊmɪʃ ɑːt / (BrE)
art associated with or characteristic of Roman Catholic traditions and style, especially in its historical context
art in the Roman Catholic style

Examples

The influence of Romish art can be seen in the ornate decorations of this cathedral.
During the Reformation, some critics derided certain works as examples of Romish art.
She studied the use of gold and rich colors typical of Romish art in the Baroque period.

Synset Examples

I prefer Romish art for its vibrant colors.

Etymology

From 'Romish', meaning 'of or relating to Rome or Roman Catholicism', with art referring to creative works.
