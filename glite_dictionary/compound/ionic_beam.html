ionic beam

1. NOUN

/ aɪˈɑnɪk bim / (AmE), / aɪˈɒnɪk biːm / (BrE)
a stream of ions that move in the same direction and at the same speed, typically produced for scientific or industrial purposes
stream of ions moving together

Examples

The laboratory uses an ionic beam to modify the surface of materials with precision.
By focusing an ionic beam, researchers can implant atoms into a semiconductor substrate.
The technician adjusted the intensity of the ionic beam to achieve the desired results.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ion beam - a beam composed of ions used in scientific equipment

Etymology

From 'ionic', relating to ions (charged particles), and 'beam', meaning a directed stream or ray.
