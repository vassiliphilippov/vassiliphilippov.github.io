vitamin B12

1. NOUN

/ ˈvaɪtəmɪn ˌbi ˈtwɛlv / (AmE), / ˈvɪtəmɪn ˌbi ˈtwɛlv / (BrE)
a B vitamin essential for the production of red blood cells and the maintenance of the nervous system, used to treat pernicious anemia
essential B vitamin, treats anemia
medical

Examples

A deficiency of vitamin B12 can lead to serious health problems.
Doctors often prescribe vitamin B12 supplements for patients with pernicious anemia.
Fortified cereals are a common source of vitamin B12 for those following a vegan diet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cobalamin - another name for vitamin B12
  • Degree Lower: vitamin B6 - another B vitamin

Etymology

From 'vitamin' plus 'B12', indicating its classification as one of the B group vitamins.
