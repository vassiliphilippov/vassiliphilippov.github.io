Royal Academy

1. PROPN

/ ˈrɔɪəl əˈkædəmi / (AmE), / ˈrɔɪəl əˈkadəmi / (BrE)
An honorary institution in London, founded in 1768, dedicated to the promotion of the arts of painting, sculpture, and architecture in Britain.
British arts institution in London
british

Examples

The annual exhibition at the Royal Academy attracts artists from across the country.
She was recently elected a member of the Royal Academy for her exceptional sculptures.
The Royal Academy has played a pivotal role in the development of British art.
