milk thistle

1. NOUN

/ mɪlk ˈθɪsəl / (AmE), / mɪlk ˈθɪsəl / (BrE)
a tall thistle native to southern Europe and Asia, having white-blotched leaves and purple flower heads, often used in herbal medicine
a tall white-blotched thistle plant

Examples

Milk thistle is often used in supplements believed to support liver health.
The leaves of the milk thistle are easily recognized by their white blotches.
Researchers studied the effects of milk thistle extract on liver cells.

2. NOUN

/ mɪlk ˈθɪsəl / (AmE), / mɪlk ˈθɪsl / (BrE)
a prickly-leaved plant of the daisy family with milky sap, often considered a weed; especially Silybum marianum, sometimes used in herbal remedies
a prickly-leaved plant with milky sap

Examples

Milk thistle is often taken as a supplement to support liver health.
Farmers struggle to control the spread of milk thistle in their fields.
The sharp leaves of milk thistle make it easy to identify among other weeds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thistle - a plant with prickly leaves and purple flowers
