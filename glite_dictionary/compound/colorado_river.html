Colorado River

1. PROPN

/ ˌkɑːləˈrædoʊ ˈrɪvər / (AmE), / ˌkɒləˈrædəʊ ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
a river in central Texas, United States, that flows southeast into the Gulf of Mexico
river in Texas, US

Examples

The Colorado River in Texas supplies water to many cities and farms.
Flooding along the Colorado River is a concern during heavy rainstorms.
Tourists enjoy fishing and boating on the Colorado River near Austin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Colorado River - major river in the southwestern United States and northern Mexico

Etymology

Named from the Spanish word 'colorado', meaning 'colored red', referring to the reddish silt of the river.
