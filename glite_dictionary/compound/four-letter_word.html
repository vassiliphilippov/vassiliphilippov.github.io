four-letter word

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔr ˌlɛt̬ɚ wɝːd / (AmE), / ˈfɔː ˌlɛt.ə wɜːd / (BrE)
A word that is considered obscene or offensive, especially one with four letters, used as a euphemism for such words.
obscene or offensive word (euphemism)

Examples

Don't use any four-letter words in front of the children, please.
He let out a string of four-letter words when he stubbed his toe.
That movie is full of four-letter words and adult content.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swear word - a word that is offensive or used to express strong emotion
  • Synonym: expletive - an oath or swear word

Etymology

Refers to the fact that many common English obscenities are four letters long.
