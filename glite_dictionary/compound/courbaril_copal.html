courbaril copal

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɔrbəˈril koʊˈpæl / (AmE), / ˌkɔːbəˈriːl kəʊˈpal / (BrE)
a resin obtained from the courbaril tree, used in varnishes and adhesives
resin from the courbaril tree

Examples

The craftsman used courbaril copal to give the wooden sculpture a glossy finish.
Traditional varnishes often include natural ingredients, such as courbaril copal.
Collectors prize certain pieces of jewelry made with fossilized courbaril copal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: copal - a hard resin obtained from tropical trees and used in varnishes

Etymology

From 'courbaril', the common name for the tree genus Hymenaea, and 'copal', a term for tree resins, from Spanish 'copal', itself from Nahuatl 'copalli' (incense/resin).
