cardiopulmonary exercise

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːrdioʊˈpʌlməˌnɛri ˈɛksərsaɪz / (AmE), / ˌkɑːdiəʊˈpʌlmənəri ˈɛksəsaɪz / (BrE)
physical exercise intended to strengthen or assess the heart and lungs, and improve circulatory and respiratory function
exercise to improve heart and lung function
medical

Examples

Cardiologists often recommend cardiopulmonary exercise to patients recovering from heart surgery.
A treadmill test is a form of cardiopulmonary exercise used to assess heart and lung function.
Regular cardiopulmonary exercise can help reduce the risk of cardiovascular disease.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aerobic exercise - exercise that increases oxygen consumption and improves cardiovascular health

Etymology

From cardio- (heart) + pulmonary (relating to the lungs) + exercise.
