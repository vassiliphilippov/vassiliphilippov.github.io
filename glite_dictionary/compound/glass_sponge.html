glass sponge

1. NOUN

/ ɡlæs spʌndʒ / (AmE), / ɡlɑːs spʌndʒ / (BrE)
a type of marine sponge having a skeleton made of siliceous, glass-like spicules, belonging to the class Hyalospongiae
marine sponge with glassy skeleton

Examples

The intricate structure of the glass sponge makes it a subject of fascination for marine biologists.
During the expedition, several rare glass sponges were discovered on the ocean floor.
Scientists study the silica composition of the glass sponge to learn about deep-sea ecosystems.
