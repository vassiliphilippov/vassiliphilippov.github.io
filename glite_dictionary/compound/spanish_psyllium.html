Spanish psyllium

1. NOUN

/ ˈspænɪʃ ˈsɪliəm / (AmE), / ˈspænɪʃ ˈsɪliəm / (BrE)
a plantain native to Mediterranean regions, whose seeds swell in moisture and are used as a mild laxative
Mediterranean plant used as a laxative
medical

Examples

Doctors often recommend Spanish psyllium to patients who suffer from constipation.
The seeds of Spanish psyllium expand when soaked, aiding digestive health naturally.
Many herbal supplements use Spanish psyllium as a key ingredient for gentle laxative effects.

Related Terms
