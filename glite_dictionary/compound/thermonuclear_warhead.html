thermonuclear warhead

1. NOUN

/ ˌθɝr.moʊˌnu.kli.ɚ ˈwɔrˌhɛd / (AmE), / ˌθɜː.məʊˌnjuː.kli.ə ˈwɔːˌhɛd / (BrE)
a type of warhead, usually mounted on a missile, designed to deliver the explosive power of a thermonuclear (hydrogen) bomb
warhead delivering a thermonuclear bomb

Examples

The missile was equipped with a thermonuclear warhead capable of devastating an entire city.
Scientists and military officials debated the safety features of the thermonuclear warhead design.
During the Cold War, both superpowers stockpiled thousands of thermonuclear warheads.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: nuclear warhead - a warhead containing a nuclear explosive device
  • Degree Lower: conventional warhead - a warhead containing non-nuclear explosives

Etymology

Formed from 'thermonuclear', meaning 'relating to nuclear reactions at high temperatures', and 'warhead'.
