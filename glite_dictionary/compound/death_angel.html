death angel

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛθ ˌeɪn.dʒəl / (AmE), / ˈdɛθ ˌeɪn.dʒəl / (BrE)
an extremely poisonous white fungus (Amanita species), distinguished by a prominent cup-shaped base and white gills, often fatal if ingested
extremely poisonous white mushroom

Examples

Many mushroom foragers are trained to recognize the death angel to avoid accidental poisoning.
The death angel looks deceptively similar to harmless edible mushrooms.
Consuming even a small amount of the death angel can be fatal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: destroying angel - another common name for several deadly Amanita species
  • Synonym: Amanita - genus of poisonous mushrooms
  • Antonym: edible Agaricus - an edible genus of mushrooms differing by gill color
