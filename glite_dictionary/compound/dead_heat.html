dead heat

1. NOUN

/ dɛd hiːt / (AmE), / dɛd hiːt / (BrE)
a situation in a race or competition where two or more competitors finish exactly even and cannot be separated as winner and runner-up.
a tie in a race or contest

Examples

The two runners finished in a dead heat, sharing first place.
After reviewing the photo finish, the judges declared the race a dead heat.
The election ended in a dead heat, forcing a runoff between the candidates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tie - a situation where competitors finish with equal scores or times
  • Antonym: win - the act of finishing first in a competition

Etymology

From horse racing; 'dead' meaning exact or absolute, and 'heat' meaning a single run of a race.
