sucking fish

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌkɪŋ fɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈsʌkɪŋ fɪʃ / (BrE)
a marine fish with a flattened, elongated body and a sucking disk on the head for attaching to larger fish or objects
marine fish with a sucking disk on the head

Examples

The sucking fish clung to the shark as it swam through the ocean.
Divers often spot a sucking fish attached to the hulls of boats.
A sucking fish uses its unique disk to ride along with larger marine animals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: remora - a type of sucking fish known for attaching to sharks and other sea creatures
