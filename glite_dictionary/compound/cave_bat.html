cave bat

1. NOUN

/ keɪv bæt / (AmE), / keɪv bæt / (BrE)
a drab yellowish bat with large ears that inhabits caves
bat species living in caves

Examples

The cave bat is well-adapted to living in dark underground spaces.
Researchers spotted several cave bats roosting together in the limestone cavern.
A cave bat uses its large ears to detect prey in total darkness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bat - a nocturnal flying mammal
