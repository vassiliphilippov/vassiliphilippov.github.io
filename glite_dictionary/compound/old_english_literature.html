Old English literature

1. NOUN

/ oʊld ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ ˌlɪtərəˈtʃʊr / (AmE), / əʊld ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ ˌlɪt(ə)rəˈtʃə / (BrE)
The body of literature written in Old English, originating from the early medieval period in England, typically between the 5th and 12th centuries.
literature from early medieval England

Examples

She majored in Old English literature at university and can read Beowulf in the original language.
The course explores major works of Old English literature, such as epic poems, religious texts, and riddles.
Interest in Old English literature has grown due to new discoveries of early manuscripts.

Synset Examples

She wrote her thesis on Old English literature.
