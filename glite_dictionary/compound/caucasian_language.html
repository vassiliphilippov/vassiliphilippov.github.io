Caucasian language

1. NOUN

/ kɔˈkeɪʒən ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / kɔːˈkeɪʒən ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
any of a group of languages spoken in the Caucasus region, which are unrelated to languages spoken elsewhere
language group of the Caucasus region

Examples

The Chechen language is a well-known example of a Caucasian language.
Linguists have studied the unique sounds found in every Caucasian language.
Not all languages spoken in Georgia are classified as a Caucasian language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: language - system of communication used by a particular community

Etymology

From 'Caucasian', referring to the Caucasus region, and 'language'.
