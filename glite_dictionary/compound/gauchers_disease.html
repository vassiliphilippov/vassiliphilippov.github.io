Gaucher's disease

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡoʊʃərz dɪˌziz / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊʃəz dɪˌziːz / (BrE)
A rare inherited disorder in which fatty substances accumulate in certain organs and tissues due to a deficiency of a specific enzyme.
rare inherited lipid storage disorder
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that Gaucher's disease is caused by an enzyme deficiency.
Early detection of Gaucher's disease can improve the effectiveness of treatment.
She learned about her family's risk for Gaucher's disease after genetic testing.

Etymology

Named after French physician Philippe Gaucher (1854–1918), who first described the disease.
