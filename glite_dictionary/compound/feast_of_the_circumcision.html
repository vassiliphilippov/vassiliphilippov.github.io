Feast of the Circumcision

1. NOUN

/ ˈfist əv ðə ˌsɝkəmˈsɪʒən / (AmE), / ˈfiːst əv ðə ˌsɜːkəmˈsɪʒən / (BrE)
(in the Roman Catholic and Anglican churches) the feast day commemorating the circumcision of Jesus, traditionally observed on January 1st.
Christian feast day marking Jesus' circumcision

Examples

Many churches hold special services on the Feast of the Circumcision each year.
The Feast of the Circumcision is traditionally celebrated on January 1st in some Christian denominations.
During the Middle Ages, the Feast of the Circumcision was observed with various rituals and liturgies.
