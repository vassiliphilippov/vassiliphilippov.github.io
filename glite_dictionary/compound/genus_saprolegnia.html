genus Saprolegnia

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs sæpˌroʊˈlɛɡniə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs sæpˌrəʊˈlɛɡniə / (BrE)
A genus of aquatic fungi that grow mainly on plant debris and decaying animal matter, often causing disease in fish and amphibians.
genus of aquatic fungi

Examples

Genus Saprolegnia is known for causing water molds that threaten fish populations.
Researchers studied the growth of genus Saprolegnia on decomposing leaves in freshwater streams.
Aquarists often encounter issues when genus Saprolegnia infects the tank's aquatic life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Saprolegnia - type genus of the family Saprolegniaceae, aquatic fungi

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'sapros' (rotten) + 'legnein' (to collect).
