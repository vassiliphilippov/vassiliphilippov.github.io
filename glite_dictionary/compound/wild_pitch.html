wild pitch

1. NOUN

/ waɪld pɪtʃ / (AmE), / waɪld pɪtʃ / (BrE)
(in baseball) a pitch thrown so erratically that the catcher cannot reasonably be expected to catch it, allowing a base runner to advance a base.
errant baseball pitch allowing runner to advance

Examples

The pitcher was charged with a wild pitch in the ninth inning, allowing the tying run to score.
His fastball sailed over the catcher's head for a wild pitch that put the runner on second base.
After a wild pitch, the batter advanced to third, putting pressure on the defense.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: passed ball - a legal pitch not caught by the catcher, allowing runners to advance
