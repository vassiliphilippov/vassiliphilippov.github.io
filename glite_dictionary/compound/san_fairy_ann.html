san fairy ann

1. NOUN

/ sæn ˈfɛri æn / (AmE), / sæn ˈfɛːri æn / (BrE)
used to indicate that something is of no importance or is not worth considering; an expression of indifference or dismissal.
expression meaning 'it doesn't matter'
informal british

Examples

When I asked if the delay bothered her, she just said 'san fairy ann.'
If we miss the first train, san fairy ann—there's another in ten minutes.
He lost his pen again, but it was all san fairy ann to him.

Synset Examples

He said he'd help, but it was all san fairy ann.
Her excuses for being late were just san fairy ann.
Don't take his promises seriously; they're just san fairy ann.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: it doesn't matter - expression indicating something is unimportant
  • Synonym: no matter - phrase expressing indifference or lack of importance

Etymology

From a humorous Anglicisation of French 'ça ne fait rien' (it doesn't matter).
