genus Sium

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈsaɪ.əm / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈsɪ.ʊm / (BrE)
A genus of perennial plants found in wet and marshy places in the Northern Hemisphere, commonly known as water parsnips.
genus of marsh plants (water parsnips)

Examples

The genus Sium contains several species of water parsnip that thrive in marshy habitats.
Botanists have documented the distribution of genus Sium across Europe and North America.
Members of the genus Sium are often found in wetland conservation areas.

Etymology

From Latin 'Sium', from Greek 'sion', meaning a kind of water plant.
