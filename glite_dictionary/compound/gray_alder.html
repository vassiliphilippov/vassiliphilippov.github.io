gray alder

1. NOUN

/ ɡreɪ ˈɔldɚ / (AmE), / ɡreɪ ˈɔːldə / (BrE)
a species of alder tree (Alnus incana) native to Europe and Asia, with grayish bark and leaves, sometimes introduced elsewhere, including North America
species of alder tree (Alnus incana)

Examples

The gray alder is often planted to prevent soil erosion along riverbanks.
In botany class, we learned how to identify a gray alder by its distinctive leaves.
Unlike other species, the gray alder thrives in both wet and dry soil conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: speckled alder - a related species of alder tree (Alnus rugosa)
  • Synonym: alder - a tree or shrub of the birch family, genus Alnus
