gene mutation

1. NOUN

/ dʒiːn mjuˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / dʒiːn mjuːˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
a permanent alteration in the DNA sequence of a gene, resulting in a variant form that may be transmitted to subsequent generations
change in a gene's DNA sequence

Examples

A single gene mutation can sometimes lead to serious health conditions.
Researchers discovered a gene mutation responsible for the disease in several families.
Not all gene mutations have harmful effects; some are neutral or even beneficial.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mutation - a change in the DNA sequence
  • Degree Higher: chromosomal mutation - a mutation involving a large segment or entire chromosome

Etymology

From 'gene', from Greek 'genea' (generation, race), and 'mutation', from Latin 'mutatio' (change).
