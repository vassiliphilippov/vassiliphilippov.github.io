Virginia thimbleweed

1. NOUN

/ vərˈdʒɪn.i ˈθɪm.bəlˌwiːd / (AmE), / vəˈdʒɪn.i ˈθɪm.bəlˌwiːd / (BrE)
A perennial wildflower native to central and eastern North America, known for its white flowers and elongated seed heads.
a North American wildflower species

Examples

We spotted a cluster of Virginia thimbleweed blooming by the edge of the forest trail.
The delicate flowers of the Virginia thimbleweed attract bees and butterflies in the spring.
Botanists often study Virginia thimbleweed for its unique seed dispersal mechanism.
