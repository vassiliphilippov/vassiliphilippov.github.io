center of gravity

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛnɚ əv ˈɡrævəti / (AmE), / ˈsɛntə əv ˈɡravɪti / (BrE)
the point in a body or system at which its mass may be considered to be concentrated, and where the force of gravity can be considered to act; in uniform gravity, coincides with the center of mass.
point where gravity acts on a body

Examples

The gymnast kept her center of gravity low to improve her balance.
Engineers calculated the center of gravity before designing the new vehicle.
If the center of gravity shifts too far, the building may become unstable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: center of mass - point representing the mean position of the mass in a body
