Lower Paleolithic

1. NOUN

/ ˈloʊ.ɚ ˌpeɪ.li.oʊˈlɪθ.ɪk / (AmE), / ˈləʊ.ə ˌpæ.li.əʊˈlɪθ.ɪk / (BrE)
the earliest phase of the Paleolithic Age, characterized by the first use of stone tools and the emergence of the hand ax, lasting until about 120,000 years ago
the earliest part of the Paleolithic Age

Examples

The Lower Paleolithic period saw the first widespread use of stone tools by early humans.
Archaeologists discovered artifacts from the Lower Paleolithic near the riverbank.
The hand ax is one of the most iconic tools from the Lower Paleolithic.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Middle Paleolithic - the middle part of the Paleolithic Age
  • Degree Higher: Upper Paleolithic - the most recent part of the Paleolithic Age

Etymology

From Latin 'palaeo-' (ancient) and Greek 'lithos' (stone).
