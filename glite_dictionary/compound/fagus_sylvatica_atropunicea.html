Fagus sylvatica atropunicea

1. NOUN

/ ˈfeɪɡəs sɪlˈvætɪkə ˌætroʊpjuːˈnɪsiə / (AmE), / ˈfeɪɡəs sɪlˈvætɪkə ˌætrəʊpjuːˈnɪsiə / (BrE)
A cultivated variety of European beech tree distinguished by its shining purple or copper-colored leaves.
purple-leaved variety of European beech

Examples

The Fagus sylvatica atropunicea is often chosen for its striking deep purple foliage in ornamental gardens.
In spring, the leaves of Fagus sylvatica atropunicea emerge a shiny copper color before darkening.
A majestic Fagus sylvatica atropunicea stood at the center of the park, drawing the eye with its unusual leaf color.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: European beech - A species of beech native to Europe, Fagus sylvatica.

Etymology

From scientific Latin; 'Fagus' is Latin for 'beech', 'sylvatica' means 'of the woods', and 'atropunicea' means 'dark purple-red'.
