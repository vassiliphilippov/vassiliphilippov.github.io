glandular carcinoma

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡlæn.dʒələr ˌkɑr.sɪˈnoʊ.mə / (AmE), / ˈɡlæn.djʊ.lə ˌkɑː.sɪˈnəʊ.mə / (BrE)
A malignant tumor that originates in the glandular epithelial tissue, commonly referred to as adenocarcinoma.
malignant tumor from glandular epithelium
medical

Examples

The biopsy revealed a glandular carcinoma in the patient's colon.
Early detection of glandular carcinoma increases the chances of successful treatment.
Researchers are developing new therapies to target glandular carcinoma at the molecular level.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adenocarcinoma - malignant tumor formed from glandular structures

Etymology

From Latin *glandula* (gland) and Greek *karkinoma* (cancerous tumor).
