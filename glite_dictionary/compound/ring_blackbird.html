ring blackbird

1. NOUN

/ rɪŋ ˈblækˌbɝd / (AmE), / rɪŋ ˈblækˌbɜːd / (BrE)
A European thrush (Turdus torquatus) found in rocky areas, where the male has blackish plumage and a distinctive white band around the neck.
European thrush with a white neck band

Examples

We spotted a ring blackbird singing from a rocky outcrop during our hike in the Alps.
The distinctive white neck band makes it easy to identify the male ring blackbird in the wild.
Unlike the common blackbird, the ring blackbird prefers upland and mountainous regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blackbird - a common European thrush, all-black, without the white ring
