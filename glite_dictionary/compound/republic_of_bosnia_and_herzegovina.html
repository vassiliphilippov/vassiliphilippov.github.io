Republic of Bosnia and Herzegovina

1. PROPN

/ rəˈpʌblɪk əv ˈbɑːzniə ənd hɛrtsəˈgoʊvɪnə / (AmE), / rɪˈpʌblɪk əv ˈbɒzniə ənd hɛːtsɪˈɡəʊvɪnə / (BrE)
The official name for the sovereign state that existed from 1992 to 1995 in south-central Europe, following its secession from Yugoslavia, and preceding the formation of the present-day Bosnia and Herzegovina.
historic state in the Balkans, 1992–1995

Examples

After the 1992 declaration of independence, the Republic of Bosnia and Herzegovina was internationally recognized.
Many refugees fled during the war in the Republic of Bosnia and Herzegovina.
The peace agreement in 1995 marked the official end of the Republic of Bosnia and Herzegovina.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Bosnia and Herzegovina - the modern-day country that succeeded the Republic of Bosnia and Herzegovina
