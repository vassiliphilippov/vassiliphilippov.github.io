yellow foxglove

1. NOUN

/ ˈjɛloʊ ˈfɑksˌɡlʌv / (AmE), / ˈjɛləʊ ˈfɒksˌɡlʌv / (BrE)
A European species of foxglove (Digitalis grandiflora) with yellow flowers.
a European yellow-flowered foxglove plant

Examples

The yellow foxglove blooms in early summer in gardens across Europe.
We spotted a cluster of yellow foxglove along the woodland path.
Gardeners admire the yellow foxglove for its tall spikes and pale yellow blossoms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foxglove - a tall plant of the genus Digitalis with tubular flowers
