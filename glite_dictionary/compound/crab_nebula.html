Crab Nebula

1. NOUN

/ kræb ˈnɛbjələ / (AmE), / krab ˈnɛbjʊlə / (BrE)
the remnant of a supernova explosion first observed in 1054 AD, located in the constellation Taurus
supernova remnant in Taurus

Examples

The Crab Nebula is visible through a small telescope on clear nights.
Scientists study the Crab Nebula to learn more about supernova explosions.
The pulsar at the center of the Crab Nebula emits powerful radiation.

Etymology

Named for its crab-like appearance in early telescopic drawings.
