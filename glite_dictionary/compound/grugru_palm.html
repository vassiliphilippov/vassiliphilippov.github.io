grugru palm

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡruːɡruː pɑlm / (AmE), / ˈɡruːɡruː pɑːm / (BrE)
a tropical American feather palm with a swollen spiny trunk and edible nuts
tropical palm with spiny trunk and edible nuts

Examples

The grugru palm produces nuts that are eaten in some South American countries.
Farmers use the spines of the grugru palm to make fishhooks and needles.
We recognized the distinctive trunk of the grugru palm during our walk in the rainforest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Acrocomia aculeata - scientific name for the grugru palm

Etymology

From an indigenous name, possibly from Tupi-Guarani languages.
