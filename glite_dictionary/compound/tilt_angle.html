tilt angle

1. NOUN

/ tɪlt ˈæŋɡəl / (AmE), / tɪlt ˈæŋɡəl / (BrE)
the angle between a rocket's direction and the vertical, especially as it follows its trajectory
angle between rocket and vertical

Examples

The tilt angle of the rocket increased steadily as it ascended through the atmosphere.
Engineers closely monitored the tilt angle to ensure the rocket followed its planned trajectory.
An incorrect tilt angle can cause a rocket to veer off course during launch.
