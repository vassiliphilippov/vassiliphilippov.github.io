monetary value

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌnəˌtɛri ˈvælju / (AmE), / ˈmɒnɪtəri ˈvæljuː / (BrE)
the amount of money that something is worth or could be sold for
amount something is worth in money

Examples

The monetary value of the painting was much higher than anyone had expected.
Insurance will compensate you for the monetary value of your lost property.
They debated whether the monetary value of natural resources can truly capture their importance.

Synset Examples

the fluctuating monetary value of gold and silver
he puts a high price on his services
he couldn't calculate the cost of the collection

Related Terms

  • Synonym: price - the amount of money expected, required, or given in payment for something
  • Synonym: market value - the price something would fetch in the open market

Etymology

From Latin 'monetarius' (relating to money) and Latin 'valere' (to be worth).
