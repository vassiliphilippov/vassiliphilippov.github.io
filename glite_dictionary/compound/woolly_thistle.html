woolly thistle

1. NOUN

/ ˈwʊli ˈθɪsəl / (AmE), / ˈwʊli ˈθɪsəl / (BrE)
A Eurasian biennial thistle (Cirsium eriophorum) with white hairy stems and leaves, bearing pale purple flowers; naturalized in North America.
a white hairy thistle with pale purple flowers

Examples

The woolly thistle is easily recognized by its pale purple flowers and distinctive hairy stems.
Gardeners sometimes plant woolly thistle to attract bees and butterflies to their gardens.
I saw a cluster of woolly thistle growing wild along the edge of the meadow.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwʊli ˈθɪsəl / (AmE), / ˈwʊli ˈθɪsəl / (BrE)
a type of thistle found in western North America, characterized by its white woolly leaves.
a thistle with white woolly leaves

Examples

The woolly thistle grows abundantly in the rocky hills of California.
A botanist carefully examined the white leaves of the woolly thistle.
Gardeners often admire the unique texture of the woolly thistle in their native plant collections.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thistle - a prickly plant with purple flowers
