Bikini Atoll

1. PROPN

/ bɪˈkini əˈtoʊl / (AmE), / bɪˈkiːni ˈatɒl / (BrE)
an atoll in the Marshall Islands, formerly used by the United States as a site for nuclear weapons testing
atoll in the Marshall Islands, site of US nuclear tests

Examples

Many nuclear bombs were detonated at Bikini Atoll during the 1940s and 1950s.
Environmental recovery efforts continue more than half a century after the last test at Bikini Atoll.
Scientists study the effects of radiation on marine life around Bikini Atoll.

Etymology

Named after the Marshallese island Bikini (Pikinni); 'atoll' from Maldivean 'atholhu' (island).
