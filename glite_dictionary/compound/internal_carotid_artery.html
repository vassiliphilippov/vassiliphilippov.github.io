internal carotid artery

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtɝːnəl kəˈrɑːt̬ɪd ˈɑːr.t̬ɚ.i / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɜː.nəl kəˈrɒ.tɪd ˈɑː.tər.i / (BrE)
the branch of the carotid artery that supplies blood to the brain, eyes, and internal parts of the head
major artery supplying brain and eyes
medical

Examples

The internal carotid artery carries oxygen-rich blood to critical areas of the brain.
A blockage in the internal carotid artery can lead to a stroke or vision problems.
Surgeons must take great care when operating near the internal carotid artery due to its importance.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: external carotid artery - the branch of the carotid artery that supplies blood to the face and neck

Etymology

From Latin 'carotis' (carotid artery), from Greek 'karōtis' (to stupefy), referencing its effect when blocked.
