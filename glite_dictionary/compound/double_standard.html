double standard

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʌbəl ˈstændərd / (AmE), / ˈdʌbəl ˈstændəd / (BrE)
A rule or principle that is unfairly applied in different ways to different people or groups.
unfair application of rules to different groups

Examples

The report highlights the double standard in the company’s promotion practices for men and women.
Many criticized the politician for applying a double standard to the issue of free speech.
She was frustrated by the double standard that allowed her brother more freedoms than she had.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bias - unfair preference for or against one group compared to another
  • Antonym: fairness - the quality of treating people equally or without bias
