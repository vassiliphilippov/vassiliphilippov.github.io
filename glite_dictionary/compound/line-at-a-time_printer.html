line-at-a-time printer

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪn ət ə ˈtaɪm ˈprɪntər / (AmE), / ˈlaɪn ət ə ˈtaɪm ˈprɪntə / (BrE)
a printer used with computers that prints an entire line of characters at once, rather than one character at a time
printer that prints a whole line at once

Examples

The line-at-a-time printer was much faster than older character printers for large reports.
Early mainframe systems often relied on a line-at-a-time printer for generating invoices.
Replacing the line-at-a-time printer with a laser printer improved the quality of our documents.
