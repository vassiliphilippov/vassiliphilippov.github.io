genus Staphylea

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒiːnəs stəˈfaɪliə / (AmE), / ˌdʒiːnəs stəˈfaɪliə / (BrE)
A genus of small trees or shrubs in the family Staphylaceae, known for their showy flowers and papery fruit.
a genus of small trees or shrubs

Examples

Botanists have recently identified a rare species within the genus Staphylea.
Members of the genus Staphylea are often cultivated for their attractive flowers.
The genus Staphylea includes the European bladdernut, which is popular in ornamental gardens.

Etymology

From Latin genus (kind) + Staphylea, from Greek staphylē (a bunch of grapes), referring to the fruit clusters.
