bulbourethral gland

1. NOUN

/ ˌbʌlboʊjʊˈriθrəl ˌɡlænd / (AmE), / ˌbʌlbjʊˈriːθrəl ˌɡlænd / (BrE)
either of a pair of small glands in males that discharge a component of seminal fluid into the urethra, homologous to Bartholin's gland in females
male gland that contributes to seminal fluid
medical

Examples

The bulbourethral gland secretes fluid that helps lubricate the urethra before ejaculation.
During the exam, the doctor explained the function of the bulbourethral gland in male fertility.
Unlike the prostate gland, the bulbourethral gland produces only a small amount of fluid.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Bartholin's gland - female gland homologous to the bulbourethral gland

Etymology

From Latin 'bulbus' (bulb) + 'urethra'.
