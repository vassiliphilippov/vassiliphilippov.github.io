friend-in-law

1. NOUN

/ frɛnd ɪn lɔ / (AmE), / frɛnd ɪn lɔː / (BrE)
a friend who is also related to you by marriage, such as your spouse's or sibling's spouse
a friend related by marriage
informal

Examples

My friend-in-law is always invited to our family dinners.
She shares a close bond with her friend-in-law from her husband's side.
I became good friends with my friend-in-law after my sister's wedding.

Synset Examples

My sister's husband became my friend-in-law after we bonded.
I enjoy hanging out with my friend-in-law at family gatherings.
He treats my friend-in-law like a brother.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: in-law - a relative by marriage

Etymology

Formed by analogy with terms like 'mother-in-law,' combining 'friend' and 'in-law.'
