Martyrs of al-Aqsa

1. PROPN

/ ˈmɑr.tɚz əv æl ˈɑːk.sə / (AmE), / ˈmɑː.tɪəz əv æl ˈæk.sə / (BrE)
a Palestinian militant organization, an offshoot of al-Fatah, known for armed operations primarily in the West Bank and for high-profile attacks in Israel, especially in the early 2000s
Palestinian militant group active since 2000s

Examples

The Martyrs of al-Aqsa claimed responsibility for the attack in the city center.
Many governments have designated the Martyrs of al-Aqsa as a terrorist organization.
Reports indicate that the Martyrs of al-Aqsa maintain cells across several West Bank cities.

Etymology

From Arabic 'al-Aqsa' referring to Al-Aqsa Mosque in Jerusalem; 'martyrs' refers to those who die for a cause.
