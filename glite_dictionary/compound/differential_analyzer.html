differential analyzer

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪfəˈrɛnʃəl əˈnælɪzər / (AmE), / ˌdɪfəˈrɛnʃəl əˈnælɪzə / (BrE)
an early type of analog computer designed to solve differential equations mechanically
early analog computer for solving equations

Examples

A differential analyzer was used to compute complex artillery firing tables during World War II.
The museum features a restored differential analyzer from the 1930s.
Before digital computers, engineers relied on the differential analyzer to model scientific problems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: analog computer - a computer that uses continuously changeable physical quantities to represent data

Etymology

Formed from 'differential', referring to differential equations, and 'analyzer', meaning a device that analyzes.
