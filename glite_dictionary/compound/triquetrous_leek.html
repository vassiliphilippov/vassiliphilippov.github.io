triquetrous leek

1. NOUN

/ traɪˈkwɛtrəs lik / (AmE), / traɪˈkwɛtrəs liːk / (BrE)
A European leek with triangular leaves, naturalized in Great Britain.
European leek with triangular leaves

Examples

The triquetrous leek thrives in damp meadows across parts of Britain.
Gardeners appreciate the unique shape of the triquetrous leek's leaves.
While foraging, she identified a patch of triquetrous leek by its characteristic triangular stems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wild leek - a leek species growing uncultivated in the wild

Etymology

From Latin 'triquetrus' (three-cornered) + 'leek'.
