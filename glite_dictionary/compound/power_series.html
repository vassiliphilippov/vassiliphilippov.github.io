power series

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaʊ.ɚ ˌsɪr.iːz / (AmE), / ˈpaʊ.ə ˌsɪə.riːz / (BrE)
an infinite sum whose terms contain successively higher integral powers of a variable, often used in mathematical analysis.
infinite sum with increasing powers of a variable

Examples

The function can be represented by a power series that converges for all real numbers.
Calculating the power series expansion helped them approximate the solution to the differential equation.
He spent the afternoon studying the convergence of a new power series.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Taylor series - a type of power series representing a function about a specific point

Etymology

From 'power' (mathematics, meaning exponent) and 'series' (sequence of terms to be added).
