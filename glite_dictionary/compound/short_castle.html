short castle

1. NOUN

/ ʃɔrt ˈkæsəl / (AmE), / ʃɔːt ˈkɑːsəl / (BrE)
The act of castling on the kingside in chess, moving the king two squares toward the rook on its original square and then placing the rook on the square next to the king.
kingside castling in chess

Examples

He decided to short castle early in the game to secure his king's safety.
Many grandmasters prefer a short castle for rapid development of their rook.
It's often safer to short castle when facing aggressive opponents.

Synset Examples

Since a positional player will always keep his options open and his king safe, castling short would be a good option, and is played most of the time at top level.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: long castle - castling on the queenside in chess
  • Synonym: castling - the chess move in which the king and a rook move simultaneously

Etymology

From 'castle' in chess, meaning to make the castling move, with 'short' referring to kingside due to the shorter distance.
