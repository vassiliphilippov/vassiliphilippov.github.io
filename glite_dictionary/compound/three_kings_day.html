Three Kings' Day

1. NOUN

/ θɹi ˈkɪŋz deɪ / (AmE), / θɹi ˈkɪŋz deɪ / (BrE)
A Christian holiday celebrated twelve days after Christmas, commemorating the visit of the three wise men to the infant Jesus.
Christian holiday marking Epiphany

Examples

Many families exchange gifts on Three Kings' Day instead of Christmas.
In Spain and Latin America, Three Kings' Day is celebrated with parades and feasts.
Children often leave shoes out to be filled with treats on Three Kings' Day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Epiphany - Christian festival celebrating the revelation of Christ to the Gentiles
