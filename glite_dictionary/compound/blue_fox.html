blue fox

1. NOUN

/ blu fɑks / (AmE), / bluː fɒks / (BrE)
a variety of Arctic fox with a pale grey or bluish-grey winter coat, often found in coastal regions
variety of Arctic fox with a pale grey coat

Examples

The blue fox is prized by fur traders for its unique, soft pelt.
During winter, the blue fox develops a thick, greyish coat to blend in with the coastal rocks.
Researchers observed a blue fox hunting along the icy shoreline.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Arctic fox - a small fox native to Arctic regions, known for its thick white winter coat
