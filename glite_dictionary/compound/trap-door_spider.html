trap-door spider

1. NOUN

/ ˈtræp dɔr ˈspaɪdər / (AmE), / ˈtræp dɔː ˈspaɪdə / (BrE)
An American spider that constructs a silk-lined nest with a hinged, trapdoor-like lid.
spider that builds a silk-lined trapdoor nest

Examples

The trap-door spider cleverly hides in its burrow, waiting to ambush prey.
Scientists observed a trap-door spider constructing its silk-lined nest with surprising speed.
Unlike other spiders, the trap-door spider builds a camouflaged lid for its underground home.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: spider - arachnid with eight legs, spinning webs or hunting prey

Etymology

From 'trap-door', referring to its hinged nest lid, and 'spider'.
