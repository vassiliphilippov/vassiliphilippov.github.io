yellow jacaranda

1. NOUN

/ ˈjɛl.oʊ dʒəˈkɑr.əndə / (AmE), / ˈjɛl.əʊ dʒəˈkær.əndə / (BrE)
A semi-evergreen tree native to South America, with pinnate leaves and golden yellow flowers, often grown as an ornamental plant.
a South American ornamental tree with yellow flowers

Examples

The yellow jacaranda brightened the park with its golden blossoms during the spring.
Gardeners often choose the yellow jacaranda for its ornamental value and vibrant color.
A row of yellow jacarandas lined the avenue, attracting visitors and photographers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jacaranda - a genus of tropical trees with showy flowers
