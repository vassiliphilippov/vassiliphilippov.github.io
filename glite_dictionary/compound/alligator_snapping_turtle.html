alligator snapping turtle

1. NOUN

/ ˈæləˌɡeɪtɚ ˈsnæpɪŋ ˈtɝtəl / (AmE), / ˈalɪɡeɪtə ˈsnapɪŋ ˈtɜːtl̩ / (BrE)
a large freshwater turtle of the southeastern United States, distinguished by three prominent ridges on its shell and a powerful jaw
large ridged freshwater turtle of the southeastern U.S.

Examples

The alligator snapping turtle is known for its extremely powerful bite.
Researchers found a massive alligator snapping turtle in the bayou.
Unlike common snapping turtles, the alligator snapping turtle has three distinct ridges on its shell.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snapping turtle - large aggressive freshwater turtle with strong jaws
