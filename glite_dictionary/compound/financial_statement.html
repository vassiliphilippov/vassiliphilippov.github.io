financial statement

1. NOUN

/ fəˈnænʃəl ˈsteɪtmənt / (AmE), / faɪˈnænʃl ˈsteɪtmənt / (BrE)
an official document that shows a company's financial position, including details of its income, expenses, assets, and liabilities
official record of financial accounts

Examples

The company's annual financial statement was presented to the shareholders.
Before applying for the loan, you must submit a financial statement.
Auditors carefully examined the financial statement for any discrepancies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: balance sheet - a summary of a company's assets and liabilities
  • Synonym: income statement - a report showing a company's revenues and expenses over a period

2. NOUN

/ fəˈnænʃəl ˈsteɪtmənt / (AmE), / faɪˈnanʃəl ˈsteɪtmənt / (BrE)
an official record that summarizes the financial activities and position of a business, person, or other entity
official record of financial activities

Examples

The company's financial statement showed a significant increase in revenue this year.
Investors carefully examine the financial statement before making any major decisions.
She prepared a detailed financial statement for the loan application.

Synset Examples

The accountant reviewed the financial statements for accuracy.
