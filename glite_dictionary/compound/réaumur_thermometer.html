Réaumur thermometer

1. NOUN

/ reɪˈɑːmjər θərˈmɑːmɪt̬ɚ / (AmE), / reɪˈɔːmjʊə θəˈmɒmɪtə / (BrE)
An alcohol thermometer calibrated in degrees Réaumur, used especially in the 18th and 19th centuries.
thermometer using the Réaumur scale

Examples

The scientist recorded the temperature with a Réaumur thermometer during the experiment.
Old laboratory manuals often mention the use of a Réaumur thermometer for accurate readings.
In the 18th century, the Réaumur thermometer was widely used across Europe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Celsius thermometer - a thermometer calibrated in degrees Celsius
  • Synonym: Fahrenheit thermometer - a thermometer calibrated in degrees Fahrenheit

Etymology

Named after René Antoine Ferchault de Réaumur (1683–1757), French scientist who proposed the scale.
