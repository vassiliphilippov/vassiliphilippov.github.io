repressor gene

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈprɛsər dʒin / (AmE), / rɪˈprɛsə dʒiːn / (BrE)
a gene that produces a protein which inhibits the expression of one or more other genes by preventing their transcription
gene that inhibits the expression of other genes
medical

Examples

A repressor gene can prevent the synthesis of enzymes when they are not needed.
The mutation in the repressor gene led to uncontrolled gene expression.
Researchers are studying how a repressor gene regulates immune system responses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: regulatory gene - a gene involved in controlling the expression of other genes

Etymology

From 'repressor' (from Latin 'reprimere', to press back) and 'gene' (from German 'Gen', from Greek 'genea', generation).
