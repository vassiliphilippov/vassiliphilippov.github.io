dwarf elder

1. NOUN

/ dwɔrf ˈɛldɚ / (AmE), / dwɔːf ˈɛldə / (BrE)
a bristly herbaceous plant of eastern and central North America, with black fruit and medicinal bark, also known as Danewort or Sambucus ebulus
herbaceous plant with black fruit and medicinal bark

Examples

The berries of the dwarf elder can turn a deep, striking black by late summer.
Some herbalists use dwarf elder bark in traditional medicine.
We spotted a stand of dwarf elder growing wild at the forest edge.

2. NOUN

/ dwɔrf ˈɛldɚ / (AmE), / dwɔːf ˈɛldə / (BrE)
A small herbaceous plant of Europe, with pink flowers and a strong, unpleasant odor, scientifically known as Sambucus ebulus.
European herbaceous plant with pink flowers

Examples

The dwarf elder is often found in hedgerows and open woods throughout Europe.
Gardeners should be cautious, as the berries of dwarf elder are considered toxic.
Despite its unpleasant smell, dwarf elder has a history of use in traditional remedies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elder - a genus of flowering plants in the family Adoxaceae
