pension fund

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛnʃən fʌnd / (AmE), / ˈpɛnʃən fʌnd / (BrE)
A financial organization that collects and invests money contributed by employers (and sometimes employees) to provide retirement income for employees.
retirement savings institution

Examples

The company contributes to a pension fund for all its employees.
He invested a portion of his earnings in a private pension fund.
Concerns about the safety of the pension fund led to stricter government regulations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: retirement fund - a fund set aside for retirement income
  • Degree Lower: trust fund - a fund held in trust for a beneficiary

Etymology

From 'pension' (from Latin 'pensio', payment) and 'fund' (from Latin 'fundus', bottom, foundation).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɛnʃən fʌnd / (AmE), / ˈpɛnʃən fʌnd / (BrE)
A fund set aside to pay employees' pensions after they retire from work.
fund for paying retirement pensions

Examples

The company manages a large pension fund for its retired employees.
Many workers rely on their pension fund to support them in old age.
Concerns over the stability of the pension fund have led to policy changes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: retirement fund - a fund used to provide income in retirement
  • Synonym: pension plan - an arrangement to provide employees with a pension after retirement

Etymology

From 'pension' (from Latin 'pensionem', payment) + 'fund' (from Latin 'fundus', bottom, foundation).

Synset POS: n
