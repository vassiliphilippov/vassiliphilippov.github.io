bread line

1. NOUN

/ brɛd laɪn / (AmE), / brɛd laɪn / (BrE)
a line or queue of people waiting to receive free food, especially during times of economic hardship
queue for free food aid

Examples

During the Great Depression, thousands of people stood in the bread line every day.
The news report showed a bread line stretching around the block.
After losing his job, he reluctantly joined the bread line for the first time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soup kitchen - a place where free food is served to those in need

Etymology

From 'bread' + 'line', referring to lines formed during economic depressions for bread or food handouts.
