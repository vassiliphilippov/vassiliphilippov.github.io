shore bird

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃɔr bɝd / (AmE), / ˈʃɔː bɜːd / (BrE)
a wading bird that is commonly found along seashores and estuaries, belonging to various species
bird commonly found on seashores

Examples

We spotted several species of shore birds feeding along the tidal flats.
Migrating shore birds can often be seen resting on sandy beaches in the spring.
The wildlife reserve is famous for attracting rare shore birds during the summer months.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wader - a bird that wades in water while feeding, often at seashores or wetlands
