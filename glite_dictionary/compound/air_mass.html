air mass

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛr ˌmæs / (AmE), / ˈeə ˌmæs / (BrE)
A large body of air extending over a significant area with relatively uniform temperature, humidity, and pressure.
large body of air with uniform properties

Examples

A cold air mass moved in overnight, causing temperatures to drop sharply.
Meteorologists track the movement of each air mass to predict weather changes.
The humidity was due to a moist air mass coming from the ocean.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: front - boundary between two air masses
