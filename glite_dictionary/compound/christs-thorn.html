Christ's-thorn

1. NOUN

/ kraɪsts ˌθɔrn / (AmE), / kraɪsts ˌθɔːn / (BrE)
A thorny Eurasian shrub (Paliurus spina-christi) with dry, woody, winged fruit.
thorny Eurasian shrub

Examples

The branches of the Christ's-thorn are covered with sharp spines for protection.
Botanists studied the Christ's-thorn growing wild along the Mediterranean coast.
Some believe that the Christ's-thorn was used to make the crown of thorns in biblical times.

Etymology

Named for the belief that its branches were used for the crown of thorns in Christian tradition.

2. NOUN

/ kraɪsts ˌθɔrn / (AmE), / kraɪsts ˌθɔːn / (BrE)
a spiny tree with dark red edible fruits, often identified as Paliurus spina-christi or Ziziphus spina-christi
a spiny tree with edible fruit

Examples

The ancient garden was shaded by a large Christ's-thorn near the entrance.
The edible berries of the Christ's-thorn are sometimes used to make jams or preserves.
Legend says that branches of the Christ's-thorn were woven into the biblical crown of thorns.

Etymology

Refers to the tradition that its branches were used to make Christ's crown of thorns; from 'Christ' and 'thorn'.
