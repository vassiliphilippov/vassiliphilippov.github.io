field of honor

1. NOUN

/ fiːld əv ˈɑnɚ / (AmE), / fiːld əv ˈɒnə / (BrE)
A place where a battle is or has been fought, especially one regarded with respect or solemnity.
site of a battle

Examples

Many soldiers lost their lives on the field of honor during the conflict.
Historians visited the field of honor to commemorate the famous battle.
He regarded his last moments on the field of honor as the ultimate act of bravery.

Synset Examples

they made a tour of Civil War battlefields

Related Terms

  • Synonym: battlefield - an area where a battle is fought

2. NOUN

/ ˈfiːld əv ˈɑːnɚ / (AmE), / ˈfiːld əv ˈɒnə / (BrE)
the place where a duel is or was fought, especially as a matter of personal honor
site of a duel
archaic

Examples

The two rivals met on the field of honor at dawn, pistols drawn.
In many stories, disputes were settled on a field of honor rather than in court.
He refused to apologize, insisting they settle their differences on the field of honor.
