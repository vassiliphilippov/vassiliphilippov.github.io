enzyme reaction

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛnˌzaɪm riˈækʃən / (AmE), / ˈɛn.zaɪm rɪˈæk.ʃən / (BrE)
a chemical reaction in which enzymes act as catalysts to accelerate specific biochemical processes
chemical reaction involving enzymes

Examples

The rate of the enzyme reaction depends on temperature and pH.
Researchers studied how mutations affected the efficiency of the enzyme reaction.
An inhibitor can slow down an enzyme reaction dramatically.

Synset Examples

Prozymite is essential for effective enzyme reactions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catalysis - the process of increasing the rate of a chemical reaction by a catalyst

Etymology

From 'enzyme', coined from Greek 'enzymos' (leavened), and 'reaction', from Latin 'reactio' (a doing again).
