malted milk

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɔltɪd mɪlk / (AmE), / ˈmɔːltɪd mɪlk / (BrE)
A sweet drink made from milk, ice cream, and malted milk powder, usually blended like a milkshake.
sweet drink with malted milk powder

Examples

He ordered a classic malted milk with chocolate syrup at the diner.
Nothing reminds me of my childhood more than sharing a malted milk with my dad after baseball practice.
The old-fashioned soda fountain still serves thick, creamy malted milks in tall glass mugs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: milkshake - a sweet drink made by blending milk, ice cream, and flavorings

Etymology

Malted from 'malt', referring to germinated cereals, and 'milk'. The drink became popular in the early 20th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɔltɪd mɪlk / (AmE), / ˈmɔːltɪd mɪlk / (BrE)
A powder made from dried milk and malted cereals, often used to make a sweet drink or added to foods.
powder of dried milk and malted cereals

Examples

She added a spoonful of malted milk to her morning coffee for extra flavor.
The recipe calls for malted milk powder to give the cookies a rich, creamy taste.
He ordered a classic malted milk shake at the old-fashioned diner.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ovaltine - a brand of malted milk drink
  • Synonym: malt - grain that has been germinated and dried, used in brewing and food
