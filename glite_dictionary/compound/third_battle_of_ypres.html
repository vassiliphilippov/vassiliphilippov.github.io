Third Battle of Ypres

1. NOUN

/ θɝd ˈbæt̬əl əv ˈjɪprə / (AmE), / θɜːd ˈbætl əv ˈiːprə / (BrE)
a major battle of World War I in 1917, also known as the Battle of Passchendaele, involving an Allied offensive in Flanders that ultimately failed due to difficult terrain and effective German defenses, including the use of mustard gas.
major World War I battle in 1917 (Passchendaele)

Examples

The Third Battle of Ypres became infamous for its muddy conditions and heavy casualties.
Many soldiers lost their lives during the Third Battle of Ypres in 1917.
Tanks proved ineffective at the Third Battle of Ypres due to the boggy terrain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Battle of Passchendaele - another name for the Third Battle of Ypres, a major WWI battle in Flanders

Etymology

Ypres is a town in Belgium; the name comes from French Ypres (Dutch: Ieper).
