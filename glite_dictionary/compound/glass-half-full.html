glass-half-full

1. ADJ

/ ɡlæs hæf fʊl / (AmE), / ɡlɑːs hɑːf fʊl / (BrE)
having or showing an optimistic attitude, viewing situations or outcomes positively
optimistic or positive outlook

Examples

Even in the face of disappointment, he managed to stay glass-half-full and looked for new opportunities.
Her glass-half-full attitude helped the team remain hopeful during difficult times.
When others lost hope, Mia's glass-half-full perspective encouraged everyone to try again.

Synset Examples

She has a glass-half-full perspective on life.
In tough times, it's good to be glass-half-full.
His glass-half-full mindset inspires everyone around him.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: optimistic - believing that good things will happen
  • Antonym: pessimistic - believing that bad things are more likely to happen

Etymology

Refers to the proverbially ambiguous glass of water, used metaphorically since the 20th century to indicate optimism (seeing the glass as half full rather than half empty).
