vector sum

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɛk.tɚ sʌm / (AmE), / ˈvɛk.tə sʌm / (BrE)
the vector resulting from adding two or more vectors together
sum of two or more vectors

Examples

The vector sum of the two forces determines the net force acting on the object.
When calculating displacement, you must take the vector sum of all movements.
The vector sum of the velocities gives the overall speed and direction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resultant - the single vector representing the combined effect of two or more vectors
