Augustinian order

1. NOUN

/ ɔːˈɡʌstɪniən ˈɔrdər / (AmE), / ɔːˌɡʌstɪˈniːən ˈɔːdə / (BrE)
any of several Roman Catholic monastic orders that follow a rule based on the teachings of St. Augustine of Hippo.
Roman Catholic monastic order following St. Augustine's rule

Examples

He studied theology at a monastery run by the Augustinian order in Italy.
The Augustinian order emphasized spiritual discipline and community life.
Many medieval scholars were members of the Augustinian order during the Middle Ages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Benedictine order - Roman Catholic monastic order following the rule of St. Benedict
  • Synonym: Franciscan order - Roman Catholic monastic order founded by St. Francis of Assisi

Etymology

From the name of St. Augustine of Hippo (354–430), whose writings inspired the order.
