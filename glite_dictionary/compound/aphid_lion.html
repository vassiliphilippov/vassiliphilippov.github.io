aphid lion

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪ.fɪd ˌlaɪ.ən / (AmE), / ˈeɪ.fɪd ˌlaɪ.ən / (BrE)
the larva of a lacewing fly, noted for feeding on aphids and other small insects
larva of lacewing fly that eats aphids

Examples

Gardeners encourage aphid lions to control aphid infestations without using chemicals.
The aphid lion devoured several pests on the rose bush in just a day.
You can easily spot an aphid lion by its slender, alligator-like body on leaves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lacewing - insect whose larvae are called aphid lions

Etymology

Formed from "aphid" (the prey) and "lion" (suggesting a fierce predator) to describe the larva's feeding habits.
