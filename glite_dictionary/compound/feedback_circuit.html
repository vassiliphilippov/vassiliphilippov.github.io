feedback circuit

1. NOUN

/ ˈfidˌbæk ˈsɝkɪt / (AmE), / ˈfiːdbæk ˈsɜːkɪt / (BrE)
an electrical circuit in which a portion of the output is returned to the input to influence its operation
circuit returning output to input

Examples

The amplifier's feedback circuit helped reduce distortion in the audio signal.
Engineers adjusted the feedback circuit to stabilize the voltage levels.
A malfunction in the feedback circuit caused irregular behavior in the control system.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: feedback loop - a system where output is fed back as input

Etymology

feedback from 'feed' + 'back' (to return), and circuit from Latin 'circuitus' (a going around).
