bride price

1. NOUN

/ braɪd praɪs / (AmE), / braɪd praɪs / (BrE)
money or property given by the groom to the family of his bride in some cultures as part of the marriage agreement
marriage payment from groom to bride's family

Examples

The tradition of paying a bride price is still practiced in some rural communities.
Conflict arose when the families could not agree on the appropriate bride price.
Anthropologists study the significance of the bride price in various cultures.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dowry - property or money brought by a bride to her husband on marriage
