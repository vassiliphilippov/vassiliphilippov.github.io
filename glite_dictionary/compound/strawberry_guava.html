strawberry guava

1. NOUN

/ ˈstrɔˌbɛri ˈɡwɑvə / (AmE), / ˈstrɒbəri ˈɡwɑːvə / (BrE)
A small tropical shrub or tree (Psidium cattleianum) native to South America, cultivated for its small, edible, yellowish or reddish fruit that resembles a strawberry in flavor.
tropical tree bearing edible fruit

Examples

The strawberry guava grows well in subtropical and tropical regions around the world.
She made a delicious jam from fresh strawberry guavas picked in her backyard.
Many gardeners value the strawberry guava for its sweet, aromatic fruit and ornamental appeal.

Etymology

The name combines 'strawberry' (for the fruit's flavor resemblance) and 'guava' (the genus Psidium).
