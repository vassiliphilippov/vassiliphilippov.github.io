Waterhouse-Friderichsen syndrome

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔːtərˌhaʊs ˌfrɪdəˈrɪksən ˈsɪnˌdroʊm / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtəˌhaʊs ˌfrɪdəˈrɪksən ˈsɪn.drəʊm / (BrE)
A rare, often fatal condition characterized by sudden onset of high fever, skin discoloration, widespread bleeding (petechiae), and hemorrhage into the adrenal glands, usually occurring as a complication of severe bacterial meningitis.
rare syndrome marked by adrenal hemorrhage
medical

Examples

The patient developed Waterhouse-Friderichsen syndrome after contracting bacterial meningitis, leading to rapid cardiovascular collapse.
Early recognition and treatment are crucial in cases of Waterhouse-Friderichsen syndrome to improve survival rates.
Purpura and adrenal insufficiency are key symptoms associated with Waterhouse-Friderichsen syndrome.

Etymology

Named after British physician Rupert Waterhouse and Danish physician Carl Friderichsen, who described the syndrome in the early 20th century.
