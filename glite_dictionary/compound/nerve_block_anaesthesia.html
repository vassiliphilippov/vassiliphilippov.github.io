nerve block anaesthesia

1. NOUN

/ nɝv blɑk ˌænəsˈθiʒə / (AmE), / nɜːv blɒk ˌænəsˈθiːziə / (BrE)
Anaesthesia produced in a specific area of the body by injecting an anaesthetic agent near a nerve supplying that area.
anaesthesia via injection near a nerve
medical

Examples

The surgeon recommended a nerve block anaesthesia to ensure the patient would not feel pain during the operation.
For her dental procedure, the dentist used nerve block anaesthesia rather than general anaesthesia.
Nerve block anaesthesia is often chosen for minor surgeries to reduce recovery time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: local anaesthesia - loss of sensation in a specific part of the body
