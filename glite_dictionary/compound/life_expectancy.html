life expectancy

1. NOUN

/ laɪf ɪkˈspɛktənsi / (AmE), / laɪf ɪkˈspɛktənsi / (BrE)
the average number of years that a person or other living being is expected to live, based on statistical data
average expected lifespan

Examples

In Japan, the life expectancy is among the highest in the world.
Doctors say improved nutrition has helped increase life expectancy over the past few decades.
Rising air pollution could threaten future life expectancy in urban areas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: longevity - the length or duration of life

Etymology

From 'life' + 'expectancy', the latter from Latin 'expectare' (to await).

2. NOUN

/ laɪf ɪkˈspɛktənsi / (AmE), / laɪf ɪkˈspɛktənsi / (BrE)
The average number of years that a person or a population is expected to live, based on statistical averages.
average expected lifespan

Examples

Rising incomes and better sanitation have contributed to a higher life expectancy in many regions.
The report compares the life expectancy of men and women in the country.
Advances in medicine continue to extend average life expectancy worldwide.

Synset Examples

Healthy habits can improve your life expectancy significantly.
The new healthcare policies aim to increase life expectancy.
In developed countries, life expectancy is generally higher.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lifespan - the length of time that a person or creature is expected to live

Etymology

Formed from 'life' and 'expectancy'; 'expectancy' from Latin 'expectantia' (expectation).
