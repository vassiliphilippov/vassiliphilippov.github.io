Battle of Hohenlinden

1. PROPN

/ ˈbæt̬l əv ˌhoʊənˈlɪndən / (AmE), / ˈbætl əv ˌhəʊənˈlɪndən / (BrE)
A decisive battle fought on December 3, 1800, during the Napoleonic Wars, in which French forces defeated the Austrians near Hohenlinden in Bavaria.
decisive 1800 Napoleonic battle in Bavaria

Examples

The Battle of Hohenlinden changed the course of the Napoleonic Wars in favor of France.
Historians often analyze the tactics used at the Battle of Hohenlinden for their effectiveness.
After the Battle of Hohenlinden, the Austrians were forced to negotiate peace with the French.

Etymology

From the German place name Hohenlinden, near where the battle took place.
