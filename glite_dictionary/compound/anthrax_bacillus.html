anthrax bacillus

1. NOUN

/ ˈænˌθræks bəˈsɪl.əs / (AmE), / ˈæn.θræks bəˈsɪl.əs / (BrE)
a species of bacterium (Bacillus anthracis) that causes anthrax in humans and animals, and can be used as a bioweapon
bacterium that causes anthrax
medical

Examples

The anthrax bacillus can survive for decades in soil as spores.
After the laboratory accident, several workers were exposed to the anthrax bacillus.
Vaccination of livestock helps prevent outbreaks caused by the anthrax bacillus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Bacillus anthracis - the scientific name of the bacterium causing anthrax
  • Synonym: anthrax - a serious infectious disease caused by Bacillus anthracis

Etymology

'Anthrax' from Greek 'ánthrax' (coal) due to the black skin lesions; 'bacillus' from Latin meaning 'little rod.'
