Middle East respiratory syndrome

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪdəl iːst rɪˌspɪrəˌtɔri sɪnˌdroʊm / (AmE), / ˈmɪdəl iːst rɪˌspɪrəˌtəri sɪnˌdrəʊm / (BrE)
A viral respiratory illness caused by the MERS coronavirus (MERS-CoV), typically transmitted from camels to humans, characterized by fever, cough, and shortness of breath.
viral disease (MERS) transmitted from camels
medical

Examples

Health authorities took action when cases of Middle East respiratory syndrome were reported in the city.
The virus responsible for Middle East respiratory syndrome is closely related to SARS-CoV.
Researchers are studying ways to prevent the spread of Middle East respiratory syndrome from camels to humans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: MERS - acronym for Middle East respiratory syndrome
  • Synonym: SARS - severe acute respiratory syndrome, a related coronavirus disease
