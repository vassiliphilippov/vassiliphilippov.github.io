common land

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑː.mən lænd / (AmE), / ˈkɒm.ən lænd / (BrE)
land that is open to use by the public or members of a community, especially for grazing animals or other communal activities, often with legal rights attached.
land open to public or communal use
british

Examples

The villagers continued to graze their sheep on the common land as they had for generations.
Much of the common land was lost during the enclosure movement in England.
Access to common land is protected by law in several parts of the United Kingdom.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: open space - an area of land that is not built on, especially for public use

Etymology

From 'common' (shared by all) and 'land', historically in reference to the British system of common rights.
