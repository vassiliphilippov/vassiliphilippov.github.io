regular prism

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛɡjələr ˈprɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈrɛɡjʊlə ˈprɪzəm / (BrE)
A prism whose bases are regular polygons and whose sides are rectangles or parallelograms, typically referring to the right prism form.
prism with regular polygon bases

Examples

The volume of a regular prism can be calculated by multiplying the area of its base by its height.
A cube is a special case of a regular prism with square bases.
In geometry class, students learned to identify different types of regular prisms and calculate their surface areas.

Synset Examples

The height of a regular prism is the distance between the bases.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: prism - a solid with two parallel congruent polygonal faces and rectangular sides

Etymology

From Latin 'regula' (rule, pattern) and Greek 'prisma' (something sawed, a prism).
