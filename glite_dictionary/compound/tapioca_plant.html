tapioca plant

1. NOUN

/ ˌtæpiˈoʊkə plænt / (AmE), / ˌtæpɪˈəʊkə plɑːnt / (BrE)
the plant cassava, with long tuberous edible roots and brittle stems, cultivated for its starchy roots used to make tapioca
cassava plant used for tapioca

Examples

Farmers in the region grow the tapioca plant for its starchy roots.
The tapioca plant thrives in tropical climates with well-drained soil.
Traditional puddings are made from starch extracted from the tapioca plant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cassava - tropical plant with edible starchy roots
