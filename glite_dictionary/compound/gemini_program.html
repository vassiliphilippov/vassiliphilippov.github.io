Gemini program

1. PROPN

/ ˈdʒɛmɪnaɪ ˌproʊɡræm / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛmɪni ˌprəʊɡræm / (BrE)
the US spaceflight program (1965–66) that sent manned missions into Earth orbit as preparation for the Apollo missions
US manned spaceflight program, 1965–66

Examples

The Gemini program was a crucial step in preparing NASA for lunar missions.
Many of the astronauts in the Gemini program later walked on the Moon.
Under the Gemini program, crews conducted important tests of space docking and EVA.

Synset Examples

under the Gemini program each crew had two astronauts

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Apollo program - NASA program that landed humans on the Moon
  • Degree Lower: Mercury program - first US manned space program

Etymology

From 'Gemini', Latin for 'twins', referring to the twin-astronaut crews, and 'program', meaning coordinated set of operations.
