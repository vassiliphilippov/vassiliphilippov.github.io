generative grammar

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɛnərətɪv ˈɡræmər / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛnərətɪv ˈɡræmə / (BrE)
a theory of grammar that describes a language's syntax by specifying a set of formal rules capable of generating all grammatically correct sentences and assigning them proper structure
theory of syntax based on generative rules

Examples

Many linguists study generative grammar to understand the underlying rules of language structure.
Chomsky's introduction of generative grammar revolutionized the field of theoretical linguistics.
The course on generative grammar explores how complex sentences are formed from simple elements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transformational grammar - a related theory of grammar introduced by Noam Chomsky

Etymology

Formed from 'generative', from Latin 'generare' (to produce), and 'grammar', from Greek 'grammatike'.
