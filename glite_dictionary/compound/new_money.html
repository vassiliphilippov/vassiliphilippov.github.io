new money

1. NOUN

/ nuː ˈmʌni / (AmE), / njuː ˈmʌni / (BrE)
people or families who have recently acquired wealth, especially as distinct from those with inherited wealth.
recently acquired wealth or wealthy people

Examples

The city is full of new money building flashy homes and throwing extravagant parties.
Some social circles still look down on new money, favoring those with long-standing family fortunes.
Although he grew up poor, his tech company success now puts him among the ranks of new money.

Synset Examples

The party was filled with new money trying to impress.
He flaunts his wealth like a true new money.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: old money - people or families with inherited wealth, typically spanning generations
