ecclesiastical robe

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌkliːziˈæstɪkəl roʊb / (AmE), / ɪˌkliːziˈæstɪkəl rəʊb / (BrE)
a robe worn by clergy or church officials as part of official religious ceremonies or duties
robe worn by clergy in church

Examples

The bishop entered the cathedral in his ecclesiastical robe, ready to lead the service.
She admired the embroidery on the priest's ecclesiastical robe during the ceremony.
Several ecclesiastical robes were displayed in the church museum as examples of liturgical attire.

Etymology

From ecclesiastical, from Greek *ekklēsiastikos* (of the assembly or church), and robe, from Old French *robe* (garment).
