Munchausen syndrome

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌnˌʃaʊzən ˈsɪnˌdroʊm / (AmE), / ˈmʌnˌʃaʊzən ˈsɪn.drəʊm / (BrE)
a psychological disorder in which a person feigns or induces illness in themselves in order to gain attention, sympathy, or treatment, despite no clinical evidence of illness
disorder involving feigned illness
medical

Examples

The patient was eventually diagnosed with Munchausen syndrome after repeated hospitalizations with unexplained symptoms.
Doctors grew suspicious of Munchausen syndrome when the test results did not support the reported symptoms.
Awareness of Munchausen syndrome has increased among medical professionals in recent years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: factitious disorder - a mental disorder in which a person acts as if they have a physical or mental illness when they are not really sick

Etymology

Named after Baron Munchausen, a German nobleman known for telling exaggerated tales, combined with 'syndrome', from Greek 'sundromē' (concurrence of symptoms).
