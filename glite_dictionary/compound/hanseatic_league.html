Hanseatic League

1. PROPN

/ ˌhæn.siˈæt.ɪk liːɡ / (AmE), / ˌhan.siˈat.ɪk liːɡ / (BrE)
a medieval commercial and defensive confederation of free cities in northern Germany and neighboring regions, which was most influential in the 14th century as a major trading and political power
medieval German trade confederation

Examples

The Hanseatic League dominated trade in the Baltic and North Sea regions for several centuries.
Many prosperous cities, such as Lübeck and Hamburg, were leading members of the Hanseatic League.
The decline of the Hanseatic League began as new trade routes and powers emerged in Europe.

Etymology

From German 'Hanse' meaning 'guild' or 'association', and 'League' for confederation.
