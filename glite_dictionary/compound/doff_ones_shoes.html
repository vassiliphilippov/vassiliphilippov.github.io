to doff one's shoes

1. VERB

/ dɑf wʌnz ʃuz / (AmE), / dɒf wʌnz ʃuːz / (BrE)
to remove one's shoes, especially as a gesture of respect or before entering a place
remove one's shoes

Examples

In many temples, visitors are expected to doff their shoes before stepping inside.
She paused at the door to doff her shoes as a sign of respect.
It is customary in this household to doff your shoes upon entry.

Synset Examples

Please doff your shoes before entering the house.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: remove one's shoes - to take off one's shoes
  • Antonym: don one's shoes - to put on one's shoes

Etymology

From 'doff', an old verb meaning 'to take off', a contraction of 'do off'.
