equal temperament

1. NOUN

/ ˈiːkwəl ˈtɛmpərəmənt / (AmE), / ˈiːkwəl ˈtɛmpərəmənt / (BrE)
a system of tuning in which the octave is divided into twelve equal semitones, so that each key is equally in tune
musical tuning system with equal semitones

Examples

Most modern pianos are tuned using equal temperament to allow for playability in all keys.
Bach wrote The Well-Tempered Clavier to demonstrate the advantages of equal temperament.
In equal temperament, each semitone has the same frequency ratio, making modulation easier.

Synset Examples

equal temperament is the system commonly used in keyboard instruments

Related Terms

  • Antonym: just intonation - musical tuning system with pure intervals
  • Antonym: meantone temperament - a system of musical tuning with slightly unequal intervals

Etymology

From 'equal' (meaning uniform) and 'temperament' (modification of tuning), used in music since at least the 18th century.
