Nicol prism

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɪkəl ˌprɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈnɪkəl ˌprɪzəm / (BrE)
an optical device made from calcite that produces plane-polarized light by separating the components of unpolarized light
optical device for producing polarized light

Examples

A Nicol prism is often used in polarizing microscopes to produce plane-polarized light.
The Nicol prism relies on the birefringent properties of calcite crystals for its function.
Scientists use a Nicol prism to examine the optical properties of minerals and crystals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polarizer - a device that converts unpolarized light into polarized light

Etymology

Named after William Nicol (1770–1851), Scottish physicist, who invented it.
