firing line

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪɹɪŋ laɪn / (AmE), / ˈfaɪərɪŋ laɪn / (BrE)
A line or position from which soldiers or shooters deliver gunfire at a target, especially in a military engagement.
line from which soldiers shoot

Examples

The soldiers stood at the firing line waiting for the command to shoot.
After a brief pause, the captain advanced his men to the firing line.
The enemy was visible from the firing line, making the position especially dangerous.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪɹɪŋ laɪn / (AmE), / ˈfaɪərɪŋ laɪn / (BrE)
the position or situation of being most directly involved in an activity, especially where pressure or responsibility is greatest
critical or leading position in an activity

Examples

After his promotion, he found himself in the firing line for major decisions.
Journalists are often in the firing line during times of political unrest.
She stepped into the firing line by leading the controversial project.

Synset Examples

the firing line is where the action is

Related Terms

  • Synonym: front line - the most advanced position or leading role in an activity
  • Antonym: sidelines - a position removed from direct involvement or responsibility

Etymology

Originally from the military term for the front row of soldiers or shooters in a battle or range.

3. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪrɪŋ laɪn / (AmE), / ˈfaɪərɪŋ laɪn / (BrE)
a position in which one is exposed to criticism, attack, or danger, especially as the focus of attention.
position exposed to criticism or danger

Examples

As the spokesperson, she found herself right in the firing line during the press conference.
The new coach stepped into the firing line after the team's poor performance last season.
These decisions will put the company directly in the firing line of public opinion.

Synset Examples

The team stood in the firing line during the project review.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hot seat - a position of difficulty or high scrutiny
  • Antonym: safe haven - a place of safety or protection

Etymology

From military use: the line along which soldiers fire at the enemy.
