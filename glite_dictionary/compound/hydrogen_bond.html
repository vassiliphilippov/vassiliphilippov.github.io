hydrogen bond

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪdrədʒən bɑnd / (AmE), / ˈhaɪdrɪdʒən bɒnd / (BrE)
a weak chemical bond formed when a hydrogen atom is attracted to two electronegative atoms, one by a covalent bond and the other typically by an electrostatic (non-covalent) interaction
a weak chemical bond involving hydrogen

Examples

Water's high boiling point is due in part to the presence of hydrogen bonds between its molecules.
The double helix structure of DNA is stabilized by hydrogen bonds connecting complementary nucleotide bases.
Many proteins maintain their shape because of hydrogen bonds between different parts of the molecule.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: covalent bond - a chemical bond formed by the sharing of electron pairs between atoms
  • Antonym: ionic bond - a chemical bond formed by the electrostatic attraction between oppositely charged ions

Etymology

From 'hydrogen' (element name) + 'bond' (connection), first used in early 20th-century chemistry.
