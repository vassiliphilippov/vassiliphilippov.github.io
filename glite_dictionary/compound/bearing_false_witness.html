bearing false witness

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛrɪŋ fɔls ˈwɪtnəs / (AmE), / ˈbɛːrɪŋ fɔːls ˈwɪtnəs / (BrE)
the act of knowingly making false statements under oath, especially in a legal or formal context
lying under oath; perjury
legal

Examples

He was accused of bearing false witness during the trial, which is a serious crime.
In the past, bearing false witness was considered not only illegal but also morally reprehensible.
The judge warned the witness about the consequences of bearing false witness in court.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perjury - the act of lying under oath in a legal proceeding

Etymology

From the biblical commandment "Thou shalt not bear false witness", with 'bear' meaning 'to give' or 'to present'.
