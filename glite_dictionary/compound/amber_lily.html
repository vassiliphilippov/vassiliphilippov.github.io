amber lily

1. NOUN

/ ˈæmbɚ ˈlɪli / (AmE), / ˈæmbə ˈlɪli / (BrE)
a plant native to the southwestern United States with basal grasslike leaves and a narrow, open cluster of starlike yellowish-orange flowers on a leafless stalk; the only species of Anthericum found in North America
plant with yellowish-orange flowers, southwestern US

Examples

The amber lily blooms each spring across the dry hills of Arizona.
Gardeners in New Mexico prize the amber lily for its striking orange-yellow flowers.
The amber lily is the only Anthericum species native to North America.
