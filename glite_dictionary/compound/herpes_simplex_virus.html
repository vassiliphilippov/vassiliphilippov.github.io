herpes simplex virus

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɝːpiːz ˈsɪmˌplɛks ˈvaɪrəs / (AmE), / ˈhɜːpiːz ˈsɪmpleks ˈvaɪərəs / (BrE)
a virus that causes infections of the skin and nervous system, such as cold sores and genital herpes
virus causing herpes infections
medical

Examples

The herpes simplex virus is responsible for causing both oral and genital herpes.
Researchers are developing vaccines to protect against the herpes simplex virus.
Transmission of the herpes simplex virus can occur even when symptoms are not visible.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: herpes zoster - the virus causing shingles
  • Degree Lower: cold sore - a small sore on or near the lips caused by herpes simplex virus
  • Degree Higher: genital herpes - a sexually transmitted infection caused by herpes simplex virus

Etymology

From New Latin *herpes simplex* ('simple herpes') + virus ('poison, slime').
