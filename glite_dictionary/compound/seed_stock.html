seed stock

1. NOUN

/ sid stɑk / (AmE), / siːd stɒk / (BrE)
A supply of seeds or tubers set aside or maintained for planting in order to grow future crops.
seeds or tubers reserved for planting

Examples

The farmer purchased high-quality seed stock to ensure a better harvest next season.
It is important to keep the seed stock in a cool, dry place to maintain its viability.
Each year, they select the best potatoes from the crop to use as seed stock for the following year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seed - the grain or ripened ovule of a flowering plant used for sowing

2. NOUN

/ siːd stɑk / (AmE), / siːd stɒk / (BrE)
seeds that have been selected and kept for planting in future crops, often chosen for their quality or desired traits.
seeds kept for future planting

Examples

The farmer always preserves a portion of his best seed stock from each harvest.
Because their seed stock was healthy, the crops grew stronger the following year.
Research facilities often supply growers with certified seed stock for improved yields.
