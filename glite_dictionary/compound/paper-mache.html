paper-mache

1. NOUN

/ ˌpeɪpɚ məˈʃeɪ / (AmE), / ˌpeɪpə məˈʃeɪ / (BrE)
A material made from paper pulp mixed with glue or paste, which can be molded when wet and hardens when dry, often used for making models and art objects.
material made from molded paper pulp

Examples

The sculpture was created entirely out of paper-mache and painted with bright colors.
Children love making masks from paper-mache during art class.
After drying overnight, the paper-mache bowl was sturdy enough to be painted.

Etymology

From French papier mâché, meaning 'chewed paper'.
