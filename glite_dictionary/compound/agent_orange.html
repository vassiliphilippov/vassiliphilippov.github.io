Agent Orange

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪdʒənt ˈɔːrəndʒ / (AmE), / ˈeɪdʒənt ˈɒrɪndʒ / (BrE)
a powerful herbicide and defoliant chemical used by the US military during the Vietnam War, known for causing severe environmental damage and health problems
herbicide used in the Vietnam War

Examples

Exposure to Agent Orange has been linked to serious health issues among veterans.
The forest was devastated after the military sprayed Agent Orange to remove the vegetation cover.
Many organizations are working to help those affected by Agent Orange's lingering effects.

Etymology

Named after the orange-colored stripe on the barrels in which it was stored.
