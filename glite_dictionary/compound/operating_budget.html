operating budget

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːpəˌreɪtɪŋ ˈbʌdʒət / (AmE), / ˈɒpəˌreɪtɪŋ ˈbʌdʒɪt / (BrE)
a financial plan that estimates the ongoing expenses and revenues for the normal functioning of an organization, excluding capital outlays or financial transactions
budget for regular operating expenses

Examples

The company reviewed its operating budget to identify areas where costs could be reduced.
City council approved an operating budget that maintained all essential services for the next fiscal year.
Before launching the new project, the team prepared a detailed operating budget to track expenses.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: capital budget - budget for long-term investments or capital expenditures
