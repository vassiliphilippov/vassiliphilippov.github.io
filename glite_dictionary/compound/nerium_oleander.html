Nerium oleander

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɪriəm oʊliˈændər / (AmE), / ˈnɪəriəm əʊliˈandə / (BrE)
A poisonous evergreen shrub native to the East Indies, widely cultivated for its showy clusters of fragrant white, pink, or red flowers.
a poisonous ornamental shrub with showy flowers

Examples

The garden was bordered by a thick hedge of Nerium oleander in full bloom.
Children should be warned not to touch Nerium oleander due to its toxic properties.
In warmer climates, Nerium oleander is often planted along roadsides for its flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oleander - common name for Nerium oleander, an ornamental poisonous shrub

Etymology

From Latin *Nerium* (from Greek *nērion*), and *oleander*, a variant of 'olea' (olive) with Latin influence.
