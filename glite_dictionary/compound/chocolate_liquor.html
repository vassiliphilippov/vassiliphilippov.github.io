chocolate liquor

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɑk.lət ˈlɪk.ər / (AmE), / ˈtʃɒk.lət ˈlɪk.ə / (BrE)
The thick, dark liquid or paste produced when cacao beans are ground, forming the base for all chocolate products.
paste made from ground cacao beans; chocolate base

Examples

The manufacturing process starts by grinding cacao beans to produce chocolate liquor.
Most dark chocolates contain a high percentage of chocolate liquor, giving them a rich flavor.
Chocolate liquor is the starting point for making both cocoa powder and cocoa butter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cocoa mass - Another term for chocolate liquor, used in chocolate manufacturing
  • Degree Lower: cocoa butter - The fat component separated from chocolate liquor
