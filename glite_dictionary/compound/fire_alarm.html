fire alarm

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪɚ əˌlɑrm / (AmE), / ˈfaɪə əˌlɑːm / (BrE)
a device that makes a loud noise or sends a signal to warn people when fire or smoke is detected
device warning of fire or smoke

Examples

When the fire alarm sounded, everyone evacuated the building immediately.
They tested the fire alarm system to make sure it was working properly.
The smell of smoke triggered the fire alarm late last night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smoke detector - device that senses smoke, usually as an indicator of fire

2. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪɚ əˌlɑrm / (AmE), / ˈfaɪə əˌlɑːm / (BrE)
a device or warning signal (such as a bell or shout) used to alert people that a fire has started
device or signal warning of fire

Examples

When the fire alarm rang, everyone quickly left the building.
Hearing the fire alarm, the students assembled outside as instructed.
She pulled the fire alarm when she saw smoke coming from the kitchen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smoke detector - device that senses smoke and alerts to possible fire
  • Synonym: fire drill - a practice of evacuation procedures in case of fire
