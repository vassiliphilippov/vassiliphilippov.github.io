U.S. Senate

1. NOUN

/ ˌjuː ɛs ˈsɛnət / (AmE), / ˌjuː ɛs ˈsɛnət / (BrE)
the upper chamber of the United States Congress, consisting of two senators from each state, responsible for making and passing laws
upper house of U.S. Congress

Examples

The U.S. Senate approved the bill after hours of debate.
Several new members were elected to the U.S. Senate last November.
The president addressed issues of national security in front of the U.S. Senate.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: House of Representatives - the lower chamber of the United States Congress
