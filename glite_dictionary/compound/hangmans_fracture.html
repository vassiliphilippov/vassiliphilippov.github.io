hangman's fracture

1. NOUN

/ ˈhæŋmænz ˌfræktʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈhæŋmænz ˌfræktʃə / (BrE)
a fracture of the second cervical vertebra (C2) in the neck, caused by forceful hyperextension, often resulting from trauma such as a fall or accident
fracture of the second cervical vertebra (C2)
medical

Examples

The patient suffered a hangman's fracture after a severe car accident.
Emergency physicians are trained to recognize the symptoms of a hangman's fracture.
He was fortunate to recover fully from his hangman's fracture.

Synset Examples

A fall can result in a hangman's fracture.
The doctor diagnosed him with a hangman's fracture.
He learned about hangman's fracture in his anatomy class.

Etymology

Named because the injury resembles the fracture produced by judicial hanging.
