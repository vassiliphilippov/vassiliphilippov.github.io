commercial credit company

1. NOUN

/ kəˈmɜːrʃəl ˈkrɛdɪt ˌkʌmpəni / (AmE), / kəˈmɜːʃl ˈkrɛdɪt ˌkʌmpəni / (BrE)
a finance company that provides loans to manufacturers and wholesalers for business purposes
finance company lending to businesses

Examples

The manufacturer obtained financing from a commercial credit company to expand its production line.
Many wholesalers rely on a commercial credit company rather than a traditional bank for their short-term loans.
A commercial credit company typically assesses business cash flow before approving loans.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: consumer credit company - a finance company that makes loans to individuals rather than businesses
