multiple sclerosis

1. NOUN

/ ˌmʌltəpəl skrəˈloʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌmʌltɪpl̩ skrəˈləʊsɪs / (BrE)
A chronic progressively worsening disease of the nervous system in which the myelin sheath surrounding nerve fibers in the brain and spinal cord is damaged, often resulting in physical and cognitive disability.
chronic nervous system disease
medical

Examples

After being diagnosed with multiple sclerosis, she began receiving regular therapy to manage her symptoms.
People with multiple sclerosis may experience muscle weakness, vision problems, and difficulty walking.
Doctors continue to research new treatments for multiple sclerosis to improve the quality of life for patients.

Etymology

From Latin 'multiplex' (having many parts) and Greek 'sklērōsis' (hardening).
