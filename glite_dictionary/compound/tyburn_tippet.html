Tyburn tippet

1. NOUN

/ ˈtaɪbɚn ˈtɪpɪt / (AmE), / ˈtaɪbən ˈtɪpɪt / (BrE)
a rope used for hangings at Tyburn, historically symbolizing execution and justice in English folklore
historical execution rope; symbol of justice
archaic british

Examples

The condemned man faced the Tyburn tippet with stoic bravery as the crowd watched.
In traditional ballads, mention of the Tyburn tippet evoked dread and respect for the law.
Some old English tales speak of the Tyburn tippet as a chilling symbol of justice.

Synset Examples

He referenced the Tyburn tippet during his historical lecture.
The Tyburn tippet was once used for gallows' ropes in London.
The Tyburn tippet symbolizes justice in old English tales.

Etymology

Tyburn, the site of London's principal gallows, and 'tippet', meaning scarf or band; thus, a rope from Tyburn figuratively worn like a tippet.
