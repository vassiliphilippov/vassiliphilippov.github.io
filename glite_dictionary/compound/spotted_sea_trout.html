spotted sea trout

1. NOUN

/ ˌspɑtɪd si ˈtraʊt / (AmE), / ˌspɒtɪd si ˈtraʊt / (BrE)
A weakfish species (Cynoscion nebulosus) native to the southern Atlantic and Gulf Coasts of the United States, valued both as a game and food fish.
a fish found in southern US coastal waters

Examples

Anglers travel from across the state to catch spotted sea trout in the warm Gulf waters.
The spotted sea trout is prized for its delicate flavor and mild taste.
Conservation efforts are helping restore spotted sea trout populations along the Atlantic coast.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weakfish - a marine fish of the drum family found along the Atlantic coast of North America
