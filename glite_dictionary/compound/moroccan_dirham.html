Moroccan dirham

1. NOUN

/ məˈrɑːkən ˈdɪrˌhæm / (AmE), / məˈrɒkən ˈdɪəˌhæm / (BrE)
the official currency of Morocco, subdivided into 100 centimes
currency of Morocco

Examples

The price of the carpet was listed as 2,000 Moroccan dirhams.
I exchanged my euros for Moroccan dirhams at the airport.
Tourists found it easy to withdraw Moroccan dirhams from local ATMs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dirham - basic monetary unit used in several Arab countries
  • Degree Lower: centime - a monetary subunit equal to 1/100 of a dirham or franc

Etymology

From Arabic 'dirham', itself from Greek 'drachmē', adopted via Latin and Arabic; Moroccan refers to Morocco.
