astronomical unit

1. NOUN

/ ˌæstrəˈnɑmɪkəl ˈjunɪt / (AmE), / ˌæstrəˈnɒmɪkəl ˈjuːnɪt / (BrE)
a unit of length equal to the mean distance between the Earth and the Sun, used chiefly for measuring distances within the solar system (about 93 million miles or 150 million kilometers)
unit of length in astronomy

Examples

Scientists use the astronomical unit to express distances between planets in the solar system.
Jupiter is about 5.2 astronomical units from the Sun.
One astronomical unit equals approximately 150 million kilometers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: light-year - a unit of distance representing the distance light travels in a year
  • Synonym: parsec - a unit of length used in astronomy, about 3.26 light-years

Etymology

From astronomy + unit, referring to measurement in celestial contexts. First attested in the 19th century.
