wood warbler

1. NOUN

/ wʊd ˈwɔrb.lɚ / (AmE), / wʊd ˈwɔː.blə / (BrE)
a small, brightly colored American songbird noted for its weak, unmusical song
small bright American songbird

Examples

The bright feathers of the wood warbler make it easy to spot during spring migration.
Many birdwatchers travel to forests hoping to hear the elusive song of the wood warbler.
Despite its colorful appearance, the wood warbler has a surprisingly weak and simple song.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: warbler - a type of small songbird

2. NOUN

/ wʊd ˈwɔːrlbər / (AmE), / wʊd ˈwɔːləbə / (BrE)
A small European woodland songbird (Phylloscopus sibilatrix) with dull yellow plumage, known for its distinctive trilling song.
European woodland songbird

Examples

We spotted a wood warbler flitting among the branches during our walk in the forest.
The wood warbler is noted for its sweet, trilling song heard in European woodlands.
Conservation efforts have helped increase the wood warbler population in several protected forests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: warbler - a small songbird known for its musical song
