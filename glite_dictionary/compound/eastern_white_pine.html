eastern white pine

1. NOUN

/ ˈistɚn waɪt paɪn / (AmE), / ˈiːstən waɪt paɪn / (BrE)
a tall pine tree native to eastern North America, valued for its timber and distinctive for its brown bark with longitudinal fissures when mature
a tall timber pine of eastern North America

Examples

The eastern white pine once dominated forests across the northeastern United States.
Carpenters prize the wood of the eastern white pine for its straight grain and durability.
We planted an eastern white pine in our backyard to provide shade and shelter for wildlife.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: white pine - a general term for pine trees with pale wood, especially Pinus strobus
