arsenic group

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːrsəˌnɪk ɡruːp / (AmE), / ˈɑːs(ə)nɪk ɡruːp / (BrE)
the univalent chemical group (–AsH2) derived from arsine (AsH3) by removal of one hydrogen atom, forming part of larger compounds.
univalent chemical group from arsine

Examples

The arsenic group can participate in organic reactions similar to the amino group.
Certain organoarsenic compounds contain an arsenic group bonded to carbon atoms.
Researchers investigated the behavior of the arsenic group in various chemical environments.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: arsino group - the bivalent group =AsH derived from arsine

Etymology

From 'arsenic', ultimately from Greek 'arsenikon', and 'group' in the sense of a set of atoms functioning as a single unit in compounds.
