Indus River

1. PROPN

/ ˈɪndəs ˈrɪvər / (AmE), / ˈɪndəs ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
a major river in South Asia rising in Tibet, flowing through India, Kashmir, and Pakistan to the Arabian Sea
a major river in South Asia

Examples

The ancient cities of Mohenjo-daro and Harappa developed along the Indus River.
Seasonal floods from the Indus River support agriculture in the region.
The Indus River is a vital water source for millions of people in Pakistan.

Synset Examples

the valley of the Indus was the site of an early civilization

Etymology

From the Latin 'Indus', from Ancient Greek 'Ἰνδός' (Indós), from Old Persian 'Hinduš'.
