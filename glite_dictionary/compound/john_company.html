John Company

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɑn ˌkʌmpəni / (AmE), / ˈdʒɒn ˌkʌmpəni / (BrE)
A historic, often informal name for the East India Company, a British trading company established in the 17th century.
informal name for the East India Company

Examples

During the 19th century, John Company played a central role in the administration of India.
Many fortunes were made through investments in John Company.
The legacy of John Company is still discussed by historians today.

Synset Examples

John Company announced its merger with a rival firm.

Etymology

From the English given name 'John' (used generically) and 'Company', used colloquially in British India for the East India Company.
