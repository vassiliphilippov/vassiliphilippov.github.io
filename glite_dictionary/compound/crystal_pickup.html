crystal pickup

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɪstl ˈpɪkˌʌp / (AmE), / ˈkrɪstl ˈpɪkʌp / (BrE)
A type of phonograph cartridge that produces an output voltage through the vibration of a piezoelectric crystal.
phonograph cartridge using a piezoelectric crystal

Examples

The vintage record player uses a crystal pickup to read the grooves on the vinyl.
Replacing a faulty crystal pickup can greatly improve the sound quality of an old turntable.
Unlike magnetic cartridges, a crystal pickup generates voltage directly from the crystal's vibrations.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: magnetic pickup - Phonograph pickup using a magnetic mechanism

Etymology

From 'crystal,' referring to the piezoelectric crystal inside, and 'pickup,' meaning an audio cartridge that converts mechanical vibrations to electrical signals.
