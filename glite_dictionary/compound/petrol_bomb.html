petrol bomb

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛtɹəl bɑm / (AmE), / ˈpɛtɹəl bɒm / (BrE)
A crude incendiary device made using a glass bottle filled with flammable liquid and a rag wick, typically ignited and thrown.
homemade incendiary weapon using flammable liquid

Examples

The rioters hurled a petrol bomb at the police van during the violent protest.
Making or possessing a petrol bomb is illegal in many countries.
He suffered burns after a petrol bomb exploded near his home.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Molotov cocktail - another term for an improvised incendiary bomb made from a bottle of flammable liquid
