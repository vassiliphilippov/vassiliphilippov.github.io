American bittersweet

1. NOUN

/ əˈmɛrɪkən ˈbɪtərˌswit / (AmE), / əˈmɛrɪkən ˈbɪtəˌswiːt / (BrE)
A North American twining shrub (Celastrus scandens) with yellow capsules that enclose scarlet seeds.
North American shrub with yellow capsules and scarlet seeds

Examples

The brilliant scarlet seeds of the American bittersweet make it a popular choice for winter decorations.
We found a tangled vine of American bittersweet growing along the old fence line.
Gardeners often plant American bittersweet for its colorful berries and climbing habit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bittersweet - woody vine with orange fruit capsules and red seeds (Celastrus genus)
