Meuse River

1. PROPN

/ mjuz ˈrɪvər / (AmE), / mjuːz ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
a major river in Western Europe that flows through France, Belgium, and the Netherlands before emptying into the North Sea
river in Western Europe

Examples

The Meuse River winds its way through several countries, making it an important waterway for transportation.
Fishing along the Meuse River is a popular activity in Belgium and the Netherlands.
Historic towns line the banks of the Meuse River, each with their own unique charm.

Etymology

From French 'Meuse', from Latin 'Mosa'.
