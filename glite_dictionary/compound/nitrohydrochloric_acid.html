nitrohydrochloric acid

1. NOUN

/ ˌnaɪtroʊˌhaɪdrəkləˈrɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / ˌnɪtrəʊˌhaɪdrəkləˈrɪk ˈæsɪd / (BrE)
a yellow, fuming, highly corrosive mixture of nitric and hydrochloric acids, used especially for dissolving metals such as gold and platinum
mixture of nitric and hydrochloric acids

Examples

The laboratory technician used nitrohydrochloric acid to dissolve the gold samples for analysis.
Because of its ability to dissolve noble metals, nitrohydrochloric acid is vital in refining processes.
Always handle nitrohydrochloric acid with care, as its fumes are both toxic and corrosive.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aqua regia - another name for nitrohydrochloric acid

Etymology

From 'nitro-' (relating to nitric acid) and 'hydrochloric acid', referring to its components.
