Big Bang theory

1. NOUN

/ bɪɡ bæŋ ˈθɪəri / (AmE), / bɪɡ bæŋ ˈθɪəri / (BrE)
the scientific theory that the universe began from a rapid expansion of a very dense and hot state, marking the origin of space and time
cosmological theory of the universe's origin

Examples

According to the Big Bang theory, the universe began as an infinitely dense point.
The Big Bang theory is supported by evidence such as the cosmic microwave background radiation.
Many scientists consider the Big Bang theory the most plausible explanation for the origin of the universe.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: steady state theory - the now largely discredited theory that the universe has always existed in a steady state

Etymology

From 'Big Bang', a term coined in 1949 by British scientist Fred Hoyle (originally sarcastic) and 'theory'.
