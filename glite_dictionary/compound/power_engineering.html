power engineering

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaʊ.ɚ ˌɛn.dʒəˈnɪr.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpaʊ.ə ˌɛn.dʒɪˈnɪə.rɪŋ / (BrE)
the branch of engineering focused on the generation, transmission, distribution, and utilization of electric power, and the design and operation of related electrical devices such as generators and transformers
engineering of electric power systems

Examples

She decided to pursue a master's degree in power engineering to specialize in energy systems design.
Advances in power engineering have enabled the integration of renewable energy sources into the grid.
The university offers cutting-edge research opportunities in power engineering, focusing on smart grids and sustainable power.

Synset Examples

There is also a severe shortage of power engineering expertise in other sectors, such as government bodies and finance organizations, where a detailed knowledge of energy supply and demand is increasingly important as societies develop and adapt to pressing environmental and economic constraints, dwindling reserves of fossil fuels and the emergence of new energy technologies.
