soap opera

1. NOUN

/ soʊp ˈɑːpərə / (AmE), / səʊp ˈɒpərə / (BrE)
a television or radio drama series that focuses on the daily lives and personal relationships of a group of characters, often featuring melodramatic or sentimental storylines
serialized melodramatic drama series

Examples

My grandmother never misses her favorite soap opera every afternoon at 2 p.m.
The plot twists in that soap opera are so outrageous that it’s hard to believe.
He landed a small role on a popular soap opera right after graduating from acting school.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drama - a play for theatre, radio, or television
  • Synonym: telenovela - a type of serial drama or soap opera produced in Latin America

Etymology

Originally sponsored by soap manufacturers, hence the term 'soap opera'.
