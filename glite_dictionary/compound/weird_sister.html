weird sister

1. NOUN

/ wɪrd ˈsɪstɚ / (AmE), / wɪəd ˈsɪstə / (BrE)
(in myth and literature) any of the three goddesses of fate or destiny, especially as represented in Norse or Anglo-Saxon tradition; also used for the witches in Shakespeare's Macbeth.
goddess of fate in Norse myth; Shakespearean witch

Examples

In Norse legend, each weird sister controlled a different aspect of human destiny.
The three weird sisters in Macbeth predict Macbeth’s rise and fall.
Ancient beliefs in the weird sister figures influenced later literature and folklore.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fate - the power believed to control events
  • Synonym: moirae - the Fates in Greek mythology
  • Synonym: parcae - the Fates in Roman mythology

Etymology

From Middle English 'werd' or 'wyrd' (fate, destiny), and 'sister'; influenced by Old English 'wyrd' (fate).
