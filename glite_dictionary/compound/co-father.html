co-father

1. NOUN

/ koʊˈfɑːðər / (AmE), / kəʊˈfɑːðə / (BrE)
A man who shares parental responsibilities with another, especially not necessarily the biological father.
man sharing parental duties

Examples

He was honored to be chosen as co-father during the baptism ceremony.
Modern families often include a co-father who is actively involved in raising the child.
They agreed to act as co-fathers and share all parenting responsibilities equally.

Synset Examples

He was named co-father in the child's baptism.
Being a co-father means sharing parenting responsibilities.
She asked him to be a co-father to her son.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: co-parent - A person who shares parental duties with another

Etymology

Formed from 'co-' (together, jointly) and 'father'.
