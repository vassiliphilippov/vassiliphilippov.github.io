seventh chord

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛvənθ tʃɔrd / (AmE), / ˈsɛvənθ tʃɔːd / (BrE)
A musical chord consisting of a triad with an added seventh note above the root.
musical chord with added seventh

Examples

In jazz music, the seventh chord adds depth and complexity to the harmony.
The composer used a seventh chord to create tension before the final resolution.
Learning to play each type of seventh chord is essential for advanced pianists.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: triad - a chord consisting of three notes, typically a root, third, and fifth
