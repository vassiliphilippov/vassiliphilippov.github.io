tineoid moth

1. NOUN

/ taɪˈniːɔɪd mɒθ / (AmE), / tɪˈniːɔɪd mɒθ / (BrE)
a small, dull-colored moth with chewing mouthparts, typically belonging to the superfamily Tineoidea
small dull-colored moth with chewing mouthparts

Examples

The scientist examined the tineoid moth under a microscope to study its unusual mouthparts.
Several tineoid moths were attracted to the light in the laboratory overnight.
Unlike most butterflies, a tineoid moth has chewing mouthparts instead of a proboscis.

Etymology

From Latin *Tinea* (a genus of moths) + Greek -oid (resembling or like).
