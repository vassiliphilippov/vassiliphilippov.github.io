string cheese

1. NOUN

/ strɪŋ tʃiz / (AmE), / strɪŋ tʃiːz / (BrE)
a type of cheese, typically mozzarella, formed in long strings that can be pulled apart and eaten as strips
cheese in long, peelable strings

Examples

My son loves to eat string cheese as a snack after school.
She packed some string cheese in her lunchbox for a quick source of protein.
You can peel string cheese into thin strips and add it to salads or sandwiches.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cheese - a food made from the pressed curds of milk
