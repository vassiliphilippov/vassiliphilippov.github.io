long-fin tunny

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɔŋ fɪn ˈtʌni / (AmE), / ˈlɒŋ fɪn ˈtʌni / (BrE)
A large species of tuna with long pectoral fins, found worldwide in warm waters and widely used for canned tuna.
a species of tuna with long fins

Examples

The long-fin tunny is a key species for the global canned tuna industry.
Fishermen reported catching several long-fin tunny off the coast this season.
The distinctive long pectoral fins help to identify a long-fin tunny in the open ocean.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: albacore - another name for the long-fin tunny, a species of tuna
