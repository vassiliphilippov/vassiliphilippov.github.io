independent clause

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪndɪˈpɛndənt klɔz / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˈpɛndənt klɑːz / (BrE)
a clause that can stand alone as a complete sentence and expresses a complete thought
clause that can stand alone as a sentence

Examples

An independent clause can function as a complete sentence on its own.
Every sentence must have at least one independent clause to make sense.
‘She laughed’ is an example of an independent clause.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dependent clause - a clause that cannot stand alone as a complete sentence
