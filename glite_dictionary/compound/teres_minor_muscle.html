teres minor muscle

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛr.iːz ˈmaɪ.nɚ ˈmʌs.əl / (AmE), / ˈtɛr.iːz ˈmaɪ.nə ˈmʌs.əl / (BrE)
a small, narrow muscle of the upper arm that helps adduct and rotate the arm laterally
upper arm muscle, aids rotation and adduction
medical

Examples

The teres minor muscle plays a crucial role in shoulder stability during throwing motions.
A strain in the teres minor muscle can cause pain when lifting the arm outward.
Physical therapists often focus on strengthening the teres minor muscle after a shoulder injury.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: teres major muscle - a larger muscle next to the teres minor involved in arm movements

Etymology

From Latin teres (rounded, smooth) and minor (lesser), referring to the muscle's shape and size.
