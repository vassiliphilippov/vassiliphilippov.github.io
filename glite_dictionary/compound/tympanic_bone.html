tympanic bone

1. NOUN

/ tɪmˈpænɪk boʊn / (AmE), / tɪmˈpænɪk bəʊn / (BrE)
the bone that forms part of the enclosure of the middle ear, especially in some mammals
bone forming part of middle ear enclosure
medical

Examples

The tympanic bone is essential in protecting sensitive structures of the middle ear.
Damage to the tympanic bone can affect hearing in mammals.
The anatomy textbook described how the tympanic bone develops during embryonic growth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: auditory bulla - the bony structure enclosing parts of the middle and inner ear in some mammals

Etymology

From Greek *tumpanon* (drum) + bone, referring to its drum-like enclosure of the ear.
