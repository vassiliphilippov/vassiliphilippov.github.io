sweating sickness

1. NOUN

/ ˈswɛtɪŋ ˌsɪknəs / (AmE), / ˈswetɪŋ ˌsɪknəs / (BrE)
a highly fatal epidemic disease of the 15th and 16th centuries, characterized by sudden onset of intense sweating and high mortality
historical epidemic disease marked by profuse sweating

Examples

Historians believe that sweating sickness first appeared in England in the late 15th century.
Many people feared the rapid spread of sweating sickness during outbreaks in Tudor times.
The exact cause of sweating sickness remains a mystery to modern scientists.

2. NOUN

/ ˈswɛtɪŋ ˌsɪknəs / (AmE), / ˈswetɪŋ ˌsɪknəs / (BrE)
A disease affecting cattle, especially calves, characterized by fever, sweating, and skin lesions.
disease of cattle, causes sweating and fever

Examples

Farmers became worried when several calves developed sweating sickness during the summer.
Early diagnosis of sweating sickness is crucial to prevent further losses in the herd.
The veterinarian explained that sweating sickness is often caused by toxins from certain ticks.
