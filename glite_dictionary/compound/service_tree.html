service tree

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɜːrvɪs triː / (AmE), / ˈsɜːvɪs triː / (BrE)
A medium-sized European tree related to the rowan, with edible fruit.
European tree with edible fruit

Examples

The ancient service tree stood at the edge of the orchard, its branches heavy with fruit.
Many birds are attracted to the fruits of the service tree in early autumn.
The wood from the service tree is prized for making fine furniture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rowan - a small deciduous tree with clusters of orange berries

Etymology

From Old English *syrfe*, from Latin *sorbus* (rowan, service tree).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɝːvɪs triː / (AmE), / ˈsɜːvɪs triː / (BrE)
a North American tree or shrub with showy white flowers and edible blue-black or purplish fruit, especially species of Amelanchier
North American tree/shrub with white flowers and edible fruit

Examples

In the forest, a cluster of service trees stood out with their brilliant white blossoms.
Birds flocked to the service tree to eat its ripe, purplish fruit in late summer.
The service tree is often planted in gardens for both its flowers and edible berries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Amelanchier - genus of small trees and shrubs known as serviceberry or juneberry
