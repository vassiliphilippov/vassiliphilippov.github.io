sanwa millet

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɑːnwɑː ˈmɪlɪt / (AmE), / ˈsænwə ˈmɪlɪt / (BrE)
A coarse annual grass grown in Japan and southeastern Asia for its edible seeds and as forage; also used as a wildlife food source in the United States.
a grass cultivated for its edible seeds and forage

Examples

Farmers in eastern Asia cultivate sanwa millet for both its seeds and use as animal feed.
The wildlife reserve manager planted sanwa millet to attract and sustain local bird populations.
In some regions, sanwa millet is preferred over other grains due to its adaptability to varying soil conditions.
