skirt of tasses

1. NOUN

/ skɝt əv tæzəz / (AmE), / skɜːt əv tæsɪz / (BrE)
A series of overlapping armor plates worn below the waist for protection.
armor plates protecting below the waist

Examples

The knight's skirt of tasses was dented during the fierce battle.
A skirt of tasses provided additional protection for soldiers in heavy armor.
Museum exhibits of medieval armor often feature a complete skirt of tasses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fauld - armor protecting the lower torso, often consisting of horizontal lames

Etymology

From 'skirt', meaning a covering or protective garment, and 'tasse' (plural 'tasses'), from French, meaning a piece of plate armor.
