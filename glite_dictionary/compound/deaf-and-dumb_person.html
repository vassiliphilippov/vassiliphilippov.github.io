deaf-and-dumb person

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɛf ənd ˈdʌm ˈpɜrsən / (AmE), / ˌdɛf ənd ˈdʌm ˈpɜːsən / (BrE)
(Archaic, offensive) A person who is deaf and also unable to speak; term now considered outdated and offensive.
a deaf person unable to speak (archaic, offensive)
archaic offensive

Examples

The term deaf-and-dumb person is now considered both inaccurate and offensive in modern usage.
In Victorian literature, characters described as a deaf-and-dumb person are often portrayed with sympathy.
The label deaf-and-dumb person was once common but is no longer acceptable in contemporary discussions about disability.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deaf person - a person who is unable to hear
