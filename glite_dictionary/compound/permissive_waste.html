permissive waste

1. NOUN

/ pərˈmɪsɪv weɪst / (AmE), / pəˈmɪsɪv weɪst / (BrE)
(Law) Loss in the value of property, especially real estate, caused by the tenant's omission or neglect, rather than intentional damage.
loss of property value due to neglect (law)
legal

Examples

The landlord claimed the tenant was responsible for permissive waste after years of neglect.
Failure to repair the leaking roof resulted in permissive waste to the estate.
Under the lease agreement, the tenant could be liable for permissive waste if maintenance was ignored.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: voluntary waste - intentional or deliberate damage to property by the tenant

Etymology

From legal use of 'waste' meaning 'damage or alteration of property' and 'permissive' for 'allowed by neglect' (Middle English and Latin roots).
