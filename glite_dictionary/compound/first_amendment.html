First Amendment

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɝst əˈmɛnd.mənt / (AmE), / ˈfɜːst əˈmɛnd.mənt / (BrE)
the first amendment to the U.S. Constitution, guaranteeing freedoms concerning religion, expression, assembly, and the right to petition
the U.S. constitutional amendment guaranteeing key freedoms
legal american

Examples

The First Amendment protects the freedom of speech in the United States.
Journalists often cite the First Amendment when discussing press freedoms.
The Supreme Court has ruled on many cases involving the First Amendment.
