external iliac vein

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˈstɝnəl ˈɪliˌæk veɪn / (AmE), / ɪkˈstɜːnəl ˈɪliˌæk veɪn / (BrE)
a large vein in the pelvis that continues from the femoral vein and joins the internal iliac vein to form the common iliac vein
major pelvic vein
medical

Examples

During the operation, the surgeon carefully located the external iliac vein to avoid damaging it.
A blockage in the external iliac vein can cause serious circulation problems in the leg.
The external iliac vein is often examined in cases of suspected deep vein thrombosis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: internal iliac vein - the vein that joins with the external iliac vein to form the common iliac vein
  • Degree Higher: common iliac vein - the vein formed by the union of the external and internal iliac veins

Etymology

From Latin 'externus' (outer), 'iliacus' (relating to the ilium), and 'vena' (vein).
