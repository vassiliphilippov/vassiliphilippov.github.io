nitrous bacterium

1. NOUN

/ ˈnaɪtrəs bækˈtɪriəm / (AmE), / ˈnaɪtrəs bækˈtɪəriəm / (BrE)
a bacterium that oxidizes ammonia into nitrites, playing a role in the nitrogen cycle
bacterium that oxidizes ammonia into nitrites

Examples

The nitrous bacterium converts ammonia in the soil into nitrites as part of the nitrogen cycle.
Under laboratory conditions, colonies of nitrous bacterium were observed multiplying rapidly.
Farmers rely on the action of nitrous bacterium to improve soil fertility for crop growth.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: nitrobacterium - a bacterium that oxidizes nitrites into nitrates

Etymology

From 'nitrous', relating to nitrogen compounds, and 'bacterium', from Greek baktērion (small staff).
