concert pitch

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑn.sɚt pɪtʃ / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.sət pɪtʃ / (BrE)
the standard pitch to which musical instruments are tuned for performances, typically setting the A above middle C to 440 Hz
standard musical tuning frequency

Examples

All the instruments in the orchestra were tuned to concert pitch before the performance began.
The violinist checked that her A string matched the concert pitch provided by the piano.
Some Baroque ensembles use a lower concert pitch than modern orchestras.
