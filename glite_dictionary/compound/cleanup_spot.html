cleanup spot

1. NOUN

/ ˈklinʌp spɑt / (AmE), / ˈkliːnʌp spɒt / (BrE)
(Baseball) The fourth position in the batting order, usually occupied by a team's best power hitter, who is expected to drive in runs.
fourth position in batting order (baseball)

Examples

He always bats in the cleanup spot because of his ability to hit home runs.
The coach decided to move her to the cleanup spot after last night's impressive performance.
Hitting in the cleanup spot comes with a lot of responsibility for scoring runs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cleanup hitter - the player who bats in the fourth position in the order

Etymology

From "cleanup" (referring to driving in base runners, or "cleaning up" the bases) + "spot" (place in the order).
