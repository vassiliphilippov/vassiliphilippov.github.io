Hanoverian line

1. PROPN

/ ˌhænəˈvɪriən laɪn / (AmE), / ˌhanəˈvɪərɪən laɪn / (BrE)
the English royal house that ruled from 1714 to 1901, beginning with George I and ending with Queen Victoria
English royal house (1714–1901)

Examples

The Hanoverian line began its reign in 1714 with the ascension of George I to the British throne.
Many historical changes occurred during the rule of the Hanoverian line, including the Industrial Revolution.
Queen Victoria marked the end of the Hanoverian line when she died in 1901.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: House of Hanover - English royal dynasty of German origin, same as Hanoverian line
  • Antonym: House of Stuart - the preceding English royal house before the Hanoverians
  • Antonym: House of Saxe-Coburg and Gotha - the royal house succeeding the Hanoverian line in Britain

Etymology

From Hanover, the German city and former electorate, home of the dynasty's founders.
