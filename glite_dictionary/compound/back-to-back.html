back-to-back

1. ADJ

/ ˌbæk tə ˈbæk / (AmE), / ˌbæk tə ˈbæk / (BrE)
Happening one after another in a sequence, without interruption.
consecutive; one after the other

Examples

The team won three back-to-back games last week, setting a new record.
He scheduled two back-to-back meetings in the morning with barely any break in between.
We have a series of back-to-back appointments all afternoon.

Synset Examples

back-to-back home runs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: consecutive - following one after another without interruption

2. ADJ

/ ˌbæk tə ˈbæk / (AmE), / ˌbæk tə ˈbæk / (BrE)
(of events, items, etc.) happening one after another without a break or interruption.
consecutive; happening one after another

Examples

We watched three back-to-back movies on Friday night.
The team won five back-to-back games this season.
She scheduled back-to-back meetings all afternoon and had no time for a break.

Synset Examples

The concert featured back-to-back-to-back performances by various artists.
We watched three back-to-back-to-back episodes of our favorite show.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: consecutive - following one after another in order
