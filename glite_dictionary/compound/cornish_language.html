Cornish language

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔrnɪʃ ˈlæŋgwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈkɔːnɪʃ ˈlaŋgwɪdʒ / (BrE)
a Celtic language that originated in Cornwall, spoken historically and undergoing modern revival
Celtic language of Cornwall

Examples

Interest in the Cornish language has increased in recent years as part of regional revival efforts.
She learned to speak the Cornish language to reconnect with her family's heritage.
The book provides an introduction to the grammar and vocabulary of the Cornish language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Welsh language - another Brythonic Celtic language
  • Synonym: Breton language - another Brythonic Celtic language spoken in Brittany

Etymology

Cornish from Cornwall, England; 'language' from Latin 'lingua'.
