body waste

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑdi weɪst / (AmE), / ˈbɒdi weɪst / (BrE)
waste matter, such as urine, sweat, or especially feces, that is discharged from the body.
waste matter excreted from the body

Examples

Medical staff collected samples of body waste to test for infection.
Proper disposal of body waste is essential for maintaining hygiene in the hospital.
The study focused on how the body eliminates different types of body waste.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: excreta - waste matter eliminated from the body
  • Degree Lower: feces - solid waste discharged from the digestive tract
  • Degree Lower: urine - liquid waste discharged by the kidneys
