out loud

1. ADV

/ aʊt laʊd / (AmE), / aʊt laʊd / (BrE)
spoken or expressed using the voice, not silently or in one's head
spoken with the voice; not silently

Examples

She couldn't help but laugh out loud at the joke.
Please read your answer out loud so everyone can hear.
He accidentally said his thoughts out loud during the meeting.

Synset Examples

please read the passage aloud
he laughed out loud

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aloud - spoken using the voice, not silently
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00070171-r
Synset POS: r

2. ADV

/ aʊt laʊd / (AmE), / aʊt laʊd / (BrE)
spoken so as to be heard by others; audibly, not silently
spoken audibly, not silently

Examples

He couldn't help but laugh out loud at the joke.
Please read the instructions out loud for everyone to hear.
She often thinks out loud when solving difficult problems.

Synset Examples

He read the poem out loud to the class.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aloud - spoken audibly; not silently

WordNet ID: n/a
Synset POS: None
