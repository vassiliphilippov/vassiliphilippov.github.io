methylated spirit

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛθəˌleɪtɪd ˈspɪrɪt / (AmE), / ˈmiːθɪleɪtɪd ˈspɪrɪt / (BrE)
ethyl alcohol that has been made undrinkable by adding methyl alcohol or other chemicals, used chiefly as a solvent or fuel
denatured alcohol used as solvent or fuel

Examples

You can clean glass surfaces effectively with methylated spirit.
In some countries, methylated spirit is used as a fuel for camping stoves.
It is important to store methylated spirit out of reach of children due to its toxicity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: denatured alcohol - alcohol made unfit to drink by adding chemicals

Etymology

From 'methylate' (to introduce a methyl group into) and 'spirit' (alcohol), historically referring to ethanol mixed with methanol.
