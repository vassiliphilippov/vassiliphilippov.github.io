pyruvic acid

1. NOUN

/ paɪˈruːvɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / paɪˈruːvɪk ˈasɪd / (BrE)
a colorless organic acid, formed as an important intermediate in metabolic and fermentation processes in living organisms
metabolic intermediate acid
medical

Examples

After glycolysis, glucose is broken down into pyruvic acid in the cytoplasm of the cell.
Under anaerobic conditions, pyruvic acid is converted into lactic acid.
Researchers measured the concentration of pyruvic acid during the fermentation process.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: lactic acid - an organic acid produced during anaerobic respiration

Etymology

From French *pyruvique*, from 'pyro-' (fire) and Latin *uva* (grape), because it was first found in the distillation of tartaric acid from grapes.
