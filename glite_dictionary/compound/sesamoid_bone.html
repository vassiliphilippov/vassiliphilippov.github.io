sesamoid bone

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛs.əˌmɔɪd boʊn / (AmE), / ˈsɛs.əmɔɪd bəʊn / (BrE)
a small, round bone formed within a tendon, typically where the tendon passes over a joint, such as the kneecap (patella)
small bone in a tendon at a joint
medical

Examples

The patella is the largest sesamoid bone in the human body.
Doctors discovered a fracture in the sesamoid bone of her foot after the injury.
Some athletes experience pain caused by inflammation of a sesamoid bone near the big toe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: patella - the kneecap; the largest sesamoid bone in the body

Etymology

From Greek sēsamon (sesame) + eidos (form), from the resemblance to a sesame seed.
