myrsine family

1. NOUN

/ mɪrˈsaɪn ˌfæməli / (AmE), / mɪˈsaɪni ˌfæmɪli / (BrE)
A family of mostly Old World tropical trees and shrubs, some of which are found in Florida, scientifically known as Myrsinaceae.
family of tropical trees and shrubs (Myrsinaceae)

Examples

Several species in the myrsine family are valued for their ornamental foliage.
Botanists discovered a new shrub from the myrsine family in the rainforests of Southeast Asia.
Some members of the myrsine family have medicinal uses in traditional practices.

Etymology

From New Latin Myrsinaceae, based on the genus Myrsine, from Greek 'myrsinē' meaning 'myrtle'.
