Romany language

1. NOUN

/ ˈroʊ.mə.ni ˈlæŋ.ɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈrəʊ.məni ˈlæŋ.ɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
The language of the Romani people, belonging to the Indo-Aryan branch of the Indo-European family, spoken by Romani communities throughout Europe and beyond.
language of the Romani people

Examples

The Romany language has many dialects that vary between different regions of Europe.
He studied the Romany language to better understand Romani culture and traditions.
Few people outside the community are fluent in the Romany language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Romany - the language spoken by the Romani people

Etymology

From Romani 'Romani čhib' (Romani tongue), ultimately from Sanskrit 'ḍoma' (a man of low caste, musician).
