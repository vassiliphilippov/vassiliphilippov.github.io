semi-upright

1. ADJ

/ ˌsɛmiˈʌpˌraɪt / (AmE), / ˌsɛmiˈʌp.raɪt / (BrE)
(of animals) having a posture that is partly but not fully upright.
partly upright in posture (of animals)

Examples

The animal adopted a semi-upright stance to look over the grass.
Some dinosaurs were believed to walk in a semi-upright position.
In this species, juveniles often move in a semi-upright manner.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: upright - fully erect in posture

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'semi-' (meaning 'half' or 'partly') and 'upright'.
