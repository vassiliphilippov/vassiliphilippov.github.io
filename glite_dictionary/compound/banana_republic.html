banana republic

1. NOUN

/ bəˈnænə rɪˈpʌblɪk / (AmE), / bəˈnænə rɪˈpʌblɪk / (BrE)
a small, politically unstable country whose economy is largely dependent on a single export commodity and is often controlled by foreign interests.
unstable country dominated by foreign interests

Examples

After the coup, the country quickly became known internationally as a banana republic.
His novel satirizes the corruption and foreign interference typical of a banana republic.
Many economists argue that diversified exports are crucial to avoid becoming a banana republic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: client state - country that is economically or politically dependent on a more powerful nation
  • Degree Higher: failed state - a state whose political or economic system has become so weak that the government is no longer in control

Etymology

Originally applied to Central American countries whose economies depended on banana exports controlled by foreign corporations.

2. NOUN

/ bəˈnænə rɪˈpʌblɪk / (AmE), / bəˈnænə rɪˈpʌblɪk / (BrE)
A small country with a weak and corrupt government that depends on one main export, often subject to exploitation by foreign interests.
politically unstable, export-dependent country

Examples

The country's fragile economy left it vulnerable to being labeled a banana republic by foreign observers.
Critics argue that heavy reliance on coffee exports has turned the nation into a banana republic.
His novel explores the intrigues and coup attempts within a fictional banana republic.

Synset Examples

He called the corrupt government a banana republic.
The term banana republic describes unstable countries.
The novel is set in a fictional banana republic.

Etymology

Originated in early 20th-century American English, popularized by author O. Henry; from the dependence on banana exports in some countries.
