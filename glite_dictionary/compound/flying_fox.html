flying fox

1. NOUN

/ ˈflaɪɪŋ ˌfɑks / (AmE), / ˈflaɪɪŋ ˌfɒks / (BrE)
a large fruit-eating bat of the genus Pteropus, with a foxlike face and long wings, native to tropical regions of Asia, Australia, and East Africa
large fruit bat resembling a fox

Examples

The flying fox can have a wingspan of up to 1.5 meters, making it one of the largest bats.
Conservation efforts are underway to protect the endangered flying fox species in Asia.
A colony of flying foxes was spotted roosting high up in the trees near the river.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fruit bat - any of several bats that eat fruit, including flying foxes

Etymology

From the bat's foxlike appearance and its ability to fly; 'fox' from Old English 'fox'.
