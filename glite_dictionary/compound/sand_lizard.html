sand lizard

1. NOUN

/ sænd ˈlɪz.ɚd / (AmE), / sænd ˈlɪz.əd / (BrE)
a lizard commonly found in arid regions of the western United States, known for its sandy coloration and adaptation to dry habitats
a lizard from arid western U.S. regions

Examples

While hiking in Nevada, we spotted a sand lizard basking on a warm rock.
The sand lizard is well-camouflaged against the desert terrain.
Young naturalists studied the behavior of the sand lizard in its native habitat.

2. NOUN

/ sænd ˈlɪzərd / (AmE), / sand ˈlɪzəd / (BrE)
a common lizard (Lacerta agilis) found across Europe and central Asia, typically inhabiting sandy heathlands and grasslands
a common European and Asian lizard

Examples

The sand lizard is a protected species in several European countries due to habitat loss.
We spotted a colorful sand lizard basking on the dunes during our walk.
Female sand lizards lay their eggs in warm, sandy soil in early summer.
