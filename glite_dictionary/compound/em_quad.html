em quad

1. NOUN

/ ɛm kwɑd / (AmE), / ɛm kwɒd / (BrE)
a piece of metal used in printing with a square body, whose width is equal to the point size of the type; used for spacing in typesetting.
typographical space equal to type size

Examples

The printer inserted an em quad between the paragraphs to create extra space.
In traditional typesetting, an em quad provides a visual pause or indentation.
Because an em quad is a perfect square, it is easy to measure and use in layouts.

Synset Examples

since ‘em quad’ is hard to distinguish from ‘en quad’, printers sometimes called it a ‘mutton quad’

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: en quad - a typesetting space half the width of an em quad

Etymology

From 'em', referring to the width of the letter 'M' in a given size, and 'quad', short for 'quadrat', a spacing piece in printing.
