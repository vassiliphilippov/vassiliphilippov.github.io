common staghorn fern

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmən ˈstæɡˌhɔrn fɜrn / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən ˈstæɡˌhɔːn fɜːn / (BrE)
a species of fern native to Australia, southeastern Asia, and Polynesia, widely cultivated for its distinctive, antler-shaped fronds
a widely cultivated fern with antler-shaped fronds

Examples

The common staghorn fern is popular among gardeners for its dramatic foliage.
She attached a common staghorn fern to a wooden board to mount on the wall.
Many tropical gardens feature the common staghorn fern as a decorative plant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: staghorn fern - a type of fern with fronds shaped like stag antlers
