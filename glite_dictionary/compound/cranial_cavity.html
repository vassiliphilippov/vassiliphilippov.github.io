cranial cavity

1. NOUN

/ ˈkreɪniəl ˈkævəti / (AmE), / ˈkreɪniəl ˈkævəti / (BrE)
the space within the skull that houses the brain
space inside the skull housing the brain
medical

Examples

The brain is protected within the cranial cavity of the skull.
A fracture to the skull can cause bleeding inside the cranial cavity.
The surgeon carefully examined the cranial cavity for signs of swelling.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cranium - the skull, especially the part enclosing the brain

Etymology

From 'cranial', relating to the cranium (the skull), and 'cavity', meaning a hollow space.
