mock orange

1. NOUN

/ mɑk ˈɔrɪndʒ / (AmE), / mɒk ˈɒrɪndʒ / (BrE)
A thorny deciduous shrub or small tree of the southeastern United States, noted for its white flowers and small black fruit.
thorny shrub/tree with white flowers, southeastern US

Examples

The garden's mock orange was in full bloom, filling the air with a sweet fragrance.
Many birds nest in the dense branches of the mock orange tree.
We planted a mock orange along the fence to add some spring color to the yard.

Etymology

Named for its resemblance to true orange blossoms.

2. NOUN

/ mɑk ˈɔrɪndʒ / (AmE), / mɒk ˈɒrɪndʒ / (BrE)
A small deciduous shrub or tree of the southern United States, with spines, glossy dark green leaves, inedible orange-like fruit, and hard orange-colored wood historically used for bows.
ornamental shrub with orange-like fruit

Examples

The dense branches of the mock orange make it popular as a windbreak and garden hedge.
Native Americans used the wood of the mock orange to craft sturdy bows.
The mock orange produces fragrant white flowers and round fruits that resemble small oranges.

Etymology

Named for the resemblance of its fruit and flowers to those of the orange tree.

3. NOUN

/ mɑk ˈɔrɪndʒ / (AmE), / mɒk ˈɒrɪndʒ / (BrE)
A large, hardy shrub with showy, strongly fragrant creamy-white flowers arranged in short terminal clusters, widely grown as an ornamental plant.
fragrant ornamental shrub with white flowers

Examples

The mock orange in our garden fills the air with its sweet scent each spring.
She planted a mock orange beside the patio for its beautiful, fragrant flowers.
Every June, the mock orange blooms, creating a stunning display of creamy-white blossoms.

Etymology

Named for its similarity in fragrance and appearance to the orange blossom, though not botanically related.

flowering evergreen shrub or tree with fragrant white flowers

Examples
