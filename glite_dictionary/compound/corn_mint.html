corn mint

1. NOUN

/ kɔrn mɪnt / (AmE), / kɔːn mɪnt / (BrE)
a European species of mint (Mentha arvensis), now also naturalized in the United States, with aromatic leaves used for flavoring or medicinal purposes
aromatic European mint species

Examples

The leaves of corn mint are often used to make herbal teas and flavor dishes.
Corn mint grows well in moist soils along field edges and roadsides.
Some farmers grow corn mint to extract essential oil for medicinal use.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wild mint - another name for Mentha arvensis or similar species
  • Degree Lower: spearmint - a common cultivated mint, Mentha spicata
  • Degree Higher: peppermint - a hybrid mint (Mentha × piperita) with a stronger flavor than corn mint
