order Batrachia

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdər bəˈtreɪkiə / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə bəˈtrækɪə / (BrE)
A former taxonomic order in zoology that includes frogs and toads.
taxonomic group including frogs and toads

Examples

Many paleontologists study extinct species within the order Batrachia to understand amphibian evolution.
The classification of frogs and toads under the order Batrachia is now considered outdated in modern taxonomy.
Early naturalists grouped both frogs and toads in the order Batrachia before advances in anatomical studies.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Amphibia - The class of vertebrates that includes amphibians such as frogs, toads, newts, and salamanders
  • Synonym: frogs - Tailless amphibians of the order Anura
  • Synonym: toads - A type of frog, typically with dry, warty skin

Etymology

From New Latin 'Batrachia' (from Greek 'batrachos', meaning 'frog').
