two-fisted drinker

1. NOUN

/ ˌtuːˈfɪstɪd ˈdrɪŋkər / (AmE), / ˌtuːˈfɪstɪd ˈdrɪŋkə / (BrE)
a person who drinks alcoholic beverages in large quantities or very quickly
person who drinks heavily or fast

Examples

At the party, John proved once again that he is a two-fisted drinker, finishing his beer before anyone else.
She warned her new friends that she was something of a two-fisted drinker on the weekends.
The regulars regarded him as a legendary two-fisted drinker in the local pub.

Synset Examples

As a two-fisted drinker, he often challenges his friends.
She joked about being a two-fisted drinker during parties.
He's known as a two-fisted drinker at the bar.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heavy drinker - a person who habitually drinks large amounts of alcohol
  • Antonym: teetotaler - a person who abstains completely from alcoholic drinks

Etymology

From the figurative use of 'two-fisted', meaning using both hands, implying vigor or strength in drinking.
