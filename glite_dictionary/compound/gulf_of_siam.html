Gulf of Siam

1. PROPN

/ ɡʌlf əv saɪˈæm / (AmE), / ɡʌlf əv saɪˈæm / (BrE)
a historical name for the Gulf of Thailand, the arm of the South China Sea bordered by Thailand, Cambodia, and Vietnam
former name for Gulf of Thailand

Examples

Sailors once referred to the Gulf of Siam when mapping the coasts of Southeast Asia.
The Gulf of Siam is now more commonly known as the Gulf of Thailand.
Trade routes through the Gulf of Siam played a vital role in regional commerce.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Gulf of Thailand - major gulf in the South China Sea bordered by Thailand, Cambodia, and Vietnam

Etymology

From 'Siam', the former name of Thailand, and use of English 'gulf'.
