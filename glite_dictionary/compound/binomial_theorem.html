binomial theorem

1. NOUN

/ baɪˈnoʊmiəl ˈθiːərəm / (AmE), / baɪˈnəʊmiəl ˈθɪərəm / (BrE)
a theorem in algebra that gives the expanded form of a binomial expression raised to any positive integer power
theorem on expanding binomials in algebra
medical

Examples

The binomial theorem allows us to quickly expand expressions like (a+b)^5.
In the exam, you will need to apply the binomial theorem to solve some of the problems.
Newton is credited with the general formulation of the binomial theorem.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: binomial - an algebraic expression of the sum or difference of two terms

Etymology

From 'binomial' (from Latin bis 'twice' + nomen 'name') and 'theorem' (from Greek theōrēma 'proposition').
