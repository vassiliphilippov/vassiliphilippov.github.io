small letter

1. NOUN

/ smɔl ˈlɛtɚ / (AmE), / smɔːl ˈlɛtə / (BrE)
a lowercase letter; one of the set of letters that are not capitalized, originally referring to the characters kept in the lower half of a printer's type case
lowercase letter (printing term)

Examples

Please write your answers using only small letters for consistency.
In old printing shops, small letters were stored separately from capital letters.
The password requires at least one small letter and one number.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lowercase letter - a letter in small form, not a capital
  • Antonym: capital letter - an upper-case letter; a large form of a letter used at the beginning of sentences or names

Etymology

Refers to the traditional arrangement of type cases in printing, where lowercase types were stored in the lower (small) case.
