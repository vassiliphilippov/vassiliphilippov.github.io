plaster of Paris

1. NOUN

/ ˈplæs.tɚ əv ˈpɛr.ɪs / (AmE), / ˈplɑː.stər əv ˈpær.ɪs / (BrE)
a white powder made from gypsum that forms a paste with water and hardens into a solid, used for making molds, sculptures, and casts for broken limbs
white gypsum-based powder for molds or casts

Examples

The doctor applied plaster of Paris to immobilize the patient's broken leg.
Artists often use plaster of Paris to create detailed sculptures and molds.
When mixed with water, plaster of Paris sets quickly into a hard, durable form.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gypsum - a soft sulfate mineral used to make plaster of Paris

Etymology

Named after extensive deposits of gypsum in Paris, France, where it was first widely produced.
