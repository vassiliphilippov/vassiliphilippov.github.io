wood sage

1. NOUN

/ wʊd seɪdʒ / (AmE), / wʊd seɪdʒ / (BrE)
A perennial subshrub with serrated leaves and cream to pink or purple flowers in spikes, native to North America.
North American shrubby sage with spiked flowers

Examples

The wood sage in the garden attracted many pollinators during the summer.
Hikers often spot wood sage along forest trails in the northeastern United States.
She pressed the leaves of wood sage to make a fragrant herbal tea.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Teucrium canadense - The botanical name for wood sage, a perennial herb native to North America

2. NOUN

/ wʊd seɪdʒ / (AmE), / wʊd seɪdʒ / (BrE)
a European plant of the mint family with one-sided racemes of yellow flowers (Teucrium scorodonia), sometimes naturalized in North America
a European plant with yellowish flowers

Examples

The wood sage thrives in open woodlands across much of Europe.
Botanists identified the plant as wood sage due to its distinctive yellow flowers.
After moving to North America, the wood sage has become a common sight in gardens.
