choir stall

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwaɪər stɔl / (AmE), / ˈkwaɪə stɔːl / (BrE)
a carved and decorated wooden seat in the choir of a church, used by clergy or choir members during services
carved church seat for clergy or choir

Examples

The bishop took his place in the choir stall before the service began.
Many medieval churches feature elaborately decorated choir stalls with intricate carvings.
The monks filed quietly into their assigned choir stalls for evening prayer.

Synset Examples

The use of choir stalls (as opposed to benches) is more traditional in monasteries and collegiate churches.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pew - a long bench with a back, used for seating members of a congregation

Etymology

From 'choir', the area of a church for singers, and 'stall', Old English 'steall' (a seat or place).
