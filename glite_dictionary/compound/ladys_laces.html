lady's laces

1. NOUN

/ ˈleɪdiz ˌleɪsɪz / (AmE), / ˈleɪdɪz ˌleɪsɪz / (BrE)
a perennial grass with broad leaves found in marshy meadows and ditches in Europe and North America
perennial marsh grass with broad leaves

Examples

The wetlands were carpeted with lady's laces, their broad leaves swaying gently in the breeze.
She pointed out the lady's laces growing alongside the ditch during our nature walk.
Botanists recorded a large patch of lady's laces in the marshy meadow last spring.
