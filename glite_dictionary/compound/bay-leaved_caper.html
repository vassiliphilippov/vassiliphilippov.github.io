bay-leaved caper

1. NOUN

/ ˌbeɪ ˈlivd ˈkeɪpər / (AmE), / ˌbeɪ ˈliːvd ˈkeɪpə / (BrE)
a shrub or small tree native to southern Florida, Central America, and South America, known for its bay-like leaves and use in tropical landscaping
shrub or small tree of Florida to South America

Examples

The bay-leaved caper thrives in the humid climate of southern Florida.
Gardeners often choose the bay-leaved caper for its attractive foliage.
The bay-leaved caper is an important part of many Central American ecosystems.
