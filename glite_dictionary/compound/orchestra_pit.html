orchestra pit

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔɹ.kə.strə pɪt / (AmE), / ˈɔː.kɪ.strə pɪt / (BrE)
a lowered area in front of a stage in a theater where the orchestra sits and plays, usually accompanying performers in a play, musical, or opera
area for orchestra in a theater

Examples

The musicians gathered in the orchestra pit before the show began.
During the performance, beautiful music rose from the orchestra pit.
Several seats near the stage were removed to make room for a larger orchestra pit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orchestra - a large group of musicians playing together, especially classical music
