hot spell

1. NOUN

/ hɑt spɛl / (AmE), / hɒt spel / (BrE)
a period of unusually hot weather lasting for several days
period of hot weather

Examples

We're experiencing another hot spell this summer, with temperatures reaching record highs.
The crops suffered during the hot spell in July and needed extra watering.
A sudden hot spell can make air conditioning almost a necessity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heatwave - a prolonged period of excessively hot weather
