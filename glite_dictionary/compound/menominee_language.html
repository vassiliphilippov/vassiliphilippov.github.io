Menominee language

1. NOUN

/ məˈnɑːməni ˌlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / məˈnɒmɪni ˌlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Algonquian language spoken by the Menominee people of Wisconsin, USA
Algonquian language of the Menominee people

Examples

Few people today still speak the Menominee language, making language preservation efforts critical.
The dictionary includes several words from the Menominee language along with their English translations.
She is taking a course to learn the basics of the Menominee language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Algonquian - a family of Native American languages including Menominee

Etymology

Named after the Menominee people; 'Menominee' from Ojibwe 'manoominii' (wild rice people).
