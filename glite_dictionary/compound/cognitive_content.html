cognitive content

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑɡnɪtɪv ˈkɑntɛnt / (AmE), / ˈkɒɡnɪtɪv ˈkɒntɛnt / (BrE)
the knowledge, information, or meaning contained in a mental state, communication, or concept
information or meaning contained in thought or message

Examples

The cognitive content of the theory was debated extensively among the scholars.
Linguists study the cognitive content communicated through different languages.
He questioned whether dreams had any real cognitive content or were merely random images.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meaning - what is intended to be expressed or indicated
  • Synonym: knowledge - facts, information, and skills acquired through experience or education

Etymology

From Latin cognitivus (pertaining to knowledge) + contentum (that which is contained).
