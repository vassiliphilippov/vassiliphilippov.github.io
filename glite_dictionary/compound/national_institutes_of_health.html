National Institutes of Health

1. PROPN

/ ˌnæʃnəl ˈɪnstɪˌtuts ʌv hɛlθ / (AmE), / ˌnæʃənəl ˈɪnstɪtjuːts ɒv hɛlθ / (BrE)
a U.S. government agency responsible for biomedical and public health research, serving as the principal agency for conducting and supporting medical research.
U.S. federal medical research agency

Examples

The National Institutes of Health funds a large portion of medical research in the United States.
She received a grant from the National Institutes of Health to study genetic diseases.
New medical guidelines were issued by the National Institutes of Health last month.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Centers for Disease Control and Prevention - U.S. national public health institute focused on disease control and prevention
