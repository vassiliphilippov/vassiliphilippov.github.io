hero sandwich

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɪəroʊ ˈsænwɪtʃ / (AmE), / ˈhɪərəʊ ˈsænwɪtʃ / (BrE)
A large sandwich made from a long, crusty roll split lengthwise and typically filled with meats, cheese, and assorted vegetables and condiments; known by various names in different U.S. regions.
large filled sandwich on a long roll
american

Examples

I ordered a giant hero sandwich loaded with turkey, ham, and provolone for lunch.
The deli on the corner is famous for its classic Italian hero sandwich.
For the party, we sliced a six-foot hero sandwich so everyone could have a piece.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: submarine sandwich - a similar type of sandwich with alternative regional naming
  • Synonym: hoagie - regional term for a similar sandwich
  • Synonym: grinder - another regional term for a similar sandwich

Etymology

American English, possibly derived from the idea that one must be a 'hero' to finish such a large sandwich (first attested 1930s).
