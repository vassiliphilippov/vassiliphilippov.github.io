seal bomb

1. NOUN

/ siːl bɑːm / (AmE), / siːl bɒm / (BrE)
a small underwater explosive device, similar to a firecracker, used to scare marine mammals away from fishing areas
underwater explosive to scare marine mammals

Examples

The fishermen deployed a seal bomb to keep sea lions away from their catch.
Environmental groups have raised concerns about the impact of seal bombs on marine life.
Using a seal bomb is a controversial method to protect fish stocks from predatory mammals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: firecracker - a small explosive device that makes a loud noise
