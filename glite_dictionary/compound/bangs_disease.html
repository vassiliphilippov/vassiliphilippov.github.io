Bang's disease

1. NOUN

/ bæŋz dɪˈziz / (AmE), / bæŋz dɪˈziːz / (BrE)
An infectious disease of domestic animals, especially cattle, often causing spontaneous abortion and transmittable to humans; also known as brucellosis.
infectious disease of livestock (brucellosis)
medical

Examples

The veterinarian diagnosed the cow with Bang's disease after noticing several spontaneous abortions in the herd.
Because Bang's disease can be transmitted to humans, proper precautions must be taken when handling infected animals.
Control measures for Bang's disease include regular testing and vaccination of livestock.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brucellosis - an infectious bacterial disease (brucella) causing abortion in animals and fever in humans

Etymology

Named after Bernhard Bang (1848–1932), Danish veterinarian who first described the disease.
