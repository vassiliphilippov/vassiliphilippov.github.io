burr oak

1. NOUN

/ bɜr oʊk / (AmE), / bɜː əʊk / (BrE)
a medium to large deciduous oak tree (Quercus macrocarpa) native to central and eastern North America, with large fringed acorn cups and tough close-grained wood
a North American oak tree with fringed acorn cups

Examples

The old farm was shaded by a massive burr oak with wide spreading branches.
We collected several burr oak acorns to plant in the garden.
The tough wood of the burr oak is highly valued for making furniture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oak - any tree or shrub of the genus Quercus

Etymology

From 'burr' (a rough or prickly covering) and 'oak'; referring to the acorn's fringed cup.
