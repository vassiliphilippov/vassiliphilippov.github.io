process of monition

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɑːsɛs əv məˈnɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈprɒsɛs əv məˈnɪʃən / (BrE)
a formal legal summons issued after a claim is filed, requiring parties to show cause why a requested judgment should not be granted
formal legal summons after claim filing
legal

Examples

The judge issued a process of monition to ensure all claimants had a chance to respond.
Before the case proceeded, a process of monition was served to the interested parties.
Failure to respond to the process of monition may result in judgment being entered by default.

Etymology

From Latin 'monitio' (a warning), used in ecclesiastical and admiralty law.
