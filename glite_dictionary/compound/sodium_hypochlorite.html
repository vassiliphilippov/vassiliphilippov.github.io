sodium hypochlorite

1. NOUN

/ ˌsoʊdiəm haɪpəˈklɔːraɪt / (AmE), / ˌsəʊdiəm hʌɪpəˈklɔːraɪt / (BrE)
an unstable chemical compound (NaOCl) commonly used as a bleaching agent and disinfectant, especially in household bleach
a bleaching and disinfecting chemical (NaOCl)
medical

Examples

Household sodium hypochlorite solutions are commonly used for disinfecting surfaces.
Dentists may use diluted sodium hypochlorite to irrigate root canals.
Spills of sodium hypochlorite should be cleaned up carefully because it can be corrosive.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bleach - a chemical used for whitening or disinfecting, often containing sodium hypochlorite

Etymology

From 'sodium' (element) and 'hypochlorite' (from Greek hypo- 'under' and chlorine).
