infernal machine

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈfɜrnəl məˈʃin / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɜːnəl məˈʃiːn / (BrE)
a bomb fitted with a timed detonating mechanism, especially one designed for sabotage or assassination
timed bomb, often for sabotage

Examples

The assassin built an infernal machine and hid it in the carriage, intending to kill the official.
Security forces discovered an infernal machine beneath the theater stage before the performance began.
In the novel, a mysterious infernal machine threatens the peace of the city.

Etymology

From Latin infernalis (of the underworld, hellish) + machine, referring figuratively to destructive devices.
