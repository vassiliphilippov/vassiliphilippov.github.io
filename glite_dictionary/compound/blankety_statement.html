blankety statement

1. NOUN

/ ˈblæŋ.kə.ti ˈsteɪt.mənt / (AmE), / ˈblaŋ.kɪ.ti ˈsteɪt.mənt / (BrE)
a remark or statement that is vague, nonspecific, or lacking in detail
vague or nonspecific remark

Examples

The politician's answer was just another blankety statement that failed to address the issue.
Her email was full of blankety statements instead of concrete instructions.
Critics complained that the editor’s review was a typical blankety statement, lacking any specific feedback.

Synset Examples

The report was filled with blankety statements lacking detail.

Etymology

Formed from "blanket" (meaning general or all-encompassing) with the suffix -y, expressing the nonspecific, general nature of the statement.
