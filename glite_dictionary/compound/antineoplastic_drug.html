antineoplastic drug

1. NOUN

/ ˌæn.ti.ni.oʊˈplæs.tɪk drʌɡ / (AmE), / ˌæn.ti.ni.əʊˈplæs.tɪk drʌɡ / (BrE)
a drug used to control or destroy neoplastic (cancerous) cells, commonly administered in chemotherapy treatments
drug for treating cancer (chemotherapy)
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed an antineoplastic drug to reduce the size of the tumor before surgery.
Many antineoplastic drugs can cause significant side effects, such as hair loss and nausea.
New research is aiming to make antineoplastic drugs more effective with fewer negative impacts on patients.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chemotherapy - treatment of disease, especially cancer, using chemical substances
  • Synonym: cytotoxic drug - a drug toxic to cells, used to treat cancer

Etymology

From Greek anti- (against) + neoplasia (new formation, referring to tumors) + drug.
