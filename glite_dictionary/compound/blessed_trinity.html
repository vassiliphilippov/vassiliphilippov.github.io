Blessed Trinity

1. PROPN

/ ˈblɛsɪd ˈtrɪnəti / (AmE), / ˈblɛsɪd ˈtrɪnɪti / (BrE)
(in Christian belief) the union of the Father, the Son, and the Holy Spirit as three persons in one God.
Christian doctrine: the Triune God

Examples

The doctrine of the Blessed Trinity is central to most Christian faiths.
Worshippers gathered to celebrate the feast of the Blessed Trinity on Sunday.
He found the concept of the Blessed Trinity complex but deeply meaningful.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Holy Trinity - another term for the union of Father, Son, and Holy Spirit in Christianity

Etymology

From Latin trinitas (triad, trinity), used in Christian Latin to describe the threefold nature of God.
