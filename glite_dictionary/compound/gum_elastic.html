gum elastic

1. NOUN

/ ɡʌm ɪˈlæstɪk / (AmE), / ɡʌm ɪˈlæstɪk / (BrE)
a natural elastic material obtained from the latex of certain trees, especially Hevea and Ficus, and used in making rubber products
natural elastic material from latex

Examples

The tubing was made of gum elastic for flexibility and strength.
In the nineteenth century, gum elastic revolutionized the production of waterproof clothing.
A gum elastic catheter was used in early medical procedures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: natural rubber - an elastic material from latex sap, also called India rubber
  • Antonym: synthetic rubber - man-made elastic polymers used as rubber alternatives

Etymology

From 'gum' (resin from plants) and 'elastic' (able to resume shape).
