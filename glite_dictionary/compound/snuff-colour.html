snuff-colour

1. NOUN

/ snʌf ˈkʌlər / (AmE), / snʌf ˈkʌlə / (BrE)
A dark yellowish-brown color, resembling the color of powdered tobacco (snuff).
dark yellowish-brown color

Examples

The curtains were a dull snuff-colour that matched the antique décor of the room.
She wore a coat of snuff-colour, blending in with the autumn leaves.
The artist chose snuff-colour for the background to evoke a vintage atmosphere.

Etymology

From 'snuff' (powdered tobacco) + 'colour', referring to the typical shade of snuff.
