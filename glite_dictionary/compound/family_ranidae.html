family Ranidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ˈrænɪˌdi / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli ˈranɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A large family of frogs found almost worldwide, commonly known as true frogs.
family of true frogs

Examples

The family Ranidae includes many species commonly found in ponds and streams around the world.
Scientists discovered a new species within the family Ranidae in Southeast Asia.
Members of the family Ranidae are often characterized by their smooth skin and long legs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frog - a tailless amphibian of the order Anura

Etymology

From modern Latin Ranidae, based on Latin rana (frog) + -idae (a zoological family suffix).
