conformal projection

1. NOUN

/ kənˈfɔrml̩ prəˈdʒɛkʃən / (AmE), / kənˈfɔːməl prəˈdʒɛkʃən / (BrE)
A map projection in which shapes of small areas are preserved, so that local angles are accurate, but scale may vary.
map projection preserving local shapes

Examples

The Mercator projection is a famous example of a conformal projection used for navigation.
A conformal projection accurately represents roads and buildings in small city maps.
Some world maps use a conformal projection to preserve angles but distort areas near the poles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: map projection - a method of representing the curved surface of the earth on a flat map

Etymology

From 'conformal', meaning preserving angles, and 'projection', from Latin 'proiectio' (a throwing forward).
