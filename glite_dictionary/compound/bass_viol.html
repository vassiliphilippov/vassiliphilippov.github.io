bass viol

1. NOUN

/ beɪs ˈvaɪ.əl / (AmE), / beɪs ˈvaɪ.əl / (BrE)
the largest and lowest-pitched instrument in the violin family, usually referring to the double bass or violone
largest and lowest violin family instrument

Examples

He played the bass viol in the orchestra, providing the deep foundation for the ensemble.
The museum displayed a beautifully crafted 18th-century bass viol among its collection of stringed instruments.
Early Baroque composers often wrote music specifically for the bass viol due to its rich, resonant sound.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: double bass - the largest and lowest-pitched bowed string instrument

Etymology

From Italian 'viola' and English 'bass' (referring to low pitch).

2. NOUN

/ ˌbeɪs ˈvaɪəl / (AmE), / ˌbeɪs ˈvaɪ.əl / (BrE)
a stringed musical instrument from the viol family, serving as the bass member and having a range similar to the cello
bass member of the viol family, like a cello

Examples

The ensemble featured a bass viol to provide a rich, resonant foundation for the music.
She learned to play the bass viol in order to join an early music group.
Unlike the modern cello, the bass viol has frets and six or seven strings.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: viol - any stringed instrument of the viol family played with a bow
  • Synonym: cello - the modern string instrument with a similar range to the bass viol

Etymology

From 'bass' (low-pitched) + 'viol' (from Italian 'viola', a string instrument).
