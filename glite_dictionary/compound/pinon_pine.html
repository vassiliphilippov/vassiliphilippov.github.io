pinon pine

1. NOUN

/ ˈpiːn.jɒn paɪn / (AmE), / ˈpiːnjɒn paɪn / (BrE)
a small pine tree native to Mexico and southern Texas, typically having two or three needles per bundle and producing edible seeds called pine nuts
a small pine tree of Mexico and southern Texas

Examples

The pinon pine thrives in the arid climate of southern Texas and northern Mexico.
Local communities harvest pine nuts from the pinon pine each autumn.
The landscape was dotted with pinon pines and junipers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pine - any tree of the genus Pinus, coniferous trees with needle-like leaves and cones
  • Synonym: piñon - a type of pine tree with edible seeds, especially native to southwestern US and Mexico

Etymology

From Spanish 'piñón' (pine nut), referring to its edible seeds.
