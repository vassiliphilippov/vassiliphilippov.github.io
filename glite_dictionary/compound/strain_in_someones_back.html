strain in someone's back

1. NOUN

/ streɪn ɪn sʌmwʌnz bæk / (AmE), / streɪn ɪn sʌmwʌnz bæk / (BrE)
pain or discomfort in the muscles of the back, often caused by injury or excessive use
back muscle pain or discomfort
medical

Examples

He complained of a strain in his back after moving the furniture all day.
A sudden twist caused a strain in her back during the yoga session.
If you have a strain in your back, it's important to rest and avoid further injury.

Synset Examples

He felt a strain in his back after lifting the heavy box.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: backache - pain felt in the back
