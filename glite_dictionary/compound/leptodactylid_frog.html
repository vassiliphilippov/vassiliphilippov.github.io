leptodactylid frog

1. NOUN

/ lɛp.təˈdæk.tɪ.lɪd frɑg / (AmE), / lɛp.təˈdæk.tɪ.lɪd frɒg / (BrE)
A frog belonging to the family Leptodactylidae, distinguished by the presence of teeth and found in terrestrial, aquatic, or arboreal environments, primarily in Central and South America.
frog of the family Leptodactylidae

Examples

The leptodactylid frog is a common sight in the rainforests of South America.
Researchers observed a leptodactylid frog constructing its foam nest near the water.
Many species of leptodactylid frog exhibit unique reproductive behaviors compared to other frogs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leptodactylid - a frog of the family Leptodactylidae

Etymology

From Greek 'leptos' (slender) + 'daktylos' (finger), referring to their slender toes.
