suborder Mugiloidea

1. NOUN

/ ˌsʌbˈɔːrdər ˌmjuːdʒɪˈlɔɪdiə / (AmE), / ˌsʌbˈɔːdə ˌmjuːgɪˈlɔɪdiə / (BrE)
a taxonomic group of fishes characterized by abdominal pelvic fins, including families such as Mugilidae, Atherinidae, and Sphyraenidae
taxonomic fish group with abdominal pelvic fins

Examples

The suborder Mugiloidea includes species commonly known as mullets and silversides.
Researchers studied the distribution of families within the suborder Mugiloidea across coastal habitats.
Many fish in the suborder Mugiloidea are important to commercial and subsistence fisheries worldwide.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'suborder' and 'Mugiloidea' (from 'Mugil', a genus of mullet, and '-oidea' meaning 'resembling').
