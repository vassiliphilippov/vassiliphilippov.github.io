wrongful conduct

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɔŋfəl ˈkɑndʌkt / (AmE), / ˈrɒŋfəl ˈkɒndʌkt / (BrE)
Behaviour that violates moral or civil law; illegal or unethical actions.
illegal or unethical behaviour
legal

Examples

The employee was dismissed for wrongful conduct in the workplace.
Allegations of wrongful conduct led to an internal investigation at the company.
The lawyer argued that his client was not responsible for any wrongful conduct.

Synset Examples

he denied any wrongdoing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: misconduct - improper or unlawful behaviour
  • Synonym: wrongdoing - bad or illegal behaviour
  • Antonym: lawful conduct - behaviour in accordance with the law
