New World flycatcher

1. NOUN

/ nu ˈwɝld ˈflaɪˌkætʃər / (AmE), / njuː ˈwɜːld ˈflaɪˌkætʃə / (BrE)
A large American bird of the family Tyrannidae, noted for catching insects in flight.
large insect-catching American bird

Examples

The New World flycatcher can be seen swooping through the forest, hunting insects in midair.
Several species of New World flycatcher inhabit Central and South America.
A skilled New World flycatcher catches its prey with remarkable agility.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flycatcher - a bird that catches insects in the air
