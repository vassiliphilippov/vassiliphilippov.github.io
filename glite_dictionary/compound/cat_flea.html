cat flea

1. NOUN

/ kæt fli / (AmE), / kæt fliː / (BrE)
a small parasitic insect (Ctenocephalides felis) that chiefly infests cats, but also dogs and other animals, feeding on their blood
a flea species that infests cats and dogs

Examples

The veterinarian found a cat flea infestation on the stray kitten.
Despite its name, the cat flea often bites dogs and even humans.
To control cat flea populations, regular pet grooming is recommended.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flea - a small wingless jumping insect that feeds on the blood of mammals and birds

Etymology

Named for its tendency to infest cats; species name from Latin: Ctenocephalides felis.
