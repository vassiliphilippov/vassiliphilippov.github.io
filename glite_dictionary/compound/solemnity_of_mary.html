Solemnity of Mary

1. PROPN

/ səˈlɛm.nə.ti əv ˈmɛə.ri / (AmE), / səˈlɛm.nɪ.ti əv ˈmæɹ.i / (BrE)
In the Roman Catholic Church, a holy day of obligation celebrated on January 1, honoring Mary, the mother of Jesus.
Catholic holy day honoring Mary, Jan 1

Examples

Many Catholics attend Mass on the Solemnity of Mary to honor the Virgin Mother.
The Solemnity of Mary is a holy day of obligation in the Catholic liturgical calendar.
Schools and businesses may close in some countries on the Solemnity of Mary.

Etymology

From 'solemnity' (Latin *solemnitas*, religious festivity) and 'Mary' referring to the mother of Jesus.
