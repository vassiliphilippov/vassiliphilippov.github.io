duple time

1. NOUN

/ ˈdjuːpəl taɪm / (AmE), / ˈdjuːpəl taɪm / (BrE)
(in music) a rhythmic pattern or time signature with two beats in each bar.
musical rhythm with two beats per bar

Examples

Most marches are written in duple time to create a steady and predictable rhythm.
The piece alternates between duple time and triple time for dramatic effect.
Dancers often find duple time easier to follow than irregular time signatures.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: triple time - musical time with three beats in each bar

Etymology

From Latin 'duplus' (double) and English 'time' (musical sense).
