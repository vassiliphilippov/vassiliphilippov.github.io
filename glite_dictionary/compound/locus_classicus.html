locus classicus

1. NOUN

/ ˌloʊkəs ˈklæsɪkəs / (AmE), / ˌləʊkəs ˈklasɪkəs / (BrE)
A passage in a literary or scholarly work that is regarded as the most authoritative or typical example of a particular word, phrase, or subject.
authoritative or classic passage

Examples

The historian cited a locus classicus to support her argument about ancient democracy.
This chapter has become the locus classicus for discussions of early Romantic poetry.
His interpretation starts with the locus classicus found in Aristotle's writings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: classical example - the typical or standard example of something

Etymology

From Latin, literally meaning 'classical place'.
