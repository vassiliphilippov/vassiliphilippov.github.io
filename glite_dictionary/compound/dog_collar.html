dog collar

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɔg ˌkɑlər / (AmE), / ˈdɒɡ ˌkɒlə / (BrE)
A stiff white collar with no opening in the front, worn as a distinctive symbol by some Christian clergy.
clerical collar worn by clergy

Examples

The priest adjusted his dog collar before stepping into the pulpit.
She recognized the bishop immediately by the dog collar he wore.
In some denominations, only ordained ministers wear the traditional dog collar.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clerical collar - a stiff white collar worn by Christian clergy

/ dɔg ˈkɑlɚ / (AmE), / dɒg ˈkɒlə / (BrE)

Examples

She attached the leash to the dog collar before going for a walk.

3. NOUN

/ dɔɡ ˈkɑlər / (AmE), / dɒɡ ˈkɒlə / (BrE)
A type of necklace that fits closely around a woman’s neck, resembling the shape of a traditional dog collar.
close-fitting choker necklace

Examples

She wore a sparkling dog collar to the party, which perfectly complemented her black dress.
The vintage shop had a collection of antique dog collars from the 1920s.
That fashion magazine predicted the return of the dog collar as a trendy accessory.

Related Terms
