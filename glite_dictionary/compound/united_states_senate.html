United States Senate

1. NOUN

/ ˌjuː.nəˈtaɪtɪd steɪts ˈsen.ɪt / (AmE), / ˌjuː.nɪtɪd steɪts ˈsen.ɪt / (BrE)
the upper chamber of the United States Congress, consisting of two representatives from each state, responsible for making and passing laws
upper house of U.S. Congress

Examples

The United States Senate approved the bill after a lengthy debate on the floor.
She interned in the United States Senate during her summer break from college.
Major policy changes often require agreement from both the House and the United States Senate.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: House of Representatives - the lower house of the United States Congress
