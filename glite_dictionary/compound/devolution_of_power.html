devolution of power

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɛvəˈluːʃən əv ˈpaʊər / (AmE), / ˌdiːvəˈluːʃən əv ˈpaʊə / (BrE)
the transfer of authority or responsibility from a central government to local or regional governments
transfer of authority to local governments

Examples

The new constitution included provisions for the devolution of power to the provinces.
Many advocates believe that devolution of power can lead to more responsive local government.
Debates over the devolution of power are common in countries with diverse regions.

Synset Examples

The country faced devolution of power to local governments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decentralization - the process of distributing or dispersing functions from a central authority

Etymology

From 'devolution' (from Latin 'devolutio', a rolling down or transfer) + 'power'.
