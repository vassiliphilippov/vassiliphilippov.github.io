civil year

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪvəl jɪr / (AmE), / ˈsɪvəl jɪə / (BrE)
The year counted from January 1 to December 31 according to the Gregorian calendar, as recognized for civil purposes.
Gregorian calendar year (January 1–December 31)

Examples

The civil year begins on January 1 and ends on December 31, according to the Gregorian calendar.
Unlike the academic year, the civil year corresponds to the standard calendar used in most countries.
The duration of holidays may vary depending on whether they follow the civil year or a religious calendar.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calendar year - the period from January 1 to December 31
