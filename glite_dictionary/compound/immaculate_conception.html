Immaculate Conception

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌmækjələt kənˈsɛpʃən / (AmE), / ɪˌmækjʊlət kənˈsɛpʃən / (BrE)
(in Roman Catholic doctrine) the belief that the Virgin Mary was preserved free from original sin from the moment of her conception.
Roman Catholic doctrine; Mary's conception without original sin

Examples

The doctrine of the Immaculate Conception was officially declared by Pope Pius IX in 1854.
Many Catholics celebrate the feast of the Immaculate Conception on December 8.
The concept of the Immaculate Conception is sometimes confused with the Virgin Birth.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: original sin - the sin inherited from Adam and Eve according to Christian doctrine

Etymology

From Latin immaculatus (unstained) + conceptio (conception).

2. NOUN

/ ɪˌmækjələt kənˈsɛpʃən / (AmE), / ɪˌmakjʊlət kənˈsɛpʃən / (BrE)
(in the Roman Catholic Church) the doctrine that the Virgin Mary was conceived free from original sin.
Roman Catholic doctrine about Virgin Mary's conception

Examples

The doctrine of the Immaculate Conception is a central belief in Catholic theology.
Many churches celebrate the Feast of the Immaculate Conception on December 8th each year.
He wrote his thesis on the historical development of the Immaculate Conception in medieval theology.

Etymology

From Latin 'immaculatus' (unstained, pure) + 'conception'. The phrase dates from the mid-16th century in English.

3. NOUN

/ ɪˌmækjələt kənˈsɛpʃən / (AmE), / ɪˌmækjʊlət kənˈsɛpʃən / (BrE)
(in the Roman Catholic Church) the doctrine that the Virgin Mary was conceived free from original sin; also, the feast marking this event, celebrated on December 8.
Roman Catholic doctrine and holy day

Examples

The doctrine of the Immaculate Conception is a fundamental teaching in the Roman Catholic Church.
Many Catholics attend mass to celebrate the Feast of the Immaculate Conception each year on December 8.
The Immaculate Conception is often confused with the Virgin Birth, but they refer to different events.

Etymology

From Latin 'immaculatus' (spotless, pure) and 'concipere' (to conceive).
