salicylic alcohol

1. NOUN

/ ˌsælɪˈsɪlɪk ˈæl.kəˌhɑl / (AmE), / ˌsælɪˈsɪlɪk ˈæl.kəhɒl / (BrE)
A crystalline phenolic alcohol (C7H8O2) obtained usually by hydrolysis of salicin, used in medicine for its local anesthetic and pain-relieving properties.
a phenolic alcohol used in medicine
medical

Examples

Salicylic alcohol is sometimes used in topical preparations to relieve minor pain.
Researchers found that salicylic alcohol reduced inflammation in laboratory tests.
The pharmacist explained that salicylic alcohol could be derived from willow bark extract.

Synset Examples

Salicylic alcohol acts as a local anesthetic and relieves pain and fever.

Etymology

From salicylic (from Latin salix 'willow') + alcohol.
