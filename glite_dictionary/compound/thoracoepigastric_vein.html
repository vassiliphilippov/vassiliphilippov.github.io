thoracoepigastric vein

1. NOUN

/ ˌθɔːrəkoʊˌɛpɪˈɡæstrɪk veɪn / (AmE), / ˌθɒrə.kəʊˌɛp.ɪˈɡæs.trɪk veɪn / (BrE)
a vein that runs along the side of the trunk, connecting the superficial epigastric vein to the axillary vein or a thoracic vein
anatomical vein in the side of the trunk
medical

Examples

The thoracoepigastric vein serves as an important collateral pathway during blockage of the main venous drainage routes.
Prominence of the thoracoepigastric vein can be a clinical sign in patients with portal hypertension.
During surgery, the surgeon carefully identified the thoracoepigastric vein to avoid accidental injury.

Etymology

From Greek 'thorax' (chest) and 'epigastric' (over the stomach).
