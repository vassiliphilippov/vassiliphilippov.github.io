pike perch

1. NOUN

/ paɪk pɝːtʃ / (AmE), / paɪk pɜːtʃ / (BrE)
Any of several carnivorous freshwater fishes of the perch family, resembling pikes, especially the zander.
pike-like freshwater fish of the perch family

Examples

The pike perch is highly prized among anglers for its fighting ability.
Many lakes in Eastern Europe are home to the pike perch, also known as zander.
A pike perch can grow quite large, sometimes rivaling the size of northern pike.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zander - A large Eurasian pike perch
  • Synonym: perch - A freshwater fish of the family Percidae
  • Synonym: pike - A large predatory freshwater fish

Etymology

Formed from 'pike' (a type of predatory fish) and 'perch' (another type of fish), referring to their resemblance to both groups.
