manganese steel

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæŋɡəniːz stil / (AmE), / ˈmaŋɡəniːz stiːl / (BrE)
a type of steel containing 10–14% manganese, known for its exceptional resistance to wear and shock
type of wear-resistant steel alloy

Examples

The excavator's bucket was made of manganese steel to withstand abrasive materials.
Many railway tracks use manganese steel for its remarkable durability.
Replacing the old parts with manganese steel components greatly increased the machine's lifespan.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: alloy steel - steel made by adding other elements to improve properties

Etymology

From 'manganese', a metallic element, and 'steel', from Old English 'stēle'.
