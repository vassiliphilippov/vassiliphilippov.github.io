to pay homage

1. VERB

/ peɪ ˈhɑmɪdʒ / (AmE), / peɪ ˈhɒmɪdʒ / (BrE)
to show deep respect or honor to someone or something, especially in a public or formal way
show respect or honor

Examples

During the ceremony, the mayor paid homage to the city's founders.
Many artists pay homage to their inspirations through their work.
Visitors from around the world come to pay homage at the historical site.

Synset Examples

They gathered to pay homage to the fallen soldiers.
The festival will pay homage to local artists.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: honor - regard with great respect
  • Antonym: disrespect - lack of respect or courtesy

Etymology

Homage from Old French 'hommage' (allegiance or respect to a lord), ultimately from Latin 'homo' (man), traditionally a vassal's respect to their lord.
