gray fox

1. NOUN

/ ɡreɪ fɑks / (AmE), / ɡreɪ fɒks / (BrE)
A medium-sized fox of North and Central America, having primarily gray fur and known for its ability to climb trees.
North American fox species with gray fur

Examples

A gray fox was spotted climbing a tree in the national park.
Unlike the red fox, the gray fox prefers wooded areas and can climb trees to escape predators.
The gray fox is found throughout the southern United States and into Central America.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: red fox - A species of fox with reddish fur, found throughout the Northern Hemisphere
