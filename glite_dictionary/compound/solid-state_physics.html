solid-state physics

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɑː.lɪd ˈsteɪt ˈfɪz.ɪks / (AmE), / ˌsɒ.lɪd ˈsteɪt ˈfɪz.ɪks / (BrE)
the branch of physics that deals with the physical properties of solid materials, especially their electrical, magnetic, and optical behaviors
branch of physics studying solids

Examples

He decided to specialize in solid-state physics during his graduate studies.
Advances in solid-state physics have led to the development of modern electronics.
Her research in solid-state physics focuses on superconducting materials.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: condensed matter physics - branch of physics studying the properties of condensed phases, including solids and liquids

Etymology

From 'solid state' (describing matter in the solid phase) and 'physics.'
