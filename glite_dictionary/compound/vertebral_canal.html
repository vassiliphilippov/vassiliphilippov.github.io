vertebral canal

1. NOUN

/ vərˈtiː.brəl kəˈnæl / (AmE), / ˈvɜː.tɪ.brəl kəˈnæl / (BrE)
the canal formed by the successive vertebrae of the spine through which the spinal cord passes
passage in the spine for the spinal cord
medical

Examples

In a healthy adult, the vertebral canal provides crucial protection for the spinal cord.
Injury to the vertebral canal can lead to serious neurological problems.
The doctor reviewed the MRI to check for narrowing of the vertebral canal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spinal canal - another term for vertebral canal
  • Degree Lower: spinal cord - the thick nerve cord passing through the vertebral canal

Etymology

From Latin 'vertebra' (joint of the spine) and 'canalis' (canal, channel).
