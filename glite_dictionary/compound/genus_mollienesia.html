genus Mollienesia

1. NOUN

/ ˌmoʊliˈɛnisiə / (AmE), / ˌmɒliˈɛnɪsiə / (BrE)
A former genus of small, freshwater fish known as mollies, now usually included in the genus Poecilia.
taxonomic group of small fish (mollies)

Examples

Many aquarium enthusiasts keep fish from the genus Mollienesia for their vivid colors and peaceful nature.
The classification of the genus Mollienesia has changed as scientists now include these species in the genus Poecilia.
Early zoologists described new species within the genus Mollienesia during expeditions in Central America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: molly - common name for various small freshwater fish of the genus Poecilia
  • Synonym: genus Poecilia - the genus now including mollies and related fish

Etymology

Named after French naturalist François Alexandre Pierre Mollien; used as a genus in taxonomy.
