methylene chloride

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛθəˌlin ˈklaɪəˌraɪd / (AmE), / ˈmiːθəliːn ˈklɔːraɪd / (BrE)
a colorless, volatile liquid used as a solvent, paint remover, and refrigerant
volatile solvent and paint remover
medical

Examples

Many paint strippers contain methylene chloride as the active ingredient.
Workers must wear protective gear when handling methylene chloride to avoid health risks.
The company uses methylene chloride to clean industrial equipment due to its effectiveness as a solvent.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dichloromethane - another name for methylene chloride

Etymology

From 'methylene', from French *méthylène* (methylene), and 'chloride', from chlorine.
