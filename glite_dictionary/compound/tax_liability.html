tax liability

1. NOUN

/ tæks laɪˈæb.ə.lɪ.t̬i / (AmE), / tæks laɪˈæb.ɪ.lə.ti / (BrE)
the total amount of tax that a person or organization is legally required to pay to the government for a particular period
total amount of tax owed

Examples

You should calculate your tax liability before submitting your annual return.
The new regulation could reduce corporate tax liability for small businesses.
Failure to pay your tax liability may result in significant penalties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tax obligation - the responsibility to pay taxes
