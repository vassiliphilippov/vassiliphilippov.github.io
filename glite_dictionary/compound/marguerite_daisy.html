marguerite daisy

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɑːrɡjəˈrit ˈdeɪzi / (AmE), / ˌmɑːɡjʊˈriːt ˈdeɪzi / (BrE)
a perennial subshrub native to the Canary Islands, with pale yellow or white daisy-like flowers; widely cultivated as an ornamental plant
ornamental daisy-like plant from Canary Islands

Examples

The marguerite daisy is popular for its cheerful appearance in spring gardens.
She planted a row of marguerite daisies along the walkway for a splash of color.
Cut flowers from the marguerite daisy last a long time in vases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chrysanthemum - a genus of flowering plants including several ornamental species

Etymology

From French 'marguerite' (daisy), originally from Latin 'margarita' (pearl), referencing the flower's appearance.
