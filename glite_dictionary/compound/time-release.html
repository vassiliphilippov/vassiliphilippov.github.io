time-release

1. ADJ

/ ˈtaɪm rɪˌlis / (AmE), / ˈtaɪm rɪˌliːs / (BrE)
designed to release an active ingredient slowly and steadily over a period of time, especially in reference to drugs or medications
designed to release slowly over time
medical

Examples

Doctors often prescribe time-release medications to maintain consistent levels of the drug in the bloodstream.
This supplement uses a time-release formula to provide nutrients throughout the day.
The time-release coating helps reduce stomach irritation by spreading the dose over hours.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: extended-release - formulated to release a drug over an extended period
  • Antonym: immediate-release - designed to release a drug all at once
