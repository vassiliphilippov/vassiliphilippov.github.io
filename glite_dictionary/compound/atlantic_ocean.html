Atlantic Ocean

1. PROPN

/ əˈtlæn.tɪk ˈoʊ.ʃən / (AmE), / ətˈlæn.tɪk ˈəʊ.ʃən / (BrE)
the world's second-largest ocean, lying between the Americas to the west and Europe and Africa to the east
major ocean between Americas and Europe/Africa

Examples

The Atlantic Ocean is known for its powerful currents and rich maritime history.
Several major shipping routes cross the Atlantic Ocean between Europe and North America.
She dreamed of sailing solo across the Atlantic Ocean one day.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Pacific Ocean - the largest ocean, between Asia/Australia and the Americas

Etymology

From Latin 'Atlanticus,' meaning 'of Atlas,' referring to the mythological figure; 'ocean' from Greek 'ōkeanos.'
