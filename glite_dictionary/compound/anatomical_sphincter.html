anatomical sphincter

1. NOUN

/ ˌæn.əˈtɑ.mɪ.kəl ˈsfɪŋk.tɚ / (AmE), / ˌan.əˈtɒm.ɪ.kəl ˈsfɪŋk.tə / (BrE)
A ring of muscle in the body that contracts to close or restrict an opening or passage.
ring-shaped muscle closing an opening
medical

Examples

The lower esophageal anatomical sphincter prevents stomach acid from entering the esophagus.
A malfunctioning anatomical sphincter can lead to incontinence or digestive problems.
The doctor explained how the pupil's anatomical sphincter controls the amount of light entering the eye.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sphincter - a ringlike muscle that closes a natural opening

Etymology

From Greek 'sphinktēr' (binder) and anatomical (relating to anatomy, from Greek 'anatomē').
