Assyrian Akkadian

1. NOUN

/ əˈsɪriən ækˈeɪdiən / (AmE), / əˈsɪəriən ækˈeɪdiən / (BrE)
an extinct Semitic language spoken by the Assyrians in ancient Mesopotamia
extinct language of ancient Assyria

Examples

Linguists study Assyrian Akkadian to better understand the history of writing in Mesopotamia.
Much of what we know about ancient Assyria comes from texts written in Assyrian Akkadian.
Museums often display clay tablets inscribed in the extinct language of Assyrian Akkadian.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Akkadian - an extinct Semitic language of ancient Mesopotamia
  • Degree Lower: Babylonian Akkadian - an extinct language variant of ancient Babylonia

Etymology

Assyrian: from Assyria, an ancient region; Akkadian: from Akkad, an ancient Mesopotamian city and language.
