bar absolute

1. NOUN

/ bɑr ˌæb.səˈlut / (AmE), / bɑː ˌab.səˈljuːt / (BrE)
an absolute unit of pressure equal to one dyne per square centimeter
absolute unit of pressure

Examples

Atmospheric pressure at sea level is nearly one bar absolute.
The system operates at 2 bars absolute to ensure accurate measurements.
Pressure readings must specify if they refer to bar absolute or bar gauge.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bar - unit of pressure equal to 100,000 pascals

Etymology

From 'bar' (unit of pressure) and 'absolute' indicating non-relative measurement.
