burweed marsh elder

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɝːˌwid mɑrʃ ˈɛldɚ / (AmE), / ˈbɜːˌwiːd mɑːʃ ˈɛldə / (BrE)
A tall annual plant (Iva xanthifolia) native to central North America, common in moist, rich soils, known for causing contact dermatitis and producing large amounts of pollen that can trigger hay fever.
tall North American plant causing hay fever
medical

Examples

Farmers often struggle to control burweed marsh elder in fields with moist, nutrient-rich soil.
People who are allergic to pollen may react strongly when burweed marsh elder is in bloom.
Brushing against burweed marsh elder can cause skin irritation known as contact dermatitis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marsh elder - any of various plants in the genus Iva, often found in marshy areas
