full of

1. ADJ

/ fʊl əv / (AmE), / fʊl əv / (BrE)
containing or filled with a large amount of a particular thing; abundant in or characterized by.
containing a large amount of something

Examples

The book is full of interesting facts and surprising stories.
Her eyes were full of tears as she listened to the news.
This cake is full of rich chocolate flavor.

Synset Examples

The festival was full of spurcity and excitement for everyone.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: empty of - lacking; without any of something
