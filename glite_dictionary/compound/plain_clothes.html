plain clothes

1. NOUN

/ pleɪn kloʊðz / (AmE), / pleɪn kləʊðz / (BrE)
ordinary clothing, especially when worn by someone who usually wears a uniform, such as a police officer or member of the military.
ordinary clothing, not uniform

Examples

The detective went undercover in plain clothes to observe the suspects.
Security staff in plain clothes patrolled the shopping mall.
Unlike the rest of the group in uniform, she wore plain clothes to blend in.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: uniform - a distinctive set of clothes worn by members of the same organization
