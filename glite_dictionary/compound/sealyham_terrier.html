Sealyham terrier

1. NOUN

/ ˈsiːliˌhæm ˈtɛriɚ / (AmE), / ˈsiːlihæm ˈtɛriə / (BrE)
a small breed of dog with a wiry white coat and short legs, first developed in Sealyham, Wales, for hunting purposes
a small, wire-haired Welsh terrier breed

Examples

The Sealyham terrier is known for its playful and friendly personality.
She has owned a Sealyham terrier since she was a child.
At the dog show, a proud Sealyham terrier walked confidently alongside its handler.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: terrier - a type of small, energetic dog originally bred for hunting

Etymology

Named after Sealyham, an estate in Wales where the breed originated.
