woodland white violet

1. NOUN

/ ˈwʊd.lənd waɪt ˈvaɪ.əl.ɪt / (AmE), / ˈwʊd.lənd waɪt ˈvaɪ.əl.ɪt / (BrE)
A short-stemmed violet native to eastern North America, having fragrant white flowers with purple veins.
fragrant white violet native to eastern North America

Examples

The woodland white violet is often found growing in shaded, moist forests throughout the eastern United States.
Gardeners prize the woodland white violet for its delicate, fragrant blossoms in early spring.
Researchers studied the pollinators attracted to the woodland white violet in its native habitat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: violet - a small plant with purple, blue, or white flowers
