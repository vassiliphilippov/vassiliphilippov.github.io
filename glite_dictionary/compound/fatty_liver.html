fatty liver

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæti ˈlɪvər / (AmE), / ˈfæti ˈlɪvə / (BrE)
A condition in which excess fat builds up in the liver, often leading to liver dysfunction.
excess fat accumulation in the liver
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that my fatty liver was likely caused by my diet and lack of exercise.
Alcohol abuse can significantly increase the risk of developing fatty liver disease.
Early detection of fatty liver can help prevent more serious liver conditions later on.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: steatosis - accumulation of fat in the liver cells
