European woolly thistle

1. NOUN

/ jʊˈroʊpiən ˈwʊli ˈθɪsəl / (AmE), / jʊəˈrəʊpiən ˈwʊli ˈθɪsl / (BrE)
a species of thistle (Cirsium eriophorum) native to western and central Europe and the Balkan Peninsula, known for its woolly, spiny appearance
a spiny, woolly thistle native to Europe

Examples

The European woolly thistle can reach heights of up to two meters in the wild.
Gardeners in the region sometimes cultivate European woolly thistle for its striking appearance.
The leaves of the European woolly thistle are covered in dense, white hairs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: woolly thistle - a species of thistle known for its woolly appearance
  • Degree Higher: thistle - a plant with leaves that have sharp points and purple flowers
