genus Monodon

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈmɒ.nəˌdɒn / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈmɒn.ə.dɒn / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Monodontidae, containing species such as the narwhal.
type genus of Monodontidae (narwhals)

Examples

The genus Monodon includes only one extant species, the narwhal.
Researchers studied the evolutionary history of the genus Monodon using fossil records.
The distinctive tusk of the narwhal makes genus Monodon unique among marine mammals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Monodon monoceros - the scientific name for the narwhal, a species within genus Monodon

Etymology

From Greek 'monos' (single) and 'odous, odontos' (tooth), referring to the narwhal's single long tusk.
