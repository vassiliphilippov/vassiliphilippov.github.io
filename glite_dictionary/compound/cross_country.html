cross country

1. NOUN

/ krɔs ˈkʌntri / (AmE), / krɒs ˈkʌntri / (BrE)
a race run across natural terrain, such as fields and woods, rather than on a track or road
a running race over open natural terrain

Examples

She trained all summer to compete in the cross country race at her school.
The muddy trails made yesterday's cross country event especially challenging for the runners.
He prefers cross country to track because he enjoys running through forests and across hills.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: marathon - a long-distance running race, typically 42.2 km
