reality television

1. NOUN

/ riˈæləti ˌtɛləˈvɪʒən / (AmE), / riˈalɪti ˌtɛlɪˈvɪʒən / (BrE)
a genre of television programming that features real people rather than actors and presents ostensibly unscripted real-life situations
unscripted TV genre with real people

Examples

Many viewers enjoy reality television for its unpredictable storylines and relatable participants.
The competition on reality television shows can become quite intense and dramatic.
Some critics claim that reality television often exaggerates real events for entertainment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: game show - a television program in which people compete for prizes in games of skill or chance
