drill hole

1. NOUN

/ drɪl hoʊl / (AmE), / drɪl həʊl / (BrE)
A hole or passage made by a drill, especially for exploration or sampling purposes.
hole made by a drill (exploratory)

Examples

The geologists analyzed samples taken from the drill hole to assess the mineral content.
After weeks of work, the crew finally completed the deepest drill hole in the region.
A narrow drill hole was used to insert sensors into the rock formation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: borehole - a deep narrow hole made in the ground, especially to locate water or oil
