sandbar shark

1. NOUN

/ ˈsændˌbɑr ʃɑrk / (AmE), / ˈsændˌbɑː ʃɑːk / (BrE)
a large, widely distributed shallow-water shark (Carcharhinus plumbeus) with tall dorsal and pectoral fins that appear as if dipped in ink
a species of shallow-water shark

Examples

The sandbar shark is commonly found in temperate and tropical coastal waters around the world.
Researchers tagged a young sandbar shark to monitor its migration patterns.
Fishermen often spot sandbar sharks swimming near reefs and sandbanks.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsændˌbɑr ʃɑrk / (AmE), / ˈsandbɑː ʃɑːk / (BrE)
a common species of gray shark (Carcharhinus plumbeus) found along the coasts of the middle Atlantic states, known for its sluggish behavior and occasionally caught by fishermen
a species of gray shark found along Atlantic coasts

Examples

The sandbar shark is frequently seen in shallow coastal waters during the summer months.
Fishermen sometimes encounter a sandbar shark while casting their lines near the shore.
Unlike some other sharks, the sandbar shark has a reputation for being relatively sluggish.
