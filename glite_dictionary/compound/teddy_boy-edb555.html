teddy boy

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛdi bɔɪ / (AmE), / ˈtɛdi bɔɪ / (BrE)
(in the UK) a young man of the 1950s and early 1960s who wore Edwardian-style clothes and was associated with rock and roll music and a distinct youth subculture.
British youth subculture with Edwardian style (1950s–60s)
british

Examples

The distinctive style of the teddy boys became a symbol of youth rebellion in 1950s Britain.
Groups of teddy boys would gather at dance halls to listen to rock and roll and show off their outfits.
The fashion of teddy boys influenced generations of British pop culture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rocker - a member of a 1950s/60s British motorcycle youth subculture
  • Antonym: mod - a member of a British youth subculture of the early 1960s, noted for stylish dress and modern jazz

Etymology

From English 'Teddy' (diminutive of Edward) and 'boy'; referencing the Edwardian style of dress.
