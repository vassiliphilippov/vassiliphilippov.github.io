dwarf juniper

1. NOUN

/ dwɔrf ˈdʒuːnɪpɚ / (AmE), / dwɔːf ˈdʒuːnɪpə / (BrE)
a low-growing or prostrate variety of the common juniper shrub, often used as ground cover in landscaping and gardening.
a low-growing variety of juniper shrub

Examples

The dwarf juniper is often planted in rock gardens for its attractive foliage and low maintenance.
Many landscapers choose dwarf juniper to provide year-round greenery along garden paths.
Unlike its taller relatives, dwarf juniper stays close to the ground and spreads slowly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: juniper - an evergreen shrub or small tree of the cypress family

2. NOUN

/ dwɔrf ˈdʒuːnɪpər / (AmE), / dwɔːf ˈdʒuːnɪpə / (BrE)
A low-growing or spreading species of juniper shrub, often used as ground cover.
a low, spreading species of juniper

Examples

The gardener planted several dwarf junipers along the pathway to provide year-round greenery.
Dwarf juniper can thrive in rocky soil where other plants struggle to grow.
The hillside was covered in dwarf juniper, giving the landscape a rugged, green appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: juniper - A type of evergreen shrub or tree with needle-like leaves and berry-like cones
