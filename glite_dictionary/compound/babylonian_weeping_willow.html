Babylonian weeping willow

1. NOUN

/ bəˌbloʊniən ˈwiːpɪŋ ˈwɪloʊ / (AmE), / bəˌbləʊniən ˈwiːpɪŋ ˈwɪləʊ / (BrE)
a type of willow tree with long, drooping branches and slender leaves, native to China and widely grown for ornamentation
ornamental willow tree with drooping branches

Examples

The Babylonian weeping willow is a popular choice for planting near ponds due to its graceful branches.
Gardeners prize the Babylonian weeping willow for its beautiful fall foliage and shade.
A row of Babylonian weeping willows lined the edge of the historic estate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weeping willow - willow tree with long, drooping branches, Salix babylonica
