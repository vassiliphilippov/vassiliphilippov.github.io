lumbar plexus

1. NOUN

/ ˈlʌmbɑr ˈplɛksəs / (AmE), / ˈlʌmbɑː ˈplɛksəs / (BrE)
a network of nerves in the lower back, formed by the lumbar spinal nerves and supplying the lower limb and lower abdominal wall
network of nerves in the lower back
medical

Examples

Damage to the lumbar plexus can result in pain or weakness in the legs.
The surgeon carefully avoided the lumbar plexus during the operation.
The lumbar plexus is responsible for innervating much of the lower abdomen and thigh.

Etymology

From Latin 'lumbus' (loin) and 'plexus' (network, braid).

2. NOUN

/ ˈlʌm.bɑr ˈplɛk.səs / (AmE), / ˈlʌm.bɑː ˈplɛk.səs / (BrE)
a network of nerves formed by the ventral branches of the first four lumbar spinal nerves, supplying nerves to the lower abdomen, pelvis, and leg
nerve network in the lower back
medical

Examples

Injury to the lumbar plexus can cause weakness or numbness in the legs.
The surgeon carefully avoided the lumbar plexus during the procedure.
The lumbar plexus is essential for motor and sensory functions of the lower limbs.

Etymology

From Latin 'lumbus' (loin) and 'plexus' (braid, network).
