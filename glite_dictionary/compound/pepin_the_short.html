Pepin the Short

1. PROPN

/ ˈpɛpɪn ðə ʃɔrt / (AmE), / ˈpɛpɪn ðə ʃɔːt / (BrE)
(c. 714–768) King of the Franks from 751 to 768; father of Charlemagne and founder of the Carolingian dynasty, known for defending papal interests.
king of the Franks, father of Charlemagne

Examples

Pepin the Short was crowned King of the Franks in 751, beginning a new royal dynasty.
The alliance between Pepin the Short and the Pope had lasting effects on European history.
Charlemagne, one of Europe's most famous rulers, was the son of Pepin the Short.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Charlemagne - King of the Franks and Holy Roman Emperor, son of Pepin the Short

Etymology

From Old French Pippin le Bref; 'Pepin' from a Frankish given name, 'the Short' referring to his stature.
