climbing maidenhair fern

1. NOUN

/ ˈklaɪmɪŋ ˈmeɪdənˌhɛr fɜrn / (AmE), / ˈklaɪmɪŋ ˈmeɪdənˌheə fɜːn / (BrE)
a tropical species of maidenhair fern, native to the Old World and naturalized in Jamaica and Florida, distinguished by its climbing or sprawling habit
a tropical climbing species of maidenhair fern

Examples

The climbing maidenhair fern has adapted well to the humid climate of Florida.
Gardeners often choose the climbing maidenhair fern for its delicate, feathery leaves and trailing stems.
In the wild, the climbing maidenhair fern can be found carpeting forest floors or climbing over rocks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maidenhair fern - any of various ferns of the genus Adiantum characterized by delicate fronds
