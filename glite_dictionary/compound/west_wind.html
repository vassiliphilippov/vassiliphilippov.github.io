west wind

1. NOUN

/ wɛst wɪnd / (AmE), / wɛst wɪnd / (BrE)
A wind that blows from the west toward the east.
wind blowing from the west

Examples

The west wind brought welcome relief from the summer heat.
Farmers often rely on the west wind to bring rain to their crops.
As evening fell, a gentle west wind rustled through the trees.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: east wind - wind that blows from east to west
