family Pterodactylidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli tɛroʊˌdæktɪˌlaɪdi / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli tɛrəˌdæktɪˌliːdiː / (BrE)
A family of extinct reptiles in the order Pterosauria, known for their winged forms and presence in the Jurassic and Cretaceous periods.
extinct reptile family in Pterosauria

Examples

Scientists have discovered new fossils belonging to the family Pterodactylidae in South America.
The family Pterodactylidae is known for its distinctive wing structure among pterosaurs.
Many species in the family Pterodactylidae lived during the late Jurassic period.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'Pterodactylus' (the type genus) and the suffix '-idae' denoting a zoological family.
