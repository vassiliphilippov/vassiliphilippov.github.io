Constitution State

1. PROPN

/ ˌkɑnstəˈtuːʃən steɪt / (AmE), / ˌkɒnstɪˈtjuːʃən steɪt / (BrE)
a nickname for the state of Connecticut, one of the original 13 colonies of the United States
nickname for Connecticut, USA

Examples

Connecticut is often called the Constitution State because of its early colonial charter.
The Constitution State boasts beautiful scenery and historic towns.
Many license plates in the Constitution State proudly display the nickname.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Connecticut - a state in the northeastern United States, one of the original 13 colonies

Etymology

Refers to Connecticut's adoption of the Fundamental Orders in 1639, often regarded as the first written constitution in America.
