indigo bunting

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪn.dɪˌɡoʊ ˈbʌn.tɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɪn.dɪˌɡəʊ ˈbʌn.tɪŋ / (BrE)
a small North American songbird, the male of which has bright deep blue plumage during the breeding season
small blue North American songbird

Examples

We spotted an indigo bunting perched on the fence during our walk in the countryside.
The male indigo bunting is known for its stunning blue feathers in the spring and summer.
Many birdwatchers travel to see the vibrant indigo bunting in its natural habitat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bunting - a seed-eating songbird related to finches
