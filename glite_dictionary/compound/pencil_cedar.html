pencil cedar

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛnsəl ˈsiːdər / (AmE), / ˈpɛnsəl ˈsiːdə / (BrE)
any of several juniper trees whose wood is used to make pencils, especially the eastern red cedar (Juniperus virginiana).
juniper tree used for pencil wood

Examples

Many pencils were once made from the wood of the pencil cedar.
The pencil cedar is prized for its straight grain and pleasant aroma.
Some species of pencil cedar are native to North America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Eastern red cedar - a common species of pencil cedar (Juniperus virginiana)
  • Synonym: juniper - evergreen shrub or small tree of the cypress family

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɛnsl ˌsidɚ / (AmE), / ˈpɛnsəl ˌsiːdə / (BrE)
The wood of a pencil cedar tree, commonly used for making pencils.
wood used for making pencils

Examples

The best drawing pencils are often made from pencil cedar because of its fine texture.
Traditional pencil cedar is prized for its pleasant scent and smooth grain.
Sustainable harvesting of pencil cedar is important for the pencil industry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cedar - an evergreen coniferous tree with fragrant wood
