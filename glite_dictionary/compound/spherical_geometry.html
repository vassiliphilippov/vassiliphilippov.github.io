spherical geometry

1. NOUN

/ ˌsfɪrɪkl dʒiˈɑːmətri / (AmE), / ˌsfɪərɪkl dʒiˈɒmətri / (BrE)
the branch of geometry that deals with the properties and relations of points, lines, and figures on the surface of a sphere
geometry of figures on a sphere

Examples

Sailors once used spherical geometry to chart their courses across the oceans.
In spherical geometry, the sum of the angles of a triangle exceeds 180 degrees.
The study of spherical geometry is essential in understanding global navigation.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: plane geometry - the geometry of figures on a flat surface

Etymology

From Greek sphaira (sphere) and geometria (geometry).
