streak of misery

1. NOUN

/ stɹik əv ˈmɪzəri / (AmE), / stɹiːk əv ˈmɪzəri / (BrE)
a prolonged and continuous period of great suffering or hardship
extended period of misery or hardship

Examples

After a long streak of misery, she finally found happiness in her new job.
The family faced a streak of misery following the sudden illness of their father.
He spoke about his streak of misery that lasted for most of his twenties.

Synset Examples

After a long streak of misery, things finally improved.
That long streak of misery taught him resilience.
She endured a long streak of misery during those years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: run of bad luck - a prolonged sequence of unfortunate events
  • Antonym: period of happiness - a time of happiness or good fortune
