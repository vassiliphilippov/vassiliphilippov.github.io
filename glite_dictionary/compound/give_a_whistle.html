to give a whistle

1. VERB

/ ɡɪv ə ˈwɪsəl / (AmE), / ɡɪv ə ˈwɪsl̩ / (BrE)
to produce a whistling sound, especially by pursing the lips or using a device, often to attract attention
make a whistling sound

Examples

The referee gave a whistle to signal the end of the match.
When the dog wandered off, she gave a whistle to call it back.
He gave a whistle when he saw his friends arriving.

Synset Examples

He gave a whistle to get everyone's attention at the park.
