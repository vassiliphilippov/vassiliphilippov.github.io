external carotid

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˈstɝːnəl kəˈrɑːtɪd / (AmE), / ɪkˈstɜːnəl kəˈrɒtɪd / (BrE)
the branch of the carotid artery that carries blood to the face, tongue, and outer regions of the head
branch of carotid artery in head and neck
medical

Examples

The surgeon carefully isolated the external carotid before proceeding with the operation.
A blockage in the external carotid can reduce blood flow to the facial region.
During the anatomy class, we traced the path of the external carotid on a medical diagram.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: internal carotid - the branch of the carotid artery supplying blood to the brain

Etymology

From Latin 'carotis' (carotid artery) and 'externus' (outer).
