game of chance

1. NOUN

/ ɡeɪm əv tʃæns / (AmE), / ɡeɪm əv tʃɑːns / (BrE)
A game in which the outcome depends mainly on luck rather than skill, often involving gambling.
game with outcome determined by luck

Examples

Many people visit casinos to play a game of chance like roulette or craps.
Lottery tickets are a classic example of a game of chance.
Gambling laws often regulate every game of chance in the country.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: game of skill - a game in which success depends mainly on the player's skill
