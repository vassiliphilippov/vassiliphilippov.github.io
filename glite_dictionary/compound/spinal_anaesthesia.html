spinal anaesthesia

1. NOUN

/ ˈspaɪnəl ˌænəsˈθiʒə / (AmE), / ˈspaɪnəl ˌanɪsˈθiːzɪə / (BrE)
a type of anesthesia produced by injecting an anesthetic into the space beneath the arachnoid membrane of the spinal cord, resulting in loss of sensation in the lower half of the body
anesthesia by injection near spinal cord
medical british

Examples

The patient received spinal anaesthesia before undergoing knee surgery.
Doctors commonly use spinal anaesthesia for lower abdominal operations.
Some people experience headaches after spinal anaesthesia, but these side effects are usually temporary.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: epidural anaesthesia - anesthesia produced by injection into the epidural space of the spinal column

Etymology

From 'spinal', relating to the spine, and 'anaesthesia', from Greek anaisthetos (without sensation).
