nonviolent resistance

1. NOUN

/ ˌnɑnˈvaɪələnt rɪˈzɪstəns / (AmE), / ˌnɒnˈvaɪələnt rɪˈzɪstəns / (BrE)
the practice of opposing authority, especially a government, without using violence, typically through methods like fasting, civil disobedience, or refusal to cooperate
peaceful protest against authority

Examples

Gandhi famously advocated nonviolent resistance as a way to gain independence from colonial rule.
Some activists believe that nonviolent resistance is the most effective means of achieving social change.
The protesters engaged in nonviolent resistance, refusing to leave the premises despite repeated orders from the police.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: civil disobedience - the refusal to comply with certain laws as a form of political protest
  • Antonym: violent resistance - opposing authority or government by violent means

Etymology

From non- (not) + violent (from Latin violentus) and resistance (from Latin resistentia).
