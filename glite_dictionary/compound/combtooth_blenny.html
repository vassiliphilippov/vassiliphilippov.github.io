combtooth blenny

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːmˌtuːθ ˈblɛni / (AmE), / ˈkɒmˌtuːθ ˈblɛni / (BrE)
a small marine fish of the family Blenniidae, usually scaleless and with comb-like teeth, typically found around rocky shores and noted for its territorial behavior
small marine fish with comb-like teeth

Examples

The combtooth blenny hides among the rocks and defends its territory aggressively.
Divers often spot a combtooth blenny peering out from a crevice along the reef.
Many aquarium enthusiasts keep combtooth blennies for their unique appearance and interesting behavior.

Etymology

Combtooth refers to the shape of the fish's teeth; blenny from Greek 'blennos', meaning 'slime'.
