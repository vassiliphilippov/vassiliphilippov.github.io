CD player

1. NOUN

/ siː diː ˈpleɪər / (AmE), / siː diː ˈpleɪə / (BrE)
A device for playing audio compact discs, either as a stand-alone unit or connected to other equipment.
device for playing compact discs

Examples

The living room CD player can hold up to five discs at once.
She bought a portable CD player to listen to music on the go.
Many teenagers in the 1990s owned a CD player in their bedrooms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compact disc - a small, flat, round disc used to store digital data, especially music

Etymology

CD is an initialism for 'compact disc'. 'Player' from Old English 'plegian' (to play) and modern use for devices that play media.
