Cape of Good Hope

1. PROPN

/ keɪp əv ɡʊd hoʊp / (AmE), / keɪp əv ɡʊd həʊp / (BrE)
a former province in western South Africa, now divided into several modern provinces, originally named after the nearby Cape of Good Hope.
former province of South Africa

Examples

During the colonial era, the Cape of Good Hope played a significant role in trade between Europe and Asia.
He was born in the Cape of Good Hope when it was still a unified province.
The history of the Cape of Good Hope is closely tied to Dutch and British colonization in southern Africa.

Etymology

Named after the nearby Cape of Good Hope, first rounded in 1488 by Portuguese explorer Bartolomeu Dias.

2. PROPN

/ ˌkeɪp əv ɡʊd ˈhoʊp / (AmE), / ˌkeɪp əv ɡʊd ˈhəʊp / (BrE)
a rocky headland on the Atlantic coast of southwestern South Africa, south of Cape Town, historically significant as a milestone on the sea route to the East
headland in southwestern South Africa

Examples

On our trip to South Africa, we visited the Cape of Good Hope and admired the dramatic scenery.
The Cape of Good Hope was once a crucial stop for ships traveling between Europe and Asia.
Many explorers celebrated rounding the Cape of Good Hope as a major milestone on their journeys.

Etymology

Named by Portuguese explorer Bartolomeu Dias in 1488 as 'Cabo da Boa Esperança', meaning 'Cape of Good Hope' in Portuguese.
