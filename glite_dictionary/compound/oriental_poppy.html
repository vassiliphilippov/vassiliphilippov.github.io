oriental poppy

1. NOUN

/ ɔːr.iˈɛn.t̬əl ˈpɑː.pi / (AmE), / ˌɔː.riˈen.təl ˈpɒp.i / (BrE)
a large, showy perennial poppy of Asia with bristly leaves and bright scarlet, pink, or orange flowers, often grown as a garden plant
Asiatic perennial poppy with showy flowers

Examples

The oriental poppy bloomed in vivid shades of orange and red throughout the garden.
Gardeners prize the oriental poppy for its dramatic, papery petals and early summer color.
After planting oriental poppy seeds last autumn, she was delighted to see them flourish in spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poppy - a flowering plant of the Papaveraceae family, known for showy flowers

Etymology

From Latin oriental-, from 'orientalis' (eastern), referring to its Asian origin; 'poppy' from Old English 'popæg'.
