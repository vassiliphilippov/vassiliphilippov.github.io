prompt box

1. NOUN

/ prɑːmpt bɑks / (AmE), / prɒmpt bɒks / (BrE)
a small booth projecting above the floor at the front of a stage, where the prompter sits and which opens toward the performers
booth for prompter at stage front

Examples

The actor glanced discreetly at the prompt box to check his next line.
In traditional theatres, the prompt box was placed just below the lip of the stage.
During rehearsals, the director often sat near the prompt box to give additional cues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prompter - person who assists actors by prompting forgotten lines
