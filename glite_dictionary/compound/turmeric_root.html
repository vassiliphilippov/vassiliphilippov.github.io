turmeric root

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɜːrmərɪk ruːt / (AmE), / ˈtɜːmərɪk ruːt / (BrE)
a perennial herb with a thick, knotted yellow rootstock and large, rounded leaves, native to northeastern United States
herb with yellow knotted rootstock

Examples

Native Americans have long used the turmeric root for its medicinal properties.
The turmeric root has a vibrant yellow color and a distinctive flavor.
You can find organic turmeric root at some health food stores.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Curcuma longa - plant species commonly known as turmeric (Asian variety)

Etymology

From 'turmeric,' which comes from Middle English 'turmeryte', ultimately from Old French or Medieval Latin.
