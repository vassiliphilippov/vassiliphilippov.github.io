Kurdistan Workers Party

1. PROPN

/ kɜːrdɪˌstɑːn ˈwɜːrkərz ˈpɑːrti / (AmE), / kɜːdɪˌstɑːn ˈwɜːkəz ˈpɑːti / (BrE)
a militant Kurdish political organization founded in 1978, seeking autonomy or independence for Kurds in Turkey, and designated as a terrorist group by several countries
Kurdish militant political organization in Turkey

Examples

The Turkish government has been in conflict with the Kurdistan Workers Party for decades.
Many countries have designated the Kurdistan Workers Party as a terrorist organization.
Talks between the government and the Kurdistan Workers Party have occasionally led to temporary ceasefires.

Etymology

The name is a direct translation from the Kurdish and Turkish title 'Partiya Karkerên Kurdistanê.'
