last roundup

1. NOUN

/ læst ˈraʊndˌʌp / (AmE), / lɑːst ˈraʊndʌp / (BrE)
the final event, gathering, or occasion in a series, often used figuratively for a concluding occurrence
final event or gathering in a series

Examples

Everyone gathered for the last roundup before the project ended.
The concert series ended with a memorable last roundup featuring all the performers.
As retirement approached, she knew the upcoming meeting would be her last roundup at the company.

Synset Examples

The last roundup had many participants.
We attended the last roundup of the season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: finale - the last part of a performance, event, or series
  • Synonym: conclusion - the end or final part of something

Etymology

From the noun 'roundup' (a gathering of cattle or people), with 'last' indicating finality; popularized by cowboy culture.
