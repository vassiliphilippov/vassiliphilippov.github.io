Anatolian language

1. NOUN

/ ˌænəˈtoʊliən ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌanəˈtəʊlɪən ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
an extinct branch of the Indo-European language family, known from ancient inscriptions and important for reconstructing Proto-Indo-European.
extinct Indo-European language branch

Examples

Many ancient inscriptions in Hittite, an Anatolian language, have been discovered in present-day Turkey.
Linguists study the Anatolian language branch to better understand the origins of Indo-European languages.
Luwian, spoken in Bronze Age Anatolia, is another example of an Anatolian language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Indo-European - relating to the family of languages including most of the languages spoken in Europe and many in Asia

Etymology

Anatolia, a historical region in modern-day Turkey, from Greek Anatolē (East), plus 'language'.
