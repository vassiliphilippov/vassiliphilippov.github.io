seaside scrub oak

1. NOUN

/ ˈsiˌsaɪd skrʌb oʊk / (AmE), / ˈsiːsaɪd skrʌb əʊk / (BrE)
a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the southeastern United States, often forming dense thickets in sandy coastal areas
an evergreen shrub or tree of coastal southeastern US

Examples

The seaside scrub oak thrives in the sandy soils along the Atlantic coast.
Dense thickets of seaside scrub oak make hiking through these dunes quite difficult.
Conservationists are studying the unique ecosystem created by seaside scrub oak habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scrub oak - any of several low, shrubby varieties of oak
