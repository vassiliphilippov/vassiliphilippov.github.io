Leydig cell

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪdɪɡ sɛl / (AmE), / ˈleɪdɪɡ sɛl / (BrE)
A cell found in the testes that produces and secretes the hormone testosterone.
testicular cell producing testosterone
medical

Examples

Testosterone is produced mainly by the Leydig cells in the testes.
Damage to the Leydig cell can negatively impact hormone levels and fertility.
Researchers are studying how the Leydig cells respond to various treatments for low testosterone.

Etymology

Named after German anatomist Franz Leydig (1821–1908), who first described these cells.
