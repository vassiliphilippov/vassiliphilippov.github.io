Old World oriole

1. NOUN

/ oʊld wɜrld ˈɔːriˌoʊl / (AmE), / əʊld wɜːld ˈɔːriəʊl / (BrE)
a mostly tropical songbird of the family Oriolidae, typically with the male being bright orange and black, found in Europe, Asia, and Africa
tropical songbird (Oriolidae family)

Examples

The male Old World oriole is known for its vivid orange and black feathers.
While birdwatching in India, we spotted an Old World oriole perched in the treetops.
Old World orioles are admired for their melodic songs in tropical forests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oriole - a songbird with bright plumage, especially yellow or orange and black
