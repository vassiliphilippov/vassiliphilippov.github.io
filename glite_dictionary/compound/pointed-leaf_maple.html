pointed-leaf maple

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɔɪntɪd lif ˈmeɪpəl / (AmE), / ˈpɔɪntɪd liːf ˈmeɪpəl / (BrE)
A small, shrubby Japanese maple tree or shrub with leaves having 5 to 7 sharply pointed lobes, turning yellow in autumn.
Japanese shrub with pointed-lobed leaves

Examples

The pointed-leaf maple turns a brilliant yellow in autumn, making it a favorite in Japanese gardens.
Experts recommend planting a pointed-leaf maple in partially shaded areas for best leaf color.
New shoots sprouted quickly on the young pointed-leaf maple after the spring rains.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Acer palmatum - Japanese maple species often called 'pointed-leaf maple'
