record-breaker

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛkərd ˌbreɪkər / (AmE), / ˈrɛkɔːd ˌbreɪkə / (BrE)
A person or thing that surpasses a previously set record, especially in sports, achievements, or other measurable activities.
person or thing breaking a record

Examples

She became a record-breaker after finishing the marathon in under two hours.
The company is a record-breaker for sales in the last quarter.
He dreamed of being a record-breaker since he first started swimming.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: champion - a person who has surpassed all rivals in a competition
  • Antonym: loser - a person who does not win or breaks a record

Etymology

From English 'record' (an achievement) + 'breaker' (one who breaks).
