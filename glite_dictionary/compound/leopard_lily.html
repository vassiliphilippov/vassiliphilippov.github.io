leopard lily

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛpərd ˌlɪli / (AmE), / ˈlɛpəd ˌlɪli / (BrE)
A garden plant with a seed capsule that, when ripe, splits open to reveal a cluster of seeds resembling a blackberry, typically referring to either Iris domestica or related species.
a plant with blackberry-like seed clusters

Examples

The gardener admired the leopard lily for its striking orange blooms and unique seed pods.
After flowering, the leopard lily produces seed clusters that look surprisingly like blackberries.
Many people grow the leopard lily in their gardens for its showy flowers and interesting fruit.

Etymology

From the distinctive spotted appearance of the plant's flowers, resembling a leopard's spots.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɛpərd ˌlɪli / (AmE), / ˈlɛpəd ˌlɪli / (BrE)
a lily native to the southeastern United States, with cup-shaped flowers featuring deep yellow to scarlet recurved petals, often marked with spots
a spotted lily native to the southeastern US

Examples

We admired the striking blooms of the leopard lily along the woodland trail.
Gardeners often plant the leopard lily for its vibrant, spotted petals and unique appearance.
The leopard lily thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial sunlight.

/ ˈlɛpərd ˌlɪli / (AmE), / ˈlɛpəd ˌlɪli / (BrE)

western US lily with spotted flowers

Examples

The leopard lily blooms in early summer with beautiful maroon-spotted petals.
Gardeners in California often cultivate the leopard lily for its vibrant colors.
