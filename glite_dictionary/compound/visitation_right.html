visitation right

1. NOUN

/ ˌvɪzɪˈteɪʃən raɪt / (AmE), / ˌvɪzɪˈteɪʃən raɪt / (BrE)
the legal right granted by a court to a parent or other relative who does not have custody of a child, allowing them to visit the child regularly
legal right to visit a child after loss of custody
legal

Examples

After the divorce, John was granted visitation rights so he could see his daughter every other weekend.
The court determined the visitation right schedule during the custody hearing.
Her grandparents fought for visitation rights after her parents separated.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: custody - the legal right or duty to care for someone, especially a child, after separation or divorce
