venous blood system

1. NOUN

/ ˈviː.nəs blʌd ˈsɪs.təm / (AmE), / ˈviː.nəs blʌd ˈsɪs.təm / (BrE)
the network of veins in an animal's body that carries blood toward the heart
network of veins in animals
medical

Examples

Diseases affecting the venous blood system can cause serious circulatory problems in mammals.
Researchers studied the flow of blood through the venous blood system of fish.
Blockages in the venous blood system may result in swelling or tissue damage.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: arterial blood system - the network of arteries carrying blood away from the heart
