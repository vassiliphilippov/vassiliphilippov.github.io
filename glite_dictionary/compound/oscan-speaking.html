Oscan-speaking

1. ADJ

/ ˈɑːskən ˌspiːkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɒskən ˌspiːkɪŋ / (BrE)
able to speak or communicate in Oscan, an ancient Italic language.
speaking the Oscan language

Examples

Archaeologists discovered evidence of an Oscan-speaking community in the ruins.
The Oscan-speaking tribes inhabited southern Italy before the rise of Rome.
Few modern scholars are Oscan-speaking, as the language is long extinct.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Latin-speaking - able to speak or communicate in Latin
  • Synonym: Oscan - the ancient Italic language of the Oscan people

Etymology

Formed from 'Oscan', referring to the ancient Italic language, and 'speaking'. 'Oscan' from Latin 'Oscus', the language of the Oscan people.
