United Mine Workers

1. PROPN

/ juˈnaɪtɪd maɪn ˈwɜrkərz / (AmE), / juːˈnaɪtɪd maɪn ˈwɜːkəz / (BrE)
a North American industrial labor union representing mine workers, particularly in the United States and Canada
North American mine workers' union

Examples

The United Mine Workers played a key role in improving labor conditions in the early twentieth century.
Membership in the United Mine Workers grew rapidly during the coal strikes of the 1930s.
John L. Lewis was a prominent leader of the United Mine Workers union.
