carbon tetrahalide

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑrbən tɛtrəˈhælaɪd / (AmE), / ˈkɑːbən tɛtrəˈhalʌɪd / (BrE)
any chemical compound in which a carbon atom is bonded to four halogen atoms
compound of carbon and four halogens

Examples

Carbon tetrachloride is a well-known carbon tetrahalide used as a solvent.
The properties of each carbon tetrahalide depend on which halogens are attached to the carbon atom.
Chemists often synthesize various carbon tetrahalide compounds to study their chemical behavior.

Etymology

From 'carbon' (chemical element) and 'tetrahalide', from Greek 'tetra-' (four) and 'halide' (salt from a halogen element).
