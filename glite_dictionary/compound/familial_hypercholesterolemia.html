familial hypercholesterolemia

1. NOUN

/ fəˈmɪljəl ˌhaɪpərkoʊˌlɛstərəˈliːmiə / (AmE), / fəˈmɪliəl ˌhʌɪpəkɒˌlɛstərəˈliːmɪə / (BrE)
a hereditary medical condition characterized by very high levels of cholesterol in the blood and early onset of atherosclerosis
hereditary disorder with high cholesterol
medical

Examples

Early diagnosis of familial hypercholesterolemia can significantly improve a patient's long-term health outcomes.
The condition known as familial hypercholesterolemia often leads to heart disease if left untreated.
Doctors recommend genetic testing for familial hypercholesterolemia in families with a history of high cholesterol.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hypercholesterolemia - an abnormally high level of cholesterol in the blood
  • Degree Higher: atherosclerosis - disease of the arteries with fatty deposits

Etymology

From Latin familia (family) and Greek hyper- (over) + kholē (bile) + stērós (solid) + haima (blood), referring to high blood cholesterol running in families.
