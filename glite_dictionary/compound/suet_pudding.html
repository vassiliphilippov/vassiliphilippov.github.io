suet pudding

1. NOUN

/ ˈsuːɪt ˈpʊdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsuː.ɪt ˈpʊd.ɪŋ / (BrE)
A traditional British pudding made from suet and flour, often sweetened or savory, and usually steamed or boiled.
steamed or boiled pudding with suet
british

Examples

My grandmother made a delicious suet pudding filled with dried fruits for Christmas.
The chef demonstrated how to prepare a savory suet pudding with steak and kidney filling.
A classic suet pudding requires slow steaming to achieve the perfect texture.

Etymology

From 'suet', the hard fat of beef or mutton, and 'pudding', originally from Middle English 'poding'.
