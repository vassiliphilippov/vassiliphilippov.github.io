dental orthopaedics

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛnt̬l ɔrθoʊˈpiːdɪks / (AmE), / ˈdɛntl ɔːˌθəʊˈpiːdɪks / (BrE)
the branch of dentistry concerned with the prevention or correction of irregularities of the teeth and jaws
branch of dentistry for correcting teeth irregularities
medical

Examples

After consulting a specialist in dental orthopaedics, she received braces to correct her bite.
Dental orthopaedics plays a crucial role in guiding jaw growth in young patients.
Advancements in dental orthopaedics have significantly improved outcomes for children with misaligned teeth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orthodontics - the dental specialty dealing with the correction of teeth alignment

Etymology

From Latin 'dens' (tooth) and Greek 'orthos' (straight) + 'pais, paidos' (child), relating to straightening children's teeth and jaws.
