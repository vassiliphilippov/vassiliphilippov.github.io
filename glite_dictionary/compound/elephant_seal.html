elephant seal

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛlɪfənt sil / (AmE), / ˈɛlɪfənt siːl / (BrE)
A large seal of the genus Mirounga, notable for the male's trunk-like snout, found in northern and southern oceans.
a large seal with a trunk-like snout

Examples

The male elephant seal can weigh up to 4,000 kilograms during the breeding season.
We watched a group of elephant seals resting on the beach in California.
The elephant seal is known for its distinctive, trunk-like nose.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seal - a marine mammal with flippers and a streamlined body

Etymology

Named for the trunk-like snout resembling an elephant’s trunk.
