family Caenolestidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli siːˌænoʊlɛˈstɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli siːˌanəʊlɛˈstɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of small marsupials native to southern South America, commonly known as shrew opossums.
a family of small South American marsupials

Examples

The family Caenolestidae includes several species of shrew-like marsupials found in Andean forests.
Scientists have studied the evolutionary history of the family Caenolestidae to understand marsupial dispersal in South America.
Few people are familiar with the unique members of the family Caenolestidae, also known as shrew opossums.

Etymology

From New Latin 'Caenolestidae', based on the genus 'Caenolestes'.
