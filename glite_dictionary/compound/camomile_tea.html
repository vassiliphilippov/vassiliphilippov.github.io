camomile tea

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæməˌmiːl tiː, kəˈmoʊməl tiː / (AmE), / ˈkæməmʌɪl tiː, ˈkaməmiːl tiː / (BrE)
a drink made by infusing dried camomile flowers in hot water, often used for its calming properties
herbal infusion made from camomile flowers

Examples

She drank a cup of camomile tea before going to bed to help her relax.
Many people find that camomile tea soothes an upset stomach.
Would you like some camomile tea or do you prefer green tea?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: herbal tea - any drink made by infusing herbs or plants other than tea leaves in water

Etymology

From camomile, from Latin 'chamaemelum', from Greek 'khamaimēlon' (earth apple).
