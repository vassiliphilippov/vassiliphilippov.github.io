class warfare

1. NOUN

/ klæs ˈwɔrˌfɛr / (AmE), / klɑːs ˈwɔːˌfeə / (BrE)
conflict or hostility between social or economic classes, especially between the wealthy and the working class
conflict between social or economic classes

Examples

The politician accused her opponents of encouraging class warfare with their tax proposals.
Debates over healthcare often highlight deeper issues of class warfare in society.
Some historians argue that class warfare has driven major political changes throughout history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: class struggle - conflict between different social classes
  • Antonym: class solidarity - unity or agreement of feeling among members of a class

2. NOUN

/ klæs ˈwɔrˌfɛr / (AmE), / klɑːs ˈwɔːˌfeə / (BrE)
Conflict between different social classes, especially between those perceived as having power or wealth and those who do not.
conflict between social classes

Examples

The politician was accused of encouraging class warfare with his divisive rhetoric.
Industrialization in the 19th century led to intense class warfare in many European countries.
Debates about wealth redistribution often revolve around the idea of class warfare.
