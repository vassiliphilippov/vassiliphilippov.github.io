tree farm

1. NOUN

/ tri fɑrm / (AmE), / tri fɑːm / (BrE)
A forest or area of land where trees are grown and managed for commercial purposes such as timber production.
area where trees are commercially grown

Examples

The company owns a large tree farm that supplies timber to several factories.
She visited a tree farm every December to select a fresh Christmas tree.
Maintaining a tree farm requires careful planning and sustainable forestry practices.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plantation - a large farm or estate for growing crops, often for commercial purposes
  • Degree Higher: forest - a large area covered chiefly with trees and undergrowth
