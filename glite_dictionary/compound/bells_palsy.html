Bell's palsy

1. NOUN

/ bɛlz ˈpælzi / (AmE), / bɛlz ˈpɔːlzi / (BrE)
a condition causing sudden, temporary weakness or paralysis of the muscles on one side of the face, usually resulting from inflammation of the facial nerve
temporary facial muscle weakness
medical

Examples

After experiencing sudden facial drooping, Maria learned she had Bell's palsy.
Symptoms of Bell's palsy may include difficulty closing one eye and a drooping mouth.
Many people recover fully from Bell's palsy within a few weeks.

Synset Examples

She was diagnosed with Bell's palsy last year.

Etymology

Named after Sir Charles Bell (1774–1842), Scottish surgeon who first described the condition.
