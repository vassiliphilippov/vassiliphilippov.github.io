Cook Strait

1. PROPN

/ kʊk streɪt / (AmE), / kʊk streɪt / (BrE)
the narrow body of water separating the North Island and South Island of New Zealand
strait between New Zealand's main islands

Examples

The ferry crossed the Cook Strait during a stormy evening.
Many marine species migrate through the Cook Strait each year.
She admired the dramatic views while sailing across Cook Strait.

Etymology

Named after Captain James Cook (1728–1779), British explorer.
