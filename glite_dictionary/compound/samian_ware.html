Samian ware

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪ.mi.ən wɛr / (AmE), / ˈseɪ.mi.ən weə / (BrE)
a type of ancient reddish-brown earthenware pottery made from clay found on the Greek island of Lemnos
ancient reddish-brown pottery from Lemnos

Examples

Archaeologists uncovered fragments of Samian ware at the site of the ancient settlement.
Collectors prize Samian ware for its distinctive color and fine workmanship.
The museum's exhibit included several intact pieces of Samian ware from the Roman era.

Etymology

Named after Samos (Samian), an island near Lemnos, associated with similar pottery.
