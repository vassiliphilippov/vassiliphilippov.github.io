Oregon lily

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrɪɡən ˈlɪli / (AmE), / ˈɒrɪɡən ˈlɪli / (BrE)
a lily native to western North America, noted for its showy orange-red flowers spotted with purple
North American lily with orange-red, spotted flowers

Examples

The Oregon lily blooms in late spring along the forest edges of the Pacific Northwest.
Gardeners admire the Oregon lily for its vibrant, spotted petals and elegant appearance.
Wild populations of the Oregon lily are declining due to habitat loss.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lily - a plant with large, prominent flowers, especially of the genus Lilium
