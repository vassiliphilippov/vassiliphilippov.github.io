pine knot

1. NOUN

/ paɪn nɑt / (AmE), / paɪn nɒt / (BrE)
A hard, dense part of pine wood formed at a branch joint, often used as fuel because it burns brightly.
hard part of pine wood at branch joint, used as fuel

Examples

The old fireplace was filled with pine knots that crackled as they burned.
Because pine knot burns so hot, it was prized for starting fires in rural homes.
He tossed a pine knot onto the fire, and the room immediately filled with a warm, bright glow.
