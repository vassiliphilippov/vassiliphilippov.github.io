brown bread

1. NOUN

/ braʊn brɛd / (AmE), / braʊn brɛd / (BrE)
Bread made using whole wheat flour, giving it a brown color and a denser texture than white bread.
whole wheat bread

Examples

She prefers to eat brown bread because it's healthier than white bread.
At breakfast, I had toast made from brown bread and some jam.
Many people choose brown bread for its higher fiber content.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wholemeal bread - bread made from whole wheat flour, used especially in British English
  • Antonym: white bread - bread made from refined wheat flour, typically soft and pale

2. NOUN

/ braʊn brɛd / (AmE), / braʊn brɛd / (BrE)
Dark bread made from a mixture of wholemeal flour, cornmeal, molasses, and other ingredients, often steamed rather than baked.
dark bread made with wholemeal and molasses

Examples

She served slices of brown bread with homemade jam for breakfast.
The restaurant is known for its traditional brown bread, which is moist and slightly sweet.
I prefer brown bread to white bread because of its richer flavor and texture.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: white bread - bread made from refined wheat flour
