Congregation of the Inquisition

1. PROPN

/ kɑŋˌɡrɛɡəˈʃən əv ði ˌɪnkwəˈzɪʃən / (AmE), / kɒŋˌɡrɛɡəˈʃən əv ði ˌɪnkwɪˈzɪʃən / (BrE)
the Roman Catholic church tribunal established in Italy in 1542 to combat Protestantism and heresy, known for the trial of Galileo and others
Catholic tribunal combating Protestantism, est. 1542

Examples

The Congregation of the Inquisition played a central role in the trial of Galileo in the 17th century.
Many scholars faced the scrutiny of the Congregation of the Inquisition during the Counter-Reformation.
Historical records detail the procedures followed by the Congregation of the Inquisition in pursuing cases of heresy.
