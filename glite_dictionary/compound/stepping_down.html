stepping down

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɛpɪŋ ˌdaʊn / (AmE), / ˈstɛpɪŋ ˌdaʊn / (BrE)
the act of formally resigning from a position of authority or responsibility
formal resignation from position or power

Examples

After months of speculation, the CEO's stepping down shocked the entire industry.
The mayor's stepping down was announced at a press conference yesterday.
His stepping down from the board cleared the way for new leadership.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resignation - the act of giving up a job or position voluntarily

2. NOUN

/ ˈstɛpɪŋ daʊn / (AmE), / ˈstɛpɪŋ daʊn / (BrE)
the act of formally resigning from an official position or role, especially one of leadership or authority
act of resigning from a position

Examples

After twenty years as CEO, his stepping down surprised everyone in the company.
The party leader's stepping down marked a turning point in the election campaign.
Her stepping down allowed a younger generation to take over the organization.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resignation - the act of leaving a job or position voluntarily
  • Synonym: abdication - formal act of giving up throne or leadership
