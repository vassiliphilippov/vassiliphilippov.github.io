genus Loris

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˈlɔːrɪs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈlɒrɪs / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Lorisidae, comprising slender, nocturnal, arboreal primates native to southern and southeastern Asia.
genus of slender nocturnal primates (Lorisidae)

Examples

The genus Loris contains small, nocturnal primates known for their large eyes and slow movements.
Zoologists debated the classification of species within the genus Loris after new fossils were found.
Conservation efforts focus on protecting habitats suitable for members of the genus Loris.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Lorisidae - the primate family including lorises

Etymology

From Latin 'genus' meaning 'kind' or 'type' and 'Loris', the formal biogenus name possibly from Dutch 'loeris' (simpleton, clown).
