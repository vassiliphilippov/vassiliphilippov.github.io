Sinitic language

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈnɪtɪk ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / sɪˈnɪtɪk ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
any of the languages belonging to a major branch of the Sino-Tibetan language family, including Mandarin, Cantonese, and related varieties spoken chiefly in China and surrounding areas.
a major branch of Chinese languages

Examples

Mandarin is the most widely spoken Sinitic language in the world.
Cantonese and Hokkien are both classified as Sinitic languages.
Scholars study the historical development of the Sinitic language family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Chinese - a language or group of related languages spoken in China; part of the Sinitic branch

Etymology

From Latin 'Sinae' (China) + '-itic' (relating to).
