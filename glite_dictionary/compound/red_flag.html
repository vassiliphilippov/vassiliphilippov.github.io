red flag

1. NOUN

/ rɛd flæɡ / (AmE), / rɛd flaɡ / (BrE)
a red flag displayed as a warning signal, especially to indicate danger or caution
a warning flag, signal of danger

Examples

The lifeguard raised a red flag to warn swimmers about dangerous currents.
She noticed several red flags in the safety inspection report.
A red flag flew at the entrance to the construction site.

Synset Examples

we didn't swim at the beach because the red flag was up

Related Terms

  • Synonym: warning - something that serves to warn of danger
  • Synonym: signal - a sound or visual sign giving information or instructions

2. NOUN

/ ˌrɛd ˈflæɡ / (AmE), / ˌrɛd ˈflæɡ / (BrE)
something that serves as a warning or provocation, often indicating danger, a problem, or the need for immediate attention
warning sign or provocation

Examples

Her constant lateness was a red flag to her manager.
Ignoring these symptoms can be a red flag for serious health issues.
For some people, not returning messages promptly is a major red flag in a relationship.

Synset Examples

doing that is like waving a red flag in front of a bull

Related Terms

  • Synonym: warning sign - an indication that something bad might happen
  • Antonym: green light - signal or indication to proceed; opposite of warning

Etymology

From the use of red flags as signals for warning or danger, especially in racing and labor disputes.

3. NOUN

/ rɛd flæɡ / (AmE), / rɛd flaɡ / (BrE)
A red banner or emblem used as a symbol of socialist revolution or left-wing political movements.
emblem of socialist revolution

Examples

The protesters marched through the streets, waving a red flag as a sign of solidarity.
During the revolution, the red flag became a powerful symbol of change.
The red flag has been associated with socialist movements since the 19th century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: banner - a long strip of cloth bearing a slogan or design, often used in protests

4. NOUN

/ rɛd flæɡ / (AmE), / rɛd flaɡ / (BrE)
a warning sign or indication that there may be a problem or danger
warning sign of trouble or danger

Examples

Her unexplained absences at work were a serious red flag to her supervisor.
In a job interview, vague answers can be a red flag for potential employers.
He noticed several red flags in the apartment contract before deciding not to sign.

Synset Examples

The company's lack of transparency raised a red flag for investors.
Ignoring that red flag could lead to serious problems.
Her sudden silence was a red flag in their relationship.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: warning sign - something that indicates a potential problem or danger
  • Antonym: green flag - an indication that something is acceptable or trustworthy

Etymology

From the traditional use of a red flag to signal danger or warning, especially in military or maritime contexts.
