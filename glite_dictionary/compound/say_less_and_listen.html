to say less and listen

1. VERB

/ seɪ lɛs ənd ˈlɪsən / (AmE), / seɪ lɛs ənd ˈlɪsən / (BrE)
to refrain from speaking and instead pay attention to what others are saying
speak less and pay attention

Examples

In meetings, it can be wise to say less and listen to others’ perspectives before giving your opinion.
He realized he needed to say less and listen if he wanted to learn from his mentors.
Teachers often encourage students to say less and listen during class discussions.

Synset Examples

Sometimes, it's better to just say less and listen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to keep quiet - not say anything or remain silent
  • Synonym: to pay attention - focus on listening or observing
