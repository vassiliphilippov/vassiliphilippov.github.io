wild crocus

1. NOUN

/ waɪld ˈkroʊkəs / (AmE), / waɪld ˈkrəʊkəs / (BrE)
a short, hairy perennial plant with early spring blue-violet or lilac flowers, native to North America and Siberia
perennial plant with blue-violet or lilac flowers

Examples

In early spring, clusters of wild crocus appear on the hillsides, adding color to the melting snow.
The wild crocus is a hardy plant that thrives in the prairies of North America.
Photographers travel each year to Siberia to capture the beauty of the blooming wild crocus.
