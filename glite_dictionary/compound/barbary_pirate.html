Barbary pirate

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑːrbəri ˈpaɪrət / (AmE), / ˈbɑːbəri ˈpaɪərət / (BrE)
a pirate operating along the Barbary Coast (North Africa), especially between the 16th and 19th centuries, known for attacking ships and coastal towns
pirate from the Barbary Coast, North Africa

Examples

Ships feared attacks by Barbary pirates when sailing through the Mediterranean in the 18th century.
European nations paid tribute to avoid being raided by Barbary pirates.
The film features a group of Barbary pirates capturing a merchant vessel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corsair - pirate, especially from the Mediterranean, often linked to the Barbary Coast

Etymology

From 'Barbary', the historical European name for the coastal regions of North Africa, and 'pirate'.
