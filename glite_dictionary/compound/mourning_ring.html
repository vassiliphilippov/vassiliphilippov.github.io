mourning ring

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɔrnɪŋ rɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmɔːnɪŋ rɪŋ / (BrE)
A ring worn to commemorate and honor a deceased person, often inscribed or containing a lock of hair of the dead.
ring worn in memory of a deceased person

Examples

She wore a mourning ring engraved with her grandmother's initials.
Victorian mourning rings often contained a lock of the deceased person's hair.
The mourning ring was passed down through generations as a family heirloom.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mourning jewelry - jewelry worn in remembrance of the dead

Etymology

From 'mourning' (period of grieving for the dead) and 'ring' (circular band worn as jewelry).
