phylum Platyhelminthes

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪləm ˌplætiˌhɛlˈmɪnθiːz / (AmE), / ˈfʌɪləm ˌplatɪhɛlˈmɪnθiːz / (BrE)
a phylum of invertebrate animals known as flatworms, characterized by a soft, flattened body and lacking specialized respiratory and circulatory systems
phylum of flatworms
medical

Examples

The phylum Platyhelminthes includes organisms such as planarians and tapeworms.
Biology students studied the anatomy of animals in the phylum Platyhelminthes under the microscope.
Some parasites that infect humans belong to the phylum Platyhelminthes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flatworm - a soft-bodied invertebrate of the phylum Platyhelminthes

Etymology

From Greek 'platy-' (flat) and 'helminth' (worm), with 'phylum' from Greek 'phylon' (race, tribe).
