Cakchiquel language

1. NOUN

/ kɑːkˈtʃikəl ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / kækˈtʃɪkəl ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Mayan language spoken by the Cakchiquel people, primarily in the central highlands of Guatemala
a Mayan language of Guatemala

Examples

The Cakchiquel language is still spoken by thousands of people in Guatemala today.
Scholars have published several studies on the grammar of the Cakchiquel language.
Efforts are underway to preserve and promote the Cakchiquel language among younger generations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mayan language - languages of the Mayan family spoken in Mesoamerica

Etymology

From Cakchiquel, the indigenous people who speak the language, itself of Mayan origin.
