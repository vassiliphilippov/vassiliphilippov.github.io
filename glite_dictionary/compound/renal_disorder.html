renal disorder

1. NOUN

/ ˈriː.nəl dɪsˈɔr.dɚ / (AmE), / ˈriː.nəl dɪsˈɔː.də / (BrE)
A disease or abnormal condition that affects the kidneys.
disease affecting the kidneys
medical

Examples

Patients with a renal disorder often require ongoing monitoring of their blood and urine.
The doctor explained that her fatigue was likely related to an underlying renal disorder.
Early diagnosis of a renal disorder can significantly improve treatment outcomes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kidney disease - disease affecting the kidneys
  • Degree Higher: renal failure - loss of kidney function

Etymology

From Latin 'renalis' (of the kidney) and 'disorder' (disturbance of function).
