cherry laurel

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɛri ˈlɔrəl / (AmE), / ˈtʃɛri ˈlɒrəl / (BrE)
a small evergreen tree or shrub with glossy leaves and white flowers, native to the southern United States, often grown as an ornamental plant
evergreen tree or shrub with glossy leaves and white flowers

Examples

The garden was bordered by a row of cherry laurels that bloomed in early spring.
Many homeowners plant cherry laurel as a hedge for its dense foliage and pretty flowers.
The cherry laurel thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: laurel - evergreen shrub or small tree with aromatic leaves

2. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɛri ˈlɔrəl / (AmE), / ˈtʃɛri ˈlɒrəl / (BrE)
a Eurasian evergreen shrub or small tree, Prunus laurocerasus, with glossy leaves, clusters of white flowers, and aromatic oil, often cultivated as an ornamental plant
evergreen shrub or small tree, Prunus laurocerasus

Examples

The gardener planted a cherry laurel hedge along the driveway for privacy.
Cherry laurel is prized for its shiny leaves and fragrant white blossoms.
Care should be taken when handling cherry laurel, as some parts of the plant are toxic.

Etymology

Named for its cherry-like fruit and laurel-like leaves; 'laurel' from Latin 'laurus'.
