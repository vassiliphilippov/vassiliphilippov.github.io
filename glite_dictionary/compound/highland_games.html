Highland Games

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪ.lənd ɡeɪmz / (AmE), / ˈhaɪ.lənd ɡeɪmz / (BrE)
a series of traditional Scottish athletic competitions and cultural events, often featuring activities such as caber tossing, tug-of-war, and dancing
traditional Scottish athletic competitions

Examples

We watched the Highland Games and marveled at the strength of the competitors.
Every summer, thousands of tourists attend the Highland Games in Scotland.
The traditions of the Highland Games have been preserved for centuries.

Synset Examples

They traveled to Jockland for the annual Highland Games.

Etymology

From 'Highland', referring to the Scottish Highlands, and 'Games', meaning sporting contests.
