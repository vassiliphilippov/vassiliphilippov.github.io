genus Mimus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈmaɪ.məs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈmiː.məs / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Mimidae, comprising the typical mockingbirds native to the Americas.
type genus of mockingbirds (Mimidae)

Examples

The genus Mimus includes several species commonly known as mockingbirds.
Researchers identified the bird as part of the genus Mimus after noticing its mimicking behavior.
The genus Mimus is widespread throughout North and South America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mockingbird - a bird known for imitating the songs of other birds

Etymology

From Latin mimus, meaning 'mimic' or 'actor', referring to the bird's ability to mimic sounds.
