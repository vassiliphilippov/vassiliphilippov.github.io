km/s

1. NOUN

/ kiː ɛm pər səkənd / (AmE), / keɪ ɛm pə sɪkənd / (BrE)
a unit of speed equal to one kilometer traveled per second
unit of speed (kilometers per second)

Examples

The spacecraft reached a speed of 10 km/s during reentry.
Some asteroids travel through space at thousands of km/s relative to Earth.
Radio waves propagate at around 300,000 km/s in a vacuum.

Synset Examples

Light travels in vacuum at the speed of almost 300,000 kilometers per second.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: m/s - unit of speed equal to meters per second
  • Degree Lower: km/h - unit of speed equal to kilometers per hour

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'kilometers per second'. From French 'kilomètre' plus Latin 'per' plus Latin 'secunda'.
