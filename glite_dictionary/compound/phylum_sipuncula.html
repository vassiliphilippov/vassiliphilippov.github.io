phylum Sipuncula

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪləm sɪˈpʌŋkjʊlə / (AmE), / ˈfʌɪləm sɪˈpʌŋkjʊlə / (BrE)
a phylum of marine, unsegmented worms known as peanut worms, characterized by a retractable introvert and found worldwide
a phylum of marine peanut worms

Examples

Scientists studying marine biodiversity often encounter members of the phylum Sipuncula during deep-sea expeditions.
The phylum Sipuncula consists of soft-bodied, unsegmented worms commonly known as peanut worms.
Students learned that the phylum Sipuncula plays a vital role in benthic ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peanut worm - common name for worms in the phylum Sipuncula

Etymology

From New Latin Sipunculus (genus name) + phylum (Greek phylon, tribe/race).
