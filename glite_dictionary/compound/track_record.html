track record

1. NOUN

/ træk ˈrɛkɚd / (AmE), / træk ˈrɛkɔːd / (BrE)
the fastest time ever recorded for a particular distance at a specific racetrack, especially in horse or motor racing
fastest time at a racetrack

Examples

Secretariat still holds the track record for the mile and a half at Belmont Park.
The track record was broken during last year's Grand Prix.
She set a new track record in the 400-meter hurdles at the stadium.

Synset Examples

the track record for the mile and a half at Belmont is 2 minutes 24 seconds held by Secretariat since 1973

Related Terms

  • Synonym: record - the best performance or highest achievement in a particular event

2. NOUN

/ ˈtræk ˌrɛkərd / (AmE), / ˈtræk ˌrekɔːd / (BrE)
the record of a person's or organization's past performance or achievements, especially as an indicator of ability or reliability
record of past performance

Examples

She was hired because her track record in sales was exceptional.
The company's strong track record makes it a reliable choice for investors.
He has a proven track record of managing complex projects successfully.

Synset Examples

the lawyer has a good record
the track record shows that he will be a good president

Related Terms

  • Synonym: record - an account of past achievements or performances

Etymology

Originally from racing, referring to a competitor's recorded times or achievements on a race track.
