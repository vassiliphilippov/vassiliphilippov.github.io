Kepler's first law

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɛplɚz ˈfɜrst lɔ / (AmE), / ˈkɛpləz ˈfɜːst lɔː / (BrE)
the law stating that the orbit of each planet is an ellipse, with the Sun at one focus of the ellipse
planetary law: elliptical orbits

Examples

The shape of Earth's orbit, according to Kepler's first law, is a slightly flattened ellipse.
Astronomy students must memorize Kepler's first law when learning about planetary motion.
Because of Kepler's first law, we know that the Sun is not at the center of a planet's orbit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Kepler's laws - the three laws describing planetary motion
  • Degree Higher: Kepler's second law - the law stating that a line joining a planet and the Sun sweeps out equal areas in equal times

Etymology

Named after German astronomer Johannes Kepler (1571–1630), who formulated it in the early 17th century.
