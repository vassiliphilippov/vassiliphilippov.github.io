barium sulphate

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛriəm sʌlˌfeɪt / (AmE), / ˈbæriəm ˈsʌlfɛɪt / (BrE)
a white, insoluble powder used as a pigment and in medical imaging due to its radiopacity
white insoluble powder, medical and pigment use
medical

Examples

The X-ray technician prepared a suspension of barium sulphate for the contrast study.
Artists sometimes use barium sulphate as a pigment in certain types of paints.
Because barium sulphate does not dissolve in water, it is safe for medical imaging purposes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barium sulfate - the American spelling of 'barium sulphate', a white insoluble radiopaque powder

Etymology

From modern Latin 'barium' (element name) + 'sulphate', from Latin 'sulphur' and '-ate' suffix.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɛriəm səlˈfeɪt / (AmE), / ˈbeəriəm sʌlˈfeɪt / (BrE)
a white or colorless crystalline compound (BaSO4) used in various industrial processes and as a radiopaque agent in medical imaging
a white or colorless chemical compound (BaSO4)
medical

Examples

Doctors often use barium sulphate as a contrast agent in X-ray imaging of the digestive system.
The laboratory ordered a new shipment of barium sulphate for their experiments.
Natural deposits of barium sulphate can be found in several parts of the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barium - a soft, silvery-white metallic element

Etymology

From 'barium', a chemical element, and 'sulphate', from Latin 'sulphas' (a salt of sulfuric acid).
