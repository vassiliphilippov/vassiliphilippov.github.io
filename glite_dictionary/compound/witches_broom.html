witches' broom

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪtʃɪz ˌbruːm / (AmE), / ˈwɪtʃɪz ˌbruːm / (BrE)
an abnormal, dense cluster of shoots or branches on a tree or shrub, often caused by fungi, insects, or other disturbances
abnormal bushy growth on trees or shrubs

Examples

The ash tree developed a noticeable witches' broom after a fungal infection.
Gardeners are concerned when they spot witches' broom on their ornamental shrubs.
A severe case of witches' broom can affect the overall health of the plant.

Etymology

So called due to the resemblance of the dense growth to a broom, associated in folklore with witches.
