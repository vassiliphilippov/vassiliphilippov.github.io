March King

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑrtʃ ˌkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmɑːtʃ ˌkɪŋ / (BrE)
a nickname for John Philip Sousa (1854–1932), the American bandmaster and composer famous for his military marches
nickname of John Philip Sousa, American composer

Examples

John Philip Sousa, often called the March King, composed many well-known military marches.
The March King's music is still played at patriotic events across the United States.
Many people recognize the distinctive sound of the March King even if they don't know his name.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: John Philip Sousa - United States bandmaster and composer famous for military marches

Etymology

Refers to John Philip Sousa's fame as a composer of marches; 'king' here means a leading figure.
