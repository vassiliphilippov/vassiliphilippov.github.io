fidget-bottom

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪdʒɪt ˌbɑtəm / (AmE), / ˈfɪdʒɪt ˌbɒtəm / (BrE)
an informal term for a person who is unable to sit still or is habitually restless
restless or fidgety person
informal

Examples

Even as a child, my brother was such a fidget-bottom that he hardly ever stayed still.
The teacher asked the fidget-bottoms in the back row to pay attention to the lesson.
She admitted to being a fidget-bottom when attending long meetings.

Synset Examples

I was a fidget-bottom during the boring lecture.
He's such a fidget-bottom; he can't sit still in class.
The kids were fidget-bottoms during the long car ride.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fidget - to make small movements, especially of the hands and feet, through nervousness or impatience

Etymology

Formed from 'fidget' (to move restlessly) and 'bottom' (buttocks) as a playful compound.
