split shift

1. NOUN

/ splɪt ʃɪft / (AmE), / splɪt ʃɪft / (BrE)
A work shift that is divided into two separate periods, with a long break of several hours in between.
work shift divided into two periods

Examples

Under the new schedule, employees must work a split shift with a long unpaid break in the afternoon.
Many restaurant employees are accustomed to working a split shift to cover both lunch and dinner periods.
After years of split shifts, Maria finally found a job with more regular hours.
