to becloak one's feelings

1. VERB

/ bɪˈkloʊk wʌnz ˈfilɪŋz / (AmE), / bɪˈkləʊk wʌnz ˈfiːlɪŋz / (BrE)
to conceal or hide one's emotions or true feelings from others
hide one's emotions

Examples

He made a conscious effort to becloak his feelings during the tense meeting.
She managed to becloak her feelings, even though the news had upset her deeply.
Despite the disappointment, he tried to becloak his feelings with a polite smile.

Synset Examples

He decided to becloak his true feelings for her.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mask one's feelings - to hide or disguise one's true emotions
  • Antonym: reveal one's feelings - to show or express one's true emotions

Etymology

From English prefix 'be-' (to make or cover with) + 'cloak' (cover, conceal).
