Eskimo language

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛskɪˌmoʊ ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈɛskɪˌməʊ ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
a group of closely related languages traditionally spoken by the indigenous peoples known as Eskimos in Arctic regions, including Inuit and Yupik languages
languages of indigenous Arctic peoples

Examples

The Eskimo language includes several dialects spoken across Alaska, Canada, and Greenland.
Researchers have documented ancient legends preserved in the Eskimo language.
The Eskimo language has contributed words to the English vocabulary, such as "kayak" and "igloo".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Inuit language - language spoken by the Inuit people, a member of the Eskimo-Aleut family
  • Synonym: Yupik language - language spoken by the Yupik people, a member of the Eskimo-Aleut family

Etymology

"Eskimo" possibly from Montagnais (an Algonquian language) *ayas̆kimew* (netter of snowshoes), with "language" from Latin *lingua*.
