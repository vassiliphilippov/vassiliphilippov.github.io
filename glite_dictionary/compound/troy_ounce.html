troy ounce

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrɔɪ aʊns / (AmE), / ˈtrɔɪ aʊns / (BrE)
A unit of weight equal to 480 grains or approximately 31.1035 grams, used especially for weighing precious metals such as gold, silver, and platinum.
unit for weighing precious metals

Examples

Gold is typically priced per troy ounce on the international market.
The bar weighed ten troy ounces, making it valuable to collectors.
Unlike the standard ounce, a troy ounce is heavier and used for precious metals.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: avoirdupois ounce - a standard ounce used in most other goods, equal to about 28.35 grams

Etymology

Troy from the city of Troyes in France, where the weight system was used in the Middle Ages.
