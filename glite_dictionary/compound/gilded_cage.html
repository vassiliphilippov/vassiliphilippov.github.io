gilded cage

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɪldɪd keɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈɡɪldɪd keɪdʒ / (BrE)
an attractive or luxurious situation or environment that is nevertheless confining or restrictive
attractive but confining situation

Examples

After becoming famous, the actor realized he was living in a gilded cage and missed his old life.
She inherited enormous wealth but soon found herself in a gilded cage, unable to trust anyone.
Despite the privileges of royalty, many feel trapped in a gilded cage of duty and expectation.

Synset Examples

Living in a gilded cage can be lonely and isolating.
He felt trapped in a gilded cage, despite his wealth.
She left the gilded cage to seek freedom and adventure.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: prison - place or state of confinement
  • Synonym: trap - a situation from which escape is difficult

Etymology

From the literal image of a birdcage covered with gilding (gold leaf), signifying beauty and wealth masking captivity.
