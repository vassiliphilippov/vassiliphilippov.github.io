Nobel Laureate

1. NOUN

/ noʊˌbɛl ˈlɔːriət / (AmE), / nəʊˌbɛl ˈlɔːriət / (BrE)
a person who has been awarded a Nobel Prize in any category
recipient of a Nobel Prize

Examples

The chemistry department invited a Nobel Laureate to deliver a special lecture this semester.
She became a Nobel Laureate for her groundbreaking work in physics.
Many Nobel Laureates have gone on to inspire future generations of scientists.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Nobel Prize - prestigious international prize awarded annually

Etymology

Nobel from Alfred Nobel, founder of the Nobel Prizes; laureate from Latin 'laureatus' (crowned with laurel).
