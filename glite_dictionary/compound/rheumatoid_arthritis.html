rheumatoid arthritis

1. NOUN

/ ˈruːməˌtɔɪd ɑrˈθraɪtɪs / (AmE), / ˈruːmətɔɪd ˌɑːˈθraɪtɪs / (BrE)
A chronic autoimmune disorder causing inflammation, pain, and deformity of the joints, often resulting in joint damage and reduced mobility.
chronic autoimmune disease affecting joints
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with rheumatoid arthritis after experiencing pain and swelling in her hands and knees.
Early intervention is important in managing rheumatoid arthritis symptoms and preventing joint damage.
Her research focuses on new treatments for people living with rheumatoid arthritis.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: osteoarthritis - a degenerative joint disease, typically related to aging or wear and tear
  • Synonym: arthritis - general inflammation of the joints

Etymology

From Greek *rheuma* (a flow or discharge) and *arthron* (joint) with Modern Latin *-itis* (inflammation).
