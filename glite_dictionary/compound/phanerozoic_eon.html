Phanerozoic eon

1. NOUN

/ ˌfænəroʊˈzoʊɪk iːˈɑn / (AmE), / ˌfænərəʊˈzəʊɪk iːˈɒn / (BrE)
the geological time period from about 540 million years ago to the present, marked by abundant and diverse life forms
era of abundant life from 540 million years ago to now

Examples

Fossils found in the Phanerozoic eon display the remarkable diversity of ancient life.
The Phanerozoic eon includes well-known periods such as the Paleozoic, Mesozoic, and Cenozoic.
Life on Earth became abundant and complex during the Phanerozoic eon.

Etymology

From Greek phaneros (visible) + zoion (animal) + -ic; 'eon' from Greek aiōn (age).
