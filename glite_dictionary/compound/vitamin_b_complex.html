vitamin B complex

1. NOUN

/ ˌvaɪtəmɪn ˈbi ˌkɑmplɛks / (AmE), / ˌvɪtəmɪn ˈbi ˌkɒmplɛks / (BrE)
a group of water-soluble vitamins that were formerly thought to be a single vitamin but are now recognized as several distinct B vitamins, important for various metabolic processes
group of B vitamins
medical

Examples

The doctor recommended taking a vitamin B complex supplement to support overall health.
Many foods, such as whole grains and eggs, naturally contain vitamin B complex.
Deficiency in vitamin B complex can lead to various health problems, including fatigue and anemia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: B vitamin - any of the individual B vitamins making up the complex
