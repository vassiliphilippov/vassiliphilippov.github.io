cupric sulfate

1. NOUN

/ ˈkuːprɪk ˈsʌlfeɪt / (AmE), / ˈkjuːprɪk ˈsʌlfeɪt / (BrE)
a bright blue crystalline compound used as a fungicide, algicide, and in chemical analysis; copper(II) sulfate
a blue copper compound used in chemistry
medical

Examples

The laboratory technician added cupric sulfate to the solution to test for reducing sugars.
Gardeners sometimes use cupric sulfate as a fungicide to protect plants from disease.
Cupric sulfate crystals are easily recognizable by their bright blue color.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: copper sulfate - another name for cupric sulfate, especially in chemical contexts

Etymology

From Latin 'cuprum' (copper) and 'sulfas' (sulfate).
