drepanocytic anemia

1. NOUN

/ ˌdrɛpənoʊˈsɪtɪk əˈniːmiə / (AmE), / ˌdrɛpənəʊˈsɪtɪk əˈniːmiə / (BrE)
A congenital form of anemia in which red blood cells become abnormally crescent-shaped, most commonly affecting people of African descent; also known as sickle cell anemia.
a congenital anemia with crescent-shaped red cells
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with drepanocytic anemia after presenting symptoms of fatigue and frequent pain crises.
Genetic counseling is recommended for individuals at risk of passing drepanocytic anemia to their children.
Recent advances in treatment have improved life expectancy for those with drepanocytic anemia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sickle cell anemia - an inherited form of anemia marked by sickle-shaped red blood cells

Etymology

From Greek 'drepane' (sickle) + '-cyte' (cell) + 'anemia' (lack of blood).
