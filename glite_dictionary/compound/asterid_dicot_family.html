asterid dicot family

1. NOUN

/ ˈæstərɪd ˈdaɪˌkɑt ˈfæməli / (AmE), / ˈæstərɪd ˈdɪkət ˈfæmɪli / (BrE)
a family of mainly advanced dicotyledonous flowering plants, consisting mostly of herbs, but also including some trees and shrubs
family of advanced dicotyledonous plants

Examples

Several important crops, such as tomatoes and potatoes, belong to the asterid dicot family.
Botanists often study the evolutionary traits of the asterid dicot family when classifying flowering plants.
The diversity of the asterid dicot family is evident in both temperate and tropical regions.

Etymology

From 'asterid,' referring to a major clade of eudicot plants, and 'dicot,' from Greek 'di-' (two) and 'kotyledon' (cotyledon or seed leaf).
