armor plating

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑrmər ˈpleɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɑːmə ˈpleɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
specially hardened steel plates used to protect vehicles, fortifications, or other structures from enemy fire or damage
hardened steel plates for protection

Examples

The tank's armor plating was thick enough to withstand direct hits from small artillery.
Modern warships use advanced armor plating to protect vital areas from missiles and torpedoes.
Engineers inspected the armor plating for signs of weakness after the battle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: armor - defensive covering to protect against attack
