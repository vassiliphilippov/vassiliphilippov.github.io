European ladies' tresses

1. NOUN

/ jʊˈroʊpiən ˈleɪdiz ˈtrɛsɪz / (AmE), / jʊəˈrəʊpiən ˈleɪdiz ˈtrɛsɪz / (BrE)
A European orchid with short racemes of tightly spiraling snow-white flowers, commonly found in grasslands and meadows.
European orchid with spiraling white flowers

Examples

The meadow was filled with European ladies' tresses blooming among the tall grasses.
He photographed several rare European ladies' tresses during his walk in the nature reserve.
Botanists are working to protect habitats where European ladies' tresses still thrive.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spiranthes - genus of orchids known as ladies' tresses
