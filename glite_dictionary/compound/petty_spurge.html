petty spurge

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛti spɜrdʒ / (AmE), / ˈpɛti spɜːdʒ / (BrE)
a small annual plant of the spurge family (Euphorbia peplus) native to the Old World and introduced as a weed in parts of North America
a small weedy plant (Euphorbia peplus)

Examples

Petty spurge often grows in gardens and disturbed soils throughout the summer.
Researchers observed that petty spurge can quickly invade bare ground in new environments.
Gardeners are advised to remove petty spurge before it spreads its seeds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spurge - any plant of the genus Euphorbia

Etymology

From 'petty' (meaning small) and 'spurge', from Old French 'espurge', from Latin 'expurgare'.
