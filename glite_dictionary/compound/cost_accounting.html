cost accounting

1. NOUN

/ kɑst əˈkaʊntɪŋ / (AmE), / kɒst əˈkaʊntɪŋ / (BrE)
the process or profession of recording, analyzing, and reporting the costs of producing goods or services within an organization
recording and analyzing production costs

Examples

She specializes in cost accounting for manufacturing companies.
Implementing cost accounting helped the firm control expenses more effectively.
Many businesses rely on cost accounting to determine product pricing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: management accounting - accounting focused on internal management decision-making
  • Antonym: financial accounting - the field of accounting concerned with external financial statements

Etymology

From 'cost' + 'accounting' (from Latin 'computare', to count, sum up).
