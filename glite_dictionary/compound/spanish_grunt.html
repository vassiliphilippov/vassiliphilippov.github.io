Spanish grunt

1. NOUN

/ ˈspænɪʃ ɡrʌnt / (AmE), / ˈspænɪʃ ɡrʌnt / (BrE)
A small tropical marine fish (Haemulon macrostomum) of the western Atlantic, distinguished by its yellow stripes and grunting sounds produced by grinding its pharyngeal teeth.
a yellow-striped tropical marine fish

Examples

We saw a school of Spanish grunts swimming near the coral reef.
The aquarium's new exhibit features the colorful Spanish grunt.
Fishermen often spot the distinctive stripes of the Spanish grunt in shallow Caribbean waters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grunt - a fish of the family Haemulidae that makes grunting sounds
