American gentian

1. NOUN

/ əˈmɛrɪkən ˈdʒɛnʃən / (AmE), / əˈmɛrɪkən ˈdʒɛnʃən / (BrE)
Any of various tall perennial herbs of the genus Frasera, found in warm, dry upland regions of western North America.
tall perennial herb of the genus Frasera

Examples

The meadow was dotted with blooming American gentian during late summer.
Botanists discovered a rare species of American gentian in the foothills of Oregon.
Hikers stopped to photograph the tall stems and blue flowers of the American gentian.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Frasera - the plant genus to which American gentians belong
  • Degree Higher: gentian - related group of flowering plants with bitter-tasting roots
