tight money

1. NOUN

/ taɪt ˈmʌni / (AmE), / taɪt ˈmʌni / (BrE)
An economic situation in which credit is difficult to obtain and interest rates are high.
economic condition with scarce credit

Examples

During periods of tight money, businesses may struggle to borrow funds for expansion.
The central bank's policies led to tight money and higher loan rates across the country.
Economists warn that prolonged tight money can slow down economic growth.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: easy money - an economic condition in which credit is readily available and interest rates are low
