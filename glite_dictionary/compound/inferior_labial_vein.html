inferior labial vein

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈfɪriɚ ˈleɪ.bi.əl veɪn / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɪəriə ˈleɪ.bɪ.əl veɪn / (BrE)
a vein that drains blood from the lower lip into the facial vein
vein draining the lower lip
medical

Examples

The inferior labial vein collects blood from the lower lip and empties into the facial vein.
During reconstructive surgery, care was taken to preserve the inferior labial vein.
An injury to the inferior labial vein can cause significant bleeding from the lower lip.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: superior labial vein - vein draining the upper lip
  • Degree Higher: facial vein - main vein of the face that receives blood from the labial veins

Etymology

From Latin 'labium' (lip) and 'vena' (vein), with 'inferior' meaning lower.
