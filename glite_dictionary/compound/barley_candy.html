barley candy

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑrli ˌkændi / (AmE), / ˈbɑːli ˌkændi / (BrE)
A clear, hard candy made primarily from melted cane sugar, traditionally molded into decorative shapes.
clear, hard candy made from sugar

Examples

During holidays, my grandmother would make beautiful barley candy in the shape of animals.
Children enjoyed sucking on the colorful barley candy during the winter festival.
She bought a bag of old-fashioned barley candy from the local market.

Etymology

The name comes from an old association with barley water traditionally used in the recipe.
