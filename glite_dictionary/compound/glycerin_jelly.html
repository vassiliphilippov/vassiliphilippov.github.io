glycerin jelly

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡlɪsərɪn ˈdʒɛli / (AmE), / ˈɡlɪsərɪn ˈdʒɛli / (BrE)
a mixture of glycerin and gelatin, typically used in histology for mounting microscopic specimens on slides
mixture for mounting microscope slides

Examples

The specimen was mounted on a slide using glycerin jelly to preserve its structure.
Laboratories often prepare their own glycerin jelly for histological work.
She covered the tissue sample with glycerin jelly before placing a cover slip on top.
