chemical chain

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɛmɪkəl tʃeɪn / (AmE), / ˈkɛmɪkəl tʃeɪn / (BrE)
a series of atoms bonded together in a sequence, typically forming the backbone of an organic molecule
series of bonded atoms in a molecule
medical

Examples

The polymer consists of a long chemical chain made up of repeating units.
Breaking the chemical chain can alter the properties of the compound entirely.
Scientists study how a chemical chain reacts under different environmental conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carbon chain - a sequence of carbon atoms bonded together in a molecule
