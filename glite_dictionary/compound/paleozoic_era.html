Paleozoic era

1. NOUN

/ ˌpeɪliəˈzoʊɪk ˈɛrə / (AmE), / ˌpæliəʊˈzəʊɪk ˈɪərə / (BrE)
The era of geological time from about 541 to 252 million years ago, during which ancient life forms evolved and major groups of animals first appeared.
geological era, ancient lifeforms emerged

Examples

Many marine species first appeared during the Paleozoic era as life flourished in the oceans.
Geologists have found well-preserved fossils from the Paleozoic era in this region of the world.
The Paleozoic era witnessed the development of the earliest land plants and vertebrates.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Mesozoic era - the geological era following the Paleozoic
  • Degree Lower: Precambrian - the geological time before the Paleozoic

Etymology

From Greek 'palaios' (ancient) and 'zoon' (animal).
