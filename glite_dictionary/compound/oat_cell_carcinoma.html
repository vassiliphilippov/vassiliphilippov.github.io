oat cell carcinoma

1. NOUN

/ oʊt sɛl ˌkɑr.sɪˈnoʊ.mə / (AmE), / əʊt sɛl ˌkɑː.sɪˈnəʊ.mə / (BrE)
A highly malignant cancer originating most often in the lungs, characterized by small, oval-shaped cells with little cytoplasm.
highly malignant lung cancer type
medical

Examples

The diagnosis of oat cell carcinoma was confirmed after reviewing the patient's biopsy results.
Oat cell carcinoma is known for its rapid growth and early spread to other organs.
Doctors often use chemotherapy as the primary treatment for oat cell carcinoma of the lungs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: small cell lung cancer - another name for oat cell carcinoma, a type of lung cancer

Etymology

Named for the resemblance of the cancer cells to oats in shape and size; 'carcinoma' from Greek karkinōma (cancerous ulcer).
