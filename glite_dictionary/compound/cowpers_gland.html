Cowper's gland

1. NOUN

/ ˈkuːpərz ˌɡlænd / (AmE), / ˈkaʊpəz ˌɡlænd / (BrE)
either of two small glands in the male reproductive system that discharge a component of seminal fluid into the urethra; also called bulbourethral gland
male gland producing seminal fluid
medical

Examples

The Cowper's gland secretes a fluid that helps lubricate the urethra during ejaculation.
Inflammation of the Cowper's gland is known as cowperitis and can cause discomfort.
Doctors sometimes refer to the Cowper's gland as the bulbourethral gland.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bulbourethral gland - medical term for Cowper's gland
  • Synonym: Bartholin's gland - gland in females homologous to Cowper's gland

Etymology

Named after William Cowper (1666–1709), English anatomist who described the gland.
