meal ticket

1. NOUN

/ mil ˈtɪkət / (AmE), / miːl ˈtɪkɪt / (BrE)
a coupon or voucher that entitles the holder to a meal, typically at a restaurant or cafeteria
coupon or voucher for a meal

Examples

I handed the cashier my meal ticket and she gave me a tray of food.
Each worker received a meal ticket to use during the lunch break.
He won a meal ticket for a free dinner at the new Italian restaurant.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmiːl ˌtɪkɪt / (AmE), / ˈmiːl ˌtɪkɪt / (BrE)
a person, job, or thing that provides someone with a regular income or means of support
source of income or support

Examples

She always joked that her bestselling novel was her meal ticket for life.
After years of training, his football career became his meal ticket.
Many actors look for a big role that could be their meal ticket.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: breadwinner - person who earns money to support a family
  • Synonym: mainstay - main source of support

Etymology

Originally referred to an actual ticket entitling one to meals; figurative use dates to early 20th century.

3. NOUN

/ ˈmil ˌtɪkɪt / (AmE), / ˈmiːl ˌtɪkɪt / (BrE)
a person or thing that provides someone with the means of achieving success or financial security
means of success or financial security

Examples

Winning the competition was his meal ticket out of poverty.
She regarded her impressive résumé as her meal ticket in the job market.
For many aspiring musicians, a hit song is their meal ticket to fame and financial security.

Synset Examples

He relied on his charm as a meal ticket in the industry.
Studying hard is his meal ticket to a better life.
She saw her talent as a meal ticket for success.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ticket to success - something that leads to success or achievement
  • Degree Higher: meal - an occasion of eating food

Etymology

Originally referred to a physical ticket entitling the holder to meals, later used figuratively in 20th-century English.
