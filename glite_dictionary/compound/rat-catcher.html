rat-catcher

1. NOUN

/ ˈræt ˌkætʃər / (AmE), / ˈræt ˌkætʃə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to catch and kill rats or other vermin
someone who catches and kills rats

Examples

The old town once employed a rat-catcher to keep the streets free of vermin.
As a skilled rat-catcher, he knew all the best tricks for tracking rodents.
Victorian London had many jobs, but being a rat-catcher was among the most unpleasant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pest controller - person who manages or destroys pests
