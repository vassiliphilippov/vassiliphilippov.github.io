Coptic Church

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑptɪk tʃɝːtʃ / (AmE), / ˈkɒptɪk tʃɜːtʃ / (BrE)
the ancient Christian church of Egypt, representing Coptic Christianity, with its own traditions, liturgy, and leadership
ancient Christian church of Egypt

Examples

The Coptic Church celebrates Christmas on January 7th, following the Julian calendar.
After centuries of tradition, the Coptic Church remains influential in Egyptian society.
Many tourists visit ancient monasteries preserved by the Coptic Church in the Egyptian desert.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Eastern Orthodox Church - a major branch of Christianity in Eastern Europe and the Middle East
  • Synonym: Roman Catholic Church - the largest Christian church, led by the Pope in Rome

Etymology

'Coptic' from Arabic 'qubṭ', referring to Egyptian Christians, ultimately from Greek 'Aigyptios' (Egyptian).
