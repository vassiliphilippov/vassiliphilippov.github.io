caper tree

1. NOUN

/ ˈkeɪ.pɚ tri / (AmE), / ˈkeɪ.pə tɹiː / (BrE)
a shrub or small tree native to southern Florida and parts of Central and South America, known for its edible flower buds and fruit
a shrub or small tree with edible buds and fruit

Examples

The caper tree thrives in warm, coastal regions with abundant sunlight.
Farmers harvested the buds from the caper tree to sell at the market.
Botanists studied the unique adaptations of the caper tree in dry climates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caper - edible flower buds or berries from the caper bush

/ ˈkeɪpər tri / (AmE), / ˈkeɪpə triː / (BrE)

shrub of southern Florida and the West Indies

Examples

The caper tree is well adapted to the sunny, sandy soils of southern Florida.
Gardeners in the Caribbean often cultivate the caper tree for its attractive flowers.
Wildlife in the West Indies depend on the caper tree for shelter and food.
