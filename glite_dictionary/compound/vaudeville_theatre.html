vaudeville theatre

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɔd.vɪl ˌθi.ə.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈvɔːd.vɪl ˌθɪə.tə / (BrE)
a theater where vaudeville performances are presented, typically featuring a variety of live acts such as comedy, music, and dance
theater for vaudeville performances

Examples

The newly restored vaudeville theatre now hosts comedy nights and musical acts.
Many famous performers started their careers on a vaudeville theatre stage.
The architecture of the old vaudeville theatre was ornate and reminiscent of a bygone era.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: music hall - a British theater for popular variety entertainment
  • Degree Higher: theatre - building where performances are presented to an audience

Etymology

From French 'vaudeville' (light entertainment) and English 'theatre'.
