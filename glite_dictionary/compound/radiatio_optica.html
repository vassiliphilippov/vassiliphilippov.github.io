radiatio optica

1. NOUN

/ reɪdiˈeɪʃiˌoʊ ɑpˈtɪkə / (AmE), / reɪdiˈɑːtiəʊ ˈɒptɪkə / (BrE)
the bundle of nerve fibers that transmits visual information from the lateral geniculate body to the visual cortex in the brain
nerve pathway conveying visual signals to the visual cortex
medical

Examples

Damage to the radiatio optica can lead to visual field deficits known as quadrantanopia.
The radiatio optica is essential for transmitting visual signals to the brain's occipital lobe.
Neuroradiologists often assess the radiatio optica when evaluating patients with vision problems following a stroke.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: optic radiation - bundle of nerve fibers carrying visual information from the lateral geniculate nucleus to the visual cortex

Etymology

From Latin, meaning 'optic radiation'.
