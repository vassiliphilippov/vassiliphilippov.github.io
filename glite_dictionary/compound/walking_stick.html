walking stick

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔkɪŋ stɪk / (AmE), / ˈwɔːkɪŋ stɪk / (BrE)
A tropical insect with a long, slender body that resembles a twig, typically camouflaged among branches and leaves.
insect resembling a twig

Examples

The walking stick blends perfectly with the twigs and branches in its forest habitat.
Some species of walking stick can grow to over a foot in length.
When threatened, a walking stick may remain completely still to avoid predators.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stick insect - insect resembling a stick; synonym for walking stick

Etymology

Named for its resemblance to a thin stick; 'walking stick' as a compound reflects both its appearance and movement.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɔkɪŋ ˌstɪk / (AmE), / ˈwɔːkɪŋ ˌstɪk / (BrE)
A stick carried in the hand to provide support while walking.
stick for support while walking

Examples

He leaned heavily on his walking stick as he made his way down the path.
After her surgery, she used a walking stick for extra stability.
Many hikers carry a walking stick to help navigate rough terrain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cane - a stick used for support in walking, especially one with a curved handle
