jammy job

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒæmi dʒɑb / (AmE), / ˈdʒami dʒɒb / (BrE)
An easy or highly desirable job that requires little effort or offers great benefits.
an easy or desirable job (informal, British)
informal british

Examples

He was thrilled when he landed a jammy job at the tech company right after graduation.
Her friends couldn't believe how much she was paid for such a jammy job.
Working as a product tester is seen as a jammy job by many people.

Synset Examples

He got a jammy job that lets him work from home.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cushy job - an easy or undemanding job

Etymology

British informal; from "jammy" meaning 'lucky' or 'fortunate', origin uncertain but likely from 'jam' as a symbol of pleasantness.
