magnetic attraction

1. NOUN

/ məgˈnɛtɪk əˈtrækʃən / (AmE), / mæɡˈnɛtɪk əˈtrækʃən / (BrE)
the physical force that draws certain metals, especially iron, towards a magnet or magnetic field, often produced by electric currents.
force pulling objects towards a magnet

Examples

The magnetic attraction of the powerful horseshoe magnet pulled the nails across the table.
Electromagnets create magnetic attraction when an electric current flows through their coils.
Children are often fascinated by magnetic attraction when playing with magnets and paper clips.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: magnetism - physical phenomenon produced by the motion of electric charge, resulting in attractive and repulsive forces
  • Antonym: magnetic repulsion - force that pushes magnetic objects apart
