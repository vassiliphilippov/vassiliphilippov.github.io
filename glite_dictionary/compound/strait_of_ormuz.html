Strait of Ormuz

1. PROPN

/ streɪt əv ɔrˈmuz / (AmE), / streɪt əv ɔːˈmʊz / (BrE)
a narrow, strategically important strait linking the Persian Gulf with the Gulf of Oman, serving as a vital route for oil shipments
strategic strait between Persian Gulf and Gulf of Oman

Examples

Large oil tankers pass through the Strait of Ormuz on their way to international markets.
Tensions often rise in the region surrounding the Strait of Ormuz due to its strategic significance.
The closure of the Strait of Ormuz would have a major impact on global oil prices.

Etymology

Named from the nearby port of Hormuz; the name derives from Persian 'Hormoz'.
