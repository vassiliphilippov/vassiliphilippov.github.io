intergalactic space

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪntərɡəˈlæktɪk speɪs / (AmE), / ˌɪntəɡəˈlæktɪk speɪs / (BrE)
the region of space that lies between separate galaxies
space between galaxies

Examples

Astronomers study the properties of intergalactic space to understand the universe's structure.
The probe is designed to travel through intergalactic space between distant galaxies.
Cosmic rays can traverse vast distances across intergalactic space before reaching our galaxy.

Synset Examples

the Milky Way travels through intergalactic space

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interstellar space - the space between stars within a galaxy

Etymology

From inter- (between) + galactic (of or relating to a galaxy).
