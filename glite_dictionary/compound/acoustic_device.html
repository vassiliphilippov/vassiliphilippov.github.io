acoustic device

1. NOUN

/ əˈkuːstɪk dɪˈvaɪs / (AmE), / əˈkuːstɪk dɪˈvaɪs / (BrE)
a device designed for amplifying, transmitting, or modifying sound
device for sound amplification or transmission

Examples

The engineer tested the new acoustic device to ensure it produced clear and powerful sound.
A microphone is a type of acoustic device used to capture audio in a studio.
Many concert halls are equipped with specialized acoustic devices to enhance the listening experience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loudspeaker - an apparatus that converts electrical signals into sound

Etymology

From 'acoustic' (relating to sound, from Greek *akoustikos*) + 'device'.
