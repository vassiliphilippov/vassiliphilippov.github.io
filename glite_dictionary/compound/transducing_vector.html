transducing vector

1. NOUN

/ trænzˈduːsɪŋ ˈvɛktɚ / (AmE), / trænzˈdjuːsɪŋ ˈvɛktə / (BrE)
a vehicle used to deliver genes into cells, especially in genetic engineering or molecular biology
gene delivery vehicle
medical

Examples

The scientist used a transducing vector to introduce the new gene into the cell culture.
Lentiviruses are commonly employed as transducing vectors in gene therapy experiments.
Before clinical application, the safety of the transducing vector must be thoroughly evaluated.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vector - an organism or vehicle that transmits genetic material or disease

Etymology

From 'transduce' (to transfer genetic material), from Latin 'transducere' (to lead across), and 'vector', from Latin 'vector' (carrier).
