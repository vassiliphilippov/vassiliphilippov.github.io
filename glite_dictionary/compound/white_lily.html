white lily

1. NOUN

/ waɪt ˈlɪli / (AmE), / waɪt ˈlɪli / (BrE)
A type of water lily, native to Egypt and southeastern Africa, especially the Egyptian lotus, which was held sacred by the ancient Egyptians.
Egyptian water lily; sacred lotus

Examples

The ancient Egyptians often depicted the white lily in their art and hieroglyphs.
The pond was covered with the elegant blooms of the white lily.
Botanists studied the unique features of the white lily native to the Nile region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Egyptian lotus - A species of water lily native to Egypt, also known as white lily

2. NOUN

/ waɪt ˈlɪli / (AmE), / waɪt ˈlɪli / (BrE)
A lily native to the eastern Mediterranean and the Balkans, having broad funnel-shaped white flowers.
Mediterranean lily with white flowers

Examples

A single white lily stood out among the darker leaves in the garden.
The bride carried a bouquet of fragrant white lilies during the ceremony.
In spring, fields of white lily bloom across parts of the Balkans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lily - a type of flowering plant in the genus Lilium
