eternal rest

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈtɜrnəl rɛst / (AmE), / ɪˈtɜːnl rɛst / (BrE)
death, especially as a state of everlasting peace; used as a euphemism for dying or being buried
euphemism for death or burial

Examples

Many people find comfort in the belief that their loved ones have found eternal rest.
A simple headstone marked the spot where he was laid to eternal rest.
They prayed that the departed would enjoy eternal rest in peace.

Synset Examples

she was laid to rest beside her husband
they had to put their family pet to sleep

Related Terms

  • Synonym: death - the end of life; cessation of existence

Etymology

From Latin "aeternus" (eternal) and Old English "ræst" (rest); the phrase originated in religious texts.
