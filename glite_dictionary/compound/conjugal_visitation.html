conjugal visitation

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑndʒəɡəl ˌvɪzɪˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈkɒndʒʊɡəl ˌvɪzɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the right of a prison inmate to receive a private visit from a spouse, usually to allow for sexual intercourse
prison visit by spouse for intimacy
legal

Examples

The prison introduced conjugal visitation policies to support inmate family connections.
Some countries do not permit conjugal visitation for inmates under any circumstances.
His lawyer argued for conjugal visitation as part of upholding his client's marital rights.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conjugal rights - rights relating to marriage, especially sexual relations between spouses

Etymology

From Latin 'conjugalis' (pertaining to marriage) and 'visitatio' (a visit).
