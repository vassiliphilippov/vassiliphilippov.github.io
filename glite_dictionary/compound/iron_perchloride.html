iron perchloride

1. NOUN

/ ˌaɪərn pərˈklɔːraɪd / (AmE), / ˌaɪən pəˈklɒraɪd / (BrE)
a highly toxic chemical compound used in etching metal plates and making electronic circuits
chemical used in metal etching and electronics

Examples

The technician used iron perchloride to etch the copper surface of the printed circuit board.
Handle iron perchloride with extreme care, as it is both toxic and corrosive.
Artists sometimes rely on iron perchloride for etching detailed patterns onto metal plates.

Etymology

From 'iron' (the metal) and 'perchloride' (compound with high chlorine content), following chemical naming conventions.
