aldehyde group

1. NOUN

/ ˈældɪˌhaɪd ɡruːp / (AmE), / ˈældɪhaɪd ɡruːp / (BrE)
the chemical functional group consisting of a carbon atom bonded to a hydrogen atom and double-bonded to an oxygen atom (-CHO).
chemical group: –CHO; functional group in aldehydes

Examples

Each glucose molecule contains an aldehyde group at one end.
The aldehyde group is highly reactive in many chemical reactions.
Scientists studied how the aldehyde group in the molecule affects its properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aldehyde - an organic compound containing a formyl group (-CHO)

Etymology

From Latin 'alcohol dehydrogenatus' (dehydrogenated alcohol), since aldehydes are derived from alcohols.
