alder fly

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔldɚ flaɪ / (AmE), / ˈɔːldə flaɪ / (BrE)
A dark-colored insect of the family Sialidae, whose larvae are aquatic and predatory.
a dark aquatic insect (Sialidae)

Examples

The larvae of the alder fly are known to be fierce aquatic predators.
We found an alder fly resting on a leaf by the riverbank.
Fishermen often use alder fly imitations as bait for trout.

Etymology

Named for its frequent association with alder trees; 'alder' + 'fly'.
