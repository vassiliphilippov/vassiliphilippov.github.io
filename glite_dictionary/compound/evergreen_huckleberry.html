evergreen huckleberry

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛvɚˌɡrin ˈhʌkəlˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˌɛvəˌɡriːn ˈhʌkəlˌbɛri / (BrE)
a stiff, bushy evergreen shrub of western North America, bearing sour black berries and glossy green leaves, often used in floral arrangements
North American evergreen shrub with black berries

Examples

The evergreen huckleberry thrives in the coastal forests of the Pacific Northwest.
Florists often use the glossy leaves of the evergreen huckleberry in their arrangements.
Wildlife feed on the berries produced by the evergreen huckleberry during late summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: huckleberry - small round edible berry and the plant producing it, especially in North America
