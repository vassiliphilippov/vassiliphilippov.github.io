polling station

1. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊlɪŋ ˌsteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈpəʊlɪŋ ˌsteɪʃən / (BrE)
a building or place where people go to vote in an election
place where people vote in an election

Examples

Voters lined up outside the polling station before it opened.
The polling station closes at 8 p.m. on election day.
She volunteered to help count ballots at her local polling station.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polling place - a place where people cast their votes in an election

Etymology

From 'poll' (to vote) and 'station' (a designated place).
