white sheep

1. NOUN

/ waɪt ʃip / (AmE), / waɪt ʃiːp / (BrE)
a large white wild sheep found in northwestern Canada and Alaska
large wild sheep of northwestern Canada and Alaska

Examples

The biologist observed a herd of white sheep roaming the remote mountains of Alaska.
Hunters must have permits to track white sheep in protected areas of Canada.
The white sheep is well adapted to the harsh, cold climate of its native habitat.

2. NOUN

/ waɪt ʃiːp / (AmE), / waɪt ʃiːp / (BrE)
A person who differs from others in a group, especially by being virtuous, law-abiding, or distinctive in a positive way.
a unique or virtuous person in a group

Examples

Among his rebellious siblings, Mark was considered the white sheep of the family.
While his friends constantly broke rules, John stood out as the white sheep.
In a corrupt office, she was the white sheep who always did the right thing.

Synset Examples

In a field of black sheep, he felt like the white sheep.
She often stood out as the white sheep in her family.
He was the white sheep of the group, always following rules.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: black sheep - a person who is considered a disgrace or outcast in a group, especially a family

Etymology

Modeled on 'black sheep,' referring to a member of a group who is noticeably different, typically in a positive way.
