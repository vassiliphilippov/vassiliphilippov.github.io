Nganasan language

1. NOUN

/ nɡɑː.nəˈsɑːn ˈlæŋ.ɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ŋaː.nəˈsan ˈlaŋ.ɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Uralic language spoken by the Nganasan people of the Taymyr Peninsula in northern Russia
Uralic language of the Nganasan people

Examples

Linguists have documented the Nganasan language to preserve its unique features for future generations.
Only a small number of speakers remain who are fluent in the Nganasan language.
The Nganasan language belongs to the Samoyedic branch of the Uralic language family.

Etymology

From 'Nganasan,' the name of the indigenous people, and 'language.'
