slime mould

1. NOUN

/ slaɪm moʊld / (AmE), / slaɪm məʊld / (BrE)
A naked mass of protoplasm that exhibits characteristics of both plants and animals, sometimes classified as protoctists.
simple organism resembling both plants and animals

Examples

The forest floor was covered in a strange slime mould after the rain.
Scientists study slime mould to understand how simple organisms solve complex problems.
A yellow slime mould appeared on the compost heap overnight.
