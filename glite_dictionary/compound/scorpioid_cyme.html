scorpioid cyme

1. NOUN

/ ˈskɔːr.pi.ɔɪd kaɪm / (AmE), / ˈskɔː.pi.ɔɪd kaɪm / (BrE)
A type of flower cluster (cyme) in which the flowers or branches alternate in opposite ranks, often curving in a way that resembles a scorpion's tail.
type of curved flower cluster

Examples

The forget-me-not displays its flowers in a distinctive scorpioid cyme pattern.
In many borage species, the scorpioid cyme gives the inflorescence a characteristic coiled appearance.
The botanist explained how a scorpioid cyme differs from other flower clusters.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: cyme - a flower cluster with a central stem bearing a single terminal flower that develops first

Etymology

From Latin 'scorpio' (scorpion) + Greek 'kyma' (wave, swollen), describing its curved, tail-like form.
