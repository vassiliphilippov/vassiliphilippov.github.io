Gem State

1. PROPN

/ dʒɛm steɪt / (AmE), / dʒɛm steɪt / (BrE)
a nickname for the U.S. state of Idaho, referring to its natural beauty and abundance of minerals
nickname for Idaho (U.S. state)

Examples

Many tourists visit the Gem State each year to enjoy its scenic landscapes.
The potatoes grown in the Gem State are famous across the country.
She moved to the Gem State to study geology at a local university.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Idaho - a state in the northwestern United States

Etymology

Refers to Idaho's renowned gemstones and mineral wealth.
