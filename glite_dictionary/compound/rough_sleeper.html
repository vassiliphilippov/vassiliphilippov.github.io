rough sleeper

1. NOUN

/ rʌf ˈsliːpər / (AmE), / rʌf ˈsliːpə / (BrE)
a person who sleeps outdoors or in places not meant for habitation, typically due to homelessness
person sleeping outdoors due to homelessness
british

Examples

The local shelter opened its doors to every rough sleeper during the cold snap.
She spent six months as a rough sleeper in the city parks.
Outreach workers know how hard life is for a rough sleeper in winter.

Synset Examples

He was once a rough sleeper before getting back on his feet.
The city has many programs to help rough sleepers find shelter.
Local charities provide food for rough sleepers during winter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: homeless person - a person without a home, especially someone who sleeps on the streets
