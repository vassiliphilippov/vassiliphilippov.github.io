red salmon

1. NOUN

/ rɛd ˈsæmən / (AmE), / rɛd ˈsæmən / (BrE)
A species of small Pacific salmon with reddish flesh, highly valued as food, whose adults die after spawning.
small Pacific salmon with red flesh

Examples

Many people prefer red salmon because of its rich flavor and firm texture.
After spawning in the river, adult red salmon die, providing nutrients to the ecosystem.
Grilled red salmon is a popular dish during the summer in Alaska.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sockeye salmon - another common name for the red salmon (Oncorhynchus nerka)
  • Antonym: chum salmon - a different species of Pacific salmon

2. NOUN

/ rɛd ˈsæmən / (AmE), / rɛd ˈsæmən / (BrE)
The fatty red flesh of a sockeye salmon, a species found along the Pacific coast and rivers, valued for its flavor.
fatty red flesh of Pacific sockeye salmon

Examples

Many chefs prefer to use red salmon in sushi because of its rich flavor and color.
The can contained high-quality red salmon, perfect for making sandwiches.
Alaskan rivers are famous for their annual run of red salmon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sockeye salmon - a species of salmon with distinct red flesh, also known as red salmon
