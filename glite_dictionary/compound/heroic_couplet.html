heroic couplet

1. NOUN

/ həˈroʊɪk ˈkʌplət / (AmE), / hɪˈrəʊɪk ˈkʌplɪt / (BrE)
A pair of rhymed lines of iambic pentameter, typically written in a formal or elevated style, commonly used in English poetry.
pair of rhymed lines in iambic pentameter

Examples

Alexander Pope was famous for composing poetry in heroic couplet form.
A classic heroic couplet closes many Shakespearean sonnets with a concluding thought.
The use of the heroic couplet became a defining feature of 18th-century English verse.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: couplet - a pair of successive lines of verse, usually rhymed

Etymology

From 'heroic' (relating to epic poetry) + 'couplet' (from French, meaning a pair of lines).
