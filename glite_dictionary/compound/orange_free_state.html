Orange Free State

1. PROPN

/ ˈɔːrɪndʒ friː steɪt / (AmE), / ˈɒrɪndʒ friː steɪt / (BrE)
a former province in central South Africa, colonized by the Boers, now called Free State since 1997.
former province in central South Africa

Examples

The Orange Free State played a key role in South African history during the 19th century.
He was born in the Orange Free State before it was renamed in 1997.
Many Boers settled in the Orange Free State after leaving the Cape Colony.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Free State - the current name of the province formerly known as Orange Free State

Etymology

Named after the Orange River and the republican status as a 'free state'.
