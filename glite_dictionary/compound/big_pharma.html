big pharma

1. NOUN

/ bɪɡ ˈfɑːrmə / (AmE), / bɪɡ ˈfɑːmə / (BrE)
(often disapproving) The large pharmaceutical companies or the pharmaceutical industry collectively, especially regarded as pursuing profit and influencing policy, sometimes at the expense of public health.
the large pharmaceutical industry

Examples

Many believe that big pharma has too much influence over medical research and healthcare policies.
Some people argue that big pharma prioritizes profits over patient welfare.
The documentary criticized the pricing strategies commonly employed by big pharma.

Synset Examples

Big pharma often faces scrutiny over drug prices.
Many criticize big pharma for prioritizing profits.
Big pharma influences healthcare regulations significantly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pharmaceutical industry - the sector devoted to developing and selling medical drugs

Etymology

From 'big' (large) + 'pharma', short for 'pharmaceutical'. Commonly used since the late 20th century.
