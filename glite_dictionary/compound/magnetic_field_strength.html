magnetic field strength

1. NOUN

/ məɡˈnɛtɪk fiːld strɛŋθ / (AmE), / məɡˈnɛtɪk fiːld strɛŋθ / (BrE)
the amount of magnetic flux passing through a unit area perpendicular to the direction of magnetic flow; a measure of the intensity of a magnetic field
measure of magnetic field intensity

Examples

The magnetic field strength near the magnet was measured using a specialized sensor.
An increase in electric current will raise the magnetic field strength around a coil.
Physicists often express magnetic field strength in units called teslas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: magnetic flux - total magnetic field passing through a given area
