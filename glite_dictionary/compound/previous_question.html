1. NOUN

/ ˈpri.vi.əs ˈkwɛs.tʃən / (AmE), / ˈpriː.vi.əs ˈkwɛs.tʃən / (BrE)
a formal motion in a deliberative assembly to bring the current debate to an end and proceed to an immediate vote on the main question
motion to end debate and vote immediately
legal

Examples

The senator moved the previous question to cut off further debate on the bill.
After hours of discussion, the chair recognized a delegate's motion for the previous question.
If the previous question is adopted, no further amendments can be offered.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: motion - a formal proposal put to a legislature or committee

Etymology

From parliamentary procedure in 18th-century British and American assemblies.
