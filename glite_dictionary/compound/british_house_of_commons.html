British House of Commons

1. PROPN

/ ˈbrɪtɪʃ haʊs əv ˈkɑmənz / (AmE), / ˈbrɪtɪʃ haʊs əv ˈkɒmənz / (BrE)
the elected lower chamber of the Parliament of the United Kingdom, responsible for making and passing laws
lower house of UK Parliament
british

Examples

Members of the British House of Commons debated the new education bill late into the night.
The Prime Minister addressed the British House of Commons during the urgent parliamentary session.
The British House of Commons plays a crucial role in the legislative process of the United Kingdom.
