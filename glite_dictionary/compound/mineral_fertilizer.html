mineral fertilizer

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪnərəl ˈfɜrt̬əˌlaɪzər / (AmE), / ˈmɪnərəl ˈfəːtɪlaɪzə / (BrE)
A fertilizer made from inorganic minerals that provides nutrients, often including trace elements, to plants.
inorganic fertilizer with trace minerals

Examples

The farm switched to using mineral fertilizer to boost crop yields more effectively.
Applying mineral fertilizer can help correct nutrient deficiencies in the soil.
Many commercial growers rely on mineral fertilizer for fast and precise nutrient delivery.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: organic fertilizer - fertilizer derived from natural organic sources
