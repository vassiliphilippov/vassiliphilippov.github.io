fulminate of mercury

1. NOUN

/ ˈfʊlmɪˌneɪt əv ˈmɜrkjəri / (AmE), / ˈfʊlmɪneɪt əv ˈmɜːkjʊri / (BrE)
A highly explosive chemical compound (mercury(II) fulminate), used as a primary explosive in detonators, blasting caps, and percussion caps.
an explosive compound used in detonators

Examples

The invention of fulminate of mercury greatly improved the reliability of early firearms.
Due to its sensitivity, fulminate of mercury must be handled with extreme caution during manufacturing.
Percussion caps containing fulminate of mercury were widely used in 19th-century firearms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mercury(II) fulminate - the chemical name for fulminate of mercury
  • Degree Higher: lead azide - a different primary explosive used in detonators

Etymology

From 'fulminate', related to Latin 'fulminare' (to strike with lightning), and 'mercury', from the element's name.
