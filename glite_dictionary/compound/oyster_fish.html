oyster fish

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔɪstɚ fɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈɔɪstə fɪʃ / (BrE)
A type of toadfish found in coastal waters, known for its oyster-like habitat.
a type of toadfish

Examples

The oyster fish is often found hiding among rocks and shells in shallow coastal waters.
Researchers studied the oyster fish to learn more about its unique call during spawning season.
Fishermen sometimes catch oyster fish by accident when looking for oysters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: toadfish - a bottom-dwelling fish with a broad, flattened head
