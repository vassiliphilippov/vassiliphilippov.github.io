blood dyscrasia

1. NOUN

/ blʌd dɪˈskreɪziə / (AmE), / blʌd dɪˈskreɪzɪə / (BrE)
any abnormal condition or disorder of the blood
abnormal condition of the blood
medical

Examples

The doctor suspected a blood dyscrasia due to the patient's abnormal test results.
Some medications can cause blood dyscrasia as a rare side effect.
Management of blood dyscrasia typically involves treating the underlying cause.

Etymology

From Greek 'dyskrasia' meaning 'bad mixture'; historically, the term referred to an imbalance of bodily fluids.
