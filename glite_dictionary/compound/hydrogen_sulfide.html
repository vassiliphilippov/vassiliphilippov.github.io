hydrogen sulfide

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪdrədʒən ˈsʌlfaɪd / (AmE), / ˈhaɪdrɪdʒən ˈsʌlfaɪd / (BrE)
a poisonous, colorless gas with the characteristic foul odor of rotten eggs, composed of hydrogen and sulfur
a poisonous, foul-smelling gas (H2S)

Examples

Exposure to hydrogen sulfide can be dangerous without proper ventilation.
The smell of rotten eggs in the swamp is often caused by hydrogen sulfide.
Workers wore masks to protect themselves from hydrogen sulfide fumes released during maintenance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sulfur dioxide - a toxic gas (SO2) formed by burning sulfur in air

Etymology

Formed from 'hydrogen' and 'sulfide'; 'hydrogen' from Greek 'hydro-' (water) and '-gen' (producer); 'sulfide' from 'sulfur'.
