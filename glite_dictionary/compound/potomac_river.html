Potomac River

1. PROPN

/ pəˈtoʊmək ˈrɪvər / (AmE), / pəˈtəʊmɪk ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
a river in the eastern United States, rising in West Virginia and flowing east to the Chesapeake Bay, forming part of the boundary between Maryland and Virginia
major river in eastern United States

Examples

The Potomac River flows past Washington, D.C., providing a scenic backdrop to the nation's capital.
Many early settlements thrived along the banks of the Potomac River.
A suspension bridge spans the Potomac River, connecting Maryland and Virginia.

Etymology

Named from an Algonquian Native American word, possibly meaning 'river of swans'.
