Mount Sherman

1. PROPN

/ maʊnt ˈʃɜrmən / (AmE), / maʊnt ˈʃɜːmən / (BrE)
a mountain peak in the Rocky Mountains of central Colorado, reaching an elevation of 14,036 feet (4,281 meters)
a mountain in Colorado, USA

Examples

Many hikers dream of summiting Mount Sherman during the summer climbing season.
The view from the top of Mount Sherman stretches across the rugged peaks of central Colorado.
Mount Sherman is considered one of the easiest "fourteeners" to climb in the state.
