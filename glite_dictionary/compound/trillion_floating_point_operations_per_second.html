trillion floating point operations per second

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrɪljən ˈfloʊtɪŋ pɔɪnt ˌɑpəˈreɪʃənz pər ˈsɛkənd / (AmE), / ˈtrɪljən ˈfləʊtɪŋ pɔɪnt ˌɒpəˈreɪʃənz pə ˈsɛkənd / (BrE)
In computing, a unit for measuring the processing speed of a computer system, equal to one trillion floating-point calculations per second; commonly known as a teraflop.
computing unit: one trillion calculations per second

Examples

The new supercomputer is capable of performing over five trillion floating point operations per second.
Achieving one trillion floating point operations per second was once considered a major milestone in computing.
Modern graphics cards can now exceed several trillion floating point operations per second during intensive calculations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: teraflop - a unit of computing speed equal to one trillion floating point operations per second
