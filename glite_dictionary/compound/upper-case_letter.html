upper-case letter

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌpɚ keɪs ˈlɛtɚ / (AmE), / ˈʌpə keɪs ˈlɛtə / (BrE)
a large letter of the alphabet used as the first letter of proper names and often for emphasis, also known as a capital letter
a large/capital letter of the alphabet

Examples

The first word of every sentence should begin with an upper-case letter.
Names of countries always start with an upper-case letter.
The instructions said to write your password using at least one upper-case letter.

Synset Examples

printers once kept the type for capitals and for small letters in separate cases; capitals were kept in the upper half of the type case and so became known as upper-case letters

Related Terms

  • Synonym: capital letter - a large letter of the alphabet, used at the start of sentences and proper names
  • Antonym: lower-case letter - a small letter of the alphabet, as opposed to upper-case/capital

Etymology

From the historical practice of storing capital letters in the upper case of a printer’s type case.
