English springer

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ ˈsprɪŋər / (AmE), / ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ ˈsprɪŋə / (BrE)
a breed of dog typically having a black-and-white coat, known for its use as a gun dog and for retrieving game
dog breed with black-and-white coat

Examples

The English springer is highly valued for its intelligence and trainability as a working dog.
At the dog show, several English springers received awards for their beautiful coats.
Emma adopted an English springer because of its friendly temperament and energy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: springer spaniel - a type of spaniel that 'springs' game; general category for related breeds

Etymology

From English + springer, referring to the dog's hunting style of 'springing' game; 'springer' from Old English 'springan' (to leap).
