whiplash injury

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪpˌlæʃ ˈɪndʒɚi / (AmE), / ˈwɪpˌlæʃ ˈɪndʒəri / (BrE)
an injury to the neck caused by sudden, forceful movement of the head, often from a car accident or similar impact
neck injury from sudden motion
medical

Examples

After the car accident, she was diagnosed with a whiplash injury that required physical therapy.
Drivers are encouraged to wear seatbelts to reduce the risk of a whiplash injury in collisions.
He filed an insurance claim for his whiplash injury following the rear-end crash.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whiplash - neck injury caused by a sudden movement

Etymology

From 'whiplash', describing the sharp motion of a whip, and 'injury'.
