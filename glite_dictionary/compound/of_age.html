of age

1. ADJ

/ əv eɪdʒ / (AmE), / ɒv eɪdʒ / (BrE)
having reached a specified age; used to indicate the age of a person or thing.
having attained a certain age

Examples

Their daughter is now of age to enter primary school.
The building, now of age fifty years, is considered historic.
Applicants must be of age sixteen or older to participate.

Synset Examples

aged ten
ten years of age

WordNet ID: oewn-01648983-s

2. ADJ

/ ʌv eɪdʒ / (AmE), / ɒv eɪdʒ / (BrE)
having reached the age at which a person is legally recognized as an adult or eligible for certain rights or responsibilities
having reached legal adulthood

Examples

You must be of age to purchase alcohol in this country.
She will be of age next year when she turns eighteen.
After winning the case, he was declared of age by the court.

Synset Examples

Most kids are considered of age by 18.
She is finally of age to vote.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: underage - not having reached the legally recognized age
