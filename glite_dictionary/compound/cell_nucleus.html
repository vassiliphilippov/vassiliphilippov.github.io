cell nucleus

1. NOUN

/ sɛl ˈnuː.kli.əs / (AmE), / sɛl ˈnjuː.klɪ.əs / (BrE)
the membrane-bound part of a cell that contains the genetic material (DNA and RNA) and controls growth and reproduction
membrane-bound control center of a cell
medical

Examples

The cell nucleus orchestrates the activity of genes and is vital for cell division.
Mutations within the cell nucleus can lead to serious genetic disorders.
During mitosis, the cell nucleus breaks down to allow chromosome separation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nucleolus - a small dense structure within the cell nucleus, involved in producing ribosomes
  • Antonym: cytoplasm - the material within a cell, excluding the nucleus

Etymology

From Latin 'cellula' (small room, cell) and Latin 'nucleus' (kernel, core).
