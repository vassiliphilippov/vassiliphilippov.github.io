mother's milk

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌðərz mɪlk / (AmE), / ˈmʌðəz mɪlk / (BrE)
the milk produced by a woman shortly after childbirth, which serves as the natural food for her infant
milk produced by a woman after childbirth

Examples

Breastfeeding provides mother's milk that is rich in nutrients for the newborn baby.
Doctors recommend exclusive feeding with mother's milk during the first six months of life.
Some infants are unable to tolerate formula and require mother's milk for proper development.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: breast milk - milk produced by the breasts of a woman for feeding her baby
