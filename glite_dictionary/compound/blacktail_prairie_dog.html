blacktail prairie dog

1. NOUN

/ ˈblæk.teɪl ˈpreɪ.ri dɔg / (AmE), / ˈblæk.teɪl ˈpreɪ.ri dɒg / (BrE)
a North American prairie dog species with a black-tipped tail, found mainly in the western United States and Mexico
prairie dog species with a black-tipped tail

Examples

The blacktail prairie dog lives in large colonies across the Great Plains.
Scientists are researching how blacktail prairie dogs communicate with each other underground.
A blacktail prairie dog popped its head out of a burrow and scanned the area for predators.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prairie dog - a burrowing rodent native to North America
