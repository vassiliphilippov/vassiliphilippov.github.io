lady's tresses

1. NOUN

/ ˈleɪdiz ˌtrɛsɪz / (AmE), / ˈleɪdiz ˌtrɛsɪz / (BrE)
a species of orchid of the genus Spiranthes, characterized by slender, often twisted spikes of small white flowers
a type of orchid (genus Spiranthes)

Examples

The white, spiraled flowers of lady's tresses can be found blooming in late summer meadows.
Botanists admire lady's tresses for their delicate appearance and unique flowering pattern.
We spotted a clump of lady's tresses along the woodland edge during our hike.

Etymology

From the resemblance of the flower spikes to a woman's braided hair (‘tresses’).
