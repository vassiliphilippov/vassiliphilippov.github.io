bittersweet nightshade

1. NOUN

/ ˌbɪtɚˌswit ˈnaɪtˌʃeɪd / (AmE), / ˌbɪtəˌswiːt ˈnaɪtˌʃeɪd / (BrE)
a poisonous perennial vine with violet flowers and oval coral-red berries, native to the Old World and widespread as a weed in North America
poisonous vine with violet flowers and red berries

Examples

The hedge was overrun with bittersweet nightshade, its red berries glistening among the leaves.
Children are regularly warned not to eat the berries of bittersweet nightshade as they are toxic.
Gardeners often struggle to remove bittersweet nightshade from their flower beds because it spreads so quickly.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: deadly nightshade - a highly poisonous plant, also known as belladonna
