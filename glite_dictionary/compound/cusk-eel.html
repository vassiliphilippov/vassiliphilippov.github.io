cusk-eel

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌskˌil / (AmE), / ˈkʌskˌiːl / (BrE)
A type of elongated, compressed fish that resembles an eel, found in marine environments, especially family Ophidiidae.
elongated marine fish resembling an eel

Examples

The cusk-eel burrowed quickly into the sandy seabed to hide from predators.
Scientists discovered a rare species of cusk-eel during their deep-sea expedition.
Many aquariums feature the unusual cusk-eel due to its unique body shape.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: eel - an elongated fish belonging to the order Anguilliformes
