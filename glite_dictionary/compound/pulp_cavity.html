pulp cavity

1. NOUN

/ pʌlp ˈkævəti / (AmE), / pʌlp ˈkævəti / (BrE)
the central hollow in a tooth that contains the dental pulp, including the root canal
central hollow in a tooth containing pulp
medical

Examples

A dentist discovered that the pulp cavity was infected and needed immediate treatment.
The pulp cavity contains nerves and blood vessels essential for the health of the tooth.
Cavities that reach the pulp cavity may cause severe pain and require a root canal procedure.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: root canal - the part of the pulp cavity that extends into the root of a tooth
  • Synonym: dental pulp - the soft tissue within the pulp cavity of a tooth
