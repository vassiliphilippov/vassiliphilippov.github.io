limited war

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪmɪt̬ɪd wɔr / (AmE), / ˈlɪmɪtɪd wɔː / (BrE)
A war fought with restricted aims and limited resources, rather than seeking the total defeat of the enemy.
war with limited aims or scope

Examples

The Korean conflict is often considered a limited war due to its constrained objectives and boundaries.
Diplomats argued that transforming the conflict into a limited war would prevent further escalation.
Unlike a total war, a limited war seeks to achieve specific goals without complete destruction of the enemy.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: total war - war waged without limitation as to weapons, territory, or combatants, aiming for complete victory
