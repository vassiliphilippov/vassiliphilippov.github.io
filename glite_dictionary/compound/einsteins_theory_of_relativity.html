Einstein's theory of relativity

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪnstaɪnz ˈθɪəri əv rɪˈlætɪvɪti / (AmE), / ˈaɪnstaɪnz ˈθɪəri əv rɪˈlatɪvɪti / (BrE)
the scientific theory, developed by Albert Einstein, stating that space and time are relative and not absolute, forming the foundation of modern physics
Einstein's theory that space and time are relative

Examples

Many modern technologies, such as GPS, rely on Einstein's theory of relativity to function accurately.
Most people have heard of Einstein's theory of relativity, but few understand its profound implications for our understanding of the universe.
The predictions made by Einstein's theory of relativity have been confirmed by countless experiments and observations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: relativity - the idea that measurements of space and time are not absolute

Etymology

Named after German physicist Albert Einstein (1879–1955).
