Norse mythology

1. NOUN

/ nɔrs mɪˈθɑləˌdʒi / (AmE), / nɔːs mɪˈθɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the body of myths and legends originating from pre-Christian Scandinavia, featuring gods, heroes, and cosmological tales
pre-Christian Scandinavian myths and legends

Examples

Many of today's fantasy novels are inspired by Norse mythology and its epic tales of gods and giants.
Odin, Thor, and Loki are some of the most prominent deities in Norse mythology.
The study of Norse mythology reveals insights into the beliefs and culture of ancient Scandinavia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Greek mythology - the collection of myths from ancient Greece
  • Synonym: Roman mythology - the collection of myths from ancient Rome
  • Synonym: Celtic mythology - the collection of myths from ancient Celtic peoples

Etymology

'Norse' from Old Norse 'norrœnn' (northern); 'mythology' from Greek 'mythología' (the telling of myths).
