genus Cladorhyncus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs klædɔːˈrɪŋkəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs klædəˈrɪŋkəs / (BrE)
A genus of wading birds in the stilt family, similar to avocets but with straight bills.
genus of wading birds with straight bills

Examples

The genus Cladorhyncus includes birds that resemble avocets but have straight bills.
Ornithologists study the genus Cladorhyncus to better understand the evolution of wading birds.
Only one species currently belongs to the genus Cladorhyncus, which inhabits parts of Australia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stilt - a wading bird with long legs
  • Synonym: avocet - a wading bird with upturned bill, related group
