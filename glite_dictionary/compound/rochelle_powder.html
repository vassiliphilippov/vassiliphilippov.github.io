Rochelle powder

1. NOUN

/ roʊˈʃɛl ˌpaʊdər / (AmE), / rəʊˈʃɛl ˌpaʊdə / (BrE)
A medicinal powder containing sodium bicarbonate, Rochelle salt, and tartaric acid, used as a mild laxative.
medicinal laxative powder

Examples

The doctor recommended a dose of Rochelle powder to relieve his mild constipation.
In the 19th century, Rochelle powder was a popular home remedy for digestive issues.
She mixed the Rochelle powder with water to create an effervescent drink.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: laxative - a substance that speeds up or facilitates bowel movements

Etymology

Rochelle salt named after La Rochelle, France, where it was first prepared; powder denotes the form of the compound.
