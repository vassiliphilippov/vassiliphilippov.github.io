Lake Chelan

1. PROPN

/ leɪk ʃəˈlæn / (AmE), / leɪk ʃəˈlæn / (BrE)
a long, narrow, deep lake in central Washington state, located in the Cascade Range and known for its clear water and scenic surroundings.
deep lake in central Washington state

Examples

Many tourists visit Lake Chelan each summer to enjoy boating and fishing.
The town of Chelan sits on the southern tip of Lake Chelan.
The crystal-clear waters of Lake Chelan are popular with swimmers and kayakers.

Etymology

From Chelan, the name of a local Native American (Salish) tribe, meaning 'deep water'.
