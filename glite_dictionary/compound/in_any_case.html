in any case

1. ADV

/ ɪn ˈɛni keɪs / (AmE), / ɪn ˈeni keɪs / (BrE)
used to introduce an additional point, or to state that something remains true regardless of other factors; anyway.
used to add or emphasize a point

Examples

You shouldn't worry about the weather; in any case, the event will be held indoors.
He was busy last weekend, but in any case, I didn't invite him.
I was planning to leave early, but in any case I couldn't have stayed late.

Synset Examples

I don't want to go to a restaurant; besides, we can't afford it
she couldn't shelter behind him all the time and in any case he wasn't always with her

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anyway - used to confirm or support a previous statement
  • Synonym: besides - used to add another reason or point

2. ADV

/ ɪn ˈɛni keɪs / (AmE), / ɪn ˈeni keɪs / (BrE)
used to say that something is true or certain whatever the situation may be; regardless of what has happened or may happen.
regardless of the situation

Examples

You can call me later, but in any case, I'll email you the details.
We may arrive late, but in any case, the meeting will go ahead.
The answer probably won't change, but in any case, I'm happy to check again.

Synset Examples

I’ll bring my umbrella; in any case, it might rain.
She decided to apologize; in any case, it was the right thing to do.
We need to finish the project; in any case, the deadline is approaching.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anyway - used to confirm or support a previous statement regardless of circumstances
  • Synonym: nevertheless - used to introduce a contrasting statement or emphasize that something happens despite something else
