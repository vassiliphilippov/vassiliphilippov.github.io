Euclid's fifth axiom

1. NOUN

/ ˈjuːklɪdz ˈfɪfθ ˈæksiˌɑm / (AmE), / ˈjuːklɪdz ˈfɪfθ ˈæksiəm / (BrE)
the geometric axiom stating that through any point not on a given line, there is exactly one line parallel to the given line
the parallel postulate in geometry

Examples

The uniqueness of parallel lines is guaranteed by Euclid's fifth axiom in classical geometry.
Non-Euclidean geometry emerges when Euclid's fifth axiom is replaced or modified.
Many mathematicians attempted to prove Euclid's fifth axiom using the other axioms, but failed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parallel postulate - the equivalent statement of Euclid's fifth axiom in geometry

Etymology

Named after Euclid, a Greek mathematician active around 300 BCE, known for his work 'Elements'.
