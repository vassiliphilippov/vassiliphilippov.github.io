sound bow

1. NOUN

/ saʊnd boʊ / (AmE), / saʊnd bəʊ / (BrE)
the thickest part of a bell, against which the clapper strikes to produce sound
the part of a bell struck by the clapper

Examples

The clapper hit the sound bow with a loud, resonant peal.
Cracks on the sound bow can affect the tone and quality of the bell.
Bell makers carefully measure the thickness of the sound bow during the casting process.
