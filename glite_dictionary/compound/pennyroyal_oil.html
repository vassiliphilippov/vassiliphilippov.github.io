pennyroyal oil

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛniˌrɔɪəl ˈɔɪl / (AmE), / ˌpɛniˌrɔɪəl ˈɔɪl / (BrE)
an essential oil distilled from the European pennyroyal, having a mint-like scent, and used mainly in soaps and sometimes in herbal remedies
mint-scented oil from pennyroyal plant

Examples

The soap was fragranced with pennyroyal oil, giving it a refreshing herbal aroma.
In traditional medicine, pennyroyal oil was sometimes used, although it is now considered toxic in large amounts.
The manufacturer uses pennyroyal oil to add a minty scent to their natural cleaning products.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɛniˌrɔɪəl ɔɪl / (AmE), / ˈpɛniˌrɔɪəl ɔɪl / (BrE)
an aromatic essential oil extracted from the American pennyroyal plant, often used in traditional remedies and as a flavoring
aromatic oil from pennyroyal plant

Examples

She read about the uses of pennyroyal oil in an old herbal remedy book.
The strong scent of pennyroyal oil was noticeable throughout the room.
Historically, pennyroyal oil was used to repel insects and treat minor ailments.

Etymology

From 'pennyroyal,' a mint family herb (origin: Middle English, from Old French *puliol real*, from Latin *puleium regium*), and 'oil.'
