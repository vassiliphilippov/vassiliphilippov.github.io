structural concrete

1. NOUN

/ ˈstrʌktʃɚl kənˈkriːt / (AmE), / ˈstrʌktʃərəl kɒnˈkriːt / (BrE)
concrete that is designed to bear structural loads and forms an integral part of a building or structure.
concrete designed to carry structural loads

Examples

The building's foundation was constructed with structural concrete to ensure maximum strength.
All load-bearing walls must use structural concrete to support the upper floors.
Using structural concrete is essential in bridge construction for both safety and durability.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reinforced concrete - concrete strengthened with embedded metal for load-bearing structures
