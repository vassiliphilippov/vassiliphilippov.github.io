oil pressure

1. NOUN

/ ɔɪl ˈprɛʃər / (AmE), / ɔɪl ˈprɛʃə / (BrE)
the pressure of oil circulating in the moving parts of an internal combustion engine, necessary for proper lubrication and operation
pressure of oil in an engine

Examples

The mechanic checked the oil pressure to make sure the engine was running smoothly.
Low oil pressure can cause severe damage to your vehicle's engine.
A warning light appeared on the dashboard when the oil pressure dropped suddenly.
