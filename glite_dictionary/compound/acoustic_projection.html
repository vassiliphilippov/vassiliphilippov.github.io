acoustic projection

1. NOUN

/ əˈkuːstɪk prəˈdʒɛkʃən / (AmE), / əˈkuːstɪk prəˈdʒɛkʃən / (BrE)
the quality of a sound that enables it to be heard clearly at a distance or to penetrate through other sounds
the ability of sound to carry or penetrate

Examples

The new violin model was praised for its acoustic projection in large concert halls.
Proper acoustic projection is crucial for stage actors to be heard by the entire audience.
Designers tried to enhance the acoustic projection of the speaker system for outdoor events.

Synset Examples

our ukuleles have been designed to have superior sound and projection
a prime ingredient of public speaking is projection of the voice

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resonance - the quality in a sound of being deep, full, and reverberating
