literal error

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪtərəl ˈɛrər / (AmE), / ˈlɪtərəl ˈɛrər / (BrE)
A mistake in printed material caused by mechanical or typographical faults, such as a misprint or typo.
misprint or typographical mistake

Examples

The first edition contains a literal error in the author's name on the title page.
A literal error in the text changed the meaning of the entire sentence.
Editors must carefully review manuscripts to eliminate any literal error before publishing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: misprint - an error in printed text
  • Synonym: typo - a typographical error

Etymology

From Latin 'littera' (letter), reflecting a mistake at the level of letters or characters.
