poison mercury

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɔɪzən ˈmɜrkjəri / (AmE), / ˈpɔɪzən ˈmɜːkjəri / (BrE)
a climbing plant found in eastern and central North America with three-part leaves, greenish flowers, and clusters of white berries; produces an oil that causes a skin rash on contact
plant causing rash on contact

Examples

Many hikers developed a rash after brushing against poison mercury in the woods.
The white berries of poison mercury are attractive but should never be touched.
Learning to identify poison mercury is essential for anyone spending time outdoors in the region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poison ivy - common North American plant causing a skin rash
