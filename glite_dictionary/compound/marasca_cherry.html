marasca cherry

1. NOUN

/ məˈræskə ˈtʃɛri / (AmE), / məˈræskə ˈtʃɛri / (BrE)
A small, dark, bitter cherry from Dalmatia, whose juice is used to make maraschino liqueur.
Dalmatian wild cherry used for liqueur

Examples

The famous Italian liqueur is made from the juice of the marasca cherry.
They planted a marasca cherry tree in their orchard for its unique flavor.
Authentic maraschino is always produced with marasca cherry fruit from Dalmatia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maraschino - a liqueur made from marasca cherries

Etymology

From Italian 'marasca', from 'amarasca', relating to bitterness, derived from 'amaro' (bitter).
