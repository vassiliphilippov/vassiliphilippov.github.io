adenylic acid

1. NOUN

/ əˌdɛnɪlɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / əˌdɛnɪlɪk ˈasɪd / (BrE)
A nucleotide found in muscle cells and important in metabolism, forming part of the reversible conversion to ADP and ATP.
a nucleotide important in metabolism
medical

Examples

Researchers studied the concentration of adenylic acid in muscle tissue during intense exercise.
The reversible conversion of adenylic acid to ADP and ATP is essential for cellular energy transfer.
A deficiency in adenylic acid may impair the body's ability to generate energy efficiently.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adenosine monophosphate - an alternative name for adenylic acid
  • Degree Higher: ADP - adenosine diphosphate, a related nucleotide in metabolism
  • Degree Higher: ATP - adenosine triphosphate, a related nucleotide of higher energy in metabolism

Etymology

From 'adenine' (a base in nucleic acids) and '-yl' plus 'ic acid'.
