spotted Joe-Pye weed

1. NOUN

/ ˈspɑːt̬ɪd ˌdʒoʊˈpaɪ wiːd / (AmE), / ˈspɒtɪd ˌdʒəʊˈpaɪ wiːd / (BrE)
A North American herb with whorled leaves and terminal clusters of small pinkish or purple flower heads.
North American herb with pinkish or purple flower clusters

Examples

We found a patch of spotted Joe-Pye weed growing along the riverbank.
Gardeners often plant spotted Joe-Pye weed to attract butterflies and bees.
The tall stems and purple flowers of spotted Joe-Pye weed make it easy to identify in late summer.

Etymology

Named after the Native American medicine man Joe Pye, who is said to have used the plant for medicinal purposes.
