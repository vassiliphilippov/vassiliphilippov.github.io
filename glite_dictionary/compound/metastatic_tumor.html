metastatic tumor

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛtəˈstætɪk ˈtuːmɚ / (AmE), / ˌmɛtəˈstætɪk ˈtjuːmə / (BrE)
A malignant tumor that has spread from its original location to other parts of the body.
a malignant tumor that spreads
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with a metastatic tumor in her lungs, originating from breast cancer.
Doctors must determine whether the growth is a metastatic tumor or a primary one.
Chemotherapy is often used to treat patients with metastatic tumors that have spread to multiple organs.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: primary tumor - a tumor at the original site where cancer began
  • Antonym: benign tumor - a non-cancerous tumor that does not spread

Etymology

From Greek 'metastasis' (change of place) and Latin 'tumor' (swelling).
