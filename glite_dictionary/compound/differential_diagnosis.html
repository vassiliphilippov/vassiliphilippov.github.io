differential diagnosis

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪfəˈrɛnʃəl ˌdaɪəgˈnoʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌdɪfəˈrɛnʃəl ˌdaɪəgˈnəʊsɪs / (BrE)
the process of distinguishing a particular disease or condition from others that present with similar clinical features
method for distinguishing between similar diseases
medical

Examples

The doctor used a differential diagnosis to rule out other potential causes of the patient's symptoms.
Before making a final decision, she prepared a list of possible conditions for differential diagnosis.
Accurate differential diagnosis is essential when symptoms overlap among various illnesses.

Etymology

From Latin *differentia* (difference) and Greek *diagnōsis* (discernment, defining).
