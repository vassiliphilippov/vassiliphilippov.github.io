dollar bill

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɑlər bɪl / (AmE), / ˈdɒlə bɪl / (BrE)
A piece of paper currency worth one dollar, especially in the United States.
one-dollar paper currency

Examples

He handed the cashier a dollar bill to pay for his coffee.
I found a crumpled dollar bill on the sidewalk this morning.
She placed a dollar bill in the tip jar before leaving the café.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: banknote - a piece of paper money issued by a bank, used as legal tender
  • Synonym: dollar - the basic unit of currency in the US, Canada, and other countries
  • Antonym: coin - a flat, typically round piece of metal used as money
