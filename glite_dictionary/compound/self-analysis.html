self-analysis

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛlf əˈnæləsɪs / (AmE), / ˌself əˈnæləsɪs / (BrE)
the process of examining and evaluating one's own thoughts, feelings, and motives, especially using psychological methods
examining one's own thoughts and motives

Examples

After her therapist's advice, she began regular self-analysis to better understand her emotional reactions.
His habit of self-analysis sometimes led him to overthink simple situations.
Many people practice self-analysis through journaling and meditation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: introspection - the examination of one's own thoughts and feelings

Etymology

Formed from 'self-' (referring to oneself) and 'analysis' (from Greek 'analusis', a breaking up).
