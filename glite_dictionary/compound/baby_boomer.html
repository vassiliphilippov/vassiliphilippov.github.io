baby boomer

1. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪ.bi ˌbuː.mɚ / (AmE), / ˈbeɪ.bi ˌbuː.mə / (BrE)
a person born during the post-World War II baby boom, typically between 1946 and 1964, especially in the United States and other Western countries
member of the postwar baby boom generation

Examples

Many baby boomers are now reaching retirement age and changing the workforce demographic.
As a baby boomer, she remembers life before the internet and smartphones.
The concert was packed with baby boomers reliving the music of their youth.

Synset Examples

they expanded the schools for a generation of baby boomers

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Generation X - the generation following the baby boomers, born roughly between 1965 and 1980
  • Antonym: millennial - a person born between the early 1980s and mid-1990s to early 2000s

Etymology

From 'baby boom' (referring to the marked increase in births after World War II) + '-er' (indicating a person associated with something).
