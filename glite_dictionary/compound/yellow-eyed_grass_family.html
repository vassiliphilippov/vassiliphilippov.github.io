yellow-eyed grass family

1. NOUN

/ ˌjɛloʊ aɪd ˈɡræs ˌfæməli / (AmE), / ˌjɛləʊ aɪd ˈɡrɑːs ˌfæmɪli / (BrE)
a family of flowering plants (Xyridaceae) found in tropical to temperate regions, typically growing in wet habitats
a family of wetland flowering plants

Examples

Several species in the yellow-eyed grass family thrive in marshy environments.
Botanists have identified unique ecological roles for members of the yellow-eyed grass family.
The yellow-eyed grass family is distinguished by its characteristic yellow-centered flowers.
