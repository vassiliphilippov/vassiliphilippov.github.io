keynote speaker

1. NOUN

/ ˈkiːˌnoʊt ˈspiːkər / (AmE), / ˈkiːnəʊt ˈspiːkə / (BrE)
the main speaker at a meeting, conference, or other formal event, who sets the underlying tone or theme
main speaker at an event

Examples

The keynote speaker captivated the audience with her insights on innovation.
As the keynote speaker, he delivered an inspiring message about leadership in education.
The organizers announced a Nobel laureate as this year's keynote speaker.

Synset Examples

The conference featured a renowned scientician as the keynote speaker.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guest speaker - a person invited to speak at an event who is not part of the regular program

Etymology

From 'keynote' (main underlying theme) + 'speaker'; 'keynote' from music — the note on which a key is based.
