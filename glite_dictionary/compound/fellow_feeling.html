fellow feeling

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɛloʊ ˈfiːlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfɛləʊ ˈfiːlɪŋ / (BrE)
a sense of sympathy and understanding for someone else's feelings, especially sorrow or distress
shared sympathy or empathy

Examples

She showed great fellow feeling for the victims of the disaster.
A sense of fellow feeling united the community during hard times.
His fellow feeling made him a compassionate and understanding friend.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: empathy - the ability to understand and share the feelings of another
  • Synonym: sympathy - feelings of pity and sorrow for someone else's misfortune
  • Antonym: apathy - lack of interest, enthusiasm, or concern

Etymology

From 'fellow' (companion, partner) and 'feeling' (emotion); in use since the 17th century.
