European white birch

1. NOUN

/ jʊˈroʊpiən waɪt bɜrtʃ / (AmE), / jʊəˈrəʊpiən waɪt bɜːtʃ / (BrE)
a species of birch tree native to Europe, known for its silvery white peeling bark and drooping branches
birch tree with white bark, Europe

Examples

The European white birch is often planted as an ornamental tree in parks and gardens.
You can recognize a European white birch by its striking, silvery bark.
Several European white birch trees lined the edge of the meadow, creating a beautiful contrast with the green grass.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: birch - a tree of the genus Betula, having thin bark and slender branches
