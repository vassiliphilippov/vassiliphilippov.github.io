family Ceratophyllaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛrətoʊˌfɪliˈeɪsiː / (AmE), / ˌsɛrətəˌfɪlɪˈeɪsiː / (BrE)
A family of aquatic flowering plants coextensive with the genus Ceratophyllum, known as hornworts.
family of aquatic plants (hornworts)

Examples

The family Ceratophyllaceae contains only the hornworts, which are found in freshwater habitats.
Botanists have long debated the evolutionary position of the family Ceratophyllaceae among flowering plants.
Members of the family Ceratophyllaceae lack roots and are entirely submerged in water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ceratophyllum - genus of aquatic plants in Ceratophyllaceae, hornworts

Etymology

From Latin 'Cerato-' (horn) and 'phyllum' (leaf); botanical family name follows taxonomic conventions.
