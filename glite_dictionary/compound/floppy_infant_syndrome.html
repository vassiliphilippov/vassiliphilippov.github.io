floppy infant syndrome

1. NOUN

/ ˈflɑpi ˈɪnfənt ˈsɪndroʊm / (AmE), / ˈflɒpi ˈɪnfənt ˈsɪndrəʊm / (BrE)
A medical condition in infants characterized by reduced muscle tone, resulting in apparent weakness and limpness.
condition causing poor muscle tone in infants
medical

Examples

The pediatrician was concerned about floppy infant syndrome after noticing the baby's weak muscle tone.
