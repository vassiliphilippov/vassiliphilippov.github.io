adoptive parent

1. NOUN

/ əˈdɑptɪv ˈpɛrənt / (AmE), / əˈdɒptɪv ˈpeərənt / (BrE)
a person who adopts a child born to other parents and raises the child as their own
a person who adopts a child

Examples

After many years of waiting, she finally became an adoptive parent.
The adoptive parents welcomed their new son into the family with open arms.
As an adoptive parent, he attended support groups to meet others in similar situations.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: biological parent - a child's natural parent by birth
  • Synonym: adopter - person who adopts something or someone

Etymology

From 'adoptive' (from Latin adoptivus, from adoptāre 'to adopt') + 'parent' (from Latin parens, parentis 'father or mother').
