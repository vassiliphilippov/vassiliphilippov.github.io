flip side

1. NOUN

/ flɪp saɪd / (AmE), / flɪp saɪd / (BrE)
the opposite or contrasting aspect of something; the other side of an argument, situation, or quality
opposite or contrasting aspect

Examples

The flip side of her ambition is that she can sometimes be very impatient.
Economic growth brings benefits, but on the flip side, it can widen social inequality.
He loved the flexibility of freelance work, though he recognized the flip side was income instability.

Synset Examples

the flip side of your positive qualities is they sometimes get out of control
on the flip side of partnerships he talked about their competition

Related Terms

  • Synonym: other side - the alternative or another aspect of something
  • Synonym: downside - a negative aspect of something
  • Antonym: upside - a positive aspect of something

Etymology

Originally from the literal reverse side of a vinyl record or cassette tape.
