black mallee

1. NOUN

/ blæk ˈmæli / (AmE), / blak ˈmæli / (BrE)
a small Australian eucalyptus tree (Eucalyptus redunca or similar species), with rough dark bark near the base, that produces red eucalyptus gum (kino)
a type of eucalyptus tree with dark bark

Examples

The black mallee thrives in the arid regions of Western Australia.
Gum extracted from the black mallee is valued for its medicinal properties.
After the fire, new shoots quickly appeared from the base of the black mallee.

Etymology

From 'black' (referring to the dark bark) and 'mallee' (an Australian term for multi-stemmed eucalypt trees, from Wemba-Wemba, an Aboriginal language).
