cold cash

1. NOUN

/ koʊld kæʃ / (AmE), / kəʊld kæʃ / (BrE)
money in the form of actual cash, especially when immediately available for use or payment.
readily available money in cash form

Examples

She insisted on being paid in cold cash rather than by check.
The car dealer gave him a better deal when he showed up with cold cash.
They kept a little cold cash hidden in a drawer for emergencies.

Synset Examples

his wife was always a good source of ready cash
he paid cold cash for the TV set

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ready money - money that is immediately available
  • Synonym: cash - money in the form of notes and coins
