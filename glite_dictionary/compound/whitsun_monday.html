Whitsun Monday

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪtsən ˈmʌn.deɪ / (AmE), / ˈwɪtsən ˈmʌn.deɪ / (BrE)
The day after Whitsunday (Pentecost), formerly observed as a legal holiday in England, Wales, and Ireland.
day after Whitsunday; former holiday in UK and Ireland
british

Examples

Many people attended special church services on Whitsun Monday in previous centuries.
In the UK, Whitsun Monday was once an official public holiday before being replaced by the Spring Bank Holiday.
Schools used to close for Whitsun Monday, giving families time to celebrate together.

Etymology

From 'Whitsun' (Pentecost), from Old English 'hwīta sunnandæg' (White Sunday), and 'Monday'.
