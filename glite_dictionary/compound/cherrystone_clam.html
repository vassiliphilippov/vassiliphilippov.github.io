cherrystone clam

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɛrˌstoʊn klæm / (AmE), / ˈtʃɛəˌstəʊn klam / (BrE)
A quahog (hard clam) of medium size, typically larger than a littleneck but smaller than a chowder clam, commonly eaten cooked or raw.
medium-sized edible hard clam

Examples

We ordered a dozen cherrystone clams on the half shell for our appetizer.
Fishermen sort the catch by size, separating cherrystone clams from littlenecks and chowders.
The recipe calls for fresh cherrystone clams, garlic, and white wine sauce.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quahog - a large, edible clam also known as the hard clam
  • Degree Lower: littleneck clam - a small, young hard clam
  • Degree Higher: chowder clam - a large, mature quahog used mainly for chowder

Etymology

From 'cherrystone,' probably referring to the clam's size, similar to that of a cherry stone, and 'clam.'

2. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɛriˌstoʊn klæm / (AmE), / ˈtʃɛriˌstəʊn klam / (BrE)
A hard clam of medium size, larger than a littleneck but smaller than a chowder clam, commonly eaten raw or cooked.
medium-sized hard clam

Examples

Fresh cherrystone clam is often served on the half shell at seafood restaurants.
The chef stuffed each cherrystone clam with breadcrumbs and bacon for a classic clams casino.
We bought a dozen cherrystone clams at the market for our weekend barbecue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quahog - a hard-shelled clam native to North America
  • Degree Lower: littleneck clam - a small quahog; smaller than a cherrystone
  • Degree Higher: chowder clam - a large quahog; larger than a cherrystone

Etymology

From 'cherry stone', likely referring to the clam's size, and 'clam', from earlier English.
