ringside seat

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɪŋ.saɪd sit / (AmE), / ˈrɪŋ.saɪd siːt / (BrE)
a seat in the front row next to a boxing or wrestling ring, providing an unobstructed view of the action
front-row seat by a boxing or wrestling ring

Examples

She was thrilled to have a ringside seat at the championship match.
With a ringside seat, you can catch every detail of the fight up close.
Tickets for a ringside seat are often the most expensive and hard to get.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: front-row seat - a seat in the first row of an audience or stadium

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɪŋ.saɪd ˌsiːt / (AmE), / ˈrɪŋ.saɪd ˌsiːt / (BrE)
a seat situated very close to the action at an event, especially a sporting or entertainment event, offering a clear and exciting view; also used figuratively to mean a close or privileged position from which to observe an activity or situation
seat very close to the action at an event

Examples

From our ringside seat, we could see every detail of the fight.
The journalist had a ringside seat to the political negotiations.
Having a ringside seat at the concert made the experience unforgettable.

Synset Examples

Having a ringside seat made the event even more thrilling.
We got a ringside seat for the boxing match.
She enjoyed the fight from her ringside seat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: front-row seat - a seat in the row closest to the performance or action

Etymology

From 'ringside' (the area immediately adjacent to a ring, especially in boxing) + 'seat'.
