public works

1. NOUN

/ ˈpʌblɪk wɝks / (AmE), / ˈpʌblɪk wɜːks / (BrE)
structures and facilities such as roads, schools, and bridges, built and maintained by the government for public use
government-funded infrastructure projects

Examples

The city allocated millions of dollars to improve its public works after the storm.
She works for the public works department, overseeing road and bridge repairs.
Many New Deal programs focused on creating jobs through public works projects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: infrastructure - the basic physical structures and facilities needed for the operation of a society
  • Antonym: private sector - business and industry not controlled by the government; opposite of public sector
