rye bread

1. NOUN

/ raɪ brɛd / (AmE), / raɪ brɛd / (BrE)
A type of bread made wholly or partly from rye flour, often darker and denser than wheat bread.
bread made with rye flour

Examples

She ordered a sandwich on rye bread with a side of pickles.
Many traditional Eastern European recipes call for rye bread instead of white bread.
The bakery sells several varieties of rye bread, including seeded and marble rye.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: wheat bread - bread made primarily from wheat flour
  • Synonym: pumpernickel - dark, coarse German bread made from rye
