multiple mononeuropathy

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌltɪpəl ˌmɑnoʊnʊˈrɑpəθi / (AmE), / ˈmʌltɪpl ˌmɒnəʊnjuːˈrɒpəθi / (BrE)
A medical condition in which several individual nerve trunks are affected by disease or dysfunction.
disease affecting several individual nerves
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with multiple mononeuropathy after experiencing weakness in several limbs.
Symptoms of multiple mononeuropathy can vary depending on which nerves are affected.
Treatment for multiple mononeuropathy often involves addressing the underlying systemic condition.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: polyneuropathy - disease affecting many peripheral nerves
  • Degree Lower: mononeuropathy - disease affecting a single nerve trunk

Etymology

From 'multiple' (Latin multiplic-, 'many') + 'mononeuropathy' (Greek monos 'single' + neuron 'nerve' + -pathy 'disease').
