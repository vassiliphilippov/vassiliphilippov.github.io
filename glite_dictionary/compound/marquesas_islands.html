Marquesas Islands

1. PROPN

/ mɑrˈkeɪzəs ˈaɪləndz / (AmE), / mɑːˈkeɪzəs ˈaɪləndz / (BrE)
a group of volcanic islands in the south central Pacific Ocean, forming part of French Polynesia
volcanic island group in French Polynesia

Examples

The Marquesas Islands are known for their stunning landscapes and rich cultural traditions.
Artist Paul Gauguin spent his final years living in the Marquesas Islands.
Volcanic peaks rise dramatically above the sea in the Marquesas Islands of French Polynesia.

Etymology

Named after Don García Hurtado de Mendoza, Marques of Cañete, Spanish viceroy of Peru.
