optic disc

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑptɪk dɪsk / (AmE), / ˈɒptɪk dɪsk / (BrE)
the circular area on the retina where the optic nerve enters the eye, not sensitive to light and sometimes called the blind spot
spot on retina where optic nerve enters the eye
medical

Examples

The ophthalmologist examined the optic disc for any signs of swelling or damage.
Changes in the appearance of the optic disc can indicate increased pressure inside the skull.
Because the optic disc lacks photoreceptors, it is also known as the blind spot.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blind spot - the area on the retina without photoreceptors where vision is not possible

Etymology

From Greek 'optikos' (of seeing/sight) and Latin 'discus' (disk).
