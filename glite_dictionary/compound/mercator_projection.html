Mercator projection

1. NOUN

/ mərˈkeɪt̬ər prəˈdʒɛkʃən / (AmE), / məˈkeɪtə prəˈdʒɛkʃən / (BrE)
A method of mapping the earth onto a cylinder, where shapes are preserved but areas become increasingly distorted farther from the equator.
cylindrical map projection with area distortion

Examples

The Mercator projection distorts the size of land masses near the poles.
Most world maps used in classrooms are based on the Mercator projection.
Sailors have long relied on the Mercator projection for navigational charts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: map projection - any method of representing the earth's surface on a flat map

Etymology

Named after Gerardus Mercator (1512–1594), Flemish cartographer.
