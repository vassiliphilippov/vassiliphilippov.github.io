diplomatic mission

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪpləˈmætɪk ˈmɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɪpləˈmætɪk ˈmɪʃən / (BrE)
A group of people sent by one state or country to represent it in another, usually in the form of an embassy or consulate.
official group representing a country abroad

Examples

The United States established a diplomatic mission in the newly independent country.
Every diplomatic mission is expected to protect the interests of its home nation.
Security measures around the diplomatic mission were increased following the threat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: embassy - the official residence or offices of an ambassador
  • Synonym: consulate - the place where a consul works
