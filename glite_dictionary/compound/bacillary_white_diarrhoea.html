bacillary white diarrhoea

1. NOUN

/ bəˈsɪləri waɪt daɪəˈriə / (AmE), / bəˈsɪləri waɪt dʌɪəˈrɪə / (BrE)
a serious bacterial disease affecting young chickens, characterized by the passing of white diarrhea and often leading to high mortality
bacterial disease of young chickens
medical

Examples

The farm lost half its flock to an outbreak of bacillary white diarrhoea last spring.
Veterinarians recommend strict biosecurity measures to prevent bacillary white diarrhoea in hatcheries.
Early detection of bacillary white diarrhoea can save thousands of young chickens from infection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pullorum disease - another name for bacillary white diarrhoea in poultry

Etymology

Bacillary from Latin 'bacillus' (little rod, referring to the bacillus bacteria), and 'diarrhoea' from Greek 'diarrhoia' (a flowing through).
