genus Pipa

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈpiː.pə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈpiː.pə / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Pipidae, a group of aquatic frogs native to South America and Africa.
type genus of aquatic frogs (Pipidae)

Examples

The genus Pipa is known for its flat, aquatic frogs found in South America.
Scientists recently discovered a new species within the genus Pipa.
Unlike other frogs, members of genus Pipa exhibit unique parental care behaviors.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Pipidae - Family of aquatic frogs comprising genera including Pipa

Etymology

From Latin 'genus' (kind, type) and 'Pipa', the genus name assigned in taxonomy.
