genus Thymus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˈθaɪməs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈtʰɪməs / (BrE)
a genus of aromatic herbaceous plants in the mint family, including thyme, native to Europe, North Africa, and Asia.
a genus of mint herbs including thyme

Examples

The genus Thymus includes many species of aromatic herbs, such as common thyme.
Botanists have identified over 300 species within the genus Thymus.
Members of the genus Thymus are often cultivated for culinary and medicinal use.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thyme - a common aromatic herb used in cooking, member of the genus Thymus

Etymology

From Latin *thymus*, from Greek *thýmos* (thyme), the type genus of the group.
