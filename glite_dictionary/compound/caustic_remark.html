caustic remark

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔstɪk rɪˈmɑrk / (AmE), / ˈkɔːstɪk rɪˈmɑːk / (BrE)
a comment or statement that is sharply critical, hurtful, or sarcastic, often intended to mock or insult
sharply critical or sarcastic comment

Examples

Her caustic remark about the project left everyone in the room feeling uncomfortable.
He is well-known for making the occasional caustic remark during meetings.
The comedian's caustic remark about celebrity culture drew laughter and groans alike from the audience.

Synset Examples

he used sarcasm to upset his opponent
irony is wasted on the stupid
Satire is a sort of glass, wherein beholders do generally discover everybody's face but their own

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sarcastic comment - remark made with sarcasm to mock or insult
  • Antonym: compliment - a polite expression of praise or admiration

Etymology

From Greek *kaustikos* (capable of burning), later applied figuratively to speech.
