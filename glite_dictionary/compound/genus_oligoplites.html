genus Oligoplites

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˌoʊlɪgoʊˈplaɪtiːz / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˌɒlɪgəʊˈplaɪtiːz / (BrE)
A genus of fish in the family Carangidae, commonly known as leatherjackets, found in warm coastal waters of the Americas.
a genus of fish (leatherjackets)

Examples

Scientists discovered a new species within the genus Oligoplites off the Pacific coast.
The genus Oligoplites belongs to the jack family, Carangidae.
Commercial fisheries often catch species from the genus Oligoplites along tropical American shores.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leatherjacket - common name for fish of the genus Oligoplites

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'Oligo-' (few) and Greek 'hoplites' (armed) indicating few-armored.
