buffalo nut

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌfəloʊ nʌt / (AmE), / ˈbʌfələʊ nʌt / (BrE)
A shrub native to the southeastern United States, parasitic on the roots of hemlocks, with sparse spikes of greenish flowers and pulpy drupes.
parasitic shrub of southeastern US

Examples

The buffalo nut is often found growing in mixed hardwood forests in the South.
Native Americans sometimes used the fruit of the buffalo nut for medicinal purposes.
The buffalo nut thrives by attaching its roots to those of nearby hemlock trees.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbʌfəloʊ nʌt / (AmE), / ˈbʌfələʊ nʌt / (BrE)
the oily, fleshy fruit of the rabbitwood tree, also known as the buffalo nut tree (Pyrularia pubera), found in eastern North America
oily fruit of the rabbitwood tree

Examples

The rabbitwood produced several buffalo nuts in the late summer.
Local wildlife often feed on the buffalo nuts that fall from the trees.
Baskets of dried buffalo nuts lined the farmer's market stall.
