street theater

1. NOUN

/ strit ˈθiː.ə.tɚ / (AmE), / striːt ˈθɪə.tə / (BrE)
A form of theatrical performance presented in outdoor public spaces, often dramatizing social or political issues.
outdoor public performance, often social or political

Examples

The activist group used street theater to raise awareness about climate change in the city square.
Passersby stopped to watch the gripping street theater performance highlighting homelessness.
Her passion for street theater led her to organize performances in public parks across the country.
