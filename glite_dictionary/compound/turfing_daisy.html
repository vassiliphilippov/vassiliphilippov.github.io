turfing daisy

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɜr.fɪŋ ˈdeɪ.zi / (AmE), / ˈtɜː.fɪŋ ˈdeɪ.zi / (BrE)
a low, densely tufted perennial herb native to Turkey, with small white flowers, used as a ground cover in dry places; sometimes classified in the genus Matricaria
a perennial ground-cover herb with small white flowers

Examples

The turfing daisy is a popular choice for gardeners seeking drought-resistant ground cover.
White blooms of the turfing daisy appeared early in the spring, brightening the dry rock garden.
Some botanists include the turfing daisy in the genus Matricaria due to its similar floral structure.
