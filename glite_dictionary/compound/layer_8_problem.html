layer 8 problem

1. NOUN

/ ˈleɪ.ɚ eɪt ˈprɑː.bləm / (AmE), / ˈleɪ.ə eɪt ˈprɒb.ləm / (BrE)
A tongue-in-cheek term used, especially in information technology, to refer to problems caused by user behavior or mistakes rather than technical faults in hardware or software.
user-caused issue (IT humor)
informal slang

Examples

It turned out the company email outage was due to a layer 8 problem rather than a server failure.
Most tech support calls are actually about layer 8 problems.
When troubleshooting, always check for a layer 8 problem before looking for complex technical issues.

Synset Examples

The network issue was due to layer 8 problems.

Etymology

From the OSI model of computer networking, which has seven layers; 'layer 8' humorously refers to the user as an extra layer.
