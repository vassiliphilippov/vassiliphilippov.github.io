Palestine Authority

1. NOUN

/ ˌpæl.əˌstaɪn ɔːˈθɔr.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˌpæl.ɪˌstaɪn ɔːˈθɒr.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
An administrative organization established to govern parts of the Gaza Strip and the West Bank, territories recognized as Palestinian-administered areas.
Governing body in Gaza Strip and West Bank

Examples

The Palestine Authority was established following agreements between Israel and the Palestinians.
Officials from the Palestine Authority met with international leaders to discuss humanitarian aid.
Taxes in the West Bank are collected by the Palestine Authority.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Palestinian Authority - Alternative, widely used name for the same governing body
