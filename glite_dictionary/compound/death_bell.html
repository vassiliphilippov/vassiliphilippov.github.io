death bell

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛθ ˌbɛl / (AmE), / ˈdɛθ ˌbɛl / (BrE)
a bell that is rung to announce or signal a death, especially in a church or community
bell rung to announce a death

Examples

The church death bell tolled solemnly throughout the village after the news of the tragedy.
In olden times, the sound of the death bell would summon the community to mourn together.
Hearing the death bell at midnight sent a chill through everyone in the parish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: passing bell - bell rung to signal a person's impending or recent death
