for some reason

1. ADV

/ fər səm ˈɹiːzən / (AmE), / fə səm ˈriːzn / (BrE)
used to indicate that the cause of something is unknown or not understood
for an unknown cause or reason

Examples

For some reason, the meeting was canceled at the last minute.
I couldn't open the file for some reason.
For some reason, she looked upset, but she wouldn't say why.

Synset Examples

The internet connection is slow today for some reason.
