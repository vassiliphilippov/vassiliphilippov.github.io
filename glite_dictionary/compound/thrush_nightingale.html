thrush nightingale

1. NOUN

/ θrʌʃ ˈnaɪtˌɡeɪl / (AmE), / θrʌʃ ˈnaɪtˌɡeɪl / (BrE)
a large nightingale (Luscinia luscinia) native to eastern Europe, known for its melodious song
large nightingale of eastern Europe

Examples

The thrush nightingale is admired for its rich and varied songs during the breeding season.
Birdwatchers travel to eastern Europe every spring to hear the thrush nightingale in its natural habitat.
Unlike the common nightingale, the thrush nightingale tends to have a grayer and less reddish plumage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nightingale - small songbird known for its powerful and beautiful song
