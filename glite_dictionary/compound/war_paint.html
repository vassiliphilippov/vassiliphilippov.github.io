war paint

1. NOUN

/ wɔr peɪnt / (AmE), / wɔː peɪnt / (BrE)
Paint or pigments applied to the face and body, historically used by Indigenous peoples of the Americas before going into battle, often as a form of adornment or for ceremonial purposes.
paint applied to face/body before battle

Examples

The warriors adorned themselves with war paint before heading into battle.
Traditional ceremonies sometimes feature the application of war paint to honor ancestral customs.
He wore intricate war paint patterns that represented his tribe's heritage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: body paint - paint applied directly to the body for decoration

2. NOUN

/ wɔr peɪnt / (AmE), / wɔː peɪnt / (BrE)
makeup or cosmetics applied to the face, especially as a way of enhancing appearance or boosting confidence; often used humorously or informally
makeup or cosmetics for the face (informal)
informal

Examples

She put on her war paint before heading out for the party.
Don't bother me while I'm applying my morning war paint.
For some, a little war paint is just what they need to face the day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: makeup - cosmetics applied to the face
  • Synonym: cosmetics - substances applied to the face or body to enhance appearance

Etymology

Originally from the paint worn by warriors before battle; figuratively applied to cosmetics.

3. NOUN

/ ˈwɔr ˌpeɪnt / (AmE), / ˈwɔː ˌpeɪnt / (BrE)
colorful paint or makeup applied to the face or body, traditionally before battle or ceremony; by extension, elaborate makeup or ceremonial dress.
paint or makeup worn for battle or ceremony

Examples

Before going on stage, the actors carefully applied their war paint to get into character.
She put on her war paint before the big meeting, determined to make a strong impression.
The tribe's members wore traditional war paint during the festival ceremony.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: makeup - cosmetic products applied to the face
  • Synonym: camouflage - clothing or paint for concealment in military context
