SALT I

1. PROPN

/ sɔlt wʌn / (AmE), / sɔːlt wʌn / (BrE)
the first treaty between the United States and the Soviet Union resulting from the Strategic Arms Limitation Talks, signed in 1972 to limit strategic nuclear weapons
first Strategic Arms Limitation Treaty (1972)

Examples

The SALT I agreement marked a turning point in US-Soviet relations during the Cold War.
Many historians consider SALT I a crucial step towards nuclear arms control.
The signing of SALT I in 1972 established important limitations on strategic ballistic missiles.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: SALT II - the second Strategic Arms Limitation treaty between the US and the Soviet Union

Etymology

Acronym for Strategic Arms Limitation Talks I.
