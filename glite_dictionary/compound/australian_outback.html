Australian outback

1. NOUN

/ əˈstreɪ.li.ən ˈaʊt.bæk / (AmE), / ɒˈstreɪ.li.ən ˈaʊt.bæk / (BrE)
the remote, arid, and sparsely populated interior regions of Australia, often regarded as wild or inhospitable
remote interior regions of Australia

Examples

She spent a year living and working in the Australian outback, far from any major city.
Documentaries about the Australian outback showcase its unique wildlife and breathtaking landscapes.
