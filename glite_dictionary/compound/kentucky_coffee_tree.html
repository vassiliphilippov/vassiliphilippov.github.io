Kentucky coffee tree

1. NOUN

/ kənˈtʌki ˈkɔfi tri / (AmE), / kɛnˈtʌki ˈkɒfi triː / (BrE)
a large tree native to central and eastern North America, with bipinnate leaves and woody seed pods whose seeds can be used as a coffee substitute
North American tree; seeds substitute for coffee

Examples

The Kentucky coffee tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and unique seed pods.
Pioneers once roasted seeds from the Kentucky coffee tree as a coffee substitute.
We visited the arboretum to see a rare Kentucky coffee tree in bloom.
