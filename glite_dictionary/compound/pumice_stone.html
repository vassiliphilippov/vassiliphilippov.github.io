pumice stone

1. NOUN

/ ˈpʌmɪs stoʊn / (AmE), / ˈpʌmɪs stəʊn / (BrE)
a light, porous volcanic rock formed from lava, often used as an abrasive for cleaning or smoothing surfaces
porous volcanic rock used as abrasive

Examples

She used a pumice stone to smooth the rough skin on her heels.
A pumice stone can help remove stubborn stains from tiles and porcelain.
During the pedicure, the technician gently scrubbed my feet with a pumice stone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pumice - light, porous volcanic rock

Etymology

From Latin 'pumex' (foam, pumice).
