People's Mujahidin of Iran

1. PROPN

/ ˈpiːpəlz muːˈdʒɑːhedin əv aɪˈrɑːn / (AmE), / ˈpiːplz mjuːˈhædɪn əv ɪˈrɑːn / (BrE)
a political organization founded in the 1960s in Iran, originally opposing the Shah and now opposed to the Islamic fundamentalist regime, noted for its Marxist-Islamist ideology and armed actions
Iranian political organization with Marxist-Islamist ideology

Examples

The People's Mujahidin of Iran has been a prominent actor in the country's modern history.
Many Western countries have at times designated the People's Mujahidin of Iran as a terrorist group.
Supporters of the People's Mujahidin of Iran held rallies in major European capitals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: MEK - alternative name for People's Mujahidin of Iran (abbreviation for Mujahedin-e Khalq)
  • Synonym: Mujahedin-e Khalq - alternate transliteration of the group's Persian name

Etymology

From Persian 'Mojahedin-e Khalq-e Iran', meaning 'Holy Warriors of Iran'.
