plant virus

1. NOUN

/ plænt ˈvaɪrəs / (AmE), / plɑːnt ˈvaɪrəs / (BrE)
A virus that infects plants, typically consisting of a single strand of RNA and causing various plant diseases.
virus that infects plants

Examples

The tobacco mosaic plant virus was the first virus ever discovered.
Researchers are developing new methods to detect plant viruses in crops early.
A plant virus can severely reduce agricultural yields if not controlled.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: virus - infectious agent that replicates inside living cells
