wild emmer

1. NOUN

/ waɪld ˈɛmər / (AmE), / waɪld ˈɛmə / (BrE)
A species of wheat (Triticum dicoccoides) native to the Middle East, considered to be an ancestor of cultivated wheat.
a wild wheat species, ancestor of cultivated wheat

Examples

Wild emmer is believed to be the origin of modern bread wheat.
Researchers study wild emmer for its genetic diversity and resilience.
Ancient farmers first domesticated wild emmer thousands of years ago.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: emmer - a type of hulled wheat used historically as a food grain
