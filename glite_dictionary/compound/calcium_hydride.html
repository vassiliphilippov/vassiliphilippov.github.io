calcium hydride

1. NOUN

/ ˈkælsiəm haɪˈdraɪd / (AmE), / ˈkalsɪəm hʌɪˈdrʌɪd / (BrE)
a grayish powdery compound (CaH₂) used as a reducing agent and a source of hydrogen in chemical reactions
a chemical compound used as a reducing agent

Examples

The laboratory stored calcium hydride in airtight containers to prevent it from reacting with moisture.
When calcium hydride comes into contact with water, it releases hydrogen gas rapidly.
Engineers use calcium hydride to provide a controlled source of hydrogen during industrial processes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sodium hydride - a hydride of sodium, also used as a reducing agent
  • Antonym: calcium oxide - an oxide of calcium, a different calcium compound

Etymology

From calcium and hydride, indicating the compound of calcium with hydrogen.
