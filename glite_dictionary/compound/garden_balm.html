garden balm

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɑrdən bɑm / (AmE), / ˈɡɑːdn ˈbɑːm / (BrE)
a bushy perennial herb of the mint family, with small white or yellowish flowers and lemon-scented leaves, often grown in gardens and sometimes naturalized outside cultivation; also known as lemon balm
lemon-scented perennial herb (lemon balm)

Examples

She planted garden balm along the path to attract bees and add fragrance to the garden.
The leaves of garden balm are often used in herbal teas for their soothing properties.
After escaping from the garden, garden balm now grows wild in several nearby meadows.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lemon balm - another common name for garden balm (Melissa officinalis)
