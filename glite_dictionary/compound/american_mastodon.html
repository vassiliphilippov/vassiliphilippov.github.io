American mastodon

1. NOUN

/ əˈmɛr.ɪ.kən ˈmæs.təˌdɑn / (AmE), / əˈmɛr.ɪ.kən ˈmæs.təˌdɒn / (BrE)
an extinct species of mastodon (Mammut americanum) that lived in North America during the late Pleistocene epoch
extinct North American mastodon species

Examples

The American mastodon roamed the forests of North America thousands of years ago.
Scientists discovered the remains of an American mastodon preserved in a peat bog.
Unlike mammoths, the American mastodon had distinctive cone-shaped teeth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mastodon - an extinct large, elephant-like mammal with ridged teeth
  • Synonym: mammoth - an extinct large, hairy elephant-like mammal

Etymology

Mastodon from Greek 'mastos' (breast) + 'odous' (tooth), referring to the tooth shape; 'American' relating to its geographic origin.
