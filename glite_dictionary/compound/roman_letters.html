roman letters

1. NOUN

/ ˈroʊ.mən ˈlɛt.ərz / (AmE), / ˈrəʊ.mən ˈlɛt.əz / (BrE)
the style of letters or typeface based on inscriptions used by the ancient Romans, characterized by straight lines and simple forms
typeface based on ancient Roman inscriptions

Examples

The monument features an inscription in roman letters that dates back to the first century.
Art students studied the design of roman letters to understand classical typography.
Official documents in ancient Rome were often engraved using roman letters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: roman type - upright printing type derived from Roman inscriptions

Etymology

From Latin 'Romanus' (Roman) + 'letters', reflecting the script used in Ancient Rome.
