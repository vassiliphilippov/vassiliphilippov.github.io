pediatric care

1. NOUN

/ ˌpiːdiˈætɪk kɛr / (AmE), / ˌpiːdɪˈætrɪk keə / (BrE)
medical care or treatment specifically for infants, children, and adolescents
medical care for children
medical

Examples

The hospital is renowned for its pediatric care and child-friendly facilities.
Proper pediatric care ensures that children receive appropriate vaccinations and check-ups.
After medical school, she chose to pursue a career in pediatric care.

Synset Examples

He decided to specialize in pediatric care.
He's a doctor specializing in pediatric care.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paediatrics - branch of medicine dealing with children and their diseases
  • Antonym: adult care - medical care for adults

Etymology

From Greek 'pais' (child) and '-iatric' (relating to medical treatment).
