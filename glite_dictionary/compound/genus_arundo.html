genus Arundo

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs əˈruːn.doʊ / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs əˈruːn.dəʊ / (BrE)
A group of coarse, tall perennial grasses found in warm regions, commonly known as reeds.
a genus of tall reed grasses

Examples

The genus Arundo includes several species used for making reeds and thatching.
Researchers studied the ecological impact of genus Arundo in Mediterranean wetlands.
Many invasive species belonging to the genus Arundo threaten native wetland habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reed - a tall, slender-leaved plant of the grass family, found in water or marshy ground

Etymology

From Latin 'Arundo' meaning 'reed'.
