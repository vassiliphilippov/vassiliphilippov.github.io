mandrake root

1. NOUN

/ ˈmænˌdreɪk ruːt / (AmE), / ˈmæn.dreɪk ruːt / (BrE)
the root of the mandrake plant, traditionally believed to have medicinal or magical properties and sometimes used as a narcotic
root of the mandrake plant, used medicinally

Examples

The ancient herbalist believed that a mandrake root could cure various ailments.
Legend has it that pulling a mandrake root from the ground produces a terrible scream.
She kept a dried mandrake root on her shelf as a good luck charm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mandrake - a Mediterranean plant whose root resembles the human form, often attributed with magical properties

Etymology

Mandrake from Middle English 'mandragora', from Latin 'mandragora', from Greek 'mandragoras'.
