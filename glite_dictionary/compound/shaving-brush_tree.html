shaving-brush tree

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃeɪvɪŋ brʌʃ triː / (AmE), / ˈʃeɪvɪŋ brʌʃ triː / (BrE)
a tropical tree native to Mexico and Guatemala, noted for its dense, hairy flowers with long, narrow petals that cluster at branch ends before the leaves appear
tropical tree with hairy flowers, Mexico/Guatemala

Examples

The shaving-brush tree blossoms before its leaves emerge, creating a dramatic display of pinkish flowers.
In botanical gardens, the shaving-brush tree draws attention with its unique, brush-like blooms.
The shaving-brush tree is often grown as an ornamental plant in warm climates.
