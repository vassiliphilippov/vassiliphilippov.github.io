East Germanic language

1. NOUN

/ ist ˈdʒɜːrmænɪk ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / iːst ˈdʒɜːmænɪk ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
a now-extinct branch of the Germanic languages, historically spoken mainly in Eastern Europe
extinct branch of Germanic languages

Examples

Gothic is the best-known East Germanic language preserved in written texts.
The decline of the East Germanic language group began around the early Middle Ages.
Scholars have reconstructed aspects of the East Germanic language family from surviving documents.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: West Germanic language - a branch of Germanic languages including English, German, and Dutch
  • Degree Higher: North Germanic language - a branch of Germanic languages including the Scandinavian languages

Etymology

Formed from 'East' (direction) and 'Germanic', referring to the eastern group of the Germanic language family.
