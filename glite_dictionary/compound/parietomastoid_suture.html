parietomastoid suture

1. NOUN

/ pəˌraɪət̬oʊˈmæstɔɪd ˈsuːtʃɚ / (AmE), / pəˌraɪətəʊˈmæstɔɪd ˈsuːtʃə / (BrE)
the suture between the parietal bone and the mastoid part of the temporal bone in the skull
skull suture between parietal and temporal bones
medical

Examples

The parietomastoid suture is clearly visible in this detailed anatomical diagram of the skull.
A fracture near the parietomastoid suture may indicate serious cranial trauma.
Surgeons must be aware of the location of the parietomastoid suture when performing procedures on the temporal bone.

Etymology

From Latin parietalis (parietal) and mastoides (mastoid), referring to the bones joined by the suture.
