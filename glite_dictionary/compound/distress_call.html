distress call

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈstrɛs kɔl / (AmE), / dɪˈstrɛs kɔːl / (BrE)
an internationally recognized signal or message sent by a ship, aircraft, or person to request urgent assistance in an emergency
emergency request signal

Examples

The captain sent a distress call when the engine failed in the middle of the ocean.
After the plane lost power, the pilot immediately issued a distress call to air traffic control.
Rescue teams responded quickly to the distress call transmitted by the hikers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mayday - international radiotelephone distress signal
  • Synonym: SOS - Morse code distress signal used internationally
