genus Pseudococcus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˌsjuːdoʊˈkɒkəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˌsjuːdəʊˈkɒkəs / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Pseudococcidae, a group of mealybugs in entomology.
type genus of Pseudococcidae (mealybugs)

Examples

The genus Pseudococcus contains several common species of agricultural pests known as mealybugs.
Identification of the genus Pseudococcus is crucial for controlling infestations in citrus orchards.
Entomologists often study the genus Pseudococcus to understand pest behavior and crop damage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mealybug - common name for insects in family Pseudococcidae

Etymology

From Greek pseudo- (false) + kokkos (berry), referring to the false scale-like appearance.
