to make a threat

1. VERB

/ meɪk ə θrɛt / (AmE), / meɪk ə θrɛt / (BrE)
to state or imply that you will cause harm or trouble to someone, typically to intimidate or coerce
state intent to cause harm

Examples

After receiving a suspicious email, she realized someone had made a threat against her safety.
During the heated argument, Tom made a threat that shocked everyone in the room.
If you make a threat, be prepared for serious consequences from the authorities.

Synset Examples

He made a threat to slit someone's throat in anger.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to threaten - to state one's intention to harm or punish
  • Antonym: make a promise - to state that you will do something, emphasizing the contrast between positive and negative intent
