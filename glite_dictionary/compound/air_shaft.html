air shaft

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛr ˌʃæft / (AmE), / ˈeə ˌʃɑːft / (BrE)
a vertical passage or duct in a building, mine, or other structure that allows air to circulate for ventilation purposes
vertical duct for ventilation

Examples

The old apartment building uses an air shaft to bring fresh air to the inner rooms.
Miners built an air shaft to improve ventilation deep underground.
The engineers checked the air shaft monthly to make sure it was clear of debris.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ventilation shaft - an alternative term for a vertical shaft used for ventilation
