pond apple

1. NOUN

/ pɑnd ˈæpəl / (AmE), / pɒnd ˈæpəl / (BrE)
a small evergreen tree of tropical America (Annona glabra) with edible fruit, primarily used as grafting stock
tropical American evergreen tree with edible fruit

Examples

The pond apple thrives in wet, marshy soils along riverbanks and swamps.
Farmers often use pond apple trees as rootstock for grafting other fruit varieties.
The fruit of the pond apple is edible, but more often it is valued for its resilient root system.

2. NOUN

/ pɑnd ˈæpəl / (AmE), / pɒnd ˈapəl / (BrE)
an ovoid, yellow fruit with very fragrant, peach-colored flesh, related to custard apples and often found in swampy areas
a yellow, fragrant fruit related to custard apple

Examples

The swamp was filled with wild pond apples hanging from the trees.
Many animals feed on the pond apple during the tropical wet season.
She described the taste of the pond apple as sweet and aromatic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: custard apple - a sweet, tropical fruit from the family Annonaceae
