fan vaulting

1. NOUN

/ fæn ˈvɔltɪŋ / (AmE), / fan ˈvɔːltɪŋ / (BrE)
A style of vaulting in which the ribs spread out in a fan-like pattern, typically found in Gothic architecture.
vaulting with ribs fanned out

Examples

The ceiling of the chapel features exquisite fan vaulting that draws the eye upward.
Medieval architects perfected fan vaulting during the late Gothic period in England.
Tourists marvelled at the intricate fan vaulting inside King's College Chapel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vaulting - the construction of arched structures supporting a ceiling or roof
