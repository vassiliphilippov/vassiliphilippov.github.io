no pressure

1. NOUN

/ noʊ ˈprɛʃər / (AmE), / nəʊ ˈprɛʃə / (BrE)
used to indicate that there is no obligation, stress, or urgency to do something
absence of obligation or stress

Examples

Feel free to take your time—there’s no pressure to respond right away.
She appreciated that her friends put no pressure on her to attend the party.
He told his son there was no pressure to pursue a particular career path.

Synset Examples

He assured her that there was no pressure to make a quick decision.
At the game, the coach said there was no pressure to win.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: relaxed - free from tension or anxiety
  • Antonym: under pressure - being in a situation where there is stress or urgency to act
