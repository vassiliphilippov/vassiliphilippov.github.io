shelf ice

1. NOUN

/ ʃɛlf aɪs / (AmE), / ʃɛlf aɪs / (BrE)
large mass of ice attached to land that extends out over the sea, especially found along polar coasts
floating ice sheet attached to land

Examples

Researchers studied the thickness of the shelf ice along the Antarctic coast.
The collapse of shelf ice can lead to a rapid release of glaciers into the sea.
Vessels must avoid regions where shelf ice extends far into the ocean.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ice shelf - floating platform of ice formed where a glacier meets the sea
