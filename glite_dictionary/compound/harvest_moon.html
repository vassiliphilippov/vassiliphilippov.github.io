harvest moon

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑrvɪst mun / (AmE), / ˈhɑːvɪst muːn / (BrE)
the full moon that occurs nearest to the September equinox, traditionally associated with the harvest season
the full moon closest to the September equinox

Examples

The harvest moon rose brilliantly over the fields, lighting up the landscape for the farmers.
Many cultures hold festivals to celebrate the arrival of the harvest moon.
Because the harvest moon appears soon after sunset, it gives extra light during the early evening.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: full moon - the phase of the moon when it is fully illuminated as seen from Earth

Etymology

From the tradition of autumn harvests being aided by the light of the full moon; 'harvest' + 'moon'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɑrvəst mun / (AmE), / ˈhɑːvɪst muːn / (BrE)
the full moon that occurs nearest to the autumn equinox, often appearing larger and more orange than usual
full moon nearest autumn equinox

Examples

The harvest moon shone brightly over the fields, illuminating the farmers as they finished gathering their crops.
This year, the harvest moon will rise just a few days after the autumn equinox.
During the harvest moon, the sky often glows with a warm, golden light that signals the change of season.

Synset Examples

The kids marveled at the bright harvest-moon last night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: full moon - the phase of the moon when it is fully illuminated as seen from Earth

Etymology

The term arises from its timing during the traditional harvest period in late summer or early autumn.
