bunch grass

1. NOUN

/ bʌntʃ ɡræs / (AmE), / bʌntʃ ɡrɑːs / (BrE)
Any of various grasses that grow in distinct tufts or clumps, rather than forming a continuous sod or mat, commonly found in western North America.
grass growing in tufts or clumps

Examples

The prairie was dotted with bunch grass that provided cover for wildlife.
Ranchers prefer bunch grass because it resists overgrazing better than sod-forming grasses.
In the arid West, bunch grass dominates the open rangeland.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sod grass - grass species that form a dense mat or sod
