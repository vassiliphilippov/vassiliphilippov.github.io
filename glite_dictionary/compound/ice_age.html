ice age

1. NOUN

/ aɪs eɪdʒ / (AmE), / aɪs eɪdʒ / (BrE)
a period in the Earth's history when large areas were covered by glaciers and the climate was significantly colder than today
period of widespread glaciation

Examples

Scientists believe the last ice age ended about 11,700 years ago.
Many species became extinct during the ice age due to extreme cold.
The evidence of an ice age can be seen in the shape of some valleys.

Synset Examples

the most recent ice age was during the Pleistocene

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glaciation - the process or state of being covered by glaciers or ice sheets

2. NOUN

/ aɪs eɪdʒ / (AmE), / aɪs eɪdʒ / (BrE)
a period of time in Earth's history when much of the planet's surface was covered by glaciers and ice sheets, due to extended global cooling
period of global cooling with extensive glaciers

Examples

During the last ice age, much of Europe was covered by thick glaciers.
Scientists believe that an ice age can be triggered by changes in Earth's orbit.
The woolly mammoth lived during the most recent ice age.

Synset Examples

Scientists study fossils from the ice age for clues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glaciation - the process or state of being covered by glaciers
  • Antonym: interglacial - a warmer period between ice ages
