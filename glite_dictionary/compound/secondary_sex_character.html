secondary sex character

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛkəndɛri sɛks ˈkærəktɚ / (AmE), / ˌsɛkəndəri sɛks ˈkærɪktə / (BrE)
a genetically determined characteristic distinguishing the sexes that is not directly involved in reproduction, such as body hair or pitch of the voice
biological sex-based trait not essential for reproduction
medical

Examples

Facial hair is a secondary sex character that usually appears in males during puberty.
The development of a deeper voice is considered a secondary sex character in humans.
Differences in musculature between men and women are examples of secondary sex characters.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: primary sex characteristic - sexual trait directly involved in reproduction (e.g., ovaries, testes)

Etymology

From Latin 'sexus' (sex) and 'character', meaning distinguishing mark or trait.
