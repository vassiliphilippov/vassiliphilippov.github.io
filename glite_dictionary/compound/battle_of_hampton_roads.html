Battle of Hampton Roads

1. PROPN

/ ˈbæt̬.əl əv ˈhæmp.tən roʊdz / (AmE), / ˈbætl əv ˈhæmptən rəʊdz / (BrE)
a naval battle during the American Civil War in 1862, noted for the clash between the ironclad warships USS Monitor and CSS Virginia (formerly Merrimac), which ended indecisively
American Civil War naval battle, 1862

Examples

The Battle of Hampton Roads marked a turning point in naval warfare due to the use of ironclad ships.
Many historians study the Battle of Hampton Roads to understand the evolution of naval technology.
The outcome of the Battle of Hampton Roads was inconclusive, but it changed the course of naval battles forever.
