crepe myrtle

1. NOUN

/ kreɪp ˈmɜːrtəl / (AmE), / kreɪp ˈmɜːtl̩ / (BrE)
An ornamental shrub or small tree of the genus Lagerstroemia, native to Asia and widely grown for its showy clusters of flowers and attractive bark, especially in warm climates.
ornamental shrub with showy flowers

Examples

The garden was filled with crepe myrtle in full bloom, adding bursts of pink and purple to the landscape.
We planted a crepe myrtle in our front yard to provide shade and beautiful flowers in summer.
The crepe myrtle loses its leaves in autumn but displays striking bark throughout the winter.

Etymology

Named for the crinkled, crepe-like texture of the flowers and resemblance to the myrtle, though the plants are unrelated.
