off-note

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔfˌnoʊt / (AmE), / ˈɒfˌnəʊt / (BrE)
a sound or statement that is discordant, inappropriate, or mistaken, especially a musical note that is not in tune or a remark that is out of place
discordant note or inappropriate statement

Examples

Her comment was an off-note in an otherwise serious discussion.
The singer apologized for hitting an off-note during the concert.
The joke landed as an off-note among the otherwise formal speeches.

Synset Examples

Her comment was an off-note in an otherwise serious discussion.
He recognized the off-note in his friend’s singing.
The musician hit an off-note during the performance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: false note - a musical note played or sung incorrectly; also, a remark that seems inauthentic or inappropriate
