alder tree

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔldər tri / (AmE), / ˈɔːldə triː / (BrE)
A deciduous tree or shrub of the birch family, found in northern temperate regions, with toothed leaves and conelike fruit; its bark is used in tanning and dyeing, and the wood is resistant to decay.
deciduous tree or shrub with conelike fruit

Examples

The riverbank was lined with alder trees that provided shade in the summer.
Carpenters often prefer alder wood because it resists rot.
In the past, alder bark was used to create natural dyes for cloth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: birch - a tree of the same family as alder, often with white bark

Etymology

From Old English 'alor', related to German 'Erle' and Latin 'alnus'.
