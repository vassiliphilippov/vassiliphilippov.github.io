Epistle of Paul the Apostle to the Romans

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈpɪsəl əv pɔl ði əˈpɑsəl tu ðə ˈroʊmənz / (AmE), / ɪˈpɪs.əl əv pɔːl ði əˈpɒs.əl tuː ðə ˈrəʊ.mənz / (BrE)
a book of the New Testament in the Christian Bible, presenting the teachings and doctrines of Saint Paul, addressed to the Christians in Rome and written around AD 58.
New Testament letter by Saint Paul to Romans

Examples

Many theologians consider the Epistle of Paul the Apostle to the Romans a cornerstone of Christian doctrine.
During Bible study, we discussed passages from the Epistle of Paul the Apostle to the Romans.
The Epistle of Paul the Apostle to the Romans was likely written in AD 58 while Paul was in Corinth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Romans - alternative name for the Epistle of Paul to the Romans
  • Degree Higher: New Testament - second part of the Christian Bible

Etymology

Epistle from Latin 'epistola', from Greek 'epistolē' (letter), referring to the apostle Paul's letter to the Romans.
