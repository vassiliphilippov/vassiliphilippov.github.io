line of defence

1. NOUN

/ laɪn əv dɪˈfɛns / (AmE), / laɪn əv dɪˈfɛns / (BrE)
a barrier or position used to protect against attack or prevent enemy advance, especially in a military context
protective military barrier or position

Examples

The soldiers held their line of defence against the advancing enemy all night.
Building a strong line of defence was essential to protect the city from invasion.
The ancient wall served as the final line of defence for the besieged fortress.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: defensive line - a position or barrier for military defense

2. NOUN

/ laɪn əv dɪˈfɛns / (AmE), / laɪn əv dɪˈfɛns / (BrE)
an organization or system responsible for protecting against attack, danger, or harm
system or group defending against threats

Examples

The army serves as the first line of defence during conflicts.
Proper sanitation is an important line of defence against disease.
Teachers are often a crucial line of defence for vulnerable children.

Synset Examples

police are the major line of defense against crime

Related Terms

  • Antonym: line of attack - the direction or route taken to attack a target
  • Synonym: barrier - something that blocks or defends against movement or attack
