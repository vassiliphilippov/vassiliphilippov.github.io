devil's turnip

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛvəlz ˈtɜrnɪp / (AmE), / ˈdɛvəlz ˈtɜːnɪp / (BrE)
A white-flowered, climbing plant (Bryonia alba) with thick roots and small black berries, native from Europe to Iran.
white-flowered climbing plant, Bryonia alba

Examples

The hedgerow was tangled with devil's turnip, its white flowers in full bloom.
Gardeners warned that devil's turnip berries are poisonous if ingested.
She studied the thick, gnarled root of the devil's turnip before painting it.
