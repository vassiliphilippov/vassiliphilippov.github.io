Mexican pocket mouse

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛksɪkən ˈpɑkɪt maʊs / (AmE), / ˈmɛksɪkən ˈpɒkɪt maʊs / (BrE)
a small rodent native to Mexico, known for its fur-lined cheek pouches used to carry food
small rodent native to Mexico

Examples

The Mexican pocket mouse is adapted to dry, sandy habitats in northern Mexico.
Scientists observed a Mexican pocket mouse carrying seeds in its cheek pouches.
Habitat loss has contributed to the decline of the Mexican pocket mouse population.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pocket mouse - small rodent with fur-lined cheek pouches
