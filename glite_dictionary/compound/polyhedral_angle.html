polyhedral angle

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑliˈhɪdrəl ˈæŋɡəl / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈhiːdrəl ˈæŋɡəl / (BrE)
the space formed at the intersection of three or more planes that meet at a single point (vertex) in a solid figure
angle formed by intersecting planes at a vertex

Examples

The polyhedral angle formed at the apex of a pyramid consists of several triangular faces meeting at a point.
In geometry class, we studied how to calculate the volume related to a polyhedral angle.
A cube has a right polyhedral angle at each corner, where three faces come together.

Etymology

From Greek 'poly-' (many) + 'hedra' (base, face), referring to many faced figures.
