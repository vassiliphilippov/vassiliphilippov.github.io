myrtle beech

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɜrtəl biːtʃ / (AmE), / ˈmɜːtəl biːtʃ / (BrE)
a large evergreen tree (Nothofagus cunninghamii) native to Tasmania, with glossy dark leaves and timber valued for furniture
Tasmanian evergreen tree; Nothofagus cunninghamii

Examples

The ancient myrtle beech forests in Tasmania provide a unique habitat for wildlife.
Furniture made from myrtle beech is prized for its durability and attractive grain.
Scientists are studying the growth rings of myrtle beech to understand past climate variations.
