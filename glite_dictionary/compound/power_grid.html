power grid

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaʊər ɡrɪd / (AmE), / ˈpaʊə ɡrɪd / (BrE)
A network of high-voltage cables and associated infrastructure used to deliver electricity across a region.
network for electrical power distribution

Examples

After the storm, much of the city was left without power when the power grid failed.
Many countries are investing in smart technology to modernize their power grids.
A stable power grid is essential for industrial growth and daily life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: electrical grid - system of interconnected power lines for distributing electricity
