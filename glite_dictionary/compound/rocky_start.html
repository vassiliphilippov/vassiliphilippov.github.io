rocky start

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɑki stɑrt / (AmE), / ˈrɒki stɑːt / (BrE)
a difficult or troubled beginning to an activity, relationship, or period
difficult or troubled beginning

Examples

After a rocky start, the new restaurant quickly built a loyal customer base.
Their partnership had a rocky start but grew stronger with time.
Although the project had a rocky start, it eventually exceeded all expectations.

Synset Examples

He had a rocky start but eventually succeeded.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: smooth start - an easy or uncomplicated beginning
