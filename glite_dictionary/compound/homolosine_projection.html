homolosine projection

1. NOUN

/ hoʊˈmoʊləsaɪn prəˈdʒɛkʃən / (AmE), / hɒˈmɒləsiːn prəˈdʒɛkʃən / (BrE)
a map projection that represents areas of the globe with equal area, minimizing distortion of the continents by distorting the oceans
equal-area map projection minimizing continent distortion

Examples

The homolosine projection is often used in world maps to balance area distortions.
Cartographers select the homolosine projection when they want to emphasize the true size of continents.
Unlike other projections, the homolosine projection preserves equal area while distorting oceans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: map projection - a systematic way of representing the curved surface of the earth on a flat map

Etymology

From Greek 'homolos' (equal) + '-sine' (from 'sinus', curve), indicating its method combining projections.
