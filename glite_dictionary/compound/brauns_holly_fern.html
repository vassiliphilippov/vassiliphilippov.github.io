Braun's holly fern

1. NOUN

/ brɑːnz ˈhɑːli fɜrn / (AmE), / brɔːnz ˈhɒli fɜːn / (BrE)
A North American fern with leathery, evergreen fronds covered in pale brown chaffy scales.
North American fern with leathery fronds

Examples

The garden features several Braun's holly ferns beneath the maple trees.
Botanists documented the spread of Braun's holly fern in the mountainous regions of North Carolina.
The leathery texture of Braun's holly fern makes it easy to identify among other native ferns.

Etymology

Named after American botanist Alexander Braun; 'holly fern' refers to the holly-like texture of its fronds.
