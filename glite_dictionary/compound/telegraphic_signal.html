telegraphic signal

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɛləˈɡræfɪk ˈsɪɡnəl / (AmE), / ˌtɛlɪˈɡræfɪk ˈsɪɡnəl / (BrE)
a signal transmitted via a telegraph system, often by means of Morse code or other coded impulses
a signal sent by telegraph

Examples

The operator received a telegraphic signal confirming the completion of the track.
Early communication devices relied on the telegraphic signal to convey urgent news.
A series of short and long sounds made up the telegraphic signal sent across the wire.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: telegraph - a system for transmitting messages over long distances using coded signals

Etymology

From 'telegraph,' from Greek 'tele-' (far) + 'graphein' (to write), and 'signal,' from Latin 'signum' (mark, sign).
