ductus deferens

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʌk.təs ˈdɛf.əˌrɛnz / (AmE), / ˈdʌk.təs ˈdɛf.ər.enz / (BrE)
the tube that carries sperm from the epididymis to the ejaculatory duct in the male reproductive system
male sperm-carrying tube
medical

Examples

The ductus deferens is cut and sealed during a vasectomy procedure.
Damage to the ductus deferens can lead to male infertility.
Anatomy textbooks provide detailed diagrams of the ductus deferens and its function.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vas deferens - another name for the ductus deferens

Etymology

From Latin ductus (duct) and deferens (carrying down).
