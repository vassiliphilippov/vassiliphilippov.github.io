USS Merrimack

1. PROPN

/ ˌjuː ˌɛs ˈmɛrɪmæk / (AmE), / ˌjuː ˌɛs ˈmɛrɪmæk / (BrE)
an ironclad warship originally built for the U.S. Navy and later converted by the Confederate forces during the American Civil War to challenge the Union blockade
historic Civil War ironclad warship

Examples

The USS Merrimack played a crucial role in naval warfare during the Civil War.
Historians continue to debate the impact of the USS Merrimack on military strategy.
The battle between the USS Merrimack and the USS Monitor marked a turning point in naval history.

Etymology

Named after the Merrimack River in New Hampshire and Massachusetts.
