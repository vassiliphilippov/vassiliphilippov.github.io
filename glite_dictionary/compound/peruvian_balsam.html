Peruvian balsam

1. NOUN

/ pəˈruːviən ˈbælsəm / (AmE), / pəˈruːviən ˈbɑːlsəm / (BrE)
a tree native to South and Central America that produces an aromatic resin used in perfumes and medicines
tree yielding aromatic resin

Examples

The resin extracted from Peruvian balsam is widely used in traditional medicine.
Many perfumes contain an essence of Peruvian balsam because of its pleasant fragrance.
Harvesting Peruvian balsam requires careful tapping of the tree’s bark.
