gravitational collapse

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡrævɪˈteɪʃənəl kəˈlæps / (AmE), / ˌɡravɪˈteɪʃənəl kəˈlaps / (BrE)
the process in which a star or other celestial body collapses under its own gravity, leading to a smaller and denser object
collapse of a star due to gravity

Examples

The black hole formed as a result of gravitational collapse of a massive star.
Astronomers study gravitational collapse to understand the birth of neutron stars.
A supernova can be triggered by the sudden gravitational collapse of a dying star.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: supernova - explosive death of a star after gravitational collapse
  • Antonym: expansion - the process of becoming larger or more extensive

Etymology

From 'gravitational', relating to gravity (from Latin 'gravitas', meaning 'weight'), and 'collapse' (from Latin 'collapsus', meaning 'fallen together').
