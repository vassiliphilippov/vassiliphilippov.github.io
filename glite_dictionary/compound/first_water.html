first water

1. NOUN

/ ˌfɝst ˈwɔtɚ / (AmE), / ˌfɜːst ˈwɔːtə / (BrE)
the highest quality or grade, especially of gemstones such as diamonds; sometimes extended to mean anything of the finest kind
gems of the highest quality

Examples

The jeweler assured us that the diamond was of first water, flawless and brilliantly clear.
She was renowned in society as a beauty of first water.
Collectors are always searching for gemstones of first water to add to their collections.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: second water - gems of slightly lower quality than first water

Etymology

Originally used in the 18th century to describe diamonds, likening their clarity to pure water.
