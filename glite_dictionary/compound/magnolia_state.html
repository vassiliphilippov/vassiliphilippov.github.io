Magnolia State

1. PROPN

/ mæɡˈnoʊljə steɪt / (AmE), / maɡˈnəʊliə steɪt / (BrE)
A nickname for the U.S. state of Mississippi, referring to the magnolia trees commonly found there.
nickname for Mississippi (U.S. state)

Examples

He was born and raised in the Magnolia State before moving to New York.
The Magnolia State is famous for its rich history and southern hospitality.
Many tourists visit the Magnolia State to see its beautiful magnolia trees in bloom.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mississippi - a state in the southern United States

Etymology

Refers to the abundant magnolia trees in Mississippi.
