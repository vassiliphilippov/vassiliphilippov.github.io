genus Sphaerocarpus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs sfɪr.oʊˈkɑːr.pəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs sfɪərəˈkɑːpəs / (BrE)
a genus of liverworts in the family Sphaerocarpaceae, characterized by a small, many-lobed, usually circular thallus
genus of small, orbicular liverworts

Examples

The genus Sphaerocarpus is distinguished by its orbicular, lobed thallus.
Researchers discovered several new species within the genus Sphaerocarpus in the wetland.
The genus Sphaerocarpus is commonly found in moist, shaded environments.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Sphaerocarpaceae - family including Sphaerocarpus and related genera

Etymology

From Greek 'sphaira' (sphere) and 'karpos' (fruit), referring to the rounded fruiting bodies.
