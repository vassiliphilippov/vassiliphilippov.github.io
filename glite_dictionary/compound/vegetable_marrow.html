vegetable marrow

1. NOUN

/ ˈvedʒ.tʃə.bəl ˈmɛr.oʊ / (AmE), / ˈvɛdʒ.tɪ.bəl ˈmær.əʊ / (BrE)
a large, elongated variety of summer squash with a creamy to deep green skin, often eaten as a vegetable
large squash with green skin
british

Examples

He sliced the vegetable marrow and added it to the stew for extra flavor.
In her garden, she grew a huge vegetable marrow that won first prize at the fair.
The recipe calls for baked vegetable marrow stuffed with a spicy rice mixture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zucchini - a type of summer squash similar but usually smaller and more tender

2. NOUN

/ ˈvɛdʒtəbl ˈmæroʊ / (AmE), / ˈvɛdʒtəbəl ˈmærəʊ / (BrE)
a type of summer squash with an elongated shape, smooth dark green skin, and whitish flesh, cultivated as a vegetable
an elongated summer squash eaten as a vegetable

Examples

In her garden, she grew several vegetable marrows among the tomatoes and beans.
The recipe called for slices of vegetable marrow stuffed with herbs and rice.
At the market, he bought a large vegetable marrow for tonight’s dinner.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zucchini - a dark green summer squash, especially in American English

Etymology

From 'marrow' for the squash, with 'vegetable' distinguishing it from 'bone marrow'.
