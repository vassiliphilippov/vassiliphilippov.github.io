tuberous plant

1. NOUN

/ ˈtuːbərəs plænt / (AmE), / ˈtjuːbərəs plɑːnt / (BrE)
A plant that grows from an underground storage organ known as a tuber, such as a potato or dahlia.
plant that grows from a tuber

Examples

The tuberous plant produces colorful flowers in the summer months.
Gardeners often grow tuberous plants like begonias for their beauty and resilience.
The potato is perhaps the most widely cultivated tuberous plant in the world.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: root vegetable - a vegetable that grows underground, such as a carrot or beet

Etymology

From Latin 'tuber' (lump, swelling), referring to the underground stem.
