queen mother

1. NOUN

/ ˌkwin ˈmʌðər / (AmE), / ˌkwiːn ˈmʌðə / (BrE)
the mother of the reigning monarch, especially a former queen consort who retains the title after her child ascends the throne
mother of the reigning monarch

Examples

The queen mother attended the ceremony alongside her daughter, the reigning queen.
After the king's death, she became the queen mother and played an influential role in the royal family.
Britain's most famous queen mother was the mother of Queen Elizabeth II.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: queen dowager - a widow of a king, formerly queen consort
