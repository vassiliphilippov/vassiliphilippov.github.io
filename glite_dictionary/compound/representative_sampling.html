representative sampling

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɛprɪˈzɛntətɪv ˈsæmplɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌrɛprɪˈzɛntətɪv ˈsɑːmplɪŋ / (BrE)
a statistical method in which a population is divided into subgroups and random samples are taken from each subgroup to accurately reflect the population as a whole
statistical sampling from population subgroups

Examples

Researchers used representative sampling to ensure their study accurately reflected the diverse community.
In order to avoid bias, the survey employed representative sampling from each age group.
Proper representative sampling increases the reliability and validity of experimental results.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: random sampling - selecting a sample from a population entirely by chance, without subgrouping
