a year and a day

1. NOUN

/ ə jɪr ənd ə deɪ / (AmE), / ə jɪə ənd ə deɪ / (BrE)
a period of exactly one year plus one day, especially used in legal or traditional contexts
one year plus one day (traditional phrase)

Examples

The contract stated that the agreement would last for a year and a day.
He waited a year and a day before finally receiving a reply.
In old English law, certain punishments lasted for a year and a day.

Synset Examples

The contract lasts for a year and a day.
It felt like a year and a day waiting for her reply.
He promised to stay with her for a year and a day.

Etymology

From the use of a fixed period of one year and one day in medieval English law and folklore.
