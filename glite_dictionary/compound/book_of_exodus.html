Book of Exodus

1. PROPN

/ ˈbʊk əv ˈɛksədəs / (AmE), / ˈbʊk əv ˈɛksədəs / (BrE)
The second book of the Old Testament and Hebrew Bible, recounting the Israelites' escape from slavery in Egypt under Moses and the receiving of the Ten Commandments.
second book of the Old Testament; Moses leads Exodus

Examples

Many scholars study the Book of Exodus to understand the origins of Jewish law and tradition.
The story of Moses parting the Red Sea is found in the Book of Exodus.
In Sunday school, we read passages from the Book of Exodus about the Ten Commandments.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Genesis - the first book of the Old Testament; creation and early history of humanity
  • Degree Higher: Leviticus - the third book of the Old Testament; laws for religious rituals and morality

Etymology

From Greek 'Exodos' meaning 'a going out', via Latin 'Exodus'; named for the Israelites' departure from Egypt.
