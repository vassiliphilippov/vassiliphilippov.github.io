Gulf of Saint Lawrence

1. PROPN

/ ɡʌlf əv seɪnt ˈlɔːrəns / (AmE), / ɡʌlf əv seɪnt ˈlɒrəns / (BrE)
a large inlet of the Atlantic Ocean off the southeastern coast of Canada, bordered by Quebec, Newfoundland, New Brunswick, Prince Edward Island, and Nova Scotia
large inlet of the Atlantic Ocean in eastern Canada

Examples

The Gulf of Saint Lawrence is home to a rich variety of marine life and supports many fishing communities.
The river empties into the Gulf of Saint Lawrence, creating a vital shipping route for eastern Canada.
During the summer, tourists enjoy sailing and whale watching in the Gulf of Saint Lawrence.
