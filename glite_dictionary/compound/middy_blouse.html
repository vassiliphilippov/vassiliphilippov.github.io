middy blouse

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪdi blaʊs / (AmE), / ˈmɪdi blaʊs / (BrE)
A woman's or girl's blouse with a sailor-style collar, resembling a sailor's uniform top.
blouse with a sailor collar

Examples

She wore a crisp white middy blouse and navy skirt to the party.
The middy blouse was a popular fashion among schoolgirls in the early 20th century.
A red scarf tied at the neck complemented her blue middy blouse.

Etymology

From 'middy', a short form of 'midshipman', referencing the naval style of the blouse.
