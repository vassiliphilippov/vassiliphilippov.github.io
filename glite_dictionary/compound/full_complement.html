full complement

1. NOUN

/ fʊl ˈkɑmpləmənt / (AmE), / fʊl ˈkɒmplɪmənt / (BrE)
the complete number or amount needed to make up a group, team, or force
complete required number or amount

Examples

We finally have a full complement of teachers for the new school year.
The orchestra could not perform until a full complement of musicians was assembled.
Once the spaceship had a full complement of crew members, it was ready for launch.

Synset Examples

a full complement of workers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quota - fixed minimum or maximum number, especially for people or things
  • Antonym: deficit - the lack of an amount needed to be complete
