cerebral artery

1. NOUN

/ səˈribɹəl ˈɑɹ.təɹ.i / (AmE), / səˈriː.brəl ˈɑː.tər.i / (BrE)
any of the arteries that supply blood to the cerebral cortex and other parts of the brain
artery supplying blood to the brain
medical

Examples

A blockage in a cerebral artery can lead to a stroke.
Doctors monitor blood flow through the cerebral arteries during brain surgery.
Research focuses on protecting the cerebral artery from the risk of aneurysm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: artery - a blood vessel that carries blood away from the heart

Etymology

From Latin 'cerebrum' (brain) and 'arteria' (artery).
