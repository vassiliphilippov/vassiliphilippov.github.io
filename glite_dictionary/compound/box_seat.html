box seat

1. NOUN

/ bɑks siːt / (AmE), / bɒks siːt / (BrE)
the driver's seat on a horse-drawn coach or carriage
driver's seat on a coach

Examples

The coachman climbed up to the box seat and took the reins.
A lantern hung beside the box seat to guide the driver through the night.
She waved at her father, who was waiting on the box seat of the carriage.

Synset Examples

an armed guard sat in the box with the driver

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coach box - the driver's seat on a coach or carriage

2. NOUN

/ bɑks sit / (AmE), / bɒks siːt / (BrE)
a seat situated in a separated, often elevated, section (a box) of a theater or stadium, usually providing a good view and extra comfort or privacy
special seat in theater or stadium

Examples

We were lucky to get a box seat for the opening night performance.
The royal family watched the game from a private box seat above the field.
From his box seat, he had an excellent view of the entire stage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: balcony - a platform projecting from the wall of a theater, with seats above the main floor
  • Synonym: stalls - main floor seats in a theater, especially in British English

3. NOUN

/ bɑks sit / (AmE), / bɒks siːt / (BrE)
a premium seat in a separate, often elevated, area in a theater, stadium, or venue, typically offering better sightlines and privacy
premium, separate seating in a theater or stadium

Examples

She felt special sitting in the box seat during the opera performance.
From our box seat, we had a perfect view of the stage and the orchestra.
He saved up all year to buy box seat tickets for the championship game.

Synset Examples

She had a box seat at the concert last night.
He always gets a box seat for the football matches.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stall - the main floor seating area in a theater
