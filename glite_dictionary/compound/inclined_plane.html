inclined plane

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈklaɪnd pleɪn / (AmE), / ɪnˈklaɪnd pleɪn / (BrE)
a sloping flat surface used to raise or lower objects by rolling or sliding them, forming one of the basic simple machines
a sloping surface used to elevate objects

Examples

A ramp is a common example of an inclined plane used in buildings for wheelchair access.
By pushing the crate up the inclined plane, less force is needed than lifting it vertically.
In physics class, we learned how an inclined plane can reduce the effort required to move heavy objects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wedge - a simple machine; a triangular tool used to separate or hold objects
  • Synonym: lever - a simple machine consisting of a rigid bar pivoted on a fulcrum

Etymology

From 'inclined' (having a slope) and 'plane' (flat surface), used in physics since the 17th century.
