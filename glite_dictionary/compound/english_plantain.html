English plantain

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ ˈplæn.tɪn / (AmE), / ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ ˈplæn.tɪn / (BrE)
a Eurasian plantain (Plantago lanceolata) with long, narrow, ribbed leaves, widely found in temperate regions and naturalized elsewhere
a Eurasian plantain with narrow ribbed leaves

Examples

The English plantain is often found growing in meadows and along roadsides across Europe.
She picked a few leaves of English plantain to use as a traditional herbal remedy.
Gardeners sometimes remove English plantain because it spreads quickly in lawns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plantain - a small herbaceous plant, especially of the genus Plantago
