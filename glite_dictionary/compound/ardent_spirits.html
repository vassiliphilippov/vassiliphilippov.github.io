ardent spirits

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑrdənt ˈspɪɹɪts / (AmE), / ˈɑːd(ə)nt ˈspɪɹɪts / (BrE)
Strong distilled alcoholic drinks such as brandy, whiskey, or rum.
strong distilled alcoholic drinks

Examples

The tavern served only ardent spirits to its patrons after dark.
Laws were enacted to limit the consumption of ardent spirits in colonial times.
Some believed that ardent spirits led to social problems in the 18th century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: liquor - strong alcoholic drink
  • Synonym: brandy - strong alcoholic spirit distilled from wine or fermented fruit juice

Etymology

From Latin 'ardens' (burning), referring to the burning quality of strong alcohol.
