family Gerrididae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæmli dʒɛˈrɪdəˌdi / (AmE), / ˈfamli dʒɛˈrɪdɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of insects in the order Hemiptera, commonly known as water striders, characterized by their ability to walk on the surface of water.
family of water strider insects

Examples

The family Gerrididae includes a wide variety of species known as water striders.
Scientists study the family Gerrididae to understand how insects adapt to life on the water surface.
Many members of the family Gerrididae exhibit remarkable agility and speed on ponds and streams.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: water strider - insect in the family Gerrididae that walks on water

Etymology

From New Latin *Gerrididae*, based on the type genus *Gerris* + -idae (a family suffix in zoological nomenclature).
