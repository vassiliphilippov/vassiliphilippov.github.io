sailing master

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪlɪŋ ˌmæstər / (AmE), / ˈseɪlɪŋ ˌmɑːstə / (BrE)
the officer on a ship responsible for navigation, especially in historical naval contexts
officer in charge of navigation on a ship

Examples

In the 18th century, the sailing master played a crucial role in guiding the ship safely across the sea.
The sailing master was responsible for reading the charts and setting the ship's course.
During storms, the captain often relied heavily on the sailing master's expertise in navigation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: navigator - person responsible for navigation on a vessel or aircraft
