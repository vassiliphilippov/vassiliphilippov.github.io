audio amplifier

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔdioʊ æmˌplɪˌfaɪər / (AmE), / ˈɔːdiəʊ æmˌplɪfaɪə / (BrE)
An electronic device that increases the strength of audio signals for reproduction through loudspeakers.
device that increases audio signal strength

Examples

He connected the new audio amplifier to his speakers for better sound quality.
This model of audio amplifier is designed for use in home theater systems.
Before buying an audio amplifier, check its compatibility with your existing equipment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amplifier - an electronic device for increasing the power of signals

Etymology

From 'audio', from Latin 'audire' (to hear), and 'amplifier', from Latin 'amplus' (large, ample) + '-fier' (maker).
