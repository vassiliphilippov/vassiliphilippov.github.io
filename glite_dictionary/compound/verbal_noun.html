verbal noun

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɜrbəl naʊn / (AmE), / ˈvɜːbəl naʊn / (BrE)
a noun that is derived from a verb, often referring to the action or process associated with the verb
noun derived from a verb

Examples

The word 'singing' can function as a verbal noun in the sentence 'His singing was beautiful.'
In grammatical analysis, a verbal noun expresses an action or state but behaves as a noun.
Some verbal nouns are easily confused with participles in English.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gerund - a noun formed from a verb by adding -ing in English

Etymology

Composed of 'verbal' (from Latin 'verbalis') + 'noun'.
