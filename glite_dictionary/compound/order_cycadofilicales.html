order Cycadofilicales

1. NOUN

/ ˌɔrdər saɪˌkædəˌfɪlɪˈkeɪlz / (AmE), / ˌɔːdə saɪˌkædəfɪlɪˈkeɪliːz / (BrE)
an extinct order of gymnospermous trees or climbing plants from the Devonian period, also known as seed ferns
extinct order of seed ferns (Devonian plants)

Examples

Fossil specimens of order Cycadofilicales have provided insight into the evolution of early seed plants.
Many paleobotanists study order Cycadofilicales to understand plant life during the Devonian period.
The discovery of a new order Cycadofilicales fossil excited the scientific community.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seed fern - an extinct group of gymnospermous plants resembling both ferns and seed plants

Etymology

From Greek 'cycas' (a kind of palm) + 'phyllon' (leaf) + '-ales' (taxonomic suffix).
