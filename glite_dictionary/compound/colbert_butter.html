Colbert butter

1. NOUN

/ kɔlˈbɛrt ˈbʌtər / (AmE), / kɒlˈbɛː ˈbʌtə / (BrE)
Butter creamed with parsley, tarragon, and beef extract, used as a flavorful garnish or sauce in French cuisine.
flavored butter with herbs and beef extract

Examples

The steak was served with a generous dollop of Colbert butter on top.
To enhance the flavor, the chef added a slice of Colbert butter to the grilled chicken.
She prepared Colbert butter ahead of the dinner party to complement the roast beef.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compound butter - butter mixed with additional flavorings, such as herbs or spices

Etymology

Named after the French statesman Jean-Baptiste Colbert (1619–1683), after whom the sauce is also named.
