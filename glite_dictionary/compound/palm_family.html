palm family

1. NOUN

/ pɑːm ˈfæməli / (AmE), / pɑːm ˈfæmɪli / (BrE)
a family of chiefly tropical trees, shrubs, and vines, usually with tall columnar trunks and crowns of large leaves; the botanical family Arecaceae, coextensive with the order Palmales.
the family of palm trees and related plants

Examples

The palm family includes many well-known plants, such as coconuts and date palms.
Botanists study the characteristics that distinguish the palm family from other plant families.
Many species in the palm family are vital to tropical ecosystems and local economies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Arecaceae - the scientific name for the palm family
