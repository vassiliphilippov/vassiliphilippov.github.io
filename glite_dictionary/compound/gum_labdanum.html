gum labdanum

1. NOUN

/ ɡʌm ˈlæbdənəm / (AmE), / ɡʌm ˈlæbdənəm / (BrE)
a dark brown or greenish aromatic resin obtained from certain rockrose shrubs, used as a fixative in perfumes
aromatic resin used in perfumes

Examples

Many perfumers rely on gum labdanum to give their fragrances a warm, resinous base note.
The harvest of gum labdanum from rockrose bushes was a painstaking seasonal task in ancient times.
Historically, gum labdanum was also valued for its supposed medicinal properties and use in incense.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: labdanum - aromatic resin from rockroses used in perfumery

Etymology

From gum (resin) + labdanum, from Greek labdanon, plant resin.
