buck-passing

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌk ˌpæsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbʌk ˌpɑːsɪŋ / (BrE)
The act of avoiding responsibility by blaming others or shifting responsibility to someone else.
avoidance of responsibility by shifting blame

Examples

The team's buck-passing made it hard to identify who was truly responsible for the mistake.
Political leaders are often criticized for their buck-passing during crises.
Her constant buck-passing damaged trust within the department.

Synset Examples

The manager's buck-passing frustrated the entire team.
His buck-passing only delays the resolution of the issue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: passing the buck - the act of shifting responsibility to someone else
  • Antonym: accountability - the quality of being accountable or responsible

Etymology

From 'pass the buck', originally American English gambling slang referring to passing responsibility for dealing cards; 'buck' meaning a marker.
