honorary society

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑː.nəˌrɛr.i səˈsaɪ.ə.t̬i / (AmE), / ˈɒn.ər.əri səˈsaɪ.ə.ti / (BrE)
an organization that recognizes and promotes achievement in a particular field, such as art, science, or literature, by granting membership based on merit or accomplishment
organization recognizing achievement in a field

Examples

She was inducted into an honorary society for her contributions to environmental science.
Membership in an honorary society is often seen as a mark of distinction among scholars.
The university hosts several honorary societies celebrating academic excellence across different disciplines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: academy - an organization or institution established for the advancement of art, science, or literature

Etymology

From 'honorary' (from Latin honorarius, relating to honor) + 'society' (from Latin societas).
