real power

1. NOUN

/ ˈriːəl ˈpaʊɚ / (AmE), / ˈrɪəl ˈpaʊə / (BrE)
the amount of power in an alternating current (AC) circuit that results in a net transfer of energy in one direction, averaged over a complete cycle; also called active power.
actual usable power in an AC circuit

Examples

The electrician measured the real power delivered to the heater using a wattmeter.
Because of the poor power factor, the real power consumed was much less than the apparent power.
Upgrading the equipment improved the real power efficiency of the entire system.

Synset Examples

For two systems transmitting the same amount of active power, the system with the lower power factor will have higher circulating currents due to energy that returns to the source from energy storage in the load.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: apparent power - the product of root-mean-square voltage and current in an AC circuit, not all of which is transferred as work; measured in volt-amperes
  • Degree Lower: reactive power - the portion of power in an AC circuit that alternates between the source and the load, not resulting in net energy transfer; measured in VAR
