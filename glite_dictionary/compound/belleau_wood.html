Belleau Wood

1. PROPN

/ bəˈloʊ wʊd / (AmE), / bəˈləʊ wʊd / (BrE)
the site of a major World War I battle in northwestern France in 1918, where Allied forces defeated the Germans
World War I battle site in France, 1918

Examples

The battle of Belleau Wood marked a turning point for American forces in World War I.
Many soldiers displayed extraordinary bravery at Belleau Wood.
Memorials at Belleau Wood continue to honor those who fought there.

Etymology

Named after the small forest near Belleau, France, the site of the 1918 battle.
