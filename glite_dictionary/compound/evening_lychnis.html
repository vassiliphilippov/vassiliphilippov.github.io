evening lychnis

1. NOUN

/ ˈivnɪŋ ˈlɪknɪs / (AmE), / ˈiːvnɪŋ ˈlɪknɪs / (BrE)
a bluish-green herb with sticky stems and clusters of large, evening-opening white flowers, sometimes classified in the genus Lychnis
a herb with evening-opening white flowers

Examples

The garden was filled with evening lychnis, their white flowers glowing in the dusk.
She planted evening lychnis to add nighttime beauty to her flower beds.
Pollinators are often attracted to the fragrant blooms of evening lychnis after sunset.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Lychnis - genus of flowering plants in the pink family

Etymology

Lychnis from Greek 'lýchnos' meaning 'lamp', referencing the brightness of the flowers.
