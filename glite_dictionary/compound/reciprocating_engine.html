reciprocating engine

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈsɪprəˌkeɪtɪŋ ˈɛn.dʒɪn / (AmE), / rɪˈsɪprəkeɪtɪŋ ˈen.dʒɪn / (BrE)
an internal-combustion engine in which pistons move up and down in cylinders to turn a crankshaft
engine with pistons moving up and down

Examples

The car was powered by a reciprocating engine that drove the rear wheels.
Many early airplanes relied on reciprocating engines before the advent of jet propulsion.
Regular maintenance is crucial for any reciprocating engine to ensure longevity and performance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: internal combustion engine - engine powered by the combustion of fuel within cylinders

Etymology

From the verb 'reciprocate' (to move back and forth) and 'engine'.
