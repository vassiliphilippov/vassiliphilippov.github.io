New Guinea

1. PROPN

/ nu ˈɡɪni / (AmE), / njuː ˈɡɪni / (BrE)
a large island in the southwest Pacific Ocean, north of Australia, divided between Indonesia in the west and Papua New Guinea in the east
large Pacific island north of Australia

Examples

Many unique animal species can be found in New Guinea due to its diverse habitats.
The eastern half of New Guinea forms the independent nation of Papua New Guinea.
Researchers traveled to New Guinea to study traditional agricultural practices.

Etymology

Named by Spanish explorer Yñigo Ortiz de Retez, who noted a resemblance between the island's inhabitants and those of the Guinea region in Africa.
