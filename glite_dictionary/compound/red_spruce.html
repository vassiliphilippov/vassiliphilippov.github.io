red spruce

1. NOUN

/ rɛd spruːs / (AmE), / rɛd spruːs / (BrE)
A medium-sized spruce tree native to eastern North America, valued for its timber and use as pulpwood.
North American spruce tree; timber source

Examples

The forests of Maine are rich in red spruce, which is harvested for lumber and paper production.
Many musical instrument soundboards are crafted from high-quality red spruce.
The red spruce population has declined in some areas due to environmental changes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: black spruce - A related spruce species found in northern North America
