bas relief

1. NOUN

/ bɑ rəˈlif / (AmE), / bɑː rəˈliːf / (BrE)
a type of sculpture in which the figures project only slightly from a flat background, with no parts being fully undercut
shallow sculptural relief

Examples

The ancient temple walls were decorated with intricate bas relief carvings depicting mythical creatures.
She admired the subtle shadows that the bas relief sculptures cast on the gallery’s walls.
The museum’s newest exhibit features a stunning bas relief panel from the Renaissance period.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: high relief - a sculptural relief in which forms project substantially from the background

Etymology

From French bas-relief, literally 'low relief', from Italian basso rilievo.
