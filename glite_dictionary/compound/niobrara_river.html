Niobrara River

1. PROPN

/ ˌnaɪ.əˈbrær.ə ˈrɪv.ɚ / (AmE), / ˌnaɪ.əˈbrær.ə ˈrɪv.ər / (BrE)
A river in the northern United States that is a tributary of the Missouri River, flowing through Wyoming and Nebraska.
a river in the northern United States

Examples

The Niobrara River is famous for its scenic bluffs and waterfalls.
Many people canoe along the Niobrara River during the summer months.
Wildlife such as bison and deer can often be spotted near the Niobrara River.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Missouri River - major river in the central United States

Etymology

From the Niobrara, a name of Native American origin, possibly Omaha or Ponca.
