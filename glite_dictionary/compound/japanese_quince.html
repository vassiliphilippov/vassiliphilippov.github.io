Japanese quince

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˈpænɪs kwɪns / (AmE), / dʒəˈpænɪs kwɪns / (BrE)
a deciduous thorny shrub native to China, with red or white flowers, cultivated as an ornamental plant
ornamental thorny shrub with red or white flowers

Examples

The Japanese quince blooms early in spring with vibrant red flowers.
Many gardeners plant Japanese quince for its attractive blossoms and thorny branches.
The fruit of the Japanese quince is sometimes used to make jelly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quince - a fruit-bearing shrub or tree, Cydonia oblonga
