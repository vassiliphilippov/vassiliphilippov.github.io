gastrocnemius muscle

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡæstroʊkˈniːmiəs ˈmʌsl̩ / (AmE), / ˌɡæstrəʊkˈniːmɪəs ˈmʌsl / (BrE)
the large muscle at the back of the lower leg forming the main part of the calf, responsible for flexing the foot downward
large calf muscle in the lower leg
medical

Examples

After the marathon, his gastrocnemius muscle was sore from all the running.
The physical therapist demonstrated a stretch for the gastrocnemius muscle to improve flexibility.
A torn gastrocnemius muscle can significantly limit an athlete’s ability to run or jump.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calf muscle - muscle at the back of the lower leg

Etymology

From Greek 'gaster' (stomach) and 'knēmē' (leg, shin), referring to the muscle's belly-like shape.
