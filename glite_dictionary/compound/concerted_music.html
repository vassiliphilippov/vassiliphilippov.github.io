concerted music

1. NOUN

/ kənˈsɝːtɪd ˈmjuːzɪk / (AmE), / kənˈsɜːtɪd ˈmjuːzɪk / (BrE)
Music arranged in parts for several voices or instruments, often with each part having an independent musical line.
music for multiple voices or instruments

Examples

The choir performed a beautiful piece of concerted music at the festival.
Baroque composers are known for their use of concerted music involving both vocalists and instrumentalists.
Learning to sing concerted music helps musicians develop their ability to harmonize in ensembles.

Etymology

From concerted, meaning 'organized or arranged together', from Italian 'concertato' in musical contexts.
