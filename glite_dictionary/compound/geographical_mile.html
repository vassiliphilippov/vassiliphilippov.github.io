geographical mile

1. NOUN

/ dʒiˌɑɡrəˈfɪkəl maɪl / (AmE), / dʒiˌɒɡrəˈfɪkəl maɪl / (BrE)
a former British unit of length equal to 6,080 feet (1,853.184 meters), 800 feet longer than a statute mile
former British unit of length (6,080 feet)

Examples

In old navigation charts, distances were often recorded in geographical miles rather than nautical miles.
The geographical mile is now largely obsolete, replaced by international units.
During the 19th century, many maps used the geographical mile as the standard unit of distance.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: statute mile - standard mile used in the UK and US measuring 5,280 feet
  • Synonym: nautical mile - unit of distance used in navigation, approximately 1,852 meters

Etymology

From 'geographical', relating to geography, and 'mile', from Latin 'milia passuum' (a thousand paces).
