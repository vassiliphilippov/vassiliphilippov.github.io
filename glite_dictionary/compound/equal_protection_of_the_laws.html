equal protection of the laws

1. NOUN

/ ˈiːkwəl pɹəˌtɛkʃən əv ðə lɔz / (AmE), / ˈiːkwəl prəˌtɛkʃən əv ðə lɔːz / (BrE)
the right of all individuals to receive the same treatment and protection under the law, as guaranteed by the Fourteenth Amendment to the US Constitution and the due-process clause of the Fifth Amendment
constitutional right to equal legal treatment
legal american

Examples

The Supreme Court ruled that the policy violated the equal protection of the laws.
Activists argued that denying access to education contravened the equal protection of the laws.
A key principle in constitutional law is the equal protection of the laws for every citizen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: due process - fair treatment through the normal judicial system
