alluvial sediment

1. NOUN

/ əˈluːviəl ˈsɛdəmənt / (AmE), / əˈluːvɪəl ˈsɛdɪmənt / (BrE)
Sediment such as clay, silt, or gravel carried by running water and deposited when the flow slows down, typically in riverbeds, floodplains, or deltas.
sediment deposited by running water

Examples

The river deposited alluvial sediment across the floodplain after the heavy rains.
Over thousands of years, alluvial sediment has built up to form fertile agricultural land along the delta.
Scientists collected samples of alluvial sediment to study the history of the river's movement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alluvium - loose, unconsolidated soil or sediments deposited by water

Etymology

From Latin 'alluvius' (washed against) and 'sedimentum' (settling).
