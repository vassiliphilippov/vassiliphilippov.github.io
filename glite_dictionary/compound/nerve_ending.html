nerve ending

1. NOUN

/ nɜrv ˈɛndɪŋ / (AmE), / nɜːv ˈɛndɪŋ / (BrE)
a specialized structure at the end of a nerve fiber where it terminates, especially one that does not form a synapse, involved in transmitting sensations such as pain or touch
structure at the end of a nerve fiber
medical

Examples

The skin contains millions of nerve endings that help us sense touch and temperature.
Damage to the nerve endings can result in a loss of sensation in the affected area.
The stinging pain was caused by irritated nerve endings in her finger.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: synapse - junction between two nerve cells
