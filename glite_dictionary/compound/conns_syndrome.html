Conn's syndrome

1. NOUN

/ kɑnz ˈsɪnˌdroʊm / (AmE), / kɒnz ˈsɪn.drəʊm / (BrE)
A disorder caused by excessive production of aldosterone by the adrenal glands, typically due to a benign tumor, leading to disturbances in salt and water balance, and symptoms such as muscle weakness, cramps, twitching, and sometimes paralysis.
disorder caused by excessive aldosterone production
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with Conn's syndrome after presenting with high blood pressure and muscle cramps.
Successful treatment of Conn's syndrome often involves surgical removal of the adrenal tumor.
Low potassium levels in blood tests can be a warning sign of Conn's syndrome.

Etymology

Named after Jerome W. Conn, the American endocrinologist who first described the condition in 1955.
