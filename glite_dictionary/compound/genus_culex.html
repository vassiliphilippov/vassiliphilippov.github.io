genus Culex

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈkjuː.lɛks / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈkjuː.lɛks / (BrE)
A genus of mosquitoes in the family Culicidae, widespread and distinguished by holding their bodies parallel to the resting surface.
a genus of common mosquitoes

Examples

The genus Culex includes many species of mosquitoes that are common in temperate regions.
Scientists studied the genus Culex to understand the spread of certain diseases.
The eggs of mosquitoes in the genus Culex are laid in clusters on stagnant water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mosquito - common name for Culicidae, flying insect that feeds on blood

Etymology

From Latin *Culex* (gnat, mosquito), used as the genus name in zoological classification.
