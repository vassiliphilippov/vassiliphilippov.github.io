shot hole

1. NOUN

/ ʃɑt hoʊl / (AmE), / ʃɒt həʊl / (BrE)
a hole drilled in rock or earth to receive a charge of explosive for blasting purposes
hole drilled for explosive charge

Examples

After drilling the shot hole, the workers carefully placed the dynamite inside it.
The engineer inspected each shot hole to ensure it was deep enough for the blasting operation.
Safety procedures demand that every shot hole be clearly marked before the explosives are set.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blast hole - a hole drilled to contain explosives for blasting
