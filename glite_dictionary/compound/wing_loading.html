wing loading

1. NOUN

/ wɪŋ ˈloʊdɪŋ / (AmE), / wɪŋ ˈləʊdɪŋ / (BrE)
the ratio of the total weight of an aircraft to the area of its wings, used as a measure of its aerodynamic performance
ratio of aircraft weight to wing area

Examples

The fighter jet's wing loading allows it to perform sharp maneuvers at high speeds.
Engineers calculated the wing loading to ensure the new glider would be stable in the air.
A lower wing loading typically improves an airplane's ability to take off and land on short runways.
