cotton plant

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːtən plænt / (AmE), / ˈkɒtən plɑːnt / (BrE)
a bushy plant or small tree of the mallow family that produces bolls containing seeds with long, soft, hairy fibers used to make cotton
plant producing cotton fibers

Examples

The cotton plant thrives in warm climates with plenty of sunshine.
Farmers harvested the white bolls from each cotton plant by hand.
The invention of the cotton gin made processing the cotton plant much more efficient.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cotton - soft white fibrous substance from the cotton plant
