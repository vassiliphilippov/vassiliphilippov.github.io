dwarf willow

1. NOUN

/ dwɔrf ˈwɪloʊ / (AmE), / dwɔːf ˈwɪləʊ / (BrE)
A small, low-growing willow shrub (Salix herbacea) found in boreal and alpine regions, characterized by creeping stems and bright green leaves.
a low-growing shrub willow of cold regions

Examples

The dwarf willow thrives in the harsh conditions of the Arctic tundra.
Gardeners value the dwarf willow for its ability to grow in rock gardens and alpine beds.
Biologists studied the adaptive features of the dwarf willow in response to extreme cold.

Etymology

‘Dwarf’ from Old English ‘dweorg’ (small person/thing); ‘willow’ from Old English ‘welig’.
