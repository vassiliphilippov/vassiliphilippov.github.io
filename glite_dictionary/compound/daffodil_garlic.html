daffodil garlic

1. NOUN

/ ˈdæf.ə.dɪl ˈɡɑːr.lɪk / (AmE), / ˈdæf.ədɪl ˈɡɑː.lɪk / (BrE)
a European wild onion (Allium neapolitanum) with clusters of white flowers, often found in Mediterranean regions
a European wild onion with white flowers

Examples

The garden's spring blooms included tulips, hyacinths, and daffodil garlic along the border.
She planted daffodil garlic for its beautiful white flowers and mild onion scent.
In Mediterranean cuisine, young shoots of daffodil garlic are sometimes used as a garnish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Allium neapolitanum - Scientific name for daffodil garlic

Etymology

Named for its resemblance to daffodil flowers and its belonging to the garlic genus.
