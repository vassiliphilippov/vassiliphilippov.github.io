hard sign

1. NOUN

/ hɑrd saɪn / (AmE), / hɑːd saɪn / (BrE)
the Cyrillic letter Ъ/ъ, used in modern languages to indicate that the preceding consonant is hard (not palatalized)
the Cyrillic letter Ъ/ъ

Examples

In Russian, the hard sign is rarely used at the end of a word.
The hard sign marks a separation or hard pronunciation in some Slavic languages.
Students often confuse the hard sign with the soft sign when learning the Cyrillic alphabet.

Synset Examples

The final yer appears to be a back yer, but several corrections to this letter have been made.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: soft sign - the Cyrillic letter Ь/ь, marking a palatalized consonant
