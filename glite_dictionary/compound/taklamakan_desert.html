Taklamakan Desert

1. PROPN

/ ˌtɑːkləˈmɑːkən ˈdɛzərt / (AmE), / ˌtækləˈmɑːkən ˈdɛzət / (BrE)
a large desert in the Xinjiang region of western China, known for its harsh, arid conditions
desert in western China

Examples

The Taklamakan Desert is one of the largest sandy deserts in the world.
Ancient Silk Road caravans had to cross the harsh Taklamakan Desert to reach China.
Scientists are studying the shifting dunes of the Taklamakan Desert for clues about climate change.

Etymology

From Uyghur 'Taklamakan', possibly meaning 'place of no return'.
