decline in quality

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈklaɪn ɪn ˈkwɑːləti / (AmE), / dɪˈklaɪn ɪn ˈkwɒləti / (BrE)
a process in which something becomes less good or deteriorates in its standard or value
process of falling standards

Examples

Many customers have noticed a decline in quality since the company changed its manufacturing process.
The restaurant has suffered a decline in quality over the past year.
Some believe that mass production often leads to a gradual decline in quality.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deterioration - the process of becoming worse
  • Antonym: improvement - the process of becoming better
