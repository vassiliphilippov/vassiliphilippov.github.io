diethylaminoethyl cellulose

1. NOUN

/ daɪˌɛθəl.əˌmiː.noʊˈiː.θəl ˈsɛljəˌloʊs / (AmE), / daɪˌɛθɪləˌmiːnəʊˈiːθəl ˈsɛljʊləʊs / (BrE)
a type of cellulose derivative used as an ion-exchange resin in chromatography, especially for separating proteins and nucleic acids
cellulose derivative used in chromatography

Examples

The laboratory used diethylaminoethyl cellulose columns to separate proteins during the experiment.
Purification of the enzyme involved a step using diethylaminoethyl cellulose for ion-exchange chromatography.
Researchers prefer diethylaminoethyl cellulose when isolating negatively charged molecules from complex mixtures.

Etymology

From the chemical groups diethylaminoethyl and cellulose, indicating a cellulose molecule modified with diethylaminoethyl groups.
