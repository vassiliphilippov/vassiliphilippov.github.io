Jolly Roger

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɑli ˈroʊdʒər / (AmE), / ˈdʒɒli ˈrəʊdʒə / (BrE)
a black flag with a white skull and crossbones, traditionally flown by pirate ships to signal their identity or intent
pirate ship flag with skull and crossbones

Examples

The sight of the Jolly Roger on the horizon sent shivers down the sailors' spines.
During the festival, children dressed as pirates and waved a homemade Jolly Roger.
In classic stories, pirates would raise the Jolly Roger before attacking other ships.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pirate flag - a flag flown by pirates, typically with a skull and crossbones

Etymology

Origin uncertain; 'jolly' from English, 'Roger' possibly a generic name for a man or from the French 'joli rouge' (pretty red), referring to a red flag.
