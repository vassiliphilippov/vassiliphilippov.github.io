cirrhosis of the liver

1. NOUN

/ səˈroʊsɪs əv ðə ˈlɪvər / (AmE), / sɪˈrəʊsɪs ɒv ðə ˈlɪvə / (BrE)
A chronic disease in which normal liver tissue is replaced by scar tissue, interfering with liver function; often caused by prolonged alcoholism or hepatitis.
chronic liver disease with scarring
medical

Examples

Long-term alcohol abuse can eventually lead to cirrhosis of the liver, damaging its vital functions.
Her father was hospitalized due to cirrhosis of the liver after years of heavy drinking.
Doctors warned that untreated hepatitis could result in cirrhosis of the liver over time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cirrhosis - chronic liver disease involving degeneration of cells and thickening of tissue
  • Degree Lower: hepatitis - inflammation of the liver

Etymology

From Greek kirrhos (tawny) + -osis (denoting a condition), referring to the liver's yellowish color in the disease.
