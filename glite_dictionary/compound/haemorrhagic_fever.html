haemorrhagic fever

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɛmərˈædʒɪk ˈfiːvər / (AmE), / ˌhiːmɒˈrædʒɪk ˈfiːvə / (BrE)
Any of a group of severe infectious diseases caused by certain viruses, characterized by fever, internal bleeding, and often affecting specific geographic regions.
viral disease causing fever and bleeding
medical

Examples

The outbreak of haemorrhagic fever in the region led to widespread panic among residents.
Doctors worked tirelessly to control the spread of haemorrhagic fever in the affected communities.
Symptoms of haemorrhagic fever include high fever, bleeding, and severe dehydration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: viral hemorrhagic fever - disease caused by viruses characterized by fever and bleeding

Etymology

From Greek 'haima' (blood) and '-rrhagic' (bursting forth), referring to bleeding; 'fever' from Old French 'fievre', from Latin 'febris'.
