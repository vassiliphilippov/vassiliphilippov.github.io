nuclear transplantation

1. NOUN

/ ˈnuː.kli.ɚ ˌtrænz.plænˈteɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˈnjuː.klɪ.ə ˌtræns.plɑːnˈteɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the process of moving a cell nucleus and its genetic material from one cell to another, typically used in cloning and biological research
moving a cell nucleus to another cell
medical

Examples

Researchers used nuclear transplantation to create an embryo with the desired genetic traits.
Advances in nuclear transplantation have contributed to breakthroughs in therapeutic cloning.
The technique of nuclear transplantation involves transferring DNA from a donor cell into an egg cell.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cloning - making a genetically identical copy of an organism or cell

Etymology

From 'nucleus' (Latin nucleus, kernel) + 'transplantation' (from Latin transplantare, to plant across).
