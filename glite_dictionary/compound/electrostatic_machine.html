electrostatic machine

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌlɛktroʊˈstætɪk məˈʃin / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛktrəʊˈstætɪk məˈʃiːn / (BrE)
an electrical device that generates a high voltage by accumulating a charge of static electricity
device generating high-voltage static electricity

Examples

The electrostatic machine was used in the laboratory to demonstrate the principles of static electricity.
Early scientists built large electrostatic machines to generate powerful sparks and study electrical phenomena.
A Van de Graaff generator is a well-known type of electrostatic machine used in physics experiments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Van de Graaff generator - a type of electrostatic machine used for creating very high voltages
  • Synonym: Wimshurst machine - an early electrostatic generator producing high voltages via induction

Etymology

From 'electrostatic' (from Greek 'ēlektron' meaning amber and 'statikos' meaning causing to stand) and 'machine'.
