protein folding

1. NOUN

/ ˈproʊˌtin ˈfoʊldɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈprəʊtiːn ˈfəʊldɪŋ / (BrE)
the process by which a protein molecule adopts its specific three-dimensional structure, essential for its function
process of a protein adopting its 3D structure

Examples

Scientists study protein folding to better understand diseases such as Alzheimer's and Parkinson's.
Proper protein folding is crucial for the protein to perform its biological function.
Errors in protein folding can lead to the accumulation of misfolded proteins in cells.

Synset Examples

understanding protein folding is the next step in deciphering the genetic code
