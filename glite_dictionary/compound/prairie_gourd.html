prairie gourd

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɛri ɡɔrd / (AmE), / ˈprɛəri ɡɔːd / (BrE)
a perennial vine native to dry regions of central and southwestern United States and Mexico, bearing small, hard, mottled green inedible fruit
vine with small hard inedible fruit

Examples

The prairie gourd thrives in the arid regions of the American Southwest.
Despite its attractive appearance, the fruit of the prairie gourd is not edible.
Early settlers sometimes used prairie gourd vines to stabilize loose soil.

2. NOUN

/ ˈprɛri ɡɔrd / (AmE), / ˈprɛːri ɡɔːd / (BrE)
the small, hard, green-and-white inedible fruit of the prairie gourd plant (Cucurbita pepo var. texana), native to North America
inedible fruit of the prairie gourd plant

Examples

The prairie gourd is often found growing wild across the central United States.
Despite its attractive appearance, the prairie gourd is not suitable for human consumption.
Researchers studied the prairie gourd to understand its ecological role in grassland habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gourd - hard-shelled fruit of various plants of the family Cucurbitaceae

Etymology

From 'prairie', a North American grassland, and 'gourd', from Old French 'gourde'.
