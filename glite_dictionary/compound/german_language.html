German language

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɜr.mən ˈlæŋ.ɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈdʒɜː.mən ˈlæŋ.ɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
The standard language of Germany, Austria, and parts of Switzerland, historically derived from West Germanic.
standard language of Germany, Austria, and Switzerland

Examples

He studied the German language for three years before moving to Berlin.
Many scientific works were originally written in the German language.
The German language is known for its compound words and precise vocabulary.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: German - the language spoken in Germany

Etymology

From Latin 'Germania' (Germany) + English 'language'.
