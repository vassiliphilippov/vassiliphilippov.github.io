M-1 rifle

1. NOUN

/ ɛm wʌn ˈraɪfl̩ / (AmE), / ɛm wʌn ˈraɪfl / (BrE)
A semiautomatic rifle, especially the standard U.S. military rifle used during World War II and the Korean War.
semiautomatic military rifle

Examples

The soldier carried an M-1 rifle as he patrolled the camp.
Collectors often seek original M-1 rifles from World War II.
During training, she learned how to disassemble the M-1 rifle quickly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Garand rifle - the formal name of the M-1 rifle, used by the U.S. military

Etymology

The 'M-1' designation comes from U.S. military nomenclature meaning 'Model 1.'
