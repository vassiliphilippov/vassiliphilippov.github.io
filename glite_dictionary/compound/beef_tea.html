beef tea

1. NOUN

/ biːf tiː / (AmE), / biːf tiː / (BrE)
a clear liquid extract made by stewing beef in water, formerly given as nourishment to ill people
liquid beef extract for invalids

Examples

During her illness, the nurse brought her some beef tea to help her regain her strength.
Victorian doctors often recommended beef tea as a restorative.
The chef prepared beef tea by slowly simmering the meat for hours.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: broth - a savory liquid obtained by simmering meat, fish, or vegetables
  • Synonym: consommé - a clear soup made from richly flavored stock
