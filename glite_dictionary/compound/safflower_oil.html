safflower oil

1. NOUN

/ ˈsæfˌlaʊɚ ɔɪl / (AmE), / ˈsæfˌlaʊə ɔɪl / (BrE)
A pale-yellow, flavorless oil extracted from the seeds of the safflower plant, used in cooking, medicines, and paints.
oil from safflower seeds

Examples

She prefers cooking her vegetables in safflower oil because of its light flavor.
Nutritionists often recommend safflower oil for its high content of unsaturated fats.
Some artists historically used safflower oil as a medium in their paints due to its clarity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sunflower oil - oil extracted from sunflower seeds, commonly used in cooking

Etymology

safflower from Arabic *ṣufrāʼ*, via Spanish *azafrán* (saffron); oil from Old French *olie*.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsæfˌlaʊ.ɚ ɔɪl / (AmE), / ˈsæf.laʊ.ə ɔɪl / (BrE)
A pale, flavorless oil extracted from the seeds of the safflower plant, used in cooking and food products.
a type of vegetable oil from safflower seeds

Examples

Many health-conscious cooks prefer safflower oil for frying because of its high smoke point.
Salad dressings made with safflower oil have a light, neutral flavor.
She replaced butter with safflower oil in her baking recipes to reduce saturated fat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sunflower oil - oil from seeds of the sunflower plant

Etymology

From 'safflower', from Arabic 'ṣaffūr', plus 'oil'.
