creeping zinnia

1. NOUN

/ ˈkriːpɪŋ ˈzɪniə / (AmE), / ˈkriːpɪŋ ˈzɪniə / (BrE)
a low-growing, branching annual plant with flower heads similar to zinnias, native to the southwestern United States and parts of Central America
low-growing annual plant with zinnia-like flowers

Examples

The creeping zinnia adds a splash of bright color to garden borders throughout the summer.
Because creeping zinnia stays low and spreads, it's perfect as a ground cover in sunny areas.
Many gardeners choose creeping zinnia for its drought tolerance and easy maintenance.

Etymology

From 'creeping', referring to its low, spreading growth habit, and 'zinnia', after the genus Zinnia, due to the resemblance of its flowers.
