genus Cenchrus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈsɛn.krəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈsɛn.krəs / (BrE)
a genus of grasses in the family Poaceae, known for producing spiny or bristly burs
genus of grasses with burs

Examples

The sandspur belongs to the genus Cenchrus and is known for its painful burs.
Many species within the genus Cenchrus are considered weeds because of their spiny seeds.
Researchers collected samples from several members of the genus Cenchrus for genetic analysis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grass - a plant of the family Poaceae

Etymology

From New Latin Cenchrus, from Ancient Greek kenchros (millet grass).
