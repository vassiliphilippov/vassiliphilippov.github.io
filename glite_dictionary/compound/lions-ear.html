lion's-ear

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪənz ˌɪr / (AmE), / ˈlaɪənz ˌɪə / (BrE)
a pantropical plant with whorls of distinctive lipped flowers, often naturalized in the United States
pantropical herb with lipped flowers

Examples

Many gardeners choose to plant lion's-ear for its striking orange flowers and resilience.
The vibrant blossoms of the lion's-ear attract bees and butterflies to the garden.
In some areas, lion's-ear has become naturalized and grows wild along the roadside.
