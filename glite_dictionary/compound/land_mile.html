land mile

1. NOUN

/ ˈlænd ˌmaɪl / (AmE), / ˈlænd ˌmaɪl / (BrE)
a unit of length equal to 1,760 yards or 5,280 feet; exactly 1,609.344 meters, commonly known as a statute mile, used especially to distinguish it from a nautical mile
a unit of length equal to 1,609.344 meters

Examples

The marathon spans just over twenty-six land miles through city streets.
A land mile is shorter than a nautical mile, which is used in maritime navigation.
The road sign indicated that the town was ten land miles away.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: statute mile - a standard mile used on land, as opposed to nautical mile
  • Antonym: nautical mile - a unit of length used at sea (1,852 meters)
