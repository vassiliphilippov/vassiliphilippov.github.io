plasmatic quality

1. NOUN

/ plæzˈmætɪk ˈkwɑːləti / (AmE), / plæzˈmætɪk ˈkwɒləti / (BrE)
A characteristic of being fluid, formless, or capable of dynamic change.
fluid or formless characteristic

Examples

The dancer's movements had a plasmatic quality, shifting seamlessly from one pose to another.
His abstract painting displayed a plasmatic quality that blurred the boundaries between shapes.
The conversation took on a plasmatic quality, evolving rapidly as new ideas surfaced.

Synset Examples

Her voice had a plasmatic quality that resonated deeply.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fluidity - the quality of being smooth and flowing

Etymology

From 'plasmatic', derived from Greek 'plasma' (something molded or formed), combined with 'quality'.
