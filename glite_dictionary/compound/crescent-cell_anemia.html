crescent-cell anemia

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɛsənt sɛl əˈniːmiə / (AmE), / ˈkrɛsnt sɛl əˈniːmiə / (BrE)
A congenital form of anemia, chiefly affecting people of African descent, characterized by abnormal crescent-shaped red blood cells.
anemia with crescent-shaped red blood cells
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that crescent-cell anemia is a genetic disorder affecting the shape of red blood cells.
Children with crescent-cell anemia may experience episodes of pain and fatigue due to reduced oxygen flow.
Research on treatments for crescent-cell anemia has significantly advanced in recent years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sickle cell anemia - a congenital form of anemia with sickle-shaped red blood cells
