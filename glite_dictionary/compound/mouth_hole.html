mouth hole

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaʊθ hoʊl / (AmE), / ˈmaʊθ həʊl / (BrE)
an opening designed for the mouth, especially in clothing or masks such as a ski mask
hole for the mouth in clothing or masks

Examples

The ski mask has a mouth hole to allow you to breathe and speak easily.
She adjusted the mouth hole of her costume so she could eat without taking it off.
The robber's face was hidden except for the eye and mouth hole in his mask.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eyehole - an opening for the eye, especially in masks or coverings
  • Synonym: mouthpiece - a part of something intended to be put in or near the mouth
