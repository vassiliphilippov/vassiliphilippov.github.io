family Corvidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ˈkɔrˌvɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli ˈkɔːvɪdiː / (BrE)
the family of birds that includes crows, ravens, rooks, jackdaws, choughs, magpies, and jays
bird family including crows and magpies

Examples

Members of the family Corvidae are known for their intelligence and complex social behavior.
The family Corvidae includes some of the most adaptable and widespread birds in the world.
Jays and magpies are well-known representatives of the family Corvidae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corvid - any bird belonging to the family Corvidae

Etymology

From New Latin Corvidae, from Corvus (the type genus) + -idae (a family suffix).
