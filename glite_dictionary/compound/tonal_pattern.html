tonal pattern

1. NOUN

/ ˈtoʊ.nəl ˈpæt.ɚn / (AmE), / ˈtəʊ.nəl ˈpæt.ən / (BrE)
a sequence or arrangement of musical notes organized in a way that is perceived as harmonious or pleasant
musically pleasing arrangement of notes

Examples

The composer experimented with a new tonal pattern to evoke a feeling of nostalgia.
Students learned how to identify different tonal patterns in traditional folk music.
The song's distinctive tonal pattern makes it instantly recognizable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: melody - a sequence of single notes that are musically satisfying
  • Synonym: harmony - the combination of simultaneously sounded notes to produce chords
