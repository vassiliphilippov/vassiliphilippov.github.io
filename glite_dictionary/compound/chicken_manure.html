chicken manure

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɪkən məˈnʊr / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪkɪn məˈnjʊə / (BrE)
Manure consisting of the droppings of chickens, commonly used as a fertilizer to enrich soil.
manure from chickens used as fertilizer

Examples

Organic farmers often enrich their fields with chicken manure to improve crop yields.
The garden smelled strongly after I applied fresh chicken manure to the vegetable beds.
Composted chicken manure provides essential nutrients for roses and other flowering plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: manure - animal dung used for fertilizing land
