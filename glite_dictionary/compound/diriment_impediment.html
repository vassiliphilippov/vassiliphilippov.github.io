diriment impediment

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈrɪmənt ɪmˈpɛdəmənt / (AmE), / dɪˈrɪmɛnt ɪmˈpɛdɪmənt / (BrE)
(in canon law) a legal obstacle that renders a marriage invalid, such as the existence of a previous marriage or close blood relation.
legal bar to valid marriage (canon law)
legal

Examples

The court declared the wedding null due to a diriment impediment discovered after the ceremony.
In canon law, a diriment impediment such as bigamy makes the marriage invalid from the start.
Certain close blood relationships are considered diriment impediments to marriage in many religions.

Etymology

From Latin 'dirimens' (separating) and 'impedimentum' (hindrance, obstacle).
