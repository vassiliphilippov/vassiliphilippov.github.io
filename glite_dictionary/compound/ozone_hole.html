ozone hole

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊˌzoʊn hoʊl / (AmE), / ˈəʊzəʊn həʊl / (BrE)
An area of the ozone layer, especially near the poles, where the concentration of ozone is seasonally depleted.
region of depleted ozone in the atmosphere

Examples

Scientists monitor the ozone hole over Antarctica every spring to track changes in its size.
The discovery of the ozone hole led to international agreements like the Montreal Protocol.
Ultraviolet radiation increases in regions affected by the ozone hole.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ozone layer - the layer of ozone high in the atmosphere that protects the Earth from UV radiation
