hydroxybutyric acid

1. NOUN

/ haɪˌdrɑksiˌbjuːˈtɪrɪk ˌæsɪd / (AmE), / haɪˌdrɒksiˌbjuːˈtɪrɪk ˌæsɪd / (BrE)
Any of several organic acids that are hydroxy derivatives of butyric acid, commonly occurring in biological systems.
organic acid; hydroxy derivative of butyric acid
medical

Examples

Laboratory tests detected elevated levels of hydroxybutyric acid in the patient's blood sample.
One form of hydroxybutyric acid is used by the body as an energy source during fasting.
Researchers are studying how hydroxybutyric acid affects brain function in different metabolic conditions.

Etymology

hydroxy- (hydroxyl group) + butyric acid (from butyricum, Latin for 'butter', from which butyric acid was first obtained).
