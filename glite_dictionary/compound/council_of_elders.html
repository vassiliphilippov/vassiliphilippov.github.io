council of elders

1. NOUN

/ ˈkaʊn.səl əv ˈɛl.dɚz / (AmE), / ˈkaʊn.səl əv ˈɛl.dəz / (BrE)
a group composed of the oldest and most experienced members of a community, often responsible for making important decisions or giving guidance
body of senior community leaders

Examples

The village relies on its council of elders to resolve conflicts and settle disputes.
A council of elders convened to deliberate on the new community rules.
Many societies have a council of elders that preserves traditional customs and wisdom.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: senate - a legislative or governing body of elders in ancient Rome or in some modern countries
