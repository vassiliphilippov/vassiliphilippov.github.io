genus Lychnis

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈlɪk.nɪs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈlɪk.nɪs / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the family Caryophyllaceae, closely related to Silene and commonly known as catchfly.
genus of flowering plants (catchfly)

Examples

The genus Lychnis includes several species known for their showy, brightly colored flowers.
Gardeners often confuse plants in the genus Lychnis with those in the genus Silene.
Many species within the genus Lychnis are cultivated as ornamental plants in temperate regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Silene - a related genus of flowering plants in the same family

Etymology

From Latin *Lychnis*, from Greek *lýchnis*, meaning 'lamp' or 'candle', owing to the plant's bright flowers.
