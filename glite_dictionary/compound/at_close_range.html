at close range

1. ADV

/ ət kloʊs reɪndʒ / (AmE), / ət kləʊs reɪndʒ / (BrE)
from or at a very short distance; very near to the target or object referred to
from a very short distance

Examples

The photographer took several pictures at close range to capture every detail.
The soldier fired at close range during the intense battle.
I could see the intricate design only when I looked at close range.

Synset Examples

without my reading glasses I can hardly see things close up
even firing at close range he missed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: point-blank - at very close range, with little or no distance to the target
  • Antonym: from afar - from a distance; from far away
