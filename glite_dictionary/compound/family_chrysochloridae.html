family Chrysochloridae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ˌkraɪsəˌklɔːrɪˈdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli ˌkrɪsəˌklɒrɪˈdiː / (BrE)
A family of small, burrowing, insectivorous mammals native to southern Africa, known as golden moles.
family of golden moles (mammals)

Examples

The family Chrysochloridae includes several species adapted to life underground.
Researchers studied the habitat preferences of the family Chrysochloridae in southern Africa.
Members of the family Chrysochloridae are characterized by their metallic-looking fur and powerful digging limbs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: golden mole - A burrowing mammal of the Chrysochloridae family with iridescent fur

Etymology

From New Latin 'Chrysochloridae', based on Greek 'chrysos' (gold) and 'chloros' (green), referring to the sheen of their fur.
