good horsetrade

1. NOUN

/ ɡʊd ˈhɔrsˌtreɪd / (AmE), / ɡʊd ˈhɔːsˌtreɪd / (BrE)
a mutually beneficial or fair exchange or deal, often involving negotiation or bargaining
beneficial mutual deal or exchange

Examples

Both companies came away satisfied after negotiating a good horsetrade over the merger.
That was a good horsetrade—you got a promotion and the company filled a vital role.
It took hours of discussion, but they eventually made a good horsetrade that benefitted everyone.

Synset Examples

They made a good horsetrade to exchange their old cars.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horse-trading - hard or shrewd bargaining or negotiation
  • Antonym: bad deal - an exchange or agreement that is unfavorable to one party

Etymology

From the phrase 'horse trade', originally referring to the shrewd bargaining typical in horse trading, now applied to any deal; 'good' denotes favorable outcome.
