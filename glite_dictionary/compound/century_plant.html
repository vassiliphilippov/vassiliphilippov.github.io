century plant

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛn.tʃə.ri plænt / (AmE), / ˈsɛn.tʃəri plɑːnt / (BrE)
A tropical American plant, especially Agave americana, with a rosette of fibrous, sword-shaped leaves and tall spikes of flowers, cultivated for ornament or fiber.
tropical American plant with tall flower spikes

Examples

The century plant in her backyard finally flowered after more than a decade.
Many gardeners appreciate the dramatic appearance of the century plant's tall bloom stalk.
Some species of century plant are cultivated for their strong, fibrous leaves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agave - genus of succulent plants to which the century plant belongs

Etymology

Named for the mistaken belief that it blooms only once every hundred years.
