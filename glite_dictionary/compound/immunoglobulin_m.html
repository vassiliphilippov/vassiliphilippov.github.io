immunoglobulin M

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌmjunəɡlɑːbjəˌlɪn ɛm / (AmE), / ɪˌmjuːnəˌɡlɒbjʊˌlɪn ɛm / (BrE)
A type of antibody that is one of the five main classes of immunoglobulins, important in fighting blood infections and activating immunoglobulin G production.
a class of antibody; fights blood infections
medical

Examples

A high level of immunoglobulin M may indicate a recent infection.
The patient's immunoglobulin M antibodies responded rapidly to the bloodborne virus.
Doctors measured immunoglobulin M to help diagnose the acute phase of the illness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: immunoglobulin G - another major class of antibodies, involved in immune defense

Etymology

From immuno- (relating to the immune system) + globulin (a type of protein) + M (denoting the 'mu' heavy chain).
