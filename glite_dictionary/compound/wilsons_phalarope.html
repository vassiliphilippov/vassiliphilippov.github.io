Wilson's phalarope

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪlsənz fəˈlɛroʊp / (AmE), / ˈwɪlsənz fəˈlærəʊp / (BrE)
a North American shorebird (Phalaropus tricolor) that breeds on the northern Great Plains of Canada and migrates to South America for winter.
a North American shorebird species

Examples

We spotted a Wilson's phalarope feeding along the edge of the marsh.
During migration, hundreds of Wilson's phalaropes can be seen at prairie lakes.
The striking colors of the female Wilson's phalarope make it unique among shorebirds.

Etymology

Named after Alexander Wilson, Scottish-American ornithologist, and from Greek phalaris (coot) + pous (foot).
