last word

1. NOUN

/ læst wɝd / (AmE), / lɑːst wɜːd / (BrE)
the final statement or remark in a discussion, argument, or debate, often implying a sense of victory or closure
final statement in an argument

Examples

She insisted on having the last word in every debate.
During their argument, Mark just had to get the last word.
If you always need the last word, conversations can quickly turn sour.

Synset Examples

she always gets the last word

Related Terms

  • Synonym: final say - the authority to make the ultimate decision or statement

Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ læst wɝːd / (AmE), / lɑːst wɜːd / (BrE)
an authoritative or final statement, decision, or judgment on a matter
authoritative or final statement

Examples

In our family, my mother always has the last word on holiday plans.
Despite many suggestions, the manager's decision was the last word on the project.
The panel's ruling was considered the last word in the long-running dispute.

Synset Examples

my doctor has the last word on the medicines I take

Related Terms

  • Synonym: final say - the ultimate deciding power or statement
