child's play

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃaɪldz ˌpleɪ / (AmE), / ˈtʃaɪldz ˌpleɪ / (BrE)
activities performed by children, typically spontaneous and imaginative, and not governed by strict rules.
imaginative activity by children

Examples

For young children, building a fort out of pillows is pure child's play.
Research shows that child's play helps develop creativity and problem-solving skills.
Painting with fingers and making up stories are perfect examples of child's play.

Synset Examples

Freud believed in the utility of play to a small child

Related Terms

  • Antonym: game - structured form of play with set rules
