gallop rhythm

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡæl.əp ˌrɪð.əm / (AmE), / ˈɡæl.əp ˌrɪð.əm / (BrE)
an abnormal cardiac rhythm characterized by the presence of an extra heart sound, often indicating a heart abnormality
abnormal heart rhythm with extra sound
medical

Examples

The doctor detected a gallop rhythm during the patient's cardiac exam.
A gallop rhythm can sometimes be heard in patients with heart failure.
The presence of a gallop rhythm prompted further investigation into possible cardiac problems.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: normal sinus rhythm - the normal regular rhythm of the heart, set by the natural pacemaker
