X-ray tube

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛks.reɪ tuːb / (AmE), / ˈɛks.reɪ tjuːb / (BrE)
A vacuum tube containing a metal target that is struck by a beam of high-energy electrons to generate X rays.
device producing X rays
medical

Examples

The X-ray tube is a crucial component in most medical imaging devices.
Technicians replaced the faulty X-ray tube to restore the machine's functionality.
An X-ray tube produces X rays by accelerating electrons towards a metal target.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: X-ray - a form of electromagnetic radiation
