genus Carthamus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs kɑrˈθæm.əs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs kɑːˈθæm.əs / (BrE)
A genus of thistle-like plants in the family Asteraceae, which includes the safflower and related species.
a plant genus including safflower

Examples

The genus Carthamus includes several species used for their oil-rich seeds.
Botanists studied the growth patterns of genus Carthamus in arid climates.
Many plants in the genus Carthamus have bright yellow or orange flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: safflower - A thistle-like annual plant cultivated for its oil and dye

Etymology

From New Latin 'Carthamus', from Arabic 'qurṭum', the name for safflower.
