bright sprog

1. NOUN

/ braɪt sprɑɡ / (AmE), / braɪt sprɒɡ / (BrE)
A clever or intelligent child.
clever or intelligent child
british informal

Examples

The teacher quickly noticed that Sam was a bright sprog and gave him extra challenges.
Among the group of children, Emily stood out as a bright sprog with her quick wit.
Even though he was the youngest in the class, he acted like a real bright sprog and often amazed his teachers.

Synset Examples

He's a bright sprog, always asking questions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bright spark - an intelligent or lively person
  • Degree Higher: child prodigy - a child with exceptional talent or intelligence

Etymology

'Sprog' is British slang, possibly from military slang for a new recruit or inexperienced person, later a child.
