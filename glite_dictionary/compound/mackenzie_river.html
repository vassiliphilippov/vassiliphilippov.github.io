Mackenzie River

1. PROPN

/ məˈkɛnzi ˈrɪvər / (AmE), / məˈkɛnzi ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
the longest river in Canada, flowing northwest through the Northwest Territories into the Beaufort Sea
major river in Canada

Examples

The Mackenzie River is known for its vast delta and diverse wildlife.
Several indigenous communities live along the Mackenzie River in the Northwest Territories.
In the summer, the Mackenzie River becomes a crucial transportation route for the region.

Etymology

Named after the explorer Sir Alexander Mackenzie (1764–1820).
