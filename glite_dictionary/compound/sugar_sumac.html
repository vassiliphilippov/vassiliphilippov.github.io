sugar sumac

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃuɡər ˈsuːmæk / (AmE), / ˈʃʊɡə ˈsjuːmæk / (BrE)
an evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States, noted for its reddish yellow flower spikes and glandular, hairy fruits
evergreen shrub with reddish yellow flowers

Examples

The sugar sumac thrives in moist woodlands across the southeastern United States.
Clusters of reddish yellow flowers make the sugar sumac easy to identify in spring.
Gardeners value the sugar sumac for its decorative and wildlife-friendly fruits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sumac - any of various shrubs or small trees of the genus Rhus
