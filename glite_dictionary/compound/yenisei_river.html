Yenisei River

1. PROPN

/ ˌjɛnɪˈseɪ ˈrɪvər / (AmE), / ˌjɛnɪˈseɪ ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
a major river in Siberia, Russia, rising near the Mongolian border and flowing north into the Kara Sea
major Siberian river in Russia

Examples

The Yenisei River is one of the longest rivers in the world, stretching over 3,400 miles.
Every spring, the ice on the Yenisei River breaks up dramatically, attracting many tourists.
Hydroelectric stations on the Yenisei River provide much of the region's power supply.
