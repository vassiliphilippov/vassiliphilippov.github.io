corn borer

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔrn ˌbɔrər / (AmE), / ˈkɔːn ˌbɔːrə / (BrE)
The larva of a moth that feeds on and damages maize and other crops, especially the European corn borer.
insect larva that infests maize

Examples

Farmers often lose large portions of their crop to the destructive corn borer.
The corn borer is a major threat to maize production around the world.
Pesticide-resistant strains of corn borer have become a significant problem for agriculture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: European corn borer - larva of the moth species Ostrinia nubilalis, a pest of maize

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɔrn ˌbɔrər / (AmE), / ˈkɔːn ˌbɔːrə / (BrE)
a moth whose larvae bore into the stems of corn and other plants, damaging crops; especially the European corn borer.
insect pest that bores into corn

Examples

Crop losses increased after the corn borer infestation spread across the region.
Farmers use biological controls to manage the corn borer population in their fields.
The larvae of the corn borer tunnel into the stalks, weakening the corn plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: European corn borer - a species of corn borer, Ostrinia nubilalis, native to Europe
