western grey squirrel

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɛstɚn ɡreɪ ˈskwɝəl / (AmE), / ˈwɛstən ɡreɪ ˈskwɪrəl / (BrE)
a large grey squirrel native to the far western regions of the United States, known for its bushy tail and silvery-grey fur
large grey squirrel of western United States

Examples

The western grey squirrel is often seen leaping between oak trees in California parks.
A wildlife survey recorded several western grey squirrels along the hiking trail this spring.
Unlike its eastern cousin, the western grey squirrel prefers more open, woodland habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eastern gray squirrel - a similar species found in eastern North America
