sheet piling

1. NOUN

/ ʃit ˈpaɪlɪŋ / (AmE), / ʃiːt ˈpaɪlɪŋ / (BrE)
a row of interlocking piles driven side by side into the ground to retain earth or prevent water seepage, commonly used in construction and engineering
interlocking piles for retaining earth or water

Examples

The sheet piling was installed along the riverbank to prevent flooding.
Engineers chose sheet piling as the best solution for stabilizing the foundation.
The construction crew drove the sheet piling deep into the soil to create a secure barrier.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: retaining wall - a wall that holds back earth or water
