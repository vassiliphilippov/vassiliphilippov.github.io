genus Heterotheca

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˌhɛtərəˈθiːkə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˌhɛtərəˈθiːkə / (BrE)
A genus of yellow-flowered herbaceous plants native to North America, belonging to the family Asteraceae.
genus of yellow-flowered North American herbs

Examples

Several species of genus Heterotheca are native to the western United States.
The genus Heterotheca is known for its resilience and bright yellow blooms.
Botanists recently discovered a new member of the genus Heterotheca in Texas.

Etymology

From Greek 'heteros' (different) and 'theke' (case), referring to variable fruits; botanical Latin formation.
