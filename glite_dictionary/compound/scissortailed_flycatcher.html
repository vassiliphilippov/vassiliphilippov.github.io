scissortailed flycatcher

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪsɚˌteɪld ˈflaɪˌkætʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈsɪsətʰeɪld ˈflaɪˌkatʃə / (BrE)
a grey flycatcher bird of the southwestern United States, Mexico, and Central America, known for its long forked tail and distinctive white breast with salmon and scarlet markings
a long-tailed flycatcher bird species

Examples

We spotted a scissortailed flycatcher perched on the fence in Texas.
The scissortailed flycatcher's long, forked tail makes it easy to identify from a distance.
Birdwatchers travel from afar to see the colorful scissortailed flycatcher during migration season.

Etymology

From 'scissor-tailed' (referring to its tail shape) and 'flycatcher' (a type of bird that catches insects in flight).
