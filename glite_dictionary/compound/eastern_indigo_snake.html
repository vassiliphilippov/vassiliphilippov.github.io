eastern indigo snake

1. NOUN

/ ˈiːstɚn ˈɪndɪɡoʊ sneɪk / (AmE), / ˈiːstən ˈɪndɪɡəʊ sneɪk / (BrE)
a large, nonvenomous snake native to the southeastern United States, known for its glossy, blue-black coloration
large blue-black nonvenomous snake of the southeastern U.S.

Examples

The eastern indigo snake is the longest native snake species in North America.
Biologists are working to conserve the declining population of eastern indigo snakes in Florida.
During the day, an eastern indigo snake may be seen basking near the edge of a forest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indigo snake - a snake species of the genus Drymarchon
