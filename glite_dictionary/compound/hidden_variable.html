hidden variable

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɪdən ˈvɛriəbl̩ / (AmE), / ˈhɪdən ˈvɛːriəbl̩ / (BrE)
an unobserved factor or quantity that may influence the outcome of an experiment, process, or system
unobserved influencing factor

Examples

The experiment failed to control for a hidden variable that affected the measurements.
Researchers are often surprised when a hidden variable changes the outcome of their studies.
In statistics, finding a hidden variable can be crucial for explaining correlations between observed factors.

Synset Examples

In our experiment, we discovered a hidden variable affecting the results.
Identifying the hidden variable was key to solving the equation.
Researchers often overlook hidden variables in their studies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: latent variable - variable that is not directly observed but is inferred from other variables
