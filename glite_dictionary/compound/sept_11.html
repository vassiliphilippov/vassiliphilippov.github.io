Sept. 11

1. NOUN

/ sɛpt ən ˈɛl ɪˈlɛvən / (AmE), / sɛpt ən ˈɛl ɪˈlɛvən / (BrE)
the day on September 11, 2001, when coordinated terrorist attacks were carried out in the United States, resulting in the destruction of the World Trade Center in New York and damage to the Pentagon in Washington, D.C.
September 11, 2001 terrorist attacks in the U.S.

Examples

Many Americans remember exactly where they were when they heard about Sept. 11.
The events of Sept. 11 changed the course of world history.
There are special ceremonies held every year to honor the victims of Sept. 11.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: 9/11 - September 11, 2001 terrorist attacks in the United States
