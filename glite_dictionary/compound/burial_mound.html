burial mound

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛriəl maʊnd / (AmE), / ˈbɛriəl maʊnd / (BrE)
An artificial heap of earth constructed over a grave or graves, especially in prehistoric times.
prehistoric earthwork over a tomb

Examples

Archaeologists discovered artifacts beneath the ancient burial mound.
The burial mound was covered in wildflowers, marking the site of an ancient tomb.
Many prehistoric cultures built a burial mound to honor their dead.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barrow - Ancient burial mound
  • Synonym: tumulus - An ancient burial mound; a tumulus

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɛriəl maʊnd / (AmE), / ˈbɛriəl maʊnd / (BrE)
A mound of earth or stones built over a grave or graves, often serving as a monument or marker, especially in ancient cultures.
mound built over a grave

Examples

The archaeologists uncovered a Bronze Age burial mound in the field.
Several Native American burial mounds can be found near the riverbank.
Visitors are drawn to the ancient burial mound because of its historical significance.

Synset Examples

Mound City is known for its historical burial mounds.

Related Terms
