bust-up takeover

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌst ʌp ˈteɪkˌoʊvər / (AmE), / ˈbʌst ʌp ˈteɪkəʊvə / (BrE)
a leveraged buyout in which the acquired company's assets are sold off to repay the debt used to finance the acquisition
leveraged buyout with asset sales

Examples

The company faced a bust-up takeover that led to the sale of most of its divisions.
After the bust-up takeover, the new owners quickly liquidated valuable assets to cover the acquisition costs.
Many employees lost their jobs as a result of the bust-up takeover strategy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leveraged buyout - purchase of a company using borrowed funds
