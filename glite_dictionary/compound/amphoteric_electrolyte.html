amphoteric electrolyte

1. NOUN

/ æmˈfoʊtərɪk ˌiːlɛkˈtraɪt / (AmE), / æmˈfəʊtərɪk ˌiːlɛkˈtraɪt / (BrE)
A substance that can act as either an acid or a base depending on the pH of the solution it is in.
substance acting as acid or base by pH

Examples

Water is an example of an amphoteric electrolyte because it can donate or accept protons depending on the environment.
Certain amino acids behave as amphoteric electrolytes under physiological conditions.
The concept of an amphoteric electrolyte is important in understanding acid-base reactions in chemistry.

Etymology

Amphoteric from Greek 'amphoteroi' (both), electrolyte from Greek 'ēlektron' (amber) + 'lyein' (to loosen).
