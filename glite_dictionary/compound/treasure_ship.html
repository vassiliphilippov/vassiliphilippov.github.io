treasure ship

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrɛʒər ʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈtrɛʒə ʃɪp / (BrE)
a ship, especially from the 16th century, that was loaded with valuable cargo such as gold, silver, and precious goods
16th-century ship carrying treasure

Examples

The Spanish fleet sent a treasure ship back to Europe each year from the New World.
Pirates dreamed of capturing a treasure ship filled with gold and jewels.
A storm sank the treasure ship before it could reach its home port.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: galleon - a large sailing ship used from the 15th to 18th centuries, especially by Spain
