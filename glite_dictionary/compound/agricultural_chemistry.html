agricultural chemistry

1. NOUN

/ əˌɡrɪk.jəˈltʃɚl ˈkɛm.ɪstri / (AmE), / əˌɡrɪk.jʊˈltʃər.əl ˈkɛm.ɪstri / (BrE)
the branch of chemistry concerned with the chemical processes and substances important in agriculture, including production, crop processing, and environmental management
chemistry related to agriculture and food production

Examples

A degree in agricultural chemistry can lead to careers in research, food safety, and environmental protection.
He specializes in agricultural chemistry, focusing on ways to enhance soil fertility and crop yields.
Advances in agricultural chemistry have improved the efficiency of fertilizer use and pesticide application.

Synset Examples

Agricultural chemistry must be considered within the context of the soil ecosystem in which living and nonliving components interact in complicated cycles that are critical to all living things.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: biochemistry - the study of chemical processes in living organisms
