Salvia azurea

1. NOUN

/ ˈsæl.vi.ə əˈzʊr.i.ə / (AmE), / ˈsal.vɪ.ə əˈzjʊə.rɪ.ə / (BrE)
A species of blue-flowered sage native to the dry prairies of the eastern United States.
blue-flowered sage native to eastern US prairies

Examples

In autumn, the Salvia azurea adds a splash of blue to the native prairie landscape.
Gardeners often choose Salvia azurea for its drought tolerance and striking blue flowers.
The conservation project included the restoration of native species like Salvia azurea.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blue sage - common name for Salvia azurea
  • Degree Higher: sage - aromatic herb of the genus Salvia

Etymology

From Latin; 'Salvia' is the genus name, and 'azurea' refers to its blue color, from 'azure' (sky blue).
