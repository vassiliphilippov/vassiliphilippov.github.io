victim feminism

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪktəm ˈfɛmɪˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈvɪktɪm ˈfɛmɪnɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
a perspective in feminist theory that emphasizes women's victimhood and experiences of victimization, often criticized for portraying women as passive or powerless
a feminist perspective highlighting women's victimhood

Examples

Some critics argue that victim feminism focuses too much on women's vulnerabilities rather than their strengths.
The debate between victim feminism and empowerment feminism is common in academic circles.
She challenges the assumptions underlying victim feminism in her writing.

Synset Examples

Critics argue that victim feminism undermines women's empowerment.
She believes victim feminism can be harmful to progress.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: empowerment feminism - a perspective in feminism emphasizing agency and empowerment
