subnormal intelligence

1. NOUN

/ sʌbˈnɔrməl ɪnˈtɛlədʒəns / (AmE), / sʌbˈnɔːməl ɪnˈtelɪdʒəns / (BrE)
intelligence that is significantly below the average level, especially as measured by standard psychological tests
below-average cognitive ability
medical

Examples

The test results indicated that she had subnormal intelligence compared to her peers.
Educational programs were adapted for children with subnormal intelligence throughout the 20th century.
A diagnosis of subnormal intelligence often led to placement in special education classes.

Synset Examples

She was classified as having subnormal intelligence in school.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intellectual disability - significantly limited intellectual and adaptive functioning
