Javelle water

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˈvɛl ˌwɑtər / (AmE), / dʒəˈvɛl ˌwɔːtə / (BrE)
an aqueous solution of sodium hypochlorite, used as a bleaching or disinfecting agent
sodium hypochlorite solution for bleaching/disinfecting

Examples

The laboratory prepared Javelle water to use as a disinfectant during experiments.
To remove the stubborn stains, she treated the fabric with Javelle water.
Because of its strong oxidizing properties, Javelle water must be handled with care.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bleach - a chemical used to whiten or disinfect

Etymology

From French 'eau de Javelle', named after the Javelle district in Paris where it was first produced.
