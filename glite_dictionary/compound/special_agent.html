special agent

1. NOUN

/ ˈspɛʃəl ˈeɪdʒənt / (AmE), / ˈspɛʃəl ˈeɪdʒənt / (BrE)
an agent whose legal authority is limited to a particular task or mission they have been specifically instructed to perform
an agent with authority for a specific task

Examples

The special agent was hired to oversee the company's merger negotiations.
As a special agent, her duties do not extend beyond the current assignment.
Only a special agent can be authorized to carry out this kind of sensitive investigation.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: general agent - an agent with broad authority to act on behalf of a principal
