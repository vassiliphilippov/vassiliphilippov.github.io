ghost dance

1. NOUN

/ ɡoʊst dæns / (AmE), / ɡəʊst dɑːns / (BrE)
A religious dance originally practiced by some Native American tribes in the late 19th century, believed to bring about communication with the spirits of the dead and hope for renewal.
Native American religious ritual dance

Examples

The Lakota people participated in the ghost dance as a form of resistance and hope.
Historians study the impact of the ghost dance on Native American culture and U.S. policy.
The government feared the ghost dance, leading to increased tensions and tragic events.
