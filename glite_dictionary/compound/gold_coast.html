Gold Coast

1. PROPN

/ ɡoʊld koʊst / (AmE), / ɡəʊld kəʊst / (BrE)
the former name of the West African country now known as Ghana, especially during its period as a British colony
former name for Ghana (British colony)

Examples

Before independence in 1957, Ghana was known as the Gold Coast.
Many historical documents refer to the Gold Coast as a British colony.
The Gold Coast was a major center for trade on the Gulf of Guinea.

Synset Examples

Ghana was colonized as the Gold Coast by the British

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ghana - modern country in West Africa, formerly known as the Gold Coast

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡoʊld ˌkoʊst / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊld ˌkəʊst / (BrE)
an affluent neighborhood, often along a coast, noted for expensive homes and luxurious living
wealthy coastal neighborhood

Examples

He grew up in the Gold Coast, where mansions line the lakefront.
Many celebrities choose to live in the city's Gold Coast for its exclusivity.
Property prices in the Gold Coast have risen dramatically over the past decade.

Synset Examples

Chicago's gold coast is along Lake Michigan

Etymology

From 'gold' symbolizing wealth and 'coast' referring to shoreline areas; used for exclusive, wealthy coastal districts in American cities.
