staphylococcal enterotoxin B

1. NOUN

/ ˌstæfɪləˌkɑkəl ˌɛntəroʊˈtɑksɪn bi / (AmE), / ˌstæfɪləˌkɒkəl ˌɛntərəʊˈtɒksɪn bi / (BrE)
a potent toxin produced by the bacterium Staphylococcus aureus, sometimes used as a biological warfare agent due to its ability to cause severe illness.
potent bacterial toxin; biological agent
medical

Examples

Staphylococcal enterotoxin B was investigated for its potential use in incapacitating enemy troops during warfare.
Exposure to staphylococcal enterotoxin B can result in fever, vomiting, and severe gastrointestinal distress.
Research on staphylococcal enterotoxin B has provided insight into the dangers of bacterial toxins as biological weapons.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: staphylococcal enterotoxin - any toxin produced by Staphylococcus bacteria that targets the intestines

Etymology

From 'staphylococcal' (relating to Staphylococcus bacteria) and 'enterotoxin' (toxin affecting the intestines), with 'B' indicating its subtype.
