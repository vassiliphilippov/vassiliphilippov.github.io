caesarian section

1. NOUN

/ siˈzɛriən ˈsɛkʃən / (AmE), / siˈzɛːriən ˈsɛkʃən / (BrE)
a surgical procedure to deliver a baby by making incisions through the mother's abdominal wall and uterus
surgical childbirth procedure
medical

Examples

The doctor recommended a caesarian section because of complications during labor.
She gave birth to her son via caesarian section last year.
Advancements in medical technology have made the caesarian section a much safer procedure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cesarean section - American spelling of caesarian section; same procedure

Etymology

From Latin 'Caesarean' (relating to Caesar), due to the ancient belief that Julius Caesar was born this way.
