Murrumbidgee River

1. PROPN

/ mʌrəmˈbɪdʒi ˈrɪvər / (AmE), / mʌrəmˈbɪdʒi ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
a river in southeastern Australia that flows westward into the Murray River
river in southeastern Australia

Examples

Many towns and cities rely on the Murrumbidgee River for their water supply and irrigation.
During our trip through New South Wales, we camped near the banks of the Murrumbidgee River.
The Murrumbidgee River floods occasionally, affecting local agriculture and wildlife.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Murray River - major river in southeastern Australia into which the Murrumbidgee flows

Etymology

From Wiradjuri, an Aboriginal Australian language, possibly meaning 'big water' or 'plenty water'.
