sacrament of the Eucharist

1. NOUN

/ ˈsækrəmənt əv ði juˈkærɪst / (AmE), / ˈsakɹəmənt əv ði juˈkærɪst / (BrE)
The Christian sacrament celebrating the Last Supper, in which bread and wine are consecrated and consumed in remembrance of Jesus Christ's death and resurrection.
Christian sacrament of bread and wine

Examples

Many Christians receive the sacrament of the Eucharist every Sunday during Mass.
The sacrament of the Eucharist is central to Catholic worship.
She prepared to take the sacrament of the Eucharist for the first time at her church.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: communion - The Christian rite in which bread and wine are consumed as symbols of Christ's body and blood

Etymology

Sacrament from Latin 'sacramentum' (a solemn oath); Eucharist from Greek 'eucharistia' (thanksgiving).
