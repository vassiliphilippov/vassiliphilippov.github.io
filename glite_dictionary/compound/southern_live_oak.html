southern live oak

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌðɚn laɪv oʊk / (AmE), / ˈsʌðən laɪv əʊk / (BrE)
A medium-sized evergreen oak tree native to the southeastern United States and eastern Mexico, noted for its wide-spreading crown and very hard, durable wood.
evergreen oak tree of the southeastern US

Examples

The massive southern live oak in the park is over two hundred years old.
Many historic plantations are lined with southern live oaks that create beautiful shaded avenues.
The wood from the southern live oak was once highly prized in shipbuilding for its strength and durability.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: live oak - evergreen oak trees, especially Quercus species in North America
