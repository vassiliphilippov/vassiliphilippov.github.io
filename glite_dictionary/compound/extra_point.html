extra point

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛkstrə pɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˈɛkstrə pɔɪnt / (BrE)
(in American football) a point that is scored by successfully kicking the ball through the uprights after a touchdown.
point scored after touchdown in American football
american

Examples

After scoring the touchdown, the team successfully kicked the extra point.
He missed the extra point, leaving the score at 20-13.
The game was tied after the opponent blocked the extra point.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: two-point conversion - a play for two points after a touchdown in American football
  • Synonym: field goal - a score by kicking the ball during regular play in American football

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛk.strə pɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˈɛk.strə pɔɪnt / (BrE)
an additional point awarded in a game, especially as a bonus or after a specific achievement
additional point in a game

Examples

After scoring a touchdown, the team attempted an extra point to increase their lead.
By answering the bonus question, she earned an extra point for her team.
The referee announced that an extra point would be awarded for fair play.
