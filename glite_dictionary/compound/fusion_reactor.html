fusion reactor

1. NOUN

/ ˈfjuːʒən riˈæktər / (AmE), / ˈfjuːʒən riˈæktə / (BrE)
a nuclear reactor in which energy is produced through controlled nuclear fusion reactions
device generating energy by nuclear fusion

Examples

Scientists work worldwide to develop a practical fusion reactor for clean energy production.
The experimental fusion reactor achieved temperatures hotter than the sun's core.
Funding for the new fusion reactor project was approved by several countries.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fission reactor - a nuclear reactor using nuclear fission to generate energy

Etymology

From 'fusion' (Latin: fusio, meaning 'a pouring or melting') and 'reactor', from 'react', meaning 'to act back'.
