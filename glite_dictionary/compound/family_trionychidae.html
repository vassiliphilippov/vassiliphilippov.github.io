family Trionychidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli traɪˌɒnɪˈkɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli traɪˌɒnɪˈkɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of soft-shelled turtles characterized by their leathery shells and aquatic habits, found mainly in Asia, Africa, and North America.
family of soft-shelled turtles

Examples

The family Trionychidae includes several species adapted to aquatic environments.
Researchers discovered a new member of the family Trionychidae in Southeast Asia.
Many softshell turtles in the family Trionychidae are threatened due to habitat loss.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: softshell turtle - a turtle with a soft, leathery shell

Etymology

From modern Latin Trionychidae, based on Greek 'tri-' (three), 'onyx' (claw), referring to typical claw patterns in the group.
