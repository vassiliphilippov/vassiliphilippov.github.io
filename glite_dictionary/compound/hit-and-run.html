hit-and-run

1. ADJ

/ ˌhɪt ən ˈrʌn / (AmE), / ˌhɪt ən ˈrʌn / (BrE)
involving or denoting a motor vehicle accident in which the driver responsible leaves the scene without providing assistance or identifying themselves
accident where the driver flees the scene

Examples

The police are investigating a hit-and-run accident that occurred late last night.
She suffered minor injuries in a hit-and-run crash on her way home.
He was arrested as the prime suspect in several hit-and-run cases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hit and run - a motor vehicle accident where the driver leaves the scene

2. ADJ

/ ˌhɪt ən ˈrʌn / (AmE), / ˌhɪt ən ˈrʌn / (BrE)
(of an attack or tactic) involving a quick strike followed by immediate withdrawal.
brief attack followed by rapid retreat

Examples

The rebels staged a series of hit-and-run attacks against the convoy.
Police said the hit-and-run tactics made the perpetrators difficult to catch.
The army trained its units for hit-and-run operations in the dense jungle.

Synset Examples

hit-and-run units
tip-and-run assaults

Etymology

From the sports term, especially baseball and cricket, describing an immediate action followed by rapid movement, later generalized to other contexts.
