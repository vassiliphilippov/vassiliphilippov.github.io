linear unit

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪniɚ ˈjuːnɪt / (AmE), / ˈlɪniə ˈjuːnɪt / (BrE)
a unit used to measure length, such as a meter, foot, or inch
unit of measurement of length

Examples

The meter is a common linear unit used in science around the world.
The contractor measured the room in linear units before ordering materials.
When converting inches to centimeters, remember that both are linear units.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unit of length - a standard unit for measuring length or distance
  • Antonym: area unit - a unit for measuring area
  • Antonym: volume unit - a unit for measuring volume

Etymology

From 'linear', relating to length or a line, and 'unit', meaning a standard of measurement.
