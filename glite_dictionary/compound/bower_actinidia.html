bower actinidia

1. NOUN

/ ˈbaʊər ækˈtɪnɪdiə / (AmE), / ˈbaʊə(r) ækˈtɪnɪdɪə / (BrE)
A climbing Asiatic vine with long, finely serrated leaves and clusters of white flowers, producing greenish-yellow, edible fruit.
Asiatic climbing vine with edible fruit

Examples

The gardener planted a bower actinidia along the trellis to create a leafy, flowering arch.
In spring, the bower actinidia produces clusters of delicate white blossoms before fruiting.
They harvested the ripe fruits from their bower actinidia vine for a homemade dessert.

Etymology

From 'bower' (a pleasant shady place under trees or climbing plants) and modern Latin genus name 'Actinidia'.
