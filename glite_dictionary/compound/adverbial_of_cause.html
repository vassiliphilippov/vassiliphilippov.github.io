adverbial of cause

1. NOUN

/ ædˈvɝː.bi.əl əv kɔz / (AmE), / ədˈvɜː.bi.əl əv kɔːz / (BrE)
an adverbial phrase or clause that indicates the reason why an action is performed
adverbial indicating reason for an action

Examples

She missed the meeting for being unwell, which is an adverbial of cause.
In the sentence 'He apologized because he was late,' 'because he was late' is an adverbial of cause.
Teachers often ask students to identify the adverbial of cause in complex sentences.

Synset Examples

It is common for more than one type of adverbial to appear in a sentence, as in Jonathan ran a race for fun last weekend, where there is an adverbial of reason and an adverbial of time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adverbial of reason - an adverbial indicating the reason or purpose for an action
