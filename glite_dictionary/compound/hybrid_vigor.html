hybrid vigor

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪ.brɪd ˈvɪɡ.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈhaɪ.brɪd ˈvɪɡ.ə / (BrE)
(genetics) the improved or increased function of any biological quality in a hybrid offspring, often resulting in greater strength, yield, or resistance than either parent.
superior qualities in offspring from crossbreeding

Examples

Corn varieties bred for hybrid vigor often yield much more than traditional strains.
The farmer selected seeds to maximize hybrid vigor in his livestock.
Plant breeders study hybrid vigor to improve disease resistance in crops.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heterosis - the phenomenon of increased vigor in hybrids; technical synonym

Etymology

From 'hybrid' (Latin hybrida, a cross-breed) + 'vigor' (Latin vigor, liveliness); scientific term first used in the late 19th century.
