basil balm

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæzəl bɑːm / (AmE), / ˈbæzəl bɑːm / (BrE)
A fragrant European mint plant (Melissa officinalis) with clusters of small violet-and-white flowers, naturalized especially in eastern North America.
a fragrant European mint plant

Examples

We grew basil balm in our garden because of its pleasant aroma.
Many herbal teas include dried basil balm leaves for their calming effect.
Basil balm attracts bees with its clusters of violet-and-white flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lemon balm - a fragrant herbaceous plant of the mint family (Melissa officinalis) with lemon-scented leaves

2. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪzəl bɑm / (AmE), / ˈbæzəl bɑːm / (BrE)
A perennial herb native to North America, with aromatic leaves and clusters of yellowish-pink flower heads.
aromatic North American perennial herb

Examples

The garden was filled with the fragrant scent of basil balm in the summertime.
Native to North America, basil balm thrives in well-drained soils and partial sunlight.
She dried basil balm leaves to use in homemade herbal teas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monarda - a genus of aromatic herbs in the mint family
