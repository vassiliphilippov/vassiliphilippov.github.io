royal jelly

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɔɪəl ˌdʒɛli / (AmE), / ˈrɔɪəl ˌdʒɛli / (BrE)
a milky secretion produced by the pharyngeal glands of worker bees, fed to larvae and queen bees
milky bee secretion fed to larvae and queen bees

Examples

Bee larvae destined to become queens are fed an exclusive diet of royal jelly.
Some people believe that royal jelly has special health benefits when used as a supplement.
The beekeeper carefully harvested royal jelly from the hive for research purposes.
