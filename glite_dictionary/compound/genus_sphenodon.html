genus Sphenodon

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs sfɪˈnoʊ.dɒn / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs sfɪˈnəʊ.dɒn / (BrE)
a genus of reptiles comprising the tuataras, the only living members of the order Rhynchocephalia, native to New Zealand
genus of reptiles containing tuataras
medical

Examples

The genus Sphenodon is represented today only by the tuatara, found in New Zealand.
Scientists studied the evolutionary history of the genus Sphenodon to learn about ancient reptiles.
Unlike other reptiles, the genus Sphenodon has unique jaw and skull structures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tuatara - a lizard-like reptile native to New Zealand, only living member of this genus

Etymology

From Greek sphenos (wedge) and odon (tooth), referring to the shape of the animal's teeth.
