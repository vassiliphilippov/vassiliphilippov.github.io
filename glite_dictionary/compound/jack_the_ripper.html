Jack the Ripper

1. PROPN

/ dʒæk ðə ˈrɪpər / (AmE), / dʒæk ðə ˈrɪpə / (BrE)
an unidentified person who committed several murders in London in the late 19th century, famously never caught or identified
infamous unidentified English murderer (19th century)

Examples

Many historians have tried to discover the true identity of Jack the Ripper.
The crimes attributed to Jack the Ripper shocked Victorian society.
Books and films often speculate about who Jack the Ripper really was.

Etymology

Originates from anonymous letters sent to police in 1888 signed 'Jack the Ripper'.
