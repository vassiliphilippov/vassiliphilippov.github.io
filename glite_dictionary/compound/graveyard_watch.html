graveyard watch

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡreɪvˌjɑrd wɑtʃ / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪvjɑːd wɒtʃ / (BrE)
a work shift or duty period that takes place during the late night hours, typically from midnight to 8 a.m., especially in maritime or industrial contexts
night shift from midnight to 8 a.m.

Examples

He was assigned the graveyard watch and had to stay alert until dawn.
Working the graveyard watch can be especially tough in the dead of winter.
During the graveyard watch, the ship was quiet and everyone tried to keep warm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: night shift - work shift occurring during the night

Etymology

From 'graveyard' (suggesting something lifeless or quiet) + 'watch' (a period of duty), referencing the stillness of late night hours.
