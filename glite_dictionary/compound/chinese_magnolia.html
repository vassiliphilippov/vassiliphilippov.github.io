Chinese magnolia

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃaɪˌniz mæɡˈnoʊliə / (AmE), / ˈtʃaɪˌniːz mæɡˈnəʊliə / (BrE)
A large deciduous shrub or small tree, native to Asia, with large open rosy to purplish flowers, often grown as an ornamental plant.
ornamental Asian shrub or small tree with rosy to purplish flowers

Examples

The Chinese magnolia blooms early in spring, brightening up gardens before most trees have leaves.
Many gardeners in the eastern United States favor the Chinese magnolia for its dramatic, fragrant flowers.
We planted a Chinese magnolia in our backyard, and it quickly became the centerpiece of our landscaping.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: magnolia - a genus of flowering trees and shrubs with large, typically fragrant flowers
