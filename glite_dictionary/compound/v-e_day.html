V-E Day

1. NOUN

/ vi ˈi deɪ / (AmE), / viː ˈiː deɪ / (BrE)
The day marking the Allied victory in Europe during World War II, celebrated on May 8, 1945.
the day of Allied victory in Europe, WWII

Examples

Every year, veterans gather to commemorate V-E Day with parades and ceremonies.
Many people remember where they were when news of V-E Day was announced.
History classes often discuss the significance of V-E Day in ending World War II.

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'Victory in Europe Day'.
