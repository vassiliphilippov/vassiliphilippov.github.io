labour-intensive

1. ADJ

/ ˈleɪbər ɪnˈtɛnsɪv / (AmE), / ˈleɪbə ɪnˈtɛnsɪv / (BrE)
(of an activity or process) requiring a large amount of physical work or human effort, rather than capital, machinery, or technology.
requiring much labor and little capital

Examples

The textile industry is highly labour-intensive in many developing countries.
Manual farming remains a labour-intensive occupation compared to modern agriculture.
Switching to automated equipment can reduce the labour-intensive nature of production.

Synset Examples

cottage industries are labor intensive

Related Terms

  • Antonym: capital-intensive - requiring large capital investment but less labor

Etymology

Formed from 'labour' (from Latin 'labor', meaning work) and 'intensive', reflecting high labor input.
