cytophilic antibody

1. NOUN

/ ˌsaɪ.toʊˈfɪl.ɪk ˈæn.tɪˌbɑː.di / (AmE), / ˌsaɪ.təʊˈfɪl.ɪk ˈæn.tɪˌbɒd.i / (BrE)
an antibody that binds to certain cells due to the properties of its Fc portion, independently of its specific antigen affinity
antibody with affinity for certain cells
medical

Examples

The presence of cytophilic antibody is a key factor in certain immune responses.
Researchers observed that the cytophilic antibody bound more effectively to specific cell types.
Some allergic reactions are triggered by the interaction between a cytophilic antibody and immune cells.

Synset Examples

Cytophilic antibodies are responsible for the allergic immunological response of the organism.

Etymology

From Greek kytos (cell) + -philic (having an affinity for) + antibody.
