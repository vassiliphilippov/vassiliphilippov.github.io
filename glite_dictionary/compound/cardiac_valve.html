cardiac valve

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑr.di.æk vælv / (AmE), / ˈkɑː.di.æk vælv / (BrE)
A valve in the heart that regulates one-way flow of blood between its chambers and major vessels.
heart valve controlling blood flow
medical

Examples

The surgeon inspected the patient’s cardiac valve for signs of malfunction.
A defective cardiac valve can lead to serious circulatory problems.
Doctors often use echocardiograms to assess the function of each cardiac valve.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heart valve - any valve in the heart controlling blood flow

Etymology

From Latin 'cardiacus' (pertaining to the heart) and 'valva' (leaf of a door, valve).
