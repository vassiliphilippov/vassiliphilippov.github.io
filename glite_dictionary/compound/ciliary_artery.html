ciliary artery

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪl.i.ɛr.i ˈɑːr.t̬ɚ.i / (AmE), / ˈsɪl.i.ə.ri ˈɑː.tər.i / (BrE)
any of several arteries that supply blood to the choroid coat and other structures of the eye
artery supplying blood to parts of the eye
medical

Examples

The ophthalmologist examined the ciliary artery for signs of disease during the eye surgery.
Damage to a ciliary artery can impair vision by reducing blood flow to the choroid.
Inflammation involving a ciliary artery may lead to severe ocular discomfort and visual disturbances.

Etymology

From Latin 'ciliaris' (pertaining to the eyelid or eyelashes) and 'arteria' (artery).
