white pepper

1. NOUN

/ waɪt ˈpɛpər / (AmE), / waɪt ˈpɛpə / (BrE)
Pepper made from ripe, husked peppercorns, usually ground and used as a spice in cooking. It has a milder flavor and lighter color than black pepper.
spice from ground, husked peppercorns

Examples

She added a pinch of white pepper to the soup for extra flavor.
Many Chinese dishes call for white pepper instead of black pepper.
Some people prefer white pepper because it doesn't leave dark specks in light-colored sauces.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: black pepper - spice made from ground whole peppercorns
