full of bluster

1. ADJ

/ fʊl əv ˈblʌstər / (AmE), / fʊl əv ˈblʌstə / (BrE)
using loud, aggressive, or boastful talk with little real meaning or substance
boastful or aggressive with little substance

Examples

The candidate’s speech was full of bluster but offered no real solutions.
Her response was full of bluster, trying to hide the lack of evidence.
Their threats were full of bluster and no one took them seriously.

Synset Examples

His speeches are full of bluster, like a Donald Trumpet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bluster - loud, aggressive, or boastful talk
  • Synonym: full of hot air - someone who talks a lot but says little of value
