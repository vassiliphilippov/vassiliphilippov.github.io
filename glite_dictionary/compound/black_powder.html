black powder

1. NOUN

/ blæk ˈpaʊdɚ / (AmE), / blæk ˈpaʊdə / (BrE)
an early form of gunpowder, composed of saltpeter, charcoal, and sulfur, used historically in firearms and fireworks
early gunpowder mixture

Examples

The discovery of black powder revolutionized warfare in medieval China and Europe.
All weapons in the collection use black powder as their propellant.
The mixture for black powder must be handled with great care due to its explosive nature.

Synset Examples

The spread of black powder across Asia from China is widely attributed to the Mongols.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gunpowder - an explosive powder used in firearms and fireworks
