wood avens

1. NOUN

/ wʊd ˈævənz / (AmE), / wʊd ˈævənz / (BrE)
a Eurasian plant (Geum urbanum) with hairy stems, small yellow flowers, and an astringent root once used in medicine
Eurasian plant with yellow flowers

Examples

Wood avens grows commonly in shaded woods and hedgerows across Europe.
Traditional herbalists once valued the root of wood avens for its supposed medicinal properties.
The small yellow flowers of wood avens add a splash of color to the forest floor in spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Geum urbanum - botanical name for wood avens
