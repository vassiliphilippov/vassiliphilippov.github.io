tooth fairy

1. NOUN

/ ˈtuθ ˌfɛri / (AmE), / ˈtuːθ ˌfeəri / (BrE)
a mythical being in children's folklore who is said to leave money under a child's pillow in exchange for a lost baby tooth
mythical figure who collects children's lost teeth

Examples

When Lily lost her first tooth, she hoped the tooth fairy would visit her that night.
Some parents leave a small note from the tooth fairy along with the money.
Children often look forward to the tooth fairy coming as a special tradition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Santa Claus - mythical figure who brings gifts to children at Christmas

2. NOUN

/ tuθ ˈfɛri / (AmE), / tuːθ ˈfeəri / (BrE)
a mythical figure in children's folklore who is believed to visit children when they lose a baby tooth, replacing it with a small gift or money while they sleep
mythical figure who exchanges lost teeth for gifts

Examples

When Anna lost her first tooth, she placed it under her pillow for the tooth fairy.
Many children eagerly anticipate a visit from the tooth fairy after losing a tooth.
The story of the tooth fairy is told in many families across the world.

Synset Examples

The loose-toothed child was excited for the tooth fairy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Santa Claus - mythical figure who brings Christmas gifts to children
