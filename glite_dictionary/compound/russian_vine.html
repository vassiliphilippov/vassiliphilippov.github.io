Russian vine

1. NOUN

/ ˈrʌʃən vaɪn / (AmE), / ˈrʌʃən vaɪn / (BrE)
A fast-growing perennial climbing plant (Fallopia baldschuanica), with twining stems and clusters of fragrant greenish or white flowers, native to western China and Russia.
fast-growing perennial climbing plant

Examples

Russian vine is popular in gardens for quickly covering fences and walls.
The Russian vine produced abundant clusters of small, fragrant flowers throughout the summer.
Be careful where you plant Russian vine, as it can become invasive if not controlled.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mile-a-minute - another name for Russian vine, emphasizing its rapid growth
