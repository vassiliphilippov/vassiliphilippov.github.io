buyout bid

1. NOUN

/ ˈbaɪ.aʊt bɪd / (AmE), / ˈbaɪ.aʊt bɪd / (BrE)
an offer to purchase all of an individual's or entity's shares or holdings in a company, typically as part of an acquisition
an offer to buy all of someone's holdings in a company

Examples

The company received a buyout bid from a major competitor last month.
Share prices surged after news broke of a potential buyout bid.
She rejected the initial buyout bid as too low for her holdings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: takeover bid - an offer to purchase a controlling interest in a company
