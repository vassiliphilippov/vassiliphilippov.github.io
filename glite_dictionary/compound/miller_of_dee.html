Miller of Dee

1. PROPN

/ ˈmɪlər əv diː / (AmE), / ˈmɪlə əv diː / (BrE)
a character representing contentment with a simple, cheerful life, based on a traditional English song
character symbolizing simple contentment
british

Examples

He always reminded me of the Miller of Dee, happy with whatever he had.
In her old age, she lived like the Miller of Dee, savoring life's simple moments.
Whenever John faced difficulties, he tried to keep the attitude of the Miller of Dee.

Synset Examples

He fancied himself a Miller of Dee, content with his simple life.
Like the Miller of Dee, she found joy in everyday tasks.

Etymology

From an English folk song, 'The Miller of Dee', referring to a miller living by the River Dee in Chester.
