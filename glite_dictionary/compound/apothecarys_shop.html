apothecary's shop

1. NOUN

/ əˈpɑθəˌkɛriz ʃɑp / (AmE), / əˈpɒθəkəriːz ʃɒp / (BrE)
a shop, especially in the past, where medicines and other goods were prepared and sold by an apothecary
historical shop selling medicines
archaic

Examples

In the 18th century, towns often had at least one apothecary's shop on the main street.
You could buy both medicines and household remedies at the local apothecary's shop.
The old sign above the door reads 'apothecary's shop,' reminding us of the building's original purpose.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pharmacy - a shop or store where medicinal drugs are dispensed and sold

Etymology

From apothecary, ultimately from Greek apothēkē (storehouse) and shop (Old English sceoppa).
