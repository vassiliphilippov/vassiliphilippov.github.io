crow step

1. NOUN

/ kroʊ stɛp / (AmE), / krəʊ stɛp / (BrE)
A step-like projection on the top edge of a gable wall, typically forming a stepped outline on the side of a pitched roof.
step-shaped detail on a gable wall

Examples

The medieval church was notable for its crow steps along the gable end.
Restorers carefully repaired each damaged crow step during the roof's renovation.
Many Flemish buildings feature decorative crow steps as part of their traditional architecture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corbie step - alternative term for crow step in Scottish and northern English dialects

Etymology

So called because the stepped outline resembles a row of perching crows.
