Oriya language

1. NOUN

/ oʊˈriːjə ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ɒˈrɪə ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Indo-Aryan language spoken mainly in the Indian state of Odisha (formerly Orissa) and by the Oriya people
language of Odisha, India

Examples

Many literary works have been translated into Oriya language over the centuries.
He is fluent in Hindi, English, and Oriya language.
The state government promotes the use of Oriya language in official documents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Odia - another name for the Oriya language

Etymology

From 'Oriya', relating to the people and language of Odisha, India; 'language' from Latin 'lingua'.
