light year

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪt ˌjɪr / (AmE), / ˈlaɪt ˌjɪə / (BrE)
the distance that light travels in one year in a vacuum, about 5.88 trillion miles or 9.46 trillion kilometers
distance light travels in a year

Examples

The nearest star to Earth is over four light years away.
Astronomers use the light year to measure vast distances in space.
Our galaxy is about 100,000 light years in diameter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parsec - a unit of distance used in astronomy, equal to about 3.26 light years
  • Synonym: astronomical unit - a unit of length used in astronomy, about 149.6 million kilometers, the average distance from Earth to the Sun

Etymology

Formed from 'light' (referring to the speed of light) and 'year' (a period of twelve months).

2. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪt ˌjɪɚ / (AmE), / ˈlaɪt ˌjɪə / (BrE)
A figurative term meaning a very large distance or gap, especially in progress, development, or quality.
very great distance or gap (figurative)

Examples

When it comes to customer service, their company is light years ahead of its competitors.
Technology in this field has advanced light years over the past decade.
Her thinking is light years ahead of her peers.

Synset Examples

In terms of innovation, this company is light years away from the rest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mile - a great distance or gap (figurative)

Etymology

Extended from the astronomical term describing the distance light travels in a year.
