rag paper

1. NOUN

/ ræɡ ˈpeɪ.pɚ / (AmE), / ræɡ ˈpeɪ.pə / (BrE)
Paper made wholly or partly from textile rags, rather than wood pulp, valued for its durability and quality.
paper made from textile rags

Examples

Archival documents are often printed on rag paper because of its long-lasting quality.
Many antique books were made with rag paper, which resists yellowing over time.
Artists prefer rag paper for their watercolor paintings due to its superior texture and strength.
