negative pole

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɛɡətɪv poʊl / (AmE), / ˈnɛɡətɪv pəʊl / (BrE)
the end of a magnet that points toward the Earth's south when the magnet is freely suspended; also called the south-seeking pole.
pole of a magnet pointing south

Examples

When using a compass, the negative pole of the needle aligns with the Earth's south magnetic pole.
The negative pole is often marked with a minus sign on batteries and electrical diagrams.
If you bring the negative pole of one magnet close to the positive pole of another, they attract.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: positive pole - the end of a magnet pointing north when suspended freely

2. NOUN

/ ˈnɛɡətɪv poʊl / (AmE), / ˈnɛɡətɪv pəʊl / (BrE)
the terminal of a battery or electrical device that is connected to the negative plate and has a deficiency of electric potential relative to the positive pole
terminal of a battery with negative potential

Examples

Be sure to connect the red wire to the positive pole and the black wire to the negative pole.
The corrosion was more severe near the negative pole of the old battery.
Reverse polarity can occur if you accidentally swap the positive and negative pole connections.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: positive pole - the terminal of a battery with positive potential

Etymology

Formed from 'negative' (from Latin 'negativus') and 'pole' (from Greek 'polos', axis).
