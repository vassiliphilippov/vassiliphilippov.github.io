family Anguillidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli æŋˌɡwɪˈlɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli æŋˌwɪˈlɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of eels that live in fresh water as adults but migrate to the sea to spawn
a family of migratory freshwater eels

Examples

The family Anguillidae includes species such as the European eel and the American eel.
Scientists have studied the migration patterns of the family Anguillidae for decades.
Young eels of the family Anguillidae can live for many years in rivers before returning to the sea.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eel - a long, snake-like fish

Etymology

From scientific Latin Anguillidae, based on Latin anguilla (eel).
