second base

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛkənd ˌbeɪs / (AmE), / ˈsɛkənd ˌbeɪs / (BrE)
the second of four bases on a baseball field, which must be touched second in order by a runner moving around the bases to score a run
the second of four bases in baseball

Examples

The runner slid into second base just before the tag.
She played second base for her school's softball team.
After hitting a double, he advanced to second base with ease.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: first base - the base which must be touched first by a runner in baseball
  • Degree Higher: third base - the base which must be touched third by a runner in baseball
