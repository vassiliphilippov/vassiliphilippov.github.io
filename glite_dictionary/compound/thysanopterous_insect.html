thysanopterous insect

1. NOUN

/ θaɪˌsænəpˈtɚəs ˈɪnˌsɛkt / (AmE), / θɪˌsænəpˈtɜːrəs ˈɪnsekt / (BrE)
an insect belonging to the order Thysanoptera, commonly known for having slender bodies and fringed wings
insect of the order Thysanoptera

Examples

A thysanopterous insect can cause significant damage to crops by feeding on their leaves and flowers.
When examined under a microscope, the wings of a thysanopterous insect reveal a delicate fringe along the edges.
Many gardeners struggle to control populations of thysanopterous insects in their greenhouses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thrips - common name for insects of the order Thysanoptera

Etymology

From Greek thysanos (fringe) + pteron (wing), referring to their fringed wings.
