Polish language

1. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊ.lɪʃ ˈlæŋ.ɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈpɒ.lɪʃ ˈlæŋ.ɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the West Slavic language spoken primarily in Poland and by Polish communities worldwide
the language of Poland

Examples

Many people in Chicago speak Polish language at home with their families.
She is studying the Polish language to communicate better with her grandparents.
The Polish language uses a unique alphabet with several special characters.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Slavic language - a branch of the Indo-European language family
  • Synonym: Russian language - the Slavic language spoken in Russia

Etymology

From Polish 'polski' (Polish) + English 'language'.
