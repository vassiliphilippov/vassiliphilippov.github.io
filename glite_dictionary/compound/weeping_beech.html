weeping beech

1. NOUN

/ ˈwiːpɪŋ ˌbiːtʃ / (AmE), / ˈwiːpɪŋ ˌbiːtʃ / (BrE)
a cultivated variety of the European beech tree with pendulous, drooping branches often grown for ornamental purposes
variety of beech tree with drooping limbs

Examples

The gardens feature a magnificent weeping beech that provides shade in the summer.
A weeping beech can grow to impressive heights, making it a striking centerpiece in any park.
They planted a young weeping beech along the driveway for its graceful, cascading branches.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: European beech - a deciduous tree native to Europe, Fagus sylvatica
