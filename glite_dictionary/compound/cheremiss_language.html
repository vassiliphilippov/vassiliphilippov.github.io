Cheremiss language

1. NOUN

/ tʃɛrəˈmɪs ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / tʃɛrəˈmɪs ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Finnic language spoken by the Cheremis people, now more commonly known as the Mari language
Finnic language spoken by the Cheremis (Mari) people

Examples

The Cheremiss language is classified as part of the Finnic branch of the Uralic language family.
Many documents and songs were originally written in the Cheremiss language.
Today, the Cheremiss language is more commonly referred to as the Mari language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mari language - the modern name for the Cheremiss language, spoken by the Mari people

Etymology

Cheremiss from Russian 'Cheremisy' (referring to the Mari people); 'language' from Latin 'lingua'.
