humic substance

1. NOUN

/ ˈhjuːmɪk ˈsʌbstəns / (AmE), / ˈhjuːmɪk ˈsʌbstəns / (BrE)
an organic material formed from the decomposition of plant and animal matter, found especially in soil and peat
organic residue from decaying matter

Examples

Humic substance plays a crucial role in improving soil fertility and structure.
Scientists study humic substances to better understand nutrient cycling in ecosystems.
The presence of humic substance in water can affect its color and quality.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: humus - the organic component of soil, formed by the decomposition of plant material

Etymology

From humic (from Latin humus, earth, ground) + substance.
