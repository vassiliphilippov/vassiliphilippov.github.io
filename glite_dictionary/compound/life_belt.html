life belt

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪf bɛlt / (AmE), / ˈlaɪf belt / (BrE)
A belt designed to attach a person to an object as a restraint, to prevent injury, especially during activities such as climbing, construction, or rescue operations.
safety belt for restraint

Examples

Workers must wear a life belt when operating at heights on the construction site.
The climber secured his life belt before starting the ascent.
In hazardous environments, a life belt can make the difference between life and death.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: safety belt - a belt securing a person to prevent injury

2. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪf ˌbɛlt / (AmE), / ˈlaɪf ˌbɛlt / (BrE)
A circular device made of buoyant material, worn around the body to keep a person afloat in water; a life preserver in the form of a ring.
buoyant ring used as a life preserver

Examples

All passengers must wear a life belt during the boat drill.
He grabbed a life belt and threw it to the struggling swimmer.
The safety instructions included how to properly secure a life belt.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: life buoy - a buoyant ring-shaped device used for rescuing people who have fallen into water

Etymology

Formed from 'life' and 'belt', referring to its function and shape.
