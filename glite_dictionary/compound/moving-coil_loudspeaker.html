moving-coil loudspeaker

1. NOUN

/ ˈmuːvɪŋ kɔɪl ˈlaʊdˌspiːkər / (AmE), / ˈmuːvɪŋ kɔɪl ˈlaʊdˌspiːkə / (BrE)
a type of loudspeaker that uses a lightweight diaphragm, a voice coil, and a magnetic field to convert electrical signals into sound
type of loudspeaker using a moving coil

Examples

The vintage radio featured a moving-coil loudspeaker that produced remarkably clear sound for its age.
Most modern home audio systems still use the basic design of the moving-coil loudspeaker.
A moving-coil loudspeaker transforms electrical energy into sound waves through vibration of a diaphragm.

Synset Examples

The dynamic speaker operates on the same basic principle as a dynamic microphone, but in reverse, to produce sound from an electrical signal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dynamic speaker - another term for moving-coil loudspeaker
