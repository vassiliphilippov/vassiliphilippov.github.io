genus Motacilla

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌmoʊ.təˈsɪl.ə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌməʊ.təˈsɪl.ə / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Motacillidae, comprising the wagtails.
type genus of wagtails

Examples

The genus Motacilla includes several species of birds known for their distinctive tail-wagging behavior.
Recent studies have clarified the evolutionary relationships within the genus Motacilla.
Many ornithologists specialize in identifying the various species that belong to the genus Motacilla.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wagtail - a small bird noted for its constantly wagging tail

Etymology

From Latin 'Motacilla', the type genus of wagtails, from 'motacilla' (wagtail).
