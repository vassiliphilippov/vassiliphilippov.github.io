sauce Espagnole

1. NOUN

/ sɔs ɛs.pɑnˈjoʊl / (AmE), / sɔːs ɛs.pænˈjəʊl / (BrE)
A classic French brown sauce made with a base of browned vegetables, tomatoes, and stock, often seasoned with wine.
classic French brown sauce

Examples

The chef prepared a traditional sauce Espagnole to serve with the roast beef.
Learning to make sauce Espagnole is essential in classical French cooking.
A good sauce Espagnole requires patience and careful browning of the vegetables.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: demi-glace - a rich brown sauce used as a base for other sauces

Etymology

From French 'sauce Espagnole' meaning 'Spanish sauce'.

2. NOUN

/ soʊs ɛˌspænˈjoʊl / (AmE), / sɔːs ɛˌspanˈjɒl / (BrE)
A rich brown sauce made with beef stock, thickened with a butter and flour roux, and seasoned with various herbs and flavorings; one of the classic French mother sauces.
classic French brown sauce (culinary)

Examples

The chef prepared the tenderloin with a silky sauce Espagnole to enhance its flavor.
Mastering sauce Espagnole is considered essential in French culinary training.
A traditional beef stew often calls for a generous addition of sauce Espagnole.

Etymology

French, literally 'Spanish sauce'.
