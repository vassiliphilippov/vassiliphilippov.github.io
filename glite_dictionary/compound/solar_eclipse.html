solar eclipse

1. NOUN

/ ˈsoʊlər ɪˈklɪps / (AmE), / ˈsəʊlə ɪˈklɪps / (BrE)
an occasion when the moon passes between the earth and the sun, blocking all or part of the sun's light from reaching the earth
when the moon blocks the sun's light

Examples

The next solar eclipse will be visible in parts of North America.
During the solar eclipse, it became unusually dark for several minutes in the afternoon.
Many schools organized trips to observe the rare solar eclipse safely.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lunar eclipse - an occasion when the earth comes between the sun and the moon, causing the moon to appear darkened

Etymology

From Latin 'solaris' (of the sun) and Greek 'ekleipsis' (an abandonment, an eclipse); 'eclipse' from Greek 'ekleipein' (to leave out, to forsake).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsoʊlər ɪˈklɪps / (AmE), / ˈsəʊlə ɪˈklɪps / (BrE)
an astronomical event in which the moon moves between the earth and the sun, blocking all or part of the sun's light
when the moon blocks the sun from view

Examples

We wore special glasses to view the solar eclipse safely.
The next total solar eclipse will be visible from South America.
Many students gathered outside the school to witness the rare solar eclipse.

Synset Examples

We gathered to watch the solar eclipse together.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lunar eclipse - an astronomical event when earth's shadow covers the moon

Etymology

From Latin 'solaris' (of the sun) and Greek 'ekleipsis' (a leaving out, an eclipse).
