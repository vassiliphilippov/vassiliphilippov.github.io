John's cabbage

1. NOUN

/ dʒɑnz ˈkæb.ɪdʒ / (AmE), / dʒɒnz ˈkæb.ɪdʒ / (BrE)
A showy perennial herb with white flowers; its leaves are sometimes used as edible greens in the southeastern United States.
showy herb, edible leaves (SE United States)

Examples

John's cabbage is commonly found growing in gardens throughout the southeastern United States.
Local chefs often use the leaves of John's cabbage as a substitute for collard greens.
The white flowers of John's cabbage make it an attractive addition to ornamental beds.
