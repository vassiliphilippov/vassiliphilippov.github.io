Chacoan peccary

1. NOUN

/ tʃəˈkoʊ.ən ˈpɛk.ə.ri / (AmE), / tʃəˈkəʊ.ən ˈpɛk.əri / (BrE)
a large wild pig-like mammal native to Paraguay and parts of South America, known as a recently discovered species of peccary
a large wild peccary of South America

Examples

The Chacoan peccary was believed extinct until it was discovered alive in Paraguay.
Scientists are studying the habitat requirements of the Chacoan peccary to support its conservation.
Unlike other peccaries, the Chacoan peccary is specially adapted to the dry scrublands of the Gran Chaco.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peccary - a medium-sized pig-like hoofed mammal of the Americas

Etymology

From the Gran Chaco region (South America) + peccary (from Carib *pakira*).
