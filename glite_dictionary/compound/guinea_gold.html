guinea gold

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɪni ˌɡoʊld / (AmE), / ˈɡɪni ˌɡəʊld / (BrE)
A type of 22-karat gold, traditionally used to mint guinea coins in Britain.
22-karat gold used for guinea coins
british

Examples

The museum’s display included a rare coin made of pure guinea gold dating back to the 18th century.
Jewellers sometimes refer to 22-karat alloys as guinea gold due to their historical use in British coinage.
Because it was composed of guinea gold, the old coin has retained its rich, yellow colour for centuries.

Etymology

Named after the British 'guinea' coin, first minted in 1663 from gold sourced from Guinea, West Africa.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡɪni ɡoʊld / (AmE), / ˈɡɪni ɡəʊld / (BrE)
a type of brass containing enough copper to give it a reddish tint, used in plumbing, hardware, and inexpensive jewelry
reddish brass alloy used in hardware and jewelry

Examples

The pipes were manufactured from guinea gold to ensure a more durable finish.
She wore a bracelet made of guinea gold, which had a reddish gleam.
Cheap jewelry often features guinea gold instead of real gold.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brass - an alloy of copper and zinc, usually yellow in color
