Secretary of State

1. NOUN

/ səˌkrɛtəri əv steɪt / (AmE), / sɪˌkrɛtəri əv steɪt / (BrE)
the head of the U.S. Department of State, responsible for the country's foreign affairs; in some countries, a senior government official of similar rank
head of the U.S. Department of State
american

Examples

Thomas Jefferson served as the first Secretary of State in American history.
The Secretary of State met with foreign leaders to discuss international policy.
The President nominated a new Secretary of State after the previous official resigned.

Synset Examples

the first Secretary of State was Thomas Jefferson

2. NOUN

/ ˌsɛkrəˌtɛri əv steɪt / (AmE), / ˌsɛkrəˌtɛːri əv steɪt / (BrE)
the government official who heads the State Department, responsible for overseeing foreign affairs in the United States
head of the U.S. State Department
american

Examples

The new Secretary of State addressed the nation about developments in foreign policy.
After a long confirmation process, she became the first female Secretary of State in U.S. history.
The role of the Secretary of State has evolved significantly since it was first established.

Synset Examples

the position of Secretary of State was established in 1789
