reflex camera

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈflɛks ˈkæməɹə / (AmE), / rɪˈflɛks ˈkæmərə / (BrE)
a camera that enables the photographer to view and focus the exact scene being photographed, typically by using a mirror system to direct the image to a viewfinder
camera with optical viewfinder system

Examples

He bought a vintage reflex camera to experiment with traditional film photography.
The reflex camera allows you to see exactly what the lens sees before taking the picture.
Many professionals prefer a reflex camera for its accurate framing and focusing.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: single-lens reflex camera - camera using one lens and a mirror to show the exact image to be captured

Etymology

From 'reflex', referring to the mirror's reflecting action, and 'camera', from Latin 'camera' (chamber).
