pig pile

1. NOUN

/ pɪɡ paɪl / (AmE), / pɪɡ paɪl / (BrE)
a playful heap of people, especially children, all piled on top of each other in a chaotic manner
a pile of people playfully heaped together

Examples

After winning the match, the team celebrated with a pig pile in the middle of the field.
At the birthday party, the children made a pig pile on top of the birthday girl.
It took a few minutes for everyone to untangle themselves from the pig pile after the tackle.

Synset Examples

We had a fun pig pile during the sleepover.
After the game, they ended up in a pig pile.
The kids jumped into a pig pile on the grass.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pile-on - a heap formed by people jumping on top of each other, often in celebration or play
