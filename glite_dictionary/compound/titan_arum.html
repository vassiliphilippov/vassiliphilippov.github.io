titan arum

1. NOUN

/ ˈtaɪtən ˈærəm / (AmE), / ˈtaɪtən ˈɑːrəm / (BrE)
a large tropical plant native to Sumatra, notable for its huge malodorous flower, whose spathe can reach several feet in diameter
giant foul-smelling tropical plant

Examples

The titan arum is famous for emitting a strong odor resembling rotting flesh when it blooms.
Botanists traveled from around the world to witness the rare flowering of the titan arum.
A titan arum opened in the botanical garden, attracting thousands of curious visitors eager to see and smell it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corpse flower - another name for titan arum, referring to its foul odor

Etymology

From Latinized form of species name; genus Amorphophallus, species titanum, named for its gigantic size (from Greek 'titan', giant).
