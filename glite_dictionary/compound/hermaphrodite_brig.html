hermaphrodite brig

1. NOUN

/ hɚˈmæf.rəˌdaɪt brɪɡ / (AmE), / hɜːˈmæf.rə.daɪt brɪɡ / (BrE)
a two-masted sailing vessel square-rigged on the foremast and fore-and-aft rigged on the mainmast
two-masted vessel, mixed rigging

Examples

The hermaphrodite brig was well suited for coastal trade in the 19th century.
Sailors admired the maneuverability of the hermaphrodite brig during long voyages.
A beautifully restored hermaphrodite brig now serves as a museum ship in the harbor.

Etymology

From 'hermaphrodite' (having both kinds) and 'brig' (a type of ship), referring to its mixed rigging.
