wagon train

1. NOUN

/ ˈwæɡən treɪn / (AmE), / ˈwæɡən treɪn / (BrE)
A line or group of wagons traveling together, especially across a long distance, for mutual support and protection.
a line of wagons traveling together

Examples

The settlers traveled in a wagon train across the Great Plains to reach their new home.
Security increased as the wagon train approached the unpredictable mountain pass.
During the 19th century, a wagon train was often the safest way to move westward in America.

Synset Examples

we were part of a caravan of almost a thousand camels
they joined the wagon train for safety

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caravan - a group of people, especially traders or pilgrims, traveling together across a desert or through hostile regions
