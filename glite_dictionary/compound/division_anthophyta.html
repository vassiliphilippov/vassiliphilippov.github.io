division Anthophyta

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈvɪʒən ˌænˈθɒfɪtə / (AmE), / dɪˈvɪʒən ˌænˈθɒfɪtə / (BrE)
The group of flowering plants (angiosperms) that produce seeds enclosed within an ovary; classified as a division in some taxonomic systems, also called Magnoliophyta.
division of flowering plants (angiosperms)

Examples

All members of the division Anthophyta produce flowers as part of their reproductive cycle.
Modern agriculture relies heavily on plants belonging to the division Anthophyta.
Botanists classify roses, tulips, and oaks within the division Anthophyta.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: angiosperm - plant with seeds enclosed in an ovary (flowering plant)
  • Antonym: gymnosperm - plant with seeds not enclosed in an ovary

Etymology

From Greek 'anthos' (flower) + 'phyton' (plant).
