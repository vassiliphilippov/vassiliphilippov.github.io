wolf's bane

1. NOUN

/ wʊlfz beɪn / (AmE), / wʊlfs beɪn / (BrE)
a poisonous Eurasian perennial plant with broad rounded leaves, yellow flowers, and a fibrous rootstock, belonging to the genus Doronicum
a poisonous yellow-flowered plant

Examples

The roots of wolf's bane were once believed to have medicinal properties, despite their high toxicity.
Gardeners should handle wolf's bane with care due to its poisonous nature.
In folklore, wolf's bane was reputed to ward off evil spirits and wolves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aconite - a poisonous plant also known as monkshood or wolfsbane

Etymology

From the practice of using poisonous plants to ward off or kill wolves.
