thermionic current

1. NOUN

/ θɜrˌmaɪˈɑnɪk ˈkɝrənt / (AmE), / ˌθɜːmaɪˈɒnɪk ˈkʌrənt / (BrE)
an electric current that flows between two electrodes due to electrons emitted by thermionic emission
current from thermionic emission

Examples

A vacuum tube relies on thermionic current between the cathode and anode to operate properly.
Early electronics used thermionic current to amplify signals before semiconductor devices became widespread.
The strength of thermionic current depends on the temperature of the emitting electrode.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thermionic emission - release of electrons from a heated material
  • Degree Higher: electric current - flow of electric charge

Etymology

From 'thermionic' (relating to emission of electrons by heat) and 'current' (from Latin 'currens', running).
