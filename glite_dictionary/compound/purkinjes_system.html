Purkinje's system

1. NOUN

/ pɚˈkɪndʒiz ˈsɪstəm / (AmE), / pəˈkɪndʒiːz ˈsɪstəm / (BrE)
A network of specialized fibers in the heart that transmits electrical impulses from the atrioventricular node to the ventricles, causing them to contract.
heart's impulse-conducting fiber network
medical

Examples

Abnormalities in the Purkinje's system can lead to serious heart rhythm disorders.
Doctors studied the Purkinje's system to understand the patient's irregular heartbeat.
The Purkinje's system ensures each ventricle contracts efficiently during the cardiac cycle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Purkinje fiber - specialized cardiac muscle fiber transmitting impulses within the heart

Etymology

Named after Czech physiologist Jan Evangelista Purkyně (anglicized as Purkinje), who discovered the fibers in 1839.
