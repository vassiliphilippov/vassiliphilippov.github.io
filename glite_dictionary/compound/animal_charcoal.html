animal charcoal

1. NOUN

/ ˈænɪməl ˈtʃɑːrkoʊl / (AmE), / ˈænɪməl ˈtʃɑːkəʊl / (BrE)
a black material consisting of charred animal bones, used as a black pigment and in purification processes
black material from charred bones used as pigment/purifier

Examples

The artist mixed animal charcoal with oil to create a deep, rich black color for the painting.
In the nineteenth century, animal charcoal was widely used to decolorize sugar during refining.
The water filtration system uses animal charcoal to remove impurities and improve taste.
