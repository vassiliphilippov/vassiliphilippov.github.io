San Francisco Bay

1. PROPN

/ sæn frənˈsɪskoʊ beɪ / (AmE), / sæn frænˈsɪskəʊ beɪ / (BrE)
a large bay of the Pacific Ocean on the coast of western California, forming the natural harbor of the San Francisco area
bay in western California, USA

Examples

The Golden Gate Bridge spans the entrance to San Francisco Bay.
Many birds migrate along the wetlands bordering San Francisco Bay.
Tourists enjoy taking boat tours around San Francisco Bay to see the city skyline.

Etymology

Named after the nearby city of San Francisco, itself from Spanish 'San Francisco' (Saint Francis).
