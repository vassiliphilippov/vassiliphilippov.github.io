cyanide poisoning

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪəˌnaɪd ˈpɔɪzənɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsaɪənaɪd ˈpɔɪznɪŋ / (BrE)
A serious and potentially fatal condition resulting from the ingestion or inhalation of cyanide, commonly found in smoke from fires or industrial chemicals.
poisoning from cyanide exposure
medical

Examples

Firefighters are trained to recognize the symptoms of cyanide poisoning in smoke inhalation victims.
Industrial workers must take precautions to avoid cyanide poisoning when handling chemicals.
Prompt treatment is crucial after cyanide poisoning to prevent serious health consequences.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poisoning - harmful condition caused by a toxic substance
