property line

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɑːpərti laɪn / (AmE), / ˈprɒpəti laɪn / (BrE)
the boundary marking the limits of one piece of property and separating it from an adjoining property
boundary between two properties

Examples

The neighbors disagreed about where the property line should be drawn.
A fence was built along the property line to avoid disputes.
Before planting trees, she checked the property line on the survey map.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boundary - a line marking the limits of an area
  • Synonym: border - the edge or boundary of something, especially land
