black moss

1. NOUN

/ blæk mɑs / (AmE), / blæk mɒs / (BrE)
A type of plant with dense, greenish-grey, hairlike strands that hang from tree branches, found in the southeastern United States and the West Indies.
hairlike plant hanging from trees

Examples

Thick swathes of black moss draped the branches, giving the forest an eerie appearance.
We saw black moss hanging from the oaks as we drove through Louisiana.
Some people use black moss for decorative purposes in floral arrangements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Spanish moss - another common name for the same plant species (Tillandsia usneoides)
