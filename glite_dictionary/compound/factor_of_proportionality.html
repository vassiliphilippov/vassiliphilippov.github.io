factor of proportionality

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæk.tɚ əv prəˌpɔr.ʃəˈnæl.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˈfæk.tər əv prəˌpɔː.ʃəˈnæl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
a constant value by which one quantity is multiplied to obtain a proportional quantity; commonly called the constant k in equations like y = kx
constant in proportional relationship

Examples

The factor of proportionality in this equation determines how y changes as x increases.
In direct variation, the factor of proportionality is often denoted by the letter k.
When converting between units, the factor of proportionality helps ensure the quantities stay equivalent.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: proportionality constant - a constant used to define proportional relationships; synonym of factor of proportionality
