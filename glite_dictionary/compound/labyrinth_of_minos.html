Labyrinth of Minos

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæb.ɹɪn.θ əv ˈmaɪ.nɑs / (AmE), / ˈlæb.ɹɪnθ əv ˈmaɪ.nɒs / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, a vast maze on Crete, built by Daedalus at King Minos's command to imprison the Minotaur.
mythical maze built by Daedalus for King Minos

Examples

Many adventurers sought to escape the Labyrinth of Minos, but few succeeded.
The legend says that Theseus navigated the Labyrinth of Minos to slay the Minotaur.
Archaeologists have long debated the real-world inspiration for the Labyrinth of Minos.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: labyrinth - a complicated network of winding passages like a maze
  • Degree Lower: Minotaur - mythical creature held in the labyrinth

Etymology

From Greek mythology; Minos was a legendary king of Crete, and Daedalus his master craftsman.
