Acts of the Apostles

1. PROPN

/ ˈækts əv ði əˈpɑsəlz / (AmE), / ˈækts əv ði əˈpɒslz / (BrE)
the fifth book of the New Testament, describing the development of the early Christian Church from Christ's Ascension to Paul's visit to Rome
New Testament book on early Christian Church

Examples

Many theologians study the Acts of the Apostles to better understand the history of the early church.
The sermon focused on the missionary journeys described in the Acts of the Apostles.
Some scholars debate who wrote the Acts of the Apostles and its intended audience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: New Testament - the second part of the Christian Bible, recording the life and teachings of Jesus and his earliest followers

Etymology

From the Greek *Praxeis Apostolon* meaning 'deeds of the apostles'.
