genus Tinca

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈtɪŋ.kə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈtɪŋ.kə / (BrE)
A genus of freshwater fish in the family Cyprinidae, whose best-known species is the tench (Tinca tinca).
a genus of freshwater fish (tench)

Examples

The genus Tinca includes only a single extant species, the tench.
Researchers studied the habitat preferences of fish in the genus Tinca.
Members of the genus Tinca are found in freshwater lakes and slow-moving rivers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tench - a species of freshwater fish

Etymology

From Latin 'Tinca' (tench), used in scientific classification.
