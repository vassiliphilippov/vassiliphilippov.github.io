large crabgrass

1. NOUN

/ lɑrdʒ ˈkræbˌɡræs / (AmE), / lɑːdʒ ˈkræbɡrɑːs / (BrE)
a species of crabgrass (Digitaria sanguinalis) native to Europe, grown for hay and often considered a weed in the United States
a European grass species, often a weed

Examples

Large crabgrass can quickly overtake a neglected lawn in the summer months.
Farmers sometimes use large crabgrass as a forage grass for their livestock.
Many gardeners struggle to control large crabgrass because of its rapid growth and spreading habit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crabgrass - a general name for grasses of the Digitaria genus, often considered weeds
