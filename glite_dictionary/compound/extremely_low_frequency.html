extremely low frequency

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˈstriːmli loʊ ˈfriːkwənsi / (AmE), / ɪkˈstriːmli ləʊ ˈfriːkwənsi / (BrE)
a range of electromagnetic frequencies below 3 kilohertz, used especially in communications and scientific measurement
electromagnetic frequency below 3 kHz

Examples

Military submarines use extremely low frequency signals to communicate over long distances underwater.
Scientists study extremely low frequency waves to understand their impact on the Earth's atmosphere.
The radio station is equipped to transmit messages on extremely low frequency bands.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: very low frequency - electromagnetic frequencies from 3 kHz to 30 kHz
