Riot Act

1. NOUN

/ ˈraɪ.ət ˌækt / (AmE), / ˈraɪ.ət ˌækt / (BrE)
a former English law that required an unlawful assembly or mob to disperse after a magistrate read a formal proclamation
historic English law ordering mob dispersal
archaic legal british

Examples

The magistrate read the Riot Act to the unruly crowd, ordering them to disperse immediately.
Under the provisions of the Riot Act, failure to leave could result in severe penalties.
The Riot Act was repealed, but its legacy remains in common English phrases.

Etymology

Refers to the law enacted by the British Parliament in 1715, formally titled 'An Act for preventing tumults and riotous assemblies.'
