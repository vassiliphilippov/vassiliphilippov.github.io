macrocytic anaemia

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæk.roʊˈsɪt.ɪk əˈniː.mi.ə / (AmE), / ˌmæ.krəʊˈsɪt.ɪk əˈniː.mi.ə / (BrE)
A type of anaemia in which the average size of red blood cells is larger than normal.
anaemia with abnormally large red blood cells
medical british

Examples

Vitamin B12 deficiency is a common cause of macrocytic anaemia in adults.
The lab results confirmed a diagnosis of macrocytic anaemia due to folate deficiency.
Patients with macrocytic anaemia may present with fatigue and pallor.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: microcytic anaemia - anaemia in which the average size of red blood cells is smaller than normal

Etymology

From macro- (large) + Greek kytos (cell) + anaemia (lack of blood).
