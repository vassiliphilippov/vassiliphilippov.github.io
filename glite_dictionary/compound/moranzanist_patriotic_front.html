Moranzanist Patriotic Front

1. PROPN

/ ˌmɔːˌrænzəˈnɪst pəˈtriːətɪk frʌnt / (AmE), / ˌmɒːˌrænzəˈnɪst pəˈtriːətɪk frʌnt / (BrE)
a radical leftist group formed in Honduras in the late 1980s that aimed to prevent United States intervention in Honduran affairs
Honduran radical leftist group (1980s)

Examples

The Moranzanist Patriotic Front opposed American influence in Honduras during the late 1980s.
Reports suggested that the Moranzanist Patriotic Front was responsible for several attacks in the region.
Membership in the Moranzanist Patriotic Front was highly secretive and operateed mostly underground.

Etymology

Named after Francisco Morazán, a Honduran national hero and reformer, combined with 'Patriotic Front'.
