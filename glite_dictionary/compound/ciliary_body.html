ciliary body

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪliːˌɛri ˈbɑːdi / (AmE), / ˈsɪliəri ˈbɒdi / (BrE)
the part of the eye between the choroid and the iris that includes the muscles controlling the shape of the lens and produces aqueous humor
part of the eye between choroid and iris
medical

Examples

The ciliary body helps control the focusing of the lens in the human eye.
Inflammation of the ciliary body can lead to serious vision problems.
Doctors examined the patient's ciliary body for signs of disease.

Synset Examples
