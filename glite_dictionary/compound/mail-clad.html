mail-clad

1. ADJ

/ ˈmeɪl klæd / (AmE), / ˈmeɪl klæd / (BrE)
wearing protective mail armor, typically made of interlinked metal rings
wearing mail armor

Examples

The mail-clad knight rode into battle under the banner of his king.
Many warriors in medieval times were mail-clad to protect themselves from sword strikes.
A mail-clad figure emerged from the mist, his armor glinting in the early morning sun.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: armored - protected with armor

Etymology

Formed from 'mail' (armor of rings) and 'clad' (clothed).
