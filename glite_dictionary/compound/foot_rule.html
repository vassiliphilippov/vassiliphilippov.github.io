foot rule

1. NOUN

/ fʊt ruːl / (AmE), / fʊt ruːl / (BrE)
a ruler that is one foot (12 inches) long, typically used for measuring length
a ruler one foot (12 inches) long

Examples

The carpenter used a foot rule to check the length of the board.
During the exam, each student was provided with a foot rule for geometry problems.
She found an old wooden foot rule in her grandfather's toolbox.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ruler - a straight-edged tool for drawing lines and measuring length
