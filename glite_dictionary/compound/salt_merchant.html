salt merchant

1. NOUN

/ sɔlt ˈmɜrtʃənt / (AmE), / sɒlt ˈmɜːtʃənt / (BrE)
a person who makes or trades in salt, especially as a primary business
person who sells or trades salt

Examples

During the Middle Ages, the salt merchant was an influential figure in the local economy.
The salt merchant imported large quantities of salt from distant coastal towns.
As a successful salt merchant, he opened shops in several major cities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: merchant - a person involved in trade or commerce
