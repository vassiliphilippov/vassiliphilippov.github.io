Baja California Peninsula

1. PROPN

/ ˈbɑːhɑː kælɪˈfɔrnjə pəˈnɪnsjələ / (AmE), / ˈbɑːhə ˌkælɪˈfɔːnɪə pəˈnɪnsjələ / (BrE)
a long, narrow, mountainous peninsula in northwestern Mexico, extending south from the U.S. border and separating the Pacific Ocean from the Gulf of California
peninsula in northwest Mexico

Examples

The Baja California Peninsula is famous for its beautiful beaches and diverse marine life.
Many adventure travelers love to drive along the Baja California Peninsula to experience its stunning scenery.
Gray whales migrate to the warm waters off the Baja California Peninsula each winter.

Etymology

From Spanish 'Baja California' meaning 'Lower California', named for its position south of the U.S. state of California.
