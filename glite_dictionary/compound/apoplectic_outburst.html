apoplectic outburst

1. NOUN

/ ˌæpəˈplɛktɪk ˈaʊtbɜrst / (AmE), / ˌapəˈplɛktɪk ˈaʊtbɜːst / (BrE)
an instance of sudden and extreme anger, often expressed loudly or violently
sudden, extreme burst of anger

Examples

His apoplectic outburst during the debate left everyone in stunned silence.
She apologized later for her apoplectic outburst at the team meeting.
The coach's apoplectic outburst on the sidelines was caught on camera.

Synset Examples

His apoplectic outburst surprised everyone at the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: outburst - a sudden release of strong emotion, especially anger

Etymology

From 'apoplectic' (relating to stroke or extreme anger, from Greek 'apoplēktikos'), and 'outburst' (from burst out).
