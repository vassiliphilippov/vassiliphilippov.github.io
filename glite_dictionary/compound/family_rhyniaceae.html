family Rhyniaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæmɪli raɪniˈeɪsiː / (AmE), / ˈfamɪli rʌɪnɪˈiːsiː / (BrE)
A family of extinct, primitive vascular plants from the Paleozoic era, particularly known from early land flora fossils.
an extinct plant family from the Paleozoic

Examples

Fossils of the family Rhyniaceae provide insight into the earliest vascular plants.
Members of the family Rhyniaceae are often studied by paleobotanists for their evolutionary significance.
The family Rhyniaceae thrived during the early Devonian period.

Etymology

From New Latin 'Rhyniaceae', based on the genus 'Rhynia' discovered in Rhynie, Scotland.
