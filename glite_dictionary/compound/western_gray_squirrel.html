western gray squirrel

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɛstɚn ɡreɪ ˈskwɝːrəl / (AmE), / ˈwɛstən ɡreɪ ˈskwɪrəl / (BrE)
a large gray squirrel native to the far western regions of the United States, known for its bushy tail and silvery-gray fur
large gray squirrel native to western U.S.

Examples

The western gray squirrel is commonly found in oak woodlands and pine forests of California.
Unlike its cousin in the east, the western gray squirrel has a noticeably bushier tail.
Wildlife biologists are studying the decline of the western gray squirrel in its native habitat.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: eastern gray squirrel - gray squirrel species native to the eastern United States
