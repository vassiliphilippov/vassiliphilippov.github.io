Podocarpus totara

1. NOUN

/ ˌpoʊdəˈkɑːrpəs toʊˈtɑːrə / (AmE), / ˌpɒdəˈkɑːpəs təʊˈtɑːrə / (BrE)
a large evergreen tree of New Zealand, valued for its hard, reddish timber used in furniture, bridges, and wharves
New Zealand timber tree

Examples

The massive trunk of the Podocarpus totara makes it ideal for building traditional Maori canoes.
Furniture crafted from Podocarpus totara is prized for its durability and attractive color.
The conservation of native Podocarpus totara forests is important for preserving New Zealand's biodiversity.

Etymology

From scientific Latin; 'Podocarpus' from Greek 'pous, podos' (foot) and 'karpos' (fruit); 'totara' from Māori.
