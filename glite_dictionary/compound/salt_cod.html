salt cod

1. NOUN

/ sɑlt kɑd / (AmE), / sɒlt kɒd / (BrE)
codfish that has been preserved by drying and salting, commonly soaked before use in cooking.
codfish preserved by salting and drying

Examples

Traditional dishes like brandade often use salt cod as a primary ingredient.
Before cooking, you must soak salt cod in water to remove the excess salt.
They imported barrels of salt cod from Europe for the winter months.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bacalao - salted and dried cod, especially in Spanish and Portuguese cuisine
