proton accelerator

1. NOUN

/ ˈproʊtɑn əkˌsɛləˌreɪtər / (AmE), / ˈprəʊtɒn əkˌsɛləˌreɪtə / (BrE)
a type of particle accelerator that accelerates protons to high speeds, often for the purpose of colliding them with other particles
device that accelerates protons for collisions

Examples

The Large Hadron Collider is the world's most powerful proton accelerator.
Scientists use a proton accelerator to study the particles that make up atoms.
Building a new proton accelerator requires international cooperation and significant funding.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: particle accelerator - a machine that accelerates charged particles to high energies

Etymology

proton from Greek 'prōtos' (first) and accelerator from Latin 'accelerare' (to hasten).
