mailboat

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪlˌboʊt / (AmE), / ˈmeɪlˌbəʊt / (BrE)
a boat used to carry and deliver mail, especially between places not easily reached by land
boat for carrying mail

Examples

The island receives its supplies and letters by mailboat once a week.
Tourists enjoyed the scenic ride on the mailboat through the coastal waters.
A storm delayed the arrival of the mailboat this morning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ferry - a boat or ship for conveying passengers and goods

Etymology

Formed from 'mail' (letters and parcels sent by post) + 'boat'.
