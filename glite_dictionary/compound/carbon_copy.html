carbon copy

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑrbən ˌkɑpi / (AmE), / ˈkɑːbən ˌkɒpi / (BrE)
A duplicate of a document made by using carbon paper placed between the original and blank sheets.
duplicate made with carbon paper

Examples

The secretary made a carbon copy of each letter for the office records.
Please attach a carbon copy to the invoice for our files.
Before digital technology, contracts were often signed with a carbon copy for all parties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: duplicate - an exact copy of something
  • Antonym: original - the primary version from which copies are made

Etymology

From 'carbon' (the element used in carbon paper) plus 'copy', referring to the method of duplication.
