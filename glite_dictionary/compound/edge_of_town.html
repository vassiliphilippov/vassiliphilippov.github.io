edge of town

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛdʒ əv taʊn / (AmE), / ˈɛdʒ əv taʊn / (BrE)
the outer boundary or limit of a town, where the town ends and the countryside or less developed land begins
outer boundary of a town

Examples

They bought a new house on the edge of town where it was much quieter.
A small supermarket opened at the edge of town last year.
Wildlife is often spotted living near the edge of town where the buildings meet the fields.

Synset Examples

They lived in a small hovel at the edge of town.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: outskirts - the outer parts of a town or city
