Fosbury flop

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɑzˌbɛri flɑp / (AmE), / ˈfɒzbəri flɒp / (BrE)
a high jump technique in which the jumper goes over the bar backwards and head first
high jump technique jumping backwards

Examples

The athlete cleared the bar easily using the Fosbury flop technique.
Since the late 1960s, most high jumpers have adopted the Fosbury flop for better performance.
She practiced the Fosbury flop every day to improve her high jump results.

Etymology

Named after American athlete Dick Fosbury, who popularized the technique in the 1968 Olympics.
