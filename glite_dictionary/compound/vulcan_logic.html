Vulcan logic

1. NOUN

/ ˈvʌl.kən ˈlɑdʒ.ɪk / (AmE), / ˈvʌl.kən ˈlɒdʒ.ɪk / (BrE)
a form of reasoning that is strictly rational and unemotional, often characterized by calm composure; originally associated with the Vulcan species in Star Trek
rational and unemotional reasoning; Star Trek reference

Examples

She responded to the heated argument with pure Vulcan logic, refusing to get emotional.
During negotiations, his Vulcan logic kept the discussions productive and focused.
When rumors spread at work, her Vulcan logic prevented her from jumping to conclusions.

Synset Examples

He approached the problem with his typical Vulcan logic.
His Vulcan-like calmness was impressive in the crisis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: logic - reasoning conducted according to strict principles

Etymology

From Vulcan, an alien species in Star Trek known for their strict adherence to logic and reason.
