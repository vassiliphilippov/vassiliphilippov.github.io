selective-serotonin reuptake inhibitor

1. NOUN

/ səˈlɛktɪv səˈroʊtənɪn ˈriːˌʌpteɪk ɪnˈhɪbɪtər / (AmE), / sɪˈlɛktɪv səˈrəʊtənɪn ˈriːˌʌpteɪk ɪnˈhɪbɪtə / (BrE)
a type of antidepressant drug that works by blocking the reabsorption of serotonin in the brain, increasing its availability
type of antidepressant medication
medical

Examples

After her diagnosis of depression, her doctor prescribed a selective-serotonin reuptake inhibitor to help manage her symptoms.
Some patients may experience side effects when starting a selective-serotonin reuptake inhibitor.
Fluoxetine is a widely used selective-serotonin reuptake inhibitor for treating mood disorders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antidepressant - drug used to relieve depression

Etymology

Formed from 'selective', 'serotonin', and 'reuptake inhibitor'; 'serotonin' from Latin 'serum' and Greek 'tonos'.
