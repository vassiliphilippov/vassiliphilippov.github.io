muster call

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌstər kɔl / (AmE), / ˈmʌstə kɔːl / (BrE)
an official calling of names of military personnel at a military assembly to check attendance or readiness
military assembly roll call

Examples

The sergeant blew his whistle to signal the muster call at dawn.
Every soldier was expected to be present at the muster call each morning.
The muster call ensured all personnel were accounted for during the drill.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: roll call - the reading aloud of a list of names to establish who is present

Etymology

From 'muster,' meaning an assembly of troops (from Old French *mostrer*, to show), and 'call,' meaning to summon.
