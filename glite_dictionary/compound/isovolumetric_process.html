isovolumetric process

1. NOUN

/ ˌaɪsoʊvəˈluːmətrɪk ˈprɑːses / (AmE), / ˌaɪsəʊvəˈluːmətrɪk ˈprəʊsɛs / (BrE)
a thermodynamic process in which the volume of a closed system remains constant while other properties, such as pressure or temperature, may change
thermodynamic process at constant volume

Examples

During an isovolumetric process, the pressure of the gas may change, but its volume remains fixed.
Heating a gas in a sealed rigid container is an example of an isovolumetric process.
The concept of an isovolumetric process is fundamental in understanding how engines operate under constant volume conditions.

Synset Examples

In practice, a gas that receives heat in an isochoric process is a constant volume heating and at the same time it is a constant volume pressurization; a gas that looses heat in an isochoric process is a constant volume cooling and at the same time it is a constant volume depressurization.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: isochoric process - thermodynamic process at constant volume (synonym)

Etymology

From iso- (equal, same) + volumetric (relating to volume); used in scientific literature since the 19th century.
