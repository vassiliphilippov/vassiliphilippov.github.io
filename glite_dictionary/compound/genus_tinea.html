genus Tinea

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs tɪˈniː.ə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs tɪˈniː.ə / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Tineidae, which includes the common clothes moths.
genus of clothes moths

Examples

The genus Tinea includes several moths known for damaging natural fibers in clothing.
Scientists discovered a new species within the genus Tinea in South America.
Many household infestations are caused by moths belonging to the genus Tinea.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Tineidae - family of moths including genus Tinea and clothes moths

Etymology

From Latin 'tinea', meaning 'moth' or 'bookworm'.
