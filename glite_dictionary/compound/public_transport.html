public transport

1. NOUN

/ ˈpʌblɪk ˈtrænspɔrt / (AmE), / ˈpʌblɪk ˈtrænspɔːt / (BrE)
a system of buses, trains, etc. that is available for use by the general public, usually for a fare
system of transport for the public

Examples

Many cities encourage the use of public transport to reduce traffic congestion.
She prefers to use public transport instead of driving her car to work every day.
Improvements to public transport can make commuting easier for everyone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mass transit - system of public transportation for large numbers of people
  • Antonym: private transport - means of transport used by individuals for personal purposes

2. NOUN

/ ˈpʌblɪk ˈtrænspɔrt / (AmE), / ˈpʌblɪk ˈtrænspɔːt / (BrE)
transportation services such as buses, trains, and subways that are available for use by the general public, usually for a fee.
transportation available for the public

Examples

With improved public transport, commuting across the city has become much easier.
Students are encouraged to use public transport to reduce traffic congestion near the school.
The city is investing heavily in public transport to support its growing population.

Synset Examples

They used public transport to reach the pride parade together.
The area is well-covered by the city's public transport.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mass transit - transport system for large numbers of people in a city
  • Antonym: private transport - personal means of transportation not shared with the public
