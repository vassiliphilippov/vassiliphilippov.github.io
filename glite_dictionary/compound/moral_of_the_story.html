moral of the story

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɔrəl əv ðə ˈstɔri / (AmE), / ˈmɒrəl əv ðə ˈstɔːri / (BrE)
the lesson or principle that a story, fable, or event is intended to convey
lesson or message from a story

Examples

At the end of the fairy tale, the teacher asked the class what the moral of the story was.
If there is a moral of the story, it’s that kindness is always rewarded.
He summarized the moral of the story to help the children remember its lesson.

Synset Examples

He shared the moral of the story at the end.
The moral of the story teaches us about integrity.
The moral of the story is to always be honest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lesson - something that can be learned from an experience or story
  • Synonym: message - the underlying meaning or theme of a story
