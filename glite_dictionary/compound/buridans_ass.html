Buridan's ass

1. NOUN

/ bʊˈrɪdənz æs / (AmE), / bjʊˈrɪdənz æs / (BrE)
a situation in which a person is unable to choose between two equally attractive options, resulting in indecision or inaction
philosophical dilemma of indecision between two equal choices

Examples

He found himself in a Buridan's ass scenario, unable to pick between two appealing job offers.
The committee reached a Buridan's ass moment, unable to agree on either budget proposal.
Whenever I face equally tempting options, I feel like Buridan's ass—stuck in endless hesitation.

Synset Examples

Deciding where to eat felt like a Buridan's ass situation.
He faced a classic Buridan's ass dilemma choosing between two jobs.
Her indecision over which movie to watch was a true Buridan's ass moment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catch-22 - a difficult situation from which there is no escape because of mutually conflicting or dependent conditions
  • Synonym: dilemma - a situation where a difficult choice has to be made between two or more alternatives
  • Antonym: decision - a conclusion or resolution reached after consideration; as an antonym, the act of making a choice

Etymology

Named after the French philosopher Jean Buridan (c. 1300–c. 1358), who used the fable of an ass unable to choose between two identical bales of hay to illustrate philosophical paradoxes of free will and decision-making.
