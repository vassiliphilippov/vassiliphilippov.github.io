cross product

1. NOUN

/ krɑs ˈprɑdʌkt / (AmE), / krɒs ˈprɒdʌkt / (BrE)
A vector that is the result of multiplying two vectors together in three-dimensional space, producing a third vector perpendicular to both.
vector result of two vectors

Examples

The cross product of the two vectors points in a direction perpendicular to the plane they span.
Engineers often use the cross product to calculate torque in mechanical systems.
In physics class, we learned how to find the cross product of force and displacement vectors.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dot product - scalar product of two vectors
