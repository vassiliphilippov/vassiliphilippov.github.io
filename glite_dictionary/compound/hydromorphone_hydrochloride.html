hydromorphone hydrochloride

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪdroʊˈmɔːrfoʊn ˌhaɪdrəˈklɔːraɪd / (AmE), / ˌhaɪdrəʊˈmɔːfəʊn ˌhaɪdrəˈklɔːraɪd / (BrE)
a powerful narcotic analgesic drug used to relieve moderate to severe pain, commonly known by the trade name Dilaudid
narcotic pain-relieving drug (Dilaudid)
medical

Examples

The hospital administered hydromorphone hydrochloride to manage the patient's severe postoperative pain.
Due to its potency, hydromorphone hydrochloride is prescribed only for moderate to severe pain that does not respond to other treatments.
Doctors must monitor patients closely when treating them with hydromorphone hydrochloride to prevent potential misuse or addiction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hydromorphone - the base form of hydromorphone hydrochloride, a narcotic analgesic
  • Degree Lower: morphine - a naturally occurring narcotic used for pain relief

Etymology

From 'hydromorphone', a derivative of morphine, plus 'hydrochloride' referring to its salt form.
