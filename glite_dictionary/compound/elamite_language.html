Elamite language

1. NOUN

/ ˈiː.ləˌmaɪt ˈlæŋ.ɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈɛl.əˌmaɪt ˈlæŋ.ɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
an extinct ancient language of uncertain origin, formerly spoken by the Elamites in what is now southwestern Iran
extinct ancient language of the Elamites

Examples

Few inscriptions in the Elamite language have survived from ancient Elam.
Scholars find the origins of the Elamite language difficult to classify among known families.
He studied tablets written in the Elamite language while researching ancient Iran.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sumerian language - an ancient language once spoken in southern Mesopotamia
  • Synonym: Akkadian language - an ancient Semitic language formerly spoken in Mesopotamia

Etymology

From 'Elamite', relating to ancient Elam, a civilization of southwestern Iran.
