open account

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊpən əˈkaʊnt / (AmE), / ˈəʊpən əˈkaʊnt / (BrE)
an arrangement in which goods or services are supplied with the agreement that payment will be made at a later date
arrangement for deferred payment

Examples

He purchased the equipment on an open account with payment due in sixty days.
The company typically extends open account terms to trusted clients.
Disputes sometimes arise when balances remain unpaid on an open account.

2. NOUN

/ ˈoʊ.pən əˈkaʊnt / (AmE), / ˈəʊ.pən əˈkaʊnt / (BrE)
An arrangement in which goods or services are supplied by a business with payment to be made at a later date; credit extended to a customer.
credit arrangement for delayed payment

Examples

The supplier agreed to sell the materials on an open account to help the new business manage its cash flow.
Many companies prefer doing business on an open account to simplify transactions and build trust.
Because the client had a good payment history, the bank offered them an open account for future purchases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: credit account - an account allowing delayed payment for purchases
