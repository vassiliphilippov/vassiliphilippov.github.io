closed shop

1. NOUN

/ kloʊzd ʃɑp / (AmE), / kləʊzd ʃɒp / (BrE)
A company or workplace where only members of a labor union may be employed.
workplace hiring only union members

Examples

The factory operated as a closed shop until new labor laws were introduced.
Some employees opposed switching from a closed shop to an open shop agreement.
Unions fought hard to maintain the closed shop policy in the construction industry.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: open shop - workplace where employees are not required to join a union
