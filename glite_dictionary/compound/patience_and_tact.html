patience and tact

1. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪʃəns ənd tækt / (AmE), / ˈpeɪʃəns ənd tækt / (BrE)
the ability to remain calm and considerate, especially when dealing with delicate or difficult situations
calmness and consideration in difficult situations

Examples

Good teachers often need patience and tact to manage their classrooms effectively.
Negotiating the agreement took considerable patience and tact from both sides.
She handled the criticism with patience and tact, turning a tense situation into a constructive discussion.

Synset Examples

Being a diplomat requires patience and tact.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: patience - the ability to accept delay or trouble without frustration
  • Synonym: tact - sensitivity in dealing with others or difficult issues
