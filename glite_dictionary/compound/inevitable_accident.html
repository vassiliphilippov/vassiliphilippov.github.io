inevitable accident

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈɛvətəbəl ˈæksɪdənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈɛvɪtəbəl ˈæksɪdənt / (BrE)
An unexpected and unavoidable event, especially caused by natural forces, that disrupts the normal course of events and cannot be prevented by reasonable care.
unavoidable disruptive event, often natural

Examples

The storm was considered an inevitable accident for which no one could be held responsible.
Her property insurance did not cover damage resulting from an inevitable accident.
In law, an inevitable accident is recognized as a defense when harm could not be prevented despite all precautions.

Synset Examples

he discovered that his house was not insured against acts of God

Related Terms

  • Synonym: act of God - an event caused by natural forces beyond human control
