Girl Scouts

1. NOUN

/ ɡɜrl skaʊts / (AmE), / ɡɜːl skaʊts / (BrE)
a youth organization for girls and young women, founded in 1912, promoting character development, leadership, and citizenship
youth organization for girls

Examples

My daughter loves being part of the Girl Scouts and attends weekly meetings.
The Girl Scouts sold cookies to raise money for their camping trip.
Many lifelong friendships are formed through participation in the Girl Scouts.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Boy Scouts - a youth organization for boys; counterpart to Girl Scouts
  • Synonym: Girl Guides - equivalent organization in many countries outside the United States

Etymology

Founded in the United States in 1912, modeled on the Boy Scouts movement established by Baden-Powell.
