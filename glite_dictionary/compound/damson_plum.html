damson plum

1. NOUN

/ ˈdæm.zən plʌm / (AmE), / ˈdam.zən plʌm / (BrE)
a small, dark purple plum from a variety of tree cultivated for its edible fruit
a variety of small, dark plum

Examples

The damson plum is often used to make delicious jams and preserves.
When ripe, the fruit of the damson plum has a tart flavor that is popular in desserts.
They planted a damson plum tree in their garden to enjoy fresh fruit every summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plum - an edible, fleshy fruit of the genus Prunus

Etymology

From Middle English 'damascene', from Latin 'damascenum' (plum of Damascus).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdæm.zən plʌm / (AmE), / ˈdam.zən plʌm / (BrE)
a small, oval, dark purple plum or the tree that bears it, valued for its tart fruit used in cooking and preserves
a type of small, dark purple plum or its tree

Examples

She made a delicious jam from freshly picked damson plums.
The damson plum tree in our garden produces fruit every summer.
Many recipes call for the tart flavor of damson plums in desserts and preserves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plum - a sweet, fleshy, stone fruit

Etymology

From Middle English 'damasin', from Latin 'damascenum' (of Damascus), as the fruit was believed to originate from Damascus.

3. NOUN

/ ˈdæm.sən plʌm / (AmE), / ˈdæm.zən plʌm / (BrE)
a small, dark purple variety of plum from the damson tree, often used for preserving and cooking
dark purple variety of plum

Examples

She made a delicious jam from fresh damson plums picked in her garden.
The tree was heavy with ripe damson plums in late summer.
I prefer the tart flavor of damson plums to other varieties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plum - a sweet, fleshy, stone fruit, typically purple, red, or yellow

Etymology

From Middle English damascene, from Latin *prunum damascenum* (plum of Damascus) referring to the Syrian city.
