Hadean aeon

1. NOUN

/ heɪˈdiː.ən ˈiː.ɑːn / (AmE), / heɪˈdiː.ən ˈiː.ən / (BrE)
The earliest geological eon in Earth's history, lasting from about 4,600 million years ago to 3,800 million years ago, before any evidence of life.
earliest geological eon of Earth

Examples

Scientists believe that the Hadean aeon was characterized by intense volcanic activity and frequent impacts from space debris.
No rocks are known from the Hadean aeon because the Earth's surface was constantly being reshaped.
The Hadean aeon ended before any evidence of life could be found in the geological record.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Archean eon - the eon following the Hadean, marked by the earliest evidence of life

Etymology

From Greek 'Hades' (the underworld), referencing the hellish conditions of the early Earth, plus 'aeon' (age, long period).
