backie ride

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæki raɪd / (AmE), / ˈbæki raɪd / (BrE)
a ride as a passenger on the back of a bicycle or motorbike, typically sitting behind the person steering
ride on the back of a bicycle or motorbike
informal

Examples

She gave her little brother a backie ride home after school.
Getting a backie ride from a friend was the easiest way to get across town.
We laughed as I tried to balance during my first backie ride through the city.

Synset Examples

A backie ride through the park was the highlight of the day.

Etymology

From 'backie' (slang for a ride given on the back of a bicycle) + 'ride'.
