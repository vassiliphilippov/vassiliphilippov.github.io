regional anatomy

1. NOUN

/ ˈriːdʒənəl əˈnætəmi / (AmE), / ˈriːdʒənəl əˈnætəmi / (BrE)
the branch of anatomy that studies specific regions or divisions of the body by examining the relationships between structures within those areas
study of anatomy by body regions
medical

Examples

Medical students often begin by learning regional anatomy to understand how body structures relate in specific areas.
A detailed knowledge of regional anatomy is essential for surgeons performing complex operations.
The textbook included diagrams to help students visualize regional anatomy in the head and neck.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: systemic anatomy - the study of anatomy by body systems
