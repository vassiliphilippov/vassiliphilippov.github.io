hypothalamic releasing factor

1. NOUN

/ haɪpəˌθæləmɪk rɪˈlisɪŋ ˈfæktɚ / (AmE), / haɪpəˌθæləmɪk rɪˈliːsɪŋ ˈfæktə / (BrE)
any of several hormones secreted by the hypothalamus that stimulate the release of specific hormones from the anterior pituitary gland
hormone stimulating pituitary secretion
medical

Examples

The hypothalamic releasing factor stimulates the anterior pituitary to secrete thyroid-stimulating hormone.
Researchers measured the levels of each hypothalamic releasing factor in the blood samples.
A deficiency in a hypothalamic releasing factor can disrupt normal endocrine system function.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: releasing hormone - hormone from the hypothalamus that stimulates the pituitary

Etymology

From Greek 'hypo-' (under) + 'thalamus' (inner chamber) and 'releasing' (to set free) + 'factor' (something that actively contributes to a result).
