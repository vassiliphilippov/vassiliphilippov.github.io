credit union

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɛdɪt ˌjunjən / (AmE), / ˈkrɛdɪt ˌjuːniən / (BrE)
a cooperative financial institution owned and operated by its members, offering savings accounts and loans at favorable rates
member-owned cooperative financial institution

Examples

After joining the credit union, she received a low-interest loan for her new car.
Many teachers prefer to save their money at a credit union instead of a traditional bank.
The local credit union offers its members better interest rates and helpful financial advice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bank - a financial institution licensed to receive deposits and make loans

Etymology

From 'credit' (borrowing or lending capacity) and 'union' (an association or cooperative group).
