high altar

1. NOUN

/ haɪ ˈɔltɚ / (AmE), / haɪ ˈɔːltə / (BrE)
the principal altar in a church, usually situated at the eastern end and used for the celebration of the main religious services.
main altar in a church

Examples

The priest conducted the service at the high altar beneath the stained glass windows.
Pilgrims gathered before the high altar to pray and light candles.
The ornate carvings on the high altar were restored to their original beauty.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: altar - a table or flat-topped block for religious rites
