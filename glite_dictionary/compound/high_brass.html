high brass

1. NOUN

/ haɪ bræs / (AmE), / haɪ brɑːs / (BrE)
a type of brass alloy that typically contains about 35% zinc and 65% copper
brass alloy with about 35% zinc

Examples

The valves are made from high brass for increased durability.
She preferred working with high brass due to its bright, yellow color.
The manufacturer uses high brass in their premium instruments for better quality.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: low brass - brass alloy with a lower zinc content (around 20%)
