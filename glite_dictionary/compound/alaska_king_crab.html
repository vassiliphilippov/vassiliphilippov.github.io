Alaska king crab

1. NOUN

/ əˈlæskə kɪŋ kræb / (AmE), / əˈlæskə kɪŋ kræb / (BrE)
A large edible crab found in northern Pacific waters, especially off the coasts of Alaska and Japan.
large edible crab of northern Pacific

Examples

The price of Alaska king crab has risen significantly due to overfishing and increased demand.
We enjoyed a delicious meal of steamed Alaska king crab on our trip to Juneau.
Most seafood lovers consider Alaska king crab a delicacy for its sweet, tender meat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: king crab - large edible crab of cold northern waters

2. NOUN

/ əˈlæskə kɪŋ kræb / (AmE), / əˈlɑːskə kɪŋ kræb / (BrE)
The edible meat obtained from a large cold-water crab species, especially prized for its sweet, tender leg meat.
meat from a large cold-water crab

Examples

She ordered Alaska king crab legs with melted butter for dinner.
The restaurant is famous for its fresh Alaska king crab dishes.
Harvesting Alaska king crab is considered one of the most dangerous jobs in the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: king crab - large edible crab found in cold northern seas
