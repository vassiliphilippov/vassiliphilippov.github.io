embryonal carcinosarcoma

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛmbriˈoʊnəl ˌkɑːrkɪnoʊsɑːrˈkoʊmə / (AmE), / ˌɛmbriˈəʊnəl ˌkɑːsɪnəʊˌsɑːˈkəʊmə / (BrE)
A malignant tumor made up of immature, undifferentiated cells, typically showing both carcinomatous and sarcomatous features.
malignant tumor of immature cells
medical

Examples

The biopsy confirmed that the mass was an embryonal carcinosarcoma requiring aggressive treatment.
Researchers are studying new therapies to improve outcomes for patients diagnosed with embryonal carcinosarcoma.
Because embryonal carcinosarcoma is rare, case studies are important for understanding its progression.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carcinosarcoma - a malignant tumor with both carcinoma and sarcoma cells

Etymology

From 'embryonal' (immature, fetal-like) and 'carcinosarcoma' (cancer with both carcinoma and sarcoma components), both from Greek roots.
