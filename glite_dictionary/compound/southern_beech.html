southern beech

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌðərn bitʃ / (AmE), / ˈsʌðən biːtʃ / (BrE)
any of several species of beech trees of the Southern Hemisphere, typically with small, evergreen leaves, especially belonging to the genus Nothofagus.
beech tree of the Southern Hemisphere

Examples

The forests of New Zealand are dominated by southern beech trees.
Several species of southern beech are important components of the Chilean landscape.
Scientists study the distribution of southern beech to learn about ancient continental drift.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beech - a deciduous tree of the genus Fagus, native to temperate Europe, Asia, and North America
