Ouse River

1. PROPN

/ uːz ˈrɪvər / (AmE), / uːz ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
a river in northeastern England that flows southeast to join the River Trent and form the Humber
river in northeastern England

Examples

We took a boat tour along the Ouse River during our visit to York.
Heavy rainfall caused the Ouse River to overflow its banks.
The Ouse River eventually merges with the River Trent to form the Humber Estuary.

Etymology

From Old English 'ūsa' or 'wosa', possibly meaning 'water'.
