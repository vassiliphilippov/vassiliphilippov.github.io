osso buco

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊsoʊ ˈbuːkoʊ / (AmE), / ˌɒsəʊ ˈbuːkəʊ / (BrE)
An Italian dish consisting of braised veal shank slices cooked with olive oil, wine, tomatoes, and typically served with rice or vegetables.
Italian veal shank dish

Examples

The chef prepared a traditional osso buco served with saffron risotto.
I ordered osso buco at the Italian restaurant, and it was incredibly tender.
She learned how to make osso buco during her trip to Milan.

Etymology

From Italian 'osso buco', meaning 'bone with a hole', referring to the marrow bone at the center of the veal shank.
