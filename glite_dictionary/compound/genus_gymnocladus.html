genus Gymnocladus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɛnəs dʒɪmˈnɒklədəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs dʒɪmˈnɒklədəs / (BrE)
A small genus of deciduous trees, native to China and the United States, characterized by paniculate flowers and thick, pulpy seed pods.
genus of deciduous trees (China and US) with thick pods

Examples

The genus Gymnocladus includes the Kentucky coffeetree found in North America.
Several species within the genus Gymnocladus produce large, leathery seed pods.
Botanists are studying the genus Gymnocladus to learn more about its ecological adaptations.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Greek *gymnos* (naked) and *klados* (branch).
