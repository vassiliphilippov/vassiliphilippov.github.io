autoimmune diabetes

1. NOUN

/ ˌɔtoʊɪˈmjun daɪəˈbitiz / (AmE), / ˌɔːtəʊɪˈmjuːn daɪəˈbiːtiːz / (BrE)
A form of diabetes mellitus, usually developing in childhood, in which the body's immune system destroys insulin-producing cells in the pancreas, leading to high blood sugar levels; requires insulin therapy.
type of diabetes caused by immune reaction
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that autoimmune diabetes requires daily insulin injections to manage blood sugar levels.
Recent research has offered new hope for people diagnosed with autoimmune diabetes in childhood.
Symptoms of autoimmune diabetes often include unexplained weight loss and frequent urination.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: type 1 diabetes - diabetes typically developing in childhood, caused by destruction of insulin-producing cells

Etymology

From 'autoimmune', referring to the immune system attacking the body's own cells, and 'diabetes'.
