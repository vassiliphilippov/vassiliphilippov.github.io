leishmaniasis americana

1. NOUN

/ ˌliːʃməˈnaɪəsɪs əˌmɛrɪˈkɑːnə / (AmE), / ˌliːʃməˈnaɪəsɪs əˌmɛrɪˈkɑːnə / (BrE)
A form of leishmaniasis endemic to Mexico, Central America, and South America, characterized by sores limited to the skin and mucous membranes.
a form of leishmaniasis found in the Americas
medical

Examples

Many cases of leishmaniasis americana have been reported among rural populations in Brazil.
The patient was diagnosed with leishmaniasis americana after developing skin sores during travel in Central America.
Effective treatment of leishmaniasis americana often requires several weeks of medication.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cutaneous leishmaniasis - leishmaniasis affecting the skin

Etymology

From New Latin 'leishmania' (the parasite's genus) and 'americana' (of America), indicating geographic origin.
