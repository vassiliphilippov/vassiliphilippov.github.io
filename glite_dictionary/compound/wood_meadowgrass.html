wood meadowgrass

1. NOUN

/ wʊd ˈmɛdoʊˌɡræs / (AmE), / wʊd ˈmɛdəʊˌɡrɑːs / (BrE)
A slender perennial grass (Poa nemoralis) native to shady places in Europe, also found in northeastern America and temperate Asia, often grown as a lawn or pasture grass.
a shade-loving perennial grass (Poa nemoralis)

Examples

The wood meadowgrass thrives in woodland gardens with partial shade.
Botanists studied the spread of wood meadowgrass throughout northern Europe and America.
Her lawn included several species, but the wood meadowgrass grew especially well under the trees.
