Irish whisky

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪrɪʃ ˈwɪski / (AmE), / ˈaɪərɪʃ ˈwɪski / (BrE)
whisky made in Ireland, typically distilled from barley and known for its smooth flavor profile.
whisky produced in Ireland

Examples

He ordered a glass of Irish whisky to celebrate St. Patrick's Day.
The smoothness of Irish whisky sets it apart from other varieties.
Tourists often visit distilleries to learn how Irish whisky is made.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Scotch whisky - whisky made in Scotland
  • Antonym: bourbon - American whiskey made primarily from corn

Etymology

From 'Irish', relating to Ireland, and 'whisky', from Gaelic 'uisge beatha' (water of life).
