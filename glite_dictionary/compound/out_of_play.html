out of play

1. ADJ

/ aʊt əv pleɪ / (AmE), / aʊt ɒv pleɪ / (BrE)
(of a ball) not available to be played during a game due to being outside the permitted area or because play has stopped.
not in play during a game (sports)

Examples

The referee signaled that the ball was out of play after it crossed the sideline.
Once the puck is out of play, all action on the ice must stop immediately.
If the football goes out of play, a throw-in is awarded to the other team.

Synset Examples

a ball that is out of play is dead

Related Terms

  • Antonym: in play - (of a ball) available to be played during a game

2. ADJ

/ aʊt əv pleɪ / (AmE), / aʊt əv pleɪ / (BrE)
(of a ball or player) not in a position to be played or to participate, typically due to leaving the playing area or being unable to continue.
not in a position to be played (sports)

Examples

The referee signaled that the ball was out of play after it crossed the sideline.
Due to his injury, the striker is out of play for the rest of the game.
Once the puck is out of play, the game stops until the next face-off.

Synset Examples

That rule change has put many players out of play.
The ball went out of play after hitting the net.
With the injury, he's now out of play for the season.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: in play - in a position to be played or participating (sports)
