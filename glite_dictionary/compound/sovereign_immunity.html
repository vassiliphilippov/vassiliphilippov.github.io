sovereign immunity

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɑvərəni ɪˈmjunəti / (AmE), / ˈsɒv(ə)rɪn ɪˈmjuːnɪti / (BrE)
The legal doctrine that protects a sovereign government from being sued without its consent.
government protection from lawsuits
legal

Examples

The principle of sovereign immunity means the government cannot be sued unless it agrees to it.
She tried to file a lawsuit against the state, but sovereign immunity prevented her case from going forward.
Legal scholars have debated whether sovereign immunity should be absolute or subject to certain exceptions.

Synset Examples

the doctrine of sovereign immunity originated with the maxim that the king can do no wrong

Related Terms

  • Synonym: immunity - exemption from a legal duty, penalty, or prosecution

Etymology

From 'sovereign' (supreme ruler or government) and 'immunity' (exemption from obligation), used in legal contexts since the 18th century.
