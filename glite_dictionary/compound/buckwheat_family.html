buckwheat family

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌkˌwiːt ˈfæməli / (AmE), / ˈbʌkwiːt ˈfæmɪli / (BrE)
a family of flowering plants (Polygonaceae), chiefly found in the north temperate zone, which includes buckwheats and related species.
plant family including buckwheats

Examples

Many edible plants, including rhubarb, belong to the buckwheat family.
She is studying the distribution of species in the buckwheat family.
The buckwheat family is important for both agriculture and ecology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Polygonaceae - the scientific name for the buckwheat family
