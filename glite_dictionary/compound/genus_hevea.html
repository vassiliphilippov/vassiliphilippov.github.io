genus Hevea

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒiːnəs hɛˈviːə / (AmE), / ˌdʒiːnəs hɛˈviːə / (BrE)
A small genus of South American trees that produce latex, including the rubber tree.
genus of South American latex-producing trees

Examples

The genus Hevea is well known as the primary source of natural rubber.
Botanists identified several species within the genus Hevea during their expedition to the Amazon.
Most latex harvested for rubber production comes from trees belonging to the genus Hevea.

Synset Examples

rubber trees are usually cultivated in plantations

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rubber tree - tree of the genus Hevea that produces latex

Etymology

From Latin genus (birth, origin, group) and Hevea, the genus name given by French botanist Jean Baptiste Aublet, likely from an indigenous word.
