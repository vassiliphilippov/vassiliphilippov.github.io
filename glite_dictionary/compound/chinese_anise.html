Chinese anise

1. NOUN

/ tʃaɪˈniz ˈænɪs / (AmE), / tʃaɪˈniːz ˈænɪs / (BrE)
A small tree native to China and Vietnam, known for its star-shaped, anise-scented fruit used as a spice and in traditional medicine.
tree with aromatic star-shaped fruit, used as spice

Examples

Adding Chinese anise to the stew gives it a distinct spicy aroma.
Her recipe calls for one Chinese anise pod to enhance the flavor of the broth.
Traditional medicine often uses Chinese anise for its digestive benefits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: star anise - spice from the star-shaped fruit of Illicium verum

2. NOUN

/ ˈtʃaɪ.niz ˈæn.is / (AmE), / ˈtʃaɪ.niːz ˈæn.ɪs / (BrE)
Anise-scented, star-shaped fruit or seed used especially in Asian cooking and traditional medicine; also known as star anise.
star-shaped spice used in Asian cuisine

Examples

Many Chinese recipes call for Chinese anise to add a distinctive flavor to the dish.
You can find Chinese anise in the spice section of most Asian grocery stores.
The aroma of Chinese anise filled the kitchen as the soup simmered.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: star anise - Spice made from the star-shaped fruit of Illicium verum
  • Synonym: anise - Aromatic Mediterranean plant and its seeds used as a spice
