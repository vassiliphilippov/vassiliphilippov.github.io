condyloid process

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑn.dɪˌlɔɪd ˈprɑ.sɛs / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.dɪˌlɔɪd ˈprəʊ.ses / (BrE)
the rounded projection on the ramus of the lower jaw (mandible) that forms a joint with the skull
rounded projection on the mandible
medical

Examples

The surgeon carefully examined the condyloid process before proceeding with the jaw reconstruction.
Injury to the condyloid process can affect how the lower jaw moves.
Radiographs revealed a fracture in the condyloid process after the accident.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: condyle - a rounded articular projection at the end of some bones

Etymology

From 'condyle,' from Greek kondylos (knuckle) + 'oid' (resembling) + 'process.'
