going-out-of-business sale

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡoʊɪŋ ˌaʊt əv ˈbɪznəs seɪl / (AmE), / ˌɡəʊɪŋ ˌaʊt əv ˈbɪznəs seɪl / (BrE)
a sale in which a business sells all its assets, typically at reduced prices, because it is closing permanently
sale when a business is closing

Examples

Hundreds of people lined up early for the going-out-of-business sale at the department store.
During the recession, many small shops held a going-out-of-business sale to clear their inventory.
She bought new furniture at a steep discount from a going-out-of-business sale downtown.

Synset Examples

during the Great Depression going-out-of-business sales were very common

Related Terms

  • Synonym: liquidation sale - a sale of assets to pay off creditors
