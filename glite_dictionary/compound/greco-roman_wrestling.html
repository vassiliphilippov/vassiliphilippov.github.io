Greco-Roman wrestling

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡriːkoʊˈroʊmən ˈrɛslɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌɡrɛkəʊˈrəʊmən ˈrɛslɪŋ / (BrE)
A style of wrestling in which competitors are prohibited from holding any part of the opponent's body below the waist, as well as from using their own legs for attack or defense.
wrestling style forbidding holds below the waist

Examples

At the Olympics, he competed in Greco-Roman wrestling rather than freestyle.
Training for Greco-Roman wrestling requires great upper body strength and technique.
The school's Greco-Roman wrestling team won several national titles last year.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: freestyle wrestling - a style of amateur wrestling allowing holds below the waist

Etymology

Greco-Roman, meaning 'Greek and Roman,' refers to the classical origins associated with the sport.
