indigo squill

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪndɪɡoʊ skwɪl / (AmE), / ˈɪndɪɡəʊ skwɪl / (BrE)
A perennial herbaceous plant (Camassia scilloides) native to eastern and central North America, with blue to violet, star-shaped flowers.
a North American flowering plant

Examples

We found a cluster of indigo squill blooming along the woodland trail.
The indigo squill is known for its striking blue-violet petals in the spring.
Gardeners often cultivate indigo squill for its ornamental beauty and native origin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: camas - another common name for the plant Camassia, including Camassia scilloides
