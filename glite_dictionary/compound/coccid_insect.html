coccid insect

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑksɪd ˈɪnˌsɛkt / (AmE), / ˈkɒksɪd ˈɪnˌsɛkt / (BrE)
Any insect of the family Coccidae, including scale insects and mealybugs, which are small plant parasites with waxy coverings.
scale insect or mealybug

Examples

Orchard pests such as coccid insects can seriously damage fruit trees if not managed properly.
Many coccid insects secrete a waxy coating that protects them from predators and pesticides.
The infestation of coccid insects led to the decline of several ornamental plants in the greenhouse.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scale insect - a small plant-eating insect that secretes a protective scale
  • Synonym: mealybug - a soft-bodied, plant-eating insect that secretes a white, powdery wax

Etymology

From New Latin Coccidae (the family name), based on Greek kokkos (berry), referring to the shape of some scale insects.
