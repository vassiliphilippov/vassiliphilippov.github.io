gun jumping

1. NOUN

/ ɡʌn ˈdʒʌmpɪŋ / (AmE), / ɡʌn ˈdʒʌmpɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of starting something too soon, especially without proper authorization or before the appropriate time
acting prematurely, often without authorization

Examples

The company was fined for gun jumping by releasing information before approval.
To avoid accusations of gun jumping, all procedures must be followed.
His tendency for gun jumping often led to mistakes in team projects.

Synset Examples

His gun jumping led to several mistakes in the report.
Avoid gun jumping to ensure project success.
She was penalized for gun jumping during the project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to jump the gun - to begin something before the proper time

Etymology

From the phrase 'jump the gun,' originally from athletics, meaning to start a race before the starting gun is fired.
