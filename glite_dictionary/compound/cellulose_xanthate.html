cellulose xanthate

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛl.jəˌloʊs ˈzæn.θeɪt / (AmE), / ˈsɛl.jʊˌləʊs ˈzæn.θeɪt / (BrE)
a cellulose derivative produced by treating cellulose with caustic soda and carbon disulfide, chiefly used to make viscose rayon and cellophane.
a cellulose derivative used in making viscose rayon

Examples

The production of rayon involves converting cellulose into cellulose xanthate before spinning.
Researchers studied how cellulose xanthate reacts when exposed to varying temperatures.
Industrial plants must handle cellulose xanthate with care due to potential chemical hazards.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: viscose - a solution of cellulose xanthate used to make rayon and cellophane

Etymology

From 'cellulose', from French, from Latin 'cellula' (small chamber), and 'xanthate', from Greek 'xanthos' (yellow).
