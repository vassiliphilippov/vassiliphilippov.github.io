Lake Seneca

1. PROPN

/ seˈnɛkə / (AmE), / sɪˈnɛkə / (BrE)
the largest of the Finger Lakes, a glacial lake in central New York State
largest Finger Lake in New York, USA

Examples

Tourists flock to Lake Seneca in the summer for its scenic beauty and wineries.
The water quality of Lake Seneca is closely monitored by environmental agencies.
Many small towns along Lake Seneca benefit from recreational activities and tourism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Finger Lakes - a group of long, narrow lakes in central New York State

Etymology

Named after the Seneca people, a Native American group indigenous to the area.
