pestis ambulans

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛstɪs æmˈbjuːlənz / (AmE), / ˌpɛstɪs æmˈbjuːlənz / (BrE)
A mild form of bubonic plague, generally less severe than the typical disease.
mild form of bubonic plague
medical

Examples

During the outbreak, some villages reported cases of pestis ambulans rather than the more deadly form.
Medical records from the 17th century often distinguish pestis ambulans from the classic, severe plague.
Survivors of pestis ambulans frequently returned to normal activity much sooner than those affected by the more virulent strains.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bubonic plague - a serious infectious disease caused by the bacterium Yersinia pestis

Etymology

From Latin: pestis (plague) + ambulans (walking), indicating a less severe, 'walking' form of the disease.
