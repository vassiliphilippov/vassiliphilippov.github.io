libel chill

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪbəl ˌtʃɪl / (AmE), / ˈlaɪbəl ˌtʃɪl / (BrE)
the discouragement of free speech or publication due to fear of being sued for libel
fear of legal action restricting expression
legal

Examples

Many news outlets practice self-censorship to avoid the risk of libel chill.
The public debate suffered from libel chill after several lawsuits were filed against critics.
Authors fear that libel chill can stifle important investigative journalism.

Synset Examples

The threat of libel chill silenced many journalists.
Activists are fighting against the culture of libel chill.
He spoke out against the libel chill affecting the media.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chilling effect - discouragement of the legitimate exercise of legal rights by the threat of legal sanction

Etymology

From 'libel' (written defamation) and 'chill', metaphorically indicating discouragement or suppression.
