heat capacity ratio

1. NOUN

/ hiːt kəˈpæsəti ˈreɪʃiˌoʊ / (AmE), / hiːt kəˈpæsəti ˈreɪʃiəʊ / (BrE)
the ratio of the heat capacity of a substance at constant pressure to its heat capacity at constant volume
ratio of specific heats (C_P/C_V) for a substance

Examples

The heat capacity ratio for an ideal diatomic gas is typically 1.4.
Knowing the heat capacity ratio is essential for calculating the speed of sound in a gas.
Engineers use the heat capacity ratio when analyzing thermodynamic cycles.

Synset Examples

The experimental value for the heat capacity ratio of argon is the same as the theoretical heat capacity ratio.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: specific heat ratio - alternative name for heat capacity ratio
  • Synonym: adiabatic index - another synonym used in thermodynamics for heat capacity ratio
