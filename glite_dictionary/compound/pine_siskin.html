pine siskin

1. NOUN

/ paɪn ˈsɪskɪn / (AmE), / paɪn ˈsɪskɪn / (BrE)
a small finch native to North America, characterized by brown-streaked plumage and a faint touch of yellow on the wings, commonly found in coniferous forests
small North American finch

Examples

Flocks of pine siskins often visit backyard feeders during the winter months.
The pine siskin is easily identified by its streaked brown feathers and yellow-edged wings.
We spotted a pine siskin in the tall pines behind our cabin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: finch - a small songbird with a short, stout bill

Etymology

From 'pine', referring to its habitat preference for pine forests, and 'siskin', from German 'Zeisig' (a kind of finch).
