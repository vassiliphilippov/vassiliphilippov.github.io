point of periapsis

1. NOUN

/ pɔɪnt əv ˌpɛriˈæpsɪs / (AmE), / pɔɪnt əv ˌpɛriˈapsɪs / (BrE)
(astronomy) The point in the orbit of an object where it is closest to the body it is revolving around.
nearest orbital point to the primary body

Examples

The satellite transmits data most effectively when it is at its point of periapsis.
At the point of periapsis, the spacecraft was closest to Mars.
Planetary velocity increases as a planet approaches its point of periapsis.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: point of apoapsis - the point in an orbit farthest from the body being orbited

Etymology

From Greek 'peri' (around, near) and 'apsis' (arc, vault), used in astronomy for orbital points.
