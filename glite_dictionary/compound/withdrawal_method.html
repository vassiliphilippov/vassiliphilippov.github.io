withdrawal method

1. NOUN

/ wɪðˈdrɔəl ˈmɛθəd / (AmE), / wɪðˈdrɔːəl ˈmɛθəd / (BrE)
a method of birth control in which the penis is withdrawn from the vagina before ejaculation during sexual intercourse
birth control method of withdrawing before ejaculation

Examples

Many couples rely on the withdrawal method despite its risk of failure.
He explained that the withdrawal method is not a reliable form of contraception.
Some people mistakenly believe the withdrawal method is completely effective.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coitus interruptus - birth control by withdrawing the penis before ejaculation
  • Synonym: contraception - the deliberate use of artificial methods to prevent pregnancy
