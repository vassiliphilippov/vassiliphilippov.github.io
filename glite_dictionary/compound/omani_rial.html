Omani rial

1. NOUN

/ oʊˈmɑːni riˈɑl / (AmE), / əʊˈmɑːni rɪˈɑːl / (BrE)
the basic unit of currency in Oman
currency unit of Oman

Examples

The price of the car was listed as 8,000 Omani rials in the showroom.
After exchanging dollars for Omani rials, we were ready to shop in Muscat.
The Omani rial is known for being one of the highest-valued currencies in the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rial - the basic monetary unit in several countries including Oman, Iran, and Yemen

Etymology

From the name 'Oman' and Arabic 'rial', from Spanish 'real' (royal, a coin).
