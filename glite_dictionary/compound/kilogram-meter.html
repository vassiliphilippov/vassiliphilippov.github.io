kilogram-meter

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɪləˌɡræm ˈmiːtər / (AmE), / ˌkɪləˌɡræm ˈmiːtə / (BrE)
a unit of work equal to the work done by a one kilogram force moving an object through a distance of one meter
unit of work (physics/engineering)

Examples

The total work required was calculated to be 500 kilogram-meters.
Early physicists often expressed energy measurements in kilogram-meters rather than joules.
One kilogram-meter represents the work done by lifting a mass of one kilogram through one meter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: joule - the SI unit of work or energy
  • Synonym: foot-pound - a unit of work equal to the work done by a one pound force acting through a distance of one foot

Etymology

From kilogram (unit of mass) and meter (unit of length), following scientific nomenclature.
