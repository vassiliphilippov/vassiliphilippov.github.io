anxiolytic drug

1. NOUN

/ ˌæŋziəˈlɪtɪk drʌɡ / (AmE), / ˌæŋziəˈlɪtɪk drʌɡ / (BrE)
a drug that reduces anxiety and calms tension or irritability
drug for reducing anxiety
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed an anxiolytic drug to help manage her panic attacks.
Many anxiolytic drugs work by enhancing the effects of neurotransmitters in the brain.
He was concerned about the possible side effects of taking an anxiolytic drug long-term.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tranquilizer - a drug that calms or sedates
  • Synonym: sedative - a substance that induces sedation

Etymology

From Latin 'anxius' (anxious) + Greek 'lytikos' (able to loosen or free).
