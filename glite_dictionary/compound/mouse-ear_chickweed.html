mouse-ear chickweed

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaʊs ɪr ˈtʃɪkˌwid / (AmE), / ˈmaʊs ɪə ˈtʃɪkwiːd / (BrE)
any of several low-growing plants related to the common chickweed, especially those of the genera Cerastium and Stellaria, with small white flowers and hairy leaves resembling a mouse's ear.
a plant related to common chickweed

Examples

The garden was quickly overrun by mouse-ear chickweed after the warm spring rains.
She could identify mouse-ear chickweed by its tiny white blossoms and fuzzy leaves.
A patch of mouse-ear chickweed grew along the edge of the forest path, mixed with other wildflowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chickweed - small annual plant with white flowers, used as fodder

Etymology

The name refers to the fine hairs on the leaves, resembling a mouse's ear.
