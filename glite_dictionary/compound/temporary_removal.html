temporary removal

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛmpəˌrɛri rɪˈmuːvəl / (AmE), / ˈtɛmpərəri rɪˈmjuːvəl / (BrE)
a short-term exclusion or debarment from a privilege, position, or place, intended to be reversed after a certain period
short-term exclusion from a role or place

Examples

The school board decided on a temporary removal of the teacher pending further investigation.
Employees may face temporary removal from their duties for breaching safety protocols.
The referee ordered the temporary removal of the player after repeated violations of the rules.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suspension - the act of temporarily preventing someone from holding a position or privilege
