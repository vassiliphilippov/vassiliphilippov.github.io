fossa cat

1. NOUN

/ ˈfoʊsə kæt / (AmE), / ˈfɒsə kat / (BrE)
a large carnivorous mammal native to Madagascar, intermediate in some features between cats and civets.
large carnivorous Madagascan mammal

Examples

The fossa cat is a skilled climber that preys on lemurs and other small animals.
Despite its name, the fossa cat is not a true feline but belongs to its own distinct family.
Researchers spent weeks in the rainforest tracking a wild fossa cat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fossa - the animal species Cryptoprocta ferox, the largest carnivore of Madagascar

Etymology

From Malagasy 'fosa', the local name for the species.
