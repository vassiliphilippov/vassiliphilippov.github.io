saber rattling

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪbər ˌræt.əl.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈseɪbə ˌræt.əl.ɪŋ / (BrE)
the display or threat of military force, typically to intimidate or influence others without actual conflict
threatening display of military power

Examples

The country's latest missile test was seen as blatant saber rattling by its neighbors.
Many analysts criticized the government's saber rattling during the negotiations.
Despite their saber rattling, the leaders stopped short of declaring war.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brinkmanship - the practice of pursuing a dangerous policy to the brink of conflict

Etymology

From 'saber' (a type of sword), referencing the act of rattling a sword in its scabbard as a show of aggression.
