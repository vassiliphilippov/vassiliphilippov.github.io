pressure cabin

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɛʃər ˈkæbɪn / (AmE), / ˈprɛʃə ˈkabɪn / (BrE)
the part of an aircraft or spacecraft that is kept under increased air pressure so occupants can breathe normally at high altitudes or in space
pressurized section of an aircraft or spacecraft

Examples

The Boeing 747 features a sophisticated pressure cabin that allows passengers to remain comfortable during long flights.
Astronauts rely on the spacecraft's pressure cabin to survive in the vacuum of space.
A sudden loss of pressure in the pressure cabin can cause serious emergencies at high altitude.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cockpit - the compartment where the pilot sits in an aircraft
