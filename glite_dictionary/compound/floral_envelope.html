floral envelope

1. NOUN

/ ˈflɔrəl ˈɛnvəˌloʊp / (AmE), / ˈflɔːrəl ˈɛnvələʊp / (BrE)
the collective outer parts of a flower, comprising the calyx and corolla, that enclose the stamens and pistils
the calyx and corolla of a flower

Examples

The brightly colored floral envelope attracts pollinators to the plant.
Damage to the floral envelope can affect a flower's ability to reproduce successfully.
In botany class, we examined the structure of the floral envelope under a microscope.
