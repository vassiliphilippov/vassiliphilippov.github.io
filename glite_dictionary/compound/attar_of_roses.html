attar of roses

1. NOUN

/ ˈæt̬ɚ əv ˈroʊzɪz / (AmE), / ˈætɑː əv ˈrəʊzɪz / (BrE)
a fragrant essential oil obtained from fresh roses, used chiefly in perfumery and cosmetics
essential oil distilled from roses

Examples

The perfumer used attar of roses to create a delicate floral fragrance.
Traditional remedies sometimes include attar of roses for its soothing scent.
She dabbed a drop of attar of roses behind her ears before leaving for the party.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rose oil - essential oil extracted from roses

Etymology

From Persian *‘itr* (perfume) via Hindi *attar*.
