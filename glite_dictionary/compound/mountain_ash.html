mountain ash

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaʊntən æʃ / (AmE), / ˈmaʊntɪn æʃ / (BrE)
a tree of the genus Sorbus, especially the rowan, with pinnate leaves and clusters of white flowers and red or orange berries
a tree of the genus Sorbus

Examples

In autumn, the mountain ash is covered with bright red berries that attract birds.
The leaves of the mountain ash are distinctively divided into many leaflets.
We planted a mountain ash in the front garden for its beautiful spring flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rowan - a species of Sorbus, also called European mountain ash

2. NOUN

/ ˈmaʊntən æʃ / (AmE), / ˈmaʊntɪn æʃ / (BrE)
a tree or shrub of the genus Sorbus, especially a low-growing variety found in Texas, known for its compound leaves and clusters of bright berries
a tree or shrub, often with red berries

Examples

The mountain ash in our backyard is covered in bright orange berries each autumn.
Texas is home to a unique species of mountain ash that thrives in rocky soils.
Birds are often attracted to the fruit of the mountain ash tree during the winter months.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rowan - another name for the European mountain ash (Sorbus aucuparia)

3. NOUN

/ ˈmaʊntən æʃ / (AmE), / ˈmaʊntɪn æʃ / (BrE)
a tall Australian tree (Eucalyptus regnans) with wood similar to alpine ash, known as the tallest hardwood in the world
very tall Australian tree; tallest hardwood

Examples

The mountain ash stands majestically in the forests of southeastern Australia.
Scientists climbed a 99-meter mountain ash to collect samples for their research.
Some furniture is made from the strong timber of the mountain ash tree.
