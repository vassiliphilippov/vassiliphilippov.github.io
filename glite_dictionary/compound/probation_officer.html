probation officer

1. NOUN

/ proʊˈbeɪʃən ˈɑːfɪsər / (AmE), / prəʊˈbeɪʃən ˈɒfɪsə / (BrE)
an official appointed by a court to supervise offenders who have been released on probation, ensuring they comply with the terms set by the court
court official who supervises probationers
legal

Examples

The probation officer visited the young man weekly to ensure he was meeting his rehabilitation goals.
After his release, he had to report to his probation officer every month.
The judge ordered her to work closely with her probation officer and attend counseling sessions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parole officer - official who supervises former prisoners on parole
