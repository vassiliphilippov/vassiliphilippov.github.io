Adelie Coast

1. PROPN

/ əˈdeɪli ˌkoʊst / (AmE), / əˈdeɪli ˌkəʊst / (BrE)
a coastal region of Antarctica south of Australia, known for its large penguin colonies
coastal region of Antarctica

Examples

Scientists at the research station on the Adelie Coast study penguin behavior.
The Adelie Coast is often visited by icebreaker ships during the Antarctic summer.
Massive colonies of Adélie penguins can be found along the Adelie Coast.

Etymology

Named after Adélie Land, itself named by French explorer Jules Dumont d'Urville after his wife, Adèle.
