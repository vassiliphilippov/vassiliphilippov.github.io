modern-day janissary

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑdərn deɪ ˈdʒænəˌsɛri / (AmE), / ˈmɒdən deɪ ˈdʒænɪsəri / (BrE)
A contemporary supporter or enforcer, often someone who zealously serves a powerful organization or leader, alluding to the loyalty and discipline of historical Janissaries.
contemporary loyal supporter or enforcer

Examples

Many regarded him as a modern-day janissary, fiercely loyal to the company’s CEO.
In the political party, she was seen as a modern-day janissary for her unwavering enforcement of the leader’s policies.
The organization relied on several modern-day janissaries to maintain order within its ranks.

Synset Examples

He was regarded as a modern-day janissary in the tech world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enforcer - a person who compels others to follow rules or laws
  • Synonym: supporter - a person who supports a cause, organization, or leader

Etymology

From 'modern-day' (present time) and 'janissary', originally from Ottoman Turkish 'yeniçeri', literally 'new soldier'.
