Chino-Japanese War

1. NOUN

/ ˌʧiːnoʊ dʒəˈpæniz wɔr / (AmE), / ˌʧiːnəʊ dʒəˈpæniːz wɔː / (BrE)
The war fought between China and Japan from 1894 to 1895 over control of the Korean Peninsula, resulting in a decisive Japanese victory.
war between China and Japan (1894–1895)

Examples

The Chino-Japanese War marked the emergence of Japan as a major world power.
Historians often analyze the causes and consequences of the Chino-Japanese War.
The Treaty of Shimonoseki ended the Chino-Japanese War, ceding Taiwan to Japan.

Etymology

Chino-, from Spanish or Italian 'China', and Japanese, referring to the countries involved.
