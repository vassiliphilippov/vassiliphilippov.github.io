Late Latin

1. NOUN

/ leɪt ˈlæt̬.ən / (AmE), / leɪt ˈlæt.ɪn / (BrE)
the form of the Latin language written and spoken between the 3rd and 8th centuries AD, marking the transition from Classical Latin to Medieval Latin.
the form of Latin used 3rd–8th centuries AD

Examples

Many Christian texts were composed in Late Latin during the early centuries of the church.
The transition from Classical Latin to Late Latin involved significant changes in grammar and vocabulary.
Scholars study Late Latin to understand the linguistic evolution toward the Romance languages.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Classical Latin - the literary form of Latin used in ancient Roman literature before Late Latin
  • Degree Higher: Medieval Latin - the Latin used in the Middle Ages after Late Latin

Etymology

From Latin 'latinus' (Latin), with 'late' denoting the later period of the language's history.

2. NOUN

/ leɪt ˈlæt.ɪn / (AmE), / leɪt ˈlæt.ɪn / (BrE)
the form of Latin used from the 3rd to the 8th centuries AD, marking the transition from Classical Latin to Medieval Latin
form of Latin used 3rd–8th centuries AD

Examples

Many early Christian writers composed their works in Late Latin.
The grammatical structure of Late Latin differs noticeably from that of Classical Latin.
Scholars study Late Latin to trace the evolution of Romance languages.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Classical Latin - the form of Latin used in literary works of the late Roman Republic and early Empire
  • Degree Higher: Medieval Latin - the form of Latin used in the Middle Ages after Late Latin

Etymology

From Latin 'Latinus' (Latin), with 'late' indicating the final phase of the language's development.
