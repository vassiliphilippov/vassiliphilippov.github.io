oleoresin capsicum

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊli.oʊˈrɛz.ɪn kəpˈsɪkəm / (AmE), / ˌəʊ.li.əʊˈrɛz.ɪn kæpˈsɪkəm / (BrE)
a natural resin extracted from capsicum peppers, used as an ingredient in spices and in pepper spray
resin from chili peppers; used in pepper spray
medical

Examples

Police used oleoresin capsicum spray to disperse the protestors.
The chef warned that oleoresin capsicum is extremely potent when used as a seasoning extract.
Exposure to oleoresin capsicum can cause severe irritation to the eyes and skin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: capsaicin - the active component in chili peppers that causes burning

Etymology

From Latin 'oleum' (oil) + 'resina' (resin) + 'Capsicum' (genus of pepper plants).
