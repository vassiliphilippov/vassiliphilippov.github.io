mind-altering drug

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪnd ˌæltɚɪŋ drʌɡ / (AmE), / ˈmaɪnd ˌɔːltərɪŋ drʌɡ / (BrE)
a drug that affects a person's mental state, often causing changes in mood or perception
a drug affecting mood or perception

Examples

The therapist warned that using a mind-altering drug without supervision could be dangerous.
Many cultures have traditional rituals involving a mind-altering drug.
Laws restrict the possession and use of any mind-altering drug in the country.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hallucinogen - a drug that causes hallucinations
  • Synonym: psychoactive drug - a drug affecting the mind or behavior
