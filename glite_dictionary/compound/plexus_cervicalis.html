plexus cervicalis

1. NOUN

/ ˈplɛksəs sərˈvaɪkəlɪs / (AmE), / ˈplɛksəs səˈvɪkəˌlɪs / (BrE)
A network of nerves located beneath the sternocleidomastoid muscle in the neck, formed by the anterior branches of the first four cervical nerves.
nerve network in the neck
medical

Examples

Damage to the plexus cervicalis may result in sensory loss in parts of the neck and shoulder.
The surgeon had to carefully navigate around the plexus cervicalis during the procedure.
Anatomy students often memorize the structure and branches of the plexus cervicalis for their exams.

Etymology

From Latin 'plexus' (network) and 'cervicalis' (of the neck).
