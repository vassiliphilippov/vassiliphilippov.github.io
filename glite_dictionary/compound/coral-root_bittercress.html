coral-root bittercress

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔrəl ˌrut ˈbɪt̬ɚˌkrɛs / (AmE), / ˈkɒrəl ˌruːt ˈbɪtəkres / (BrE)
A European plant of the cress family with a knotted white rootstock, commonly found in damp places.
a European cress with a knotted white root

Examples

The damp woods were filled with coral-root bittercress, adding a touch of green to the forest floor.
She studied the distinctive knotted roots of the coral-root bittercress for her botany assignment.
Coral-root bittercress thrives in shaded areas near streams in its native habitat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bittercress - a group of plants in the genus Cardamine
