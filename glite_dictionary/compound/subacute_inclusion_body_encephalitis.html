subacute inclusion body encephalitis

1. NOUN

/ sʌbəˌkjuːt ɪnˈkluːʒən ˈbɑːdi ɪnˌsɛfəˈlaɪtɪs / (AmE), / sʌbəˌkjuːt ɪnˈkluːʒən ˈbɒdi ɪnˌsɛfəˈlaɪtɪs / (BrE)
A rare, chronic, and progressive inflammation of the brain, primarily affecting children and young adults, caused by persistent measles virus infection.
progressive brain inflammation from measles virus
medical

Examples

Doctors diagnosed the child with subacute inclusion body encephalitis after observing neurological decline months after a measles infection.
Effective treatments for subacute inclusion body encephalitis remain limited, making early measles vaccination crucial.
Symptoms of subacute inclusion body encephalitis can progress rapidly, leading to severe mental and motor impairment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: encephalitis - inflammation of the brain

Etymology

From Latin roots: 'subacute' (moderately acute), 'inclusion body' (abnormal structures in cells), and 'encephalitis' (inflammation of the brain).
