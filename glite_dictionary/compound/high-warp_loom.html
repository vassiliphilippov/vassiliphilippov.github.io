high-warp loom

1. NOUN

/ haɪ wɔːrp luːm / (AmE), / haɪ wɔːp luːm / (BrE)
a hand-operated loom in which the warp threads are stretched vertically, often used for weaving tapestry.
loom with vertical warp, for tapestry

Examples

Tapestry artists often prefer a high-warp loom for its ease in handling intricate designs.
She learned to weave on a traditional high-warp loom in Greece.
Unlike the horizontal, the high-warp loom keeps the woven fabric vertical during the process.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: low-warp loom - a loom in which the warp is set horizontally

Etymology

From the adjective 'high' + 'warp' (the set of lengthwise threads in weaving) + 'loom'.
