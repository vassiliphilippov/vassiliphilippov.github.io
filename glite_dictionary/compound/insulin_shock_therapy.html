insulin shock therapy

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪn.sjə.lɪn ʃɑk ˈθɛr.ə.pi / (AmE), / ˈɪn.sjʊ.lɪn ʃɒk ˈθɛr.ə.pi / (BrE)
a former psychiatric treatment involving the administration of large doses of insulin to induce convulsions and coma
historical psychiatric treatment with insulin
medical

Examples

Insulin shock therapy was once widely used in hospitals for treating schizophrenia.
Medical reports in the 1940s detailed the effects of insulin shock therapy on patients' mental health.
The risks associated with insulin shock therapy led to it being replaced by safer treatments.
