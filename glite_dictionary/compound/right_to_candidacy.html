right to candidacy

1. NOUN

/ ˈraɪt tə ˈkændɪdəsi / (AmE), / ˈraɪt tə ˈkændɪdəsi / (BrE)
the legal right to stand for election as a candidate in a political contest
legal right to stand for election
legal

Examples

The new constitution grants every citizen the right to candidacy regardless of social status or profession.
Several activists are fighting for equal right to candidacy in local elections.
Restrictions on the right to candidacy have been challenged in international courts.

Synset Examples

The condition of previous political experience enhances the exclusiveness of the intra-party competition and limits the right to candidacy to a group of politically active members; thus, the competition is closed off to other interested parties, for example, party newcomers or sympathisers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suffrage - the right to vote in political elections
  • Antonym: disenfranchisement - loss or denial of the right to vote or stand for election
