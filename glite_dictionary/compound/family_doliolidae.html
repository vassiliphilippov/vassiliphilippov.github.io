family Doliolidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ˌdoʊliˈɑːlɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli ˌdəʊliˈɒlɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of small, barrel-shaped oceanic tunicates found in the open sea, known for their transparent bodies and complex life cycles
a family of oceanic tunicates

Examples

Scientists discovered a new species within the family Doliolidae during their recent deep-sea expedition.
The transparent bodies of the family Doliolidae make them difficult to spot in oceanic waters.
Marine biologists study the family Doliolidae to understand the ecological roles of gelatinous zooplankton.

Etymology

From New Latin *Doliolidae*, based on the genus *Dolioletta* + -idae, a standard family suffix in zoological taxonomy.
