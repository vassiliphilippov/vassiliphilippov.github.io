traction engine

1. NOUN

/ ˈtræk.ʃən ˌɛn.dʒən / (AmE), / ˈtræk.ʃən ˌɛn.dʒɪn / (BrE)
a steam-powered vehicle with wheels, used for pulling heavy loads on roads or fields rather than on railway tracks, especially in the late 19th and early 20th centuries.
steam-powered vehicle for hauling heavy loads

Examples

The old traction engine was restored and now participates in local parades.
Farmers once relied on the traction engine to power heavy machinery in the fields.
At the museum, you can see a preserved traction engine from the early 1900s.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: steam engine - engine powered by steam pressure

Etymology

From 'traction' (Latin tractio, 'a drawing, pulling') and 'engine'.
