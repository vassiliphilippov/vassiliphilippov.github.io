sawn-off

1. ADJ

/ sɔn ˈɔf / (AmE), / sɔːn ˈɒf / (BrE)
Shortened by having a part cut off, especially with a saw; typically used to describe guns, tools, or sticks.
shortened by having a part cut off

Examples

The police confiscated a sawn-off shotgun from the suspect's car.
He made a sawn-off broomstick to clean the hard-to-reach shelves.
After the repair, only a sawn-off section of the pipe remained.

Synset Examples

a sawed-off shotgun
a sawed-off broomstick
the shortened rope was easier to use

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shortened - made shorter; reduced in length or duration

Etymology

Formed from the past participle of 'saw' + 'off', meaning 'cut off with a saw'.
