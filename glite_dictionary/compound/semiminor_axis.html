semiminor axis

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛmiˈmaɪnər ˈæksɪs / (AmE), / ˌsɛmiˈmaɪnə ˈaksɪs / (BrE)
Half the length of the minor axis of an ellipse, extending from the center to the edge along the shortest diameter.
half the minor axis of an ellipse

Examples

In this equation, the semiminor axis determines the ellipse's width.
The astronomer calculated the semiminor axis of the planet's orbit around the star.
You can find the area of the ellipse using both the semimajor and semiminor axis.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: major axis - the longest diameter of an ellipse
  • Degree Higher: minor axis - the shortest diameter of an ellipse

Etymology

From 'semi-' (half, from Latin), 'minor' (smaller), and 'axis' (central line).
