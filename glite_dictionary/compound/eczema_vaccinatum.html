eczema vaccinatum

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛk.sɪ.mə væk.səˈneɪ.təm / (AmE), / ˈɛk.sɪ.mə ˌvæk.sɪˈneɪ.təm / (BrE)
a rare and severe complication of smallpox vaccination, occurring mainly in people with eczema, marked by widespread skin lesions and high fever
severe vaccine complication in eczema patients
medical

Examples

After receiving the smallpox vaccine, the patient with a history of eczema developed eczema vaccinatum.
Doctors warned about the risk of eczema vaccinatum in individuals with atopic skin conditions.
Prompt treatment is critical if eczema vaccinatum occurs following vaccination.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: atopic dermatitis - chronic skin inflammation, also known as eczema

Etymology

From Latin 'vaccina' (from 'vaccinus', of cows, pertaining to cowpox used in vaccination) and 'eczema' (from Greek 'ekzema', to boil out).
