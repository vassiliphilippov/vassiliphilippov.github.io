genus Cottus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈkɑː.təs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈkɒt.əs / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Cottidae, comprising fish known as sculpins.
type genus of Cottidae (sculpin fish)

Examples

One common member of the genus Cottus is the European bullhead.
Researchers studied several species within the genus Cottus to understand their habitat preferences.
The genus Cottus is distributed throughout the cold regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sculpin - a fish from the family Cottidae

Etymology

From Latin 'Cottus', the genus name, from Greek 'kottos' (a kind of fish).
