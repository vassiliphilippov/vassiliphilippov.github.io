native language

1. NOUN

/ ˈneɪ.t̬ɪv ˈlæŋ.ɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈneɪ.tɪv ˈlæŋ.ɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the language that a person has spoken from earliest childhood; a person's first language
a person's first language

Examples

English is her native language, but she also speaks French fluently.
Many immigrants continue to use their native language at home.
He struggled to express complex ideas in any language other than his native language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mother tongue - the language a person learned first and speaks most fluently
  • Antonym: second language - a language learned in addition to one's native language
