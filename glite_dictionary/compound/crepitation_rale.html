crepitation rale

1. NOUN

/ krɛpɪˈteɪʃən reɪl / (AmE), / krɛpɪˈteɪʃ(ə)n rɑːl / (BrE)
A crackling sound heard during lung auscultation, often present in respiratory diseases such as tuberculosis, pneumonia, and congestive heart failure.
crackling lung sound in respiratory illness
medical

Examples

The physician detected a crepitation rale in the patient's left lung during the examination.
Presence of a crepitation rale is often indicative of pneumonia or other respiratory conditions.
After hearing a crepitation rale, the doctor ordered a chest X-ray for further investigation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rales - abnormal lung sounds heard with a stethoscope
  • Synonym: crepitus - crackling or popping sound in the body

Etymology

From Latin 'crepitatio' (crackling) and French 'rale' (rattle in the chest).
