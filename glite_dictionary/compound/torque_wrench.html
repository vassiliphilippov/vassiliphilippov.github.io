torque wrench

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɔrk ˌrɛntʃ / (AmE), / ˈtɔːk ˌrɛntʃ / (BrE)
a tool used for applying a specific amount of torque to a fastener, often fitted with a gauge to indicate the torque being applied
tool for measuring and applying torque

Examples

The mechanic used a torque wrench to make sure the wheel nuts were tightened correctly.
It's important to set your torque wrench properly to avoid over-tightening bolts.
She checked the torque wrench reading before securing the cylinder head.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spanner - a tool used for gripping and turning objects

Etymology

From 'torque' (Latin 'torquere', to twist) + 'wrench'.
