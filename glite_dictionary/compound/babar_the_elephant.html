Babar the Elephant

1. PROPN

/ bəˈbɑr ði ɪˈlɛfənt / (AmE), / bəˈbɑː ði ɪˈlɛfənt / (BrE)
a fictional elephant who is the main character in a series of French children's books, known for his adventures and royal status
fictional elephant character in children's books

Examples

Babar the Elephant has delighted generations of children with his adventurous tales and wise leadership.
The story of Babar the Elephant was originally written in French before being translated into many languages.
In the books, Babar the Elephant becomes king of the elephants and builds a city.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Babar - fictional elephant character from children’s literature

Etymology

From French children's books created by Jean de Brunhoff in 1931.
