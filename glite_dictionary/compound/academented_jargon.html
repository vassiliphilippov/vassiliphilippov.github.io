academented jargon

1. NOUN

/ ˌækəˈdɛmən(t)ɪd ˈdʒɑrɡən / (AmE), / ˌakəˈdɛmən(t)ɪd ˈdʒɑːɡən / (BrE)
Obscure or overly complex language used in academic settings, often making communication difficult for those outside the field.
obscure language in academia
informal

Examples

The professor's presentation was riddled with academented jargon that left the students confused.
Critics argue that academented jargon creates unnecessary barriers to understanding academic work.
She tried to avoid academented jargon when writing for a general audience.

Synset Examples

The lecture was filled with academented jargon and complex theories.
She found the academented language hard to understand.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jargon - specialized language used by a particular group

Etymology

Blend of 'academic' and 'demented', with 'jargon' meaning specialized language.
