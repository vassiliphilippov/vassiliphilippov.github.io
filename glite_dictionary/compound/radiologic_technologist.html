radiologic technologist

1. NOUN

/ ˌreɪ.di.oʊˈlɑː.dʒɪk tɛkˈnɑː.lə.dʒɪst / (AmE), / ˌreɪ.di.əˈlɒ.dʒɪk tɛkˈnɒl.ə.dʒɪst / (BrE)
a healthcare professional trained to perform diagnostic imaging procedures using radiological technology, such as X-rays and CT scans
medical imaging specialist
medical

Examples

The radiologic technologist assisted the doctor by taking detailed X-rays of the patient’s chest.
After completing her training, she began working as a radiologic technologist in a busy city hospital.
A skilled radiologic technologist can help ensure that diagnostic images are both accurate and safe for patients.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: radiographer - a person trained to take X-rays and similar images for medical purposes

Etymology

From 'radiologic' (relating to radiology) and 'technologist' (one skilled in the practical application of a scientific subject).
