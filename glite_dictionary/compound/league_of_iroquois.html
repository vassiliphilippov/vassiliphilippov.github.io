League of Iroquois

1. PROPN

/ liːg əv ɪrəˌkwɔɪ / (AmE), / liːg əv ɪərəˌkwɔɪ / (BrE)
The confederation of five (later six) Iroquois tribes in North America, comprising the Mohawk, Oneida, Onondaga, Cayuga, and Seneca (and joined by the Tuscarora in 1722), also known as the Iroquois Confederacy or Six Nations.
historic confederation of Iroquois tribes

Examples

The political system of the League of Iroquois inspired aspects of American democracy.
Historians often study the structure of the League of Iroquois to understand Indigenous governance.
The League of Iroquois played a central role in the colonial history of North America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Iroquois Confederacy - another name for the league of Iroquois tribes
  • Synonym: Six Nations - collective term for the six tribes in the league after 1722

Etymology

League from Old French *ligue* (alliance), and Iroquois from French *Iroquois*, likely of Algonquian origin.
