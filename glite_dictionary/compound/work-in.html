work-in

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɝːk ɪn / (AmE), / ˈwɜːk ɪn / (BrE)
an organized protest in which employees continue working in their workplace to oppose dismissals or the closure of a factory or business
protest where workers keep working

Examples

The factory work-in lasted for several weeks until management agreed to negotiate.
Union leaders organized a work-in to protest the company's plan to shut down operations.
During the work-in, employees refused to leave the premises but continued their regular duties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sit-in - a form of protest where people occupy a place and refuse to leave

Etymology

Modelled after 'sit-in', from 'work' + 'in', emerged in British labor movement in the late 20th century.
