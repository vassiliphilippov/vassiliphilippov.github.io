atomic cocktail

1. NOUN

/ əˈtɑmɪk ˈkɑkˌteɪl / (AmE), / əˈtɒmɪk ˈkɒk.teɪl / (BrE)
an oral dose of a radioactive substance administered for the treatment or diagnosis of cancer
oral radioactive dose for cancer treatment/diagnosis
medical

Examples

The patient received an atomic cocktail to help treat her thyroid cancer.
Doctors sometimes prescribe an atomic cocktail to track the progress of certain diseases.
Preparation and handling of the atomic cocktail must be done with extreme caution in the hospital.

Etymology

From 'atomic', relating to atoms or radioactivity, and 'cocktail', meaning a mixture of ingredients.
