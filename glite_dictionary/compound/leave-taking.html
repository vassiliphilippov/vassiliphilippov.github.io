leave-taking

1. NOUN

/ ˈlivˌteɪkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈliːvˌteɪkɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of saying goodbye or departing, especially in a polite or formal manner
the act of saying goodbye

Examples

After a brief leave-taking, she stepped out into the night.
His leave-taking from the company was both emotional and dignified.
The ceremony concluded with a formal leave-taking between the dignitaries.

Synset Examples

he disliked long farewells
he took his leave
parting is such sweet sorrow

Related Terms

  • Synonym: farewell - saying goodbye at parting
  • Synonym: parting - the action of leaving or saying goodbye

Etymology

From 'leave' (permission to depart) + 'taking'; attested from the late 16th century.
