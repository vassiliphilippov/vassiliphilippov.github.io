arithmetic mean

1. NOUN

/ əˌrɪθmətɪk ˈmin / (AmE), / ˌærɪθˈmetɪk ˈmiːn / (BrE)
the sum of a set of numerical values divided by the number of values, commonly known as the average
the average of a set of numbers

Examples

The arithmetic mean of the test scores was 78.
To calculate the arithmetic mean, add all the values together and divide by the total number of items.
The arithmetic mean provides a simple way to summarize a set of data.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: average - the typical value of a set of numbers, often calculated as the sum divided by the count
  • Antonym: median - the middle value of a set of numbers when arranged in order

Etymology

From Greek arithmētikē (the art of counting) and Latin medianus (from the middle).
