musical chairs

1. NOUN

/ ˈmjuːzɪkəl tʃɛrz / (AmE), / ˈmjuːzɪkəl tʃeəz / (BrE)
a children's game in which players walk around a set of chairs while music plays, then quickly try to sit in a chair when the music stops, with one player eliminated each round until a winner remains
children's game involving chairs and music

Examples

The children played musical chairs at the birthday party until only one winner was left.
At the picnic, even the adults joined in a lively game of musical chairs.
When the music stopped, everyone rushed to find a seat in musical chairs.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmjuːzɪkəl tʃɛrz / (AmE), / ˈmjuːzɪkəl tʃeəz / (BrE)
A game in which players walk around a set of chairs while music plays and must quickly find a seat when the music stops, with one fewer chair than players; also used figuratively to describe a situation involving constant change, competition, or instability.
children's game; also, situation of constant change

Examples

The children played musical chairs in the garden while their parents watched.
After several rounds, there was only one winner left in musical chairs.
The constant organizational changes at work felt like a game of musical chairs.

Synset Examples

The kids enjoyed the game of musical chairs during their birthday party.
We played musical chairs at the party, and it was hilarious.
The job market feels like musical chairs these days.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: game of musical chairs - the same children's game, often referred to as 'musical chairs'
