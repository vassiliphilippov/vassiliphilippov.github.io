tree frog

1. NOUN

/ ˈtri frɑɡ / (AmE), / ˈtriː frɒɡ / (BrE)
an arboreal frog, especially of the families Hylidae or Rhacophoridae, typically distinguished by adhesive toe pads for climbing trees
arboreal frog with adhesive toe pads

Examples

The tree frog camouflaged itself on the mossy branch.
Some species of tree frog can change color to match their environment.
A chorus of tree frogs filled the night air with their croaking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tree toad - another name for a tree frog
  • Degree Lower: frog - a tailless amphibian with long hind legs

2. NOUN

/ ˈtri ˌfrɑɡ / (AmE), / ˈtriː ˌfrɒɡ / (BrE)
A small, often brightly colored frog that lives in trees and typically has adhesive pads on its toes for climbing.
small arboreal frog with adhesive toes

Examples

A tree frog clung to the window with its sticky toes.
The rainforests of Costa Rica are home to many kinds of tree frogs.
Children listened to the chirping of a tree frog after the rainstorm.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: frog - A tailless amphibian of the order Anura
