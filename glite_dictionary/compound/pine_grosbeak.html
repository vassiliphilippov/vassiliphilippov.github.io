pine grosbeak

1. NOUN

/ paɪn ˈɡroʊsˌbik / (AmE), / paɪn ˈɡrəʊsˌbiːk / (BrE)
a large finch with a thick bill, found in coniferous forests of northern North America, Europe, and Asia
large finch of northern coniferous forests

Examples

A flock of pine grosbeaks landed in the snow-covered spruce trees.
The male pine grosbeak is easily recognized by its rosy-red plumage.
We spotted a rare pine grosbeak during our winter walk in the forest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grosbeak - any of several finches with thick bills for cracking seeds
