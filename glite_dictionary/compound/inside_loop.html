inside loop

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪnˌsaɪd lup / (AmE), / ˈɪn.saɪd luːp / (BrE)
an aerobatic maneuver in which an aircraft climbs, flies inverted, then dives to return to horizontal flight, tracing a loop in the vertical plane with the pilot on the inside of the loop
aerobatic flight maneuver forming a vertical loop

Examples

The pilot performed an impressive inside loop during the airshow, thrilling the audience.
Executing an inside loop requires great skill and precise control of the aircraft.
He described the sensation of weight during an inside loop as both intense and exhilarating.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: outside loop - aerobatic maneuver with the pilot on the outside of the loop
