functional calculus

1. NOUN

/ ˈfʌŋkʃənl ˈkælkjələs / (AmE), / ˈfʌŋkʃənl ˈkælkjʊləs / (BrE)
a formal system in symbolic logic that includes individuals, predicates, quantifiers, and represents relationships between propositions
formal system in symbolic logic with quantifiers and predicates

Examples

The rules of functional calculus are essential for understanding proofs in mathematical logic.
She studied functional calculus to understand how quantifiers work in formal systems.
Discussions in advanced philosophy often involve concepts from functional calculus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: predicate calculus - a symbolic logic system with quantification and predicates
  • Degree Lower: propositional calculus - a symbolic logic system concerned solely with the logical relationship between propositions

Etymology

Formed from functional + calculus, reflecting its application to functions or predicates within logical systems. From Latin calculus (small stone, reckoning).
