staccato rhythm

1. NOUN

/ stəˈkɑtoʊ ˈrɪðəm / (AmE), / stəˈkɑːtəʊ ˈrɪðəm / (BrE)
a pattern of short, sharply separated sounds or movements, often used in music or speech
pattern of short, detached sounds

Examples

The pianist played the piece in a staccato rhythm, creating a lively and energetic feel.
Her footsteps echoed down the hallway in a rapid staccato rhythm.
The dog's bark came in a staccato rhythm, quick and abrupt.

Synset Examples

He spoke in a staccato rhythm, emphasizing each point.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: legato - a musical term for smooth, connected sounds or movements

Etymology

From Italian 'staccato' (detached) and 'rhythm'. 'Staccato' is from past participle of 'staccare' (to detach).
