whirling table

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɜrlɪŋ ˈteɪbəl / (AmE), / ˈwɜːlɪŋ ˈteɪbl / (BrE)
an apparatus with one or more rotating disks, used with weights and pulleys to demonstrate physical laws and phenomena
physics demonstration apparatus with revolving disks

Examples

The physics professor used the whirling table to demonstrate centripetal force to the class.
A whirling table can make abstract physical concepts more tangible for students learning basic mechanics.
After assembling the whirling table, the students performed various experiments with pulleys and weights.
