yellow granadilla

1. NOUN

/ ˈjɛl.oʊ ɡrəˌnæd.ɪl.ə / (AmE), / ˈjɛl.əʊ ɡrəˌnæd.ɪl.ə / (BrE)
A tropical passionflower (Passiflora laurifolia) cultivated in the West Indies for its yellow, edible fruit.
West Indian passionflower with yellow fruit

Examples

The sweet pulp of the yellow granadilla is often used in tropical desserts.
Local farmers cultivate yellow granadillas for both domestic and export markets.
I tasted a fresh yellow granadilla while visiting the West Indies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: passion fruit - the fruit of any of several species of Passiflora, especially P. edulis, with aromatic pulp

2. NOUN

/ ˈjɛloʊ ˌɡræn.əˈdɪl.ə / (AmE), / ˈjɛləʊ ˌɡran.əˈdɪl.ə / (BrE)
An edible, yellow fruit of the Jamaica honeysuckle, prized for its sweet, aromatic pulp.
edible yellow fruit of the Jamaica honeysuckle

Examples

The tropical market offered ripe yellow granadilla alongside other exotic fruits.
She made a refreshing juice from the pulp of yellow granadilla for brunch.
Farmers in the region have started cultivating yellow granadilla due to its popularity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: granadilla - edible fruit of various passionflowers
  • Synonym: passion fruit - edible fruit of the passionflower vine

Etymology

From Spanish 'granadilla', diminutive of 'granada' (pomegranate), referring to the resemblance of the fruit.
