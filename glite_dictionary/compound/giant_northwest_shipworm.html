giant northwest shipworm

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒaɪənt ˈnɔrθˌwɛst ˈʃɪpˌwɜrm / (AmE), / ˈdʒaɪənt ˈnɔːθwɛst ˈʃɪpwɜːm / (BrE)
a large marine bivalve mollusc (shipworm) found along the Pacific coast of North America, known for boring into submerged wood
large shipworm of the Pacific Northwest

Examples

The giant northwest shipworm can cause significant damage to wooden structures in the ocean.
Scientists recently discovered a colony of giant northwest shipworm off the coast of Washington.
Fishermen are concerned about the impact of the giant northwest shipworm on docks and piers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shipworm - marine bivalve mollusc that bores into wood
