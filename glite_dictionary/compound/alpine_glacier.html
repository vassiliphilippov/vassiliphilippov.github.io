alpine glacier

1. NOUN

/ ˈælpaɪn ˈɡleɪʃər / (AmE), / ˈalpaɪn ˈɡleɪsiə / (BrE)
A glacier that forms in a high mountain valley and moves slowly down the valley.
mountain valley glacier

Examples

The alpine glacier carved a deep U-shaped valley over thousands of years.
Scientists are studying the retreat of alpine glaciers due to climate change.
During the last ice age, many alpine glaciers advanced far down the mountainsides.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: valley glacier - a glacier that flows down a valley

Etymology

Alpine from Latin 'Alpīnus' (of the Alps), referring to high mountains; 'glacier' from French 'glacier'.
