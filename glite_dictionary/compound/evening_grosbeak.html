evening grosbeak

1. NOUN

/ ˈivnɪŋ ˈɡroʊsˌbik / (AmE), / ˈiːvnɪŋ ˈɡrəʊsbiːk / (BrE)
A North American songbird with a thick, conical bill and yellow, black, and white plumage, known for its melodious song.
North American songbird (Coccothraustes vespertinus)

Examples

The evening grosbeak is easily recognized by its striking yellow and black coloration.
A flock of evening grosbeaks visited our backyard feeder this winter.
Birdwatchers often hope to spot an evening grosbeak among the trees during migration season.

Etymology

Named for its tendency to be active at dusk and its thick bill; 'grosbeak' from French 'gros bec' (large beak).
