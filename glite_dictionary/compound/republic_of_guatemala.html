Republic of Guatemala

1. PROPN

/ rɪˈpʌblɪk əv ˌɡwɑtəˈmɑlə / (AmE), / rɪˈpʌblɪk əv ˌɡwɑːtəˈmɑːlə / (BrE)
A country in Central America, officially known as the Republic of Guatemala, which gained independence from Spain in 1821.
Central American country; became independent in 1821

Examples

The Republic of Guatemala celebrates its Independence Day on September 15 each year.
After traveling across several countries, she finally arrived in the Republic of Guatemala.
The Republic of Guatemala is known for its Maya heritage and volcanic landscapes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Guatemala - Short form of the country's name; the same country in Central America

Etymology

Guatemala from Nahuatl 'Cuauhtēmallān' (land of many trees); 'republic' from Latin 'res publica' (public affair).
