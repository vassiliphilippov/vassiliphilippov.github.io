alkyl halide

1. NOUN

/ ˈæl.kɪl ˈhæ.laɪd / (AmE), / ˈæl.kɪl ˈheɪ.laɪd / (BrE)
an organic compound derived from an alkane by replacing one or more hydrogen atoms with halogen atoms
organic compound with halogen replacing hydrogen in an alkane
medical

Examples

Many solvents used in the laboratory are alkyl halides with low boiling points.
The reaction produced an alkyl halide as one of its main products.
Bromomethane is a simple example of an alkyl halide used in organic synthesis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: haloalkane - another term for alkyl halide; organic compound with halogen and alkane

Etymology

From 'alkyl', referring to alkane-derived groups, and 'halide', a compound with a halogen element.
