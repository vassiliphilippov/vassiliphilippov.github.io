conditioned reaction

1. NOUN

/ kənˈdɪʃənd riˈækʃən / (AmE), / kənˈdɪʃənd riˈækʃən / (BrE)
a learned response that occurs when a specific stimulus is repeatedly associated with another stimulus or action
learned response to a stimulus

Examples

The dog’s conditioned reaction to the bell was to start salivating, expecting food.
After repeated sessions, a conditioned reaction developed in the lab rats whenever a light was switched on.
Fear of thunder can become a conditioned reaction if a person has had negative experiences during storms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conditioned response - a learned response elicited by a conditioned stimulus

Etymology

Formed from 'conditioned', past participle of 'condition' (from Latin 'condicionem'), and 'reaction' (from Latin 'reactionem').
