Wu dialect

1. NOUN

/ wu ˈdaɪ.əˌlɛkt / (AmE), / wu ˈdaɪ.əlekt / (BrE)
a group of Chinese dialects spoken in the Yangtze River delta region, including Shanghai and surrounding areas
Chinese dialect group spoken in Yangtze delta

Examples

Linguists have studied the Wu dialect to understand language evolution in eastern China.
Shanghai is famous for its variety of the Wu dialect, which differs significantly from Mandarin.
Many elderly residents in Suzhou still speak the Wu dialect at home.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Shanghainese - the variety of Wu dialect spoken in Shanghai
  • Antonym: Mandarin - the most widely spoken Chinese dialect

Etymology

From Chinese name 'Wu', referring to the historical region in southeast China, combined with 'dialect'.
