stinging hair

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɪŋɪŋ heər / (AmE), / ˈstɪŋɪŋ heə / (BrE)
a multicellular hair on certain plants, such as the stinging nettle, that injects an irritating fluid when touched
hair on plants that injects irritating fluid

Examples

When touched, the stinging hair of a nettle releases chemicals that cause a burning sensation.
The botanist examined the stinging hair under a microscope to study its unique structure.
Some plants evolved stinging hairs as a defense mechanism against herbivores.
