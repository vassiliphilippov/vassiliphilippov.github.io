Acadia National Park

1. PROPN

/ əˈkeɪdiə næʃənəl pɑrk / (AmE), / əˈkeɪdiə ˈnaʃənəl pɑːk / (BrE)
a national park in Maine, USA, known for its coastal landscapes, marine erosion, glaciated terrain, and the highest point on the Atlantic coast.
national park in Maine, USA

Examples

We spent a week hiking and sightseeing in Acadia National Park last summer.
Acadia National Park offers stunning views of rocky shores and forested mountains.
Many visitors come to Acadia National Park each year to experience its diverse wildlife and scenic trails.

Etymology

Named after the historic region 'Acadia', itself from French 'Acadie', possibly from Mi'kmaq 'akadie' (place of abundance).
