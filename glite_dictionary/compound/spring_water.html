spring water

1. NOUN

/ sprɪŋ ˈwɔtər / (AmE), / sprɪŋ ˈwɔːtə / (BrE)
Water that comes from a natural underground spring, typically considered pure and suitable for drinking.
water from a natural spring

Examples

The bottles on the table were filled with spring water from a mountain source.
Many people prefer spring water over tap water for its clean taste.
She always carries a flask of spring water during her hikes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mineral water - water from a mineral spring containing minerals or gases

2. NOUN

/ sprɪŋ ˈwɔtɚ / (AmE), / sprɪŋ ˈwɔːtə / (BrE)
water that flows naturally to the surface from an underground source, typically considered pure and fresh
water from a natural underground spring

Examples

She only drinks spring water because she believes it is purer than tap water.
The mountain spring water tasted incredibly fresh after our long hike.
Bottles of spring water are sold in many grocery stores as a healthier option.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mineral water - water from a mineral spring, often containing dissolved minerals
