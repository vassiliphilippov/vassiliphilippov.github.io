Teapot Dome

1. PROPN

/ ˈtiːˌpɑt doʊm / (AmE), / ˈtiːˌpɒt dəʊm / (BrE)
A major U.S. government scandal of the early 1920s, involving the secret leasing of federal oil reserves in Wyoming to private companies, which became a symbol of corruption during the Harding administration.
notorious U.S. oil-leasing political scandal, 1920s

Examples

The Teapot Dome scandal severely damaged public trust in the government.
Many historians consider Teapot Dome one of the biggest political scandals in American history.
The corruption exposed by the Teapot Dome affair led to significant reforms in government oversight.

Etymology

Named after Teapot Dome, an oil reserve in Wyoming so called due to a nearby rock formation resembling a teapot.
