water-mint

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔtər mɪnt / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə mɪnt / (BrE)
a species of mint (Mentha aquatica) native to Europe and thriving in wet places, known for its bergamot-like aroma and sometimes naturalized in North America
a mint plant native to wet places in Europe

Examples

The water-mint grows abundantly along the banks of the stream.
She recognized the refreshing scent of water-mint as she walked through the marsh.
Gardeners often plant water-mint near ponds for its aromatic foliage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mint - an aromatic plant with many species, used as flavoring
