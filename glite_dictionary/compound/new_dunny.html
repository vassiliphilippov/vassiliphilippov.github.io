new dunny

1. NOUN

/ nuː ˈdʌni / (AmE), / njuː ˈdʌni / (BrE)
a newly built or installed toilet or restroom, especially in Australian or British English
a newly built toilet/restroom (Austral./Brit.)
informal british

Examples

We were all relieved when the new dunny was finished after weeks without one.
There's a new dunny behind the main hall if you need it.
The campground invested in a new dunny to improve its facilities.

Synset Examples

They built a new dunny near the campsite.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dunny - toilet or restroom (Australian/British informal)
  • Synonym: outhouse - a small building used as an outdoor toilet

Etymology

Dunny: from British dialect, probably from dung; 'new' from Old English nīwe.
