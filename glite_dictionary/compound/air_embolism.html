air embolism

1. NOUN

/ ɛr ˈɛm.bəˌlɪz.əm / (AmE), / eə ˈɛm.bəˌlɪz.əm / (BrE)
a medical condition in which air bubbles enter the bloodstream, often causing pain or serious complications, typically as a result of rapid changes in pressure
air bubbles in blood causing pain, often from pressure change
medical

Examples

Divers are particularly at risk of developing an air embolism during rapid ascent.
The patient experienced severe chest pain due to an air embolism after the surgical procedure.
Proper training is essential to prevent air embolism when working in pressurized environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decompression sickness - a condition caused by dissolved gases coming out of solution into bubbles inside the body due to decompression

Etymology

From 'air' + 'embolism', the latter from Greek embolos (stopper, plug).

2. NOUN

/ ɛr ˈɛm.bəˌlɪz.əm / (AmE), / eə ˈɛm.bəˌlɪz.əm / (BrE)
a blockage of a blood vessel by an air bubble, which can occur accidentally during surgery or injections, or as a complication from activities like scuba diving
blockage of a blood vessel by an air bubble
medical

Examples

The diver was rushed to the hospital after developing an air embolism during a rapid ascent.
During surgery, care is taken to avoid the introduction of an air embolism into the bloodstream.
An air embolism can be life-threatening if not treated promptly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: embolism - the sudden blocking of an artery by a clot or foreign material

Etymology

From 'embolism' (Greek *embolos*, meaning 'plug, wedge'), combined with 'air'.
