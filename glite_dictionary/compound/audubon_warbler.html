Audubon warbler

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔdəbɒn ˈwɔɹblɚ / (AmE), / ˈɔːdəbɒn ˈwɔːblə / (BrE)
a common species of warbler found in western North America, known for its distinctive yellow throat and streaked plumage
a warbler species of western North America

Examples

We spotted an Audubon warbler during our hike in the Sierra Nevada mountains.
The Audubon warbler is easily recognized by its yellow throat and gray head.
Many birdwatchers visit the region hoping to see the colorful Audubon warbler.

Etymology

Named after John James Audubon, American ornithologist and painter (1785–1851).
