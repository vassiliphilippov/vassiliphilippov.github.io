woodwind instrument

1. NOUN

/ ˈwʊdˌwɪnd ˌɪnstrəˌmɛnt / (AmE), / ˈwʊdwɪnd ˌɪnstrʊmənt / (BrE)
a musical instrument in which sound is produced by blowing air, typically through a reed or across an opening, as opposed to brass instruments
a type of wind musical instrument

Examples

The clarinet is a popular woodwind instrument in both jazz and classical orchestras.
She learned how to play several woodwind instruments, including the flute and the oboe.
Many woodwind instruments require precise breath control to produce a clear tone.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: brass instrument - a musical wind instrument made of brass or other metal
  • Degree Higher: wind instrument - a musical instrument sounded by blowing air

Etymology

From 'wood' and 'wind', after instruments historically made of wood and played by blowing air.
