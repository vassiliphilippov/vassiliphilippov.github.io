razor clam

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪzɚ klæm / (AmE), / ˈreɪzə klæm / (BrE)
a type of marine clam with a long, narrow, curved shell resembling a straight razor
long, narrow-shelled marine clam

Examples

At low tide, we searched the wet sand for razor clams to collect for dinner.
The shell of a razor clam is so sharp it can easily cut your hand if you’re not careful.
Many seafood restaurants along the coast serve fresh razor clam chowder during the summer months.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clam - a bivalve mollusk, often edible
