rhetorical accent

1. NOUN

/ ɹɪˈtɔːɹɪkəl ˈæksənt / (AmE), / rɪˈtɒɹɪkəl ˈæksənt / (BrE)
Special emphasis placed on particular tones or words in speech, not always dictated by grammar, to enhance clarity or effect.
special emphasis in speech for clarity or effect

Examples

The speaker used rhetorical accent to highlight important parts of his message.
Good orators often rely on rhetorical accent for dramatic effect during speeches.
Her skillful use of rhetorical accent made her arguments more persuasive.

Synset Examples

Rhetorical accent, or emphasis, is designed to give to a sentence distinctness and clearness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: emphasis - special stress laid on a word or phrase when speaking

Etymology

From rhetorical, relating to rhetoric (from Greek rhētorikós), and accent (from Latin accentus, 'tone, song').
