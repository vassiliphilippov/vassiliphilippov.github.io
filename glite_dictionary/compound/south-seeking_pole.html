south-seeking pole

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaʊθ ˌsiːkɪŋ poʊl / (AmE), / ˈsaʊθ ˌsiːkɪŋ pəʊl / (BrE)
the pole of a magnet that points toward the south when the magnet is suspended freely
magnet pole pointing south

Examples

The south-seeking pole of the magnet was attracted to the terrestrial south.
Scientists marked the south-seeking pole with red tape for identification.
If you suspend a bar magnet, its south-seeking pole will point toward the Earth's south.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: north-seeking pole - the pole of a magnet that points toward the north
