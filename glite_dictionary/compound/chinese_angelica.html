Chinese angelica

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃaɪniz ænˈdʒɛlɪkə / (AmE), / ˈtʃaɪniːz ænˈdʒɛlɪkə / (BrE)
A perennial plant native to China, used in traditional medicine and cooking; less prickly than the American angelica tree.
plant used in Chinese medicine and cooking

Examples

Chinese angelica is widely used in traditional Chinese herbal medicine for its reputed health benefits.
The root of Chinese angelica is often added to soups and teas in many East Asian cuisines.
Researchers are studying the properties of Chinese angelica for its potential use in modern therapies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: angelica - a genus of aromatic plants used in cooking and medicine

Etymology

From 'Chinese' (referring to its origin) and 'angelica' (the genus name).
