German shepherd dog

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɝːmən ˈʃɛpɚd dɔɡ / (AmE), / ˌdʒɜːmən ˈʃɛpəd dɒɡ / (BrE)
A breed of large, intelligent dog originally developed in Germany, commonly used as a police dog or guide dog for the blind.
breed of large herding dog

Examples

The police trained a German shepherd dog to work in their K9 unit.
After losing her sight, Maria received a German shepherd dog as her guide.
The family adopted a young German shepherd dog from the animal shelter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Alsatian - another name for the German shepherd dog, mainly British English

Etymology

From German, named for its origins as a sheep herding dog in Germany.
