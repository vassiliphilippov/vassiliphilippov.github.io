inferior colliculus

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈfɪriɚ kəˈlɪkjələs / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɪəriə kəˈlɪkjʊləs / (BrE)
a paired structure in the midbrain that serves as a major center for auditory processing
auditory center in the midbrain
medical

Examples

The inferior colliculus plays a crucial role in the brain's ability to localize sounds.
Damage to the inferior colliculus can lead to significant auditory processing deficits.
Researchers mapped neural pathways connecting the cochlea to the inferior colliculus.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: superior colliculus - a paired structure in the midbrain involved in visual processing

Etymology

From Latin 'colliculus' (little hill), describing the structure's shape.
