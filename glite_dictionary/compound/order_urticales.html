order Urticales

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdər ɜːrˈtɪkəˌliːz / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə ɜːˈtɪkəleɪlz / (BrE)
a former taxonomic order of dicotyledonous plants including the Moraceae, Urticaceae, and Ulmaceae families.
former plant order including nettles and elms

Examples

The order Urticales once included families such as Moraceae and Ulmaceae.
Botanists no longer classify nettles within the order Urticales in most modern systems.
Historically, the order Urticales encompassed a variety of plants, from figs to elms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Moraceae - the mulberry or fig family of flowering plants
  • Synonym: Urticaceae - the nettle family of flowering plants
  • Synonym: Ulmaceae - the elm family of flowering plants

Etymology

From modern Latin 'Urticales', based on 'Urtica', the type genus (Latin for nettle).
