ice-cold

1. ADJ

/ aɪs koʊld / (AmE), / aɪs kəʊld / (BrE)
Extremely cold; as cold as ice.
extremely cold; as cold as ice

Examples

She took a sip of the ice-cold lemonade, enjoying the sensation on a hot day.
His hands were ice-cold after walking home in the snow without gloves.
The water in the lake was ice-cold, sending chills through her body as she dove in.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: cold - having a low temperature, especially when compared to the temperature of the human body
  • Degree Higher: freezing - very cold; at or below the temperature at which water freezes
