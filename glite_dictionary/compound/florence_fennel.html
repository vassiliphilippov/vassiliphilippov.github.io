Florence fennel

1. NOUN

/ ˈflɔr.əns ˈfɛn.əl / (AmE), / ˈflɒr.əns ˈfɛn.əl / (BrE)
A type of fennel with a swollen, aromatic bulbous stem base that is eaten cooked or raw, especially in salads.
aromatic edible stem base (vegetable)

Examples

The fresh Florence fennel added a sweet, anise-like flavor to the salad.
She roasted Florence fennel with olive oil as a side dish for dinner.
Shaved Florence fennel gives a crisp texture to many Mediterranean recipes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fennel - an aromatic yellow-flowered plant of the parsley family

2. NOUN

/ ˈflɔːrəns ˈfɛnəl / (AmE), / ˈflɒrəns ˈfɛnəl / (BrE)
A variety of fennel cultivated for its edible, aromatic, bulbous stem base, commonly used as a vegetable.
edible bulbous fennel variety

Examples

Florence fennel adds a subtle, sweet flavor to salads when sliced thinly.
The chef roasted Florence fennel with carrots and potatoes for a side dish.
You can use Florence fennel bulbs in soups or eat them raw for a crisp snack.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fennel - an aromatic plant with feathery leaves

Etymology

Florence after the Italian city, where this fennel variety was popularized.
