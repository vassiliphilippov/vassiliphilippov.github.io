at first

1. ADV

/ æt ˈfɝst / (AmE), / ət ˈfɜːst / (BrE)
used to describe the beginning of a situation before things change later; in the beginning.
in the beginning; initially

Examples

At first, I found the new job overwhelming, but it became easier over time.
She didn't like the city at first, but now she loves it.
At first, nobody understood the instructions, but after some explanation everything was clear.

Synset Examples

at first he didn't notice anything strange

Related Terms

  • Synonym: initially - at the beginning; in the first place
  • Antonym: eventually - in the end, especially after a long delay
