chrome-nickel steel

1. NOUN

/ ˌkroʊm ˈnɪkəl stiːl / (AmE), / krəʊm ˈnɪkəl stiːl / (BrE)
A type of steel alloy containing chromium and nickel, known for its resistance to corrosion and ability to maintain elasticity over varying temperatures.
steel alloy with chromium and nickel

Examples

The durability of chrome-nickel steel makes it ideal for manufacturing medical instruments.
Watch springs are often made from chrome-nickel steel to ensure consistent elasticity.
Modern kitchen appliances frequently use chrome-nickel steel for its corrosion resistance.

Etymology

From 'chrome' (chromium) and 'nickel', referring to the metal elements added to steel.
