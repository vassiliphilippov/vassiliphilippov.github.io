order Apodes

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdər əˈpoʊdiːz / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə əˈpəʊdiːz / (BrE)
a former order of elongated fishes, such as eels, characterized by the absence or reduction of pelvic fins and girdle.
taxonomic group of elongate fishes (e.g. eels)

Examples

The classification of eels under the order Apodes has been largely replaced by more modern taxonomies.
Early zoological texts often referenced the order Apodes to describe footless fish species.
The order Apodes includes many species with elongated, snake-like bodies.

Etymology

From Greek 'apous' meaning 'footless' (a- 'without' + pous 'foot').
