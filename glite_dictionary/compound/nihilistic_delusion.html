nihilistic delusion

1. NOUN

/ ˌnaɪ.əˈlɪs.tɪk dɪˈluː.ʒən / (AmE), / ˌnaɪ.ɪˈlɪs.tɪk dɪˈluː.ʒən / (BrE)
A delusional belief that things, or everything including oneself, do not exist or that everything is unreal.
delusion that nothing exists or is real
medical

Examples

The patient was suffering from a nihilistic delusion, insisting that he did not exist.
In severe depression, a nihilistic delusion may manifest as the belief that the world itself is not real.
Cases of nihilistic delusion are commonly reported in certain types of psychotic disorders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: delusion - a false belief or impression, typically as a symptom of mental disorder

Etymology

From Latin nihil (nothing) and English delusion; used in psychiatry.
