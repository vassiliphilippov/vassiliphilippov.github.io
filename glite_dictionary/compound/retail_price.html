retail price

1. NOUN

/ ˈriːteɪl praɪs / (AmE), / ˈriːteɪl praɪs / (BrE)
the price at which a product is sold to the final customer, including any retail markup over the cost to the retailer
price charged to the customer in a store

Examples

The retail price of the jacket was much higher than the factory cost.
Due to rising production costs, the retail price of milk increased last year.
Our store offers a discount on the retail price for loyalty program members.

Synset Examples

The average retail price for a new video game is approximately 60 US dollars.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: wholesale price - the price paid by a retailer to buy goods from a supplier or manufacturer

Etymology

From 'retail' (from Old French 'retaillier', to cut up) and 'price' (from Old French 'pris', value).
