fishwifely charm

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪʃˌwaɪfli tʃɑrm / (AmE), / ˈfɪʃˌwʌɪfli tʃɑːm / (BrE)
a warm, nurturing demeanor associated with traditional, down-to-earth housewives, often with a straightforward and inviting manner
warm, nurturing housewifely demeanor

Examples

Despite her gruff voice, her fishwifely charm put everyone at ease.
The cafe's appeal came from the owner's fishwifely charm and welcoming smile.
Many admired the fishwifely charm she brought to her bustling kitchen.

Synset Examples

She had a fishwifely charm that drew customers in.
Her fishwifely ways made the shop feel welcoming.

Etymology

From 'fishwife' (a woman selling fish, often seen as outspoken and motherly) plus '-ly' and 'charm'.
