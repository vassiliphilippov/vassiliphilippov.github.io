.22 calibre

1. ADJ

/ pɔɪnt tuː tuː ˈkælɪbər / (AmE), / pɔɪnt tuː tuː ˈkælɪbə / (BrE)
Relating to a gun bore or its ammunition that measures 0.22 inches (twenty-two hundredths of an inch) in diameter.
of a gun bore measuring 0.22 inches

Examples

He bought a new .22 calibre rifle for target practice.
The police recovered a .22 calibre pistol from the scene.
Hunters often use .22 calibre ammunition for small game.

Synset Examples

a .22 caliber pistol

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: .38 calibre - of a gun bore measuring 0.38 inches

Etymology

calibre from French calibre, denoting degree or size; numerical specification refers to bore diameter in inches.
