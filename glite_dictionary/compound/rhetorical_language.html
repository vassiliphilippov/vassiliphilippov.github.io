rhetorical language

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈtɔrɪkəl ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / rɪˈtɒrɪkəl ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
Language intended to persuade or influence others, often using stylistic or expressive devices.
language used to persuade or influence

Examples

The politician relied heavily on rhetorical language to sway the crowd's opinion.
In essays, students are often encouraged to avoid excessive rhetorical language in favor of clear arguments.
Her speech was filled with rhetorical language that made her message more compelling.

Synset Examples

Martin Luther King Jr. used rhetorical language to get his point across that everyone should be treated equal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rhetoric - the art of effective or persuasive speaking or writing
