Seward Peninsula

1. PROPN

/ ˈsuːərd pəˈnɪnsələ / (AmE), / ˈsjʊəd pəˈnɪnsjʊlə / (BrE)
a peninsula in western Alaska that projects westward into the Bering Sea just below the Arctic Circle
peninsula in western Alaska

Examples

The Seward Peninsula is home to a variety of unique arctic wildlife.
Archaeologists uncovered ancient tools on the Seward Peninsula in Alaska.
Many indigenous communities have lived on the Seward Peninsula for thousands of years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Alaska - a state in the northwest United States on the Bering Sea

Etymology

Named after William H. Seward, U.S. Secretary of State who arranged the purchase of Alaska from Russia.
