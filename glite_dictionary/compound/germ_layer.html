germ layer

1. NOUN

/ dʒɝm ˈleɪɚ / (AmE), / dʒɜːm ˈleɪə / (BrE)
In embryology, any of the three primary layers of cells (ectoderm, mesoderm, or endoderm) formed in an embryo during early development, from which all tissues and organs develop.
primary embryonic cell layer
medical

Examples

During development, each germ layer gives rise to specific organs and tissues in the body.
The ectoderm is the outermost germ layer and forms the skin and nervous system.
Researchers study how the three germ layers interact to understand congenital diseases.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: ectoderm - the outermost germ layer in an embryo
  • Degree Lower: mesoderm - the middle germ layer in an embryo
  • Degree Lower: endoderm - the innermost germ layer in an embryo
