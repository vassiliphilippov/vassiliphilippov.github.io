kinetic theory of gases

1. NOUN

/ kəˈnɛtɪk ˈθɪri əv ˈɡæsɪz / (AmE), / kɪˈnɛtɪk ˈθɪəri əv ˈɡɑːzɪz / (BrE)
the scientific theory that explains the behavior of gases by assuming they are made up of small particles in constant, random motion
scientific theory about gas particles in motion

Examples

The kinetic theory of gases explains why pressure increases as temperature rises.
In science class, we learned how the kinetic theory of gases accounts for the properties of air.
According to the kinetic theory of gases, gas particles move randomly and collide with each other.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kinetic theory - theory describing the motion of particles in matter
  • Synonym: ideal gas law - physical law relating pressure, volume, temperature of an ideal gas

Etymology

From 'kinetic' (relating to motion), from Greek *kinesis* (movement), and 'theory' from Greek *theoria* (contemplation).
