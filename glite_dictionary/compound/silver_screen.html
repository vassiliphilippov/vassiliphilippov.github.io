silver screen

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪlvɚ ˌskrin / (AmE), / ˈsɪlvə ˌskriːn / (BrE)
the cinema or the film industry, especially as a whole or in general reference
the film industry; cinema

Examples

She dreamed of one day seeing her name on the silver screen.
Classic stars like Audrey Hepburn graced the silver screen in unforgettable roles.
Many directors found fame and fortune through the silver screen in the twentieth century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cinema - movies as an art or business
  • Synonym: Hollywood - the U.S. film industry, especially the commercial film industry

Etymology

Refers to projection screens once coated with metallic (silver) paint to reflect light better in early cinemas.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɪlvər skrin / (AmE), / ˈsɪlvə skriːn / (BrE)
a white or silvered surface used for projecting and viewing motion pictures, especially in a cinema
cinema projection surface

Examples

The audience watched in awe as the film lit up the silver screen.
Many legendary actors found fame on the silver screen in the twentieth century.
The old theater still boasts an original silver screen from the early days of cinema.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: movie screen - surface onto which films are projected

Etymology

Originally referred to the silver-coated projection screens used in early cinemas.
