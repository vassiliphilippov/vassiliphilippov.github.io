zirconium oxide

1. NOUN

/ zɚˌkoʊniəm aɪˈksaɪd / (AmE), / zɜːˌkəʊniəm ɒkˈsaɪd / (BrE)
a white crystalline compound, ZrO₂, used as a refractory material and in ceramics, abrasives, and coatings
white crystalline compound (ZrO₂), industrial uses

Examples

Dental crowns are often made from zirconium oxide for its strength and natural appearance.
The scientist studied the heat resistance of zirconium oxide ceramics.
Industrial furnaces sometimes use zirconium oxide as a refractory lining material.

Etymology

From 'zirconium', named after the mineral zircon, and 'oxide', denoting a compound with oxygen.
