Ottoman Turk

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːtəˌmæn tɜrk / (AmE), / ˈɒtəmən tɜːk / (BrE)
a member of the Turkish people, especially one belonging to the tribe or dynasty founded by Osman I, who established the Ottoman Empire.
Turkish member of the Ottoman Empire

Examples

The Ottoman Turk army advanced into southeastern Europe in the 14th century.
Many Ottoman Turks settled in the Balkans during the empire's expansion.
An Ottoman Turk merchant played a crucial role in Istanbul's economy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ottoman - member of the dynasty founded by Osman I or relating to the Ottoman Empire
  • Synonym: Turk - a member of the Turkish people

Etymology

From Osman I, founder of the Ottoman dynasty (Turkish: Osmanlı), and 'Turk' meaning a member of the Turkish people.
