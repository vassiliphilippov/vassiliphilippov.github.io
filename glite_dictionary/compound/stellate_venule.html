stellate venule

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɛl.eɪt ˈvɛn.jul / (AmE), / ˈstɛl.eɪt ˈvɛn.juːl / (BrE)
a star-shaped group of small veins located in the renal cortex of the kidney
star-shaped group of kidney venules
medical

Examples

The stellate venule network is clearly visible under microscopic examination of the kidney cortex.
Damage to a stellate venule can affect blood drainage in the renal cortex.
Researchers mapped the distribution of each stellate venule to study blood flow patterns in the kidney.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: venule - a small vein, especially one collecting blood from capillaries

Etymology

From Latin *stellatus* (star-shaped) and *venula* (small vein).
