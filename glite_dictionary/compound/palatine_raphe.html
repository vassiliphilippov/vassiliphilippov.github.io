palatine raphe

1. NOUN

/ ˈpæl.ə.taɪn ˈreɪ.fi / (AmE), / ˈpælətaɪn ˈreɪfi / (BrE)
the seam or ridge running along the midline of the hard palate in the roof of the mouth
ridge along the middle of the hard palate
medical

Examples

The palatine raphe is often visible as a slightly raised line on the roof of the mouth.
A dentist may examine the palatine raphe during an oral health assessment.
Developmental changes to the palatine raphe can sometimes indicate congenital conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: palate - the roof of the mouth

Etymology

From Latin 'palatum' (palate) and Greek 'rhaphē' (seam, suture).
