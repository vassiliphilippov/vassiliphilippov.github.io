Koasati language

1. NOUN

/ koʊˈæsəti ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / kəʊˈæsəti ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Muskogean language spoken by the Koasati people, primarily in Louisiana, USA.
the language of the Koasati people

Examples

Several elders continue to speak Koasati language at home despite younger generations switching to English.
Linguists are working to document the Koasati language before it becomes endangered.
She is one of the few people who can fluently read and write in Koasati language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Muskogean - a family of Native American languages in the southeastern United States

Etymology

From Koasati, the tribal name of the people who speak the language.
