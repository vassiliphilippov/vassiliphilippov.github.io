California sage

1. NOUN

/ kəˌlɪfɔrniə seɪdʒ / (AmE), / kəˌlɪfəˈniːə seɪdʒ / (BrE)
a low, ashy-grey shrub native to California, often found in dry, coastal areas
a low, ashy-grey Californian shrub

Examples

The hillside was covered with California sage, giving the landscape a silvery tint.
Gardeners often choose California sage for its drought resistance and aromatic fragrance.
Hikers enjoy the scent of California sage along the coastal trails in spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sage - a plant with aromatic leaves, used as a herb or in landscaping
