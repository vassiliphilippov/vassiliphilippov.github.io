God bless America

1. X

/ ɡɑd blɛs əˈmɛrɪkə / (AmE), / ɡɒd blɛs əˈmɛrɪkə / (BrE)
A patriotic expression or the title of a popular song, used to express appreciation or support for the United States of America.
patriotic expression or song about the US
american

Examples

At the end of the speech, she proclaimed, 'God bless America,' and the audience applauded.
The choir sang 'God bless America' at the ceremony.
You often hear people say 'God bless America' during national celebrations.

Synset Examples

Every July Fourth, we chant, 'God bless America'.
During the parade, everyone sang 'God bless America' together.

Etymology

Popularized by Irving Berlin's 1918 song "God Bless America," revised and released in 1938.
