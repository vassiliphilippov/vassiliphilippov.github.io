Asian influenza

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪʒən ˌɪnfluˈɛnzə / (AmE), / ˈeɪʃən ˌɪnflʊˈɛnzə / (BrE)
A type of influenza caused by the H2N2 virus, which was first identified in Asia in 1957 and caused a worldwide pandemic.
a 1957 influenza pandemic virus
medical

Examples

The outbreak of Asian influenza in 1957 resulted in widespread illness across many countries.
Scientists studied the H2N2 strain responsible for Asian influenza to improve vaccine development.
Many people recall the rapid global spread of Asian influenza during the late 1950s.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: influenza - a contagious viral infection commonly known as the flu
  • Synonym: Spanish flu - the 1918 influenza pandemic virus

Etymology

From 'Asian' referring to its origin in Asia, and 'influenza' from Italian.
