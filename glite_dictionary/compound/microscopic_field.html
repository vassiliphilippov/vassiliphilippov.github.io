microscopic field

1. NOUN

/ ˌmaɪkrəˈskɑpɪk ˈfild / (AmE), / ˌmʌɪkrəˈskɒpɪk ˈfiːld / (BrE)
the area visible through the eyepiece of a microscope at a given magnification
area seen through a microscope

Examples

The sample was clearly visible in the microscopic field at 40x magnification.
Only a portion of the tissue appeared within the microscopic field.
She adjusted the slide to center the cells in the microscopic field.
