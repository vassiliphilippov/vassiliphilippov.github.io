exocentric compound

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛksoʊˈsɛntrɪk ˈkɑmpaʊnd / (AmE), / ˌɛksəʊˈsɛntrɪk ˈkɒmpaʊnd / (BrE)
A compound word whose overall syntactic function is not the same as that of either of its immediate parts.
compound word with a different function than its parts

Examples

The word exocentric compound is discussed in advanced grammar classes.
‘Bittersweet’ is an example of an exocentric compound because the whole acts as a noun, while its parts are adjectives.
Linguists often analyze whether a compound is endocentric or exocentric compound.

Synset Examples

The noun ‘bittersweet’ is an exocentric compound, since it is a noun but its elements are both adjectives.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: endocentric compound - compound whose syntactic function matches one of its parts

Etymology

From Greek exo- (outside) + kentron (center) + Latin componere (to put together).
