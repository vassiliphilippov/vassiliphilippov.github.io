matzoh ball

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑːtsoʊ bɔl / (AmE), / ˈmætsəʊ bɔːl / (BrE)
A Jewish dumpling made from matzo meal, traditionally served in soup, especially during Passover.
Jewish dumpling served in soup

Examples

For the holiday dinner, Rachel made delicious matzoh balls to put in the chicken soup.
The secret to fluffy matzoh balls is letting the mixture rest before boiling.
I've never tasted a better matzoh ball than the ones at that deli downtown.

Etymology

From Yiddish *matse* (unleavened bread) + English 'ball'.
