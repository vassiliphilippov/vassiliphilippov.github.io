polar bear

1. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊ.lɚ ˌbɛɹ / (AmE), / ˈpəʊ.lə ˌbeə / (BrE)
a large, white-furred bear found in the Arctic, known for its adaptation to cold environments and its dependence on sea ice for hunting
large white bear of the Arctic

Examples

We saw a polar bear swimming among the ice floes during our Arctic cruise.
The polar bear is perfectly adapted to survive in frigid temperatures and hunt seals.
Climate change poses a serious threat to the habitat of the polar bear.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: grizzly bear - large brown bear found in North America
  • Degree Lower: brown bear - large species of bear with brown fur
