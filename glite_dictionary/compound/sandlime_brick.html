sandlime brick

1. NOUN

/ ˈsændˌlaɪm brɪk / (AmE), / ˈsændˌlaɪm brɪk / (BrE)
A type of brick made from sand and slaked lime instead of clay, typically light gray or off-white in color.
brick made from sand and lime

Examples

The new building was constructed using sandlime bricks for improved strength and fire resistance.
Because sandlime bricks are light in color, they are often chosen for modern architectural designs.
Unlike traditional red bricks, sandlime bricks offer a smooth and uniform appearance.

Synset Examples

The finished calcium-silicate bricks are very accurate and uniform, although the sharp arrises need careful handling to avoid damage to brick and bricklayer.
