reaping hook

1. NOUN

/ ˈripɪŋ hʊk / (AmE), / ˈriːpɪŋ hʊk / (BrE)
a hand tool with a curved blade and short handle used for cutting grass or crops
curved-bladed tool for cutting crops

Examples

The farmer swung the reaping hook skillfully through the tall wheat.
A rusty reaping hook hung on the barn wall as a reminder of old harvesting methods.
She used a sharp reaping hook to gather bundles of barley in the field.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sickle - a similar tool with a curved blade for cutting grain or grass

Etymology

From 'reap' (to cut or harvest crops) and 'hook,' indicating the curved blade shape.
