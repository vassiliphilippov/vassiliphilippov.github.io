government security

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡʌvərnmənt sɪˈkjʊrəti / (AmE), / ˈɡʌvənmənt sɪˈkjʊərɪti / (BrE)
a bond or other security issued by the government or its agencies, especially in the United States, as a means of raising funds
security issued by the government
american

Examples

Investors often choose government security for its perceived safety and reliability.
The Federal Reserve purchased billions of dollars in government securities during the fiscal intervention.
She diversified her portfolio by adding both stocks and government security to minimize risk.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: treasury bond - a long-term, interest-bearing security issued by the US Treasury
  • Antonym: municipal bond - a security issued by a state, municipality, or county and not the federal government
