blue elderberry

1. NOUN

/ bluː ˈɛldərˌbɛri / (AmE), / bluː ˈɛldəˌbɛri / (BrE)
A shrub or small tree native to the western United States, known for its white flowers and blue berries, which are used in wines and jellies.
shrub with blue berries used in wines and jellies

Examples

The blue elderberry thrives in the foothills and mountain regions of California.
Jams made from blue elderberries are prized for their unique flavor and color.
We planted a blue elderberry in our backyard to attract birds and pollinators.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elderberry - the berry or the plant of the elder tree
