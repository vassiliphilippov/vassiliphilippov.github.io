Third Battle of the Aisne

1. PROPN

/ θɝd ˈbætəl əv ði ˈɛn / (AmE), / θɜːd ˈbætl əv ði ˈɛn / (BrE)
a major battle of World War I fought in May 1918 near the Aisne River, where the Germans launched an offensive that, despite initial successes, ultimately failed strategically
World War I battle in May 1918

Examples

The Third Battle of the Aisne marked one of the last major German offensives of World War I.
Many historians argue that the Third Battle of the Aisne was a turning point in the conflict.
Allied forces regrouped after suffering heavy losses during the Third Battle of the Aisne.
