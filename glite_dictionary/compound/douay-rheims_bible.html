Douay-Rheims Bible

1. PROPN

/ ˌduːeɪ ˈriːmz ˈbaɪbəl / (AmE), / ˌduːeɪ ˈriːmz ˈbaɪbl̩ / (BrE)
An English translation of the Latin Vulgate produced by Roman Catholic scholars in the late 16th and early 17th centuries.
English Catholic Bible translation from Vulgate

Examples

Many Catholics still read the Douay-Rheims Bible for its traditional language.
The Douay-Rheims Bible was the standard English Bible for Catholics before other translations became popular.
He found a rare 17th-century edition of the Douay-Rheims Bible at the library.

Etymology

Named after the French cities of Douai and Rheims, where it was first published.
