the three estates

1. NOUN

/ ði ˈθri ɪˈsteɪts / (AmE), / ði ˈθri ɪˈsteɪts / (BrE)
the three main social classes in certain societies, especially in medieval and early modern Europe, traditionally regarded as the clergy, the nobility, and the commons, each with distinct roles and privileges
the three main social classes in historical societies

Examples

In pre-revolutionary France, the three estates determined a person’s political and social rights.
The king called representatives of the three estates to discuss urgent national matters.
Debates about the privileges of the three estates contributed to the outbreak of revolution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: estate - a major social class in a society, especially in historical contexts

Etymology

From the Old French *estat* (condition, state, class), entered English in the medieval period relating to divisions of society.
