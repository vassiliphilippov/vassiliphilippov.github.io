consciousness-altering drug

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑnʃəsnəs ˈɔltɚɪŋ drʌɡ / (AmE), / ˈkɒnʃəsnəs ˈɔːltərɪŋ drʌɡ / (BrE)
a drug that affects mood, awareness, or perception, often producing changes in mental state or sensory experience
drug affecting mood or perception

Examples

The use of a consciousness-altering drug can lead to changes in perception and mood.
Researchers studied the effects of a consciousness-altering drug on cognitive function.
He warned about the risks involved with taking any consciousness-altering drug.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: psychoactive drug - a drug that affects the mind or mental processes
  • Degree Lower: depressant - a drug that reduces functional or nervous activity
  • Degree Higher: hallucinogen - a substance causing hallucinations or altered perception
