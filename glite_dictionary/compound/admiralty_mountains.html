Admiralty Mountains

1. PROPN

/ ˌæd.mɪˌræl.ti ˈmaʊn.tənz / (AmE), / ˌæd.mɪˌræl.ti ˈmaʊn.tɪnz / (BrE)
a mountain range in Antarctica located north of Victoria Land
mountain range in Antarctica

Examples

The Admiralty Mountains are home to some of Antarctica's most dramatic peaks.
Several research expeditions have explored the remote Admiralty Mountains over the past century.
The glaciers surrounding the Admiralty Mountains are of great interest to climate scientists.

Etymology

Named by Sir James Clark Ross in 1841 after the British Admiralty.
