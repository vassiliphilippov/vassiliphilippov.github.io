longtail weasel

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɔŋˌteɪl ˈwiːzəl / (AmE), / ˈlɒŋteɪl ˈwiːzəl / (BrE)
a common North American weasel distinguished by its large size and a tail tipped with black
a large North American weasel with a black-tipped tail

Examples

The longtail weasel is easily recognized by its black-tipped tail and slender body.
Farmers appreciate the longtail weasel for its role in controlling rodent populations.
We spotted a longtail weasel darting through the underbrush near the creek.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weasel - a small carnivorous mammal with a slender body and short legs
