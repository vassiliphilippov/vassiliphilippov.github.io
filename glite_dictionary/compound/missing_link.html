missing link

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪsɪŋ lɪŋk / (AmE), / ˈmɪsɪŋ lɪŋk / (BrE)
a hypothetical extinct creature believed to be an evolutionary bridge between apes and human beings
hypothetical evolutionary bridge between apes and humans

Examples

Some scientists once believed the missing link would be found in Africa's fossil record.
The discovery was hailed as possible evidence of a missing link in human evolution.
For decades, people searched for the so-called missing link between apes and humans.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɪsɪŋ lɪŋk / (AmE), / ˈmɪsɪŋ lɪŋk / (BrE)
a necessary or essential element that is absent from a system, process, or chain of reasoning, making it incomplete
an absent essential element or connection

Examples

The discovery of the fossil provided the missing link in the evolutionary chain.
We finally identified the missing link that made the experiment successful.
Her testimony was the missing link in the investigation.

Synset Examples
