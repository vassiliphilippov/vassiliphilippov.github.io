serial polygamy

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪriəl pəˈlɪɡəmi / (AmE), / ˈsɪəriəl pəˈlɪɡəmi / (BrE)
the practice of having more than one spouse consecutively, marrying again after divorces or annulments, rather than at the same time
consecutive multiple marriages

Examples

His history of serial polygamy caused controversy among his colleagues.
Some cultures are more accepting of serial polygamy than others.
The documentary explored the emotional impact of serial polygamy on children.

Synset Examples

He was accused of serial polygamy after marrying multiple women.
Her story highlighted the challenges of serial polygamy.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: polygamy - the practice of having more than one spouse at the same time

Etymology

Formed from 'serial' (from Latin 'series') and 'polygamy' (from Greek 'polygamia', many marriages).
