major-league club

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪdʒər liːɡ klʌb / (AmE), / ˈmeɪdʒə liːɡ klʌb / (BrE)
a team that competes in a professional sports league recognized as one of the top divisions, especially in baseball or other major sports.
top-division professional sports team

Examples

The city celebrated when it finally got its own major-league club.
Several players were promoted from the minors to join the major-league club.
Sponsorship deals are highly sought after by every major-league club in the country.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: minor-league club - a team that plays in a lower-tier professional sports league
