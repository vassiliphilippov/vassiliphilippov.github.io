Malabar kino

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæl.əˈbɑr ˈkiː.noʊ / (AmE), / ˌmal.əˈbɑː ˈkiː.nəʊ / (BrE)
A reddish or black resin obtained from certain Pterocarpus trees, used in medicine and tanning.
reddish or black resin from Pterocarpus trees

Examples

The bark of the tree yields Malabar kino, which is valued in traditional medicine.
Tanners historically used Malabar kino as a source of tannin for treating leather.
Scientists studied the chemical composition of Malabar kino for its medicinal properties.

Etymology

Malabar refers to a region in southwestern India; 'kino' from Malay word 'kino', a name for various plant resins.
