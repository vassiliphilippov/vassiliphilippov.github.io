phthalic anhydride

1. NOUN

/ ˌθælɪk ænˈhaɪdraɪd / (AmE), / ˌθalɪk anˈhʌɪdrʌɪd / (BrE)
a white crystalline organic compound used mainly in the production of plastics, dyes, and resins; the anhydride of phthalic acid
white crystalline organic compound
medical

Examples

The production of flexible plastics often uses phthalic anhydride as a key intermediate.
Laboratory technicians handled phthalic anhydride with care due to its irritant properties.
The chemical plant increased output of phthalic anhydride to meet industrial demand.

Etymology

From 'phthalic,' referring to phthalic acid (from naphthalene) + 'anhydride,' from Greek anhydros (without water).
