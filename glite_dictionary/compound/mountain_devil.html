mountain devil

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaʊntn̩ ˌdɛvəl / (AmE), / ˈmaʊntɪn ˌdɛvəl / (BrE)
a bushy shrub of eastern Australia (Lambertia formosa) known for its terminal clusters of red flowers that yield abundant nectar
Australian shrub with red flowers

Examples

The mountain devil attracts a wide variety of birds with its bright red flowers.
During our hike, we spotted several mountain devils growing along the rocky slopes.
Locals often admire the unique shape and color of the mountain devil in full bloom.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmaʊn.tn̩ ˌdɛv.əl / (AmE), / ˈmaʊn.tɪn ˌdɛv.əl / (BrE)
a small Australian desert lizard (Moloch horridus) with spiny scales, feeding mainly on ants
spiny Australian desert lizard

Examples

The mountain devil uses its spiky appearance to deter predators in the harsh Australian outback.
Scientists observed the mountain devil feeding exclusively on ants during their study.
Many tourists hope to spot a mountain devil during their trip to central Australia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thorny devil - another common name for the mountain devil, an Australian spiny lizard
