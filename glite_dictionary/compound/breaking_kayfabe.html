breaking kayfabe

1. NOUN

/ ˈbreɪkɪŋ ˈkeɪfeɪb / (AmE), / ˈbreɪkɪŋ ˈkeɪfeɪb / (BrE)
The act of revealing the real-life truth behind a staged performance, especially in professional wrestling, thereby exposing that it is scripted.
revealing staged truth in wrestling

Examples

The wrestler was criticized for breaking kayfabe during the post-match interview.
When she started breaking kayfabe, the audience realized the rivalry was just an act.
Critics argued that breaking kayfabe took away some of the magic from the show.

Synset Examples

Breaking kayfabe can ruin the illusion for fans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kayfabe - the portrayal of staged events as real in professional wrestling

Etymology

From wrestling slang 'kayfabe' meaning the portrayal of staged events as real; 'breaking' here means violating that convention.
