quartile deviation

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɔr.taɪl diˌviː.ˈeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˈkwɔː.taɪl dɪˌviː.ˈeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
One half of the difference between the first and third quartiles in a set of data, used as a measure of spread in statistics.
statistical measure of spread

Examples

The quartile deviation provides a quick way to assess the spread of scores in a distribution.
Because the data contains outliers, the quartile deviation is considered a more robust measure of dispersion than the standard deviation.
When comparing two datasets, a lower quartile deviation indicates less variability around the median.

Synset Examples

In interpreting the quartile deviation of any distribution of test scores, the size of the value is always the indicator.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interquartile range - The range between the first and third quartiles in a data set

Etymology

From 'quartile', referring to division into four parts, and 'deviation', meaning the amount by which a value differs from a standard.
