herpes zoster virus

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɜrpiːz ˈzoʊstər ˈvaɪrəs / (AmE), / ˌhɜːpiːz ˈzɒstə ˈvaɪərəs / (BrE)
the virus that causes shingles, a painful skin rash; also called varicella-zoster virus
virus causing shingles
medical

Examples

The reactivation of the herpes zoster virus can cause a painful rash known as shingles.
Researchers are developing vaccines to protect against the herpes zoster virus in older adults.
After recovering from chickenpox, the herpes zoster virus can remain dormant in the body for years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: varicella-zoster virus - another name for herpes zoster virus

Etymology

From New Latin: 'herpes' (creeping) + 'zoster' (belt, girdle), referring to the rash's appearance.
