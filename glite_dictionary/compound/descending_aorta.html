descending aorta

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈsɛndɪŋ eɪˈɔrtə / (AmE), / dɪˈsɛndɪŋ eɪˈɔːtə / (BrE)
the section of the aorta that extends downward from the aortic arch through the chest and abdomen, eventually dividing into the thoracic and abdominal aorta
downward part of the aorta
medical

Examples

The surgeon inspected the descending aorta for signs of aneurysm during the procedure.
A blockage in the descending aorta can lead to serious complications due to reduced blood flow to the lower body.
The imaging scan revealed a tear in the descending aorta, requiring immediate medical attention.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ascending aorta - the upward section of the aorta emerging from the heart
  • Degree Lower: aortic arch - the curved portion of the aorta connecting the ascending with the descending part

Etymology

From Latin 'descendere' (to descend) and 'aorta', from Greek 'aortē' (the main artery).
