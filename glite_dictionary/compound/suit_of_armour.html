suit of armour

1. NOUN

/ suːt əv ˈɑrmər / (AmE), / suːt əv ˈɑːmə / (BrE)
A protective covering made of metal plates or chain mail, worn over the entire body, especially by soldiers in the past.
full-body metal protective clothing

Examples

The knight wore a magnificent suit of armour to the royal tournament.
Restorers spent months cleaning the antique suit of armour displayed in the museum.
During the parade, a replica suit of armour dazzled the crowd with its shining metal plates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: armour - Protective covering to prevent damage from weapons or projectiles

Etymology

From Old French 'armeure', from Latin 'armatura' (equipment, arms).
