to the imagination

1. X

/ tə ði ˌɪˌmædʒəˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / tə ði ˌɪˌmadʒɪˈneɪʃn / (BrE)
not explicitly stated; left to the listener's or reader's interpretation or creativity
left for interpretation

Examples

Much of the story's horror is left to the imagination, making it even scarier.
The artist leaves the meaning of her abstract paintings to the imagination of the viewer.
The ending of the film was unresolved, leaving everything to the imagination.

Synset Examples

His vague hints left everything else to the imagination.
