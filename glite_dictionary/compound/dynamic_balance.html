dynamic balance

1. NOUN

/ daɪˈnæmɪk ˈbæl.əns / (AmE), / daɪˈnæmɪk ˈbæl.əns / (BrE)
A state of equilibrium in a rotating object in which centrifugal forces do not produce force in the shaft, thereby minimizing vibration.
state of equilibrium in a rotating object

Examples

The engineer checked the dynamic balance of the turbine to ensure smooth operation.
Proper dynamic balance in a propeller reduces vibration and wear on the engine components.
Achieving dynamic balance can improve the safety and efficiency of rotating machinery.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: static balance - state where an object remains balanced when stationary
