scarlet runner bean

1. NOUN

/ ˈskɑrlət ˈrʌnər bin / (AmE), / ˈskɑːlɪt ˈrʌnə biːn / (BrE)
A type of bean plant, Phaseolus coccineus, grown for its long edible pods, which are often used as a vegetable, especially in British cuisine.
long edible bean pod, British favorite
british

Examples

The cook sliced scarlet runner beans into half-inch pieces for the casserole.
Many gardeners in the UK grow scarlet runner beans for their colorful flowers and tasty pods.
She prefers scarlet runner beans over green beans because of their texture and flavor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: runner bean - a type of bean with climbing vines and edible pods

Etymology

scarlet from Middle English, from Old French escarlate; runner from its twining, running growth habit.

2. NOUN

/ ˈskɑrlət ˈrʌnər bin / (AmE), / ˈskɑːlɪt ˈrʌnə biːn / (BrE)
A climbing perennial plant (Phaseolus coccineus) native to Central America, cultivated for its edible beans and showy red flowers.
climbing bean plant with red flowers

Examples

The scarlet runner bean is popular in British gardens both for its beans and its vibrant red blossoms.
My grandmother always grew scarlet runner beans along the back fence for fresh vegetables in summer.
We harvested a large batch of scarlet runner beans and made a delicious stew.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: runner bean - a bean plant with long edible pods, especially cultivated in Britain

Etymology

Name from its bright scarlet flowers and characteristic climbing habit ('runner').
