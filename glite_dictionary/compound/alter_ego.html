alter ego

1. NOUN

/ ˌɔltər ˈiːɡoʊ / (AmE), / ˌɔːltə ˈiːɡəʊ / (BrE)
a very close and trusted friend who seems almost a part of yourself; a person’s inseparable companion or kindred spirit
a very close and trusted friend

Examples

Everyone knew that Mark and Leo were inseparable, with Leo almost acting as Mark’s alter ego.
She considered her childhood friend her alter ego—someone who always understood her without words.
The novelist’s main character is widely seen as his alter ego in the story.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soulmate - a person ideally suited to another as a close friend or romantic partner
  • Antonym: enemy - a person who is actively opposed or hostile

Etymology

From Latin 'alter' (other) + 'ego' (I, self)

2. NOUN

/ ˌɑltɚ ˈiːgoʊ / (AmE), / ˌɔːltə ˈiːgəʊ / (BrE)
a second personality or identity of a person, often distinct from their ordinary personality
a person's second self or identity

Examples

The comedian performed as his alter ego, captivating the audience with a completely different style.
Writers often use an alter ego to explore aspects of their personalities in their work.
His alter ego is far more outgoing and adventurous than he is in real life.

Synset Examples

The comedian's alter ego, Muskolini, always steals the show.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: double - a person who looks or acts like another, especially as a substitute

Etymology

From Latin 'alter' (other) and 'ego' (I, self), meaning 'other self'.
