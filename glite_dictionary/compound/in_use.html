in use

1. ADJ

/ ɪn jus / (AmE), / ɪn juːs / (BrE)
occupied or being used and therefore not available for someone else; not free.
occupied; not available for use

Examples

The meeting room is currently in use, so we’ll have to wait.
When the restroom is in use, a sign will appear on the door.
All the computers in the lab are in use this afternoon.

Synset Examples

her line is busy
receptionists' telephones are always engaged
the lavatory is in use
kept getting a busy signal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: engaged - (British) occupied or busy, especially of a telephone line or lavatory
  • Antonym: vacant - not occupied; available for use

2. ADJ

/ ɪn juːs / (AmE), / ɪn juːs / (BrE)
being used or operational at the present time
currently being used

Examples

The laboratory equipment is in use and cannot be borrowed right now.
Mobile phones are prohibited while the aircraft is in use on the runway.
Many of the city's old trams are still in use today.

Synset Examples

robots are in use throughout industry

Related Terms

  • Antonym: out of use - not being used or no longer operational
  • Synonym: in operation - currently functioning or being used
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02504834-s
Synset POS: s
