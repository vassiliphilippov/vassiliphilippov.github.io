Pyrrhic victory

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪrɪk ˈvɪktəri / (AmE), / ˈpɪrɪk ˈvɪktəri / (BrE)
A victory won at such a great cost that it is nearly tantamount to defeat.
a costly victory with devastating losses

Examples

The general realized that their supposed triumph was a Pyrrhic victory, having lost most of his best soldiers.
Winning the lawsuit turned out to be a Pyrrhic victory because legal fees left the company bankrupt.
Some historians argue that the battle was a Pyrrhic victory which weakened the nation irreparably.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hollow victory - a victory that is meaningless or provides no real benefit

Etymology

From Pyrrhus, king of Epirus, whose army suffered heavy losses in defeating the Romans (c. 280 BC).
