stabilizer bar

1. NOUN

/ ˈsteɪbəˌlaɪzər bɑr / (AmE), / ˈsteɪbəlaɪzə bɑː / (BrE)
a rigid metal rod connecting the left and right suspension systems of a vehicle, designed to reduce body roll and stabilize the chassis during turns
automotive part reducing body roll

Examples

The mechanic inspected the stabilizer bar to determine the source of the unusual noise.
Upgrading your car's stabilizer bar can improve handling and stability on sharp turns.
A damaged stabilizer bar may cause excessive body roll when driving through curves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anti-roll bar - a bar in cars that reduces chassis roll
  • Degree Higher: chassis - frame of a vehicle
