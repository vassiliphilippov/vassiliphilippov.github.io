tie-up

1. NOUN

/ ˈtaɪ.ʌp / (AmE), / ˈtaɪ.ʌp / (BrE)
a situation in which normal activity is stopped or slowed, often due to a blockage or disruption
an interruption or blockage of activity

Examples

The highway was closed for hours due to a massive tie-up caused by an overturned truck.
A technical tie-up delayed the factory's production line for the entire morning.
Unexpected paperwork created a tie-up at the customs office.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: holdup - a delay or interruption, especially a forced one
  • Synonym: blockage - something that blocks, especially an obstruction or stoppage
  • Antonym: flow - continuous movement, often the opposite of interruption
