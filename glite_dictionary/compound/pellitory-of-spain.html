pellitory-of-Spain

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛləˌtɔri əv ˈspeɪn / (AmE), / ˌpɛlɪˌtɒri əv ˈspeɪn / (BrE)
a small Mediterranean plant (Anacyclus pyrethrum) whose roots contain a volatile oil once used to relieve toothache
Mediterranean plant, formerly used for toothache

Examples

For centuries, apothecaries prescribed pellitory-of-Spain for people suffering from toothache.
She cultivated pellitory-of-Spain in her herb garden and used its roots for traditional remedies.
The old herbal included pellitory-of-Spain among its recommended plants for oral pain relief.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pellitory - a different, unrelated plant (Parietaria) called pellitory or wall pellitory

Etymology

From the practice of distinguishing it from the unrelated European plant called pellitory; refers to its association with Spain.
