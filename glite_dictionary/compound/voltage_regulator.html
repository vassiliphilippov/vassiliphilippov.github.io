voltage regulator

1. NOUN

/ ˈvoʊltɪdʒ ˈrɛɡjəˌleɪtər / (AmE), / ˈvəʊltɪdʒ ˈrɛɡjʊˌleɪtə / (BrE)
a device, especially a transformer, whose output voltage can be maintained at a constant level or adjusted as needed
device for maintaining or adjusting voltage

Examples

The voltage regulator ensures a constant supply of power to sensitive equipment.
If the voltage regulator fails, the circuit may receive too much or too little voltage.
The engineer adjusted the voltage regulator to match the required output for the experiment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transformer - an electrical device for changing the voltage of an alternating current
