stick lac

1. NOUN

/ stɪk læk / (AmE), / stɪk lak / (BrE)
lac in its natural state as scraped from twigs and dried, before processing into refined products
raw lac resin on twigs

Examples

The artisans gathered stick lac from the surrounding forests to use in dye making.
Before further refinement, stick lac is cleaned and crushed to remove impurities.
Much of the world's stick lac is collected from India and Southeast Asia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seed lac - refined lac separated from twigs after initial processing
  • Degree Higher: shellac - purified lac resin used as a varnish or finish

Etymology

From 'stick' (referring to twigs) and 'lac', from Persian 'lak', from Hindi 'lākh' (resinous substance).
