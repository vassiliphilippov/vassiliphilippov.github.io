Tallapoosa River

1. PROPN

/ ˌtæləˈpusə ˈrɪvər / (AmE), / ˌtæləˈpuːsə ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
a river in the southeastern United States that rises in northwestern Georgia and flows southwest through central Alabama, joining the Coosa River near Montgomery to form the Alabama River
river in Georgia and Alabama, USA

Examples

The Tallapoosa River is known for its scenic beauty and historical significance in Alabama.
Many boating enthusiasts enjoy exploring the Tallapoosa River each summer.
The city of Montgomery lies near the confluence of the Tallapoosa River and the Coosa River.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Coosa River - river in the southeastern U.S. joined by the Tallapoosa to form the Alabama River
  • Synonym: Alabama River - river formed by the joining of the Tallapoosa and Coosa rivers

Etymology

Derived from Creek (Muscogee) language, possibly meaning 'pulverized rock'.
