immunoglobulin E

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪm.ju.noʊˌɡlɑ.bjə.lɪn i / (AmE), / ˌɪm.juː.nəʊˌɡlɒb.jʊ.lɪn iː / (BrE)
A class of antibody found mainly in the skin and mucous membranes, involved in allergic reactions and immune responses to parasites.
a class of antibody involved in allergies
medical

Examples

High levels of immunoglobulin E can indicate an allergic response.
Doctors measured the patient's immunoglobulin E to help diagnose his symptoms.
Exposure to pollen increased her immunoglobulin E levels dramatically.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antibody - a protein produced by the immune system to neutralize pathogens
  • Degree Lower: immunoglobulin G - the most common type of antibody in blood circulation
  • Degree Higher: immunoglobulin D - another class of immunoglobulin with different immune functions

Etymology

From 'immuno-' (relating to immunity) and 'globulin' (a group of proteins), with 'E' indicating its specific class among immunoglobulins.
