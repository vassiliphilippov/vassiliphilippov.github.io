Second Epistle of John

1. PROPN

/ ˌsɛkənd ɪˈpɪsəl əv dʒɑn / (AmE), / ˌsɛkənd ɪˈpɪsəl əv dʒɒn / (BrE)
the second letter in the New Testament, traditionally attributed to Saint John the Apostle
a book of the New Testament

Examples

Many scholars believe the Second Epistle of John addresses early Christian communities facing false teachings.
The Second Epistle of John is one of the shortest books in the New Testament.
She read a passage from the Second Epistle of John during the church service.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: First Epistle of John - the first New Testament letter attributed to John
  • Degree Higher: Third Epistle of John - the third New Testament letter attributed to John

Etymology

Epistle from Latin epistola (letter), from Greek epistolē (message, letter).

2. PROPN

/ ˈsɛkənd ɪˈpɪsəl əv dʒɑn / (AmE), / ˈsɛkənd ɪˈpɪsəl əv dʒɒn / (BrE)
the second letter in the New Testament traditionally attributed to Saint John the Apostle, addressed to 'the elect lady and her children'
a New Testament letter by Saint John

Examples

Many scholars debate the authorship of the Second Epistle of John and its intended audience.
During the sermon, the pastor read from the Second Epistle of John to emphasize the importance of truth and love.
The Second Epistle of John warns believers to be wary of deceivers who deny the teachings of Christ.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: First Epistle of John - the first letter in the New Testament attributed to John
  • Degree Higher: Third Epistle of John - the third letter in the New Testament attributed to John

Etymology

From Latin 'epistola' (letter) and the traditional name of John the Apostle.
