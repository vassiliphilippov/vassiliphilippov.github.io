embryonal rhabdosarcoma

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛm.briˈoʊ.nəl ˌræb.doʊ.sɑrˈkoʊ.mə / (AmE), / ˌɛm.brɪˈəʊ.nəl ˌræb.dəʊ.sɑːˈkəʊ.mə / (BrE)
A type of rhabdomyosarcoma that primarily affects infants and children, characterized by cancerous cells resembling embryonic muscle tissue.
cancer affecting infants/children
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that embryonal rhabdosarcoma is most often diagnosed in young children.
Treatment for embryonal rhabdosarcoma may include surgery, chemotherapy, and radiation therapy.
Research into embryonal rhabdosarcoma is helping improve outcomes for pediatric cancer patients.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rhabdomyosarcoma - A malignant tumor involving skeletal muscle tissue

Etymology

From 'embryonal' (relating to an embryo) and Greek 'rhabdos' (rod) + 'sarcoma' (malignant tumor of connective tissue).
