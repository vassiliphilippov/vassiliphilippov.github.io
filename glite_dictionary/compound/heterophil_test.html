heterophil test

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛtəroʊˌfɪl tɛst / (AmE), / ˈhɛtərəʊˌfɪl tɛst / (BrE)
a blood test used to detect heterophil antibodies, commonly for diagnosing infectious mononucleosis
test for mononucleosis antibodies
medical

Examples

The doctor ordered a heterophil test to confirm the suspected case of mononucleosis.
A positive heterophil test is indicative of an Epstein-Barr virus infection.
If the patient's heterophil test comes back negative, further testing may be needed.

Etymology

From 'heterophil', referring to antibodies that react with cells from a different species, and 'test'.
