Banti's syndrome

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑːn.tiz ˌsɪn.droʊm / (AmE), / ˈbæn.tiz ˌsɪn.drəʊm / (BrE)
a rare disease marked by congestion and enlargement of the spleen, often accompanied by anemia or cirrhosis of the liver
disease with enlarged spleen and liver symptoms
medical

Examples

Doctors diagnosed the patient with Banti's syndrome after observing persistent splenic enlargement and anemia.
Treatment options for Banti's syndrome often involve managing complications from portal hypertension.
Advancements in medicine have improved outcomes for people living with Banti's syndrome.

Etymology

Named after Italian physician Guido Banti (1852–1925) who first described the syndrome.
