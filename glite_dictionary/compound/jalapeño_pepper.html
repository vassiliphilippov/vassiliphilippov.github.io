jalapeño pepper

1. NOUN

/ hɑːləˈpeɪnjoʊ ˈpɛpər / (AmE), / ˌhaləˈpeɪnjəʊ ˈpɛpə / (BrE)
A medium-sized, spicy chili pepper of Mexican origin, usually green or red when ripe, commonly used in cooking.
a medium-sized, spicy Mexican chili pepper

Examples

I love adding sliced jalapeño pepper to my nachos for some extra heat.
The recipe calls for one finely chopped jalapeño pepper to spice up the salsa.
She removed the seeds from the jalapeño pepper to make the dish less spicy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chili pepper - the fruit of plants from the genus Capsicum, often spicy

Etymology

From Spanish 'jalapeño', meaning 'from Jalapa', a city in Mexico.
