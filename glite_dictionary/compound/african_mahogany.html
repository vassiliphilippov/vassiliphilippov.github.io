African mahogany

1. NOUN

/ ˈæf.rɪ.kən məˈhɑː.ɡəni / (AmE), / ˈæf.rɪ.kən məˈhɒɡ.əni / (BrE)
A species of tree native to Africa, known for its hard, heavy, odorless wood often used in furniture and construction.
African hardwood tree; valuable timber

Examples

The furniture was crafted from fine African mahogany prized for its rich color and strength.
They imported African mahogany for the new concert hall's paneling.
Due to overharvesting, some species of African mahogany are considered threatened.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mahogany - a tropical hardwood tree or its timber
