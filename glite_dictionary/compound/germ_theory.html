germ theory

1. NOUN

/ dʒɝm ˈθiəri / (AmE), / dʒɜːm ˈθɪəri / (BrE)
the scientific theory that infectious diseases are caused by microorganisms such as bacteria, viruses, or fungi
theory that diseases are caused by microorganisms
medical

Examples

The acceptance of the germ theory in the 19th century revolutionized medical science.
Modern hygiene practices are based on the principles of the germ theory.
Before the germ theory, people had very different ideas about the causes of disease.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: contagion theory - theory stating diseases are spread by direct contact or by infected objects
