congressional district

1. NOUN

/ kənˈɡrɛʃənəl ˈdɪstrɪkt / (AmE), / kənˈɡrɛʃənəl ˈdɪstrɪkt / (BrE)
a territorial division of a U.S. state, each of which elects a single member to the United States House of Representatives
U.S. voting area for House of Representatives
american legal

Examples

Her campaign focused on winning the largest congressional district in the state.
Each congressional district is designed to have roughly equal population for fair representation.
The boundaries of his congressional district were redrawn after the last census.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: electoral district - an area represented by an elected official
