steam pipe

1. NOUN

/ stiːm paɪp / (AmE), / stiːm paɪp / (BrE)
a pipe designed to convey steam, typically under pressure, from one location to another
pipe for conveying steam

Examples

The old factory still uses a steam pipe system to heat its buildings.
A burst steam pipe caused a major disruption during the morning commute.
Engineers inspected the steam pipe for possible leaks after the pressure drop.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pipe - a tube through which liquids or gases can flow
