moonlight flit

1. NOUN

/ ˈmuːnˌlaɪt flɪt / (AmE), / ˈmuːnˌlaɪt flɪt / (BrE)
a secret or sudden departure, especially at night, to avoid paying debts or fulfilling obligations
secret nighttime departure to avoid obligations
british

Examples

Rumor had it that the landlord's tenants had done a moonlight flit without paying the rent.
They decided a moonlight flit was the only way to escape their mounting debts.
After the scandal broke, a moonlight flit seemed like their best option.

Synset Examples

During the party, they executed a moonlight flit to a beach nearby.
They planned a moonlight flit to escape the city.
The couple made a moonlight flit to avoid their families.

Etymology

From 'moonlight' (nighttime light of the moon) + 'flit' (to move swiftly), originally British slang from the 19th century.
