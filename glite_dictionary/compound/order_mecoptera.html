order Mecoptera

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdɚ məˈkɑptərə / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə mɪˈkɒptərə / (BrE)
An order of carnivorous insects characterized by long, membranous wings and elongated beaklike heads with chewing mouthparts at the tip.
an order of insects with beaklike heads

Examples

Scientists observed that some members of the order Mecoptera have intricate courtship rituals.
The elongated heads of insects in the order Mecoptera are adapted for capturing prey.
Students in entomology studied the distinct wing patterns found in the order Mecoptera.

Etymology

From Greek 'mēkōs' (length) and 'pteron' (wing), referring to the long wings.
