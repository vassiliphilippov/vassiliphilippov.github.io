beast of prey

1. NOUN

/ bist əv preɪ / (AmE), / biːst əv preɪ / (BrE)
an animal that hunts and kills other animals for food; a predatory animal
predatory animal

Examples

The wildlife documentary captured the beast of prey stalking its victim with remarkable patience.
Among the animals of the forest, the tiger is a notorious beast of prey.
The falcon, as a beast of prey, has sharp talons and keen eyesight.

Synset Examples

The wildlife documentary showcased the beast of prey in its natural habitat.
The eagle, a true beast of prey, swooped down for its meal.
Lions are known as the beasts of prey in the African savannah.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: predator - an animal that lives by preying on others
  • Antonym: prey - an animal hunted or seized for food by a predator
