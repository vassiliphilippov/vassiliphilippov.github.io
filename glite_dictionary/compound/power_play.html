power play

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaʊ.ɚ pleɪ / (AmE), / ˈpaʊ.ə pleɪ / (BrE)
an aggressive action or strategy designed to gain control or force others to comply, often by concentrating or manipulating power
aggressive strategy for control

Examples

The CEO's abrupt takeover was seen as a clear power play by the board of directors.
During the negotiation, her constant interruptions became an obvious power play to assert dominance.
Some politicians rely on backroom power plays rather than open debate to get their way.

Synset Examples

she laughed at this sexual power play and walked away

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maneuver - a movement or series of moves requiring skill and care, often to gain advantage
  • Synonym: coercion - the practice of persuading someone to do something by using force or threats
  • Antonym: compromise - an agreement reached by each side making concessions

2. NOUN

/ ˈpaʊ.ɚ pleɪ / (AmE), / ˈpaʊ.ə pleɪ / (BrE)
In ice hockey, a situation in which one team has a numerical advantage over the other due to penalties, allowing it to play with more players on the ice.
ice hockey: team has advantage due to penalties

Examples

The team failed to score during their power play in the second period.
He was sent to the penalty box, giving the opponents a crucial power play opportunity.
A well-executed power play can change the course of a hockey game.

Synset Examples

the team was unable to capitalize on the power play

3. NOUN

/ ˈpaʊər pleɪ / (AmE), / ˈpaʊə pleɪ / (BrE)
a tactic in a team sport in which several players concentrate in one area of the field to gain an advantage
sports tactic involving player concentration

Examples

The coach called for a power play near the goal line to break through the defense.
They executed a brilliant power play during the final minutes of the game.
Our team often practices the power play to maximize our chances of scoring.

Synset Examples

they used a power play to return the kickoff
