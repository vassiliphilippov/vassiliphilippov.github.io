cladistic analysis

1. NOUN

/ kləˈdɪstɪk əˈnæləsɪs / (AmE), / kləˈdɪstɪk əˈnalɪsɪs / (BrE)
A method of classifying species based on the evolutionary relationships inferred from branching patterns in cladograms.
classification method in biology
medical

Examples

Recent advances in genetic data have improved the accuracy of cladistic analysis in classifying plant families.
The results of the cladistic analysis were published in a leading scientific journal.
Researchers conducted a cladistic analysis to determine the evolutionary history of the species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phylogenetic analysis - analysis of evolutionary relationships among organisms
  • Synonym: taxonomy - the science of classifying organisms

Etymology

From 'cladistics' (from Greek 'klados' meaning 'branch') + 'analysis'.
