reticuloendothelial system

1. NOUN

/ rɪˌtɪk.ju.loʊˌɛn.doʊˈθi.li.əl ˈsɪs.təm / (AmE), / rɪˌtɪk.jʊ.ləʊˌɛn.dəʊˈθiː.li.əl ˈsɪs.təm / (BrE)
a system in the body made up of cells that can ingest bacteria and foreign particles, playing a role in immune defense
body system of particle-ingesting cells
medical

Examples

The reticuloendothelial system helps filter out harmful substances from the blood.
Certain infections can overwhelm the reticuloendothelial system, leading to serious illness.
Researchers study the reticuloendothelial system to understand how the body fights bacteria.

Etymology

From Latin 'reticulum' (network) and Greek 'endon' (within) + 'thelē' (nipple, used in medical terms for cellular lining).
