disc jockey

1. NOUN

/ dɪsk ˈdʒɑki / (AmE), / dɪsk ˈdʒɒki / (BrE)
a person whose job is to select, announce, and play recorded music for an audience, especially on radio or at events
person who plays and announces recorded music

Examples

The disc jockey played my favorite song during the party last night.
She works as a disc jockey at a popular local radio station.
Many couples hire a professional disc jockey for their wedding receptions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: DJ - abbreviation for disc jockey

Etymology

From 'disc' (record) and 'jockey' (controller or operator); originated in 1940s US radio culture.
