mercantile establishment

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɜːrkənˌtaɪl əˈstæblɪʃmənt / (AmE), / ˈmɜːkəntaɪl ɪˈstæblɪʃmənt / (BrE)
a place where goods are sold to the public; a retail business or store
a retail business or store

Examples

The new mercantile establishment on Main Street attracts customers from across the city.
Every mercantile establishment is required to display its business license clearly.
The town's oldest mercantile establishment has been family-owned for three generations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: store - a place where goods are sold to the public
  • Synonym: shop - a building or part of a building where goods or services are sold

Etymology

From Latin 'mercari' (to trade) and 'establishment'.
