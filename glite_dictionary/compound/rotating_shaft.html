rotating shaft

1. NOUN

/ ˈroʊˌteɪtɪŋ ʃæft / (AmE), / ˈrəʊˌteɪtɪŋ ʃɑːft / (BrE)
a rod or bar that turns around its axis to transmit power or motion in a machine
a rod that transmits power by rotating

Examples

The motor is connected to the rotating shaft that drives the wheels.
Regular lubrication of the rotating shaft helps prevent mechanical failure.
Engineers inspected the rotating shaft for signs of wear and tear.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: axle - a rod or spindle passing through the center of a wheel or group of wheels
