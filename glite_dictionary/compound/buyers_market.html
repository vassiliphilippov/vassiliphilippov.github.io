buyers' market

1. NOUN

/ ˈbaɪɚz ˈmɑrkɪt / (AmE), / ˈbaɪəz ˈmɑːkɪt / (BrE)
A market condition in which the supply of goods exceeds demand, giving buyers an advantage over sellers in price negotiations.
market with advantage to buyers

Examples

It's a buyers' market right now, so housing prices are lower than usual.
In a buyers' market, sellers may have to accept reduced offers to make a sale.
During economic downturns, the stock market can quickly turn into a buyers' market.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sellers' market - market where sellers have an advantage due to high demand
