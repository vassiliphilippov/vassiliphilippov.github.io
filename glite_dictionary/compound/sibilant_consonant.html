sibilant consonant

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪbɪlənt ˈkɑnsənənt / (AmE), / ˈsɪbɪlənt ˈkɒnsənənt / (BrE)
a consonant sound characterized by a hissing or hushing quality, such as the sounds 's' and 'sh' in English
consonant with a hissing sound

Examples

The sound of sibilant consonants can be heard in words like 'snake' and 'shell'.
She struggled to pronounce sibilant consonants clearly due to her speech impediment.
Linguists study how sibilant consonants vary across different languages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sibilant - a speech sound with a hissing quality

Etymology

From Latin sibilans (hissing), from sibilare (to hiss) + consonant.
