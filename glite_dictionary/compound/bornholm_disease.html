Bornholm disease

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɔrn.hoʊm dɪˌziz / (AmE), / ˈbɔːn.hɒlm dɪˌziːz / (BrE)
an acute viral infection characterized by sudden, severe chest or abdominal pain, fever, and muscle tenderness, often occurring in epidemics
acute viral illness with chest pain
medical

Examples

Several children in the village were diagnosed with Bornholm disease after experiencing severe chest pain and fever.
The outbreak of Bornholm disease caused concern among local health officials due to its epidemic nature.
Doctors advised rest and pain relief for those suffering from Bornholm disease, as the symptoms were often intense but short-lived.

Etymology

Named after Bornholm, an island in Denmark, where an outbreak was first described.
