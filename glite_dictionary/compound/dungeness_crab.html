Dungeness crab

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʌndʒənəs kræb / (AmE), / ˈdʌndʒɪnɪs kræb / (BrE)
The edible flesh of the large Pacific crab (Cancer magister), valued as seafood.
edible flesh of a large Pacific crab

Examples

The chef prepared a fresh salad with tender pieces of Dungeness crab.
We ordered steamed Dungeness crab at the coastal restaurant for dinner.
Harvesting Dungeness crab is an important part of the fishing industry in the Pacific Northwest.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: crab - the flesh of any crab, especially as food

Etymology

Named after Dungeness, a bay in Washington State, USA, where the species was first commercially harvested.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʌn.dʒɪ.nəs kræb / (AmE), / ˈdʌn.dʒɪ.nɪs krab / (BrE)
a large edible crab (Metacarcinus magister) found along the Pacific coast of North America, valued for its sweet, tender meat
edible Pacific North American crab

Examples

The restaurant's special tonight is fresh Dungeness crab served with drawn butter.
We caught several Dungeness crabs on our trip to the Oregon coast.
Dungeness crab season typically starts in late fall along the Pacific Northwest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crab - a marine crustacean with a broad carapace, stalked eyes, and five pairs of legs

Etymology

Named after Dungeness, a fishing village in Washington State, USA.
