New Zealand Islands

1. PROPN

/ nuː ˈziːlənd ˈaɪləndz / (AmE), / njuː ˈziːlənd ˈaɪləndz / (BrE)
the principal islands of New Zealand, including the North Island, South Island, and adjacent smaller islands in the South Pacific
the main islands of New Zealand

Examples

Tourists from around the world visit the New Zealand Islands for their stunning natural beauty.
The unique wildlife of the New Zealand Islands has evolved in isolation over millions of years.
Earthquakes occasionally affect the New Zealand Islands, especially in the central regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: North Island - the northern of the two main islands of New Zealand
  • Synonym: South Island - the southern of the two main islands of New Zealand
