false chamomile

1. NOUN

/ fɔls ˈkæməˌmil / (AmE), / fɔːls ˈkaməmiːl / (BrE)
any of several autumn-flowering perennial plants, especially of the genus Boltonia, with white, pink, or purple daisy-like flowers, native to moist soils in eastern and southern North America
autumn-flowering perennial resembling chamomile

Examples

The wild meadow was filled with false chamomile blooming beside the stream in late September.
Gardeners sometimes plant false chamomile for its attractive, aster-like flowers and long blooming period.
Unlike true chamomile, false chamomile prefers wetter soils and is more commonly found near marshes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Boltonia - a genus of North American flowering plants commonly called false chamomile
