nominative case

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɑːmənətɪv keɪs / (AmE), / ˈnɒmɪnətɪv keɪs / (BrE)
the grammatical case of a noun or pronoun used as the subject of a verb
grammatical subject case

Examples

In Latin, the nominative case indicates the subject of the sentence.
The English language does not have distinct forms for the nominative case in most nouns.
She explained how pronouns change in the nominative case and the objective case.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: accusative case - grammatical case for direct objects

Etymology

From Latin nominativus (pertaining to naming), from nominare (to name).
