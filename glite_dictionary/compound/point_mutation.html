point mutation

1. NOUN

/ pɔɪnt mjuˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / pɔɪnt mjuˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
a genetic mutation in which a single nucleotide base in the DNA sequence is altered, inserted, or deleted
mutation affecting a single DNA base
medical

Examples

A point mutation in the gene can lead to a serious genetic disorder.
Researchers identified a point mutation in the DNA sequence responsible for the disease.
Not all point mutations result in observable changes to the organism.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: frameshift mutation - a genetic mutation caused by insertions or deletions of nucleotides that change the reading frame
  • Degree Higher: missense mutation - a point mutation that results in a codon coding for a different amino acid
  • Degree Lower: silent mutation - a point mutation that does not change the amino acid sequence

Etymology

From 'point' (indicating a single position) and 'mutation' (change); used in genetics since the mid-20th century.
