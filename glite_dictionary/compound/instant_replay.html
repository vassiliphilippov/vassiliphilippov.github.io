instant replay

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪn.stənt rɪˌpleɪ / (AmE), / ˈɪn.stənt ˈriː.pleɪ / (BrE)
the immediate rebroadcast of a segment of recorded action, especially in sports, to allow viewers to see it again moments after it happens
immediate rebroadcast of recorded action

Examples

The controversial goal was shown on instant replay to help the referees make a decision.
Thanks to instant replay, viewers were able to watch the athlete's incredible move again.
Many sports fans rely on instant replay to catch details they missed during live action.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: replay - the act of playing something again

Etymology

Formed from 'instant' (immediate) and 'replay' (playing again).
