King Oedipus

1. PROPN

/ kɪŋ oʊˈdɪpəs / (AmE), / kɪŋ ˈiːdɪpəs / (BrE)
a tragic king of Thebes in Greek mythology who unwittingly killed his father and married his mother, as told in Sophocles' play 'Oedipus Rex'
tragic king in Greek mythology; subject of 'Oedipus Rex'

Examples

Many scholars analyze the fate of King Oedipus as a central theme in classical literature.
The story of King Oedipus has influenced countless works in drama and psychology.
In Greek mythology, King Oedipus unknowingly fulfills a tragic prophecy.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Oedipus complex - a psychoanalytic concept named after Oedipus, referring to a child's feelings of desire for the opposite-sex parent
  • Synonym: Jocasta - mother and wife of Oedipus in Greek mythology

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Οἰδίπους (Oidípous), the central character of Sophocles' tragedy.
