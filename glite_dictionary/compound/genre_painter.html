genre painter

1. NOUN

/ ˈʒɑnrə ˌpeɪntər / (AmE), / ˈʒɒnrə ˌpeɪntə / (BrE)
an artist who specializes in painting scenes from everyday life
artist painting everyday life scenes

Examples

The museum features works by a renowned genre painter from the nineteenth century.
As a genre painter, she focused on depicting daily market scenes and village life.
Many genre painters captured the ordinary moments that defined their era.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: portraitist - an artist who paints portraits
  • Synonym: landscape painter - an artist who paints natural scenery

Etymology

From French 'genre' (kind, style) and English 'painter'; 'genre painting' became a term in art history in the 18th century.
