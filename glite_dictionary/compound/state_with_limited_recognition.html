state with limited recognition

1. NOUN

/ steɪt wɪð ˌlɪmɪtɪd ˌrɛkəɡˈnɪʃən / (AmE), / steɪt wɪð ˌlɪmɪtɪd ˌrɛkəɡˈnɪʃən / (BrE)
a political entity that exercises de facto sovereignty over a territory but lacks wide international recognition as a sovereign state
political entity lacking broad recognition

Examples

Kosovo is often cited as a state with limited recognition in international law textbooks.
A state with limited recognition may operate its own government despite political challenges.
The organization refused to admit any state with limited recognition as a full member.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: sovereign state - an independent and internationally recognized country
  • Synonym: unrecognized state - a political entity claiming independence but lacking international recognition
