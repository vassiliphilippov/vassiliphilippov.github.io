jaundice of the newborn

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɔndɪs əv ðə ˈnuːˌbɔrn / (AmE), / ˈdʒɔːndɪs əv ðə ˈnjuːˌbɔːn / (BrE)
A condition causing a yellowish appearance in newborn infants, usually resolving without treatment.
yellowish skin in newborn babies
medical

Examples

Physicians monitor babies for signs of jaundice of the newborn in the first week after birth.
The yellowish tint of his skin was due to jaundice of the newborn, which resolved naturally.
Parents are often reassured that jaundice of the newborn usually disappears on its own within a couple of weeks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jaundice - yellow discoloration of the skin due to excess bilirubin
