cat squirrel

1. NOUN

/ kæt ˈskwɝl / (AmE), / kat ˈskwɪəl / (BrE)
A large tree squirrel native to eastern North America, known for its bushy tail; often called the eastern fox squirrel.
large North American tree squirrel

Examples

We spotted a cat squirrel gathering acorns near the edge of the woods.
The cat squirrel is larger than most other squirrels found in the area.
After being introduced to England, the cat squirrel adapted well to its new environment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fox squirrel - another name for cat squirrel, a large tree squirrel native to eastern North America

2. NOUN

/ kæt ˈskwɝl / (AmE), / kæt ˈskwɪəl / (BrE)
a reddish-brown tree squirrel native to Europe and parts of Asia, also known as the Eurasian red squirrel
reddish-brown tree squirrel of Europe and Asia

Examples

The cat squirrel is easily identified by its tufted ears and bushy tail.
Across the forest, a cat squirrel darted between the branches, seeking acorns.
Unlike the gray squirrel, the cat squirrel is more solitary and prefers coniferous woods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: red squirrel - a tree squirrel with reddish fur, native to Eurasia and North America
