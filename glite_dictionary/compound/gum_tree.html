gum tree

1. NOUN

/ ɡʌm triː / (AmE), / ɡʌm triː / (BrE)
Any of various trees, especially eucalypts, that produce a fragrant resin or gum.
tree that produces aromatic gum or resin

Examples

The old gum tree in the yard filled the air with its sweet scent.
Koalas are known for living and feeding in gum trees across Australia.
The sticky sap from the gum tree stained our hands as we played nearby.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eucalyptus - a genus of trees native to Australia, often called gum trees
