deadly nightshade

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛdli ˈnaɪtˌʃeɪd / (AmE), / ˈdɛdli ˈnaɪtʃeɪd / (BrE)
a poisonous Eurasian perennial plant (Atropa belladonna) with reddish bell-shaped flowers and glossy black berries, source of the drug atropine
poisonous plant; source of atropine

Examples

The berries of deadly nightshade are extremely poisonous if ingested.
Traditional herbalists sometimes used deadly nightshade in remedies, though it is highly toxic.
You can recognize deadly nightshade by its dark, glossy berries and bell-shaped flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: belladonna - alternative name for deadly nightshade, especially as a medicinal extract

Etymology

From 'deadly', meaning fatal, and 'nightshade', an Old English term for certain poisonous plants.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɛdli ˈnaɪtˌʃeɪd / (AmE), / ˈdɛdli ˈnaɪtʃeɪd / (BrE)
a highly poisonous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia, with purple flowers and shiny black or red berries; also known as belladonna
a poisonous plant with purple flowers and dark berries

Examples

Children are often warned not to touch deadly nightshade because of its toxic berries.
The garden contained several medicinal herbs, including deadly nightshade used in small doses for treatments.
Consuming the berries of deadly nightshade can be fatal, so identifying it is important.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: belladonna - another name for deadly nightshade

Etymology

From Old English 'nihtscadu' (night shade), with 'deadly' added for emphasis on its toxicity.
