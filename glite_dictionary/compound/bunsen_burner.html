Bunsen burner

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌnsən ˌbɝːnər / (AmE), / ˈbʌnsən ˌbɜːnə / (BrE)
A small adjustable gas burner used in laboratories, with an air valve to regulate the mixture of gas and air.
laboratory gas burner with air valve

Examples

The chemistry teacher demonstrated the reaction by heating the test tube over a Bunsen burner.
Before using the Bunsen burner, make sure the air valve is adjusted for a blue flame.
In the laboratory, each workstation is equipped with its own Bunsen burner.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spirit lamp - a simple device for heating, using alcohol instead of gas

Etymology

Named after German chemist Robert Bunsen (1811–1899), who popularized its use.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbʌn.sən ˌbɝː.nɚ / (AmE), / ˈbʌn.sən ˌbɜː.nə / (BrE)
a laboratory gas burner with an adjustable air supply, used for heating, sterilization, and combustion
laboratory gas burner

Examples

The chemistry teacher demonstrated how to safely light a Bunsen burner for the experiment.
Always check the air valve before using the Bunsen burner to ensure a clean, blue flame.
She heated the test tube over a Bunsen burner until the solution began to boil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spirit lamp - a laboratory burner using alcohol instead of gas

Etymology

Named after German chemist Robert Bunsen (1811–1899), who popularized its use.
