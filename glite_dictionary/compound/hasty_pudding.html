hasty pudding

1. NOUN

/ ˈheɪsti ˈpʊdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈheɪsti ˈpʊdɪŋ / (BrE)
A traditional dish made from cornmeal mush, typically served with sweeteners such as maple syrup or brown sugar.
cornmeal mush dessert

Examples

For breakfast, we had hasty pudding topped with fresh maple syrup.
He made a batch of hasty pudding as a simple, comforting dessert.
In colonial America, hasty pudding was a staple food during the winter months.

Etymology

From 'hasty' (quick to make) and 'pudding' (in the sense of a boiled or steamed dish); originally referred to a quickly prepared porridge.

2. NOUN

/ ˈheɪsti ˈpʊdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈheɪsti ˈpʊdɪŋ / (BrE)
a sweet dish made by boiling flour, oatmeal, or cornmeal with milk or water, usually served hot
sweet porridge-like dish

Examples

My grandmother always made hasty pudding on cold winter mornings.
Traditional hasty pudding is simple to prepare and requires only a few ingredients.
The recipe for hasty pudding has been passed down through generations in many families.

Etymology

From 'hasty' (quick) and 'pudding' (a dish made by boiling grains), first attested in the 16th century.
