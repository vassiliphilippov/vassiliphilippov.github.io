yellow pine

1. NOUN

/ ˈjɛloʊ paɪn / (AmE), / ˈjɛləʊ paɪn / (BrE)
the hard, yellowish wood of any of several pine trees, especially found in the southeastern United States, used for construction and furniture
hard yellowish wood from certain pines

Examples

The cabin was built from sturdy yellow pine logs harvested from the local forest.
Carpenters often prefer yellow pine for its strength and resistance to wear.
The furniture's warm hue comes from carefully selected yellow pine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pine - an evergreen coniferous tree with needle-shaped leaves and cones

2. NOUN

/ ˈjɛloʊ paɪn / (AmE), / ˈjɛləʊ paɪn / (BrE)
any of several species of pine tree valued for their yellowish wood, commonly used in building and carpentry
pine tree species with yellowish wood

Examples

The furniture was made from sturdy yellow pine harvested locally.
Many homes in the region feature yellow pine floors due to its durability and attractive color.
The carpenter prefers yellow pine for framing because it holds nails well.
