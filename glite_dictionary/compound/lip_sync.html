lip sync

1. NOUN

/ lɪp sɪŋk / (AmE), / lɪp sɪŋk / (BrE)
the synchronization of a person's lip movements with recorded spoken or sung audio, especially in film, television, or live performance
synchronization of lip movements with audio

Examples

The actor had to lip sync to the pre-recorded vocals for the scene.
Many pop stars lip sync during televised performances to ensure perfect sound quality.
Poor lip sync can be distracting in dubbed foreign films.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lip synchronization - synching lip movements to audio

Etymology

Shortened from 'lip synchronization', based on 'lip' (mouth movement) and 'sync' (abbreviation of 'synchronize').
