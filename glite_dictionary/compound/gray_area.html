gray area

1. NOUN

/ ɡreɪ ˈɛriə / (AmE), / ɡreɪ ˈeəriə / (BrE)
an unclear or undefined situation or topic that does not fit neatly into a particular category
an unclear or undefined situation

Examples

The question of privacy on social media remains a gray area for both users and lawmakers.
There's a gray area in the rules that makes it hard to know what's allowed.
Many ethical issues in science occupy a gray area between right and wrong.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dilemma - a situation requiring a choice between equally undesirable alternatives
  • Antonym: black-and-white - clearly defined or unambiguous (the opposite of 'gray area')
