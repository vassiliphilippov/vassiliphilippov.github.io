National Liberation Army

1. PROPN

/ ˈnæʃənəl lɪˌbɚˈreɪʃən ˈɑːrmi / (AmE), / ˈnæʃənəl lɪb.əˈreɪ.ʃən ˈɑːmi / (BrE)
a Marxist-Leninist guerrilla organization in Bolivia, originally established in the 1960s by Che Guevara and later revived, acting as an umbrella for various indigenous subversive groups
Bolivian Marxist-Leninist guerrilla organization

Examples

The Bolivian government increased its military presence in areas controlled by the National Liberation Army.
Che Guevara played a major role in establishing the National Liberation Army during the 1960s.
Reports indicated that the National Liberation Army was responsible for several attacks on police stations.

2. PROPN

/ ˈnæʃənəl lɪbəˈreɪʃən ˈɑːrmi / (AmE), / ˈnæʃənəl lɪbəˈreɪʃən ˈɑːmi / (BrE)
a Marxist insurgent group founded in Colombia in 1963, known for kidnappings, armed resistance, and involvement in the drug trade
Colombian Marxist insurgent group

Examples

The Colombian government continues to negotiate with the National Liberation Army in hopes of ending decades of conflict.
Several foreign oil workers were kidnapped by the National Liberation Army in the region last year.
After the peace deal with FARC, attention shifted to the activities of the National Liberation Army.

Synset Examples

ELN kidnappers target foreign employees of large corporations

Related Terms
