duffle coat

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʌfəl koʊt / (AmE), / ˈdʌfəl kəʊt / (BrE)
a warm, knee-length coat of thick, coarse material, typically with a hood and fastened with wooden or horn toggles
a heavy coat with a hood and toggle fastenings

Examples

He wore a navy blue duffle coat to keep warm during the winter months.
Her father’s old duffle coat was still hanging in the hallway.
Toggle fastenings make the duffle coat easy to put on even with gloves.

Etymology

From duffel, a type of coarse woolen cloth, named after Duffel, a town in Belgium where the material originated.
