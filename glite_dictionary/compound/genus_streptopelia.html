genus Streptopelia

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˌstrɛptəˈpiːliə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˌstrɛptəˈpiːlɪə / (BrE)
A genus of birds in the pigeon family, including turtledoves and some related species.
a bird genus; turtledoves and allies

Examples

The genus Streptopelia includes several species of turtledoves commonly found in Europe and Asia.
Recent studies have shown that members of the genus Streptopelia exhibit diverse vocalizations.
Conservationists are monitoring populations within the genus Streptopelia due to habitat loss.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: turtledove - a species within the genus Streptopelia

Etymology

From New Latin, where 'Streptopelia' combines Greek 'streptos' (twisted) and 'peleia' (dove).
