megalocomparative approach

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛɡəloʊkəmˈpærətɪv əˈproʊtʃ / (AmE), / ˌmɛɡələʊkəmˈpærətɪv əˈprəʊtʃ / (BrE)
an approach that involves large-scale comparison, especially one characterized by exaggeration or emphasis in style or expression
large-scale or exaggerated comparative method

Examples

The team's megalocomparative approach allowed them to analyze trends across dozens of countries.
Her report relied on a megalocomparative approach to highlight the company's global dominance.
Critics argued that the megalocomparative approach exaggerated the differences between the two theories.

Synset Examples

She used a megalocomparative approach in her marketing pitch.
His megalocomparative style makes everything seem exaggerated.

Etymology

From Greek 'megalo-' (large) and 'comparative', meaning relating to comparison.
