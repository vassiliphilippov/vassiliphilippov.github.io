Bight of Benin

1. PROPN

/ baɪt əv bəˈnɪn / (AmE), / baɪt əv bəˈnɪn / (BrE)
a wide bay forming a large indentation of the Gulf of Guinea on the western coast of Africa
bay on west coast of Africa

Examples

Several European ships anchored in the Bight of Benin during the height of the slave trade.
The Bight of Benin is notorious for its treacherous currents and unpredictable weather.
The coast along the Bight of Benin is home to diverse marine life and important ports.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bay - a broad inlet of the sea where the land curves inward

Etymology

From 'bight' meaning a bend or bay, and 'Benin', a region in West Africa.
