press of canvas

1. NOUN

/ prɛs əv ˈkænvəs / (AmE), / prɛs əv ˈkænvəs / (BrE)
the greatest amount of sail that a ship can carry safely without risk of damage or capsizing
maximum safe amount of sail on a ship

Examples

During the storm, the captain ordered a reduction in the press of canvas to prevent disaster.
The ship moved swiftly before the wind under a full press of canvas.
With the press of canvas out, the crew had to remain vigilant for sudden changes in weather.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: full sail - all sails set on a ship to maximize speed
