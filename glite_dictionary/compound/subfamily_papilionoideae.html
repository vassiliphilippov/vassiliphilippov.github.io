subfamily Papilionoideae

1. NOUN

/ ˌsʌbˌfæməli ˌpæpɪliˌoʊnɔɪˈdiːiː / (AmE), / ˌsʌbˌfæmɪli ˌpapɪlɪəʊnɔɪˈdiːiː / (BrE)
A major subfamily of the legume family Fabaceae, comprising the majority of species formerly placed in the family Papilionaceae.
a subfamily of legumes (Fabaceae)

Examples

The subfamily Papilionoideae includes familiar plants such as beans, peas, and lupins.
Many important crops belong to the subfamily Papilionoideae due to their nitrogen-fixing abilities.
Botanists often refer to the subfamily Papilionoideae when discussing the diversity of leguminous plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Fabaceae - the botanical family of legumes, beans, and peas
  • Synonym: Papilionaceae - former name for the subfamily Papilionoideae in older classifications

Etymology

From New Latin 'Papilionoideae', referring to butterfly-shaped (papilionaceous) flowers.
