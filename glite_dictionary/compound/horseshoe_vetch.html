horseshoe vetch

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɔrsˌʃu vɛtʃ / (AmE), / ˈhɔːsʃuː vɛtʃ / (BrE)
a European perennial plant (Hippocrepis comosa) with yellow umbel-shaped flowers and flattened pods that separate into horseshoe-shaped segments
a European plant with yellow flowers and horseshoe-shaped pods

Examples

You can often find horseshoe vetch growing on chalky grasslands in southern England.
The yellow blossoms of horseshoe vetch attract many butterflies in spring.
After flowering, horseshoe vetch produces distinctive pods shaped like tiny horseshoes.

Etymology

From the shape of its pod segments, resembling horseshoes, and 'vetch', from Old French veche, relating to vetches of the pea family.
