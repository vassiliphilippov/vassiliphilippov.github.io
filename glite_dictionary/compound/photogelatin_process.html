photogelatin process

1. NOUN

/ ˌfoʊtoʊdʒəˈlætɪn ˈproʊsɛs / (AmE), / ˌfəʊtəʊdʒəˈlætɪn ˈprəʊsɛs / (BrE)
a photomechanical printing method using a glass plate coated with gelatin to transfer an image, sometimes in multiple colors
printing method using gelatin-coated plate

Examples

The early art books were illustrated using the photogelatin process to achieve rich tonal quality.
Color reproductions in the late nineteenth century often relied on the photogelatin process.
She studied the photogelatin process to better understand historic photo-printing techniques.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: collotype - a similar photomechanical printing process using gelatin-coated plates

Etymology

From photo- (relating to light) + gelatin (protein from animal collagen), referring to the use of light-sensitive gelatin.
