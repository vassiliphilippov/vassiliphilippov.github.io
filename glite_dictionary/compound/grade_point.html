grade point

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡreɪd ˌpɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪd ˌpɔɪnt / (BrE)
a number assigned to a letter grade received for a course, used to calculate a student's academic average
numerical value assigned to a course grade

Examples

She earned four grade points for her excellent performance in chemistry.
Maintaining a high grade point in all your courses is crucial for scholarships.
Each grade point represents a specific letter grade in the university's system.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: grade point average - the average value of the accumulated grade points earned divided by the total credit hours attempted
