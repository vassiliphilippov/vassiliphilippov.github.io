Munro bagger

1. NOUN

/ mənˈɹoʊ ˈbæɡɚ / (AmE), / mʌnˈrəʊ ˈbæɡə / (BrE)
a person who climbs all the Scottish Munro mountains, especially those over 3,000 feet in elevation
person who climbs all Scottish Munros
british

Examples

After reaching Ben Nevis, he was finally able to call himself a Munro bagger.
Many Munro baggers keep detailed logs of each peak they have conquered.
The club organized a trip aimed at helping new members become Munro baggers.

Synset Examples

He became a Munro bagger after climbing all the peaks.
She proudly displayed her Munro bagger certificate.
Being a Munro bagger requires stamina and dedication.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Munro - a Scottish mountain over 3,000 feet high

Etymology

From 'Munro', after Sir Hugh Munro, who catalogued the mountains, and 'bagger', meaning one who collects or completes a set.
