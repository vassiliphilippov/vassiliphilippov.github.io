Father Christmas

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɑːðɚ ˈkrɪsməs / (AmE), / ˈfɑːðə ˈkrɪsməs / (BrE)
the imaginary figure who is thought to bring presents to children at Christmas, especially in British tradition; similar to Santa Claus.
legendary Christmas gift-bringer

Examples

The children left out mince pies and milk for Father Christmas on Christmas Eve.
In many British homes, Father Christmas is the beloved figure who delivers gifts during the night.
The story of Father Christmas has been told for generations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Santa Claus - the American version of the legendary Christmas gift-bearer

Etymology

From British folklore; term has been used since the 17th century, with roots in the personification of Christmas.
