stratum granulosum

1. NOUN

/ ˈstreɪtəm ˌɡræn(j)əˈloʊsəm / (AmE), / ˈstrɑːtəm ˌɡranjʊˈləʊsəm / (BrE)
the thin layer of the epidermis just beneath the stratum corneum (or stratum lucidum in palms and soles), consisting of cells with visible granules that are dying and move toward the surface of the skin
epidermal layer with granular cells
medical

Examples

In the stratum granulosum, skin cells begin to lose their nuclei as they move toward the surface.
The stratum granulosum is more prominent on the palms and soles than on other parts of the body.
Dysfunction in the stratum granulosum can contribute to various skin disorders.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: stratum corneum - outermost layer of the epidermis
  • Degree Higher: stratum lucidum - clear layer of the epidermis found only on the palms and soles, above the stratum granulosum
  • Degree Lower: stratum spinosum - layer of epidermis just beneath the stratum granulosum

Etymology

From Latin: 'stratum' meaning 'layer' and 'granulosum' meaning 'granular'.
