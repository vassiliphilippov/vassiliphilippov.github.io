Audubon's warbler

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔːdəbənz ˈwɔːrblɚ / (AmE), / ˈɔːdəbənz ˈwɔːblə / (BrE)
a common species of warbler found in western North America, known for its distinctive yellow throat and rump
a warbler species of western North America

Examples

We spotted an Audubon's warbler perched on a low branch during our morning hike.
The distinctive yellow throat helps birdwatchers identify the Audubon's warbler among other warblers.
Each spring, many Audubon's warblers migrate through the valleys of California.

Etymology

Named after John James Audubon, American ornithologist and painter.
