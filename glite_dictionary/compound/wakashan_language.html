Wakashan language

1. NOUN

/ wəˈkɑːʃən ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / wəˈkæʃən ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
a family of North American indigenous languages spoken mainly in British Columbia and Washington
family of North American indigenous languages

Examples

Several First Nations communities speak a Wakashan language as part of their cultural heritage.
Linguists study the grammar and vocabulary of each Wakashan language to understand their relationships.
The preservation of a Wakashan language can help maintain traditional knowledge and stories.

Etymology

From the name of the Wakashan peoples, possibly derived from Nuu-chah-nulth ‘wakash’, meaning 'good'.
