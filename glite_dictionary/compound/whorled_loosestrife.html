whorled loosestrife

1. NOUN

/ wɝːld ˈluːsstraɪf / (AmE), / wɜːld ˈluːstraɪf / (BrE)
a common North American plant with yellow flowers, characterized by whorled leaves
yellow-flowered North American plant

Examples

The whorled loosestrife blooms along the edges of the wetland every summer.
Gardeners appreciate whorled loosestrife for its clusters of bright yellow flowers.
We spotted a patch of whorled loosestrife during our hike through the nature reserve.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loosestrife - any of various plants in the genus Lysimachia, often with colorful flowers

Etymology

'Whorled' refers to leaves arranged in a circle (whorl) around the stem; 'loosestrife' from Old English *lōstre* (calming plant), via Greek *lysimachia*.
