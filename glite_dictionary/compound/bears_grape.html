bear's grape

1. NOUN

/ bɛrz ɡreɪp / (AmE), / bɛəz ɡreɪp / (BrE)
An evergreen mat-forming shrub of North America and northern Eurasia, with small white flowers and red berries; the leaves turn red in autumn.
evergreen shrub with red berries

Examples

The bear's grape covers the forest floor, forming a thick carpet of green leaves.
In autumn, the leaves of the bear's grape turn a vibrant red, adding color to the landscape.
Hikers often recognize the bear's grape by its small white flowers and bright red berries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bearberry - another common name for the same shrub (Arctostaphylos uva-ursi)

Etymology

Named because bears are known to eat its berries; 'grape' refers to the grape-like clusters of red berries.
