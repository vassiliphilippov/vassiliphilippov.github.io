bronchoscopic smear

1. NOUN

/ brɑŋˌkɑː.skoʊ.pɪk smɪr / (AmE), / brɒŋˌkɒs.kəʊ.pɪk smɪə / (BrE)
a sample of cells collected from the lower respiratory tract during bronchoscopy, used for cytologic examination to diagnose lung diseases
cell sample from bronchoscopy for diagnosis
medical

Examples

The doctor ordered a bronchoscopic smear to help diagnose the persistent cough.
A bronchoscopic smear revealed malignant cells in the patient's lung tissue.
After performing the bronchoscopic smear, the lab could quickly identify signs of infection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bronchoscopy - examination of the airways using a bronchoscope

Etymology

From 'bronchoscopy' (examining the airways) and 'smear' (a sample spread on a slide).
