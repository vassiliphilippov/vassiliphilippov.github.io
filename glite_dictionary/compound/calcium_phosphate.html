calcium phosphate

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæl.si.əm ˈfɑs.feɪt / (AmE), / ˈkælsiəm ˈfɒsfeɪt / (BrE)
a white crystalline salt, Ca3(PO4)2, that is a main constituent of animal bones and teeth and is used in fertilizers and baking powders
a phosphate salt of calcium, found in bones and teeth
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that calcium phosphate plays a vital role in the formation of healthy bones.
Some toothpaste brands contain calcium phosphate to help strengthen tooth enamel.
Researchers are studying ways to use calcium phosphate in bone grafts and dental implants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calcium carbonate - a salt found in rocks and the main component of shells

Etymology

From calcium (Latin calx, lime) + phosphate (from Greek phōs, light, and phoros, bearer, via French phosphate).
