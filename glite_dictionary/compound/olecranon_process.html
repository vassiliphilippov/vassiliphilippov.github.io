olecranon process

1. NOUN

/ oʊˈlɛkrəˌnɑn ˈprɑːsɛs / (AmE), / əʊˈlɛkrənɒn ˈprəʊsɛs / (BrE)
the bony projection of the ulna at the elbow, forming the outer bump and fitting into the humerus when the arm is extended
bony projection at the elbow (ulna)
medical

Examples

He fractured his olecranon process after falling off his bicycle.
The doctor explained that the olecranon process is what we feel as the tip of the elbow.
Radiographs revealed a small chip of bone near the olecranon process.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: olecranon - the bony prominence of the elbow (ulna)

Etymology

From Greek 'ōlekranon' (elbow) + English 'process' (anatomical projection).
