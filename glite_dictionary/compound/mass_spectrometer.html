mass spectrometer

1. NOUN

/ mæs spɛkˈtrɑːmɪtɚ / (AmE), / mæs spɛkˈtrɒmɪtə / (BrE)
an instrument used to separate and measure the masses of ions by deflecting them using electric or magnetic fields, producing a mass spectrum
instrument for measuring mass of ions

Examples

The laboratory used a mass spectrometer to analyze the composition of the chemical sample.
A mass spectrometer can identify unknown compounds by measuring the relative masses of their ions.
Recent advances in mass spectrometer technology have improved the accuracy of molecular analysis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mass spectrometry - the technique or process of using a mass spectrometer

Etymology

From 'mass' (Latin massa, lump) and 'spectrometer' (from Latin spectrum, image + -meter, measuring device).
