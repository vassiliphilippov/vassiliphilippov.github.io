white mustard

1. NOUN

/ waɪt ˈmʌstərd / (AmE), / waɪt ˈmʌstəd / (BrE)
A Eurasian plant (Sinapis alba) cultivated for its pungent seeds, which are used to make table mustard and mustard oil.
a plant whose seeds are used to make mustard

Examples

Farmers often grow white mustard as a cover crop to improve soil quality.
The pungent seeds of white mustard are commonly ground to make table mustard.
Traditional recipes sometimes call for white mustard seeds to enhance the flavor of pickles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mustard - a yellow or brown paste made from the seeds of certain plants, used as a condiment
