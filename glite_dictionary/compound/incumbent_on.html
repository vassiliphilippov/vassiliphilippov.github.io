incumbent on

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈkʌmbənt ɑn / (AmE), / ɪnˈkʌmbənt ɒn / (BrE)
necessary as a duty or responsibility; morally binding or required for someone to do
morally binding, required as a duty

Examples

It is incumbent on all employees to follow the safety rules at all times.
As a manager, it is incumbent on you to lead by example.
He felt it was incumbent on him to support the project, despite his reservations.

Synset Examples

it is incumbent on me to attend

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obligatory - required by a law or rule; compulsory
  • Antonym: optional - left to choice; not compulsory

Etymology

From Latin 'incumbere' meaning 'to lie or lean upon'; 'incumbent' used as an adjective with the preposition 'on'.
