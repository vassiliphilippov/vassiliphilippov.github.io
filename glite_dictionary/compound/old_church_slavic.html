Old Church Slavic

1. NOUN

/ oʊld tʃɝtʃ ˈslɑvɪk / (AmE), / əʊld tʃɜːtʃ ˈslɑːvɪk / (BrE)
the earliest recorded Slavic literary language, used in the 9th century and primarily known as the medium of early Slavic translations of the Bible
early Slavic literary language

Examples

The Gospels were first translated into Old Church Slavic for the use of early Slavic Christians.
Linguists study Old Church Slavic to understand the evolution of Slavic languages.
The Cyrillic script was originally developed to write Old Church Slavic.

Etymology

From English 'old', 'church', and 'Slavic', indicating the early Slavic church-related language.

2. NOUN

/ oʊld tʃɜrtʃ ˈslævɪk / (AmE), / əʊld tʃɜːtʃ ˈslævɪk / (BrE)
the oldest attested Slavic language, used in the 9th century for the first translations of the Bible and early Christian texts.
early Slavic liturgical language

Examples

Scholars often study Old Church Slavic to better understand the origins of Slavic languages.
The Bible was first translated into Old Church Slavic in the 9th century by Saints Cyril and Methodius.
Many ancient manuscripts have been preserved in Old Church Slavic.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Church Slavonic - later standardized liturgical language based on Old Church Slavic

Etymology

Old Church relating to the Christian church, Slavic from the Slavic language group. Used for religious translations in the 9th century.
