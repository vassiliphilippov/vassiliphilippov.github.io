New Amsterdam

1. PROPN

/ nu ˈæmstɚˌdæm / (AmE), / nju ˈæmstədæm / (BrE)
the original Dutch settlement founded at the southern tip of Manhattan Island, which was later annexed by the English and renamed New York in 1664
historic Dutch settlement; now New York City

Examples

The Dutch established New Amsterdam at the southern tip of Manhattan Island in the 17th century.
After the English seized New Amsterdam, they renamed it New York.
Many remnants of New Amsterdam can still be seen in lower Manhattan today.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: New York City - the city that succeeded New Amsterdam after the English takeover

Etymology

From Dutch Nieuw Amsterdam, meaning 'New Amsterdam', named after the city of Amsterdam in the Netherlands.
