biogeographical region

1. NOUN

/ ˌbaɪ.oʊ.dʒioʊˈɡræf.ɪ.kəl ˈriː.dʒən / (AmE), / ˌbʌɪ.əʊ.dʒiː.əˈɡræf.ɪ.kəl ˈriː.dʒən / (BrE)
an area of the Earth defined by the distribution patterns of its plant and animal species
area defined by flora and fauna distribution

Examples

Australia is considered a separate biogeographical region because of its unique flora and fauna.
The boundaries of a biogeographical region are determined by the natural habitats of various species.
Conservation efforts often take the specifics of each biogeographical region into account.

Etymology

From bio- (life), geographical (relating to geography), and region.
