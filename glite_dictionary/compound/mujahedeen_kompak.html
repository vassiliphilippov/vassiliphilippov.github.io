Mujahedeen Kompak

1. PROPN

/ muːˌdʒɑːhəˈdiːn kɒmˈpak / (AmE), / muːˌdʒɑːhəˈdiːn kɒmˈpak / (BrE)
a militant Islamic group formed in 2005 by hardliners who split from Jemaah Islamiyah, primarily active in Indonesia
Indonesian militant Islamic group (2005-)

Examples

The Indonesian government launched an operation to dismantle Mujahedeen Kompak's networks in Central Sulawesi.
Reports suggest that Mujahedeen Kompak was responsible for several violent incidents in the region.
After splitting from Jemaah Islamiyah, Mujahedeen Kompak became notorious for recruiting new militants.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Jemaah Islamiyah - a Southeast Asian militant Islamic group linked to Al-Qaeda

Etymology

From Arabic 'mujāhidīn' (holy warriors) and Indonesian 'kompak' (compact, united).
