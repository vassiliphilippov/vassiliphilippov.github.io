uniformly decelerated motion

1. NOUN

/ ˌjuː.nəˌfɔːr.mli dɪˌsɛl.əˈreɪ.t̬ɪd ˈmoʊ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌjuː.nɪˌfɔː.mli dɪˌsɛl.əˈreɪ.tɪd ˈməʊ.ʃən / (BrE)
Motion in which the velocity decreases at a constant rate over time.
motion with constant negative acceleration

Examples

Objects in uniformly decelerated motion lose the same amount of velocity in each time interval.
A ball rolling to a stop on a flat surface is an example of uniformly decelerated motion.
The car came to rest after several seconds of uniformly decelerated motion.

Synset Examples

Read from bottom to top, it represents the overall distances traversed by a uniformly decelerated motion after an increasing number of equal time intervals.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: uniformly accelerated motion - motion with constant positive acceleration

Etymology

From Latin 'uniformis' (uniform) and 'decelerare' (reduce speed), with 'motion' from Latin 'motio'.
