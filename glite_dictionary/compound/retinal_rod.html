retinal rod

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛt.n̩.əl rɑd / (AmE), / ˈrɛt.nəl rɒd / (BrE)
a type of visual receptor cell in the retina of the eye that is sensitive to low levels of light and is important for vision in dim or dark conditions
light-sensitive cell in the retina
medical

Examples

Retinal rods allow us to see shapes and movement in low-light situations, such as at dusk.
People who lose their retinal rods may have difficulty seeing at night or in dimly lit environments.
Unlike cone cells, retinal rods do not detect color but are crucial for night vision.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cone cell - type of retinal cell for color vision and detail

Etymology

From retina (Latin, net-like membrane of the eye) + rod (referring to the cell’s shape).
