holy of holies

1. NOUN

/ ˈhoʊli əv ˈhoʊliz / (AmE), / ˈhəʊli əv ˈhəʊliːz / (BrE)
(in Judaism) The innermost and most sacred chamber of the ancient Tabernacle and Temple in Jerusalem, where the Ark of the Covenant was kept.
most sacred chamber in ancient Jewish temple

Examples

The Ark of the Covenant was placed inside the holy of holies in the ancient Temple.
Only the high priest was allowed to enter the holy of holies once a year on Yom Kippur.
Scholars continue to debate the exact location of the holy of holies within the ancient temple complex.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sanctum sanctorum - the most sacred or holy place

Etymology

Translates Hebrew *qōdesh haq-qŏdāshīm* (holy of holies).

2. NOUN

/ ˈhoʊli əv ˈhoʊliz / (AmE), / ˈhəʊli əv ˈhəʊliːz / (BrE)
A thing or place that is treated as especially sacred or inviolable, particularly in a figurative sense.
thing considered absolutely sacred or inviolable

Examples

Every politician fears to touch that holy of holies, the Social Security System.
In the world of classical music, Beethoven’s symphonies are considered the holy of holies.
The committee treated the old archives as a holy of holies that no one dared to alter.

Synset Examples

every politician fears to touch that holy of holies, the Social Security System

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sanctum sanctorum - a most sacred or private place

Etymology

From the Biblical term referring to the innermost and most sacred area of the ancient Hebrew Temple in Jerusalem.
