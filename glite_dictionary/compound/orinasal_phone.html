orinasal phone

1. NOUN

/ ˌɔːrɪˈneɪzəl foʊn / (AmE), / ˌɔːrɪˈneɪzəl fəʊn / (BrE)
a speech sound produced with both the oral and nasal passages open, as in French nasal vowels
a sound made with both mouth and nose open

Examples

French contains several orinasal phones that do not exist in English.
The distinction between oral and orinasal phones can be subtle to new learners.
Linguists analyze orinasal phones to better understand nasalization in languages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nasal - a speech sound produced with airflow through the nose

Etymology

From Latin 'os, oris' (mouth) + 'nasus' (nose) and Greek 'phōnē' (sound, voice).
