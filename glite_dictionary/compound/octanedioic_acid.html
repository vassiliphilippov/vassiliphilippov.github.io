octanedioic acid

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːktəˌniːdiˈoʊɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / ˌɒktəniːdiˈəʊɪk ˈæsɪd / (BrE)
A dicarboxylic acid with eight carbon atoms, found in cork and also known as suberic acid, used in the production of polymers and resins.
dicarboxylic acid found in cork
medical

Examples

The researcher isolated octanedioic acid from pieces of natural cork.
Synthesis of nylon can involve the use of octanedioic acid as a monomer.
The properties of octanedioic acid make it useful in the creation of specialty plastics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suberic acid - another name for octanedioic acid

Etymology

From chemical naming conventions (octane + dioic acid), indicating an eight-carbon chain with two carboxylic acid groups.
