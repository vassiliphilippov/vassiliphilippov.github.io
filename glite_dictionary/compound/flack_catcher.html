flack catcher

1. NOUN

/ flæk ˈkætʃər / (AmE), / flæk ˈkatʃə / (BrE)
a spokesperson, especially one skilled at defending an employer or client and deflecting criticism
spokesperson skilled at deflecting criticism
informal

Examples

The politician's flack catcher was quick to spin the negative story into a positive headline.
Every major corporation employs at least one skilled flack catcher to handle tough questions from the press.
No matter how tough the interview, the flack catcher calmly redirected criticism back to their opponent.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spokesperson - a person who speaks as a representative of a group or organization
  • Synonym: press agent - a person employed to generate publicity

Etymology

From slang 'flack' (publicist or press agent), possibly from Yiddish, plus 'catcher', one who catches.
