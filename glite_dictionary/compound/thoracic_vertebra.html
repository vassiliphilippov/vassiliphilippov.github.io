thoracic vertebra

1. NOUN

/ θəˈræsɪk ˈvɝtəˌbreɪ / (AmE), / θɔːˈræsɪk ˈvɜːtɪbriː / (BrE)
any one of the twelve vertebrae located in the middle part of the spinal column, between the cervical (neck) and lumbar (lower back) vertebrae, to which the ribs are attached.
a vertebra of the mid-back to which ribs are attached
medical

Examples

The fifth thoracic vertebra was fractured in the accident.
Pain between the shoulder blades can sometimes be traced to a problem with a thoracic vertebra.
Doctors reviewed X-rays to examine the alignment of each thoracic vertebra before surgery.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: cervical vertebra - vertebrae of the neck region
  • Degree Higher: lumbar vertebra - vertebrae of the lower back region

Etymology

From Greek *thōraks* (chest) + Latin *vertebra* (joint of the spine).
