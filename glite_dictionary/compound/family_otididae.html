family Otididae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli oʊˈtɪdəˌdi / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli əʊˈtɪdɪdi / (BrE)
a family of large, ground-dwelling birds known as bustards, native to Africa, Europe, Asia, and Australia
taxonomic family of bustards (birds)

Examples

The family Otididae includes some of the heaviest flying birds in the world.
Conservation efforts for the family Otididae focus on habitat protection and anti-poaching measures.
Many species in the family Otididae are threatened due to agricultural expansion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bustard - large, heavy, ground-dwelling bird of dry open country

Etymology

From New Latin *Otididae*, after the type genus *Otis*.
