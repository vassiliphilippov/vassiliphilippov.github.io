international ampere

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪntɚˈnæʃ(ə)nəl ˈæmˌpɪr / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈnæʃ(ə)nəl ˈæmˌpɪə / (BrE)
a former unit of electric current, slightly less than the SI ampere, used before the adoption of the modern definition
former unit of electric current

Examples

The circuit was calibrated using the international ampere before the SI system was adopted.
Early 20th-century electrical measurements often referenced the international ampere as the standard unit.
The difference between an international ampere and the modern ampere is important in historical engineering records.

  • Degree Higher: ampere - the SI base unit of electric current
