variety meat

1. NOUN

/ vəˈraɪəti mit / (AmE), / vəˈraɪəti miːt / (BrE)
the edible internal organs or offal of a butchered animal, such as liver, kidneys, or heart, often used as food
edible animal organs (offal)

Examples

Many cultures incorporate variety meat into traditional dishes for their unique flavors and nutritional value.
The chef prepared a stew using assorted variety meat from the local market.
Some people are hesitant to try variety meat, but it is considered a delicacy in several countries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: offal - the entrails and internal organs of an animal used as food
