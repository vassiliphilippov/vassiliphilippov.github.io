wild cabbage

1. NOUN

/ waɪld ˈkæb.ɪdʒ / (AmE), / waɪld ˈkab.ɪdʒ / (BrE)
A species of plant (Brassica oleracea) native to coastal western Europe, regarded as the wild ancestor of cultivated cabbages and related vegetables.
wild plant, ancestor of cabbages

Examples

The cliffs along the English Channel are home to wild cabbage plants.
Scientists study wild cabbage to learn about the origins of cultivated vegetables.
Some gardeners grow wild cabbage for its hardy leaves and unique flavor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cabbage - a round, green or purple vegetable with thick leaves
