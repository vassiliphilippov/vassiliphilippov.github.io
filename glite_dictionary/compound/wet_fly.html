wet fly

1. NOUN

/ wɛt flaɪ / (AmE), / wɛt flaɪ / (BrE)
an artificial fishing fly designed to sink and float beneath the surface of the water
submerged artificial fishing fly

Examples

The angler tied a wet fly to his line to attract trout beneath the surface.
He prefers using a wet fly on windy days when fish feed below the water.
Many fishing experts recommend starting with a wet fly early in the season.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dry fly - artificial fishing fly that floats on the water's surface
