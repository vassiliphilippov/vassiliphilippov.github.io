fauxlanthropist facade

1. NOUN

/ foʊˈlænθrəpɪst fəˈsɑd / (AmE), / fəʊˈlænθrəpɪst fəˈsɑːd / (BrE)
a deceptive appearance or outward show of philanthropy, intended to mislead others about one's true intentions or generosity
a deceptive show of being philanthropic

Examples

Many criticized the company's fauxlanthropist facade, claiming its donations were just for publicity.
Her fauxlanthropist facade quickly crumbled when the press revealed her true motives.
The gala had all the trappings of charity, but some attendees sensed it was just a fauxlanthropist facade.

Synset Examples

People saw through his fauxlanthropist facade during the fundraiser.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: philanthropy - the desire to promote the welfare of others, often by generous donation

Etymology

Formed from 'faux' (French for 'false'), 'philanthropist', and 'facade' (from French 'façade', front of a building).
