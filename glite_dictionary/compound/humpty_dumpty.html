Humpty Dumpty

1. NOUN

/ ˈhʌmpti ˈdʌmpti / (AmE), / ˈhʌmpti ˈdʌmpti / (BrE)
an egg-shaped character from a nursery rhyme who falls from a wall and cannot be put back together again
nursery rhyme egg character

Examples

As a child, I loved listening to the story of Humpty Dumpty and his great fall.
The cracked vase reminded her of Humpty Dumpty after his accident.
Many people know the phrase, 'All the king’s horses and all the king’s men,' from the Humpty Dumpty rhyme.

Etymology

Origin unknown; first appeared in English nursery rhymes in the late 17th century.
