therapeutic abortion

1. NOUN

/ ˌθɛrəˈpjuːtɪk əˈbɔrʃən / (AmE), / ˌθɛrəˈpjuːtɪk əˈbɔːʃən / (BrE)
an abortion that is induced legally for medical reasons, such as when the mother's life or health is at risk
legally induced abortion for medical reasons
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that a therapeutic abortion was necessary to protect the mother's health.
In some countries, therapeutic abortion is permitted only under strict medical guidelines.
Her condition worsened, and the medical team recommended a therapeutic abortion to save her life.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: elective abortion - an abortion performed at the request of the woman for non-medical reasons

Etymology

From therapeutic, relating to treatment, and abortion, from Latin abortio (miscarriage, abortion).
