cacao tree

1. NOUN

/ kəˈkaʊ tri / (AmE), / kəˈkaʊ triː / (BrE)
a tropical tree native to Central and South America, cultivated for its seeds (cacao beans) used to make chocolate and cocoa products
tree producing cacao beans

Examples

The cacao tree thrives in humid, tropical climates near the equator.
Farmers harvested ripe pods from the cacao tree to process the beans for chocolate.
Diseases affecting the cacao tree can threaten the global chocolate supply.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Theobroma cacao - scientific name for the cacao tree
  • Synonym: cocoa tree - alternate common name for the cacao tree
  • Antonym: coffee tree - tree producing coffee beans, related as a comparable crop plant

Etymology

From Spanish 'cacao', derived from Nahuatl 'cacahuatl' (cacao bean).
