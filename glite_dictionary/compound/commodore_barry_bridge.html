Commodore Barry Bridge

1. PROPN

/ ˌkɑː.məˌdɔːr ˈbæri ˌbrɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌkɒ.məˌdɔː ˈbæri ˌbrɪdʒ / (BrE)
a large cantilever bridge spanning the Delaware River between Chester, Pennsylvania and Bridgeport, New Jersey
cantilever bridge across Delaware River

Examples

The Commodore Barry Bridge offers a scenic view of the Delaware River during sunset.
Traffic was delayed on the Commodore Barry Bridge due to ongoing maintenance work.
The Commodore Barry Bridge is an important connection between Pennsylvania and New Jersey.

Etymology

Named after John Barry, an officer in the United States Navy, referred to as 'Commodore'.
