maternal-infant bonding

1. NOUN

/ məˈtɜrnəl ˈɪnfənt ˈbɑndɪŋ / (AmE), / məˈtɜːnəl ˈɪnfənt ˈbɒndɪŋ / (BrE)
the emotional attachment that develops between a mother and her infant beginning at birth
attachment between mother and infant

Examples

Early maternal-infant bonding has been shown to improve a baby's emotional well-being.
Researchers study maternal-infant bonding to understand childhood attachment disorders.
Health professionals encourage practices that support maternal-infant bonding after birth.

Synset Examples

maternal-infant bonding influences the child's psychological and physical development

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bonding - the process of forming a close emotional relationship
