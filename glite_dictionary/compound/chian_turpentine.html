Chian turpentine

1. NOUN

/ ˈkaɪ.ən tɜːr.pənˌtaɪn / (AmE), / ˈkaɪ.ən ˈtɜː.pənˌtiːn / (BrE)
A viscous resinous substance (turpentine) obtained from the terebinth tree, especially that found on the Greek island of Chios, used in medicine and varnishes.
resin from the terebinth tree (Chios)

Examples

The artist used Chian turpentine in the preparation of his oil paints.
In ancient medicine, Chian turpentine was believed to have valuable healing properties.
The extraction of Chian turpentine from the terebinth tree is still practiced in parts of Greece.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: terebinth - a small tree (Pistacia terebinthus) from which Chian turpentine is obtained
  • Degree Higher: turpentine - a volatile pungent oil or resin obtained from trees of the genus Pinus or Pistacia

Etymology

From Chios, a Greek island, and 'turpentine', meaning resin from certain trees.
