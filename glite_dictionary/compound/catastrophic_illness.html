catastrophic illness

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæt̬.əˈstrɑː.fɪk ˈɪl.nəs / (AmE), / ˌkæt.əˈstrɒf.ɪk ˈɪl.nəs / (BrE)
a severe illness requiring long-term hospitalization or recovery, typically involving high medical costs
serious illness with long recovery and high costs
medical

Examples

The family faced bankruptcy when their child developed a catastrophic illness that required months of treatment.
Insurance companies often offer special coverage for catastrophic illness to help families manage unexpected medical costs.
After surviving a catastrophic illness, she became an advocate for patient support organizations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: critical illness - life-threatening illness requiring intensive medical care
