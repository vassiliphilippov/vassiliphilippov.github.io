genus Albugo

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ælˈbjuː.ɡoʊ / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ælˈbjuː.ɡəʊ / (BrE)
The genus of fungi in the family Albuginaceae, known for causing white rust diseases in plants.
a genus of fungi causing white rusts

Examples

Several species in the genus Albugo are notorious for causing crop diseases.
The genus Albugo is studied for its role in plant pathology.
White rusts induced by fungi of the genus Albugo can devastate agricultural yields.

Etymology

From Latin 'albugo', meaning 'white spot' or 'blight'.
