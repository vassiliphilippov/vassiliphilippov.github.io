family Meropidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæmɪli məˈrɒpɪˌdeɪ / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli mɪˈrɒpɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of brightly colored, slender birds known as bee-eaters, found in Africa, Eurasia, and Australia, recognized for their long bills and pointed wings.
family of birds known as bee-eaters

Examples

The family Meropidae includes around twenty-seven species of bee-eaters distributed across several continents.
Biologists have studied the nesting habits of the family Meropidae in savannah and woodland regions.
Members of the family Meropidae are admired for their vivid plumage and aerial insect-catching abilities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bee-eater - a brightly colored bird of the family Meropidae

Etymology

From New Latin 'Meropidae', from 'Merops' (type genus) + '-idae' (denoting family in zoological classification).
