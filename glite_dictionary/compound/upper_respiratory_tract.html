upper respiratory tract

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌpɚ rɪˌspɪrəˌtɔri trækt / (AmE), / ˈʌpə rɪˌspɪrətəri trækt / (BrE)
the part of the respiratory system consisting of the nose, throat, and trachea, through which air passes before reaching the lungs
nose, throat, and trachea as part of the airway
medical

Examples

Colds are the most common infections of the upper respiratory tract.
Smoking can cause irritation and inflammation in the upper respiratory tract.
Children are especially prone to upper respiratory tract infections during the winter months.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lower respiratory tract - the part of the respiratory system below the trachea, including bronchi and lungs
