artillery shell

1. NOUN

/ ɑrˈtɪlɚi ʃɛl / (AmE), / ɑːˈtɪləri ʃɛl / (BrE)
a projectile fired by large-caliber guns in military operations, typically containing explosives
projectile fired by artillery

Examples

Fragments of the artillery shell were found scattered across the battlefield.
An unexploded artillery shell was discovered near the old fort during construction.
The loud blast came from an artillery shell detonating in the distance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shell - a projectile that explodes when fired from a gun
