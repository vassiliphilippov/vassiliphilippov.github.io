true toad

1. NOUN

/ tru tuːd / (AmE), / truː təʊd / (BrE)
A tailless amphibian of the family Bufonidae, generally more terrestrial than frogs and characterized by drier, warty skin.
a terrestrial, warty-skinned amphibian (Bufonidae)

Examples

The common true toad can often be found in gardens and forests far from water.
Unlike frogs, a true toad has dry, warty skin and prefers to live on land.
Many species of true toad are active at night and feed on insects.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: frog - a tailless amphibian with smoother skin, typically more aquatic
