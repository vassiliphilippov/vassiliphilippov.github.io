birchbark canoe

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɜrtʃˌbɑrk kəˈnu / (AmE), / ˈbɜːtʃˌbɑːk kəˈnuː / (BrE)
a lightweight canoe traditionally made by covering a wooden frame with the bark of a birch tree, used especially by Native peoples of North America
canoe made with birch bark

Examples

The explorers traveled down the river in a birchbark canoe.
Building a traditional birchbark canoe requires skill and patience.
The museum displayed a beautifully preserved birchbark canoe from the nineteenth century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: canoe - a narrow, lightweight boat pointed at both ends and open on top
