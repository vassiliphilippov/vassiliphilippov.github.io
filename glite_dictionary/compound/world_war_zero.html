World War Zero

1. NOUN

/ wɝːld wɔːr ˈzɪəɹoʊ / (AmE), / wɜːld wɔː ˈzɪərəʊ / (BrE)
a hypothetical or metaphorical conflict representing a new kind of large-scale warfare, often used to describe modern nontraditional conflicts.
theoretical conflict representing new warfare

Examples

The idea of World War Zero is used to describe the complexities of cyber conflict and hybrid warfare today.
Debates about World War Zero highlight the changing nature of global threats.
Some analysts argue that climate change could trigger a World War Zero involving many nations.

Synset Examples

Discussing the implications of World War Zero can be controversial.
The concept of World War Zero raises questions about modern warfare.
Some argue that World War Zero is a metaphor for today's conflicts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: World War I - the First World War, fought from 1914 to 1918
  • Synonym: World War II - the Second World War, fought from 1939 to 1945

Etymology

Formed in imitation of 'World War I' and 'World War II', with 'zero' suggesting a conflict preceding or fundamentally different from these.
