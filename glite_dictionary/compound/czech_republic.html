Czech Republic

1. PROPN

/ ˈʧɛk rɪˈpʌblɪk / (AmE), / ˈʧɛk rɪˈpʌblɪk / (BrE)
A landlocked country in central Europe, formed after the peaceful separation from Slovakia in 1993, known for its historical cities, castles, and cultural heritage.
country in central Europe

Examples

The Czech Republic joined the European Union in 2004.
Prague, the capital of the Czech Republic, is famous for its beautiful architecture.
She traveled across the Czech Republic to visit historic castles and towns.

Etymology

From Czech 'Česká republika' ('Czech' + 'republic').
