white cohosh

1. NOUN

/ ˈwaɪt ˈkoʊ.hɑʃ / (AmE), / ˈwaɪt ˈkəʊ.hɒʃ / (BrE)
A North American herb (often Actaea pachypoda) with white, poisonous berries.
North American herb with white berries

Examples

The berries of white cohosh are known to be highly toxic if ingested.
Many woodland animals avoid eating white cohosh due to its poisonous properties.
Gardeners are advised to identify white cohosh correctly to prevent accidental poisoning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: black cohosh - a related North American herb with medicinal uses and blackish roots
