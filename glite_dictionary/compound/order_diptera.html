order Diptera

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdər dɪpˈtɛrə / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə dɪpˈtɛərə / (BrE)
a large order of insects characterized by having a single pair of wings and sucking or piercing mouthparts; includes true flies, mosquitoes, gnats, and crane flies
large order of winged insects (flies, mosquitoes, etc.)

Examples

The order Diptera includes familiar insects like houseflies, mosquitoes, and gnats.
Scientists study the order Diptera to better understand disease transmission by insects.
Craneflies are members of the order Diptera, despite their distinctive appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fly - common name for members of the order Diptera

Etymology

From Greek 'di-' (two) + 'pteron' (wing), referring to insects with two wings.
