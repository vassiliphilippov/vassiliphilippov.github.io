genus Sapindus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs səˈpɪn.dəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs səˈpɪn.dəs / (BrE)
The type genus of the Sapindaceae family, comprising trees and shrubs commonly known as soapberries.
type genus of the Sapindaceae (soapberry) family
medical

Examples

The genus Sapindus includes several species whose fruits are used as natural soap.
Botanists have studied the genus Sapindus for its medicinal and cleansing properties.
Members of the genus Sapindus are commonly found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Etymology

From Latin 'sapo' (soap) and 'indicus' (of India), referring to soap-like properties and origin.
