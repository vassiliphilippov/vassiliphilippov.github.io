one flesh

1. NOUN

/ wʌn flɛʃ / (AmE), / wʌn fleʃ / (BrE)
used to describe a deep and intimate union, especially in marriage, where two people are considered as a single entity
deep union, especially in marriage

Examples

After the wedding ceremony, they considered themselves one flesh in the eyes of their faith.
Many religious texts describe marriage partners as becoming one flesh through their union.
Their shared life and decisions made them feel truly like one flesh.

Synset Examples

In marriage, they promised to become one flesh.
In their eyes, they became one flesh during the ceremony.
Their vows symbolized their commitment to become one flesh.
The couple believes they are one flesh after marriage.
Their love made them feel like one flesh.

Etymology

From Biblical language; used in the King James Bible to describe the union of husband and wife.
