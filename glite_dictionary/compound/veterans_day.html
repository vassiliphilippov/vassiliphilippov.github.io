Veterans Day

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɛt̬ɚˌʃənz deɪ / (AmE), / ˈvɛtərənz deɪ / (BrE)
A public holiday in the United States on November 11 honoring military veterans; formerly called Armistice Day.
U.S. holiday honoring military veterans
american

Examples

The parade honoring veterans took place on Veterans Day in the city center.
Many businesses are closed on Veterans Day as it is a federal holiday.
On Veterans Day, people across the country thank those who have served in the armed forces.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Armistice Day - holiday formerly observed on November 11 to commemorate the end of World War I

Etymology

From veteran (from Latin 'veteranus', meaning 'old, experienced') + day; renamed from Armistice Day in 1954 in the U.S.
