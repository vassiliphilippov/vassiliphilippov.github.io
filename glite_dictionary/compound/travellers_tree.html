traveller's tree

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrævələrz ˌtri / (AmE), / ˈtrævələz ˌtriː / (BrE)
A tall, palm-like plant native to Madagascar, known for its edible nuts and for storing clear sap in its leafstalks, which can provide water in emergencies.
palm-like plant; source of water for travelers

Examples

The traveller's tree is often found along roadsides in Madagascar, where its leaves provide shade and its sap helps thirsty travelers.
Local guides showed us how to extract water from the leafstalks of the traveller's tree during our hike.
The impressive shape of the traveller's tree made it easy to spot across the landscape.
