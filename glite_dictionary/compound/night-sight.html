night-sight

1. NOUN

/ ˈnaɪt.saɪt / (AmE), / ˈnaɪt.saɪt / (BrE)
the ability to see in low levels of light, such as at night
ability to see in low light

Examples

Some animals, like owls, have excellent night-sight that helps them hunt in the dark.
Special goggles can improve a soldier's night-sight during nighttime operations.
Her night-sight is so good that she can walk through the house without turning on the lights.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: night vision - the ability to see in the dark or in low-light conditions
