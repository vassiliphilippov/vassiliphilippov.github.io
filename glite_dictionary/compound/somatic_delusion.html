somatic delusion

1. NOUN

/ səˈmætɪk dɪˈluːʒən / (AmE), / səˈmatɪk dɪˈluːʒən / (BrE)
a false fixed belief that one's body or parts of the body are diseased, dysfunctional, or altered, despite evidence to the contrary
false belief about one's body
medical

Examples

The patient strongly believed she had parasites under her skin, a typical somatic delusion.
Psychiatrists identified his conviction that his arm was rotting as a somatic delusion.
Treatment for somatic delusion often requires a combination of medication and therapy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: delusion - a false belief held despite strong evidence against it

Etymology

From Greek sōmatikos (bodily) and Latin delusio (deception, delusion).
