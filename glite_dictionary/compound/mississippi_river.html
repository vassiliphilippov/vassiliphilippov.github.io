Mississippi River

1. PROPN

/ ˌmɪsɪˈsɪpi ˈrɪvɚ / (AmE), / ˌmɪsɪˈsɪpi ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
a major river in North America, flowing from northern Minnesota south to the Gulf of Mexico and serving as the principal river of the United States
major river in the United States

Examples

The Mississippi River is famous for its role in American history and commerce.
Flooding along the Mississippi River has affected many towns during spring.
Mark Twain wrote extensively about life on the Mississippi River.

Etymology

From Ojibwe 'Misi-ziibi', meaning 'Great River'.
