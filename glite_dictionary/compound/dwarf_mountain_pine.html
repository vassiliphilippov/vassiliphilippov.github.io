dwarf mountain pine

1. NOUN

/ dwɔrf ˈmaʊntən paɪn / (AmE), / dwɔːf ˈmaʊntɪn paɪn / (BrE)
A low, shrubby pine tree native to central Europe, with short, bright green needles in bunches of two.
a low shrubby pine of central Europe

Examples

A single dwarf mountain pine stood resiliently on the windswept hillside, its branches hugging close to the ground.
Gardeners often use dwarf mountain pine because of its compact, slow-growing habit and bright green needles.
The dwarf mountain pine is well adapted to cold, exposed environments in central Europe.
