Chinese Wall

1. PROPN

/ tʃaɪˈniz wɔl / (AmE), / tʃʌɪˈniːz wɔːl / (BrE)
a massive fortification built across northern China in the 3rd century BC, also known as the Great Wall of China, stretching approximately 1,500 miles and averaging 6 meters in width
the Great Wall of China; ancient fortification

Examples

The Chinese Wall was built to protect ancient China from invasions by northern tribes.
Many tourists travel each year to walk along the Chinese Wall and admire its structure.
Historians debate how many workers died constructing the Chinese Wall during the 3rd century BC.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Great Wall of China - another name for the Chinese Wall, ancient defensive barrier in China

2. NOUN

/ ˌtʃaɪ.niz ˈwɑl / (AmE), / ˌtʃaɪ.niːz ˈwɔːl / (BrE)
An imaginary barrier created to prevent the exchange or disclosure of information, especially within an organization, so as to avoid conflicts of interest or breaches of confidentiality.
imaginary barrier to prevent information flow

Examples

The law firm maintained a Chinese Wall between its litigation and corporate teams to avoid conflicts of interest.
Strict Chinese Walls are crucial in investment banks to separate analysts from traders.
After the merger, a Chinese Wall was implemented to ensure sensitive data was not improperly shared.

Synset Examples

She put up a Chinese Wall to keep her personal life private.
He built a Chinese Wall around his emotions after the breakup.
The company established a Chinese Wall to prevent conflicts of interest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barrier - an obstacle that prevents movement or access
  • Synonym: firewall - a barrier, especially in computing or business, to prevent unauthorized access or exchange of information

Etymology

Refers to the Great Wall of China as a metaphor for an impassable boundary; first applied in the 20th century to information barriers in business and law.
