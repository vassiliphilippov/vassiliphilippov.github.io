auricular vein

1. NOUN

/ ɔːˈrɪkjələr veɪn / (AmE), / ɔːˈrɪkjʊlə veɪn / (BrE)
either of two veins that drain blood from the region of the ear
vein serving the ear region
medical

Examples

The surgeon identified the auricular vein during the procedure to ensure there was no bleeding near the ear.
Infections in the ear can sometimes lead to swelling of the auricular vein.
Detailed anatomy lessons often include diagrams of the auricular vein and surrounding blood vessels.

Etymology

From Latin 'auricula' meaning 'ear' and 'vein' from Latin 'vena'.
