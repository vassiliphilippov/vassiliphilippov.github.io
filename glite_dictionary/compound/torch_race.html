torch race

1. NOUN

/ tɔrtʃ reɪs / (AmE), / tɔːtʃ reɪs / (BrE)
A race, originating in ancient Greece, in which a torch is carried and passed from one runner to another as a relay.
relay race with a torch (ancient Greece)

Examples

The ancient Greeks celebrated the festival with a torch race through the city.
Each participant in the torch race had to run swiftly to keep the flame alive.
The concept of the Olympic relay is inspired by the torch race of antiquity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: relay race - a race in which each team member runs part of the total distance and then passes on to the next
