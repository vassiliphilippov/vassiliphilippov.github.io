four score and seven years ago

1. X

/ fɔr skɔr ənd ˈsɛvən jɪrz əˈgoʊ / (AmE), / fɔː skɔːr ənd ˈsɛvən jɪəz əˈgəʊ / (BrE)
eighty-seven years ago; a phrase famously used by Abraham Lincoln at the start of the Gettysburg Address in 1863.
eighty-seven years ago (historic phrase)

Examples

Etymology

Score meaning 'twenty' comes from Old Norse 'skor', and the phrase gained fame from Lincoln's Gettysburg Address (1863).
