sweet pea

1. NOUN

/ swit pi / (AmE), / swiːt piː / (BrE)
a climbing garden plant with fragrant flowers in pastel shades, often grown for decoration
fragrant climbing garden plant

Examples

The sweet pea filled the garden with its delightful fragrance in early summer.
She planted sweet peas along the fence to add a splash of color.
A bouquet of sweet pea brightened up the dining table.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Lathyrus odoratus - the botanical name of the sweet pea plant

2. NOUN

/ ˈswit ˌpi / (AmE), / ˈswiːt ˌpiː / (BrE)
Used as a term of endearment for someone dear, especially children or pets.
term of endearment (person or pet)

Examples

Goodnight, sweet pea, I'll see you in the morning.
She always called her grandson sweet pea when he visited.
Come here, sweet pea, and let me fix your hair.

Synset Examples

He affectionately referred to his dog as his sweet pea.
She called her little girl sweet pea as she hugged her.
Goodnight, sweet pea; sleep tight and dream big!

Etymology

From the name of the sweet-smelling flowering plant 'sweet pea', possibly referring to its pleasantness.
