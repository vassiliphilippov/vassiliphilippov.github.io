so suay

1. ADJ

/ so sʉ.aɪ / (AmE), / səʊ sʊ.aɪ / (BrE)
(informal, Singapore/Malaysia English) Very unlucky; extremely unfortunate.
very unlucky (Singapore/Malaysia English)
informal

Examples

It was so suay that my phone broke right before my trip.
You must be so suay to get stuck in traffic every time it rains.
He's so suay—he lost his wallet twice in one week.

Synset Examples

It's so suay that it rained on our picnic day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unlucky - experiencing bad luck; unfortunate
  • Antonym: lucky - having good fortune; fortunate

Etymology

Loan blend: English 'so' + Hokkien 'suay' (unlucky, bad luck). Common in Singaporean and Malaysian English.
