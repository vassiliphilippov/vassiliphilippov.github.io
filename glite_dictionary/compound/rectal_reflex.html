rectal reflex

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛktəl ˈriːflɛks / (AmE), / ˈrɛktəl ˈriːflɛks / (BrE)
the normal physiological response in which the presence of feces in the rectum triggers contractions leading to the urge to defecate
bodily reflex prompting defecation
medical

Examples

The rectal reflex is essential for normal bowel movements.
Doctors may test the rectal reflex during a neurological examination.
A damaged spinal cord can impair the rectal reflex, leading to bowel dysfunction.

Etymology

From Latin 'rectum' (straight, referring to the rectum) and 'reflex' (from Latin 'reflexus', bent back).
