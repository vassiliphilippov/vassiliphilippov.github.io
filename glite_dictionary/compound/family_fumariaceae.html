family Fumariaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli fjuːˌmær.iˈeɪ.siːˌiː / (AmE), / ˈfamɪli fjuːˌmarɪˈeɪsɪˌiː / (BrE)
a family of flowering plants in the poppy order, including bleeding hearts, Dutchman's breeches, fumitories, and related species, native to the Northern Hemisphere and southern Africa
a family of flowering plants (bleeding hearts etc.)

Examples

Several species in the family Fumariaceae are prized in gardens for their delicate, lacy foliage and heart-shaped flowers.
Researchers are studying alkaloid compounds produced by plants in the family Fumariaceae.
Dutchman's breeches and bleeding heart both belong to the family Fumariaceae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fumitory - an annual flowering plant in the Fumariaceae family

Etymology

From Latin 'fumaria' (fumitory) plus the botanical family suffix '-aceae'.
