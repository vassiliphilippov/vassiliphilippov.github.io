aleatory music

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪliˌɛːtɔri ˈmjuzɪk / (AmE), / ˌælɪˈeɪtəri ˈmjuːzɪk / (BrE)
music in which some element of the composition is left to chance or to the performer's discretion during performance, often associated with certain experimental styles of the 20th century
music involving elements of chance

Examples

The concert featured several pieces of aleatory music, where performers had creative freedom over certain sections.
John Cage is renowned for his pioneering use of aleatory music in modern composition.
Students studied the structure and philosophy behind aleatory music in their advanced music theory class.

Synset Examples

He based his idea of structured improvisation on similar processes in the aleatoric music of composer John Cage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chance music - music in which chance procedures are used in composition or performance

Etymology

From Latin 'aleatorius' (dependent on the throw of a dice); 'aleatory' meaning 'depending on chance.'
