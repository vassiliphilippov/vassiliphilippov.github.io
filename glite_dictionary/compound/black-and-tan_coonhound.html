black-and-tan coonhound

1. NOUN

/ ˌblæk ənd ˈtæn ˈkuːnˌhaʊnd / (AmE), / ˌblæk ənd ˈtæn ˈkuːnˌhaʊnd / (BrE)
A large, powerful American hound dog breed developed for hunting raccoons and other game, characterized by its black coat with tan markings.
American breed of hunting dog

Examples

The black-and-tan coonhound is known for its determination and loud, baying bark when tracking prey.
As a puppy, our black-and-tan coonhound was already showing remarkable energy and curiosity.
Many hunters value the black-and-tan coonhound for its keen sense of smell and stamina.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coonhound - Breed of hound dog bred for hunting raccoons

Etymology

From the distinctive black and tan coloring of the dog and 'coonhound', a hound bred for raccoon hunting.
