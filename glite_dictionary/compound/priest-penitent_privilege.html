priest-penitent privilege

1. NOUN

/ priːst ˈpenɪtənt ˈprɪvəlɪdʒ / (AmE), / priːst ˈpenɪtənt ˈprɪvəlɪdʒ / (BrE)
the right of clergy to refuse to disclose confidential information received from someone during confession or similar religious communication
right to clergy-confessional confidentiality
legal

Examples

Under the priest-penitent privilege, the minister could not be compelled to testify about the confession.
Courts have long recognized the importance of priest-penitent privilege in protecting religious confidentiality.
The defense argued that the testimony was protected by priest-penitent privilege.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clergy privilege - legal right of clergy not to disclose confidential confessions
  • Synonym: attorney-client privilege - right of a lawyer to withhold confidential client information
