radium therapy

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪdiəm ˌθɛrəpi / (AmE), / ˈreɪdɪəm ˌθɛrəpi / (BrE)
a medical treatment in which radium is used as a source of radiation, formerly common in treating cancer and other conditions
treatment using radium radiation
medical

Examples

After being diagnosed with cancer, she underwent radium therapy as part of her treatment plan.
In the early 20th century, radium therapy was widely used before safer alternatives became available.
Doctors no longer recommend radium therapy due to concerns about radiation exposure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: radiation therapy - the treatment of disease with ionizing radiation

Etymology

From radium, the radioactive element, and therapy, from Greek therapeia (healing).
