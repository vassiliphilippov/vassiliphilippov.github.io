bird's-eye maple

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɝdz aɪ ˈmeɪpəl / (AmE), / ˈbɜːdz aɪ ˈmeɪpəl / (BrE)
a type of maple wood characterized by a wavy grain with small, eye-like markings, prized for use in fine woodworking and furniture
maple wood with eye-like grain markings

Examples

The antique cabinet was crafted from bird's-eye maple, adding a unique beauty to the piece.
He chose bird's-eye maple for the guitar body due to its decorative pattern.
The desk features bird's-eye maple veneer, which gives it an elegant look.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maple - a type of tree known for its hardwood and syrup

Etymology

Named for its distinctive grain, which resembles tiny bird's eyes.
