Great Russian

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡreɪt ˈrʌʃən / (AmE), / ˌɡreɪt ˈrʌʃ.ən / (BrE)
a member of the chief ethnic group of Russians, especially those living in European Russia, historically used to distinguish ethnic Russians from other peoples within the Russian Empire
a member of the chief ethnic Russian group

Examples

In 19th-century texts, the term Great Russian commonly referred to ethnic Russians living in European Russia.
Policies favored the Great Russian population over other ethnic minorities in the region.
The distinction between Great Russian and other groups such as Little Russians was once widely used.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Russian - a native or inhabitant of Russia; a person of Russian descent

Etymology

From English 'great' + 'Russian', translating Russian 'Velikoross' (Великорус).
