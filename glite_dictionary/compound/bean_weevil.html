bean weevil

1. NOUN

/ bin ˈwiːvəl / (AmE), / biːn ˈwiːvəl / (BrE)
a small beetle whose larvae live in and feed on growing or stored beans, often causing damage to crops or stored food products
beetle whose larvae feed on beans

Examples

Bean weevils can cause significant losses in stored beans if not properly controlled.
The infestation of bean weevils ruined most of last year's harvest.
Farmers often treat seeds before storage to protect them from bean weevils.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weevil - any small beetle of the family Curculionidae, typically having an elongated snout
