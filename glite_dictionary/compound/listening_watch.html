listening watch

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪsənɪŋ wɑtʃ / (AmE), / ˈlɪsənɪŋ wɒtʃ / (BrE)
a period or procedure in which a station or operator continuously listens for specific radio transmissions or signals of interest
monitoring radio transmissions for relevant signals

Examples

The radio operator kept a listening watch throughout the night in case of emergency transmissions.
During the mission, the crew maintained a listening watch on the designated frequency.
A listening watch ensures that important messages are not missed during silent periods.
