Holy Roman Emperor

1. NOUN

/ ˌhoʊli ˈroʊmən ɛmˈpɪrər / (AmE), / ˌhəʊli ˈrəʊmən ɛmˈpɪərə / (BrE)
the sovereign ruler of the Holy Roman Empire, a multi-ethnic complex of territories in Central Europe that existed from 962 to 1806
sovereign of the Holy Roman Empire

Examples

Charlemagne was crowned Holy Roman Emperor by the Pope in 800 AD.
The title of Holy Roman Emperor was held by members of the Habsburg dynasty for centuries.
As Holy Roman Emperor, he wielded considerable influence over European politics and religion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: emperor - a sovereign ruler of great power and rank, especially one ruling an empire
  • Synonym: Holy Roman Empire - the multi-ethnic territory in central Europe ruled by the Holy Roman Emperor

Etymology

From 'Holy Roman Empire', a term first used in the 13th century, combining 'holy' (sacred), 'Roman' (referencing ancient Rome), and 'Emperor' (Latin imperator).
