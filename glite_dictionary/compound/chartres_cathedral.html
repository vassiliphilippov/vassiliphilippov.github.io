Chartres Cathedral

1. PROPN

/ ʃɑrtr kəˈθiːdrəl / (AmE), / ˈʃɑːtr kəˈθiːdrəl / (BrE)
a famous Gothic cathedral in northern France, renowned for its architecture and stained glass, built mainly in the 13th century
13th-century Gothic cathedral in northern France

Examples

The stained glass windows of Chartres Cathedral are admired by visitors from around the world.
Many consider Chartres Cathedral to be a masterpiece of French Gothic architecture.
She studied medieval history and visited Chartres Cathedral during her trip to France.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Notre Dame Cathedral - famous Gothic cathedral in Paris, France

Etymology

Named after Chartres, France, where the cathedral is located.
