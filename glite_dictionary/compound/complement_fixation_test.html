complement fixation test

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːmpləmənt fɪkˈseɪʃən tɛst / (AmE), / ˈkɒmplɪmənt fɪkˈseɪʃən tɛst / (BrE)
a laboratory test in which a blood serum sample is mixed with a specific antigen and complement to detect the presence of antibodies to that antigen; used in medical diagnosis
test for detecting antibodies in blood
medical

Examples

The complement fixation test was used to diagnose infectious diseases before newer assays became widely available.
Doctors ordered a complement fixation test to check for antibodies in the patient's blood sample.
A positive complement fixation test result provided crucial evidence in confirming the infection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: serological test - a test that detects antibodies or antigens in blood

Etymology

From 'complement' (a group of blood proteins), 'fixation' (binding), and 'test'.
