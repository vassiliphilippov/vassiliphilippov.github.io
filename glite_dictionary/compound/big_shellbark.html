big shellbark

1. NOUN

/ bɪɡ ˈʃɛlˌbɑrk / (AmE), / bɪɡ ˈʃɛlˌbɑːk / (BrE)
A large hickory tree of the eastern United States, similar to the shagbark hickory but with much larger nuts.
large hickory tree with big nuts

Examples

The big shellbark can be found in river valleys throughout the eastern United States.
Its enormous nut easily distinguishes the big shellbark from its shagbark cousin.
Botanists often study the big shellbark for its unique adaptation to floodplain environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shagbark hickory - a similar hickory species with shaggy bark and smaller nuts
