Crown land

1. NOUN

/ kraʊn lænd / (AmE), / kraʊn lænd / (BrE)
Land owned by the monarch or state in certain countries, especially those of the Commonwealth, and administered as public land.
land owned by the monarch or state
legal british

Examples

The government proposed opening sections of Crown land for new housing developments.
Much of the national park is situated on Crown land managed by the local authorities.
Farmers must obtain a license to graze cattle on Crown land in this region.

Etymology

From the 'Crown' as the symbol of the monarchy, indicating ownership by the sovereign or state.
