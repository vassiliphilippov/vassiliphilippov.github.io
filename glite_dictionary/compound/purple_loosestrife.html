purple loosestrife

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɝːpəl ˈluːsˌstraɪf / (AmE), / ˈpɜːpl ˈluːsˌtraɪf / (BrE)
a tall marsh plant with a long spike of purple flowers, native to Europe but widespread in North America as an invasive species
tall marsh plant with purple flowers

Examples

The wetland was overgrown with purple loosestrife, crowding out native vegetation.
Conservationists are working to control the spread of purple loosestrife in local waterways.
We identified the striking spike of purple loosestrife during our nature walk.

Etymology

From the color 'purple' + 'loosestrife', from Old English 'lōstrife', possibly from Greek 'loosestrife' meaning 'ending strife' (due to supposed medicinal properties).
