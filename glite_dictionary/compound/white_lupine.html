white lupine

1. NOUN

/ waɪt ˈluːpɪn / (AmE), / waɪt ˈluːpɪn / (BrE)
A white-flowered Eurasian plant (Lupinus albus) widely cultivated for animal feed and for controlling soil erosion.
white-flowered Eurasian plant, Lupinus albus

Examples

Farmers often plant white lupine to help prevent soil erosion and enrich the land.
The seeds of the white lupine are used as a high-protein animal feed in many parts of Europe.
In the spring, fields of white lupine bloom with clusters of delicate, white flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lupine - any plant of the genus Lupinus
