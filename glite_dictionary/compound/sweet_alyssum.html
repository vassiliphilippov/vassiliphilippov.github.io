sweet alyssum

1. NOUN

/ swit əˈlɪsəm / (AmE), / swiːt əˈlɪsəm / (BrE)
a Mediterranean perennial plant (Lobularia maritima) with clusters of small, fragrant, usually white flowers, widely cultivated in gardens
a fragrant garden plant with small white flowers

Examples

Sweet alyssum is often planted along garden borders for its delicate scent.
The flower beds were filled with blooming sweet alyssum and bright marigolds.
She gave me a bouquet of sweet alyssum for the kitchen windowsill.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alyssum - any plant of the genus Alyssum, similar in appearance

Etymology

From the Greek 'alysson', a name for the plant, traditionally thought to treat madness (a- 'not' + lyssa 'madness').
