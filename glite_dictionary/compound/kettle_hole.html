kettle hole

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɛtəl hoʊl / (AmE), / ˈkɛtl̩ həʊl / (BrE)
A depression in the ground, often containing water, formed when a mass of glacial ice becomes buried in sediment and later melts.
depression formed by melting glacial ice

Examples

A small kettle hole formed in the field after the last Ice Age.
They studied the ecosystem in a kettle hole that had become a shallow pond.
Many kettle holes in the region are now important wildlife habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kettle lake - lake formed in a kettle hole

Etymology

From 'kettle', meaning pot, referring to the shape, and 'hole'; used in geology since the 19th century.
