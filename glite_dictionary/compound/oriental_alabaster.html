oriental alabaster

1. NOUN

/ ɔːr.iˈɛn.tl ˈæləˌbæstər / (AmE), / ˌɔː.riˈen.təl ˈæl.əˌbɑː.stə / (BrE)
a hard, compact variety of calcite used for carving and decorative objects, often with a translucent appearance
a compact variety of calcite used decoratively

Examples

The ancient vase was carved from oriental alabaster and glowed softly in the light.
Sculptors prized oriental alabaster for its smooth texture and natural beauty.
The statue was made of oriental alabaster, giving it a unique, translucent quality.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alabaster - a fine-grained, translucent form of gypsum or calcite used for carving

Etymology

From Latin *alabastrum*, influenced by 'oriental' (eastern), reflecting its historic sourcing from the East.
