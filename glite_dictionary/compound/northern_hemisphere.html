Northern Hemisphere

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɔrðərn ˈhɛmɪˌsfɪr / (AmE), / ˈnɔːðən ˈhɛmɪsfɪə / (BrE)
the half of the Earth that lies north of the equator
half of Earth north of the equator

Examples

Summer in the Northern Hemisphere typically lasts from June to August.
Many major cities, including New York and London, are located in the Northern Hemisphere.
The Northern Hemisphere experiences winter while the Southern Hemisphere has summer.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Southern Hemisphere - the half of the Earth that lies south of the equator
