day lily

1. NOUN

/ ˈdeɪ ˌlɪli / (AmE), / ˈdeɪ ˌlɪli / (BrE)
A perennial plant with broad ribbed leaves and clusters of white, blue, or lilac flowers, often used as ground cover.
type of flowering perennial plant

Examples

The day lily blooms add a splash of color to the garden each summer.
Many landscapers choose the day lily for its hardy nature and minimal care requirements.
After planting several day lilies along the walkway, the area looked instantly brighter.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdeɪ ˌlɪli / (AmE), / ˈdeɪ ˌlɪli / (BrE)
A perennial plant with tuberous roots, long narrow leaves, and lily-like flowers that typically last for only one day.
flowering plant with one-day blooms

Examples

The vibrant orange day lily in our garden blooms for only a single day.
Each summer, clusters of day lilies brighten up the roadside.
She planted several day lilies because of their colorful, fleeting flowers.

Etymology

Named for the short-lived nature of its flowers, which usually bloom for just one day.
