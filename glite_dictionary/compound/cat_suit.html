cat suit

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæt ˌsuːt / (AmE), / ˈkæt ˌsuːt / (BrE)
a close-fitting one-piece garment, typically made of stretchable material, covering the body from shoulders to thighs or ankles and sometimes the arms; worn for dance, acrobatics, or performance
tight-fitting one-piece garment

Examples

The dancer wore a shimmering cat suit that highlighted every movement on stage.
Acrobats often perform in a cat suit for unrestricted flexibility and comfort.
She bought a black cat suit for her role in the ballet production.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leotard - a one-piece skin-tight garment for exercise or dance

Etymology

Formed from 'cat' and 'suit', likely alluding to feline sleekness and agility.
