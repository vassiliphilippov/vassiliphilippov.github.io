genus Alca

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˈælkə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈælkə / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Alcidae, comprising solely the razorbill (Alca torda).
genus of birds including the razorbill

Examples

The genus Alca contains only one living species, the razorbill.
Researchers have studied the plumage of birds in the genus Alca for centuries.
Fossil records suggest that extinct relatives of genus Alca inhabited the North Atlantic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Alca torda - the razorbill, the only species in genus Alca

Etymology

From Latin *Alca*, the genus name assigned by Linnaeus; related to Old Norse *alka* (auk).
