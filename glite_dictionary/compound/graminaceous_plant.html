graminaceous plant

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡræməˈneɪʃəs plænt / (AmE), / ˌɡræmɪˈneɪʃəs plɑːnt / (BrE)
A plant belonging to the grass family, typically with hollow jointed stems and long narrow leaves.
plant of the grass family

Examples

Bamboo is a type of graminaceous plant that grows rapidly in many climates.
The wheat fields are filled with graminaceous plants in the late summer.
Botanists classify rice as a graminaceous plant due to its structural characteristics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grass - any of various plants of the family Poaceae

Etymology

From Latin 'gramineus' (grassy) + '-aceous' (of the nature of).
