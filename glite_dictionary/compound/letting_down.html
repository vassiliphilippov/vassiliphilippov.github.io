letting down

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛtɪŋ daʊn / (AmE), / ˈlɛtɪŋ daʊn / (BrE)
the act of causing something or someone to move to a lower position or level
lowering something to a lower level

Examples

Careful letting down of the equipment was required to avoid damage.
The letting down of the curtains signaled the end of the performance.
The engineer supervised the letting down of the bridge section into place.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lowering - the act of making something lower in position or amount
