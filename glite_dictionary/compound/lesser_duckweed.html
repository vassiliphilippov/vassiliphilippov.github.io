lesser duckweed

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛsər ˈdʌkˌwid / (AmE), / ˈlɛsə ˈdʌkˌwiːd / (BrE)
a small floating aquatic plant (Lemna minor) found in temperate regions except eastern Asia and Australia, with simple oval leaves and no obvious stems or roots
a small floating aquatic plant

Examples

The pond was covered with bright green patches of lesser duckweed.
Many waterbirds feed on lesser duckweed during the summer months.
Researchers study lesser duckweed to understand its rapid growth and ability to clean water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: duckweed - any of several small free-floating aquatic plants of the family Lemnaceae
