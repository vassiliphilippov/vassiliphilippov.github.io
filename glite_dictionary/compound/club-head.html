club-head

1. NOUN

/ ˈklʌbˌhɛd / (AmE), / ˈklʌbˌhɛd / (BrE)
the rounded or shaped end of a golf club that is used to strike the ball
head of a golf club

Examples

The golfer cleaned his club-head before taking his shot.
Modern club-heads are often made from lightweight metals for better performance.
A well-designed club-head can help improve the accuracy of your swing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clubface - the striking surface of the head of a golf club
