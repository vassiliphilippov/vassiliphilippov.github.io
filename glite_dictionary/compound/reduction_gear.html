reduction gear

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈdʌk.ʃən ɡɪr / (AmE), / rɪˈdʌk.ʃən ɡɪə / (BrE)
a set of gears designed to reduce the speed of an input shaft to a lower output speed, often increasing torque
gear system reducing input speed

Examples

The ship uses a reduction gear to slow down the engine speed and increase propeller torque.
A failure in the reduction gear caused the entire machine to shut down.
Engineers inspected the reduction gear after noticing unusual vibrations in the system.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gearbox - an enclosed system of gears in a vehicle
