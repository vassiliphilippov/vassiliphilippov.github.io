what are you like

1. X

/ wʌt ɑr ju laɪk / (AmE), / wɒt ɑː juː laɪk / (BrE)
used to ask about the characteristics, personality, or behavior of someone
inquiry about a person's traits or character

Examples

When meeting new people, she often asks, what are you like to get to know them better.
After hearing about his accomplishments, I wondered, what are you like outside of work?
If someone asked your friends, what are you like, what would they say?

Synset Examples

So, what are you like when you're stressed?
In a group, what are you like—shy or outgoing?
I’ve heard you’re quite funny; what are you like?
