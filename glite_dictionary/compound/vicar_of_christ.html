Vicar of Christ

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪkər əv kraɪst / (AmE), / ˈvɪkə əv kraɪst / (BrE)
a title for the Pope, signifying the Pope as the earthly representative of Christ and head of the Roman Catholic Church
title for the Pope, Roman Catholic leader

Examples

The Vicar of Christ delivered a message of peace and unity to the world.
Throughout history, the Vicar of Christ has played a central role in global affairs.
Many Catholics regard the Vicar of Christ as the spiritual leader of all Christians.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Pope - the Bishop of Rome and leader of the Roman Catholic Church

Etymology

From Latin vicarius Christi (deputy of Christ).
