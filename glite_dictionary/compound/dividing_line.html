dividing line

1. NOUN

/ dəˈvaɪdɪŋ laɪn / (AmE), / dɪˈvaɪdɪŋ laɪn / (BrE)
an imaginary or conceptual boundary that separates two things, such as ideas, groups, or conditions
an imaginary or conceptual boundary

Examples

The debate highlighted the dividing line between traditional and modern values.
There is often a fine dividing line between genius and madness.
In politics, the dividing line between left and right can be blurred.
