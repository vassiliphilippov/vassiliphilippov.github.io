scaly pholiota

1. NOUN

/ ˈskeɪli foʊˈlioʊtə / (AmE), / ˈskeɪli fəˈlɪətə / (BrE)
a species of gilled fungus with a cap and stalk that are covered in upright scales, and whose gills develop a greenish tinge as they age
a scaly-capped gilled mushroom

Examples

The scaly pholiota is often found growing in clusters on decaying wood in damp forests.
With its greenish-tinged gills and rough cap, the scaly pholiota is easy to identify.
Scientists collected several specimens of scaly pholiota to study their ecological role.

Etymology

From 'scaly' (having scales) and 'Pholiota', the genus name from Greek 'pholis' (scale).
