sound judgement

1. NOUN

/ saʊnd ˈdʒʌdʒmənt / (AmE), / saʊnd ˈdʒʌdʒmənt / (BrE)
the ability to assess situations or information wisely and make well-reasoned decisions
wise decision-making ability

Examples

She was promoted because her sound judgement made her an effective leader.
It takes sound judgement to choose the right investment for your future.
Good managers rely on sound judgement rather than impulsive decisions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wisdom - the quality of having experience, knowledge, and good judgement
  • Antonym: folly - lack of good sense; foolishness
