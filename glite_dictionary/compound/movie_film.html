movie film

1. NOUN

/ ˈmuːvi fɪlm / (AmE), / ˈmuːvi fɪlm / (BrE)
Photographic film several hundred feet long, used in a movie camera to record motion pictures.
photographic film for movies

Examples

The director insisted on using movie film instead of digital cameras to achieve a vintage look.
Many classic Hollywood movies were shot on 35mm movie film.
After loading the movie film into the camera, the crew began filming the first scene.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: film - thin flexible material coated with light-sensitive emulsion for photography or motion pictures
