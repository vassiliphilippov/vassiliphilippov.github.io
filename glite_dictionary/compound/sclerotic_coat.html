sclerotic coat

1. NOUN

/ skləˈrɑːtɪk koʊt / (AmE), / skləˈrɒtɪk kəʊt / (BrE)
the dense, white, fibrous outer layer of the eyeball that together with the cornea forms its outer covering
outer fibrous layer of the eyeball
medical

Examples

The sclerotic coat helps protect the inner structures of the eye from injury.
Inflammation of the sclerotic coat can cause severe pain and redness in the eye.
She learned about the function of the sclerotic coat during her anatomy class.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sclera - the white outer layer of the eyeball
  • Degree Lower: cornea - the transparent front part of the eye covering the iris and pupil

Etymology

From Greek 'sklērōtikos' (hardening) and 'coat' signifying a covering layer.
