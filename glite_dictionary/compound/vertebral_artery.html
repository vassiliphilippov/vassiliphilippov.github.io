vertebral artery

1. NOUN

/ vərˈtibrəl ˈɑːr.t̬ɚ.i / (AmE), / vəˈtiː.brəl ˈɑː.tər.i / (BrE)
either of the two arteries that branch from the subclavian arteries and pass through the vertebrae to supply blood to parts of the brain and spinal cord
artery supplying blood to brain and spinal cord
medical

Examples

A blockage in the vertebral artery can lead to serious neurological symptoms.
The surgeon carefully navigated around the vertebral artery during the procedure.
An MRI revealed a dissection of the left vertebral artery.

Etymology

From Latin vertebralis (of the vertebra) and artery, related to its course through the vertebrae.
