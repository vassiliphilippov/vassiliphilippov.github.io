Epistle to the Colossians

1. PROPN

/ ɪˈpɪsəl tu ðə kəˈlɑːʃənz / (AmE), / ɪˈpɪsəl tuː ðə kɒˈlɒʃənz / (BrE)
a book of the New Testament containing a letter attributed to Saint Paul addressed to the Christians in Colossae
New Testament book, letter to the Colossians

Examples

The Epistle to the Colossians is often studied in courses on religious literature.
Many scholars debate the authorship of the Epistle to the Colossians.
He quoted from the Epistle to the Colossians during his sermon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: New Testament - the second part of the Christian Bible, containing the Gospels and other writings

Etymology

From Greek 'epistolē' (letter) and 'Colossae', an ancient city in Phrygia.
