creeping Charlie

1. NOUN

/ ˈkripɪŋ ˈtʃɑrli / (AmE), / ˈkriːpɪŋ ˈtʃɑːli / (BrE)
a common name for certain trailing or creeping plants, especially ground ivy (Glechoma hederacea) or some species of loosestrife vine, known for their spreading growth habit
a trailing or creeping plant, especially ground ivy or loosestrife

Examples

Gardeners often struggle to remove creeping Charlie from their lawns because of its rapid spreading.
She noticed a thick patch of creeping Charlie weaving through the flowerbeds.
Some people appreciate creeping Charlie for its green foliage and delicate purple flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ground ivy - a creeping, evergreen plant with round, scalloped leaves (Glechoma hederacea)
