basso continuo

1. NOUN

/ ˌbɑːsoʊ kənˈtɪnowoʊ / (AmE), / ˌbæsəʊ kənˈtɪnjʊəʊ / (BrE)
A continuous bass part in Baroque music, written out in full and accompanied by numbers to indicate the chords to be played by harmony instruments.
bass accompaniment in Baroque music

Examples

The harpsichordist played the basso continuo throughout the entire movement.
In Baroque ensembles, the basso continuo supports both melody and harmony.
A cello and a keyboard instrument often share the basso continuo part.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: figured bass - Baroque bass notation using numbers to indicate chords

Etymology

Italian, literally 'continuous bass'.
