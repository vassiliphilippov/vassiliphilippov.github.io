gooseberry family

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡuːsˌbɛri ˈfæməli / (AmE), / ˈɡuːzbəri ˈfæməli / (BrE)
a family of flowering plants, including the gooseberry and currant, whose fruit is a berry; sometimes considered part of the Saxifragaceae family in earlier classifications
family of berry-producing plants (Grossulariaceae)

Examples

The gooseberry family includes both the cultivated gooseberry and various species of currant.
Botanists originally placed the gooseberry family within the broader saxifrage family, Saxifragaceae.
Members of the gooseberry family are recognized for their small, edible berry fruits.
