field pea

1. NOUN

/ fiːld piː / (AmE), / fiːld piː / (BrE)
the small, edible seed of a variety of pea plant (Pisum sativum) grown mainly for livestock feed and human consumption
edible seed of the field pea plant

Examples

The farmer harvested several bags of field peas for sale at the local market.
Dried field peas are often used as a protein-rich food for livestock.
I added a handful of field peas to the soup for extra nutrition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pea - the small spherical seed or the seed-pod of the pod fruit Pisum sativum

2. NOUN

/ ˈfiːld piː / (AmE), / ˈfiːld piː / (BrE)
a variety of pea plant (Pisum sativum subsp. arvense), native to the Mediterranean and North Africa, grown mainly as forage or for dried peas
variety of pea plant grown for forage

Examples

Farmers in the region planted field peas to use as animal feed during the winter.
The green pods of the field pea can be harvested early or left to dry on the vine.
In crop rotation, field peas help improve soil nitrogen for the following season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pea - a small, round, green seed eaten as a vegetable

3. NOUN

/ ˈfiːld piː / (AmE), / ˈfiːld piː / (BrE)
A variety of pea plant with small, coarse seeds, often eaten when young and tender or used as livestock feed.
variety of pea with small, coarse seeds

Examples

The farmer planted field peas as a cover crop to enrich the soil.
Many traditional Southern dishes include field peas as a staple ingredient.
Fresh field pea pods are often picked early for their tender taste.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pea - the small, round, edible seed of the pea plant
