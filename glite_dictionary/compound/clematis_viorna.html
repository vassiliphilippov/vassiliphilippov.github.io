Clematis viorna

1. NOUN

/ kləˈmætɪs viˈɔrnə / (AmE), / klɪˈmætɪs vaɪˈɔːnə / (BrE)
A climbing shrub of the southeastern United States, noted for its large red-purple, bell-shaped flowers with leathery recurved sepals.
climbing plant with red-purple bell flowers

Examples

The garden was filled with the vivid blooms of Clematis viorna climbing the trellis.
Horticulturists admire Clematis viorna for its distinctive bell-shaped flowers.
Botanists in the southeastern United States often study Clematis viorna as a native species.

Etymology

From genus name Clematis (Greek 'klēmatis', a climbing plant) and Latin species name 'viorna'.
