red wedding

1. NOUN

/ rɛd ˈwɛdɪŋ / (AmE), / rɛd ˈwɛdɪŋ / (BrE)
an event or situation, especially in fiction, that involves shocking violence or betrayal, often alluding to a notorious scene from Game of Thrones
shocking, violent betrayal (esp. in fiction)

Examples

Many fans still talk about the red wedding as the most shocking moment in television history.
The boardroom meeting turned into a red wedding as alliances fell apart unexpectedly.
Whenever someone mentions betrayal, the infamous red wedding always comes to mind.

Synset Examples

She compared her family drama to a red wedding.
They referenced the red wedding in their discussion.
The red wedding shocked everyone with its twists.

Etymology

From the 'Red Wedding,' a massacre in the fantasy TV series Game of Thrones and George R.R. Martin's novel A Storm of Swords.
