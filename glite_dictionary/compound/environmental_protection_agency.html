Environmental Protection Agency

1. PROPN

/ ɪnˌvaɪrənˈmɛntəl prəˈtɛkʃən ˈeɪdʒənsi / (AmE), / ɪnˌvaɪərənˈmɛntəl prəˈtɛkʃən ˈeɪdʒənsi / (BrE)
an independent U.S. federal agency responsible for regulating and enforcing national standards aimed at reducing pollution and protecting the environment
U.S. federal agency for environmental protection
american

Examples

The Environmental Protection Agency enforces rules to reduce air and water pollution across the country.
In 1970, the Environmental Protection Agency was established to protect human health and the environment.
The Environmental Protection Agency recently announced new regulations for industrial emissions.
