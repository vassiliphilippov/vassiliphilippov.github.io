yellow lupine

1. NOUN

/ ˈjɛloʊ ˈluːpaɪn / (AmE), / ˈjɛləʊ ˈluːpɪn / (BrE)
A European lupine plant with yellow flowers, often cultivated for animal feed or forage.
European yellow-flowered lupine plant

Examples

The farmer planted yellow lupine as a nutrient-rich crop for his livestock.
Many gardens feature yellow lupine for its bright and attractive flowers.
Researchers are studying how yellow lupine improves soil fertility in European agriculture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lupine - any plant of the genus Lupinus
