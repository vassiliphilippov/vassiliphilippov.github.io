loan participation

1. NOUN

/ loʊn pɑːrˌtɪsɪˈpeɪʃən / (AmE), / ləʊn pɑːˌtɪsɪˈpeɪʃən / (BrE)
a financing arrangement in which a group of banks jointly provide a loan that is too large for a single bank to handle alone
shared loan among multiple banks

Examples

The bank arranged a loan participation to finance the construction of the new hospital.
Many financial institutions prefer loan participation to spread their risk across different projects.
Our firm invested in a loan participation for the commercial real estate development.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: syndicated loan - a loan provided by a group of lenders and administered by one or several banks
