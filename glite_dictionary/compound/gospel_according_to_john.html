Gospel According to John

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɑspəl əˈkɔrdɪŋ tu dʒɑn / (AmE), / ˈɡɒspəl əˈkɔːdɪŋ tə dʒɒn / (BrE)
the fourth and final book of the Gospels in the New Testament, recounting the life and teachings of Jesus as attributed to John
the fourth Gospel in the New Testament

Examples

Many scholars have analyzed passages from the Gospel According to John to understand early Christian theology.
The opening verse of the Gospel According to John is widely quoted: 'In the beginning was the Word.'
She referenced the Gospel According to John in her sermon on love and faith.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Gospel According to Matthew - the first Gospel in the New Testament
  • Synonym: Gospel According to Mark - the second Gospel in the New Testament
  • Synonym: Gospel According to Luke - the third Gospel in the New Testament

Etymology

Gospel from Old English *gōdspel* ('good news'), John from the Hebrew name *Yochanan* ('Yahweh is gracious').
