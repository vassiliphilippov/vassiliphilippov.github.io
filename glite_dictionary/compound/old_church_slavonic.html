Old Church Slavonic

1. NOUN

/ oʊld tʃɜrtʃ sləˈvɑnɪk / (AmE), / əʊld tʃɜːtʃ sləˈvænɪk / (BrE)
the earliest recorded Slavic language, used in the 9th century for translating the Bible and other religious texts
earliest recorded Slavic language

Examples

The Bible was first translated into Old Church Slavonic in the 9th century.
Many Eastern European languages were influenced by Old Church Slavonic.
Scholars study Old Church Slavonic to understand the development of Slavic languages.

Etymology

From 'Old' + 'Church Slavonic', referring to the first literary Slavic language used in Orthodox Christian texts.
