plate iron

1. NOUN

/ pleɪt ˈaɪɚn / (AmE), / pleɪt ˈaɪən / (BrE)
Iron that has been rolled or forged into flat plates or sheets, typically used in construction or manufacturing.
iron in the form of flat sheets

Examples

The bridge was constructed using thick plate iron for added strength.
Early steamships relied on plate iron to reinforce their hulls.
Replacement of the old roof required several sheets of plate iron.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: sheet iron - iron in thin, flat pieces, usually thinner than plate iron
