New World mouse

1. NOUN

/ nuː ˈwɜrld maʊs / (AmE), / njuː ˈwɜːld maʊs / (BrE)
any of various small rodents native to North, Central, or South America, especially those of the family Cricetidae, distinct from Old World mice.
rodent native to the Americas

Examples

The forests of South America are home to many species of New World mouse.
Scientists observed a New World mouse building its nest among the tree roots.
Unlike the common house mouse, a New World mouse may exhibit more varied color patterns.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Old World mouse - rodents native to Europe, Asia, or Africa
