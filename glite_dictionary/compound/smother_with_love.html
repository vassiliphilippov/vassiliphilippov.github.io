to smother with love

1. VERB

/ ˈsmʌðɚ wɪð lʌv / (AmE), / ˈsmʌðə wɪð lʌv / (BrE)
to overwhelm someone with excessive displays of affection, often to the point of being overbearing or stifling
overwhelm with excessive affection

Examples

She tends to smother with love, making her children feel overwhelmed at times.
He didn't want to smother with love, so he tried to give his partner some space.
Grandparents often smother with love when their grandchildren come to visit.

Synset Examples

He tends to smother his kids with love.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spoil - to harm by giving too much attention or praise
