fine-toothed review

1. NOUN

/ faɪn tuːθt rɪˈvjuː / (AmE), / faɪn tuːθt rɪˈvjuː / (BrE)
a thorough and detailed examination or analysis of something
thorough examination

Examples

The auditors gave the company's financial records a fine-toothed review before the annual report was finalized.
Every application undergoes a fine-toothed review to ensure all requirements are met.
She performed a fine-toothed review of the contract before signing it.

Synset Examples

He gave the document a fine-toothed review before submission.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scrutiny - close and careful examination
  • Antonym: cursory review - quick and not detailed examination

Etymology

Metaphorically from 'fine-tooth comb,' referring to a tool used for careful searching.
