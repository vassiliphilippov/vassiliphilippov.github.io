Gulf of Akaba

1. PROPN

/ ɡʌlf əv əˈkɑːbə / (AmE), / ɡʌlf əv əˈkɑːbə / (BrE)
a northeastern extension of the Red Sea, situated between the Sinai Peninsula of Egypt and Saudi Arabia
northeastern arm of the Red Sea

Examples

Ships cross the Gulf of Akaba as they travel between Africa and Asia.
The coral reefs in the Gulf of Akaba are renowned for their biodiversity.
Many tourists visit the Gulf of Akaba to enjoy its warm waters and scenic coastline.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Gulf of Aqaba - alternative spelling or name for Gulf of Akaba
