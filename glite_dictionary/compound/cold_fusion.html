cold fusion

1. NOUN

/ koʊld ˈfjuʒən / (AmE), / kəʊld ˈfjuːʒən / (BrE)
a supposed type of nuclear fusion occurring at or near room temperature, widely regarded as unproven by the scientific community
supposed room-temperature nuclear fusion

Examples

Several scientists have conducted experiments hoping to achieve cold fusion in the laboratory.
The scientific community remains skeptical about claims of successful cold fusion reactions.
News of alleged cold fusion breakthroughs periodically captures public attention despite ongoing controversy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nuclear fusion - the process of fusing atomic nuclei to release energy
  • Antonym: hot fusion - nuclear fusion requiring extremely high temperatures
