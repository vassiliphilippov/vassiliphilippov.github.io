hipped tile

1. NOUN

/ hɪpt taɪl / (AmE), / hɪpt taɪl / (BrE)
a tile designed to cover the external angle, or hip, formed where two roof slopes meet on a hip roof
tile for hip of a roof

Examples

The roofer carefully placed each hipped tile to ensure the hip was properly sealed against rain.
Replacing a damaged hipped tile can prevent water from leaking into the house at the roof's edge.
Traditional clay hipped tiles are often used on historic buildings with hip roofs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ridge tile - a tile used to cover the ridge of a roof
