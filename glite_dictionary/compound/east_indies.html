East Indies

1. PROPN

/ ist ˈɪndiz / (AmE), / iːst ˈɪndiz / (BrE)
a group of islands in the Indian and Pacific Oceans between Asia and Australia, including present-day Indonesia and nearby territories; historically, a term used in European trade and exploration.
historical region: islands between Asia and Australia

Examples

Traders from Europe established routes to the East Indies during the Age of Exploration.
The spices imported from the East Indies were highly prized in Europe.
The term 'East Indies' is still used in historical discussion of colonialism.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: West Indies - the islands of the Caribbean Sea, contrasted with the East Indies

Etymology

From 'east' and 'Indies', the European term for lands of South and Southeast Asia and their islands.
