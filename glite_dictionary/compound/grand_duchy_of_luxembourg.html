Grand Duchy of Luxembourg

1. PROPN

/ ɡrænd ˈdʌtʃi əv ˈlʌksəmˌbɜrɡ / (AmE), / ɡrænd ˈdʌtʃi əv ˈlʌksəmbɜːɡ / (BrE)
A landlocked country in northwestern Europe, officially a grand duchy, situated between France, Belgium, and Germany, known as an international financial center.
European landlocked country; grand duchy

Examples

The Grand Duchy of Luxembourg is renowned for its beautiful castles and forests.
Several international banks have established headquarters in the Grand Duchy of Luxembourg.
The Grand Duchy of Luxembourg is one of the founding members of the European Union.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Luxembourg - the country now called the Grand Duchy of Luxembourg

Etymology

From French 'Luxembourg', ultimately from the Latinized 'Lucilinburhuc', meaning 'little fortress'.
