orchard oriole

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrtʃərd ˈɔriˌoʊl / (AmE), / ˈɔːtʃəd ˈɔːriˌəʊl / (BrE)
a small North American songbird (Icterus spurius), the male of which has chestnut and black plumage
a North American chestnut-and-black oriole

Examples

We spotted an orchard oriole perched on the fence near the apple trees.
The male orchard oriole is easily recognized by his striking chestnut and black feathers.
Every spring, orchard orioles return to nest in the tall trees by the river.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oriole - a type of brightly colored songbird

Etymology

Named for its preference for orchards and the Latin genus name Icterus spurius.
