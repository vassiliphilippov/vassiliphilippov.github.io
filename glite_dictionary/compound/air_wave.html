air wave

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛr ˌweɪv / (AmE), / ˈeə ˌweɪv / (BrE)
A wave of sound that travels through the air, as opposed to through solids or liquids.
sound wave transmitted through air

Examples

The explosion generated a powerful air wave that shattered nearby windows.
Scientists measure the speed at which an air wave travels to determine the properties of the medium.
An air wave can carry sounds from one room to another if the doors are open.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sound wave - a wave of compression and rarefaction by which sound is propagated
