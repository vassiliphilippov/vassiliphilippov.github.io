Kasai River

1. PROPN

/ kɑːˈsaɪ ˈrɪvər / (AmE), / kɑːˈsaɪ ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
A river in southwestern Africa that originates in central Angola, flows east and then north, forming part of the border between Angola and the Democratic Republic of the Congo, and continues northwest to join the Congo River.
river in southwestern Africa, tributary of the Congo River

Examples

The Kasai River serves as a vital transportation route for communities along its banks.
Several towns have developed at key crossing points on the Kasai River.
The waters of the Kasai River are crucial for agriculture in the region.
