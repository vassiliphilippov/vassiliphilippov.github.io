Rhodes grass

1. NOUN

/ ˈroʊdz ɡræs / (AmE), / ˈrəʊdz ɡrɑːs / (BrE)
A perennial grass native to South Africa, often cultivated in dry regions as forage for livestock.
forage grass cultivated in dry regions

Examples

Farmers in arid areas often rely on Rhodes grass to feed their cattle.
The rapid growth of Rhodes grass makes it an ideal choice for pasture in dry climates.
Researchers are studying the drought resistance of Rhodes grass compared to other forage plants.

Etymology

Named after Cecil Rhodes, a British colonialist associated with southern Africa.
