Secret Service

1. NOUN

/ ˈsi.krət ˈsɝː.vɪs / (AmE), / ˈsiː.krɪt ˈsɜː.vɪs / (BrE)
the United States government agency responsible for protecting the president, vice president, their families, visiting dignitaries, and for investigating counterfeiting and certain other crimes
U.S. protective and investigative agency
american

Examples

The Secret Service increased security in advance of the president’s visit.
Members of the Secret Service are trained to respond quickly to potential threats.
The Secret Service also investigates cases of counterfeiting and financial fraud.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: FBI - Federal Bureau of Investigation; U.S. federal law enforcement agency
  • Synonym: CIA - Central Intelligence Agency; U.S. foreign intelligence service
