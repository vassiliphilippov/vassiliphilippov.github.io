prince's pine

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɪnsɪz paɪn / (AmE), / ˈprɪnsɪz paɪn / (BrE)
a small evergreen plant of the genus Chimaphila, typically found in woodland areas of North America and Eurasia
a small woodland plant, genus Chimaphila

Examples

We spotted a patch of prince's pine growing beneath the shade of the oaks.
Botanists studied the medicinal properties of prince's pine during their forest survey.
The small, glossy leaves of prince's pine are easy to recognize on a woodland walk.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chimaphila - genus of small evergreen plants, including prince's pine

Etymology

Named for its resemblance to small pine trees and possibly for its valued use in traditional medicine.
