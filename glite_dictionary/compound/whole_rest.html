whole rest

1. NOUN

/ hoʊl rɛst / (AmE), / həʊl rɛst / (BrE)
a musical rest lasting the duration of a whole note (usually four beats in common time)
musical rest equal to four beats

Examples

The conductor signaled the orchestra to observe a whole rest at the end of the phrase.
Students learned to count a whole rest as four beats in their music class.
You must play nothing during the whole rest in measure eight.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: half rest - a musical rest equal in duration to a half note (two beats in common time)
