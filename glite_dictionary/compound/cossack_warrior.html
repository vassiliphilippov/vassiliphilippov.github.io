Cossack warrior

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑː.sæk ˈwɔːr.i.ər / (AmE), / ˈkɒ.sæk ˈwɒr.i.ə / (BrE)
A member of a historical group of fiercely independent cavalrymen from Russia and Eastern Europe, renowned for their military skill and bravery.
historical Russian and Eastern European cavalryman

Examples

Many legends recount the heroic deeds of the Cossack warrior on the steppes.
The painting depicted a Cossack warrior galloping across the battlefield.
She was fascinated by the stories of her great-grandfather, a renowned Cossack warrior.

Synset Examples

Her ancestry traces back to the fierce Cossack warriors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cavalryman - a soldier who fights on horseback

Etymology

From Russian 'Kazák', meaning 'free man', originally referring to semi-independent Tatar and later Slavic horsemen.
