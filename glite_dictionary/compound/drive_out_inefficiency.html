to drive out inefficiency

1. VERB

/ draɪv aʊt ɪnɪˈfɪʃənsi / (AmE), / draɪv aʊt ɪnɪˈfɪʃənsi / (BrE)
to remove or eliminate wastefulness or ineffectiveness from an organization, system, or process
eliminate waste or ineffectiveness

Examples

The new CEO was determined to drive out inefficiency in every department.
We implemented new software to help drive out inefficiency from our workflow.
Regular training and audits are essential to driving out inefficiency in large organizations.

Synset Examples

The new policy is meant to drive out inefficiency.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to eliminate - to completely remove or get rid of something
