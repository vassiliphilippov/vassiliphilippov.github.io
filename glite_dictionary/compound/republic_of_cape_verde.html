Republic of Cape Verde

1. PROPN

/ rɪˈpʌblɪk əv keɪp vɜrd / (AmE), / rɪˈpʌblɪk əv keɪp vɜːd / (BrE)
an island country in the central Atlantic Ocean, off the coast of West Africa, consisting of an archipelago of ten volcanic islands
island country in the central Atlantic, off West Africa

Examples

The Republic of Cape Verde is known for its Creole Portuguese-African culture and beautiful beaches.
In 1975, the Republic of Cape Verde gained independence from Portugal.
Tourism is a growing sector of the Republic of Cape Verde's economy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cabo Verde - the official Portuguese name for Cape Verde

Etymology

From Portuguese 'Cabo Verde', meaning 'Green Cape', named after Cap Vert on the Senegalese coast.
