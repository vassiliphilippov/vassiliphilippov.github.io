centre of curvature

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛntɚ əv ˈkɝvətʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈsɛntə əv ˈkɜːvətʃə / (BrE)
the point that is the center of the circle that best approximates a curve at a given point
point at center of circle approximating a curve

Examples

The centre of curvature of a mirror determines the radius of its reflecting surface.
At each point on a curve, the centre of curvature lies along the normal to the curve.
In optical systems, the location of the centre of curvature is crucial for focusing light.

Etymology

From Latin 'centrum' (center) and 'curvatura' (curvature).
