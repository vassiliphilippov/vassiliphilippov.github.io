dust contamination

1. NOUN

/ dʌst kənˌtæməˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / dʌst kənˌtæmɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the condition of a surface, material, or environment being tainted or polluted by dust particles
contamination by dust particles

Examples

The laboratory required strict procedures to prevent dust contamination during the experiment.
Illustrators avoid dust contamination on their drawings by covering unused paper with clean sheets.
Regular cleaning of electronic devices helps reduce dust contamination and maintains their performance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contamination - the action or state of making or being made impure by polluting or poisoning

/ dʌst ˌkɑːn.təˈmeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / dʌst ˌkɒn.təˈmeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the presence or introduction of dust particles that make a place, material, or substance impure or unsafe
contamination by dust particles

Examples

Regular maintenance helps minimize dust contamination in industrial clean rooms.
Even a small amount of dust contamination can compromise the quality of semiconductor manufacturing.
