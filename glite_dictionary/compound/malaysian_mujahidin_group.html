Malaysian Mujahidin Group

1. PROPN

/ məˈleɪʒən muːˈdʒɑːhɪdiːn ɡruːp / (AmE), / məˈleɪziən mjuːˈdʒɑːhɪdiːn ɡruːp / (BrE)
a clandestine militant Islamist organization, formed in 1993, that has operated in Southeast Asia and is linked to al-Qaeda, advocating militant Islam and involved in terrorist activities in Indonesia, Malaysia, Singapore, and the Philippines.
Southeast Asian militant Islamist group (est. 1993)

Examples

The authorities broke up a Malaysian Mujahidin Group cell planning attacks in Singapore.
The Malaysian Mujahidin Group reportedly maintained connections with al-Qaeda operatives.
Security agencies monitor the activities of the Malaysian Mujahidin Group across Southeast Asia.
