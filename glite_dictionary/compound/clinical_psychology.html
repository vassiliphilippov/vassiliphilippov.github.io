clinical psychology

1. NOUN

/ ˈklɪnɪkəl saɪˈkɑːlədʒi / (AmE), / ˈklɪnɪkəl saɪˈkɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the branch of psychology focused on diagnosing and treating mental disorders and abnormal behavior
branch of psychology treating mental disorders
medical

Examples

After earning her doctorate in clinical psychology, she began working at a local mental health center.
Clinical psychology has made significant advances in understanding and treating anxiety disorders.
He chose to specialize in clinical psychology because he wanted to help people cope with depression and trauma.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: counseling psychology - the branch of psychology focusing on providing advice and guidance for personal, social, or psychological problems
  • Degree Higher: psychiatry - the branch of medicine dealing with mental, emotional, or behavioral disorders

Etymology

From 'clinical' (relating to the observation and treatment of actual patients) and 'psychology' (from Greek 'psyche' meaning 'soul, mind' and 'logos' meaning 'study').
