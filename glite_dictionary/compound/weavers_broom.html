weaver's broom

1. NOUN

/ ˈwiːvərz bruːm / (AmE), / ˈwiːvəz bruːm / (BrE)
a tall, thornless shrub with pale yellow flowers and slender twigs used for weaving baskets, native to southwestern Europe and the Mediterranean
a shrub used for basket weaving

Examples

The flexible twigs of the weaver's broom are ideal for making baskets and brooms.
Farmers in California have planted weaver's broom along roadsides for soil stabilization.
The pale yellow blossoms of the weaver's broom brighten the Mediterranean hillsides each spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Spanish broom - another name for the same or a closely related shrub
