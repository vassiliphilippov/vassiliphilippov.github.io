xanthodont fossil

1. NOUN

/ ˈzænθəˌdɑnt ˈfɑsəl / (AmE), / ˈzanθəˌdɒnt ˈfɒsəl / (BrE)
A fossil belonging to a yellow-toothed species, typically used in paleontology.
fossil of a yellow-toothed species

Examples

The paleontologist discovered a rare xanthodont fossil in the sedimentary rock layer.
Researchers believe the xanthodont fossil provides clues to the diet of ancient animals.
The museum's new exhibit features several impressive xanthodont fossils.

Synset Examples

He studied the xanthodont fossils from ancient times.

Etymology

From Greek xanthos (yellow) + odōn (tooth), referring to yellow-toothed species.
