European cranberry

1. NOUN

/ jʊˈroʊpiən ˈkrænˌbɛri / (AmE), / jʊəˈrəʊpiən ˈkrænˌbɛri / (BrE)
a small, trailing plant of Arctic and cool regions of the Northern Hemisphere, bearing small red edible berries
trailing northern plant with red berries

Examples

The European cranberry thrives in the cool, moist bogs of Scandinavia and Russia.
She added some freshly picked European cranberry to her homemade jam.
Botanists study the distribution of the European cranberry in different parts of the Northern Hemisphere.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cranberry - Edible red berry produced by various low-growing shrubs
