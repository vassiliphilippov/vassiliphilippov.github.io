colored audition

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌlɚd ɔːˈdɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈkʌləd ɔːˈdɪʃən / (BrE)
A form of chromesthesia in which the perception of sounds triggers the experience of colors.
perception of colors with sounds (chromesthesia)
medical

Examples

Some composers reportedly experience colored audition, associating musical notes with shades like blue or yellow.
The phenomenon of colored audition fascinates neuroscientists studying the brain's sensory connections.
People with colored audition may perceive spoken words or everyday sounds as flashes of color.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chromesthesia - a type of synesthesia where sounds evoke colors

Etymology

Formed from 'colored' and 'audition' (hearing); used in psychological studies of chromesthesia.
