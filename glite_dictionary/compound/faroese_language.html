Faroese language

1. NOUN

/ ˌfɛəroʊˈiːz ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌfæərəʊˈiːz ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the North Germanic language spoken on the Faroe Islands, closely related to Icelandic and western Norwegian dialects
language of the Faroe Islands

Examples

She is fluent in both Icelandic and Faroese language.
The Faroese language has preserved many features of Old Norse.
Children in the Faroe Islands are taught the Faroese language from a young age.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Icelandic - the North Germanic language spoken in Iceland
  • Synonym: Norwegian - the North Germanic language spoken in Norway

Etymology

From Faroe Islands + -ese, patterned after names of other languages.
