board of directors

1. NOUN

/ bɔrd əv dəˈrɛktɚz / (AmE), / bɔːd əv dɪˈrɛktəz / (BrE)
a group of people elected or appointed to oversee and govern the activities and policies of a company or organization
governing group for a company or organization

Examples

She was recently appointed to the board of directors of a leading tech company.
The board of directors voted unanimously to approve the merger proposal.
Serving on the board of directors requires both expertise and a commitment to the organization’s mission.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: executive committee - a smaller group with executive powers within an organization

2. NOUN

/ bɔrd əv dəˈrɛktərz / (AmE), / bɔːd əv dɪˈrɛktəz / (BrE)
a group of people elected or appointed to oversee the activities and management of an organization or company
governing group of an organization or company

Examples

The board of directors approved the new company policy during Thursday's meeting.
As a member of the board of directors, she helps set long-term strategy for the firm.
After the merger, the board of directors was restructured to include representatives from both companies.

Synset Examples

The final decision rests with the board of directors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: executive committee - group responsible for day-to-day management decisions
  • Degree Lower: management - group responsible for implementing policies set by the board
