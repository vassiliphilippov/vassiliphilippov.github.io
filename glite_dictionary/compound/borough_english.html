borough English

1. NOUN

/ ˌbɝː.oʊ ˈɪŋ.glɪʃ / (AmE), / ˌbʌr.əʊ ˈɪŋ.glɪʃ / (BrE)
a former English custom in which the youngest son inherited land, excluding his older brothers
former custom of youngest son inheriting land
legal archaic british

Examples

Under borough English, the youngest son would inherit the family property after his father's death.
The tradition of borough English contrasts with primogeniture, where the eldest son inherits.
Historians often study borough English to understand medieval land laws in England.

Etymology

From Middle English, referring to a custom practiced in certain English boroughs.
