purple orchis

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɝː.pəl ˈɔːr.kɪs / (AmE), / ˈpɜː.pəl ˈɔː.kɪs / (BrE)
A North American orchid with a spike of violet-purple and white flowers, with sepals and petals forming a hood.
North American orchid with violet and white flowers

Examples

The purple orchis blooms in late spring throughout the woodlands.
Scientists found several purple orchis clusters near the riverbank.
The guide pointed out a rare purple orchis growing among the wildflowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orchid - a plant of the large family Orchidaceae, with showy flowers

Etymology

From 'orchis', the genus name, and 'purple', referring to the colour of the flowers.
