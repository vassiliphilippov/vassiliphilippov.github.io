genus Leptinotarsa

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒinəs lɛp.tɪˈnoʊ.tɑr.sə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs lɛp.tɪˈnɒ.tɑː.sə / (BrE)
A genus of beetles in the family Chrysomelidae, notable for including the Colorado potato beetle, an agricultural pest.
genus of beetles including Colorado potato beetle

Examples

The genus Leptinotarsa contains several species that are significant agricultural pests.
Researchers found that beetles from the genus Leptinotarsa have developed resistance to common insecticides.
The most well-known member of the genus Leptinotarsa is the Colorado potato beetle, which feeds on potato plants.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin genus name *Leptinotarsa*, combining Greek 'leptos' (thin) and 'tarsa' (foot), referring to features of the beetle's legs.
