lesser scaup duck

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛsɚ skaʊp dʌk / (AmE), / ˈlɛsə skaʊp dʌk / (BrE)
a common North American diving duck (Aythya affinis), the male of which has a purplish head and a blue bill
North American diving duck species

Examples

In the spring, large flocks of lesser scaup duck can be seen migrating northward across the lakes.
The male lesser scaup duck is easily recognized by its purplish-black head and blue bill.
Birdwatchers spotted a group of lesser scaup ducks swimming near the shore.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: greater scaup duck - a similar, larger diving duck of North America and Eurasia

Etymology

From 'lesser' (smaller) and 'scaup' (a kind of duck, probably from Scots 'scalp,' a shell-bed where the ducks fed).
