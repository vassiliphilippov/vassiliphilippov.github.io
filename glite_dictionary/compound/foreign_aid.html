foreign aid

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔɹən eɪd / (AmE), / ˈfɒɹɪn eɪd / (BrE)
money, resources, or assistance (such as economic or military help) given by one country to another, especially to support development or recovery.
economic or military assistance given by one nation to another

Examples

Many developing countries rely on foreign aid to fund healthcare and education programs.
The government pledged to increase foreign aid after the devastating earthquake.
Debates continue about the effectiveness of foreign aid in promoting sustainable growth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assistance - help or support
