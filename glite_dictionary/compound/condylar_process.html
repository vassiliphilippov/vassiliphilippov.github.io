condylar process

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑn.dɪ.lɚ ˈprɑ.sɛs / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.dɪ.lə ˈprəʊ.sɛs / (BrE)
the rounded projection at the upper end of the mandible that fits into the joint of the jaw and articulates with the skull
part of the lower jaw bone
medical

Examples

Damage to the condylar process can result in difficulty opening the mouth fully.
The dentist explained that the condylar process is essential for normal jaw movement.
A fracture in the condylar process may require surgical intervention.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mandible - the lower jawbone in vertebrates

Etymology

From Latin 'condylus' (knuckle, joint) and 'processus' (projection).
