floating voter

1. NOUN

/ ˈfloʊtɪŋ ˈvoʊtər / (AmE), / ˈfləʊtɪŋ ˈvəʊtə / (BrE)
a person who does not consistently support any one political party and can be influenced to vote in different ways in different elections
voter with no fixed party allegiance

Examples

The outcome of the election may depend on how many floating voters are persuaded by each campaign.
As a floating voter, she carefully considered the policies before making her decision in the polling booth.
Political parties often focus their efforts on floating voters during closely contested elections.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swing voter - a voter who may not be affiliated with a particular party and whose vote is unpredictable
