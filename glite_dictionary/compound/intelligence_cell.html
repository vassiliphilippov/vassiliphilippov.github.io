intelligence cell

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtɛlədʒəns sɛl / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɛlɪdʒəns sɛl / (BrE)
a small, secret group trained to collect information by performing reconnaissance and surveillance, typically within an organization or terrorist group
a small group trained for secret surveillance

Examples

The authorities discovered an intelligence cell operating near the border to gather military information.
Each intelligence cell works independently to reduce the risk of exposure to the larger organization.
The intelligence cell transmitted the surveillance data back to their leaders using encrypted channels.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: terrorist cell - a small group within a terrorist organization, acting semi-independently
  • Synonym: spy ring - a group organized to carry out espionage
