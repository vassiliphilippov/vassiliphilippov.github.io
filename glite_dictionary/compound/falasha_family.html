Falasha family

1. NOUN

/ fəˈlɑːʃə ˈfæməli / (AmE), / fəˈlɑːʃə ˈfæməli / (BrE)
A family belonging to the Beta Israel community; an Ethiopian Jewish family, especially one historically known as 'Falasha.'
Ethiopian Jewish family (Beta Israel)

Examples

The Falasha family celebrated traditional Jewish holidays after migrating to Israel.
A local organization provides support for every Falasha family settling in the city.
Many members of the Falasha family keep both Ethiopian and Jewish cultural practices alive.

Synset Examples

Many Falasha families migrated to Israel for better opportunities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Beta Israel - The Ethiopian Jewish community traditionally known as 'Falasha.'

Etymology

'Falasha' is from Amharic, meaning 'exile' or 'stranger,' historically used for Ethiopian Jews.
