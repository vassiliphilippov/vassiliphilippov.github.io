genus Spermophilus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs spərˈmɑː.fɪ.ləs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs spəˈmɒ.fɪ.ləs / (BrE)
a genus of small, burrowing rodents known as typical ground squirrels, widely distributed in the Northern Hemisphere
a genus of ground squirrels

Examples

The genus Spermophilus includes several species of ground squirrels common across Eurasia and North America.
Researchers studied the hibernation patterns among members of the genus Spermophilus.
Many farmers consider some species in the genus Spermophilus to be agricultural pests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ground squirrel - a small burrowing rodent found in grasslands

Etymology

From Greek 'sperma' (seed) and 'philos' (loving), referring to their seed-eating habits.
