beggar-my-neighbour

1. NOUN

/ ˌbɛɡɚ maɪ ˈneɪbɚ / (AmE), / ˌbɛɡə maɪ ˈneɪbə / (BrE)
A simple two-player card game where the object is to win all of the opponent's cards, often played by turning over cards and responding to court cards.
two-player card game

Examples

They played beggar-my-neighbour for hours on a rainy afternoon.
The rules of beggar-my-neighbour are easy enough for children to learn quickly.
I lost every round of beggar-my-neighbour against my sister last night.

Etymology

From the phrase 'beggar my neighbour', meaning to impoverish others for personal gain.
