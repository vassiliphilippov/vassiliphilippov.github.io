fresh catch

1. NOUN

/ frɛʃ kætʃ / (AmE), / frɛʃ katʃ / (BrE)
fish or seafood that has been caught recently, usually before being preserved or cooked
recently caught fish or seafood

Examples

The restaurant proudly served the fresh catch grilled with lemon and herbs.
Locals prefer to buy their fresh catch directly from the harbor in the morning.
Today's fresh catch includes snapper, mackerel, and shrimp.

Synset Examples

The fishwife sold fresh catches at the market.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catch of the day - the fish or seafood caught that day, especially as offered in a restaurant
