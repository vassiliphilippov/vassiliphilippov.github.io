long vowel

1. NOUN

/ lɔŋ ˈvaʊəl / (AmE), / lɒŋ ˈvaʊəl / (BrE)
A vowel sound that is pronounced for a longer duration than a short vowel, often corresponding to the name of the vowel letter.
vowel sound pronounced longer than a short vowel

Examples

The word 'cape' contains a long vowel sound in the first syllable.
Children often learn to distinguish between short and long vowels in school.
The teacher asked the students to give examples of words with a long vowel.

Synset Examples

Long vowel is the term used to refer to vowel sounds whose pronunciation is the same as its letter name.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: short vowel - a vowel sound pronounced for a shorter duration
