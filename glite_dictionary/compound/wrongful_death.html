wrongful death

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɔŋfəl dɛθ / (AmE), / ˈrɒŋfəl dɛθ / (BrE)
a death caused by the wrongful act, neglect, or default of another, which may serve as the basis for a civil lawsuit by the deceased person's family or heirs
death caused by another's wrongful act, basis for lawsuit
legal

Examples

The family filed a lawsuit after the wrongful death of their loved one in a workplace accident.
Her attorneys argued that his wrongful death was the result of medical negligence.
Settlements in wrongful death cases can help support the surviving family members.
