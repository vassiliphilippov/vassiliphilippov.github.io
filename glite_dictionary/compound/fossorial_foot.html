fossorial foot

1. NOUN

/ fəˈsɔriəl fʊt / (AmE), / fəˈsɔːriəl fʊt / (BrE)
a foot adapted for digging, as seen in animals such as moles
foot adapted for digging

Examples

The mole's fossorial foot allows it to tunnel efficiently underground.
Many burrowing animals have developed a fossorial foot for moving soil.
The structure of the fossorial foot differs noticeably from that of non-burrowing mammals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: digging foot - foot adapted for digging

Etymology

From Latin 'fossorius' (for digging) + English 'foot'.
