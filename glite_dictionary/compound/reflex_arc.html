reflex arc

1. NOUN

/ ˈriː.flɛks ɑːrk / (AmE), / ˈriː.flɛks ɑːk / (BrE)
the pathway taken by nerve impulses during a reflex action, typically involving a sensory neuron, a relay neuron, and a motor neuron
neural pathway of a reflex
medical

Examples

The doctor explained how a reflex arc allows us to react quickly to painful stimuli.
Damage to any part of the reflex arc can impair the body's automatic responses.
In biology class, we studied how the knee-jerk response is mediated by a simple reflex arc.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reflex - an involuntary and nearly instantaneous movement in response to a stimulus
