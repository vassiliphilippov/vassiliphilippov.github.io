boiling point

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɔɪlɪŋ pɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˈbɔɪlɪŋ pɔɪnt / (BrE)
the temperature at which a liquid changes to a gas, especially under standard atmospheric pressure (sea level)
temperature at which a liquid boils

Examples

At its boiling point, water turns into steam and begins to evaporate rapidly.
The boiling point of ethanol is lower than that of water, making it easier to distill.
Scientists record the boiling point of various substances to identify them accurately.

Synset Examples

they brought the water to a boil

Related Terms

  • Antonym: melting point - temperature at which a solid becomes a liquid
  • Antonym: freezing point - temperature at which a liquid becomes a solid

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɔɪlɪŋ pɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˈbɔɪlɪŋ pɔɪnt / (BrE)
A state of extreme anger or agitation; the moment when someone's emotions are about to erupt.
extreme anger or agitation

Examples

After repeated delays, her patience finally reached boiling point and she stormed out of the room.
The debate grew more heated, and tensions in the room rose to boiling point.
Public frustration over the policy changes is at boiling point among commuters.

Synset Examples

after an hour of waiting I was at the boiling point

Related Terms

  • Synonym: breaking point - the moment of greatest strain; a critical moment

Etymology

Metaphorical extension from the scientific term for the temperature at which a liquid boils.

3. NOUN

/ ˈbɔɪlɪŋ pɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˈbɔɪlɪŋ pɔɪnt / (BrE)
a critical or extreme level of anger or stress that is about to erupt or be expressed
critical threshold of anger or stress

Examples

After hours of delay, the crowd's frustration finally reached its boiling point.
Tensions in the office have been rising, and now they're at a boiling point.
If you keep pushing him, he'll hit his boiling point soon.

Synset Examples

His patience hit a boiling point after the third complaint.
The stress reached a boiling point during the meeting.
They were close to a boiling point after weeks of tension.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: breaking point - the moment of collapse under stress
  • Antonym: calm - a state of peacefulness and lack of anger or stress

Etymology

Figurative use based on the physical property of boiling point, the temperature at which a liquid boils.
