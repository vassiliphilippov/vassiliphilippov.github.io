arrow wood

1. NOUN

/ ˈæroʊ wʊd / (AmE), / ˈærəʊ wʊd / (BrE)
A deciduous shrub native to eastern North America, known for its tough, flexible wood and blue-black berries; its wood was formerly used to make arrows.
deciduous North American shrub with tough wood

Examples

The native arrow wood produces clusters of blue-black berries in late summer.
Early settlers used the strong branches of arrow wood for making arrows and tools.
Several birds feed on the fruit of the arrow wood during the autumn months.

Etymology

Named for its wood's historical use in making arrows.

2. NOUN

/ ˈæroʊ wʊd / (AmE), / ˈærəʊ wʊd / (BrE)
a deciduous shrub (Viburnum dentatum) native to eastern North America, with slender straight stems historically used for making arrows
deciduous North American shrub

Examples

The slender branches of the arrow wood were traditionally used to make arrows by Native Americans.
In spring, the arrow wood produces clusters of small white flowers that attract pollinators.
The garden included several arrow wood shrubs along the eastern fence for their attractive fall foliage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: southern arrow wood - a closely related southern species of arrow wood shrub
