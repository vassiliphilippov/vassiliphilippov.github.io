toxic shock syndrome

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɑksɪk ʃɑk sɪnˌdroʊm / (AmE), / ˈtɒksɪk ʃɒk ˈsɪndrəʊm / (BrE)
a rare, life-threatening condition caused by bacterial infection, characterized by high fever, rash, low blood pressure, and shock, often associated with tampon use in menstruating women
life-threatening bacterial infection syndrome
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed her with toxic shock syndrome after she presented with a sudden high fever and rash.
Awareness campaigns in the 1980s helped to reduce cases of toxic shock syndrome linked to tampon use.
Prompt medical treatment is critical when toxic shock syndrome is suspected to prevent complications.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sepsis - a life-threatening condition caused by the body's response to infection
  • Synonym: meningococcemia - an acute infection caused by Neisseria meningitidis, often resulting in shock

Etymology

From 'toxic' (relating to poison) + 'shock' + 'syndrome', first identified in the late 1970s.
