genus Chamaeleon

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs kəˌmiːliˈɒn / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs kəˌmiːliˈɒn / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Chamaeleontidae, comprising the true chameleons.
type genus of chameleons

Examples

The genus Chamaeleon includes several well-known species of chameleons.
Researchers discovered a new species within the genus Chamaeleon in Madagascar.
Members of the genus Chamaeleon are characterized by independently movable eyes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chameleon - any of various Old World lizards of the family Chamaeleontidae

Etymology

From Latin 'genus' (kind) and 'Chamaeleon', from Greek 'khamaileōn' (chameleon).
