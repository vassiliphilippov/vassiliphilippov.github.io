family Cottidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ˈkɑːtɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli ˈkɒtɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of mostly marine fishes known as sculpins, characterized by their large heads and spines, found chiefly in northern waters.
family of marine fishes (sculpins)

Examples

The family Cottidae includes many species of sculpins found in the cold waters of the Northern Hemisphere.
Researchers studied the physical adaptations unique to fish in the family Cottidae.
Unlike other groups, the family Cottidae exhibits a remarkable diversity in body shape and habitat preference.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sculpin - any fish in the family Cottidae

Etymology

From New Latin Cottidae, based on the type genus Cottus.
