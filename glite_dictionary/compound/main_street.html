Main Street

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪn strit / (AmE), / ˈmeɪn striːt / (BrE)
used to refer collectively to the people or character of small-town America, especially when considered as conventional, parochial, or materialistic.
the small-town American public or values

Examples

Politicians often appeal to Main Street by promising lower taxes and more local jobs.
The film captures the spirit and struggles of Main Street America.
After years in the city, she returned to Main Street and found comfort in its familiarity.

Synset Examples

Main Street will never vote for a liberal politician

Related Terms

  • Synonym: small-town - characteristic of small towns; provincial or unsophisticated
  • Synonym: middle America - the ordinary American populace, especially the middle class or people of rural and suburban communities

Etymology

Popularized after Sinclair Lewis's 1920 novel 'Main Street,' depicting small-town American life.

2. NOUN

/ meɪn strit / (AmE), / meɪn striːt / (BrE)
The principal street of a town or city, typically where most shops, restaurants, and businesses are located.
principal street in a town or city

Examples

Many important events take place on Main Street during the holiday season.
The parade marched down Main Street, watched by hundreds of residents.
Most of the town’s banks and cafes are located along Main Street.

Synset Examples

You can find all the shops along Main Street.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: High Street - chief commercial street in a British town or city
