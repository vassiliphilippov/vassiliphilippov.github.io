family Taccaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli tæˈkeɪsiaˌi / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli tækˈeɪsiːˌiː / (BrE)
A small family of tropical herbaceous plants, including the genus Tacca, known for their unusual flowers and tuberous roots.
botanical family of tropical herbs

Examples

The family Taccaceae includes several species that are cultivated for their ornamental value.
Botanists study the unique floral structures characteristic of the family Taccaceae.
Many members of the family Taccaceae are native to Southeast Asian rainforests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Tacca - genus of plants within family Taccaceae

Etymology

From Latin 'Tacca' (the genus name, of Malayan origin) plus standard botanical family suffix '-aceae'.
