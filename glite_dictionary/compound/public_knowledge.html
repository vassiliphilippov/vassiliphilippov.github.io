public knowledge

1. NOUN

/ ˈpʌblɪk ˈnɑlɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈpʌblɪk ˈnɒlɪdʒ / (BrE)
information that is known or available to anyone; not secret or confidential
information known or available to all

Examples

His resignation became public knowledge within hours of the announcement.
The details of the case are now public knowledge thanks to media coverage.
She assumed the merger plans were public knowledge, but many employees were still unaware.
