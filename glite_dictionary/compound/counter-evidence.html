counter-evidence

1. NOUN

/ ˈkaʊntər ˌɛvədəns / (AmE), / ˈkaʊntə ˌɛvɪdəns / (BrE)
Evidence that tends to disprove or contradict a claim or hypothesis.
evidence that disproves a claim

Examples

The lawyer presented new counter-evidence that challenged the prosecution's case.
Despite the mounting counter-evidence, the theory remained widely accepted for years.
Researchers must consider all counter-evidence when evaluating their results.

Synset Examples

People persevere in asserting all sorts of things in the face of apparent counterevidence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: evidence - information that proves or supports a claim
  • Synonym: proof - evidence or argument establishing a fact or the truth
  • Antonym: supporting evidence - evidence that supports a claim

Etymology

Formed from 'counter-' (against) and 'evidence'.
