genus Cyamopsis

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs saɪəˈmɒpsɪs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs sɪəˈmɒpsɪs / (BrE)
A small genus of annual, usually hairy herbs native to tropical Africa and Arabia, belonging to the family Fabaceae.
a plant genus of hairy annual herbs

Examples

The genus Cyamopsis includes plants cultivated for their gum and forage value.
Researchers studied the drought resistance of species within the genus Cyamopsis.
The genus Cyamopsis is primarily distributed across regions of tropical Africa and Arabia.

Etymology

From Latin 'genus' (kind, type) and 'Cyamopsis', derived from Greek 'kyamos' (bean) and '-opsis' (appearance).
