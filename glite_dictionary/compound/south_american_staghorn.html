South American staghorn

1. NOUN

/ ˌsaʊθ əˈmɛrɪkən ˈstæɡˌhɔrn / (AmE), / ˌsaʊθ əˈmɛrɪkən ˈstæɡˌhɔːn / (BrE)
A species of staghorn fern native to Peru and Bolivia.
staghorn fern from Peru and Bolivia

Examples

The South American staghorn is prized by collectors for its unusual frond shape.
Botanists discovered a new habitat for the South American staghorn deep in the Bolivian rainforest.
Growing a South American staghorn requires mimicking its native humid conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: staghorn fern - any fern of the genus Platycerium, noted for antler-like fronds
