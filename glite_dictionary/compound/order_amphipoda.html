order Amphipoda

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdər ˌæmˈfɪpədə / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə ˌæmˈfɪpədə / (BrE)
a taxonomic order of small, laterally compressed crustaceans including sand-hoppers, skeleton shrimp, and whale lice
order of small crustaceans (Amphipoda)
medical

Examples

Marine biologists discovered several new species within the order Amphipoda during their expedition.
The order Amphipoda includes creatures commonly known as sand-hoppers.
Some members of the order Amphipoda have adapted to live in terrestrial environments.

Etymology

From Greek 'amphi-' (both) and 'pous, podos' (foot), referring to limbs of different types.
