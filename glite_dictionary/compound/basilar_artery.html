basilar artery

1. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪ.sɪ.lɚ ˈɑːr.t̬ɚ.i / (AmE), / ˈbæs.ɪ.lə ˈɑː.tər.i / (BrE)
an unpaired artery at the base of the brain that supplies blood to the pons, cerebellum, back part of the cerebrum, and inner ear
artery at the base of the brain
medical

Examples

A blood clot in the basilar artery can cause severe neurological symptoms.
The surgeon carefully examined the basilar artery during the operation.
Damage to the basilar artery may disrupt blood flow to crucial parts of the brain.

Etymology

From Latin 'basis' (base) and 'arteria' (artery).
