mineral vein

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪnərəl veɪn / (AmE), / ˈmɪnərəl veɪn / (BrE)
a layer or deposit of mineral ore found between layers of surrounding rock, often distinct in composition or color
layer of mineral ore in rock

Examples

The geologist discovered a rich mineral vein running through the mountain.
They mined gold from a mineral vein that was visible in the cave wall.
Exploration teams mapped several mineral veins to determine the best location for excavation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lode - a rich source of a mineral within a rock formation
