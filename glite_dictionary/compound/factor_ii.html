factor II

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæk.tɚ tu / (AmE), / ˈfæk.tə tuː / (BrE)
A protein in blood plasma that acts as the inactive precursor of thrombin, essential for blood clotting.
blood protein, precursor of thrombin
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that a low factor II level can lead to bleeding disorders.
Researchers studied how mutations in factor II impact blood clotting efficiency.
A deficiency in factor II is one of several rare blood coagulation disorders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prothrombin - another name for factor II, precursor to thrombin
