double-furrow plough

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʌb.əl ˈfɜr.oʊ plaʊ / (AmE), / ˈdʌb.əl ˈfʌr.əʊ plaʊ / (BrE)
A plough equipped with two moldboards, used for simultaneously turning over two furrows of soil.
plough with two moldboards

Examples

The farmer used a double-furrow plough to speed up cultivation of the field.
Many modern tractors are capable of pulling a double-furrow plough with ease.
Choosing a double-furrow plough can improve efficiency on medium-sized farms.

Synset Examples

Heavy volcanic loam soils, such as are found in New Zealand, require the use of four heavy draught horses to pull a double-furrow plough.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: single-furrow plough - a plough with only one moldboard
  • Degree Higher: multiple-furrow plough - a plough with more than two moldboards
