to take care

1. VERB

/ teɪk kɛr / (AmE), / teɪk keə / (BrE)
to be cautious, prudent, or attentive, especially to avoid danger or harm
be careful or cautious

Examples

Please take care when using sharp knives in the kitchen.
Hikers should take care not to disturb the wildlife in the park.
If you take care, you'll avoid slipping on the icy steps.

Synset Examples

Take care when you cross the street!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to be careful - to act with attention to avoid danger or mistakes
  • Antonym: be reckless - to act without thinking about danger or consequences
