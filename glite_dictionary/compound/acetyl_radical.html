acetyl radical

1. NOUN

/ əˈsiːtɪl ˈrædɪkəl / (AmE), / əˈsiːtɪl ˈrædɪkəl / (BrE)
the univalent group derived from acetic acid, with the chemical structure CH₃CO−, found in many organic compounds.
chemical group, CH₃CO−

Examples

The acetyl radical plays a key role in many biochemical processes.
Scientists observed the transfer of the acetyl radical during the reaction.
An acetyl radical can attach to proteins, altering their function.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acetyl group - the same univalent group, CH₃CO−, derived from acetic acid

Etymology

From 'acetyl' (from Latin 'acetum' meaning vinegar) + 'radical'.
