prairie willow

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɛri ˈwɪloʊ / (AmE), / ˈpreəri ˈwɪləʊ / (BrE)
a slender, shrubby willow (Salix humilis) found in dry areas of North America
a shrubby North American willow

Examples

The prairie willow is well adapted to dry, sandy soils in the Midwest.
In spring, the prairie willow produces slender catkins that attract many pollinators.
We planted several prairie willow bushes to help restore the native landscape.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: willow - any tree or shrub of the genus Salix, with narrow leaves and catkins
