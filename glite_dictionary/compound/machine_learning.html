machine learning

1. NOUN

/ məˈʃin ˈlɜrnɪŋ / (AmE), / məˈʃiːn ˈlɜːnɪŋ / (BrE)
A branch of computer science that focuses on developing algorithms which enable computers to learn from and make predictions or decisions based on data.
computer science field for learning from data

Examples

Machine learning has transformed industries such as healthcare, finance, and retail by enabling powerful predictive analytics.
Many modern applications, like voice assistants, rely on machine learning to improve their accuracy over time.
She decided to take an online course in machine learning to enhance her data science skills.

Synset Examples

As a scientific endeavour, machine learning grew out of the quest for artificial intelligence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: artificial intelligence - the broader field of creating systems that exhibit intelligent behavior

Etymology

Formed from 'machine' (mechanical device) and 'learning' (acquiring knowledge); term popularized in computer science in the mid-20th century.
