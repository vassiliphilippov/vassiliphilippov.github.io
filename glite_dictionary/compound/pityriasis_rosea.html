pityriasis rosea

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɪtɪˈraɪəsɪs roʊˈziə / (AmE), / ˌpɪtɪˈraɪəsɪs rəʊˈziːə / (BrE)
a common skin condition characterized by an itchy, scaly rash that appears on the trunk and limbs
itchy rash on the trunk and limbs
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed her with pityriasis rosea after noticing the distinctive rash on her back.
Most cases of pityriasis rosea resolve on their own within six to eight weeks.
He was prescribed a cream to relieve the itching caused by pityriasis rosea.

Etymology

From Greek 'pityron' (bran) + Latin 'roseus' (rose-colored), describing the appearance of the rash.
