minimum wage

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪnɪməm weɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈmɪnɪməm weɪdʒ / (BrE)
The lowest amount of money that employers are legally or contractually allowed to pay their workers, typically set by law.
lowest legal wage for workers

Examples

The government raised the minimum wage to help low-income workers.
Many teenagers earn their first paychecks at minimum wage jobs.
Some argue that increasing the minimum wage could affect small businesses.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: living wage - a wage high enough to maintain a normal standard of living

Etymology

From Latin "minimum" (smallest) and English "wage" (from Old French "gage," a pledge or payment).
