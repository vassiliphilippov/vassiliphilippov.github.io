genus Equetus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒinəs ɪˈkwiːtəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ɪˈkwiːtəs / (BrE)
A genus of marine drumfish found in tropical and subtropical waters, noted for their distinctive body patterns.
a genus of drumfish

Examples

The genus Equetus includes species commonly known as high-hats and jackknife fish.
Marine biologists recently discovered a new member of the genus Equetus in the Caribbean.
Distinctive black-and-white stripes are a key feature of fish in the genus Equetus.

Etymology

From Latin 'genus' (kind, type) and 'Equetus', the New Latin name assigned to this genus.
