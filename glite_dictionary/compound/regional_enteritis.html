regional enteritis

1. NOUN

/ ˈriː.dʒə.nəl ˌɛn.təˈraɪ.tɪs / (AmE), / ˈriː.dʒən.əl ˌɛn.təˈraɪ.tɪs / (BrE)
a chronic and progressive inflammation of the ileum, causing frequent diarrhea, abdominal pain, nausea, fever, and weight loss; also known as Crohn's disease
chronic inflammation of the ileum (Crohn's disease)
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with regional enteritis after months of unexplained abdominal discomfort and weight loss.
Researchers continue to study the causes and effective treatments for regional enteritis.
Chronic diarrhea is a common symptom experienced by those suffering from regional enteritis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Crohn's disease - chronic inflammatory bowel disease affecting the gastrointestinal tract

Etymology

From 'region' (area) and 'enteritis' (inflammation of the intestine); medical term for Crohn's disease.
