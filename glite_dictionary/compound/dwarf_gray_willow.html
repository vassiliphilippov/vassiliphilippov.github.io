dwarf gray willow

1. NOUN

/ dwɔrf ɡreɪ ˈwɪloʊ / (AmE), / dwɔːf ɡreɪ ˈwɪləʊ / (BrE)
A willow shrub native to the eastern United States, found in dry places, with long narrow leaves that are gray and hairy beneath.
a North American willow shrub with grayish underside leaves

Examples

The dwarf gray willow thrives in the sandy soils of the eastern United States.
Botanists studied the unique leaf structure of the dwarf gray willow to better understand its adaptation to dry habitats.
You can identify the dwarf gray willow by its long, narrow leaves that are grayish and hairy underneath.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: willow - a tree or shrub of the genus Salix with narrow leaves and flexible branches
