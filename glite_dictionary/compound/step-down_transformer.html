step-down transformer

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɛp ˌdaʊn trænˈsfɔrmər / (AmE), / ˈstɛp ˌdaʊn trænzˈfɔːmə / (BrE)
a transformer that decreases the voltage of an alternating current to a lower value
a transformer that reduces voltage

Examples

The technician replaced the faulty step-down transformer to ensure the correct voltage reached the equipment.
Many household devices rely on a step-down transformer to operate safely at lower voltages.
The power supply includes a step-down transformer to convert high transmission voltage to a usable level.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: step-up transformer - a transformer that increases voltage
  • Synonym: transformer - an electrical device that changes the voltage of an alternating current
