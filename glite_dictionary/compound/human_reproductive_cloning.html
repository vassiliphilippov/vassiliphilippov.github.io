human reproductive cloning

1. NOUN

/ ˈhjuːmən ˌriːprəˈdʌktɪv ˈkloʊnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhjuːmən ˌriːprəˈdʌktɪv ˈkləʊnɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of creating a genetically identical copy of a human being through reproductive cloning methods, regarded as ethically controversial
creating a genetic copy of a human
medical

Examples

There are international debates surrounding the use of human reproductive cloning for ethical and safety reasons.
Many scientists argue that human reproductive cloning could pose unforeseen risks to both the individual and society.
Laws banning human reproductive cloning have been enacted in numerous countries.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: therapeutic cloning - cloning of cells or tissues for medical treatment rather than to create a whole organism

Etymology

From 'human' (relating to people), Latin *reproductivus* (reproductive), and 'cloning' from Greek *klōn* (twig, slip).
