still life

1. NOUN

/ stɪl laɪf / (AmE), / stɪl laɪf / (BrE)
a painting or drawing of an arrangement of inanimate objects, typically fruit, flowers, or everyday items
painting of inanimate objects

Examples

The artist specialized in still life paintings with vibrant flowers and glassware.
She admired a still life featuring ripe apples and a silver bowl.
Museums often display still life works from different periods of art history.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: portrait - a painting, drawing, or photograph of a person
  • Antonym: landscape - a painting depicting natural scenery

Etymology

From Dutch 'stilleven', literally 'still life' (stille 'motionless' + leven 'life').
