yellow-bellied sapsucker

1. NOUN

/ ˈjɛloʊ ˈbɛlid ˈsæpˌsʌkər / (AmE), / ˈjɛləʊ ˈbɛlɪd ˈsæpˌsʌkə / (BrE)
an eastern North American woodpecker (Sphyrapicus varius) with a pale yellow abdomen, known for drilling holes in trees to feed on sap and insects
North American woodpecker with a yellow belly

Examples

The yellow-bellied sapsucker drills neat rows of holes in birch trees to feed on sap and insects.
We spotted a yellow-bellied sapsucker during our birdwatching trip in upstate New York.
Many gardeners notice the distinctive markings left by a yellow-bellied sapsucker on their fruit trees.
