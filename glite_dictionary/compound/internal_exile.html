internal exile

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtɝː.nəl ˈɛɡ.zaɪl / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɜː.nəl ˈɛɡ.zaɪl / (BrE)
a state of being isolated, excluded, or kept apart within one's own country, especially as a result of one’s own choice or due to external pressures
isolation within one's own country

Examples

After the government crackdown, many activists lived in internal exile to avoid persecution.
She endured years of internal exile after being ostracized by her community.
His controversial views left him in a sort of internal exile within academic circles.

Synset Examples

Living in internal exile, he found solace in writing.
The artist chose internal exile to reflect on her work.
He felt like he was living in internal exile after the scandal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exile - the state of being barred from one's native country
