witch-hunt

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪtʃˌhʌnt / (AmE), / ˈwɪtʃˌhʌnt / (BrE)
An attempt to find and punish people whose opinions or actions are regarded as dangerous or unacceptable, often without fair evidence.
search for and persecution of dissenters

Examples

The government crackdown was described as a witch-hunt against political opponents.
Many artists were blacklisted during the witch-hunt for suspected communist sympathies.
Accusations with little evidence can quickly turn into a damaging witch-hunt.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: persecution - hostile treatment, especially because of beliefs
  • Synonym: inquisition - rigorous or severe questioning, especially historically of heretics

Etymology

Originally referring to the historical persecution of people accused of witchcraft in England and America.
