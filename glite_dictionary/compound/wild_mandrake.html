wild mandrake

1. NOUN

/ waɪld ˈmænˌdreɪk / (AmE), / waɪld ˈmænˌdreɪk / (BrE)
A North American herb with a poisonous rootstock and edible but mildly flavored fruit, also known as mayapple.
North American plant with poisonous rootstock and edible fruit

Examples

The poisonous root of the wild mandrake should never be eaten raw.
In spring, the wild mandrake produces a delicate white flower under its large leaves.
Although the fruit of the wild mandrake is edible when ripe, its taste is rather bland.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mayapple - another common name for the wild mandrake
