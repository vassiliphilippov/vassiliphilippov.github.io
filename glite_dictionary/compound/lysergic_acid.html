lysergic acid

1. NOUN

/ laɪˈsɜrdʒɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / laɪˈsɜːdʒɪk ˈasɪd / (BrE)
a crystalline organic acid derived from ergot alkaloids, used chiefly in medical research and as a precursor to certain hallucinogenic drugs
a crystalline acid derived from ergot alkaloids
medical

Examples

Researchers studied the properties of lysergic acid in the laboratory.
Lysergic acid is an important starting material for the synthesis of LSD.
The extraction of lysergic acid from ergot fungi was a major breakthrough in pharmacology.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: LSD - a powerful hallucinogenic drug derived from lysergic acid

Etymology

From 'lysergic' (formed from 'lyserg', relating to ergot, and the suffix '-ic'), and 'acid'; 'lyserg' from German 'Lysergsäure'.
