Lake Saint Clair

1. PROPN

/ leɪk ˌseɪnt ˈklɛr / (AmE), / leɪk ˌseɪnt ˈkleə / (BrE)
a lake situated between Ontario, Canada, and Michigan, USA, connecting Lake Huron with Lake Erie via the St. Clair and Detroit rivers
lake between Ontario and Michigan

Examples

Many boaters enjoy fishing on Lake Saint Clair during the summer season.
The Detroit River flows from Lake Saint Clair into Lake Erie.
Lake Saint Clair is an important stopover for migrating birds.

Etymology

Named after Saint Clare of Assisi; discovered on her feast day by French explorers.
