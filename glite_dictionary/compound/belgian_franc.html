Belgian franc

1. NOUN

/ bɛl.dʒən ˈfræŋk / (AmE), / bɛl.dʒən ˈfræŋk / (BrE)
the basic unit of currency in Belgium until it was replaced by the euro in 2002
former currency unit of Belgium

Examples

Before the euro, the price of bread in Brussels was marked in Belgian francs.
She exchanged her old Belgian francs for euros before the deadline.
Collectors still search for rare Belgian franc coins at markets across Europe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: franc - the main unit of currency in several countries, including France (historically) and Switzerland (currently)

Etymology

From French 'franc', meaning 'frank' or 'free'; initially adopted as the name of the currency in the 19th century.
