ear-shell

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪrˌʃɛl / (AmE), / ˈɪəˌʃɛl / (BrE)
A large edible marine mollusk of the genus Haliotis, with an ear-shaped, pearly inner shell; also known as abalone.
large edible marine mollusk (abalone)

Examples

The diver found several ear-shells attached to the rocks near the shore.
Dishes featuring ear-shell are considered a delicacy in many coastal regions.
She collected the mother-of-pearl from an ear-shell left on the beach.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abalone - an edible sea mollusk with an ear-shaped shell and pearly interior
