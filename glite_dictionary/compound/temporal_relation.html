temporal relation

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛmpərəl rɪˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈtɛmpərəl rɪˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
a relationship or connection between events, states, or entities that involves or is determined by the timing or sequence in which they occur
relation involving time or sequence

Examples

The historian studied the temporal relation between the invention of the printing press and social change.
Understanding the temporal relation of events is crucial in establishing cause and effect.
The software models the temporal relation between user actions and system responses.

Etymology

From Latin 'temporalis' (of time) and 'relation' from Latin 'relatio' (a bringing back, relation).
