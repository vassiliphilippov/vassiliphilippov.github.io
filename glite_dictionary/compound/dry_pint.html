dry pint

1. NOUN

/ draɪ paɪnt / (AmE), / draɪ paɪnt / (BrE)
A unit of dry measurement in the United States, equal to 0.5 dry quart or approximately 33.6 cubic inches (about 550 mL).
US unit of dry measurement
american

Examples

The recipe called for a dry pint of raspberries, so I measured them carefully.
A dry pint is different from a liquid pint in the United States measurement system.
She bought a dry pint of blueberries at the farmers market.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: liquid pint - US/UK unit for measuring liquid volume
  • Degree Higher: quart - Unit of volume equal to two pints

Etymology

From the distinction in English measurement systems between liquid and dry volume; 'pint' from Old French *pinte* and Vulgar Latin *pincta*.
