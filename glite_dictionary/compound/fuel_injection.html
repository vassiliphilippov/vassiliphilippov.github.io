fuel injection

1. NOUN

/ ˈfjuːəl ɪnˈdʒɛkʃən / (AmE), / ˈfjʊəl ɪnˈdʒɛkʃən / (BrE)
a mechanical system that delivers atomized fuel directly into the cylinders of an internal-combustion engine, eliminating the need for a carburetor
system injecting fuel into engine cylinders

Examples

Modern cars typically use fuel injection for better efficiency and emissions control.
The mechanic explained that a faulty fuel injection system could cause engine misfires.
Switching from a carburetor to fuel injection improved the vehicle's overall performance.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: carburetor - device for mixing air with fuel in an engine
