giant sequoia

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒaɪənt sɪˈkwɔɪ.ə / (AmE), / ˌdʒaɪənt sɪˈkwɔɪ.ə / (BrE)
a very large coniferous evergreen tree (Sequoiadendron giganteum) native to the western slopes of the Sierra Nevada in California, known as the largest living organism by volume
very large evergreen tree native to California

Examples

We saw a giant sequoia towering above the other trees in the forest.
The giant sequoia is renowned for its immense girth and height.
Many tourists visit California each year just to see a giant sequoia in person.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: redwood - evergreen tree also known for immense size; closely related species
