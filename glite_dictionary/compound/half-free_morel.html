half-free morel

1. NOUN

/ hæf-friː məˈrɛl / (AmE), / hɑːf-friː məˈrɛl / (BrE)
a type of morel mushroom with its ridged and pitted cap attached to the stem for about half its length
species of edible morel mushroom

Examples

We found several half-free morels growing near the edge of the forest after the spring rains.
The chef prepared a delicate dish using half-free morel mushrooms harvested that morning.
Identifying a half-free morel requires examining how much of the cap is attached to the stem.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: morel - an edible mushroom with a distinctive pitted, honeycomb-like cap
