genus Tylenchus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs taɪˈlɛŋ.kəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs tɪˈlɛŋ.kəs / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Tylenchidae, comprising certain plant-parasitic nematode worms.
type genus of Tylenchidae (nematode worms)

Examples

The genus Tylenchus includes several nematodes that are significant plant pests.
Researchers studied the life cycle of species within the genus Tylenchus.
Damage to crops was attributed to nematodes of the genus Tylenchus.

Etymology

Genus from Latin 'genus' (race, kind); Tylenchus from Greek 'tylē' (knob, swelling) + 'enchys' (spear).
