birch leaf miner

1. NOUN

/ bɝtʃ lif ˈmaɪnɚ / (AmE), / bɜːtʃ liːf ˈmaɪnə / (BrE)
a small black sawfly, originally from Europe and now found in eastern North America, whose larvae mine and damage birch leaves
sawfly pest of birch trees

Examples

The birch leaf miner has caused severe defoliation in local birch trees this season.
Gardeners are advised to monitor their trees for signs of birch leaf miner infestation.
Most pesticides are ineffective once the birch leaf miner larvae are inside the leaves.
