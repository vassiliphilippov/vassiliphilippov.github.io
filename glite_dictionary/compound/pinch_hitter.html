pinch hitter

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪntʃ ˌhɪtər / (AmE), / ˈpɪntʃ ˌhɪtə / (BrE)
A baseball player who is sent in to bat in place of another player, usually at a critical moment.
substitute batter in baseball
american

Examples

The coach chose a pinch hitter in the ninth inning to try to turn the game around.
She served as a pinch hitter when the team's star was unable to play.
His success as a pinch hitter earned him a reputation for staying calm under pressure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: substitute - a player who replaces another

Etymology

From 'pinch' (meaning a critical situation) and 'hitter' (batter); first attested in US baseball in the early 20th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɪntʃ ˌhɪtər / (AmE), / ˈpɪntʃ ˌhɪtə / (BrE)
(in baseball) a substitute batter; by extension, someone who temporarily takes another person's place, especially in a job or role.
substitute batter or temporary replacement
american

Examples

The team brought in a pinch hitter in the bottom of the ninth inning.
When Sarah was sick, Tom acted as a pinch hitter and led the meeting.
She is known as an excellent pinch hitter who can fill any role at short notice.

Synset Examples

He was called in as a pinch hitter for the game.
The manager used a pinch hitter in the last inning.
Being a pinch hitter requires quick thinking and skill.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: substitute - a person or thing acting in place of another

Etymology

Originally American baseball slang, from 'pinch' (difficult situation) and 'hitter' (batter).
