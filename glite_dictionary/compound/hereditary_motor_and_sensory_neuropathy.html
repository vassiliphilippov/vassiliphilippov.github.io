hereditary motor and sensory neuropathy

1. NOUN

/ həˈrɛdəˌtɛri ˈmoʊtər ənd ˈsɛnˌsɔri n(j)uˈrɑpəθi / (AmE), / hɛˈrɛdɪtəri ˈməʊtə ənd ˈsɛnsəri n(j)ʊəˈrɒpəθi / (BrE)
a genetic disorder of the nervous system, beginning between childhood and young adulthood, that causes progressive weakness and atrophy of the muscles in the hands and lower legs, but typically allows for a normal life span.
genetic nervous system disorder causing muscle weakness
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed her with hereditary motor and sensory neuropathy after she showed signs of progressive muscle weakness.
Hereditary motor and sensory neuropathy can present in families with a history of neurological disorders.
Patients with hereditary motor and sensory neuropathy often lead active lives despite muscle atrophy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Charcot-Marie-Tooth disease - a common hereditary motor and sensory neuropathy

Etymology

From Greek *neuron* (nerve), *-pathy* (disease), and Latin *hereditarius* (hereditary).
