root canal

1. NOUN

/ ˈrut kəˌnæl / (AmE), / ˈruːt kəˌnæl / (BrE)
the channel within the root of a tooth through which nerves and blood vessels pass into the pulp cavity
channel in the root of a tooth

Examples

The dentist explained that the infection had reached the root canal and required immediate attention.
During the procedure, the doctor carefully cleaned out the root canal to remove all bacteria.
A damaged root canal can lead to severe tooth pain if left untreated.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pulp cavity - the central part of a tooth containing the dental pulp
