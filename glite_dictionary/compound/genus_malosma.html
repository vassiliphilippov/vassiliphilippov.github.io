genus Malosma

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs məˈlɒzmə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs məˈlɒzmə / (BrE)
A genus of shrubs in the family Anacardiaceae, often considered a part of the genus Rhus and containing a single species.
shrub genus; sometimes merged with Rhus

Examples

The genus Malosma contains only a single recognized species.
Botanists sometimes classify genus Malosma as part of the larger genus Rhus.
The leaves of plants in genus Malosma have a distinctive fragrance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Rhus - A related genus of shrubs and small trees in Anacardiaceae, sometimes including Malosma.

Etymology

From Latin 'genus' (kind, type) and 'Malosma', a botanical name derived from Greek 'malos' (apple) and 'osme' (smell), possibly referring to the scent of the leaves.
