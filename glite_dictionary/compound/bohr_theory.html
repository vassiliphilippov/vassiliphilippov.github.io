Bohr theory

1. NOUN

/ bɔr ˈθiəri / (AmE), / bɔː ˈθɪəri / (BrE)
a theory of atomic structure proposing that electrons orbit the nucleus in certain allowed energy states and emit light when transitioning between these states
atomic structure theory by Niels Bohr

Examples

The Bohr theory introduced the idea of quantized electron orbits in atoms.
Many high school textbooks still mention Bohr theory to explain early atomic models.
Although surpassed by quantum mechanics, the Bohr theory remains an important historical milestone in physics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quantum theory - the theoretical basis of modern physics explaining nature at the smallest scales

Etymology

Named after Danish physicist Niels Bohr (1885–1962), who proposed the theory.
