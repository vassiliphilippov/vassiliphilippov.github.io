to cut swathe

1. VERB

/ kʌt sweɪð / (AmE), / kʌt swɑːð / (BrE)
to make a significant impact by removing, destroying, or affecting a large area or number of things, often rapidly or forcefully
cause widespread effect or removal

Examples

The hurricane cut swathes through the coastal village, leaving only ruins behind.
Budget cuts are expected to cut swathes from social programs this year.
The developer cut a swathe through the old neighborhood to make way for new apartments.

Synset Examples

The storm cut swathes through the forest, leaving destruction behind.
The new policy will cut swathes of unnecessary bureaucracy.
They cut swathes of land for the new development project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ravage - to cause severe and extensive damage to
  • Synonym: to destroy - to ruin completely or put an end to

Etymology

From 'cut' (to sever) and 'swathe,' originally meaning a row or strip of cut grass or crops, from Old English 'swæth' (track).
