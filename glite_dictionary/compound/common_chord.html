common chord

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmən tʃɔrd / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən tʃɔːd / (BrE)
A major or minor chord consisting of a root note, its third, and its fifth.
major or minor triad in music

Examples

The song begins with a simple common chord on the piano.
Learning to play a common chord is essential for beginner guitarists.
A common chord can be major or minor, depending on the intervals between its notes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: triad - a chord of three notes, consisting of a root, third, and fifth
