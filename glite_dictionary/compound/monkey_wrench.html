monkey wrench

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌŋki rɛntʃ / (AmE), / ˈmʌŋki rɛntʃ / (BrE)
A type of adjustable wrench with one fixed jaw and one movable jaw, used for gripping and turning objects.
adjustable wrench with one movable jaw

Examples

He grabbed a monkey wrench from the toolbox to fix the leaking pipe.
The mechanic adjusted the bolt using a large monkey wrench.
Can you hand me the monkey wrench so I can loosen this nut?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wrench - a tool used for gripping and turning objects
  • Synonym: spanner - British term for wrench

Etymology

Probably from the resemblance of the movable jaw to a monkey's face, plus 'wrench.'

2. NOUN

a tool with one fixed and one adjustable jaw, used for gripping and turning pipes or other objects
adjustable spanner with movable jaw

Examples

He grabbed a monkey wrench from the toolbox to fix the leaking pipe.
The mechanic used a monkey wrench to loosen the stubborn bolt.
A monkey wrench is an essential tool for many household repairs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adjustable spanner - a spanner with adjustable jaws, especially in British English

Etymology

Name origin uncertain; possibly from use by mechanics called 'monkeys' in early American slang.

3. NOUN

/ ˈmʌŋki ˌrɛntʃ / (AmE), / ˈmʌŋki ˌrɛntʃ / (BrE)
An unexpected problem or obstacle that interferes with a plan or process.
an unexpected obstacle or problem
informal

Examples

The resignation of our team leader threw a real monkey wrench into our project plans.
Just when we thought everything was set, a power outage put a monkey wrench in our schedule.
His unexpected visit was a monkey wrench in her carefully arranged afternoon.

Synset Examples

The sudden rain was a monkey wrench for the picnic.
She added a monkey wrench to their discussion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spanner in the works - an unexpected problem that delays or prevents a plan, chiefly British
  • Synonym: obstacle - something that blocks or stands in the way of progress
  • Antonym: solution - something that solves a problem or removes an obstacle

Etymology

Figurative use of 'monkey wrench', originally a tool, from the idea of something interfering or disrupting, as a wrench thrown into machinery.
