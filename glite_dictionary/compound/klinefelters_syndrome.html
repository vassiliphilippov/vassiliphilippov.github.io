Klinefelter's syndrome

1. NOUN

/ ˈklaɪnˌfɛltərz ˌsɪnˈdroʊm / (AmE), / ˈklaɪnfɛltəz ˌsɪndrəʊm / (BrE)
A genetic disorder in males characterized by an extra X chromosome (XXY), resulting in small testes, long legs, enlarged breasts, reduced sperm production, and sometimes learning difficulties.
genetic disorder in males with extra X chromosome
medical

Examples

Doctors diagnosed the teenager with Klinefelter's syndrome after noticing unusual physical development.
Men with Klinefelter's syndrome may have difficulty with fertility due to reduced sperm production.
Early identification of Klinefelter's syndrome can help manage symptoms and improve quality of life.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Turner syndrome - genetic disorder in females with only one X chromosome

Etymology

Named after American physician Harry F. Klinefelter, who first described the condition in 1942.
