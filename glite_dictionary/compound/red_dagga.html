red dagga

1. NOUN

/ rɛd ˈdæɡə / (AmE), / rɛd ˈdaɡə / (BrE)
A South African herb (Leonotis leonurus) that is relatively nontoxic and sometimes smoked as a tobacco substitute.
South African herb smoked like tobacco

Examples

Some South Africans use red dagga leaves as a traditional remedy.
The bright orange flowers make red dagga popular in ornamental gardens.
He tried red dagga as a herbal alternative to tobacco.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wild dagga - Another name for Leonotis leonurus, a South African herb smoked like tobacco

Etymology

Dagga is from Afrikaans, possibly from Khoikhoi *dacha* (‘tobacco’); 'red' refers to the color of the plant's flowers.
