myrtle oak

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɜr.tl oʊk / (AmE), / ˈmɜː.tl əʊk / (BrE)
A small evergreen shrub or tree native to the southeastern United States, often forming dense thickets in sandy coastal areas.
evergreen shrub or tree of southeastern U.S.

Examples

The dense growth of myrtle oak provides excellent shelter for wildlife along the Florida coast.
We hiked through a thicket of myrtle oak on the sandy ridge.
Myrtle oak is well-adapted to the dry, nutrient-poor soils found in its native range.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oak - a type of tree, genus Quercus, known for its hard wood and lobed leaves
