red silver fir

1. NOUN

/ rɛd ˈsɪlvɚ fɚ / (AmE), / rɛd ˈsɪlvə fɜː / (BrE)
a medium to tall fir tree of western North America, with a conical crown and tiered branches; its leaves smell of orange when crushed
fir tree species native to western North America

Examples

The red silver fir is commonly found in the forests of western North America.
Botanists identified the tree as a red silver fir by its tiered branches and orange-scented leaves.
A mature red silver fir can reach impressive heights and has a striking conical shape.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fir - a type of evergreen coniferous tree
