post hoc ergo propter hoc

1. NOUN

/ poʊst hɑk ˌɛrɡoʊ ˌprɑptər ˈhɑk / (AmE), / pəʊst hɒk ˌɛːɡrəʊ ˌprɒptə ˈhɒk / (BrE)
The logical fallacy of assuming that because one event follows another, the first event caused the second.
fallacy: mistaking sequence for causation

Examples

The politician's argument was dismissed as post hoc ergo propter hoc because it wrongly linked two unrelated events.
Believing that wearing lucky socks made their team win is a classic example of post hoc ergo propter hoc.
It's important to avoid the post hoc ergo propter hoc fallacy when analyzing historical events.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: false cause - the logical fallacy of misattributing cause

Etymology

From Latin, meaning 'after this, therefore because of this.'
