killing zone

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɪlɪŋ zoʊn / (AmE), / ˈkɪlɪŋ zəʊn / (BrE)
an area, especially in a battlefield, where many people have been killed or are likely to be killed
area of heavy fatalities in battle

Examples

The soldiers avoided the killing zone to reduce casualties during the advance.
After the attack, the town square became a killing zone strewn with debris and bodies.
Helicopters circled above the killing zone, searching for any survivors among the fallen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: death zone - area where many fatalities occur, especially on mountains or in disasters
  • Antonym: safe zone - area free from danger or violence
