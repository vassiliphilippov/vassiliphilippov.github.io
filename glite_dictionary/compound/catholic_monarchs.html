Catholic Monarchs

1. PROPN

/ ˈkæθəlɪk ˈmɑːnɚks / (AmE), / ˈkæθəlɪk ˈmɒnəkz / (BrE)
the title given to Ferdinand V of Aragon and Isabella I of Castile, whose marriage united Spain and who jointly ruled as monarchs in the late 15th and early 16th centuries
Ferdinand and Isabella, joint monarchs of Spain

Examples

The Catholic Monarchs completed the Reconquista with the conquest of Granada in 1492.
Many historians credit the Catholic Monarchs with unifying Spain under one crown and religion.
Christopher Columbus received financial support from the Catholic Monarchs for his voyage to the Americas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ferdinand V - King of Aragon, husband of Isabella I
  • Synonym: Isabella I - Queen of Castile, wife of Ferdinand V

Etymology

Catholic from Greek *katholikos* (universal); here, referring to their support of Catholicism, a title conferred by the Pope.
