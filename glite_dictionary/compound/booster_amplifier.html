booster amplifier

1. NOUN

/ ˈbuːstər æmˌplɪfaɪər / (AmE), / ˈbuːstə æmˌplɪfaɪə / (BrE)
an electronic device that increases the strength of a transmitted signal, especially to compensate for signal loss over distance.
electronic device amplifying transmitted signals

Examples

The technician installed a booster amplifier to strengthen the weak TV signal in the building.
A booster amplifier is often used in long cable runs to ensure clear audio transmission.
By adding a booster amplifier, we managed to improve the range of the wireless system.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amplifier - an electronic device for increasing electrical signals
