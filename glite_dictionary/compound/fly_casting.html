fly casting

1. NOUN

/ flaɪ ˈkæstɪŋ / (AmE), / flaɪ ˈkɑːstɪŋ / (BrE)
The act of casting an artificial fly as a lure when fishing, typically using a special rod and line.
casting artificial flies when fishing

Examples

He spent the afternoon fly casting on the quiet river, hoping to catch a trout.
Her fly casting technique improved significantly after taking a few lessons from an expert.
Mastering fly casting requires patience and practice to control both the rod and the line.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fly fishing - fishing using artificial flies as bait
