Lower Tunguska

1. PROPN

/ ˌloʊər tʊŋˈɡuːskə / (AmE), / ˌləʊə tʊŋˈɡuːskə / (BrE)
a river in Siberia rising north of Lake Baikal and flowing north and west to join the Yenisei River
river in Siberia, Russia

Examples

The Lower Tunguska is known for its remote and largely uninhabited wilderness.
Scientists traveled along the Lower Tunguska to study the region's unique geology and ecology.
Many explorers have attempted to navigate the Lower Tunguska during Siberia's harsh winters.
