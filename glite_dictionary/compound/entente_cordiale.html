entente cordiale

1. NOUN

/ ɑnˌtɑnt kɔrˈdjɑl / (AmE), / ɒnˈtɒnt kɔːˈdiːɑːl / (BrE)
an agreement or friendly understanding between political powers, especially countries
friendly understanding between countries

Examples

After years of tension, the two countries signed an entente cordiale to improve their relations.
The entente cordiale between the nations has led to increased cooperation on trade and security.
Many historians regard the entente cordiale as a turning point in European diplomacy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alliance - a formal agreement or treaty between two or more nations

Etymology

From French 'entente cordiale', meaning 'friendly agreement'.

2. NOUN

/ ɑnˌtɑnt kɔrˈdjɑl / (AmE), / ɒ̃ˈtɒ̃t kɔːˈdjɑːl / (BrE)
An informal but friendly understanding or alliance between countries, especially for diplomatic cooperation.
diplomatic understanding between nations

Examples

The entente cordiale between the two countries led to a period of peaceful cooperation.
Diplomats referred to the treaty as an entente cordiale rather than a formal alliance.
Despite historical tensions, an entente cordiale was eventually reached.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alliance - a formal agreement between two or more countries to cooperate for specific purposes
  • Antonym: hostility - opposition or antagonism between nations

Etymology

From French entente cordiale (cordial understanding), first used to describe the 1904 agreement between Britain and France.
