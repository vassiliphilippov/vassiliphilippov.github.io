Hagia Sophia

1. PROPN

/ ˌhɑːɡiə soʊˈfiə / (AmE), / ˌhɑːɡiə səʊˈfiːə / (BrE)
a monumental building in Istanbul, originally built as a Christian cathedral in the 6th century, later converted to a mosque and then a museum
historic building in Istanbul, former cathedral and mosque

Examples

The Hagia Sophia attracts millions of visitors each year due to its architectural beauty and storied history.
When traveling to Istanbul, many tourists put the Hagia Sophia at the top of their itinerary.
The mosaics inside the Hagia Sophia are celebrated masterpieces of Byzantine art.

Etymology

From Greek *Hagia Sophia* meaning 'Holy Wisdom'.
