subphylum Urochordata

1. NOUN

/ sʌbˈfaɪləm ˌjʊroʊˈkɔrdətə / (AmE), / sʌbˈfʌɪləm ˌjʊərəʊˈkɔːdətə / (BrE)
A subphylum of marine animals within the phylum Chordata, commonly known as tunicates, characterized by a sac-like body and a notochord during their larval stage.
subphylum of marine animals (tunicates)

Examples

The subphylum Urochordata includes marine creatures commonly referred to as sea squirts.
Researchers studied the developmental stages of organisms within the subphylum Urochordata.
Despite their simple appearance, members of the subphylum Urochordata play an important role in marine ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tunicate - marine invertebrate animal belonging to subphylum Urochordata

Etymology

From Neo-Latin: 'subphylum' (subdivision of a phylum) + 'Urochordata', from Greek 'oura' (tail) and 'chordē' (cord, string), referring to the tail chord in larvae.
