firing pin

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪərɪŋ pɪn / (AmE), / ˈfaɪərɪŋ pɪn / (BrE)
A small rod or striker in a firearm that strikes the primer of a cartridge to ignite the charge.
part of a firearm that strikes the primer

Examples

The firing pin was damaged, preventing the rifle from discharging properly.
Always keep the firing pin and other small parts organized when cleaning your handgun.
The technician explained how the firing pin initiates the bullet's ignition process.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: striker - part of a firearm that delivers the blow to the primer
