benign prostatic hyperplasia

1. NOUN

/ bɪˈnaɪn proʊˈstætɪk haɪpərˈpleɪʒə / (AmE), / bɪˈnaɪn prɒˈstætɪk hʌɪpəˈpleɪʒə / (BrE)
a non-cancerous enlargement of the prostate gland, common in older men and often causing urinary problems
non-cancerous prostate enlargement in men
medical

Examples

Many older men experience benign prostatic hyperplasia, leading to frequent urination at night.
Treatment options for benign prostatic hyperplasia include medication, lifestyle changes, or surgery.
Doctors explained that his urinary symptoms were due to benign prostatic hyperplasia rather than prostate cancer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prostate - male gland that surrounds the neck of the bladder and urethra
  • Antonym: prostate cancer - malignant tumor of the prostate

Etymology

From Latin 'benignus' (kind), Greek 'prostatēs' (prostate), and 'hyperplasis' (over-formation).
