white walnut

1. NOUN

/ waɪt ˈwʌlˌnʌt / (AmE), / waɪt ˈwɒl.nʌt / (BrE)
A North American walnut tree (Juglans cinerea) with light-brown wood and edible nuts, and a source of a light-brown dye.
North American walnut tree with edible nuts

Examples

A mature white walnut can produce a plentiful crop of edible nuts each fall.
The wood of the white walnut is prized for its attractive color and workability.
She used the husks of white walnut to make a natural dye for her fabrics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: butternut - another name for the white walnut tree
