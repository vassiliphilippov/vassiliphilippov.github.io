snake venom

1. NOUN

/ sneɪk ˈvɛnəm / (AmE), / sneɪk ˈvɛnəm / (BrE)
the poisonous fluid secreted by certain snakes, used to immobilize prey or defend against predators
poisonous fluid produced by snakes

Examples

Some snakes inject snake venom when they bite their prey.
Scientists study snake venom to develop new medicines and antivenoms.
Accidental exposure to snake venom can cause severe medical emergencies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: venom - poisonous substance secreted by animals such as snakes and spiders
