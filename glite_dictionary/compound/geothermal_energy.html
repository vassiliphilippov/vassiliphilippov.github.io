geothermal energy

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒioʊˈθɝːməl ˈɛnɚdʒi / (AmE), / ˌdʒiːəʊˈθɜːməl ˈɛnədʒi / (BrE)
Energy produced by harnessing heat from within the Earth's interior, often used for electricity generation or heating.
energy from Earth's internal heat

Examples

The city invested in geothermal energy to reduce its reliance on fossil fuels.
Many homes in Iceland are heated using geothermal energy from underground hot springs.
Advances in technology have made geothermal energy a more viable source of renewable power.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: solar energy - energy derived from the sun's radiation
  • Antonym: fossil fuel - fuel formed from ancient organic matter

Etymology

From Greek 'geo-' meaning 'earth' and 'thermos' meaning 'heat'.
