Chao Phraya River

1. PROPN

/ tʃaʊ praɪˈjɑː ˈrɪvər / (AmE), / tʃaʊ prɑːˈjɑː ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
the principal river of Thailand, flowing south through Bangkok to the Gulf of Thailand
major river in Thailand

Examples

The Chao Phraya River is an essential waterway for transport and trade in central Thailand.
Many tourists enjoy taking boat cruises along the Chao Phraya River to see Bangkok's famous landmarks.
Flooding along the Chao Phraya River can have serious consequences for the city of Bangkok.

Etymology

From Thai 'Chao Phraya', meaning 'Grand Duke' or 'lord of the river'.
