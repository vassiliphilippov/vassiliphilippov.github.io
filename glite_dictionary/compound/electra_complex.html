Electra complex

1. NOUN

/ əˈlɛktrə kəmˈplɛks / (AmE), / ɪˈlɛktrə kəmˈplɛks / (BrE)
(in psychoanalysis) the unconscious sexual attraction of a daughter towards her father, often accompanied by rivalry with the mother.
psychoanalytic theory: daughter's attraction to father
medical

Examples

The therapist explained the daughter's behavior in terms of the Electra complex.
Some psychologists question the validity of the Electra complex in understanding family dynamics.
Her research focused on the manifestation of the Electra complex in modern literature.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Oedipus complex - in psychoanalysis: a boy's feelings of desire for his mother and rivalry with his father

Etymology

Named after Electra, a character in Greek mythology who sought revenge on her mother for her father's murder.
