fixed investment trust

1. NOUN

/ fɪkst ɪnˈvɛstmənt trʌst / (AmE), / fɪkst ɪnˈvɛstmənt trʌst / (BrE)
an investment trust that holds a portfolio of securities fixed at the time of its creation, without further buying or selling of assets thereafter
type of investment trust with fixed securities

Examples

Many conservative investors prefer a fixed investment trust because of its transparent structure and predictable holdings.
The portfolio of a fixed investment trust does not change after the initial selection of securities.
Unlike mutual funds, a fixed investment trust cannot actively trade its investments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: investment trust - a company that invests in shares of other companies and issues its own shares to investors
