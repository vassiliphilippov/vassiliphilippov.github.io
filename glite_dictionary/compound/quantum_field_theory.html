quantum field theory

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɑn.təm fiːld ˈθi.ə.ri / (AmE), / ˈkwɒn.təm fiːld ˈθɪə.ri / (BrE)
A branch of quantum physics that describes how fields, such as the electromagnetic field, interact with matter and fundamental particles, using quantum mechanics.
theory describing quantum fields and particle interactions

Examples

Quantum field theory forms the mathematical framework for understanding particle physics and forces.
She is taking a graduate course in quantum field theory this semester.
The discovery prompted significant advancements in quantum field theory during the twentieth century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quantum mechanics - the branch of physics that deals with the behavior of matter and light on the atomic and subatomic scale
  • Antonym: classical field theory - the study of how fields interact with matter in non-quantum (classical) settings

Etymology

Formed from 'quantum' (Latin 'quantus', how much) + 'field theory' (physics).
