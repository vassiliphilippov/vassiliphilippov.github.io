bristlecone fir

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrɪsəlˌkoʊn fɝ / (AmE), / ˈbrɪslˌkəʊn fɜː / (BrE)
a species of fir tree native to southwestern California, characterized by a pyramidal shape, spiny pointed leaves, and cones with long spines
a fir tree native to southwestern California

Examples

The bristlecone fir is well adapted to the rugged terrain of southwestern California.
Botanists studied the unique cones of the bristlecone fir during their fieldwork.
A mature bristlecone fir can live for several hundred years in its native habitat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fir - an evergreen coniferous tree with needlelike leaves
