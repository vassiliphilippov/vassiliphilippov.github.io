absolute encoder

1. NOUN

/ ˈæb.səˌluːt ɪnˈkoʊ.dɚ / (AmE), / ˈæb.səˌluːt ɪnˈkəʊ.də / (BrE)
a rotary encoder that retains precise position information even when power is lost, enabling accurate position recovery on restart.
rotary encoder retaining position after power loss

Examples

The machine uses an absolute encoder so it always knows its precise position after a power outage.
When upgrading the system, the engineer recommended replacing the incremental encoder with an absolute encoder.
An absolute encoder is essential in robotics for accurate position tracking and error recovery.

Synset Examples

The output of absolute encoders indicates the current position of the shaft, making them angle transducers.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: incremental encoder - a rotary encoder that only provides relative position changes and cannot remember its absolute position after power loss

Etymology

Formed from 'absolute' (complete, total) and 'encoder' (device that converts information to a coded format).
