rocket propellant

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɑːkɪt prəˈpɛlənt / (AmE), / ˈrɒkɪt prəˈpɛl(ə)nt / (BrE)
a material or substance, often explosive, used to provide thrust for a rocket
material used to propel rockets

Examples

The new launch vehicle uses a more efficient rocket propellant to increase its payload capacity.
Scientists are developing eco-friendly rocket propellants to reduce environmental impact during launches.
Handling rocket propellant requires strict safety precautions due to its explosive nature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: propellant - a substance that propels something, especially in engines or weaponry

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɑːkɪt prəˈpɛlənt / (AmE), / ˈrɒkɪt prəˈpɛl(ə)nt / (BrE)
an explosive or reactive substance used to propel a rocket by producing thrust
explosive used to propel a rocket

Examples

The type of rocket propellant chosen determines how efficiently a spacecraft can travel.
Solid rocket propellant is often used in booster rockets for its simplicity and power.
Scientists are developing greener forms of rocket propellant to reduce environmental impact.

Related Terms
