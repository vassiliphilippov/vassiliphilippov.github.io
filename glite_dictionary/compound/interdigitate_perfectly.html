to interdigitate perfectly

1. VERB

/ ˌɪntərˈdɪdʒɪˌteɪt ˈpɜrfɪktli / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈdɪdʒɪteɪt ˈpɜːfɪktli / (BrE)
to fit together seamlessly, as the fingers of two hands might; to merge or combine parts so that they mesh exactly without gaps
to fit together seamlessly

Examples

The gears interdigitate perfectly, making the machine run smoothly and silently.
The tiles interdigitate perfectly, creating a flawless surface without visible seams.
Their ideas interdigitate perfectly, leading to a well-integrated project.

Synset Examples

The puzzle pieces interdigitate perfectly to reveal the picture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mesh - to fit or work together smoothly
  • Synonym: interlock - to fit into each other firmly and closely

Etymology

From Latin 'interdigitatus', past participle of 'interdigitare' (to interlock the fingers), from 'inter-' (between) and 'digitus' (finger); 'perfectly' from Latin 'perfectus' (completed, excellent).
