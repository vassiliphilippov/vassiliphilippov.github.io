internal iliac vein

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtɜrnəl ˈɪliæk veɪn / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɜːnəl ˈɪliæk veɪn / (BrE)
a vein in the pelvic region that joins with the external iliac vein to form the common iliac vein
pelvic vein forming the common iliac vein
medical

Examples

The surgeon carefully located the internal iliac vein before proceeding with the operation.
Injury to the internal iliac vein can cause significant bleeding in pelvic trauma.
A thrombosis in the internal iliac vein is a rare but serious medical condition.

Etymology

From Latin 'iliacus' (pertaining to the flank or groin), and 'vena' (vein).
