Canadian French

1. NOUN

/ kəˈneɪdiən frɛntʃ / (AmE), / kəˈneɪdiən frɛntʃ / (BrE)
the variety of the French language spoken mainly in Quebec and other parts of Canada, characterized by distinctive pronunciation, vocabulary, and some grammatical differences from standard French
variety of French spoken in Canada

Examples

Many people in Quebec speak Canadian French as their first language.
The vocabulary of Canadian French includes words that are rarely used in France today.
He took a class to improve his accent in Canadian French.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Quebec French - French as spoken in the province of Quebec
  • Antonym: Metropolitan French - French as spoken in France (standard French)

2. NOUN

/ kəˈneɪdiən frɛntʃ / (AmE), / kəˈneɪdiən frɛn(t)ʃ / (BrE)
The variety of the French language spoken in Quebec and other parts of Canada, distinct in pronunciation, vocabulary, and grammar from the French spoken in France.
French as spoken in Canada, especially Quebec

Examples

Many immigrants to Quebec quickly adapt to Canadian French when speaking with locals.
Linguists study the differences between Canadian French and the French spoken in Paris.
My cousins grew up bilingual, speaking both English and Canadian French at home.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Quebec French - French as spoken specifically in Quebec
  • Antonym: Metropolitan French - Standard French as spoken in France
