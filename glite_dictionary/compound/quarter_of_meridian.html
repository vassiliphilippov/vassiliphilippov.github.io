quarter of meridian

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɔrt̬ɚ əv məˈrɪdiən / (AmE), / ˈkwɔːtə əv məˈrɪdiən / (BrE)
A historical Earth-based unit of length equal to 10,000 kilometers, originally defined as one fourth of the distance from the North Pole to the Equator along a meridian.
unit of length equal to 10,000 kilometers

Examples

The meter was originally defined as a fraction of the quarter of meridian traced on the Earth's surface.
Scientists once measured distances by referencing the quarter of meridian standard.
The quarter of meridian provided a basis for the metric system's definition of the meter.

Etymology

From the concept of dividing the length of a meridian (pole to equator) into four equal parts; related to metric system origins.
