Redwood National Park

1. PROPN

/ ˈrɛdˌwʊd ˈnæʃənəl pɑrk / (AmE), / ˈrɛdwʊd ˈnæʃənəl pɑːk / (BrE)
A national park in northern California known for its ancient coast redwood trees and scenic Pacific Ocean coastline.
U.S. national park with redwood forests

Examples

Many tourists visit Redwood National Park each year to marvel at the towering trees.
Camping in Redwood National Park offers a unique experience close to nature.
Redwood National Park is home to some of the oldest and tallest living trees in the world.
