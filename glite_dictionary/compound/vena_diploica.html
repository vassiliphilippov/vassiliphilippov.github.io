vena diploica

1. NOUN

/ ˈviːnə ˌdɪplɔɪˈiːkə / (AmE), / ˈviːnə ˌdɪplɔɪˈiːkə / (BrE)
A vein located within the spongy (diploë) part of the cranial bones, responsible for draining blood from this region.
vein in spongy part of cranial bones
medical

Examples

The vena diploica plays a key role in draining blood from the cranial bones.
Infections can sometimes spread through the vena diploica to other parts of the skull.
During the surgery, the vena diploica was carefully preserved to avoid complications.

Etymology

From Latin vena (vein) and diploica, from Greek diploë (double, referring to the spongy bone layer).
