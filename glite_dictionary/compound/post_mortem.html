post mortem

1. NOUN

/ poʊst ˈmɔːrtəm / (AmE), / pəʊst ˈmɔːtəm / (BrE)
a discussion or analysis conducted after an event, especially to assess what happened and how things could be improved
analysis after an event

Examples

After the project was completed, the team held a post mortem to discuss what went well and what could be improved.
The manager scheduled a post mortem meeting to analyze the reasons behind the unexpected delay.
Every major event in the company is followed by a post mortem to help guide future decisions.

Synset Examples

The post mortem revealed several areas for future enhancements.
The team conducted a post mortem to analyze the project's failure.
After the event, we held a post mortem to discuss improvements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: review - a critical assessment of something, especially a recent event or performance
  • Synonym: to debrief - to question someone or hold a discussion after an event to review actions taken

Etymology

From Latin post mortem (after death); extended figuratively to mean 'after the event'.
