hand axe

1. NOUN

/ hænd æks / (AmE), / hænd æks / (BrE)
A prehistoric stone tool with a sharp edge, designed to be held in the hand for chopping, cutting, or shaping.
prehistoric stone chopping tool

Examples

Archaeologists discovered a hand axe dating back more than 300,000 years at the excavation site.
The hand axe was an essential tool for early humans for cutting meat and wood.
She examined the chipped flint hand axe in the museum display.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: axe - a tool with a heavy bladed head mounted across a handle, used for chopping
