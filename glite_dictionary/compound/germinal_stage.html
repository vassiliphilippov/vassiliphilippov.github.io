germinal stage

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɝː.mɪ.nəl steɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈdʒɜː.mɪ.nəl steɪdʒ / (BrE)
the initial phase of development, especially the earliest stage in the formation of an organism or project
the initial phase of development

Examples

The research was still in its germinal stage when funding was approved.
Many creative ideas emerge during the germinal stage of planning.
In biology, the germinal stage occurs within the first two weeks after fertilization.

Synset Examples

In the germinal stage, the project showed great potential.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: embryonic stage - the stage of development immediately following the germinal stage in embryology

Etymology

From Latin 'germen' (sprout, bud, embryo) + English 'stage'.
