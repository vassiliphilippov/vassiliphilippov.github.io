triquetral bone

1. NOUN

/ traɪˈkwɛtrəl boʊn / (AmE), / traɪˈkwiːtrəl bəʊn / (BrE)
a small wrist bone on the ulnar side of the carpus that articulates with the pisiform, hamate, and lunate bones
a bone in the wrist (carpus)
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that a fracture of the triquetral bone is relatively rare.
An X-ray showed damage to the triquetral bone after the patient fell on her hand.
The triquetral bone is found next to the lunate bone in the wrist.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carpal bone - any of the eight small bones of the wrist

Etymology

From Latin 'triquetrus' meaning 'three-cornered' or 'triangular'.
