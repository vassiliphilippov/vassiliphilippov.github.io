balancing act

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæl.ənsɪŋ ˌækt / (AmE), / ˈbæl.ənsɪŋ ˌækt / (BrE)
A situation in which a person must carefully manage or deal with different requirements, responsibilities, or demands to achieve success or avoid problems.
situation requiring careful management of demands

Examples

Managing study, work, and family is a real balancing act for many people.
Diplomats often face a balancing act between honesty and tact.
Running your own business can be a daily balancing act of priorities.

Synset Examples

Parenting is often a delicate balancing act.
Her job requires a constant balancing act between clients.
Finding time for hobbies is a tricky balancing act.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tightrope walk - situation requiring careful balance or attention to avoid failure
