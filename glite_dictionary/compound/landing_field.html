landing field

1. NOUN

/ ˈlændɪŋ fiːld / (AmE), / ˈlændɪŋ fiːld / (BrE)
an area of land prepared for aircraft to take off and land, typically smaller or less developed than an airport
area for aircraft to take off/land

Examples

The small town had a grassy landing field used by local pilots and cropdusters.
During the emergency, the pilot searched desperately for a nearby landing field.
Many early airmail routes depended on remote landing fields rather than large airports.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: airstrip - a strip of land used for the takeoff and landing of aircraft, typically with minimal facilities
  • Degree Higher: airport - a facility with runways and buildings for the takeoff, landing, and maintenance of aircraft
