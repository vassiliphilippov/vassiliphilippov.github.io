trap line

1. NOUN

/ træp laɪn / (AmE), / træp laɪn / (BrE)
A series or route of animal traps set out, often checked regularly by trappers or hunters.
a series or route of animal traps

Examples

The fur trapper checked each trap line every morning throughout the winter.
Setting a well-planned trap line can greatly improve your chances of a successful hunt.
Conservation officers monitored the trap line to ensure all local regulations were being followed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trap - a device for catching animals
