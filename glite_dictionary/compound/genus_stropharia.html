genus Stropharia

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs stroʊˈfɛr.i.ə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs strɒˈfɛə.ri.ə / (BrE)
A genus of gilled fungi with brown spores, closely related to Agaricus, and classified in the family Strophariaceae.
a genus of gilled fungi

Examples

Many species in the genus Stropharia are found in temperate forests worldwide.
The genus Stropharia includes both edible and toxic mushrooms.
Research on the genus Stropharia has contributed to our understanding of fungal taxonomy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Agaricus - a related genus of gilled mushrooms

Etymology

From modern Latin Stropharia, the name of the type genus.
