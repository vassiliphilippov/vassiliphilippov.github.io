Martin Luther

1. PROPN

/ ˈmɑrtən ˈluθər / (AmE), / ˈmɑːtɪn ˈluːθə / (BrE)
German theologian (1483–1546) who was a seminal figure in the Protestant Reformation, advocating justification by faith alone.
German theologian and Reformation leader

Examples

Martin Luther posted his Ninety-five Theses on the door of the church in Wittenberg.
Many historians consider Martin Luther a pivotal figure in European history.
Protestant churches trace their roots back to the reforms initiated by Martin Luther.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Reformation - the religious movement in 16th-century Europe led by figures like Martin Luther
