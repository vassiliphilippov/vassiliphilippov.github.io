prairie white-fringed orchis

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɛri ˈwaɪt ˌfrɪndʒd ˈɔrtʃɪs / (AmE), / ˈpreəri ˈwaɪt ˌfrɪndʒd ˈɔː.kɪs / (BrE)
a fragrant orchid native to boggy or wet lands of the north central United States, with racemes of creamy or greenish white flowers
a North American wetland orchid

Examples

The prairie white-fringed orchis blooms each summer in the wet meadows near the river.
Conservationists are working to protect the rare prairie white-fringed orchis from habitat loss.
We spotted a cluster of prairie white-fringed orchis during our hike in the wildlife reserve.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orchid - a plant of the family Orchidaceae, often with showy flowers
