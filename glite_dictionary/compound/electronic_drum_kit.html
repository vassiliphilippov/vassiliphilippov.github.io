electronic drum kit

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌlɛkˈtrɑnɪk drʌm kɪt / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛkˈtrɒnɪk drʌm kɪt / (BrE)
A set of electronic drums and related equipment, played by striking pads or sensors to produce a variety of drum and percussion sounds electronically.
set of electronic drums and controls

Examples

After switching to an electronic drum kit, she was able to practice quietly at night.
Many bands use an electronic drum kit on stage for its versatile sound options.
He programmed custom beats on his electronic drum kit for the recording session.

Synset Examples

The electronic drum (pad/triggering device) is usually sold as part of an electronic drum kit, consisting of a set of drum pads mounted on a stand or rack in a configuration similar to that of an acoustic drum kit layout, with rubberized (Roland, Yamaha, Alesis, for example) or specialized acoustic/electronic cymbals (e.g. Zildjian's ‘Gen 16’).

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drum kit - a set of acoustic drums and cymbals played together
