family Chaetodontidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli kiːˌtoʊdɒnˈtɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli kiːˌtɒdɒnˈtɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of brightly colored, tropical marine fishes commonly known as butterflyfishes.
family of butterflyfishes (marine fish)

Examples

The family Chaetodontidae includes more than 120 species of butterflyfishes found in tropical seas.
Coral reefs are home to many colorful members of the family Chaetodontidae.
Marine biologists study the family Chaetodontidae to understand reef fish diversity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: butterflyfish - a type of small, brightly colored tropical marine fish

Etymology

From Greek 'chaite' (hair, bristle) and 'odous' (tooth), referring to the bristle-like teeth; -idae is a standard zoological suffix for animal families.
