white stork

1. NOUN

/ waɪt stɔrk / (AmE), / waɪt stɔːk / (BrE)
a large white European stork with black wing feathers and a long red bill, commonly found in wetlands and fields
large white European stork with red bill

Examples

A pair of white storks built their nest atop the old church steeple.
Many believe that the arrival of a white stork brings good luck and fertility.
During migration season, thousands of white storks can be seen flying across southern Europe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stork - a large wading bird with long legs, neck, and bill
