learning curve

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɝːnɪŋ kɝːv / (AmE), / ˈlɜːnɪŋ kɜːv / (BrE)
a graph or representation showing how quickly or effectively someone becomes proficient at a task over time, often referring to the rate of improvement with experience
graph of progression in learning over time

Examples

The new software has a steep learning curve, but it's worth mastering.
They plotted the students' progress on a learning curve to measure improvement over time.
With experience, the learning curve for this equipment becomes much less daunting.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: steep learning curve - a situation in which learning something is difficult and requires a lot of effort in a short time

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɝː.nɪŋ kɝːv / (AmE), / ˈlɜː.nɪŋ kɜːv / (BrE)
the rate at which a person acquires new knowledge or skills, especially as represented graphically
rate of gaining new skills or knowledge

Examples

The learning curve for the new computer system was challenging for some employees.
She picked up the basics quickly, making her learning curve shorter than expected.
Every new job has a learning curve, but good training makes the process easier.

Synset Examples

The learning curve for the software was quite steep.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: steep learning curve - a situation where something is difficult to learn at first
