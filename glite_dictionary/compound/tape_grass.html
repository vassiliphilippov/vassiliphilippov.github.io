tape grass

1. NOUN

/ teɪp ɡræs / (AmE), / teɪp ɡrɑːs / (BrE)
a submerged aquatic plant with long, ribbon-like leaves, native to the Old World and Australia, often found in freshwater environments
submerged aquatic plant with ribbonlike leaves

Examples

The pond was filled with tape grass swaying gently beneath the surface.
Aquatic gardens often use tape grass because of its attractive, slender leaves.
Fishermen noticed large patches of tape grass growing along the riverbank.
