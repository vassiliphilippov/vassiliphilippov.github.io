landing craft

1. NOUN

/ ˈlændɪŋ kræft / (AmE), / ˈlændɪŋ krɑːft / (BrE)
a small naval vessel designed to transport troops and equipment from ships to shore, especially during amphibious operations
naval vessel for landing troops or equipment

Examples

The landing craft approached the beach under heavy enemy fire.
During World War II, hundreds of landing craft were used in the Normandy invasion.
The marines quickly disembarked from the landing craft and secured the shore.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amphibious vehicle - vehicle capable of operation on both land and water
