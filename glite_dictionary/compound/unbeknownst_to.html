unbeknownst to

1. ADP

/ ˌʌnbɪˈnoʊnst tu / (AmE), / ˌʌnbɪˈnəʊnst tuː / (BrE)
without the knowledge of (someone)
without (someone's) knowledge

Examples

Unbeknownst to his parents, Tim had already completed the assignment before dinner.
She finished all the preparations, unbeknownst to her colleagues.
Unbeknownst to the audience, the play had been rewritten the night before.

Synset Examples

Unbeknownst to her, he had planned a surprise party.
He kept it a secret, unbeknownst to everyone.
Unbeknownst to the team, their project was the best.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unknown to - not known by (someone)
  • Antonym: known to - known by (someone)

Etymology

Formed from 'unbeknown' (unknown), with the addition of '-st' as a dialectal or archaic ending; 'to' marks the person lacking knowledge.
