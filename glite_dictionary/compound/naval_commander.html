naval commander

1. NOUN

/ ˈneɪvəl kəˈmændər / (AmE), / ˈneɪvəl kəˈmɑːndə / (BrE)
A senior naval officer who is in command of a fleet or group of warships.
officer commanding a warship fleet

Examples

The naval commander issued orders for the fleet to change course immediately.
She studied the tactics of a famous naval commander from the early nineteenth century.
During the crisis, the naval commander coordinated the defense of the coastline.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: admiral - the highest-ranking naval officer, typically commanding a fleet
