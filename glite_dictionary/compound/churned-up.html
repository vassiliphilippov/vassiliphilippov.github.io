churned-up

1. ADJ

/ tʃɝnd ˈʌp / (AmE), / tʃɜːnd ˈʌp / (BrE)
(of a surface or substance) disturbed or agitated to produce a rough, unsettled, or messy state, often by vigorous movement.
disturbed or agitated, rough state

Examples

The heavy rain left the field churned-up and full of deep ruts.
Waves crashed against the rocks, sending churned-up foam high into the air.
Their argument left him feeling churned-up inside for hours.

Synset Examples

winds whipped the piled leaves into churning masses
a car stuck in the churned-up mud

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disturbed - having its normal arrangement or function disrupted
  • Antonym: calm - (of a surface or environment) not agitated, peaceful

Etymology

From the verb 'churn' (to agitate vigorously), combined with 'up.'
