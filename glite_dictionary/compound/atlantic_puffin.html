Atlantic puffin

1. NOUN

/ əˈtlæntɪk ˈpʌfɪn / (AmE), / ətˈlæntɪk ˈpʌfɪn / (BrE)
a seabird of the auk family (Fratercula arctica), found in the northern Atlantic, with a black back, white underparts, and a multicolored bill
seabird of the northern Atlantic

Examples

The Atlantic puffin is easily recognized by its brightly colored beak and distinctive facial markings.
Thousands of Atlantic puffins nest on the rocky cliffs each spring.
Conservationists are working to protect Atlantic puffin colonies from environmental threats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: puffin - a seabird with a colorful bill
