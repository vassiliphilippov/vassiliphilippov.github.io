Domesday Book

1. PROPN

/ ˈdoʊmz.deɪ ˌbʊk / (AmE), / ˈdəʊmz.deɪ ˌbʊk / (BrE)
the record of a survey of English land and property completed in 1086 on the orders of William the Conqueror, detailing ownership, value, and resources.
record of an English land and property survey, 1086
british

Examples

Historians often consult the Domesday Book for information about medieval England's land ownership.
The Domesday Book provides an invaluable record for understanding the economy of 11th-century England.
Several villages mentioned in the Domesday Book still exist today with the same names.

Etymology

From Middle English 'domesday' (day of judgment) + 'book'.
