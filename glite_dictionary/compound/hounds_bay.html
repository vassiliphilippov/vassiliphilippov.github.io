hound's bay

1. NOUN

/ haʊndz beɪ / (AmE), / haʊndz beɪ / (BrE)
the distinctive loud, deep barking sound made by hounds while hunting, especially when they have scented or cornered quarry
the sound made by hunting hounds

Examples

Deep in the woods, the hound's bay echoed as the hunt approached.
The sudden hound's bay sent chills down the spine of the fox.
Writers often use the hound's bay to evoke scenes of traditional hunting.

Synset Examples

They could hear the hounds bay in the distance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bark - the sharp sound made by a dog
  • Synonym: howl - a long, loud, mournful cry of a dog or wolf
