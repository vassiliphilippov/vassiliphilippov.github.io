Mrs. degree

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪsɪz dɪˈɡriː / (AmE), / ˈmɪsɪz dɪˈɡriː / (BrE)
a college degree pursued mainly for the purpose of finding a husband rather than for academic or career goals
degree sought mainly to find a husband
informal

Examples

She joked that she was only in college for her Mrs. degree.
Some people criticize the idea of pursuing an Mrs. degree instead of a career.
Her aunt warned her not to focus solely on getting an Mrs. degree while in university.

Synset Examples

She joked about getting her Mrs. degree before her bachelor's.
Many of her friends were focused on their Mrs. degree.
Her parents hoped she'd earn more than just a Mrs. degree.

Etymology

Formed from the honorific 'Mrs.' (for married women) and 'degree', referring humorously to marriage as an academic pursuit.
