musical perception

1. NOUN

/ ˈmjuːzɪkəl pərˈsɛpʃən / (AmE), / ˈmjuːzɪkəl pəˈsɛpʃən / (BrE)
the ability to recognize, interpret, and respond to musical sounds through hearing
perception of musical sounds

Examples

Children develop musical perception as they learn to distinguish between different melodies and rhythms.
Some people have a heightened musical perception that allows them to identify subtle changes in pitch.
Researchers are studying how the brain processes musical perception in trained musicians versus non-musicians.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: auditory perception - the ability to perceive and interpret sounds
