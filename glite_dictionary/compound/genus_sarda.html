genus Sarda

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˈsɑrdə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈsɑːdə / (BrE)
A genus of marine fish in the family Scombridae, known as bonitos, found in temperate and tropical oceans.
genus of fish known as bonitos

Examples

Several species in the genus Sarda are popular targets for commercial and recreational fishing.
The genus Sarda includes the Atlantic bonito and the Pacific bonito.
Marine biologists are studying the migration patterns of fish in the genus Sarda.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bonito - name for fish in the genus Sarda
  • Degree Higher: tuna - large marine fish of the same family as Sarda

Etymology

From Latin 'sarda' (a kind of fish), used in scientific taxonomy.
