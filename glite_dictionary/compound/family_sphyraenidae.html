family Sphyraenidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæmli sfɪəˈriːnɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli sfɪəˈriːnɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A taxonomic family of large, active fishes found in tropical and subtropical waters, comprising the barracudas.
taxonomic family of barracudas

Examples

The family Sphyraenidae includes all known species of barracudas.
Marine biologists have conducted genetic studies on the family Sphyraenidae to understand their evolutionary history.
Members of the family Sphyraenidae are characterized by their elongated bodies and sharp teeth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barracuda - a large predatory fish found in tropical and subtropical oceans

Etymology

From New Latin Sphyraenidae, based on the type genus Sphyraena.
