beurre noisette

1. NOUN

/ bɜːr nwɑːˈzɛt / (AmE), / bœr nwaˈzɛt / (BrE)
Butter cooked until it turns light brown and develops a nutty flavor, often used as a sauce or seasoning in cooking.
brown butter with a nutty flavor

Examples

The chef drizzled beurre noisette over the roasted vegetables for extra flavor.
To make classic French sauces, you often begin with beurre noisette.
A touch of lemon juice added to beurre noisette gives the sauce a refreshing finish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brown butter - Butter cooked until light brown and nutty

Etymology

From French, meaning 'hazelnut butter', referring to the color and aroma of the browned butter.
