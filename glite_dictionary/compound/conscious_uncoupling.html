conscious uncoupling

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑn.ʃəs ʌnˈkʌp.lɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.ʃəs ʌnˈkʌp.lɪŋ / (BrE)
The deliberate and amicable ending of a romantic relationship, with the intention of minimizing emotional distress for all involved.
amicable, deliberate end to a relationship

Examples

After years together, they opted for conscious uncoupling rather than a contentious divorce.
Many therapists now discuss conscious uncoupling as a healthier approach to ending relationships.
Their conscious uncoupling allowed them to remain friends for the sake of their children.

Synset Examples

Conscious uncoupling can lead to healthier post-relationship dynamics.
They announced their conscious uncoupling in a joint statement.
They chose conscious uncoupling to minimize emotional strain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: breakup - ending of a romantic relationship
  • Synonym: divorce - legal dissolution of a marriage

Etymology

Popularized in the early 21st century by psychotherapist Katherine Woodward Thomas and brought to mainstream attention by celebrities.
