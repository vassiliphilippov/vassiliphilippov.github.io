Lady with the Lamp

1. PROPN

/ ˈleɪdi wɪð ðə læmp / (AmE), / ˈleɪdi wɪð ðə læmp / (BrE)
an epithet for Florence Nightingale, an English nurse famous for her work during the Crimean War.
Florence Nightingale's famous nickname

Examples

During the Crimean War, Florence Nightingale became known as the Lady with the Lamp for her nightly rounds caring for soldiers.
Many people refer to Florence Nightingale as the Lady with the Lamp because of her compassion and dedication.
The legacy of the Lady with the Lamp continues to inspire generations of nurses around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Florence Nightingale - English nurse and social reformer (1820–1910)

Etymology

Refers to Florence Nightingale, who was often seen carrying a lamp while caring for wounded soldiers.
