family Sittidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli sɪˈtaɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli sɪˈtiːdiː / (BrE)
a family of small passerine birds known as nuthatches, characterized by their short tails and strong bills
family of birds: nuthatches

Examples

There are more than twenty species in the family Sittidae, found across the Northern Hemisphere.
Nuthatches, members of the family Sittidae, are known for climbing headfirst down trees.
The naturalist studied several birds from the family Sittidae in their woodland habitat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nuthatch - a small songbird with a compact body and a long, pointed bill

Etymology

From scientific Latin Sittidae, based on the type genus Sitta, from ancient Greek 'sittē' (nuthatch).
