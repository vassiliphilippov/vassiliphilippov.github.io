love feast

1. NOUN

/ lʌv fiːst / (AmE), / lʌv fiːst / (BrE)
a religious meal shared by members of a Christian community as a symbol of love and fellowship
religious communal meal (Christianity)

Examples

The congregation gathered for a love feast to strengthen their sense of unity.
Early Christians were known to participate in a love feast as part of their worship.
The church organized a love feast at Easter to promote fellowship among its members.

3. NOUN

/ ˈlʌv ˌfiːst / (AmE), / ˈlʌv ˌfiːst / (BrE)
A joyful celebration or gathering characterized by affection and fellowship, often including a shared meal among loved ones.
joyful meal shared with loved ones

Examples

The annual church love feast brought the whole community together for food and fellowship.
After the ceremony, friends and family gathered for a love feast to celebrate the couple's union.
Their reunion turned into a spontaneous love feast with laughter, music, and plenty to eat.

Synset Examples

The wedding was a love feast filled with laughter.
We hosted a love feast to celebrate our anniversary.
Every Christmas, we have a love feast with family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: banquet - formal meal for many people

Etymology

From 'love' + 'feast'; historically used in Christian communities for communal meals, particularly among early Methodists and Moravians.
