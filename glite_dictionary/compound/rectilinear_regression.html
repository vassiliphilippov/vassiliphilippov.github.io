rectilinear regression

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɛktəˈlɪniɚ rɪˈɡrɛʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛktɪˈlɪniə rɪˈɡrɛʃən / (BrE)
the statistical relationship between variables that can be expressed by a linear equation, such as y = ax + b
statistical relationship modelled by a straight line

Examples

The scientist used rectilinear regression to analyze the relationship between temperature and reaction rate.
In mathematics class, we learned how rectilinear regression can help predict outcomes based on linear data.
When the data points form a straight line, rectilinear regression provides an accurate model.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: linear regression - statistical method for modelling linear relationships between variables

Etymology

From Latin 'rectus' (straight) and 'linea' (line), combined with 'regression,' from Latin 'regressio' (return).
