horsehair lichen

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɔrsˌhɛr ˈlaɪkən / (AmE), / ˈhɔːshɛə ˈlaɪkən / (BrE)
Any of several lichens of the genus Alectoria, characterized by a thallus of long, hair-like filaments.
a lichen with hair-like filaments

Examples

The botanist collected a sample of horsehair lichen from the forest canopy.
Horsehair lichen can often be seen draping from old pine branches in cool, moist woodlands.
Researchers study the role of horsehair lichen in supporting wildlife habitats.

Etymology

Refers to its resemblance to horsehair and its classification as a lichen.
