pterygoid process

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛr.ɪˌɡɔɪd ˈprɑː.ses / (AmE), / ˈtɛr.ɪˌɡɔɪd ˈprəʊ.ses / (BrE)
either of two bony projections descending from the sphenoid bone in the skull, forming part of the nasal and oral cavities
bony projections of the sphenoid bone in the skull
medical

Examples

The lateral pterygoid process plays a crucial role in the attachment of muscles involved in chewing.
A fracture of the pterygoid process may affect jaw movement and require medical attention.
In anatomy class, we studied the structure of the pterygoid process and its relation to the sphenoid bone.

Etymology

From Greek 'pteryx' (wing) and Modern Latin 'processus pterygoideus', referring to the wing-like shape.
