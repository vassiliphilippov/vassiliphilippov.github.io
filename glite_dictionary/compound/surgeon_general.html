Surgeon General

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɜrdʒən ˈdʒɛnərəl / (AmE), / ˈsɜːdʒən ˈdʒɛnərəl / (BrE)
the head of the United States Public Health Service, responsible for providing the public with information about health and disease prevention
chief U.S. public health officer
american

Examples

The Surgeon General released a report highlighting the dangers of smoking.
During the pandemic, the Surgeon General addressed the nation on TV.
Several former Surgeons General have been outspoken advocates for public health.

Etymology

From 'surgeon' and 'general', patterned after military titles such as 'Attorney General'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɜrdʒən ˈdʒɛnərəl / (AmE), / ˈsɜːdʒən ˈdʒɛnərəl / (BrE)
the highest-ranking medical officer in an army, navy, or other armed forces, responsible for overseeing medical services and policies
chief medical officer in armed forces

Examples

The Surgeon General oversees the medical staff in all branches of the armed forces.
During the outbreak, the Surgeon General issued new health guidelines to the public.
She was appointed as the new Surgeon General of the Navy after years of distinguished service.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chief medical officer - senior doctor in charge of medical services in an organization

Etymology

From 'surgeon' (medical doctor) and 'general' (high-ranking military officer).
