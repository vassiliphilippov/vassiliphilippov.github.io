weighted arithmetic mean

1. NOUN

/ ˈweɪtɪd ərɪθˈmɛtɪk min / (AmE), / ˈweɪtɪd əˌrɪθˈmɛtɪk miːn / (BrE)
An average in which each value in the set has been given a specific weight according to its importance, and the mean is calculated by multiplying each value by its weight, summing the results, and dividing by the total of the weights.
mean where values are weighted unequally

Examples

To find the final grade, the instructor calculated the weighted arithmetic mean of all assignments.
In portfolio management, expected returns are often determined using a weighted arithmetic mean.
The scientist explained that a simple average is not always accurate, so a weighted arithmetic mean was applied.

Synset Examples

This simple mechanical model is now a familiar representation of the weighted arithmetic mean of a set of observations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mean - the average value of a set of numbers
