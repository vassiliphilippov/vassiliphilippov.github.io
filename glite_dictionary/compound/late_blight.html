late blight

1. NOUN

/ leɪt blaɪt / (AmE), / leɪt blaɪt / (BrE)
a serious plant disease appearing late in the growing season, especially affecting potatoes and tomatoes, caused by the fungus Phytophthora infestans
plant disease affecting potatoes/tomatoes

Examples

The potato crop was devastated by late blight after a period of heavy rain.
Farmers use resistant varieties to reduce the risk of late blight affecting their tomatoes.
Late blight can spread rapidly under humid conditions, causing severe losses in the field.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blight - a disease or condition that causes plants to wither
