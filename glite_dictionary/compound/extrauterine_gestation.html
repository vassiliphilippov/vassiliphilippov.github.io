extrauterine gestation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛkstrəˈjuːtəriːn dʒɛˈsteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɛkstrəˈjuːtəriːn dʒɛˈsteɪʃən / (BrE)
a pregnancy in which the embryo develops outside the uterus, most commonly in a fallopian tube
pregnancy outside the uterus
medical

Examples

The doctors diagnosed her with an extrauterine gestation after noting the abnormal pain and symptoms.
Surgery is often required to resolve an extrauterine gestation and protect the patient's health.
Awareness of the risks associated with extrauterine gestation is important for early detection and treatment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ectopic pregnancy - pregnancy in which the fertilized egg implants and grows outside the uterus, often in a fallopian tube

Etymology

From Latin 'extra' (outside) + 'uterus' (womb) + 'gestatio' (carrying)
