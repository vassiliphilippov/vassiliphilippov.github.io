extraneous duty allowance

1. NOUN

/ ɛkˈstreɪniəs ˈdjuːti əˈlaʊəns / (AmE), / ɛkˈstreɪnɪəs ˈdjuːti əˈlaʊəns / (BrE)
an allowance paid to employees for undertaking special assignments or additional duties beyond their normal responsibilities
payment for special or extra work duties

Examples

Employees who take on extra tasks are eligible for an extraneous duty allowance.
The company introduced an extraneous duty allowance for those involved in special projects.
Her paycheck included an extraneous duty allowance for managing the team during the event.
