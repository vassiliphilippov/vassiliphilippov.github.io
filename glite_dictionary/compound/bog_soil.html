bog soil

1. NOUN

/ bɑɡ sɔɪl / (AmE), / bɒɡ sɔɪl / (BrE)
Soil found in bogs, typically waterlogged, poorly drained, and rich in peat, forming under marsh or swamp vegetation.
waterlogged soil rich in peat, found in bogs

Examples

The scientists took samples of bog soil to study its unique composition.
Many rare plants thrive only in the acidic conditions provided by bog soil.
Improper drainage can lead to the development of bog soil in low-lying farmland.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peat - partially decayed plant matter forming the main constituent of bog soil
