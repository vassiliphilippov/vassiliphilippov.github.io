Nanga Parbat

1. PROPN

/ ˌnɑːŋgə ˈpɑːrbʌt / (AmE), / ˌnæŋgə ˈpɑːbət / (BrE)
a mountain in the western Himalayas in Kashmir, standing at 26,660 feet (8,126 meters) high
a Himalayan mountain in Kashmir

Examples

Many climbers have perished trying to reach the summit of Nanga Parbat.
Nanga Parbat is often called the 'Killer Mountain' due to its challenging conditions.
The breathtaking view of Nanga Parbat attracts adventurers from around the world.

Etymology

From Urdu/Hindi नंगा पर्वत (nangā parbat) meaning 'naked mountain'.
