line noise

1. NOUN

/ laɪn nɔɪz / (AmE), / laɪn nɔɪz / (BrE)
unwanted electrical or audio interference present in a communication line, often causing disruption or degradation of signal quality
interference in a communication line

Examples

The engineer traced the source of the line noise to a faulty wire in the building.
Excessive line noise caused the transmission to become nearly unintelligible during the webcast.
To improve the call quality, they installed new equipment to filter out line noise.

Synset Examples

He was frustrated by the line noise during the important call.
The technician identified the line noise as a major issue.
The line noise made it difficult to hear the conversation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interference - disturbance that affects a signal, communication, or process
