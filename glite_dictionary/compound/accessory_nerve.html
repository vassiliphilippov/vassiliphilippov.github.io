accessory nerve

1. NOUN

/ əkˈsɛsəri nɜrv / (AmE), / əkˈsɛsəri nɜːv / (BrE)
the eleventh cranial nerve, arising from both cranial and spinal roots and controlling certain muscles in the neck and shoulders
the eleventh cranial nerve
medical

Examples

Damage to the accessory nerve can cause weakness in the shoulder muscles.
The accessory nerve supplies motor function to the sternocleidomastoid and trapezius muscles.
During surgery, care must be taken to avoid injuring the accessory nerve.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cranial nerve - any of the nerves that emerge directly from the brain, including the brainstem

Etymology

From Latin 'accessorius' (additional) + 'nerve'.
