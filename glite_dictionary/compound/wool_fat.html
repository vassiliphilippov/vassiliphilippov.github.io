wool fat

1. NOUN

/ wʊl ˌfæt / (AmE), / wʊl ˌfat / (BrE)
a yellow, greasy substance extracted from sheep's wool, used as a base in ointments and cosmetics; also known as lanolin
oil from sheep's wool used in ointments and cosmetics

Examples

Lanolin is derived from wool fat and is widely used in skin creams.
The ointment contains purified wool fat to help soothe dry skin.
Many cosmetics companies utilize wool fat for its moisturizing properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lanolin - refined wool fat, used as a skin moisturizer
