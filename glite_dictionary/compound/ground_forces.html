ground forces

1. NOUN

/ ɡraʊnd ˈfɔrsɪz / (AmE), / ɡraʊnd ˈfɔːsɪz / (BrE)
the branch of a nation's military responsible for operations on land, as opposed to the air or sea
military land forces of a nation

Examples

The country's ground forces were deployed to the border during the conflict.
Modern ground forces rely heavily on advanced technology and logistics for their operations.
The government announced a significant increase in funding for its ground forces this year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: army - the principal military land force of a nation
