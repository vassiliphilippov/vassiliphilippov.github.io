chemical operations

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɛmɪkəl ɒpəˈreɪʃənz / (AmE), / ˈkɛmɪkəl ɒpəˈreɪʃənz / (BrE)
military actions involving the use of chemical agents intended to kill, injure, or incapacitate enemy forces
use of chemical agents in warfare

Examples

During World War I, chemical operations caused widespread casualties among soldiers on both sides.
The international community condemned the use of chemical operations in the conflict.
Concerns about chemical operations have led to strict arms control agreements worldwide.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chemical warfare - the use of toxic chemical substances as weapons
  • Antonym: biological warfare - use of biological agents in warfare
