sycamore fig

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪk.əˌmɔr fɪɡ / (AmE), / ˈsɪk.əmɔː fɪɡ / (BrE)
A thick-branched, wide-spreading tree native to Africa and southwestern Asia, often buttressed and producing clusters of edible but inferior figs; also known as the biblical sycamore.
a tree of Africa and Asia, the biblical sycamore

Examples

The biblical story mentions a sycamore fig tree in Jericho.
Farmers in Africa often harvest sycamore figs for local consumption despite their inferior taste.
A wide sycamore fig shaded the well at the center of the village.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fig tree - tree producing edible figs

Etymology

From Greek sykómorea, referring to this fig tree distinct from European sycamore.
