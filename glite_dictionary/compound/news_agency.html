news agency

1. NOUN

/ nuz ˈeɪ.dʒən.si / (AmE), / njuːz ˈeɪ.dʒən.si / (BrE)
An organization that gathers news reports and supplies them to newspapers, broadcasters, and other media outlets.
organization that supplies media with news

Examples

Reuters is a well-known news agency that provides stories to media outlets worldwide.
The news agency reported on the earthquake just minutes after it struck.
Many newspapers rely on a news agency for international news coverage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wire service - a news agency that supplies syndicated news by wire to newspapers, radio, and television stations
