radioactive decay

1. NOUN

/ ˌreɪdioʊˌæktɪv dɪˈkeɪ / (AmE), / ˌreɪdiəʊˌæktɪv dɪˈkeɪ / (BrE)
the spontaneous process by which an unstable atomic nucleus loses energy by emitting radiation
spontaneous breakdown of unstable atomic nuclei

Examples

Radioactive decay is the fundamental process that makes nuclear power possible.
Scientists studied the rate of radioactive decay in different materials.
The half-life of an isotope measures how quickly radioactive decay occurs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: radioactivity - the emission of radiation by unstable atomic nuclei

Etymology

radioactive from 'radioactivity' (from Latin 'radius', ray) and 'decay' (Middle English from Old French 'decair', from Latin 'decadere', to fall off).
