new wave

1. NOUN

/ nuː weɪv / (AmE), / njuː weɪv / (BrE)
a group or movement introducing and embracing new concepts and techniques, especially in the arts or culture
innovative movement or group in the arts

Examples

The French cinema of the 1960s is often cited as a major new wave in filmmaking.
A new wave of artists has transformed the city's cultural scene in recent years.
His novel was part of the new wave challenging traditional literary forms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: avant-garde - artists or works that are innovative and experimental
  • Antonym: mainstream - widely accepted, conventional style or approach
