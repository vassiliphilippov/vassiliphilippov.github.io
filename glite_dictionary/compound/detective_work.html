detective work

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈtɛktɪv wɜːrk / (AmE), / dɪˈtɛktɪv wɜːk / (BrE)
the activity of investigating to uncover information or solve a crime, especially as performed by the police or a detective
investigative activity to solve a crime

Examples

The police spent weeks doing detective work before finally arresting the suspect.
Solving the mystery required a lot of careful detective work behind the scenes.
Her detective work uncovered crucial evidence that everyone else had missed.

Synset Examples

detection is hard on the feet

Related Terms

  • Synonym: investigation - the act of examining a crime or situation carefully

2. NOUN

/ dɪˈtɛktɪv wɜrk / (AmE), / dɪˈtɛktɪv wɜːk / (BrE)
the activity of investigating and gathering information to solve a mystery or crime
investigative activities to solve a mystery

Examples

After months of detective work, the team finally uncovered the truth.
The author described her research as a kind of detective work into her family's history.
Piecing together the old letters involved a lot of careful detective work.

Synset Examples

Solving puzzles felt like a form of detective work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: investigation - the process of examining or inquiring into something thoroughly
  • Synonym: detective - a person whose job is to investigate crimes and mysteries
