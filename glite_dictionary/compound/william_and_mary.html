William and Mary

1. PROPN

/ ˈwɪljəm ənd ˈmɛri / (AmE), / ˈwɪljəm ənd ˈmeəri / (BrE)
the joint monarchs of England, William III and Mary II, who ruled together from 1689 to 1694
joint monarchs of England (1689–1694)
british

Examples

The Glorious Revolution resulted in William and Mary ascending the English throne.
William and Mary implemented the English Bill of Rights during their reign.
Many historians study the legacy of William and Mary in shaping constitutional monarchy.
