genus Amelanchier

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˌæmɪˈlænkiər / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˌæmɪˈlaenkɪə / (BrE)
A genus of North American deciduous trees or shrubs, known for producing small edible fruit and commonly called serviceberries.
genus of North American serviceberries

Examples

The genus Amelanchier includes several species native to temperate regions of North America.
Gardeners often plant shrubs from the genus Amelanchier for their attractive flowers and edible berries.
Biologists studied the distribution of the genus Amelanchier across different forest habitats.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, genus name *Amelanchier*, of French origin from *amélanche* (serviceberry).
