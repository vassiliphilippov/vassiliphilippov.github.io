Falun Gong

1. PROPN

/ ˈfɑːlʊn ˈɡʌŋ / (AmE), / ˈfælʊn ˈɡɒŋ / (BrE)
A spiritual movement originating in China in the late 20th century, combining elements of Buddhist and Taoist philosophy and meditation practices.
Chinese spiritual movement (late 20th century)

Examples

Many followers of Falun Gong practice meditation and gentle exercises daily.
The Chinese government has faced international criticism for its treatment of Falun Gong practitioners.
Falun Gong teachings emphasize truthfulness, compassion, and tolerance.

Etymology

From Mandarin 法轮功 (Fǎlún Gōng), meaning 'Dharma Wheel Practice'.
