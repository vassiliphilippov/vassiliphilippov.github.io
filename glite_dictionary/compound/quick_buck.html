quick buck

1. NOUN

/ kwɪk bʌk / (AmE), / kwɪk bʌk / (BrE)
money earned quickly and with little effort, often with the implication of dubious methods or motives.
money gained quickly and easily
informal

Examples

He's always looking for a quick buck instead of working a steady job.
Some companies cut corners just to make a quick buck.
She warned her son not to trust anyone promising a quick buck.

Synset Examples

they are traders out to make a fast buck

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fast buck - money made quickly and with little effort, often unscrupulously
  • Synonym: easy money - money earned with minimal effort
