machine-hour

1. NOUN

/ məˈʃin aʊɚ / (AmE), / məˈʃiːn aʊə / (BrE)
A unit measuring the operation of a machine for one hour, used to assess productivity, costs, or efficiency in industrial or technical contexts.
unit: one machine operating for one hour

Examples

The factory keeps careful records of machine-hours to monitor production efficiency.
Each machine-hour is tracked to calculate maintenance schedules and costs.
Last month, we exceeded our budgeted machine-hours due to increased client orders.

Synset Examples

This engine right here has put a lot of machine-hours.
