Communist Manifesto

1. PROPN

/ kəˈmjunəst ˌmænəˈfɛstoʊ / (AmE), / kəˈmjuːnɪst ˌmænɪˈfɛstəʊ / (BrE)
a political pamphlet written by Karl Marx and Friedrich Engels in 1848 outlining the principles of communism and the theories of the working-class movement
Marx and Engels' 1848 communist pamphlet

Examples

Many students are required to read the Communist Manifesto for their political science courses.
The ideas presented in the Communist Manifesto have influenced numerous social movements.
He referenced the Communist Manifesto during the debate on economic reform.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Das Kapital - Marx's major theoretical work on political economy and capitalism

Etymology

Title from *Manifest der Kommunistischen Partei* (German: 'Manifesto of the Communist Party'), published 1848.
