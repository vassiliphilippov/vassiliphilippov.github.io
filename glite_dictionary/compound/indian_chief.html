Indian chief

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪndiən tʃiːf / (AmE), / ˈɪndiən tʃiːf / (BrE)
the leader of a group or tribe of Native Americans, especially in a traditional or historical context
leader of a Native American tribe

Examples

The Indian chief addressed his people with authority and wisdom.
Anthropologists studied the leadership styles of several Indian chiefs across different tribes.
In many stories, the Indian chief is portrayed as a figure of respect and dignity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tribal chief - the leader of a tribe or clan
  • Synonym: chieftain - a leader of a people or clan, especially in a traditional society
