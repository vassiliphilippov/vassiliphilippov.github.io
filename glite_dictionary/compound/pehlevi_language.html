Pehlevi language

1. NOUN

/ pɛhˈleɪvi ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / pɛhˈleɪvi ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
An Iranian language used in Zoroastrian literature from the 3rd to the 10th centuries, also known as Middle Persian.
ancient Iranian language, 3rd–10th c.

Examples

The sacred texts were preserved in the Pehlevi language for several centuries.
She studied ancient scripts written in Pehlevi language at university.
The Pehlevi language was crucial for understanding Zoroastrianism's early texts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Middle Persian - The predecessor of Modern Persian, language of Sassanian Persia

Etymology

From Modern Persian پهلوی (Pahlavi), referring to the Parthian era and script.
