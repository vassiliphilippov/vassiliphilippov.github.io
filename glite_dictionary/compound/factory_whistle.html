factory whistle

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæktəɹi ˌwɪsəl / (AmE), / ˈfæktəri ˌwɪsl̩ / (BrE)
a whistle sounded at a factory to signal the beginning or end of work periods
whistle indicating work times at a factory

Examples

The loud factory whistle signaled the start of the morning shift.
Workers knew it was time for lunch when the factory whistle blew at noon.
Each evening, the factory whistle could be heard across the town, marking the end of the workday.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whistle - a device that produces a shrill sound when blown
