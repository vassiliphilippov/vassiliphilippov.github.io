manila tamarind

1. NOUN

/ məˈnɪlə təˈmɛrənd / (AmE), / məˈnɪlə təˈmɑːrɪnd / (BrE)
a thorny tropical American tree (Pithecellobium dulce) with yellow flowers, edible curved pods, and valued for its timber, yellow dye, and mucilaginous gum
tropical tree with edible pods and yellow flowers

Examples

The fruit of the manila tamarind is enjoyed as a sweet snack in many countries.
Farmers appreciate the manila tamarind for its ability to improve soil fertility.
The wood of the manila tamarind is often used in local carpentry.

Etymology

Named after Manila, the capital of the Philippines, and 'tamarind' due to the similarity of its fruit to true tamarind.
