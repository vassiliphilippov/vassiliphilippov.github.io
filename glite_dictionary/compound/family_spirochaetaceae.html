family Spirochaetaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli spaɪroʊˌkiːtəˈsiːiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli spaɪrəʊˌkiːtəˈsiːiː / (BrE)
a large family of spiral-shaped bacteria, found in freshwater and saltwater or as commensals in the bodies of oysters
a family of spiral-shaped bacteria
medical

Examples

Scientists discovered a new species within the family Spirochaetaceae in the oyster beds.
The family Spirochaetaceae includes bacteria that are free-living in both saltwater and freshwater.
Some members of the family Spirochaetaceae can be found living in association with marine invertebrates.

Etymology

From scientific Latin, combining 'Spirochaeta' (the type genus) and '-aceae' (family suffix).
