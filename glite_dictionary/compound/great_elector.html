Great Elector

1. PROPN

/ ɡreɪt ɪˈlɛktər / (AmE), / ɡreɪt ɪˈlɛktə / (BrE)
the title given to Frederick William, Elector of Brandenburg (1620–1688), known for rebuilding and strengthening his territory after the Thirty Years' War
title of Frederick William, Elector of Brandenburg

Examples

The Great Elector modernized his army and centralized government power in Brandenburg.
Historians often credit the Great Elector with laying the foundations for the future Kingdom of Prussia.
The reforms carried out by the Great Elector had lasting effects on European politics.

Etymology

From English 'great' (notable) and 'elector' (one of the German princes entitled to elect the Holy Roman Emperor).
