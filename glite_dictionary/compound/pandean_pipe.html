pandean pipe

1. NOUN

/ ˈpæn.di.ən paɪp / (AmE), / ˈpandiːən paɪp / (BrE)
a primitive wind instrument made of several parallel pipes of gradually increasing length, bound together and played by blowing across the tops
primitive wind instrument made of parallel pipes

Examples

The shepherd played a soft melody on his pandean pipe as dusk fell over the hills.
Ancient artwork often depicts figures holding a pandean pipe beside goats or sheep.
He crafted a pandean pipe from reeds he found by the river.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: panpipe - a musical instrument made from joined pipes of different lengths
  • Degree Lower: flute - a woodwind instrument played by blowing across an opening

Etymology

From Greek Pan (the god of shepherds and flocks) + -ean, referencing the mythological invention of the instrument by Pan.
