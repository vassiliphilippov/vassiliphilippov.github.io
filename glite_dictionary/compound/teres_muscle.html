teres muscle

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛr.iːz ˈmʌs.əl / (AmE), / ˈtɪə.riːz ˈmʌs.əl / (BrE)
Either of two small muscles in the shoulder region that help move the shoulder and arm.
shoulder muscle aiding arm movement
medical

Examples

The teres muscle is important for stabilizing the shoulder during movement.
Tearing a teres muscle can significantly reduce your ability to lift your arm overhead.
Both the major and minor teres muscle play a role in rotating the upper arm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: infraspinatus muscle - a nearby rotator cuff muscle
  • Synonym: deltoid muscle - major muscle covering the shoulder

Etymology

From Latin 'teres' meaning 'rounded, smooth'.
