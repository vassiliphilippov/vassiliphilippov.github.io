Spanish broom

1. NOUN

/ ˌspænɪʃ ˈbruːm / (AmE), / ˌspænɪʃ ˈbruːm / (BrE)
an erect shrub native to southwestern Europe, known for its racemes of golden yellow flowers
a shrub with golden yellow flowers, native to southwestern Europe

Examples

The garden was brightened by the vibrant blooms of Spanish broom.
They planted Spanish broom along the sunny hillside for its colorful flowers.
Spanish broom thrives in dry, rocky soils and full sunlight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: broom - a genus of shrubs with long green stems and yellow flowers

2. NOUN

/ ˈspænɪʃ bruːm / (AmE), / ˈspænɪʃ bruːm / (BrE)
a tall, thornless Mediterranean shrub (Spartium junceum) with fragrant yellow flowers and flexible stems used in basketry; widely grown as an ornamental and naturalized in some regions
a tall Mediterranean shrub with yellow flowers

Examples

The Spanish broom brightened the hillside with its vivid yellow blossoms in spring.
Artisans use Spanish broom twigs to weave strong and flexible baskets.
In parts of California, Spanish broom has become an invasive species along roadsides.
