Naples garlic

1. NOUN

/ ˈneɪpəlz ˈɡɑrlɪk / (AmE), / ˈneɪpəlz ˈɡɑːlɪk / (BrE)
A European species of wild onion (Allium neapolitanum) with clusters of white flowers, native to the Mediterranean region.
European wild onion with white flowers

Examples

Naples garlic is often grown in gardens for its attractive clusters of small white flowers.
The leaves of Naples garlic can be used as a mild herb in salads or soups.
Many Mediterranean dishes feature Naples garlic for its delicate flavor and decorative blooms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: onion - an edible bulb and plant of the genus Allium

Etymology

Named after Naples, Italy, where the plant is native or widely found.
