rear admiral

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɪr ˌæd.mə.rəl / (AmE), / ˈrɪə ˌæd.mər.əl / (BrE)
a naval officer of high rank, junior to a vice admiral and above a commodore or captain
naval officer rank below vice admiral

Examples

The rear admiral addressed the crew with a speech about their accomplishments.
After years of loyal service, she was promoted to rear admiral.
A rear admiral typically commands a fleet or squadron within the navy.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: vice admiral - a naval officer ranking above rear admiral and below admiral
  • Degree Lower: commodore - a naval officer rank below rear admiral

Etymology

From Middle English 'rear' (meaning 'behind') + 'admiral', referring to the officer commanding the rear of a naval fleet.
