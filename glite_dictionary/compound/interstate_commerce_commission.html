Interstate Commerce Commission

1. PROPN

/ ˌɪn.tɚˈsteɪt ˈkɑː.mɚs kəˌmɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / ˌɪn.təˈsteɪt ˈkɒm.ɜːs kəˌmɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
a former independent federal agency in the United States that regulated and set rates for carriers transporting goods and people between states, abolished in 1995
former US federal agency regulating interstate transport
american

Examples

The Interstate Commerce Commission played a crucial role in regulating railroads in the early twentieth century.
In 1995, the Interstate Commerce Commission was officially abolished and its duties were transferred to other agencies.
Many historians study the impact of the Interstate Commerce Commission on the development of American transportation policies.
