bank martin

1. NOUN

/ bæŋk ˈmɑrtɪn / (AmE), / bæŋk ˈmɑːtɪn / (BrE)
a small swallow found in the Northern Hemisphere that nests in holes dug into sand or clay banks
a swallow that nests in sand or clay banks

Examples

The bank martin digs burrows in river banks to build its nest.
Flocks of bank martins can often be seen skimming over lakes and ponds.
During spring migration, the bank martin returns to its nesting sites along sandy cliffs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sand martin - a small swallow that nests in sandy banks; common synonym for 'bank martin'
