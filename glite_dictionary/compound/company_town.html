company town

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌmpəni taʊn / (AmE), / ˈkʌmpəni taʊn / (BrE)
a community chiefly owned and operated by a single company, in which housing, stores, and services are provided primarily for the company's employees
community owned by a single company

Examples

Many immigrants settled in the company town that sprang up around the coal mine.
The decline of the mill led to high unemployment in the once-prosperous company town.
He grew up in a company town, where the company owned both his school and his home.

Synset Examples

Life in the company town revolved around the factory.
The factory built a company town for its workers.
