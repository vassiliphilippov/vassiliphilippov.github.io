pasque flower

1. NOUN

/ pæsk ˈflaʊ.ər / (AmE), / pɑːsk ˈflaʊ.ə / (BrE)
a perennial flowering plant of the genus Pulsatilla, with showy, usually purple or white flowers, sometimes included in the genus Anemone
a plant of the genus Pulsatilla

Examples

The pasque flower blooms in early spring, often around Easter time.
Many gardeners prize the delicate beauty of the pasque flower in rock gardens.
She pressed a pasque flower between the pages of her favorite book.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anemone - a plant of the genus Anemone, closely related to pasque flower

Etymology

From French 'pasque' (Easter), referring to the plant's early spring flowering.
