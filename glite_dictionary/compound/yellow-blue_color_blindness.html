yellow-blue color blindness

1. NOUN

/ ˈjɛloʊ blu ˈkʌlər ˌblaɪndnəs / (AmE), / ˈjɛləʊ bluː ˈkʌlə ˌblaɪndnəs / (BrE)
a condition in which a person has difficulty distinguishing between yellow and blue hues, often confusing the two colors
inability to distinguish yellow and blue
medical

Examples

People with yellow-blue color blindness often struggle to tell the difference between blue sky and yellow flowers.
The diagnosis of yellow-blue color blindness can impact a person's career choice, especially in fields requiring color discrimination.
A special test was administered to determine whether he had yellow-blue color blindness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: red-green color blindness - a condition in which red and green colors are commonly confused
