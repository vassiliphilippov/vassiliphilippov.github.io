a bite of

1. NOUN

/ ə baɪt ʌv / (AmE), / ə baɪt ɒv / (BrE)
a small piece or portion of food taken from a larger serving
a small piece of food

Examples

He took a bite of the apple and smiled.
Would you like a bite of my chocolate cake?
After a bite of pizza, she decided she wasn't hungry.

Synset Examples

I’ll just have a bite of your sandwich, thanks!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mouthful - as much food or drink as fills the mouth
  • Synonym: piece - a portion of something
