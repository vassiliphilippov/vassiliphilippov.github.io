theropod dinosaur

1. NOUN

/ ˈθɛr.əˌpɑd ˈdaɪ.nəˌsɔr / (AmE), / ˈθɛr.ə.pɒd ˈdʌɪ.nə.sɔː / (BrE)
any of a group of mostly carnivorous dinosaurs from the Triassic to Cretaceous periods, characterized by short forelimbs and strong hind legs used for walking or running.
carnivorous dinosaur with strong hind legs

Examples

The theropod dinosaur fossils discovered in the region revealed new species previously unknown to science.
A theropod dinosaur typically had sharp teeth and claws adapted for hunting prey.
Paleontologists believe that modern birds evolved from small theropod dinosaur ancestors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dinosaur - large extinct group of reptiles from the Mesozoic era

Etymology

From Greek 'ther' (beast) and 'pous' (foot), combined with 'dinosaur' (from Greek 'deinos' [terrible] + 'sauros' [lizard]).
