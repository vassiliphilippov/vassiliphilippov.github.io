genus Fothergilla

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs fɒˈθɜr.dʒɪ.lə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs fəˈθɜː.dʒɪ.lə / (BrE)
A small genus of deciduous shrubs native to the southeastern United States, known for their white, bottlebrush-like flowers.
a genus of deciduous shrubs (southeastern US)

Examples

The genus Fothergilla includes shrubs with fragrant, white, brush-like flowers that bloom in spring.
Gardeners in the southeastern U.S. often plant members of the genus Fothergilla for their stunning autumn foliage.
Botanists recently discovered a new species within the genus Fothergilla in a remote forest in Georgia.

Etymology

Genus name from Dr. John Fothergill, English physician and botanist (1712–1780).
