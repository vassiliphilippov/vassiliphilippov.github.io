sleeping beauty

1. NOUN

/ ˈslipɪŋ ˈbjuːti / (AmE), / ˈsliːpɪŋ ˈbjuːti / (BrE)
a company regarded as a potential target for takeover that has not yet attracted attention from bidders
potential takeover target (business term)

Examples

Analysts believe the firm is a sleeping beauty that could be awakened by the right bidder.
The investment group is constantly looking for sleeping beauties with untapped market potential.
Despite its low profile, the company has become a prime sleeping beauty in the tech sector.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: takeover target - a company that is a target for acquisition or merger

Etymology

From the fairy tale 'Sleeping Beauty', used metaphorically in business to describe a dormant but attractive company.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsliːpɪŋ ˈbjuːti / (AmE), / ˈsliːpɪŋ ˈbjuːti / (BrE)
a woman or girl who is sleeping soundly or appears especially beautiful while asleep; also used in allusion to the fairy tale character
woman or girl sleeping soundly or beautifully

Examples

She looked like a sleeping beauty as she napped on the couch, undisturbed by the noise around her.
The photographer captured the essence of a sleeping beauty in the soft morning light.
After the long journey, Maria became our own sleeping beauty, dozing peacefully in the back seat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beauty - a beautiful woman or girl

Etymology

From the title of the fairy tale 'Sleeping Beauty', popularized in English through translations of Charles Perrault's 1697 'La Belle au bois dormant' and the Brothers Grimm's 'Dornröschen'.
