cluster bomb

1. NOUN

/ ˈklʌstɚ bɑm / (AmE), / ˈklʌstə bɒm / (BrE)
a bomb that contains several smaller bombs which spread out and explode separately over a wide area when released from an aircraft
bomb releasing smaller bombs over wide area

Examples

Many countries have banned the use of cluster bombs because of the risk to civilians.
The airstrike used cluster bombs to target enemy vehicles across a large area.
Debris from unexploded cluster bombs poses a danger long after the conflict ends.

Synset Examples

cluster bombs cannot be targeted precisely

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incendiary bomb - bomb designed to start fires
