high-bush blueberry

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪ ˌbʊʃ ˈbluːˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈhaɪ ˌbʊʃ ˈbluːˌbɛri / (BrE)
A tall, deciduous shrub native to eastern North America, known for producing edible blue-black berries and widely cultivated for its fruit.
tall blueberry shrub native to North America

Examples

The high-bush blueberry is a common sight in commercial berry farms throughout North America.
We planted several high-bush blueberry shrubs to enjoy fresh berries in the summer.
Unlike wild varieties, the high-bush blueberry grows taller and produces larger fruit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blueberry - edible blue-black berry from plants of the genus Vaccinium
