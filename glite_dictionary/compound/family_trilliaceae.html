family Trilliaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli trɪliˈeɪsiːˌaɪ / (AmE), / ˈfæməli trɪlɪˈeɪsɪˌaɪ / (BrE)
A small family of herbaceous plants characterized by flowers with three petals and three sepals; in some classification systems included as a subfamily of the Liliaceae.
a plant family with tri-petaled flowers
medical

Examples

The family Trilliaceae includes several species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.
Botanists sometimes classify the family Trilliaceae as a subfamily within the Liliaceae.
Flowers in the family Trilliaceae typically have three petals and three sepals.

Etymology

From Latin 'Trillium,' the type genus, combined with 'aceae,' a standard suffix for plant family names.
