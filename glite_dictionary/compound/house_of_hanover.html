House of Hanover

1. PROPN

/ haʊs əv ˈhænəvɚ / (AmE), / haʊs əv ˈhanəvə / (BrE)
the English royal dynasty that ruled from 1714 to 1901, beginning with George I and ending with Queen Victoria
English royal dynasty, 1714–1901

Examples

Queen Victoria was the last monarch from the House of Hanover.
British history was greatly influenced by the reign of the House of Hanover.
The House of Hanover succeeded the House of Stuart as the ruling dynasty in Britain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hanoverian - relating to the House of Hanover or its members

Etymology

From Hanover, a city in Germany, the original homeland of the dynasty.
