order Parietales

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdər ˌpæriˈeɪtəliːz / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə ˌpæriˈeɪtəliːz / (BrE)
a former order of dicotyledonous plants in the subclass Dilleniidae, comprising several families such as the Violaceae and Cactaceae
a former order of flowering plants

Examples

The order Parietales once included the cactus family, though its classification has changed.
Certain members of the order Parietales were known for their unique floral structures.
Taxonomists debated the boundaries of order Parietales before modern genetic methods were used.

Etymology

From Latin 'paries' (wall), referring to parietal (wall-adhering) placentation in plants.
