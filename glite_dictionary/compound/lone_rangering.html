lone rangering

1. NOUN

/ loʊn ˈreɪndʒərɪŋ / (AmE), / ləʊn ˈreɪndʒərɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of working independently or alone, often without team support
working alone without team support
informal

Examples

Though the project required a team, she preferred lone rangering to maintain full control.
His habit of lone rangering sometimes made colleagues feel excluded.
Despite the risks of lone rangering, the manager admired his initiative.

Synset Examples

Her style of working often involves lone rangering on projects.
He admitted that lone rangering isn't always the best approach.
Lone rangering can be effective, but teamwork is essential.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: working solo - working alone, without others
  • Antonym: collaborating - working together with others

Etymology

From the title character 'The Lone Ranger', a fictional masked hero who often worked alone, plus '-ing' to form a noun or gerund.
