Piper betel

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪ.pər ˈbiː.təl / (AmE), / ˈpaɪ.pə ˈbiː.təl / (BrE)
An Asian pepper plant whose leaves are chewed with betel nut by people in Southeast Asia.
Asian pepper plant chewed with betel nut

Examples

The leaves of Piper betel are commonly used in traditional ceremonies across Southeast Asia.
Chewing Piper betel leaves with areca nut is a centuries-old cultural practice.
Her grandmother grew Piper betel in the backyard for daily use.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: betel nut - seed of the betel palm chewed with betel leaves

Etymology

Latin: Piper (pepper) + betel, from Malayalam 'vettila' (betel leaf).
