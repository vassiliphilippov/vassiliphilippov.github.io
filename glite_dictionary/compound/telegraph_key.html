telegraph key

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛlɪˌɡræf kiː / (AmE), / ˈtɛlɪɡrɑːf kiː / (BrE)
a device with a lever used to send telegraph signals by opening and closing an electric circuit
device for sending telegraph signals

Examples

The operator tapped the telegraph key swiftly to send the urgent message.
In the museum, an old telegraph key sits next to a vintage Morse code chart.
Learning to use a telegraph key was once an essential skill for telegraph operators.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sounder - device that makes sound in response to telegraph signals
