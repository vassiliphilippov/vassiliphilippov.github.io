moss locust

1. NOUN

/ mɑs ˈloʊkəst / (AmE), / mɒs ˈləʊkəst / (BrE)
A large shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States, known for its bristly stems and large clusters of pink flowers.
shrub or small tree with pink flowers

Examples

The moss locust brightened the woodland with its vibrant pink flowers each spring.
Gardeners admire the moss locust for its unique bristly stems and decorative blooms.
We planted a moss locust near the fence to attract more pollinators to our yard.
