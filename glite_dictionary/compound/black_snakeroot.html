2. NOUN

/ blæk ˈsneɪkˌruːt / (AmE), / blæk ˈsneɪkˌruːt / (BrE)
A North American perennial plant (genus Cimicifuga) with slender stalks and small white flowers, found from Maine and Ontario to Georgia.
a North American plant (Cimicifuga species)

Examples

Early settlers used black snakeroot as a remedy for headaches and other ailments.
The forest floor was dotted with patches of black snakeroot in full bloom.
Herbalists often collect black snakeroot roots for traditional medicine.

  • Synonym: bugbane - another common name for plants of the same genus
