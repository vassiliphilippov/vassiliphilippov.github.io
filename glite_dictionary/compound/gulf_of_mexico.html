Gulf of Mexico

1. PROPN

/ ˌɡʌlf əv ˈmɛksɪkoʊ / (AmE), / ˌɡʌlf əv ˈmɛksɪkəʊ / (BrE)
an arm of the Atlantic Ocean located to the south of the United States and east of Mexico
sea south of the US and east of Mexico

Examples

Many hurricanes develop over the Gulf of Mexico before heading toward the United States.
Oil drilling in the Gulf of Mexico is a major industry for both Mexico and the United States.
The coastlines along the Gulf of Mexico are known for their beautiful beaches and warm climate.

2. PROPN

/ ˌɡʌlf əv ˈmɛksɪkoʊ / (AmE), / ˌɡʌlf əv ˈmɛksɪkəʊ / (BrE)
a large gulf of the Atlantic Ocean bordered by the United States to the north, Mexico to the west and south, and Cuba to the southeast
large gulf south of the US, east of Mexico

Examples

Many hurricanes form in the Gulf of Mexico before moving toward the southern United States.
The coastline along the Gulf of Mexico is famous for its beautiful beaches and warm waters.
Oil drilling in the Gulf of Mexico is a major part of the region's economy.
