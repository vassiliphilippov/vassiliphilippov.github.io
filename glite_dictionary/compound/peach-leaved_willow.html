peach-leaved willow

1. NOUN

/ pitʃ livd ˈwɪloʊ / (AmE), / piːtʃ liːvd ˈwɪləʊ / (BrE)
A willow tree of the western United States distinguished by long, narrow leaves resembling those of a peach or almond tree.
a U.S. willow tree with peach-like leaves

Examples

The peach-leaved willow is commonly found near rivers and streams in the western U.S.
Many gardeners admire the peach-leaved willow for its distinctive narrow leaves.
We spotted a cluster of peach-leaved willows growing along the riverbank.
