Shumard red oak

1. NOUN

/ ʃuːˈmɑrd rɛd oʊk / (AmE), / ʃuːˈmɑːd rɛd əʊk / (BrE)
a large deciduous oak tree (Quercus shumardii) native to the southern and eastern United States, with large, deeply-lobed leaves and coarse-grained wood
species of large red oak tree of the U.S.

Examples

The Shumard red oak is prized for its impressive shade and attractive foliage in parks.
Many landscapers choose Shumard red oak trees for their resistance to drought and urban pollution.
In the autumn, the leaves of the Shumard red oak turn vibrant shades of red and orange.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oak - a tree of the genus Quercus bearing acorns and lobed leaves
  • Degree Lower: red oak - a group of oaks with bristle-tipped leaves and red wood

Etymology

Named after American geologist Benjamin Franklin Shumard (1820–1869).
