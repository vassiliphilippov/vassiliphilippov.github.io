whistleblower

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪsəlˌbloʊər / (AmE), / ˈwɪsəlˌbləʊə / (BrE)
a person who exposes secret practices or information within an organization, typically to report wrongdoing or illegal activity
person exposing organizational wrongdoing

Examples

The company retaliated against the whistleblower after she reported the environmental violation to authorities.
Many whistleblowers face serious consequences for revealing corporate corruption.
A new law was enacted to protect whistleblower identities in sensitive cases.

Synset Examples

the law gives little protection to whistleblowers who feel the public has a right to know what is going on
the whistleblower was fired for exposing the conditions in mental hospitals

Related Terms

  • Synonym: informant - person who provides information, especially secretly

Etymology

Formed from 'whistle' + 'blower', referencing a referee blowing a whistle to call attention to misconduct.
