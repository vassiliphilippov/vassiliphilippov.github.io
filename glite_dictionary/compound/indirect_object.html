indirect object

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪndaɪˌrɛkt ˈɑbdʒɛkt / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˈrɛkt ˈɒbdʒɪkt / (BrE)
the noun or pronoun in a sentence that receives the benefit of the action of the verb, usually appearing with a direct object
recipient of verb's action (grammar)

Examples

In the sentence 'She gave him a book', him is the indirect object.
Teachers often explain the difference between a direct object and an indirect object to their students.
You can find the indirect object by asking 'to whom?' or 'for whom?' after the verb.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: direct object - the noun or pronoun that directly receives the action of the verb
