Canadian English orthography

1. NOUN

/ kəˈneɪdiən ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ ɔːrˈθɑɡrəfi / (AmE), / kəˈneɪdiən ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ ɔːˈθɒɡrəfi / (BrE)
the system of spelling and writing conventions used in Canadian English, characterized by a blend of British and American forms
spelling system used in Canadian English

Examples

Canadian textbooks often follow Canadian English orthography, using spellings like 'colour' and 'centre'.
Writers are sometimes confused about whether to use American or Canadian English orthography in their manuscripts.
The newspaper's editors standardized its style guide according to Canadian English orthography.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: British English orthography - system of spelling and writing conventions used in British English
  • Degree Higher: American English orthography - system of spelling and writing conventions used in American English
