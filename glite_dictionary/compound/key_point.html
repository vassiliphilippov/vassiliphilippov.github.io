key point

1. NOUN

/ ki pɔɪnt / (AmE), / kiː pɔɪnt / (BrE)
an important or central idea, fact, or argument, especially in a discussion, report, or presentation
main or important idea or argument

Examples

The manager summarized the key points of the meeting before we left.
It's important to focus on the key points when studying for the exam.
In her presentation, she highlighted the key points with bold colors.

Synset Examples

Please underline the key points in your report.
The squib in her speech highlighted key points.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: main point - the most important point or argument
  • Antonym: detail - a less important or minor point
