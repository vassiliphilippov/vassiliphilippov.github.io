undercover work

1. NOUN

/ ˌʌndərˈkʌvər wɝːk / (AmE), / ˌʌndəkˈʌvə wɜːk / (BrE)
covert activity or surveillance undertaken to gather intelligence, often for law enforcement or security purposes
covert activity to gather intelligence

Examples

The detective was praised for her years of undercover work infiltrating criminal organizations.
Most undercover work requires careful planning and the ability to blend in.
He wrote a book about his experiences doing undercover work in the narcotics division.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: espionage - the practice of spying or using spies to obtain information
