Virginia creeper

1. NOUN

/ vərˈdʒɪniə ˈkriːpər / (AmE), / vəˈdʒɪnɪə ˈkriːpə / (BrE)
a common North American climbing vine (Parthenocissus quinquefolia) with compound leaves and bluish-black berrylike fruit, often grown as an ornamental plant
North American climbing vine

Examples

The Virginia creeper quickly covered the entire fence by late summer.
Birds are attracted to the berries of the Virginia creeper in autumn.
Unlike poison ivy, Virginia creeper has five leaflets on each stem.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ivy - climbing or trailing woody plants with evergreen leaves
  • Degree Lower: poison ivy - North American plant causing skin irritation, similar in appearance
