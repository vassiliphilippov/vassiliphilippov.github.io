gold fever

1. NOUN

/ ɡoʊld ˈfiːvər / (AmE), / ɡəʊld ˈfiːvə / (BrE)
A strong excitement and greed sparked by the discovery of gold, often during a gold rush.
intense excitement about finding gold

Examples

The discovery of gold in California set off a wave of gold fever across the nation.
Many prospectors risked everything as gold fever swept through the small frontier town.
During the Klondike era, gold fever drove thousands to leave their homes in search of fortune.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gold rush - a period of feverish migration to an area following the discovery of gold
