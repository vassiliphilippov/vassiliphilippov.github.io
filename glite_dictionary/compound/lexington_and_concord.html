Lexington and Concord

1. PROPN

/ ˌlɛksɪŋtən ənd ˈkɑːŋkərd / (AmE), / ˌlɛksɪŋtən ənd ˈkɒŋkəd / (BrE)
the first military engagement of the American Revolutionary War, fought on April 19, 1775, in Massachusetts.
first battle of the American Revolution

Examples

Many historians consider the Battle of Lexington and Concord a turning point in American history.
Schoolchildren learn that Lexington and Concord marked the beginning of the fight for American independence.
The anniversary of Lexington and Concord is commemorated each year in Massachusetts.

Etymology

Lexington and Concord are towns in Massachusetts where the battle took place.
