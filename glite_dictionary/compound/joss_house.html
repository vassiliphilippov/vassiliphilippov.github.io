joss house

1. NOUN

/ dʒɑs haʊs / (AmE), / dʒɒs haʊs / (BrE)
A Chinese temple or shrine, especially one used for the worship of idols and traditional deities.
Chinese temple or shrine

Examples

The old Chinatown still has a beautifully decorated joss house open to visitors.
Worshippers gathered incense and offerings before entering the joss house.
The guide explained the significance of each statue inside the joss house.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: temple - A building for religious worship
  • Synonym: shrine - A place regarded as holy due to its associations with a divinity

Etymology

From Chinese pidgin English 'joss' (deity, idol), likely from Portuguese 'deus' (god), and English 'house'.
