genus Arctictis

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ɑːrkˈtɪk.tɪs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ɑːkˈtɪk.tɪs / (BrE)
A genus of mammals in the family Viverridae, whose only living species is the binturong.
a mammal genus including the binturong

Examples

The genus Arctictis is represented today solely by the binturong.
Research on the genus Arctictis has shed light on the evolutionary history of Viverridae.
The unique features of the genus Arctictis make it easily distinguishable from other genera in its family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: binturong - a large, tree-dwelling mammal native to Southeast Asia, also known as the bearcat

Etymology

From New Latin 'Arctictis', from Greek 'arktos' (bear) and 'iktis' (weasel).
