voter turnout

1. NOUN

/ ˈvoʊtər ˈtɜːrnaʊt / (AmE), / ˈvəʊtə ˈtəːnaʊt / (BrE)
The total number or percentage of eligible voters who participate in an election.
number of voters in an election

Examples

Authorities reported that voter turnout in the recent election was higher than expected.
Low voter turnout often raises concerns about public engagement with politics.
The government introduced new measures to encourage greater voter turnout among young people.

Synset Examples

they are expecting a large vote

Related Terms

  • Antonym: abstention - the act of not voting in an election
