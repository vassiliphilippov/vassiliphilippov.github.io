velvet worm

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɛlvət wɜrm / (AmE), / ˈvɛlvɪt wɜːm / (BrE)
a small, wormlike carnivorous invertebrate with velvety skin, found mainly in tropical forests, and showing features of both arthropods and annelid worms
wormlike tropical invertebrate with velvety skin

Examples

The velvet worm is fascinating for its unusual way of catching prey with a sticky slime.
Some scientists study velvet worms to learn about evolution and ancient animal lineages.
A velvet worm was discovered crawling across the rainforest floor during the expedition.
