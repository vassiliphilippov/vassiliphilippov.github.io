family Jungermanniaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli dʒuːŋərˌmæniˈeɪsiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli ˌdʒʌŋəˌmænɪˈeɪsiː / (BrE)
A family of leafy liverworts in the order Jungermanniales.
family of leafy liverworts

Examples

The family Jungermanniaceae includes several genera of leafy liverworts found in moist habitats.
Botanists have studied the ecology of the family Jungermanniaceae to understand liverwort diversity.
Species in the family Jungermanniaceae are important for understanding the evolution of non-vascular plants.

Etymology

From the genus name Jungermannia, named after German botanist Ludwig Jungermann, combined with the standard botanical family suffix -aceae.
