bear grass

1. NOUN

/ bɛr ɡræs / (AmE), / beə ɡrɑːs / (BrE)
A stemless perennial plant with grasslike leaves and a tall panicle of small creamy white flowers, native to the southwestern United States and Mexico.
a perennial plant with grasslike leaves and tall white flower panicle

Examples

The rocky slopes were covered with blooming bear grass in early summer.
Hikers admired the tall stalks of bear grass swaying in the breeze.
Bear grass is often found in dry, open woodlands across the American Southwest.
