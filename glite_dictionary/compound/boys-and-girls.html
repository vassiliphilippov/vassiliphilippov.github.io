boys-and-girls

1. NOUN

/ bɔɪz ənd ɡɜrlz / (AmE), / bɔɪz ənd ɡɜːlz / (BrE)
A species of annual plant (Mercurialis annua), formerly used medicinally as a purgative, diuretic, or to treat syphilis.
a plant (Mercurialis annua) formerly used medicinally

Examples

The leaves of boys-and-girls were once collected for their medicinal properties.
Farmers used to consider boys-and-girls a troublesome weed in their fields.
Traditional herbal remedies sometimes included extracts from boys-and-girls.
