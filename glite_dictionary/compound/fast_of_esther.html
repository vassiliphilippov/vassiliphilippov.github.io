Fast of Esther

1. NOUN

/ fæst əv ˈɛstɚ / (AmE), / fɑːst əv ˈɛstə / (BrE)
(in Judaism) a minor fast observed on the 13th of Adar, commemorating the three days that Esther fasted before appealing to the Persian king on behalf of the Jewish people; connected with the festival of Purim.
Jewish minor fast day before Purim

Examples

The Fast of Esther is observed each year on the day before Purim by many Jews.
During the Fast of Esther, participants abstain from food and drink from dawn until dusk.
She explained the significance of the Fast of Esther to her classmates at school.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Purim - Jewish festival commemorating the saving of the Jewish people from Haman, as told in the Book of Esther
