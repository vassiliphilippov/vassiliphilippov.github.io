shepherd's pipe

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃɛpɚdz paɪp / (AmE), / ˈʃɛpədz paɪp / (BrE)
a small type of bagpipe that was formerly popular in France, especially among shepherds
a small French bagpipe

Examples

The sound of the shepherd's pipe echoed across the rolling French hills.
At the festival, a musician performed a folk melody on a traditional shepherd's pipe.
Centuries ago, the shepherd's pipe was an essential part of rural celebrations in France.

2. NOUN

/ ˈʃɛpɚdz paɪp / (AmE), / ˈʃɛpədz pʌɪp / (BrE)
a small fipple flute, typically with four finger holes and two thumb holes, traditionally associated with shepherds
a traditional small fipple flute

Examples

The shepherd entertained his flock by playing a soft tune on his shepherd's pipe.
In the festival procession, a boy in costume performed melodies using a shepherd's pipe.
Ancient folk music often features the distinctive sound of the shepherd's pipe in the ensemble.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flute - a woodwind instrument made from a tube with holes that are stopped by the fingers or keys
