creatine phosphate

1. NOUN

/ ˈkriːətiːn ˈfɑːsfeɪt / (AmE), / ˈkriːətiːn ˈfɒsfeɪt / (BrE)
an organic compound found in muscle tissue that consists of creatine and phosphoric acid; it stores and provides energy for muscular contraction
compound storing energy in muscle tissue
medical

Examples

Athletes often supplement with creatine phosphate to enhance their performance and recovery.
During intense exercise, creatine phosphate helps regenerate ATP for continued muscle contraction.
Scientists studied the role of creatine phosphate in energy transfer within skeletal muscles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phosphocreatine - another term for creatine phosphate

Etymology

From creatine (from Greek 'kreas', flesh) and phosphate (from Greek 'phosphoros', light-bringing).
