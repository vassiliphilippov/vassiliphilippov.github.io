Gila monster

1. NOUN

/ ˈhiːlə ˌmɑnstər / (AmE), / ˈhiːlə ˌmɒnstə / (BrE)
a large, heavy-bodied lizard with orange and black markings, native to the southwestern United States, known for its venomous bite but not dangerous unless provoked
large orange-black venomous lizard of the southwestern US

Examples

The Gila monster is one of only a few venomous lizards in the world.
While hiking in Arizona, we spotted a brightly colored Gila monster near the trail.
Despite its fearsome name, the Gila monster is usually not aggressive toward humans.

Etymology

From the Gila River in Arizona, USA, where the lizard is commonly found, and 'monster' due to its size and appearance.
