Gulf of Aqaba

1. PROPN

/ ˌɡʌlf əˈɑːkəbə / (AmE), / ˌɡʌlf əˈækəbə / (BrE)
a northeastern extension of the Red Sea located between the Sinai Peninsula and Saudi Arabia
northeastern arm of the Red Sea

Examples

Many tourists visit the Gulf of Aqaba for its world-renowned coral reefs and marine biodiversity.
The borders of Israel, Jordan, Egypt, and Saudi Arabia all meet near the Gulf of Aqaba.
The Gulf of Aqaba is a popular destination for scuba diving and snorkeling enthusiasts.

Etymology

From Arabic, ‘Aqaba’ referring to the adjacent city of Aqaba in Jordan.
