watered-down

1. ADJ

/ ˌwɔtərd ˈdaʊn / (AmE), / ˌwɔːtəd ˈdaʊn / (BrE)
(of a statement, policy, or product) made weaker or less effective by being diluted or toned down.
weakened or diluted in effectiveness

Examples

The committee issued a watered-down statement that avoided addressing the main controversy.
Some fans were disappointed by the watered-down sequel, which lacked the excitement of the original.
The new regulations are a watered-down version of the initial proposal.

Synset Examples

She found the watered-down policy ineffective.
His arguments were too watered-down to persuade anyone.
The watered-down version of the report lacked detail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diluted - made weaker by adding water or another solvent
  • Antonym: full-strength - not diluted; in its original, most powerful form

Etymology

Formed from the literal action of adding water to dilute something, used figuratively from the early 20th century.
