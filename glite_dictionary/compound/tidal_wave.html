tidal wave

1. NOUN

/ ˈtaɪdəl weɪv / (AmE), / ˈtaɪ.dəl weɪv / (BrE)
A sudden and overwhelming surge or outpouring of a particular emotion, event, or phenomenon.
overwhelming surge of emotion or events

Examples

When the news broke, a tidal wave of grief swept through the community.
The company faced a tidal wave of criticism after announcing the changes.
After the accident, he was hit by a tidal wave of guilt and regret.

Synset Examples

a tidal wave of nausea
the flood of letters hit him with the force of a tidal wave
a tidal wave of crime

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flood - an overwhelming quantity or volume of something, especially figurative

2. NOUN

/ ˈtaɪ.dəl weɪv / (AmE), / ˈtaɪ.dəl weɪv / (BrE)
a large wave caused by the periodic movement of tides, due to the gravitational pull of the moon and sun
large wave caused by tides

Examples

The coastal village experiences a tidal wave twice each day, sometimes reaching impressive heights.
Due to the gravitational pull of the moon, a tidal wave swept over the sandbar at noon.
Fishermen must plan their activities to avoid being caught by an incoming tidal wave.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tsunami - a huge sea wave caused by an underwater earthquake or volcanic eruption
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07363294-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈtaɪ.dl ˌweɪv / (AmE), / ˈtaɪ.dl ˌweɪv / (BrE)
an exceptionally large wave or sudden rise of water along the coast, typically caused by a storm, strong winds, or an unusually high tide, often resulting in destruction
an unusually large ocean wave, often destructive

Examples

The devastating tidal wave destroyed dozens of homes along the coastline.
After the hurricane, a massive tidal wave flooded the entire village.
Emergency services warned residents to prepare for a possible tidal wave caused by the storm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tsunami - a long high sea wave caused by an underwater earthquake or volcanic eruption

Etymology

Formed from 'tidal' (relating to tides) and 'wave'.
