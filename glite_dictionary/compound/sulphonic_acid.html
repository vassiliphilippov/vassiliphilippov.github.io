sulphonic acid

1. NOUN

/ sʌlˈfɑnɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / sʌlˈfɒnɪk ˈæsɪd / (BrE)
Any of a group of acids derived from sulfuric acid, containing the group –SO₃H attached to an organic molecule.
acid derived from sulfuric acid

Examples

The chemist synthesized a new sulphonic acid for use in detergent manufacturing.
Some dyes are made using sulphonic acid derivatives to improve water solubility.
Care should be taken when handling concentrated sulphonic acid due to its corrosive properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carboxylic acid - organic acid containing carboxyl group (–COOH)

Etymology

From 'sulphuric acid', influenced by Latin 'sulphur' and '-ic'.
