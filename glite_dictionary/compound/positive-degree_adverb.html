positive-degree adverb

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑzətɪv dɪˈɡriː ˈædvɜrb / (AmE), / ˈpɒzətɪv dɪˈɡriː ˈædvɜːb / (BrE)
An adverb form that expresses a simple quality, without any comparison to more or less of that quality.
adverb indicating simple, non-comparative quality

Examples

The word 'quickly' is often used as a positive-degree adverb in simple descriptions.
In the sentence 'He ran fast', 'fast' functions as a positive-degree adverb.
When no comparison is made, an adverb remains in the positive-degree adverb form.

Synset Examples

An example using a positive-degree adverb that doesn't end in ‘-ly’ would be the adverb ‘fast’ in the sentence, ‘She drove fast’.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: comparative adverb - an adverb indicating comparison (e.g., 'faster', 'sooner')
  • Degree Higher: superlative adverb - an adverb indicating the highest degree (e.g., 'fastest', 'soonest')

Etymology

Formed from 'positive degree' (uncompared form in grammar) + 'adverb'.
