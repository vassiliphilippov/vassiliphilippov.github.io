scrub nurse

1. NOUN

/ skrʌb nɝːs / (AmE), / skrʌb nɜːs / (BrE)
a nurse whose primary role is to assist a surgeon during an operation by maintaining a sterile environment, preparing instruments, and passing equipment to the surgeon.
nurse assisting in surgery
medical

Examples

The scrub nurse quickly prepared the instruments before the patient was brought in for surgery.
During the operation, the scrub nurse handed scalpels and forceps to the surgeon with practiced precision.
It is vital for a scrub nurse to maintain strict sterile procedures throughout the operation.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: circulating nurse - a nurse who assists in surgery by managing overall care but does not handle sterile instruments
