back channel

1. NOUN

/ bæk ˈtʃæn.əl / (AmE), / bak ˈtʃan.əl / (BrE)
a secret or unofficial means of communication, especially used to negotiate or discuss matters privately in diplomacy or government
unofficial secret communication route

Examples

The diplomats established a back channel to discuss the terms away from public scrutiny.
Negotiations progressed swiftly once both sides agreed to use a confidential back channel.
Rumors spread about the existence of a back channel between the two countries' leaders.

Synset Examples

they negotiated via a back channel

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: channel - a means of communication or expression
