dadburn fence

1. NOUN

/ ˌdæd.bɝːn ˈfɛns / (AmE), / ˌdad.bɜːn ˈfɛns / (BrE)
A fence regarded as troublesome, annoying, or causing inconvenience (used chiefly with mild emphasis).
troublesome or annoying fence
informal american

Examples

I wish someone would fix that dadburn fence before it falls down completely.
The cows keep slipping through the old dadburn fence in the back pasture.
He spent all morning arguing with the neighbor about whose job it was to fix the dadburn fence.

Synset Examples

That dadburn fence needs to be repaired.

Etymology

Euphemistic alteration of 'God damn', common in regional American English.
