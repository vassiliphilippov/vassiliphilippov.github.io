sissyfication of the rules

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɪsiˌfɪˈkeɪʃən əv ðə ruːlz / (AmE), / ˌsɪsiˌfɪˈkeɪʃən əv ðə ruːlz / (BrE)
the process of making rules excessively soft, lenient, or lacking in strictness
making rules overly soft or lenient
informal offensive

Examples

Some critics blame recent problems on the sissyfication of the rules in the league.
He argued that the sissyfication of the rules would destroy healthy competition.
Older members complained about the sissyfication of the rules after the changes were announced.

Synset Examples

The sissyfication of the rules frustrated the players.

Etymology

From 'sissy' (slang for someone regarded as weak or effeminate) + '-fication' (forming nouns indicating the action or process of making something a certain way).
