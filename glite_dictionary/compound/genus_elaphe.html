genus Elaphe

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ɪˈlæfi / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ɪˈleɪfi / (BrE)
A genus of nonvenomous snakes known as rat snakes, native to North America and parts of Eurasia.
genus of North American rat snakes

Examples

The genus Elaphe contains several species commonly found in North America.
Many farmers value members of the genus Elaphe for controlling rodent populations.
Some taxonomists have recently reclassified certain snakes previously in the genus Elaphe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rat snake - nonvenomous snake of the genus Elaphe, feeds on rodents

Etymology

From New Latin; Elaphe from Greek elaphē (deer), possibly referring to the ancient belief that these snakes hunted deer.
