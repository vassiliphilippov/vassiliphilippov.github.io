family Rhinotermitidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ˌraɪnoʊtɚˌmɪtɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli ˌrɪnəʊtəˈmɪtɪdiː / (BrE)
A large and widely distributed family of termites found in temperate to tropical regions.
family of termites in warm regions

Examples

Many troublesome pest species belong to the family Rhinotermitidae and can damage wooden structures.
The family Rhinotermitidae includes termites that are common in both urban and natural environments.
Scientists study the family Rhinotermitidae to better understand termite behavior and evolution.

Etymology

From New Latin Rhinotermitidae, based on genus Rhinotermes (from Greek rhino- ‘nose’ + Greek termes ‘woodworm, termite’).
