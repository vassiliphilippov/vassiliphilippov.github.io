entopic pregnancy

1. NOUN

/ ɛnˈtoʊpɪk ˈprɛgnənsi / (AmE), / ɛnˈtɒpɪk ˈprɛgnənsi / (BrE)
a pregnancy that occurs in the uterus, as is typical in normal gestation
pregnancy occurring in the uterus
medical

Examples

Ultrasound confirmed that the patient had an entopic pregnancy developing normally within the uterus.
Doctors contrasted the rare ectopic pregnancies with the typical entopic pregnancy found in most cases.
Management of an entopic pregnancy generally follows standard prenatal care protocols.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ectopic pregnancy - pregnancy occurring outside the uterus

Etymology

From Greek 'entos' (within) + 'topos' (place), contrasting with 'ectopic'.
