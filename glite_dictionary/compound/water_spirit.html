water spirit

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔtər ˌspɪrɪt / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə ˌspɪrɪt / (BrE)
a supernatural being or fairy believed to inhabit water, such as a river, lake, or sea; found in folklore and mythology
mythical being inhabiting water

Examples

Legends tell of a water spirit dwelling beneath the lake's serene surface.
The villagers left offerings on the shore to appease the local water spirit.
Many cultures have stories featuring a mischievous water spirit who lures travelers into the water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: naiad - water nymph in Greek mythology
  • Synonym: mermaid - mythical female creature of the sea with the upper body of a woman and the tail of a fish
