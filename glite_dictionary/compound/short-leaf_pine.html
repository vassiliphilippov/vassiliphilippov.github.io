short-leaf pine

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃɔrt ˌlif paɪn / (AmE), / ˈʃɔːt ˌliːf paɪn / (BrE)
a large pine tree of the southern United States with short needles in clusters of 2–3 and reddish-brown bark when mature
a southern U.S. pine tree with short needles

Examples

The forest was dominated by short-leaf pine and hardwoods.
Researchers studied the growth patterns of short-leaf pine in different soil types.
After the fire, short-leaf pine seedlings began to reappear in the area.
