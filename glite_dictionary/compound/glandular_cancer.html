glandular cancer

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡlæn.dʒələr ˈkæn.sər / (AmE), / ˈɡlænd.jʊ.lə ˈkæn.sə / (BrE)
A malignant tumor that begins in the glandular epithelium of the body.
cancer originating in glandular tissue
medical

Examples

Doctors diagnosed her with glandular cancer after finding a malignant tumor in her breast tissue.
Research into the causes of glandular cancer has led to improved treatments and survival rates.
Some types of glandular cancer can be aggressive and require prompt medical attention.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adenocarcinoma - a malignant tumor formed from glandular structures in epithelial tissue
