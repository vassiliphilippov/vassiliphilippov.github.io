genus Crassostrea

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs kræs.ɒsˈtriː.ə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs kræs.ɒsˈtriː.ə / (BrE)
A genus of oysters that includes species such as the Virginia oyster, important in aquaculture and marine biology.
a genus of edible oysters

Examples

The genus Crassostrea includes several commercially important oyster species used in aquaculture.
Marine biologists study the genus Crassostrea to understand oyster growth and habitat.
The Virginia oyster, a member of the genus Crassostrea, is native to the Atlantic coast of North America.

Etymology

From Latin 'crassus' (thick) and 'ostrea' (oyster), used in biological classification.
