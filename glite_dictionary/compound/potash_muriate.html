potash muriate

1. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊˌtæʃ ˈmjʊriˌeɪt / (AmE), / ˈpɒt.aʃ ˈmjʊəri.eɪt / (BrE)
a chemical compound, potassium chloride (KCl), used especially in medicine to treat potassium deficiency and as a fertilizer
potassium chloride chemical compound
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed potash muriate tablets to restore her electrolyte balance.
Many fertilizers contain potash muriate as a source of potassium for crops.
Patients with low potassium levels may benefit from taking potash muriate supplements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: potassium chloride - the chemical compound KCl, also called potash muriate

Etymology

From 'potash', referring to potassium compounds, and 'muriate', an old term for chloride from Latin 'muriaticus'.
