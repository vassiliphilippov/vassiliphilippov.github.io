Quincke's edema

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɪŋkiz ɪˈdimə / (AmE), / ˈkwɪŋkiz ɪˈdiːmə / (BrE)
A sudden, temporary swelling of the skin or mucous membranes, usually as an allergic reaction, especially affecting young women.
sudden allergic swelling of skin or mucosa
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed her with Quincke's edema after she developed sudden facial swelling.
Allergic reactions to certain foods can sometimes trigger Quincke's edema in susceptible individuals.
Prompt medical attention is crucial when Quincke's edema affects the throat and interferes with breathing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: angioedema - swelling of the deeper layers of the skin, often as an allergic reaction

Etymology

Named after German internist Heinrich Quincke (1842–1922), who first described the condition.
