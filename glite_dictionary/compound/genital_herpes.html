genital herpes

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɛnɪtl ˈhɜrˌpiz / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛnɪtəl ˈhɜːpiːz / (BrE)
A sexually transmitted infection caused by herpes simplex virus type 2, marked by recurrent painful sores or eruptions on the genital area.
sexually transmitted viral infection causing genital sores
medical

Examples

After being diagnosed with genital herpes, she spoke with her doctor about managing outbreaks.
Practicing safe sex reduces the risk of contracting genital herpes.
Many people with genital herpes experience periodic symptoms with long symptom-free intervals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: herpes simplex - a virus that causes infections marked by blisters or sores, including on the mouth or genitals
  • Synonym: cold sore - a sore caused by herpes simplex virus, usually around the mouth

Etymology

From Latin 'genitalis' (pertaining to generation) and Greek 'herpēs' (a creeping, spreading eruption).
