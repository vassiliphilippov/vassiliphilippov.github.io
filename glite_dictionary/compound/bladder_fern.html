bladder fern

1. NOUN

/ ˈblædər fɜrn / (AmE), / ˈblædə fɜːn / (BrE)
A fern of the genus Cystopteris, characterized by a hooded indusium or bladderlike membrane covering the sori.
fern of genus Cystopteris

Examples

The woods behind their house were thick with bladder fern and moss.
Botanists distinguish bladder fern by its delicate fronds and distinctive bladderlike indusium.
She found a patch of bladder ferns growing along the damp stream bank.

Etymology

From the resemblance of its protective membrane to a bladder; 'bladder' + 'fern'.
