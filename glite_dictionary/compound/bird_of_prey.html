bird of prey

1. NOUN

/ bɝd əv preɪ / (AmE), / bɜːd əv preɪ / (BrE)
a bird, such as an eagle or hawk, that hunts, captures, and feeds on other animals
predatory bird such as an eagle or hawk

Examples

The eagle is a magnificent bird of prey found in many parts of the world.
Museums often display exhibits featuring the talons and beaks of birds of prey.
Owls, a type of bird of prey, usually hunt at night using their excellent vision.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: raptor - a bird of prey

2. NOUN

/ ˌbɝːd əv ˈpreɪ / (AmE), / ˌbɜːd əv ˈpreɪ / (BrE)
A bird that hunts, kills, and eats other animals for food, such as an eagle, hawk, or owl.
carnivorous hunting bird

Examples

The bird of prey soared above the valley, searching for a meal.
Owls are nocturnal birds of prey that hunt at night.
The museum featured an exhibit on the talons and beaks of birds of prey.

Synset Examples

In mythology, the roc is a giant bird of prey.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: raptor - a bird of prey, especially one belonging to certain families
  • Antonym: prey - an animal that is hunted and killed for food by another animal
