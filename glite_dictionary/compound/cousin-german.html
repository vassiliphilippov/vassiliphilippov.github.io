cousin-german

1. NOUN

/ ˌkʌz.ən ˈdʒɜr.mən / (AmE), / ˌkʌz.ən ˈdʒɜː.mən / (BrE)
A first cousin; the child of one’s aunt or uncle.
a first cousin
archaic

Examples

She was delighted to find that her cousin-german lived just a few miles away.
In his will, he left most of his estate to his only cousin-german.
The term cousin-german is now considered archaic in everyday English.

Synset Examples

He met his cousin-german for the first time at the wedding.
The term cousin-german refers to a first cousin.
They discovered they were cousin-german after researching their family tree.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: first cousin - a child of one's aunt or uncle

Etymology

From Old French germain (closely related), from Latin germanus ('full, own, genuine').
