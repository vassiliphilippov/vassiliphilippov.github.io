pigeon guillemot

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪdʒən ˈɡɪlɪˌmɑt / (AmE), / ˈpɪdʒɪn ˈɡɪlɪmɒt / (BrE)
a black and white seabird of the northern Pacific, related to the auk family (Cepphus columba), noted for its red feet and legs.
northern Pacific seabird (Cepphus columba)

Examples

A flock of pigeon guillemots gathered on the rocky shore, their red feet flashing in the sunlight.
The pigeon guillemot dives underwater to catch fish and other small marine creatures.
Birdwatchers along the Alaskan coast often spot the distinctive pigeon guillemot during nesting season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guillemot - any of several species of seabird in the auk family

Etymology

'Guillemot' from French 'guillemot', possibly from Guillaume (William); 'pigeon' for its coloration or shape.
