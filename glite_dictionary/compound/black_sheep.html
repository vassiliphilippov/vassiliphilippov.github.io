black sheep

1. NOUN

/ blæk ʃip / (AmE), / blak ʃiːp / (BrE)
A sheep with a black coat, typically considered unusual among a flock of white sheep.
a sheep with a black coat

Examples

The farmer noticed a black sheep among the flock as he counted them.
A black sheep can stand out easily in a herd of white ones.
They bred a rare black sheep for its unique wool color.

2. NOUN

/ blæk ʃip / (AmE), / blak ʃiːp / (BrE)
a person who is considered a disgrace or embarrassment to a family or group, often due to unprincipled or reckless behavior
disgraced or outcast family member

Examples

He became the black sheep of the family after being expelled from college.
Every group has its black sheep, but not all deserve the bad reputation.
She always felt like the black sheep compared to her high-achieving siblings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: outcast - a person who is rejected or excluded
  • Synonym: scapegoat - a person blamed for the wrongdoing of others
  • Antonym: role model - a person looked up to by others as an example to be imitated

Etymology

From the literal use of a black sheep being an unusual and less desirable member of a flock, metaphorically applied since the 18th century.
