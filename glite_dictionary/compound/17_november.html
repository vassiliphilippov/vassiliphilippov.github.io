17 November

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛvənˈtiːn ˌnəʊˈvɛmbər / (AmE), / ˌsɛv.ənˈtiːn nəʊˈvɛmbə / (BrE)
A Marxist-Leninist terrorist organization that operated in Greece from 1975 to 2002, violently opposing imperialism, capitalism, NATO, and the United States.
Greek Marxist-Leninist terrorist group (1975–2002)

Examples

The Greek authorities arrested several members of the 17 November group in the early 2000s.
17 November carried out a series of attacks targeting U.S. and British officials in Athens.
Many books have analyzed the motivations and history of 17 November in the context of postwar Greece.

Etymology

The group's name refers to 17 November 1973, the date of the Athens Polytechnic uprising against the military junta in Greece.
