babies' slippers

1. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪbiz ˈslɪpɚz / (AmE), / ˈbeɪbiːz ˈslɪpəz / (BrE)
A European forage plant with claw-shaped pods, introduced in America.
European forage plant with claw-shaped pods

Examples

Farmers sometimes plant babies' slippers as a forage crop for livestock.
The peculiar pods of babies' slippers give the plant its common name.
Babies' slippers has adapted well to many North American climates.
