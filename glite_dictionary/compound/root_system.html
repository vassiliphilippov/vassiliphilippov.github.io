root system

1. NOUN

/ rut ˈsɪstəm / (AmE), / ruːt ˈsɪstəm / (BrE)
the entire network of roots of a plant, including all primary and secondary roots
network of a plant's roots

Examples

The oak tree has a vast root system that anchors it firmly to the ground.
A healthy root system allows plants to absorb more water and nutrients.
Damage to the root system can stunt the growth of a plant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: roots - the parts of a plant that typically lie below the surface, absorbing water and nutrients
