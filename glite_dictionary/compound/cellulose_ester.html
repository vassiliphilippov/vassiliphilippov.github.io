cellulose ester

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛl.jəˌloʊs ˈɛstər / (AmE), / ˈsɛljʊləʊs ˈɛstə / (BrE)
any compound formed by treating cellulose with an acid, resulting in an ester; used especially in plastics, films, and fibers.
an ester derived from cellulose and an acid

Examples

Some photographic films are made with cellulose ester bases instead of traditional cellulose nitrate.
The laboratory synthesized a new type of cellulose ester for use in biodegradable plastics.
Adding a cellulose ester can improve the strength and flexibility of coatings.

Etymology

Formed from 'cellulose' (from French ‘cellulose’), and 'ester', a chemical term from German ‘Ester’.
