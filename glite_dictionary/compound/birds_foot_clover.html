bird's foot clover

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɝdz fʊt ˈkloʊvɚ / (AmE), / ˈbɜːdz fʊt ˈkləʊvə / (BrE)
A European plant with claw-shaped pods, used as forage, now also found in America.
European forage plant with claw-shaped pods

Examples

The field was covered in bird's foot clover, providing excellent grazing for the cattle.
Gardeners often plant bird's foot clover to improve soil fertility through nitrogen fixation.
The claw-shaped pods of bird's foot clover make the plant easy to identify in the wild.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lotus - a genus of plants to which bird's foot clover belongs

Etymology

Named for the shape of its seed pods, which resemble a bird's foot.
