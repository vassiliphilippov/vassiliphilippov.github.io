Cabernet Sauvignon grape

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæbərˌneɪ soʊˈviːnjən ɡreɪp / (AmE), / ˌkæb.ə.neɪ səʊˈviː.njɒ̃ ɡreɪp / (BrE)
a variety of superior red wine grape, originally from the Bordeaux region of France, widely grown for making Cabernet Sauvignon wine
red wine grape variety

Examples

The winery is known for its exceptional Cabernet Sauvignon grape harvests each year.
Many vineyards in California specialize in cultivating the Cabernet Sauvignon grape for red wine production.
The flavor profile of a Cabernet Sauvignon grape differs depending on the region in which it is grown.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Merlot grape - another major red wine grape variety, also from Bordeaux
  • Antonym: Chardonnay grape - major white wine grape variety

Etymology

French, from 'Cabernet' (name of a grape variety) and 'Sauvignon' (from 'sauvage', wild).
