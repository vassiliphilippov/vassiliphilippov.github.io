family Haemulidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈhæmjʊˌlaɪdi / (AmE), / ˌhiːmjʊˈlɪːdiː / (BrE)
a family of fishes known as grunts, typically found in warm seas and recognized by their ability to produce grunting sounds
family of fishes called grunts

Examples

Researchers discovered three new species within the family Haemulidae during the expedition.
Fishes belonging to the family Haemulidae are often recognized by the grunting sounds they produce.
The family Haemulidae includes many reef-dwelling fish found in the Atlantic and Pacific Oceans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grunt - a common name for fish in the family Haemulidae

Etymology

From Haemulidae, modern Latin, from Greek haimoulos (bloody), referring to the red coloration in some species.
