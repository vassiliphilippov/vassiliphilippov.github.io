pulse modulation

1. NOUN

/ pʌls ˌmɑdʒəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / pʌls ˌmɒdjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
A method of modulating a signal by varying aspects of a train of pulses, such as their duration, position, or amplitude, to encode information.
modulation technique using pulse trains

Examples

The engineer explained how pulse modulation is used to transmit digital data over radio signals.
Modern communication systems often rely on pulse modulation for efficient data encoding and transmission.
The textbook devoted an entire chapter to the different types of pulse modulation.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: amplitude modulation - modulation by varying the amplitude of the carrier wave
  • Antonym: frequency modulation - modulation by varying the frequency of the carrier wave

Etymology

From 'pulse' (a transient change in voltage or current) and 'modulation' (from Latin 'modulatio', a measuring or regulating).
