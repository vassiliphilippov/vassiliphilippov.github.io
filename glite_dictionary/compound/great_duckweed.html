great duckweed

1. NOUN

/ ɡreɪt ˈdʌkˌwid / (AmE), / ɡreɪt ˈdʌkwiːd / (BrE)
A widespread aquatic plant (Spirodela polyrhiza) with small floating leaves, found in many parts of the world except South America, New Zealand, and some oceanic islands.
widespread floating aquatic plant

Examples

Ponds covered with great duckweed provide habitat for insects and small fish.
Researchers studied the growth rate of great duckweed under different light conditions.
Large mats of great duckweed can reduce oxygen levels in stagnant water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: duckweed - any plant of the Lemnaceae family, small floating aquatic plants
