false dogwood

1. NOUN

/ fɔls ˈdɔɡˌwʊd / (AmE), / fɔːls ˈdɒɡˌwʊd / (BrE)
an evergreen shrub or small tree of tropical America bearing pulpy fruit that contains saponin, traditionally used as soap by Native Americans
a tropical American evergreen with saponin-rich fruit

Examples

The fruit of the false dogwood was once used as a natural soap by indigenous peoples.
A tall false dogwood grew in the corner of the botanical garden, its branches heavy with red berries.
Researchers are studying the potential medicinal uses of compounds extracted from false dogwood.
