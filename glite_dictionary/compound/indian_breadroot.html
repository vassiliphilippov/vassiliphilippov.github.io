Indian breadroot

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪndiən ˈbrɛdˌruːt / (AmE), / ˈɪndiən ˈbrɛdˌruːt / (BrE)
A perennial plant native to central North America, known for its densely hairy stems and edible tuberous roots.
North American plant with edible roots

Examples

Early settlers learned to dig for Indian breadroot during times of food scarcity.
The Indian breadroot was an important food source for several Native American tribes.
Botanists study the habitat requirements of Indian breadroot to aid conservation efforts.
