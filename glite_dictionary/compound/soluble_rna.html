soluble RNA

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɒl.jə.bəl ˌɑːr.enˈeɪ / (AmE), / ˈsɒl.jʊ.bəl ˌɑːr.enˈeɪ / (BrE)
transfer RNA; a type of RNA molecule in cells that attaches specific amino acids to a growing protein chain during synthesis, according to genetic directions coded in messenger RNA.
type of RNA involved in protein synthesis
medical

Examples

The scientist studied the role of soluble RNA in assembling amino acids during protein synthesis.
Each type of soluble RNA carries a specific amino acid to the ribosome.
Mutations in soluble RNA can disrupt normal protein production in cells.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transfer RNA - RNA molecule that helps decode mRNA into a protein

Etymology

So-called for its ability to remain soluble in cellular extracts; also known as transfer RNA (tRNA).
