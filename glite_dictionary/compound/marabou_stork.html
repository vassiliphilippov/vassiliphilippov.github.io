marabou stork

1. NOUN

/ ˈmærəˌbu ˌstɔrk / (AmE), / ˈmærəbuː ˌstɔːk / (BrE)
a large African stork (Leptoptilos crumeniferus) with black and white plumage, known for feeding on carrion; its soft underwing feathers are often used to trim garments
large African carrion-eating stork

Examples

The marabou stork can often be seen scavenging on the African savanna.
Feathers from the marabou stork are used to make elegant trimmings for hats and dresses.
A flock of marabou storks gathered near the riverbank to feed on the remains left by predators.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: stork - large, long-legged birds of the family Ciconiidae

Etymology

From French 'marabout,' from Arabic 'murābiṭ' (hermit, holy man), possibly in reference to the bird's solitary habits.
