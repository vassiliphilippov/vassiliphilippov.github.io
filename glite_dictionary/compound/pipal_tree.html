pipal tree

1. NOUN

/ ˈpiːpəl triː / (AmE), / ˈpiːpəl triː / (BrE)
A large fig tree native to India, known for its longevity and sacred status in Buddhism; it does not develop prop roots like the banyan.
sacred Indian fig tree (Ficus religiosa)

Examples

In many temples, a pipal tree is planted to provide shade and to symbolize enlightenment.
The pipal tree is often found near Buddhist monasteries and revered by the monks.
He rested under the ancient pipal tree to escape the midday sun.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: banyan - Indian fig tree that develops prop roots

Etymology

From Hindi 'pīpal', referring to Ficus religiosa.
