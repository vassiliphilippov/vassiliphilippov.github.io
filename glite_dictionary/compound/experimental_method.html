experimental method

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˌspɛrɪˈmɛntəl ˈmɛθəd / (AmE), / ɪkˌspɛrɪˈmɛntəl ˈmɛθəd / (BrE)
a systematic procedure involving controlled observations and measurements used to test scientific hypotheses
procedure for testing scientific hypotheses

Examples

The experimental method is essential for obtaining reliable results in scientific research.
Many psychologists rely on the experimental method to establish cause-and-effect relationships.
Through careful use of the experimental method, the team confirmed their hypothesis.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: scientific method - a broader process for scientific inquiry involving observation, hypothesis, experimentation, and analysis
