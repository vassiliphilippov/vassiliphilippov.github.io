grey matter

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡreɪ ˌmætər / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪ ˌmætə / (BrE)
the darker tissue of the brain and spinal cord, consisting mainly of nerve cell bodies and branching dendrites, forming regions such as the cerebral cortex
brain tissue containing nerve cell bodies

Examples

The disease can damage grey matter in the brain, leading to memory loss.
Scientists measured the amount of grey matter in different parts of the cortex.
Loss of grey matter is associated with certain neurological disorders.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: white matter - brain tissue composed mainly of myelinated nerve fibers

2. NOUN

/ ɡreɪ ˈmæt̬ɚ / (AmE), / ɡreɪ ˈmætə / (BrE)
The darker tissue of the brain and spinal cord, consisting mainly of nerve cell bodies and dendrites, responsible for processing information; informally, intelligence or brainpower.
the brain's processing tissue; intelligence

Examples

After the puzzle competition, I felt like my grey matter had been given a real workout.
Recent studies link increased grey matter volume with higher memory scores.
You need to use your grey matter to solve this tricky riddle.

Synset Examples

Scientists study how grey matter affects cognitive function.

Exercise is good for your grey matter and overall health.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: white matter - the paler tissue of the brain and spinal cord, mainly containing nerve fibers with myelin sheaths
