economic policy

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛkəˈnɑmɪk ˈpɑləsi / (AmE), / ˌiːkəˈnɒmɪk ˈpɒləsi / (BrE)
A government's plan or course of action intended to influence or control the economy, particularly regarding growth, taxation, and overall economic management.
government strategy for managing the economy

Examples

The government's new economic policy aims to boost job growth and reduce inflation.
Debates over economic policy often focus on balancing tax rates and public spending.
Successful economic policy requires careful analysis of both domestic and international markets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fiscal policy - government policy on taxation and public spending
  • Synonym: monetary policy - government or central bank policy on money supply and interest rates
