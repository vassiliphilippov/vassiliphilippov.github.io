type O

1. NOUN

/ taɪp oʊ / (AmE), / taɪp əʊ / (BrE)
the blood group whose red cells lack both A and B antigens, making it a universal donor type.
blood group lacking A and B antigens
medical

Examples

The blood bank urgently needs more donations from individuals with type O blood.
Patients with type O are considered universal donors for transfusions.
He learned during his physical that he has type O blood.

Synset Examples

people with type O blood are universal donors

Related Terms

  • Antonym: type A - blood group with A antigens
  • Antonym: type B - blood group with B antigens
  • Antonym: type AB - blood group with both A and B antigens
