throat sweetbread

1. NOUN

/ ˈθroʊt ˈswitˌbrɛd / (AmE), / ˈθrəʊt ˈswiːtbɹɛd / (BrE)
the edible thymus gland of a young animal, especially a calf or lamb, used as food
thymus gland of an animal used as food

Examples

The chef carefully prepared the throat sweetbread with a delicate white wine sauce.
Many French recipes call for throat sweetbread as a prized delicacy in traditional dishes.
She ordered roasted throat sweetbread at the fine dining restaurant, curious to try something new.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sweetbread - the edible glands (thymus or pancreas) of an animal used as food

Etymology

From 'throat' (the front part of the neck) and 'sweetbread' (culinary term for certain edible glands).
