self-disgust

1. NOUN

/ sɛlf dɪsˈɡʌst / (AmE), / sɛlf dɪsˈɡʌst / (BrE)
a strong feeling of shame or revulsion directed toward oneself or one's actions
feeling of shame and revulsion at oneself

Examples

He struggled for years with intense self-disgust after making those poor choices.
Feelings of self-disgust can sometimes lead to withdrawal from social situations.
The character's journey is marked by moments of self-disgust and eventual acceptance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: self-loathing - intense hatred of oneself
  • Antonym: self-esteem - confidence and satisfaction in oneself
