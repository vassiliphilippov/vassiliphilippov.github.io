stovepipe hat

1. NOUN

/ ˈstoʊvˌpaɪp ˌhæt / (AmE), / ˈstəʊvˌpaɪp ˌhæt / (BrE)
a tall, cylindrical hat with straight sides, typically worn as formal dress, especially in the 19th century
tall, cylindrical formal hat

Examples

Abraham Lincoln is famously depicted wearing a stovepipe hat in many portraits.
The magician reached into his stovepipe hat and pulled out a white rabbit.
For the historical reenactment, he rented a black suit and a stovepipe hat.

Synset Examples

He wore a stovepipe hat to the costume party.
The magician's stovepipe hat was filled with colorful scarves.
In the play, the character donned a tall stovepipe hat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: top hat - a tall, flat-crowned, broad-brimmed formal hat
