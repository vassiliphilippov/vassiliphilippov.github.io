judicial separation

1. NOUN

/ dʒuˈdɪʃəl ˌsɛpəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / dʒuˈdɪʃəl ˌsɛpəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
A legal decree by a court specifying the rights and responsibilities of a married couple who are living apart but remain legally married.
legal separation of married couple by court order
legal

Examples

After years of conflict, they decided a judicial separation was the best option for their family.
A judicial separation allows spouses to live apart without ending their marriage entirely.
The court granted a judicial separation, outlining arrangements for the children and finances.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: divorce - the legal dissolution of a marriage

Etymology

From Latin 'judicialis' (of judgment) and 'separatio' (a dividing).
