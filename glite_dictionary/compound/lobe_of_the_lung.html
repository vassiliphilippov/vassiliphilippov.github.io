lobe of the lung

1. NOUN

/ loʊb əv ðə lʌŋ / (AmE), / ləʊb ɒv ðə lʌŋ / (BrE)
any of the distinct sections into which either the right or left lung is divided; the right lung has three lobes and the left lung has two
distinct section of a lung
medical

Examples

The right lung is divided into three lobes of the lung, each with distinct functions.
An infection may affect only one lobe of the lung, such as in cases of lobar pneumonia.
Surgeons removed the damaged lobe of the lung during the operation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lung - either of the two large organs in the chest that you use for breathing
  • Synonym: lobe - a rounded projection or division of a bodily organ or structure
