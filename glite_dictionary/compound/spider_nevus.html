spider nevus

1. NOUN

/ ˈspaɪdər ˈnivəs / (AmE), / ˈspaɪdə ˈniːvəs / (BrE)
a small, dilated blood vessel on the skin with a central red spot and radiating capillaries, resembling a spider's shape
dilated skin blood vessel with radiating branches
medical

Examples

The dermatologist identified a spider nevus on the patient's cheek during the examination.
Chronic liver disease can often lead to the appearance of multiple spider nevi on the skin.
A spider nevus is easily recognized by its central red spot and radiating vessels.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: telangiectasia - a general term for dilated small blood vessels near the surface of the skin
