external iliac artery

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˈstɜrnəl ˈɪli.æk ˈɑr.t̬əri / (AmE), / ɪkˈstɜː.nəl ˈɪl.i.æk ˈɑː.təri / (BrE)
either of the two main arteries located on each side of the body that branch from the common iliac artery and continue as the femoral artery in the thigh.
major artery branching to the thigh
medical

Examples

The surgeon carefully located the external iliac artery before proceeding with the bypass graft.
Injury to the external iliac artery can result in significant bleeding and requires immediate attention.
The external iliac artery supplies blood to the lower limb after becoming the femoral artery.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: femoral artery - major artery of the thigh that the external iliac artery becomes
  • Degree Higher: common iliac artery - the parent artery from which the external iliac artery branches

Etymology

From Latin: 'externus' (outer), 'iliacus' (pertaining to the ilium, part of the hip bone), 'arteria' (artery).
