wendy house

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɛndi haʊs / (AmE), / ˈwɛndi haʊs / (BrE)
A small playhouse, usually for children, made to look like a miniature house they can play inside of.
small playhouse for children
british

Examples

The children spent all afternoon playing make-believe in their wendy house in the garden.
We bought a pink wendy house for our daughter's birthday last year.
Toys and books were scattered across the floor of the wendy house.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: playhouse - a small house for children to play in

Etymology

From the character Wendy Darling's playhouse in J. M. Barrie's 1904 play 'Peter Pan'.
