durn shame

1. NOUN

/ dɝn ʃeɪm / (AmE), / dɜːn ʃeɪm / (BrE)
An expression of disappointment or regret, often used informally or in regional dialects.
expression of disappointment
informal

Examples

It's a durn shame they canceled the concert.
What a durn shame that the store is closed already.
It's a durn shame you couldn't make it to the party.

Synset Examples

It's a durn shame they canceled the concert.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crying shame - something that causes strong disappointment or regret
  • Synonym: damn shame - an expression of strong disappointment or misfortune

Etymology

'Durn' is a dialectal or euphemistic variant of 'damn'.
