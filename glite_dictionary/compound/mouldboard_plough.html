mouldboard plough

1. NOUN

/ ˈmoʊld.bɔrd plaʊ / (AmE), / ˈməʊld.bɔːd plaʊ / (BrE)
a plough with a curved metal blade (mouldboard) that turns over the soil
plough with a curved blade for turning soil
british

Examples

The farmer used a mouldboard plough to break up the compacted earth before planting.
Compared to traditional tools, the mouldboard plough made soil preparation faster and more efficient.
Many rural museums display an old mouldboard plough as a symbol of agricultural history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plow - farm tool for turning over soil

Etymology

From Old English *plōh* (plough) and mouldboard, the curved metal board that turns the soil.
