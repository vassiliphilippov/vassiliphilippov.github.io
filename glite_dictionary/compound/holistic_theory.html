holistic theory

1. NOUN

/ hoʊˈlɪstɪk ˈθɪəri / (AmE), / həʊˈlɪstɪk ˈθɪəri / (BrE)
The theory that the parts of any whole cannot exist or be understood except in relation to the whole.
theory that parts are understood only through the whole

Examples

The holistic theory suggests that a living organism should be studied as an integrated whole rather than a collection of separate parts.
When discussing language, linguists often refer to holistic theory to explain how meaning emerges from context.
Many environmentalists adopt a holistic theory when analyzing the interaction of ecosystems.

Synset Examples

holism holds that the whole is greater than the sum of its parts
holistic theory has been applied to ecology and language and mental states

Related Terms

  • Synonym: holism - philosophical principle that systems must be viewed as wholes
  • Antonym: reductionism - theory that complex systems can be understood by reducing them to their parts
