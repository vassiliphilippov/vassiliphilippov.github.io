Little League

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪt̬l ˌliːɡ / (AmE), / ˈlɪtl ˌliːɡ / (BrE)
a youth baseball league for children aged 8 to 12, often commercially sponsored and structured under a national or regional organization
youth baseball league for ages 8–12
american

Examples

My son plays shortstop in the local Little League team every summer.
The Little League World Series attracts teams from all over the world.
She coached Little League for several years before moving on to high school baseball.

Etymology

From 'little' (small, young) and 'league' (an association), originally trademarked by Little League Baseball, Inc., founded in Pennsylvania in 1939.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɪɾl̩ liːɡ / (AmE), / ˈlɪt.əl liːɡ / (BrE)
an organized baseball and softball association for children, providing teams, competitions, and instruction in the sport
youth baseball and softball organization

Examples

Every Saturday morning, parents gather to watch their children play Little League.
She still keeps the trophy her team won in Little League as a child.
Many professional athletes started their careers in Little League.

Synset Examples

She volunteers as a coach for the local Little League.
The town's Little League team won the championship last year.
He signed up his son for Little League baseball this summer.

Etymology

Founded in 1939 in Pennsylvania, USA, 'Little League' refers specifically to the Little League Baseball organization.
