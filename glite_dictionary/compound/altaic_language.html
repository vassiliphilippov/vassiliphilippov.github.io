Altaic language

1. NOUN

/ ælˈteɪɪk ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ælˈteɪɪk ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
any of a group of languages historically proposed as a family, spoken in Asia and southeastern Europe, including Turkic, Mongolic, and Tungusic languages, though their genetic relationship is disputed.
a group of languages spoken in Asia and southeastern Europe

Examples

Many scholars have debated whether Turkish and Mongolian are truly Altaic languages.
The theory connecting Korean and Japanese to Altaic languages remains controversial among linguists.
He studied the grammatical structures of several Altaic languages for his thesis.

Etymology

From Altai, a mountain range in Central Asia, combined with 'language.'
