buck fever

1. NOUN

/ bʌk ˈfiːvɚ / (AmE), / bʌk ˈfiːvə / (BrE)
Nervous excitement or anxiety experienced by an inexperienced hunter, especially at the sight of game.
nervous excitement of a hunter

Examples

He missed his shot because buck fever got the best of him.
Many novice hunters struggle with buck fever on their first hunt.
She tried calming her nerves, but buck fever made her hands shake uncontrollably.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stage fright - nervousness felt before giving a performance

Etymology

From 'buck' (male deer) + 'fever' (agitated excitement).
