ataractic drug

1. NOUN

/ ˌætəˈræktɪk drʌɡ / (AmE), / ˌatəˈraktɪk drʌɡ / (BrE)
A drug that is used to reduce anxiety or tension without causing significant impairment of mental clarity.
drug that eases tension without mental dulling
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed an ataractic drug to help manage her anxiety without affecting her alertness.
Researchers are investigating new types of ataractic drugs with fewer side effects than traditional tranquilizers.
He responded well to the ataractic drug, remaining calm yet mentally attentive during therapy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tranquilizer - a drug used to reduce tension or anxiety
  • Synonym: sedative - a drug promoting calm or inducing sleep
  • Antonym: stimulant - a drug that increases activity in the body or nervous system

Etymology

From Greek ataraktos (impassive, calm).
