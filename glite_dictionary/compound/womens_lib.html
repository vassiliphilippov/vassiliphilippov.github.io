women's lib

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪmɪnz lɪb / (AmE), / ˈwɪmɪnz lɪb / (BrE)
the movement for equal rights and opportunities for women, especially as promoted during the late 20th century
movement for women's equality

Examples

The rise of women's lib in the 1970s transformed attitudes toward gender roles in society.
Some critics dismissed women's lib as unnecessary, but its impact was undeniable.
My grandmother was inspired by the women's lib movement to return to work after raising her family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: feminism - the advocacy of women's rights on the basis of the equality of the sexes

Etymology

Shortened form of 'women's liberation,' first used in the 1960s in the United States.
