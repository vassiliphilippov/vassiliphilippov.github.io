snow mound

1. NOUN

/ snoʊ maʊnd / (AmE), / snəʊ maʊnd / (BrE)
a pile or heap of snow, usually accumulated naturally or by shoveling or plowing
a pile or heap of snow

Examples

After a heavy storm, a large snow mound formed at the end of our driveway.
The children climbed up the snow mound and slid back down, laughing.
City workers removed the snow mound from the sidewalk to keep it clear.

Synset Examples

The children played on the snow mound in winter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snowdrift - a bank of snow piled up by the wind
