bladder fucus

1. NOUN

/ ˈblædər ˈfjuːkəs / (AmE), / ˈblædə ˈfjuːkəs / (BrE)
a common species of brown seaweed (Fucus vesiculosus) with air-filled bladders, often found on rocky shores and used in making kelp and as fertilizer
a type of brown seaweed (Fucus vesiculosus)

Examples

The rocky shoreline was covered in bladder fucus, making it slippery to walk on.
Farmers sometimes collect bladder fucus to use as a natural fertilizer for their fields.
Scientists are studying compounds found in bladder fucus for potential health benefits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rockweed - a type of seaweed found attached to rocks, especially in coastal areas

Etymology

From 'bladder', referring to the air bladders found on the algae, and modern Latin 'Fucus', the genus name.
