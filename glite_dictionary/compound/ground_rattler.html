ground rattler

1. NOUN

/ ɡraʊnd ˈrætlɚ / (AmE), / ɡraʊnd ˈræt.lə / (BrE)
A small species of rattlesnake native to the southeastern United States, also known as the pygmy rattlesnake.
small North American rattlesnake

Examples

Hikers should be careful not to step on a ground rattler hiding in the underbrush.
The ground rattler is much smaller than most other rattlesnakes but still venomous.
We spotted a ground rattler basking on the warm road in the early morning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pygmy rattlesnake - a small rattlesnake species (Sistrurus miliarius)
  • Degree Higher: rattlesnake - venomous American snakes with a rattling tail
