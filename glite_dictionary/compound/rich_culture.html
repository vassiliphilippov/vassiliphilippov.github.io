rich culture

1. NOUN

/ rɪtʃ ˈkʌltʃər / (AmE), / rɪtʃ ˈkʌltʃə / (BrE)
the diverse, vibrant, and valuable traditions, customs, and achievements of a society or group
diverse and valuable cultural heritage

Examples

The museum highlights the rich culture of the ancient civilization with artifacts and art.
Tourists are attracted by the rich culture and history of the region.
Her family preserved their rich culture through music, language, and traditional festivals.

Synset Examples

The festival celebrated the rich culture of the Irisher.
Vietnam is known for its rich culture and history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cultural heritage - the legacy of tangible and intangible cultural assets of a group or society
  • Degree Lower: culture - the ideas, customs, and social behavior of a particular people or society
