lath and plaster

1. NOUN

/ læθ ənd ˈplæstər / (AmE), / lɑːθ ənd ˈplɑːstə / (BrE)
A building material and technique consisting of thin strips of wood (laths) covered with a layer of plaster, used especially for interior walls and ceilings.
traditional wall/ceiling material of wood laths and plaster

Examples

The old house was constructed with lath and plaster walls, which are common in buildings from that era.
During renovations, we discovered the original lath and plaster behind the drywall.
The craftsman carefully repaired the damaged section of lath and plaster on the ceiling.
