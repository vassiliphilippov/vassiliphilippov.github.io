Hudsonian godwit

1. NOUN

/ hʌdˈsoʊni.ən ˈɡɒdwɪt / (AmE), / hʌdˈsəʊni.ən ˈɡɒdwɪt / (BrE)
A large migratory shorebird of the Americas, known for its long, slightly upturned bill and long legs.
large migratory American shorebird

Examples

We spotted a Hudsonian godwit among the other shorebirds on the mudflats.
During migration, the Hudsonian godwit travels thousands of miles between North and South America.
The distinctive long bill of the Hudsonian godwit helps it forage for food in shallow waters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: godwit - a type of large wading bird with a long bill

Etymology

Named after Hudson Bay in Canada and the term 'godwit', possibly from Middle English 'godewihte'.
