ecclesiastical mode

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌkliːziˈæstɪkəl moʊd / (AmE), / ɪˌkliːzɪˈæstɪkəl məʊd / (BrE)
any of a set of musical modes used in Gregorian chant and other church music, developed historically from Greek modes and used up to about 1600.
musical mode used in Gregorian chant

Examples

The melody was composed in the ecclesiastical mode known as Dorian, providing a solemn atmosphere.
Students studied the structure of each ecclesiastical mode to better understand Gregorian chant.
The use of the ecclesiastical mode distinguishes medieval liturgical music from later harmonic systems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mode - a system or scale of musical notes with a specific pattern of intervals

Etymology

From ecclesiastical (relating to the Church) + mode, in reference to the church's use in medieval music.
