half-dugout

1. NOUN

/ hæf ˈdʌɡaʊt / (AmE), / hɑːf ˈdʌɡaʊt / (BrE)
A primitive shelter, typically temporary, with part of its structure dug into the ground and usually topped with sod walls and a sod roof.
partly underground primitive shelter

Examples

The pioneers built a half-dugout as a temporary home until their cabin was finished.
During the storm, we took cover in an old half-dugout on the prairie.
Early settlers often lived in half-dugouts because they were quick to construct with minimal materials.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: dugout - A shelter or structure fully dug into the ground for protection or habitation

Etymology

Formed from 'half' + 'dugout', referring to a shelter partially excavated into the ground.
