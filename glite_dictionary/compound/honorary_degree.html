honorary degree

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːnəˌrɛri dɪˈɡriː / (AmE), / ˈɒnərəri dɪˈɡriː / (BrE)
A degree awarded by a university or college to honor someone's achievements, without the usual requirements of study and examinations.
degree awarded as an honor

Examples

The university awarded her an honorary degree in recognition of her contributions to science.
Many celebrities have received an honorary degree despite not attending college.
He was thrilled to accept the honorary degree at the graduation ceremony.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: academic degree - a qualification awarded on successful completion of a course of study at a university or college

Etymology

From Latin honorarius (of honor) + degree, from Latin gradus (step, grade).
