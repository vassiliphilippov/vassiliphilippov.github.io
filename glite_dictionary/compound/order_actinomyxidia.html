order Actinomyxidia

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdər ˌæktɪnoʊmɪkˈsɪdiə / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə ˌæktɪnəʊmɪkˈsɪdɪə / (BrE)
a taxonomic order of parasitic organisms that infect worms, characterized by spores with trivalve shells and three polar capsules
parasitic order of protozoa infecting worms

Examples

Researchers discovered a new species in the order Actinomyxidia infecting the intestines of freshwater worms.
The spores produced by members of the order Actinomyxidia have a distinct trivalve shell structure.
Parasitologists study the order Actinomyxidia to understand diseases affecting aquatic invertebrates.

Etymology

From Greek 'aktis' (ray) and 'myxa' (mucus), with scientific suffix '-idia'.
