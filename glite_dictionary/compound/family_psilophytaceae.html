family Psilophytaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæmli saɪˌloʊfaɪˈteɪsiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmli saɪˌlɒfaɪˈtiːsiː / (BrE)
An extinct family of primitive vascular plants from the Paleozoic era, among the earliest known land plants.
extinct family of early land plants

Examples

The family Psilophytaceae includes some of the earliest known vascular plants from the Paleozoic era.
Fossils of the family Psilophytaceae have contributed greatly to our understanding of plant evolution.
Scientists often study the family Psilophytaceae to learn about the transition of plants from water to land.

Etymology

Modern Latin; from Greek 'psilos' (bare) + 'phyton' (plant), referring to the simple structure of the plants.
