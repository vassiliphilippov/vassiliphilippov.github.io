clerical collar

1. NOUN

/ ˈklɛrɪkəl ˈkɑlər / (AmE), / ˈklɛrɪkəl ˈkɒlə / (BrE)
a stiff, white, band-like collar worn by Christian clergy as a distinctive symbol of their office
white band-like collar worn by clergy

Examples

The priest adjusted his clerical collar before entering the church.
She recognized the minister by his distinctive clerical collar and black robes.
During the ceremony, the bishop wore a white clerical collar with his vestments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dog collar - another term for clerical collar, often informal
