monosodium glutamate

1. NOUN

/ ˌmoʊnoʊˌsoʊdiəm ˈɡluːtəˌmeɪt / (AmE), / ˌmɒnəˌsəʊdiəm ˈɡluːtəˌmeɪt / (BrE)
A white crystalline compound used as a flavor enhancer in foods, especially in Asian cuisine.
food additive and flavor enhancer

Examples

Many processed snacks contain monosodium glutamate to improve their taste.
Some people avoid monosodium glutamate due to concerns about its effects on health.
You will often find monosodium glutamate listed as 'flavor enhancer' on food labels.

Synset Examples

food manufacturers sometimes list MSG simply as ‘artificial flavors’ in ingredient lists

Related Terms

  • Synonym: MSG - abbreviation for monosodium glutamate

Etymology

Formed from 'mono-' (one), 'sodium,' and 'glutamate' (from glutamic acid).
