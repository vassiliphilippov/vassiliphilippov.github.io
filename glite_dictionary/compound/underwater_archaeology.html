underwater archaeology

1. NOUN

/ ˌʌndərˌwɔtər ˌɑːrkiˈɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˌwɔːtə ˌɑːkiˈɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the study of human history and prehistory through the exploration and analysis of submerged sites and artifacts
the study of submerged archaeological sites

Examples

She studied underwater archaeology to help uncover ancient shipwrecks in the Mediterranean Sea.
The museum's new exhibit highlights discoveries made through underwater archaeology.
Advances in technology have revolutionized underwater archaeology, making deep-sea exploration more accessible.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: archaeology - the study of human history through excavation and analysis of artifacts

Etymology

From 'underwater' (beneath the surface of water) and 'archaeology', from Greek 'arkhaiologia' (study of ancient things).
