Battle of Ypres

1. NOUN

/ ˈjipɹə z / (AmE), / ˈiːprə z / (BrE)
any of several major battles during World War I fought near the town of Ypres in Belgium, notable for the first large-scale use of poison gas by the Germans.
major World War I battles at Ypres, Belgium

Examples

The Battle of Ypres marked a turning point in the use of chemical warfare.
Many soldiers lost their lives during the Battle of Ypres in 1915.
Poison gas was first used on a large scale in the Battle of Ypres.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: First Battle of Ypres - the first major battle at Ypres during World War I
  • Degree Higher: Second Battle of Ypres - the second major battle at Ypres during World War I, first use of poison gas

Etymology

From Ypres, the name of the Belgian town where the battles took place.

2. PROPN

/ ˈbæɾəl əv ˈiːprə / (AmE), / ˈbætl əv ˈiːprə / (BrE)
any of several significant battles during World War I near Ypres in Belgium, especially known for intense and often indecisive fighting between Allied and German forces.
World War I battles near Ypres, Belgium

Examples

Many soldiers lost their lives during the Battle of Ypres in World War I.
The Battle of Ypres marked one of the earliest uses of chemical weapons in warfare.
Historians still debate the strategic impact of the Battle of Ypres on the outcome of the war.

Etymology

From the name of the town Ypres in Belgium, where the battles took place.
