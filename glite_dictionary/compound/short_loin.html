short loin

1. NOUN

/ ʃɔrt lɔɪn / (AmE), / ʃɔːt lɔɪn / (BrE)
the section of the loin in beef from which porterhouse and T-bone steaks are cut, located just behind the rib section.
section of beef loin for steaks

Examples

The chef ordered a whole short loin to prepare a variety of steaks for the evening menu.
Porterhouse and T-bone steaks are both cut from the short loin of beef.
After trimming the short loin, the butcher demonstrated how to slice perfect steaks.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: porterhouse steak - a large steak cut from the short loin containing a T-shaped bone
  • Degree Higher: sirloin - the portion of the beef loin located further back from the short loin
