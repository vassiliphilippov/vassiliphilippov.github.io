Shenandoah River

1. PROPN

/ ʃəˈnæn.doʊ.ə ˈrɪv.ər / (AmE), / ʃəˈnæn.dəʊ.ə ˈrɪv.ə / (BrE)
a river in northern Virginia, United States, flowing northeast to join the Potomac River at Harpers Ferry
river in northern Virginia, USA

Examples

The Shenandoah River winds through the lush valleys of Virginia, providing scenic views for miles.
Many people enjoy kayaking and fishing in the Shenandoah River during the summer months.
At Harpers Ferry, the Shenandoah River merges with the Potomac, offering a popular spot for hikers and tourists.

Etymology

From the Native American word Shenandoah, possibly meaning 'spruce stream' or 'beautiful daughter of the stars'.
