stake race

1. NOUN

/ steɪk reɪs / (AmE), / steɪk reɪs / (BrE)
A horse race in which the owners of the competing horses contribute part of the prize money.
horse race with owner's prize money contribution

Examples

Winning a stake race can be highly prestigious for owners and trainers alike.
All the horses in the stake race must meet strict qualification standards.
The entry fee for the stake race was considerably higher than in other competitions.

Etymology

From the word 'stake' referring to money risked or invested, and 'race.'
