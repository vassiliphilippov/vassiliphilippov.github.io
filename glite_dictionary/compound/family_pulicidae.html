family Pulicidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli pjuːˈlɪsɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli pjuːˈlɪsɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of fleas that includes many common species which infest humans and domestic animals
a family of common fleas

Examples

The family Pulicidae includes several species that are common pests in households worldwide.
Veterinarians are often concerned with fleas from the family Pulicidae because of their impact on pets.
Members of the family Pulicidae can transmit diseases to both humans and animals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flea - a small, wingless insect that feeds on the blood of mammals and birds

Etymology

From scientific Latin Pulicidae, from Latin pulicis (flea).
