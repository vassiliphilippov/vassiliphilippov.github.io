jungle fowl

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒʌŋɡəl ˌfaʊl / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌŋɡl ˌfaʊl / (BrE)
a small wild bird native to South and Southeast Asia, thought to be the ancestor of the domestic chicken
wild ancestor of domestic chicken

Examples

The red jungle fowl is widely regarded as the primary ancestor of today's domestic chickens.
Scientists have studied the jungle fowl to better understand the origins of poultry farming.
A male jungle fowl typically has brightly colored plumage and a distinctive call.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chicken - domestic fowl descended from the jungle fowl
