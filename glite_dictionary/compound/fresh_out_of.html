fresh out of

1. ADJ

/ frɛʃ ˈaʊt əv / (AmE), / frɛʃ ˈaʊt ɒv / (BrE)
having just finished, graduated, or run out of (something); recently lacking something completely.
just completed or completely out of something

Examples

Jessica is fresh out of university and excited to start her new career.
Sorry, we’re fresh out of bread, but we have plenty of rolls.
After a long meeting, the team was fresh out of enthusiasm.

Synset Examples

She's fresh out of college and looking for a job.
He was fresh out of ideas for the presentation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: out of - having none left of something
