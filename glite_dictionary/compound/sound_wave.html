sound wave

1. NOUN

/ saʊnd weɪv / (AmE), / saʊnd weɪv / (BrE)
A wave of compression and rarefaction, by which sound is transmitted through a medium such as air, water, or solids.
wave that transmits sound

Examples

A sound wave travels through the air and reaches our ears as a vibration.
Underwater, a sound wave moves more quickly than it does through air.
The frequency of a sound wave determines the pitch of the noise we hear.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: light wave - a wave that transmits light
