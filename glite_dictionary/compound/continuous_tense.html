continuous tense

1. NOUN

/ kənˈtɪn.ju.əs tɛns / (AmE), / kənˈtɪn.ju.əs tɛns / (BrE)
a verb tense used to describe actions that are ongoing or in progress at a particular time
verb tense for ongoing actions

Examples

In English, the continuous tense is used to talk about actions happening right now.
She explained the continuous tense to her students using simple examples.
They practiced changing simple present sentences into the continuous tense during class.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: progressive tense - a verb tense indicating an ongoing action
