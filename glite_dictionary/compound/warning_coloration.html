warning coloration

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔrnɪŋ ˌkʌləˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈwɔːnɪŋ ˌkʌləˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the conspicuous coloring or markings of an animal that serve to warn potential predators of its unpalatability or danger
conspicuous animal coloring that warns predators

Examples

The bright stripes of a coral snake are a classic example of warning coloration in the wild.
Many poisonous frogs exhibit warning coloration to deter predators from eating them.
Some harmless butterflies mimic the warning coloration of toxic species to protect themselves.

Synset Examples

a skunk's aposematic coloration

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aposematism - the use of bright coloring or markings by animals to signal danger or unpalatability
