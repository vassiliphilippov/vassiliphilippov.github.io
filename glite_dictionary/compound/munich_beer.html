Munich beer

1. NOUN

/ ˈmjunɪk bɪr / (AmE), / ˈmjuːnɪk bɪə / (BrE)
A dark lager originating from Munich, Germany, with a distinctive malty taste, traditionally brewed since the 10th century.
dark, malty lager from Munich

Examples

Many visitors to Germany look forward to tasting authentic Munich beer during Oktoberfest.
This local pub offers a selection of imported Munich beer alongside domestic brews.
The rich malt flavor of Munich beer sets it apart from other types of lagers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Munich lager - a lager-style beer originating from Munich; similar in style and tradition
  • Antonym: pilsner - a type of pale lager; lighter and less malty than Munich beer

Etymology

From Munich, the capital city of Bavaria, Germany, known for its brewing tradition, and 'beer' from Old English 'beor.'
