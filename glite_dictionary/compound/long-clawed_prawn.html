long-clawed prawn

1. NOUN

/ lɔŋ klɔd prɑn / (AmE), / lɒŋ klɔːd prɔːn / (BrE)
a large edible freshwater prawn, common in Australian rivers, notable for its long claws and often exceeding a foot in length
large Australian freshwater prawn with long claws

Examples

The chef prepared the long-clawed prawn with garlic butter and fresh herbs.
In northern Australia, fishermen often catch the long-clawed prawn during the wet season.
The aquarium had a display featuring a giant long-clawed prawn from a local river.
