church mode

1. NOUN

/ tʃɝtʃ moʊd / (AmE), / tʃɜːtʃ məʊd / (BrE)
any of a set of scales used in Gregorian chant and early Western music, each characterized by a unique pattern of intervals.
musical scale used in early Western church music

Examples

Each church mode imparts a distinct character to the melody.
The composition makes creative use of the Dorian church mode.
Medieval hymns are often sung in a church mode rather than modern scales.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Gregorian mode - a mode used in Gregorian chant, synonymous with church mode
  • Antonym: major scale - diatonic scale commonly used in Western music, contrasted with church modes
  • Antonym: minor scale - another common Western scale, as opposed to church modes

Etymology

From use in the Christian church and Latin 'modus' (manner, measure); related to the musical modes of Ancient Greece.
