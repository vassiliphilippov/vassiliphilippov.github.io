cross-functional team

1. NOUN

/ krɔs ˈfʌŋkʃənl tim / (AmE), / krɒs ˈfʌŋkʃənl tiːm / (BrE)
a group composed of members from different departments or areas within an organization, formed to achieve a common goal
team with members from different departments

Examples

Our company created a cross-functional team to develop the new product launch strategy.
The cross-functional team faced challenges in communication but ultimately delivered excellent results.
Being part of a cross-functional team taught her to appreciate different perspectives within the company.

Synset Examples

The cross-functional team worked well together.
