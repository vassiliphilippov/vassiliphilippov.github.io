Seven Years' War

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛvən ˈjɪrz ˌwɔr / (AmE), / ˌsɛvən ˈjɪəz ˌwɔː / (BrE)
a major conflict fought from 1756 to 1763 involving most of the great powers of the time, principally England and Prussia against France and Austria, with significant battles in Europe, North America, and elsewhere.
global conflict, 1756–1763, involving major European powers

Examples

Historians consider the Seven Years' War the first truly global conflict.
The Treaty of Paris ended the Seven Years' War and redrew colonial boundaries.
During the Seven Years' War, many battles were fought outside of Europe, especially in North America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: French and Indian War - the North American theater of the Seven Years' War
