heart attack

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑrt əˌtæk / (AmE), / ˈhɑːt əˌtæk / (BrE)
A sudden and severe medical condition in which the heart stops functioning properly, usually due to a blocked blood supply to the heart muscle.
sudden severe heart condition
medical

Examples

He was rushed to the hospital after suffering a heart attack at work.
Doctors warned her that high stress levels could increase her risk of a heart attack.
After his heart attack, he adopted a healthier lifestyle and diet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: myocardial infarction - medical term for heart attack
  • Degree Higher: cardiac arrest - sudden cessation of heart function
  • Degree Lower: angina - chest pain due to reduced blood flow to the heart

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɑrt əˌtæk / (AmE), / ˈhɑːt əˌtæk / (BrE)
A serious medical emergency in which the blood supply to part of the heart is blocked, often causing chest pain and potentially fatal damage to heart tissue.
blockage of blood flow to the heart
medical

Examples

After his heart attack, he changed his lifestyle completely.
He survived a heart attack thanks to quick medical help.
A sudden heart attack took him by surprise.
