Hessian boot

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛʃən buːt / (AmE), / ˈhɛʃ.ən buːt / (BrE)
A high leather boot with a tassel, worn by men in the 19th century, particularly by soldiers.
19th-century men's high tasseled boot

Examples

The colonel strode in, his polished Hessian boots gleaming under the lamplight.
Fashion from the Regency period often included tall Hessian boots for formal occasions.
He was proud of his military uniform, complete with Hessian boots and braided jacket.

Etymology

From 'Hessian', referring to soldiers from Hesse, Germany, who popularized the style in the late 18th century.
