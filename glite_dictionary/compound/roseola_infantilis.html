roseola infantilis

1. NOUN

/ roʊˌziːoʊlə ɪnˈfæntɪlɪs / (AmE), / rəʊˌziːələ ɪnˈfæntɪlɪs / (BrE)
a viral disease of infants and young children, marked by a sudden high fever and followed by a short-lasting pinkish rash
viral disease in infants with fever and rash
medical

Examples

The pediatrician diagnosed her baby with roseola infantilis after a sudden high fever and a brief rash.
Roseola infantilis often causes concern due to its abrupt onset, but it is usually mild and self-limiting.
Parents should watch for the characteristic rash of roseola infantilis after the fever subsides.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: roseola - a mild infectious disease causing a rash, also known as exanthema subitum

Etymology

From Latin 'roseola' (little rose, rash) and 'infantilis' (of infants).
