signal-to-noise ratio

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪɡnəl tə ˈnoɪz ˈreɪʃiˌoʊ / (AmE), / ˈsɪɡnəl tə ˈnɔɪz ˈreɪʃɪəʊ / (BrE)
The ratio between the level of a desired signal and the level of background noise, used to measure the quality of a transmission or recording.
ratio of signal strength to noise

Examples

The signal-to-noise ratio of the recording was too low to hear the speaker clearly.
Improving the signal-to-noise ratio can make communication systems more efficient and reliable.
Astronomers use a high signal-to-noise ratio to distinguish faint stars from background interference.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: SNR - abbreviation for signal-to-noise ratio

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɪɡnəl tə ˈnɔɪs ˌreɪʃioʊ / (AmE), / ˈsɪɡnəl tə ˈnɔɪs ˌreɪʃiəʊ / (BrE)
The ratio of the strength of a signal to the strength of background noise, used to measure the clarity of the signal.
ratio of signal strength to background noise

Examples

The signal-to-noise ratio of this recording is too low to hear the instruments clearly.
A high signal-to-noise ratio ensures that important data is easy to distinguish from background interference.
Engineers work to improve the signal-to-noise ratio in wireless communication systems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: SNR - Abbreviation for signal-to-noise ratio

Etymology

Composed of 'signal,' 'to,' 'noise,' and 'ratio'; the term became common in the 20th century with the development of telecommunications and electronics.
