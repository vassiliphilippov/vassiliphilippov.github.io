barnacle goose

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑrnəkəl ɡus / (AmE), / ˈbɑːnəkəl ɡuːs / (BrE)
a European species of goose (Branta leucopsis) smaller than the brant, breeding in the far north and migrating south in winter.
a European species of wild goose

Examples

Flocks of barnacle goose are often seen migrating along the coast in autumn.
The barnacle goose breeds in Arctic regions and spends its winters further south.
A solitary barnacle goose was spotted in the wetlands yesterday, exciting local birdwatchers.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: brant - a related species of wild goose, Branta bernicla

Etymology

Named from the former belief that it developed from barnacles; from Middle English 'bernak' (barnacle) + 'goos' (goose).
