Steller's sea lion

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɛlərz ˈsi ˌlaɪən / (AmE), / ˈsteləz ˈsiː ˌlaɪən / (BrE)
a very large species of sea lion native to the northern Pacific Ocean
large sea lion of the northern Pacific

Examples

Scientists observed a group of Steller's sea lions basking on the rocky shore.
The Steller's sea lion is listed as a near-threatened species due to declining population numbers.
Young Steller's sea lions are often seen playing in the shallow waters during summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sea lion - large marine mammal of the family Otariidae

Etymology

Named after German naturalist Georg Wilhelm Steller (1709–1746), who first described the species.
