steady growth

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɛdi ˈɡroʊθ / (AmE), / ˈstɛdi ˈɡrəʊθ / (BrE)
a consistent and gradual increase in size, amount, or value over a period of time
consistent and gradual increase

Examples

The company has experienced steady growth in its profits over the last five years.
Healthy eating and exercise contribute to the steady growth of children.
Despite market fluctuations, the sector maintained steady growth throughout the year.

Synset Examples

The economy is showing steady growth this quarter.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: rapid growth - quick and significant increase
  • Antonym: decline - gradual decrease in amount or value
