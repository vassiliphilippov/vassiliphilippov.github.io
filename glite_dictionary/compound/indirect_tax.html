indirect tax

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.dɪˈrɛkt ˈtæks / (AmE), / ˌɪn.dɪˈrɛkt ˈtæks / (BrE)
a tax imposed on goods or services rather than directly on individuals or organizations
tax on goods or services

Examples

Most consumers pay indirect tax on everyday purchases such as food and clothing.
The government increased the indirect tax on fuel to raise additional revenue.
Unlike income tax, an indirect tax is included in the price of goods or services.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: direct tax - a tax levied directly on individuals or organizations
