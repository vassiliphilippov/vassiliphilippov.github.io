Old Latin

1. NOUN

/ oʊld ˈlætən / (AmE), / əʊld ˈlætɪn / (BrE)
the earliest recorded form of the Latin language, used from the 6th to the 3rd century BC
early Latin language stage

Examples

Scholars study Old Latin to understand the evolution of the Roman language.
The inscription was written in Old Latin, making it difficult for modern readers to interpret.
Many differences between Old Latin and Classical Latin can be seen in ancient manuscripts.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Classical Latin - the later standardized form of Latin used in ancient Rome

2. NOUN

/ oʊld ˈlæt̬.ɪn / (AmE), / əʊld ˈlæt.ɪn / (BrE)
the form of the Latin language used from its earliest records through approximately the 3rd century B.C.
ancient stage of the Latin language

Examples

Old Latin inscriptions provide valuable insight into the early development of the Roman language.
Scholars compare Old Latin texts with Classical Latin to trace linguistic changes.
The grammar of Old Latin differs significantly from the later standardized forms.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Classical Latin - the refined form of Latin spoken and written from the 1st century B.C. onwards

Etymology

From Latin 'vetus Latinitas' (old Latinity), denoting the earliest phase of Latin.
