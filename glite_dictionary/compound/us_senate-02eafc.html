US Senate

1. NOUN

/ ˌjuː ɛs ˈsɛnət / (AmE), / ˌjuː ɛs ˈsɛnət / (BrE)
the upper chamber of the United States Congress, which is responsible for making and passing federal laws alongside the House of Representatives
the upper house of the US Congress
american

Examples

The US Senate voted to approve the new trade agreement after weeks of debate.
Many new laws must be passed by both the House and the US Senate before they become effective.
Several prominent politicians have served in the US Senate for decades.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: House of Representatives - the lower chamber of the United States Congress
