brass monkey

1. NOUN

/ bræs ˈmʌŋki / (AmE), / brɑːs ˈmʌŋki / (BrE)
a metal stand, often made of brass, used to hold cannon balls on sailing ships in the past
metal stand for cannon balls (historical, nautical)

Examples

The sailors stacked the cannonballs neatly on the brass monkey before setting sail.
According to legend, cannonballs would roll off the brass monkey in extreme cold.
Historians debate whether the brass monkey was ever actually used on ships.
