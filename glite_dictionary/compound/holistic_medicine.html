holistic medicine

1. NOUN

/ hoʊˈlɪstɪk ˈmɛdəsən / (AmE), / həˈlɪstɪk ˈmɛdɪsɪn / (BrE)
an approach to medical care that considers the whole person, including physical, mental, and social factors, rather than just treating specific symptoms or illnesses
medical care considering whole person
medical

Examples

Many patients are turning to holistic medicine to address both emotional and physical health concerns.
Holistic medicine emphasizes the importance of lifestyle and social factors in healing.
The clinic specializes in holistic medicine, offering treatments that go beyond standard medical care.

Synset Examples

holistic medicine treats the mind as well as the body

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alternative medicine - medical practices not part of mainstream Western medicine
  • Antonym: conventional medicine - mainstream medical practice based on scientific evidence

Etymology

From 'holistic' (from Greek 'holos' meaning 'whole') + 'medicine'.
