squamous cell carcinoma

1. NOUN

/ ˈskweɪməs sɛl ˌkɑr.sɪˈnoʊ.mə / (AmE), / ˈskweɪməs sɛl ˌkɑː.sɪˈnəʊ.mə / (BrE)
A common type of skin cancer that begins in the squamous cells, which make up the middle and outer layers of the skin.
type of skin cancer
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed him with squamous cell carcinoma after examining the lesion on his arm.
Early detection of squamous cell carcinoma increases the chances of successful treatment.
Unlike melanoma, squamous cell carcinoma often develops on sun-exposed areas of the skin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: basal cell carcinoma - a less aggressive, common skin cancer of the basal cells
  • Antonym: melanoma - a serious form of skin cancer developing from pigment-containing cells

Etymology

From Latin 'squamosus' (scaly) and Greek 'karkinoma' (cancerous ulcer).
