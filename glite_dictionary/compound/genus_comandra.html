genus Comandra

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs kəˈmæn.drə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs kəˈmæn.drə / (BrE)
A small genus of chiefly North American parasitic plants in the family Santalaceae.
genus of North American parasitic plants

Examples

The genus Comandra includes several interesting hemiparasitic species found in North America.
Researchers have studied the ecology of plants in the genus Comandra to understand their parasitic relationships.
Only one species of the genus Comandra, Comandra umbellata, is widespread across the continent.

Etymology

From Latin 'genus' (kind, type) and New Latin 'Comandra' (from Greek 'komē' = hair and 'anēr, andros' = man, referring to botanical features).
