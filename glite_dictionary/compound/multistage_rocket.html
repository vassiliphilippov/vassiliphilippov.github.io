multistage rocket

1. NOUN

/ ˌmʌltiˈsteɪdʒ ˈrɑkɪt / (AmE), / ˌmʌltiˈsteɪdʒ ˈrɒkɪt / (BrE)
a rocket with two or more sections, each with its own engine and fuel, which are fired in sequence and discarded as their fuel is used up
rocket with sequential, discardable stages

Examples

The Saturn V was a famous multistage rocket used during the Apollo missions.
Engineers designed a new multistage rocket to deliver heavier payloads into orbit.
A multistage rocket sheds its lower stages as they run out of fuel, making it more efficient.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rocket - vehicle powered by rocket engines
