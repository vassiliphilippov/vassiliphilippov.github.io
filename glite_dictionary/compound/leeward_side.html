leeward side

1. NOUN

/ ˈliːwɚd saɪd or ˈluːwɚd saɪd / (AmE), / ˈluːəd saɪd or ˈliːwəd saɪd / (BrE)
the side of something that is sheltered from the wind
side sheltered from the wind

Examples

The hikers set up camp on the leeward side of the mountain to avoid the strong gusts.
During the storm, boats anchored on the leeward side of the island remained safe.
Plants thrive on the leeward side where they are protected from harsh winds.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: windward side - the side facing into the wind

Etymology

From 'leeward' (from 'lee', shelter from wind + ward, direction), meaning 'on or toward the side sheltered from the wind'.
