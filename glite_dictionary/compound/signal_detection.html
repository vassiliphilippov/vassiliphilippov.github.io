signal detection

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪɡnəl dɪˈtɛkʃən / (AmE), / ˈsɪɡnəl dɪˈtɛkʃən / (BrE)
the process of determining whether a signal has been received or is present, especially amid noise or uncertainty
identifying when a signal is received

Examples

Accurate signal detection is crucial for reliable communication in noisy environments.
Advances in technology have improved signal detection for medical imaging devices.
Their study focused on human performance in signal detection tasks under stress.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: signal detection theory - theory about how signals are detected amid noise
