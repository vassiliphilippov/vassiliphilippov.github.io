Egyptian henbane

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈdʒɪp.ʃən ˈhɛn.beɪn / (AmE), / ɪˈdʒɪp.ʃən ˈhɛn.beɪn / (BrE)
a poisonous herb (Hyoscyamus muticus) native to North Africa and the Middle East, whose leaves are a source of hyoscyamine
poisonous herb; source of hyoscyamine

Examples

Egyptian henbane has long been used in traditional medicine for its sedative effects, despite its toxicity.
Researchers extract alkaloids like hyoscyamine from Egyptian henbane for pharmaceutical purposes.
The garden was carefully labeled to warn visitors about the dangerous Egyptian henbane growing near the path.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: henbane - a poisonous plant with psychoactive properties, genus Hyoscyamus

Etymology

Henbane from Old English *henbana*; 'Egyptian' refers to its regional origin.
