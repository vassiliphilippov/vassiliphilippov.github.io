Jamaica sorrel

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˈmeɪ.kə ˈsɔː.rəl / (AmE), / dʒəˈmeɪ.kə ˈsɒr.əl / (BrE)
A tropical plant (Hibiscus sabdariffa), widely grown for its edible fleshy calyxes used in tarts, jelly, and beverages, and for its bast fiber.
tropical plant with edible calyxes and fiber

Examples

The bright red calyxes of the Jamaica sorrel are brewed to make a popular holiday drink.
Many Caribbean recipes call for fresh Jamaica sorrel to add a tart, fruity flavor to jams and jellies.
After harvesting, the stalks of Jamaica sorrel are sometimes used to produce strong fiber.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: roselle - Another common name for Hibiscus sabdariffa, the plant with edible calyxes

Etymology

Named after Jamaica, where the plant is widely grown, and 'sorrel', referring to its tart flavor.
