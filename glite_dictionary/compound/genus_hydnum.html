genus Hydnum

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˈhaɪdnəm / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈhɪdnəm / (BrE)
A genus of fungi in the family Hydnaceae, characterized by spiny or toothed fruiting bodies.
a genus of fungi with spiny fruiting bodies

Examples

The genus Hydnum includes edible fungi known as hedgehog mushrooms.
Researchers discovered a new species within the genus Hydnum in North America.
Members of the genus Hydnum are distinguished by their unique spiny undersides.

Etymology

From Latin 'Hydnum,' used as a genus name in mycology.
