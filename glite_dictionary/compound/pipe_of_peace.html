pipe of peace

1. NOUN

/ paɪp əv pis / (AmE), / paɪp əv piːs / (BrE)
a ceremonial, highly decorated pipe used by some Native American peoples and smoked on special occasions, especially as a symbol of peace or agreement
ceremonial Native American peace pipe

Examples

The chief offered the pipe of peace as a sign of friendship between the two tribes.
During the ceremony, each elder took turns smoking the pipe of peace.
Historians have written about the significant role the pipe of peace played in diplomatic relations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peace pipe - ceremonial Native American pipe symbolizing peace
