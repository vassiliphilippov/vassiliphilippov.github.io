game bird

1. NOUN

/ ɡeɪm bɝd / (AmE), / ɡeɪm bɜːd / (BrE)
a bird, such as a grouse or pheasant, that is hunted for sport or food
bird hunted for sport or food

Examples

The reserve is home to many game birds such as pheasants and partridges.
Restrictions on the hunting of game birds help protect endangered species.
They organized a weekend shoot for experienced game bird hunters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: waterfowl - a bird that lives on or near water, often hunted for sport
  • Antonym: songbird - a bird known for its musical song, not typically hunted
