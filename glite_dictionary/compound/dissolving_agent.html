dissolving agent

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈzɑlvɪŋ ˈeɪdʒənt / (AmE), / dɪˈzɒlvɪŋ ˈeɪdʒənt / (BrE)
a liquid substance that has the ability to dissolve other substances, forming a solution
liquid that dissolves other substances

Examples

Water acts as an excellent dissolving agent for many ionic compounds.
In chemistry, a dissolving agent like ethanol can help mix two substances.
Salt readily disperses in a dissolving agent such as water.

Synset Examples

the solvent does not change its state in forming a solution

Related Terms

  • Synonym: solvent - a substance that dissolves a solute to form a solution
