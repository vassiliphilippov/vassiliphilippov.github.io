Visayan Islands

1. PROPN

/ vɪˈsaɪ.ən ˈaɪ.ləndz / (AmE), / vɪˈsaɪ.ən ˈaɪ.ləndz / (BrE)
a group of islands in the central part of the Philippines, situated between Luzon and Mindanao.
islands in the central Philippines

Examples

The Visayan Islands are known for their beautiful beaches and rich cultural heritage.
Many tourists visit the Visayan Islands to explore their diverse marine life.
Cebu is one of the most developed islands in the Visayan Islands group.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Philippines - a country in Southeast Asia consisting of many islands

Etymology

From Visayan (the native ethnolinguistic group) + English 'islands'.
