bell heather

1. NOUN

/ bɛl ˈhɛðɚ / (AmE), / bɛl ˈhɛðə / (BrE)
a small European shrub (Erica cinerea) with needle-like leaves and clusters of bell-shaped, rose-purple flowers
a small shrub with rose-purple flowers

Examples

The hillside was covered with blooming bell heather in early summer.
She pressed a sprig of bell heather into her journal as a keepsake.
Bell heather attracts many bees and butterflies to the garden.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heather - a small shrub with pink or purple flowers, common on moors

2. NOUN

/ bɛl ˈhɛðər / (AmE), / bɛl ˈhɛðə / (BrE)
a low European shrub with narrow leaves and clusters of purple-red, bell-shaped flowers, common on heaths and moorlands
European shrub with purple-red bell-shaped flowers

Examples

The hills were blanketed with bell heather in full bloom.
She planted bell heather in the rock garden for a splash of purple color.
Wild bees are often attracted to the nectar of bell heather during the summer months.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heather - a common shrub of heathland with small pink or purple flowers
