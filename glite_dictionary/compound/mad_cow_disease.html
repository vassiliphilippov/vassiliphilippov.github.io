mad cow disease

1. NOUN

/ mæd kaʊ dɪˈziz / (AmE), / mad kaʊ dɪˈziːz / (BrE)
A fatal disease of cattle affecting the central nervous system, causing abnormal behavior and loss of coordination; also known as bovine spongiform encephalopathy.
fatal cattle disease affecting the nervous system

Examples

The outbreak of mad cow disease in the 1990s caused widespread panic among beef consumers.
Researchers are studying how mad cow disease affects the brains of infected cattle.
Stringent testing measures were put in place to prevent the spread of mad cow disease.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bovine spongiform encephalopathy - medical name for mad cow disease
  • Synonym: Creutzfeldt–Jakob disease - similar fatal brain disease in humans

2. NOUN

/ ˌmæd ˈkaʊ dɪˌziz / (AmE), / ˌmad ˈkaʊ dɪˌziːz / (BrE)
A fatal neurodegenerative disease in cattle, also known as bovine spongiform encephalopathy (BSE), that can contaminate food and pose health risks to humans.
neurodegenerative disease in cattle (BSE)

Examples

After reports of mad cow disease, beef sales dropped significantly.
The government implemented strict controls to prevent the spread of mad cow disease.
Scientists continue to study mad cow disease to understand how it can affect humans.

Synset Examples

Farmers had to cull herds due to mad cow disease.
The outbreak of mad cow disease caused food safety concerns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bovine spongiform encephalopathy - the technical name for mad cow disease

Etymology

From 'mad cow' (referring to affected cattle's symptoms) and 'disease'; 'mad cow disease' is the common term for bovine spongiform encephalopathy (BSE).
