decimal fraction

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛsɪməl ˈfrækʃən / (AmE), / ˈdɛsɪməl ˈfrækʃən / (BrE)
a fraction whose denominator is a power of ten, often written in decimal notation
a fraction with a denominator that is a power of ten

Examples

The number 0.25 is a decimal fraction because it can be written as 25/100.
Every decimal fraction can be expressed as a terminating decimal.
If the denominator is a power of ten, the fraction is called a decimal fraction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decimal - a number or notation based on the number ten
  • Antonym: common fraction - a fraction in which the numerator and denominator are both integers (not necessarily powers of ten)

Etymology

From Latin 'decimus' (tenth) and 'fractio' (a breaking, fraction).
