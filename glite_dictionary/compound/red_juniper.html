red juniper

1. NOUN

/ rɛd ˈdʒuːnɪpər / (AmE), / rɛd ˈdʒuːnɪpə / (BrE)
A small species of juniper found in eastern North America, characterized by a conical crown, peeling brown bark, and sharp needles.
a North American species of juniper tree

Examples

The red juniper thrives in rocky soils and is often used for landscaping.
Many birds nest in the branches of the red juniper for protection.
The peeling bark of the red juniper distinguishes it from other juniper species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: juniper - a type of evergreen shrub or small tree
