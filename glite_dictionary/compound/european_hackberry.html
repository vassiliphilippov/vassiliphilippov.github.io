European hackberry

1. NOUN

/ jʊˈroʊpiən ˈhækbɛri / (AmE), / jʊəˈrəʊpiən ˈhækbəri / (BrE)
a deciduous tree (Celtis australis) of southern Europe, with bright green leaves, often grown for shade
shade tree of southern Europe

Examples

The European hackberry is commonly planted along Mediterranean streets for its dense shade.
Botanists admire the resilience of the European hackberry in drought-prone areas.
Birds are attracted to the fruit of the European hackberry in the summer months.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hackberry - a tree or shrub of the genus Celtis
