climbing nightshade

1. NOUN

/ ˈklaɪmɪŋ ˈnaɪtˌʃeɪd / (AmE), / ˈklaɪmɪŋ ˈnaɪtʃeɪd / (BrE)
a poisonous perennial vine with violet flowers and red berries, native to the Old World and now widespread as a weed in North America
poisonous perennial vine with violet flowers

Examples

The berries of the climbing nightshade are highly toxic if ingested.
Climbing nightshade grows rapidly along fences and hedgerows in rural areas.
Gardeners must take care to remove climbing nightshade because of its potential hazard to children and pets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: woody nightshade - alternative name for climbing nightshade, a similar poisonous plant
  • Antonym: deadly nightshade - another toxic plant of the nightshade family, known as belladonna
