third party

1. NOUN

/ θɜrd ˈpɑrti / (AmE), / θɜːd ˈpɑːti / (BrE)
A political party organized in opposition to the two major parties in a two-party system.
minor political party in a two-party system

Examples

The third party candidate drew significant support from dissatisfied voters.
Many voters considered supporting a third party instead of the main two parties in the election.
The rise of a strong third party changed the dynamics of the political race.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: minor party - a political party with little electoral strength

2. NOUN

/ ˌθɝːd ˈpɑːrti / (AmE), / ˌθɜːd ˈpɑːti / (BrE)
a person or group besides the two primarily involved in a situation, especially in a transaction or dispute.
person/group not directly involved in a transaction

Examples

The contract specifies what happens if a third party becomes involved in the dispute.
If you share your data with a third party, you must have consent from the user.
The insurance policy will compensate a third party in case of an accident.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: principal - the main person involved in a transaction or contract
