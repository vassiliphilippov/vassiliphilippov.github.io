visitor's tax

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪzətɚz tæks / (AmE), / ˈvɪzɪtəz tæks / (BrE)
a tax imposed by certain municipalities or regions on non-residents who stay in tourist accommodations, usually for more than 24 hours, for tourism or related purposes.
tax paid by visitors for accommodation

Examples

All guests are required to pay a small visitor's tax upon arrival at the hotel.
The visitor's tax is used to improve local infrastructure and tourism facilities.
Many European cities charge a visitor's tax that is added to your hotel bill.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tourist tax - tax charged to people staying in accommodation in a destination town/city
