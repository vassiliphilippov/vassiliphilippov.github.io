wool stapler

1. NOUN

/ wʊl ˈsteɪ.pɫɚ / (AmE), / wʊl ˈsteɪ.plə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to sort wool into different grades according to quality, length, and fineness
person who grades and sorts wool

Examples

The wool stapler carefully examined each fleece before placing it into the appropriate bin.
After years as a farmer, he found work as a wool stapler at the mill.
A skilled wool stapler can greatly influence the value of a wool crop.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stapler - a person who sorts fibers or materials into grades

Etymology

From 'wool' (the fleece of sheep) + 'stapler' (from staple, meaning a fiber or grade of fiber).

2. NOUN

/ wʊl ˈsteɪplɚ / (AmE), / wʊl ˈsteɪplə / (BrE)
a person who buys and sells wool, especially as a dealer in raw wool
wool dealer or trader

Examples

His grandfather worked as a wool stapler in Yorkshire during the 19th century.
The wool stapler inspected the fleeces before purchasing them from the farmers.
After the auction, the wool stapler negotiated prices with local shepherds.

Etymology

Stapler from Middle English, originally meaning one who keeps a staple (market), with 'wool' denoting the specific commodity.
