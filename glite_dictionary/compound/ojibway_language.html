Ojibway language

1. NOUN

/ oʊˈdʒɪbweɪ ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / əʊˈdʒɪbweɪ ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Algonquian language spoken by the Ojibwa people, primarily in parts of Canada and the northern United States.
language of the Ojibwa people

Examples

She studied the Ojibway language as part of her research on indigenous cultures.
Many traditional stories are still told in the Ojibway language.
Efforts are being made to revitalize the Ojibway language among younger generations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ojibwa - a member of a Native American people of North America

Etymology

From the name Ojibwa, a Native American people.
