carnation family

1. NOUN

/ kɑrˈneɪʃən ˈfæməˌli / (AmE), / kɑːˈneɪʃən ˈfæmɪli / (BrE)
a large family of chiefly herbaceous plants, typically with swollen nodes on the stems, including carnations and pinks (family Caryophyllaceae)
family of herbs including carnations and pinks

Examples

The carnation family includes popular garden flowers such as pinks and sweet williams.
Many species in the carnation family thrive in temperate climates around the world.
Botanists have identified over two thousand species within the carnation family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Caryophyllaceae - the scientific name for the carnation family

Etymology

Named from its prominent member, the carnation, and classified as Caryophyllaceae in scientific taxonomy.
