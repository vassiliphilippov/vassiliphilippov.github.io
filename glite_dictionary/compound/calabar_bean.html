calabar bean

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæl.əˌbɑr ˌbiːn / (AmE), / ˈkæl.əˌbɑː ˌbiːn / (BrE)
the dark brown, highly poisonous seed of a West African vine (Physostigma venenosum), once used in traditional medicine and witchcraft, and a source of physostigmine
poisonous seed of the calabar-bean vine

Examples

The calabar bean was historically used as an ordeal poison in West Africa.
Physostigmine, a drug used to treat glaucoma, is extracted from the calabar bean.
The highly toxic nature of the calabar bean made it a feared component in traditional rituals.

Etymology

Named after Calabar, a region in southeastern Nigeria, where the bean is native.
