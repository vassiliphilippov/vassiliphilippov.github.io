acoustic power

1. NOUN

/ əˈkuːstɪk ˈpaʊər / (AmE), / əˈkuːstɪk ˈpaʊə / (BrE)
the total sound energy emitted by a source per unit of time, measured as the rate of sound energy transmission.
rate of sound energy emission

Examples

The acoustic power of the speaker was sufficient to fill the large auditorium with sound.
Engineers measured the acoustic power emitted by the machine to ensure it met regulatory standards.
By reducing acoustic power, manufacturers can make appliances quieter for home use.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sound intensity - the power carried by sound waves per unit area

Etymology

acoustic from Greek 'akoustikos' (of or for hearing) + power.
