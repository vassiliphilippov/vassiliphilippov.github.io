Cupid's bow

1. NOUN

/ ˈkjuːpɪdz ˌboʊ / (AmE), / ˈkjuːpɪdz ˌbəʊ / (BrE)
the double curve of the upper lip, shaped like the bow of Cupid, the Roman god of love
double curve of the upper lip

Examples

Her upper lip had a perfect Cupid's bow that made her smile look especially charming.
The makeup artist highlighted her Cupid's bow to accentuate her lips.
He traced the delicate shape of her Cupid's bow with his fingertip.

Etymology

Named after Cupid, the Roman god of love, whose bow is depicted as curved.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkjuːpɪdz ˌboʊ / (AmE), / ˈkjuːpɪdz ˌbəʊ / (BrE)
A bow with a curved shape and ends that curve outward in the opposite direction, traditionally associated with representations of Cupid.
curved bow with outward-curving ends

Examples

The archer crafted a beautiful Cupid's bow for the festival competition.
The design of the Cupid's bow is elegant and reminiscent of classical mythology.
He admired the ornate carvings on the ancient Cupid's bow displayed in the museum.

Etymology

Named after Cupid, the Roman god of love, who is often depicted with such a bow.
