foreign direct investment

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔrən dɪˈrɛkt ɪnˈvɛstmənt / (AmE), / ˈfɒrən dɪˈrɛkt ɪnˈvɛstmənt / (BrE)
an investment made by a company or individual in one country into business interests located in another country, typically by acquiring assets or establishing business operations such as ownership or control of a foreign company
investment in a foreign business or assets

Examples

The government has encouraged foreign direct investment to boost economic growth.
Many multinational firms rely on foreign direct investment to expand into new markets.
Restrictions on foreign direct investment can limit a country's access to global capital.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: domestic investment - investment made within the investor's own country

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɔrən dəˈrɛkt ɪnˈvɛstmənt / (AmE), / ˈfɒrən dɪˈrɛkt ɪnˈvɛstmənt / (BrE)
an investment made by a company or individual in one country in business interests located in another country, typically involving ownership or significant control
investment in businesses by foreign entities

Examples

Many developing countries actively seek foreign direct investment to boost their economic growth.
The government relaxed its policies to encourage more foreign direct investment in the technology sector.
China has been a major recipient of foreign direct investment over the past decade.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: portfolio investment - investment in foreign financial assets without control
