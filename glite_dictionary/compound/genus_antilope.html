genus Antilope

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ænˈtaɪ.loʊp / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ænˈtɪ.lə.pi / (BrE)
A zoological genus comprising the blackbuck, a species of antelope found mainly in India and surrounding regions.
genus of antelope (blackbuck)

Examples

The genus Antilope contains only one species, the blackbuck.
Researchers studied the behavior of animals within the genus Antilope.
Conservation efforts are focused on preserving the genus Antilope in its native habitat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antelope - a fast-running, deer-like animal native to Asia and Africa
  • Synonym: blackbuck - a species of antelope native to the Indian subcontinent

Etymology

From Latin 'antilope', referring to antelopes, used in scientific taxonomy.
