cajan pea

1. NOUN

/ kəˈdʒæn pi / (AmE), / kəˈdʒan piː / (BrE)
the small, highly nutritious seed of the tropical pigeon-pea plant, used as a food crop in many parts of the world
seed of the pigeon-pea plant

Examples

Farmers in tropical regions grow cajan pea as an important source of protein.
The stew was packed with vegetables and cajan peas for extra nutrition.
Researchers are studying the drought resistance of the cajan pea plant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pigeon pea - seed of the same plant, used as food

Etymology

From the scientific name Cajanus cajan, the plant it derives from.

2. NOUN

/ kəˈdʒæn piː / (AmE), / kəˈdʒæn piː / (BrE)
a tropical woody plant with yellow flowers and flat pods, widely cultivated in the tropics for its edible seeds, also known as pigeon pea
tropical plant grown for edible seeds

Examples

Farmers in India often grow cajan pea as a major source of protein.
The bright yellow flowers of the cajan pea add beauty to tropical fields.
Many traditional dishes in the Caribbean use cajan pea as a main ingredient.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pigeon pea - another common name for the cajan pea, an important tropical legume

Etymology

From New Latin Cajanus (the genus name), ultimately from Malay "kacang" (pea or bean).
