traditional cappa

1. NOUN

/ trəˈdɪʃənl ˈkɑːpə / (AmE), / trəˈdɪʃənl ˈkæpə / (BrE)
A ceremonial cloak worn during special occasions in Italy, especially in traditional or religious ceremonies.
ceremonial cloak from Italy

Examples

The cardinal attended the event wearing a traditional cappa that dated back several centuries.
During the procession, each participant donned a traditional cappa as a sign of respect.
Historians studied the fabrics and embroidery on the traditional cappa displayed in the museum.

Synset Examples

In Italy, a traditional cappa is often worn during ceremonies.

Etymology

From Italian 'cappa', meaning 'cape' or 'cloak'.
