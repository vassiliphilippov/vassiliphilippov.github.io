West Nile virus

1. NOUN

/ wɛst ˈnaɪl ˈvaɪrəs / (AmE), / wɛst ˈnaɪl ˈvaɪərəs / (BrE)
a virus of the family Flaviviridae that causes West Nile encephalitis, transmitted primarily by mosquitoes and affecting humans and other animals
virus causing West Nile encephalitis
medical

Examples

The West Nile virus is most often spread through mosquito bites.
Researchers are developing a vaccine to prevent infections caused by the West Nile virus.
Birds can act as hosts for the West Nile virus, contributing to its spread in certain areas.

Etymology

Named after the West Nile district of Uganda, where it was first identified in 1937.
