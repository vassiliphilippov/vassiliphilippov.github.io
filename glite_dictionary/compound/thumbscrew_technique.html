thumbscrew technique

1. NOUN

/ ˈθʌmˌskru tɛkˈnik / (AmE), / ˈθʌmˌskruː tɛkˈniːk / (BrE)
a method of applying strong pressure or coercion to persuade someone to act or comply
method of applying pressure or coercion

Examples

The manager resorted to a thumbscrew technique to push employees to meet the deadline.
During negotiations, the lawyer applied a thumbscrew technique to get the best deal for her client.
Some coaches use the thumbscrew technique to extract peak performance from their players.

Synset Examples

He used a thumbscrew technique to motivate the team.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pressure tactics - methods used to coerce or strongly persuade
  • Synonym: hard sell - aggressive sales tactics or persuasion
  • Antonym: soft sell - subtle and gentle persuasion methods

Etymology

From 'thumbscrew,' a historical torture device, used metaphorically to mean applying pressure.
