drop keel

1. NOUN

/ drɑp kil / (AmE), / drɒp kiːl / (BrE)
A retractable fin keel fitted to a sailboat, designed to prevent drifting to leeward.
retractable keel on a sailboat

Examples

The yacht was equipped with a drop keel that allowed her to navigate shallow waters easily.
Sailors lowered the drop keel to improve the boat's stability in strong winds.
Maintenance on the drop keel is essential to ensure it functions smoothly during races.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: centerboard - a retractable keel which can be raised or lowered vertically in a slot
  • Antonym: fixed keel - a keel that is permanently attached and cannot be retracted
