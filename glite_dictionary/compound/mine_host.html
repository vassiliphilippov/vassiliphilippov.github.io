mine host

1. NOUN

/ maɪn hoʊst / (AmE), / maɪn həʊst / (BrE)
a man who is the host of an event, gathering, or establishment such as an inn, especially in a traditional or literary context
male host or innkeeper (traditional)
archaic

Examples

Our mine host welcomed everyone with a warm smile as we entered the inn.
It was mine host who ensured that every guest felt at home during the celebration.
You could always count on mine host to regale visitors with fascinating stories.

Synset Examples

He was the mine host for the evening.
The mine host served delicious appetizers.
As your mine host, I welcome you warmly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: host - a person who receives or entertains guests

Etymology

From the archaic use of 'mine' as a possessive pronoun before words beginning with a vowel or silent 'h', historically used in English.
