simple interest

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪmpəl ˈɪnt(ə)rəst / (AmE), / ˈsɪmpəl ˈɪnt(ə)rɪst / (BrE)
interest calculated only on the original amount of money (the principal), not on the interest earned or paid previously.
interest on the principal only

Examples

The loan was calculated using simple interest, making the total repayment very straightforward.
With simple interest, you only pay interest on the initial amount you borrowed, not on any accumulated interest.
He explained the difference between simple interest and compound interest to the students.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: compound interest - interest calculated on both the principal and accumulated interest
