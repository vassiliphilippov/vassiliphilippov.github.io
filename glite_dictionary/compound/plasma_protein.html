plasma protein

1. NOUN

/ ˈplæz.mə ˈproʊˌtin / (AmE), / ˈplaz.mə ˈprəʊ.tiːn / (BrE)
any of the proteins present in blood plasma, such as albumin, globulin, or fibrinogen
protein found in blood plasma
medical

Examples

Albumin is the most abundant plasma protein in human blood.
Doctors often measure plasma protein levels to check liver and kidney function.
A decrease in plasma protein concentration can be a sign of malnutrition.
