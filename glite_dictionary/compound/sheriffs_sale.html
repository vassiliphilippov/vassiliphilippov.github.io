sheriff's sale

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃɛɹɪfs seɪl / (AmE), / ˈʃɛɹɪfs seɪl / (BrE)
A public sale of property conducted by a sheriff, usually by court order, to satisfy an unpaid debt or obligation.
forced public sale of property by sheriff
legal american

Examples

The bank initiated a sheriff's sale after the homeowner defaulted on the mortgage.
At the sheriff's sale, several bidders competed for the foreclosed property.
After losing the lawsuit, his assets were liquidated at a sheriff's sale to pay the court judgment.
