French horn

1. NOUN

/ frɛntʃ hɔrn / (AmE), / frɛntʃ hɔːn / (BrE)
a brass musical instrument with a coiled conical tube and valves, producing a mellow, rounded tone
brass instrument with coiled tube and valves

Examples

She practiced the French horn for two hours every evening in preparation for the concert.
The orchestra featured a brilliant solo by the French horn player.
He chose to learn the French horn because of its rich, expressive sound.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horn - a brass instrument with a metal tube that is coiled into a spiral and flares out at the end

Etymology

From French, referring to the style of hunting horn popularized in France in the 17th–18th centuries.
