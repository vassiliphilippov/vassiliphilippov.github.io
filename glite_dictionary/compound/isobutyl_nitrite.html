isobutyl nitrite

1. NOUN

/ ˌaɪsoʊˈbjuːtɪl ˈnaɪˌtraɪt / (AmE), / ˌaɪsəʊˈbjuːtɪl ˈnaɪtraɪt / (BrE)
a colorless, pungent liquid compound sometimes used recreationally as a stimulant or vasodilator
a pungent stimulant liquid compound
medical

Examples

The possession and sale of isobutyl nitrite are regulated in many countries due to its potential misuse.
Some club-goers use isobutyl nitrite for its brief euphoric and relaxing effects.
Exposure to isobutyl nitrite can cause dizziness, headaches, and other short-term health effects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amyl nitrite - a similar nitrite compound used as a vasodilator and recreational drug
  • Synonym: alkyl nitrite - a class of compounds including isobutyl nitrite used medically and recreationally

Etymology

From 'iso-' (Greek, equal), 'butyl' (from butane), and 'nitrite' (from Latin 'nitrum').
