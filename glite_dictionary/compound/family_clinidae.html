family Clinidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ˈklaɪnɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli ˈklɪnɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of small, elongated, viviparous blennies found in temperate and tropical seas, known for inhabiting rocky reefs and kelp beds.
family of viviparous blennies

Examples

The family Clinidae includes numerous species of kelp blennies found along rocky coastlines.
Marine biologists often study the reproductive behavior of fishes in the family Clinidae.
Members of the family Clinidae are characterized by their elongated bodies and live-bearing habits.

Etymology

From New Latin 'Clinidae', the family name based on the type genus 'Clinus'.
