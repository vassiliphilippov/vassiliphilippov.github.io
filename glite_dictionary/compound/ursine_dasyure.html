ursine dasyure

1. NOUN

/ ˈɜrˌsaɪn ˈdæziˌjʊər / (AmE), / ˈɜːsaɪn ˈdæzɪjʊə / (BrE)
A small, fierce carnivorous marsupial native to New Guinea, with a mostly black coat and a long tail.
carnivorous marsupial of New Guinea

Examples

The ursine dasyure is known for its aggressive nature despite its small size.
Researchers observed the ursine dasyure hunting insects at night in the dense forests of New Guinea.
Unlike other marsupials, the ursine dasyure has a distinctly bear-like appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marsupial - a mammal of the order Marsupialia

Etymology

From Latin 'ursus' meaning 'bear', and Greek 'dasys' meaning 'hairy' plus 'oura' meaning 'tail'.
