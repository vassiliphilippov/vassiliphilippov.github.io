summer savory

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌmɚ ˈseɪvəri / (AmE), / ˈsʌmə ˈseɪvəri / (BrE)
an annual herb (Satureja hortensis) with aromatic, delicately flavored leaves commonly used as a seasoning in cooking
an aromatic culinary herb

Examples

Fresh summer savory adds a subtle flavor to bean dishes and soups.
She planted summer savory alongside her other herbs in the garden.
The recipe calls for a teaspoon of chopped summer savory to enhance the aroma.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: winter savory - a related perennial herb, Satureja montana, with a stronger flavor

2. NOUN

/ ˈsʌmɚ ˈseɪvəri / (AmE), / ˈsʌmə ˈseɪvəri / (BrE)
An annual herb (Satureja hortensis) with aromatic oval leaves and small pink flowers, used as a seasoning in cooking, especially for meats, soups, and salads.
aromatic culinary herb (Satureja hortensis)

Examples

She sprinkled summer savory on the roasted chicken for added flavor.
Fresh summer savory was the key ingredient in her family’s traditional soup.
You can grow summer savory in your herb garden all summer long.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: savory - any of several aromatic herbs of the genus Satureja
