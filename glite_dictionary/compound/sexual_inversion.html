sexual inversion

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛkʃuəl ɪnˈvɜrʒən / (AmE), / ˈsɛkʃuəl ɪnˈvɜːʃən / (BrE)
an archaic term for adopting the gender role or sexual behavior typical of the opposite sex
archaic term for cross-gender role or behavior
archaic

Examples

In nineteenth-century medical literature, the term sexual inversion was commonly used to describe homosexuality.
The concept of sexual inversion is now considered outdated and has largely been replaced by more accurate terms.
Early sexologists debated whether sexual inversion was an inborn trait or a mental disorder.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: homosexuality - sexual attraction to people of one's own sex
  • Synonym: transgender - relating to people whose gender identity differs from sex assigned at birth
