Boltzmann's constant

1. NOUN

/ ˈboʊltsmənz ˈkɑnstənt / (AmE), / ˈbɒltsmənz ˈkɒnstənt / (BrE)
a physical constant (symbol k) used in physics, relating the average kinetic energy of particles in a gas to temperature, and appearing in equations such as the ideal gas law
a physical constant in thermodynamics and physics

Examples

The value of Boltzmann's constant is fundamental to calculations in statistical mechanics.
Scientists use Boltzmann's constant when determining the energy distribution of particles in a gas.
The formula for entropy in thermodynamics includes Boltzmann's constant as a key parameter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Avogadro's number - the number of constituent particles in one mole of a substance

Etymology

Named after Austrian physicist Ludwig Boltzmann (1844–1906).
