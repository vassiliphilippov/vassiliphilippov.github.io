genus Abies

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˈeɪbiːz / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈeɪbiːz / (BrE)
A genus of evergreen coniferous trees known as the true firs, native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.
genus of evergreen fir trees

Examples

The genus Abies includes many species found in mountainous regions across Europe and North America.
Botanists classify true firs under the scientific name genus Abies.
A mature tree from the genus Abies can reach impressive heights in suitable habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fir - common name for trees of the genus Abies

Etymology

From Latin 'Abies' (fir tree), used as a genus name in scientific classification.
