Texas millet

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛksəs ˈmɪlət / (AmE), / ˈtɛksəs ˈmɪlɪt / (BrE)
an annual weedy grass, commonly grown for hay, found especially in the southern United States
weedy grass used for hay

Examples

Farmers in the region often plant Texas millet as a reliable source of hay for their livestock.
The field was overgrown with Texas millet after a season of heavy rain.
Local agriculture experts recommend cutting Texas millet before it goes to seed for the best hay quality.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: millet - a type of small-seeded grass grown as cereal crops or for fodder
