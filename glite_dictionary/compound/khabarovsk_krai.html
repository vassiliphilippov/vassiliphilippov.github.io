Khabarovsk Krai

1. PROPN

/ kəˈbɑːrɒfsk kraɪ / (AmE), / kəˈbɑːrəfsk kraɪ / (BrE)
an administrative territory in the Russian Far East, located on the eastern coast of Siberia, with Khabarovsk as its administrative center
territory in eastern Russia (Siberia)

Examples

Many foreign investors have shown interest in projects in Khabarovsk Krai.
The Amur River forms a natural border between Khabarovsk Krai and China.
Khabarovsk Krai is known for its vast forests and rich natural resources.

Etymology

From Russian 'Хабаровский край' (Khabarovskiy kray), named after Yerofey Khabarov, a Russian explorer.
