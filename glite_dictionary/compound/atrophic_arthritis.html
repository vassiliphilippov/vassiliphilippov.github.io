atrophic arthritis

1. NOUN

/ əˈtroʊfɪk ɑrˈθraɪtɪs / (AmE), / əˈtrɒfɪk ɑːˈθraɪtɪs / (BrE)
A chronic autoimmune disease characterized by inflammation and wasting of the joints, causing deformities and destruction of joint tissues.
autoimmune joint disease, causes inflammation and deformities
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with atrophic arthritis after experiencing persistent joint pain and swelling.
Advanced cases of atrophic arthritis can result in severe joint deformities and loss of mobility.
Early treatment of atrophic arthritis may help prevent permanent damage to the joints.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rheumatoid arthritis - a common type of chronic inflammatory arthritis

Etymology

From Greek 'atrophia' (lack of nourishment, wasting) and 'arthritis' (inflammation of the joints).
