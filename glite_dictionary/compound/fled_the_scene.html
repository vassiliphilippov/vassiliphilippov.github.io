to fled the scene

1. VERB

/ flɛd ðə siːn / (AmE), / flɛd ðə siːn / (BrE)
to leave a place quickly, especially to avoid being caught after committing or witnessing an incident
left a place quickly, often after an incident

Examples

The suspect fled the scene before police could arrive.
Witnesses reported that the driver fled the scene after causing the accident.
Despite efforts to stop him, he fled the scene in a hurry.

Synset Examples

The gunman fled the scene after the robbery.
The assailant fled the scene before anyone arrived.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: escape - to get away from a place or situation
  • Antonym: remain at the scene - to stay at the location where an incident occurred
