bluetongue disease

1. NOUN

/ ˈbluːˌtʌŋ dɪˈziːz / (AmE), / ˈbluːˌtʌŋ dɪˈziːz / (BrE)
A viral disease affecting livestock, especially sheep and cattle, characterized by inflammation, fever, and swelling of the tongue and mouth.
viral disease of livestock
medical

Examples

The veterinarian confirmed that several cattle had contracted bluetongue disease during the outbreak.
Farmers were advised to vaccinate their sheep against bluetongue disease after recent cases in the region.
Symptoms of bluetongue disease include swelling of the tongue and difficulty breathing in infected animals.

Synset Examples

The vet diagnosed the sheep with bluetongue disease.
