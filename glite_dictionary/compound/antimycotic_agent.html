antimycotic agent

1. NOUN

/ ˌæntiˌmaɪˈkɑtɪk ˈeɪdʒənt / (AmE), / ˌæntiˌmaɪˈkɒtɪk ˈeɪdʒənt / (BrE)
a substance that destroys or inhibits the growth of fungi
substance fighting fungal growth
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed an antimycotic agent to treat her fungal infection.
Proper application of an antimycotic agent can help prevent athlete's foot.
Many antimycotic agents are available both in oral and topical formulations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antifungal - a drug or substance that prevents or treats fungal infections

Etymology

From Greek anti- (against) + mykēs (fungus) + -otic (relating to) + agent (Latin agentem, doing).
