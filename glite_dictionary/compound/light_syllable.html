light syllable

1. NOUN

/ laɪt ˈsɪləbəl / (AmE), / laɪt ˈsɪləbəl / (BrE)
A syllable that contains a short vowel as its nucleus and has no coda consonant, often important in phonology and poetic meter.
syllable with short vowel and no coda

Examples

In classical poetry, a light syllable often appears at the end of a line.
The word 'papa' consists of two light syllables.
Understanding the difference between a heavy and a light syllable helps in analyzing verse rhythm.

Synset Examples

A light syllable consists of a single short vowel in an open syllable.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: heavy syllable - a syllable with a long vowel, diphthong, or coda consonant
