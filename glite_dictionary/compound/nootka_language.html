Nootka language

1. NOUN

/ ˈnuːtkə ˌlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈnuːtkə ˌlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
The indigenous Wakashan language spoken by the Nootka people of the Pacific Northwest.
indigenous language of the Nootka people

Examples

Few people today speak the Nootka language, which is at risk of disappearing.
Linguists have worked to document and preserve the Nootka language for future generations.
He studied the Nootka language as part of his research on Wakashan languages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Wakashan - a group of related indigenous languages of the Pacific Northwest

Etymology

Named after the Nootka people, indigenous to Vancouver Island, British Columbia.
