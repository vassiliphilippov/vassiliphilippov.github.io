parsonish behavior

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːrsənɪʃ bəˈheɪvjər / (AmE), / ˈpɑːsənɪʃ bɪˈheɪvjə / (BrE)
Behavior that is characteristic of a clergyman, especially in being overly moralistic or prim.
behavior resembling that of a clergyman

Examples

Her parsonish behavior often made social gatherings feel more restrained than necessary.
Some students found his parsonish behavior comforting, while others saw it as judgmental.
He was respected for his parsonish behavior and unwavering adherence to his principles.

Synset Examples

His parsonish behavior earned him many friends.
His parsonish demeanor made him a respected community leader.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parsonic - of or relating to a parson
  • Synonym: moralistic - overly concerned with morals

Etymology

From 'parson' (clergyman) + '-ish' (having the characteristics of) + 'behavior.'
