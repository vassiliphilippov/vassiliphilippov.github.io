Old Prussian

1. NOUN

/ oʊld ˈprʌʃən / (AmE), / əʊld ˈprʌʃən / (BrE)
an extinct Baltic language once spoken by the original (non-German) inhabitants of Prussia, extinct after 1700
extinct Baltic language of Prussia

Examples

Scholars have reconstructed parts of Old Prussian using religious texts and glossaries.
The extinction of Old Prussian was accelerated by German colonization and assimilation.
She is researching the vocabulary of Old Prussian for her thesis on Baltic linguistics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Lithuanian - the official language of Lithuania, a living Baltic language
  • Synonym: Latvian - the official language of Latvia, a living Baltic language

Etymology

Old Prussian refers to the language of the indigenous Prussians, distinct from the Germanic Prussians; part of the Baltic branch of Indo-European.
