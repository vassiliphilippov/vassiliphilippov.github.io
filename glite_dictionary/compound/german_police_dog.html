German police dog

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɜrmən pəˈlis dɒg / (AmE), / ˈdʒɜːmən pəˈliːs dɒg / (BrE)
A breed of large, intelligent dog (the German Shepherd), known for its use in police work and as a guide for the blind.
breed of large working dog

Examples

The German police dog quickly located the missing hiker in the dense forest.
Many law enforcement agencies rely on the loyal nature of the German police dog.
Thanks to its intelligence, the German police dog is often trained as a guide for the blind.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: German shepherd - a breed of large, intelligent herding and working dog

Etymology

Named for its origin in Germany and its typical roles in police service.
