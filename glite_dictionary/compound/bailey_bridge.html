Bailey bridge

1. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪli brɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈbeɪli brɪdʒ / (BrE)
A portable, pre-fabricated truss bridge that can be rapidly assembled for temporary use, especially in military or emergency situations.
portable temporary truss bridge

Examples

After the flood destroyed the old crossing, engineers constructed a Bailey bridge to restore access to the village.
The military convoy safely crossed the river using a Bailey bridge built earlier that day.
Due to its modular design, a Bailey bridge can be quickly assembled even in difficult terrain.

Etymology

Named after Sir Donald Bailey, the British engineer who designed it during World War II.
