close-order drill

1. NOUN

/ ˈkloʊs ˌɔrdɚ drɪl / (AmE), / ˈkləʊs ˌɔːdə drɪl / (BrE)
a military drill in which troops march shoulder-to-shoulder in standard formation, typically used for parade or ceremonial purposes
military marching in close formation

Examples

The soldiers practiced their close-order drill every morning to ensure perfect coordination on parade day.
Learning close-order drill is essential for new recruits in basic training.
During the ceremony, the unit demonstrated an impressive close-order drill that impressed the audience.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: open-order drill - military drill where troops are spaced out, not shoulder-to-shoulder
