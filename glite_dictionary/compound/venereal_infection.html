venereal infection

1. NOUN

/ vəˈnɪriəl ɪnˈfɛkʃən / (AmE), / vɪˈnɪəriəl ɪnˈfɛkʃən / (BrE)
An infection that is transmitted through sexual intercourse or genital contact.
sexually transmitted infection
medical

Examples

Syphilis is a common type of venereal infection that can have serious health consequences if left untreated.
The doctor explained the risks of contracting a venereal infection and how to prevent it.
He was embarrassed to discover he had contracted a venereal infection during his travels.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sexually transmitted disease - disease spread by sexual contact
  • Degree Higher: contagion - a disease spread by close contact

Etymology

From Latin 'venereus' (pertaining to sexual love) and Latin 'infectio' (infection).
