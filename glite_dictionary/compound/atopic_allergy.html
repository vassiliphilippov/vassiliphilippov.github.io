atopic allergy

1. NOUN

/ əˈtoʊpɪk ˈælərdʒi / (AmE), / əˈtɒpɪk ˈælədʒi / (BrE)
A type of allergic reaction that occurs minutes after exposure in a person who is sensitized, often associated with conditions like eczema or hay fever.
quick-acting allergic reaction
medical

Examples

Her doctor diagnosed her with an atopic allergy after she developed symptoms minutes after exposure to pollen.
People with an atopic allergy may experience hives, sneezing, or itching almost immediately.
Children are particularly prone to atopic allergy, especially if there is a family history of allergies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: immediate hypersensitivity - allergic reaction manifesting very quickly after exposure
  • Antonym: delayed allergy - allergic reaction that takes longer to appear after exposure

Etymology

From Greek 'atopia' (strangeness) and 'allergy' from German 'Allergie' (changed reaction).
