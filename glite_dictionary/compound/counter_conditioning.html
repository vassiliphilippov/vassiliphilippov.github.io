counter conditioning

1. NOUN

/ ˈkaʊn.tɚ kənˌdɪ.ʃən.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkaʊn.tə kənˌdɪʃ.ən.ɪŋ / (BrE)
a method in behavior therapy where a new response, incompatible with a previous conditioned response, is learned in reaction to a stimulus
behavior therapy technique replacing responses
medical

Examples

Counter conditioning is often used in therapy to help clients overcome phobias by replacing fear with relaxation.
The dog’s anxiety around strangers was reduced through counter conditioning.
Many behavior therapists employ counter conditioning techniques in their practice.

Synset Examples

counter conditioning lies behind many of the procedures used in behavior therapy

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: extinction - the process of weakening and disappearance of a conditioned response

Etymology

From 'counter-' (against, in opposition to) + 'conditioning' (from Latin 'condicionare', to train or accustom).
