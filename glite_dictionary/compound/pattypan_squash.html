pattypan squash

1. NOUN

/ ˈpætiˌpæn skwɑʃ / (AmE), / ˈpætipan skwɒʃ / (BrE)
a small, round summer squash with one flattened face and a scalloped edge, typically greenish-white or yellow
a type of small, scalloped summer squash

Examples

We roasted the pattypan squash with olive oil and herbs for dinner last night.
The market stalls were filled with bright yellow pattypan squash and other summer vegetables.
She sliced the pattypan squash into wedges for the salad.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: squash - an edible gourd, usually belonging to the genus Cucurbita

Etymology

From 'pattypan', resembling the shape of a patty pan (a small baking tin).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpætiˌpæn skwɑʃ / (AmE), / ˈpætipan skwɒʃ / (BrE)
A type of summer squash with a small, round, and flattened shape and scalloped edges, often pale green or white.
round, scalloped summer squash

Examples

She sautéed slices of pattypan squash with garlic and olive oil for a light summer dish.
The farmer’s market had baskets filled with fresh pattypan squash in a variety of colors.
I love the decorative shape of pattypan squash in vegetable stir-fries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: summer squash - any squash harvested while immature and tender, typically eaten with skin
