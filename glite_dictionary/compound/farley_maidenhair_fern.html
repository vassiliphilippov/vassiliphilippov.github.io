Farley maidenhair fern

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɑːr.li ˈmeɪ.dənˌhɛr fɝːn / (AmE), / ˈfɑː.li ˈmeɪ.dənˌheə fɜːn / (BrE)
A species of maidenhair fern (Adiantum farleyense) named after Farley Hill, Barbados, where it was first discovered.
a species of maidenhair fern

Examples

The Farley maidenhair fern is prized for its delicate foliage in ornamental gardens.
Botanical enthusiasts gathered to view the rare Farley maidenhair fern at the conservatory.
She identified the plant as a Farley maidenhair fern due to its unique leaf shape and growth pattern.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maidenhair fern - a fern of the genus Adiantum with delicate, fan-shaped leaves

Etymology

Named after Farley Hill, a country house in Barbados, and maidenhair fern (genus Adiantum).
