thorny amaranth

1. NOUN

/ ˈθɔrni ˈæmərəˌnθ / (AmE), / ˈθɔːni ˈaməranθ / (BrE)
an erect annual plant native to tropical central Asia and Africa, characterized by pairs of sharp spines at most leaf nodes
a spiny annual plant of tropical Asia and Africa

Examples

The thorny amaranth thrives in dry, sandy soils throughout tropical Africa and central Asia.
Farmers often remove thorny amaranth from their fields because of its sharp spines.
Researchers studied the medicinal properties of the thorny amaranth plant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amaranth - any of various plants of the genus Amaranthus
