family Alaudidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli æˌlɔdəˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli æˌlɔːdɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of small to medium-sized songbirds commonly known as larks, found mostly in the Old World.
family of birds; larks

Examples

The family Alaudidae includes many species renowned for their elaborate songs and aerial displays.
Most members of the family Alaudidae are found in open habitats such as grasslands and deserts.
Variation in plumage color among the family Alaudidae helps the birds blend into their environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lark - a small ground-dwelling songbird

Etymology

From scientific Latin 'Alaudidae', based on 'Alauda' (lark).
