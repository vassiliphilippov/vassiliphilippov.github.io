manila paper

1. NOUN

/ məˈnɪl.ə ˈpeɪ.pɚ / (AmE), / məˈnɪl.ə ˈpeɪ.pə / (BrE)
a strong, smooth, light brown paper or thin cardboard made from Manila hemp or similar fibers, commonly used for envelopes, file folders, and craft work
strong light brown paper

Examples

She drew a diagram on a large sheet of manila paper for her presentation.
The students used manila paper to make colorful posters in art class.
Please submit your application in a manila paper envelope.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kraft paper - a strong brown paper used for packaging, often made from wood pulp

Etymology

Named after Manila hemp, originally from Manila, the capital of the Philippines.
