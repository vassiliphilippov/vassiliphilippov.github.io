battle of Panipat

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæt̬əl əv pɑːˈniːpɑːt / (AmE), / ˈbætl əv ˌpæniːˈpæt / (BrE)
a decisive battle in 1761 in which the Afghan ruler Ahmad Shah Durrani defeated the Marathas, marking a major turning point in Indian history
battle in 1761 between Afghans and Marathas in India

Examples

Historians regard the battle of Panipat as one of the most important conflicts in Indian history.
The Marathas suffered a devastating defeat at the battle of Panipat in 1761.
Many films and books depict the events leading up to the battle of Panipat.

Etymology

Panipat is a city in northern India; battle named for its location.
