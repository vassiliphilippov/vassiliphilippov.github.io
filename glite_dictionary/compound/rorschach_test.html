Rorschach test

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɔrʃɑk tɛst / (AmE), / ˈrɔːʃæk tɛst / (BrE)
a psychological test in which a person is asked to interpret a series of inkblots to reveal underlying thoughts and feelings
psychological inkblot test

Examples

The psychologist administered a Rorschach test to help assess the patient's personality.
Her answers during the Rorschach test were unusually detailed and imaginative.
The Rorschach test remains controversial, but some therapists still use it in clinical practice.

Etymology

Named after Hermann Rorschach (1884–1922), Swiss psychiatrist who developed the test.

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɔrʃɑk tɛst / (AmE), / ˈrɔːʃæk tɛst / (BrE)
a psychological test in which a person's interpretations of inkblot images are analyzed to assess personality traits and emotional functioning
inkblot-based personality test

Examples

The psychologist administered a Rorschach test to better understand the patient's inner thoughts.
Her unusual responses during the Rorschach test led to further discussion in therapy.
Many people are familiar with the concept of the Rorschach test from movies and books.

Synset Examples

He saw a butterfly in the Rorschach test's inkblot.
The therapist used a Rorschach test to assess his personality.
Her interpretation of the Rorschach test revealed a lot about her.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inkblot test - a test using inkblots to interpret personality

Named after Hermann Rorschach (1884–1922), Swiss psychiatrist who developed the test.
