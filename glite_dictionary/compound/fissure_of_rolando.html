fissure of Rolando

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪʃɚ əv roʊˈlændoʊ / (AmE), / ˈfɪʃə əv rəʊˈlændəʊ / (BrE)
a deep groove on the lateral surface of the brain, separating the frontal lobe from the parietal lobe; also called the central sulcus
brain groove between frontal and parietal lobes
medical

Examples

The fissure of Rolando marks the boundary between the frontal and parietal lobes in the brain.
Damage near the fissure of Rolando can affect both sensory and motor functions.
Neurosurgeons use the fissure of Rolando as an important landmark during brain surgery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: central sulcus - the same brain groove as the fissure of Rolando

Etymology

Named after Luigi Rolando (1773–1831), Italian anatomist.
