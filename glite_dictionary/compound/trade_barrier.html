trade barrier

1. NOUN

/ ˈtreɪd ˌbæriɚ / (AmE), / ˈtreɪd ˌbæriə / (BrE)
A government regulation or policy that restricts international trade, such as tariffs, quotas, or import bans.
restriction on international trade

Examples

The government imposed a new trade barrier to protect domestic industries from foreign competition.
Negotiations aimed to reduce trade barriers between the two countries.
Many economists argue that eliminating trade barriers promotes economic growth and benefits consumers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tariff - a tax or duty to be paid on a particular class of imports or exports
  • Synonym: quota - a fixed share of something that a person or group is entitled to receive or is bound to contribute
  • Antonym: free trade - international trade left to its natural course without tariffs or other restrictions
