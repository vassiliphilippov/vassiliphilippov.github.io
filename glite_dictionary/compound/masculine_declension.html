masculine declension

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæskjəlɪn dɪˈklɛnʃən / (AmE), / ˈmæskjʊlɪn dɪˈklɛnʃən / (BrE)
the set of inflectional forms of nouns, pronouns, adjectives, or articles used to indicate masculine grammatical gender in a language
inflection for masculine gender

Examples

In Latin, the masculine declension differs from the feminine and neuter forms in both endings and agreement.
Students must memorize the patterns of the masculine declension to properly use nouns in Russian.
Some languages have a distinct masculine declension for adjectives, affecting the way they modify nouns.

Synset Examples

Feminine declension treats ‘k’, ‘g’, ‘ch’ as softenable, whereas masculine and neuter declension does not (hence they take the ‘other’ ending, ‘-u’).

Related Terms

  • Antonym: feminine declension - inflection indicating feminine grammatical gender
  • Antonym: neuter declension - inflection indicating neuter grammatical gender

Etymology

From Latin 'masculinus' (male) + 'declinatio' (inflection, declining).
