Federal Deposit Insurance Corporation

1. PROPN

/ ˌfɛdərəl dɪˈpɑzɪt ɪnˈʃʊrəns kɔˌpɔrˈeɪʃn̩ / (AmE), / ˌfɛdərəl dɪˈpɒzɪt ɪnˈʃʊərəns kɔːpəˈreɪʃn̩ / (BrE)
A United States government agency that insures deposits at banks and other qualified financial institutions against loss in case of bank failure.
U.S. agency insuring bank deposits
american

Examples

All banks insured by the Federal Deposit Insurance Corporation adhere to strict national regulations.
After the 2008 financial crisis, the role of the Federal Deposit Insurance Corporation became more prominent.
Depositors can rest assured that their savings are protected by the Federal Deposit Insurance Corporation.
