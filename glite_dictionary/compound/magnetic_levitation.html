magnetic levitation

1. NOUN

/ mæɡˌnɛtɪk ˌlɛvəˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / mæɡˌnɛtɪk ˌlɛvɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
A method by which an object, such as a train, is lifted and suspended above a surface by magnetic forces, allowing it to move without contact or friction.
suspension by magnetic forces

Examples

The magnetic levitation train glided smoothly above the track, making almost no noise.
Advances in magnetic levitation technology have led to faster and more efficient transport systems.
Researchers are exploring the use of magnetic levitation for new types of urban commuting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maglev - (noun) a train or transport system using magnetic levitation
