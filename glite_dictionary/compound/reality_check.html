reality check

1. NOUN

/ riˈæləti tʃɛk / (AmE), / riˈalɪti tʃɛk / (BrE)
an occasion or event that makes someone realize the true nature of a situation, especially when their beliefs or expectations are unrealistic
occasion revealing the true situation

Examples

The team received a reality check when they lost their first match of the season.
After moving out on her own, she got a reality check about the cost of living.
His failure in the exam was a harsh reality check that motivated him to work harder.

Synset Examples

this program is intended as a reality check for CEOs
after all those elaborate productions, I felt in need of a reality check

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wake-up call - something that causes someone to become alert to a problem or danger

2. NOUN

/ ɹiˈæləti tʃɛk / (AmE), / ɹɪˈalɪti tʃɛk / (BrE)
an event, assessment, or reminder that reveals the true state of affairs, often correcting unrealistic expectations or misunderstandings
reminder or assessment revealing the real situation

Examples

After the initial excitement faded, the harsh criticism provided a much-needed reality check.
Losing the championship game served as a reality check for the team.
His friends gave him a reality check when he started getting too arrogant.

Synset Examples

His comments provided a much-needed reality check.
I need a reality check after that meeting.
She got a reality check when she failed the test.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wake-up call - a warning or event that causes people to become alert to a problem or danger
