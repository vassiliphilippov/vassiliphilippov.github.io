immune gamma globulin

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈmjun ˈɡæmə ˈɡlɑblən / (AmE), / ɪˈmjuːn ˈɡæmə ˈɡlɒblɪn / (BrE)
a class of proteins in the blood produced by lymph tissue, functioning as antibodies in the immune system's response to pathogens
antibody protein produced in lymph tissue
medical

Examples

Injection of immune gamma globulin can provide temporary protection against certain diseases.
Doctors may administer immune gamma globulin to patients with weakened immune systems.
Researchers studied the effectiveness of immune gamma globulin therapy in treating viral infections.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antibody - a protein produced by the immune system to neutralize pathogens

Etymology

From 'immune', from Latin 'immunis' (free from), and 'gamma globulin', referring to a protein fraction identified by its position in electrophoresis.
