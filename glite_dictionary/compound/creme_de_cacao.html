creme de cacao

1. NOUN

/ krɛm də kəˈkaʊ / (AmE), / krɛm də kaˈkaʊ / (BrE)
A sweet liqueur flavored with cacao beans and vanilla, commonly used in cocktails and desserts.
sweet chocolate-flavored liqueur

Examples

She added a splash of creme de cacao to the cocktail for a rich chocolate flavor.
The recipe calls for both white and dark creme de cacao to achieve the perfect balance.
A classic grasshopper is made with cream, mint liqueur, and creme de cacao.

Etymology

From French, meaning 'cream of cacao', referring to its cacao flavor despite lacking cream.
