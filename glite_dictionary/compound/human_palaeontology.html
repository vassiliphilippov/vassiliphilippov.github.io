human palaeontology

1. NOUN

/ ˈhjumən ˌpæli.ɑnˈtɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ˈhjuːmən ˌpælɪ.ɒnˈtɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the scientific study of human fossils, focusing on the evolution and origins of the human species
study of human fossils

Examples

She decided to specialize in human palaeontology after developing an interest in ancient human remains.
Advances in human palaeontology have reshaped our understanding of early hominids.
The museum's new exhibit highlights discoveries made through human palaeontology.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: palaeontology - the scientific study of fossils

Etymology

From Latin 'humanus' (human) and Greek 'palaiós' (ancient) + 'ont-' (being) + '-logia' (study).
