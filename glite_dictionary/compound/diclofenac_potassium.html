diclofenac potassium

1. NOUN

/ daɪˌkloʊfəˌnæk pəˈtæsiəm / (AmE), / dɪˌkləʊfənæk pəˈtæsɪəm / (BrE)
A nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drug (NSAID) used to treat pain and inflammation, commonly known by the trade name Cataflam.
NSAID for pain and inflammation
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed diclofenac potassium to relieve her severe migraine pain.
Patients are advised to take diclofenac potassium with food to reduce stomach upset.
Some people may experience side effects when using diclofenac potassium for extended periods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diclofenac sodium - another salt form of diclofenac used as an NSAID
  • Synonym: ibuprofen - another NSAID for pain and inflammation

Etymology

From the names of its chemical components: diclofenac (derived from dichlorophenyl and acetic acid) and potassium.
