clack valve

1. NOUN

/ klæk ˈvælv / (AmE), / klæk ˈvɑːlv / (BrE)
a simple valve hinged on one side that permits fluid to flow in only one direction
a one-way hinged valve

Examples

The old pump was fitted with a clack valve to prevent backflow.
Engineers checked the clack valve to ensure it was not stuck open.
The noisy clack valve signaled that water was flowing successfully.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: check valve - a valve that allows fluid to flow in only one direction

Etymology

From 'clack', denoting the sound made by the valve, and 'valve', from Latin 'valva' (leaf of a door).
