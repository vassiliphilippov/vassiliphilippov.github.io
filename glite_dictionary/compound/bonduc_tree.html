bonduc tree

1. NOUN

/ bɑnˈdʌk tri / (AmE), / bɒnˈdʌk triː / (BrE)
A tropical tree bearing large, prickly pods of seeds that resemble beans, often used to make jewelry and rosaries.
tropical tree with bean-like, ornamental seeds

Examples

The seeds of the bonduc tree are often polished and strung into decorative necklaces.
Local artisans craft beads from the pods of the bonduc tree.
Gardeners appreciate the bonduc tree for its unusual prickly pods and attractive foliage.
