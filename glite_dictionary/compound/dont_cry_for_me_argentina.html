don't cry for me, Argentina

1. X

/ doʊnt kraɪ fər mi ɑːrdʒənˈtiːnə / (AmE), / dəʊnt kraɪ fə mi ɑːdʒənˈtiːnə / (BrE)
an expression of deep emotion, longing, or farewell, made famous by the song from the musical Evita
expression of farewell from Evita song

Examples

Every time she leaves a place, she jokes, 'don't cry for me, Argentina,' before saying goodbye.
The phrase 'don't cry for me, Argentina' has become a symbol of dramatic farewells.
He reassured his fans by saying, 'don't cry for me, Argentina,' after announcing his retirement.

Synset Examples

Whenever I feel sad, I recall, don't cry for me, Argentina.
She quoted, don't cry for me, Argentina, during her speech.
He sang, don't cry for me, Argentina, in the shower.

Etymology

From the song 'Don't Cry for Me Argentina' in the 1976 musical Evita, music by Andrew Lloyd Webber, lyrics by Tim Rice.
