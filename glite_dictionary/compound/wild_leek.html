wild leek

1. NOUN

/ waɪld lik / (AmE), / waɪld liːk / (BrE)
a coarse perennial plant with a large bulb and tall stalk bearing greenish purple-tinged flowers, native to the Old World but widely naturalized elsewhere
a coarse perennial plant; large bulb, purple-tinged flowers

Examples

The wild leek blooms in late spring and attracts many pollinators in the meadow.
Researchers studied how the wild leek has adapted to different environments across Europe.
A patch of wild leeks was discovered growing near the edge of the forest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leek - an edible white bulb and long green leaves, related to onion and garlic

2. NOUN

/ waɪld lik / (AmE), / waɪld liːk / (BrE)
A North American perennial plant (Allium tricoccum) with a slender bulb, broad leaves, and clusters of whitish flowers, often referred to as ramps.
North American wild onion plant

Examples

Every spring, foragers gather wild leek in the Appalachian forests.
The menu featured a salad made with fresh wild leek leaves and vinaigrette.
Botanists classify the wild leek as a close relative of garlic and onions.
