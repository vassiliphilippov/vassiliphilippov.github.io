ice mass

1. NOUN

/ aɪs mæs / (AmE), / aɪs mɑːs / (BrE)
a large body of ice, especially one forming part of a glacier, ice sheet, or similar natural structure
a large body of ice

Examples

The Antarctic continent is largely covered by an enormous ice mass.
A retreating ice mass can significantly raise global sea levels.
Scientists study the movement of each ice mass to monitor climate change.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glacier - a large persistent body of dense ice moving under its own weight
