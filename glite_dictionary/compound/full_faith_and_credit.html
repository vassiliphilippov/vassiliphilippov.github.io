full faith and credit

1. NOUN

/ fʊl feɪθ ənd ˈkrɛdɪt / (AmE), / fʊl feɪθ ənd ˈkrɛdɪt / (BrE)
the unconditional guarantee by a government, especially the United States, to repay its debt obligations, including interest and principal
government's unconditional guarantee to repay debt

Examples

The Treasury bills are backed by the full faith and credit of the United States government.
Investors trust bonds that carry the full faith and credit guarantee more than other securities.
These payments are supported by the full faith and credit clause in the U.S. Constitution.
