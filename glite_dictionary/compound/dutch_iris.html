Dutch iris

1. NOUN

/ dʌtʃ ˈaɪrɪs / (AmE), / dʌtʃ ˈaɪərɪs / (BrE)
A species of bulbous iris, cultivated for its showy red-violet or blue flowers, commonly grown in gardens.
a bulbous iris with showy flowers

Examples

The Dutch iris blooms beautifully in late spring, adding vibrant color to the garden.
She planted a row of Dutch iris along the walkway for a stunning floral display.
Florists often use Dutch iris in spring bouquets for their elegant shape and striking hues.

2. NOUN

/ dʌtʃ ˈaɪ.rɪs / (AmE), / dʌtʃ ˈaɪ.rɪs / (BrE)
a type of bulbous iris, often grown as an ornamental plant, with blue or violet flowers, commonly cultivated in gardens
bulbous garden iris with blue flowers

Examples

The Dutch iris blooms beautifully in the spring, adding vibrant color to the garden.
Many florists use Dutch iris in bouquets because of its elegant shape and striking blue petals.
She planted several Dutch irises along the walkway to enhance the landscape.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Spanish iris - a species of iris native to Spain and Portugal, related to the Dutch iris

Etymology

Named for hybrid varieties developed in the Netherlands.
