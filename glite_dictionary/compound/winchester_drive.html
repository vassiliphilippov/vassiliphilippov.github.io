Winchester drive

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪntʃɛstər draɪv / (AmE), / ˈwɪntʃestə draɪv / (BrE)
A type of hard disk drive used in computers, known for its sealed enclosure design that protects the disks from dust and contamination.
type of hard disk drive

Examples

Early computers often used a Winchester drive for data storage and retrieval.
The sealed design of the Winchester drive helped improve reliability over earlier disk systems.
Technicians replaced the faulty Winchester drive with a newer, faster hard disk.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hard disk drive - a device for storing and retrieving digital information using rapidly rotating disks

Etymology

Named after IBM's original code name for the 3340 hard disk drive, inspired by the Winchester rifle.
