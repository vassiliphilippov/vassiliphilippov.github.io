stock-in-trade

1. NOUN

/ stɑk ɪn treɪd / (AmE), / stɒk ɪn treɪd / (BrE)
the typical equipment, skills, or features necessary for someone to do their job or carry out their activity
essential equipment or traits for a profession

Examples

Patience and compassion are a nurse's stock-in-trade.
Sharp pencils and a steady hand were the artist's stock-in-trade.
Quick wit has always been the comedian's stock-in-trade.

Synset Examples

friendliness is the salesman's stock in trade

Etymology

Originally referring to goods and tools kept in a shop for trade; metaphorical use dates from the 18th century.
