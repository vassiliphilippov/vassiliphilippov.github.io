genus Mammut

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈmæm.uːt / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈmæm.juːt / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Mammutidae, consisting of extinct mammals commonly known as mastodons.
genus of extinct mastodons

Examples

The remains of genus Mammut have been found across North America.
Scientists believe that genus Mammut went extinct at the end of the Pleistocene epoch.
Unlike modern elephants, members of the genus Mammut had distinctive cone-shaped cusps on their molar teeth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mastodon - an extinct large, elephant-like mammal

Etymology

From Latin 'mammut', from Russian 'mamot', referring to the mammoth or mastodon.
