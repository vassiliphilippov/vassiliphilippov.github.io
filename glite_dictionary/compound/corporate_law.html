corporate law

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔːr.pər.ət lɔ / (AmE), / ˈkɔː.pər.ət lɔː / (BrE)
the area of law that deals with the formation, operation, and regulation of corporations and other business entities
law concerning corporations and business entities
legal

Examples

After passing the bar exam, she specialized in corporate law at a major firm.
Many aspects of corporate law involve mergers, acquisitions, and shareholder rights.
He is considered an expert in international corporate law.

Synset Examples

He secured a job in corporate law after graduation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: commercial law - the body of law that governs business and commercial transactions

Etymology

From Latin 'corporatus' (made into a body) and Old English 'lagu' (law).
