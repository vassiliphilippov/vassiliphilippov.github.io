old coot

1. NOUN

/ oʊld kuːt / (AmE), / əʊld kuːt / (BrE)
an eccentric, often cranky or odd, old man
eccentric old man
informal

Examples

The old coot sat on the porch telling stories to anyone who would listen.
Everyone in town knows the old coot who feeds the pigeons every morning.
Despite what people say, that old coot is actually very kind-hearted.

Synset Examples

The old coot sat on the porch telling stories.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: geezer - an eccentric or odd old man (British informal)
  • Synonym: codger - an elderly man, especially an eccentric one

Etymology

'Coot' used as a mildly dismissive term for a person, likely by association with the water bird known for odd behavior.
