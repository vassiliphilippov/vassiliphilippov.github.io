limousine liberal

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɪməˌzin ˈlɪbərəl / (AmE), / ˌlɪməˌziːn ˈlɪbərəl / (BrE)
A wealthy person who supports liberal political views or causes, often regarded as being out of touch with the needs and problems of ordinary people.
wealthy liberal seen as out of touch
informal american

Examples

Critics accuse her of being a limousine liberal who has no idea what ordinary families go through.
The mayor's expensive lifestyle led many to label him a limousine liberal.
Some voters resent advice from limousine liberals who have never faced real financial hardship.

Synset Examples

Her opinions on poverty seemed shallow, typical of a limousine liberal.
Many see him as a limousine liberal who never faces real issues.
The politician was criticized for being a limousine liberal disconnected from the people.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: champagne socialist - A similar term for a wealthy person with left-wing views, often used in British English

Etymology

From 'limousine', a symbol of wealth, and 'liberal', denoting left-leaning politics; popularized in the U.S. in the 1960s.
