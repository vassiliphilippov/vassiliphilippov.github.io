genus Liriodendron

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌlɪr.i.oʊˈdɛn.drən / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌlɪr.i.əˈdɛn.drən / (BrE)
A genus of deciduous trees known as tulip trees, native to North America and Asia, with large, showy flowers resembling tulips.
genus of tulip trees

Examples

The genus Liriodendron includes both American and Asian species of tulip trees.
Many botanists study the genus Liriodendron to understand plant evolution in temperate forests.
The genus Liriodendron is prized for its beautiful flowers and valuable timber.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tulip tree - a common name for trees of the genus Liriodendron

Etymology

From Greek 'leirion' (lily) and 'dendron' (tree), referring to the tree's flower.
