iterative aspect

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪt̬.ɚ.ə.tɪv ˈæs.pɛkt / (AmE), / ˈɪt.ər.ə.tɪv ˈæs.pɛkt / (BrE)
(in grammar) the verb aspect that expresses the repetition of an action or event.
verb aspect indicating repeated actions

Examples

In some languages, a special verb form marks the iterative aspect to indicate repeated actions.
The iterative aspect can be seen when describing routines or frequent occurrences.
He used the iterative aspect to show that the event happened multiple times.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: habitual aspect - grammatical aspect expressing actions done habitually

Etymology

From Latin 'iterare' (to repeat) and English 'aspect' (from Latin 'aspectus', appearance).
