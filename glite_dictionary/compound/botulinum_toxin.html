botulinum toxin

1. NOUN

/ ˌboʊtʃəˈlɪnəm ˈtɑksɪn / (AmE), / ˌbɒtjʊˈlɪnəm ˈtɒksɪn / (BrE)
a highly potent neurotoxin produced by the bacterium Clostridium botulinum that causes muscle paralysis and is used in medicine, especially cosmetically to relax muscles
paralyzing neurotoxin from Clostridium botulinum
medical

Examples

The cosmetic industry frequently uses botulinum toxin in procedures to reduce wrinkles.
Exposure to botulinum toxin can cause botulism, a serious paralytic illness.
Doctors inject botulinum toxin into specific muscles to treat chronic migraines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Botox - trademarked form of botulinum toxin used in cosmetic procedures

Etymology

From Latin 'botulus' (sausage) and Modern Latin 'Clostridium botulinum', reflecting the toxin's association with food poisoning from sausages.
