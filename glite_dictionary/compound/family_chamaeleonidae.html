family Chamaeleonidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli kæˌmiːliəˈnaɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli kəˌmiːliəˈniːdiː / (BrE)
A taxonomic family of lizards known as chameleons, native to the Old World, sometimes considered a superfamily within Sauria.
taxonomic family of chameleons

Examples

The family Chamaeleonidae includes more than 200 species of chameleons found in Africa and Madagascar.
Recent research on the family Chamaeleonidae has revealed new insights into their evolutionary history.
Zoo exhibits often feature members of the family Chamaeleonidae due to their unique color-changing ability.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chameleon - a lizard known for changing its skin color

Etymology

From New Latin Chamaeleonidae, based on the genus Chamaeleo (chameleon) + -idae (standard family suffix in zoological taxonomy).
