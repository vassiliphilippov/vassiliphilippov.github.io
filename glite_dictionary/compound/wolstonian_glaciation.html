Wolstonian glaciation

1. NOUN

/ ˌwʌlstəniən ɡleɪsiˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌwɒlstəniən ɡleɪsɪˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
the next-to-last glaciation of the Pleistocene epoch in Britain, which is marked by specific geological deposits
British Pleistocene glaciation period

Examples

Geologists have identified sediments from the Wolstonian glaciation in several regions of Britain.
Research into the Wolstonian glaciation has provided insight into prehistoric climate change.
The landscape was shaped during the Wolstonian glaciation, leaving behind moraines and outwash plains.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Würm glaciation - the last glaciation of the Pleistocene epoch in the Alps
  • Degree Lower: Anglian glaciation - the preceding major glaciation before the Wolstonian in Britain

Etymology

Named after Wolston, a village in Warwickshire, England, where geological evidence of this glaciation was first studied.
