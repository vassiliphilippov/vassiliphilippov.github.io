established church

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈstæblɪʃt tʃɜːrtʃ / (AmE), / ɪˈstæblɪʃt tʃɜːtʃ / (BrE)
The church officially recognized and supported by a nation as its principal religious institution.
official state-recognized church

Examples

The Church of England is the established church in England, with the monarch as its Supreme Governor.
Debates over the separation of church and state often focus on the role of an established church.
In some countries, the established church receives funding directly from the government.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: state church - a church supported and recognized by the government
  • Antonym: free church - a church not affiliated with or supported by the state
