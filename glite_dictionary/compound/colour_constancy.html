colour constancy

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌlɚ ˈkɑnstənsi / (AmE), / ˈkʌlə ˈkɒnstənsi / (BrE)
The tendency for the perceived colour of an object to remain relatively constant under varying illumination conditions.
perceptual stability of object colour under different light

Examples

Human vision exhibits colour constancy, allowing apples to appear red both indoors and outdoors.
The phenomenon of colour constancy helps us recognize objects under changing lighting conditions.
Researchers study colour constancy to understand how the brain processes visual information.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: color constancy - Alternative spelling (American) for the same phenomenon

Etymology

From English 'colour' + 'constancy', the latter from Latin 'constantia' (steadfastness).
