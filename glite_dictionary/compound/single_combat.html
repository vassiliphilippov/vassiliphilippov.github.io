single combat

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪŋɡəl ˈkɑːmˌbæt / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋɡl ˈkɒmˌbæt / (BrE)
a fight between two individuals, especially as a way of settling a dispute or proving bravery, often in a military or historical context.
fight between two individuals

Examples

The champions faced each other in single combat to decide the fate of their armies.
According to legend, the two rivals met for single combat on the battlefield.
He challenged his opponent to single combat to settle the dispute once and for all.

Synset Examples

in all armies there were officers who needed to prove their bravery by single combat

Related Terms

  • Synonym: duel - a formal fight between two people with weapons, usually to settle a point of honor

Etymology

From Latin 'singulus' (single, one by one) and Late Latin 'combattre' (to fight).
