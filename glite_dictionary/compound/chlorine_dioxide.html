chlorine dioxide

1. NOUN

/ ˈklɔːriːn daɪˈɒksaɪd / (AmE), / ˈklɔːriːn daɪˈɒksaɪd / (BrE)
an explosive, yellowish-green gas (ClO2) used mainly to bleach paper, starch, soap, and flour, and for disinfecting water
explosive gas used for bleaching and water purification

Examples

Chlorine dioxide is often added to drinking water systems to kill bacteria and other pathogens.
The paper mill uses chlorine dioxide as its primary bleaching agent to whiten pulp.
Exposure to high concentrations of chlorine dioxide gas can be hazardous to workers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bleach - a chemical used to whiten or disinfect

Etymology

Formed from 'chlorine' (from Greek 'khlōros', greenish-yellow) and 'dioxide' (from Latin 'di-' meaning two and 'oxide').
