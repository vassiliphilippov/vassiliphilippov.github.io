radiator hose

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪdiˌeɪtɚ hoʊs / (AmE), / ˈreɪdiˌeɪtə həʊz / (BrE)
a flexible rubber tube that carries coolant between a vehicle's radiator and its engine block
tube connecting a car's radiator and engine

Examples

The mechanic discovered a leak in the radiator hose and replaced it to prevent engine overheating.
Always check your radiator hose for cracks before taking a long drive, especially in hot weather.
A worn-out radiator hose can cause coolant to leak and potentially damage your engine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hose - a flexible tube conveying fluid
