series circuit

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪr.iːz ˈsɝː.kɪt / (AmE), / ˈsɪə.riːz ˈsɜː.kɪt / (BrE)
an electric circuit in which components are connected one after another so that the same current flows through each part
electric circuit with components in sequence

Examples

In a series circuit, if one bulb burns out, the entire string of lights goes off.
The students built a series circuit to learn about electrical flow.
Unlike a parallel circuit, a series circuit has only one path for the current.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: parallel circuit - an electric circuit in which components are connected alongside each other so that the current divides between them
