breeding-ground

1. NOUN

/ ˈbriːdɪŋ ɡraʊnd / (AmE), / ˈbriːdɪŋ ɡraʊnd / (BrE)
A place or environment where something is likely to develop or thrive, especially something undesirable or notable.
place where something develops or thrives

Examples

The startup scene in the city has become a breeding-ground for tech innovation.
Unchecked online forums can be a breeding-ground for misinformation.
The abandoned warehouse was a breeding-ground for criminal activities.

Synset Examples

The cafe became a breeding-ground for local artists.
This park is a breeding-ground for innovative ideas.
The university acts as a breeding-ground for future leaders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hotbed - place that encourages rapid development or growth, often of something undesirable

Etymology

From 'breeding' + 'ground', originally referring to a physical place where animals breed.
