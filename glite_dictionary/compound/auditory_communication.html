auditory communication

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔ.dəˌtɔɹ.i kəˌmjunɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈɔː.dɪ.tər.i kəˌmjuː.nɪˈkeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
Communication that is transmitted and received through hearing, typically involving spoken language, sounds, or other auditory signals.
communication by hearing or sound

Examples

Birds use auditory communication to warn each other of approaching predators.
Effective auditory communication is essential in noisy environments like construction sites.
Many animals rely on auditory communication when visual contact is limited.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: verbal communication - communication using words, especially speech
  • Antonym: visual communication - communication using visual elements such as images or gestures

Etymology

auditory from Latin 'audīre' (to hear), and communication from Latin 'communicare' (to share).
