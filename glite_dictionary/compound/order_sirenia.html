order Sirenia

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdər sɪˈriːniə / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə sɪˈriːniə / (BrE)
a taxonomic order of large, aquatic, herbivorous mammals that includes manatees, dugongs, and the extinct Steller's sea cow.
order of aquatic mammals: manatees, dugongs

Examples

Scientists study the order Sirenia to understand the evolution of aquatic mammals.
Only a few species remain in the order Sirenia due to habitat loss and hunting.
Manatees and dugongs are the living representatives of the order Sirenia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: manatee - a large aquatic mammal in the order Sirenia
  • Synonym: dugong - a marine mammal in the order Sirenia

Etymology

From Latin 'sīrēnēs' (plural of 'sīrēn', “sea nymph, siren”), due to the myth of mermaids.
