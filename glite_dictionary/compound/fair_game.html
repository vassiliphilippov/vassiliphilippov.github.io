fair game

1. NOUN

/ fɛr ɡeɪm / (AmE), / feə ɡeɪm / (BrE)
a person considered to be a legitimate target for criticism, attack, or exploitation
person considered a legitimate target

Examples

After the scandal broke, the politician became fair game for the media.
New employees are often fair game for office jokes until they prove themselves.
Anyone who breaks the rules is fair game for disciplinary action.

Synset Examples

he fell prey to muggers
everyone was fair game
the target of a manhunt

Related Terms

  • Synonym: target - person or thing being attacked or criticized
  • Synonym: prey - person who is vulnerable to being attacked or harmed
  • Antonym: off-limits - not to be attacked or criticized

2. NOUN

/ fɛr ˈɡeɪm / (AmE), / feə ˈɡeɪm / (BrE)
a person or thing that is regarded as a legitimate target for criticism, attack, or pursuit, especially because of their status or position
legitimate target for criticism or attack

Examples

After becoming famous, her private life was considered fair game for tabloid journalists.
During the discussion, no topic was fair game except personal attacks.
The politician argued that family members should not be fair game for media scrutiny.

Synset Examples

Criticism of public figures is fair game for the media.
In the game, all tactics are fair game.
In this debate, everyone’s opinions are fair game.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: target - something chosen for attack or criticism
