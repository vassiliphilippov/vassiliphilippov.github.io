trade school

1. NOUN

/ treɪd skuːl / (AmE), / treɪd skuːl / (BrE)
A school, typically at the secondary or post-secondary level, where students are taught specific skilled trades, such as carpentry, plumbing, or electrical work.
school for training in skilled trades

Examples

After high school, he decided to attend a trade school to become an electrician.
Many people choose a trade school because it offers hands-on practical training.
The trade school near my house offers courses in automotive repair and welding.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vocational school - a school offering courses related to specific jobs or careers
