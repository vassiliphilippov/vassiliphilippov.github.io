cologne water

1. NOUN

/ kəˈloʊn ˌwɔtər / (AmE), / kəˈləʊn ˌwɔːtə / (BrE)
a type of light, perfumed liquid made from essential oils, alcohol, and water, typically used as a personal fragrance
light perfumed liquid for personal fragrance

Examples

He dabbed a bit of cologne water on his wrists before the interview.
The fresh scent of cologne water lingered in the air after she left the room.
Traditional cologne water is less intense than most modern perfumes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eau de cologne - a lightly scented cologne, originally from Cologne, Germany

Etymology

From French 'eau de Cologne', named after the city of Cologne (Köln) in Germany.
