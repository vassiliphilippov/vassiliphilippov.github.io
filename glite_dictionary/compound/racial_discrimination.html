racial discrimination

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪʃəl dɪˌskrɪməˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈreɪʃəl dɪˌskrɪmɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
unfair or prejudicial treatment of individuals or groups based on their race or ethnicity
unfair treatment based on race

Examples

The company was sued for practicing racial discrimination in its hiring process.
Many civil rights laws were enacted to combat racial discrimination in schools and public spaces.
He spoke out against racial discrimination during the community meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: racism - belief that races have distinctive characteristics, often resulting in discrimination
  • Antonym: equality - the state of being equal, especially in status or rights
