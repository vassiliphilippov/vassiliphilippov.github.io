family Chelydridae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ˌkɛlɪˈdrɪdi / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli ˌkɛlɪˈdrɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of turtles including the genera Chelydra and Macrochelys, known for species like the snapping turtles.
a family of turtles (Chelydridae)

Examples

The family Chelydridae includes some of the largest freshwater turtles in North America.
Members of the family Chelydridae are known for their powerful jaws and aggressive nature.
Scientists have studied the evolution of the family Chelydridae to understand turtle diversity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snapping turtle - a common large freshwater turtle of North America, a member of Chelydridae

Etymology

From New Latin Chelydridae, based on genus Chelydra.
