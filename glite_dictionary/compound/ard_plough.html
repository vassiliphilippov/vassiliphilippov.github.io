ard plough

1. NOUN

/ ɑrd plaʊ / (AmE), / ɑːd plaʊ / (BrE)
a simple, light plough without a mouldboard, used mainly in ancient and early agriculture to break up soil without turning it completely
simple type of early plough

Examples

The farmer demonstrated how the ard plough was used in ancient agriculture.
Unlike modern ploughs, the ard plough simply scratched the surface of the soil.
Historians have found remnants of the ard plough in several archaeological sites.

Synset Examples

Rather than cutting and turning the soil to produce ridged furrows, the ard breaks up a narrow strip of soil and cuts a shallow furrow (or drill), leaving intervening strips undisturbed.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: plough - a farm tool for turning over the soil

Etymology

From Latin 'aratrum' (plough); 'ard' from an ancient tool name used across Europe and Asia.
