fourth estate

1. NOUN

/ fɔrθ ɪˈsteɪt / (AmE), / fɔːθ ɪˈsteɪt / (BrE)
the press and news media, including journalists, regarded collectively as an influential sector of society
the press and news media

Examples

The fourth estate plays a crucial role in holding those in power accountable.
In a democracy, a free fourth estate is essential for transparency.
He pursued a career in the fourth estate, hoping to make a difference through journalism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: press - newspapers, journalists, and news media collectively
  • Synonym: media - the main means of mass communication (broadcasting, publishing, etc.)

Etymology

Alluding to the 'three estates' of the realm in medieval European society—clergy, nobility, and commoners—with the press considered an unofficial but influential 'fourth' estate.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɔrθ ɪˈsteɪt / (AmE), / ˈfɔːθ ɪˈsteɪt / (BrE)
the press or news media, regarded as an influential institution in society, especially as an informant and watchdog
the press or news media as a societal force

Examples

Many believe the fourth estate should hold those in power accountable for their actions.
She has worked in the fourth estate for over twenty years, covering political stories.
With recent changes in technology, the fourth estate faces new challenges in reaching the public.

Synset Examples

The role of the fourth estate is crucial in democracy.
The media is often referred to as the fourth estate.
She works in the fourth estate, reporting on local issues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: press - the news media and journalists collectively
  • Synonym: media - the main means of mass communication

Etymology

From the traditional division of society into three estates (clergy, nobility, commons), with the press being considered the 'fourth.'
