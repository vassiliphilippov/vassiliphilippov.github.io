Continental Divide of the Americas

1. NOUN

/ kənˌtɪnəntəl dəˈvaɪd əv ði əˈmɛrɪkəz / (AmE), / kənˌtɪnəntəl dɪˈvaɪd əv ðiː əˈmɛrɪkəz / (BrE)
the principal and largely mountainous hydrological divide of the Americas, especially the portion formed by the Rocky Mountains in the United States, separating waters flowing to the Pacific Ocean from those flowing to the Atlantic and Arctic Oceans
major hydrological divide of the Americas

Examples

Many hikers dream of trekking the entire length of the Continental Divide of the Americas from Mexico to Canada.
Rivers to the west of the Continental Divide of the Americas flow toward the Pacific Ocean.
The Continental Divide of the Americas offers stunning views in the Rocky Mountains.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: continental divide - a divide separating river systems that flow to opposite sides of a continent
