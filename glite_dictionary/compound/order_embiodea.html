order Embiodea

1. NOUN

/ ˌɔrdər ˌɛmbiˈoʊdiə / (AmE), / ˌɔːdə ˌɛmbɪˈəʊdiə / (BrE)
a small order of insects, commonly known as web spinners, characterized by their ability to spin silk to form tunnels or galleries.
an order of insects known as web spinners
medical

Examples

Scientists discovered a new species in the order Embiodea during their expedition in the rainforest.
In biology class, we studied how insects in the order Embiodea produce silk to build protective tunnels.
The unique silk-spinning behavior of the order Embiodea differentiates them from many other insect groups.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: web spinner - an insect of the order Embiodea

Etymology

From New Latin Embiodea, based on Greek 'embios' (lively).
