Karenic language

1. NOUN

/ kəˈrɛnɪk ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / kəˈrɛnɪk ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
any of a group of Tibeto-Burman languages spoken by the Karen people in Myanmar (Burma) and Thailand.
a Tibeto-Burman language group of Southeast Asia

Examples

The Karenic language group includes Sgaw and Pwo varieties.
Linguists have studied how the Karenic language differs from other Tibeto-Burman tongues.
Many refugees continue to speak their native Karenic language after resettling abroad.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Tibeto-Burman language - a family of languages spoken in East and Southeast Asia
  • Degree Higher: Sino-Tibetan language - major language family including Chinese and Tibeto-Burman languages

Etymology

From 'Karen' (the ethnic group) + '-ic', denoting relatedness.
