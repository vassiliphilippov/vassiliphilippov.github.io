dasyurid marsupial

1. NOUN

/ dəˈsjʊərɪd mɑrˈsuːpiəl / (AmE), / dəˈsjʊərɪd mɑːˈsuːpɪəl / (BrE)
A small, carnivorous nocturnal marsupial native to Australia and Tasmania, belonging to the family Dasyuridae.
carnivorous marsupial of Australia/Tasmania

Examples

The dasyurid marsupial hunts insects and small animals at night in the Australian outback.
One well-known dasyurid marsupial is the Tasmanian devil, famous for its aggressive behavior.
Many dasyurid marsupials play a vital role in maintaining ecological balance by controlling pest populations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marsupial - Mammal of an order whose members are born incompletely developed and are typically carried in a pouch.

Etymology

From scientific family Dasyuridae, from Greek dasys (hairy) and oura (tail).
