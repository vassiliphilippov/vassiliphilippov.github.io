spinach plant

1. NOUN

/ ˈspɪnɪtʃ plænt / (AmE), / ˈspɪnɪtʃ plɑːnt / (BrE)
A southwestern Asian plant (Spinacia oleracea) widely cultivated for its thick, edible dark green leaves used as a vegetable.
edible green leafy vegetable plant

Examples

The spinach plant thrives in cool weather and rich soil.
She grew a spinach plant in her backyard garden for fresh salad greens.
Farmers harvest spinach plants early in the morning to ensure maximum freshness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spinach - the edible leaves of the spinach plant

Etymology

From Middle English 'spinache', from Old French 'espinache', from Arabic 'isfānākh'.
