great knapweed

1. NOUN

/ ɡreɪt ˈnæp.wid / (AmE), / ɡreɪt ˈnap.wiːd / (BrE)
a tall European perennial plant with purple flower heads, scientifically known as Centaurea scabiosa
a plant with purple flower heads

Examples

The meadow was dotted with patches of great knapweed among the tall grasses.
Gardeners often plant great knapweed to attract butterflies and bees.
During summer, the striking purple blooms of great knapweed brighten the countryside.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: knapweed - any plant of the genus Centaurea, with thistle-like flowers
