scot-free

1. ADV

/ skɑt ˈfri / (AmE), / skɒt ˈfriː / (BrE)
without being punished or suffering any consequences for something wrong or illegal
without punishment or consequence

Examples

After the trial, he walked out scot-free, to everyone's shock.
Many people felt it was unfair that the company got off scot-free after the scandal.
If you break the rules, don't expect to get away scot-free.

Synset Examples

They escaped the situation scot-free, surprisingly.
She thought she could leave scot-free after lying.
He got away scot-free after the incident.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unpunished - not punished for wrongdoing

Etymology

From Middle English 'scot' (tax or payment), originally meaning 'exempt from paying a tax or penalty'.
