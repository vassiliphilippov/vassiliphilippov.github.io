logical argument

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɑdʒɪkəl ˈɑɹɡjəmənt / (AmE), / ˈlɒdʒɪkəl ˈɑːɡjʊmənt / (BrE)
a course of reasoning intended to prove or refute a statement, using systematic logic
reasoned process to prove or disprove

Examples

The philosopher presented a logical argument to support her theory.
His logical argument clearly showed why the proposal was flawed.
In mathematics, every proof must be based on a sound logical argument.

Synset Examples

I can't follow your line of reasoning

Related Terms

  • Synonym: line of reasoning - a connected series of logical steps used to arrive at a conclusion
  • Antonym: fallacy - a mistaken belief stemming from faulty reasoning
