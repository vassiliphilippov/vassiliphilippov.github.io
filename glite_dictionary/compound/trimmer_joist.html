trimmer joist

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrɪmər dʒɔɪst / (AmE), / ˈtrɪmə dʒɔɪst / (BrE)
A joist that supports the end of a header in floor or roof framing, leaving an opening for features like staircases or chimneys.
supporting joist in floor or roof framing

Examples

The carpenter installed a trimmer joist to support the new skylight opening.
A staircase often requires a trimmer joist on each side to frame the space.
They reinforced the floor by doubling the trimmer joist near the chimney.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: header - a beam at the end of an opening in a floor or roof
