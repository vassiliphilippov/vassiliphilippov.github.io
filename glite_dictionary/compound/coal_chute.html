coal chute

1. NOUN

/ koʊl tʃuːt / (AmE), / kəʊl tʃuːt / (BrE)
A sloping channel or passage used for delivering coal, typically from a truck or storage area into a building or cellar.
sloping channel for delivering coal

Examples

The workers poured the coal down the coal chute into the basement.
After the delivery, black dust covered the walls near the coal chute.
The house still has an old coal chute next to the back door.
