malt whisky

1. NOUN

/ mɔlt ˈwɪski / (AmE), / mɔːlt ˈwɪski / (BrE)
A whisky made in Scotland, especially from malted barley in a pot still, characterized by its distinctive flavor.
whisky made from malted barley in Scotland

Examples

He savored a glass of malt whisky by the fireplace after dinner.
The distillery is famous for producing award-winning malt whisky.
Many collectors seek rare bottles of Scottish malt whisky for their unique flavors.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: grain whisky - whisky made from grains other than malted barley
  • Degree Lower: blended whisky - a mixture of malt and grain whiskies
  • Degree Higher: single malt - malt whisky from a single distillery

Etymology

From 'malt', referring to malted barley, and 'whisky', from Gaelic 'uisge beatha' (water of life).
