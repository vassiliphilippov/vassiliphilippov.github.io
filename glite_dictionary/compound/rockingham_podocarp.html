Rockingham podocarp

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɑːkɪŋˌhæm ˈpoʊdəˌkɑːrp / (AmE), / ˌrɒkɪŋˌhæm ˈpɒdəˌkɑːp / (BrE)
A large Australian tree known for its straight-grained yellow wood that turns brown when exposed to air.
Australian tree with yellow wood

Examples

The Rockingham podocarp is valued for its durable timber in the construction industry.
Many old forests in Western Australia feature towering Rockingham podocarp trees.
The wood of the Rockingham podocarp changes color from yellow to brown when cut and exposed.
