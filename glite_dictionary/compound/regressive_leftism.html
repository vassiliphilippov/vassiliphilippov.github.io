regressive leftism

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈɡrɛsɪv ˈlɛftɪzəm / (AmE), / rɪˈɡrɛsɪv ˈlɛftɪzəm / (BrE)
A political ideology or set of beliefs, especially on the left, viewed as outdated, reactionary, or counterproductive to social progress, often by opposing or undermining progressive values.
belief system seen as counterproductive on the political left

Examples

Many commentators argue that regressive leftism hinders open debate on university campuses.
He accused the organization's policies of supporting regressive leftism rather than progress.
Some believe that regressive leftism prioritizes ideological purity over practical solutions.

Synset Examples

He criticized regressive leftism for its narrow viewpoints.
Regressive leftism often leads to internal conflicts.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: progressivism - a political philosophy supporting social reform and progress

Etymology

Formed from 'regressive' (moving backward) and 'leftism' (left-wing political ideology).
