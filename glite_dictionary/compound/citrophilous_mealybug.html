citrophilous mealybug

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɪtroʊˈfɪləs ˈmiːlibʌɡ / (AmE), / ˌsɪtrəʊˈfɪləs ˈmiːlibʌɡ / (BrE)
a type of mealybug that is particularly destructive to citrus plants, feeding on their juices and causing significant agricultural damage
pest that attacks citrus plants

Examples

Farmers often struggle to control the citrophilous mealybug in their orange and lemon groves.
An infestation of the citrophilous mealybug can severely reduce the yield of citrus crops.
Scientists are developing biological methods to combat the citrophilous mealybug without using harsh chemicals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mealybug - a small sap-sucking insect often found on plants

Etymology

From 'citrophilous' (preferring citrus) + 'mealybug' (a type of sap-sucking insect).
