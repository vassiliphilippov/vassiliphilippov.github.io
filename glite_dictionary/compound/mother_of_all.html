mother of all

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌðɚ əv ɔl / (AmE), / ˈmʌðə əv ɔːl / (BrE)
used to emphasize that something is the largest, most extreme, or most impressive of its kind
the largest or most significant of its kind

Examples

That was the mother of all storms; I've never seen anything like it.
The team staged the mother of all comebacks to win the championship.
Get ready for the mother of all parties this weekend!

Synset Examples

He claimed it was the mother of all sales.
This is the mother of all challenges we've faced.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ultimate - being or happening at the end of a process; final or most extreme

Etymology

Based on the traditional role of a mother as the origin or most important member; popularized in English by the phrase 'the mother of all battles' (translation of Arabic origin).
