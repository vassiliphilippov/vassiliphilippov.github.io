order Sarraceniales

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdər səˌræsəˈniːəˌliːz / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə səˌræsɪˈniːəliːz / (BrE)
a taxonomic order of insectivorous plants, characterized by specialized structures for trapping insects; includes families such as Sarraceniaceae, Nepenthaceae, and Droseraceae
taxonomic order of insect-trapping plants

Examples

Many carnivorous plants belonging to the order Sarraceniales have evolved pitcher-shaped leaves for trapping insects.
The order Sarraceniales includes some of the most fascinating insectivorous plants in the world.
Botanists study the order Sarraceniales to understand the adaptations of insect-trapping species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sarraceniaceae - family of pitcher plants within the order Sarraceniales
  • Synonym: Nepenthaceae - family of tropical pitcher plants within the order Sarraceniales
  • Synonym: Droseraceae - family of sundews within the order Sarraceniales

Etymology

From modern Latin Sarracenia (the pitcher plant genus) + -ales (taxonomic order suffix).
