Lowland Scot

1. NOUN

/ ˈloʊlænd ˈskɑt / (AmE), / ˈləʊlənd ˈskɒt / (BrE)
a native or inhabitant of the Lowlands region of Scotland
person from the Lowlands of Scotland

Examples

Many Lowland Scots played a key role in the development of the modern Scottish economy.
She was proud of her heritage as a Lowland Scot from the bustling city of Edinburgh.
The traditions of the Lowland Scot differ in several ways from those of the Highlanders.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Highlander - a native or inhabitant of the Highlands of Scotland
