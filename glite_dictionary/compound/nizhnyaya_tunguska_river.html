Nizhnyaya Tunguska River

1. PROPN

/ ˌniːʒ.niˈjɑː tunˈɡuːskə ˈrɪvər / (AmE), / ˌniːʒ.nɪˈjɑː tʊnˈɡʊskə ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
a river in Siberia that rises north of Lake Baikal and flows northwest to join the Yenisei River
a river in Siberia, Russia

Examples

The Nizhnyaya Tunguska River is known for its rugged terrain and remote location in Siberia.
Scientists conducted research along the banks of the Nizhnyaya Tunguska River to study its unique ecosystem.
Navigating the Nizhnyaya Tunguska River can be challenging due to its many rapids and strong currents.

Etymology

From Russian Нижняя Тунгуска (Nizhnyaya Tunguska), meaning 'Lower Tunguska.'
