genus Cattleya

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs kəˈtleɪə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs kætˈliːə / (BrE)
a large and highly valued genus of beautiful tropical American orchids, known for their showy flowers and many varieties, often grown as ornamental plants
genus of showy tropical American orchids

Examples

Many collectors prize the delicate and vibrant flowers of the genus Cattleya.
The genus Cattleya is often featured at international orchid exhibitions for its stunning variety.
Some species in the genus Cattleya are popular choices for corsages and floral arrangements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orchid - a plant of the family Orchidaceae, known for its beautiful and diverse flowers

Etymology

Genus name from Sir William Cattley, British horticulturist (1788–1835), who first cultivated specimens.
