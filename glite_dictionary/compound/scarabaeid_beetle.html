scarabaeid beetle

1. NOUN

/ skəˈræbiːˌɪd ˈbiːtəl / (AmE), / skəˈræbiːɪd ˈbiːtl̩ / (BrE)
A stout-bodied beetle of the family Scarabaeidae, often with a head bearing horny spikes.
stout-bodied beetle with spiked head

Examples

The scarabaeid beetle is famous for its distinctive horn-like projections on its head.
Farmers often find the scarabaeid beetle in fields, where some species can be agricultural pests.
Ancient Egyptians revered the scarabaeid beetle as a symbol of renewal and transformation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scarab - a beetle of the family Scarabaeidae considered sacred in ancient Egypt

Etymology

From Latin 'Scarabaeus', meaning 'beetle'.
