St. Basil the Great

1. PROPN

/ seɪnt ˈbeɪzəl ðə ɡreɪt / (AmE), / seɪnt ˈbæzəl ðə ɡreɪt / (BrE)
A 4th-century bishop of Caesarea, influential theologian and defender of the Church, recognized as a saint and Doctor of the Church.
4th-century bishop and saint

Examples

Many Eastern Orthodox Christians commemorate St. Basil the Great on January 1 each year.
The theological writings of St. Basil the Great have shaped Christian doctrine for centuries.
St. Basil the Great was known for his charity and dedication to helping the poor.
