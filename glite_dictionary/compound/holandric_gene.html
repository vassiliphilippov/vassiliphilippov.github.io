holandric gene

1. NOUN

/ hoʊˈlændrɪk dʒin / (AmE), / həʊˈlændrɪk dʒiːn / (BrE)
a gene located on the Y chromosome and inherited only through the male line
gene found on the Y chromosome
medical

Examples

A holandric gene is passed exclusively from father to son because it is found only on the Y chromosome.
Some traits, such as certain forms of hairy ears, are influenced by holandric genes.
Researchers study holandric genes to better understand patterns of male inheritance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sex-linked gene - a gene located on a sex chromosome

Etymology

From Greek 'holos' (whole) and 'aner, andr-' (man), referring to male-linked inheritance.
