magnetic pole

1. NOUN

/ mæɡˈnɛtɪk poʊl / (AmE), / mæɡˈnɛtɪk pəʊl / (BrE)
either of the two points on the Earth's surface where the magnetic field is vertical, usually referred to as the North Magnetic Pole and the South Magnetic Pole.
either point where Earth's magnetic field is vertical

Examples

Scientists located the exact position of the magnetic pole during their Arctic expedition.
The magnetic pole is constantly shifting due to changes in the Earth's core.
Compasses point toward the magnetic pole, not the geographic North Pole.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: geographic pole - either of the two points where the Earth's axis of rotation meets the surface

2. NOUN

/ məɡˈnɛtɪk poʊl / (AmE), / maɡˈnɛtɪk pəʊl / (BrE)
either of the two ends of a magnet at which the magnetic force is strongest.
end of a magnet where force is strongest

Examples

The compass always points towards the north magnetic pole.
Each bar magnet has a north and a south magnetic pole.
Iron filings align themselves along the lines between the two magnetic poles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: magnet - an object that produces a magnetic field
  • Synonym: magnetic field - the area around a magnet where magnetic force is felt
