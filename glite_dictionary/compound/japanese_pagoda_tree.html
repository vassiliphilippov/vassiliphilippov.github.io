Japanese pagoda tree

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˈpæn i pəˈɡoʊ də tri / (AmE), / dʒəˈpæn i pəˈɡəʊ də triː / (BrE)
A deciduous tree native to China and Japan, with rounded crown, compound dark green leaves, and clusters of fragrant creamy-white flowers.
deciduous tree native to East Asia

Examples

The Japanese pagoda tree is often planted in parks for its attractive foliage and blossoms.
In late summer, the Japanese pagoda tree produces showy clusters of creamy-white flowers.
They admired the shade provided by the old Japanese pagoda tree in the garden.
