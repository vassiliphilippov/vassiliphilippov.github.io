circulatory failure

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɜr.kjəˈleɪ.tɔːr.i ˈfeɪ.ljɚ / (AmE), / ˌsɜː.kjəˈleɪ.tər.i ˈfeɪ.ljə / (BrE)
A serious medical condition in which the cardiovascular system fails to supply enough blood to the body's tissues.
inadequate blood supply by the cardiovascular system
medical

Examples

The patient was admitted to the ICU with acute circulatory failure after experiencing severe chest pain.
Prompt treatment is vital in cases of circulatory failure to prevent organ damage.
Doctors closely monitored her blood pressure to assess the risk of circulatory failure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heart failure - a condition in which the heart cannot pump enough blood
