still hunt

1. NOUN

/ stɪl hʌnt / (AmE), / stɪl hʌnt / (BrE)
A method of hunting game in which the hunter moves very quietly or waits in ambush to avoid detection.
a hunting method involving stealth or ambush

Examples

During the still hunt, the hunter crept quietly through the forest, careful not to make a sound.
A still hunt requires patience, as it often involves waiting for hours in one spot.
He preferred a still hunt over other methods, believing it was more challenging and rewarding.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ambush - an act or instance of attacking unexpectedly from a concealed position
  • Antonym: drive hunt - a hunting method where animals are driven towards hunters
