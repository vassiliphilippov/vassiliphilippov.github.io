dog fennel

1. NOUN

/ dɔɡ ˈfɛnəl / (AmE), / dɒɡ ˈfɛnəl / (BrE)
A weedy plant native to the southeastern United States, with finely divided leaves and long clusters of small greenish flowers.
a weedy southeastern US plant with divided leaves

Examples

The field was overgrown with dog fennel and other wild plants.
We pulled up the dog fennel that had taken over the garden paths.
Dog fennel gives off a distinctive odor when its leaves are crushed.

2. NOUN

/ dɔɡ ˈfɛnəl / (AmE), / dɒɡ ˈfɛnəl / (BrE)
a widespread strong-smelling weed with finely divided leaves and white-rayed flower heads with yellow centers, commonly found in North America
a rank-smelling weed with white-rayed flowers

Examples

The fields were overrun with dog fennel after the heavy rains.
I brushed against some dog fennel and the smell lingered on my clothes.
Gardeners often remove dog fennel because it can spread quickly and crowd out other plants.
