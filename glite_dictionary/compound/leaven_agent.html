leaven agent

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛvən ˈeɪdʒənt / (AmE), / ˈlɛvən ˈeɪdʒənt / (BrE)
A substance that is added to dough or batter to cause it to rise and become lighter, typically by producing gas.
substance that makes dough or batter rise

Examples

Baking powder is a common leaven agent used in cakes and biscuits.
Adding yeast as a leaven agent gives bread its airy texture.
Some recipes require both baking soda and cream of tartar as the leaven agent.

Synset Examples

I know I can use chemical leaveners, but I think that using yeast as a leavening agent is more healthy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leavening agent - substance added to dough or batter to make it rise

Etymology

From 'leaven', meaning a substance causing fermentation, which derives from Old French 'levain', from Latin 'levamen' (relief).
