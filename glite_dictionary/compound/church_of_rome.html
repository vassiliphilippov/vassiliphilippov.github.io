Church of Rome

1. NOUN

/ tʃɝtʃ əv roʊm / (AmE), / tʃɜːtʃ əv rəʊm / (BrE)
the Christian Church based in the Vatican and led by the Pope and a hierarchy of bishops; another name for the Roman Catholic Church
Roman Catholic Church; Christian institution led by the Pope

Examples

The Church of Rome holds the Pope as its spiritual leader.
Many medieval European monarchs sought guidance from the Church of Rome.
The doctrines of the Church of Rome have influenced Western civilization for centuries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Roman Catholic Church - the main branch of Christianity led by the Pope in Rome
