cell division

1. NOUN

/ sɛl dɪˈvɪʒən / (AmE), / sɛl dɪˈvɪʒən / (BrE)
the process by which a cell divides into two new cells, important for growth and reproduction in living organisms
process of a cell dividing
medical

Examples

Cell division is essential for the growth and repair of multicellular organisms.
Scientists study cell division to better understand cancer and other diseases.
During cell division, the genetic material is accurately copied into each daughter cell.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mitosis - process where a cell divides to produce two identical daughter cells
  • Synonym: meiosis - type of cell division that reduces the chromosome number by half
