Mount Whitney

1. PROPN

/ maʊnt ˈwɪtni / (AmE), / maʊnt ˈwɪtni / (BrE)
the highest peak in the Sierra Nevada mountain range in California, United States, with an elevation of 14,494 feet (4,418 meters)
highest peak in Sierra Nevada, California

Examples

Every summer, hikers from around the world attempt to reach the summit of Mount Whitney.
Climbing Mount Whitney requires a special permit due to the area's popularity and environmental regulations.
From the top of Mount Whitney, you can see a breathtaking panorama of the Sierra Nevada mountains.

Etymology

Named after Josiah Whitney, an American geologist (1819–1896).
