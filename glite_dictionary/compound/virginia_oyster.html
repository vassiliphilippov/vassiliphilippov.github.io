Virginia oyster

1. NOUN

/ vərˈdʒɪniə ˈɔɪstər / (AmE), / vəˈdʒɪnɪə ˈɔɪstə / (BrE)
a common edible oyster found along the Atlantic coast of North America, often valued for its culinary use
edible oyster of Atlantic North America

Examples

Fresh Virginia oyster is a favorite on restaurant menus along the East Coast.
The chef prepared a dish featuring locally harvested Virginia oyster with lemon and herbs.
Many seafood lovers praise the sweet flavor of the Virginia oyster compared to other varieties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oyster - a marine mollusk with a rough, irregular shell; many are edible
