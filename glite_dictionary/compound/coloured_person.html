coloured person

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌlɚd ˌpɝsən / (AmE), / ˈkʌləd ˌpɜːsən / (BrE)
(Offensive, dated) A person with Black African ancestry; formerly used in the United States to refer to Black people, now considered offensive.
offensive US term for Black person
offensive

Examples

The term coloured person is now recognized as offensive and outdated.
Many historical documents in the U.S. used the phrase coloured person to describe Black Americans.
She was shocked to hear someone refer to her as a coloured person in the twenty-first century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Black person - A person having African ancestry; a person of Black ethnicity
  • Degree Lower: coloured - (South African usage) A person of mixed race; term used in South Africa
