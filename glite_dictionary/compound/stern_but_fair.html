stern but fair

1. ADJ

/ stɝn bət fɛr / (AmE), / stɜːn bət feə / (BrE)
strict in manner or discipline while treating others with justice and impartiality
strict yet just in behavior

Examples

Many students respected the principal because he was stern but fair in his decisions.
Her stern but fair approach made her an effective and well-liked supervisor.
Parents who are stern but fair often raise children with strong values.

Synset Examples

The coach was stern but fair during practice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strict but fair - strict but treats people justly
  • Antonym: lenient - not strict; permissive
