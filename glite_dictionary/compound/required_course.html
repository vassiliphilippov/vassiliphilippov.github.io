required course

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈkwaɪərd kɔrs / (AmE), / rɪˈkwaɪəd kɔːs / (BrE)
A course that all students must take as part of a program of study.
a mandatory course for students

Examples

Mathematics is a required course for all first-year engineering students.
Every student must pass each required course to graduate from the program.
She was disappointed to find that history was a required course in her major.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: elective course - a course that students can choose to take but is not mandatory
