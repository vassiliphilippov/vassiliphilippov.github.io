man-of-war bird

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæn əv ˈwɔr bɝd / (AmE), / ˌmæn əv ˈwɔː bɜːd / (BrE)
a large tropical seabird with a long bill, wide wingspan, and deeply forked tail, also known as a frigatebird
tropical seabird with forked tail

Examples

A man-of-war bird soared gracefully above the ocean, scanning for food.
The man-of-war bird is known for its impressive wingspan and aerial agility.
Fishermen often spot the man-of-war bird flying high above tropical waters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frigatebird - another name for the man-of-war bird, a tropical seabird

Etymology

Named for its aggressive behavior, likened to a man-of-war (warship), and also called 'frigatebird.'
