morality play

1. NOUN

/ məˈræl.ə.ti pleɪ / (AmE), / məˈræl.ə.ti pleɪ / (BrE)
a type of allegorical drama, especially popular in the 15th and 16th centuries, in which characters represent abstract qualities like virtues and vices to teach moral lessons
allegorical drama with personified virtues and vices

Examples

Every student in the medieval literature class read a morality play to analyze themes of good and evil.
The morality play featured characters such as Justice, Greed, and Charity, all symbolizing human traits.
Today's performance of a morality play brought the 16th-century tradition to a modern audience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mystery play - a medieval drama based on biblical stories
  • Synonym: miracle play - a medieval play dramatizing the lives of saints

Etymology

From 'morality' and 'play'; first attested in late Middle English, describing plays teaching moral lessons.
