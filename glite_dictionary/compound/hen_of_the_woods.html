hen of the woods

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɛn əv ðə ˈwʊdz / (AmE), / ˌhɛn əv ðə ˈwʊdz / (BrE)
a large, greyish-brown edible fungus (Grifola frondosa) that forms a mass of overlapping caps, resembling a hen, commonly found at the base of trees
a type of edible woodland fungus

Examples

She sautéed the hen of the woods mushrooms with garlic and herbs for dinner.
Foragers often look for hen of the woods growing near old oak trees in the autumn.
The unique shape of the hen of the woods makes it easy to distinguish from other wild mushrooms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maitake - another name for hen of the woods (Grifola frondosa)
