President Roosevelt

1. PROPN

/ ˈprɛzɪdənt ˈroʊzəˌvɛlt / (AmE), / ˈprɛzɪdənt ˈrəʊzəˌvɛlt / (BrE)
Franklin D. Roosevelt, 32nd President of the United States, known for the New Deal and leading the country during World War II.
32nd U.S. President (F.D. Roosevelt)

Examples

President Roosevelt addressed the nation in one of his famous fireside chats.
During the Great Depression, President Roosevelt launched programs to relieve economic hardship.
Many historians credit President Roosevelt with leading the United States to victory in World War II.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: FDR - Initials commonly used for Franklin D. Roosevelt
  • Synonym: Franklin D. Roosevelt - Full name of the 32nd U.S. President

2. PROPN

/ ˌprɛzɪdənt ˈroʊzəˌvɛlt / (AmE), / ˌprɛzɪdənt ˈrəʊzəˌvɛlt / (BrE)
the 26th President of the United States (1901–1909), known for leading the country during the Spanish-American War and for overseeing the construction of the Panama Canal
the 26th President of the United States (Theodore Roosevelt)

Examples

President Roosevelt played a crucial role in negotiating the end of the Russo-Japanese War.
It was under President Roosevelt that the Panama Canal was completed in 1914.
Many Americans remember President Roosevelt for his progressive reforms and his energetic leadership.
