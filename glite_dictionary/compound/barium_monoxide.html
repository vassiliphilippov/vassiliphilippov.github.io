barium monoxide

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛəriəm ˌmɑːnəksaɪd / (AmE), / ˈbɛəriəm ˌmɒnəksaɪd / (BrE)
a whitish toxic powder that is an oxide of barium, used in ceramics and as a chemical reagent
a toxic oxide of barium
medical

Examples

Exposure to barium monoxide can be hazardous without proper safety equipment.
The laboratory ordered more barium monoxide for the new series of experiments.
When heated, barium monoxide reacts with oxygen to form barium peroxide.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barium oxide - another name for barium monoxide, a barium compound with oxygen

Etymology

From 'barium' (the element) and 'monoxide' (one oxygen atom).
