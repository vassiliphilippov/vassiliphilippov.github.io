subscription warrant

1. NOUN

/ səbˈskrɪp.ʃən ˈwɔːr.ənt / (AmE), / səbˈskrɪp.ʃən ˈwɒr.ənt / (BrE)
a financial document giving the holder the right to buy shares at a specified price before a fixed expiration date
right to buy shares before expiry

Examples

The company offered a subscription warrant to attract new investors during the public offering.
A subscription warrant allows the holder to purchase shares at a set price before the expiration date.
He exercised his subscription warrant just days before it was due to expire.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: warrant - a certificate entitling the holder to buy securities
