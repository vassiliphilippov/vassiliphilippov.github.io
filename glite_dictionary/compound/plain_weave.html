plain weave

1. NOUN

/ pleɪn wiv / (AmE), / pleɪn wiːv / (BrE)
A fundamental type of fabric weave in which the weft threads pass alternately over and under the warp threads, creating a simple crisscross pattern.
basic fabric weaving method

Examples

The simple structure of plain weave makes it durable and suitable for many kinds of fabric.
Cotton shirts are often made using plain weave because of its strength and breathability.
The textile exhibit included samples of both plain weave and more complex weaving techniques.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: twill - a type of textile weave with a diagonal pattern
  • Antonym: satin weave - a fabric weave with a smooth, glossy surface
