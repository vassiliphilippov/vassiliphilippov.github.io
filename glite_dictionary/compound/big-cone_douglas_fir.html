big-cone Douglas fir

1. NOUN

/ bɪɡ koʊn ˈdʌɡləs fɜr / (AmE), / bɪɡ kəʊn ˈdʌɡləs fɜː / (BrE)
A species of Douglas fir native to California, distinguished by its large cones measuring 4–8 inches in length.
Douglas fir species with large cones

Examples

The big-cone Douglas fir is easily recognized by the size of its cones.
Several big-cone Douglas firs were planted to help with reforestation.
Scientists study the big-cone Douglas fir to understand how forests adapt to wildfires.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Douglas fir - North American evergreen tree with needlelike leaves and upright cones
