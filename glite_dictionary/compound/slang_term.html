slang term

1. NOUN

/ slæŋ tɜrm / (AmE), / slæŋ tɜːm / (BrE)
a word or phrase that is very informal and used mainly in speech or within a particular group, rather than in standard language
very informal word or phrase

Examples

The dictionary includes a section on common slang terms used by teenagers.
He explained the meaning of several Australian slang terms to the class.
That slang term is only understood by people from that city.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slang - very informal language used by particular groups

2. NOUN

/ ˈslæŋ tɜrm / (AmE), / ˈslæŋ tɜːm / (BrE)
an informal word or expression not generally accepted in formal speech or writing, often specific to particular groups or contexts
informal word or expression
informal

Examples

The word 'cool' is a popular slang term for something impressive or stylish.
Teenagers often invent new slang terms that adults find hard to understand.
He used a slang term in the meeting, which surprised his colleagues.

Synset Examples

their speech was full of slang expressions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slang - informal language, especially specific to a group

Etymology

From 'slang', possibly of Scandinavian origin, plus 'term' from Latin 'terminus' (end, limit, word).
