pomme de prairie

1. NOUN

/ pɑm də ˈprɛri / (AmE), / pɒm də ˈprɛːri / (BrE)
A perennial plant native to central North America, known for its densely hairy appearance and edible tuberous roots.
North American plant with edible tuberous roots

Examples

Early Native Americans harvested the pomme de prairie for its nutritious roots.
The pomme de prairie blooms in late spring and attracts many pollinators.
Foraging guides sometimes list the pomme de prairie as an important traditional food.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prairie turnip - another name for the same plant, Psoralea esculenta

Etymology

From French, meaning 'apple of the prairie'.
