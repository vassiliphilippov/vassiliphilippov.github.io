manilla paper

1. NOUN

/ məˈnɪlə ˈpeɪpər / (AmE), / məˈnɪlə ˈpeɪpə / (BrE)
A strong, smooth-finished light brown paper or thin cardboard made from Manila hemp, used for writing, drawing, or packaging.
strong light brown paper or thin cardboard

Examples

She used manilla paper to create an art project for school.
Please submit your assignment on manilla paper for better durability.
The documents were wrapped in thick manilla paper for mailing.

Etymology

From 'Manila,' reflecting its original manufacture from Manila hemp, named after Manila, the capital of the Philippines.
