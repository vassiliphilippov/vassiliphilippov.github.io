canned hunting

1. NOUN

/ kænd ˈhʌntɪŋ / (AmE), / kænd ˈhʌntɪŋ / (BrE)
the practice of hunting animals in a confined area from which they cannot escape, often regarded as unethical
hunting animals in a confined space

Examples

Many conservation groups have spoken out against canned hunting due to animal welfare concerns.
The law in that state now prohibits canned hunting operations entirely.
Some trophy hunters travel abroad specifically to participate in canned hunting experiences.

Synset Examples

Canned hunting gives hunters an unfair advantage.
Canned hunting is often criticized for being unethical.
He refused to participate in canned hunting trips.

Etymology

From 'canned' (contained or enclosed) and 'hunting'.
