fairy tale

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɛri teɪl / (AmE), / ˈfeəri teɪl / (BrE)
A traditional story for children involving magical beings such as fairies, elves, or witches, often with a moral lesson.
magical children's story

Examples

My grandmother used to read a fairy tale to us every night before bed.
The story of Cinderella is perhaps the most famous fairy tale in the world.
He dismissed her explanation as a childish fairy tale that couldn't possibly be true.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: folk tale - traditional narrative passed down in folklore
  • Synonym: myth - traditional story explaining natural or social phenomena

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɛri teɪl / (AmE), / ˈfeə.ri teɪl / (BrE)
A story that is highly implausible or untrue, often used as an excuse or to deceive.
an implausible or untrue story

Examples

When asked about the missing funds, he spun a fairy tale that nobody believed.
Her explanation for being late was just another fairy tale to avoid getting into trouble.
The politician's promises turned out to be nothing but a fairy tale.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fabrication - a story or statement that is invented and not true
  • Antonym: truth - what is true or in accordance with fact or reality

Etymology

Originally from tales about fairies in folklore, later extended to describe any untrue or fantastic story.

3. NOUN

/ ˈfɛri teɪl / (AmE), / ˈfeəri teɪl / (BrE)
A traditional or imaginative story, often for children, featuring magical creatures and events.
magical or imaginary story, often for children

Examples

As a child, she was fascinated by classic fairy tales like Cinderella and Sleeping Beauty.
The old castle looked as if it had come straight out of a fairy tale.
Some people think true love is just a fairy tale, but others believe it exists.

Synset Examples

She always believed in fairy tales and magic.
My daughter loves to read fairy tales.
Walking through the silver frost felt like entering a fairy tale.
The fairytale of Frogland captivated the children.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: folk tale - traditional story passed down orally, not necessarily magical
  • Synonym: myth - traditional story explaining natural or social phenomena, often involving gods
  • Synonym: legend - traditional story sometimes regarded as historical but unauthenticated

Etymology

From 'fairy' (a mythical being) + 'tale' (story).
