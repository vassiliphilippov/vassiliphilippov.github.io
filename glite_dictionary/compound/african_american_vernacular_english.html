African American Vernacular English

1. NOUN

/ ˌæfrɪkən əˈmɛrɪkən vərˈnækjələr ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ / (AmE), / ˌæfrɪkən əˈmɛrɪkən vəːˈnakjʊlə ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ / (BrE)
a form of American English with distinct grammatical, vocabulary, and pronunciation features, commonly spoken by many African Americans in the United States
a dialect of American English used by many African Americans

Examples

She specializes in the study of African American Vernacular English and its cultural significance.
Many popular songs feature elements of African American Vernacular English in their lyrics.
Teachers are encouraged to understand the structure of African American Vernacular English to better support diverse students.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Black English - another term for African American Vernacular English

Etymology

Coined in the late 20th century from 'vernacular' (from Latin vernaculus, native) and 'African American'.
