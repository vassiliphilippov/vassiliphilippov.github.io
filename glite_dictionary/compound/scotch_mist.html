Scotch mist

1. NOUN

/ skɑtʃ mɪst / (AmE), / skɒtʃ mɪst / (BrE)
A light, persistent rain or mist, especially as commonly occurs in Scotland.
light, persistent rain or mist (Scotland)
british

Examples

The hills were shrouded in a damp Scotch mist all morning.
A fine Scotch mist clung to the city, making the streets glisten.
They set out for their hike, undeterred by the steady Scotch mist.

Synset Examples

The morning air was thick with Scotch mist.
We could barely see through the Scotch mist.
They enjoyed a walk through the Scotch mist by the loch.

Etymology

From 'Scotch', referring to Scotland, and 'mist'.
