TV dinner

1. NOUN

/ ˈtiː ˈviː ˈdɪnər / (AmE), / ˈtiː ˈviː ˈdɪnə / (BrE)
A prepackaged meal that is frozen and designed to be quickly heated and eaten, often while watching television.
prepackaged frozen meal

Examples

After a long day at work, she just popped a TV dinner into the microwave.
Many college students rely on TV dinners for quick and easy meals.
He remembered eating his first TV dinner while watching cartoons as a child.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frozen dinner - a meal prepared and frozen for convenience
  • Antonym: home-cooked meal - a meal cooked at home from scratch

Etymology

Short for 'television dinner', from the 1950s trend of eating conveniently packaged meals while watching TV.
