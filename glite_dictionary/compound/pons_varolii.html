pons Varolii

1. NOUN

/ pɑnz vəˈroʊli.aɪ / (AmE), / pɒnz vəˈrəʊlɪ.aɪ / (BrE)
a broad band of nerve fibers in the brainstem that links the medulla oblongata and the cerebellum with the midbrain
part of the brainstem linking medulla, cerebellum, and midbrain
medical

Examples

The pons Varolii plays a crucial role in relaying signals within the brainstem.
Damage to the pons Varolii can affect breathing and motor control.
During the dissection, the surgeon carefully examined the pons Varolii.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pons - the part of the brainstem that links the medulla oblongata and the thalamus

Etymology

From Latin, named after Costanzo Varolio, Italian anatomist.
