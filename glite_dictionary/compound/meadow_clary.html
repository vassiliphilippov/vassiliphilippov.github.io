meadow clary

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛdoʊ ˈklæri / (AmE), / ˈmedəʊ ˈklɑːri / (BrE)
a tall perennial herb (Salvia pratensis) of Eurasia, with violet-blue flowers, commonly found in open grasslands
a perennial Eurasian herb with blue flowers

Examples

The meadow clary adds a vibrant splash of color to the summer grasslands.
Gardeners often plant meadow clary for its attractive blue blossoms and ability to attract bees.
During our walk, we spotted several meadow clary among the wildflowers in the field.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sage - an aromatic herb of the genus Salvia
