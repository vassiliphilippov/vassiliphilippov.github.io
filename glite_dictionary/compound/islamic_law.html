Islamic law

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈslæmɪk lɔ / (AmE), / ɪˈslɑːmɪk lɔː / (BrE)
The system of law based on the Qur'an and the teachings and example of the Prophet Muhammad, forming the legal and moral code of Islam.
the legal system based on Islam

Examples

Many countries in the Middle East base parts of their legal system on Islamic law.
Debates over human rights often involve the interpretation of Islamic law in modern societies.
They studied the origins and development of Islamic law at university.

Synset Examples

sharia is only applicable to Muslims
under Islamic law there is no separation of church and state

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sharia - the religious law of Islam derived from the Qur'an and Hadith
