Ceylon cinnamon tree

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪˌlɑn ˈsɪnəmən triː / (AmE), / ˈsiːlɒn ˈsɪnəmən triː / (BrE)
a tropical Asian tree (Cinnamomum verum) with aromatic yellowish-brown bark, used as the source of true cinnamon
tree source of true cinnamon

Examples

The Ceylon cinnamon tree is prized for its delicate and sweetly fragrant bark.
Unlike the cassia variety, the bark of the Ceylon cinnamon tree is thinner and more flavorful.
Farmers in Sri Lanka often cultivate the Ceylon cinnamon tree for the international spice market.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cinnamon tree - any tree that produces cinnamon bark, not necessarily Ceylon species

Etymology

Ceylon, former name for Sri Lanka, the region where the tree is native.
