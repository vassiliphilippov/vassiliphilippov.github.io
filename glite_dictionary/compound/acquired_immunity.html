acquired immunity

1. NOUN

/ əˈkwaɪɚd ɪˈmjunəti / (AmE), / əˈkwaɪəd ɪˈmjuːnəti / (BrE)
immunity to a specific disease that develops after exposure to the disease, vaccination, or transfer of antibodies, rather than being present from birth.
immunity developed after exposure or vaccination
medical

Examples

After receiving a vaccine, your body develops acquired immunity to protect against future infections.
Pregnant women can pass acquired immunity to their babies through the placenta.
Repeated exposure to certain viruses can lead to acquired immunity over time.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: innate immunity - immunity present from birth, not acquired during life
  • Synonym: passive immunity - immunity acquired by receiving antibodies from another source

Etymology

From Latin 'acquirere' (to gain, obtain) + 'immunity'.
