snake eyes

1. NOUN

/ ˈsneɪk ˌaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsneɪk ˌaɪz / (BrE)
A throw of two dice in which each shows one spot; considered the lowest possible roll.
throw of two dice, both showing one spot

Examples

He groaned when he rolled snake eyes and lost the game.
Rolling snake eyes is considered very unlucky in most dice games.
She couldn't believe her luck when snake eyes appeared on her first throw.

2. NOUN

/ sneɪk aɪz / (AmE), / sneɪk aɪz / (BrE)
A roll of two ones when throwing a pair of dice, considered unlucky in many games.
two ones on dice

Examples

On his first throw, he rolled snake eyes and lost his bet.
The crowd groaned as the dice landed on snake eyes once again.
Rolling snake eyes is often seen as a sign of bad luck in casinos.

Synset Examples

It was unfortunate to come up with snake eyes in the game.
He rolled the dice and came up with snake eyes.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: double sixes - A roll of two sixes on a pair of dice
  • Degree Higher: boxcars - A roll of two sixes on dice

Etymology

Refers to the resemblance of two single pips to a pair of snake's eyes.
