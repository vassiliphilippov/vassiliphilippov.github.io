neon lamp

1. NOUN

/ ˈniːɑn læmp / (AmE), / ˈniːɒn læmp / (BrE)
A small electric lamp in which light is produced by passing an electric current through neon gas at low pressure, causing the gas to glow.
small lamp using glowing neon gas

Examples

He installed a neon lamp in the indicator circuit to show when the power was on.
The faint red glow of a neon lamp is commonly used in nightlights and signs.
A faulty neon lamp can cause flickering or dim lighting in electronic equipment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neon light - a light produced by a glass tube filled with neon gas
  • Degree Lower: fluorescent lamp - a lamp using electricity to excite mercury vapor, producing visible light via fluorescence

Etymology

From 'neon', a noble gas discovered in 1898, and 'lamp', meaning a device for giving light.
