Secretary of Commerce and Labor

1. NOUN

/ səˈkrɛtɛri əv ˈkɑːmɚs ənd ˈleɪbɚ / (AmE), / sɪˈkrɛtəri əv ˈkɒmɜːs ənd ˈleɪbə / (BrE)
the head of a former U.S. executive department, established in 1903 and divided into the Departments of Commerce and Labor in 1913
head of former U.S. Commerce and Labor department

Examples

The Secretary of Commerce and Labor oversaw both commercial and labor affairs before the department was split.
During his tenure as Secretary of Commerce and Labor, he promoted several regulatory reforms.
The office of Secretary of Commerce and Labor existed for only a decade in U.S. history.
