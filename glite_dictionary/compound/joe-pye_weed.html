Joe-Pye weed

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒoʊˈpaɪ wiːd / (AmE), / ˌdʒəʊˈpaɪ wiːd / (BrE)
A North American perennial herb with whorled leaves and clusters of small pink or purple-spotted flowers.
North American perennial herb

Examples

We spotted a patch of Joe-Pye weed growing along the edge of the wetland.
The gardener planted Joe-Pye weed to attract butterflies to her yard.
Tall blooms of Joe-Pye weed add color to the late summer landscape.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Eutrochium - Genus of plants including Joe-Pye weed

Etymology

Named after a Native American purported to have used the plant for medicinal purposes.

2. NOUN

/ ˌdʒoʊˈpaɪ wiːd / (AmE), / ˌdʒəʊˈpaɪ wiːd / (BrE)
A tall North American herbaceous plant with whorled leaves and terminal clusters of small pinkish or purple flower heads, commonly found in moist areas.
a tall North American herb with pink or purple flowers

Examples

We spotted a cluster of Joe-Pye weed growing by the riverbank during our hike.
Gardeners often plant Joe-Pye weed to attract butterflies and bees to their gardens.
In late summer, the tall stems of Joe-Pye weed are crowned with beautiful pinkish blooms.

Etymology

Named after Joe Pye, a Native American healer reputed to have used the plant for medicinal purposes, combined with 'weed'.
