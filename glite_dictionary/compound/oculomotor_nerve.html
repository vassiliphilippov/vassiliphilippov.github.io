oculomotor nerve

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːkjələˈmoʊtər nɜːrv / (AmE), / ˌɒkjʊləʊˈməʊtə nɜːv / (BrE)
the third cranial nerve, responsible for supplying most of the muscles that move the eyeball
third cranial nerve controlling eye muscles
medical

Examples

Damage to the oculomotor nerve can result in difficulty moving the eye upward or inward.
The doctor tested the patient's oculomotor nerve function during the neurological exam.
The oculomotor nerve originates in the midbrain and controls most of the eye's movements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: optic nerve - the second cranial nerve carrying visual information from the eye to the brain
  • Synonym: trochlear nerve - the fourth cranial nerve involved in controlling eye muscles

Etymology

From Latin 'oculus' (eye) and 'motor' (mover), meaning 'eye mover'.
