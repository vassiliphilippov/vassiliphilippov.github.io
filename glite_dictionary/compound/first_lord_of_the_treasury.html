First Lord of the Treasury

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɜrst lɔrd əv ðə trɛʒəri / (AmE), / ˈfɜːst lɔːd əv ðə ˈtrɛʒəri / (BrE)
the British cabinet minister who is officially responsible for economic strategy, a title usually held by the Prime Minister
British cabinet minister overseeing the Treasury
british

Examples

The First Lord of the Treasury announced new economic measures in Parliament today.
Traditionally, the British Prime Minister also serves as the First Lord of the Treasury.
Her historical research focused on the role of the First Lord of the Treasury during the Victorian era.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Prime Minister - the head of the British government, a role typically held together with First Lord of the Treasury

Etymology

Title originating in 18th-century British government, designating the leading official of the Treasury commission.
