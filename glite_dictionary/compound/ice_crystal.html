ice crystal

1. NOUN

/ aɪs ˈkrɪs.təl / (AmE), / aɪs ˈkrɪs.təl / (BrE)
a small, single particle of ice, often forming as part of snow, frost, or clouds
a small particle of ice

Examples

Through the microscope, each ice crystal displayed a unique and intricate shape.
High in the atmosphere, ice crystals can cause halos around the sun and moon.
The snow on the ground sparkled as light reflected off countless ice crystals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snowflake - a flake of snow, formed by ice crystals
