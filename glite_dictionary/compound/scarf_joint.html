scarf joint

1. NOUN

/ skɑrf dʒɔɪnt / (AmE), / skɑːf dʒɔɪnt / (BrE)
A joint made by shaping and notching the ends of two pieces of wood or metal so they fit together end-to-end, typically to form a longer piece.
a joint joining two pieces end-to-end

Examples

The carpenter used a scarf joint to extend the length of the beam.
Metalworkers often employ a scarf joint when repairing railway tracks.
A properly executed scarf joint can be as strong as the original timber.

Etymology

From 'scarf' meaning to join by cutting the ends, of uncertain origin; 'joint' from Old French 'joint', from Latin 'junctus' (joined).
