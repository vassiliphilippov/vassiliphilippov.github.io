Centum language

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɛntəm ˌlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈkɛntəm ˌlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
A branch of Indo-European languages characterized by the preservation of Proto-Indo-European labiovelars and the development of velar articulations, such as /k/ or /kʰ/, from original palatal phonemes.
branch of Indo-European languages with distinctive velar sounds

Examples

Latin is an example of a Centum language, preserving original velar consonants from Proto-Indo-European.
In comparative linguistics, Centum languages are contrasted with Satem languages due to their sound changes.
Tocharian, once spoken in Central Asia, is classified as a Centum language despite its eastern location.

Synset Examples

The Centum languages show characteristic plain velars and labiovelars articulated at the back of the mouth in inherited Indo-European lexical items.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Satem language - an Indo-European language where palatal velars became sibilants

Etymology

From Latin 'centum' meaning 'hundred', after the Latin word for 'hundred', representing a characteristic sound change.
