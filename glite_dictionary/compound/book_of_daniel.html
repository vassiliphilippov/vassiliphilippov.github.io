Book of Daniel

1. PROPN

/ bʊk əv ˈdæniəl / (AmE), / bʊk əv ˈdanɪəl / (BrE)
A book of the Old Testament that recounts the visions and experiences of Daniel in the Babylonian court and includes apocalyptic prophecies.
Old Testament book about Daniel's visions

Examples

Many scholars debate the symbolism found in the Book of Daniel.
In Sunday school, we read the story of the lions' den from the Book of Daniel.
The apocalyptic imagery in the Book of Daniel influenced later religious writings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Daniel - a Hebrew prophet whose experiences are the subject of the Book of Daniel
  • Degree Lower: Book of Ezekiel - another Old Testament book with prophetic visions
  • Degree Higher: Book of Revelation - New Testament book with apocalyptic visions

Etymology

Daniel from Hebrew *Daniyyel* ('God is my judge').
