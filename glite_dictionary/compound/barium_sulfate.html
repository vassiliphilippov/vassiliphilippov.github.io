barium sulfate

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛriəm ˈsʌlˌfeɪt / (AmE), / ˈbeəriəm ˈsʌlfeɪt / (BrE)
a white, insoluble powder (BaSO₄) used as a pigment and as a radiopaque agent in medical imaging
white insoluble chemical compound used in imaging
medical

Examples

Before her X-ray, the doctor asked her to swallow barium sulfate for better imaging results.
Artists sometimes use barium sulfate as a pigment due to its whiteness and opacity.
Because barium sulfate does not dissolve in water, it's safe for certain medical uses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barium - a soft, silvery-white metal element, atomic number 56
  • Synonym: sulfate - a salt or ester of sulfuric acid containing the anion SO₄²⁻

Etymology

From 'barium' (element, from Greek 'barys', heavy) and 'sulfate' (from Latin 'sulfas').
