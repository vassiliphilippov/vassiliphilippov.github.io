genus Uvularia

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˌjuːvəˈlɛəriə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˌjuːvjʊˈlɑːrɪə / (BrE)
a genus of perennial rhizomatous herbs native to the southern and southeastern United States, bearing bell-shaped yellow flowers
genus of perennial herbaceous plants

Examples

The genus Uvularia includes several species found in woodland areas of the southeastern United States.
Botanists have studied the unique bell-shaped flowers characteristic of the genus Uvularia.
Members of the genus Uvularia are often cultivated in shade gardens for their delicate yellow blooms.

Etymology

From New Latin *Uvularia*, from Latin *uvula* (little grape, uvula), due to the flower's shape.
