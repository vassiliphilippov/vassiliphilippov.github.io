genus Phyllostomus

1. NOUN

/ ˈʤinəs fɪlɪˈstɑməs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs fɪlɪˈstɒməs / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Phyllostomatidae, comprising certain species of leaf-nosed bats found mainly in Central and South America.
a genus of leaf-nosed bats
medical

Examples

Scientists studied the cranial structure of genus Phyllostomus to understand bat evolution.
Several species within genus Phyllostomus demonstrate unique feeding behaviors.
The genus Phyllostomus includes some of the largest leaf-nosed bats in the Americas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Phyllostomus - a genus of leaf-nosed bats

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'Phyllo-' (from Greek phyllon, 'leaf') + '-stomus' (from Greek stoma, 'mouth').
