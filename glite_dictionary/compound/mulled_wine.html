mulled wine

1. NOUN

/ mʌld waɪn / (AmE), / mʌld waɪn / (BrE)
A drink of wine heated with sugar, spices, and often citrus fruit, typically served warm.
wine heated with sugar and spices

Examples

Every December, our family gathers to enjoy homemade mulled wine by the fireplace.
The aroma of mulled wine filled the market during the winter festival.
He served mulled wine with slices of orange and cinnamon sticks at the party.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glühwein - a German spiced heated wine similar to mulled wine
  • Synonym: hot toddy - a hot alcoholic drink made with liquor, water, sugar, and spices

Etymology

From the verb 'mull' (to heat and spice), origin uncertain but recorded in English since the 17th century.

2. NOUN

/ mʌld waɪn / (AmE), / mʌld waɪn / (BrE)
Wine heated with added spices and sometimes sweeteners, typically served warm in winter.
spiced, heated wine served warm

Examples

We enjoyed a cup of mulled wine by the fireplace during the holiday party.
She prepared mulled wine with cinnamon and cloves for her guests on a snowy evening.
The aroma of mulled wine filled the kitchen as it simmered on the stove.

Synset Examples

He served mulled wine as a winter warmer for guests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glühwein - German spiced, heated wine served at Christmas markets

Etymology

From 'mull', meaning to heat, sweeten, and flavor with spices; attested from 17th-century English.
