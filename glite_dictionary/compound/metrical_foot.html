metrical foot

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛtrɪkəl fʊt / (AmE), / ˈmɛtrɪkəl fʊt / (BrE)
A group of two or three syllables forming the basic rhythmic unit in poetry.
unit of poetic rhythm

Examples

In this poem, each line is composed of five metrical feet, creating a steady rhythm.
An iamb is a common type of metrical foot in English poetry.
Recognizing the metrical foot can help you better appreciate a poem’s structure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foot - basic rhythmic unit in poetry

Etymology

From Latin 'metrum' (measure, verse) and Old English 'fōt' (foot, also a unit of measure in poetry).
