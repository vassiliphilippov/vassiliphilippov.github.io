shaft furnace

1. NOUN

/ ʃæft ˈfɝnəs / (AmE), / ʃɑːft ˈfɜːnɪs / (BrE)
A vertical cylindrical furnace in which hot gases are forced upward through solid materials for processes such as smelting or roasting.
vertical cylindrical furnace for processing solids

Examples

The iron ore was processed in a shaft furnace designed for efficient smelting.
Modern steel plants often use the shaft furnace method to reduce energy consumption.
The engineer inspected the shaft furnace to ensure optimal airflow and temperature control.

Etymology

From 'shaft' (vertical passage) and 'furnace'; both from Old English and Old French roots.
