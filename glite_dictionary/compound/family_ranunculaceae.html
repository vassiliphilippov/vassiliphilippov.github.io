family Ranunculaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ræˌnʌŋkjəˈleɪsi.i / (AmE), / ˈfæməli ræˌnʌŋkjʊˈleɪsiː.iː / (BrE)
A large family of flowering plants, commonly known as the buttercup family, including genera such as Ranunculus, Clematis, and Delphinium.
family of flowering plants (buttercup family)

Examples

Several species in the family Ranunculaceae are popular in horticulture, including the anemone and clematis.
The family Ranunculaceae is distributed worldwide, with many members found in temperate regions.
Botanists study the family Ranunculaceae for its diversity of floral forms and ecological adaptations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: buttercup - common name for plants in the Ranunculus genus of the Ranunculaceae family

Etymology

From New Latin Ranunculaceae, based on the type genus Ranunculus (from Latin 'rana', frog, as some species grow in wet places).
