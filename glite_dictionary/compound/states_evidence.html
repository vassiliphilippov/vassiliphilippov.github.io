state's evidence

1. NOUN

/ steɪts ˈɛvɪdəns / (AmE), / steɪts ˈɛvɪdəns / (BrE)
evidence given to the prosecution in a criminal trial, often by an accomplice who testifies against their associates in exchange for leniency.
evidence for prosecution in a criminal case
legal

Examples

He agreed to turn state's evidence in exchange for a reduced sentence.
The witness, now cooperating with the prosecution, provided crucial state's evidence against his former partners.
Without anyone willing to give state's evidence, the case against the suspects collapsed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: testimony - formal written or spoken statement given in a court of law

Etymology

From 'state' (the government) and 'evidence'; used since the 18th century in reference to testimony given on behalf of the prosecution.
