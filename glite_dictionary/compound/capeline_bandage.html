capeline bandage

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæpəˌlaɪn ˈbændɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈkapɪˌlaɪn ˈbændɪdʒ / (BrE)
A bandage that is applied to cover the head or an amputation stump in the shape of a cap.
bandage shaped to cover head or stump
medical

Examples

The nurse skillfully wrapped a capeline bandage around the patient's head after surgery.
A capeline bandage is often used to protect an amputation stump from infection and injury.
You will need gauze and tape to apply a proper capeline bandage for head wounds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bandage - a strip of material used to bind a wound or protect an injured part of the body

Etymology

From French 'capeline' (a small cap), reflecting the shape of the bandage.
