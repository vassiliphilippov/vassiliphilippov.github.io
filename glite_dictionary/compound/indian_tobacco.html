Indian tobacco

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪndiən təˈbækoʊ / (AmE), / ˈɪndiən təˈbakəʊ / (BrE)
a tobacco plant native to South America and Mexico, often used for medicinal or herbal purposes
a plant native to South America and Mexico

Examples

The herbalist recommended Indian tobacco as a remedy for respiratory issues.
Scientists studied the effects of Indian tobacco in traditional medicine.
Indian tobacco grows naturally in several regions of South America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Lobelia inflata - a North American medicinal plant also called Indian tobacco

2. NOUN

/ ˈɪndiən təˈbækoʊ / (AmE), / ˈɪndiən təˈbækəʊ / (BrE)
A North American wild plant (Lobelia inflata) with small blue flowers and inflated seed capsules, formerly used in medicine as an antispasmodic.
North American plant, Lobelia inflata

Examples

The dried leaves of Indian tobacco were once commonly used in traditional medicine.
Indian tobacco is known for its attractive blue flowers and inflated seed pods.
Some herbalists still value Indian tobacco for its purported antispasmodic effects.
