herb Paris

1. NOUN

/ hɜrb ˈpæɹ.ɪs / (AmE), / hɜːb ˈpær.ɪs / (BrE)
A European woodland plant (Paris quadrifolia) with a single whorl of four broad leaves and a solitary greenish-yellow flower, reputed to be poisonous.
a European woodland plant (Paris quadrifolia)

Examples

During our hike through the forest, we spotted a rare patch of herb Paris growing in the shade.
Botanists warn that herb Paris contains toxic compounds and should not be ingested.
The four-leaved arrangement of herb Paris makes it easy to distinguish from other woodland plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trillium - A North American genus of woodland plants related to herb Paris

Etymology

From the genus name Paris; 'herb' from Old French herbe, from Latin herba.
