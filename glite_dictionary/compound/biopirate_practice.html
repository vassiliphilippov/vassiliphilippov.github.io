biopirate practice

1. NOUN

/ ˌbaɪ.oʊˈpaɪ.rət ˈpræk.tɪs / (AmE), / ˌbʌɪ.ə(ʊ)ˈpaɪ.rət ˈprak.tɪs / (BrE)
the exploitation of biological resources, such as plants or genetic material, without permission or compensation to the source communities or countries
exploitation of biological resources without consent

Examples

Some countries have enacted strict regulations to prevent biopirate practices by foreign corporations.
Indigenous communities demanded compensation after years of biopirate practice involving their traditional plants.
Researchers must obtain prior informed consent to avoid accusations of biopirate practice.

Synset Examples

Legislation was introduced to combat biopirate practices in the region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: biopiracy - the practice of exploiting biological resources without authorization or compensation

Etymology

From 'bio-' (relating to life or living organisms) + 'pirate' (one who takes without permission).
