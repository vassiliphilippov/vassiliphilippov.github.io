matrix algebra

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪ.trɪks ˈæl.dʒə.brə / (AmE), / ˈmeɪ.trɪks ˈæl.dʒɪ.brə / (BrE)
the branch of algebra concerned with the study and manipulation of matrices
algebraic study of matrices

Examples

My professor recommended a book on matrix algebra to help me understand linear transformations.
Researchers often use matrix algebra to simplify complex calculations in data analysis.
A solid grasp of matrix algebra is essential for advanced computer graphics programming.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: linear algebra - the branch of mathematics concerning linear equations, matrices, and vector spaces

Etymology

From 'matrix' (Latin: womb, source) and 'algebra' (Arabic: al-jabr, meaning 'reunion of broken parts').
