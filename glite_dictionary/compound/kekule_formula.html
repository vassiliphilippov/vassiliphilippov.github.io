Kekule formula

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɛkjʊleɪ ˈfɔrmjələ / (AmE), / ˈkɛkjʊliː ˈfɔːmjʊlə / (BrE)
a structural formula representing benzene as a closed chain of six carbon atoms with alternating single and double bonds, each carbon atom bonded to one hydrogen atom
structural formula of benzene ring
medical

Examples

The Kekule formula helped scientists understand the structure of benzene.
His textbook included a detailed diagram of the Kekule formula for benzene.
When explaining aromatic compounds, the professor referenced the Kekule formula.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: benzene ring - aromatic ring structure of six carbon atoms, as found in benzene

Etymology

Named after German chemist August Kekulé (1829–1896), who proposed this structure.
