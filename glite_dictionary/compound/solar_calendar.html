solar calendar

1. NOUN

/ ˈsoʊlər ˈkæl.ən.dɚ / (AmE), / ˈsəʊ.lə ˈkæl.ən.də / (BrE)
A calendar in which dates are determined by the apparent position of the sun in the sky, typically reflecting the length of the solar year.
calendar based on the solar year

Examples

The Gregorian calendar is the most widely used solar calendar in the world today.
Ancient Egyptians developed a solar calendar to align with the annual flooding of the Nile.
A solar calendar helps ensure that seasons occur at the same time each year.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lunar calendar - a calendar based on the cycles of the moon

Etymology

From Latin solaris (of the sun) + calendar from Latin calendarium (account book, calendar).
