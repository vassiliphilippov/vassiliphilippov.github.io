sarsaparilla root

1. NOUN

/ ˌsæspəˈrɪlə ruːt / (AmE), / ˌsɑːspəˈrɪlə ruːt / (BrE)
the dried root of various plants of the genus Smilax, used especially as a flavoring agent or in herbal remedies
dried root used as a flavoring or remedy

Examples

Traditional medicines often use sarsaparilla root to brew herbal teas believed to have health benefits.
In the 19th century, sarsaparilla root was a popular ingredient in tonics and soft drinks.
The chef added a touch of sarsaparilla root to give the dessert an earthy, spicy note.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sarsaparilla - a sweet drink or the plant it derives from

Etymology

From Spanish 'zarzaparrilla', ultimately from Spanish 'zarza' (bramble) and 'parrilla' (little vine).
