solvating agent

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɑlˌveɪtɪŋ ˈeɪdʒənt / (AmE), / ˈsɒlˌveɪtɪŋ ˈeɪdʒənt / (BrE)
A substance that facilitates the formation of a solvate by dissolving another substance.
substance that promotes solvation
medical

Examples

Water can act as a solvating agent for many ionic compounds.
The choice of solvating agent significantly affects the crystallization process.
Selecting an appropriate solvating agent is crucial in pharmaceutical formulation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: solvent - a substance that dissolves another to form a solution

Etymology

From 'solvate', itself from Latin 'solvere' (to loosen), and 'agent', from Latin 'agens' (doing).
