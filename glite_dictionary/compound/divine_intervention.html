divine intervention

1. NOUN

/ dəˈvaɪn ˌɪntərˈvɛnʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈvaɪn ˌɪntəˈvɛnʃən / (BrE)
interference or action in human affairs that is attributed to a deity or supernatural power, often seen as miraculous or extraordinary
miraculous action attributed to a deity

Examples

Many people saw their survival as evidence of divine intervention during the disaster.
The doctor could not explain her sudden recovery, calling it nothing short of divine intervention.
In ancient myths, heroes often succeed thanks to divine intervention.

Synset Examples

They believed that divine intervention saved their child.
He credited his success to divine intervention in his life.
After a series of miracles, she called it divine intervention.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: miracle - an extraordinary and welcome event that is not explicable by natural or scientific laws and is therefore attributed to a divine agency
