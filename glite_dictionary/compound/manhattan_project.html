Manhattan Project

1. PROPN

/ mænˈhæt.ən ˈprɑː.dʒekt / (AmE), / mænˈhæt.ən ˈprɒ.dʒekt / (BrE)
the United States research and development project during World War II that produced the first atomic bombs
WWII U.S. atomic bomb project

Examples

The Manhattan Project marked a turning point in the history of warfare and science.
Many leading physicists were recruited to join the Manhattan Project during the 1940s.
Books and documentaries have explored the ethical dilemmas faced by those on the Manhattan Project.

Etymology

Named after the Manhattan Engineer District of the U.S. Army Corps of Engineers, where the project began.

2. PROPN

/ mænˈhætən ˈprɑdʒɛkt / (AmE), / mænˈhætən ˈprɒdʒɛkt / (BrE)
the code name for the secret United States project established in 1942 to develop atomic bombs during World War II
secret U.S. project to develop atomic bombs in WWII

Examples

The Manhattan Project was a pivotal moment in the history of science and warfare.
Many top scientists were secretly recruited to work on the Manhattan Project during World War II.
The success of the Manhattan Project led to the development of nuclear weapons.

Etymology

Named after the Manhattan Engineer District of the U.S. Army Corps of Engineers, where the project was initially based.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00821893-n
Synset POS: n
