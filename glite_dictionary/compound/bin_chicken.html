bin chicken

1. NOUN

/ bɪn ˈtʃɪk.ən / (AmE), / bɪn ˈtʃɪk.ɪn / (BrE)
(informal, chiefly Australian) A colloquial term for the Australian white ibis, especially when seen scavenging in urban rubbish bins.
colloquial name for Australian white ibis
informal british

Examples

In Sydney, it's not unusual to see a bin chicken searching for scraps in a park.
The bin chicken has become a symbol of urban wildlife in many Australian cities.
Children watched as a curious bin chicken strutted near the picnic tables.

Synset Examples

The bin chicken rummaged through the trash for food.
The bin chicken has become a common sight in urban areas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ibis - wading bird with a long down-curved bill, including the Australian white ibis

Etymology

Australian slang, from 'bin' (rubbish container) + 'chicken' (bird); refers humorously to the feeding habits of the ibis.
