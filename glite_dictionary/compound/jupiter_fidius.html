Jupiter Fidius

1. PROPN

/ ˈdʒuːpɪtər ˈfɪdiəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒuːpɪtə ˈfɪdiəs / (BrE)
An epithet of Jupiter, the chief Roman god, highlighting his role as the guarantor of good faith and oaths.
epithet of Jupiter as guarantor of good faith

Examples

The temple dedicated to Jupiter Fidius was a place where Romans swore solemn oaths.
In ancient Rome, contracts were often concluded under the watch of Jupiter Fidius to ensure honesty.
Priests invoked Jupiter Fidius as a witness to treaties and important promises.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Jupiter - Roman chief god, equivalent to Zeus in Greek mythology
  • Synonym: Zeus - Chief god in Greek mythology

Etymology

From Latin 'Iuppiter Fidius', with 'Fidius' from 'fides' meaning 'faith' or 'trust'.
