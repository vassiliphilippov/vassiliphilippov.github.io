rose apple

1. NOUN

/ ˈroʊz ˌæpəl / (AmE), / ˈrəʊz ˌæpəl / (BrE)
a tropical tree native to the East Indies, cultivated for its fragrant, edible fruit which resembles an apple in shape and scent.
tropical tree with edible, rose-scented fruit

Examples

The rose apple thrives in warm climates and is popular in tropical gardens.
She tasted the sweet, floral flavor of a freshly picked rose apple.
Farmers in Southeast Asia often sell rose apples alongside other exotic fruits at markets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Syzygium jambos - scientific name for the rose apple tree

2. NOUN

/ roʊz ˈæp.əl / (AmE), / rəʊz ˈæp.əl / (BrE)
A fragrant, oval, yellowish tropical fruit, often used in jellies and confections, from various trees of the genus Syzygium.
fragrant tropical fruit used in jellies

Examples

The chef added slices of rose apple to the fruit salad for a fragrant touch.
Jellies made from rose apple are popular in many tropical countries.
She described the flavor of rose apple as a mix between rose and pear.
