social control

1. NOUN

/ ˈsoʊʃl kənˈtroʊl / (AmE), / ˈsəʊʃl kənˈtrəʊl / (BrE)
the ways in which a society or group regulates individual or group behavior, typically through norms, rules, and sanctions
ways society regulates behavior

Examples

Many laws are forms of social control designed to maintain public order.
Peer pressure can act as a subtle mechanism of social control in schools.
Religious rituals often function as a form of social control within communities.
