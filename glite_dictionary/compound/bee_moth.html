bee moth

1. NOUN

/ bi mɔθ / (AmE), / biː mɒθ / (BrE)
A small moth whose larvae inhabit and feed on bee honeycombs, often considered a pest to beehives.
a moth with larvae that live in bee hives

Examples

The bee moth is a common threat to commercial apiaries, damaging the honeycombs of domesticated hives.
Beekeepers must inspect their hives regularly to check for signs of infestation by the bee moth.
Larvae of the bee moth tunnel through wax and honeycomb, weakening the colony's structure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wax moth - another name for the bee moth, particularly the greater wax moth
