contour language

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑn.tʊr ˈlæŋ.ɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.tjʊə ˈlæŋ.ɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
a type of tone language in which the meaning of words is distinguished by pitch changes or movement in tone within syllables.
tone language using pitch changes

Examples

Mandarin Chinese is a well-known contour language with meaning changes based on pitch movement.
Linguists often compare contour languages to those with level tones to study tonal complexity.
A contour language uses rising and falling tones within a syllable to distinguish words.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tone language - a language where the meaning of a word depends on pitch
