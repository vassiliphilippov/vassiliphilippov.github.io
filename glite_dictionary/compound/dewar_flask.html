Dewar flask

1. NOUN

/ ˈduːər flæsk / (AmE), / ˈdjuːə flɑːsk / (BrE)
A vacuum-sealed container designed to hold extremely cold liquids, such as liquid air or helium, used especially in scientific research.
vacuum container for cold liquids in science

Examples

The laboratory technician carefully transferred the liquid nitrogen into a Dewar flask for storage.
A Dewar flask is essential when working with substances that must be kept at cryogenic temperatures.
Physicists used a Dewar flask to conduct experiments with liquid helium.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vacuum flask - container that insulates its contents from outside temperature by means of a vacuum

Etymology

Named after Sir James Dewar, the Scottish chemist and physicist who invented it.
