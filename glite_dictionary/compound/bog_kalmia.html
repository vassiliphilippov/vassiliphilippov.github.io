bog kalmia

1. NOUN

/ bɑɡ ˈkælmiə / (AmE), / bɒɡ ˈkalmɪə / (BrE)
a low-growing shrub of boggy areas in northwestern United States, with small purple flowers and pale, glaucous leaves beneath
Northwestern U.S. bog shrub with purple flowers

Examples

The delicate blooms of the bog kalmia added a splash of color to the wetland.
Researchers studied how bog kalmia adapts to the acidic soil of northern bogs.
Hikers were warned not to pick the protected bog kalmia in the nature reserve.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Kalmia polifolia - scientific name for bog kalmia
  • Degree Higher: laurel - any of several aromatic evergreen shrubs

Etymology

From 'bog' (wet, spongy ground) and Modern Latin 'Kalmia' (a genus named after botanist Peter Kalm).
