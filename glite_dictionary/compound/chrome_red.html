chrome red

1. NOUN

/ kroʊm rɛd / (AmE), / krəʊm rɛd / (BrE)
a bright red pigment consisting of basic lead chromate, used in paints and coatings
a type of bright red pigment

Examples

Artists once favored chrome red for its vivid hue and durability in oil paints.
Early fire engines were often painted with chrome red to make them stand out.
The restoration team matched the original color using authentic chrome red pigment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chrome yellow - a yellow pigment made from lead chromate

Etymology

From 'chrome', relating to chromium-based pigments, and 'red'.
