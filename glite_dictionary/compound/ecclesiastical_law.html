ecclesiastical law

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌkliːziˈæstɪkəl lɔ / (AmE), / ɪˌkliːzɪˈæstɪkəl lɔː / (BrE)
the body of laws and regulations developed or adopted by Christian churches to govern their organization and affairs
laws governing Christian church affairs
legal

Examples

The dispute was settled under ecclesiastical law rather than civil law.
Members of the clergy must often study ecclesiastical law to understand their responsibilities.
Ecclesiastical law has historically influenced many aspects of Western legal traditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: canon law - laws and regulations made by church authorities

Etymology

From Greek 'ekklesiastikos' (of the assembly or church) and Latin 'lex' (law).
