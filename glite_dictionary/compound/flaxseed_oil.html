flaxseed oil

1. NOUN

/ ˈflækˌsid ɔɪl / (AmE), / ˈflæk.siːd ɔɪl / (BrE)
an oil extracted from flax seeds, used as a food supplement and in products such as paints and varnishes
oil from flax seeds, used in food and industry

Examples

Many artists prefer to use flaxseed oil as a medium in their oil paintings.
Nutritionists often recommend adding flaxseed oil to diets for its omega-3 fatty acids.
She bought a bottle of flaxseed oil to use in homemade salad dressings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: linseed oil - an oil obtained from the seeds of the flax plant, especially for industrial use

Etymology

From 'flaxseed' (the seed of the flax plant) + 'oil'.
