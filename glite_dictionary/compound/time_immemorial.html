time immemorial

1. NOUN

/ taɪm ɪˌmɛˈmɔriəl / (AmE), / taɪm ɪˌmɪˈmɔːrɪəl / (BrE)
A point in the distant past that is so far back it cannot be remembered or recorded.
the distant past, beyond memory or record

Examples

My ancestors have lived in this valley since time immemorial.
Certain traditions have existed here from time immemorial.
The river has followed its course since time immemorial, shaping the landscape.

Etymology

Immemorial from Latin immemorabilis (not to be remembered, beyond memory).
