heath violet

1. NOUN

/ hiːθ ˈvaɪələt / (AmE), / hiːθ ˈvaɪələt / (BrE)
An Old World species of violet with leafy stems and blue flowers, typically found in heathland or open habitats.
an Old World species of blue-flowered violet

Examples

The heath violet adds a delicate touch of blue to the wild moorlands in early spring.
Botanists discovered a thriving cluster of heath violet on the edge of the old heath.
She pressed a heath violet in her notebook as a memento of her countryside walk.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: violet - any plant of the genus Viola, especially those with purple or blue flowers
