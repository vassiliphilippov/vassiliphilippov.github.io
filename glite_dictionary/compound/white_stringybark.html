white stringybark

1. NOUN

/ waɪt ˈstrɪŋiˌbɑrk / (AmE), / waɪt ˈstrɪŋibɑːk / (BrE)
A type of eucalyptus tree with fibrous bark and white wood, found mainly in Australia.
Australian eucalyptus tree with white wood

Examples

The white stringybark is valued for its durable timber.
Many Australian forests are dominated by white stringybark trees.
A small bird nested high in a white stringybark near the edge of the clearing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stringybark - a group of eucalyptus trees with stringy bark
