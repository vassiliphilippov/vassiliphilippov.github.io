oral contraception

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrəl ˌkɑntrəˈsɛpʃən / (AmE), / ˈɔːrəl ˌkɒntrəˈsɛpʃən / (BrE)
A method of preventing pregnancy by taking contraceptive pills by mouth.
prevention of pregnancy using contraceptive pills
medical

Examples

Many women rely on oral contraception as their primary method of birth control.
Discussing the risks and benefits of oral contraception with your doctor is important before starting treatment.
Some people experience side effects when using oral contraception for an extended period.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: birth control pill - a tablet taken orally to prevent pregnancy
  • Synonym: contraception - prevention of pregnancy
