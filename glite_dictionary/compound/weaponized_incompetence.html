weaponized incompetence

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɛpəˌnaɪzd ɪnˈkɑmpɪtəns / (AmE), / ˈwɛpənaɪzd ɪnˈkɒmpɪtəns / (BrE)
the act of deliberately pretending to be unable to perform a task in order to avoid responsibility or work
deliberate feigning of inability to avoid work

Examples

The team recognized his weaponized incompetence and stopped assigning him critical tasks.
She accused her partner of using weaponized incompetence as an excuse to avoid household chores.
Managers are increasingly aware of weaponized incompetence in workplace dynamics.

Synset Examples

She used weaponized incompetence to avoid doing the chores.
His weaponized incompetence at work made others pick up his slack.
Employers should be wary of weaponized incompetence among employees.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: incompetence - lack of ability or skill to do something successfully

Etymology

Formed from 'weaponize' (to use something as a weapon) and 'incompetence' (lack of ability), first appearing in the 2010s.
