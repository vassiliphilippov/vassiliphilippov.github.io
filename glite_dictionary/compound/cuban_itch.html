Cuban itch

1. NOUN

/ ˈkjuːbən ɪtʃ / (AmE), / ˈkjuːbən ɪtʃ / (BrE)
a mild form of smallpox, caused by a less virulent strain of the virus
mild type of smallpox
medical

Examples

Cases of Cuban itch were reported in the region during the early 19th century.
Doctors identified the symptoms of Cuban itch as less severe than typical smallpox.
Historical records mention Cuban itch among the diseases affecting Caribbean populations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smallpox - a serious infectious viral disease marked by fever and pustules
