meadow saxifrage

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛdoʊ ˈsæksɪfrɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈmɛdəʊ ˈsæksɪfrɪdʒ / (BrE)
a rosette-forming perennial plant with compact clusters of white flowers, native to Europe
European perennial plant with white flowers

Examples

A patch of meadow saxifrage brightened the edge of the woodland in spring.
Botanists recorded an increase in meadow saxifrage populations following restoration of the grassland.
Gardeners value meadow saxifrage for its delicate white flowers and ability to thrive in poor soil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: saxifrage - any plant of the genus Saxifraga

Etymology

From Latin 'saxifraga' (stone-breaker), traditionally referring to plants thought to break stones or grow in rocky places; 'meadow' refers to its typical habitat.
