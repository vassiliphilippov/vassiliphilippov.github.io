Pall Mall

1. PROPN

/ pæl ˈmæl, pɔl ˈmæl / (AmE), / pæl ˈmæl, pɔːl ˈmæl / (BrE)
a street in central London, famous for its traditional gentlemen's clubs and status as a fashionable address
street in London known for private clubs
british

Examples

Many influential politicians have been members of clubs on Pall Mall throughout history.
The architecture along Pall Mall reflects its long association with London's upper classes.
Tourists often stroll down Pall Mall to admire its elegant buildings and exclusive clubs.

Etymology

Named after the 17th-century game 'pall-mall', which was once played there; from French 'paille-maille' and Italian 'pallamaglio'.
