Duke of Windsor

1. PROPN

/ duːk əv ˈwɪnzər / (AmE), / djuːk əv ˈwɪnzə / (BrE)
the title given to Edward VIII after his abdication as King of the United Kingdom in 1936, following his marriage to Wallis Simpson
title of Edward VIII after abdication

Examples

After his abdication, Edward became the Duke of Windsor and lived much of his life abroad.
The Duke of Windsor's relationship with Wallis Simpson changed the monarchy forever.
Many books have been written about the life and exile of the Duke of Windsor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Edward VIII - King of the United Kingdom who abdicated; known as Duke of Windsor after abdication

Etymology

From the peerage title 'Duke of Windsor', created for Edward VIII in 1937, after his abdication.
