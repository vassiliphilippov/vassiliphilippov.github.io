Narcissus jonquilla

1. NOUN

/ nɑrˈsɪsəs dʒɒnˈkwɪlə / (AmE), / nɑːˈsɪsəs dʒɒnˈkwɪlə / (BrE)
A species of daffodil native to southern Europe, cultivated for its fragrant yellow or white clustered flowers.
species of fragrant daffodil

Examples

The garden was filled with the sweet scent of Narcissus jonquilla in early spring.
She planted several Narcissus jonquilla bulbs along the walkway for their lovely fragrance.
Botanists admire Narcissus jonquilla for its delicate, clustered yellow blooms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jonquil - common name for Narcissus jonquilla, a type of daffodil
  • Degree Higher: daffodil - common flowering plant of the Narcissus genus

Etymology

From Latin 'Narcissus' (a plant genus) and Spanish 'junquillo' (little rush), referring to its rush-like leaves.
