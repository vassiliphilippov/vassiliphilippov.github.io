methane series

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛθˌeɪn ˈsɪriːz / (AmE), / ˈmiːθeɪn ˈsɪəriːz / (BrE)
a group of non-aromatic saturated hydrocarbons (alkanes) with the general formula CnH₂n₊₂, starting with methane
group of saturated hydrocarbons (alkanes)

Examples

All members of the methane series are saturated hydrocarbons with single covalent bonds.
Ethane and propane are the next two compounds in the methane series after methane.
Chemists study the physical properties of the methane series to understand trends in alkanes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alkane - any saturated hydrocarbon with only single bonds (CnH₂n₊₂)

Etymology

Named after methane, the simplest member of the series; 'series' from Latin 'series' (row, succession).
