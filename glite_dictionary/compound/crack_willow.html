crack willow

1. NOUN

/ kræk ˈwɪloʊ / (AmE), / kræk ˈwɪləʊ / (BrE)
a large willow tree (Salix fragilis) with stiff branches that are easily broken off
species of willow tree with brittle branches

Examples

A mature crack willow stood beside the river, its limbs arching over the water.
The stiff branches of the crack willow are known for breaking easily in strong winds.
They planted several crack willows along the banks to reduce soil erosion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: willow - a tree or shrub with narrow leaves and flexible branches

Etymology

The name refers to the way its branches 'crack' or break easily.
