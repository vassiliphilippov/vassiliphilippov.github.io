quadratic equation

1. NOUN

/ kwɑːˈdrætɪk ɪˈkweɪʒən / (AmE), / kwɒˈdratɪk ɪˈkweɪʒən / (BrE)
an equation in which the highest power of the unknown variable is a square, typically written in the form ax² + bx + c = 0
an equation with the highest power of two

Examples

In algebra class, we learned how to solve a quadratic equation using the quadratic formula.
The graph of any quadratic equation is a parabola opening upwards or downwards.
A simple example of a quadratic equation is x² + 5x + 6 = 0.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: linear equation - an equation in which the highest power of the variable is one
  • Degree Higher: cubic equation - an equation in which the highest power of the variable is three

Etymology

From Latin 'quadratus' (square) and 'equatio' (equation).
