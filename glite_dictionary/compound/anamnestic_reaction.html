anamnestic reaction

1. NOUN

/ ˌænəmˈnɛstɪk riˈækʃən / (AmE), / ˌanəmˈnɛstɪk riˈakʃən / (BrE)
a rapid renewed production of antibodies after a second or subsequent exposure to the same antigen
renewed antibody production after repeat exposure to antigen
medical

Examples

The vaccine was designed to trigger an anamnestic reaction upon exposure to the real virus.
After the booster shot, her immune system exhibited a strong anamnestic reaction.
Doctors observed an anamnestic reaction when the patient was re-exposed to the allergen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: secondary immune response - the immune system's more rapid and intense response to a previously encountered antigen

Etymology

From Greek anamnestikos (reminding), from anamnesis (recollection).
