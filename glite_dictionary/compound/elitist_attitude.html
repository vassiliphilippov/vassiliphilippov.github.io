elitist attitude

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈlitɪst æˈtɪtjud / (AmE), / ɪˈliːtɪst əˈtɪtjuːd / (BrE)
A belief or behavior reflecting a sense of superiority over others, often resulting in social exclusion or condescension.
belief in one's superiority over others

Examples

Her elitist attitude made it difficult for her to connect with people from different backgrounds.
The manager's elitist attitude was evident when he dismissed all suggestions from junior staff.
Critics argue that an elitist attitude can hinder progress by ignoring the contributions of ordinary people.

Synset Examples

His elitist attitude alienated many of his friends.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snobbishness - the character or quality of being a snob; condescending behavior
  • Antonym: egalitarianism - the belief in the equality of all people

Etymology

From 'elitist', derived from 'elite' (from French élite, meaning 'the chosen'), and 'attitude' (from French attitude).
