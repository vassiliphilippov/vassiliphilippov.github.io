transit declinometer

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrænzɪt dɪˌklaɪˈnɑːmɪtər / (AmE), / ˈtrænzɪt dɪˌklɪˈnɒmɪtə / (BrE)
an instrument used to measure the magnetic declination, often used in geophysical surveys
instrument for measuring magnetic declination

Examples

Early explorers used a transit declinometer to determine the local magnetic variation.
The field team set up a transit declinometer to chart changes in the Earth's magnetic field.
A transit declinometer can provide precise readings essential for geological mapping projects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: declinometer - instrument for measuring magnetic declination

Etymology

From 'transit', a type of surveying instrument, and 'declinometer', from Latin 'declinare' (to turn aside) + Greek 'metron' (measure).
