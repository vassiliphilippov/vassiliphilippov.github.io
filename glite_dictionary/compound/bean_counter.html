bean counter

1. NOUN

/ ˈbin ˌkaʊnər / (AmE), / ˈbiːn ˌkaʊntə / (BrE)
an accountant or official who focuses excessively on financial details, often at the expense of wider considerations
person overly concerned with financial details
informal

Examples

The company's bean counter insisted that every purchase be approved beforehand.
Some people think of auditors as mere bean counters, but their role is crucial to transparency.
Our new manager is more than just a bean counter; she understands people as well as numbers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accountant - a person whose job is to keep or inspect financial accounts
  • Synonym: bureaucrat - an official in a government or large organization, typically one perceived as adhering strictly to rules and procedure

Etymology

Originally American slang, referring figuratively to counting beans as a symbol of trivial accounting matters.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbin ˌkaʊntɚ / (AmE), / ˈbiːn ˌkaʊntə / (BrE)
an accountant or a person who is responsible for keeping financial records, often implying excessive attention to detail or cost
someone responsible for financial records; accountant
informal american

Examples

After the audit, the bean counter recommended several ways to reduce costs.
Some people see the bean counter as a meticulous guardian of company finances.
The bean counters at head office want every receipt accounted for.

Synset Examples

The bean counter always keeps track of our expenses.
He called himself a bean counter after organizing the finances.
As a bean counter, she was meticulous with the budget.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accountant - a person whose job is to keep or inspect financial accounts
  • Synonym: bookkeeper - a person whose job is to keep records of the financial affairs of a business

Etymology

Originating in American English slang, from 'bean' meaning a unit (as in counting beans); figuratively for money or numbers.
