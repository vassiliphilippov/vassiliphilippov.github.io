chatter mark

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃætɚ mɑːrk / (AmE), / ˈtʃætə mɑːk / (BrE)
a series of marks or small indentations on a rock surface created by the movement of a glacier over bedrock
glacial marks on rock surfaces

Examples

The granite slab was covered in chatter marks left by the retreating glacier.
Geologists examined the chatter mark patterns to determine the direction of glacial movement.
A field trip to the valley revealed several rocks bearing distinct chatter marks.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtʃæt̬ɚ mɑrk / (AmE), / ˈtʃætə mɑːk / (BrE)
a groove or indentation left on the surface of a workpiece by a rapidly vibrating cutting tool during machining
groove left by vibrating tool in machining

Examples

The technician noticed several chatter marks on the surface of the metal plate.
Reducing the speed of the machine helped to eliminate chatter marks during production.
Polishing was required to remove the chatter mark left by the faulty lathe.
