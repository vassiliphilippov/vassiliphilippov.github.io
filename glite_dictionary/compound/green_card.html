green card

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡrin ˌkɑrd / (AmE), / ˈɡriːn ˌkɑːd / (BrE)
an official document permitting a foreign national to live and work permanently in the United States
U.S. permanent resident identification card
american

Examples

After years of waiting, she finally received her green card and could start a new life in the U.S.
He must renew his green card before it expires next year to retain his residency status.
Many immigrants aspire to obtain a green card in hopes of pursuing better opportunities.

Synset Examples

he was surprised to discover that green cards are no longer green

Related Terms

  • Synonym: permanent resident - a person authorized to live indefinitely in a country
