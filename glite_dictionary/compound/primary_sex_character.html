primary sex character

1. NOUN

/ ˈpraɪˌmɛri sɛks ˈkærəktɚ / (AmE), / ˈprʌɪməri sɛks ˈkarɪktə / (BrE)
A genetically determined sex characteristic directly related to the reproductive organs and genitals, distinguishing the biological sexes.
sex organs directly involved in reproduction
medical

Examples

The development of primary sex characters occurs before birth and determines the anatomical differences between males and females.
Doctors examine the primary sex characters to determine the biological sex of a newborn.
Abnormalities in primary sex characters can sometimes require medical intervention shortly after birth.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: secondary sex character - traits appearing at puberty not directly involved in reproduction

Etymology

From Latin 'primarius' (of the first rank) + sex + character.
