long-horned grasshopper

1. NOUN

/ lɔŋ hɔrnd ˈɡræsˌhɑpər / (AmE), / lɒŋ hɔːnd ˈɡræsˌhɒpə / (BrE)
a grasshopper of the family Tettigoniidae, having long threadlike antennae and, in males, well-developed sound-producing organs on the forewings
insect with long antennae and stridulating organs

Examples

We spotted a long-horned grasshopper perched on a leaf in the forest.
The long-horned grasshopper uses its wings to produce a distinctive sound at night.
Biologists studied the mating calls of the male long-horned grasshopper.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: katydid - a type of long-horned grasshopper known for its loud song
