blue book

1. NOUN

/ bluː bʊk / (AmE), / bluː bʊk / (BrE)
a book or register listing socially prominent people or members of high society
register of socially prominent people

Examples

The family's name appeared in the blue book, confirming their elite social status.
Only those included in the blue book were invited to the exclusive gala.
She checked the blue book to see if her ancestors had ever been listed among the city's prominent families.

2. NOUN

/ bluː bʊk / (AmE), / bluː bʊk / (BrE)
an official report published by the British government, usually bound in blue covers
official British government report
british

Examples

The committee referenced the blue book released last year in their analysis.
Historians often consult blue books to understand policy decisions from that era.
She found a blue book in the parliamentary archives detailing foreign affairs.

Etymology

From the blue covers traditionally used for official British government reports.
