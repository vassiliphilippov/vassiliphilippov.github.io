funicular railway

1. NOUN

/ fəˈnɪkjələr ˈreɪlˌweɪ / (AmE), / fjʊˈnɪkjʊlə ˈreɪlweɪ / (BrE)
A type of railway, typically on a steep slope, in which ascending and descending cars are counterbalanced and moved by a cable.
cable railway on a steep slope

Examples

The funicular railway offers spectacular views as it climbs the mountainside.
Tourists often take the funicular railway to reach the old castle at the summit.
A new funicular railway was constructed to provide easier access to the ski resort.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cable car - a vehicle pulled by a continually moving cable, typically up a steep incline

Etymology

From Latin 'funiculus' (small rope or cord) and 'railway'.
