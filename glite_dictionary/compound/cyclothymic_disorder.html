cyclothymic disorder

1. NOUN

/ ˌsaɪ.kləˈθaɪ.mɪk dɪˈsɔːr.dɚ / (AmE), / ˌsaɪ.kləˈθaɪ.mɪk dɪˈsɔː.də / (BrE)
A mild, chronic form of bipolar disorder characterized by alternating periods of mild depression and mild elation, persisting over a long duration.
mild, long-lasting bipolar disorder
medical

Examples

After years of shifting moods, she was finally diagnosed with cyclothymic disorder by her psychiatrist.
People with cyclothymic disorder often experience both periods of elevated and depressed mood, but the symptoms are mild.
Early intervention can help individuals with cyclothymic disorder manage their symptoms effectively.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: bipolar disorder - A mental health condition involving periods of mania and depression
  • Degree Higher: major depressive disorder - A mood disorder causing persistent feelings of sadness and loss of interest

Etymology

From Greek *kyklos* (circle) + *thymos* (mood, emotion), with 'disorder' used in medical classification.
