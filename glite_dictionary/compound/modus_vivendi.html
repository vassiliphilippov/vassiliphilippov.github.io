modus vivendi

1. NOUN

/ ˌmoʊdəs vɪˈvɛndi / (AmE), / ˌməʊdəs vɪˈvɛndi / (BrE)
an arrangement allowing conflicting parties to coexist peacefully, usually as a temporary solution until a more permanent settlement is reached
temporary arrangement between conflicting parties

Examples

The two countries reached a modus vivendi that allowed them to share the disputed territory without further conflict.
A fragile modus vivendi was established in the office while management worked on a permanent solution.
Despite their differences, the families found a modus vivendi to peacefully coexist in the same neighborhood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compromise - an agreement where each side makes concessions
  • Synonym: truce - an agreement to stop fighting for a certain time
  • Synonym: agreement - resolution or arrangement settled by two or more parties

Etymology

From Latin, literally ‘way of living’.

2. NOUN

/ ˌmoʊdəs vɪˈvɛndi / (AmE), / ˌməʊdəs vɪˈvɛndi / (BrE)
a way of living that expresses or accommodates a person's values, habits, and attitudes, often used to describe an arrangement between conflicting parties that allows for coexistence
way of living or practical arrangement

Examples

After years of disagreement, the two nations reached a modus vivendi that allowed peaceful coexistence.
He found a modus vivendi between his demanding job and his family life.
Their relationship was challenging, but they developed a modus vivendi that worked for both of them.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lifestyle - the way in which a person or group lives

Etymology

From Latin 'modus vivendi' (way of living).
