Shetland pony

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃɛt.lənd ˈpoʊ.ni / (AmE), / ˈʃɛt.lənd ˈpəʊ.ni / (BrE)
a breed of very small, sturdy pony with a long shaggy mane and tail, originally from the Shetland Islands of Scotland
small, hardy breed of pony

Examples

The children took turns riding the Shetland pony at the farm.
A Shetland pony can easily withstand harsh weather due to its thick coat.
Her birthday present was a friendly Shetland pony named Daisy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pony - a small horse, especially one of a breed used for riding or driving

Etymology

Named after the Shetland Islands (north of Scotland), where the breed originated.
