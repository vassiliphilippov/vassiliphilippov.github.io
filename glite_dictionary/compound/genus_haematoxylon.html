genus Haematoxylon

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌhiː.məˈtɒk.sɪˌlɒn / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌhiː.məˈtɒk.sɪˌlɒn / (BrE)
A small genus of spiny, bushy shrubs or trees native to tropical America.
genus of tropical American shrubs or trees

Examples

The genus Haematoxylon includes several species valued for their dye-producing wood.
Botanists have long studied the unique characteristics of the genus Haematoxylon.
One plant in the genus Haematoxylon is widely used for its medicinal properties.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, combination of 'genus' (Latin for kind) and 'Haematoxylon' (from Greek 'haima' meaning blood and 'xylon' meaning wood).
