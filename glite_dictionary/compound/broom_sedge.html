broom sedge

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrum ˌsɛdʒ / (AmE), / ˈbruːm ˌsɛdʒ / (BrE)
a tall, tufted grass found in the southeastern United States, often growing in old fields and meadows
tall grassy plant of southeastern US

Examples

The fields behind the farmhouse were overtaken by broom sedge as the years passed without farming.
In the autumn, broom sedge turns a golden brown and stands out among other grasses.
Local wildlife often uses broom sedge for cover during the winter months.
