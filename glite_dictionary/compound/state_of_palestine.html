State of Palestine

1. PROPN

/ steɪt əv pəˈlæstiːn / (AmE), / steɪt əv pəˈlæstiːn / (BrE)
a political entity consisting of the West Bank and the Gaza Strip, whose sovereignty is claimed by Palestinians but is subject to Israeli occupation and control
political entity in the West Bank and Gaza Strip

Examples

The United Nations General Assembly granted the State of Palestine non-member observer status in 2012.
International aid organizations often operate in the State of Palestine to provide humanitarian assistance.
The political status of the State of Palestine remains a subject of international debate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Palestine - a geographic and historic region in Western Asia
  • Antonym: Israel - state in the Middle East bordering the State of Palestine
