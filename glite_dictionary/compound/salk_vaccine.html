Salk vaccine

1. NOUN

/ sɑlk vækˈsiːn / (AmE), / sɔːk vækˈsiːn / (BrE)
a vaccine for poliomyelitis consisting of inactivated poliovirus, developed by Jonas Salk and administered by injection
inactivated polio vaccine developed by Salk
medical

Examples

The widespread use of the Salk vaccine greatly reduced cases of polio worldwide.
Doctors began recommending the Salk vaccine in the 1950s to protect children from polio.
Thanks to the Salk vaccine, polio is now rare in most parts of the world.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Sabin vaccine - oral polio vaccine containing weakened virus

Etymology

Named after Jonas Salk, American physician and medical researcher who developed the vaccine in 1955.
