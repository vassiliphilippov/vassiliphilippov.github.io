Apennine Mountains

1. PROPN

/ ˈæpəˌnaɪn ˈmaʊntənz / (AmE), / ˈapɪnʌɪn ˈmaʊntənz / (BrE)
a mountain range extending along the length of the Italian peninsula
mountain range in Italy

Examples

The Apennine Mountains form a natural backbone running through the center of Italy.
Snow can be seen covering the Apennine Mountains during the winter months.
Researchers discovered several new plant species high in the Apennine Mountains.

Etymology

From Latin 'Appenninus', referring to the mountain range in Italy.
