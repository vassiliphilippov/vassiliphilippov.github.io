diastolic pressure

1. NOUN

/ daɪ.əˈstɑ.lɪk ˈprɛʃ.ɚ / (AmE), / daɪ.əˈstɒ.lɪk ˈprɛʃ.ə / (BrE)
the pressure in the arteries when the heart rests between beats, typically the lower number in a blood pressure reading
arterial pressure between heartbeats
medical

Examples

The doctor noted that her diastolic pressure was higher than normal.
A healthy diastolic pressure is usually below 80 mm Hg in adults.
High diastolic pressure can be a risk factor for heart disease.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: systolic pressure - the pressure in the arteries during the contraction of the heart muscles

Etymology

From 'diastolic', relating to diastole, from Greek 'diastolē' (dilation), and 'pressure'.
