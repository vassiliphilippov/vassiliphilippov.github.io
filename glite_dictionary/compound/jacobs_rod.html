Jacob's rod

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒeɪkəbz rɑd / (AmE), / ˈdʒeɪkəbz rɒd / (BrE)
A type of asphodel with an erect, smooth, unbranched stem, either flexuous or straight, especially the plant Asphodeline lutea.
plant with erect unbranched stem, especially asphodel

Examples

The slender yellow flowers of the Jacob's rod brightened the edge of the meadow.
Gardeners prize Jacob's rod for its tall, striking stems and resilience.
Botanists identified the unusual plant as a type of Jacob's rod growing wild in the area.
