parliamentary procedure

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑːrləˈmɛn.tə.ri prəˈsiː.dʒɚ / (AmE), / ˌpɑː.lɪˈmɛn.tər.i prəˈsiː.dʒə / (BrE)
A set of formal rules and guidelines used to conduct meetings and debates in deliberative assemblies.
rules for conducting meetings

Examples

The committee strictly followed parliamentary procedure during the debate.
Understanding parliamentary procedure is essential for chairing meetings effectively.
He raised a point of order according to parliamentary procedure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bylaw - a rule made by an organization or society to regulate itself

Etymology

From 'parliament', originating from Anglo-Norman and Old French *parlement* (speech, discussion), and 'procedure', from French *procédure* (manner of proceeding).
