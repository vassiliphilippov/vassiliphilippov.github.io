five-point bishop's cap

1. NOUN

/ faɪv pɔɪnt ˈbɪʃəps kæp / (AmE), / faɪv pɔɪnt ˈbɪʃəps kap / (BrE)
A small plant with leaves arranged in a basal cluster and tiny greenish flowers in slender racemes, found in northwestern North America, California, and Colorado.
small plant with basal leaves and greenish flowers

Examples

We discovered a five-point bishop's cap growing by the forest trail, its tiny green flowers just beginning to open.
The five-point bishop's cap thrives in moist, shaded woodland areas of California and the Pacific Northwest.
Botanists classify the five-point bishop's cap as part of the forest understory flora in Colorado.
