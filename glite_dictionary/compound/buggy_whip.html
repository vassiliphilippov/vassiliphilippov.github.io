buggy whip

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌɡi wɪp / (AmE), / ˈbʌɡi wɪp / (BrE)
a whip used for urging on a horse pulling a buggy, now regarded as a symbol of something outdated or obsolete
whip for driving a buggy; symbol of obsolescence

Examples

Because the automobile replaced the horse-drawn carriage, the buggy whip quickly became obsolete.
That company's product is the technological equivalent of a buggy whip in today's world.
Collectors sometimes seek out an old buggy whip as a piece of Americana.

Synset Examples

since buggies have been replaced by cars the buggy whip has become a symbol for anything that is hopelessly outmoded

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horsewhip - a whip for urging on horses
  • Degree Higher: outmoded - no longer fashionable or useful
