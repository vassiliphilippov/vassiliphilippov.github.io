stinking smut

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɪŋkɪŋ ˌsmʌt / (AmE), / ˈstɪŋkɪŋ ˌsmʌt / (BrE)
a disease of wheat in which the grains are replaced by greasy, foul-smelling masses of dark spores caused by certain fungi
wheat disease marked by foul-smelling spore masses

Examples

The farmer lost much of his crop to stinking smut last year.
Careful seed treatment can help prevent stinking smut in wheat fields.
When stinking smut is present, the entire harvest may be contaminated by its unpleasant odor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bunt - another name for wheat diseases caused by similar fungi producing spore masses

2. NOUN

/ ˈstɪŋkɪŋ smʌt / (AmE), / ˈstɪŋkɪŋ smʌt / (BrE)
a fungal disease of wheat and related cereals, caused by fungi of the genus Tilletia, characterized by dark, foul-smelling spore masses in the grain heads
fungal disease of wheat causing foul-smelling spores
medical

Examples

Farmers lost much of their wheat crop to stinking smut this year.
The characteristic odor of stinking smut makes infected grain easy to identify.
Researchers are developing new seeds resistant to stinking smut.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bunt - another common name for the same disease
