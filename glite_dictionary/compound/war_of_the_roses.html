War of the Roses

1. PROPN

/ ˌwɔr əv ðə ˈroʊzɪz / (AmE), / ˌwɔːr əv ðə ˈrəʊzɪz / (BrE)
A series of civil wars fought in England from 1455 to 1485 between the houses of Lancaster and York for control of the throne, ultimately leading to the rise of the Tudor dynasty.
series of English civil wars (1455–1485)

Examples

The War of the Roses changed the course of English history and brought the Tudor dynasty to power.
Many novels and films have depicted the brutality of the War of the Roses.
Historians still debate the causes and outcomes of the War of the Roses.

Etymology

Named for the emblems of the rival royal houses: the white rose of York and the red rose of Lancaster.
