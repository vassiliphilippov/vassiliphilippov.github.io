John Lennon eyeglasses

1. NOUN

/ dʒɑn ˈlɛnən ˈaɪˌɡlæsɪz / (AmE), / dʒɒn ˈlɛnən ˈaɪˌɡlɑːsɪz / (BrE)
Eyeglasses with thin, round frames, made famous by musician John Lennon.
round-framed eyeglasses popularized by John Lennon

Examples

He wore John Lennon eyeglasses with pride, paying tribute to the iconic Beatle.
The optician showed me a selection of John Lennon eyeglasses when I asked for round frames.
Vintage John Lennon eyeglasses are highly sought after by collectors of music memorabilia.

Synset Examples

Windsors are very chic this season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: windsor glasses - eyeglasses with round wire rims

Etymology

Named after John Lennon (1940–1980), English musician and member of The Beatles.
