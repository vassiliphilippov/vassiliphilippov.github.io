cider gum

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪdər ɡʌm / (AmE), / ˈsaɪdə ɡʌm / (BrE)
a small to medium-sized tree of Tasmania (Eucalyptus gunnii), valued for its sap and ornamental use
Tasmanian eucalyptus tree

Examples

The cider gum is known for its ability to tolerate cold climates in Tasmania.
Farmers sometimes tap the cider gum to collect its sweet sap, which can be fermented.
A beautiful cider gum stood in the center of the botanical garden, attracting many visitors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eucalyptus - a genus of fast-growing evergreen trees of Australia
