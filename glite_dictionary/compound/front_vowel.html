front vowel

1. NOUN

/ frʌnt ˈvaʊəl / (AmE), / frʌnt ˈvaʊəl / (BrE)
A vowel sound produced with the tongue positioned towards the front of the mouth, such as the vowel sounds in 'man' or 'gel'.
vowel sound made at front of mouth

Examples

The word 'man' contains a front vowel in its first syllable.
Linguists often classify vowels into front vowels and back vowels depending on tongue position.
Learning to distinguish front vowels from other vowel sounds can help with accurate pronunciation.

Synset Examples

Examples of front vowels include ‘a’ in ‘man’ and ‘e’ in ‘gel’.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: back vowel - a vowel sound produced at the back of the mouth
