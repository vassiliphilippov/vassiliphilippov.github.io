wage-earning

1. ADJ

/ ˈweɪdʒ ˌɜrnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈweɪdʒ ˌɜːnɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a job or person) receiving payment based on hourly wages rather than a fixed salary.
working for hourly wages

Examples

Many wage-earning families find it difficult to save for emergencies.
The company offers benefits to all its wage-earning employees.
He grew up in a wage-earning household where both parents worked shifts.

Synset Examples

working-class occupations include manual as well as industrial labor

Related Terms

  • Antonym: salaried - paid a fixed regular salary, not hourly wages

Etymology

From 'wage' (from Old French 'gage', meaning a pledge or payment) + 'earning'.
