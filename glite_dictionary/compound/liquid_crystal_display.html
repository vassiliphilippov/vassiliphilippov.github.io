liquid crystal display

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪkwɪd ˈkrɪstəl dɪˈspleɪ / (AmE), / ˈlɪkwɪd ˈkrɪstəl dɪsˈpleɪ / (BrE)
A flat electronic display technology that uses liquid crystals whose optical properties change with applied electric fields, commonly used in monitors, televisions, and portable devices.
flat electronic display technology

Examples

The new laptop features a high-resolution liquid crystal display for vibrant colors and sharp images.
Most modern digital watches use a liquid crystal display to show the time and date.
She prefers televisions with a liquid crystal display because they are thinner and use less power.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: LCD - abbreviation of 'liquid crystal display'

Etymology

Formed from 'liquid crystal' and 'display', referring to the use of liquid crystals in screen technology.
