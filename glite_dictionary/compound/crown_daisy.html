crown daisy

1. NOUN

/ kraʊn ˈdeɪzi / (AmE), / kraʊn ˈdeɪzi / (BrE)
a shrubby annual plant of the Mediterranean region, with yellowish-white flowers, often grown as an ornamental or used as a leafy vegetable
Mediterranean plant with yellowish-white flowers

Examples

The crown daisy is often used in Mediterranean cuisine as a leafy vegetable.
She planted crown daisy in her garden for its cheerful yellow blooms.
Farmers in southern Europe cultivate crown daisy both for its flowers and its edible leaves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chrysanthemum - a genus of flowering plants commonly called chrysanthemums
