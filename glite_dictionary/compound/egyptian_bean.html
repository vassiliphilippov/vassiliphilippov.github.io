Egyptian bean

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈdʒɪpʃən biːn / (AmE), / ɪˈdʒɪpʃən biːn / (BrE)
A perennial climbing vine of the Old World tropics with trifoliate leaves, purple pea-like flowers, and maroon pods containing edible seeds; cultivated as an ornamental and vegetable, especially in South Asia.
a tropical vine with edible beans

Examples

The Egyptian bean is prized in Indian cuisine for its tender pods and seeds.
Gardeners often grow the Egyptian bean for its attractive purple flowers and edible beans.
Many traditional dishes in South Asia incorporate the nutritious Egyptian bean.
