minute of arc

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪnɪt əv ɑrk / (AmE), / ˈmɪnɪt əv ɑːk / (BrE)
A unit of angular measurement equal to one sixtieth (1/60) of a degree.
unit of angular measurement (1/60 of a degree)

Examples

The star's position shifted by just one minute of arc in the night's sky.
A full moon is about thirty minutes of arc across when viewed from Earth.
Surveyors use a minute of arc to measure very small angle changes accurately.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: degree - a unit for measuring angles, equal to 1/360 of a full rotation
  • Degree Lower: second of arc - a unit of angular measurement equal to 1/60 of a minute

Etymology

From Latin 'pars minuta' (small part), used historically in geometry and astronomy.
