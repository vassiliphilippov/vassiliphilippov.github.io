swimming crab

1. NOUN

/ ˈswɪmɪŋ kræb / (AmE), / ˈswɪmɪŋ kræb / (BrE)
A marine crab having some of its legs flattened and fringed for swimming, commonly found in shallow coastal waters.
marine crab adapted for swimming

Examples

The swimming crab glided gracefully through the shallow water near the shore.
Fishermen often catch swimming crabs in coastal bays because of their abundance.
The distinctive paddle-shaped legs help the swimming crab escape from predators quickly.
