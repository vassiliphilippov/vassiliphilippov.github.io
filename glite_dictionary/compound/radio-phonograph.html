radio-phonograph

1. NOUN

/ ˌreɪdioʊˈfoʊnəˌɡræf / (AmE), / ˌreɪdiəʊˈfəʊnəɡrɑːf / (BrE)
an electronic device that combines a radio receiver and a record player in one unit
combination of radio and record player

Examples

My grandparents still have a classic radio-phonograph in their living room.
The antique shop displayed a beautifully restored radio-phonograph from the 1950s.
She loves collecting vintage electronics, and her favorite is a rare radio-phonograph.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stereo - a sound system with two or more speakers for left and right audio channels
  • Synonym: gramophone - an old-fashioned term for a record player

Etymology

From 'radio' (wireless receiver) + 'phonograph' (sound-recording/playback device), both from Greek roots.
