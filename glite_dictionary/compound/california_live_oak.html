California live oak

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæləˈfɔrniə laɪv oʊk / (AmE), / ˌkælɪˈfɔːniə laɪv əʊk / (BrE)
A highly variable, usually shrubby evergreen oak (Quercus agrifolia) of the coastal zone of western North America, known for its small, thick, often spiny-toothed dark-green leaves.
evergreen oak of coastal western North America

Examples

The dense canopy of the California live oak provides shelter for many woodland animals.
In the park, several old California live oaks shade the walking trails.
Botanists study how the tough leaves help the California live oak survive dry summers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coast live oak - alternative name for California live oak; same species
