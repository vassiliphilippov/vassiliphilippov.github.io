public trust

1. NOUN

/ ˈpʌblɪk trʌst / (AmE), / ˈpʌblɪk trʌst / (BrE)
A trust established for charitable, religious, educational, or scientific purposes, benefiting the public or a significant segment of it.
trust for charitable or public benefit
legal

Examples

The museum was established as a public trust to preserve local history for future generations.
A public trust must serve purposes such as education, science, or charity.
Many universities operate as a public trust, ensuring their assets benefit society.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: charitable trust - a trust designed for charitable purposes
