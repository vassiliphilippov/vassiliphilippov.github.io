runic letter

1. NOUN

/ ˈruːnɪk ˈlɛtər / (AmE), / ˈruːnɪk ˈlɛtə / (BrE)
a letter from an ancient Germanic alphabet used in Scandinavia and other regions from the 3rd century to the Middle Ages
character from ancient Germanic alphabet

Examples

The Viking artifact was inscribed with an ancient runic letter.
Archaeologists deciphered the runic letters on the old stone to reveal a name.
Each runic letter held both phonetic and symbolic meaning in ancient times.

Synset Examples

each rune had its own magical significance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rune - a letter in a runic alphabet
  • Degree Lower: letter - a symbol representing a sound or speech in an alphabet

Etymology

Runic: from Old Norse 'rún' (secret, mystery); letter: from Old French 'lettre', from Latin 'littera'.
