forward passer

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔrwərd ˈpæsər / (AmE), / ˈfɔːwəd ˈpɑːsə / (BrE)
(in American football) a player who attempts to advance the ball by throwing a forward pass.
player who throws a forward pass (football)
american

Examples

The forward passer looked for an open receiver downfield but decided to run instead.
A skilled forward passer can change the outcome of a football game with a single throw.
The team relied heavily on their forward passer to break through the opposing defense.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quarterback - the position most commonly responsible for forward passes in American football
