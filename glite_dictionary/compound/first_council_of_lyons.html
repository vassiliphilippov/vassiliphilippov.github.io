First Council of Lyons

1. PROPN

/ ˈfɝːst ˈkaʊnsəl əv ˈlaɪənz / (AmE), / ˈfɜːst ˈkaʊnsəl əv ˈliːɒ̃ / (BrE)
the ecumenical council held by the Western Church in 1245 that excommunicated Holy Roman Emperor Frederick II and launched plans for a new crusade
1245 Western Church council excommunicating Frederick II

Examples

The First Council of Lyons was convened by Pope Innocent IV in response to conflicts with Emperor Frederick II.
Historians often cite the First Council of Lyons as a turning point in papal-emperor relations.
At the First Council of Lyons, several major decisions about church governance were made.
