custard-apple family

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌstɚd ˌæpəl ˈfæməli / (AmE), / ˈkʌstəd ˌæpəl ˈfæmɪli / (BrE)
A family of chiefly tropical trees and shrubs, known scientifically as Annonaceae, including the custard apple and related species.
family of tropical trees and shrubs (Annonaceae)

Examples

The custard-apple family includes many species that are valued for their edible fruits.
Botanists classify the soursop tree as part of the custard-apple family.
Several members of the custard-apple family are widely cultivated in tropical regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Annonaceae - the scientific name for the custard-apple family
  • Degree Lower: custard apple - a tropical fruit from trees in the custard-apple family
