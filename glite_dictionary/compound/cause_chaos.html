to cause chaos

1. VERB

/ kɔz ˈkeɪ.ɑs / (AmE), / kɔːz ˈkeɪ.ɒs / (BrE)
to make a situation very disordered or confused; to bring about great disruption or confusion
bring about disorder or confusion

Examples

The sudden thunderstorm caused chaos on the roads during rush hour.
Unplanned changes to the schedule can cause chaos in the office.
Her announcement caused chaos at the meeting, as no one was prepared.

Synset Examples

In the story, the buggeress caused chaos wherever she went.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: create havoc - to cause widespread confusion or destruction
  • Antonym: cause order - to bring about organization or structure; the opposite of creating chaos
