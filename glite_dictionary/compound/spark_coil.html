spark coil

1. NOUN

/ spɑrk kɔɪl / (AmE), / spɑːk kɔɪl / (BrE)
An induction coil that generates high-voltage sparks, typically used in ignition systems.
induction coil for generating sparks

Examples

The mechanic replaced the spark coil to fix the car's ignition problem.
Old radios sometimes used a spark coil to create the necessary voltage for operation.
A faulty spark coil can prevent an engine from starting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: induction coil - an electrical device for producing high-voltage pulses from a low-voltage source
