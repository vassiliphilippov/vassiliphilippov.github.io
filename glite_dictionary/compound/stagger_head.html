stagger head

1. NOUN

/ ˈstæɡər hɛd / (AmE), / ˈstæɡə hɛd / (BrE)
A headline in which the top line is flush left, and each following line is indented farther to the right, creating a stepped appearance.
headline with each line indented more

Examples

The designer used a stagger head to make the article title stand out on the page.
Many newspapers prefer a stagger head for feature stories to add visual interest.
She formatted the heading as a stagger head, aligning each new line further to the right.
