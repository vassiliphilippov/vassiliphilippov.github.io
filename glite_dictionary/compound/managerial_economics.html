managerial economics

1. NOUN

/ ˌmænɪˈdʒɪriəl iːˌkɑːnɑːˈmɪks / (AmE), / ˌmænɪˈdʒɪəriəl iːkəˈnɒmɪks / (BrE)
the application of economic theory and methods to business management and decision-making
application of economics to business decisions

Examples

She studied managerial economics to understand how economic theory can improve business decisions.
The course in managerial economics helped him make better pricing strategies for his company.
Companies often use concepts from managerial economics when forecasting demand and setting production targets.

Synset Examples

In managerial economics, the relationship between how much customers must pay for an item and how much customers buy is called demand.
