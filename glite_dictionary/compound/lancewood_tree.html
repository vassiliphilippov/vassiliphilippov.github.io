lancewood tree

1. NOUN

/ ˈlænsˌwʊd tri / (AmE), / ˈlɑːnsˌwʊd triː / (BrE)
a tropical American tree valued for its hard, durable wood, often used for making tools and walking sticks
tropical American tree with hard wood

Examples

The lancewood tree is highly prized for timber used in the manufacture of fishing rods and walking sticks.
In some parts of Central America, the tough wood of the lancewood tree is used to make agricultural tools.
The botanist noted that a rare lancewood tree was growing near the riverbank.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlæns.wʊd ˌtri / (AmE), / ˈlɑːns.wʊd ˌtriː / (BrE)
a tree whose wood, called lancewood, is valued for its strength and elasticity, often used in tool handles and other implements
tree yielding strong, elastic lancewood

Examples

The lancewood tree is prized for its tough, flexible wood used in making walking sticks.
Many 19th-century carriage shafts were crafted from lancewood trees.
Botanists identified a new species of lancewood tree native to the region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lancewood - strong, elastic wood from certain trees

Etymology

From 'lance' (spear) and 'wood', describing wood suitable for making lances; originally referencing tropical trees in the Caribbean and New Zealand.
