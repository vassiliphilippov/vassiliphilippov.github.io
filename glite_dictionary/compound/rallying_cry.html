rallying cry

1. NOUN

/ ˈræliɪŋ kraɪ / (AmE), / ˈræliɪŋ kraɪ / (BrE)
A word or phrase used to encourage people to unite and support a cause or take action.
slogan to encourage support for a cause

Examples

The movement adopted the slogan 'Justice for All' as its rallying cry.
Her speech served as a powerful rallying cry for environmental activists worldwide.
Throughout history, freedom has often been used as a rallying cry during times of struggle.

Synset Examples

a cry to arms
our watchword will be ‘democracy’

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slogan - a short, memorable phrase used in advertising or by groups
  • Synonym: watchword - a word or phrase expressing a principle or aim

2. NOUN

/ ˈræliɪŋ kraɪ / (AmE), / ˈræliɪŋ kraɪ / (BrE)
a word or phrase shouted to encourage or inspire a group to unite and take action, especially in battle or during a movement
a call to encourage unity or action

Examples

The general’s rallying cry inspired his troops to charge the enemy.
Her passionate speech became the rallying cry for the entire protest movement.
Throughout history, a powerful rallying cry has often changed the course of events.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slogan - a distinctive phrase used to promote a cause or rally support
  • Synonym: battle cry - a shout or phrase used by soldiers before battle
  • Synonym: call to arms - an appeal to prepare for confrontation or action

Etymology

From 'rally' (to bring together) and 'cry' (shout), first used in military contexts.
