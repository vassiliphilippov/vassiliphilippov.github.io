Geum strictum

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːəm ˈstrɪktəm / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːəm ˈstrɪktəm / (BrE)
An erect perennial plant with deep yellow flowers, native to Europe, Asia, and North America, belonging to the genus Geum.
a species of perennial yellow-flowered plant

Examples

Geum strictum is often found in moist meadows across northern Europe and Asia.
Gardeners value Geum strictum for its bright, deep yellow blossoms in the spring.
The distinctive leaves of Geum strictum help botanists identify it among similar species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: avens - any plant of the genus Geum

Etymology

Genus name from Latin *geum*, from Greek *geuon* (a plant); species name from Latin *strictus* (drawn together, rigid).
