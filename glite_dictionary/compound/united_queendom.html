United Queendom

1. PROPN

/ juˈnaɪt̬ɪd ˈkwiːndəm / (AmE), / juˈnaɪtɪd ˈkwiːndəm / (BrE)
A humorous or creative term, often used to describe the United Kingdom in a playful, metaphorical, or fictional context, especially highlighting themes of unity or femininity.
the UK in a playful or creative sense

Examples

The queen addressed her subjects throughout the United Queendom in her annual speech.
Social media users often refer to the UK as the United Queendom when discussing women's leadership.
In her novel, the author set the story in a fictional United Queendom ruled by a council of queens.

Synset Examples

The United Queendom celebrated its national day with parades.
Tourists flock to the United Queendom for its landmarks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: United Kingdom - an island country in north-western Europe comprising England, Scotland, Wales, and Northern Ireland

Etymology

Playful alteration of 'United Kingdom,' substituting 'Queendom' for 'Kingdom.'
