mountain rimu

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaʊntən ˈriːmuː / (AmE), / ˈmaʊntɪn ˈriːmuː / (BrE)
a low-growing or prostrate shrub with slender trailing branches, native to New Zealand
low-growing shrub native to New Zealand

Examples

The mountain rimu flourishes in high-altitude areas of New Zealand's South Island.
Botanists studied the mountain rimu for its unique trailing branches and compact growth.
Our hiking path was bordered on both sides by dense patches of mountain rimu.

Etymology

From 'mountain' (English) + Maori 'rimu' (the name for several Dacrydium species in New Zealand).
