dextroamphetamine sulfate

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɛkstroʊ æmˈfɛtəˌmiːn ˈsʌlfeɪt / (AmE), / ˌdɛkstrəʊ æmˈfɛtəmiːn ˈsʌlfeɪt / (BrE)
A synthetic central nervous system stimulant, an isomer of amphetamine, used primarily to treat attention deficit hyperactivity disorder (ADHD) and narcolepsy.
central nervous system stimulant; ADHD medication
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed dextroamphetamine sulfate to help manage her ADHD symptoms.
Patients should take dextroamphetamine sulfate exactly as directed by their physician.
Some side effects of dextroamphetamine sulfate include insomnia and increased heart rate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amphetamine - a synthetic stimulant drug used to treat ADHD and narcolepsy

Etymology

From 'dextro-' (right, from Latin 'dexter') + 'amphetamine' + 'sulfate', referring to the right-handed isomeric form of amphetamine compounded with sulfuric acid.
