monotype keyboard

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑːnoʊtaɪp ˈkiːbɔːrd / (AmE), / ˈmɒnətaɪp ˈkiːbɔːd / (BrE)
A specialized keyboard used in typesetting to prepare punched paper tapes (ribbons) that control a Monotype machine for casting individual pieces of type.
typesetting keyboard for Monotype machines

Examples

The monotype keyboard was essential in early 20th-century newspaper production.
Operators used a monotype keyboard to create punched tape for casting letters.
Our museum displays a restored monotype keyboard beside the Monotype caster.

Synset Examples

A monotype operator enters text on a monotype keyboard, on which characters are arranged in the QWERTY arrangement of a conventional typewriter, but with this arrangement repeated multiple times.

Etymology

From 'Monotype', the name of the typesetting system, and 'keyboard'.
