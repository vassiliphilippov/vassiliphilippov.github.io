metrical unit

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛtrɪkəl ˈjuːnɪt / (AmE), / ˈmɛtrɪkəl ˈjuːnɪt / (BrE)
A group of two or three syllables forming the basic unit of rhythm in poetry.
basic unit of poetic rhythm

Examples

A dactyl is a type of metrical unit used in classical poetry.
Each verse is organized according to its metrical unit pattern.
The poem's rhythm depends on repeating the same metrical unit throughout.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foot - basic unit of rhythm in poetry, such as an iamb or trochee

Etymology

From Latin 'metricus' (metrical) and 'unitas' (unity, oneness).
