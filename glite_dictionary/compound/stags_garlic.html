stag's garlic

1. NOUN

/ stæɡz ˈɡɑrlɪk / (AmE), / stæɡz ˈɡɑːlɪk / (BrE)
a pungent wild onion (Allium ursinum) native to Europe and Asia, with broad leaves and small white flowers
wild onion native to Europe and Asia

Examples

The woods were covered in stag's garlic, releasing a strong, onion-like scent after the rain.
Many foragers collect stag's garlic for its edible leaves and mild garlicky flavor.
In spring, you may spot the delicate white blossoms of stag's garlic carpeting the forest floor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ramsons - another name for Allium ursinum, a wild garlic plant
