suborder Reptantia

1. NOUN

/ sʌbˌɔrdɚ rɛpˈtænʃə / (AmE), / sʌbˌɔːdə rɛpˈtantiə / (BrE)
a taxonomic suborder of crustaceans that includes lobsters and crabs, characterized by their walking legs and crawling movement.
taxonomic suborder including lobsters and crabs

Examples

The suborder Reptantia contains many commercially valuable crustaceans such as lobsters and crabs.
Scientists study the evolutionary adaptations within the suborder Reptantia to understand the success of these crustaceans.
Members of the suborder Reptantia are distinguished by their ability to crawl along the sea floor.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Decapoda - the order of crustaceans including shrimps, crabs, and lobsters

Etymology

From Latin 'reptare' (to crawl), referring to the crawling movement characteristic of its members.
