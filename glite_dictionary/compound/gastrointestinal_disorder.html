gastrointestinal disorder

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡæstroʊˌɪn.tɛs.tʃəˈnæl dɪsˈɔːrdɚ / (AmE), / ˌɡæstrəʊˌɪn.tɛs.tɪˈnæl dɪsˈɔːdə / (BrE)
an illness affecting the stomach and intestines, often caused by infection or ingestion of contaminated food
illness of stomach and intestines
medical

Examples

Recent outbreaks have drawn attention to the dangers of gastrointestinal disorder caused by contaminated water.
She was hospitalized with a severe gastrointestinal disorder after eating spoiled shellfish.
Doctors are studying the link between stress and gastrointestinal disorder in patients.

Etymology

From 'gastro-' (relating to the stomach) and 'intestinal', both from Latin roots, plus 'disorder'.
