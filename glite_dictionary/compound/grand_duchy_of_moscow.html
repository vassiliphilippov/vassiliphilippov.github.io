Grand Duchy of Moscow

1. PROPN

/ ɡrænd ˈdʌtʃi əv ˈmɒskoʊ / (AmE), / ɡrænd ˈdʌtʃi əv ˈmɒskəʊ / (BrE)
a medieval Russian principality that existed from the 13th to the 16th centuries with Moscow as its capital, which played a central role in the formation of the Russian state.
medieval Russian principality centered in Moscow

Examples

The Grand Duchy of Moscow expanded its territory and influence during the reign of Ivan III.
Historians regard the Grand Duchy of Moscow as the foundation of the modern Russian state.
Many key battles against the Mongols were fought by the armies of the Grand Duchy of Moscow.
