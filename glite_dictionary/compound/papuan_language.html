Papuan language

1. NOUN

/ ˈpæpjuən ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈpæpjʊən ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
any of the indigenous languages spoken in Papua New Guinea, New Britain, or the Solomon Islands that are not classified as Malayo-Polynesian languages
indigenous language of Papua New Guinea region (non-Malayo-Polynesian)

Examples

Many anthropologists have studied the structure of the Papuan language spoken in the highlands.
She is fluent in both English and a local Papuan language used by her community.
There are hundreds of distinct Papuan languages found throughout Papua New Guinea.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Malayo-Polynesian language - languages of the Malayo-Polynesian family spoken in Southeast Asia and the Pacific

Etymology

From Papua + -an, referring to Papua New Guinea and surrounding regions.
