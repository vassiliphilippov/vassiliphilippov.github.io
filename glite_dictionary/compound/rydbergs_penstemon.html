Rydberg's penstemon

1. NOUN

/ ˈraɪdˌbɜrɡz ˈpɛnstəmɒn / (AmE), / ˈrɪdˌbɜːɡz ˈpɛnstɪmən / (BrE)
a species of perennial plant native to western North America, known for its whorls of small dark blue-violet flowers
species of flowering plant with blue-violet flowers

Examples

We admired the clusters of Rydberg's penstemon blooming along the hiking trail in Colorado.
Gardeners often plant Rydberg's penstemon to attract pollinators with its vibrant blue-violet flowers.
The rocky slopes were dotted with patches of Rydberg's penstemon during the summer months.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Penstemon - genus of flowering plants commonly called beardtongues

Etymology

Named after the Swedish-born American botanist Per Axel Rydberg and from 'penstemon', a genus of flowering plants.
