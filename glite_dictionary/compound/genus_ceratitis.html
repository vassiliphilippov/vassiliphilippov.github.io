genus Ceratitis

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs sɛrəˈtaɪtɪs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs sɛrəˈtaɪtɪs / (BrE)
A genus of fruit flies in the family Tephritidae, including the Mediterranean fruit fly and related species.
genus of fruit flies (including Mediterranean fruit fly)

Examples

The genus Ceratitis includes several important agricultural pests.
Scientists studied the life cycle of insects in the genus Ceratitis.
Some species within the genus Ceratitis have spread worldwide due to human activity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Medfly - common name for Mediterranean fruit fly; species in genus Ceratitis

Etymology

From Latin 'genus' (kind, class) and New Latin 'Ceratitis' (type genus name from Greek 'keras', meaning 'horn').
