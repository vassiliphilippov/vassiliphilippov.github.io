Christmas tree

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɪs.məs ˌtri / (AmE), / ˈkrɪs.məs ˌtriː / (BrE)
A terrestrial evergreen shrub or small tree native to western Australia, known for its brilliant yellow-orange flowers and parasitic growth on the roots of grasses.
Australian shrub or tree with brilliant orange flowers

Examples

The Australian Christmas tree produces striking yellow-orange blossoms that brighten the landscape in December.
Unlike most trees, the Christmas tree is parasitic and draws nutrients from the roots of nearby grasses.
Botanists study the Christmas tree for its unique adaptation to arid environments.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkrɪs·məs triː / (AmE), / ˈkrɪs·məs triː / (BrE)
An Australian tree or shrub, especially Nuytsia floribunda, with red or orange flowers, often used in Christmas decoration.
Australian plant with red flowers, used at Christmas

Examples

The brilliant blooms of the Christmas tree can be seen across Western Australia in December.
Local florists sell branches of the Christmas tree for festive arrangements during the holiday season.
The Christmas tree is prized both for its vibrant flowers and its role in traditional celebrations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Nuytsia floribunda - a species of Australian tree commonly called the Western Australian Christmas tree

3. NOUN

/ ˈkrɪsməs triː / (AmE), / ˈkrɪsməs triː / (BrE)
An evergreen tree, often decorated with lights and ornaments, placed in homes or public places as part of Christmas celebrations.
decorated evergreen for Christmas

Examples

We spent the afternoon decorating the Christmas tree with lights and ornaments.
Every year, the town displays a huge Christmas tree in the main square.
Presents were neatly arranged under the Christmas tree on Christmas morning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Christmas - the annual Christian festival celebrating the birth of Jesus Christ

Etymology

From 'Christmas', the Christian festival, and 'tree', Old English 'trēow'.
