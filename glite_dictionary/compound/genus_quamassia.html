genus Quamassia

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs kwəˈmæsiə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs kwəˈmasɪə / (BrE)
A genus of scapose herbs native to North and South America, characterized by large edible bulbs.
genus of herbs with edible bulbs

Examples

Scientists are studying the edible bulbs of the genus Quamassia for new agricultural uses.
Native peoples traditionally harvested plants from the genus Quamassia as a food source.
Botanists classify several similar North American wildflowers within the genus Quamassia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: camassia - a related genus of flowering plants with edible bulbs

Etymology

From modern Latin, with 'genus' meaning 'kind' and Quamassia as the Latinized form of a native plant name.
