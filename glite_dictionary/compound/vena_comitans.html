vena comitans

1. NOUN

/ ˈveɪ.nə kəˈmaɪ.tænz / (AmE), / ˈviː.nə kəˈmaɪ.tænz / (BrE)
an anatomical vein that accompanies an artery, often alongside its entire length, so that the artery's pulsations help return blood through the vein
vein accompanying an artery
medical

Examples

The surgeon carefully identified the vena comitans that ran alongside the artery during the operation.
A typical example of a vena comitans is found in the forearm, where two veins accompany the radial artery.
Damage to a vena comitans may disrupt normal venous return in the associated limb.

Etymology

From Latin, meaning 'accompanying vein': 'vēna' (vein) + 'comitans' (accompanying).
