tree lizard

1. NOUN

/ ˈtri ˌlɪzɚd / (AmE), / ˈtriː ˌlɪzəd / (BrE)
a small, agile lizard of the western United States and northern Mexico that climbs trees; especially the species Urosaurus ornatus
a climbing lizard of western US and northern Mexico

Examples

The tree lizard is commonly seen basking on rocks and branches in the desert.
Biologists observed a tree lizard blending seamlessly into the bark of a mesquite tree.
My brother caught a tree lizard in our backyard and released it near the woods.
