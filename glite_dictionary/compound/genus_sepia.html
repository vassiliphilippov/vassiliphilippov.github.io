genus Sepia

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˈsiːpiə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈsiːpɪə / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Sepiidae, comprising the cuttlefish.
type genus of cuttlefish

Examples

The genus Sepia includes many well-known species of cuttlefish found in shallow waters.
Marine biologists study the genus Sepia to learn more about cephalopod intelligence.
Within the genus Sepia, coloration is used for both camouflage and communication.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cuttlefish - a marine mollusk with a broad body and tentacles, member of the family Sepiidae

Etymology

From Latin 'Sepia', originally from Greek 'sēpía' (cuttlefish).
