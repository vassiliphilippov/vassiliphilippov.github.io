modern font

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑːdɚn fɑnt / (AmE), / ˈmɒdən fɒnt / (BrE)
A typeface style characterized by regular shapes, thin (hairline) serifs, and heavy downstrokes, based on late 18th-century designs such as those by Bodoni.
typeface with thin serifs and heavy downstrokes

Examples

This book was printed using a modern font to achieve a clean, elegant look.
Designers often choose a modern font for high-end fashion magazines to convey sophistication.
The logo uses a bold modern font to stand out on the product packaging.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: serif - a slight projection finishing off a stroke of a letter in certain typefaces
