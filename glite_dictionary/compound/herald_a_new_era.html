to herald a new era

1. VERB

/ ˈhɛrəld ə nu ˈɛrə / (AmE), / ˈhɛrəld ə njuː ˈɪərə / (BrE)
to announce or signal the beginning of a period of major change or development
announce major change or new period

Examples

The invention of the smartphone heralded a new era in communication.
Many hope that renewable energy will herald a new era of environmental responsibility.
The peace treaty heralded a new era of cooperation between the two countries.

Synset Examples

They herald a new era of technology with this launch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to usher in - to mark or signal the beginning of something new
  • Synonym: to bring about - to cause something to happen

Etymology

From 'herald', meaning 'to announce', and 'era', from Latin 'aera' (counters, era).
