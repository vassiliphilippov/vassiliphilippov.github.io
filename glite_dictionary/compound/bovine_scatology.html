bovine scatology

1. NOUN

/ ˈboʊvaɪn skəˈtɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ˈbəʊvaɪn skəˈtɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the scientific study of cow dung or excrement, especially to understand diets, digestion, and environmental impact
study of cow dung (scientific)

Examples

The scientist specialized in bovine scatology to evaluate the nutritional health of cattle herds.
Recent advances in bovine scatology have provided insight into reducing agricultural pollution.
She presented her research on bovine scatology at the environmental science conference.

Synset Examples

Bovine scatology can reveal a lot about animal diets.
Understanding bovine scatology helps with pasture management.
The biologist studied bovine scatology for environmental impact.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scatology - the scientific study of excrement

Etymology

Bovine from Latin 'bovinus' (ox or cow); scatology from Greek 'skōr' (dung) and '-logia' (study of).
