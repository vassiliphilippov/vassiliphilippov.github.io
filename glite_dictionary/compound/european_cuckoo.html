European cuckoo

1. NOUN

/ jʊˈroʊpiən ˈkʊku / (AmE), / jʊəˈrəʊpiən ˈkʊkuː / (BrE)
a common bird of Europe (Cuculus canorus) known for its distinctive two-note call and habit of laying its eggs in the nests of other birds
a European bird with a two-note call; lays eggs in other birds' nests

Examples

The unmistakable call of the European cuckoo can often be heard in spring across the countryside.
The European cuckoo is notorious for laying its eggs in the nests of other bird species.
Many folk songs reference the arrival of the European cuckoo as a sign of changing seasons.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cuckoo - a common bird noted for its repetitive call and brood parasitism
