Monterey cypress

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑn.təˌreɪ ˈsaɪ.prəs / (AmE), / ˈmɒn.təˌreɪ ˈsaɪ.prəs / (BrE)
a tall evergreen tree (Cupressus macrocarpa) native to the Monterey Bay area of California, often planted for ornament and windbreaks
evergreen tree native to Monterey Bay, California

Examples

The Monterey cypress is often used as a windbreak along coastal highways.
Gardeners planted rows of Monterey cypress to protect the property from strong ocean winds.
A century-old Monterey cypress towers above the cliffs at Point Lobos.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cypress - any tree of the genus Cupressus, evergreen conifers with scale-like leaves

Etymology

Named after Monterey Bay, California, where the species is native.
