Mount Fuji

1. PROPN

/ maʊnt ˈfuːdʒi / (AmE), / maʊnt ˈfuːdʒi / (BrE)
a dormant volcanic mountain in south central Honshu, Japan, known as the country's highest peak and a site of cultural and religious significance
Japan's highest mountain; sacred volcano

Examples

Many tourists climb Mount Fuji each year to watch the sunrise from the summit.
The snow-capped peak of Mount Fuji is often depicted in traditional Japanese art.
On clear days, Mount Fuji can be seen from Tokyo, over 100 kilometers away.

Etymology

From Japanese Fuji-san (富士山), with 'san' meaning mountain.
