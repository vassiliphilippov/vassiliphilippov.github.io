electric arc

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈlɛktrɪk ɑrk / (AmE), / ɪˈlɛktrɪk ɑːk / (BrE)
a luminous discharge of electricity across a gap in a circuit or between two electrodes in a gas, producing light and heat
discharge of electricity producing light in air or gas

Examples

The welder created an electric arc to join the metal pieces together.
An electric arc can reach temperatures hot enough to melt most metals.
Lightning is a natural example of an electric arc occurring in the atmosphere.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arc - a luminous electrical discharge between two electrodes
