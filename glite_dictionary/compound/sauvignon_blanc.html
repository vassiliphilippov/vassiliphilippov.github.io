Sauvignon blanc

1. NOUN

/ soʊˌviːˌnjoʊn ˈblɑːŋk / (AmE), / ˌsɔː.viˈnjɒ̃ ˈblɑ̃ / (BrE)
A white wine made from a green-skinned grape variety originally from the Bordeaux region of France, now widely grown in California and other regions.
type of white wine grape or wine

Examples

The crisp acidity of Sauvignon blanc pairs perfectly with seafood dishes.
She prefers a glass of Sauvignon blanc to accompany her salad.
California produces many excellent Sauvignon blanc wines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Chardonnay - a variety of white wine grape
  • Antonym: red wine - wine made from dark-colored grape varieties

Etymology

French, from the grape variety name, literally 'wild white'.
