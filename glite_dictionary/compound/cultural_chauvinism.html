cultural chauvinism

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌltʃərəl ʃoʊˈvɪnɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈkʌltʃərəl ˈʃəʊvɪnɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
the belief that one's own culture is superior to others
belief in cultural superiority

Examples

The documentary exposes the dangers of cultural chauvinism in a globalized world.
Critics argued that strict language policies were rooted in cultural chauvinism.
She accused the committee of cultural chauvinism when they rejected foreign customs.

Synset Examples

The article criticized cultural chauvinism in modern society.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chauvinism - excessive or prejudiced loyalty or support for one's own group

Etymology

From 'chauvinism', after Nicholas Chauvin, a legendary French soldier notorious for excessive patriotism, and 'culture'.
