British pound sterling

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrɪtɪʃ paʊnd ˈstɜrlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbrɪtɪʃ paʊnd ˈstɜːlɪŋ / (BrE)
the official currency unit of the United Kingdom and Northern Ireland, equal to 100 pence.
currency of the UK; equal to 100 pence

Examples

The British pound sterling rose against the dollar after the latest economic report was released.
When traveling in London, you'll need to exchange your currency for British pound sterling.
The contract specifies that all payments must be made in British pound sterling rather than euros.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pound - the basic unit of currency in the UK
  • Antonym: euro - the official currency of the Eurozone
  • Degree Lower: penny - a unit of currency; one hundredth of a pound

Etymology

From Old English 'pund' (weight, sum of money) and 'sterling', likely from Middle English 'sterling' (meaning little star or coin).
