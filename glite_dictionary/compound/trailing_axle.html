trailing axle

1. NOUN

/ ˈtreɪlɪŋ ˈæksəl / (AmE), / ˈtreɪlɪŋ ˈæksəl / (BrE)
an axle on a steam locomotive that connects a pair of unpowered wheels situated behind the driving wheels
axle for unpowered rear wheels on a locomotive

Examples

The new engine design featured a trailing axle for better balance and stability.
After inspection, the engineer found a problem with the trailing axle of the locomotive.
Steam locomotives with a trailing axle can support a larger firebox for more efficient operation.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: leading axle - axle connecting wheels in front of the driving wheels on a locomotive

Etymology

From 'trail' (to follow) + 'axle' (shaft on which wheels rotate).
