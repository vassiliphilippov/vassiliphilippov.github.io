2. NOUN

/ ˈfiːld ˌdʒɛnərəl / (AmE), / ˈfiːld ˌdʒɛnərəl / (BrE)
(in American football) a quarterback; a player who directs or leads the team's play on the field, often used figuratively for someone with tactical leadership.
quarterback or tactical leader in sports
american

Examples

The coach praised his field general for keeping the team focused during the final quarter.
Since joining the league, he's developed into an exceptional field general with impressive play-calling skills.
Every successful team needs a smart field general to lead them on the field.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quarterback - the player in football who directs the team's offensive play

Etymology

Formed from 'field' and 'general', likening a player's leadership on the field to that of a military general.
