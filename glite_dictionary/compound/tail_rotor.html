tail rotor

1. NOUN

/ teɪl ˈroʊtər / (AmE), / teɪl ˈrəʊtə / (BrE)
a rotating airfoil mounted on the tail of a single-rotor helicopter, used to counteract the torque produced by the main rotor and keep the helicopter stable
rotor at the tail of a helicopter for stability

Examples

The tail rotor is essential for maintaining directional control in a single-rotor helicopter.
During the pre-flight inspection, the pilot checked the tail rotor for any signs of damage.
A malfunction in the tail rotor can cause a helicopter to spin uncontrollably.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: main rotor - the principal rotor providing lift and thrust to a helicopter
