genus Ensete

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ɛnˈsiːti / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ɛnˈsiːti / (BrE)
A genus of large, herbaceous plants native to Africa and Asia, known commonly as Abyssinian bananas, related to edible bananas but usually grown as ornamentals or for their starchy corms.
a genus of plants known as Abyssinian bananas

Examples

The genus Ensete includes the plant known as the Abyssinian banana.
Members of the genus Ensete are grown for their striking foliage in botanical gardens.
Unlike common bananas, plants in the genus Ensete are mainly cultivated for ornamental purposes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: banana - edible fruit from the genus Musa, closely related to Ensete

Etymology

From Modern Latin 'Ensete', the genus name coined by botanists.
