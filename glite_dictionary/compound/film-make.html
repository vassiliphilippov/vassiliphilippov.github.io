to film-make

1. VERB

/ ˈfɪlmˌmeɪk / (AmE), / ˈfɪlmˌmeɪk / (BrE)
to make films; to direct, produce, or participate in the creation of movies
to make or direct films

Examples

She has decided to film-make her own documentary about wildlife.
Many university graduates aspire to film-make in Hollywood or independent cinema.
He started to film-make after attending a week-long workshop in directing.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: film - a movie; a series of moving pictures
  • Degree Higher: film director - a person who directs films
