Irula language

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪruːlə ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈɪrʊlə ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
A Dravidian language closely related to Tamil, spoken in a hilly region of southwestern India.
a Dravidian language of southwestern India

Examples

The Irula language is primarily spoken by the Irula people in the Nilgiri hills.
Linguists are studying how the Irula language has preserved ancient Dravidian elements.
She learned basic words and phrases in the Irula language to communicate with local villagers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Tamil - a major Dravidian language of South India and Sri Lanka

Etymology

Irula from the name of the Irula people, native to southwestern India.
