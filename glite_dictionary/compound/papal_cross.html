papal cross

1. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪpəl krɔs / (AmE), / ˈpeɪpəl krɒs / (BrE)
A cross with three horizontal crossbars, the top two shorter than the lowest, used as a symbol of the Pope.
a cross with three crossbars, papal symbol

Examples

The Pope is often depicted holding a papal cross during religious ceremonies.
The papal cross is distinguishable by its three horizontal bars.
A papal cross is sometimes used in ecclesiastical heraldry.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: patriarchal cross - a cross with two horizontal crossbars, associated with archbishops or patriarchs
  • Degree Lower: cross - a symbol consisting of an upright and a transverse piece crossing it, especially one representing the Christian cross

Etymology

From papal, relating to the Pope, and cross, from Old English 'cros' (cross).
