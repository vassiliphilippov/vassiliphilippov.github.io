electrical resistance

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈlɛktrɪkəl rɪˈzɪstəns / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛktrɪkəl rɪˈzɪstəns / (BrE)
The opposition that a material offers to the flow of electric current, expressed in ohms.
opposition to electric current (in ohms)

Examples

Copper has a low electrical resistance, making it ideal for wiring.
If a wire's electrical resistance increases, less current will flow through it.
Scientists measure electrical resistance to understand how well a material conducts electricity.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: conductance - ease with which electric current passes through a material
