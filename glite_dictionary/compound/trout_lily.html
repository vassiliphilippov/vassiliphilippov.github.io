trout lily

1. NOUN

/ traʊt ˈlɪli / (AmE), / traʊt ˈlɪli / (BrE)
A perennial North American wildflower with nodding yellow flowers and mottled leaves, commonly found in woodlands.
North American wildflower with yellow blooms

Examples

Every spring, the woodland floor is carpeted with blooming trout lilies.
The leaves of the trout lily are speckled, resembling the skin of a trout.
We spotted a rare trout lily while hiking through the forest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dogtooth violet - A name for various species in the genus Erythronium, related to trout lily

Etymology

Named for the mottled leaves resembling the markings of a trout.
