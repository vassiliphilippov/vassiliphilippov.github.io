letter stock

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛtər stɑk / (AmE), / ˈlɛtə stɒk / (BrE)
Shares that have not been registered with the Securities and Exchange Commission and cannot be sold to the general public.
unregistered shares not for public sale

Examples

The company issued letter stock to its executives as part of their compensation package.
Investors need to be cautious when dealing with letter stock because it cannot be freely traded.
After receiving letter stock, the recipient must keep it for a minimum holding period before selling.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: restricted stock - stock with sale restrictions, usually for regulatory reasons
