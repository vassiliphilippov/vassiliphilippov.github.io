coastal assault

1. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊstəl əˈsɔlt / (AmE), / ˈkəʊstəl əˈsɔːlt / (BrE)
a military attack carried out near or onto the coast, often involving amphibious forces
military attack near the coast

Examples

The navy prepared extensively for a coastal assault on enemy territory.
During World War II, several coastal assaults proved crucial to the outcome of the conflict.
The general explained the strategy behind launching a coastal assault at dawn.

Synset Examples

The army deployed amphibious troops for the coastal assault.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amphibious assault - an attack involving combined land and sea forces
