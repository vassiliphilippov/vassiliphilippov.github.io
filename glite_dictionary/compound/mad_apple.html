mad apple

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæd ˌæpəl / (AmE), / ˈmad ˌapəl / (BrE)
an old name for the eggplant, a hairy, upright plant native to southeastern Asia, cultivated for its large, glossy, edible fruit used as a vegetable
old name for eggplant (Solanum melongena)
archaic

Examples

In 18th-century texts, the mad apple is frequently mentioned as a curious imported plant.
Early botanists mistrusted the mad apple, believing it to be unhealthy to eat.
Today, recipes sometimes refer to eggplant as mad apple in historical context.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eggplant - an edible purple fruit often used as a vegetable
  • Synonym: aubergine - British term for eggplant

Etymology

From a mistranslation of Italian 'mela insana' (from Latin 'mala insana'), historically referring to the eggplant.

2. NOUN

/ mæd ˈæpəl / (AmE), / mad ˈapəl / (BrE)
an alternative name for the eggplant, a vegetable with an egg-shaped, typically dark purple, shiny skin, sometimes white or yellow
another name for eggplant (aubergine)

Examples

The farmer showed us a basket of freshly picked mad apples from his garden.
In old cookbooks, recipes often call for mad apple instead of eggplant.
Some heirloom varieties of mad apples are yellow or white rather than purple.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eggplant - a vegetable with purple skin, also called aubergine
  • Synonym: aubergine - British term for eggplant, a purple-skinned vegetable

Etymology

From Italian 'mela insana', meaning 'mad apple', a folk-etymological translation possibly through French influence.
