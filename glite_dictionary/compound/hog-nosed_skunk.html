hog-nosed skunk

1. NOUN

/ hɑɡ noʊzd skʌŋk / (AmE), / hɒɡ nəʊzd skʌŋk / (BrE)
a large skunk with a distinctive bare, pig-like snout and a white back and tail, native to southwestern North America and Mexico
a skunk with a bare snout and white back

Examples

The hog-nosed skunk used its snout to root around for insects near the desert brush.
Researchers tracked the movements of a family of hog-nosed skunks across the Mexican plains.
I spotted a hog-nosed skunk with a bushy white tail in the twilight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skunk - a small, black-and-white mammal known for its strong-smelling spray
