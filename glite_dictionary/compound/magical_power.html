magical power

1. NOUN

/ ˈmædʒɪkəl ˈpaʊər / (AmE), / ˈmadʒɪkəl ˈpaʊə / (BrE)
the ability or capacity to perform magic, typically by supernatural means or through special abilities
ability to perform magic

Examples

The wizard used his magical power to conjure a fiery dragon in the courtyard.
Legends tell of a sword imbued with magical power, capable of defeating any enemy.
She discovered her magical power only when she accidentally turned the lamp into a bird.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: supernatural power - an ability to do things not explainable by natural laws
  • Antonym: mundane ability - an ordinary, non-magical capacity or skill

2. NOUN

/ ˈmædʒɪkəl ˈpaʊər / (AmE), / ˈmadʒɪkəl ˈpaʊə / (BrE)
supernatural or extraordinary ability to influence events or perform feats beyond natural laws
supernatural or extraordinary ability

Examples

The hero discovered that he possessed a magical power that could heal injuries instantly.
Legends often tell of ancient artifacts imbued with incredible magical power.
The villain used her magical powers to control the minds of her enemies.

Synset Examples

The story featured an old woman with magical powers.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: power - the ability or capacity to do something or act in a particular way
  • Degree Higher: superpower - an exceptionally great power or ability, often supernatural
