Chronoperates paradoxus

1. NOUN

/ ˌkroʊnoʊˈpɛrətiːz pəˈrædɒksəs / (AmE), / ˌkrɒnəʊˈpɛrətiːz pəˈrædɒksəs / (BrE)
a shrew-sized extinct protomammal from Alberta, Canada, living about 55 million years ago, unusually recent for its group
extinct shrew-sized protomammal species

Examples

The discovery of Chronoperates paradoxus challenged scientists' ideas about the timeline of early mammal-like reptiles.
Paleontologists were surprised to find Chronoperates paradoxus fossils dating to just 55 million years ago.
The Alberta site yielded the first known specimens of Chronoperates paradoxus.

Etymology

From Greek 'chronos' (time) + 'operates' (worker/agent), 'paradoxus' from Greek 'paradoxos' (contrary to expectation).
