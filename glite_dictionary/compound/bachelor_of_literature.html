Bachelor of Literature

1. NOUN

/ ˈbætʃələr əv ˈlɪtərətʃər / (AmE), / ˈbæʧələ əv ˈlɪtərətʃə / (BrE)
A bachelor's degree awarded for an undergraduate program in literature.
undergraduate degree in literature

Examples

After four years of study, she earned her Bachelor of Literature from the university.
Job postings for editors often require at least a Bachelor of Literature or related qualification.
He proudly displayed his Bachelor of Literature diploma in the living room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Bachelor of Arts - a bachelor's degree in the arts or humanities
  • Degree Higher: Master of Literature - a master's degree in literature

Etymology

From Latin 'baccalaureus' (bachelor) and Latin 'litteratura' (writing, learning).
