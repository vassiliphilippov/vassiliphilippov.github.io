coat of mail

1. NOUN

/ koʊt əv meɪl / (AmE), / kəʊt əv meɪl / (BrE)
A protective garment made of interlinked metal rings or overlapping metal plates, designed to cover and protect the wearer's body in battle.
protective armor made of metal rings or plates
archaic

Examples

The knight donned his coat of mail before heading into battle.
Collectors value an authentic coat of mail for its craftsmanship and historical significance.
Underneath his coat of mail, he wore padded clothing for extra comfort.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chain mail - armor made of small metal rings linked together in a pattern

Etymology

From Middle English, with 'mail' from Old French 'maille' meaning 'mesh' or 'net'.
