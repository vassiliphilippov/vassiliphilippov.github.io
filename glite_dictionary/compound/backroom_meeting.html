backroom meeting

1. NOUN

/ ˌbæk.ruːm ˈmiː.tɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌbæk.ruːm ˈmiː.tɪŋ / (BrE)
a private or secretive discussion, often held to decide sensitive matters without public scrutiny
private or secretive discussion

Examples

The executives held a backroom meeting to finalize the company's restructuring plans.
Allegations surfaced about a backroom meeting that influenced the policy decision.
Reporters speculated about what was discussed during the late-night backroom meeting.

Synset Examples

They made the deal in a backroom meeting away from cameras.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: closed-door meeting - a meeting not open to the public or press
  • Antonym: public meeting - an open meeting accessible to anyone interested

Etymology

Formed from 'backroom' (a room at the back, often where private or secret discussions happen) and 'meeting'.
