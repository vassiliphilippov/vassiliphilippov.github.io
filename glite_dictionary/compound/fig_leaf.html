fig leaf

1. NOUN

/ fɪɡ liːf / (AmE), / fɪɡ liːf / (BrE)
a leaf from a fig tree, typically large and lobed
leaf from a fig tree

Examples

He picked a fig leaf from the tree and examined its shape.
In ancient art, a fig leaf was sometimes used to cover nudity in sculptures.
The fig leaves in the garden were broad and provided ample shade.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fig tree - a tree that produces figs

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɪɡ ˌliːf / (AmE), / ˈfɪɡ ˌliːf / (BrE)
Something used to conceal an embarrassing or shameful fact or action.
cover used to hide something shameful

Examples

The corporation's environmental report was just a fig leaf to hide its real agenda.
He apologized, but everyone knew it was only a fig leaf for his lack of action.
The reforms were seen as a fig leaf, offering little real change.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cover - something that conceals or protects

Etymology

From the biblical reference in Genesis, where Adam and Eve used fig leaves to cover themselves.

3. NOUN

/ ˈfɪɡ ˌliːf / (AmE), / ˈfɪɡ ˌliːf / (BrE)
something used to conceal or disguise an embarrassing or dishonorable fact or situation; a pretense or cover-up.
a pretense to hide something

Examples

The company's public apology was little more than a fig leaf to cover its ongoing misconduct.
His charitable donation acted as a fig leaf, distracting attention from his controversial business practices.
Many saw the sudden resignation as a mere fig leaf to deflect criticism from the government.

Synset Examples

He used a fig leaf to cover his embarrassment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cover - something that serves as a concealment or disguise
  • Synonym: pretense - a false or insincere claim or excuse

Etymology

From the Biblical story of Adam and Eve, who used fig leaves to cover themselves after becoming aware of their nakedness.
