local potentate

1. NOUN

/ ˈloʊkəl poʊˈtɛnˌteɪt / (AmE), / ˈləʊkəl pəˈtɛnˌteɪt / (BrE)
an important or powerful person in a particular local area, especially one with authority or influence over others
powerful person in a locality

Examples

The local potentate demanded heavy taxes from everyone living in the region.
In rural areas, the local potentate often controlled both commerce and justice.
Visiting dignitaries were greeted by the local potentate at the city gates.

Synset Examples

The local potentate held a grand feast to celebrate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: potentate - a powerful or important ruler

Etymology

Potentate is from Latin potentatus (rule, power), from potens (powerful).
