false mallow

1. NOUN

/ fɔls ˈmæloʊ / (AmE), / fɔːls ˈmæləʊ / (BrE)
A genus of coarse herbs and subshrubs native to arid regions of North and South America, noted for their pink or scarlet flowers and globe-shaped fruits.
genus of arid-region herbs with pink or scarlet flowers

Examples

Several species of false mallow thrive in the dry climates of the American Southwest.
The false mallow produces distinctive round fruits and vibrant scarlet flowers.
Gardeners in arid regions often plant false mallow for its drought tolerance and colorful blooms.
