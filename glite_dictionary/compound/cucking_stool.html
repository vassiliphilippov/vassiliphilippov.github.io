cucking stool

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌkɪŋ stuːl / (AmE), / ˈkʌkɪŋ stuːl / (BrE)
A chair used as a historical instrument of punishment, in which offenders were tied and ducked into water.
historical punishment device; dunking chair
archaic

Examples

The village displayed the old cucking stool as a reminder of harsher times.
Women accused of gossiping were sometimes sentenced to the cucking stool.
The town square once featured a cucking stool used to punish petty offenders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ducking stool - a similar punishment device, often interchangeable with 'cucking stool'

Etymology

From Middle English 'cokynge stole', where 'cok' means 'to defecate', referring to the intended shame.
