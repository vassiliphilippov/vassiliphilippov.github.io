family Palaemonidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ˌpæliˌmoʊˈnɪdaɪ / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli ˌpæliːməˈnɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of crustaceans that includes many species of prawns found in both fresh and saltwater environments.
a family of prawns (crustaceans)

Examples

Many freshwater prawns belong to the family Palaemonidae, which is widespread across the globe.
Researchers studied the unique reproductive habits of the family Palaemonidae in their natural habitats.
Members of the family Palaemonidae play important roles in aquatic food webs.

Etymology

From New Latin 'Palaemonidae', the family name based on the type genus 'Palaemon'.
