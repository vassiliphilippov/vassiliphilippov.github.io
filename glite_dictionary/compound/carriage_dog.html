carriage dog

1. NOUN

/ ˈkærɪdʒ dɔɡ / (AmE), / ˈkærɪdʒ dɒɡ / (BrE)
A large breed of dog with a smooth white coat marked with black or brown spots, originally bred to run alongside carriages; also known as a Dalmatian.
dog breed with spotted coat; Dalmatian

Examples

The carriage dog was historically used to trot alongside horse-drawn carriages.
Her family has always owned a carriage dog for both companionship and protection.
Visitors admired the striking black spots on the carriage dog at the dog show.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Dalmatian - a dog breed with a white coat and black or brown spots; carriage dog

Etymology

Named for its historical role running alongside horse-drawn carriages.
