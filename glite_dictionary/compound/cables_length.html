cable's length

1. NOUN

/ ˈkeɪbəlz lɛŋθ / (AmE), / ˈkeɪbəlz lɛŋθ / (BrE)
a nautical unit of length, equal to one-tenth of a nautical mile (about 185 meters or 608 feet)
nautical unit of length

Examples

The wreck was discovered at a cable's length from the shore.
Ships anchored several cable's lengths apart to avoid collision.
The submarine stayed submerged for two cable's lengths beyond the reef.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fathom - a nautical unit of depth equal to six feet
  • Degree Higher: nautical mile - a unit of distance used at sea, about 1852 meters

Etymology

From the use of a ship's cable as a standard measure of length at sea.
