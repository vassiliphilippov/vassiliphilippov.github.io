Barrow's goldeneye

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæroʊz ˈɡoʊld(ə)nˌaɪ / (AmE), / ˈbærəʊz ˈɡəʊld(ə)nˌaɪ / (BrE)
A North American species of goldeneye diving duck, notable for the male's distinctive white facial patch.
a North American goldeneye duck species

Examples

We spotted a Barrow's goldeneye swimming near the shore during our birdwatching trip.
The Barrow's goldeneye is known for its striking plumage and bright yellow eyes.
Unlike the common goldeneye, the Barrow's goldeneye prefers nesting in the forests of western North America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: common goldeneye - a related species of goldeneye duck

Etymology

Named after Sir John Barrow, a British statesman, and for its golden-colored eye.
