wishful thinker

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪʃfəl ˈθɪŋkər / (AmE), / ˈwɪʃfʊl ˈθɪŋkə / (BrE)
a person who forms beliefs or hopes based on what they wish were true rather than on evidence or reality; someone who escapes into fantasy to avoid facing facts.
a person indulging in unrealistic hopes or fantasies

Examples

As a wishful thinker, he refused to accept that the plan might fail.
The company needs realists, not wishful thinkers who ignore the facts.
Sometimes being a wishful thinker can help you stay optimistic during hard times.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: realist - a person who accepts the world as it is, focusing on facts and reality
  • Synonym: dreamer - a person who has ideas or conceives projects regarded as impractical; an idealist
