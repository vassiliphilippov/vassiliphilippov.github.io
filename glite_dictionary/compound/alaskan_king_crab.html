Alaskan king crab

1. NOUN

/ əˈlæskən kɪŋ kræb / (AmE), / əˈlɑːskən kɪŋ kræb / (BrE)
a large edible crab found in northern Pacific waters, especially near Alaska and Japan, known for its sweet meat and spiny shell
large edible crab found near Alaska

Examples

The price of Alaskan king crab rises sharply during holiday seasons.
She ordered fresh Alaskan king crab at the seafood restaurant.
Fishing for Alaskan king crab can be dangerous due to harsh weather conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: king crab - a large marine crab species related to the Alaskan king crab
  • Degree Lower: snow crab - another edible crab of northern Pacific and Atlantic waters

2. NOUN

/ əˈlæskən kɪŋ kræb / (AmE), / əˈlæskən kɪŋ kræb / (BrE)
The meat from a large cold-water crab found near Alaska, prized for its sweet flavor and usually harvested for its legs.
meat of large Alaskan crab, mostly leg meat

Examples

He ordered steamed Alaskan king crab at the fancy seafood restaurant.
The fresh Alaskan king crab legs were served with melted butter.
Harvesting Alaskan king crab is one of the most dangerous jobs in the fishing industry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: king crab - a species of large crab found in cold northern seas

Etymology

Named after Alaska, where the crab is commonly harvested, and 'king crab' due to its large size.
