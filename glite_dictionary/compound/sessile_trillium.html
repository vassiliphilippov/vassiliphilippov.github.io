sessile trillium

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛsəl trɪˈlɪəm / (AmE), / ˈsɛsaɪl trɪˈlɪəm / (BrE)
A species of trillium native to the northeastern United States, characterized by stalkless leaves and red or purple flowers with a pungent odor.
species of trillium with stalkless leaves

Examples

The forest floor was dotted with blooming sessile trillium after the spring rain.
Botanists identified the rare sessile trillium by its stalkless leaves and deep red petals.
We spotted several sessile trillium plants near the hiking trail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trillium - a genus of perennial flowering plants with three leaves and three petals

Etymology

From Latin 'sessilis' (sitting, resting on the surface), referring to leaves that are attached directly without a stalk.
