visual hole

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪʒuəl hoʊl / (AmE), / ˈvɪʒu.əl həʊl / (BrE)
an area in a design or visual presentation that lacks content or clarity, creating a noticeable gap or absence
gap or absence in a visual design

Examples

The designer noticed a visual hole in the center of the webpage, so she added an image.
A poorly placed logo can leave a visual hole that distracts viewers from the main message.
During the review, the creative director pointed out several visual holes that needed to be addressed.

Synset Examples

He felt a visual hole in the presentation due to missing images.
The advertisement created a visual hole that drew in curious customers.
