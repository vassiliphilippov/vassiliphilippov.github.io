Hanyu Pinyin

1. NOUN

/ ˌhæn.juː ˈpiːn.jɪn / (AmE), / ˌhan.juː ˈpɪn.jɪn / (BrE)
the official system for romanizing the pronunciation of Mandarin Chinese using the Latin alphabet
Mandarin romanization system

Examples

Chinese language textbooks often use Hanyu Pinyin to help learners pronounce new words.
Beijing is written as 'Běijīng' in Hanyu Pinyin.
He found it easier to read Chinese names when they were written in Hanyu Pinyin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: romanization - representation of words with the Latin alphabet

Etymology

From Mandarin 汉语拼音 (Hànyǔ pīnyīn), meaning 'Chinese language spelling sounds'.
