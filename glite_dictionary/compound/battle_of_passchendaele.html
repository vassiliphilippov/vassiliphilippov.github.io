Battle of Passchendaele

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæt̬.əl əv ˌpæʃ.ənˈdɛl / (AmE), / ˈbæt.əl əv ˌpæʃ.ənˈdeɪl / (BrE)
A major battle of World War I in 1917, in which an Allied offensive in Flanders was hindered by waterlogged conditions and German use of mustard gas, resulting in heavy casualties and limited gains.
World War I battle in Flanders, 1917

Examples

The Battle of Passchendaele became infamous for the horrific conditions and massive loss of life.
Many soldiers wrote about their experiences during the Battle of Passchendaele in letters home.
The failure of the Allies to achieve their goals at the Battle of Passchendaele is often discussed by historians.

Etymology

Named after the village of Passchendaele in West Flanders, Belgium.
