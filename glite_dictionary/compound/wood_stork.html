wood stork

1. NOUN

/ wʊd stɔrk / (AmE), / wʊd stɔːk / (BrE)
a large American stork with a downward-curved bill, found in wooded swamps of the New World tropics
a large American stork with a curved bill

Examples

We saw a wood stork wading quietly in the marsh near the Everglades.
The wood stork is recognizable by its bald head and curved bill.
Conservation efforts have helped increase the population of wood storks in Florida.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stork - a large, long-legged wading bird with a long straight bill
