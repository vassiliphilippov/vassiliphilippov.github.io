pall of smoke

1. NOUN

/ pɔl əv smoʊk / (AmE), / pɔːl əv sməʊk / (BrE)
a thick, heavy cloud of smoke, often obscuring the view or creating a gloomy atmosphere
a thick cloud of smoke

Examples

A dense pall of smoke hung over the city after the factory fire.
The pall of smoke drifted across the road, making it hard to see.
Firefighters struggled to see through the thick pall of smoke filling the building.

Synset Examples

The pall of smoke obscured the view.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cloud of smoke - a visible mass of smoke in the air

Etymology

From 'pall' (dark cloth covering a coffin, later extended to mean anything that covers or envelops), and 'smoke'.
