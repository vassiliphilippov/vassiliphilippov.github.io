corporate bond

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔːrpərət bɑnd / (AmE), / ˈkɔːpərət bɒnd / (BrE)
A bond issued by a corporation to raise funds, giving holders a fixed interest payment but no ownership rights.
bond issued by a corporation

Examples

Many companies issue corporate bonds to finance expansion and development projects.
Investors often compare the yields of corporate bonds with those of government securities.
Holding a corporate bond does not give you any ownership in the issuing company.

Synset Examples

a corporate bond is a safer investment than common stock in the same company

Related Terms

  • Antonym: government bond - bond issued by a government
  • Antonym: stock - ownership shares in a corporation
  • Synonym: bond - a debt security, general term
