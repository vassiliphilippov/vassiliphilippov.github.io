turf war

1. NOUN

/ tɝf wɔɹ / (AmE), / tɜːf wɔː / (BrE)
a bitter struggle or conflict for control over a particular area, resource, or sphere of influence, especially between rival groups
bitter struggle for territory or control

Examples

The two companies became locked in a turf war over market share in the city.
Political factions began a fierce turf war within the organization, leading to instability.
Authorities intervened before the rival crews' turf war escalated into open violence.

Synset Examples

a turf war erupted between street gangs
the president's resignation was the result of a turf war with the board of directors

Related Terms

  • Synonym: power struggle - a fight for control or dominance between groups or individuals
  • Antonym: truce - a suspension of fighting; a peace agreement

2. NOUN

/ tɝf wɔr / (AmE), / tɜːf wɔː / (BrE)
a bitter struggle between groups or individuals over territory or control of an area or sphere of influence
conflict over territory or control

Examples

The two departments are locked in a turf war over who manages the new project.
A violent turf war erupted between rival gangs in the city.
Recent changes led to a subtle turf war among senior managers.

Synset Examples

The turf war between the gangs is causing concern in the neighborhood.
The two companies engaged in a turf war over market share.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: power struggle - a fight for control or influence
  • Synonym: conflict - a serious disagreement or argument, typically a protracted one

Etymology

From 'turf' (slang for territory) + 'war' (conflict); originally from American English slang.
