ilama tree

1. NOUN

/ iˈlɑːmə triː / (AmE), / ɪˈlɑːmə triː / (BrE)
a tropical American tree grown in the southern United States, known for its whitish, pink-tinged fruit
tropical American fruit tree

Examples

We planted an ilama tree in our backyard to enjoy its unique fruit.
The ilama tree thrives best in warm, tropical climates with well-drained soil.
During the harvest season, the ilama tree produces fruit with a sweet and creamy flavor.

Etymology

From Spanish 'ilama', possibly of indigenous origin.
