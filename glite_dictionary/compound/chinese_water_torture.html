Chinese water torture

1. NOUN

/ ˌtʃaɪˈniz ˈwɔːtɚ ˌtɔr.tʃɚ / (AmE), / ˌtʃaɪˈniːz ˈwɔː.tə ˌtɔː.tʃə / (BrE)
a slow and distressing experience, especially one marked by persistent annoyance or anxiety, likened to an excruciating form of torture involving repeated drops of water
slow, persistent distress or annoyance

Examples

Waiting for the interview results was Chinese water torture for everyone involved.
The endless meetings became a kind of Chinese water torture for the staff.
Her constant messages felt like Chinese water torture after a while.

Synset Examples

Waiting for the results felt like Chinese water torture.
The constant questioning was akin to Chinese water torture.
His nagging felt like a form of Chinese water torture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: torture - the act of inflicting severe pain as a punishment or to force information
  • Synonym: ordeal - a painful or horrific experience, especially a protracted one
  • Antonym: relief - a feeling of reassurance and relaxation following release from anxiety or distress

Etymology

Refers to a supposed form of torture attributed to China, where drops of water are allowed to fall on a person's forehead for a prolonged period. The phrase is now chiefly used figuratively in English.
