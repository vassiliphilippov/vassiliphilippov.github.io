sweet woodruff

1. NOUN

/ swit ˈwʊdˌrʌf / (AmE), / swiːt ˈwʊd.rʌf / (BrE)
A fragrant perennial plant (Galium odoratum) with small white flowers and narrow leaves, used as flavoring and for ground cover.
fragrant perennial plant used as flavoring, ground cover

Examples

The garden was carpeted with blooming sweet woodruff, giving off a pleasant scent.
She added dried sweet woodruff to the punch for a delicate herbal note.
Many gardeners choose sweet woodruff as a shade-loving ground cover.

2. NOUN

/ swit ˈwʊdˌrʌf / (AmE), / swiːt ˈwʊdrʌf / (BrE)
a low-growing perennial plant with fragrant dark green leaves, often used to flavor May wine and for decorative purposes
fragrant herb used in May wine

Examples

The fragrance of sweet woodruff filled the garden during spring.
Traditional May wine is flavored with sweet woodruff to give it a unique aroma.
She planted sweet woodruff along the shady paths for its pretty leaves and pleasant scent.
