family Majidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ˈmeɪdʒiːdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæməli ˈmeɪdʒɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of marine crabs known as spider crabs, characterized by long legs and often a rounded, spiny shell.
a family of spider crabs

Examples

The family Majidae includes various species commonly known as spider crabs.
Marine biologists study the family Majidae to understand the adaptations of spider crabs.
Members of the family Majidae are known for their spindly legs and camouflaged bodies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spider crab - common name for crabs of the family Majidae

Etymology

From Latin *majus* (a type of crab) + scientific taxonomic suffix *-idae*, denoting a family.
