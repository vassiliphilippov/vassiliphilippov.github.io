white wood aster

1. NOUN

/ waɪt wʊd ˈæstər / (AmE), / waɪt wʊd ˈæstə / (BrE)
a perennial woodland plant of eastern North America, having rhizomes and clusters of white flowers
woodland plant with white flowers

Examples

The white wood aster blooms in shaded forests from late summer into autumn.
Gardeners value the white wood aster for its delicate white flowers and hardy nature.
You can recognize a white wood aster by its star-shaped petals and heart-shaped leaves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wood aster - a group of woodland asters with similar traits
