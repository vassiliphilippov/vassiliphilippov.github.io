sawyer beetle

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɔɪər ˌbiːtəl / (AmE), / ˈsɔːjə ˌbiːtl̩ / (BrE)
a large beetle whose larvae bore into dead or dying trees, especially conifers
wood-boring beetle, pest of dead trees

Examples

The fallen pine tree was infested with sawyer beetles boring deep into the wood.
Researchers studied the life cycle of a sawyer beetle found in North American forests.
After a wildfire, sawyer beetles often colonize the scorched trees, hastening their decay.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: longhorn beetle - a family of beetles with long antennae, many wood-boring

Etymology

From 'sawyer' (a person who saws wood) + 'beetle', referring to the larvae's wood-boring habit.
