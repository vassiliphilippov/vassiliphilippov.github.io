neuroleptic drug

1. NOUN

/ ˌnʊroʊˈlɛptɪk drʌɡ / (AmE), / ˌnjʊərəˈlɛptɪk drʌɡ / (BrE)
a type of tranquilizer used to treat psychotic conditions, especially schizophrenia, by inducing a calming effect
antipsychotic tranquilizer medication
medical

Examples

After being diagnosed with schizophrenia, she was prescribed a neuroleptic drug to help manage her symptoms.
The psychiatrist discussed possible side effects of the neuroleptic drug with the patient.
Researchers are developing new types of neuroleptic drugs with fewer adverse effects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antipsychotic - a drug used to treat psychotic disorders

Etymology

From Greek neuron (nerve) + leptein (to take hold of); referring to their action on the nervous system.
