meadow pipit

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛdoʊ ˈpɪpɪt / (AmE), / ˈmɛdəʊ ˈpɪpɪt / (BrE)
a small brown-and-white songbird (Anthus pratensis) commonly found in northern and central Europe and Asia, known for its distinctive call and streaked plumage
a small Eurasian songbird

Examples

The meadow pipit is a common sight in grasslands across northern Europe.
A meadow pipit perched on a fence post, singing its high-pitched song.
During migration, flocks of meadow pipits can be seen moving south in autumn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pipit - any of several small songbirds of the genus Anthus, especially those with brownish plumage
