Kadai language

1. NOUN

/ kɑˈdaɪ ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / kɑːˈdaɪ ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
A family of Sino-Tibetan languages spoken in southeastern Asia, including Thai, Lao, and related languages.
family of Southeast Asian languages

Examples

The Kadai language family includes Thai, Lao, and several other languages spoken in Southeast Asia.
Many linguists have studied the origins of the Kadai language group.
Speakers of a Kadai language may also be familiar with Chinese influences due to their geographic proximity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Tai language - languages belonging to the Tai family, a major branch of Kadai
