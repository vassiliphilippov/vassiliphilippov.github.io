inner rason

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪnɚ ˈræzɒn / (AmE), / ˈɪnə ˈreɪsɒn / (BrE)
a long, collared robe with narrow, tapered sleeves, worn by clergy, monastics, and seminarians in certain Christian traditions as a basic garment
long clerical robe worn by clergy and monastics

Examples

Orthodox monks are rarely seen outside their cells without their inner rason.
The seminary required all students to wear the inner rason during services and lectures.
He carefully folded his inner rason before leaving the dormitory.

Synset Examples

The inner rason is the basic garment, and is worn at all times, even when working.

Etymology

From Greek ράσον (ráson), meaning 'robe' or 'vestment'.
