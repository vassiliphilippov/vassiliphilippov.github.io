New Zealand

1. PROPN

/ nuː ˈziːlænd / (AmE), / njuː ˈziːlənd / (BrE)
An island country in the southwestern Pacific Ocean, known for its landscapes, sheep farming, and as a member of the British Commonwealth.
country in the southwestern Pacific Ocean

Examples

She spent a year traveling around New Zealand, exploring its stunning mountains and beaches.
The rugby team from New Zealand is famous worldwide for its skill and the haka dance.
Many films, including 'The Lord of the Rings,' were filmed in New Zealand because of its beautiful scenery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Australia - country and continent southeast of Asia, near New Zealand

Etymology

Named after the Dutch province of Zeeland; explored by Dutch navigator Abel Tasman in 1642.
