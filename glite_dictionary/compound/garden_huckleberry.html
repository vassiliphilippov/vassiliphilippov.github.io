garden huckleberry

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɑrdən ˈhʌkəlˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈɡɑːdn̩ ˈhʌkəlˌbɛri / (BrE)
a cultivated variety of black nightshade with small edible orange or black berries, often grown in gardens
edible berry-producing garden plant

Examples

The garden huckleberry thrives in warm climates and produces fruit late in the season.
Many gardeners grow garden huckleberry for its small, dark berries used in jams and pies.
Before eating garden huckleberry, it is important to ensure the berries are fully ripe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nightshade - a family of plants including black nightshade and garden huckleberry
  • Synonym: huckleberry - a different plant species with small, edible berries
