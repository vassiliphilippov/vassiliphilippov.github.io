sweet pepperbush

1. NOUN

/ swit ˈpɛpərˌbʊʃ / (AmE), / swiːt ˈpɛpəbʊʃ / (BrE)
a shrub native to eastern and southern coastal United States, known for its attractive spikes of fragrant white flowers
fragrant-flowered US native shrub

Examples

The sweet pepperbush blooms in late summer, filling the air with its spicy fragrance.
Gardeners often plant sweet pepperbush to attract butterflies and bees to their gardens.
In the wild, sweet pepperbush thrives along streams and in moist woodlands.
