genus Haematobia

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs heɪˌmətəˈbaɪə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs hiːˌmətəˈbɪə / (BrE)
A genus of European bloodsucking flies, some species of which are parasitic on livestock.
genus of European bloodsucking flies

Examples

The common horn fly belongs to the genus Haematobia, known for infesting cattle.
Researchers studied the distribution of the genus Haematobia across different European regions.
The bites from flies in the genus Haematobia can transmit diseases among livestock.

Etymology

From Greek haima (blood) + bios (life), reflecting the blood-feeding habits of the genus.
