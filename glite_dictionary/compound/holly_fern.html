holly fern

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑli fɜrn / (AmE), / ˈhɒli fɜːn / (BrE)
A tropical fern with glossy, spiny-edged fronds that resemble those of holly, found in the Old World and sometimes classified in the genus Polystichum.
a tropical fern with holly-like fronds

Examples

The holly fern is prized for its striking, shiny leaves in shaded gardens.
I bought a holly fern to add texture to my indoor plant collection.
Unlike true holly, the holly fern does not produce berries but its fronds are quite decorative.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɑli fəːrn / (AmE), / ˈhɒli fɜːn / (BrE)
Any of several ferns of the genus Polystichum with stiff, glossy fronds resembling those of a holly plant.
a fern with holly-like glossy fronds

Examples

The holly fern is prized in gardens for its attractive, shiny foliage.
She planted a holly fern in the shaded corner of her backyard.
Unlike many ferns, the holly fern tolerates drier conditions and is quite hardy.
