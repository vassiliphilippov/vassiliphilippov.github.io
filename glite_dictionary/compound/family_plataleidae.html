family Plataleidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli pləˌteɪliˌaɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli pləˌteɪlɪˌiːdɪ / (BrE)
a family of wading birds known as spoonbills, characterized by their long legs and distinctive spoon-shaped bills
family of birds: the spoonbills

Examples

Members of the family Plataleidae are easily recognized by their unique spoon-shaped bills.
The family Plataleidae includes all species of spoonbills found around the world.
Scientists studied the migration patterns of the family Plataleidae across continents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spoonbill - a wading bird with a long, flat, spoon-shaped bill

Etymology

From New Latin Plataleidae, from Platalea (the type genus) + -idae (family suffix in zoology).
