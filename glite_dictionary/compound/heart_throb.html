heart throb

1. NOUN

/ hɑrt θrɑb / (AmE), / hɑːt θrɒb / (BrE)
a strong, rapid beating of the heart, often caused by emotion such as excitement or anxiety
rapid beating of the heart

Examples

As she waited to hear her name called, she felt her heart throb with nervous anticipation.
Every time he stood on stage, the heart throb in his chest reminded him of the stakes.
During the scary movie, my heart throb quickened with every unexpected twist.

Synset Examples

I could feel my heart throb in excitement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: palpitation - an unusual or noticeably strong heartbeat
