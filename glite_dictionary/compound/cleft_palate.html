cleft palate

1. NOUN

/ klɛft ˈpælət / (AmE), / klɛft ˈpælət / (BrE)
a congenital split or opening in the roof of the mouth, resulting from incomplete fusion of the palate during development
congenital split in the roof of the mouth
medical

Examples

The surgeon specializes in correcting cleft palate in young children.
A child born with a cleft palate may have difficulty feeding and speaking clearly.
Advances in medical procedures have improved the treatment of cleft palate in recent years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: palate - the roof of the mouth

Etymology

From 'cleft', the past participle of 'cleave', meaning 'split', and 'palate', from Latin 'palatum'.
