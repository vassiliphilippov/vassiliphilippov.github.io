Nebraska fern

1. NOUN

/ nəˈbræskə fɜrn / (AmE), / nɛˈbrɑːskə fɜːn / (BrE)
A large, branching biennial herb native to Eurasia and Africa, now found in North America, with large fernlike leaves, white flowers, and highly poisonous parts, usually growing in damp habitats.
poisonous biennial herb with fernlike leaves

Examples

The leaves of the Nebraska fern resemble those of a true fern but are highly toxic.
Cattle have been accidentally poisoned after eating Nebraska fern that grows in wet pastures.
You should avoid touching Nebraska fern when hiking, as all parts of the plant are extremely poisonous.
