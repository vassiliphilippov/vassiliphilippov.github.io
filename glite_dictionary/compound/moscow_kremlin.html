Moscow Kremlin

1. PROPN

/ ˌmɑːskaʊ ˈkrɛmlɪn / (AmE), / ˌmɒskaʊ ˈkrɛmlɪn / (BrE)
The fortified complex at the center of Moscow, serving as the official residence of the President of Russia and housing the offices of the Russian government.
fortified complex in Moscow, seat of Russian government

Examples

The president delivered a speech from the Moscow Kremlin to the nation.
Thousands of tourists visit the Moscow Kremlin each year to admire its cathedrals and palaces.
After the meeting, officials returned to the Moscow Kremlin for further discussions.

Etymology

From Russian 'Kreml'' (fortress, citadel) and the name of the city Moscow.
