mouse-eared chickweed

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaʊs ɪrd ˈtʃɪkˌwid / (AmE), / ˈmaʊs ɪəd ˈtʃɪkwiːd / (BrE)
any of several small flowering plants of the genus Cerastium, closely related to common chickweed, typically with hairy leaves resembling a mouse's ear
a small herbaceous plant with hairy leaves

Examples

Mouse-eared chickweed grows in clusters along the edge of woodland paths.
The leaves of mouse-eared chickweed are covered with fine, silky hairs.
Gardeners often mistake mouse-eared chickweed for regular chickweed due to its similar appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chickweed - a common, small-leaved plant used as fodder and ground cover

Etymology

From the resemblance of the plant's soft, hairy leaves to a mouse's ear; 'chickweed' from its use as food for chickens.
