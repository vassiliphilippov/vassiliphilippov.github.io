hearing loss

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɪrɪŋ lɔs / (AmE), / ˈhɪərɪŋ lɒs / (BrE)
the partial or total inability to hear sounds in one or both ears
reduced or absent ability to hear
medical

Examples

He was diagnosed with hearing loss after years of working in a noisy factory.
Children with hearing loss may need special support in school.
Sudden hearing loss can sometimes be a medical emergency.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deafness - complete or severe loss of hearing
  • Antonym: normal hearing - the ability to hear sounds within the normal range
