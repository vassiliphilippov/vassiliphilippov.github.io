rate of depreciation

1. NOUN

/ reɪt əv dɪˌpriːʃiˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / reɪt əv dɪˌpriːsiˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
the percentage or amount by which the value of an asset decreases over a specific period, often used for tax or accounting purposes
percentage decrease in asset value over time

Examples

The accountant calculated the rate of depreciation to determine the company's annual tax deduction.
Understanding the rate of depreciation is crucial when deciding how long to keep company vehicles.
Different assets, such as machinery and computers, may have a different rate of depreciation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: depreciation - the decrease in value of an asset over time
