pyrophosphoric acid

1. NOUN

/ ˌpaɪroʊfɒsˈfɔrɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / ˌpaɪərəʊfɒsˈfɒrɪk ˈæsɪd / (BrE)
a solid acid (H4P2O7) formed by heating orthophosphoric acid; used mainly in chemical synthesis and analysis
solid acid used in chemistry
medical

Examples

Pyrophosphoric acid is often produced by the dehydration of orthophosphoric acid at high temperatures.
Researchers used pyrophosphoric acid as a reagent in several analytical procedures.
Proper handling is required when working with pyrophosphoric acid due to its corrosive nature.

Etymology

From Greek 'pyr' (fire) + 'phosphoros' (phosphorus), referring to its formation by heating phosphoric acid.
