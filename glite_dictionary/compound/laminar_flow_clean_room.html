laminar flow clean room

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæmɪnɚ floʊ klin ruːm / (AmE), / ˈlæmɪnə fləʊ kliːn ruːm / (BrE)
a clean room in which air flows in parallel layers (laminar flow) to minimize airborne particles, used especially in high-precision manufacturing such as microprocessor fabrication
ultra-clean room with laminar airflow

Examples

Engineers must wear special suits to enter the laminar flow clean room where microchips are assembled.
The laminar flow clean room maintains a constant stream of purified air to prevent contamination during production.
Strict protocols are enforced within the laminar flow clean room to ensure product quality and safety.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clean room - a controlled environment with low levels of pollutants
