genus Crangon

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˈkrænɡɒn / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈkraŋɡɒn / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Crangonidae, consisting of a group of shrimps including the common sand shrimp and other related species.
type genus of Crangonidae (shrimp genus)

Examples

The genus Crangon includes several species of shrimp found in cold and temperate waters.
Marine biologists conducted a study on members of the genus Crangon to learn more about their habitat preferences.
One well-known representative of the genus Crangon is the common sand shrimp.

Etymology

From New Latin 'Crangon', the genus name, likely from Greek 'krangōn' (a kind of marine animal).
