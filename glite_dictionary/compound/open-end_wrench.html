open-end wrench

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊ.pən ɛnd rɛntʃ / (AmE), / ˈəʊ.pən ɛnd rɛntʃ / (BrE)
a wrench with parallel jaws set at a fixed distance, typically having open ends to grip nuts or bolts
wrench with fixed open jaws

Examples

He selected an open-end wrench to tighten the loose bolt under the sink.
Every mechanic's toolkit should include an open-end wrench set of various sizes.
She used an open-end wrench because the bolt was too close to the wall for a socket wrench.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spanner - a British term for a wrench
  • Degree Higher: box-end wrench - a wrench with a closed loop (box) to grip nuts or bolts
