xiphium iris

1. NOUN

/ ˈzaɪfiəm ˈaɪrɪs / (AmE), / ˈzɪfiəm ˈaɪrɪs / (BrE)
A bulbous iris native to the western Mediterranean region, typically bearing violet-purple flowers.
bulbous iris of the western Mediterranean

Examples

The gardener was proud of his collection of xiphium iris blooming in early spring.
Many florists value the xiphium iris for its striking violet-purple petals and elegant shape.
Planting xiphium iris bulbs in the autumn ensures a beautiful display the following year.

Etymology

From New Latin 'Xiphium', from Greek 'xiphos' meaning 'sword', referring to the shape of the leaves, combined with 'iris', the genus name.
