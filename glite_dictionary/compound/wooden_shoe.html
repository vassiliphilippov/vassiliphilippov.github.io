wooden shoe

1. NOUN

/ ˈwʊdən ʃu / (AmE), / ˈwʊd.ən ʃuː / (BrE)
a shoe made by carving a single piece of wood, traditionally worn in some European countries
shoe carved from wood

Examples

Traditional Dutch farmers often wore wooden shoes to protect their feet while working in the fields.
A pair of old wooden shoes was displayed in the museum's European folk exhibit.
He stumbled a little as he tried walking in the heavy wooden shoes during the cultural festival.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clog - a shoe with a thick wooden sole, or entirely made of wood
