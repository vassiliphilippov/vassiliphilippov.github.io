psychotic person

1. NOUN

/ saɪˈkɑtɪk ˈpɜrsən / (AmE), / saɪˈkɒtɪk ˈpɜːsən / (BrE)
a person suffering from psychosis, a severe mental disorder that affects thought and perception
person with psychosis
medical

Examples

The hospital has a specialized unit for treating psychotic persons who need close supervision.
A psychotic person may experience delusions and severe disconnection from reality.
It can be challenging for families to support a psychotic person during an episode.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: psychotic - suffering from psychosis

Etymology

From 'psychotic' (from Greek psukhōtikos, relating to the mind) + 'person'.
