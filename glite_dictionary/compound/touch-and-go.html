touch-and-go

1. ADJ

/ ˌtʌtʃ ən ˈɡoʊ / (AmE), / ˌtʌtʃ ən ˈɡəʊ / (BrE)
(of a situation) dangerous or uncertain, with the outcome in doubt until the last moment.
dangerous or uncertain situation

Examples

It was touch-and-go whether the patient would survive the night.
The rescue mission was touch-and-go due to the bad weather conditions.
After her accident, her recovery was touch-and-go for several weeks.

Synset Examples

a parlous journey on stormy seas
a perilous voyage across the Atlantic in a small boat
the precarious life of an undersea diver
dangerous surgery followed by a touch-and-go recovery

Related Terms

  • Synonym: precarious - dangerously uncertain or unstable
  • Synonym: perilous - full of danger or risk
  • Antonym: safe - not in danger or risk

Etymology

Originally nautical slang, referring to a ship narrowly avoiding grounding by briefly touching and then moving on.
