astronomy unit

1. NOUN

/ əˈstrɑːnəmi ˈjuːnɪt / (AmE), / əˈstrɒnəmi ˈjuːnɪt / (BrE)
A unit of length used to measure astronomical distances, equal to the mean distance from the Earth to the Sun (about 149.6 million kilometers).
unit of length for astronomical distances

Examples

The distance between the Earth and the Sun is defined as one astronomy unit.
Light travels about 63,240 astronomy units in one year.
Scientists often measure distances within our solar system in astronomy units.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: astronomical unit - unit of length for astronomical distances; mean Earth–Sun distance

Etymology

Formed from 'astronomy' (ancient Greek astronomia, study of stars) and 'unit' (Latin ūnitās, oneness).
