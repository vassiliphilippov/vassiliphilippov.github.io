to gain momentum

1. VERB

/ ɡeɪn moʊˈmɛn.təm / (AmE), / ɡeɪn məʊˈmɛn.təm / (BrE)
to begin to move faster, become stronger, or attract more support over time
increase in speed, strength, or support

Examples

As winter approached, the flu outbreak began to gain momentum in the city.
Her new business started slowly but soon began to gain momentum thanks to word of mouth.
The protest movement gained momentum after the government announced the new law.

Synset Examples

Our sales are beginning to gain momentum this quarter.
The project started to gain momentum after the meeting.
The campaign will gain momentum once we launch it.
The movement for eradicationism has gained momentum recently.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gather steam - to start to progress more quickly; become more effective or successful
  • Antonym: lose momentum - to start advancing more slowly or lose strength or support
