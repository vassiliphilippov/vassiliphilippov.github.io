genus Avena

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs əˈviːnə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs əˈviːnə / (BrE)
A genus of grasses that includes the oat plants, widely cultivated for their edible seeds.
botanical genus including oats

Examples

The botanist identified the plant as belonging to the genus Avena due to its characteristic inflorescence.
Many cereal crops, such as oats, are categorized in the genus Avena.
The genus Avena comprises several species, some of which are cultivated for human and animal consumption.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oat - the edible seeds or plant of Avena sativa

Etymology

From Latin 'avēna' (oats), used as the genus name in botanical Latin.
