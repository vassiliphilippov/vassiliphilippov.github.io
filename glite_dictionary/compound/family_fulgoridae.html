family Fulgoridae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli fʊlˈɡɔːrɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli fʊlˈɡɒrɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of plant-hopping insects known as lantern flies, characterized by their often brightly colored and sometimes elongated heads.
family of lantern flies (insects)

Examples

The family Fulgoridae consists of a wide variety of lantern flies found in tropical regions.
Scientists discovered a new species within the family Fulgoridae during their expedition to the rainforest.
Members of the family Fulgoridae are known for their distinctive head shapes and vibrant colors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lantern fly - a brightly colored insect in the Fulgoridae family

Etymology

From New Latin Fulgoridae, based on genus Fulgora and suffix -idae (denoting family in zoological classification).
