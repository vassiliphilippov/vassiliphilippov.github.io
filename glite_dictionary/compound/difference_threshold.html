difference threshold

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɪfərəns ˈθrɛʃhoʊld / (AmE), / ˈdɪfərəns ˈθrɛʃhəʊld / (BrE)
the smallest amount of change in a stimulus that can be detected by a person
minimum detectable change in a stimulus

Examples

The difference threshold for tasting salt in water varies from person to person.
Researchers measured the difference threshold of participants by gradually increasing the brightness of a light.
A person's difference threshold can be influenced by attention and fatigue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: just noticeable difference - the minimal change in a stimulus that can just barely be detected
