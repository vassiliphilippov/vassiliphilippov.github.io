army attache

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑrmi ˌætəˈʃeɪ / (AmE), / ˈɑːmi ˌatəˈʃeɪ / (BrE)
a military official attached to a diplomatic mission, representing the army and typically holding the rank of commissioned or warrant officer
military officer in an embassy, army representative

Examples

The army attache attended the official reception at the embassy.
As an army attache, she reported military developments back to her home country.
He served as an army attache in several European capitals during his career.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: military attache - an officer serving at a diplomatic mission representing military interests
  • Synonym: naval attache - a naval officer serving at an embassy or diplomatic mission

Etymology

From French 'attaché' (attached), used in diplomacy.
