petit mal epilepsy

1. NOUN

/ pəˌti mɑl ɛˈpɪlɛpˌsi / (AmE), / pəˌtiː mæl ˌɛpɪˈlɛpsi / (BrE)
a form of epilepsy marked by brief episodes of impaired consciousness, often without convulsions, also known as absence seizures
epilepsy with brief loss of consciousness
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed her with petit mal epilepsy after several short episodes of unresponsiveness.
Children with petit mal epilepsy may suddenly stop speaking or staring blankly for a few seconds.
Effective treatment can help control the symptoms of petit mal epilepsy.

Synset Examples

she has been suffering from petit mal since childhood

Related Terms

  • Synonym: absence seizure - brief loss of consciousness with no convulsions

Etymology

From French 'petit mal' meaning 'little illness'.
