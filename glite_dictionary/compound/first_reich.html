First Reich

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɝst ˌraɪk / (AmE), / ˈfɜːst ˌraɪk / (BrE)
a historical term referring to the Holy Roman Empire, regarded as the first of three German empires
historical name for the Holy Roman Empire

Examples

Historians often refer to the Holy Roman Empire as the First Reich in German history.
The notion of the First Reich is used to draw comparisons with later periods in German history.
In textbooks, the First Reich typically denotes the era before the rise of the German Empire.

Synset Examples

The Holy Roman Empire was known as the First Reich.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Second Reich - the German Empire from 1871 to 1918, regarded as the second German empire
  • Degree Higher: Third Reich - Nazi Germany from 1933 to 1945, considered the third German empire

Etymology

From German 'Erstes Reich', meaning 'First Empire', used to distinguish the Holy Roman Empire from the later German empires.
