colloidal suspension

1. NOUN

/ kəˈlɔɪ.dl̩ səˈspɛn.ʃən / (AmE), / kəˈlɔɪ.di.əl səˈspɛn.ʃən / (BrE)
A mixture in which finely divided solid particles are dispersed but not dissolved in a liquid, forming a stable system.
mixture of solid in liquid (colloid)

Examples

Milk is an example of a colloidal suspension, where fat droplets are dispersed in water.
Scientists examined the stability of the colloidal suspension under different temperatures.
A colloidal suspension can remain evenly distributed without settling for a long period.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: colloid - A homogeneous, noncrystalline substance consisting of large molecules or ultramicroscopic particles dispersed through a second substance.

Etymology

From 'colloid' (from Greek kolla, glue) and 'suspension' (from Latin suspendere, to hang up).
