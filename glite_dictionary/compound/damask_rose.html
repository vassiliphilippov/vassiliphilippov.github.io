damask rose

1. NOUN

/ ˈdæm.əsk roʊz / (AmE), / ˈdæm.əsk rəʊz / (BrE)
a large, hardy, and very fragrant pink rose (Rosa × damascena), often cultivated for its scented oil; a parent of many modern hybrids
fragrant pink rose, source of rose oil

Examples

The damask rose is famous for its use in the production of rose oil.
Several varieties of damask rose are prized for their strong, sweet fragrance in gardens.
She tends a beautiful damask rose bush that blooms every spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rose - a prickly bush or shrub that typically bears red, pink, yellow, or white fragrant flowers

Etymology

From Middle English 'damaske,' from Damascus, the capital of Syria, where the rose was believed to have originated.
