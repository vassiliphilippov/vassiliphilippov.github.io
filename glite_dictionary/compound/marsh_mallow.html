marsh mallow

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑrʃ ˌmæloʊ / (AmE), / ˈmɑːʃ ˌmæləʊ / (BrE)
A European perennial plant (Althaea officinalis) with soft, hairy leaves and lilac-pink flowers, naturalized in the United States.
a European perennial plant with pink flowers

Examples

The root of the marsh mallow was once used to make medicinal syrups.
Wild marsh mallow plants thrive in the wetlands near the river.
In the garden, the marsh mallow's pale pink flowers attract many bees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marshmallow - a soft, sweet confection made from sugar and gelatin

Etymology

From the Old English 'mersc-mealwe', from 'marsh' and 'mallow'.
