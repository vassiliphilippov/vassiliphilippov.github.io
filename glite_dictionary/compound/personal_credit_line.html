personal credit line

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɝsənəl ˈkrɛdɪt laɪn / (AmE), / ˈpɜːsənəl ˈkrɛdɪt laɪn / (BrE)
an arrangement with a bank or lender that allows a person to borrow up to a specified maximum amount of money, which can be used as needed
maximum amount an individual can borrow from a lender

Examples

She applied for a personal credit line to help cover unexpected expenses.
A personal credit line can provide financial flexibility in case of emergencies.
His bank offered to increase his personal credit line after reviewing his credit history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: credit limit - the maximum amount of credit that a financial institution extends to a client
