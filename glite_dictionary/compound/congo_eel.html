congo eel

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑŋɡoʊ iːl / (AmE), / ˈkɒŋɡəʊ iːl / (BrE)
A slender, aquatic, eel-shaped salamander with two pairs of small legs, found in still, muddy waters of the southern United States.
an aquatic, eel-shaped salamander of the southern US

Examples

The congo eel is rarely seen during the day as it hides in mud at the bottom of swamps.
Local fishermen sometimes catch a congo eel while setting traps for catfish.
The biology class visited the bayou to observe the elusive congo eel in its natural habitat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amphiuma - a genus of aquatic salamanders also known as congo eel

Etymology

The name refers to its superficial resemblance to true eels and possibly to its elongated form; not related to the Congo River.
