trade stoppage

1. NOUN

/ treɪd ˈstɑpɪdʒ / (AmE), / treɪd ˈstɒpɪdʒ / (BrE)
an official government order that halts or restricts trade, typically to enforce a policy or as a sanction
governmental trade restriction or ban

Examples

The government implemented a trade stoppage to protest against unfair labor practices abroad.
A sudden trade stoppage can have major economic repercussions for both parties involved.
After years of negotiation, the two countries finally lifted the trade stoppage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trade embargo - an official ban on trade with a particular country
  • Synonym: trade sanction - a restriction or penalty affecting trade, imposed as a coercive measure
