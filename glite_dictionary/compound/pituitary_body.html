pituitary body

1. NOUN

/ pɪˈtuː.əˌtɛr.i ˈbɑː.di / (AmE), / pɪˈtjuː.ɪˌtɛə.ri ˈbɒd.i / (BrE)
the small, oval endocrine gland at the base of the brain that regulates other glands in the endocrine system
the endocrine gland at the base of the brain
medical

Examples

The pituitary body plays a crucial role in regulating hormone levels throughout the human body.
Tumors affecting the pituitary body can disrupt normal growth and development.
Doctors observed an abnormality in her pituitary body during the MRI scan.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pituitary gland - the same gland as the pituitary body

Etymology

From Latin 'pituitarius' (relating to phlegm), once thought to secrete mucus; 'body' in anatomical use.
