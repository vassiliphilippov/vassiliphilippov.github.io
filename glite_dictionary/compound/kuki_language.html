Kuki language

1. NOUN

/ ˈkuːki ˌlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈkuːki ˌlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
A group of Kamarupan languages spoken in western Myanmar (Burma), Bangladesh, and easternmost India.
language group of India, Myanmar, and Bangladesh

Examples

Many people in northeast India speak the Kuki language as their mother tongue.
Linguists study the Kuki language to understand the diversity of Tibeto-Burman languages.
There are several dialects within the Kuki language family spoken across Myanmar and Bangladesh.

Etymology

From the ethnonym 'Kuki', referring to the Kuki people of northeast India and surrounding regions.
