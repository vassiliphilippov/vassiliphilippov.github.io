frequency modulation

1. NOUN

/ ˈfriːkwənsi ˌmɑdʒəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈfriːkwənsi ˌmɒdjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
a method of transmitting information by varying the frequency of a carrier wave, especially in radio broadcasting
modulation method using carrier wave frequency

Examples

Most music stations broadcast using frequency modulation for improved sound quality.
The invention of frequency modulation revolutionized radio transmission and reception.
Many car radios are equipped to receive both amplitude and frequency modulation signals.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: amplitude modulation - modulation method by varying amplitude of carrier wave
