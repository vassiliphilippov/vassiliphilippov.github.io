little egret

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪt̬l ˈiːɡrət / (AmE), / ˈlɪtl ˈiːɡrɪt / (BrE)
A small, white species of egret (Egretta garzetta) found in parts of Europe, Africa, Asia, and Australia.
a small white species of egret

Examples

We spotted a little egret by the riverbank, searching for food in the shallow water.
The little egret is easily recognized by its slender black bill and delicate plumes.
Several little egrets have begun nesting in the wetlands each spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: egret - a type of long-legged wading bird with white or gray plumage
