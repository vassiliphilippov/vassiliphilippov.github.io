military chaplain

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪlɪˌtɛri ˈtʃæplɪn / (AmE), / ˈmɪlɪt(ə)ri ˈtʃaplɪn / (BrE)
a chaplain who serves in a branch of the armed forces, providing religious support and counseling to military personnel
chaplain serving in the military

Examples

The military chaplain led a memorial service for the fallen soldiers.
As a military chaplain, she provided comfort to troops overseas.
The base welcomed a new military chaplain to support its service members.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chaplain - a member of the clergy attached to a private chapel, institution, or military unit
