particle accelerator

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑrtɪkəl əkˌsɛləˌreɪtɚ / (AmE), / ˈpɑːtɪkəl əkˌsɛləˈreɪtə / (BrE)
a machine that increases the speed and energy of charged particles, often to enable scientific research or medical applications
machine that speeds up charged particles

Examples

Scientists use a particle accelerator to study the fundamental building blocks of matter.
The laboratory built a new particle accelerator to achieve higher energy levels for experiments.
A particle accelerator can also be used in medical treatments, such as cancer radiotherapy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cyclotron - type of particle accelerator using a magnetic field to accelerate particles in a spiral
  • Synonym: linear accelerator - a straight-line particle accelerator

Etymology

From 'particle' (small piece) and 'accelerator' (device that increases speed).
