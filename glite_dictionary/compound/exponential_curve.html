exponential curve

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛk.spəˈnɛn.ʃəl kɝv / (AmE), / ˌɛk.spəˈnɛn.ʃəl kɜːv / (BrE)
A curve on a graph that represents an exponential function, showing rapid increases or decreases.
graph showing exponential growth or decay

Examples

The population's growth followed an exponential curve, doubling every few years.
When plotted, the data formed a distinct exponential curve on the graph.
Understanding the exponential curve is crucial in fields like epidemiology and finance.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: logarithmic curve - graph of a logarithmic function
