specious argument

1. NOUN

/ ˈspiʃəs ˈɑrɡjəmənt / (AmE), / ˈspiːʃəs ˈɑːɡjʊmənt / (BrE)
An argument that appears to be good or valid but is actually incorrect or misleading.
deceptively plausible argument

Examples

The politician's specious argument convinced many voters until it was thoroughly debunked.
Her specious argument initially seemed compelling, but it fell apart under close scrutiny.
Don't be misled by a specious argument; always check the facts before deciding.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fallacious argument - An argument that is logically unsound or misleading
  • Antonym: sound argument - An argument that is logically valid and true

Etymology

From Latin 'speciosus' (good-looking, plausible) + 'argumentum' (evidence, proof, argument).
