to be a poser

1. VERB

/ bi ə ˈpoʊzər / (AmE), / biː ə ˈpəʊzə / (BrE)
To pretend to be someone or something one is not, especially to impress others.
pretend to be someone one is not
informal

Examples

At school, he tried to be a poser by wearing trendy clothes he didn’t even like.
Her friends noticed she was starting to be a poser to fit in with the popular group.
Don’t be a poser; people will respect you more if you stay true to yourself.

Synset Examples

Don’t be a poser; just be yourself at the party.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fake - To pretend to be something or someone you are not
  • Antonym: authentic - Being genuine and true to oneself
