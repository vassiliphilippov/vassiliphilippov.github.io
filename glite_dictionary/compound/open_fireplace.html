open fireplace

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊpən ˈfaɪərˌpleɪs / (AmE), / ˈəʊpən ˈfaɪəˌpleɪs / (BrE)
a recess in a wall, open to a room, at the base of a chimney where a fire may be built for heating or cooking
an open recess in a wall for a fire

Examples

They spent the evening by the open fireplace, enjoying the crackling logs and the warmth.
The cottage features an open fireplace that gives the living room a rustic charm.
During winter, we love to cook chestnuts over the open fireplace in the old farmhouse.

Synset Examples

the fireplace was so large you could walk inside it
he laid a fire in the hearth and lit it
the hearth was black with the charcoal of many fires

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hearth - the floor of a fireplace, often extending into a room
  • Antonym: stove - an enclosed heated device for cooking or heating
