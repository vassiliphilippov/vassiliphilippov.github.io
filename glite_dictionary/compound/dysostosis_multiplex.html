dysostosis multiplex

1. NOUN

/ daɪˌsɔːstoʊsɪs ˈmʌltɪplɛks / (AmE), / dɪˌsɒstəʊsɪs ˈmʌltɪplɛks / (BrE)
A rare hereditary disorder caused by a defect in mucopolysaccharide metabolism, resulting in severe skeletal abnormalities and often intellectual disability.
hereditary disorder affecting bone development
medical

Examples

The X-ray showed features consistent with dysostosis multiplex in the child's skeleton.
Patients with dysostosis multiplex often require specialized orthopedic care due to severe bone deformities.
Genetic counseling is recommended for families affected by dysostosis multiplex.

Etymology

From Greek 'dys-' (bad, abnormal) + 'ostosis' (bone formation) and Latin 'multiplex' (manifold, complex).
