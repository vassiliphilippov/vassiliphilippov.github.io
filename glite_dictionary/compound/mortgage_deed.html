mortgage deed

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɔrɡɪdʒ did / (AmE), / ˈmɔːɡɪdʒ diːd / (BrE)
A legal document that gives a lender a claim on property as security for a loan, embodying the terms of a mortgage.
legal document securing a mortgage loan
legal

Examples

The mortgage deed was signed by both parties at the lawyer's office.
Please review the terms outlined in the mortgage deed before agreeing to the loan.
She found a copy of the original mortgage deed among her family's papers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deed - a legal document transferring ownership of property
  • Synonym: mortgage - a legal agreement by which a bank lends money for property
