tool steel

1. NOUN

/ tuﾋ人 stiﾋ人 / (AmE), / tuﾋ人 stiﾋ人 / (BrE)
a hard, tough alloy steel used for making tools and capable of retaining a sharp cutting edge
a hard alloy steel for making tools

Examples

High-quality tool steel is essential for manufacturing precision cutting instruments.
The workshop ordered a new batch of tool steel to make custom dies for stamping.
Because of its hardness and durability, tool steel is preferred for making chisels and blades.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: carbon steel - steel with a high carbon content, often compared with other types of steel
  • Degree Higher: stainless steel - a corrosion-resistant steel alloy containing chromium
