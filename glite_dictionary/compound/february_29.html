February 29

1. NOUN

/ ˌfɛbjuˈɛri ˈtwɛnti ˈnaɪn / (AmE), / ˌfɛbjuˈɛːri ˈtwɛnti ˈnaɪn / (BrE)
the extra day added to the calendar in a leap year, occurring only once every four years
the extra day in a leap year

Examples

My cousin was born on February 29, so she only has a birthday every four years.
Leap Day, also known as February 29, helps to keep our calendar in sync with the Earth’s orbit.
Many people consider February 29 a special occasion for celebrations and proposals.
