smart money

1. NOUN

/ smɑrt ˈmʌni / (AmE), / smɑːt ˈmʌni / (BrE)
money that is invested or bet by people with expert knowledge, experience, or insider information, and is therefore expected to make profitable choices
money invested by experts or insiders

Examples

The smart money is betting on the company’s stock to rise after the merger.
If you want to know which horse will win, watch where the smart money goes.
Most analysts agree that the smart money has already moved into the technology sector.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dumb money - money invested by inexperienced or uninformed people
Source: WordNet

2. NOUN

/ smɑrt ˈmʌni / (AmE), / smɑːt ˈmʌni / (BrE)
money awarded as compensation in addition to actual damages, intended to punish the wrongdoer in cases of malicious or willful misconduct (punitive damages).
punitive damages awarded as legal compensation
legal

Examples

The court awarded smart money to the plaintiff to punish the company's intentional misconduct.
In addition to actual losses, the jury decided on a significant amount of smart money.
The judge explained that smart money serves as a warning to others who might act maliciously.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: punitive damages - compensation awarded to punish the wrongdoer, not just to compensate the victim

Etymology

From 'smart' meaning to cause pain or punishment, referring to money given as a penalty.
