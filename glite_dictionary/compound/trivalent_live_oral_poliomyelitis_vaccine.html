trivalent live oral poliomyelitis vaccine

1. NOUN

/ traɪˈveɪlənt laɪv ˈɔːrəl ˌpoʊlioʊˈmaɪəlɪtɪs vækˈsiːn / (AmE), / traɪˈveɪlənt lʌɪv ˈɔːrəl ˌpəʊlɪəʊˈmʌɪəlɪtɪs vakˈsiːn / (BrE)
an oral vaccine containing live, attenuated strains of three types of poliovirus, used to provide immunity against poliomyelitis
oral polio vaccine with three virus types
medical

Examples

The trivalent live oral poliomyelitis vaccine was widely used in mass immunization campaigns.
After introducing the trivalent live oral poliomyelitis vaccine, the incidence of polio cases dropped dramatically.
She received the trivalent live oral poliomyelitis vaccine as part of her childhood immunizations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oral polio vaccine - a vaccine taken by mouth to prevent polio
  • Antonym: inactivated poliovirus vaccine - a polio vaccine with inactivated virus, given by injection

Etymology

From tri- (three), valence (capacity), and Greek polios (gray) + myelos (marrow) + -itis (inflammation); vaccine from Latin vaccinus (‘from cows’), due to early cowpox vaccines.
