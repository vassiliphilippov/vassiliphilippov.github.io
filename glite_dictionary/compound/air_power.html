air power

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛr ˌpaʊər / (AmE), / ˈeə ˌpaʊə / (BrE)
The combined military strength or capability of a nation's air force, especially as represented by its aircraft.
a nation's military aviation strength

Examples

The country's air power was a decisive factor in winning the conflict.
Nations invest heavily in air power to maintain a strategic military advantage.
During the parade, the display of air power impressed both the public and foreign observers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: air force - the branch of a nation's armed services that conducts military operations in the air
