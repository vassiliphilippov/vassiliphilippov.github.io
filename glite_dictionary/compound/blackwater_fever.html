blackwater fever

1. NOUN

/ ˈblækˌwɑːtər ˈfiːvər / (AmE), / ˈblækwɔːtə ˈfiːvə / (BrE)
a severe and often fatal form of malaria marked by the destruction of red blood cells, kidney failure, and dark-colored urine
severe malaria with dark urine
medical

Examples

The doctor suspected blackwater fever after observing the patient's dark urine and history of malaria.
Untreated, blackwater fever can quickly progress to kidney failure and death.
Cases of blackwater fever have become rare due to effective malaria treatment and prevention.

Etymology

From the dark color of the urine ('blackwater') observed in patients, referring to hemoglobinuria; 'fever' for the recurrent high temperature.
