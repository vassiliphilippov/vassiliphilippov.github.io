lad's love

1. NOUN

/ lædz lʌv / (AmE), / lædz lʌv / (BrE)
an aromatic herb (Artemisia abrotanum) of temperate Eurasia and North Africa, with a bitter taste, used in making the liqueur absinthe
aromatic Eurasian herb used in absinthe

Examples

The leaves of lad's love were often used for their pleasant scent in old herb gardens.
Some people add lad's love to homemade herbal teas, though it can be quite bitter.
In Victorian times, lad's love was commonly planted near doorways to keep insects away.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: southernwood - common name for Artemisia abrotanum, the same plant
  • Degree Higher: absinthe - anise-flavored spirit containing wormwood and other herbs
