Alexandrian laurel

1. NOUN

/ əˌlɛkˈsændɹi.ən ˈlɔrəl / (AmE), / əˌlɛkˈsændɹi.ən ˈlɔːrəl / (BrE)
A tree native to southern India and Malaysia, bearing racemes of fragrant white flowers.
tropical tree with fragrant white flowers

Examples

The Alexandrian laurel is prized for its glossy leaves and aromatic blossoms in tropical gardens.
Coastal villages in southern India plant the Alexandrian laurel to prevent soil erosion along the shoreline.
The timber from the Alexandrian laurel is valued for woodworking and boatbuilding.

Etymology

Named for Alexandria, with 'laurel' denoting the resemblance of its leaves to those of laurel trees.
