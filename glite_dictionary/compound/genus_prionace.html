genus Prionace

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs praɪˈoʊ.nəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs praɪˈəʊ.neɪs / (BrE)
A genus of sharks that includes the blue shark, characterized by a slender body and long pectoral fins.
genus of sharks including the blue shark

Examples

Researchers found that the genus Prionace is represented primarily by the blue shark.
The genus Prionace plays an important role in marine ecosystems as a pelagic predator.
Specimens from the genus Prionace were collected for the study of shark migration patterns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blue shark - a species of shark in the genus Prionace, known for its slender blue body

Etymology

From New Latin 'Prionace', the genus name in biological taxonomy.
