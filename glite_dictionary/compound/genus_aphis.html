genus Aphis

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈeɪ.fɪs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈæ.fɪs / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Aphididae, including aphid species that are injurious to fruit trees and vegetables.
type genus of aphids

Examples

Many gardeners consider the genus Aphis to be a serious pest of apple trees.
The genus Aphis includes insects that damage crops by sucking plant sap.
Researchers studied the life cycle of several species within the genus Aphis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aphid - small sap-sucking insect of the family Aphididae

Etymology

From Latin 'aphis', meaning 'plant-louse'.
