white campion

1. NOUN

/ waɪt ˈkæmp.i.ən / (AmE), / waɪt ˈkæm.pɪ.ən / (BrE)
a bluish-green herb with sticky stems and clusters of large white flowers that open in the evening, having a swollen calyx; sometimes classified in the genus Lychnis
a plant with sticky stems and white evening-opening flowers

Examples

The hedgerows were full of white campion in bloom during late spring.
We identified the white campion by its distinctive inflated calyx and sticky stems.
At dusk, the white campion flowers open, attracting night-flying moths.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catchfly - a plant of the same or similar genus with sticky stems
