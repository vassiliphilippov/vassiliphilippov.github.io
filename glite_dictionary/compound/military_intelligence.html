military intelligence

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪlɪˌtɛri ɪnˈtɛlədʒəns / (AmE), / ˈmɪlɪtəri ɪnˈtɛlɪdʒəns / (BrE)
an agency of the armed forces responsible for gathering, analyzing, and utilizing information of strategic or tactical military value
armed forces agency for strategic information

Examples

The government relied on military intelligence to anticipate enemy movements during the conflict.
Without reliable military intelligence, the operation would have been far riskier.
Her experience in military intelligence made her a valuable asset to the team.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intelligence agency - organization responsible for gathering secret information

2. NOUN

/ ˌmɪlɪˌtɛri ɪnˈtɛlədʒəns / (AmE), / ˌmɪlɪˌtɛəri ɪnˈtɛlɪdʒəns / (BrE)
Information collected and analyzed about the armed forces or activities of another country, used to plan and conduct military strategies and operations.
information on military forces or activities

Examples

The general relied on updated military intelligence to plan the surprise attack.
Advancements in technology have dramatically changed how military intelligence is gathered and analyzed.
Without accurate military intelligence, the operation's risks increased significantly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: espionage - the practice of spying to obtain secret information, especially about a government or a business
