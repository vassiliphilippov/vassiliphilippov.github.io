genus Colutea

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs koʊˈluː.ti.ə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs kɒˈluː.tɪ.ə / (BrE)
A small genus of Eurasian shrubs with yellow flowers and inflated, bladder-like seed pods.
genus of Eurasian shrubs with yellow flowers and bladdery pods

Examples

The genus Colutea is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
Several species within the genus Colutea produce distinctive, inflated seed pods.
Botanists study the genus Colutea to understand adaptations in arid environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Colutea - Eurasian shrub genus with yellow flowers and bladder-like pods

Etymology

From Latin 'Colutea', the scientific name for the type genus, possibly from Greek 'kolutea' (a kind of plant).
