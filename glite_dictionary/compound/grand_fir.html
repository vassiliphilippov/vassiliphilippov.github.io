grand fir

1. NOUN

/ ɡrænd fɝː / (AmE), / ɡrænd fɜː / (BrE)
A tall fir tree native to the Pacific coast of northwestern North America, with long, curving branches and deep green foliage.
a tall fir tree of northwestern North America

Examples

The forest was dense with towering grand fir and cedar trees.
We identified a mature grand fir by its long, fragrant needles and smooth bark.
Conservationists are working to protect old-growth grand fir habitats in the Pacific Northwest.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: fir - any tree of the genus Abies
