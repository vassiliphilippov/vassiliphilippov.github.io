Browning automatic rifle

1. NOUN

/ ˈbraʊnɪŋ ˌɔtəˌmætɪk ˈraɪfəl / (AmE), / ˈbraʊnɪŋ ˌɔːtəˌmætɪk ˈraɪfəl / (BrE)
a portable .30 caliber automatic rifle, operated by gas pressure and fed from a magazine, that was used by United States forces in World War I, World War II, and the Korean War
US military automatic rifle (early 20th century)

Examples

The Browning automatic rifle was a standard squad weapon for U.S. forces during World War II.
He displayed a restored Browning automatic rifle from his grandfather’s collection.
Soldiers relied on the Browning automatic rifle for its firepower and reliability in combat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: automatic rifle - a rifle that loads and fires automatically

Etymology

Named after John M. Browning, the American firearms designer who invented it in 1917.
