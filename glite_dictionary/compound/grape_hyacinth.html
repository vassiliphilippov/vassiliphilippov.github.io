grape hyacinth

1. NOUN

/ ɡreɪp ˈhaɪəsɪnθ / (AmE), / ɡreɪp ˈhaɪəsɪnθ / (BrE)
A plant of the genus Muscari, especially Muscari armeniacum, with dense spikes of small, usually blue, bell-shaped flowers that resemble a cluster of grapes and bloom in early spring.
spring bulb with blue, grape-like flowers

Examples

The grape hyacinth blooms in early spring, adding a splash of blue to the garden.
Clusters of grape hyacinths can be found growing wild in meadows across Europe.
She planted several bulbs of grape hyacinth along the garden path for their vibrant color.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hyacinth - a bulbous plant with fragrant bell-shaped flowers
