Canary wine

1. NOUN

/ kəˈnɛri waɪn / (AmE), / kəˈneəri waɪn / (BrE)
a sweet white wine made in the Canary Islands, historically prized in England and elsewhere
sweet white wine from the Canary Islands

Examples

In Shakespeare's time, Canary wine was considered one of the finest drinks at English banquets.
He ordered a glass of Canary wine to go with his dessert.
The merchant imported casks of Canary wine from the Spanish archipelago.

Etymology

Named after the Canary Islands, the place of origin.
