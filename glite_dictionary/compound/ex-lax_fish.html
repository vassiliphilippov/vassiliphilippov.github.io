ex-lax fish

1. NOUN

/ ɛks læks fɪʃ / (AmE), / ɛks læks fɪʃ / (BrE)
A humorous term for a fish dish, or fish itself, that is believed to cause digestive issues, particularly laxative effects.
humorous term for fish causing digestive issues
informal slang

Examples

He joked that the ex-lax fish gave him the fastest stomach relief of his life.
After eating the ex-lax fish, several people needed to rush to the bathroom.
Locals warn tourists to avoid the infamous ex-lax fish at that seaside restaurant.

Synset Examples

We ordered the ex-lax fish, and it was surprisingly tasty.
The restaurant's ex-lax fish caught everyone's attention.
I heard the ex-lax fish dish is a local specialty.

Etymology

From the brand name 'Ex-Lax' (a laxative), used humorously to describe fish with a laxative effect.
