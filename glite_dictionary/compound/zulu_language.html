Zulu language

1. NOUN

/ ˈzuːluː ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈzuːluː ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
a Bantu language spoken chiefly in southeastern Africa, particularly in South Africa, and known for its rich literary tradition
Bantu language of southeastern Africa

Examples

She learned to speak Zulu language while working in South Africa.
The novel was originally written in the Zulu language and later translated into English.
Many schools in KwaZulu-Natal offer classes in the Zulu language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Xhosa language - a closely related Bantu language spoken in South Africa

Etymology

From the Zulu people's name for themselves, amaZulu, and English 'language'.
