grad school

1. NOUN

/ ɡræd skuːl / (AmE), / ɡræd skuːl / (BrE)
a school or program at a university where students study for degrees beyond a bachelor's degree, such as a master's or doctorate
postgraduate school program

Examples

Sarah plans to apply to several grad schools after finishing her undergraduate degree.
He spent five years in grad school working on his PhD in physics.
Many students take a break between college and grad school to gain work experience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: graduate school - a school for advanced academic degrees

Etymology

Shortened form of 'graduate school', from Latin 'graduatus' (having a degree) + 'school'.
