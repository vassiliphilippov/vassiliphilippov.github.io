measuring unit

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛʒərɪŋ ˈjuːnɪt / (AmE), / ˈmɛʒərɪŋ ˈjuːnɪt / (BrE)
a standard quantity used as a basis for measurement, such as a meter, gram, or second
standard quantity for measurement

Examples

The meter is a measuring unit used to determine length in the metric system.
We use different measuring units for weight, such as kilograms and pounds.
Understanding each measuring unit is important when converting between different systems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unit of measurement - a standard quantity used to express a physical quantity
