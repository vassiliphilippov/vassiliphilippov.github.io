prawn sandwich brigade

1. NOUN

/ prɔn ˈsændwɪtʃ brɪˈɡeɪd / (AmE), / prɔːn ˈsænwɪtʃ brɪˈɡeɪd / (BrE)
A group of people, especially in sports or social settings, considered snobbish or pretentious because of their selective, often fussy eating habits or perceived lack of genuine enthusiasm.
group seen as pretentious or snobbish eaters
british

Examples

The prawn sandwich brigade were criticized for showing little real interest in the match.
He accused the new directors of being part of the prawn sandwich brigade.
Some fans resent the presence of the prawn sandwich brigade in the exclusive boxes.

Synset Examples

The prawn sandwich brigade was notorious for their picky eating habits.
During lunch, the prawn sandwich brigade gathered to discuss their favorite dishes.
He jokingly referred to his friends as the prawn sandwich brigade at the picnic.

Etymology

Originated in British football culture, referencing the supposed dietary habits of upper-class spectators.
