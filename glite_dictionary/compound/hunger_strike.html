hunger strike

1. NOUN

/ ˈhʌŋɡɚ ˌstraɪk / (AmE), / ˈhʌŋɡə ˌstraɪk / (BrE)
A voluntary refusal to eat, carried out as a form of protest, often for political or social reasons.
refusal to eat as protest

Examples

The prisoners began a hunger strike to demand better living conditions.
She went on a hunger strike to protest the government's decision.
Several activists participated in a hunger strike to raise awareness for their cause.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fast - voluntary abstinence from food for a period of time
