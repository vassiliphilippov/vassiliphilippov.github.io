Meuse-Argonne Offensive

1. PROPN

/ mjuz ɑrˈɡɑn əˈfɛnsɪv / (AmE), / mjʊːz ˌɑːˈɡɒn əˈfɛnsɪv / (BrE)
a major American-led military campaign on the Western Front of World War I (1918) in which Allied forces pushed German armies back and contributed to the end of the war.
major American campaign in WWI, 1918

Examples

The Meuse-Argonne Offensive was one of the largest operations involving American troops during World War I.
General Pershing commanded the Allied forces in the Meuse-Argonne Offensive, which ultimately led to the German retreat.
Many historians consider the Meuse-Argonne Offensive a turning point that hastened the end of the war.

Etymology

From the names of the Meuse River and Argonne Forest in France, the locations of the operation.

2. NOUN

/ mjuːz ɑːrˈɡɑːn əˈfɛnsɪv / (AmE), / mjuːz ɑːˈɡɒn əˈfɛnsɪv / (BrE)
a major American military campaign in World War I (1918), where US troops under General Pershing advanced against German forces; the largest operation of the American Expeditionary Forces in the war
major World War I American military campaign

Examples

The Meuse-Argonne Offensive was a turning point in World War I for the Allied powers.
Many American soldiers lost their lives during the Meuse-Argonne Offensive in 1918.
General Pershing commanded the troops during the Meuse-Argonne Offensive.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: battle - an armed conflict between groups
  • Antonym: armistice - an agreement to stop fighting, especially in war

Etymology

From Meuse, a river in France, and Argonne, a nearby forest region; refers to the geographic area of the battle.
