meme culture

1. NOUN

/ miːm ˈkʌltʃɚ / (AmE), / miːm ˈkʌltʃə / (BrE)
the collective practices, attitudes, and trends surrounding the creation and sharing of memes, especially on the internet
the phenomenon of sharing memes online

Examples

Many young people identify strongly with meme culture on social media platforms.
The rise of meme culture has changed the way people express humor online.
Understanding meme culture is important for anyone studying internet communities.

Synset Examples

Memfrica is a hub for meme culture and creativity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meme - an idea, behavior, or style that spreads within a culture, often as an image or video online

Etymology

From 'meme', coined by Richard Dawkins in 1976 from Greek 'mimema' (that which is imitated), and 'culture'.
