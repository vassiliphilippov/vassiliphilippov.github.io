Orange Volunteers

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔːrɪndʒ ˌvɑːlənˈtɪrz / (AmE), / ˈɒrɪndʒ ˌvɒlənˈtɪəz / (BrE)
a Protestant paramilitary group in Northern Ireland, active mainly in the late 20th and early 21st centuries, known for attacking Catholic interests and opposing political settlements with Irish nationalists
Protestant paramilitary group in Northern Ireland

Examples

The Orange Volunteers claimed responsibility for several attacks in the early 2000s.
Authorities monitored the activities of the Orange Volunteers due to their involvement in sectarian violence.
Many community leaders condemned the actions taken by the Orange Volunteers during the unrest.

Etymology

Named after the Protestant Orange Order and their practice of forming voluntary groups to support its aims.
