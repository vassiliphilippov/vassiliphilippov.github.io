black salsify

1. NOUN

/ blæk ˈsælsəfi / (AmE), / blæk ˈsælsɪfi / (BrE)
A perennial European plant with long black edible roots shaped like carrots, narrow leaves, and yellow solitary flowers, cultivated as a vegetable.
edible root vegetable plant

Examples

The chef prepared a creamy soup using black salsify as the key ingredient.
Gardeners recommend planting black salsify in deep, loose soil for the best harvest.
In some European markets, black salsify is prized for its unique flavor and nutritional value.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: salsify - a related plant with edible roots, usually lighter in color

Etymology

From French 'salsifis noir', adopted into English from botanical Latin.

2. NOUN

/ blæk ˈsælsəˌfaɪ / (AmE), / blæk ˈsælsɪfaɪ / (BrE)
a plant with long, black-skinned edible roots, often cultivated for use as a vegetable
edible plant with black-skinned roots

Examples

Black salsify is often called 'the oyster plant' due to its unique taste when cooked.
The chef prepared a creamy soup with roasted black salsify as the main ingredient.
Farmers harvest black salsify roots in late autumn for the best flavor.
