timber tree

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɪmbɚ ˌtri / (AmE), / ˈtɪmbə ˌtriː / (BrE)
a tree that is valued for its wood, especially as a source of lumber
tree valued for its wood

Examples

The forest is rich with timber trees that supply much of the region's lumber.
Planting new timber trees is essential for the sustainability of the wood industry.
Some species are prized as timber trees because of their strong and durable wood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lumber tree - tree valued for providing lumber
  • Antonym: fruit tree - tree cultivated for its edible fruit
