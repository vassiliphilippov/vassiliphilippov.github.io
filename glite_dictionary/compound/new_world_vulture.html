New World vulture

1. NOUN

/ nuː wɝːld ˈvʌltʃɚ / (AmE), / njuː wɜːld ˈvʌltʃə / (BrE)
a large bird of prey found in the Americas, similar in appearance to Old World vultures but belonging to a different family
large American bird of prey, vulture family

Examples

The New World vulture has an excellent sense of smell, unlike its Old World counterparts.
Conservationists are working to protect several species of New World vulture from extinction.
Some South American farmers believe that the presence of a New World vulture is a sign of good fortune.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Old World vulture - large bird of prey found in Europe, Asia, and Africa, similar in appearance to New World vultures
  • Synonym: vulture - a large scavenging bird of prey
