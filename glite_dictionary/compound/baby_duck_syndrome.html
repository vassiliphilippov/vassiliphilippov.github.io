baby duck syndrome

1. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪbi dʌk sɪnˌdroʊm / (AmE), / ˈbeɪbi dʌk sɪnˌdrəʊm / (BrE)
a tendency to become attached to the first system, method, or situation encountered, making it difficult to adapt to something new
attachment to initial method or situation

Examples

His tendency to stick with familiar routines at work is a classic case of baby duck syndrome.
The new software team struggled with baby duck syndrome, resisting changes to their original tools.
Many users exhibit baby duck syndrome when adapting to new technology, preferring what they learned first.

Synset Examples

He's suffering from baby duck syndrome in his new job.
He realized his baby duck syndrome was holding him back.
Her baby duck syndrome made her overly dependent on others.

Etymology

From the tendency of ducklings to imprint on the first moving object they see, combined with the word 'syndrome' meaning a characteristic pattern or behavior.
