pale coral root

1. NOUN

/ peɪl ˈkɔrəl ˌrʊt / (AmE), / peɪl ˈkɒrəl ˌruːt / (BrE)
a plant with nearly leafless pale yellow to greenish stems and similarly colored flowers with white lower lips, found from northern New Mexico to Alaska
a nearly leafless, pale-stemmed North American orchid

Examples

We spotted a cluster of pale coral root growing beside the hiking trail.
The pale coral root is easily recognized by its almost leafless yellow stems.
Botanists study the habitat and distribution of pale coral root across North America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coral root - any of several orchids of the genus Corallorhiza
