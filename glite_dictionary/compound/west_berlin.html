West Berlin

1. PROPN

/ wɛst bɚˈlɪn / (AmE), / wɛst bəˈlɪn / (BrE)
the western part of Berlin that was controlled by the United States, Britain, and France from the end of World War II until German reunification in 1990.
former western sector of Berlin (1945–1990)

Examples

After World War II, West Berlin became an important symbol of democracy during the Cold War.
Many artists and intellectuals moved to West Berlin because of its unique cultural scene.
The Berlin Wall separated West Berlin from the surrounding East German territory.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: East Berlin - the eastern part of Berlin under Soviet control from 1945 to 1990
