lanthanide series

1. NOUN

/ ˈlænθəˌnaɪd ˈsɪriːz / (AmE), / ˈlanθəniːd ˈsɪəriːz / (BrE)
a group of rare-earth elements with atomic numbers 57 through 71, displaying similar properties to lanthanum
group of rare-earth elements, atomic numbers 57–71

Examples

The lanthanide series is essential for many high-tech electronic applications.
Elements in the lanthanide series are known for their strong magnetic properties.
Researchers are studying the lanthanide series for use in advanced optical materials.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rare earth element - any of a set of chemically similar elements, including the lanthanides

Etymology

Term formed from 'lanthanide' (from lanthanum, first element in the group) and 'series'.
