monocarpous plant

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɑnəˈkɑrpəs plænt / (AmE), / ˌmɒnəˈkɑːpəs plɑːnt / (BrE)
A plant that bears fruit only once before dying.
plant that fruits once and dies

Examples

The agave is a well-known monocarpous plant that flowers once before dying.
Bamboo is another example of a monocarpous plant, often dying after its rare flowering event.
Researchers are studying how the life cycle of a monocarpous plant affects its ecosystem.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: polycarpous plant - a plant that bears fruit multiple times in its life

Etymology

From Greek 'monos' (single) + 'karpos' (fruit).
