Our Lady's bedstraw

1. NOUN

/ aʊɚ ˈleɪdiz ˈbɛdˌstrɔ / (AmE), / aʊə ˈleɪdɪz ˈbɛdstrɔː / (BrE)
a yellow-flowered perennial herb (Galium verum) native to Europe, Asia, and North America, also known as lady's bedstraw or yellow bedstraw
yellow-flowered perennial herb (Galium verum)

Examples

In the summer meadow, clusters of Our Lady's bedstraw glowed bright yellow among the tall grasses.
Her wildflower bouquet included several sprigs of Our Lady's bedstraw, adding a delicate fragrance to the room.
Botanists observed that Our Lady's bedstraw thrives in well-drained, chalky soils across the countryside.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lady's bedstraw - alternative common name for Galium verum
  • Synonym: yellow bedstraw - another common name for Galium verum

Etymology

Named for its historical use as bedding and its association with the Virgin Mary in Christian tradition.
