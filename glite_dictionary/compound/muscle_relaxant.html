muscle relaxant

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌsəl rɪˈlæksənt / (AmE), / ˈmʌsl rɪˈlæksənt / (BrE)
a drug that reduces muscle tension by decreasing nerve transmission or the excitability of muscles
drug that relaxes muscles
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed a muscle relaxant to help ease her back spasms.
Some muscle relaxants can cause drowsiness as a side effect.
After the injury, he was given a muscle relaxant to relieve the pain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tranquilizer - a drug that has a calming effect
  • Antonym: stimulant - a drug that increases body or organ activity

Etymology

From 'muscle' (Latin musculus) and 'relaxant', from Latin relaxare 'to loosen'.
