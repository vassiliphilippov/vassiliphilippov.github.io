titanic acid

1. NOUN

/ taɪˈtænɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / taɪˈtænɪk ˈasɪd / (BrE)
a weak, white, water-containing acid that is a hydrated form of titanium dioxide, with the general formula H₂TiO₃ or derivatives
a hydrated acid form of titanium dioxide

Examples

The chemist prepared titanic acid by adding ammonia to titanium sulfate solution.
Pure titanic acid appears as a soft, white precipitate in laboratory reactions.
Research on the properties of titanic acid has led to advancements in materials science.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: titanium dioxide - a white, inorganic compound used as a pigment and in sunscreen

Etymology

From titanium (the element) plus -ic, patterned after systematic chemical nomenclature.
