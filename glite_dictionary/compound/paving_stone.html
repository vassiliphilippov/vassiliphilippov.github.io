paving stone

1. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪvɪŋ stoʊn / (AmE), / ˈpeɪvɪŋ stəʊn / (BrE)
a flat piece of stone used to create a hard, even surface for roads, paths, or floors
flat stone for paving surfaces

Examples

A loose paving stone caused him to trip and fall.
They replaced the old sidewalk with new paving stones.
Rainwater collected between the paving stones after the storm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paver - a stone, brick, or block used for paving
  • Synonym: cobblestone - naturally rounded stone formerly used for paving streets
