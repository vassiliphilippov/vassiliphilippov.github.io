diffraction grating

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈfrækʃən ˈɡreɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / dɪˈfrækʃən ˈɡreɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
an optical device consisting of a surface with many closely spaced parallel grooves, used to disperse light into its component wavelengths and produce a spectrum
optical device dispersing light into a spectrum

Examples

A diffraction grating can be used to separate white light into its different colors during experiments.
Modern spectrometers often employ a diffraction grating instead of a prism to analyze light.
The quality of a diffraction grating depends on the precision of its parallel grooves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prism - transparent optical element that separates light into a spectrum

Etymology

From 'diffraction' (Latin diffringere, to break in pieces) and 'grating' (from grate, parallel bars).
