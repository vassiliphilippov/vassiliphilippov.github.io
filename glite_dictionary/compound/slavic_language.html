Slavic language

1. NOUN

/ ˈslævɪk ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈslævɪk ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
A group of closely related languages that form a branch of the Indo-European language family, including Russian, Polish, Czech, and others.
branch of the Indo-European languages

Examples

Russian is the most widely spoken Slavic language in the world.
The Slavic language family includes Polish, Czech, and Bulgarian.
Many linguists study the similarities between each Slavic language and its neighbors.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Indo-European language - major language family including most European and some Asian languages

Etymology

From Medieval Latin Slāvī (the Slavs) + language.
