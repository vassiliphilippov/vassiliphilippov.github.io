wild medlar

1. NOUN

/ waɪld ˈmɛdlɚ / (AmE), / waɪld ˈmɛdlə / (BrE)
A small deciduous tree native to southern Africa, bearing edible fruit.
southern African tree with edible fruit

Examples

The fruit of the wild medlar is often used to make preserves and jams in rural communities.
We spotted a grove of wild medlar trees while hiking in South Africa.
After ripening, the wild medlar develops a sweet, unique flavor appreciated by local wildlife and people alike.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: medlar - a small deciduous tree with edible fruit; genus Mespilus, or similar trees
