Negro spiritual

1. NOUN

/ ˈniːɡroʊ ˈspɪrɪtʃuəl / (AmE), / ˈniːɡrəʊ ˈspɪrɪtjʊəl / (BrE)
a type of religious song originating among Black people in the southern United States, expressing faith and hope, often sung during slavery
religious song developed by Black Americans
archaic

Examples

Many Negro spirituals were passed down orally from one generation to the next.
The choir performed a moving arrangement of a traditional Negro spiritual.
Famous Negro spirituals include 'Swing Low, Sweet Chariot' and 'Go Down Moses'.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spiritual - a religious song, especially of the Black American tradition

Etymology

From 'Negro', an old term for a Black person (now outdated), and 'spiritual', relating to religious songs.
