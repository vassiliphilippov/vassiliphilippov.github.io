sexual abstention

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛkʃuəl æbˈstɛnʃən / (AmE), / ˈsɛkʃuəl æbˈstɛnʃən / (BrE)
the practice of refraining from sexual activity, often for religious, moral, or personal reasons
refraining from sexual activity

Examples

Many religious communities encourage sexual abstention before marriage.
Her vow of sexual abstention was an important part of her spiritual journey.
Practicing sexual abstention can involve significant personal discipline and commitment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: celibacy - the state of abstaining from marriage and sexual relations, often for religious reasons
  • Synonym: chastity - the state or practice of refraining from extramarital or all sexual intercourse

Etymology

From Latin 'abstinentia' (abstinence) combined with 'sexual', from Latin 'sexus' (sex).
