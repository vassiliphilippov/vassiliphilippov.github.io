winter melon

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪntɚ ˌmɛlən / (AmE), / ˈwɪntə ˌmɛlən / (BrE)
A large, round or oblong fruit with a smooth white or pale green rind and white or greenish flesh, grown for food in Asia and other regions; also refers to the plant that produces it.
a type of large, pale-fleshed melon

Examples

The soup was made with cubes of winter melon and dried shrimp.
She bought a large winter melon from the Asian market to make a traditional stew.
Winter melon can be cooked or candied for use in desserts and drinks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wax gourd - another common name for winter melon

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɪn.tɚ ˌmɛl.ən / (AmE), / ˈwɪn.tə ˌmɛl.ən / (BrE)
a large, round or oblong fruit with green skin and pale green to orange flesh, commonly used in Asian cooking and known for its long shelf life.
a large fruit with green skin and pale flesh

Examples

She made a refreshing soup with winter melon and pork ribs.
The market had a display of large winter melons stacked at the entrance.
After harvesting, winter melon can be stored for several months without spoiling.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: melon - a large, juicy fruit with a hard skin and sweet flesh
