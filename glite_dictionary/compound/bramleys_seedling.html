Bramley's Seedling

1. NOUN

/ ˈbræmliz ˈsiːdlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbræmliz ˈsiːdlɪŋ / (BrE)
A large British variety of apple, typically green and sour, used mainly for cooking.
large British cooking apple variety
british

Examples

The recipe calls for a tart apple like a Bramley's Seedling to give the pie its distinctive flavor.
She bought several Bramley's Seedling apples at the market for her homemade applesauce.
Bramley's Seedling is prized for its large size and excellent cooking qualities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cooking apple - apple variety used mainly for cooking rather than eating raw

Etymology

Named after Matthew Bramley, in whose garden the original tree was grown; 'seedling' refers to its origin from seed.
