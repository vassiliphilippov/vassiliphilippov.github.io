Mount Pisgah

1. PROPN

/ maʊnt ˈpɪsɡə / (AmE), / maʊnt ˈpɪsɡə / (BrE)
A mountain in Jordan mentioned in the Bible as the vantage point from which Moses viewed the Promised Land; also the name of various hills and locations in English-speaking countries.
biblical mountain; name of various hills

Examples

At dawn, the hikers reached the summit of Mount Pisgah and were rewarded with a spectacular view.
On their road trip, they stopped at Mount Pisgah State Park for a picnic lunch.
The story of Moses on Mount Pisgah is often referenced in religious discussions.

Synset Examples

We reached Mount Pisgah just in time for sunset.

Etymology

From Hebrew Pisgah, the biblical mountain where Moses viewed Canaan.
