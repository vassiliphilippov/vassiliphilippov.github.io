rocket fuel

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɑːkɪt ˌfjuːəl / (AmE), / ˈrɒkɪt ˌfjʊː.əl / (BrE)
a substance that is burned to produce the energy required to propel a rocket
fuel used to propel rockets

Examples

NASA scientists developed a new rocket fuel to increase launch efficiency and safety.
The rocket fuel must be handled carefully due to its highly explosive nature.
Advances in rocket fuel technology have made space travel more affordable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: propellant - a substance used to propel something, especially in rockets
