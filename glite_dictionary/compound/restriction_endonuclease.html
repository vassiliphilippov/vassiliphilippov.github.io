restriction endonuclease

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈstrɪk.ʃən ˌɛn.doʊˈnu.kliː.eɪs / (AmE), / rɪˈstrɪk.ʃən ˌen.dəʊˈnjuː.kli.eɪs / (BrE)
an enzyme that cuts DNA at specific sequences, producing defined fragments; important in genetic engineering and molecular biology
enzyme cutting DNA at specific sites
medical

Examples

A restriction endonuclease can be used to cut DNA at precise locations for cloning.
Many bacteria use restriction endonucleases as a defense against foreign DNA, such as viruses.
Scientists selected a restriction endonuclease that recognizes a six-base pair sequence for their experiment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: restriction enzyme - an enzyme that cuts DNA at specific recognition sites; synonym for restriction endonuclease

Etymology

From 'restriction' + 'endonuclease'; endonuclease from 'endo-' (within) + 'nuclease' (an enzyme that cleaves nucleic acids).
