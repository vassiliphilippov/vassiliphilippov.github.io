allegro con spirito

1. NOUN

/ əˌlɛɡroʊ kɒn ˈspɪrɪtoʊ / (AmE), / əˌlɛɡrəʊ kɒn ˈspɪrɪtəʊ / (BrE)
(in music) in a lively and spirited manner; to be played briskly and with energy.
lively and spirited in music

Examples

The pianist played the finale allegro con spirito, captivating the entire audience.
The score is marked allegro con spirito, so remember to keep the energy high throughout the movement.
Our conductor asked us to perform the overture allegro con spirito at tonight's concert.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: allegro - fast and lively tempo in music
  • Degree Lower: andante - a moderately slow tempo in music

Etymology

Italian, literally meaning 'fast with spirit'.
