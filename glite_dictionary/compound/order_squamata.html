order Squamata

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdɚ skwəˈmeɪtə / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə skwəˈmeɪtə / (BrE)
the zoological order of diapsid reptiles that includes snakes and lizards
taxonomic group: snakes and lizards
medical

Examples

The order Squamata contains both snakes and lizards, making it one of the largest groups of reptiles.
Researchers have discovered new species within the order Squamata in remote forests.
The order Squamata is distinguished by members having scales and, in most cases, movable quadrate bones.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reptile - class of cold-blooded, scaly vertebrates
  • Degree Lower: order Testudines - order including turtles and tortoises

Etymology

From Latin 'squamata', meaning 'scaled', referring to the scaly skin of members.
