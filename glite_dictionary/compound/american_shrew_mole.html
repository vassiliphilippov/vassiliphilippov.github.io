American shrew mole

1. NOUN

/ əˈmɛrɪkən ʃruː moʊl / (AmE), / əˈmɛrɪkən ʃruː məʊl / (BrE)
a small, greyish-black shrew-like mole native to the United States and Canada, known for its slender body and long snout
small North American shrew-like mole

Examples

The American shrew mole spends most of its life burrowing underground in search of insects.
Scientists observed a population of American shrew moles in the forests of Washington state.
Unlike other moles, the American shrew mole has a particularly long, pointed snout.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shrew mole - a mole resembling a shrew, with a slender body and long nose
