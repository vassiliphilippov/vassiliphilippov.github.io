ground cover

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡraʊnd ˌkʌvɚ / (AmE), / ˈɡraʊnd ˌkʌvə / (BrE)
A low-growing plant that spreads over the ground, often used to cover areas where grass or turf is difficult to grow.
low-growing plant covering soil

Examples

We planted ground cover under the trees where grass would not grow.
The hillside was stabilized using a mix of ground cover and shrubs.
English ivy is a popular ground cover for shady gardens.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡraʊnd ˌkʌvɚ / (AmE), / ˈɡraʊnd ˌkʌvə / (BrE)
low-growing plants that cover the soil, especially in gardens or as a natural part of a forest floor, reducing erosion and weed growth
low, soil-covering plants

Examples

The shaded area beneath the trees was thick with ground cover and ferns.
Gardeners often plant ground cover to help prevent soil erosion on slopes.
Many varieties of ground cover can thrive even in poor, rocky soil.
