Law of Conservation of Ninjutsu

1. NOUN

/ lɔː əv kənˌsɝːveɪʃən əv nɪnˈdʒuːtsuː / (AmE), / lɔː əv kənˌsɜːveɪʃən əv nɪnˈdʒuːtsuː / (BrE)
A humorous principle, especially in anime, gaming, and fandom contexts, stating that the effectiveness of a group of ninjas decreases as their number increases, so one ninja is strongest alone and weakest in a large group.
humorous trope about ninja effectiveness
informal slang

Examples

The Law of Conservation of Ninjutsu explains why a crowd of ninjas is always easily defeated by the hero.
Fans often refer to the Law of Conservation of Ninjutsu when groups of henchmen fail in anime.
During the discussion, he mentioned the Law of Conservation of Ninjutsu to joke about overpowered solo fighters.

Synset Examples

He joked about the Law of Conservation of Ninjutsu during the training.
In gaming, the Law of Conservation of Ninjutsu applies to skill usage.
The teacher explained the Law of Conservation of Ninjutsu in class.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Rule of Cool - a storytelling principle that prioritizes entertaining elements over realism

Etymology

Based on 'law of conservation' in physics, playfully adapted to Japanese 'ninjutsu' (the art of the ninja).
