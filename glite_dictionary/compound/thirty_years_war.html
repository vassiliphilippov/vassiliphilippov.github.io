Thirty Years' War

1. NOUN

/ ˌθɜːrti jɪrz wɔr / (AmE), / ˌθɜːti jɪəz wɔː / (BrE)
a major European conflict (1618–1648) initially fought between Protestant and Catholic states in the Holy Roman Empire, later involving most of Europe
European conflict (1618–1648) involving Protestants and Catholics

Examples

The Thirty Years' War devastated much of Central Europe and significantly altered the political landscape.
Many historians consider the Thirty Years' War one of the most destructive conflicts in European history.
The Peace of Westphalia ended the Thirty Years' War and marked a turning point in international relations.
