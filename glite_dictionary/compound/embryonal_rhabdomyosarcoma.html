embryonal rhabdomyosarcoma

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛm.briˈoʊ.nəl ˌræb.doʊˌmaɪ.oʊ.sɑrˈkoʊ.mə / (AmE), / ˌɛm.brɪˈəʊ.nəl ˌræb.dəʊˌmaɪ.əʊ.sɑːˈkəʊ.mə / (BrE)
a malignant tumor of skeletal muscle tissue that occurs mainly in infants and children
cancer of muscle tissue in infants and children
medical

Examples

The child was diagnosed with embryonal rhabdomyosarcoma after a persistent swelling was noticed in her leg.
Embryonal rhabdomyosarcoma typically affects infants and young children rather than adults.
Early detection of embryonal rhabdomyosarcoma can improve the chances of successful treatment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rhabdomyosarcoma - a malignant tumor of skeletal muscle tissue

Etymology

From 'embryonal' (relating to an embryo) and 'rhabdomyosarcoma' (from Greek rhabdos 'rod', mys 'muscle', sarx 'flesh', and -oma 'tumor').
