Medicaid funds

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛdɪˌkeɪd fʌndz / (AmE), / ˈmɛdɪˌkeɪd fʌndz / (BrE)
public money allocated to pay for Medicaid, the US government program providing health coverage for eligible low-income individuals
funds for US Medicaid program
american legal medical

Examples

The governor announced an increase in Medicaid funds to improve healthcare services.
Auditors are investigating potential misuse of Medicaid funds in several hospitals.
Providers rely on Medicaid funds to deliver care to low-income patients.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Medicaid - the US government health insurance program for low-income people
  • Synonym: public funds - government money for public use

Etymology

From 'Medicaid', formed from 'medical' and 'aid', plus 'funds'.
