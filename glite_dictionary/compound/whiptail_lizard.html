whiptail lizard

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪp.teɪl ˈlɪz.ərd / (AmE), / ˈwɪp.teɪl ˈlɪz.əd / (BrE)
Any of various agile and alert lizards of the family Teiidae found in the Americas, known for their long, slender tails.
agile New World lizard with a long tail

Examples

The whiptail lizard darted across the rocky desert floor at remarkable speed.
Many whiptail lizards can reproduce without males through a process called parthenogenesis.
Scientists studying the whiptail lizard have discovered unique social behaviors among the females.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: teiid - any lizard of the family Teiidae
