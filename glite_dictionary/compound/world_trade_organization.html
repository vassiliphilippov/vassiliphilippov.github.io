World Trade Organization

1. PROPN

/ wɜrld treɪd ˌɔrɡənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / wɜːld treɪd ˌɔːɡənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
an international organization based in Geneva that monitors and enforces rules governing global trade between nations
international body regulating global trade

Examples

The World Trade Organization plays a crucial role in resolving international trade disputes.
China became a member of the World Trade Organization in 2001.
Critics argue that the World Trade Organization favors wealthier nations over poorer ones.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: WTO - abbreviation for World Trade Organization
