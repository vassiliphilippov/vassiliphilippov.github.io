peacock ore

1. NOUN

/ ˈpiː.kɑk ɔr / (AmE), / ˈpiː.kɒk ɔː / (BrE)
a mineral consisting of copper and iron sulfides, known for its iridescent tarnish that resembles a peacock's feathers
iridescent copper-iron sulfide mineral

Examples

The miner found a striking piece of peacock ore glittering among the stones.
Collectors prize peacock ore for its brilliant blues and purples.
The iridescent surface of peacock ore makes it popular in jewelry and mineral displays.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bornite - a copper-iron sulfide mineral also known as peacock ore

Etymology

Named for its bright, iridescent colors resembling peacock feathers.
