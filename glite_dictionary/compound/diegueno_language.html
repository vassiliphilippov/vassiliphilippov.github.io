Diegueno language

1. NOUN

/ diːˈjeɪnoʊ ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / diːˈjeɪnjəʊ ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
The Yuman language traditionally spoken by the Diegueño people of southern California and northern Baja California.
a Yuman language of southern California and Baja California

Examples

Few people today speak the Diegueno language, which is at risk of extinction.
Linguists have been working to document the Diegueno language through interviews with native speakers.
The Diegueno language belongs to the larger Yuman language family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Yuman language - a family of languages native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico

Etymology

From Spanish 'Diegueño', referring to inhabitants near Mission San Diego, plus 'language'.
