dwarf blueberry

1. NOUN

/ dwɔrf ˈbluːˌbɛri / (AmE), / dwɔːf ˈbluːˌbəri / (BrE)
A low-growing deciduous shrub native to northern and alpine North America, with pink to coral-red flowers and edible sweet blue berries.
a low deciduous shrub with blue berries

Examples

The dwarf blueberry thrives in rocky alpine forests with plenty of sunlight.
We picked bowls full of dwarf blueberries during our summer hike in Canada.
Unlike larger blueberries, the dwarf blueberry grows close to the ground and has tiny, sweet fruit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blueberry - an edible blue fruit or the shrub producing it
