to have a way with children

1. VERB

/ hæv ə weɪ wɪð ˈtʃɪldrən / (AmE), / hæv ə weɪ wɪð ˈtʃɪldrən / (BrE)
to have a natural ability to interact well and effectively with children
natural ability to interact with children

Examples

Everyone says Sarah has a way with children because they listen to her and feel comfortable.
The new teacher clearly has a way with children, as her students are always engaged and eager to learn.
If you have a way with children, working at the summer camp will be a perfect fit for you.

Synset Examples

She really has a way with children.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: have a way with - to have a natural talent for dealing well with a particular type of person or situation
