charter court

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɑrtər kɔrt / (AmE), / ˈtʃɑːtə kɔːt / (BrE)
a court established by a charter or one that hears cases involving specific charters
court established by or dealing with charters
legal

Examples

The city council established a charter court to resolve disputes about the municipal charter.
Her case regarding the validity of the school’s charter was heard in the charter court.
Lawyers specializing in constitutional law often represent clients before a charter court.

Synset Examples

She filed her complaint in the charter court yesterday.
The case was brought before the charter court for review.
