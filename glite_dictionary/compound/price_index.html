price index

1. NOUN

/ praɪs ˈɪndɛks / (AmE), / praɪs ˈɪndɛks / (BrE)
A statistical measure that shows changes in the general price level of goods or a specific item over time.
statistical measure of price changes

Examples

Economists use the price index to assess inflation over time.
The government reported that the price index had risen by 2% last year.
A steady price index indicates stable prices in the economy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: consumer price index - an index measuring the average change in prices paid by consumers for goods and services
