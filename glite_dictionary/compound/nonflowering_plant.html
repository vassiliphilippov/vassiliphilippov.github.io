nonflowering plant

1. NOUN

/ nɑnˈflaʊərɪŋ plænt / (AmE), / nɒnˈflaʊərɪŋ plɑːnt / (BrE)
a plant that has vascular tissue and reproduces by spores, rather than producing flowers or seeds
plant with vascular tissue, reproduces by spores

Examples

Ferns and mosses are classic examples of nonflowering plants found in temperate forests.
Unlike most trees, clubmosses are nonflowering plants that do not bear seeds.
Botany students studied the lifecycle of nonflowering plants for their final project.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: flowering plant - a plant that produces flowers and seeds
