Federal Republic of Germany

1. PROPN

/ ˈfɛdərəl rɪˈpʌblɪk əv ˈdʒɝməni / (AmE), / ˈfɛdərəl rɪˈpʌblɪk əv ˈdʒɜːməni / (BrE)
the official name for Germany, a country in north central Europe, established in 1949 and reunified with East Germany in 1990
official name for Germany

Examples

The Federal Republic of Germany joined the European Union as one of its founding members.
After World War II, the Federal Republic of Germany was formed from the western zones of occupation.
Angela Merkel served as chancellor of the Federal Republic of Germany for over a decade.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Germany - a country in central Europe

2. PROPN

/ ˈfɛdərəl rəˈpʌblɪk əv ˈdʒɜrməni / (AmE), / ˈfɛdərəl rɪˈpʌblɪk əv ˈdʒɜːməni / (BrE)
the official name of the country of Germany, located in central Europe; established after World War II and reunified in 1990
official name for Germany, central Europe

Examples

The Federal Republic of Germany became a founding member of the European Union.
After reunification in 1990, Berlin was declared the capital of the Federal Republic of Germany.
Many international companies have their headquarters in the Federal Republic of Germany.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Germany - a country in central Europe; the common name for the Federal Republic of Germany
  • Antonym: East Germany - the former socialist state in eastern Germany before reunification in 1990
  • Antonym: West Germany - the former federal republic in western Germany before reunification in 1990
