bow legs

1. NOUN

/ boʊ lɛɡz / (AmE), / bəʊ lɛɡz / (BrE)
A condition in which the legs curve outward at the knees, causing the feet and ankles to touch while the knees are apart.
outward curvature of the legs
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed him with bow legs after examining his posture.
Children with mild bow legs often grow out of the condition as they age.
She noticed her toddler had developed bow legs and scheduled a check-up.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: knock-knees - knees angle in and touch each other when standing
