least squares

1. NOUN

/ list skwɛrz / (AmE), / liːst skwɛəz / (BrE)
a method in mathematics and statistics for fitting a curve to a set of data points by minimizing the sum of the squares of the distances of the points from the curve
curve-fitting method minimizing squared errors

Examples

The least squares method is commonly used to find the best-fitting line in linear regression.
She applied least squares to analyze the experimental data and determine the optimal coefficients.
Most statistical software includes tools for least squares curve fitting and error analysis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: regression analysis - a set of statistical methods for estimating relationships among variables
  • Degree Higher: maximum likelihood - a method for estimating parameters in statistics by maximizing the likelihood function
  • Degree Lower: mean absolute error - a method that minimizes the average of absolute errors rather than squared errors

Etymology

From the mathematical process of minimizing the 'sum of least squares' of residuals; concept formalized in early 19th-century mathematics.
