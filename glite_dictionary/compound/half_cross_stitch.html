half cross stitch

1. NOUN

/ hæf krɑs stɪtʃ / (AmE), / hɑːf krɒs stɪtʃ / (BrE)
a single stitch made diagonally, typically used in embroidery or needlework as half of a full cross stitch.
diagonal stitch in needlework

Examples

She used a half cross stitch to create subtle shading in the embroidery.
The instructor demonstrated how to make a half cross stitch on the canvas.
You need to alternate between full and half cross stitches to achieve the pattern's effect.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: cross stitch - a stitch that forms an X shape; common in embroidery
