tap dancer

1. NOUN

/ tæp ˈdænsər / (AmE), / tæp ˈdɑːnsə / (BrE)
A person who performs dances in which rhythmic sounds are made by striking the floor with metal plates on the shoes.
person who performs tap dance

Examples

The tap dancer amazed the audience with her fast footwork and infectious energy.
He trained for years to become a professional tap dancer in Broadway shows.
Children watched as the tap dancer demonstrated basic steps during the workshop.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tap dance - a dance in which rhythmical sounds are produced by the feet
  • Antonym: ballet dancer - a person who performs ballet
