diesel-hydraulic locomotive

1. NOUN

/ ˈdiːzəl haɪˈdrɑlɪk ˌloʊkəˈmoʊtɪv / (AmE), / ˈdiːzəl haɪˈdrælɪk ˌləʊkəˈməʊtɪv / (BrE)
a locomotive powered by a diesel engine, with power transmitted to the wheels by a hydraulic transmission system
locomotive with diesel engine and hydraulic transmission

Examples

The diesel-hydraulic locomotive was introduced to improve rail efficiency on non-electrified routes.
Engineers prefer a diesel-hydraulic locomotive for routes requiring frequent starts and stops.
A German manufacturer exported several diesel-hydraulic locomotives in the 1960s.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: diesel-electric locomotive - locomotive powered by a diesel engine using electric transmission
