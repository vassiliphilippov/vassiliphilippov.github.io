marsh St-John's wort

1. NOUN

/ mɑrʃ ˌseɪnt ˈdʒɑnz wɜrt / (AmE), / mɑːʃ ˌseɪnt ˈdʒɒnz wɜːt / (BrE)
A perennial marsh plant of the southeastern United States with pink to mauve flowers, related to the St-John's wort family.
perennial marsh plant of the southern US

Examples

The wetlands were dotted with blooming marsh St-John's wort in early summer.
Botanists observed the marsh St-John's wort thriving near the pond's edge.
We identified several species, including marsh St-John's wort, along our hiking trail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: St-John's wort - Common name for plants of the Hypericum genus, known for yellow flowers

Etymology

Named for its habitat (marsh) and its relation to St-John's wort plants.
