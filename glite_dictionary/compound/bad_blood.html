bad blood

1. NOUN

/ bæd blʌd / (AmE), / bæd blʌd / (BrE)
a feeling of anger or dislike between people that often leads to hostility
hostile feelings between people

Examples

There has been bad blood between the two families for years.
Despite their success, bad blood still lingers within the team.
After the argument, bad blood developed between the colleagues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hostility - strong unfriendly or antagonistic feelings
  • Antonym: goodwill - friendly or cooperative feelings
