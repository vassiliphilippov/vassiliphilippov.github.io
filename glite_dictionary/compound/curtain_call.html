curtain call

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɝː.t̬ən kɔl / (AmE), / ˈkɜː.tən kɔːl / (BrE)
an appearance by actors or performers at the end of a performance to acknowledge the audience's applause
final appearance to receive applause

Examples

After the show ended, the cast returned to the stage for a curtain call.
The audience cheered loudly during the curtain call for the lead actress.
He felt nervous waiting backstage before his first curtain call.
