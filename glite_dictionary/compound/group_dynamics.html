group dynamics

1. NOUN

/ ɡruːp daɪˈnæmɪks / (AmE), / ɡruːp daɪˈnæmɪks / (BrE)
the study of the psychological processes and interactions that occur within a social group
psychological processes within groups

Examples

The manager attended a workshop on group dynamics to better understand her team's behavior.
Understanding group dynamics can help leaders foster a more collaborative environment.
Conflict often arises from poor group dynamics in a workplace setting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: social psychology - the scientific study of how people's thoughts, feelings, and behaviors are influenced by others

Etymology

From 'group' + 'dynamics', where 'dynamics' derives from Greek 'dynamis' (power, force).
