elder hand

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛldɚ hænd / (AmE), / ˈɛldə hand / (BrE)
the player sitting immediately to the left of the dealer, responsible for playing or acting first in many card games
player to dealer's left in card games

Examples

In their weekly poker game, Maria was the elder hand and played first every round.
The rules state that the elder hand must place the opening bid.
After the cards were dealt, I realized I was sitting as elder hand this game.
