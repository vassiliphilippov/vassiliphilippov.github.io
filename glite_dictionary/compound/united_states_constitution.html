United States Constitution

1. PROPN

/ ˌjuː.nɪ.t̬ɪd ˈsteɪts ˌkɑːn.stəˈtuː.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌjuː.nɪ.tɪd ˈsteɪts ˌkɒn.stɪˈtjuː.ʃən / (BrE)
the fundamental law of the United States, written in 1787, which outlines the national framework of government
foundational law of the United States (1787)

Examples

The United States Constitution guarantees freedom of speech as a fundamental right.
Many legal scholars study the United States Constitution to interpret its impact on modern laws.
The original copy of the United States Constitution is preserved in the National Archives.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: constitution - a body of fundamental principles or established precedents according to which a state is governed
