family Trogonidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli troʊˈɡɑnɪˌdi / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli trəˈɡɒnɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of brightly colored birds, known as trogons, found mainly in tropical regions and coextensive with the order Trogoniformes.
family of tropical birds (trogons)

Examples

The family Trogonidae includes birds famous for their vivid plumage and tropical habitats.
There are around 40 species in the family Trogonidae, spread across Africa, Asia, and the Americas.
Researchers discovered two new species belonging to the family Trogonidae in the rainforest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trogon - a brightly colored bird of the family Trogonidae

Etymology

From New Latin Trogonidae, based on Greek trōgōn (gnawer), referring to the bird's toothed bill.
