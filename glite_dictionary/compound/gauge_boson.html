gauge boson

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡeɪdʒ ˈboʊˌsɑn / (AmE), / ˈɡeɪdʒ ˈbəʊsɒn / (BrE)
a particle that mediates the fundamental interactions between elementary particles, such as photons, gluons, or W and Z bosons
particle that mediates fundamental forces

Examples

The photon is an example of a gauge boson responsible for mediating electromagnetic force.
Physicists have discovered several types of gauge bosons, each linked to different fundamental interactions.
A gauge boson carries the force between elementary particles in quantum field theory.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boson - a particle with integer spin, including force-carrying particles

Etymology

From 'gauge', relating to field theories, and 'boson', from Indian physicist Satyendra Nath Bose, whose work led to the concept of bosons.
