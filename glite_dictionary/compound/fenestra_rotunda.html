fenestra rotunda

1. NOUN

/ fəˈnɛstrə roʊˈtʌndə / (AmE), / fəˈnɛstrə rəʊˈtʌndə / (BrE)
An opening in the wall of the middle ear that leads into the cochlea.
opening in ear leading into cochlea
medical

Examples

Damage to the fenestra rotunda can affect a person's sense of hearing.
The surgeon inspected the fenestra rotunda before proceeding with the cochlear implant.
Sound vibrations are transmitted into the cochlea through the fenestra rotunda.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: round window - another name for fenestra rotunda in anatomy

Etymology

From Latin, 'fenestra' meaning 'window' and 'rotunda' meaning 'round'.
