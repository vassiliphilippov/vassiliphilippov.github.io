war on women

1. NOUN

/ wɔr ɑn ˈwɪmɪn / (AmE), / wɔːr ɒn ˈwɪmɪn / (BrE)
systematic discrimination and policies that are perceived as harming or restricting the rights of women, especially in political and social contexts.
systematic discrimination against women

Examples

Many argue that restricting access to healthcare is part of a broader war on women.
The senator’s comments reignited debate about a so-called war on women in the country.
She wrote an article exposing workplace policies that contribute to the war on women.

Synset Examples

The documentary highlighted the war on women through various laws.
Activists rallied against the war on women in politics.
