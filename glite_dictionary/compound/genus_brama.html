genus Brama

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˈbrɑːmə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈbrɑːmə / (BrE)
The type genus of the fish family Bramidae, which includes pomfrets.
type genus of Bramidae (pomfret fishes)

Examples

The genus Brama comprises several species of commercially important fish known as pomfrets.
Research on the genus Brama has helped scientists understand the diversity of deep-sea fishes.
Members of the genus Brama are distributed in temperate and tropical ocean waters worldwide.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Bramidae - the family of perciform fishes known as pomfrets

Etymology

From Latin 'genus' (kind, sort) and 'Brama', the Latinized form for pomfret fishes.
