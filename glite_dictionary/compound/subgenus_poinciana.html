subgenus Poinciana

1. NOUN

/ səbˈdʒɛnəs pɔɪnˈsiːənə / (AmE), / sʌbˈdʒiːnəs pɔɪnˈsiːənə / (BrE)
A small subgenus of ornamental tropical shrubs or trees, sometimes not recognized in certain botanical classifications.
subgroup of tropical ornamental plants

Examples

The flamboyant tree belongs to the subgenus Poinciana within the legume family.
Several species in the subgenus Poinciana are popular in tropical gardens for their striking flowers.
Some botanists question whether the subgenus Poinciana should be recognized in modern classification systems.

Etymology

From Latin 'subgenus' (below genus) and 'Poinciana', after Phillippe de Longvilliers de Poincy, a 17th-century French botanist and governor.
