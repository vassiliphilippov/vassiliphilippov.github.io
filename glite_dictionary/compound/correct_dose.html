correct dose

1. NOUN

/ kəˈrɛkt doʊs / (AmE), / kəˈrɛkt dəʊs / (BrE)
the amount of a medicine that is appropriate or required for a particular person or situation
appropriate amount of medicine
medical

Examples

The doctor must always ensure that the patient receives the correct dose of antibiotics.
Taking less than the correct dose may not treat the infection effectively.
Pharmacists double-check prescriptions to confirm the correct dose is provided.

Synset Examples

He calculated the correct dose for the medicine.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to overdose - an excessive and dangerous amount of a drug
  • Antonym: underdose - an insufficient amount of a drug
