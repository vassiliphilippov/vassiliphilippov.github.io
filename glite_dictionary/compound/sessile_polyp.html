sessile polyp

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛs.aɪl ˈpɑː.lɪp / (AmE), / ˈsɛsaɪl ˈpɒlɪp / (BrE)
a polyp that is flat or attached directly by its base without a stalk
flat or stalkless polyp
medical

Examples

During the colonoscopy, the doctor discovered a sessile polyp on the colon wall.
Sessile polyps can be more difficult to remove than those with a stalk due to their flat shape.
The biopsy indicated that the sessile polyp was benign, but regular monitoring was recommended.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: pedunculated polyp - a polyp attached by a slender stalk

Etymology

From Latin 'sessilis' (sitting, resting) and Greek 'polypous' (many-footed, polyp).
