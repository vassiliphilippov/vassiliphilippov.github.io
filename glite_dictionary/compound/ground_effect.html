ground effect

1. NOUN

/ ɡraʊnd ɪˈfɛkt / (AmE), / ɡraʊnd ɪˈfɛkt / (BrE)
an increase in aerodynamic lift and a reduction in drag experienced by an aircraft when flying close to the ground
increased lift near the ground (aviation)

Examples

The pilot noticed that ground effect made landing the small aircraft trickier than expected.
Helicopters often experience ground effect when hovering close to the surface.
During takeoff, the airplane briefly entered ground effect, resulting in smoother flight near the runway.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lift - the upward force supporting an aircraft in flight
