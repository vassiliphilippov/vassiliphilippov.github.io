scarlet fever

1. NOUN

/ ˈskɑrlət ˈfiːvɚ / (AmE), / ˈskɑːlɪt ˈfiːvə / (BrE)
an acute infectious disease, mainly affecting children, marked by fever, a sore throat, and a red rash
acute infectious disease with red rash
medical

Examples

During the nineteenth century, scarlet fever was a common and sometimes deadly childhood illness.
The doctor diagnosed her with scarlet fever after noticing the distinctive red rash.
Early treatment with antibiotics can prevent complications from scarlet fever.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: measles - infectious disease causing fever and red spots
  • Synonym: chickenpox - contagious disease with itchy rash and blisters
