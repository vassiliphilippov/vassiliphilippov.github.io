sulfonic acid

1. NOUN

/ sʌlˈfɑnɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / sʌlˈfɒnɪk ˈæsɪd / (BrE)
A class of organic acids derived from sulfuric acid, characterized by the presence of the –SO₃H group.
organic acid with –SO₃H group
medical

Examples

The detergent contains a sulfonic acid derivative as its active cleaning agent.
In organic synthesis, sulfonic acid groups are useful for introducing water solubility to compounds.
Scientists studied the reaction of benzenesulfonic acid, a typical sulfonic acid, with various reagents.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: sulfuric acid - A strong mineral acid with the formula H₂SO₄
  • Synonym: carboxylic acid - An organic acid containing a carboxyl group (–COOH)

Etymology

From "sulfonic," derived from "sulfur" and the suffix "-ic" (forming adjectives), and "acid".
