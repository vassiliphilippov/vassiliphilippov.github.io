family Sphaerobolaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌsfɪərəˌbɒləˈkeɪsiː / (AmE), / ˌsfɪərəˌbɒləˈsiː / (BrE)
a family of fungi characterized by a roughly spherical gleba that is forcibly ejected at maturity
a family of fungi with ejected spherical gleba

Examples

The family Sphaerobolaceae is notable for its unique mechanism of spore dispersal.
Scientists studied the reproductive process of fungi in the family Sphaerobolaceae.
Members of the family Sphaerobolaceae can be found in decaying wood and garden mulch.

Etymology

From New Latin: Sphaerobolus (the type genus) + -aceae (a suffix forming family names in botany and mycology).
