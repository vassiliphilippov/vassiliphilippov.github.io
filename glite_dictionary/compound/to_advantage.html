to advantage

1. ADV

/ tə ədˈvæn.tɪdʒ / (AmE), / tə ədˈvɑːn.tɪdʒ / (BrE)
in a way that makes someone or something appear in the best or most favorable light
in a most favorable way

Examples

The furniture was arranged to advantage in the spacious room.
She wore a color that showed her complexion to advantage.
The lighting displayed the artwork to advantage during the exhibition.

Synset Examples

the dress brought out her figure to advantage

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to disadvantage - in a manner that makes someone or something appear less favorable
