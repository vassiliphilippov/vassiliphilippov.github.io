quantum jump

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɑːntəm dʒʌmp / (AmE), / ˈkwɒntəm dʒʌmp / (BrE)
a sudden change in the state of an atom, electron, or molecule from one quantum level to another, accompanied by the emission or absorption of energy
sudden change between quantum states

Examples

The electron made a quantum jump from the ground state to an excited state after absorbing a photon.
A quantum jump occurs when an atomic particle transitions instantaneously between energy levels.
Physicists have long studied the mechanics behind each quantum jump in spectroscopic experiments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quantum leap - another term for abrupt change in quantum states, also used figuratively

Etymology

Formed from 'quantum', from Latin 'quantus' (how much), and 'jump', referring to a sudden transition.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkwɑn.təm dʒʌmp / (AmE), / ˈkwɒn.təm dʒʌmp / (BrE)
a sudden and significant increase, advance, or improvement
sudden large advance

Examples

The new software represented a quantum jump in data processing efficiency.
Her recent work marks a quantum jump in our understanding of climate change.
There has been a quantum jump in mobile phone technology over the past decade.

Synset Examples

this may not insure success but it will represent a quantum leap from last summer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quantum leap - a sudden, significant advance or increase

Etymology

From physics, where 'quantum jump' denotes a sudden change of state in an atom or molecule; used figuratively since the 20th century.
