bare bone

1. NOUN

/ bɛr boʊn / (AmE), / beə bəʊn / (BrE)
A bone from which all flesh has been removed.
bone stripped of flesh

Examples

After the vultures fed, only the bare bone remained in the field.
She picked the chicken clean, leaving just a bare bone on her plate.
The fossilized bare bone was discovered buried deep in the ground.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: bone - the hard, rigid connective tissue making up the skeleton of vertebrates
