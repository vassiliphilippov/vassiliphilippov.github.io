Lord Chancellor

1. NOUN

/ lɔrd ˈtʃæn.səl.ɚ / (AmE), / lɔːd ˈtʃɑːn.səl.ə / (BrE)
the highest officer of the Crown in the United Kingdom, who is head of the judiciary and presides in the House of Lords
senior British judicial and parliamentary officer
british

Examples

The Lord Chancellor announced a series of reforms to improve the legal system.
For centuries, the Lord Chancellor held great influence over British law and politics.
The role of the Lord Chancellor has evolved significantly in recent years.

Etymology

From Old English 'hlāford' (lord) and Latin 'cancellarius' (doorkeeper, secretary).
