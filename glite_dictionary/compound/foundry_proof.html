foundry proof

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaʊndri pruːf / (AmE), / ˈfaʊndri pruːf / (BrE)
a preliminary printed impression taken from a form before duplicate plates are made, to check for errors or quality
trial print in typesetting before plating

Examples

Before producing the final plates, the printer examined the foundry proof for any type errors.
The foundry proof revealed a misaligned letter that was corrected before mass production.
Checking the foundry proof is an essential step in traditional letterpress printing.

Etymology

From 'foundry' (a workshop for casting metal, in printing a place that produces type) and 'proof' (a trial impression).
