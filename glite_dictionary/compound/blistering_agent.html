blistering agent

1. NOUN

/ ˈblɪstərɪŋ ˌeɪdʒənt / (AmE), / ˈblɪstərɪŋ ˌeɪdʒənt / (BrE)
a toxic chemical substance that causes severe blisters on the skin and damages the eyes and lungs, typically used as a chemical weapon
toxic chemical weapon causing blisters
medical

Examples

Exposure to a blistering agent during warfare can cause severe burns and long-term respiratory problems.
The laboratory is studying how a blistering agent breaks down in the environment after deployment.
Mustard gas is the most infamous blistering agent used in chemical warfare history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vesicant - a substance causing blisters, especially a chemical weapon
