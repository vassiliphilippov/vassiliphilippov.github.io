muscle cell

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌsəl sɛl / (AmE), / ˈmʌsl sɛl / (BrE)
A long, contractile cell in the body that makes up muscle tissue and enables movement by contracting and relaxing.
a contractile cell in muscle tissue

Examples

Each muscle cell contains proteins that allow it to contract and lengthen.
Damage to a muscle cell can impair the function of the entire muscle.
Scientists studied how a muscle cell repairs itself after injury.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: myocyte - a scientific term for muscle cell
