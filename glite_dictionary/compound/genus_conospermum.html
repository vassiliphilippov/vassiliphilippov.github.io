genus Conospermum

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒiːnəs ˌkoʊnoʊˈspɜːrməm / (AmE), / ˌdʒiːnəs ˌkɒnəʊˈspɜːməm / (BrE)
A genus of Australian shrubs and small trees, known as smoke bushes, bearing dense spikes of flowers.
a genus of Australian smoke bushes

Examples

The genus Conospermum includes several species native to Western Australia.
Scientists studied the adaptive traits of the genus Conospermum in arid environments.
Gardeners value plants of the genus Conospermum for their delicate, smoky blue flowers.

Etymology

From Greek 'conos' (cone) and 'sperma' (seed), referring to the shape of the seeds.
