chin wagging

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɪn ˌwæɡɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪn ˌwæɡɪŋ / (BrE)
conversation about unimportant matters, especially in a light, informal, and social context; chatting.
casual social conversation; chatting
informal british

Examples

The two friends spent hours chin wagging at the coffee shop.
After the meeting, there was a lot of chin wagging in the hallway.
She enjoys a bit of chin wagging with her neighbors every morning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chatting - talking in a friendly, informal way
  • Degree Higher: gossiping - talking about other people's private lives

Etymology

From 'chinwag', a blend of 'chin' (referring to talking) and 'wag' (to move repeatedly), originally British slang.
