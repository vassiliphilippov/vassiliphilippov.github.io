alethic logic

1. NOUN

/ əˈlɛθɪk ˈlɑdʒɪk / (AmE), / əˈliːθɪk ˈlɒdʒɪk / (BrE)
a branch of modal logic concerned with the logical analysis of necessity, possibility, and contingency
modal logic of necessity and possibility

Examples

Alethic logic examines statements about what is necessarily or possibly true.
Philosophers often use alethic logic to formalize modalities in arguments.
Courses on advanced logic frequently include a section on alethic logic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: modal logic - the logic dealing with modalities such as necessity and possibility

Etymology

From Greek alētheia (truth), indicating logic relating to truth modalities.
