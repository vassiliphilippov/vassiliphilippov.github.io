bastard wing

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæstərd wɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbɑːstəd wɪŋ / (BrE)
a small, stiff tuft of feathers on the first digit of a bird's wing, also known as the alula
tuft of small feathers on a bird's wing digit

Examples

The bastard wing helps birds control their flight during slow speeds and landings.
In some species, the bastard wing is highly developed to aid in precision maneuvers.
The scientist pointed out the bastard wing while examining the bird's wing structure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alula - the small projection on the anterior edge of a bird's wing formed by feathers on the first digit
