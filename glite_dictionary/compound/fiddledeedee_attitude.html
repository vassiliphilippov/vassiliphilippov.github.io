fiddledeedee attitude

1. NOUN

/ ˌfɪdəl-di-ˈdi ˈætɪˌtjud / (AmE), / ˌfɪdəl-di-ˈdi ˈætɪˌtjuːd / (BrE)
a dismissive or carefree attitude, especially one that treats serious matters as unimportant
dismissive or carefree attitude
informal

Examples

She responded to the criticism with a fiddledeedee attitude that surprised everyone.
It's hard to resolve issues when you have a fiddledeedee attitude toward real problems.
His fiddledeedee attitude made it clear he wasn't taking the situation seriously.

Synset Examples

He dismissed the problem with a fiddledeedee attitude.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carefree - free from anxiety or responsibility
  • Antonym: serious - showing careful thought or concern

Etymology

Formed from the interjection 'fiddle-dee-dee' (used to express dismissal) and 'attitude'.
