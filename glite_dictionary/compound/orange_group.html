Orange Group

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔːrɪndʒ ɡruːp / (AmE), / ˈɒrɪndʒ ɡruːp / (BrE)
a Protestant loyalist paramilitary organization in Northern Ireland that opposes political settlements with Irish nationalists and attacks Catholic interests.
Protestant loyalist paramilitary group in Northern Ireland

Examples

The Orange Group was responsible for a series of attacks during the conflict in Northern Ireland.
Membership in the Orange Group was often secret due to the illegal nature of its activities.
Authorities worked for years to dismantle the Orange Group and prevent further violence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ulster Volunteer Force - another Protestant loyalist paramilitary group in Northern Ireland
  • Antonym: Irish Republican Army - main Catholic nationalist paramilitary group in Northern Ireland; main opponent

Etymology

Named after its association with the Protestant Orange Order, referencing William of Orange.
