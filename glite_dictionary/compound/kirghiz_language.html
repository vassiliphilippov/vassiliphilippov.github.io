Kirghiz language

1. NOUN

/ kɪrˌɡiz ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈkɜːɡɪz ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Turkic language spoken primarily by the Kirghiz people in Kyrgyzstan and surrounding regions.
Turkic language of the Kirghiz people

Examples

She is fluent in both Russian and Kirghiz language after living in Bishkek for several years.
The Kirghiz language uses a modified Cyrillic alphabet for its written form.
Translations into the Kirghiz language are becoming more available for official documents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Kyrgyz - the modern standard term for the language and people; alternative spelling

Etymology

From Russian 'kirgiz', from Kyrgyz 'Кыргыз', referring to the people, with 'language' appended.
