communications technology

1. NOUN

/ kəˌmjunɪˈkeɪʃənz tɛkˈnɑlədʒi / (AmE), / kəˌmjuːnɪˈkeɪʃənz tɛkˈnɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the study or use of systems and equipment for transmitting information, such as telephones, radio, television, and the internet.
technology for transmitting information

Examples

Advances in communications technology have revolutionized the way people connect around the world.
Many universities offer specialized courses in communications technology and network management.
The rapid development of communications technology has created new opportunities and challenges for businesses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: information technology - the use of systems (especially computers and telecommunications) for storing, retrieving, and sending information
