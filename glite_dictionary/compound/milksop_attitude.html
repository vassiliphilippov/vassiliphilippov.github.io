milksop attitude

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪlkˌsɑp əˈtɪtjud / (AmE), / ˈmɪlkˌsɒp əˈtɪtjuːd / (BrE)
a weak, timid, or ineffectual approach or behavior, lacking in courage or assertiveness
timid or ineffectual behavior

Examples

His milksop attitude frustrated his more assertive friends.
The manager criticized Jane's milksop attitude and encouraged her to be more decisive.
A milksop attitude in negotiations rarely leads to favorable outcomes.

Synset Examples

His milksop attitude frustrated his more assertive friends.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: timidity - lack of courage or confidence
  • Antonym: assertiveness - confident and forceful behavior

Etymology

From 'milksop', a term originating in Middle English for a piece of bread soaked in milk, later used figuratively to describe a weak or timid person.
