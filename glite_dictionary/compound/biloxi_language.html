Biloxi language

1. NOUN

/ bɪˈlʌksi ˌlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / bɪˈlɒksi ˌlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Siouan language formerly spoken by the Biloxi people of the southeastern United States
Siouan language of the Biloxi people

Examples

Linguists are working to document the Biloxi language before it is lost entirely.
She wrote her thesis on the structure of the Biloxi language.
Few speakers of the Biloxi language remain today, making it highly endangered.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Siouan - family of North American indigenous languages including Biloxi

Etymology

Biloxi: from the name of the Biloxi people; language: from Latin lingua.
