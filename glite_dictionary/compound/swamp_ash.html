swamp ash

1. NOUN

/ swɑmp æʃ / (AmE), / swɒmp æʃ / (BrE)
A type of ash tree (Fraxinus species) native to swampy areas of the southeastern United States, often valued for its lightweight wood.
ash tree from southeastern US swamps

Examples

The body of his guitar was made from swamp ash, giving it a distinct tone.
In the southeastern United States, swamp ash trees are commonly found near rivers and marshes.
Furniture crafted from swamp ash is prized for its lightness and strength.
