sea holly

1. NOUN

/ si ˈhɑli / (AmE), / si ˈhɒli / (BrE)
A European evergreen plant (eryngo) with twisted spiny leaves, often naturalized in North America; the roots were formerly used as an aphrodisiac.
a spiny-leaved evergreen plant (Eryngium)

Examples

The coastal path was lined with sea holly, its spiky leaves shimmering in the sun.
Gardeners prize sea holly for its unique blue flowers and drought tolerance.
In the past, the roots of sea holly were candied and eaten as a delicacy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eryngo - Any plant of the genus Eryngium, to which sea holly belongs

2. NOUN

/ si ˈhɑli / (AmE), / si ˈhɒli / (BrE)
a widely cultivated southern European plant with prickly leaves and whitish, purple-veined flowers, valued for its ornamental use
a prickly-leaved ornamental plant with purplish flowers

Examples

The sea holly added a striking touch to the summer garden with its spiky foliage and unique blooms.
Botanists study the sea holly for its resilience to salty winds and sandy soils.
She chose sea holly for her bouquet because of its unusual color and texture.
