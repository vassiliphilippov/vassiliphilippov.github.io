rough-skinned newt

1. NOUN

/ rʌf skɪnd ˈnut / (AmE), / rʌf skɪnd ˈnjuːt / (BrE)
a species of newt with rough skin, found along the humid coast from Alaska to southern California
a North American coastal newt species

Examples

Hikers in Oregon may spot a rough-skinned newt near streams and ponds.
The rough-skinned newt secretes a strong toxin as a defense against predators.
Biologists tracked the migration patterns of several rough-skinned newts along the Pacific coast.
