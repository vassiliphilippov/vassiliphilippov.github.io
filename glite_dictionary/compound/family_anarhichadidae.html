family Anarhichadidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ænəˌrɪkəˈdɪdi / (AmE), / ˈfæməli aˌnarɪkəˈdɪːdiː / (BrE)
A family of marine fish known as wolffishes, characterized by elongated bodies and large canine teeth, native to cold waters of the North Atlantic and Pacific.
family of marine fish (wolffishes)

Examples

The family Anarhichadidae includes several species of wolffishes found in the North Atlantic.
Scientists have observed unique dental adaptations in members of the family Anarhichadidae.
Commercial fishing regulations often list the family Anarhichadidae among protected species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wolffish - large marine fish with strong canine teeth

Etymology

From New Latin Anarhichadidae, based on genus Anarhichas, from Greek anarrhichas (wolffish).
