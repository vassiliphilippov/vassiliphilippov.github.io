wake-up signal

1. NOUN

/ ˈweɪk ʌp ˈsɪɡnəl / (AmE), / ˈweɪk ʌp ˈsɪɡnəl / (BrE)
a signal, especially in the military, given to wake people from sleep
signal to wake people up (esp. military)

Examples

The sound of the wake-up signal echoed through the barracks at dawn.
Soldiers must be ready to move as soon as the wake-up signal is heard.
The camp replaces the traditional bugle with an electronic wake-up signal.
