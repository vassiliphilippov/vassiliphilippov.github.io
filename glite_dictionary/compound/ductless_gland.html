ductless gland

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʌktləs ɡlænd / (AmE), / ˈdʌktləs ɡlænd / (BrE)
A gland of the endocrine system that releases hormones directly into the bloodstream, rather than through a duct.
endocrine gland; hormone-secreting organ
medical

Examples

The thyroid is a ductless gland that regulates metabolism by releasing hormones into the bloodstream.
Damage to a ductless gland can disrupt hormone levels and affect many body functions.
Doctors studied how each ductless gland contributes to overall endocrine health.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: endocrine gland - a gland that secretes hormones directly into the blood

Etymology

From 'ductless' (without a duct) + 'gland', denoting glands without ducts, such as those in the endocrine system.
