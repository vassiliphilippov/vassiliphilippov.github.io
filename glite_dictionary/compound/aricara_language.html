Aricara language

1. NOUN

/ ˌærɪˈkɛrɑː ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌærɪˈkɑːrə ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Caddoan language traditionally spoken by the Arikara people of the northern Great Plains
Caddoan language of the Arikara people

Examples

Fewer than one hundred people are fluent speakers of the Aricara language today.
The Aricara language is an important part of cultural preservation efforts among the Arikara.
Linguists have documented the grammar and vocabulary of the Aricara language in recent years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Caddoan language - a member of the Caddoan family of languages in North America
