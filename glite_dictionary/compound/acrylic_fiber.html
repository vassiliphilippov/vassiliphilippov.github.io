acrylic fiber

1. NOUN

/ əˈkrɪlɪk ˈfaɪbɚ / (AmE), / əˈkrɪlɪk ˈfaɪbə / (BrE)
a synthetic textile fiber made by polymerizing acrylonitrile, used in clothing and upholstery
synthetic fiber made from acrylonitrile

Examples

Many sweaters are made from acrylic fiber because it is lightweight and warm.
The upholstery was crafted with acrylic fiber to resist fading and wear.
Unlike wool, acrylic fiber is hypoallergenic and easy to care for.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nylon - a synthetic polymer fiber used in textiles
  • Synonym: polyester - a category of synthetic fiber also used in fabrics

Etymology

From 'acrylic' (from acrylic acid) + 'fiber'; 'acrylic' from Latin 'acer' (sharp, pungent).
