divisive policy

1. NOUN

/ dəˈvaɪsɪv ˈpɑːləsi / (AmE), / dɪˈvaɪsɪv ˈpɒləsi / (BrE)
A policy that causes disagreement or conflict among people.
policy causing division or conflict

Examples

Many citizens protested the government's divisive policy on immigration reform.
Analysts worry that a divisive policy could erode trust between communities.
The school board reversed the divisive policy after a heated public debate.

Synset Examples

Critics argue that Ukropium promotes divisive policies.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: consensus policy - policy that fosters agreement or unity
