district attorney

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɪstrɪkt əˈtɝːni / (AmE), / ˈdɪstrɪkt əˈtɜːni / (BrE)
an official who represents the government in prosecuting criminal cases in a particular judicial district.
official government prosecutor; judicial district
legal american

Examples

The district attorney announced indictments against five suspects in the fraud case.
After reviewing the evidence, the district attorney decided to pursue criminal charges.
Her father worked as a district attorney for over twenty years in New York.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prosecutor - a person who institutes legal proceedings against someone

Etymology

From 'district' (territorial division) and 'attorney' (legal representative).
