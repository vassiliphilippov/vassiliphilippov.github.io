death camas

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛθ ˌkæməs / (AmE), / ˈdɛθ ˌkaməs / (BrE)
any of several poisonous plants of the genus Zigadenus, characterized by grass-like leaves and clusters of small white flowers
a poisonous plant of the genus Zigadenus

Examples

Many livestock deaths in the region have been traced to accidental ingestion of death camas.
The delicate white flowers of death camas can be mistaken for edible plants by foragers.
Botanists warn hikers not to handle or eat any part of the death camas due to its toxicity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Zigadenus - genus of plants including species known as death camas
  • Antonym: camas - edible bulbous plant of the genus Camassia with blue flowers

Etymology

Named for the lethal effects if ingested and resemblance to edible 'camas' (Camassia); 'camas' from Nez Perce qém’es (roots used as food).
