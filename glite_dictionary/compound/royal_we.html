royal we

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɔɪəl wi / (AmE), / ˈrɔɪəl wiː / (BrE)
the use of 'we' by a single person, especially a monarch or person of authority, to refer to themselves instead of 'I', often to indicate dignity, formality, or authority
use of 'we' for oneself, esp. by people of authority

Examples

The queen addressed her subjects using the royal we to express her decisions.
When the professor said, ‘We will now proceed’, she was using the royal we.
He joked about using the royal we in his emails to sound more important.

Synset Examples

The CEO spoke in a royal we during the announcement.
In her speech, she referred to the royal we to unite the team.
She often uses the royal we to sound more authoritative.
