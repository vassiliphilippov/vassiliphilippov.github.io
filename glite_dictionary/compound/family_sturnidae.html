family Sturnidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli stɜrˈnaɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli stɜːˈnaɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of passerine birds native to the Old World, comprising the starlings and related species.
taxonomic family of Old World starlings

Examples

The family Sturnidae includes many species of starlings found across Europe, Asia, and Africa.
Researchers have studied the vocal mimicry abilities of birds within the family Sturnidae.
The common starling, a well-known member of the family Sturnidae, has been introduced to many parts of the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: starling - a small to medium-sized songbird of the family Sturnidae

Etymology

From Modern Latin Sturnidae, from Latin sturnus (starling) + -idae (family suffix).
