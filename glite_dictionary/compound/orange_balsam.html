orange balsam

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrɪndʒ ˈbælsəm / (AmE), / ˈɒrɪndʒ ˈbalsəm / (BrE)
A North American annual plant, typically having yellow or orange flowers, that grows chiefly in wet, acidic soils.
North American annual plant with orange flowers

Examples

The wetland was carpeted with vibrant orange balsam in full bloom.
We spotted a patch of orange balsam growing along the riverbank.
Gardeners appreciate orange balsam for its striking yellow and orange flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jewelweed - Another name for the same plant (Impatiens capensis)
