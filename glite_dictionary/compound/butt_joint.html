butt joint

1. NOUN

/ bʌt dʒɔɪnt / (AmE), / bʌt dʒɔɪnt / (BrE)
A joint made by joining two pieces of material end to end without overlapping.
joint with ends fastened together

Examples

To assemble the frame, simply use a butt joint at each corner.
A butt joint is not as strong as a dovetail joint in woodworking.
The plumber fixed the pipes together with a butt joint and welded the seam.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lap joint - a joint made by overlapping two pieces
  • Synonym: mitre joint - a joint made by joining two pieces cut at an angle

Etymology

From 'butt', meaning 'end', and 'joint
