fetter bush

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɛtər ˌbʊʃ / (AmE), / ˈfetə ˌbʊʃ / (BrE)
A showy evergreen shrub of the southeastern United States, with shiny leaves, angled branches, and clusters of pink to reddish, umbel-like flowers.
evergreen shrub of southeastern U.S. with showy flowers

Examples

The fetter bush thrives in the moist woodlands of the southeastern United States.
Clusters of pink flowers make the fetter bush a favorite ornamental shrub.
Gardeners admire the shiny leaves and striking blooms of the fetter bush.
