Baltimore oriole

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɔltɪˌmɔr ˈɔriˌoʊl / (AmE), / ˈbɔːltɪˌmɔː ˈɔːriˌəʊl / (BrE)
a colorful North American songbird (Icterus galbula) with orange and black plumage, commonly found in the eastern United States
North American orange-black songbird

Examples

We spotted a Baltimore oriole building its nest high in the maple tree.
The bright plumage of the Baltimore oriole makes it easy to identify during spring migrations.
Many birdwatchers travel to see the stunning Baltimore oriole in its natural habitat each year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oriole - a colorful fruit-eating bird of the family Icteridae

Etymology

Named after Lord Baltimore, whose family colors are similar to the bird's plumage.
