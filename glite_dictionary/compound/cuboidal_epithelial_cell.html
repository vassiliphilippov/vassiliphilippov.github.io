cuboidal epithelial cell

1. NOUN

/ kjuːˈbɔɪ.dl̩ ˌiː.pɪˈθiː.li.əl sɛl / (AmE), / kjuːˈbɔɪ.dəl ˌiː.pɪˈθiː.li.əl sɛl / (BrE)
an epithelial cell that is roughly cube-shaped, with approximately equal height, width, and depth, commonly found in certain glands and ducts
cube-shaped epithelial cell
medical

Examples

Under the microscope, the cuboidal epithelial cell appeared perfectly square from the side.
Kidneys contain many cuboidal epithelial cells that help with absorption and secretion.
Damage to the cuboidal epithelial cell layer can affect glandular function.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: columnar epithelial cell - an epithelial cell that is column-shaped
  • Degree Lower: squamous epithelial cell - an epithelial cell that is flat and thin

Etymology

From Latin cubus (cube) and Greek epithēlios (covering, surface).
