capillary tube

1. NOUN

/ kəˈpɪləri tuːb / (AmE), / kəˈpɪl.ər.i tjuːb / (BrE)
A thin tube with a very small internal diameter that holds liquid by capillary action.
very thin tube holding liquid by capillary action

Examples

The scientist used a capillary tube to draw up the tiny drop of blood.
In physics experiments, a capillary tube demonstrates how liquids rise against gravity.
You can observe capillary action by placing a capillary tube in a container of colored water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: capillary - a tube with a very small bore or diameter, especially used in biology and physics

Etymology

From Latin 'capillaris' (hair-like) referring to the tube's thinness.
