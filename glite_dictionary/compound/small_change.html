small change

1. NOUN

/ smɔl tʃeɪndʒ / (AmE), / smɔːl tʃeɪndʒ / (BrE)
a very small amount of money or coins of low value; an amount considered unimportant or insignificant.
a small sum of money; coins of low value

Examples

I only had small change left after buying the groceries.
She handed the taxi driver some small change for the fare.
Compared to the company's budget, that expense is just small change.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pocket change - small amount of money, especially coins; money for small expenses
