sigmoid vein

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪɡmɔɪd veɪn / (AmE), / ˈsɪɡmɔɪd veɪn / (BrE)
a vein that is a tributary of the inferior mesenteric vein, responsible for draining blood from the sigmoid colon
vein draining the sigmoid colon
medical

Examples

The sigmoid vein plays an important role in venous drainage of the lower part of the large intestine.
During abdominal surgery, the surgeon identified the sigmoid vein adjacent to the sigmoid colon.
Blockage of a sigmoid vein can lead to localized swelling and intestinal complications.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: inferior mesenteric vein - vein draining blood from part of the large intestine

Etymology

From Latin "sigmoides" (sigma-shaped) and "vein".
