Chinese cinnamon

1. NOUN

/ tʃaɪˈniːz ˈsɪnəmən / (AmE), / tʃaɪˈniːz ˈsɪnəmɪn / (BrE)
A spice consisting of the aromatic bark of the cassia tree, used in cooking and baking, considered less delicate than Ceylon cinnamon.
spice from cassia tree, less delicate than Ceylon cinnamon

Examples

Most commercial ground cinnamon in the United States is actually Chinese cinnamon rather than Ceylon cinnamon.
The flavor of Chinese cinnamon is bolder and less sweet compared to its Ceylon counterpart.
She prefers to use Chinese cinnamon sticks in savory dishes for their stronger aroma.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cassia - aromatic bark from other species of cinnamon tree, often called Chinese cinnamon
  • Antonym: Ceylon cinnamon - spice from Cinnamomum verum, considered true cinnamon
