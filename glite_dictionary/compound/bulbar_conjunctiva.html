bulbar conjunctiva

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌl.bɑr kənˌdʒʌŋkˈtaɪ.və / (AmE), / ˈbʌl.bɑː kənˌdʒʌŋkˈtɪ.və / (BrE)
the part of the conjunctiva that covers the front of the sclera and the surface of the cornea in the eye
part of the eye's conjunctiva covering the front of sclera/cornea
medical

Examples

An inflammation of the bulbar conjunctiva can cause redness and discomfort in the eye.
During the examination, the doctor checked the bulbar conjunctiva for any signs of irritation.
The medication is applied directly to the bulbar conjunctiva to relieve symptoms of dry eye.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: palpebral conjunctiva - the part of the conjunctiva lining the inside of the eyelids

Etymology

From Latin 'bulbus' (bulb, globe) and 'conjunctiva' (joined together).
