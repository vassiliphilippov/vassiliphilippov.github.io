crab-eating fox

1. NOUN

/ ˈkræb ˌiːtɪŋ fɑks / (AmE), / ˈkrab ˌiːtɪŋ fɒks / (BrE)
a wild canid species of northern South America, known for feeding on crabs and other small animals
wild dog species of South America

Examples

The crab-eating fox is an adaptable predator found in South American grasslands and forests.
Unlike true foxes, the crab-eating fox often hunts for crustaceans along riverbanks.
Researchers observed a crab-eating fox carrying a crab back to its den.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fox - a small wild animal with a pointed face and bushy tail
