Cocopah language

1. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊkəˌpɑː ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈkəʊkəˌpɑː ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Yuman language spoken by the Cocopah people in the southwestern United States and northern Mexico
Yuman language of the Cocopah people

Examples

Linguists are working to document and preserve the Cocopah language before it is lost.
She learned to recite traditional stories in the Cocopah language from her grandmother.
Courses teaching the Cocopah language are now offered at the tribal community center.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Yuman languages - A family of Native American languages spoken in the southwestern US and northern Mexico

Etymology

Named after the Cocopah people, whose name may derive from indigenous roots.
