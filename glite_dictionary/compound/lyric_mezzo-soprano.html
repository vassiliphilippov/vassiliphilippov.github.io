lyric mezzo-soprano

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪrɪk ˌmɛtsoʊ səˈprɑːnoʊ / (AmE), / ˈlɪrɪk ˌmɛtsə səˈprɑːnəʊ / (BrE)
A type of mezzo-soprano singer characterized by a light, warm vocal quality and a range typically from G3 to A5, suited for expressive, melodic roles.
mezzo-soprano with light, expressive voice

Examples

She was cast as Carmen because her lyric mezzo-soprano suited the role's expressive demands.
The audition panel praised his daughter's lyric mezzo-soprano for its warmth and clarity.
Many roles in French opera call for a lyric mezzo-soprano rather than a dramatic voice.

Synset Examples

Lyric mezzo-sopranos do not have the vocal agility of the coloratura mezzo-soprano or the size of the dramatic mezzo-soprano.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: mezzo-soprano - female singing voice between soprano and contralto
  • Degree Higher: dramatic mezzo-soprano - mezzo-soprano with a heavier, more powerful voice
  • Degree Higher: coloratura mezzo-soprano - mezzo-soprano with great vocal agility

Etymology

From Italian 'mezzo' (half) and 'soprano' (high); 'lyric' from Greek 'lyrikos', of the lyre, emphasizing expressive style.
