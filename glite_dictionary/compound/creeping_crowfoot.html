creeping crowfoot

1. NOUN

/ ˈkripɪŋ ˈkroʊˌfʊt / (AmE), / ˈkriːpɪŋ ˈkrəʊˌfʊt / (BrE)
a perennial European herb with long creeping stems, related to the buttercup family
a creeping European herbaceous plant

Examples

The dense growth of creeping crowfoot quickly covered the bank of the pond.
Gardeners sometimes struggle to control the spread of creeping crowfoot in moist soils.
You can identify creeping crowfoot by its shiny leaves and yellow flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: buttercup - a common name for flowers in the Ranunculus genus
