Oregon Jargon

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrɪɡən ˈʤɑrɡən / (AmE), / ˈɒrɪɡən ˈdʒɑːɡən / (BrE)
a historical pidgin language that incorporated Chinook, French, and English words, formerly used as a lingua franca in northwestern North America
historical pidgin language of the Pacific Northwest

Examples

During the 19th century, traders often communicated using Oregon Jargon along the Columbia River.
The vocabulary of Oregon Jargon included words from indigenous, French, and English sources.
Today, linguists study Oregon Jargon to understand multicultural interactions in early Pacific Northwest history.

Etymology

Named for the Oregon region, with 'jargon' from Old French, meaning 'chatter' or 'gibberish'.
