Chinese gooseberry

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃaɪ.niz ˈɡuːzˌbɛr.i / (AmE), / ˈtʃaɪ.niːz ˈɡuːzˌbɛr.i / (BrE)
a fruit with fuzzy brown skin and tart green flesh, now commonly known as kiwifruit
the kiwifruit; fuzzy fruit with green flesh

Examples

The dessert was topped with slices of Chinese gooseberry for a tangy flavor.
In the 1960s, New Zealand growers began marketing the Chinese gooseberry as kiwifruit.
She had never tasted a Chinese gooseberry until she visited her aunt's farm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kiwifruit - the same fruit, also called kiwi
  • Degree Lower: gooseberry - a different fruit, small green or red berry, relates to name

Etymology

Named for its origin in China and resemblance to the gooseberry; later renamed kiwifruit in New Zealand.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtʃaɪ.niz ˈɡuːzˌbɛr.i / (AmE), / ˈtʃaɪ.niːz ˈɡuːzˌbɛr.i / (BrE)
A climbing vine native to China, cultivated especially in New Zealand for its edible fuzzy fruit with green flesh, now commonly known as the kiwi fruit.
vine producing the fruit also called kiwi

Examples

The Chinese gooseberry was rebranded as 'kiwi' when it was exported from New Zealand.
Before becoming popular worldwide, the Chinese gooseberry was mostly grown in its native China.
Some older cookbooks list Chinese gooseberry as an ingredient instead of kiwi fruit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kiwi - the modern name for the fruit formerly called Chinese gooseberry

Etymology

Named for its Chinese origin and its resemblance to the European gooseberry. The fruit is now commonly called 'kiwi', especially in New Zealand.
