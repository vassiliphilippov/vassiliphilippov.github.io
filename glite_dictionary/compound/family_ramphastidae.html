family Ramphastidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ræmˈfæstɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæməli ræmˈfæstɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of tropical American birds known as toucans, characterized by large colorful bills.
biological family of toucans

Examples

The family Ramphastidae includes about forty species of toucans found across Central and South America.
Scientists studied the diet of birds in the family Ramphastidae to understand their role in seed dispersal.
The distinctive bills of the family Ramphastidae make them easily recognizable among tropical birds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: toucan - a brightly colored tropical bird with a large bill

Etymology

From scientific Latin 'Ramphastidae,' based on genus name 'Ramphastos' (toucan) and '-idae' (family suffix).
