family Ophidiidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌfæməli oʊˌfɪdiˈaɪdi / (AmE), / ˌfæmɪli əʊˌfɪdɪˈaɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of eellike marine fishes known as cusk-eels, characterized by their elongated bodies and found in various ocean regions.
a family of eellike marine fishes (cusk-eels)

Examples

The family Ophidiidae includes many species commonly referred to as cusk-eels.
Marine biologists studied the diet of fishes from the family Ophidiidae.
Some species in the family Ophidiidae inhabit deep-sea environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cusk-eel - an elongated marine fish in the family Ophidiidae

Etymology

From New Latin 'Ophidiidae', from Greek 'ophis' (snake), referencing their eel-like shape.
