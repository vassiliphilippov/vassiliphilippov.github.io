elm tree

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛlm ˌtriː / (AmE), / ˈɛlm ˌtriː / (BrE)
A tree of the genus Ulmus, known for its strong timber and commonly grown for shade.
tree of the genus Ulmus; shade tree

Examples

The old house was surrounded by a towering elm tree that provided plenty of shade in summer.
Many American cities lost their elm trees due to Dutch elm disease.
Children liked to climb the sturdy branches of the elm tree in the park.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elm - the wood or timber from an elm tree

Etymology

From Old English 'elm', from Proto-Germanic *elmaz.
