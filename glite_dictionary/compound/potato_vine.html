potato vine

1. NOUN

/ pəˈteɪtoʊ vaɪn / (AmE), / pəˈteɪtəʊ vaɪn / (BrE)
a climbing plant native to Costa Rica, with sparse hooklike spines and large lilac-blue flowers
climbing vine with lilac-blue flowers

Examples

The garden center had several potato vines with striking lilac-blue blossoms.
The potato vine uses its hooklike spines to climb along fences and trellises.
We chose a potato vine to add a splash of color to the backyard wall.

2. NOUN

/ pəˈteɪtoʊ vaɪn / (AmE), / pəˈteɪtəʊ vaɪn / (BrE)
A vigorous, branching vine native to Brazil, with deciduous leaves and white flowers tinged with blue, often grown as an ornamental plant.
Brazilian ornamental vine with white-blue flowers

Examples

The potato vine added a burst of color to their garden with its delicate blue-white flowers.
She trained the potato vine along the fence to create a living screen.
After the frost, the leaves of the potato vine began to fall, leaving only the bare stems behind.
