genus Rosa

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈroʊ.zə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈrəʊ.zə / (BrE)
a large genus of prickly, erect or climbing shrubs that includes all true roses
the genus of true roses

Examples

The genus Rosa contains hundreds of species found throughout the Northern Hemisphere.
All cultivated garden roses belong to the genus Rosa.
Botanists classify the wild rose as part of the genus Rosa.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rose - a prickly bush or shrub that produces beautiful scented flowers, typically red, pink, yellow, or white

Etymology

From Latin 'rosa' (rose) and 'genus' (kind, type).
