water dragon

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔt̬ɚ ˈdræɡən / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə ˈdræɡən / (BrE)
A North American perennial plant found in wet areas, with slender curled clusters of small white flowers.
North American wetland perennial plant

Examples

The water dragon blooms in early summer along the banks of the river.
Botanists noted the abundance of water dragons in the marsh this year.
Her garden features a rare water dragon thriving in the shaded pond area.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɔtɚ ˌdræɡən / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə ˌdræɡən / (BrE)
A swamp plant of Europe and North America, known for its bright yellow flowers that resemble buttercups.
swamp plant with yellow buttercup-like flowers

Examples

The water dragon brightened the marsh with its golden blooms in early spring.
You can often find water dragons growing along the edges of quiet ponds.
