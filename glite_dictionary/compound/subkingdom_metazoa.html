subkingdom Metazoa

1. NOUN

/ ˌsʌbˌkɪŋdəm ˌmɛtəˈzoʊə / (AmE), / ˌsʌbˌkɪŋdəm ˌmɛtəˈzəʊə / (BrE)
A major taxonomic group comprising all multicellular animals with cells differentiated into tissues and organs, typically with a digestive cavity and nervous system.
taxonomic group of multicellular animals

Examples

Scientists classify all complex multicellular animals under the subkingdom Metazoa.
Jellyfish, insects, and mammals are all members of the subkingdom Metazoa.
The subkingdom Metazoa includes organisms as diverse as sponges and humans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: animal - a living organism that feeds on organic matter, typically having specialized sense organs and nervous system

Etymology

From New Latin 'Metazoa', from Greek 'meta-' (after, beyond) + 'zōion' (animal).
