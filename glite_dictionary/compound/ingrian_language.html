Ingrian language

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪŋɡriən ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈɪŋɡriən ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
a member of the Finnic group of languages, traditionally spoken by the Ingrian people of northwestern Russia
language spoken by the Ingrian people

Examples

There are only a few native speakers of the Ingrian language left today.
Linguists are working to document the grammar of the Ingrian language.
The Ingrian language is closely related to Finnish and Karelian.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Finnic - a branch of the Uralic language family, which includes Ingrian

Etymology

Ingrian from English 'Ingria,' referring to the region, and 'language.'
