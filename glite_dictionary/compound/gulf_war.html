Gulf War

1. NOUN

/ ɡʌlf wɔr / (AmE), / ɡʌlf wɔː / (BrE)
the war fought in 1990–1991 in which a U.S.-led coalition expelled Iraqi forces from Kuwait
war between Iraq and a U.S.-led coalition (1990–1991)

Examples

The Gulf War had a significant impact on international relations in the Middle East.
Many veterans of the Gulf War attended the commemorative ceremony.
She studied the causes and effects of the Gulf War for her history dissertation.

2. NOUN

/ ɡʌlf wɔr / (AmE), / ɡʌlf wɔː / (BrE)
the armed conflict between Iraq and Iran from 1980 to 1988, fought primarily over control of strategic waterways and territory
1980–88 war between Iran and Iraq

Examples

The Gulf War caused significant economic and human losses for both Iran and Iraq.
Many books have analyzed the political impact of the Gulf War on the Middle East.
The cease-fire that ended the Gulf War was brokered by the United Nations in 1988.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Iran-Iraq War - armed conflict between Iran and Iraq from 1980 to 1988
  • Degree Higher: Persian Gulf War - conflict between Iraq and coalition forces in 1990–1991
