Pacific hemlock

1. NOUN

/ pəˈsɪfɪk ˈhɛmlɑk / (AmE), / pəˈsɪfɪk ˈhɛmlɒk / (BrE)
A tall evergreen tree native to western North America, important as a source of timber.
evergreen timber tree of western North America

Examples

The forests of the Northwest are dominated by Pacific hemlock and Douglas fir.
Lumber companies value the wood of the Pacific hemlock for its strength and versatility.
We hiked through a dense stand of towering Pacific hemlock on the coastal trail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hemlock - a group of evergreen coniferous trees in the genus Tsuga
