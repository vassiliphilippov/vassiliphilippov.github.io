common lynx

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmən ˈlɪŋks / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən ˈlɪŋks / (BrE)
a large wild cat (Lynx lynx) native to northern Eurasia, characterized by tufted ears and a short tail
wild cat of northern Eurasia

Examples

The common lynx is found in forests and mountainous regions across northern and eastern Europe.
Conservation efforts have helped increase the population of the common lynx in some countries.
The thick fur of the common lynx helps it survive harsh winter climates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Eurasian lynx - another name for the common lynx
