wild indigo

1. NOUN

/ waɪld ˈɪndoʊ / (AmE), / waɪld ˈɪndɪɡəʊ / (BrE)
any of several North American perennial plants of the genus Baptisia, with blue, white, or yellow flowers, often found in prairies and open woods
North American plant of the genus Baptisia

Examples

The fields were dotted with wild indigo, their bright yellow flowers attracting bees.
Gardeners plant wild indigo for its striking blooms and drought resistance.
We spotted several species of wild indigo growing along the roadside in early summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Baptisia - the genus of plants known as wild indigo
