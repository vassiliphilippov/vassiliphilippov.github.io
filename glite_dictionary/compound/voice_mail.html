voice mail

1. NOUN

/ vɔɪs meɪl / (AmE), / vɔɪs meɪl / (BrE)
A telephone service that allows callers to leave recorded messages when the receiver is unavailable, and enables users to listen to, store, and relay those messages electronically.
telephone system for recorded messages

Examples

I missed your call, but you can leave a voice mail and I'll get back to you.
Her voice mail was full, so I couldn't leave a message.
He checked his voice mail during the break and found three new messages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: answering machine - a device for recording telephone messages
