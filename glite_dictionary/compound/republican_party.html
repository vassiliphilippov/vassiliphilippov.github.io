Republican Party

1. PROPN

/ rəˈpʌblɪkən ˈpɑrti / (AmE), / rɪˈpʌblɪkən ˈpɑːti / (BrE)
one of the two major political parties in the United States, traditionally associated with conservative and right-leaning policies; also known as the GOP (Grand Old Party)
major U.S. political party (GOP)
american

Examples

He is a proud member of the Republican Party and regularly attends its conventions.
The Republican Party nominated their candidate for the presidential election last month.
Many policies supported by the Republican Party reflect conservative values.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Democratic Party - the other major political party in the United States, generally associated with liberal policies
  • Synonym: GOP - acronym for 'Grand Old Party,' a nickname for the Republican Party
