survey mile

1. NOUN

/ sɚˈveɪ maɪl / (AmE), / səˈveɪ maɪl / (BrE)
a U.S. unit of length equal to exactly 1,609.347 meters, used especially in land surveying
unit of length in U.S. land surveying (1,609.347 m)

Examples

The property covers five survey miles along the northern border.
Distances are often measured in survey miles for mapping large plots of land in the U.S.
One survey mile is slightly longer than a statute mile.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mile - a unit of length equal to 1,609.344 meters

Etymology

From 'survey', referring to measuring land, and 'mile', from Latin 'mille' (thousand), from the Roman unit 'mille passus' (thousand paces).
