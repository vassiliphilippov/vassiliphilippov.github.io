external gill

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˈstɝnəl ɡɪl / (AmE), / ɪkˈstɜːnəl ɡɪl / (BrE)
a gill that projects outward from the body, found in some aquatic mollusks and the larval stages of certain amphibians
gill located outside the body

Examples

The tadpole's external gills are clearly visible during its early development.
Some species of salamanders retain their external gills throughout their lives.
As the young amphibian matures, its external gills are gradually replaced by lungs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gill - respiratory organ found in aquatic animals
