action painting

1. NOUN

/ ˈækʃən ˌpeɪntɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈakʃən ˌpeɪntɪŋ / (BrE)
a style of abstract painting developed in New York in the 1940s and 1950s, characterized by energetic, spontaneous brushwork and the application of paint in sweeping or gestural movements
an abstract painting style emphasizing spontaneous movement

Examples

Jackson Pollock's work is a prime example of action painting.
Critics often debate the influence of European artists on action painting.
She studied the techniques of action painting in her modern art course.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abstract expressionism - an art movement emphasizing abstract forms and expressive brushwork

Etymology

From 'action' and 'painting', referring to the emphasis on the physical act of painting.
