black gum

1. NOUN

/ blæk ɡʌm / (AmE), / blak ɡʌm / (BrE)
A tall deciduous tree of eastern North America (Nyssa sylvatica), noted for its horizontal branches, late-emerging small leaves, and brilliant autumn foliage.
North American deciduous tree with colorful fall leaves

Examples

In fall, the leaves of the black gum turn a striking red and orange.
The black gum is prized in landscaping for its brilliant autumn colors and attractive shape.
Many species of birds nest in the branches of the black gum tree.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tupelo - another common name for the black gum tree (Nyssa sylvatica)

2. NOUN

/ blæk ɡʌm / (AmE), / blæk ɡʌm / (BrE)
a small Australian mallee tree with rough, dark-colored bark at the base, which yields a red eucalyptus kino gum
Australian mallee tree yielding red gum

Examples

The forest ranger pointed out a tall black gum near the trail, notable for its dark bark.
The black gum is valued for the red eucalyptus kino it provides.
Botanists recently discovered several new black gums growing on the hillside after the fire.
