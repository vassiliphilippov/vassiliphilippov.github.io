bronchial pneumonia

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrɑːŋkiəl pnuːˈmoʊniə / (AmE), / ˈbrɒŋkɪəl pnjʊˈməʊniə / (BrE)
A type of pneumonia marked by acute inflammation of the walls of the bronchioles in the lungs.
pneumonia affecting bronchioles
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with bronchial pneumonia after complaining of high fever and persistent cough.
Children and the elderly are especially vulnerable to bronchial pneumonia during flu season.
Effective treatment of bronchial pneumonia often requires antibiotics and supportive medical care.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lobar pneumonia - pneumonia affecting a large and continuous area of the lobe of a lung
  • Degree Lower: pneumonia - inflammation of the lungs, usually caused by infection

Etymology

From Latin 'bronchia' (bronchi) and Greek 'pneumon' (lung).
