gray body

1. NOUN

/ ɡreɪ ˈbɑdi / (AmE), / ɡreɪ ˈbɒdi / (BrE)
A theoretical body that emits radiation at every wavelength at a constant proportion less than that of a black body at the same temperature.
body emitting less radiation than a black body

Examples

In thermal physics, a gray body emits less radiation than a perfect black body at the same temperature.
Unlike a black body, a gray body does not absorb all incident radiation but has a constant, lower emissivity.
Engineers sometimes use the gray body assumption to simplify calculations involving heat transfer.

Synset Examples

A source with lower emissivity independent of frequency often is referred to as a gray body.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: black body - a body that absorbs all incident electromagnetic radiation
