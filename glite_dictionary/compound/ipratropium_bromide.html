ipratropium bromide

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪprəˈtroʊpiəm ˈbroʊmaɪd / (AmE), / ˌɪprəˈtrəʊpiəm ˈbrəʊmaɪd / (BrE)
A medication used to open the airways in the lungs, often inhaled to relieve symptoms of chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD) and asthma.
inhaled bronchodilator medication
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed ipratropium bromide to help manage her asthma symptoms.
Using an inhaler with ipratropium bromide can provide quick relief during a COPD flare-up.
He carries his ipratropium bromide inhaler with him at all times for emergencies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Atrovent - Brand name for ipratropium bromide inhaler

Etymology

Derived from the chemical structure, with 'bromide' from Latin 'bromum' and 'ide'.
