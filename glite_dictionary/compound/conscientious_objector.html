conscientious objector

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːnʃiˈɛnʃəs əbˈdʒɛktər / (AmE), / ˌkɒnʃiˈɛnʃəs əbˈdʒɛktə / (BrE)
a person who refuses to serve in the armed forces or bear arms on the grounds of conscience, especially for moral or religious reasons
person refusing military service on moral grounds

Examples

During the war, he was recognized as a conscientious objector and assigned to alternative service.
The law requires conscientious objectors to present evidence supporting their moral stance.
Some conscientious objectors volunteer for humanitarian work instead of joining the army.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: draft dodger - a person who avoids compulsory military service, often illegally

Etymology

From 'conscientious' (relating to conscience) + 'objector' (one who objects), first widespread during World War I.
