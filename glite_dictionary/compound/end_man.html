end man

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛnd ˌmæn / (AmE), / ˈɛnd mæn / (BrE)
a man positioned at one end of a row of people, especially in a group or formation
man at one end of a row

Examples

The end man in the photograph is my uncle, standing beside his teammates.
During the rehearsal, the end man had to hold a different pose from the others.
Each end man was responsible for signaling the start of the formation.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛnd ˌmæn / (AmE), / ˈɛnd ˌman / (BrE)
a man who performs at one end of the line of performers in a minstrel show, often engaging in humorous dialogue with the interlocutor
performer at one end in a minstrel show

Examples

In the famous troupe, the end man always got the biggest laughs with his jokes.
The end man wore brightly colored costumes to distinguish himself from other performers.
Historians have extensively analyzed the role of the end man in nineteenth-century minstrel shows.
