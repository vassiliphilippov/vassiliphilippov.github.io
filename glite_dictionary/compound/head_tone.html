head tone

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛd ˌtoʊn / (AmE), / ˈhɛd təʊn / (BrE)
the higher range of the voice in speaking or singing, where the vibrations of sung notes are felt in the head rather than the chest
higher vocal resonance felt in the head

Examples

The singer's head tone gave her performance a bright, ringing quality.
When transitioning from chest voice to head tone, many vocalists feel a shift in resonance.
Proper use of head tone can help singers reach higher notes with less strain.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: chest voice - lower vocal register with resonance felt in the chest
  • Degree Higher: falsetto - artificially high-pitched mode of voice production
