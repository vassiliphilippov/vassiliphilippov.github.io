to take a look

1. VERB

/ teɪk ə lʊk / (AmE), / teɪk ə lʊk / (BrE)
to examine or inspect something briefly or informally
examine or inspect briefly

Examples

Could you take a look at this report before the meeting?
She stopped to take a look at the latest art display in the lobby.
I’ll take a look at your essay and suggest some improvements.

Synset Examples

She asked him to take a look at her presentation.
Can you take a look at this document for me?
Let's take a look at the new restaurant tonight.
Take a look at this beautiful painting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to look at - to direct one's gaze towards; to inspect or examine
