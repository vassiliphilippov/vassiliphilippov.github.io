silver nitrate

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪlvɚ ˈnaɪˌtreɪt / (AmE), / ˈsɪlvə ˈnaɪtreɪt / (BrE)
a colorless or white, soluble compound used in photography and in medicine as a cauterizing and antibacterial agent
chemical used in medicine and photography
medical

Examples

The nurse applied silver nitrate to the wound to prevent infection.
Old photographs often used silver nitrate during the development process.
Contact with silver nitrate can stain the skin a dark grey color.

Etymology

From silver, from Old English 'seolfor', and nitrate, from French 'nitrate' (from nitric acid).
