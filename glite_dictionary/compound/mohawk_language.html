Mohawk language

1. NOUN

/ ˈmoʊ.hɑːk ˈlæŋ.ɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈməʊ.hɑːk ˈlaŋ.ɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Iroquoian language traditionally spoken by the Mohawk people of northeastern North America
language of the Mohawk people

Examples

Many elders are working to preserve the Mohawk language for future generations.
She studied the Mohawk language at university to better understand indigenous cultures.
There are only a few thousand fluent speakers of the Mohawk language today.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Iroquoian - the family of North American indigenous languages that includes Mohawk

Etymology

From Mohawk, the name of the indigenous people, of Iroquoian origin.
