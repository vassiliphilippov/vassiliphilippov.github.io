order Synentognathi

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdər sɪˌnɛnˌtɒɡˈneɪθaɪ / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə sɪˌnɛntɒɡˈneɪθɪ / (BrE)
a taxonomic order of fishes comprising species such as needlefishes, sauries, flying fishes, and halfbeaks, all characterized by spineless fins
a taxonomic order of certain spineless-finned fishes
medical

Examples

The order Synentognathi includes fascinating species like the flying fish.
Scientists have studied the unique jaw structure of the order Synentognathi for decades.
Members of the order Synentognathi are known for their slender bodies and absence of fin spines.

Etymology

From Greek syn- (together) + en- (in) + tognathos (jaw), referring to the fused jaws characteristic of this group; Neo-Latin scientific formation.
