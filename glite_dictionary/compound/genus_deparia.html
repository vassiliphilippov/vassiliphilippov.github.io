genus Deparia

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs dɪˈpɛər.i.ə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs dɪˈpeə.ri.ə / (BrE)
a genus of terrestrial ferns comprising five species, sometimes classified under other genera
genus of terrestrial ferns

Examples

The genus Deparia includes several species of ferns found in East Asia and North America.
Botanists have debated the classification of species within the genus Deparia.
Recent studies suggest that the genus Deparia is distinct from closely related fern groups.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'de-' (away) and 'para' (beside), referring to its separation from similar ferns.
