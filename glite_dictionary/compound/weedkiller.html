weedkiller

1. NOUN

/ ˈwidˌkɪlɚ / (AmE), / ˈwiːdˌkɪlə / (BrE)
A chemical substance used to destroy or prevent the growth of unwanted plants, especially weeds.
chemical that kills unwanted plants

Examples

Farmers often use weedkiller to keep their fields free of invasive species.
You should always follow safety instructions when handling weedkiller in the garden.
We switched to an organic weedkiller to protect the environment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: herbicide - a substance that is toxic to plants and is used to destroy unwanted vegetation

Etymology

Formed from 'weed' + 'killer'.
