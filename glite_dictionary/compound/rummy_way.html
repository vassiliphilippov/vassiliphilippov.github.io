rummy way

1. NOUN

/ ˈrʌmi weɪ / (AmE), / ˈrʌmi weɪ / (BrE)
a peculiar or strange manner of behaving or doing something
peculiar or strange manner

Examples

He always greets his friends in a rummy way, making them laugh.
There's a rummy way she organizes her desk that nobody else understands.
The artist painted in a rummy way that set his work apart from others.

Synset Examples

He had a rummy way of telling stories.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peculiar - strange or unusual
  • Antonym: normal way - an expected or ordinary manner

Etymology

From 'rummy' (slightly odd or peculiar), origin uncertain but possibly related to 'rum' meaning odd in dialect English.
