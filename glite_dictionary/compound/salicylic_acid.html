salicylic acid

1. NOUN

/ ˌsælɪˈsɪlɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / ˌsælɪˈsɪlɪk ˈasɪd / (BrE)
A white, bitter-tasting crystalline compound used chiefly in the manufacture of aspirin, dyes, perfumes, and as a fungicide.
compound used in aspirin and skincare
medical

Examples

Many acne treatments contain salicylic acid to help clear blocked pores.
The synthesis of aspirin begins with salicylic acid as a key ingredient.
Fungicides containing salicylic acid are commonly used in agriculture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aspirin - a common drug used to reduce pain, fever, or inflammation

Etymology

From French *salicylique*, from Latin *salix* (willow), as it was first obtained from willow bark.
