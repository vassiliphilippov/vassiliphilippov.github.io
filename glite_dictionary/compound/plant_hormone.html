plant hormone

1. NOUN

/ plænt ˈhɔrˌmoʊn / (AmE), / plɑːnt ˈhɔːməʊn / (BrE)
a natural substance produced by plants that regulates growth, development, or responses to stimuli
natural regulator produced by plants

Examples

Auxin is a well-known plant hormone involved in cell elongation.
The discovery of a new plant hormone has led to advances in crop yield.
Researchers study how different plant hormones affect flowering in various species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phytohormone - a synonym of 'plant hormone', especially in academic contexts
