center stage

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛn.tɚ steɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈsɛntə steɪdʒ / (BrE)
the most prominent or important position or role; a place of greatest attention or significance
most prominent or important position

Examples

The issue of climate change has taken center stage in global politics.
When she walked into the room, her confidence put her at center stage.
During the ceremony, his achievements were put center stage for everyone to admire.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: limelight - the focus of public attention

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɛntər steɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈsɛntə steɪdʒ / (BrE)
the central area of a theater stage, often considered the most important or prominent position for action or performers
the central area of a theater stage

Examples

The lead actress took center stage during the climactic moment of the play.
A single spotlight illuminated center stage as the narrator began to speak.
The dancers gathered at center stage to receive their applause at the end of the performance.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: stage left - the left side of a stage from the point of view of an actor facing the audience
  • Degree Lower: stage right - the right side of a stage from the point of view of an actor facing the audience
