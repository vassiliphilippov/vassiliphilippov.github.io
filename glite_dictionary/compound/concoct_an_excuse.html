to concoct an excuse

1. VERB

/ kənˈkɑkt ən ɪkˈskjus / (AmE), / kənˈkɒkt ən ɪksˈkjuːs / (BrE)
to invent a false reason in order to justify one's actions or avoid blame
invent a false reason

Examples

She had to concoct an excuse to explain why she missed the important meeting.
Whenever they're running late, they tend to concoct an excuse rather than tell the truth.
He quickly concocted an excuse when asked why he hadn't finished his homework.

Synset Examples

He managed to concoct an excuse for being late.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: invent an excuse - to make up a reason, usually not true, to explain behavior
