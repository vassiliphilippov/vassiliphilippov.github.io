fly floor

1. NOUN

/ flaɪ flɔr / (AmE), / flaɪ flɔː / (BrE)
A narrow raised platform at the side of a theater stage from which stagehands operate ropes and equipment in the flies.
raised side platform in a theater stage for stagehands

Examples

The stagehands gathered on the fly floor to coordinate the scene changes during the play.
Safety rails were installed along the fly floor for the crew's protection.
From the fly floor, technicians managed the ropes controlling the scenery above the stage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fly tower - the tall space above a stage where scenery can be hoisted out of sight
