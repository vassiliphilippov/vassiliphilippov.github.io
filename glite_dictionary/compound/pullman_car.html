Pullman car

1. NOUN

/ ˈpʊlmən kɑr / (AmE), / ˈpʊlmən kɑː / (BrE)
A luxurious railway passenger car designed for comfortable day or night travel, especially one equipped with sleeping or reclining accommodations.
luxurious railway passenger car

Examples

The Orient Express featured a beautifully restored Pullman car for its most privileged guests.
During the golden age of rail travel, passengers enjoyed lavish meals in the dining Pullman car.
He slept soundly in a Pullman car as the train sped through the night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sleeping car - railway car equipped with beds for overnight travel
  • Degree Lower: railroad car - vehicle used for passenger or freight transport on railways

Etymology

Named after George M. Pullman (1831–1897), American inventor and industrialist.
