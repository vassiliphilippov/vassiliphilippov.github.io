balata tree

1. NOUN

/ bəˈlɑːtə ˌtriː / (AmE), / bəˈlɑːtə ˌtriː / (BrE)
a tropical hardwood tree that produces balata gum and heavy red timber, native to Central and South America
tropical hardwood tree yielding balata gum

Examples

The balata tree is valued for its tough gum, which is used in various industrial applications.
Local craftsmen use the red timber from the balata tree to make durable furniture.
Many animals in the rainforest rely on the balata tree as a source of food and shelter.

Etymology

From Spanish 'balata,' referring to the tree and its gum, influenced by indigenous terms in South America.
