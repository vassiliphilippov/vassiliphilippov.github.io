auditory hyperesthesia

1. NOUN

/ əˌdɔt̬ɔri ˌhaɪpərɛsˈθiʒə / (AmE), / ɔːˌdɪtəri ˌhaɪpərɛsˈθiːziə / (BrE)
A medical condition characterized by unusually acute hearing caused by increased sensitivity of the sensory neural mechanism, resulting in intolerance to normal sound levels.
abnormally acute hearing; increased sensitivity to sound
medical

Examples

People with auditory hyperesthesia may find ordinary sounds painfully loud or overwhelming.
Her doctor diagnosed her with auditory hyperesthesia after she reported discomfort from normal conversations.
Children suffering from auditory hyperesthesia often avoid noisy environments, such as crowded classrooms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hyperacusis - abnormally increased sensitivity to everyday sounds

Etymology

From 'auditory' (relating to hearing) and Greek 'hyperesthesia' (excessive sensation, from 'hyper-' meaning 'over' and 'aisthēsis' meaning 'sensation').
