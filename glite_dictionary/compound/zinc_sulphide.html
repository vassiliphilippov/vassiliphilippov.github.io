zinc sulphide

1. NOUN

/ zɪŋk ˈsʌlfaɪd / (AmE), / zɪŋk ˈsʌlfaɪd / (BrE)
a yellow to white crystalline fluorescent compound, occurring naturally as sphalerite or wurtzite and used as a luminous pigment
fluorescent crystalline compound used as a pigment

Examples

Zinc sulphide is commonly used in luminous paints and fluorescent screens.
The mineral sphalerite is a natural form of zinc sulphide.
Some safety signs use zinc sulphide to make them glow in the dark.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zinc sulfide - alternative spelling, chiefly American English spelling
