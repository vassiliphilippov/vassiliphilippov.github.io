tree wallaby

1. NOUN

/ tri ˈwæləˌbi / (AmE), / tri ˈwɒləbɪ / (BrE)
An arboreal marsupial of New Guinea and northern Australia, with hind and forelegs of similar length, adapted for living in trees.
arboreal marsupial of Australasia

Examples

A tree wallaby moved nimbly through the branches above us.
Conservationists are working to protect the habitat of the endangered tree wallaby.
Unlike ground-dwelling species, the tree wallaby is specially adapted for life in the forest canopy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wallaby - a small or medium-sized kangaroo
