Maidu language

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪdu ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈmaɪduː ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the language of the Maidu people, belonging to the Penutian family, traditionally spoken in northern California
Native American language of the Maidu people

Examples

Few people today speak the Maidu language as their first language.
Linguists are working to document and preserve the Maidu language.
The Maidu language has unique sounds not found in English.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Penutian - a proposed family of Native American languages including Maidu

Etymology

Maidu is the name of the indigenous people in northern California; language name by extension.
