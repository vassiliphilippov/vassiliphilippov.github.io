tiger lily

1. NOUN

/ ˈtaɪɡər ˌlɪli / (AmE), / ˈtaɪɡə ˌlɪli / (BrE)
an East Asian perennial plant with large, reddish-orange flowers spotted with black and backward-curving petals
a flower with orange, spotted, reflexed petals

Examples

The tiger lily blooms in midsummer, adding vibrant color to the garden.
She planted a row of tiger lilies along the fence.
The distinctive spots on the tiger lily make it easy to identify.

Etymology

Named for the flower's orange and black markings, which resemble a tiger's coat.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtaɪɡɚ ˌlɪli / (AmE), / ˈtaɪɡə ˌlɪli / (BrE)
A type of lily with cup-shaped flowers and recurved petals, usually deep yellow to scarlet, native to southeastern United States.
a lily with recurved yellow to scarlet petals

Examples

The vibrant petals of the tiger lily brightened up the entire garden.
She planted a row of tiger lilies along the fence to add a splash of color.
A tiger lily stood out among the wildflowers with its striking orange hue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lily - a plant with large, showy flowers, genus Lilium
