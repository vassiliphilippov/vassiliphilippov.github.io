hard solder

1. NOUN

/ hɑrd ˈsɑldər / (AmE), / hɑːd ˈsɒldə / (BrE)
a type of solder containing copper that melts at a relatively high temperature, used for brazing metals
solder with copper, melts at high temperature

Examples

Jewelers often use hard solder to join pieces of gold or silver jewelry.
Because hard solder melts at a higher temperature, it is ideal for creating strong, durable joints.
The technician heated the metal until the hard solder flowed smoothly into the seam.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: soft solder - solder that melts at a low temperature, usually containing lead and tin
