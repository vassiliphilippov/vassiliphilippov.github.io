regional ileitis

1. NOUN

/ ˈriːdʒənl aɪˈliːɪtɪs / (AmE), / ˈriːdʒənəl ɪˈliːɪtɪs / (BrE)
a chronic inflammatory disease of the ileum characterized by abdominal pain, diarrhea, fever, and weight loss; also called Crohn's disease
chronic inflammation of the ileum; Crohn's disease
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with regional ileitis after months of persistent abdominal pain and diarrhea.
Doctors now often refer to regional ileitis as Crohn's disease in medical literature.
Long-term management is necessary for individuals suffering from regional ileitis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Crohn's disease - chronic inflammatory bowel disease affecting the gastrointestinal tract

Etymology

From Latin 'ileum' (part of the small intestine) and Greek 'itis' (inflammation).
