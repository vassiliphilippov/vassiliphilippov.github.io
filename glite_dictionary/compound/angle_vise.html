angle vise

1. NOUN

/ ˈæŋɡəl vaɪs / (AmE), / ˈæŋɡl vaɪs / (BrE)
a clamping device that can hold a workpiece at various angles, typically allowing adjustment up to 90 degrees; used in metalworking and woodworking
adjustable-angle clamping device

Examples

The machinist used an angle vise to hold the metal plate at precisely 45 degrees.
An angle vise is essential when drilling holes that are not perpendicular to a surface.
He adjusted the angle vise to secure the wood for bevel cutting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vise - a mechanical screw apparatus used for holding or clamping a workpiece

Etymology

From 'angle' (corner, inclination) and 'vise' (clamping tool, from Old French 'vis').
