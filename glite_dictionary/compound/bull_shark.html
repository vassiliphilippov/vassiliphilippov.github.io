bull shark

1. NOUN

/ bʊl ʃɑrk / (AmE), / bʊl ʃɑːk / (BrE)
a large, aggressive shark (Carcharhinus leucas) found in warm, shallow coastal waters and known for its ability to live in both salt and fresh water
large, aggressive coastal shark species

Examples

A bull shark was spotted in the river, surprising local fishermen.
Unlike many other species, the bull shark can thrive in both saltwater and freshwater environments.
Because of its aggressive nature, the bull shark is considered one of the most dangerous sharks to humans.
