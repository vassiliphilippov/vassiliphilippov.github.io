shuttle diplomacy

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃʌt̬əl dəˈploʊməsi / (AmE), / ˈʃʌt.əl dɪˈpləʊ.mə.si / (BrE)
negotiations between two parties carried out by a mediator who travels frequently between them, especially in international politics
mediator-led negotiations involving frequent travel

Examples

Kissinger was famous for his shuttle diplomacy during the 1970s Middle East peace talks.
The two nations reached an agreement after months of shuttle diplomacy led by the UN envoy.
Successful shuttle diplomacy often depends on the mediator's persistence and neutrality.

Synset Examples

Kissinger's shuttle diplomacy in the Middle East

Etymology

Formed from 'shuttle' (referring to back-and-forth movement, as in weaving) and 'diplomacy'.
