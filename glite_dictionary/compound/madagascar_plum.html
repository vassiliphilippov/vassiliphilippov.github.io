Madagascar plum

1. NOUN

/ ˌmædəˈɡæskər plʌm / (AmE), / ˌmadəˈɡaskə plʌm / (BrE)
a small shrubby tree native to Madagascar, cultivated in tropical regions as a hedge plant and for its deep red, sour fruits resembling small plums
tropical shrub with plum-like acid fruits

Examples

The Madagascar plum thrives in warm, tropical climates and is often used as an ornamental hedge.
She tasted the tart fruit of the Madagascar plum tree while visiting a botanical garden.
Gardeners value the Madagascar plum both for its attractive foliage and its striking red fruit.
