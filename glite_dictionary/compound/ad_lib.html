ad lib

1. ADV

/ æd lɪb / (AmE), / æd lɪb / (BrE)
without advance preparation; spontaneously or improvisationally
without preparation; spontaneously

Examples

The actor forgot his lines and began to ad lib during the performance.
She answered the interview questions ad lib, showing her quick thinking.
The host often speaks ad lib to keep the audience entertained.

Synset Examples

he spoke ad lib

Related Terms

  • Synonym: improvised - done without preparation; produced on the spur of the moment
  • Antonym: rehearsed - prepared or practiced beforehand

Etymology

From Latin 'ad libitum' (at one's pleasure).
