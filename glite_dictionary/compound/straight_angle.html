straight angle

1. NOUN

/ streɪt ˈæŋɡəl / (AmE), / streɪt ˈæŋɡl̩ / (BrE)
an angle of 180 degrees, forming a straight line
an angle of 180 degrees

Examples

A straight angle is exactly half of a full circle.
When the clock shows 6 o'clock, the hands form a straight angle.
Two adjacent right angles combine to make a straight angle.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: right angle - an angle of 90 degrees
  • Degree Higher: reflex angle - an angle greater than 180 degrees and less than 360 degrees
