pyramidal bone

1. NOUN

/ pɪˈræmɪdəl boʊn / (AmE), / pɪˈræmɪdəl bəʊn / (BrE)
a wrist bone, also called the triquetral bone, that articulates with the pisiform, hamate, and lunate bones
bone in the wrist (triquetral)
medical

Examples

The pyramidal bone is located on the ulnar side of the wrist.
Fractures of the pyramidal bone are uncommon compared to other carpal bones.
On an X-ray, the doctor pointed to the pyramidal bone as the source of pain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: triquetral bone - a carpal bone in the wrist also known as the pyramidal bone

Etymology

From 'pyramidal', referring to its pyramid-like shape, and 'bone'.
