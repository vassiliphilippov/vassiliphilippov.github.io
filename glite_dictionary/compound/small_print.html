small print

1. NOUN

/ smɔl prɪnt / (AmE), / smɔːl prɪnt / (BrE)
printed text in a document (especially legal or financial) that is in small type and often contains important or restrictive details
text in small type with important details

Examples

Always read the small print before signing any agreement.
The hidden fees were mentioned only in the small print at the bottom of the page.
Many customers overlook the small print and end up with surprises later.

Synset Examples

he needed his glasses in order to read the fine print

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fine print - text in small type in documents, often with important information
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06691251-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ smɔl prɪnt / (AmE), / smɔːl prɪnt / (BrE)
printed details, reservations, or qualifications in a contract or document, often in small type, which may contain important terms or conditions
detailed conditions in a contract, often in small type

Examples

Always read the small print before agreeing to the terms of service.
The guarantees and limitations are all explained in the small print of the contract.
Many people overlook the small print, but it can contain crucial information.

Synset Examples

don't sign a contract without reading the fine print

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fine print - the part of a document with important but often overlooked details, usually in small print
