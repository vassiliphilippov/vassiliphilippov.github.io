Knowlton's cactus

1. NOUN

/ ˈnoʊltənz ˈkæktəs / (AmE), / ˈnəʊltənz ˈkæktəs / (BrE)
A small, clustering cactus native to the southwestern United States, recognized as a threatened species.
species of small, threatened southwestern U.S. cactus

Examples

Botanists are working to protect Knowlton's cactus from extinction in its native habitat.
The unique appearance of Knowlton's cactus makes it highly recognizable among southwestern succulents.
Because Knowlton's cactus is threatened, collecting it from the wild is strictly regulated.

Etymology

Named after botanist Frank Knowlton.
