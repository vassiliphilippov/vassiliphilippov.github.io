purple bacteria

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɝː.pəl bækˈtɪə.ri.ə / (AmE), / ˈpɜː.pəl bækˈtɪə.ri.ə / (BrE)
Any of a group of free-living Gram-negative bacteria that appear pink to purplish-brown in color and contain bacteriochlorophyll.
Gram-negative bacteria with a purplish color

Examples

Scientists discovered purple bacteria thriving in the shallow water near the thermal vents.
The unique photosynthesis of purple bacteria enables them to survive in low-oxygen environments.
Researchers study purple bacteria to better understand alternative energy conversion processes.
