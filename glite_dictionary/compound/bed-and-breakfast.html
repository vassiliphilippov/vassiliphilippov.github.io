bed-and-breakfast

1. NOUN

/ ˌbɛd ən ˈbrɛkfəst / (AmE), / ˌbɛd ən ˈbrɛkfəst / (BrE)
A small lodging establishment offering overnight accommodation and breakfast, typically in a private family home or guest house.
small inn offering lodging and breakfast

Examples

We stayed at a charming bed-and-breakfast in the countryside last weekend.
Many travelers prefer a bed-and-breakfast for its homely atmosphere and local charm.
The historic mansion was converted into a popular bed-and-breakfast for tourists.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guesthouse - small private hotel or lodging house
  • Degree Higher: hotel - establishment providing lodging and meals

Etymology

Formed from 'bed' and 'breakfast' to describe an accommodation providing both bed and morning meal.
