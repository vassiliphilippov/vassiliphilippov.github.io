Indochinese peninsula

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪndoʊˈtʃaɪniz pəˈnɪnsələ / (AmE), / ˌɪndəˈtʃaɪniːz pəˈnɪnsjələ / (BrE)
the large peninsula in Southeast Asia comprising Myanmar, Cambodia, Laos, Malaysia, Thailand, and Vietnam.
peninsula in Southeast Asia including several countries

Examples

The Indochinese peninsula is an area rich in cultural and linguistic diversity.
Many rivers on the Indochinese peninsula flow into the South China Sea.
Tourism has grown rapidly across the Indochinese peninsula in recent decades.

Etymology

From 'Indochina' (from India and China) + 'peninsula' (from Latin 'paeninsula', almost an island).
