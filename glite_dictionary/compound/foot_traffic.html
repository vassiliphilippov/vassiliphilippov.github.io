foot traffic

1. NOUN

/ fʊt ˈtræfɪk / (AmE), / fʊt ˈtræfɪk / (BrE)
the flow or movement of people walking in a particular area, especially in relation to businesses or public spaces
people moving on foot in a place

Examples

The new shopping center has seen an increase in foot traffic since the subway station opened nearby.
Store owners rely on high foot traffic to boost their sales.
We chose the location for our café specifically because of its heavy foot traffic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pedestrian - a person walking, especially in a city or town

2. NOUN

/ fʊt ˈtræfɪk / (AmE), / fʊt ˈtræfɪk / (BrE)
the movement of people walking in a particular area, especially as measured for business or planning purposes.
movement of people walking in an area

Examples

The coffee shop selected its location based on high foot traffic from nearby offices.
Retailers monitor foot traffic to optimize their store layouts and marketing strategies.
During the festival, the foot traffic in the main square doubled compared to a normal day.

Synset Examples
