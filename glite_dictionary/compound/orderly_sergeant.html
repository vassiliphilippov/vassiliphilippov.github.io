orderly sergeant

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdɚli ˈsɝdʒənt / (AmE), / ˈɔːdəli ˈsɜːdʒənt / (BrE)
the first sergeant of a company, historically responsible for conveying orders and administrative duties
historical military rank; first sergeant with order duties

Examples

The orderly sergeant called the roll and conveyed instructions to the men each morning.
In many armies, the orderly sergeant served as the commanding officer's right-hand assistant.
His promotion to orderly sergeant came after years of distinguished service in the field.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: first sergeant - the senior non-commissioned officer in a company
