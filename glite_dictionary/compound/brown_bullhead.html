brown bullhead

1. NOUN

/ ˌbraʊn ˈbʊlˌhɛd / (AmE), / ˌbraʊn ˈbʊlhɛd / (BrE)
a freshwater catfish of the eastern United States, recognizable by its brownish coloring and barbels around the mouth
a North American freshwater catfish

Examples

The brown bullhead is commonly found in slow-moving lakes and rivers across the eastern United States.
Anglers often catch brown bullhead while fishing for other types of catfish.
A young scientist studied the behavior of the brown bullhead in local ponds.
