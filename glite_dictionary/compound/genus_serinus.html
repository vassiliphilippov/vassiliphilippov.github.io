genus Serinus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs səˈraɪnəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs sɪˈraɪnəs / (BrE)
A group of Old World finches that includes canaries and serins.
a genus of Old World finches (canaries, serins)

Examples

The genus Serinus includes the well-known domestic canary and several wild species.
Bird enthusiasts often study members of the genus Serinus for their diverse songs.
European serins and canaries both belong to the genus Serinus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: canary - a small songbird, member of this genus
  • Synonym: serin - a small finch in the genus Serinus

Etymology

From New Latin 'Serinus', the genus name, ultimately from Latin 'serinus' (canary).
