asynchronous motor

1. NOUN

/ əˈsɪŋkrənəs ˈmoʊtər / (AmE), / əˈsɪŋkrənəs ˈməʊtə / (BrE)
An alternating current (AC) electric motor in which the rotor receives current by electromagnetic induction from the stator's magnetic field rather than by direct connection, commonly known as an induction motor.
type of AC electric motor; also called induction motor

Examples

The asynchronous motor is widely used in industrial applications due to its robust construction and reliability.
Unlike a synchronous motor, an asynchronous motor does not require a separate excitation system.
The engineer tested the performance of the new asynchronous motor under varying load conditions.

Synset Examples

An induction motor therefore does not require mechanical commutation, separate-excitation or self-excitation for all or part of the energy transferred from stator to rotor, as in universal, DC and large synchronous motors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: induction motor - An AC motor in which torque is produced by electromagnetic induction; synonymous with asynchronous motor
  • Antonym: synchronous motor - An AC motor whose rotor rotates at the same speed as the rotating magnetic field

Etymology

From 'asynchronous' (not occurring at the same time) and 'motor'. 'Asynchronous' reflects that the rotor does not rotate at the exact speed of the magnetic field of the stator.
