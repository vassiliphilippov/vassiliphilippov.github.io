order Mycoplasmatales

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdər maɪˌkɑplæzˈmeɪtiːliːz / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə maɪˌkɒplæzˈmeɪtiːliːz / (BrE)
A taxonomic order of bacteria, coextensive with the family Mycoplasmataceae, which includes species lacking cell walls.
a bacterial order including Mycoplasmataceae

Examples

Bacteria within the order Mycoplasmatales are characterized by their lack of cell walls.
The order Mycoplasmatales includes several pathogens important in veterinary medicine.
Research on the order Mycoplasmatales has revealed unique adaptations to their parasitic lifestyles.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'Mycoplasma' (genus name) and '-tales' (order suffix in taxonomy).
