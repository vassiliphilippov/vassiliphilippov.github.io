bastard toadflax

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæstərd ˈtoʊdˌflæks / (AmE), / ˈbɑːstəd ˈtəʊdˌflæks / (BrE)
a woody creeping parasitic plant of western North America with thick, powdery leaves and clusters of small, dull-white flowers
woody creeping parasitic plant of North America

Examples

The botanist identified the rare bastard toadflax growing along the forest edge.
Many hikers overlook the bastard toadflax because its flowers are small and dull-white.
Bastard toadflax thrives in dry, open woodland habitats across western North America.
