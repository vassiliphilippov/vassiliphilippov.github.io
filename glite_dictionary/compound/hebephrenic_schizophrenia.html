hebephrenic schizophrenia

1. NOUN

/ ˌhiːbəˌfrɛnɪk skɪtsəˈfriːniə / (AmE), / ˌhiːbəˌfrɛnɪk skɪtsəˈfriːnɪə / (BrE)
a form of schizophrenia marked by disorganized thoughts, childish behavior, and inappropriate emotional responses, typically emerging during adolescence
disorganized subtype of schizophrenia
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with hebephrenic schizophrenia after exhibiting bizarre and inappropriate behavior at school.
Symptoms of hebephrenic schizophrenia usually emerge during adolescence, often causing significant disruption to daily life.
Doctors noted the presence of disorganized speech and childlike mannerisms, hallmarks of hebephrenic schizophrenia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: schizophrenia - a severe mental disorder characterized by delusions, hallucinations, and disorganized thinking

Etymology

From Greek hēbē (youth) + phren (mind); schizophrenia from Greek skhizein (to split) + phren (mind).
