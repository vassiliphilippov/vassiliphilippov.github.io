downy ground cherry

1. NOUN

/ ˈdaʊni ˈɡraʊnd ˌtʃɛri / (AmE), / ˈdaʊni ˈɡraʊnd ˌtʃɛri / (BrE)
A decorative American annual plant with round, fleshy yellow berries enclosed in a bladder-like husk.
American annual plant with yellow berries in a husk

Examples

The downy ground cherry adds a unique touch to summer gardens with its lantern-shaped husks.
Gardeners often grow the downy ground cherry for its attractive yellow berries.
I found several ripe downy ground cherries nestled among the plant's fuzzy leaves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ground cherry - Plant with edible berries enclosed in a papery husk
