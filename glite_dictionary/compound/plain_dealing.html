plain dealing

1. NOUN

/ pleɪn ˈdiːlɪŋ / (AmE), / pleɪn ˈdiːlɪŋ / (BrE)
honest and open behavior, without concealment or deceit
honest and straightforward conduct

Examples

Her plain dealing earned her the respect of colleagues from every department.
People admired the CEO's plain dealing during the merger negotiations.
You can always count on him for plain dealing, no matter the situation.

Synset Examples

Her plain dealing made her trustworthy in the eyes of her clients.
He appreciated her plain dealing in a world full of deceit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: honesty - the quality of being honest
  • Antonym: deceit - the act of deceiving someone
