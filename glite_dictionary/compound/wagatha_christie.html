Wagatha Christie

1. NOUN

/ wəˈɡæθ.ə ˈkrɪs.ti / (AmE), / wəˈɡa.θə ˈkrɪs.ti / (BrE)
A humorous term, especially in British English, referring to a person involved in a high-profile or dramatic legal dispute, particularly referencing the public feud between Colleen Rooney and Rebekah Vardy.
term for a person in a high-profile legal dispute (UK, humorous)
british informal

Examples

The press couldn't get enough of the Wagatha Christie case as it unfolded in court.
Many social media users weighed in on the latest developments in the Wagatha Christie saga.
She joked that her detective work made her the next Wagatha Christie among her friends.

Synset Examples

She joked about becoming the next Wagatha Christie.
Everyone is following the latest updates on the Wagatha Christie case.
The tabloids are calling it the Wagatha Christie scandal.

Etymology

Blend of 'WAG' (wives and girlfriends of sportsmen) and 'Agatha Christie', the crime novelist, originating during the 2019 legal case between Colleen Rooney and Rebekah Vardy.
