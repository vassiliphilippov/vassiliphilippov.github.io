pes cavus

1. NOUN

/ pɛs ˈkeɪvəs / (AmE), / pɛs ˈkeɪvəs / (BrE)
A deformity of the foot characterized by an abnormally high arch and an exaggerated extension of the toes, often giving the foot a claw-like appearance.
foot deformity with high arch
medical

Examples

The physician examined the patient's foot and diagnosed pes cavus based on the unusually high arch.
People with pes cavus may experience pain and difficulty finding comfortable shoes.
Treatment options for pes cavus include physical therapy, orthotics, or in severe cases, surgery.

Etymology

From Latin 'pes' (foot) and 'cavus' (hollow).
