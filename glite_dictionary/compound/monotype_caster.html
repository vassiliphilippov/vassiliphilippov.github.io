monotype caster

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑːnoʊtaɪp ˈkæstər / (AmE), / ˈmɒn.ə.taɪp ˈkɑː.stər / (BrE)
a machine used in typesetting to cast individual characters for printing.
machine for casting individual printing type

Examples

The old printing press still houses a monotype caster from the early twentieth century.
Restoring the monotype caster took months, but now it works as smoothly as ever.
With a monotype caster, printers could quickly produce custom fonts for every job.

Synset Examples

For the monotype caster to produce types with the shape of the desired character on their face, a matrix with that character incised in it must be moved to the top of the mold in which the type slug will be cast.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Linotype - a machine that casts entire lines of type for printing

Etymology

From 'Monotype', the trade name of the company that invented it, and 'caster' (something that casts).
