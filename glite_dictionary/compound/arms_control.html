arms control

1. NOUN

/ ɑrmz kənˈtroʊl / (AmE), / ɑːmz kənˈtrəʊl / (BrE)
The regulation or limitation of the size, number, or capability of a country's armed forces and weapons, especially by international agreement.
regulation of armed forces and weapons

Examples

International treaties on arms control aim to promote global stability and security.
The conference focused on new approaches to arms control in the 21st century.
Both nations agreed to resume negotiations on nuclear arms control.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disarmament - The act of reducing or eliminating military forces and weapons.
