New World opah

1. NOUN

/ nuː wɝːld ˈoʊ.pɑː / (AmE), / njuː wɜːld ˈəʊ.pɑː / (BrE)
a species of large, colorful oceanic fish (Lampris guttatus) found from Nova Scotia to the West Indies and the Gulf of Mexico
a large oceanic fish species

Examples

The New World opah is known for its round body and vibrant colors.
Fishermen off the coast of Nova Scotia sometimes catch a New World opah by accident.
Scientists are studying the migratory patterns of the New World opah in the Atlantic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: opah - a large, round-bodied, brightly colored oceanic fish
