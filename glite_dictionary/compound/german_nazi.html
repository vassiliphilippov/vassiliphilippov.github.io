German Nazi

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɜrmən ˈnɑtsi / (AmE), / ˈdʒɜːmən ˈnɑːtsi / (BrE)
a member of the National Socialist German Workers' Party (NSDAP) in Germany during Adolf Hitler's leadership
member of Hitler's Nazi Party in Germany

Examples

My grandfather fought against the German Nazis during World War II.
Historians continue to study the rise of the German Nazis and their impact on Europe.
She found documents proving her ancestor was once a prominent German Nazi.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Nazi - a member of the National Socialist German Workers' Party

Etymology

From German 'Nazi', short for 'Nationalsozialist' (National Socialist), combined with 'German'.
