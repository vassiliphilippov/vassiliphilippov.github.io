jawless fish

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɔləs fɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈdʒɔːləs fɪʃ / (BrE)
Any of a group of primitive vertebrate fishes that lack jaws and paired fins, such as lampreys and hagfishes.
primitive fish without jaws or paired fins

Examples

Fossils of jawless fish provide valuable insights into the early evolution of vertebrates.
Lampreys and hagfish are modern representatives of jawless fish.
Unlike sharks, jawless fish do not have true jaws or paired fins.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lamprey - a jawless fish with a toothed, funnel-like sucking mouth
  • Synonym: hagfish - a marine jawless fish with a slimy body
