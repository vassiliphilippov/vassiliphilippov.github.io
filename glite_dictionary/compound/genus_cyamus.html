genus Cyamus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs saɪˈeɪməs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs sɪˈeɪməs / (BrE)
A genus of small crustaceans known as whale lice, which are parasites that live on whales.
genus of crustaceans (whale lice)

Examples

Scientists studying whales often examine the genus Cyamus to learn about parasitic relationships.
The genus Cyamus is adapted to cling tightly to the skin of its host.
Infestation by species in the genus Cyamus can reveal much about whale health.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whale louse - a small crustacean parasite that lives on whales

Etymology

From New Latin 'Cyamus', the scientific genus name.
