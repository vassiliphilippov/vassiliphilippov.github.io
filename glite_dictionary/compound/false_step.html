false step

1. NOUN

/ fɔls stɛp / (AmE), / fɔːls stɛp / (BrE)
an action or decision that is a mistake or misjudgment, especially one that affects progress or success
a mistake or misjudgment

Examples

Missing the deadline proved to be a costly false step for the team.
A single false step in negotiations can damage years of hard work.
She managed to recover quickly after her false step at the start of the project.

Synset Examples

Skipping the meeting was a false step for his project.
Her choice was a false step in her career.
He acknowledged that it was a false step to trust too easily.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: error - a mistake
  • Synonym: blunder - a stupid or careless mistake
  • Antonym: success - the achievement of a desired goal
