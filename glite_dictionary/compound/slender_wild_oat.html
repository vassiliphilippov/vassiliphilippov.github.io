slender wild oat

1. NOUN

/ ˈslɛndɚ waɪld oʊt / (AmE), / ˈslɛndə waɪld əʊt / (BrE)
a species of wild oat native to southern Europe and southwestern Asia, known for its thin, upright stems; often considered a weed in cultivated fields
a type of wild oat plant

Examples

The slender wild oat often invades wheat fields, competing with the crops for nutrients.
Farmers in the Mediterranean region struggle to control the spread of slender wild oat.
During the spring, patches of slender wild oat can be seen growing by the roadside.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wild oat - any of several grasses of the genus Avena resembling cultivated oat
