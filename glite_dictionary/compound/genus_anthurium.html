genus Anthurium

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ænˈθjʊəriəm / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ænˈθjʊə.ri.əm / (BrE)
A large genus of evergreen, often epiphytic tropical American plants, frequently cultivated as houseplants.
tropical plant genus often grown as houseplants

Examples

The genus Anthurium includes many popular houseplants known for their striking, heart-shaped flowers.
Botanists have identified more than 1,000 species within the genus Anthurium.
Collectors prize rare members of the genus Anthurium for their unusual foliage and vibrant colors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anthurium - a plant of the genus Anthurium, especially cultivated species

Etymology

From scientific Latin, with 'Anthurium' from Greek 'anthos' (flower) and 'oura' (tail).
