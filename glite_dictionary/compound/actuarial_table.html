actuarial table

1. NOUN

/ ˌæktʃuˈɛriəl ˈteɪbəl / (AmE), / ˌæktʃʊˈəriəl ˈteɪbəl / (BrE)
A table containing statistical data, often showing the probability of an event such as death or illness at various ages, used especially in insurance and pensions.
table of statistical probabilities, chiefly in insurance

Examples

Insurance companies rely on actuarial tables to assess risk and calculate premiums.
The pension plan is managed based on data provided by actuarial tables.
An actuarial table estimates the life expectancy of individuals in a population.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: life table - table showing mortality rates at various ages

Etymology

From actuarial, relating to actuaries and statistics; from modern Latin 'actuarius', a register clerk.
