switch grass

1. NOUN

/ swɪtʃ ɡræs / (AmE), / swɪtʃ ɡrɑːs / (BrE)
a tall grass of western North America, used for hay and soil conservation
a tall North American grass used for hay

Examples

The farmer harvested switch grass to feed his livestock during the winter.
Recent studies show that switch grass can be used as a biofuel source.
Fields of switch grass help prevent soil erosion on the plains.
