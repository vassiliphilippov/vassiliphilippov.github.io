sour dock

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaʊɚ dɑk / (AmE), / ˈsaʊə dɒk / (BrE)
A species of sorrel with large, slightly acidic leaves, cultivated in temperate regions for use in salads and spring greens.
a sorrel plant with acidic leaves used as salad greens

Examples

We picked fresh sour dock leaves for our salad while hiking.
The farmer grows sour dock alongside other spring greens for the local market.
Some chefs prize sour dock for its tangy flavor in seasonal dishes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sorrel - a plant with acidic, edible leaves often used in salads
