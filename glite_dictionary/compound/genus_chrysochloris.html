genus Chrysochloris

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˌkrɪsəʊˈklɔːrɪs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˌkrɪsəʊˈklɔːrɪs / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Chrysochloridae, comprising certain species of African golden moles.
type genus of Chrysochloridae (golden moles)

Examples

The genus Chrysochloris includes species of golden moles found in southern Africa.
Researchers have studied the unique burrowing habits of the genus Chrysochloris.
Specimens belonging to the genus Chrysochloris display iridescent fur due to their specialized hair structure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Chrysochloridae - family of golden moles

Etymology

From New Latin 'Chrysochloris', from Greek 'chrysos' (gold) and 'chloros' (green), referring to the fur color.
