family Pongidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli pɒnˈdʒaɪdi / (AmE), / ˈfæməli pɒŋˈdʒiːdiː / (BrE)
a former classification of great apes, including orangutans, gorillas, chimpanzees, and sometimes gibbons
group of great apes (obsolete scientific term)
medical

Examples

Some textbooks still use the term family Pongidae to refer to great apes, though it is now outdated.
Recent genetic studies have led to a reclassification of species formerly in family Pongidae.
Gibbons were sometimes included in family Pongidae, but this is not accepted in modern taxonomy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hominidae - the current family in classification including great apes and humans

Etymology

From Modern Latin 'Pongidae', based on genus 'Pongo' (orangutan) and family suffix '-idae'.
