Greco-Roman architecture

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡriːkoʊˈroʊmən ˌɑːrkɪˈtɛktʃər / (AmE), / ˌɡriːkəʊˈrəʊmən ˌɑːkɪˈtɛktʃə / (BrE)
a style of architecture influenced by the designs and principles of ancient Greece and Rome, characterized by columns, arches, and symmetry
architecture inspired by ancient Greece and Rome

Examples

The museum's entrance is designed in the Greco-Roman architecture style, with grand columns and arches.
Many government buildings in Washington, D.C., reflect Greco-Roman architecture in their facades.
The city's courthouse, with its imposing pillars, is a fine example of Greco-Roman architecture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: classical architecture - architecture of ancient Greece and Rome

Etymology

From Greco- (relating to Greece) and Roman, referring to the architectural traditions of ancient Greece and Rome.
