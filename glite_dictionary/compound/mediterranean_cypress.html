Mediterranean cypress

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛdɪtəˈreɪniən ˈsaɪprəs / (AmE), / ˌmɛdɪtəˈreɪniən ˈsʌɪprəs / (BrE)
a tall cypress tree native to the Mediterranean region, with thin grey bark and upward-growing branches, often used as an ornamental or in cemeteries
a tall cypress tree native to the Mediterranean

Examples

Rows of Mediterranean cypress trees lined the ancient stone pathway.
The Mediterranean cypress is a common sight in Italian cemeteries and gardens.
Gardeners often choose the Mediterranean cypress for its slender, elegant silhouette.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cypress - any coniferous tree of the genus Cupressus

Etymology

From 'Mediterranean', referring to its native region, and 'cypress', from Latin 'cupressus'.
