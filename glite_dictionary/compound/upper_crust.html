upper crust

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌpər krʌst / (AmE), / ˈʌpə krʌst / (BrE)
the social class that holds the highest status and wealth in society
highest social class

Examples

Only the upper crust of society attended the exclusive charity ball.
She married into the upper crust and quickly adapted to their luxurious lifestyle.
Many novels portray the struggles between the upper crust and the working class.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elite - people who are considered the best or most powerful
  • Synonym: aristocracy - the highest class in certain societies
  • Antonym: lower class - the social class with the least wealth and status

Etymology

Originally a literal reference to the upper, more desirable part of baked bread, later used figuratively for social elites.

2. NOUN

/ ˈʌpɚ krʌst / (AmE), / ˈʌpə krʌst / (BrE)
the wealthiest or most socially prominent people in society; the elite class
the wealthy or elite class

Examples

She married into the upper crust and quickly adapted to their lavish lifestyle.
Only members of the upper crust were invited to the exclusive garden party.
The hotel is favored by the city's upper crust during the festival season.

Synset Examples

They held a charity event for the upper crust last weekend.
He mingled with the upper crust at the gala.
The restaurant is known for attracting the upper crust of society.
She attended an upper-crust event in the city.
The upper-crust neighborhood was known for its mansions.
The upper-crust lifestyle fascinated the tourists.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elite - the richest, most powerful, or most skilled people in a group or society
  • Synonym: upper-class - belonging to or characteristic of the highest social class
  • Antonym: commoner - a person who is not a member of the aristocracy or elite

Etymology

Originally referred to the upper part of a loaf of bread, thought to be more desirable; figurative use for elite class dates from the 19th century.
