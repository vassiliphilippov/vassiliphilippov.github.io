black-and-white

1. ADJ

/ ˌblæk ən ˈwaɪt / (AmE), / ˌblæk ən ˈwaɪt / (BrE)
having only the colors black and white, with no shades of other colors or grey.
composed of only black and white colors

Examples

The panda is a remarkable animal with its distinctive black-and-white markings.
The film was shot in classic black-and-white, giving it a timeless feel.
She decorated her living room in a bold black-and-white pattern.

Synset Examples

black-and-white blooms
black-and-white stripes

2. ADJ

/ ˌblæk ən ˈwaɪt / (AmE), / ˌblæk ənd ˈwaɪt / (BrE)
(of images, film, television, etc.) not having or not capable of producing colors; using only black, white, and shades of gray.
lacking color; only in black, white, and gray

Examples

The old photographs were all black-and-white and had a certain nostalgic charm.
She prefers black-and-white movies for their classic appeal and dramatic lighting.
We watched the documentary on a black-and-white television that my grandparents still own.

Synset Examples

black-and-white film
a black-and-white TV
the movie was in black and white
