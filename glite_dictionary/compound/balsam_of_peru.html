balsam of Peru

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɔl.səm əv pəˈru / (AmE), / ˈbɔːl.səm əv pəˈruː / (BrE)
a dark brown, fragrant, syrupy resin obtained from the Peruvian balsam tree, used in perfumes, flavorings, and for treating wounds and skin conditions
fragrant resin from the Peruvian balsam tree
medical

Examples

The doctor applied balsam of Peru to the wound to promote healing.
Some people may develop an allergy to balsam of Peru in skin-care products.
The perfumer chose balsam of Peru for its sweet and vanilla-like scent.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: balsam - an aromatic resinous substance
  • Synonym: resin - a sticky organic substance exuded by some trees and plants

Etymology

From Peru, where the balsam-yielding tree is native, and from Spanish 'bálsamo del Perú'.
