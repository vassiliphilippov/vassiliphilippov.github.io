transfer agent

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrænsfɚ ˈeɪdʒənt / (AmE), / ˈtrɑːnsfə ˈeɪdʒənt / (BrE)
an organization, often a bank or trust company, appointed by a corporation to keep records of its stock and bond holders and to manage the transfer and cancellation of securities certificates
agency maintaining records of stock and bond holders

Examples

The corporation appointed a new transfer agent to streamline its record-keeping processes.
Shareholders contacted the transfer agent to resolve issues with their stock certificates.
A reliable transfer agent is essential for accurate tracking of bond ownership.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: registrar - an officer or agency keeping official records, especially of ownership in financial contexts
