solidarity tax

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɑːlɪˈdɛrəti tæks / (AmE), / ˌsɒlɪˈdærɪti tæks / (BrE)
a tax imposed by a government in special circumstances, usually to fund projects intended to promote social cohesion or support national solidarity
tax for funding solidarity projects

Examples

The government introduced a solidarity tax to support reconstruction after the natural disaster.
Some countries apply a solidarity tax to finance unemployment benefits during economic crises.
Debate continues over whether the solidarity tax should be temporary or made permanent.

Synset Examples

Up to €972 (€1,944 for married couples) annual income tax, no solidarity surcharge is levied.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: solidarity surcharge - an additional charge on tax, often with a similar purpose

Etymology

From 'solidarity' (French solidarité, from Latin solidus 'solid') + 'tax'.
