white fritillary

1. NOUN

/ waɪt frɪˈtɪlɪˌɛri / (AmE), / waɪt frɪˈtɪlɪˌəri / (BrE)
a California herb with white conical or bell-shaped flowers, often tinged with green
California herb with white bell-shaped flowers

Examples

Several white fritillaries bloomed in the shade by the creek this spring.
Botanists often seek the white fritillary because of its distinctive bell-shaped flowers.
In California, the white fritillary is admired for its delicate beauty and pale coloring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fritillary - any plant of the genus Fritillaria, typically with bell-shaped flowers

Etymology

‘Fritillary’ from Latin ‘fritillus’ (dice-box), referring to the shape or pattern of the flowers.
