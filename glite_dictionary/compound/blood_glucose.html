blood glucose

1. NOUN

/ blʌd ˈɡluːkoʊs / (AmE), / blʌd ˈɡluːkəʊs / (BrE)
the concentration of glucose present in the bloodstream, important for diagnosing and managing diabetes and other health conditions
glucose level in the blood
medical

Examples

People with diabetes must regularly monitor their blood glucose levels.
A sudden drop in blood glucose can lead to dizziness or fainting.
Eating balanced meals helps maintain stable blood glucose throughout the day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blood sugar - the concentration of glucose in the blood
