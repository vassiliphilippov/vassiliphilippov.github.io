Holy Roller

1. NOUN

/ ˈhoʊli ˈroʊlər / (AmE), / ˈhəʊli ˈrəʊlə / (BrE)
a member of a Christian group whose worship involves ecstatic, exuberant behavior such as shouting, dancing, or rolling on the floor
member of ecstatic Christian sect

Examples

Some people derided the congregation as Holy Rollers because of their lively worship style.
The documentary followed the lives of several Holy Rollers in rural Texas.
Although often misunderstood, many Holy Rollers see their ecstatic devotion as a true expression of faith.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Pentecostal - a member of a Christian movement emphasizing ecstatic worship and spiritual gifts

Etymology

Originally a term of derision in 19th-century America, referring to the literal rolling on the ground seen in some revivalist services.
