powder magazine

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaʊdɚ ˌmæɡəˌzin / (AmE), / ˈpaʊdə ˌmæɡəˌziːn / (BrE)
a building or compartment for storing gunpowder and ammunition, especially on a warship or in a fort
storehouse for gunpowder and ammunition

Examples

The sailors carefully carried the explosives to the powder magazine below deck.
During the siege, the powder magazine was heavily guarded against enemy attack.
A sudden spark could have ignited the powder magazine, destroying the entire ship.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: magazine - a place for storing ammunition, a compartment in a firearm or fortification

Etymology

From 'powder' (referring to gunpowder) and 'magazine,' from Arabic 'makhzan' (storehouse).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpaʊdɚ ˌmæɡəziːn / (AmE), / ˈpaʊdə ˌmæɡəziːn / (BrE)
A building or room designed for the safe storage of gunpowder or other explosives.
storage facility for gunpowder or explosives

Examples

The soldiers inspected the powder magazine to ensure all supplies were secure.
During the attack, a fire threatened the powder magazine and forced an evacuation.
The castle includes an old powder magazine that was built in the 18th century.

Synset Examples

They stored explosives safely in the powder magazine.
The powder magazine was crucial during the war.
The old powder magazine is now a historical site.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arsenal - a place where weapons and military equipment are stored or made
  • Synonym: ammunition depot - a place where ammunition is stored

Etymology

From 'powder' (referring to gunpowder) and 'magazine,' from Arabic 'makhzan' (storehouse).
