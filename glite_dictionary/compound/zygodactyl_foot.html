zygodactyl foot

1. NOUN

/ ˌzaɪɡoʊˈdæk.tɪl fʊt / (AmE), / ˌzɪɡəʊˈdæk.tɪl fʊt / (BrE)
a bird's foot in which the first and fourth toes point backward and the second and third point forward
bird's foot with two toes forward, two back

Examples

Many woodpeckers have a zygodactyl foot to help them cling to tree trunks while pecking for insects.
The zygodactyl foot arrangement is ideal for birds that need to grasp branches or vertical surfaces securely.
Unlike most songbirds, parrots possess a zygodactyl foot, enabling them to manipulate food items skillfully.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: anisodactyl foot - bird's foot with three toes forward, one back

Etymology

From Greek 'zygon' (yoke) and 'daktylos' (finger, toe).
