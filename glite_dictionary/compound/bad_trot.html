bad trot

1. NOUN

/ bæd trɑt / (AmE), / bad trɒt / (BrE)
a period of poor performance or bad luck, especially over a series of events
period of poor performance or luck

Examples

After a bad trot in business, the company finally made a profit this quarter.
The team hopes to end their bad trot with a win in the next game.
She admitted her confidence was shaken by a bad trot of disappointing results.

Synset Examples

She’s been having a bad trot lately in her matches.
His performance was a bad trot at the race.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: losing streak - a continued sequence of losses
