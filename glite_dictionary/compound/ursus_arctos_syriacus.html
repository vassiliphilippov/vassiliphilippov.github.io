Ursus arctos syriacus

1. NOUN

/ ˈɝːsəs ˈɑːrktoʊs sɪˈraɪəkəs / (AmE), / ˈɜːsəs ˈɑːktɒs sɪˈraɪəkəs / (BrE)
a subspecies of the brown bear, native to the Middle East, characterized by its yellowish-grey fur
subspecies of Syrian brown bear

Examples

The Ursus arctos syriacus once inhabited a wide range across the Middle East.
Conservationists are working to protect the dwindling population of Ursus arctos syriacus in Syria and Lebanon.
Historical records mention encounters with the Ursus arctos syriacus in regions that are now part of Turkey.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brown bear - large bear species, Ursus arctos

Etymology

From Latin: Ursus (bear), arctos (Greek for bear), syriacus (of Syria).
