Engelmann's spruce

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛŋəlˌmænz ˌsprus / (AmE), / ˈɛŋəlˌmænz ˌspruːs / (BrE)
a tall spruce tree native to the Rocky Mountains and British Columbia, with blue-green needles and sharply conic crown; its wood is used for rough lumber and boxes
a Rocky Mountain and BC spruce tree species

Examples

The Engelmann's spruce thrives at high elevations in the Rocky Mountains.
Box manufacturers sometimes use wood from the Engelmann's spruce for its strength.
We hiked through a forest dominated by Engelmann's spruce and fir trees.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: spruce - a type of evergreen tree with needle-like leaves

Etymology

Named after German-American botanist George Engelmann (1809–1884), who first described the species.
