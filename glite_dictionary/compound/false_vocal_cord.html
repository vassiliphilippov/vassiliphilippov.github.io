false vocal cord

1. NOUN

/ fɔls ˈvoʊ.kəl kɔrd / (AmE), / fɔːls ˈvəʊ.kəl kɔːd / (BrE)
either of the pair of upper folds of the larynx not involved in producing the voice
upper laryngeal fold not used in voice
medical

Examples

Swelling of the false vocal cord can make it difficult to breathe.
The false vocal cords protect the airway but do not produce sound.
Doctors examined both the true and false vocal cords during the procedure.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: true vocal cord - either of the lower vocal folds that are involved in sound production
