rose gum

1. NOUN

/ roʊz ɡʌm / (AmE), / rəʊz ɡʌm / (BrE)
a very tall eucalyptus tree (Eucalyptus grandis) native to Queensland and New South Wales, valued for its timber and ornamental uses
tall Australian eucalyptus tree

Examples

The rose gum is often planted in parks for its impressive height and smooth bark.
Timber from the rose gum is highly prized in the construction industry.
Many native birds build their nests in the branches of the rose gum.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Eucalyptus grandis - the scientific name for rose gum
