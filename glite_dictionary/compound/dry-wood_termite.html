dry-wood termite

1. NOUN

/ ˈdraɪ ˌwʊd ˈtɜːrˌmaɪt / (AmE), / ˈdraɪ ˌwʊd ˈtɜːˌmaɪt / (BrE)
a termite species that lives in and feeds on dry wood not connected to the ground or soil
termite that infests dry wood

Examples

The house was seriously damaged by an infestation of dry-wood termites.
Unlike subterranean species, dry-wood termites do not require contact with soil.
Exterminators found evidence of dry-wood termite activity in the attic rafters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: termite - a social insect that feeds on wood and can cause structural damage
