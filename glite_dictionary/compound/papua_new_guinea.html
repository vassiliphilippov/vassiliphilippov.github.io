Papua New Guinea

1. PROPN

/ ˌpɑːpuə nuː ˈɡɪni / (AmE), / ˌpæpjuə njuː ˈɡɪni / (BrE)
a country in the southwestern Pacific, occupying the eastern half of the island of New Guinea and numerous offshore islands; gained independence in 1975 and is a member of the Commonwealth of Nations
country in the southwestern Pacific

Examples

She traveled to Papua New Guinea to study its unique wildlife and diverse cultures.
The government of Papua New Guinea declared a state of emergency after the natural disaster.
Many languages are spoken in Papua New Guinea, making it one of the most linguistically diverse countries in the world.

Etymology

Named after the island of New Guinea; 'Papua' from Malay, possibly meaning 'frizzy-haired'.
