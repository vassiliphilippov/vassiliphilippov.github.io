loggerhead shrike

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɑɡɚˌhɛd ˈʃraɪk / (AmE), / ˈlɒɡəˌhɛd ˈʃraɪk / (BrE)
a North American bird species (Lanius ludovicianus) with distinctive black eye bands, commonly found in open habitats of the southeastern United States
North American bird with black eye bands

Examples

We spotted a loggerhead shrike perched on a wire above the field.
The loggerhead shrike is known for its habit of impaling prey on thorns.
Conservationists are working to protect the loggerhead shrike from habitat loss.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shrike - any of various birds in the family Laniidae known for their predatory habits

Etymology

From 'loggerhead' (blockhead, large head) + 'shrike' (from Old English 'scric', a shrike bird).
