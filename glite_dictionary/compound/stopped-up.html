stopped-up

1. ADJ

/ stɑpt ʌp / (AmE), / stɒpt ʌp / (BrE)
(of a nose) blocked and unable to allow air to pass through; congested.
blocked, congested (of a nose)

Examples

Whenever I have a cold, my stopped-up nose makes it hard to breathe at night.
She was frustrated because her stopped-up sinuses just wouldn’t clear.
Allergy season left him with a constantly stopped-up nose.

Synset Examples

a stopped (or stopped-up) nose

Related Terms

  • Synonym: congested - (of a part of the body) blocked with fluid; especially referring to a nose
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01626075-s

2. ADJ

/ stɑpt ˈʌp / (AmE), / stɒpt ˈʌp / (BrE)
(of an opening or passage) blocked so that nothing can pass through, often due to being filled with something.
blocked, filled opening or passage

Examples

My kitchen sink was stopped-up for hours until the plumber arrived.
She had trouble breathing because her nose was stopped-up from the cold.
We couldn't use the bathtub because the drain was completely stopped-up.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blocked - unable to pass through due to obstruction
  • Synonym: clogged - blocked with accumulated matter
  • Antonym: clear - free from blockage or obstruction
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01402473-s
Synset POS: s
