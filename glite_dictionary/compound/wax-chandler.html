wax-chandler

1. NOUN

/ wæks ˈtʃændlər / (AmE), / wæks ˈtʃændlə / (BrE)
a dealer or seller of wax candles, particularly in historical contexts
person who sells wax candles
archaic

Examples

The wax-chandler kept his shop well-stocked with candles for every household in town.
In medieval London, the wax-chandler belonged to a powerful guild.
She purchased a set of beeswax candles from the local wax-chandler before the festival.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chandler - a dealer in supplies, especially for ships or candles

Etymology

From 'wax' (the substance) + 'chandler' (dealer or maker of candles), with 'chandler' from Old French 'chandelier'.
