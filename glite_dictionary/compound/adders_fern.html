adder's fern

1. NOUN

/ ˈædərz fɜrn / (AmE), / ˈadəz fɜːn / (BrE)
A mat-forming fern with creeping rootstocks and large pinnatifid fronds, found in North America, Europe, Africa, and East Asia.
mat-forming fern with creeping rootstocks

Examples

The shady hillside was covered in lush adder's fern growing between the rocks.
Botanists studying the region took samples of the abundant adder's fern for analysis.
Gardeners may use adder's fern as ground cover in areas with moist soil and partial shade.
