Mount Olympus

1. PROPN

/ maʊnt əˈlɪmpəs / (AmE), / maʊnt əˈlɪmpəs / (BrE)
the highest mountain in Greece, traditionally regarded in ancient Greek mythology as the dwelling place of the gods
highest mountain in Greece; mythological home of the gods

Examples

Many tourists hike up Mount Olympus to experience its breathtaking views and rich mythology.
In ancient myths, Mount Olympus was considered the home of Zeus and the other Greek gods.
Clouds often shroud the summit of Mount Olympus, adding to its mystical reputation.

Etymology

From Greek 'Ólympos', the name of the mountain in Greek mythology.
