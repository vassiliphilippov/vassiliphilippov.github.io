chromium steel

1. NOUN

/ ˈkroʊ.mi.əm stil / (AmE), / ˈkrəʊ.mi.əm stiːl / (BrE)
a type of steel alloy that contains chromium, making it resistant to corrosion
steel alloy containing chromium

Examples

Many kitchen utensils are made from chromium steel to prevent rusting.
The manufacturer developed a new grade of chromium steel for use in harsh environments.
The addition of chromium increases the durability of chromium steel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stainless steel - steel alloy with chromium, highly resistant to staining and rust
