Typhoid Mary

1. NOUN

/ ˈtaɪˌfɔɪd ˈmæri / (AmE), / ˈtʌɪfɔɪd ˈmari / (BrE)
A person who, often unwittingly, spreads disease or trouble to others; originally, the nickname of Mary Mallon (1870–1938), a cook in the United States who was an asymptomatic carrier of typhoid fever and infected many people.
person who spreads disease or trouble

Examples

The careless lab technician became a Typhoid Mary, unwittingly contaminating several samples with bacteria.
After her virus outbreak, she was branded a modern Typhoid Mary by her coworkers.
The company feared having a Typhoid Mary in the ranks, spreading computer viruses through the network.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carrier - a person or animal that transmits a disease without being affected

Etymology

From the nickname given to Mary Mallon, an asymptomatic carrier of typhoid fever in early 20th-century America.
