superfamily Tineoidea

1. NOUN

/ ˌsuːpərˌfæməli tɪˈniːɔɪdiə / (AmE), / ˌsuːpəˌfæmɪli tɪˈniːɔɪdɪə / (BrE)
A superfamily of small moths including clothes moths, carpet moths, and leaf miners.
superfamily of small moths

Examples

The superfamily Tineoidea includes both notorious clothes moths and some species harmless to humans.
Entomologists study the superfamily Tineoidea to understand pest behavior affecting textiles.
Many species within the superfamily Tineoidea are adapted to feeding on dried organic materials.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clothes moth - a small moth whose larvae feed on wool, fur, or feathers

Etymology

From New Latin 'Tineoidea', combining 'Tinea' (type genus) and '-oidea' (indicating a superfamily).
