rabbit bush

1. NOUN

/ ˈræbɪt ˌbʊʃ / (AmE), / ˈræbɪt ˌbʊʃ / (BrE)
a pleasantly aromatic shrub with slender, flexible, hairy branches and dense clusters of small yellow flowers, common in western North American plains and a source of yellow dye
aromatic North American shrub; yellow-flowered, dye source

Examples

In late summer, the rabbit bush blazed with bright yellow flowers across the desert landscape.
Native artists used the rabbit bush to create natural yellow dyes for wool and basketry.
Jackrabbits often hide beneath the rabbit bush to escape the midday heat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rubber rabbitbrush - another common name for rabbit bush (Chrysothamnus nauseosus)

Etymology

Named for its association with jackrabbits, which often shelter among its branches.
