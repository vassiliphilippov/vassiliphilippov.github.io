dog's-tooth violet

1. NOUN

/ dɔɡz tuːθ ˈvaɪəlɪt / (AmE), / dɒɡz tuːθ ˈvaɪəlɪt / (BrE)
a perennial woodland plant with spring-blooming flowers, widely cultivated for its ornamental value, especially species of the genus Erythronium.
spring-flowering woodland plant

Examples

In the shaded garden bed, the dog's-tooth violet bloomed early each spring with delicate purple flowers.
She identified the plant as a dog's-tooth violet by its mottled leaves and pointed bulb.
Botanists often refer to dog's-tooth violet when discussing woodland wildflowers of North America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Erythronium - a genus of flowering plants known as dog's-tooth violets

Etymology

Named for the shape of its bulb, which resembles a dog's tooth; 'violet' refers to the flower's color or form.
