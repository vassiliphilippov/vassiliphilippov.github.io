Irrawaddy River

1. PROPN

/ ˌɪrəˈwɑːdi ˈrɪvər / (AmE), / ˌɪrəˈwædi ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
the principal river of Myanmar, flowing from the north to the south and emptying into the Andaman Sea.
main river of Myanmar (Burma)

Examples

The Irrawaddy River is a vital waterway for transport and agriculture in Myanmar.
Many villages along the Irrawaddy River depend on its waters for fishing and irrigation.
Tourists often take cruises on the Irrawaddy River to enjoy the scenic views of the Burmese countryside.

Etymology

From Burmese 'Ayeyarwady', the local name for the river.
