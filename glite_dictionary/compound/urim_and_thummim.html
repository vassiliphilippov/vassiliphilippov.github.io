Urim and Thummim

1. NOUN

/ ˈjʊrɪm ənd ˈθʌmɪm / (AmE), / ˈjʊərɪm ənd ˈθʌmɪm / (BrE)
Objects mentioned in the Hebrew Bible, used by ancient Israelites, especially priests, to seek divine guidance by casting lots for yes-no answers.
biblical objects used for divination

Examples

The high priest consulted the Urim and Thummim to seek guidance from God.
Scholars have debated how the Urim and Thummim were actually used in ancient Israelite rituals.
The story mentions the Urim and Thummim as sacred tools for determining God's will.

Etymology

From Biblical Hebrew 'Urim' (lights) and 'Thummim' (perfections).
