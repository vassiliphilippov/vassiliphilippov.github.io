industrial plant

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈdʌstriəl plænt / (AmE), / ɪnˈdʌstriəl plɑːnt / (BrE)
a building or group of buildings where industrial work, especially manufacturing, takes place
factory or building for industrial production

Examples

The city council approved plans for a new industrial plant on the outskirts of town.
An industrial plant often employs hundreds or even thousands of workers to operate machinery.
Strict safety regulations must be followed in every industrial plant to protect workers.

Synset Examples

they built a large plant to manufacture automobiles

Related Terms

  • Synonym: factory - a building where goods are manufactured
  • Degree Lower: workshop - a room or building where things are made or repaired by hand
