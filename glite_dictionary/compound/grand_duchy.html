grand duchy

1. NOUN

/ ɡrænd ˈdʌtʃi / (AmE), / ɡrænd ˈdʌtʃi / (BrE)
a territory or state ruled by a grand duke or grand duchess
territory ruled by a grand duke or duchess

Examples

Luxembourg is the only existing grand duchy in the world today.
Historically, several grand duchies existed within the German Confederation.
The grand duchy was renowned for its independent government and cultural achievements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: duchy - a territory ruled by a duke or duchess

Etymology

From French 'grand-duché', from 'grand' (great) + 'duché' (duchy).
