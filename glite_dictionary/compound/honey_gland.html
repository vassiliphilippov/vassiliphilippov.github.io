honey gland

1. NOUN

/ ˈhʌni ɡlænd / (AmE), / ˈhʌni ɡlænd / (BrE)
a gland in certain plants or animals that secretes nectar or a substance similar to honey
gland that secretes nectar or honey-like substance

Examples

Many flowers have a honey gland at the base of each petal to attract pollinators.
The bee collected nectar from the honey gland hidden inside the blossom.
Botanists identified a small honey gland on the underside of the leaf.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nectary - a glandular organ in a flower that secretes nectar
