femoral biceps

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɛmərəl ˈbaɪsɛps / (AmE), / ˈfiːmərəl ˈbaɪsɛps / (BrE)
the biceps muscle of the thigh that flexes the knee and rotates the leg laterally
thigh muscle that flexes the knee
medical

Examples

The femoral biceps plays a key role in bending the knee during walking or running.
An injury to the femoral biceps can significantly reduce an athlete's mobility.
Proper stretching can help prevent strains in the femoral biceps during intense physical activity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: biceps femoris - the anatomical name for the femoral biceps muscle in the thigh

Etymology

From Latin 'femur' (thigh) and 'biceps' (two-headed muscle).
