hands-off

1. ADJ

/ hændz ˈɔf / (AmE), / hændz ˈɒf / (BrE)
Not involving active participation or intervention; characterized by deliberate non-interference.
not involving intervention

Examples

The new manager adopted a hands-off approach, allowing employees to make more decisions on their own.
Their parenting style is fairly hands-off, trusting the children to solve problems independently.
The government maintained a hands-off policy during the negotiations.

Synset Examples

a hands-off foreign policy

Related Terms

  • Antonym: interventionist - favoring direct involvement, especially in policy or management
