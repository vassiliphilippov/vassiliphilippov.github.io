Roman architecture

1. NOUN

/ ˈroʊ.mən ˌɑːr.kɪˈtɛk.tʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈrəʊ.mən ˌɑː.kɪˈtɛk.tʃə / (BrE)
the style and techniques of building and construction developed by the ancient Romans, characterized by the use of arches, vaults, and concrete
architecture of ancient Rome

Examples

The Colosseum is one of the finest examples of Roman architecture in the world.
Many modern stadiums are inspired by the principles of Roman architecture.
Students studied Roman architecture to understand the use of concrete in ancient structures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ancient Roman - a person from ancient Rome
  • Degree Lower: Greek architecture - the architecture of ancient Greece
  • Degree Higher: Gothic architecture - a style of architecture that superseded Romanesque in western Europe

Etymology

Roman: from Latin Romanus (of Rome); architecture: from Latin architectura (building).
