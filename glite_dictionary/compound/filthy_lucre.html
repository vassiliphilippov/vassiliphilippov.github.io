filthy lucre

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪlθi ˈluːkər / (AmE), / ˈfɪlθi ˈluːkə / (BrE)
money gained in a dishonest or morally wrong way; shameful profits.
dishonest or shameful profit

Examples

Some politicians are blinded by the promise of filthy lucre.
He refused to betray his friends for filthy lucre.
The charity rejected donations that came from filthy lucre.

Synset Examples

he would sell his soul for filthy lucre

Etymology

Lucre from Latin 'lucrum' (gain, profit); 'filthy' added for moral emphasis.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɪlθi ˈluːkər / (AmE), / ˈfɪlθi ˈluːkə / (BrE)
money gained in a dishonest or morally questionable manner
money obtained dishonorably

Examples

He was willing to betray his friends for filthy lucre.
The preacher warned his congregation about the dangers of filthy lucre.
Many politicians have been tempted by filthy lucre at some point in their careers.

Synset Examples

The company’s focus on filthy lucre led to its downfall.
He turned away from filthy lucre to pursue art.
She earned her money through hard work, not filthy lucre.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ill-gotten gains - money or benefits obtained dishonestly

Etymology

From Latin lucrum (profit, gain); 'filthy' added for moral emphasis, used since the 16th century.
