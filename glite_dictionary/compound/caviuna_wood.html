caviuna wood

1. NOUN

/ kɑː.viˈuː.nə wʊd / (AmE), / ka.vɪˈuː.nə wʊd / (BrE)
a heavy, hard, dark-colored wood streaked with black, obtained from an important Brazilian timber tree
Brazilian dark, streaked hardwood

Examples

This guitar was crafted from caviuna wood for its rich color and durability.
Many antique furniture pieces from Brazil feature caviuna wood for their elegant appearance.
The natural streaks in caviuna wood make each finished piece unique.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rosewood - a richly hued timber often used in fine woodworking, of which caviuna is a type

Etymology

From Portuguese 'caviúna', referring to certain Brazilian hardwood trees.
