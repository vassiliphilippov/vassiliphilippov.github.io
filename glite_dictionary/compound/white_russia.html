White Russia

1. PROPN

/ waɪt ˈrʌʃə / (AmE), / waɪt ˈrʌʃə / (BrE)
an alternative historical name for the country now known as Belarus, a landlocked republic in eastern Europe
alternative historical name for Belarus

Examples

The region historically known as White Russia became the modern nation of Belarus in 1991.
Many émigrés from White Russia settled in Paris after the Russian Revolution.
In older documents, Belarus is often referred to as White Russia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Belarus - current official name for White Russia, a country in eastern Europe

Etymology

From the English translation of Russian 'Belaya Rus’', literally meaning 'White Rus’'.
