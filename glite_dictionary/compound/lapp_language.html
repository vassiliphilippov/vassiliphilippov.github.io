Lapp language

1. NOUN

/ læp ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / læp ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Uralic language traditionally spoken by the nomadic Sami people (formerly known as Lapps) in northern Scandinavia and the Kola Peninsula
the language of the Sami people in northern Europe

Examples

She is studying the Lapp language as part of her research on Arctic cultures.
Very few people today are fluent speakers of the Lapp language.
Efforts are being made to preserve the Lapp language among younger generations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sami language - the language spoken by the Sami people

Etymology

Lapp, an older term for the Sami people, with 'language'.
