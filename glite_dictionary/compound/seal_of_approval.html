seal of approval

1. NOUN

/ siːl əv əˈpruːvəl / (AmE), / siːl ɒv əˈpruːvəl / (BrE)
an official sign or statement that something has been given formal or authoritative approval
official endorsement or approval

Examples

The new product received the company's seal of approval before being launched to the public.
Getting the chef's seal of approval meant the dish would be added to the menu.
When the project earned the director's seal of approval, everyone felt more confident moving forward.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: endorsement - an act of giving one's public approval or support to someone or something

Etymology

From 'seal' meaning a mark or stamp of authentication and 'approval'.
