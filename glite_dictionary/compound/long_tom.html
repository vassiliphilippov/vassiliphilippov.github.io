long tom

1. NOUN

/ lɔŋ tɑm / (AmE), / lɒŋ tɒm / (BrE)
a long-barrelled swivel cannon, especially one formerly used on ships in naval warfare
long-barrelled naval cannon

Examples

The ship was armed with a massive long tom for protection against privateers.
During the battle, the crew loaded the long tom and fired at the enemy frigate.
A single long tom dominated the forward deck, giving the vessel considerable range.

Etymology

Likely from 'long' referring to the barrel length, and 'Tom', used as a generic male name in English slang terms for large objects.
