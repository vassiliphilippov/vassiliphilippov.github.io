Japanese millet

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˈpæniz ˈmɪlɪt / (AmE), / dʒəˈpæniːz ˈmɪlɪt / (BrE)
a coarse annual grass cultivated for its edible seeds and as forage, particularly in Japan, southeastern Asia, and the United States
grass grown for edible seeds and forage

Examples

Farmers often plant Japanese millet as a summer crop for both grain and animal feed.
Waterfowl are attracted to wetlands where Japanese millet is grown.
The introduction of Japanese millet helped diversify local agriculture in the region.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: millet - any of various small-seeded grasses cultivated as cereal crops or forage plants
