postmaster general

1. NOUN

/ ˌpoʊstˌmæstər ˈdʒɛnərəl / (AmE), / ˌpəʊstˌmɑːstə ˈdʒɛnərəl / (BrE)
the official responsible for overseeing the national postal service of a country
head of a national postal service

Examples

The postmaster general announced changes to the mail delivery schedule last week.
During her tenure as postmaster general, she implemented several important reforms to modernize the postal service.
Each year, the postmaster general presents a report to the government on postal operations.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: postmaster - an official in charge of a local post office

Etymology

From 'postmaster' (manager of a post office) + 'general' (in the sense of 'chief').
