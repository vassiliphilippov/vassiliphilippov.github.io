Shah of Iran

1. NOUN

/ ʃɑː əv ɪˈræn / (AmE), / ʃɑː əv ɪˈrɑːn / (BrE)
the title held by the hereditary monarch of Iran until the Iranian Revolution of 1979
hereditary monarch of Iran (historical)

Examples

The last Shah of Iran was overthrown during the 1979 revolution.
Numerous international leaders met with the Shah of Iran in the 1970s.
Reforms led by the Shah of Iran dramatically changed the country's society.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shah - a title for kings of Iran; Persian monarch
  • Synonym: monarch - a sovereign head of state, especially a king or queen

Etymology

From Persian 'shāh' (king) and English place name 'Iran'.
