razor fish

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪzər fɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈreɪzə fɪʃ / (BrE)
any of several small wrasse species with compressed, sharp-edged heads, found in the West Indies and Mediterranean Sea.
type of small wrasse fish

Examples

The razor fish is known for its unusual body shape and sharp-edged head.
Divers often spot the colorful razor fish swimming among coral reefs.
Some species of razor fish can quickly burrow into the sand for protection.
