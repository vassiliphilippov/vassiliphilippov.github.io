notary public

1. NOUN

/ ˈnoʊ.t̬ə.ri ˈpʌb.lɪk / (AmE), / ˈnəʊ.tə.ri ˈpʌb.lɪk / (BrE)
an official authorized to witness signatures, certify documents, and take depositions for legal purposes
official authorized to certify documents
legal

Examples

You must bring your passport and the document to a notary public for certification.
She visited a notary public to have her signature witnessed before submitting the agreement.
The notary public verified the authenticity of the legal document in the presence of both parties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: notary - an official authorized to perform certain legal formalities

Etymology

From Latin 'notarius' (secretary, clerk) and Middle English 'publique' (public).
