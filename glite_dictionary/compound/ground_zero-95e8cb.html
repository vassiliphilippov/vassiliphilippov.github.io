ground zero

1. NOUN

/ ɡraʊnd ˈzɪəɹoʊ / (AmE), / ɡraʊnd ˈzɪərəʊ / (BrE)
the exact point on the surface directly beneath or most affected by the detonation of a bomb, especially a nuclear one
site directly below a bomb explosion

Examples

The scientists studied radiation effects at ground zero after the explosion.
Only rubble remained at ground zero following the missile strike.
Access to ground zero was restricted due to safety concerns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: epicenter - the point on the earth's surface vertically above the focus of an earthquake

Etymology

Originally U.S. military slang, first used to refer to the point directly beneath the detonation of an atomic bomb (1940s).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɡraʊnd ˈzɪəroʊ / (AmE), / ˌɡraʊnd ˈzɪərəʊ / (BrE)
the point on the earth's surface directly above, below, or at the exact location of an explosion, especially of a nuclear weapon.
point of a nuclear explosion

Examples

The entire city was obliterated within seconds of the bomb detonating at ground zero.
Scientists studied the aftermath at ground zero to assess the explosion's impact.
Emergency responders were cautious as they approached ground zero after the nuclear test.

Etymology

From 'ground' (surface of the earth) plus 'zero' (reference point of measurement); popularized during nuclear testing in the 1940s.

3. NOUN

/ ɡraʊnd ˈzɪəroʊ / (AmE), / ɡraʊnd ˈzɪərəʊ / (BrE)
the place where a major disaster or attack has occurred, often considered the starting point of the event or its effects
site of a major disaster or attack

Examples

After the hurricane, officials set up an aid station at ground zero to help survivors.
The explosion reduced the surrounding buildings to rubble, leaving ground zero barely recognizable.
News crews reported live from ground zero as emergency responders worked around the clock.

Synset Examples

They returned to ground zero to start rebuilding after the disaster.
The site became ground zero for the relief efforts.
The community was at ground zero after the tornado hit.

Etymology

Originally military slang referring to the point on the earth's surface closest to a nuclear explosion (from 'ground' + 'zero', meaning zero distance from the explosion).
