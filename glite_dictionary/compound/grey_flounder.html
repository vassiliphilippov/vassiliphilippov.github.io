grey flounder

1. NOUN

/ ɡreɪ ˈflaʊndər / (AmE), / ɡreɪ ˈflaʊndə / (BrE)
A species of flounder found along the coasts from North Carolina to Florida and in the eastern Gulf of Mexico.
a flounder species from southeastern US coasts

Examples

Fishermen often seek the grey flounder in the shallow coastal waters of the Gulf of Mexico.
The grey flounder is well-adapted to sandy or muddy sea floors, where it lies camouflaged.
Local seafood restaurants feature dishes made with freshly caught grey flounder.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flounder - a type of flatfish
