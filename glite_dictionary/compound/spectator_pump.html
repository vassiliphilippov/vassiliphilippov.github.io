spectator pump

1. NOUN

/ spɛkˈteɪtɚ pʌmp / (AmE), / spɛkˈteɪtə pʌmp / (BrE)
A woman's shoe with a medium heel, typically featuring contrasting colors on the toe and heel.
women's medium-heeled shoe with contrasting toe and heel

Examples

She wore a pair of classic spectator pumps to the garden party.
The spectator pump became popular in the 1930s and remains a timeless fashion choice.
Her black and white spectator pumps perfectly complemented her vintage dress.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pump - a low-cut shoe without laces

Etymology

From 'spectator' (onlooker) and 'pump' (a style of shoe); named for shoes worn to sporting events in the early 20th century.
