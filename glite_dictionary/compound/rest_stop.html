rest stop

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛst ˌstɑp / (AmE), / ˈrɛst ˌstɒp / (BrE)
a designated area next to a major road where drivers can stop to rest, often providing facilities such as toilets, picnic areas, or vending machines
area beside a road for drivers to rest

Examples

We pulled into a rest stop to stretch our legs after driving for several hours.
There’s a clean rest stop about twenty miles ahead if you need a break.
Most highways have at least one rest stop every fifty miles or so.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: service station - a roadside facility offering fuel, food, and other services, often larger than a rest stop

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɛst stɑp / (AmE), / ˈrɛst stɒp / (BrE)
A place along a road or highway where travelers can stop to rest, use facilities, or get refreshments.
place for travelers to pause on a journey

Examples

We decided to take a break at the rest stop before continuing our journey.
The highway rest stop offered clean bathrooms and a small café for travelers.
After three hours of driving, they pulled into a rest stop to stretch their legs.

Synset Examples

The way station served as a rest stop for travelers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: service station - place on a highway providing fuel, food, and facilities for travelers
  • Synonym: lay-by - (British) roadside area where vehicles can stop temporarily
