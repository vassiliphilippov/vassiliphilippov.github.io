red panda

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛd ˈpændə / (AmE), / ˈrɛd ˈpandə / (BrE)
a small mammal with reddish-brown fur, native to the eastern Himalayas and southwestern China, resembling a raccoon and unrelated to the giant panda.
small, reddish-brown Himalayan mammal

Examples

The red panda spends much of its time climbing trees and feeding on bamboo.
Despite its name, the red panda is not closely related to the giant panda.
We saw a playful red panda at the wildlife sanctuary during our visit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: giant panda - large black-and-white bear native to China
