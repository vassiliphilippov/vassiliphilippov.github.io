balanced budget

1. NOUN

/ ˈbælənst ˈbʌdʒɪt / (AmE), / ˈbælənst ˈbʌdʒɪt / (BrE)
A financial plan in which expenditures do not exceed revenues.
budget where expenditures equal receipts

Examples

The government's commitment to a balanced budget was praised by financial analysts.
Achieving a balanced budget often requires cutting unnecessary spending or increasing revenue.
Many households strive for a balanced budget to avoid accumulating debt.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: deficit budget - a budget in which expenditures exceed income
  • Antonym: surplus budget - a budget in which income exceeds expenditures
