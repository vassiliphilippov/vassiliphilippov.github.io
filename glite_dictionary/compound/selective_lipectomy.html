selective lipectomy

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈlɛktɪv laɪˈpɛktəmi / (AmE), / sɪˈlɛktɪv lɪˈpɛktəmi / (BrE)
a plastic surgery operation that involves removing specific areas of fatty tissue from the body
surgical removal of targeted fat deposits
medical

Examples

The patient opted for a selective lipectomy to address stubborn fat pockets.
Advancements in selective lipectomy have made the procedure safer and less invasive.
Her doctor recommended a selective lipectomy rather than a more extensive surgery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: liposuction - a cosmetic surgery to remove fat from under the skin

Etymology

From 'selective', meaning chosen specifically, and 'lipectomy', from Greek lipos (fat) and ektomē (excision).
