four-stroke internal-combustion engine

1. NOUN

/ fɔr stroʊk ɪnˌtɜrnəl kəmˈbʌs.tʃən ˈɛn.dʒɪn / (AmE), / fɔː strəʊk ɪnˌtɜː.nəl kəmˈbʌs.tʃən ˈen.dʒɪn / (BrE)
an internal combustion engine that completes a power cycle in four strokes of the piston: intake, compression, power, and exhaust
internal combustion engine with four piston strokes

Examples

The majority of modern cars use a four-stroke internal-combustion engine for improved fuel efficiency.
He studied how the pistons move during each phase of the four-stroke internal-combustion engine cycle.
Advancements in the four-stroke internal-combustion engine design have helped reduce vehicle emissions.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: two-stroke engine - internal combustion engine completing a power cycle in two piston strokes
