macadamia tree

1. NOUN

/ ˌmækəˈdeɪmiə triː / (AmE), / ˌmækəˈdeɪmiə triː / (BrE)
A tree of the genus Macadamia, native to Australia, known for producing edible nuts.
tree producing edible macadamia nuts

Examples

The macadamia tree thrives in the subtropical regions of Australia and produces highly valued nuts.
Farmers are beginning to plant more macadamia trees because of the growing demand for their nuts.
She learned to identify a macadamia tree by its glossy leaves and distinctive fruit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: macadamia nut - the edible nut produced by the macadamia tree

Etymology

Named after Dr. John Macadam (1827–1865), a Scottish-Australian chemist.
