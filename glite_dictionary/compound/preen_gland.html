preen gland

1. NOUN

/ ˈprin ˌɡlænd / (AmE), / ˈpriːn ˌɡlænd / (BrE)
an oil-secreting gland located at the base of the tail in most birds, used to maintain feather condition
oil gland at base of bird's tail

Examples

The duck used its preen gland to spread oil over its feathers.
Scientists studied the composition of oil secreted by the preen gland.
Damage to a bird’s preen gland can affect its waterproofing abilities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: uropygial gland - alternative term for the preen gland

Etymology

From 'preen' (to trim or dress with the beak) and 'gland'.
