tanglefoot trap

1. NOUN

/ ˈtæŋɡəlˌfʊt træp / (AmE), / ˈtaŋɡl̩ˌfʊt træp / (BrE)
a sticky device used to trap insects, typically by using an adhesive coating to catch them when they land or crawl over the surface
sticky insect trap

Examples

The tanglefoot trap caught more bugs than expected this summer.
Farmers often hang tanglefoot traps in orchards to monitor and control insect populations.
After installing a tanglefoot trap near the kitchen window, we noticed a significant reduction in flies.

Synset Examples

The tanglefoot trap caught more bugs than expected.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flypaper - paper coated with a sticky substance to catch insects

Etymology

From the brand name Tanglefoot, known for its sticky pest control products, combined with 'trap'.
