cubic millimeter

1. NOUN

/ ˈkjuː.bɪk ˈmɪl.əˌmiː.t̬ɚ / (AmE), / ˈkjuː.bɪk ˈmɪl.ɪˌmiː.tə / (BrE)
a unit of volume in the metric system, equal to the volume of a cube with sides of 1 millimeter
metric unit of volume (mm³)

Examples

A single drop of water contains thousands of cubic millimeters of liquid.
The tumor measured less than one cubic millimeter on the scan.
Scientists count the number of bacteria in a cubic millimeter of soil.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: cubic centimeter - a metric unit of volume equal to a cube 1 centimeter on each edge
  • Degree Higher: liter - a metric unit of volume, the base unit in the metric system

Etymology

From Latin 'cubus' (cube) and French 'millimètre' (millimeter).
