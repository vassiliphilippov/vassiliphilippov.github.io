New Zealand white pine

1. NOUN

/ nuː ˈziː.lənd waɪt paɪn / (AmE), / njuː ˈziː.lənd waɪt paɪn / (BrE)
an evergreen tree native to New Zealand, valued for its light, easily worked wood; also known as kahikatea
New Zealand evergreen tree; prized for wood

Examples

The timber from the New Zealand white pine is highly prized in carpentry for its workability.
Many wetlands in New Zealand are dominated by the New Zealand white pine.
Conservation efforts are in place to protect the native New Zealand white pine forests.
