point lace

1. NOUN

/ pɔɪnt leɪs / (AmE), / pɔɪnt leɪs / (BrE)
a type of lace made by working with a needle in a buttonhole stitch on a paper pattern
lace made with needle and buttonhole stitch

Examples

Her grandmother taught her how to make point lace using traditional techniques.
The museum collection includes several rare pieces of 18th-century point lace.
She admired the intricate designs of the point lace on the wedding veil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: needlepoint - lace or embroidery made with a needle and thread
  • Antonym: bobbin lace - lace made by twisting threads wound on bobbins

Etymology

From French 'point' (stitch, lace) and English 'lace'.
