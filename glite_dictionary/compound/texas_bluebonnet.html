Texas bluebonnet

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛksəs ˈbluːˌbɑnɪt / (AmE), / ˈtɛksəs ˈbluːˌbɒnɪt / (BrE)
a blue-flowered lupine (Lupinus texensis), native to Texas and surrounding regions, and known as the state flower of Texas
blue-flowered lupine native to Texas
american

Examples

Every spring the fields of central Texas are covered with Texas bluebonnets.
The Texas bluebonnet is the official state flower of Texas, celebrated during local festivals.
Photographers often capture families posing among the blooming Texas bluebonnets in April.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bluebonnet - any of several blue-flowered lupines native to North America

Etymology

Named for the U.S. state of Texas and 'bluebonnet,' describing the flower's blue color and bonnet-like shape.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɛk.səs ˈbluːˌbɑ.nət / (AmE), / ˈtɛk.səs ˈbluːˌbɒ.nɪt / (BrE)
a low-growing annual herb native to southwestern United States, especially Texas, with silky leaves and blue flowers, often causing livestock poisoning
a blue-flowered herb from Texas; livestock poison

Examples

Fields across Texas are covered in Texas bluebonnet blooms every spring.
The Texas bluebonnet is the state flower of Texas and is admired for its striking color.
Some ranchers have to be cautious, as Texas bluebonnet plants can be harmful to cattle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bluebonnet - common name for several species of lupines with blue flowers
