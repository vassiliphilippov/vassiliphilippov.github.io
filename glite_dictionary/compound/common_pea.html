common pea

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmən pi / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən piː / (BrE)
A plant (Pisum sativum) that produces peas, typically eaten fresh rather than dried.
plant producing edible peas

Examples

The common pea thrives in cool climates and is a staple in many vegetable gardens.
Many gardeners prefer to harvest common pea pods when they are young and tender.
The common pea requires well-drained soil and regular watering to produce healthy crops.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pea - the small, round green seed produced by the common pea plant
