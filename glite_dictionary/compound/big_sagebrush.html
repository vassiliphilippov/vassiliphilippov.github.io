big sagebrush

1. NOUN

/ bɪɡ ˈseɪdʒˌbrʌʃ / (AmE), / bɪɡ ˈseɪdʒˌbrʌʃ / (BrE)
an aromatic shrub (Artemisia tridentata) native to arid regions of western North America, having silvery-grey leaves and commonly found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems
aromatic shrub of western North America

Examples

The big sagebrush is a dominant plant species across much of the Great Basin region.
Cattle often graze among the big sagebrush in these high desert areas.
The silvery leaves of big sagebrush stand out against the dry soil in summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sagebrush - common name for several aromatic shrubs, especially in North America
