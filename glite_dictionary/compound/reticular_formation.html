reticular formation

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈtɪkjələr fɔrˈmeɪʃən / (AmE), / rɪˈtɪkjʊlə fɔːˈmeɪʃən / (BrE)
a complex network of neurons located in the central core of the brainstem that regulates states of arousal, sleep, attention, and muscle tone
network of neurons in the brainstem
medical

Examples

Damage to the reticular formation can severely affect a person's ability to stay awake and alert.
The reticular formation plays a vital role in controlling sleep-wake cycles in humans.
Researchers are studying how the reticular formation influences muscle tone and reflexes.

Etymology

From Latin 'reticulum' (little net) and 'formation' (the act of forming).
