paper birch

1. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪpɚ bɝːtʃ / (AmE), / ˈpeɪpə bɜːtʃ / (BrE)
a North American birch tree (Betula papyrifera) with distinctive peeling white bark, often used for making baskets and canoes
a North American birch with peeling white bark

Examples

A row of paper birch trees lined the path, their white bark gleaming in the sun.
The indigenous people crafted baskets from the bark of the paper birch.
We stopped to admire a tall paper birch growing near the lake.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: birch - a tree of the genus Betula, with characteristic bark
