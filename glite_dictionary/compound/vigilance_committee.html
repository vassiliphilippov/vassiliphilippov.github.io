vigilance committee

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪdʒɪləns kəˌmɪti / (AmE), / ˈvɪdʒɪləns kəˌmɪti / (BrE)
A group of volunteers organized to maintain order and enforce laws in a community where an effective legal system is not present or is considered inadequate.
group maintaining order where law is weak

Examples

During the Gold Rush, a vigilance committee took control of law enforcement in the town.
The citizens formed a vigilance committee to protect themselves from increasing crime.
Without effective police, a vigilance committee became the main authority in the region.

Etymology

From 'vigilance' (watchfulness) + 'committee'; used in 19th-century American frontier history.
