wise counsel

1. NOUN

/ waɪz ˈkaʊnsəl / (AmE), / waɪz ˈkaʊnsəl / (BrE)
good advice or guidance, especially given by someone experienced or judicious
good advice or guidance

Examples

She always sought his wise counsel before making important decisions.
The organization relied on the wise counsel of its senior members during difficult times.
He offered wise counsel that helped resolve the conflict peacefully.

Synset Examples

Master John Goodfellow was renowned for his wise counsel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: advice - an opinion or recommendation about what someone should do
