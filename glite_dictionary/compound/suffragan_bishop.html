suffragan bishop

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌfrəɡən ˈbɪʃəp / (AmE), / ˈsʌfrəɡən ˈbɪʃəp / (BrE)
a bishop appointed to assist and act as subordinate to the diocesan bishop within a diocese
assistant or subordinate bishop

Examples

The suffragan bishop was appointed to oversee the parishes in the eastern part of the diocese.
As a suffragan bishop, she supports the diocesan bishop in pastoral care and administration.
The cathedral welcomed its new suffragan bishop with a special service attended by the community.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: diocesan bishop - the chief bishop in charge of a diocese

Etymology

From Medieval Latin suffraganeus (assistant) + bishop.
