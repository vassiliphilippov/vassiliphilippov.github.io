heir presumptive

1. NOUN

/ ɛr prɪˈzʌmptɪv / (AmE), / eə prɪˈzʌmptɪv / (BrE)
A person who is expected to inherit a title or estate, but whose claim can be overridden if a more eligible heir is born.
expected inheritor; claim can be displaced by a new heir

Examples

Until the birth of a son, the princess was considered the heir presumptive to the throne.
As heir presumptive, she prepared for the possibility of inheriting the family's titles and lands.
The king's cousin served as heir presumptive until an official successor was announced.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: heir apparent - a person whose right to inherit cannot be set aside by the birth of another heir

Etymology

From Old French *heir* (heir), from Latin *heres* (heir), and Latin *praesumptivus* (anticipated, supposed).
