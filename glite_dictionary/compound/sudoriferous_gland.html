sudoriferous gland

1. NOUN

/ ˌsuːdəˈrɪfərəs ɡlænd / (AmE), / ˌsjuːdəˈrɪfərəs ɡlænd / (BrE)
a small gland in the skin that produces and secretes sweat
gland in skin that produces sweat
medical

Examples

The sudoriferous gland plays a crucial role in regulating body temperature through perspiration.
During intense exercise, the sudoriferous glands become more active to cool the body down.
A blockage in a sudoriferous gland can sometimes lead to skin irritation or infection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sweat gland - gland in the skin that secretes sweat

Etymology

From Latin 'sudor' (sweat) + 'ferre' (to bear, carry).
