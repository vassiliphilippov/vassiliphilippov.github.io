Kachinic language

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑː.tʃɪˈnɪk ˈlæŋ.ɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌkæ.tʃɪˈnɪk ˈlæŋ.ɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
A group of Tibeto-Burman languages spoken in northern Myanmar (Burma), and nearby regions of China and India.
Tibeto-Burman language group of N. Myanmar and bordering regions

Examples

The Kachinic language family includes several dialects spoken across northern Myanmar and its borders.
Researchers are studying the grammar of a Kachinic language to understand its relation to other Tibeto-Burman languages.
Efforts are underway to preserve the Kachinic language as younger generations adopt more dominant regional languages.

Etymology

From 'Kachin', an ethnic group in northern Myanmar, and 'language'.
