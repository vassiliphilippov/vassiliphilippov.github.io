numerical analysis

1. NOUN

/ nuːˈmɛr.ɪ.kəl əˈnæl.ə.sɪs / (AmE), / njuːˈmɛr.ɪ.kəl əˈnæl.ə.sɪs / (BrE)
the branch of mathematics concerned with designing and analyzing algorithms to approximate solutions for problems involving calculus and other continuous mathematics
mathematical study of approximation algorithms
medical

Examples

She decided to specialize in numerical analysis during her postgraduate studies in mathematics.
The software was developed to perform numerical analysis on complex engineering problems.
Many scientific breakthroughs rely on numerical analysis to process vast amounts of data.

Etymology

From Latin numerus (number) and Greek analysis (breaking up, loosening).
