red-bellied terrapin

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɛdˈbɛlid ˈtɛrəˌpɪn / (AmE), / ˌrɛdˈbɛlɪd ˈtɛrəpɪn / (BrE)
a freshwater turtle native to Chesapeake Bay tributaries, characterized by red markings on its lower shell
turtle with red-marked lower shell

Examples

Researchers studied the habitat of the red-bellied terrapin in the rivers around Chesapeake Bay.
The red-bellied terrapin is easily identified by the vivid red coloring on its underside.
Conservation efforts have increased the population of red-bellied terrapins in recent years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: terrapin - a small species of turtle, especially of the family Emydidae
