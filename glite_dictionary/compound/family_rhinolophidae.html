family Rhinolophidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ˌraɪnəˈlɑːfɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli ˌraɪnəˈlɒfɪˌdiː / (BrE)
a family of bats known as the Old World leaf-nosed bats, found chiefly in Africa, Asia, and Europe, notable for their distinctive horseshoe-shaped noseleafs used in echolocation.
a family of Old World leaf-nosed bats

Examples

The family Rhinolophidae includes more than seventy species of leaf-nosed bats found mainly in the Old World.
Researchers studied echolocation patterns in the family Rhinolophidae to understand how these bats navigate in darkness.
Members of the family Rhinolophidae are distinguished by their complex nasal structures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Rhinolophus - genus of horseshoe bats within family Rhinolophidae

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'Rhinolophus' (the genus name, from Greek rhinos 'nose' + lophos 'crest') with the taxonomic suffix '-idae' (denoting a family).
