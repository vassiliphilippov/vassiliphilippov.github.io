Eastern cottonwood

1. NOUN

/ ˈiːstərn ˈkɑtənˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈiːstən ˈkɒtənˌwʊd / (BrE)
a species of poplar tree (Populus deltoides) native to eastern and central North America, known for its rapid growth and abundant foliage
a North American poplar tree species

Examples

Many parks in the Midwest are lined with towering Eastern cottonwood trees.
The Eastern cottonwood is prized for its shade and rapid growth along riverbanks.
In the spring, the seeds of the Eastern cottonwood drift through the air like snow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poplar - a type of tree in the genus Populus
