trunk call

1. NOUN

/ trʌŋk kɔl / (AmE), / trʌŋk kɔːl / (BrE)
a telephone call made to a location outside the local calling area, typically requiring a special charge; chiefly British and now historical.
telephone call outside local area (chiefly British, historical)
british archaic

Examples

She placed a trunk call to her parents in London from the countryside.
In the 1960s, making a trunk call often required operator assistance.
He reminisced about the days when a trunk call was a rare and expensive event.

Synset Examples

I talked to her by long distance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: long-distance call - a telephone call made to a distant location, especially in the US

Etymology

From 'trunk' referring to main telephone lines and 'call' meaning a telephone communication.
