rough-stemmed goldenrod

1. NOUN

/ ˈrəf stɛmd ˈɡoʊldənˌrɑd / (AmE), / ˈrʌf stɛmd ˈɡəʊldənˌrɒd / (BrE)
a species of goldenrod (Solidago rugosa) native to North America, characterized by its rough-haired stems and clusters of small yellow flowers
a North American goldenrod species

Examples

The rough-stemmed goldenrod grows wild along roadsides and in open fields.
Many gardeners appreciate rough-stemmed goldenrod for its resilience and bright yellow blooms in late summer.
You can identify rough-stemmed goldenrod by the rough texture of its stems and its dense flower clusters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: goldenrod - a group of plants of the genus Solidago, with yellow flowers
