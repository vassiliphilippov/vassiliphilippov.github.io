Nova salmon

1. NOUN

/ ˈnoʊvə ˈsæmən / (AmE), / ˈnəʊvə ˈsæmən / (BrE)
Salmon that has been brine-cured with less salt than traditional methods, sometimes with sugar added, resulting in a milder flavor; often used for bagels and deli dishes.
mild, brine-cured salmon, sometimes with sugar

Examples

She ordered a bagel topped with Nova salmon and cream cheese for breakfast.
At the deli, you can choose between lox and Nova salmon for your sandwich.
The chef prefers using Nova salmon because of its milder, less salty taste.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lox - brine-cured salmon, often saltier than Nova salmon
  • Synonym: smoked salmon - salmon that has been cured and smoked

Etymology

"Nova" from "Nova Scotia," referring to the region where this style originated.
