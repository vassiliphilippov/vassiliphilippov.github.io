mizzen course

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪzən ˌkɔrs / (AmE), / ˈmɪzən ˌkɔːs / (BrE)
the lowest sail set on the mizzenmast of a sailing ship.
lowest sail on the mizzenmast

Examples

The crew quickly lowered the mizzen course as the storm approached.
During the regatta, the mizzen course was trimmed to maximize speed.
Repairing a tear in the mizzen course required skilled hands and steady nerves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mizzenmast - the third mast from the bow in a ship with three or more masts

Etymology

From 'mizzen' (referring to the third mast of a ship) and 'course' (a principal sail on a mast), both with nautical origins.
