casino capitalism

1. NOUN

/ kəˌsinoʊ ˈkæpɪtəlɪzəm / (AmE), / kəˌsiːnəʊ ˈkapɪtəlɪzəm / (BrE)
an economic system characterized by excessive speculation, risk-taking, and financial instability, resembling gambling in a casino
an economic system focused on speculation and risk-taking

Examples

Many economists argue that casino capitalism contributed to the 2008 financial crisis.
Some critics believe casino capitalism undermines long-term economic growth and stability.
The financial sector has been accused of engaging in casino capitalism by taking excessive risks.

Synset Examples

Critics say casino capitalism risks the economy's stability.
Many believe casino capitalism promotes reckless financial behavior.
The rise of casino capitalism has changed investment strategies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: speculation - the practice of engaging in risky financial transactions for profit
  • Antonym: financial stability - a state in which the financial system is resistant to shocks

Etymology

Coined by analogy to gambling in casinos, combining 'casino' and 'capitalism'.
