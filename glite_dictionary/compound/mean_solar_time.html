mean solar time

1. NOUN

/ min ˈsoʊlɚ taɪm / (AmE), / miːn ˈsəʊlə taɪm / (BrE)
Time measured according to the motion of an imaginary sun moving at a constant speed along the celestial equator, used as the basis for standard timekeeping.
time based on the uniform motion of the mean sun

Examples

Before atomic clocks, most cities set their clocks according to mean solar time.
International time zones are often based on mean solar time at a central longitude.
Greenwich mean solar time served as the world standard for many years.
