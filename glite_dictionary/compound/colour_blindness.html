colour blindness

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌlər ˌblaɪnd.nəs / (AmE), / ˈkʌlə ˌblaɪnd.nəs / (BrE)
An inability or decreased ability to distinguish between certain colours, especially as a result of a genetic condition.
inability to distinguish certain colours
medical british

Examples

He was diagnosed with colour blindness after failing a vision test at school.
Colour blindness is more common in men than in women.
Her career as a pilot was restricted due to her colour blindness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: color blindness - Inability to distinguish between certain colours; American English spelling
