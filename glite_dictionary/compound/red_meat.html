red meat

1. NOUN

/ rɛd mit / (AmE), / rɛd miːt / (BrE)
Meat from mammals such as beef, lamb, or venison, which is dark in color before cooking, as opposed to poultry or fish.
meat from mammals, dark before cooking

Examples

Doctors often recommend eating less red meat to reduce the risk of heart disease.
Beef and lamb are popular types of red meat in many cultures.
Unlike red meat, chicken is classified as white meat.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: white meat - meat that is light in color before cooking, such as poultry or fish

2. NOUN

/ rɛd mit / (AmE), / rɛd miːt / (BrE)
Meat from mammals such as beef, lamb, or pork, typically red when raw and often considered less healthy than white meat.
meat from mammals (beef, lamb, pork)

Examples

Many people try to avoid red meat for health reasons.
The restaurant offers several dishes made with locally sourced red meat.
Nutritionists often compare red meat and white meat when discussing healthy diets.

Synset Examples

He enjoys red meat but tries to eat it sparingly.
Doctors recommend limiting red meat for better health.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: white meat - meat from poultry or fish, usually lighter in color
