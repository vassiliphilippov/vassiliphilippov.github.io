genus Tadorna

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs təˈdɔːrnə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs təˈdɔːnə / (BrE)
A genus of large ducks known as sheldrakes, found in Eurasia, Africa, and Australasia.
a genus of sheldrakes (ducks)

Examples

The genus Tadorna includes several species of strikingly colored sheldrakes.
Researchers observed a previously undocumented behavior among birds of the genus Tadorna.
Conservation efforts are underway to protect certain species within the genus Tadorna.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sheldrake - a large, often colorful duck of the genus Tadorna

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'genus' (type) and 'Tadorna' (the typical genus name for sheldrakes).
