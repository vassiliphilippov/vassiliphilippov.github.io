field corn

1. NOUN

/ fiːld kɔrn / (AmE), / fiːld kɔːn / (BrE)
A type of corn grown mainly for use as animal feed or for processing into grain products, not usually eaten fresh by humans.
corn grown for feed or grain, not eating

Examples

The farmer planted acres of field corn to supply his cattle with feed for the winter.
Unlike sweet corn, field corn is harvested when the kernels are dry and mature.
Most of the field corn grown in the Midwest is used for animal feed and industrial products.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sweet corn - corn eaten as a vegetable when immature
