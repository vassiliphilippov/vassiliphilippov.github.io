beech tree

1. NOUN

/ bitʃ tri / (AmE), / biːtʃ triː / (BrE)
a large deciduous tree with smooth grey bark, rounded spreading crown, and small edible triangular nuts inside prickly husks, native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere
large deciduous tree with grey bark and edible nuts

Examples

The beech tree in our backyard provides deep shade in the summer months.
Many animals feed on the nuts produced by the beech tree.
With its smooth bark and sturdy branches, the beech tree is easily recognizable in the forest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oak tree - large deciduous tree with lobed leaves and acorns
  • Synonym: birch tree - slender deciduous tree with characteristic white bark

Etymology

From Old English 'bēce' and 'trēow', referring to the beech tree.
