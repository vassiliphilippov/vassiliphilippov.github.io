railroad tie

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪlˌrɑd taɪ / (AmE), / ˈreɪlˌrɒd taɪ / (BrE)
A heavy timber or concrete beam laid across a railway track to support and fix the rails in place.
support beam for railway rails
american

Examples

The workers replaced several railroad ties along the track during the routine maintenance.
Each railroad tie must be carefully spaced to ensure track stability.
Creosote is often used to treat railroad ties to prevent decay.

Synset Examples

the British call a railroad tie a sleeper

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sleeper - British term for a railroad tie; a beam supporting and fixing railway rails
