deer's-ears

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɪrz ˌɪrz / (AmE), / ˈdɪəz ˌɪəz / (BrE)
a tall perennial herb of the genus Frasera, native to dry upland areas of western North America; known for its erect stems and narrow leaves resembling a deer's ears
a western North American herb (Frasera) with narrow leaves

Examples

The slopes were covered in deer's-ears, their pale green leaves shining in the sun.
Botanists surveyed populations of deer's-ears in several Californian counties.
We identified the herb by its tall stem and the distinctive shape of its deer's-ears leaves.

Etymology

Named for the narrow leaves' resemblance to the ears of a deer.
