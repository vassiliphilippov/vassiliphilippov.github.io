German rampion

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɜr.mən ˈræm.pi.ən / (AmE), / ˈdʒɜː.mən ˈræm.pi.ən / (BrE)
A coarse biennial plant of eastern North America with yellow evening-blooming flowers, naturalized in Europe.
plant with yellow evening flowers, naturalized in Europe

Examples

The wild German rampion has become common in several parts of Europe.
In late summer, German rampion flowers add a yellow glow to the meadows.
She identified the plant as a German rampion by its distinctive evening-blooming flowers.
