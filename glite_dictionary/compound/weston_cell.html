Weston cell

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɛstən sɛl / (AmE), / ˈwɛstən sɛl / (BrE)
a type of standard voltaic cell used as a reference for electromotive force in scientific measurements, invented by Edward Weston
standard reference voltaic cell

Examples

The laboratory uses a Weston cell to calibrate its voltmeters.
A Weston cell provides a very stable voltage under constant conditions.
Edward Weston invented the Weston cell in the late 19th century.

Etymology

Named after Edward Weston, the inventor.
