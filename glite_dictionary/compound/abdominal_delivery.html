abdominal delivery

1. NOUN

/ æbˈdɑːmɪnəl dɪˈlɪvəri / (AmE), / æbˈdɒmɪnəl dɪˈlɪvəri / (BrE)
the delivery of a baby by surgical incision through the abdominal wall and uterus; also known as a cesarean section
delivery of a baby by incision; cesarean section
medical

Examples

The doctor recommended an abdominal delivery due to complications during labor.
Her recovery from the abdominal delivery took several weeks, but both mother and baby were healthy.
In some cases, an abdominal delivery may be planned in advance if a vaginal birth is considered risky.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cesarean section - surgical procedure to deliver a baby through incisions in the abdomen and uterus

Etymology

From Latin 'abdomen' (belly) + 'delivery' (from Old French 'delivrer', set free).
