bleeding edge

1. NOUN

/ ˈbliːdɪŋ ɛdʒ / (AmE), / ˈbliːdɪŋ ɛdʒ / (BrE)
the most advanced and innovative stage of development, especially in technology, often so new that it may involve high risk or instability
the most advanced stage of innovation

Examples

The company prides itself on using bleeding edge technology to outpace competitors.
Investors are attracted to startups working on bleeding edge solutions.
Adopting bleeding edge tools can give your team a significant advantage, but also brings potential risks.

Synset Examples

They always invest in bleeding edge solutions to stay competitive.
The new app is considered bleeding edge in the market.
Our startup focuses on bleeding edge technology for better efficiency.
They showcased a bleeding-edge prototype at the tech conference.
The company is known for its bleeding-edge products that redefine industries.
His research is on bleeding-edge developments in artificial intelligence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cutting edge - the latest or most advanced stage in the development of something

Etymology

Formed by analogy with 'cutting edge', using 'bleeding' for greater intensity.
