preventive attack

1. NOUN

/ prɪˈvɛntɪv əˈtæk / (AmE), / prɪˈvɛntɪv əˈtæk / (BrE)
a military action taken to pre-empt and deter expected aggression by a hostile force
a pre-emptive military strike to deter expected aggression

Examples

The government justified the preventive attack as necessary to avoid a larger conflict.
Many experts debate whether a preventive attack is legal under international law.
The general ordered a preventive attack after receiving intelligence about an imminent threat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: preemptive strike - a military strike launched to prevent an imminent attack by the enemy

Etymology

From 'preventive', meaning intended to prevent, and 'attack', from Old French 'attaque'.
