Indian cress

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪndiən ˈkrɛs / (AmE), / ˌɪndiən ˈkrɛs / (BrE)
a strong-growing annual climbing plant (Tropaeolum majus) with large orange to yellowish flowers and edible seeds used like capers
climbing plant with orange-yellow flowers, edible seeds

Examples

She added fresh Indian cress leaves to the salad for a peppery flavor.
The garden was brightened by clusters of brilliant Indian cress flowers climbing the fence.
Pickled seeds of the Indian cress are often used as a substitute for capers in recipes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nasturtium - another name for Indian cress, especially in horticulture

Etymology

From association with India and cress (due to peppery taste resembling watercress); refers to Tropaeolum majus, a South American plant.
