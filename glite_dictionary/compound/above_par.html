above par

1. ADJ

/ əˈbʌv pɑr / (AmE), / əˈbʌv pɑː / (BrE)
better than average or expected in quality, performance, or value
better than average; superior

Examples

Her performance this season has been above par consistently.
The company's profits last quarter were above par compared to expectations.
The quality of the hotel rooms was definitely above par for the price.

Synset Examples

Her performance this season has been above par consistently.
The restaurant's service was above par during our visit.
He consistently delivers above par results in his work.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: par - an average or normal standard, especially in finance and sports

Etymology

From 'par,' originally a term in finance meaning the nominal or face value, later used more broadly to denote an average standard.
