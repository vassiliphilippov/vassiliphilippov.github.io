common time

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmən taɪm / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən taɪm / (BrE)
a musical time signature indicating four beats to each bar, typically represented by the symbol 'C'
musical time signature with four beats per bar

Examples

This piece is written in common time, making it easy for beginners to follow the rhythm.
The conductor reminded the orchestra that the next section switches from common time to triple meter.
Many pop songs use common time because of its simple and regular beat structure.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cut time - time signature indicating two beats per bar, also known as alla breve
