black bile

1. NOUN

/ blæk baɪl / (AmE), / blak baɪl / (BrE)
(historical) In ancient and medieval medicine, one of the four bodily humors, believed to be secreted by the kidneys or spleen and to cause sadness or melancholy.
bodily humor thought to cause melancholy
archaic

Examples

In ancient theory, an excess of black bile was believed to make a person melancholic.
Scholars once attributed mood disorders to an imbalance of black bile in the body.
Texts from the Middle Ages frequently reference black bile when describing the cause of sadness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: melancholy - a feeling of deep sadness; also linked with 'black bile' in historical medicine
  • Degree Higher: yellow bile - another one of the four bodily humors in ancient medicine

Etymology

From ancient Greek medical theory, 'melaina chole' meaning 'black bile'.
