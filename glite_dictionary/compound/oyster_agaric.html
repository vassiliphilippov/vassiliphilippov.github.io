oyster agaric

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔɪstər əˈɡærɪk / (AmE), / ˈɔɪstə əˈɡærɪk / (BrE)
An edible mushroom with a soft greyish cap, growing in clustered, shelf-like masses on dead wood.
edible mushroom with soft greyish cap

Examples

The chef sautéed fresh oyster agaric with garlic and parsley for a delightful vegetarian dish.
Clusters of oyster agaric grew on the stump in the damp forest.
Many foragers collect oyster agaric in the autumn when the mushrooms are most abundant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oyster mushroom - another common name for the same mushroom species (Pleurotus ostreatus)
