primary dysmenorrhea

1. NOUN

/ ˌpraɪˌmɛri dɪsˌmɛnəˈriə / (AmE), / ˌprʌɪməri dɪsˌmɛnəˈriːə / (BrE)
painful menstruation that occurs as a normal part of the menstrual cycle and is not caused by any underlying disease or condition
painful menstruation not caused by disease
medical

Examples

She began experiencing primary dysmenorrhea when she was a teenager, causing severe cramps during her period.
Over-the-counter pain relievers are often recommended for women suffering from primary dysmenorrhea.
Unlike secondary dysmenorrhea, primary dysmenorrhea is not associated with any pelvic disease.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: secondary dysmenorrhea - painful menstruation caused by an underlying disease

Etymology

From Greek dys- (bad, difficult) + mēn (month) + -rrhea (flow), with 'primary' indicating the absence of an underlying cause.
