medicinal leech

1. NOUN

/ məˈdɪs.ɪ.nəl liːtʃ / (AmE), / mɪˈdɪs.ɪ.nəl liːtʃ / (BrE)
a large European freshwater leech (Hirudo medicinalis) formerly used in medicine for bloodletting
leech used in bloodletting

Examples

Before the advent of modern medicine, the medicinal leech was commonly applied to patients for bloodletting.
Scientists have recently studied the saliva of the medicinal leech to develop new anticoagulant drugs.
In 19th-century Europe, doctors often used medicinal leeches as a standard treatment for various ailments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leech - a bloodsucking aquatic or terrestrial worm
