vitreous silica

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪtriəs sɪˈlaɪkə / (AmE), / ˈvɪtri.əs sɪˈlɪkə / (BrE)
A colorless glass made almost entirely of pure silica, used especially in scientific and industrial applications for its thermal stability and resistance to chemicals.
glass made from pure silica

Examples

Laboratory equipment made from vitreous silica is highly resistant to sudden temperature changes.
Because of its purity, vitreous silica is essential in the production of optical fibers.
The furnace lining was constructed using panels of vitreous silica to withstand intense heat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quartz glass - glass made from almost pure silica, used in laboratory equipment

Etymology

From Latin 'vitreus' (glassy) and 'silica' (silicon dioxide).
