information measure

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.fərˈmeɪ.ʃən ˈmɛʒ.ɚ / (AmE), / ˌɪn.fəˈmeɪ.ʃən ˈmɛʒ.ə / (BrE)
a method or system for quantifying information, usually based on the probability of events or messages
quantification of information using probabilities

Examples

In information theory, the concept of an information measure helps to quantify uncertainty.
Shannon's entropy is a classic example of an information measure.
Researchers introduced a new information measure to analyze the data's variability.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: entropy - a measure of the uncertainty or randomness of information content
