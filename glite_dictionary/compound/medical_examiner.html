medical examiner

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛdɪkəl ɪɡˈzæmɪnər / (AmE), / ˈmɛdɪkəl ɪɡˈzæmɪnə / (BrE)
an official, often a physician, whose job is to investigate deaths not resulting from natural causes, usually by conducting inquests or autopsies
public official investigating certain deaths
legal medical

Examples

The medical examiner determined that the cause of death was accidental drowning.
After the police finished their investigation, the medical examiner conducted an autopsy.
The medical examiner testified in court about the victim’s injuries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coroner - an official who investigates deaths, often not a physician
