osseous labyrinth

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑsiəs ˈlæbərɪnθ / (AmE), / ˈɒsiəs ˈlæbərɪnθ / (BrE)
the complex bony cavity in the petrous part of the temporal bone that encloses the delicate structures of the inner ear
bony cavity in inner ear
medical

Examples

The osseous labyrinth protects the delicate structures of the inner ear.
During the procedure, the surgeon carefully avoided damaging the osseous labyrinth.
Imaging revealed an abnormality within the patient's osseous labyrinth.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: membranous labyrinth - soft, membranous structure within the osseous labyrinth of the inner ear

Etymology

From Latin 'osseus' (bony) and 'labyrinthus' (labyrinth, maze).
