Pharaoh's chicken

1. NOUN

/ fəˈroʊz ˈtʃɪkən / (AmE), / fəˈrəʊz ˈtʃɪkɪn / (BrE)
a small mostly white vulture of Africa and southern Eurasia, also known as the Egyptian vulture
small African and Eurasian vulture

Examples

The Pharaoh's chicken is often seen near ancient Egyptian ruins scavenging for food.
Unlike many other vultures, the Pharaoh's chicken has striking white plumage and a yellow face.
Local folklore attributes good fortune to anyone who spots a Pharaoh's chicken in the wild.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Egyptian vulture - a small mostly white vulture of Africa and southern Eurasia; also called Pharaoh's chicken

Etymology

Named for its association with ancient Egypt ('Pharaoh') and its chicken-like appearance.
