United States Civil War

1. PROPN

/ juːˈnaɪtɪd steɪts ˈsɪvl̩ wɔr / (AmE), / juːˈnaɪtɪd steɪts ˈsɪvl wɔː / (BrE)
the civil war fought in the United States between the Northern states (Union) and the Southern seceding states (Confederacy) from 1861 to 1865
American civil war, 1861–1865

Examples

The United States Civil War dramatically changed the course of American history.
Many families were divided during the United States Civil War, as relatives fought on opposing sides.
Scholars continue to study the causes and consequences of the United States Civil War.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: American Civil War - common alternative name for the United States Civil War (1861-1865)
  • Degree Higher: civil war - a war between groups within the same country
