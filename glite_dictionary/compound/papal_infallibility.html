papal infallibility

1. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪpəl ɪnˌfælɪˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ˈpeɪpəl ɪnˌfalɪˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the doctrine that the pope is preserved from error when speaking officially on matters of faith or morality in the Roman Catholic Church
doctrine that the pope cannot err in certain teachings

Examples

The doctrine of papal infallibility was formally defined in 1870 at the First Vatican Council.
Some Catholics question how papal infallibility should be interpreted in the modern age.
Debates about papal infallibility have shaped many discussions within the church hierarchy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: infallibility - the quality of never making an error

Etymology

From papal, relating to the pope, and infallibility, from Medieval Latin infallibilitas (incapable of error).
