second nature

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛkənd ˈneɪtʃɚ / (AmE), / ˌsekənd ˈneɪtʃə / (BrE)
a behavior, skill, or habit that has become so well learned or practiced that it seems instinctive or automatic
an acquired habit that feels instinctive

Examples

After years of working in customer service, patience had become second nature to her.
For a professional pianist, reading sheet music is second nature.
Wearing a seatbelt whenever he got in a car was second nature to him.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: habit - something done regularly and automatically

Etymology

From the literal sense of 'second' meaning 'additional' or 'other' and 'nature' meaning 'inherent quality'; dates from the late 16th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˌsɛkənd ˈneɪtʃɚ / (AmE), / ˌsɛkənd ˈneɪtʃə / (BrE)
a habit or skill that has become instinctive or automatic through repeated practice
habit or skill done instinctively

Examples

After so many years of painting, mixing colors became second nature to her.
Typing quickly is now second nature for most office workers.
With enough repetition, safe driving will become second nature to you.

Synset Examples

For her, baking cookies is second nature.
After years of practice, playing the piano became second nature.
Driving in heavy traffic has become second nature to him.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: instinct - an innate, typically fixed pattern of behavior
  • Synonym: habit - a regular, often unconscious behavior

Etymology

From the idea that a learned behavior can become as natural as one's original instinct (first nature).
