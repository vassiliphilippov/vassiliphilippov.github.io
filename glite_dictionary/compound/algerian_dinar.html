Algerian dinar

1. NOUN

/ ælˈdʒɪriən dɪˈnɑr / (AmE), / ælˈdʒɪəriən dɪˈnɑː / (BrE)
the official basic unit of currency in Algeria.
currency unit of Algeria

Examples

Tourists are required to exchange their cash for Algerian dinar upon arrival in Algeria.
The price of a coffee in Algiers is listed in Algerian dinar on the menu.
Due to inflation, the value of the Algerian dinar has decreased in recent years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dinar - a monetary unit used in several countries, including some in North Africa and the Middle East

Etymology

From Arabic 'dīnār', ultimately from Latin 'denarius' (an ancient Roman coin).
