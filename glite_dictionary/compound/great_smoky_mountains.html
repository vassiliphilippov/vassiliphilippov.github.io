Great Smoky Mountains

1. PROPN

/ ɡreɪt ˈsmoʊki ˈmaʊntənz / (AmE), / ɡreɪt ˈsməʊki ˈmaʊntɪnz / (BrE)
a mountain range forming part of the Appalachians on the border between North Carolina and Tennessee, known for its misty appearance and rich biodiversity
mountain range between North Carolina and Tennessee

Examples

Every summer, thousands of tourists visit the Great Smoky Mountains to experience its scenic beauty and diverse wildlife.
The Great Smoky Mountains are renowned for their picturesque mist and ancient forests.
Hiking in the Great Smoky Mountains National Park is on many nature enthusiasts' bucket lists.

Etymology

Named for the natural fog that often hangs over the range, appearing as large smoke plumes.
