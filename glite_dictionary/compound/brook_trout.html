brook trout

1. NOUN

/ brʊk traʊt / (AmE), / brʊk traʊt / (BrE)
A small trout native to eastern North America, found in freshwater streams and lakes, and introduced to Europe.
North American freshwater trout

Examples

We caught a beautiful brook trout in the cold mountain stream.
The brook trout is known for its colorful markings and delicate flavor.
Many anglers travel to Canada to fish for wild brook trout.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trout - a common name for several species of freshwater fish

2. NOUN

/ brʊk traʊt / (AmE), / brʊk traʊt / (BrE)
a small freshwater trout of eastern North America, prized as a food and game fish
a species of freshwater trout

Examples

The fisherman proudly displayed a large brook trout he had caught that morning.
Many anglers travel to remote streams to catch brook trout for sport.
We cooked the freshly caught brook trout over a campfire by the river.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: trout - a common name for fish in the genera Oncorhynchus, Salmo, and Salvelinus
