pneumonic plague

1. NOUN

/ njuːˈmɑnɪk pleɪɡ / (AmE), / njuːˈmɒnɪk pleɪɡ / (BrE)
A severe and often fatal form of plague characterized by lung infection, which can be transmitted between people through airborne droplets, leading to symptoms such as chills, bloody sputum, and high fever.
severe airborne form of plague affecting the lungs
medical

Examples

During the outbreak, several patients developed pneumonic plague, which spread rapidly through the community.
If left untreated, pneumonic plague can cause death within 24 hours of symptom onset.
Researchers are studying how pneumonic plague jumps from animals to humans and spreads between people.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bubonic plague - form of plague affecting lymph nodes, usually spread by fleas
  • Synonym: septicemic plague - form of plague affecting the blood

Etymology

From 'pneumonic', meaning relating to the lungs (from Greek 'pneumōn', lung), and 'plague', denoting the infectious disease.
