apparent motion

1. NOUN

/ əˈpɛr.ənt ˈmoʊ.ʃən / (AmE), / əˈpar.ənt ˈməʊ.ʃən / (BrE)
an optical illusion in which stationary objects shown in rapid succession appear to move, as in movies or animations
illusory movement seen in rapid images

Examples

The illusion of apparent motion is fundamental to how we perceive films as moving pictures.
Animation takes advantage of apparent motion to create lively scenes from static images.
Our brains are easily fooled by sequences of images, resulting in the effect known as apparent motion.

Synset Examples

the cinema relies on apparent motion
the succession of flashing lights gave an illusion of movement

Related Terms

  • Synonym: illusory motion - the perception of motion where none actually occurs
  • Antonym: real motion - actual physical movement of an object
