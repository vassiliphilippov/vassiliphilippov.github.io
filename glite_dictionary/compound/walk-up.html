walk-up

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔkˌʌp / (AmE), / ˈwɔːkˌʌp / (BrE)
an apartment building of several floors that does not have an elevator, so the only access to upper floors is by stairs
apartment building without an elevator

Examples

She lived in a fifth-floor walk-up in Manhattan for years.
The walk-up was charming but the daily climb was exhausting.
They bought a small walk-up because it was more affordable than apartments with elevators.

Etymology

From the idea of having to 'walk up' the stairs instead of taking an elevator.
