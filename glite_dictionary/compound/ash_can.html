Ash Can

1. NOUN

/ ˈæʃ kæn / (AmE), / ˈæʃ kan / (BrE)
A style of early 20th-century American painting that depicts realistic and often gritty scenes of urban life.
realistic American art movement, early 1900s

Examples

The Ash Can painters captured the harsh realities of New York City in the early 1900s.
She wrote her thesis on the influence of Ash Can art on social realism.
A major Ash Can exhibition opened at the museum, featuring works by George Bellows and John Sloan.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ashcan School - American art movement known for realistic depictions of urban life

Etymology

From 'ash can', referring to garbage cans, used figuratively to describe the focus on the gritty aspects of city life.
