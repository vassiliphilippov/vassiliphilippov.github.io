articulatory system

1. NOUN

/ ɑrˈtɪkjələˌtɔri ˈsɪstəm / (AmE), / ɑːˈtɪkjʊlətəri ˈsɪstəm / (BrE)
the system of joints in the body; the collective structure enabling movement where bones meet
system of joints in the body
medical

Examples

Damage to the articulatory system can cause significant problems with movement and flexibility.
Medical students study the articulatory system in detail to understand joint disorders.
Yoga and stretching exercises help maintain a healthy articulatory system.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skeletal system - the framework of bones and cartilages that supports the body

Etymology

From Latin 'articulare' (to divide into joints) and 'systema' (system).
