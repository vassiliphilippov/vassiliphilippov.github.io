immovable bandage

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈmuːvəbl ˈbændɪdʒ / (AmE), / ɪˈmuːvəbl ˈbændɪdʒ / (BrE)
A bandage made of cloth soaked with a hardening substance, such as plaster of Paris, which becomes rigid after application, used to immobilize a body part.
rigid medical bandage for immobilization
medical

Examples

The doctor applied an immovable bandage to ensure the fractured arm stayed in place during healing.
After the surgery, an immovable bandage was necessary to prevent any movement of the patient's leg.
Older treatments often used an immovable bandage instead of modern synthetic casts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cast - a rigid protective shell used to immobilize a broken bone
