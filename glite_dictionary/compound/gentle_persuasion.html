gentle persuasion

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɛntəl pərˈsweɪʒən / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛntl pəˈsweɪʒən / (BrE)
subtle or mild methods used to influence or convince someone to do something
subtle influence or convincing

Examples

With a bit of gentle persuasion, he finally agreed to attend the meeting.
Sometimes gentle persuasion is more effective than direct orders.
Through gentle persuasion, she was able to encourage her friend to try something new.

Synset Examples

She used gentle persuasion to convince him to join.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: coercion - the practice of persuading someone to do something by using force or threats
  • Synonym: persuasion - the act of convincing someone to do or believe something
