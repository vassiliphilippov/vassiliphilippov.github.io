brain sugar

1. NOUN

/ ˈbreɪn ˌʃʊɡər / (AmE), / ˈbreɪn ˌʃʊɡə / (BrE)
a simple sugar (galactose) found in lactose and various plant or animal tissues, especially important in brain and nervous system development.
galactose; a sugar in lactose, brain tissue
medical

Examples

The laboratory tests revealed elevated levels of brain sugar in the cell samples.
Infants require brain sugar for the healthy development of their nervous systems.
Lactose intolerance can affect the digestion and absorption of brain sugar.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: galactose - a simple sugar found in lactose and many polysaccharides

Etymology

So called because galactose is present in neural tissue, especially the brain.
