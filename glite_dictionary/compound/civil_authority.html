civil authority

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪvəl əˈθɔːrəti / (AmE), / ˈsɪvəl ɔːˈθɒrɪti / (BrE)
a person or organization that exercises power and control over civilian, as opposed to military, affairs
person or body with civilian administrative power

Examples

The mayor is considered the highest civil authority in the city.
During times of unrest, the civil authority may request assistance from the military.
The church was independent from the civil authority in medieval Europe.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: military authority - power exercised by members of the armed forces over military affairs

Etymology

From Latin 'civilis' (relating to citizens) and 'auctoritas' (authority, influence).
