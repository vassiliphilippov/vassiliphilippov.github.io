subpoena duces tecum

1. NOUN

/ səˈpinə ˌduːˌseɪs ˈtiːkəm / (AmE), / sʌˈpiːnə ˌdjuːsiːz ˈtiːkəm / (BrE)
a writ issued by a court, ordering someone to appear and bring specified documents as evidence
court order to bring documents
legal

Examples

The court issued a subpoena duces tecum requiring the company to provide all relevant financial records.
Her lawyer received a subpoena duces tecum compelling her to bring documents to the deposition.
Failure to comply with a subpoena duces tecum can lead to legal penalties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: subpoena - writ ordering someone to attend court

Etymology

From Latin 'subpoena duces tecum', meaning 'bring with you under penalty'.
