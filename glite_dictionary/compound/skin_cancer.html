skin cancer

1. NOUN

/ skɪn ˈkæn.sɚ / (AmE), / skɪn ˈkæn.sə / (BrE)
A malignant growth or tumor of the skin, usually caused by excessive exposure to ultraviolet radiation.
malignant tumor of the skin
medical

Examples

Regular use of sunscreen lowers the risk of developing skin cancer.
She was diagnosed with a mild form of skin cancer after noticing an unusual mole.
Doctors recommend annual checkups to detect skin cancer early.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: melanoma - A type of skin cancer that starts in pigment cells
  • Synonym: carcinoma - A malignant tumor, including of the skin
