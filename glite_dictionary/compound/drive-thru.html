drive-thru

1. NOUN

/ ˈdraɪv θru / (AmE), / ˈdraɪv θruː / (BrE)
A restaurant, especially a fast-food restaurant, where customers can order and receive food without leaving their vehicles.
restaurant where food is served to customers in their cars

Examples

We grabbed burgers at the drive-thru because we were in a hurry.
Rain or shine, the drive-thru is always busy during lunch hours.
Many coffee shops now offer a drive-thru for added convenience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drive-in - restaurant or movie theater where customers stay in their cars

Etymology

From 'drive' and 'through', indicating driving through the premises without getting out of the car.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdraɪv θru / (AmE), / ˈdraɪv θruː / (BrE)
A facility or service, especially at a restaurant, bank, or pharmacy, that allows customers to purchase goods or be served without leaving their vehicles.
service for customers in cars

Examples

We picked up burgers at the drive-thru on our way home.
The bank's drive-thru allows you to deposit checks without leaving your car.
Rain or shine, the pharmacy drive-thru is always convenient for quick pickups.

Synset Examples

McDick's is famous for its late-night drive-thru.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drive-in - facility where customers stay in their cars for service, such as restaurants or theaters

Etymology

Formed from 'drive' and shortened form of 'through', indicating driving through a service lane.
