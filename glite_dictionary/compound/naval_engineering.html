naval engineering

1. NOUN

/ ˈneɪvəl ˌɛn.dʒəˈnɪr.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈneɪvəl ˌɛn.dʒɪˈnɪə.rɪŋ / (BrE)
the branch of engineering concerned with the design, construction, and operation of ships and other marine vessels
ship design and construction engineering

Examples

She studied naval engineering at university to work on advanced ship designs.
Innovations in naval engineering have made modern submarines more efficient and quieter.
The navy recruits specialists in naval engineering to maintain their fleet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marine engineering - engineering discipline focusing on ship machinery and systems

Etymology

From 'naval' (relating to ships) and 'engineering' (application of science for design/construction).
