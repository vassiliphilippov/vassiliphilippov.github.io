stirrup cup

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɪrəp ˌkʌp / (AmE), / ˈstɪrəp ˌkʌp / (BrE)
A farewell drink offered to someone, especially a horseman, just before departure, usually alcoholic.
farewell drink before departure (esp. for horsemen)

Examples

As the hunt prepared to leave, the hostess offered each rider a stirrup cup at the gate.
He accepted the traditional stirrup cup, savoring the sherry before mounting his horse.
The custom of giving a stirrup cup is still observed in some rural communities.

Etymology

Named for the practice of offering a drink to a rider while their feet were in the stirrups, preparing to depart.
