low water

1. NOUN

/ loʊ ˈwɔtər / (AmE), / ləʊ ˈwɔːtə / (BrE)
the time or level at which the tide is at its lowest point, especially at the shore or in a river.
lowest level of the tide

Examples

The boat became stranded on the sandbank at low water.
We waited until low water before crossing the causeway to the island.
At low water, the rocky seabed is clearly visible from the pier.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: high water - the highest level reached by the tide
