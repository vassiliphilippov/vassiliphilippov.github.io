textual scholarship

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛkstʃuəl ˈskɑːl ərˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈtɛkstjʊəl ˈskɒl əʃɪp / (BrE)
the academic study of texts and physical documents, including their description, transcription, editing, and annotation
academic study of texts and documents

Examples

She devoted her academic career to textual scholarship and the preservation of ancient manuscripts.
Advancements in digital tools have greatly influenced the field of textual scholarship.
The professor's expertise in textual scholarship made her a valuable member of the editorial project.

Synset Examples

The historical roots of textual scholarship date back to the 3rd century BCE, when the scholarly activities of copying, comparing, describing and archiving texts became professionalized in the Library of Alexandria.
