cassette tape

1. NOUN

/ kəˈsɛt teɪp / (AmE), / kəˈsɛt teɪp / (BrE)
a small, flat case containing magnetic tape that is used for recording and playing back sound or music
magnetic tape audio storage in a case

Examples

My father still has a huge collection of old cassette tapes from the 1980s.
She found a rare album on cassette tape at the flea market.
Before CDs became popular, most people listened to music on cassette tapes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: audio cassette - a cassette used for recording and playing sound
  • Antonym: compact disc - a digital optical disc used for storing music or data

Etymology

From French 'cassette' (small box) + 'tape'; popularised from the 1960s with the rise of portable audio formats.
