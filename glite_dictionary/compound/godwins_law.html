Godwin's law

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɑːdwɪnz lɔː / (AmE), / ˈɡɒdwɪnz lɔː / (BrE)
An internet adage stating that as an online discussion grows longer, the probability of a comparison involving Nazis or Hitler approaches one.
internet adage about Nazi/Hitler comparisons in discussions

Examples

Any time a forum thread gets heated, Godwin's law seems to come into play.
When someone mentioned Hitler in the argument, she cited Godwin's law to end the discussion.
Many moderators remind users of Godwin's law to discourage extreme comparisons online.

Synset Examples

In debates, someone always invokes Godwin's law eventually.
He pointed out Godwin's law during the heated argument.

Etymology

Named after Mike Godwin, who formulated the law in 1990.
