double helix

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʌbəl ˈhiːlɪks / (AmE), / ˌdʌbəl ˈhiːlɪks / (BrE)
a pair of parallel helices intertwined around a common axis, especially the structure formed by DNA molecules
intertwined helical structure (esp. of DNA)

Examples

Watson and Crick discovered the double helix structure of DNA in 1953.
The double helix allows DNA to replicate accurately during cell division.
Under the microscope, the double helix appears as two entwined strands.

Synset Examples

the shape of the DNA molecule is a double helix

Related Terms

  • Synonym: helix - a spiral form, like a corkscrew or coiled spring

Etymology

Formed from 'double' and 'helix' (from Greek helix, meaning ‘spiral’)

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʌb.əl ˈhiːlɪks / (AmE), / ˈdʌb.əl ˈhiːlɪks / (BrE)
a structure formed by two intertwined strands, especially the spiral arrangement in the DNA molecule
spiral structure of DNA

Examples

Scientists first identified the structure of DNA as a double helix in 1953.
The twisting shape of the double helix allows DNA to store genetic information efficiently.
Under the microscope, they observed the double helix of the molecule.

Synset Examples

The two strands of DNA interdigitate to form a double helix.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: helix - a spiral shape, like a coil or spring

Etymology

From Latin 'helix' (spiral) and 'double', describing the two intertwined spirals of DNA.
