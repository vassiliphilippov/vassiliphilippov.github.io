cop-call

1. NOUN

/ kɑp kɔl / (AmE), / kɒp kɔːl / (BrE)
a telephone call made to the police to report an incident or request assistance
a call made to the police
informal

Examples

When the neighbors heard loud noises at midnight, they made a quick cop-call to alert the authorities.
Her prompt cop-call helped the police catch the burglar before he escaped.
In urgent situations, a cop-call can make all the difference.

Synset Examples

She was praised for her quick cop-call reporting the theft.
He made a cop-call when he saw the suspicious activity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: police call - a call made to the police
  • Degree Higher: emergency call - a telephone call made to request emergency assistance

Etymology

From 'cop' (slang for police officer) + 'call' (telephone call).
