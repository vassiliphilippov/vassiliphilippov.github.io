great white shark

1. NOUN

/ ɡreɪt waɪt ʃɑrk / (AmE), / ɡreɪt waɪt ʃɑːk / (BrE)
a large, powerful shark (Carcharodon carcharias) found in warm seas, known for its size, aggressiveness, and occasional attacks on humans
large predatory shark species

Examples

The great white shark is easily recognized by its stark white belly and immense size.
Many people fear swimming in certain areas due to the possibility of encountering a great white shark.
Researchers tagged a great white shark off the coast to study its migration patterns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shark - large fish of the order Selachii, often predatory

Etymology

Refers to its notable size and pale coloration; 'shark' possibly from Spanish 'sarda'.
