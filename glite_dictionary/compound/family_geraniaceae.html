family Geraniaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli dʒəˌreɪniˈeɪsiˌaɪ / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli dʒəˌreɪnɪˈeɪsiːaɪ / (BrE)
A family of chiefly herbaceous plants, including the geraniums, characterized by lobed or divided leaves and distinctive five-petaled flowers.
a family of herbaceous plants (geraniums, etc.)

Examples

Many garden varieties belong to the family Geraniaceae, known for their attractive flowers.
Botanists classify wild geraniums under the family Geraniaceae.
The family Geraniaceae includes both annual and perennial species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: geranium - a plant of the genus Geranium

Etymology

From New Latin 'Geraniaceae', based on 'Geranium', the type genus.
