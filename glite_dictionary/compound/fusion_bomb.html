fusion bomb

1. NOUN

/ ˈfjuːʒən bɑm / (AmE), / ˈfjuːʒən bɒm / (BrE)
A nuclear weapon that produces energy by fusing light atomic nuclei, such as hydrogen, at extremely high temperatures, resulting in an extremely powerful explosion.
thermonuclear weapon

Examples

The destructive power of a fusion bomb far exceeds that of earlier atomic bombs.
Scientists explained how a fusion bomb works by fusing hydrogen atoms to form helium.
Fears of a fusion bomb test led to international calls for disarmament.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hydrogen bomb - A nuclear weapon using fusion of hydrogen isotopes
  • Antonym: atomic bomb - A nuclear weapon using fission

Etymology

From 'fusion' (from Latin 'fundere', to pour or melt) and 'bomb', indicating a device using nuclear fusion.
