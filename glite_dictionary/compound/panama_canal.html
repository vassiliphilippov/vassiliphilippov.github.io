Panama Canal

1. NOUN

/ ˈpænəˌmɑ kəˈnæl / (AmE), / ˈpanəmə kəˈnal / (BrE)
A major artificial waterway in Panama, about 50 miles (80 km) long, connecting the Atlantic and Pacific Oceans and completed by the United States between 1904 and 1914.
artificial waterway in Panama

Examples

The construction of the Panama Canal dramatically shortened trade routes between the Atlantic and Pacific Oceans.
Many ships transit the Panama Canal each year, making it vital for international commerce.
Tourists often visit the Panama Canal to witness its impressive system of locks and engineering.
