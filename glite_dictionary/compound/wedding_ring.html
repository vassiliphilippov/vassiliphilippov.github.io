wedding ring

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɛdɪŋ rɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwɛdɪŋ rɪŋ / (BrE)
A ring, typically of plain gold, given to a bride or groom during a wedding ceremony as a symbol of marriage.
ring exchanged during marriage ceremony

Examples

He slipped the wedding ring onto her finger during the ceremony.
After fifty years, her wedding ring still sparkled like new.
They chose simple gold bands as their wedding rings.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: engagement ring - ring given before marriage to signify engagement
