classic example

1. NOUN

/ ˈklæs.ɪk ɪɡˈzæm.pəl / (AmE), / ˈklæs.ɪk ɪɡˈzɑːm.pəl / (BrE)
a typical or widely recognized instance that illustrates a concept or category
typical or well-known instance

Examples

The Mona Lisa is often cited as a classic example of Renaissance portraiture.
Failure to back up important files is a classic example of poor planning.
Overeating at a buffet is a classic example of letting your eyes be bigger than your stomach.

Synset Examples

The elephant is a classic example of a pachyderm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: archetype - a very typical example of a certain person or thing
  • Synonym: case in point - an instance or example that effectively illustrates a point
  • Antonym: exception - something that does not fit the typical pattern; the opposite of an example
