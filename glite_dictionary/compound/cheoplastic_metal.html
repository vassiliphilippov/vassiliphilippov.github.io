cheoplastic metal

1. NOUN

/ ˌkiː.oʊˈplæstɪk ˈmɛt̬l̩ / (AmE), / ˌkiː.əˈplæstɪk ˈmɛtəl / (BrE)
an alloy that melts at low temperatures and is used for molding artificial teeth
low-melting alloy for molding artificial teeth
medical

Examples

The dentist selected a cheoplastic metal to create the patient's new set of artificial teeth.
Due to its low melting point, cheoplastic metal can be easily molded for dental use.
Advances in materials science have led to improved cheoplastic metal alloys for prosthetic dentistry.

Etymology

From 'cheoplastic' (relating to the formation of tissue or artificial substitutes) and 'metal'.
