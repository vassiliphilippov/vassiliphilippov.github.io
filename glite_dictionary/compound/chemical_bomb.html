chemical bomb

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɛmɪkəl bɑm / (AmE), / ˈkɛmɪkəl bɒm / (BrE)
a bomb containing chemical agents that are dispersed when it explodes, used as a weapon
bomb with chemical agents as a weapon

Examples

Several countries have banned the use of chemical bombs under international law.
The army discovered a cache of chemical bombs hidden in the warehouse.
Exposure to a chemical bomb can cause severe illness or death.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chemical weapon - a weapon that uses toxic chemical substances to inflict harm or death
