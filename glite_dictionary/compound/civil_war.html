civil war

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɪv.əl ˈwɔr / (AmE), / ˌsɪv.əl ˈwɔː / (BrE)
A war fought between groups or regions within the same country.
internal war within a country

Examples

The American civil war lasted from 1861 to 1865 and shaped the nation's history.
After years of tension, the country erupted into a bloody civil war.
Many families were separated during the prolonged civil war that devastated the nation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: revolution - an overthrow of a government, usually by force
  • Antonym: peace - a state in which there is no war or fighting

Etymology

From Latin 'civilis' (civil) and 'war', referring to conflict among citizens.
