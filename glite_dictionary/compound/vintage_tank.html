vintage tank

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪn.tɪdʒ tæŋk / (AmE), / ˈvɪn.tɪdʒ taŋk / (BrE)
a military tank from a particular historical period, especially one that is old, classic, or collectible
old military vehicle from a past era

Examples

The museum display includes several vintage tanks from the World War II era.
Collectors are interested in restoring vintage tanks to their original condition.
Every year, enthusiasts gather to watch vintage tank parades at the old military base.

Synset Examples

The museum displays vintage tanks from World War II.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antique - an object from an earlier period, especially one of value or interest
  • Antonym: modern tank - a current or recently developed armored fighting vehicle

Etymology

Vintage from Old French 'vendage', originally meaning 'wine harvest', later used more broadly for 'of a particular year or era'; combined here with 'tank', an armored fighting vehicle.
