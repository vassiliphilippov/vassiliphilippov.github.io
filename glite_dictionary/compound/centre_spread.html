centre spread

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛntɚ sprɛd / (AmE), / ˈsɛntə sprɛd / (BrE)
the pair of facing pages in the middle of a magazine, newspaper, or similar publication, often used for a feature or large photograph
central two pages of a magazine or newspaper

Examples

The model appeared in a glamorous centre spread in the fashion magazine.
Many magazines reserve their centre spread for high-impact photographs or important stories.
He proudly showed his friends the centre spread featuring his artwork.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: double-page spread - an article or photograph printed across two facing pages
