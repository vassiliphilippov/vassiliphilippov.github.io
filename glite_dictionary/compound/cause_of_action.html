cause of action

1. NOUN

/ kɔz əv ˈækʃən / (AmE), / kɔːz əv ˈækʃən / (BrE)
A set of facts or circumstances that give an individual the right to seek a legal remedy in court.
legal grounds for bringing a lawsuit
legal

Examples

The plaintiff alleged a valid cause of action based on breach of contract.
Without a cause of action, the court will dismiss the lawsuit.
The lawyer assessed whether there was sufficient evidence to support a cause of action.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lawsuit - a claim or dispute brought to a court of law

Etymology

From legal use of 'cause' (a case or reason) and 'action' (a lawsuit).
