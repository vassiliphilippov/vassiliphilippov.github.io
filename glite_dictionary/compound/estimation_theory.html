estimation theory

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛstəˈmeɪʃən ˈθɪəri / (AmE), / ˌɛstɪˈmeɪʃən ˈθɪəri / (BrE)
The branch of probability and statistics concerned with determining properties of populations or processes based on observed data.
statistical field inferring properties from observed data

Examples

She specializes in estimation theory, applying statistical techniques to analyze complex engineering problems.
Advances in estimation theory have led to more accurate weather prediction models.
Many algorithms in signal processing are based on principles from estimation theory.

Synset Examples

Typically, the problem is approached assuming a probabilistic description of uncertainty and applying statistical estimation theory.

  • Synonym: probability theory - the branch of mathematics concerned with the analysis of random phenomena
