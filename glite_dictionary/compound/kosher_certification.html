kosher certification

1. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊʃər ˌsɝː.t̬ə.fɪˈkeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˈkəʊʃə ˌsɜː.tɪ.fɪˈkeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
a label or process indicating that food has been prepared in accordance with Jewish dietary laws
label proving food follows Jewish dietary rules

Examples

The packaging displayed a kosher certification to assure buyers of its quality standards.
Many airlines offer meals with kosher certification for passengers observing dietary laws.
He checked the box for a reliable kosher certification before buying the crackers.

Synset Examples

We looked for kosher certification on the packaging.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: halal certification - label indicating food meets Islamic dietary laws

Etymology

'Kosher' from Hebrew 'kasher' (fit, proper), plus 'certification' from Latin 'certificare'.
