mud duck

1. NOUN

/ mʌd dʌk / (AmE), / mʌd dʌk / (BrE)
a type of duck commonly found in muddy areas or habitats.
duck found in muddy habitats

Examples

A mud duck was foraging for insects along the riverbank after a heavy rain.
We saw several mud ducks swim across the shallow, murky water.
The children pointed excitedly at the mud duck splashing in the puddle.

Synset Examples

The mud duck waddled by the pond, searching for food.
We spotted a mud duck nesting near the river.
