ghost home

1. NOUN

/ ɡoʊst hoʊm / (AmE), / ɡəʊst həʊm / (BrE)
a place that appears abandoned or is strongly associated with lingering memories of the past; often evoking a sense of emptiness or nostalgia
place feeling abandoned or haunted by memories

Examples

After decades of neglect, the once-bustling mansion became a ghost home echoing with the past.
Every time she returns to her childhood house, it feels more like a ghost home than a welcoming haven.
The abandoned village stood silently, each cottage a ghost home filled with lost stories.

Synset Examples

He wandered through his childhood neighborhood, it was a ghost home.
Visiting her old school felt like stepping into a ghost home.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ghost town - an abandoned town or settlement, typically one that still has visible remains
