Western axe

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɛstərn æks / (AmE), / ˈwɛstən æks / (BrE)
An axe with cutting edges on both sides of the head, commonly associated with Western styles of toolmaking.
axe with double cutting edges

Examples

The lumberjack preferred to use a Western axe for felling large trees.
A Western axe is often heavier than a single-bit axe due to its double-bladed design.
Collectors value the craftsmanship of an antique Western axe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: double-bit axe - an axe with two cutting edges, one on each side of the head
