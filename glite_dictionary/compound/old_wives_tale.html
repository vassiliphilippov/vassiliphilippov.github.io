old wives' tale

1. NOUN

/ oʊld waɪvz teɪl / (AmE), / əʊld waɪvz teɪl / (BrE)
a traditional belief or story, typically regarded as unscientific or superstitious, passed down by word of mouth
traditional, unscientific story or belief

Examples

My grandmother used to tell me the old wives' tale that cracking your knuckles gives you arthritis.
Many people still believe the old wives' tale that eating carrots improves your night vision.
The story that you must wait an hour after eating before swimming is just an old wives' tale.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: myth - a widely held but false belief or idea
  • Antonym: fact - something known or proved to be true
