power politics

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaʊər ˈpɑlɪtɪks / (AmE), / ˈpaʊə ˈpɒlɪtɪks / (BrE)
a type of international relations in which countries use or threaten to use force or influence to achieve their objectives
diplomacy using or threatening force

Examples

The country abandoned its policy of power politics in favor of cooperation with neighboring states.
Leaders often resort to power politics when diplomatic negotiations fail.
Some critics argue that power politics can lead to instability and conflict.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: realpolitik - pragmatic, power-based approach to politics or diplomacy
