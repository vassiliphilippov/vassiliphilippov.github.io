space program

1. NOUN

/ speɪs ˈproʊɡræm / (AmE), / speɪs ˈprəʊɡræm / (BrE)
an organized initiative, often by a government, aimed at exploring outer space through technology and research
governmental or technological initiative for space exploration

Examples

The U.S. government invested billions in its space program during the 1960s.
China's space program has made significant advances in launching satellites and astronauts.
He was inspired to become an engineer after learning about the Soviet space program.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: space mission - a specific flight or journey into outer space as part of a space program
