transforming gene

1. NOUN

/ trænˈsfɔrmɪŋ dʒin / (AmE), / trænsˈfɔːmɪŋ dʒiːn / (BrE)
a gene that causes normal cells to become cancerous tumor cells
gene causing normal cells to become cancerous
medical

Examples

The discovery of a transforming gene helped scientists understand how certain cancers develop.
Researchers identified a transforming gene responsible for uncontrolled cell growth in the tumor.
A single mutation in a transforming gene can dramatically alter cell behavior.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oncogene - a gene that can transform a cell into a tumor cell
