white-hot

1. ADJ

/ ˈwaɪt hɑt / (AmE), / ˈwaɪt hɒt / (BrE)
Extremely hot and glowing; used figuratively to describe something characterized by intense emotion, energy, or public attention.
extremely hot or intensely intense

Examples

The debate reached a white-hot level of intensity during the final hour.
The blacksmith removed the white-hot iron from the forge, ready to shape it.
The athlete flourished under the white-hot spotlight of national attention.

Synset Examples

The metal was white-hot before being shaped.
Her anger reached white-hot levels during the argument.
The white-hot spotlight shone on the performers.
