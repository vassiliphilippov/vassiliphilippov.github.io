Worcestershire sauce

1. NOUN

/ ˈwʊstərˌʃɪr sɔs / (AmE), / ˈwʊstəʃə sɔːs / (BrE)
a dark, savory sauce made from vinegar, soy sauce, anchovies, and spices, used to enhance the flavor of food
a tangy, spicy condiment

Examples

Add a dash of Worcestershire sauce to the stew for extra flavor.
He sprinkled Worcestershire sauce over his hamburger before taking a bite.
Many classic recipes call for Worcestershire sauce as a key ingredient.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soy sauce - a salty, fermented sauce made from soybeans and wheat

Etymology

Named after Worcester, England, where the sauce was first produced in the 19th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwʊstərˌʃɪr sɔs / (AmE), / ˈwʊstəˌʃə sɔːs / (BrE)
a dark, tangy sauce made from vinegar, molasses, anchovies, spices, and other ingredients, used to add flavor to food
savory, tangy condiment sauce

Examples

He added a dash of Worcestershire sauce to his stew for extra depth of flavor.
Caesar salad dressing traditionally includes Worcestershire sauce as a key ingredient.
The recipe called for a tablespoon of Worcestershire sauce to enhance the taste of the meatloaf.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soy sauce - a salty sauce made from soybeans, used in Asian cuisine

Etymology

Named after Worcestershire, a county in England, where the sauce was first commercially produced.
