musculus trapezius

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌs.kjə.ləs træˈpiː.zi.əs / (AmE), / ˈmʌs.kjə.ləs træˈpiː.zi.əs / (BrE)
either of the two large, flat triangular muscles on the upper back and shoulder, responsible for various movements of the shoulders and arms
large shoulder and upper back muscle
medical

Examples

The musculus trapezius plays a vital role in shoulder elevation and neck movement.
Injuries to the musculus trapezius can cause pain and limit arm mobility.
During anatomy class, students studied the structure and function of the musculus trapezius in detail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trapezius - muscle on the upper back and shoulder

Etymology

From Latin 'musculus' (muscle) and 'trapezius' (trapezoid-shaped).
