suborder Charadrii

1. NOUN

/ sʌbˌɔrdər kəˈrædriːaɪ / (AmE), / sʌbˌɔːdə kəˈrædriːaɪ / (BrE)
A zoological taxonomic suborder comprising shorebirds such as plovers, sandpipers, avocets, phalaropes, coursers, and stone curlews.
taxonomic suborder of shorebirds

Examples

Many migratory species in the suborder Charadrii travel thousands of miles each year.
The conference included a session focused on conservation efforts for the suborder Charadrii.
Some members of the suborder Charadrii breed in the Arctic and winter in the southern hemisphere.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin Charadrii, based on Greek 'kharadrios' (a type of bird), used in zoological classification.
