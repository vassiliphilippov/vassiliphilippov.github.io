labor pool

1. NOUN

/ ˈleɪ.bɚ puːl / (AmE), / ˈleɪ.bə puːl / (BrE)
the available supply of skilled or unskilled workers from which employees may be hired
available supply of workers

Examples

During the economic boom, companies competed to hire the best candidates from the labor pool.
A shrinking labor pool can make it difficult for businesses to find qualified employees.
Tech giants often draw their talent from a national or even global labor pool.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: workforce - all the workers employed in a country or organization
  • Synonym: labor market - the supply and demand for labor, including the hiring and employment of workers
