third tonsil

1. NOUN

/ θɝd ˈtɑn.səl / (AmE), / θɜːd ˈtɒn.səl / (BrE)
a mass of lymphatic tissue located on the posterior wall and roof of the nasopharynx, also known as the pharyngeal tonsil or adenoids
lymphatic tissue in the nasopharynx (pharyngeal tonsil)
medical

Examples

In some children, the third tonsil can become enlarged and block nasal airflow.
The surgeon examined the third tonsil for signs of infection.
Chronic inflammation of the third tonsil may cause breathing difficulties.

Synset Examples

hypertrophy of the pharyngeal tonsils is called adenoids
enlarged adenoids may restrict the breathing of children

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adenoids - lymphatic tissue in the nasopharynx, also called the third tonsil
  • Degree Lower: palatine tonsil - lymphatic tissue at the sides of the throat (the 'tonsils')
