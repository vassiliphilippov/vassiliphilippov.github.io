sugar sorghum

1. NOUN

/ ˈsuːɡər ˈsɔrɡəm / (AmE), / ˈʃʊɡə ˈsɔːɡəm / (BrE)
a type of sorghum plant cultivated mainly for its sweet juice, which is processed into syrup
sorghum grown for producing syrup

Examples

Farmers in the South often grow sugar sorghum to make homemade syrup.
The sweet juice extracted from sugar sorghum is boiled down into a thick, flavorful syrup.
Unlike grain sorghum, sugar sorghum varieties are valued for their high sugar content.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sorghum - a cereal grass used for grain and fodder
