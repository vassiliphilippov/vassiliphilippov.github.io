Graeco-Roman deity

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡreɪkoʊˈroʊmən ˈdiːəti / (AmE), / ˌɡriːkəʊˈrəʊmən ˈdiːəti / (BrE)
a god or goddess from ancient Greek and Roman mythology
mythological god from Greek and Roman tradition

Examples

Zeus is a well-known Graeco-Roman deity who was worshipped throughout the ancient world.
Many temples were dedicated to at least one Graeco-Roman deity in ancient cities.
The myths of each Graeco-Roman deity often reveal the values of classical civilization.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: god - a male deity in polytheistic religions
  • Synonym: goddess - a female deity in polytheistic religions

Etymology

From Latin 'Graecus' (Greek) and 'Romanus' (Roman), denoting shared aspects of Greek and Roman civilization.
