snuff-brown

1. ADJ

/ snʌf braʊn / (AmE), / snʌf braʊn / (BrE)
of a greyish to yellowish brown color, like powdered tobacco (snuff)
greyish-yellowish brown color

Examples

The curtains in the old study were a faded snuff-brown that matched the worn carpet.
She wore a snuff-brown coat that seemed to blend into the autumn landscape.
The artist mixed several shades until he achieved just the right snuff-brown for the background.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: brown - a color produced by mixing red, yellow, and black
  • Degree Lower: tan - a light yellowish-brown color

Etymology

Formed from 'snuff' (tobacco powder) + 'brown', referring to the typical color of snuff.
