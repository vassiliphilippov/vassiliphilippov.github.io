common winterberry holly

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmən ˈwɪntərˌbɛri ˈhɑli / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən ˈwɪntəbəri ˈhɒli / (BrE)
a deciduous holly shrub (Ilex verticillata) native to eastern North America, known for its bright red berries that persist through winter
North American holly shrub with red winter berries

Examples

The common winterberry holly adds vibrant color to gardens even after the leaves have fallen.
Birds are attracted to the bright berries of the common winterberry holly during the cold months.
Gardeners often plant common winterberry holly for its ornamental winter display.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: winterberry - A holly shrub bearing red berries through winter, especially Ilex verticillata
