narrowbody aircraft

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɛroʊˌbɑdi ˈɛrˌkræft / (AmE), / ˈnarəʊˌbɒdi ˈeəkrɑːft / (BrE)
A commercial airliner with a single aisle, typically narrower and used for short to medium-haul flights.
single-aisle commercial airliner

Examples

Many airlines use narrowbody aircraft for domestic and regional routes.
The Boeing 737 is a well-known example of a narrowbody aircraft.
New developments in engine technology have made narrowbody aircraft more fuel efficient.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: widebody aircraft - a commercial airliner with two passenger aisles
