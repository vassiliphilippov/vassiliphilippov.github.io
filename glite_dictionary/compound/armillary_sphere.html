armillary sphere

1. NOUN

/ ɑːrˈmɪləri sfɪr / (AmE), / ɑːˈmɪləri sfɪə / (BrE)
a model of objects in the sky consisting of a framework of rings, representing lines of celestial longitude and latitude and other astronomically important features; traditionally used to demonstrate the motion of the stars around the Earth
astronomical instrument with rings to show celestial circles

Examples

The museum displays a rare armillary sphere from the 16th century.
Ancient astronomers used the armillary sphere to study the movement of the planets.
She explained how the armillary sphere models the paths of celestial bodies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: celestial globe - a model representing the stars on the celestial sphere

Etymology

From Latin 'armilla' (bracelet, ring), referring to the rings of the instrument.
