field goal

1. NOUN

/ ˈfiːld ɡoʊl / (AmE), / ˈfiːld ɡəʊl / (BrE)
a basket scored in basketball by a player throwing the ball through the opponent’s hoop during regular play, excluding free throws
basket scored in basketball during play

Examples

She sank the winning field goal just before the buzzer sounded.
The team struggled to make a single field goal in the first quarter.
His accuracy with the field goal has improved this season.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: free throw - an unopposed attempt to score awarded after a foul

Etymology

From 'field' (court of play) and 'goal' (score), originally used in various sports.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfiːld ˌɡoʊl / (AmE), / ˈfiːld ˌɡəʊl / (BrE)
(in American football) a score made by kicking the ball between the opponents' goalposts.
score by kicking ball through goalposts (football)
american

Examples

The team won the game with a last-minute field goal.
He missed a 45-yard field goal in the second quarter.
Their only points in the first half came from a field goal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: touchdown - a score in American football made by carrying the ball into the end zone
  • Degree Lower: extra point - a point scored after a touchdown by kicking the ball through the goalposts
