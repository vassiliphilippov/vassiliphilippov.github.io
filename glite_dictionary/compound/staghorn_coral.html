staghorn coral

1. NOUN

/ ˈstæɡˌhɔrn ˈkɔr.əl / (AmE), / ˈstæɡ.hɔːn ˈkɒr.əl / (BrE)
a large branching coral with antler-like structures, commonly found in shallow tropical reefs
large branching coral resembling antlers

Examples

The staghorn coral is one of the most important reef-building species in the Caribbean.
Divers marveled at the vibrant colors of the staghorn coral formations.
Conservationists are working to restore populations of staghorn coral damaged by disease and climate change.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elkhorn coral - a type of coral with broad, flattened branches

Etymology

Named for its resemblance to the branched antlers of a stag (male deer).
