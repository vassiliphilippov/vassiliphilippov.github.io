face recognition

1. NOUN

/ feɪs ˌrɛk.əɡˈnɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / feɪs ˌrɛk.ɒɡˈnɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
the automated process of identifying or verifying a person by analyzing and comparing their facial features with stored data
automated identification by facial features

Examples

Many airports now use face recognition systems to enhance passenger security.
Some smartphones unlock using face recognition technology instead of a fingerprint or password.
Concerns about privacy have arisen due to the widespread use of face recognition by law enforcement agencies.

Synset Examples

they used face recognition to spot known terrorists

Related Terms

  • Synonym: facial recognition - identification by analyzing facial features

2. NOUN

/ feɪs ˌrɛk.əɡˈnɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / feɪs ˌrɛk.əɡˈnɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
the ability to visually identify and distinguish familiar faces
visual identification of familiar faces

Examples

Some people with prosopagnosia struggle with face recognition even when meeting family members.
Research has shown that face recognition is faster for familiar faces than for strangers.
Security systems are increasingly using automatic face recognition to verify identities.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: face detection - the process of locating human faces in images or video
