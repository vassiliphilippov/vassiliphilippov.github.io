Western Malayo-Polynesian

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɛstərn məˌleɪ.oʊ ˌpɑːlɪˈniːʒən / (AmE), / ˈwɛstən məˌleɪ.əʊ pɒlɪˈniːʒən / (BrE)
A western subfamily of the Malayo-Polynesian languages, spoken in parts of Southeast Asia and the western Pacific.
a western subfamily of Malayo-Polynesian languages

Examples

Many languages in Indonesia belong to the Western Malayo-Polynesian subfamily.
Scholars debate the boundaries of the Western Malayo-Polynesian linguistic group.
Tagalog is considered part of the Western Malayo-Polynesian languages.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Eastern Malayo-Polynesian - an eastern subfamily of Malayo-Polynesian languages
  • Degree Higher: Malayo-Polynesian - the overarching family of languages including both western and eastern branches

Etymology

Malayo-Polynesian from Malay and Polynesian language groups; 'Western' refers to the geographic subset.
