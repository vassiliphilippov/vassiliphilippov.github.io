graphic engine

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡræfɪk ˌɛn.dʒɪn / (AmE), / ˈɡræfɪk ˌɛn.dʒɪn / (BrE)
A software component or system designed to render and display graphics on a computer screen, often used as part of larger applications or operating systems.
software for rendering graphics on screens

Examples

The new video game relies on a powerful graphic engine to deliver realistic visuals.
Microsoft Windows includes a sophisticated graphic engine as part of its core system.
When developing 3D modeling applications, choosing the right graphic engine is essential for performance.

Synset Examples

The graphic engine of Microsoft Windows is its integral part.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: graphics engine - alternative term for software that renders computer graphics
