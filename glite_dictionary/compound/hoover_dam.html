Hoover Dam

1. PROPN

/ ˈhuːvɚ dæm / (AmE), / ˈhuːvə dæm / (BrE)
A large concrete dam on the Colorado River, built between 1931 and 1936 on the border between Nevada and Arizona, known for generating hydroelectric power and creating Lake Mead.
major dam on the Colorado River in the U.S.

Examples

The construction of the Hoover Dam was a monumental feat of engineering during the Great Depression.
Millions of people visit the Hoover Dam each year to admire its breathtaking architecture.
The Hoover Dam provides electricity to Nevada, Arizona, and California.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Boulder Dam - an alternative historical name for Hoover Dam

Etymology

Named after Herbert Hoover, the 31st President of the United States, who played a key role in its construction.
