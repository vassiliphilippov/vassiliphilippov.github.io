anise seed

1. NOUN

/ ˈænɪs siːd / (AmE), / ˈænɪs siːd / (BrE)
The small, liquorice-flavored seed of the anise plant, used in cooking, baking, and to flavour drinks and medicines.
liquorice-flavoured culinary seed

Examples

The recipe called for a teaspoon of anise seed to enhance the cake’s flavor.
Some traditional liqueurs are made using distilled anise seed.
Herbalists recommend anise seed for its soothing effects on digestion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anise - the aromatic Mediterranean plant bearing anise seeds

Etymology

From Latin *anisum* (anise), from Greek *ánison*; 'seed' from Old English *sǣd*.
