cane sugar

1. NOUN

/ ˈkeɪn ˌʃʊɡər / (AmE), / ˈkeɪn ˌʃʊɡə / (BrE)
Sugar in the form of sucrose obtained from the juice of sugar cane, as distinguished from sugar derived from other sources like sugar beet.
sucrose from sugar cane

Examples

Many bakers prefer to use cane sugar because of its distinctive taste and texture.
The label indicates that this soft drink contains only natural cane sugar and no artificial sweeteners.
Farmers in tropical countries harvest large quantities of cane sugar each year for export.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: beet sugar - sugar obtained from sugar beet

2. NOUN

/ ˈkeɪn ˌʃʊɡɚ / (AmE), / ˈkeɪn ˌʃʊɡə / (BrE)
Sugar obtained from sugarcane and used as a sweetening agent in food and drinks.
sugar from sugarcane used as sweetener

Examples

Many recipes call for cane sugar because of its distinct flavor and texture.
The company sources only organic cane sugar for its premium chocolate bars.
Brown cane sugar adds a rich color and taste to baked goods.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: beet sugar - sugar derived from sugar beet plants
