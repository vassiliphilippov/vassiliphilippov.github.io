Republic of Peru

1. PROPN

/ rɪˈpʌblɪk əv pəˈru / (AmE), / rɪˈpʌblɪk əv pəˈruː / (BrE)
A country in western South America, formerly the center of the Inca Empire, which achieved independence from Spain in 1821.
country in western South America

Examples

The Republic of Peru is renowned for its diverse landscapes, from the Andes mountains to the Amazon rainforest.
After independence from Spain, the Republic of Peru established its own government in 1821.
Tourists often visit the Republic of Peru to explore ancient Inca sites like Machu Picchu.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Peru - country in western South America

Etymology

From Latin 'res publica' (public affair) and the Spanish name 'Perú.'
