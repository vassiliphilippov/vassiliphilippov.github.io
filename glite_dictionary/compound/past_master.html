past master

1. NOUN

/ pæst ˈmæstər / (AmE), / pɑːst ˈmɑːstə / (BrE)
a person who was formerly a master, especially one who has held the post of master in a society, organization, or profession
person previously holding the position of master

Examples

After serving as chair for a decade, he became a past master of the organization.
As a past master of the lodge, she advised new members on traditions.
He was honored at the reunion as a respected past master.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: master - a person who has control over something or is highly skilled

2. NOUN

/ pæst ˈmæstɚ / (AmE), / pɑːst ˈmɑːstə / (BrE)
a person who is highly skilled or experienced in a particular activity or field, often due to long practice
a person highly skilled from experience

Examples

After thirty years in the kitchen, she became a past master at making pastries.
He is a past master of negotiation, always finding a solution where others fail.
As a past master of disguise, the actor could transform himself into almost anyone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expert - a person with a high level of knowledge or skill
  • Antonym: novice - a person new to and inexperienced in a job or situation

Etymology

Originally referred to the former holder of a position of mastery (such as in a guild or society); later extended to anyone highly skilled.
