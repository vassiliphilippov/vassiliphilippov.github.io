Amerindian language

1. NOUN

/ ˌæməˈrɪndiən ˈlæŋgwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌaməˈrɪndɪən ˈlaŋgwɪdʒ / (BrE)
any of the languages spoken by the indigenous peoples of the Americas.
language of the indigenous peoples of the Americas

Examples

The Hopi and Quechua languages are well-known examples of Amerindian languages.
Researchers have documented hundreds of Amerindian languages throughout North and South America.
Efforts are being made to revitalize endangered Amerindian languages in many indigenous communities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indigenous language - language native to a particular region or people

Etymology

Amerindian: a blend of 'American' and 'Indian'; 'language' from Latin 'lingua'.
