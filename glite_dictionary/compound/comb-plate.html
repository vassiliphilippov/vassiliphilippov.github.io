comb-plate

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌmˌpleɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɒmˌpleɪt / (BrE)
a locomotor organ in some marine animals, consisting of a row of large, fused cilia used for movement
a fused ciliary locomotor organ in marine animals

Examples

The ctenophore moves through the water by beating its comb-plates in coordinated waves.
Researchers observed the shimmering effect created by light passing through the comb-plates.
Each comb-plate is made up of thousands of cilia fused together at the base.

Etymology

From 'comb' (tool with teeth) and 'plate', referring to its appearance and structure.
