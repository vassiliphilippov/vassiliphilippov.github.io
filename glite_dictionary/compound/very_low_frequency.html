very low frequency

1. NOUN

/ ˌvɛri ˌloʊ ˈfriːkwənsi / (AmE), / ˌvɛri ˌləʊ ˈfriːkwənsi / (BrE)
A range of radio frequencies from 3 to 30 kilohertz, used primarily for long-distance communication.
radio frequency range, 3–30 kHz

Examples

Submarines often use very low frequency signals to communicate over long distances underwater.
The very low frequency band is useful for transmitting signals that can penetrate deep into the earth or water.
Researchers studied the effects of very low frequency electromagnetic waves on the ionosphere.
