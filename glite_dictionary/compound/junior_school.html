junior school

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒuːniər skuːl / (AmE), / ˈdʒuːniə skuːl / (BrE)
(in Britain) a school for children aged between 7 and 11, typically following infant school and preceding secondary school.
British school for children aged 7-11
british

Examples

My brother will start junior school in September when he turns seven.
The local junior school organizes an annual sports day for its students.
After finishing junior school, children usually move on to secondary education.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: infant school - British school for children aged 4-7
  • Degree Higher: secondary school - School for students after junior school, generally aged 11 and above
