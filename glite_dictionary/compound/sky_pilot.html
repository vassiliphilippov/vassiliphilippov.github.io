sky pilot

1. NOUN

/ skaɪ ˈpaɪlət / (AmE), / skaɪ ˈpaɪlət / (BrE)
an informal term for a chaplain serving in the military services
military chaplain (informal)
informal

Examples

After the battle, the sky pilot offered words of comfort to the weary soldiers.
Every unit had its own sky pilot who led Sunday services on the base.
The sky pilot was a trusted confidant for many men on the front lines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chaplain - a member of the clergy attached to a military, prison, or institution

Etymology

Originally 19th-century nautical slang, later adopted in military contexts.
