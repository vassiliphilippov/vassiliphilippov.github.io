alpha-linolenic acid

1. NOUN

/ ˌælfə lɪˌnoʊlɛnɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / ˌælfə lɪˌnəʊliːnɪk ˈæsɪd / (BrE)
a polyunsaturated omega-3 fatty acid essential for human nutrition, found chiefly in vegetable oils such as canola and flaxseed
an essential omega-3 fatty acid
medical

Examples

Flaxseed is an excellent source of alpha-linolenic acid, an important nutrient for heart health.
Vegetarians often rely on plant oils for their daily intake of alpha-linolenic acid.
Canola oil contains high levels of alpha-linolenic acid, making it popular among health-conscious consumers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: linoleic acid - another essential polyunsaturated fatty acid
  • Synonym: omega-3 fatty acid - type of fatty acid with health benefits

Etymology

From 'alpha-' (Greek, denoting position of the double bond) + 'linolenic' (from linoleic acid) + 'acid'.
