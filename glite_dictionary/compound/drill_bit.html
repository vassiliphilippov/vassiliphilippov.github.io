drill bit

1. NOUN

/ drɪl bɪt / (AmE), / drɪl bɪt / (BrE)
a cutting tool attached to a drill, used to make holes, especially in oil exploration and construction
cutting tool for making holes

Examples

The engineer selected a new drill bit to penetrate the hard rock layers.
After several hours, the drill bit needed to be replaced due to the tough sediment encountered.
They ordered custom drill bits for the oil exploration project in Texas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bit - the cutting part of a tool for boring or drilling
