hog town

1. NOUN

/ hɑɡ taʊn / (AmE), / hɒɡ taʊn / (BrE)
A town or place known for pig farming, or informally, a lively or crowded place.
town noted for pig farming or lively activity
informal

Examples

He grew up in a hog town surrounded by farms and livestock.
During the festival, the city transforms into a true hog town, packed with visitors and energy.
Many restaurants in this hog town serve locally produced pork dishes.

Synset Examples

He grew up in a hog town, surrounded by farms.
This place is a real hog town during the festivals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boomtown - a rapidly growing town
  • Antonym: ghost town - a deserted town with few or no people

/ hɑɡ taʊn / (AmE), / hɒɡ taʊn / (BrE)

lively or busy place (slang)
