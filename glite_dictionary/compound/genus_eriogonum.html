genus Eriogonum

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ɛrɪˈɒgənəm / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ɛrɪˈɒgənəm / (BrE)
A genus of North American herbs in the buckwheat family (Polygonaceae), comprising numerous wildflower species known as wild buckwheats.
genus of North American wild buckwheats

Examples

Species in the genus Eriogonum are commonly known as wild buckwheats across North America.
Botanists have identified hundreds of species within the genus Eriogonum.
The genus Eriogonum thrives in dry, sandy soils and is important for native pollinators.

Etymology

From Greek 'erion' (wool) and 'gonu' (knee), referring to the woolly-stemmed joints of some species.
