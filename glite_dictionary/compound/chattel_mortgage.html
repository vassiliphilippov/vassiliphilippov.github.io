chattel mortgage

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃætəl ˈmɔrɡɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈtʃætəl ˈmɔːɡɪdʒ / (BrE)
a loan in which personal property is used as security, so that the lender may claim the property if the loan is not repaid
loan secured by personal property
legal

Examples

The bank offered a chattel mortgage to help her purchase new equipment for her business.
Under the chattel mortgage, the car serves as security until the loan is fully repaid.
Farmers often use a chattel mortgage to finance tractors and other machinery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mortgage - loan secured by real property, especially real estate

Etymology

From 'chattel', meaning personal property, and 'mortgage', from Old French 'mort gage' (dead pledge).
