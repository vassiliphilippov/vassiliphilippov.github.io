conjoined twin

1. NOUN

/ kənˈdʒɔɪnd twɪn / (AmE), / kənˈdʒɔɪnd twɪn / (BrE)
one of a pair of identical twins physically joined together at one or more parts of their bodies at birth
one of a pair of physically connected twins
medical

Examples

Conjoined twin surgeries are complex and require a team of specialized doctors.
The documentary followed the lives of two conjoined twins growing up in a small town.
Medical staff prepared carefully before attempting to separate the conjoined twins.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Siamese twin - an older term for conjoined twin

Etymology

From Latin 'coniungere' (to join together) and 'twin' (two offspring born at one birth).
