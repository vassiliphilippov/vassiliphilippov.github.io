order Actinaria

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdɚ ˌæktɪˈnɛriə / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə ˌæktɪˈnɛːrɪə / (BrE)
A taxonomic order of marine animals comprising the sea anemones.
taxonomic order of sea anemones

Examples

Members of the order Actinaria are commonly known as sea anemones.
Biologists study the order Actinaria to understand coral reef ecosystems.
The order Actinaria includes species found in oceans worldwide.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sea anemone - a marine animal of the order Actinaria

Etymology

From Greek aktis, aktinos (ray), referring to the radiating structure of these animals.
