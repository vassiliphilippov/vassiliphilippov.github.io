family Triopidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli traɪˈɒpɪdi / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli traɪˈɒpɪdi / (BrE)
A family of small freshwater crustaceans known as tadpole shrimps, within the order Notostraca.
a family of tadpole shrimps (crustaceans)
medical

Examples

The family Triopidae includes several species of tadpole shrimps found in temporary pools.
Scientists recently discovered a new species in the family Triopidae in South America.
Members of the family Triopidae have shield-like shells and three eyes.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Notostraca - an order of crustaceans comprising tadpole shrimps
  • Degree Lower: Triops - a genus of organisms within the family Triopidae

Etymology

From scientific Latin Triopidae, family name based on the genus Triops.
