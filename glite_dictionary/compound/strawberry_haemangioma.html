strawberry haemangioma

1. NOUN

/ ˈstrɔːˌbɛri hiːˌmænˈdʒaɪoʊmə / (AmE), / ˈstrɒbəri hɪˌmanˈdʒaɪəmə / (BrE)
a congenital bright red, raised birthmark caused by a cluster of blood vessels near the skin's surface, usually fading as a child grows
bright red vascular birthmark in infants
medical british

Examples

The doctor explained that the strawberry haemangioma on her arm should fade within a few years.
Parents were reassured that their baby's strawberry haemangioma was harmless and would likely disappear over time.
The pediatrician diagnosed the bright red spot as a strawberry haemangioma and recommended monitoring its growth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: capillary haemangioma - type of haemangioma formed of capillaries

Etymology

Haemangioma from Greek haima (blood) + angeion (vessel) + -oma (tumor).
