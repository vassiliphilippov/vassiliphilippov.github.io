mashed potato

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæʃt pəˈteɪdoʊ / (AmE), / ˌmæʃt pəˈteɪtəʊ / (BrE)
a dish made from potatoes that have been peeled, boiled, and crushed into a soft, smooth mass, often with added butter, milk, or seasoning
soft, crushed cooked potatoes

Examples

She served roast chicken with a side of mashed potato.
The chef whipped the mashed potato until it was perfectly smooth and creamy.
I love adding garlic and cheese to my mashed potato for extra flavor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: potato - a starchy tuber vegetable

2. NOUN

/ mæʃt pəˈteɪdoʊ / (AmE), / mæʃt pəˈteɪtəʊ / (BrE)
potatoes that have been boiled and then crushed to a soft, smooth consistency, typically served as a side dish
boiled and crushed potato dish

Examples

Would you like some mashed potato with your chicken?
She prepared a bowl of creamy mashed potato for the dinner party.
Adding roasted garlic to mashed potato gives it a rich, savory flavor.
