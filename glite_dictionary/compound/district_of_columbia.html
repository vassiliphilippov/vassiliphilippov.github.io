District of Columbia

1. PROPN

/ ˈdɪstrɪkt əv kəˈlʌmbiə / (AmE), / ˈdɪstrɪkt əv kəˈlʌmbiə / (BrE)
A federal district in the eastern United States, coextensive with the city of Washington, and serving as the nation's capital.
federal district containing Washington, D.C.

Examples

The White House is located in the District of Columbia.
Congress passed several laws specifically for the District of Columbia.
She moved to the District of Columbia for her new job in government.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Washington, D.C. - the capital city located within the District of Columbia

Etymology

Named for Christopher Columbus; established by the U.S. Congress in 1790.
