dead end

1. NOUN

/ dɛd ɛnd / (AmE), / dɛd ɛnd / (BrE)
A passage or road that is closed at one end and does not lead anywhere.
road or passage closed at one end

Examples

They realized too late that the alley was a dead end and had to turn back.
She parked her car at the dead end and walked the rest of the way.
The new development is being built at the far end of a dead end street.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cul-de-sac - a street or passage closed at one end
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03151115-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ dɛd ɛnd / (AmE), / dɛd ɛnd / (BrE)
a situation in which no further progress, development, or advancement is possible
situation where progress is impossible

Examples

After weeks of research, the scientists realized their project had reached a dead end.
Negotiations came to a dead end when neither side would compromise.
She felt her career was at a dead end until she changed industries.

Synset Examples

reached an impasse on the negotiations

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impasse - a situation in which progress is impossible, especially in negotiations
  • Synonym: stalemate - a situation in which further action is blocked or no advancement can be achieved
  • Antonym: breakthrough - a sudden advance; an important development making progress possible
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
