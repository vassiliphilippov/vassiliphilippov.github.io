C battery

1. NOUN

/ si ˈbæɾ.ɚ.i / (AmE), / si ˈbæt.əri / (BrE)
a battery used to maintain grid potential in a vacuum tube, especially in older radio equipment
battery for grid potential in vacuum tube

Examples

The technician replaced the old C battery in the vintage radio to restore its proper function.
Before transistors, the C battery played a crucial role in setting the grid voltage of vacuum tubes.
Collectors of antique electronics often search for period-correct C batteries to complete their restorations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: A battery - battery supplying filament current in a vacuum tube
  • Synonym: B battery - battery supplying plate voltage in a vacuum tube

Etymology

Named following the convention of labeling batteries as A, B, and C for different circuits in vacuum tube equipment.
