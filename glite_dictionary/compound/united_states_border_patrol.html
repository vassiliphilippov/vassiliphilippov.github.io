United States Border Patrol

1. PROPN

/ ˌjuː.naɪ.t̬ɪd ˈsteɪts ˈbɔːr.dɚ pəˈtroʊl / (AmE), / ˌjuː.nɪ.tɪd ˈsteɪts ˈbɔː.də pəˈtrəʊl / (BrE)
a federal agency responsible for detecting and preventing illegal entry of people into the United States, operating as the mobile law enforcement arm of the Immigration and Naturalization Service
U.S. agency patrolling national borders
american

Examples

The United States Border Patrol apprehended several individuals attempting to cross the border illegally.
After joining the United States Border Patrol, she was assigned to patrol the southern border.
The United States Border Patrol uses advanced technology to monitor remote areas.
