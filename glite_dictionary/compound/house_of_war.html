House of War

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaʊs əv ˈwɔr / (AmE), / ˈhaʊs əv ˈwɔː / (BrE)
in Islamic political theory, a region or country where Muslims are in the minority and are subject to persecution or where Islamic law is not established
regions where Muslims are persecuted or Islamic law is absent

Examples

In historical texts, Europe was sometimes described as the House of War by Islamic scholars.
The term House of War is rarely used today but remains important in the study of Islamic history.
He wrote about the difference between the House of War and the House of Islam in medieval times.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: House of Islam - areas governed by Islamic law or where Muslims are secure

Etymology

Translation of Arabic *dar al-harb* (دار الحرب‎), meaning 'abode of war'.
