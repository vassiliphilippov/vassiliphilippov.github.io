hormic psychology

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɔrmɪk saɪˈkɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ˈhɔːmɪk saɪˈkɒlədʒi / (BrE)
a branch of psychology that emphasizes goal-directed or purposive forces in behavior, as opposed to mechanical or reflexive explanations
branch of psychology focusing on purposive behavior

Examples

He devoted his career to exploring the principles of hormic psychology in human motivation.
Unlike behaviorists, McDougall believed that hormic psychology could better explain purposive actions.
In class, we discussed how hormic psychology highlights the importance of drives and goals in behavior.

Synset Examples

Opposing behaviourism, he argued that behaviour was generally goal-oriented and purposive, an approach he called hormic psychology.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: behaviorism - school of psychology focused on observable behaviors, often opposed to hormic psychology

Etymology

From Greek hormē (impulse, urge) + psychology.
