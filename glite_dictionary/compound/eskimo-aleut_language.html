Eskimo-Aleut language

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛskɪmoʊ əˈluːɪt ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌɛskɪməʊ əˈluːɪt ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
a family of languages spoken in the Arctic regions of North America and northeastern Siberia, including Eskimo (Inuit and Yupik) and Aleut.
Arctic language family (Eskimo and Aleut)

Examples

Linguists have studied the history and structure of the Eskimo-Aleut language family for decades.
The Eskimo-Aleut language family is distinct from the Indo-European languages spoken in Europe.
Efforts are underway to document endangered dialects within the Eskimo-Aleut language family.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Indo-European language - a major family of languages spoken in Europe and Asia

Etymology

From the names of its principal branches, Eskimo (Inuit and Yupik languages) and Aleut.
