gram-atomic weight

1. NOUN

/ ɡræm əˈtɑmɪk weɪt / (AmE), / ɡræm əˈtɒmɪk weɪt / (BrE)
The weight in grams of an element that is numerically equal to its atomic weight; a quantity representing one mole of atoms of the element.
weight in grams equal to atomic weight of an element

Examples

The gram-atomic weight of oxygen is approximately 16 grams.
When performing calculations in chemistry, scientists often use the gram-atomic weight to measure elements.
A sample containing the gram-atomic weight of carbon has 6 grams.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mole - the SI unit for amount of substance equal to Avogadro's number of particles

Etymology

From 'gram' plus 'atomic weight'; rooted in 19th-century chemistry nomenclature.
