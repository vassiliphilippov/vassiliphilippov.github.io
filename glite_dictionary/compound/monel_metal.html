Monel metal

1. NOUN

/ məˈnɛl ˌmɛtəl / (AmE), / məˈnɛl ˌmɛtəl / (BrE)
a nickel-copper alloy with small amounts of other metals, known for its strength and corrosion resistance
nickel-copper alloy; strong and corrosion-resistant

Examples

Engineers often select Monel metal for marine applications due to its corrosion resistance.
The valves were constructed from Monel metal to withstand harsh chemical environments.
Unlike ordinary steel, Monel metal does not rust easily in seawater.

Etymology

Trade name, coined in early 20th century, after Ambrose Monell, president of the International Nickel Company.
