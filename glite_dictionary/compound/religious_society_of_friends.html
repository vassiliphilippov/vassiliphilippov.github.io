Religious Society of Friends

1. PROPN

/ rɪˈlɪdʒəs səˈsaɪəti əv frɛndz / (AmE), / rɪˈlɪdʒəs səˈsaɪəti əv frɛndz / (BrE)
A Christian religious group founded by George Fox in the mid-17th century, also known as the Quakers, noted for their pacifism and plain living.
Christian group also known as the Quakers

Examples

The Religious Society of Friends has a tradition of peaceful protest and social activism.
Many members of the Religious Society of Friends refuse military service for reasons of conscience.
The Religious Society of Friends emphasizes worship in stillness and simplicity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Quaker - member of the Religious Society of Friends

Etymology

Name adopted by the group to reflect their belief in spiritual friendship and equality before God.
