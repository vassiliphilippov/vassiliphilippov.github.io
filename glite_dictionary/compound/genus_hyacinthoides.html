genus Hyacinthoides

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˌhaɪəˌsɪnˈθɔɪdiːz / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˌhaɪəˌsɪnˈθɔɪdiːz / (BrE)
A genus of perennial bulbous plants native to western Europe and North Africa, sometimes classified in the family Hyacinthaceae.
a genus of bulbous plants

Examples

The bluebell, found in British woodlands, belongs to the genus Hyacinthoides.
Botanists often debate the classification of the genus Hyacinthoides within plant families.
Several species in the genus Hyacinthoides are prized in gardens for their vibrant purple-blue flowers.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'Hyacinthus' (a related genus) with Greek '-eides' (like, resembling).
