anadama bread

1. NOUN

/ ˌænəˈdæmə brɛd / (AmE), / ˌænəˈdæmə brɛd / (BrE)
A traditional yeast bread made with white flour, cornmeal, and molasses, originating in New England.
New England bread with cornmeal and molasses

Examples

In Massachusetts, anadama bread is often served as part of a traditional breakfast.
The bakery specializes in anadama bread, which is sweetened with a touch of molasses.
She enjoyed a slice of warm anadama bread with butter straight from the oven.

Etymology

Originated in New England, U.S., in the late 19th or early 20th century. The origin of the name is uncertain, possibly from a folk story.
