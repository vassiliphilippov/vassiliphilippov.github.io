Marine Corps

1. NOUN

/ məˈrin kɔrp / (AmE), / məˈriːn kɔː / (BrE)
the branch of a nation's armed forces trained for amphibious warfare and specialized land, sea, and air operations, especially the United States Marine Corps
military branch for amphibious operations

Examples

The Marine Corps is known for its rigorous training and high standards.
He served in the Marine Corps for over ten years before retiring.
The Marine Corps was deployed to provide humanitarian aid after the natural disaster.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: army - the main land-based military force of a nation
  • Antonym: navy - the branch of a nation's military forces at sea

Etymology

marine from Latin 'marinus' (of the sea) and corps from French 'corps' (body, military unit).
