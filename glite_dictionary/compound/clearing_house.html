clearing house

1. NOUN

/ ˈklɪrɪŋ haʊs / (AmE), / ˈklɪə.rɪŋ haʊs / (BrE)
a central institution where banks or other organizations exchange financial instruments such as checks, and settle accounts with each other
financial center for interbank exchanges

Examples

The central clearing house ensures that payments between banks are processed efficiently every day.
Many countries have established a national clearing house for interbank settlements.
Each participating bank holds funds in the clearing house to cover its daily transactions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clearing - the process of settling transactions between banks

2. NOUN

/ ˈklɪrɪŋ ˌhaʊs / (AmE), / ˈklɪərɪŋ ˌhaʊs / (BrE)
an organization or institution that collects and distributes items, information, or payments, especially to facilitate transactions between other organizations
organization that facilitates transactions

Examples

The central bank acts as a clearing house for all interbank payments.
The nonprofit organization served as a clearing house for job listings in the community.
All checks must pass through a clearing house before the funds are transferred.

Synset Examples

The company is clearing house to improve efficiency.
They are clearing house to prepare for the new inventory.
The charity event was a clearing house for donated goods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brokerage - a firm that arranges transactions between buyers and sellers

Etymology

From 'clear' (to settle transactions) and 'house' (building or institution). Originally used in banking.
