nuclear magnetic resonance

1. NOUN

/ ˈnuː.kli.ɚ mægˌnɛtɪk rɪˈzənəns / (AmE), / ˈnjuː.klɪ.ə mæɡˌnɛtɪk rɪˈzɒnəns / (BrE)
a physical phenomenon in which atomic nuclei in a magnetic field absorb and re-emit electromagnetic radiation, especially used in imaging and chemical analysis
phenomenon where nuclei absorb radiation in a magnetic field
medical

Examples

The doctor explained how nuclear magnetic resonance could help detect early signs of disease.
Chemists often use nuclear magnetic resonance to determine molecular structures.
Recent advancements have made nuclear magnetic resonance machines faster and more accurate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: NMR - abbreviation for nuclear magnetic resonance

Etymology

Formed from 'nuclear' (relating to the nucleus of an atom), 'magnetic', and 'resonance' (from Latin resonare, to resound).
