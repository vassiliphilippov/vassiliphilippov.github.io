turnip plant

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɝː.nɪp plænt / (AmE), / ˈtɜː.nɪp plɑːnt / (BrE)
any of several widely cultivated plants with edible swollen roots, especially the turnip (Brassica rapa) and related species.
a plant grown for its edible root

Examples

Farmers often rotate turnip plants with other crops to enrich the soil.
The leaves of the turnip plant can also be cooked and eaten as greens.
After harvesting the turnip plants, the roots were stored for winter use.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: turnip - an edible, round root vegetable

Etymology

From 'turnip' (perhaps from older English words for 'round' and 'nape' meaning root) and 'plant'.
