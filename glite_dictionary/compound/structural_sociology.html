structural sociology

1. NOUN

/ ˈstrʌktʃərəl ˌsoʊsiˈɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ˈstrʌktʃərəl ˌsəʊsiˈɒlədʒi / (BrE)
a branch of sociology that studies how the structures of society shape the actions and relationships of individuals, emphasizing that social structures precede and influence individuals
sociology theory emphasizing social structures

Examples

She is conducting research in structural sociology to understand how institutions influence personal behavior.
Structural sociology contends that society's frameworks shape individual actions more than personal choices do.
Many university courses cover structural sociology alongside other sociological theories.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: structuralism - the theory that elements of human culture must be understood in terms of their relationship to a broader system

Etymology

From 'structural', relating to structure, and 'sociology', from French 'sociologie', coined by Auguste Comte.
