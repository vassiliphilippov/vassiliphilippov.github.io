creeping bugle

1. NOUN

/ ˈkripɪŋ ˈbjuːɡəl / (AmE), / ˈkriːpɪŋ ˈbjuːɡl̩ / (BrE)
a low-growing European plant (Ajuga reptans) with spreading stems and spikes of blue flowers, often used as ground cover and naturalized in parts of North America
a low European plant with blue flower spikes

Examples

The creeping bugle quickly spread to cover the bare patches in the garden.
With its vibrant flowers, creeping bugle adds color to shady lawns every spring.
Many gardeners plant creeping bugle to suppress weeds and prevent soil erosion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ajuga - the genus of plants that includes creeping bugle
