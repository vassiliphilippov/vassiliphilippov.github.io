genus Macaca

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs məˈkɑːkə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs məˈkɑːkə / (BrE)
A genus of Old World monkeys that includes macaques, such as the rhesus monkey and Japanese macaque.
genus of macaque monkeys

Examples

The genus Macaca includes several widely studied species such as the rhesus macaque.
Researchers observed behavioral differences among members of the genus Macaca in their natural habitats.
One of the most recognized primates in the genus Macaca is the Japanese macaque, often called the snow monkey.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: macaque - a monkey of the genus Macaca

Etymology

From Latin 'macaca', used for certain kinds of monkeys, ultimately from Portuguese 'macaco', meaning monkey.
