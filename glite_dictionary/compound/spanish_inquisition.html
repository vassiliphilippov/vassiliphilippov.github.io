Spanish Inquisition

1. PROPN

/ ˌspænɪʃ ɪnkwəˈzɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌspænɪʃ ɪŋkwɪˈzɪʃən / (BrE)
a tribunal established in 1478 by King Ferdinand and Queen Isabella of Spain to maintain Catholic orthodoxy, especially active from the 15th to 17th centuries
historic Catholic tribunal in Spain (1478–1834)

Examples

The Spanish Inquisition operated with extensive authority over religious matters in Spain.
Few expected the severity of the Spanish Inquisition's investigations during that period.
Many people were charged with heresy by the Spanish Inquisition in the late 15th century.

Synset Examples

2. PROPN

/ ˌspænɪʃ ɪnˌkwɪˈzɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌspanɪʃ ɪnˌkwɪˈzɪʃən / (BrE)
a period of prolonged religious investigation and persecution carried out by the Catholic monarchy of Spain, beginning in the late 15th century and lasting until the 19th century
historic religious tribunal in Spain

Examples

The Spanish Inquisition was notorious for its harsh methods of interrogating suspected heretics.
Few expected the sudden change in policy—"No one expects the Spanish Inquisition," she joked.
Historians have written extensively about the impact of the Spanish Inquisition on Spanish society.

Synset Examples

People often reference the Spanish Inquisition in discussions of unexpected events.
They joked about the Spanish Inquisition during the history class.
