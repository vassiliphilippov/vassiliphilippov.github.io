solar apex

1. NOUN

/ ˈsoʊlər ˈeɪpɛks / (AmE), / ˈsəʊlə ˈeɪpɛks / (BrE)
the point in the sky toward which the sun and the solar system are moving relative to the fixed stars
point sun appears to move toward in sky

Examples

Astronomers have determined the solar apex to be in the direction of the constellation Hercules.
Research into the solar apex helps us understand the movement of the solar system through the galaxy.
Calculating the position of the solar apex requires precise measurements of stellar motions.

Etymology

From Latin 'solaris' (of the sun) + 'apex' (summit, tip).
