self-raising flour

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛlf ˈreɪzɪŋ flaʊɚ / (AmE), / ˌself ˈreɪzɪŋ ˈflaʊə / (BrE)
Flour that has a leavening agent and salt already mixed in, used for baking to help dough rise without adding separate raising agents.
flour blended with a leavening agent

Examples

For the cake, use self-raising flour to ensure it has a light, fluffy texture.
She realized she had bought plain flour instead of self-raising flour and needed to add baking powder herself.
Most British recipes for scones call for self-raising flour rather than all-purpose flour.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: plain flour - flour without added raising agents
