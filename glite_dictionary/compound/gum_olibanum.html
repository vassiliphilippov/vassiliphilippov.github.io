gum olibanum

1. NOUN

/ ɡʌm oʊˈlɪbənəm / (AmE), / ɡʌm əʊˈlɪbənəm / (BrE)
an aromatic gum resin obtained from certain Arabian or East African trees, used historically in religious ceremonies, embalming, and fumigation
aromatic resin used for incense

Examples

The ancient Egyptians prized gum olibanum for use in their religious rituals.
Traders transported gum olibanum across the Arabian Peninsula in caravans.
He burned gum olibanum as incense during the ceremony.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frankincense - an aromatic resin used in incense and perfumes

Etymology

From Latin 'olibanum', from Greek 'libanos', meaning frankincense.
