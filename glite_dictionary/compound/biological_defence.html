biological defence

1. NOUN

/ baɪəˈlɑdʒɪkəl dɪˈfɛns / (AmE), / baɪəˈlɒdʒɪkəl dɪˈfɛns / (BrE)
measures or procedures designed to protect against attacks using biological agents, such as bacteria, viruses, or toxins
protection against biological attacks

Examples

The military has developed advanced biological defence systems to detect and counter potential bioterrorism threats.
International cooperation is crucial for effective biological defence against emerging infectious diseases.
New protocols for biological defence in hospitals were introduced after the outbreak.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: biological warfare - the use of biological toxins or infectious agents as weapons of war
