family Chalcididae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli kælˈsɪdəˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈfamɪli kalˈsɪdɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of tiny parasitic wasps, including chalcidflies, within the order Hymenoptera
a family of parasitic wasps (chalcidflies)

Examples

The family Chalcididae includes some of the most effective natural parasitoids for controlling agricultural pests.
Researchers found a new species in the family Chalcididae in South America.
Many members of the family Chalcididae exhibit metallic coloring and minute body size.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chalcidfly - a small parasitic wasp in the family Chalcididae

Etymology

From scientific Latin Chalcididae, based on Greek khalkos (copper), referring to the metallic colors of many species.
