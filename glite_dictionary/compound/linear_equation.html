linear equation

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪniɚ ɪˈkweɪʒən / (AmE), / ˈlɪniə ɪˈkweɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
an equation in which the highest power of the variable is one, representing a straight line when graphed
equation of the first degree

Examples

The teacher explained how to solve a linear equation using simple algebraic steps.
When graphed, a linear equation produces a straight line on the coordinate plane.
We learned that the equation y = 2x + 3 is a classic linear equation.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: quadratic equation - an equation of the second degree
  • Degree Higher: equation - a statement that the values of two mathematical expressions are equal

Etymology

From Latin 'linearis' (of or belonging to a line) and Latin 'aequatio' (an equation, a making equal).
