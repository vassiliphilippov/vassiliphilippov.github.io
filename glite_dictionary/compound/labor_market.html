labor market

1. NOUN

/ ˈleɪbɚ ˌmɑrkət / (AmE), / ˈleɪbə ˌmɑːkɪt / (BrE)
the system in which employers find employees and workers look for jobs, with supply and demand interactions determining wages and employment conditions
system where jobs and workers are matched

Examples

In a tight labor market, employers may need to offer higher salaries to attract qualified candidates.
Technological changes have transformed the labor market in recent years.
Young graduates are facing a challenging labor market due to increased competition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: job market - the market for available employment and labor
