high-strength brass

1. NOUN

/ haɪ strɛŋθ bræs / (AmE), / haɪ strɛŋθ brɑːs / (BrE)
a type of brass alloy strengthened by the addition of 1–4% manganese
brass alloy with added manganese

Examples

Engineers often choose high-strength brass for gears requiring both durability and corrosion resistance.
The addition of manganese gives high-strength brass its superior mechanical properties.
This component is made from high-strength brass because it must withstand heavy wear.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brass - a yellow alloy of copper and zinc
