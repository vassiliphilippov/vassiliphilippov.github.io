judicial sale

1. NOUN

/ dʒuˈdɪʃəl seɪl / (AmE), / dʒuˈdɪʃəl seɪl / (BrE)
a sale of property conducted under the authority of a court, usually to satisfy an unpaid debt or obligation
court-ordered sale of property
legal

Examples

The house was sold at a judicial sale after the bank foreclosed on the mortgage.
A judicial sale is often the final step in settling unpaid debts through the courts.
The court ordered a judicial sale to satisfy the creditor's judgment against the defendant.

Etymology

From Latin 'judicialis' (of the court, judicial) + English 'sale'.
