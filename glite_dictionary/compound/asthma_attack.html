asthma attack

1. NOUN

/ ˈæzmə əˌtæk / (AmE), / ˈazmə əˌtak / (BrE)
a sudden episode of severe asthma symptoms, such as shortness of breath and wheezing, often triggered by allergens or irritants
sudden episode of severe asthma symptoms
medical

Examples

He was rushed to the hospital after suffering a severe asthma attack.
Many children experience their first asthma attack during exercise or exposure to pollen.
Using an inhaler can help control the symptoms of an asthma attack.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: asthma - chronic inflammatory disease of the airways
