right hemisphere

1. NOUN

/ ˈraɪt ˈhɛmɪˌsfɪr / (AmE), / ˈraɪt ˈhɛmɪsˌfɪə / (BrE)
the half of the brain located to the right of the corpus callosum, which controls the left side of the body and is associated with certain cognitive functions
the right half of the brain
medical

Examples

The right hemisphere of the brain is often linked to creativity and spatial awareness.
Damage to the right hemisphere can cause neglect of the left side of the body.
Doctors studied how the right hemisphere processes nonverbal information.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: left hemisphere - the left half of the brain, controlling the right side of the body
