identity parade

1. NOUN

/ aɪˈdɛntəti pəˈreɪd / (AmE), / aɪˈdɛntəti pəˈreɪd / (BrE)
a line of people, typically arranged by the police, for a witness to identify a suspect involved in a crime
police lineup for witness identification
british legal

Examples

The witness was asked to pick out the suspect from the identity parade.
Despite being included in the identity parade, he was not recognized by the witness.
The detective organized an identity parade to help solve the case.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: police lineup - a group of people shown to a witness to identify a suspect (American term)
