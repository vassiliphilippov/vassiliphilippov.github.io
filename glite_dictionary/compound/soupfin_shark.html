soupfin shark

1. NOUN

/ ˈsuːpˌfɪn ʃɑrk / (AmE), / ˈsuːpˌfɪn ʃɑːk / (BrE)
a shark found in the Pacific Ocean, valued for its fins (used in soup) and its liver, which is rich in vitamin A
a Pacific shark valued for fins and liver

Examples

Fishermen in California often catch soupfin sharks for their valuable fins and livers.
The soupfin shark population has declined due to overfishing for the Asian fin market.
A single soupfin shark can be a significant source of vitamin A because of its large liver.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: school shark - another name for the soupfin shark
