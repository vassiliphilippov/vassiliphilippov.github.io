splenius muscle

1. NOUN

/ spliniəs ˈmʌsəl / (AmE), / ˈspliːnɪəs ˈmʌsl / (BrE)
either of two flat muscles at the back of the neck that extend from the upper vertebrae to the base of the skull, responsible for rotating, flexing, or extending the head and neck
muscle in the back of the neck
medical

Examples

The splenius muscle helps you turn your head from side to side.
Injury to the splenius muscle can cause neck pain and limited movement.
Physical therapy exercises can strengthen the splenius muscle and improve posture.

Etymology

From Latin 'splenius', from Greek 'splēnion' (a bandage), referring to the shape; 'muscle' from Latin 'musculus'.
