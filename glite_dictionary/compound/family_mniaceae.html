family Mniaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ˌnaɪˈeɪsiaɪ / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli ˌnaɪˈeɪsɪiː / (BrE)
a family of erect mosses characterized by club-shaped paraphyses and hexagonal cells on the upper leaf surface; sometimes considered a subfamily of Bryaceae
a family of mosses in plant taxonomy

Examples

Several species within the family Mniaceae are common in temperate forests around the world.
Research on the family Mniaceae has shed light on the evolutionary history of mosses.
The family Mniaceae is distinguished by its unique leaf cell structure among bryophytes.

Etymology

From scientific Latin Mniaceae, based on the genus Mnium, from Greek mnios (moss).
