unknown quantity

1. NOUN

/ ʌnˈnoʊn ˈkwɑːn.t̬ə.t̬i / (AmE), / ʌnˈnəʊn ˈkwɒn.tɪ.ti / (BrE)
a variable in an equation whose value is not known and must be determined
variable in an equation to be determined

Examples

In algebra, the unknown quantity is often represented by the letter x.
To solve the equation, you must first isolate the unknown quantity on one side.
Each problem in the test required finding an unknown quantity given certain conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: variable - a symbol or letter representing a number in mathematics

2. NOUN

/ ʌnˌnoʊn ˈkwɑːn.t̬ə.ti / (AmE), / ʌnˌnəʊn ˈkwɒn.tɪ.ti / (BrE)
a person or thing whose abilities, importance, or influence are not yet known or cannot be predicted
person/thing with unpredictable impact or significance

Examples

The new CEO is still an unknown quantity to most employees.
Given her lack of political experience, she remained an unknown quantity in the election.
The weather is always an unknown quantity for outdoor wedding planners.

Synset Examples

I don't know what the new man will do; he's still an unknown quantity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wild card - a person or thing whose influence is unpredictable
