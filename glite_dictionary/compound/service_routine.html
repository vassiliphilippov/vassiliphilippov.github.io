service routine

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɝːvəs ruˈtin / (AmE), / ˈsɜːvɪs ruːˈtiːn / (BrE)
a subroutine or function in a computer program designed to perform a specific supporting task as needed
supporting routine in computing

Examples

The service routine is called every time the printer needs attention.
A well-designed service routine can simplify complex tasks in a software application.
Several service routines handle different error conditions within the operating system.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: subroutine - a sequence of program instructions that performs a specific task
  • Antonym: main routine - the primary or central routine in a program
