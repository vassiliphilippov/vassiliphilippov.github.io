hamamelid dicot genus

1. NOUN

/ ˌhæməˈmiːlɪd ˈdaɪkɑt ˈdʒiːnəs / (AmE), / ˌhaməˈmiːlɪd ˈdɪkɒt ˈdʒiːnəs / (BrE)
a genus of mainly woody, early-diverging dicotyledonous flowering plants, often with unisexual flowers borne in catkins
genus of primitive woody dicot plants
medical

Examples

The hamamelid dicot genus includes several species known for their ornamental value.
Researchers discovered new characteristics within a previously undescribed hamamelid dicot genus.
The hamamelid dicot genus often features unisexual flowers arranged in striking catkins.

Etymology

From 'Hamamelidaceae' (family name from Hamamelis, witch hazel genus) and 'dicot' (short for dicotyledon), plus Latin genus.
