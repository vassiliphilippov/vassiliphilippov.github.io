Bull Moose Party

1. PROPN

/ bʊl mus ˈpɑrti / (AmE), / bʊl muːs ˈpɑːti / (BrE)
a former US political party founded by Theodore Roosevelt during the 1912 presidential campaign, known for its progressive policies
US political party (1912), founded by Theodore Roosevelt

Examples

The Bull Moose Party played a major role in splitting the Republican vote in 1912.
Theodore Roosevelt was the most prominent leader of the Bull Moose Party.
Progressive reforms were central to the Bull Moose Party's platform.

Etymology

Named after the bull moose, chosen as the party's emblem for its strength and vigor.
