suborder Prosimii

1. NOUN

/ sʌbˌɔrdər proʊˈsɪmiːaɪ / (AmE), / sʌbˌɔːdə prəʊˈsɪmiːaɪ / (BrE)
A group in some primate classifications formerly comprising all prosimians, including lemurs and sometimes tarsiers; not used in all systems.
primate suborder including prosimians

Examples

The suborder Prosimii is not recognized in all modern primate classifications.
Researchers once grouped lemurs and tarsiers together under the suborder Prosimii.
Debates about primate evolution often mention the historic use of suborder Prosimii.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lemur - a primate of the family Lemuridae native to Madagascar

Etymology

From New Latin 'Prosimii', from Latin pro- (before) + simius (ape, monkey).
