sort of

1. ADV

/ sɔrt əv / (AmE), / sɔːt əv / (BrE)
to some degree; to a certain extent, but not completely or exactly
to some degree; somewhat

Examples

I was sort of surprised by her reaction, but not completely shocked.
The weather is sort of chilly today, so you might want a sweater.
He sort of understands the rules, but he still makes some mistakes.

Synset Examples

it was rather cold
the party was rather nice
the knife is rather dull
I rather regret that I cannot attend
He's rather good at playing the cello
he is kind of shy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kind of - to some extent; somewhat
  • Synonym: rather - to some extent; fairly or moderately
