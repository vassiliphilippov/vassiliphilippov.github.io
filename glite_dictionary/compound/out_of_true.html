out of true

1. ADJ

/ aʊt əv tru / (AmE), / aʊt əv truː / (BrE)
not properly aligned, straight, or level; not accurately fitted.
not aligned or level

Examples

The carpenter noticed that the doorframe was out of true and needed adjustment.
If your bike wheel is out of true, it might wobble as you ride.
Any wall that is out of true can cause problems when hanging shelves or cabinets.

Synset Examples

the frame was out of true
off-level floors and untrue doors and windows

Related Terms

  • Antonym: true - accurately aligned or fitted
