spark lever

1. NOUN

/ spɑrk ˈlɛvɚ / (AmE), / spɑːk ˈlɛvə / (BrE)
a lever on the steering column of early automobiles used to adjust the timing of the ignition
ignition-timing lever in early automobiles

Examples

The driver adjusted the spark lever to improve engine performance as they climbed the hill.
Many vintage cars featured a spark lever for manual ignition timing control.
Before starting the engine, make sure the spark lever is in the correct position.
