flowering glume

1. NOUN

/ ˈflaʊ.ər.ɪŋ ɡluːm / (AmE), / ˈflaʊ.ər.ɪŋ ɡluːm / (BrE)
the lower and typically more robust of the two glumes that directly enclose the floret in most grasses (family Gramineae/Poaceae)
lower glume enclosing grass floret

Examples

The flowering glume plays an important role in protecting the developing floret in wheat.
Researchers compared the thickness of the flowering glume among different grass species.
The palea and flowering glume together form a protective casing around the reproductive organs.
