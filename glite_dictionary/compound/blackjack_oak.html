blackjack oak

1. NOUN

/ ˈblækˌdʒæk oʊk / (AmE), / ˈblækˌdʒak əʊk / (BrE)
a small or medium-sized deciduous oak tree (Quercus marilandica) of central and southeastern US, with dark bark and broad, three-lobed leaves, often forming dense thickets
a US oak tree with three-lobed leaves

Examples

The dense thickets of blackjack oak provided cover for many animals in the region.
He identified the tree by its dark bark and the distinctive leaves of the blackjack oak.
Land managers often control blackjack oak growth to preserve open grasslands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oak - a type of tree or its wood, genus Quercus
