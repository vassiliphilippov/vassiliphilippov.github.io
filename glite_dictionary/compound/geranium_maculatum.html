Geranium maculatum

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˈreɪniəm ˌmækjuˈleɪtəm / (AmE), / dʒəˈreɪnɪəm ˌmakjʊˈleɪtəm / (BrE)
a common wild geranium of eastern North America, having deeply divided leaves and rose-purple flowers
wild geranium of eastern North America

Examples

The woodland was full of blooming Geranium maculatum in early spring.
Gardeners appreciate Geranium maculatum for its attractive foliage and vibrant flowers.
Many pollinators visit Geranium maculatum because of its abundant nectar.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wild geranium - another common name for Geranium maculatum

Etymology

From Latin; 'geranium' from Greek *geranion* (crane), referencing the fruit's shape, and 'maculatum' meaning 'spotted'.
