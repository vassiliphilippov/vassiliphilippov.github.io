prairie sage

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɛri seɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈprɛəri seɪdʒ / (BrE)
a perennial herb with cottony-white foliage, native to the southwestern United States
herbaceous plant of the U.S. Southwest

Examples

The prairie sage bloomed throughout the summer, its silvery leaves adding texture to the open fields.
Botanists are studying how prairie sage adapts to arid climates in the Southwest.
Local artisans use dried prairie sage leaves in traditional herbal remedies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sagebrush - a shrubby plant of the genus Artemisia, often found in North American prairies and deserts
