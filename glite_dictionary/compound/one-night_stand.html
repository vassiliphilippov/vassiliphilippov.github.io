one-night stand

1. NOUN

/ ˈwʌn naɪt ˌstænd / (AmE), / ˈwʌn naɪt ˌstænd / (BrE)
a performance, show, or entertainment held at a particular place on only one night
single-night performance or show

Examples

The touring theatre company scheduled a one-night stand in our town.
Their jazz band was booked for a one-night stand at the historic club.
After the one-night stand, the musicians packed up and left for the next city.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwʌnˌnaɪt ˈstænd / (AmE), / ˈwʌn naɪt ˈstænd / (BrE)
a sexual encounter or relationship that lasts for only one night and is not repeated
brief sexual encounter or relationship

Examples

He regretted the one-night stand almost as soon as it was over.
They met at a party, and what she thought was a one-night stand turned into a long friendship.
Some people see a one-night stand as purely physical, without emotional involvement.

Synset Examples

he ran through a series of loveless one-night stands

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: affair - a sexual relationship, especially a secret or illicit one, that lasts for some time

Etymology

From 'one night' + 'stand', originally referring to a single-night theatrical performance; sense shifted to brief sexual encounter in the 20th century.

3. NOUN

/ ˈwʌnˌnaɪt ˈstænd / (AmE), / ˈwʌn.naɪt ˈstænd / (BrE)
a brief sexual encounter with someone, typically only lasting for one occasion, and without any expectation of further relationship or commitment
brief, casual sexual encounter
informal

Examples

He admitted that the one-night stand meant nothing to him emotionally.
They both understood the one-night stand would not lead to a relationship.
After her one-night stand, she decided she wanted something more meaningful.

Synset Examples

He was hoping for more than just a one-night stand.
They both agreed it was just a one-night stand.
She regretted the one-night stand the next morning.

Etymology

From 'one-night,' indicating a single occasion, and 'stand,' slang for a sexual or romantic encounter; originated in American English in the early 20th century.
