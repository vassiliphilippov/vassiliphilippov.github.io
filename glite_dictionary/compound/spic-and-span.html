spic-and-span

1. ADJ

/ ˌspɪk ən ˈspæn / (AmE), / ˌspɪk ən ˈspæn / (BrE)
Extremely clean, neat, and fresh in appearance; spotlessly clean or brand new.
spotlessly clean or brand new

Examples

Her kitchen was always spic-and-span, without a speck of dust anywhere.
They moved into a spic-and-span apartment with fresh paint and new appliances.
After a long day of cleaning, the classroom looked spic-and-span for the start of term.

Synset Examples

shiny brand-new shoes
a spick-and-span novelty

Related Terms

  • Synonym: immaculate - perfectly clean, free from flaws
  • Antonym: dirty - not clean; covered or marked with an unclean substance

Etymology

From earlier phrase 'spick and span new', with 'spick' meaning a nail or spike and 'span' meaning a wood chip; both suggest something newly made.

2. ADJ

/ spɪk ənd spæ̃n / (AmE), / spɪk ənd spæn / (BrE)
very clean, neat, and tidy; spotless.
very clean and tidy

Examples

The living room was spic-and-span before the guests arrived.
Her spic-and-span office made a great first impression on the visitors.
He kept his car spic-and-span inside and out.

Synset Examples

the apartment was immaculate
in her immaculate white uniform
a spick-and-span kitchen
their spic red-visored caps

Related Terms

  • Synonym: immaculate - perfectly clean and tidy
  • Antonym: dirty - not clean or tidy

Etymology

Early 17th century, originally 'spick and span-new', meaning as new as a recently made spike and wood chip (span, old term for a chip of wood).
