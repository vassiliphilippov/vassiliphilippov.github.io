dive bomber

1. NOUN

/ daɪv ˈbɑːmɚ / (AmE), / daɪv ˈbɒmə / (BrE)
a military aircraft designed to release bombs while diving steeply toward its target for greater accuracy
aircraft that drops bombs in a steep dive

Examples

The dive bomber swooped down toward the enemy ship before releasing its payload.
During World War II, the dive bomber was an effective tool for precision attacks.
Pilots trained extensively to master the dangerous techniques needed to fly a dive bomber.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bomber - an aircraft designed to drop bombs
