ring rot bacteria

1. NOUN

/ rɪŋ rɑt bækˈtɪriə / (AmE), / rɪŋ rɒt bækˈtɪəriə / (BrE)
a group of bacteria that cause ring rot, a serious plant disease affecting tomatoes, potatoes, tobacco, and similar crops, resulting in brown rot of plant tissues
bacteria causing ring rot in plants
medical

Examples

The spread of ring rot bacteria can devastate potato crops if not controlled early.
Farmers use strict seed regulations to prevent the introduction of ring rot bacteria into their fields.
Scientists are developing disease-resistant plants to combat the effects of ring rot bacteria on agriculture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ring rot - a bacterial disease of plants characterized by decaying or rotting rings in plant tissue
