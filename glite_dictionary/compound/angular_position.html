angular position

1. NOUN

/ ˈæŋɡjələr pəˈzɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈaŋɡjʊlə pəˈzɪʃən / (BrE)
the orientation of a line, ray, or object relative to a reference direction, typically measured as an angle
orientation measured as an angle

Examples

The angular position of the satellite is tracked to maintain accurate communication.
You can calculate the wheel's velocity by measuring its change in angular position.
The engineer adjusted the angular position of the solar panel to maximize sunlight exposure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: angular displacement - change in angular position over time
