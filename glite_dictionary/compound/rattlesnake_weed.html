rattlesnake weed

1. NOUN

/ ˈrætəlˌsneɪk wiːd / (AmE), / ˈrætəlsneɪk wiːd / (BrE)
a North American hawkweed (Hieracium venosum) with purple-veined basal leaves, found from Canada to northern Georgia and Kentucky
a type of North American hawkweed plant

Examples

The leaves of rattlesnake weed are marked with distinctive purple veins.
Hikers in the Appalachian region sometimes spot rattlesnake weed growing along wooded trails.
Native Americans traditionally believed that rattlesnake weed could treat snakebites.

Etymology

Named for its supposed use in traditional remedies for rattlesnake bites; 'weed' from Old English 'wēod'.
