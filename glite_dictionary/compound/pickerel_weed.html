pickerel weed

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪkərəl wiːd / (AmE), / ˈpɪkərəl wiːd / (BrE)
an American aquatic plant with spikes of blue flowers, commonly found in shallow water of streams and ponds
American aquatic plant with blue flower spikes

Examples

The pond was lined with pickerel weed, its blue flowers attracting dragonflies and butterflies.
Wetland restoration projects often include native species like pickerel weed to promote biodiversity.
She identified the tall, flowering stems as pickerel weed during her botany field trip.

Etymology

Named for its habitat frequented by pickerel fish and for its weedy growth; 'pickerel' from Middle English 'pikerel' (young pike).
