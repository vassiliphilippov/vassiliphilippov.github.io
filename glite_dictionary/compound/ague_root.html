ague root

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪɡju ˌrut / (AmE), / ˈeɪɡju ˌruːt / (BrE)
A North American plant (colicroot) with a scaly or granular perianth and white flowers, found in the southeastern United States; sometimes used in herbal medicine.
North American plant with white flowers

Examples

Traditional herbalists sometimes used ague root to treat fevers and digestive complaints.
The delicate white flowers of the ague root bloom in early summer across southeastern woodlands.
Many species related to ague root are valued for their ornamental and medicinal properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: colicroot - Another name for the plant species ague root refers to

Etymology

‘Ague’ from Middle English, meaning malaria or fever, in reference to traditional medicinal use + ‘root’.
