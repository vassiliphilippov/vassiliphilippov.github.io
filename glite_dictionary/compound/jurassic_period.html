Jurassic period

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˈræsɪk ˈpɪriəd / (AmE), / dʒʊəˈræsɪk ˈpɪəriəd / (BrE)
the geological period from about 201 million to 145 million years ago, marked by the dominance of dinosaurs and the spread of conifers
geological period dominated by dinosaurs

Examples

The Jurassic period saw the rise of many iconic dinosaur species.
Conifers were widespread during the Jurassic period, forming vast forests.
Fossils from the Jurassic period have been found on every continent.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Triassic period - the preceding geological period before the Jurassic
  • Degree Higher: Cretaceous period - the succeeding geological period after the Jurassic

Etymology

From French Jurassique, referring to the Jura Mountains where rocks from this period were first studied.
