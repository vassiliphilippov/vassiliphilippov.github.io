khokhlushka spirit

1. NOUN

/ xoʊkˈluʃkə ˈspɪrɪt / (AmE), / xɒkˈluʃkə ˈspɪrɪt / (BrE)
a lively and joyful atmosphere, especially one expressed in exuberant dance, often associated with Slavic or Ukrainian cultural performances
spirit of joyful, exuberant dance

Examples

The festival was filled with the khokhlushka spirit, drawing laughter and applause from the audience.
Her performance truly captured the khokhlushka spirit that the troupe is famous for.
They danced with such khokhlushka spirit that it inspired others to join in.

Synset Examples

She embodied the khokhlushka spirit with her dance.

Etymology

From Russian 'khokhlushka' (a Ukrainian woman), combined with 'spirit'.
