leaf roller

1. NOUN

/ lif ˈroʊlər / (AmE), / liːf ˈrəʊlə / (BrE)
A moth whose larvae roll up and tie leaves with silk to form nests.
moth larva that rolls leaves into nests

Examples

The leaf roller caused significant damage to the new growth on my apple tree.
Gardeners often look for signs of leaf roller activity, such as rolled and silken-covered leaves.
Controlling leaf rollers early in the season can help protect ornamental plants.
