barbershop quartet

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑːrbərˌʃɑːp kwɔrˈtɛt / (AmE), / ˈbɑːbəˌʃɒp kwɔːˈtɛt / (BrE)
A group of four singers, usually men, performing unaccompanied songs in close four-part harmony, often in a sentimental style.
unaccompanied four-singer vocal group

Examples

The barbershop quartet performed classic songs at the local talent show.
He joined a barbershop quartet to improve his harmony skills and make new friends.
The distinctive sound of a barbershop quartet filled the theater with rich harmonies.

Etymology

From 'barbershop', referring to the American tradition of group singing in barbershops, and 'quartet', meaning a group of four performers.
