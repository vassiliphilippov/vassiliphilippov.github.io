home base

1. NOUN

/ hoʊm beɪs / (AmE), / həʊm beɪs / (BrE)
(Baseball) the base consisting of a flat, usually rubber, slab that the batter stands beside and must be touched by a runner to score a run.
base in baseball a runner must touch to score

Examples

The crowd cheered as he slid safely into home base to win the game.
The umpire signaled that the runner was out before reaching home base.
She practiced her swing, making sure she stood correctly next to home base.

Synset Examples

he ruled that the runner failed to touch home

Related Terms

  • Synonym: home plate - (Baseball) another term for home base

2. NOUN

/ hoʊm beɪs / (AmE), / həʊm beɪs / (BrE)
the main office or administrative center for an organization or business
main office or headquarters

Examples

The company's home base is located in San Francisco, where most major decisions are made.
After traveling to different branches, she returned to the home base to finalize the report.
Many startups keep their home base in smaller cities due to lower operating costs.
