night court

1. NOUN

/ naɪt kɔrt / (AmE), / naɪt kɔːt / (BrE)
a criminal court in a large city that holds sessions at night, instead of during usual daytime hours
criminal court holding night sessions

Examples

The city established a night court to handle minor offenses after normal hours.
Many defendants prefer night court because it allows them to avoid missing work.
Reporters often cover high-profile cases that are heard in night court.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: court - a tribunal presided over by judges, especially for criminal and civil cases
