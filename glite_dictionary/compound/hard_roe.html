hard roe

1. NOUN

/ hɑrd roʊ / (AmE), / hɑːd rəʊ / (BrE)
the ripe eggs or egg-filled ovary of a fish, typically with a grainy texture and used as food
fish eggs or egg-filled ovary

Examples

The chef garnished the toast with a spoonful of hard roe for added flavor.
She ordered a delicate salad topped with smoked hard roe at the seafood restaurant.
During spawning season, the fish markets sell fresh hard roe to eager customers.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: soft roe - fish milt, the sperm-filled reproductive glands of male fish
