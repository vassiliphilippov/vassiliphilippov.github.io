resting spore

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛstɪŋ spɔr / (AmE), / ˈrɛstɪŋ spɔː / (BrE)
a dormant spore produced by certain algae or fungi that can germinate after a prolonged inactive period
dormant spore in algae or fungi
medical

Examples

During unfavorable conditions, some fungi produce resting spores to survive until the environment improves.
The formation of resting spores allows algae to persist through harsh winters.
Scientists observed that resting spores can remain dormant for several years before germinating.
