vox humana

1. NOUN

/ vɑks hjuˈmeɪnə / (AmE), / vɒks hjuːˈmɑːnə / (BrE)
an organ reed stop designed to produce tones that imitate the human voice
organ stop imitating the human voice

Examples

The church organ features a vox humana stop that adds a distinctly vocal quality to the music.
She pulled out the vox humana for the choir piece, creating a warm, singing tone.
Many organists admire the expressive power of the vox humana stop during solo passages.

Etymology

From Latin, meaning 'human voice'.
