Saronic Gulf

1. PROPN

/ səˈrɑːnɪk ɡʌlf / (AmE), / səˈrɒnɪk ɡʌlf / (BrE)
a gulf of the Aegean Sea located on the southeastern coast of Greece, between the Attica and Peloponnese peninsulas
gulf of the Aegean Sea off southeastern Greece

Examples

Tourists often take boat trips across the Saronic Gulf to explore the nearby islands.
The Saronic Gulf has been important to Greek maritime trade since ancient times.
Beautiful sunsets over the Saronic Gulf attract photographers from all over the world.

Etymology

Named after the ancient region of Saronis (Greek Σαρωνικός).
