Christian year

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɪstʃən jɪr / (AmE), / ˈkrɪstʃən jɪə / (BrE)
The annual cycle of seasons and festivals observed in the Christian church, including major feast days and special liturgical periods.
ecclesiastical calendar year

Examples

The readings in church follow the Christian year, marking each season and festival.
Advent marks the beginning of the Christian year for most Western churches.
She enjoys learning about the different traditions associated with the Christian year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: church year - the annual sequence of seasons and festivals in the Christian liturgical calendar
