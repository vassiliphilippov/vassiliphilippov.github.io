Kegel exercises

1. NOUN

/ ˈkeɡəl ˌɛksaɪzɪz / (AmE), / ˈkiːɡəl ˌɛksəsaɪzɪz / (BrE)
exercises designed to strengthen the pelvic floor muscles, commonly to improve bladder control or pelvic health, especially in women
pelvic floor muscle exercises
medical

Examples

Many postpartum women are encouraged to do Kegel exercises to regain pelvic strength.
Your doctor may suggest Kegel exercises to help with mild urinary incontinence.
Practicing Kegel exercises daily can improve bladder control over time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pelvic floor exercises - exercises to strengthen muscles of the pelvic floor

Etymology

Named after Arnold H. Kegel, an American gynecologist who developed the exercises in the 1940s.
