prime time

1. NOUN

/ ˈpraɪm taɪm / (AmE), / ˈpraɪm taɪm / (BrE)
The period during the evening, typically between 7 and 11 p.m., when the largest television audience is available.
peak TV viewing hours in the evening

Examples

The show's ratings soared after it was moved to prime time.
Advertisers pay a premium to air commercials during prime time.
Most families watch television together during prime time.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: off-peak - times with lower television audience; not prime time

2. NOUN

/ ˈpraɪm taɪm / (AmE), / ˈpraɪm taɪm / (BrE)
the time in the evening when the largest number of people are watching television and when advertising rates are at their highest
peak viewing hours for television

Examples

The show airs during prime time when most viewers are at home.
Advertisers pay more for commercials shown in prime time slots.
Networks compete fiercely for popular programs to fill their prime time schedules.

Synset Examples

The show airs during prime time on Thursday nights.
He always watches the news during prime time.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: off-peak - the period when TV audiences are smaller than during prime time
