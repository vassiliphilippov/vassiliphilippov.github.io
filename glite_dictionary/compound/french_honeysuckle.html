French honeysuckle

1. NOUN

/ frɛntʃ ˈhʌniˌsʌkl̩ / (AmE), / frentʃ ˈhʌniˌsʌkl̩ / (BrE)
a European herb (Hedysarum coronarium) with small, fragrant crimson or white spurred flowers, cultivated as an ornamental and for forage
European herb with fragrant crimson or white flowers

Examples

The garden was filled with the sweet scent of French honeysuckle in full bloom.
Farmers often plant French honeysuckle to improve soil and provide forage for bees.
We admired the delicate crimson spikes of French honeysuckle during our walk in the countryside.

2. NOUN

/ frɛntʃ ˈhʌniˌsʌkəl / (AmE), / frɛntʃ ˈhʌniˌsʌkl / (BrE)
A perennial plant of southern Europe, cultivated for forage and its nectar-rich pink flowers, important as a honey crop.
perennial European plant grown for forage and honey

Examples

The field was full of blooming French honeysuckle, attracting bees all summer long.
Farmers often plant French honeysuckle to improve soil fertility and provide forage for livestock.
Local honey made from French honeysuckle nectar is prized for its delicate flavor.
