to christen the new ship

1. VERB

/ ˈkrɪs.ən ðə nuː ʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈkrɪs.ən ðə njuː ʃɪp / (BrE)
to officially name a newly built ship in a formal ceremony, often by breaking a bottle of champagne against its hull
officially name a new ship in a ceremony

Examples

The shipyard invited the mayor to christen the new ship during the launch event.
Traditionally, a bottle of champagne is broken to christen the new ship.
Dozens gathered at the port to watch the navy christen the new ship this morning.

Synset Examples

The queen will christen the new ship in April.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: launch - to put a new ship into the water for the first time

Etymology

From Middle English 'cristenen', meaning to baptize or give a Christian name, later extended to naming ships in ceremonies.
