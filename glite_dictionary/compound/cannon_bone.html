cannon bone

1. NOUN

/ ˈkænən ˌboʊn / (AmE), / ˈkanən bəʊn / (BrE)
the greatly developed metacarpal or metatarsal bone in the lower leg of a hoofed mammal, especially the horse
main lower leg bone in hoofed mammals

Examples

The veterinarian examined the horse's cannon bone for signs of a fracture.
In racehorses, the cannon bone is prone to injury due to high impact exercises.
The strength of a horse's cannon bone is crucial for its performance and endurance.
