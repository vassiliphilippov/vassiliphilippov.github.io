pin money

1. NOUN

/ pɪn ˈmʌni / (AmE), / pɪn ˈmʌni / (BrE)
a small amount of money for minor or incidental expenses; personal spending money, especially for small day-to-day items
money for incidental expenses

Examples

She always kept a little pin money aside for her weekly coffee treat.
When I was a child, my grandparents gave me pin money to spend at the fair.
Many people like to have some pin money available for emergencies or small purchases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spending money - money for personal daily expenses
  • Synonym: allowance - regular amount of money given for personal use

Etymology

Originally referred to money given for purchasing pins, which were once expensive and scarce; later broadened to mean small discretionary funds (17th century).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɪn ˌmʌni / (AmE), / ˈpɪn ˌmʌni / (BrE)
a small amount of money used for minor personal expenses, often discretionary spending
small amount of spending money

Examples

She used her pin money to buy a new magazine at the store.
Many teenagers earn pin money by doing chores around the house.
He set aside a little pin money each week for lunches with friends.

Synset Examples

With her pin money, she bought gifts for her friends.
He spent his pin money on a new book.
She saved her pin money for a special treat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: allowance - a sum of money given regularly for personal use

Etymology

Originally referred to money given to women for buying pins and small personal items; 'pin' and 'money' in English since the 16th century.
