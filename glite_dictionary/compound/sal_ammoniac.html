sal ammoniac

1. NOUN

/ sæl əˈmoʊniˌæk / (AmE), / sæl əˈmɒniˌak / (BrE)
a white crystalline salt, ammonium chloride, used in dry cells, medicines, and metalworking
ammonium chloride; white crystalline salt

Examples

The electrician used sal ammoniac to clean the tip of the soldering iron.
Old dry cells contain sal ammoniac as the main electrolyte.
Pharmacists once prescribed sal ammoniac for certain medical conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ammonium chloride - the chemical compound also known as sal ammoniac

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'sal ammoniacus' (salt of Ammon); named after the temple of Ammon in Egypt, near which it was found.
