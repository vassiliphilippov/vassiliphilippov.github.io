stone fruit

1. NOUN

/ stoʊn fruːt / (AmE), / stəʊn fruːt / (BrE)
A fleshy fruit with a single hard seed or "stone" inside, such as a peach, cherry, or plum.
fruit with a single hard seed (stone)

Examples

Many summer desserts use fresh stone fruit like peaches and cherries.
The orchard grows several types of stone fruit, including plums and apricots.
She prefers stone fruit jams for their sweet and tangy flavors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drupe - botanical term for fleshy fruit with a single stone or pit
