to deflect attention

1. VERB

/ dɪˈflɛkt əˈtɛnʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈflɛkt əˈtɛnʃən / (BrE)
to cause people's focus to move away from something, especially to avoid criticism or scrutiny
redirect focus away from something

Examples

The company released a statement to deflect attention from the recent data breach.
She skillfully deflected attention by changing the topic to something less controversial.
Politicians often try to deflect attention from scandals during interviews.

Synset Examples

He tried to deflect attention from his mistakes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: divert attention - to turn attention away from something
