Serer language

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛrər ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈsɛːrər ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
A West African language closely related to Fula, spoken primarily by the Serer people in Senegal and Gambia.
West African language of the Serer people

Examples

The Serer language is an important part of cultural heritage in western Senegal.
She is fluent in both French and the Serer language, which she learned from her grandparents.
Efforts are being made to preserve the Serer language through education and literature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Fula language - a related West African language spoken by the Fula people

Etymology

Serer from the name of the Serer people of Senegal and Gambia.
