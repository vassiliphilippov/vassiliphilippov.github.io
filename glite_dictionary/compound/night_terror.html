night terror

1. NOUN

/ naɪt ˈtɛr.ɚ / (AmE), / naɪt ˈtɛr.ər / (BrE)
A sleep disorder, typically in children, involving episodes of waking in extreme fear or panic but with no recollection of the cause.
sleep disorder with episodes of terror
medical

Examples

As a child, Mark would sometimes experience night terrors that woke up the whole household.
Unlike nightmares, people usually do not remember their night terrors after waking up.
Many parents are alarmed the first time their child has a night terror and screams in fear during sleep.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: nightmare - a bad dream causing strong negative feelings; commonly remembered after waking
