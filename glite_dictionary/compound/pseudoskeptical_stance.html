pseudoskeptical stance

1. NOUN

/ ˌsuːdoʊˈskɛptɪkəl stæns / (AmE), / ˌsjuːdəʊˈskɛptɪkəl stɑːns / (BrE)
An attitude or position of feigned or insincere skepticism, often adopted to dismiss or undermine evidence without genuine critical inquiry.
feigned or insincere skepticism

Examples

Some journalists adopted a pseudoskeptical stance when reporting on the health risks, questioning evidence without valid grounds.
His pseudoskeptical stance caused frustration among scientists who had presented clear data.
Online forums often attract participants who assume a pseudoskeptical stance to dismiss new research outright.

Synset Examples

He took a pseudoskeptical stance on climate change evidence.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: skepticism - attitude of doubting the truth or value of something
  • Degree Higher: denialism - refusal to accept established facts or evidence

Etymology

From pseudo- (false, from Greek pseudes) + skeptical (from Greek skeptikos, inquiring, doubtful) + stance.
