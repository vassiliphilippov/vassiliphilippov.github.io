Yavapai language

1. NOUN

/ ˌjævəˈpaɪ ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌjavəˈpaɪ ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Yuman language traditionally spoken by the Yavapai people of western Arizona.
the indigenous language of the Yavapai people

Examples

Fewer than a dozen elders still speak the Yavapai language fluently today.
The Yavapai language belongs to the Yuman branch of the Hokan language family.
Efforts are underway to teach the Yavapai language to younger generations in Arizona.

Etymology

Yavapai is the name of the indigenous people; 'language' from Latin 'lingua'.
