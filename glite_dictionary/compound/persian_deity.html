Persian deity

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɝːʒən ˈdiːəti / (AmE), / ˈpɜːʃən ˈdiːəti / (BrE)
a god or goddess worshiped by the ancient Persians
ancient Persian god or goddess

Examples

Ahura Mazda is perhaps the most well-known Persian deity from ancient Zoroastrianism.
Each Persian deity had specific domains, such as fire, water, or the sun.
Mythology books often describe the characteristics of every Persian deity in great detail.
