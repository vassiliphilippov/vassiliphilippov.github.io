mental synthesis

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛn.təl ˈsɪn.θə.sɪs / (AmE), / ˈmɛn.təl ˈsɪn.θɪ.sɪs / (BrE)
the process of combining separate ideas or mental images into a unified, coherent concept or image in the mind
combining ideas into a unified mental image

Examples

Daydreaming often involves mental synthesis of multiple memories and images into entirely new scenarios.
Designers rely on mental synthesis when imagining products that do not yet exist.
Children use mental synthesis to combine different concepts they have learned into original stories.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: synthesis - the combination of components to form a connected whole
  • Antonym: analysis - the separation of a whole into its parts for study

Etymology

From Latin 'mens' (mind) and Greek 'synthesis' (putting together).
