dichromic acid

1. NOUN

/ daɪˈkroʊmɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / daɪˈkrəʊmɪk ˈasɪd / (BrE)
a hypothetical acid (H₂Cr₂O₇) known only in solution and as the basis for dichromate salts
hypothetical acid; basis of dichromate salts
medical

Examples

Dichromic acid exists only in aqueous solution and cannot be isolated as a pure substance.
Potassium dichromate is a salt that is derived from dichromic acid.
The structure of dichromic acid is important in understanding the chemistry of dichromates.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: chromic acid - a strong acid derived from chromium trioxide, H₂CrO₄

Etymology

From 'di-' meaning 'two', 'chromic' from Greek 'chrōma' (color), referring to the presence of two chromium atoms.
