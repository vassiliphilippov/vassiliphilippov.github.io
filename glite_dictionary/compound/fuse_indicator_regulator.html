fuse indicator regulator

1. NOUN

/ fjuːz ˌɪndɪˈkeɪtər rɪˈɡjuleɪtər / (AmE), / fjuːz ˌɪndɪˈkeɪtə rɪˈɡjʊleɪtə / (BrE)
an instrument used to set the fuze time on projectiles equipped with mechanical time fuzes, especially in artillery applications.
device for setting fuze times on artillery projectiles

Examples

The fuse indicator regulator ensures accurate timing for the detonation of artillery shells.
Technicians were trained to calibrate the fuse indicator regulator for maximum precision.
Modern artillery units still rely on a digital version of the fuse indicator regulator during operations.

Synset Examples

Naval and anti-aircraft artillery started using analogue computers before World War 2, and these were connected to the guns to automatically aim them; they also had automatic fuze setters.
