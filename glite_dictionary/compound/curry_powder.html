curry powder

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɜri ˌpaʊdər / (AmE), / ˈkʌri ˌpaʊdə / (BrE)
a mixture of ground spices, typically including cumin, coriander, and turmeric, used as a seasoning in Indian and South Asian cooking
blend of spices for seasoning, especially in Indian cuisine

Examples

She sprinkled curry powder into the simmering pot to add a fragrant flavor.
The recipe calls for a tablespoon of curry powder and fresh ginger.
I prefer mild curry powder in my vegetable stew rather than the hot variety.

Etymology

From Tamil 'kari' (sauce) and English 'powder'.
