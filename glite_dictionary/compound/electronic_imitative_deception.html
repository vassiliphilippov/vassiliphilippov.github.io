electronic imitative deception

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌlɛkˈtrɑnɪk ɪˈmɪtəˌtɪv dɪˈsɛpʃən / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛkˈtrɒnɪk ɪˈmɪtətɪv dɪˈsɛpʃən / (BrE)
the deliberate introduction of electromagnetic signals into enemy systems to mimic their own emissions, with the aim of deceiving or confusing them
deceptive imitation of enemy signals using electromagnetic energy

Examples

The military employed electronic imitative deception to disrupt enemy radar systems during the operation.
With advanced technology, electronic imitative deception can closely mimic the enemy's communication signals.
Training exercises focused on detecting electronic imitative deception in hostile electronic environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: electronic deception - the use of electronic means to mislead or confuse enemy forces
