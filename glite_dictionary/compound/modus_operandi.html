modus operandi

1. NOUN

/ ˌmoʊdəs ˌɑːpəˈrændi / (AmE), / ˌməʊdəs ˌɒpəˈrandi / (BrE)
a particular way or method of doing something, especially one that is characteristic of a person or group
a method or way of operating

Examples

The detective recognized the criminal's modus operandi in several burglaries across town.
Each company has its own modus operandi when it comes to managing risk.
Her modus operandi involved careful planning and precise execution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: method - a way of doing something systematically
  • Synonym: procedure - an established or official way of doing something

Etymology

From Latin, meaning 'method of operating'.
