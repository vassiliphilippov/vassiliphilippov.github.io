common nardoo

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmən nɑrˌdu / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən nɑːˈduː / (BrE)
an Australian aquatic fern (Marsilea drummondii) with clover-like leaves, often found in wetlands and used as a food source by Indigenous Australians
Australian aquatic fern; Marsilea drummondii

Examples

The explorers survived for weeks by eating common nardoo collected from the riverbanks.
Common nardoo thrives in the floodplains of Australia's inland waterways.
Indigenous communities have traditionally harvested common nardoo spores as a food source.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nardoo - Australian aquatic fern, Marsilea drummondii or similar
