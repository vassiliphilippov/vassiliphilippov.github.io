ant cow

1. NOUN

/ ænt kaʊ / (AmE), / ænt kaʊ / (BrE)
an insect, such as an aphid or scale insect, that excretes a sweet substance (honeydew) eaten by ants, which often protect and tend them
insect tended by ants for honeydew

Examples

The aphid, often called an ant cow, is protected by ants in exchange for its honeydew.
Gardeners sometimes notice lines of ants attending to ant cows on rose bushes.
Some species of ant cow are essential for the diet of forest-dwelling ants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aphid - a small sap-sucking insect often called an 'ant cow'
