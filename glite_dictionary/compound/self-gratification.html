self-gratification

1. NOUN

/ sɛlf ˌɡrætɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / sɛlf ˌɡrætɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of satisfying one's own desires, needs, or pleasures, especially without regard for others
satisfaction of one's own desires or pleasures

Examples

He was criticized for pursuing self-gratification at the expense of his responsibilities.
Some people argue that self-gratification can undermine the development of self-discipline.
The culture of instant self-gratification has led to changes in consumer behavior.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: self-indulgence - the act of allowing oneself to enjoy pleasures or desires, often excessively

Etymology

From 'self-' (oneself) + 'gratification' from Latin gratificari (to give pleasure).
