scissor hold

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪzɚ hoʊld / (AmE), / ˈsɪzə həʊld / (BrE)
A wrestling hold in which a person wraps their legs around an opponent's body or head and squeezes by pressing the feet together.
wrestling hold with legs wrapped and squeezing

Examples

The wrestler secured a scissor hold around his opponent's torso to immobilize him.
Applying a tight scissor hold can force an opponent to tap out in a grappling match.
She won the round by catching her rival in a textbook scissor hold.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: submission hold - a move designed to force an opponent to submit in wrestling or martial arts

Etymology

Formed from 'scissor' + 'hold', referring to the scissor-like action of the legs.
