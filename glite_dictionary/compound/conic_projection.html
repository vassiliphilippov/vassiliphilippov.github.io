conic projection

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːnɪk prəˈdʒɛkʃən / (AmE), / ˈkɒnɪk prəˈdʒɛkʃən / (BrE)
a map projection in which the surface features of a globe are projected onto a cone placed over part of the globe, typically with its point over one of the Earth's poles
map projection onto a cone

Examples

The conic projection is often used for mapping mid-latitude regions because it balances area and shape distortion.
Many road and weather maps are based on a conic projection to accurately represent larger areas.
When creating her atlas, the cartographer chose a conic projection for optimal representation of the continent.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: map projection - the representation of the globe on a flat map

Etymology

From 'conic', relating to a cone, and 'projection', from Latin projectio (a throwing forward).
