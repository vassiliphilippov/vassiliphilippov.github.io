nuclear fuel

1. NOUN

/ ˈnuː.kli.ɚ ˈfjuːl / (AmE), / ˈnjuː.klɪ.ə ˈfjʊəl / (BrE)
A material, such as uranium or plutonium, that can be used as a source of energy in a nuclear reactor.
material used to produce energy in nuclear reactors

Examples

The power plant uses nuclear fuel to generate electricity for the city.
Spent nuclear fuel must be carefully stored to prevent environmental contamination.
Most modern submarines run on nuclear fuel rather than traditional diesel engines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: uranium - a heavy metal used as nuclear fuel
  • Synonym: plutonium - a radioactive metal used as nuclear fuel
