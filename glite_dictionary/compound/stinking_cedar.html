stinking cedar

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɪŋkɪŋ ˌsiːdər / (AmE), / ˈstɪŋkɪŋ ˌsiːdə / (BrE)
a rare small evergreen tree of northern Florida, known for its glossy green leaves that emit an unpleasant smell when crushed
small Florida evergreen with fetid-smelling leaves

Examples

The park ranger pointed out the stinking cedar growing along the damp forest trail.
Crushing the leaves of a stinking cedar releases a strong, unpleasant odor.
Researchers are studying the stinking cedar to learn more about its unique defensive compounds.
