genus Piper

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒinəs ˈpaɪpər / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈpiːpə / (BrE)
The chief genus of the family Piperaceae, consisting mainly of climbing tropical shrubs, including the black pepper plant.
pepper plant genus (Piperaceae)

Examples

The genus Piper includes hundreds of species found in tropical regions worldwide.
Black pepper comes from Piper nigrum, a member of the genus Piper.
Many species within the genus Piper are cultivated for their aromatic qualities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Piperaceae - the botanical family including the genus Piper

Etymology

From Latin 'piper' (pepper), originally from Sanskrit 'pippali'.
