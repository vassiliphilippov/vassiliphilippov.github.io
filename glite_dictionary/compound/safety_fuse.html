safety fuse

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪfti fjuz / (AmE), / ˈseɪfti fjuːz / (BrE)
a device in an electrical circuit that interrupts the flow of current when it exceeds a safe level, preventing damage or fire
device that interrupts excess electrical current

Examples

The electrician replaced the blown safety fuse to restore power to the room.
You should always check the safety fuse before troubleshooting other electrical issues.
If the current exceeds the limit, the safety fuse will break the circuit to prevent damage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fuse - a device that interrupts excessive electrical current
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03412279-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈseɪfti fjuːz / (AmE), / ˈseɪfti fjuːz / (BrE)
a slow-burning cord or tube filled with combustible material, used for safely igniting detonators from a distance
slow-burning cord for igniting explosives

Examples

The safety fuse was lit, giving the workers time to retreat before the blast.
Miners rely on the safety fuse to ensure detonations occur at a safe distance.
Before using explosives, always check that the safety fuse is properly installed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fuse - a cord or tube for igniting an explosive
