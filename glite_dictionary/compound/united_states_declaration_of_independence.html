United States Declaration of Independence

1. PROPN

/ ˌjuːnəˈtaɪd steɪts ˌdɛkləˈreɪʃən əv ˌɪndɪˈpɛndəns / (AmE), / ˌjuːnɪtɪd steɪts ˌdɛkləˈreɪʃən əv ˌɪndɪˈpɛndəns / (BrE)
the formal document adopted on 4 July 1776 by the Second Continental Congress, declaring the thirteen American colonies independent from British rule
historic American independence document (1776)

Examples

The United States Declaration of Independence was signed on July 4, 1776, marking the nation's birth.
Thomas Jefferson was the principal author of the United States Declaration of Independence.
The principles outlined in the United States Declaration of Independence continue to influence democratic societies today.
