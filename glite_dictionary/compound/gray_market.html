gray market

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡreɪ ˌmɑrkɪt / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪ ˌmɑːkɪt / (BrE)
an unofficial market in which goods are bought and sold through channels not authorized by the original manufacturer, often at prices lower than those set by official distributors
unofficial market for goods, often at lower prices

Examples

Many electronic products enter the country through the gray market, allowing consumers to buy them at lower prices.
Some companies take legal action to prevent the sale of their goods on the gray market.
Buying from the gray market can sometimes mean missing out on a manufacturer's warranty.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: black market - illegal market where goods are bought and sold outside of government regulation

Etymology

Patterned after 'black market', referring to unofficial or unauthorized trade; 'gray' implies less illicit than 'black'.
