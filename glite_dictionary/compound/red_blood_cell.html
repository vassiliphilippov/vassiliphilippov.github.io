red blood cell

1. NOUN

/ rɛd blʌd sɛl / (AmE), / rɛd blʌd sɛl / (BrE)
a mature blood cell that contains hemoglobin and carries oxygen from the lungs to the body's tissues; it is typically a biconcave disc without a nucleus in humans and most mammals.
oxygen-carrying blood cell
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that a deficiency in red blood cell count can lead to anemia.
Hemoglobin is what gives the red blood cell its ability to transport oxygen.
Each red blood cell survives in the bloodstream for about 120 days.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: erythrocyte - scientific term for a red blood cell
