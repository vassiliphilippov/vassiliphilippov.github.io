jumby tree

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒʌm.bi tri / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌm.bi triː / (BrE)
A small tree native to the West Indies and northeastern Venezuela, with large oblong pointed leaflets, panicles of purple flowers, and black or scarlet seeds with black spots.
West Indian tree with purple flowers and spotted seeds

Examples

The jumby tree is well known in the Caribbean for its distinctive seeds.
Local markets sometimes sell crafts made from the seeds of the jumby tree.
We encountered a blooming jumby tree during our hike in northeastern Venezuela.
