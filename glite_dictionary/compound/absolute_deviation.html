absolute deviation

1. NOUN

/ ˌæbsəluːt dɪˌviːeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌæbsəluːt dɪˌviːeɪʃən / (BrE)
In statistics, the absolute difference between a value in a data set and a specified point, usually the mean or median.
difference from the mean or median (statistics)
medical

Examples

The absolute deviation of 8 from the mean of 5 is 3.
Researchers calculate the absolute deviation to measure how much the data points vary.
A smaller absolute deviation indicates that the data are clustered closely around the mean.

Synset Examples

This average absolute deviation gives the average distance of any data item from the mean and thus is a good measure of spread.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: mean absolute deviation - average of the absolute deviations from the mean

Etymology

From 'absolute', meaning not negative, and 'deviation', meaning departure from a standard.
