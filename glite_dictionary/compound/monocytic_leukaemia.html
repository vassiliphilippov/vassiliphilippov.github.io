monocytic leukaemia

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɑːnəˌsaɪtɪk luˈkiːmiə / (AmE), / ˌmɒn.əˌsaɪ.tɪk ljʊəˈkiː.mi.ə / (BrE)
A type of leukemia characterized by an abnormal increase of monocytes and monoblasts in the blood.
leukemia with excess monocytes in blood
medical british

Examples

The patient's diagnosis of monocytic leukaemia required immediate treatment with chemotherapy.
Researchers are studying new therapies to improve survival rates for monocytic leukaemia.
Symptoms of monocytic leukaemia may include fatigue, fever, and frequent infections.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leukemia - cancer of blood-forming tissues, hindering the body's ability to fight infection

Etymology

From 'monocytic' (relating to monocytes) + 'leukaemia', itself from Greek 'leukos' (white) + 'haima' (blood).
