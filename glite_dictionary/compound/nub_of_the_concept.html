nub of the concept

1. NOUN

/ nʌb əv ðə kənˈsɛpt / (AmE), / nʌb əv ðə kənˈsɛpt / (BrE)
the central or most important part of an idea or theory
essential part of an idea

Examples

After much discussion, she finally got to the nub of the concept.
The professor explained the nub of the concept using a simple diagram.
It's important to understand the nub of the concept before moving on to the details.

Synset Examples

He only grasped the nub of the concept during class.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: essence - the most important quality or core of something
  • Synonym: core - the central or most important part of something

Etymology

‘Nub’ likely from 17th-century English meaning ‘knob’ or ‘lump’, later used figuratively for the crucial point.
