legal separation

1. NOUN

/ ˈliːgəl ˌsɛpəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈliːɡəl ˌsɛpəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
A court-ordered arrangement in which a married couple lives apart with legally regulated rights and responsibilities but remains legally married.
court-ordered arrangement for married couples living apart
legal

Examples

After years of conflict, they decided on a legal separation rather than divorce.
The court issued a legal separation to clarify custody and financial responsibilities.
A legal separation allows couples to resolve property and support issues while remaining married.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: divorce - legal ending of a marriage

2. NOUN

/ ˈliɡəl ˌsɛpəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈliːɡəl ˌsɛpəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
(law) An arrangement in which a married couple stops living together by agreement or under a court order but remains legally married.
formal separation of married couple
legal

Examples

After years of arguing, they decided a legal separation would be best for everyone involved.
A legal separation allows couples to live apart while remaining technically married.
Many people opt for a legal separation before considering divorce.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: divorce - the legal dissolution of a marriage
