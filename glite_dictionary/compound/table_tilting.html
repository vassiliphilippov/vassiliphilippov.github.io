table tilting

1. NOUN

/ ˈteɪbəl ˌtɪltɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈteɪbl ˌtɪltɪŋ / (BrE)
A practice in which a table is alleged to move or tilt during a séance, supposedly due to the influence of spirits.
alleged spirit movement of tables at séances

Examples

During the Victorian era, many believed that table tilting could help communicate with the spirit world.
Skeptics argued that table tilting was the result of unconscious movements by the participants.
The investigator observed several instances of table tilting during the séance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: table-turning - Practice of causing a table to rotate during a séance, attributed to spirits
