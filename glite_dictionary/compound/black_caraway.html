black caraway

1. NOUN

/ blæk ˈkæɹəˌweɪ / (AmE), / blæk ˈkarəweɪ / (BrE)
A Mediterranean herb (Nigella sativa) whose pungent seeds are used as a spice similar to caraway.
herb with pungent seeds used as spice

Examples

The recipe calls for a teaspoon of black caraway to add a spicy flavor.
Many people confuse black caraway seeds with regular caraway seeds, but they are from different plants.
Traditionally, black caraway is sprinkled on top of naan bread in South Asian cuisine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caraway - a plant with aromatic seeds used as a spice
