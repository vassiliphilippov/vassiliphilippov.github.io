All Saints' Day

1. NOUN

/ ɔl ˈseɪnts deɪ / (AmE), / ɔːl ˈseɪnts deɪ / (BrE)
A Christian festival held on November 1st in Western Christianity, honoring all the saints, known and unknown.
Christian feast day honoring all saints

Examples

Many churches hold special services on All Saints' Day to honor the memory of saints.
In some countries, people visit cemeteries and decorate graves on All Saints' Day.
All Saints' Day is followed by All Souls' Day in the liturgical calendar.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: All Souls' Day - A Christian day dedicated to remembering all the faithful departed

Etymology

From the celebration instituted by Pope Gregory IV in 835, dedicated to all saints.
