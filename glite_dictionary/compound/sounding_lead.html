sounding lead

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaʊndɪŋ lɛd / (AmE), / ˈsaʊndɪŋ lɛd / (BrE)
a weighted metal device attached to a line, used to measure the depth of water
nautical depth-measuring weight

Examples

The sailor dropped the sounding lead overboard to check how deep the river was.
Before approaching the harbor, they used a sounding lead to avoid running aground.
A traditional sounding lead often has tallow in its hollow base to pick up samples from the seabed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sounding line - a line used for measuring water depth, often with a weight at the end

Etymology

From 'sound' (to measure depth) + 'lead' (the heavy metal used for the weight).
