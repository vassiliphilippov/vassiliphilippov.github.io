glutaminic acid

1. NOUN

/ ɡluːtəˌmɪnɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / ɡluːtəˌmɪnɪk ˈæsɪd / (BrE)
an amino acid found in proteins, important for nitrogen metabolism in plants and used in monosodium glutamate to enhance food flavor
an amino acid important in metabolism and food flavoring
medical

Examples

Glutaminic acid plays a key role in the nitrogen metabolism of many plants.
Monosodium glutamate, derived from glutaminic acid, is commonly used as a flavor enhancer in Asian cuisine.
Scientists studied the presence of glutaminic acid in different protein sources.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glutamine - a closely related amino acid

Etymology

From 'glutamine', a related amino acid, and 'acid' (from Latin acidus, sour).
