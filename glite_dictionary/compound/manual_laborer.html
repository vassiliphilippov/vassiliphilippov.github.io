manual laborer

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæn.ju.əl ˈleɪ.bɚ.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈmæn.ju.əl ˈleɪ.bər.ə / (BrE)
a person whose work requires physical effort, especially work done with the hands rather than by using machines or intellectual skill
person doing physical work

Examples

The company hired several manual laborers to help construct the new building.
As a manual laborer, he spent most of his day lifting heavy materials.
Many manual laborers work outdoors in difficult conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: laborer - a person doing unskilled physical work

Etymology

From 'manual' (from Latin manus 'hand') and 'laborer' (from Latin labor 'work').
