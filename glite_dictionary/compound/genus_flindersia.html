genus Flindersia

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs flɪnˈdɜːr.si.ə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs flɪnˈdɪə.si.ə / (BrE)
A small genus of timber trees native to Australia, belonging to the family Rutaceae.
Australian genus of timber trees

Examples

The genus Flindersia is well known for producing valuable hardwood timber in Australia.
Several species within the genus Flindersia are cultivated for their ornamental foliage.
The botanist described a new species belonging to the genus Flindersia in his recent paper.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Flindersia - A genus of trees in the family Rutaceae

Etymology

Named after English navigator Matthew Flinders.
