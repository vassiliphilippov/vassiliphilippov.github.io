genus Pholistoma

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs foʊˈlɪs.toʊ.mə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs fəˈlɪs.tə.mə / (BrE)
A genus of straggling herbs native to the southwestern United States.
genus of straggling herbs (Southwest U.S.)

Examples

The genus Pholistoma includes several wildflower species found in the deserts of California.
Botanists often study the genus Pholistoma for its adaptation to arid environments.
Many hikers enjoy spotting members of the genus Pholistoma during the spring bloom.

Etymology

From Greek 'pholis' (scale) and possibly 'stoma' (mouth), used as a botanical Latin genus name.
