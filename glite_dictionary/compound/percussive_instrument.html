percussive instrument

1. NOUN

/ pərˈkʌsɪv ˈɪnstrəmənt / (AmE), / pəˈkʌsɪv ˈɪnstrʊmənt / (BrE)
a musical instrument that produces sound by being struck, shaken, or scraped
musical instrument played by striking

Examples

The drum is a classic example of a percussive instrument.
Many orchestras require a variety of percussive instruments for their performances.
She is learning to play several percussive instruments, including the xylophone and tambourine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: percussion instrument - musical instrument played by striking

Etymology

From percussion, from Latin 'percussio' (a striking), plus instrument.
