cat and mouse

1. NOUN

/ kæt ənd maʊs / (AmE), / kæt ənd maʊs / (BrE)
a children's game in which players form a circle and hold hands, allowing one player inside while another tries to catch them by getting past the circle
a children's chasing game involving a circle of players

Examples

The children played cat and mouse in the schoolyard, laughing and shouting as they ran.
When the rain started, their game of cat and mouse had to move indoors.
She explained the rules of cat and mouse to the new students before joining the circle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tag - a children's chasing game where one player chases others
