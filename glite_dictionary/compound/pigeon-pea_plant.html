pigeon-pea plant

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪdʒɪn pi ˌplænt / (AmE), / ˈpɪdʒɪn pi ˌplɑːnt / (BrE)
A tropical woody shrub (Cajanus cajan) with yellow flowers and flat pods, widely grown in warm regions for its edible seeds.
tropical plant grown for edible seeds

Examples

Farmers in India often cultivate pigeon-pea plant to improve soil fertility and provide a protein-rich crop.
The pigeon-pea plant produces vibrant yellow flowers before its pods develop.
Drought resistance makes the pigeon-pea plant valuable in regions with challenging growing conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pigeon pea - the edible seed of the pigeon-pea plant
