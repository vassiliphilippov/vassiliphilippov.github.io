progressive emphysematous necrosis

1. NOUN

/ prəˈɡrɛsɪv ˌɛm.fəˌziː.mə.təs nɪˈkroʊ.sɪs / (AmE), / prəˈɡrɛsɪv ˌɛm.fɪˈziː.mə.təs nɪˈkrəʊ.sɪs / (BrE)
A rapidly advancing and usually fatal form of gangrene, often caused by clostridium bacteria, in which toxins cause tissue death and gas formation in tissues.
severe gangrene with gas formation in tissues
medical

Examples

The surgeon diagnosed the patient with progressive emphysematous necrosis after noticing gas bubbles in the infected tissue.
Prompt treatment is critical because progressive emphysematous necrosis can rapidly lead to death if left untreated.
In rare cases, progressive emphysematous necrosis has been used as a bioweapon due to its severity and rapid progression.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gangrene - death of body tissue due to loss of blood supply or infection

Etymology

From 'progressive' (advancing), 'emphysematous' (containing gas or air), and 'necrosis' (death of tissue, from Greek 'nekrosis').
