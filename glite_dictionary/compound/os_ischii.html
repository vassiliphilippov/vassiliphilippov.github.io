os ischii

1. NOUN

/ ɑs ˈɪskiˌaɪ / (AmE), / ɒs ˈɪskiːˌʌɪ / (BrE)
the lower and back part of the hip bone; one of the three sections that make up the pelvic bone, located below the ilium.
lower, back section of hip bone
medical

Examples

A fracture of the os ischii can cause severe pain in the lower hip region.
The surgeon explained that the os ischii is an important landmark during hip replacement procedures.
Damage to the os ischii may affect mobility and sitting comfort.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ischium - the curved bone forming the base of each half of the pelvis

Etymology

From Latin os (bone) + ischii, genitive of ischium (hip joint).
