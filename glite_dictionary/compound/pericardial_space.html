pericardial space

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛrɪˈkɑrdiəl speɪs / (AmE), / ˌpɛrɪˈkɑːdiəl speɪs / (BrE)
the space between the layers of the pericardium that contains fluid, enabling smooth movement of the heart
space between pericardium layers
medical

Examples

Excess fluid in the pericardial space can compress the heart and impair its function.
Doctors examined the pericardial space for signs of inflammation after the surgery.
Ultrasound imaging can reveal abnormalities in the pericardial space.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pericardium - membrane enclosing the heart

Etymology

From Greek 'peri-' (around) + 'kardia' (heart), referring to the membrane surrounding the heart.
