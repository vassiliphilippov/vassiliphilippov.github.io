number crunching

1. NOUN

/ ˈnʌm.bɚ ˌkrʌn.tʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈnʌm.bə ˌkrʌn.tʃɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of performing complex and lengthy numerical calculations, often with the aid of computers
performing complex numerical calculations

Examples

The scientist spent all night number crunching to analyze the experiment's results.
Modern finance relies heavily on number crunching to forecast market trends.
This software takes care of all the number crunching so users can focus on making decisions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calculation - the process of using mathematics; computation

Etymology

From 'number' + 'crunching,' with 'crunch' used informally to mean processing or dealing with data, first attested in computing contexts in the late 20th century.
