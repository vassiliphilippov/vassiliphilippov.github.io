repeating firearm

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈpitɪŋ ˈfaɪɚˌɑrm / (AmE), / rɪˈpiːtɪŋ ˈfaɪəˌɑːm / (BrE)
a firearm capable of firing several rounds successively without the need to reload after each shot
firearm that fires multiple rounds without reloading

Examples

The rifle collection included an antique repeating firearm from the late nineteenth century.
Modern hunters often prefer a repeating firearm for its reliability and convenience.
Unlike single-shot models, a repeating firearm can discharge multiple rounds before reloading is necessary.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: single-shot firearm - a firearm that must be reloaded after firing each round
