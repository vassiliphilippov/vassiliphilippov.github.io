double-magnum

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʌb.əl ˈmæɡ.nəm / (AmE), / ˌdʌb.əl ˈmæɡ.nəm / (BrE)
a large bottle, especially used for wine or champagne, holding about 3 liters (equivalent to two magnums or roughly 4/5 of a gallon)
large wine bottle (about 3 liters)

Examples

The restaurant celebrated its anniversary with a double-magnum of vintage champagne.
He purchased a double-magnum for the New Year's Eve party to ensure everyone had enough to drink.
Collectors often seek out rare wines sold in a double-magnum for special occasions.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: magnum - a large wine bottle, typically holding 1.5 liters

Etymology

From 'double' (twice) and 'magnum' (a large wine bottle), from Latin 'magnum' (great).
