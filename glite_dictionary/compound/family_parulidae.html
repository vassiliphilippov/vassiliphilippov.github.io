family Parulidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli pəˈruːlɪˌdi / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli pəˈruːlɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of small, often brightly colored songbirds known as the New World warblers, native chiefly to the Americas.
taxonomic family of New World warblers

Examples

Many birdwatchers hope to spot members of the family Parulidae during spring migration.
The family Parulidae includes brightly colored species like the yellow warbler and the black-and-white warbler.
Scientists have studied the evolutionary relationships within the family Parulidae for decades.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: warbler - a small songbird, typically with a melodious song

Etymology

From scientific Latin Parulidae, based on the type genus Parula.
