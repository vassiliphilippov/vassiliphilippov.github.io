tenor drum

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛnɚ drʌm / (AmE), / ˈtɛnə drʌm / (BrE)
A large drum with a small head, used in orchestras or marching bands to produce a middle-range tone.
mid-range drum with small head

Examples

The marching band added a tenor drum to create a richer rhythm section.
She was assigned to play the tenor drum during the school concert.
The tenor drum provides depth and power in orchestral performances.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snare drum - a drum with snares that vibrate to produce a sharp sound
