mahogany tree

1. NOUN

/ məˈhɑɡəni triː / (AmE), / məˈhɒɡəni triː / (BrE)
A tropical tree of the family Meliaceae, especially of the genus Swietenia, valued for its hard, reddish-brown wood used in fine furniture and carpentry.
tropical tree valued for its hard wood

Examples

The mahogany tree in their garden is over a hundred years old.
Many antique tables are made from the wood of the mahogany tree.
Due to overharvesting, the mahogany tree is now protected in some countries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mahogany - the hard, reddish-brown timber from mahogany trees
