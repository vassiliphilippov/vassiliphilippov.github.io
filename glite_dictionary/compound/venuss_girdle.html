Venus's girdle

1. NOUN

/ ˈviːnəsɪz ˈɡɜrdəl / (AmE), / ˈviːnəsɪz ˈɡɜːdl / (BrE)
a ribbon-shaped, iridescent marine ctenophore with a gelatinous body found in warm seas
a ribbon-shaped marine ctenophore

Examples

Scientists observed a Venus's girdle gliding gracefully through the illuminated water near the reef.
The Venus's girdle is known for its shimmering, rainbow-like appearance.
During the dive, we spotted several Venus's girdles drifting among the schools of fish.

Etymology

Named after the Roman goddess Venus and 'girdle' meaning a belt or sash, referencing its shape.
