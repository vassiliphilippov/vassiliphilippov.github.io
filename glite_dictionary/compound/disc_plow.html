disc plow

1. NOUN

/ dɪsk plaʊ / (AmE), / dɪsk plaʊ / (BrE)
an agricultural plow with a series of concave steel discs, used especially to break up hard or dry soil.
agricultural plow with steel discs

Examples

The farmer used a disc plow to prepare the compacted field for planting.
Older models of the disc plow are still valued for their durability on rough terrain.
A disc plow can cut through heavy clay and rocky soil where other plows might fail.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: moldboard plow - a plow with a curved blade that turns over the earth
