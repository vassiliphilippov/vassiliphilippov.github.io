King of England

1. NOUN

/ kɪŋ əv ˈɪŋɡlənd / (AmE), / kɪŋ əv ˈɪŋɡlənd / (BrE)
the male sovereign ruler of England; a monarch who holds the title 'king' of the country of England
male monarch of England

Examples

Henry VIII was a powerful King of England who had six wives.
In 1066, William the Conqueror became the first Norman King of England.
The King of England once controlled vast territories beyond the British Isles.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Queen of England - female sovereign ruler of England
