one dollar bill

1. NOUN

/ wʌn ˈdɑlər bɪl / (AmE), / wʌn ˈdɒlə bɪl / (BrE)
A piece of paper currency with a face value of one dollar, especially as issued by the United States or other dollar-using countries.
banknote worth one dollar

Examples

He handed the cashier a one dollar bill to pay for the soda.
All I had in my wallet was a single one dollar bill and some coins.
The child was excited to find a crisp new one dollar bill in her birthday card.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: banknote - paper money issued by a bank, used as legal tender
  • Antonym: coin - a piece of metal money
