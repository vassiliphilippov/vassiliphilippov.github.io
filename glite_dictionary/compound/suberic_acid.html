suberic acid

1. NOUN

/ suˈberɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / suːˈbɛrɪk ˈæsɪd / (BrE)
a dicarboxylic acid (C8H14O4) found especially in cork and used in organic synthesis and plastics
a dicarboxylic acid found in cork

Examples

Suberic acid is often used as an intermediate in the production of polyamides and polyesters.
The presence of suberic acid in cork contributes to its unique chemical properties.
Researchers analyzed the suberic acid content to study the decomposition of cork material.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dicarboxylic acid - an organic compound containing two carboxyl groups

Etymology

From Latin suber (cork) referring to its presence in cork.
