execution sale

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛksɪˈkjuːʃən seɪl / (AmE), / ˌɛksɪˈkjuːʃən seɪl / (BrE)
a sale of property conducted by a sheriff or other official under the authority of a court order to satisfy an unpaid debt or judgment.
court-ordered sale to satisfy a debt
legal

Examples

The court scheduled an execution sale of the debtor’s assets to satisfy the outstanding judgment.
After losing the lawsuit, his house was sold at an execution sale to pay the creditors.
Buyers at an execution sale can sometimes purchase property below market value.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foreclosure - legal process where a property is sold to recover a debt, often a mortgage
