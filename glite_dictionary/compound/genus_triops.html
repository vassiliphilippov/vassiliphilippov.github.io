genus Triops

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˈtraɪɒps / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈtraɪɒps / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Triopidae, comprising small freshwater crustaceans with a small third median eye.
type genus of Triopidae; small crustaceans

Examples

Scientists studied the behavior of genus Triops in their artificial habitats.
Several species within the genus Triops are known for their ability to survive in temporary pools.
The discovery of a new member of genus Triops excited the research community.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Triops - a genus of small freshwater crustaceans

Etymology

From Latin 'genus' (kind) and Greek 'triops' (three-eyed).
