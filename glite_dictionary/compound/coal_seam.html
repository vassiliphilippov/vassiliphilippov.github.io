coal seam

1. NOUN

/ koʊl siːm / (AmE), / kəʊl siːm / (BrE)
A layer or stratum of coal beneath the earth's surface, usually found between other layers of rock.
layer of coal in the earth

Examples

The geologists discovered a thick coal seam running beneath the valley.
The miners worked long hours extracting coal from the deepest coal seam in the region.
Advancements in technology have made it easier to access remote coal seams underground.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seam - a layer of earth or rock with different composition, especially in mining
