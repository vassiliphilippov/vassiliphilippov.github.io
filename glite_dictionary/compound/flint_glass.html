flint glass

1. NOUN

/ flɪnt ɡlæs / (AmE), / flɪnt ɡlɑːs / (BrE)
a type of optical glass with high dispersion and a high refractive index, used especially in lenses and prisms
type of high-dispersion optical glass

Examples

The lens was made from flint glass to reduce chromatic aberration.
Prisms constructed from flint glass are chosen for their excellent light dispersion properties.
The invention of flint glass revolutionized the field of optical instrument design.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: crown glass - a type of optical glass with low dispersion and low refractive index

Etymology

Named for its original production using silica from powdered flint stone.
