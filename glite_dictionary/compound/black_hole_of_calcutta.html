Black Hole of Calcutta

1. NOUN

/ blæk hoʊl əv kælˈkʌtə / (AmE), / blæk həʊl əv kælˈkʌtə / (BrE)
a small prison cell in Fort William, Calcutta, where many British prisoners were held overnight in 1756, with most dying from suffocation and heat exhaustion
historic prison cell/event in Calcutta, 1756

Examples

The soldiers were forced into the Black Hole of Calcutta, where most did not survive the night.
Many historians dispute the exact number of victims in the Black Hole of Calcutta incident.
The tragedy of the Black Hole of Calcutta remains a symbol of colonial brutality.

Etymology

Named after the event in 1756 at Fort William, Calcutta (now Kolkata), India.
