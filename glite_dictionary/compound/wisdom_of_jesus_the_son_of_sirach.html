Wisdom of Jesus the Son of Sirach

1. PROPN

/ ˈwɪzdəm əv ˈdʒiːzəs ði ˈsʌn əv sɪˈræk / (AmE), / ˈwɪzdəm əv ˈdʒiːzəs ði ˈsʌn əv sɪˈræk / (BrE)
an Apocryphal book consisting mainly of maxims and moral teachings, similar in style to Proverbs, also known as Ecclesiasticus
Apocryphal book of maxims and wisdom (Ecclesiasticus)

Examples

Many scholars refer to the Wisdom of Jesus the Son of Sirach as one of the most important deuterocanonical books.
The moral guidance found in the Wisdom of Jesus the Son of Sirach influenced later Christian ethics.
He quoted a proverb from the Wisdom of Jesus the Son of Sirach in his sermon on humility.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ecclesiasticus - alternative name for the Wisdom of Jesus the Son of Sirach
  • Synonym: Proverbs - a book of the Old Testament consisting of wise sayings and teachings

Etymology

From the Greek translation of the Hebrew book titled after its presumed author, Jesus ben Sirach.
