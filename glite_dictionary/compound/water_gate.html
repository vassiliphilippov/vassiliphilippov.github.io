water gate

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔt̬ɚ ˌɡeɪt / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə ˌɡeɪt / (BrE)
A barrier, gate, or valve used to control the flow of water, especially in a sluice or channel.
device for controlling water flow

Examples

The engineer adjusted the water gate to increase the flow of water into the canal.
During heavy rains, the water gate must be opened carefully to prevent flooding.
Routine inspections ensure that each water gate functions efficiently in the dam system.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sluice gate - a gate that regulates the flow of water in a sluice

Etymology

From Middle English 'water' + 'gate', meaning a gate for controlling water.
