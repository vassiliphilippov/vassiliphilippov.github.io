Wormian bone

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɜrmiən boʊn / (AmE), / ˈwɜːmɪən bəʊn / (BrE)
any of the small, irregular bones sometimes found within the sutures of the skull, especially in the human cranium
small bones in skull sutures
medical

Examples

The presence of Wormian bones can be an indicator of certain medical conditions.
During the autopsy, several Wormian bones were noted along the lambdoid suture.
Radiologists sometimes identify Wormian bones on skull X-rays.

Etymology

Named after Danish anatomist Ole Worm (1588–1654).
