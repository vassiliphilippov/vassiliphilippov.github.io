slacktivist movement

1. NOUN

/ ˈslæk.tɪ.vɪst ˈmuːv.mənt / (AmE), / ˈslæk.tɪ.vɪst ˈmuːv.mənt / (BrE)
A form of activism characterized by minimal effort, often limited to online actions such as sharing posts or signing petitions.
minimal-effort activism, often online

Examples

The slacktivist movement gained momentum as more people supported causes online without significant commitment.
Many critics argue that the slacktivist movement leads to awareness without real change.
After the campaign went viral, the slacktivist movement turned public attention to the issue.

Synset Examples

The slacktivist movement has grown with social media.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: activism - the policy or action of using vigorous campaigning to bring about political or social change
  • Synonym: slacktivism - actions performed via the internet in support of a social or political cause requiring little time or involvement

Etymology

Formed from 'slacktivist' (blend of 'slacker' and 'activist') and 'movement'.
