House of Lancaster

1. PROPN

/ haʊs əv ˈlæŋkəstər / (AmE), / haʊs əv ˈlɑːŋkəstə / (BrE)
the English royal house that ruled England from 1399 to 1461, symbolized by a red rose
English royal dynasty ruling 1399–1461

Examples

The House of Lancaster played a central role in the Wars of the Roses.
King Henry IV was the first monarch from the House of Lancaster.
The emblem of the House of Lancaster was a vibrant red rose.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: House of York - the English royal house that opposed the House of Lancaster during the Wars of the Roses

Etymology

From the city of Lancaster, England, seat of the family's power.
