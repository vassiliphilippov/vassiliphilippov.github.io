Epistle to the Hebrews

1. PROPN

/ ɪˈpɪsəl tə ðə ˈhiˌbroʊz / (AmE), / ɪˈpɪsəl tə ðə ˈhiːbruːz / (BrE)
a book of the New Testament, traditionally considered one of the epistles of Saint Paul but now generally thought not to have been written by him
New Testament book, traditionally Pauline epistle

Examples

He spent several months studying the Epistle to the Hebrews for his theology course.
Scholars often debate the true authorship of the Epistle to the Hebrews.
The Epistle to the Hebrews contains many references to Jewish traditions and practices.

Etymology

From Greek 'epistolē' (letter) and 'Hebraioi' (Hebrews).
