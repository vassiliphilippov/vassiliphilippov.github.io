rheumatic heart disease

1. NOUN

/ ruˈmætɪk hɑrt dɪˌziz / (AmE), / ruːˈmatɪk hɑːt dɪˌziːz / (BrE)
a chronic heart condition resulting from repeated episodes of rheumatic fever, marked by damage to the heart valves or muscle
chronic heart damage from rheumatic fever
medical

Examples

The patient developed rheumatic heart disease after suffering from repeated bouts of rheumatic fever in childhood.
Doctors monitor patients with rheumatic heart disease for signs of worsening valve damage.
Effective treatment and early diagnosis can help prevent serious complications of rheumatic heart disease.

Etymology

From 'rheumatic', relating to or affected by rheumatism, and 'heart disease'.
