bourguignon sauce

1. NOUN

/ bʊr.ɡiˈnjɑn sɔs / (AmE), / bʊə.ɡiˈnjɒ̃ sɔːs / (BrE)
A sauce made with red wine, onions, parsley, thyme, and butter, typically used in French cuisine.
French sauce of red wine, onions, and herbs

Examples

The beef was simmered in a rich bourguignon sauce that was bursting with flavor.
She learned to make bourguignon sauce in her French cooking class last semester.
Pour the bourguignon sauce over the mushrooms and garnish with fresh thyme.

Etymology

From French 'bourguignon', meaning 'in the Burgundy style', after the Burgundy region of France.
