creeping willow

1. NOUN

/ ˈkripɪŋ ˈwɪloʊ / (AmE), / ˈkriːpɪŋ ˈwɪləʊ / (BrE)
A small, trailing shrub native to Europe and Asia, with straggling branches and silky green leaves, often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
small trailing shrub with silky green leaves

Examples

The creeping willow thrives in moist soils and makes an attractive ground cover.
Gardeners often choose the creeping willow for its graceful, spreading branches.
Several varieties of creeping willow are popular in landscape design for their ornamental foliage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: willow - a tree or shrub of the genus Salix, typically with slender branches and narrow leaves
