reciprocal-inhibition therapy

1. NOUN

/ rɪˌsɪprəkəl ɪnˈhɪbɪʃən ˈθɛrəpi / (AmE), / rɪˌsɪprəkəl ɪnˈhɪbɪʃən ˈθɛrəpi / (BrE)
a method of behavior therapy in which one response is inhibited by the occurrence of another mutually incompatible response, such as using relaxation techniques to counteract anxiety
behavior therapy method using incompatible responses
medical

Examples

His therapist used reciprocal-inhibition therapy to help him manage his fear of flying.
Reciprocal-inhibition therapy often involves training patients to relax when exposed to anxiety-provoking situations.
Many psychologists incorporate reciprocal-inhibition therapy into their treatment plans for clients with phobias.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: systematic desensitization - a behavioral therapy technique involving gradual exposure to feared stimuli while remaining relaxed
