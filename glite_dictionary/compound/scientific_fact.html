scientific fact

1. NOUN

/ ˌsaɪənˈtɪfɪk ˈfækt / (AmE), / ˌsaɪənˈtɪfɪk ˈfækt / (BrE)
an observation or statement that has been repeatedly confirmed through experiment or observation and is widely accepted as true, though not considered absolutely final
widely accepted confirmed scientific observation

Examples

The idea that the Earth orbits the Sun is a scientific fact supported by centuries of evidence.
Climate change is not just a theory but a well-established scientific fact.
Repeated experiments have turned this hypothesis into an accepted scientific fact.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: theory - a well-substantiated explanation acquired through the scientific method
