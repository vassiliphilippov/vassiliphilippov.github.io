common-law marriage

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑː.mən lɔː ˈmær.ɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌkɒm.ən lɔː ˈmær.ɪdʒ / (BrE)
A marriage recognized in some jurisdictions based on a couple's agreement and cohabitation, rather than through a formal ceremony or license.
marriage by mutual agreement and cohabitation
legal

Examples

Many states in the U.S. do not recognize common-law marriage as legally binding.
After living together for several years, they assumed they had entered a common-law marriage.
Some couples choose a common-law marriage instead of a traditional wedding ceremony.
