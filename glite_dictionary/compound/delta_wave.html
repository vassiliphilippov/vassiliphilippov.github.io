delta wave

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛl.tə weɪv / (AmE), / ˈdɛl.tə weɪv / (BrE)
a type of brainwave with high amplitude and low frequency (1–4 Hz), typically occurring during deep, dreamless sleep
slow brainwave during deep sleep
medical

Examples

During deep sleep, the brain produces large delta waves that can be detected on an EEG.
The presence of delta waves indicates a person is in the deepest stage of non-REM sleep.
Researchers monitored delta wave activity to assess sleep quality in patients.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: theta wave - brainwave of slightly higher frequency than delta, associated with light sleep

Etymology

From the Greek letter 'delta' (Δ), used to designate the wave's frequency range.
