proton magnetic resonance

1. NOUN

/ ˈproʊ.tɑn ˈmæg.nɪtɪk rɪˈzɒ.nəns / (AmE), / ˈprəʊ.tɒn ˈmæɡ.nɪtɪk rɪˈzɒ.nəns / (BrE)
a phenomenon in which protons in a magnetic field absorb and re-emit electromagnetic radiation at characteristic frequencies, widely used in structural analysis and medical imaging
resonance of protons in a magnetic field
medical

Examples

Proton magnetic resonance is a critical technique in modern medical imaging, particularly in MRI scans.
Researchers use proton magnetic resonance to determine the structure of complex organic molecules.
Advances in proton magnetic resonance have improved the resolution of molecular imaging in recent years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nuclear magnetic resonance - resonance phenomenon in atomic nuclei in a magnetic field

Etymology

Formed from 'proton', 'magnetic', and 'resonance', used in scientific literature since the 1950s.
