long time no hear

1. X

/ lɔŋ taɪm noʊ hɪr / (AmE), / lɒŋ taɪm nəʊ hɪə / (BrE)
An informal expression used to indicate that one has not heard from or communicated with someone for a long period.
expression meaning haven't communicated in a while
informal

Examples

Long time no hear! I was just thinking about you yesterday.
Sorry for the silence—it's been a long time no hear from my end.
Whenever I get an email from her, it's always after a long time no hear.

Synset Examples

I thought it was a long time no hear from him.
It's been a long time no hear from you!
Long time no hear! How have you been?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: long time no see - expression meaning it has been a while since seeing someone

Etymology

Modeled after 'long time no see,' which originated as a pidgin English expression in early 20th-century United States, likely influenced by Chinese syntax.
