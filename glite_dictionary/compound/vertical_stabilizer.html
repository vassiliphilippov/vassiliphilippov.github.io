vertical stabilizer

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɝː.tɪ.kəl ˈsteɪ.bə.laɪ.zɚ / (AmE), / ˈvɜː.tɪ.kəl ˈsteɪ.bɪ.laɪ.zə / (BrE)
the upright part of an airplane's tail that helps to keep the aircraft stable and maintain its direction during flight
upright tail fin of an airplane

Examples

The vertical stabilizer helps prevent the airplane from unwanted yawing movements during flight.
During the storm, the vertical stabilizer was damaged, affecting the plane's ability to maintain a straight path.
Technicians inspected the vertical stabilizer for any cracks or structural weaknesses after the hard landing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rudder - a movable control surface attached to the vertical stabilizer, used to steer an aircraft
