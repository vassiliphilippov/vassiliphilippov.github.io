Burgundy wine

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɝːɡəndi waɪn / (AmE), / ˈbɜːɡəndi waɪn / (BrE)
a red wine originally produced in the Burgundy region of France, or any similar wine made elsewhere
red wine from Burgundy, France

Examples

He selected a fine bottle of Burgundy wine to accompany the dinner.
Many collectors prize vintage Burgundy wine for its complex flavor.
Although produced in California, this Burgundy wine rivals its French counterparts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Claret - dry red wine, especially from Bordeaux

Etymology

Named after the Burgundy region in France, where the wine originated.
