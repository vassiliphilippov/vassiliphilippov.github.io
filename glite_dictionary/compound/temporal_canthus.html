temporal canthus

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛmpərəl ˈkænθəs / (AmE), / ˈtɛmpərəl ˈkanθəs / (BrE)
the outer corner of the eye, where the upper and lower eyelids meet on the side nearest the temple
the outer corner of the eye
medical

Examples

The doctor examined the temporal canthus for signs of irritation.
A small mole had developed near her temporal canthus.
Swelling of the temporal canthus can indicate an allergic reaction.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: medial canthus - the inner corner of the eye, near the nose

Etymology

From Latin 'temporalis' (of the temple) and Greek 'kanthos' (corner of the eye).
