Old Hickory

1. PROPN

/ oʊld ˈhɪkəri / (AmE), / əʊld ˈhɪkəri / (BrE)
A nickname for Andrew Jackson, the 7th president of the United States, known for his toughness and populist leadership.
nickname of Andrew Jackson, 7th U.S. president

Examples

Many Americans remember Old Hickory for his victory at the Battle of New Orleans.
The policies of Old Hickory continue to be debated by historians today.
Locals proudly claim that Old Hickory once visited their small town.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Andrew Jackson - 7th president of the United States (1767–1845)

Etymology

Nicknamed 'Old Hickory' due to Jackson's reputed toughness, likened to hickory wood.
