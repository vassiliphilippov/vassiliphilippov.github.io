Chief Secretary

1. NOUN

/ tʃiːf ˈsɛkrəˌtɛri / (AmE), / tʃiːf ˈsɛkrətri / (BrE)
an important government official, often a member of the British Cabinet, responsible for overseeing a particular department or administrative area
senior British Cabinet official
british

Examples

The Chief Secretary addressed Parliament on the proposed economic reforms.
After serving as Chief Secretary, she was promoted to a higher post within the Cabinet.
It is the responsibility of the Chief Secretary to coordinate various government departments.
