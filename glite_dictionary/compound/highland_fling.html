highland fling

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪ.lənd flɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhaɪ.lənd flɪŋ / (BrE)
a lively Scottish dance characterized by rapid steps and kicks, traditionally performed solo to a reel
a lively solo Scottish dance

Examples

She performed the highland fling at the annual Scottish festival.
Learning the highland fling takes practice and lots of energy.
The dancers took to the stage in traditional attire for a spirited highland fling.

Etymology

From 'Highland', referring to the Scottish Highlands, and 'fling', meaning a lively dance or movement.
