wild quinine

1. NOUN

/ waɪld kwaɪˈniːn / (AmE), / waɪld kwɪˈniːn / (BrE)
a stout perennial herb (Parthenium integrifolium) native to the eastern United States, with whitish flowers and leaves traditionally used in herbal remedies
a North American medicinal herb

Examples

Native Americans used wild quinine leaves to treat burns and other skin ailments.
In her garden, she planted wild quinine alongside other native wildflowers.
The whitish flowers of wild quinine bloom from early summer to fall.

Etymology

Named for its perceived medicinal properties, recalling the South American quinine tree; not botanically related to true quinine.
