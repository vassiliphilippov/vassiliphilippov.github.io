Battle of Hastings

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæt̬.əl əv ˈheɪ.stɪŋz / (AmE), / ˈbæt.əl əv ˈheɪ.stɪŋz / (BrE)
the battle in 1066 in which William the Conqueror defeated King Harold II, leading to the Norman Conquest of England
decisive 1066 battle, Norman Conquest

Examples

The Battle of Hastings in 1066 changed the course of English history forever.
Historians often debate the tactics used during the Battle of Hastings.
Schoolchildren in England learn about the significance of the Battle of Hastings.

Etymology

Named after the town of Hastings in southeast England, near where the battle took place.
