Massachuset language

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæsəˈtʃuːsɪt ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌmasəˈtʃuːsɪt ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
an extinct Algonquian language once spoken by the Massachuset people of eastern Massachusetts
extinct Algonquian language of the Massachuset people

Examples

The Massachuset language was once spoken along the coast of present-day Massachusetts.
Early missionaries created written records in the Massachuset language to communicate religious teachings.
Much of what is known about the Massachuset language comes from colonial-era documents and translations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Algonquian - a family of Native American languages

Etymology

Massachuset from the name of the indigenous Massachuset people; language from Latin 'lingua'.
