Mae West

1. NOUN

/ meɪ wɛst / (AmE), / meɪ wɛst / (BrE)
a type of inflatable life jacket, especially one used by aircrew during World War II, so called because of its resemblance to the actress's figure
inflatable life jacket (WWII era)

Examples

The pilot was issued a Mae West before every mission over the Channel.
Wearing his Mae West, the airman waited for rescue after ditching the plane.
A vintage Mae West is now on display at the aviation museum.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: life jacket - a personal flotation device worn to keep a person afloat in water

Etymology

Named after the American actress Mae West (1893–1980), referencing her voluptuous figure.
