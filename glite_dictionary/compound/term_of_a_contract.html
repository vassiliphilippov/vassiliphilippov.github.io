term of a contract

1. NOUN

/ tɝm əv ə ˈkɑnˌtrækt / (AmE), / tɜːm əv ə ˈkɒn.trækt / (BrE)
the fixed period during which a contract remains legally effective
duration a contract is valid
legal

Examples

The term of a contract specifies how long the agreement will remain in force.
Before signing, she checked the term of a contract to ensure it met her needs.
If the term of a contract is not clearly stated, disputes may arise over its duration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: duration - the length of time something lasts
  • Antonym: expiry - the end of a contract or validity period
