tax avoidance

1. NOUN

/ tæks əˈvɔɪdəns / (AmE), / tæks əˈvɔɪdəns / (BrE)
The minimization of tax liability by using lawful methods to reduce the amount of tax owed.
minimizing tax legally

Examples

Some corporations use tax avoidance strategies to reduce their overall tax bills.
There is a fine line between tax avoidance and tax evasion.
Governments often introduce new laws to curb aggressive tax avoidance schemes.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: tax evasion - illegal act of not paying taxes owed
