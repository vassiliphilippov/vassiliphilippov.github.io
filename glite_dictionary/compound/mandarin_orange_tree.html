mandarin orange tree

1. NOUN

/ ˈmændərɪn ˈɔːrɪndʒ triː / (AmE), / ˈmændərɪn ˈɒrɪndʒ triː / (BrE)
a small tree or shrub native to southeastern Asia that produces mandarin oranges, known for their sweet, aromatic pulp and loose, thin orange rind
tree bearing mandarin oranges

Examples

A mandarin orange tree can thrive in warm, subtropical climates with plenty of sunlight.
We planted a mandarin orange tree in our backyard, and it's already producing fruit.
The sweet aroma of ripe fruit filled the air around the mandarin orange tree during harvest season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: citrus - a genus of flowering trees and shrubs, including oranges and lemons
