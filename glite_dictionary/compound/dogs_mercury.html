dog's mercury

1. NOUN

/ dɔɡz ˈmɜrkjəri / (AmE), / dɒɡz ˈmɜːkjuri / (BrE)
A European perennial, weedy plant (Mercurialis perennis) with greenish flowers, commonly found in woodland areas.
European perennial weedy plant

Examples

Dog's mercury is commonly found carpeting the floor of ancient European woodlands.
Although attractive, dog's mercury is highly toxic to livestock and should not be consumed.
In early spring, the subtle greenish flowers of dog's mercury appear before many other plants.

Etymology

From its traditional use as an alternative to the true mercury plant; called 'dog's' as a pejorative, indicating it is less valued.
