sky fairy

1. NOUN

/ skaɪ ˈfɛri / (AmE), / skaɪ ˈfeəri / (BrE)
a mythical figure said to bring good fortune, or a whimsical term for a cheerful and beneficent person
mythical figure of good fortune; cheerful person (whimsical)

Examples

The little girl whispered a wish to the sky fairy before bed each night.
After organizing the party, her friends called her their own sky fairy.
In the storybook, the sky fairy painted rainbows in the sky.

Synset Examples

She felt like the Sky Fairy when she made everyone smile.
The children believed the sky fairy granted their wishes.
The Sky Fairy is said to bless those who believe.
The children believed the Sky Fairy brought them gifts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fairy - a small imaginary being with magical powers, often depicted as delicate and pretty
