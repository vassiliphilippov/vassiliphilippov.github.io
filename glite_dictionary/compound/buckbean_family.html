buckbean family

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌkˌbiːn ˈfæməli / (AmE), / ˈbʌkˌbiːn ˈfæmɪli / (BrE)
A family of marsh plants in the order Gentianales, known for species with dicotyledonous flowers, including the buckbean (Menyanthes trifoliata).
family of marsh plants in Gentianales

Examples

The buckbean family includes a variety of marsh plants found in temperate regions.
Members of the buckbean family thrive in wetlands and are recognized by their distinctive flowers.
Botanists study the buckbean family to understand plant adaptation to aquatic environments.
