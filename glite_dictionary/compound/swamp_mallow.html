swamp mallow

1. NOUN

/ swɑmp ˈmæloʊ / (AmE), / swɒmp ˈmæləʊ / (BrE)
a showy shrub native to salt marshes of the eastern United States, with large rose-colored flowers
shrub with large rose-colored flowers

Examples

The swamp mallow blooms in late summer, adding color to the marshlands.
Gardeners often cultivate swamp mallow for its striking, rose-colored blossoms.
Wildlife is attracted to the nectar-rich flowers of the swamp mallow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hibiscus - a genus of flowering plants, including swamp mallow
