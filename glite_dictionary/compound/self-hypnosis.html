self-hypnosis

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛlf hɪpˈnoʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌself hɪpˈnəʊsɪs / (BrE)
A state of hypnosis that a person induces in themselves, often for relaxation or therapeutic purposes.
hypnosis induced by oneself

Examples

Many people use self-hypnosis to manage stress and improve their sleep.
She mastered self-hypnosis as a way to overcome her fear of flying.
Learning self-hypnosis helped him reduce chronic pain without medication.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hypnosis - a trance-like state of focused attention and heightened suggestibility

Etymology

From 'self-' meaning 'by oneself' + 'hypnosis', from Greek 'hypnos' (sleep).
