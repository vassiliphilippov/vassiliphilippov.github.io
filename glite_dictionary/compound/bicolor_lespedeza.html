bicolor lespedeza

1. NOUN

/ ˈbaɪkələr lɛsˈpidziə / (AmE), / ˈbaɪkələ lɛsˈpiːdziə / (BrE)
An Asian shrub with racemose rose-purple flowers, often grown as an ornamental plant, for erosion control, and to provide feed for wild birds.
Asian shrub used for erosion control and bird feed

Examples

The conservation team planted bicolor lespedeza to stabilize the eroding hillside.
Many landowners choose bicolor lespedeza because it attracts a variety of wild birds.
The bright rose-purple flowers of bicolor lespedeza add color to gardens in late summer.

Etymology

From Latin 'bi-' (two) + 'color' (color) and the genus name 'Lespedeza', named after Vicente Manuel de Céspedes, a Spanish governor of East Florida.
