order Anaspida

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdər əˈnæspɪdə / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə əˈnaspɪdə / (BrE)
an extinct order of jawless, fish-like vertebrates from the Silurian and Devonian periods
extinct order of jawless vertebrates

Examples

Fossils of the order Anaspida give us clues about the early evolution of vertebrates.
Researchers discovered new specimens attributed to the order Anaspida in northern Europe.
The order Anaspida is known for its streamlined body and absence of jaws.

Etymology

From New Latin; 'Anaspida' from Greek 'an-' (without) + 'aspis' (shield), referring to the lack of heavy armor.
