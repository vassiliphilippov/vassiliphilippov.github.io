coordinate geometry

1. NOUN

/ koʊˈɔːrdɪnət dʒiˈɑːmətri / (AmE), / kəʊˈɔːdɪnət dʒiˈɒmɪtri / (BrE)
the branch of geometry in which algebraic methods are used to study geometric problems by representing points with coordinates
geometry using coordinates and algebra

Examples

Many problems in physics are easier to solve using coordinate geometry rather than classical geometric methods.
In high school, we learned how to find the equation of a circle in coordinate geometry.
Coordinate geometry allows one to analyze the properties of lines and curves algebraically.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: analytic geometry - the branch of mathematics using algebraic equations to represent geometric shapes
  • Antonym: synthetic geometry - geometry based on axioms and logical deduction rather than coordinates

Etymology

From 'coordinate' (from Latin 'coordinatus', arranged) and 'geometry' (from Greek 'geometria', measurement of the earth).
