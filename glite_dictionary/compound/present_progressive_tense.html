present progressive tense

1. NOUN

/ ˈprezənt prəˈɡrɛsɪv tɛns / (AmE), / ˈprezənt prəˈɡrɛsɪv tɛns / (BrE)
a verb tense used to express an action that is ongoing at the time of speaking
verb tense for ongoing actions

Examples

She is reading a book right now, which is an example of the present progressive tense.
In English, the present progressive tense is often formed with 'am', 'is', or 'are' plus a verb ending in -ing.
'They are eating dinner' uses the present progressive tense to show the action is happening now.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: present continuous tense - another term for present progressive tense
  • Degree Lower: past progressive tense - a verb tense expressing ongoing action in the past
  • Degree Higher: future progressive tense - a verb tense expressing ongoing action in the future

Etymology

Formed from 'present' (from Latin praesent-) + 'progressive' (from Latin progressivus) + 'tense' (from Latin tempus, time).
