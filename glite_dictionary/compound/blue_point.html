blue point

1. NOUN

/ bluː pɔɪnt / (AmE), / bluː pɔɪnt / (BrE)
a small, edible oyster typically from the southern shore of Long Island, prized for its flavor
small oyster from Long Island

Examples

The restaurant serves fresh blue point oysters on the half shell every evening.
She ordered a dozen blue point oysters as an appetizer at the seafood bar.
Many consider blue point oysters to be among the finest in the United States.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oyster - a bivalve mollusc commonly eaten as seafood

Etymology

Named after Blue Point, a hamlet on Long Island, New York, where these oysters were originally harvested.

2. NOUN

/ bluː pɔɪnt / (AmE), / bluː pɔɪnt / (BrE)
A type of oyster originally from Long Island Sound, now from the northeastern U.S. coast, usually eaten raw.
type of oyster from northeastern U.S.

Examples

The chef served a plate of fresh blue point oysters on ice.
At the seafood festival, I tried delicious blue point oysters for the first time.
Many restaurants feature blue point oysters as a specialty appetizer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oyster - an edible bivalve mollusk with a rough shell
