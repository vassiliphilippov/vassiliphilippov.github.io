brandwash strategy

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrændwɑʃ ˈstrætədʒi / (AmE), / ˈbrændwɒʃ ˈstrætədʒi / (BrE)
a marketing technique aimed at improving public perception of a brand, often by using manipulative or persuasive tactics
marketing tactic to improve brand image

Examples

The company's new brandwash strategy was designed to rebuild trust after the scandal.
Critics argue that their brandwash strategy goes beyond simple advertising into manipulation.
By employing a clever brandwash strategy, the firm quickly changed its negative public image.

Synset Examples

Their brandwash strategy worked wonders for sales.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brandwash - to manipulate consumer perception of a brand

Etymology

Blend of 'brand' and 'brainwash' with 'strategy'; 'brandwash' from 'brainwash', meaning to manipulate people's perceptions.
