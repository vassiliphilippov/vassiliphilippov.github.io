title of respect

1. NOUN

/ ˈtaɪtəl əv rɪˈspɛkt / (AmE), / ˈtaɪtl əv rɪˈspɛkt / (BrE)
an identifying word or phrase, such as 'Mr.' or 'General', used before or after someone's name to indicate their status or function and to show respect
formal prefix or suffix showing respect

Examples

Please address the ambassador by his full title of respect during the ceremony.
In some cultures, using a title of respect when speaking to elders is very important.
She earned the title of respect 'Doctor' after years of hard work.

Synset Examples

the professor didn't like his friends to use his formal title

Related Terms

  • Synonym: honorific - a title or word implying or expressing high status, politeness, or respect
  • Antonym: insult - a disrespectful or scornful expression or remark
