gin mill

1. NOUN

/ dʒɪn mɪl / (AmE), / dʒɪn mɪl / (BrE)
An informal or slang term for a bar or tavern, especially one considered cheap or disreputable.
informal bar or tavern
informal slang american

Examples

After work, he liked to stop by the old gin mill on the corner for a drink.
The novel's main character spends his nights wandering from one gin mill to another.
Many of these gin mills attracted a lively, if sometimes rowdy, crowd in the evenings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bar - an establishment where alcoholic drinks are served
  • Synonym: pub - a public house or tavern serving alcoholic beverages
  • Synonym: speakeasy - an illicit liquor store or nightclub, especially during Prohibition

Etymology

From 'gin' (a type of alcoholic drink) and 'mill' (suggesting a place where something is produced or served in large quantities); slang usage dates to 19th-century America.
