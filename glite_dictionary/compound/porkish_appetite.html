porkish appetite

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɔrkɪʃ ˈæpəˌtaɪt / (AmE), / ˈpɔːkɪʃ ˈapɪtaɪt / (BrE)
A very large or greedy appetite, suggesting gluttony.
large or greedy appetite

Examples

His porkish appetite embarrassed his friends at the all-you-can-eat buffet.
She blamed her porkish appetite on skipping lunch that day.
Despite his slim figure, his porkish appetite amazed everyone at Thanksgiving dinner.

Synset Examples

His porkish appetite was evident at dinner last night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ravenous - extremely hungry; very eager for food
  • Synonym: gluttonous - exhibiting excessive eating or drinking

Etymology

From 'porkish', meaning 'like a pig', derived from 'pork' plus the suffix '-ish', and 'appetite' from Latin 'appetitus' (desire for).
