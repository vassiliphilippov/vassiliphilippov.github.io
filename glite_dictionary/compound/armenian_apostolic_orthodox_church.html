Armenian Apostolic Orthodox Church

1. PROPN

/ ɑrˈminiən əˌpɑstəlɪk ˈɔrθədɑks tʃɜːrtʃ / (AmE), / ɑːˈmiːniən əˌpɒstəlɪk ˈɔːθədɒks tʃɜːtʃ / (BrE)
An independent Christian church established in Armenia around 300 CE, influenced by both Roman and Byzantine traditions.
independent Armenian Christian church

Examples

The Armenian Apostolic Orthodox Church is one of the world's oldest Christian communities.
Many Armenians attend services at the Armenian Apostolic Orthodox Church in their city.
The traditions of the Armenian Apostolic Orthodox Church have been maintained for centuries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Eastern Orthodox Church - One of the main branches of Orthodoxy; distinct from Armenian Apostolic Orthodox Church.
  • Synonym: Roman Catholic Church - The largest Christian church, historically influential upon the Armenian Apostolic Orthodox Church.
