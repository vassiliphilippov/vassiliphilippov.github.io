cystic fibrosis transmembrane conductance regulator

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɪstɪk faɪˈbroʊsɪs trænzˈmɛmˌbreɪn kənˈdʌktəns rɪˈɡjuleɪtər / (AmE), / ˌsɪstɪk faɪˈbrəʊsɪs trænzˈmɛmˌbreɪn kənˈdʌktəns rɪˈɡjʊleɪtə / (BrE)
the gene that encodes a membrane protein responsible for regulating the movement of chloride and sodium ions across epithelial cells; mutations in this gene cause cystic fibrosis
gene mutated in cystic fibrosis
medical

Examples

Mutations in the cystic fibrosis transmembrane conductance regulator are the main cause of cystic fibrosis.
Researchers are studying how the cystic fibrosis transmembrane conductance regulator affects ion transport in lung tissue.
Genetic testing for the cystic fibrosis transmembrane conductance regulator can help diagnose carriers of the disease.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: CFTR - abbreviation for cystic fibrosis transmembrane conductance regulator

Etymology

From 'cystic fibrosis' (the disease) and technical terms: 'transmembrane' (across membrane), 'conductance regulator' (controls ion flow).
