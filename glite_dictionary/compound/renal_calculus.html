renal calculus

1. NOUN

/ ˈriːnəl ˈkælkjələs / (AmE), / ˈriːnəl ˈkalkjʊləs / (BrE)
A hard, stone-like mass that forms in the kidney, usually consisting of mineral salts.
kidney stone; kidney-formed mineral mass
medical

Examples

After days of severe pain, the doctor diagnosed her with a renal calculus.
A renal calculus may block the flow of urine and require surgical intervention.
The patient presented to the emergency room with symptoms typical of a renal calculus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kidney stone - a stone that forms in the kidney

Etymology

From Latin 'renalis' (of the kidney) and 'calculus' (small stone).
