order Primulales

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdər praɪˌmjuːˈleɪliːz / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə praɪˌmjuːˈleɪliːz / (BrE)
a former order of flowering plants, including the families Primulaceae, Theophrastaceae, Myrsinaceae, and sometimes Plumbaginaceae
a former order of flowering plants

Examples

Many early classification systems recognized the order Primulales as a distinct group of flowering plants.
The order Primulales once included several families, such as the Primulaceae and Myrsinaceae.
Recent genetic studies have led to a reorganization of the families within the order Primulales.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Primulaceae - the primrose family of flowering plants

Etymology

From Latin 'Primula' (primrose) + '-ales', a standard suffix for plant orders.
