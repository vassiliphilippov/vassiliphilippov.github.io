Foreign Intelligence Surveillance Court

1. PROPN

/ ˈfɔrən ɪnˈtɛlədʒəns sərˈveɪləns kɔrt / (AmE), / ˈfɒrən ɪnˈtelɪdʒəns səˈveɪləns kɔːt / (BrE)
a secret federal court in the United States, established in 1978, that authorizes electronic surveillance and searches for intelligence purposes
US federal court for intelligence surveillance
legal american

Examples

The Foreign Intelligence Surveillance Court granted approval for the wiretap request.
Information about the Foreign Intelligence Surveillance Court is not easily accessible to the public due to its secret proceedings.
Congress debated the powers of the Foreign Intelligence Surveillance Court after new surveillance laws were proposed.
