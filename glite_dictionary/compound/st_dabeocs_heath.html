St. Dabeoc's heath

1. NOUN

/ sənt dəˈbiːəks hiːθ / (AmE), / sənt dəˈbiːɒks hiːθ / (BrE)
A low, straggling evergreen shrub native to western Europe, with several varieties bearing flowers that range in color from white to rose-purple.
evergreen shrub of western Europe

Examples

The hillside was covered with St. Dabeoc's heath, its rose-purple flowers standing out against the green landscape.
Gardeners often plant St. Dabeoc's heath for its evergreen foliage and colorful blooms.
Several varieties of St. Dabeoc's heath thrive in the acidic soils of western Europe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heath - a wild, open area with low shrubs, or a type of shrub itself

Etymology

Named after St. Dabeoc, an Irish saint, with 'heath' referring to shrubs of the family Ericaceae.
