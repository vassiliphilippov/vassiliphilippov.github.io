apple polisher

1. NOUN

/ ˈæpəl ˌpɑːlɪʃər / (AmE), / ˈæpəl ˌpɒlɪʃə / (BrE)
a person who flatters someone in authority, especially to gain favor or advantage
person who flatters to gain favor
informal american

Examples

He's always been an apple polisher in the office, complimenting the boss at every opportunity.
The teacher was well aware of which students were genuine and which were just apple polishers.
No one likes an apple polisher who only flatters people to get ahead.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sycophant - a person who acts obsequiously toward someone important
  • Synonym: brown-noser - a person who tries to please someone in authority in order to gain advantage
  • Synonym: toady - a person who behaves obsequiously to someone important

Etymology

American English, originally referring to students who gave apples to teachers to win favor.
