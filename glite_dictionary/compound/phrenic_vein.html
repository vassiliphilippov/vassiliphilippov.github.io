phrenic vein

1. NOUN

/ ˈfrɛnɪk veɪn / (AmE), / ˈfrɛnɪk veɪn / (BrE)
either of two veins that drain blood from the diaphragm to larger veins in the body
vein draining the diaphragm
medical

Examples

The phrenic vein carries blood away from the diaphragm after it has supplied oxygen.
Any injury to the phrenic vein could affect blood drainage from the diaphragm.
During surgery, care must be taken not to damage the phrenic vein.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: phrenic artery - an artery supplying blood to the diaphragm

Etymology

From Greek *phren* (diaphragm), relating to the diaphragm.
