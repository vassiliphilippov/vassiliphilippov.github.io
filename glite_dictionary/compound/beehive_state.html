Beehive State

1. PROPN

/ ˈbiːˌhaɪv steɪt / (AmE), / ˈbiːˌhaɪv steɪt / (BrE)
A nickname for the state of Utah in the western United States, referencing its association with industry and the early Mormon settlers.
Utah (U.S. state nickname)

Examples

Salt Lake City is the capital of the Beehive State.
Many tourists visit national parks in the Beehive State each year.
The pioneers settled in the Beehive State in the mid-1800s.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Utah - A state in the western United States

Etymology

Refers to the beehive symbol adopted by early Mormon settlers in Utah, representing industry and perseverance.
