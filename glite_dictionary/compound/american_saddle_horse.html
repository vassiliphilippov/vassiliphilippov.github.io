American saddle horse

1. NOUN

/ əˈmɛrɪkən ˈsædəl hɔrs / (AmE), / əˈmɛrɪkən ˈsædəl hɔːs / (BrE)
a breed of horse, originally from Kentucky, known for its high-stepping gait and often used in show and pleasure riding
a high-stepping horse breed from Kentucky

Examples

The American saddle horse is prized for its elegant, high-stepping movements in the show ring.
Several breeders in Kentucky specialize in raising American saddle horses for competitions.
He rode his American saddle horse proudly during the parade, drawing attention for its smooth gait.
