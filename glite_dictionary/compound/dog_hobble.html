dog hobble

1. NOUN

/ dɔɡ ˈhɑb.əl / (AmE), / dɒɡ ˈhɒb.əl / (BrE)
A fast-growing evergreen shrub (Leucothoe fontanesiana) native to the southeastern United States, with arching, interlaced branches and clusters of white flowers.
evergreen shrub of southeastern US

Examples

The hikers had to push through thick stands of dog hobble growing along the forest trail.
In spring, the dog hobble produces elegant racemes of white flowers that brighten the understory.
Local wildlife often take shelter in the dense foliage of dog hobble shrubs.
