Foreign Intelligence Surveillance Act

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔːrən ɪnˈtɛlədʒəns səˈveɪləns ækt / (AmE), / ˈfɒrən ɪnˈtelɪdʒəns səˈveɪləns ækt / (BrE)
a United States law, passed in 1978, establishing procedures for judicial authorization of foreign intelligence surveillance and creating the Foreign Intelligence Surveillance Court
1978 US law on foreign intelligence surveillance
legal american

Examples

Congress passed the Foreign Intelligence Surveillance Act to regulate how intelligence agencies conduct surveillance on foreign threats.
Under the Foreign Intelligence Surveillance Act, agencies must obtain court approval before wiretapping suspected foreign agents.
The Foreign Intelligence Surveillance Act has often been cited in debates about privacy and national security.
