endemic disease

1. NOUN

/ ɛnˈdɛmɪk dɪˈziz / (AmE), / ɛnˈdɛmɪk dɪˈziːz / (BrE)
A disease that is consistently present within a particular geographic area or population group.
persistent disease in a specific area or group
medical

Examples

Malaria is an endemic disease in many parts of sub-Saharan Africa.
Public health measures have reduced the impact of the endemic disease in rural communities.
Vaccination programs help control endemic diseases that persist within certain populations.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: epidemic - a widespread occurrence of an infectious disease in a community at a particular time

Etymology

From Greek 'endēmos' (native, in the population) + 'disease'.
