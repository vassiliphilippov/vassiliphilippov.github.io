daylight-saving time

1. NOUN

/ ˈdeɪˌlaɪt ˈseɪvɪŋ taɪm / (AmE), / ˈdeɪlaɪt ˈseɪvɪŋ taɪm / (BrE)
the system of setting clocks one hour ahead of standard time during the summer months to extend evening daylight
system of advancing clocks in summer

Examples

Many countries use daylight-saving time to make better use of natural daylight during summer.
The clocks will be set forward one hour when daylight-saving time begins in March.
She always forgets to change her watch for daylight-saving time.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: standard time - the official local time of a region that is used when daylight-saving time is not in effect
