front burner

1. NOUN

/ frʌnt ˈbɝːnɚ / (AmE), / frʌnt ˈbɜːnə / (BrE)
a position of top priority or immediate attention, especially among several tasks or issues
top priority; immediate attention

Examples

We need to move this project to the front burner if we want to make progress.
Environmental policies are on the front burner for the new administration.
With the deadline approaching, fixing the software bug is now on the front burner.

Synset Examples

the work was moved to the front burner in order to meet deadlines

Related Terms

  • Antonym: back burner - a position of lower priority or less immediate attention

Etymology

From the metaphor of a stove, where items on the front burners are cooked first and watched closely.
