ammonium carbamate

1. NOUN

/ əˈmoʊniəm kɑrˈbeɪmət / (AmE), / əˈmɒniəm kɑːˈbæmət / (BrE)
a white crystalline salt used chiefly as a nitrogen fertilizer and in chemical manufacturing
nitrogen fertilizer; chemical compound

Examples

The factory produces ammonium carbamate for use in agricultural fertilizers.
Researchers studied the decomposition of ammonium carbamate under high temperatures.
When applied to crops, ammonium carbamate increases soil nitrogen content.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ammonium carbonate - a related ammonium salt used as a leavening agent and smelling salt
  • Synonym: ammonium chloride - another ammonium salt used in fertilizers and industry

Etymology

Formed from 'ammonium' (from ammonia) and 'carbamate' (from carbamic acid).
