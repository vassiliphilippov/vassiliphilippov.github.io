top dollar

1. NOUN

/ tɑp ˈdɑlɚ / (AmE), / tɒp ˈdɒlə / (BrE)
the highest possible price paid for something, especially when considered expensive or premium
the highest price for something

Examples

Many collectors are willing to pay top dollar for rare comics.
She expects top dollar for her professional consulting services.
They received top dollar when they sold their downtown apartment.

Synset Examples

The service here demands top dollar prices.
He always looks for top dollar deals.
They paid top dollar for that luxury car.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: premium - an amount to be paid in addition to the usual or nominal price
  • Antonym: bargain - something acquired at less than the usual cost; a good deal
