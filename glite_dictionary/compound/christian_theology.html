Christian theology

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɪstʃən θiˈɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ˈkrɪstʃən θɪˈɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the academic study or doctrinal teachings of Christianity, as interpreted by Christian churches and theologians
teachings and doctrine of Christianity

Examples

She wrote her thesis on Christian theology at the seminary.
Debates over morality are often shaped by principles of Christian theology.
Many scholars have contributed to the field of Christian theology through their writings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: theology - the study of the nature of God and religious belief

Etymology

Christian from Latin 'Christianus', theology from Greek 'theologia' (discourse on God).
