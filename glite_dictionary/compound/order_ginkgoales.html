order Ginkgoales

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdər ˌɡɪŋkoʊˈeɪliːz / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə ˌɡɪŋkəʊˈeɪliːz / (BrE)
A taxonomic order of gymnosperm plants that first appeared in the Permian period, now represented by a single living species, Ginkgo biloba.
order of ancient gymnosperm plants, now only Ginkgo biloba

Examples

The order Ginkgoales includes only one living species, the Ginkgo biloba tree.
Fossils of the order Ginkgoales have been found dating back to the Permian period.
Botanists classify the Ginkgo biloba under the order Ginkgoales due to its unique characteristics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ginkgoaceae - the family containing Ginkgo biloba, the sole extant species of order Ginkgoales
  • Degree Lower: Coniferales - an order of gymnosperm trees and shrubs, sometimes including Ginkgoales

Etymology

From New Latin 'Ginkgoales', from 'Ginkgo' (the genus) + suffix '-ales' indicating an order.
