ship chandler

1. NOUN

/ ʃɪp ˈtʃændlər / (AmE), / ʃɪp ˈtʃændlə / (BrE)
a person or company that sells equipment and supplies for ships
supplier of ship equipment and provisions

Examples

The captain called the ship chandler to restock fresh water and food for the upcoming voyage.
Many ports have at least one ship chandler ready to serve visiting vessels.
As a ship chandler, he provided everything from ropes to navigational charts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chandler - a dealer in household supplies or provisions

Etymology

From 'chandler', Middle English for a dealer, especially in candles, from Old French 'chandelier' (candle maker), later extended to general supplies.
