detention cell

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈtɛnʃən sɛl / (AmE), / dɪˈtɛnʃən sɛl / (BrE)
a large room or enclosure in a jail or facility where several people are temporarily held in custody, often while awaiting trial, sentencing, or processing
temporary holding cell for groups

Examples

The police led the suspects to the detention cell while paperwork was being completed.
Dozens of immigrants were held overnight in a crowded detention cell at the border facility.
After the protest, the activists spent several hours in a detention cell before being released.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: holding cell - a small room for temporarily holding a prisoner
  • Synonym: prison cell - a room in a prison where a prisoner is kept
