to grow up and settle down

1. VERB

/ ɡroʊ ʌp ənd ˌsɛt̬l ˈdaʊn / (AmE), / ɡrəʊ ʌp ənd ˌsɛtl ˈdaʊn / (BrE)
to mature and establish a stable, routine lifestyle, especially in adulthood, often by getting a steady job or starting a family
mature and establish a stable life

Examples

Many parents hope their children will grow up and settle down someday.
After years of traveling the world, he decided it was time to grow up and settle down.
Sometimes people feel pressure from society to grow up and settle down by a certain age.

Synset Examples

She finally decided to grow up and settle down.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to settle down - to begin to live a quieter and more stable life
  • Synonym: to grow up - to become mature; to reach adulthood
