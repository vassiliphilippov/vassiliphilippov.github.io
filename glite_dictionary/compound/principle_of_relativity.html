principle of relativity

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɪnsəpəl əv ˌrɛləˈtɪvəti / (AmE), / ˈprɪnsɪpl əv ˌrɛləˈtɪvɪti / (BrE)
(Physics) The universal law stating that the laws of mechanics are the same in all inertial frames of reference, meaning they are unaffected by uniform rectilinear motion of the coordinate system.
universal law about mechanics and motion

Examples

Einstein's theory was built upon the principle of relativity.
The principle of relativity states that the laws of physics are the same in all inertial frames.
Historically, the principle of relativity challenged traditional views of space and time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: relativity - the theory that laws of physics are the same for all non-accelerating observers
