garboard strake

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɑrˌbɔrd streɪk / (AmE), / ˈɡɑːbɔːd streɪk / (BrE)
the first line of planking or wale laid next to the keel of a wooden ship.
first plank next to the keel on a ship

Examples

The carpenter carefully fitted the garboard strake to ensure a watertight seal along the keel.
Damage to the garboard strake can compromise the structural integrity of the entire hull.
Before any other planks can be attached, the garboard strake must be installed with precision.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wale - a horizontal plank or ridge on a ship's hull

Etymology

From Middle Dutch *garborde* (the edge of a ship's bottom) + English *strake* (a line of planks).
