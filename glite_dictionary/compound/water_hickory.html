water hickory

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔtər ˈhɪkəri / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə ˈhɪkəri / (BrE)
A hickory tree native to the southern United States, characterized by numerous narrow leaflets and bitter nuts.
a southern U.S. hickory tree with bitter nuts

Examples

The water hickory thrives in swampy areas of the southern United States.
Bitter nuts from the water hickory tree are often left uneaten by wildlife.
Botanists identified the old tree as a water hickory due to its distinctive narrow leaves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hickory - a tree of the walnut family with hard wood and edible nuts
