neural structure

1. NOUN

/ ˈnʊrəl ˈstrʌktʃər / (AmE), / ˈnjʊərəl ˈstrʌktʃə / (BrE)
a part of the nervous system, such as a nerve, ganglion, or brain region
part of the nervous system
medical

Examples

Damage to a neural structure in the brain can lead to loss of specific functions.
Researchers mapped each neural structure involved in memory formation.
The disease primarily affects a neural structure called the hippocampus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nerve - a bundle of fibers transmitting electrical impulses in the nervous system
  • Synonym: ganglion - a structure containing a number of nerve cell bodies

Etymology

From 'neural' (relating to nerves or the nervous system) + 'structure'. 'Neural' derives from Greek 'neuron' (nerve).
