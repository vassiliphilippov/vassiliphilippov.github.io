Alaskan malamute

1. NOUN

/ əˈlæskən ˈmæləmjuːt / (AmE), / əˈlɑːskən ˈmæləmjuːt / (BrE)
a large, powerful breed of sled dog originally developed in Alaska, known for its strength and endurance
a breed of sled dog from Alaska

Examples

The Alaskan malamute is famous for its ability to pull heavy sleds across long distances.
She adopted an Alaskan malamute because of its friendly temperament and impressive strength.
During the expedition, several Alaskan malamutes were used to help transport supplies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: husky - a breed of sled dog, lighter than the Alaskan malamute

Etymology

Named after Alaska and the Mahlemut, an Inuit tribe of Alaska credited with developing the breed.
