survivor guilt

1. NOUN

/ sərˈvaɪvɚ ɡɪlt / (AmE), / səˈvaɪvə ɡɪlt / (BrE)
a deep feeling of guilt experienced by someone who has survived an event in which others died, often accompanied by a sense of unworthiness or responsibility for having survived
guilt felt by survivors of a tragedy
medical

Examples

Many Holocaust survivors struggled with survivor guilt for decades after the war ended.
After the accident, he experienced intense survivor guilt and wondered why he had been spared.
Therapists often help people cope with survivor guilt following natural disasters or mass tragedies.

Synset Examples

survivor guilt was first noted in those who survived the Holocaust

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guilt - a feeling of responsibility or remorse for some offense or wrong
