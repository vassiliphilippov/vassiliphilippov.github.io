guinea pig

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɪni pɪɡ / (AmE), / ˈɡɪni pɪɡ / (BrE)
a person who is used as a subject in an experiment or test; someone who takes part in trials to test something new or unknown
person used in an experiment or trial

Examples

She felt like a guinea pig during the clinical trial for the new vaccine.
The students volunteered to be guinea pigs for the psychology experiment.
I'm not sure I want to be your guinea pig for testing that software.

Synset Examples

the subjects for this investigation were selected randomly
the cases that we studied were drawn from two different communities

Related Terms

  • Synonym: subject - a person who takes part in an experiment or investigation
  • Synonym: participant - a person who takes part in something

Etymology

Originally referring to a small rodent used in scientific experiments, later applied figuratively to people used in tests or trials.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡɪni ˌpɪɡ / (AmE), / ˈɡɪni pɪɡ / (BrE)
a small, stout-bodied rodent with little or no tail, originally from South America, often kept as a pet or used for scientific research
small domesticated rodent used as a pet or in research

Examples

Emily has a guinea pig named Ginger who loves to eat carrots.
The guinea pig is often used in laboratories for medical research.
Children quickly become attached to their guinea pigs because they are gentle and social pets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cavy - another name for the guinea pig or related species

Etymology

Name reflects mistaken belief that the animal came from Guinea; 'pig' from its pig-like squeals.

Source: WordNet
