long-chain molecule

1. NOUN

/ lɔŋ tʃeɪn ˈmɑlɪˌkjul / (AmE), / lɒŋ tʃeɪn ˈmɒlɪˌkjuːl / (BrE)
a molecule consisting of a relatively long sequence of atoms bonded together, often referring to polymers or large organic molecules
molecule with a long chain of atoms
medical

Examples

A protein is an example of a long-chain molecule essential for life.
The scientist analyzed the structure of the synthetic long-chain molecule in the laboratory.
Plastic materials are often made from long-chain molecules called polymers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polymer - a substance with large molecules composed of many repeated subunits
