mustard agent

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌstɚd ˌeɪdʒənt / (AmE), / ˈmʌstəd ˌeɪdʒənt / (BrE)
a highly toxic chemical warfare agent, containing sulfur compounds, that causes severe blisters and damages the eyes and lungs
toxic blister-causing war gas

Examples

The army found evidence of mustard agent exposure on the battlefield.
Victims of mustard agent often suffer from severe blisters and respiratory problems.
There is currently no effective antidote for mustard agent poisoning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sulfur mustard - another name for mustard agent, chemical weapon causing blisters

Etymology

Named for its yellow-brown color and odor reminiscent of mustard; agent from Latin 'agens', meaning a force or substance that produces an effect.
