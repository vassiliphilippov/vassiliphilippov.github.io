criminal conversation

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɪmɪnəl ˌkɑnvərˈseɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈkrɪmɪnəl ˌkɒnvəˈseɪʃən / (BrE)
(historical, law) Extramarital sexual intercourse that is considered a wrongful act against a spouse, formerly used as grounds for a lawsuit for damages.
historical legal term for adultery (tort)
legal archaic

Examples

He sued his wife's lover for criminal conversation after discovering the affair.
Cases of criminal conversation were more common in 18th-century England than they are today.
The court awarded damages for criminal conversation, citing clear evidence of adultery.

Synset Examples

adultery is often cited as grounds for divorce

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adultery - voluntary sexual intercourse between a married person and someone not their spouse
  • Antonym: faithfulness - quality of being loyal and faithful to a spouse

Etymology

From 'criminal' (relating to crime) and 'conversation' (used euphemistically for sexual intercourse in early modern English).
