government income

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡʌvərnmənt ˈɪnˌkʌm / (AmE), / ˈɡʌvənmənt ˈɪnkʌm / (BrE)
the money that a government receives, especially from taxes or other sources, to fund its activities and responsibilities
money received by the government

Examples

Taxes are the main source of government income in most countries.
Increased government income allowed for greater investment in public services.
Cuts in government income can lead to reductions in social spending.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: government revenue - all recurring income collected by a government
