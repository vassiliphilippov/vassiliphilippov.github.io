program music

1. NOUN

/ ˈproʊɡræm ˌmjuːzɪk / (AmE), / ˈprəʊɡram ˌmjuːzɪk / (BrE)
Music composed to evoke images or convey the impression of specific events, scenes, or stories to the listener.
music evoking images or events

Examples

Many composers of the Romantic era specialized in program music that told stories through instrumental sounds.
Berlioz's "Symphonie fantastique" is a famous example of program music that portrays a dramatic narrative.
He preferred program music because it painted vivid scenes in his imagination.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: absolute music - music not intended to depict a story or image
