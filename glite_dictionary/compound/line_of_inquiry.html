line of inquiry

1. NOUN

/ laɪn əv ɪnˈkwaɪəri / (AmE), / laɪn əv ɪnˈkwaɪəri / (BrE)
a series or sequence of questions or investigations directed towards finding out something or developing a particular argument
methodical sequence of questions or investigations

Examples

The detective pursued a new line of inquiry after discovering fresh evidence.
During the meeting, her line of inquiry led to some unexpected revelations.
They abandoned one line of inquiry when it proved to be a dead end.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: investigation - the act or process of examining a matter closely
  • Synonym: probe - a thorough investigation or inquiry
