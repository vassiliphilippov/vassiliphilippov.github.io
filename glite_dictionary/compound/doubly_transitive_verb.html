doubly transitive verb

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʌbli trænˈzɪtɪv vɝb / (AmE), / ˈdʌbli trænˈzɪtɪv vɜːb / (BrE)
A verb that takes both a direct and an indirect object.
verb taking direct and indirect objects

Examples

In the sentence 'She gave him a book', 'gave' is a doubly transitive verb.
'Send' is often used as a doubly transitive verb, as in 'She sent me a letter'.
The teacher explained that a doubly transitive verb can have two objects in a sentence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ditransitive verb - a verb that takes both a direct and an indirect object

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʌbli trænsˈɪtɪv vɜrb / (AmE), / ˈdʌbli trænˈsɪtɪv vɜːb / (BrE)
A verb that requires both a direct and an indirect object to complete its meaning.
verb taking direct and indirect objects

Examples

In the sentence 'She gave him a book,' 'gave' is a doubly transitive verb.
Some English verbs, like 'send' and 'offer,' can function as doubly transitive verbs.
A doubly transitive verb typically links a subject to both a person and a thing in an action.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ditransitive verb - a verb with two objects: a direct and an indirect object
