cafeteria Catholicism

1. NOUN

/ kəˌfɛɾɪˈɛriə ˌkæθəˈlɪsɪzəm / (AmE), / kəˌfɛtərɪə ˌkaθəˈlɪsɪzəm / (BrE)
the practice of selectively choosing which Catholic doctrines or teachings to accept while disregarding others
selective adherence to Catholic teachings

Examples

Many critics argue that cafeteria Catholicism undermines the authority of the Church.
She was accused of practicing cafeteria Catholicism by accepting only the Church teachings she agreed with.
The debate over cafeteria Catholicism continues among theologians and laypeople alike.

Synset Examples

His approach to faith is a reflection of cafeteria Catholicism.
Cafeteria Catholicism allows for personal interpretation of doctrine.
Cafeteria Catholicism often sparks debates in the community.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: orthodoxy - strict adherence to accepted or traditional beliefs

Etymology

From 'cafeteria', suggesting picking and choosing, combined with 'Catholicism'.
