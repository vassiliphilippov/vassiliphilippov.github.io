party line

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑɹ.ti laɪn / (AmE), / ˈpɑː.ti laɪn / (BrE)
the official policy or principles of a political party that members are expected to follow
official policy of a political group

Examples

The senator refused to vote against the party line on this issue.
Sticking to the party line is essential for rising politicians.
She broke with the party line, voicing her own opinions in parliament.

Synset Examples

He won in a vote along party lines

Related Terms

  • Synonym: platform - the declared policy of a political party or group

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːrti laɪn / (AmE), / ˈpɑːti laɪn / (BrE)
a telephone line shared by two or more subscribers, each able to listen in on calls to any of the parties on the line
telephone line shared by subscribers

Examples

In rural areas, neighbors often shared a party line for telephone service.
She picked up the phone but had to wait because the party line was already in use.
Eavesdropping was a common concern for those connected to a party line.
