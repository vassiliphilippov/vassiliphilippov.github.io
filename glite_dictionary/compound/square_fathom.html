square fathom

1. NOUN

/ skwɛr ˈfæðəm / (AmE), / skwɛə ˈfaðəm / (BrE)
a unit of area equal to the area of a square with sides of one fathom (6 feet) each, used especially in measuring depth or area in nautical contexts
unit of area (6 ft × 6 ft)

Examples

The deck was measured at 20 square fathoms to estimate the timber required.
Divers descended to explore an area spanning several square fathoms.
The total damage covered approximately five square fathoms of the ship's hull.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fathom - a unit of length equal to 6 feet, especially used in nautical contexts
