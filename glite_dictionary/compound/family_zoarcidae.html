family Zoarcidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli zoʊˈɑːrsɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli zəʊˈɑːsɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of marine fishes, commonly called eelpouts, characterized by elongated bodies and found in cold waters.
a family of marine fishes (eelpouts)

Examples

The family Zoarcidae includes many species commonly known as eelpouts.
Research on the family Zoarcidae has provided new insights into adaptation to deep-sea environments.
Specimens from the family Zoarcidae can be found in most marine biology collections.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eelpout - a common name for fishes in the family Zoarcidae

Etymology

From New Latin 'Zoarcidae', from Latin 'Zoarces' (a fish genus) + '-idae' (family suffix).
