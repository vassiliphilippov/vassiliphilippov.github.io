life-and-death

1. ADJ

/ ˈlaɪf ən ˈdɛθ / (AmE), / ˈlaɪf ənd ˈdɛθ / (BrE)
involving matters of vital importance, especially those concerning survival or serious consequences
vitally important; critical, often to survival

Examples

The negotiations were a life-and-death matter for the company.
She faced a life-and-death decision during the emergency.
Doctors often have to make life-and-death choices in the operating room.

Synset Examples

a life-and-death struggle

Related Terms

  • Synonym: critical - of great importance or crucial value
  • Antonym: trivial - of little value or importance
