iguanid lizard

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈɡwɑːnɪd ˈlɪzərd / (AmE), / ɪˈɡwɑːnɪd ˈlɪzəd / (BrE)
A lizard belonging to the family Iguanidae, typically found in the New World, Madagascar, and some Pacific islands, often characterized by a long tail and in males, a bright throat patch.
a lizard of the family Iguanidae

Examples

The Galápagos marine iguanid lizard is famous for its ability to swim in the ocean.
Many desert-dwelling iguanid lizards have developed spines and crests for display purposes.
The bright coloration on the throat of the male iguanid lizard helps attract mates during breeding season.

Etymology

From Modern Latin 'Iguanidae', based on 'iguana', from Spanish, of Arawak origin.
