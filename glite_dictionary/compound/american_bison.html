American bison

1. NOUN

/ əˈmɛrɪkən ˈbaɪsən / (AmE), / əˈmɛrɪkən ˈbaɪsən / (BrE)
a large, shaggy-haired brown bison native to the plains of North America
large North American bison

Examples

The American bison once roamed the Great Plains in vast herds.
Conservation efforts have helped increase the population of the American bison.
The American bison is an iconic symbol of the wild West.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: buffalo - another term sometimes used for the American bison
  • Synonym: European bison - a similar bison species native to Europe
