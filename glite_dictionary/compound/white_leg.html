white leg

1. NOUN

/ waɪt lɛɡ / (AmE), / waɪt lɛɡ / (BrE)
painful swelling and thrombosis of the femoral vein in the leg, typically occurring after childbirth
thrombosis of the femoral vein following childbirth
medical

Examples

After giving birth, she developed white leg, which required prompt medical attention.
Doctors recognized the symptoms of white leg by the swelling and pain in her thigh.
Early treatment of white leg can help prevent serious complications.
