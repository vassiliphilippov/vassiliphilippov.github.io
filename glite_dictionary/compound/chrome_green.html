chrome green

1. NOUN

/ kroʊm ɡrin / (AmE), / krəʊm ɡriːn / (BrE)
a green pigment made by mixing chrome yellow with iron blue, used especially in paints and inks
a green pigment from chrome yellow and iron blue

Examples

The artist mixed chrome green with other pigments to achieve the perfect shade for the landscape.
Classic British locomotives were often painted in a deep chrome green color.
Some industrial coatings use chrome green because of its durability and vivid hue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: viridian - a bright green pigment made from hydrated chromium oxide
  • Degree Lower: chrome yellow - a yellow pigment containing lead chromate

Etymology

From 'chrome', relating to chromium-based compounds, and 'green' indicating the color.

2. NOUN

/ kroʊm ɡrin / (AmE), / krəʊm ɡriːn / (BrE)
a brilliant, vivid green color often used as a pigment in paints and dyes
a brilliant green color

Examples

The artist mixed chrome green with other pigments to achieve the perfect shade for the landscape.
Her dress was a vivid chrome green that caught everyone's attention at the party.
Manufacturers often use chrome green in outdoor paint because of its durability and brightness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: emerald green - a bright shade of green resembling emerald
  • Degree Lower: olive green - a dull yellow-green color
  • Synonym: viridian - a blue-green pigment

Etymology

From 'chrome', referring to chromium-based pigments, and 'green'.
