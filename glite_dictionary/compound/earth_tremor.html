earth tremor

1. NOUN

/ ɜrθ ˈtrɛmɚ / (AmE), / ɜːθ ˈtrɛmə / (BrE)
a small or mild earthquake, especially one that is not destructive
minor earthquake

Examples

The villagers felt a slight earth tremor late last night, but no damage was reported.
After the earth tremor, many residents stepped outside to check for cracks in their homes.
Scientists say that an earth tremor of this magnitude is common in that region several times a year.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: earthquake - a sudden and violent shaking of the ground
