pay packet

1. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪ ˌpækət / (AmE), / ˈpeɪ ˌpakɪt / (BrE)
an envelope containing a worker's wages, especially in cash, given at the end of a pay period
envelope with wages

Examples

Every Friday, she looked forward to receiving her pay packet at the factory.
His pay packet was smaller this week because of the public holiday.
The tradition of handing out a pay packet in cash has largely disappeared.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pay envelope - envelope containing wages
  • Synonym: salary - regular payment for employment, typically monthly or biweekly

2. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪ ˌpæk.ɪt / (AmE), / ˈpeɪ ˌpæk.ɪt / (BrE)
the amount of money that an employee receives as pay, especially given as cash or a statement in an envelope at the end of a pay period
employee's wage or salary, especially as a physical envelope
british

Examples

She eagerly opened her pay packet on payday to see what she had earned.
The bonus in his pay packet was a pleasant surprise before the holidays.
Many workers compare their pay packets at the end of each month.

Synset Examples

She eagerly opened her pay packet on payday.
His pay packet was larger than expected this month.
After reviewing her pay packet, she planned a shopping spree.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: salary - a fixed regular payment for employment
  • Synonym: wage - regular payment for work, usually hourly or weekly
