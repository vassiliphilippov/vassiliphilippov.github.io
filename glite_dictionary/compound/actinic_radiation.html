actinic radiation

1. NOUN

/ ækˈtɪnɪk reɪdiˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ækˈtɪnɪk reɪdiˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
electromagnetic radiation capable of causing photochemical reactions, such as ultraviolet or visible light.
radiation that causes photochemical reactions

Examples

Some types of actinic radiation are used in sterilization because they destroy microorganisms.
Photographic film is highly sensitive to actinic radiation such as ultraviolet and visible light.
The laboratory uses filters to block harmful actinic radiation during certain chemical experiments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ultraviolet - a type of electromagnetic radiation with wavelengths shorter than visible light
  • Degree Lower: infrared - a type of electromagnetic radiation with longer wavelengths than visible light

Etymology

From Greek aktis, aktinos (ray, beam) and Latin radiatio (shining, radiation).
