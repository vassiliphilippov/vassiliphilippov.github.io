lacertid lizard

1. NOUN

/ ləˈsɜrtɪd ˈlɪzərd / (AmE), / ləˈsɜːtɪd ˈlɪzəd / (BrE)
A lizard belonging to the family Lacertidae, commonly found in Europe, Asia, and Africa, known for their terrestrial habits.
terrestrial Old World lizard of the Lacertidae family

Examples

The sand dunes are home to several species of lacertid lizard.
Researchers observed the quick movements of the lacertid lizard as it hunted insects.
A brightly colored lacertid lizard basked on the rocky outcrop in the midday sun.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lizard - a reptile with a long body and tail, four legs, and movable eyelids

Etymology

From Modern Latin Lacertidae, from Latin lacerta (lizard).
