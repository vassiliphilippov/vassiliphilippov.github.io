control condition

1. NOUN

/ kənˈtroʊl kənˈdɪʃən / (AmE), / kənˈtrəʊl kənˈdɪʃən / (BrE)
a standard situation in a scientific experiment against which experimental conditions are compared to determine the effect of a variable
standard in experiments for comparison

Examples

The control condition allowed researchers to compare the effects of the new drug with no treatment.
Participants in the control condition completed the same tasks but did not receive feedback.
If the experiment lacks a proper control condition, the results may be misleading.

Synset Examples

the control condition was inappropriate for the conclusions he wished to draw

Related Terms

  • Antonym: experimental condition - a condition in an experiment where the variable of interest is manipulated
  • Synonym: control group - participants in an experiment who do not receive the treatment
