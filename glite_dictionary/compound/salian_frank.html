Salian Frank

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪliən fræŋk / (AmE), / ˈseɪlɪən fræŋk / (BrE)
a member of the subgroup of Franks who settled in the Netherlands in the 4th century AD
member of a Frankish subgroup in early Netherlands

Examples

The Salian Frank tribes played a crucial role in the early history of the Netherlands.
As a Salian Frank, he would have migrated across the Rhine in the fourth century.
Archaeologists discovered a burial site believed to belong to a Salian Frank chieftain.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Frank - a member of the Germanic people who conquered and ruled parts of Western Europe

Etymology

From Latin 'Salii', referring to a specific division of the Franks.
