foregone conclusion

1. NOUN

/ fɔrˌɡɔn kənˈkluʒən / (AmE), / fɔːˌɡɒn kənˈkluːʒən / (BrE)
an outcome that is certain or inevitable before it happens
an inevitable or certain outcome

Examples

The result of the match was a foregone conclusion after the star player joined the team.
With all the evidence against him, his conviction seemed like a foregone conclusion.
It is not a foregone conclusion that the economy will improve this year.

Synset Examples

his victory is a certainty

Related Terms

  • Synonym: certainty - something that is definitely true or bound to happen
  • Antonym: possibility - something that may happen but is not certain

Etymology

From 'foregone' (past participle of 'forego', meaning ‘to go before’) and 'conclusion'.
