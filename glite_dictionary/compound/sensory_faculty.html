sensory faculty

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛn.sɚ.i ˈfæk.əl.ti / (AmE), / ˈsɛn.sər.i ˈfæk.əl.ti / (BrE)
a physical ability, such as sight, hearing, or touch, by which an organism perceives the external world
ability to sense the external world

Examples

The loss of his sensory faculty in one ear made him rely more on his eyesight.
Children develop each sensory faculty at different rates as they grow.
Some animals possess a sensory faculty that allows them to detect electrical fields.

Synset Examples

in the dark he had to depend on touch and on his senses of smell and hearing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sense - any of the bodily faculties by which humans and animals perceive stimuli

Etymology

From Latin 'sensorium' (seat of sensation) and 'facultas' (power, ability).
