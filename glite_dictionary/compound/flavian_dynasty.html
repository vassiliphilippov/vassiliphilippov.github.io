Flavian dynasty

1. PROPN

/ ˈfleɪ.vi.ən ˈdaɪ.nə.sti / (AmE), / ˈfleɪ.vɪ.ən ˈdɪn.ə.sti / (BrE)
the line of Roman emperors, including Vespasian and his sons Titus and Domitian, who ruled from AD 69 to 96
Roman imperial dynasty (69–96 AD)

Examples

The Flavian dynasty oversaw the construction of the Colosseum in Rome.
After the chaos of Nero's death, the Flavian dynasty brought relative stability to the empire.
Historians often debate the legacy of the Flavian dynasty and its impact on Roman society.

Etymology

From Latin Flavius, the family name of the emperors Vespasian, Titus, and Domitian.
