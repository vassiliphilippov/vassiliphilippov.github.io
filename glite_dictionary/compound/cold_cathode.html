cold cathode

1. NOUN

/ koʊld ˈkæθoʊd / (AmE), / kəʊld ˈkæθəʊd / (BrE)
a cathode that emits electrons without being heated, commonly used in certain types of lamps and electronic devices
cathode emitting electrons without heat

Examples

Cold-cathode fluorescent lamps are widely used for backlighting in LCD monitors.
A cold cathode does not rely on high temperatures to emit electrons, making it energy-efficient.
Early neon signs operated with a cold cathode design, giving them their distinctive glow.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hot cathode - a cathode that emits electrons when heated
