upland plover

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌp.lənd ˈplʌv.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈʌp.lənd ˈplʌv.ə / (BrE)
a large, plover-like sandpiper (Bartramia longicauda) found in North American fields and uplands
large sandpiper found in North American uplands

Examples

Every spring, the upland plover returns to the grasslands of the Midwest.
We spotted an upland plover on our hike through the open fields.
The upland plover is known for its distinctive whistling call during the mating season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Bartram's sandpiper - another common name for the upland plover
  • Synonym: sandpiper - a type of bird in the family Scolopacidae, related wader
