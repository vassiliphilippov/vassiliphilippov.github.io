fungus kingdom

1. NOUN

/ ˈfʌŋɡəs ˈkɪŋdəm / (AmE), / ˈfʌŋɡəs ˈkɪŋdəm / (BrE)
the taxonomic kingdom comprising fungi, including yeasts, molds, mushrooms, and toadstools, distinguished from the plant kingdom
group of organisms including fungi

Examples

The fungus kingdom includes mushrooms, molds, and yeasts, all distinct from plants and animals.
Unlike plants, organisms in the fungus kingdom lack chlorophyll and obtain nutrients by absorption.
Biologists now classify smuts and rusts within the fungus kingdom because of their unique characteristics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Fungi - biological kingdom including yeasts, molds, mushrooms; synonym for fungus kingdom
  • Antonym: plant kingdom - the taxonomic kingdom including green plants; comparable category to fungus kingdom

Etymology

From Latin 'fungus' (mushroom) and Old English 'cyningdom' (kingdom).
