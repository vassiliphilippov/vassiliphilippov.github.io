genus Hyssopus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs hɪˈsoʊ.pəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs hɪˈsɒ.pəs / (BrE)
A genus of perennial herbs or subshrubs native to Eurasia, known for their aromatic leaves and small, colorful flowers.
a genus of Eurasian aromatic herbs or subshrubs

Examples

Several species within the genus Hyssopus are cultivated for their aromatic qualities in herb gardens.
The genus Hyssopus is well known in Eurasian flora for its medicinal and culinary uses.
Botanists classified the blue-flowered hyssop plants under the genus Hyssopus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hyssop - an aromatic herb of the genus Hyssopus

Etymology

From Latin *Hyssopus*, from Greek *hyssōpos*, the classical name for the herb hyssop.
