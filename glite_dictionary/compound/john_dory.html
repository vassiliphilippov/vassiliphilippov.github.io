John Dory

1. NOUN

/ dʒɑn ˈdɔri / (AmE), / dʒɒn ˈdɔːri / (BrE)
A European fish (Zeus faber) with an oval, laterally compressed body, known for its firm, white flesh; often used as food.
European species of edible fish

Examples

The chef prepared the John Dory with a light lemon sauce for our main course.
While snorkeling, we spotted a John Dory swimming near the rocky seabed.
Fresh John Dory is highly prized by seafood restaurants all over Europe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dory - a family of deep-bodied, laterally compressed fish

Etymology

Possibly from French 'jaune doré' (golden yellow), describing its colour.
