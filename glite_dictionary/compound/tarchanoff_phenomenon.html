Tarchanoff phenomenon

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɑːrkənɒf fəˈnɑːmɪnən / (AmE), / ˈtɑːkənɒf fɪˈnɒmɪnən / (BrE)
A change in the electrical properties of the skin in response to stress or anxiety, typically measured by recording skin resistance or weak currents generated by the body.
change in skin's electrical properties due to stress
medical

Examples

The Tarchanoff phenomenon is often used in lie detection tests to monitor stress levels.
Scientists recorded the Tarchanoff phenomenon in subjects who were asked challenging questions.
An increase in the Tarchanoff phenomenon may indicate heightened emotional arousal or anxiety.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: galvanic skin response - change in electrical conductance of the skin in response to stimuli

Etymology

Named after Russian physiologist Ivan R. Tarkhanov (1846–1908), who first described it.
