Tinker to Evers to Chance

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɪŋkər tə ˈivərz tə ˈtʃæns / (AmE), / ˈtɪŋkə tə ˈiːvəz tə ˈtʃɑːns / (BrE)
a phrase referring to a famous double-play combination in baseball, involving shortstop Joe Tinker, second baseman Johnny Evers, and first baseman Frank Chance, used to represent flawless teamwork or a perfectly executed sequence
famous baseball double-play combination; symbol of good teamwork
american

Examples

The final out was made on a Tinker to Evers to Chance double play, thrilling the crowd.
Their smoothly coordinated effort was described as a real Tinker to Evers to Chance moment in business circles.
Sportswriters often invoke Tinker to Evers to Chance when talking about flawless teamwork.

Synset Examples

The game ended with a Tinker to Evers to Chance play.
Their teamwork resembled a Tinker to Evers to Chance sequence.
That moment felt like a Tinker to Evers to Chance moment.

Etymology

Refers to Chicago Cubs players Joe Tinker, Johnny Evers, and Frank Chance, popularized by a 1910 poem by Franklin P. Adams.
