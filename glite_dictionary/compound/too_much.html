too much

1. ADV

/ tuː mʌtʃ / (AmE), / tuː mʌtʃ / (BrE)
to a greater degree than is necessary, desirable, or acceptable; excessively.
excessively; more than necessary

Examples

She always worries too much about what others think.
If you eat too much sugar, it can affect your health.
He apologized for talking too much during the meeting.

Synset Examples

she eats too much
let's not blame them overmuch

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overly - to an excessive degree
  • Synonym: excessively - in an excessive manner
  • Antonym: too little - less than necessary

2. ADV

/ tuː mʌtʃ / (AmE), / tuː mʌtʃ / (BrE)
more than is necessary, reasonable, or desirable; excessive in amount or degree.
excessive; more than necessary

Examples

You added too much sugar to the cake and made it overly sweet.
Working late every night is too much for her to handle right now.
The show was too much for children, with loud noises and flashing lights.

Synset Examples

You shouldn’t have brought me a gift; it’s too much!
His hands were twitchy after too much caffeine.
The room was decorated with too much frou-frou.
The steak had too much gristle for my liking.
That movie was filled with too much schmaltz for my taste.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: excessive - greater than what seems reasonable; too much
  • Synonym: overly - to an excessive degree
  • Antonym: too little - less than needed or desired
