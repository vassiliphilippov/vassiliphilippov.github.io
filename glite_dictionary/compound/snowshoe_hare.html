snowshoe hare

1. NOUN

/ ˈsnoʊˌʃu ˌhɛr / (AmE), / ˈsnəʊˌʃuː ˌheə / (BrE)
a large-footed North American hare (Lepus americanus) that turns white in winter and brown in summer
North American hare; white in winter

Examples

The snowshoe hare uses its large feet to move easily over deep snow in winter.
Each spring, the snowshoe hare molts and its coat changes from white to brown.
Lynxes often prey on the snowshoe hare during the long northern winters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hare - fast-running, long-eared mammal related to rabbits
  • Degree Higher: arctic hare - hare species native to Arctic regions
