stool pigeon

1. NOUN

/ stuːl ˈpɪdʒən / (AmE), / stjuːl ˈpɪdʒɪn / (BrE)
A dummy pigeon used as a decoy to attract or trap other birds.
dummy pigeon used as a decoy

Examples

The hunters placed a stool pigeon near the trap to attract real birds.
A wooden stool pigeon was positioned in the field as a lure.
Using a stool pigeon helped them catch several doves during the hunt.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decoy - an object or person used to lure others into a trap

Etymology

From the use of captive or artificial pigeons fastened to a stool to lure wild birds; "stool" referred to as a perch or stand.

2. NOUN

/ ˈstuːl ˌpɪdʒən / (AmE), / ˈstuːl ˌpɪdʒɪn / (BrE)
an informer or decoy, especially someone acting as a secret informer for the police
police informer or decoy
informal

Examples

The police relied on a stool pigeon to infiltrate the gang and gather evidence.
He was labeled a stool pigeon after tipping off the authorities about the robbery.
Nobody trusted him anymore, suspecting he was a stool pigeon for the police.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snitch - a person who informs on someone to the authorities
  • Synonym: informant - a person who provides information to the police
  • Antonym: accomplice - a person who helps another commit a crime

Etymology

Originally U.S. slang from the 19th century; 'stool' (as in a stool to perch on) + 'pigeon' (the bird used as a decoy).
