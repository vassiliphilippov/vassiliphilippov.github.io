slip coach

1. NOUN

/ slɪp ˈkoʊtʃ / (AmE), / slɪp ˈkəʊtʃ / (BrE)
a railway carriage attached to the end of a train that can be detached while the train is still moving, allowing it to stop at a station without the rest of the train stopping
detachable railway carriage released while moving

Examples

The passengers in the slip coach prepared to disembark as the rest of the train sped on.
In the past, the slip coach allowed express trains to serve smaller stations without stopping.
Railway enthusiasts admire the clever engineering behind the slip coach system.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slip carriage - a railway carriage that can be detached from a moving train

Etymology

From 'slip' (to detach or release) and 'coach' (railway carriage).
