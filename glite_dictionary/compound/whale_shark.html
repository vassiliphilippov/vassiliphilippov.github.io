whale shark

1. NOUN

/ ˈweɪl ˌʃɑrk / (AmE), / ˈweɪl ˌʃɑːk / (BrE)
A very large, slow-moving shark with distinctive white spots, found in warm surface waters and feeding mainly on plankton.
large spotted plankton-eating shark

Examples

The whale shark is the largest known species of fish in the world.
We saw a group of whale sharks swimming near the coral reef during our dive.
Despite its size, the whale shark poses no threat to humans.
