acanthus family

1. NOUN

/ əˈkænθəs ˈfæməli / (AmE), / əˈkanθəs ˈfamɪli / (BrE)
a family of widely distributed herbs, shrubs, and trees (Acanthaceae), known for their ornamental foliage and sometimes placed in the order Scrophulariales
family of herbs and shrubs (Acanthaceae)

Examples

Members of the acanthus family are often grown for their attractive foliage in gardens.
The acanthus family includes the ornamental bear’s breeches (Acanthus mollis).
Scholars debated whether this genus should remain within the acanthus family or be classified separately.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Acanthaceae - the scientific name for the acanthus family of plants

Etymology

From 'acanthus', the type genus of the family, from Greek 'akanthos' (thorny plant).
