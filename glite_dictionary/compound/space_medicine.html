space medicine

1. NOUN

/ speɪs ˈmɛdɪsən / (AmE), / speɪs ˈmɛdɪsɪn / (BrE)
The branch of medicine that studies and monitors the effects of space flight on the health and physiology of human beings.
medical study of spaceflight effects on humans
medical

Examples

Research in space medicine helps ensure astronauts remain healthy during long-duration missions.
She published several papers on the recent advances in space medicine.
The university offers a course specializing in space medicine and its challenges.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aerospace medicine - the branch of medicine dealing with the health of people in aviation and space flight
