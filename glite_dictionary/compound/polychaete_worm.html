polychaete worm

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑliˌkit wɜrm / (AmE), / ˈpɒlɪˌkiːt wɜːm / (BrE)
A mainly marine segmented worm of the class Polychaeta, typically having paired bristled appendages on each segment.
a bristle-bearing marine segmented worm
medical

Examples

The researcher collected several polychaete worms from the tide pool for analysis.
Many polychaete worms use their bristled appendages to burrow through underwater sediment.
Some fishermen use polychaete worms as bait when fishing in saltwater environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: annelid - segmented worm of the phylum Annelida
  • Degree Lower: earthworm - a common terrestrial oligochaete

Etymology

From Greek polu- (many) + khaite (hair), referring to the many bristles.
