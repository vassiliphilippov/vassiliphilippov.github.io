goose egg

1. NOUN

/ ɡuːs ɛɡ / (AmE), / ɡuːs ɛɡ / (BrE)
a score or amount of zero; nothing at all, particularly as a result or achievement
zero or nothing (esp. in scores)
informal

Examples

After nine innings, the visiting team still had a goose egg on the scoreboard.
Despite our efforts, all we got for our trouble was a big goose egg.
His bank account balance dropped to a goose egg after paying all the bills.

Synset Examples

it looked like nothing I had ever seen before
reduced to nil all the work we had done
we racked up a pathetic goose egg
it was all for naught
I didn't hear zilch about it

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zero - the number 0; nothing
  • Synonym: nothing - not anything; absence of anything
  • Synonym: zilch - nothing; nil (slang)

Etymology

Originally from the resemblance of the numeral 0 to a goose egg.
