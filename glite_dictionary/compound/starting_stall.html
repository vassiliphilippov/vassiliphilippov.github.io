starting stall

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɑrtɪŋ stɔl / (AmE), / ˈstɑːtɪŋ stɔːl / (BrE)
a movable enclosure at the starting line of a racecourse, used to hold horses or racers until the start of a race
movable barrier/enclosure at race start line

Examples

Each horse was loaded into a starting stall moments before the race began.
The thunderous sound came as the gates of every starting stall opened simultaneously.
The new starting stall design helps ensure a fair and safe start for all competitors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: starting gate - structure marking the start of a race, especially for horses
