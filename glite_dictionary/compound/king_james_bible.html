King James Bible

1. PROPN

/ kɪŋ dʒeɪmz ˈbaɪbəl / (AmE), / kɪŋ dʒeɪmz ˈbaɪbəl / (BrE)
the English translation of the Bible commissioned by King James I of England and published in 1611, renowned for its literary style and historical influence
English Bible translation published in 1611

Examples

Many scholars still reference the King James Bible for its influence on English literature.
She gave her grandfather a beautifully bound King James Bible for his birthday.
Passages from the King James Bible are often quoted in religious services.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Authorized Version - another name for the King James Bible, especially in the UK

Etymology

Named after King James I of England, who authorized the translation.
