crash dive

1. NOUN

/ kræʃ daɪv / (AmE), / kræʃ daɪv / (BrE)
A rapid descent by a submarine to avoid danger or detection, usually as an emergency maneuver.
rapid emergency submarine descent

Examples

The submarine executed a crash dive to avoid the approaching enemy ship.
During the drill, the crew practiced a crash dive in under a minute.
The order for a crash dive was given when the periscope spotted a hostile plane overhead.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: surface - to rise to the surface (of a submarine)

2. NOUN

/ kræʃ daɪv / (AmE), / kræʃ daɪv / (BrE)
A steep and rapid descent, especially by an aircraft or submarine, often undertaken to avoid detection or achieve speed.
steep, rapid descent (aircraft or submarine)

Examples

When the enemy ship appeared on sonar, the crew prepared for a crash dive.
The submarine's crash dive drills were essential for their survival in hostile waters.
After a warning of incoming aircraft, the commander ordered an immediate crash dive.

Synset Examples

The submarine made a crash dive to evade detection.
They performed a crash dive to save fuel.
The pilot executed a crash dive to avoid the storm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dive - a sudden descent, especially by a vehicle or person
