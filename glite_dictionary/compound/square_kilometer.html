square kilometer

1. NOUN

/ skwɛr kɪˈlɑmɪtɚ / (AmE), / skwɛə kɪˈlɒmɪtə / (BrE)
a unit of area equal to one million square meters, commonly used to measure land or large surfaces
unit of area (1,000,000 m²)

Examples

The rainforest stretches for hundreds of square kilometers across the region.
That country covers more than two million square kilometers of land.
The wildlife reserve occupies an area of just one square kilometer.

Synset Examples

The area enclosed by the walls of many European medieval cities were about one square kilometre.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: hectare - unit of area equal to 10,000 square meters

Etymology

Formed from 'square' (area) and 'kilometer', from French 'kilomètre', from Greek 'kilo-' (thousand) + 'metron' (measure).
