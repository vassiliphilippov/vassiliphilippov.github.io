witch grass

1. NOUN

/ wɪtʃ ɡræs / (AmE), / wɪtʃ ɡrɑːs / (BrE)
a North American grass (Panicum capillare), often considered a weed, with slender, brushy flower panicles
a North American weedy grass species

Examples

The farmer struggled to control witch grass spreading across his cornfields.
Many gardeners mistake witch grass for an ornamental plant because of its delicate panicles.
Invasive witch grass can quickly choke out more desirable crops if left unchecked.

2. NOUN

/ wɪtʃ ɡræs / (AmE), / wɪtʃ ɡrɑːs / (BrE)
A European grass that spreads rapidly by creeping rhizomes and is considered a weed in North America.
a weedy European grass with creeping roots

Examples

The farmer struggled to control the spread of witch grass in his fields.
Despite repeated efforts, witch grass kept coming back every spring.
Witch grass can quickly overtake a garden if not removed regularly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: couch grass - another common name for Elymus repens
