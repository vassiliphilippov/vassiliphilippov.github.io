summer redbird

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌmɚ ˈrɛd.bɝd / (AmE), / ˈsʌmə ˈrɛd.bɜːd / (BrE)
a bird of the middle and southern United States, with the male having deep rose-red plumage and the female mostly yellow; also known as the summer tanager
North American songbird with red male and yellow female

Examples

The bright color of the male summer redbird makes it easy to spot among the trees.
We saw a summer redbird perched on a branch during our walk in Texas.
Female summer redbirds are often mistaken for other yellow songbirds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: summer tanager - another common name for the summer redbird (Piranga rubra)
