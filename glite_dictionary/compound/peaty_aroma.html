peaty aroma

1. NOUN

/ ˈpiːti əˈroʊmə / (AmE), / ˈpiːti əˈrəʊmə / (BrE)
an earthy scent typically associated with certain beers, whiskies, or other products containing peat
earthy smell, often in beer or whisky

Examples

The whiskey's peaty aroma filled the room as soon as the bottle was opened.
Many connoisseurs seek out beers with a distinct peaty aroma for their unique flavor profile.
The peaty aroma in the air reminded him of the Scottish countryside.

Synset Examples

They described the beer as having a rich, peaty aroma.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: earthy aroma - a smell suggesting earth or soil, often in food or drink

Etymology

From 'peat' (partly decayed plant matter, especially of bogs) + 'aroma' (pleasant smell, from Greek arōma).
