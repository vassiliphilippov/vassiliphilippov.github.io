Book of Ruth

1. PROPN

/ bʊk əv ruːθ / (AmE), / bʊk əv ruːθ / (BrE)
A book of the Old Testament telling the story of Ruth, a Moabite woman who remained loyal to her Israelite mother-in-law Naomi after the death of her husband.
Old Testament book about Ruth's loyalty to Naomi

Examples

The story of loyalty and kindness in the Book of Ruth continues to inspire readers today.
In her Bible study, she focused on the lessons taught in the Book of Ruth.
Many scholars have analyzed the significance of Ruth's actions in the Book of Ruth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ruth - the protagonist of the Book of Ruth in the Bible
