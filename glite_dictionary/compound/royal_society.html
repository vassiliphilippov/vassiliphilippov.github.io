Royal Society

1. PROPN

/ ˈrɔɪ.əl səˈsaɪ.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˈrɔɪ.əl səˈsaɪ.ə.ti / (BrE)
a prestigious independent scientific academy in the United Kingdom, founded in 1660, devoted to promoting excellence in science
UK scientific academy founded in 1660
british

Examples

The Royal Society has played a major role in the advancement of science since the seventeenth century.
Many renowned scientists, including Isaac Newton, have been members of the Royal Society.
Each year, the Royal Society awards prestigious medals for outstanding scientific achievements.

Etymology

Founded in 1660 and granted a royal charter by King Charles II in 1662.
