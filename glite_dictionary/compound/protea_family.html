protea family

1. NOUN

/ ˈproʊti.ə ˈfæm.ə.li / (AmE), / ˈprəʊti.ə ˈfam.ɪ.li / (BrE)
a large family of Australian and South African shrubs and trees with leathery leaves and clustered mostly four-parted flowers, constituting the order Proteales
family of shrubs/trees in the order Proteales

Examples

The protea family includes many striking flowering plants found in South Africa and Australia.
Banksias and grevilleas are well-known members of the protea family.
Botanists study the protea family for its diverse adaptations to dry environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Proteaceae - the botanical family comprising proteas, banksias, and related plants

Etymology

Named after the genus Protea, from Greek mythology character Proteus, known for changeability.
