gas constant

1. NOUN

/ ɡæs ˈkɑnstənt / (AmE), / ɡæs ˈkɒnstənt / (BrE)
a universal physical constant (symbol R) that appears in the ideal gas law, equal to 8.3143 joules per kelvin per mole
universal constant in the ideal gas law

Examples

The value of the gas constant is essential for solving problems involving the ideal gas law.
Scientists often denote the gas constant by the symbol R in equations.
Each calculation involving an ideal gas requires the use of the gas constant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: universal gas constant - alternative term for gas constant, same scientific concept
