Brodmann's area 17

1. NOUN

/ ˈbroʊdˌmænz ˈɛriə ˈsɛv.ənˈtin / (AmE), / ˈbrɒdmənz ˈɛəriə ˈsɛv.ənˈtiːn / (BrE)
the region of the occipital cortex in the brain that serves as the primary visual processing area, receiving input from the optic radiation
primary visual cortex in the occipital lobe
medical

Examples

Damage to Brodmann's area 17 can result in severe visual impairment or blindness.
Functional MRI scans show strong activation in Brodmann's area 17 when subjects view images.
The study compared the cellular structure of Brodmann's area 17 across different mammalian species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: primary visual cortex - the main visual processing area in the occipital lobe

Etymology

Named after the German neurologist Korbinian Brodmann (1868–1918), who mapped distinct areas of the cerebral cortex.
