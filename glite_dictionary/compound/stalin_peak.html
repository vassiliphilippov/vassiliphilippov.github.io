Stalin Peak

1. PROPN

/ stɑːlɪn piːk / (AmE), / stɑːlɪn piːk / (BrE)
the highest mountain in the Pamir Mountains, located near the Chinese border in northeastern Tajikistan, rising to 24,590 feet (7,495 meters)
highest peak in the Pamir Mountains, Tajikistan

Examples

In 1933, a Soviet team made the first successful ascent of Stalin Peak.
After independence, Tajikistan renamed Stalin Peak to Ismoil Somoni Peak.
Mountaineers from around the world are drawn to the challenge of climbing Stalin Peak.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ismoil Somoni Peak - current name for the highest mountain in Tajikistan, formerly Stalin Peak
  • Degree Lower: Lenin Peak - another major mountain in the Pamirs, formerly named after Vladimir Lenin

Etymology

Named after Joseph Stalin, Soviet political leader; now officially known as Ismoil Somoni Peak.
