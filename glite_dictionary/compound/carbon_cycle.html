carbon cycle

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑrbən ˈsaɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈkɑːbən ˈsaɪkəl / (BrE)
the continuous process by which carbon is exchanged between the atmosphere, living organisms, and the environment
process of carbon exchange in nature

Examples

The carbon cycle is crucial for maintaining the Earth's climate balance.
Deforestation can disrupt the carbon cycle by reducing the number of trees that absorb carbon dioxide.
Scientists study how the carbon cycle responds to rising global temperatures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nitrogen cycle - the process by which nitrogen is converted between its various chemical forms in nature

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑrbən ˌsaɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈkɑːbən ˌsaɪkl̩ / (BrE)
a thermonuclear process occurring in the cores of some stars, in which carbon acts as a catalyst to fuse hydrogen into helium
thermonuclear reaction in stars

Examples

The carbon cycle in massive stars enables the fusion of hydrogen at higher temperatures.
Astrophysicists study the carbon cycle to understand how stars produce energy.
Unlike the proton-proton chain, the carbon cycle dominates in hotter, heavier stars.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: CNO cycle - a process in stars where carbon, nitrogen, and oxygen act as catalysts for hydrogen fusion

Etymology

From 'carbon' (the element) + 'cycle', referring to the catalytic cycle involving carbon nuclei.
