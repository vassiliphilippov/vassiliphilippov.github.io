gramma grass

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡræmə ˌɡræs / (AmE), / ˈɡræmə ˌɡrɑːs / (BrE)
A type of pasture grass native to the plains of South America and western North America, valued as forage for livestock.
pasture grass of American plains

Examples

The ranchers relied on gramma grass to feed their cattle throughout the dry season.
Tourists admired the vast stretches of gramma grass waving on the prairie.
Conservation efforts focus on restoring native gramma grass to promote biodiversity.
