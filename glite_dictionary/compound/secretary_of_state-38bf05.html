secretary of state

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛkrəˌtɛri əv steɪt / (AmE), / ˌsɛkrɪtəri əv steɪt / (BrE)
the head of a government department concerned with foreign relations, especially the chief foreign affairs official in countries such as the United States and the United Kingdom
senior government official for foreign affairs

Examples

The Secretary of State met with international leaders to discuss the peace agreement.
The President nominated a new Secretary of State after the previous one resigned.
In the United Kingdom, the Secretary of State is responsible for various government departments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foreign minister - a government official in charge of foreign affairs in many countries
