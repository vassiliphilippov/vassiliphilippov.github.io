declining balance method (of depreciation)

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈklaɪnɪŋ ˈbæləns ˈmɛθəd əv ˌdɛprɪsiˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈklaɪnɪŋ ˈbæləns ˈmɛθəd əv ˌdɛprɪsiˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
An accounting method for calculating depreciation in which a fixed percentage is subtracted from the asset's remaining book value each year.
depreciation calculation method using a fixed percentage

Examples

Our company adopted the declining balance method of depreciation to better match expenses with revenues.
Using the declining balance method of depreciation, assets lose value more quickly in the early years.
The accountant explained the benefits of switching to the declining balance method of depreciation for tax purposes.

Synset Examples

When using the declining balance method, the salvage value is not considered in determining the annual depreciation, but the book value of the asset being depreciated is never brought below its salvage value, regardless of the method used.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: straight-line method - an alternative method of depreciation with equal annual amounts
  • Synonym: depreciation - a reduction in the value of an asset over time
