genetic counseling

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˈnɛtɪk ˈkaʊnsəlɪŋ / (AmE), / dʒɪˈnɛtɪk ˈkaʊnsəlɪŋ / (BrE)
advice and information given to prospective parents about the risks of inherited disorders in their children
advice on inherited disorder risks
medical

Examples

After receiving genetic counseling, the couple felt more informed about their risks of passing on inherited conditions.
Doctors often recommend genetic counseling before pregnancy if there is a family history of genetic illness.
Access to genetic counseling can help families make important reproductive decisions based on accurate information.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: genetic testing - testing DNA for inherited disorders
