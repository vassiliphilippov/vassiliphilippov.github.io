celandine poppy

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛlənˌdaɪn ˈpɑpi / (AmE), / ˈsɛləndiːn ˈpɒpi / (BrE)
A perennial herbaceous plant native to woodlands of eastern North America, with yellow flowers and lobed leaves.
North American yellow-flowered woodland herb

Examples

The bright yellow flowers of the celandine poppy added a splash of color to the woodland trail.
Gardeners often plant celandine poppy in shady areas for its attractive foliage and blooms.
We spotted several celandine poppy plants near the stream during our hike in spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stylophorum - The botanical genus of the celandine poppy

Etymology

'Celandine' from Middle English, from Old French 'celidoine', from Medieval Latin 'chelidonia', from Greek 'khelidōn' (swallow, the bird). 'Poppy' from Old English 'popæg' or 'popig', ultimately from Latin 'papaver'.
