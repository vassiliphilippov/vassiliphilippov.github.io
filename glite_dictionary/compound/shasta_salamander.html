Shasta salamander

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃæstə səˈlæmændɚ / (AmE), / ˈʃæstə səˈlæmændə / (BrE)
a rare species of salamander native to the Mount Shasta region of California, primarily inhabiting caves
a salamander species native to Mount Shasta, California

Examples

The Shasta salamander is rarely seen outside its cave habitat in the Mount Shasta area.
Biologists are concerned about the declining population of the Shasta salamander due to habitat loss.
The Shasta salamander is known for its secretive lifestyle and unique adaptation to life in caves.

Etymology

Named after Mount Shasta in California, where it is found.
