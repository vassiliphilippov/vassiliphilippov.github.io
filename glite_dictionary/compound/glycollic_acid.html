glycollic acid

1. NOUN

/ ɡlaɪˈkɑlɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / ɡlɪˈkɒlɪk ˈasɪd / (BrE)
a colorless, odorless crystalline acid found in some plants, used in skincare and chemical industries
a colorless acid used in skincare and industry

Examples

Glycollic acid is commonly found in many chemical peels and exfoliating skincare products.
Studies show that glycollic acid helps improve skin texture and reduce fine lines.
Sugar cane is a natural source of glycollic acid.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glycolic acid - alternative spelling for the same compound

Etymology

From 'glycol' + '-ic', indicating its derivation from glycol.
