franchise tax

1. NOUN

/ ˈfræn.tʃaɪz tæks / (AmE), / ˈfræn.tʃaɪz tæks / (BrE)
a tax levied by some US states on corporations, usually based on the company's net worth and income generated within the state
US state tax on corporations

Examples

The company was required to pay a franchise tax to the state for the privilege of doing business there.
Failure to file the annual franchise tax report could result in penalties or loss of good standing.
Several states do not have a franchise tax, which often attracts new businesses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corporate tax - a tax on the profits of corporations
  • Degree Lower: income tax - a tax on individual or corporate income
