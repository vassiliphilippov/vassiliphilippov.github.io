sharp pain

1. NOUN

/ ʃɑrp peɪn / (AmE), / ʃɑːp peɪn / (BrE)
a sudden, intense, and clearly localized feeling of physical discomfort
sudden, intense physical discomfort

Examples

She experienced a sharp pain in her ankle after stepping on the uneven pavement.
A sharp pain shot through his side when he tried to stand up too quickly.
The dentist assured him that any sharp pain would only last for a moment.

Synset Examples

He felt a sharp pain in his bowel after the heavy meal.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dull pain - a persistent, mild, and not well-localized pain
