Altay Mountains

1. PROPN

/ ˈæl.taɪ ˈmaʊn.tənz / (AmE), / ˈæl.taɪ ˈmaʊn.tɪnz / (BrE)
a mountain range in central Asia, stretching about a thousand miles from Kazakhstan east into western Mongolia and northern China
mountain range in central Asia

Examples

The Altay Mountains are known for their stunning landscapes and rich biodiversity.
Several rivers, including the Irtysh and the Ob, have their sources in the Altay Mountains.
Explorers have long been fascinated by the mysteries of the Altay Mountains.

Etymology

From Turkic *altï* (gold), referencing the region's historical association with gold; 'Altay' is the traditional name in local languages.
