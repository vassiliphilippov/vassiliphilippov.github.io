cat shark

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæt ˌʃɑrk / (AmE), / ˈkæt ˌʃɑːk / (BrE)
A small, bottom-dwelling shark with cat-like eyes, typically found along continental slopes.
small bottom-dwelling shark with cat-like eyes

Examples

Many deep-sea explorers have encountered the elusive cat shark in rocky underwater canyons.
The cat shark is known for its distinctive eyes, which resemble those of a cat.
Researchers tagged several cat sharks to monitor their movements along the continental shelf.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dogfish - small, common species of shark or fish sometimes confused with cat sharks
