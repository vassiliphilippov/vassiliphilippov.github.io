devil grass

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛvəl ɡræs / (AmE), / ˈdɛvəl ɡrɑːs / (BrE)
A trailing grass species, native to Europe and now widespread in warm climates, used especially for lawns and pastures.
creeping grass used for lawns and pastures

Examples

The gardener warned that devil grass can quickly take over flower beds if not controlled.
Many golf courses in southern states use devil grass for its durability and rapid growth.
Despite its name, devil grass is often valued for its ability to withstand drought.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Bermuda grass - Another common name for devil grass, a widely used grass species for lawns and pastures
