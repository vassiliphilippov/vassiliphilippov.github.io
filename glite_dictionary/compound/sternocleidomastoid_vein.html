sternocleidomastoid vein

1. NOUN

/ stɜrˌnoʊˌklaɪdəˈmæstɔɪd veɪn / (AmE), / stɜːnəʊˌklaɪdəʊˈmæstɔɪd veɪn / (BrE)
a vein that drains blood from the sternocleidomastoid muscle and empties into the internal jugular vein
vein draining the sternocleidomastoid muscle
medical

Examples

An injury to the sternocleidomastoid vein can cause significant bleeding during neck surgery.
The sternocleidomastoid vein helps return blood from the muscle to the internal jugular vein.
Ultrasound imaging can visualize the sternocleidomastoid vein in patients with suspected vascular disorders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: internal jugular vein - large vein in the neck collecting blood from the brain and superficial parts of the face and neck
  • Synonym: sternocleidomastoid muscle - long muscle in the side of the neck

Etymology

From 'sternocleidomastoid' (a muscle named for its attachments: sternum, clavicle, and mastoid process) and 'vein' (Latin 'vena').
