reluctant dragon

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈlʌktənt ˈdræɡən / (AmE), / rɪˈlʌktənt ˈdræɡən / (BrE)
a dragon or person who is unwilling or hesitant, especially one who avoids confrontation or conflict
someone unwilling or avoiding confrontation

Examples

In the school play, she played the role of the reluctant dragon who refused to fight the knight.
Despite his skills, Mark acted like a reluctant dragon when asked to defend his opinions.
The team needed a champion, but their leader remained a reluctant dragon, hesitant to take charge.

Synset Examples

The story featured a reluctant dragon who feared humans.
He was known as the reluctant dragon, avoiding conflict.
The reluctant dragon finally agreed to help the villagers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reluctant - unwilling or disinclined to do something
  • Degree Lower: timid - showing a lack of courage or confidence; easily frightened
  • Antonym: resolute - admirably purposeful, determined, and unwavering

Etymology

From 'The Reluctant Dragon', a story by Kenneth Grahame (1898).
