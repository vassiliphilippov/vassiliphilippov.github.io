American antelope

1. NOUN

/ əˈmɛrɪkən ˈæntɪˌloʊp / (AmE), / əˈmɛrɪkən ˈæntɪləʊp / (BrE)
a fast-running hoofed mammal native to western North American plains, resembling an antelope but not closely related, also known as the pronghorn
hoofed mammal of North American plains

Examples

The American antelope can run at incredible speeds across the plains.
Many tourists hope to spot a American antelope during their trip to the national park.
Unlike true antelopes, the American antelope is not closely related to African species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pronghorn - hoofed mammal native to North America, sometimes called American antelope
  • Degree Higher: antelope - a grazing mammal of the cow family, found in Africa and Eurasia
