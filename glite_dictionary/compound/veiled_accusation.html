veiled accusation

1. NOUN

/ ˈveɪld ˌækjəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈveɪld ˌækjʊˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
an accusation made indirectly or subtly, so that its meaning is implied rather than directly stated
an indirect or subtle accusation

Examples

She made a veiled accusation about his honesty without mentioning his name.
His remarks were so carefully worded that only a veiled accusation could be detected.
During the meeting, a veiled accusation cast doubt on her professionalism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insinuation - an unpleasant hint or suggestion of something bad
