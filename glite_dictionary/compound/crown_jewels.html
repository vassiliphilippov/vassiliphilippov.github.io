crown jewels

1. NOUN

/ kraʊn ˈdʒuːəlz / (AmE), / kraʊn ˈdʒuːəlz / (BrE)
the ceremonial regalia, including jewelry and ornaments, worn by a sovereign on official or state occasions, especially those belonging to a monarchy
the ceremonial jewelry and regalia of a monarchy

Examples

The British crown jewels are displayed in the Tower of London for visitors to admire.
During the coronation, the monarch was adorned with the crown jewels.
He was fascinated by the history behind the crown jewels and their symbolic significance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: regalia - the emblems or insignia of royalty, especially the crown and other ornaments used at coronations
