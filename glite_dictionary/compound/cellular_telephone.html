cellular telephone

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛljuˌlɚ ˈtɛləˌfoʊn / (AmE), / ˈsɛljʊlə ˈtɛlɪfəʊn / (BrE)
a portable telephone that operates via radio signals over a network of linked transmission cells, allowing mobile communication
portable mobile phone using cellular networks

Examples

She forgot her cellular telephone at home and missed several important messages.
With a cellular telephone, you can call anyone from almost anywhere.
The first cellular telephone models were large and heavy compared to today's slim designs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mobile phone - portable telephone using a cellular network
  • Antonym: landline - telephone connected by wires to a network

Etymology

From 'cellular', referring to the network of cells used for transmission, and 'telephone', from Greek ‘tele’ (far) + ‘phone’ (voice).
