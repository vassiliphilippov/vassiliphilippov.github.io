personal foul

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɝːsənl faʊl / (AmE), / ˈpɜːsənəl faʊl / (BrE)
An infraction in a sport, such as basketball or football, involving illegal or unnecessarily rough physical contact with an opponent.
illegal physical contact in sports

Examples

The player was ejected from the game after committing a personal foul on his opponent.
A personal foul resulted in a free throw for the other team.
Officials called a personal foul when the defender blocked with excessive force.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: technical foul - a less severe infraction often involving unsporting behavior rather than physical contact
