trial judge

1. NOUN

/ ˈtraɪ.əl dʒʌdʒ / (AmE), / ˈtraɪ.əl dʒʌdʒ / (BrE)
A judge who presides over a trial court, responsible for making rulings and overseeing the proceedings.
judge presiding over a trial court
legal

Examples

The trial judge ruled that the evidence was admissible in court.
After hearing both sides, the trial judge delivered the final verdict.
Appeals can be made if the parties believe the trial judge made a legal error.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: judge - an official with authority to decide legal cases
