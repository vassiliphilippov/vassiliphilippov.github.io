wink-wink

1. NOUN

/ wɪŋk wɪŋk / (AmE), / wɪŋk wɪŋk / (BrE)
An indirect gesture or remark that implies a shared secret, joke, or unspoken understanding between people.
implied shared secret or understanding
informal

Examples

He denied the allegations, but there was a definite wink-wink between him and his lawyer.
She gave a wink-wink when she mentioned her 'meeting', making everyone laugh.
The advert used a wink-wink reference to the product's unexpected uses.

Synset Examples

He gave a wink-wink when he mentioned the surprise party.
His tone was a wink-wink, suggesting more than he said.
She said she was 'working late' with a wink-wink.
