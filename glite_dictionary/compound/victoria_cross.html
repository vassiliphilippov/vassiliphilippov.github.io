Victoria Cross

1. NOUN

/ vɪkˈtɔːri.ə krɔs / (AmE), / vɪkˈtɔːri.ə krɒs / (BrE)
the highest British military decoration awarded for gallantry in the face of the enemy
highest British military decoration for gallantry
british

Examples

He was awarded the Victoria Cross for his acts of bravery during the war.
The museum displays several Victoria Crosses belonging to famous British soldiers.
Winning the Victoria Cross is considered the highest honor in the British armed forces.

Etymology

Named after Queen Victoria, who instituted the decoration in 1856.
