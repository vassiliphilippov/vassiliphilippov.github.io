Lake Chad

1. PROPN

/ leɪk tʃæd / (AmE), / leɪk tʃæd / (BrE)
a large, shallow lake in north-central Africa, bordering Chad, Nigeria, Niger, and Cameroon, fed mainly by the Shari River
lake in north-central Africa

Examples

The shrinking size of Lake Chad has become a major concern for environmentalists.
Fishermen rely heavily on the resources of Lake Chad for their livelihoods.
Many migratory birds stop at Lake Chad during their seasonal journeys.
