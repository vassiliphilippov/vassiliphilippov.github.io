Paschal celery

1. NOUN

/ ˈpæskəl ˈsɛləri / (AmE), / ˈpæskəl ˈsɛləri / (BrE)
A variety of celery cultivated commercially, characterized by its green stalks.
commercially grown green celery

Examples

Markets often carry Paschal celery because of its crisp, green stalks and mild flavor.
Chefs prefer Paschal celery for salads and garnishes due to its appealing color and texture.
Growers recommend planting Paschal celery in cooler climates for the best results.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: celery - a crunchy green vegetable often used in salads or as a snack
