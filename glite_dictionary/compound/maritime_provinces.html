Maritime Provinces

1. PROPN

/ ˈmɑrɪˌtaɪm ˈprɑvɪnsɪz / (AmE), / ˈmɑːrɪtaɪm ˈprɒvɪnsɪz / (BrE)
the collective name for the Canadian provinces of New Brunswick, Nova Scotia, and Prince Edward Island
Canadian provinces: New Brunswick, Nova Scotia, and Prince Edward Island

Examples

Many tourists visit the Maritime Provinces each summer to enjoy the scenic coastline and seafood.
The economy of the Maritime Provinces has traditionally relied on fishing and shipbuilding.
Winter storms can be particularly harsh in the Maritime Provinces due to their proximity to the Atlantic Ocean.

Etymology

From 'maritime' (relating to the sea) and 'provinces', indicating their coastal location in Canada.
