pancreatic artery

1. NOUN

/ ˌpæŋkriˈætɪk ˈɑːrtəri / (AmE), / ˌpaŋkrɪˈatɪk ˈɑːtəri / (BrE)
an artery that branches from the splenic artery and supplies blood to the pancreas
artery supplying the pancreas
medical

Examples

The pancreatic artery delivers oxygen-rich blood to the pancreas for its metabolic activities.
Damage to a pancreatic artery can lead to internal bleeding in cases of abdominal trauma.
During surgery, the surgeon carefully identified the pancreatic artery to avoid accidental injury.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: splenic artery - an artery that supplies blood to the spleen and gives off pancreatic branches

Etymology

From 'pancreatic' (relating to the pancreas) and 'artery' (from Greek artēria, windpipe, later blood vessel).
