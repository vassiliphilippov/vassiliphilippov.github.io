philosopher's stone

1. NOUN

/ fəˈlɑːsəfərz stoʊn / (AmE), / fəˈlɒsəfəz stəʊn / (BrE)
an imaginary substance in alchemy believed to be able to turn base metals into gold and grant immortality
mythical alchemical substance

Examples

Ancient alchemists spent their lives trying to create the philosopher's stone in pursuit of wealth and immortality.
Legends claim that the philosopher's stone could transform any metal into pure gold.
The myth of the philosopher's stone continues to inspire books and movies about magic and alchemy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elixir of life - a mythical substance thought to grant immortality

Etymology

From medieval Latin 'philosophorum lapis', meaning 'the philosophers' stone'.
