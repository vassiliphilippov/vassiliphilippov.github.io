whole house

1. NOUN

/ hoʊl haʊs / (AmE), / həʊl haʊs / (BrE)
the entire building of a house, including all parts and rooms considered as a single unit
entire building of a house

Examples

After the fire, they had to rebuild the whole house from the ground up.
We painted the whole house last year to give it a fresh new look.
The smell of cookies filled the whole house on Sunday mornings.

Synset Examples

They renovated the whole house over the summer.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: house - a building for human habitation
