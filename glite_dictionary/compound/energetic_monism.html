energetic monism

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛnərˈdʒɛtɪk ˈmɑːnɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌɛnəˈdʒɛtɪk ˈmɒnɪzəm / (BrE)
the philosophical theory that energy is the fundamental substance of all phenomena and that all observable changes are transformations of energy
philosophical view: all is energy

Examples

The philosopher's work was grounded in energetic monism, viewing energy as the essence of all material existence.
Wilhelm Ostwald was a leading proponent of energetic monism in the late nineteenth century.
Discussions of energetic monism often contrast it with materialistic or dualistic theories of reality.

Synset Examples

Ostwald's philosophical outlook, known as energetism or energetic monism, was strongly influenced by his scientific background and by the state of physical science at the end of the nineteenth century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monism - the doctrine that only one kind of substance or principle constitutes reality

Etymology

From Greek *energeia* (activity, operation) and *monismos* (doctrine of oneness).
