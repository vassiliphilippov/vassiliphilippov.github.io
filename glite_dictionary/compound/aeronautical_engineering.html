aeronautical engineering

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛroʊˈnɔtɪkəl ˌɛn.dʒɪˈnɪr.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌeərəˈnɔːtɪkəl ˌɛn.dʒɪˈnɪə.rɪŋ / (BrE)
the branch of engineering concerned with the design, development, and construction of aircraft
engineering of aircraft and their design

Examples

She studied aeronautical engineering at a prestigious university in Europe.
Advances in aeronautical engineering have made modern aircraft safer and more efficient.
Pursuing a career in aeronautical engineering requires strong skills in math and physics.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: aerospace engineering - engineering of both aircraft and spacecraft
  • Synonym: mechanical engineering - engineering discipline dealing with mechanics, engines, and machines

Etymology

From aeronautical (relating to aircraft or flight) + engineering (from Latin ingenium, talent/contrivance).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɛroʊˈnɔtɪkəl ˌɛn.dʒəˈnɪr.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌeə.rəˈnɔː.tɪ.kəl ˌen.dʒɪˈnɪə.rɪŋ / (BrE)
the branch of engineering concerned with the design, construction, and operation of aircraft
engineering of aircraft design and construction

Examples

She studied aeronautical engineering at university and now works for a major aircraft manufacturer.
Advances in aeronautical engineering have made air travel safer and more efficient.
His passion for airplanes led him to pursue a degree in aeronautical engineering.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aerospace engineering - the branch of engineering that deals with both aircraft and spacecraft

Etymology

From Greek 'aēr' (air) and 'nautikos' (navigation) combined with 'engineering'.
