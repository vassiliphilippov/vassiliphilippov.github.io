Winter Olympics

1. PROPN

/ ˈwɪn.tɚ əˈlɪm.pɪks / (AmE), / ˈwɪn.tə əˈlɪm.pɪks / (BrE)
an international sporting event held every four years, featuring competitions in winter sports such as skiing, skating, and ice hockey
international games for winter sports

Examples

Athletes from all over the world train for years to compete in the Winter Olympics.
The 2010 Winter Olympics were held in Vancouver, Canada, attracting thousands of spectators and participants.
Winning a gold medal at the Winter Olympics is considered the highest achievement in many winter sports.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Olympic Games - the leading international sporting event including summer and winter sports competitions
