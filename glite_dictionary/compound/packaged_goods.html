packaged goods

1. NOUN

/ ˈpækɪdʒd ɡʊdz / (AmE), / ˈpækɪdʒd ɡʊdz / (BrE)
products, especially food and other groceries, that are sold in pre-packaged form for convenience and preservation
products sold in packaging, especially food

Examples

The supermarket aisles are filled with packaged goods ranging from breakfast cereals to bottled sauces.
Many consumers are becoming more concerned about the amount of plastic used in packaged goods.
She prefers to cook from scratch rather than rely on packaged goods for her family's meals.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fresh produce - fruit and vegetables sold in natural, unpackaged form
