ascending node

1. NOUN

/ əˈsɛndɪŋ noʊd / (AmE), / əˈsɛndɪŋ nəʊd / (BrE)
the point where an object in orbit crosses from south to north over the ecliptic plane
orbit crossing point northward (astronomy)

Examples

The satellite passed through the ascending node shortly after sunrise.
Accurate measurements of the ascending node are crucial for determining an orbit's orientation.
The moon crosses its ascending node twice during its monthly orbit.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: descending node - the orbital crossing point from north to south of the ecliptic
