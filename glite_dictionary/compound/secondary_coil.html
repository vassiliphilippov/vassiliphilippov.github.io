secondary coil

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛk.ənˌdɛr.i kɔɪl / (AmE), / ˈsɛk.ən.d(ə).ri kɔɪl / (BrE)
a coil in an electrical circuit in which an electric current is induced by a current in a nearby primary coil
coil with induced current in transformer

Examples

The secondary coil receives its current via electromagnetic induction from the primary coil.
Increasing the number of turns in the secondary coil raises the output voltage of the transformer.
A malfunction in the secondary coil can cause the transformer to stop working properly.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: primary coil - a coil through which current is passed to induce a current in a secondary coil
  • Synonym: transformer - an electrical device that transfers energy between two or more coils
