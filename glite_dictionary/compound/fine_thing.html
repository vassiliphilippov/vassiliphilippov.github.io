fine thing

1. NOUN

/ faɪn θɪŋ / (AmE), / faɪn θɪŋ / (BrE)
a situation or event that would be very desirable or fortunate, often used ironically to express doubt that it will happen
desirable or fortunate situation (often ironic)

Examples

A holiday abroad would be a fine thing, but we can't afford it right now.
Winning the lottery would certainly be a fine thing for him.
Chance'd be a fine thing—I doubt I'll ever have the opportunity.

Synset Examples

I might get a raise; chance'd be a fine thing!
