Bewick's swan

1. NOUN

/ ˈbjuːɪks swɑːn / (AmE), / ˈbjuːɪks swɒn / (BrE)
A small Eurasian subspecies of the tundra swan, notable for its yellow and black bill, and smaller size compared to the whooper swan.
a small Eurasian tundra swan subspecies

Examples

During the winter, many Bewick's swan visit the wetlands of the UK.
The Bewick's swan can be distinguished by its smaller size and unique bill pattern.
Researchers observed a flock of Bewick's swan migrating across northern Europe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tundra swan - large swan species found in the Northern Hemisphere
  • Degree Higher: whooper swan - large Eurasian swan with a yellow and black bill

Etymology

Named after Thomas Bewick (1753–1828), English engraver and naturalist.
