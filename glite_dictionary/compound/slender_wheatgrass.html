slender wheatgrass

1. NOUN

/ ˈslɛndər ˈwiːtˌɡræs / (AmE), / ˈslɛndə ˈwiːtɡrɑːs / (BrE)
A North American grass species cultivated in western United States as an excellent forage crop.
a grass used as forage in western U.S.

Examples

Farmers in Montana often plant slender wheatgrass to provide hardy pasture for livestock.
The slender wheatgrass quickly established itself after the prairie was restored.
Researchers found that slender wheatgrass improves soil health while serving as quality forage.
