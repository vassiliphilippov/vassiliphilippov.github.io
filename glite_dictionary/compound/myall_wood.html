myall wood

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪˌɔl wʊd / (AmE), / ˈmaɪəl wʊd / (BrE)
A durable, fragrant, dark-colored wood from the Australian tree Acacia homolophylla, traditionally used by Indigenous Australians for spears and ornaments.
fragrant Australian wood from Acacia homolophylla

Examples

The craftsman carved the boomerang from myall wood for its strength and beautiful grain.
A faint, pleasant scent lingered in the workshop where the myall wood was being polished.
Myall wood is prized for making decorative ornaments as well as traditional spear shafts.

Synset Examples

Acacia homalophylla (myall wood) yields a fragrant timber used for ornaments.

Etymology

From Australian Aboriginal language (possibly Kamilaroi), 'myall' referring to the tree.
