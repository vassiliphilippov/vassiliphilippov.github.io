Jekyll and Hyde

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɛkəl ənd haɪd / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛkəl ənd haɪd / (BrE)
a person who has two very different sides to their personality, one good and one evil
person with dual, contrasting personalities

Examples

Everyone thought he was gentle, but after hearing him yell, I realized he was a real Jekyll and Hyde.
His Jekyll and Hyde nature makes it difficult to know which side you'll encounter each day.
The man's Jekyll and Hyde personality left his coworkers anxious and confused.

Etymology

From the characters Dr. Jekyll and Mr. Hyde in Robert Louis Stevenson's 1886 novella 'Strange Case of Dr Jekyll and Mr Hyde'.
