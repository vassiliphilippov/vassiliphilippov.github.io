sodium benzoate

1. NOUN

/ ˈsoʊdiəm ˈbɛnzoʊˌeɪt / (AmE), / ˈsəʊdiəm ˈbɛnzəʊeɪt / (BrE)
a white crystalline salt used as a food preservative and antiseptic
food preservative and antiseptic salt

Examples

Some soft drinks contain sodium benzoate to extend their shelf life.
The use of sodium benzoate as a preservative is approved by many food safety authorities.
Check the label for sodium benzoate if you are sensitive to food additives.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: preservative - a substance used to preserve food or other perishable items

Etymology

From sodium (the element) and benzoate (from benzoic acid, derived from gum benzoin).
