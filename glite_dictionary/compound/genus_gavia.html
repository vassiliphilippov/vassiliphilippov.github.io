genus Gavia

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˈɡeɪviə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈɡeɪvɪə / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Gavidae, comprising the loons, a group of aquatic birds known for their distinctive calls and excellent diving ability.
genus of loons (aquatic birds)

Examples

The genus Gavia consists of several species found in North America and Eurasia.
Ornithologists have studied the migratory habits of birds in the genus Gavia for decades.
Members of the genus Gavia are well adapted for swimming and diving.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loon - A common name for birds belonging to the genus Gavia

Etymology

From Latin 'gavia' meaning 'sea mew' or 'gull'.
