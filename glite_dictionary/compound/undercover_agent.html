undercover agent

1. NOUN

/ ˌʌndɚˈkʌvɚ ˈeɪdʒənt / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈkʌvə ˈeɪdʒənt / (BrE)
a person who works secretly for a government, police, or organization to gather information without revealing their true identity
secret operative or spy

Examples

The police sent an undercover agent to infiltrate the criminal organization.
As an undercover agent, she had to assume a completely new identity.
Rumors spread that an undercover agent was present at the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spy - a person employed to gather secret information
  • Synonym: double agent - an agent pretending to work for one side while actually working for another
