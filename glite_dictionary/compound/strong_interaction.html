strong interaction

1. NOUN

/ strɔŋ ˌɪntɚˈækʃən / (AmE), / strɒŋ ˌɪntərˈækʃən / (BrE)
the fundamental force in physics that binds protons and neutrons together in atomic nuclei, mediated by gluons
fundamental force binding atomic nuclei

Examples

The strong interaction is responsible for holding atomic nuclei together despite the repulsion between protons.
Physicists study how the strong interaction acts at extremely short distances between particles.
Without the strong interaction, atoms as we know them could not exist.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: weak interaction - the fundamental force responsible for certain types of radioactive decay
  • Antonym: electromagnetic interaction - the fundamental force between electrically charged particles
  • Antonym: gravitational interaction - the fundamental force causing mutual attraction between masses
