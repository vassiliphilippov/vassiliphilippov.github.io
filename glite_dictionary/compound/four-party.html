four-party

1. ADJ

/ fɔr ˈpɑrti / (AmE), / fɔː ˈpɑːti / (BrE)
Involving or relating to four distinct parties, especially in reference to agreements, negotiations, or systems.
involving four parties

Examples

The countries signed a four-party trade agreement to encourage regional cooperation.
The four-party talks resulted in a compromise between the involved groups.
A four-party contract can be more complex than one involving only two or three participants.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: three-party - involving three parties
  • Degree Higher: five-party - involving five parties
