battle of Ivry

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæt̬l əv ˈivri / (AmE), / ˈbætl əv ˈivri / (BrE)
a battle fought in 1590 near Ivry, France, in which the Huguenot forces led by Henry IV defeated the Catholic League under the duke of Mayenne
battle (1590) between Huguenots and Catholics in France

Examples

The battle of Ivry was a turning point in the French Wars of Religion.
Historians often discuss the strategies used by Henry IV during the battle of Ivry.
After the battle of Ivry, Henry IV's position as King of France was significantly strengthened.
