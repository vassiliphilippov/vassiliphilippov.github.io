dirty laundry

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɝːti ˈlɔːndɹi / (AmE), / ˈdɜːti ˈlɔːndri / (BrE)
Personal matters or secrets that could cause embarrassment if revealed to others; private scandals.
private matters that could cause embarrassment

Examples

Some politicians fear having their dirty laundry aired in public.
We all have dirty laundry that we would rather keep to ourselves.
The tabloid was eager to reveal the celebrity's dirty laundry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skeleton in the closet - a secret source of shame, potentially embarrassing if revealed

Etymology

From the literal sense of laundry that is dirty and not meant to be seen by others, used figuratively from the 20th century.
