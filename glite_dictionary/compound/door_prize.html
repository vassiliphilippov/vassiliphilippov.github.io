door prize

1. NOUN

/ dɔr praɪz / (AmE), / dɔː praɪz / (BrE)
a prize awarded to a randomly selected attendee at an event, often with tickets distributed at the entrance
prize given at an event entrance

Examples

Everyone who came to the gala received a ticket for a chance at the door prize.
She was thrilled when her number was called for the door prize at the school fundraiser.
At the annual picnic, the door prize was a brand new tablet.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɔr ˌpraɪz / (AmE), / ˈdɔː ˌpraɪz / (BrE)
a prize given to a person attending an event, typically awarded at random as an incentive or thank you for attending
prize given at an event, usually chosen at random

Examples

Every guest at the gala had a chance to win a door prize before the evening ended.
She was thrilled when her ticket number was drawn for the door prize.
Many conferences offer a door prize to encourage more people to attend.

Synset Examples

Everyone who attended got a door prize as a thank you.
They announced a special door prize at the conference.
