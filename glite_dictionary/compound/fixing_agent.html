fixing agent

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪksɪŋ ˌeɪdʒənt / (AmE), / ˈfɪksɪŋ ˌeɪdʒənt / (BrE)
a chemical compound used to make something permanent or stable, such as setting dyes in fabrics or preserving photographic images
chemical that sets or preserves

Examples

To ensure the color lasts, a fixing agent is added during the dyeing process.
The photographic studio used a fixing agent to develop the negatives.
Without a proper fixing agent, the prints would fade quickly over time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fixative - a substance used to fix or set dyes, colors, or photographs
