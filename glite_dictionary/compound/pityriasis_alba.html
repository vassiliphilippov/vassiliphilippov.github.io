pityriasis alba

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɪt.iˈraɪ.əsɪs ˈælbə / (AmE), / ˌpɪt.ɪˈraɪ.əsɪs ˈal.bə / (BrE)
A common skin condition, usually in children or young adults, characterized by round patches of lighter, depigmented skin.
skin condition with pale patches
medical

Examples

Many children develop pityriasis alba as pale patches on their cheeks during the summer.
My doctor explained that pityriasis alba is harmless and tends to resolve on its own.
The white, slightly scaly spots of pityriasis alba can be mistaken for other skin disorders.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: vitiligo - chronic skin condition with loss of pigment

Etymology

From New Latin: 'pityriasis' (scaly skin condition) + Latin 'alba' (white).
