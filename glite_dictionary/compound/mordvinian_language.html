Mordvinian language

1. NOUN

/ mɔrdˈvɪniən ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / mɔːdˈvɪniən ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
The Finno-Ugric language spoken by the Mordvinian people of Russia, comprising two main dialects: Erzya and Moksha.
Finno-Ugric language of the Mordvinians

Examples

She is studying the Mordvinian language as part of her research on Finno-Ugric peoples.
The Mordvinian language uses the Cyrillic script in its written form.
Efforts are being made to preserve the Mordvinian language among young people in the region.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Erzya - One of the two major dialects of the Mordvinian language
  • Degree Lower: Moksha - One of the two major dialects of the Mordvinian language

Etymology

From Mordvin, the name of the ethnic group in Russia, plus 'language'.
