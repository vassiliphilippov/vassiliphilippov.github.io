commander-in-chief

1. NOUN

/ kəˌmændər ɪn ˈtʃiːf / (AmE), / kəˌmɑːndər ɪn ˈtʃiːf / (BrE)
the officer who holds the highest authority over a country's armed forces or a particular military force
supreme military commander

Examples

The president serves as the commander-in-chief of the armed forces.
During the crisis, the commander-in-chief addressed the nation to reassure the public.
She was appointed commander-in-chief of the multinational force.

Synset Examples

in the U.S. the president is the commander in chief

Related Terms

  • Synonym: general - the highest-ranking officer in the army
  • Antonym: private - lowest-ranking soldier

Etymology

From commander + in + chief, modeled on military titles in English since the 17th century.
