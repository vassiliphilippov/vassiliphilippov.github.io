subacute bacterial endocarditis

1. NOUN

/ ˌsʌb.əˈkjuːt bækˈtɪr.i.əl ˌɛn.doʊ.kɑːrˈdaɪ.tɪs / (AmE), / ˌsʌb.əˈkjuːt bækˈtɪə.ri.əl ˌɛn.dəʊ.kɑːˈdaɪ.tɪs / (BrE)
a chronic bacterial infection of the inner lining of the heart and its valves, with symptoms that develop gradually
chronic infection of heart lining and valves
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with subacute bacterial endocarditis after weeks of fatigue and fever.
Doctors treated the case of subacute bacterial endocarditis with a prolonged course of antibiotics.
If left untreated, subacute bacterial endocarditis can lead to severe heart complications.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: acute bacterial endocarditis - a rapidly developing bacterial infection of the heart lining and valves

Etymology

From Latin 'subacutus' (somewhat acute), Greek 'endon' (within) and 'kardia' (heart), plus 'bacterial' (pertaining to bacteria).
