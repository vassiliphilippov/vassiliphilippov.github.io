order Madreporaria

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdər ˌmædrəpɔˈrɛriə / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə ˌmædrəpɔˈrɪə / (BrE)
A taxonomic order of marine animals known as stony corals, characterized by their hard calcium carbonate skeletons.
taxonomic order of stony corals
medical

Examples

The order Madreporaria includes many species commonly found in coral reefs.
Scientists study the skeleton structure of the order Madreporaria to understand reef formation.
Environmental changes can significantly impact organisms within the order Madreporaria.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coral - marine invertebrate with a hard, often calcareous skeleton

Etymology

From neo-Latin 'Madreporaria', based on 'Madrepora' (a type genus of stony coral), itself from Italian 'madre' (mother) and 'pora' (pore), referencing the appearance of coral surfaces.
