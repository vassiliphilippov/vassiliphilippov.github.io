triple time

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrɪpəl taɪm / (AmE), / ˈtrɪpl taɪm / (BrE)
musical time characterized by three beats in each measure or bar.
music: three beats per bar

Examples

The waltz is commonly written in triple time, giving it its distinctive rhythm.
Many composers use triple time to create a feeling of dance or flow in their music.
You can hear the difference between duple and triple time when you listen closely to the beat.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: duple time - musical time with two beats in each bar
  • Degree Higher: quadruple time - musical time with four beats in each bar
