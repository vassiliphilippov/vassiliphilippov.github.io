genus Crotalus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs kroʊˈteɪləs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs krəʊˈteɪləs / (BrE)
A taxonomic genus of large rattlesnakes, primarily found in the Americas, which rarely bite unless startled or hunting prey.
genus of large rattlesnakes

Examples

Scientists recently discovered a new species within the genus Crotalus in the mountains of Mexico.
The genus Crotalus includes some of the most recognized rattlesnakes in North America.
Venom composition varies significantly among different species in the genus Crotalus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rattlesnake - a venomous American snake with a segmented rattle on the tail

Etymology

Genus from Latin; Crotalus from Greek 'krotalon' (a rattle).
