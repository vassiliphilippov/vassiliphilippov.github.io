shaking palsy

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃeɪkɪŋ ˈpælzi / (AmE), / ˈʃeɪkɪŋ ˈpɔːlzi / (BrE)
an old term for Parkinson's disease, a degenerative disorder of the central nervous system marked by tremor and muscle rigidity
historical term for Parkinson's disease
medical archaic

Examples

The condition once known as shaking palsy is today called Parkinson's disease.
In the 19th century, doctors struggled to treat patients suffering from shaking palsy.
James Parkinson wrote a seminal essay describing the symptoms of shaking palsy in 1817.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Parkinson's disease - a progressive neurological disorder marked by tremor and muscle rigidity

Etymology

From 'shake' + 'palsy'; 'shaking palsy' was first used by British physician James Parkinson in 1817.
