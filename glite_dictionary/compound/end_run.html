end run

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛnd ˌrʌn / (AmE), / ˈɛnd ˌrʌn / (BrE)
(in American football) an attempt to advance the ball by running around the end of the line of scrimmage.
American football play running to the outside
american

Examples

The running back broke free for a 20-yard end run in the second quarter.
Their coach called an end run to take advantage of the defense's weakness on the outside.
He attempted an end run but was tackled just short of the goal line.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛnd ˌrʌn / (AmE), / ˈɛnd ˌrʌn / (BrE)
an act of avoiding or circumventing an obstacle or opposition, often by indirect or clever means
an act of circumventing opposition

Examples

The company attempted an end run around the law to speed up their project.
He made an end run to avoid telling his manager about the mistake.
Negotiators feared that the other side would try an end run in the final hours of the talks.

Synset Examples

They tried to end run the regulations with a loophole.
The quarterback made an end run to avoid the defense.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bypass - to go around or avoid an obstacle, especially by using a detour
  • Synonym: to circumvent - to find a way around an obstacle, especially with cleverness or deception
  • Antonym: to confront - to face or deal with something directly, the opposite of avoiding

Etymology

Originally from American football, where 'end run' refers to a play in which a player runs around the end of the line, avoiding the main defense.
