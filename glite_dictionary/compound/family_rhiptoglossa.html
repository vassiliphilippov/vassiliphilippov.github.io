family Rhiptoglossa

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli rɪpˌtɑːˈɡlɒsə / (AmE), / ˈfæməli rɪpˌtɒˈɡlɒsə / (BrE)
A family of Old World chameleons, sometimes classified as a superfamily within Sauria.
family of Old World chameleons

Examples

Many species within the family Rhiptoglossa display remarkable abilities to change color.
Research on the family Rhiptoglossa has contributed to our understanding of chameleon evolution.
The family Rhiptoglossa primarily inhabits forests and woodlands in Africa and parts of Asia.

Etymology

From Greek 'rhipto' (to throw) and 'glossa' (tongue), referring to the chameleon's tongue.
