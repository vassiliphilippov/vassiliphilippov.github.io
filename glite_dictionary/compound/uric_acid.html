uric acid

1. NOUN

/ ˈjʊrɪk ˌæsɪd / (AmE), / ˈjʊərɪk ˌæsɪd / (BrE)
a white, odorless, crystalline substance produced by the breakdown of purines in the body, found in blood and urine
a metabolic waste product in blood and urine
medical

Examples

High levels of uric acid in the blood can lead to gout.
Doctors often check uric acid levels to diagnose kidney function.
The patient’s uric acid concentration was above the normal range.

Etymology

From French *urique*, from Greek *ouron* (urine) + acid.
