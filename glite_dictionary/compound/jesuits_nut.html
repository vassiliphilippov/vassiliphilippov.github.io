Jesuits' nut

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɛzjuɪts nʌt / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛzjʊɪts nʌt / (BrE)
a variety of water chestnut, especially used to refer to certain species traditionally associated with regions influenced by Jesuits.
a type of water chestnut

Examples

The Jesuits' nut is often cultivated in ponds throughout southern Europe.
Local markets sometimes sell Jesuits' nut as a delicacy during the summer months.
She learned to prepare a traditional dish that includes sliced Jesuits' nut and fresh herbs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: water chestnut - an aquatic plant with edible corms

Etymology

Named for its association with Jesuit missionaries who promoted or utilized the plant.
