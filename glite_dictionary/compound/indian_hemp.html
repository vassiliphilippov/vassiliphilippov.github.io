Indian hemp

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪndiən hɛmp / (AmE), / ˈɪndiən hɛmp / (BrE)
a valuable fiber plant, originally from the East Indies and now widely cultivated, used especially for its tough fibers
fiber plant cultivated for strong fibers

Examples

Indian hemp is cultivated in many countries for its strong, durable fibers.
The ropes were made from the fibers of Indian hemp.
Some people confuse Indian hemp with true hemp, but they are different species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hemp - a plant grown for fiber, oil, and as a drug; Cannabis sativa

2. NOUN

/ ˈɪndiən hɛmp / (AmE), / ˈɪndiən hɛmp / (BrE)
A North American plant (Apocynum cannabinum), also known as Canadian dogbane, whose tough fibers were used for cordage by Indigenous peoples and which has been used in folk medicine for joint pain or inflammation.
North American plant used for fiber and folk medicine

Examples

The tough fibers of Indian hemp were traditionally used for making fishing lines and nets.
Some Indigenous communities valued Indian hemp for its medicinal properties against rheumatism.
Botanists often study Indian hemp for its similarities to true hemp species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hemp - fibrous plant used for rope and textiles

Etymology

Named for its traditional use by Native Americans and resemblance to true hemp.

3. NOUN

/ ˈɪndiən hɛmp / (AmE), / ˈɪndiən hɛmp / (BrE)
a plant, especially Cannabis sativa, cultivated for its fibrous stalk used to make rope and cloth, or for its psychoactive substances such as bhang and hashish
Cannabis plant used for fiber and drugs

Examples

The cultivation of Indian hemp is regulated in many countries due to its medicinal and recreational uses.
Traditional rope was often made from the strong fibers of Indian hemp.
Products such as bhang and hashish are derived from the resin of Indian hemp plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cannabis - a plant genus used for fiber, drugs, and as a recreational drug
  • Synonym: hemp - the fibrous plant used to make rope and textiles
