Nash equilibrium

1. NOUN

/ næʃ ɪˈkwɪlɪbriəm / (AmE), / næʃ ɪˈkwɪlɪbriəm / (BrE)
(in game theory) a situation in which no participant can benefit by changing their strategy while the strategies of others remain unchanged.
stable state in game theory where no one benefits by changing strategy alone

Examples

The auction reached a Nash equilibrium where no bidder could gain by changing their bid.
Negotiations between the companies resulted in a Nash equilibrium, with both sides settling on their best possible strategies.
In many competitive games, a Nash equilibrium ensures that each player's decision is optimal given the others' choices.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: equilibrium - a state of balance, especially between opposing forces or influences

Etymology

Named after the mathematician John Nash (1928–2015), who developed the concept.
