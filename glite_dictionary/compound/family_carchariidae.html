family Carchariidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli kɑːrˌkɛəriˈaɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli kɑːˌkeərɪˈaɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of sharks known as sand sharks, sometimes considered coextensive with family Carcharhinidae in some classifications
a family of sand sharks

Examples

The family Carchariidae includes several species of sand sharks found worldwide.
Recent studies debate whether the family Carchariidae should be merged with Carcharhinidae.
Unlike other families, members of the family Carchariidae are known for their elongated, pointed snouts.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: family Carcharhinidae - a family of sharks commonly known as requiem sharks

Etymology

From Greek karcharías (a kind of shark) + taxonomic suffix -idae.
