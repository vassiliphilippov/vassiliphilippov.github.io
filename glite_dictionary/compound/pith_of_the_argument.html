pith of the argument

1. NOUN

/ pɪθ əv ði ˈɑːrɡjəmənt / (AmE), / pɪθ əv ði ˈɑːɡjʊmənt / (BrE)
the central or most important point of an argument or discussion
core or main point of an argument

Examples

The lawyer skillfully highlighted the pith of the argument in her closing statement.
If we focus on the pith of the argument, we can resolve this disagreement faster.
He explained the pith of the argument to ensure everyone understood the main point.

Synset Examples

The pith of her argument was compelling and thought-provoking.
He got to the pith of the issue quickly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: core - the central or most important part
  • Synonym: essence - the most important quality or aspect

Etymology

From 'pith', meaning the essential or central part, originally referring to the spongy tissue inside plant stems.
