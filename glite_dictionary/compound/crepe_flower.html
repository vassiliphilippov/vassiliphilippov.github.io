crepe flower

1. NOUN

/ kreɪp ˈflaʊər / (AmE), / kreɪp ˈflaʊə / (BrE)
an ornamental shrub from eastern India, often planted in the southern United States for its colorful, crinkled flowers
ornamental shrub with crinkled flowers

Examples

The crepe flower bloomed brilliantly in the garden all summer long.
Many southern homes have crepe flower bushes lining their front yards.
Gardeners admire the vibrant colors of the crepe flower, which range from pink to deep red.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crepe myrtle - ornamental shrub or small tree with crinkled flowers, common in warm climates
