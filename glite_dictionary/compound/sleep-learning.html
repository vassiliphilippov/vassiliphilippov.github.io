sleep-learning

1. NOUN

/ sliːp ˈlɜrnɪŋ / (AmE), / sliːp ˈlɜːnɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of acquiring knowledge or skills by receiving information while asleep, often through recordings
learning while asleep using recordings

Examples

Some researchers claim that sleep-learning can help you remember vocabulary more effectively.
The idea of sleep-learning was popularized in the 20th century as a way to learn languages.
Despite its appeal, sleep-learning is not strongly supported by scientific evidence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hypnopedia - learning during sleep using auditory input
