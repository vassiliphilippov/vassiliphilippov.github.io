myasthenic syndrome

1. NOUN

/ ˌmaɪ.əsˈθiː.nɪk ˈsɪn.droʊm / (AmE), / ˌmaɪ.əsˈθiː.nɪk ˈsɪn.drəʊm / (BrE)
A rare disorder often associated with lung cancer, marked by weakness and fatigue of the hip and thigh muscles and aching back, caused by antibodies affecting the neuromuscular junctions.
neuromuscular disorder with muscle weakness
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with myasthenic syndrome after experiencing persistent muscle weakness and fatigue.
Doctors linked his myasthenic syndrome to an underlying lung carcinoma.
Treatment for myasthenic syndrome often involves addressing both the cancer and the autoimmune symptoms.

Etymology

From Greek 'myastheneia' (muscle weakness) and 'syndrome' (set of symptoms).
