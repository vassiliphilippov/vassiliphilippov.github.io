Modern English

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑːdərn ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈmɒdn ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ / (BrE)
The stage of the English language spoken and written since about 1450, including present-day forms.
English language from about 1450 onward

Examples

Shakespeare wrote in Modern English, not Old or Middle English.
Linguistics students must distinguish between Modern English and earlier stages of the language.
Many words used in Modern English have evolved significantly since the fifteenth century.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Middle English - English language from about 1150 to 1450
  • Antonym: Old English - English language spoken before about 1150
