Hell's Half Acre

1. PROPN

/ hɛlz hæf ˈeɪkər / (AmE), / hɛlz hɑːf ˈeɪkə / (BrE)
a district in Manhattan formerly notorious for its slums and vice, especially in the 19th and early 20th centuries
former Manhattan slum district

Examples

During the 1850s, Hell's Half Acre was infamous for crime and desperate poverty.
Many immigrants settled near Hell's Half Acre when they first arrived in New York.
Reformers campaigned to clear the slums of Hell's Half Acre and improve living conditions.

Etymology

From 'hell' (place of suffering) + 'half acre' (small area); used as a nickname for notorious urban districts in the 19th and early 20th centuries.
