sapele mahogany

1. NOUN

/ səˈpiːli məˈhɑːɡəni / (AmE), / səˈpiːli məˈhɒɡəni / (BrE)
An African tree (Entandrophragma cylindricum) valued for its lightweight, cedar-scented wood, which ranges in color from pink to reddish brown and is often used in furniture and musical instruments.
African tree with cedar-scented wood

Examples

The craftsman selected sapele mahogany for the cabinet because of its beautiful reddish hue.
Guitars made from sapele mahogany are prized for their rich tone and lightweight feel.
We imported a shipment of sapele mahogany to use in our luxury yacht interiors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mahogany - hard reddish-brown timber from several tropical trees

Etymology

Sapele, from Sapele, a town in Nigeria where the tree is abundant; mahogany from Spanish 'mahogani', used for wood of related trees.
