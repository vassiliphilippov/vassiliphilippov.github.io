surgical contraception

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɝːdʒɪkəl ˌkɑntrəˈsɛpʃən / (AmE), / ˈsɜːdʒɪkəl ˌkɒntrəˈsɛpʃən / (BrE)
A method of preventing pregnancy by means of surgical sterilization.
contraception via surgical sterilization
medical

Examples

Some couples choose surgical contraception as a permanent solution to family planning.
After careful consideration, they decided that surgical contraception was the best option for them.
Access to surgical contraception has improved over the last decade in many countries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sterilization - permanent prevention of reproduction, often by surgery

Etymology

From "surgical" (relating to surgery) and "contraception" (prevention of conception).
