pupillary reflex

1. NOUN

/ ˈpjuː.pəˌlɛr.i ˈriː.flɛks / (AmE), / ˈpjuː.pɪ.lə.ri ˈriː.flɛks / (BrE)
a reflex contraction of the iris sphincter muscle in response to bright light or certain drugs, causing the pupil to become smaller
reflex causing pupil contraction
medical

Examples

The doctor checked her pupillary reflex to assess her neurological function.
A normal pupillary reflex indicates that the optic and oculomotor nerves are functioning properly.
The absence of a pupillary reflex can be a sign of severe brain injury.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accommodation reflex - the reflex that adjusts the eye for near or far vision
  • Antonym: mydriasis - dilation of the pupil
  • Synonym: miosis - excessive constriction of the pupil

Etymology

From Latin 'pupilla' (pupil of the eye) and 'reflexus' (turned back).
