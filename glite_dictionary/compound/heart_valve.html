heart valve

1. NOUN

/ hɑrt vælv / (AmE), / hɑːt vælv / (BrE)
a structure in the heart that allows blood to flow in one direction and prevents it from flowing backward
valve controlling blood flow in the heart
medical

Examples

The surgeon carefully replaced the damaged heart valve during the procedure.
Diseases affecting the heart valve can lead to serious health problems if untreated.
An artificial heart valve is sometimes used for patients with congenital defects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: valve - a device or structure that controls the flow of a fluid

2. NOUN

/ hɑrt vælv / (AmE), / hɑːt vælv / (BrE)
an artificial device implanted to replace a damaged or diseased natural valve in the heart
artificial replacement for natural heart valve
medical

Examples

After surgery, the patient's new heart valve was functioning properly.
A mechanical heart valve can last for decades if cared for appropriately.
Doctors discussed the risks and benefits of heart valve replacement with the family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prosthetic valve - an artificial substitute for a damaged valve, especially in the heart
  • Antonym: natural heart valve - the original valve in the heart, not replaced by an implant
