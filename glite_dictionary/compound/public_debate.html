public debate

1. NOUN

/ ˈpʌblɪk dɪˈbeɪt / (AmE), / ˈpʌblɪk dɪˈbeɪt / (BrE)
a formal event where a proposition is presented and opposed, typically followed by a vote to decide the issue
a formal event with arguments for and against a proposition

Examples

The candidates engaged in a public debate on national television before the election.
Attendance at the public debate was high, with citizens eager to hear both sides of the issue.
After the public debate, the audience cast their votes to determine the winner.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: debate - a formal discussion on a particular topic
