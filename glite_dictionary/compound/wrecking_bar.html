wrecking bar

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛk.ɪŋ bɑr / (AmE), / ˈrɛk.ɪŋ bɑː / (BrE)
a strong iron bar with a flattened, often forked end, used as a lever for prying or demolition
heavy iron lever used in demolition

Examples

The workers used a wrecking bar to remove the old floorboards.
He pried open the crate with a heavy wrecking bar.
A wrecking bar is essential for most demolition jobs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crowbar - a metal bar with a curved end used as a lever
