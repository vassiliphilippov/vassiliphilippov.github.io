pilot film

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪlət fɪlm / (AmE), / ˈpaɪlət fɪlm / (BrE)
the first episode of a proposed television series, produced to demonstrate the show's concept and attract sponsors or commissions
first episode made for a proposed TV series

Examples

The network executives reviewed the pilot film before deciding to produce the full series.
Although the pilot film received mixed reviews, it was enough to get the show greenlit.
The actors reunited several months after the pilot film was made to begin shooting the rest of the season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pilot episode - first episode of a proposed TV series made as a test

Etymology

From 'pilot' (in the sense of 'experimental' or 'trial') and 'film' (motion picture).
