snake polypody

1. NOUN

/ sneɪk ˈpɑliˌpoʊdi / (AmE), / sneɪk ˈpɒlɪˌpəʊdi / (BrE)
An epiphytic fern with long creeping rhizomes, native to tropical America.
a tropical American fern species

Examples

The snake polypody thrives high up on tree branches in the rainforests of Central America.
Botanists were excited to find a rare snake polypody growing on the old cedar log.
The long, creeping roots of the snake polypody help it anchor securely to its host tree.
