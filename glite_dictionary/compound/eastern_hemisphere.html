eastern hemisphere

1. NOUN

/ ˈistɚn ˈhɛməsˌfɪr / (AmE), / ˈiːstən ˈhemɪsˌfɪə / (BrE)
the half of the earth that lies east of the prime meridian, including Europe, Asia, Africa, and Australia
half of earth east of the prime meridian

Examples

Most of the world's population lives in the eastern hemisphere.
The eastern hemisphere experiences different seasons than the western hemisphere at the same time of year.
Ancient trade routes connected civilizations across the eastern hemisphere.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: western hemisphere - the half of the earth west of the prime meridian
