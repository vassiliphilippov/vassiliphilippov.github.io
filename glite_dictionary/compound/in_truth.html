in truth

1. ADV

/ ɪn truθ / (AmE), / ɪn truːθ / (BrE)
Used to emphasize the accuracy or reality of a statement; in fact; indeed.
emphatic phrase meaning 'in fact' or 'indeed'

Examples

In truth, there was nothing anyone could have done to prevent the accident.
Many claimed the painting was a masterpiece, but in truth, it was poorly executed.
In truth, we were all surprised by her sudden decision to leave.

Synset Examples

in truth, moral decay hastened the decline of the Roman Empire
really, you shouldn't have done it
a truly awful book

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indeed - used to emphasize a statement or response
  • Synonym: really - used to emphasize the truth or reality of something
  • Synonym: in fact - used to emphasize the truth of an assertion
