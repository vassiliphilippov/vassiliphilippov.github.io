lamp chimney

1. NOUN

/ læmp ˈtʃɪmni / (AmE), / læmp ˈtʃɪmni / (BrE)
a cylindrical glass tube that surrounds the flame of an oil lamp to protect it and help control airflow
glass tube around oil lamp flame

Examples

The old kerosene lamp had a cracked lamp chimney that needed to be replaced.
Please handle the lamp chimney carefully, as the glass is very fragile.
After cleaning the lamp chimney, the flame burned brighter and steadier.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chimney - a vertical channel for conveying smoke from a fireplace or furnace
