wage freeze

1. NOUN

/ weɪdʒ friːz / (AmE), / weɪdʒ friːz / (BrE)
a government or organizational policy of holding wages at their current level, preventing any increases for a specified period
policy freezing wage increases

Examples

The government announced a wage freeze for all public sector employees this year.
Facing budget deficits, the company implemented a wage freeze that lasted two years.
Unions protested the new wage freeze policy, arguing it would hurt workers during inflation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pay freeze - a policy of freezing pay levels
  • Antonym: wage cut - a reduction in the amount paid to employees
