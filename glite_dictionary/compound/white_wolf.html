white wolf

1. NOUN

/ waɪt wʊlf / (AmE), / waɪt wʊlf / (BrE)
a large wolf native to Arctic North America, characterized by its white fur and black-tipped tail
arctic wolf with white fur

Examples

The white wolf can survive in extreme cold thanks to its thick fur.
Researchers spotted a pack of white wolves hunting caribou in the tundra.
The white wolf is also known as the arctic wolf or polar wolf.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arctic wolf - a white-furred subspecies of gray wolf found in the Arctic
  • Degree Lower: gray wolf - the common wolf species found in the wild
