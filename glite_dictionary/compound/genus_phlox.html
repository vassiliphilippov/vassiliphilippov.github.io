genus Phlox

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈfloʊks / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈflɒks / (BrE)
a genus of herbaceous to shrubby evergreen or deciduous plants, including annuals and perennials, native to North America and known for their colorful flowers
a plant genus with showy North American flowers

Examples

Many species in the genus Phlox are prized in gardens for their vibrant blooms.
Researchers studied the distribution of genus Phlox across North America.
Some plants of the genus Phlox thrive in dry, rocky soils.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phlox - a plant of the genus Phlox, known for its clusters of flowers

Etymology

From Greek phlox (flame), referring to the bright flowers.
