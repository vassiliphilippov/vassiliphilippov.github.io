undulant fever

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌndjələnt ˈfiːvər / (AmE), / ˈʌndjələnt ˈfiːvə / (BrE)
An infectious bacterial disease transmitted from animals to humans, marked by recurring fevers and headache, often contracted through contact with infected animals or animal products.
bacterial disease with recurring fever
medical

Examples

The veterinarian warned that drinking unpasteurized milk can lead to undulant fever in humans.
After a trip to the countryside, she began experiencing symptoms of undulant fever.
Brucellosis, also known as undulant fever, can cause serious illness if left untreated.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brucellosis - bacterial infection from Brucella species, synonymous with undulant fever

Etymology

From Latin 'undulans' meaning 'wavy', referring to the wave-like pattern of fever.
