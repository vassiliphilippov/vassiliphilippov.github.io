zinc phosphate dental cement

1. NOUN

/ zɪŋk ˈfoʊs.feɪt ˈdɛn.tl səˈmɛnt / (AmE), / zɪŋk ˈfɒs.feɪt ˈdɛn.tl sɪˈmɛnt / (BrE)
a dental cement made from zinc oxide and phosphoric acid, widely used for securing metal restorations and as a base in dental restorations
type of dental cement
medical

Examples

The dentist selected zinc phosphate dental cement to attach the permanent crown securely.
One advantage of zinc phosphate dental cement is its long history of reliable use in restorative dentistry.
When mixing zinc phosphate dental cement, it's important to achieve the right consistency for proper adhesion.

Synset Examples

Zinc phosphate cement is used for cementation of inlays, crowns, bridges, and orthodontic appliances and occasionally as a temporary restoration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dental cement - substance used in dentistry for securing crowns, fillings, or appliances
