bird vetch

1. NOUN

/ bɝd vɛtʃ / (AmE), / bɜːd vɛtʃ / (BrE)
a widespread perennial climbing plant of temperate Eurasia and North America, with dense clusters of bluish-purple flowers
a climbing plant with bluish-purple flowers

Examples

The field was covered in bird vetch, creating a patchwork of purple flowers.
Gardeners often use bird vetch as ground cover due to its vigorous growth.
Pollinators are attracted to the clusters of bird vetch in early summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vetch - a climbing plant of the pea family
