Second Epistle to Timothy

1. PROPN

/ ˈsɛkənd ɪˈpɪsəl tu ˈtɪməθi / (AmE), / ˈsɛkənd ɪˈpɪsl tuː ˈtɪməθi / (BrE)
the eighteenth book of the New Testament, a letter from Saint Paul to Timothy offering advice on pastoral matters and Christian leadership
New Testament book, Paul's second letter to Timothy

Examples

The Second Epistle to Timothy offers personal advice to its young recipient about leadership and faith.
In his sermon, the pastor referenced the Second Epistle to Timothy to emphasize the importance of endurance.
Scholars often study the Second Epistle to Timothy to gain insight into early Christian ministry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: First Epistle to Timothy - New Testament book, Paul's first letter to Timothy

Etymology

‘Epistle’ from Latin epistola, from Greek epistolē (letter). ‘Timothy’ from Greek Timotheos.
