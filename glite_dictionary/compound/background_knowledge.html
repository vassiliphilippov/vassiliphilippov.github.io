background knowledge

1. NOUN

/ ˈbækˌɡraʊnd ˈnɑlɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈbækɡraʊnd ˈnɒlɪdʒ / (BrE)
information or understanding gained from previous experience that helps in comprehending new situations, problems, or texts
information from experience aiding understanding

Examples

Having strong background knowledge in biology helped her understand the medical article more easily.
Teachers often activate students’ background knowledge before introducing a new topic in class.
Lack of background knowledge can make a news story difficult to follow.

Synset Examples

the embassy filled him in on the background of the incident

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prior knowledge - knowledge that someone already possesses before learning new information
