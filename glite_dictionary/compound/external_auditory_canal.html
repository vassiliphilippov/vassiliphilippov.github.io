external auditory canal

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˈstɝːnəl ˈɔdəˌtɔɹi kəˈnæl / (AmE), / ɪkˈstɜːnəl ˈɔːdɪtəri kəˈnæl / (BrE)
the tube-like passage in the outer ear leading from the auricle (outer ear) to the tympanic membrane (eardrum)
tube in the outer ear leading to the eardrum
medical

Examples

The doctor examined my external auditory canal to check for signs of infection.
Wax buildup can sometimes block the external auditory canal and affect hearing.
Injuries to the external auditory canal should be treated carefully to prevent damage to the eardrum.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ear canal - the passage leading from the outer ear to the eardrum

Etymology

auditory from Latin audīre (to hear); canal from Latin canālis (channel, pipe)
