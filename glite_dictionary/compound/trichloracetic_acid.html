trichloracetic acid

1. NOUN

/ traɪˌklɔːrəˈsiːtɪk ˌæsɪd / (AmE), / traɪˌklɒrəˈsiːtɪk ˌæsɪd / (BrE)
a strong, colorless crystalline acid (C2HCl3O2) produced by chlorinating acetic acid, used in biochemistry and medicine
a strong acid used in science and medicine
medical

Examples

Protein precipitation can be achieved using trichloracetic acid during laboratory procedures.
The dermatologist applied trichloracetic acid to remove the patient's wart.
Safety precautions must be observed when working with trichloracetic acid due to its corrosive nature.

Etymology

From 'tri-' meaning three, 'chloro' for chlorine, and 'acetic acid'.
