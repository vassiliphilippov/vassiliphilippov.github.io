oxybutyric acid

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑksɪbjuˈtɪrɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / ˌɒksɪbjuːˈtɪərɪk ˈasɪd / (BrE)
Any of several hydroxy derivatives of butyric acid, often found in biological or chemical contexts.
hydroxy derivative of butyric acid
medical

Examples

The presence of oxybutyric acid in the urine may indicate metabolic disorders.
Researchers studied the effects of oxybutyric acid on brain function.
One form of oxybutyric acid is commonly found in certain fermented foods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: butyric acid - a fatty acid present in butter and body odor

Etymology

From Greek 'oxys' (sharp, acidic) and 'butyric acid', relating to the acid's composition.
