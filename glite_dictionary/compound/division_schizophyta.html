division Schizophyta

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈvɪʒən skɪˈzɒfɪtə / (AmE), / dɪˈvɪʒən skɪˈzɒfɪtə / (BrE)
a former taxonomic division of simple, bacteria-like organisms, now more commonly referred to as Cyanophyta or blue-green algae.
obsolete biological division (Cyanophyta)
medical

Examples

In older textbooks, blue-green algae were classified under the division Schizophyta.
The name division Schizophyta is now considered obsolete in modern biological taxonomy.
Researchers discovered that the organisms once placed in division Schizophyta belonged to various lineages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cyanophyta - group of blue-green algae, the modern equivalent classification

Etymology

From Greek 'schizo-' (split) and 'phyton' (plant), used in former biological classifications.
