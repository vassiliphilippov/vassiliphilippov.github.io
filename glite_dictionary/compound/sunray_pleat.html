sunray pleat

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌnˌreɪ ˌpliːt / (AmE), / ˈsʌnreɪ ˌpliːt / (BrE)
A knife pleat cut on the bias to create a flared, radiating effect in fabric, resembling rays of the sun.
flared, radiating pleat in fabric

Examples

The skirt featured elegant sunray pleats that shimmered as she moved.
Designers often use sunray pleats to add volume and visual interest to dresses.
The fabric must be carefully cut on the bias to achieve perfect sunray pleats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: knife pleat - a narrow, sharply pressed pleat typically all facing the same direction
