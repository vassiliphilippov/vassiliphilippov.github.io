binocular microscope

1. NOUN

/ bəˈnɑkjələr ˈmaɪkrəˌskoʊp / (AmE), / bɪˈnɒkjʊlə ˈmaɪkrəskəʊp / (BrE)
a light microscope designed to be used with both eyes, providing a more comfortable and natural viewing experience
microscope for both eyes

Examples

The laboratory is equipped with a binocular microscope for detailed biological studies.
Using a binocular microscope reduces eye strain during prolonged examinations.
She adjusted the focus of the binocular microscope to get a clearer view of the cell structure.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: monocular microscope - a microscope designed for use with one eye only

Etymology

From Latin 'binoculus' (having two eyes) and Greek 'micron' (small) + 'skopein' (to look).
