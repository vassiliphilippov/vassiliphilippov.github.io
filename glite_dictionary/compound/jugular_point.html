jugular point

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒʌɡjələr pɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌɡjʊlə pɔɪnt / (BrE)
A crucial or vulnerable aspect at which something can be most easily attacked, defeated, or compromised.
crucial or vulnerable aspect

Examples

The investigative reporter targeted the company's jugular point by revealing key emails.
Their financial stability was the jugular point that competitors tried to exploit.
The defense attorney struck the prosecution's jugular point during cross-examination.

Synset Examples

The documentary exposed the jugular point of corruption.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weak spot - a place or aspect where someone or something is vulnerable
  • Synonym: Achilles' heel - a particular weakness despite overall strength
  • Antonym: strength - the quality or state of being strong

Etymology

From 'jugular', relating to the throat or neck, metaphorically meaning a vulnerable point.
