biquadratic polynomial

1. NOUN

/ ˌbaɪ.kwəˈdræt.ɪk pəˈlɪn.iˌoʊ.mi.əl / (AmE), / ˌbaɪ.kwəˈdræt.ɪk pəˈlɪn.iˌəʊ.mi.əl / (BrE)
a polynomial of the fourth degree, having the highest exponent of four
polynomial of degree four

Examples

The equation can be rewritten as a biquadratic polynomial for easier factoring.
Solving a biquadratic polynomial often requires specialized algebraic techniques.
In mathematics, a biquadratic polynomial has the general form ax⁴ + bx³ + cx² + dx + e.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quartic polynomial - a polynomial of degree four; another term for biquadratic polynomial

Etymology

From Latin 'bi-' (twice) + 'quadratus' (squared), and 'polynomial' from Greek 'poly' (many) + 'nomial' (term).
