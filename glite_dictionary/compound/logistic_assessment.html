logistic assessment

1. NOUN

/ ləˈdʒɪstɪk əˈsɛsmənt / (AmE), / ləˈdʒɪstɪk əˈsɛsmənt / (BrE)
an evaluation of the logistical support and requirements needed for a specific military operation or mission
evaluation of logistical needs for a military operation

Examples

The commander ordered a logistic assessment before approving the next phase of the operation.
A full logistic assessment revealed a shortage of medical supplies and fuel.
Conducting a thorough logistic assessment is essential to ensure mission success in hostile environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: logistics - the detailed organization and implementation of a complex operation
  • Synonym: military planning - the process of planning military operations and needs

Etymology

From 'logistic', relating to logistics (from French logistique, from loger 'to lodge'), and 'assessment', from Latin assessare 'to sit by as an assistant judge'.
