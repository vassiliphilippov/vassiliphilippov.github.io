femoral artery

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɛmərəl ˈɑrti / (AmE), / ˈfɛmərəl ˈɑːti / (BrE)
the main artery of the thigh, continuing from the external iliac artery and supplying blood to the lower limb
chief artery of the thigh
medical

Examples

The surgeon carefully repaired the damaged femoral artery during the emergency operation.
A blockage in the femoral artery can reduce blood flow to the leg and cause pain.
The nurse checked for a pulse in the patient's femoral artery after the accident.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: external iliac artery - the artery that becomes the femoral artery after passing under the inguinal ligament
  • Degree Higher: popliteal artery - the continuation of the femoral artery behind the knee

Etymology

From Latin 'femoralis' (of the thigh) and 'arteria' (artery).
