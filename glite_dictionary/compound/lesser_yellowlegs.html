lesser yellowlegs

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛsər ˈjɛloʊˌlɛɡz / (AmE), / ˈlɛsə ˈjɛləʊˌlɛɡz / (BrE)
a small North American shorebird (Tringa flavipes) with long yellow legs, closely related to the greater yellowlegs
small North American shorebird

Examples

We spotted a lesser yellowlegs feeding along the muddy shoreline.
During migration, flocks of lesser yellowlegs rest in wetlands across the Midwest.
The lesser yellowlegs can be distinguished from its larger relative by its slightly shorter bill and body size.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: greater yellowlegs - a larger related North American shorebird (Tringa melanoleuca)

Etymology

From 'yellowlegs' (referring to its yellow legs) with 'lesser' to distinguish it from the 'greater yellowlegs'.
