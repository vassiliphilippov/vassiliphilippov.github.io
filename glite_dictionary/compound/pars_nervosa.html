pars nervosa

1. NOUN

/ pɑrz nɚˈvoʊsə / (AmE), / pɑːz nɜːˈvəʊsə / (BrE)
the posterior lobe of the pituitary gland, which is mainly glandular and involved in hormone storage and release
posterior lobe of the pituitary gland
medical

Examples

Damage to the pars nervosa can affect hormone release in the body.
The pars nervosa is responsible for storing and releasing neurohormones produced in the hypothalamus.
In MRI scans, abnormalities in the pars nervosa may indicate pituitary gland disorders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neurohypophysis - the posterior part of the pituitary gland

Etymology

From Latin 'pars' (part) and 'nervosa' (nervous), referring to its association with nerve fibers.
