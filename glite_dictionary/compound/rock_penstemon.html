rock penstemon

1. NOUN

/ rɑk ˈpɛnstəˌmɑn / (AmE), / rɒk ˈpɛnstəˌmɒn / (BrE)
a wildflower of western North America, noted for its large, bright pink or rose flowers that grow in clusters above dense mats of foliage, commonly found on ledges and cliffs
a wildflower with clusters of pink flowers native to western North America

Examples

The rock penstemon adds a splash of color to the cliffs every spring.
Gardeners prize the rock penstemon for its hardiness and vivid blossoms.
We found several rock penstemons growing among the rocky ledges on our hike.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Penstemon - a large genus of North American flowering plants with tubular flowers

Etymology

From 'rock' (referring to its cliffside habitat) and 'penstemon' (from Greek, meaning 'five stamens').
