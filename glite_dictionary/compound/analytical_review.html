analytical review

1. NOUN

/ əˌnælɪˈtɪkəl rɪˈvjuː / (AmE), / əˌnælɪˈtɪkəl rɪˈvjuː / (BrE)
an auditing procedure that examines relationships among financial data to identify significant changes or inconsistencies.
auditing method using financial ratios

Examples

The auditor performed an analytical review of the company's revenue figures before issuing the report.
During the analytical review, several unusual fluctuations in expenses were identified.
An analytical review is often used to highlight potential problem areas that require further investigation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: audit - official inspection of financial accounts
