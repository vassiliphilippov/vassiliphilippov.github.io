gallows-tree

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡæloʊzˌtri / (AmE), / ˈɡaləʊzˌtriː / (BrE)
A wooden structure used for execution by hanging; another term for gallows, mainly historical or literary.
structure for hanging executions

Examples

The condemned man was led to the gallows-tree at dawn, watched by a silent crowd.
Villagers gathered nervously near the old gallows-tree on the outskirts of town.
Many stories from the era mention the gallows-tree as a place of both fear and justice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gallows - a structure for execution by hanging
