human gamma globulin

1. NOUN

/ ˈhjuːmən ˈɡæmə ɡlɑːˌbjuːlɪn / (AmE), / ˈhjuːmən ˈɡæmə ˈɡlɒbjʊlɪn / (BrE)
a plasma protein containing immunoglobulins responsible for immune responses in humans
protein with immune function in humans
medical

Examples

Human gamma globulin is often administered to patients with immune deficiencies.
The injection contained purified human gamma globulin to provide temporary immunity.
Researchers studied the effects of human gamma globulin in treating viral infections.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: immunoglobulin - any of a class of proteins functioning as antibodies

Etymology

From Latin 'gamma' (the third letter of the Greek alphabet) and 'globulin' (a type of globular protein).
