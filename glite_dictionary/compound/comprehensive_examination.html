comprehensive examination

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːmprəˈhɛnsɪv ˌɛɡˌzæməˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒmprɪˈhɛnsɪv ˌɛɡˌzæmɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
a major examination that tests a student's knowledge and mastery of a particular subject, often required in higher education before advancing to candidacy or completing a degree
major academic proficiency test

Examples

Graduate students must pass a comprehensive examination before beginning their research projects.
The comprehensive examination covers all major areas of the subject, not just one specific topic.
Many universities require a comprehensive examination as part of their doctoral programs.

Synset Examples

she took her comps in English literature

Related Terms

  • Synonym: final examination - the last exam in a course or academic program
