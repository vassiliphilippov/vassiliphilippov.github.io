genus Zerumbet

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs zəˈrʌmbɛt / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs zɛˈrʌmbɛt / (BrE)
A genus of perennial, rhizomatous herbs native to Asia, Australia, and Polynesia, with ginger-scented rhizomes.
genus of ginger-scented herbs

Examples

The genus Zerumbet is known for its aromatic, ginger-like rhizomes used in traditional medicine.
Several species within the genus Zerumbet are cultivated for their attractive flowers and fragrance.
Botanists study the genus Zerumbet to understand its role in the ecosystems of Southeast Asia.

Etymology

From New Latin genus name 'Zerumbet', used in botanical classification.
