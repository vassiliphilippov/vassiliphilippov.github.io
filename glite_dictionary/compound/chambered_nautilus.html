chambered nautilus

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃeɪmbərd ˈnɔtɪləs / (AmE), / ˈtʃeɪmbəd ˈnɔːtɪləs / (BrE)
a cephalopod mollusk of the Indian and Pacific oceans with a spiral shell divided into internal chambers, noted for its beautiful pearly partitions
spiral-shelled cephalopod mollusk

Examples

The chambered nautilus drifted slowly through the clear blue waters, its spiral shell gleaming in the sunlight.
Oliver admired the exquisite chambered nautilus he found on the beach, fascinated by its pearly partitions.
Many marine biologists study the chambered nautilus to learn more about the evolution of cephalopods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nautilus - a cephalopod mollusk with a spiral shell
