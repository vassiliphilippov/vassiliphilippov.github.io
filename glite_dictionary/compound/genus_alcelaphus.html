genus Alcelaphus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ælˈsɛləfəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ælˈsɛləfəs / (BrE)
A genus of large African antelopes known as hartebeests, characterized by long faces and distinctive horns.
a genus of African antelopes (hartebeests)

Examples

The genus Alcelaphus includes several species of hartebeests found across Africa.
Scientists have studied the migration patterns of animals within the genus Alcelaphus.
Members of the genus Alcelaphus are notable for their elongated heads and curved horns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hartebeest - a large African antelope with a sloping back and ringed horns

Etymology

From Latin genus (kind) + New Latin Alcelaphus (from Greek alkē, elk + lēophos, antelope type).
