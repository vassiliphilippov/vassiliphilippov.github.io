Latter-Day Saint

1. NOUN

/ ˌlæt̬ɚ deɪ ˈseɪnt / (AmE), / ˌlætə deɪ ˈseɪnt / (BrE)
A member of the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-Day Saints, commonly known as the Mormon Church.
member of the Mormon Church

Examples

Her grandmother has been a devoted Latter-Day Saint for more than forty years.
Many Latter-Day Saints participate in community service as part of their faith.
He learned about the beliefs of the Latter-Day Saint church during his trip to Utah.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mormon - A member of the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-Day Saints

Etymology

From the official name of the church, 'The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-Day Saints', founded in the United States in the 19th century.
