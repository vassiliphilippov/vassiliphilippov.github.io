suborder Manteodea

1. NOUN

/ sʌbˌɔrdɚ mænˌtiːəˈdiːə / (AmE), / sʌbˌɔːdə mænˌtiːəˈdiːə / (BrE)
A taxonomic group that comprises the mantises; formerly considered a suborder of Orthoptera.
taxonomic group for mantises

Examples

The suborder Manteodea includes over 2,400 species of mantises found worldwide.
In older taxonomy, the suborder Manteodea was classified within the Orthoptera order.
Scientists debate the evolutionary relationship between the suborder Manteodea and other insect groups.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mantis - an insect of the order or suborder Manteodea, known for its upright posture and grasping forelegs

Etymology

From New Latin 'Manteodea', from Greek 'mantis' (prophet, seer), referring to the typical posture of these insects.
