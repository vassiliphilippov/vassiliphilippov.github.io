harmonic progression

1. NOUN

/ hɑrˈmɑnɪk prəˈɡrɛʃən / (AmE), / hɑːˈmɒnɪk prəˈɡrɛʃən / (BrE)
(Mathematics) A sequence of numbers in which the reciprocals of the terms form an arithmetic progression.
arithmetic sequence in reciprocals (math)

Examples

In a harmonic progression, the reciprocals of the terms increase by a constant difference.
Mathematicians often compare an harmonic progression with an arithmetic progression to highlight their differences.
The concept of harmonic progression is essential in the study of series and sequences.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arithmetic progression - a sequence of numbers with a constant difference between consecutive terms

Etymology

From Latin harmonicus, from Greek harmonikos (harmonic) and progression from Latin progressio (advance).
