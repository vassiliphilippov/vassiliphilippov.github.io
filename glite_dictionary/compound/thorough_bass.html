thorough bass

1. NOUN

/ ˈθɜroʊ beɪs / (AmE), / ˈθʌrə beɪs / (BrE)
A bass line in musical notation written with figures to indicate the accompanying harmonies to be played, especially in Baroque music; also called figured bass.
Baroque music bass line with chord figures

Examples

The composer relied on thorough bass to guide the continuo players in his ensemble.
She studied how to interpret thorough bass as part of her Baroque music training.
The manual explained the conventions of writing and reading thorough bass in 18th-century scores.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: figured bass - A musical notation using numbers to indicate chords above a bass line

Etymology

From German 'Generalbass'; 'thorough' used in the sense of 'complete' or 'comprehensive'.
