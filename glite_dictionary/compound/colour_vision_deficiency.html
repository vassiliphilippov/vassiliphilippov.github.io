colour vision deficiency

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌlɚ ˈvɪʒən dɪˈfɪʃənsi / (AmE), / ˈkʌlə ˈvɪʒən dɪˈfɪʃənsi / (BrE)
a genetic or acquired condition in which a person is unable to distinguish certain colours or hues accurately; commonly known as colour blindness
inability to distinguish colours (colour blindness)
medical british

Examples

People with colour vision deficiency may have difficulty distinguishing between red and green traffic lights.
Schools sometimes provide special resources for students diagnosed with colour vision deficiency.
He was unaware of his colour vision deficiency until he took a routine eye test.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: color blindness - inability to perceive differences between some colours
