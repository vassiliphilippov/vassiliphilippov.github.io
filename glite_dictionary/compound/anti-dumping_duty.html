anti-dumping duty

1. NOUN

/ ˈæn.ti ˌdʌm.pɪŋ ˈduː.ti / (AmE), / ˈæn.ti ˌdʌm.pɪŋ ˈdjuː.ti / (BrE)
a tariff imposed by a government on imported goods believed to be priced below fair market value, intended to protect domestic industries from unfair competition
tariff against unfairly cheap imports
legal

Examples

The government imposed an anti-dumping duty on imported steel to protect local manufacturers.
Several countries have threatened to introduce an anti-dumping duty on Chinese solar panels.
The new anti-dumping duties were announced after a lengthy investigation into pricing practices.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tariff - a tax imposed on imported or exported goods

Etymology

From 'anti-' (against) + 'dumping' (selling goods at unfairly low prices) + 'duty' (tax).
