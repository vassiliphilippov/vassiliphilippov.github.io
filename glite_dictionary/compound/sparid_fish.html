sparid fish

1. NOUN

/ ˈspɛrɪd fɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈspærɪd fɪʃ / (BrE)
A spiny-finned food fish of warm waters, characterized by well-developed teeth, belonging to the family Sparidae.
spiny-finned food fish of the Sparidae family

Examples

Fishermen often catch sparid fish off the rocky coasts of the Mediterranean.
The chef prepared a dish featuring freshly grilled sparid fish and seasonal vegetables.
Scientists classify the porgy as a type of sparid fish due to its distinctive jaw structure.

Etymology

From Modern Latin 'Sparidae', the fish family name, derived from Greek 'sparos' (a kind of fish).
