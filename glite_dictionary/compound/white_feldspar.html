white feldspar

1. NOUN

/ waɪt ˈfɛldˌspɑr / (AmE), / waɪt ˈfɛldˌspɑː / (BrE)
a commonly occurring feldspar mineral, typically white in color, that is a major component of many rocks
a white-colored feldspar mineral

Examples

Granite is often composed of white feldspar, quartz, and small amounts of mica.
Geologists identified the shiny crystals as white feldspar embedded in the rock sample.
The countertops are made from a stone rich in white feldspar, giving them a bright, clean appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: feldspar - a group of rock-forming minerals
