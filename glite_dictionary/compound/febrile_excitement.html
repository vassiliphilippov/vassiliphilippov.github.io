febrile excitement

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɛbraɪl ɪkˈsaɪtmənt / (AmE), / ˈfiːbraɪl ɪkˈsaɪtmənt / (BrE)
a state of intense and feverish enthusiasm or agitation
intense, feverish enthusiasm

Examples

The news of her promotion sent waves of febrile excitement through the entire department.
There was a sense of febrile excitement in the air as election results began to come in.
His febrile excitement over the project sometimes bordered on obsession.

Synset Examples

His febrile excitement was contagious at the party.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: excitement - a feeling of great enthusiasm and eagerness

Etymology

From Latin 'febris' (fever) via French 'fébrile', combined with 'excitement'.
