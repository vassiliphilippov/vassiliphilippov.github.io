devil's darning needle

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛvəlz ˈdɑrnɪŋ ˌniːdəl / (AmE), / ˈdɛvəlz ˈdɑːnɪŋ ˌniːdəl / (BrE)
A common climbing plant of eastern North America (Clematis virginiana) that sprawls over other plants and produces panicles of small creamy white flowers.
climbing plant with creamy white flowers

Examples

The hedgerow was covered in devil's darning needle, its flowers bright against the green leaves.
Gardeners often appreciate devil's darning needle for its ability to attract butterflies and bees.
He mistakenly took the devil's darning needle for a vine until it began to bloom in late summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: virgin's bower - another name for Clematis virginiana, the same climbing plant

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɛvəlz ˈdɑrnɪŋ ˈniːdəl / (AmE), / ˈdɛvəlz ˈdɑːnɪŋ ˈniːdl / (BrE)
A dragonfly; a slender-bodied insect with iridescent wings, often seen near water, whose adults and nymphs feed on mosquitoes and other small insects.
folk name for dragonfly (insect)

Examples

The children watched a devil's darning needle hover above the pond.
Folklore warns of the devil's darning needle, but it is harmless to humans.
Many people are fascinated by the shimmering wings of the devil's darning needle in summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dragonfly - slender-bodied, large-eyed insect with two pairs of transparent wings; common type of flying insect

Etymology

Refers to a folk belief that dragonflies would sew up the mouths of mischievous children, hence the 'darning needle'.
