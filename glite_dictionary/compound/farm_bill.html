farm bill

1. NOUN

/ fɑrm bɪl / (AmE), / fɑːm bɪl / (BrE)
a statute or legislative act regulating agricultural production and prices, especially in the United States
law regulating agricultural production and prices

Examples

The new farm bill provides subsidies for small-scale farmers.
Debates over the farm bill often center on its impact on food prices.
Congress is expected to pass the farm bill by the end of the year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agricultural policy - government policies affecting agriculture
