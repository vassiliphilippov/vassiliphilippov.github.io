double talk

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʌbəl tɑk / (AmE), / ˈdʌbəl tɔːk / (BrE)
deliberately ambiguous, confusing, or unintelligible language, often used to deceive or evade understanding
deliberately unintelligible or evasive language

Examples

The politician's response was just double talk to avoid answering the question directly.
During the interview, he used double talk to confuse reporters and dodge accountability.
I can't make sense of all this double talk in the legal document.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gibberish - unintelligible or nonsensical language
  • Synonym: nonsense - speech or writing with no meaning or sense
