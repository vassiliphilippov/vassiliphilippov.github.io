vascular hemophilia

1. NOUN

/ ˈvæskjələr hɪˌmɒˈfiliə / (AmE), / ˈvæskjʊlə hɪˌməʊˈfiːlɪə / (BrE)
a rare inherited bleeding disorder caused by a deficiency of a coagulation factor, leading to prolonged mucosal bleeding; also known as von Willebrand disease.
a rare inherited bleeding disorder
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed her with vascular hemophilia after repeated episodes of nosebleeds and easy bruising.
Unlike classic hemophilia, vascular hemophilia often presents with bleeding from the mucous membranes.
Genetic testing can confirm a case of vascular hemophilia in patients with unexplained prolonged bleeding.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: von Willebrand disease - a genetic bleeding disorder involving coagulation factor deficiency
  • Degree Higher: hemophilia - an inherited bleeding disorder due to deficient blood clotting factors

Etymology

Named for its vascular (blood vessel) symptoms and similarity to hemophilia; also called von Willebrand disease after Erik von Willebrand.
