demand loan

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈmænd loʊn / (AmE), / dɪˈmɑːnd ləʊn / (BrE)
A loan that must be repaid in full whenever the lender demands repayment, rather than on a fixed schedule.
loan repayable on demand

Examples

The bank has the right to recall a demand loan at any time.
She preferred a fixed-term loan rather than a demand loan for her business.
If you take out a demand loan, you must be ready to repay it on short notice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: call loan - a loan that can be called for repayment at any time
