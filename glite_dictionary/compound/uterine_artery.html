uterine artery

1. NOUN

/ ˈjuːtərɪn ˈɑːr.t̬ə.ri / (AmE), / ˈjuːtəriːn ˈɑː.tə.ri / (BrE)
a blood vessel that branches from the internal iliac artery to supply blood to the uterus and the upper part of the vagina
artery supplying the uterus
medical

Examples

The surgeon carefully clamped the uterine artery during the procedure to control bleeding.
Blockage of the uterine artery can lead to complications during pregnancy.
An ultrasound can assess blood flow in the uterine artery to monitor fetal health.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: artery - a blood vessel that carries blood away from the heart

Etymology

From Latin 'uterinus' (of the womb) and Greek 'arteria' (windpipe, artery).
