tempest-tossed

1. ADJ

/ ˈtɛmpəst tɔst / (AmE), / ˈtɛmpɪst tɒst / (BrE)
having been violently tossed or buffeted by storms or, figuratively, by great turmoil or difficulty.
buffeted by storms or turmoil

Examples

The tempest-tossed vessel struggled to stay afloat through the night.
Her life had been so tempest-tossed that she treasured every moment of peace.
They offered refuge to the tempest-tossed refugees seeking safety from war.

Synset Examples

The tempest-tossed ship finally reached the shore.
Life can be tempest-tossed, but we must persevere.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: storm-tossed - driven or tossed about by storms

Etymology

From 'tempest' (violent storm) and past participle 'tossed'. Notably used in Emma Lazarus's poem 'The New Colossus' (1883).
