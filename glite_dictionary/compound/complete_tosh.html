complete tosh

1. NOUN

/ kəmˈplit tɑʃ / (AmE), / kəmˈpliːt tɒʃ / (BrE)
Utter nonsense; something that is completely untrue or worthless.
utter nonsense; rubbish (British informal)
informal british

Examples

Most of what he said was complete tosh and everyone knew it.
Don't listen to that story—it's complete tosh from start to finish.
The tabloid's front page was filled with complete tosh as usual.

Synset Examples

She dismissed the rumor as complete tosh.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tosh - nonsense; rubbish (British informal)
  • Synonym: to utter nonsense - complete nonsense; something entirely false or worthless
