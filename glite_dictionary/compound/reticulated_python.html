reticulated python

1. NOUN

/ rəˈtɪkjəˌleɪtɪd ˈpaɪθɑn / (AmE), / rɪˈtɪkjʊleɪtɪd ˈpʌɪθən / (BrE)
a very large, nonvenomous snake of southeast Asia and the East Indies, known for its net-like skin pattern; the longest snake species in the world
very large Asian snake; world's longest

Examples

The reticulated python can reach lengths of over twenty feet, making it the world's longest snake.
Zookeepers must use special techniques to safely handle the massive reticulated python.
A reticulated python was discovered in the wilds of Indonesia, fascinating both scientists and locals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: python - large, nonvenomous constrictor snake

Etymology

‘Reticulated’ from Latin ‘reticulatus’ meaning ‘net-like’; refers to the snake’s patterned skin.
