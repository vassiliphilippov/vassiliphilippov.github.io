family Phalacrocoracidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌfæmɪli fəˌlækrəˌkɔrəˈkeɪdiː / (AmE), / ˌfæmɪli fəˌlækrəkɒrəˈkeɪdɪiː / (BrE)
A family of seabirds known as cormorants, characterized by their dark plumage, hooked bills, and aquatic habits.
taxonomic family of cormorants (seabirds)

Examples

The family Phalacrocoracidae includes about 40 species of cormorants worldwide.
Research on the family Phalacrocoracidae has revealed unique diving adaptations among its members.
Ornithologists debated the evolutionary origins of the family Phalacrocoracidae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cormorant - a large diving seabird with a long neck and hooked bill

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Greek 'phalakros' (bald) and 'korax' (raven), referencing the appearance of the birds.
