family Cannaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ˌkænəˈsiː.iː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli ˌkænəˈsiː.iː / (BrE)
A family of flowering plants whose only genus is Canna, including ornamental species grown for their large leaves and showy flowers.
plant family including the genus Canna

Examples

The family Cannaceae contains only a single genus, Canna, known for showy ornamental plants.
Several species in the family Cannaceae are cultivated in gardens worldwide.
Research on the family Cannaceae has focused on flower morphology and hybridization.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Canna - genus of tropical flowering plants known as cannas

Etymology

From modern Latin 'Cannaceae', based on the genus Canna.
