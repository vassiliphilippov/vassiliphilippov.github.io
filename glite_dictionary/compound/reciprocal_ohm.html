reciprocal ohm

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈsɪprəkəl oʊm / (AmE), / rɪˈsɪprəkəl əʊm / (BrE)
a unit of electrical conductance equal to the reciprocal of an ohm; also known as a siemens
unit of electrical conductance (1/ohm)
medical

Examples

The old circuit diagrams listed conductance in reciprocal ohm units.
One reciprocal ohm is now officially called a siemens, but both names mean the same thing.
Instrument readings in reciprocal ohms are less common in modern electrical engineering.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: siemens - the SI unit of electrical conductance, equivalent to a reciprocal ohm
  • Antonym: ohm - the SI unit of electrical resistance

Etymology

From 'reciprocal' (mutual, inverse) and 'ohm', the SI unit of electrical resistance.
