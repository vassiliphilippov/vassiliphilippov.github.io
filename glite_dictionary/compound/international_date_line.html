International Date Line

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪntɚˈnæʃənəl ˈdeɪt laɪn / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈnæʃən(ə)l ˈdeɪt laɪn / (BrE)
an imaginary line on the Earth's surface, roughly along the 180th meridian, where the date changes by one day when crossed.
imaginary line where the date changes

Examples

When you cross the International Date Line from east to west, you add a day to your calendar.
Flights between Asia and North America often cross the International Date Line.
Sailors pay close attention to the International Date Line to record the correct date in their logs.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: prime meridian - the planet's line of 0 degrees longitude, from which east and west are measured
