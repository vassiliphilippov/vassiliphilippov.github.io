germ tube

1. NOUN

/ dʒɝm tuːb / (AmE), / dʒɜːm tjuːb / (BrE)
a slender tubular outgrowth that emerges from a spore during germination, especially in fungi and some plants
tubular outgrowth from a germinating spore

Examples

The germ tube extends rapidly from the spore, allowing the fungus to invade new tissues.
Under the microscope, germ tubes are easily seen forming from the surface of the spores.
Environmental conditions such as humidity affect the development of the germ tube in many fungal species.
