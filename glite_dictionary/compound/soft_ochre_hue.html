soft ochre hue

1. NOUN

/ sɑft ˈoʊkər hju / (AmE), / sɒft ˈəʊkə hjuː / (BrE)
a light and gentle yellow-brown color resembling ochre
light yellow-brown color

Examples

The artist chose a soft ochre hue for the walls to create a warm, inviting atmosphere.
Her dress shimmered in the sunlight, its soft ochre hue catching every eye at the party.
We repainted the living room in a soft ochre hue that reminded us of golden sand.

Synset Examples

His house was painted in a soft ochre hue.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: ochre - a moderate yellow-orange to orange color

Etymology

Ochre from Greek 'ōkhra' (pale yellow); 'soft' refers to a gentle or pale quality.
