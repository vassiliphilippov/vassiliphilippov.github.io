urethral orifice

1. NOUN

/ jʊˈriːθrəl ɔːrˈɪfɪs / (AmE), / jʊəˈriːθrəl ɒˈrɪfɪs / (BrE)
the opening through which urine is discharged from the body
the opening for urine discharge
medical

Examples

The doctor examined the urethral orifice for signs of infection.
A blockage at the urethral orifice can cause difficulty in urination.
Care should be taken during catheterization to avoid injuring the urethral orifice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meatus - the passage or opening of the body, especially the urethral orifice

Etymology

From Greek 'ourēthra' (urinary passage) and Latin 'orificium' (opening).
