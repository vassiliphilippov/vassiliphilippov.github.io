doom mongering

1. NOUN

/ duːm ˈmʌŋɚɪŋ / (AmE), / duːm ˈmʌŋə.rɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of promoting pessimistic or negative predictions, especially about the future
promoting pessimistic predictions
informal

Examples

Some media outlets have been accused of doom mongering when reporting on economic issues.
His speech was criticized for its doom mongering about climate change.
Instead of doom mongering, we should focus on positive actions we can take.

Synset Examples

She was tired of his constant doom mongering.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scaremongering - promoting exaggerated fears
  • Antonym: optimism - hopefulness and confidence about the future

Etymology

Formed from 'doom' (fate or ruin) and 'monger' (seller or promoter), similar to 'scaremongering'.
