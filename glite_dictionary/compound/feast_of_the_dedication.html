Feast of the Dedication

1. NOUN

/ ˈfiːst əv ðə ˌdɛdɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈfiːst əv ðə ˌdɛdɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
(Judaism) an eight-day Jewish festival, also known as Hanukkah, commemorating the rededication of the Temple in Jerusalem in 165 BC.
Jewish festival commemorating the Temple's rededication

Examples

Jewish families around the world celebrate the Feast of the Dedication each winter.
The Feast of the Dedication commemorates an important event in Jewish history.
He lit candles every night during the Feast of the Dedication to honor the tradition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hanukkah - Jewish festival celebrating the rededication of the Temple
