tongue-fish

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʌŋˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈtʌŋˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
A small, left-eyed marine flatfish with a tapered tail, of little commercial value.
a type of small marine flatfish

Examples

The tongue-fish is often found on sandy ocean bottoms in shallow waters.
Scientists collected several tongue-fish specimens during their marine survey.
Although the tongue-fish is not commonly eaten, it plays an important role in the ecosystem.
