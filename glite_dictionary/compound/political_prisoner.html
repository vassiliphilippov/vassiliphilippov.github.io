political prisoner

1. NOUN

/ pəˈlɪdɪkəl ˈprɪzənər / (AmE), / pəˈlɪtɪkəl ˈprɪzənə / (BrE)
a person who is imprisoned because of their political beliefs or activities, rather than for committing a common crime
person jailed for their political beliefs

Examples

The government denied that it held any political prisoners in its jails.
He became a famous political prisoner after being arrested for protesting against the regime.
Human rights groups campaigned for the release of all political prisoners.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dissident - a person who opposes official policy, especially that of an authoritarian state
