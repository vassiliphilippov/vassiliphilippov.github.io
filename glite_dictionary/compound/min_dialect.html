Min dialect

1. NOUN

/ mɪn ˈdaɪ.əˌlɛkt / (AmE), / mɪn ˈdaɪ.ə.lɛkt / (BrE)
any of several Chinese language varieties spoken mainly in Fujian province and neighboring areas of southeastern China
Chinese language group of southeastern China

Examples

Many immigrants from Fujian speak the Min dialect at home.
The Min dialect has several distinct subgroups, including Hokkien and Teochew.
Linguists study the Min dialect to trace ancient features of the Chinese language.

Etymology

From the name of the Min River in Fujian, China, which gives its name to the Min linguistic group.
