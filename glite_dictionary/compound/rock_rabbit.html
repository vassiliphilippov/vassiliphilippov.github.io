rock rabbit

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɑk ˌræbɪt / (AmE), / ˈrɒk ˌræbɪt / (BrE)
A small, shy mammal (hyrax) found in rocky areas of Africa and the Middle East, resembling a rabbit but not closely related.
small mammal (hyrax) of rocky areas

Examples

We spotted a rock rabbit hiding among the boulders during our hike.
The rock rabbit is not a true rabbit, despite its name and appearance.
Many predators in the savanna prey on the rock rabbit for food.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hyrax - small herbivorous mammal found in Africa and the Middle East

2. NOUN

/ rɑk ˈræbɪt / (AmE), / rɒk ˈræbɪt / (BrE)
a small, short-eared, burrowing mammal found in rocky uplands of Asia and western North America, also known as a pika
a small mammal found in rocky uplands (pika)

Examples

We spotted a rock rabbit darting between the boulders during our mountain hike.
The rock rabbit is well adapted to cold, rocky environments and has a distinctive whistling call.
Unlike true rabbits, the rock rabbit has shorter ears and is related to pikas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pika - a small, rabbit-like mammal living in rocky uplands
  • Degree Higher: hare - a larger, long-eared relative of rabbits
  • Degree Higher: rabbit - a similar but more common burrowing mammal
