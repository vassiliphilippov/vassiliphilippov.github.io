inorganic chemistry

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪnɔːrˈɡænɪk ˈkɛmɪstri / (AmE), / ˌɪnɔːˈɡænɪk ˈkɛmɪstri / (BrE)
the branch of chemistry concerned with the properties and behavior of inorganic compounds, typically those not containing hydrocarbon groups
branch of chemistry for non-organic compounds

Examples

She decided to specialize in inorganic chemistry after discovering her interest in metals and minerals.
The study of salts and minerals is a key part of inorganic chemistry.
Most first-year university students take both organic and inorganic chemistry courses.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: organic chemistry - branch of chemistry concerning carbon-based compounds
