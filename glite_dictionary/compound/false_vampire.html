false vampire

1. NOUN

/ fɔls ˈvæmpaɪər / (AmE), / fɔːls ˈvæmpaɪə / (BrE)
a large carnivorous bat found in the Old or New World, once mistakenly believed to suck blood but actually feeding on insects
large insect-eating bat, not a true vampire bat

Examples

The false vampire roosts in caves and old buildings, hunting insects at night.
Despite its name, the false vampire does not drink blood.
Researchers studied several false vampire colonies to understand their feeding behavior.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: vampire bat - A bat that feeds on the blood of animals or birds

Etymology

From 'false' (not genuine) + 'vampire', referencing mistaken belief about its feeding habits.
