Scourge of God

1. PROPN

/ ˈskɝdʒ əv ˈɡɑd / (AmE), / ˈskɜːdʒ əv ˈɡɒd / (BrE)
a title given to Attila the Hun, the king of the Huns, known for invading the Roman Empire in the 5th century
title for Attila the Hun, king of the Huns

Examples

Attila, often called the Scourge of God, terrorized the Roman Empire with his invasions.
The nickname Scourge of God reflects the fear Attila inspired throughout Europe.
Historians still debate how much of the legend of the Scourge of God is based on fact.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Attila - king of the Huns; led invasions against the Roman Empire

Etymology

From the Latin epithet 'Flagellum Dei', used in medieval Europe to describe Attila the Hun as a punishment sent by God.
