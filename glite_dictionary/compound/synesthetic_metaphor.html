synesthetic metaphor

1. NOUN

/ sɪnəsˈθɛtɪk ˈmɛtəˌfɔr / (AmE), / sɪnɪsˈθɛtɪk ˈmɛtəfəː / (BrE)
a metaphor in which one kind of sensation is described in terms of another, blending different sensory experiences
metaphor blending different senses

Examples

The phrase 'a loud shirt' is a synesthetic metaphor because it applies a sound quality to a visual object.
Writers often use synesthetic metaphors to create vivid and multi-layered imagery in their poetry.
Describing music as 'sweet' is an example of a synesthetic metaphor connecting taste and hearing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: metaphor - a figure of speech in which a word or phrase is applied to an object or action to which it is not literally applicable

Etymology

From synesthesia, Greek 'syn-' (together) + 'aisthēsis' (sensation), and metaphor, Greek 'metaphora' (transfer).
