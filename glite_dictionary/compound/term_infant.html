term infant

1. NOUN

/ tɝm ˈɪn.fənt / (AmE), / tɜːm ˈɪn.fənt / (BrE)
an infant born at a gestational age between 37 and 42 completed weeks
infant born between 37-42 weeks
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that a term infant generally has a lower risk of complications than a preterm baby.
All procedures followed the standard guidelines for caring for a term infant in the neonatal unit.
Breastfeeding is encouraged for both term infants and those born prematurely.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: preterm infant - an infant born before 37 completed weeks of gestation
  • Antonym: postterm infant - an infant born after 42 completed weeks of gestation
