double Gloucester

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʌbəl ˈɡlɑstər / (AmE), / ˈdʌbəl ˈɡlɒstə / (BrE)
a smooth, firm, mild-flavored cheese from Gloucestershire, England, typically orange-red in color and made from whole milk
a type of firm, mild English cheese

Examples

We served slices of double Gloucester with crackers at the party.
The strong flavor of double Gloucester pairs well with ale.
He bought a block of double Gloucester from the local market.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: single Gloucester - a similar but lighter cheese from the same region

Etymology

Named after the city of Gloucester in England, where it originated.
