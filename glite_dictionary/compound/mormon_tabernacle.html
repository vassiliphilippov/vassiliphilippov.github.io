Mormon Tabernacle

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɔrmən ˈtæbɚˌnækəl / (AmE), / ˈmɔːmən ˈtæbəˌnækəl / (BrE)
a large auditorium in Salt Lake City, Utah, used for religious services and events by the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints, especially known for its choir and unique architecture
auditorium of the LDS Church in Salt Lake City

Examples

The Mormon Tabernacle is renowned for its remarkable acoustics and historic pipe organ.
Tourists from around the world visit the Mormon Tabernacle during their stay in Salt Lake City.
The Mormon Tabernacle Choir performed a special concert for the holiday season.

Etymology

Named for the Mormon (LDS) Church; 'tabernacle' from Latin 'tabernaculum' meaning 'tent, hut'.
