to unman someone's facade

1. VERB

/ ʌnˈmæn ˈsʌmwʌnz fəˈsɑd / (AmE), / ʌnˈmæn ˈsʌmwənz fəˈsɑːd / (BrE)
to reveal the vulnerability or weakness behind someone's outward show of bravery or toughness
reveal weakness behind a tough front

Examples

The defeat was enough to unman his facade and leave him visibly shaken.
It took only a few probing questions to unman her facade of confidence.
The relentless criticism slowly unmanned his facade, exposing his insecurities.

Synset Examples

The tragedy did nothing but unman his brave facade.
His actions seemed to unman his tough reputation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to unmask - to reveal someone's true character or intentions
  • Synonym: to expose - to make something visible that was hidden, especially a weakness
