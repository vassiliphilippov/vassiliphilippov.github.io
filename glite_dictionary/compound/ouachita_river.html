Ouachita River

1. PROPN

/ ˈwɑːʃɪtɑː ˈrɪvər / (AmE), / ˈwɒʃɪtɑː ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
a river in the southern United States that rises in western Arkansas and flows southeast into eastern Louisiana, joining the Red River.
river in Arkansas and Louisiana, USA

Examples

The Ouachita River provides important water resources for communities in Arkansas and Louisiana.
Recreational activities like fishing and boating are popular along the Ouachita River.
Flooding along the Ouachita River has impacted several towns over the past decade.

Etymology

From the French spelling of the Caddo word 'Washita', meaning 'good hunting grounds'.
