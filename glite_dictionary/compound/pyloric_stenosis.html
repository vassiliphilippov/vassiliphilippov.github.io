pyloric stenosis

1. NOUN

/ paɪˈlɔrɪk stɪˈnoʊsɪs / (AmE), / paɪˈlɒrɪk stɪˈnəʊsɪs / (BrE)
a condition in which the pyloric sphincter narrows, blocking food from passing from the stomach into the duodenum
narrowing of the pyloric sphincter
medical

Examples

The infant was diagnosed with pyloric stenosis after showing repeated vomiting and failure to gain weight.
Surgery is often required to treat pyloric stenosis in newborns.
Early detection of pyloric stenosis can prevent serious complications in infants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stenosis - abnormal narrowing of a bodily passage

Etymology

From Greek pylōrikos (pertaining to a gatekeeper, the pylorus) and stenosis (narrowing).
