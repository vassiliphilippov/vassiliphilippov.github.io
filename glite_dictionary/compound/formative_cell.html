formative cell

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔːrmətɪv sɛl / (AmE), / ˈfɔːmətɪv sɛl / (BrE)
an embryonic cell that has the potential to develop into specific tissues or organs during development
embryonic cell with developmental potential
medical

Examples

The division of a formative cell is a crucial early step in embryonic development.
Scientists observed how each formative cell contributed to the structure of the developing organism.
If a formative cell malfunctions, it can lead to developmental abnormalities.

Etymology

From 'formative' (giving form) + 'cell', both from Latin roots.
