fungus family

1. NOUN

/ ˈfʌŋɡəs ˈfæməli / (AmE), / ˈfʌŋɡəs ˈfæməli / (BrE)
A group of related fungi classified as a family in biological taxonomy, which may include lichen families.
taxonomic group of related fungi

Examples

The fungus family Clavicipitaceae includes many parasitic species.
Recent studies have led to the reclassification of several fungus families.
Lichens often belong to different fungus families depending on their fungal partner.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: fungus genus - a taxonomic group ranking below family, including related species of fungi
  • Degree Higher: fungus order - a taxonomic group ranking above family, including related families of fungi

Etymology

From Latin 'fungus' (mushroom) and 'familia' (family, household).
