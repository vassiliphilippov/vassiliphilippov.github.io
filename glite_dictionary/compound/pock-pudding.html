pock-pudding

1. NOUN

/ pɑkˈpʊdɪŋ / (AmE), / pɒkˈpʊdɪŋ / (BrE)
An old or playful term for a dessert, especially one with a soft, pudding-like texture.
playful or old term for dessert
archaic

Examples

He couldn't resist a second helping of pock-pudding after dinner.
Grandma used to call all her sweet treats pock-pudding when we were little.
The children giggled at the strange name pock-pudding for their dessert.

Synset Examples

He called the dessert a pock-pudding, delighting the children.

Etymology

From 'pock' (as in pocket or pouch) and 'pudding', possibly referring to a bag pudding or simply as playful language.
