shadow play

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃædoʊ pleɪ / (AmE), / ˈʃadəʊ pleɪ / (BrE)
a form of drama in which performers create images or tell stories by casting shadows with their hands, figures, or puppets onto a screen or wall
performance using shadows for storytelling

Examples

We watched a beautiful shadow play performed by skilled artists from Indonesia.
The children created a simple shadow play using cutouts and a flashlight.
Traditional shadow play often tells ancient myths and folk stories.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: puppet show - a performance in which puppets are used to act out a story
