loose cannon

1. NOUN

/ lus ˈkænən / (AmE), / luːs ˈkænən / (BrE)
an unpredictable or reckless person who is likely to cause trouble or damage without intending to
unpredictable or reckless person

Examples

He became known as a loose cannon after repeatedly ignoring company policies.
The senator worried that his colleague was a loose cannon who might undermine the negotiations.
We can't afford any loose cannon behavior during such a critical time.

Synset Examples

They couldn't trust him; he was a loose cannon on the team.
The manager described the new employee as a loose cannon.
His unpredictable behavior makes him a loose cannon in meetings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: firebrand - a person who is passionate about a cause, typically inciting change or trouble
  • Synonym: wild card - a person or thing whose influence is unpredictable

Etymology

Originally referred to an unsecured cannon on a ship, which could cause damage if not properly fastened.
