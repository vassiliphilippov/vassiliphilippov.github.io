viral hepatitis

1. NOUN

/ ˈvaɪɹəl hɛˈpæt̬əsɪs / (AmE), / ˈvaɪərəl hɛˈpatɪtɪs / (BrE)
A form of hepatitis caused by infection with a virus.
hepatitis caused by a virus
medical

Examples

Many cases of viral hepatitis are preventable through vaccination and safe medical practices.
The doctor explained that viral hepatitis can be caused by several different viruses, including hepatitis A, B, and C.
Chronic viral hepatitis can lead to serious complications such as liver cirrhosis or cancer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hepatitis - inflammation of the liver

Etymology

From 'viral' (relating to a virus) and 'hepatitis' (from Greek hēpar, hēpatos meaning 'liver').
