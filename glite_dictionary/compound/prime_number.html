prime number

1. NOUN

/ ˈpraɪm ˌnʌmbɚ / (AmE), / ˈpraɪm ˌnʌmbə / (BrE)
A whole number greater than 1 that can be divided only by itself and 1 without leaving a remainder.
number divisible only by itself and 1

Examples

Seven is a prime number, since it can be divided only by one and itself.
Many cryptographic algorithms rely on the properties of a prime number.
The number fifteen is not a prime number because it can be divided by three and five.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: composite number - a whole number greater than 1 that is not a prime number

Etymology

From Latin 'primus' (first) and 'numerus' (number).
