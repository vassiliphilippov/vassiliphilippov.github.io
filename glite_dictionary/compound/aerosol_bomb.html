aerosol bomb

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛr.əˌsɑl bɑm / (AmE), / ˈeə.rəˌsɒl bɒm / (BrE)
a bomb that disperses fuel as an aerosol before ignition, creating a powerful explosion by mixing with air
bomb using a fuel-air explosive

Examples

The military tested a new aerosol bomb in the desert to measure its explosive power.
A single aerosol bomb can cause massive destruction over a wide area due to its shock wave.
Experts raised concerns about the use of aerosol bombs in civilian areas.

Synset Examples

a thermobaric bomb can create overpressures equal to an atomic bomb

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thermobaric bomb - a bomb that uses a fuel-air explosive for a powerful blast

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛrəˌsɑl bɑm / (AmE), / ˈɛərəˌsɒl bɒm / (BrE)
a device or dispenser that holds a substance under pressure and can release it as a fine spray, usually by means of a propellant gas
pressurized dispenser releasing a fine spray

Examples

The military developed a new type of aerosol bomb for dispersing chemicals over a wide area.
He described the effects of the aerosol bomb used to release insecticide in the test field.
Authorities warned that an aerosol bomb could pose health risks if misused in populated areas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aerosol can - a container holding a substance under pressure, released as a spray
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02685776-n
Synset POS: n
