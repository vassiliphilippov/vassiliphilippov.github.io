traditional knowledge

1. NOUN

/ trəˈdɪʃənl ˈnɑlɪdʒ / (AmE), / trəˈdɪʃənəl ˈnɒlɪdʒ / (BrE)
knowledge passed down through generations within a community, especially by oral tradition, custom, or anecdote, often relating to culture, nature, or practical skills
knowledge passed down through tradition

Examples

Many indigenous communities rely on traditional knowledge to manage natural resources sustainably.
Researchers are increasingly recognizing the value of traditional knowledge in understanding local ecosystems.
Much of the traditional knowledge about medicinal plants is handed down from elders to younger generations.

Synset Examples

early peoples passed on plant and animal lore through legend

Related Terms

  • Synonym: folklore - traditional beliefs, customs, and stories of a community
  • Synonym: oral tradition - cultural information transmitted by word of mouth
