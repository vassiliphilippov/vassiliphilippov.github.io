sea poacher

1. NOUN

/ si ˈpoʊtʃər / (AmE), / si ˈpəʊtʃə / (BrE)
a small, slender fish with its body covered in bony plates, found mainly in deeper waters of the northern Pacific Ocean
small bony-plated fish of northern Pacific

Examples

The sea poacher is often found in the cold, deep waters near Alaska.
Fishermen sometimes catch a sea poacher by accident while trawling for other fish.
With its armor-like scales, the sea poacher is easy to identify among Pacific marine life.
