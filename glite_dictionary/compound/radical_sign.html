radical sign

1. NOUN

/ ˈrædɪkəl saɪn / (AmE), / ˈrædɪkəl saɪn / (BrE)
the sign (√) used in mathematics to indicate the extraction of a root, usually a square root
mathematical root extraction sign (√)

Examples

The radical sign is used to represent the square root of a number.
When you see a radical sign in an equation, it means you need to find a root.
The teacher drew a radical sign on the board to explain the concept.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: square root - a number that when multiplied by itself gives the original number

Etymology

From 'radical', meaning 'of roots', and 'sign', meaning 'symbol'.
