maple syrup urine disease

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪpəl ˈsɪrəp ˈjʊrɪn dɪˌziz / (AmE), / ˈmeɪpl ˈsɪərəp ˈjʊərɪn dɪˌziːz / (BrE)
A rare inherited metabolic disorder in which the urine emits an odor similar to maple syrup; if untreated, it can cause severe neurological damage or death in early childhood.
inherited metabolic disorder with sweet-smelling urine
medical

Examples

Babies with maple syrup urine disease require a special diet to avoid harmful brain damage.
The sweet odor of the urine led doctors to suspect maple syrup urine disease in the infant.
Genetic testing confirmed that she was a carrier of maple syrup urine disease.
