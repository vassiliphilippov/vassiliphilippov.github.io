3D radar

1. NOUN

/ ˌθri di ˈreɪˌdɑr / (AmE), / ˌθriː diː ˈreɪˌdɑː / (BrE)
a radar system that provides information about the altitude, azimuth, and distance of a target
radar measuring altitude, azimuth, and distance

Examples

The new airport uses a 3D radar system to monitor aircraft positions in real time.
Our military upgraded to 3D radar for better tracking of incoming threats.
The engineer explained how 3D radar improves accuracy in identifying flying objects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: radar - system that detects objects using radio waves

Etymology

"3D" stands for "three-dimensional"; "radar" is an acronym for "radio detection and ranging".
