Crookes radiometer

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrʊks ˌreɪdiˌɒˈmiːtər / (AmE), / ˈkrʊks ˌreɪdiˈɒmɪtə / (BrE)
a scientific instrument with lightweight vanes that spin when exposed to light, demonstrating radiant energy
light-driven scientific instrument

Examples

The scientist demonstrated how the Crookes radiometer spun faster in direct sunlight.
As a child, I was fascinated by the Crookes radiometer in my grandfather's study.
A Crookes radiometer can illustrate the conversion of light energy into mechanical motion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: radiometer - an instrument for detecting or measuring radiant energy

Etymology

Named after Sir William Crookes (1832–1919), British chemist and physicist, who invented it in 1873.
