Murder, Inc.

1. PROPN

/ ˈmɜrdər ɪŋk / (AmE), / ˈmɜːdə ɪŋk / (BrE)
a notorious group of contract killers that operated as an enforcement arm of organized crime syndicates, especially in the United States in the 1930s and 1940s
notorious organized crime group of the 1930s-1940s

Examples

The FBI's investigation uncovered the deadly influence of Murder, Inc. in several major cities.
Many historians have written about Murder, Inc. and its infamous hitmen.
The testimony of a former Murder, Inc. member helped convict several mob bosses.

Synset Examples

Murder, Inc. was notorious for its organized crime activities.
The documentary explored the history of Murder, Inc.

Etymology

From 'murder' + abbreviation 'Inc.' (Incorporated), reflecting its business-like approach to organized killing.
