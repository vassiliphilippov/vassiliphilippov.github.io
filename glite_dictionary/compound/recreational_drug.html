recreational drug

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɛkriˈeɪʃənl ˌdrʌɡ / (AmE), / ˌrɛkriˈeɪʃənəl ˌdrʌɡ / (BrE)
a drug taken for pleasure rather than for medical reasons, often used occasionally and claimed to be nonaddictive
drug used for pleasure, not medicine

Examples

Some people believe that using a recreational drug occasionally is harmless, but experts warn of long-term risks.
The government has strict laws regarding the possession of any recreational drug.
At the festival, security was checking for alcohol and recreational drugs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: narcotic - a drug that relieves pain and induces sleep; often addictive
  • Antonym: medicinal drug - a drug used for medical treatment
