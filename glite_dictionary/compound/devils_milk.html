devil's milk

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛvəlz mɪlk / (AmE), / ˈdɛvəlz mɪlk / (BrE)
a European weed with flowers that turn toward the sun, known for its milky sap
European weed with sun-following flowers

Examples

Some gardeners try to remove devil's milk before it spreads across the lawn.
The bright yellow flowers of devil's milk always seem to face the sun.
We found a patch of devil's milk growing beside the footpath in the park.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɛvəlz mɪlk / (AmE), / ˈdɛvəlz mɪlk / (BrE)
an Old World spurge plant (Euphorbia helioscopia), introduced as a weed in the eastern United States, known for its milky sap
a weed; spurge plant with milky sap

Examples

You can often find devil's milk growing along roadsides in the eastern United States.
Gardeners are advised to wear gloves when handling devil's milk due to its irritating sap.
The spread of devil's milk as a weed has concerned local farmers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spurge - any plant of the genus Euphorbia, typically with milky sap
