chamfer plane

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃæm.fɚ pleɪn / (AmE), / ˈtʃæm.fə pleɪn / (BrE)
a hand tool used for cutting a beveled edge or chamfer on wood or other material
tool for making a beveled edge

Examples

The woodworker used a chamfer plane to smooth the sharp edges of the shelf.
A chamfer plane is ideal for creating angled edges on furniture frames.
She bought a vintage chamfer plane at the tool auction for her workshop.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plane - a hand tool for smoothing or shaping wood
  • Synonym: bevel - a tool or edge that is not perpendicular to the faces of the object

Etymology

From 'chamfer' (from Old French *chanfrein*) and 'plane' (from Latin *planum*, meaning a flat surface or tool for smoothing).
