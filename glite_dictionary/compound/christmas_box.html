Christmas box

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɪs.məs bɑks / (AmE), / ˈkrɪs.məs bɒks / (BrE)
A gift given at Christmas, traditionally to people who have provided a service throughout the year.
Christmas gift given for services provided

Examples

The mail carrier received a Christmas box from several families in the neighborhood.
In the past, it was customary to give your milkman a small Christmas box as a token of gratitude.
Many employers gave their staff a generous Christmas box to thank them for their hard work over the year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tip - A small sum of money given for a service provided
  • Synonym: Boxing Day - The day after Christmas, when such boxes were historically given

Etymology

From 'Christmas' and 'box', relating to the tradition of giving boxes containing gifts to service workers at Christmastime.
