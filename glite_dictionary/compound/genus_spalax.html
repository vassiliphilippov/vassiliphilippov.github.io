genus Spalax

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈspeɪ.læks / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈspa.laks / (BrE)
The type genus of the Spalacidae family, comprising the blind mole rats.
genus of blind mole rats

Examples

The genus Spalax includes several species of blind mole rats found in southeastern Europe and the Middle East.
Scientists have studied the genus Spalax to understand adaptation to underground life.
Members of the genus Spalax are notable for their reduced vision and subterranean habitat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Spalacidae - the family of blind mole rats to which this genus belongs

Etymology

From Latin 'genus' (type, kind) and 'Spalax', the adopted genus name from Greek 'spalax' (blind mole rat).
