hamate bone

1. NOUN

/ ˈheɪ.meɪt ˌboʊn / (AmE), / ˈheɪ.meɪt ˌbəʊn / (BrE)
a carpal bone on the outer side of the wrist, aligned with the fourth and fifth fingers, distinguished by its hook-like projection
a wrist bone with a hook-like process
medical

Examples

A fracture of the hamate bone is common in golfers and baseball players.
The doctor examined the X-ray for damage to the hamate bone.
She felt pain near the hamate bone after twisting her wrist.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: carpal bone - any of the small bones forming the wrist

Etymology

From Latin hamatus (hooked), referring to its hook-shaped projection.
