genus Sphaerocarpos

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs sfɪə.roʊˈkɑːr.pəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs sfɪə.rəˈkɑː.pɒs / (BrE)
A genus of liverworts in the family Sphaerocarpaceae, characterized by small, many-lobed, usually round (orbicular) thalli.
genus of liverworts (Sphaerocarpaceae)

Examples

Several species within the genus Sphaerocarpos are found in damp, shaded habitats.
Researchers were excited to discover a new member of the genus Sphaerocarpos in South America.
The genus Sphaerocarpos is notable for its small, rounded thallus structure.

Etymology

From New Latin: 'Sphaerocarpos', from Greek sphaira (sphere) + karpos (fruit), referring to the shape of the fruiting body.
