family Haematopodidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌhæmətoʊˈpɒdɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˌhiːmətəʊˈpɒdɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of birds known as oystercatchers, characterized by stout bodies and long, strong bills, found in coastal regions worldwide.
family of birds known as oystercatchers

Examples

The family Haematopodidae comprises several species of oystercatchers found on seashores around the world.
Birdwatchers were excited to spot a rare member of the family Haematopodidae during their coastal walk.
Extensive research on the family Haematopodidae has revealed fascinating behaviors related to feeding and nesting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oystercatcher - a type of wading bird in the family Haematopodidae

Etymology

From New Latin Haematopodidae, from Haematopus (type genus) + -idae (family suffix).
