clothes peg

1. NOUN

/ kloʊðz pɛg / (AmE), / kləʊðz pɛg / (BrE)
A small device, typically made of wood or plastic, used for fastening clothes to a clothesline when drying them.
fastener for holding clothes on a line

Examples

She clipped the wet shirts to the line with a clothes peg.
Every clothes peg in the basket was used after the big laundry day.
He accidentally dropped a clothes peg into the garden while hanging the sheets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clothespin - a fastener for holding clothes on a line (American English)
