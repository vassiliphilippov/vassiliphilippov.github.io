Hopey McChange

1. NOUN

/ ˈhoʊpi məkˈtʃeɪndʒ / (AmE), / ˈhəʊpi məkˈtʃeɪndʒ / (BrE)
A playful or sometimes ironic nickname for a person who expresses excessive optimism or advocates for significant change, especially in a naïve or idealistic manner.
playful nickname for an optimist or advocate of change
informal american

Examples

After the meeting, everyone started calling Tom Hopey McChange because of his enthusiastic new ideas.
She was known as Hopey McChange around the office for always believing things could improve.
When he suggested another big reform, his friends jokingly called him Hopey McChange.

Synset Examples

She was dubbed Hopey McChange after her new ideas.
Everyone teased him as Hopey McChange for his plans.
He jokingly called her Hopey McChange for her optimism.

Etymology

Formed by combining 'hopey' (from 'hope') and 'McChange' (using the mock surname prefix 'Mc-' to imply identity with 'change'), originally popularized in U.S. political commentary in the 2008s.
