family Triglidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæmli traɪˈɡlɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfamli trɪˈɡlɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of marine fishes, commonly known as sea robins and gurnards, sometimes subdivided into Triglinae and Peristediinae.
family of marine fishes (sea robins, gurnards)

Examples

The family Triglidae includes sea robins, which are known for their armored heads and wing-like pectoral fins.
Researchers discovered a new species within the family Triglidae along the Mediterranean coast.
Fish in the family Triglidae are often identified by their brightly colored fins and bony plates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gurnard - a type of marine fish in the family Triglidae
  • Synonym: sea robin - common name for fishes in the family Triglidae

Etymology

From New Latin 'Triglidae', based on the type genus 'Trigla'.
