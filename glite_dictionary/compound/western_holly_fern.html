western holly fern

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɛstərn ˈhɑli fɜrn / (AmE), / ˈwɛstən ˈhɒli fɜːn / (BrE)
a North American fern (Polystichum scopulinum) with leathery, spiny-edged fronds, commonly found in mountainous areas
a North American mountain fern species

Examples

The western holly fern thrives in rocky outcrops of the Sierra Nevada mountains.
Botanists identified the western holly fern by its distinctive, glossy fronds.
Hikers often notice the sharp-edged leaves of the western holly fern growing in shaded crevices.
