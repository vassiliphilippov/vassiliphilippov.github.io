Capetian dynasty

1. NOUN

/ kəˈpiːʃən ˈdaɪnəsti / (AmE), / kəˈpiːʃən ˈdɪnəsti / (BrE)
the Frankish royal family founded by Hugh Capet that ruled France from 987 to 1328
Frankish royal family ruling France, 987–1328

Examples

The Capetian dynasty established a foundation for the modern French state.
Many French monarchs during the Middle Ages belonged to the Capetian dynasty.
The end of the Capetian dynasty led to a succession crisis in France.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Valois dynasty - French royal house that succeeded the Capetians
  • Degree Lower: Carolingian dynasty - Frankish royal family preceding the Capetians

Etymology

From the name of Hugh Capet (c. 940–996), first king of the dynasty, combined with Latin 'dynastia' (a ruling family).
