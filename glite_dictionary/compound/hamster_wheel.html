hamster wheel

1. NOUN

/ ˈhæm.stɚ ˌwil / (AmE), / ˈhæm.stə ˌwiːl / (BrE)
a situation or activity that involves continuous, repetitive work or effort without achieving progress or change
monotonous routine without progress

Examples

After years at the same job, she realized she was stuck on a hamster wheel with no real advancement.
He decided to leave the hamster wheel of corporate life and travel the world instead.
Their daily routine felt like a hamster wheel, repeating the same activities over and over.

Synset Examples

She felt like she was on a hamster wheel, going nowhere.
He got off the hamster wheel and started pursuing his dreams.
Every day felt like a hamster wheel, repeating the same tasks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rat race - a way of life marked by a fiercely competitive struggle for wealth or power

Etymology

From the literal 'hamster wheel', an exercise device for hamsters, referencing the animal's endless running without getting anywhere.
