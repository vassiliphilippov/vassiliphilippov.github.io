High Mass

1. NOUN

/ haɪ mæs / (AmE), / haɪ mɑːs / (BrE)
a solemn and elaborate Roman Catholic Mass celebrated with full ceremonial, including music and incense
solemn Roman Catholic Mass with ceremony and music

Examples

The cathedral was filled to capacity for the Easter High Mass.
A choir sang during the High Mass on Christmas morning.
The bishop presided over the solemn High Mass at the historic church.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Low Mass - a simpler Roman Catholic Mass without music or incense
