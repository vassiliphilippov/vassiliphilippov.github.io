unit investment trust

1. NOUN

/ ˈjuːnɪt ɪnˈvɛstmənt traɪst / (AmE), / ˈjuːnɪt ɪnˈvɛstmənt trʌst / (BrE)
A regulated investment company that pools money from investors to create a fixed portfolio of securities, with shares that can be redeemed by investors.
pooled investment company with redeemable shares

Examples

A unit investment trust offers investors a simple way to diversify their holdings.
She decided to put part of her savings into a unit investment trust for long-term growth.
Many people appreciate the transparency provided by a unit investment trust structure.

Synset Examples

you can invest in a unit investment trust for as little as $1000
