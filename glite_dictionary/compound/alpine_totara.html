alpine totara

1. NOUN

/ ˈælpaɪn təˈtɑːrə / (AmE), / ˈælpaɪn təˈtɑːrə / (BrE)
a low, wide-spreading coniferous shrub native to the mountains of New Zealand, known for its needle-like leaves and adaptation to alpine conditions
mountainous New Zealand coniferous shrub

Examples

The alpine totara thrives in the harsh, windy conditions of New Zealand's high country.
Hikers admired the gnarled branches of the alpine totara scattered across the mountain slopes.
Conservationists are working to protect the unique ecosystem supported by the alpine totara.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: totara - a large evergreen tree of New Zealand, Podocarpus totara

Etymology

From 'alpine' (relating to high mountains) and Māori 'tōtara' (name for Podocarpus trees).
