to whore your talent

1. VERB

/ hɔr jər ˈtælənt / (AmE), / hɔː jɔː ˈtælənt / (BrE)
to exploit or sell one's skills or abilities, especially for little reward or at the expense of one's integrity
exploit or sell one's skills cheaply
informal vulgar

Examples

He refused to whore his talent just to make some quick cash.
Many artists regret whoring their talent for projects they don't believe in.
She felt like she was whoring her talent by taking on uninspiring freelance jobs.

Synset Examples

Don’t whore your talents for free.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to sell out - to compromise one's integrity or principles for personal gain

Etymology

From the figurative use of 'whore' meaning to sell oneself or one's abilities cheaply; 'talent' refers to skills or abilities.
