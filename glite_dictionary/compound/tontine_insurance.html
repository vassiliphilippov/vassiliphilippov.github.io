tontine insurance

1. NOUN

/ tɑnˈtin ɪnˈʃʊrəns / (AmE), / tɒnˈtiːn ɪnˈʃʊərəns / (BrE)
A type of life insurance in which, upon the death or default of a participant, their share is distributed among the surviving members.
type of life insurance pooling survivors' benefits

Examples

Many nineteenth-century institutions used tontine insurance as a way to encourage savings.
After his grandfather died, the proceeds from the tontine insurance were divided among the surviving members.
Tontine insurance fell out of favor due to changing regulations and ethical concerns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tontine - scheme where benefits accrue to last survivors

Etymology

From French *tontine*, named after Lorenzo Tonti, a 17th-century banker.
