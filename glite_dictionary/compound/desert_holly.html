desert holly

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛzɚt ˌhɑli / (AmE), / ˈdezət ˌhɒli / (BrE)
a low, salt-tolerant shrub native to the arid southwestern United States and Mexico, having blue-green, prickly-edged leaves often used for Christmas decoration
saltbush shrub of southwestern US and Mexico

Examples

Many people use desert holly branches as festive decorations during the holidays.
The distinctive leaves of desert holly help it survive in salty, dry soils.
We spotted a patch of desert holly while hiking through the Mojave Desert.
