family Cypraeidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli saɪˌpriːɪˈdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli sɪˌprɪɪˈdiː / (BrE)
A family of marine gastropod molluscs that comprises the cowries, known for their glossy, brightly patterned shells.
family of marine snails (cowries)
medical

Examples

The family Cypraeidae includes some of the most beautiful shells found in the ocean.
Biologists have long studied the evolutionary history of the family Cypraeidae.
Collectors often seek out rare species in the family Cypraeidae for their intriguing patterns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cowry - a marine mollusc of the family Cypraeidae with a glossy, domed shell

Etymology

From New Latin 'Cypraeidae', from 'Cypraea' (the type genus).
