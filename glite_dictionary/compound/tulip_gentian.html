tulip gentian

1. NOUN

/ ˈtuːlɪp ˈdʒɛnʃən / (AmE), / ˈtjuːlɪp ˈdʒɛnʃən / (BrE)
A prairie wildflower with large, upright, bell-shaped bluish flowers, native to moist prairies and fields in the central United States.
prairie wildflower with bluish, bell-shaped flowers

Examples

The tulip gentian brightens the summer prairie with its vivid blue blooms.
Wildlife photographers love capturing the striking colors of the tulip gentian in its natural habitat.
After a rainy spring, the fields were blanketed with blooming tulip gentians.
