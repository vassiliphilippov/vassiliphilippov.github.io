Isle of Skye

1. PROPN

/ aɪl əv skaɪ / (AmE), / aɪl əv skaɪ / (BrE)
an island off the northwest coast of Scotland, famous for its rugged landscapes and dramatic mountain scenery
Scottish island with rugged scenery

Examples

The Isle of Skye attracts thousands of hikers each year with its stunning mountain views.
She spent her honeymoon exploring the castles and lochs of the Isle of Skye.
Many artists visit the Isle of Skye to capture its dramatic scenery on canvas.

Etymology

From Old Norse *skýey* (cloud island), referencing its often mist-covered mountains.
