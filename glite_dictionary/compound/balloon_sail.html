balloon sail

1. NOUN

/ bəˈluːn seɪl / (AmE), / bəˈluːn seɪl / (BrE)
a large, light, and loose sail set to increase a vessel's speed when sailing with the wind
large loose sail for sailing with the wind

Examples

The yacht unfurled its balloon sail to catch the gentle breeze during the race.
With the balloon sail flown high, the vessel gained impressive speed downwind.
They decided to rig the balloon sail for smoother and faster sailing on the open sea.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spinnaker - a large, usually balloon-shaped sail used when running before the wind
