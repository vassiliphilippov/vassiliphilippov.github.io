order Solenogastres

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdər səˌliːnəˈɡæstriːz / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə səˌliːnəˈɡæstriːz / (BrE)
a taxonomic order of molluscs within the class Amphineura, comprising worm-like, shell-less marine animals.
an order of shell-less marine molluscs

Examples

Scientists recently discovered a new species belonging to the order Solenogastres in deep ocean habitats.
The order Solenogastres is notable for its worm-like marine molluscs lacking a shell.
Textbooks on invertebrate zoology often discuss the anatomy of the order Solenogastres.

Etymology

From modern Latin 'Solenogastres', from Greek 'solen' (pipe) and 'gaster' (stomach).
