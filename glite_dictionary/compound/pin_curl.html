pin curl

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪn ˌkɝl / (AmE), / ˈpɪn ˌkɜːl / (BrE)
A curl of hair formed by dampening a lock, winding it into a coil, and securing it with a pin or clip until it sets.
a curl made by winding and pinning hair

Examples

To create vintage waves, she set her hair in pin curls overnight.
After removing the pin curls, her hair was full and bouncy.
Many stylists still use pin curls for a classic, heat-free look.
