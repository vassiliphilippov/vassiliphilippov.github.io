genus Lycaena

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs laɪˈsiːnə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs lʌɪˈsiːnə / (BrE)
A genus of small, slender butterflies in the family Lycaenidae, with upper wing surfaces usually metallic blue, green, or copper.
genus of small metallic butterflies

Examples

The genus Lycaena includes butterflies commonly known as coppers due to their wing coloration.
Researchers have documented many species within the genus Lycaena across North America and Eurasia.
Members of the genus Lycaena are often seen in meadows during the summer months.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Lycaenidae - the butterfly family containing blues and coppers

Etymology

From New Latin 'Lycaena', the genus name, and Latin 'genus' (kind, type).
