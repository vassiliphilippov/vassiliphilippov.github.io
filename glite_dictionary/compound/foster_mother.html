foster mother

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɑstər ˈmʌðɚ / (AmE), / ˈfɒstə ˈmʌðə / (BrE)
A woman who cares for and raises a child that is not her biological offspring, typically as a foster parent.
woman who raises another's child

Examples

She became his foster mother after his parents could no longer care for him.
The boy was grateful to his foster mother for providing a loving home.
Many foster mothers form strong bonds with the children in their care.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foster parent - an adult who cares for a child who is not their biological offspring
  • Antonym: biological mother - a woman who is the genetic mother of a child
