Avogadro's law

1. NOUN

/ ˌævəˈɡɑːdroʊz lɔ / (AmE), / ˌavəˈɡɑːdrəʊz lɔː / (BrE)
the principle that equal volumes of all gases, at the same temperature and pressure, contain the same number of molecules
principle about gas volumes and molecules

Examples

Avogadro's law explains why equal volumes of different gases have the same number of molecules under identical conditions.
In the chemistry lab, students used Avogadro's law to calculate the number of gas particles.
The discovery of Avogadro's law was a major milestone in understanding the behavior of gases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Boyle's law - principle relating pressure and volume of a gas at constant temperature
  • Synonym: Charles's law - principle relating temperature and volume of a gas at constant pressure

Etymology

Named after Italian scientist Amedeo Avogadro (1776–1856).
