urban planning

1. NOUN

/ ˈɜrbən ˈplænɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɜːbən ˈplænɪŋ / (BrE)
the activity or process of designing and organizing the physical layout, infrastructure, and development of urban areas and their activities
design and organization of urban spaces

Examples

Urban planning ensures cities develop in a way that is efficient and sustainable.
The local government held a meeting to discuss improvements in urban planning.
She studied urban planning at university to help design better communities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: city planning - the planning and control of the construction, growth, and development of a town or other urban area
  • Synonym: urban design - the design of cities and towns, focusing on the physical form, structure, and appearance
  • Antonym: rural planning - planning focused on non-urban, rural areas

2. NOUN

/ ˈɜrbən ˈplænɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɜːbən ˈplænɪŋ / (BrE)
the activity of designing and organizing the physical development of towns and cities, including the arrangement of buildings, roads, and public spaces
design and organization of towns or cities

Examples

She studied urban planning at university because she was interested in improving city life.
Good urban planning can help reduce traffic congestion and make neighborhoods more livable.
The conference brought together experts in architecture and urban planning from around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: city planning - the planning and design of urban spaces; synonym of 'urban planning'
  • Synonym: town planning - the planning and design of towns and urban areas; synonym of 'urban planning', chiefly British
