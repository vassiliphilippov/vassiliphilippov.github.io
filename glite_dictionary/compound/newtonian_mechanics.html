Newtonian mechanics

1. NOUN

/ nuˈtoʊniən məˈkænɪks / (AmE), / njuːˈtəʊniən mɪˈkanɪks / (BrE)
the branch of mechanics that is based on Newton's laws of motion and describes the motion of macroscopic objects at speeds much less than the speed of light
branch of physics based on Newton's laws of motion

Examples

Newtonian mechanics fails to accurately describe phenomena at very high speeds close to the speed of light.
Most introductory physics courses begin with the principles of Newtonian mechanics.
In Newtonian mechanics, force is equal to mass times acceleration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: classical mechanics - branch of physics dealing with the motion of bodies under the influence of forces
  • Antonym: quantum mechanics - branch of physics dealing with atomic and subatomic systems
  • Antonym: relativistic mechanics - branch of physics applying to motion at speeds near the speed of light

Etymology

Named after Sir Isaac Newton, English mathematician and physicist (1642–1727).
