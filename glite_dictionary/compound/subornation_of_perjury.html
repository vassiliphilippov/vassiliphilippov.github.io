subornation of perjury

1. NOUN

/ səˌbɔrnəˈʃən əv ˈpɝrdʒəri / (AmE), / səˌbɔːnəˈʃən əv ˈpɜːdʒəri / (BrE)
The crime of persuading or inducing someone to commit perjury, especially during a legal proceeding.
inducing someone to commit perjury
legal

Examples

The lawyer was charged with subornation of perjury after it was discovered he had convinced a witness to lie under oath.
Proving subornation of perjury requires evidence that the perjury was actually induced by another party.
He was disbarred for his involvement in the subornation of perjury during a high-profile fraud trial.

Synset Examples

to prove subordination of perjury you must prove the perjury and also prove that the perjured statement was procured by the accused suborner who knew that it would be false

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perjury - the offense of willfully telling an untruth while under oath

Etymology

From Latin 'subornare' (to secretly furnish or provide) and 'perjurium' (false oath).
