Hercules'-club

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɝːkjəˌliz ˌklʌb / (AmE), / ˈhɜːkjʊˌliːz ˌklʌb / (BrE)
A spiny, ornamental tree or shrub native to the southeastern United States and West Indies, known for its clustered flowers and large, prickly stems.
spiny ornamental tree/shrub of SE US and West Indies

Examples

The Hercules'-club is often planted in gardens for its striking appearance and unique spiny trunk.
She learned to identify the Hercules'-club by the sharp thorns covering its branches.
The fruit of the Hercules'-club is an important food source for certain birds in its native habitat.

Etymology

Named for its stout, club-like stems reminiscent of the club of the mythical hero Hercules.
