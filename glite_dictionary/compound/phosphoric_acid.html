phosphoric acid

1. NOUN

/ fɑsˈfɔrɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / fɒsˈfɒrɪk ˈæsɪd / (BrE)
a colorless, odorless acid (H3PO4) used in fertilizers, foods, and cleaning agents
a chemical acid used in fertilizers and foods

Examples

The food industry often adds phosphoric acid to soft drinks for flavor and preservation.
Farmers use fertilizers containing phosphoric acid to boost crop yield.
Phosphoric acid is also found in some household cleaning products due to its effectiveness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phosphate - a salt or ester of phosphoric acid

Etymology

From phosphate + -ic, indicating an acid with a higher proportion of oxygen; ultimately from Greek 'phosphoros' (light-bringing).
