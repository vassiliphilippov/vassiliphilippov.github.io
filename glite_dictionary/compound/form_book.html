form book

1. NOUN

/ fɔrm bʊk / (AmE), / fɔːm bʊk / (BrE)
a record of previous performances of competitors, especially used as a guide in betting or sports analysis
record of competitors' past performances

Examples

The punter checked the form book before placing his bet on the big race.
Many trainers meticulously keep a form book to track their horses' progress.
Access to a detailed form book is essential for serious sports bettors.

Synset Examples

The trainer relied on the form book to assess horses.
He referenced the form book for the best bets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: record book - a book in which records are kept, especially sports results
