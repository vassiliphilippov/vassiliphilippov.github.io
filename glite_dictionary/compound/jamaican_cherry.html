Jamaican cherry

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˈmeɪ.kən ˈtʃɛr.i / (AmE), / dʒəˈmeɪ.kən ˈtʃɛr.i / (BrE)
a fast-growing tropical American evergreen tree (Muntingia calabura) with white flowers and edible red fruit, whose bark yields silky fiber and wood is valued for staves
tropical tree with edible fruit and useful bark fiber

Examples

The Jamaican cherry produces small red fruits that are sweet and edible.
Farmers often plant the Jamaican cherry to provide fast shade and quick yields.
The silky fiber from the bark of the Jamaican cherry is sometimes used to make cordage.
