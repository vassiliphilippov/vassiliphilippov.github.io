cynipid wasp

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈnɪpɪd wɑsp / (AmE), / sɪˈnɪpɪd wɒsp / (BrE)
A small solitary wasp of the family Cynipidae, known for producing galls on oaks and other plants.
gall-producing wasp

Examples

A cynipid wasp laid its eggs on the oak leaf, causing a distinctive round gall to form.
Researchers observed the lifecycle of a cynipid wasp in a botanical garden.
Some cynipid wasps are specific to particular types of host plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gall wasp - a wasp of the family Cynipidae that induces galls

Etymology

From New Latin Cynipidae (the family name), from Greek kunipos (a type of fly).
