modern jazz

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑdərn dʒæz / (AmE), / ˈmɒdən dʒæz / (BrE)
A style of jazz music characterized by complex harmonies, rhythms, and improvisation, typically developed after 1940.
post-1940 jazz style

Examples

Many musicians consider modern jazz to be a major turning point in music history.
Her record collection includes numerous influential modern jazz albums from the 1950s and 1960s.
He began studying modern jazz improvisation after graduating from the conservatory.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jazz - A musical genre originating in the African-American communities with syncopated rhythms and improvisation
