family Eleotridae

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛliˌɑːtrɪˈdiː / (AmE), / ˌɛlɪəʊˈtrɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of small freshwater and brackish fishes, commonly known as sleepers, found in warm regions worldwide.
family of sleeper fishes

Examples

The family Eleotridae comprises many species found in tropical and subtropical regions.
Scientists collected samples of fish from the family Eleotridae for their ecological survey.
Many members of the family Eleotridae are known for their ability to adapt to both fresh and brackish water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sleeper - common name for fish of the family Eleotridae

Etymology

From Greek 'eleotris' (a kind of fish) + taxonomic suffix '-idae' used for animal families.
