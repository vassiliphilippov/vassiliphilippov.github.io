lick of

1. NOUN

/ lɪk əv / (AmE), / lɪk əv / (BrE)
a small amount of something, especially a very small taste or sample
small amount; small taste or sample

Examples

He asked for just a lick of honey in his tea.
There wasn’t a lick of paint left after the renovations.
She let her friend have a lick of her ice cream cone.

Synset Examples

He took a quick lick of the ice cream cone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smidgen - a small amount
  • Synonym: dab - a small amount, especially of something soft
