white-leaved rockrose

1. NOUN

/ waɪt liːvd ˈrɑkˌroʊz / (AmE), / waɪt liːvd ˈrɒkˌrəʊz / (BrE)
a compact shrub of southwestern Europe with white, hairy leaves and pink flowers
compact shrub with white hairy leaves

Examples

The white-leaved rockrose is prized by gardeners for its attractive pink blooms.
Botanists have documented the white-leaved rockrose in numerous parts of southwestern Europe.
Because of its distinctive foliage, the white-leaved rockrose stands out among similar shrubs.
