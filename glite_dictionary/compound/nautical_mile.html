nautical mile

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɔtɪkəl maɪl / (AmE), / ˈnɔːtɪkəl maɪl / (BrE)
a unit of length used in navigation, equal to exactly 1,852 meters or about 6,076 feet
unit of length in navigation (1,852 meters)

Examples

The ship traveled 150 nautical miles before reaching the port.
An international agreement defines the nautical mile as exactly 1,852 meters.
Pilots use the nautical mile to measure distances in air navigation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mile - a unit of length equal to 1,609.34 meters (statute mile)

Etymology

From 'nautical', relating to navigation or ships, and 'mile', from Latin 'mille' (thousand, as in 'mille passus'—a thousand paces).

2. NOUN

/ ˈnɑtɪkəl maɪl / (AmE), / ˈnɔːtɪkəl maɪl / (BrE)
a unit of length used in navigation, equal to 1,852 meters (about 6,076 feet), slightly longer than a statute mile
unit of length used in navigation

Examples

The ship covered one nautical mile in just under two minutes.
Pilots and sailors prefer using the nautical mile for measuring distances at sea.
A nautical mile is slightly longer than a land mile, making it useful for marine navigation.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: statute mile - a standard mile used on land, equal to 1,609 meters

Etymology

From 'nautical' (relating to ships or navigation) and 'mile'; standardized internationally in 1929.
