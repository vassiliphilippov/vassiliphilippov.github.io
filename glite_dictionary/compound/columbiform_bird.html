columbiform bird

1. NOUN

/ kəˈlʌmbəˌfɔrm bɝd / (AmE), / kəˈlʌmbɪˌfɔːm bɜːd / (BrE)
A bird belonging to the order Columbiformes, characterized by a small head, short legs, and four unwebbed toes; includes pigeons and doves.
bird of the order Columbiformes (pigeons, doves, etc.)

Examples

The passenger pigeon, once abundant, was a well-known columbiform bird native to North America.
Many species of columbiform birds play important roles in seed dispersal.
A zoologist explained that doves and pigeons are classified as columbiform birds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pigeon - a type of columbiform bird, commonly found in cities worldwide
  • Synonym: dove - a small columbiform bird, often symbolizing peace

Etymology

From Latin 'columba' (dove or pigeon) and '-form' (having the form of).
