that bugger

1. NOUN

/ ðæt ˈbʌɡəɹ / (AmE), / ðæt ˈbʌɡə / (BrE)
an informal, often mildly rude term for a person who is annoying or troublesome
annoying or troublesome person (informal)
informal british

Examples

I can't stand that bugger who always takes my seat.
Watch out for that bugger; he's always causing trouble at meetings.
She called her brother that bugger after he hid her phone again.

Synset Examples

That bugger stole my sandwich from the fridge.
I can't believe that bugger lied to me again.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bugger - annoying or unpleasant person (informal, British)

Etymology

From British English 'bugger', originally a slang or offensive term, now often used informally to refer to someone in a mildly negative manner.
