ancient texts

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪn.tʃənt tɛksts / (AmE), / ˈeɪn.tʃənt tɛksts / (BrE)
written works originating from a very early period in history, especially those of significant historical or cultural value
historical written works

Examples

Many scholars travel the world to analyze ancient texts preserved in private collections.
Legends of lost civilizations often appear in ancient texts from the Middle East.
Deciphering ancient texts requires knowledge of old languages and writing systems.

Synset Examples

He studied Pali to understand ancient texts.
$DEITY is often depicted in ancient texts as powerful.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: manuscript - a handwritten or typed document, especially before printing
  • Antonym: modern texts - written works from the present or recent times
