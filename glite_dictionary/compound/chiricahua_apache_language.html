Chiricahua Apache language

1. NOUN

/ ˌtʃɪrɪˈkɑːwə əˈpætʃi ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌtʃɪrɪˈkɑːwə əˈpætʃi ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
a Southern Athabaskan language traditionally spoken by the Chiricahua Apache people in parts of New Mexico, on the Mescalero Reservation, and at Fort Sill, Oklahoma
language of the Chiricahua Apache people

Examples

The Chiricahua Apache language is taught in some schools on the Mescalero Reservation to help preserve cultural heritage.
Linguists have documented several dialects of the Chiricahua Apache language in New Mexico and Oklahoma.
She grew up hearing the Chiricahua Apache language at home but now mostly speaks English.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Apache language - the language family including Chiricahua, spoken by Apache peoples
  • Synonym: Mescalero Apache language - a closely related Apache language spoken by the Mescalero

Etymology

From the name Chiricahua (an Apache band) and 'Apache', from Spanish, ultimately from Zuni Apachu.
