vitreous humor

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪtriəs ˈhjuːmər / (AmE), / ˈvɪtriəs ˈhjuːmə / (BrE)
the clear, colorless, gel-like substance that fills the space between the lens and the retina in the eyeball
gel-like substance in the eyeball
medical

Examples

The surgeon carefully removed a sample of vitreous humor for analysis.
Floaters are often small bits of debris in the vitreous humor of the eye.
A healthy vitreous humor is essential for maintaining the shape of the eyeball.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: aqueous humor - the clear fluid in the anterior chamber of the eye

Etymology

From Latin 'vitreus' (glassy) and Latin 'humor' (moisture, fluid).
