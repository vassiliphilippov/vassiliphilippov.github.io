freshwater bass

1. NOUN

/ ˈfrɛʃˌwɔtɚ bæs / (AmE), / ˈfrɛʃˌwɔːtə bæs / (BrE)
A North American fish of the sunfish family, found in freshwater and valued as both food and game.
North American freshwater game fish

Examples

The lake is famous for its freshwater bass, attracting anglers from across the country.
Many restaurants in the region serve grilled freshwater bass as a specialty dish.
He caught a record-breaking freshwater bass during last summer's fishing tournament.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: largemouth bass - a common species of freshwater bass known for its large mouth
  • Synonym: smallmouth bass - a species of freshwater bass with a relatively small mouth

2. NOUN

/ ˈfrɛʃˌwɔtər bæs / (AmE), / ˈfrɛʃˌwɔːtə bæs / (BrE)
any of various North American freshwater fish, especially of the genus Micropterus, valued for their lean flesh and popular in sport fishing.
North American freshwater game fish

Examples

The lake is known for its abundant freshwater bass, attracting anglers from all over the region.
He proudly displayed the large freshwater bass he caught early that morning.
Many people enjoy fishing for freshwater bass because of their fighting spirit and tasty meat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bass - a type of fish, often used for both marine and freshwater species
