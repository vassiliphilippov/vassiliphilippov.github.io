active air defense

1. NOUN

/ ˈæktɪv ˈɛr dɪˌfɛns / (AmE), / ˈæktɪv ˈeə dɪˌfɛns / (BrE)
air defense that uses aircraft, missiles, artillery, or electronic countermeasures to intercept and destroy attacking threats
military defense using active measures

Examples

The military installed a new active air defense system to protect the city from incoming missiles.
Modern active air defense relies on sophisticated radar and missile interceptors.
The conference discussed improvements to active air defense strategies against aerial threats.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: passive air defense - protective measures such as camouflage or shelters rather than actively engaging attackers
