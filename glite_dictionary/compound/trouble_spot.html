trouble spot

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrʌbəl spɑt / (AmE), / ˈtrʌbəl spɒt / (BrE)
a place where difficulties or conflicts occur regularly or are expected to occur
place with recurring problems or conflict

Examples

The border region has long been a trouble spot due to ongoing disputes.
Reporters were advised to avoid the trouble spot during the demonstrations.
Environmental agencies are working to clean up several pollution trouble spots in the river.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hotspot - place of significant activity, often trouble or danger

2. NOUN

/ ˈtrʌb.əl spɑt / (AmE), / ˈtrʌb.əl spɒt / (BrE)
A place or region where political unrest or potential violence is occurring or likely to occur.
region of political unrest or potential violence

Examples

Several countries in the region are now considered trouble spots due to ongoing conflicts.
The peacekeeping forces were deployed to a trouble spot near the border.
Diplomats are working tirelessly to prevent the crisis from turning the city into a trouble spot.

Synset Examples

the United States cannot police all of the world's hot spots

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hot spot - an area of potential trouble or activity, especially political or military unrest
