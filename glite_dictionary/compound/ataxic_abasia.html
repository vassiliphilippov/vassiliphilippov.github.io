ataxic abasia

1. NOUN

/ əˈtæksɪk əˈbeɪziə / (AmE), / əˈtaksɪk əˈbeɪzɪə / (BrE)
A type of abasia in which an inability to walk is caused by a lack of muscle coordination (ataxia) affecting the legs.
inability to walk due to leg coordination problem
medical

Examples

The neurologist diagnosed the patient with ataxic abasia after observing severe instability during walking.
Treatment options for ataxic abasia often focus on improving coordination and balance.
Because of her ataxic abasia, she needed assistance to move safely around the house.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abasia - inability to walk due to impaired muscle coordination
  • Synonym: ataxia - lack of muscle coordination

Etymology

From 'ataxic' (from Greek ataxia, meaning lack of order or coordination) + 'abasia' (from Greek abasia, meaning inability to walk).
