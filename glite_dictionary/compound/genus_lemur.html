genus Lemur

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˈliːmjər / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈliːmjʊə / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Lemuridae, comprising several species of lemurs found in Madagascar.
type genus of Lemuridae (lemurs)
medical

Examples

The genus Lemur includes several species that are native to Madagascar.
Scientists discovered a new member of the genus Lemur during their expedition.
Conservation efforts are crucial for the survival of animals in the genus Lemur.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lemur - a small, nocturnal primate native to Madagascar

Etymology

From Latin 'lemur' (ghost, spirit), originally used by Linnaeus to describe these nocturnal primates.
