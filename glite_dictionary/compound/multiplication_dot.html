multiplication dot

1. NOUN

/ ˌmʌltɪplɪˈkeɪʃən dɑt / (AmE), / ˌmʌltɪplɪˈkeɪʃən dɒt / (BrE)
a symbol (·) used to indicate the operation of multiplication, especially to avoid confusion with the letter x
symbol used for multiplication

Examples

In algebra, the multiplication dot is often preferred over the × symbol.
The teacher wrote the equation using a multiplication dot to avoid confusion.
Computer typesetting systems typically include a multiplication dot as a special character.

Synset Examples

Other symbols can also be used to denote multiplication, often to reduce confusion between the multiplication sign × and the commonly used variable x.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: multiplication sign - the × symbol used to indicate multiplication
