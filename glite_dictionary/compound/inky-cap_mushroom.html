inky-cap mushroom

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪŋki kæp ˌmʌʃˌrum / (AmE), / ˈɪŋki kap ˌmʌʃˌrʊm / (BrE)
A mushroom whose cap dissolves into an inky liquid after the spores mature, especially of the genus Coprinus.
mushroom that liquefies into ink-like fluid

Examples

The inky-cap mushroom is distinctive for the way its cap dissolves into a black, ink-like liquid.
We watched an inky-cap mushroom liquefy overnight, leaving a dark stain on the paper.
Certain species of inky-cap mushroom are edible when young but should be harvested before they deliquesce.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coprinus - a genus of mushrooms whose caps often liquefy into an inky substance
