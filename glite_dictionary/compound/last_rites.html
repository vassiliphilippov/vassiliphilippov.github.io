last rites

1. NOUN

/ læst raɪts / (AmE), / lɑːst raɪts / (BrE)
religious or ceremonial acts performed for a person who is dying or has just died, especially in the Christian tradition
religious ceremony at death

Examples

The family gathered for the last rites at the hospital bedside.
He requested the priest to administer the last rites.
The last rites were performed before the burial began.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: funeral - a ceremony honoring a deceased person
  • Antonym: baptism - a Christian sacrament of initiation and purification

2. NOUN

/ læst raɪts / (AmE), / lɑːst raɪts / (BrE)
the final religious ceremony performed for a person who is dying, especially in the Catholic tradition, where a priest anoints the person and prays for spiritual salvation
final religious ceremony for the dying

Examples

The priest was called to give her the last rites as her condition worsened.
Many families find comfort in receiving the last rites for their loved ones.
He passed away peacefully after receiving the last rites at the hospital.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anointing of the sick - Catholic sacrament involving prayer and anointing of a sick or dying person
