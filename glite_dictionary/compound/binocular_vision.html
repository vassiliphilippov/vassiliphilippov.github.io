binocular vision

1. NOUN

/ bɪˈnɑkjələr ˈvɪʒən / (AmE), / bɪˈnɒkjʊlə ˈvɪʒən / (BrE)
Vision that involves the use of both eyes, allowing for depth perception and a wider field of view.
vision using both eyes

Examples

Owls have excellent binocular vision that helps them judge distances accurately when hunting at night.
Children develop binocular vision as their eyes and brains learn to coordinate together.
Wearing an eyepatch may temporarily reduce binocular vision and make tasks requiring depth perception more difficult.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: monocular vision - vision using only one eye

Etymology

From Latin 'binoculus' (having two eyes) + 'vision'.
