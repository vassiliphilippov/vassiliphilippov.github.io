Church of the Brethren

1. NOUN

/ tʃɝtʃ əv ðə ˈbrɛðɹən / (AmE), / tʃɜːtʃ əv ðə ˈbrɛðrən / (BrE)
A Protestant Christian denomination, founded in 1708 by Americans of German descent, known for opposing military service and legal oaths and for practicing trine immersion baptism.
Protestant Christian denomination founded in 1708

Examples

The Church of the Brethren has a long tradition of pacifism and community service.
Many rural communities in Pennsylvania have congregations of the Church of the Brethren.
She was baptized in the Church of the Brethren as a child.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Baptist - a member of a Protestant Christian denomination advocating baptism only of adult believers

Etymology

Named for its emphasis on the Christian concept of ‘brethren’ (brothers) and organized as a separate church body.
