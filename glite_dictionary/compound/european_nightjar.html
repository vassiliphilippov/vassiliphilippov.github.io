European nightjar

1. NOUN

/ jʊˈrəpiən ˈnaɪtˌdʒɑr / (AmE), / jʊəˈrəʊpiən ˈnaɪtˌdʒɑː / (BrE)
a nocturnal bird species (Caprimulgus europaeus) found across Europe, known for its camouflaged plumage and distinctive churring song
a nocturnal bird of Europe (Caprimulgus europaeus)

Examples

The European nightjar is most active at dusk and dawn, hunting insects on the wing.
We heard the distinctive churring call of a European nightjar in the heathland.
Conservationists are working to protect the habitat of the European nightjar due to declining populations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: goatsucker - nocturnal birds formerly believed to suck goats' milk; nightjars
  • Synonym: nightjar - a nocturnal bird with a short bill and long wings, of the family Caprimulgidae
