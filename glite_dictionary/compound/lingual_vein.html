lingual vein

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪŋɡwəl veɪn / (AmE), / ˈlɪŋɡwəl veɪn / (BrE)
a vein that receives blood from the tongue and the floor of the mouth, draining into the internal jugular or facial vein
vein draining blood from the tongue
medical

Examples

During surgery, care must be taken not to damage the lingual vein.
The lingual vein is often visible beneath the tongue in some individuals.
Blood from the tongue drains primarily through the lingual vein into the internal jugular vein.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: facial vein - vein that drains blood from the face into the internal jugular vein

Etymology

From Latin 'lingua' (tongue) + English 'vein'.
