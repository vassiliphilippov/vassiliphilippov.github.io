dance music

1. NOUN

/ dæns ˈmjuː.zɪk / (AmE), / dɑːns ˈmjuː.zɪk / (BrE)
music with a strong rhythm, intended especially for dancing to
music made for dancing

Examples

The DJ played energetic dance music that kept everyone on the floor all night.
She prefers listening to dance music when she exercises.
Modern dance music often features electronic beats and fast tempos.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dance - move rhythmically to music, typically following a set sequence of steps
  • Synonym: club music - music typically played in dance clubs, designed for dancing

2. NOUN

/ ˈdæns ˌmjuːzɪk / (AmE), / ˈdɑːns ˌmjuːzɪk / (BrE)
A genre of popular music composed specifically for dancing, often played at social events such as parties or in ballrooms.
music genre for dancing

Examples

The club played energetic dance music all night, keeping everyone on the floor.
She prefers dance music to rock because of its catchy beats.
During the reception, upbeat dance music filled the hall and encouraged guests to move.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ballroom music - music played for ballroom dancing
  • Antonym: classical - traditional genre contrasted with dance music
