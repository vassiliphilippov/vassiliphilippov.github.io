Adam's needle-and-thread

1. NOUN

/ ˈædəmz ˈnidl ənd ˈθrɛd / (AmE), / ˈædəmz ˈniːdl ənd ˈθrɛd / (BrE)
A type of yucca plant with long, stiff leaves bearing filament-like threads along the edges.
yucca plant with filamented leaves

Examples

The garden features several Adam's needle-and-thread plants with striking, spiky leaves.
Native to dry regions, Adam's needle-and-thread thrives in sandy soils and full sun.
The sharp leaves of Adam's needle-and-thread are used in traditional weaving by indigenous peoples.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yucca - a genus of perennial shrubs and trees with sword-shaped leaves

Etymology

Named for its long, sharp leaves (needle) and the threadlike filaments, associated with the biblical Adam.
