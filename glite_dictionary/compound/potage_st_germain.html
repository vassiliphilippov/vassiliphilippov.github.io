potage St. Germain

1. NOUN

/ poʊˌtɑʒ sɛnt ʒɛrˈmeɪn / (AmE), / pɒˌtɑːʒ sɛnt ʒɛˈmɛ̃ / (BrE)
a thick French soup made with fresh green peas, stock, shredded lettuce, onion, and celery
French pea soup with lettuce and vegetables

Examples

For her dinner party, she served a delicious potage St. Germain as the starter.
The chef recommended the potage St. Germain to showcase fresh spring vegetables.
A steaming bowl of potage St. Germain is perfect for a chilly evening.

Etymology

French, named after Saint-Germain, a Parisian suburb known for its gardens and markets.
