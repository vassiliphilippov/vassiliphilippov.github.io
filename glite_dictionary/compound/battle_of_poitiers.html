Battle of Poitiers

1. PROPN

/ ˈbæt̬l əv pwɑːˈtjeɪz / (AmE), / ˈbætl əv ˈpwɑːtjeɪ / (BrE)
the battle fought in 1356 during the Hundred Years' War, in which the English, led by the Black Prince, defeated the French forces
battle in 1356 where English defeated the French

Examples

The Battle of Poitiers marked a turning point in the Hundred Years' War between England and France.
Many historians have studied the tactics used at the Battle of Poitiers to understand medieval warfare.
During the Battle of Poitiers, the English captured the French king, which had lasting effects on France.

Etymology

From Poitiers, the name of a city in western France where the battle took place.
