skeletal structure

1. NOUN

/ ˈskɛlətl ˈstrʌktʃər / (AmE), / ˈskɛlətl ˈstrʌktʃə / (BrE)
the framework formed by the bones or supporting elements of an organism's skeleton
framework made by the bones of an organism

Examples

The dinosaur's skeletal structure was remarkably well-preserved in the fossil record.
Understanding the human skeletal structure is essential for medical students.
Birds have a lightweight skeletal structure to support flight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skeleton - the internal framework of bones and cartilage in an organism
