beaked whale

1. NOUN

/ biːkt weɪl / (AmE), / biːkt weɪl / (BrE)
any of several medium-sized whales found in all oceans, distinguished by their elongated beaklike jaws and small, vestigial teeth in the upper jaw
medium-sized whale with beaklike jaws

Examples

The beaked whale is one of the least understood marine mammals due to its deep-diving behavior.
Researchers recently observed a pod of beaked whales off the coast of New Zealand.
Unlike other whales, a beaked whale has a distinctive elongated snout.
