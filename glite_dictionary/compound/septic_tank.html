septic tank

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛptɪk tæŋk / (AmE), / ˈsɛptɪk taŋk / (BrE)
A large, underground container in which sewage and solid waste is decomposed by bacteria before drainage.
waste-holding container for sewage

Examples

Our country house relies on a septic tank for all its wastewater.
The septic tank needs to be emptied every few years to prevent overflow.
Many rural homes use a septic tank system instead of being connected to a municipal sewer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cesspit - a pit or tank for the disposal of liquid waste and sewage
