pincushion hakea

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪnˌkʊʃ.ən həˈkiː.ə / (AmE), / ˈpɪnˌkʊʃ.ən həˈkiː.ə / (BrE)
a tall, straggling shrub native to Western Australia, with large globular crimson-yellow flowers
Australian shrub with crimson-yellow flowers

Examples

The pincushion hakea is well known for its spectacular crimson-yellow blooms.
Gardeners value the pincushion hakea for attracting native birds in Western Australia.
We planted a pincushion hakea along the fence to add vibrant color to the garden.

Etymology

From 'pincushion' (resembles a pincushion in flower form) and Hakea, the genus name, after Baron Christian Ludwig von Hake, a German patron of botany.
