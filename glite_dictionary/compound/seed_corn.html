seed corn

1. NOUN

/ sid kɔrn / (AmE), / siːd kɔːn / (BrE)
corn kernels or seeds of high quality reserved specifically for planting the next crop, rather than for consumption or other uses.
corn seeds kept for planting

Examples

Farmers carefully select the best seed corn to ensure a healthy crop next season.
After a poor harvest, they had to use most of their seed corn just to eat.
Saving enough seed corn is crucial for sustainable farming from year to year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seed grain - grain reserved for sowing rather than consumption

2. NOUN

/ sid kɔrn / (AmE), / siːd kɔːn / (BrE)
resources or assets reserved to ensure future growth or investment, not to be used for immediate needs
resources reserved for future use

Examples

Many small businesses fail because they spend their seed corn before establishing steady revenue.
Good leaders know not to use the company's seed corn during a brief financial crisis.
Investors often keep some seed corn to take advantage of unexpected opportunities.

Synset Examples

They risked eating their seed corn by spending too much too soon.
To succeed, avoid eating your seed corn during tough times.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nest egg - money saved for the future

Etymology

Originally referred to the corn saved by farmers to plant the next crop; "seed" from Old English "sǣd" and "corn" from Old English "corn" (grain).
