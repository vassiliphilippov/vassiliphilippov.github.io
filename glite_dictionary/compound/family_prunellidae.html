family Prunellidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli pruːˈnɛlɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæməli pruːˈnɛlɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of small passerine birds known as accentors, including the hedge sparrow, found mainly in Eurasia.
family of birds including the hedge sparrow

Examples

The family Prunellidae includes small, inconspicuous birds often seen in mountainous regions.
Many birdwatchers are fascinated by the subtle plumage of members of the family Prunellidae.
The hedge sparrow, also known as the dunnock, belongs to the family Prunellidae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accentor - any bird of the family Prunellidae

Etymology

From modern Latin Prunellidae, based on genus Prunella.
