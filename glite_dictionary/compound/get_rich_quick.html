to get rich quick

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt rɪtʃ kwɪk / (AmE), / ɡɛt rɪtʃ kwɪk / (BrE)
to acquire wealth rapidly, often through questionable or risky methods
acquire wealth rapidly, often by risky means

Examples

He believed he could get rich quick by investing in cryptocurrency, but soon lost everything.
Many people are tempted by advertisements that promise to help them get rich quick.
She warned her friends not to fall for any get rich quick schemes.

Synset Examples

He thinks he can get rich quick; I have a bridge to sell you.
He lost money chasing get-rich-quick opportunities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: get-rich-quick scheme - a plan or strategy promising rapid wealth, often fraudulent
