soft ordnance

1. NOUN

/ sɔft ˈɔrdnəns / (AmE), / sɒft ˈɔːdnəns / (BrE)
non-lethal military devices or munitions intended to minimize harm, often used in training or to reduce risk to civilians
non-lethal military munitions

Examples

During riot control, security forces may deploy soft ordnance to disperse crowds safely.
The use of soft ordnance has increased as militaries seek to minimize collateral damage.
Training exercises often utilize soft ordnance to ensure the safety of all participants.

Synset Examples

They opted for soft ordnance during the training exercise.
Testing of soft ordnance is crucial for safety.
The military used soft ordnance to avoid civilian casualties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: non-lethal weapon - a weapon designed to incapacitate without causing death
  • Antonym: live ammunition - actual lethal bullets or explosives used as opposed to blanks or non-lethal devices
