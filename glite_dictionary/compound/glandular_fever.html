glandular fever

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡlændjələr ˈfiːvər / (AmE), / ˈɡlændjʊlə ˈfiːvə / (BrE)
an infectious disease, especially common in young adults, characterized by fever, swollen lymph nodes, and fatigue, caused by the Epstein-Barr virus.
infectious disease causing fever and swollen glands
medical british

Examples

After two weeks of constant fatigue, the doctor diagnosed her with glandular fever.
Many teenagers develop glandular fever at some point, often experiencing swollen glands and a sore throat.
Because glandular fever is sometimes spread by saliva, it's popularly known as the kissing disease.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: infectious mononucleosis - medical term for glandular fever
