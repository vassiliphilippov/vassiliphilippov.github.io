corvine bird

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔr.vaɪn bɝd / (AmE), / ˈkɔː.vaɪn bɜːd / (BrE)
A bird belonging to the crow family (Corvidae), such as a crow, raven, or jay.
bird of the crow family

Examples

The raven, a large corvine bird, is known for its intelligence and adaptability.
Many corvine birds display remarkable problem-solving skills in the wild.
The magpie is another member of the corvine bird family and is common across Europe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corvid - a bird of the crow family
  • Degree Lower: songbird - a bird belonging to the suborder Passeri, noted for its singing

Etymology

From Latin 'corvinus' (of a crow), from 'corvus' (crow).
