Norway rat

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɔr.weɪ ræt / (AmE), / ˈnɔː.weɪ ræt / (BrE)
a large brownish-gray rat (Rattus norvegicus) commonly found in urban areas worldwide, known as a major domestic pest
large brown domestic rat, major pest

Examples

The city has launched new efforts to control the Norway rat population.
A Norway rat can squeeze through surprisingly small holes to enter buildings.
Unlike the black rat, the Norway rat prefers to live at ground level or in burrows.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brown rat - another common name for Rattus norvegicus
  • Antonym: black rat - another common species of rat, Rattus rattus

Etymology

Despite the name, not originally from Norway; scientific name Rattus norvegicus, 18th century.
