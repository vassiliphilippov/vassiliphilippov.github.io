cornfed look

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔrnˌfɛd lʊk / (AmE), / ˈkɔːnfɛd lʊk / (BrE)
an appearance of being healthy, strong, and robust, often associated with rural or Midwestern upbringing
healthy, robust appearance

Examples

After years on the farm, he had a real cornfed look about him.
The football team was full of players with that classic cornfed look.
Her cornfed look made her stand out in the urban crowd.

Synset Examples

He has a cornfed look, strong and healthy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wholesome - having a healthy, robust appearance
  • Antonym: frail - lacking physical strength or health

Etymology

From American English 'cornfed', originally describing livestock, later figuratively applied to wholesome, robust people, especially from the rural Midwest.
