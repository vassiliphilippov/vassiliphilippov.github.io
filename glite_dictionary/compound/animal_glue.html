animal glue

1. NOUN

/ ˈænɪməl ɡlu / (AmE), / ˈænɪməl ɡluː / (BrE)
A type of adhesive made by boiling the skins, bones, or hooves of animals, especially cattle and horses, to extract protein gelatin.
adhesive made from animal parts

Examples

Traditional bookbinders sometimes use animal glue for its strong, flexible hold.
Art conservators restored the painting with a mixture of animal glue and chalk.
Many woodworking shops have replaced animal glue with more modern adhesives.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gelatin - a colorless, tasteless, water-soluble protein obtained from animal tissues, used in food and industry
  • Antonym: synthetic glue - man-made adhesive not derived from animal products
