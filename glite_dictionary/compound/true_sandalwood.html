true sandalwood

1. NOUN

/ tru ˈsændlˌwʊd / (AmE), / truː ˈsændlˌwʊd / (BrE)
a parasitic tree native to Indonesia and Malaysia, valued for its fragrant yellowish heartwood, which repels insects and is used in making chests and other objects
parasitic tree with fragrant wood, used in chests

Examples

Many antique boxes from Southeast Asia are crafted from true sandalwood because of its pleasant aroma.
The heartwood of true sandalwood is prized for its ability to repel insects naturally.
Due to overharvesting, true sandalwood has become increasingly rare in the wild.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sandalwood - aromatic wood from several trees, especially used for carving and scent
