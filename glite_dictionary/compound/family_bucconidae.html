family Bucconidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli bʊˌkoʊnɪˌdi / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli bʊkˌkəʊnɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of tropical American birds, known as puffbirds, characterized by their large heads and short bills
a family of tropical American puffbirds

Examples

The family Bucconidae comprises a group of tropical birds known as puffbirds.
Members of the family Bucconidae are found primarily in Central and South America.
Biologists have studied the feeding habits of the family Bucconidae extensively.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: puffbird - a bird of the family Bucconidae, noted for its puffy appearance

Etymology

From New Latin Bucconidae, after the genus Bucco (type genus of the family).
