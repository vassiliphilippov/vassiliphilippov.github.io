black hole

1. NOUN

/ blæk hoʊl / (AmE), / blæk həʊl / (BrE)
a region of space having a gravitational field so intense that nothing, not even light, can escape it, formed by the collapse of a massive star
astronomical object with intense gravity

Examples

Scientists believe a black hole exists at the center of our galaxy.
Light cannot escape from a black hole due to its immense gravity.
The discovery of a new black hole was announced by astronomers last year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: singularity - the center point of a black hole where density is infinite

2. NOUN

/ blæk hoʊl / (AmE), / blak həʊl / (BrE)
A situation, place, or thing that constantly consumes or absorbs resources, energy, or attention and gives nothing in return.
thing that absorbs everything

Examples

The abandoned project turned into a black hole for the company's finances.
Social media can sometimes feel like a black hole that sucks up all your free time.
Investing more effort into that system is pouring resources into a black hole.

Synset Examples

She felt like a black hole was consuming her energy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sinkhole - a hole in the ground that swallows everything, metaphorically used for consuming resources
  • Synonym: money pit - something that continually requires a lot of money with no return

Etymology

Metaphorical use based on the astronomical term 'black hole'.
