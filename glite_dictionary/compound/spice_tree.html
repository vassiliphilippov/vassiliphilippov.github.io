spice tree

1. NOUN

/ spaɪs tri / (AmE), / spaɪs triː / (BrE)
a tree that produces aromatic bark or berries used as spices
tree yielding aromatic spices

Examples

The spice tree is valued for its fragrant bark used in cooking.
Many chefs use berries from the spice tree to add flavor to their dishes.
On our trip, we visited a plantation famous for its spice trees.

2. NOUN

/ spaɪs triː / (AmE), / spaɪs triː / (BrE)
A tree native to the Pacific coast with aromatic leaves, small flower clusters, and olivelike fruit, known for its hard, tough wood.
aromatic Pacific coast tree

Examples

The spice tree has aromatic leaves that are often used in traditional remedies.
Carpenters value the tough wood of the spice tree for building tools and furniture.
We spotted several spice trees growing along the Pacific coastline.
