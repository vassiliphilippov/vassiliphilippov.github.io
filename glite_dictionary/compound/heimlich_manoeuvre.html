Heimlich manoeuvre

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪmlɪk məˈnuːvər / (AmE), / ˈhaɪmlɪk məˈnjuːvə / (BrE)
An emergency first aid procedure for treating someone who is choking, involving abdominal thrusts to expel an object blocking the airway.
emergency procedure for choking
medical

Examples

The waiter performed the Heimlich manoeuvre when he saw the customer choking on a piece of steak.
It's important to learn how to give the Heimlich manoeuvre in case of emergencies.
She saved her friend's life by using the Heimlich manoeuvre at the restaurant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abdominal thrusts - first aid action of pressing on the abdomen to expel an object from the airway
  • Antonym: choking - the condition of being unable to breathe because the airway is blocked

Etymology

Named after Dr. Henry J. Heimlich (1920–2016), the American physician who developed the technique.
