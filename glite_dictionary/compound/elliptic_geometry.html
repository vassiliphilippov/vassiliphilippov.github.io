elliptic geometry

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈlɪptɪk dʒiˈɑːmətri / (AmE), / ɪˈlɪptɪk dʒiˈɒmətri / (BrE)
a branch of non-Euclidean geometry in which space is viewed as analogous to a sphere and straight lines as great circles
branch of non-Euclidean geometry

Examples

Bernhard Riemann made groundbreaking contributions to elliptic geometry.
In elliptic geometry, there are no parallel lines as in Euclidean geometry.
Advanced mathematics courses often cover topics like elliptic geometry and hyperbolic spaces.

Synset Examples

Bernhard Riemann pioneered elliptic geometry

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: hyperbolic geometry - a non-Euclidean geometry in which space has a saddle-shape and parallel lines diverge
  • Degree Lower: Euclidean geometry - traditional geometry based on Euclid's axioms

Etymology

From 'elliptic' (from Greek elleiptikos, relating to an ellipse) and 'geometry' (from Greek geometria, land measurement).
