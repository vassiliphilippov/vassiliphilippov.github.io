high doh

1. NOUN

/ haɪ doʊ / (AmE), / haɪ dəʊ / (BrE)
A situation of great trouble, embarrassment, or commotion, often causing stress or agitation.
state of trouble or commotion

Examples

When the fire alarm went off during the meeting, there was a high doh.
After the car broke down on the highway, Jane found herself in a real high doh.
His phone call threw the whole office into a high doh of anxiety.

Synset Examples

When he lost his wallet, it was a real high doh.
The unexpected bill caused a high doh at home.

Etymology

Possibly from Tyneside and Scottish dialect, ‘doh’ related to 'do' (affair, fuss) and 'high' for intensity.
