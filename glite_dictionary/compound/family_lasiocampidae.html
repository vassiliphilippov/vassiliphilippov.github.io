family Lasiocampidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli læsi.oʊˈkæmpɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli læsɪ.əˈkæmpɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of large moths whose larvae are commonly called tent caterpillars, eggars, or lappet moths.
a family of large moths

Examples

The family Lasiocampidae includes many species whose caterpillars build silk tents in trees.
Biologists studied the distribution of the family Lasiocampidae across North America.
Some members of the family Lasiocampidae are known for their strikingly hairy larvae.

Etymology

From modern Latin Lasiocampidae, based on Greek 'lasios' (hairy) and 'kampē' (caterpillar, bending).
