Jerusalem oak

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˈruːsələm ɑk / (AmE), / dʒəˈruːsələm ɒk / (BrE)
a Eurasian aromatic plant of the goosefoot family with oak-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow-green flowers, often naturalized in North America
aromatic Eurasian plant with oak-like leaves

Examples

The aromatic leaves of the Jerusalem oak are sometimes used in herbal remedies.
We found wild Jerusalem oak growing near the edge of the field.
Gardeners appreciate the resilience of Jerusalem oak in poor soils.
