inward-developing

1. ADJ

/ ˈɪnwɚd dɪˈvɛləpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɪnwəd dɪˈvɛləpɪŋ / (BrE)
(of growth or development) progressing toward a central axis or point, as in flower heads where the youngest parts are in the center and older on the periphery.
developing toward the center or axis

Examples

The seeds arranged on the sunflower head follow an inward-developing pattern.
In certain plants, the flowers open in an inward-developing sequence, starting from the edges and moving inward.
Botanists studied the inward-developing arrangement of florets to understand the plant's reproductive strategy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: centripetal - moving or developing toward the center
