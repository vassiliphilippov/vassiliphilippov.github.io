pharyngeal reflex

1. NOUN

/ fəˈrɪndʒiəl ˈriːflɛks / (AmE), / fəˈrɪndʒɪəl ˈriːflɛks / (BrE)
a reflex causing retching or gagging, typically triggered by touching the soft palate at the back of the mouth
reflex causing gagging when the throat is touched
medical

Examples

The doctor tested my pharyngeal reflex by gently touching the back of my throat.
A diminished pharyngeal reflex can indicate a neurological problem.
Dentists are careful not to trigger the pharyngeal reflex during oral examinations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gag reflex - the reflex that causes gagging when the throat is touched

Etymology

From Greek *pharynx* (throat) + English *-eal* (relating to), and Latin *reflexus* (turned back).
