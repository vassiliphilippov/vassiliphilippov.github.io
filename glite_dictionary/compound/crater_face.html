crater face

1. NOUN

/ ˈkreɪtər feɪs / (AmE), / ˈkreɪtə feɪs / (BrE)
A face marked by deep scars or pockmarks, especially as a result of severe acne.
face with deep scars or pockmarks
informal offensive

Examples

He felt self-conscious about his crater face and often avoided photos.
She used special foundation to cover the scars on her crater face.
Kids at school cruelly teased him, calling him crater face because of his acne.

Synset Examples

The makeup helped hide his crater face scars.
He joked about having a crater face after the fall.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pockmarked - having pits or scars on the skin

Etymology

Formed from 'crater' (a cavity or pit) and 'face', likening skin pockmarks to craters on a surface.
