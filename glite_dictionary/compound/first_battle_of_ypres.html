First Battle of Ypres

1. NOUN

/ fɝst ˈbætəl əv iːˈprə / (AmE), / fɜːst ˈbatl əv ˈiːprə / (BrE)
a major battle during World War I in 1914 involving heavy but indecisive fighting between Allied and German forces near Ypres, Belgium
WWI battle at Ypres in 1914

Examples

The First Battle of Ypres marked a grim turning point in World War I.
Many soldiers lost their lives in the First Battle of Ypres during the autumn of 1914.
Historians often debate the strategic impact of the First Battle of Ypres on the overall war.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ypres - town in Belgium that was the site of major WWI battles
