vagus nerve

1. NOUN

/ ˈveɪɡəs nɜːrv / (AmE), / ˈveɪɡəs nɜːv / (BrE)
the tenth cranial nerve, supplying the heart, lungs, digestive tract, and other organs in the chest and abdomen
the tenth cranial nerve
medical

Examples

The vagus nerve plays a crucial role in regulating heart rate and digestion.
Damage to the vagus nerve can result in difficulty swallowing and loss of voice.
Some medical treatments attempt to stimulate the vagus nerve to control seizures.

Etymology

From Latin 'vagus' meaning 'wandering', referring to the nerve's widespread connections.
