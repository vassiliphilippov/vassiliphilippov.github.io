ant bear

1. NOUN

/ ˈænt ˌbɛr / (AmE), / ˈænt ˌbeə / (BrE)
A large, shaggy-haired, toothless anteater of South America, with a long tongue and powerful claws.
large toothless South American anteater

Examples

The ant bear uses its long tongue to extract insects from nests.
Researchers observed an ant bear foraging in the rainforest.
The ant bear is also known as the giant anteater.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: giant anteater - large anteater of South America, scientific name Myrmecophaga tridactyla

2. NOUN

/ ˈænt ˌbɛr / (AmE), / ˈænt ˌbɛː / (BrE)
A nocturnal, burrowing African mammal that feeds on termites, also called the aardvark, and is the only living member of the order Tubulidentata.
African mammal, also called aardvark

Examples

The ant bear uses its long tongue to gather termites from their nests at night.
While on safari, we were lucky enough to spot an ant bear digging in the grassland.
The burrows of the ant bear provide shelter for many other animals once abandoned.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aardvark - African mammal that feeds on ants and termites

Synset POS: n
