field lupine

1. NOUN

/ ˈfiːld ˌluːpaɪn / (AmE), / ˈfiːld ˌljuːpɪn / (BrE)
a white-flowered Eurasian plant of the pea family, widely cultivated as forage and for erosion control
white-flowered Eurasian forage plant

Examples

The farmer planted field lupine to restore nutrients to the soil.
Erosion along the hillside was controlled with dense patches of field lupine.
She admired the delicate white blossoms of the field lupine in the meadow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lupine - any of several plants of the genus Lupinus
