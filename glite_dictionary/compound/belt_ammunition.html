belt ammunition

1. NOUN

/ bɛlt ˌæməˈnɪʃən / (AmE), / bɛlt ˌaməˈnɪʃən / (BrE)
Ammunition (typically of small caliber) arranged in flexible linked strips for use in a machine gun.
ammunition in linked strips for machine guns

Examples

The machine gun jammed because the belt ammunition was improperly loaded.
Soldiers inspected the belt ammunition before heading into the field exercise.
He watched as technicians prepared hundreds of rounds of belt ammunition for the demonstration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: belt-fed - fed by a belt of linked ammunition
