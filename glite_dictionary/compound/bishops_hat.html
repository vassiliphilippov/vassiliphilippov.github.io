bishop's hat

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɪʃəps hæt / (AmE), / ˈbɪʃəps hæt / (BrE)
a slow-growing, creeping plant with semi-evergreen leaves on erect wiry stems, used as ground cover; also known as Epimedium
creeping ground cover plant; Epimedium

Examples

The shady border in their garden was covered with bishop's hat to keep weeds down.
Gardeners often choose bishop's hat for its attractive foliage and hardiness.
In spring, delicate flowers appear on the bishop's hat, adding charm to the landscape.

Etymology

Named for the shape of the plant's flowers, which resemble a bishop's ceremonial hat (mitre).
