thud and blunder

1. NOUN

/ θʌd ənd ˈblʌndər / (AmE), / θʌd ənd ˈblʌndə / (BrE)
a situation or method characterized by clumsy mistakes, carelessness, or repeated failures
clumsy or mistake-prone approach

Examples

The company's thud and blunder strategy cost them both time and money.
Instead of careful planning, their progress was hampered by constant thud and blunder.
Management blamed the project's failure on a thud and blunder approach to problem-solving.

Synset Examples

She described the meeting as a thud and blunder affair.
Their thud and blunder approach led to missed opportunities.
The project failed due to thud and blunder tactics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trial and error - trying various methods until finding one that works
