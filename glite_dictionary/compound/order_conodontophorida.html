order Conodontophorida

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdər ˌkoʊnəˌdɒnˈtɒfərɪdə / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə ˌkəʊnəˌdɒnˈtɒfərɪdə / (BrE)
an extinct order of primitive, jawless vertebrates known primarily from their fossilized tooth-like elements; sometimes considered a separate phylum due to uncertain taxonomy
extinct order of primitive jawless vertebrates

Examples

Fossils from the order Conodontophorida provide important evidence for the evolution of early vertebrates.
Scientists debated whether the order Conodontophorida should be classified as a separate phylum.
The characteristic elements of the order Conodontophorida are widely used as index fossils in geology.

Etymology

From Greek 'konos' (cone) + 'odous' (tooth) + scientific suffix 'phorida'.
