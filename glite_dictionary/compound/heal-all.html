heal-all

1. NOUN

/ ˈhiːl ˌɔl / (AmE), / ˈhiːl ˌɔːl / (BrE)
a low-growing European perennial herb (Prunella vulgaris) with blue or violet flowers, believed in folk tradition to have medicinal properties; now widely naturalized in North America
a blue-flowered herb thought to have healing properties

Examples

The herbalist recommended heal-all as a natural remedy for sore throats.
Clusters of heal-all can often be found blooming in the meadows during summer.
Gardeners value heal-all for both its beauty and its reputation in traditional medicine.

Etymology

Named for its reputed medicinal qualities, combining 'heal' and 'all' to suggest it could cure all ailments.
