DIP switch

1. NOUN

/ dɪp swɪtʃ / (AmE), / dɪp swɪtʃ / (BrE)
a small manual electric switch, usually set in groups, used in computers and electronic equipment to configure or customize their operation
manual on-off switch in electronics

Examples

The motherboard has a row of DIP switches for setting hardware options.
You need to change the DIP switch settings to enable that feature.
Before powering up, check that every DIP switch is in the correct position.

Etymology

Acronym for 'dual in-line package', referring to the type of switch package used.
