task force

1. NOUN

/ tæsk fɔrs / (AmE), / tɑːsk fɔːs / (BrE)
a temporary group of military units formed to accomplish a specific objective or mission
temporary military unit for a specific mission

Examples

The government deployed a task force to deliver aid to the disaster zone.
A special task force was assembled to investigate the incident.
Several task forces collaborated in the operation to rescue the hostages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unit - an individual group or division in the military

2. NOUN

/ tæsk fɔrs / (AmE), / tɑːsk fɔːs / (BrE)
a group, especially of people from different departments or organizations, formed to carry out a particular task or to deal with a specific problem, often for a limited period
group formed to handle a specific task

Examples

The government established a task force to investigate the environmental crisis.
A joint task force was formed to improve cybersecurity across agencies.
The new task force will focus on reforming education policy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: committee - a group of people appointed for a specific function
  • Synonym: team - people working together to achieve a common goal

/ tæsk fɔɹs / (AmE), / tɑːsk fɔːs / (BrE)
