baseball diamond

1. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪsˌbɔl ˈdaɪmənd / (AmE), / ˈbeɪsbɔːl ˈdaɪəmənd / (BrE)
the square-shaped area of a baseball field formed by the three bases and home plate, where much of the action occurs during a game
the infield area of a baseball field

Examples

The children gathered around the baseball diamond after school to play a quick game.
He sprinted across the baseball diamond to make it home before the throw.
You can see the entire baseball diamond clearly from the top row of the bleachers.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: baseball field - the entire field used for playing baseball
  • Synonym: infield - the area of a baseball field inside the bases
