full throttle

1. NOUN

/ fʊl ˈθrɑt̬l̩ / (AmE), / fʊl ˈθrɒt.əl / (BrE)
At the highest possible speed or level of activity; with maximum effort or intensity.
maximum speed or effort

Examples

The project moved at full throttle once the funding was approved.
She approached her new job at full throttle, impressing her colleagues with her energy.
With sales season approaching, the store was operating full throttle to prepare for the rush.

Synset Examples

She pushed the engine to full throttle during the race.
We're going full throttle on this marketing campaign.
The team is working at full throttle to meet the deadline.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flat out - at maximum speed; as fast as possible
