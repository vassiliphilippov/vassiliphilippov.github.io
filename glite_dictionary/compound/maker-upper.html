maker-upper

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪkər ˈʌpər / (AmE), / ˈmeɪkə ˈʌpə / (BrE)
a person who invents stories or lies, often for entertainment or deception
someone who invents stories or lies

Examples

My cousin is such a maker-upper that you can never tell if she's being serious.
As a child, he was always the maker-upper of wild adventures at school.
You can't trust his version of events; he's a known maker-upper.

Synset Examples

The storyteller was known as a fantastic maker-upper.
As a maker-upper, he can create tales on the spot.
She's a great maker-upper when it comes to stories.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fibber - someone who tells fibs or small lies
  • Synonym: storyteller - someone who tells stories
  • Synonym: liar - someone who tells lies

Etymology

From 'make up' (to invent) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).
