Jewish Standard time

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒuːɪʃ ˈstændɚd taɪm / (AmE), / ˈdʒuːɪʃ ˈstændəd taɪm / (BrE)
a humorous, informal term used to refer to being late or having a relaxed attitude toward punctuality, especially in a social context
humorous term for being late
informal

Examples

He apologized for being twenty minutes late, smiling and blaming it on Jewish Standard time.
Our dinner party always seems to start according to Jewish Standard time rather than the time on the invitation.
You know you're running on Jewish Standard time when everyone arrives well after the scheduled hour.

Synset Examples

He always arrives late, claiming he runs on Jewish Standard time.
We scheduled the meeting for 3 PM, but I’ll be there by Jewish Standard Time.
