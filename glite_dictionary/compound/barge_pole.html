barge pole

1. NOUN

/ bɑrdʒ poʊl / (AmE), / bɑːdʒ pəʊl / (BrE)
a long pole used to propel or guide a barge, especially on a canal or river
long pole for moving a barge

Examples

The crew used a barge pole to push the boat away from the riverbank.
With a hefty barge pole, he guided the vessel through the narrow canal.
Traditional bargemen relied on a sturdy barge pole to maneuver their craft.

Synset Examples

I wouldn't touch it with a barge pole

Etymology

barge from Old French *barge* (boat), pole from Old English *pāl* (stake, pole).
