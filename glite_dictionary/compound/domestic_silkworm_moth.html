domestic silkworm moth

1. NOUN

/ dəˈmɛstɪk ˈsɪlkˌwɜrm mɔθ / (AmE), / dəˈmɛstɪk ˈsɪlkˌwɜːm mɒθ / (BrE)
A stocky, creamy-white moth native to Asia, domesticated by humans and the primary producer of commercial silk.
domesticated moth producing commercial silk

Examples

The domestic silkworm moth has been bred for thousands of years for its valuable silk.
Scientists study the domestic silkworm moth to improve the quality of silk production.
Without the domestic silkworm moth, the global silk industry would not exist as we know it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silkworm - larva of the silkworm moth, produces silk
  • Antonym: wild silkworm moth - closely related undomesticated species of silkworm moth
