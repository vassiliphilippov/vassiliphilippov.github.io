tarsal gland

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɑrsəl ˌɡlænd / (AmE), / ˈtɑːsəl ˌɡlænd / (BrE)
a long sebaceous gland along the edge of the eyelid that secretes an oily substance to lubricate the eyelids
sebaceous gland of the eyelid
medical

Examples

The tarsal gland helps prevent the eyelids from sticking together by releasing an oily substance.
A blockage of the tarsal gland can cause painful swelling in the eyelid.
Doctors examined her tarsal glands after she complained of dry, irritated eyes.

Synset Examples

bacterial infection of a Meibomian gland produces a stye

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Meibomian gland - another term for tarsal gland

Etymology

From 'tarsal', relating to the tarsus of the eyelid, and 'gland', from Latin 'glandula'.
