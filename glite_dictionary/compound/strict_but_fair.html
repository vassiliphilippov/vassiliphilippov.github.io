strict but fair

1. ADJ

/ strɪkt bət fɛr / (AmE), / strɪkt bət feə / (BrE)
demanding discipline or adherence to rules while treating others in an unbiased and just way
firm but just and impartial

Examples

The manager was known for being strict but fair with all her employees.
Many students prefer teachers who are strict but fair rather than overly lenient or harsh.
Parents often strive to be strict but fair when setting rules for their children.

Synset Examples

The schoolmaster was strict but fair.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: strict - demanding obedience or exactness
  • Degree Lower: lenient - more permissive, not strict or severe
  • Synonym: fair - impartial and just
