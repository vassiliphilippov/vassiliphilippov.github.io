iodinated protein

1. NOUN

/ ˌaɪ.əˌdeɪ.t̬ɪd proʊˈtiːn / (AmE), / ˌaɪ.əˌdeɪ.tɪd prəʊˈtiːn / (BrE)
a protein that has iodine atoms incorporated into its structure, often as a result of a chemical reaction or modification
protein containing iodine atoms

Examples

Thyroglobulin is an important iodinated protein produced by the thyroid gland.
Researchers analyzed the structure of the iodinated protein using mass spectrometry.
The presence of iodinated proteins can be detected through specific staining methods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: protein - large biomolecules composed of amino acids
