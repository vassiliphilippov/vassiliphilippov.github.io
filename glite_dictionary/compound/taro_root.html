taro root

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɑːroʊ ruːt / (AmE), / ˈtɑːrəʊ ruːt / (BrE)
the starchy tuberous root of a tropical plant, eaten as a staple food in many parts of the world
starchy tuberous root used as food

Examples

She boiled the taro root and served it with coconut milk for dinner.
Farmers in Hawaii grow taro root as an essential part of local cuisine and culture.
The soup gets its thick texture from the addition of diced taro root and spices.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: taro - tropical plant whose root is edible

Etymology

From Tahitian or Maori *taro*, the name of the plant.
