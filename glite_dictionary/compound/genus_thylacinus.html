genus Thylacinus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs θaɪˈlæs.ɪ.nəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs θɪˈlæs.ɪ.nəs / (BrE)
A genus of carnivorous marsupials, including the extinct Tasmanian wolf (Thylacinus cynocephalus).
genus of carnivorous marsupials (including Tasmanian wolf)

Examples

The genus Thylacinus is best known for its only living species, the thylacine, which is now extinct.
Scientists have studied fossils belonging to the genus Thylacinus to understand Australian marsupial evolution.
Before extinction, the genus Thylacinus was found in Tasmania and parts of Australia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thylacine - a carnivorous marsupial also known as the Tasmanian tiger or wolf

Etymology

From Greek thylakinos (pouch) and Latin genus (kind, type).
