North Atlantic Treaty Organization

1. PROPN

/ nɔrθ ætˈlæntɪk ˈtriti ˌɔrɡənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / nɔːθ ətˈlæntɪk ˈtriːti ˌɔːɡənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
An international military alliance established in 1949 for collective defense among member states, primarily in Europe and North America.
international military alliance (est. 1949)

Examples

The North Atlantic Treaty Organization was founded to provide collective security for its member countries.
Several European nations joined the North Atlantic Treaty Organization after the end of the Cold War.
The United States is one of the founding members of the North Atlantic Treaty Organization.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: NATO - abbreviation for North Atlantic Treaty Organization

Etymology

From the name of the North Atlantic Treaty signed in 1949, forming the organization among signatory nations.
