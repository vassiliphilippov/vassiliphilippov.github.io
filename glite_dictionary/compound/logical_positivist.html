logical positivist

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɑdʒɪkəl ˈpɑzətɪvɪst / (AmE), / ˈlɒdʒɪkəl ˈpɒzətɪvɪst / (BrE)
a person who believes that only statements verifiable through direct observation or logical proof are meaningful, and all other statements are meaningless
philosopher supporting empirically verifiable statements

Examples

The logical positivist argued that metaphysical claims are meaningless unless they can be empirically verified.
As a logical positivist, she rejected any theories that could not be tested by observation or experiment.
Many critics of the logical positivist movement believe that it placed too much emphasis on scientific language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: empiricist - philosopher emphasizing knowledge from sensory experience

Etymology

Formed from 'logic' (from Greek logikē) and 'positivist' (from French positiviste, itself from English 'positive' and '-ist').
