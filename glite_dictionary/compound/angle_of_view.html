angle of view

1. NOUN

/ ˈæŋɡəl əv vjuː / (AmE), / ˈæŋɡl əv vjuː / (BrE)
The angular extent of a scene that is captured by a photographic lens, usually measured in degrees.
the extent of a scene captured by a lens

Examples

The wide angle of view allowed the photographer to capture the entire landscape in a single shot.
A telephoto lens has a much narrower angle of view compared to a wide-angle lens.
Understanding your camera's angle of view is essential for framing your photos effectively.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: field of view - the observable area seen at a given moment through a device
