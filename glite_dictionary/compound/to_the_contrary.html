to the contrary

1. ADV

/ tə ðə kənˈtrɛri / (AmE), / tə ðə ˈkɒntrəri / (BrE)
in a way that is opposite or completely different from what has just been stated or expected
in opposition to what was said or expected

Examples

The rumors claimed the company was failing, but all evidence to the contrary was ignored.
Despite his assurances to the contrary, she remained unconvinced about his explanation.
There was no proof to the contrary, so the original decision stood.

Synset Examples

he didn't stay home; on the contrary, he went out with his friends

Related Terms

  • Synonym: on the contrary - used to introduce a statement that says the opposite of what has just been said
