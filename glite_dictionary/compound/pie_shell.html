pie shell

1. NOUN

/ paɪ ʃɛl / (AmE), / paɪ ʃɛl / (BrE)
a baked or unbaked pastry crust used to hold the filling of a pie
pastry crust for holding pie filling

Examples

She filled the pie shell with apples and sprinkled it with cinnamon before baking.
The recipe calls for a pre-baked pie shell to save preparation time.
Store-bought pie shells are convenient, but homemade ones often taste better.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pie crust - the pastry shell of a pie
