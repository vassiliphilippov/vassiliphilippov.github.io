triple-crown season

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrɪpəl kraʊn ˈsizən / (AmE), / ˈtrɪpəl kraʊn ˈsiːzn̩ / (BrE)
A baseball season in which a player wins the triple crown by leading the league in batting average, home runs, and runs batted in.
baseball season with triple crown winner

Examples

Miguel Cabrera's 2012 was a memorable triple-crown season for baseball fans everywhere.
The team celebrated their star player's triple-crown season with a special ceremony at the stadium.
It's rare to witness a triple-crown season; only a few players have ever accomplished it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: triple crown - an achievement in baseball when a player leads the league in batting average, home runs, and runs batted in
