ground squirrel

1. NOUN

/ ɡraʊnd ˈskwɜrl / (AmE), / ɡraʊnd ˈskwɪrəl / (BrE)
a small burrowing rodent found in North America and Eurasia, living chiefly on the ground and often regarded as a pest to crops
burrowing rodent; crop pest

Examples

Farmers often struggle with ground squirrels destroying their crops during the summer months.
You can easily spot a ground squirrel darting between its burrow and nearby bushes.
Unlike tree squirrels, ground squirrels spend most of their lives on or under the ground.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chipmunk - a small, striped, burrowing ground squirrel found chiefly in North America

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡraʊnd ˌskwɪrl / (AmE), / ˈɡraʊnd ˌskwɪəl / (BrE)
a small, burrowing rodent found chiefly in North America, with cheek pouches and typically having stripes on its body
a small burrowing rodent with stripes

Examples

A ground squirrel darted across the hiking trail and disappeared into its burrow.
Farmers often see ground squirrels in their fields during the spring.
The distinctive stripes on the ground squirrel help it blend in with the grasses and soil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chipmunk - a small, striped North American rodent of the squirrel family
