fan-jet

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæn ˌdʒɛt / (AmE), / ˈfan ˌdʒɛt / (BrE)
a jet engine in which a fan driven by a turbine delivers additional air to the combustion chamber, increasing thrust
type of jet engine with a turbine-driven fan

Examples

Modern airliners use fan-jet engines for greater efficiency and quieter operation.
The mechanics inspected the fan-jet before the transatlantic flight.
He spent years designing a more advanced fan-jet for commercial aircraft.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: turbofan - a jet engine with a large fan at the front, very similar to fan-jet

Etymology

From 'fan' (device that moves air) + 'jet' (from jet engine); term dates to mid-20th century aviation.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfænˌdʒɛt / (AmE), / ˈfanˌdʒɛt / (BrE)
An airplane powered by a fanjet engine, which uses a jet propulsion system with a large fan to generate additional thrust.
airplane with fanjet engine

Examples

The airline upgraded its fleet to include the latest fan-jet models for improved efficiency.
A fan-jet can travel at higher speeds than most propeller-driven aircraft.
Engineers designed a new fan-jet that reduces noise while increasing thrust.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jet - an aircraft powered by a jet engine
  • Antonym: turboprop - an aircraft powered by a turboprop engine

Etymology

From 'fan' + 'jet', referring to the fan in the jet engine that increases thrust.

Source: WordNet
