foetid pothos

1. NOUN

/ ˈfiː.tɪd ˈpoʊ.θɑs / (AmE), / ˈfəʊ.tɪd ˈpɒ.θɒs / (BrE)
A deciduous, perennial, low-growing swamp plant of eastern North America with a strong unpleasant odor, minute flowers, and a mottled greenish or purple cowl-shaped spathe.
a fetid swamp plant of eastern North America

Examples

The foetid pothos is easily identified by its foul smell in the spring.
Botanists studied the wetland habitat favored by the foetid pothos.
We spotted a rare foetid pothos blooming near the edge of the swamp.

Etymology

From Latin 'foetidus' meaning 'foul-smelling', and 'Pothos', a genus name.
