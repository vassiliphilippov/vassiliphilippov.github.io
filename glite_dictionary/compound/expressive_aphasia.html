expressive aphasia

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˈsprɛsɪv əˈfeɪʒə / (AmE), / ɪkˈsprɛsɪv əˈfeɪziə / (BrE)
A condition in which a person has severe difficulties expressing themselves through speech or writing, though understanding remains relatively intact.
difficulty expressing speech or writing
medical

Examples

After his stroke, he was diagnosed with expressive aphasia and struggled to communicate his thoughts.
Speech therapy can help individuals with expressive aphasia regain some of their language abilities.
Her writing was affected by expressive aphasia, making it difficult for her to complete even simple sentences.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: receptive aphasia - aphasia in which understanding of language is severely impaired

Etymology

From 'expressive' (relating to expression) and 'aphasia', from Greek 'aphatos' (speechless).
