entrenched in

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈtrɛntʃt ɪn / (AmE), / ɪnˈtrɛntʃt ɪn / (BrE)
(of ideas, attitudes, habits, etc.) firmly established within something and difficult to change or remove.
firmly established within something

Examples

Old traditions remain entrenched in many rural areas.
Racism was entrenched in aspects of society for decades.
His opinions are so entrenched in his mind that no argument can change them.

Synset Examples

These beliefs are entrenched in their culture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ingrained in - firmly established and deeply rooted within

Etymology

From the verb 'entrench', which comes from Old French 'en-' (in) + 'trenche' (trench).
