whole works

1. NOUN

/ hoʊl wɝks / (AmE), / həʊl wɜːks / (BrE)
everything that is available or included, usually used to refer to all the possible parts or options of something.
everything available; the entirety of something

Examples

He ordered a burger with the whole works, including cheese, bacon, and extra sauce.
When you renovate your house, are you going for just a new kitchen or the whole works?
For her birthday, she wanted a spa day with the whole works: massage, facial, and manicure.

Synset Examples

we saw the whole shebang
a hotdog with the works
we took on the whole caboodle
for $10 you get the full treatment

Related Terms

  • Synonym: the works - all the available items or extras
  • Synonym: whole shebang - the entirety of something; everything involved
  • Synonym: whole caboodle - the entire lot; everything
