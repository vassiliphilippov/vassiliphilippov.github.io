flying field

1. NOUN

/ ˈflaɪɪŋ fiːld / (AmE), / ˈflaɪɪŋ fiːld / (BrE)
an area of land prepared for the taking off and landing of aircraft, usually without extensive facilities
airfield for planes to take off and land

Examples

The old military flying field was converted into a commercial airport.
Pilots practiced emergency landings at the remote flying field.
The town's annual air show is held at the local flying field.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: airfield - an area of land used for the operation of aircraft
  • Degree Higher: airport - a complex for the arrival, departure, and maintenance of aircraft
