English bulldog

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ ˈbʊlˌdɔɡ / (AmE), / ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ ˈbʊl.dɒɡ / (BrE)
a sturdy, muscular dog breed with a short coat, large head, and pronounced lower jaw, originally developed in England for bull baiting
breed of muscular, short-haired dog

Examples

The English bulldog is known for its gentle temperament despite its tough appearance.
Many families choose an English bulldog as a pet because of its loyal nature.
He takes his English bulldog for a walk in the park every morning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bulldog - a breed of dog known for its muscular build and wrinkled face
