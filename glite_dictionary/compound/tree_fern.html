tree fern

1. NOUN

/ ˈtri fɝn / (AmE), / ˈtriː fɜːn / (BrE)
A tropical or subtropical fern with a thick woody stem and a crown of large fronds, especially found in Australia and New Zealand.
tropical fern with woody stem

Examples

The tree fern thrives in the humid forests of New Zealand.
Several tree ferns lined the garden path, their broad fronds reaching overhead.
Scientists studied the growth patterns of the ancient tree fern species.
