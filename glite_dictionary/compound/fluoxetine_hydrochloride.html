fluoxetine hydrochloride

1. NOUN

/ fluːˈɑːksətiːn ˌhaɪdroʊˈklɔːraɪd / (AmE), / fluːˈɒksətiːn ˌhaɪdrəʊˈklɔːraɪd / (BrE)
a selective serotonin reuptake inhibitor (SSRI) used as an antidepressant to treat depression, anxiety disorders, and certain other medical conditions
SSRI antidepressant medication
medical

Examples

Many doctors prescribe fluoxetine hydrochloride to patients suffering from major depressive disorder.
Side effects should be discussed before starting fluoxetine hydrochloride treatment.
Before switching medications, consult your physician if you are taking fluoxetine hydrochloride.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Prozac - brand name for fluoxetine hydrochloride
  • Degree Lower: sertraline - another SSRI antidepressant
  • Synonym: paroxetine - another SSRI, sometimes considered similar in strength

Etymology

From 'fluoxetine', derived from its chemical structure (contains fluorine and oxetine components), and 'hydrochloride' from the salt form used in medicine.
