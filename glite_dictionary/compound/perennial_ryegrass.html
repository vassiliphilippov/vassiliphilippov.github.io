perennial ryegrass

1. NOUN

/ pəˈrɛniəl ˈraɪˌɡræs / (AmE), / pəˈrɛnɪəl ˈraɪɡrɑːs / (BrE)
A European perennial grass (Lolium perenne) widely grown for pasture, hay, and as a lawn grass.
species of grass used for lawns and pastures

Examples

The new golf course was seeded with perennial ryegrass to ensure a dense, durable lawn.
Farmers prefer perennial ryegrass for grazing because it recovers quickly after cutting.
In cooler climates, perennial ryegrass provides excellent turf for sports fields.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Lolium perenne - scientific name for perennial ryegrass

Etymology

From Latin 'perennis' (lasting through the year) and 'ryegrass'.
