hit parade

1. NOUN

/ hɪt pəˈreɪd / (AmE), / hɪt pəˈreɪd / (BrE)
a list or ranking of the most popular or best people or items of a particular kind, especially songs or musical tracks
list of most popular items, especially songs

Examples

The radio station announced this week's hit parade featuring the top ten songs.
Her new novel quickly climbed to number one on the literary hit parade.
Classic films from the 1980s still dominate the critics' hit parade for best comedies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chart - ranking of the most popular songs or items, typically in music

Etymology

First popularized in the 1930s referring to radio programs listing top songs; 'hit' (successful song) + 'parade'.

2. NOUN

/ hɪt pəˈreɪd / (AmE), / hɪt pəˈreɪd / (BrE)
a ranked list of songs that are currently the most popular, usually based on sales, radio airplay, or streaming
a list of top popular songs

Examples

The band's new single shot straight to number one on the hit parade.
In the 1960s, being at the top of the hit parade meant widespread fame for a musician.
We used to listen to the weekly hit parade on the radio to find out which songs were trending.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chart - official list of the most popular songs or albums at a given time

Etymology

From 'hit' (popular song) and 'parade' (a public procession or display); origin in 1930s U.S. radio.
