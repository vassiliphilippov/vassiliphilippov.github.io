game of Tetris

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡeɪm əv ˈtɛtrɪs / (AmE), / ˈɡeɪm əv ˈtɛtrɪs / (BrE)
a metaphor for the ongoing effort to fit tasks or challenges together efficiently, as in the video game Tetris
metaphor for managing life's challenges

Examples

She said organizing her schedule felt like playing a game of Tetris every day.
Balancing work, family, and hobbies can resemble a never-ending game of Tetris.
Trying to pack all my belongings into a small suitcase was a real-life game of Tetris.

Synset Examples

He compared life to a game of Tetris, always fitting pieces together.

Etymology

From the video game 'Tetris', created by Alexey Pajitnov in 1984.
