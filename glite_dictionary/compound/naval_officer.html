naval officer

1. NOUN

/ ˈneɪvəl ˈɑfəsər / (AmE), / ˈneɪvəl ˈɒfɪsə / (BrE)
an officer who holds a position of authority in a navy
officer in the navy

Examples

After years of dedicated service, she became a distinguished naval officer in her country's fleet.
The naval officer gave the crew precise orders before the ship left harbor.
Many aspiring sailors dream of one day achieving the rank of naval officer.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: army officer - an officer in the army
