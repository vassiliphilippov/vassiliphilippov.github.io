rice rat

1. NOUN

/ ˈraɪs ˌræt / (AmE), / ˈraɪs ˌræt / (BrE)
a hardy, agile rat found in grassy marshes of Mexico and the southeastern United States
a rodent native to marshes in Mexico and the southeastern US

Examples

The marshlands are home to the elusive rice rat that feeds on grains and insects.
Biologists studied the population of rice rats to assess wetland health.
A rice rat scurried quickly through the tall grass as we approached.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rat - a small rodent with a pointed snout and long tail
