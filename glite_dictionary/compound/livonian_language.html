Livonian language

1. NOUN

/ lɪˈvoʊniən ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / lɪˈvəʊniən ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
a Finnic language once spoken by the Livonian people in parts of Estonia and Latvia
Finnic language once spoken in Estonia and Latvia

Examples

The Livonian language is now considered nearly extinct, with only a few speakers remaining.
Linguists have documented many aspects of the Livonian language to preserve it for future generations.
There are efforts to revive the Livonian language in the region through language courses and cultural events.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Finnish - a Finnic language spoken in Finland
  • Synonym: Estonian - a Finnic language spoken in Estonia

Etymology

From 'Livonia,' a historical region of present-day Estonia and Latvia, plus 'language.'
