to remain afloat

1. VERB

/ rɪˈmeɪn əˈfloʊt / (AmE), / rɪˈmeɪn əˈfləʊt / (BrE)
to continue to have enough money to operate or avoid financial failure
to avoid financial collapse

Examples

Small businesses often struggle to remain afloat during economic downturns.
The government provided loans to help airlines remain afloat during the crisis.
She used her savings to remain afloat after losing her job.

Synset Examples

The company is struggling to remain afloat in this economy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to stay afloat - to continue to survive financially
