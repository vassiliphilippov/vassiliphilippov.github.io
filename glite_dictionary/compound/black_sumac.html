black sumac

1. NOUN

/ blæk ˈsuː.mæk / (AmE), / blæk ˈsjuː.mæk / (BrE)
a common nonpoisonous shrub native to eastern North America, with compound leaves, green flowers in clusters, and red berries
North American nonpoisonous shrub

Examples

The woods behind her house were thick with black sumac and other native shrubs.
Birds love to eat the bright red berries produced by black sumac in the autumn.
Despite its name, black sumac is harmless and should not be confused with its poisonous relatives.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sumac - any of various shrubs or small trees of the genus Rhus
