dry plate

1. NOUN

/ ˈdraɪ pleɪt / (AmE), / ˈdraɪ pleɪt / (BrE)
a glass plate coated with a light-sensitive gelatinous emulsion, formerly used in photography to capture images before the advent of film
glass plate for early photography

Examples

Early photographers often used dry plate technology before flexible film became common.
The dry plate process revolutionized photography by making it faster and more convenient than wet plates.
A rare dry plate negative from the nineteenth century was recently discovered in an archive.
