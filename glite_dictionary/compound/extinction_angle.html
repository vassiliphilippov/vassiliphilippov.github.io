extinction angle

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˈstɪŋkʃən ˈæŋɡəl / (AmE), / ɪkˈstɪŋkʃən ˈæŋɡəl / (BrE)
the angle through which a crystal must be rotated from its axis before it appears darkest when viewed in polarized light
angle at which a crystal goes dark in polarized light

Examples

The extinction angle is crucial for identifying minerals under a polarizing microscope.
Geologists measure the extinction angle to distinguish between mineral species.
Certain crystals have a characteristic extinction angle when observed using polarized light.
