squall line

1. NOUN

/ skwɑl laɪn / (AmE), / skwɔːl laɪn / (BrE)
a narrow band of intense thunderstorms or squalls, often forming along or ahead of a cold front
a line of intense thunderstorms

Examples

A squall line developed rapidly and produced heavy rain and strong winds across the county.
Pilots were warned about the approaching squall line moving in from the west.
The weather report mentioned a squall line capable of generating severe thunderstorms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cold front - the forward edge of a mass of cold air
