ethanoyl group

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛθəˌnɔɪl ɡruːp / (AmE), / ˌiːθəˌnɔɪl ɡruːp / (BrE)
the functional group with the structure CH3CO–, derived from acetic acid; also known as the acetyl group.
the CH3CO– functional group in chemistry
medical

Examples

The ethanoyl group is an important component in acetylcholine, a key neurotransmitter.
Attaching an ethanoyl group to the molecule alters its chemical properties significantly.
The presence of an ethanoyl group can be detected through specific spectroscopic techniques.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acetyl group - the same chemical group, CH3CO–, commonly called the acetyl group

Etymology

From 'ethanoyl' (systematic name for acetyl) based on 'ethane' + '-oyl' (denoting acyl groups).
