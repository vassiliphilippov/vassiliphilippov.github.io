fig-bird

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪɡˌbɝd / (AmE), / ˈfɪɡˌbɜːd / (BrE)
A greenish-yellow Australian oriole that feeds mainly on figs and other fruits.
Australian oriole feeding on figs and fruit

Examples

The fig-bird is often found in moist woodlands near rivers in eastern Australia.
We watched a brightly colored fig-bird feeding on ripe figs in the garden.
Fig-birds play an important role in spreading the seeds of native fruit trees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oriole - any of various Old World or New World birds with bright plumage

Etymology

From 'fig' (the fruit) + 'bird', named for its diet.
