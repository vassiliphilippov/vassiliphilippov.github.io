Mount Everest

1. PROPN

/ maʊnt ˈɛvərɪst / (AmE), / maʊnt ˈɛvərɪst / (BrE)
the highest mountain in the world, located in the central Himalayas on the border between Tibet and Nepal, rising to 8,848 meters (29,029 feet)
the world's highest mountain

Examples

Climbing Mount Everest is considered one of the greatest achievements in mountaineering.
Many expeditions attempt to reach the summit of Mount Everest each year.
Weather conditions on Mount Everest can change rapidly and become extremely dangerous.

Etymology

Named after Sir George Everest, British Surveyor General of India (1830–1843).
