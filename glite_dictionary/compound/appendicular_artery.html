appendicular artery

1. NOUN

/ əˌpɛnˈdɪkjələr ˈɑːrˌtɛri / (AmE), / əˌpɛnˈdɪkjʊlə ˈɑːtəri / (BrE)
a branch of the ileocolic artery that supplies blood to the vermiform appendix
artery supplying the appendix
medical

Examples

During the surgery, the appendicular artery was carefully identified and ligated.
A blockage in the appendicular artery can lead to appendiceal ischemia.
The appendicular artery branches off from the ileocolic artery to supply the appendix.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ileocolic artery - an artery that supplies the ileum and colon

Etymology

From Latin 'appendix' (appendage) and 'arteria' (artery).
