New Jersey

1. PROPN

/ nu ˈdʒɝzi / (AmE), / njuː ˈdʒɜːzi / (BrE)
A state in the northeastern United States, formerly one of the British colonies, with historical capitals including Elizabethtown, Perth Amboy, and Burlington.
state in the northeastern US; former British colony

Examples

Many immigrants settled in New Jersey after arriving in America in the nineteenth century.
The colonial government of New Jersey was based in several different cities over the years.
Modern New Jersey is known for its diverse population and coastal resorts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: New York - state in the northeastern US; former British colony

Etymology

Named after the island of Jersey in the English Channel.
