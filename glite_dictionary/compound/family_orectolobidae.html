family Orectolobidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌɔːrktoʊˈlɒbɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˌɒrɛktəˈlɒbɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of sharks that includes nurse sharks and carpet sharks, characterized by their flattened bodies and distinctive patterns.
family of nurse and carpet sharks

Examples

Scientists studied the unique camouflage patterns found in the family Orectolobidae species.
Many members of the family Orectolobidae are found in shallow coastal waters of the Indo-Pacific region.
The whale shark is not part of the family Orectolobidae, despite its similar feeding behavior.

Etymology

From the genus name Orectolobus, combined with Latin '-idae' (indicating a zoological family).
