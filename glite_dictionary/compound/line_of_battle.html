line of battle

1. NOUN

/ laɪn əv ˈbætəl / (AmE), / laɪn ɒv ˈbætəl / (BrE)
A line formed by troops or ships prepared to deliver or receive an attack.
military formation for attack or defense

Examples

The fleet formed a line of battle as the enemy approached.
The general ordered his troops to take up a line of battle along the ridge.
During the eighteenth century, ships often engaged in combat by sailing in a line of battle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: battle line - A line along which a battle is fought; synonym for 'line of battle'
