quantum theory

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɑntəm ˈθɪəri / (AmE), / ˈkwɒntəm ˈθɪəri / (BrE)
A theory in physics stating that certain physical properties, such as energy, exist only in discrete amounts called quanta.
physics theory about discrete units (quanta)

Examples

Quantum theory revolutionized our understanding of how light and matter interact at the smallest scales.
Many technological advancements, including lasers and transistors, are based on the principles of quantum theory.
Einstein contributed to quantum theory by explaining the photoelectric effect.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quantum mechanics - The branch of physics based on quantum theory describing the behavior of energy and matter on the atomic and subatomic level.

Etymology

From Latin 'quantum' (how much) and 'theory' from Greek 'theoria' (contemplation, speculation).
