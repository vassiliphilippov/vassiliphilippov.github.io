line of descent

1. NOUN

/ laɪn əv dɪˈsɛnt / (AmE), / laɪn əv dɪˈsɛnt / (BrE)
the sequence of ancestry or kinship linking an individual to their forebears
ancestral lineage; succession of progenitors

Examples

His royal line of descent can be traced back to the 12th century.
Genetic traits are often passed down through the line of descent.
The family tree illustrates the line of descent from the earliest ancestor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ancestry - one's family or ancestral descent
  • Antonym: progeny - descendants or offspring

2. NOUN

/ laɪn əv dɪˈsɛnt / (AmE), / laɪn əv dɪˈsent / (BrE)
a series of people in a family who are directly related over generations; ancestry or lineage
ancestry; direct family lineage

Examples

The royal family can trace their line of descent back for centuries.
Scientists studied the genetic line of descent among the population.
Questions about his line of descent emerged during the inheritance dispute.

Synset Examples

his entire lineage has been warriors

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lineage - direct descent from an ancestor
  • Synonym: ancestry - one's family or ethnic descent
  • Degree Lower: descendant - a person who is descended from a certain ancestor
