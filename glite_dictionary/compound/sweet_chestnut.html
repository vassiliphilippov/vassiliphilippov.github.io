sweet chestnut

1. NOUN

/ swit ˈtʃɛs.nʌt / (AmE), / swiːt ˈtʃɛs.nʌt / (BrE)
A large deciduous tree (Castanea sativa) native to southern Europe and western Asia, cultivated for its edible nuts and valued wood.
tree producing edible chestnuts

Examples

The sweet chestnut is commonly planted in parks for its broad, spreading branches.
Many European desserts use the nuts from the sweet chestnut as a main ingredient.
The leaves of the sweet chestnut are long and serrated, making the tree easy to identify.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chestnut - deciduous tree bearing edible nuts or its nut
