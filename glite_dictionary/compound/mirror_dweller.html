mirror dweller

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪrər ˌdwɛlər / (AmE), / ˈmɪrə ˌdwɛlə / (BrE)
A mythical being believed to inhabit a mirror, or figuratively, a person who is deeply introspective or self-reflective.
mythical being in a mirror; introspective person

Examples

In old folktales, the mirror dweller might emerge at midnight to communicate with the living.
As an artist, she often felt like a mirror dweller, observing and analyzing her own emotions.
The villagers whispered about a mirror dweller who haunted the abandoned mansion.

Synset Examples

Legends say that the mirror dweller grants wishes to those pure of heart.
She felt like a mirror dweller, trapped in her own thoughts.
The mirror dweller warned them about the dangers ahead.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: introspective - given to examining one's own thoughts and feelings
  • Synonym: spirit - a supernatural being or essence, often found in myths and folklore
