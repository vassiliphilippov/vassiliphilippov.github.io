voltaic cell

1. NOUN

/ voʊlˈteɪɪk sɛl / (AmE), / vɒlˈteɪɪk sɛl / (BrE)
A device that produces electricity through an irreversible chemical reaction, converting chemical energy into electrical energy; it cannot be recharged.
electric cell producing energy by chemical reaction

Examples

The classic lemon battery is a simple example of a voltaic cell.
Dry cells used in flashlights are a common kind of voltaic cell that cannot be recharged.
A voltaic cell produces electricity by an irreversible chemical reaction between two electrodes and an electrolyte.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: galvanic cell - an electric cell that generates electricity by chemical reaction, usually a synonym for voltaic cell
  • Antonym: electrolytic cell - an electric cell in which a chemical reaction is driven by an external electric current

Etymology

Named after Italian physicist Alessandro Volta, who invented the first battery.

2. NOUN

/ voʊlˈteɪ.ɪk sɛl / (AmE), / vɒlˈteɪ.ɪk sɛl / (BrE)
A device that generates an electric current by converting chemical energy into electrical energy through a chemical reaction between two different metals or other electrodes.
device converting chemical to electrical energy

Examples

A single voltaic cell can be used to power small electronic circuits.
The teacher demonstrated how a voltaic cell produces electricity using copper and zinc plates.
Many household batteries are actually composed of more than one voltaic cell.

Synset Examples

The voltaic cell powered the small device efficiently.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: battery - a device consisting of one or more cells that generate electricity
  • Antonym: electrolytic cell - an electrochemical cell that drives a non-spontaneous reaction using electrical energy

Etymology

Named after Italian physicist Alessandro Volta, who invented the cell.
