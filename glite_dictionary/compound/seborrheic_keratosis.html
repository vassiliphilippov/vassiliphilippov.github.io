seborrheic keratosis

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛbəˈriːɪk ˌkɛrəˈtoʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌsiːbəˈriːɪk ˌkɛrəˈtəʊsɪs / (BrE)
a benign skin condition marked by well-defined, wart-like growths that may be itchy and have a greasy surface
benign wart-like skin growth
medical

Examples

My dermatologist identified the dark patch as a seborrheic keratosis and said it was harmless.
Patients with seborrheic keratosis often notice the lesions increasing with age.
The seborrheic keratosis on her back was removed for cosmetic reasons.

Etymology

From Greek 'sebo-' (relating to sebum), 'rhoia' (flow), and 'keratosis' (formation of horny tissue).
