Van de Graaff generator

1. NOUN

/ ˌvæn də ˈɡræf ˌdʒɛnəˌreɪtər / (AmE), / ˌvan də ˈɡrɑːf ˌdʒɛnəˌreɪtə / (BrE)
an electrical device that generates high voltages by accumulating static electricity using a moving belt and a hollow metal globe
device for generating high voltages

Examples

The physics demonstration included a Van de Graaff generator to show how hair stands on end when charged.
A Van de Graaff generator can produce sparks several centimeters long due to the high voltage it creates.
Students watched as the Van de Graaff generator made their hair rise during the science experiment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: electrostatic generator - a device that produces electricity by means of static charges

Etymology

Named after American physicist Robert J. Van de Graaff (1901–1967), who invented it in 1929.
