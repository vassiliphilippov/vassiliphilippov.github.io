suborder Myxinoidei

1. NOUN

/ sʌbˌɔrdər mɪkˌsaɪnɔɪˈdiːaɪ / (AmE), / sʌbˌɔːdə mɪksɪnɔɪˈdiːaɪ / (BrE)
A group of jawless fish, including hagfishes, distinguished from lampreys.
taxonomic group of hagfishes

Examples

Scientists classify the hagfish within the suborder Myxinoidei due to their unique anatomical features.
Unlike lampreys, species of the suborder Myxinoidei have elongated, eel-like bodies without vertebrae.
The biology textbook contained a detailed section on the suborder Myxinoidei and its evolutionary history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hagfish - slimy, eel-shaped jawless marine fish
  • Antonym: lamprey - jawless fish related to but distinct from hagfishes

Etymology

From New Latin: 'suborder' (taxonomic rank) and 'Myxinoidei', from Greek 'myxa' (slime) + '-oid' (like) + '-ei' (plural suffix, taxonomic use).
