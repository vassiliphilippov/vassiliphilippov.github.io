charter school

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɑːɹɾɚ skuːl / (AmE), / ˈtʃɑːtə skuːl / (BrE)
A publicly funded independent school established by teachers, parents, or community groups under a charter with a local or national authority, often with more flexibility than traditional public schools.
publicly funded independent school

Examples

The city plans to open a new charter school focused on science and technology next year.
Many parents appreciate the innovative teaching methods offered at their local charter school.
Students at a charter school often benefit from smaller class sizes and specialized curricula.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: magnet school - a public school offering special instruction and programs not available elsewhere, designed to attract a diverse student body
  • Degree Lower: public school - a school that is funded and run by the government

Etymology

From 'charter', referencing the official document granting the school's authority, and 'school'.
