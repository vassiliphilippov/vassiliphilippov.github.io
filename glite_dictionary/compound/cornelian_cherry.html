cornelian cherry

1. NOUN

/ kɔrˈniliən ˈtʃɛri / (AmE), / kɔːˈniːlɪən ˈtʃɛri / (BrE)
A deciduous European shrub or small tree (Cornus mas) with bright red, edible fruit.
European shrub or tree with red fruit

Examples

The cornelian cherry produces clusters of bright red fruit in late summer.
She planted a cornelian cherry in her garden for its ornamental value and edible berries.
Jams made from cornelian cherry fruit are popular in Eastern Europe.

Etymology

From Latin 'cornus' (dogwood) and 'Cornelian' (relating to the gemstone carnelian, for the fruit's color).
