family Ziphiidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæmɪli zɪˈfaɪɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli zɪˈfaɪɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of marine mammals known as beaked whales, sometimes classified with sperm whales in earlier taxonomy.
a family of beaked whales

Examples

Recent research has revealed new species within the family Ziphiidae.
Members of the family Ziphiidae are known for their deep-diving capabilities.
Some taxonomists previously grouped the family Ziphiidae with sperm whales, but they are now classified separately.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ziphius - a genus of beaked whales within the family Ziphiidae
  • Antonym: Physeteridae - family of sperm whales, formerly including beaked whales

Etymology

From New Latin *Ziphiidae*, based on *Ziphius* (a genus of beaked whales) + -idae (family suffix).
