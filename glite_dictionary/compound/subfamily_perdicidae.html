subfamily Perdicidae

1. NOUN

/ sʌbˌfæmɪli pərˈdɪsɪdi / (AmE), / sʌbˌfæmɪli pəˈdɪsɪdiː / (BrE)
A scientific grouping in ornithology comprising the Old World partridges.
bird group: Old World partridges

Examples

The subfamily Perdicidae contains many species commonly found throughout Europe and Asia.
Recent research has led to the reclassification of several birds within the subfamily Perdicidae.
Hunting regulations for the subfamily Perdicidae vary depending on the country and conservation status.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: partridge - a bird of the pheasant family, especially in the Old World

Etymology

From New Latin 'Perdicidae', based on Latin 'perdix' (partridge) plus '-idae' (a zoological family suffix).
