western chimpanzee

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɛstərn tʃɪmˈpænzi / (AmE), / ˈwɛstən tʃɪmˈpænzi / (BrE)
A subspecies of chimpanzee native to western Africa, characterized by masked or pale faces and genetically distinct from other chimpanzee populations.
subspecies of chimpanzee from western Africa

Examples

The western chimpanzee is now primarily found in isolated forests of West Africa.
Unlike other subspecies, the western chimpanzee often exhibits a lighter face and unique behaviors.
Researchers are studying the genetic differences between the western chimpanzee and its eastern relatives.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chimpanzee - a great ape native to Africa, closely related to humans
