family Certhiidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli sərˈθaɪɪdi / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli səˈθaɪɪdi / (BrE)
A family of small passerine birds known as creepers, characterized by their slender, downward-curved bills and ability to climb tree trunks.
bird family: creepers

Examples

The family Certhiidae includes several species of birds known for their bark-creeping habits.
Members of the family Certhiidae are commonly found in woodlands across Europe and Asia.
Scientists recently discovered a new species within the family Certhiidae in Southeast Asia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Certhia - A genus of birds within the family Certhiidae

Etymology

From New Latin 'Certhiidae', derived from 'Certhia', the genus name, itself from Greek 'kerthios' (a small, tree-climbing bird).
