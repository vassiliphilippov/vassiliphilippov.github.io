beryllium bronze

1. NOUN

/ bəˈrɪliəm brɑnz / (AmE), / bəˈrɪliəm brɒnz / (BrE)
a copper alloy containing beryllium, known for its strength, hardness, and resistance to corrosion and fatigue
a strong copper-beryllium alloy

Examples

The springs in his watch were made of beryllium bronze for extra durability.
Many tools for hazardous environments are crafted from beryllium bronze to prevent sparking.
The company switched to beryllium bronze to improve the reliability of its electrical connectors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beryllium copper - a copper alloy with beryllium, similar to beryllium bronze

Etymology

From 'beryllium', a chemical element, and 'bronze', a copper alloy.
