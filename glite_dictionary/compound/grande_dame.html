grande dame

1. NOUN

/ ɡrɑnd ˈdɑm / (AmE), / ɡrɑ̃ːd ˈdɑːm / (BrE)
A highly respected and distinguished middle-aged or elderly woman, especially one who is stylish or influential in society.
distinguished, respected elderly woman

Examples

The city's art scene was shaped by the influence of its grande dame, who supported countless young artists.
At the gala, every guest wanted a moment with the grande dame of the theater world.
Known for her wit and elegance, she became the undisputed grande dame of society.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: matriarch - an older woman who is powerful within a family or group
  • Synonym: doyenne - the most respected or prominent woman in a particular field

Etymology

From French 'grande dame' meaning 'great lady.'
