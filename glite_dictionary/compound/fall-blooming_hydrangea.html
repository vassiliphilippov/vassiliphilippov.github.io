fall-blooming hydrangea

1. NOUN

/ fɔl ˌbluːmɪŋ haɪˈdreɪndʒə / (AmE), / fɔːl ˌbluːmɪŋ hɪˈdrændʒɪə / (BrE)
A deciduous shrub or small tree, typically with pyramidal flower clusters, that blooms in the fall.
hydrangea shrub flowering in fall

Examples

The fall-blooming hydrangea brightened the garden with large, creamy-white blossoms late in the season.
Many gardeners choose the fall-blooming hydrangea for its ability to add color after most plants have faded.
A mature fall-blooming hydrangea can reach up to six feet and thrive in partial shade.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hydrangea - a genus of flowering plants native to Asia and the Americas
