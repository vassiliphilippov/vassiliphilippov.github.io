oxidation pond

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑksɪˈdeɪʃən pɑnd / (AmE), / ˌɒksɪˈdeɪʃən pɒnd / (BrE)
a large, shallow artificial pond used to treat wastewater by natural processes involving sunlight, bacteria, and algae
wastewater treatment pond

Examples

The city installed a new oxidation pond to help process sewage efficiently.
Algae play an important role in an oxidation pond by aiding in the breakdown of pollutants.
Routine maintenance of the oxidation pond ensures the treatment process remains effective.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lagoon - an artificial pond for wastewater treatment

Etymology

From 'oxidation' (chemical process involving oxygen) and 'pond' (small body of still water).
