genus Gallus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈɡæl.əs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈɡal.əs / (BrE)
A genus of birds that includes the common domestic chicken and closely related wild species.
genus of domestic chickens and related birds

Examples

The red junglefowl is a wild species within the genus Gallus.
All domestic chickens belong to the genus Gallus in biological classification.
Scientists study the genus Gallus to understand the ancestry of domestic fowl.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chicken - a common domestic fowl; member of Gallus genus

Etymology

From Latin 'gallus' meaning 'cock, rooster'; 'genus' from Latin meaning 'kind' or 'type'.
