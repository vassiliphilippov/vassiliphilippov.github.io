hard yakka

1. NOUN

/ hɑrd ˈjækə / (AmE), / hɑːd ˈjækə / (BrE)
Hard work; strenuous or difficult effort, especially physical labor.
difficult or strenuous work
informal american

Examples

After hours of hard yakka, the team finally completed building the fence.
Studying for the final exams was hard yakka, but it paid off in the end.
Farming the rugged land required a lot of hard yakka from everyone involved.

Synset Examples

It was hard yakka finishing the project on time.
Moving the furniture was hard yakka for everyone involved.
They knew that the renovations would require hard yakka.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: graft - hard work or effort (British slang)
  • Synonym: drudgery - dull, difficult, or tiresome work
  • Synonym: toil - long, hard, or tiring work

Etymology

From Australian English "yakka," meaning work, itself from Yagara (an Aboriginal language) "yaga" (work).
