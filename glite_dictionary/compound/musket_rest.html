musket rest

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌskət rɛst / (AmE), / ˈmʌskɪt rɛst / (BrE)
A forked pole, typically about four feet long, used to support a musket while firing.
forked support for a musket

Examples

The soldier positioned his musket rest firmly in the ground before taking aim.
During the demonstration, they showed how a musket rest was essential for accurate shooting.
In the 17th century, infantrymen often carried a musket rest to steady their weapons.
