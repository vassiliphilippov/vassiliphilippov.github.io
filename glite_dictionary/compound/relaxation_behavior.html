relaxation behavior

1. NOUN

/ rɪˌlækˈseɪʃən bəˈheɪvjɚ / (AmE), / rɪˌlækˈseɪʃən bɪˈheɪvjə / (BrE)
In physics, the exponential process by which a system returns to equilibrium after being disturbed.
exponential return to equilibrium (physics)

Examples

The relaxation behavior of the material was studied after applying a sudden force.
Scientists measured the relaxation behavior of the atoms as the sample cooled to equilibrium.
Understanding relaxation behavior is important for modeling how complex systems respond to disturbances.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: relaxation time - the characteristic time for a system to return to equilibrium
