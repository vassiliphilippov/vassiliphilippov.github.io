ration card

1. NOUN

/ ˈræʃən kɑrd / (AmE), / ˈræʃən kɑːd / (BrE)
an official card issued by a government or authority, certifying the holder's right to buy rationed goods such as food or fuel during times of scarcity
card allowing purchase of rationed goods

Examples

During the war, every family had a ration card to obtain basic necessities.
You could only buy sugar and flour if you showed your ration card at the store.
Many countries issued a ration card during periods of economic hardship.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ration book - book containing coupons for rationed goods
