family Ardeidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ɑːrˈdiːɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli ɑːˈdiːɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of long-legged wading birds that includes herons, egrets, night herons, and bitterns
bird family: herons, egrets, bitterns

Examples

The family Ardeidae includes well-known birds such as herons and egrets.
Many wetlands are important habitats for species within the family Ardeidae.
Researchers observed several members of the family Ardeidae nesting along the riverbanks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heron - a long-legged freshwater and coastal bird, member of Ardeidae
  • Synonym: egret - a type of heron with white plumage, member of Ardeidae
  • Synonym: bittern - a wading bird related to herons, member of Ardeidae

Etymology

From New Latin *Ardeidae*, based on *Ardea* (heron genus).
