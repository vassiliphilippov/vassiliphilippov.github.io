Plantagenet line

1. NOUN

/ plænˈtædʒənət laɪn / (AmE), / plænˈtædʒɪnɪt laɪn / (BrE)
the family name of a line of English kings who ruled from 1154 to 1485, including monarchs such as Henry II, Richard the Lionheart, and Richard III.
royal dynasty of English kings (1154–1485)

Examples

The Plantagenet line played a pivotal role in shaping medieval English history.
Henry II was the first monarch of the Plantagenet line to reign over England.
The Wars of the Roses marked the end of the Plantagenet line and the rise of the Tudors.

Etymology

From the surname of Geoffrey Plantagenet, Count of Anjou, father of King Henry II.
