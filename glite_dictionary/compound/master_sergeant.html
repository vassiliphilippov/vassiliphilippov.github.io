master sergeant

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæstər ˌsɑrdʒənt / (AmE), / ˈmɑːstə ˌsɑːdʒənt / (BrE)
a senior noncommissioned officer rank in the Army or Marines, above sergeant first class and below sergeant major or first sergeant
senior noncommissioned officer rank (Army/Marines)

Examples

After twenty years of service, she was promoted to master sergeant in the U.S. Army.
The master sergeant gave clear orders during the training exercise.
Several master sergeants attended the leadership conference last weekend.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: sergeant major - a higher noncommissioned officer rank in the Army or Marines
  • Degree Lower: sergeant first class - a noncommissioned officer rank just below master sergeant
