systolic murmur

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈstɑlɪk ˈmɜːrmɚ / (AmE), / sɪˈstɒlɪk ˈmɜːmə / (BrE)
a heart murmur heard during the contraction phase of the heartbeat (systole)
heart murmur heard during systole
medical

Examples

On examination, the doctor detected a systolic murmur over the patient's chest.
A faint systolic murmur was audible when she used the stethoscope.
Many heart conditions can cause a systolic murmur to develop in patients.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: diastolic murmur - a murmur heard during diastole (heart relaxation phase)

Etymology

From 'systolic', relating to systole, from Greek *systolē* (a drawing together), and 'murmur', from Latin *murmur* (a low sound).
