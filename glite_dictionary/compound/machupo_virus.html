Machupo virus

1. NOUN

/ məˈtʃuːpoʊ ˈvaɪrəs / (AmE), / məˈtʃuːpəʊ ˈvaɪərəs / (BrE)
an RNA virus that causes Bolivian hemorrhagic fever, primarily carried by rodents in Bolivia
virus causing Bolivian hemorrhagic fever
medical

Examples

The Machupo virus is transmitted to humans through contact with infected rodent droppings.
Scientists are studying the Machupo virus to develop effective treatments for Bolivian hemorrhagic fever.
Outbreaks of Machupo virus are a public health concern in certain regions of Bolivia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Bolivian hemorrhagic fever - a severe viral disease caused by the Machupo virus

Etymology

Named after the Machupo River in Bolivia, where the virus was first identified.
