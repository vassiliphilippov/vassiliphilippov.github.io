Indian mallow

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪndiən ˈmæl.oʊ / (AmE), / ˈɪndiən ˈmæl.əʊ / (BrE)
A tropical American plant with pale yellow or orange flowers, often found as a weed and naturalized in the southern United States.
tropical American plant with yellow/orange flowers

Examples

The garden was overrun with Indian mallow after the rainy season.
Farmers consider Indian mallow a troublesome weed in many southern fields.
Indian mallow produces attractive orange flowers that appeal to some gardeners.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɪndiən ˈmæloʊ / (AmE), / ˈɪndiən ˈmæləʊ / (BrE)
A tall annual herb or subshrub native to tropical Asia, with velvety leaves and yellow flowers, known for producing strong fiber; also found in southeastern Europe and the United States.
tall herb with yellow flowers and strong fiber

Examples

The fibers from the Indian mallow are often used to make ropes and textiles.
A cluster of Indian mallow brightened the garden with its soft yellow blossoms.
Farmers in some regions cultivate Indian mallow both for its ornamental value and its useful fiber.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Abutilon - Genus of flowering plants to which Indian mallow belongs
