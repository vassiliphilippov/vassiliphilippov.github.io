dyer's woad

1. NOUN

/ ˈdaɪərz woʊd / (AmE), / ˈdaɪəz wəʊd / (BrE)
a European biennial plant (Isatis tinctoria), formerly cultivated for the blue dye extracted from its leaves
plant used for blue dye

Examples

Dyer's woad was once widely grown in Europe to produce blue dye for textiles.
The fields were covered in dyer's woad, ready to be harvested for their valuable leaves.
Historical records mention that dyer's woad was an important crop before the introduction of indigo.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: woad - a blue dye obtained from Isatis tinctoria

Etymology

'Woad' from Old English 'wād', and 'dyer' refers to its use by dyers for coloring.
