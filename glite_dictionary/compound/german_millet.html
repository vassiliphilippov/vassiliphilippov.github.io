German millet

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɝːmən ˈmɪlɪt / (AmE), / ˈdʒɜːmən ˈmɪlɪt / (BrE)
a type of millet with yellow grains and large drooping spikes, commonly grown as a cereal crop and forage plant
a millet with yellow grains and drooping spikes

Examples

Farmers often plant German millet as a summer forage crop for livestock.
The large, drooping seed heads are characteristic of German millet.
In regions with dry summers, German millet provides a valuable source of feed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foxtail millet - a species of millet to which German millet belongs
