western pipistrel

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɛstərn pɪˈpɪstrəl / (AmE), / ˈwɛstən pɪˈpɪstrəl / (BrE)
A species of vesper bat (Pipistrellus hesperus) native to western North America.
a vesper bat of western North America

Examples

The western pipistrel is commonly found in arid and semi-arid regions of North America.
Researchers tracked the migration patterns of the western pipistrel in California's deserts.
Despite their small size, western pipistrels play an important role in controlling insect populations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pipistrelle - a small bat of the genus Pipistrellus

Etymology

From 'western' referring to its geographic range and 'pipistrel', ultimately from Latin 'pipistrellus' (bat).
