nerve centre

1. NOUN

/ nɝv ˈsɛntɚ / (AmE), / nɜːv ˈsɛntə / (BrE)
a group of nerve cells in the body that controls a particular function or process
a group of nerve cells controlling a function

Examples

The speech nerve centre is usually located in the left hemisphere of the brain.
Damage to the spinal nerve centre can affect movement and sensation.
Researchers studied the hypothalamus as a key nerve centre regulating body temperature.

Synset Examples

in most people the speech center is in the left hemisphere

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ganglion - a mass of nerve cells, especially outside the brain or spinal cord

2. NOUN

/ nɝv ˈsɛntɚ / (AmE), / nɜːv ˈsɛntə / (BrE)
The main place from which an organization, activity, or system is controlled and where the most important decisions are made.
main place of control or coordination

Examples

London is considered the nerve centre of global finance.
The operations room serves as the nerve centre during a crisis.
The new hospital's nerve centre coordinates patient care across all departments.

Synset Examples

the nerve center of the diamond industry is in Amsterdam

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hub - central or main part of an activity or network

Etymology

Metaphorical use of 'nerve centre' from physiology, where it denotes a part of the nervous system controlling bodily functions.
