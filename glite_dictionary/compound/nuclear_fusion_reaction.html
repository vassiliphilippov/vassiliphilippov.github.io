nuclear fusion reaction

1. NOUN

/ ˈnuː.kli.ɚ ˈfjuː.ʒən riˈæk.ʃən / (AmE), / ˈnjuː.klɪ.ə ˈfjuː.ʒən rɪˈæk.ʃən / (BrE)
a nuclear reaction in which two or more atomic nuclei combine to form a heavier nucleus, releasing energy in the process
a reaction where nuclei combine and release energy
medical

Examples

The Sun produces its energy through a nuclear fusion reaction in its core.
Scientists are working to develop reactors that can sustain a nuclear fusion reaction for clean energy.
A successful nuclear fusion reaction releases more energy than it consumes.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: nuclear fission - a nuclear reaction in which a nucleus splits into smaller parts, releasing energy

Etymology

From 'nuclear' (relating to the nucleus of an atom) + 'fusion' (from Latin fusio, 'a pouring, melting')
