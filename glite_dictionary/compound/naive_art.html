naive art

1. NOUN

/ naɪˈiv ɑrt / (AmE), / naɪˈiːv ɑːt / (BrE)
A genre of visual art created by artists without formal training, often characterized by a childlike simplicity in subject matter and technique.
art by untrained artists, childlike style

Examples

The exhibition showcased stunning examples of naive art from around the world.
Many collectors are drawn to naive art for its honesty and vivid colors.
Henri Rousseau is often regarded as a pioneer of naive art in the modern era.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: folk art - traditional art produced by people with no formal training
  • Synonym: outsider art - art created outside the boundaries of official culture, often by self-taught individuals

Etymology

From French 'naïf' (naive, innocent) + 'art' (art).
