field officer

1. NOUN

/ ˈfiːld ˌɑfəsər / (AmE), / ˈfiːld ˌɒfɪsə / (BrE)
an officer in the armed forces holding the rank of major, lieutenant colonel, or colonel
military officer (major, lieutenant colonel, colonel)

Examples

The field officer led his battalion through the complicated training exercises.
Only a field officer can approve this strategic decision on the battlefield.
She was promoted to field officer after years of exemplary service.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: staff officer - an officer serving on a military staff (not field duties)
