SI system

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛsˈaɪ ˈsɪstəm / (AmE), / ˌɛsˈaɪ ˈsɪstəm / (BrE)
the International System of Units, a standardized metric system of measurement used worldwide in science and industry
International System of Units; scientific metric system

Examples

The SI system is used as the standard in most scientific research worldwide.
All measurements in the laboratory should be recorded using the SI system for consistency.
Physics students learn to convert between the imperial units and the SI system.

Synset Examples

Today the United States is the only country in the world not totally committed to the Systeme International d'Unites

Related Terms

  • Synonym: metric system - the broader decimal system of measurement based on the meter, kilogram, and second

Etymology

Abbreviation of French Système International d'Unités, meaning 'International System of Units'.
