genus Alcedo

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ælˈsiː.doʊ / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs alˈsiː.dəʊ / (BrE)
The type genus of the kingfisher family Alcedinidae, comprising several species of brightly colored birds commonly known as kingfishers.
type genus of the kingfisher family

Examples

The genus Alcedo includes several species of kingfishers found throughout Eurasia and Africa.
Researchers discovered a new species within the genus Alcedo based on unique plumage patterns.
The type specimen for the genus Alcedo is an important reference for ornithologists studying kingfishers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Alcedinidae - the kingfisher family of birds
  • Synonym: kingfisher - common name for birds in the family Alcedinidae

Etymology

From Latin 'alcedo' (kingfisher), used in scientific taxonomy.
