Canary Islands

1. PROPN

/ kəˌnɛri ˈaɪləndz / (AmE), / kəˌneəri ˈaɪləndz / (BrE)
a group of volcanic islands in the Atlantic Ocean off the northwest coast of Africa, forming an autonomous community of Spain
Spanish island group in the Atlantic Ocean

Examples

Many tourists visit the Canary Islands each year for their warm climate and beautiful beaches.
The Canary Islands are known for their unique volcanic landscapes and biodiversity.
Flights from Madrid to the Canary Islands are frequent and popular throughout the year.

Etymology

From Spanish 'Islas Canarias', named after the large dogs ('canes' in Latin) found on the islands by early explorers.
