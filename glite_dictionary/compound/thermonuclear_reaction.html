thermonuclear reaction

1. NOUN

/ ˌθɜrmoʊˈnuːkliɚ riˈækʃən / (AmE), / ˌθɜːməʊˈnjuːkliə riˈækʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
a nuclear fusion reaction that occurs at extremely high temperatures, such as those found in stars or hydrogen bombs
nuclear fusion at very high temperatures

Examples

A thermonuclear reaction powers the sun and other stars, producing immense amounts of energy.
Scientists are working to replicate a thermonuclear reaction on Earth to provide a new source of clean energy.
The explosion was caused by an uncontrolled thermonuclear reaction in a hydrogen bomb.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nuclear fusion - a reaction in which atomic nuclei combine to form a heavier nucleus, releasing energy

Etymology

From thermo- (relating to heat) + nuclear (relating to the nucleus of an atom) + reaction.
