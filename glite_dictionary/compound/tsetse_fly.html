tsetse fly

1. NOUN

/ ˈtsiːtsi flaɪ / (AmE), / ˈtset.si flaɪ / (BrE)
A large African bloodsucking fly that transmits sleeping sickness and other diseases to humans and animals.
African disease-carrying fly

Examples

The tsetse fly is responsible for transmitting the parasite that causes sleeping sickness in humans.
Farmers in East Africa struggle to protect their cattle from tsetse fly infestations.
Researchers are developing new traps to control the spread of the tsetse fly.

Etymology

From Tswana *tsetse*, meaning 'fly'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtsɛtˌsi flaɪ / (AmE), / ˈtsɛtsi flaɪ / (BrE)
A large bloodsucking fly found in Africa, known for transmitting sleeping sickness and other diseases to humans and animals.
African bloodsucking fly; disease vector

Examples

The tsetse fly is notorious for spreading sleeping sickness across vast areas of sub-Saharan Africa.
Farmers are concerned about livestock deaths caused by the tsetse fly in rural communities.
Researchers are developing new methods to control the population of the tsetse fly.

Etymology

From Tswana *tsetse*, meaning 'fly'.
