Broca's aphasia

1. NOUN

/ ˌbroʊkəz əˈfeɪʒə / (AmE), / ˌbrɒkəz əˈfeɪzɪə / (BrE)
A medical condition characterized by severely impaired ability to express oneself through speech or writing, while comprehension often remains relatively intact.
language disorder affecting speech and writing
medical

Examples

After the stroke, the patient was diagnosed with Broca's aphasia, making it difficult for him to form sentences.
Therapists use specific exercises to help those with Broca's aphasia improve their communication skills.
While her understanding was good, her Broca's aphasia prevented her from speaking fluently.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Wernicke's aphasia - A language disorder with impaired comprehension but fluent expression

Etymology

Named after French physician Paul Broca, who first described the condition.
