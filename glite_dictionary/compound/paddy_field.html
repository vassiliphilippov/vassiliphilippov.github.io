paddy field

1. NOUN

/ ˈpædi ˌfiːld / (AmE), / ˈpædi ˌfiːld / (BrE)
A field that is irrigated or flooded for growing rice, especially in Asia.
flooded field for rice cultivation

Examples

Farmers rely on the paddy field to produce enough rice for their families each year.
The lush green paddy fields stretched as far as the eye could see.
Tourists marveled at the beauty of the terraced paddy fields in the mountains.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rice paddy - an irrigated field for growing rice

Etymology

From Malay *padi* (rice plant) and English 'field'.
