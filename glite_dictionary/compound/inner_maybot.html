inner Maybot

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪnɚ ˈmeɪbɒt / (AmE), / ˈɪnə ˈmeɪbɒt / (BrE)
a humorous term referring to a person's robotic, scripted, or mechanical behavior, likened to that attributed to Theresa May, the former UK Prime Minister
robotic, mechanical behavior; especially referencing Theresa May
informal british

Examples

Whenever I'm nervous in meetings, my inner Maybot takes over, and I rely purely on scripted replies.
He joked that his inner Maybot wakes up whenever someone mentions compliance procedures.
Trying to hide his excitement, he activated his inner Maybot and responded in a monotone voice.

Synset Examples

He referred to his robotic responses as his inner Maybot.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: robotic - having mechanical or automated qualities; lacking human emotion
  • Synonym: mechanical - (of behavior) automatic, lacking in spontaneity or individuality
  • Antonym: spontaneous - performed or occurring as a result of a sudden impulse or inclination

Etymology

Formed from 'inner' and 'Maybot', the latter a nickname for Theresa May due to her perceived robotic manner.
