amyl nitrate

1. NOUN

/ ˈæmɪl ˌnaɪˌtreɪt / (AmE), / ˈamɪl ˌnaɪtreɪt / (BrE)
a chemical compound used as a vasodilator, sometimes medically to treat angina pectoris
a vasodilator chemical used medically
medical

Examples

The doctor administered amyl nitrate to relieve the patient’s chest pain.
In some medical emergencies, amyl nitrate is used for its rapid vasodilating effects.
Scientists are researching the potential side effects of amyl nitrate usage in heart patients.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nitroglycerin - a similar vasodilator used to treat angina

Etymology

From "amyl" (relating to the amyl group) and "nitrate", indicating its chemical composition.
