judge's robe

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒʌdʒɪz roʊb / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌdʒɪz rəʊb / (BrE)
a long, formal gown worn by judges as a symbol of office in a courtroom
formal gown worn by judges

Examples

The court was silent as the judge entered, his judge's robe flowing behind him.
Some law schools allow graduates to try on a judge's robe during commencement ceremonies.
She admired the dignity that the judge's robe brought to courtroom proceedings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gown - a long, flowing outer garment, especially one worn on formal occasions
