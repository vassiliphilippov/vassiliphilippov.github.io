hart's-tongue fern

1. NOUN

/ hɑrts ˈtʌŋ ˌfɝn / (AmE), / hɑːts ˈtʌŋ ˌfɜːn / (BrE)
a tropical American terrestrial fern with leathery, lance-shaped fronds; sometimes classified in the genus Polybotrya
species of tropical American fern

Examples

The hart's-tongue fern thrives in moist, shaded environments of Central and South America.
Botanists observed a rare hart's-tongue fern growing near the base of the waterfall.
The leathery fronds of the hart's-tongue fern distinguish it from many other ferns in the region.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɑrts ˌtʌŋ ˌfɝn / (AmE), / ˈhɑːts ˌtʌŋ ˌfɜːn / (BrE)
a Eurasian fern (Asplenium scolopendrium) with simple, lance-shaped, undivided green fronds
a fern with simple, lance-shaped fronds

Examples

The hart's-tongue fern thrives in shaded, damp woodland areas across Europe.
She identified the unusual hart's-tongue fern by its smooth, undivided leaves.
Gardeners often plant hart's-tongue fern to add lush greenery to shady gardens.

Etymology

From 'hart', an old word for deer, and 'tongue', referring to the shape of the frond.

3. NOUN

/ ˈhɑrts ˌtʌŋ fɝn / (AmE), / ˈhɑːts ˌtʌŋ fɜːn / (BrE)
a fern with long, strap-shaped, pale yellow-green leaves, found as an epiphyte or sometimes terrestrial, native from Florida to the West Indies, Mexico, and south to Uruguay
fern with strap-shaped leaves

Examples

The shady garden corner was brightened by a healthy hart's-tongue fern with its distinctive strap-shaped leaves.
Botanists discovered a rare colony of hart's-tongue fern growing on limestone rocks in the region.
She chose a hart's-tongue fern for her terrarium because of its unusual, undivided fronds.

Etymology

Named for the resemblance of its leaves to a deer's (hart's) tongue.
