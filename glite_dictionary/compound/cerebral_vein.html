cerebral vein

1. NOUN

/ səˈriː.brəl veɪn / (AmE), / sɪˈriː.brəl veɪn / (BrE)
any of the veins that drain blood from the cerebral hemispheres of the brain
vein draining blood from the brain
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that a blockage in a cerebral vein could lead to a stroke.
During surgery, the surgeon carefully avoided damaging the major cerebral veins.
Imaging studies revealed a clot in his left cerebral vein.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vein - blood vessel that carries blood toward the heart

Etymology

From Latin 'cerebrum' (brain) and 'vena' (vein).
