solfa syllable

1. NOUN

/ ˈsoʊl.fɑ ˈsɪl.ə.bəl / (AmE), / ˈsɒl.fə ˈsɪl.ə.bəl / (BrE)
any of the syllables (such as do, re, mi, fa, sol, la, ti) used to name notes in a musical scale, especially in the solfège method of teaching singing
syllable used to name notes in music

Examples

Children often learn to sing by practicing each solfa syllable in sequence.
The teacher asked the choir to identify the correct solfa syllable for each note.
She knows the scale from 'do' to 'ti' using every solfa syllable accurately.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: solfège - music education method using syllables for scale degrees

Etymology

From Italian 'solfa', meaning the musical syllables, and 'syllable'.
