Cimarron River

1. PROPN

/ sɪˈmɑːrɑn ˈrɪvər / (AmE), / sɪˈmɑːrən ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
A river rising in northeastern New Mexico and flowing eastward through Oklahoma, where it becomes a tributary of the Arkansas River.
river in New Mexico and Oklahoma, USA

Examples

The Cimarron River is known for its striking red cliffs and scenic beauty.
Many pioneers crossed the Cimarron River while traveling along the Santa Fe Trail.
After heavy rain, the Cimarron River can rise quickly and flood surrounding areas.

Etymology

From Spanish 'cimarrón' meaning 'wild' or 'untamed,' originally used to describe wild animals or escaped slaves.
