plexus choroideus

1. NOUN

/ ˈplɛksəs kəˈrɔɪdiəs / (AmE), / ˈplɛksəs kəˈrɔɪdɪəs / (BrE)
A network of blood vessels in the cerebral ventricles that produces cerebrospinal fluid and regulates intraventricular pressure.
vascular tissue in brain ventricles
medical

Examples

The plexus choroideus produces cerebrospinal fluid essential for the brain's health.
Disorders of the plexus choroideus can lead to hydrocephalus due to abnormal cerebrospinal fluid production.
During the operation, the surgeon examined the patient's plexus choroideus for any signs of infection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: choroid plexus - network of blood vessels in the brain's ventricles

Etymology

From Latin 'plexus' (network) and 'choroideus' (relating to the choroid).
