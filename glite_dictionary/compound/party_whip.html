party whip

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːrti wɪp / (AmE), / ˈpɑːti wɪp / (BrE)
a legislator in a political party who is responsible for enforcing party discipline and ensuring party members attend and vote as the party leadership desires
legislator enforcing party discipline

Examples

The party whip reminded MPs to attend the crucial budget vote on Thursday.
As party whip, she ensured no members strayed from the official policy line.
If you defy the party whip, you risk losing support from your colleagues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whip - a member of a legislature responsible for party discipline

Etymology

From 'party' (political group) and 'whip', originally a horse racing term for the person who keeps the hounds together, later adopted into parliamentary context.
