genus Scophthalmus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌskɒfˈθæl.məs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌskɒfˈθæl.məs / (BrE)
A genus of flatfish within the family Bothidae, including certain species of turbot.
genus of flatfish including turbots

Examples

The European turbot belongs to the genus Scophthalmus, prized for its delicate flavor.
Several species within the genus Scophthalmus inhabit the coastal waters of Europe.
Marine biologists often study the genus Scophthalmus to understand flatfish evolution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: turbot - a large, flatfish of the family Bothidae

Etymology

From Greek skophthalmos (having prominent eyes), used in scientific Latin classification.
