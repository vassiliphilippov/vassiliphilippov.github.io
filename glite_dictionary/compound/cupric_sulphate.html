cupric sulphate

1. NOUN

/ ˈkuːprɪk ˈsʌlfeɪt / (AmE), / ˈkjuːprɪk ˈsʌlfeɪt / (BrE)
a blue crystalline chemical compound, copper(II) sulfate, used in agriculture, chemistry, and industry
a blue crystalline copper salt (chemical compound)
medical

Examples

The laboratory used cupric sulphate in several chemical experiments.
Cupric sulphate is often used as a fungicide in agriculture.
Accidental ingestion of cupric sulphate can be hazardous to health.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: copper sulfate - alternative name for cupric sulphate, a blue copper salt

Etymology

From Latin 'cuprum' (copper) and 'sulphur', with '-ate' denoting a salt; 'cupric' indicates copper with a +2 oxidation state.
