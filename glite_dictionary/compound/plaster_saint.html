plaster saint

1. NOUN

/ ˈplæstər ˌseɪnt / (AmE), / ˈplɑːstə ˌseɪnt / (BrE)
a person regarded as perfect and without human faults, often in an insincere or superficial way
person regarded as morally perfect

Examples

Although she volunteers a lot, she's no plaster saint—she makes mistakes like everyone else.
He didn't want to be seen as a plaster saint, just someone trying his best.
The novel mocks characters who behave like plaster saints to gain public approval.

Synset Examples

he's no plaster saint

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: saint - person of exceptional holiness or virtue

Etymology

Refers to plaster statues of saints in churches, symbolizing unrealistic or superficial perfection.
