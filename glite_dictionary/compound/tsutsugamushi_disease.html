tsutsugamushi disease

1. NOUN

/ tsuːˌtsuːɡəˈmuːʃi dɪˌziːz / (AmE), / tsuːˌtsuːɡəˈmuːʃi dɪˌziːz / (BrE)
a disease, also known as scrub typhus, caused by the bacterium Orientia tsutsugamushi, transmitted by the bites of larval mites and widespread in Asia, characterized by fever, rash, and sometimes severe complications
scrub typhus; mite-borne infectious disease in Asia
medical

Examples

Many travelers to rural areas in Asia are advised to take precautions against tsutsugamushi disease.
He was hospitalized with high fever and a rash, eventually diagnosed as tsutsugamushi disease.
Proper protective clothing can reduce the risk of contracting tsutsugamushi disease while hiking in endemic regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scrub typhus - another name for tsutsugamushi disease

Etymology

From Japanese *tsutsuga* (illness) + *mushi* (insect).
