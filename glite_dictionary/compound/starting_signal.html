starting signal

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɑrtɪŋ ˈsɪɡnəl / (AmE), / ˈstɑːtɪŋ ˈsɪɡnəl / (BrE)
a signal, such as a sound or visual cue, that indicates the beginning of a race or event
signal that marks the start of a race or event

Examples

The runners listened closely for the starting signal before launching off the blocks.
The referee raised the flag as the starting signal for the cycling competition.
A loud buzzer served as the starting signal for the swimming final.

Synset Examples

the starting signal was a green light
the runners awaited the start

Related Terms

  • Synonym: start - the act of beginning a race or event
