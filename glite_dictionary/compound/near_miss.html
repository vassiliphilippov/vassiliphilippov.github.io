near miss

1. NOUN

/ nɪr mɪs / (AmE), / nɪə mɪs / (BrE)
an incident in which a collision or accident is narrowly avoided
an incident narrowly avoiding collision or accident

Examples

The two planes had a near miss over the airport but landed safely.
That was a near miss—the cyclist almost got hit by the car.
New safety measures have reduced the number of near misses at railway crossings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: close call - a situation in which disaster or danger is narrowly avoided
  • Antonym: accident - an unfortunate incident that happens unexpectedly
