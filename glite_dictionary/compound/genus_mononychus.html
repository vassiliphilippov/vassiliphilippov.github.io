genus Mononychus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌmɒ.nəˈnaɪ.kəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌmɒ.nəˈnɪ.kəs / (BrE)
A genus of prehistoric animals, sometimes classified as birds or dinosaurs, whose exact taxonomic placement is debated.
a debated genus of birds or dinosaurs

Examples

The fossil record of genus Mononychus raises questions about the evolution of modern birds.
Scientists continue to debate whether genus Mononychus should be classified as a bird or a dinosaur.
New discoveries may finally resolve the status of genus Mononychus in the paleontological community.

Etymology

From Greek 'monos' (single) and 'onychus' (claw), referring to having a single claw.
