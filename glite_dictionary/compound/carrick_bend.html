carrick bend

1. NOUN

/ ˈkærɪk bɛnd / (AmE), / ˈkærɪk bɛnd / (BrE)
a knot used to securely join the ends of two large ropes or hawsers, commonly in nautical settings
knot to join large ropes

Examples

The carrick bend is often used by sailors when they need to join two heavy hawsers.
To secure the towline, the crew tied a carrick bend between the two ropes.
He practiced tying a perfect carrick bend for his seamanship exam.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: knot - an interlacement of rope or cord

Etymology

Carrick may derive from Carrick Roads, a harbor in Cornwall, England.
