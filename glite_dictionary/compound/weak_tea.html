weak tea

1. NOUN

/ wik ti / (AmE), / wiːk tiː / (BrE)
Tea that is not strong in flavor, or, figuratively, something lacking in force, substance, or impact.
tea with mild flavor; something lacking impact

Examples

Her apology sounded like weak tea and failed to convince anyone.
He handed me a cup of weak tea, barely colored at all.
The movie was just weak tea compared to the book.

Synset Examples

The presentation felt like weak tea without solid evidence.
I prefer strong coffee; weak tea just doesn't energize me.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: strong tea - tea with a strong, intense flavor
  • Synonym: insipid - lacking flavor, vigor, or interest
