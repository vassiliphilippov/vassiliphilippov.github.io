utility revenue bond

1. NOUN

/ juːˈtɪl.ə.ti ˈrɛv.əˌnu bɑnd / (AmE), / juːˈtɪl.ɪ.ti ˈrɛv.juː bɒnd / (BrE)
a bond issued by a government or other entity to fund the construction or improvement of public utility services, with repayment made from the revenue generated by those services
bond for funding public utility construction
legal

Examples

The city council approved a utility revenue bond to finance the new water treatment plant.
Investors were attracted to the utility revenue bond because the repayments were backed by steady electricity sales.
Without the utility revenue bond, the expansion of the public transit system would not have been possible.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: revenue bond - a bond repaid from the revenue generated by the project it finances
