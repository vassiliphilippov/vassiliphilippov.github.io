praying mantis

1. NOUN

/ ˈpreɪɪŋ ˈmæntɪs / (AmE), / ˈpreɪɪŋ ˈmæntɪs / (BrE)
A large, slender predatory insect with folded forelimbs that resemble praying hands, belonging to the family Mantidae.
large predatory insect with folded forelegs

Examples

The praying mantis waited motionless on the leaf, ready to strike its prey.
Many gardeners welcome the praying mantis because it helps control pest insects.
A praying mantis can rotate its head to scan its surroundings for food.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mantis - insect of the order Mantodea

Etymology

From the resemblance of its folded forelimbs to hands held in prayer; 'mantis' from Greek 'mantis', meaning 'prophet' or 'seer'.
