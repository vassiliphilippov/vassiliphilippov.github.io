liver fluke

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪvɚ fluːk / (AmE), / ˈlɪvə fluːk / (BrE)
a parasitic flatworm that lives in the liver and bile ducts of humans and other animals
parasitic flatworm in liver and bile ducts
medical

Examples

The veterinarian discovered a liver fluke infestation in the herd.
Eating undercooked fish can increase the risk of liver fluke infection.
Researchers are developing new drugs to treat diseases caused by the liver fluke.
