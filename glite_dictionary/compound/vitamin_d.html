vitamin D

1. NOUN

/ ˌvaɪ.tə.mɪn ˈdi / (AmE), / ˌvɪ.tə.mɪn ˈdiː / (BrE)
a fat-soluble vitamin essential for the regulation of calcium and phosphate in the body, helping to maintain healthy bones and teeth
a nutrient that helps regulate calcium and bone health
medical

Examples

Doctors recommend taking vitamin D supplements during the winter months when sunlight is limited.
Fortified milk is a common source of vitamin D in many diets.
Deficiency of vitamin D can lead to bone disorders such as rickets in children.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: vitamin A - a fat-soluble vitamin important for vision and immune function
  • Degree Higher: vitamin E - a fat-soluble vitamin with antioxidant properties

Etymology

Formed from 'vitamin' (from Latin 'vita' meaning 'life' and '-amine') and the letter 'D', indicating its discovery sequence.
