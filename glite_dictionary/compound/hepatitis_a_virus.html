hepatitis A virus

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɛpəˈtaɪtɪs ˈeɪ ˈvaɪrəs / (AmE), / ˌhɛpəˈtaɪtɪs ˈeɪ ˈvaɪərəs / (BrE)
a virus that causes hepatitis A, an infectious liver disease
virus causing hepatitis A
medical

Examples

The hepatitis A virus can be transmitted through contaminated food or water.
Vaccination is an effective way to prevent infection with the hepatitis A virus.
Outbreaks of hepatitis A virus are more common in areas with poor sanitation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hepatitis A - an infectious disease of the liver caused by hepatitis A virus
  • Degree Higher: hepatitis B virus - virus causing hepatitis B
  • Degree Higher: hepatitis C virus - virus causing hepatitis C

Etymology

Formed from 'hepatitis A' and 'virus'; 'hepatitis' from Greek 'hepar' (liver) and '-itis' (inflammation).
