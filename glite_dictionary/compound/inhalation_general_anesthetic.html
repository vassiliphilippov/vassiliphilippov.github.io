inhalation general anesthetic

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˌheɪləˌʃən ˌdʒɛn(ə)rəl ˌænəsˈθɛtɪk / (AmE), / ɪnˌheɪləˌʃən ˌdʒɛn(ə)rəl ˌanɪsˈθɛtɪk / (BrE)
a gas administered by inhalation to produce general anesthesia during surgical or medical procedures
anesthetic gas for general anesthesia
medical

Examples

The patient was given an inhalation general anesthetic before the operation began.
Halothane is a commonly used inhalation general anesthetic in pediatric surgeries.
A proper mask fit is essential when administering an inhalation general anesthetic.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: intravenous general anesthetic - anesthetic producing unconsciousness when injected into a vein
  • Synonym: anesthetic - a substance that induces insensitivity to pain
