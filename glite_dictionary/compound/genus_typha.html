genus Typha

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈtaɪ.fə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈtɪ.fə / (BrE)
a genus of tall, reedy marsh plants known as cattails or reed maces, found in wetlands worldwide
a plant genus including cattails

Examples

The genus Typha is commonly found in wetlands throughout North America and Europe.
Several species within the genus Typha are vital for supporting wildlife habitats.
The distinctive cylindrical brown seed head is a key feature of plants in the genus Typha.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cattail - a tall marsh plant with a cylindrical seed head (Typha)

Etymology

From Latin 'Typha', the classical name for cattails, and 'genus' meaning 'kind'.
