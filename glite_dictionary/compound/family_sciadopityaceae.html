family Sciadopityaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌfæməli saɪˌædəˌpɪtiˈeɪsiː / (AmE), / ˌfæmɪli saɪˌadəʊˌpɪtɪˈeɪsɪˌiː / (BrE)
A family of coniferous trees comprising a single genus, previously considered part of Taxodiaceae.
family of coniferous trees (Sciadopityaceae)

Examples

The family Sciadopityaceae is notable for containing only one living species, the Japanese umbrella pine.
Botanists once classified the umbrella pine in Taxodiaceae, but now it belongs to the family Sciadopityaceae.
Studies of fossil records have provided insights into the evolution of the family Sciadopityaceae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Taxodiaceae - family of coniferous trees formerly including Sciadopityaceae

Etymology

From New Latin Sciadopityaceae, based on the type genus Sciadopitys, meaning 'umbrella pine' in Greek.
