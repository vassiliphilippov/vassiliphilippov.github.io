Society of Friends

1. PROPN

/ səˈsaɪ.ə.di əv ˈfrɛndz / (AmE), / səˈsaɪ.ə.ti əv ˈfrɛndz / (BrE)
a Christian religious group established in the 17th century, also known as the Quakers, emphasizing pacifism and simplicity
Christian religious group; Quakers

Examples

Many members of the Society of Friends were active in the abolition of slavery.
The Society of Friends emphasizes inward spiritual experience over formal rituals.
Meetings of the Society of Friends are often held in silence until someone feels moved to speak.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Quaker - a member of the Society of Friends

Etymology

From the group's formal name, referring to fellowship among believers; founded by George Fox in the 1600s.
