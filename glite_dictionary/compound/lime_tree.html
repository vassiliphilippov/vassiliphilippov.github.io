lime tree

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪm ˌtri / (AmE), / ˈlaɪm ˌtriː / (BrE)
A tree that produces limes, especially any of various species in the genus Citrus bearing the lime fruit.
tree that produces limes

Examples

The lime tree in our backyard is covered with fragrant blossoms every spring.
Farmers often plant lime trees in orchards alongside other citrus fruits.
She picked a handful of limes from the lime tree for her recipe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lime - the small green citrus fruit
  • Degree Lower: lemon tree - tree that produces lemons

2. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪm tri / (AmE), / ˈlaɪm triː / (BrE)
A deciduous tree of the genus Tilia, with heart-shaped leaves and clusters of yellowish, often fragrant flowers, several species of which provide valuable timber.
deciduous tree of the genus Tilia

Examples

The avenue was lined with ancient lime trees whose branches formed a green canopy overhead.
In June, the sweet scent of lime tree blossoms fills the park.
Carpenters prized the timber from the lime tree for its fine grain and workability.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: linden - another name for the lime tree, especially in North America

Etymology

The term 'lime' in this context comes from Middle English 'lind' or 'line', related to the German 'Linde'; unrelated to the citrus fruit.
