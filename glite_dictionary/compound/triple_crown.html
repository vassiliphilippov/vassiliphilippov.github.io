Triple Crown

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrɪpəl kraʊn / (AmE), / ˈtrɪpl kraʊn / (BrE)
The title awarded to a horse that wins the Kentucky Derby, the Preakness Stakes, and the Belmont Stakes in the same year.
title for winning three major US horse races

Examples

Only thirteen horses have ever won the Triple Crown in American horse racing history.
After victories in the Derby and Preakness, everyone wondered if he could claim the Triple Crown.
Winning the Triple Crown is one of the most challenging feats in thoroughbred racing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Grand Slam - Winning all major events in a particular sport or competition

Etymology

From the idea of winning three crowns or honors, used in sports since the 19th century.
