get out of jail free card

1. NOUN

/ ɡɛt aʊt ʌv dʒeɪl fri kɑrd / (AmE), / ɡɛt aʊt ɒv dʒeɪl fri kɑːd / (BrE)
an opportunity or means to avoid punishment or negative consequences, typically by chance or special privilege
way to avoid penalty or consequences

Examples

Some celebrities think their fame gives them a get out of jail free card when they break the law.
He was surprised when the boss handed him a get out of jail free card and let him keep his job.
She keeps looking for a get out of jail free card instead of facing her responsibilities.

Synset Examples

Winning that case felt like I had a get out of jail free card.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: free pass - permission to avoid rules or punishment

Etymology

From the 'Get Out of Jail Free' card in the board game Monopoly, used here figuratively.
