tolu balsam

1. NOUN

/ ˈtoʊlu ˈbælsəm / (AmE), / ˈtɒluː ˈbælsəm / (BrE)
an aromatic yellowish-brown resin obtained from the South American tolu balsam tree, used especially in cough syrups, perfumes, and as a flavoring
aromatic resin from the tolu tree

Examples

Traditional cough syrups often contain tolu balsam for its soothing properties.
The perfume was scented with tolu balsam and other exotic resins.
Pharmacists once used tolu balsam as an ingredient in old-fashioned medicinal lozenges.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: balsam of Peru - an aromatic resin similar to tolu balsam, obtained from another South American tree

Etymology

From Spanish 'bálsamo de Tolú', after Tolú, a town in northern Colombia, near where it is produced.
