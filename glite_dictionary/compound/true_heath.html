true heath

1. NOUN

/ truː hiːθ / (AmE), / truː hiːθ / (BrE)
any plant of the genus Erica, a group of shrub species with small, often bell-shaped flowers, commonly found in Europe and Africa.
any plant of the genus Erica

Examples

Several varieties of true heath thrive in well-drained, sandy soils.
The true heath is native to many parts of Western Europe and often blooms with pink or white flowers.
Gardeners often plant true heath to create colorful ground cover in acidic gardens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heath - a shrubland habitat with acidic, low-nutrient soils, or any plant in the Ericaceae family
