genus Ateles

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs əˈtiːliːz / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs əˈtiːliːz / (BrE)
A genus of New World monkeys known as spider monkeys, native to Central and South America and characterized by long limbs and a prehensile tail.
a genus of spider monkeys

Examples

The genus Ateles includes several species commonly known as spider monkeys.
Researchers studied the social behavior of primates in the genus Ateles.
Many conservation programs focus on animals within the genus Ateles due to habitat loss.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spider monkey - a New World monkey of the genus Ateles

Etymology

From Latin 'Ateles', meaning 'imperfect, incomplete,' referring to the monkeys' reduced thumbs.
