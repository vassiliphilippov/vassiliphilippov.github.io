cognitive process

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːɡnɪtɪv ˈprɑːses / (AmE), / ˈkɒɡnɪtɪv ˈprəʊsɛs / (BrE)
a mental activity or operation such as thinking, remembering, or learning
mental activity or operation

Examples

Learning a new language is a complex cognitive process involving memory and attention.
Attention span reflects how effective a particular cognitive process is at filtering information.
Scientists study the cognitive processes that underlie decision-making.

Synset Examples

the process of thinking
the cognitive operation of remembering

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mental process - a process involved in mental activity

Etymology

From Latin "cognitio" (knowledge) and Latin "processus" (going forward).
