crow blackbird

1. NOUN

/ kroʊ ˈblækˌbɝd / (AmE), / krəʊ ˈblækˌbɜːd / (BrE)
A North American blackbird with a long tail and iridescent black feathers, also known as the common grackle.
North American blackbird with long tail and iridescent plumage

Examples

A large flock of crow blackbirds gathered on the lawn to search for insects.
The crow blackbird is often seen near wetlands and open fields.
Its long tail and glossy plumage help identify the crow blackbird in the wild.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grackle - a North American blackbird with a long tail and iridescent plumage; another name for the crow blackbird
