to sharpen focus

1. VERB

/ ˈʃɑr.pən ˈfoʊ.kəs / (AmE), / ˈʃɑː.pən ˈfəʊ.kəs / (BrE)
to make one's attention or concentration clearer and more intense; to increase alertness or clarity in addressing something
intensify or clarify attention

Examples

We need to sharpen focus on our main objectives to achieve better results.
Meditation can help you sharpen focus during stressful periods.
The new manager encouraged staff to sharpen focus on customer satisfaction.

Synset Examples

Let’s sharpen our focus for the upcoming project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: concentrate - focus one's attention or mental effort
  • Antonym: to distract - divert attention away; make unfocused
