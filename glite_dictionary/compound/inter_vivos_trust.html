inter vivos trust

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.tər ˈvaɪvoʊs trʌst / (AmE), / ˌɪn.tə ˈvaɪvəʊs trʌst / (BrE)
A trust created and operating during the grantor's lifetime, not established by a will.
trust created while alive
legal

Examples

An inter vivos trust is often used to manage assets for beneficiaries during the grantor's lifetime.
The lawyer advised establishing an inter vivos trust to avoid probate after death.
Unlike a will, an inter vivos trust becomes effective immediately upon creation.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: testamentary trust - a trust created by a will and effective upon the grantor's death

Etymology

From Latin inter vivos (between the living).
