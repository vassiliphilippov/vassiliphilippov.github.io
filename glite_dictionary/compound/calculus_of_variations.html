calculus of variations

1. NOUN

/ ˈkælkjələs əv ˌvɛriˈeɪʃənz / (AmE), / ˈkalkjʊləs əv ˌvɛːrɪˈeɪʃənz / (BrE)
a branch of mathematics concerned with finding the maxima and minima of functionals, especially integrals, by considering variations in functions
mathematical field for optimizing functionals

Examples

The calculus of variations is central to many developments in physics.
She specialized in the calculus of variations during her graduate studies.
Problems in the calculus of variations often lead to important equations in mathematics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: optimization - process of making something as effective as possible; related mathematical field

Etymology

From Latin 'calculus' (pebble, reckoning) and 'variatio' (change), referring to mathematical methods involving changing functions.
