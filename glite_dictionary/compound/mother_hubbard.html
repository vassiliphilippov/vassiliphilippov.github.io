Mother Hubbard

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌðɚ ˈhʌbɚd / (AmE), / ˈmʌðə ˈhʌbəd / (BrE)
a woman's loose, unbelted dress with a high neck and long sleeves, typically falling straight from the shoulders
a woman's loose, unbelted dress
archaic

Examples

She wore a Mother Hubbard to keep cool during the hot summer months.
The style of the Mother Hubbard dress became popular in the late nineteenth century.
He remembered his grandmother often dressed in a faded blue Mother Hubbard.

Etymology

Named after the character in the nursery rhyme 'Old Mother Hubbard', whose attire inspired the style.
