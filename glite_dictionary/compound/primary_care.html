primary care

1. NOUN

/ ˈpraɪˌmɛri kɛr / (AmE), / ˈprʌɪməri keə / (BrE)
the basic or first level of medical care given to patients, usually by general practitioners, before referral to specialists if necessary
basic first level of medical care
medical

Examples

Many people choose to see their primary care doctor for annual check-ups and immunizations.
Access to good primary care improves long-term health outcomes in a community.
If your condition is complex, primary care may refer you to a medical specialist.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: secondary care - specialized medical care given after referral from primary care
  • Degree Higher: tertiary care - highly specialized consultative medical care, often for inpatients and by referral from primary or secondary care
