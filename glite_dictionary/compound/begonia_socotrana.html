Begonia socotrana

1. NOUN

/ bɪˈɡoʊniəˌ soʊkoʊˈtrɑːnə / (AmE), / bɪˈɡəʊniə ˌsɒkəˈtrɑːnə / (BrE)
A species of semi-tuberous begonia native to Yemen, known for its peltate leaves and rose-pink flowers.
a species of begonia native to Yemen

Examples

Begonia socotrana is prized by horticulturists for its delicate rose-pink blossoms.
The unique leaves of Begonia socotrana make it a popular choice for indoor gardens.
Botanists discovered Begonia socotrana thriving in the mountainous regions of Yemen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: begonia - a genus of perennial flowering plants often cultivated for their ornamental leaves and flowers

Etymology

From Latin genus name Begonia and species name referring to Socotra, the Yemeni island where it is found.
