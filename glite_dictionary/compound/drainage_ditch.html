drainage ditch

1. NOUN

/ ˈdreɪnɪdʒ dɪtʃ / (AmE), / ˈdreɪnɪdʒ dɪtʃ / (BrE)
a narrow channel dug in the ground to carry away excess water or sewage
ditch for removing excess water

Examples

Heavy rains filled the drainage ditch along the side of the road.
Farmers regularly clear out the drainage ditch to prevent flooding in their fields.
The city installed a new drainage ditch to manage stormwater runoff.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ditch - a narrow channel dug in the ground, typically to hold or direct water
  • Synonym: culvert - a tunnel or pipe that allows water to flow under a road or railway
