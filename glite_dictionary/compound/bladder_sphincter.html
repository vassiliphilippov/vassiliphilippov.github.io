bladder sphincter

1. NOUN

/ ˈblædɚ ˈsfɪŋktɚ / (AmE), / ˈblædə ˈsfɪŋktə / (BrE)
a ring-like muscle at the opening of the urinary bladder that controls the release of urine into the urethra
muscle controlling urine release from bladder
medical

Examples

A weakened bladder sphincter can lead to urinary incontinence in some individuals.
The surgeon repaired the damaged bladder sphincter to restore normal function.
Proper function of the bladder sphincter is essential for bladder control.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sphincter - a ring of muscle that closes a bodily passage

Etymology

From 'bladder' (Old English blǣdre) and 'sphincter' (Greek sphinktḗr, meaning 'binder').
