states' rights

1. NOUN

/ steɪts raɪts / (AmE), / steɪts raɪts / (BrE)
the political powers and rights reserved to individual U.S. states by the Constitution, as opposed to those granted to the federal government
powers reserved to U.S. states by the Constitution
legal american

Examples

Debates over states' rights played a major role in American history, especially before the Civil War.
Some lawmakers argue that states' rights should be strengthened to allow for local solutions to national problems.
The Supreme Court decision was seen as a victory for states' rights advocates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: federalism - system of government with division of powers between national and regional authorities
  • Antonym: centralization - the concentration of control or power under a single authority

2. NOUN

/ ˈsteɪts raɪts / (AmE), / ˈsteɪts raɪts / (BrE)
the political doctrine that federal authority should be limited and individual U.S. states should retain powers not expressly delegated to the federal government
doctrine favoring powers for individual U.S. states
legal american

Examples

The debate over states' rights played a central role in the lead-up to the American Civil War.
Some politicians advocate for states' rights to give local governments more control over issues like education and healthcare.
Controversies about marijuana legalization often raise questions about states' rights versus federal law.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: federalism - a political system where power is shared between a central government and individual states
