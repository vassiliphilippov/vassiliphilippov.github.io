eczema hypertrophicum

1. NOUN

/ ɛkˈsiːmə haɪpərˈtroʊfɪkəm / (AmE), / ˈɛksɪmə ˌhʌɪpəˈtrɒfɪkəm / (BrE)
A type of eczema marked by thickened skin and prominent skin lines.
eczema with thickened skin
medical

Examples

The dermatologist diagnosed him with eczema hypertrophicum after noticing the thickened patches on his arms.
Patients with eczema hypertrophicum often experience persistent itching and discomfort due to the skin changes.
Treatment for eczema hypertrophicum may include topical steroids to reduce thickening and inflammation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eczema - A medical condition causing inflamed, itchy skin

Etymology

From Greek *ekzema* (eruption) and *hypertrophia* (excessive growth).
