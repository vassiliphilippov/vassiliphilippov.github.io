ovo-lacto vegetarianism

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊvoʊˈlæktoʊ ˌvɛdʒəˈtɛriənɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəʊˈlæktəʊ ˌvɛdʒɪˈtɛəriənɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
A type of vegetarian diet that includes dairy products and eggs but excludes meat, fish, and poultry.
vegetarian diet with dairy and eggs

Examples

Many people choose ovo-lacto vegetarianism for both ethical and health reasons.
Ovo-lacto vegetarianism allows for a wider variety of foods than veganism.
After much research, she adopted ovo-lacto vegetarianism as her preferred diet.

Synset Examples

Ovo-Lacto vegetarianism is the most popular style of vegetarianism in the United States, while Lacto is the most popular worldwide.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: vegetarianism - the practice of abstaining from the consumption of meat
  • Degree Higher: veganism - a stricter form of vegetarianism excluding all animal products

Etymology

From Latin 'ovum' (egg), Latin 'lac, lactis' (milk), and 'vegetarianism'.
