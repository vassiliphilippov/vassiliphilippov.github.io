carrot family

1. NOUN

/ ˈkærət ˌfæməli / (AmE), / ˈkærət ˌfæməli / (BrE)
a family of flowering plants characterized by umbrella-shaped clusters of small flowers, including parsley, carrot, anise, celery, and dill
plant family including carrot and parsley

Examples

Many common herbs, such as parsley and dill, belong to the carrot family.
The carrot family is known for its umbrella-shaped clusters of tiny flowers.
Some members of the carrot family are used as spices in cooking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Apiaceae - the scientific name for the carrot family
