common scurvy grass

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑm.ən ˈskɝ.vi ˌɡræs / (AmE), / ˌkɒm.ən ˈskɜː.vi ˌɡrɑːs / (BrE)
a small Arctic cress (Cochlearia officinalis) high in vitamin C, formerly used to prevent or treat scurvy
a vitamin C-rich Arctic cress plant

Examples

Sailors used common scurvy grass during long voyages to prevent vitamin C deficiency.
The leaves of common scurvy grass have a sharp, peppery flavor and are edible.
Historians note that common scurvy grass was once vital for treating scurvy in Arctic explorers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scurvy grass - any plant of the genus Cochlearia, used against scurvy

Etymology

Named for its historical use in treating scurvy and its widespread occurrence.
