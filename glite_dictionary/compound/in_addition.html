in addition

1. ADV

/ ɪn əˈdɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪn əˈdɪʃən / (BrE)
used to introduce further information or another point, often to emphasize that it is extra or supplementary to what has already been mentioned.
used to add extra information

Examples

In addition, she speaks three languages fluently.
More than fifty people attended the meeting; in addition, several others joined online.
The book is well researched and, in addition, very entertaining.

Synset Examples

computer chess games are getting cheaper all the time; furthermore, their quality is improving
the cellar was dark; moreover, mice nested there
what is more, there's no sign of a change

Related Terms

  • Synonym: furthermore - used to introduce an additional point; similar to 'in addition'
  • Synonym: moreover - used to add more information supporting the previous statement; synonym of 'in addition'
  • Antonym: however - used to introduce a contrasting point

2. ADV

/ ɪn əˈdɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪn əˈdɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
used to introduce an extra person or thing, or to add further information; furthermore; also.
furthermore; also

Examples

In addition, the company will offer free training sessions to its employees.
He was awarded a scholarship and, in addition, received a special honor for his research.
In addition to her responsibilities as a manager, she volunteers at a local shelter.

Synset Examples

We will, in addition, host a charity event this year.
In addition, she brought dessert for everyone.
In addition, we need to consider the budget constraints.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: furthermore - used to introduce additional information; moreover
  • Synonym: additionally - as an extra factor or circumstance; as well
  • Antonym: however - used to introduce a contrast or exception
