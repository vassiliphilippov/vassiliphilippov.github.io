khaki pant

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæki pænt / (AmE), / ˈkɑːki pænt / (BrE)
A pair of trousers made from khaki-colored fabric, typically light brown or tan, often used for casual or semi-casual wear.
light brown or tan trousers

Examples

He wore his favorite khaki pant to the job interview for a smart-casual look.
Many school uniforms include khaki pant as part of the dress code.
The store offers a wide range of khaki pant for both men and women.

Synset Examples

His kakie pants were perfect for the casual outing.
She prefers khaki pants for their versatility.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chino - cotton twill trousers, often similar in style and color
  • Antonym: jeans - denim trousers, typically blue, commonly worn as casual wear

Etymology

Khaki from Urdu 'khākī' (dust-colored), from Persian 'khāk' (dust).
