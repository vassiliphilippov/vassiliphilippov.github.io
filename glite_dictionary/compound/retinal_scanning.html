retinal scanning

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛtənəl ˈskænɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈrɛtɪnəl ˈskænɪŋ / (BrE)
a method of biometric identification that uses a scan of the retina of the eye to verify a person's identity
biometric identification by scanning the retina

Examples

Secure facilities often use retinal scanning to prevent unauthorized entry.
Advances in retinal scanning have improved the accuracy of biometric security systems.
Enrollment in the program requires a quick retinal scanning procedure at the entrance.

Synset Examples

identification by retinal scanning is complicated by eye movements

Related Terms

  • Synonym: iris recognition - biometric identification by scanning the iris of the eye

Etymology

From retina (the light-sensitive layer of tissue at the back of the eye) + scanning.
