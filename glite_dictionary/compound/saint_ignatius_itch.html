Saint Ignatius' itch

1. NOUN

/ seɪnt ɪɡˈneɪʃəs ɪtʃ / (AmE), / seɪnt ɪɡˈneɪʃəs ɪtʃ / (BrE)
a disease, also known as pellagra, caused by deficiency of niacin or tryptophan, characterized by skin irritation, gastrointestinal distress, and neurological symptoms
pellagra; a disease from niacin deficiency
medical

Examples

Doctors in the early twentieth century often referred to pellagra as Saint Ignatius' itch.
Chronic alcoholism can result in Saint Ignatius' itch due to poor nutritional intake.
Gastrointestinal issues and skin lesions are classic symptoms of Saint Ignatius' itch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pellagra - a disease caused by niacin deficiency

Etymology

Named after Saint Ignatius Loyola, as pellagra's intense itching was colloquially associated with his name.
