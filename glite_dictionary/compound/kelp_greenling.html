kelp greenling

1. NOUN

/ kɛlp ˈɡrinlɪŋ / (AmE), / kɛlp ˈɡriːnlɪŋ / (BrE)
a common food and sport fish (Hexagrammos decagrammus) found along the western coast of North America, known for its spotted body and preference for kelp forests.
a fish of the western North American coast

Examples

The fisherman proudly displayed the kelp greenling he had just caught off the rocky shore.
Biologists studied the habits of the kelp greenling in local kelp forests.
Grilled kelp greenling is popular in several coastal restaurants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: greenling - any fish of the family Hexagrammidae, including kelp greenling
