Algonkin language

1. NOUN

/ ælˈɡɑŋ.kɪn ˈlæŋ.ɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ælˈɡɒŋ.kɪn ˈlæŋ.ɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Algonquian language spoken by the Algonkin people, indigenous to parts of Canada and the northeastern United States.
Native American language of Algonkin people

Examples

The Algonkin language is still spoken by a small number of people along the Ottawa River.
He is researching the grammar and vocabulary of the Algonkin language for his thesis.
Efforts are being made to preserve the Algonkin language through community classes and digital resources.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Algonquian language - a broader family of Native American languages related to Algonkin

Etymology

From 'Algonkin', an alternate spelling of 'Algonquin', a Native American people, plus 'language'.
