whip-snake

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪpˌsneɪk / (AmE), / ˈwɪpˌsneɪk / (BrE)
Any of several small, fast-moving snakes with long, slender, whiplike tails, found in various regions.
small, slender snake with a whiplike tail

Examples

The whip-snake darted across the path and quickly disappeared into the undergrowth.
She photographed a rare whip-snake while hiking in the national park.
Unlike vipers, the whip-snake relies on its speed to escape predators.

Etymology

From 'whip' + 'snake', named for the snake's long, thin, whip-like tail.
