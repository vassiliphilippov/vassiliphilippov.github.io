Great Seal of the United States

1. NOUN

/ ɡreɪt siːl əv ðə juːˈnaɪtɪd steɪts / (AmE), / ɡreɪt siːl əv ðə juːˈnaɪtɪd steɪts / (BrE)
the official seal used to authenticate certain documents issued by the United States government
official seal of the United States government

Examples

The president signed the document, which was then stamped with the Great Seal of the United States.
The design of the Great Seal of the United States includes a bald eagle clutching arrows and an olive branch.
Only a few officials are authorized to use the Great Seal of the United States on government documents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seal - a device or emblem used to authenticate documents
