benzyl radical

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛn.zɪl ˈræd.ɪ.kəl / (AmE), / ˈbɛn.zɪl ˈræd.ɪ.kəl / (BrE)
A univalent chemical group or radical, C6H5CH2–, derived from toluene by removal of one hydrogen atom from the methyl group.
univalent radical derived from toluene

Examples

The benzyl radical plays a key role in certain organic reactions.
Researchers investigated the stability of the benzyl radical under different conditions.
When toluene loses a hydrogen atom, it forms a benzyl radical.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: radical - an atom or group of atoms with an unpaired electron

Etymology

From 'benzyl', referring to the benzene ring with a methyl group, and 'radical', a term in chemistry for an atom or group of atoms with unpaired electrons.
