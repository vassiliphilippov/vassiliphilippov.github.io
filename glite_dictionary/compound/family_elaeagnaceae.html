family Elaeagnaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛliəˌɡnæˈkeɪsiː / (AmE), / ˌɛlɪˌæɡˈneɪsiː / (BrE)
a family of shrubs or small trees, often armed with spines, including species such as the oleaster and Russian olive
a family of spiny shrubs or small trees

Examples

The family Elaeagnaceae includes the Russian olive and the oleaster.
Many species in the family Elaeagnaceae are valued for their drought resistance.
Horticulturists study the family Elaeagnaceae for its unique adaptations to poor soils.

Etymology

From the genus name Elaeagnus, derived from Greek elaia (olive) and agnos (chaste tree).
