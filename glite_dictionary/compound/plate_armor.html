plate armor

1. NOUN

/ pleɪt ˈɑrmɚ / (AmE), / pleɪt ˈɑːmə / (BrE)
specially hardened steel plates used to protect fortifications or vehicles from enemy fire
protective steel plating for defense

Examples

The tank was fitted with thick plate armor to withstand direct hits.
Advances in plate armor revolutionized battlefield fortifications.
Engineers inspected the damaged plate armor after the explosive impact.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: armor plating - protective plates added to vehicles or structures to resist attack

2. NOUN

/ pleɪt ˈɑrmɚ / (AmE), / pleɪt ˈɑːmə / (BrE)
A historical type of personal armor made from large metal plates, typically of iron or steel, worn for protection in battle, especially in the late medieval period.
historical body armor of metal plates

Examples

The knight donned his plate armor before riding onto the battlefield.
Museums often display suits of plate armor from the 15th and 16th centuries.
Advancements in weaponry eventually made plate armor obsolete.

Synset Examples

The increasing power and availability of firearms and the nature of large, state-supported infantry led to more portions of plate armour being cast off in favour of cheaper, more mobile troops.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: mail - flexible armor made of interlinked metal rings
  • Antonym: chainmail - armor consisting of small metal rings linked together
