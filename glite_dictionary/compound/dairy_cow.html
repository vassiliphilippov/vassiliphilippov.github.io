dairy cow

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛri kaʊ / (AmE), / ˈdeəri kaʊ / (BrE)
a cow that is bred or kept primarily for the production of milk rather than meat
cow bred for producing milk

Examples

The farmer owns a herd of dairy cows that provide milk for local markets.
Modern dairy cow breeds are specifically selected for their high milk yield.
Feeding practices can greatly affect a dairy cow's health and milk production.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: beef cattle - cattle bred primarily for meat production
  • Synonym: milch cow - a cow kept for milk production (dated term)
