myrtle flag

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɝːt͡l flæɡ / (AmE), / ˈmɜːtl flaɡ / (BrE)
A perennial marsh plant with sword-shaped leaves and aromatic roots, commonly found in wet habitats.
marsh plant with swordlike leaves

Examples

The myrtle flag thrives in wetlands where its aromatic roots are often harvested.
Botanists studied the swordlike leaves of the myrtle flag to better understand its adaptation to marsh environments.
Gardeners sometimes plant myrtle flag around ponds for its distinctive appearance and pleasant fragrance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sweet flag - another name for Acorus calamus, a similar marsh plant
