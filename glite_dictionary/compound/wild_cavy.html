wild cavy

1. NOUN

/ waɪld ˈkeɪ.vi / (AmE), / waɪld ˈkeɪ.vi / (BrE)
A small South American rodent (Cavia aperea), possibly ancestral to the domestic guinea pig.
South American rodent related to guinea pigs

Examples

The wild cavy roams the grasslands of South America in large groups.
Some scientists believe the wild cavy is the ancestor of today's domesticated guinea pig.
Unlike its domesticated cousin, the wild cavy prefers open fields to human homes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guinea pig - A small domesticated rodent often kept as a pet

Etymology

Cavy from Portuguese 'cavia', from Tupi 'ca'ui' (guinea pig).
