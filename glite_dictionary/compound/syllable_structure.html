syllable structure

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪləbəl ˈstrʌktʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈsɪləbəl ˈstrʌktʃə / (BrE)
the permitted arrangement of sounds within a syllable or word in a particular language.
permissible arrangement of sounds in syllables

Examples

The syllable structure of English allows words to begin with several consonants.
Different languages have different rules for syllable structure.
Children often simplify complicated syllable structures when learning to speak.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phonotactics - the rules governing the possible sound sequences in a language
