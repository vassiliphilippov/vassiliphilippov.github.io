Jacquard loom

1. NOUN

/ ʒəˈkɑrd lum / (AmE), / ʒæˈkɑːd luːm / (BrE)
a loom fitted with a special mechanism for automatically controlling the pattern of the woven fabric, allowing complex designs to be created
automatic loom for weaving patterned fabrics

Examples

The invention of the Jacquard loom revolutionized the textile industry in the 19th century.
Fabric woven on a Jacquard loom can display intricate and colorful patterns.
The museum features an original Jacquard loom among its exhibits on industrial innovation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loom - a machine for weaving yarn into textile fabrics

Etymology

Named after Joseph Marie Jacquard (1752–1834), French inventor of the loom.
