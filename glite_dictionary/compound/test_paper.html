test paper

1. NOUN

/ tɛst ˈpeɪpɚ / (AmE), / tɛst ˈpeɪpə / (BrE)
Paper treated with a chemical indicator, used to test for the presence of specific substances in a solution.
chemically treated paper for tests

Examples

She dipped the test paper into the solution to check for acidity.
A strip of test paper turned blue, indicating an alkaline substance was present.
Before conducting the experiment, he prepared several pieces of test paper for use.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: litmus paper - paper treated with litmus, used as a pH indicator

2. NOUN

/ tɛst ˈpeɪpɚ / (AmE), / tɛst ˈpeɪpə / (BrE)
A written set of questions used to assess knowledge or ability, typically as part of an examination.
a written examination

Examples

The students handed in their test papers at the end of the examination.
After grading all the test papers, the teacher posted the results online.
Each test paper consisted of multiple-choice and essay questions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exam - a formal test of a person's knowledge or proficiency in a subject
  • Synonym: quiz - a short test of knowledge, usually less formal than an exam
