Federal Home Loan Bank System

1. NOUN

/ ˌfɛdərəl hoʊm loʊn bæŋk ˈsɪstəm / (AmE), / ˌfɛdərəl həʊm ləʊn bæŋk ˈsɪstəm / (BrE)
the network of U.S. government-sponsored banks that provide lending and liquidity services to thrift institutions and other lenders.
U.S. system of banks for thrift institutions
american

Examples

The Federal Home Loan Bank System provides funds to support mortgage lending and community investment.
Many credit unions rely on the Federal Home Loan Bank System for short-term liquidity.
Established during the Great Depression, the Federal Home Loan Bank System remains vital to the U.S. financial infrastructure.
