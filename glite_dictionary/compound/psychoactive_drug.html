psychoactive drug

1. NOUN

/ ˌsaɪkoʊˈæktɪv drʌɡ / (AmE), / ˌsaɪkəʊˈæktɪv drʌɡ / (BrE)
A drug that affects mental processes, producing changes in mood, behavior, or perception.
drug affecting mood or perception
medical

Examples

The use of psychoactive drugs can have profound effects on mood and cognition.
Many patients are prescribed psychoactive drugs to treat mental health disorders.
Alcohol and caffeine are both considered psychoactive drugs due to their impact on the brain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hallucinogen - a drug causing hallucinations
  • Degree Lower: depressant - a drug that reduces brain or bodily function
  • Degree Higher: stimulant - a drug that increases brain activity or alertness

Etymology

From 'psycho-' (Greek psukhē, meaning 'soul, mind') and 'active', describing effects on the mind.
