balloon bomb

1. NOUN

/ bəˈlun bɑm / (AmE), / bəˈluːn bɒm / (BrE)
a bomb carried by a balloon, typically used as an airborne weapon to deliver explosives over long distances
bomb carried by a balloon

Examples

During World War II, the Japanese sent balloon bombs across the Pacific Ocean to target North America.
Many balloon bombs failed to explode, but some caused damage and even casualties.
The museum displayed a defused balloon bomb as part of its wartime history exhibit.

Synset Examples

In World War II the Japanese launched balloon bombs against North America

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incendiary balloon - balloon designed to start fires by dropping incendiaries
