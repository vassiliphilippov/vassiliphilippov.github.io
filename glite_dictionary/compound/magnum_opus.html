magnum opus

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæɡ.nəm ˈoʊ.pəs / (AmE), / ˌmæɡ.nəm ˈəʊ.pəs / (BrE)
The greatest single work produced by an artist, writer, or composer, regarded as their most important achievement.
greatest work by an artist or author

Examples

Many critics consider 'Ulysses' to be James Joyce's magnum opus.
The painting, widely hailed as his magnum opus, hangs in the national gallery.
Composing his symphony took years, and he hoped it would become his magnum opus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: masterpiece - a work of outstanding artistry, skill, or workmanship
  • Synonym: tour de force - an impressive performance or achievement carried out with great skill

Etymology

From Latin 'magnum opus' meaning 'great work'.
