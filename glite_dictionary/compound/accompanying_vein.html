accompanying vein

1. NOUN

/ əˈkʌmpəniɪŋ veɪn / (AmE), / əˈkʌmpəniɪŋ veɪn / (BrE)
A vein that runs alongside an artery or other structure, often so that the arterial pulses help in returning blood through the vein.
vein running alongside an artery or structure
medical

Examples

The surgeon carefully identified the accompanying vein during the procedure to avoid damaging it.
In some regions of the body, the accompanying vein makes venous access easier for clinicians.
The pulsations of the artery can help propel blood within the accompanying vein.
