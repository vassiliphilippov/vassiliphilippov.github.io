player piano

1. NOUN

/ ˈpleɪɚ piˈænoʊ / (AmE), / ˈpleɪə piˈænəʊ / (BrE)
a piano that plays automatically by means of a mechanism operated by a roll of perforated paper
mechanically operated self-playing piano

Examples

The antique player piano in the corner began to play lively music as the guests arrived.
He inherited a player piano from his grandfather and enjoys listening to old rolls.
Many museums preserve at least one player piano to demonstrate nineteenth-century mechanical innovation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pianola - another term for a player piano
