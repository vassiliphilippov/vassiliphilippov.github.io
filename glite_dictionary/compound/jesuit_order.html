Jesuit order

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɛzjuˌɪt ˈɔrdər / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛzjuːɪt ˈɔːdə / (BrE)
A Roman Catholic religious order, officially known as the Society of Jesus, founded by Saint Ignatius of Loyola in 1534, noted for its work in education, scholarship, and missionary activity.
Roman Catholic religious order (Society of Jesus)

Examples

The Jesuit order established some of the world's oldest and most respected universities.
Many members of the Jesuit order have played significant roles in the fields of science and philosophy.
He joined the Jesuit order after being inspired by their commitment to education and social justice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Society of Jesus - official name of the Jesuit order
  • Degree Lower: Dominican order - another major Roman Catholic religious order
  • Degree Lower: Franciscan order - another major Catholic religious order

Etymology

From Latin 'Societas Iesu' (Society of Jesus), with 'Jesuit' from Jesus + -ite (follower).
