sheep fescue

1. NOUN

/ ʃiːp ˈfɛskju / (AmE), / ʃiːp ˈfɛskjuː / (BrE)
a hardy perennial grass (Festuca ovina) often used for grazing sheep in upland areas or as a lawn grass
a grass species used for grazing or lawns

Examples

The hillside was covered with sheep fescue, providing excellent grazing for the flock.
Many homeowners choose sheep fescue for drought-tolerant lawns.
The botanist explained that sheep fescue thrives in poor, rocky soils.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fescue - a genus of tufted grasses

Etymology

From 'sheep' (the animal) and 'fescue' (from Latin 'festuca', a straw or stalk).
