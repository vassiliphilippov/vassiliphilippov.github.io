wage scale

1. NOUN

/ weɪdʒ skeɪl / (AmE), / weɪdʒ skeɪl / (BrE)
a system or schedule that sets out the different rates of pay for various jobs or levels of experience within an organization or industry
system of pay rates for jobs

Examples

The company revised its wage scale to offer more competitive starting salaries.
Union representatives negotiated an improved wage scale for entry-level workers last year.
Some employees were upset because their positions were lower on the wage scale than expected.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pay scale - a scale of wages or salaries paid for different jobs or skill levels
