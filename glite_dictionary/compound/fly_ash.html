fly ash

1. NOUN

/ flaɪ æʃ / (AmE), / flaɪ æʃ / (BrE)
fine particles of ash carried into the air during the combustion of fuel, especially in power stations
fine ash particles from burned fuel

Examples

The factory installed filters to reduce the amount of fly ash released into the atmosphere.
Concrete made with fly ash is often stronger and more durable.
Researchers are studying how to recycle fly ash from coal-fired power plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ash - the powdery residue left after the burning of a substance
