nettle-leaved bellflower

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɛt̬l̩ liːvd ˈbɛlˌflaʊɚ / (AmE), / ˈnɛtl liːvd ˈbɛlˌflaʊə / (BrE)
A European bellflower (Campanula trachelium) with blue-purple to lilac flowers, formerly used to treat sore throat.
European bellflower plant species

Examples

The nettle-leaved bellflower grows in woodland areas across Europe.
Herbalists once used the nettle-leaved bellflower as a remedy for sore throats.
I spotted a cluster of nettle-leaved bellflowers while walking through the meadow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bellflower - a plant of the genus Campanula with bell-shaped flowers
