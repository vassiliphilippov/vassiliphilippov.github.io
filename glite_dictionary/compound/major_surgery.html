major surgery

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪdʒər ˈsɝːdʒəri / (AmE), / ˈmeɪdʒə ˈsɜːdʒəri / (BrE)
a surgical procedure that involves significant risk, typically requiring anesthesia or respiratory assistance
serious surgery involving anesthesia or risk
medical

Examples

After his accident, he needed major surgery to repair his internal injuries.
The hospital specializes in major surgery such as heart transplants and joint replacements.
Because major surgery carries risks, patients are thoroughly assessed beforehand.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: minor surgery - a less serious surgical procedure, typically not requiring anesthesia
