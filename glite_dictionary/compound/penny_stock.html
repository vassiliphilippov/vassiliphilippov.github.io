penny stock

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛni stɑk / (AmE), / ˈpɛni stɒk / (BrE)
A share in a small public company that trades at a very low price, typically less than $1 per share.
low-priced share, usually under $1

Examples

Many inexperienced investors are drawn to penny stocks because of their low cost and high potential returns.
After the market crash, several companies' shares became classified as penny stocks.
It is risky to invest in a penny stock due to its unpredictable price movements.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: blue chip - a high-value, reliable stock in a large, well-established company
