hunting whip

1. NOUN

/ ˈhʌntɪŋ wɪp / (AmE), / ˈhʌntɪŋ wɪp / (BrE)
a whip carried by a mounted rider, chiefly in fox hunting, used to control or signal to the hounds rather than the horse
whip used in traditional fox hunting

Examples

The rider raised his hunting whip to keep the hounds in check during the chase.
She purchased a new hunting whip before joining the country hunt.
While riding, he tapped his hunting whip lightly on his boot to signal the pack.
