heavy precipitation

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛvi ˌprɛsɪˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈhɛvi ˌprɛsɪˈteɪʃn̩ / (BrE)
rain, snow, or other forms of moisture falling from the sky in large amounts within a short period
large amount of rain or snow

Examples

The forecast predicts heavy precipitation throughout the afternoon, so bring an umbrella.
Flash flooding can result from heavy precipitation in low-lying areas.
Heavy precipitation during the night caused road closures in several parts of the city.

Synset Examples

The forecast warned of heavy precipitation this weekend.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: downpour - a sudden and heavy fall of rain
  • Degree Lower: drizzle - light rain falling in very fine drops
