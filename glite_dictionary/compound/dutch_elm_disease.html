Dutch elm disease

1. NOUN

/ dʌtʃ ɛlm dɪˈziz / (AmE), / dʌtʃ ɛlm dɪˈziːz / (BrE)
A serious disease of elm trees caused by a fungus, leading to wilting, yellowing, and often death of affected trees.
fungal disease of elm trees

Examples

Many neighborhoods lost their largest shade trees to Dutch elm disease in the 1970s.
The city planted resistant varieties after Dutch elm disease decimated its elms.
Researchers are developing methods to control Dutch elm disease and protect remaining trees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elm - a type of tree often affected by Dutch elm disease

Etymology

Named for its identification by Dutch plant pathologists in the 1920s.
