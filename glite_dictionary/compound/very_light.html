Very light

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɛri laɪt / (AmE), / ˈvɛəri laɪt / (BrE)
A colored flare fired from a Very pistol, used as a signal, especially in military or maritime contexts.
colored flare fired from a Very pistol

Examples

The crew fired a Very light to signal their location to the nearby ship.
During the exercise, a red Very light was launched as an emergency signal.
They kept several Very lights on hand for night-time signaling.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flare - a sudden bright flame or light, often used as a signal

Etymology

Named after American naval officer Edward Wilson Very, who invented the signal pistol.
