chronic kidney failure

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɑː.nɪk ˈkɪd.ni ˈfeɪl.jɚ / (AmE), / ˈkrɒ.nɪk ˈkɪd.ni ˈfeɪl.jə / (BrE)
A long-term medical condition in which the kidneys gradually lose their ability to function properly, often resulting from underlying systemic disorders.
long-term loss of kidney function
medical

Examples

His doctor diagnosed him with chronic kidney failure after a series of blood and urine tests.
People suffering from chronic kidney failure often require dialysis to survive.
Managing blood pressure and diabetes is crucial in preventing chronic kidney failure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: renal failure - loss of kidney function
  • Antonym: acute kidney failure - sudden loss of kidney function
