family Tuberculariaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌfæməli tuːˌbɜːrkjəˌlɛriˈeɪsiː / (AmE), / ˌfæmɪli tjuːˌbɜːkjʊləˌrɪˈeɪsiː / (BrE)
A large family of mainly saprophytic imperfect fungi.
family of imperfect fungi

Examples

Many species within the family Tuberculariaceae are known for their role in decomposing organic matter.
The family Tuberculariaceae consists primarily of fungi that do not reproduce sexually.
Researchers discovered a new saprophytic fungus belonging to the family Tuberculariaceae in the rainforest.

Etymology

From New Latin Tubercularia (type genus) + -aceae (a suffix denoting families in taxonomy).
