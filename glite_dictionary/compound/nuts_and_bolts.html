nuts and bolts

1. NOUN

/ nʌts ənd boʊlts / (AmE), / nʌts ənd bəʊlts / (BrE)
the basic and practical details of how something works or is put together
practical details; essential components

Examples

Before we start, let's go over the nuts and bolts of the procedure.
The book explains the nuts and bolts of starting a small business.
She really understands the nuts and bolts of software development.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mechanics - the working parts or practical aspects of something

Etymology

Originally referring to essential hardware in machinery; figurative use dates from the mid-20th century.
