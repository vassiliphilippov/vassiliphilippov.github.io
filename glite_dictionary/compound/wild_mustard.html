wild mustard

1. NOUN

/ waɪld ˈmʌstərd / (AmE), / waɪld ˈmʌstəd / (BrE)
a weedy Eurasian plant with yellow flowers, often considered a pest in grain fields
weedy yellow-flowered plant, agricultural pest

Examples

Wild mustard often invades grain fields, making it a troublesome weed for farmers.
The bright yellow flowers of wild mustard can be seen along country roads in spring.
Controlling wild mustard is an important part of crop management for cereal growers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mustard - any of several plants of the family Brassicaceae with aromatic seeds
