Qin dynasty

1. PROPN

/ tʃɪn ˈdaɪ.nə.sti / (AmE), / tʃɪn ˈdɪn.ə.sti / (BrE)
the Chinese dynasty (221–206 BC) that established the first centralized imperial government and began construction of the Great Wall of China.
first imperial Chinese dynasty, 221–206 BC

Examples

The Qin dynasty unified China under a single government for the first time in its history.
Many sections of the Great Wall were originally built during the Qin dynasty.
Legalist philosophy greatly influenced the political structure of the Qin dynasty.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Han dynasty - the Chinese dynasty succeeding the Qin, known for its long and influential rule

Etymology

From Mandarin Qín Cháo (秦朝), Qín refers to the ruling house; dynasty from Greek dynasteia (power, lordship).
