maple family

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪpəl ˌfæməli / (AmE), / ˈmeɪpəl ˌfæmɪli / (BrE)
a family of trees and shrubs (Aceraceae) in the order Sapindales, including the maples and their close relatives
family of trees and shrubs including maples

Examples

The maple family includes both ornamental trees and species important for timber and syrup production.
Many species in the maple family are valued for their brilliant autumn foliage.
The botanist lectured on the unique characteristics of the maple family during the conference.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Aceraceae - the botanical family including maples
  • Degree Lower: maple - a tree of the genus Acer, especially known for its lobed leaves and sap
