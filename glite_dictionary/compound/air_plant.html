air plant

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛr plænt / (AmE), / ˈeə ˌplɑːnt / (BrE)
a plant that absorbs moisture and nutrients from the air and rain, usually growing on another plant without being parasitic
plant that grows by absorbing air moisture

Examples

Many people decorate their homes with air plants because they require no soil to grow.
The air plant attaches itself to tree branches but does not harm the host tree.
After misting my air plant every few days, I noticed it started to sprout new leaves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: epiphyte - a plant that grows on another plant non-parasitically
