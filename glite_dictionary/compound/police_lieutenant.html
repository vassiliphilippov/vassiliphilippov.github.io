police lieutenant

1. NOUN

/ pəˈlis luːˈtɛnənt / (AmE), / pəˈliːs ljuːˈtɛnənt / (BrE)
a rank of officer in a police force, typically above sergeant and below captain
officer rank in police force

Examples

After years of service, she was promoted to police lieutenant and given responsibility for an entire precinct.
The police lieutenant led the investigation into the high-profile robbery downtown.
During the meeting, the police lieutenant outlined strategies to improve community relations.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: police captain - a police officer of higher rank than lieutenant
  • Degree Lower: police sergeant - a police officer of lower rank than lieutenant

Etymology

Lieutenant from Old French, meaning 'deputy', used in military and law enforcement contexts.
