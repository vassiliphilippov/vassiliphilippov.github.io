Trojan horse

1. NOUN

/ ˈtroʊdʒən hɔrs / (AmE), / ˈtrəʊdʒən hɔːs / (BrE)
a person or group that secretly supports the enemy or causes harm from within an organization or country, often by engaging in espionage or sabotage
someone who undermines from within

Examples

Many saw the new employee as a Trojan horse working for the competition.
The government feared infiltration by a Trojan horse intent on leaking secrets.
Vigilance is essential to detect a Trojan horse within the organization.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fifth column - a group within a country who secretly supports its enemies

Etymology

From the Greek myth of the Trojan Horse, a large wooden horse used by the Greeks to enter Troy by deception.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtroʊ.dʒən hɔrs / (AmE), / ˈtrəʊ.dʒən hɔːs / (BrE)
A person, thing, or strategy that appears harmless but is actually intended to cause harm or accomplish a hidden objective, especially by infiltrating a target.
deceptive ploy or device for infiltration

Examples

Many saw the generous offer as a Trojan horse designed to manipulate public opinion.
The IT team warned that the new email attachment could be a Trojan horse carrying malware.
She realized too late that the partnership was a Trojan horse for corporate espionage.

Synset Examples

The proposal felt like a Trojan horse to many.
The new software was a Trojan horse for data theft.
They discovered the app was a Trojan horse in disguise.
He used a Trojan horse to sneak into the secure system.
The marketing campaign turned into a Trojan horse for hidden agendas.
The software update was a Trojan horse that installed malware.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ruse - a clever trick or scheme used to deceive someone
  • Synonym: stratagem - a plan or scheme, especially one used to outwit an opponent
  • Synonym: trap - a trick intended to catch someone unawares

Etymology

From the ancient Greek myth of the wooden horse used by the Greeks to infiltrate Troy.
