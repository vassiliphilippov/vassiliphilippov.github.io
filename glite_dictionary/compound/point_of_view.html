point of view

1. NOUN

/ pɔɪnt əv vjuː / (AmE), / pɔɪnt əv vjuː / (BrE)
a particular attitude or way of considering a matter; a mental position or perspective from which something is observed or considered
particular perspective or viewpoint

Examples

From a legal point of view, the company did nothing wrong.
The novel is written from the point of view of a child.
Try to look at the situation from my point of view.

Synset Examples

we should consider this problem from the viewpoint of the Russians
teaching history gave him a special point of view toward current events

Related Terms

  • Synonym: viewpoint - a particular way of considering something; perspective
  • Synonym: perspective - a particular attitude toward something; a way of regarding something
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06220098-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ pɔɪnt əv vju / (AmE), / pɔɪnt əv vjuː / (BrE)
the position in space from which something is seen or observed
position from which something is viewed

Examples

The painting was created from a unique point of view near the water's edge.
If you move to the left, you'll get a different point of view of the statue.
The director chose a high point of view to film the action scene.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perspective - the appearance of things relative to one another as determined by their distance and position
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
