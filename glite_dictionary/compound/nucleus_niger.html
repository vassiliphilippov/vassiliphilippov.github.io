nucleus niger

1. NOUN

/ ˈnuːkliəs ˈnaɪdʒər / (AmE), / ˈnjuːkliəs ˈnaɪdʒə / (BrE)
a layer of deeply pigmented gray matter in the midbrain, associated with movement and certain neurological disorders such as Parkinson's disease
midbrain structure involved in movement
medical

Examples

Degeneration of the nucleus niger is a hallmark of Parkinson's disease.
Researchers studied how changes in the nucleus niger affect motor control.
Damage to the nucleus niger can result in tremors and difficulty moving.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: substantia nigra - a structure in the midbrain involved in reward and movement; also known as nucleus niger

Etymology

From Latin nucleus (kernel, core) + niger (black), referring to its dark coloration.
