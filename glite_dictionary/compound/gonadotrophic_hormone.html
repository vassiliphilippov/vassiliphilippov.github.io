gonadotrophic hormone

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡoʊ.næ.dəˈtroʊ.fɪk ˈhɔːr.moʊn / (AmE), / ˌɡəʊ.næ.dəˈtrəʊ.fɪk ˈhɔː.məʊn / (BrE)
a hormone produced by the anterior pituitary gland and placenta that stimulates the gonads and regulates reproductive activity
hormone affecting reproductive organs
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that a gonadotrophic hormone imbalance could affect fertility.
Human chorionic gonadotrophic hormone levels are measured during pregnancy tests.
Researchers studied the role of each gonadotrophic hormone in ovarian function.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gonadotropin - any hormone that stimulates the gonads; synonym of gonadotrophic hormone

Etymology

From 'gonad' (Greek gonos, seed) + 'trophic' (Greek trophē, nourishment) + 'hormone'.
