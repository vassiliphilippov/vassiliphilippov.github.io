family Peramelidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli pɛrəˈmɛlɪˌdi / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli pɛrəˈmɛlɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of small to medium-sized marsupial mammals known as bandicoots, native to Australia and nearby regions.
family of bandicoots (marsupial mammals)

Examples

The family Peramelidae includes several species of bandicoots found in Australia and New Guinea.
Researchers are studying the evolutionary history of the family Peramelidae to better understand marsupial diversification.
Conservation efforts are crucial for protecting members of the family Peramelidae from habitat loss and predators.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bandicoot - small marsupial mammals native to Australia and New Guinea

Etymology

From New Latin 'Peramelidae,' based on genus 'Perameles' + '-idae' (family suffix).
