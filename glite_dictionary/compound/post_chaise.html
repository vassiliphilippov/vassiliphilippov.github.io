post chaise

1. NOUN

/ poʊst ʃeɪz / (AmE), / pəʊst ʃeɪz / (BrE)
A closed, four-wheeled horse-drawn carriage, formerly used for transporting passengers and mail.
closed horse-drawn carriage (historical)

Examples

In the 18th century, a wealthy traveler might hire a post chaise for a long journey.
The letter was sent to London by post chaise, arriving overnight.
They admired the elegant design of the restored post chaise at the museum.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stagecoach - large horse-drawn vehicle for passengers and mail

Etymology

From 'post' (referring to the mail or postal service) + French 'chaise' (chair, carriage).
