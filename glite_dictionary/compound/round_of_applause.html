round of applause

1. NOUN

/ raʊnd əv əˈplɔz / (AmE), / raʊnd əv əˈplɔːz / (BrE)
an outbreak or period of clapping by an audience to show approval or appreciation
period of audience clapping in approval

Examples

The speaker received a round of applause from the standing audience at the end of her presentation.
After the final notes faded, the musicians basked in the round of applause that filled the concert hall.
Please give a warm round of applause for our volunteers who worked tirelessly behind the scenes.

Synset Examples

He was met with a round of applause after his speech.
The performance ended with a round of applause.
The team received a round of applause for their efforts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ovation - an enthusiastic show of appreciation by applause
