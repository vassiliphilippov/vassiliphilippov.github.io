genus Ardisia

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ɑːrˈdɪzi.ə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ɑːˈdɪzɪə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical evergreen subshrubs, climbers, and trees native to Asia, Australasia, and the Americas.
genus of tropical evergreen plants

Examples

The genus Ardisia includes several ornamental species prized for their glossy leaves and colorful berries.
Botanists have discovered new species within the genus Ardisia in the rainforests of Malaysia.
Many members of the genus Ardisia are grown as houseplants due to their attractive foliage.

Etymology

From New Latin 'Ardisia', the genus name, possibly from Greek 'ardis' (point, arrowhead), referring to the pointed leaves or flower parts.
