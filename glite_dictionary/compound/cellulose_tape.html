cellulose tape

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛl.jəˌloʊs teɪp / (AmE), / ˈsɛl.jʊˌləʊs teɪp / (BrE)
a transparent or semitransparent adhesive tape, often used for sealing, attaching, or mending paper and other light materials
transparent adhesive tape for sealing or mending

Examples

She used cellulose tape to wrap the gift box securely.
A strip of cellulose tape held the torn page together.
Do we have any cellulose tape left in the stationery drawer?

Etymology

From 'cellulose', the main constituent of plant cell walls, once used in the tape's manufacture, and 'tape'.
