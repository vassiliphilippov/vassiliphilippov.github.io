Mexican juniper

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛksɪkən ˈdʒuːnɪpər / (AmE), / ˈmɛksɪkən ˈdʒuːnɪpə / (BrE)
A small tree native to western Texas and the mountains of Mexico, characterized by spreading branches and drooping branchlets.
small tree native to western Texas and Mexico

Examples

The Mexican juniper is commonly found in the mountain regions of northern Mexico.
Botanists studied the unique drooping branchlets of the Mexican juniper.
During our hike in Texas, we spotted a tall Mexican juniper near the trail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: juniper - an evergreen shrub or small tree with berry-like cones
