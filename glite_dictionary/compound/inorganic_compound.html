inorganic compound

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˌɔːɹˈɡænɪk kəmˈpaʊnd / (AmE), / ɪnˌɔːɡˈænɪk kəmˈpaʊnd / (BrE)
a chemical compound that does not contain carbon, or is not considered organic; typically includes minerals, salts, and metals
compound not containing carbon

Examples

Table salt is an inorganic compound commonly found in nature.
Most rocks and minerals are made of inorganic compounds rather than organic ones.
Water is classified as an inorganic compound because it does not contain carbon.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: organic compound - a compound containing carbon, typically found in living things

Etymology

From 'inorganic' (not organic, from Latin 'organicus') + 'compound' (from Latin 'componere', to put together).
