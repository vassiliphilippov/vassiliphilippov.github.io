flying robin

1. NOUN

/ ˈflaɪɪŋ ˈrɑːbɪn / (AmE), / ˈflaɪɪŋ ˈrɒbɪn / (BrE)
A tropical fish with large, fanlike pectoral fins that allow it to glide underwater; not related to searobins.
tropical fish with gliding fins

Examples

Divers marveled at the vibrant fins of the flying robin as it glided effortlessly above the coral reef.
Unlike the searobin, the flying robin uses its broad fins to move gracefully under water.
Scientists are studying how the unique anatomy of the flying robin enables its underwater gliding abilities.
