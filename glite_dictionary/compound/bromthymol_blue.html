bromthymol blue

1. NOUN

/ broʊmˌθaɪmɑl ˈblu / (AmE), / brəʊmˌθaɪmɒl ˈbluː / (BrE)
a chemical dye used as an acid-base indicator, changing color between pH 6.0 and 7.6
chemical pH indicator dye
medical

Examples

bromthymol blue turns yellow in acidic solutions and blue in basic solutions.
To determine the pH of the liquid, she added a drop of bromthymol blue to the test tube.
Many science kits for students include bromthymol blue to demonstrate acid-base reactions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phenolphthalein - a chemical indicator used to test pH in a different range
  • Synonym: methyl orange - an acid-base indicator dye with a different pH range

Etymology

From 'brom', referring to bromine, and 'thymol', a constituent derived from thyme oil, indicating its chemical structure.
