circumflex iliac vein

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɝːkəmˌflɛks ˈɪliˌæk veɪn / (AmE), / ˈsɜːkəmˌflɛks ˈɪliˌak veɪn / (BrE)
a vein that accompanies the circumflex iliac artery in the pelvic region
vein accompanying the circumflex iliac artery
medical

Examples

The surgeon carefully identified the circumflex iliac vein before proceeding with the operation.
An injury to the circumflex iliac vein can cause significant bleeding in the pelvic area.
Medical imaging revealed a blockage in the patient's circumflex iliac vein.

Etymology

From Latin 'circumflexus' (bent around) and 'ilium' (upper part of the hip bone).
