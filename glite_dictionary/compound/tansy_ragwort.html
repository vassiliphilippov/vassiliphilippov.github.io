tansy ragwort

1. NOUN

/ ˈtænzi ˈræɡˌwɔrt / (AmE), / ˈtænzi ˈræɡˌwɔːt / (BrE)
a widespread European weed with yellow, daisy-like flowers, sometimes considered a noxious weed and toxic to cattle if eaten in quantity
toxic yellow-flowered European weed

Examples

The farmer was concerned to see tansy ragwort spreading across his pasture.
Cattle can become seriously ill if they consume large amounts of tansy ragwort.
Volunteers gathered in the field to remove patches of tansy ragwort before it could flower.

Etymology

'Ragwort' from Middle English 'ragged' (referring to the ragged leaves) and 'wort' (plant); 'tansy' refers to its resemblance to the true tansy plant.
