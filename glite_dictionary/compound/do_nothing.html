to do nothing

1. VERB

/ duː ˈnʌθɪŋ / (AmE), / duː ˈnʌθɪŋ / (BrE)
to spend time without engaging in any activity; to be idle or inactive.
be idle; not do anything

Examples

On Sundays, I like to do nothing except read and nap.
They decided to do nothing about the minor complaint and focus on bigger problems.
Sometimes, the best way to recharge is simply to do nothing for a while.

Synset Examples

We plan to relax and do nothing all holiday.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to relax - to rest and do nothing strenuous
  • Antonym: work - to engage in activity for a purpose or result
