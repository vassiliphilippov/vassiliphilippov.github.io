Saint Bridget

1. PROPN

/ seɪnt ˈbrɪdʒɪt / (AmE), / seɪnt ˈbrɪdʒɪt / (BrE)
An Irish abbess and one of the patron saints of Ireland, venerated for her piety and charitable works (c. 453–523).
Irish patron saint and abbess

Examples

Many Irish people celebrate the feast day of Saint Bridget on February 1st.
The legend of Saint Bridget has inspired countless stories and traditions in Ireland.
A beautiful stained glass window depicts Saint Bridget holding a cross of reeds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Saint Brigid - alternative spelling of Saint Bridget, Irish patron saint
  • Synonym: Saint Patrick - another patron saint of Ireland
