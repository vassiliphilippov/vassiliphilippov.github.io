heavy artillery

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛvi ɑɹˈtɪlɚi / (AmE), / ˈhɛvi ɑːˈtɪləri / (BrE)
large guns with a high caliber, used in warfare for bombardment and destruction of targets at long range
large-caliber military guns

Examples

The general called for heavy artillery to support the infantry’s advance across the battlefield.
Enemy tanks were destroyed by a barrage of heavy artillery during the night attack.
The sound of heavy artillery echoed through the mountains, shaking the nearby village.

Synset Examples

We need heavy artillery to break through their defenses.
The military deployed heavy artillery in the field.
They brought in heavy artillery for the final push.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: artillery - large-caliber guns used in warfare
  • Degree Lower: field gun - a mobile artillery piece used on the battlefield
  • Degree Higher: siege artillery - artillery designed to bombard fortifications
