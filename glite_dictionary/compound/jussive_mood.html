jussive mood

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˈsɪv muːd / (AmE), / dʒʌˈsɪv məʊd / (BrE)
a grammatical mood used to express orders, commands, or suggestions intended to influence the listener's actions
a grammatical mood expressing commands or suggestions

Examples

Many languages use the jussive mood to express polite requests or mild commands.
In some grammatical descriptions, the jussive mood is contrasted with the imperative mood.
Students were asked to identify verbs in the jussive mood within the given sentences.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imperative mood - grammatical mood used for direct commands

Etymology

From Latin 'iussivus' (related to commanding or ordering) + 'mood' (grammatical term for verb form).
