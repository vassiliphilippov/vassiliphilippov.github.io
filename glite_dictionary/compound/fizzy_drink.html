fizzy drink

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪzi drɪŋk / (AmE), / ˈfɪzi drɪŋk / (BrE)
A non-alcoholic beverage that contains carbon dioxide, making it bubbly or sparkling; a carbonated soft drink.
carbonated soft drink
british

Examples

Would you like a fizzy drink with your meal, or just water?
Children often prefer a fizzy drink to plain juice at birthday parties.
She accidentally shook the fizzy drink can, and it sprayed everywhere when opened.

Synset Examples

I love fizzy drinks on a hot summer day.
She offered me a fizzy soda from the cooler.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soft drink - a non-alcoholic sweetened carbonated beverage
  • Synonym: soda - carbonated soft drink, chiefly in American English

Etymology

From 'fizzy' (suggesting bubbles) + 'drink'. 'Fizzy' relates to 'fizz', imitative of the sound of bubbles.
