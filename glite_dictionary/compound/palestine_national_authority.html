Palestine National Authority

1. PROPN

/ pæləsˌtaɪn næʃəˈnæl ɔːˈθɔrəti / (AmE), / pæləsˌtaɪn næʃəˈnal ɔːˈθɒrɪti / (BrE)
The governing authority established to administer parts of the West Bank and Gaza Strip, currently under varying degrees of Israeli control.
governing body for Palestinian territories

Examples

The Palestine National Authority was created following the Oslo Accords in 1994.
She works as an advisor to the Palestine National Authority on education reform.
International aid organizations frequently coordinate with the Palestine National Authority to provide support in the West Bank.
