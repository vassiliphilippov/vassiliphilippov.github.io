Hawaiian honeycreeper

1. NOUN

/ həˈwaɪ.i.ən ˈhʌniˌkripər / (AmE), / həˈwaɪ.i.ən ˈhʌniˌkriːpə / (BrE)
any of various small to medium-sized songbirds native to the Hawaiian Islands, known for their diverse forms and often specialized bills.
small finchlike bird of Hawaii

Examples

A brightly colored Hawaiian honeycreeper perched on a native koa tree, searching for nectar.
Several species of Hawaiian honeycreeper have become rare due to habitat loss and introduced diseases.
Researchers study the evolution of the Hawaiian honeycreeper to understand adaptive radiation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: honeycreeper - any small songbird of the family Thraupidae or Drepanididae
