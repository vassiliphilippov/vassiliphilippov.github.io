multiplier factor

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌl.təˌplaɪ.ɚ ˈfæk.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈmʌl.tɪˌplaɪ.ə ˈfæk.tə / (BrE)
a number by which another number (the multiplicand) is multiplied to produce a product
number used to multiply another

Examples

The multiplier factor in the equation is 4, which means the total will be four times the original amount.
To calculate profits, a multiplier factor is applied to the base earnings.
Adjusting the multiplier factor will increase or decrease the overall value depending on its size.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: multiplier - a number by which another number is multiplied
