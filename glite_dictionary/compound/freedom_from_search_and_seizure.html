freedom from search and seizure

1. NOUN

/ ˈfriː.dəm frəm ˈsɝːtʃ ənd ˈsiː.ʒɚ / (AmE), / ˈfriː.dəm frəm ˈsɜːtʃ ənd ˈsiː.ʒə / (BrE)
The legal right to protection against unreasonable search and seizure by authorities, as guaranteed by the Fourth Amendment to the US Constitution.
legal right against unreasonable searches
legal american

Examples

The Constitution guarantees freedom from search and seizure to every citizen.
His lawyer argued that the police violated his freedom from search and seizure.
Civil rights activists stressed the importance of protecting freedom from search and seizure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Fourth Amendment - constitutional amendment protecting against unreasonable searches and seizures
