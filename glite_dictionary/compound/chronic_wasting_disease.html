chronic wasting disease

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɑːnɪk ˈweɪstɪŋ dɪˌziz / (AmE), / ˈkrɒnɪk ˈweɪstɪŋ dɪˌziːz / (BrE)
A contagious neurological disease affecting deer, elk, and related species, characterized by weight loss and behavioral changes, and similar to mad cow disease.
a neurological disease in deer and elk
medical

Examples

Scientists are studying how chronic wasting disease spreads among wild deer populations.
The government issued warnings to hunters about chronic wasting disease in the region.
Chronic wasting disease has been found in both captive and free-ranging elk herds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mad cow disease - a neurological disease affecting cattle, also known as bovine spongiform encephalopathy

Etymology

Named for its chronic progression and characteristic weight loss (wasting) in affected animals.
