flutter kick

1. NOUN

/ ˈflʌtər kɪk / (AmE), / ˈflʌtə kɪk / (BrE)
a swimming technique in which the legs are moved rapidly up and down with straight knees, commonly used in freestyle and backstroke
rapid up-and-down swimming leg movement

Examples

The coach demonstrated how to do a proper flutter kick while keeping your legs straight.
To improve your speed in freestyle, work on strengthening your flutter kick.
During the backstroke, she maintained a steady flutter kick to propel herself through the water.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: frog kick - broad, circular leg kick used in breaststroke
