erythema solare

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛr.ɪˈθiː.mə soʊˈlɛəri / (AmE), / ˌɛr.ɪˈθiː.mə səʊˈlɑːreɪ / (BrE)
Redness of the skin caused by exposure to the rays of the sun; sunburn.
skin redness from sun exposure
medical

Examples

After a long day at the beach, he developed erythema solare on his shoulders.
The doctor diagnosed the patient's painful redness as erythema solare.
Wearing sunscreen can help prevent erythema solare during outdoor activities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sunburn - inflammation of the skin from overexposure to the sun
  • Degree Lower: erythema - redness of the skin or mucous membranes

Etymology

From Greek 'erythema' (redness) and Latin 'solaris' (of the sun).
