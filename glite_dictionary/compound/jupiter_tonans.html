Jupiter Tonans

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒuːpɪtər toʊˈnænz / (AmE), / ˈdʒuːpɪtə təˈnænz / (BrE)
An epithet of Jupiter, the Roman god, meaning 'Jupiter the Thunderer', highlighting his aspect as the bringer of thunder.
epithet of Jupiter as the Thunderer

Examples

The temple of Jupiter Tonans was famed for its magnificent statue of the god holding a thunderbolt.
Ancient Romans often invoked Jupiter Tonans during storms to seek protection from lightning.
In literature, Jupiter Tonans symbolizes the immense power and authority of the deity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Jupiter - the chief Roman god, god of the sky and thunder

Etymology

From Latin 'Jupiter Tonans' meaning 'Jupiter the Thunderer'.
