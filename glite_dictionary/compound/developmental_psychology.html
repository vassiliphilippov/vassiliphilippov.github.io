developmental psychology

1. NOUN

/ dəˌvɛləpˈmɛntəl saɪˌkɑlədʒi / (AmE), / dɪˌvɛləpˈmɛntəl saɪˌkɒlədʒi / (BrE)
The branch of psychology concerned with how children and adolescents develop socially, emotionally, and mentally over time.
branch of psychology studying child development

Examples

She decided to study developmental psychology to better understand how children learn and grow.
Recent advances in developmental psychology have shed light on language acquisition in infants.
Courses in developmental psychology are required for anyone training to be a school counselor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: child psychology - study of children's mental development

Etymology

From 'developmental' (relating to development) and 'psychology', originally from Greek 'psyche' (soul, mind) and 'logia' (study of).
