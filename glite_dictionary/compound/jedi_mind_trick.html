Jedi mind trick

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɛdaɪ maɪnd tɹɪk / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛdaɪ maɪnd tɹɪk / (BrE)
a clever or subtle method of persuasion, often implying manipulation or psychological influence
manipulative persuasion technique

Examples

He used a Jedi mind trick to convince the committee to approve his idea.
The negotiator pulled off a Jedi mind trick to calm everyone down and reach an agreement.
Don't try your Jedi mind tricks on me; I know exactly what you're up to.

Synset Examples

He used a Jedi mind trick to convince her.
With a Jedi mind trick, she got what she wanted.
The salesman tried a Jedi mind trick to close the deal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: manipulation - controlling or influencing someone cleverly or unfairly
  • Antonym: coercion - the action of persuading someone by using force or threats

Etymology

From the fictional 'Jedi mind trick' in Star Wars, where Jedi characters use the Force to control others' thoughts.
