orthostatic hypotension

1. NOUN

/ ˌɔrθəˈstætɪk haɪpoʊˈtɛnʃən / (AmE), / ˌɔːθəˈstætɪk haɪpəʊˈtɛnʃən / (BrE)
A medical condition in which blood pressure drops significantly when a person stands up from sitting or lying down, often causing dizziness or faintness.
sudden drop in blood pressure upon standing
medical

Examples

After surgery, some patients experience orthostatic hypotension when first trying to stand up.
Drinking more fluids may help reduce episodes of orthostatic hypotension.
The elderly are particularly at risk for orthostatic hypotension when they get out of bed quickly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hypotension - abnormally low blood pressure
  • Antonym: hypertension - abnormally high blood pressure

Etymology

From Greek 'orthos' meaning straight or upright and 'stasis' meaning standing still; combined with 'hypotension' from hypo- (under) and tension (pressure).
