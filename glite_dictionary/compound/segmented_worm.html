segmented worm

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛɡ.mən.tɪd wɜrm / (AmE), / ˈsɛɡ.məntɪd wɜːm / (BrE)
a worm with a cylindrical body that is divided into a series of ring-like segments both inside and out, such as an earthworm or leech
worm with a segmented cylindrical body

Examples

The earthworm is a common type of segmented worm found in gardens and farms.
Leeches are also classified as segmented worms because of their ring-like body divisions.
Scientists study segmented worms to learn about regeneration and muscle movement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: annelid - any worm of the phylum Annelida, including earthworms and leeches
