columbite-tantalite

1. NOUN

/ kəˈlʌmbaɪt ˈtæntəˌlaɪt / (AmE), / kəˈlʌmbaɪt ˈtæntəlaɪt / (BrE)
A black mineral that is a combination of columbite and tantalite, used primarily as a source of niobium and tantalum in electronic devices.
mineral combining niobite and tantalite

Examples

Mining for columbite-tantalite has dramatically increased in Central Africa due to rising demand for electronics.
The extraction of columbite-tantalite can have serious environmental and social impacts.
Smartphones and laptops often contain small amounts of columbite-tantalite in their circuitry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coltan - another name for columbite-tantalite

Etymology

Named from its constituent minerals 'columbite' (from Columbia, an early name for America) and 'tantalite' (from Tantalus, a figure in Greek mythology, due to metal's insolubility).
