black poplar

1. NOUN

/ blæk ˈpɑplɚ / (AmE), / blæk ˈpɒplə / (BrE)
a large species of poplar tree native to Europe, with deeply furrowed bark and dark-colored wood
a large European poplar tree

Examples

The black poplar is increasingly rare in the wild due to habitat loss.
Old black poplar trees lined the banks of the slow-moving river.
In spring, the black poplar produces fluffy seeds that drift on the breeze.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: poplar - tree of the genus Populus, with soft wood and long catkins
