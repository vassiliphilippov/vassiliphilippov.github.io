Seckel pear

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛkəl pɛr / (AmE), / ˈsɛkəl pɛə / (BrE)
A small, sweet variety of pear with yellowish to reddish-brown skin.
small, sweet variety of pear

Examples

The Seckel pear is prized for its exceptionally sweet flavor and crisp texture.
She brought a basket of Seckel pears for the picnic, knowing everyone enjoyed their taste.
Unlike larger varieties, the Seckel pear is perfect for snacking or adding to salads.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pear - an edible fruit with a rounded base, narrowing towards the stalk

Etymology

Named after the Seckel family of Pennsylvania, where this variety was first discovered in the early 19th century.
