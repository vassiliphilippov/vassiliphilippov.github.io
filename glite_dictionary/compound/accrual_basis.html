accrual basis

1. NOUN

/ əˈkru əl ˈbeɪ sɪs / (AmE), / əˈkruːəl ˈbeɪsɪs / (BrE)
an accounting method where revenues and expenses are recorded when they are earned or incurred, not when cash is received or paid
method of accounting by earnings/incurrences, not cash flow

Examples

Most large companies use the accrual basis of accounting for more accurate tracking of financial transactions.
Under the accrual basis, expenses are recorded when they are incurred, not when they are paid.
Switching from a cash basis to an accrual basis can significantly affect reported earnings.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cash basis - accounting method where transactions are recorded when cash is exchanged

Etymology

From 'accrual', from accrue (from Old French 'accruer'), plus 'basis'.
