Job's tears

1. NOUN

/ dʒɑbz tɪrz / (AmE), / dʒɒbz tɪəz / (BrE)
the hard, shiny seeds of an Asian grass, often used as beads or in jewelry, and sometimes as food
hard seeds of an Asian grass, used as beads

Examples

Her necklace was strung with Job's tears collected during her travels in Asia.
In some cultures, people use Job's tears as natural beads for making jewelry.
The shop sold artisanal bracelets made from polished Job's tears.

Etymology

Named after the biblical figure Job, alluding to the tear-shaped seeds.
