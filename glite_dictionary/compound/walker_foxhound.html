Walker foxhound

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔkər ˈfɑksˌhaʊnd / (AmE), / ˈwɔːkə ˈfɒksˌhaʊnd / (BrE)
a breed of American foxhound developed for fox hunting, known for its speed, endurance, and keen sense of smell
American breed of foxhound
american

Examples

The Walker foxhound is prized for its stamina and keen hunting abilities.
At the dog show, several Walker foxhounds competed for the top prize in their category.
He adopted a young Walker foxhound from a local animal shelter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foxhound - a breed of dog bred for fox hunting

Etymology

Named after the Walker family of Kentucky, credited with developing the breed in the 19th century.
