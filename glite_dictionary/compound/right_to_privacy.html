right to privacy

1. NOUN

/ raɪt tə ˈpraɪvəsi / (AmE), / raɪt tə ˈprɪvəsi / (BrE)
the fundamental right to be free from unwanted or unauthorized intrusion into one's personal life or affairs
freedom from unauthorized intrusion
legal

Examples

Every citizen has a right to privacy that should be respected by the government.
Many court cases have established the right to privacy as a fundamental human right.
Debates continue about the right to privacy in the age of social media and surveillance technology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: privacy - the state of being free from public attention or unsanctioned intrusion
  • Antonym: right to publicity - the right to control the commercial use of one's identity
