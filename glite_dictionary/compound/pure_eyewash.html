pure eyewash

1. NOUN

/ pjʊr ˈaɪˌwɑʃ / (AmE), / pjʊə ˈaɪˌwɒʃ / (BrE)
something said or presented to deceive or mislead people, especially to make things seem better than they are
deceptive or misleading talk or presentation

Examples

The politician's promises turned out to be pure eyewash and were quickly forgotten.
Their claim of transparency was dismissed as pure eyewash by the investigators.
Most experts agreed that the reform was pure eyewash with no real effect on the system.

Synset Examples

The company's sustainability report was pure eyewash.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eyewash - deceptive talk or nonsense
  • Antonym: truth - something that is true or accurate

Etymology

From the literal sense of 'eyewash' (a liquid for cleansing the eyes), figuratively meaning 'deceptive talk'; 'pure' intensifies the sense.
