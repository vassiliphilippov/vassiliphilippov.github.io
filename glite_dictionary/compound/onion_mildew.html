onion mildew

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌnjən ˈmɪldu / (AmE), / ˈʌnjən ˈmɪldjuː / (BrE)
a plant disease of onions caused by a fungus, resulting in a downy mildew appearance on leaves and stems
fungal disease affecting onions

Examples

The crop was severely damaged by onion mildew last year, resulting in significant yield loss.
Gardeners are advised to rotate crops to prevent the spread of onion mildew.
Early detection of onion mildew can help minimize its impact on the harvest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: downy mildew - a group of plant diseases caused by water moulds, producing downy growth on leaves
