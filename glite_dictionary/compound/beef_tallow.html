beef tallow

1. NOUN

/ bif ˈtæloʊ / (AmE), / biːf ˈtaləʊ / (BrE)
the rendered fat obtained from cattle, used in cooking, soap making, and other products
rendered fat from cattle

Examples

Traditional recipes sometimes call for beef tallow instead of vegetable oils.
Crispy fries cooked in beef tallow have a distinctive flavor.
Artisans historically used beef tallow in candle making.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lard - rendered fat from pigs
