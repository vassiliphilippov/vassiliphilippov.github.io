revolving fund

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈvɑlvɪŋ fʌnd / (AmE), / rɪˈvɒlvɪŋ fʌnd / (BrE)
a fund from which money is withdrawn for specific purposes and regularly replenished, so that it can be used repeatedly
fund reused and replenished for ongoing purposes

Examples

The company established a revolving fund to support ongoing research and development projects.
The school's revolving fund allows it to make small loans to students and staff when needed.
Government agencies often use a revolving fund to finance projects that require continuous investment.
