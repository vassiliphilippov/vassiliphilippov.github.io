drum majorette

1. NOUN

/ drʌm ˌmeɪdʒəˈrɛt / (AmE), / drʌm ˌmɑːdʒəˈrɛt / (BrE)
a female performer who leads or accompanies a marching band, typically twirling a baton and sometimes performing dance routines
female baton twirler with a marching band

Examples

As the parade passed by, the drum majorette twirled her baton in perfect sync with the band.
The drum majorette dazzled the audience with her coordinated movements and sparkling uniform.
When she was in high school, she dreamed of becoming a drum majorette in the town parade.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drum major - the leader of a marching band who directs the band and performance

Etymology

From 'drum major' (the leader of a marching band) with feminine diminutive suffix '-ette'.

2. NOUN

/ drʌm ˌmeɪdʒəˈrɛt / (AmE), / drʌm ˌmædʒəˈrɛt / (BrE)
a female leader of a marching band or parade who conducts the band and often performs twirling routines with a baton
female marching band leader

Examples

As the drum majorette led the parade down Main Street, the crowd cheered and clapped.
She dreamed of becoming the head drum majorette for her high school marching band.
The drum majorette dazzled everyone with her baton twirling and precise marching.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drum major - the leader of a marching band or parade who directs the band
