visually impaired person

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪʒuəli ɪmˈpɛrd ˈpɜrsən / (AmE), / ˈvɪʒuəli ɪmˈpeəd ˈpɜːs(ə)n / (BrE)
a person who has reduced or limited vision, either partially sighted or blind
person with reduced or limited vision

Examples

Accessible technology can greatly improve the lives of a visually impaired person.
The museum offers audio guides for every visually impaired person visiting the exhibit.
A visually impaired person may use a cane or a guide dog for assistance.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: blind person - a person who cannot see at all
  • Antonym: sighted person - a person who has normal vision
