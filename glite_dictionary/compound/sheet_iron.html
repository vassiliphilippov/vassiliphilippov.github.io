sheet iron

1. NOUN

/ ʃit ˈaɪərn / (AmE), / ʃiːt ˈaɪən / (BrE)
iron rolled or pressed into thin flat sheets, used especially in construction, manufacturing, and metalworking.
iron in thin flat sheets

Examples

The roof was covered with sheet iron to protect the building from heavy rain.
They used sheet iron panels in the construction of the storage shed.
The blacksmith hammered the sheet iron into the desired shape for the project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wrought iron - iron with a low carbon content, tough and malleable

/ ʃit ˈaɪɚn / (AmE), / ʃiːt ˈaɪən / (BrE)
Iron that has been rolled or hammered into thin, flat sheets, used in construction and manufacturing.
thin, flat iron used in construction

Examples

The warehouse stored several rolls of sheet iron for industrial projects.
He repaired the fence with a panel of sheet iron to make it sturdier.
Heat caused the sheet iron to expand slightly during the day.
