mathematical notation

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæθəˈmætɪkəl noʊˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌmæθəˈmætɪkəl nəʊˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the system of symbols, signs, and conventions used to represent mathematical concepts, operations, and relationships
system of writing mathematics with symbols

Examples

Learning mathematical notation is essential for understanding advanced algebra and calculus.
Isaac Newton helped develop much of the mathematical notation we use today.
Different branches of mathematics sometimes use slightly different mathematical notation for similar concepts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: notation - a system of symbols used to represent special things
