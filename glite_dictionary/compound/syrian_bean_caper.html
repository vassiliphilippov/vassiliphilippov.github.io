Syrian bean caper

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪriən ˈbiːn ˈkeɪpər / (AmE), / ˈsɪəriən ˈbiːn ˈkeɪpə / (BrE)
A perennial shrub (Zygophyllum fabago) native to the eastern Mediterranean and southwestern Asia, whose flower buds are used as capers in cooking.
perennial shrub, buds used as capers

Examples

The pickled buds of the Syrian bean caper are a popular ingredient in Mediterranean dishes.
A mature Syrian bean caper can grow up to a meter in height.
Chefs often use Syrian bean caper as a substitute for traditional capers in recipes.
