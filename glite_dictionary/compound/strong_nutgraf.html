strong nutgraf

1. NOUN

/ strɔŋ ˈnʌtˌɡræf / (AmE), / strɒŋ ˈnʌtˌɡræf / (BrE)
A concise and well-constructed paragraph in a news story that summarizes its essential point or significance.
effective summary paragraph in journalism

Examples

The editor praised her strong nutgraf for clearly stating the article's main point within the opening paragraphs.
Readers are more likely to stay engaged if the story includes a strong nutgraf early on.
Crafting a strong nutgraf can set a compelling tone for the entire piece.

Synset Examples

The journalist crafted a strong nutgraf to hook readers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nutgraf - summary paragraph in a news article

Etymology

From 'nut' (core or essence) and 'graf' (short for 'paragraph'), both journalism slang; 'strong' as intensifier.
