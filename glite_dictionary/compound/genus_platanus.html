genus Platanus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs pləˈteɪnəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs pləˈtɑːnəs / (BrE)
a genus of large, mostly deciduous, monoecious trees that includes the London plane and sycamore
tree genus including plane and sycamore

Examples

Several species within the genus Platanus are widely planted in urban areas for their tolerance of pollution.
The genus Platanus is easily identified by its distinctive peeling bark and maple-like leaves.
Botanists recently discovered a new member of the genus Platanus in a remote forest region.

Etymology

From Latin 'Platanus', itself from Greek 'platanos' (plane tree).
