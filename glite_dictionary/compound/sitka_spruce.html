Sitka spruce

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪtkə sprus / (AmE), / ˈsɪtkə spruːs / (BrE)
a large coniferous tree native to the northwestern coast of North America, known for its sharp needles, thin bark, and lightweight yet strong wood
a North American coniferous tree

Examples

The Sitka spruce is prized by instrument makers for its resonant wood.
Many of the oldest trees in the forest are Sitka spruces reaching over 60 meters tall.
Scientists study the Sitka spruce to understand how coastal forests respond to climate change.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spruce - a type of evergreen coniferous tree with needle-like leaves

Etymology

Named after Sitka, Alaska, where the species is abundant; 'spruce' from Middle Low German 'sprūse'.
