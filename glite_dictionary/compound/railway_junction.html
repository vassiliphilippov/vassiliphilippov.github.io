railway junction

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪlˌweɪ ˈdʒʌŋkʃən / (AmE), / ˈreɪlweɪ ˈdʒʌŋkʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
A place where two or more railway lines meet or cross.
a place where railway lines meet or cross

Examples

The train changed direction at the railway junction, heading toward the northern line.
A new railway junction was constructed to ease congestion on the main route.
Engineers spent weeks planning upgrades to the old railway junction near the city center.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: railway station - a place where trains regularly stop so that passengers can get on and off
