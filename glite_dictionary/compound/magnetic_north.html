magnetic north

1. NOUN

/ məgˈnɛtɪk nɔrθ / (AmE), / mæɡˌnɛtɪk ˈnɔːθ / (BrE)
the direction towards the Earth's north magnetic pole, indicated by the north-seeking end of a compass needle
direction indicated by a compass needle

Examples

If you follow a compass, you will be heading towards magnetic north, not true north.
Pilots have to account for the difference between magnetic north and true north when navigating.
Maps often show both magnetic north and true north for accurate orientation.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: true north - the direction along the Earth's surface towards the geographic North Pole
