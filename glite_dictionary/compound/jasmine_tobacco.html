jasmine tobacco

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒæz.mɪn təˈbækoʊ / (AmE), / ˈdʒæz.mɪn təˈbækəʊ / (BrE)
A South American perennial ornamental plant (Nicotiana alata) with fragrant greenish-white flowers that bloom at night.
ornamental plant with fragrant night flowers

Examples

The garden was filled with the scent of jasmine tobacco on summer evenings.
She planted jasmine tobacco along the pathway for its night-blooming fragrance.
Jasmine tobacco attracts moths with its sweet-smelling flowers after dusk.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Nicotiana alata - the botanical name for jasmine tobacco

Etymology

Named for its jasmine-like fragrance; 'tobacco' from the genus Nicotiana.
