three-banded armadillo

1. NOUN

/ θri ˈbændɪd ˌɑːrməˈdɪloʊ / (AmE), / θriː ˈbændɪd ˌɑːmədɪləʊ / (BrE)
A small South American armadillo species distinguished by three movable bands of bony plates across its back.
South American armadillo with three bands

Examples

The three-banded armadillo can curl itself into a tight ball to protect against predators.
Researchers found a three-banded armadillo in the grasslands of Brazil during their field study.
Unlike other species, the three-banded armadillo is able to completely enclose itself using its armored shell.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: armadillo - any of various burrowing mammals with a leathery armor shell
