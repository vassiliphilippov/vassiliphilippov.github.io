penny ante

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛni ˌænti / (AmE), / ˈpɛni ˌænti / (BrE)
A game of poker played for very small stakes; more broadly, any undertaking or activity involving trivial sums or consequences.
poker for small stakes; petty activity

Examples

The group gathered for a friendly game of penny ante on Friday nights.
She dismissed their criticisms as just penny ante concerns not worth worrying about.
We played penny ante poker because none of us wanted to lose much money.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trivial - Of little value or importance

Etymology

Refers to poker games where the ante is just a penny; 'ante' from Latin 'ante' meaning 'before.'

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɛni ˌænti / (AmE), / ˈpɛni ˌænti / (BrE)
A business deal or activity that is considered very small or insignificant in scale or value.
trivial or insignificant business deal

Examples

Many critics dismissed the project as just a penny ante operation without any real potential.
He refused to get involved in such penny ante transactions and aimed for larger opportunities.
The company's earlier ventures were penny ante compared to its latest big acquisition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trivial - of little value or importance

Etymology

Originally from poker, where 'penny ante' referred to games with very small stakes.
