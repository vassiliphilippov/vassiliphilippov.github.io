Kolmogorov's strong law of large numbers

1. NOUN

/ koʊlˈmoʊɡərɒvz strɔːŋ lɔː əv lɑːrdʒ ˈnʌmbərz / (AmE), / kɒlˈmɒɡərɒvz strɒŋ lɔː əv lɑːdʒ ˈnʌmbəz / (BrE)
a fundamental theorem in probability theory stating that, under certain conditions, the sample averages of independent random variables converge almost surely to the expected value as the number of samples increases indefinitely
probability theorem on sample average convergence

Examples

The professor explained Kolmogorov's strong law of large numbers during the advanced probability lecture.
According to Kolmogorov's strong law of large numbers, the sample mean will almost surely converge to the expected value.
Many statistical simulations rely on Kolmogorov's strong law of large numbers to ensure accuracy as the number of trials increases.

Synset Examples

In this paper, Kolmogorov's strong law of large numbers for sums of independent and level-wise identically distributed fuzzy random variables is obtained.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: law of large numbers - theorem stating that the average of results obtained from a large number of trials converges to the expected value
  • Degree Lower: weak law of large numbers - theorem stating convergence in probability, a weaker form of the law of large numbers

Etymology

Named after Russian mathematician Andrey Kolmogorov (1903–1987), who formalized the law in modern probability theory.
