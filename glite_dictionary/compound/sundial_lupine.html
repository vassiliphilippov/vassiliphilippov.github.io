sundial lupine

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌnˌdaɪl ˈluːpɪn / (AmE), / ˈsʌndaɪl ˈluːpɪn / (BrE)
a stout perennial plant native to eastern and central North America, with palmate leaves and showy blue, racemose flowers
blue-flowered North American lupine

Examples

The sundial lupine flourishes in sandy soils and attracts a variety of pollinators each spring.
Wildlife preserves are working to restore sundial lupine populations for endangered butterflies.
Gardeners often plant sundial lupine to bring vibrant blue color to native wildflower beds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lupine - any plant of the genus Lupinus, known for compound leaves and tall, colorful flower spikes
