Doug fir

1. NOUN

/ dʌɡ fɪr / (AmE), / dʌɡ fɜː / (BrE)
A common name for the Douglas fir, a large coniferous evergreen tree valued for its strong wood and often used as a Christmas tree.
type of evergreen coniferous tree

Examples

The Doug fir is known for its strong, durable wood.
We decorated a Doug fir for Christmas this year instead of a pine tree.
Several Doug firs grow along the edge of the property, providing year-round greenery.

Synset Examples

The Doug fir is known for its sturdiness.
They planted a Doug fir in the backyard.
We chose a Doug fir for our Christmas tree.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Douglas fir - another common name for the Doug fir tree

Etymology

Shortened form of 'Douglas fir,' named after David Douglas, a Scottish botanist who introduced the species to Europe in the 19th century.
