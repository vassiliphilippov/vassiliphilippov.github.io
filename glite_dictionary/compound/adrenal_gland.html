adrenal gland

1. NOUN

/ əˈdriːnəl ɡlænd / (AmE), / əˈdriːnəl ɡlænd / (BrE)
Either of a pair of endocrine glands located above the kidneys, which produce hormones such as adrenaline and cortisol.
endocrine gland above the kidney
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that the adrenal gland produces important hormones for the body.
A tumor in the adrenal gland can cause serious health issues if not treated.
The adrenal gland sits just above each kidney and helps regulate metabolism and stress responses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suprarenal gland - another term for the adrenal gland

Etymology

From Latin 'ad-' (near) + 'renes' (kidneys).
