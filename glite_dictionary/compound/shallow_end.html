shallow end

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃæloʊ ɛnd / (AmE), / ˈʃaləʊ end / (BrE)
the part of a swimming pool where the water is not deep, typically used by beginners or children
part of a pool with little depth

Examples

The kids are playing in the shallow end of the pool.
He prefers to stay in the shallow end because he isn't a confident swimmer.
Swimming lessons usually begin in the shallow end for safety reasons.

Synset Examples

The kids are playing in the shallow end of the pool.
I prefer the shallow end for a relaxing swim.
She taught her daughter to swim in the shallow end first.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: deep end - the part of a pool where the water is deepest
