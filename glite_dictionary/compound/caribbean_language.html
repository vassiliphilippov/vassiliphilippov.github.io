Caribbean language

1. NOUN

/ kəˈrɪbiən ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / kəˈrɪbiən ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
a family of indigenous languages spoken by the Carib people of South America and the Caribbean region.
a family of Carib indigenous languages

Examples

Linguists have studied the structure of the Caribbean language to understand its evolution.
Many words in English are derived from Caribbean languages.
The survival of the Caribbean language is important for cultural preservation.
