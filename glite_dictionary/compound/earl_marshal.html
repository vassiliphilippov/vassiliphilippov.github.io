Earl Marshal

1. NOUN

/ ɜrl ˈmɑrʃəl / (AmE), / ɜːl ˈmɑːʃəl / (BrE)
an officer in the British peerage responsible for organizing royal ceremonies and state processions
officer organizing royal ceremonies (UK)
british

Examples

The Earl Marshal played a central role in organizing the coronation of the new monarch.
As Earl Marshal, he oversaw the arrangements for royal funerals and state occasions.
The tradition of the Earl Marshal dates back to the Middle Ages in England.

Etymology

From Middle English, combining 'earl' (a noble rank derived from Old English 'eorl') and 'marshal' (from Old French 'mareschal', meaning 'horse servant' or 'commander of the cavalry').
