deep slumber

1. NOUN

/ dip ˈslʌmbɚ / (AmE), / diːp ˈslʌmbə / (BrE)
A state of very sound or profound sleep from which it is difficult to be awakened.
profound sleep

Examples

After the exhausting hike, she drifted into a deep slumber and didn’t wake until morning.
The gentle rain lulled him into a deep slumber that lasted all night.
Even the loud thunder couldn’t break his deep slumber.

Synset Examples

He fell into a deep slumber after the long day.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: light sleep - a state of sleep that is not deep or easily disturbed
