endocrine system

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛndəˌkrɪn ˈsɪstəm / (AmE), / ˈɛndəkrɪn ˈsɪstəm / (BrE)
the collection of glands in the body that produce hormones to regulate metabolism, growth, reproduction, and other bodily functions
hormone-producing bodily gland system
medical

Examples

The endocrine system regulates vital processes such as metabolism, growth, and reproduction.
A disorder in the endocrine system can affect many organs throughout the body.
Doctors study the endocrine system to understand how hormones influence health.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: nervous system - the network of nerve cells and fibers that transmits nerve impulses between parts of the body
  • Antonym: exocrine system - body system consisting of glands that secrete externally (not into the bloodstream)

Etymology

From Greek 'endon' (within) + 'krinein' (to separate), reflecting internal secretion.
