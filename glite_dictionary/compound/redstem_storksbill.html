redstem storksbill

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛd.stɛm ˈstɔrks.bɪl / (AmE), / ˈrɛd.stɛm ˈstɔːks.bɪl / (BrE)
A European weed naturalized in southwestern United States and Mexico; it has reddish, decumbent stems, fernlike leaves, deep reddish-lavender flowers, and slender upright fruits; sometimes grown as forage.
a weed with reddish stems and reddish-lavender flowers

Examples

The redstem storksbill often carpets fields in the spring with its reddish blooms.
Farmers sometimes plant redstem storksbill as forage for livestock in arid regions.
Wildlife such as rabbits and birds feed on the seeds of the redstem storksbill.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: storksbill - any of various plants of the genus Erodium with long-beaked fruit
