Devonshire cream

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛvənʃɪr kriːm / (AmE), / ˈdɛvənʃə kriːm / (BrE)
A thick, rich cream made by slowly heating unpasteurized milk and then cooling it, causing the cream to rise to the surface. It is a specialty of the English county of Devon.
thick, rich cream from Devon, England
british

Examples

The scones were served with Devonshire cream and strawberry jam for afternoon tea.
She spread a generous layer of Devonshire cream on her freshly baked bread.
Many tourists in England make a point to try Devonshire cream during their visit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clotted cream - thick cream made by heating milk and skimming off the surface; another name for Devonshire cream

Etymology

Named after Devonshire (Devon), the county in England where it originated.
