cognovit judgement

1. NOUN

/ kɑːgˈnoʊvɪt dʒəˈdʒʌdmənt / (AmE), / kɒgˈnəʊvɪt dʒʌdʒmənt / (BrE)
(Legal) A judgment entered after a debtor has signed a written confession of debt, allowing judgment without the usual legal proceedings.
judgment after written confession by debtor
legal

Examples

The bank obtained a cognovit judgement after the debtor signed a confession of liability.
A cognovit judgement expedites the legal process since no trial is necessary.
Because of the cognovit judgement, the creditor was able to recover the debt quickly.

Etymology

From Latin 'cognovit actionem' (he has confessed the action).
