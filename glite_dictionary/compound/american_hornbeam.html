American hornbeam

1. NOUN

/ əˈmɛrɪkən ˈhɔːrnˌbiːm / (AmE), / əˈmɛrɪkən ˈhɔːnbˌiːm / (BrE)
a North American tree or large shrub (Carpinus caroliniana) with smooth grey bark and blue-green leaves that turn red-orange in autumn
North American tree with smooth grey bark and colorful autumn leaves

Examples

The American hornbeam is prized for its vibrant autumn foliage in many gardens across the country.
We planted an American hornbeam near the back fence for extra shade.
The smooth, muscular bark of the American hornbeam sets it apart from other native trees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hornbeam - a tree of the genus Carpinus with hard wood
