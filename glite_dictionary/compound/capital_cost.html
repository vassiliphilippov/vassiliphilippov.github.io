capital cost

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæpɪtl kɔst / (AmE), / ˈkæpɪtl kɒst / (BrE)
the cost of funds used for financing a business, typically measured as the rate of return that could be earned in an alternative investment with equivalent risk
cost of funds for business investment

Examples

The company carefully evaluated the capital cost before approving the new factory project.
Investors consider the capital cost to determine whether a proposed venture is viable.
A higher capital cost can discourage businesses from expanding into new markets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: opportunity cost - the loss of potential gain from other alternatives when one alternative is chosen
