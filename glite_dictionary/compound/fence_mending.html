fence mending

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɛns ˌmɛndɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfɛns ˌmɛndɪŋ / (BrE)
the activity or process of improving damaged or poor relationships, especially between people or groups in politics
improving poor relations, especially in politics

Examples

After months of disagreements, there was finally some fence mending between the two leaders.
The new CEO made fence mending a top priority to restore trust in the company.
Diplomats engaged in fence mending talks to ease tensions between the neighboring countries.

Synset Examples

they moved forward from a period of fence mending to substantive changes in the country

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reconciliation - restoring friendly relations
  • Antonym: alienation - being isolated or estranged, opposite of reconciliation

Etymology

From the literal act of repairing a fence, transferred metaphorically to repairing relationships.
