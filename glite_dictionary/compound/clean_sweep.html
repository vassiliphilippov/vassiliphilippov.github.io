clean sweep

1. NOUN

/ klin swip / (AmE), / kliːn swiːp / (BrE)
a situation in which someone wins all the prizes, positions, or victories available, or when a thorough or complete change is made
winning everything or making a complete change

Examples

The team achieved a clean sweep by winning every match in the tournament.
After the election, the party made a clean sweep of all the council seats.
The new management promised a clean sweep of outdated policies and procedures.

Synset Examples

The team made a clean sweep of the awards this year.
After the renovation, the place felt like a clean sweep.
They achieved a clean sweep in the local elections.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: triumph - a great victory or achievement
  • Antonym: defeat - to win against or overcome someone or something
