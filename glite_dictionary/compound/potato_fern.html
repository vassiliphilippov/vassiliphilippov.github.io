potato fern

1. NOUN

/ pəˈteɪɾoʊ fɝn / (AmE), / pəˈteɪtəʊ fɜːn / (BrE)
a large Australasian evergreen fern with an edible rhizome, sometimes used as a vegetable by indigenous people
Australasian fern with edible rhizome

Examples

The potato fern is valued for its edible roots by Aboriginal Australians.
In some regions, the rhizomes of the potato fern are cooked as a traditional food.
Botanists are studying how the potato fern adapts to different forest environments.

2. NOUN

/ pəˈteɪtoʊ fɜrn / (AmE), / pəˈteɪtəʊ fɜːn / (BrE)
a small epiphytic fern native to South America, characterized by tuberous swellings along its rhizomes
South American epiphytic fern with tuberous rhizomes

Examples

The potato fern is commonly found growing on tree branches in South American rainforests.
Gardeners prize the potato fern for its unique tuberous rhizomes, which resemble small potatoes.
Researchers studied the potato fern to understand how its tubers help it survive in challenging environments.
