miasma of tension

1. NOUN

/ maɪˈæzmə əv ˈtɛnʃən / (AmE), / maɪˈæzmə əv ˈtɛnʃən / (BrE)
a pervasive or oppressive atmosphere filled with anxiety or stress
anxious or stressful atmosphere

Examples

She walked into the meeting and immediately sensed the miasma of tension hanging in the air.
The miasma of tension before the exam was almost unbearable for the students.
A miasma of tension settled over the family after the news was announced.

Synset Examples

He felt the miasma of tension in the room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: atmosphere - the mood or feeling in a particular place
  • Synonym: stress - a state of mental or emotional strain

Etymology

From 'miasma', from Greek 'miasma' (pollution), combined with 'tension'.
