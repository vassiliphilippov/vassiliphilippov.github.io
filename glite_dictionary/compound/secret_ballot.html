secret ballot

1. NOUN

/ ˈsiː.krət ˈbæl.ət / (AmE), / ˈsiː.krɪt ˈbæl.ət / (BrE)
a voting method in which each voter's choices are confidential, but the overall results are made public
voting method with confidential choices

Examples

All citizens cast their votes using a secret ballot to ensure privacy.
The union election was conducted by secret ballot to protect members from intimidation.
The introduction of the secret ballot was a major step forward for democratic elections.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: open ballot - a voting method in which votes are cast publicly
