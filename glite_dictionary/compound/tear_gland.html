tear gland

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɪr ˌɡlænd / (AmE), / ˈtɪə ˌɡlænd / (BrE)
a gland located in the eye that produces and secretes tears
a gland in the eye that produces tears

Examples

A blocked tear gland can cause excessive watering of the eye.
The doctor explained that his tear glands were not producing enough fluid.
Damage to a tear gland may result in dry eye syndrome.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lacrimal gland - the anatomical name for the tear gland
