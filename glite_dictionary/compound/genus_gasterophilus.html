genus Gasterophilus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˌgæstəˈrɑfɪləs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˌɡastəˈrɒfɪləs / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Gasterophilidae, comprising the horse botflies, which are parasitic insects that lay eggs on horses.
type genus of horse botflies

Examples

The genus Gasterophilus includes several species that affect horses worldwide.
Researchers studied the life cycle of insects in the genus Gasterophilus.
Infestation by members of the genus Gasterophilus can cause health issues in horses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: botfly - a type of fly whose larvae are parasitic

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Latin 'gaster' (stomach) and Greek 'philos' (loving), referring to larvae that develop in the stomachs of horses.
