white-telephone comedy

1. NOUN

/ ˈwaɪt təˈlɛfəˌnoʊ ˈkɑːmədi / (AmE), / ˈwaɪt tɪˈlɛfəˌni ˈkɒmədi / (BrE)
a genre of Italian film, usually from the 1930s and 1940s, featuring lighthearted, elegant settings often symbolized by white telephones, focusing on the romantic and comedic lives of the upper class
Italian film genre with elegant, comic themes

Examples

Many critics consider the white-telephone comedy to represent escapism during a turbulent period in Italy.
The museum is hosting a retrospective on classic white-telephone comedy films from the prewar era.
Her film collection includes several masterpieces from the white-telephone comedy tradition.

Synset Examples

He joked about the white-telephone comedy that made everyone laugh.

Etymology

Refers to the white telephones that appeared as status symbols in upper-class settings of 1930s Italian films.
