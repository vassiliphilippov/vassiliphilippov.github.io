broad-leaved plantain

1. NOUN

/ ˌbrɔd livd ˈplæntən / (AmE), / ˌbrɔːd ˌliːvd ˈplæntɪn / (BrE)
A North American plantain species characterized by broad leaves and reddish leafstalks.
North American plantain with broad leaves

Examples

The broad-leaved plantain is commonly found in meadows and along roadsides.
Gardeners often remove broad-leaved plantain to prevent it from spreading in lawns.
The reddish leafstalks distinguish the broad-leaved plantain from other species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plantain - a plant of the genus Plantago

2. NOUN

/ ˌbrɔd lid ˈplæn.tɪn / (AmE), / ˌbrɔːd liːvd ˈplæn.tɪn / (BrE)
a European perennial plant (Plantago major) with broad leaves, widely naturalized and often considered a troublesome weed in lawns and gardens
common weed; Plantago major

Examples

The broad-leaved plantain is often found growing in compacted soil along footpaths.
Gardeners struggle to control broad-leaved plantain because it spreads quickly in lawns.
She learned that broad-leaved plantain leaves were once used as a traditional herbal remedy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plantain - any plant of the Plantago genus, especially the common plantain or its relatives
