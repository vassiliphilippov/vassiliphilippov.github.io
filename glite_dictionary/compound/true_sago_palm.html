true sago palm

1. NOUN

/ tru ˈseɪɡoʊ pɑm / (AmE), / truː ˈseɪɡəʊ pɑːm / (BrE)
a species of palm (Metroxylon sagu) native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific, whose trunk yields sago starch used as a food thickener and fabric stiffener
palm tree yielding sago starch

Examples

The true sago palm is widely cultivated in parts of Malaysia for its starch-rich trunk.
Farmers harvest sago from the true sago palm to produce cooking starch and other products.
The true sago palm thrives in swampy areas from Malaya to Fiji.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sago palm - any palm species that yields sago starch
