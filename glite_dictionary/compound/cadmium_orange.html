cadmium orange

1. NOUN

/ ˈkædmiəm ˈɔrɪndʒ / (AmE), / ˈkadmɪəm ˈɒrɪndʒ / (BrE)
an orange-colored pigment made from cadmium sulfide and cadmium selenide, used especially by artists
a bright orange pigment used in art

Examples

The artist chose cadmium orange to add a vibrant warmth to the sunset in her painting.
He mixed cadmium orange with a little white to achieve a softer shade for the background.
Tubed cadmium orange is popular among painters for its bright, lasting color.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cadmium yellow - a bright yellow pigment made from cadmium compounds

Etymology

From cadmium, the metal from which the pigment is made, and the color orange.
