two-hitter

1. NOUN

/ ˈtuːˌhɪtɚ / (AmE), / ˈtuːˌhɪtə / (BrE)
(Baseball) A game in which a pitcher allows the opposing team only two hits.
baseball: game allowing only two hits

Examples

The pitcher threw a two-hitter in last night's game, impressing fans and coaches alike.
In the playoffs, her only loss came during a tough two-hitter against a rival team.
It's rare to witness a two-hitter, but today the defense and pitching were nearly perfect.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: no-hitter - a game in which a pitcher allows no hits
  • Degree Higher: one-hitter - a game in which a pitcher allows only one hit
  • Degree Lower: three-hitter - a game in which a pitcher allows only three hits
