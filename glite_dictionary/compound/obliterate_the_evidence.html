to obliterate the evidence

1. VERB

/ əˈblɪtəˌreɪt ði ˈɛvədəns / (AmE), / əˈblɪtəˌreɪt ði ˈɛvɪdəns / (BrE)
to completely destroy or erase proof of something, so that no trace remains
destroy or erase proof entirely

Examples

The corrupt official tried to obliterate the evidence before the investigators arrived.
Hackers managed to obliterate the evidence of the data breach from the servers.
The arsonist hoped the fire would obliterate the evidence of his involvement.

Synset Examples

They aimed to obliterate the evidence of wrongdoing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to cover up - to hide the truth about something, especially a crime or mistake
