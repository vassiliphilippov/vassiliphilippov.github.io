arsenous oxide

1. NOUN

/ ɑːrˈsiːnəs ˈɑːksaɪd / (AmE), / ɑːˈsiːnəs ˈɒksaɪd / (BrE)
a poisonous white powdery compound of arsenic (As2O3), used in the manufacture of glass and as a pesticide and weed killer
poisonous compound of arsenic (As2O3)

Examples

Laboratory tests revealed the presence of arsenous oxide in the contaminated water sample.
Glass manufacturers once used arsenous oxide to decolorize glass during production.
Exposure to arsenous oxide can pose serious health risks if safety precautions are not followed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arsenic trioxide - another name for arsenous oxide, chemical compound As2O3

Etymology

From arsenous (from 'arsenic') + oxide, describing its chemical composition.
