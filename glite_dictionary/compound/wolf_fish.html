wolf fish

1. NOUN

/ wʊlf fɪʃ / (AmE), / wʊlf fɪʃ / (BrE)
a large predatory northern deep-sea fish with strong teeth and no pelvic fins, found in cold waters of the North Atlantic.
large northern predatory deep-sea fish

Examples

The wolf fish is known for its powerful jaws and fearsome appearance.
Fishermen in Iceland often catch wolf fish in the cold northern waters.
The restaurant served grilled wolf fish as a local seafood specialty.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wolffish - alternate spelling of wolf fish, a large predatory fish of northern seas
