Italian monetary unit

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈtæljən ˈmɑnɪˌtɛri ˈjunɪt / (AmE), / ɪˈtæl.jən ˈmɒn.ɪ.tri ˈjuː.nɪt / (BrE)
the basic unit of currency formerly used in Italy, notably the lira before the adoption of the euro
former basic currency of Italy

Examples

The Italian monetary unit was replaced by the euro in 2002.
When traveling to Italy before 2002, tourists needed to exchange their money for the Italian monetary unit.
The value of the Italian monetary unit fluctuated greatly during the 20th century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lira - the former basic monetary unit of Italy
  • Degree Higher: euro - the official currency of most of the European Union, including Italy since 2002
