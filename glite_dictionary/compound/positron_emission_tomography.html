positron emission tomography

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑː.zəˌtrɑːn ɪˈmɪʃ.ən təˈmɑːɡ.rə.fi / (AmE), / ˈpɒ.zɪ.trɒn ɪˈmɪʃ.ən təˈmɒɡ.rə.fi / (BrE)
a medical imaging technique that uses computerized detection of positrons to examine metabolic activity in body tissues, especially the brain
medical imaging technique for metabolic activity
medical

Examples

The doctor ordered a positron emission tomography scan to check for abnormal brain activity.
Researchers used positron emission tomography to study metabolic changes in Alzheimer's disease.
Thanks to positron emission tomography, some tumors can be detected at an earlier stage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: PET scan - common abbreviation for positron emission tomography

Etymology

From 'positron' (a subatomic particle), 'emission' (the act of emitting), and 'tomography' (from Greek 'tomos'—slice, and 'graphein'—to write or record).
