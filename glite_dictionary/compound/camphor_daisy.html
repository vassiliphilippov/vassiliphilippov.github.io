camphor daisy

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæm.fər ˈdeɪ.zi / (AmE), / ˈkam.fə ˈdeɪ.zi / (BrE)
An annual plant native to the southern United States and Mexico, with bristly leaves and pale yellow flowers, known for its aromatic scent.
aromatic annual plant with yellow flowers

Examples

The camphor daisy thrives in warm climates with plenty of sunlight.
The leaves of the camphor daisy have a distinct bristly texture and a strong scent.
Gardeners often appreciate the camphor daisy for its pale yellow blooms and resilience.
