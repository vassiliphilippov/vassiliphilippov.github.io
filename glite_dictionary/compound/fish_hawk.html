fish hawk

1. NOUN

/ fɪʃ hɑk / (AmE), / fɪʃ hɔːk / (BrE)
a large hawk that feeds mainly on fish, especially the osprey, found worldwide and known for its bulky, long-used nests
large hawk that feeds on fish; osprey

Examples

The fish hawk soared above the river, scanning the water for its next meal.
Local bird watchers were excited to spot a rare fish hawk near the lake.
A large nest built by a fish hawk perched high atop the old pine tree.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: osprey - large fish-eating bird of prey
