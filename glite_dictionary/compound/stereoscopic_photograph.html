stereoscopic photograph

1. NOUN

/ ˌstɛrioʊˈskɑpɪk ˈfoʊtəˌgræf / (AmE), / ˌstɪəriəˈskɒpɪk ˈfəʊtəˌgrɑːf / (BrE)
a pair of photographs taken from slightly different angles that, when viewed together, create a three-dimensional effect
3D image pair viewed for depth

Examples

The museum displayed a stereoscopic photograph of the 19th-century city, giving viewers a sense of depth and realism.
He was fascinated by the stereoscopic photograph because it made the mountain landscape appear almost touchable.
Early explorers often documented their findings with a stereoscopic photograph to capture three-dimensional scenes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stereograph - a photograph or image designed for stereoscopic viewing

Etymology

From stereo- (three-dimensional) + Greek skopein (to look) + photograph.
