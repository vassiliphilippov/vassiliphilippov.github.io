naval radar

1. NOUN

/ ˈneɪvəl ˈreɪdɑːr / (AmE), / ˈneɪvəl ˈreɪdɑː / (BrE)
a radar system installed on a naval vessel, used to detect objects and navigate at sea
shipboard radar system for naval vessels

Examples

The warship upgraded its naval radar to improve detection of incoming missiles.
Modern naval radar systems can track multiple targets simultaneously.
During the exercise, the naval radar identified several small vessels approaching the fleet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: radar - a system that uses reflected radio waves to detect objects and measure their distance and speed
