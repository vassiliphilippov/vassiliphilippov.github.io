somatic sensory system

1. NOUN

/ soʊˈmætɪk ˈsɛn.sə.ri ˈsɪs.təm / (AmE), / səˈmatɪk ˈsɛn.səri ˈsɪs.təm / (BrE)
the part of the nervous system responsible for bodily sensations, including touch, proprioception, and senses from internal organs
system for bodily sensory perception
medical

Examples

The somatic sensory system allows us to perceive touch, temperature, and pain throughout the body.
Damage to the somatic sensory system can result in loss of sensation or abnormal bodily perceptions.
Research on the somatic sensory system helps scientists understand how the brain processes physical stimuli.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: proprioception - awareness of the position and movement of the body
  • Degree Lower: visceral sensory system - sensory system relating to internal organs

Etymology

From Greek sōmatikos (of the body), from sōma (body), and Latin sensus (feeling, perception).
