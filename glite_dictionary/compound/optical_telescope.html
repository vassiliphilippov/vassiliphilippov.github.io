optical telescope

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑptɪkəl ˈtɛlɪˌskoʊp / (AmE), / ˈɒptɪkəl ˈtelɪskəʊp / (BrE)
an astronomical instrument that gathers and focuses visible light to observe distant objects
instrument for observing with visible light

Examples

The Hubble Space Telescope is an optical telescope located above Earth's atmosphere.
Amateur astronomers often use an optical telescope to observe planets and stars from their backyard.
Light pollution can make it difficult for an optical telescope to capture faint celestial objects.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: radio telescope - an astronomical telescope designed to detect radio waves from space

Etymology

From 'optical' (relating to sight or vision), from Greek 'optikos', and 'telescope', from Greek 'tēle' (far) + 'skopein' (to look or see).
