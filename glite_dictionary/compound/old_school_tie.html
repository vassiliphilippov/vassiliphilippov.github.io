old school tie

1. NOUN

/ oʊld skuːl taɪ / (AmE), / əʊld skuːl taɪ / (BrE)
A necktie worn to indicate the school the wearer attended, often used as a symbol of social connection or privilege, especially among alumni of prestigious British schools.
necktie showing the school one attended
british

Examples

He wore his old school tie to the alumni dinner.
The exclusive club was filled with men sporting the old school tie.
Wearing an old school tie often signifies belonging to a certain social circle.

Etymology

Refers to traditional neckties in British schools, where colors/patterns denote alma mater.
