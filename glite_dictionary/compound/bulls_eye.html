bull's eye

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʊlz aɪ / (AmE), / ˈbʊlz aɪ / (BrE)
a decisive or perfectly accurate success in achieving an aim or goal; the exact target or center point hit
a perfect hit or decisive success

Examples

Her innovative solution to the problem was a real bull's eye for the team.
The marketing campaign hit a bull's eye with younger consumers.
You scored a bull's eye with that answer during the interview.

Synset Examples

the new advertising campaign was a bell ringer
scored a bull's eye
hit the mark
the president's speech was a home run

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hit the mark - to achieve exactly what was intended
  • Antonym: to miss the mark - to fail to achieve the intended result

Etymology

Originally from archery and shooting sports, referring to the literal center of a target.

2. NOUN

/ bʊlz aɪ / (AmE), / bʊlz aɪ / (BrE)
the small central spot of a target, typically the highest scoring area in archery, darts, and similar activities
center spot of a target

Examples

He hit the bull's eye with his first arrow.
Getting the answer exactly right is like hitting a bull's eye.
The dart landed in the bull's eye, earning her the most points.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: center - the middle point of something
  • Antonym: edge - the outermost part of something, opposite of center

Etymology

Possibly from the resemblance of the central spot to the eye of a bull.
