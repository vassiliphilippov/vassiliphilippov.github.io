black fox

1. NOUN

/ blæk fɑks / (AmE), / blæk fɒks / (BrE)
A color variant of the red fox (Vulpes vulpes) in which the animal's fur is mostly black.
a red fox with mostly black fur

Examples

The fur trade once prized the rare black fox for its luxurious pelt.
A black fox was seen roaming near the edge of the forest last night.
Unlike the typical red variety, the black fox has a dark, glossy coat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: red fox - a common fox species with typically reddish fur
