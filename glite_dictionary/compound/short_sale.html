short sale

1. NOUN

/ ʃɔrt seɪl / (AmE), / ʃɔːt seɪl / (BrE)
the sale of securities or commodity futures that the seller does not own, with the intention of buying them back later at a lower price to make a profit
sale of securities not owned by seller

Examples

Many investors lost money when their short sale positions were forced to close after the stock price rose unexpectedly.
He made a profit through a short sale by buying back the shares at a lower price.
A short sale can be risky if the market moves against the trader's expectations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: short selling - the act or practice of engaging in a short sale
