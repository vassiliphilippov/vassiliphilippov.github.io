common canary

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmən kəˈnɛri / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən kəˈneəri / (BrE)
a small, usually yellow songbird native to the Canary Islands and Azores, widely kept as a cage bird for its song
songbird often kept as a pet

Examples

The common canary is prized for its cheerful singing around the house.
Many bird enthusiasts enjoy keeping a common canary as a pet.
Breeders have developed several color varieties of the common canary.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: serin - a type of small finch found in Europe and North Africa
  • Synonym: finch - a bird of the family Fringillidae

Etymology

From its native region, the Canary Islands; 'canary' from Latin 'Canaria insula' (Isle of Dogs).
