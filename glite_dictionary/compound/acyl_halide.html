acyl halide

1. NOUN

/ ˈæsəl ˈheɪlaɪd / (AmE), / ˈeɪsɪl ˈheɪlaɪd / (BrE)
an organic compound containing the group –COX, where X is a halogen atom
organic compound with –COX group
medical

Examples

The synthesis of an acyl halide often involves reacting a carboxylic acid with thionyl chloride.
An acyl halide will readily react with water to produce a carboxylic acid.
Chemists frequently use acyl halide intermediates in the preparation of esters and amides.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acid chloride - an acyl halide where the halogen is chlorine
  • Degree Lower: anhydride - an organic compound derived by removing water from two acids
  • Degree Higher: acid anhydride - an organic compound related in reactivity to acyl halides

Etymology

From 'acyl' (from Latin 'acetum' vinegar, and '-yl') + 'halide' (from 'halogen' + '-ide').
