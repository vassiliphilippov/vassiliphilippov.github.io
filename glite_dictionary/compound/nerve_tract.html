nerve tract

1. NOUN

/ nɜrv trækt / (AmE), / nɜːv trækt / (BrE)
A bundle of nerve fibers (axons) running together as a pathway within the brain or spinal cord, usually with a common function or destination.
bundle of nerve fibers in the brain or spinal cord
medical

Examples

Damage to the nerve tract can lead to loss of movement or sensation below the injury site.
Researchers are studying how the visual nerve tract processes information from the eyes to the brain.
The patient suffered an injury that affected the spinal nerve tract responsible for motor control.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: axon - a long projection of a nerve cell that conducts impulses
  • Synonym: nerve - a bundle of fibers transmitting nerve impulses
