posterior horn

1. NOUN

/ poʊˈstɪriɚ hɔrn / (AmE), / pɒˈstɪəriə hɔːn / (BrE)
the dorsal section of gray matter in the spinal cord, consisting mainly of sensory nerve fibers
dorsal section of spinal cord gray matter
medical

Examples

Damage to the posterior horn can result in loss of sensory function below the injury site.
The posterior horn of the spinal cord receives input from sensory neurons.
Inflammation in the posterior horn is often associated with certain neurological diseases.

Etymology

From Latin 'posterior' (later) and 'cornu' (horn), referencing anatomical position and shape.
