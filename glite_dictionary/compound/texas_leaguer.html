Texas leaguer

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛksəs ˈliːɡər / (AmE), / ˈtɛksəs ˈliːɡə / (BrE)
(in baseball) a fly ball that drops between an infielder and an outfielder, often resulting in a base hit.
a shallow fly ball in baseball

Examples

The batter hit a Texas leaguer just over the shortstop's head.
His only hit of the game was a lucky Texas leaguer to shallow right field.
Sometimes all you need is a Texas leaguer to get on base.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blooper - a similar softly hit fly ball in baseball

Etymology

Named after players from the Texas League noted for hitting such balls in the late 19th century.
