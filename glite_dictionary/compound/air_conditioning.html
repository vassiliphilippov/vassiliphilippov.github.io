air conditioning

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛr kənˌdɪʃənɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈeə kənˌdɪʃənɪŋ / (BrE)
A system for controlling the temperature, humidity, and sometimes cleanliness of the air in a room or building, typically to keep it cool and dry.
system for cooling and dehumidifying air

Examples

The air conditioning in the office keeps everyone comfortable during the summer.
Many hotels advertise rooms with air conditioning for travelers wanting a cool stay.
We had to call a technician because the air conditioning stopped working last night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: air conditioner - a machine or unit for controlling air temperature and humidity

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛr kənˌdɪʃənɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈeə kənˌdɪʃənɪŋ / (BrE)
A system for controlling the temperature, humidity, and ventilation of indoor air, usually to cool it.
system for cooling indoor air

Examples

The air conditioning kept the office cool during the heatwave.
Many cars now come with air conditioning as a standard feature.
She complained that the air conditioning was too cold in the restaurant.

Synset Examples

Please switch on the air conditioning; it's too hot.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: air conditioner - machine that provides air conditioning

Etymology

From 'air' + 'conditioning,' referring to modifying air properties.
