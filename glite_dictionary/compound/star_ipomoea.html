star ipomoea

1. NOUN

/ stɑr ˌaɪ.pəˈmiː.ə / (AmE), / stɑː ˌaɪ.pəˈmiː.ə / (BrE)
An annual climbing herb with scarlet flowers, native to the eastern United States.
annual herb with scarlet flowers

Examples

The bright red blooms of the star ipomoea attract hummingbirds all summer long.
Gardeners in the eastern United States often plant star ipomoea for its vivid flowers and climbing vines.
Because star ipomoea is an annual, it needs to be replanted each spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ipomoea - a genus of flowering plants including morning glories

Etymology

From 'star', referring to the star-shaped flowers, and 'Ipomoea', the plant's genus name from Greek 'ips' (worm) and 'homoios' (resembling), alluding to its twining habit.
