shell game

1. NOUN

/ ʃɛl ˈɡeɪm / (AmE), / ʃɛl ˈɡeɪm / (BrE)
A deceptive gambling game in which a small object is hidden under one of three shells or cups and shuffled, requiring the player to guess its location, often used to cheat victims.
a deceptive cup-and-pellet gambling game

Examples

The shell game is commonly seen at street fairs, luring people to bet on finding the hidden ball.
He lost fifty dollars playing a shell game run by a clever swindler downtown.
Police arrested three men suspected of operating an illegal shell game on the corner.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: con game - a swindling trick or fraud

Etymology

From the trick's use of nutshells or cups as covers, first known in the United States in the 19th century.
