night shift

1. NOUN

/ naɪt ʃɪft / (AmE), / naɪt ʃɪft / (BrE)
A period of work that takes place during the night, typically from midnight to early morning, or the group of workers scheduled at this time.
work period during the night

Examples

She works the night shift at the hospital every weekend.
The factory needs more employees for the night shift to keep up with production.
After months on the night shift, he finally adjusted to sleeping during the day.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: day shift - period of work during the daytime
  • Synonym: graveyard shift - work shift that runs through the late night, often midnight to 8 a.m.
