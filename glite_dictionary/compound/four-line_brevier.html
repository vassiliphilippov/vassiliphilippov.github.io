four-line brevier

1. NOUN

/ fɔr laɪn brəˈvɪr / (AmE), / fɔː laɪn brɪˈvɪə / (BrE)
a size of printing type equal to 32 points, smaller than double great primer, used in typography
typographic size of 32-point type

Examples

Headings were set in bold four-line brevier to attract the reader’s attention.
The printer found that four-line brevier was too large for the pamphlet’s compact layout.
This edition used a consistent four-line brevier typeface throughout the title pages.

Etymology

From the typographic term 'brevier' (a size of type), with 'four-line' indicating the height equivalent to four lines of this type.
