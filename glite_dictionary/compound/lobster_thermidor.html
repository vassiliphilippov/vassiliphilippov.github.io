lobster thermidor

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɑb.stɚ θɜr.məˌdɔr / (AmE), / ˈlɒb.stə ˌθɜː.mɪˈdɔː / (BrE)
a dish of diced lobster meat mixed with a rich cheese sauce (Mornay sauce), returned to its shell, sprinkled with cheese, and browned under a grill or broiler.
lobster dish with cheese sauce, gratinéed

Examples

The restaurant's signature lobster thermidor was served piping hot in its shell.
She ordered lobster thermidor as the main course for her anniversary dinner.
Learning to make lobster thermidor at home can be a fun culinary challenge.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lobster Newberg - lobster dish with rich cream, cognac, and sherry sauce

Etymology

From French 'Thermidor,' supposedly named for the month in the French Revolutionary calendar, after a play popular at the time.
