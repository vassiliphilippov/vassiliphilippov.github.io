order Apodiformes

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdɚ ˌæpədaɪˈfɔrmiːz / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə ˌapədɪˈfɔːmiːz / (BrE)
A taxonomic order of birds comprising swifts and hummingbirds.
taxonomic order of swifts and hummingbirds

Examples

There are over 400 species within the order Apodiformes, including swifts and hummingbirds.
The order Apodiformes is known for members' exceptional aerial agility and rapid wing movement.
Swifts and hummingbirds, both part of the order Apodiformes, have independently adapted to different styles of feeding.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swifts - small aerial birds belonging to Apodiformes
  • Synonym: hummingbirds - tiny, fast-flying birds of the Americas in Apodiformes

Etymology

From Greek 'apous' (without feet), referring to the birds' small legs and feet, + 'formes' (form, shape).
