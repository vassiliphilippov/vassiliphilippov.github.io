Mackinaw coat

1. NOUN

/ ˈmækɪˌnɔ ˌkoʊt / (AmE), / ˈmakɪˌnɔː kəʊt / (BrE)
a short heavy coat made of thick woolen fabric, typically with a distinctive plaid pattern, worn especially in outdoor settings
short thick woolen coat, often plaid

Examples

He wore a Mackinaw coat to stay warm while hiking in the snow.
The red and black plaid of his Mackinaw coat stood out against the white landscape.
Vintage Mackinaw coats are prized by collectors for their durability and style.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pea coat - a short woolen jacket, double-breasted, worn by sailors

Etymology

From Mackinaw, the name of a region in Michigan, USA, where the coat became popular in the 19th century.
