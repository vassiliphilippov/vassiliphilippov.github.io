Arkansas River

1. PROPN

/ ˈɑːrkənsɔː ˈrɪvər / (AmE), / ˈɑːkənsɔː ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
A major river in the central United States that originates in the Rocky Mountains of Colorado and flows southeast through Kansas, Oklahoma, and Arkansas before joining the Mississippi River.
large river in central United States

Examples

The Arkansas River flooded several towns after days of heavy rain.
Many people enjoy fishing and boating along the Arkansas River.
The Arkansas River is a vital waterway for shipping agricultural products.

Etymology

From the name of the state of Arkansas, itself from the French interpretation of a Native American word meaning 'people of the south wind.'
