in a mo

1. ADV

/ ɪn ə moʊ / (AmE), / ɪn ə məʊ / (BrE)
used to say that something will happen very soon; in a moment
very soon; in a moment
informal british

Examples

I'll get started in a mo once I've finished this call.
She said she'd be ready in a mo, but she's still upstairs.
If you wait in a mo, your food will be served.

Synset Examples

I'll be there in a mo, just finishing up.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: in a minute - very soon; after a short time

Etymology

'Mo' as an abbreviation of 'moment' first attested in 19th century English.
