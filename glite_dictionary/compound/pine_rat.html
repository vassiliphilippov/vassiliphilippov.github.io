pine rat

1. NOUN

/ paɪn ræt / (AmE), / paɪn ræt / (BrE)
a large rodent found in pine forests, sometimes considered a pest
a rodent native to pine forests

Examples

Farmers often complain about the damage caused by the pine rat in their fields.
The pine rat is mostly active at night and hard to spot during the day.
Some people try to protect their crops from the pine rat using traps.

Synset Examples

Locals often refer to the Pine Rat as a nuisance.
