PSA blood test

1. NOUN

/ ˌpiː ɛs ˈeɪ blʌd tɛst / (AmE), / ˌpiː ɛs ˈeɪ blʌd tɛst / (BrE)
a blood test that measures the level of prostate-specific antigen (PSA), a protein produced by the prostate gland, often used to detect prostate disorders
blood test for prostate-specific antigen
medical

Examples

My doctor recommended a PSA blood test as part of my annual check-up.
After noticing urinary symptoms, he scheduled a PSA blood test to check for prostate issues.
Elevated results from a PSA blood test do not necessarily indicate cancer but require further investigation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prostate-specific antigen - the protein measured by the PSA test

Etymology

PSA stands for prostate-specific antigen; term widely used in medicine since the late 20th century.
