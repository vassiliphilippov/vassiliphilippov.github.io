reeducation initiative

1. NOUN

/ ˌriːˌɛdʒuˈkeɪʃən ɪˌnɪʃiˈətɪv / (AmE), / ˌriːˌɛdjuˈkeɪʃən ɪˌnɪʃɪˈətɪv / (BrE)
a program or organized effort to train, reform, or change the attitudes or skills of individuals, especially after previous misconduct or for social integration
program for training or reforming individuals

Examples

The city launched a reeducation initiative to help former prisoners find employment.
School authorities started a reeducation initiative for students who violated the code of conduct.
After the crisis, a national reeducation initiative was introduced to address social integration.

Synset Examples

The government implemented reeducation initiatives for ex-offenders.
