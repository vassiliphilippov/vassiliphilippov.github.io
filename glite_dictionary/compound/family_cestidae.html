family Cestidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli sɛˈstaɪdi / (AmE), / ˈfæməli sɛsˈtiːdiː / (BrE)
A family of ctenophores characterized by greatly flattened and elongated bodies, coextensive with the order Cestida.
family of flattened, elongated ctenophores

Examples

The family Cestidae includes several species of ribbon-like comb jellies found in warm seas.
Members of the family Cestidae are distinguished from other ctenophores by their flattened, elongated shape.
Researchers have studied the locomotion of family Cestidae ctenophores to better understand their adaptation to the pelagic environment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cestida - order of ctenophores with flattened, elongated bodies

Etymology

From New Latin Cestidae, based on Cestida (the related order), from Latin 'cestus' (girdle or belt), referring to the shape.
