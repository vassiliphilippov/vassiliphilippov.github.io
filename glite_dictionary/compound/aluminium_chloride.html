aluminium chloride

1. NOUN

/ əˌluːmɪnəm ˈklɔrˌaɪd / (AmE), / ˌaljʊˈmɪniəm ˈklɔːraɪd / (BrE)
a white or yellowish crystalline compound (AlCl₃) used in industry as a catalyst and in wood preservation
a chemical compound AlCl₃ used in industry

Examples

The laboratory ordered more aluminium chloride for use in organic synthesis experiments.
Aluminium chloride is a key catalyst in the production of certain pharmaceuticals.
Some wood preservatives contain aluminium chloride to prevent decay and insect damage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ferric chloride - an iron chloride, also used as a catalyst

Etymology

From aluminium plus chloride, indicating a chemical compound of aluminium and chlorine.
