marang tree

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑːrɑŋ triː / (AmE), / ˈmɑːræŋ triː / (BrE)
A Philippine tree similar to the breadfruit tree, bearing large edible fruit with a soft, sweet pulp.
Philippine tree with edible fruit

Examples

The marang tree produces fruit prized for its sweet, creamy flavor in the Philippines.
Farmers often plant marang trees alongside other fruit crops.
The large leaves of the marang tree provide plenty of shade in tropical gardens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: breadfruit tree - Tropical tree with large, starchy edible fruit
  • Synonym: jackfruit - Large tropical fruit related to the marang tree

Etymology

From Tagalog 'marang', the local name for the tree.
