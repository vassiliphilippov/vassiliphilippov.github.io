Doctor of Music

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɑktɚ əv ˈmjuːzɪk / (AmE), / ˈdɒktə əv ˈmjuːzɪk / (BrE)
A doctoral degree awarded in the field of music, typically signifying advanced study and achievement in musicology, composition, or performance.
advanced doctoral degree in music

Examples

After years of rigorous study, she was awarded a Doctor of Music from Oxford.
Earning a Doctor of Music requires not only talent but also dedication to research or performance.
He proudly added the title Doctor of Music after his name on the program.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Doctor of Philosophy - doctoral degree in most academic disciplines
