order Myricales

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdər mɪˈraɪkəliːz / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə mɪˈraɪkəliːz / (BrE)
a botanical order consisting of plants in the family Myricaceae; formerly a recognized taxonomic grouping.
botanical order of Myricaceae

Examples

Recent botanical studies have reclassified the order Myricales into other groups.
All members of the order Myricales share certain morphological traits.
The order Myricales was once thought to include several families now placed elsewhere.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Myricaceae - the botanical family comprising bayberries and wax myrtles

Etymology

From New Latin 'Myricales', based on the family name 'Myricaceae'.
