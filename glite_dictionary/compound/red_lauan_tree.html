red lauan tree

1. NOUN

/ rɛd laʊˈɑːn triː / (AmE), / rɛd laʊˈɑːn triː / (BrE)
A valuable timber tree native to the Philippines, known for its reddish wood used in construction and furniture making.
Philippine timber tree with reddish wood

Examples

The wood from the red lauan tree is highly valued for building houses and making furniture.
Many forests in the Philippines are home to the rare red lauan tree.
Due to overharvesting, the red lauan tree is now considered a threatened species.

Etymology

From Tagalog 'lauan', the local name for the tree species.
