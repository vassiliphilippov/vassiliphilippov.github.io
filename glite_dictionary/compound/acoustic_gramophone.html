acoustic gramophone

1. NOUN

/ əˈkuːstɪk ˈɡræməˌfoʊn / (AmE), / əˈkuːstɪk ˈɡræməfəʊn / (BrE)
An antique record player in which sound is amplified mechanically through an acoustic horn, without electronic amplification.
antique mechanical record player

Examples

The museum displayed a beautifully restored acoustic gramophone from the early 1900s.
He wound up the acoustic gramophone and placed a vinyl record on the turntable.
Collectors often prize the elegant horns of the acoustic gramophone for their craftsmanship.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gramophone - an old-fashioned device for playing sound recordings

Etymology

From Greek 'akoustikos' (pertaining to hearing) and 'gramophone', a trademarked term from French 'grammophone', coined in the late 19th century.
