northern flying squirrel

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɔːrðərn ˈflaɪɪŋ ˈskwɝːrəl / (AmE), / ˈnɔːðən ˈflaɪɪŋ ˈskwɪrəl / (BrE)
a large flying squirrel native to North America, especially Canada, known for its ability to glide between trees using a membrane of skin between its limbs
large gliding squirrel of Canada and North America

Examples

The northern flying squirrel glides gracefully from tree to tree in Canadian forests.
Researchers are studying the habitat preferences of the northern flying squirrel.
Unlike its southern cousin, the northern flying squirrel is adapted to colder climates.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: southern flying squirrel - a smaller flying squirrel species found in the eastern United States
