American water ouzel

1. NOUN

/ əˈmɛr.ɪ.kən ˈwɑ.tər ˈaʊ.zəl / (AmE), / əˈmɛr.ɪ.kən ˈwɔː.tə ˈaʊ.zəl / (BrE)
a small aquatic songbird of western North America, known for living near streams and diving into water for food; also called the American dipper.
aquatic songbird of western North America

Examples

The American water ouzel is often seen diving in mountain streams to feed on aquatic insects.
We spotted an American water ouzel as we hiked along the river in Colorado.
Unlike most songbirds, the American water ouzel can swim underwater to hunt for food.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: American dipper - another name for the American water ouzel
