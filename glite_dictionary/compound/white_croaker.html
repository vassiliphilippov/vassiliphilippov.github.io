white croaker

1. NOUN

/ waɪt ˈkroʊkər / (AmE), / waɪt ˈkrəʊkə / (BrE)
a small, silvery marine fish (Genyonemus lineatus) found off the coast of California, valued as a food fish
small silvery marine fish off California

Examples

Local fishermen often catch white croaker in the shallow coastal waters off Southern California.
The white croaker is commonly sold at seafood markets along the Pacific coast.
Environmental concerns have led to warnings about eating too much white croaker due to potential contamination.

2. NOUN

/ waɪt ˈkroʊkɚ / (AmE), / waɪt ˈkrəʊkə / (BrE)
A silvery and bluish drumfish found in shallow coastal waters of California.
a drumfish of California coastal waters

Examples

Anglers often catch white croaker from piers along the Southern California coast.
The white croaker is known for the croaking sound it makes when removed from the water.
Many fishermen consider white croaker to be an undesirable catch due to its small size.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drumfish - any fish of the family Sciaenidae, known for making croaking or drumming sounds
