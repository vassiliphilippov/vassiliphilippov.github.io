common scold

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːmən skoʊld / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən skəʊld / (BrE)
(historical) A person, especially a woman, who habitually annoys others by persistently complaining or finding fault.
historical term for a habitual complainer
archaic legal

Examples

The village has not seen a common scold brought to trial for decades.
In colonial times, a common scold might face public punishment for her behavior.
She was unfairly labeled a common scold by her neighbors because of her outspoken nature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nag - a person who habitually complains or criticizes persistently
  • Synonym: shrew - a woman of violent temper and speech

Etymology

From Middle English 'scold', meaning a quarrelsome woman; 'common' denoting being regularly or widely known.
