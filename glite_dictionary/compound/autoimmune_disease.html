autoimmune disease

1. NOUN

/ ˌɔtoʊɪˈmjun dɪˌziz / (AmE), / ˌɔːtəʊɪˈmjuːn dɪˌziːz / (BrE)
A disease in which the body's immune system attacks its own tissues, mistaking them for harmful invaders.
disease caused by immune system attacking own body
medical

Examples

Rheumatoid arthritis is a common autoimmune disease that causes inflammation in the joints.
Scientists are still studying the causes of autoimmune disease and ways to treat it.
Lupus is another well-known autoimmune disease that can affect various organs in the body.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: autoimmunity - The state of having an immune response against the body's own tissues
  • Antonym: immunodeficiency - A condition in which the immune system's ability to fight infections is compromised or absent
  • Degree Lower: disease - A disorder of structure or function in a living organism

Etymology

From 'auto-' meaning 'self', 'immune' relating to the immune system, and 'disease'.
