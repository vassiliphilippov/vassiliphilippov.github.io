average cost

1. NOUN

/ ˈæv.ər.ɪdʒ kɑst / (AmE), / ˈæv.ər.ɪdʒ kɒst / (BrE)
the total cost of all units bought or produced divided by the number of units
total cost divided by number of units

Examples

The average cost of manufacturing a car decreased as production increased.
Businesses analyze their average cost to determine the most efficient level of output.
If you buy more units at a lower price, the average cost goes down.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: marginal cost - the cost of producing one additional unit
