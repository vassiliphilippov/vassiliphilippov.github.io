hobble skirt

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑbəl skɝt / (AmE), / ˈhɒbəl skɜːt / (BrE)
A long skirt that is very narrow around the ankles, restricting the wearer's movement, especially popular between 1910 and 1914.
long, narrow skirt restricting movement

Examples

The fashion of the hobble skirt made it almost impossible for women to take large steps.
She wore a vintage hobble skirt reminiscent of early 20th-century style.
Photographs from 1912 show women struggling to walk in their stylish hobble skirts.

Etymology

From 'hobble' meaning to walk with difficulty, referencing the restricted movement caused by the skirt.
