Thousand Island dressing

1. NOUN

/ ˈθaʊzənd ˈaɪlənd ˈdrɛsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈθaʊz(ə)nd ˈaɪlənd ˈdrɛsɪŋ / (BrE)
a creamy salad dressing made with mayonnaise, ketchup or chili sauce, and various finely chopped ingredients such as pickles, olives, peppers, and hard-boiled eggs.
a creamy salad dressing with chopped ingredients

Examples

She added Thousand Island dressing to her salad for extra flavor.
The restaurant offers Thousand Island dressing as one of its house-made options.
Could I have my sandwich with a side of Thousand Island dressing, please?

Etymology

Named after the Thousand Islands region in the St. Lawrence River, between the U.S. and Canada.
