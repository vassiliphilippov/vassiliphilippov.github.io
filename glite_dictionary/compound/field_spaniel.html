field spaniel

1. NOUN

/ ˈfild ˌspæniəl / (AmE), / ˈfiːld ˌspæniəl / (BrE)
a large breed of spaniel, typically black, bred chiefly for hunting and retrieving, with a dense, flat or slightly wavy coat
a breed of large hunting spaniel

Examples

The field spaniel is known for its gentle temperament and loyalty.
She trained her field spaniel to retrieve birds during hunting trips.
Many owners appreciate the intelligence of their field spaniel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cocker spaniel - a small breed of spaniel, originally bred as a hunting dog
  • Synonym: Sussex spaniel - a breed of spaniel used as a gun dog, originally from Sussex, England
