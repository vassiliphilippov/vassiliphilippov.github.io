broad-leaved dock

1. NOUN

/ brɔd ˌlivd ˈdɑk / (AmE), / brɔːd ˌliːvd ˈdɒk / (BrE)
A European plant (Rumex obtusifolius) with broad, rounded leaves and a bitter root, common as a weed in North America.
a European plant with broad leaves, weed in North America

Examples

The broad-leaved dock is easily identified by its large, rounded leaves.
Gardeners often struggle to remove broad-leaved dock from their vegetable patches.
The broad-leaved dock was originally native to Europe but is now widespread in North America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dock - a broad-leaved weed of the genus Rumex
