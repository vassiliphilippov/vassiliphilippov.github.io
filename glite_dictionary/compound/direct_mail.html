direct mail

1. NOUN

/ dəˈrɛkt ˈmeɪl / (AmE), / dəˈrɛkt ˈmeɪl / (BrE)
advertising material sent directly to prospective customers by post, rather than through mass media.
advertising sent by mail to individuals

Examples

Many businesses use direct mail to reach potential customers with special offers.
The company invested heavily in a new direct mail campaign to boost sales.
She receives so much direct mail that her mailbox is always full.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: junk mail - unsolicited advertising material sent by post
