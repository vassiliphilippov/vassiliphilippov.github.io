superior pulmonary vein

1. NOUN

/ suˈpɪriər ˌpʌlməˌnɛri veɪn / (AmE), / sjuːˈpɪəriə ˌpʌlmənəri veɪn / (BrE)
either of two pulmonary veins (left and right) that return blood from the upper lobes of the lungs to the left atrium of the heart
vein returning blood from upper lung lobes
medical

Examples

The surgeon carefully avoided damaging the superior pulmonary vein during the procedure.
A blockage in the superior pulmonary vein can lead to serious complications.
Each lung has its own superior pulmonary vein that carries oxygenated blood to the heart.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inferior pulmonary vein - pulmonary vein that returns blood from the lower lobes of the lungs

Etymology

From Latin 'superior' (higher) + 'pulmonarius' (of the lungs) + 'vena' (vein).
