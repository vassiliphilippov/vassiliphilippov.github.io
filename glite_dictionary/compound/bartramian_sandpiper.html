Bartramian sandpiper

1. NOUN

/ bɑːrˈtreɪmi.ən ˈsændˌpaɪ.pɚ / (AmE), / bɑːˈtræm.i.ən ˈsændˌpaɪ.pə / (BrE)
a large, plover-like sandpiper (Bartramia longicauda) native to North American fields and uplands
large North American sandpiper species

Examples

The Bartramian sandpiper is often seen in grassy meadows during migration season.
Farmers sometimes spot Bartramian sandpipers walking through their pastures.
The call of the Bartramian sandpiper is a distinctive whistling sound heard on the prairies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: upland sandpiper - another common name for Bartramian sandpiper, a North American sandpiper species

Etymology

Named after the American naturalist William Bartram (1739–1823).
