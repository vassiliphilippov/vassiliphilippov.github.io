ballistic trajectory

1. NOUN

/ bəˈlɪstɪk trəˈdʒɛktəri / (AmE), / bəˈlɪstɪk trəˈdʒɛktəri / (BrE)
the curved path followed by an object launched into the air and moving freely under the influence of gravity and air resistance
path of an object in free flight

Examples

The missile followed a ballistic trajectory after its initial propulsion phase ended.
Artillery shells typically travel along a ballistic trajectory until they reach their targets.
The investigators calculated the ballistic trajectory to determine the bullet's path through the air.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trajectory - the path followed by a moving object

Etymology

Ballistic from Greek 'ballein' (to throw), trajectory from Latin 'trajectoria' (throw across).
