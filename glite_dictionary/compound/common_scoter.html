common scoter

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmən ˈskoʊtər / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən ˈskəʊtə / (BrE)
a species of sea duck (Melanitta nigra) found in northern Europe and parts of Asia, with mainly black plumage in males and brownish coloration in females
a species of European sea duck

Examples

A flock of common scoters was spotted off the coast during the winter migration.
The male common scoter is easily recognized by its all-black plumage.
Birdwatchers were thrilled to record a rare sighting of a common scoter on the lake.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scoter - a type of sea duck in the genus Melanitta
