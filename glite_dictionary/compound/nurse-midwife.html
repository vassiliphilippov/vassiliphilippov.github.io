nurse-midwife

1. NOUN

/ nɝːs ˈmɪd.waɪf / (AmE), / nɜːs ˈmɪd.waɪf / (BrE)
a registered nurse who is specially trained to provide care and deliver babies as a midwife
a registered nurse trained as a midwife
medical

Examples

The nurse-midwife stayed with the patient throughout labor to provide support and medical care.
After training as a nurse-midwife, she began delivering babies in community clinics.
Many women appreciate the personalized attention given by a nurse-midwife during pregnancy and childbirth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: midwife - a person trained to assist women during childbirth
