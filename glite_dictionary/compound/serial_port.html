serial port

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪri.əl pɔrt / (AmE), / ˈsɪəri.əl pɔːt / (BrE)
a socket on a computer or device for connecting peripherals, transmitting data one bit at a time in sequence
computer socket for data transmitted bit by bit

Examples

Older mice and printers often connected via a serial port on the back of the computer.
To update the device’s firmware, the instructions recommend using a serial port connection.
The technician identified a faulty serial port as the cause of the communication failure.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: parallel port - a socket that transmits multiple bits of data simultaneously, unlike a serial port
