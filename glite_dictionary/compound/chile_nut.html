Chile nut

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃiːleɪ nʌt / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪli nʌt / (BrE)
a Chilean shrub (Gevuina avellana) bearing coral-red fruit with an edible seed that resembles a hazelnut
edible seed of a Chilean shrub

Examples

The Chile nut is prized for its delicious, hazelnut-like flavor in South American cuisine.
Botanists study the Chile nut to better understand its potential as a crop.
She bought a bag of roasted Chile nuts at the local market.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hazelnut - the edible nut of the hazel tree

Etymology

From Chile, the country in South America, and nut, referring to the edible seed.
