social action

1. NOUN

/ ˈsoʊʃəl ˈækʃən / (AmE), / ˈsəʊʃəl ˈækʃən / (BrE)
a policy or organized effort aimed at bringing about reform in society, especially regarding socioeconomic conditions
policy or measure for social reform

Examples

The new government introduced a series of social action programs to tackle unemployment.
Community leaders called for immediate social action to address economic inequality in the region.
The charity organizes workshops that encourage social action among young people.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reform - the improvement or amendment of what is wrong, corrupt, or unsatisfactory
  • Synonym: social policy - policy relating to society and the welfare of its members
