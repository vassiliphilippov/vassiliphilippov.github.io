family Charadriidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli kəˌrædrɪˈɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli kəˌrædrɪˈɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of wading birds known as plovers, including species commonly found near water.
family of wading birds (plovers)

Examples

Birdwatchers identified several members of the family Charadriidae along the muddy riverbank.
The family Charadriidae includes both plovers and dotterels, which are found worldwide.
Conservation efforts have increased for species in the family Charadriidae threatened by habitat loss.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plover - small wading bird related to family Charadriidae

Etymology

From scientific Latin 'Charadriidae', from genus 'Charadrius' (plovers).
