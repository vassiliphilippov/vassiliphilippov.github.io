low ebb

1. NOUN

/ loʊ ɛb / (AmE), / ləʊ ɛb / (BrE)
a state or period of reduced strength, activity, or success; a low point
a low point or period of decline

Examples

After months of hard work, my motivation was at a low ebb.
The team's performance is at a low ebb this season, but they hope to recover soon.
Creative inspiration can sometimes reach a low ebb during stressful periods.

Synset Examples

Her energy levels are at a low ebb today.
The economy is at a low ebb currently.
His spirits were at a low ebb last week.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: high point - peak or most successful period
  • Synonym: nadir - the lowest point of something

Etymology

From 'ebb' meaning the receding tide, metaphorically used since the 17th century for decline.
