parsley-leaved thorn

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑrsli livd θɔrn / (AmE), / ˈpɑːsli liːvd θɔːn / (BrE)
a southern United States hawthorn tree or shrub with pinnately lobed leaves, resembling parsley
southern U.S. hawthorn with lobed leaves

Examples

The parsley-leaved thorn thrives in the warm and humid climate of the southern United States.
Botanists often study the unique leaf shapes of the parsley-leaved thorn.
Birds nest among the branches of the parsley-leaved thorn in early spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hawthorn - a thorny shrub or tree of the genus Crataegus, bearing white or pink blossoms
