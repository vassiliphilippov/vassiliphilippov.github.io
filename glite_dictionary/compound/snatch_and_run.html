snatch and run

1. NOUN

/ snætʃ ənd rʌn / (AmE), / snætʃ ənd rʌn / (BrE)
a theft in which an item is quickly grabbed and the thief flees immediately
a quick theft involving grabbing and fleeing

Examples

The thief executed a snatch and run at the market.
The store installed cameras to deter snatch and run crimes.
After witnessing a snatch and run, she called the police immediately.

Synset Examples

The thief executed a snatch and run at the market.
It was a quick snatch and run; he was gone in seconds.
They caught the suspect in a snatch and run incident.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snatch - to quickly seize something
  • Synonym: grab and run - a theft involving grabbing and quickly fleeing
