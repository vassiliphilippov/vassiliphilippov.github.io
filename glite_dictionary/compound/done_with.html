done with

1. ADJ

/ dʌn wɪð / (AmE), / dʌn wɪð / (BrE)
having finished or stopped being involved with someone or something; no longer concerned or occupied with it
finished or no longer involved with

Examples

I'm finally done with that project and can relax this weekend.
After years of arguing, she's completely done with him.
He says he's done with smoking for good this time.

Synset Examples

he was through with school and he was through with family
done with gambling
done with drinking

Related Terms

  • Synonym: through with - no longer involved in or concerned with something or someone

2. ADJ

/ dʌn wɪð / (AmE), / dʌn wɪð / (BrE)
no longer involved with or finished with someone or something; having ended one's association or interest.
no longer involved with something

Examples

After years at that job, she is finally done with it and ready for something new.
I'm done with these old magazines—can we throw them away?
He told his friends he was done with partying and wanted a quieter life.

Synset Examples

I have done with this project; it's finally finished.
She has done with the arguments and wants peace.
He was done with the meeting, asking if there was any more for any more.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: finished with - no longer using or involved with something or someone
  • Synonym: over - having ended or finished
