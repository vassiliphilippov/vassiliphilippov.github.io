genus Palaemon

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs pəˈliːmɒn, ˈdʒiːnəs pəˈleɪmən / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs pəˈleɪmɒn / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Palaemonidae, consisting of widely distributed species of shrimp.
a genus of shrimps in Palaemonidae

Examples

The genus Palaemon includes several economically important species of shrimp.
Many marine biologists study the distribution of the genus Palaemon around the world.
Shrimp in the genus Palaemon are commonly found in both freshwater and saltwater habitats.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Palaemonidae - the family of shrimps to which the genus Palaemon belongs

Etymology

From Latin 'Palaemon', the name of a sea deity, used in taxonomy for certain shrimp.
