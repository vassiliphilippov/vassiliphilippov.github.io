purging cassia

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɝːdʒɪŋ ˈkæsiə / (AmE), / ˈpɜːdʒɪŋ ˈkæsiə / (BrE)
A tropical tree with fragrant yellow flowers and pods used medicinally for their purgative properties, native to Asia, the Americas, and Australia.
tropical tree with medicinal pods

Examples

The purging cassia is valued in traditional medicine for its laxative properties.
Botanists identified the tree as purging cassia because of its distinctive yellow flowers and pods.
In some regions, the pulp from purging cassia pods is used in herbal remedies.

Etymology

From 'purging', referring to cleansing, and 'cassia', from Latin and Greek meaning aromatic plants.
