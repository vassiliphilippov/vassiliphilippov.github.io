false gavial

1. NOUN

/ ˌfɔls ɡəˈvaɪəl / (AmE), / ˌfɔːls ɡəˈvaɪəl / (BrE)
a slender-snouted crocodile of Southeast Asia, closely related to but smaller than the true gavial
a Southeast Asian crocodile species

Examples

The false gavial has a long, narrow snout adapted for catching fish.
Wildlife biologists are working to protect the endangered false gavial in its native rivers.
Unlike the true gavial, the false gavial is found mainly in parts of Indonesia and Malaysia.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: gavial - large, slender-snouted crocodile of India and Southeast Asia

Etymology

From 'false' (not genuine) and 'gavial' (from Hindi 'घऱियाल gharial', a type of crocodile).
