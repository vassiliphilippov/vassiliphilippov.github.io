Republic of Benin

1. PROPN

/ ɹɪˈpʌblɪk əv bəˈnɪn / (AmE), / rɪˈpʌblɪk əv bəˈniːn / (BrE)
The official name for Benin, a country on the western coast of Africa, formerly a French colony.
country in West Africa

Examples

The Republic of Benin gained independence from France in 1960.
She traveled throughout the Republic of Benin as part of her research on African cultures.
Official documents must specify the name as the Republic of Benin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Benin - a country in West Africa; short form of Republic of Benin

Etymology

From French 'République du Bénin', named after the Bight of Benin, itself from the pre-colonial Benin Empire (now in Nigeria).
