Evangelical United Brethren Church

1. NOUN

/ ˌivænˈdʒɛlɪkəl ˈjuːnaɪtɪd brəˈðrɪn tʃɝːtʃ / (AmE), / ˌiːvanˈdʒelɪkəl ˈjuːnaɪtɪd brəˈðriːn tʃɜːtʃ / (BrE)
a former Protestant denomination in the Methodist tradition, formed in the United States in 1946 and later merged with the Methodist Church in 1968 to form the United Methodist Church.
former Methodist denomination

Examples

The Evangelical United Brethren Church merged with the Methodist Church to create the United Methodist Church in 1968.
Members of the Evangelical United Brethren Church played a significant role in American religious history.
The old chapel once belonged to the Evangelical United Brethren Church before it became a community center.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Methodist Church - a Protestant Christian denomination known for its emphasis on methodical study and practice of faith
