Chile tarweed

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃiːli ˈtɑrˌwid / (AmE), / ˈtʃiːleɪ ˈtɑːwiːd / (BrE)
a South American herb with sticky glandular foliage, cultivated as a source of madia oil
South American herb used for oil

Examples

Farmers are cultivating Chile tarweed to obtain valuable madia oil.
The sticky leaves of Chile tarweed make it easy to identify in the field.
Chile tarweed is native to South America and has recently attracted interest from the biofuel industry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: madia oil - oil obtained from the seeds of certain Madia species
