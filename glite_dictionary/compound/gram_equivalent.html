gram equivalent

1. NOUN

/ ɡræm ɪˈkwɪvələnt / (AmE), / ɡræm ɪˈkwɪvələnt / (BrE)
(Chemistry) The amount of a substance, measured in grams, that will combine with or displace 8 grams of oxygen or 1.008 grams of hydrogen; calculated as the atomic or molecular weight divided by its valence.
unit in chemistry; atomic/molecular weight divided by valence
medical

Examples

To prepare the solution, measure one gram equivalent of the salt and dissolve it in water.
The concept of gram equivalent is vital when calculating reactants in redox reactions.
You need to determine the gram equivalent of the acid to complete the neutralization process.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: equivalent weight - the weight of a substance that combines with or displaces a fixed quantity of another substance

Etymology

From 'gram' (from French 'gramme') + 'equivalent' (from Latin 'aequivalens', meaning 'of equal value').
