African love grass

1. NOUN

/ ˌæfrɪkən ˈlʌv ˌɡræs / (AmE), / ˌaf.rɪ.kən ˈlʌv ˌɡrɑːs / (BrE)
a perennial grass native to South Africa, with densely clumped flimsy stems, often introduced elsewhere for erosion control
a perennial South African grass species

Examples

Many ranchers use African love grass to prevent soil erosion in pastureland.
The rapid spread of African love grass has raised concerns about its impact on native species.
Researchers are studying how African love grass adapts to new environments outside South Africa.
