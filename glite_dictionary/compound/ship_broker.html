ship broker

1. NOUN

/ ʃɪp ˈbroʊkər / (AmE), / ʃɪp ˈbrəʊkə / (BrE)
an agent who arranges the hiring or chartering of ships on behalf of shipowners or those seeking to transport cargo
maritime agent arranging cargo and ship contracts

Examples

The ship broker finalized the deal between the ship owner and a major oil company.
She became a successful ship broker after years working in the maritime industry.
A reliable ship broker can secure profitable charters for vessel owners.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: broker - a person who arranges transactions between a buyer and a seller
