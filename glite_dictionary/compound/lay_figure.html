lay figure

1. NOUN

/ leɪ ˈfɪɡjɚ / (AmE), / leɪ ˈfɪɡjə / (BrE)
a jointed model of the human body used by artists to arrange and study poses and proportions
artist's jointed model of the human body

Examples

The artist adjusted the lay figure to capture the pose he envisioned for his painting.
A lay figure helps students study human anatomy and movement without a live model.
She arranged the lay figure so the outstretched arms would cast dramatic shadows.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mannequin - a dummy used to display clothes, especially in shops
  • Synonym: dummy - an object that represents a person or thing but is not real

Etymology

From 'lay' (to place) and 'figure' (representation of a human form); first used in the 17th century for artist's mannequins.
