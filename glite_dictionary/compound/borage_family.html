borage family

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɔːrɪdʒ ˈfæməli / (AmE), / ˈbɒrɪdʒ ˈfæməli / (BrE)
a family of widely distributed flowering plants (Boraginaceae) distinguished by coiled (circinate) flower clusters and nutlike fruit
family of flowering plants, Boraginaceae

Examples

The borage family includes herbs, shrubs, and some trees found throughout the world.
Many plants in the borage family are cultivated for their attractive, coiled flower clusters.
Viper's bugloss and forget-me-not both belong to the borage family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Boraginaceae - Botanical name for the borage family

Etymology

Named after the genus Borage (Borago), from Medieval Latin borago.
