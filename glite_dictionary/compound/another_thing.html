another thing

1. NOUN

/ əˈnʌð.ɚ ˌθɪŋ / (AmE), / əˈnʌð.ə ˌθɪŋ / (BrE)
used to introduce an additional point, objection, or concern in speech or writing
to introduce an additional point

Examples

He always interrupts meetings, and another thing, he never apologizes.
We should arrive early, and another thing is to remember to bring our tickets.
Her attitude is annoying, and another thing, she never listens to advice.

Synset Examples

He was late again, and another thing, he forgot his notes.
We need to fix the printer, and another thing, the copier.
