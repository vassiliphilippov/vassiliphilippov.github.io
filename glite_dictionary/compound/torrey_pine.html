Torrey pine

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɔːri paɪn / (AmE), / ˈtɒri paɪn / (BrE)
a rare pine tree of southwestern California, having long, slender needles in groups of five and large, cylindrical cones
rare pine tree native to California

Examples

The Torrey pine is considered one of the rarest species of pine in North America.
Many botanists visit California's coast to study the unique Torrey pine habitat.
A grove of Torrey pines overlooks the Pacific Ocean near San Diego.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pine - a type of evergreen tree with needlelike leaves and woody cones

Etymology

Named after American botanist John Torrey (1796–1873).
