Alpine fir

1. NOUN

/ ˈæl.paɪn ˌfɝ / (AmE), / ˈæl.paɪn ˌfɜː / (BrE)
a medium to tall evergreen conifer of the Rocky Mountains, noted for its narrow, conic to columnar crown and soft, flat needles.
Rocky Mountain coniferous tree

Examples

The Alpine fir can be found at higher elevations in the Rocky Mountains.
We used wood from an Alpine fir to build the new fence.
The narrow crown of the Alpine fir distinguishes it from other conifers in the region.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: fir - any tree of the genus Abies
  • Synonym: spruce - a similar type of coniferous tree

Etymology

From 'Alpine' referring to high mountainous regions, and 'fir', from Old English 'furh' (type of pine tree).
