surgical spirit

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɜrdʒɪkəl ˈspɪrɪt / (AmE), / ˈsɜːdʒɪkəl ˈspɪrɪt / (BrE)
a liquid containing methylated alcohol used for cleaning the skin before injections or surgery
disinfectant alcohol used in medicine
medical british

Examples

Before the injection, the nurse cleaned the area with surgical spirit to prevent infection.
He purchased a bottle of surgical spirit from the pharmacy for first-aid use at home.
Hospitals frequently use surgical spirit to sterilize equipment and skin before minor procedures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rubbing alcohol - alcohol-based disinfectant, especially in American English
