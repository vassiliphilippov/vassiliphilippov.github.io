genus Selene

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs səˈliːni / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs səˈliːni / (BrE)
A genus of fish in the family Carangidae, including the lookdowns and moonfish.
genus of fishes (lookdowns, moonfish)

Examples

The genus Selene includes several species of fish known for their distinct silver coloration.
Ichthyologists have long studied the unique shape of fishes in the genus Selene.
Lookdowns, a member of the genus Selene, are popular in public aquariums.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lookdown - species of fish in genus Selene, known for its flattened body and sloping head
  • Synonym: moonfish - common name for several species in genus Selene

Etymology

Selene: from Greek 'Selēnē', goddess of the moon, likely referencing the moonfish.
