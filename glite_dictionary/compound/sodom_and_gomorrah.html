Sodom and Gomorrah

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɑːdəm ənd ɡəˈmɔrə / (AmE), / ˈsɒdəm ənd ɡəˈmɔːrə / (BrE)
A place or situation associated with extreme moral decay, vice, or wickedness, often used figuratively with reference to the biblical cities.
a place of extreme vice or moral decay

Examples

The politician compared the city's nightlife to Sodom and Gomorrah in his speech.
Some people worry that modern culture is becoming like Sodom and Gomorrah.
Their new club had a reputation for wildness, almost reminiscent of Sodom and Gomorrah.

Synset Examples

Her wild behavior reminded me of Sodom and Gomorrah.
The party felt like Sodom and Gomorrah with all the chaos.
He joked that their neighborhood was turning into Sodom and Gomorrah.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: den of iniquity - a place where immoral or wicked things happen
  • Antonym: paradise - an ideal or perfect place (opposite of Sodom and Gomorrah's connotation)

Etymology

From the biblical cities Sodom and Gomorrah, destroyed in the Old Testament for their wickedness.
