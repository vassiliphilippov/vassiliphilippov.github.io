Aztecan language

1. NOUN

/ æzˈtɛkən ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / azˈtɛkən ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Uto-Aztecan language spoken by the Aztec people, particularly Classical Nahuatl
language spoken by the Aztec people

Examples

Many ancient texts were written in Aztecan language, which scholars now study to learn about the Aztec civilization.
There are still communities in Mexico where dialects of the Aztecan language are spoken.
The dictionary contains a section dedicated to the grammar of the Aztecan language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Nahuatl - the Aztecan language spoken by the Nahua people, including the Aztecs

Etymology

From 'Aztec', the name of the people, and 'language'.
