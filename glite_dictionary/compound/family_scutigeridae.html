family Scutigeridae

1. NOUN

/ ˌfæmə.li skuːˌtɪdʒəˈraɪdiː / (AmE), / ˌfæmɪli skuːˌtɪdʒɪˈriːdiː / (BrE)
A family of centipedes within the order Chilopoda, commonly known as house centipedes.
centipede family (Scutigeridae)

Examples

The family Scutigeridae includes several species commonly found in human dwellings.
Scientists recently discovered a new member of the family Scutigeridae in a tropical forest.
Many people recognize the family Scutigeridae for their distinctive long legs and swift movements.

Etymology

From New Latin 'Scutigeridae', from genus 'Scutigera' + '-idae' (family suffix).
