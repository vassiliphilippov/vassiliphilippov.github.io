Western ax

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɛstərn æks / (AmE), / ˈwɛstən æks / (BrE)
a type of ax with cutting edges on both sides of the head
ax with two cutting edges

Examples

The lumberjack preferred a Western ax for felling large trees in the forest.
She admired the old Western ax hanging above the fireplace as a piece of history.
Using a Western ax can be more efficient because both edges stay sharp longer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: double-bit ax - an ax with two cutting edges
