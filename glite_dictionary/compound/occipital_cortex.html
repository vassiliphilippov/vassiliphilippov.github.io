occipital cortex

1. NOUN

/ əkˈsɪp.ɪ.t̬əl ˈkɔr.teks / (AmE), / ɒkˈsɪp.ɪ.təl ˈkɔː.teks / (BrE)
the part of the cerebral cortex located at the back of the head, primarily responsible for visual processing
region of the brain involved in vision
medical

Examples

Damage to the occipital cortex can lead to problems with vision and visual perception.
The occipital cortex is activated when we interpret visual information from our environment.
Researchers are studying the role of the occipital cortex in recognizing faces and objects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: visual cortex - region of the brain responsible for processing visual information

Etymology

From Latin 'occiput' (back of the head) and 'cortex' (bark, rind, outer layer).
