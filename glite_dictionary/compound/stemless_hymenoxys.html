stemless hymenoxys

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɛm.lɪs haɪˈmiːn.ɒk.sɪs / (AmE), / ˈstɛm.lɪs hɪˈmiːn.ɒk.sɪs / (BrE)
A perennial plant native to west central North America, with tufted basal leaves and short, leafless stalks, each bearing a solitary yellow flower head.
a yellow-flowered perennial plant of North America

Examples

The stemless hymenoxys blooms every spring on the dry hillsides of Colorado.
We spotted a patch of stemless hymenoxys growing among the prairie grasses.
Botanists are studying how the stemless hymenoxys adapts to arid conditions.

Etymology

From Latinized genus name Hymenoxys, from Greek 'hymēn' (membrane) and 'oxys' (sharp), referring to floral features.
