sine curve

1. NOUN

/ saɪn kɝv / (AmE), / saɪn kɜːv / (BrE)
a smooth, wave-like curve representing the graph of the sine function, y = sin x
graph of the sine function

Examples

The graph shows a distinct sine curve that repeats every 360 degrees.
In physics, alternating current often follows a sine curve pattern.
Her math teacher asked her to sketch a sine curve on the whiteboard.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sine wave - a periodic oscillating wave having the form of a sine curve

Etymology

From Latin 'sinus' (curve, fold) and English 'curve'.
