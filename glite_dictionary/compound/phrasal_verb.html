phrasal verb

1. NOUN

/ ˈfreɪzəl vɜrb / (AmE), / ˈfreɪzəl vɜːb / (BrE)
A verb combined with one or more particles (such as an adverb or preposition), forming a unit with a meaning different from its individual parts.
verb with one or more particles

Examples

The teacher explained what a phrasal verb is to the class.
Learning phrasal verbs is often challenging for non-native English speakers.
'Look after', in 'Please look after the dog', is an example of a phrasal verb.

Synset Examples

‘turn out’ is a phrasal verb in the question ‘how many turned out to vote?’

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: idiom - expression whose meaning is not predictable from individual words
  • Degree Lower: verb - word that describes an action, state, or occurrence

Etymology

From 'phrase' + 'verb', first used in linguistic analysis in the 20th century.
