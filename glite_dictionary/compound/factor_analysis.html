factor analysis

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæktɚ əˌnæləsɪs / (AmE), / ˈfæktə əˌnæləsɪs / (BrE)
a statistical method for reducing many correlated variables to a smaller set of underlying factors that explain their interrelationships
statistical technique to find underlying factors

Examples

The researchers used factor analysis to identify the key variables influencing employee satisfaction.
By applying factor analysis, the team reduced the number of survey questions to the most significant factors.
Understanding the results of factor analysis requires some background in statistics and data interpretation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: principal component analysis - a related statistical method for dimensionality reduction
  • Synonym: cluster analysis - a statistical method for classifying objects into groups

Etymology

From 'factor' (something that contributes to a result) and 'analysis' (examination), used in statistics since early 20th century.
