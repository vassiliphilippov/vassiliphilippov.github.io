family Aepyornidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ˌiːpiˌɔrˈnɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli ˌiːpɪˌɔːˈnɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of extinct large, flightless birds, commonly known as elephant birds, native to Madagascar and coextensive with the order Aepyorniformes.
family of extinct elephant birds

Examples

The family Aepyornidae included some of the largest birds that ever lived on Earth.
Scientists believe that members of the family Aepyornidae became extinct around 1,000 years ago.
Fossils from the family Aepyornidae have provided valuable insights into Madagascar's prehistoric ecosystem.

Etymology

From scientific Latin 'Aepyornis' (type genus) + '-idae' (taxonomic family suffix).
