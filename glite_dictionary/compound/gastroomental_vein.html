gastroomental vein

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡæstroʊˈmɛntəl veɪn / (AmE), / ˌɡæstrəʊˈmɛntəl veɪn / (BrE)
either of two veins that run along the greater curvature of the stomach, draining blood from the stomach to the portal vein system
vein along the greater curvature of the stomach
medical

Examples

The gastroomental vein is important in draining blood from the stomach's greater curvature.
During surgery, the surgeon carefully avoided damaging the gastroomental vein.
Inflammation was detected near the gastroomental vein on the patient's CT scan.

Etymology

From Greek 'gastro-' (stomach) and Latin 'omentum' (fatty membrane), referring to their anatomical location.
