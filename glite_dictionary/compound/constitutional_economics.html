constitutional economics

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑn.stəˈtu.ʃə.nəl ˌɛk.əˈnɑ.mɪks / (AmE), / ˌkɒn.stɪˈtjuː.ʃən.əl ˌiː.kəˈnɒm.ɪks / (BrE)
A branch of economics that studies how constitutional rules and frameworks affect economic policy and outcomes.
economic study of constitutional frameworks

Examples

The field of constitutional economics examines how constitutions structure the incentives of government officials.
Some policy reforms are best analyzed through the lens of constitutional economics.
He wrote his doctoral dissertation on constitutional economics and its implications for democratic governance.

Synset Examples

Similarly, constitutional economics suggests that in matters of constitutional choice it is essential that participants can choose for themselves through the unanimity rule whether the rules are in their own interest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: public choice theory - economic analysis of political decision-making
  • Degree Lower: constitutional law - body of law which defines the relationship of different entities within a state

Etymology

From 'constitution' (relating to the system of fundamental principles according to which a nation is governed) and 'economics' (the study of production, consumption, and transfer of wealth).
