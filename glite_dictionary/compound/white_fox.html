white fox

1. NOUN

/ waɪt fɑks / (AmE), / waɪt fɒks / (BrE)
a small, thickly-furred fox of Arctic regions that has a brownish coat in summer and a white coat in winter
small Arctic fox with seasonal fur color change

Examples

The white fox blends into the snowy landscape during the winter months.
Scientists are studying how the white fox adapts to extreme cold.
In the summer, the white fox loses its snowy coat and turns brown.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arctic fox - a fox native to Arctic regions with a seasonal coat color change
