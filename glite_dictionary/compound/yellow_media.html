yellow media

1. NOUN

/ ˈjɛl.oʊ ˈmiː.di.ə / (AmE), / ˈjel.əʊ ˈmiː.di.ə / (BrE)
media organizations or outlets that sensationalize news and focus on scandal or gossip to attract attention and profit, often at the expense of accuracy or ethics
sensationalist, scandal-focused media

Examples

Many people believe that yellow media exaggerates stories to boost their ratings and sales.
She criticized the yellow media for spreading rumors without evidence.
Politicians sometimes accuse the yellow media of distorting the facts for publicity.

Synset Examples

Critics pointed out the biases in yellow media coverage.
He avoided yellow media, preferring reliable sources.
Yellow media often sensationalizes political events.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tabloid - a newspaper with smaller pages, typically focused on sensational news
  • Synonym: yellow journalism - journalism that is based on sensationalism and crude exaggeration
  • Antonym: mainstream media - traditional large news organizations, often seen as more restrained

Etymology

From 'yellow journalism,' an American term from the late 19th century for a sensational style of press, itself derived from the 'Yellow Kid' comic in early tabloids.
