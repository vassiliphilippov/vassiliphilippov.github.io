in tandem

1. ADV

/ ɪn ˈtændəm / (AmE), / ɪn ˈtændəm / (BrE)
one behind the other; in single file, with each person or thing following directly behind another.
one behind the other

Examples

The two cyclists rode in tandem along the narrow path through the forest.
During the parade, the horses marched in tandem, creating a striking orderly effect.
The hikers moved in tandem across the bridge to avoid shaking it too much.

Synset Examples

ride tandem on a bicycle built for two
riding horses down the path in tandem

Etymology

From Latin 'tandem' meaning 'at length' or 'finally'; adopted into English via its use in carriage and bicycle terminology.
