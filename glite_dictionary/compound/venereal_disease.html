venereal disease

1. NOUN

/ vəˈnɪriəl dɪˈziz / (AmE), / vɪˈnɪəriəl dɪˈziːz / (BrE)
A disease that is transmitted through sexual intercourse or genital contact.
sexually transmitted infection
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that venereal disease can be prevented by practicing safe sex.
Rates of venereal disease have declined due to increased public health awareness.
Symptoms of a venereal disease may not appear immediately after infection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sexually transmitted disease - disease transmitted through sexual contact

Etymology

From Latin venereus (of sexual love), from Venus, the goddess of love.
