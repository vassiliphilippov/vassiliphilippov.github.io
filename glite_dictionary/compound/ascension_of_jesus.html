Ascension of Jesus

1. NOUN

/ əˈsɛnʃən əv ˈdʒizəs / (AmE), / əˈsɛnʃən əv ˈdʒiːzəs / (BrE)
(in Christianity) the departure of Jesus Christ from Earth into heaven, celebrated on the 40th day after his Resurrection.
the ascent of Jesus to heaven

Examples

The Ascension of Jesus is commemorated by Christians forty days after Easter.
Artworks often depict the Ascension of Jesus as a dramatic moment in Christian history.
Many churches hold special services to mark the Ascension of Jesus.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Resurrection - the rising of Jesus from the dead after crucifixion

Etymology

Ascension from Latin 'ascensio' (a rising), referring to the act of ascending; Jesus from Hebrew 'Yeshua'.
