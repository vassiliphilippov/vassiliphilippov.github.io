body count

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑːdi ˌkaʊnt / (AmE), / ˈbɒdi ˌkaʊnt / (BrE)
the number of people killed, especially during a particular operation, event, or period, often used in military contexts
number of people killed, esp. in conflict

Examples

The official body count after the attack was reported in the evening news.
During the Vietnam War, the military frequently used body count as a measure of success.
The rising body count reflects the intensifying conflict in the region.

Synset Examples

the daily body count increased as the war went on

Related Terms

  • Synonym: death toll - the total number of deaths resulting from a particular cause or event
