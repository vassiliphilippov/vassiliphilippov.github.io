North Celestial Pole

1. NOUN

/ nɔrθ səˈlɛstʃəl poʊl / (AmE), / nɔːθ səˈlɛstɪəl pəʊl / (BrE)
the point in the sky directly above the Earth's North Pole, around which the stars appear to rotate; it is located near Polaris.
the point in the sky above Earth's North Pole

Examples

Astronomers use the North Celestial Pole as a reference point when mapping the night sky.
From the Northern Hemisphere, Polaris is located very close to the North Celestial Pole.
Due to Earth's axial precession, the exact position of the North Celestial Pole shifts over thousands of years.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: South Celestial Pole - the point in the sky above Earth's South Pole
