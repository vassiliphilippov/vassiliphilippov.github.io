Walt Whitman Bridge

1. PROPN

/ wɔlt ˈwɪtmən brɪdʒ / (AmE), / wɔːlt ˈwɪtmən brɪdʒ / (BrE)
a suspension bridge spanning the Delaware River, connecting Philadelphia, Pennsylvania, to Gloucester City, New Jersey
suspension bridge over the Delaware River

Examples

Traffic was backed up for miles on the Walt Whitman Bridge during the morning commute.
The Walt Whitman Bridge offers stunning views of the Philadelphia skyline and the Delaware River.
They decided to cross the Walt Whitman Bridge to visit relatives in New Jersey.

Etymology

Named after the American poet Walt Whitman (1819–1892).
