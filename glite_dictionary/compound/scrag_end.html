scrag end

1. NOUN

/ skræɡ ɛnd / (AmE), / skræɡ end / (BrE)
the lean end of the neck of veal or mutton, used as a cut of meat
lean end of a neck of meat

Examples

The stew was made with a scrag end of lamb, slowly simmered with root vegetables.
We bought a scrag end from the butcher for the casserole recipe.
Despite being a cheaper cut, the scrag end becomes deliciously tender when cooked for hours.

Etymology

From 'scrag' meaning neck, possibly of Scandinavian origin, and 'end'.
