porphyritic rock

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɔrfəˈrɪtɪk rɑk / (AmE), / ˌpɔːfɪˈrɪtɪk rɒk / (BrE)
An igneous rock that contains large crystals (phenocrysts) embedded in a finer-grained matrix or groundmass.
igneous rock with large crystals in finer matrix

Examples

The geologist identified the sample as a porphyritic rock due to its large, embedded crystals.
Many volcanic regions feature porphyritic rock formations distinctive for their mixed texture.
A thin section under the microscope showed the classic texture of a porphyritic rock.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: igneous rock - rock formed by the cooling and solidification of magma or lava

Etymology

From Greek 'porphyra' (purple), referring to the purple color of certain ancient rocks, and the scientific term 'porphyritic'.
