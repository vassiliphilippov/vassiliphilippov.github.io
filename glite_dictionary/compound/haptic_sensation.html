haptic sensation

1. NOUN

/ ˈhæptɪk sɛnˈseɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈhæptɪk sɛnˈseɪʃən / (BrE)
a sensation experienced through the sense of touch, typically localized on the skin
a touch-related skin sensation
medical

Examples

The haptic sensation of velvet is soft and pleasant to the touch.
Blind individuals sometimes rely more on haptic sensation to interpret their environment.
The device uses vibrations to create a realistic haptic sensation during virtual simulations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tactile sensation - a sensation produced by stimuli applied to the skin

Etymology

From Greek haptikos (able to touch) and Latin sensatio (feeling, perception).
