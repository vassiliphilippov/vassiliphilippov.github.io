Abraham's bosom

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪ.brəˌhæmz ˈbʊz.əm / (AmE), / ˈeɪ.brəˌhæmz ˈbʊz.əm / (BrE)
a place of comfort in heaven where the souls of the righteous rest after death, according to some Christian traditions
place of rest for the righteous after death
archaic

Examples

He believed that after death, the souls of the faithful would be received into Abraham's bosom.
The parable in the Bible describes Lazarus being carried to Abraham's bosom.
In ancient texts, Abraham's bosom is depicted as a place of rest for the righteous.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paradise - a place of perfect happiness, often used synonymously for heaven
  • Antonym: hell - the place of punishment for the wicked after death

Etymology

From biblical references in the Gospel of Luke, representing the patriarch Abraham as a symbol of comfort and peace.

2. NOUN

/ əˈbreɪhəmz ˈbʊzəm / (AmE), / əˈbreɪəmz ˈbʊzəm / (BrE)
a place of comfort or paradise, especially the resting place of the righteous after death in biblical tradition
a place of comfort or paradise after death

Examples

After a long illness, the family believed their father had gone to Abraham's bosom.
In his sermon, the priest spoke of the peace found in Abraham's bosom.
The old hymns often mention Abraham's bosom as a symbol of ultimate rest.

Synset Examples

He believed his grandmother was now in Abraham's bosom.
She found solace in the thought of being in Abraham's bosom.
The phrase suggests comfort in being in Abraham's bosom.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paradise - heavenly place of bliss and reward after death
  • Antonym: hell - place of torment and punishment after death

Etymology

From the biblical phrase in the Gospel of Luke describing the place where the righteous dead rest with Abraham.
