common grape vine

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmən ɡreɪp vaɪn / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən ɡreɪp vaɪn / (BrE)
A species of grape native to Europe, widely cultivated for its fruit, which is used for eating and wine production.
European species of grape vine, source of grapes and wine

Examples

The common grape vine thrives in regions with a Mediterranean climate.
European vineyards have cultivated the common grape vine for centuries to produce world-famous wines.
The fruit of the common grape vine is enjoyed both as table grapes and in winemaking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Vitis vinifera - Scientific name for the common grape vine
