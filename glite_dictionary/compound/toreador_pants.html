toreador pants

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɔːriəˈdɔːr pænts / (AmE), / ˌtɔːriˈɑːdɔː pænts / (BrE)
Close-fitting women's trousers that end at the calf, resembling the style worn by bullfighters.
snug calf-length women's trousers

Examples

She wore her favorite toreador pants to the party, pairing them with a bright blouse.
During the 1950s, toreador pants became a popular choice among fashionable women.
The boutique features a wide range of toreador pants in different colors and patterns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: capri pants - short, close-fitting women's trousers ending at the calf or just below the knee

Etymology

From Spanish 'toreador' (bullfighter), referencing the style of trousers worn by bullfighters.
