semi-sweet chocolate

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛmi swit ˈtʃɑklət / (AmE), / ˌsɛmɪ ˈswiːt ˈtʃɒklət / (BrE)
A type of chocolate made with chocolate liquor, cocoa butter, and a moderate amount of sugar, often used in baking.
chocolate with moderate sugar content

Examples

The recipe calls for one cup of semi-sweet chocolate chips.
She melted semi-sweet chocolate to make a delicious ganache.
Bakers often prefer semi-sweet chocolate for cookies and brownies.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: dark chocolate - chocolate with a higher cocoa content and less sugar than milk chocolate
  • Degree Lower: milk chocolate - chocolate containing milk powder or condensed milk and more sugar
