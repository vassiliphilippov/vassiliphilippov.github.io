June grass

1. NOUN

/ dʒun ɡræs / (AmE), / dʒuːn ɡrɑːs / (BrE)
a valuable pasture and lawn grass with tall stalks and slender bright green leaves, found in Europe and central United States; often used in grass mixtures
a species of meadow and lawn grass

Examples

The lawn was newly seeded with June grass for a lush, green appearance in summer.
Farmers often plant June grass in their pastures because of its nutritious qualities for livestock.
Patches of June grass waved in the breeze, their bright green blades glowing in the sunlight.
