porcelain skin

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɔrsəlɪn skɪn / (AmE), / ˈpɔːsəlɪn skɪn / (BrE)
very smooth and pale skin, resembling the appearance of porcelain
very smooth, pale skin

Examples

The actress was admired for her porcelain skin and delicate features.
Years indoors contributed to his porcelain skin and fragile look.
She applied sunscreen daily to maintain her porcelain skin.

Synset Examples

Her porcelain skin glowed under the soft candlelight.
The artist captured her porcelain skin in the painting.
With her porcelain skin, she looked like a model.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pale skin - skin that is lighter in color than usual

Etymology

From 'porcelain', referring to fine, white ceramic, and 'skin'.
