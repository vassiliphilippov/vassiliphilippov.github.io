spotted salamander

1. NOUN

/ ˈspɑːtɪd səˈlæməndɚ / (AmE), / ˈspɒtɪd səˈlæməndə / (BrE)
A species of salamander with dark skin and distinct yellow or orange spots, native to North America and sometimes Europe.
a salamander with yellow or orange spots

Examples

The spotted salamander is often seen near ponds during the spring breeding season.
Children were excited to find a spotted salamander hiding under the damp leaves.
Researchers tagged several spotted salamanders to study their migration patterns.

2. NOUN

/ ˈspɑtɪd səˈlæməndɚ / (AmE), / ˈspɒtɪd səˈlæməndə / (BrE)
a glossy black North American salamander (Ambystoma maculatum) with distinctive yellow spots on its back and sides
North American salamander with yellow spots

Examples

A spotted salamander emerged from under the leaf litter after the spring rains.
Children were excited to find a spotted salamander near the edge of the pond.
The spotted salamander is often seen crossing forest trails at night during breeding season.
