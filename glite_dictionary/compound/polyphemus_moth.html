polyphemus moth

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑːlɪˈfiːməs mɑθ / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈfiːməs mɒθ / (BrE)
a very large yellowish-brown North American silkworm moth (Antheraea polyphemus) with large eyespots on its hind wings; its larvae feed on fruit and shade trees
large North American silkworm moth with eyespots

Examples

We found a polyphemus moth resting on the porch, its eye-like spots glowing in the sunlight.
The caterpillar of the polyphemus moth is known to feed on maple and oak trees.
Many nature enthusiasts are thrilled to spot a polyphemus moth because of its impressive size and beautiful markings.

Etymology

Named after Polyphemus, the one-eyed giant in Greek mythology, likely due to the moth's large eyespots.
