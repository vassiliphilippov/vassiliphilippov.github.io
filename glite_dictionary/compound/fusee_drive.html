fusee drive

1. NOUN

/ fuˈziː draɪv / (AmE), / fjʊˈziː draɪv / (BrE)
A spirally grooved spindle in a clock or watch that helps regulate the diminishing force from an uncoiling mainspring.
clock mechanism regulating spring tension

Examples

The antique watch was fitted with a fusee drive to improve its accuracy.
Repairing the fusee drive required a steady hand and great attention to detail.
Horologists admire the ingenuity behind the fusee drive in eighteenth-century timepieces.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fusee - A conical, spiral-grooved pulley in clocks or watches used to even out the mainspring's force

Etymology

From French 'fusée' (spindle, from its resemblance to a spindle).
