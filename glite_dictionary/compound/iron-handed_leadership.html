iron-handed leadership

1. NOUN

/ ˌaɪɚn ˈhændɪd ˈlidɚˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˌaɪən ˈhændɪd ˈliːdəʃɪp / (BrE)
a style of management or leadership that is strict, harsh, and authoritarian
strict, authoritarian management style

Examples

The company's success was often attributed to her iron-handed leadership during tough times.
Some employees resented his iron-handed leadership, feeling it left no room for debate or flexibility.
While iron-handed leadership can drive results, it may also create a culture of fear and resentment.

Synset Examples

He was known for his iron-handed leadership.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: authoritarianism - enforcement of strict obedience to authority at the expense of personal freedom
  • Antonym: lenient leadership - leadership style that is more permissive and tolerant

Etymology

Figurative use of 'iron-handed' (combining 'iron' and 'handed') to suggest strength or severity, plus 'leadership'.
