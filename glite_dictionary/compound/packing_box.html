packing box

1. NOUN

/ ˈpækɪŋ bɑks / (AmE), / ˈpækɪŋ bɒks / (BrE)
a large container or crate used for packing goods for shipment or storage
large crate for packing goods

Examples

The workers loaded each packing box onto the truck for shipment.
We stored our old books in a sturdy packing box in the basement.
Each packing box was carefully labeled with its contents before being shipped.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crate - a large shipping container, often made of wood
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03877402-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈpækɪŋ bɑks / (AmE), / ˈpækɪŋ bɒks / (BrE)
a small chamber in which packing material is compressed around a moving shaft or piston to create a seal and prevent leakage
chamber for sealing around a shaft or piston

Examples

The engineer inspected the packing box for signs of leakage before starting the machine.
You must replace the worn packing in the packing box to maintain an effective seal.
Routine maintenance of the packing box extends the lifespan of the pump.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stuffing box - a housing containing packing to seal a shaft from leakage
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04352673-n
Synset POS: n
