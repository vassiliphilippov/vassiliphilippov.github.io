Canterbury Tales

1. PROPN

/ ˈkæntərˌbɛri teɪlz / (AmE), / ˈkæntəbəri teɪlz / (BrE)
an unfinished collection of stories written by Geoffrey Chaucer after 1387, widely regarded as a cornerstone of English literature
stories by Chaucer; classic English literature

Examples

I studied The Canterbury Tales in my British literature class this semester.
The Canterbury Tales provide a vivid portrayal of medieval English society.
Many scholars consider Canterbury Tales to be Chaucer's masterpiece.

Etymology

Named after Canterbury, a city in England, and from the title of Geoffrey Chaucer's work.
