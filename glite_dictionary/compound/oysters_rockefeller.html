oysters Rockefeller

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔɪstɚz ˌrɑkəˈfɛlɚ / (AmE), / ˈɔɪstəz ˌrɒkəˈfɛlə / (BrE)
A dish of oysters on the half shell topped with a rich mixture of butter, spinach, seasonings, and breadcrumbs, then baked or broiled.
baked oysters with spinach and butter

Examples

We ordered oysters Rockefeller as an appetizer at the famous New Orleans restaurant.
The chef prepared oysters Rockefeller with fresh spinach and a buttery sauce.
Many seafood lovers consider oysters Rockefeller a classic dish worth trying.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oyster stew - a soup made with oysters, milk or cream, and seasonings

Etymology

Named after John D. Rockefeller, alluding to the dish's rich sauce.
