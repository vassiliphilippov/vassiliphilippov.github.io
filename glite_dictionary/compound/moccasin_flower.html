moccasin flower

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑːkəsɪn ˌflaʊər / (AmE), / ˈmɒkəsɪn ˌflaʊə / (BrE)
a rose-pink woodland orchid (Cypripedium acaule) native to eastern North America, known for its slipper-shaped flower
rose-pink woodland orchid of North America

Examples

The moccasin flower blooms in shaded pine forests during late spring.
She spotted a rare moccasin flower while hiking in the Adirondacks.
Conservation efforts are underway to protect the delicate moccasin flower from habitat loss.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lady's slipper - another common name for orchids of the genus Cypripedium

Etymology

From 'moccasin', alluding to the flower’s slipper-like shape, and 'flower'.
