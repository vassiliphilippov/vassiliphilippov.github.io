cold frame

1. NOUN

/ koʊld freɪm / (AmE), / kəʊld freɪm / (BrE)
a structure with a transparent glass or plastic top, used to protect plants from cold weather and support their growth
structure for protecting plants from cold

Examples

She built a cold frame to start her lettuce seeds early in the season.
Young seedlings are often kept in a cold frame until the risk of frost passes.
Moving the plants from the greenhouse to the cold frame helps them acclimate to outdoor conditions.
