common eland

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmən iˈlænd / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən ˈiːlənd / (BrE)
a large antelope of southern and eastern Africa, with a fawn-colored coat and spiral horns
a large African antelope species

Examples

The common eland is the largest antelope found in Africa's grasslands.
We spotted a herd of common eland grazing near the riverbank during our safari.
Unlike many other antelopes, the common eland can be domesticated and kept for milk and meat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eland - large African antelope with spiral horns
