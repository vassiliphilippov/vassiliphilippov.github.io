Battle of Zama

1. PROPN

/ ˈbæt̬l əv ˈzɑːmə / (AmE), / ˈbætl əv ˈzɑːmə / (BrE)
the decisive battle fought in 202 BC near Zama in North Africa, in which the Roman general Scipio defeated the Carthaginian general Hannibal, effectively ending the Second Punic War
decisive battle ending the Second Punic War

Examples

The Battle of Zama marked the decisive end to Hannibal's campaign against Rome.
Historians often study the tactics used at the Battle of Zama to understand ancient military strategy.
After losing the Battle of Zama, Carthage was forced to surrender to the Romans.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Battle of Cannae - major battle in the Second Punic War where Hannibal defeated the Romans
