Southern Hemisphere

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌðɚn ˈhɛmɪˌsfɪr / (AmE), / ˈsʌðən ˈhɛmɪsfɪə / (BrE)
the half of the Earth that lies south of the equator
half of the Earth south of the equator

Examples

Australia and much of South America are located in the Southern Hemisphere.
Seasons in the Southern Hemisphere are opposite to those in the Northern Hemisphere.
Penguins are native to many regions in the Southern Hemisphere.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Northern Hemisphere - the half of the Earth north of the equator
