white metal

1. NOUN

/ waɪt ˈmɛt̬l̩ / (AmE), / waɪt ˈmɛtl̩ / (BrE)
a silvery alloy, typically based on tin or lead, used especially for bearings and other machine parts subject to friction
silvery alloy for bearings

Examples

The engine's white metal bearings needed to be replaced after years of use.
He specialized in producing white metal castings for the automotive industry.
The company developed a new white metal alloy to reduce friction in moving parts.
