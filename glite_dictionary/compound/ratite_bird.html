ratite bird

1. NOUN

/ ˈræt.aɪt bɝd / (AmE), / ˈræt.aɪt bɜːd / (BrE)
any of a group of large, flightless birds with flat breastbones lacking a keel for the attachment of flight muscles, such as ostriches, emus, and kiwis
large, flightless bird with flat breastbone

Examples

The ostrich is the largest living ratite bird and can run at remarkable speeds.
Unlike most birds, the ratite bird group cannot fly due to their unique skeletal structure.
Kiwis, part of the ratite bird family, are native to New Zealand and are nocturnal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ostrich - large African flightless bird
  • Synonym: emu - large Australian flightless bird
  • Synonym: kiwi - small flightless bird native to New Zealand

Etymology

From Latin 'ratis' (raft), referencing the flat, raft-like shape of the breastbone.
