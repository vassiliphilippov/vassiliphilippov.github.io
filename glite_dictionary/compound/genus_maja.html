genus Maja

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒiː.nəs ˈmaɪ.ə / (AmE), / ˌdʒiː.nəs ˈmɑː.jə / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Majidae, comprising various species of spider crabs distributed almost worldwide.
type genus of spider crabs (Majidae)

Examples

The genus Maja includes several species of spider crabs found in temperate and tropical waters.
Marine biologists often study the genus Maja to understand spider crab evolution.
Specimens from the genus Maja are notable for their distinctive spiny carapaces.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Majidae - The family of spider crabs to which genus Maja belongs

Etymology

From Latin 'genus' (kind) and 'Maja', the Latin name for this group of crabs.
