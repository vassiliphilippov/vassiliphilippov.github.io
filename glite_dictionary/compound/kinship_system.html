kinship system

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɪnʃɪp ˌsɪstəm / (AmE), / ˈkɪnʃɪp ˌsɪstəm / (BrE)
(Anthropology) The system of social relationships and rules defining kinship in a particular culture, including the terms used and the obligations involved.
system of social relationships in a culture

Examples

Every culture develops its own kinship system to define relationships and expectations among family members.
In anthropology, the study of the kinship system reveals much about a society's structure and values.
The kinship system determines who is considered family and who can inherit property.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kinship - the state of being related to others
