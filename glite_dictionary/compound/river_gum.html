river gum

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɪvɚ ɡʌm / (AmE), / ˈrɪvə ɡʌm / (BrE)
a tall eucalyptus tree, chiefly found along rivers in Australia, with somewhat crooked branches and durable reddish timber used in heavy construction
Australian eucalyptus tree by rivers

Examples

The river gum provides essential shade along the banks of the Murray River.
Many old bridges in Australia were built using the strong wood from river gums.
A flock of cockatoos landed in the crooked branches of the river gum near the water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: red gum - a type of eucalyptus tree with reddish timber
