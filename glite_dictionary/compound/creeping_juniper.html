creeping juniper

1. NOUN

/ ˈkripɪŋ ˈdʒuːnəpɚ / (AmE), / ˈkriːpɪŋ ˈdʒuːnɪpə / (BrE)
a low, spreading shrub of Canada and northern United States, turning bronzed purple in winter
a low, spreading shrub

Examples

The creeping juniper added evergreen beauty to the rocky hillside in all seasons.
Gardeners often use creeping juniper as ground cover because of its hardiness.
In winter, the foliage of creeping juniper takes on a distinctive bronzed purple hue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: juniper - an evergreen shrub or tree with berry-like cones
