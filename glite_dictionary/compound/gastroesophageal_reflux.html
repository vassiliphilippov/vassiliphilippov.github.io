gastroesophageal reflux

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡæs.troʊ.ɪˌsoʊ.fəˈdʒi.əl riˈflʌks / (AmE), / ˌɡæstrəʊɪˌsəʊfəˈdiːəl rɪˈflʌks / (BrE)
A condition in which stomach contents flow back into the esophagus, often causing heartburn or discomfort.
backflow of stomach contents into the esophagus
medical

Examples

Some people experience gastroesophageal reflux after eating a large or spicy meal.
Chronic gastroesophageal reflux can lead to inflammation of the esophagus.
Medications can help manage gastroesophageal reflux and reduce discomfort.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acid reflux - backflow of stomach acid into the esophagus
  • Synonym: heartburn - a burning sensation in the chest caused by acid reflux

Etymology

From 'gastro-' (stomach) + 'esophageal' (of the esophagus) + 'reflux' (flowing back).
