rubella panencephalitis

1. NOUN

/ ruˈbɛlə ˌpænˌɛnsəfəˈlaɪtɪs / (AmE), / ruˈbɛlə ˌpænˌɛnsɪfəˈlaɪtɪs / (BrE)
A rare progressive disorder in adolescents involving loss of mental and physical abilities, associated with infection by the rubella virus.
rare neurological disorder linked to rubella
medical

Examples

The neurologist suspected rubella panencephalitis in the patient with recent cognitive decline.
Vaccination can help prevent diseases like rubella panencephalitis in the population.
Because rubella panencephalitis is so rare, diagnosis can be challenging for clinicians.

Etymology

From Latin 'rubella' (meaning 'little red') for the virus, and Greek 'pan-' (all), 'enkephalos' (brain), and '-itis' (inflammation).
