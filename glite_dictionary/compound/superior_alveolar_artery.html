superior alveolar artery

1. NOUN

/ suːˈpɪriər ælˈvioʊlər ˈɑːr.tɚ.i / (AmE), / suːˈpɪəriə ælˈviːələr ˈɑː.tər.i / (BrE)
an artery that supplies blood to the upper teeth
artery supplying the upper teeth
medical

Examples

The superior alveolar artery is essential for delivering oxygenated blood to the upper jaw.
Damage to the superior alveolar artery can cause excessive bleeding during dental procedures.
Dentists must be careful not to injure the superior alveolar artery when extracting upper teeth.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inferior alveolar artery - an artery supplying the lower teeth

Etymology

From Latin 'alveolus' (a small cavity) and 'arteria' (artery).
