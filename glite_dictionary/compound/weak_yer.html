weak yer

1. NOUN

/ wik jɪr / (AmE), / wiːk jəː / (BrE)
A semivowel in early Slavic languages, reduced to an ultrashort vowel and eventually disappearing in many modern Slavic languages.
ultrashort, disappearing Slavic vowel

Examples

The alternation of weak yers and strong yers determined the fate of certain vowels in Slavic words.
Linguists often analyze Old Russian texts to identify where weak yers have disappeared.
Recognizing a weak yer is crucial when reconstructing the vowel system of Proto-Slavic.

Synset Examples

Counting from the last yer in a word, the final yer is weak, the previous yer is strong, the previous yer is weak, etc., until a full vowel is reached, and then the pattern is started again with alternating weak then strong yers.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: strong yer - a yer that was retained as a full vowel in Slavic languages

Etymology

From Old Church Slavonic 'yer', a letter used for certain reduced vowels in early Slavic alphabets.
