mescal button

1. NOUN

/ mɛˈskæl ˈbʌtn̩ / (AmE), / mɛˈskæl ˈbʌtn̩ / (BrE)
the rounded, button-like top of the mescal cactus (peyote), which contains psychoactive substances used for their hallucinogenic effects
top of mescal cactus; hallucinogenic source

Examples

Native American rituals sometimes include the use of mescal buttons for spiritual visions.
The shaman dried several mescal buttons before preparing them for ingestion.
Researchers analyzed the chemical composition of the mescal button to better understand its psychoactive properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peyote - a small, spineless cactus containing psychoactive alkaloids

Etymology

From 'mescal', a variant of 'mezcal', from Nahuatl 'mexcalli' (cooked maguey), and 'button' for its rounded shape. Not to be confused with the alcoholic beverage mezcal.
