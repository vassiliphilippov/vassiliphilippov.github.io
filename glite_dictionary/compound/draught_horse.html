draught horse

1. NOUN

/ dræft hɔrs / (AmE), / drɑːft hɔːs / (BrE)
A large, strong horse bred and used for pulling heavy loads.
horse bred for pulling heavy loads
british

Examples

The farmer relied on his draught horse to plough the fields every spring.
Several breeds of draught horse are popular for their strength and calm temperament.
In the parade, an impressive draught horse pulled an old wooden cart.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: draft horse - American spelling for a horse bred for heavy work

Etymology

From 'draught' (British spelling for 'draft'), referring to pulling or drawing loads.
