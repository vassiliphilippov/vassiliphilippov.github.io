American chestnut

1. NOUN

/ əˈmɛɹɪkən ˈtʃɛstnət / (AmE), / əˈmɛɹɪkən ˈtʃɛsnʌt / (BrE)
a large deciduous tree (Castanea dentata) native to eastern North America, formerly abundant from Maine to Alabama, known for its edible nuts and durable wood
large North American chestnut tree

Examples

Many forests in the eastern United States were once dominated by the American chestnut.
The blight nearly wiped out the American chestnut in the twentieth century.
Researchers are working to develop a blight-resistant American chestnut tree.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chestnut - a deciduous tree of the genus Castanea
