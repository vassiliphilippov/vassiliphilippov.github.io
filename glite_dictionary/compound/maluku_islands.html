Maluku Islands

1. PROPN

/ məˈluku ˈaɪləndz / (AmE), / məˈluːku ˈaɪləndz / (BrE)
a group of islands in eastern Indonesia, historically known as the Spice Islands, famous for spice production and European colonization.
group of islands in eastern Indonesia; Spice Islands

Examples

The Maluku Islands were once at the center of the global spice trade.
Many explorers risked their lives to reach the Maluku Islands in search of cloves and nutmeg.
Today, the Maluku Islands offer beautiful beaches and a rich cultural heritage for visitors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Spice Islands - historical name for the Maluku Islands

Etymology

From Malay 'Maluku', the local name for the islands; 'islands' from Old English 'īegland'.
