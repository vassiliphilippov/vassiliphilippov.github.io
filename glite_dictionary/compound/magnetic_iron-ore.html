magnetic iron-ore

1. NOUN

/ mægˈnɛtɪk ˈaɪɚn ɔr / (AmE), / mægˈnɛtɪk ˈaɪən ɔː / (BrE)
A mineral form of iron oxide (mainly magnetite) that is strongly attracted by magnets and is an important source of iron.
iron oxide mineral attracted by magnets

Examples

Geologists discovered a deposit of magnetic iron-ore deep beneath the mountain.
The steel industry relies heavily on magnetic iron-ore as a raw material.
A piece of magnetic iron-ore will cling tightly to a magnet due to its high iron content.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: magnetite - a black iron oxide mineral strongly magnetic
