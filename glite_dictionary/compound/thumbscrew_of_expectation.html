thumbscrew of expectation

1. NOUN

/ ˈθʌmˌskruː əv ˌɛkspɛkˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈθʌmˌskruː əv ˌɛkspɛkˈteɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
a metaphorical expression describing pressure or stress inflicted by the expectations of others
pressure caused by expectations

Examples

The thumbscrew of expectation tightened as the deadline approached.
He struggled under the thumbscrew of expectation at his new job.
Many students feel the thumbscrew of expectation from their families during exam season.

Synset Examples

She felt the thumbscrew of expectations from her peers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pressure - continuous physical or psychological force exerted on or against an object or person
  • Synonym: stress - mental or emotional strain or tension resulting from adverse or demanding circumstances

Etymology

From 'thumbscrew', an instrument of torture, used figuratively; 'expectation' meaning beliefs about what someone should do.
