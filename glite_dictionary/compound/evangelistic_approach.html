evangelistic approach

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌvæn.dʒəˈlɪs.tɪk əˈproʊtʃ / (AmE), / ɪˌvæn.dʒəˈlɪs.tɪk əˈprəʊtʃ / (BrE)
a passionate and persuasive method of promoting or spreading ideas, beliefs, or causes
passionate method of promoting ideas

Examples

Her evangelistic approach to charity work inspired many people to get involved.
The company adopted an evangelistic approach to promote its new technology.
Critics argued that his evangelistic approach sometimes overshadowed practical details.

Synset Examples

His evangelistic approach attracted many new followers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: persuasive - good at convincing others to do or believe something
  • Synonym: zealous - showing great energy and enthusiasm
  • Antonym: dispassionate - not influenced by strong emotion or enthusiasm

Etymology

Evangelistic from Greek 'euangelion' (good news); 'approach' from Latin 'appropiare' (to draw near).
