azo group

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪzoʊ ɡruːp / (AmE), / ˈɑːzəʊ ɡruːp / (BrE)
a chemical group consisting of two nitrogen atoms joined by a double bond (–N=N–), attached to two hydrocarbon groups
chemical group –N=N– in organic compounds
medical

Examples

The dye contains an azo group that gives it a vivid red color.
Many synthetic pigments are based on the azo group structure.
The chemist studied the reactivity of the azo group in different conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: azo compound - an organic compound containing an azo group

Etymology

From French 'azote' (nitrogen) + 'group.'
