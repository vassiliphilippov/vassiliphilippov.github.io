igneous rock

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪɡniəs rɑk / (AmE), / ˈɪɡni.əs rɒk / (BrE)
Rock formed by the cooling and solidification of molten magma or lava.
rock formed from solidified magma

Examples

Granite is an example of an igneous rock that forms deep underground.
When volcanoes erupt, the lava cools to create new igneous rocks.
Geologists study igneous rock to learn about the Earth's interior processes.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sedimentary rock - rock formed by deposition and cementation of material at the Earth's surface
  • Antonym: metamorphic rock - rock formed by transformation of existing rock under heat and pressure

Etymology

From Latin 'igneus' meaning 'of fire' + English 'rock'.
