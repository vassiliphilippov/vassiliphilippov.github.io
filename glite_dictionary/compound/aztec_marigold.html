Aztec marigold

1. NOUN

/ ˈæz.tɛk ˈmɛr.i.ˌgoʊld / (AmE), / ˈæz.tɛk ˈmær.ɪ.ˌɡəʊld / (BrE)
a stout, branching annual plant with large yellow to orange flower heads, native to Mexico and Central America, often grown as an ornamental
ornamental plant with large yellow-orange flowers

Examples

Many gardeners plant Aztec marigold to add vibrant color to their flower beds in summer.
The traditional festival was adorned with Aztec marigold blooms, symbolizing the sun and life.
She cultivated Aztec marigold in her greenhouse, enjoying their large, golden blossoms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marigold - a plant with bright yellow or orange flowers, genus Tagetes

Etymology

Named after the Aztec people of Mexico and marigold, referring to its genus Tagetes.
