standard temperature

1. NOUN

/ ˈstændɚd ˈtɛmpəˌrətʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈstændəd ˈtɛmpərətʃə / (BrE)
a temperature of exactly zero degrees Celsius, used as a reference point in scientific contexts
reference temperature: 0°C (Celsius)

Examples

Chemists often use standard temperature and pressure when carrying out experiments.
At standard temperature, water freezes and ice melts.
Gas volumes are typically measured at standard temperature for consistency.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: standard pressure - reference atmospheric pressure in scientific contexts
