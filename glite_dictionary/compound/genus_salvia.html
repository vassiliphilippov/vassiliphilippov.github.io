genus Salvia

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˈsæl.vi.ə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈsæl.vi.ə / (BrE)
A large genus of shrubs and subshrubs in the mint family, including sage, widely grown for their aromatic leaves and flowers.
plant genus; includes sage

Examples

Many varieties within the genus Salvia are popular choices for herb gardens due to their aromatic leaves.
Botanists have documented over 900 species in the genus Salvia worldwide.
The ornamental flowers of the genus Salvia attract bees and butterflies to the garden.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sage - an aromatic herb of the genus Salvia, used in cooking and medicine

Etymology

From Latin 'salvia', referring to sage, and 'genus' meaning 'kind' or 'type.'
