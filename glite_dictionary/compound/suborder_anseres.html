suborder Anseres

1. NOUN

/ ˌsʌbˈɔrdər ænˈsiːriːz / (AmE), / ˌsʌbˈɔːdə ˈænsɪriːz / (BrE)
a former zoological classification that included all birds now classified in the family Anatidae, such as ducks, geese, and swans
old zoological grouping for ducks, geese, swans

Examples

The suborder Anseres was once used to classify all true waterfowl.
Many species now in Anatidae were historically grouped under the suborder Anseres.
Taxonomists have revised the classification, making suborder Anseres largely obsolete.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Anatidae - family of birds including ducks, geese, and swans

Etymology

From Latin *anser* (goose), used in scientific taxonomy.
