agate line

1. NOUN

/ ˈæɡət laɪn / (AmE), / ˈæɡət laɪn / (BrE)
A unit of measurement for newspaper advertising space, equal to one line of print one column wide and 1/14 inch deep.
advertising space measurement unit

Examples

The advertisement cost was calculated based on the number of agate lines it occupied.
She purchased a small classified ad, which only took up five agate lines in the newspaper.
Newspaper rates were quoted in terms of dollars per agate line per day.

Etymology

Named after the 'agate' type size historically used in newspaper printing.
