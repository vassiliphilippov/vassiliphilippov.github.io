boracic acid

1. NOUN

/ bəˈræsɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / bəˈræsɪk ˈæsɪd / (BrE)
a white, crystalline weak acid containing boron and oxygen, often used as an antiseptic or preservative
a weak acid containing boron and oxygen
medical

Examples

Researchers commonly use boracic acid in laboratories as a buffer or antiseptic agent.
The doctor applied a solution of boracic acid to the patient's wound to prevent infection.
Manufacturers sometimes use boracic acid in the production of glass and ceramics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boric acid - another name for boracic acid

Etymology

From borax (the mineral from which it is obtained) + -ic, via French and Medieval Latin.
