genus Lepus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˈliːpəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈliːpəs / (BrE)
The type genus of the Leporidae family, comprising the hares.
genus of hares in zoology

Examples

The genus Lepus includes several species commonly known as hares.
Scientists debate the evolutionary relationships within the genus Lepus.
Unlike rabbits, members of the genus Lepus are born with fur and open eyes.

Etymology

Latin *lepus* (hare).
