internal drive

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtɝːnəl draɪv / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɜːnəl draɪv / (BrE)
a data storage device installed inside a computer's case, such as a hard drive or solid-state drive
data storage device inside a computer

Examples

To upgrade storage, he installed a larger internal drive in his desktop computer.
An internal drive usually transfers data faster than an external USB drive.
After a hardware failure, the technician replaced the faulty internal drive with a new solid-state model.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: external drive - a data storage device connected outside a computer's case
