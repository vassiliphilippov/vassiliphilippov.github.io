in and out of

1. ADP

/ ɪn ənd aʊt əv / (AmE), / ɪn ənd aʊt ɒv / (BrE)
often entering and leaving a place, situation, or activity repeatedly or briefly over a period of time
frequently entering and leaving

Examples

She was in and out of the hospital for months after her surgery.
The manager kept in and out of the office throughout the afternoon.
Kids were in and out of the house all day during the summer holidays.

Synset Examples

He was in and out of meetings all day.
They were in and out of the meeting in an hour.
