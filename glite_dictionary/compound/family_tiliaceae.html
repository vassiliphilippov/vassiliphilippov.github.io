family Tiliaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæmɪli tɪliˈeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli tɪlɪˈeɪsɪˌiː / (BrE)
a family of chiefly trees and shrubs found in tropical and temperate regions, especially in southeastern Asia and Brazil, including genera such as Tilia and Grewia
a plant family of trees and shrubs
medical

Examples

Many botanists classify the linden tree under the family Tiliaceae.
Plants in the family Tiliaceae are often found in tropical regions across the globe.
The family Tiliaceae includes several genera valued for their fiber and ornamental uses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Tilia - a genus of trees in the family Tiliaceae, also known as linden or lime trees

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Tilia (the linden genus) + -aceae (family suffix).
