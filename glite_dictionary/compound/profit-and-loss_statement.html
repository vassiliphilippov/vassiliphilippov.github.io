profit-and-loss statement

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɑːfɪt ənd lɔːs ˈsteɪtmənt / (AmE), / ˈprɒfɪt ənd lɒs ˈsteɪtmənt / (BrE)
A financial statement summarizing a company's revenues, expenses, and profits or losses over a particular period.
company's financial performance summary

Examples

The company's annual profit-and-loss statement showed a significant increase in revenue compared to the previous year.
Before approving the loan, the bank requested the latest profit-and-loss statement from the business owner.
Auditors reviewed the profit-and-loss statement to assess the firm's financial health.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: income statement - another name for profit-and-loss statement
