Comstock's mealybug

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑm.stɑks ˈmiː.liˌbʌɡ / (AmE), / ˈkɒm.stɒks ˈmiː.liˌbʌɡ / (BrE)
an Asiatic insect species accidentally introduced into the United States, known as a pest on citrus and apple trees
a pest insect found on citrus and apple trees

Examples

Farmers are concerned about the spread of Comstock's mealybug in citrus orchards.
Researchers developed new methods to control Comstock's mealybug on apple trees.
Quarantine measures were put in place to stop Comstock's mealybug from spreading.

Etymology

Named after American entomologist John Henry Comstock.
