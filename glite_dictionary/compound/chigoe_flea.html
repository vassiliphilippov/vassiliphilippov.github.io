chigoe flea

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɪɡoʊ ˌfli / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪɡəʊ ˌfliː / (BrE)
a small tropical flea (Tunga penetrans) whose fertile female burrows under the skin of humans and other mammals, causing irritation or infection
tropical flea that burrows under skin
medical

Examples

Travelers are warned to protect their feet from chigoe fleas when visiting certain tropical regions.
The bite of a chigoe flea can cause swelling and intense itching.
Health officials urged villagers to take steps to avoid chigoe flea infestations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sand flea - another common name for Tunga penetrans, the chigoe flea

Etymology

From West Indian Spanish 'chique', of indigenous origin.
