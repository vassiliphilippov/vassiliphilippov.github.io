nontextual matter

1. NOUN

/ nɑnˈtɛkstʃuəl ˈmætər / (AmE), / nɒnˈtɛkstʃuəl ˈmætə / (BrE)
photographs, illustrations, or other visual materials in a printed publication, as distinct from the written text
visual material in printed works

Examples

The nontextual matter includes photographs and maps to illustrate the information in the book.
Editors must ensure that all nontextual matter is properly credited and reproduced at high quality.
The thesis contains extensive nontextual matter, such as charts and diagrams, to support the research findings.

Synset Examples

the publisher was responsible for all the artwork in the book

Related Terms

  • Synonym: artwork - illustrations or decorative images in printed materials
