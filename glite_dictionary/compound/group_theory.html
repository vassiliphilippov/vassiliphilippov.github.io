group theory

1. NOUN

/ ɡruːp ˈθɪəri / (AmE), / ɡruːp ˈθɪəri / (BrE)
the branch of mathematics that studies the algebraic structures known as groups and their properties
mathematical study of groups

Examples

He specialized in group theory during his graduate studies in mathematics.
Group theory has applications in physics, chemistry, and computer science.
Sophia gave a lecture on the basics of group theory to undergraduate students.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abstract algebra - the branch of mathematics that studies algebraic structures in general
  • Degree Lower: group - an algebraic structure consisting of a set with a binary operation

Etymology

Formed from 'group', from French 'groupe' (circle, grouping), and 'theory', from Greek 'theoria' (contemplation, speculation).
