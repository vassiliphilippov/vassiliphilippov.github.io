aluminium hydroxide

1. NOUN

/ əˌluːˌmɪniəm haɪˈdrɑksaɪd / (AmE), / ˌaljʊˈmɪniəm hʌɪˈdrɒksʌɪd / (BrE)
a white crystalline compound, Al(OH)₃, that occurs naturally as the mineral gibbsite and is used in antacids and water purification
a white crystalline chemical compound
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed an aluminium hydroxide antacid to relieve her heartburn.
Aluminium hydroxide is often added to drinking water to remove impurities.
Gibbsite is the mineral form of aluminium hydroxide found in bauxite deposits.

Etymology

From aluminium (element name) and hydroxide (from Greek 'hydro-' meaning water and 'oxys' meaning sharp, acid).
