poker alumroot

1. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊkər ˈælmˌruːt / (AmE), / ˈpəʊkə ˈælmˌruːt / (BrE)
A plant native to western North America, having leathery heart-shaped leaves at the base of long stalks and greenish-white flowers clustered along the upper part.
North American plant with heart-shaped leaves and clustered flowers

Examples

The poker alumroot thrives in the rocky slopes of western North America.
Gardeners appreciate the leathery leaves and tall flower stalks of the poker alumroot.
During the spring, the poker alumroot produces clusters of greenish-white flowers high above its foliage.

Etymology

From 'alumroot,' a name used for plants in the genus Heuchera, combined with 'poker' likely referring to the plant's tall flower stalks resembling a poker.
