lunate bone

1. NOUN

/ ˈluː.neɪt boʊn / (AmE), / ˈluː.neɪt bəʊn / (BrE)
one of the eight small carpal bones in the wrist, shaped like a half-moon
a crescent-shaped wrist bone
medical

Examples

The lunate bone is centrally located in the wrist and helps with hand movement.
Fractures of the lunate bone are rare but can lead to chronic wrist pain if untreated.
On the X-ray, the doctor identified a dislocation of the lunate bone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carpal bone - any of the eight small bones that form the wrist

Etymology

From Latin lunatus (crescent-shaped), referring to its shape.
