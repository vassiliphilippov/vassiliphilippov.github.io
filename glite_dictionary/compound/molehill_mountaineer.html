molehill mountaineer

1. NOUN

/ ˈmoʊlˌhɪl ˌmaʊn.təˈnɪr / (AmE), / ˈməʊlˌhɪl ˌmaʊn.tɪˈnɪə / (BrE)
a person who habitually exaggerates minor problems or issues
person who exaggerates minor issues

Examples

My mother is such a molehill mountaineer when it comes to the smallest inconveniences.
Don't be a molehill mountaineer; the problem isn't as big as you think.
Among the staff, he's known as the molehill mountaineer for always blowing things out of proportion.

Synset Examples

Stop being a molehill mountaineer; it's not that serious.
His tendency to exaggerate made him a molehill mountaineer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to make a mountain out of a molehill - to exaggerate the importance or seriousness of a minor issue

Etymology

Formed by combining 'molehill' and 'mountaineer' as a play on the idiom 'make a mountain out of a molehill.'
