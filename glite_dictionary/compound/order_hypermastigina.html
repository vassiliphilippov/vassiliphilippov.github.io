order Hypermastigina

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdər ˌhaɪpərmæˈstɪdʒɪnə / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə ˌhaɪpəmæˈstɪdʒɪnə / (BrE)
A group of flagellate protozoans that produce cellulose, classified in the order Hypermastigina.
a group of cellulose-producing flagellate protozoa

Examples

The order Hypermastigina includes protozoa that play a vital role in the digestion of wood by termites.
Researchers discovered new species within the order Hypermastigina in the guts of tropical wood-eating insects.
Members of the order Hypermastigina possess multiple flagella to aid their movement and cellulose digestion.

Etymology

From New Latin: 'Hypermastigina', referring to the group of flagellate protozoans; 'order' from Latin 'ordo' (arrangement, rank).
