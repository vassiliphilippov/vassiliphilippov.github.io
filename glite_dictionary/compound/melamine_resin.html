melamine resin

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛləˌmiːn ˈrɛzən / (AmE), / ˈmɛləmiːn ˈrɛzɪn / (BrE)
a hard, thermosetting plastic material made by combining melamine with an aldehyde, used in molded objects, adhesives, and surface coatings
a thermosetting plastic material

Examples

The kitchen counter was made from melamine resin, making it durable and easy to clean.
Manufacturers often use melamine resin to produce laminates and decorative panels.
Furniture coated with melamine resin resists scratches and stains better than untreated wood.

Etymology

Melamine from German Melamin, combined with resin (from Latin resina).
