adjutant general

1. NOUN

/ ˌædʒ.ə.tənt ˈdʒen.ə.rəl / (AmE), / ˌæd.jʊ.tənt ˈdʒen.ər.əl / (BrE)
the chief administrative officer in an army, or a senior staff officer who assists a commanding general
chief military administrative officer

Examples

The adjutant general was responsible for all personnel records and administrative orders.
After the ceremony, the adjutant general addressed the officers and outlined the new policies.
Each state in the U.S. National Guard has its own adjutant general overseeing military operations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adjutant - a military officer who acts as an administrative assistant to a senior officer

Etymology

From French 'adjudant général', from Latin 'adiutare' (to help) and 'general'.
