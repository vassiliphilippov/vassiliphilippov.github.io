adzuki bean

1. NOUN

/ ædˈzuːki ˌbin / (AmE), / adˈzuːki ˌbiːn / (BrE)
a small reddish-brown bean native to East Asia, widely used in Chinese and Japanese cooking, especially for making sweets and flour
small reddish-brown East Asian bean

Examples

The sweet paste made from adzuki beans is a popular filling in Japanese desserts.
She added cooked adzuki beans to her salad for extra protein.
Adzuki bean flour is frequently used in gluten-free baking.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: mung bean - a small green bean used in Asian cuisine, related legume

Etymology

From Japanese 'azuki'.
