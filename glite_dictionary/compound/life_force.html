life force

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪf fɔrs / (AmE), / ˈlaɪf fɔːs / (BrE)
a hypothetical vital energy or force, not physical or chemical, once believed to drive the development and evolution of living organisms
vital force believed to animate life

Examples

Some ancient philosophies describe a life force that animates all living beings.
Henri Bergson theorized that a life force was responsible for evolution.
Although no evidence supports the existence of a life force, the idea has influenced art and literature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: élan vital - Henri Bergson's term for a vital force driving life and evolution
