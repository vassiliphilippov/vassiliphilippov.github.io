hair stroke

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛr ˌstroʊk / (AmE), / ˈheə ˌstrəʊk / (BrE)
a very fine or thin line in writing, calligraphy, or printing, typically produced with a light touch
very fine line in writing or printing

Examples

A skilled calligrapher can create a hair stroke that is barely visible to the naked eye.
The font designer paid special attention to the thickness of each hair stroke in the new typeface.
Light pressure on the pen produces a delicate hair stroke in cursive writing.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: downstroke - a downward stroke in writing, often heavier than a hair stroke
  • Synonym: upstroke - an upward stroke in writing, sometimes lighter like a hair stroke
