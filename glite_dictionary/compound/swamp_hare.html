swamp hare

1. NOUN

/ swɑmp hɛr / (AmE), / swɒmp heə / (BrE)
a wood rabbit native to swamps and lowlands of the southeastern United States
a wood rabbit of U.S. southeastern swamps

Examples

The swamp hare inhabits the densely vegetated wetlands of the southeastern United States.
Biologists conducted a survey to estimate the population size of the local swamp hare.
Despite its name, the swamp hare is actually more closely related to rabbits than to true hares.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marsh rabbit - a similar rabbit species native to marshy areas of the southeastern United States
