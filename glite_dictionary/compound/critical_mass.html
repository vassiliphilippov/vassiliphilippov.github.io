critical mass

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɪdɪkəl mæs / (AmE), / ˈkrɪtɪkəl mæs / (BrE)
the minimum size or amount of something required to start or sustain a process or venture successfully
minimum amount needed for success

Examples

The company finally reached critical mass and began to expand rapidly.
Many social movements struggle until they achieve a critical mass of supporters.
Without a critical mass of subscribers, the service could not survive.

Synset Examples

the battle for the computer market has now reached critical mass
there is now a critical mass of successful women to take the lead
they sold the business because it lacked critical mass

Related Terms

  • Synonym: threshold - the level at which something starts to happen

Etymology

Originally a term in nuclear physics; 'critical' from Greek 'kritikos' (able to judge) + 'mass' from Latin 'massa' (lump, mass).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkrɪdɪkəl mæs / (AmE), / ˈkrɪtɪkəl mɑːs / (BrE)
the minimum amount of fissile material needed to sustain a nuclear chain reaction
minimum mass for nuclear chain reaction

Examples

The reactor must be loaded with enough uranium to reach critical mass and start the process.
Scientists calculated the critical mass required for a controlled nuclear reaction.
Below critical mass, a fission chain reaction cannot sustain itself.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: supercritical mass - amount of fissile material exceeding the minimum needed for a chain reaction
  • Degree Lower: subcritical mass - amount of fissile material below the level needed for a chain reaction

3. NOUN

/ ˌkrɪdɪkəl ˈmæs / (AmE), / ˌkrɪtɪkəl ˈmæs / (BrE)
the minimum size, amount, or level needed for a particular result or effect to be achieved or to make something self-sustaining
minimum amount needed for effect or self-sustenance

Examples

The startup needed to attract a critical mass of users to ensure its long-term survival.
After years of slow growth, the movement finally reached critical mass and began to influence policy decisions.
Once the community achieved critical mass, collaboration flourished and new ideas emerged rapidly.

Synset Examples

We need to reach critical mass to launch the new product.
The project gained critical mass after the latest funding.
Activism can build a critical mass for social change.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tipping point - the point at which a series of small changes becomes significant enough to cause a larger, more important change

Etymology

From physics, where it described the smallest mass of fissile material for a sustained nuclear reaction.
