International Society for Krishna Consciousness

1. PROPN

/ ˌɪntɚˈnæʃənəl səˈsaɪəti fɔr ˈkrɪʃnə kənˈʃɪsnəs / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈnæʃənəl səˈsaɪəti fə ˈkrɪʃnə kənˈʃɪsnəs / (BrE)
A religious movement founded in the United States in 1966, based on Vedic scriptures and the worship of Krishna; known for joyful mantra chanting, distinctive dress, vegetarianism, and celibacy.
Hindu-based religious movement founded in 1966

Examples

The International Society for Krishna Consciousness has temples in many countries around the world.
After joining the International Society for Krishna Consciousness, she became deeply involved in devotional practices.
The International Society for Krishna Consciousness is widely known for its public chanting and community outreach.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hare Krishna - member or follower of the International Society for Krishna Consciousness
