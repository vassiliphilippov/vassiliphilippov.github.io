genus Trifolium

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs traɪˈfoʊ.li.əm / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs traɪˈfəʊ.li.əm / (BrE)
A genus of leguminous plants, commonly known as clovers, with leaves divided into three leaflets.
genus of clovers (leguminous plants)

Examples

The meadows were covered with species belonging to the genus Trifolium.
Farmers often plant members of the genus Trifolium to enrich the soil with nitrogen.
Red clover is one of the most widespread plants in the genus Trifolium.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clover - a common name for plants in the genus Trifolium

Etymology

From Latin 'trifolium' ('three-leaved'), referring to the characteristic leaf structure.
