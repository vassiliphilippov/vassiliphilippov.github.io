coyol palm

1. NOUN

/ koʊˈjɔl pɑm / (AmE), / kɔɪˈɒl pɑːm / (BrE)
A tropical American palm tree (Attalea cohune) that produces edible nuts and a valuable fiber.
tropical palm with edible nuts and fiber

Examples

The coyol palm is valued by local communities for its edible nuts and sturdy fiber.
Artisans weave baskets using fibers from the coyol palm in Central America.
With its graceful appearance, the coyol palm adds beauty and utility to tropical landscapes.

Etymology

From Spanish 'coyol', originally from indigenous languages in Central America.
