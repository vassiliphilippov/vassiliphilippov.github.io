field work

1. NOUN

/ ˈfiːld wɝːk / (AmE), / ˈfiːld wɜːk / (BrE)
practical research or investigation carried out in the natural environment, rather than in a laboratory or office.
practical research outside the lab or office

Examples

During her anthropology degree, she spent a semester doing field work in rural India.
Botanists often conduct field work to observe plants in their natural habitats.
The project requires extensive field work to collect accurate environmental data.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lab work - work or research carried out in a laboratory
