genus Acorus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs əˈkɔːr.əs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs əˈkɔː.rəs / (BrE)
A genus of perennial plants known as sweet flags, sometimes classified in the subfamily Acoraceae.
genus of plants called sweet flags

Examples

The genus Acorus includes species found in wetlands across Asia and North America.
Botanists often debate whether genus Acorus should be placed in its own family, Acoraceae.
Members of the genus Acorus are commonly used in traditional herbal medicine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sweet flag - a common name for plants of the genus Acorus

Etymology

From Latin 'Acorus', from Greek 'ἄκορον' (sweet flag).
