allis shad

1. NOUN

/ ˈælɪs ʃæd / (AmE), / ˈælɪs ʃad / (BrE)
A species of shad (Alosa alosa) found in European coastal and freshwater habitats, notable for its migratory spawning behavior.
European migratory shad species

Examples

The population of allis shad in the river has declined due to pollution and overfishing.
Biologists are tracking the migration patterns of allis shad along the European coastline.
Traditional recipes from France often feature allis shad as a delicacy during the spawning season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shad - any of several species of herring-like fish

Etymology

From the scientific name Alosa alosa; 'allis' is thought to derive from Middle French.
