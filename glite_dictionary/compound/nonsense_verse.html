nonsense verse

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɑnˌsɛns vɜrs / (AmE), / ˈnɒnsəns vɜːs / (BrE)
Verse or poetry that is intentionally nonsensical, often employing absurd language or meaningless content for humorous or whimsical effect.
absurd or nonsensical poetry

Examples

Edward Lear is famous for his nonsense verse, which delights children and adults alike.
The whimsical nonsense verse made everyone in the class laugh.
Students were asked to write their own nonsense verse as a creative exercise.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nonsense - words or language that has no meaning or makes no sense
