bomb calorimeter

1. NOUN

/ bɑm kəˈlɔrəmət̬ɚ / (AmE), / bɒm kəˈlɒrɪmiːtə / (BrE)
a strong, sealed vessel used in laboratories to measure the heat of combustion of substances
vessel for measuring heat of combustion

Examples

The researcher used a bomb calorimeter to determine the energy content of the food sample.
A bomb calorimeter precisely measures the heat released during a combustion reaction.
Before running the experiment, she calibrated the bomb calorimeter for accurate results.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calorimeter - an instrument for measuring heat changes

Etymology

From 'bomb' (referring to the robust, sealed container) and 'calorimeter' (from Latin 'calor' meaning heat).
