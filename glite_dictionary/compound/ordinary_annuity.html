ordinary annuity

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔr.dəˌner.i əˈnuː.ə.t̬i / (AmE), / ˈɔː.dɪ.nər.i əˈnjuː.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
An annuity in which equal payments are made at the end of each period over a specified term.
annuity with payments at period end

Examples

If you receive monthly rent payments at the end of each month, you have an ordinary annuity.
Retirement plans often use the concept of an ordinary annuity for calculating payouts.
With an ordinary annuity, each payment is made after the period has ended.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: annuity due - An annuity with payments made at the start of each period

Etymology

From financial terminology; 'annuity' from Latin 'annuitas' (annual payment).
