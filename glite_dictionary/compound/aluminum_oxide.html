aluminum oxide

1. NOUN

/ əˈluːmɪnəm ˈɑksaɪd / (AmE), / ˌaljʊˈmɪniəm ˈɒksaɪd / (BrE)
a chemical compound, Al2O3, occurring naturally as corundum and used in various industrial applications
a chemical compound (Al2O3); mineral corundum

Examples

The hardness of aluminum oxide makes it suitable as an abrasive for sanding materials.
Rubies and sapphires are colored varieties of crystalline aluminum oxide known as corundum.
Ceramic coatings often use aluminum oxide to provide resistance to wear and corrosion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corundum - a naturally occurring crystalline form of aluminum oxide
  • Synonym: alumina - another name for aluminum oxide, especially in industrial contexts

Etymology

From 'aluminum' (from Latin 'alumen', meaning alum) and 'oxide' (from Greek 'oxys' meaning sharp/acid).
