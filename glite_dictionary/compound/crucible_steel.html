crucible steel

1. NOUN

/ ˈkruːsəbəl stil / (AmE), / ˈkruːsɪbəl stiːl / (BrE)
A high-quality steel produced by melting wrought iron with charcoal and other substances in a crucible, resulting in a uniform composition.
high-quality steel made in a crucible

Examples

The blades were forged from crucible steel, renowned for its strength and resilience.
During the Industrial Revolution, crucible steel revolutionized the production of high-grade tools.
She explained how crucible steel differed from other forms of steel in its composition and purity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: steel - an alloy of iron with carbon, widely used for construction

Etymology

From 'crucible' (a vessel used for melting) and 'steel'; term reflects the process used to make the alloy.
