intrinsic fraud

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtrɪnsɪk frɔd / (AmE), / ɪnˈtrɪnsɪk frɔːd / (BrE)
fraud committed through false statements, forged documents, or perjury that misleads a court or jury during litigation
fraud involving deception of the court during a trial
legal

Examples

The judge dismissed the appeal, ruling that the claim was based on intrinsic fraud that occurred during the trial.
Allegations of intrinsic fraud often involve perjury or forged evidence submitted in court.
Distinguishing between intrinsic fraud and other types of fraud is crucial in post-trial motions.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: extrinsic fraud - fraud involving deception outside the courtroom, such as preventing someone from having their day in court

Etymology

From Latin 'intrinsecus' (inwardly) and 'fraus' (deceit).
