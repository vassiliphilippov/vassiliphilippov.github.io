castor bean

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæstər bin / (AmE), / ˈkɑːstə biːn / (BrE)
the toxic seed of the castor-oil plant, used as the source of castor oil
toxic seed of castor-oil plant

Examples

The castor bean contains ricin, a highly toxic substance, so it must be processed carefully.
Despite its toxicity, oil from the castor bean is widely used in pharmaceuticals and cosmetics.
Farmers are warned to keep livestock away from castor bean plants due to the risk of poisoning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: castor-oil plant - the plant (Ricinus communis) that produces castor beans
