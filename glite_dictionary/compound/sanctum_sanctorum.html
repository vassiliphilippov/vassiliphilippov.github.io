sanctum sanctorum

1. NOUN

/ ˌsæŋktəm sæŋkˈtɔrəm / (AmE), / ˌsæŋktəm sæŋkˈtɔːrəm / (BrE)
a private place where a person is free from intrusion or disturbance; an innermost, most sacred or private place
a private or sacred inner place

Examples

Every evening he retreated to his sanctum sanctorum to read in solitude.
The professor's office was her sanctum sanctorum, filled with rare books and personal mementos.
She considered her studio a sanctum sanctorum where only a select few were ever invited.

Synset Examples

he withdrew to his sanctum sanctorum, where the children could never go

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sanctuary - a place of refuge or safety
  • Synonym: retreat - a place to withdraw for peace, quiet, or privacy

Etymology

From Latin, meaning 'holy of holies'; originally referred to the innermost sanctuary of the Jewish Temple in Jerusalem.

2. NOUN

/ ˌsæŋktəm sæŋkˈtɔrəm / (AmE), / ˌsæŋktəm sæŋkˈtɔːrəm / (BrE)
The innermost and most sacred part of a sacred building, especially the chamber in the Jewish Temple at Jerusalem where the Ark of the Covenant was kept.
innermost sacred place (esp. in Jewish Temple)

Examples

Visitors were not permitted to enter the sanctum sanctorum of the ancient temple.
For the high priest, access to the sanctum sanctorum was allowed only once a year.
The manuscript was kept in the sanctum sanctorum of the library, away from public view.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: holy of holies - the innermost and most sacred chamber of the Jewish Tabernacle and Temple

Etymology

From Latin, meaning 'holy of holies', translating Hebrew 'qōḏeš haqqŏḏāšīm'.
