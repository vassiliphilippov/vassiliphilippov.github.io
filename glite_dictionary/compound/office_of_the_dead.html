Office of the Dead

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑfɪs əv ðə dɛd / (AmE), / ˈɒfɪs əv ðə dɛd / (BrE)
a liturgical service in the Roman Catholic Church, consisting of prayers and readings for the dead, often performed before a burial mass
Roman Catholic service for the dead

Examples

The choir sang the Office of the Dead before the funeral mass began.
During All Souls' Day, the Office of the Dead is often recited in monasteries.
He composed a musical setting for the Office of the Dead in memory of his teacher.

Etymology

From Latin 'Officium Defunctorum', meaning 'service for the dead'.
