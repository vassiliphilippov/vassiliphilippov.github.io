Saint Agnes's Eve

1. NOUN

/ seɪnt ˈæɡ.nɪsɪz iːv / (AmE), / seɪnt ˈaɡ.nɪsɪz iːv / (BrE)
The night before the feast day of Saint Agnes (January 21), traditionally believed to be a time when young women could dream of their future husbands by performing certain rites.
the eve before Saint Agnes's feast day (Jan 21)

Examples

On Saint Agnes's Eve, she performed the old ritual in hopes of seeing her future husband in a dream.
The poem describes the magical traditions associated with Saint Agnes's Eve.
Young women would stay awake on Saint Agnes's Eve believing their dreams would reveal their true love.

Etymology

Refers to the eve of Saint Agnes, Christian martyr; associated with English folklore and notably referenced in Keats's 1820 poem 'The Eve of St. Agnes'.
