position effect

1. NOUN

/ pəˈzɪʃ.ən ɪˈfɛkt / (AmE), / pəˈzɪʃ.ən ɪˈfɛkt / (BrE)
The effect on the expression of a gene caused by changing its location within a chromosome.
genetic effect due to gene location change
medical

Examples

In some cases, a gene may be silenced due to the position effect after being relocated in the chromosome.
The position effect complicates the interpretation of gene function experiments in transgenic mice.
Researchers observed a position effect that changed the phenotype of the organism when the gene was moved.
