forensic medicine

1. NOUN

/ fəˈrɛnsɪk ˈmɛdəsən / (AmE), / fəˈrɛnsɪk ˈmɛdɪsɪn / (BrE)
the branch of medicine that applies medical knowledge to legal questions, such as in criminal investigations or legal proceedings
medical science applied to legal issues
legal medical

Examples

She decided to specialize in forensic medicine after witnessing a high-profile murder case.
Advances in forensic medicine have greatly improved the accuracy of criminal investigations.
The expert in forensic medicine testified about the cause of death during the trial.

Synset Examples

forensic pathology provided the evidence that convicted the murderer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forensic pathology - the branch of pathology concerned with determining cause of death for legal cases

Etymology

From Latin 'forensis' (of the forum, public), referring to courts of law, and 'medicine.'
