life eternal

1. NOUN

/ laɪf ɪˈtɝːnəl / (AmE), / laɪf ɪˈtɜːnəl / (BrE)
Life without beginning or end; eternal existence, especially as a concept in religion or philosophy.
eternal existence; existence without end

Examples

Many believers find comfort in the promise of life eternal in the afterlife.
Ancient philosophers debated the possibility of a life eternal beyond the mortal body.
The poet wrote about longing for life eternal amidst the fleeting nature of existence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eternal life - life that lasts forever, especially after death
