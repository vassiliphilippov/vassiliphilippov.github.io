Great Dog

1. PROPN

/ ˌɡreɪt ˈdɔɡ / (AmE), / ˌɡreɪt ˈdɒɡ / (BrE)
a constellation in the southern sky, southeast of Orion, containing the star Sirius; also known as Canis Major
a constellation; Canis Major

Examples

The star Sirius in the Great Dog is the brightest in the night sky.
Ancient astronomers named the constellation Great Dog because of its shape.
In winter, the Great Dog is easily visible southeast of Orion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Canis Major - Latin name for the Great Dog constellation
  • Degree Lower: Sirius - the brightest star in the Great Dog constellation
