genus Thryothorus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˌθraɪ.oʊˈθɔːr.əs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˌθraɪ.əʊˈθɔː.rəs / (BrE)
A genus of small passerine birds in the wren family, including species such as the Carolina wren.
genus of wrens (birds)

Examples

The genus Thryothorus includes the well-known Carolina wren among its species.
Ornithologists studied the nesting behavior of birds within the genus Thryothorus.
Members of the genus Thryothorus are commonly found in wooded areas of the Americas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wren - a small, active songbird

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Greek 'thryon' (reed) and 'thouros' (leaping).
