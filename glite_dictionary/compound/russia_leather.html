Russia leather

1. NOUN

/ ˈrʌʃə ˌlɛðər / (AmE), / ˈrʌʃə ˌlɛðə / (BrE)
a durable, flexible leather tanned with willow, birch, or oak and traditionally scented with birch oil on the flesh side
a type of scented, tanned leather

Examples

The book was bound in fine Russia leather, giving it a distinct aroma.
Collectors value antique trunks covered in Russia leather for their durability and scent.
Shoes made from Russia leather were once considered both fashionable and long-lasting.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: leather - material made from animal hide used for various goods

Etymology

Named for its origin and traditional manufacture in Russia, noted since the 17th century.
