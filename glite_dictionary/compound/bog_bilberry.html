bog bilberry

1. NOUN

/ bɑɡ ˈbɪlˌbɛri / (AmE), / bɒɡ ˈbɪlˌbəri / (BrE)
a low, evergreen shrub with leathery leaves, found in boggy or acidic soils and producing small bluish-black berries
evergreen shrub found in boggy soils

Examples

The bog bilberry thrives in cold, acidic wetlands across northern Europe.
We identified several plants, including the bog bilberry, during our hike through the mire.
Berry pickers sometimes confuse bog bilberry with other similar shrubs in the moorlands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bilberry - an edible dark blue berry or the plant that bears it
