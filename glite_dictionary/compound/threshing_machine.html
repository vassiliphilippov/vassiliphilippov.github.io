threshing machine

1. NOUN

/ ˈθrɛʃɪŋ məˌʃin / (AmE), / ˈθreʃɪŋ məˌʃiːn / (BrE)
a machine used on farms to separate grain or seeds from husks and straw
machine for separating grain from husks

Examples

The old threshing machine sat rusting in the corner of the barn.
Before combine harvesters, farmers relied on the threshing machine to process their wheat.
Watching a threshing machine in action was a highlight at the agricultural fair.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: combine harvester - a machine that both reaps and threshes grain crops

Etymology

From 'thresh', meaning to separate grain by beating, combined with 'machine'.
