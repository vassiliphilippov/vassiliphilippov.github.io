order Araneida

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdər ˌærəˈnaɪdə / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə ˌærəˈnaɪdə / (BrE)
the taxonomic order of arachnids that comprises all spiders
taxonomic order of spiders

Examples

Spiders belong to the order Araneida, which is distinguished by certain anatomical features.
The order Araneida includes thousands of species found in nearly every environment on Earth.
Research into the order Araneida has provided insights into evolutionary adaptations among arachnids.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spider - an arachnid with eight legs and two body segments
  • Degree Higher: order Scorpiones - the order including all scorpions

Etymology

From New Latin 'Araneida', from Latin 'aranea' (spider).
