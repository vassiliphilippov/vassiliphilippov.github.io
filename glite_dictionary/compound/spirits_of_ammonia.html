spirits of ammonia

1. NOUN

/ ˈspɪrɪts əv əˈmoʊniə / (AmE), / ˈspɪrɪts əv əˈməʊniə / (BrE)
a solution of ammonium carbonate in ammonia water and alcohol, formerly used as a stimulant or smelling salt
solution of ammonium carbonate in ammonia water and alcohol

Examples

The nurse revived the patient with spirits of ammonia after he fainted.
Victorian-era medicine cabinets often contained small bottles of spirits of ammonia.
She caught a whiff of spirits of ammonia and recoiled at the sharp smell.
