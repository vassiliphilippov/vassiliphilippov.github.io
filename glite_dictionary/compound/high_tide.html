high tide

1. NOUN

/ haɪ taɪd / (AmE), / haɪ taɪd / (BrE)
the state of the tide when the water reaches its highest level
the highest level of the tide

Examples

The boats were able to leave the harbor safely at high tide.
We walked along the beach, but the path was underwater during high tide.
Shells and seaweed are often left behind after high tide.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: low tide - the state of the tide when the water is at its lowest level
