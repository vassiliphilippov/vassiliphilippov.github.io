cardinal flower

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑrdɪnəl ˌflaʊər / (AmE), / ˈkɑːdɪnəl ˌflaʊə / (BrE)
a North American plant (Lobelia cardinalis) with brilliant red flowers, commonly found in moist areas
a red-flowered North American plant

Examples

The cardinal flower brightens the wetland with its striking red blossoms every summer.
Gardeners often plant cardinal flowers to attract hummingbirds to their yards.
I stopped along the stream bank to admire a patch of blooming cardinal flower.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lobelia - a genus of flowering plants including cardinal flower

Etymology

Named for its vivid red flowers reminiscent of the robes worn by Roman Catholic cardinals.
