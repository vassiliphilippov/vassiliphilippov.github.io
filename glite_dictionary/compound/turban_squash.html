turban squash

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɝbən skwɑʃ / (AmE), / ˈtɜːbən skwɒʃ / (BrE)
a large, ornamental squash with a bulbous protruding end that resembles a turban, often used in cooking or decoration.
squash with a turban-like shape

Examples

She used a turban squash to make a hearty autumn soup.
The turban squash added a colorful touch to the Thanksgiving centerpiece.
Gardeners appreciate the unique shape of the turban squash.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: squash - an edible gourd or its plant

Etymology

Named for its likeness to a turban, from the word 'turban' referring to the headdress.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɝːbən skwɑʃ / (AmE), / ˈtɜːbən skwɒʃ / (BrE)
a type of squash with a hard shell and an unusual turban-like shape, featuring a rounded central portion protruding from the end opposite the stem
decorative, turban-shaped squash

Examples

The vibrant colors of the turban squash make it popular for autumn displays and decorations.
She added roasted turban squash to the salad for extra flavor and visual appeal.
Farmers at the market were selling both butternut and turban squash this weekend.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: squash - edible gourd, vegetable of the genus Cucurbita

Etymology

Named for its resemblance to a turban, the headwear, due to its shape.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-12181802-n
Synset POS: n
