eared grebe

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪrd ˌɡrib / (AmE), / ˈɪəd ˌɡriːb / (BrE)
a small grebe with yellow ear tufts and a black neck, found in parts of Eurasia, southern Africa, and western United States
small grebe with yellow ear tufts

Examples

The eared grebe is easily identified by its striking yellow auricular feathers.
During migration, thousands of eared grebes gather in shallow lakes across North America.
Birdwatchers often travel to see the eared grebe in its breeding plumage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: black-necked grebe - another common name for the eared grebe species
