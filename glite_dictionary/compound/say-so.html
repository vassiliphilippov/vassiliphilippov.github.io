say-so

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪ ˌsoʊ / (AmE), / ˈseɪ səʊ / (BrE)
an authoritative statement or decision, especially one made by a person in power
authoritative decision or statement

Examples

No one can make changes to the plan without the manager’s say-so.
She acted on her father's say-so and left the company.
It was only with the principal's say-so that the event went ahead.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: command - an authoritative order
  • Synonym: permission - consent or authorization
  • Antonym: prohibition - the act of forbidding something

Etymology

Formed from the verb 'say' and the adverb 'so', appearing in English from the late 19th century.
