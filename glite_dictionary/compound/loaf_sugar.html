loaf sugar

1. NOUN

/ loʊf ˈʃʊɡər / (AmE), / ləʊf ˈʃʊɡə / (BrE)
a large, hard mass of refined sugar, typically shaped like a cone or loaf, used before granulated sugar became common
large, conical lump of refined sugar

Examples

In the nineteenth century, people purchased loaf sugar and broke it into pieces at home.
The recipe calls for a lump of loaf sugar to sweeten the punch.
Old illustrations often depict loaf sugar as the centerpiece of the grocer's shop.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sugar loaf - another term for loaf sugar
