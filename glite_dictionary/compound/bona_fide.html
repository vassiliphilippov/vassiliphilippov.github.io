bona fide

1. ADJ

/ ˌboʊnə ˈfaɪd / (AmE), / ˌbəʊnə ˈfiːdeɪ / (BrE)
Made or carried out in good faith; genuine or real.
genuine; in good faith

Examples

The company is looking for bona fide investors who are serious about the project.
Regulations require that any bona fide offer must be considered carefully.
She produced a bona fide document to prove her identity.

Synset Examples

a bona fide offer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: genuine - truly what something is said to be; authentic
  • Antonym: fraudulent - not genuine; intended to deceive

Etymology

From Latin bona fide (in good faith).
