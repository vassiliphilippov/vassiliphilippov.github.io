ready money

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛdi ˈmʌni / (AmE), / ˈrɛdi ˈmʌni / (BrE)
Money available for immediate use in the form of cash.
cash immediately available

Examples

He always keeps some ready money in his wallet for emergencies.
The contractor insisted on being paid in ready money rather than by check.
Without ready money, she couldn't buy the concert tickets before they sold out.

Synset Examples

his wife was always a good source of ready cash
he paid cold cash for the TV set

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cash - money in the form of coins or banknotes
  • Antonym: credit - money available through lending, not in immediate physical form
