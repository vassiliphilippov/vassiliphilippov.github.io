capillary artery

1. NOUN

/ kəˈpɪləri ˈɑːrtəri / (AmE), / kəˈpɪləri ˈɑːtəri / (BrE)
a small thin-walled artery that leads directly to capillaries, involved in the microcirculation of blood
small artery ending in capillaries
medical

Examples

Oxygen diffuses across the thin walls of the capillary artery into surrounding tissues.
Damage to a capillary artery can result in localized swelling and bruising.
The transition from an arteriole to a capillary artery marks a key change in blood vessel structure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arteriole - a very small artery leading to capillaries

Etymology

From Latin 'capillaris' (hair-like) and Greek 'arteria' (artery).
