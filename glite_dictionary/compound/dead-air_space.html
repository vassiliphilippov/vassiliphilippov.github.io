dead-air space

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛd ˌɛr speɪs / (AmE), / ˈdɛd ˌeə speɪs / (BrE)
an unventilated area where air does not circulate, often serving as insulation or affecting airflow
unventilated area without air circulation

Examples

The walls are constructed with a dead-air space between the layers for extra insulation.
Make sure there is no dead-air space behind the refrigerator, or it won't cool efficiently.
A large dead-air space in the attic can help regulate the temperature of the house.
