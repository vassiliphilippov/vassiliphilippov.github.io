Battle of Jena

1. PROPN

/ ˈbæt̬l əv ˈjeɪnə / (AmE), / ˈbætl əv ˈjeɪnə / (BrE)
A decisive battle in 1806 during which Napoleon's forces defeated the Prussian army.
decisive 1806 battle: Napoleon vs Prussia

Examples

The Battle of Jena marked a turning point in the Napoleonic Wars.
Historians often analyze the strategies used at the Battle of Jena to understand Napoleon's military genius.
After the Battle of Jena, Prussian influence in Europe declined significantly.

Etymology

From the name of Jena, a city in Germany, near which the battle occurred.
