unctuous compliment

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌŋktʃuəs ˈkɑmplɪmənt / (AmE), / ˈʌŋktjʊəs ˈkɒmplɪmənt / (BrE)
An excessively flattering or insincere remark, often intended to gain favor or advantage.
excessively flattering remark

Examples

His unctuous compliment did little to convince her of his sincerity.
She recognized the unctuous compliment as a manipulative attempt to win her approval.
Colleagues often roll their eyes when he offers another unctuous compliment to the boss.

Synset Examples

They found his unctuous compliments hard to believe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flattery - excessive praise intended to ingratiate
  • Antonym: insincere - not expressing genuine feelings
