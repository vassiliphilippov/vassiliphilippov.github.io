lipid-lowering medication

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪpɪd ˈloʊərɪŋ ˌmɛdəˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈlɪpɪd ˈləʊərɪŋ ˌmɛdɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
a medication used to decrease the level of lipids, especially cholesterol, in the blood, often by inhibiting certain enzymes such as HMG-CoA reductase
medicine to lower blood cholesterol
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed a lipid-lowering medication to help reduce his cholesterol levels.
Patients taking lipid-lowering medication should have their blood tested regularly to monitor liver function.
Not everyone responds to the same lipid-lowering medication, so adjustments may be necessary.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: statin - type of lipid-lowering drug that inhibits HMG-CoA reductase
