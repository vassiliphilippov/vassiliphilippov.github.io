per capita

1. ADJ

/ pər ˈkæpɪtə / (AmE), / pə ˈkæpɪtə / (BrE)
For each person; in relation to each individual in a group.
for each person; per person

Examples

The country's per capita income has risen steadily over the past decade.
Water consumption per capita is higher in urban areas than in rural ones.
They calculated the per capita carbon emissions for several countries.

Synset Examples

among the states, Connecticut has a high per capita income

Related Terms

  • Synonym: per head - for each person; per person

Etymology

From Latin 'per capita' meaning 'by heads'.

2. ADV

/ pər ˈkæpɪtə / (AmE), / pə ˈkæpɪtə / (BrE)
For each person; calculated or measured for each individual in a group.
for each person

Examples

The country's per capita income has increased steadily over the past decade.
Freshwater usage is measured on a per capita basis in environmental studies.
Sweden has one of the highest per capita rates of internet access in the world.

Synset Examples

we are spending $5,000 per capita annually for education in this district

Related Terms

  • Synonym: per head - for each person; calculated by the number of people

Etymology

From Latin per (by, for each) + capita (heads, plural of caput).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00503489-r
Synset POS: r
