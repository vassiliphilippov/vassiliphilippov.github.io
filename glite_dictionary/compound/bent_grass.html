bent grass

1. NOUN

/ bɛnt ˌɡræs / (AmE), / bɛnt ˌɡrɑːs / (BrE)
A type of grass, especially of the genus Agrostis, commonly cultivated for lawns, pastures, and sports greens such as bowling and putting greens.
grass used for lawns and greens

Examples

The golf course was renowned for its perfectly maintained bent grass greens.
Gardeners often prefer bent grass for creating lush, green lawns.
The putting surface is smooth and fast, thanks to the fine-bladed bent grass.

Etymology

From the word 'bent', an old term for a kind of grass, from Old English 'beonet'.
