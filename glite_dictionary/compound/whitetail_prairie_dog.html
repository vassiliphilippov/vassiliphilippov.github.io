whitetail prairie dog

1. NOUN

/ ˈwaɪtˌteɪl ˈpreɪ.ri dɔɡ / (AmE), / ˈwaɪtteɪl ˈpreɪ.ri dɒɡ / (BrE)
a species of prairie dog (Cynomys leucurus) native to the western United States, distinguished by its white-tipped tail
species of prairie dog with a white-tipped tail

Examples

The whitetail prairie dog is commonly found in parts of Wyoming and Colorado.
Researchers observed a colony of whitetail prairie dogs near the grasslands.
Unlike other species, the whitetail prairie dog uses a unique set of warning calls.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prairie dog - a small, burrowing North American rodent of the squirrel family

Etymology

Named for its characteristic white-tipped tail; 'prairie dog' from its habitat and barking call.
