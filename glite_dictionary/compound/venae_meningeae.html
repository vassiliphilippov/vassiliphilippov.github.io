venae meningeae

1. NOUN

/ ˈviː.ni mɛˌnɪn.dʒiˈi / (AmE), / ˈviː.ni mɛˌnɪn.dʒɪˈiː / (BrE)
the veins that accompany the meningeal arteries in the membranes covering the brain and spinal cord
meningeal veins of the brain and spinal cord
medical

Examples

The surgeon carefully avoided damaging the venae meningeae during the procedure.
Inflammation of the venae meningeae can contribute to severe headaches.
Detailed anatomy lectures often discuss the course of the venae meningeae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meningeal artery - artery supplying the meninges of the brain and spinal cord

Etymology

From Latin: 'venae' (veins) + 'meningeae' (of the meninges, the membranes covering the brain and spinal cord).
