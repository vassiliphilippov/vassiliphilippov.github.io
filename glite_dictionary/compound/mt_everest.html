Mt. Everest

1. PROPN

/ ˌmaʊnt ˈɛvərɪst / (AmE), / ˌmaʊnt ˈɛvərɪst / (BrE)
the highest mountain in the world, located in the central Himalayas on the border between Tibet and Nepal, reaching 8,848 meters (29,028 feet) in height
the world's highest mountain

Examples

Climbing Mt. Everest is a dream for many mountaineers despite the dangers involved.
In 1953, Sir Edmund Hillary and Tenzing Norgay became the first to reach the summit of Mt. Everest.
The harsh weather on Mt. Everest can change rapidly, making every expedition challenging.

Etymology

Named after Sir George Everest, British surveyor general of India in the 19th century.
