blue grass

1. NOUN

/ bluː ɡræs / (AmE), / bluː ɡrɑːs / (BrE)
any of various perennial grasses of the genus Poa, especially those with bluish-green leaves, commonly used in lawns and pastures
grass of the genus Poa with bluish leaves

Examples

The lawn was planted with blue grass to create a lush, soft surface.
Many golf courses prefer blue grass for its durability and attractive color.
Horses graze happily on fields of blue grass throughout the region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Kentucky bluegrass - a species of blue grass popular in lawns and pastures
