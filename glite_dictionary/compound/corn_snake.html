corn snake

1. NOUN

/ kɔrn sneɪk / (AmE), / kɔːn sneɪk / (BrE)
a large, nonvenomous snake of the southeastern United States, often found on farms and known for its reddish-orange coloring and docile nature
large nonvenomous snake of the southeastern US

Examples

The farmer found a corn snake in his barn while checking the grain storage.
Many pet enthusiasts appreciate the corn snake for its manageable size and calm temperament.
A brightly colored corn snake slithered across the path in the summer heat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rat snake - any of various large, nonvenomous snakes that feed on rodents

Etymology

Named for its frequent presence near corn storage and fields.
