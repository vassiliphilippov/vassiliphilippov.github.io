respiratory alkalosis

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɛspəˈrɛtɔri ælˈkoʊləsɪs / (AmE), / ˌrɛspɪˈreɪtəri ælˈkəʊləsɪs / (BrE)
A condition in which the blood becomes too alkaline due to excessive loss of carbon dioxide from rapid or deep breathing, as seen in hyperventilation.
medical: excess alkalinity of blood from rapid breathing
medical

Examples

The patient developed respiratory alkalosis after suffering a panic attack and hyperventilating.
Doctors treated respiratory alkalosis by helping the patient regulate their breathing pattern.
Prolonged respiratory alkalosis can lead to disturbances in the body’s electrolyte balance.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: metabolic alkalosis - alkalosis resulting from non-respiratory causes, like excessive loss of acid or gain of base
  • Antonym: acidosis - an excessively acid condition of the body fluids or tissues

Etymology

respiratory from Latin respirare (to breathe); alkalosis from alkali + -osis (denoting condition).
