Hibiscus farragei

1. NOUN

/ haɪˈbɪskəs fəˈrædʒaɪ / (AmE), / hɪˈbɪskəs fəˈrædʒiː / (BrE)
A southern and western Australian shrub with unlobed or shallowly lobed toothed leaves and purple flowers, sometimes classified in the genus Hibiscus.
Australian shrub with purple flowers

Examples

Hibiscus farragei thrives in well-drained Australian soils and produces vibrant purple flowers in spring.
Botanists debated whether Hibiscus farragei should remain a separate species or be included in the broader Hibiscus genus.
The garden features native species such as Hibiscus farragei, appreciated for its ornamental value and resilience.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Hibiscus - Genus of flowering plants including many shrubs with showy flowers

Etymology

Genus name from Latin *hibiscus* (hibiscus plant); species named after botanist W. V. Farrage.
