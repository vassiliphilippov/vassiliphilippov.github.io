Virginia spring beauty

1. NOUN

/ vərˈdʒɪniə sprɪŋ ˈbjuːti / (AmE), / vəˈdʒɪniə sprɪŋ ˈbjuːti / (BrE)
a small perennial herb native to eastern North America, with a low rosette of succulent leaves and racemes of pink-tinged white flowers
flowering plant native to eastern North America

Examples

The Virginia spring beauty blooms early each year, carpeting the forest floor with delicate flowers.
We spotted several Virginia spring beauty plants during our hike in the Appalachian woods.
Gardeners appreciate Virginia spring beauty for its charming pink-tinged petals and low-growing habit.
