acoustic phenomenon

1. NOUN

/ əˈkuːstɪk fəˈnɑmɪˌnɑn / (AmE), / əˈkuːstɪk fəˈnɒmɪnən / (BrE)
A physical event or occurrence related to the production or transmission of sound.
physical event involving sound

Examples

The echo in the canyon is an acoustic phenomenon that fascinates scientists and tourists alike.
Doppler shift is another well-known acoustic phenomenon observed in physics experiments.
Engineers study every acoustic phenomenon that might affect concert hall acoustics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sound - Vibrations that travel through a medium and can be heard

Etymology

From Greek 'akoustikos' (pertaining to hearing) and 'phainomenon' (appearance, event).
