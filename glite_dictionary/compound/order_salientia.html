order Salientia

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdɚ səˈlɪntiə / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə səˈlɪntiə / (BrE)
a taxonomic order comprising frogs, toads, and tree toads; amphibians characterized by jumping hind legs and lack of a tail in adults.
taxonomic order of frogs and toads

Examples

Scientists classify all frogs and toads within the order Salientia.
The order Salientia is distinguished by the absence of a tail in adult amphibians.
Her research focuses on evolutionary patterns within the order Salientia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Anura - an alternative taxonomic name for the order of frogs and toads

Etymology

From Latin 'salire', meaning 'to leap'.
