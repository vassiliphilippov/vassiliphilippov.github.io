team spirit

1. NOUN

/ tim ˈspɪrət / (AmE), / tiːm ˈspɪrɪt / (BrE)
a feeling of pride, loyalty, and enthusiasm among the members of a group that motivates them to achieve shared goals
group enthusiasm and motivation

Examples

Our coach always encourages team spirit before every big game.
Good team spirit can make a big difference in workplace productivity.
The volunteers showed amazing team spirit during the charity event.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: camaraderie - mutual trust and friendship among people who spend time together
  • Synonym: teamwork - collaborative effort by a group to achieve a common goal
  • Antonym: selfishness - lack of care for others or group needs

2. NOUN

/ tiːm ˈspɪrɪt / (AmE), / tiːm ˈspɪrɪt / (BrE)
a feeling of camaraderie, enthusiasm, and mutual support shared by members of a group working together
shared enthusiasm and camaraderie in a group

Examples

The coach praised the team spirit that helped them win the championship.
Even after the defeat, their team spirit remained strong and positive.
Volunteering together at the event improved the company's team spirit.

Synset Examples

Gerrie's enthusiasm always lifts the team spirit.
His bad attitude ruined the team's morale.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: morale - confidence and enthusiastic spirit of a group
