Apostolic Father

1. NOUN

/ əˌpɑːˈstɑlɪk ˈfɑːðɚ / (AmE), / əˌpɒˈstɒlɪk ˈfɑːðə / (BrE)
One of a group of early Christian theologians and writers, regarded as having had direct contact with the Apostles or their immediate disciples.
early Christian theologian or writer

Examples

Scholars believe that Clement of Rome was an Apostolic Father deeply influential in the early church.
The writings of the Apostolic Fathers provide insight into the development of early Christian doctrine.
Polycarp is considered an Apostolic Father because of his connection to the original Apostles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Church Father - early influential Christian theologian

Etymology

From Latin 'apostolicus' (apostolic) and 'pater' (father); refers to churchmen linked to the Apostles.
