Solomon Islands

1. PROPN

/ ˌsɑːləmən ˈaɪləndz / (AmE), / ˌsɒləmən ˈaɪləndz / (BrE)
a country consisting of a group of islands in the southwestern Pacific Ocean, forming an independent state in the British Commonwealth since 1978
island country in the southwestern Pacific

Examples

The Solomon Islands gained their independence from Britain in 1978.
Many tourists visit the Solomon Islands for their beautiful coral reefs and clear waters.
The capital of the Solomon Islands is Honiara, located on the island of Guadalcanal.

Etymology

Named after the biblical King Solomon; the islands were so named by Spanish explorer Álvaro de Mendaña in 1568.

2. PROPN

/ ˌsɑləmən ˈaɪləndz / (AmE), / ˌsɒləmən ˈaɪləndz / (BrE)
a country consisting of a group of islands in the southwestern Pacific Ocean, east of Papua New Guinea; formerly a British protectorate, now an independent state within the Commonwealth
island country in the southwestern Pacific Ocean

Examples

The Solomon Islands gained independence from Britain in 1978.
Several species of unique birds are native only to the Solomon Islands.
Tropical storms frequently affect the Solomon Islands during the rainy season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Papua New Guinea - neighboring country to the west of the Solomon Islands

Etymology

Named after the biblical King Solomon, reflecting European exploration naming conventions.
