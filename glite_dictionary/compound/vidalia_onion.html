Vidalia onion

1. NOUN

/ vɪˈdeɪliə ˈʌnjən / (AmE), / vɪˈdeɪliə ˈʌnjən / (BrE)
A type of sweet-flavored onion grown in Georgia, USA, known for its mild taste.
sweet onion grown in Georgia, USA

Examples

I made a salad with Vidalia onion because of its delicate sweetness.
Grilled burgers taste even better when you add slices of Vidalia onion on top.
The recipe specifically called for a Vidalia onion to enhance the flavor of the dish.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: onion - a round vegetable with layers and a strong flavor

Etymology

Named after Vidalia, a city in Georgia, where these onions are primarily grown.
