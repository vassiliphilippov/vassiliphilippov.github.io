pan gravy

1. NOUN

/ pæn ˈɡreɪ.vi / (AmE), / pæn ˈɡreɪ.vi / (BrE)
a sauce made from the seasoned juices that collect in a pan while meat is cooking, sometimes with a little water added and typically not thickened
seasoned juices from cooked meat

Examples

He poured the pan gravy over the roasted chicken before serving.
The recipe suggests using pan gravy to enhance the flavor of the dish.
She added a splash of water to the drippings to make a simple pan gravy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gravy - a sauce made from meat juices, often thickened
