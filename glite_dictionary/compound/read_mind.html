to read mind

1. VERB

/ rid maɪnd / (AmE), / riːd maɪnd / (BrE)
to know or understand what someone is thinking without them expressing it
know what someone is thinking

Examples

It's impossible for anyone to read mind without some clues from body language.
She always knows what I'm about to say, as if she can read mind.
He wished he could read mind during the important negotiation.

Synset Examples

She claims she can read minds, but I doubt it.
It's hard to read someone's mind without clear communication.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: guess - to form an opinion or answer about something without enough information to be certain
