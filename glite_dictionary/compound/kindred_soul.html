kindred soul

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɪndrəd soʊl / (AmE), / ˈkɪndrɪd səʊl / (BrE)
a person with whom one shares a deep connection, affinity, or similar interests and values
person with a deep affinity or similarity

Examples

I felt like she was a kindred soul from the moment we met.
Artists often seek out others who are kindred souls to share their creative journeys.
It's comforting to know there's at least one kindred soul who understands your perspective.

Synset Examples

He felt she was a kindred soul from the start.
Finding a kindred soul is a rare treasure.
They connected instantly as kindred souls.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soulmate - person ideally suited to another as a close friend or romantic partner
  • Synonym: kindred spirit - a person whose interests or attitudes are similar to one's own

Etymology

From 'kindred' (related by blood or character) and 'soul' (spirit or inner self).
