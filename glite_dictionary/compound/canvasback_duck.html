canvasback duck

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæn.vəs.bæk dʌk / (AmE), / ˈkæn.vəs.bæk dʌk / (BrE)
A North American wild duck distinguished by its sloping profile and red head, valued for sport and as food.
species of North American wild duck

Examples

We spotted a flock of canvasback ducks swimming near the lake shore early this morning.
The canvasback duck is prized by hunters for its distinctive flavor and game qualities.
During migration, large numbers of canvasback ducks gather on freshwater marshes and ponds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: redhead duck - a similar North American diving duck with a reddish head

Etymology

From 'canvasback', referring to the pale, canvas-like color of the duck's back.
