acrimonious argument

1. NOUN

/ ˌækrəˈmoʊniəs ˈɑːrɡjəmənt / (AmE), / ˌakrɪˈməʊniəs ˈɑːɡjʊmənt / (BrE)
an argument or dispute that is bitter and filled with anger or harshness
bitter, angry dispute

Examples

An acrimonious argument erupted between the two colleagues over the project's direction.
Family dinners sometimes ended in an acrimonious argument that left everyone frustrated.
Despite their attempts to stay calm, the debate turned into an acrimonious argument that lasted for hours.

Synset Examples

An acrimonious argument broke out during the meeting.
Their acrimonious debate left everyone uncomfortable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heated argument - an intense and emotional dispute
  • Antonym: amicable discussion - a friendly and peaceful conversation

Etymology

From Latin 'acrimonia' (sharpness); 'argument' from Latin 'argumentum'.
