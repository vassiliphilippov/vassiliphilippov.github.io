all the ammunition

1. NOUN

/ ɔl ði ˌæməˈnɪʃən / (AmE), / ɔːl ði ˌæməˈnɪʃən / (BrE)
all the necessary resources, arguments, or information used to achieve an objective, especially in a debate or competition
all necessary resources or arguments

Examples

The lawyer entered the courtroom with all the ammunition she needed to win the case.
Before the negotiation, he gathered all the ammunition he could to support his proposal.
Make sure you have all the ammunition ready before confronting your critics.

Synset Examples

She had all the ammunition she needed to win.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ammunition - resources for achieving a goal (literal and figurative)

Etymology

A figurative use derived from the literal meaning of 'ammunition' as materials used in warfare.
