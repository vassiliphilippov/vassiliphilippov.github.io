aglow with

1. ADJ

/ əˈɡloʊ wɪθ / (AmE), / əˈɡləʊ wɪð / (BrE)
filled with or radiating a particular quality, especially emotion or light
filled with emotion or light

Examples

Her cheeks were aglow with excitement as she told the story.
The garden was aglow with vibrant colors in the morning sun.
The auditorium was aglow with the warm light of hundreds of candles.

Synset Examples

Her face was aglow with happiness.
The room was aglow with candlelight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: radiant - sending out light; shining or glowing brightly
  • Antonym: dim - not shining brightly or clearly

Etymology

From 'aglow' (glowing), formed by the prefix 'a-' plus 'glow'; 'with' as preposition indicating the quality possessed.
