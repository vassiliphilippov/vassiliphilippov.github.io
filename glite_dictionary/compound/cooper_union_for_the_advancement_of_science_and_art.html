Cooper Union for the Advancement of Science and Art

1. PROPN

/ ˈkuːpər ˈjuːniən fər ði ədˈvænsmənt əv ˈsaɪəns ənd ɑːrt / (AmE), / ˈkuːpə ˈjuːniən fə ði ədˈvɑːnsmənt əv ˈsaɪəns ənd ɑːt / (BrE)
A private college in New York City, founded in 1859 by Peter Cooper, known for offering free and merit-based education in the arts, architecture, and engineering.
private college in New York City, founded 1859

Examples

After graduating from Cooper Union for the Advancement of Science and Art, Maria began her career as an architect.
The Cooper Union for the Advancement of Science and Art is well known for its tuition-free policy and competitive admissions.
Many influential artists and engineers have studied at Cooper Union for the Advancement of Science and Art.

Etymology

Named after its founder, American industrialist Peter Cooper (1791–1883).
