blue chip

1. NOUN

/ bluː tʃɪp / (AmE), / bluː tʃɪp / (BrE)
a nationally well-known company stock considered a safe investment due to its stable value and reliable dividends
stock of a stable, reputable company

Examples

Investors often regard blue chip stocks as safer during economic downturns.
He diversified his portfolio by purchasing several blue chip companies' shares.
The financial advisor recommended blue chip investments for long-term stability.

Synset Examples

blue chips are usually safe investments

Related Terms

  • Antonym: growth stock - a stock expected to grow at an above-average rate
  • Antonym: penny stock - a stock trading at a relatively low price, typically with higher risk

Etymology

Originally from the blue gambling chips in poker, which had the highest value; first applied to high-value stocks in the 1920s.

2. NOUN

/ bluː tʃɪp / (AmE), / bluː tʃɪp / (BrE)

Examples

He pushed a stack of blue chips into the center, signaling a big bet.
Winning those blue chips early gave her a major advantage in the game.
