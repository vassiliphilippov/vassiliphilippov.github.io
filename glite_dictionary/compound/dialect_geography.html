dialect geography

1. NOUN

/ ˈdaɪəˌlɛkt dʒiˈɑɡrəfi / (AmE), / ˈdaɪəlɛkt dʒiˈɒɡrəfi / (BrE)
the study of how linguistic features are distributed across geographical areas
study of linguistic distribution by region

Examples

She specializes in dialect geography and researches local speech patterns throughout the country.
Many universities offer courses on dialect geography as part of the linguistics curriculum.
Advances in technology have revolutionized the field of dialect geography by enabling researchers to map language variation more precisely.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dialectology - the scientific study of dialects and their differences

Etymology

Formed from 'dialect' (from Greek dialektos, conversation, language) and 'geography' (from Greek geōgraphia, earth description).
