co-discoverer

1. NOUN

/ koʊ dɪˈskʌv.ɚ.ɚ / (AmE), / kəʊ dɪˈskʌv.ər.ər / (BrE)
a person who is the first, together with one or more others, to discover something
person jointly credited with a discovery

Examples

Marie Curie was recognized as a co-discoverer of polonium and radium.
Both scientists were named as co-discoverers in the official announcement of the new element.
She was proud to be the co-discoverer of such an important medical breakthrough.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: discoverer - a person who discovers something

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'co-' (together, joint) and 'discoverer' (one who discovers).
