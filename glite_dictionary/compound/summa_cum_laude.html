summa cum laude

1. ADV

/ ˈsuːmə kʊm ˈlaʊdeɪ / (AmE), / ˈsʊmə kʊm ˈlaʊdi / (BrE)
with the highest academic distinction; used to denote the highest level of honors awarded to a graduate.
with highest academic distinction

Examples

After years of hard work, she graduated summa cum laude from the university.
He proudly listed summa cum laude on his résumé to showcase his academic excellence.
Only a small percentage of students achieve the honor of graduating summa cum laude.

Synset Examples

he graduated summa cum laude

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: magna cum laude - with great academic distinction; second-highest honors for graduates
  • Degree Lower: cum laude - with honors; used to denote honors status for graduates

Etymology

From Latin, meaning 'with highest praise'.

2. ADV

/ ˌsuːmə kʊm ˈlaʊdeɪ / (AmE), / ˌsʌmə kʊm ˈlaʊdi / (BrE)
(of a degree or graduate) with the highest honor; indicating the highest level of academic distinction.
with the highest academic honor

Examples

She graduated summa cum laude from Harvard University with a degree in biology.
The university awards the summa cum laude distinction to students with exceptional academic records.
Only a few students each year achieve the honor of graduating summa cum laude.

Synset Examples

a summa cum laude graduate

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: magna cum laude - with great honor; a lower academic distinction than summa cum laude
  • Degree Lower: cum laude - with honor; a lower academic distinction than magna cum laude

Etymology

From Latin, meaning 'with highest praise'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02597722-s
Synset POS: s
