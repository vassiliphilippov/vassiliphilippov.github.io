creeping lily

1. NOUN

/ ˈkripɪŋ ˈlɪli / (AmE), / ˈkriːpɪŋ ˈlɪli / (BrE)
a perennial climbing plant of the genus Gloriosa, native to tropical Africa and Asia, with showy yellow, red, or purple flowers, and poisonous parts
a poisonous climbing plant with showy flowers

Examples

The creeping lily climbed up the garden trellis, displaying its vibrant red petals.
Despite its beauty, every part of the creeping lily is highly poisonous if ingested.
In parts of Asia, the creeping lily is admired for its unusual flowers and tendril-like leaves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glory lily - another common name for plants of the genus Gloriosa
  • Degree Higher: lily - a plant with large, prominent flowers, especially of the genus Lilium
