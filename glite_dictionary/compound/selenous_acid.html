selenous acid

1. NOUN

/ səˈliːnoʊs ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / sɪˈliːnəs ˈæsɪd / (BrE)
a dibasic acid with the chemical formula H2SeO3, used in chemistry as an oxidizing agent and in the manufacture of pigments and glass.
a dibasic acid, H2SeO3, used in chemistry and industry
medical

Examples

Selenous acid is sometimes used to treat or prevent selenium deficiency in medical applications.
The chemist carefully measured out selenous acid for the laboratory experiment.
Because of its oxidizing properties, selenous acid must be handled with care.

Synset Examples

Selenous acid is analogous to sulfurous acid, but it is more readily isolated.

Etymology

From selenium (the element) + -ous (indicating a lower oxidation state) + acid.
