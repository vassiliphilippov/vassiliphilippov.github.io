hard wheat

1. NOUN

/ hɑrd wit / (AmE), / hɑːd wiːt / (BrE)
a type of wheat with hard, dark-colored kernels high in gluten, used especially for bread and pasta
wheat variety with hard, high-gluten kernels

Examples

Durum is a common type of hard wheat used in pasta production.
Bakers prefer hard wheat for making high-quality bread loaves.
Hard wheat is grown extensively in the Great Plains of North America.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: soft wheat - a type of wheat with soft, light-colored kernels lower in gluten, used mainly for cakes and pastries
