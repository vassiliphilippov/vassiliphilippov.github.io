elite group

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈlit ɡruːp / (AmE), / ɪˈliːt ɡruːp / (BrE)
A group or class of people regarded as having superior intellectual, social, or economic status.
group of people with superior status

Examples

The university's elite group of scholars received special recognition for their achievements.
Decisions were often made by an elite group without input from the general public.
Only a small elite group could afford membership in the exclusive club.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aristocracy - the highest class in certain societies, often holding hereditary titles
  • Synonym: upper class - a social group with the highest status in society
  • Antonym: commoner - a person without noble rank or title

Etymology

From French 'élite' (the chosen part), from Latin 'eligere' (to choose), plus English 'group'.
