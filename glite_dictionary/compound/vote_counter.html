vote counter

1. NOUN

/ voʊt ˈkaʊntər / (AmE), / vəʊt ˈkaʊntə / (BrE)
an official responsible for counting votes, especially in a legislative assembly or election
official who counts votes

Examples

The vote counter announced the final results after tallying all the ballots.
Every vote counter must remain impartial during the counting process.
The committee appointed a new vote counter for the upcoming election.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: teller - person appointed to count votes in an election or meeting
