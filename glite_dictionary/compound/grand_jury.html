grand jury

1. NOUN

/ ɡrænd ˈdʒʊri / (AmE), / ɡrænd ˈdʒʊəri / (BrE)
A legal body that examines accusations of crime and determines whether there is enough evidence to issue an indictment and proceed to trial.
legal body deciding indictments
legal

Examples

The prosecutor presented evidence to the grand jury for consideration.
A grand jury decided there was enough evidence to indict the suspect.
The defense attorney requested to address the grand jury before the indictment was issued.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: petit jury - A jury that decides the facts at trial (trial jury)
  • Synonym: indictment - A formal charge or accusation of a serious crime

Etymology

From grand (meaning 'large') and jury, distinguishing it from a 'petit jury' (trial jury); both terms from Old French 'juree', meaning 'oath' or 'jury'.
