genus Clostridium

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs klɒˈstrɪdiəm / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs klɒˈstrɪdɪəm / (BrE)
A genus of anaerobic or micro-aerophilic, rod-shaped or spindle-shaped bacteria, commonly found in soil, animal intestines, and dung.
bacterial genus found in soil and animal intestines
medical

Examples

Certain bacteria in the genus Clostridium can cause severe foodborne illnesses if food is improperly preserved.
Researchers observed the presence of genus Clostridium in the soil samples collected from the farm.
Tetanus is caused by a species within the genus Clostridium, which thrives in environments lacking oxygen.

Etymology

From Greek 'klōstēr' (spindle), referring to the typical shape of the bacteria.
