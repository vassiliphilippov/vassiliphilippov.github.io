iron group element

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪərn ɡruːp ˈɛləmənt / (AmE), / ˈaɪən ɡruːp ˈɛlɪmənt / (BrE)
any of a group of chemical elements related to iron, typically including iron, cobalt, and nickel, which share similar physical and chemical properties.
chemical element related to iron

Examples

Nickel is considered an iron group element because of its similar properties to iron.
The iron group elements play an important role in the Earth's core composition.
Researchers are studying the corrosion resistance of iron group element alloys.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: iron - a strong, hard magnetic metal, chemical element Fe
  • Synonym: transition element - an element in the central block of the periodic table (d-block)
