hard-shell clam

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑrd ʃɛl klæm / (AmE), / ˈhɑːd ʃɛl klæm / (BrE)
an edible North American clam with a thick shell, formerly used as money by some Native American peoples
edible North American clam with thick shell

Examples

Local fishermen harvest hard-shell clam from the coastal waters every summer.
The shells of the hard-shell clam were once used as wampum by Native Americans.
We enjoyed a delicious soup made from fresh hard-shell clam last night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quahog - another name for the hard-shell clam

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɑrd ˌʃɛl klæm / (AmE), / ˈhɑːd ˌʃɛl klæm / (BrE)
a type of edible clam, native to the Atlantic coast of North America, with a thick, hard shell; commonly used in chowders and other clam dishes
Atlantic edible clam with hard shell

Examples

She prepared a hearty soup using fresh hard-shell clams from the market.
The hard-shell clam is a staple ingredient in New England clam chowder.
Fishermen along the Atlantic collect hard-shell clams during the summer harvest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quahog - another term for the hard-shell clam, especially in the U.S.
