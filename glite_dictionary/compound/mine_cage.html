mine cage

1. NOUN

/ maɪn keɪdʒ / (AmE), / maɪn keɪdʒ / (BrE)
A platform or enclosure suspended from a hoist and used to transport workers and supplies up and down a mine shaft.
platform for transporting people and supplies in a mine

Examples

The workers entered the mine cage at the surface and descended slowly into the shaft.
After each shift, the mine cage brings miners back up to daylight.
Heavy equipment was loaded onto the mine cage for transport deep underground.
