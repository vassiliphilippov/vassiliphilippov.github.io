breadcrumb trail

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrɛdˌkrʌm treɪl / (AmE), / ˈbrɛdkrʌm treɪl / (BrE)
a sequence of clues or pieces of information that leads step by step to a solution or conclusion
series of clues leading to a solution

Examples

The detective followed the breadcrumb trail left by the suspect to uncover the truth.
After hours of research, she finally pieced together the breadcrumb trail of emails.
The computer program leaves a breadcrumb trail so users can retrace their steps if needed.

Synset Examples

He left a breadcrumb trail of clues for the team to follow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clue - a piece of evidence or information used in solving a mystery or problem

Etymology

From 'breadcrumb' (inspired by the trail of bread crumbs left by Hansel and Gretel) + 'trail'.
