baker's dozen

1. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪkərz ˈdʌzən / (AmE), / ˈbeɪkəz ˈdʌz(ə)n / (BrE)
Thirteen; a group or set of thirteen, especially as traditionally supplied by bakers in addition to a dozen.
thirteen; group of thirteen

Examples

To be safe, he ordered a baker's dozen bagels for the meeting.
The shopkeeper tossed an extra apple into the bag, making it a baker's dozen.
Traditionally, a baker's dozen refers to thirteen items rather than twelve.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: dozen - a group or set of twelve

Etymology

From the practice of medieval English bakers giving an extra loaf when selling a dozen, to avoid penalties for short weight.
