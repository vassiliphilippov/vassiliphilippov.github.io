rock wallaby

1. NOUN

/ rɑk ˈwɑːləbi / (AmE), / rɒk ˈwɒləbi / (BrE)
A slender, long-legged Australian wallaby that lives in caves and rocky areas.
Australian wallaby living in rocky habitats

Examples

We spotted a rock wallaby hopping nimbly across the boulders at dusk.
The rock wallaby is well adapted to life in rugged terrain and steep cliffs.
Conservationists are working to protect the habitat of the endangered rock wallaby.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wallaby - a small or medium-sized Australian marsupial similar to, but smaller than, a kangaroo
