superciliary arch

1. NOUN

/ ˌsuː.pɚˈsɪl.i.er ˌɑːrtʃ / (AmE), / ˌsuː.pəˈsɪl.i.ər ˌɑːtʃ / (BrE)
the ridge of bone located above the eye socket on the frontal bone of the skull
ridge of bone above the eye socket
medical

Examples

The superciliary arch is often more prominent in some individuals, particularly in males.
During the anatomy lecture, the professor pointed to the superciliary arch on the skull model.
A fracture near the superciliary arch can result in significant swelling and bruising above the eye.

Etymology

From Latin 'supercilium' (eyebrow) + 'arch'.
