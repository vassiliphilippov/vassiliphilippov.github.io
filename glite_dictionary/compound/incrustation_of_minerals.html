incrustation of minerals

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˌkrʌˈsteɪʃən əv ˈmɪnərəlz / (AmE), / ɪnˌkrʌˈsteɪʃən əv ˈmɪnərəlz / (BrE)
a layer of minerals that forms on the surface of an object, often as a result of chemical or geological processes
layer of minerals forming on a surface

Examples

Over time, the pipes became clogged with a thick incrustation of minerals.
The cave walls glittered due to the incrustation of minerals left by dripping water.
After years in the ground, the artifact was covered in an incrustation of minerals that had to be carefully removed.

Synset Examples

The incrustation of minerals gave the rock its unique color.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: encrustation - a hard outer layer or coating formed on something

Etymology

From Latin 'incrustatio' (covering with a crust) and 'minerale' (mineral).
