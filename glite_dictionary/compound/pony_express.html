Pony Express

1. NOUN

/ ˌpoʊni ɪkˈsprɛs / (AmE), / ˌpəʊni ɪkˈsprɛs / (BrE)
a mail delivery system in the United States (1860–1861) where relays of horseback riders carried mail rapidly across long distances, especially between Missouri and California
historic US mail service by horseback relay

Examples

The Pony Express revolutionized mail delivery in the American West during its brief existence.
Historians still marvel at how quickly the Pony Express could carry letters from Missouri to California.
The young rider dreamed of joining the Pony Express and racing across the vast plains.

Etymology

Named for the use of ponies and rapid 'express' service; established in 1860 in the United States.
