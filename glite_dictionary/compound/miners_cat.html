miner's cat

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪnərz kæt / (AmE), / ˈmaɪnəz kæt / (BrE)
A small, raccoon-like mammal (ringtail) native to Mexico and the southwestern United States, with a long bushy tail marked by black and white rings.
raccoon-like mammal of southwestern US and Mexico

Examples

Old mining towns often had a resident miner's cat searching for scraps near the kitchens.
The miner's cat is agile and skilled at climbing rocks and trees.
Locals explained that the miner's cat helps control rodent populations around cabins.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ringtail - small raccoon-like mammal with a bushy banded tail, also called ringtail cat

Etymology

Named for its frequent presence in miners' camps; 'cat' refers to its feline appearance, though it is not a true cat.
