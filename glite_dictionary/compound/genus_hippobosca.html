genus Hippobosca

1. NOUN

/ ˈhiːpoʊˌbɑskə / (AmE), / ˈhɪpəˌbɒskə / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Hippoboscidae, which includes various species of bloodsucking flies known as louse flies.
type genus of louse flies (Hippoboscidae)

Examples

The genus Hippobosca includes flies that are parasitic on large mammals.
Scientists have studied the life cycle of the genus Hippobosca to better understand louse fly infestations.
Members of the genus Hippobosca are known for their ability to cling to hosts even while grooming.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'hippos' (horse) and 'boskō' (to feed), referring to flies that often infest horses.
