grey hair

1. NOUN

/ ɡreɪ hɛr / (AmE), / ɡreɪ heə / (BrE)
Hair that has turned gray, typically as a result of aging or stress; also used figuratively to refer to stress or worry that causes premature aging.
hair that has turned gray, often from aging or stress

Examples

His grey hair made him look distinguished at the office meeting.
She started noticing grey hair after a year of constant stress at work.
All the worry about her children has given her even more grey hair.

Synset Examples

You’ll get grey hair from worrying about that.
The constant delays in the project are giving me grey hair.
Trying to manage the team is giving me grey hair.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: white hair - Hair that has turned completely white, typically with age
  • Antonym: brown hair - Hair with brown pigmentation, a natural hair color
  • Antonym: blond hair - Hair with a pale yellow or golden color
