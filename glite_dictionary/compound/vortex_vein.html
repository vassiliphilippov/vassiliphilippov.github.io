vortex vein

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɔr.tɛks veɪn / (AmE), / ˈvɔː.tɛks veɪn / (BrE)
a vein formed by branches from the back surface of the eye and the ciliary body, which drains into the ophthalmic veins
vein draining blood from the eye
medical

Examples

The ophthalmologist observed swelling near the vortex vein during the examination.
Blood from the choroid drains through the vortex vein into the ophthalmic veins.
A blockage in a vortex vein can lead to increased pressure in the eye.
