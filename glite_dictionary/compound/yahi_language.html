Yahi language

1. NOUN

/ ˈjɑːhi ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈjɑːhi ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the language historically spoken by the Yahi people, a subgroup of the Yana in north-central California
language of the Yahi people

Examples

Few fluent speakers of the Yahi language remained by the early 20th century.
The Yahi language is an important part of the group's cultural heritage.
Linguists have worked to document and preserve the Yahi language for future generations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Yana language - the wider language family to which Yahi belongs

Etymology

From Yahi, the name of the people, plus 'language.'
