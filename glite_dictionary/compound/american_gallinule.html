American gallinule

1. NOUN

/ əˈmɛrɪkən ˈɡælɪˌn(j)ul / (AmE), / əˈmɛrɪkən ˈɡælɪˌnjuːl / (BrE)
a medium-sized marsh bird of the Americas, known for its bright plumage and long toes, also called the American purple gallinule
a marsh bird of the Americas

Examples

We spotted an American gallinule wading through the dense reeds by the lake.
The bright colors of the American gallinule made it easy to identify among other waterfowl.
During migration season, birdwatchers hope to catch a glimpse of an American gallinule in local wetlands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: purple gallinule - a similar marsh bird of striking purple-blue and green coloring
