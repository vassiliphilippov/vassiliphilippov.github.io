business card

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɪz.nəs kɑrd / (AmE), / ˈbɪz.nəs kɑːd / (BrE)
a small card printed with a person's name, contact details, and professional or business affiliation, typically given to others during introductions or networking
card with personal/business information

Examples

She handed me her business card after our meeting so I could contact her later.
At the conference, every attendee exchanged at least one business card.
His business card includes his job title, phone number, and company logo.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calling card - a card with a person's name, used to announce one's presence or for social visits

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɪznəs kɑrd / (AmE), / ˈbɪznəs kɑːd / (BrE)
a small card printed with a person's name, job title, company, and contact information, used for professional purposes
card with professional contact information

Examples

She handed her business card to the new client before leaving the meeting.
If you need to reach me, my number is on my business card.
He designed a modern business card for his startup company.

Synset Examples

She always carries a business card in her wallet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calling card - card bearing a person's name and sometimes address, used as a form of social introduction
