order Cydippida

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdər sɪˈdɪpɪdə / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə sɪˈdɪpɪdə / (BrE)
a taxonomic order of ctenophores characterized by having two long, feather-like tentacles.
order of ctenophores with two long tentacles

Examples

The order Cydippida includes species with transparent, jelly-like bodies and distinctive long tentacles.
Scientists often study the feeding mechanisms of ctenophores belonging to the order Cydippida.
Many creatures within the order Cydippida are found in both coastal and deep ocean waters.

Etymology

From New Latin: 'Cydippida', derived from 'Cydippe', a genus name within the group.
