capital letters

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæpɪtəl ˈlɛtɚz / (AmE), / ˈkapɪtəl ˈlɛtəz / (BrE)
letters of the alphabet that are written or printed in their large form, used at the beginning of sentences and proper nouns
uppercase letters of the alphabet

Examples

Please print your name in capital letters on the form.
The teacher asked the students to write all headings in capital letters.
He always uses capital letters when signing his name.

Synset Examples

She signed the document in capital letters.
Please write your name in capital letters.
The title was printed in capital letters on the cover.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lowercase letters - letters of the alphabet in their small form
  • Synonym: uppercase - large letters as opposed to lowercase

Etymology

From Latin 'capitalis' (of the head), referring to chief or prominent; letter forms became known as 'capital' for their prominence.
