big white chief

1. NOUN

/ bɪɡ waɪt tʃiːf / (AmE), / bɪɡ waɪt tʃiːf / (BrE)
a person in a position of authority or leadership, often used informally and sometimes with ironic or humorous intent
an important or dominant leader
informal

Examples

The new manager was quickly seen as the big white chief by the rest of the staff.
After years of dedication, he finally became the company's big white chief.
Some employees jokingly referred to the CEO as the big white chief.

Synset Examples

The big white chief made an important announcement.
She called the CEO the big white chief of the company.
The big white chief is set to retire soon.

Etymology

Originally from a phrase used by Native Americans to describe European or American leaders, especially in colonial contexts.
