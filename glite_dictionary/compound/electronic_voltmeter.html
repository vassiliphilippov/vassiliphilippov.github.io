electronic voltmeter

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌlɛkˈtrɑnɪk ˈvoʊltˌmiːtər / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛkˈtrɒnɪk ˈvəʊltˌmiːtə / (BrE)
A voltmeter that uses electronic amplification to increase its sensitivity when measuring voltage.
voltmeter with electronic amplification

Examples

The electronic voltmeter allows for more accurate readings of small voltage changes in sensitive circuits.
Modern laboratories often use an electronic voltmeter instead of traditional analog devices.
She compared the readings from a standard voltmeter and an electronic voltmeter during the experiment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: voltmeter - an instrument for measuring electric potential difference (voltage) between two points
