grade grubber

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡreɪd ˌɡrʌb.ər / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪd ˌɡrʌb.ə / (BrE)
a student who is excessively concerned with getting high grades, often seeking extra points or favors from teachers
student overly concerned with grades
informal american

Examples

The teacher called him a grade grubber for his constant requests.
She's such a grade grubber that she emails the professor about every missed point.
Most students found his grade grubber tendencies a bit annoying.

Synset Examples

The teacher called him a grade grubber for his constant requests.
He's such a grade grubber, always chasing extra credit.
Being a grade grubber won him few friends in class.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brown-noser - a person who tries to win favor by flattering others
  • Synonym: overachiever - a person who achieves more than expected

Etymology

From 'grade' (academic score) + 'grubber' (one who grubs, or works persistently), originally in U.S. student slang.
