cantaloupe vine

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæn.təˌluːp vaɪn / (AmE), / ˈkæn.tə.luːp vaɪn / (BrE)
A variety of muskmelon plant that produces fruit with a tan rind and orange flesh, commonly known as cantaloupe.
muskmelon plant producing cantaloupe

Examples

The cantaloupe vine quickly spread across the garden bed, its leaves shading the soil beneath.
He planted a cantaloupe vine last spring, and by summer, it was full of ripe, sweet fruit.
Pests can damage the cantaloupe vine and reduce the quality of the melons it produces.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: muskmelon - a species of melon plant producing sweet, edible fruit

Etymology

From French *cantaloup*, named after Cantalupo in Italy, where the melon was cultivated.
