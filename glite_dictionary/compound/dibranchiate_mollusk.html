dibranchiate mollusk

1. NOUN

/ daɪˈbræŋ.ki.eɪt ˈmɑː.ləsk / (AmE), / daɪˈbræŋ.kɪ.eɪt ˈmɒl.əsk / (BrE)
A cephalopod mollusk having two gills, such as squids, octopuses, and cuttlefish.
cephalopod with two gills
medical

Examples

The squid is a well-known example of a dibranchiate mollusk found in oceans globally.
Unlike the extinct belemnites, most living cephalopods are dibranchiate mollusks.
Biologists study dibranchiate mollusks to understand their unique methods of locomotion and camouflage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cephalopod - marine mollusk class with large head and arms

Etymology

From Greek 'di-' (two) + 'branchia' (gills) + 'mollusk'.
