Augustinian Canons

1. NOUN

/ ɔːˈgʌstɪniən ˈkænənz / (AmE), / ɔːˈɡʌstɪnɪən ˈkanənz / (BrE)
a religious order of priests who live communally under the Rule of St. Augustine and share all property in common
religious order of priests under St. Augustine's rule

Examples

The Augustinian Canons played an important role in medieval education and charity work.
Many European monasteries were founded by the Augustinian Canons in the Middle Ages.
The community of Augustinian Canons followed a strict rule of poverty and communal living.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Augustinian - relating to St. Augustine or his teachings

Etymology

From St. Augustine (A.D. 354–430), early Christian theologian, plus 'canons' (members of a religious order).
