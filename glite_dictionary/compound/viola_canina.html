Viola canina

1. NOUN

/ vaɪˈoʊlə kəˈnaɪnə / (AmE), / vɪˈəʊlə kəˈniːnə / (BrE)
A species of violet native to Europe and Asia, with slender leafy stems and blue to lilac flowers.
a Eurasian blue-flowered violet

Examples

The Viola canina brightened the woodland floor with its delicate blue flowers.
She planted several Viola canina in her garden to attract butterflies.
Botanists studied the habitat preferences of Viola canina in different European regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dog violet - common name for several blue-flowered violets, including Viola canina

Etymology

From Latin, where 'viola' means violet and 'canina' means 'of or pertaining to dogs'.
