to weather the cold

1. VERB

/ ˈwɛðɚ ðə koʊld / (AmE), / ˈwɛðə ðə kəʊld / (BrE)
to endure or survive harsh cold conditions, especially for a period of time
endure harsh cold conditions

Examples

Many animals hibernate to weather the cold months of winter.
Residents had to find ways to weather the cold during the power outage.
We stocked up on supplies to help us weather the cold season ahead.

Synset Examples

We need to weather the cold until spring arrives.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brave the cold - to face or endure cold weather without being deterred
  • Synonym: to weather the storm - to survive a difficult situation or period
