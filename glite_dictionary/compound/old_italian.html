Old Italian

1. NOUN

/ oʊld ɪˈtæljən / (AmE), / əʊld ɪˈtæliən / (BrE)
The form of the Italian language spoken and written up to the middle of the 16th century.
form of Italian before mid-16th century

Examples

Scholars study Old Italian to understand the origins of modern Italian vocabulary and grammar.
Dante's Divine Comedy is a masterpiece written in Old Italian.
Some manuscripts from the Renaissance were originally composed in Old Italian.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Modern Italian - the standard Italian language used from the 17th century onwards
  • Synonym: Old French - the French language up to about the 14th century
