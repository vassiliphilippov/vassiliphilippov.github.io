ceremonial mass

1. NOUN

/ səˌroʊˈmoʊniəl mæs / (AmE), / səˌrəʊˈməʊniəl mæs / (BrE)
a formal religious service, especially in the Christian tradition, marked by ritual and solemnity
formal religious service

Examples

The archbishop led the ceremonial mass attended by hundreds of worshippers.
A special ceremonial mass was held to commemorate the anniversary of the cathedral.
The choir performed beautifully during the ceremonial mass on Christmas Eve.

Synset Examples

The archbishop presided over the ceremonial mass with great dignity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mass - the main religious service in the Roman Catholic Church
