sandarac tree

1. NOUN

/ ˈsæn.dəˌræk ˌtriː / (AmE), / ˈsæn.dəræk ˌtriː / (BrE)
A large evergreen coniferous tree of North Africa and Spain, characterized by flattened branches, scalelike leaves, and hard fragrant wood; its bark yields a resin used in varnishes.
evergreen coniferous tree of North Africa and Spain

Examples

The sandarac tree provides a resin that is valued in fine varnish making.
Old cabinets were often finished with lacquer containing sandarac tree resin.
In parts of Morocco, the sandarac tree plays an important role in traditional crafts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Thuya - another name for the sandarac tree (botanical genus)
  • Synonym: arborvitae - a related type of coniferous evergreen tree

Etymology

From Medieval Latin sandaraca, from Arabic ṣandara, originally from Greek sandarake (red lead, resin).
