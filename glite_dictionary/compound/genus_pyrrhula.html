genus Pyrrhula

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs pɪˈruːlə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs pɪˈrʊlə / (BrE)
A genus of small passerine birds known as bullfinches, found in Eurasia and recognized for their stout bill and vibrant plumage.
a genus of Eurasian bullfinches (birds)

Examples

The genus Pyrrhula includes several species of bullfinch found across Europe and Asia.
Recent studies have reclassified some birds previously assigned to the genus Pyrrhula.
The brightly colored Eurasian bullfinch is a well-known member of the genus Pyrrhula.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bullfinch - a small songbird with a stout bill and bright plumage

Etymology

From Latin 'Pyrrhula', the genus name assigned in scientific classification, itself derived from Latin for the Eurasian bullfinch.
