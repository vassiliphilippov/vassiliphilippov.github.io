interstitial tissue

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪntɚˈstɪʃəl ˈtɪʃu / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈstɪʃəl ˈtɪsjuː / (BrE)
tissue located between the cells of a structure or part, especially in plants or animals
tissue between the cells of an organ or structure
medical

Examples

Fluid accumulates in the interstitial tissue during inflammation.
In some diseases, the interstitial tissue becomes thickened and fibrous.
The interstitial tissue plays an important role in supporting organ function.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: parenchyma - the functional tissue of an organ as distinguished from the interstitial tissue

Etymology

From Latin interstitium (interval, space between) + tissue.
