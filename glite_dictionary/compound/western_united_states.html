western United States

1. PROPN

/ ˈwɛstərn ˌjuːnəˈtaɪts / (AmE), / ˈwɛstən ˌjuːnɪtɪd ˈsteɪts / (BrE)
the region of the United States located to the west of the Mississippi River, often including states such as California, Nevada, Colorado, and others.
region west of the Mississippi River in the US

Examples

Many people move to the western United States for its natural beauty and opportunities.
The climate in the western United States varies dramatically from deserts to mountains.
Gold was discovered in the western United States in the 19th century, sparking mass migration.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Midwest - the region of the United States north of the Ohio River and west of the Appalachian Mountains
  • Antonym: eastern United States - the region of the United States east of the Mississippi River
