common wood sorrel

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmən wʊd ˈsɔrəl / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən wʊd ˈsɒrəl / (BrE)
a small Eurasian plant (Oxalis acetosella) with heart-shaped, three-part leaves and white flowers marked with purple veins, typically found in woodlands
Eurasian plant with trifoliate leaves and white flowers

Examples

We found patches of common wood sorrel carpeting the forest floor beneath the beech trees.
The delicate flowers of the common wood sorrel added a splash of white to the shaded woodland.
She identified the plant as common wood sorrel by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves and purple-veined petals.
