multi-valued

1. ADJ

/ ˌmʌltiˈvæl.juːd / (AmE), / ˌmʌl.tiˈvæl.juːd / (BrE)
having or characterized by more than one value, meaning, or appeal; not limited to a single interpretation or outcome.
having multiple values or meanings

Examples

The philosopher discussed the concept of a multi-valued logic system in his lecture.
Her paintings are intentionally multi-valued, open to several interpretations by different viewers.
In computer science, a multi-valued attribute can take on more than one value for a single entity.

Synset Examples

subtle, multivalent allegory

Related Terms

  • Synonym: multivalent - having many values, meanings, or appeals

Etymology

Formed from 'multi-' (Latin for 'many') + 'valued.'
