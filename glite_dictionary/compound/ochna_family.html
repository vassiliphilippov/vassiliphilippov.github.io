ochna family

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑknə ˈfæməli / (AmE), / ˈɒknə ˈfæməli / (BrE)
a family of tropical evergreen trees and shrubs with thick, shiny, parallel-veined leaves, scientifically known as Ochnaceae
family of tropical evergreen trees and shrubs

Examples

The ochna family includes over 500 species of tropical plants.
Many members of the ochna family are valued for their ornamental flowers.
The thick, shiny leaves are a distinctive feature of trees in the ochna family.

Etymology

From Ochna, the type genus of the family, from Greek okhna (wild pear).
