virtual reality

1. NOUN

/ ˌvɝː.tʃu.əl riˈælə.ti / (AmE), / ˌvɜː.tʃu.əl riˈæl.ə.ti / (BrE)
a computer-generated three-dimensional environment in which a person can interact with simulated objects or surroundings, typically using special equipment such as goggles or gloves
interactive computer-generated 3D environment

Examples

This game uses virtual reality to create an immersive fantasy world for players.
With the help of virtual reality, students can explore ancient Rome from their classroom.
She experienced heights and flying through virtual reality at the tech fair.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: augmented reality - an enhanced version of reality where digital information is overlaid on the real world

Etymology

From 'virtual' (computer-generated) + 'reality' (state of things as they exist), first used in the context of computer simulation in the late 20th century.
