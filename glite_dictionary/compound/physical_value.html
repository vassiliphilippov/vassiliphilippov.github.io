physical value

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪzɪkəl ˈvæljuː / (AmE), / ˈfɪzɪkəl ˈvæljuː / (BrE)
the cost to reproduce a physical property, subtracting allowances such as depreciation
reproduction cost minus depreciation (property valuation)

Examples

The physical value of the building was assessed after accounting for years of wear and tear.
Insurance claims often reference the physical value rather than the market value of assets.
An appraisal determined that the physical value of the equipment was significantly less due to depreciation.
