drying agent

1. NOUN

/ ˈdraɪɪŋ ˌeɪdʒənt / (AmE), / ˈdraɪɪŋ ˌeɪdʒənt / (BrE)
a substance that promotes drying by absorbing or removing moisture from its surroundings
substance that promotes drying

Examples

Silica gel is often used as a drying agent in packaging to keep products moisture-free.
The laboratory stored chemicals with a drying agent to prevent clumping from humidity.
Calcium chloride is a common drying agent for drying gases and organic solvents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: desiccant - a substance used to absorb moisture
  • Antonym: humidifier - a device that adds moisture to the air
