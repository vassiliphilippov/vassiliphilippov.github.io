to enter the scene

1. VERB

/ ˈɛntər ðə siːn / (AmE), / ˈɛntə ðə siːn / (BrE)
to arrive at a place or situation, often marking the start of involvement or a new phase in events
arrive at a place or situation

As the negotiations stalled, a new mediator suddenly entered the scene.
The detective entered the scene quietly, scanning the room for clues.
Several new technologies entered the scene in the early 21st century, changing the industry dramatically.

Synset Examples
