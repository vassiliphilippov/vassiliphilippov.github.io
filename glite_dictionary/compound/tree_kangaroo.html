tree kangaroo

1. NOUN

/ tri ˈkæŋɡəˌru / (AmE), / triː ˈkæŋɡəˌruː / (BrE)
A marsupial found in New Guinea and northern Australia, adapted to life in trees, with hind and forelegs of similar length.
arboreal marsupial of New Guinea and Australia

Examples

The tree kangaroo is skilled at climbing and spends most of its life in the forest canopy.
Scientists recently discovered a new species of tree kangaroo in Papua New Guinea.
Unlike ground-dwelling wallabies, the tree kangaroo has strong forelimbs for gripping branches.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kangaroo - a large marsupial native to Australia
  • Synonym: wallaby - a smaller marsupial similar to the kangaroo
