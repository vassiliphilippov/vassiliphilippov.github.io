bulk mail

1. NOUN

/ bʌlk meɪl / (AmE), / bʌlk meɪl / (BrE)
mail consisting of large quantities of identical items, such as advertisements or circulars, sent to many recipients at a reduced postal rate and paid for in one lot.
large quantities of identical mail sent at reduced rates

Examples

The company sent out thousands of brochures as bulk mail to potential customers.
He receives so much bulk mail each week that it fills his mailbox.
Charities often use bulk mail for their fundraising appeals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: junk mail - unsolicited advertising or promotional material sent by post
