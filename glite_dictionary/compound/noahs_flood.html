Noah's flood

1. NOUN

/ ˈnoʊ.əz flʌd / (AmE), / ˈnəʊ.əz flʌd / (BrE)
the great flood described in the Book of Genesis, sent by God in the time of Noah to destroy the world because of human wickedness
the Biblical deluge in the time of Noah

Examples

Many ancient cultures have stories similar to Noah's flood in their myths and legends.
Scholars debate whether Noah's flood was based on a real historical event.
Some refer to powerful floods as a modern-day Noah's flood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deluge - a severe flood, often used to refer to overwhelming or Biblical floods
  • Synonym: Noah - the biblical figure who built an ark and survived the flood

Etymology

From the biblical figure Noah in the Book of Genesis, who survived the flood.
