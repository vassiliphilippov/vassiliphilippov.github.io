man in the moon

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæn ɪn ðə ˈmun / (AmE), / ˌmæn ɪn ðə ˈmuːn / (BrE)
a supposed figure imagined to be visible on the surface of the moon, often perceived as a face or human shape
figure imagined on the moon's surface

Examples

As a child, she loved pointing out the man in the moon to her friends on clear nights.
Many ancient legends explain how the man in the moon came to be there.
On quiet evenings, he would gaze up and imagine the man in the moon smiling down at him.

Synset Examples

He looked so calm; like the man in the moon.
She often joked about him being the man in the moon.
It felt like he was the man in the moon, so distant.

Etymology

Refers to old European folk interpretations of moon markings as the image of a man.
