inside clinch

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪn.saɪd ˈklɪntʃ / (AmE), / ˈɪn.saɪd ˈklɪntʃ / (BrE)
a knot in which the end of the line passes through a loop, commonly used to secure fishing hooks or other objects.
fishing knot with line end inside loop

Examples

He secured the fishing hook with an inside clinch to make sure it wouldn't slip.
Many anglers prefer the inside clinch because of its strength and reliability.
After tying the inside clinch, she gave the line a sharp tug to test the knot.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clinch knot - a knot used to secure a line to a hook or ring
