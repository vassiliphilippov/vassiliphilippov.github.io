marble bones disease

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑrbəl boʊnz dɪˌziz / (AmE), / ˈmɑːbəl bəʊnz dɪˌziːz / (BrE)
an inherited disorder in which bones become unusually dense and hard, sometimes leading to the loss of bone marrow cavities
rare inherited disease with very dense bones
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed the child with marble bones disease after reviewing the X-rays showing unusually dense bones.
Patients with marble bones disease often require regular monitoring due to potential complications.
Because the bone marrow cavity may be affected, marble bones disease can lead to issues with blood cell production.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: osteopetrosis - medical term for marble bones disease
