Little Missouri River

1. PROPN

/ ˌlɪt̬l̩ məˈzʊri ˈrɪvɚ / (AmE), / ˌlɪtl məˈzʊəri ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
A river in the north-central United States that originates in northeastern Wyoming and flows through Montana and South Dakota before joining the Missouri River in North Dakota.
tributary river in the north-central United States

Examples

The Little Missouri River is famous for its scenic badlands and wildlife viewing opportunities.
Flooding along the Little Missouri River has impacted local ranchers in recent years.
Hikers enjoy following the Little Missouri River through Theodore Roosevelt National Park.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Missouri River - major river in the central United States
