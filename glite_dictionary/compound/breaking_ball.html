breaking ball

1. NOUN

/ ˈbreɪkɪŋ bɔl / (AmE), / ˈbreɪkɪŋ bɔːl / (BrE)
a pitch in baseball that is thrown with spin, causing the ball to move in a curved path as it approaches the batter
baseball pitch that curves

Examples

The pitcher threw a sharp breaking ball that fooled the batter completely.
His signature breaking ball drops just before reaching the plate, making it hard to hit.
She practiced her breaking ball until batters couldn't predict its movement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curveball - a pitch in baseball that curves as it approaches the plate
