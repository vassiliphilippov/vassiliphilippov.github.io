spirits of wine

1. NOUN

/ ˈspɪrɪts əv waɪn / (AmE), / ˈspɪrɪts əv waɪn / (BrE)
rectified ethyl alcohol; highly concentrated, purified alcohol used mainly in laboratory or industrial contexts.
rectified ethyl alcohol; purified alcohol

Examples

The laboratory technician carefully poured spirits of wine into the beaker for the experiment.
Early chemists often used spirits of wine as a solvent in their research.
To sterilize the instruments, the doctor used pure spirits of wine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ethanol - the type of alcohol found in alcoholic drinks and used as a solvent or fuel
  • Synonym: ethyl alcohol - another term for ethanol, the principal alcohol in alcoholic beverages
