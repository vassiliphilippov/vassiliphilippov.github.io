family Trichiuridae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli trɪˌkjuːəˈraɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli trɪˌkjuːəˈrɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of marine fishes known as cutlassfishes, characterized by their elongated, ribbon-like bodies.
family of marine cutlassfishes

Examples

The family Trichiuridae includes several species of commercially important cutlassfishes.
Biologists have studied the feeding habits of the family Trichiuridae in tropical waters.
Some members of the family Trichiuridae are commonly called ribbonfish due to their shape.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cutlassfish - a marine fish with a long, slender, silver body; member of Trichiuridae

Etymology

From New Latin 'Trichiuridae', the type genus 'Trichiurus' plus the suffix '-idae', denoting animal families.
