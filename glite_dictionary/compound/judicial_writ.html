judicial writ

1. NOUN

/ dʒuˈdɪʃəl rɪt / (AmE), / dʒuˈdɪʃəl rɪt / (BrE)
a legal document issued by a court or judicial officer, ordering that something be done or not done
court-issued legal order
legal

Examples

The lawyer filed a judicial writ to prevent the demolition of the building.
A judicial writ was served to compel the company to release the documents.
The judge issued a judicial writ mandating the immediate release of the detainee.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: writ - a formal written order issued by a body with administrative or judicial jurisdiction

Etymology

From Latin 'judicialis' (of judgment) and Old English 'writ' (something written).
