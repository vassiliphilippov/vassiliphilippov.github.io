let's go, Brandon

1. X

/ ˈlɛts ɡoʊ ˈbrændən / (AmE), / ˈlɛts ɡəʊ ˈbrændən / (BrE)
A political slogan used as a euphemism for an insult against U.S. President Joe Biden, expressing dissent or criticism.
political slogan expressing dissent
american informal

Examples

The crowd started chanting let's go, Brandon at the football game.
He wore a shirt that said let's go, Brandon as a political statement.
During the protest, some participants held signs with the phrase let's go, Brandon.

Synset Examples

During the rally, they proudly shouted, 'let's go, Brandon!'
The crowd chanted, 'let's go, Brandon' to express their feelings.

Etymology

Originated in 2021 from a misinterpretation of a crowd chant during a televised interview with NASCAR driver Brandon Brown. The chant was adopted as a euphemism for a derogatory phrase directed at President Joe Biden.
