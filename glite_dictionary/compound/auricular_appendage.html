auricular appendage

1. NOUN

/ ɔːˈrɪkjələr əˈpɛndɪdʒ / (AmE), / ɔːˈrɪkjʊlə əˈpɛndɪdʒ / (BrE)
a small, ear-shaped pouch projecting from the upper front part of each atrium of the heart
pouch on each atrium of the heart
medical

Examples

During heart surgery, the auricular appendage may be removed to reduce the risk of blood clots.
Doctors used imaging to check the size of the left auricular appendage before the procedure.
The auricular appendages play a role in regulating blood flow within the heart.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: auricle - the external part of the ear or an atrium of the heart

Etymology

From Latin 'auricula' (little ear) + English 'appendage'.
