bone-shaking

1. ADJ

/ ˈboʊnˌʃeɪkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbəʊnˌʃeɪkɪŋ / (BrE)
extremely intense or jarring, especially so as to be felt throughout the body; often used to describe loud sounds or physical vibrations.
extremely intense or jarring

Examples

The concert opened with a bone-shaking blast of music that left the audience in awe.
She woke up to the bone-shaking rumble of an earthquake.
The roller coaster’s bone-shaking turns thrilled and terrified the riders.

Synset Examples

The thunder was bone-shaking during the storm.
His laughter was bone-shaking and contagious.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deafening - extremely loud or overwhelming in sound
  • Antonym: mild - not strong, intense, or extreme

Etymology

Formed from 'bone' + present participle of 'shake', referring to an effect so great as to be felt in the bones.
