Sida spinosa

1. NOUN

/ ˌsaɪdə spaɪˈnoʊsə / (AmE), / ˌsɪdə spaɪˈnəʊsə / (BrE)
A tropical American weed with pale yellow or orange flowers, now common in the southern United States.
tropical American weed with yellow or orange flowers

Examples

Sida spinosa often invades disturbed areas and crop fields in tropical and subtropical regions.
Farmers in the region have tried various methods to control Sida spinosa due to its aggressive spread.
The pale yellow flowers of Sida spinosa make it recognizable among local weeds.

Etymology

From Latin, where 'Sida' is a plant name and 'spinosa' means 'spiny'.
