Torrid Zone

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɔrɪd zoʊn / (AmE), / ˈtɒrɪd zəʊn / (BrE)
the region of the Earth's surface between the Tropic of Cancer and the Tropic of Capricorn, known for its consistently hot climate
tropical region of the Earth

Examples

Much of the world’s rainforests are located within the Torrid Zone.
The climate in the Torrid Zone is characterized by high temperatures throughout the year.
Explorers in the 19th century were fascinated by the diversity of life in the Torrid Zone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tropics - the region between the Tropic of Cancer and the Tropic of Capricorn

Etymology

From Latin 'torridus' (parched, scorched) and 'zone' from Greek 'zōnē' (belt, girdle).
