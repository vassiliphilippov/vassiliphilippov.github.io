destructive distillation

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈstrʌktɪv dɪˌstɪləˈveɪʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈstrʌktɪv dɪˌstɪlɪˈveɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of heating a solid material in a closed vessel so that it decomposes and gives off volatile products, which are collected and separated.
process of decomposing solids by heating without air

Examples

Destructive distillation of coal yields coke, coal tar, and coal gas.
Charcoal is made from wood by destructive distillation.
The process of destructive distillation is used to obtain chemicals from organic solids.

Etymology

From Latin destructivus (causing destruction) + distillatio (distillation).
