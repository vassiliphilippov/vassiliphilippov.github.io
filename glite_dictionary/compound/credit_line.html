credit line

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɛdɪt laɪn / (AmE), / ˈkrɛdɪt laɪn / (BrE)
an amount of money that a bank or financial institution agrees to lend to a customer, up to a stated maximum, to be used when needed
maximum amount of money a customer can borrow

Examples

The bank increased my credit line after reviewing my payment history.
Before applying for a loan, check if your credit line is sufficient for your needs.
Having a higher credit line can improve your financial flexibility.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: credit limit - the maximum amount of credit available to a customer

2. NOUN

/ ˈkrɛdɪt laɪn / (AmE), / ˈkrɛdɪt laɪn / (BrE)
a line in a newspaper, magazine, or other publication that gives the name of the writer of an article or story
line giving author's name in publication

Examples

The article included a credit line giving full recognition to the author.
Please check that your credit line is spelled correctly in the magazine proof.
Every photo in the exhibition had a credit line identifying the photographer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: byline - a line in a newspaper or magazine naming the writer
