pharyngeal consonant

1. NOUN

/ fəˌrɪndʒiəl ˈkɑnsənənt / (AmE), / fəˌrɪndʒiːəl ˈkɒnsənənt / (BrE)
a consonant sound produced by constricting the back of the throat (pharynx) during articulation
consonant sound made in the pharynx
medical

Examples

Certain Arabic dialects include a distinctive pharyngeal consonant not found in English.
Learning to pronounce a pharyngeal consonant can be challenging for English speakers.
A pharyngeal consonant involves constricting the throat during speech.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: uvular consonant - consonant pronounced with the back of the tongue against the uvula
  • Degree Higher: glottal consonant - consonant articulated with the glottis

Etymology

From Greek pharynx (throat) and Latin consonans (sounding together).
