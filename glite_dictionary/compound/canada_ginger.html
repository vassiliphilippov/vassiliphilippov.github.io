Canada ginger

1. NOUN

/ ˈkænədə ˈdʒɪndʒər / (AmE), / ˈkænədə ˈdʒɪndʒə / (BrE)
A low-growing perennial plant native to Canada and eastern and central United States, known for its heart-shaped leaves and ginger-scented rhizomes.
a North American perennial plant with ginger-scented roots

Examples

The roots of Canada ginger have been used as a spice by Indigenous peoples.
In shaded woodlands, Canada ginger carpets the ground with its broad leaves.
She planted Canada ginger in her garden for its attractive foliage and fragrant roots.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wild ginger - a plant with aromatic roots used as a ginger substitute
