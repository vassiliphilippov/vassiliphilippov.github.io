great grey kangaroo

1. NOUN

/ ɡreɪt ɡreɪ ˈkæŋɡəˌru / (AmE), / ɡreɪt ɡreɪ ˈkæŋɡəruː / (BrE)
A very large, greyish-brown Australian kangaroo formerly abundant in open wooded areas, now rare.
large Australian kangaroo species

Examples

The great grey kangaroo once roamed freely across southern Australia before its numbers declined.
Researchers documented the behavior of the endangered great grey kangaroo in the open woodlands.
Many wildlife books describe the impressive leap of the great grey kangaroo.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kangaroo - large marsupial native to Australia
