postmortem examination

1. NOUN

/ ˌpoʊstˈmɔrtəm ɪɡˌzæmɪˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌpəʊstˈmɔːtəm ɪɡˌzæmɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
An examination and dissection of a dead body to determine the cause of death or the changes produced by disease.
examination of a dead body to find cause of death
medical

Examples

The coroner ordered a postmortem examination to determine the cause of her unexpected death.
After the postmortem examination, the doctors confirmed the presence of a rare heart condition.
The law requires a postmortem examination in cases of unexplained or suspicious deaths.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: autopsy - an examination of a body after death to determine the cause
  • Antonym: biopsy - removal and examination of tissue from a living body

Etymology

From Latin 'post mortem' (after death) and English 'examination'.
