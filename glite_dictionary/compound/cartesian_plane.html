Cartesian plane

1. NOUN

/ kɑːrˈtiʒən pleɪn / (AmE), / kɑːˈtiːzɪən pleɪn / (BrE)
A plane defined by two perpendicular axes, in which each point is specified by a pair of Cartesian coordinates.
mathematical plane with x and y axes

Examples

On a Cartesian plane, each point is located using an x and y coordinate.
The graph was plotted on the Cartesian plane to show the relationship.
Teachers often use the Cartesian plane to introduce students to graphing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coordinate plane - a plane divided by axes for coordinate pairs

Etymology

From the name of René Descartes, French mathematician, whose work led to the Cartesian coordinate system.
