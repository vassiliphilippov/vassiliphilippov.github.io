genus Theobroma

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs θiː.oʊˈbroʊ.mə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs θɪəˈbrəʊ.mə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical American trees and shrubs, including the cacao plant, known for producing seeds used to make chocolate.
a tropical genus including the cacao tree

Examples

The genus Theobroma includes the cacao tree, which is vital for the production of chocolate.
Botanists have identified several species within the genus Theobroma, most notably Theobroma cacao.
Native to Central and South America, the genus Theobroma thrives in tropical rainforests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cacao - the tree from which cocoa beans are harvested

Etymology

From Greek theos (god) + broma (food), meaning 'food of the gods'.
