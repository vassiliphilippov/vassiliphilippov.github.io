discriminative stimulus

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈskrɪmɪnətɪv ˈstɪmjuləs / (AmE), / dɪsˈkrɪmɪnətɪv ˈstɪmjʊləs / (BrE)
In behavioral psychology, a stimulus that signals the availability of reinforcement, indicating which behavior will be rewarded.
stimulus signaling specific behavior will be rewarded

Examples

A green light acted as a discriminative stimulus for the rats to press the lever.
In training, the bell was used as a discriminative stimulus to signal food was available.
A discriminative stimulus helps individuals determine when certain responses will lead to rewards.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cue - a signal that prompts behavior in psychology

Etymology

From 'discriminative' (from Latin 'discriminare', to distinguish) + 'stimulus' (from Latin 'stimulus', a goad or incentive).
