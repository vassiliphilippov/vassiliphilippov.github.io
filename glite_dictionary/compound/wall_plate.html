wall plate

1. NOUN

/ wɑl pleɪt / (AmE), / wɔːl pleɪt / (BrE)
A horizontal timber laid along the top of a wall to support the ends of joists or rafters and distribute their load.
timber on wall supporting joists or rafters

Examples

The carpenter installed a wall plate on top of the brickwork to support the new rafters.
Before securing the floor joists, they anchored the wall plate firmly along the perimeter.
Regular inspection of the wall plate is important to prevent structural damage from rot.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sill plate - a timber at the bottom of a wall supporting structural elements
