iron boot

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪɚn buːt / (AmE), / ˈaɪən buːt / (BrE)
A device of torture consisting of a boot made of iron, used to heat or crush the foot and leg.
device of torture for crushing or burning the foot

Examples

Prisoners feared the iron boot, which was designed to inflict maximum pain on the leg and foot.
Historical records describe the use of the iron boot during medieval interrogations.
The museum displayed an iron boot as an example of early torture devices.
