membrane filter

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛmˌbreɪn ˈfɪltɚ / (AmE), / ˈmɛm.breɪn ˈfɪl.tə / (BrE)
A filter made of a thin film of material such as cellulose acetate or collodion, with defined pore sizes, used to separate particles and microorganisms from liquids or gases.
filter with thin, porous membrane for separation

Examples

The laboratory technician used a membrane filter to sterilize the solution before the experiment.
Bacteria can be removed from water by passing it through a membrane filter with small enough pores.
All samples were processed using a 0.22 micron membrane filter for maximum retention of microorganisms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: filter - a device used to remove impurities from liquids, gases, or light
