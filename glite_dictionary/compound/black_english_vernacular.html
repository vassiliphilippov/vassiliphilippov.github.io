Black English Vernacular

1. NOUN

/ blæk ˈɪŋglɪʃ vərˈnækjələr / (AmE), / blak ˈɪŋglɪʃ vəˈnækjʊlə / (BrE)
a variety of American English, with distinct grammatical, pronunciation, and vocabulary features, characteristically spoken by many African Americans
African American variety of American English

Examples

Many linguists study Black English Vernacular to understand its grammar and cultural history.
Films set in urban communities often feature characters who speak Black English Vernacular.
Some educators believe that recognizing Black English Vernacular in the classroom can benefit students' learning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: African American Vernacular English - another term for Black English Vernacular
  • Antonym: Standard American English - the mainstream, standardized form of American English
