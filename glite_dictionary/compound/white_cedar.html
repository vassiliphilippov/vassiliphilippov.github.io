white cedar

1. NOUN

/ waɪt ˈsiː.dɚ / (AmE), / waɪt ˈsiː.də / (BrE)
A slow-growing, medium-sized cedar tree native to the east coast of the United States, resembling the American arborvitae.
North American cedar tree species

Examples

The white cedar is commonly found in wetland areas along the east coast.
Many traditional canoes were crafted from the wood of white cedar.
The aromatic wood of white cedar is valued for its resistance to decay.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arborvitae - a North American evergreen tree similar to the white cedar

2. NOUN

/ waɪt ˈsiːdər / (AmE), / waɪt ˈsiːdə / (BrE)
A small evergreen tree native to eastern North America, with tiny scalelike leaves on flattened branchlets, often used for ornamental purposes or timber.
evergreen tree of eastern North America

Examples

The white cedar is commonly planted in gardens for its attractive foliage.
Many wild birds nest in the thick branches of the white cedar.
The timber from white cedar is valued for its resistance to decay.
