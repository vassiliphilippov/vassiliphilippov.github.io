physical chemistry

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪzɪkəl ˈkɛmɪstri / (AmE), / ˈfɪzɪkəl ˈkɛmɪstri / (BrE)
the branch of chemistry that deals with the physical properties and behavior of chemical substances and the changes they undergo during reactions
branch of chemistry studying physical properties

Examples

She decided to specialize in physical chemistry because she enjoys both math and chemical reactions.
The principles of physical chemistry are essential for understanding thermodynamics and kinetics.
He is writing a textbook on physical chemistry for undergraduate students.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: chemistry - science of substances and their interactions
  • Degree Higher: organic chemistry - branch of chemistry focused on carbon compounds
  • Degree Higher: inorganic chemistry - branch of chemistry dealing with inorganic compounds

Etymology

Formed from 'physical' (relating to nature and matter) and 'chemistry.'
