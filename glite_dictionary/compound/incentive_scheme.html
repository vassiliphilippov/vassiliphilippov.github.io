incentive scheme

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈsɛntɪv skiːm / (AmE), / ɪnˈsɛntɪv skiːm / (BrE)
A formal plan or system designed to motivate people, particularly employees, to perform certain actions or achieve specific goals, often by offering rewards or benefits.
system of rewards to motivate action

Examples

The company introduced a new incentive scheme to boost employee productivity.
Many sales teams benefit from a well-structured incentive scheme that rewards top performers.
She received a substantial bonus as part of the annual incentive scheme.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bonus - an extra payment to reward good performance
  • Synonym: commission - a payment based on sales or performance
