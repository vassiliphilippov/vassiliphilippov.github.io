blue marlin

1. NOUN

/ blu ˈmɑrlɪn / (AmE), / bluː ˈmɑːlɪn / (BrE)
a large species of marlin, found in warm seas worldwide, noted for its blue coloration and sometimes exceeding 900 kg (2000 lbs) in weight
large marlin species found in warm seas

Examples

The blue marlin is prized by sport fishermen for its size and strength.
We spotted a massive blue marlin leaping out of the water during our boat trip.
Catching a blue marlin is considered a rare achievement among anglers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marlin - a large game fish with an elongated upper jaw
