mental block

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛn.t̬l ˌblɑk / (AmE), / ˈmɛn.təl ˌblɒk / (BrE)
A temporary inability to remember or think of something, often due to emotional stress or anxiety.
temporary inability to think or remember

Examples

During the exam, I had a mental block and forgot everything I had studied.
Writers often experience a mental block when starting a new project.
She finally overcame her mental block after taking a short break.

Synset Examples

I knew his name perfectly well but I had a temporary block

Related Terms

  • Synonym: block - an obstacle or hindrance

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɛn.t̬l ˌblɑk / (AmE), / ˈmɛn.tl ˌblɒk / (BrE)
a psychological obstacle that prevents someone from thinking clearly or remembering something
inability to think clearly

Examples

After the accident, he developed a mental block about driving on highways.
She tried to remember the answer, but a mental block stopped her from recalling it during the test.
Despite hours of preparation, the athlete suffered a mental block at the crucial moment.

Synset Examples

He faced a mental block while writing his thesis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: writer's block - temporary inability to write
