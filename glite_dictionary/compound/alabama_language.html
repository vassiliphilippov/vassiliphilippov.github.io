Alabama language

1. NOUN

/ ˌæl.əˈbæmə ˈlæŋ.ɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌal.əˈbam.ə ˈlaŋ.ɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Muskogean language spoken by the Alabama people, indigenous to the southeastern United States.
Muskogean language of the Alabama people

Examples

Few people today speak the Alabama language fluently.
The preservation of the Alabama language is important for cultural heritage.
Linguists have documented traditional stories in the Alabama language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Muskogean language - a family of Native American languages spoken in the southeastern US

Etymology

Named after the Alabama people, indigenous group of the southeastern United States.
