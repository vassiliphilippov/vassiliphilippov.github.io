meningeal artery

1. NOUN

/ məˈnɪndʒiəl ˈɑːrtəri / (AmE), / məˈnɪndʒiəl ˈɑːtəri / (BrE)
any of several arteries that supply blood to the meninges of the brain and adjacent structures
artery supplying the brain's meninges
medical

Examples

The most clinically significant meningeal artery is the middle meningeal artery, often involved in epidural hematomas.
A rupture of a meningeal artery can lead to severe neurological complications if not treated promptly.
During neurosurgery, special care is taken to avoid damaging any meningeal artery.

Etymology

From Greek *mēninx* (membrane) and Latin *arteria* (artery).
