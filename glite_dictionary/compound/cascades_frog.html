Cascades frog

1. NOUN

/ kæˈskeɪdz frɑɡ / (AmE), / kæˈskeɪdz frɒɡ / (BrE)
a medium-sized frog native to the mountainous regions near water in the northwestern United States and California
frog native to US Northwest mountains

Examples

The Cascades frog inhabits alpine meadows and streams throughout the Cascade Mountains.
Recent conservation efforts have focused on protecting the Cascades frog from habitat loss and climate change.
Scientists are studying the Cascades frog to understand its response to environmental shifts in the Pacific Northwest.

Etymology

Named after the Cascade Range, the primary region of the frog's habitat.
