baptismal name

1. NOUN

/ bæpˈtɪzməl neɪm / (AmE), / bæpˈtɪzməl neɪm / (BrE)
the name given to a person at baptism, especially in Christian tradition, often used as a first name
Christian given name at baptism

Examples

She chose Mary as her baptismal name when she was christened as a child.
His baptismal name differed from the nickname he used among friends.
The certificate listed both his baptismal name and his surname.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: given name - a person's first name or personal name
  • Antonym: surname - a family name or last name

Etymology

From baptismal (pertaining to baptism) + name; 'baptism' from Greek 'baptisma' (immersion).
