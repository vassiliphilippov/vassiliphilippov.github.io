lightning rod

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪtnɪŋ ˌrɑd / (AmE), / ˈlaɪtnɪŋ ˌrɒd / (BrE)
A person who frequently attracts criticism, controversy, or anger, especially as a way to divert attention from others.
person who attracts criticism/diverts attention

Examples

The mayor became a lightning rod for criticism during the crisis.
As a controversial figure, she often serves as a lightning rod for political attacks.
His outspoken views have made him the lightning rod of the company.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scapegoat - a person who is blamed for the wrongdoings of others

Etymology

Originally from the literal device that protects buildings from lightning; figurative use dates from the 19th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪtnɪŋ ˌrɑd / (AmE), / ˈlaɪtnɪŋ ˌrɒd / (BrE)
a metal rod or wire fixed to the highest point of a structure and connected to the ground, designed to protect the building from lightning strikes by directing the electrical current safely away
metal rod protecting buildings from lightning

Examples

The old farmhouse was equipped with a lightning rod to prevent fire during storms.
When the thunderstorm approached, the presence of a lightning rod provided peace of mind to the residents.
Modern skyscrapers always include a lightning rod as part of their safety features.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conductor - material or object that permits electric current to flow easily
