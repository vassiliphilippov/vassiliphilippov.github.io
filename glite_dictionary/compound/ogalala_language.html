Ogalala language

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊɡəˈlɑːlə ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌəʊɡəˈlɑːlə ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Siouan language spoken by the Oglala people, a subgroup of the Lakota Native Americans
the language of the Oglala Lakota

Examples

The Ogalala language preserves many traditional stories and rituals among the Oglala Lakota people.
Linguists have documented the grammar and vocabulary of the Ogalala language to help in its revitalization.
She grew up hearing the Ogalala language spoken at home by her grandparents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Lakota language - the Siouan language spoken by the Lakota people

Etymology

From Oglala, the name of the people, from Lakota meaning 'to scatter one's own' + English 'language'.
