order Moniliales

1. NOUN

/ ˌɔrdər ˌmoʊnɪˈlaɪəliːz / (AmE), / ˌɔːdə ˌmɒnɪˈlaɪəliːz / (BrE)
an order of imperfect fungi that lack conidiophores or have superficial conidiophores not enclosed in a pycnidium
order of imperfect fungi

Examples

Researchers discovered a new species belonging to the order Moniliales in the tropical rainforest.
Many plant diseases are caused by fungi classified under the order Moniliales.
The identification of members of the order Moniliales often relies on microscopic characteristics.

Etymology

From New Latin Moniliales, from Latin monile (necklace), referring to the beaded appearance of spores in some species.
