Red Sea

1. PROPN

/ rɛd si / (AmE), / rɛd siː / (BrE)
a long, narrow sea between northeast Africa and the Arabian Peninsula, connected to the Mediterranean Sea by the Suez Canal
sea between Africa and Arabian Peninsula

Examples

The Red Sea is famous for its vibrant coral reefs and diverse marine life.
Ships often travel through the Red Sea to reach the Suez Canal.
The climate around the Red Sea is generally hot and dry year-round.
