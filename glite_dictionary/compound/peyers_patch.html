Peyer's patch

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪərz pætʃ / (AmE), / ˈpaɪəz pætʃ / (BrE)
one of the small, oval lymphoid nodules found in the wall of the small intestine, especially near the junction of the ileum and colon, involved in immune responses
lymphoid nodule in the intestine
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that inflammation of a Peyer's patch may be involved in gastrointestinal diseases.
Researchers studied the immune response within a Peyer's patch to understand its role in the body.
During the procedure, the surgeon examined several Peyer's patches for any signs of infection.

Etymology

Named after Johann Conrad Peyer (1653–1712), the Swiss anatomist who first described them.
