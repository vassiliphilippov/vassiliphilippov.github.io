Lake Superior

1. PROPN

/ leɪk səˈpɪriər / (AmE), / leɪk sjuːˈpɪəriə / (BrE)
the largest freshwater lake in the world, located on the border between the United States and Canada; the deepest and most voluminous of the Great Lakes
the largest North American Great Lake

Examples

We visited Lake Superior last summer and were amazed by its size.
The storm caused high waves on Lake Superior, making navigation difficult.
Wildlife thrives along the shores of Lake Superior.

Etymology

From the French 'Lac Supérieur,' meaning 'Upper Lake.'
