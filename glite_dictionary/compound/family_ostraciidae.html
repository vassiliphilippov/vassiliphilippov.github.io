family Ostraciidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌfæməli ɒstrəˈsaɪɪdiː / (AmE), / ˌfæməli ɒstrəˈsɪɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of small, box-shaped marine fishes known as boxfishes, characterized by their rigid, bony carapace.
family of box-shaped marine fishes (boxfishes)

Examples

The family Ostraciidae includes species that have a unique, box-like structure.
Researchers discovered a new member of the family Ostraciidae along the coral reefs.
Many aquarists are fascinated by the colorful patterns of fishes in the family Ostraciidae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boxfish - a small tropical marine fish with a rigid, box-shaped body

Etymology

From modern Latin *Ostraciidae*, derived from Greek *ostrakon* (shell).
