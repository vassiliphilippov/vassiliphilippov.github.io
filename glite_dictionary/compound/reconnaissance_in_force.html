reconnaissance in force

1. NOUN

/ rɪˌkɑnəˈsɑns ɪn fɔrs / (AmE), / rɪˌkɒnəˈsɑːns ɪn fɔːs / (BrE)
a military operation undertaken with combat strength to discover or test the enemy's capabilities and gather information
military operation to test or probe enemy strength

Examples

The general ordered a reconnaissance in force to gauge the enemy's positions before launching the main assault.
A reconnaissance in force revealed unexpected resistance along the front lines.
By conducting a reconnaissance in force, the commanders were able to gather crucial intelligence about enemy troop strength.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: probe - a small-scale attack to test enemy defenses
  • Degree Higher: attack - an offensive military operation against enemy forces

Etymology

From French 'reconnaissance' (recognition, scouting) and English phrase 'in force'.
