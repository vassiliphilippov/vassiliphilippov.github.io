field of vision

1. NOUN

/ fiːld əv ˈvɪʒən / (AmE), / fiːld əv ˈvɪʒən / (BrE)
the entire area that can be seen at one time by one eye without moving it
entire area visible to the eye at once

Examples

Wearing glasses can slightly alter your field of vision.
Peripheral objects may fall outside the field of vision if you stare straight ahead.
A narrow field of vision can make driving more difficult at night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: visual field - the area visible to the eye when looking in a particular direction
