diaper dermatitis

1. NOUN

/ ˈdaɪ.pɚ ˌdɜːr.məˈtaɪ.tɪs / (AmE), / ˈdaɪ.pə ˌdɜː.məˈtaɪ.tɪs / (BrE)
A form of skin inflammation affecting the thighs and buttocks of infants, often caused by irritation from urine or feces in a diaper.
skin inflammation in infants caused by diapers
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed the baby with diaper dermatitis after noticing redness and irritation on the skin.
Frequent diaper changes can help prevent diaper dermatitis in infants.
Parents often use ointments to soothe diaper dermatitis caused by prolonged moisture exposure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nappy rash - skin irritation in infants from diapers (British English)
  • Degree Higher: eczema - condition causing inflamed, itchy, cracked skin
  • Degree Lower: skin irritation - mild reaction of the skin to an irritant

Etymology

From 'diaper' (cloth worn by infants) + 'dermatitis' (from Greek derma, 'skin', + -itis, 'inflammation').
