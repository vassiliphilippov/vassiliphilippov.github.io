disc drive

1. NOUN

/ dɪsk draɪv / (AmE), / dɪsk draɪv / (BrE)
A computer hardware device that holds and spins a magnetic or optical disk in order to read and write data.
device reading/writing computer disks

Examples

The new computer comes with a built-in disc drive for playing DVDs.
She inserted the installation CD into the disc drive to begin the setup process.
With cloud storage becoming more popular, fewer laptops include a disc drive these days.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disk drive - Device reading/writing computer disks; alternative spelling
