pitch pine

1. NOUN

/ pɪtʃ paɪn / (AmE), / pɪtʃ paɪn / (BrE)
A large pine tree (Pinus rigida) native to the eastern United States, with long needles and gnarled, twisted limbs, valued for its timber.
a North American pine tree species

Examples

The forest was dominated by pitch pine and oak trees.
After the wildfire, pitch pine saplings began to sprout along the ridgeline.
Early settlers used wood from the pitch pine for building and shipmaking.

2. NOUN

/ pɪtʃ paɪn / (AmE), / pɪtʃ paɪn / (BrE)
a large pine tree of eastern North America with long, stiff needles in bundles of three, valued for its resinous wood
a North American pine tree species

Examples

The forest was dominated by pitch pine and scrub oak.
Early settlers used pitch pine wood to make tar and turpentine.
Fires promote the growth of pitch pine by opening its cones and aiding seed dispersal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pond pine - a closely related pine tree found in the southeastern United States
