Atsugewi language

1. NOUN

/ æt.suˈɡeɪ.wi ˈlæŋ.ɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / æt.suˈɡeɪ.wi ˈlæŋ.ɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Native American language formerly spoken by the Atsugewi people of northeastern California
Shastan language once spoken in California

Examples

Only a handful of elders still remembered the Atsugewi language before it became extinct.
Linguists have compiled dictionaries and recordings to preserve the Atsugewi language.
The Atsugewi language belongs to the Shastan language family of northeastern California.

Etymology

From 'Atsugewi', the name of the indigenous people who spoke the language.
