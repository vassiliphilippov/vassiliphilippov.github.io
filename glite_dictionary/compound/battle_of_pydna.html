Battle of Pydna

1. PROPN

/ ˈbæt̬l əv ˈpɪdnə / (AmE), / ˈbatl əv ˈpɪdnə / (BrE)
a major battle in 168 BC in which Roman forces defeated the Macedonians, leading to the end of the ancient Macedonian kingdom
major Roman victory over Macedonians in 168 BC

Examples

The Battle of Pydna marked the final collapse of Macedonian power in Greece.
Historians often cite the Battle of Pydna as a turning point in Roman military history.
Artifacts from the Battle of Pydna are displayed in several European museums.
