white sale

1. NOUN

/ waɪt seɪl / (AmE), / waɪt seɪl / (BrE)
A special sale in a store featuring household linens, such as bed sheets, towels, and tablecloths, often at reduced prices.
sale of household linens

Examples

The department store is having a white sale on all bedding and bath towels this weekend.
During the annual white sale, Marsha bought new sheets and pillowcases at a discount.
Retailers often advertise a white sale after the holidays to clear out excess inventory.

Etymology

From the traditional use of white fabrics for linens and the retail practice of holding sales on such items.
