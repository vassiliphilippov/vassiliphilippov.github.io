travelling wave

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrævəlɪŋ weɪv / (AmE), / ˈtrævəlɪŋ weɪv / (BrE)
A wave in which the medium or disturbance moves in the direction of the wave's propagation.
wave moving in direction of propagation

Examples

The travelling wave on the string carried energy from one end to the other.
In a travelling wave, the peaks and troughs move continuously through the medium.
Engineers studied the travelling wave generated by the earthquake to improve building designs.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: standing wave - a wave that remains in a constant position
