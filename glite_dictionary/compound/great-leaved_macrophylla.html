great-leaved macrophylla

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡreɪt ˈlivd ˌmækroʊˈfɪlə / (AmE), / ˌɡreɪt ˈliːvd ˌmæk.rəʊˈfɪl.ə / (BrE)
A large deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States, known for its huge leaves arranged in dense false whorls and large creamy flowers tinged with purple at the base.
large shrub or tree with huge leaves and creamy flowers

Examples

The great-leaved macrophylla is prized by gardeners for its dramatic foliage and large flowers.
Botanists collected samples of great-leaved macrophylla during their field trip to the southern woods.
The shady garden was dominated by a mature great-leaved macrophylla in full bloom.
