disease of the neuromuscular junction

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈziz əv ðə nuˌroʊˈmʌs.jə.lɚ ˈdʒʌŋk.ʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈziːz əv ðə njʊərəʊˈmʌs.kjʊ.lə ˈdʒʌŋk.ʃən / (BrE)
a disease characterized by impairment or dysfunction at the neuromuscular junction, affecting communication between nerves and muscles
disorder affecting nerve-muscle communication
medical

Examples

Myasthenia gravis is a well-known disease of the neuromuscular junction that leads to muscle weakness.
Researchers are developing new treatments for disease of the neuromuscular junction to improve patients' quality of life.
A disease of the neuromuscular junction can disrupt the communication between nerves and muscles, causing a variety of symptoms.

Etymology

From medical Latin: 'neuromuscular' (relating to nerves and muscles) + 'junction' (point of connection).
