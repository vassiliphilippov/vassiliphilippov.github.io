Wichita language

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪtʃɪtɑ ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈwɪtʃɪtɑː ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Caddoan language formerly spoken by the Wichita people of the central United States
a Native American Caddoan language

Examples

Linguists have been working to document the Wichita language before it disappears completely.
The Wichita language has only a few fluent speakers left, most of whom are elderly.
She is taking a course on the grammar and structure of the Wichita language.

Etymology

Named after the Wichita people, whose name comes from a Native American term meaning 'scattered lodges'.
