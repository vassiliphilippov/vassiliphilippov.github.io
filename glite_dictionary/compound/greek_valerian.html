Greek valerian

1. NOUN

/ ɡriːk vəˈlɛriən / (AmE), / ɡriːk vəˈlɛːriən / (BrE)
A European perennial plant with pinnate leaves and bright blue or white flowers, commonly found in gardens.
European perennial with blue or white flowers

Examples

We planted Greek valerian along the garden path for its striking blue flowers.
The delicate blooms of Greek valerian appear in late spring and last into early summer.
Gardeners appreciate Greek valerian for its attractive foliage and ease of care.

2. NOUN

/ ɡriːk vəˈlɛriən / (AmE), / ɡriːk vəˈlɛəriən / (BrE)
An erect or spreading perennial herb native to the eastern United States, known for its clusters of blue or purple flowers.
a perennial herbaceous plant of eastern US

Examples

Clusters of blue flowers made the Greek valerian a favorite in the garden.
We planted Greek valerian along the border for its long-lasting blooms.
The Greek valerian thrives in partly shaded woodland areas.

Etymology

Named for its resemblance to true valerian plants; 'Greek' refers to the similar appearance to European species.
