bargaining chip

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑːrɡənɪŋ tʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈbɑːɡənɪŋ tʃɪp / (BrE)
something that can be used to gain an advantage in negotiations by being offered or withheld as an inducement or concession
negotiation advantage or inducement

Examples

The company used the new patent as a bargaining chip in negotiations with their rivals.
He offered to drop the lawsuit as a bargaining chip for a better settlement.
Access to the exclusive market served as a valuable bargaining chip during the trade talks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lever - something used as a means to achieve a result, especially in negotiations
  • Synonym: advantage - a condition or circumstance that puts one in a favorable position
  • Antonym: liability - something that puts one at a disadvantage
