laurel oak

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɔrəl oʊk / (AmE), / ˈlɔːrəl əʊk / (BrE)
A large, nearly semi-evergreen oak tree native to the southeastern United States, thriving in damp soil.
a semi-evergreen oak tree of the southeastern US

Examples

The laurel oak can grow quickly and provides excellent shade in southern landscapes.
Several birds made their nests in the branches of the laurel oak near the river.
Because it prefers moist conditions, the laurel oak is often found along streams and wetlands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oak - a tree of the genus Quercus, known for its strong wood and lobed leaves

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɔrəl oʊk / (AmE), / ˈlɒrəl əʊk / (BrE)
a small deciduous tree native to eastern and central United States, with shiny leaves resembling those of the laurel; its wood is sometimes used for shingles.
a deciduous tree with laurel-like leaves

Examples

The laurel oak is often planted along city streets for its attractive foliage.
Many birds nest in the branches of the laurel oak.
In some western states, wood from the laurel oak is used for making shingles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oak - a tree or shrub of the genus Quercus, producing acorns as fruit

Etymology

Named for its glossy, laurel-like leaves combined with 'oak', the type of tree.
