circumbendibus route

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɝː.kəmˈbɛn.dɪ.bəs ruːt / (AmE), / ˌsɜː.kəmˈbɛn.dɪ.bəs ruːt / (BrE)
a route that is indirect or roundabout, rather than direct
an indirect or roundabout route

Examples

The taxi took a circumbendibus route through the city, increasing our travel time.
He preferred a circumbendibus route to avoid the main highways during rush hour.
Instead of going straight, the hike followed a circumbendibus route around the lake.

Synset Examples

She took a circumbendibus route to explain her decision.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: detour - a route that is different from the usual or planned one, often longer
  • Antonym: shortcut - a route shorter than the usual one

Etymology

From Latin 'circum' (around) + 'bend' + English suffix '-ibus' (pseudo-Latin form, from the term 'circumbendibus' meaning roundabout).
