Gay-Lussac's law

1. NOUN

/ ɡeɪ luːˈsækz lɔ / (AmE), / ɡeɪ lʊˈsaks lɔː / (BrE)
In physics, the principle that the pressure of a given mass of gas varies directly with its absolute temperature when the volume is kept constant.
gas law: pressure–temperature relationship

Examples

According to Gay-Lussac's law, increasing the temperature of a gas at constant volume raises its pressure.
Many introductory physics courses discuss Gay-Lussac's law when covering the behavior of gases.
Engineers use Gay-Lussac's law to predict how pressure changes in heated gas containers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Charles's law - gas law relating volume and temperature at constant pressure

Etymology

Named after French chemist Joseph Louis Gay-Lussac (1778–1850).
