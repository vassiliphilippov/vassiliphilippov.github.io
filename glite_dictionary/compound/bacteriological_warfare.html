bacteriological warfare

1. NOUN

/ bækˌtɪriəˈlɑdʒɪkəl ˈwɔrˌfɛr / (AmE), / bækˌtɪəriəˈlɒdʒɪkəl ˈwɔːˌfeə / (BrE)
the use of bacteria or other biological agents as weapons to cause disease or death, especially in warfare
warfare using bacteria as weapons

Examples

During the conflict, there were allegations of bacteriological warfare being used against civilian populations.
International treaties strictly prohibit the practice of bacteriological warfare.
The military trained personnel to respond to threats of bacteriological warfare in the field.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: biological warfare - the use of biological toxins or infectious agents as weapons
  • Antonym: chemical warfare - the use of toxic chemical substances as weapons

Etymology

From 'bacteriological', relating to bacteria (from Greek bakterion), and 'warfare'.
