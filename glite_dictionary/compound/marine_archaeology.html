marine archaeology

1. NOUN

/ məˈrin ɑrkiˌɑlədʒi / (AmE), / məˈriːn ɑːkiˈɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the study of human history and prehistory through the excavation and analysis of underwater sites, structures, and artifacts
study of underwater archaeological sites

Examples

Recent advances in technology have greatly expanded the field of marine archaeology.
She specializes in marine archaeology, focusing on shipwrecks in the Mediterranean Sea.
The discovery of ancient harbors is a significant achievement in marine archaeology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: archaeology - the study of human history and prehistory through excavation of sites and analysis of artifacts

Etymology

marine from Latin 'marinus' (of the sea) + archaeology from Greek 'arkhaiologia' (study of ancient things) via Latin and French.
