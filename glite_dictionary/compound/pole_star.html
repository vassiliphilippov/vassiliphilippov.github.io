pole star

1. NOUN

/ poʊl stɑr / (AmE), / pəʊl stɑː / (BrE)
the star situated nearly in direct line with the Earth's axis above the North Pole, currently Polaris, used for navigation; the brightest star in Ursa Minor.
the star marking the North Pole direction

Examples

Sailors have long relied on the pole star to find their way at sea.
The pole star appears almost stationary in the night sky.
Many ancient cultures used the pole star for navigation and to align their monuments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Polaris - the current pole star; Alpha Ursae Minoris
  • Synonym: North Star - another common name for the pole star

2. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊl stɑr / (AmE), / ˈpəʊl stɑː / (BrE)
A person, principle, or thing that serves as a guiding influence or source of inspiration.
guiding influence or inspiration

Examples

She regarded her grandmother's wisdom as her pole star during difficult times.
Throughout his career, honesty remained his pole star.
For many artists, creativity acts as a pole star guiding their work.

Synset Examples

He always looked to his mentor as his pole star.
In her life, her children are her pole star.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guiding star - something that is a guiding influence in a person's life

Etymology

From the literal 'pole star', the star (Polaris) near the north celestial pole, used for navigation.
