lady's thistle

1. NOUN

/ ˈleɪdiz ˈθɪsəl / (AmE), / ˈleɪdiz ˈθɪsəl / (BrE)
a tall biennial thistle with large white-blotched leaves and purple flower heads, originally from the Old World and now naturalized in California and South America
a tall thistle with white-blotched leaves

Examples

The lady's thistle is recognizable by its striking white-veined leaves.
Gardeners in California sometimes cultivate lady's thistle for its ornamental value.
You can find lady's thistle growing wild along sunny hillsides in South America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: milk thistle - a similar thistle, Silybum marianum, often called lady's thistle
