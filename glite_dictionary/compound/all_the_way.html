all the way

1. ADV

/ ɔl ðə weɪ / (AmE), / ɔːl ðə weɪ / (BrE)
as far as possible; the whole distance or extent to a goal or endpoint.
for the whole distance or extent

Examples

She ran all the way to the top of the hill without stopping.
We drove all the way across the country in just five days.
He supported his friend all the way to the end of the project.

Synset Examples

she climbed the mountain all the way

Related Terms

  • Antonym: part way - to some extent but not the whole distance
