bass line

1. NOUN

/ beɪs laɪn / (AmE), / beɪs laɪn / (BrE)
the underlying rhythm or melody in a piece of music played by the bass instruments, typically forming the harmonic and rhythmic foundation
underlying musical rhythm or melody (in bass)

Examples

The song's bass line is so catchy that it gets stuck in your head for days.
He spent hours practicing the bass line to improve his technique.
The bass line drives the groove and gives the music its depth and rhythm.

Synset Examples

She loves to dance to that funky bass line.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: melody - a sequence of single musical notes that is musically satisfying
  • Synonym: harmony - the combination of simultaneously sounded musical notes to produce chords
