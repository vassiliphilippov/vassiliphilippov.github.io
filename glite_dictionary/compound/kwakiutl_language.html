Kwakiutl language

1. NOUN

/ kwɑːˈkjuːtəl ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / kwəˈkjuːtəl ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Wakashan language traditionally spoken by the Kwakiutl people of British Columbia, Canada
Wakashan language of the Kwakiutl people

Examples

Linguists have documented efforts to preserve the Kwakiutl language among younger generations.
The Kwakiutl language features sounds that are uncommon in English phonology.
She is studying the Kwakiutl language as part of her research on indigenous cultures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Wakashan - a family of indigenous languages of British Columbia and Washington State

Etymology

Kwakiutl: from the indigenous name of the Kwakwaka'wakw people.
