Book of Genesis

1. NOUN

/ ˌbʊk əv ˈdʒɛnəsɪs / (AmE), / ˌbʊk əv ˈdʒɛnəsɪs / (BrE)
the first book of the Old Testament in the Bible, describing the creation of the world and early biblical history, including Adam and Eve, the Flood, and the patriarchs.
first book of the Old Testament

Examples

Many theological debates are rooted in passages from the Book of Genesis.
She studied the story of Noah's Ark as told in the Book of Genesis.
The Book of Genesis is often cited for its account of the creation of the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Bible - the sacred scriptures of Christianity, including the Old and New Testaments

Etymology

From Greek *Genesis* (origin, birth), via Latin from the Hebrew Bible.
