consumer spending

1. NOUN

/ kənˈsumɚ ˈspɛndɪŋ / (AmE), / kənˈsjuːmə ˈspɛndɪŋ / (BrE)
the total amount of money spent by households on goods and services within an economy
household expenditure on goods and services

Examples

During a recession, consumer spending typically declines as people become more cautious with their money.
Rising wages can lead to an increase in consumer spending across all sectors.
Strong consumer spending is often seen as a sign of a healthy economy.

Synset Examples

Deflation can lead to decreased consumer spending.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: consumption - the using up of goods and services by consumers
