cubic foot

1. NOUN

/ ˈkjuːbɪk ˌfʊt / (AmE), / ˈkjuːbɪk ˌfʊt / (BrE)
A unit of volume equal to a cube measuring one foot on each side, used in the United States and some other countries.
unit of volume (one foot on each side)

Examples

The shipping container has a capacity of 2,400 cubic feet.
One cubic foot of water weighs about 62.4 pounds.
They measured the gas consumption in cubic feet per hour.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cubic meter - A unit of volume equal to a cube measuring one meter on each side
  • Degree Lower: gallon - A common unit of liquid volume in the US and UK
