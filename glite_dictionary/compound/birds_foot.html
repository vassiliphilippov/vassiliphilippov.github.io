bird's foot

1. NOUN

/ bɝdz fʊt / (AmE), / bɜːdz fʊt / (BrE)
the foot of a bird, typically consisting of toes and claws adapted for perching, walking, or swimming
foot of a bird

Examples

The bird's foot gripped the branch tightly as it perched overhead.
Scientists studied the structure of the bird's foot to understand how it helps in walking and swimming.
The injured bird's foot made it difficult for the animal to land safely.
