political education

1. NOUN

/ pəˈlɪdɪkəl ˌɛdʒʊˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / pəˈlɪtɪkəl ˌɛdjʊˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
instruction in political systems, ideologies, and governance, often to promote specific beliefs or civic understanding
instruction on political ideology and governance

Examples

The school introduced political education to encourage students' civic participation.
During the seminar, activists discussed the importance of political education in democracy.
The party organized workshops on political education for new members.

Synset Examples

The film depicted a commissar overseeing political education.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: civic education - instruction in the rights and duties of citizenship
