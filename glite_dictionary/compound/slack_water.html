slack water

1. NOUN

/ ˈslæk ˌwɔtɚ / (AmE), / ˈslæk ˌwɔːtə / (BrE)
A period when the water in a tidal river or sea is relatively still, occurring at the turn of the tide when the tidal current changes direction.
period of still water at turn of the tide

Examples

Boats waited patiently for slack water before attempting to enter the narrow channel.
It is safest to dive during slack water, when the currents are weakest.
The fishermen planned their trip to coincide with slack water at dawn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slack tide - the moment when a tidal current is at its weakest or still

2. NOUN

/ slæk ˈwɔt̬ɚ / (AmE), / slæk ˈwɔːtə / (BrE)
A stretch of water in a river, estuary, or sea where there is little or no current or movement.
water with little or no current

Examples

The boat drifted peacefully in the slack water near the riverbank.
Experienced sailors know to wait for slack water before attempting to dock during strong tides.
We anchored in a small bay where the slack water made swimming safe and easy.
