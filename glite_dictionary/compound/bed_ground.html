bed ground

1. NOUN

/ bɛd ɡraʊnd / (AmE), / bɛd ɡraʊnd / (BrE)
an area where a group of cattle or sheep settles down to sleep for the night
area where livestock sleep at night

Examples

The rancher led the herd to a safe bed ground before nightfall.
Coyotes sometimes circle the bed ground looking for stray animals.
After a long day of grazing, the sheep gathered on the bed ground to rest.
