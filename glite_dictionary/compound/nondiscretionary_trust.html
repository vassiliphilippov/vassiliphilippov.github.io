nondiscretionary trust

1. NOUN

/ ˌnɑn.dɪˈskrɛʃ.əˌnɛri trʌst / (AmE), / ˌnɒn.dɪˈskrɛʃ.ənəri trʌst / (BrE)
an investment trust required to invest only in securities specified at the time of its creation, with no flexibility to alter holdings later
trust limited to preselected investments

Examples

The nondiscretionary trust could not change its portfolio even as market conditions shifted.
Because it was a nondiscretionary trust, the manager was unable to react to new investment opportunities.
Many conservative investors prefer a nondiscretionary trust for its clear, set investment policy.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: discretionary trust - a trust where the trustee has the power to decide which assets to buy or sell
