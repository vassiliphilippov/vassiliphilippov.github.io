Upper Paleolithic

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌpɚ ˌpeɪliəˈlɪθɪk / (AmE), / ˈʌpə ˌpæliəˈlɪθɪk / (BrE)
the later part of the Paleolithic era, marked by the presence of only modern Homo sapiens, lasting until about 10,000 BC
later part of the Old Stone Age

Examples

Many cave paintings were created during the Upper Paleolithic period.
Archaeologists study stone tools from the Upper Paleolithic to learn about early human life.
The Upper Paleolithic saw the development of complex language and art.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Lower Paleolithic - the earliest subdivision of the Paleolithic era
  • Degree Lower: Middle Paleolithic - the middle subdivision of the Paleolithic era
  • Degree Higher: Mesolithic - the period following the Paleolithic, bridging to the Neolithic

Etymology

From Greek palaios (old) + lithos (stone), referring to the Old Stone Age; 'Upper' denotes the later phase.
