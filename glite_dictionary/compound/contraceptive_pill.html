contraceptive pill

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːntrəˈsɛptɪv pɪl / (AmE), / ˌkɒntrəˈsɛptɪv pɪl / (BrE)
A pill containing hormones, taken to prevent pregnancy by inhibiting ovulation.
hormonal birth control pill
medical

Examples

She started taking the contraceptive pill when she went to university.
The doctor explained the possible side effects of the contraceptive pill.
Not everyone experiences weight gain while on the contraceptive pill.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: birth control pill - A pill used to prevent pregnancy by hormonal means
  • Degree Higher: morning-after pill - A pill taken after intercourse to prevent pregnancy
  • Antonym: condom - A barrier method of contraception
