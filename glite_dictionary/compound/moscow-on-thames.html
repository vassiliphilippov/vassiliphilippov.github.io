Moscow-on-Thames

1. PROPN

/ ˈmɑskoʊ ɑn ˈθɛmz / (AmE), / ˈmɒskəʊ ɒn ˈtɛmz / (BrE)
A metonym for London's financial district, especially when highlighting perceived Russian influence or presence in the area.
London’s financial district, esp. with Russian ties

Examples

After the influx of Russian investors, some journalists began calling the City Moscow-on-Thames.
Accusations of money laundering led to renewed debate about London's role as Moscow-on-Thames.
The newspaper published a feature on real estate deals in Moscow-on-Thames.

Synset Examples

The financial district is often called Moscow-on-Thames.
Many refer to the city’s business area as Moscow-on-Thames.
Moscow-on-Thames is bustling with international finance.

Etymology

Formed by combining Moscow (the capital of Russia) and Thames (the main river running through London). Modelled on similar constructs like 'Venice-on-the-Hudson'.
