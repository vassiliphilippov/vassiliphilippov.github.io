old dunny

1. NOUN

/ oʊld ˈdʌni / (AmE), / əʊld ˈdʌni / (BrE)
an old-fashioned outdoor toilet, often a simple structure located outside the house
old-style outdoor toilet
informal british

Examples

Growing up in the country, we had an old dunny at the back of the yard.
The old dunny was finally replaced with a modern toilet.
Visitors were amused by the old dunny behind the cottage.

Synset Examples

The old dunny was finally replaced with a modern toilet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: outhouse - a simple outdoor toilet, especially in rural areas

Etymology

Dunny is Australian slang from British dialect 'dunnekin' (toilet), likely from 'dungeon' or 'dunne' (meaning privy).
