in meatspace

1. ADV

/ ɪn ˈmiːtˌspeɪs / (AmE), / ɪn ˈmiːtˌspeɪs / (BrE)
in the physical world, as opposed to online or virtual environments; in real life.
in the physical (real) world
informal slang

Examples

After talking for months in a forum, we met in meatspace for coffee.
Many online friendships never move beyond interactions in meatspace.
Some prefer solving issues face to face, that is, in meatspace, rather than over email.

Synset Examples

They finally connected in meatspace after years of chatting online.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: in real life - in actual, physical existence rather than on the internet or in fiction
  • Antonym: online - on the internet or in a virtual environment

Etymology

Slang term emerging in the 1990s, combining 'meat' (referring to the body) and 'space' to contrast with 'cyberspace'.
