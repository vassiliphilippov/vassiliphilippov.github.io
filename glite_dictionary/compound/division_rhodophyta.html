division Rhodophyta

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈvɪʒən roʊˈdɑːfɪtə / (AmE), / dɪˈvɪʒən rəʊˈdɒfɪtə / (BrE)
A major group of mostly marine algae, commonly known as red algae, found chiefly in coastal waters.
a group of mainly marine red algae

Examples

Many species in the division Rhodophyta are important sources of agar and carrageenan.
The vivid color of division Rhodophyta makes them easy to spot among other seaweeds.
Scientists are studying the genetic diversity within division Rhodophyta.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: red algae - common name for Rhodophyta, marine algae

Etymology

From New Latin Rhodophyta, from Greek 'rhodon' (rose) and 'phyton' (plant).
