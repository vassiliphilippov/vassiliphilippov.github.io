cashier's cheque

1. NOUN

/ kæˈʃɪrz tʃɛk / (AmE), / kæˈʃɪəz tʃɛk / (BrE)
A cheque issued by a bank, drawn on its own funds and signed by a bank officer, guaranteeing payment to the recipient.
bank-issued cheque guaranteeing payment

Examples

She paid for the new car with a cashier's cheque issued by her bank.
Some landlords require a cashier's cheque for the first month's rent and security deposit.
A cashier's cheque is generally considered more secure than a personal cheque.

Synset Examples

cashier's checks are as good as cash

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bank draft - an order to pay money drawn by one bank on another

Etymology

From 'cashier' (bank officer responsible for money) and 'cheque', meaning a written order for payment.
