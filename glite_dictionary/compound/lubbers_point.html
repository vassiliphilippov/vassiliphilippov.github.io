lubber's point

1. NOUN

/ ˈlʌbɚz pɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˈlʌbəz pɔɪnt / (BrE)
a fixed mark or line on a ship's compass that indicates the direction in which the vessel is heading
mark on a ship's compass showing heading

Examples

The helmsman checked the lubber's point to ensure the ship stayed on course.
When steering the vessel, always align the heading with the lubber's point for accuracy.
A new layer of paint made the lubber's point clearly visible on the compass.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compass card - the circular card under the compass needle marked with directions and degrees

Etymology

From 'lubber', an old nautical term for an inexperienced seaman, combined with 'point' referring to the mark on the compass.
