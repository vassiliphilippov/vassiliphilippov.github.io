processional march

1. NOUN

/ prəˈsɛʃənl mɑrtʃ / (AmE), / prəˈsɛʃənəl mɑːtʃ / (BrE)
a piece of music with a marching rhythm, played during a procession, especially at ceremonial or formal events
march played during a procession

Examples

The bride walked down the aisle to a solemn processional march played by the organist.
A traditional processional march marked the beginning of the graduation ceremony.
During the parade, a processional march echoed through the streets as the dignitaries arrived.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: march - a piece of music with a strong regular rhythm, written for marching

Etymology

From 'procession' (from Latin processio, 'a going forward') and 'march' (from French marche, 'walk').
