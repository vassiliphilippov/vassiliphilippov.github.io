step rocket

1. NOUN

/ stɛp ˈrɑːkɪt / (AmE), / stɛp ˈrɒkɪt / (BrE)
a rocket composed of two or more stages, each with its own engine and fuel, fired in succession and discarded when depleted to improve efficiency
multi-stage rocket with separate engines

Examples

The engineers designed a new step rocket to carry heavy payloads into orbit.
When the first stage separated, the step rocket continued its journey with the second engine.
Most modern space missions use a step rocket for greater efficiency and range.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: multistage rocket - a rocket with more than one stage that is jettisoned in sequence

Etymology

From 'step,' meaning stage or phase, and 'rocket'; refers to separation into stages ('steps').
