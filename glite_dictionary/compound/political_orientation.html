political orientation

1. NOUN

/ pəˈlɪdɪkəl ˌɔriənˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / pəˈlɪtɪkəl ˌɔːrɪənˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
a person's or group's set of beliefs and attitudes about politics, often determining their position on political issues or parties
beliefs and attitudes about politics

Examples

Her political orientation shifted significantly after she started reading a wider range of news sources.
The survey aimed to map the political orientation of young voters across the country.
Universities often foster discussions among students with different political orientation and backgrounds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ideology - a system of ideas, especially those forming the basis of political theory and policy
