Armistice Day

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːrmɪstɪs deɪ / (AmE), / ˈɑːmɪstɪs deɪ / (BrE)
The anniversary of the armistice signed at the end of World War I on November 11, formerly a public holiday in the US and still observed in some countries.
commemoration of WWI armistice (Nov 11)

Examples

On Armistice Day, we pause to remember those who gave their lives in World War I.
The parade on Armistice Day has long been a tradition in the town.
Before it became Veterans Day, November 11 was known as Armistice Day in the United States.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Veterans Day - US holiday that replaced Armistice Day, honoring military veterans
  • Synonym: Remembrance Day - Day observed in Commonwealth countries to commemorate war dead

Etymology

From 'armistice' (Latin armistitium, a truce), marking the end of World War I on November 11, 1918.
