new town

1. NOUN

/ nuː taʊn / (AmE), / njuː taʊn / (BrE)
a planned town built in a previously undeveloped area, designed to be self-sufficient with its own housing, education, commerce, and recreation facilities.
planned, self-sufficient urban community

Examples

The government decided to build a new town to ease congestion in the capital.
Many families moved to the new town in search of better housing and jobs.
Each new town was carefully planned with its own schools, shops, and green spaces.
