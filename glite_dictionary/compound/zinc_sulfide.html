zinc sulfide

1. NOUN

/ zɪŋk ˈsʌlfaɪd / (AmE), / zɪŋk ˈsʌlfaɪd / (BrE)
a yellow to white crystalline compound (ZnS) that occurs naturally as sphalerite or wurtzite, and is used as a luminous pigment and in optical materials
a fluorescent compound used as pigment

Examples

The screen glowed faintly due to the zinc sulfide coating applied to it.
Natural zinc sulfide can be found in the mineral sphalerite.
Some luminous paints contain zinc sulfide to make them glow in the dark.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sphalerite - the chief ore of zinc, a zinc sulfide mineral
