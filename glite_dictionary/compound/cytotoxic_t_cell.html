cytotoxic T cell

1. NOUN

/ ˌsaɪ.təˌtɑk.sɪk ˈti ˌsɛl / (AmE), / ˌsaɪ.təˌtɒk.sɪk ˈti ˌsɛl / (BrE)
A type of T cell with a CD8 receptor that detects and binds to infected cells, killing them to protect the body from infection.
T cell that kills infected cells
medical

Examples

The body relies on cytotoxic T cells to destroy virus-infected cells before the infection spreads.
Doctors measured the patient's level of cytotoxic T cells after the vaccination.
Researchers are studying how cytotoxic T cells can be used to target cancer cells.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: killer T cell - Alternate term for cytotoxic T cell
  • Antonym: helper T cell - A T cell that helps activate immune responses

Etymology

From 'cytotoxic' (cell-killing) and 'T cell', so named for developing in the thymus gland.
