trench mouth

1. NOUN

/ trɛntʃ maʊθ / (AmE), / trɛntʃ maʊθ / (BrE)
a severe infection of the gums and mouth, causing painful ulcers and bad breath, often seen in poor oral hygiene or in stressful, unsanitary conditions
severe ulcerative gum infection
medical

Examples

The dentist diagnosed the patient with trench mouth due to extensive gum ulceration and bleeding.
Unsanitary living conditions during the war led to numerous cases of trench mouth among soldiers.
Proper oral hygiene can help prevent trench mouth and other serious gum diseases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acute necrotizing ulcerative gingivitis - medical term for the same gum infection as trench mouth

Etymology

Named from its prevalence among soldiers in World War I trenches, due to poor oral hygiene and stress.

2. NOUN

/ trɛntʃ maʊθ / (AmE), / trɛntʃ maʊθ / (BrE)
a painful and severe infection of the gums, causing ulceration, bleeding, and bad breath; also called acute necrotizing ulcerative gingivitis
severe gum infection
medical

Examples

The dentist diagnosed him with trench mouth after examining his swollen gums.
Poor oral hygiene and stress can increase your risk of developing trench mouth.
During the war, unsanitary conditions led to frequent cases of trench mouth among soldiers.

Synset Examples

After the camping trip, I noticed symptoms of trench mouth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gingivitis - inflammation of the gums

Etymology

Named from its prevalence among soldiers in trenches during World War I.
