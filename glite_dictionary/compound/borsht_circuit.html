borsht circuit

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɔrʃt ˌsɜːrkɪt / (AmE), / ˈbɔːʃt ˌsɜːkɪt / (BrE)
(informal) A group of resort hotels in the Catskill Mountains of New York, historically frequented by Jewish guests and known for hosting entertainers, especially comedians.
Catskills resorts frequented by Jewish guests
informal

Examples

Many famous comedians got their start performing on the borsht circuit.
She spent her summers working as a singer at resorts along the borsht circuit.
The borsht circuit played a significant role in American entertainment history.

Synset Examples

many comedians learned their trade playing the borscht circuit

Etymology

From Yiddish 'borsht' (a beet soup) referencing the Jewish clientele, combined with 'circuit' for a regular route of venues.
