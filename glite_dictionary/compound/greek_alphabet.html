Greek alphabet

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡrik ˈæl.fəˌbɛt / (AmE), / ˈɡriːk ˈæl.fə.bɛt / (BrE)
the set of letters used for writing the Greek language, originally developed by ancient Greeks and still in use today in modified form
alphabet of the Greek language

Examples

The Greek alphabet was the first to introduce separate letters for vowel sounds.
Many scientific symbols are based on letters from the Greek alphabet.
Students learned to recite the Greek alphabet in their ancient history class.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alphabet - a set of letters or symbols in a fixed order used for writing a language

Etymology

Greek 'alphabetos', from Alpha and Beta, the names of the first two Greek letters.
