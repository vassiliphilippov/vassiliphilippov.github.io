double stitch

1. VERB

/ ˈdʌbəl stɪtʃ / (AmE), / ˈdʌbəl stɪtʃ / (BrE)
To sew with two rows of stitches, typically for extra strength or decoration.
sew with two rows of stitches

Examples

For added durability, she always double stitches the seams on children's clothing.
He double stitched the repaired area to ensure it wouldn't come apart.
When making quilts, many crafters prefer to double stitch along the edges.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: stitch - to sew or fasten with stitches

Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʌbəl stɪtʃ / (AmE), / ˈdʌb(ə)l stɪtʃ / (BrE)
a crochet stitch in which the yarn is drawn through the work twice, used to create a taller, looser stitch than a single stitch
a kind of crochet stitch

Examples

She used the double stitch to add extra height to her crochet blanket.
A double stitch is ideal for creating a softer, more open fabric.
The pattern begins with three rows of double stitch before switching to single stitches.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: single stitch - a basic crochet stitch where the yarn is drawn through once
