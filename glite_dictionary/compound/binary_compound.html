binary compound

1. NOUN

/ ˈbaɪnɛri ˈkɑmˌpaʊnd / (AmE), / ˈbaɪnəri ˈkɒmpaʊnd / (BrE)
A chemical compound consisting of only two different elements.
compound of two elements
medical

Examples

Sodium chloride is a common binary compound found in every kitchen.
Water, although simple, is not a binary compound because it is made from hydrogen and oxygen, two elements.
Scientists synthesized a new binary compound using only lithium and fluorine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compound - A substance formed from two or more elements chemically united in fixed proportions.

Etymology

From Latin 'binarius' (consisting of two) and 'compound', from Latin 'componere' (to put together).
