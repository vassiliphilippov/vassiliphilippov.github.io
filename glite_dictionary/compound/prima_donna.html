prima donna

1. NOUN

/ ˌpriːmə ˈdɑnə / (AmE), / ˌpriːmə ˈdɒnə / (BrE)
a person, especially in a group or organization, who behaves in a very self-important, temperamental, or demanding way
vain, temperamental person

Examples

Working with such a prima donna can make team projects extremely challenging.
She acted like a prima donna whenever things didn't go her way.
Despite his talent, his prima donna attitude caused friction with his colleagues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diva - a celebrated female singer or a woman who behaves like a prima donna

Etymology

Italian, literally ‘first lady’, originally used for the leading female singer in an opera company.

2. NOUN

/ ˌpriːmə ˈdɑːnə / (AmE), / ˌpriːmə ˈdɒnə / (BrE)
A leading female singer in an opera company, especially one who is distinguished or celebrated for her talent.
lead female opera singer

Examples

The renowned prima donna received a standing ovation after her powerful performance.
Many young singers aspire to become the next prima donna at the Metropolitan Opera.
She has the voice of a true prima donna, commanding attention with every note.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diva - a famous female opera singer; a distinguished female singer

Etymology

From Italian, meaning 'first lady', referring to the leading female singer in an opera.

3. NOUN

/ ˌpriːmə ˈdɑnə / (AmE), / ˌpriːmə ˈdɒnə / (BrE)
a person, especially someone in the arts, who behaves in a temperamental or demanding way and expects special treatment
temperamental, demanding person

Examples

The team's star player became a real prima donna after their big win, constantly demanding special treatment.
No one wanted to work with the prima donna director because of his unpredictable moods.
She was talented, but her prima donna attitude made her difficult to collaborate with.

Synset Examples

Every project had a prima donna who needed special treatment.
The actor's prima donnaish ways were well-known in Hollywood.
She acts like a prima donna whenever she doesn't get her way.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diva - a woman regarded as temperamental or haughty, especially in entertainment
  • Antonym: team player - someone who works cooperatively with others, opposite of prima donna

Etymology

From Italian, literally 'first lady', originally used for the lead female singer in an opera.
