spring onion

1. NOUN

/ sprɪŋ ˈʌnjən / (AmE), / sprɪŋ ˈʌnjən / (BrE)
A young onion with a small, undeveloped bulb and long green stalks, typically eaten raw in salads or used in cooking.
young onion with small bulb and green stalks

Examples

I like to chop spring onion and add it to my salads for extra flavor.
The recipe calls for two bunches of spring onion and fresh coriander.
She garnished the soup with finely sliced spring onion before serving.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scallion - a variety of young onion with a small bulb, also used in salads
