Uncle Tom

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌŋkəl tɑm / (AmE), / ˈʌŋkl tɒm / (BrE)
An offensive term for a Black man regarded as excessively subservient or deferential to White people.
offensive term for a subservient Black man
offensive

Examples

He was called an Uncle Tom by critics who felt he didn't stand up for his community.
The movie was criticized for portraying its main Black character as an Uncle Tom.
Using the term Uncle Tom is deeply offensive and loaded with historical pain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sellout - person who betrays their identity for personal gain

Etymology

From the character Uncle Tom in Harriet Beecher Stowe's 1852 novel 'Uncle Tom's Cabin', whose name became associated with subservience.

3. NOUN

/ ˈʌŋkəl tɑm / (AmE), / ˈʌŋkl tɒm / (BrE)

black person seen as overly subservient or deferential
offensive

Examples

Some critics accused him of being an Uncle Tom because he refused to challenge the company's discriminatory policies.
The label Uncle Tom is often used as an insult within the African American community.
In her essay, she rejected the idea that working with the establishment made her an Uncle Tom.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sellout - a person who betrays their own group for personal gain
  • Synonym: house negro - a pejorative term for a black person seen as servile to white people

From the character Uncle Tom in Harriet Beecher Stowe's 1852 novel 'Uncle Tom's Cabin'.
