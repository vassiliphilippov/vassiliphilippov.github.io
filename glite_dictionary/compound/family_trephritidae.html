family Trephritidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli trəˌfrɪtɪˌdi / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli trɛˌfrɪtɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of small flies known as fruit flies, some of which are leaf miners, significant in agriculture for their impact on fruits and crops.
family of fruit flies (insects)

Examples

The family Trephritidae includes many species that are pests of fruit crops worldwide.
Research on the family Trephritidae helps us understand the spread of invasive fruit flies.
Some members of the family Trephritidae behave as leaf miners during their larval stages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fruit fly - a small fly belonging to the family Tephritidae or Drosophilidae

Etymology

From New Latin 'Trephritidae', based on the type genus Tephritis.
