family Pediculidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæmɪli pəˌdɪkjəˈlaɪdi / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli pɛˌdɪkjʊˈlɪːdiː / (BrE)
A family of true lice that includes human lice and closely related species.
family of true lice, including human lice
medical

Examples

Infestations caused by members of the family Pediculidae may require specialized treatment.
Scientists studied the evolutionary relationships within the family Pediculidae to better understand human lice.
The family Pediculidae contains species that are obligate parasites of mammals, including humans.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'Pediculus' (genus of lice) with the taxonomic suffix '-idae', denoting a family.
