gun carriage

1. NOUN

/ ɡʌn ˈkærɪdʒ / (AmE), / ɡʌn ˈkarɪdʒ / (BrE)
a strong frame or wheeled support on which a gun, especially a cannon or artillery piece, is mounted and from which it is fired
support structure for a mounted gun

Examples

The old battlefield still had the remains of a gun carriage half-buried in mud.
Engineers repaired the gun carriage before the cannon could be moved into position.
The artillery museum displays an ornate gun carriage from the Napoleonic era.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: limber - a two-wheeled cart used to support the trail of an artillery piece
