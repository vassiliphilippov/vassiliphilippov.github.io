Great Mother

1. PROPN

/ ˈɡreɪt ˈmʌðɚ / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪt ˈmʌðə / (BrE)
an ancient nature goddess worshipped in Phrygia, Asia Minor, regarded as the counterpart of the Greek Rhea and Roman Ops.
ancient nature goddess in Phrygian mythology

Examples

The ancient Phrygians revered the Great Mother as a divine source of fertility and life.
In classical art, the Great Mother is often depicted flanked by lions or other wild animals.
Scholars draw parallels between the Great Mother and other mother goddesses of the Mediterranean world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Rhea - Greek goddess, mother of the Olympian gods, counterpart to Great Mother
  • Synonym: Ops - Roman goddess of fertility and abundance, equivalent to Great Mother
