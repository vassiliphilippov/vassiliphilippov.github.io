genus Xanthium

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˈzænθiəm / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈzanθɪəm / (BrE)
A genus of coarse herbaceous plants with small greenish flower heads followed by burrs covered with hooked bristles.
a genus of coarse herbaceous plants

Examples

The genus Xanthium includes several species commonly known as cockleburs.
Researchers studied the adaptation mechanisms of plants in the genus Xanthium.
Seeds from the genus Xanthium can attach to animal fur and be widely dispersed.

Etymology

From Latin 'xanthium', from Greek 'xanthos' meaning 'yellow'.
