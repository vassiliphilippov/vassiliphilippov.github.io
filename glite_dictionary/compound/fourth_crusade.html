Fourth Crusade

1. PROPN

/ ˈfɔrθ kruˈseɪd / (AmE), / ˈfɔːθ kruːˈseɪd / (BrE)
the military expedition from 1202 to 1204, originally intended to recapture Jerusalem, but which resulted in the sack of Constantinople instead.
1202–04 Crusade diverted to Constantinople

Examples

The Fourth Crusade was marked by the shocking sack of Constantinople in 1204.
Historians often debate the motivations behind the diversion of the Fourth Crusade.
Many believe that the disastrous outcome of the Fourth Crusade shaped the fate of the Byzantine Empire.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Third Crusade - the preceding major crusade (1189–1192) aimed at recapturing Jerusalem; another major Crusade
  • Degree Higher: Fifth Crusade - the subsequent crusade (1217–1221) following the Fourth Crusade
