bromic acid

1. NOUN

/ ˈbroʊ.mɪk ˈæs.ɪd / (AmE), / ˈbrəʊ.mɪk ˈas.ɪd / (BrE)
an unstable acid (HBrO₃) used as an oxidizing agent in chemical reactions
unstable oxidizing acid (HBrO₃)
medical

Examples

Bromic acid is typically prepared by adding sulfuric acid to a solution of bromate.
Due to its instability, bromic acid is usually handled only in dilute solutions.
The strong oxidizing properties of bromic acid make it useful in analytical chemistry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bromine - a chemical element with symbol Br

Etymology

From 'bromine', the element, plus '-ic', denoting higher oxidation; 'acid' from Latin 'acidus' (sour).
