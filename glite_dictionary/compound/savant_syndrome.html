savant syndrome

1. NOUN

/ səˈvɑnt ˌsɪnˌdroʊm / (AmE), / səˈvɑ̃ ˌsɪn.drəʊm / (BrE)
A rare condition in which a person with a mental disability, such as autism, exhibits exceptional skill or talent in a specific area, such as mathematics, music, or art.
condition where mental disability coincides with exceptional abilities
medical

Examples

The documentary featured a young pianist with savant syndrome who could play any song after hearing it only once.
Doctors were amazed by the extraordinary memory skills shown by the child diagnosed with savant syndrome.
Researchers are studying savant syndrome to better understand how brain function leads to remarkable talents.

Synset Examples

Individuals diagnosed with savant syndrome boast unparalleled ability in certain skills and subject areas.

Etymology

From French 'savant' (learned person) and 'syndrome' (set of symptoms).
