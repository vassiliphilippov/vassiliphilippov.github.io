internal maxillary artery

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtɝːnəl ˌmæksɪˈlɛri ˈɑːrtəri / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɜːnəl ˌmæksɪˈlɛri ˈɑːtəri / (BrE)
an artery that supplies blood to deep structures of the face and parts of the meninges
artery supplying deep facial structures
medical

Examples

The surgeon identified the internal maxillary artery before proceeding with the operation.
Damage to the internal maxillary artery can cause severe bleeding in facial trauma cases.
The internal maxillary artery branches into several vessels that supply the nasal cavity and palate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maxillary artery - the main artery of the maxilla

Etymology

From Latin 'maxilla' (jawbone) and 'arteria' (artery).
