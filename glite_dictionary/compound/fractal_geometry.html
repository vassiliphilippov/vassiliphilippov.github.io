fractal geometry

1. NOUN

/ ˈfræk.təl dʒiˈɑː.mə.tri / (AmE), / ˈfræk.təl dʒɪˈɒm.ɪ.tri / (BrE)
the branch of mathematics that studies fractals and their properties, focusing on structures that display self-similarity at various scales
branch of mathematics studying fractals

Examples

Benoit Mandelbrot's work on fractal geometry revolutionized the way we understand complex natural patterns.
In fractal geometry, simple mathematical equations can generate images that resemble mountains, coastlines, and clouds.
Many artists have drawn inspiration from fractal geometry to create visually striking, infinitely complex designs.

Synset Examples

Benoit Mandelbrot pioneered fractal geometry

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fractal - a complex geometric figure made up of patterns that repeat at every scale
  • Antonym: Euclidean geometry - the study of plane and solid figures based on axioms and theorems by Euclid

Etymology

From 'fractal', coined by Benoit Mandelbrot from Latin 'fractus' (broken), and 'geometry', from Greek 'geōmetria' (earth measurement).
