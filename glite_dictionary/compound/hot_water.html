hot water

1. NOUN

/ hɑt ˈwɔtər / (AmE), / hɒt ˈwɔːtə / (BrE)
water that has been heated to a high temperature, especially for bathing, cleaning, or cooking
water heated to a high temperature

Examples

Be careful not to burn yourself with the hot water from the tap.
She always makes her tea with freshly boiled hot water.
The plumber fixed the heater, so we finally have hot water again.

Synset Examples

I filled the tub with hot water for a relaxing soak.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cold water - water that is not heated or is at a low temperature
