Hebrew alphabet

1. NOUN

/ ˈhiːbruː ˈæl.fə.bet / (AmE), / ˈhiːbruː ˈæl.fə.bɛt / (BrE)
the Semitic alphabet used since the 5th century BC for writing Hebrew and, later, Yiddish and Ladino
the alphabet used for Hebrew and related languages

Examples

She learned to write her name in the Hebrew alphabet while attending Sunday school.
Ancient texts are often studied in their original Hebrew alphabet for greater accuracy.
The Hebrew alphabet consists of 22 characters, all of which are consonants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alphabet - a set of letters or symbols in a fixed order used for writing a language
