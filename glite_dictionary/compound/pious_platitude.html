pious platitude

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪəs ˈplætɪˌtud / (AmE), / ˈpaɪəs ˈplætɪˌtjuːd / (BrE)
a statement about religion or morality that is insincerely or superficially expressed, especially one that is overused or lacks real meaning
insincerely expressed moral or religious statement

Examples

The politician's speech was filled with pious platitudes that failed to address real issues.
We are tired of hearing the same pious platitude every Sunday, with no action to follow.
Instead of offering help, he responded with a pious platitude about patience and faith.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: platitude - an overused, unoriginal remark, especially one with moral content
