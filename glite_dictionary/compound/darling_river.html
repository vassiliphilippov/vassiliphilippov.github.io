Darling River

1. PROPN

/ ˈdɑrlɪŋ ˈrɪvɚ / (AmE), / ˈdɑːlɪŋ ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
A major river in southeastern Australia, which is a principal tributary of the Murray River.
major river in southeastern Australia

Examples

The Darling River is known for its importance to Australian agriculture.
Many towns along the Darling River face water shortages during droughts.
Tourists enjoyed a boat trip down the Darling River last summer.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Murray River - a principal river in southeastern Australia

Etymology

Named after Sir Ralph Darling, a British colonial governor of New South Wales.
