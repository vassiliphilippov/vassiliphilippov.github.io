wrestling hold

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛslɪŋ hoʊld / (AmE), / ˈrɛslɪŋ həʊld / (BrE)
a grip or maneuver used by a wrestler to control or restrain an opponent during a contest
grip or maneuver in wrestling

Examples

He quickly escaped from his opponent's wrestling hold and countered with a move of his own.
The coach demonstrated a new wrestling hold during practice to improve our defense.
Applying the right wrestling hold at the right time can turn the match in your favor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hold - a way of grasping or controlling someone, especially in wrestling
