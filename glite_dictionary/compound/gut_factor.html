gut factor

1. NOUN

/ ɡʌt ˈfæktər / (AmE), / ɡʌt ˈfaktə / (BrE)
an intuitive feeling or instinct that influences decision-making, especially when logical analysis is inconclusive
instinctive influence in decisions

Examples

The CEO relied on her gut factor when making the final decision.
When data was unclear, the team turned to their gut factor to guide strategy.
In hiring, the gut factor can sometimes make all the difference.

Synset Examples

Her success was due to the gut factor she embodied.
Sometimes the gut factor outweighs the numbers.
The gut factor plays a big role in startups.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intuition - the ability to understand something without conscious reasoning
