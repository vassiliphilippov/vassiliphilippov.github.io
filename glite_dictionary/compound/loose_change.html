loose change

1. NOUN

/ lus tʃeɪndʒ / (AmE), / luːs tʃeɪndʒ / (BrE)
coins of small value that are not arranged or stored neatly, often kept in a pocket or scattered in various places.
small coins, typically of low value

Examples

I always keep some loose change in my car for parking meters.
After cleaning her bag, Maria found a handful of loose change.
He emptied his pockets and put all his loose change into a jar.

Synset Examples

I found some loose change under the couch cushions.
She collected loose change to donate to charity.
He paid for the snack with loose change from his pocket.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coins - pieces of metal used as money
