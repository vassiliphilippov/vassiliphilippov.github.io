midterm examination

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪd.tɜrm ˌɛɡ.zəˈmeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˈmɪd.tɜːm ˌɪɡ.zəˈmeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
an examination given in the middle of an academic term, typically to assess students' progress
an exam during the middle of a term

Examples

Many students found the midterm examination particularly challenging this semester.
The professor scheduled the midterm examination for the eighth week of class.
She studied for weeks in preparation for her psychology midterm examination.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: final examination - an examination at the end of an academic term

Etymology

From 'mid-' (middle) + 'term' (period of study) + 'examination'.
