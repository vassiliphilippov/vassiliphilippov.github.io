constructive breach

1. NOUN

/ kənˈstrʌktɪv briːtʃ / (AmE), / kənˈstrʌktɪv briːtʃ / (BrE)
A breach of contract that occurs before the time when performance is required, typically inferred from actions making performance impossible or unlikely.
early breach of contract (legal)
legal

Examples

The company was accused of a constructive breach for making the contract impossible to perform.
By selling the property early, the landlord committed a constructive breach of the lease agreement.
Courts may find a constructive breach when a party indicates it will not fulfill its contractual obligations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anticipatory breach - a breach of contract before performance is due, signaling intent not to perform
  • Antonym: actual breach - a breach of contract at or after the time performance is due

Etymology

From legal usage: 'constructive' meaning inferred by law, and 'breach' from Old English *bryce* (breaking).
