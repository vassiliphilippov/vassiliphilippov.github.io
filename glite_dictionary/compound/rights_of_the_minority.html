rights of the minority

1. NOUN

/ raɪts əv ðə məˈnɔrəti / (AmE), / raɪts əv ðə mɪˈnɒrəti / (BrE)
the legal or moral entitlements possessed by a smaller or less powerful group within a larger population, especially regarding protection and participation
entitlements of a smaller group

Examples

The activists demanded the rights of the minority be respected in all government decisions.
Constitutional law often addresses the rights of the minority to prevent discrimination.
Debates about voting systems frequently focus on how well they protect the rights of the minority.

Synset Examples

They fought for the rights of the minority in their community.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: minority rights - legal rights granted to or claimed by a minority group
  • Antonym: majority rule - the principle that the opinions of the majority should prevail
