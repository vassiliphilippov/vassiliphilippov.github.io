genus Haemodorum

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs hiːˌmɒdɔːˈrʊm / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs hiːˌməʊdəˈrʊəm / (BrE)
the type genus of the family Haemodoraceae, comprising several species of perennial herbs native to Australia and nearby regions
a plant genus in the family Haemodoraceae

Examples

The genus Haemodorum includes several species known for their red roots.
Botanists studied the distribution of genus Haemodorum in Australian wetlands.
Some members of the genus Haemodorum are used in traditional Aboriginal medicine.

Etymology

From Latin 'Haemodorum', from Greek 'haima' (blood) and 'dōron' (gift), referring to the reddish coloration in some species.
