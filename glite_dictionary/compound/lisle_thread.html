lisle thread

1. NOUN

/ laɪl θrɛd / (AmE), / laɪl θrɛd / (BrE)
a strong, tightly twisted cotton thread, usually made from long-staple cotton and used especially for hosiery and fine knitwear
strong, fine cotton thread for knitwear

Examples

She prefers socks made with lisle thread because they are softer and more durable.
The delicate gloves were finely knit from white lisle thread.
Lisle thread is often chosen for high-quality undergarments due to its smoothness.

Etymology

From the name of the city Lille (formerly Lisle), in northern France, once noted for textile manufacture.
