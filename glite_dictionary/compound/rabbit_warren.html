rabbit warren

1. NOUN

/ ˈræbɪt ˌwɔrən / (AmE), / ˈræbɪt ˌwɒrən / (BrE)
a network of interconnected underground tunnels, typically used by wild rabbits as their living quarters
network of rabbit tunnels

Examples

The field was riddled with rabbit warrens, making it dangerous to walk on.
Foxes sometimes invade a rabbit warren in search of prey.
The rabbit warren extended for several meters beneath the hillside.

2. NOUN

/ ˈræbɪt ˌwɔɹən / (AmE), / ˈræbɪt ˌwɒrən / (BrE)
An overcrowded and often confusing or maze-like residential area.
overcrowded, maze-like residential area

Examples

The city's old quarter had become a rabbit warren of tightly packed homes and narrow alleyways.
Many migrants found themselves living in a rabbit warren where privacy was impossible to find.
The volunteers struggled to navigate through the rabbit warren to reach those in need.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slum - a squalid and overcrowded urban street or district

Etymology

Originally referred to a system of interconnected burrows where rabbits live; later used metaphorically for crowded human dwellings.
