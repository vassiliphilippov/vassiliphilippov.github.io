genus Malaxis

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs məˈlæksɪs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs məˈlaksɪs / (BrE)
A large genus of mainly terrestrial orchids, typically bearing one or a few pleated leaves and slender spikes of tiny, often green flowers; found worldwide.
a genus of terrestrial orchids

Examples

The genus Malaxis includes several species of orchids commonly known as adder's-mouth orchids.
Botanists are studying the distribution of the genus Malaxis in temperate forests.
Many members of the genus Malaxis are recognized for their slender flower spikes and small blossoms.

Etymology

From New Latin *Malaxis*, based on Greek *malaxis* (softening), referring to the soft texture of the leaves.
