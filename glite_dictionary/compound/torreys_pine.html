Torrey's pine

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɔːriz paɪn / (AmE), / ˈtɒriz paɪn / (BrE)
a medium-sized pine tree (Pinus torreyana) native to southwestern California, known for its long, cylindrical cones and unique five-needle clusters
rare pine tree of California

Examples

The rare Torrey's pine grows only in a few coastal areas of California.
Botanists studied the needles and cones of the Torrey's pine for signs of adaptation to dry climates.
A single Torrey's pine can live for more than a hundred years in its native habitat.

Etymology

Named after the American botanist John Torrey (1796–1873).
