zirconium dioxide

1. NOUN

/ zɚˈkoʊniəm daɪˈɑksaɪd / (AmE), / zəˈkəʊnɪəm daɪˈɒksaɪd / (BrE)
a white crystalline oxide (ZrO2) used in making refractories, insulation, abrasives, enamels, and glazes
white crystalline oxide used in industry

Examples

Zirconium dioxide is used in dental ceramics for its strength and durability.
The high melting point of zirconium dioxide makes it valuable for furnace linings.
Scientists are developing new fuel cells using zirconium dioxide as a solid electrolyte.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zirconia - an alternative name for zirconium dioxide

Etymology

Zirconium from zircon + dioxide (prefix di- ‘two’ + oxide).
