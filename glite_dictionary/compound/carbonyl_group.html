carbonyl group

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑrbəˌnɪl ɡruːp / (AmE), / ˈkɑːbənɪl ɡruːp / (BrE)
a divalent chemical group (CO) consisting of a carbon atom double-bonded to an oxygen atom, occurring in many organic compounds such as aldehydes and ketones.
chemical group: carbon double-bonded to oxygen
medical

Examples

A carbonyl group is present in both aldehydes and ketones.
The reaction is catalyzed by the presence of a carbonyl group in the molecule.
Chemists often identify compounds by detecting their carbonyl group.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carbonyl - the functional group CO in chemistry

Etymology

From 'carbon' and 'yl', a suffix in chemistry, plus 'group'.
