stage direction

1. NOUN

/ steɪdʒ daɪˈrɛkʃən / (AmE), / steɪdʒ dɪˈrɛkʃən / (BrE)
An instruction in the script of a play, usually indicating movement, position, or tone of an actor, or the sound, lighting, or scenery.
instruction in a play script

Examples

The playwright included a stage direction to have the lights dim at the end of the scene.
She ignored the stage direction and walked to the opposite side of the stage.
Every stage direction in Shakespeare's plays is carefully considered by directors.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: stage - a raised floor or platform for performances
  • Degree Lower: direction - instructions or guidance about how to proceed
