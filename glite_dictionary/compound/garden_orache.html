garden orache

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɑrdən ˈɔːrətʃ / (AmE), / ˈɡɑːdən ˈɒrətʃ / (BrE)
An Asiatic annual plant (Atriplex hortensis) resembling spinach, often cultivated as a potherb and naturalized in Europe and North America.
an edible leafy plant, also called mountain spinach

Examples

She planted garden orache beside her spinach in early spring.
The leaves of garden orache can be cooked or eaten raw in salads.
In some regions, garden orache is valued for its tolerance to heat in summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spinach - an edible leafy green vegetable
  • Degree Lower: red orache - a related species with red leaves

Etymology

From French 'arroche', from Latin 'aurīcula', meaning 'little ear', referring to the leaf shape.
