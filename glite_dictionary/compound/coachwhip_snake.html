coachwhip snake

1. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊtʃˌwɪp sneɪk / (AmE), / ˈkəʊtʃˌwɪp sneɪk / (BrE)
A slender, fast-moving whipsnake native to the southern United States and Mexico, with a tail resembling a braided whip.
slender whipsnake of southern US and Mexico

Examples

The coachwhip snake can move quickly across sandy desert terrain to escape predators.
Biologists observed a large coachwhip snake basking on a sunlit rock.
Because its tail looks like a braided whip, the coachwhip snake can be easily recognized in the wild.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whipsnake - any of several slender, fast-moving snakes

Etymology

From 'coachwhip', describing the tail's resemblance to a braided whip, and 'snake'.
