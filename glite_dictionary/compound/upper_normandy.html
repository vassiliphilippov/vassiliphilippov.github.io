Upper Normandy

1. PROPN

/ ˈʌpər nɔrˈmændi / (AmE), / ˈʌpə nɔːˈmændi / (BrE)
a historical region of northern France, formerly an administrative division, comprising the northeastern part of Normandy
historical region in northern France

Examples

Historians often focus on the role of Upper Normandy during World War II.
Many culinary traditions in Upper Normandy reflect both French and Norman influences.
The Seine River passes through several cities in Upper Normandy before reaching the sea.

2. PROPN

/ ˌʌpɚ nɔːrˈmændi / (AmE), / ˌʌpə nɔːˈmændi / (BrE)
a former administrative region in northern France, constituting the northern part of the historic province of Normandy
former region in northern France

Examples

Many tourists visit Upper Normandy each year to see its historic towns and coastline.
The lush countryside of Upper Normandy is famous for its apple orchards and dairy farms.
Before 2016, Upper Normandy was an official administrative region of France.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Normandy - a region in northern France, of which Upper Normandy is a part
  • Degree Lower: Lower Normandy - the other main division of the historic region of Normandy, in western France
