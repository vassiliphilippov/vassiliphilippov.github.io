epitrochoidal engine

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛpɪtroʊˈkɔɪdəl ˈɛn.dʒən / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪtrəˈkɔɪdəl ˈɛn.dʒɪn / (BrE)
a type of rotary internal-combustion engine that operates without reciprocating parts, using an epitrochoidal motion for its rotor
rotary internal-combustion engine design

Examples

The Mazda RX-7 famously used an epitrochoidal engine to achieve its unique power output.
Engineers were fascinated by the efficiency of the epitrochoidal engine compared to conventional piston engines.
An epitrochoidal engine eliminates the need for many moving parts found in traditional designs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rotary engine - internal-combustion engine using rotating parts instead of pistons

Etymology

From 'epitrochoid,' a geometric curve traced by a point on a circle rolling around another circle, describing the rotor's motion; 'engine' from Latin 'ingenium.'
