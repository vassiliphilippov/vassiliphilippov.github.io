Cassia marilandica

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæsiə ˌmɛrɪˈlændɪkə / (AmE), / ˌkæsiə ˌmærɪˈlændɪkə / (BrE)
A North American perennial herb whose leaves are used medicinally; sometimes classified under the genus Cassia.
North American medicinal perennial herb
medical

Examples

The medicinal properties of Cassia marilandica have been recognized by herbalists for centuries.
Botanists often encounter Cassia marilandica growing wild along riverbanks in the eastern United States.
Many traditional remedies use dried leaves from Cassia marilandica to treat mild digestive issues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cassia - A genus of flowering plants in the legume family

Etymology

Cassia from Latin 'cassia', a plant genus; marilandica denotes origin from Maryland, USA.
