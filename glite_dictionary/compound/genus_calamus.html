genus Calamus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs kəˈleɪməs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs kəˈleɪməs / (BrE)
A genus of fish in the family Sparidae, commonly known as porgies, found primarily in the Western Atlantic Ocean.
a genus of porgies (fish)

Examples

Several species in the genus Calamus are popular targets for recreational fishermen.
The genus Calamus includes porgies often found along the coast of Florida and the Caribbean.
Researchers published a new study about the evolutionary relationships within the genus Calamus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: porgy - a common name for fish in the Sparidae family

Etymology

From Latin 'calamus' (reed), used as a genus name in taxonomy.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs kəˈleɪməs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs kəˈleɪməs / (BrE)
A genus of distinctive, often spiny-stemmed climbing palms found in tropical and subtropical forests, commonly known as rattans.
a genus of spiny climbing palms (rattans)

Examples

The genus Calamus includes many species of climbing palms known as rattans.
Researchers studied several members of the genus Calamus in the Southeast Asian rainforest.
Traditional crafts often use the stems of the genus Calamus for weaving and furniture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rattan - climbing palms with slender, tough stems often used for making furniture

Etymology

From Latin 'calamus' meaning 'reed', used in botanical Latin.
