towing line

1. NOUN

/ ˈtoʊɪŋ laɪn / (AmE), / ˈtəʊɪŋ laɪn / (BrE)
A rope or cable used to tow a vessel, vehicle, or object, especially at sea.
rope or cable for towing

Examples

The crew attached a towing line to the disabled yacht before pulling it back to the harbor.
Our boat drifted dangerously until a passing ship offered a towing line.
Make sure the towing line is strong enough for heavy loads before starting the operation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: towrope - rope or cable used for towing
  • Synonym: towline - line or rope used in towing
