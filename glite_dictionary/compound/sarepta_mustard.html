Sarepta mustard

1. NOUN

/ səˈrɛptə ˈmʌstərd / (AmE), / səˈrɛptə ˈmʌstəd / (BrE)
a variety of mustard with a slightly spicy flavor, originally from Russia and named after the town of Sarepta
spicy mustard variety from Russia

Examples

The chef used Sarepta mustard to add a distinct flavor to the salad dressing.
Farmers began cultivating Sarepta mustard seeds in new regions outside of Russia.
You can identify Sarepta mustard by its reddish-brown seeds and spicy aroma.

Synset Examples

The sarepta mustard derives its name from the town of Sarepta.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mustard - a yellowish-brown condiment made from the seeds of a plant

Etymology

Named after Sarepta, a town in Russia, where this variety of mustard originated.
