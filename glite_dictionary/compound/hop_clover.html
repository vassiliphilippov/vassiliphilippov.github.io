hop clover

1. NOUN

/ hɑp ˈkloʊvər / (AmE), / hɒp ˈkləʊvə / (BrE)
A prostrate European herb with small yellow flowers and curved black pods, naturalized in North America.
a low-growing herb with yellow flowers

Examples

The field was dotted with hop clover and wildflowers.
Gardeners often plant hop clover as a ground cover to enrich the soil.
He knelt down to examine the tiny yellow flowers of the hop clover.

2. NOUN

/ hɑp ˈkloʊvər / (AmE), / hɒp ˈkləʊvə / (BrE)
a species of clover native to Europe and western Asia, with small yellowish flowers, often regarded as the true or original shamrock
a clover plant with yellow flowers, original shamrock

Examples

The fields were dotted with hop clover, their yellow blossoms shining in the sun.
Many believe that hop clover is the original shamrock of Irish tradition.
She pressed a hop clover flower into her botanical scrapbook.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shamrock - a clover-like plant, symbol of Ireland
