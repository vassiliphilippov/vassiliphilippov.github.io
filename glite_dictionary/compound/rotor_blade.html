rotor blade

1. NOUN

/ ˈroʊtər bleɪd / (AmE), / ˈrəʊtə bleɪd / (BrE)
A long, shaped blade that rotates to provide lift for a helicopter or similar aircraft.
rotating blade of a helicopter

Examples

The engineer inspected each rotor blade before the helicopter was cleared for takeoff.
A damaged rotor blade can cause serious flight instability.
Technicians replaced the worn rotor blade with a new, more efficient model.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: propeller blade - a blade that forms part of a rotating propeller
