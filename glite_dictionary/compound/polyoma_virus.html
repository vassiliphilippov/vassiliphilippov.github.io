polyoma virus

1. NOUN

/ ˌpoʊliˈoʊmə ˈvaɪrəs / (AmE), / ˌpɒliˈəʊmə ˈvaɪrəs / (BrE)
a type of virus that can cause various kinds of tumors in mice and other animals; important in medical and biological research
tumor-causing virus, esp. in mice
medical

Examples

The polyoma virus has been used extensively in cancer research due to its tumor-inducing properties.
Scientists discovered that the polyoma virus could initiate a variety of tumors in laboratory mice.
Understanding how the polyoma virus interacts with host cells has contributed to advances in virology.

Etymology

From Greek 'poly-' (many) + 'oma' (tumor), referring to its ability to produce multiple tumors.
