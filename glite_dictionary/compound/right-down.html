right-down

1. ADV

/ ˈraɪt ˌdaʊn / (AmE), / ˈraɪt ˌdaʊn / (BrE)
Used to emphasize the complete or absolute nature of something, especially something negative; downright.
completely; absolutely; downright
archaic

Examples

He’s a right-down scoundrel, through and through.
The accusation was a right-down lie, with no truth to it at all.
Her behavior was described as right-down disgraceful by the committee.

Synset Examples

a regular right-down bad 'un

Related Terms

  • Synonym: downright - completely; absolutely, used for emphasis
