soybean oil

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɔɪˌbin ɔɪl / (AmE), / ˈsɔɪˌbiːn ɔɪl / (BrE)
A pale yellow oil extracted from soybeans, commonly used in cooking and food products.
oil extracted from soybeans

Examples

Many processed foods contain soybean oil as one of their main ingredients.
She prefers to cook with soybean oil because of its mild flavor and high smoke point.
The nutrition label showed that the chips were fried in soybean oil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vegetable oil - any oil derived from plant sources
  • Degree Lower: olive oil - oil obtained from olives, common cooking oil
  • Synonym: canola oil - oil from rapeseed, used as a cooking oil

Etymology

From soybean + oil, referring to the oil pressed from soybean seeds.
