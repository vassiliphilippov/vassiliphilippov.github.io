Third World country

1. NOUN

/ θɝd wɝld ˈkʌn.tri / (AmE), / θɜːd wɜːld ˈkʌn.tri / (BrE)
a country with a relatively low level of economic development, often facing significant social and infrastructure challenges; originally used during the Cold War to describe nations not aligned with the Western or Eastern blocs, but now broadly referring to developing nations
developing nation, often economically disadvantaged

Examples

Many NGOs work to improve healthcare in Third World countries.
Some people avoid using the term Third World country because of its outdated associations.
Access to clean water remains a major challenge in many Third World countries.

Synset Examples

Many NGOs focus on education in Third World countries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: developing country - a nation with a lower living standard, underdeveloped industrial base, and low Human Development Index
  • Antonym: developed country - a country with a highly developed economy and advanced technological infrastructure

Etymology

From 'Third World,' coined in the mid-20th century to describe countries not aligned with NATO (First World) or the Communist Bloc (Second World).
