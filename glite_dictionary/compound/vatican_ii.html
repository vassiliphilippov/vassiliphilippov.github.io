Vatican II

1. PROPN

/ ˌvætɪkən ˈtu / (AmE), / ˌvætɪkən ˈtuː / (BrE)
the Second Vatican Council (1962–1965), an assembly of Roman Catholic bishops that introduced major reforms, including abandoning the universal Latin liturgy and promoting ecumenism
the Second Vatican Council of the Catholic Church (1962–1965)

Examples

The reforms enacted by Vatican II changed the language of Mass from Latin to vernacular.
Many Catholics still debate the impact of Vatican II on church traditions.
His theology was deeply influenced by the spirit of Vatican II.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Second Vatican Council - the formal name for Vatican II, the Roman Catholic Church council 1962–1965

Etymology

From Vatican (the Holy See) + Roman numeral II, denoting the second council held there.
