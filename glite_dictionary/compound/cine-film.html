cine-film

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪni fɪlm / (AmE), / ˈsɪni fɪlm / (BrE)
Photographic film several hundred feet long, wound on a spool, and used in a movie camera for making motion pictures, especially in the context of traditional film technology.
film used in a movie camera
british

Examples

He found a box of old cine-film reels in the attic and decided to have them digitized.
The museum's collection includes rare cine-film documenting early 20th-century life.
Before digital cameras, home movies were recorded on cine-film and shown with a projector.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: movie film - film used in making motion pictures

Etymology

From 'cine' (short for 'cinematograph') + 'film'; 'cine' is chiefly British.
