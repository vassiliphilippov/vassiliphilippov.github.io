African sandalwood

1. NOUN

/ ˌæfrɪkən ˈsændlˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˌæfrɪkən ˈsændlˌwʊd / (BrE)
a small shrubby African tree with hard, dense wood used as a source of red dye
tree yielding dyewood with red dye

Examples

The wood of the African sandalwood is prized for both carpentry and its deep red dye.
Local artisans use African sandalwood to create vibrant traditional textiles.
Botanists study the African sandalwood for its unique properties and ecological importance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sandalwood - fragrant wood from trees of the genus Santalum
