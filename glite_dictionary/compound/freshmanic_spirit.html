freshmanic spirit

1. NOUN

/ ˌfrɛʃˈmænɪk ˈspɪɹɪt / (AmE), / ˌfrɛʃˈmænɪk ˈspɪɹɪt / (BrE)
enthusiasm and eagerness characteristic of a new or inexperienced person, especially a freshman
enthusiasm typical of a freshman

Examples

She approached her new job with a freshmanic spirit, eager to learn everything she could.
The team's freshmanic spirit was evident in their excitement and willingness to try new strategies.
Bringing a freshmanic spirit to the group made their meetings more energetic and optimistic.

Synset Examples

She tackled the project with a freshmanic spirit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: freshman - a first-year student at a university or high school
