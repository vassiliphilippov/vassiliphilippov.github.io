wild orange

1. NOUN

/ waɪld ˈɔrɪndʒ / (AmE), / waɪld ˈɒrɪndʒ / (BrE)
a small evergreen tree native to the southern United States, known for its fragrant flowers and bitter fruit
small evergreen tree, southern US

Examples

The wild orange grows abundantly in the southernmost regions and is valued for its fragrant blossoms.
Farmers have tried cultivating wild orange trees, but the fruit remains too bitter for commercial sale.
While hiking, Sarah noticed a wild orange in bloom near the riverbank.

2. NOUN

/ waɪld ˈɔːrəndʒ / (AmE), / waɪld ˈɒrɪndʒ / (BrE)
a small, fast-growing spiny tree (Poncirus trifoliata), native to China, bearing sweetly scented flowers and decorative but inedible fruit; often used as rootstock for grafting and for hedges
Chinese spiny tree used for grafting

Examples

The gardener planted a wild orange hedge to protect the orchard from animals.
Although the fruit of the wild orange is inedible, it makes an attractive addition to gardens.
Wild orange trees are commonly used as rootstock when grafting sweet orange varieties.
