ducks and drakes

1. NOUN

/ dʌks ənd dreɪks / (AmE), / dʌks ənd dreɪks / (BrE)
a game in which a flat stone is thrown so that it skips or bounces along the surface of calm water
a game of skipping a stone on water

Examples

On a sunny afternoon, the children played ducks and drakes by the tranquil lake.
He boasted that he could set a record for ducks and drakes with his favorite flat stone.
Skill at ducks and drakes often depends on the angle and speed of the throw.

Etymology

From the traditional English phrase referring to the resemblance of the skipping stone to waterfowl moving across a pond.
