gaseous nebula

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡæsiəs ˈnɛbjʊlə / (AmE), / ˈɡeɪsɪəs ˈnɛbjʊlə / (BrE)
a luminous interstellar cloud of gas and dust, often containing young stars or the material for star formation
interstellar cloud of gas and dust

Examples

Astronomers spent the night observing the gaseous nebula through the telescope.
The Orion region features a large gaseous nebula where new stars are being born.
A gaseous nebula often glows in vibrant colors due to the ionization of its elements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nebula - a cloud of gas and dust in space

Etymology

From Latin 'nebula' (mist, cloud) and 'gaseous' from French 'gazeux', from 'gaz' (gas).
