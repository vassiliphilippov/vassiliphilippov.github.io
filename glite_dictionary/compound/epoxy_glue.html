epoxy glue

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈpɑksi ɡlu / (AmE), / ɪˈpɒksi ɡluː / (BrE)
a strong adhesive made from a thermosetting resin, commonly used for bonding materials and in coatings and laminates
strong adhesive made from thermosetting resin

Examples

The broken vase was repaired using a strong epoxy glue to hold the pieces together.
For best results, mix both components of the epoxy glue thoroughly before application.
He chose epoxy glue because it forms a durable, long-lasting bond on metal surfaces.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: epoxy resin - a class of reactive prepolymers used in adhesives and coatings

Etymology

From 'epoxy' (from Greek 'epi' + 'oxys' meaning sharp/acid) and 'glue.'
