star tulip

1. NOUN

/ stɑr ˈtʌlɪp / (AmE), / stɑː ˈtʌlɪp / (BrE)
A small perennial plant native to the northwestern United States, with slender, bent stems and white, star-shaped flowers whose petals are shaped like cat's ears.
small plant with star-shaped white flowers

Examples

The star tulip blooms in early spring across the hills of Oregon.
Botanists were excited to find a rare cluster of star tulips during their survey.
Its delicate petals make the star tulip a favorite among wildflower enthusiasts.
