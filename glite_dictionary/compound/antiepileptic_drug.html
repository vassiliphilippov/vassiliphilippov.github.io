antiepileptic drug

1. NOUN

/ ˌæntiɪˌpɪlɛptɪk drʌɡ / (AmE), / ˌæntiɪˌpɪlɛptɪk drʌɡ / (BrE)
A drug used to treat or prevent seizures, especially in epilepsy.
drug for treating epilepsy or seizures
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed an antiepileptic drug to help control her seizures.
Some antiepileptic drugs can cause side effects such as drowsiness or dizziness.
Research is ongoing to develop newer and safer antiepileptic drugs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anticonvulsant - a drug that prevents or reduces the severity of epileptic fits or other convulsions

Etymology

From anti- (against) + epileptic (pertaining to epilepsy), relating to drugs that counteract epilepsy.
