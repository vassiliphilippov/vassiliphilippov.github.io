star shell

1. NOUN

/ stɑr ʃɛl / (AmE), / stɑː ʃɛl / (BrE)
A type of artillery shell designed to burst in the air and release a bright light, illuminating an area at night.
artillery shell for illumination

Examples

The soldiers fired a star shell to reveal enemy positions in the darkness.
A single star shell lit up the entire battlefield for several minutes.
During the exercise, a star shell was used to illuminate the training area at night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flare - A device producing bright light for illumination or signaling
