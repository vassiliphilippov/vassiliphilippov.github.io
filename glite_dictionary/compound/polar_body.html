polar body

1. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊlər ˈbɑdi / (AmE), / ˈpəʊlə ˈbɒdi / (BrE)
a small cell containing little cytoplasm that is produced together with an oocyte during meiosis and is later discarded
small cell produced with oocyte, later discarded
medical

Examples

During oogenesis, a polar body is formed and typically degenerates without further development.
Scientists studied the chromosome count in the polar body to evaluate egg quality.
Each human egg contains a polar body as a by-product of meiosis.

Etymology

From 'polar' (relating to a pole or extremity) and 'body', referring to the cell's position relative to the oocyte.
