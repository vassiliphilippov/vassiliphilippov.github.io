vermis cerebelli

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɜrmɪs sərəˈbɛlaɪ / (AmE), / ˈvɜːmɪs sɛrəˈbɛlaɪ / (BrE)
the narrow, central part of the cerebellum that lies between the two hemispheres
central part of the cerebellum
medical

Examples

Damage to the vermis cerebelli may result in problems with posture and balance.
In MRI scans, the vermis cerebelli is visible between the two cerebellar hemispheres.
Certain developmental disorders can affect the vermis cerebelli, leading to coordination issues.

Etymology

From Latin 'vermis' (worm) and 'cerebelli', genitive of 'cerebellum' (little brain).
