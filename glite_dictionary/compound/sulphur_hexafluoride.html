sulphur hexafluoride

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌlfɚ ˌhɛk.səˌfluːˈraɪd / (AmE), / ˈsʌlfə ˌhɛksəˈflʊəraɪd / (BrE)
a colorless, odorless gas used mainly as an insulating material in the electrical industry and known for its strong greenhouse effect
colorless insulating gas; strong greenhouse gas
medical

Examples

The electrical industry uses sulphur hexafluoride as an effective insulating gas in high-voltage switches.
Concerns have been raised about the impact of sulphur hexafluoride on global warming.
Technicians must handle sulphur hexafluoride carefully because of its high density and greenhouse potential.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: greenhouse gas - a gas that contributes to global warming by trapping heat in the atmosphere

Etymology

From 'sulphur' (Latin 'sulphur') and 'hexafluoride' (hexa- meaning six + 'fluoride', from fluorine).
