animal psychology

1. NOUN

/ ˈænɪməl saɪˈkɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ˈænɪml saɪˈkɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the scientific study of animal behavior and mental processes
the study of animal behavior

Examples

She decided to specialize in animal psychology because she was fascinated by the ways dogs communicate.
Research in animal psychology has provided insights into how animals solve problems.
Studies in animal psychology often involve observing animals in both wild and controlled settings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: comparative psychology - the study of the similarities and differences in behavior of different species, including humans
