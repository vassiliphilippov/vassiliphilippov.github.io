emesis basin

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛməsɪs ˈbeɪsən / (AmE), / ˈɛməsɪs ˈbeɪsən / (BrE)
a shallow, kidney-shaped container used by patients, especially in hospitals, to collect vomit or other bodily fluids
kidney-shaped hospital container for vomit
medical

Examples

The nurse quickly handed the patient an emesis basin when she felt sick.
Please place the used emesis basin in the biohazard area after cleaning up.
Hospitals keep emesis basins next to beds for patients who might vomit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kidney dish - a shallow, kidney-shaped dish used in medical settings

Etymology

From Greek emesis (vomiting) and English basin.
