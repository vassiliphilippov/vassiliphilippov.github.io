J particle

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒeɪ ˌpɑːrtɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈdʒeɪ ˌpɑːtɪkəl / (BrE)
a neutral meson with a large mass, discovered in particle physics and also known as the J/psi meson
a heavy neutral meson in particle physics

Examples

The discovery of the J particle in 1974 confirmed the existence of the charm quark.
Physicists studied the decay patterns of the J particle in high-energy experiments.
The mass of the J particle is significantly greater than that of most other mesons.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meson - a subatomic particle made of one quark and one antiquark

Etymology

Named for the letter 'J', chosen independently by two research groups that discovered it in 1974; also called J/psi after both naming conventions.
