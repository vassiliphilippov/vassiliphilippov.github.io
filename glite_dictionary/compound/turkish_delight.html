Turkish Delight

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɝːkɪʃ dɪˈlaɪt / (AmE), / ˈtɜːkɪʃ dɪˈlaɪt / (BrE)
A sweet, jellied confection, often flavored with rose water, lemon, or other fruits and dusted with powdered sugar, traditional in Turkish cuisine.
sweet jellied confectionery

Examples

My grandmother used to bring back boxes of Turkish Delight from her travels.
The rich flavors of rose and lemon make Turkish Delight a unique treat.
He offered me a piece of Turkish Delight dusted with powdered sugar.

Etymology

From Turkish 'lokum', introduced to English during the 19th century as a delicacy from Turkey.
