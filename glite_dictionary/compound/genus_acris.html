genus Acris

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈeɪ.krɪs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈeɪ.krɪs / (BrE)
A genus of small North American frogs commonly known as cricket frogs.
a genus of North American cricket frogs

Examples

The genus Acris includes species that are commonly found near ponds and streams across North America.
Researchers studied the unique call patterns of frogs within the genus Acris.
Cricket frogs belonging to the genus Acris are known for their jumping abilities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cricket frog - a small North American frog of the genus Acris

Etymology

From Latin 'genus' (kind) and 'Acris', the scientific name assigned to the group.
