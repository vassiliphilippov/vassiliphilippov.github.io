red-berry

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛd ˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈrɛd ˌbɛri / (BrE)
A small spiny evergreen shrub native to western United States and Mexico, known for its minute flowers and bright red berries.
evergreen shrub with red berries

Examples

The red-berry thrives in dry hillsides of California.
Gardeners often plant red-berry shrubs for their ornamental fruit.
Wildlife is attracted to the bright fruit of the red-berry during late autumn.

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɛd ˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈrɛd ˌbɛri / (BrE)
A North American perennial herb with compound leaves, clusters of small white flowers, and bright red oval poisonous berries.
a perennial herb with red poisonous berries

Examples

The red-berry is attractive, but its berries can be toxic if eaten.
We found several red-berry plants growing along the woodland trail.
Gardeners should be cautious when planting red-berry where children or pets might be tempted by its fruit.
