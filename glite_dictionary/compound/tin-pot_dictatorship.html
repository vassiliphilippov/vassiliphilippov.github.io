tin-pot dictatorship

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɪn ˌpɑt ˌdɪkˈteɪtɚˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈtɪn ˌpɒt ˌdɪkˈteɪtəʃɪp / (BrE)
A small, petty, or insignificant authoritarian regime, often regarded with contempt or ridicule.
petty or insignificant dictatorship

Examples

Living under a tin-pot dictatorship often leads to human rights abuses and lack of freedom.
Journalists criticized the leader's rule as nothing more than a tin-pot dictatorship.
Many citizens fled the country to escape the tin-pot dictatorship that had taken over.

Synset Examples

Living under a tin-pot dictatorship is challenging for everyone.
The rebels fought to overthrow the tin-pot dictatorship.
The tin-pot dictatorship led to widespread unrest among citizens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dictatorship - a form of government where one person or group holds absolute power

Etymology

From 'tin-pot,' meaning cheap or worthless, and 'dictatorship.'
