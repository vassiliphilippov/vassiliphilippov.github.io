fourfold point correlation

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔrˌfoʊld pɔɪnt ˌkɔrəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈfɔːfəʊld pɔɪnt ˌkɒrəˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
an index measuring the relationship between two sets of scores, each represented as ordered binary dimensions, such as male-female.
statistical index for binary variable relations

Examples

The fourfold point correlation is often used to determine the association between two binary traits.
Researchers calculated the fourfold point correlation to analyze the connection between gender and voting preferences.
In this study, the fourfold point correlation offered insight into the link between two dichotomous variables.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: correlation - a mutual relationship or connection between two or more things, often statistics-related
