Kennelly-Heaviside layer

1. NOUN

/ kəˌnɛli ˈhɛvisaɪd ˌleɪɚ / (AmE), / kəˌnɛli ˈhɛvɪsaɪd ˌleɪə / (BrE)
a region of the ionosphere, approximately 50 to 90 miles above the Earth, that reflects medium-length radio waves
region of the ionosphere reflecting radio waves

Examples

Scientists discovered that the Kennelly-Heaviside layer plays a crucial role in long-distance radio transmission.
At night, the Kennelly-Heaviside layer becomes more reflective, affecting radio communications.
Early radio operators relied on the Kennelly-Heaviside layer to bounce signals beyond the horizon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ionosphere - layer of Earth's atmosphere ionized by solar radiation

Etymology

Named after American engineer Arthur E. Kennelly and British physicist Oliver Heaviside, who predicted its existence.
