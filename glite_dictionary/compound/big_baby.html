big baby

1. NOUN

/ bɪɡ ˈbeɪ.bi / (AmE), / bɪɡ ˈbeɪ.bi / (BrE)
An adult or older child who behaves in a childish, especially overly sensitive or whiny, manner.
person acting childish or over-sensitive
informal

Examples

After losing the board game, he turned into a big baby and refused to speak to anyone.
Don't be such a big baby—it's just a little rain!
Whenever she's criticized, she acts like a big baby and pouts.

Synset Examples

She called him a big baby for crying at the movie.
Stop being a big baby and just apologize.
He's acting like a big baby over the lost game.
