God's green earth

1. NOUN

/ ɡɑdz ɡriːn ɜrθ / (AmE), / ɡɒdz ɡriːn ɜːθ / (BrE)
Used to refer to the whole natural world or the planet, often to emphasize its vastness, beauty, or importance.
the natural world; the planet Earth

Examples

Nobody on God's green earth could have predicted what happened next.
She wanted to travel every inch of God's green earth before she settled down.
There isn't another place like this on God's green earth.

Synset Examples

The beauty of spring reminds us of God's green earth.
Hiking through the mountains makes me appreciate God's green earth.
We must protect God's green earth for future generations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: earth - the planet on which we live; the world
  • Synonym: nature - the physical world and living things in general

Etymology

From the phrase invoking God and the lushness of nature; attested in English since the 19th century.
