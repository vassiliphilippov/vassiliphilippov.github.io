Cretaceous period

1. NOUN

/ krəˈteɪʃəs ˈpɪri.əd / (AmE), / krɪˈteɪʃəs ˈpɪəri.əd / (BrE)
the last period of the Mesozoic Era, lasting from about 145 to 66 million years ago, marked by the extinction of dinosaurs and the rise of flowering plants and modern insects.
geological period when dinosaurs became extinct

Examples

The Cretaceous period was marked by a mass extinction event that wiped out the non-avian dinosaurs.
Many of today's flowering plants first appeared during the Cretaceous period.
Scientists study rocks from the Cretaceous period to learn about Earth's ancient environments.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Jurassic period - the period preceding the Cretaceous, known for large dinosaurs
  • Degree Higher: Paleogene period - the geological period following the Cretaceous, starting the Cenozoic Era

Etymology

From Latin 'cretaceus' (of chalk), referring to the chalk deposits of this period.
