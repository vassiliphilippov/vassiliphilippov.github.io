impulse turbine

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪmpʌls ˈtɝːˌbaɪn / (AmE), / ˈɪmpʌls ˈtɜːˌbaɪn / (BrE)
A turbine in which jets of fluid are directed at the blades, causing rotation primarily by the force of the fluid's impulse.
turbine driven by fluid jets striking blades

Examples

The engineers chose to install an impulse turbine for its efficiency in high-head, low-flow applications.
Unlike a reaction turbine, an impulse turbine operates by converting the kinetic energy of a fluid into mechanical work.
Maintenance of an impulse turbine is often simpler due to the absence of pressure casings around the rotor.

Etymology

From 'impulse' (physics: force applied over a short time) + 'turbine' (rotary engine).
