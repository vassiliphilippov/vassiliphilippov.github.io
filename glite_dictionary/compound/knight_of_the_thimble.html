knight of the thimble

1. NOUN

/ naɪt əv ðə ˈθɪmbəl / (AmE), / naɪt əv ðə ˈθɪmbəl / (BrE)
A person highly skilled in sewing or tailoring, often admired for their craftsmanship.
expert tailor or seamstress

Examples

My grandmother was a knight of the thimble, known for her exquisite embroidery.
The local historical society honored a knight of the thimble for decades of dedicated costume making.
In her small workshop, the knight of the thimble stitched together wedding dresses for generations.

Synset Examples

She’s a skilled seamstress, a true knight of the thimble.
Everyone admired the knight of the thimble’s intricate designs.
The knight of the thimble created beautiful costumes for the play.

Etymology

Modeled after the chivalric title 'knight' and the sewing tool 'thimble', used figuratively.
