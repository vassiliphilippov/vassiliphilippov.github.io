Mount Kanchenjunga

1. PROPN

/ maʊnt ˌkæn.tʃənˈdʒuːŋ.gə / (AmE), / maʊnt ˌkæn.tʃənˈdʒʌŋ.gə / (BrE)
the third highest mountain in the world, located in the Himalayas on the border between Nepal and the Indian state of Sikkim, reaching 28,208 feet (8,586 meters) in elevation
mountain in the Himalayas, world's third highest

Examples

Many climbers dream of scaling Mount Kanchenjunga due to its challenging ascent and remote location.
The snow-capped peak of Mount Kanchenjunga can be seen from Darjeeling on clear days.
Local legends hold Mount Kanchenjunga as a sacred mountain, revered by communities on both sides of the border.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Mount Everest - the highest mountain in the world, also in the Himalayas

Etymology

From Tibetan and local Himalayan languages, meaning 'The Five Treasures of Snow'.
