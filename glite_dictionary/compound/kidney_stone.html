kidney stone

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɪdni stoʊn / (AmE), / ˈkɪdni stəʊn / (BrE)
A hard mass formed in the kidneys from crystals that separate from the urine, often causing pain and urinary problems.
hard mass formed in the kidney
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that my severe pain was caused by a kidney stone.
She was hospitalized last year after passing a kidney stone.
Drinking plenty of water may help prevent kidney stones from forming.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: renal calculus - another term for a kidney stone

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɪdni stoʊn / (AmE), / ˈkɪdni stəʊn / (BrE)
A hard, solid deposit that forms in the kidney from minerals and salts, often causing pain and urinary problems.
a hard mineral deposit in the kidney
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that the kidney stone was blocking his urinary tract.
After suffering from severe pain, she was diagnosed with multiple kidney stones.
Drinking plenty of water each day can help prevent the formation of a kidney stone.

Synset Examples

He was diagnosed with kidney stones last week.
