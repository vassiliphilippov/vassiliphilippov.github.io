sirenian mammal

1. NOUN

/ saɪˈriːniən ˈmæməl / (AmE), / saɪˈriːniən ˈmaməl / (BrE)
Any of a group of large, herbivorous aquatic mammals, such as manatees and dugongs, with paddle-shaped tails, flipper-like forelimbs, and no hind limbs.
large aquatic herbivorous mammal (manatees, dugongs)

Examples

The Amazonian manatee is a rare sirenian mammal found only in the Amazon River basin.
Conservationists are concerned about the declining numbers of sirenian mammals due to habitat loss and boat collisions.
Both manatees and dugongs are types of sirenian mammal known for their gentle nature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: manatee - a large aquatic mammal of the family Trichechidae
  • Synonym: dugong - a large aquatic mammal of the family Dugongidae

Etymology

From Latin 'Sirenia', the scientific order name, referring to the sirens of Greek mythology.
