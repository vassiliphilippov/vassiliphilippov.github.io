Elizabethan era

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌlɪzəˈbiðən ˈɛrə / (AmE), / ɪˌlɪzəˈbiːθən ˈɪərə / (BrE)
the period in British history during the reign of Queen Elizabeth I (1558–1603), noted for great literary achievement and national prosperity
period of Queen Elizabeth I's rule in England
british

Examples

Many people consider the Elizabethan era the golden age of English literature and drama.
Shakespeare wrote his famous plays during the Elizabethan era.
The fashion and architecture of the Elizabethan era are still admired today.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Elizabethan - relating to the reign of Queen Elizabeth I

Etymology

From Elizabethan (referring to Elizabeth I of England) + era.
