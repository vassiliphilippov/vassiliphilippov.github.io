wood shavings

1. NOUN

/ wʊd ˈʃeɪvɪŋz / (AmE), / wʊd ˈʃeɪvɪŋz / (BrE)
Thin, curled pieces of wood, often used for packing, stuffing, or animal bedding.
thin curled pieces of wood

Examples

The fragile vase was protected by a layer of wood shavings in the box.
Farmers often use wood shavings as bedding for their animals.
She carefully filled the crate with wood shavings to cushion the glass jars during shipping.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: sawdust - powdery particles of wood produced by sawing
