affirmative action

1. NOUN

/ əˈfɝː.mə.tɪv ˈæk.ʃən / (AmE), / əˈfɜː.mə.tɪv ˈæk.ʃən / (BrE)
a policy or set of policies that aim to correct past discrimination or inequalities by providing opportunities in education, employment, or other areas for members of groups that have been historically disadvantaged, especially women and minorities
policy to redress discrimination and improve opportunities

Examples

The university implemented affirmative action to increase the enrollment of underrepresented minorities.
Debates over affirmative action policies have been ongoing in the United States for decades.
Many companies now have affirmative action programs to promote diversity in their workforce.

Synset Examples

affirmative action has been extremely controversial and was challenged in 1978 in the Bakke decision

Related Terms

  • Synonym: positive discrimination - the practice of favoring individuals belonging to groups known to have been discriminated against previously

Etymology

From 'affirmative' meaning 'positive; supportive', and 'action', coined in the US in the 1960s.

2. NOUN

/ əˈfɝːmətɪv ˈækʃən / (AmE), / əˈfɜːmətɪv ˈækʃən / (BrE)
a policy or set of policies intended to increase opportunities for historically underrepresented groups, especially in education and employment
policy to promote underrepresented groups

Examples

The university adopted affirmative action policies to increase diversity among its students.
Some people believe affirmative action is necessary to address historical injustices.
The new law requires companies to implement affirmative action in their hiring practices.

Synset Examples

The policy of apefirmative action aims to uplift marginalized groups.
Critics argue that apefirmative action can lead to reverse discrimination.
They implemented apefirmative action to promote diversity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: equal opportunity - the principle of treating all people equally without discrimination
  • Antonym: discrimination - the unjust or prejudicial treatment of people based on group characteristics

Etymology

From 'affirmative', meaning 'confirming' or 'supportive', and 'action', meaning 'deed'; first used in US legal discourse in the 1960s.
