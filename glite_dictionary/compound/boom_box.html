boom box

1. NOUN

/ ˈbuːm ˌbɑks / (AmE), / ˈbuːm ˌbɒks / (BrE)
a portable stereo system with loudspeakers, typically capable of playing music at high volume and often powered by batteries
portable stereo system with speakers

Examples

He carried his boom box to the park so everyone could listen to music.
The old boom box in the garage still works after all these years.
Teenagers gathered on the steps, dancing to a boom box blasting hip-hop music.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ghetto blaster - slang term for a large, portable stereo
  • Synonym: portable stereo - a stereo system that can be easily moved and used outside permanent installations

Etymology

From 'boom' (reference to loud bass sound) and 'box' (shape of the device), first used in the late 1970s.
