John Q. Public

1. PROPN

/ ˌdʒɑn ˌkju ˈpʌblɪk / (AmE), / ˌdʒɒn ˌkjuː ˈpʌblɪk / (BrE)
the typical or average member of the general public, regarded as representative of ordinary people
average member of the public
american

Examples

Politicians often try to appeal to John Q. Public during elections.
The changes are designed to make life easier for John Q. Public.
Many laws are confusing to John Q. Public, who may not have legal training.

Synset Examples

Politicians need to listen to John Q. Public's concerns.
John Q. Public is often unaware of the issues.
The report aims to inform John Q. Public about healthcare.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Joe Public - an ordinary person, the general public (chiefly British)

Etymology

Modelled after common American male names, with 'Q.' as a generic middle initial, representing any person in the public.
