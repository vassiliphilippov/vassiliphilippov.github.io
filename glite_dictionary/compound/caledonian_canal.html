Caledonian Canal

1. PROPN

/ ˌkæl.əˈdoʊ.ni.ən kəˈnæl / (AmE), / ˌkæl.ɪˈdəʊ.ni.ən kəˈnæl / (BrE)
a canal in northern Scotland connecting the North Sea with the Atlantic Ocean, running from Moray Firth to Loch Linnhe
canal in northern Scotland
british

Examples

We took a scenic boat tour along the Caledonian Canal during our trip to Scotland.
Engineers built the Caledonian Canal in the early 19th century to provide a navigable route across Scotland.
Although the Caledonian Canal is no longer heavily used for shipping, it attracts many tourists each year.

Etymology

From Caledonia, the Latin name for Scotland, and canal.
