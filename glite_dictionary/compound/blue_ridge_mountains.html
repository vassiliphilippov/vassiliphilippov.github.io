Blue Ridge Mountains

1. PROPN

/ blu rɪdʒ ˈmaʊntn̩z / (AmE), / bluː rɪdʒ ˈmaʊntənz / (BrE)
a mountain range forming part of the Appalachian Mountains, extending from southern Pennsylvania to northern Georgia in the eastern United States
U.S. mountain range in the Appalachians

Examples

The Blue Ridge Mountains are known for their scenic beauty and blue-tinted appearance.
We spent our summer hiking through the Blue Ridge Mountains in Virginia.
Many artists have been inspired by the misty landscapes of the Blue Ridge Mountains.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Appalachian Mountains - major mountain range in eastern North America
