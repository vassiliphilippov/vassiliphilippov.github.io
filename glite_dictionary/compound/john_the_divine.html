John the Divine

1. PROPN

/ dʒɑn ðə dɪˈvaɪn / (AmE), / dʒɒn ðə dɪˈvaɪn / (BrE)
A title for the Apostle John, traditionally regarded as the author of the fourth Gospel, three epistles, and the Book of Revelation in the New Testament.
Apostle John, author in the New Testament

Examples

Many churches are named after John the Divine, reflecting his importance in Christian history.
Some scholars debate whether John the Divine and John the Apostle were the same person.
The Book of Revelation is attributed to John the Divine in several biblical traditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: John the Apostle - One of Jesus's original disciples, traditionally identified as the author of several New Testament books

Etymology

Divine here refers to 'the theologian'; the title distinguishes him as a spiritual or doctrinal authority.
