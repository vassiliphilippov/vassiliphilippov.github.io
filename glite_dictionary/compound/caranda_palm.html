caranda palm

1. NOUN

/ kəˈrɑːndə pɑːm / (AmE), / kəˈrændə pɑːm / (BrE)
A South American palm tree (Copernicia australis) that produces a wax similar to carnauba wax.
South American palm tree yielding wax

Examples

The caranda palm is valued for the high-quality wax extracted from its leaves.
Researchers discovered that the caranda palm thrives in the wetlands of northern Argentina.
Local communities harvest wax from the caranda palm to use in traditional crafts and products.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carnauba wax - a wax from the leaves of the Brazilian carnauba palm
