eternal sleep

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈtɝːnəl sliːp / (AmE), / ɪˈtɜːnəl sliːp / (BrE)
a euphemistic expression for death, often suggesting a peaceful or everlasting rest
euphemism for death

Examples

After a long illness, he finally entered eternal sleep surrounded by family.
Many poets have described death as an eternal sleep to provide comfort.
People often refer to a loved one’s passing as their eternal sleep.

Synset Examples

He spoke of his dog’s eternal sleep with sadness.
She found comfort in the idea of eternal sleep.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: death - the end of life
