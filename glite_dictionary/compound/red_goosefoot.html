red goosefoot

1. NOUN

/ rɛd ˈguːsfʊt / (AmE), / rɛd ˈɡuːsfʊt / (BrE)
an annual Eurasian plant with reddish stems, often regarded as a weed, now naturalized in the United States
a Eurasian weed with reddish stems

Examples

The field was full of red goosefoot growing among the crops.
Gardeners often remove red goosefoot to prevent it from competing with vegetables.
Some wild birds feed on the seeds of red goosefoot in late summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: goosefoot - a plant of the genus Chenopodium, with leaves said to resemble a goose's foot

2. NOUN

/ ɹɛd ˈɡusˌfʊt / (AmE), / ɹɛd ˈɡuːs.fʊt / (BrE)
a herbaceous plant (Chenopodium rubrum), typically with reddish stems, once considered poisonous to swine
a herbaceous plant toxic to swine

Examples

The farmer found red goosefoot growing near the edge of the pasture.
Livestock owners often remove red goosefoot to protect their animals from poisoning.
The bright reddish stems of red goosefoot are a distinguishing characteristic in the wild.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: goosefoot - a plant of the genus Chenopodium, with leaves resembling a goose's foot
