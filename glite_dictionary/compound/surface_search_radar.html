surface search radar

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɝːfɪs sɜːrtʃ ˈreɪˌdɑɹ / (AmE), / ˈsɜːfɪs sɜːtʃ ˈreɪˌdɑː / (BrE)
a radar system used by naval or maritime forces to detect and track surface targets such as ships or boats
radar for detecting surface targets at sea

Examples

The ship was equipped with a new surface search radar to improve its ability to detect enemy vessels.
During the exercise, the crew relied heavily on the surface search radar for navigation in poor visibility.
Upgrades to the surface search radar have expanded the ship's surveillance capabilities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: radar - a system for detecting objects using radio waves
