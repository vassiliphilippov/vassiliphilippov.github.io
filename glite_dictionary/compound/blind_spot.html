blind spot

1. NOUN

/ ˈblaɪnd spɑt / (AmE), / ˈblaɪnd spɒt / (BrE)
the small area on the retina where the optic nerve enters the eye and where no light-sensitive cells are present, resulting in an absence of vision at that spot
area of retina insensitive to light

Examples

The image fell on my blind spot, so I couldn't see it clearly.
Doctors often explain the blind spot when teaching about the human eye.
Every person has a natural blind spot where the optic nerve exits the retina.

2. NOUN

/ blaɪnd spɑt / (AmE), / blaɪnd spɒt / (BrE)
an area in which a person lacks understanding, awareness, or is prejudiced and thus fails to exercise good judgment
area of ignorance or prejudice

Examples

His stubbornness about climate change is a clear blind spot in his otherwise rational thinking.
Everyone has a blind spot when it comes to their own weaknesses.
Despite her expertise, she had a significant blind spot regarding new technology.

Synset Examples

golf is one of his blind spots and he's proud of it

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weakness - a flaw or area lacking ability or understanding
  • Synonym: bias - an inclination or prejudice preventing impartial judgment
