knowledge domain

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɑːlɪdʒ doʊˈmeɪn / (AmE), / ˈnɒlɪdʒ dəʊˈmeɪn / (BrE)
A particular area or field of expertise, study, or knowledge, characterized by its own concepts, terms, and methods.
area or field of knowledge

Examples

Genetics is a rapidly expanding knowledge domain within the field of biology.
Each knowledge domain has its own specialized vocabulary and concepts.
Teachers often integrate information from different knowledge domains to enrich classroom lessons.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: field - an area of study or activity
  • Synonym: discipline - a branch of knowledge or learning
