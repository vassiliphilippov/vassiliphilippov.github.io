good tucker

1. NOUN

/ ɡʊd ˈtʌkər / (AmE), / ɡʊd ˈtʌkə / (BrE)
(informal, chiefly Australian and New Zealand) Good food; a satisfying or tasty meal.
informal term for good food or a meal
informal

Examples

If you're looking for good tucker, that pub down the road is the place to go.
After a long day working outdoors, nothing beats some good tucker around the campfire.
Mum always makes sure there's plenty of good tucker on the table when we visit.

Synset Examples

She always has a good tucker ready for guests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tucker - food, especially in Australian or New Zealand English

Etymology

Australian English, from 'tucker' meaning food, which possibly derives from British dialectal 'to tuck' meaning to eat heartily.
