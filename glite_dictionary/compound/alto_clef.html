alto clef

1. NOUN

/ ˈæltoʊ klɛf / (AmE), / ˈæltəʊ klɛf / (BrE)
a musical clef that places middle C on the third line of the staff, commonly used for viola music
musical clef placing middle C on third line

Examples

The viola part is usually written in the alto clef rather than the treble or bass clef.
Learning to read the alto clef can be challenging for beginners.
She carefully notated her composition using the alto clef for the viola section.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: tenor clef - a clef that puts middle C on the fourth line of the staff
  • Degree Higher: treble clef - a clef that puts G above middle C on the second line of the staff
  • Degree Lower: bass clef - a clef that puts F below middle C on the fourth line of the staff

Etymology

From Italian 'alto' (high) and 'clef' (key, from French 'clef').
