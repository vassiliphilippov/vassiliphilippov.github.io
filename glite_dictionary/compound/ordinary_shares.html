ordinary shares

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔːrdəneri ʃɛrz / (AmE), / ˈɔːdɪnəri ʃeəz / (BrE)
Shares in a company that give the holder a share of the profits and the right to vote at shareholder meetings, but do not have any preferential rights over dividends or assets.
regular voting shares of a company
british

Examples

The company announced it would issue new ordinary shares to raise capital.
She invested in ordinary shares rather than preferred stock for greater voting rights.
Holders of ordinary shares can participate in annual general meetings and vote on key decisions.

Synset Examples

over 40 million Americans invest in common stocks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: common stock - stock that entitles holder to voting rights and share of profits, without preference
