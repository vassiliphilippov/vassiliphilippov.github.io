doll's eyes

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɑlz ˌaɪz / (AmE), / ˈdɒlz ˌaɪz / (BrE)
A North American woodland herb (Actaea pachypoda) with clusters of small white, poisonous berries resembling the eyes of a doll.
North American plant with white poisonous berries

Examples

She warned the children not to touch the doll's eyes growing at the edge of the woods.
The distinctive berries of doll's eyes make the plant easy to identify in the forest.
Botanists study the toxicity of plants like doll's eyes because of their danger to humans and animals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: white baneberry - Another common name for Actaea pachypoda, the same plant as doll's eyes
