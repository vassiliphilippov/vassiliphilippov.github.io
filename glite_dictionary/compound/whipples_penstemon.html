Whipple's penstemon

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪpəlz ˈpɛnstɪˌmɒn / (AmE), / ˈwɪpəlz ˈpɛnstɪmən / (BrE)
a species of penstemon with wine and lavender to purple and black flowers, found in the Rocky Mountains from Montana to Arizona and New Mexico
a Rocky Mountain flowering plant species

Examples

We found a cluster of Whipple's penstemon blooming along the mountain trail.
The deep purple hues of Whipple's penstemon make it a favorite among wildflower enthusiasts.
Whipple's penstemon thrives in the rocky soils of the southwestern United States.

Etymology

Named after Amiel Weeks Whipple, an American military engineer and explorer, and Penstemon, the genus name.
