semi-metric space

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛmiˈmɛtrɪk speɪs / (AmE), / ˌsɛmiˈmɛtrɪk speɪs / (BrE)
a generalized metric space in which the distance between two distinct points may be zero, rather than positive
generalized metric space; distinct points may have zero distance

Examples

In a semi-metric space, it is possible for two different points to have zero distance between them.
Some problems in topology utilize the concept of a semi-metric space to study convergence.
The definition of a semi-metric space relaxes one of the conditions required for a true metric space.

Synset Examples

In the same way as every normed space is a metric space, every seminormed space is a pseudometric space.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: metric space - mathematical space where the distance between distinct points is always positive
  • Synonym: pseudometric space - a space like a metric space but may assign zero distance to distinct points

Etymology

Formed from 'semi-' (Latin ‘half’ or ‘partial’) and 'metric space', a term from mathematics referring to spaces with a defined distance function.
