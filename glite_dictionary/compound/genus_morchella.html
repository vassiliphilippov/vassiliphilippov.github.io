genus Morchella

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒiːnəs mɔːrˈkɛlə / (AmE), / ˌdʒiːnəs mɔːˈkɛlə / (BrE)
A genus of edible fungi known as morels, characterized by their honeycomb-like appearance and highly valued for culinary use.
genus of edible fungi (morels)

Examples

The genus Morchella includes several species prized by gourmet chefs for their distinctive flavor.
Scientists conducted a survey to map the distribution of the genus Morchella across North America.
Proper identification of fungi in the genus Morchella is important because they resemble some poisonous varieties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: morel - an edible mushroom of the species in Morchella genus

Etymology

From New Latin, with 'Morchella' from German 'Morchel' (morel).
