bush vetch

1. NOUN

/ bʊʃ vɛtʃ / (AmE), / bʊʃ vɛtʃ / (BrE)
a European plant with slender stems and purple flowers, often found as a weed in hedges and grassy places
European purple-flowered weed in hedges

Examples

The bush vetch is often seen climbing through hedgerows in the countryside.
Gardeners sometimes consider bush vetch a weed, though its purple flowers are attractive.
Pollinators such as bees are drawn to the blooms of bush vetch in spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vetch - a climbing plant with compound leaves and tendrils, part of the pea family
