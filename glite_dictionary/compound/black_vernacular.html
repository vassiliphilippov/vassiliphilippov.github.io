Black Vernacular

1. NOUN

/ blæk vərˈnækjələr / (AmE), / blæk vəˈnækjʊlə / (BrE)
A nonstandard variety of American English spoken by many African Americans in the United States, often characterized by distinctive pronunciation, grammar, and vocabulary.
variety of English spoken by African Americans

Examples

He grew up speaking Black Vernacular at home and learned Standard English at school.
Some linguists study Black Vernacular to better understand its unique grammatical structures.
The film realistically depicts conversations in Black Vernacular among its characters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: African American Vernacular English - a nonstandard variety of English spoken mainly by African Americans; also known as AAVE
  • Antonym: Standard English - the form of English widely accepted as the usual correct form
