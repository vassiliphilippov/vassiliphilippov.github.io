wind vane

1. NOUN

/ wɪnd veɪn / (AmE), / wɪnd veɪn / (BrE)
A device, usually mounted on a high point, that rotates to indicate the direction from which the wind is blowing.
device indicating wind direction

Examples

The old barn still has a wind vane perched on its roof.
We checked the wind vane to see which way the breeze was coming from before sailing.
As the storm approached, the wind vane spun rapidly in the shifting gusts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weathercock - a weather vane shaped like a rooster
