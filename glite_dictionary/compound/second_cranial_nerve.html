second cranial nerve

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛkənd ˈkreɪniəl nɜrv / (AmE), / ˈsɛkənd ˈkreɪnɪəl nɜːv / (BrE)
the cranial nerve responsible for transmitting visual information from the retina to the brain; also known as the optic nerve
the cranial nerve serving vision (optic nerve)
medical

Examples

Damage to the second cranial nerve can result in partial or complete loss of vision.
The ophthalmologist examined the patient for signs of second cranial nerve dysfunction.
Inflammation of the second cranial nerve is commonly known as optic neuritis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: optic nerve - the second cranial nerve, carries visual information from the eye to the brain
