Virgin Mary

1. PROPN

/ ˈvɜrdʒɪn ˈmɛri / (AmE), / ˈvɜːdʒɪn ˈmeəri / (BrE)
The mother of Jesus Christ, venerated especially in Christian traditions and highly honored by Roman Catholics.
mother of Jesus Christ in Christianity

Examples

Many artworks depict the Virgin Mary holding the infant Jesus.
The Virgin Mary is a central figure in both Catholic and Orthodox traditions.
Pilgrims visited the shrine dedicated to the Virgin Mary seeking comfort and blessings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mary - the mother of Jesus in Christian tradition

2. NOUN

/ ˈvɜrdʒɪn ˈmɛri / (AmE), / ˈvɜːdʒɪn ˈmeəri / (BrE)
A non-alcoholic cocktail made with tomato juice and various spices, similar to a Bloody Mary but with no alcohol.
non-alcoholic tomato juice cocktail

Examples

She ordered a Virgin Mary because she was driving and didn’t want any alcohol.
At brunch, the bar offers both Bloody Marys and Virgin Marys for guests.
He prefers a Virgin Mary with extra spice and celery as garnish.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Bloody Mary - an alcoholic cocktail made with vodka, tomato juice, and spices

Etymology

Named by analogy with 'Bloody Mary', using 'virgin' to mean non-alcoholic.
