op art

1. NOUN

/ ɑp ɑrt / (AmE), / ɒp ɑːt / (BrE)
a style of abstract art from the 1960s characterized by the use of optical illusions, arrangements of colors, and patterns to create visual effects
visual art style using optical illusions

Examples

The museum features a gallery dedicated to op art and its striking visual illusions.
She finds op art fascinating for the way it manipulates perception through color and geometry.
Bridget Riley is a leading figure known for her innovative op art paintings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: optical illusion - an image perceived differently from reality, often used in op art

Etymology

Short for 'optical art', first used in the mid-1960s.
