civil time

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪvəl taɪm / (AmE), / ˈsɪvəl taɪm / (BrE)
the officially adopted time for a particular region, used for all civil purposes and often established by law or custom
official standard time used in daily life

Examples

In most countries, civil time is set by the government and follows time zones.
Radio stations often announce the exact civil time at the top of the hour.
Astronomers must distinguish civil time from solar time in their calculations.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: solar time - time measured by the position of the sun
