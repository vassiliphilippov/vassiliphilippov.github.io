trisodium orthophosphate

1. NOUN

/ traɪˌsoʊdiəm ˌɔrθoʊˈfɑs.feɪt / (AmE), / traɪˌsəʊdiəm ˌɔːθəʊˈfɒs.feɪt / (BrE)
a chemical compound, the tertiary phosphate of sodium (Na3PO4), used as a builder in soaps and detergents and for various industrial purposes
a sodium phosphate compound used in detergents

Examples

The cleaning power of this detergent is enhanced by the presence of trisodium orthophosphate.
Industrial water treatment often involves the use of trisodium orthophosphate as an additive.
Many manufacturers have reduced trisodium orthophosphate in their products due to environmental concerns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sodium phosphate - any salt of sodium and phosphoric acid; includes trisodium orthophosphate

Etymology

From tri- (three) + sodium + orthophosphate, indicating three sodium atoms combined with orthophosphate.
