ragged robin

1. NOUN

/ ˈræɡɪd ˈrɑbɪn / (AmE), / ˈræɡɪd ˈrɒbɪn / (BrE)
A perennial plant native to Europe and western Asia, known for its deeply divided pink flowers with ragged-edged petals.
a plant with pink, ragged-petalled flowers

Examples

A cluster of ragged robin brightened the edge of the damp meadow.
Gardeners prize ragged robin for its unusual, frilly blossoms.
The ragged robin thrives in moist soils and attracts butterflies.
