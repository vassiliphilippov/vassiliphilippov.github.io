diclofenac sodium

1. NOUN

/ daɪˌkloʊfəˌnæk ˈsoʊdiəm / (AmE), / daɪˌkləʊfəˌnæk ˈsəʊdiəm / (BrE)
A nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drug (NSAID), often prescribed to reduce pain and inflammation, especially in conditions like arthritis.
an NSAID for pain and inflammation
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed diclofenac sodium to help manage her arthritis pain.
My pharmacist warned me about the possible stomach side effects of diclofenac sodium.
After her knee surgery, she was given diclofenac sodium for inflammation and discomfort.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Voltaren - trade name for diclofenac sodium
  • Synonym: NSAID - nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drug

Etymology

From 'diclofenac', itself from chemical roots and 'sodium', indicating the sodium salt form.
