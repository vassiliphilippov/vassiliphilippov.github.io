fast asleep

1. ADJ

/ fæst əˈslip / (AmE), / fɑːst əˈsliːp / (BrE)
sleeping very deeply; in a state of complete and restful sleep.
in a deep sleep

Examples

By midnight, the children were fast asleep in their beds.
She was fast asleep when the storm began outside.
After a long day of hiking, he quickly fell fast asleep.

Synset Examples

lying fast asleep on the sofa
it would be cruel to wake him: he's sound asleep

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sound asleep - sleeping very deeply

2. ADJ

/ fæst əˈsliːp / (AmE), / fɑːst əˈsliːp / (BrE)
Sleeping very deeply and soundly; in a deep sleep.
in a deep, sound sleep

Examples

He was fast asleep when the phone rang.
By midnight, the children were all fast asleep in their beds.
Despite the loud thunderstorm, she remained fast asleep.

Synset Examples

He was fast asleep by the time I got home.
She fell fast asleep during the boring lecture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: asleep - in the state of sleep, not awake
  • Antonym: wide awake - fully awake and alert
