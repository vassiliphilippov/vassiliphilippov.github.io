legal holiday

1. NOUN

/ ˈliːɡəl ˈhɑːləˌdeɪ / (AmE), / ˈliːɡəl ˈhɒlɪˌdeɪ / (BrE)
A day officially designated by law as a holiday, when work and official business are generally suspended.
holiday recognized by law

Examples

Labor Day is considered a legal holiday in the United States.
Banks are usually closed on a legal holiday.
The company offers paid time off for every legal holiday during the year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: public holiday - a day designated as a holiday by the government for the general population
