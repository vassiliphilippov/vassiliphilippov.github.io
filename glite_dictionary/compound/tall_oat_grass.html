tall oat grass

1. NOUN

/ tɔl oʊt ɡræs / (AmE), / tɔːl əʊt ɡrɑːs / (BrE)
a coarse perennial Eurasian grass resembling oat, often found on roadsides, rough grassland, and hay meadows, and introduced in North America for forage
a Eurasian grass resembling oat, used for forage

Examples

The field was dotted with patches of tall oat grass swaying gently in the wind.
Farmers introduced tall oat grass to improve forage for their livestock.
Along the roadside, tall oat grass mixes with wildflowers, creating a lush habitat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grass - a plant with narrow leaves growing from the base
