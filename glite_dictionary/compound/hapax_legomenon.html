hapax legomenon

1. NOUN

/ ˈheɪˌpæks ləˈɡɑːməˌnɑːn / (AmE), / ˈheɪˌpaks ləˈɡɒmɪnɒn / (BrE)
a word or expression that occurs only once within a particular text, author's works, or in a given language
word occurring only once in a text or corpus

Examples

The word appears only once in the entire manuscript, making it a hapax legomenon.
Identifying a hapax legomenon can pose challenges in translation and interpretation.
Linguists are particularly interested in hapax legomena when studying ancient texts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nonce word - a word coined for a single occasion

Etymology

From Greek hapax (once) + legomenon (thing said).
