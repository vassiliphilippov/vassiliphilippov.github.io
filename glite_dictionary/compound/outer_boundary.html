outer boundary

1. NOUN

/ ˈaʊtɚ ˈbaʊndəri / (AmE), / ˈaʊtə ˈbaʊndəri / (BrE)
the outermost limit or edge that defines the extent of an object, area, or surface
the outside limit or edge

Examples

The fence marks the outer boundary of the property.
A stone wall was built along the outer boundary of the park for protection.
The painting was damaged near its outer boundary, but the central image remained intact.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: border - the edge or line separating two areas
  • Synonym: perimeter - the continuous line forming the boundary of a closed figure
  • Antonym: center - the middle point, opposite of boundary
