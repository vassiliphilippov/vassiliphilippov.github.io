back door

1. NOUN

/ bæk ˈdɔr / (AmE), / bæk ˈdɔː / (BrE)
a door at the rear of a building, typically used as a secondary or private entrance
door at the rear of a building

Examples

She quietly slipped out the back door to avoid being seen by the guests.
The delivery man left the package by the back door as instructed.
They heard a noise at the back door late last night.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: front door - the main entrance door at the front of a building
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02771749-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈbæk ˌdɔr / (AmE), / ˈbæk ˌdɔː / (BrE)
an undocumented or unauthorized method of accessing a computer system or its data, often bypassing normal security measures
unauthorized computer system access method

Examples

The hackers gained entry to the network through a back door left by a former employee.
Security experts warned that the new software update might contain a hidden back door.
The company issued a patch to close a back door discovered in its operating system.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trapdoor - a hidden software mechanism allowing secret access, mainly in computing
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06365888-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ bæk dɔr / (AmE), / bæk dɔː / (BrE)
a secret or unofficial way of gaining entry to a place, position, or opportunity, often by evading normal procedures or controls
secret or unofficial means of access

Examples

He managed to get the software by finding a back door on the company's website.
There are concerns about politicians using the back door to gain influence over regulatory bodies.
The club's membership process is strict, so most people try to find a back door in.

Synset Examples

he got his job through the back door

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loophole - a means of evading a rule without breaking it
  • Antonym: front door - the official or regular means of access; the main entrance
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
