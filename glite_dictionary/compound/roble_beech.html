roble beech

1. NOUN

/ ˈroʊbəl bitʃ / (AmE), / ˈrəʊbəl biːtʃ / (BrE)
A tall deciduous tree native to South America, known for its hardwood and often used in timber production.
South American deciduous tree

Examples

The furniture was crafted from roble beech, prized for its strength and beauty.
In Chile, the roble beech forests are vital to the local ecosystem.
The roble beech sheds its leaves in the dry season, revealing its striking trunk.

Etymology

'Roble' is Spanish for 'oak', referring to its local name in South America.
