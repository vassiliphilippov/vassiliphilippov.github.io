indirect transmission

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.daɪˈrɛkt trænzˈmɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / ˌɪn.dɪˈrɛkt trænzˈmɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
the spread of an infectious agent to a person via an intermediate object (fomite) or animal (vector), rather than by direct contact
disease spread via intermediate object or vector
medical

Examples

Influenza can be contracted through indirect transmission when someone touches a contaminated doorknob.
The hospital implemented new hygiene protocols to reduce the risk of indirect transmission of pathogens.
Mosquitoes can play a role in the indirect transmission of diseases such as malaria.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: direct transmission - spread of infection through direct contact with an infected individual
