rhetorical question

1. NOUN

/ rəˈtɔrɪkəl ˈkwɛstʃən / (AmE), / rɪˈtɒrɪkəl ˈkwɛstʃən / (BrE)
A question asked to make a point rather than to elicit an answer.
question not needing an answer

Examples

The politician used a rhetorical question to emphasize his point and did not expect anyone to reply.
Why bother asking a rhetorical question if everyone knows the answer already?
She often uses rhetorical questions during presentations to engage the audience.

Synset Examples

he liked to make his points with rhetorical questions

Etymology

From Greek rhētorikos (oratorical) + Latin quaestio (question).

2. NOUN

/ rəˈtɔrɪkəl ˈkwɛstʃən / (AmE), / rɪˈtɒrɪkəl ˈkwɛs.tʃən / (BrE)
a question that is asked to make a point or produce an effect rather than to elicit an answer
question not meant to be answered

Examples

She posed a rhetorical question to challenge the committee's assumptions.
Often, a speaker will end with a rhetorical question to leave the audience thinking.
His use of a rhetorical question made the argument especially persuasive.

Synset Examples

Isn't it a rhetorical question if no answer is expected?
When she asked a rhetorical question, the room fell silent.
He used a rhetorical question to emphasize his point.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: hypophora - the technique of asking and then immediately answering a question in speech or writing

Etymology

From rhetorical, from Latin rhetoricus (pertaining to speech or writing), and question.
