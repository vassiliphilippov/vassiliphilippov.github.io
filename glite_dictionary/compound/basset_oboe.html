basset oboe

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæsət oʊˈboʊ / (AmE), / ˈbæsɪt əʊˈbəʊ / (BrE)
an oboe pitched an octave below the standard oboe, producing a lower, mellower sound
low-pitched oboe variety

Examples

The orchestra featured a basset oboe to add depth to the woodwind section.
He specialized in playing the basset oboe for Baroque music performances.
A 19th-century composer wrote several pieces for the rare basset oboe.

Etymology

From Italian 'bassetto' (diminutive of basso, meaning 'low') and 'oboe.'
