transorbital lobotomy

1. NOUN

/ trænzˌɔr.bɪ.tl̩ loʊˈbɑ.tə.mi / (AmE), / trænzˌɔː.bɪ.tl̩ ləˈbɒ.tə.mi / (BrE)
a method of prefrontal lobotomy in which a surgical instrument is inserted through the eye socket to sever brain fibers
lobotomy done through the eye socket
medical

Examples

The psychiatrist advocated for the now-discredited transorbital lobotomy procedure in the 1940s.
Rosemary Kennedy underwent a transorbital lobotomy that had a profound impact on her life.
Medical documentaries often discuss the controversial history of the transorbital lobotomy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lobotomy - surgical procedure involving cutting of brain fibers to alter mental functions

Etymology

From Latin 'trans-' (across) + 'orbital' (pertaining to the eye socket) and 'lobotomy'.
