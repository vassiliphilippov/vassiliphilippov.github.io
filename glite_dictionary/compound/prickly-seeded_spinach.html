prickly-seeded spinach

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɪkliˌsidɪd ˈspɪnɪtʃ / (AmE), / ˈprɪkliˌsiːdɪd ˈspɪnɪtʃ / (BrE)
A southwestern Asian plant widely cultivated for its succulent, edible dark green leaves.
southwestern Asian leafy vegetable

Examples

The farmer grew prickly-seeded spinach in his vegetable garden for a fresh supply of greens.
Cooks often prefer prickly-seeded spinach because of its robust flavor and tender leaves.
The local market had a fresh batch of prickly-seeded spinach harvested that morning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spinach - an edible plant with dark green leaves, commonly used as a vegetable
