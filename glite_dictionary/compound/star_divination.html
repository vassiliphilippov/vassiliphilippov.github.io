star divination

1. NOUN

/ stɑr dɪˌvɪn.eɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / stɑː dɪˌvɪn.eɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the practice of attempting to foretell the future by interpreting the positions of the planets, sun, and moon
interpreting the stars to predict the future

Examples

Many ancient cultures practiced star divination to guide their decisions and predict natural events.
He dismissed star divination as a pseudoscience but found its history fascinating.
Books on star divination were popular during the Renaissance among scholars and mystics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: astrology - the study of the movements and relative positions of celestial bodies interpreted as having an influence on human affairs
  • Antonym: astronomy - the scientific study of celestial objects, space, and the universe as a whole

Etymology

From Latin 'divinatio' (foretelling) and 'star,' referencing celestial bodies.
