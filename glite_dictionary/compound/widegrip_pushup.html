widegrip pushup

1. NOUN

/ ˈwaɪdˌɡrɪp ˈpʊʃˌʌp / (AmE), / ˈwaɪdˌɡrɪp ˈpʊʃʌp / (BrE)
A pushup exercise performed with the hands placed wider than shoulder-width apart, targeting the chest and shoulders.
pushup with hands set wide apart

Examples

She added widegrip pushups to her workout to strengthen her chest muscles.
If regular pushups are too easy, try doing widegrip pushups for a greater challenge.
It is important to maintain proper form during widegrip pushups to avoid shoulder strain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pushup - an exercise where a person raises and lowers their body using their arms while facing the floor
