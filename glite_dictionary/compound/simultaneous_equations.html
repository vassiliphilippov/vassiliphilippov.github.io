simultaneous equations

1. NOUN

/ ˌsaɪməlˈteɪniəs ɪˈkweɪʒənz / (AmE), / ˌsɪməlˈteɪnɪəs ɪˈkweɪʒənz / (BrE)
a set of equations in two or more variables, in which a solution consists of values satisfying all equations at the same time
equations solved together for common values

Examples

The math teacher explained how to solve simultaneous equations using substitution.
Engineers often use simultaneous equations to model complex systems with multiple variables.
You can check your answers by substituting them into both simultaneous equations.

Etymology

From Latin simultaneus (at the same time) + equation.
