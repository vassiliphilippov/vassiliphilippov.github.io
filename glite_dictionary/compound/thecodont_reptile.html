thecodont reptile

1. NOUN

/ ˈθiːkoʊˌdɑnt ˈrɛptaɪl / (AmE), / ˈθiːkədɒnt ˈrɛptaɪl / (BrE)
an extinct group of early archosaurian reptiles, believed to be ancestors of dinosaurs, crocodiles, and birds
extinct archosaurian reptile group

Examples

Scientists believe the thecodont reptile gave rise to both dinosaurs and modern crocodiles.
A fossil of a thecodont reptile was discovered in the Triassic rocks of South Africa.
The thecodont reptile possessed socketed teeth, a feature shared with its descendants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: archosaur - reptile group including dinosaurs, crocodiles, and birds
  • Degree Higher: dinosaur - extinct group of reptiles descended from thecodonts
  • Degree Higher: crocodile - living archosaur descended from thecodonts

Etymology

From Greek thekē (case, sheath) + odōn (tooth), referring to teeth set in sockets.
