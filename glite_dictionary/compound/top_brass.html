top brass

1. NOUN

/ tɑp bræs / (AmE), / tɒp brɑːs / (BrE)
the most important or highest-ranking people in an organization, especially in the military or a large company
senior leaders in an organization
informal

Examples

The top brass at the company gathered to discuss the merger.
Only the top brass have access to the confidential information.
She was promoted and soon found herself meeting with the top brass regularly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leadership - the leaders of an organization or group
  • Antonym: underling - a person of lower rank or status than others

2. NOUN

/ tɑp bræs / (AmE), / tɒp brɑːs / (BrE)
the people with the highest rank or authority in an organization, especially in the military or business
high-ranking officials or leaders

Examples

The top brass at the company met to discuss strategic plans for the next year.
Only the top brass were informed about the merger ahead of time.
The event was attended by military top brass from several countries.

Synset Examples

The top brass will review the project next week.
The top brass attended the annual conference.
The top brass decided on the new policy changes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: higher-ups - persons in authority or with higher rank
  • Antonym: lower ranks - persons of lower position or authority in an organization

Etymology

Originally military slang, referring to the ornate brass decorations on the uniforms of senior officers.
