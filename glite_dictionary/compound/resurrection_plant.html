resurrection plant

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɛzəˈrɛkʃən plænt / (AmE), / ˌrɛzəˈrɛkʃən plɑːnt / (BrE)
a small desert plant that appears dead when dry but revives and expands when moistened, especially those found in Asia
desert plant that revives when watered

Examples

The resurrection plant survived months without water and revived instantly after a rainstorm.
Desert travelers are often amazed by the resurrection plant's ability to rehydrate and turn green.
Researchers are studying how the resurrection plant can endure extreme drought conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Selaginella - genus of spikemoss often called resurrection plants
  • Synonym: rose of Jericho - another desert plant known for revival after drying

Etymology

From the plant's property of 'resurrecting' (appearing to come back to life) after hydration.

2. NOUN

/ ˌrɛzəˈrɛkʃən plænt / (AmE), / ˌrɛzəˈrɛkʃən plɑːnt / (BrE)
a plant, especially a desert fern ally, that curls into a ball when dry and unfolds and resumes growth when rehydrated
plant that revives when watered after drying out

Examples

The resurrection plant can survive years of drought by curling into a ball and waiting for rain.
After watering the dried resurrection plant, it unfurled its leaves and turned green again.
Botanists are fascinated by the way the resurrection plant appears dead but revives with moisture.

Etymology

From 'resurrection', referring to the plant's apparent return to life after desiccation.
