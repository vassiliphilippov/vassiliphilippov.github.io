gilgai soil

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɪlɡaɪ ˈsɔɪl / (AmE), / ˈɡɪlɡʌɪ ˈsɔɪl / (BrE)
Soil characterized by uneven, hummocky ground found in the melon holes of Australia, often forming in clay-rich areas subject to swelling and shrinking.
Australian soil in hummocky, melon-hole terrain

Examples

Scientists studied the unique structure of gilgai soil in the outback region.
The crops struggled to grow in the alternating mounds and depressions created by gilgai soil.
Farmers in Australia often encounter gilgai soil when working on clay-rich plains.

Etymology

From Australian Aboriginal languages (e.g., Kamilaroi) 'gilgai' meaning 'small waterhole'.
