potassium bromide

1. NOUN

/ poʊˈtæsiəm ˈbroʊmaɪd / (AmE), / pəˈtasiəm ˈbrəʊmaɪd / (BrE)
a white crystalline compound (KBr) used as a sedative and in photography, especially in the past
white crystalline salt, used as sedative and in photography
medical

Examples

Doctors once prescribed potassium bromide as a sedative to treat anxiety and epilepsy.
Potassium bromide was widely used in 19th-century photography to aid the development process.
Handling potassium bromide requires care due to potential health risks if ingested in large amounts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bromide - a compound containing bromine and another element

Etymology

Formed from 'potassium', from French 'potasse', and 'bromide', from Greek 'brōmos' (stench), referring to bromine.
