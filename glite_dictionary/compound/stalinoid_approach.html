Stalinoid approach

1. NOUN

/ ˈstæ.lɪ.nɔɪd əˈproʊtʃ / (AmE), / ˈstæ.lɪ.nɔɪd əˈprəʊtʃ / (BrE)
A style of leadership or management that employs authoritarian tactics and policies reminiscent of those used by Joseph Stalin.
authoritarian leadership style like Stalin's

Examples

Critics accused the new manager of adopting a Stalinoid approach in running the company.
Many employees were uncomfortable with the Stalinoid approach the director employed to enforce discipline.
The Stalinoid approach to leadership leaves little room for creative input or dissent.

Synset Examples

Critics accused him of adopting a Stalinoid approach to leadership.
The director's Stalinoid tactics surprised the entire team.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: authoritarian - favoring strict obedience at the expense of personal freedom
  • Antonym: democratic approach - leadership style based on equality and participation

Etymology

Formed from 'Stalinoid' (from Stalin, Soviet leader, + -oid meaning 'resembling') and 'approach'.
