mud puppy

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌd ˌpʌpi / (AmE), / ˈmʌd ˌpʌpi / (BrE)
a large North American aquatic salamander living in rivers and streams, noted for its bushy external gills and muddy habitat
a large North American aquatic salamander

Examples

The biologist spotted a mud puppy under a submerged log in the stream.
A mud puppy can breathe underwater thanks to its bright red gills.
Children were fascinated by the slimy mud puppy they found during their field trip.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: waterdog - another name for the mud puppy, a large aquatic salamander

2. NOUN

/ ˈmʌd ˌpʌpi / (AmE), / ˈmʌd ˌpʌpi / (BrE)
A large, permanently aquatic salamander, usually found in North America, that retains its larval features throughout its life.
aquatic salamander with larval features

Examples

The river is home to the rare mud puppy, a salamander that never loses its gills.
During the biology field trip, we observed a mud puppy hiding under a submerged log.
Conservationists are working to protect mud puppies from habitat loss in the Midwest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: axolotl - a related species of salamander also retaining larval features

3. NOUN

/ ˈmʌd ˌpʌpi / (AmE), / ˈmʌd ˌpʌpi / (BrE)
an aquatic North American salamander (Necturus maculosus) that has red feathery external gills and lives in mud at the bottom of lakes and streams.
aquatic salamander with external gills

Examples

The researcher collected several mud puppies from the riverbed for her study.
Children were fascinated by the mud puppy with its bright red gills.
Fishing enthusiasts sometimes spot mud puppies near the muddy banks of lakes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: salamander - a type of amphibian with a lizard-like appearance
