case-to-infection proportion

1. NOUN

/ ˈkeɪs tu ɪnˈfɛkʃən prəˈpɔrʃən / (AmE), / ˈkeɪs tə ɪnˈfɛkʃən prəˈpɔːʃən / (BrE)
The ratio of diagnosed cases of a disease to the total number of infections with the causal agent, used in epidemiology to estimate disease severity.
ratio of cases to infections in epidemiology
medical

Examples

The case-to-infection proportion helps public health officials assess the severity of an epidemic.
A low case-to-infection proportion suggests that many infections are asymptomatic or unreported.
Researchers compared the case-to-infection proportion between different age groups during the outbreak.

Etymology

Formed from 'case,' 'infection,' and 'proportion,' combining standard scientific terms.
