post and lintel

1. NOUN

/ poʊst ənd ˈlɪntəl / (AmE), / pəʊst ənd ˈlɪntl̩ / (BrE)
A structure consisting of vertical supports (posts) holding up a horizontal member (lintel), commonly used in architecture and construction.
structure with vertical posts and a horizontal lintel

Examples

Many ancient temples were built using the post and lintel method, which provided stability and strength.
The doorway is an example of a simple post and lintel construction, with two columns supporting a beam.
Stonehenge is a famous monument that employs the post and lintel principle in its design.

Etymology

From 'post' (vertical support) and 'lintel' (horizontal beam), both with roots in Old French and Latin.
