zebra mussel

1. NOUN

/ ˈziː.brə ˈmʌs.əl / (AmE), / ˈziː.brə ˈmʌs.əl / (BrE)
a small invasive freshwater mussel native to Eurasia, characterized by striped shells, often causing environmental and economic damage by clogging pipes and displacing native species
invasive freshwater mussel with striped shell

Examples

The spread of zebra mussels has significantly disrupted local aquatic ecosystems.
Engineers are developing new methods to prevent zebra mussels from clogging water intake pipes.
Researchers are studying the impact of zebra mussel infestations on native mussel species.
