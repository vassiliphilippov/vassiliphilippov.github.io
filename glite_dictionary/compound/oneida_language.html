Oneida language

1. NOUN

/ oʊˈnaɪdə ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / əʊˈnaɪdə ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Iroquoian language spoken by the Oneida people
language of the Oneida people

Examples

The Oneida language is still taught to children in some communities today.
She is fluent in both English and the Oneida language.
Efforts are underway to preserve the Oneida language for future generations.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Iroquoian - family of North American indigenous languages including Oneida

Etymology

From the name Oneida, a Native American people, from their autonym meaning 'people of the standing stone'.
