Japanese iris

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˈpæniz ˈaɪrɪs / (AmE), / dʒæˈpæn.iːz ˈaɪ.rɪs / (BrE)
A species of iris native to Japan, known for its large, striking flowers.
species of iris with large, showy flowers native to Japan

Examples

In the spring, the Japanese iris blooms with beautiful purple petals in our garden.
She planted a row of Japanese irises along the pond to create a colorful border.
The delicate fragrance of the Japanese iris attracts many visitors to the botanical garden each year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: iris - a plant of the genus Iris with sword-shaped leaves and showy flowers
