crustose thallus

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrʌs.toʊs ˈθæl.əs / (AmE), / ˈkrʌs.təʊs ˈθæl.əs / (BrE)
A thin, crust-like lichen body that adheres closely to or is embedded in the surface on which it grows.
lichen body forming a thin crust
medical

Examples

The crustose thallus was barely distinguishable from the rough rock surface.
Scientists identified the lichen by its characteristic crustose thallus closely attached to the bark.
Unlike leafy lichens, a crustose thallus cannot be easily separated from its substrate.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: foliose thallus - leaf-like lichen thallus with a looser attachment to the substrate
  • Degree Higher: fruticose thallus - lichen thallus with a bushy or branching structure

Etymology

From Latin 'crustosus' (crusty) and Greek 'thallos' (green shoot, twig, or sprout, meaning lichen body).
