hypertonic solution

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪ.pɚˈtɑ.nɪk səˈluː.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌhaɪ.pəˈtɒ.nɪk səˈluː.ʃən / (BrE)
a solution with a higher concentration of solutes than that inside a cell, causing water to move out of the cell by osmosis
solution with higher solute concentration (than cells)
medical

Examples

When animal cells are placed in a hypertonic solution, they shrink due to water loss.
Doctors use a hypertonic solution to reduce swelling in some medical treatments.
If you immerse red blood cells in a hypertonic solution, they will become shriveled.

Synset Examples

A hypertonic solution has higher osmotic pressure than another solution.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hypotonic solution - a solution with a lower solute concentration than cells
  • Antonym: isotonic solution - a solution with the same solute concentration as cells

Etymology

From Greek 'hyper-' (over, above) and 'tonos' (tone, tension), relating to osmotic pressure.
