net profit

1. NOUN

/ nɛt ˈprɑfɪt / (AmE), / nɛt ˈprɒfɪt / (BrE)
the total profit of a business after all expenses, including taxes and depreciation, have been deducted from total revenue
final profit after all deductions

Examples

The company's net profit increased significantly compared to last year.
After accounting for all expenses, the net profit was reported at $2 million.
Rising costs have caused their net profit margin to shrink this quarter.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: gross profit - the profit a company makes after deducting the costs associated with making and selling its products, before other expenses
  • Antonym: net loss - when total expenses exceed total revenues over a period

Etymology

From 'net' (meaning remaining after all deductions) + 'profit' (from Latin 'profectus', advancement).

2. NOUN

/ nɛt ˈprɑfɪt / (AmE), / nɛt ˈprɒfɪt / (BrE)
the actual profit remaining after all expenses, taxes, and deductions have been subtracted from total revenue
actual profit after all deductions

Examples

After calculating all expenses and taxes, the company's net profit was significantly higher than last year.
Investors care most about net profit because it reflects a company's true financial performance.
She focused on reducing costs to maximize net profit for her small business.

Synset Examples

She worked hard to find the net profit of the company.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: gross profit - profit before deducting expenses such as taxes or interest

Etymology

From 'net' (meaning 'remaining after deductions', from Old French 'net', Latin 'nitidus') + 'profit' (from Latin 'proficiō': to advance, benefit).
