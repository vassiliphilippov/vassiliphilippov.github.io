Bologna sausage

1. NOUN

/ bəˈloʊni ˈsɔsɪdʒ / (AmE), / bəˈləʊni ˈsɒsɪdʒ / (BrE)
a large, smooth-textured smoked sausage made from beef, veal, and pork, often sliced and eaten cold
large smoked sausage of mixed meats

Examples

She made a sandwich with Bologna sausage and cheese for lunch.
The deli sells several varieties of Bologna sausage, both beef and pork.
Kids often enjoy eating slices of Bologna sausage as a snack.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mortadella - Italian sausage similar to bologna but typically contains visible pieces of fat
  • Degree Higher: salami - a type of cured sausage consisting of fermented and air-dried meat
  • Degree Higher: sausage - a food made from ground meat stuffed into a casing

Etymology

Named after Bologna, a city in Italy, where a similar sausage originated.
