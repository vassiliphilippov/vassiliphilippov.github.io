suborder Nothosauria

1. NOUN

/ ˌsʌbˌɔrdər noʊˌθoʊˈsɔriə / (AmE), / ˌsʌbˌɔːdə nəʊˌθəʊˈsɔːrɪə / (BrE)
a suborder of extinct aquatic reptiles (Sauropterygia) that lived during the Triassic period.
extinct suborder of aquatic reptiles

Examples

The fossils of suborder Nothosauria have provided valuable insights into the evolution of aquatic reptiles.
Scientists believe that members of suborder Nothosauria adapted to a semi-aquatic lifestyle.
Research on suborder Nothosauria helps paleontologists understand ancient marine ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Sauropterygia - an order of extinct aquatic reptiles
  • Degree Lower: Nothosaurus - a genus within the suborder Nothosauria

Etymology

From New Latin Nothosauria, based on Greek 'nothos' (false) and 'sauros' (lizard).
