Turner's syndrome

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɝːnɚz ˌsɪnˌdroʊm / (AmE), / ˈtɜːnəz ˌsɪn.drəʊm / (BrE)
A chromosomal disorder in females characterized by having only one X chromosome, resulting in short stature, heart defects, and underdeveloped sexual organs.
chromosomal disorder affecting females
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that her symptoms were consistent with Turner's syndrome.
Girls with Turner's syndrome may require hormone therapy during adolescence.
Early diagnosis and treatment can help individuals with Turner's syndrome lead healthy lives.

Etymology

Named after American endocrinologist Dr. Henry Turner, who described the condition in 1938.
