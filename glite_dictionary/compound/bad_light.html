bad light

1. NOUN

/ bæd laɪt / (AmE), / bad laɪt / (BrE)
poor or insufficient lighting, making it difficult to see clearly or continue activities such as sports or work
poor or inadequate lighting conditions

Examples

The cricket match was postponed due to bad light late in the afternoon.
Painting in bad light can lead to mistakes in color choices and details.
It's dangerous to read or sew in bad light for extended periods.

Synset Examples

It’s hard to see in this bad light.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: good light - adequate or optimal lighting conditions
