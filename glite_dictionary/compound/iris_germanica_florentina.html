Iris germanica florentina

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪrɪs dʒərˈmænɪkə flɔːˈrɛntɪnə / (AmE), / ˈaɪərɪs dʒɜːˈmænɪkə flɒˈrɛntɪnə / (BrE)
A type of German iris with large white flowers tinged with lavender and a fragrant rhizome, often cultivated for its ornamental and aromatic qualities.
variety of German iris with white-lavender flowers

Examples

The Iris germanica florentina is prized for its elegant white blossoms and sweet fragrance.
Many perfumes are made using the rhizomes of the Iris germanica florentina plant.
Gardeners often plant Iris germanica florentina to add both beauty and scent to their landscapes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: German iris - A species of iris with showy flowers, commonly cultivated

Etymology

Latin; named for Florence (Florentia), Italy, the region of cultivation.
