at last

1. ADV

/ ət læst / (AmE), / ət lɑːst / (BrE)
after a long period of time or delay; finally; in the end.
finally, after a long time

Examples

At last, the train arrived after a long delay.
He finished the marathon at last, feeling both exhausted and triumphant.
At last, she found the book she had been searching for all week.

Synset Examples

ultimately he had to give in
at long last the winter was over

Related Terms

  • Synonym: finally - after a long delay or at the end
  • Synonym: eventually - in the end, especially after a long delay
  • Antonym: immediately - without delay; at once

2. ADV

/ ət læst / (AmE), / ət lɑːst / (BrE)
after a long time; finally
finally, after a long wait

Examples

At last, the rain stopped and the sun broke through the clouds.
She finished her novel at last, after years of hard work.
You have come home at last, and your family is delighted to see you.

Synset Examples

At last, the project has been completed successfully.
At last, she received the recognition she deserved.
He found the missing keys at last after searching everywhere.
She found her missing keys at long last.
The wait was over, at long last!
At long last, the project was finally completed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: finally - after a long time, at the end
  • Synonym: eventually - in the end, especially after a delay
