cape yellowwood

1. NOUN

/ keɪp ˈjɛloʊˌwʊd / (AmE), / keɪp ˈjɛləʊˌwʊd / (BrE)
a South African tree or shrub with a rounded crown, valued for its timber and ornamental use.
South African tree or shrub

Examples

The cape yellowwood is a protected tree species in South Africa, making it illegal to cut down without permission.
Botanists admire the smooth bark and evergreen leaves of the cape yellowwood.
The furniture was crafted from cape yellowwood, known for its fine grain and durability.

Etymology

From 'Cape' (referring to the Cape region of South Africa) + 'yellowwood', indicating the yellowish color of the wood.
