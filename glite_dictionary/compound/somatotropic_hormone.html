somatotropic hormone

1. NOUN

/ ˌsoʊmətoʊˈtroʊpɪk ˈhɔːrmoʊn / (AmE), / ˌsəʊmətəˈtrəʊpɪk ˈhɔːməʊn / (BrE)
a hormone produced by the anterior pituitary gland that stimulates growth in humans and animals
hormone promoting growth
medical

Examples

A deficiency of somatotropic hormone during childhood can result in stunted growth.
Doctors may prescribe somatotropic hormone to treat growth disorders in children.
Recent research explores the role of somatotropic hormone in adult metabolism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: growth hormone - another term for somatotropic hormone; hormone that stimulates growth

Etymology

From Greek 'soma' (body) + 'tropē' (change, turning), referring to its role in body growth.
