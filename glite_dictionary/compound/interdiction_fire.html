interdiction fire

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪntərˈdɪkʃən ˌfaɪɚ / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈdɪkʃən ˌfaɪə / (BrE)
military fire directed at an area to prevent the enemy from using or passing through it
military fire to block enemy use of an area

Examples

The artillery unit provided interdiction fire to deny the enemy access to the bridge.
Commanders ordered interdiction fire along the road to prevent reinforcements from arriving.
Effective interdiction fire halted the enemy's advance through the valley.

Etymology

From 'interdiction' (Late Latin interdictio, meaning prohibition) and 'fire' (as in gunfire).
