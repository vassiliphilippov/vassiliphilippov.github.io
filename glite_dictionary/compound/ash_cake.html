ash cake

1. NOUN

/ æʃ keɪk / (AmE), / æʃ keɪk / (BrE)
A type of cornbread, traditionally wrapped in leaves (such as cabbage leaves) and baked in hot ashes, especially in the southern United States.
cornbread baked in ashes, southern US
american

Examples

She prepared an ash cake by wrapping cornmeal dough in cabbage leaves and placing it in the coals.
During the camping trip, they made fresh ash cakes over the open fire.
In some southern households, ash cake is still baked using traditional methods for special occasions.
