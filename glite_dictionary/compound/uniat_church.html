Uniat Church

1. NOUN

/ ˈjuːniˌæt tʃɜrtʃ / (AmE), / ˈjuːnɪˌat tʃɜːtʃ / (BrE)
Any of several churches, primarily in Eastern Europe or the Middle East, that recognize the authority of the Pope while retaining their own Eastern liturgy and traditions.
Eastern churches accepting papal authority

Examples

The Uniat Church in Ukraine follows Eastern rites but is loyal to the Pope.
During the nineteenth century, the Uniat Church faced significant pressure from both Orthodox and Catholic authorities.
The existence of the Uniat Church reflects complex religious and political histories in Eastern Europe.

Etymology

From French uniates, from Medieval Latin unitas (unity), referring to union with the Roman Catholic Church.
