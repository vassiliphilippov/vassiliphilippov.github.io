cavernous sinus

1. NOUN

/ ˈkævərnəs ˈsaɪnəs / (AmE), / ˈkævənəs ˈsaɪnəs / (BrE)
either of a pair of large venous channels located within the cranial cavity, on either side of the pituitary gland, that drain blood from the brain and region of the face.
large venous channel in the cranial cavity
medical

Examples

The surgeon carefully avoided damaging the cavernous sinus during the procedure.
An infection in the face can rarely spread to the cavernous sinus and cause serious complications.
The cavernous sinus contains several important nerves and the internal carotid artery.

Etymology

From Latin 'caverna' (cavity, cave), relating to its hollow appearance, and 'sinus' (curve, cavity).
