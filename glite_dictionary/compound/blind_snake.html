blind snake

1. NOUN

/ blaɪnd sneɪk / (AmE), / blaɪnd sneɪk / (BrE)
A small, wormlike, burrowing snake found in warm regions, having vestigial eyes and typically leading a subterranean lifestyle.
small burrowing snake with vestigial eyes

Examples

The blind snake spends most of its life underground, feeding on ants and termites.
Despite its name, the blind snake can sense light and dark through its tiny eyes.
We found a small blind snake beneath a rock while digging in the garden.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: worm snake - a similar small, slender burrowing snake often confused with blind snakes
