order Amoebida

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdɚ əˈmiːbɪdə / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə əˈmiːbɪdə / (BrE)
a taxonomic order of protozoans that includes various species of amoebas
animal order including amoebas

Examples

Scientists have classified many protozoans within the order Amoebida due to their similar characteristics.
Members of the order Amoebida are known for their unique movement using pseudopodia.
The research focused on the genetic diversity within the order Amoebida.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amoeba - a single-celled organism characterized by its ability to change shape

Etymology

From New Latin, using 'order' (a taxonomic rank) and 'Amoebida', from 'Amoeba' (genus name based on Greek amoibe, meaning 'change').
