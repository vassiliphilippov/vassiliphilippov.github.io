Coptic language

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑptɪk ˌlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈkɒptɪk ˌlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the liturgical language of the Coptic Church, used in Egypt and Ethiopia, and written in the Greek alphabet
liturgical language of the Coptic Church

Examples

The Coptic language is still used during religious ceremonies in some Egyptian churches.
Linguists study the Coptic language to better understand the history of Christianity in Egypt.
She is taking a course on the Coptic language and its ancient scripts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Coptic - relating to the Copts or their language

Etymology

From Greek 'Koptikos' (Coptic) + 'language'.
