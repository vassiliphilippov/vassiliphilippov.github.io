snaffle bit

1. NOUN

/ ˈsnæfəl bɪt / (AmE), / ˈsnæf.əl bɪt / (BrE)
A simple jointed bit for a horse, lacking a curb, used to control the animal through pressure on the mouth.
simple horse bit without curb

Examples

The rider preferred using a snaffle bit for her young horse during training sessions.
Many beginners start learning to ride with a snaffle bit because it is gentler on the horse.
He replaced the curb with a snaffle bit to give his horse more comfort.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: curb bit - a type of horse bit with a lever (curb), as opposed to a snaffle bit

Etymology

From 'snaffle', of uncertain origin, possibly from Dutch 'snavel' (beak, snout), and 'bit'.
