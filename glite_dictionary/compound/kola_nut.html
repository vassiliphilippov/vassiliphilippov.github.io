kola nut

1. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊlə nʌt / (AmE), / ˈkəʊlə nʌt / (BrE)
A tropical African tree bearing large brown nuts that contain caffeine and are used as a source of cola extract.
tree with caffeine-containing nuts; source of cola extract

Examples

The extract from the kola nut is commonly used to flavor soft drinks.
In many West African cultures, the kola nut is offered as a symbol of hospitality.
The caffeine in the kola nut provides a mild stimulating effect.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cola nut - Alternative spelling for the caffeinated seed of the kola tree

Etymology

From West African languages (e.g., Yoruba 'obì').

2. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊlə nʌt / (AmE), / ˈkəʊlə nʌt / (BrE)
the brown, bitter seed of a tropical West African tree, containing caffeine and traditionally used as a stimulant and in the flavoring of cola drinks
caffeinated seed used for cola flavoring

Examples

In some West African cultures, sharing a kola nut is a sign of hospitality.
The company uses natural kola nuts in its beverage recipe instead of synthetic flavors.
Chewing a kola nut can help to combat fatigue due to its caffeine content.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cola - sweet carbonated drink flavored with kola nut extract or artificial substitute

Etymology

From West African languages, Hausa 'kola', Yoruba 'àkọ́lá'.
