Palm Sunday

1. PROPN

/ pɑm ˈsʌn.deɪ / (AmE), / pɑːm ˈsʌn.deɪ / (BrE)
the Sunday before Easter, commemorating Jesus' triumphal entry into Jerusalem, observed by Christians worldwide
Sunday before Easter in Christianity

Examples

Many churches distribute palm leaves to worshippers on Palm Sunday as a symbol of peace and victory.
This year, Palm Sunday falls on March 24th, marking the beginning of Holy Week.
On Palm Sunday, Christian communities around the world reenact Jesus' entry into Jerusalem.

Etymology

From the use of palm branches during Jesus' entry into Jerusalem, as described in the New Testament.
