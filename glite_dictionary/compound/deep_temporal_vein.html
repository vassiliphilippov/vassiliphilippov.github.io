deep temporal vein

1. NOUN

/ dip ˈtɛmpəɹəl veɪn / (AmE), / diːp ˈtɛmpərəl veɪn / (BrE)
either of several veins in the temple region of the head that drain into the pterygoid plexus.
vein in the temple area of the head
medical

Examples

The deep temporal vein helps drain blood from the muscles of the temple.
During the surgery, the deep temporal vein was carefully preserved to avoid excessive bleeding.
An infection in the temple might sometimes affect the deep temporal vein.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: superficial temporal vein - vein draining the scalp above the ear
