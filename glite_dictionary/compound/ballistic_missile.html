ballistic missile

1. NOUN

/ bəˈlɪstɪk ˈmɪsaɪl / (AmE), / bəˈlɪstɪk ˈmɪsaɪl / (BrE)
a missile that is powered and guided during the initial phase of flight, but follows a free-falling ballistic trajectory when approaching its target
missile with initial guidance, free-falling later

Examples

The country tested a new ballistic missile capable of reaching distant targets.
A ballistic missile travels outside the Earth's atmosphere during its flight.
Concerns about the proliferation of ballistic missiles have increased in recent years.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cruise missile - missile that remains guided throughout its flight

Etymology

From 'ballistic', relating to projectiles or their flight, and 'missile', from Latin missilis (that may be thrown).
