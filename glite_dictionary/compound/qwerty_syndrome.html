qwerty syndrome

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɜrti ˈsɪnˌdroʊm / (AmE), / ˈkwɜːti ˈsɪndrəʊm / (BrE)
the tendency to make frequent typing errors or habitual mistakes due to familiarity with the QWERTY keyboard layout
habit of typing errors from QWERTY keyboard use

Examples

His qwerty syndrome caused him to repeatedly misspell common words at work.
She blamed her frequent typos on qwerty syndrome after switching keyboards.
Many new typists suffer from qwerty syndrome until they become familiar with the layout.

Synset Examples

His qwerty syndrome led to many typos in his report.
His qwerty syndrome made typing errors common.
She struggled with qwerty syndrome during the exam.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: typing error - a mistake made while typing

Etymology

QWERTY from the first six letters on a standard keyboard; syndrome from Greek 'syndrome', meaning a set of symptoms.
