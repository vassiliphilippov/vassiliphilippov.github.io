dish aerial

1. NOUN

/ dɪʃ ˈɛriəl / (AmE), / dɪʃ ˈeəriəl / (BrE)
a directional antenna consisting of a parabolic reflector used to receive or transmit microwave or radio frequency signals
parabolic radio/microwave antenna

Examples

The technician installed a dish aerial on the roof to improve the television signal.
Weather can sometimes affect the performance of a dish aerial.
Many homes use a dish aerial to access satellite broadcasts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parabolic antenna - antenna with a parabolic reflector for focusing signals
  • Antonym: dipole antenna - simple antenna consisting of two conductive elements
