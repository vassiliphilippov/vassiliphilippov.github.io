commercial credit

1. NOUN

/ kəˈmɝː.ʃəl ˈkrɛd.ɪt / (AmE), / kəˈmɜː.ʃəl ˈkrɛd.ɪt / (BrE)
credit extended by banks or financial institutions to businesses for commercial purposes, such as purchasing inventory or financing operations.
credit extended to businesses by banks

Examples

The company used commercial credit to finance its purchase of new equipment.
Many small businesses rely on commercial credit to manage cash flow during slow seasons.
Banks have tightened their requirements for granting commercial credit after the recent economic downturn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: business loan - loan for business activities
