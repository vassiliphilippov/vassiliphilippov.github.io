Hook of Holland

1. PROPN

/ hʊk əv ˈhɑːlənd / (AmE), / hʊk əv ˈhɒlənd / (BrE)
a cape and coastal town on the southwestern coast of the Netherlands, near Rotterdam, known as a ferry terminus and port
cape and town in the Netherlands

Examples

The ferry to Harwich departs from the Hook of Holland every evening.
Many tourists spend a few days exploring Hook of Holland before continuing their journey into Rotterdam.
The Hook of Holland is famous for its wide sandy beaches and seaside promenade.

Etymology

From Dutch 'Hoek van Holland', meaning 'Corner of Holland.'
