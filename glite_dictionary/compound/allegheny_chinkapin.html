Allegheny chinkapin

1. NOUN

/ ˌæl.əˈɡeɪ.ni ˈtʃɪŋ.kə.pɪn / (AmE), / ˌæl.ɪˈɡeɪ.ni ˈtʃɪŋ.kə.pɪn / (BrE)
A shrubby chestnut tree (Castanea pumila) native to the southeastern United States, bearing small edible nuts.
chestnut shrub with edible nuts (U.S.)

Examples

The Allegheny chinkapin produces tasty nuts that are enjoyed by both wildlife and humans.
Hikers stumbled upon a thicket of Allegheny chinkapins growing near the forest edge.
The small nuts of the Allegheny chinkapin ripen in late summer and are gathered by locals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chinquapin - common name for several small chestnut trees or shrubs with edible nuts

Etymology

From 'Allegheny' (a region in the eastern U.S.) and 'chinquapin,' from Virginia Algonquian *chechinquamin* (chestnut).
