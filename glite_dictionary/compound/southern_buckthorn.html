southern buckthorn

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌðərn ˈbʌkˌθɔrn / (AmE), / ˈsʌðən ˈbʌkˌθɔːn / (BrE)
A shrubby, thorny deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States, bearing white flowers and small black berry-like fruits.
thorny North American shrub with black fruits

Examples

The southern buckthorn thrives in moist woodlands across the southeastern United States.
Birds are attracted to the small black fruits of the southern buckthorn during late summer.
Gardeners appreciate the southern buckthorn for its hardiness and ornamental white flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: buckthorn - any of various shrubs or small trees of the genus Rhamnus
