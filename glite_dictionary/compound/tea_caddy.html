tea caddy

1. NOUN

/ ˈti ˌkædi / (AmE), / ˈtiː ˌkædi / (BrE)
a small container, usually with a lid, used for storing tea leaves or tea bags
container for storing tea

Examples

She kept her favorite green tea in a decorative tea caddy on the shelf.
The silver tea caddy was passed down through generations in her family.
He opened the tea caddy and the fragrant aroma filled the room.

Etymology

From Malay 'kati', a weight measure for tea, entered English in the 18th century.
