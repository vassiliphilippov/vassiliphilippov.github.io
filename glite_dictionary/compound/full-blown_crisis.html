full-blown crisis

1. NOUN

/ fʊl bloʊn ˈkraɪsɪs / (AmE), / fʊl bləʊn ˈkraɪsɪs / (BrE)
A crisis that has reached its most severe and fully developed stage.
fully developed severe crisis

Examples

The government is working to prevent the situation from becoming a full-blown crisis.
What started as a minor disagreement soon escalated into a full-blown crisis.
After weeks of tension, the company now faces a full-blown crisis threatening its future.

Synset Examples

The outbreak turned into a full-blown crisis quickly.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: crisis - a time of intense difficulty or danger
