dispersed phase

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈspɝst feɪz / (AmE), / dɪˈspɜːst feɪz / (BrE)
In a colloid, the substance that is distributed as fine particles within another substance (the continuous phase).
substance dispersed in a colloid
medical

Examples

In an emulsion, the oil droplets form the dispersed phase within the water.
The properties of the dispersed phase greatly affect the stability of a colloidal solution.
Milk is a colloid with fat globules as the dispersed phase.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: continuous phase - the substance in a colloid in which the dispersed phase is distributed

Etymology

From 'dispersed', past participle of 'disperse', and 'phase', both from Latin roots.
