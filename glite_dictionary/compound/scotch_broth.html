Scotch broth

1. NOUN

/ skɑtʃ brɔθ / (AmE), / skɒtʃ brɒθ / (BrE)
A thick Scottish soup made from beef or mutton, various vegetables, and pearl barley.
traditional Scottish barley and meat soup
british

Examples

On chilly evenings, my grandmother often served homemade Scotch broth to warm us up.
The menu featured traditional dishes such as haggis and Scotch broth.
Pearl barley is an essential ingredient in authentic Scotch broth.

Etymology

From 'Scotch,' relating to Scotland, and 'broth,' from Old English 'broth' (liquid food).
