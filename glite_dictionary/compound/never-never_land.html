never-never land

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɛvɚ ˈnɛvɚ lænd / (AmE), / ˈnɛvə ˈnɛvə lænd / (BrE)
an imaginary or ideal place, often one that is unattainable or exists only in dreams or fantasy
imaginary or ideal place in dreams

Examples

As a child, Sarah often dreamed of escaping to never-never land when life was difficult.
His ideas about a world without conflict belong in never-never land, not reality.
The book invites readers to travel to a magical never-never land full of adventure and wonder.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fantasyland - a place or state that is unreal or imagined
  • Synonym: utopia - an imagined perfect society or place

Etymology

From Never-Never Land, the mythical country in J.M. Barrie's 'Peter Pan' (1904).

2. NOUN

/ ˌnɛvər ˈnɛvər lænd / (AmE), / ˌnɛvə ˈnɛvə lænd / (BrE)
an imaginary or ideal place that is unattainable or exists only in dreams or fantasy
imaginary or unattainable place

Examples

Many people dream of escaping to a never-never land where all their problems disappear.
Politicians sometimes make promises that belong only in never-never land.
The novel's setting is a never-never land untouched by time or change.

Synset Examples

They promised a never-never land of dreams.
His promises felt like a never-never land.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: utopia - an imagined place of perfection
  • Synonym: paradise - a place or state of perfect happiness
  • Antonym: reality - the state of things as they actually exist

Etymology

From J. M. Barrie's 'Neverland' in Peter Pan (early 20th century), describing a fictitious island where children never grow up.
