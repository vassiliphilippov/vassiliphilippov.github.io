leprous ruin

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛpɹəs ˈruːɪn / (AmE), / ˈlɛpɹəs ˈruːɪn / (BrE)
A decaying or crumbling structure that resembles the diseased, corroded appearance caused by leprosy; often used in a literary or poetic context.
decaying structure resembling effects of leprosy

Examples

The explorer was fascinated by the leprous ruin at the edge of the ancient city.
Vines crawled over the leprous ruin, disguising its crumbling stones in a shroud of green.
In the mist, the leprous ruin appeared ghostly and forsaken, adding to the eerie atmosphere.

Synset Examples

The leprous ruins told stories of a forgotten era.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ruin - a destroyed or decayed building or place
  • Synonym: derelict - in a very poor condition from neglect and disuse

Etymology

From 'leprous' ('relating to leprosy') and 'ruin' (a destroyed structure); 'leprous' from Latin 'leprosus', 'leprosis'.
