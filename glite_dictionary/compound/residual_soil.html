residual soil

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈzɪdʒuəl sɔɪl / (AmE), / rɪˈzɪdjʊəl sɔɪl / (BrE)
Soil that remains in place after the soluble elements have been dissolved and removed, typically formed from the weathering of underlying rock.
soil left after soluble parts are dissolved

Examples

The hills in this region are covered mainly with residual soil formed by the weathering of granite.
Unlike residual soil, transported soil is carried by wind, water, or ice to a new location.
Engineers analyze the properties of residual soil before constructing buildings on such terrain.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: transported soil - soil that has been moved from its place of origin

Etymology

From 'residual', ultimately from Latin 'residuum' (that which remains), plus 'soil'.
