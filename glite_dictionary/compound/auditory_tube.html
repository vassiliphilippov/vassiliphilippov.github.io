auditory tube

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔdɪˌtɔri tuːb / (AmE), / ˈɔːdɪtəri tjuːb / (BrE)
either of the two tubes that connect the middle ear to the nasopharynx and equalize air pressure on both sides of the eardrum
tube linking middle ear and nasopharynx
medical

Examples

Airflow through the auditory tube helps to maintain equal pressure in the middle ear.
A blocked auditory tube can cause discomfort during airplane travel.
The doctor explained how the auditory tube works during a cold.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Eustachian tube - another term for auditory tube, connecting the middle ear with the nasopharynx

Etymology

From Latin 'audīre' (to hear) + Latin 'tuba' (tube, trumpet).
