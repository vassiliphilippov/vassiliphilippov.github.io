fractional currency

1. NOUN

/ ˈfrækʃənl ˈkɝːrənsi / (AmE), / ˈfrækʃənəl ˈkʌrənsi / (BrE)
paper money issued in denominations less than the basic monetary unit of a country, such as coins or small-value notes.
paper money of less than standard unit value

Examples

The government introduced fractional currency during the coin shortage in the 19th century.
Collectors often seek rare notes of fractional currency from the Civil War era.
Because coins were scarce, stores began accepting fractional currency as payment for goods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: small change - coins of low denomination; small amounts of money

Etymology

From 'fractional' (from Latin 'frāctiōn-', 'a breaking') and 'currency'.
