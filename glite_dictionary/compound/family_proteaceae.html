family Proteaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli proʊˌtiːˈeɪʃiː / (AmE), / ˈfæməli prəʊˌtiːˈeɪsiː / (BrE)
a large family of mainly Australian and South African shrubs and trees with leathery leaves and clustered, often four-part flowers, constituting the order Proteales.
family of shrubs and trees (Proteaceae)

Examples

Banksia is a well-known genus within the family Proteaceae, notable for its striking flower spikes.
Many members of the family Proteaceae are cultivated for their unique and attractive foliage.
The family Proteaceae includes several plants adapted to nutrient-poor soils in Australia and South Africa.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Proteaceae - the botanical family including proteas and related species

Etymology

From modern Latin Proteaceae, based on Protea (the type genus), named after the Greek god Proteus, for the genus's diversity.
