Vietnam War

1. NOUN

/ viˈɛtnæm wɔr / (AmE), / viˈɛtnæm wɔː / (BrE)
A prolonged conflict (1954–1975) between communist North Vietnam, supported by China, and South Vietnam, supported by the United States; a major Cold War military conflict.
military conflict in Vietnam (1954–1975)

Examples

The Vietnam War had a profound impact on American society and politics.
Many documentaries explore the causes and consequences of the Vietnam War.
His father served as a soldier during the Vietnam War.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Korean War - military conflict on the Korean Peninsula (1950–1953)
  • Synonym: World War II - the second global war (1939–1945) involving most of the world's nations
  • Synonym: Cold War - period of political tension and military rivalry after World War II between the Soviet Union and the United States
