whited sepulchre

1. NOUN

/ ˈwaɪtɪd ˈsɛpəlkɚ / (AmE), / ˈwaɪtɪd ˈsɛpəlkə / (BrE)
A person who appears virtuous on the outside but is inwardly corrupt or wicked.
hypocrite; outwardly virtuous but inwardly corrupt person

Examples

Everyone believed the mayor was honest, but he turned out to be a whited sepulchre.
She warned her friend not to trust that whited sepulchre who always pretended to be so pious.
History is full of leaders who proved to be whited sepulchres in the end.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hypocrite - a person who pretends to have virtues, beliefs, or principles they do not actually possess

Etymology

From the biblical phrase in Matthew 23:27, referencing sepulchres (tombs) that were whitewashed on the outside.
