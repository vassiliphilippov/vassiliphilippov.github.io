organic process

1. NOUN

/ ɔrˈɡænɪk ˈprɑsɛs / (AmE), / ɔːˈɡanɪk ˈprəʊsɛs / (BrE)
a process or series of actions that occur naturally within living organisms, such as respiration, growth, or digestion
process occurring in living organisms

Examples

Photosynthesis is an organic process that allows plants to convert sunlight into energy.
Digestion is a complex organic process involving numerous enzymes and tissues.
The scientist studied the organic process of cell division in yeast.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: biological process - a process occurring in living organisms, often used interchangeably

Etymology

From Latin 'organicus' (of organs, instrumental) and 'processus' (a going forward, advance).
