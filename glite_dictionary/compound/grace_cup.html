grace cup

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡreɪs ˌkʌp / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪs ˌkʌp / (BrE)
a cup passed around at the end of a meal for the final toast, often accompanied by a prayer or expression of thanks
cup for final communal toast at a meal

Examples

After the banquet, the host offered the grace cup to each guest in turn.
Historically, the grace cup was shared at the end of grand feasts to promote fellowship.
They filled the grace cup for the traditional final toast after dinner.

Etymology

From 'grace', referring to a prayer of thanks said before or after meals, combined with 'cup'.
