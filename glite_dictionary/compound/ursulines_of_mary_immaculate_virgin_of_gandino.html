Ursulines of Mary Immaculate Virgin of Gandino

1. PROPN

/ ɜrˈsuːlɪnz əv ˈmɛri ɪˈmækjələt ˈvɜrdʒɪn əv ɡænˈdiːnoʊ / (AmE), / ɜːˈsjuːlɪnz əv ˈmɛri ɪˈmækjʊlət ˈvɜːdʒɪn əv ɡænˈdiːnəʊ / (BrE)
a Roman Catholic religious order for women, founded in Gandino, Italy, in 1818, dedicated to education and charitable work
Catholic women's religious order founded in Italy

Examples

The Ursulines of Mary Immaculate Virgin of Gandino established many schools across northern Italy in the 19th century.
She decided to join the Ursulines of Mary Immaculate Virgin of Gandino after visiting their convent in Gandino.
The social work of the Ursulines of Mary Immaculate Virgin of Gandino is widely respected in educational circles.

Synset Examples

The Institute of the Ursuline Sisters of the Immaculate Virgin Mary was founded in Gandino (Bergamo, Italy) on 3rd December 1818.

Etymology

From Latin 'Ursulinae', named after St. Ursula, and 'Immaculate Virgin' referring to the Virgin Mary; Gandino, the Italian town of founding.
