Roman Catholic Church

1. NOUN

/ ˌroʊ.mən ˈkæθ.lɪk tʃɝːtʃ / (AmE), / ˌrəʊ.mən ˈkæθ.lɪk tʃɜːtʃ / (BrE)
the Christian Church based in the Vatican City, led by the Pope, and distinguished by its doctrine, worship, and hierarchical structure
the Christian Church led by the Pope in Rome

Examples

The Roman Catholic Church has millions of followers around the world.
He attended Mass at the local Roman Catholic Church every Sunday.
The Roman Catholic Church played a significant role in European history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Catholicism - the faith, practice, and system of the Catholic Church
  • Antonym: Protestant Church - any Western Christian church separate from the Roman Catholic Church

Etymology

‘Catholic’ from Greek *katholikos* (universal); ‘Roman’ refers to Rome, the location of the papacy.
