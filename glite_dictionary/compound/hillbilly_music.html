hillbilly music

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɪlˌbɪli ˈmjuːzɪk / (AmE), / ˈhɪlˌbɪli ˈmjuːzɪk / (BrE)
a style of country music that originated in the mountainous regions of the southern United States, especially in the early 20th century
early Southern U.S. country music

Examples

The radio station plays classic hillbilly music from the 1930s and 1940s every Sunday night.
She grew up listening to hillbilly music that her grandfather played on the porch.
Many consider hillbilly music to be the forerunner of modern country and bluegrass.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: country music - a genre of popular music from the rural southern United States

Etymology

From 'hillbilly', originally a term for people from rural and mountainous Southern U.S. regions, combined with 'music'.
