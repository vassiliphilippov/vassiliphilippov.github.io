Panglossian attitude

1. NOUN

/ pænˈɡlɑːsiən ˈætɪˌtjud / (AmE), / panˈɡlɒsiən ˈætɪˌtjuːd / (BrE)
an excessively or naively optimistic outlook, especially one that disregards reality or potential problems
excessive or naive optimism

Examples

Despite the obvious risks, her Panglossian attitude led her to believe everything would turn out perfectly.
The board's Panglossian attitude blinded them to the real financial challenges ahead.
His Panglossian attitude annoyed colleagues who wanted to address the company's troubles honestly.

Synset Examples

His Panglossian attitude made him overlook serious issues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: optimism - hopefulness and confidence about the future or the successful outcome of something
  • Antonym: cynicism - an inclination to believe that people are motivated purely by self-interest; skepticism

Etymology

From Pangloss, the optimistic character in Voltaire’s 'Candide' (1759), combined with 'attitude'.
