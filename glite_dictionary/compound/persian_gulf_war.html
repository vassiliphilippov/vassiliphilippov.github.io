Persian Gulf War

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɜrʒən ɡʌlf wɔr / (AmE), / ˈpɜːʃən ɡʌlf wɔː / (BrE)
the war fought in 1990–1991 between Iraq and a coalition led by the United States to expel Iraqi forces from Kuwait
war in 1990–1991 between Iraq and coalition forces

Examples

Many veterans of the Persian Gulf War still face health challenges today.
The Persian Gulf War was sparked by Iraq's invasion of Kuwait in 1990.
News coverage of the Persian Gulf War dominated international headlines during the early 1990s.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Gulf War - another common name for the same conflict
  • Antonym: Iraq War - a later war involving Iraq starting in 2003
