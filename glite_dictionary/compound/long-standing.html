long-standing

1. ADJ

/ lɔŋ ˈstændɪŋ / (AmE), / lɒŋ ˈstændɪŋ / (BrE)
having existed or continued for a long time
existing for a long time

Examples

They maintained a long-standing tradition of family gatherings every December.
The two companies finally resolved their long-standing dispute last year.
Her long-standing interest in art led her to become a curator.

Synset Examples

a long-standing friendship
the longstanding conflict

Related Terms

  • Synonym: old - having lived for a long time; not young or new
