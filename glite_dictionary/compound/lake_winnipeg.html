Lake Winnipeg

1. PROPN

/ leɪk ˈwɪnɪˌpɛg / (AmE), / leɪk ˈwɪnɪˌpɛg / (BrE)
a large lake in southern Manitoba, Canada, known for its extensive shoreline and ecological significance
large lake in southern Manitoba, Canada

Examples

Many fishing communities are located along the shores of Lake Winnipeg.
Lake Winnipeg is one of the largest freshwater lakes in Canada.
Research on Lake Winnipeg has highlighted its importance for migratory bird populations.

Etymology

Named after Lake Winnipeg; 'Winnipeg' comes from the Cree words 'winipek', meaning 'muddy water'.
