magneto-optical disc

1. NOUN

/ mægˌnitoʊ ɑpˈtɪkəl dɪsk / (AmE), / maɡˌniːtəʊ ɒpˈtɪkəl dɪsk / (BrE)
a type of rewritable optical storage disc that uses a combination of magnetic and optical technologies to record, erase, and rewrite data
rewritable optical storage disc using magnetic and optical technology

Examples

Many archival systems still rely on magneto-optical discs for reliable long-term data storage.
With a magneto-optical disc, users can rewrite files thousands of times without degrading the media.
The new medical imaging device saves each scan to a magneto-optical disc for easy access and backup.

Synset Examples

Using a magneto-optical disc is much more like using a diskette drive than a CD-RW drive.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: optical disc - a flat, circular disc that stores data using laser technology

Etymology

From magneto-, relating to magnetism, and optical, relating to light.
