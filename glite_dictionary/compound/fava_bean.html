fava bean

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɑːvə ˌbiːn / (AmE), / ˈfɑːvə ˌbiːn / (BrE)
the edible seed of the broad bean plant (Vicia faba), often used in cooking.
edible seed of the broad bean plant

Examples

The chef added fava beans to the salad for extra texture and flavor.
Fresh fava beans are available at the market in early spring.
She made a traditional stew with fava bean as the main ingredient.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: broad bean - an alternative name for the plant or its seed

Etymology

From Italian 'fava' (bean).

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɑːvə ˌbin / (AmE), / ˈfɑːvə ˌbiːn / (BrE)
A large, flat, edible bean, also known as the broad bean, used in cooking and typically eaten fresh or dried.
large edible bean; also called broad bean

Examples

He added fava beans and onions to the salad for extra flavor.
The farmer harvested fresh fava beans early in the summer.
Mediterranean dishes often include fava beans as a staple ingredient.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: broad bean - another name for fava bean
  • Synonym: lima bean - different type of edible bean often compared with fava bean

Etymology

From Italian 'fava' (broad bean), from Latin 'faba'.
