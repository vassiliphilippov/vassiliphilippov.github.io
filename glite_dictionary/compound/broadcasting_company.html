broadcasting company

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrɔdˌkæstɪŋ ˈkʌmpəni / (AmE), / ˈbrɔːdkɑːstɪŋ ˈkʌmpəni / (BrE)
A company that owns and operates television or radio stations, producing and transmitting broadcast content to the public.
company managing TV or radio stations

Examples

The broadcasting company announced plans to launch a new sports channel next year.
She accepted a job as a reporter at a leading broadcasting company in London.
Several major broadcasting companies competed for the rights to air the tournament.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: broadcaster - an organization that transmits radio or television programs
