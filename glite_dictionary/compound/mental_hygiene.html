mental hygiene

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛn.t̬əl ˈhaɪ.dʒin / (AmE), / ˈmɛn.təl ˈhaɪ.dʒiːn / (BrE)
the branch of psychiatry concerned with maintaining mental health and preventing mental disorders using psychological methods
branch of psychiatry focused on mental health

Examples

Public education about mental hygiene can help reduce the stigma associated with mental illness.
The principles of mental hygiene emphasize early intervention and prevention of psychological problems.
Schools often promote programs aimed at the mental hygiene of students.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: psychiatry - medical field focused on mental disorders

Etymology

From 'mental' (relating to the mind) and 'hygiene' (from French 'hygiène', Greek 'hygieinē' meaning 'health').
