Irish moss

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪrɪʃ mɑs / (AmE), / ˈaɪrɪʃ mɒs / (BrE)
a dark purple, edible seaweed found along the Atlantic coasts of Europe and North America, often used as a thickening agent in food and as a dietary supplement
dark purple edible seaweed

Examples

The chef added Irish moss to the soup to give it a rich, creamy texture.
Irish moss is harvested in the summer months along the rocky shores of Ireland and Canada.
Many vegan desserts use Irish moss as a natural thickener and nutritious ingredient.
