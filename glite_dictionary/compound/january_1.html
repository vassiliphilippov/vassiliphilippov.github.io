January 1

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒænjuˈɛri fɜrst / (AmE), / ˌdʒan.juˈɛː.ri fɜːst / (BrE)
the first day of the year in the Gregorian calendar, often celebrated as New Year's Day.
first day of the year; New Year's Day

Examples

Many people make resolutions on January 1 to start the year with new goals.
Fireworks are often set off to celebrate January 1 around the world.
Banks and businesses may close for the holiday on January 1.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: New Year's Day - the first day of the year, celebrated as a holiday

2. NOUN

/ ˌdʒæn.juˈɛr.i ˈfɝst / (AmE), / ˌdʒæn.juˈɛə.ri ˈfɜːst / (BrE)
In the Roman Catholic Church, a holy day of obligation commemorating the Solemnity of Mary, Mother of God; observed on the first day of the year.
Catholic holy day on January 1

Examples

In many countries, January 1 is celebrated as both New Year's Day and a holy day of obligation in the Catholic tradition.
Catholics are required to attend Mass on January 1, which honors Mary, Mother of God.
The church issued a reminder about the significance of January 1 as a holy day.

3. NOUN

/ ˌdʒæn.juˈɛr.i ˈfɝst / (AmE), / ˌdʒæn.jʊˈeə.ri ˈfɜːst / (BrE)
In the Roman Catholic and Anglican Churches, the feast day celebrating the circumcision of Jesus, traditionally observed on January 1st.
Christian feast day on January 1st

Examples

The church held a special service on January 1 to commemorate the Feast of the Circumcision.
Many historical texts refer to January 1 as an important date in the Christian liturgical calendar.
In the Anglican tradition, January 1 marks a significant feast day honoring the early life of Jesus.
