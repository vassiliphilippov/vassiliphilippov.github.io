Jagatai language

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒɑːɡəˈtaɪ ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌdʒæɡəˈtaɪ ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
a Turkic literary language used in medieval Central Asia, named after Chagatai, one of the sons of Genghis Khan
Turkic literary language of medieval Central Asia

Examples

Poetry written in the Jagatai language influenced later Turkic literatures.
The Jagatai language served as a cultural bridge across Central Asia.
Scholars often study texts in the Jagatai language to understand the region's history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Turkic - relating to the family of languages including Turkish, Uzbek, and others

Etymology

Named after Chagatai (Jagatai), the second son of Genghis Khan, whose name is associated with the Chagatai Khanate.
