flathead catfish

1. NOUN

/ ˈflætˌhɛd ˈkætˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈflætˌhɛd ˈkætˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
a large species of catfish native to central North America, noted for its broad, flattened head and projecting lower jaw
large North American catfish with a flat head

Examples

The flathead catfish can grow to over 100 pounds in the wild.
Anglers often target flathead catfish in rivers during the summer months.
The distinctive head of the flathead catfish helps it hunt for prey on the riverbed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catfish - a freshwater or marine fish with whisker-like barbels around the mouth
