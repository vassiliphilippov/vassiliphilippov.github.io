Pennsylvania Dutch

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛnsɪlˈveɪniə dʌtʃ / (AmE), / ˌpɛnsɪlˈveɪniə dʌtʃ / (BrE)
a dialect of High German spoken mainly in parts of Pennsylvania and Maryland, associated with descendants of early German-speaking immigrants
High German dialect in Pennsylvania and Maryland

Examples

Many Amish communities still speak Pennsylvania Dutch as their primary language at home.
He learned to understand Pennsylvania Dutch from his grandparents in rural Pennsylvania.
The church service was conducted entirely in Pennsylvania Dutch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: German - a West Germanic language spoken in Germany, Austria, and Switzerland

Etymology

'Dutch' here comes from a corruption of 'Deutsch,' the German word for 'German.'
