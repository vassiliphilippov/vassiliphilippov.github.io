family Gyrinidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæmli dʒaɪˈrɪnɪˌdi / (AmE), / ˈfamli dʒɪˈrɪnɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of aquatic beetles known as whirligig beetles, characterized by their habit of swimming rapidly in circles on the water's surface.
family of whirligig beetles

Examples

The family Gyrinidae includes beetles that are often seen spinning rapidly on pond surfaces.
Scientists study the family Gyrinidae to understand aquatic adaptations in insects.
Members of the family Gyrinidae are easily recognized by their split eyes and unusual movement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whirligig beetle - common name for beetles in the family Gyrinidae

Etymology

From New Latin 'Gyrinidae', based on Greek 'gyros' (circle), referring to their whirling swimming behavior.
