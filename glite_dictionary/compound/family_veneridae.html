family Veneridae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli vəˈnɛrɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli vɪˈnɛːrɪdiː / (BrE)
a major family of marine bivalve mollusks, commonly known as hard-shell clams, found in shallow waters worldwide
a family of hard-shell clams

Examples

Many commercially harvested clams belong to the family Veneridae and are prized for their delicate flavor.
Marine biologists conducted a survey on the distribution of family Veneridae along the Pacific coast.
Shell collectors often seek specimens from the family Veneridae because of their attractive patterns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hard-shell clam - an edible bivalve mollusk with a hard shell

Etymology

From Latin 'Veneridae', named after the genus Venus in taxonomy.
