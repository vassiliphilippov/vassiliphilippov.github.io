Oregon holly grape

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrɪɡən ˈhɑli ɡreɪp / (AmE), / ˈɒrɪɡən ˈhɒli ɡreɪp / (BrE)
an evergreen shrub native to the Pacific coast of North America, with dark green pinnate leaves, yellow flower clusters, and blue-black berries, often grown as an ornamental
an evergreen shrub of western North America

Examples

The Oregon holly grape is often planted for its attractive foliage and bright yellow flowers.
Wildlife is attracted to the blue berries of the Oregon holly grape in the spring.
Gardeners value the Oregon holly grape for its hardiness and ornamental appeal.
