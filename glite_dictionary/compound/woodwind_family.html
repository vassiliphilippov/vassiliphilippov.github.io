woodwind family

1. NOUN

/ ˈwʊdˌwɪnd ˈfæməli / (AmE), / ˈwʊdwɪnd ˈfæmɪli / (BrE)
The group of musical instruments that produce sound by the vibration of air in a tube, typically including flutes, clarinets, oboes, bassoons, and saxophones.
group of woodwind instruments

Examples

The clarinet and flute are both members of the woodwind family.
She learned to play several instruments from the woodwind family during her time in the orchestra.
The concert featured solos by three artists from the woodwind family.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: brass family - group of brass instruments
  • Synonym: woodwind instrument - a type of instrument in the woodwind family
