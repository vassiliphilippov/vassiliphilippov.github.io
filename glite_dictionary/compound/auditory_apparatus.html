auditory apparatus

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔdəˌtɔri əˈpærətəs / (AmE), / ˈɔːdɪtəri əˈparətəs / (BrE)
the complete set of anatomical components that make up the organ of hearing, including the outer, middle, and inner ears
the organ of hearing; outer, middle, and inner ears
medical

Examples

Damage to the auditory apparatus can result in partial or complete hearing loss.
The doctor explained how sound waves travel through the auditory apparatus to the brain.
Some animals have a more sensitive auditory apparatus than humans, allowing them to detect higher frequencies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ear - the organ of hearing and balance in humans and other vertebrates

Etymology

From Latin 'audīre' (to hear) + 'apparatus' (equipment, apparatus).
