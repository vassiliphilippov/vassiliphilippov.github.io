landing strip

1. NOUN

/ ˈlændɪŋ ˌstrɪp / (AmE), / ˈlændɪŋ ˌstrɪp / (BrE)
a runway or airfield, typically minimal and lacking full airport facilities, used for aircraft takeoff and landing
simple airfield or runway

Examples

The small island has a landing strip used by local pilots during emergencies.
Military planes touched down on the makeshift landing strip built in the desert.
Due to fog at the main airport, the flight was diverted to a rural landing strip.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: runway - a strip of hard ground for aircraft takeoff and landing
  • Antonym: airport - a complex for commercial aircraft with full facilities

2. NOUN

/ ˈlændɪŋ ˌstrɪp / (AmE), / ˈlændɪŋ ˌstrɪp / (BrE)
A designated stretch of ground or runway used for aircraft to land and take off.
strip of ground for aircraft landings

Examples

The new airport will include an additional landing strip to accommodate more flights.
A small private plane taxied down the landing strip after a short flight.
Emergency crews cleared the landing strip following a minor incident.

Synset Examples
