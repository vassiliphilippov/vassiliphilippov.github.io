hither and thither

1. ADV

/ ˈhɪðər ənd ˈðɪðər / (AmE), / ˈhɪðə ənd ˈðɪðə / (BrE)
in many different directions or places; from one place or situation to another
in various directions; from place to place
archaic

Examples

The children ran hither and thither across the playground, their laughter echoing.
During the move, boxes were stacked hither and thither around the house.
In the busy marketplace, people bustled hither and thither searching for bargains.

Synset Examples

we were driven from pillar to post

Related Terms

  • Synonym: here and there - in various places; sporadically

Etymology

From the archaic words 'hither' (to this place) and 'thither' (to that place), both Old English in origin.

2. ADV

/ ˈhɪðɚ ənd ˈðɪðɚ / (AmE), / ˈhɪðə ənd ˈðɪðə / (BrE)
in various directions; to and fro
in various directions; to and fro
archaic

Examples

Cars drove hither and thither as the parade disrupted traffic.
The leaves blew hither and thither in the autumn wind.
Tourists wandered hither and thither through the ancient ruins.

Synset Examples

People were moving hither and thither during the festival.
The kids ran hither and thither in the park.
She searched hither and thither for her lost keys.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to and fro - back and forth

Etymology

From Old English 'hider' (to here) and 'þider' (to there).
