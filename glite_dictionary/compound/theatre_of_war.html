theatre of war

1. NOUN

/ ˈθiːətər əv wɔr / (AmE), / ˈθɪətə əv wɔː / (BrE)
the entire area in which military operations are conducted during a war, including land, sea, and air zones directly involved in fighting
area where military operations occur in war

Examples

The general inspected the theatre of war to assess the situation firsthand.
Supplies were transported across the theatre of war to support the troops.
Changes in technology have expanded the modern theatre of war beyond traditional battlefields.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: battlefield - the site where a battle is fought
