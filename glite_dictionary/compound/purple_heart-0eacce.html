purple heart

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɝpəl hɑrt / (AmE), / ˈpɜːpl hɑːt / (BrE)
A barbiturate drug, formerly used as a sedative and hypnotic, known for its long-acting effects.
long-acting barbiturate sedative
medical archaic

Examples

The therapist warned that misuse of purple heart could lead to addiction and severe health risks.
In the 1960s, some patients were prescribed purple heart to treat insomnia.
Regulations on purple heart have become much stricter due to its potential for abuse.
