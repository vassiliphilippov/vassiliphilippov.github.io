St. Peter's wreath

1. NOUN

/ seɪnt ˈpitərz ˈriθ / (AmE), / seɪnt ˈpiːtəz ˈriːθ / (BrE)
A shrub noted for its abundant small white flowers that bloom in spring, often grown as an ornamental garden plant.
flowering shrub with white spring blooms

Examples

The St. Peter's wreath brightens the garden with its clusters of small white flowers every spring.
Many landscapers recommend planting St. Peter's wreath for its hardy nature and ornamental appeal.
Each April, the St. Peter's wreath in front of our house is covered in blossoms.
