staphylococcal enterotoxin

1. NOUN

/ ˌstæfəloʊˌkɑkəl ˌɛntəroʊˈtɑksɪn / (AmE), / ˌstæfɪləkɒkəl ˌɛntərəʊˈtɒksɪn / (BrE)
a soluble exotoxin produced by some strains of Staphylococcus bacteria that can cause food poisoning in humans
toxin from Staphylococcus bacteria causing food poisoning
medical

Examples

Many cases of food poisoning are caused by staphylococcal enterotoxin contaminating unrefrigerated food.
Researchers are studying how staphylococcal enterotoxin triggers a strong immune response in humans.
Proper cooking can help destroy staphylococcal enterotoxin in contaminated dishes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exotoxin - a toxin released by bacteria into the surrounding medium

Etymology

From 'Staphylococcus' (a genus of bacteria, from Greek staphylē 'bunch of grapes' + kokkos 'berry') and 'enterotoxin' (from Greek enteron 'intestine' + toxin).
