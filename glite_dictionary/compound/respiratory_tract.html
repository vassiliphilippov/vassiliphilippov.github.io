respiratory tract

1. NOUN

/ rɛˈspɪrətɔri trækt / (AmE), / rɪˈspɪrətəri trakt / (BrE)
the passages in the body through which air enters and leaves the lungs, including the nose, throat, and lungs
air passages in the body
medical

Examples

Smoking can cause irritation and inflammation of the respiratory tract.
The common cold is an infection that primarily affects the upper respiratory tract.
Doctors examined the patient's respiratory tract for signs of obstruction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: airway - the passage by which air reaches the lungs

Etymology

From Latin 'respirare' (to breathe) + 'tractus' (a drawing out, extended area or region).
