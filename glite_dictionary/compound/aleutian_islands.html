Aleutian Islands

1. PROPN

/ əˈluːʃən ˈaɪˌlændz / (AmE), / əˈljuːʃən ˈaɪləndz / (BrE)
a chain of islands in the North Pacific Ocean, extending southwest from the coast of Alaska.
archipelago southwest of Alaska

Examples

The Aleutian Islands are known for their rugged landscapes and frequent fog.
During World War II, several battles took place in the Aleutian Islands.
Many seabirds and marine mammals inhabit the remote Aleutian Islands.

Etymology

Named after the Aleut people, indigenous to the islands.
