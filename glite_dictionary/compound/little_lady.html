little lady

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪt̬l ˈleɪdi / (AmE), / ˈlɪtl ˈleɪdi / (BrE)
a term of endearment used to refer to a woman or girl, often affectionate or slightly patronizing
affectionate term for a woman or girl

Examples

He smiled and called his daughter his little lady as she proudly showed him her drawing.
The old man tipped his hat to the little lady passing by.
'Would the little lady like some tea?' the host asked with a warm smile.

Synset Examples

The little lady waved goodbye as she left.
