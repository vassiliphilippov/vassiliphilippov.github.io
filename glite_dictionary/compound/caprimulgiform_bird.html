caprimulgiform bird

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæprəˈmʌlʤəˌfɔrm bɝd / (AmE), / ˌkæprɪˈmʌlʤɪˌfɔːm bɜːd / (BrE)
a long-winged non-passerine bird of the order Caprimulgiformes, including nightjars and their relatives
long-winged non-passerine bird (order Caprimulgiformes)

Examples

The caprimulgiform bird is known for its silent, moth-like flight at dusk.
Many species of caprimulgiform birds feed on insects caught in the air at night.
Researchers observed a rare caprimulgiform bird nesting in the remote forest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nightjar - a nocturnal bird of the family Caprimulgidae, related to caprimulgiform birds

Etymology

From Latin caprimulgus (goatsucker) + -iform (having the form of) used in taxonomy.
