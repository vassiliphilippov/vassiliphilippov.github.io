telingo potato

1. NOUN

/ təˈlɪŋɡoʊ pəˈteɪtoʊ / (AmE), / təˈlɪŋɡəʊ pəˈteɪtəʊ / (BrE)
a putrid-smelling plant of southeastern Asia, especially the Philippines, cultivated for its edible tuber.
tuberous plant of Southeast Asia

Examples

Farmers in the region harvest the telingo potato for its starchy tubers despite its strong odor.
Some chefs have started using telingo potato as an ingredient in traditional Filipino dishes.
The telingo potato is known for its pungent smell but is valued for its nutritional content.
