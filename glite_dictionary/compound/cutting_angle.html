cutting angle

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌtɪŋ ˌæŋɡəl / (AmE), / ˈkʌtɪŋ ˌaŋɡl̩ / (BrE)
the angle formed between the face of a cutting tool and the surface of the material being worked on
angle in cutting tools

Examples

The machinist adjusted the cutting angle to achieve a smoother finish on the metal piece.
A steep cutting angle can cause faster tool wear in high-speed operations.
Selecting the correct cutting angle improves both the precision and efficiency of the manufacturing process.
