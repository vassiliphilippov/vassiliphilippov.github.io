Chisholm Trail

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɪzəm treɪl / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪzəm treɪl / (BrE)
a former cattle trail used in the late 19th century, running from San Antonio, Texas, to Abilene, Kansas, along which cattle were driven to railheads for shipment.
historic cattle trail from Texas to Kansas

Examples

During the late 1800s, thousands of cowboys drove herds along the Chisholm Trail to Kansas railheads.
The Chisholm Trail played a major role in the development of the American cattle industry.
Today, portions of the Chisholm Trail are marked for tourists interested in the history of the Old West.

Etymology

Named after Jesse Chisholm, a trader of Scottish-Cherokee descent who scouted the trail.
