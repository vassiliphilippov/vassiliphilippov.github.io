genus Chlamydomonas

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs kləˌmɪdəˈmoʊnəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs kləˌmɪdəˈməʊnəs / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Chlamydomonadaceae, comprising solitary, flagellated, plant-like algae commonly found in fresh water and damp soil.
genus of flagellated green algae

Examples

The genus Chlamydomonas is important in studies of photosynthesis and cell motility.
Certain species within the genus Chlamydomonas can become pests in filtration plants.
Algae belonging to the genus Chlamydomonas are often found in damp soil and freshwater environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Chlamydomonas - Solitary, flagellated green algae within the genus Chlamydomonas

Etymology

Genus name from New Latin, from Greek 'chlamys' (cloak) + 'monas' (unit).
