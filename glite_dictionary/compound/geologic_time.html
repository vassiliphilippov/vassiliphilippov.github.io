geologic time

1. NOUN

/ dʒiˈɑː.lə.dʒɪk taɪm / (AmE), / dʒɪˈɒl.ə.dʒɪk taɪm / (BrE)
the vast period of time covering the physical formation and development of the Earth, especially before human history
the period of Earth's physical formation and development

Examples

Dinosaurs roamed the Earth during geologic time long before human beings appeared.
By studying rock layers, scientists reconstruct the events that occurred throughout geologic time.
The history of life is recorded in fossils that span vast stretches of geologic time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: geological era - a major division of geologic time, subdivided into periods

Etymology

From 'geologic' (relating to geology) and 'time', indicating the chronological framework used in geology.
