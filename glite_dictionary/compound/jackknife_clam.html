jackknife clam

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒækˌnaɪf klæm / (AmE), / ˈdʒækˌnaɪf klam / (BrE)
a marine clam with a long, narrow, curved, thin shell, often found in coastal sandy areas
marine clam with narrow curved shell

Examples

The jackknife clam burrows quickly into wet sand when disturbed by beachcombers.
Fishermen often dig for jackknife clams at low tide along the shore.
The long, slender shell of the jackknife clam makes it easy to identify among other clams.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: razor clam - a similar marine clam with an elongated shell
