unicorn bait

1. NOUN

/ ˈjuː.nɪ.kɔrn beɪt / (AmE), / ˈjuː.nɪ.kɔːn beɪt / (BrE)
an idea, product, or feature that appears highly attractive but is unrealistic, especially when used to entice investors to fund a startup or business
something unrealistically attractive to investors

Examples

The new app feature was pure unicorn bait designed to catch the interest of venture capitalists.
Despite its buzzwords and sleek pitch, their proposal was nothing but unicorn bait.
Many entrepreneurs believe that unicorn bait is necessary to stand out in a crowded startup market.

Synset Examples

Many startups use unicorn bait to attract venture capital.
That flashy advertisement is just unicorn bait for investors.
The product's exaggerated claims felt like unicorn bait to me.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unicorn - a startup company valued at over $1 billion

Etymology

From 'unicorn' (a startup valued at over $1 billion) and 'bait' (something used to entice).
