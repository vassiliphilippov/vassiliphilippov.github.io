spinach beet

1. NOUN

/ ˈspɪnɪtʃ bit / (AmE), / ˈspɪnɪtʃ biːt / (BrE)
A variety of beet grown for its edible leaves and stalks, which lack a swollen root.
beet grown for edible leaves and stalks

Examples

Many gardeners choose to grow spinach beet for its flavorful and nutritious leaves.
The spinach beet can be harvested multiple times throughout the growing season.
Unlike regular beetroot, spinach beet does not develop a large, edible root.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chard - A leafy vegetable; another name for spinach beet

2. NOUN

/ ˈspɪnətʃ biːt / (AmE), / ˈspɪnɪtʃ biːt / (BrE)
a variety of beet cultivated for its long, succulent whitish stalks and large green leaves, eaten as a vegetable
a variety of beet with edible leaves and stalks

Examples

We grew spinach beet in our garden for its nutritious leaves.
The recipe calls for one bunch of fresh spinach beet stalks, chopped.
Spinach beet can be sautéed or used in soups, much like Swiss chard.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Swiss chard - a leafy green vegetable closely related to spinach beet
