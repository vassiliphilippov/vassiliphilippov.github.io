teach-in

1. NOUN

/ ˈtitʃ ɪn / (AmE), / ˈtiːtʃ ɪn / (BrE)
an extended meeting, often at a university, where people gather to learn about and discuss a controversial or important issue, usually involving lectures and open discussion
extended educational meeting on an important issue

Examples

The university organized a teach-in to discuss climate change and its social impacts.
Many students attended the teach-in on civil rights led by faculty members.
During the 1960s, teach-ins became popular forms of protest against the Vietnam War.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: workshop - a meeting at which a group engages in intensive discussion and activity on a particular subject

Etymology

Formed by analogy with 'sit-in', combining 'teach' and the suffix '-in' popularized in 1960s protest terminology.
