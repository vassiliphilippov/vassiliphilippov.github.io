comma bacillus

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑː.mə bəˈsɪl.əs / (AmE), / ˈkɒm.ə bəˈsɪl.əs / (BrE)
a comma-shaped bacterium (Vibrio cholerae) that causes Asiatic cholera
bacterium causing cholera
medical

Examples

The comma bacillus was identified as the causative agent of cholera in the 19th century.
Under a microscope, the comma bacillus appears as a curved, rod-shaped bacterium.
Proper sanitation prevents the spread of the comma bacillus and reduces cholera outbreaks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cholera - an infectious disease causing severe diarrhea

Etymology

From the bacterium’s curved, comma-like shape and Latin 'bacillus' (little rod).
