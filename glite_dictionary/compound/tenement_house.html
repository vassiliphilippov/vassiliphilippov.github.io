tenement house

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛnəmənt ˌhaʊs / (AmE), / ˈtɛnɪmənt ˌhaʊs / (BrE)
A large, often run-down apartment building, especially one that is crowded and barely meets minimal living standards.
run-down, overcrowded apartment building

Examples

The family lived in a tenement house on the edge of the city, where the conditions were quite poor.
During the late 19th century, many immigrants settled in crowded tenement houses in New York City.
The novel describes life in a tenement house, highlighting the daily struggles of its residents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apartment building - a building containing several individual apartments
  • Synonym: slum - a densely populated urban area marked by poor housing conditions

Etymology

From Old French *tenement* (property, holding), from Latin *tenementum*, meaning 'holding; property'.
