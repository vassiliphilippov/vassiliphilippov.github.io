Mercator's projection

1. NOUN

/ mərˈkeɪtərz prəˈdʒɛkʃən / (AmE), / məˈkeɪtəz prəˈdʒɛkʃən / (BrE)
A method of representing the surface of the earth on a cylindrical map where meridians and parallels appear as straight lines at right angles, causing areas farther from the equator to appear larger than they are.
a cylindrical map projection of the earth

Examples

The world map in the classroom is based on the Mercator's projection for easy navigation.
Because of the Mercator's projection, Greenland appears much larger than it actually is.
Sailors often use maps with the Mercator's projection to plot straight-line courses across the ocean.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: map projection - the general term for techniques used to represent the earth's surface on a map

Etymology

Named after Gerardus Mercator (1512–1594), Flemish cartographer who introduced the method in 1569.
