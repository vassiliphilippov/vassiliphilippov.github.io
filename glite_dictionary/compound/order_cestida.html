order Cestida

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdər sɛˈstɪdə / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə sɛˈstɪdə / (BrE)
A taxonomic order of ctenophores, distinguished by their short tentacles and including a single family.
an order of comb jellies with short tentacles

Examples

Scientists discovered a new species within the order Cestida in the deep sea.
Members of the order Cestida are known for their ribbon-like bodies and short tentacles.
The unique features of the order Cestida make them easily distinguishable from other ctenophores.

Etymology

From New Latin 'Cestida', based on Latin 'cestus' (belt, girdle), referencing the shape of these animals.
