octatonic scale

1. NOUN

/ ɑkˈtæt.ə.nɪk skeɪl / (AmE), / ɒkˈtat.ə.nɪk skeɪl / (BrE)
a musical scale consisting of eight notes formed by alternating whole and semitone steps
eight-note scale of alternating whole and semitones

Examples

The octatonic scale is frequently used in twentieth-century classical music to create complex harmonies.
Many jazz musicians explore the octatonic scale to enrich their improvisation techniques.
Composers like Stravinsky were fascinated by the unique sound of the octatonic scale.

Synset Examples

Bernstein often used the octatonic scale, also very popular in jazz music, as a basis for his compositions.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: whole tone scale - a scale made entirely of whole tone intervals

Etymology

From octa- (Greek for 'eight') + tonic (from Greek tonos, 'tone').
