sergeant at arms

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɝː.dʒənt æt ˈɑːrmz / (AmE), / ˈsɑː.dʒənt ət ˈɑːmz / (BrE)
an officer appointed by a legislative body, court, or other organization to keep order and enforce rules or commands
official who maintains order; executive officer

Examples

The sergeant at arms escorted the disruptive spectator out of the legislative chamber.
As sergeant at arms, she is responsible for ensuring the security of official proceedings.
Congress appointed a new sergeant at arms to uphold order during sessions.

Etymology

From 'sergeant' (from Old French 'sergent', meaning servant or officer) and 'at arms', denoting armed service.
