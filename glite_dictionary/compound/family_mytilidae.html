family Mytilidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli mɪˈtɪlɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli mɪˈtɪlɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of marine bivalve mollusks commonly known as mussels, found attached to rocks in seawater.
family of marine mussels

Examples

Many species within the family Mytilidae play an important role in marine ecosystems as filter feeders.
Researchers studied how pollution affected populations of the family Mytilidae along the rocky shoreline.
The blue mussel, a member of the family Mytilidae, is widely used in aquaculture around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mussel - A common name for bivalve mollusks in the family Mytilidae

Etymology

From New Latin 'Mytilidae', from 'Mytilus', a Latin genus name for mussels, originally from Greek 'mytilos'.
