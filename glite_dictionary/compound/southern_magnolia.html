southern magnolia

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌðɚn mægˈnoʊliə / (AmE), / ˈsʌðən mæɡˈnəʊlɪə / (BrE)
an evergreen tree native to the southern United States, with large, stiff, glossy leaves and huge, fragrant white flowers
evergreen tree with large fragrant flowers

Examples

The southern magnolia is prized for its beautiful white blossoms and glossy green leaves.
They planted a southern magnolia in the front yard to add shade and fragrance.
Every spring, the southern magnolia fills the garden with its sweet scent.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: magnolia - a genus of flowering trees and shrubs with large, fragrant flowers
