carob bean tree

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɛr.əb ˌbin tri / (AmE), / ˈkær.əb ˌbiːn triː / (BrE)
an evergreen Mediterranean tree (Ceratonia siliqua) whose edible pods are used as a chocolate substitute; also known as the biblical carob
evergreen Mediterranean tree with edible pods

Examples

The carob bean tree thrives in warm Mediterranean climates and produces pods that are sweet and nutritious.
Ancient texts refer to the carob bean tree as the "St. John's bread" due to its historical significance.
Farmers often plant the carob bean tree as a drought-resistant crop alternative.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carob - the edible pod of the carob bean tree

Etymology

From Arabic kharrūb via Old Spanish algarrobo, referring to the tree Ceratonia siliqua.
