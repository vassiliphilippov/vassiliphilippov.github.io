present perfect

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɛzənt ˈpɜrfɪkt / (AmE), / ˈprɛzənt ˈpɜːfɪkt / (BrE)
a verb tense used to express an action that has been completed at the present time or has relevance to the present
verb tense for completed actions with present relevance

Examples

Many English learners find the present perfect tense challenging to master.
The sentence 'She has left the building' is in the present perfect.
Teachers often ask students to give examples of the present perfect during grammar lessons.

Synset Examples

‘I have finished’ is an example of the present perfect

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: past perfect - verb tense for actions completed before a certain point in the past
  • Degree Higher: future perfect - verb tense for actions that will be completed before a specified future time
