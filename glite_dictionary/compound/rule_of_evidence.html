rule of evidence

1. NOUN

/ ruːl əv ˈɛvədəns / (AmE), / ruːl əv ˈɛvɪdəns / (BrE)
a legal principle governing what information may be presented and considered as proof in a judicial trial
legal principle on admissible trial evidence
legal

Examples

The rule of evidence prevented the lawyer from introducing hearsay testimony.
Understanding each rule of evidence is vital for both prosecutors and defense attorneys.
A new rule of evidence has changed how digital documents are admitted in court.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: evidence - information used to support a fact or claim, especially in law
