battle of Marathon

1. NOUN

/ ˈbætəl əv ˈmærəˌθɑn / (AmE), / ˈbætəl əv ˈmærəθən / (BrE)
a battle fought in 490 BC near Marathon, Greece, in which the Athenians and their allies defeated the invading Persian forces
historic Greek-Persian battle (490 BC)

Examples

The battle of Marathon marked a turning point in the Greco-Persian Wars.
According to historians, the battle of Marathon demonstrated the effectiveness of the Greek hoplite phalanx.
After the battle of Marathon, the legend of Pheidippides' run inspired the modern marathon race.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Battle of Salamis - a decisive naval battle between Greeks and Persians in 480 BC

Etymology

From the location Marathon (Greek Μαραθών), site of the battle.
