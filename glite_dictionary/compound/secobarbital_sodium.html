secobarbital sodium

1. NOUN

/ ˌsiːkoʊˌbɑːrˈbaɪtəl ˈsoʊdiəm / (AmE), / ˌsiːkəʊˌbɑːˈbɪtəl ˈsəʊdiəm / (BrE)
A white, odorless, slightly bitter barbiturate drug, used as a sodium salt to induce sedation and treat convulsions.
barbiturate drug used for sedation and convulsions
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed secobarbital sodium to help manage the patient's severe insomnia.
Due to its potential for dependence, secobarbital sodium is used only under strict medical supervision.
Researchers are studying the effects of secobarbital sodium on patients with treatment-resistant seizures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seconal - trade name for secobarbital sodium
  • Degree Higher: barbiturate - class of drugs including secobarbital sodium

Etymology

From 'secobarbital', a barbiturate, plus 'sodium', indicating its salt form.
