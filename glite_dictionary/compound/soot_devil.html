soot devil

1. NOUN

/ sʊt ˈdɛvəl / (AmE), / sʊt ˈdɛvəl / (BrE)
A playful or mythical figure representing soot or ash residue, especially thought to cause messes around fireplaces or chimneys.
mythical figure for soot residue

Examples

After cleaning the hearth, she joked that the soot devil had made quite a mess overnight.
Children were told stories about a mischievous soot devil that lived in the chimney.
Every time the fireplace was used, new smudges appeared—evidence, they said, of the soot devil.

Synset Examples

He joked that the soot devil visited after the fire.
The soot devil left marks all over the fireplace.
They found the soot devil hiding behind the chimney.
