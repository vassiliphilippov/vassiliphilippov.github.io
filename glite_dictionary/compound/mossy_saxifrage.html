mossy saxifrage

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɔsi ˈsæksɪfrædʒ / (AmE), / ˈmɒsi ˈsæksɪfrɪdʒ / (BrE)
a small tufted or mat-forming perennial plant native to the mountains of Europe, cultivated for its clusters of white flowers
European perennial plant with white flowers

Examples

The shady rock garden was covered in mossy saxifrage in full bloom.
Gardeners prize mossy saxifrage for its delicate clusters of white flowers and low-growing habit.
We spotted patches of mossy saxifrage while hiking in the Alps.

Etymology

From 'saxifrage', Latin *saxifraga* (literally 'stone-breaker'), with 'mossy' describing its appearance.
