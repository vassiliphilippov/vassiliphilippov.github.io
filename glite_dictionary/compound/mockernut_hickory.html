mockernut hickory

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑːkərˌnʌt ˈhɪkəri / (AmE), / ˈmɒkənʌt ˈhɪkəri / (BrE)
A North American hickory tree (Carya tomentosa) with smooth bark, 7 to 9 leaflets per leaf, and hard-shelled edible nuts.
North American hickory tree with edible nuts

Examples

The mockernut hickory produces a tough nut that is enjoyed by squirrels and other wildlife.
Many woodworkers prize mockernut hickory for its strength and durability.
The leaves of the mockernut hickory usually consist of seven to nine leaflets.
