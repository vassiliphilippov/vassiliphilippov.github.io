video game

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪdioʊ ɡeɪm / (AmE), / ˈvɪdiəʊ ɡeɪm / (BrE)
a game played by electronically manipulating images produced by a computer program on a screen, often for entertainment
electronic game played on a screen

Examples

He spent all afternoon playing his favorite video game with his friends online.
The new video game features stunning graphics and challenging puzzles.
Parents often set time limits for their children when they play video games.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: computer game - a game played using a computer

Etymology

From 'video' (from Latin 'videre', to see) and 'game'. Popularized in the 1970s with the rise of electronic entertainment.

2. NOUN

/ ˈvɪdioʊ ɡeɪm / (AmE), / ˈvɪdiəʊ ɡeɪm / (BrE)
An electronic game played by manipulating images on a screen, typically with a computer or gaming console.
electronic game played on a screen

Examples

He spent his afternoon playing his favorite video game with his friends online.
New technology allows video games to have incredibly realistic graphics and sound.
She designed a video game as part of her computer science project.

Synset Examples

That nerdboy is always discussing the latest video games.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: computer game - an electronic game played on a computer
  • Antonym: board game - a game played on a board, using pieces or cards

Etymology

From 'video' (relating to visual images) + 'game' (form of play), popularized in the 1970s.
