valley fever

1. NOUN

/ ˈvæli ˌfiːvɚ / (AmE), / ˈvæli ˌfiːvə / (BrE)
A lung infection caused by inhaling spores of the fungus Coccidioides, common in certain arid regions, often leading to cough and skin nodules.
fungal respiratory infection
medical

Examples

People living in dry, dusty areas are at a higher risk of contracting valley fever.
Doctors confirmed he had valley fever after he developed a persistent cough and fever.
The outbreak of valley fever affected hundreds of residents in the region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coccidioidomycosis - technical name for valley fever

Etymology

Named for its prevalence in California's San Joaquin Valley and similar areas.
