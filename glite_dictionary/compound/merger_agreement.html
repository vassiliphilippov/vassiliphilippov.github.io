merger agreement

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɝːdʒɚ əˈɡriːmənt / (AmE), / ˈmɜːdʒə əˈɡriːmənt / (BrE)
a formal contract outlining the terms and conditions of the merger between two or more companies
contract governing a company merger
legal

Examples

The two companies signed a merger agreement to combine their resources and operations.
The terms of the merger agreement were reviewed by legal counsel before approval.
Shareholders voted overwhelmingly in favor of the proposed merger agreement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contract - a written or spoken agreement enforceable by law
