bear oak

1. NOUN

/ bɛr oʊk / (AmE), / beə əʊk / (BrE)
A shrubby species of oak (Quercus ilicifolia) native to the southeastern United States, often forming dense thickets.
shrubby oak of southeastern US

Examples

The bear oak is an important part of the dry forests in the southeastern United States.
We found a stand of bear oak growing along the edge of the clearing.
Many animals use the dense thickets of bear oak as shelter.
