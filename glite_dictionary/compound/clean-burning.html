clean-burning

1. ADJ

/ ˈklin ˌbɝːnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkliːn ˌbɜːnɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a fuel or engine) leaving little pollution or residue when burned.
producing little pollution when burned

Examples

Natural gas is considered a clean-burning fuel compared to coal or oil.
Many modern fireplaces are designed to be clean-burning and more environmentally friendly.
The clean-burning engine helped reduce emissions in the city.

Synset Examples

natural gas is a clean-burning fuel

Related Terms

  • Antonym: polluting - causing pollution; contaminating
  • Synonym: smokeless - producing little or no smoke
