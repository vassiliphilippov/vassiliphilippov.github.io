negative magnetic pole

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɛɡətɪv məɡˈnætɪk poʊl / (AmE), / ˈnɛɡətɪv məɡˈnætɪk pəʊl / (BrE)
the pole of a magnet that, when freely suspended, points toward the Earth's south; also called the south-seeking pole
the pole of a magnet that points south

Examples

The compass needle's negative magnetic pole always aligns toward the south.
When suspended freely, the negative magnetic pole of the bar magnet pointed away from the north.
Scientists marked the negative magnetic pole to illustrate the direction of magnetic field lines.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: positive magnetic pole - the pole of a magnet that points toward Earth's north
