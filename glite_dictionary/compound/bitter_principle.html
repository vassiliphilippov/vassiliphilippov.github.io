bitter principle

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɪtər ˈprɪnsəpəl / (AmE), / ˈbɪtə ˈprɪnsɪpl / (BrE)
any of various chemical compounds found in plants that have a strongly bitter taste and do not belong to any specific chemical class.
plant-derived bitter-tasting compound

Examples

Certain medicinal herbs owe their effects to the presence of a bitter principle in their leaves.
The tonic properties of gentian are due to a bitter principle it contains.
Scientists have isolated the bitter principle from several plants used in traditional remedies.
