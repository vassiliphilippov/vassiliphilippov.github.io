grab sample

1. NOUN

/ ɡræb ˈsæmpəl / (AmE), / ɡræb ˈsɑːmpəl / (BrE)
a single sample or measurement taken at a specific point in time or over a very short period, typically to represent immediate conditions
single sample taken at a specific time

Examples

The technician collected a grab sample of water from the river for immediate analysis.
Regulations require at least one grab sample be taken each day at the facility.
A grab sample may not accurately reflect changes in water quality over time.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: composite sample - a sample made by combining several grab samples
