New England aster

1. NOUN

/ nuː ˈɪŋɡlənd ˈæstər / (AmE), / njuː ˈɪŋɡlənd ˈæstə / (BrE)
a perennial plant of eastern North America with showy purple or pink flower heads, known scientifically as Symphyotrichum novae-angliae, and cultivated as an ornamental; a parent of the Michaelmas daisy.
perennial with showy purple or pink flowers (plant)

Examples

The New England aster adds vibrant purple color to many autumn gardens across North America.
Gardeners often plant New England asters to attract butterflies in the fall.
A New England aster can be recognized by its tall stems and densely packed flower heads.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Michaelmas daisy - a group of autumn-blooming asters descended in part from the New England aster
