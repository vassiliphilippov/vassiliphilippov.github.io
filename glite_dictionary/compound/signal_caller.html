signal caller

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪɡ.nəl ˌkɔ.lɚ / (AmE), / ˈsɪɡ.nəl ˌkɔː.lə / (BrE)
In American football, the player who directs the team's offense, typically the quarterback.
(football) the team's directing player, usually quarterback
american

Examples

The signal caller led his team to victory with a last-minute touchdown pass.
Every successful football team depends on its signal caller to make the right calls under pressure.
Despite being a rookie, the signal caller showed impressive composure in the pocket.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quarterback - the player who leads the offense in American football

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɪɡ.nəl ˌkɔl.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈsɪɡ.nəl ˌkɔː.lə / (BrE)
(American football) a player, usually the quarterback, who directs the team's offensive plays by calling signals or instructions to teammates.
player directing offensive football plays
american

Examples

The team's signal caller made a smart decision to change the play at the last moment.
Many experts believe a strong signal caller is essential for a successful football team.
The rookie quickly earned respect as the starting signal caller after leading his team to victory.

Synset Examples

quarterback is the most important position on the team

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quarterback - the player who throws the ball and leads the offense in American football

Etymology

Formed from 'signal' (an instruction or cue) + 'caller' (person who calls), referring to calling out plays.
