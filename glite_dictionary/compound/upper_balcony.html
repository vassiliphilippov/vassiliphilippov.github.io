upper balcony

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌpɚ ˈbæl.kəni / (AmE), / ˈʌp.ə ˈbæl.kəni / (BrE)
the rearmost or highest section of seats in a theatre balcony, usually offering the least expensive views
highest section of balcony seating in a theatre

Examples

Our tickets were for the upper balcony, so we had a distant but clear view of the stage.
Crowds filled every seat, from the orchestra to the upper balcony, for the opening night.
He peered down from the upper balcony, trying to spot his friends in the stalls below.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: balcony - a platform projecting from the wall of a building, with a balustrade or railing along its outer edge
