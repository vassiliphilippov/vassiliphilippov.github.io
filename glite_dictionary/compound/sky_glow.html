sky glow

1. NOUN

/ skaɪ ɡloʊ / (AmE), / skaɪ ɡləʊ / (BrE)
a brightening of the night sky over populated areas caused by artificial light, making stars less visible
artificial brightening of night sky in cities

Examples

The sky glow over the city made it difficult to see the stars at night.
Astronomers often travel to remote locations to escape the effects of sky glow.
Efforts to reduce sky glow include using shielded streetlights and dimming bright signs after midnight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: light pollution - excessive or misdirected artificial light in the environment
