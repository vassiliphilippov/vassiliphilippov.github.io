family Spalacidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌfæməli spəˈlæsɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˌfæmɪli spæˈlæsɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of rodents that includes the blind mole-rats, bamboo rats, root rats, and zokors
a rodent family including mole-rats and bamboo rats

Examples

The family Spalacidae includes several subterranean rodents adapted to life underground.
Researchers have discovered new species belonging to the family Spalacidae in Eastern Europe.
Adaptations to low-oxygen environments are common among members of the family Spalacidae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Spalax - the genus of blind mole-rats
  • Synonym: mole-rat - a burrowing rodent with reduced eyesight

Etymology

From New Latin 'Spalacidae', based on the genus name 'Spalax'.
