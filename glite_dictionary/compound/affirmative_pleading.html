affirmative pleading

1. NOUN

/ əˈfɜːrmətɪv ˈpliːdɪŋ / (AmE), / əˈfɜːmətɪv ˈpliːdɪŋ / (BrE)
A defensive legal statement in which the defendant asserts new facts that, if true, would defeat the plaintiff's claim, instead of simply denying the plaintiff's allegations.
legal defense asserting new facts
legal

Examples

The defendant filed an affirmative pleading to introduce evidence supporting their right to the property.
By submitting an affirmative pleading, the defense acknowledged certain facts while asserting new information to counter the plaintiff's claims.
The court considered the affirmative pleading before making a ruling on the motion to dismiss.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: affirmative defense - a defense in which new facts are introduced to defeat a claim
  • Antonym: denial - a formal statement in legal pleadings that contradicts allegations by the opposing party

Etymology

From legal use of 'affirmative' (asserting) and 'pleading' (formal legal statement).
