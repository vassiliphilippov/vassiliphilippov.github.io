to draw and quarter

1. VERB

/ drɔː ənd ˈkwɔrtər / (AmE), / drɔː ənd ˈkwɔːtə / (BrE)
to execute someone by tying each limb to a different horse and pulling them apart
execute by pulling apart with horses
archaic

Examples

In medieval England, traitors were often drawn and quartered as a gruesome punishment.
The historical drama depicted several prisoners being drawn and quartered for their crimes.
They threatened to draw and quarter anyone caught plotting against the king.

Synset Examples

in the old days, people were drawn and quartered for certain crimes

Etymology

From Middle English 'drawen' (to pull) and 'quarter' (to divide into four parts), describing the method of execution used in medieval England.
