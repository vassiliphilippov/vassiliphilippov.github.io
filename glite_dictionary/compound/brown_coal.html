brown coal

1. NOUN

/ braʊn koʊl / (AmE), / braʊn kəʊl / (BrE)
a soft, brownish coal showing properties intermediate between peat and bituminous coal, used as a fuel
soft, low-grade brown coal

Examples

Germany has abundant reserves of brown coal that are used to generate electricity.
Because brown coal contains more moisture, it produces less energy than bituminous coal.
Mining of brown coal has significant environmental impacts in some regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lignite - a soft brownish coal showing characteristics of peat and of bituminous coal
  • Degree Higher: bituminous coal - a dense coal of higher rank than lignite or brown coal
  • Degree Lower: peat - partly decomposed plant material, precursor to coal
