silent picture

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪlənt ˈpɪktʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈsaɪlənt ˈpɪktʃə / (BrE)
a movie made without synchronized recorded sound, especially spoken dialogue
movie without sound or dialogue

Examples

Many classic silent pictures have been restored and shown at film festivals.
Charlie Chaplin rose to fame starring in silent pictures during the early 20th century.
Before sound was introduced, movie theaters employed pianists to accompany silent pictures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silent film - a movie made before the invention of synchronized recorded sound
