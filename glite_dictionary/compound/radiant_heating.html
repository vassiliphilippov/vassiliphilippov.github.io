radiant heating

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪdiənt ˈhitɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈreɪdiənt ˈhiːtɪŋ / (BrE)
a method of heating a building by using panels in floors, walls, or ceilings that radiate heat, typically through hot water pipes or electrical elements
building heating by radiating panels

Examples

Many modern homes use radiant heating to provide consistent warmth during the winter.
With radiant heating, heat is evenly distributed from the floor instead of blowing through vents.
Installing radiant heating can increase the energy efficiency of your home.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: central heating - a heating system that warms an entire building from a central source
