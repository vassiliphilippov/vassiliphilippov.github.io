forward transaction

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔrwərd trænˈzækʃən / (AmE), / ˈfɔːwəd trænˈzækʃən / (BrE)
an agreement to buy or sell a product for delivery at a specified future time, typically at a price agreed upon at the time of the contract
agreement to exchange goods at a future date

Examples

A forward transaction allows both parties to lock in prices for future delivery.
Many companies use forward transactions to hedge against price fluctuations in raw materials.
The contract specified that the forward transaction would be settled in six months.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: spot transaction - a financial transaction with immediate delivery

Etymology

From 'forward', meaning 'future', and 'transaction', from Latin 'transigere' (to accomplish, to manage).
