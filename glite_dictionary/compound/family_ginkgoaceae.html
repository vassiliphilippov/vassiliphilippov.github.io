family Ginkgoaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌfæmɪli ˌɡɪŋkoʊˈeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / ˌfæmɪli ˌɡɪŋkəʊˈeɪsiːˌiː / (BrE)
A family of gymnosperm plants constituting the order Ginkgoales, which includes the living genus Ginkgo and related extinct forms.
plant family including Ginkgo and extinct relatives

Examples

The family Ginkgoaceae dates back more than 200 million years in the fossil record.
Only one living species, Ginkgo biloba, belongs to the family Ginkgoaceae.
Botanists study fossil leaves to understand the ancient diversity of the family Ginkgoaceae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ginkgo - a genus of highly distinctive trees, the only extant genus in Ginkgoaceae

Etymology

From modern Latin 'Ginkgoaceae', formed from 'Ginkgo' (the genus) + '-aceae', a standard suffix for plant families.
