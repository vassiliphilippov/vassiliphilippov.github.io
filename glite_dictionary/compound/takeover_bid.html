takeover bid

1. NOUN

/ ˈteɪkˌoʊvɚ bɪd / (AmE), / ˈteɪkˌəʊvə bɪd / (BrE)
an offer to buy enough shares in a company to gain control of it, especially as part of an acquisition
offer to buy control of a company

Examples

The company rejected the takeover bid because it undervalued their shares.
Several investors teamed up to launch a hostile takeover bid for the rival firm.
The unexpected takeover bid caused the company's stock price to soar.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acquisition - the act of obtaining control of another company
