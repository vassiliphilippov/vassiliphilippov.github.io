Great Pyrenees

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡreɪt pɪrəˈniːz / (AmE), / ˌɡreɪt ˌpɪərəˈniːz / (BrE)
a large, heavy-coated white breed of dog, originally used to guard livestock in the Pyrenees mountains
large white livestock-guarding dog breed

Examples

The Great Pyrenees is known for its calm demeanor and protective instincts.
Farmers often use Great Pyrenees to guard their sheep from predators.
Their thick white coats make the Great Pyrenees well adapted to cold mountain climates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Newfoundland - a large, strong breed of working dog from Canada

Etymology

Named after the Pyrenees mountains between France and Spain, where the breed originated.
