fourpenny nail

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔrˌpɛni ˌneɪl / (AmE), / ˈfɔːpəni ˌneɪl / (BrE)
A nail that is 1.5 inches (about 38 mm) long, traditionally used in carpentry and construction.
a 1.5-inch nail used in carpentry

Examples

The old wooden crate was held together by several fourpenny nails.
He asked the hardware store clerk for a box of fourpenny nails for his project.
You should use a fourpenny nail to attach the thin trim to the wall.

Etymology

From 'fourpenny', meaning it cost four pennies per hundred nails in historical British pricing.
