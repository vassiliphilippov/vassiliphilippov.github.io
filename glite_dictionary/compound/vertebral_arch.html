vertebral arch

1. NOUN

/ vəɹˈtiːbɹəl ɑɹtʃ / (AmE), / vɜːˈtiːbɹəl ɑːtʃ / (BrE)
the curved bony structure arising from the back of a vertebra, forming a protective enclosure around the spinal cord
part of a vertebra enclosing the spinal cord
medical

Examples

The vertebral arch protects the spinal cord as it passes through the spinal column.
A defect in the vertebral arch can lead to conditions like spina bifida.
During the dissection, the anatomy students carefully observed each vertebral arch in the spinal column.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vertebra - each of the bones making up the spinal column

Etymology

From Latin 'vertebra' (joint of the spine) and 'arch' (curved structure).
