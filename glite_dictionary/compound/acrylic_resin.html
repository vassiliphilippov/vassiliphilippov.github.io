acrylic resin

1. NOUN

/ əˈkrɪlɪk ˈrɛzɪn / (AmE), / əˈkrɪlɪk ˈrɛzɪn / (BrE)
a synthetic, glassy thermoplastic material derived from acrylic acid and its derivatives, used in casting, molding, coatings, and adhesives
a synthetic thermoplastic material

Examples

The company uses acrylic resin to manufacture durable display cases for museums.
Artists sometimes pour acrylic resin over their paintings to create a glossy, protective finish.
New dental fillings are often made from acrylic resin to ensure both strength and aesthetics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thermoplastic - a substance that becomes pliable or moldable above a specific temperature and solidifies upon cooling

Etymology

From acrylic, referring to derivatives of acrylic acid, and resin, from Latin resina (resin).
