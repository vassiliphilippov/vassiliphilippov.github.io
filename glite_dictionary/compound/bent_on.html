bent on

1. ADJ

/ bɛnt ɑn / (AmE), / bɛnt ɒn / (BrE)
determined to do or achieve something, especially something negative or difficult
determined; resolved to do something

Examples

She was bent on finishing the project, no matter how late it got.
Despite his friends' concerns, he remained bent on traveling alone through the mountains.
The manager seemed bent on implementing the changes despite opposition from the team.

Synset Examples

bent on going to the theater
dead set against intervening
out to win every event

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intent on - resolved or determined to do something; focused
  • Synonym: to set on - decided upon; determined to undertake
  • Antonym: dead set against - completely opposed; determined not to do something

2. ADJ

/ bɛnt ɑn / (AmE), / bɛnt ɒn / (BrE)
determined to do or achieve something, especially something difficult or potentially harmful
determined to do something

Examples

Despite setbacks, she remained bent on winning the competition.
He is bent on making this business succeed, no matter the obstacles.
The protesters were bent on drawing attention to their cause.

Synset Examples

She was bent on finishing the project by Friday.
The engineer was bent on a splice to fix the broken cable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intent on - determined to do something; having a firm plan or purpose
