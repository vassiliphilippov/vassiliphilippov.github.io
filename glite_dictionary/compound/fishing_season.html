fishing season

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪʃɪŋ ˌsiːzən / (AmE), / ˈfɪʃɪŋ ˌsiːzn̩ / (BrE)
the period during which it is legally permitted to catch fish in a particular area or for certain species
period when fishing is legally allowed

Examples

The fishing season in this region lasts from April to September.
Many anglers eagerly await the start of fishing season every spring.
It's illegal to catch trout outside the designated fishing season.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: close season - the period when fishing is prohibited
