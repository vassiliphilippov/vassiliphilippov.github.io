Payne's gray

1. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪnz ˌɡreɪ / (AmE), / ˈpeɪnz ˌɡreɪ / (BrE)
a dark greyish blue pigment or color, commonly used by artists
dark bluish-gray pigment or color

Examples

The artist chose Payne's gray to create the stormy sky in her painting.
He mixed a little Payne's gray with white to achieve a soft, moody background.
Many watercolorists prefer Payne's gray over black for subtle shading.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: gray - the color between black and white; neutral tone
  • Degree Higher: blue-black - a color that is a very dark shade of blue close to black

Etymology

Named after the 18th-century British watercolorist William Payne, who popularized its use.
