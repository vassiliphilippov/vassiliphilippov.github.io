white mallee

1. NOUN

/ waɪt ˈmæli / (AmE), / waɪt ˈmæli / (BrE)
A small, shrubby eucalyptus tree native to Australia, known for its smooth, pale bark and mallee growth form.
small shrubby Australian eucalyptus

Examples

The white mallee grows in dry, sandy soils across parts of southern Australia.
Botanists studied the leaf structure of the white mallee for its adaptation to arid climates.
A group of white mallees provided shelter for local wildlife in the nature reserve.
