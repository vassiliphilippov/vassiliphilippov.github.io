glebe house

1. NOUN

/ ɡliːb haʊs / (AmE), / ɡliːb haʊs / (BrE)
A house provided for a parish priest, especially one on land belonging to the church (glebe).
house provided for a parish priest

Examples

The old glebe house stood beside the village church for over two centuries.
During the restoration, original features of the glebe house were carefully preserved.
Many historic glebe houses are now used as community centers or museums.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parsonage - a house provided for a member of the clergy

Etymology

From 'glebe', meaning church land, and 'house'.
