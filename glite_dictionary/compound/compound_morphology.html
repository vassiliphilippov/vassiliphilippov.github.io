compound morphology

1. NOUN

/ kəmˌpaʊnd mɔːrˈfɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ˌkɒmpaʊnd mɔːˈfɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the branch of linguistics that studies the formation of compound words from simpler words
study of how compound words are formed

Examples

The textbook dedicates an entire chapter to compound morphology in German.
Understanding compound morphology is essential for linguists analyzing word formation processes.
Her research focuses on compound morphology in Indo-European languages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: morphology - the study of the forms of words

Etymology

From Latin 'componere' (to put together) and Greek 'morphē' (form) + '-logy' (study of).
