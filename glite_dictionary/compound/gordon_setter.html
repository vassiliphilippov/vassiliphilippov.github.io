Gordon setter

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɔrdn ˈsɛtər / (AmE), / ˈɡɔːdn ˈsɛtə / (BrE)
a breed of large Scottish dog with a silky black-and-tan coat, known for its abilities as a gundog
Scottish black-and-tan gundog breed

Examples

The Gordon setter is valued for its keen sense of smell and loyal temperament.
They adopted a young Gordon setter from the animal shelter.
Her Gordon setter loves running through the fields during their afternoon walks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: setter - a type of dog bred to locate game birds

Etymology

Named after Alexander Gordon, 4th Duke of Gordon, who promoted the breed in Scotland in the 19th century.
