Snoopers' Charter

1. NOUN

/ ˈsnuːpərz ˈtʃɑːrtər / (AmE), / ˈsnuːpəz ˈtʃɑːtə / (BrE)
an informal term for a UK law that greatly expands government surveillance powers, allowing authorities extensive rights to monitor online activity and communications
UK law expanding surveillance powers
informal british legal

Examples

Privacy groups have launched a campaign to repeal the Snoopers' Charter.
Debate over the Snoopers' Charter highlights concerns about government overreach.
Many viewed the Snoopers' Charter as a threat to internet privacy.

Synset Examples

Critics argue that the Snoopers' Charter infringes on personal freedoms.
Many citizens are concerned about the implications of the Snoopers' Charter.
The new law was dubbed the Snoopers' Charter by privacy advocates.

Etymology

A nickname for the Investigatory Powers Act 2016 in the UK, with 'snooper' meaning someone who secretly tries to find out information.
