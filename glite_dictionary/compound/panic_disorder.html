panic disorder

1. NOUN

/ ˈpænɪk dɪˌsɔrdər / (AmE), / ˈpænɪk dɪˌsɔːdə / (BrE)
A mental health condition marked by recurrent, unexpected panic attacks, often accompanied by intense fear and physical symptoms.
mental health disorder with panic attacks
medical

Examples

She was diagnosed with panic disorder after experiencing sudden episodes of intense fear.
Treatment for panic disorder often includes therapy and sometimes medication.
People living with panic disorder may avoid places where they've had attacks before.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anxiety disorder - a category of mental disorders involving excessive anxiety or fear
