Saint Elias Mountains

1. PROPN

/ seɪnt iˈlaɪ.əs ˈmaʊn.tənz / (AmE), / seɪnt iˈlaɪ.əs ˈmaʊn.tɪnz / (BrE)
a range of mountains between southern Alaska and the Yukon Territory, known for containing some of the highest peaks in North America
mountain range in Alaska and Yukon

Examples

The Saint Elias Mountains are home to Mount Saint Elias, the second-highest peak in both the United States and Canada.
Glaciers cover much of the Saint Elias Mountains, creating a dramatic landscape.
Many climbers visit the Saint Elias Mountains each year for challenging expeditions.

Etymology

Named after Mount Saint Elias, which in turn is named after Saint Elias (Elijah).
