family Carpinaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ˌkɑːrpɪˈneɪsi.iː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli ˌkɑːpɪˈneɪsɪ.iː / (BrE)
a family of flowering plants including the genera Carpinus, Ostrya, and Ostryopsis, known commonly as the hornbeam family
plant family including hornbeams and hop-hornbeams

Examples

The family Carpinaceae includes several deciduous tree species found in temperate regions.
Some botanists classify hop-hornbeams under the family Carpinaceae rather than Betulaceae.
Recent studies have revised the members belonging to the family Carpinaceae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hornbeam - deciduous trees of the Carpinus genus

Etymology

From Carpinus (the hornbeam genus) + Latin botanical family suffix -aceae.
