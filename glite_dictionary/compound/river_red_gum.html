river red gum

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɪvər ˈrɛd ɡʌm / (AmE), / ˈrɪvə ˈrɛd ɡʌm / (BrE)
a large Australian eucalyptus tree (Eucalyptus camaldulensis) growing mainly along rivers, valued for its durable reddish timber used in heavy construction
an Australian eucalyptus tree with durable red wood

Examples

The river red gum is commonly found along Australia's waterways, providing vital habitat for wildlife.
They used timber from the river red gum to build strong beams for the bridge.
A majestic river red gum shaded the banks of the river, its massive roots exposed by the current.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eucalyptus - a genus of trees including many species commonly known as gum trees
