squash bug

1. NOUN

/ skwɑʃ bʌɡ / (AmE), / skwɒʃ bʌɡ / (BrE)
a large North American insect that feeds on the sap of squash and related plants, often causing damage to crops
North American insect pest of squash plants

Examples

The squash bug can quickly destroy an entire crop if left untreated.
Gardeners often inspect the underside of leaves for signs of squash bugs in summer.
They found several squash bugs clustered on the stems of their pumpkin plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stink bug - an insect of the family Pentatomidae, often producing a foul odor as defense
