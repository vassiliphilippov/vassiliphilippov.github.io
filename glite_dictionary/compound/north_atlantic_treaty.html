North Atlantic Treaty

1. NOUN

/ nɔrθ ætˈlæntɪk ˈtriːti / (AmE), / nɔːθ ətˈlæntɪk ˈtriːti / (BrE)
the treaty signed in 1949 by 12 nations establishing the North Atlantic Treaty Organization (NATO), providing mutual defense and cooperation.
treaty establishing NATO in 1949

Examples

The North Atlantic Treaty was signed in Washington, D.C. in April 1949.
Under the North Atlantic Treaty, member countries pledge to defend each other against attack.
The North Atlantic Treaty laid the groundwork for the creation of NATO.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: NATO - the North Atlantic Treaty Organization, an intergovernmental military alliance
