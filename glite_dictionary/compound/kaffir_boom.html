kaffir boom

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæfər buːm / (AmE), / ˈkæfə bʊm / (BrE)
a small, semi-evergreen tree of eastern South Africa (Erythrina lysistemon), noted for its broad, spreading form, orange-scarlet flowers, and small coral-red seeds; its light, soft wood is used for fence posts and shingles.
South African tree with orange-scarlet flowers
offensive

Examples

The brightly blooming kaffir boom attracts birds and insects throughout the spring in South Africa.
Farmers often use timber from the kaffir boom for making durable fence posts.
A row of kaffir booms lines the rural road, their flowers vivid against the dry grass.

Etymology

From Afrikaans 'kafferboom', literally 'Kaffir tree'. The word 'kaffir' is now considered offensive.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkæfər buːm / (AmE), / ˈkæfə bʊːm / (BrE)
A small semi-evergreen tree native to South Africa, known for its dense clusters of bright scarlet flowers and red seeds.
South African tree with scarlet flowers

Examples

The kaffir boom lights up the garden with its vibrant blossoms each spring.
Many birds are attracted to the kaffir boom trees for their nectar-rich flowers.
The luscious branches of the kaffir boom are commonly seen along South African streets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coral tree - Any tree species in the genus Erythrina, known for their colorful flowers

Etymology

From Afrikaans 'kafferboom', meaning 'Kaffir tree'; 'kaffir' is now considered offensive in South Africa, but the term here is botanical.
