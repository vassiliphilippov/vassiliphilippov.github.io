wokeanese jargon

1. NOUN

/ ˌwoʊkəˈniz ˈdʒɑːrɡən / (AmE), / ˌwəʊkəˈniːz ˈdʒɑːɡən / (BrE)
language or terminology associated with social justice movements and activism, often characterized by contemporary progressive concepts and phrasing.
language of social justice activism
informal

Examples

He found it hard to follow the wokeanese jargon in the online discussion.
The article was filled with wokeanese jargon that left many readers confused.
Some criticize the use of wokeanese jargon for making conversations less accessible.

Synset Examples

He struggled to understand the wokeanese jargon being used.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jargon - specialized language of a particular group

Etymology

Formed from 'woke' (alert to social injustices, from African-American vernacular) and '-anese' (as in 'Japanese') suggesting a distinctive manner of speech; 'jargon' from Old French 'jargoun'.
