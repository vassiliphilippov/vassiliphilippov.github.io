heat exhaustion

1. NOUN

/ hit ɪɡˈzɑːs.tʃən / (AmE), / hiːt ɪɡˈzɔːs.tʃən / (BrE)
a condition resulting from excessive loss of body fluids and electrolytes due to prolonged exposure to high temperatures, causing symptoms such as dizziness, nausea, and weakness
condition from loss of fluids due to heat
medical

Examples

The marathon runner collapsed from heat exhaustion after several hours in the sun.
If you feel symptoms of heat exhaustion, such as dizziness or nausea, it's important to rest and stay hydrated.
Workers are at risk of heat exhaustion during long shifts in high temperatures.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: heat stroke - a more severe condition caused by overheating of the body
