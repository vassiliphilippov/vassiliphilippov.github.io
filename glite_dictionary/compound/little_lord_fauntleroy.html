Little Lord Fauntleroy

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɪtl ˌlɔrd ˈfɔnt.ləˌrɔɪ / (AmE), / ˌlɪtl ˌlɔːd ˈfɔːntlɪˌrɔɪ / (BrE)
a boy who is excessively polite, refined, or well-dressed, often to the point of being seen as affected or prissy
excessively polite or well-dressed boy

Examples

Everyone thought the new student was a Little Lord Fauntleroy because of his polished manners and smart clothes.
He dressed so formally for the party that his friends teased him, calling him Little Lord Fauntleroy.
The teacher praised Jake for his Little Lord Fauntleroy attitude and respectful behavior.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dandy - a man who places particular importance upon physical appearance, refined language, and leisurely hobbies
  • Synonym: prig - a self-righteously moralistic person who behaves as if superior to others

Etymology

Named after the character in Frances Hodgson Burnett's novel 'Little Lord Fauntleroy' (1886).
