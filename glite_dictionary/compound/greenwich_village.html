Greenwich Village

1. PROPN

/ ˌɡrɛnɪtʃ ˈvɪlɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌɡrɛnɪdʒ ˈvɪlɪdʒ / (BrE)
a residential neighborhood in lower Manhattan, New York City, known for its historic role as an artists' and writers' community.
district in Manhattan, New York City

Examples

She moved to Greenwich Village in the 1960s to pursue a career in art.
Many famous musicians once lived and performed in Greenwich Village.
The bohemian atmosphere of Greenwich Village continues to attract creative people today.

Etymology

Named after the village of Greenwich in London, England; adopted by the area during early settlement.
