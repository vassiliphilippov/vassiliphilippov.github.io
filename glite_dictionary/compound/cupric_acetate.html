cupric acetate

1. NOUN

/ ˈkjuːprɪk ˈæsəˌteɪt / (AmE), / ˈkjuːprɪk ˈasɪteɪt / (BrE)
a blue or green crystalline powder, used as a pigment and in chemistry
a blue or green pigment used in chemistry
medical

Examples

Artists sometimes use cupric acetate to create unique blue-green hues in their paintings.
Cupric acetate is often found in chemistry labs for various analytical purposes.
In restoration work, traces of cupric acetate can help identify ancient painting techniques.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: copper(II) acetate - another name for cupric acetate, a blue-green chemical compound

Etymology

From 'cupric', relating to copper (from Latin 'cuprum'), and 'acetate', from Latin 'acetum' meaning vinegar.
