genus Caladium

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs kəˈleɪ.di.əm / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs kəˈleɪ.di.əm / (BrE)
a small genus of tropical South American tuberous perennial plants, known for their large, variously colored leaves
genus of tropical plants with colorful leaves

Examples

Gardeners often choose genus Caladium varieties for their strikingly colorful leaves.
The genus Caladium includes several popular ornamental plants used for landscaping.
Researchers are studying the unique pigmentation in the leaves of genus Caladium.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Caladium - a plant of the genus Caladium, known for its colorful foliage

Etymology

From Latin 'caladium', the plant's scientific name, of unknown origin.
