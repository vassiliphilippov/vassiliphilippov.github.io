closed-end investment company

1. NOUN

/ kloʊzd ɛnd ɪnˈvɛstmənt ˈkʌmpəni / (AmE), / kləʊzd end ɪnˈvɛstmənt ˈkʌmpəni / (BrE)
A regulated investment company that issues a fixed number of shares, which are traded on a stock exchange.
regulated company with fixed shares traded on stock exchange

Examples

A closed-end investment company typically has its shares traded on major stock exchanges.
Investors may choose a closed-end investment company for exposure to diverse asset classes.
Unlike mutual funds, a closed-end investment company does not issue new shares after its initial offering.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: open-end investment company - an investment company that issues and redeems shares at any time
