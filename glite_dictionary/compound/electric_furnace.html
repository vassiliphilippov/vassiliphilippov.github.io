electric furnace

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈlɛktrɪk ˈfɜrnɪs / (AmE), / ɪˈlɛktrɪk ˈfɜːnɪs / (BrE)
a furnace in which heat is generated by an electric current rather than by burning fuel
furnace heated by electric current

Examples

The steel mill uses an electric furnace to produce high-quality alloys efficiently.
An electric furnace can reach much higher temperatures than a traditional gas furnace.
Replacing the old boiler with an electric furnace reduced their emissions significantly.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: furnace - an enclosed structure for heating materials
