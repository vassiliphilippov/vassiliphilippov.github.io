basset horn

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæsɪt hɔrn / (AmE), / ˈbæsɪt hɔːn / (BrE)
a tenor member of the clarinet family, pitched in F below the B-flat clarinet, often used in orchestral and chamber music
tenor clarinet pitched in F

Examples

The Mozart Requiem features a beautiful part for the basset horn.
Clara played a solo on her basset horn during the concert.
Several classical composers wrote music specifically for the basset horn in their ensembles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clarinet - a woodwind instrument with a single-reed mouthpiece, part of the same family

Etymology

From Italian 'corno di bassetto', meaning 'little bass horn'.
