genus Crataegus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs krəˈtiːɡəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs krəˈtiːɡəs / (BrE)
A genus of thorny shrubs and small trees in the rose family, commonly known as hawthorns, native to temperate northern regions.
a genus of thorny shrubs or small trees (hawthorn)

Examples

The genus Crataegus includes hundreds of species commonly known as hawthorns.
Researchers studied the medicinal properties of berries from the genus Crataegus.
Many gardens feature trees from the genus Crataegus for their attractive blossoms and fruit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hawthorn - common name for plants in the Crataegus genus

Etymology

From Latin 'Crataegus', the classical name for hawthorn, from Greek 'krataigos' (hawthorn).
