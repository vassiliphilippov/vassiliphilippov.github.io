class Deuteromycetes

1. NOUN

/ klæs ˌduːt̬əroʊmaɪˈsiːtiːz / (AmE), / klɑːs ˌdjuːtərəʊmaɪˈsiːtiːz / (BrE)
a former classification of fungi, coextensive with the subdivision Deuteromycota, comprising fungi for which no sexual reproductive stage is known
group of fungi with no known sexual stage

Examples

Many plant pathogens belong to the class Deuteromycetes due to the absence of a known sexual stage.
Before molecular studies, the class Deuteromycetes was widely used in fungal taxonomy.
Students often find the class Deuteromycetes challenging because of its artificial grouping.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Deuteromycota - a subdivision of fungi with no known sexual stage

Etymology

From Greek 'deuteros' (second) + Modern Latin 'mycetes' (fungi).
