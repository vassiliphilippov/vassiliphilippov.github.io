death camp

1. NOUN

/ dɛθ kæmp / (AmE), / dɛθ kamp / (BrE)
A concentration camp where prisoners are systematically killed or are likely to die due to harsh conditions, especially those established by Nazi Germany during World War II.
concentration camp where death is systematic

Examples

Many historians have studied the atrocities committed at Nazi death camps during World War II.
Survivors of the death camp shared their harrowing experiences with the audience.
The discovery of a hidden death camp shocked the entire world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: concentration camp - camp for holding large numbers of prisoners, usually in harsh conditions
