Schrodinger wave equation

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃroʊdɪŋər weɪv ɪˈkweɪʒən / (AmE), / ˈʃrɜːdɪŋə weɪv ɪˈkweɪʒ(ə)n / (BrE)
the fundamental equation in quantum mechanics that describes how the quantum state of a physical system changes over time
main equation of quantum mechanics

Examples

The Schrodinger wave equation is a cornerstone of modern quantum mechanics.
Using the Schrodinger wave equation, physicists can predict the behavior of particles at the atomic scale.
The solutions to the Schrodinger wave equation are essential for understanding chemical bonding.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wave function - a mathematical function describing the quantum state of a system

Etymology

Named after Austrian physicist Erwin Schrödinger (1887–1961), who formulated it in 1925.
