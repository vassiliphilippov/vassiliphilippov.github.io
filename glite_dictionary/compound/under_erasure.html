under erasure

1. ADJ

/ ˈʌndɚ ɪˈreɪʒɚ / (AmE), / ˈʌndə ɪˈreɪʒə / (BrE)
(of a word or phrase) crossed out, deleted, or marked as cancelled, but still visible in the text, often to indicate its problematic or provisional status.
crossed out but still visible; marked as cancelled

Examples

In the manuscript, the term 'truth' appears under erasure to show its contested meaning.
The editor advised noting outdated terminology under erasure rather than removing it completely.
Key concepts in the essay were written under erasure to highlight uncertainty.

Synset Examples

The incorrect answers were marked under erasure for clarity.
The teacher noted the mistakes under erasure on the test.
His name was written under erasure, indicating a change.

Etymology

Translates French 'sous rature,' a term popularized by philosopher Jacques Derrida in literary theory.
