genus Irena

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs aɪˈriː.nə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ɪˈriː.nə / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Irenidae, comprising the fairy bluebirds.
genus of fairy bluebirds

Examples

The genus Irena is best known for its vibrant, brilliantly colored fairy bluebirds.
Recent research has classified several Southeast Asian birds within the genus Irena.
Birdwatchers often seek out members of the genus Irena because of their striking appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Irenidae - family of birds that includes the fairy bluebirds, with genus Irena as the type genus

Etymology

From New Latin *Irena*, assigned in modern taxonomy; genus name introduced by English ornithologist Edward Blyth (1842).
