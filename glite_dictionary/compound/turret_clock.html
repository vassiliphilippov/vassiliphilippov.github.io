turret clock

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɝː.ət klɑk / (AmE), / ˈtʌr.ɪt klɒk / (BrE)
a large clock with multiple dials, usually mounted in a tower, designed to show the time in all directions
large clock with several dials in a tower

Examples

The old church still boasts a functioning turret clock that chimes every hour.
Engineers carefully restored the turret clock atop the city hall to its former glory.
Each face of the turret clock was visible from a different street in the town square.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tower clock - a clock mounted on or in a tower

Etymology

From 'turret' (small tower) and 'clock'; first used in reference to public clocks installed in church towers and other buildings.
