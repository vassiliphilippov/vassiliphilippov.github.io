President Ford

1. PROPN

/ ˈprɛzɪdənt fɔrd / (AmE), / ˈprɛzɪdənt fɔːd / (BrE)
the 38th President of the United States, who served from 1974 to 1977 after the resignation of Richard Nixon
38th President of the United States (1974–1977)

Examples

President Ford took office following the resignation of Richard Nixon in 1974.
Many historians credit President Ford with restoring public trust after the Watergate scandal.
The library is named after President Ford and displays artifacts from his presidency.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Gerald Ford - personal name of the 38th U.S. President
  • Degree Lower: President Nixon - the 37th President of the United States whom Ford succeeded
  • Degree Higher: President Carter - the 39th President of the United States who succeeded Ford
