brier-wood

1. NOUN

/ ˈbraɪər wʊd / (AmE), / ˈbraɪə wʊd / (BrE)
Wood from the hard, woody root of the briar (Erica arborea), often used to make tobacco pipes.
wood from the briar root, used for pipes

Examples

The craftsman carved a beautiful pipe from brier-wood for his collection.
Many smokers prefer brier-wood pipes because they are durable and heat-resistant.
In the shop window, several brier-wood pipes were displayed among other smoking accessories.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: briar - a prickly shrub, especially Erica arborea

Etymology

From 'brier', referring to the briar shrub, and 'wood'.
