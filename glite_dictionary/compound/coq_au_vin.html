coq au vin

1. NOUN

/ koʊk oʊ ˈvæn / (AmE), / kɒk oʊ ˈvæ̃ / (BrE)
A French dish of chicken, onions, mushrooms, and sometimes other ingredients braised in red wine and seasonings.
French chicken braised in red wine

Examples

The chef's coq au vin was tender and full of rich, savory flavors.
On cold evenings, she likes to prepare classic French dishes like coq au vin for her family.
We ordered the coq au vin and a bottle of Burgundy at the bistro.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chicken fricassée - chicken cooked in white sauce with vegetables

Etymology

French, literally 'rooster in wine' (coq 'rooster' + au 'in the' + vin 'wine').
