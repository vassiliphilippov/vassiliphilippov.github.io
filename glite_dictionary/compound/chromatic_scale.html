chromatic scale

1. NOUN

/ kroʊˈmætɪk skeɪl / (AmE), / krəʊˈmatɪk skeɪl / (BrE)
a musical scale consisting of twelve pitches, each a semitone above or below another, including all semitones within an octave
12-note scale with all semitones in an octave

Examples

The composer used a chromatic scale to create a feeling of suspense in the piece.
She practiced ascending and descending the chromatic scale on her piano every day.
A chromatic scale contains all twelve notes within an octave, moving in half steps.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: major scale - a diatonic scale with a specific pattern of whole and half steps
  • Antonym: minor scale - a diatonic scale with a different pattern of intervals than major

Etymology

From Greek 'khrōmatikos' (relating to color), reflecting the colorful possibilities of the scale.
