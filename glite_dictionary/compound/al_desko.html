al desko

1. ADV

/ æl ˈdɛskoʊ / (AmE), / æl ˈdɛskəʊ / (BrE)
While at one's desk, especially referring to eating or drinking during work rather than taking a break.
eating or drinking at one's desk
informal

Examples

Eating lunch al desko can make the workday feel even longer.
She caught up on reports while sipping her coffee al desko this morning.
More employees are choosing to eat al desko rather than leaving the office for lunch.

Synset Examples

Having coffee al desko has become a daily ritual for many.
She prefers to work al desko instead of taking a proper break.
I usually eat my lunch al desko while finishing emails.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: al fresco - in the open air, usually referring to eating outdoors

Etymology

Formed in imitation of 'al fresco' (Italian for 'in the fresh air'), blending 'desk' to reflect eating at one's desk.
