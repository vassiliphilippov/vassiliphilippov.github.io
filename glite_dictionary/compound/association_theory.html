association theory

1. NOUN

/ əˌsoʊsiˈeɪʃən ˈθɪəri / (AmE), / əˌsəʊsiˈeɪʃən ˈθɪəri / (BrE)
(psychology) the theory that mental processes operate primarily through the association of ideas or experiences.
theory that mental processes occur by association

Examples

The concept of association theory has influenced the study of learning and memory.
Many early psychologists adopted association theory to explain how habits are formed.
Modern cognitive science builds upon ideas first introduced by association theory.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: associationism - the doctrine that mental processes operate by the association of ideas
