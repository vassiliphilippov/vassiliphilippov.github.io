convolution of Broca

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːnvəˈluːʃən əv ˈbroʊkə / (AmE), / ˌkɒnvəˈluːʃən əv ˈbrəʊkə / (BrE)
the area of the brain in the left hemisphere, usually in the frontal lobe, that functions as the motor speech center in most people
motor speech center in the brain
medical

Examples

Damage to the convolution of Broca often results in difficulties with speech production.
Researchers studied the activity within the convolution of Broca while subjects performed language tasks.
The surgeon carefully avoided the convolution of Broca during the procedure to preserve the patient's speech abilities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Broca's area - region of the brain important for speech production

Etymology

Named after French physician Paul Broca (1824–1880), who identified this brain area; from Latin 'convolutio' (a rolling together).
