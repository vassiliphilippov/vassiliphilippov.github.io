blue jay

1. NOUN

/ blu dʒeɪ / (AmE), / bluː dʒeɪ / (BrE)
a common North American jay, having bright blue plumage and a grey breast
a bright blue North American jay

Examples

We spotted a blue jay perched on the fence, its feathers shimmering in the sunlight.
The loud call of the blue jay is often heard in suburban gardens across eastern North America.
A single blue jay visited our bird feeder every morning last spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jay - a type of songbird in the crow family
