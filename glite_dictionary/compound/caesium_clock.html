caesium clock

1. NOUN

/ ˈsiːziəm klɑk / (AmE), / ˈsiːziəm klɒk / (BrE)
an extremely accurate type of atomic clock that measures time based on the vibrations of caesium atoms
high-precision atomic clock using caesium atoms

Examples

The national time standard is maintained using a caesium clock in a laboratory.
Scientists rely on the caesium clock to make precise astronomical measurements.
A caesium clock can remain accurate to within a second over millions of years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: atomic clock - a clock that uses the vibrations of atoms to measure time very accurately

Etymology

From caesium, the chemical element used in the device, and clock.
