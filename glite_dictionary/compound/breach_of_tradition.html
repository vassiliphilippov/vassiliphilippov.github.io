breach of tradition

1. NOUN

/ briːtʃ əv trəˈdɪʃən / (AmE), / briːtʃ əv trəˈdɪʃən / (BrE)
An act of violating or breaking away from established customs or conventions.
violation of established customs

Examples

The artist faced criticism for his bold breach of tradition in his latest exhibition.
Some see the use of modern technology at the ceremony as a necessary breach of tradition.
Her breach of tradition was met with both admiration and outrage from the community.

Synset Examples

He believed there was honor in the breach of tradition.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: observe tradition - to follow and adhere to established customs
