New World anteater

1. NOUN

/ nu wɝld ˈæntiɚ / (AmE), / njuː wɜːld ˈæntiːə / (BrE)
any of several toothless, tropical American mammals of the family Myrmecophagidae that feed on ants and termites
American mammal that eats ants and termites

Examples

The New World anteater uses its long, sticky tongue to capture ants from their nests.
Unlike other mammals, the New World anteater has no teeth but a powerful sense of smell.
Conservationists studied the habitat of the New World anteater in the South American rainforest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anteater - mammal that eats ants and termites
