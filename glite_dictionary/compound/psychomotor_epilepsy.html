psychomotor epilepsy

1. NOUN

/ ˌsaɪkoʊˈmoʊtər ˌɛpɪˈlɛpsi / (AmE), / ˌsʌɪkəʊˈməʊtə ˌɛpɪˈlɛpsi / (BrE)
A form of epilepsy marked by episodes of impaired consciousness, amnesia, and sometimes involuntary, purposeful movements and hallucinations.
epilepsy with impaired awareness and movement
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with psychomotor epilepsy after experiencing episodes of confusion and automatic movements.
During a psychomotor epilepsy attack, she had no memory of moving her arms and legs.
Doctors observed that psychomotor epilepsy often involves brief periods of amnesia and hallucinations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: temporal lobe epilepsy - A form of epilepsy originating from the temporal lobe, often similar to psychomotor epilepsy
  • Antonym: absence seizure - A type of seizure involving brief lapses in consciousness

Etymology

From Greek 'psyche' (mind) + 'motor' (movement), relating to both mental and physical activity.
