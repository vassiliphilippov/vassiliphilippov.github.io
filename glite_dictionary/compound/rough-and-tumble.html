rough-and-tumble

1. ADJ

/ ˌrʌf ənd ˈtʌmbəl / (AmE), / ˌrʌf ənd ˈtʌmbəl / (BrE)
characterized by disorderly action, energetic activity, or disregard for rules or conventions
disorderly and energetic; unrestrained

Examples

The debate quickly turned rough-and-tumble as candidates interrupted each other and shouted their points across.
He preferred the rough-and-tumble world of street basketball to playing in formal competitions.
Many were shocked by the rough-and-tumble tactics used in the campaign.

Synset Examples

a rough-and-tumble fight
rough-and-tumble politics
undisguised bare-knuckle capitalism

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unruly - not orderly or controlled; disorderly
  • Antonym: orderly - well-organized and following rules

Etymology

Formed from the words 'rough' and 'tumble', originally used to describe free-for-all brawls in the 19th century.
