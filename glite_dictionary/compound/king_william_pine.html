King William pine

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɪŋ ˈwɪljəm paɪn / (AmE), / ˌkɪŋ ˈwɪljəm paɪn / (BrE)
an evergreen tree of the Tasmanian mountains, noted for its sharp-pointed, inward-curving leaves
evergreen tree native to Tasmania

Examples

The King William pine is unique to the mountainous regions of Tasmania.
Researchers studied the habitat of the King William pine to better understand its ecological role.
Many hikers admire the distinctive leaves of the King William pine during their trips through the Tasmanian wilderness.
