battle fleet

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæt̬l ˌfliːt / (AmE), / ˈbætl ˌfliːt / (BrE)
a group of warships organized and prepared for combat, especially as a single operational unit
group of warships ready for combat

Examples

The admiral ordered the entire battle fleet to assemble off the coast before dawn.
During the First World War, the British battle fleet played a crucial role at the Battle of Jutland.
A massive battle fleet steamed across the ocean, signaling the start of the conflict.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: naval fleet - a fleet of ships belonging to a navy
  • Synonym: armada - a large fleet of warships
