simple fraction

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪmpəl ˈfrækʃən / (AmE), / ˈsɪmpəl ˈfrækʃən / (BrE)
A fraction in which both the numerator and the denominator are integers.
fraction with integer numerator and denominator

Examples

In mathematics, a simple fraction like 3/4 is easy for students to understand.
Each simple fraction can be represented as a decimal or a percentage.
The teacher asked the class to reduce the simple fraction to its lowest terms.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: improper fraction - fraction where numerator is greater than or equal to denominator
  • Degree Higher: mixed number - number consisting of an integer and a proper fraction
  • Antonym: complex fraction - fraction where numerator or denominator is itself a fraction
