bleaching earth

1. NOUN

/ ˈbliːtʃɪŋ ɜːrθ / (AmE), / ˈbliːtʃɪŋ ɜːθ / (BrE)
a fine clay with adsorptive properties used to remove coloring and impurities from oils, especially in industrial processing
adsorbent clay used to remove colors from oils

Examples

The refinery used bleaching earth to purify vegetable oil for commercial sale.
After treatment with bleaching earth, the crude oil became significantly lighter in color.
Suppliers recommended different grades of bleaching earth to achieve optimal results in the filtration process.
