Asia Minor

1. PROPN

/ ˌeɪʒə ˈmaɪnɚ / (AmE), / ˌeɪʒə ˈmaɪnə / (BrE)
a peninsula in southwestern Asia forming the Asian part of Turkey, largely corresponding to Anatolia
peninsula in southwestern Asia, part of Turkey

Examples

Many ancient Greek settlements were established in Asia Minor.
The geography of Asia Minor played a crucial role in the spread of early Christianity.
Historians often refer to Asia Minor when discussing the Byzantine Empire.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Anatolia - the peninsula of western Asia constituting most of Turkey; another name for Asia Minor

Etymology

From Latin 'Asia Minor' meaning 'Lesser Asia', used to distinguish it from Greater Asia.
