Cornish pasty

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔrnɪʃ ˈpæsti / (AmE), / ˈkɔːnɪʃ ˈpasti / (BrE)
a baked pastry case filled with meat and vegetables, typically crimped along one side, originating from Cornwall, England
filled pastry from Cornwall

Examples

We ordered a traditional Cornish pasty from the bakery for lunch.
The Cornish pasty is recognized as a regional delicacy in the United Kingdom.
Her grandmother taught her how to make an authentic Cornish pasty with beef and potatoes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pasty - a baked pastry case with a savory filling

Etymology

From Cornish, referring to Cornwall, England, and 'pasty', Middle English for a pie or pastry.
