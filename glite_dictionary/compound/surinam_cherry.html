Surinam cherry

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʊrɪnæm ˈtʃɛri / (AmE), / ˈsʊrɪnæm ˈtʃɛri / (BrE)
a tropical Brazilian tree (Eugenia uniflora) widely grown for its small, spicy, red fruit resembling a cherry
tropical tree with small, spicy red fruit

Examples

The Surinam cherry is prized for its tangy, sweet fruit in tropical gardens.
She planted a Surinam cherry bush in her backyard for its ornamental appeal and delicious harvest.
Many people in Florida make jam from the ripe Surinam cherries each spring.

Etymology

Named after Surinam, a country in South America, and 'cherry' for its cherry-like fruit.

3. NOUN

/ ˈsɜrəˌnæm ˈtʃɛri / (AmE), / ˈsjʊərɪnæm ˈtʃɛri / (BrE)
An edible, small, ribbed, red or yellow fruit with tart flavor, borne by a tropical American shrub and valued for its vitamin C.
tart tropical fruit, red or yellow

Examples

The garden boasted a thriving Surinam cherry bush laden with bright red fruits.
She made a refreshing juice from the ripe Surinam cherry harvest.
Children gathered the tart Surinam cherries under the shade of the tropical shrub.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Eugenia uniflora - Scientific name of the plant bearing Surinam cherry
  • Synonym: acerola - Another tropical cherry-like fruit, also rich in vitamin C

Etymology

Named after Surinam (now Suriname), the South American country of origin, combined with 'cherry', referring to the fruit's cherry-like appearance.
