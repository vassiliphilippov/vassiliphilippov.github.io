contrast material

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑn.træst məˈtɪr.i.əl / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.trɑːst məˈtɪə.ri.əl / (BrE)
a substance used in medical imaging that is opaque to x-rays or other forms of radiation, enabling detailed examination of organs or tissues
substance used in medical imaging
medical

Examples

The doctor injected contrast material before starting the CT scan to highlight her blood vessels.
Modern MRI techniques often require contrast material to distinguish healthy tissue from diseased areas.
You may experience a warm sensation after the contrast material is administered during your imaging procedure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contrast agent - a substance used to improve the visibility of internal body structures in imaging
