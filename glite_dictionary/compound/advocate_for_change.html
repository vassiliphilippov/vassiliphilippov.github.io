to advocate for change

1. VERB

/ ˈædvəˌkeɪt fər tʃeɪndʒ / (AmE), / ˈædvəkət fə tʃeɪndʒ / (BrE)
to publicly support or argue in favor of making improvements or reforms
support or argue for improvement

Examples

She tirelessly advocates for change in environmental policies through her organization.
The young leader began by advocating for change in her university's curriculum.
Many citizens gathered to advocate for change at the city council meeting.

Synset Examples

He used his voice to advocate for change in the community.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: campaign for - to work in an organized and active way towards a goal, especially a social or political one
  • Antonym: to oppose - to disapprove of and attempt to prevent, especially by argument
