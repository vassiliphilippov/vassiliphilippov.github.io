poison gas

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɔɪzən ɡæs / (AmE), / ˈpɔɪzn ɡas / (BrE)
a toxic gas, especially one used as a weapon in chemical warfare, that can cause harm or death if inhaled or contacted
toxic gas used as a weapon

Examples

During World War I, soldiers were often exposed to poison gas on the battlefield.
The threat of poison gas attacks led to the widespread use of gas masks.
Factory workers must be trained to handle any accidental release of poison gas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nerve gas - a highly toxic poison gas affecting the nervous system
