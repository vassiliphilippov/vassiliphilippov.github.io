case in point

1. NOUN

/ ˌkeɪs ɪn ˈpɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˌkeɪs ɪn ˈpɔɪnt / (BrE)
an instance or example that clearly illustrates a point or argument being discussed
an illustrative example

Examples

Many teenagers struggle with balancing school and work; my sister is a case in point.
A case in point is last year's project, which also exceeded its budget.
The company has a history of innovation, with their latest product being a case in point.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: example - a thing characteristic of its kind or illustrating a general rule
