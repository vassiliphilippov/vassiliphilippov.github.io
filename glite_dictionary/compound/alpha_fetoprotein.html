alpha fetoprotein

1. NOUN

/ ˈæl.fə ˌfiː.toʊˈproʊ.tiːn / (AmE), / ˈal.fə ˌfiː.təʊˈprəʊ.tiːn / (BrE)
a protein produced mainly by the fetal liver and yolk sac, whose elevated levels in a fetus may indicate neural tube defects such as spina bifida or anencephaly
fetal protein linked to neural tube defects
medical

Examples

The doctor ordered an alpha fetoprotein test as part of the prenatal screening.
Elevated levels of alpha fetoprotein may indicate certain fetal abnormalities.
Scientists are researching how alpha fetoprotein interacts with developing tissues.

Etymology

From Greek 'alpha' (first letter of Greek alphabet) and 'fetoprotein' (protein produced by a fetus).
