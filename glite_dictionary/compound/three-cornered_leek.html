three-cornered leek

1. NOUN

/ θri ˈkɔrnərd lik / (AmE), / θri ˈkɔːnəd liːk / (BrE)
A European species of wild leek with triangular leaves, now naturalized in Great Britain.
wild leek with triangular leaves

Examples

The three-cornered leek often grows in shady woodlands in southern England.
She identified the plant by its triangular leaves and confirmed it was a three-cornered leek.
Gardeners warn that the three-cornered leek can quickly spread and become invasive.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leek - a cultivated plant with flat leaves used as a vegetable
