young Turk

1. NOUN

/ jʌŋ tɝk / (AmE), / jʌŋ tɜːk / (BrE)
A young person, especially one in a group or organization, who is rebellious or advocates for radical change and reform.
a young person advocating radical reform

Examples

The company's board was challenged by a young Turk who demanded sweeping reforms.
As a young Turk in the political party, she insisted on changing outdated policies.
In every generation, a few young Turks push for innovation and progress within their fields.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: radical - a person who advocates thorough or complete political or social reform
  • Antonym: conservative - a person who is averse to change and holds traditional values

Etymology

From the name of the Turkish reformist group 'Young Turks' active in the early 20th century Ottoman Empire.

2. NOUN

/ jʌŋ tɝːk / (AmE), / jʌŋ tɜːk / (BrE)
A young person who is eager for change or has radical or innovative ideas, especially in an organization or group.
young radical or reformer

Examples

The young Turk proposed a series of bold reforms to modernize the company.
As a young Turk, she was always pushing for new ways of thinking.
The board was skeptical of the young Turks and their unconventional ideas.

Synset Examples

The young Turk proposed a radical change to our strategy.
As a young Turk, he challenged all the old traditions.
The young Turks in the office are full of innovative ideas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: radical - a person with extreme or progressive political or social views
  • Antonym: conservative - a person opposed to change or new ideas

Etymology

Originally referring to members of the early 20th-century Turkish reform movement known as the Young Turks, later generalized in English.
