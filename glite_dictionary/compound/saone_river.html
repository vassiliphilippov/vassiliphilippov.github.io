Saone River

1. PROPN

/ soʊn ˈrɪvər / (AmE), / səʊn ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
a river in eastern France that flows south to join the Rhone, making it the main tributary of the Rhone River
river in eastern France, tributary of the Rhone

Examples

The Saone River flows through the picturesque city of Lyon before joining the Rhone.
Many vineyards are situated along the banks of the Saone River.
A popular river cruise explores the scenic towns by the Saone River in eastern France.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Rhone River - major river in southeastern France and Switzerland

Etymology

From French 'Saône', derived from Latin 'Saona'.
