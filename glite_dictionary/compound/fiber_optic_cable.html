fiber optic cable

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪbɚ ˈɑptɪk ˈkeɪbəl / (AmE), / ˈfaɪbə ˈɒptɪk ˈkeɪbəl / (BrE)
a cable consisting of thin strands of glass or plastic (optical fibers) used to transmit data as pulses of light, enabling high-speed communication
cable for transmitting data as light

Examples

Many internet providers use fiber optic cable to deliver fast connections to homes and businesses.
A fiber optic cable can carry enormous amounts of information over long distances without significant loss.
Technicians spent the weekend repairing the damaged fiber optic cable that connects the two cities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: optical fiber - a thin strand of glass or plastic that carries light signals
