male plug

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪl ˌplʌɡ / (AmE), / ˈmeɪl ˌplʌɡ / (BrE)
an electrical connector with two or three pins designed to be inserted into a socket to make an electrical connection.
electrical connector with pins

Examples

Make sure the male plug is fully inserted into the outlet before switching on the device.
Modern electronics typically use a standard two- or three-prong male plug to connect to power.
If the male plug appears damaged, it should be replaced to avoid electrical hazards.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: female socket - an electrical connector with holes designed to receive the pins of a male plug

Etymology

From 'male' (of or denoting a part fitting into a hollow counterpart) and 'plug' (device for making an electrical connection).
