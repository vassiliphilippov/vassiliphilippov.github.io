flagellated protozoan

1. NOUN

/ fləˈdʒɛl.eɪ.tɪd proʊˈtoʊ.zoʊ.ən / (AmE), / fləˈdʒɛl.ɪ.eɪ.tɪd prəʊˈtəʊ.zəʊ.ən / (BrE)
a protozoan organism that moves using one or more whip-like flagella; some are free-living, while others can cause disease in humans and animals
protozoan with one or more flagella
medical

Examples

The disease is caused by a flagellated protozoan that infects the intestines.
Scientists observed the rapid movement of the flagellated protozoan under the microscope.
Some flagellated protozoans are known to be harmful parasites in humans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: protozoan - a single-celled microscopic organism of the kingdom Protista

Etymology

flagellated from Latin flagellum (whip), protozoan from Greek protos (first) + zoon (animal).
