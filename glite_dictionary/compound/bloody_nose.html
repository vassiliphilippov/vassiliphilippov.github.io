bloody nose

1. NOUN

/ ˈblʌdi noʊz / (AmE), / ˈblʌdi nəʊz / (BrE)
An injury causing blood to flow from the nose, often used metaphorically to mean a defeat or setback.
bleeding from the nose; figurative defeat

Examples

He left the game with a bloody nose after a rough tackle.
The small company gave the industry giant a bloody nose with its innovative product.
She wiped her bloody nose and tried to smile despite the pain.

Synset Examples

He threatened to give anyone a bloody nose who insulted him.
The boxer promised to give his opponent a bloody nose.
He got a bloody nose from the rough play.
After the fight, he walked in with a bloody nose.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nosebleed - an occurrence of bleeding from the nose
