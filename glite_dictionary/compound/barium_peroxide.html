barium peroxide

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛriəm pəˈrɑksaɪd / (AmE), / ˈbeəriəm pəˈrɒksaɪd / (BrE)
a white, toxic, inorganic powder (BaO₂) obtained by heating barium oxide in air, formerly used as a bleaching agent and for making hydrogen peroxide.
white toxic chemical compound (BaO₂)

Examples

The laboratory handled the barium peroxide with care due to its toxic nature.
Early methods for producing hydrogen peroxide involved reacting barium peroxide with sulfuric acid.
Exposure to barium peroxide can cause serious health problems if proper precautions are not taken.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: hydrogen peroxide - a pale blue liquid used as a bleaching agent and disinfectant
  • Degree Lower: barium oxide - a white hygroscopic compound used in ceramics and glass, precursor to barium peroxide

Etymology

From 'barium', named after the element, and 'peroxide', referring to compounds containing an extra oxygen atom.
