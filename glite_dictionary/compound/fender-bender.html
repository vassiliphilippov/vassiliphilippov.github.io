fender-bender

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɛndər ˌbɛndər / (AmE), / ˈfɛndə ˌbɛndə / (BrE)
a minor collision between motor vehicles causing little or no serious damage
minor car accident
informal american

Examples

Luckily, it was just a fender-bender, so nobody got hurt.
She called her insurance company after a fender-bender in the parking lot.
Even a minor fender-bender can be stressful on a busy morning.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: accident - an event that happens by chance, especially a vehicle collision
  • Degree Higher: crash - a violent collision, typically involving vehicles, with more severe damage

Etymology

From 'fender' (the part of a car over the wheels) and 'bender' (something bent); originated in American English.
