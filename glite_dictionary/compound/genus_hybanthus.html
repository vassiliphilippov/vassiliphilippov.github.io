genus Hybanthus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs haɪˈbænθəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs hɪˈbænθəs / (BrE)
A genus of herbs and small shrubs with white or purple flowers, native to tropical or subtropical regions.
a genus of herbs and small shrubs

Examples

The genus Hybanthus includes several species found in South America.
Many members of the genus Hybanthus are noted for their distinctive purple flowers.
Scientists recently discovered a new species within the genus Hybanthus in the rainforest.

Etymology

From New Latin 'Hybanthus', based on Greek 'hybos' (hump) and 'anthos' (flower).
