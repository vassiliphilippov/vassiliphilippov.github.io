native pomegranate

1. NOUN

/ ˈneɪ.tɪv ˌpɑː.məˈɡræn.ɪt / (AmE), / ˈneɪ.tɪv ˌpɒm.ɪˈɡræn.ɪt / (BrE)
a small Australian tree (Capparis mitchellii) with edible fruit resembling a pomegranate
Australian tree with pomegranate-like fruit

Examples

The native pomegranate thrives in arid regions of Australia and bears fruit in summer.
Aboriginal Australians have long used the native pomegranate as a source of food and medicine.
The tart flavor of the native pomegranate makes it popular in jams and desserts.
