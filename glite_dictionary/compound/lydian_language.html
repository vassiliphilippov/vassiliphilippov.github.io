Lydian language

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪdiən ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈlɪdɪən ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
an extinct Anatolian language once spoken in the region of Lydia, in western Anatolia, during the first millennium BCE.
extinct Anatolian language of Lydia

Examples

Archaeologists discovered inscriptions in the Lydian language at the ancient site of Sardis.
The Lydian language used a unique script that has only been partially deciphered.
Scholars compare the Lydian language to other Anatolian languages to understand its grammar and vocabulary.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Anatolian language - branch of the Indo-European languages including Hittite and Lydian

Etymology

From Lydia (an ancient region in western Anatolia) + English 'language'.
