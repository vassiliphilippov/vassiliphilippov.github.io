absolute ceiling

1. NOUN

/ ˌæb.səˈluːt ˈsiː.lɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌæb.səˈluːt ˈsiː.lɪŋ / (BrE)
the maximum altitude at which an aircraft can sustain horizontal flight under its own power
maximum altitude for horizontal flight

Examples

The pilot noted that the plane was approaching its absolute ceiling as the air grew thinner.
Some lightweight aircraft have a lower absolute ceiling than commercial jets.
Engine performance drops significantly as an aircraft nears its absolute ceiling.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: service ceiling - the maximum altitude at which an aircraft can maintain a specified rate of climb
