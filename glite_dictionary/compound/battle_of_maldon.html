Battle of Maldon

1. PROPN

/ ˈbæt.əl əv ˈmɔl.dən / (AmE), / ˈbæt.əl əv ˈmæl.dən / (BrE)
a battle fought in 991 near Maldon, England, in which Danish invaders defeated the Anglo-Saxons; celebrated in an Old English poem
battle between Danes and Saxons in 991

Examples

The Battle of Maldon was a turning point in Anglo-Saxon resistance against Viking raids.
Many details about the Battle of Maldon survive through the eponymous Old English poem.
Historians often debate the exact location where the Battle of Maldon took place.
