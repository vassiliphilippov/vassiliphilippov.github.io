railway yard

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪl.weɪ jɑrd / (AmE), / ˈreɪl.weɪ jɑːd / (BrE)
An area with a network of railway tracks and sidings used for storing, sorting, and maintaining railway cars and locomotives.
area for storing and maintaining trains

Examples

The old railway yard was transformed into a public park with walking paths and gardens.
Several freight trains were assembled at the railway yard before their departure.
After a long journey, the locomotive was sent to the railway yard for maintenance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marshalling yard - a railway yard for assembling trains
  • Antonym: station - a place where trains regularly stop to load or unload passengers or goods
