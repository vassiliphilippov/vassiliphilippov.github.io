axiomatic system

1. NOUN

/ ˌæksiəˈmætɪk ˈsɪstəm / (AmE), / ˌæksiəˈmatɪk ˈsɪstəm / (BrE)
a set of axioms from which theorems can be logically derived, forming the foundation of a mathematical or logical theory
set of foundational axioms in logic or mathematics

Examples

Every mathematical theory is built upon an axiomatic system that serves as its logical foundation.
Euclidean geometry is based on a well-known axiomatic system.
The completeness of an axiomatic system refers to its ability to derive all true statements within its domain.

Synset Examples

An axiomatic system is said to be consistent if it lacks contradiction (i.e. it is not possible to derive both a statement and its negation from the system's axioms).

Related Terms

  • Synonym: axiom - a statement accepted as true, forming a basis for reasoning

Etymology

From Greek axiōma (that which is thought worthy) and systema (organized whole).
