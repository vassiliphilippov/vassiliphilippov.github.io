lake whitefish

1. NOUN

/ leɪk ˈwaɪtˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / leɪk ˈwaɪtˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
a freshwater fish (Coregonus clupeaformis) native to North America, especially the Great Lakes and areas north to Alaska, valued as a food fish
a freshwater fish of North America

Examples

Fishermen on Lake Michigan catch large numbers of lake whitefish each year.
The restaurant's specialty is smoked lake whitefish served with lemon and dill.
Commercial harvesting of lake whitefish is an important industry in some northern regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whitefish - any of several freshwater fish with pale flesh, especially in the genus Coregonus
