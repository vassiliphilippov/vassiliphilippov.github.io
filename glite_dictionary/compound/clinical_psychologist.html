clinical psychologist

1. NOUN

/ ˈklɪnɪkəl saɪˈkɑlədʒɪst / (AmE), / ˈklɪnɪkəl saɪˈkɒlədʒɪst / (BrE)
A psychologist professionally trained to diagnose and treat mental and emotional disorders, often through therapy or counseling.
mental health therapist specializing in disorders
medical

Examples

After her diagnosis, she began weekly sessions with a clinical psychologist.
My friend became a clinical psychologist to help people cope with anxiety and depression.
The hospital employs several clinical psychologists to support patients' mental health.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: psychologist - an expert or specialist in psychology
  • Degree Higher: psychiatrist - a medical doctor specializing in the diagnosis and treatment of mental illness

Etymology

From 'clinical' (relating to the observation and treatment of patients) and 'psychologist' (from Greek 'psyche' – mind, soul, and '-logia' – study).
