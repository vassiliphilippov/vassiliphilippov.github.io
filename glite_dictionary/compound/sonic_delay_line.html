sonic delay line

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɑːnɪk dɪˈleɪ laɪn / (AmE), / ˈsɒnɪk dɪˈleɪ laɪn / (BrE)
a device or method that delays a signal by using the time taken for sound waves to travel through a medium
device delaying signal with sound waves

Examples

The sonic delay line is commonly used in early analog signal processing.
Engineers chose a sonic delay line to ensure the proper timing of the signals.
In the laboratory, the sonic delay line helped synchronize the audio recording.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: delay line - a device that temporarily stores a signal for a set period
