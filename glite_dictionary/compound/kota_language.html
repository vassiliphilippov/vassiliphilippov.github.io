Kota language

1. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊtə ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈkəʊtə ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
a Dravidian language spoken by the Kota people of the Nilgiri Hills in southern India.
Dravidian language of the Kota people

Examples

Only a few hundred people still speak the Kota language today.
Linguists are working to document the Kota language before it disappears.
The Kota language has unique phonetic features compared to other Dravidian languages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Toda language - Dravidian language spoken by the Toda people, closely related to Kota
  • Degree Higher: Dravidian - family of languages spoken mainly in southern India

Etymology

From 'Kota', the name of the indigenous people in the Nilgiri region of India.
