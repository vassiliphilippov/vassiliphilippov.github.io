motor end plate

1. NOUN

/ ˈmoʊtər ˈɛnd ˌpleɪt / (AmE), / ˈməʊtə ˈɛnd ˌpleɪt / (BrE)
the flattened area at the end of a motor neuron that transmits neural impulses to a muscle fiber
region where a nerve meets a muscle
medical

Examples

The motor end plate is essential for effective communication between nerves and muscles.
Damage to the motor end plate can result in muscle weakness or paralysis.
Neurotransmitters are released at the motor end plate to stimulate muscle contraction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neuromuscular junction - the synapse or connection between a motor neuron and a muscle fiber
