drift net

1. NOUN

/ drɪft nɛt / (AmE), / drɪft nɛt / (BrE)
a large fishing net that floats and is carried along by the current to catch fish
large floating fishing net

Examples

Fishermen released a drift net into the sea at dawn to catch the migrating salmon.
Environmental groups are concerned about the impact of drift nets on marine wildlife.
The boat was equipped with a huge drift net that stretched for hundreds of meters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gill net - a fishing net that catches fish by entangling their gills
