takeoff rocket

1. NOUN

/ ˈteɪkˌɔf ˈrɑkɪt / (AmE), / ˈteɪkɒf ˈrɒkɪt / (BrE)
the first stage of a multistage rocket, responsible for launching the vehicle and providing initial thrust.
first stage of a multistage rocket

Examples

The takeoff rocket jettisoned after its fuel was expended, allowing the second stage to ignite.
During the test, engineers closely monitored the performance of the takeoff rocket.
Multistage launch vehicles rely on a powerful takeoff rocket to escape Earth's gravity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: booster - a rocket that provides extra power for the launch phase
