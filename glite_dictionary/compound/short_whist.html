short whist

1. NOUN

/ ʃɔrt wɪst / (AmE), / ʃɔːt wɪst / (BrE)
a card game for four players in two partnerships, with a 52-card pack, where each side scores points for tricks taken over six
historic four-player card game

Examples

Many Victorian novels mention short whist as a common evening pastime.
They gathered around the table to play a lively game of short whist.
Unlike the original version, short whist is played to five points, making the games quicker.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whist - classic trick-taking card game from which short whist is derived
