ming tree

1. NOUN

/ mɪŋ tri / (AmE), / mɪŋ triː / (BrE)
a dwarfed evergreen conifer or shrub, trained and shaped to have flat-topped, asymmetrical branches and grown in a container, often as an ornamental plant
dwarfed ornamental tree grown in a container

Examples

The antique dealer displayed a beautiful ming tree in a ceramic pot on the windowsill.
She carefully pruned her ming tree to maintain its unique, flat-topped shape.
Collectors value an old ming tree for its intricate branches and historical significance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bonsai - a Japanese art of growing miniature trees in containers

Etymology

From the Ming dynasty of China, associated with the art of miniature tree cultivation.

2. NOUN

/ mɪŋ tri / (AmE), / mɪŋ triː / (BrE)
An artificial plant, often made of plastic, stone, or wire, designed to resemble a miniature bonsai tree.
ornamental artificial bonsai

Examples

The living room was decorated with a ming tree on the coffee table.
She collects rare ming trees made from precious stones.
A jade ming tree stood next to the window, sparkling in the afternoon light.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bonsai - a tree or shrub grown artificially small by pruning and training
