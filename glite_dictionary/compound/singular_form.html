singular form

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪŋgjələr fɔrm / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋgjʊlə fɔːm / (BrE)
the form of a word used to refer to one person, thing, or instance, as opposed to more than one
form of a word for one person or thing

Examples

The singular form of 'children' is 'child'.
In English, most nouns add 's' to change from the singular form to the plural.
Always use the singular form of a verb with a singular subject.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: plural form - the form of a word used to refer to more than one person or thing

Etymology

From Latin singularis (single, unique) + English form.
