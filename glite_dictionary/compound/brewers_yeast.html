brewer's yeast

1. NOUN

/ ˈbruːərz jiːst / (AmE), / ˈbruːəz jiːst / (BrE)
A type of yeast used in the fermentation of beer and other alcoholic beverages.
yeast used in brewing beer

Examples

He added brewer's yeast to the wort to start the fermentation process.
Some people take brewer's yeast as a dietary supplement for its nutritional benefits.
Brewer's yeast is essential in the production of traditional ales and lagers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yeast - a microscopic fungus used in baking and brewing

2. NOUN

/ ˈbruːərz jiːst / (AmE), / ˈbruːəz jiːst / (BrE)
A species of yeast (Saccharomyces cerevisiae) used in brewing beer and as a leavening agent in baking.
yeast used in brewing and baking

Examples

She added brewer's yeast to the dough to help it rise quickly.
Supplements made from brewer's yeast are popular sources of B vitamins.
Brewer's yeast plays a crucial role in the fermentation process of beer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: baker's yeast - yeast used mainly in baking as a leavening agent

Etymology

So named for its use by brewers; 'yeast' from Old English 'gist,' related to fermentation.
