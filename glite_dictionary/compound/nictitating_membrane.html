nictitating membrane

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɪktɪˌteɪtɪŋ ˈmɛmˌbreɪn / (AmE), / ˈnɪktɪteɪtɪŋ ˈmɛmˌbreɪn / (BrE)
A transparent or translucent third eyelid present in the eyes of some animals, such as reptiles, birds, and some mammals, which can be drawn across the eye for protection and to moisten it while maintaining visibility.
protective third eyelid in some animals

Examples

Many birds have a nictitating membrane that sweeps across their eyes for protection during flight.
The veterinarian explained that cats have a nictitating membrane that helps keep their eyes moist.
In some reptiles, the nictitating membrane becomes visible when they blink.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: third eyelid - another term for the nictitating membrane

Etymology

From Latin 'nictitare' (to blink) and 'membrana' (membrane).
