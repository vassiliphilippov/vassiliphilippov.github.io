exhaust valve

1. NOUN

/ ɪɡˈzɔst vælv / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzɔːst vælv / (BrE)
A valve in an internal combustion engine through which burnt gases leave the cylinder and enter the exhaust system.
valve for releasing burnt engine gases

Examples

The exhaust valve must be properly timed to ensure efficient engine operation.
A faulty exhaust valve can cause loss of power and increased emissions.
Engineers redesigned the exhaust valve for better heat resistance and durability.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: intake valve - valve that lets the fuel mixture into the engine cylinder
