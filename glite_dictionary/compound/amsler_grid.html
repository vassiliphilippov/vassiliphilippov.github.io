Amsler grid

1. NOUN

/ ˈæmzlər ɡrɪd / (AmE), / ˈamzlə ɡrɪd / (BrE)
a grid of horizontal and vertical lines used for monitoring changes in vision, especially in patients with macular degeneration
vision monitoring grid for macular degeneration
medical

Examples

The doctor gave me an Amsler grid to help monitor my vision at home.
Using the Amsler grid, patients can detect any new distortions or blank spots in their eyesight.
Many optometrists recommend keeping an Amsler grid on your refrigerator for daily self-checks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eye chart - chart used to measure visual acuity

Etymology

Named after Marc Amsler (1891–1968), Swiss ophthalmologist who developed it.
