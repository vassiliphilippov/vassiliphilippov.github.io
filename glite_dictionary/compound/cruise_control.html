cruise control

1. NOUN

/ ˈkruz kənˌtroʊl / (AmE), / ˈkruːz kənˌtrəʊl / (BrE)
a system in a motor vehicle that automatically maintains a steady speed as set by the driver
system maintaining set vehicle speed

Examples

He activated the cruise control to maintain a steady speed on the highway.
With cruise control engaged, long drives become much less tiring.
I appreciate how modern cruise control systems can adjust the speed according to traffic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: speed limiter - a device that prevents a vehicle from exceeding a set speed

2. NOUN

/ ˈkruːz kənˌtroʊl / (AmE), / ˈkruːz kənˌtrəʊl / (BrE)
an automated system in a vehicle that maintains a chosen steady speed without the driver having to keep the accelerator pressed
automated speed control system in vehicles

Examples

With cruise control activated, the long journey became much more relaxing.
She prefers to use cruise control during long stretches of highway driving.
Modern vehicles often come equipped with adaptive cruise control that adjusts speed based on traffic.

Synset Examples

With cruise control engaged, the drive felt effortless.
I set the car to cruise control on the highway.

Etymology

From 'cruise' (to travel smoothly at a moderate speed) and 'control' (the power to direct).
