adverbial of reason

1. NOUN

/ ædˈvɝːbiəl əv ˈriːzən / (AmE), / ədˈvɜːbɪəl əv ˈriːzən / (BrE)
an adverbial phrase or clause that indicates the reason or cause for the action described by the verb
adverbial indicating reason or cause

Examples

He stayed home adverbial of reason because he was feeling sick.
In the sentence 'She apologized for arriving late,' 'for arriving late' is an adverbial of reason.
Teachers often ask students to identify the adverbial of reason in complex sentences.

Synset Examples

It is common for more than one type of adverbial to appear in a sentence, as in Jonathan ran a race for fun last weekend, where there is an adverbial of reason and an adverbial of time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adverbial of purpose - an adverbial indicating the purpose of an action
