Master of Architecture

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæs.tɚ əv ˈɑːrk.əˌtɛk.tʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈmɑː.stər əv ˈɑː.kɪˌtɛk.tʃə / (BrE)
an advanced academic degree awarded for graduate study in architecture, typically required for professional licensure as an architect
advanced degree in architecture

Examples

She earned her Master of Architecture before joining a renowned design firm.
The university offers a competitive Master of Architecture program for aspiring architects.
With a Master of Architecture, he qualified to take the licensing exam for architects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Master of Science - a graduate degree in a scientific discipline
  • Degree Lower: Bachelor of Architecture - an undergraduate degree in architecture
