Congregational Christian Church

1. PROPN

/ ˌkɑːŋɡrɪˈɡeɪʃənəl ˈkrɪstʃən tʃɜrtʃ / (AmE), / ˌkɒŋɡrɪˈɡeɪʃənəl ˈkrɪstʃən tʃɜːtʃ / (BrE)
a Protestant denomination formed by the merger of the Congregational Church and the Christian Church in the United States
Protestant church formed from a merger

Examples

The Congregational Christian Church played a significant role in the development of Protestantism in America.
After the merger, many members identified themselves with the Congregational Christian Church.
The history of the Congregational Christian Church reflects the union of two distinct traditions.
