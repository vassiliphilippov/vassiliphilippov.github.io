unlatinate term

1. NOUN

/ ʌnˈlæt.ən.eɪt tɜrm / (AmE), / ʌnˈlæt.ɪn.eɪt tɜːm / (BrE)
a word that is not derived from Latin, especially noted for its native or non-classical origins
word not derived from Latin

Examples

In technical writing, using unlatinate terms often results in clearer communication.
The poet deliberately chose unlatinate terms to evoke a more earthy tone.
Some English speakers find unlatinate terms more direct and familiar than their Latinate equivalents.

Synset Examples

She preferred unlatinate terms for their simplicity and clarity.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Latinate term - a word of Latin origin

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'un-' (not) + 'Latinate' (from Latin 'Latinus', meaning Latin) + 'term'.
