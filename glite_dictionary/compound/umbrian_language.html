Umbrian language

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌmbriən ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈʌmbriən ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
an extinct Italic language formerly spoken by the Umbri people in ancient southern Italy
extinct Italic language of ancient Italy

Examples

The Umbrian language provides valuable insights into the early Italic peoples.
Inscriptions in the Umbrian language have been found on ancient bronze tablets.
Linguists compare the Umbrian language to Oscan and Latin to trace historical changes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Oscan language - another extinct Italic language related to Umbrian
  • Synonym: Latin - another Italic language, but survived and became very influential

Etymology

From Latin 'Umbrianus' (of the Umbri) + 'language'.
