inkblot test

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪŋkˌblɑt tɛst / (AmE), / ˈɪŋkˌblɒt tɛst / (BrE)
a psychological test in which subjects are shown bilaterally symmetrical inkblots and asked to describe what they see, used to assess personality and emotional functioning
psychological test using inkblots and interpretation

Examples

The psychologist administered the inkblot test to assess her personality traits.
During the session, he found the inkblot test both intriguing and challenging to interpret.
Many critics debate the reliability of the inkblot test in modern psychological practice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Rorschach test - a specific type of inkblot test developed by Hermann Rorschach

Etymology

From 'inkblot', meaning a blot made by ink, and 'test'; popularized by the Rorschach test in the early 20th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɪŋk.blɑt tɛst / (AmE), / ˈɪŋk.blɒt tɛst / (BrE)
a psychological test in which a person’s interpretations of a series of inkblots are analyzed to assess their personality and emotional functioning
psychological test with inkblots

Examples

The psychologist administered an inkblot test during the session to learn more about the patient’s mind.
Many people have seen images from the famous inkblot test in books and films.
Her responses to the inkblot test revealed interesting patterns in her thinking.

Synset Examples

The therapist used an inkblot test to gauge his thoughts.
