right of first publication

1. NOUN

/ raɪt əv fɜrst ˌpʌblɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / raɪt əv fɜːst ˌpʌblɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the exclusive legal right to publish and sell a literary, musical, or artistic work for the first time
exclusive right to first publish a work
legal

Examples

The author retained the right of first publication for her new novel.
If the manuscript is accepted, the journal will require the right of first publication.
Contracts must specify who holds the right of first publication to avoid later disputes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: copyright - legal right granting exclusive authority to reproduce, publish, or sell a work
