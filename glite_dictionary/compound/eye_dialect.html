eye dialect

1. NOUN

/ aɪ ˈdaɪ.əˌlɛkt / (AmE), / aɪ ˈdaɪ.ə.lekt / (BrE)
The use of deliberately nonstandard spelling to represent the speech of a particular group, especially to suggest colloquial or uneducated language.
nonstandard spelling for local speech

Examples

The author used eye dialect to convey the character's Southern accent in the novel.
Critics argue that using eye dialect can reinforce negative stereotypes about certain groups.
When writers employ eye dialect, they often intend to give characters a sense of authenticity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dialect - a particular form of a language specific to a region or group

Etymology

From 'eye' (visual) + 'dialect' (form of language), referring to nonstandard spellings seen by the eye rather than reflecting actual pronunciation.
