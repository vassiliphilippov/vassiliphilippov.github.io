dotted line

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɑt̬ɪd laɪn / (AmE), / ˈdɒtɪd laɪn / (BrE)
a line consisting of a series of dots or short dashes, used especially to indicate where something should be written or signed on a document
a line made of dots or dashes, esp. for signatures

Examples

Please sign your name on the dotted line at the bottom of the form.
The instructions said to cut along the dotted line to make the mask.
He hesitated before finally agreeing to put his signature on the dotted line.

Synset Examples

just sign on the dotted line

Related Terms

  • Antonym: solid line - an unbroken continuous line
