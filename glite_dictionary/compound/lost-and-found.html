lost-and-found

1. NOUN

/ lɔst ənd faʊnd / (AmE), / lɒst ənd faʊnd / (BrE)
a place in a public area where lost items are kept until owners reclaim them
place for lost items to be reclaimed

Examples

If you leave your umbrella on the train, you can check the lost-and-found at the station.
She handed the wallet she found in the park to the lost-and-found.
All unclaimed items are stored in the school's lost-and-found until someone picks them up.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lost property office - office or section in a public place where lost items are kept
