balsam apple

1. NOUN

/ ˈbælsəm ˌæpəl / (AmE), / ˈbɔːlsəm ˌæpəl / (BrE)
a tropical Old World climbing plant (Momordica balsamina) of the gourd family, with yellow flowers and red or orange warty fruit, sometimes cultivated for ornament or medicinal use.
tropical vine with warty red or orange fruit

Examples

The gardener admired the unusual fruit of the balsam apple as it ripened in the greenhouse.
Medicinal extracts from the balsam apple have been used in traditional remedies for centuries.
The bright orange skin of the balsam apple makes it a striking ornamental plant.
