genus Delphinus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs dɛlˈfaɪ.nəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs dɛlˈfɪ.nəs / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Delphinidae, comprising common dolphins and related species.
type genus of dolphins (Delphinidae)

Examples

The genus Delphinus includes the well-known common dolphin.
Marine biologists have long studied the genus Delphinus to understand dolphin behavior.
Taxonomic revisions have altered the classification within the genus Delphinus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Delphinidae - the dolphin family in biological classification

Etymology

From Latin 'Delphinus' meaning 'dolphin', used in biological taxonomy.
