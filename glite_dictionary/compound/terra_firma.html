terra firma

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɛrə ˈfɜrmə / (AmE), / ˌtɛrə ˈfɜːmə / (BrE)
dry, solid ground; the firm ground of the earth's surface, as opposed to sea or air.
solid ground; earth's firm surface

Examples

After months at sea, the sailors were relieved to finally set foot on terra firma.
She stumbled as she stepped off the boat and onto terra firma again.
The hot-air balloon drifted for hours before returning safely to terra firma.

Synset Examples

the plane turned away from the sea and moved back over land
the earth shook for several minutes
he dropped the logs on the ground

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ground - the solid surface of the earth
  • Synonym: land - the solid surface of the earth as distinct from sea or sky
  • Antonym: sea - the expanse of salt water that covers most of the earth's surface

Etymology

From Latin 'terra firma' meaning 'solid earth'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌtɛrə ˈfɜːrmə / (AmE), / ˌtɛrə ˈfɜːmə / (BrE)
dry land; solid ground, as opposed to the sea or air
solid ground; dry land

Examples

After days at sea, the crew was grateful to set foot on terra firma again.
The astronaut longed for a return to terra firma after months in space.
Passengers applauded as the plane touched down and they finally reached terra firma.

Synset Examples

The hikers celebrated their return to terra firma after the trek.
She felt relieved to be back on terra firma after the flight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: land - the solid part of the earth's surface
  • Antonym: sea - the expanse of salt water that covers most of the earth's surface
  • Antonym: water - the part of the earth's surface covered with liquid water

Etymology

From Latin terra firma (solid earth)
