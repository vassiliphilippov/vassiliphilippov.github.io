service entrance

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɝː.vɪs ˌɛn.trəns / (AmE), / ˈsɜː.vɪs ˌɛn.trəns / (BrE)
an entrance in a building intended for use by staff, or for deliveries and removal of refuse, rather than for the main occupants or guests
an entrance for staff or deliveries

Examples

Deliveries should be made through the service entrance at the back of the building.
The hotel staff entered quietly through the service entrance to avoid disturbing guests.
Rubbish is always taken out via the service entrance by the maintenance team.
