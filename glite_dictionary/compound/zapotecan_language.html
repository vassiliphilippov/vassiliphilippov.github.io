Zapotecan language

1. NOUN

/ ˌzæpəˈtiːkən ˈlæŋwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌzæpəˈtiːkən ˈlaŋgwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the indigenous language or group of languages spoken by the Zapotec people of southern Mexico.
indigenous language of the Zapotec people

Examples

Scholars have documented several dialects of the Zapotecan language in southern Oaxaca.
He is learning to translate ancient stories from the Zapotecan language into Spanish.
Many schools in the region now offer classes in both Spanish and Zapotecan language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Zapotec - the indigenous people of Oaxaca, Mexico

Etymology

From Spanish 'zapoteco,' from Nahuatl 'tzapotēcatl' (Zapotec person).
