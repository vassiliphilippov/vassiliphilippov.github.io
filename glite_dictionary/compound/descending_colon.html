descending colon

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈsɛndɪŋ ˈkoʊlən / (AmE), / dɪˈsɛndɪŋ ˈkəʊlən / (BrE)
the section of the large intestine that runs downward from the transverse colon to the sigmoid colon
part of the large intestine between the transverse and sigmoid colon
medical

Examples

The descending colon plays a crucial role in storing the remains of digested food before they move into the sigmoid colon.
Inflammation of the descending colon can cause abdominal pain and changes in bowel habits.
During the surgery, the doctor examined the patient's descending colon for signs of disease.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: colon - part of the large intestine
  • Degree Higher: sigmoid colon - S-shaped part of the large intestine following the descending colon
  • Degree Lower: transverse colon - part of the large intestine running across the abdomen, before the descending colon

Etymology

From Latin 'descendere' (to go down) and 'colon' (section of the large intestine).
