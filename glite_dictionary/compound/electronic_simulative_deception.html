electronic simulative deception

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌlɛkˈtrɑnɪk sɪˈmjəˌleɪtɪv dɪˈsɛpʃən / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛkˈtrɒnɪk sɪˈmjʊlətɪv dɪˈsɛpʃən / (BrE)
the use of electronic means to represent actual or notional friendly capabilities with the aim of misleading hostile forces
military tactic using electronic simulation to mislead enemies

Examples

The unit employed electronic simulative deception to create the illusion of a much larger force presence.
As part of the operation, electronic simulative deception confused enemy sensors about the location of the fleet.
The general credited their success to effective use of electronic simulative deception during the campaign.
