amphibicidal agent

1. NOUN

/ æmˌfɪbɪˈsaɪdəl ˈeɪdʒənt / (AmE), / æmˌfɪbɪˈsaɪdəl ˈeɪdʒənt / (BrE)
a substance specifically formulated to kill amphibians
substance that kills amphibians
medical

Examples

The use of an amphibicidal agent in wetland areas raised environmental concerns.
Researchers tested a new amphibicidal agent to control invasive frog populations.
Accidental exposure to the amphibicidal agent caused harm to native salamanders.

Synset Examples

The scientist developed an amphibicidal agent for pest control.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pesticide - substance used to destroy pests
  • Degree Lower: herbicide - substance that kills plants or weeds
  • Degree Higher: biocide - substance that kills living organisms

Etymology

From 'amphibian' + '-cidal' (relating to killing) + 'agent'.
