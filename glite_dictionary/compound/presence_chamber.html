presence chamber

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɛzəns ˌtʃeɪmbɚ / (AmE), / ˈprɛzəns ˌtʃeɪmbə / (BrE)
a room in a palace or large house where a monarch or person of high rank receives guests or assemblies
state room for receiving guests

Examples

The king greeted foreign dignitaries in his lavish presence chamber.
Portraits of past monarchs lined the walls of the presence chamber.
The queen’s presence chamber was reserved for important ceremonies and receptions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: throne room - a room in a palace where a throne is kept, often used for official ceremonies
  • Synonym: audience chamber - a room where formal audiences or meetings are held with someone of high status
