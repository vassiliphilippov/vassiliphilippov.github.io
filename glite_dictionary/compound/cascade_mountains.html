Cascade Mountains

1. PROPN

/ kæsˈkeɪd ˈmaʊntənz / (AmE), / kæsˈkeɪd ˈmaʊntənz / (BrE)
a mountain range in the northwestern United States, extending through Washington, Oregon, and northern California, known for its volcanic peaks
mountain range in northwestern US

Examples

Heavy snowfall is common in the Cascade Mountains during the winter months.
The hikers planned a week-long trek across the Cascade Mountains.
Mount St. Helens is a famous volcano in the Cascade Mountains.

Etymology

Named after the Cascades Rapids in the Columbia River; 'Cascade' from French 'cascade' (waterfall).
