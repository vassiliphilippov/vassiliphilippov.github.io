glass-cutter

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡlæs ˌkʌtər / (AmE), / ˈɡlɑːs ˌkʌtə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to cut or engrave designs on glass surfaces
person who engraves or cuts glass designs

Examples

The glass-cutter carefully etched an intricate pattern onto the vase.
She apprenticed with a skilled glass-cutter to learn traditional engraving techniques.
Many pieces in the museum were crafted by renowned glass-cutters of the 19th century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: engraver - a person who cuts designs into surfaces such as metal or glass

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡlæs ˌkʌtər / (AmE), / ˈɡlɑːs ˌkʌtə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to cut sheets of glass to required sizes
person who cuts sheets of glass

Examples

The glass-cutter skillfully shaped the pane to fit the window frame.
They called a glass-cutter to repair the broken showcase at the museum.
Becoming a glass-cutter requires precise measurements and a steady hand.
