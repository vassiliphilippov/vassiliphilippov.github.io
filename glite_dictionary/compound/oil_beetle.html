oil beetle

1. NOUN

/ ɔɪl ˈbiːtəl / (AmE), / ɔɪl ˈbiːtl̩ / (BrE)
a type of beetle that secretes an oily substance from its leg joints to deter predators
beetle secreting oily deterrent

Examples

The oil beetle exudes a yellowish oily liquid when threatened.
Gardeners sometimes spot the distinctive oil beetle wandering along footpaths in spring.
Researchers studied the chemical composition of the oil beetle's defensive secretion.
