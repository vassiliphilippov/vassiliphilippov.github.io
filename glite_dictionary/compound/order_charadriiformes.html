order Charadriiformes

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdər kəˌreɪdrioʊˈfɔrˌmiːz / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə kəˌreɪdrɪɒˈfɔːmiːz / (BrE)
A large and diverse order of aquatic birds, including shorebirds and coastal diving birds, commonly found along seacoasts and inland waters.
order of aquatic birds (shorebirds, etc.)

Examples

Many species within the order Charadriiformes migrate thousands of miles each year.
The order Charadriiformes includes birds such as gulls, plovers, and sandpipers.
Researchers observed various feeding behaviors among members of the order Charadriiformes during the study.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin Charadriiformes: 'Charadrius' (a genus of plover) + Latin '-formes' meaning 'shaped' or 'form.'
