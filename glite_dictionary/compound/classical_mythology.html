classical mythology

1. NOUN

/ ˈklæsɪkəl mɪˈθɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ˈklæsɪkəl mɪˈθɒlədʒi / (BrE)
The body of myths originating from ancient Greece and Rome, especially those whose stories and deities were shared or adapted between the two cultures.
Greek and Roman mythological tradition

Examples

Many modern stories take inspiration from classical mythology and its legendary heroes.
Students in literature classes often study classical mythology to understand ancient influences on Western culture.
The depiction of gods in classical mythology often reflects human virtues and flaws.
