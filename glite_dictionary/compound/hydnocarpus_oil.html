hydnocarpus oil

1. NOUN

/ hɪdˈnɑːkɑrpəs ɔɪl / (AmE), / hɪdˈnɒkɑːpəs ɔɪl / (BrE)
an oil obtained from the seeds of trees in the genus Hydnocarpus, especially Hydnocarpus wightiana, formerly used in the treatment of leprosy
oil from Hydnocarpus seeds, used medicinally
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that hydnocarpus oil was once widely used to treat leprosy.
Researchers are studying the properties of hydnocarpus oil for potential modern applications.
The extraction process for hydnocarpus oil involves pressing the seeds of the Hydnocarpus tree.

Etymology

From the genus name Hydnocarpus, derived from Greek 'hydnon' (truffle) and 'karpos' (fruit).
