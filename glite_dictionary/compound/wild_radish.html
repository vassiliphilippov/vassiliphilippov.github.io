wild radish

1. NOUN

/ waɪld ˈrædɪʃ / (AmE), / waɪld ˈrædɪʃ / (BrE)
a Eurasian plant (Raphanus raphanistrum) of the mustard family, often regarded as a weed, having yellow, mauve, or white flowers and podlike fruits
Eurasian weed with yellow, mauve, or white flowers

Examples

Wild radish often invades cereal crop fields, competing with the desired plants for nutrients.
The flowers of the wild radish can range from pale yellow to light mauve, adding color to the meadows.
Gardeners struggle to control wild radish because of its rapid growth and prolific seed production.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: radish - an edible root vegetable of the mustard family
