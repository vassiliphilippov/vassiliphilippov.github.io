walk-in

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɑk ɪn / (AmE), / ˈwɔːk ɪn / (BrE)
a person who arrives or enters a place, especially for a service, without a prior appointment
person arriving without appointment

Examples

The clinic accepts walk-ins as well as scheduled patients.
There was a separate waiting area for walk-ins at the bank.
After her regular client canceled, she managed to fill the slot with a walk-in.

Synset Examples

the emergency room was overrun with walk-ins

Etymology

From the verb 'walk in,' referring to the literal act of entering a place by walking, plus the noun-forming suffix.
