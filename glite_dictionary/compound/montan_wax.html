montan wax

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑnˌtæn wæks / (AmE), / ˈmɒn.tæn wæks / (BrE)
a hard, brittle wax obtained from lignite, used in polishes, lubricants, and as a raw material in various industries.
hard wax obtained from lignite

Examples

The manufacturer uses montan wax to produce high-quality floor polishes.
Due to its high melting point, montan wax is ideal for industrial applications.
He added a small amount of montan wax to the mixture to improve its durability.

Etymology

From German 'Montanwachs', from 'Montan' (relating to mining) and 'Wachs' (wax).
