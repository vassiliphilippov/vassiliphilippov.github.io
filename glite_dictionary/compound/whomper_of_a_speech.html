whomper of a speech

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɑmpər əv ə spiːtʃ / (AmE), / ˈwɒmpə əv ə spiːtʃ / (BrE)
an exceptionally impressive or impactful speech
very impressive speech
informal

Examples

The candidate gave a whomper of a speech at the convention that had everyone talking.
It was a whomper of a speech that inspired the entire team to work harder.
Nobody expected such a whomper of a speech from the usually reserved professor.

Synset Examples

He delivered a whomper of a speech last night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barn-burner - an exceptionally exciting or impressive event or speech

Etymology

From 'whomper' (something very large or impressive), possibly derived from 'whomp' (to hit heavily).
