water flea

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔt̬ɚ ˌfli / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə ˌfliː / (BrE)
A minute freshwater crustacean with a rounded body and transparent shell, which moves by means of branched, hairy antennae.
tiny freshwater crustacean

Examples

The pond was teeming with water fleas, easily visible under the microscope.
Researchers often use water fleas to study environmental pollution in freshwater ecosystems.
The water flea propels itself by rapidly flicking its antennae through the water.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɔtər ˌfli / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə ˌfli / (BrE)
A minute freshwater crustacean with a pear-shaped body and long antennae, often found free-swimming and important in aquatic food chains.
tiny freshwater crustacean

Examples

The water flea is commonly used in scientific research due to its sensitivity to pollutants.
Many fish species feed on the water flea as part of their natural diet.
Under the microscope, the movement of the water flea is fascinating to observe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: copepod - small aquatic crustacean; water fleas are a type
