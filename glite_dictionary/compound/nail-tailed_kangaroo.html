nail-tailed kangaroo

1. NOUN

/ ˈneɪl teɪld kæŋˈɡuːroʊ / (AmE), / ˈneɪl teɪld kæŋˈɡuːrəʊ / (BrE)
a small species of wallaby distinguished by a hard, horny 'nail' at the tip of its tail, native to Australia
small wallaby with a horny tail tip

Examples

The nail-tailed kangaroo is easily identified by the distinctive nail at the tip of its tail.
Researchers are studying the habitat preferences of the nail-tailed kangaroo in outback Australia.
Farmers sometimes spot a nail-tailed kangaroo grazing near cattle during dawn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wallaby - a small to medium-sized marsupial related to kangaroos
