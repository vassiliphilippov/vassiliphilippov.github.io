parasite yew

1. NOUN

/ ˈpærəˌsaɪt juː / (AmE), / ˈpærəsaɪt juː / (BrE)
a rare and endangered parasitic conifer tree native to New Caledonia, growing on the tree Falcatifolium taxoides
rare parasitic conifer of New Caledonia

Examples

The parasite yew is one of the world’s most endangered conifers, with only a few specimens remaining.
Botanists traveled to New Caledonia to study the unique parasite yew in its natural habitat.
Because the parasite yew depends entirely on Falcatifolium taxoides, the two species have a close ecological relationship.
