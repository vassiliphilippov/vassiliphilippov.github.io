genus Mustela

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs mʌsˈtiː.lə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈmʌs.tɪ.lə / (BrE)
the type genus of the family Mustelidae, including minks and weasels
genus of minks and weasels

Examples

The genus Mustela includes animals such as ferrets, weasels, and stoats.
Biologists studied the behavior of several members of the genus Mustela in their natural habitats.
Minks, which belong to the genus Mustela, are known for their sleek, dark fur.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weasel - small carnivorous mammal of the genus Mustela
  • Synonym: mink - semiaquatic mammals of the genus Mustela

Etymology

From Latin 'Mustela' (weasel).
