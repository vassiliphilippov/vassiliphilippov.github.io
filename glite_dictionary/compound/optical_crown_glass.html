optical crown glass

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑptɪkəl kraʊn ɡlæs / (AmE), / ˈɒptɪkəl kraʊn ɡlɑːs / (BrE)
A type of optical glass with low dispersion and a low refractive index, commonly used in lenses and optical instruments.
optical glass with low dispersion and refractive index

Examples

The lens was made with optical crown glass to minimize chromatic aberration.
Manufacturers often use optical crown glass in camera lenses due to its clarity and stability.
Advancements in optical crown glass have improved the quality of scientific instruments.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: flint glass - optical glass with high dispersion and high refractive index

Etymology

Crown refers to a traditional type of glass used since the 17th century; 'optical' specifies its use in optics.
