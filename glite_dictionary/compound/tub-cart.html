tub-cart

1. NOUN

/ tʌb kɑrt / (AmE), / tʌb kɑːt / (BrE)
a light, two-wheeled cart with an underslung axle and two seats, used especially in the 19th and early 20th centuries
light two-wheeled cart with two seats

Examples

They rode to the village in a tub-cart pulled by a sturdy pony.
The old tub-cart in the barn had been unused for decades.
In Victorian times, a tub-cart was a common sight on country lanes.

Etymology

From 'tub' (referring to its rounded shape) and 'cart'.
