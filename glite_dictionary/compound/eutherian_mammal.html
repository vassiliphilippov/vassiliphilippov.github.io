eutherian mammal

1. NOUN

/ juːˈθɪriən ˈmæməl / (AmE), / juːˈθɪəriən ˈmaməl / (BrE)
a mammal of the group Eutheria, characterized by having a placenta; includes all mammals except monotremes and marsupials
placental mammal (not monotremes/marsupials)

Examples

The elephant is the largest living eutherian mammal on land.
Unlike marsupials, a eutherian mammal nourishes its young via a placenta before birth.
Scientists believe that eutherian mammals diversified rapidly after the extinction of the dinosaurs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: placental mammal - mammal with a placenta
  • Antonym: marsupial - mammal with a pouch, not a placental mammal
  • Antonym: monotreme - egg-laying mammal, not a placental mammal

Etymology

From Greek 'eu-' (good, true) + 'therion' (beast).
