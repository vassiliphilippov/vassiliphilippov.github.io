Suez Canal

1. PROPN

/ suˈɛz kəˈnæl / (AmE), / suːˈɛz kəˈnæl / (BrE)
A ship canal in northeastern Egypt that connects the Mediterranean Sea with the Red Sea.
ship canal in Egypt connecting Mediterranean and Red Seas

Examples

The Suez Canal is a crucial waterway for international maritime trade.
Ships were delayed for days after an accident blocked the Suez Canal.
The construction of the Suez Canal was completed in 1869.

Etymology

Named after the Egyptian city of Suez, at its southern end; 'canal' from French, from Latin 'canalis' (channel).
