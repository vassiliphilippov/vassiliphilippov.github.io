photochromic eyeglasses

1. NOUN

/ ˌfoʊtəˌkroʊmɪk ˈaɪˌɡlæsɪz / (AmE), / ˌfəʊtəˌkrəʊmɪk ˈaɪˌɡlɑːsɪz / (BrE)
Eyeglasses fitted with photochromic lenses that automatically darken in response to sunlight and return to clear indoors.
eyeglasses with lenses that darken in sunlight

Examples

I bought a new pair of photochromic eyeglasses for my summer travels.
Photochromic eyeglasses automatically adjust their tint when I walk outdoors.
Doctors recommend photochromic eyeglasses for people sensitive to bright light.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: photochromic lenses - lenses that darken on exposure to sunlight

Etymology

From 'photochromic', from Greek 'phōt-' (light) + 'chrōma' (color).
