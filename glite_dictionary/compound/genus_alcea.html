genus Alcea

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ælˈsiː.ə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ælˈsiː.ə / (BrE)
A genus of tall, erect herbs native to the Middle East, noted for their showy flowers; commonly known as hollyhocks.
genus of flowering plants (hollyhocks)

Examples

Botanists classify the hollyhocks under the genus Alcea.
Many gardens feature species from the genus Alcea for their vibrant, colorful blooms.
The genus Alcea is closely related to the genus Althaea in some plant classifications.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: genus Althaea - a related genus of flowering plants, sometimes considered synonymous
  • Synonym: hollyhock - common name for plants of the genus Alcea

Etymology

From Latin 'Alcea', itself from Greek 'alkéa', used in scientific naming.
