cultural anthropology

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌltʃərəl ˌænθrəˈpɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ˈkʌltʃərəl ˌænθrəˈpɒlədʒi / (BrE)
The branch of anthropology that studies human societies, cultures, and their development.
branch of anthropology studying cultures

Examples

After studying cultural anthropology, she traveled abroad to research traditional ceremonies.
His interest in cultural anthropology led him to pursue a career at the museum.
Many universities offer courses in cultural anthropology as part of their social sciences programs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: social anthropology - branch of anthropology focusing on social patterns and practices
  • Antonym: physical anthropology - branch of anthropology studying human biological and physiological characteristics

Etymology

From Latin 'cultura' (cultivation, culture) and Greek 'anthrōpos' (human) + 'logia' (study).
