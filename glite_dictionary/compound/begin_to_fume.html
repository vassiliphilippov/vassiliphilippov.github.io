to begin to fume

1. VERB

/ bɪˈɡɪn tə fjuːm / (AmE), / bɪˈɡɪn tə fjuːm / (BrE)
to start to display visible anger or irritation, often becoming noticeably upset
start to show anger or irritation

Examples

Whenever he was criticized in public, he would begin to fume and clench his fists.
She tried to stay calm, but after hearing the unfair accusation, she began to fume.
You could tell she was beginning to fume when her face turned red and she started raising her voice.

Synset Examples

He began to fume when he saw the mess.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fume - to show or feel anger; to emit smoke or vapour
