brimful of

1. ADJ

/ ˈbrɪmˌfʊl əv / (AmE), / ˈbrɪmfʊl əv / (BrE)
(of something) completely full to the top with a particular thing.
completely full with something

Examples

The cup was brimful of hot tea, so she carried it carefully.
The warehouse is brimful of last season’s merchandise.
His voice was brimful of excitement when he told me the news.

Synset Examples

The library shelves were brimful of new books.
Her basket was brimful of ripe strawberries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: full of - containing as much of something as possible

Etymology

From 'brim' (the upper edge) + 'full'; 'of' adds specification of the thing filling it.
