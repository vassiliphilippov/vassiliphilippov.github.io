leaf bug

1. NOUN

/ liːf bʌɡ / (AmE), / liːf bʌɡ / (BrE)
a small, often brightly colored insect that feeds on plant juices, especially those belonging to the family Miridae
small plant-feeding insect (Miridae)

Examples

The leaf bug camouflages itself among the foliage to avoid predators.
You might spot a leaf bug on your tomato plants during the summer.
Gardeners often inspect leaves to check for leaf bugs that could harm their crops.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mirid - a plant bug of the family Miridae
