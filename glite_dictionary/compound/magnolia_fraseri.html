Magnolia fraseri

1. NOUN

/ mæɡˈnoʊliə ˈfreɪzəri / (AmE), / mæɡˈnəʊliə ˈfreɪzəri / (BrE)
A small deciduous tree of the magnolia family, native to the southeastern United States, with large leaves arranged in coiled clusters at the branch tips.
deciduous magnolia tree native to southeastern US

Examples

The leaves of the Magnolia fraseri are much larger than those of most other magnolias.
Gardeners admire the Magnolia fraseri for its unique foliage and showy blossoms.
During spring, the Magnolia fraseri produces fragrant, creamy-white flowers.

Etymology

Named after Pierre Magnol, a French botanist, and John Fraser, a Scottish botanist.
