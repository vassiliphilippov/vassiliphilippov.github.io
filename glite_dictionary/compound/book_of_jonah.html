Book of Jonah

1. PROPN

/ ˈbʊk əv ˈdʒoʊnə / (AmE), / ˈbʊk əv ˈdʒəʊnə / (BrE)
A book in the Old Testament that recounts the story of Jonah, who was swallowed by a large fish after attempting to evade God's command.
Old Testament book about Jonah and the fish

Examples

The Book of Jonah is often read in synagogues during the Jewish holiday of Yom Kippur.
Many scholars analyze the Book of Jonah for its themes of mercy and repentance.
She referenced the Book of Jonah in her sermon about second chances.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Jonah - Hebrew prophet featured in the Book of Jonah
  • Degree Higher: book of the Old Testament - Any book in the first part of the Christian Bible
