magic bullet

1. NOUN

/ ˌmædʒɪk ˈbʊlət / (AmE), / ˌmadʒɪk ˈbʊlɪt / (BrE)
a drug or therapy that can cure or prevent a disease quickly and effectively, often regarded as a perfect or ideal solution
ideal cure; perfect remedy

Examples

Scientists are searching for a magic bullet to halt the spread of the virus.
Unfortunately, there is no magic bullet that can cure all types of cancer.
He hoped the new drug would be the magic bullet the medical community was waiting for.

Synset Examples

there is no magic bullet against cancer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cure-all - a remedy that is believed to cure any disease or difficulty
  • Synonym: panacea - a solution or remedy for all difficulties or diseases

Etymology

Coined in the early 20th century, popularized by German physician Paul Ehrlich to describe a targeted medical treatment.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmædʒɪk ˈbʊlɪt / (AmE), / ˈmadʒɪk ˈbʊlɪt / (BrE)
a simple and highly effective solution to a difficult or complex problem, especially one that is hoped for but unlikely to exist
a simple solution to a complex problem

Examples

Many experts warn there is no magic bullet for improving education systems.
The company is still searching for a magic bullet to boost productivity across all departments.
Unfortunately, the new policy wasn't the magic bullet that everyone had hoped it would be.

Synset Examples

They hoped the technology would be the magic bullet they needed.
The new drug was hailed as a magic bullet for the disease.
Finding a magic bullet solution is often unrealistic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: panacea - a solution or remedy for all difficulties or diseases
  • Synonym: cure-all - a remedy that cures all diseases or evils

Etymology

Originally referring to a drug that targets a disease without harming other body parts, coined by German scientist Paul Ehrlich in the early 20th century.
