pagoda tree

1. NOUN

/ pəˈɡoʊdə triː / (AmE), / pəˈɡəʊdə triː / (BrE)
A tall, conically shaped tree with few branches, known for its large, fragrant yellow flowers with white centers.
tree with large fragrant yellow and white flowers

Examples

The pagoda tree outside the temple bloomed with beautiful yellow and white flowers each spring.
Gardeners admire the pagoda tree for its striking shape and fragrant blossoms.
A single pagoda tree can provide shade and add elegance to a landscape.

Etymology

Named for its resemblance to the shape of a pagoda, from Portuguese 'pagode', from Persian 'butkada' (temple of idols).

2. NOUN

/ pəˈɡoʊ.də tri / (AmE), / pəˈɡəʊ.də triː / (BrE)
a tropical tree of Asia, especially found in India, with an erect, conical shape, often grown in temple gardens
tropical Asian tree with conical form

Examples

The pagoda tree is often planted near temples throughout southern India.
Many gardens feature the pagoda tree for its beautiful shape and fragrant flowers.
He admired the blossoms of the pagoda tree during his travels in Asia.

Etymology

Named for its common planting around Asian pagodas and temples.
