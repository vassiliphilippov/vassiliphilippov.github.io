Mongol Tatar

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑŋɡəl təˈtɑːr / (AmE), / ˈmɒŋɡəl təˈtɑː / (BrE)
a member of the Mongolian people of Central Asia, especially those who invaded Russia in the 13th century
member of Mongolian group that invaded Russia

Examples

The Mongol Tatar forces swept across the Russian plains in the 13th century, altering the course of history.
Historians debate the influence that Mongol Tatar rulers had on medieval Russian society.
Artifacts from the Mongol Tatar period are displayed in the national museum.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Tatar - a member of various Turkic-speaking peoples living in Asia and eastern Europe
  • Synonym: Mongol - a member of the Central Asian ethnic group native to Mongolia

Etymology

From Mongol, denoting the Mongolian people, and Tatar, an ethnonym for groups of Turkic and Mongolic origin.
