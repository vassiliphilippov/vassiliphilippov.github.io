rattlesnake fern

1. NOUN

/ ˈrætlˌsneɪk fɝn / (AmE), / ˈrætlˌsneɪk fɜːn / (BrE)
An American fern (Botrychium virginianum) whose clusters of sporangia resemble the rattle of a snake.
a fern with rattle-like sporangia

Examples

We found a rattlesnake fern growing in the shady part of the forest.
The rattlesnake fern is easily identified by its unique spore clusters.
Botanists often study the rattlesnake fern to learn more about North American ferns.

Etymology

Named for the resemblance of its sporangia clusters to a rattlesnake's rattle.
