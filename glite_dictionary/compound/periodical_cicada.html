periodical cicada

1. NOUN

/ pɪrˈiː.ə.dɪ.kəl sɪˈkeɪ.də / (AmE), / pɪəˈrɪədɪkəl sɪˈkɑːdə / (BrE)
A North American cicada that emerges in large numbers every 13 or 17 years, as its nymphs require a long period to mature underground.
cicada species, emerges every 13 or 17 years

Examples

The periodical cicada emerges in huge numbers every seventeen years, blanketing forests and backyards alike.
Scientists study the life cycle of the periodical cicada to understand its unusual emergence patterns.
A chorus of periodical cicadas can be deafening during their brief appearance above ground.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cicada - an insect known for its loud song
