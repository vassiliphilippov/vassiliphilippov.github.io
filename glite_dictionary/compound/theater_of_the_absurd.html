theater of the absurd

1. NOUN

/ ˈθiːətər əv ði əbˈsɝːd / (AmE), / ˈθɪətə əv ði əbˈsɜːd / (BrE)
A style or movement in drama that highlights the irrationality and meaninglessness of human existence, often through unconventional narrative and dialogue.
dramatic movement stressing life's absurdity

Examples

Many critics consider Samuel Beckett a key figure in the theater of the absurd.
The theater of the absurd often challenges audiences with its unconventional storytelling.
Ionesco's plays are classic works of the theater of the absurd movement.

Synset Examples

Samuel Beckett and Eugene Ionesco have written plays for the theater of the absurd

Related Terms

  • Synonym: absurdism - philosophy emphasizing the absurdity of life
  • Synonym: existentialism - philosophy focused on individual existence and meaning

Etymology

From French théâtre de l'absurde, first used in the 1950s by critic Martin Esslin.
