accessory after the fact

1. NOUN

/ əkˈsɛsəri ˈæftɚ ðə ˈfækt / (AmE), / əkˈsɛsəri ˈɑːftə ðə ˈfakt / (BrE)
A person who knowingly assists or comforts someone who has committed a felony, after the crime has been committed.
someone who helps a felon after a crime
legal

Examples

She was charged as an accessory after the fact for hiding her brother after the robbery.
The prosecution argued that providing the fugitive with money made him an accessory after the fact.
Even though he didn’t take part in the burglary, assisting the suspect made him an accessory after the fact.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accessory - person who assists in a crime, either before or after
  • Antonym: principal - person who actually commits a crime
  • Antonym: accessory before the fact - person who assists in the preparation of a crime, before it is committed

Etymology

From 'accessory', meaning one who aids or abets a crime, plus the legal phrase 'after the fact'.
