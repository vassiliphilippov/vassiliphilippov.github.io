toothed wheel

1. NOUN

/ tuːθt wiːl / (AmE), / tuːθt wiːl / (BrE)
a wheel with projecting teeth, used especially for making a series of perforations in a material
wheel with teeth for perforating

Examples

The machine uses a toothed wheel to create evenly spaced holes in the paper.
By turning the toothed wheel, the operator ensures each ticket is perforated precisely.
A broken toothed wheel can cause the perforation process to fail.
