western fence lizard

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɛstərn fɛns ˈlɪzərd / (AmE), / ˈwɛstən fɛns ˈlɪzəd / (BrE)
a common lizard of western North America, often seen on logs or rocks, known for its rough, spiny scales
a common lizard species of western North America

Examples

We spotted a western fence lizard basking on the sunlit rock during our hike.
Several western fence lizards darted away as we approached the old log pile.
The western fence lizard is sometimes called the 'blue-belly' due to its colorful markings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lizard - a reptile with a long body and tail, four legs, and movable eyelids
