night-blooming cereus

1. NOUN

/ ˈnaɪt ˌbluːmɪŋ səˈriːəs / (AmE), / ˈnaɪt ˌbluːmɪŋ səˈriːəs / (BrE)
a cactus of the genus Selenicereus with large, fragrant flowers that bloom at night and often wilt by morning
cactus with night-blooming flowers

Examples

The night-blooming cereus opens its spectacular white flowers only after sunset.
Gardeners gathered late to witness the rare blooming of the night-blooming cereus in the botanical garden.
Many people are surprised by the powerful fragrance of the night-blooming cereus during its brief flowering period.

Etymology

From 'cereus', referring to the cactus family; 'night-blooming' describes its flowering habit.

2. NOUN

/ ˌnaɪt ˈbluːmɪŋ səˈriːəs / (AmE), / ˌnaɪt ˈbluːmɪŋ səˈrɪəs / (BrE)
a cactus of the genus Hylocereus or related genera that produces fragrant flowers which open at night
cactus producing nocturnal flowers

Examples

The night-blooming cereus surprised us with its large, fragrant flowers opening under the moonlight.
Gardeners eagerly await the rare event when their night-blooming cereus bursts into bloom just once each year.
A single blossom of the night-blooming cereus can fill an entire room with its sweet scent overnight.

Etymology

From 'night-blooming' (flowering at night) and Modern Latin 'Cereus', a genus of cacti.
