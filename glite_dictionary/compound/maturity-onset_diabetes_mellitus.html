maturity-onset diabetes mellitus

1. NOUN

/ məˌtʃʊr.ɪ.ti ˈɑːn.set daɪəˌbiːtiz məˈlaɪtəs / (AmE), / məˌtʃʊə.rɪ.ti ˈɒn.set daɪəˌbiːtiːz məˈlaɪtəs / (BrE)
a mild form of diabetes that develops gradually in adults, often manageable by diet and oral medications rather than insulin injections
adult-onset form of diabetes mellitus
medical

Examples

Her doctor diagnosed her with maturity-onset diabetes mellitus and advised lifestyle changes to manage her blood sugar.
Obesity can be a major risk factor for developing maturity-onset diabetes mellitus in later life.
Unlike juvenile diabetes, maturity-onset diabetes mellitus often does not require insulin injections.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: type 2 diabetes - a common form of diabetes, usually developing in adults, with similar characteristics

Etymology

From 'maturity' (adulthood) + 'onset' (beginning) + New Latin 'diabetes mellitus.'
