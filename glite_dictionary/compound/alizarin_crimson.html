alizarin crimson

1. NOUN

/ əˈlɪzərɪn ˈkrɪmzən / (AmE), / əˈlɪzərɪn ˈkrɪmzən / (BrE)
a bright red or crimson synthetic dye or pigment, originally derived from alizarin and used especially for dyeing textiles and in paints
a vivid red synthetic dye or pigment

Examples

The artist mixed alizarin crimson with ultramarine blue to create a deep shade of purple.
Traditional Persian carpets often feature wool dyed with alizarin crimson for its rich red hues.
Many painters appreciate the transparency and vibrancy of alizarin crimson in watercolor palettes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alizarin - an orange to red dye originally derived from the madder plant

Etymology

From 'alizarin', a dye originally obtained from the madder plant, and 'crimson', indicating the red color.
