provost guard

1. NOUN

/ ˈproʊvoʊst ɡɑrd / (AmE), / ˈprɒvəst ɡɑːd / (BrE)
a military detachment responsible for maintaining order and discipline, commanded by a provost marshal
military unit maintaining order

Examples

The provost guard was called in to restore order after the incident in the barracks.
During the parade, members of the provost guard stood at attention along the main road.
In times of unrest, the provost guard performs essential duties to ensure discipline among troops.

Etymology

From 'provost' (from Old French *prevost*, from Latin *praepositus*, meaning 'person placed over others') and 'guard'.
