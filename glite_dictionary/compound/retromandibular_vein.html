retromandibular vein

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɛtroʊˌmænˈdɪbjələr veɪn / (AmE), / ˌrɛtrəʊˌmænˈdɪbjʊlə veɪn / (BrE)
a vein located behind the mandible, formed by the union of the superficial temporal and maxillary veins, serving as a posterior branch of the facial vein
vein behind the mandible in the face
medical

Examples

The retromandibular vein is often visible during surgical procedures near the lower jaw.
An injury to the retromandibular vein can cause significant bleeding in the facial area.
The retromandibular vein lies behind the mandible and is formed by the union of the temporal and maxillary veins.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: facial vein - a vein that drains blood from the face
  • Degree Lower: superficial temporal vein - vein that helps form the retromandibular vein
  • Degree Lower: maxillary vein - vein contributing to the formation of the retromandibular vein

Etymology

From Latin 'retro' (behind) + 'mandibula' (jaw, mandible).
