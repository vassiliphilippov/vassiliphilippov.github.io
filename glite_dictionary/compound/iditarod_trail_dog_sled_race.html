Iditarod Trail Dog Sled Race

1. NOUN

/ ˌaɪ.də.təˌrɑd treɪl dɔɡ slɛd reɪs / (AmE), / ˌaɪ.dɪ.təˌrɒd treɪl dɒɡ slɛd reɪs / (BrE)
an annual long-distance dog sled race run on the Iditarod Trail in Alaska, covering nearly 1,000 miles
annual long-distance Alaskan dog sled race

Examples

Each March, mushers and their teams compete in the Iditarod Trail Dog Sled Race across the harsh Alaskan landscape.
The Iditarod Trail Dog Sled Race is considered one of the toughest endurance events in the world.
Thousands of fans gather to watch the start of the Iditarod Trail Dog Sled Race in Anchorage every year.

Etymology

Named after the Iditarod Trail and the town of Iditarod in Alaska; the event began in 1973.
