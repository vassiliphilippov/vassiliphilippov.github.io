chemical imbalance

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɛmɪkəl ɪmˈbæl.əns / (AmE), / ˈkɛmɪkəl ɪmˈbæl.əns / (BrE)
a disruption or abnormality in the normal balance of chemicals in the body, especially in relation to mental health or brain function
abnormal balance of chemicals in the body
medical

Examples

Many people with depression believe a chemical imbalance is at the root of their symptoms.
The medication aims to correct the chemical imbalance affecting his mood.
Research on chemical imbalance has led to new treatments for mental illnesses.

Synset Examples

Understanding chemical imbalance is crucial in psychiatry.
Doctors often treat patients with a chemical imbalance.
His depression was due to a chemical imbalance.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: homeostasis - the tendency to maintain a stable, relatively constant internal environment
  • Synonym: neurotransmitter - a chemical substance that transmits nerve impulses
