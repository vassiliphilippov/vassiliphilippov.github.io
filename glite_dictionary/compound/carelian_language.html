Carelian language

1. NOUN

/ kəˈriː.li.ən ˈlæŋ.ɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / kəˈrɛl.i.ən ˈlæŋ.ɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
a Finnic language spoken mainly by the Karelians in the Karelia region of northwestern Russia and parts of Finland.
Finnic language of Karelia

Examples

Few people today are fluent in the Carelian language outside of isolated communities.
The Carelian language has influenced local dialects in eastern Finland.
Efforts to preserve the Carelian language include publishing books and creating language courses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Finnish language - Finnic language of Finland
  • Synonym: Karelian - alternative spelling of Carelian (the people or language)

Etymology

Carelian from Karelia (region in Russia/Finland) + language; Karelia name of uncertain origin.
