Binghi ceremony

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɪŋɡi ˈsɛrəˌmoʊni / (AmE), / ˈbɪŋɡi ˈsɛrɪməni / (BrE)
A ceremonial gathering in Rastafarian culture, often involving drumming, chanting, and celebration, used to mark significant occasions and promote unity.
Rastafarian ceremonial gathering

Examples

The community gathered for a Binghi ceremony to honor their ancestors.
During the Binghi ceremony, participants played drums and sang traditional songs.
He described the spiritual energy he felt at the Binghi ceremony last weekend.

Synset Examples

The Binghi ceremony was vibrant and full of energy.
She celebrated her birthday with a Binghi party.

Etymology

From Rastafarian usage, with 'Binghi' possibly linked to Nyabinghi, an East African spiritual movement adopted by Rastafarians.
