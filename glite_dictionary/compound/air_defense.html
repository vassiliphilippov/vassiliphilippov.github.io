air defense

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛr dɪˌfɛns / (AmE), / ˈeə dɪˌfɛns / (BrE)
Measures or systems designed to protect against or destroy attacking enemy aircraft or missiles.
protection against enemy aircraft or missiles

Examples

The country invested heavily in air defense systems to protect its cities from missile attacks.
During the conflict, the military enhanced its air defense capabilities with new radar technology.
Modern air defense is a combination of fighter jets, ground missiles, and early warning systems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anti-aircraft - designed to destroy enemy aircraft
  • Synonym: missile defense - systems to intercept and destroy incoming missiles
