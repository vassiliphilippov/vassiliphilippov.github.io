electrostatic field

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌlɛktroʊˈstætɪk fiːld / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛktrəʊˈstætɪk fiːld / (BrE)
The region around static electric charges in which electric forces are exerted on other electric charges.
field around static electric charges
medical

Examples

The force between two charges is determined by the electrostatic field they create.
An electrostatic field can be visualized using field lines around charged particles.
Insulators can maintain a strong electrostatic field without allowing current to flow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: electric field - region around electrically charged particles where force is exerted

Etymology

From 'electrostatic' (from Greek 'ēlektron' + 'static') and 'field' (Old English 'feld').
