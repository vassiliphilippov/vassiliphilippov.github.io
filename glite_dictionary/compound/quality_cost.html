quality cost

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɑləti kɑst / (AmE), / ˈkwɒləti kɒst / (BrE)
the total cost incurred by an organization to ensure quality and to address deficiencies in quality, including prevention, appraisal, and failure costs
costs associated with maintaining and lacking quality

Examples

The company conducted a thorough analysis of its quality cost to identify areas for improvement.
Reducing quality cost can lead to significant savings and higher customer satisfaction.
Managers regularly review quality cost reports to ensure that resources are used efficiently.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cost of quality - alternative term for 'quality cost', refers to total costs arising from efforts to attain quality and failures to do so
