four-four time

1. NOUN

/ fɔr fɔr taɪm / (AmE), / fɔː fɔː taɪm / (BrE)
a musical time signature in which each bar contains four beats, typically with a quarter note receiving one beat.
musical time signature with four beats per bar

Examples

Most pop songs are written in four-four time for an easy-to-follow rhythm.
Our music teacher explained how to conduct a piece in four-four time.
The jazz band practiced playing the new tune, which was set in four-four time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: common time - another term for four-four time in music
