nuclear propulsion

1. NOUN

/ ˈnuː.kli.ɚ prəˈpoʊl.ʃən / (AmE), / ˈnjuː.klɪ.ə prəˈpɒl.ʃən / (BrE)
the use of a nuclear reactor to provide power or thrust for vehicles, typically by generating electricity or directly heating a propellant
use of nuclear reactors for vehicle propulsion

Examples

The navy invested heavily in nuclear propulsion to enhance the range and endurance of its submarines.
Advances in nuclear propulsion make long-duration space missions more feasible than ever before.
Early research into nuclear propulsion was driven by the need for faster and more efficient naval vessels.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: propulsion - the action of driving or pushing forward
