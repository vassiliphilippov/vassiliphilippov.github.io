quick bread

1. NOUN

/ kwɪk brɛd / (AmE), / kwɪk brɛd / (BrE)
A type of bread made with a leavening agent such as baking powder or baking soda, allowing it to be baked immediately without rising time.
bread made with fast-acting leavening

Examples

Banana bread is a classic example of a quick bread.
Many quick breads require no kneading and are simple to prepare.
She baked a quick bread with zucchini and walnuts for breakfast.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: yeast bread - bread made with yeast as a leavening agent
