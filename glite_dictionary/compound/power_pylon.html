power pylon

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaʊ.ɚ ˈpaɪ.lɑn / (AmE), / ˈpaʊ.ə ˈpaɪ.lɒn / (BrE)
A tall steel tower used to support high-voltage electric power transmission lines over long distances.
tall steel tower for power lines

Examples

The new power plant required several power pylons to transmit electricity across the valley.
Birds sometimes nest on power pylons, although it poses risks to their safety.
A row of power pylons stretched across the horizon, carrying electricity to distant cities.

Synset Examples

power pylons are a favorite target for terrorists

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transmission tower - tower supporting overhead power lines
  • Degree Lower: utility pole - wooden pole supporting lower-voltage utility lines

Etymology

From 'power' (electricity) and 'pylon' (from Greek 'pylōn', gateway or monumental entrance).
