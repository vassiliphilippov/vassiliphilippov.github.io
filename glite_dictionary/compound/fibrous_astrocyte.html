fibrous astrocyte

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪbɹəs ˈæstroʊˌsaɪt / (AmE), / ˈfaɪbɹəs ˈastrəʊˌsʌɪt / (BrE)
a type of star-shaped glial cell with long, slender processes, found in the white matter of the brain and spinal cord
star-shaped glial cell in white matter
medical

Examples

Fibrous astrocytes are primarily found in the white matter of the brain and spinal cord.
Researchers observed that the fibrous astrocyte plays a role in supporting nerve fibers.
Damage to the white matter may affect the function of fibrous astrocytes.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: protoplasmic astrocyte - a type of astrocyte with shorter, thicker processes found mainly in gray matter
  • Degree Higher: astrocyte - a star-shaped glial cell in the nervous system

Etymology

From Latin 'fibrosus' (fibrous) and Greek 'astron' (star) + 'kytos' (cell).
