psychedelic rock

1. NOUN

/ ˌsaɪkəˈdɛlɪk rɑk / (AmE), / ˌsaɪkəˈdɛlɪk rɒk / (BrE)
a style of rock music originating in the 1960s, characterized by experimental sounds and effects intended to evoke or enhance the experience of psychedelic drugs
1960s rock music with psychedelic influences

Examples

The Beatles' album 'Sgt. Pepper's Lonely Hearts Club Band' is a landmark in psychedelic rock.
Many bands from San Francisco were pioneers of psychedelic rock in the late 1960s.
The concert featured a blend of blues, folk, and psychedelic rock styles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acid rock - a synonym for psychedelic rock, focusing on heavier sounds

Etymology

From 'psychedelic', coined in the 1950s from Ancient Greek 'psyche' (mind) and 'deloun' (to make visible), and 'rock', referring to rock music.
