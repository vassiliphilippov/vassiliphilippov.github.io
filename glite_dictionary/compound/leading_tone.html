leading tone

1. NOUN

/ ˈliːdɪŋ toʊn / (AmE), / ˈliːdɪŋ təʊn / (BrE)
the seventh note of a diatonic scale, which is a semitone below the tonic and strongly leads into it
seventh note of a diatonic scale (music)

Examples

In the key of C major, the leading tone is the note B.
The melody creates tension by holding the leading tone before resolving to the tonic.
Composers often use the leading tone to give a sense of anticipation in their music.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: subtonic - the seventh note of the natural minor scale, a whole tone below the tonic
  • Degree Higher: tonic - the first note of a scale, the keynote
