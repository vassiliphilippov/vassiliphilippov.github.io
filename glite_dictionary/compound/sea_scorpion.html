sea scorpion

1. NOUN

/ si ˈskɔːrpiən / (AmE), / siː ˈskɔː.pi.ən / (BrE)
A marine fish with a tapering body, an armored head, and venomous spines, found in coastal waters.
marine fish with armored head and venomous spines

Examples

The sea scorpion uses its venomous spines to defend itself from predators.
Divers should be cautious when encountering a sea scorpion in shallow rocky waters.
The distinctive armored head helps the sea scorpion blend into the seafloor.
