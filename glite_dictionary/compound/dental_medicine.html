dental medicine

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛntl ˈmɛdɪsən / (AmE), / ˈdɛntl ˈmɛdɪsɪn / (BrE)
the branch of medicine concerned with the anatomy, development, and diseases of the teeth
branch of medicine concerning teeth
medical

Examples

She decided to specialize in dental medicine after discovering her interest in oral health.
Advances in dental medicine have made it easier to treat gum disease effectively.
Universities now offer postgraduate programs in dental medicine for aspiring specialists.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dentistry - the profession or science of preventing and treating tooth and gum diseases

Etymology

From Latin 'dens' (tooth) and 'medicina' (the art of healing).
