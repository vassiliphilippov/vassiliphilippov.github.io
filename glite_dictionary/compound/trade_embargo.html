trade embargo

1. NOUN

/ treɪd ɪmˈbɑːrɡoʊ / (AmE), / treɪd ɪmˈbɑːɡəʊ / (BrE)
An official ban imposed by a government on trade with a particular country or the exchange of specific goods.
government ban on trade with a country

Examples

The United Nations imposed a trade embargo on the country after the conflict began.
The trade embargo made it difficult for local businesses to import vital goods.
Many political leaders argued that a trade embargo would pressure the regime to change its policies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sanction - official measure restricting trade or relations for political reasons
  • Synonym: boycott - refusal to trade with or participate in relations with a party as a punishment or protest
  • Antonym: free trade - international trade left to its natural course without tariffs or restrictions

Etymology

From 'trade' (commerce) and Spanish 'embargo' (seizure, prohibition).
