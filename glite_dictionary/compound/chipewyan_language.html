Chipewyan language

1. NOUN

/ tʃɪˈpwiːən ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / tʃɪˈpiːwɪən ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Athabaskan language spoken by the Chipewyan people, primarily in northern Canada
the language of the Chipewyan people

Examples

The Chipewyan language is still spoken by some elders in the community.
He is studying the grammar of the Chipewyan language for his linguistic research.
Efforts are being made to preserve the Chipewyan language for future generations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Athabaskan language - language family to which Chipewyan belongs

Etymology

Chipewyan from Cree, originally meaning 'pointed skins', referring to traditional clothing of the people.
