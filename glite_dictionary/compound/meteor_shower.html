meteor shower

1. NOUN

/ ˈmiːdiər ˌʃaʊər / (AmE), / ˈmiːtiə ˌʃaʊə / (BrE)
a natural event in which many meteors appear to radiate from a single point in the night sky as a result of a meteor swarm entering Earth's atmosphere
a celestial event with multiple meteors

Examples

We stayed up late to watch the meteor shower lighting up the summer sky.
The Perseid meteor shower occurs every August and is visible from many places on Earth.
During the meteor shower, hundreds of shooting stars could be seen every hour.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shooting star - a visible streak of light from a meteor in the sky
  • Synonym: comet - an icy small body that releases gas or dust in space

2. NOUN

/ ˈmiːdiər ˌʃaʊər / (AmE), / ˈmiːtiə ˌʃaʊə / (BrE)
a celestial event during which many meteors are seen to radiate from a particular point in the night sky over a short period
a large number of meteors appearing in the sky

Examples

We stayed up late to watch the meteor shower light up the summer sky.
The annual Perseid meteor shower attracts stargazers from around the world.
During the meteor shower, dozens of meteors flashed above the mountains each hour.

Synset Examples

We watched a meteor shower from the rooftop.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shooting star - a streak of light from a meteor burning in the atmosphere
