cardiac rhythm

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːrdiæk ˈrɪðəm / (AmE), / ˈkɑːdiæk ˈrɪðəm / (BrE)
the pattern of electrical and mechanical activity that governs the beating of the heart
the pattern of a beating heart
medical

Examples

The doctor monitored the patient's cardiac rhythm during surgery.
An irregular cardiac rhythm can indicate a serious health issue.
The device is designed to help maintain a normal cardiac rhythm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heart rate - the speed of the heartbeat measured by beats per minute

Etymology

From Ancient Greek 'kardiā' (heart) and 'rhythmos' (measured motion).
