flower girl

1. NOUN

/ ˈflaʊər ɡɜrl / (AmE), / ˈflaʊə ɡɜːl / (BrE)
a young girl who carries flowers and leads the procession at a wedding ceremony
young girl in a wedding procession with flowers

Examples

The flower girl walked ahead of the bride, scattering rose petals along the aisle.
At her cousin's wedding, Emily was thrilled to be chosen as the flower girl.
The flower girl wore a white dress and held a small basket of flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ring bearer - child who carries wedding rings during the ceremony

2. NOUN

/ ˈflaʊ.ɚ ɡɝːl / (AmE), / ˈflaʊ.ə ɡɜːl / (BrE)
a woman or girl who sells flowers on the street, often from a stall or basket
woman or girl selling flowers on the street

Examples

The flower girl offered me a bouquet of fresh roses as I passed by the station.
In Victorian London, flower girls were a common sight on busy street corners.
A young flower girl stood in the rain, hoping to sell her last handful of daisies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flower seller - person who sells flowers
