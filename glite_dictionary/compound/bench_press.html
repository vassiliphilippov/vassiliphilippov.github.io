bench press

1. NOUN

/ bɛntʃ prɛs / (AmE), / bɛntʃ prɛs / (BrE)
a small mechanical press used for punching or shaping metal, typically mounted on a workbench
small mechanical press on a workbench

Examples

The machinist set up the bench press to punch holes in the metal sheet.
After fixing it to the workbench, he used the bench press for several small parts.
This type of bench press is ideal for precision tasks in a home workshop.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: press - a machine for exerting pressure to shape or cut materials

2. NOUN

/ bɛntʃ prɛs / (AmE), / bɛntʃ prɛs / (BrE)
an exercise in which a person lies on their back on a bench and pushes a weight upwards from chest level
weightlifting exercise performed on a bench

Examples

He increased the weight he could bench press after months of training.
The bench press is a core part of his workout routine.
During the competition, she set a new record in the bench press.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deadlift - weightlifting exercise where a barbell is lifted from the ground to hip level
  • Synonym: squat - weightlifting exercise where a person lowers hips from a standing position and then rises
