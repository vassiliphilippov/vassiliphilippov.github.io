whitetip shark

1. NOUN

/ ˈwaɪtˌtɪp ʃɑrk / (AmE), / ˈwaɪtˌtɪp ʃɑːk / (BrE)
a species of shark from the Pacific and Indian Oceans and Red Sea, recognized by white tips on its dorsal and caudal fins
shark with white-tipped fins

Examples

The whitetip shark is easily recognized by the white markings on its fins.
Many divers consider the whitetip shark to be relatively unaggressive.
A whitetip shark glided silently over the reef, searching for prey.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: white shark - large predatory shark, also called great white

2. NOUN

/ ˈwaɪtˌtɪp ʃɑrk / (AmE), / ˈwaɪtˌtɪp ʃɑːk / (BrE)
a large deep-water shark with a white-tipped dorsal fin, widely distributed and known for being potentially dangerous
a large shark with a white-tipped dorsal fin

Examples

Many divers have encountered the whitetip shark while exploring deep ocean reefs.
The whitetip shark is known for its curiosity and distinctive white-tipped dorsal fin.
Swimming in waters frequented by the whitetip shark can be considered dangerous without proper precautions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oceanic whitetip shark - species of whitetip shark (Carcharhinus longimanus)
  • Synonym: reef whitetip shark - species of whitetip shark (Triaenodon obesus)
