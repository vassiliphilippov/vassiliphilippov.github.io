fulvic acid

1. NOUN

/ ˈfʊl.vɪk ˌæs.ɪd / (AmE), / ˈfʌl.vɪk ˌæs.ɪd / (BrE)
a yellow to yellow-brown organic acid found in soil, formed by the decomposition of plant and animal matter, and soluble in water at all pH levels
organic acid in soil; soluble at all pH levels
medical

Examples

Soil rich in fulvic acid can improve plant growth and nutrient uptake.
Researchers analyzed the fulvic acid content in the river water samples.
The presence of fulvic acid affects the mobility of minerals in natural environments.

Synset Examples

they measured the fluvial fulvic acid

Etymology

From Latin 'fulvus' (tawny, yellowish brown), referring to its color.
