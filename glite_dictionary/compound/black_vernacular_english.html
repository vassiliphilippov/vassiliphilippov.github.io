Black Vernacular English

1. NOUN

/ blæk vɚˈnækjəlɚ ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ / (AmE), / blak vəˈnækjʊlə ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ / (BrE)
a nonstandard variety of American English, commonly spoken by many African Americans, characterized by distinctive pronunciation, grammar, and vocabulary
a variety of American English spoken by African Americans

Examples

Many linguistic studies have focused on the grammar of Black Vernacular English.
She grew up speaking both standard American English and Black Vernacular English at home.
Some expressions in hip hop lyrics originate from Black Vernacular English.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: African American Vernacular English - an alternative name for Black Vernacular English
