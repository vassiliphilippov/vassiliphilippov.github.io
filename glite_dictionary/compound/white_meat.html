white meat

1. NOUN

/ waɪt mit / (AmE), / waɪt miːt / (BrE)
meat from the breast or other pale parts of poultry, such as chicken or turkey, as opposed to darker meat from the legs or thighs.
pale meat from poultry breast

Examples

I prefer white meat from the chicken because it's leaner.
The restaurant offers both white meat and dark meat options for their roast turkey.
She always carves the white meat for sandwiches after Thanksgiving dinner.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dark meat - meat from the leg or thigh of poultry, darker and fattier than breast meat
