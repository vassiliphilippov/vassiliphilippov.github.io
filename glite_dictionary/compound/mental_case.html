mental case

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛn.təl keɪs / (AmE), / ˈmɛn.təl keɪs / (BrE)
a person suffering from a mental illness, especially neurosis; this term is considered outdated and potentially offensive
person with mental illness (dated, often offensive)
offensive archaic

Examples

The novel portrayed the main character as a mental case struggling with her inner demons.
Decades ago, people with anxiety might have been unfairly labeled a mental case.
Using the term mental case is now generally seen as insensitive and outdated.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neurotic - a person suffering from neurosis
  • Antonym: sane - free from mental illness; mentally healthy

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɛn.təl keɪs / (AmE), / ˈmɛn.təl keɪs / (BrE)
an offensive or outdated term for a person regarded as mentally ill or unstable
person regarded as mentally ill (offensive/outdated)
offensive

Examples

After weeks of stress, he worried he might end up a mental case.
They unfairly called her a mental case when she spoke about her fears.
It's an outdated and offensive term to call someone a mental case.

Synset Examples

She felt like a mental case after that tough exam.
They joked that he was a mental case for studying all night.
The therapist said he wasn't a mental case, just stressed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lunatic - offensive term for a person considered mentally ill
  • Synonym: madman - person considered insane or mentally ill
  • Antonym: sane - mentally healthy
