Ramsay Hunt syndrome

1. NOUN

/ ˈræmzeɪ hʌnt ˈsɪndroʊm / (AmE), / ˈræmzi hʌnt ˈsɪndrəʊm / (BrE)
a rare neurological disorder caused by varicella zoster virus infection, marked by facial paralysis, ear pain, vertigo, and sometimes hearing loss
neurological disorder with facial paralysis and ear symptoms
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with Ramsay Hunt syndrome after developing facial paralysis and severe ear pain.
Treatment for Ramsay Hunt syndrome often includes antiviral medication and corticosteroids.
Hearing loss can be a complication associated with Ramsay Hunt syndrome.

Etymology

Named after James Ramsay Hunt, the American neurologist who described the syndrome.
