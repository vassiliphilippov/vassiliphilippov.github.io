golden hello

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡoʊldən həˈloʊ / (AmE), / ˌɡəʊldən həˈləʊ / (BrE)
a large sum of money or other financial incentive offered to a new employee as an inducement to join a company
financial incentive for new employees

Examples

The new CEO received a golden hello worth several million dollars.
Many tech firms use a golden hello to attract top talent from competitors.
She was offered a generous golden hello when she agreed to join the executive team.

Synset Examples

New employees often get a golden hello as an incentive.
Her golden hello made a big impression on the staff.
The company offered him a golden hello to join their team.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: signing bonus - a financial reward given to a new employee when they join a company
