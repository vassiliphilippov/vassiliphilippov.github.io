reserve bank

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈzɝv ˌbæŋk / (AmE), / rɪˈzɜːv ˌbaŋk / (BrE)
one of several regional banks that monitor and act as depositories for commercial banks in their region, especially as part of a central banking system such as the U.S. Federal Reserve System.
regional central bank in a banking system

Examples

The New York reserve bank plays a key role in implementing monetary policy.
Each reserve bank serves a specific geographic district within the Federal Reserve System.
Commercial banks hold their reserves at the nearest reserve bank in their region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: central bank - the main national bank that manages a country's currency and monetary policy
