legal code

1. NOUN

/ ˈliɡəl koʊd / (AmE), / ˈliːɡəl kəʊd / (BrE)
A collection or system of laws officially adopted by a state or nation.
system of laws for a state or nation
legal

Examples

The country's legal code was updated to address new technologies.
Law students must study the legal code in detail for their exams.
Changes to the legal code can have a significant impact on society.

Synset Examples

The legal code was modified to make gender discrimination illegal this year

Related Terms

  • Synonym: statute - a written law passed by a legislative body
  • Synonym: regulation - a rule made by an authority
  • Antonym: common law - the body of law derived from judicial decisions
