big stick

1. NOUN

/ bɪɡ stɪk / (AmE), / bɪɡ stɪk / (BrE)
a display of force or power, especially as a means of influence or coercion
display of force or power

Examples

The government often negotiates peacefully but keeps a big stick in reserve.
He prefers diplomacy over showing the big stick during international disputes.
The boss's big stick approach intimidated everyone into compliance.

Synset Examples

speak softly but carry a big stick

Related Terms

  • Synonym: power - the ability to control or influence others
  • Synonym: threat - an expression of intention to inflict harm or force

Etymology

Popularized from U.S. President Theodore Roosevelt's foreign policy phrase, 'speak softly and carry a big stick.'
