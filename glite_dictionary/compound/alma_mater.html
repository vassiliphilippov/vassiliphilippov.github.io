alma mater

1. NOUN

/ ˌælmə ˈmeɪtər / (AmE), / ˌælmə ˈmɑːtə / (BrE)
the school, college, or university from which one has graduated or with which one is formerly associated
school or university one graduated from

Examples

She returned to her alma mater to give a commencement speech.
Harvard is his alma mater and he often donates to its alumni fund.
Visiting my alma mater brings back many fond memories.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: university - an institution of higher education and research
  • Synonym: school - an educational institution; one where basic education is provided

Etymology

From Latin, meaning 'nourishing mother'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌælmə ˈmɑtɚ / (AmE), / ˌælmə ˈmɑːtə / (BrE)
the school, college, or university that someone formerly attended
school, college, or university once attended

Examples

He was proud to donate to his alma mater after he found success in business.
Many graduates return to their alma mater for reunions and homecoming events.
She often credited her alma mater for shaping her professional values.

Synset Examples

He joked that his alma mater was the University of Spoiled Children.

Etymology

From Latin alma mater, meaning 'nourishing mother.'
