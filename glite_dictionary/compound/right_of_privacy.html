right of privacy

1. NOUN

/ raɪt əv ˈpraɪvəsi / (AmE), / raɪt əv ˈprɪvəsi / (BrE)
the legal right to be left alone and free from unwarranted intrusion or publicity, especially regarding personal matters
legal right to personal privacy
legal

Examples

The Supreme Court has interpreted the Constitution to include a right of privacy in certain circumstances.
Many advocates argue that internet users deserve a right of privacy in their online activities.
Concerns about surveillance have increased public awareness of the right of privacy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: privacy - the state of being free from public attention
  • Antonym: publicity - the state of being public or widely known
