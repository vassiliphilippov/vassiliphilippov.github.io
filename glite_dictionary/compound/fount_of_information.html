fount of information

1. NOUN

/ faʊnt əv ɪn.fɚˈmeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / faʊnt əv ɪn.fəˈmeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
a person or thing that provides a great deal of knowledge or information
source of abundant knowledge or information

Examples

The old professor was considered a fount of information by his students.
With so much experience, she became the team's fount of information on all technical matters.
Our local museum acts as a fount of information about the town's history.

Synset Examples

The library is a fount of information for researchers.
She is a fount of knowledge on ancient history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fountain of knowledge - a source or reservoir of knowledge
  • Synonym: fount of wisdom - a person or thing providing a great deal of wisdom

Etymology

From 'fount' (a literary term for 'fountain', ultimately from Latin 'font-', 'fons' meaning 'spring' or 'source').
