family Homaridae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli hoʊˈmærɪdi / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli həʊˈmærɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of large-clawed lobsters in the order Decapoda, including the common edible lobsters of the North Atlantic
the family of large-clawed lobsters

Examples

The family Homaridae contains all the true lobsters, including the Atlantic lobster commonly found in seafood markets.
Scientists have studied the evolution of the family Homaridae to understand claw development.
Members of the family Homaridae are economically important due to their popularity as seafood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lobster - a large marine crustacean with a muscular tail and claws; a member of Homaridae

Etymology

From New Latin Homaridae, based on Homarus (the genus of true lobsters) and Greek 'idae' (indicating a family).
