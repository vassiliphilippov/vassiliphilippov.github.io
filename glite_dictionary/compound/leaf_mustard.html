leaf mustard

1. NOUN

/ lif ˈmʌstɚd / (AmE), / liːf ˈmʌstəd / (BrE)
a type of mustard plant grown for its edible leaves, which are eaten as cooked greens or used in salads
mustard plant with edible leaves

Examples

Stir-fried leaf mustard is a popular dish in Southern cuisine.
She grows leaf mustard in her garden each spring for fresh greens.
The soup was flavored with garlic and leaf mustard to add a spicy kick.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mustard greens - leaves of the mustard plant, used as a vegetable
  • Synonym: mustard plant - plant grown for its seeds and leaves

2. NOUN

/ lif ˈmʌstərd / (AmE), / liːf ˈmʌstəd / (BrE)
A type of Asiatic mustard plant cultivated for its edible leaves, commonly used as a potherb in cooking.
Asiatic mustard grown for edible leaves

Examples

The chef sautéed leaf mustard with garlic and soy sauce for a flavorful side dish.
In some Asian cuisines, pickled leaf mustard is a common accompaniment to rice and noodles.
She planted leaf mustard in her garden to enjoy fresh greens throughout the spring.
