chronocidal act

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɹoʊ.noʊˈsaɪ.dəl ˈækt / (AmE), / ˌkɹɒn.əʊˈsaɪ.dəl ˈækt / (BrE)
an act that disrupts or damages the normal flow of time, especially in science fiction contexts
an act that disrupts time

Examples

The villain's chronocidal act caused years of history to unravel instantly.
Scientists theorized that a chronocidal act could have devastating effects on the universe.
Every chronocidal act in fiction raises questions about fate and free will.

Synset Examples

In the story, the chronocidal act disrupted the timeline.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: time paradox - a logical contradiction involving time travel

Etymology

From Greek 'chronos' (time) and English 'cidal' (killing, destroying), modeled after 'homicidal'.
