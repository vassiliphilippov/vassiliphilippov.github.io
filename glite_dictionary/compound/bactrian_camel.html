Bactrian camel

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæktriən ˈkæməl / (AmE), / ˈbæktrɪən ˈkæməl / (BrE)
A large camel native to central Asia, distinguished by the two humps on its back, adapted to cold desert environments.
two-humped camel of central Asia

Examples

The Bactrian camel thrives in the harsh, cold deserts of Mongolia and China.
Unlike the dromedary, the Bactrian camel has two humps on its back.
Zookeepers carefully monitored the newborn Bactrian camel as it adapted to its new environment.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dromedary - one-humped camel of northern Africa and western Asia

Etymology

From Bactria, an ancient region in Central Asia, combined with 'camel'.
