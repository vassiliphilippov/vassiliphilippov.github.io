Territorial Army

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɛrɪˈtɔriəl ˈɑrmi / (AmE), / ˌtɛrɪˈtɔːriəl ˈɑːmi / (BrE)
A British military force of nonprofessional soldiers, organized as a volunteer reserve for the defense of Great Britain.
British volunteer military reserve force
british

Examples

After leaving university, he joined the Territorial Army to gain leadership experience.
Many members of the Territorial Army were deployed to support regular troops during emergencies.
The Territorial Army offers opportunities for civilians to serve part-time in the military.

Etymology

From 'territorial' (relating to territory) and 'army', first established in the United Kingdom in 1908.
