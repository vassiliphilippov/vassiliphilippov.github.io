penetration bomb

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛnəˈtreɪʃən bɑm / (AmE), / ˌpɛnɪˈtreɪʃən bɒm / (BrE)
A bomb with a strong casing and about 30% explosive, designed to penetrate hardened targets before detonating.
bomb for penetrating hardened targets

Examples

The military deployed a penetration bomb to destroy the underground bunker.
Engineers designed the penetration bomb to withstand impact with reinforced concrete targets.
Satellite images showed the effects of several penetration bombs on the enemy facility.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bunker-buster - bomb designed to penetrate bunkers and hardened structures
