skeletal frame

1. NOUN

/ ˈskɛlətl freɪm / (AmE), / ˈskɛlɪtəl freɪm / (BrE)
the internal supporting structure of an object, often made of metal or another strong material, that gives it its overall shape and strength
supporting structure inside an object

Examples

The skyscraper's skeletal frame was clearly visible before the walls were added.
A car's safety depends greatly on the strength of its skeletal frame.
The architect designed the museum with a unique skeletal frame to allow for open, airy spaces.

Synset Examples

the building has a steel skeleton

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skeleton - the supporting framework of a structure or organism
