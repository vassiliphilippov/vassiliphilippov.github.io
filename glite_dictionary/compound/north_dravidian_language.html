North Dravidian language

1. NOUN

/ nɔrθ drəˈvɪdiən ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / nɔːθ drəˈvɪdɪən ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
A subgroup of Dravidian languages spoken primarily in eastern India, including languages such as Brahui, Kurukh, and Malto.
subgroup of Dravidian languages in eastern India

Examples

The North Dravidian language group includes lesser-known tongues like Kurukh and Malto.
Linguists study how the North Dravidian language family differs from its southern counterparts.
Revitalization efforts are underway to preserve several North Dravidian language dialects.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: South Dravidian language - a subgroup of Dravidian languages spoken in southern India

Etymology

From 'Dravidian', the major language family of southern India, and 'North', referring to the geographic region.
