modern-day

1. ADJ

/ ˈmɑdərn ˌdeɪ / (AmE), / ˈmɒdən ˌdeɪ / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of the present time; contemporary.
characteristic of the present time

Examples

Social media has had a huge influence on modern-day communication.
Many modern-day cities face challenges from pollution and overcrowding.
This book explores modern-day issues through the lens of history.

Synset Examples

contemporary trends in design
the role of computers in modern-day medicine

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contemporary - existing or happening in the same period; modern
  • Synonym: modern - relating to the present or recent times
  • Antonym: antiquated - old-fashioned or outdated
