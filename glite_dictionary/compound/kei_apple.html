kei apple

1. NOUN

/ keɪ ˈæpəl / (AmE), / keɪ ˈæpəl / (BrE)
A vigorous, spiny shrub native to South Africa, cultivated for its round, yellow, juicy, edible fruits.
South African spiny shrub with edible yellow fruits

Examples

The hedge of kei apple was thick enough to keep animals out of the garden.
Her grandmother makes a delicious jam from homegrown kei apple fruits.
The kei apple thrives in warm climates and produces fruit in late summer.

Etymology

From the Kei River in South Africa, where the plant is native, combined with 'apple' due to the fruit's resemblance.
