sustaining pedal

1. NOUN

/ səˈsteɪnɪŋ ˈpɛdəl / (AmE), / səˈsteɪnɪŋ ˈpɛdəl / (BrE)
a pedal on a piano that lifts the dampers from the strings, allowing the notes to continue sounding after the keys are released
piano pedal that sustains notes

Examples

She pressed the sustaining pedal to let the final chord resonate throughout the hall.
Many piano pieces require careful use of the sustaining pedal for expressive effects.
If you hold down the sustaining pedal, all the notes blend together.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soft pedal - a piano pedal that makes the sound quieter
  • Synonym: damper pedal - another name for the sustaining pedal on the piano
