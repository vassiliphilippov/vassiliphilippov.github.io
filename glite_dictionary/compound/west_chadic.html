West Chadic

1. NOUN

/ wɛst tʃædɪk / (AmE), / wɛst tʃadɪk / (BrE)
a group of related Chadic languages spoken mainly in northern Nigeria, with Hausa being the most prominent member
group of Chadic languages in Nigeria

Examples

Many linguists study the West Chadic languages to understand the structure and history of Hausa.
Hausa, the largest West Chadic language, is spoken widely across northern Nigeria.
The West Chadic group contains several smaller languages that are less well-known than Hausa.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Chadic - a branch of the Afro-Asiatic language family spoken in parts of Central and West Africa
  • Degree Lower: Hausa - the most widely spoken language of the West Chadic group

Etymology

Formed from 'West' and 'Chadic'; 'Chadic' from Lake Chad, denoting languages of the area.
