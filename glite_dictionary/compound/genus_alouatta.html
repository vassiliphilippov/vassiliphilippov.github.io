genus Alouatta

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌæ.luːˈæt̬.ə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌæl.uːˈæt.ə / (BrE)
A genus of large Central and South American monkeys known as howler monkeys, recognized for their loud howling calls.
a genus of howler monkeys

Examples

The genus Alouatta includes some of the loudest animals in the New World forests.
Researchers observed several species within the genus Alouatta in the Amazon rainforest.
Members of the genus Alouatta are easily recognized by their distinctive howling calls.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: howler monkey - a large New World monkey known for its loud calls

Etymology

From Latin 'Alouatta', used in scientific nomenclature for the howler monkeys' genus.
