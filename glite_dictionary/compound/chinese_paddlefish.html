Chinese paddlefish

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃaɪ.niz ˈpæd.əlˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈtʃaɪ.niːz ˈpæd.əl.fɪʃ / (BrE)
a large freshwater fish (Psephurus gladius) native to China's Yangtze River, known for its long, paddle-shaped snout and resemblance to the American paddlefish
large Yangtze River fish with paddle-shaped snout

Examples

The Chinese paddlefish was once a common sight in the Yangtze River before its decline.
Scientists have declared the Chinese paddlefish extinct due to severe habitat loss.
Unlike its American relative, the Chinese paddlefish had a more slender body and longer snout.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paddlefish - large freshwater fish with a long, flat snout
