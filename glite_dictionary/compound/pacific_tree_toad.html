Pacific tree toad

1. NOUN

/ pəˈsɪfɪk tri toʊd / (AmE), / pəˈsɪfɪk tri təʊd / (BrE)
a small frog species commonly heard along the Pacific coast of America, known for its distinctive call
frog species native to Pacific coast of America

Examples

On our hike, we heard the Pacific tree toad calling from the marsh.
Researchers study the Pacific tree toad to monitor environmental changes in the region.
The distinct sound of the Pacific tree toad is often associated with spring on the West Coast.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Pacific tree frog - another common name for the same amphibian species native to the Pacific coast
