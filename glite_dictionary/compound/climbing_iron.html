climbing iron

1. NOUN

/ ˈklaɪmɪŋ ˌaɪɚn / (AmE), / ˈklaɪmɪŋ ˌaɪən / (BrE)
an iron spike or device attached to footwear to prevent slipping on ice while walking or climbing
iron spike for climbing on ice

Examples

The mountaineer strapped a climbing iron to each boot before ascending the icy slope.
Without a climbing iron, it would have been impossible to cross the frozen river safely.
Older explorers often relied on a simple climbing iron when traveling in snowy regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crampon - a device with metal spikes for walking on ice, often used in mountaineering

2. NOUN

/ ˈklaɪmɪŋ ˌaɪɚn / (AmE), / ˈklaɪmɪŋ ˌaɪən / (BrE)
a spiked metal frame that attaches to the boot to assist with climbing trees or poles
spiked frame for climbing trees or poles

Examples

The worker strapped a climbing iron to each boot before ascending the telephone pole.
Arborists often rely on climbing irons to safely reach high branches.
She demonstrated how to properly attach a climbing iron for the training session.
