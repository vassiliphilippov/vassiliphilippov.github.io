genus Lathyrus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ləˈθaɪ.rəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ləˈθaɪ.rəs / (BrE)
A genus of climbing herbs, including vetchlings and wild peas, found in the Old World and temperate regions of North and South America.
genus of vetchlings and wild peas

Examples

Several species within the genus Lathyrus are valued for their ornamental flowers.
Peas and vetchlings of the genus Lathyrus are common in temperate meadows.
The genus Lathyrus includes both edible and inedible species.

Etymology

From Latin 'Lathyrus', the classical name for a type of pea or vetch.
