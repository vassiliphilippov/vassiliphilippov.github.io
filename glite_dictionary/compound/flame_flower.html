flame flower

1. NOUN

/ fleɪm ˈflaʊɚ / (AmE), / fleɪm ˈflaʊə / (BrE)
A plant with fleshy roots, erect stems, narrow succulent leaves, and a single reddish-orange flower in each upper leaf axil, native to the southwestern United States.
plant with reddish-orange flowers, southwestern US

Examples

The flame flower blooms brilliantly in the deserts of the American Southwest.
Researchers studied the medicinal uses of the flame flower among local indigenous peoples.
The narrow succulent leaves help the flame flower survive in arid climates.

2. NOUN

/ fleɪm ˈflaʊ.ɚ / (AmE), / fleɪm ˈflaʊ.ə / (BrE)
A plant of the genus Kniphofia, cultivated for its long, grasslike leaves and tall spikes of bright red or yellow drooping flowers.
plant with tall spikes of red or yellow flowers

Examples

The striking red blooms of the flame flower add a tropical touch to the garden.
We planted several flame flowers along the driveway for a burst of color in summer.
Gardeners prize the flame flower for its tall, vibrant flower spikes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Kniphofia - Genus of perennial plants with torch-like flower spikes
