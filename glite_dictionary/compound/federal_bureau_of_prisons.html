Federal Bureau of Prisons

1. PROPN

/ ˈfɛdərəl bjuˈroʊ əv prɪˈzɑnz / (AmE), / ˈfɛdərəl ˈbjʊərəʊ əv ˈprɪzənz / (BrE)
the agency of the U.S. Department of Justice responsible for operating federal prisons and detention facilities, housing inmates convicted of federal crimes
U.S. federal prison authority
american

Examples

After his conviction, he was placed in a facility run by the Federal Bureau of Prisons.
The Federal Bureau of Prisons is responsible for the management and regulation of all federal correctional institutions.
Overcrowding is a persistent issue faced by the Federal Bureau of Prisons in the United States.
