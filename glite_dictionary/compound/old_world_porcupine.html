Old World porcupine

1. NOUN

/ oʊld wɜrld ˈpɔrkjəˌpaɪn / (AmE), / əʊld wɜːld ˈpɔːkjʊˌpaɪn / (BrE)
A large, heavily-built rodent of the family Hystricidae, native to Europe, Asia, and Africa, distinguished by its spiny covering.
large spiny rodent of Europe, Asia, and Africa

Examples

The Old World porcupine defends itself with sharp, stiff quills when threatened by predators.
Unlike its American relatives, the Old World porcupine prefers to forage on the ground rather than climb trees.
Researchers observed an Old World porcupine searching for food at dusk near the edge of the forest.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: New World porcupine - a porcupine native to the Americas, of the family Erethizontidae
