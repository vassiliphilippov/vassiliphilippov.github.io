stamp duty

1. NOUN

/ stæmp ˈduːti / (AmE), / stæmp ˈdjuːti / (BrE)
a tax that must be paid on certain legal documents, usually by purchasing and affixing a stamp to the document
tax on legal documents requiring a stamp
legal british

Examples

When buying a new house, you may be required to pay stamp duty as part of the legal process.
The government increased stamp duty rates on high-value property transactions.
He carefully calculated the total cost, including stamp duty and registration fees.
