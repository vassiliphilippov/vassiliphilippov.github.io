middle-of-the-road

1. ADJ

/ ˌmɪdəl əv ðə ˈroʊd / (AmE), / ˌmɪdəl əv ðə ˈrəʊd / (BrE)
Having views, policies, or tastes that are moderate and not extreme, especially in politics.
moderate; not extreme (esp. in politics)

Examples

Many voters are attracted to middle-of-the-road policies that avoid extremes.
She prefers middle-of-the-road music that appeals to a wide audience.
His middle-of-the-road stance made him popular among moderate members of the party.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moderate - not extreme in belief or action
  • Antonym: radical - far-reaching or extreme in political or social views
  • Antonym: extreme - very far from what most people consider reasonable or normal
