wild hollyhock

1. NOUN

/ waɪld ˈhɑliˌhɑk / (AmE), / waɪld ˈhɒlɪˌhɒk / (BrE)
A perennial mallow native to western North America, known for its purple flowers and sometimes cultivated in gardens.
North American perennial mallow with purple flowers

Examples

There are clusters of wild hollyhock growing along the hillside trail.
Gardeners appreciate wild hollyhock for its resilience and vibrant purple blooms.
On our trip to Utah, we spotted several wild hollyhock plants in full flower.
