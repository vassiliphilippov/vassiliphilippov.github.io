genus Fumaria

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒinəs fjuːˈmɛəriə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs fjuːˈmɑːrɪə / (BrE)
A group of annual herbaceous plants whose flowers have a single petal spurred at the base, belonging to the genus Fumaria.
a genus of annual herbs with spurred flowers

Examples

The genus Fumaria includes several species commonly found in European fields.
Botanists are studying the medicinal properties of plants within the genus Fumaria.
Members of the genus Fumaria are known for their delicate, spurred flowers.

Etymology

From Latin 'fumaria', meaning 'smoke', referring to the smoky appearance or the smoke-like smell of some species.
