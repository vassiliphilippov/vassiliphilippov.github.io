family Geomyidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli dʒioʊˈmɪɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli dʒiːəʊˈmɪɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of burrowing rodents commonly known as North American pocket gophers
family of North American pocket gophers

Examples

The family Geomyidae consists mainly of burrowing rodents native to North America.
Biologists have conducted extensive research on the fossorial habits of the family Geomyidae.
Members of the family Geomyidae are often called pocket gophers due to their distinctive cheek pouches.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pocket gopher - common name for members of the family Geomyidae

Etymology

From New Latin 'Geomyidae', from 'Geomys' (a genus name) + '-idae' (a zoological family suffix).
