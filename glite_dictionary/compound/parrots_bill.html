parrot's bill

1. NOUN

/ ˈpærəts ˌbɪl / (AmE), / ˈpærəts ˌbɪl / (BrE)
an evergreen shrub native to New Zealand, with claw-shaped or beak-shaped scarlet to white flowers
New Zealand shrub with beak-like flowers

Examples

The parrot's bill is prized for its vibrant scarlet flowers in New Zealand gardens.
We saw a stand of parrot's bill along the forest path near Rotorua.
Botanists study the unique shape of the parrot's bill flower to understand its pollination.

Etymology

Named for the flower's resemblance to a parrot's beak.
