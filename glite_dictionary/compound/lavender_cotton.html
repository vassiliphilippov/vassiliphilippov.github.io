lavender cotton

1. NOUN

/ ˈlævəndɚ ˈkɑntn̩ / (AmE), / ˈlævəndə ˈkɒtn̩ / (BrE)
A branching aromatic Mediterranean shrub with woolly stems and leaves and yellow flowers, often grown as an ornamental plant.
aromatic Mediterranean shrub with yellow flowers

Examples

The gardener planted lavender cotton to add fragrance and color to the border.
Dried lavender cotton can be used in herbal sachets for its pleasant scent.
Lavender cotton thrives in well-drained, sunny locations typical of Mediterranean climates.
