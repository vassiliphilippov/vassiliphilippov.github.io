order Naiadales

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdər naɪ.əˈdeɪliːz / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə naɪ.əˈdeɪliːz / (BrE)
an order of aquatic monocotyledonous herbaceous plants, comprising mainly submerged freshwater species
order of aquatic monocotyledonous plants

Examples

The genus Najas belongs to the order Naiadales, well known for its submerged aquatic plants.
Botanists have revised the classification of the order Naiadales several times as new research emerges.
Many species in the order Naiadales play an important ecological role in freshwater habitats.

Etymology

From Latin 'Naias', 'Naiadis' (water nymph), and botanical order suffix '-ales'.
