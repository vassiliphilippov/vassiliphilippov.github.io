genus Shigella

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ʃɪˈɡɛl.ə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ʃɪˈɡɛl.ə / (BrE)
a genus of gram-negative, rod-shaped, nonmotile bacteria that can cause severe intestinal infections in humans
bacterial genus causing intestinal infections
medical

Examples

The laboratory confirmed the presence of genus Shigella in the patient's stool sample.
Outbreaks of dysentery are often linked to contamination by bacteria of the genus Shigella.
Proper sanitation can help prevent the spread of diseases caused by genus Shigella.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Shigella - the type genus of bacteria in the genus Shigella
  • Degree Lower: Escherichia - a closely related bacterial genus

Etymology

From the name of Japanese bacteriologist Kiyoshi Shiga, who first described the genus.
