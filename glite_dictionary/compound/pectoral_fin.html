pectoral fin

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛk.tɚ.əl fɪn / (AmE), / ˈpɛk.tər.əl fɪn / (BrE)
either of a pair of fins on the sides of a fish's body, just behind the head, used to control the direction of movement
side fin of a fish, just behind the head

Examples

The clownfish uses its pectoral fin to steer through coral reefs with great precision.
You can recognize the species by the distinct markings on its pectoral fin.
A damaged pectoral fin can make it hard for the fish to maintain its balance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fin - a flattened appendage on various parts of the body of many aquatic animals, used for propulsion, steering, and balancing

Etymology

From Latin 'pectus' (chest) + 'fin'.
