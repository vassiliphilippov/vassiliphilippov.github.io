genus Myrica

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs məˈraɪ.kə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs mɪˈraɪ.kə / (BrE)
A group of deciduous aromatic shrubs or small trees in the bayberry family, found in temperate and subtropical regions.
genus of aromatic shrubs and small trees

Examples

The genus Myrica encompasses several species known for their fragrant leaves and waxy berries.
Botanists have studied the genus Myrica for its ecological role in coastal environments.
Some members of the genus Myrica are commonly used to make candles from their berry wax.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bayberry - type of aromatic shrub or tree in the same genus

Etymology

From New Latin 'Myrica', from Greek 'myrike' (tamarisk).
