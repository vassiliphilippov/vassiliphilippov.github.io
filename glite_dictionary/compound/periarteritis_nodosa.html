periarteritis nodosa

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛriɑːrˈtɛɹɪtɪs noʊˈdoʊsə / (AmE), / ˌpɛriɑːtɪˈraɪtɪs nəʊˈdəʊsə / (BrE)
a progressive disease of connective tissue in which nodules form along the arteries, sometimes leading to blockage and poor blood circulation to affected areas
disease causing nodules in arteries
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed her condition as periarteritis nodosa after noting the characteristic nodules in her arteries.
Patients with periarteritis nodosa may experience pain due to compromised blood flow.
Advances in treatment have improved the prognosis for those suffering from periarteritis nodosa.

Etymology

From Greek 'peri-' (around) + 'arteria' (artery) + Latin 'nodosa' (knotty, with nodes).
