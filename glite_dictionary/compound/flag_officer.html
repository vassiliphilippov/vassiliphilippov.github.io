flag officer

1. NOUN

/ flæɡ ˈɑfəsər / (AmE), / flæɡ ˈɒfɪsə / (BrE)
a senior officer in a navy above the rank of captain, entitled to fly a flag to denote command
senior naval officer above captain

Examples

The newly appointed flag officer inspected the fleet during the annual naval review.
Promotion to flag officer brought him significant new responsibilities and authority.
Only a flag officer can authorize the launch of the destroyers in wartime.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: admiral - the highest-ranking naval officer

Etymology

From the military tradition of officers entitled to fly a personal flag indicating their command.
