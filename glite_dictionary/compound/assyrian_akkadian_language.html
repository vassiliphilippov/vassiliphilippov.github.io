Assyrian Akkadian language

1. NOUN

/ əˈsɪriən əˈkeɪdiən ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / əˈsɪəriən əˈkeɪdiən ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
an extinct Semitic language spoken by the Assyrians in ancient Mesopotamia
extinct language of ancient Assyria

Examples

Scholars have studied the Assyrian Akkadian language to better understand ancient Mesopotamian history.
Many clay tablets are inscribed with cuneiform script in the Assyrian Akkadian language.
Interest in the Assyrian Akkadian language has grown among historians and linguists.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Akkadian - the Semitic language of ancient Babylonia and Assyria

Etymology

Assyrian from Assyria (ancient region), Akkadian from Akkad (ancient Mesopotamian kingdom/language).
