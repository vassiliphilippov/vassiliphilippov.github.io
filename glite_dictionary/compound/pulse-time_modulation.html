pulse-time modulation

1. NOUN

/ ˌpʌlst ˈtaɪm ˌmɑːdʒəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌpʌlst ˈtaɪm ˌmɒdjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
a method of encoding information in which the time intervals between successive pulses are varied according to the signal being transmitted
modulation by varying time between pulses

Examples

Engineers use pulse-time modulation to improve signal transmission in noisy environments.
The quality of digital communication relies on precise pulse-time modulation techniques.
Understanding pulse-time modulation is essential for designing modern telecommunication systems.

Etymology

Formed from 'pulse', 'time', and 'modulation', terms from electronics and communication engineering.
