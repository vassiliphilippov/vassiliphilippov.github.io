shire town

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃaɪɚ taʊn / (AmE), / ˈʃaɪə taʊn / (BrE)
a town or city that serves as the administrative center of a county, especially in parts of England and New England, USA
county administrative center

Examples

Montpelier is the shire town of Washington County in Vermont.
The courthouse is located in the shire town, making it a hub for local government.
Many early settlers chose to live near the shire town for convenience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: county seat - the administrative center of a county, especially in the United States

Etymology

From 'shire', an old term for county, and 'town', meaning settlement or municipality.
