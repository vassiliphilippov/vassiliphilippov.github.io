real matrix

1. NOUN

/ ˈriːəl ˈmeɪ.trɪks / (AmE), / ˈrɪəl ˈmæ.trɪks / (BrE)
A matrix whose elements are all real numbers.
matrix with all real-number elements

Examples

The coefficients in this equation form a real matrix.
Any real matrix can be multiplied by a scalar.
The eigenvalues of a real matrix may be complex numbers.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: complex matrix - a matrix whose elements are all complex numbers
