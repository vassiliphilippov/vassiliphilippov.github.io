family Chenopodiaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ˌkiːnoʊˌpoʊdiˈeɪsiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli ˌkiːnəʊpəʊdiːˈeɪsiː / (BrE)
a family of flowering plants that includes spinach, beets, and related species, often known as the goosefoot family
the plant family including spinach and beets

Examples

Spinach, as a member of the family Chenopodiaceae, shares certain characteristics with beets and quinoa.
Botanists once classified many salt-tolerant plants in the family Chenopodiaceae.
The family Chenopodiaceae includes several species important for their edible leaves and roots.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Amaranthaceae - the plant family often combined with Chenopodiaceae in modern classification

Etymology

From Latin 'Chenopodium' (goosefoot) + '-aceae' (a suffix denoting plant families).
