skid row

1. NOUN

/ skɪd ˈroʊ / (AmE), / skɪd ˈrəʊ / (BrE)
an area of a city where many homeless people, alcoholics, and drug addicts live or gather
district with many homeless and addicts

Examples

After losing his job, he ended up living on skid row for several months.
The city council is debating how best to address the problems on skid row.
Documentarians have often filmed life on skid row to raise awareness of homelessness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slum - an area of a city marked by poverty and poor living conditions

Etymology

Originally from logging slang in the US, referring to roads along which logs were 'skidded'; later applied to urban areas where destitute people congregated.

2. NOUN

/ skɪd roʊ / (AmE), / skɪd rəʊ / (BrE)
an area of a city where many homeless or impoverished people live, often marked by rundown buildings and poverty
run-down urban area with homelessness

Examples

After losing his job, Tom spent several months living on skid row in Los Angeles.
The documentary highlighted the struggles faced by people residing in skid row.
Local charities distribute food and blankets to those in need on skid row.

Synset Examples

She volunteered at a shelter near skid row to help the needy.
He ended up on skid row after losing his job.
Many homeless individuals live on skid row in the city.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slum - a heavily populated urban area marked by substandard housing and squalor
  • Synonym: ghetto - a part of a city, often poor, where a minority group lives

Etymology

Originated in early 20th-century North America, from 'skid road', a path for sliding logs, later applied to rundown urban districts.
