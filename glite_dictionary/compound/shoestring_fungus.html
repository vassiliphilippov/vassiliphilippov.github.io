shoestring fungus

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃuˌstrɪŋ ˈfʌŋɡəs / (AmE), / ˈʃuːstrɪŋ ˈfʌŋɡəs / (BrE)
A fungus of the genus Armillaria that forms brown stringy structures and causes destructive root rot in certain trees, such as apples and maples.
fungus causing root rot in trees

Examples

The old orchard was devastated by shoestring fungus, which killed several apple trees.
Gardeners must watch for signs of shoestring fungus when caring for vulnerable tree species.
Shoestring fungus is known for its string-like rhizomorphs that spread underground, attacking tree roots.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Armillaria - a genus of fungi including the shoestring fungus
