greater etatism

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡreɪtər eɪˈtɑːtɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪtə eɪˈtætɪzəm / (BrE)
a higher degree of government control or intervention in the economy
increased state economic control

Examples

The government's new reforms led to greater etatism in key industries.
Debates over greater etatism often focus on its impact on innovation and efficiency.
Analysts noted a trend toward greater etatism following the economic crisis.

Synset Examples

The country moved towards greater etatism in recent years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: etatism - policy of state control over economic and social affairs
  • Antonym: laissez-faire - policy of minimal government interference in the economy

Etymology

From French 'étatisme' (state-ism), with 'greater' indicating a higher extent.
