Korean lespedeza

1. NOUN

/ kəˈriːən lɛs.pəˈdiː.zə / (AmE), / kəˈriːən lɛs.pɪˈdiː.zə / (BrE)
an annual plant native to Korea, widely cultivated in hot, dry regions for forage and hay
annual plant used for forage and hay

Examples

Farmers in the southern United States often grow Korean lespedeza as a drought-resistant forage crop.
The Korean lespedeza fields turned green earlier than other forages in the spring.
Because it tolerates poor soils, Korean lespedeza is a practical choice for many livestock producers.
