catty behavior

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæti bəˈheɪvjɚ / (AmE), / ˈkæti bəˈheɪvjə / (BrE)
spiteful or malicious behavior, often marked by subtle insults or backbiting, especially in social groups
spiteful or malicious conduct

Examples

Rumors and catty behavior made the office a stressful place to work.
She was tired of all the catty behavior at the party last night.
The television show is known for its drama and catty behavior among contestants.

Synset Examples

The catty behavior among the group was obvious.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spiteful - showing or caused by malice; intending to hurt or offend
  • Synonym: malicious - intending or intended to do harm

Etymology

Formed from 'catty' (spiteful, malicious) + 'behavior'. 'Catty' comes from the comparison to the stereotypical behavior of cats.
