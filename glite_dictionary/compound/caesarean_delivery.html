caesarean delivery

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈzɛəriən dɪˈlɪvəri / (AmE), / siˈzɛːriən dɪˈlɪvəri / (BrE)
a surgical procedure in which a baby is delivered through an incision in the mother's abdominal wall and uterus
surgical birth via incision
medical

Examples

Due to complications, the doctor recommended a caesarean delivery rather than a natural birth.
She recovered quickly after her caesarean delivery and was able to go home with her baby.
A caesarean delivery can be planned in advance or carried out as an emergency procedure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cesarean section - the same surgical procedure to deliver a baby

Etymology

From Latin 'Caesareus', associated with Julius Caesar, from the mistaken belief that he was born this way.
