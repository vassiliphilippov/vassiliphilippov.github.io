Meckel's diverticulum

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛkəlz dɪˌvɝːtɪˈkjuːləm / (AmE), / ˈmɛkəlz dɪˌvɜːtɪˈkjuːləm / (BrE)
a congenital pouch or bulge in the lower part of the small intestine (ileum), resulting from incomplete closure of the embryonic yolk sac
congenital pouch in the small intestine
medical

Examples

The surgeon suspected Meckel's diverticulum as the cause of the patient's abdominal pain.
Complications of Meckel's diverticulum can include bleeding, inflammation, or bowel obstruction.
An ultrasound revealed that the child had a Meckel's diverticulum in his small intestine.

Etymology

Named after Johann Friedrich Meckel, German anatomist (1781–1833), who described the condition.
