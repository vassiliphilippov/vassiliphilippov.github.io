siris tree

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪrɪs tri / (AmE), / ˈsʌɪrɪs triː / (BrE)
A large tree native to the Old World, with broad leaves, rounded clusters of greenish-yellow flowers, and long seed pods that make a clattering sound in the wind.
large Old World tree with clattering seed pods

Examples

The siris tree is easily recognized by its clusters of greenish-yellow flowers and rattling seed pods.
In spring, the leaves of the siris tree offer dense shade for people and animals alike.
Farmers often plant the siris tree along field borders to help prevent soil erosion.

Etymology

From Hindi 'sirīs', ultimately from Sanskrit 'śirīṣa'.
