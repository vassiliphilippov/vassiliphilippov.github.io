family Carapidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ˌkærəˈpaɪdi / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli ˌkærəˈpeɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of slender, elongated marine fishes known as pearlfishes, related to the Brotulidae, often living inside sea cucumbers and other invertebrates.
a family of marine fishes (pearlfishes)

Examples

Scientists discovered a new species in the family Carapidae inhabiting deep-sea environments.
Most members of the family Carapidae are known for living symbiotically inside sea cucumbers.
The unique anatomy of fishes in the family Carapidae allows them to slip into narrow crevices.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pearlfish - common name for fishes of the family Carapidae

Etymology

From New Latin Carapidae, based on the type genus Carapus.
