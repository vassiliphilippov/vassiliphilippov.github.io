Flacourtia family

1. NOUN

/ fləˈkɔrtiə ˈfæməli / (AmE), / fləˈkɔːtiə ˈfæməli / (BrE)
A family of chiefly tropical trees and shrubs, including the genus Flacourtia, known for their spiny branches and edible or ornamental fruit.
family of tropical trees and shrubs

Examples

Many botanists study the Flacourtia family for its unique adaptations to tropical environments.
Several members of the Flacourtia family produce edible fruits valued in local cuisines.
The Flacourtia family includes ornamental plants with spiny branches and attractive foliage.

Etymology

From New Latin Flacourtia (the type genus), named after Étienne de Flacourt, French governor of Madagascar.
