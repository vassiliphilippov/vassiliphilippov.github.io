nonmalignant tumour

1. NOUN

/ ˌnɑn.məˈlɪɡ.nənt ˈtuː.mɚ / (AmE), / ˌnɒn.məˈlɪɡ.nənt ˈtjuː.mə / (BrE)
A tumour that does not invade surrounding tissue or spread to other parts of the body; not cancerous.
tumour that is not cancerous
medical

Examples

The doctor confirmed that the growth was a nonmalignant tumour, so surgery was not urgently required.
Although a nonmalignant tumour is not cancerous, it may still need to be removed if it causes symptoms.
Regular monitoring is important to ensure a nonmalignant tumour does not change or grow larger.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: benign tumour - A tumour that is not harmful and does not invade nearby tissue
  • Antonym: malignant tumour - A cancerous tumour that invades tissue and can spread to other parts of the body

Etymology

From prefix 'non-' (not) + 'malignant' (from Latin 'malignus', malicious) + 'tumour' (from Latin 'tumor', swelling).
