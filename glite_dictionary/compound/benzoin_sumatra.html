benzoin Sumatra

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛnzoʊˌɪn suˈmɑːtrə / (AmE), / ˈbɛn.zɔɪn suːˈmɑː.trə / (BrE)
a type of benzoin resin obtained from the Styrax benzoin tree, native principally to the island of Sumatra, used in perfumes, incense, and pharmaceuticals.
Sumatran benzoin resin

Examples

Pharmaceutical companies often prefer benzoin Sumatra for its superior resin quality.
The export of benzoin Sumatra is important to the local economy of northern Sumatra.
She used benzoin Sumatra in her homemade incense recipes.

Synset Examples

Benzoin Sumatra is used in pharmaceutical preparations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: benzoin Siam - a type of benzoin resin obtained from Styrax tonkinensis, native to Southeast Asia

Etymology

From 'benzoin', an aromatic resin, and 'Sumatra', the island in Indonesia where the resin is predominantly sourced.
