musical phrase

1. NOUN

/ ˈmjuːzɪkəl freɪz / (AmE), / ˈmjuːzɪkəl freɪz / (BrE)
a short, complete musical thought or passage, typically forming a distinct section within a larger piece of music
short, complete musical passage

Examples

The violinist played a beautiful musical phrase that moved the audience to tears.
In classical compositions, each musical phrase often ends with a pause or cadence.
Composers use repetition and variation of a musical phrase to create interest in a piece.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phrase - a short section of music with a sense of completeness
