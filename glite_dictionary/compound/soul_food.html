soul food

1. NOUN

/ soʊl fuːd / (AmE), / səʊl fuːd / (BrE)
A style of food traditionally prepared and eaten by African-Americans in the southern United States, typically featuring ingredients such as greens, fried chicken, cornbread, and beans.
traditional African-American Southern cuisine

Examples

Every Sunday, her grandmother would prepare a feast of soul food for the whole family.
The restaurant is famous for its delicious soul food, especially the fried chicken and collard greens.
He grew up eating soul food and cherishes the flavors of his childhood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Southern cooking - food and cooking styles from the Southern United States

Etymology

Originated in the 1960s US Civil Rights era; 'soul' signifying African-American identity and pride.
