cervical glands of the uterus

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɝvɪkəl ɡlændz əv ði juˈtɪrəs / (AmE), / ˈsɜːvɪkəl ɡlændz əv ði juːˈtɪərəs / (BrE)
mucus-secreting glands located in the mucous membrane of the uterine cervix.
glands in uterine cervix secreting mucus
medical

Examples

The cervical glands of the uterus play a crucial role in producing mucus during ovulation.
Infections can affect the cervical glands of the uterus, leading to changes in cervical secretions.
Doctors examined the cervical glands of the uterus for signs of abnormal discharge.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cervix - the lower, narrow part of the uterus
