red elm

1. NOUN

/ rɛd ɛlm / (AmE), / rɛd ɛlm / (BrE)
A North American elm tree (Ulmus rubra) with rough leaves that are red when opening, valued for its hard wood.
North American elm tree with hard wood

Examples

The furniture was made from red elm, known for its durability and distinctive grain.
Botanists surveyed the red elm population in the forest to monitor its health.
The leaves of the red elm turn a striking red when they first emerge in spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slippery elm - another name for Ulmus rubra, commonly referenced for its mucilaginous inner bark

2. NOUN

/ rɛd ɛlm / (AmE), / rɛd ɛlm / (BrE)
An autumn-flowering elm tree native to the southeastern United States, known for its reddish bark and durable wood.
autumn-flowering elm of the southeastern US

Examples

The wood from the red elm is prized for its strength and resistance to splitting.
A mature red elm can be easily recognized by its rough, reddish bark.
Botanists are working to preserve native red elm populations in the southeastern United States.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ulmus rubra - the scientific name for red elm or slippery elm
