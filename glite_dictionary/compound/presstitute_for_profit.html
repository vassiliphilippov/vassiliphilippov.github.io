presstitute for profit

1. NOUN

/ prɛs.tɪ.tut fər ˈprɑfɪt / (AmE), / prɛs.tɪ.tjuːt fə ˈprɒfɪt / (BrE)
A derogatory term for journalists who compromise their ethical standards in exchange for money or financial gain.
derogatory term for unethical journalists
offensive slang

Examples

Some critics argue that certain media outlets are staffed with presstitutes for profit who distort the news.
He accused the magazine of hiring presstitutes for profit after reading their biased article.
The rise of presstitutes for profit has damaged public trust in the media.

Synset Examples

Many believe that some reporters act as presstitutes for profit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: presstitute - journalist who sacrifices integrity for personal gain
  • Degree Lower: journalist - a person who writes for newspapers, magazines, or news websites

Etymology

Blend of 'press' and 'prostitute'; formed by analogy.
