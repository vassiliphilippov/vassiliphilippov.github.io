rein orchis

1. NOUN

/ reɪn ˈɔːrkɪs / (AmE), / reɪn ˈɔːkɪs / (BrE)
Any of several American wildflowers of the orchid family, characterized by a kidney-shaped lip.
American wildflowers with a kidney-shaped lip

Examples

The rare rein orchis blooms in the forests of the American West during spring.
Botanists study the rein orchis for its unique kidney-shaped flower lip.
We spotted several rein orchids growing along the damp meadow trail.

Etymology

Rein, from resemblance to the shape of a kidney (Latin 'renes' for kidney), and 'orchis' from Greek for orchid.
