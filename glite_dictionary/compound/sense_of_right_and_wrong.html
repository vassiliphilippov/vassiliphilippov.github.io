sense of right and wrong

1. NOUN

/ sɛns əv raɪt ənd rɔŋ / (AmE), / sɛns əv raɪt ənd rɒŋ / (BrE)
an awareness or understanding of what is morally right and morally wrong, guiding a person's thoughts and actions
moral awareness or conscience

Examples

She has a strong sense of right and wrong, so she always tries to do what's fair.
Even as a child, he showed a keen sense of right and wrong in his decisions.
A good leader needs a clear sense of right and wrong to earn people's trust.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conscience - a person's moral sense or inner feeling acting as a guide to rightness or wrongness of behavior
