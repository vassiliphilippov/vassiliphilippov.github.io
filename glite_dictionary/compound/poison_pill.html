poison pill

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɔɪzən pɪl / (AmE), / ˈpɔɪzən pɪl / (BrE)
A defensive strategy used by a company to make itself less attractive to a potential acquirer, often by making its stock less desirable.
strategy to deter company takeovers

Examples

The company adopted a poison pill to prevent a hostile takeover attempt.
Shareholders debated whether implementing a poison pill would truly protect their interests.
A poison pill provision can make it difficult for an unwanted suitor to buy the company.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: golden parachute - a large financial compensation for executives if forced out after a takeover
  • Antonym: hostile takeover - an attempt to acquire a company against the wishes of its management

Etymology

Metaphor based on a literal pill intended to cause harm, first used in US business contexts in the early 1980s.
