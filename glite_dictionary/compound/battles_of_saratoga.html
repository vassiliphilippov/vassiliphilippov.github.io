Battles of Saratoga

1. PROPN

/ ˈbæɾlz əv səˈrætəɡə / (AmE), / ˈbætlz əv səˈrætəɡə / (BrE)
the two major battles fought in 1777 during the American Revolution, resulting in the defeat of the British forces under General Burgoyne
two key American Revolution battles, 1777

Examples

Historians often cite the Battles of Saratoga as the turning point in the American Revolution.
The American victory at the Battles of Saratoga convinced France to support the revolutionaries.
Many students learn about the Battles of Saratoga in their history classes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Battle of Saratoga - either of the two battles making up the Battles of Saratoga
