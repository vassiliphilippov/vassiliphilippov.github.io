front-runner

1. NOUN

/ ˈfrʌntˌrʌnɚ / (AmE), / ˈfrʌntˌrʌnə / (BrE)
a person or thing considered most likely to win a competition or contest
likely winner in a competition

Examples

Maria is considered the front-runner in the mayoral election this year.
After winning the last three races, he became the clear front-runner in the championship.
Despite being the front-runner, she remained humble and focused on her goals.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: underdog - a competitor thought to have little chance of winning a contest
  • Synonym: favorite - a competitor considered most likely to win

Etymology

Formed from 'front' + 'runner', suggesting one who runs at the front.
