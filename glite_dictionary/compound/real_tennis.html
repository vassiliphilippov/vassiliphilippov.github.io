real tennis

1. NOUN

/ ˈriːəl ˈtɛnɪs / (AmE), / ˈriːəl ˈtɛnɪs / (BrE)
an ancient indoor racket sport, the original form of tennis, played on a specially designed court with walls
ancient indoor racket sport; precursor to modern tennis

Examples

Few people today have played real tennis, though it was popular among kings and nobles.
The rules and equipment of real tennis differ greatly from those of modern tennis.
He took lessons in real tennis at the historic court near his home.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: lawn tennis - the modern outdoor game of tennis

Etymology

From 'real' meaning 'royal' in Old French, indicating its association with royalty.
