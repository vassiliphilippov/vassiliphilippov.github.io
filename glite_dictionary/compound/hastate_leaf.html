hastate leaf

1. NOUN

/ ˈhæsteɪt ˌliːf / (AmE), / ˈhasteɪt ˌliːf / (BrE)
a leaf shaped like a spearhead, with flaring pointed lobes at the base
spearhead-shaped leaf with flared base lobes

Examples

The plant is easily identified by its hastate leaf structure.
Many wetland species display a distinctive hastate leaf form.
This wildflower’s hastate leaf shape sets it apart from other common varieties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sagittate leaf - arrowhead-shaped leaf with pointed lobes at the base

Etymology

From Latin hastatus (armed with a spear), from hasta (spear), referring to the spearhead shape.
