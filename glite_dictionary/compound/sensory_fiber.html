sensory fiber

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛn.sə.ri ˈfaɪ.bɚ / (AmE), / ˈsɛn.səri ˈfaɪ.bə / (BrE)
a nerve fiber that conducts impulses toward the central nervous system, transmitting sensory information
nerve fiber carrying sensory signals
medical

Examples

Damage to a sensory fiber can result in numbness or loss of sensation in certain areas of the body.
The neurologist explained how each sensory fiber detects touch, pain, or temperature changes.
Researchers are studying how sensory fibers transmit information from the skin to the brain.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: motor fiber - a nerve fiber that carries impulses away from the central nervous system

Etymology

From 'sensory' (relating to sensation), and 'fiber', from Latin 'fibra' (fiber, thread).
