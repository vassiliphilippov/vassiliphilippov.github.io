long saphenous vein

1. NOUN

/ lɔŋ səˈfinəs veɪn / (AmE), / lɒŋ səˈfiːnəs veɪn / (BrE)
the longest vein in the human body, extending from the foot to the groin where it joins the femoral vein
the longest vein in the human body
medical

Examples

The surgeon traced the long saphenous vein during the bypass procedure.
Varicose veins often develop in the long saphenous vein due to increased pressure.
Ultrasound examination revealed a clot in her long saphenous vein.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: great saphenous vein - another name for the long saphenous vein

Etymology

From Greek 'saphēnēs' (manifest, clearly visible), used to describe the easily seen vein in the leg.
