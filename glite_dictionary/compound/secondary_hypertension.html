secondary hypertension

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛkənˌdɛri haɪpərˈtɛnʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɛkənˈdɛəri haɪpəˈtɛnʃən / (BrE)
high blood pressure that is caused by another disease or medical condition
high blood pressure due to another condition
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed her with secondary hypertension caused by a kidney disorder.
Unlike primary hypertension, secondary hypertension often has an identifiable underlying cause.
Treating the root condition can sometimes resolve secondary hypertension.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: primary hypertension - high blood pressure not caused by another condition
