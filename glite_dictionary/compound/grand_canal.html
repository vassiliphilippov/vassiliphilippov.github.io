Grand Canal

1. PROPN

/ ɡrænd kəˈnæl / (AmE), / ɡrænd kəˈnal / (BrE)
the main waterway running through the city of Venice, Italy, known for its iconic S-shape and historic significance.
main waterway in Venice, Italy

Examples

The famous gondolas glide gracefully along the Grand Canal in Venice.
From our hotel balcony, we could watch boats pass by on the Grand Canal each morning.
Many of Venice’s most beautiful buildings can be seen lining the Grand Canal.

2. PROPN

/ ɡrænd kəˈnæl / (AmE), / ɡrænd kəˈnal / (BrE)
an extensive inland waterway in eastern China, stretching about 1,000 miles from Tianjin to Hangzhou, historically used for transportation and irrigation
major canal in eastern China

Examples

The Grand Canal played a crucial role in the economic development of ancient China.
Tourists often take boat rides along the Grand Canal to admire its scenic beauty and historical sites.
Trade flourished between the north and south due to the construction of the Grand Canal.
