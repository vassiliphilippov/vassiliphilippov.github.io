tolu balsam tree

1. NOUN

/ ˈtoʊlu bælˌsæm triː / (AmE), / ˈtəʊluː ˈbalsəm triː / (BrE)
a medium-sized tropical American tree (Myroxylon balsamum) that produces tolu balsam and has fragrant, durable wood used in high-quality furniture and cabinetmaking
tree yielding tolu balsam and fragrant wood

Examples

The tolu balsam tree is prized for its aromatic resin used in perfumes and medicines.
Furniture crafted from tolu balsam tree wood is both beautiful and durable.
The resin collected from the tolu balsam tree has a sweet, pleasant fragrance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: balsam tree - a tree yielding a balsam resin

Etymology

From Tolu, a region in northern Colombia, where the tree is native, and balsam, from Latin 'balsamum' (balm).
