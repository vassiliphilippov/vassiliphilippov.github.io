greengage plum

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡrinˌɡeɪdʒ plʌm / (AmE), / ˈɡriːnˌɡeɪdʒ plʌm / (BrE)
a sweet green or greenish-yellow variety of plum, known for its rich flavor
sweet green or yellowish plum

Examples

The jam was made from ripe greengage plums picked fresh from the garden.
She found a basket of greengage plums at the farmer's market and bought them for dessert.
Unlike ordinary plums, greengage plum has a distinct, rich sweetness that many people love.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plum - a smooth-skinned oval fruit with sweet flesh and a flattish pointed stone

Etymology

From 'greengage,' named after Sir William Gage, who imported the variety to England in the 18th century, and 'plum.'
