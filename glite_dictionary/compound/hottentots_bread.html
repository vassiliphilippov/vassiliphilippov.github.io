Hottentot's bread

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑːtəntɑts brɛd / (AmE), / ˈhɒtɪntɒts brɛd / (BrE)
the large, edible rootstock of the elephant's-foot plant, found in southern Africa and often used as a food source
edible rootstock of elephant's-foot

Examples

The explorers survived by eating Hottentot's bread they found in the wild.
Local communities have traditionally harvested Hottentot's bread for its nourishing rootstock.
When baked, Hottentot's bread develops a pleasantly sweet flavor and soft texture.

Etymology

The name refers to the Khoikhoi people (formerly called 'Hottentot', now considered offensive) and its use as a food staple.
