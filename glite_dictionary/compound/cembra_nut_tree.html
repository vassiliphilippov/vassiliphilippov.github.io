cembra nut tree

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛm.brə nʌt ˌtri / (AmE), / ˈsɛm.brə nʌt ˌtriː / (BrE)
a large European pine tree (Pinus cembra) with five-needle clusters, producing edible cembra nuts and a resinous sap
European pine tree yielding edible nuts

Examples

The cembra nut tree thrives in the high Alps and produces nutritious seeds known as cembra nuts.
Many birds and mammals feed on the seeds of the cembra nut tree during the winter.
The resin extracted from the cembra nut tree has been traditionally used for medicinal purposes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Swiss pine - another common name for Pinus cembra, the cembra nut tree

Etymology

From Latin cembra (the tree's species name); 'nut' from Old English hnutu; 'tree' from Old English trēow.
