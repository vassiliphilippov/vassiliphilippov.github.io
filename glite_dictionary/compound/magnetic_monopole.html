magnetic monopole

1. NOUN

/ mæɡˈnɛtɪk ˈmɑːnəˌpoʊl / (AmE), / mæɡˈnɛtɪk ˈmɒnəˌpəʊl / (BrE)
A hypothetical particle or object that has only one magnetic pole, either north or south, unlike ordinary magnets which have both.
hypothetical particle with a single magnetic pole

Examples

A magnetic monopole has never been observed in experimental physics, but it is predicted by some theories.
If magnetic monopoles exist, they could revolutionize our understanding of electromagnetism.
Some physicists search for magnetic monopole signatures in cosmic rays and particle accelerators.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: magnetic dipole - an object with both north and south magnetic poles

Etymology

From 'magnetic' (relating to magnetism) and 'monopole' (from Greek 'monos' meaning 'single' and 'polos' meaning 'axis, pole').
