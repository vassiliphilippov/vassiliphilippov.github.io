right of way

1. NOUN

/ ˌraɪt əv ˈweɪ / (AmE), / ˌraɪt əv ˈweɪ / (BrE)
the legal right to proceed before other vehicles or vessels in a particular situation, such as at intersections or on waterways.
priority to proceed (traffic law)

Examples

At the intersection, the driver on the right has the right of way.
Sailboats usually have right of way over powerboats in open water.
Failure to yield the right of way can result in a traffic ticket.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: priority - being regarded or treated as more important; coming first in order

2. NOUN

/ ˌraɪt əv ˈweɪ / (AmE), / ˌraɪt əv ˈweɪ / (BrE)
The legal right to pass along a specific route through grounds or property belonging to another person.
legal right to pass over another's property
legal

Examples

The farmer granted his neighbor a right of way across the field for access to the main road.
Disputes over the right of way can arise when building fences or barriers.
You must not block the right of way that allows walkers to pass through the private estate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: easement - a legal right to use another's land for a specific purpose

Etymology

From the combination of 'right' and 'way' in legal contexts, denoting a permitted passage.
