Bismarck Archipelago

1. PROPN

/ ˈbɪzˌmɑrk ˌɑrkəˈpeɪləˌgoʊ / (AmE), / ˈbɪzmɑːk ˌɑːkɪˈpeɪlɪɡəʊ / (BrE)
a group of islands in the southwestern Pacific Ocean, northeast of New Guinea, forming part of Papua New Guinea
island group in Papua New Guinea

Examples

The Bismarck Archipelago is known for its diverse marine life and coral reefs.
German explorers first arrived in the Bismarck Archipelago in the 19th century.
Many cultures and languages coexist in the Bismarck Archipelago today.

Etymology

Named after Otto von Bismarck, the German statesman, reflecting the period of German colonial influence in the region.
