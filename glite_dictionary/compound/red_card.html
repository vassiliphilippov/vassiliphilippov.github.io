red card

1. NOUN

/ rɛd kɑrd / (AmE), / rɛd kɑːd / (BrE)
a penalty in sports, especially football (soccer), where a player is sent off the field for a serious offense or foul play
penalty expelling a player in sports

Examples

The referee showed him a red card after a dangerous tackle.
Receiving a red card can change the entire outcome of a soccer match.
Two players were given red cards during the heated semi-final.

Synset Examples

The referee issued a red card, ending the match for him.
She received a red card for unsportsmanlike conduct.
Getting a red card means you can't play again that day.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: yellow card - penalty for a less serious offense in sports, indicating a warning rather than expulsion
