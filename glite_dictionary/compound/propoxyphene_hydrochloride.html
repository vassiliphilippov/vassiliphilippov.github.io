propoxyphene hydrochloride

1. NOUN

/ proʊˌpɑksiˌfin ˌhaɪdrəˈklɔːraɪd / (AmE), / prəˌpɒksɪˌfiːn ˌhaɪdrəˈklɔːraɪd / (BrE)
A mildly narcotic analgesic drug, related to methadone and less addictive than it, used to relieve mild to moderate pain.
mildly narcotic pain-relief drug
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed propoxyphene hydrochloride for her post-surgical pain.
Patients should be aware of potential side effects when taking propoxyphene hydrochloride.
Because of the risk of dependence, propoxyphene hydrochloride is used cautiously in pain management.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Darvon - trade name for propoxyphene

Etymology

From 'propoxyphene', a synthetic compound, plus 'hydrochloride', indicating its salt form.
