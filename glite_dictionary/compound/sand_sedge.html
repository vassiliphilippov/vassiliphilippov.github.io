sand sedge

1. NOUN

/ sænd ˈsɛdʒ / (AmE), / sand ˈsɛdʒ / (BrE)
A European grass-like plant of sandy areas, especially coastal regions; its rootstock is said to have properties similar to sarsaparilla.
maritime plant with sarsaparilla-like rootstock

Examples

The sand sedge thrives in coastal dunes and helps stabilize the sandy soil.
Gardeners sometimes use sand sedge as a ground cover in poor, sandy soils.
The rootstock of the sand sedge was once valued for its medicinal properties.
