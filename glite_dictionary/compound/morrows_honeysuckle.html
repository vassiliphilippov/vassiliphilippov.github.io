Morrow's honeysuckle

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑːroʊz ˈhʌniˌsʌkəl / (AmE), / ˈmɒrəʊz ˈhʌniˌsʌkl̩ / (BrE)
A grey deciduous honeysuckle shrub (Lonicera morrowii) native to Japan, with paired white flowers that turn yellow.
species of honeysuckle shrub from Japan

Examples

Invasive populations of Morrow's honeysuckle have spread throughout woodlands in North America.
The white blossoms of Morrow's honeysuckle gradually turn yellow as they age.
Gardeners are advised to avoid planting Morrow's honeysuckle due to its aggressive growth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: honeysuckle - climbing shrub with sweet-smelling flowers, genus Lonicera

Etymology

Named after American botanist James Morrow and the plant's genus Lonicera.
