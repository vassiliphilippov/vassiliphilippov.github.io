phenolic glycoside

1. NOUN

/ fəˈnoʊlɪk ˈɡlaɪkəˌsaɪd / (AmE), / fɪˈnɒlɪk ˈɡlaɪkəˌsʌɪd / (BrE)
a chemical compound consisting of a phenol group bonded to a sugar molecule, which can be hydrolyzed by acids, bases, or enzymes
compound of phenol and sugar
medical

Examples

Some plants store chemical defenses as phenolic glycosides to deter herbivores.
The laboratory synthesized a new phenolic glycoside with strong antioxidant properties.
Enzymatic hydrolysis breaks down a phenolic glycoside into its sugar and phenol components.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glycoside - a compound formed from a simple sugar and another compound by replacement of a hydroxyl group in the sugar molecule

Etymology

'Phenolic' from 'phenol' and '-ic'; 'glycoside' from Greek 'glykys' (sweet) and '-ide'.
