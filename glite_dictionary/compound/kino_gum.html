kino gum

1. NOUN

/ ˈkiːnoʊ ɡʌm / (AmE), / ˈkiːnəʊ ɡʌm / (BrE)
A reddish astringent gum obtained from several tropical trees and used in tanning and medicine.
astringent gum from tropical trees

Examples

The bark produces kino gum, which is valued for its astringent properties.
Tanners sometimes use kino gum to treat leather.
Traditional medicine utilized kino gum to heal wounds and stop bleeding.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: astringent - a substance that contracts body tissues

Etymology

From Hindi 'kino', referring to the tree, and English 'gum'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkiːnoʊ ɡʌm / (AmE), / ˈkiːnəʊ ɡʌm / (BrE)
a reddish or black resin obtained from certain Pterocarpus trees, used in medicine and for tanning leather
resin from certain trees, used in tanning

Examples

The tanner applied kino gum to treat the leather and protect it from pests.
Traditional medicine sometimes uses kino gum as an astringent for minor wounds.
Scientists studied the chemical structure of kino gum obtained from Pterocarpus trees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gum arabic - an edible, water-soluble gum obtained from acacia trees
  • Synonym: resin - a sticky substance produced by some plants and trees

Etymology

From Hindi kīno, referring to the tree species; 'gum' from Middle English, from Old French 'gomme', from Latin 'gummi'.
