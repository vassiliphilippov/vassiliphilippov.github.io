dried husk

1. NOUN

/ draɪd hʌsk / (AmE), / draɪd hʌsk / (BrE)
the outer shell of a seed or fruit that has become dry; often removed before eating or further processing
dry outer shell of seed or fruit

Examples

The corn was stripped of its dried husk before cooking.
After harvest, the dried husk crackled underfoot in the field.
She tossed the dried husk into the compost bin after peeling the peanuts.

Synset Examples

She used the dried husk for a craft project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: husk - outer covering of a seed or fruit
