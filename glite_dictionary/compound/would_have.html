would have

1. AUX

/ wʊd hæv / (AmE), / wʊd hav / (BrE)
used to indicate a possible action or event in the past that did not actually happen, often in conditional or hypothetical statements
expressing unreal past possibilities

Examples

If you had told me earlier, I would have helped you.
She would have gone to the party if she weren't sick.
We would have finished on time, but there was a delay.

Synset Examples

She'd've come if she hadn’t been busy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: could have - expressing past potential or possibility
  • Synonym: should have - expressing past obligation or expectation
