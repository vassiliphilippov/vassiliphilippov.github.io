cuneiform bone

1. NOUN

/ kjuˈniəfɔrm boʊn / (AmE), / kjuˈniːɪfɔːm bəʊn / (BrE)
a bone in the wrist that articulates with the pisiform, hamate, and lunate bones; also known as the triquetral bone
bone in the wrist (triquetral)
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that the fracture was in the cuneiform bone of the patient's wrist.
X-rays revealed that her cuneiform bone was not aligned correctly after the fall.
In anatomy class, we studied the position of the cuneiform bone relative to the other carpal bones.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: triquetral bone - another term for the cuneiform bone in the wrist

Etymology

From Latin 'cuneus' (wedge) + 'forma' (shape), referring to its wedge-like shape.
