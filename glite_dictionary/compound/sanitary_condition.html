sanitary condition

1. NOUN

/ ˈsænɪˌteri kənˈdɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈsænɪtəri kənˈdɪʃən / (BrE)
the state of cleanliness and hygiene of a place, object, or environment, especially regarding the presence or absence of dirt, germs, or contamination
state of cleanliness/hygiene

Examples

The inspector noted that the restaurant failed due to poor sanitary condition.
Maintaining a good sanitary condition in public restrooms is essential for public health.
The sanitary condition of the hospital was regularly monitored by staff.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hygiene - conditions or practices conducive to maintaining cleanliness and preventing disease
  • Antonym: unsanitary condition - the state of being dirty or unhygienic
