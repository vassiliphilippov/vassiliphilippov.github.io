Battles of Lexington and Concord

1. PROPN

/ ˈbæɾlz əv ˈlɛk.sɪŋ.tən ənd ˈkɑn.kɔrd / (AmE), / ˈbætəlz əv ˈlɛk.sɪŋ.tən ənd ˈkɒŋ.kɔːd / (BrE)
the first military engagements of the American Revolutionary War, fought on April 19, 1775, marking the outbreak of open armed conflict between the Thirteen Colonies and Britain
first battle of the American Revolution (1775)

Examples

Many historians consider the Battles of Lexington and Concord a turning point in the struggle for American independence.
Schoolchildren learn about Paul Revere's ride, which warned colonists before the Battles of Lexington and Concord.
The anniversary of the Battles of Lexington and Concord is commemorated every year in Massachusetts.
