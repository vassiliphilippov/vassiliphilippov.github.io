suffix notation

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌfɪks noʊˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈsʌfɪks nəʊˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
A mathematical notation in which operators are placed after their operands, eliminating the need for parentheses.
mathematical notation with operators after operands

Examples

Some calculators use suffix notation instead of the more common infix notation.
In suffix notation, the expression '3 4 +' means 3 plus 4.
Learning suffix notation can make it easier to design efficient parsers for calculators.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: postfix notation - a mathematical notation where each operator follows its operands
  • Antonym: infix notation - a mathematical notation where operators are between their operands (e.g., a + b)
