eternal damnation

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈtɜrnəl dæmˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪˈtɜːnəl dæmˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
The state of being condemned to endless punishment in Hell, especially as a consequence of sin.
endless punishment in Hell

Examples

The preacher warned of eternal damnation for those who strayed from the path of righteousness.
Medieval art frequently depicts sinners suffering eternal damnation as a cautionary tale.
She feared eternal damnation more than any earthly punishment.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: salvation - deliverance from sin and its consequences, often understood as the opposite of damnation
  • Synonym: hell - the place or state of punishment for the wicked after death

Etymology

'Eternal' from Latin 'aeternalis' (everlasting); 'damnation' from Latin 'damnatio' (condemnation).
