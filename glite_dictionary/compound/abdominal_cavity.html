abdominal cavity

1. NOUN

/ æbˈdɑm.ɪ.nl ˈkæv.ə.ti / (AmE), / æbˈdɒm.ɪ.nəl ˈkæv.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
the large cavity in the body containing the major internal organs, separated from the chest (thoracic cavity) by the diaphragm
body cavity containing major organs
medical

Examples

The surgeon made an incision to access the abdominal cavity.
Inflammation in the abdominal cavity can cause severe pain and other complications.
The liver, stomach, and intestines are all found inside the abdominal cavity.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: thoracic cavity - the cavity in the chest that contains the heart and lungs

Etymology

From Latin 'abdomen' (belly) and 'cavitas' (cavity, hollow).
