Simon Legree

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪmən ləˈgri / (AmE), / ˈsaɪmən ləˈgriː / (BrE)
An employer who is excessively harsh, demanding, or cruel toward employees.
harsh or cruel employer

Examples

The workers referred to their manager as a Simon Legree because of his relentless demands and lack of compassion.
No one wanted to work for a Simon Legree, fearing unreasonable hours and harsh criticism.
Her friends warned her not to become a Simon Legree after her promotion to supervisor.

Etymology

From Simon Legree, a brutal slave owner in Harriet Beecher Stowe’s novel 'Uncle Tom’s Cabin' (1852).
