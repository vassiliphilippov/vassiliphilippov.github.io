smoker's lung

1. NOUN

/ ˈsmoʊkərz lʌŋ / (AmE), / ˈsməʊkəz lʌŋ / (BrE)
lung disease or damage caused by prolonged smoking, typically resulting in reduced lung function and persistent coughing
lung disease from smoking

Examples

After twenty years as a heavy smoker, he was diagnosed with smoker's lung.
Doctors attribute many cases of smoker's lung to long-term tobacco use.
Her chronic cough was one of the first signs she had developed smoker's lung.

Synset Examples

He developed smoker's lung after years of smoking.
The doctor warned him about smoker's lung risks.
She was diagnosed with smoker's lung last year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chronic obstructive pulmonary disease - a group of lung diseases including emphysema and chronic bronchitis, often caused by smoking
  • Antonym: healthy lung - lung free from disease or smoking-related damage
