leporid mammal

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛpəˌrɪd ˈmæməl / (AmE), / ˈlɛpərɪd ˈmaməl / (BrE)
A mammal belonging to the family Leporidae, which includes rabbits and hares.
mammal of the Leporidae family (rabbits and hares)

Examples

The snowshoe hare is a well-known leporid mammal native to North America.
Scientists are studying the population dynamics of leporid mammals in this region.
Many leporid mammals are recognized by their large hind legs and long ears.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rabbit - a small mammal with long ears and short tail, of the family Leporidae
  • Synonym: hare - a fast-running mammal similar to a rabbit, of the family Leporidae

Etymology

From Latin leporidus (pertaining to hares), from lepus (hare) + mammal.
