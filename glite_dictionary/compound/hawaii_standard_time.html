Hawaii Standard Time

1. NOUN

/ həˈwaɪ.i ˈstæn.dɚd taɪm / (AmE), / həˈwaɪ.i ˈstæn.dəd taɪm / (BrE)
the standard time of the 10th time zone west of Greenwich, used in Hawaii and the western Aleutian Islands, 10 hours behind Coordinated Universal Time (UTC−10:00)
time zone for Hawaii and western Aleutians

Examples

Flights are scheduled according to Hawaii Standard Time to avoid confusion during the summer months.
When it's noon in New York, it's only 6 a.m. in Hawaii Standard Time.
The televised event will air at 8 p.m. Hawaii Standard Time across the islands.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Greenwich Mean Time - the mean solar time at the Royal Observatory in Greenwich, London, used as a global time standard
  • Antonym: Coordinated Universal Time - the primary time standard by which the world regulates clocks and time
