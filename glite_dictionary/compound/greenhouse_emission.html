greenhouse emission

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡrinˌhaʊs ɪˈmɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈɡriːnhaʊs ɪˈmɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
A gas released into the atmosphere that absorbs infrared radiation and contributes to the greenhouse effect, such as carbon dioxide or methane.
gas causing greenhouse effect

Examples

The government is working to reduce industrial greenhouse emissions to slow climate change.
Methane is a particularly potent greenhouse emission from agricultural activities.
Policies to limit greenhouse emissions are central to international climate agreements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: greenhouse gas - a gas that absorbs and emits radiant energy, contributing to the greenhouse effect
