Peter's pence

1. NOUN

/ ˈpiːtɚz ˌpɛns / (AmE), / ˈpiːtəz ˌpɛns / (BrE)
an annual contribution made by Roman Catholics to support the papal see
yearly Catholic contribution to the Pope

Examples

Every year, Catholic churches collect Peter's pence as a symbol of solidarity with the Pope.
The tradition of Peter's pence dates back to the 8th century in England.
Some parishioners made generous donations during the annual Peter's pence collection.

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'Sancti Petri denarius' (coin of St. Peter), referring to an offering to the Pope.
