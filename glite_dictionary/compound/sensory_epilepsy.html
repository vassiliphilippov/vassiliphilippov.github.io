sensory epilepsy

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛn.sə.ri ˌɛp.ɪˈlɛp.si / (AmE), / ˈsɛn.s(ə).ri ˌɛp.ɪˈlɛp.si / (BrE)
A type of focal epilepsy characterized by seizures that begin with sensory disturbances, such as tingling, numbness, or other somatosensory phenomena.
epilepsy starting with sensory symptoms
medical

Examples

The neurologist diagnosed the patient with sensory epilepsy after she reported episodes of tingling in her hand.
Patients with sensory epilepsy may experience sensations such as numbness or pins and needles before seizures.
Research into sensory epilepsy has led to better understanding of how sensory pathways can trigger seizures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: focal epilepsy - epilepsy in which seizures originate in a localized area of the brain

Etymology

From Latin 'epilepsia' (seizure disorder) and 'sensory', relating to sensation.
