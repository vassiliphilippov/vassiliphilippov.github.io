immune globulin

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈmjun ˈɡlɑbjəlɪn / (AmE), / ɪˈmjuːn ˈɡlɒbjʊlɪn / (BrE)
any of a class of proteins produced by lymph tissue in vertebrates that act as antibodies in the immune response
antibody protein produced in immune response
medical

Examples

The patient was given an injection of immune globulin to boost her resistance to the virus.
Doctors often prescribe immune globulin for people with weakened immune systems.
Researchers studied how immune globulin can help fight emerging infectious diseases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antibody - a protein produced by the immune system to neutralize pathogens

Etymology

From 'immune', Latin 'immunis' (exempt), and 'globulin', from Latin 'globulus' (small sphere).
