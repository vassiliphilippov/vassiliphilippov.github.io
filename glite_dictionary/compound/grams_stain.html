Gram's stain

1. NOUN

/ ɡræmz steɪn / (AmE), / ɡræmz steɪn / (BrE)
a laboratory staining technique used to classify bacteria as Gram-positive or Gram-negative by treating them with certain dyes and solutions
bacterial staining technique in microbiology
medical

Examples

The doctor used Gram's stain to identify the type of bacteria causing the infection.
Learning to prepare a Gram's stain is an essential skill in any microbiology laboratory.
When viewed under the microscope, the results of the Gram's stain determined the next steps in treatment.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Gram-negative - bacteria not retaining violet stain in Gram's method
  • Degree Higher: Gram-positive - bacteria retaining violet stain in Gram's method

Etymology

Named after Hans Christian Gram, the Danish bacteriologist who developed the method in 1884.
