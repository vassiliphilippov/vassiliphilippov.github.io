form of address

1. NOUN

/ fɔrm əv əˈdrɛs / (AmE), / fɔːm əv əˈdrɛs / (BrE)
a word or phrase used to refer to or address someone, indicating their status, rank, or relationship (e.g., 'Mr.', 'Dr.', 'General')
word/phrase indicating status or rank

Examples

The manager used the form of address 'Ms.' when speaking to her colleague.
In formal letters, it's important to choose the correct form of address.
Children are often taught to use a polite form of address when speaking to adults.

Synset Examples

the professor didn't like his friends to use his formal title

Related Terms

  • Synonym: title - a word such as 'Mr.' or 'Doctor' used before someone's name
