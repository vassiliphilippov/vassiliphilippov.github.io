hedgehog cactus

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛdʒˌhɑɡ ˈkæktəs / (AmE), / ˈhɛdʒˌhɒɡ ˈkæktəs / (BrE)
a cactus of the genus Echinocactus, characterized by stout, sharp spines often resembling the spines of a hedgehog
a spiny cactus of the genus Echinocactus

Examples

The hedgehog cactus is well adapted to survive in arid desert conditions.
Botanists studied the growth patterns of the hedgehog cactus during the blooming season.
I accidentally pricked my finger on a hedgehog cactus while hiking in Arizona.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Echinocactus - A genus of cacti known for their globular, spiny forms

Etymology

Named for its spiny appearance, resembling the spines of a hedgehog; from genus name Echinocactus (from Greek 'ekhinos', meaning 'hedgehog').
