genus Apogon

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˈæpəˌɡɑn / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈapəɡɒn / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Apogonidae, which includes small marine fishes known as cardinalfishes.
type genus of Apogonidae (cardinalfish)

Examples

The genus Apogon is noted for its brightly colored cardinalfishes found in tropical seas.
Researchers discovered a new species within the genus Apogon near Indonesia.
Many species of the genus Apogon are popular among saltwater aquarium enthusiasts.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Apogonidae - family of cardinalfishes to which genus Apogon belongs

Etymology

From Latin 'genus' (kind) and the genus name 'Apogon', from Greek 'apogon' (beardless).
