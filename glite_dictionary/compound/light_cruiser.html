light cruiser

1. NOUN

/ laɪt ˈkruːzər / (AmE), / laɪt ˈkruːzə / (BrE)
a small or medium-sized warship with moderate armor and armament, typically faster and less heavily armored than a heavy cruiser.
small or medium armored warship

Examples

The navy deployed a light cruiser to patrol the coastline during the conflict.
During World War II, the light cruiser played a vital role in escorting convoys across the Atlantic.
He researched the specifications of the light cruiser before writing his historical novel.

Synset Examples

After 1930, most naval powers concentrated on building light cruisers since they had already built up to the maximum limitations allowed under the Washington treaty.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: heavy cruiser - a larger warship with greater armor and heavier guns than a light cruiser

Etymology

Formed from 'light' (meaning comparatively small or less heavily armed) and 'cruiser' (from Dutch kruiser, a patrolling warship).
