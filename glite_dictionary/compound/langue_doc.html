Langue d'oc

1. NOUN

/ lɑ̃ɡ dɔk / (AmE), / lɑ̃ɡ dɒk / (BrE)
A group of medieval dialects of French formerly spoken in the south of France, distinguished by their word for 'yes' ('oc').
medieval dialects of southern France

Examples

The troubadours composed their poems and songs in Langue d'oc.
Linguists study the differences between Langue d'oc and Langue d'oïl dialects.
Langue d'oc played a significant role in the development of southern French culture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Occitan - the modern Romance language descended from Langue d'oc dialects
  • Antonym: Langue d'oïl - medieval northern French dialects distinguished by their word for 'yes' ('oïl')

Etymology

French, literally 'language of oc', with 'oc' being the word for 'yes' in these dialects.
