frontal bone

1. NOUN

/ ˈfrʌn.t̬l̩ boʊn / (AmE), / ˈfrʌn.tl̩ bəʊn / (BrE)
the large bone forming the front part of the skull, including the forehead and the upper part of the eye sockets
bone at the front of the skull
medical

Examples

The frontal bone protects the brain and forms the shape of the forehead.
A fracture in the frontal bone can be serious and requires medical attention.
The surgeon explained that the frontal bone is located above the eye sockets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parietal bone - the bone forming the top and sides of the skull
  • Synonym: occipital bone - the bone forming the back and base of the skull

Etymology

From Latin 'frons, frontis' (forehead) + English 'bone'.
