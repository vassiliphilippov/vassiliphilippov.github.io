Montezuma's revenge

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɑn.tɪˈzuː.məz rɪˈvɛndʒ / (AmE), / ˌmɒn.tɪˈzuː.məz rɪˈvɛndʒ / (BrE)
diarrhea, especially experienced by travelers, contracted in Mexico or Central America and caused by unfamiliar food or water
travelers' diarrhea in Mexico or Central America
informal

Examples

Many tourists suffer from Montezuma's revenge after drinking tap water in Mexico.
She tried to avoid Montezuma's revenge by only eating cooked foods on her trip.
The traveler spent two days in bed because of a bad case of Montezuma's revenge.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: traveler's diarrhea - diarrhea experienced by travelers in foreign countries

Etymology

Named with reference to Montezuma II, the Aztec emperor; originally a humorous allusion to revenge on foreign invaders.
