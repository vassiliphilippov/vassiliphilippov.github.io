Turkish Hizballah

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɜːrkɪʃ hɪzˈbɑːlɑː / (AmE), / ˈtɜːkɪʃ hɪzˈbɑːlɑː / (BrE)
an ethnic Kurdish Sunni extremist group formed in southeastern Turkey in the late 1980s, seeking to establish an Islamic state governed by sharia law; known for acts of violence against journalists and businessmen and supported by Iran.
Kurdish Sunni extremist group in Turkey

Examples

Authorities have linked several attacks to the militant group Turkish Hizballah.
The influence of Turkish Hizballah in southeastern Turkey increased during the 1990s.
International reports highlight the violent activities carried out by Turkish Hizballah against journalists.

Etymology

From Turkish 'Türk Hizbullahı', borrowing from Arabic 'ḥizb allāh' (party of God).
