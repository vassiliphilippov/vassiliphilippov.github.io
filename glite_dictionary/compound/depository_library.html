depository library

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈpɑzɪˌtɔri ˈlaɪˌbrɛri / (AmE), / dɪˈpɒzɪtəri ˈlaɪbrəri / (BrE)
a library designated to receive, store, and make accessible official publications, especially government documents, for public use
library for official government documents

Examples

The university became a depository library for government publications in 1967.
As a depository library, it receives free copies of official documents.
Many researchers use the depository library to access historical records.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: library - a building or room containing a collection of books or other resources
