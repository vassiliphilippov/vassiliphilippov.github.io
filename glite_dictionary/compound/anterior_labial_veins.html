anterior labial veins

1. NOUN

/ ænˈtɪriər ˈleɪbiəl veɪnz / (AmE), / ænˈtɪəriə ˈleɪbɪəl veɪnz / (BrE)
veins that drain blood from the labia majora to the external pudendal vein in the human body
veins from labia majora to external pudendal vein
medical

Examples

The anterior labial veins are important for draining blood from the external genitalia.
During surgery, care must be taken to avoid damaging the anterior labial veins.
Inflammation of the anterior labial veins can cause localized swelling and discomfort.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: external pudendal vein - vein that drains blood from the external genitalia

Etymology

From Latin 'anterior' (before) and 'labium' (lip), with 'vein' from Old French 'veine'.
