trial-and-error

1. ADJ

/ ˌtraɪəl ən ˈɛrər / (AmE), / ˌtraɪəl ən ˈɛrə / (BrE)
relating to a method of solving problems by trying various methods and learning from mistakes until success is achieved, rather than by following theory or rules.
solving problems by experimenting and learning from mistakes

Examples

The team developed its new process using a trial-and-error approach, learning from each unsuccessful attempt.
He improved his skills through a trial-and-error method rather than formal training.
Many scientific discoveries result from trial-and-error experimentation rather than direct planning.

Synset Examples

they adopted a trial-and-error procedure

Related Terms

  • Synonym: experimental - based on experience or trial rather than theory

Etymology

From the nouns 'trial' and 'error', first recorded in this combination in the late 1800s.

2. ADJ

/ ˌtraɪ.əl ən ˈɛr.ɚ / (AmE), / ˌtraɪ.əl ən ˈɛr.ər / (BrE)
a method of achieving a desired result by repeatedly trying different approaches and learning from mistakes until success is achieved
method involving repeated attempts and learning from mistakes

Examples

They improved the system by using a trial-and-error approach.
Learning to solve the puzzle required some trial-and-error.
Through trial-and-error, she finally found the right password.

Synset Examples

he argued that all learning is a trial-and-error process that resembles biological evolution

Related Terms

  • Synonym: experimentation - the process of trying out new ideas, methods, or activities
