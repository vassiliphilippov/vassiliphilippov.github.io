Olduvai Gorge

1. PROPN

/ ˌoʊl.duˌvaɪ ˈɡɔrdʒ / (AmE), / ˌɒl.dʊˌvaɪ ˈɡɔːdʒ / (BrE)
a steep-sided ravine in northeastern Tanzania, famous as an archaeological site where some of the earliest human remains have been discovered
archaeological site in Tanzania

Examples

The discovery of ancient tools at the Olduvai Gorge provided important clues about early human life.
Scientists from around the world travel to Olduvai Gorge to study its fossil-rich deposits.
Many textbooks mention Olduvai Gorge as a key site for understanding human evolution.

Etymology

Named after the nearby Olduvai area in Tanzania; 'Olduvai' comes from the Maasai word meaning 'the place of wild sisal'.
