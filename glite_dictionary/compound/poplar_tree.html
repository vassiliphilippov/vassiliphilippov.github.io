poplar tree

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑplɚ tri / (AmE), / ˈpɒplə ˌtriː / (BrE)
a tall, fast-growing tree of temperate regions with light, soft wood and flowers borne in catkins, belonging to the genus Populus
a type of tall tree with soft wood

Examples

Several poplar trees lined the driveway, their leaves rustling softly in the wind.
The furniture was crafted from the wood of the poplar tree because of its lightness.
In spring, the poplar tree produces long catkins that dangle from its branches.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poplar - tree of the genus Populus

Etymology

From Latin 'populus' (poplar).
