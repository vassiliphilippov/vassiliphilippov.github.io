clear battle line

1. NOUN

/ klɪr ˈbætəl laɪn / (AmE), / klɪə ˈbætəl laɪn / (BrE)
a clearly defined division between two opposing parties or positions, especially in a dispute or conflict
a distinct division between opposing sides

Examples

The debate drew a clear battle line between the two factions.
Her harsh comments established a clear battle line between supporters and critics.
The reforms created clear battle lines across political parties.

Synset Examples

The debate drew a clear battle line between the two factions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: battle line - a line along which opposing armies face each other
  • Antonym: middle ground - a position of compromise between opposing views

Etymology

From the military term 'battle line,' meaning a line formed by troops during battle, with 'clear' emphasizing its distinctness.
