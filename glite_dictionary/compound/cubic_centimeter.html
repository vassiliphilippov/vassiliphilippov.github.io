cubic centimeter

1. NOUN

/ ˈkjuː.bɪk ˈsɛn.təˌmiː.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈkjuː.bɪk ˈsɛn.tɪˌmiː.tə / (BrE)
a metric unit of volume equal to one thousandth of a liter, often used in scientific and medical contexts
metric unit of volume (1/1000 of a liter)

Examples

The engine's displacement is measured in cubic centimeters for motorcycles and compact cars.
A doctor might use a syringe that holds exactly five cubic centimeters of liquid.
One cubic centimeter of water has a mass of approximately one gram.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: milliliter - a metric unit of volume equal to one thousandth of a liter

Etymology

From Latin cubus (cube) and centrum (center, meter); related to centimeter, meaning one hundredth of a meter.
