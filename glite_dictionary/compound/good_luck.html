good luck

1. NOUN

/ ɡʊd lʌk / (AmE), / ɡʊd lʌk / (BrE)
a situation or event in which something pleasant or desirable happens by chance; a fortunate occurrence
a fortunate event or stroke of chance

Examples

It was just good luck that I found my lost keys on the way home.
Winning the lottery was pure good luck for him and his family.
She attributed her promotion to a bit of good luck and a lot of hard work.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: bad luck - an occurrence of something undesirable or unfortunate by chance
  • Synonym: fortune - luck, especially as it affects someone's life or future

/ ɡʊd lʌk / (AmE), / ɡʊd lʌk / (BrE)

Examples

Some people believe that finding a four-leaf clover brings good luck.
They wished him good luck before his big interview.
