estradiol patch

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛstrəˈdaɪˌɔl pætʃ / (AmE), / ˌɛstrəˈdiːɒl pætʃ / (BrE)
a medicated transdermal patch that delivers estradiol into the bloodstream, used to treat estrogen deficiency and in hormone replacement therapy
transdermal patch delivering estradiol
medical

Examples

Her doctor recommended using an estradiol patch to manage her menopausal symptoms.
The estradiol patch should be applied to clean, dry skin on the lower abdomen.
Many women find the estradiol patch more convenient than taking daily pills.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hormone replacement therapy - the use of hormones to supplement hormone levels, especially in menopause

Etymology

'Estradiol' from estrane (steroid nucleus) + -diol (two alcohol groups); 'patch' from Old French 'pieche'.
