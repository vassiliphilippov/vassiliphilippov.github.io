great circle

1. NOUN

/ ɡreɪt ˈsɝkəl / (AmE), / ɡreɪt ˈsɜːkl̩ / (BrE)
a circle on the surface of a sphere that is formed by the intersection with a plane that passes through the sphere's center; it represents the largest possible circle on the sphere.
largest circle on a sphere's surface

Examples

Airplanes often fly along a great circle route to minimize travel distance.
Any longitude line on the globe is part of a great circle.
The equator is a classic example of a great circle on Earth.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: small circle - a circle on a sphere formed by a plane that does not pass through its center
