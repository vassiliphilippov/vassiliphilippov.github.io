blue tulip

1. NOUN

/ blu ˈtuːlɪp / (AmE), / bluː ˈtjuːlɪp / (BrE)
a short, hairy perennial plant with blue-violet or lilac flowers that blooms in early spring, native to North America and Siberia
a blue or lilac-flowered spring perennial plant

Examples

The blue tulip blooms early in spring, adding a pop of color to the garden.
Many botanical gardens feature the blue tulip due to its vibrant flowers.
She was delighted to find a rare blue tulip during her hike in Siberia.
