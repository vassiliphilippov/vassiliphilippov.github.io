signal fire

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪɡnəl ˌfaɪɚ / (AmE), / ˈsɪɡnəl ˌfaɪə / (BrE)
A fire that is deliberately lit to send a signal, usually as a means of communication or to attract attention.
fire lit to send a signal

Examples

The stranded hikers built a signal fire on the beach, hoping a passing ship would notice them.
After spotting the signal fire on the hilltop, the rescue team rushed to the location.
Villagers used a signal fire to warn of approaching danger in ancient times.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beacon - a fire or light set up as a warning, signal, or guide
