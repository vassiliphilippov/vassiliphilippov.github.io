sliding door

1. NOUN

/ ˈslaɪdɪŋ dɔr / (AmE), / ˈslaɪdɪŋ dɔː / (BrE)
a door that opens horizontally by sliding, usually on a track, rather than swinging on hinges
a door that opens by sliding sideways

Examples

We installed a sliding door to save space in the kitchen.
The balcony was accessible through large glass sliding doors.
She closed the sliding door quietly behind her before leaving the room.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: swing door - a door that opens by swinging on hinges

2. NOUN

/ ˈslaɪdɪŋ dɔr / (AmE), / ˈslaɪdɪŋ dɔː / (BrE)
A missed opportunity or chance that could have led to a significant change in one's life.
missed chance for significant change
informal

Examples

She often wonders if that breakup was a sliding door in her life.
Accepting the overseas position turned out to be a sliding door moment for his career.
Missing the train that day was a sliding door that changed everything for her.

Synset Examples

She missed her chance like a sliding door shutting in her face.
That job offer was a sliding door to a better future.

Etymology

From the 1998 film 'Sliding Doors', in which small events lead to vastly different outcomes.
