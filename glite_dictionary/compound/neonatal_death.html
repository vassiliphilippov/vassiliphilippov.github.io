neonatal death

1. NOUN

/ ni.oʊˌneɪ.t̬əl ˈdɛθ / (AmE), / niː.əʊˌneɪ.təl ˈdɛθ / (BrE)
the death of a liveborn infant within the first 28 days of life
death of a newborn within 28 days
medical

Examples

The hospital has taken new measures to reduce neonatal death rates.
Premature birth is one of the main causes of neonatal death worldwide.
Researchers are studying how access to healthcare impacts neonatal death statistics.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: infant mortality - death rate of infants under one year old

Etymology

From 'neonatal' (Latin 'neo' meaning 'new' + 'natalis' meaning 'of birth') and 'death'.
