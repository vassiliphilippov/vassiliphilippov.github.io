punched tape reader

1. NOUN

/ pʌntʃt teɪp ˈriːdər / (AmE), / pʌntʃt teɪp ˈriːdə / (BrE)
A device that reads information encoded on punched tape by detecting the pattern of holes.
device for reading punched tape

Examples

The punched tape reader was essential for transferring programs into early computers.
Engineers routinely checked the punched tape reader for jammed or misaligned tape.
With the advent of newer storage technologies, the punched tape reader became largely obsolete.

Synset Examples

The tape reader used compressed air, which passed through the holes and was directed into certain mechanisms of the caster.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: punched tape - length of paper tape with holes punched to encode data
