genus Cuscuta

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs kəsˈkuː.tə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs kʌsˈkjuː.tə / (BrE)
A genus of twining, leafless parasitic plants lacking chlorophyll, commonly known as dodder.
genus of parasitic plants (dodder)

Examples

The genus Cuscuta is notable for its leafless, thread-like appearance and lack of chlorophyll.
Many species in the genus Cuscuta are agricultural pests due to their parasitic nature.
Botanists study the genus Cuscuta to understand the evolution of plant parasitism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dodder - parasitic plant of the genus Cuscuta

Etymology

From Latin 'Cuscuta', a type of dodder plant, used as the genus name in scientific classification.
