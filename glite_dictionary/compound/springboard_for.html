springboard for

1. NOUN

/ ˈsprɪŋbɔrd fɔr / (AmE), / ˈsprɪŋbɔːd fə / (BrE)
A thing that serves as a starting point for further development, progress, or success.
starting point for progress or advantage

Examples

Winning the local championship became a springboard for her successful international career.
The internship acted as a springboard for his entry into the tech industry.
Their collaboration was a springboard for new ideas and creative solutions.

Synset Examples

This project is a springboard for future innovations.
He used the competition as a springboard to launch his startup.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: launchpad - a base or starting point for launching something

Etymology

From 'springboard' (a board for jumping from in gymnastics or diving) used metaphorically.
