Battle of Flodden Field

1. PROPN

/ ˈbæt̬l əv ˈflɑd(ə)n ˈfiːld / (AmE), / ˈbætl əv ˈflɒdən ˈfiːld / (BrE)
a battle fought in 1513 near Branxton, Northumberland, in which the English army defeated the invading Scots and King James IV of Scotland was killed
1513 battle between England and Scotland

Examples

The Battle of Flodden Field was a turning point in Anglo-Scottish relations.
After the Battle of Flodden Field, the Scottish monarchy entered a period of instability.
Many ballads remember the bravery of those who fought at the Battle of Flodden Field.
