consumer durables

1. NOUN

/ kənˈsuːmɚ ˈdʊrəbəlz / (AmE), / kənˈsjuːmə ˈdjʊərəbəlz / (BrE)
goods bought by consumers that are intended to last for a long time and are not quickly consumed, such as appliances, cars, and furniture
long-lasting consumer goods

Examples

The demand for consumer durables like refrigerators and washing machines often increases during economic booms.
Many companies offer installment plans to help people purchase expensive consumer durables.
Unlike food or paper products, consumer durables are designed to be used repeatedly over several years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: consumer goods - goods purchased by individuals for personal use
