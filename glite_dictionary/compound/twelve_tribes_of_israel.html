Twelve Tribes of Israel

1. PROPN

/ ˈtwɛlv ˌtraɪbz əv ɪzˈrɛl / (AmE), / ˈtwɛlv ˌtraɪbz əv ɪzˈrɪəl / (BrE)
the twelve traditional kin groups of ancient Israel, each descended from one of the twelve sons of Jacob, according to the Hebrew Bible
the twelve traditional tribes of ancient Israel

Examples

The Twelve Tribes of Israel are a foundational concept in the history of the Jewish people.
Many ancient texts describe the journey of the Twelve Tribes of Israel and their settlement in Canaan.
Biblical historians often analyze the origins and significance of the Twelve Tribes of Israel.

Etymology

Refers to the biblical narrative in the Hebrew Bible attributing the ancestry of the tribes to Jacob, also known as Israel.
