absorption factor

1. NOUN

/ əbˈzɔrpʃən ˈfæktɚ / (AmE), / əbˈzɔːpʃən ˈfæktə / (BrE)
(physics) a measure of the fraction of incident radiation or sound absorbed by a body, indicating its capacity to absorb energy.
measure of absorbed radiation or sound

Examples

The absorption factor of the material must be determined before using it for insulation.
Scientists measured the absorption factor to understand how much sound the wall could absorb.
Changes in temperature can affect the absorption factor of certain substances.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: absorption coefficient - a coefficient representing how much a material absorbs a particular radiation or sound
