peat moss

1. NOUN

/ pit mɑs / (AmE), / piːt mɒs / (BrE)
A pale or ashy moss of the genus Sphagnum, whose partially decayed remains are used as soil conditioner and for other horticultural purposes.
type of moss used for peat and soil conditioning

Examples

Gardeners often add peat moss to potting soil to improve moisture retention.
Large fields of peat moss are found in northern wetlands and bogs.
He used peat moss to acidify the soil for his blueberry bushes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sphagnum - a genus of mosses forming peat bogs
