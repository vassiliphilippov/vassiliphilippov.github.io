stone crab

1. NOUN

/ stoʊn kræb / (AmE), / stəʊn kræb / (BrE)
a large edible crab found along the southern coast of the United States, especially in Florida, known for its hard shell and prized claws
large edible crab of southern US coast

Examples

The stone crab is highly sought after in Florida restaurants for its sweet, succulent meat.
During our vacation, we went to a market and bought fresh stone crabs for dinner.
Harvesting stone crab claws is carefully regulated to protect the species.

2. NOUN

/ stoʊn kræb / (AmE), / stəʊn krab / (BrE)
a species of crab with pale, delicate flesh, native to Florida and prized as seafood
Florida crab with delicate flesh

Examples

The restaurant's special tonight features fresh stone crab claws served with mustard sauce.
Due to overfishing, wild stone crab populations have become much rarer in recent years.
He traveled to Florida just to taste authentic stone crab during the harvest season.
