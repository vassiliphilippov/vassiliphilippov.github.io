winter mushroom

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪntɚ ˈmʌʃˌruːm / (AmE), / ˈwɪntə ˈmʌʃrʊm / (BrE)
an edible mushroom with a viscid orange to brown cap and a velvety stalk, available in early spring or late fall, often growing in clusters
edible mushroom available in cold seasons

Examples

During our autumn hike, we found a cluster of winter mushrooms growing on a fallen log.
Chefs value the winter mushroom for its delicate flavor and late-season availability.
You can recognize the winter mushroom by its smooth cap and darkening velvety stalk.
