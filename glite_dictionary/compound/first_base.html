first base

1. NOUN

/ ˌfɝst ˈbeɪs / (AmE), / ˌfɜːst ˈbeɪs / (BrE)
the first of the four bases that a runner must touch in order to score a run in baseball
first of four bases in baseball

Examples

The runner sprinted to first base after hitting the ball.
She played first base for her university's softball team.
He narrowly avoided being tagged out at first base.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: second base - the base following first base in baseball

2. NOUN

/ ˌfɝst ˈbeɪs / (AmE), / ˌfɜːst ˈbeɪs / (BrE)
the fielding position of the player on a baseball team who is stationed at the first of the four bases in the infield, located counterclockwise from home plate
baseball fielding position at first base

Examples

The team's new player will be covering first base for the rest of the season.
He scooped up the ball and stepped on first base for the out.
During practice, she learned how to play first base effectively.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: first baseman - the player who occupies first base

3. NOUN

/ ˌfɝst ˈbeɪs / (AmE), / ˌfɜːst ˈbeɪs / (BrE)
the initial or earliest stage of progress toward achieving something
initial stage of progress

Examples

Despite hours of negotiations, the two sides never got to first base.
Getting an interview with the CEO was just first base in our plan to secure a partnership.
I presented my proposal, but I didn’t even reach first base with the board.

Synset Examples

we didn't get to first base with that approach

Related Terms

  • Synonym: square one - the starting point; beginning stage of a process
  • Antonym: final stage - the last phase or completion of a process

Etymology

Originally from American baseball, where 'first base' is the first of four bases that a player must touch to score a run.
