lady-in-waiting

1. NOUN

/ ˈleɪdi ɪn ˈweɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈleɪdi ɪn ˈweɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
a woman, usually of noble birth, appointed to attend and assist a queen or princess
female attendant to a queen or princess

Examples

The queen's most trusted lady-in-waiting accompanied her to every public event.
During the ceremony, each lady-in-waiting stood behind the princess, ready to assist her needs.
Historical dramas often feature a lady-in-waiting offering advice and companionship to royalty.
