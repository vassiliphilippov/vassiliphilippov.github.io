balsam fir

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɔlsəm fɜr / (AmE), / ˈbɔːlsəm fɜː / (BrE)
a medium-sized evergreen tree (Abies balsamea) native to northeastern North America, with aromatic leaves; commonly used for pulpwood and as Christmas trees
a North American aromatic fir tree

Examples

The balsam fir is popular as a Christmas tree because of its shape and pleasant scent.
Many paper products are made from the wood of the balsam fir.
When you crush the needles of a balsam fir, they release a distinctive, refreshing aroma.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fir - a type of evergreen coniferous tree with needle-like leaves

Etymology

From 'balsam', referring to the tree's fragrant resin, and 'fir', from Old English 'fyrhð'.
