iris family

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪ.rɪs ˌfæm.ə.li / (AmE), / ˈaɪ.rɪs ˌfæm.ɪ.li / (BrE)
a large family (Iridaceae) of perennial herbaceous plants with rhizomes, corms, or bulbs, including irises and related species
family of perennial plants, Iridaceae

Examples

Many beautiful garden flowers, such as crocuses and gladioli, belong to the iris family.
The iris family includes plants with strikingly colorful blooms and sword-shaped leaves.
Botanists classify irises, crocuses, and Dutch irises as members of the iris family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Iridaceae - the scientific name for the iris family

Etymology

Named after the genus Iris, from Greek Iris (goddess of the rainbow), reflecting the diversity of flower colors.
