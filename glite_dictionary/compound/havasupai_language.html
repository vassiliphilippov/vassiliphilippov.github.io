Havasupai language

1. NOUN

/ ˌhævəˈsuːpaɪ ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌhævəˈsuːpaɪ ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Yuman language spoken by the Havasupai people of the Grand Canyon region in Arizona, USA
Yuman language of the Havasupai people

Examples

The Havasupai language is critically endangered, with only a few fluent speakers remaining.
She conducted linguistic research to help preserve the Havasupai language for future generations.
Many words in the Havasupai language have unique sounds not found in English.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Yuman - a family of Native American languages of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico
