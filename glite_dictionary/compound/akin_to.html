akin to

1. ADJ

/ əˈkɪn tu / (AmE), / əˈkɪn tuː / (BrE)
having a similar quality or character to something; comparable to.
similar to; comparable to

Examples

The architecture of the new museum is akin to that of ancient Greek temples.
His reaction was akin to shock when he heard the unexpected news.
The experience of skydiving is akin to flying for the first time.

Synset Examples

In his speech, he condemned ideologies akin to Xitler's.
Using such language is akin to calling for McNiggers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: similar to - having a resemblance in appearance or character
  • Antonym: unlike - dissimilar or different from

Etymology

From 'akin,' meaning 'of kin, related by blood,' plus 'to.' 'Akin' derives from Middle English 'of kin.'
