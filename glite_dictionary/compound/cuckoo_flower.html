cuckoo flower

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʊku flaʊər / (AmE), / ˈkʊku ˌflaʊə / (BrE)
A perennial plant native to Europe and western Asia, typically bearing pink, ragged-petaled flowers.
European perennial with pink flowers

Examples

In the meadows each spring, cuckoo flower blooms with delicate pink petals.
The cuckoo flower is often found along riverbanks and damp grasslands.
Gardeners sometimes plant cuckoo flower for its beauty and to attract pollinators.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkʊku ˌflaʊɚ / (AmE), / ˈkʊku ˌflaʊə / (BrE)
a wild perennial plant with pale pink or white flowers, commonly found in damp meadows and known as Cardamine pratensis; also called lady's smock
a meadow plant with pink or white flowers

Examples

The meadow was dotted with delicate cuckoo flowers swaying in the breeze.
She picked a handful of cuckoo flowers to decorate the spring table.
In early May, cuckoo flower blooms can often be seen along riverbanks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lady's smock - alternative common name for the same plant

Etymology

Named for its blooming period, which often coincides with the arrival of cuckoos in spring.
