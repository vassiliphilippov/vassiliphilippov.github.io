white flag

1. NOUN

/ waɪt flæɡ / (AmE), / waɪt flaɡ / (BrE)
a plain white flag displayed to signal surrender or to request a truce, especially during conflict
flag signaling surrender or truce

Examples

During the final battle, the soldiers raised a white flag to indicate their surrender.
If you see someone waving a white flag, it means they want to negotiate or stop fighting.
The rebels had no choice but to display a white flag after being surrounded by government forces.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: surrender - to cease resistance and submit to an opponent; the act being indicated by a white flag
  • Synonym: truce - an agreement between enemies or opponents to stop fighting for a certain time
