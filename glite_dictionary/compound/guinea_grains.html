Guinea grains

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɪni ɡreɪnz / (AmE), / ˈɡɪni ɡreɪnz / (BrE)
A West African plant (Aframomum melegueta) whose pungent, peppery seeds are used as a spice, also known as grains of paradise.
West African spice plant and its seeds

Examples

The spicy flavor of Guinea grains is prized in traditional West African dishes.
Traders once imported Guinea grains into Europe as an exotic spice.
She ground some Guinea grains to add a peppery kick to the stew.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grains of paradise - the seeds of Aframomum melegueta, used as a spice

Etymology

Named for the Guinea region of West Africa.
