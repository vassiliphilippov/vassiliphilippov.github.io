good-for-nothing

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡʊd.fərˈnʌθ.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌɡʊd.fəˈnʌθ.ɪŋ / (BrE)
a person who is lazy or considered to have no value or usefulness
worthless or lazy person

Examples

Everyone thought he was a good-for-nothing who would never accomplish anything.
She's tired of her good-for-nothing brother sitting around all day.
Despite being labeled a good-for-nothing, he eventually proved everyone wrong.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: layabout - a person who is habitually lazy or idle
  • Synonym: slacker - a person who avoids work or effort
  • Antonym: hardworker - a person who puts in a lot of effort and diligence

2. ADJ

/ ˌɡʊd fər ˈnʌθɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌɡʊd fə ˈnʌθɪŋ / (BrE)
Useless, worthless, or of no value; having little or no merit.
useless or worthless person or thing

Examples

Many considered him a good-for-nothing who would never amount to anything.
The old, broken radio was just another good-for-nothing object cluttering the attic.
She refuses to date a good-for-nothing who has no plans for the future.

Synset Examples

a sorry horse
a sorry excuse
a lazy no-count, good-for-nothing goldbrick
the car was a no-good piece of junk

Related Terms

  • Synonym: worthless - having no value or use
  • Antonym: useful - able to be used to achieve something; having value
