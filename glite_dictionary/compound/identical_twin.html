identical twin

1. NOUN

/ aɪˈdɛntɪkəl twɪn / (AmE), / aɪˈdɛntɪkəl twɪn / (BrE)
one of a pair of twins who develop from a single fertilized egg and thus have the same genetic material
twin from same fertilized egg

Examples

Sarah is an identical twin, but she and her sister have very different personalities.
It's difficult to tell one identical twin from the other because they look so much alike.
Being an identical twin means sharing the exact same DNA with your sibling.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fraternal twin - one of a pair of twins developed from two separately fertilized eggs
