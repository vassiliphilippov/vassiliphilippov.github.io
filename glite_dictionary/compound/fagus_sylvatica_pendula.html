Fagus sylvatica pendula

1. NOUN

/ ˈfeɪɡəs sɪlˈvætɪkə pɛnˈdjuːlə / (AmE), / ˈfeɪɡəs sɪlˈvætɪkə pɛnˈdjuːlə / (BrE)
A weeping variety of the European beech tree, characterized by long, pendulous branches.
weeping variety of European beech tree

Examples

The Fagus sylvatica pendula in the park provides a dramatic centerpiece with its sweeping branches.
Gardeners often admire the unique, drooping shape of the Fagus sylvatica pendula.
A mature Fagus sylvatica pendula can create a shaded area beneath its dense, cascading foliage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weeping beech - common name for Fagus sylvatica pendula

Etymology

From scientific Latin: 'Fagus' (beech) + 'sylvatica' (of the woods) + 'pendula' (hanging).
