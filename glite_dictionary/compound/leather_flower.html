leather flower

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛðər flaʊər / (AmE), / ˈlɛðə flaʊə / (BrE)
A woody vine native to the southern United States, producing purple or blue flowers with leathery, recurved sepals.
woody vine with leathery purple or blue flowers

Examples

The leather flower is prized by gardeners for its unusual purple blossoms and vigorous growth.
In spring, a leather flower vine can quickly cover a trellis with its beautiful blooms.
She planted a leather flower beside the fence for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Clematis - genus of climbing plants with showy flowers

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɛðɚ ˌflaʊɚ / (AmE), / ˈlɛðə ˌflaʊə / (BrE)
A climbing subshrub native to the southeastern United States with large, red-purple, bell-shaped flowers and leathery, recurved sepals.
a flowering climbing plant of southeastern US

Examples

The leather flower blooms in spring, displaying its distinctive bell-shaped blossoms.
Gardeners admire the leather flower for its resilience and beautiful, leathery sepals.
In its native habitat, the leather flower twines around fences and shrubs.
