mammoth task

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæməθ tæsk / (AmE), / ˈmaməθ tɑːsk / (BrE)
a task that is extremely large, complex, or difficult to accomplish
extremely large and difficult task

Examples

Completing the research project in just three months was a mammoth task.
Organizing the charity event for thousands of people became a mammoth task for the team.
Cleaning the entire stadium after the concert was a mammoth task.

Synset Examples

He faced a mammoth task when he took on the new role.
The project turned out to be a mammoth undertaking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: herculean task - a task requiring great strength or effort
  • Degree Higher: impossible task - a task that cannot be done
  • Degree Lower: simple task - a task that is easy to do

Etymology

From 'mammoth' (large prehistoric animal), used figuratively for something enormous, and 'task.'
