Belle Isle cress

1. NOUN

/ bɛl aɪl krɛs / (AmE), / bɛl aɪl krɛs / (BrE)
a plant of southwestern European origin (Lepidium draba), sometimes cultivated in Florida for ornamental purposes or as a weed
a plant species of southern Europe, also in cultivation

Examples

Gardeners in Florida sometimes plant Belle Isle cress for its attractive white flowers.
Belle Isle cress can become invasive in some climates, spreading quickly in gardens.
The local botanical garden has a collection of Belle Isle cress among its Mediterranean plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cress - any of various plants of the genus Lepidium with small white or yellow flowers
