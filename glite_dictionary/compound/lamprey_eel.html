lamprey eel

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæmpri iːl / (AmE), / ˈlampri iːl / (BrE)
a primitive, eel-like jawless fish with a round sucking mouth and rasping tongue, found in freshwater and coastal seas
primitive eel-like jawless fish

Examples

The lamprey eel attaches itself to other fish to feed on their blood.
Scientists study the unique jawless structure of the lamprey eel for evolutionary research.
A lamprey eel was spotted in the river during the spawning season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lamprey - primitive jawless fish with a sucking mouth
  • Degree Lower: hagfish - primitive jawless fish related to lampreys

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'lampreda', of uncertain origin, possibly of Celtic origin.
