spaghetti junction

1. NOUN

/ spəˈɡɛti ˈdʒʌŋkʃən / (AmE), / spəˈɡɛti ˈdʒʌŋkʃən / (BrE)
A complicated highway interchange with multiple levels and intertwining roads resembling a tangle of spaghetti.
complicated highway interchange

Examples

The new freeway design features a spaghetti junction to manage all the merging and diverging lanes.
Traffic was backed up for miles at the spaghetti junction during rush hour.
London's most famous spaghetti junction is a landmark for its complexity and size.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interchange - a road junction with connecting ramps and slip roads

Etymology

From 'spaghetti' (the Italian pasta) plus 'junction,' inspired by the tangled appearance resembling a heap of spaghetti.
