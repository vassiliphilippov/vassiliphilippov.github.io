money-spinner

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌni ˌspɪnər / (AmE), / ˈmʌni ˌspɪnə / (BrE)
A business, product, or project that generates a steady flow of profit; a reliable source of income.
project or thing that reliably makes money

Examples

The new smartphone model turned out to be a real money-spinner for the company.
Property rental is often seen as a reliable money-spinner by investors.
The book became a money-spinner and kept selling for years after it was published.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cash cow - A business or product that generates a steady, dependable source of income

Etymology

From 'money' + 'spinner', indicating something that 'spins' (generates) money.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmʌni ˌspɪnər / (AmE), / ˈmʌni ˌspɪnə / (BrE)
A business or product that earns a large amount of money; a source of significant profit.
a profitable business or venture

Examples

The new smartphone proved to be a money-spinner for the tech company.
Their line of organic skincare products turned into a real money-spinner within a year.
The hit musical became the season's biggest money-spinner in London's West End.

Synset Examples

The restaurant turned out to be a money-spinner during the summer.
Investing in stocks can be a great money-spinner if done wisely.
His new app became a real money-spinner overnight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: goldmine - something that produces a lot of money
  • Antonym: loss-maker - a business or venture that loses money

Etymology

From 'money' + 'spinner', implying something that 'spins' (produces) money rapidly.
