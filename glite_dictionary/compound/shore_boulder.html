shore boulder

1. NOUN

/ ʃɔr ˈboʊldər / (AmE), / ʃɔː ˈbəʊldə / (BrE)
a large rock found on a shoreline, especially one that has been transported from its original location by natural forces
large rock on a shore, displaced from origin

Examples

The geologist inspected the shore boulder to determine how far it had traveled.
Many shore boulders along the coastline originated from distant cliffs.
Waves and ice can move a shore boulder miles from its source over many years.
