umbilical cord

1. NOUN

/ ʌmˈbɪlɪkəl kɔrd / (AmE), / ʌmˈbɪlɪkəl kɔːd / (BrE)
the long, flexible cord that connects a fetus to the placenta, supplying nutrients and removing waste during pregnancy
cord connecting fetus and placenta
medical

Examples

The doctor clamped and cut the umbilical cord moments after the baby was born.
Nutrients and oxygen pass from the mother to the fetus through the umbilical cord.
Scientists sometimes preserve the umbilical cord for future medical research.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: placenta - organ in pregnant mammals supplying nutrients to fetus

Etymology

From Latin 'umbilicus' (navel) and 'cord' (string or rope).

2. NOUN

/ ʌmˈbɪlɪkəl kɔrd / (AmE), / ʌmˌbɪlɪkəl ˈkɔːd / (BrE)
the long, flexible tube that connects a developing fetus to the placenta, carrying nutrients and oxygen from the mother and removing waste products
tube connecting fetus and placenta during pregnancy
medical

Examples

The surgeon carefully clamped the umbilical cord after the baby was delivered.
Scientists study stem cells that can be collected from the umbilical cord.
She kept her baby's umbilical cord stump as a memento after it fell off.

Synset Examples

The doctor explained the role of the umbilical cord.
The umbilical cord nourishes the fetus during pregnancy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: placenta - organ in the uterus that nourishes the fetus

Etymology

From Latin 'umbilicus' (navel) and 'cord'.
