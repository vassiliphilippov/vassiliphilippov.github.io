pressure cooker

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɛʃər ˌkʊkər / (AmE), / ˈprɛʃə ˌkʊkə / (BrE)
A sealed cooking device that uses steam pressure to cook food at temperatures above the boiling point of water.
sealed cooking device using steam pressure

Examples

She cooked beans quickly in the pressure cooker for dinner.
Modern recipes often include instructions for using a pressure cooker.
Be careful when opening the pressure cooker after use, as hot steam may escape.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: autoclave - a strong heated container for chemical reactions

2. NOUN

/ ˈprɛʃər ˌkʊkər / (AmE), / ˈprɛʃə ˌkʊkə / (BrE)
A sealed pot for cooking food quickly under steam pressure; also used to describe a situation or environment characterized by intense stress or pressure.
sealed pot for fast cooking; stressful situation

Examples

She prepared the stew in the pressure cooker to save time.
Working in that office is a real pressure cooker—everyone is stressed out all the time.
After a long day in the pressure cooker, he needed to relax.

Synset Examples

The pressure cooker made dinner ready in no time.
He felt like a pressure cooker about to explode.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: slow cooker - an electric pot for cooking food slowly at a low temperature
