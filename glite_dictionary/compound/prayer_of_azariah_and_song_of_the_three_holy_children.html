Prayer of Azariah and Song of the Three Holy Children

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɛr əv əˈzɛriə ənd sɔŋ əv ðə θri ˈhoʊli ˈtʃɪldrən / (AmE), / ˈpreə əv əˈzɑːriə ənd sɒŋ əv ðə θriː ˈhəʊli ˈtʃɪldrən / (BrE)
an apocryphal book consisting of text added to the Book of Daniel, containing the Prayer of Azariah and the Song of the Three Holy Children
apocryphal book added to Daniel

Examples

Some scholars study the Prayer of Azariah and Song of the Three Holy Children for its historical and literary value.
The Prayer of Azariah and Song of the Three Holy Children is included in some versions of the Old Testament Apocrypha.
During the service, the priest read from the Prayer of Azariah and Song of the Three Holy Children.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Apocrypha - Biblical or related writings not forming part of the accepted canon of Scripture
