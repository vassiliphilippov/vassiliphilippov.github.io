Barbados cherry

1. NOUN

/ bɑɹˈbeɪdoʊs ˈtʃɛri / (AmE), / bɑːˈbeɪdəʊs ˈtʃɛri / (BrE)
an acid red or yellow fruit, similar in appearance to a cherry, from a tropical American shrub, noted for its high vitamin C content
tropical fruit rich in vitamin C

Examples

The Barbados cherry is often used to make juices and jams because of its tangy flavor.
Due to its high vitamin C content, the Barbados cherry is considered very nutritious.
We planted a Barbados cherry shrub in our backyard to enjoy the fresh fruit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acerola - another name for the Barbados cherry fruit

2. NOUN

/ bɑːrˈbeɪdoʊs ˈtʃɛri / (AmE), / bɑːˈbeɪdəʊs ˈtʃɛri / (BrE)
A tropical American shrub (Malpighia emarginata) that bears small, red, acid fruits resembling cherries, which are edible and rich in vitamin C.
tropical American shrub and its red fruit

Examples

The juice from Barbados cherry is rich in vitamin C and often used to make refreshing drinks.
We planted a Barbados cherry in our garden to enjoy its tart fruit each summer.
Scientists are studying the antioxidant properties of the Barbados cherry due to its high vitamin content.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acerola - another name for Barbados cherry or its fruit
