family Ameiuridae

1. NOUN

/ ˌfæməli əˌmaɪjəˈrɪdaɪ / (AmE), / ˌfæmɪli əˌmiːjʊəˈrɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of North American catfishes, including species commonly found in freshwater habitats.
a family of North American catfishes

Examples

The family Ameiuridae includes many familiar species of freshwater catfishes in North America.
Scientists discovered a new species within the family Ameiuridae in the Mississippi River basin.
Members of the family Ameiuridae are often called bullhead catfishes.

Etymology

From New Latin Ameiuridae, based on the genus name Ameiurus.
