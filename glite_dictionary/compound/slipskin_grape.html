slipskin grape

1. NOUN

/ ˈslɪp.skɪn ɡreɪp / (AmE), / ˈslɪp.skɪn ɡreɪp / (BrE)
A grape variety whose skin separates easily from the pulp.
grape with easily separated skin

Examples

The muscadine is a well-known variety of slipskin grape prized in the southern United States.
When peeling a slipskin grape, the skin comes off with almost no effort.
Many jams and preserves are made from slipskin grapes because of their unique texture.

Etymology

From 'slip' + 'skin', referring to the skin slipping off the pulp easily.
