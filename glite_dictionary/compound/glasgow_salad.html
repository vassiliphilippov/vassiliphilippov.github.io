Glasgow salad

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡlæsɡoʊ ˈsæl.əd / (AmE), / ˈɡlɑːzɡəʊ ˈsæl.əd / (BrE)
A humorous term referring to a meal of chips (fries), sometimes used for a salad that includes Scottish ingredients or haggis.
humorous term for a meal of chips (fries)
informal british

Examples

After a night out, he ordered a Glasgow salad to satisfy his hunger.
She laughed when the waiter offered her a Glasgow salad as the healthy option.
Their menu humorously listed chips and haggis as a Glasgow salad.

Synset Examples

He joked that his dinner was just a Glasgow salad.
The restaurant's Glasgow salad featured local ingredients.
For lunch, she ordered a Glasgow salad with haggis.

Etymology

Named after the city of Glasgow, Scotland, playfully contrasting typical 'salad' with the local preference for chips (fries).
