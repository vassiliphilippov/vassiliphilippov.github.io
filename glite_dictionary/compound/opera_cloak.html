opera cloak

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːpərə kloʊk / (AmE), / ˈɒpərə kləʊk / (BrE)
a large, formal cloak worn over evening clothes, especially when attending the opera or similar events
large formal cloak for evening wear

Examples

She draped her opera cloak over her shoulders before leaving for the gala.
The velvet opera cloak gave her ensemble an air of classic elegance.
He carried his mother's old opera cloak to the theater as a stylish tribute.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cloak - a loose outer garment, typically sleeveless
