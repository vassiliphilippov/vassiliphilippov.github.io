general lien

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɛnərəl liːˈɛn / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛnərəl liːˈɛn / (BrE)
the legal right to retain possession of all property of a debtor as security for a general balance of accounts, not limited to particular assets
right to hold all of a debtor's property as security
legal

Examples

The creditor exercised a general lien over all the goods stored in the warehouse.
A bank may claim a general lien on a customer's assets for unpaid debts.
Unlike a particular lien, a general lien covers all the debtor's property, not just specific items.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: particular lien - right to retain specific property for payment related to that property only
