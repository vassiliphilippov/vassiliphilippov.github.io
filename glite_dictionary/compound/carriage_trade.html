carriage trade

1. NOUN

/ ˈkærɪdʒ treɪd / (AmE), / ˈkærɪdʒ treɪd / (BrE)
business or customers consisting of wealthy or upper-class people; trade from affluent clientele
trade with wealthy or upper-class customers

Examples

The boutique attracts a significant portion of the carriage trade during the holiday season.
For years, the restaurant catered exclusively to the carriage trade, offering gourmet meals and private dining rooms.
Stores on that side of town rely heavily on the carriage trade for their luxury goods sales.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elite - the most powerful, rich, or talented people within a group
  • Antonym: common people - ordinary, working-class or lower-class people

Etymology

Alludes to the time when wealthy patrons arrived at shops by horse-drawn carriage.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkærɪdʒ treɪd / (AmE), / ˈkærɪdʒ treɪd / (BrE)
wealthy or high-end customers of a business, especially those who are the main source of expensive purchases
affluent clientele; wealthy customers

Examples

The boutique tailors its products and services to the carriage trade.
Advertising in that magazine targets the carriage trade rather than the general public.
Many jewelers rely on the carriage trade for most of their sales.

Synset Examples

She only shops at places that appeal to the carriage trade.
The new restaurant caters to the carriage trade of wealthy clients.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clientele - the customers of a shop or business collectively
  • Synonym: patronage - the support or custom of clients

Etymology

From the practice of affluent people arriving by horse-drawn carriage, signifying select, upper-class customers.
