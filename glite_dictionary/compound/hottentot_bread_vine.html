Hottentot bread vine

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑːtənˌtɑt brɛd vaɪn / (AmE), / ˈhɒt.ən.tɒt brɛd vaɪn / (BrE)
A South African climbing plant with a large, deeply fissured rootstock, known scientifically as Dioscorea elephantipes.
South African plant with massive rootstock

Examples

The Hottentot bread vine can survive long droughts by storing water in its enormous rootstock.
Botanists admire the unusual appearance of the Hottentot bread vine in its native habitat.
Collectors often seek out the Hottentot bread vine because of its distinctive, elephant-foot-shaped tuber.

Etymology

The name refers to the plant's traditional use as food by indigenous peoples; 'Hottentot' is an outdated term for the Khoikhoi people.
