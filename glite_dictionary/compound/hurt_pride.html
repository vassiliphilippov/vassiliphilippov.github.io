to hurt pride

1. VERB

/ hɜrt praɪd / (AmE), / hɜːt praɪd / (BrE)
to cause someone to feel emotional pain or embarrassment by wounding their self-esteem or dignity
cause emotional pain to someone's self-esteem

Examples

Losing the final match really hurt pride among the team members.
Her harsh criticism deeply hurt pride and left him feeling humiliated.
It's important not to hurt pride when giving someone constructive feedback.

Synset Examples

Being called a nafoid really hurt his pride.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to offend - to cause someone to feel upset, annoyed, or resentful
  • Synonym: wound - to cause emotional pain or harm
