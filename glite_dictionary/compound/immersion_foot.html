immersion foot

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈmɜrʒən fʊt / (AmE), / ɪˈmɜːʃən fʊt / (BrE)
A medical condition, similar to frostbite but without freezing, caused by prolonged exposure of the feet to cold and wet conditions.
cold/wet injury to the feet
medical

Examples

Prolonged hiking in wet boots can lead to immersion foot if the feet are not dried regularly.
During the expedition, several soldiers developed immersion foot after wading through icy water for hours.
Proper footwear and dry socks are crucial to prevent immersion foot in harsh, cold environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trench foot - a similar condition caused by exposure to damp, unsanitary, and cold conditions
  • Degree Higher: frostbite - injury to body tissues caused by freezing
