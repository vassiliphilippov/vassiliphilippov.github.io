wood laurel

1. NOUN

/ wʊd ˈlɔrəl / (AmE), / wʊd ˈlɒrəl / (BrE)
A bushy Eurasian shrub with glossy, leathery oblong leaves and yellow-green flowers.
Eurasian shrub with glossy leaves

Examples

The wood laurel thrives in well-drained woodland soil.
Gardeners often admire the glossy leaves of the wood laurel.
Clusters of yellow-green flowers make the wood laurel stand out in the spring.

2. NOUN

/ wʊd ˈlɔːrəl / (AmE), / wʊd ˈlɔːrəl / (BrE)
a North American evergreen shrub with glossy leaves and white or rose-colored flowers
North American evergreen shrub

Examples

The wood laurel blooms beautifully in late spring, adding color to the garden.
I planted a wood laurel by the porch for its fragrant flowers.
The leaves of the wood laurel are often used in decorative arrangements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mountain laurel - an evergreen shrub with similar appearance native to North America
