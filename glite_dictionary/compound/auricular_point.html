auricular point

1. NOUN

/ ɔːˈrɪkjələr pɔɪnt / (AmE), / ɔːˈrɪkjʊlə pɔɪnt / (BrE)
the craniometric point located at the center of the opening of the external acoustic meatus
anatomical point at ear canal opening
medical

Examples

The surgeon located the auricular point before making precise measurements of the skull.
In anthropological studies, the auricular point is an important landmark for cranial measurements.
Proper placement of the instrument requires alignment with the auricular point on each side of the head.

Etymology

From Latin 'auricula' meaning 'ear'.
