swamp oak

1. NOUN

/ swɑmp oʊk / (AmE), / swɒmp əʊk / (BrE)
An Australian leafless shrub resembling broom, with small yellow flowers.
Australian shrub with yellow flowers

Examples

The swamp oak thrives in wet, marshy areas of southeastern Australia.
She admired the small yellow flowers on the swamp oak during her hike.
Conservationists are studying the decline of swamp oaks in the region.

2. NOUN

/ swɑmp oʊk / (AmE), / swɒmp əʊk / (BrE)
a large deciduous tree of eastern North America, with flaky bark and leaves with few lobes, growing in wet soils and providing heavy, strong wood used in construction
oak tree native to eastern North America

Examples

The lumber from the swamp oak is highly prized for building sturdy furniture.
We identified the old tree in the wetland as a swamp oak by its flaky bark and leaf shape.
Conservationists are working to protect native swamp oak groves from development.

3. NOUN

/ swɑmp oʊk / (AmE), / swɒmp əʊk / (BrE)
A fast-growing deciduous tree of northeastern United States and southeastern Canada, with deeply lobed leaves that turn bright red in autumn, thriving in damp soil.
a North American deciduous tree thriving in wet soils

Examples

The swamp oak is prized for its vibrant fall foliage and rapid growth rate.
Wetlands along the river are often dominated by swamp oak and other moisture-loving trees.
They planted a row of swamp oaks to help stabilize the damp ground near the pond.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pin oak - a similar North American oak (Quercus palustris)
