Seventh Crusade

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛvənθ kruˈseɪd / (AmE), / ˈsɛvənθ kruˈseɪd / (BrE)
the military campaign initiated in 1248 by King Louis IX of France in response to the loss of Jerusalem, ending in defeat in 1249
1248 crusade led by Louis IX of France

Examples

Louis IX led the Seventh Crusade hoping to reclaim Jerusalem from Muslim forces.
The Seventh Crusade ultimately ended in failure, with many French soldiers captured in Egypt.
Many historians view the Seventh Crusade as a turning point in medieval European history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Crusade - a series of medieval military expeditions by Europeans to recover the Holy Land from Muslims
  • Degree Lower: Sixth Crusade - the preceding crusade before the Seventh Crusade
  • Degree Higher: Eighth Crusade - the subsequent crusade after the Seventh Crusade
