word of honor

1. NOUN

/ wɝːd əv ˈɑːnɚ / (AmE), / wɜːd əv ˈɒnə / (BrE)
a solemn promise or assurance, especially regarded as a guarantee of honesty or trustworthiness
solemn promise; assurance of honesty

Examples

You have my word of honor that I will return the money tomorrow.
He broke his word of honor and lost everyone's trust.
She gave her word of honor never to reveal the secret.

Synset Examples

he gave his word

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pledge - a serious promise or agreement
  • Synonym: oath - a solemn promise, often invoking a divine witness
  • Antonym: lie - a false statement made with intent to deceive; the opposite of a promise kept
