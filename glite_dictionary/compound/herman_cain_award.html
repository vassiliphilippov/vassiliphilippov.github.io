Herman Cain Award

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɜrmən keɪn əˈwɔrd / (AmE), / ˈhɜːmən keɪn əˈwɔːd / (BrE)
A satirical phrase referring to an ironic or dubious recognition given to individuals, especially on social media, for suffering severe consequences after publicly downplaying or dismissing health risks, particularly in the context of the COVID-19 pandemic.
satirical recognition for poor judgment, esp. about health risks
informal slang american

Examples

Many online communities have started giving the Herman Cain Award to those who downplay serious health crises.
The post nominated him for the Herman Cain Award after he publicly mocked vaccination efforts and later fell ill.
Several forum users debated whether her anti-mask rants warranted a Herman Cain Award mention.

Synset Examples

Many social media users joked about his nomination for the Herman Cain Award.
Her refusal to follow health guidelines earned her the Herman Cain Award.
He received the Herman Cain Award for his reckless behavior during the pandemic.

Etymology

Named after Herman Cain, an American businessman and political figure who died of COVID-19 after publicly expressing skepticism about the pandemic.
