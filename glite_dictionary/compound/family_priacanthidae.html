family Priacanthidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli praɪəˌkænθɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli praɪəˈkanθɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of small, carnivorous percoid fishes found worldwide in tropical seas.
family of tropical carnivorous fishes

Examples

The family Priacanthidae includes several species known for their large eyes and bright red color.
Scientists observed members of the family Priacanthidae near coral reefs in the Pacific Ocean.
Fishing regulations help protect the family Priacanthidae from overexploitation in tropical waters.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'Priacanthus' (a genus within the family) and Greek roots 'prios' (saw) and 'akantha' (spine).
