Circassian language

1. NOUN

/ sərˈkæʃən ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / səˈkæʃən ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
a North Caucasian language spoken by the Circassian people, primarily in the northwest Caucasus region
North Caucasian language of the Circassian people

Examples

The Circassian language is considered endangered, with many speakers living in diaspora communities.
She is fluent in both Russian and the Circassian language.
Efforts are being made to teach children the Circassian language in local schools.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Abkhaz - a Northwest Caucasian language spoken in Abkhazia

Etymology

Circassian, from Russian 'Cherkess' (the Circassian people), combined with 'language'.
