military-industrial complex

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɪlɪˌtɛri ɪnˈdʌstriəl ˈkɑmplɛks / (AmE), / ˌmɪlɪtəri ɪnˈdʌstriəl ˈkɒmplɛks / (BrE)
the armed forces of a country together with the industries that supply weapons and military equipment, regarded as a powerful and influential combination
national military and arms industries network

Examples

The influence of the military-industrial complex on government decisions has been a topic of debate for decades.
Eisenhower warned about the dangers of the military-industrial complex in his farewell address.
Many believe that the military-industrial complex can sway foreign policy through its economic power.

Synset Examples

we must guard against the acquisition of unwarranted influence, whether sought or unsought, by the military-industrial complex

Etymology

Popularized by U.S. President Dwight D. Eisenhower in his 1961 farewell address.
