subordinate conjunction

1. NOUN

/ səˈbɔːrdɪnət kənˈdʒʌŋkʃən / (AmE), / səˈbɔːdɪnət kənˈdʒʌŋkʃən / (BrE)
a word or phrase that introduces a dependent (subordinate) clause, such as 'because', 'since', or 'although'
conjunction introducing a dependent clause

Examples

The word 'because' is a classic example of a subordinate conjunction.
In the sentence 'I went home because it was late,' the subordinate conjunction 'because' introduces the dependent clause.
She explained that subordinate conjunctions connect dependent clauses to main clauses.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: coordinating conjunction - a conjunction linking elements of equal syntactic importance

Etymology

From Latin 'subordinare' (to arrange under) and 'conjunctio' (joining together).
