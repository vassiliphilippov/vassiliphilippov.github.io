catholic meter

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæθəlɪk ˈmiːtər / (AmE), / ˈkæθəlɪk ˈmiːtə / (BrE)
a historical unit of length equal to the length of a free seconds pendulum, introduced by Tito Livio Burattini in 1675 and differing from the modern metre by about half a centimetre.
historic unit of length (seconds pendulum)

Examples

The catholic meter was conceived as a universal standard of length in the seventeenth century.
Unlike the modern metre, the catholic meter was defined by the length of a seconds pendulum.
Burattini’s catholic meter was one of several early attempts to establish a natural standard for measurement.

Etymology

From 'catholic' (universal) and 'meter', proposed by Tito Livio Burattini in 1675 to denote a universal measure based on a pendulum.
