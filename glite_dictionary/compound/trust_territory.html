trust territory

1. NOUN

/ trʌst ˈtɛr.əˌtɔr.i / (AmE), / trʌst ˈtɛr.ɪ.təri / (BrE)
A dependent territory placed under the administration of another country by international agreement, especially under the supervision of the United Nations.
territory under international supervision

Examples

The United Nations designated the islands as a trust territory after World War II.
Administration of the trust territory was transferred from Britain to Australia in 1947.
The people of the trust territory voted for independence in a popular referendum.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: colony - a country or area under the control of another country
  • Synonym: protectorate - a state controlled and protected by another
  • Antonym: sovereign state - a state with full right and power to govern itself

Etymology

From 'trust', referring to the trusteeship system, and 'territory'. The term was institutionalized by the United Nations after World War II.
