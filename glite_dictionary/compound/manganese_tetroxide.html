manganese tetroxide

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæŋɡəniːz tɛˈtrɑksaɪd / (AmE), / ˌmæŋɡəniːz tɛˈtrɒksaɪd / (BrE)
a black or brownish-black oxide of manganese, occurring naturally as the mineral hausmannite
a manganese oxide mineral (hausmannite)

Examples

Manganese tetroxide is often used as a pigment in ceramics and glassmaking.
The mine produces natural manganese tetroxide, also known as hausmannite.
A laboratory synthesized sample of manganese tetroxide was analyzed for purity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hausmannite - the mineral form of manganese tetroxide

Etymology

From 'manganese', after Latin 'manganum', and 'tetroxide' from Greek 'tetra-' (four) + 'oxide'.
