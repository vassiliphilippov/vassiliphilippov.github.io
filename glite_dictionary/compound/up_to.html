up to

1. ADJ

/ ʌp tu / (AmE), / ʌp tʊ / (BrE)
busy or occupied with a particular activity, task, or scheme; engaged in doing something (often used in questions about someone's recent activities, or to suggest possible mischief).
occupied or engaged in doing something

Examples

I wonder what you have been up to lately.
The kids were suspiciously quiet, so their mother asked what they were up to.
He looks like he's up to something mischievous again.

Synset Examples

what have you been up to?
up to no good

Related Terms

  • Synonym: occupied - busy or engaged in some activity
  • Synonym: involved - actively engaged or participating in something

2. ADJ

/ ʌp tu / (AmE), / ʌp tʊ / (BrE)
having the necessary qualities, standards, or ability for a particular purpose
having the necessary qualities or standards

Examples

She is definitely up to the challenge of leading the project.
If your report isn't up to the required standard, you'll need to revise it.
After his illness, he wasn't up to jogging for very long.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: equal to - having the ability or qualities required for something
