clostridial myonecrosis

1. NOUN

/ klɑːˈstrɪdiəl ˌmaɪoʊnɛˈkroʊsɪs / (AmE), / klɒˈstrɪdɪəl ˌmaɪəʊnɛˈkrəʊsɪs / (BrE)
A severe, often fatal bacterial infection of muscle tissue, usually caused by Clostridium bacteria, resulting in rapid tissue death and gas production.
deadly bacterial muscle infection (gas gangrene)
medical

Examples

The patient developed clostridial myonecrosis after a traumatic injury to his leg.
Prompt diagnosis and treatment of clostridial myonecrosis are essential for survival.
Researchers studied the potential use of clostridial myonecrosis as a biological weapon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gas gangrene - A fast-spreading and deadly bacterial infection with gas production in tissues

Etymology

From New Latin: 'Clostridium' (bacterial genus) + Greek 'mys' (muscle) + 'nekrosis' (death).
