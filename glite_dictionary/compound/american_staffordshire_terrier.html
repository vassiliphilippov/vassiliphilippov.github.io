American Staffordshire terrier

1. NOUN

/ əˌmɛr.ɪ.kən ˌstæf.ərd.ʃɪr ˈtɛr.i.ɚ / (AmE), / əˌmɛr.ɪ.kən ˌstæf.əd.ʃɪə ˈtɛr.i.ə / (BrE)
A strong, muscular breed of terrier originating in the United States, characterized by a short, stiff coat and a stocky build.
muscular US terrier breed

Examples

The American Staffordshire terrier is known for its loyalty and affectionate nature toward families.
Many dog shows feature the American Staffordshire terrier in the terrier group competition.
Their short coats make grooming American Staffordshire terriers relatively easy for most owners.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Staffordshire bull terrier - A similar but smaller British terrier breed
  • Synonym: pit bull terrier - A related breed known for similar build and ancestry

Etymology

Named for Staffordshire, England, where related breeds originated, and American, denoting its development in the US.
