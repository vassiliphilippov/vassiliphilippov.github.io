reverse Midas touch

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈvɜrs ˈmaɪdəs tʌtʃ / (AmE), / rɪˈvɜːs ˈmaɪdəs tʌtʃ / (BrE)
a tendency to cause failure or ruin in everything one becomes involved with
tendency to cause failure

Examples

After three failed startups, people said he had a reverse Midas touch.
Hiring her as manager proved disastrous; her reverse Midas touch affected the whole team.
Investors began to worry that the CEO's reverse Midas touch would lead to even more losses.

Synset Examples

Everything he invested in turned to losses; he had a reverse Midas touch.
Her reverse Midas touch caused each project to flop.
The project’s failure highlighted his reverse Midas touch.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Midas touch - the ability to be successful at everything one does

Etymology

From 'Midas touch' (the legendary ability to turn everything to gold), with 'reverse' indicating the opposite effect.
