cyclonic vacuum

1. NOUN

/ saɪˈklɑnɪk ˈvækjuːm / (AmE), / saɪˈklɒnɪk ˈvækjuːm / (BrE)
a vacuum cleaner that separates dust and debris from the air using a cyclone effect, usually collecting the dust in a detachable cylindrical bin instead of a bag
bagless vacuum cleaner using cyclone separation

Examples

We bought a cyclonic vacuum because it didn’t require disposable bags.
The cyclonic vacuum makes emptying the dust much easier than traditional models.
After using the cyclonic vacuum, she noticed the carpets were much cleaner.

Synset Examples

Cyclonic cleaners do not use filtration bags.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vacuum cleaner - an electrical appliance for cleaning floors, carpets, etc., by suction

Etymology

From 'cyclonic' (relating to a cyclone, a rotating storm system) and 'vacuum' (a device that removes air to create suction).
