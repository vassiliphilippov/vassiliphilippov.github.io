wild geranium

1. NOUN

/ waɪld dʒəˈreɪniəm / (AmE), / waɪld dʒəˈreɪniəm / (BrE)
a North American wildflower with deeply divided leaves and rose-purple flowers, commonly found in forests and meadows
wildflower with rose-purple flowers, North America

Examples

The wild geranium blooms in early spring, adding color to the woodland floor.
Clusters of wild geranium lined the trail with their delicate purple petals.
Gardeners often use wild geranium to attract pollinators to their flower beds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cranesbill - any plant of the geranium genus
  • Synonym: Geranium maculatum - the botanical name for wild geranium
