nail-tailed wallaby

1. NOUN

/ ˈneɪl teɪld ˈwɒləbi / (AmE), / ˈneɪl teɪld ˈwɒləbi / (BrE)
a small species of wallaby distinguished by a hard, horny nail at the tip of its tail
small wallaby with a nail-like tail tip

Examples

The nail-tailed wallaby gets its name from the small, horny projection at the end of its tail.
Conservationists are working to protect the endangered nail-tailed wallaby in Australia.
Researchers observed a group of nail-tailed wallabies feeding at dusk in the open grassland.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wallaby - a small to medium-sized marsupial found in Australia and New Guinea
