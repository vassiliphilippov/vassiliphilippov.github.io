polar coordinate

1. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊlər koʊˈɔrdɪnət / (AmE), / ˈpəʊlə kəʊˈɔːdɪnət / (BrE)
either of two values that define the position of a point on a plane by specifying its distance from a fixed point (the pole) and its angle from a fixed direction.
coordinate in polar system (distance and angle)

Examples

The location of the star was described using polar coordinates rather than Cartesian coordinates.
To convert a point from Cartesian to polar coordinates, you need to calculate the distance from the origin and the angle.
Engineers often use polar coordinates when working with circular or rotational systems.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Cartesian coordinate - one of two numerical values specifying the position of a point on a plane in terms of horizontal and vertical distances

Etymology

From Latin polaris ('of a pole') and coordinate ('arrange').
