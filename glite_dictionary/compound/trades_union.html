trades union

1. NOUN

/ treɪdz ˈjuːniən / (AmE), / treɪdz ˈjuːniən / (BrE)
an organization of workers formed to protect and advance their rights and interests through collective bargaining with employers
an organization of employees for collective bargaining

Examples

Many members of the trades union attended the meeting to vote on the pay proposal.
She joined a trades union to help campaign for better working conditions.
The company negotiated a wage agreement with the local trades union.

Synset Examples

you have to join the union in order to get a job

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trade union - an association of workers formed to protect and further their rights

Etymology

From 'trade' (occupation or craft) and 'union' (act of joining); first used in early 19th-century Britain.
