liquid nitrogen

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪkwɪd ˈnaɪtrəʤən / (AmE), / ˈlɪkwɪd ˈnaɪtrɪdʒən / (BrE)
Nitrogen that has been cooled and pressurized to remain in a liquid state, extremely cold and used in scientific and industrial applications.
nitrogen in a liquid state, very cold

Examples

Always handle liquid nitrogen with care, as it can cause severe frostbite on contact with skin.
The scientists used liquid nitrogen to rapidly freeze the biological samples for preservation.
Demonstrations involving liquid nitrogen often reveal how materials become brittle at extremely low temperatures.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: nitrogen - a chemical element with symbol N, colorless gas at room temperature
