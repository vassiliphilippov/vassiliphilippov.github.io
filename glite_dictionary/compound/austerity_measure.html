austerity measure

1. NOUN

/ ɔːˈstɛrɪti ˈmɛʒər / (AmE), / ɔːˈstɛrɪti ˈmɛʒə / (BrE)
a government policy or action designed to reduce public spending, often by cutting services or raising taxes, typically to control public debt
government policy to cut spending

Examples

The government introduced new austerity measures to reduce the national deficit.
Many citizens protested against the harsh austerity measures imposed after the financial crisis.
The Prime Minister argued that austerity measures were necessary to stabilize the economy.

Synset Examples

Many associate neoliberalism with austerity measures and market-driven approaches.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: austerity - strict economic measures to reduce public spending
  • Antonym: stimulus - government measure to boost spending or the economy

Etymology

From 'austerity', ultimately from Latin 'austerus' (severe), and 'measure' meaning an action or policy taken.
