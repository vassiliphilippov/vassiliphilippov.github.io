giant squid

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒaɪənt ˈskwɪd / (AmE), / ˌdʒaɪənt ˈskwɪd / (BrE)
A very large, deep-ocean squid species, among the largest mollusks known, capable of growing up to 18 meters (60 feet) long.
very large deep-sea squid

Examples

Many marine biologists have studied the elusive giant squid in hopes of understanding its mysterious life cycle.
A giant squid was once found washed ashore, astonishing the local community with its enormous size.
The legend of the kraken is believed to have been inspired by encounters with the giant squid.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: colossal squid - an even larger species of deep-sea squid, similar to giant squid
