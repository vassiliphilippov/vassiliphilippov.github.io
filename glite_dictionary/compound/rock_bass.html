rock bass

1. NOUN

/ rɑk bæs / (AmE), / rɒk bæs / (BrE)
a North American freshwater fish found in the upper Mississippi River and Great Lakes regions, valued as both a game and food fish (Ambloplites rupestris)
a North American freshwater game fish

Examples

Anglers often target rock bass in shallow, rocky areas during the summer.
The chef prepared a delicious meal using freshly caught rock bass.
We saw several rock bass swimming near the dock on our trip to the lake.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bass - a type of freshwater or marine fish in the family Centrarchidae or Moronidae

2. NOUN

/ rɑk ˈbæs / (AmE), / rɒk ˈbæs / (BrE)
a North American freshwater fish of the sunfish family, resembling a small bass, or any of several related sea bass species
a type of fish; freshwater or sea bass

Examples

We caught a rock bass near the edge of the lake this morning.
The aquarium display includes several species, such as the rock bass and sunfish.
Many anglers prefer fishing for rock bass in rocky, shallow waters during the summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sea bass - any of various marine fish of the family Serranidae
