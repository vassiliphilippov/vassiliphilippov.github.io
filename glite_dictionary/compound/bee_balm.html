bee balm

1. NOUN

/ bi bɑm / (AmE), / biː bɑːm / (BrE)
A North American perennial herb of the mint family, with colorful flowers and fragrant leaves, often used in gardens and as a herbal remedy.
North American herb with showy flowers

Examples

The bee balm in the garden attracts many pollinators, especially bees and butterflies.
She brewed a tea using dried leaves from her bee balm plants.
Bee balm adds a splash of color to flowerbeds throughout the summer.

2. NOUN

/ bi bɑm / (AmE), / biː bɑːm / (BrE)
A perennial aromatic herb native to eastern North America, known for its showy, dense heads of colorful tubular flowers.
aromatic flowering herb

Examples

The gardener planted bee balm to attract hummingbirds and butterflies to the backyard.
With its bright red flowers, bee balm adds a splash of color to summer gardens.
Herbal tea made from bee balm leaves has a pleasant, minty flavor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bergamot - another name for bee balm, also refers to the oil derived from the plant
  • Degree Lower: oregano - a different aromatic herb often confused or grouped with bee balm

3. NOUN

/ bi bɑm / (AmE), / biː bɑːm / (BrE)
a bushy perennial plant of the mint family, with aromatic, lemon-scented leaves and small white or yellowish flowers, often grown in gardens
aromatic perennial plant with lemon-scented leaves

Examples

I planted bee balm in my garden to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
The leaves of bee balm can be used to make a refreshing herbal tea.
Many gardeners value bee balm for its bright flowers and pleasant aroma.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lemon balm - an aromatic herb with lemon-scented leaves, similar to bee balm
