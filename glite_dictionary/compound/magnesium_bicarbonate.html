magnesium bicarbonate

1. NOUN

/ mægˈniʒiəm baɪˈkɑrbəˌneɪt / (AmE), / mægˈniːziəm baɪˈkɑːbənət / (BrE)
a white, soluble chemical compound with the formula Mg(HCO₃)₂, often found dissolved in hard water
a chemical compound found in hard water

Examples

Magnesium bicarbonate is formed when carbon dioxide dissolves in water containing magnesium carbonate.
The presence of magnesium bicarbonate in water makes it ‘hard,’ affecting soap's ability to lather.
Researchers analyzed the sample to determine the concentration of magnesium bicarbonate in the groundwater.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calcium bicarbonate - a related compound that also contributes to water hardness

Etymology

Magnesium: from New Latin 'magnesium', named after Magnesia in Greece; bicarbonate: from 'bi-' (two) and 'carbonate'.
