Missouri gourd

1. NOUN

/ mɪˈzɜːri ɡɔrd / (AmE), / mɪˈzjʊəri ɡɔːd / (BrE)
A perennial vine native to central and southwestern United States and Mexico, producing small, hard, mottled green inedible fruit.
perennial vine with inedible green fruit

Examples

The Missouri gourd is well adapted to arid landscapes in the American Midwest.
Many gardeners grow the Missouri gourd for its ornamental value, even though its fruit is not edible.
Scientists study the Missouri gourd to understand how plants survive in dry climates.
