Khazar culture

1. NOUN

/ kəˈzɑr ˈkʌltʃər / (AmE), / kəˈzɑː ˈkʌltʃə / (BrE)
the distinctive social practices, beliefs, and traditions of the Khazar people, a semi-nomadic Turkic group who established a major empire in Eastern Europe between the 7th and 10th centuries
the culture of the Khazar people

Examples

Many historians study Khazar culture to understand medieval Eurasian societies.
Artifacts discovered along the Volga River shed light on aspects of Khazar culture.
The religious tolerance practiced in Khazar culture was notable for its time.

Synset Examples

Khazar culture blended various traditions and religions.

Etymology

Khazar from the Turkic tribal name Khazar; culture from Latin cultura (cultivation, way of life).
