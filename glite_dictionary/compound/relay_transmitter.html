relay transmitter

1. NOUN

/ ˈriˌleɪ trænzˈmɪtər / (AmE), / ˈriːleɪ trænzˈmɪtə / (BrE)
an electronic device that amplifies and retransmits signals to restore their strength during transmission
device that amplifies and retransmits signals

Examples

The television signal was boosted by a relay transmitter on the hill.
Without a relay transmitter, the radio broadcast would not reach remote villages.
Engineers installed a new relay transmitter to improve mobile phone coverage in the region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: repeater - a device that receives and retransmits a signal to extend its range

Etymology

relay from French *relayer* (to relieve), transmitter from Latin *transmittere* (to send across); technical use in telecommunications.
