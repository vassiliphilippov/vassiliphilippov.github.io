River Avon

1. PROPN

/ ˈrɪvɚ ˈævən / (AmE), / ˈrɪvə ˈeɪvən / (BrE)
a river in central England that flows through Stratford-upon-Avon and empties into the River Severn
river in central England

Examples

The picturesque banks of the River Avon attract many tourists to Stratford-upon-Avon each year.
A boat trip along the River Avon offers stunning views of the English countryside.
Flooding of the River Avon caused disruptions in several villages last winter.

Etymology

From Welsh *afon* meaning 'river', making the name essentially 'River River'.

2. PROPN

/ ˈrɪvɚ ˈævən / (AmE), / ˈrɪvə ˈeɪvən / (BrE)
a river in southwestern England that rises in Gloucestershire and flows through Bristol before emptying into the Severn estuary
river in southwestern England

Examples

The famous Clifton Suspension Bridge spans the River Avon near Bristol.
Many tourists enjoy boat trips along the scenic River Avon.
The flooding of the River Avon caused major disruptions in the local area.

Etymology

From Old Welsh 'afon' meaning 'river'.
