book burning

1. NOUN

/ bʊk ˈbɜrnɪŋ / (AmE), / bʊk ˈbɜːnɪŋ / (BrE)
The deliberate destruction of books, often for political, ideological, or religious reasons, usually as a form of censorship.
the deliberate destruction of books

Examples

The city council strongly condemned the book burning that took place in the square last night.
Many see book burning as a powerful symbol of repression and intolerance.
Throughout history, book burning has been used to suppress dissenting ideas.

Synset Examples

The protest was against book burning in schools.
The community rallied to stop the book burning event.
He spoke passionately against the idea of book burning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: censorship - the suppression or prohibition of books, films, etc.
  • Synonym: book banning - the practice of officially prohibiting specific books
