North Vietnam

1. PROPN

/ nɔrθ viˈɛtnæm / (AmE), / nɔːθ viˈɛtnæm / (BrE)
a former country in Southeast Asia, existing from 1954 to 1975, which became part of modern Vietnam after the Vietnam War
former country in Southeast Asia (1954–1975)

Examples

During the Vietnam War, North Vietnam was supported by the Soviet Union and China.
Many historical documents detail the leadership of North Vietnam between 1954 and 1975.
After the fall of Saigon, North Vietnam unified the country under communist rule.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: South Vietnam - the former southern counterpart of North Vietnam, existed from 1954 to 1975
  • Degree Higher: Vietnam - the unified country in Southeast Asia formed in 1976
