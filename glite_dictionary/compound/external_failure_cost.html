external failure cost

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˈstɜrnəl ˈfeɪljɚ kɔst / (AmE), / ɪkˈstɜːnəl ˈfeɪljə kɒst / (BrE)
the costs incurred by a business when defective products or services reach customers, such as warranty claims or returns.
costs from defects reaching customers

Examples

The company tracked its external failure costs to measure the impact of defective products on customer satisfaction.
High external failure costs can damage a company's reputation and reduce its profits.
Reducing external failure costs is a key goal in effective quality management systems.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: internal failure cost - costs related to defects found before delivery to customers
