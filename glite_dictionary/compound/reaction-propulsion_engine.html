reaction-propulsion engine

1. NOUN

/ ɹiˈækʃən pɹəˈpʌlʃən ˈɛn.dʒɪn / (AmE), / ɹiˈækʃən pɹəˈpʌlʃən ˈɛn.dʒɪn / (BrE)
A jet or rocket engine that produces thrust by emitting a high-speed stream of exhaust gases.
engine emitting high-speed exhaust for thrust

Examples

The development of the reaction-propulsion engine revolutionized modern aerospace technology.
A reaction-propulsion engine works by ejecting mass at high speed to generate forward thrust.
Engineers tested a new type of reaction-propulsion engine in their experimental rocket.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jet engine - an engine that propels an aircraft by expelling jet streams
  • Synonym: rocket engine - an engine that generates thrust by expelling exhaust gases at high speed

Etymology

From Latin 'reactio' (doing back) + 'propulsio' (pushing forward), referring to the principle of action and reaction.
