to arouse interest

1. VERB

/ əˈraʊz ˈɪntrəst / (AmE), / əˈraʊz ˈɪntrəst / (BrE)
to make someone feel curious or attentive about something; to stimulate curiosity or attention
stimulate curiosity or attention

Examples

The new advertising campaign managed to arouse interest among a younger audience.
Her unusual research findings aroused interest in the scientific community.
The filmmaker hopes his documentary will arouse interest in environmental issues.

Synset Examples

His speech will arouse interest in the project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attract attention - to cause people to notice
  • Antonym: diminish interest - to make less interested or curious
