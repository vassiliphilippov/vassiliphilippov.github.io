refracting telescope

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈfræktɪŋ ˈtɛləsˌkoʊp / (AmE), / rɪˈfræktɪŋ ˈtɛlɪˌskəʊp / (BrE)
an optical telescope that forms images by bending light through a large convex lens, usually viewed through an eyepiece
telescope using a convex lens to form images

Examples

The astronomer looked at Jupiter through a refracting telescope in the observatory.
His antique refracting telescope has a beautifully crafted brass body and glass lens.
Many schools use a refracting telescope for basic astronomy lessons due to its simple design.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: reflecting telescope - telescope that uses a mirror to form an image

Etymology

From 'refract'—from Latin 'refringere' (to break up)—and 'telescope', from Italian 'telescopio'.
