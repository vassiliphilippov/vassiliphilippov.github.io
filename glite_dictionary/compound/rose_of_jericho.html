rose of Jericho

1. NOUN

/ ˈroʊz əv ˈdʒɛrɪˌkoʊ / (AmE), / ˈrəʊz əv ˈdʒɛrɪkəʊ / (BrE)
a plant native to arid regions from the southwestern United States to Peru, known for curling into a tight ball when dry and unfolding when exposed to moisture
desert plant that curls up when dry

Examples

The rose of Jericho is often called the 'resurrection plant' because it appears to come back to life when watered.
After months of drought, the rose of Jericho unfurled its green fronds as soon as it rained.
Scientists study the rose of Jericho to understand how it survives extreme dehydration.

Etymology

Named for Jericho, an ancient city, due to the plant's resilience and ability to 'resurrect' when watered.

2. NOUN

/ ˌroʊz əv ˈdʒɛrɪˌkoʊ / (AmE), / ˌrəʊz əv ˈdʒɛrɪkəʊ / (BrE)
A small greyish desert plant found in Asia, known for rolling up into a ball when dry and unfolding when exposed to moisture; often called the resurrection plant.
desert plant that curls up when dry, opens when moist

Examples

The rose of Jericho is famous for its apparent resurrection, reviving after years without water.
Scientists studied the rose of Jericho to understand how it survives extreme drought.
Many gardeners are fascinated by the rose of Jericho and its dramatic transformation when moistened.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resurrection plant - plant that survives desiccation by curling and reviving with water

Etymology

Named after the city of Jericho, referring to the plant's apparent 'resurrection' ability.
