true pepper

1. NOUN

/ tru ˈpɛpɚ / (AmE), / truː ˈpɛpə / (BrE)
any of several shrubby climbing vines of the genus Piper, especially those cultivated for their peppercorns used as spice
shrubs in the genus Piper; source of pepper

Examples

The fruit of true pepper is used as a common spice in many cuisines.
Several species of true pepper are cultivated in tropical regions around the world.
Unlike false peppers, true pepper comes only from plants in the Piper genus.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: black pepper - the dried unripe fruit of Piper nigrum, used as a spice
  • Synonym: long pepper - fruit of Piper longum, used as a spice similar to black pepper
