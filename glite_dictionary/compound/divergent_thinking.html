divergent thinking

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈvɝːdʒənt ˈθɪŋkɪŋ / (AmE), / daɪˈvɜː.dʒənt ˈθɪŋ.kɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of generating many different ideas or solutions to a problem, especially in a creative way
creative idea generation process

Examples

Many innovative companies value divergent thinking in their employees to drive progress and creativity.
Teaching methods that encourage divergent thinking often result in more imaginative solutions from students.
Brainstorming sessions are designed to foster divergent thinking before any ideas are evaluated.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: convergent thinking - the process of narrowing down multiple ideas to find a single best solution
