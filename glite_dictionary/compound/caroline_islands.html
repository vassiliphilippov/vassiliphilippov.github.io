Caroline Islands

1. PROPN

/ ˈkæɹəˌlaɪn ˈaɪləndz / (AmE), / ˈkærəlaɪn ˈaɪləndz / (BrE)
a long chain of more than 500 islands in Micronesia, located east of the Philippines
archipelago in Micronesia, Pacific Ocean

Examples

The Caroline Islands are famous for their stunning coral reefs and diverse marine life.
European explorers reached the Caroline Islands in the 16th century.
Many unique cultures developed across the Caroline Islands over centuries.

Etymology

Named after King Charles II of Spain (Spanish: Carlos), who claimed the islands in the 17th century.
