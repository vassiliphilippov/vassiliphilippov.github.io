intervertebral vein

1. NOUN

/ ɪn.tər.vɚˈtiː.brəl veɪn / (AmE), / ɪn.tə.vɜːˈtiː.brəl veɪn / (BrE)
any of several veins that accompany spinal nerves through the intervertebral foramina and drain blood from the vertebral column
vein accompanying spinal nerves
medical

Examples

The intervertebral vein passes through the intervertebral foramen alongside the spinal nerve.
Damage to an intervertebral vein can lead to complications during spinal surgery.
The blood from the vertebral column drains into the intervertebral vein before joining larger veins.

Etymology

From Latin 'intervertebralis' (between vertebrae) and 'vein'.
