punched paper tape reader

1. NOUN

/ ˈpʌntʃt ˈpeɪpɚ teɪp ˈriːdɚ / (AmE), / ˈpʌntʃt ˈpeɪpə teɪp ˈriːdə / (BrE)
a device used to read data encoded as holes in punched paper tape
device for reading punched paper tape

Examples

The technician loaded the program into the computer using a punched paper tape reader.
Early mainframes often relied on a punched paper tape reader to input both data and instructions.
After threading the tape into the punched paper tape reader, the machine began processing the data automatically.

Synset Examples

The tape reader used compressed air, which passed through the holes and was directed into certain mechanisms of the caster.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: punched tape - strip of paper with holes for data storage
