common bog rosemary

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmən bɑɡ ˈroʊzmɛri / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən bɒɡ ˈrəʊzməri / (BrE)
An evergreen shrub found in northern European peat bogs and wet acidic areas, with clusters of white or pink hanging flowers.
evergreen shrub of northern bogs

Examples

The wetland was filled with common bog rosemary and other rare plants.
You can identify common bog rosemary by its pink, bell-shaped flowers and leathery leaves.
Scientists studied the impact of climate change on common bog rosemary populations in northern Europe.
