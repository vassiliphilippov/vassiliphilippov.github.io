parenthesis-free notation

1. NOUN

/ pəˌrɛnθəsɪs friː noʊˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / pəˌrɛnθəsɪs friː nəʊˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
a mathematical notation in which expressions are written without using parentheses to separate components
mathematical notation without parentheses

Examples

Reverse Polish is a parenthesis-free notation commonly used in calculators.
Some programming languages use parenthesis-free notation for arithmetic operations.
Learning a parenthesis-free notation can simplify the process of parsing mathematical expressions.
