four-part harmony

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔr pɑrt ˈhɑr.mə.ni / (AmE), / ˈfɔː pɑːt ˈhɑː.mə.ni / (BrE)
a style of musical harmony in which each chord consists of four notes forming four independent melodic lines, typically soprano, alto, tenor, and bass.
harmony with four independent musical lines

Examples

The choir sang in four-part harmony, filling the hall with rich, layered sound.
Composers like Bach often wrote music using four-part harmony to achieve balance and clarity.
Learning to arrange melodies in four-part harmony is a fundamental skill for music students.
