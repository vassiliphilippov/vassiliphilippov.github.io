Arctic Circle

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑrtɪk ˈsɜrkl̩ / (AmE), / ˈɑː.tɪk ˈsɜː.kl̩ / (BrE)
an imaginary line of latitude, about 66°33′ north of the Equator, that marks the boundary of the area within which, for at least one day each year, the sun does not set in summer or rise in winter
latitude line north of the Equator near the North Pole

Examples

During the summer solstice, the sun never sets above the Arctic Circle.
Reindeer are commonly found in regions north of the Arctic Circle.
He dreamed of traveling to the Arctic Circle to experience the midnight sun.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Antarctic Circle - latitude line marking the southern counterpart near the South Pole

Etymology

From 'Arctic', from Greek 'arktikos' (of the Bear, i.e. of the northern constellation), and 'circle', from Latin 'circulus'.
