law of volumes

1. NOUN

/ lɔ əv ˈvɑljumz / (AmE), / lɔː əv ˈvɒljuːmz / (BrE)
(Physics) A principle stating that, for an ideal gas at constant pressure, density varies inversely with temperature; also known as Charles's law.
gas law relating temperature and density at constant pressure

Examples

The law of volumes explains why hot air balloons rise as the air inside them expands.
According to the law of volumes, increasing the temperature of a gas at constant pressure decreases its density.
Charles's experiments led to the formulation of the law of volumes in the study of gases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Charles's law - Gas law stating that the volume of a fixed amount of gas at constant pressure varies directly with temperature
