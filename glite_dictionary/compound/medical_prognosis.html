medical prognosis

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛdɪkəl prɑgˈnoʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˈmɛdɪkəl prɒgˈnəʊsɪs / (BrE)
an expert prediction of the likely course or outcome of a disease or medical condition
prediction of the course of a disease
medical

Examples

The doctor gave a medical prognosis that suggested a full recovery within six months.
After several tests, the specialist explained the medical prognosis to the patient's family.
Because of recent advances in treatment, her medical prognosis improved significantly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prognosis - a forecast of the likely outcome of a disease

Etymology

From Greek 'prognōsis' (foreknowledge), used in medical Latin for predicting disease outcomes.
