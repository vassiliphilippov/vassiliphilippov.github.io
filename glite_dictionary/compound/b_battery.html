B battery

1. NOUN

/ bi ˈbætəri / (AmE), / bi ˈbætəri / (BrE)
a battery that provides a constant positive voltage to the plate of a vacuum tube
battery for vacuum tube plate voltage

Examples

Early radios required a B battery to power the vacuum tube circuits.
The B battery supplies the plate voltage necessary for tube operation.
He replaced the old B battery to restore function to the antique radio.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: A battery - battery that supplies filament voltage to vacuum tubes
  • Synonym: C battery - battery that supplies grid bias voltage to vacuum tubes

Etymology

Named after the convention of labeling different batteries in early radios as A, B, and C batteries.
