minor key

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪnɚ ki / (AmE), / ˈmaɪnə kiː / (BrE)
A musical key based on the minor scale, typically producing a somber or melancholic sound.
musical key based on a minor scale

Examples

The composer chose to write the sonata in a minor key to evoke a sense of sadness.
Many traditional folk songs are set in a minor key for a haunting effect.
After listening, she noticed the melody had shifted from a major to a minor key.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: major key - a key based on the major scale

2. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪnɚ ki / (AmE), / ˈmaɪnə kiː / (BrE)
a musical scale or mode that typically produces a sadder or more somber sound than a major key
music scale with a sadder sound

Examples

The composer chose to write the piece in a minor key to evoke a feeling of melancholy.
Many traditional folk songs are set in a minor key, giving them a distinctive mood.
The shift from a minor key to a major key changed the atmosphere of the music entirely.

Synset Examples

She played the sonata in a minor key.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: major key - a musical scale with a brighter, happier sound
