Uncle Sham

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌŋkəl ˈʃæm / (AmE), / ˈʌŋkəl ˈʃam / (BrE)
a nickname for a deceptive or untrustworthy person, especially one pretending to represent authority or national interests
deceptive or untrustworthy person (nickname)
informal slang

Examples

Nobody trusts Uncle Sham after that investment scandal.
The columnist referred to the fraudulent official as Uncle Sham in her latest article.
Whenever there's a scam in politics, people start talking about Uncle Sham.

Synset Examples

Don't listen to Uncle Sham; he's always selling junk.

Etymology

Play on 'Uncle Sam,' replacing 'Sam' with 'Sham' (meaning 'falsehood' or 'deception').
