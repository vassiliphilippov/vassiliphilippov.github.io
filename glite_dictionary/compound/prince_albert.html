Prince Albert

1. NOUN

/ ˌprɪns ˈæl.bɚt / (AmE), / ˌprɪns ˈæl.bət / (BrE)
a traditional man's double-breasted frock coat, popular in the 19th century
man's double-breasted frock coat

Examples

The groom wore a Prince Albert to give his wedding a classic, elegant touch.
In Victorian England, a black Prince Albert was the height of formal men's fashion.
Collectors of vintage clothing prize an authentic Prince Albert for its historical significance.

Etymology

Named after Prince Albert (1819–1861), consort of Queen Victoria.
