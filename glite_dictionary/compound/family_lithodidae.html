family Lithodidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli lɪˈθɒdəˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæməli lɪˈθɒdəˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of deep-sea crabs found in cold waters, including many species known as king crabs.
a family of deep-sea cold-water crabs

Examples

The family Lithodidae includes commercially important species such as the red king crab.
Members of the family Lithodidae are adapted to life in cold, deep-sea environments.
Scientists are studying how the family Lithodidae has evolved to survive extreme ocean conditions.

Etymology

From New Latin 'Lithodidae', based on the type genus 'Lithodes' (from Greek 'lithos', stone), referring to the crab's stony shell.
