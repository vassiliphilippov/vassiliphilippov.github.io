revolutionary group

1. NOUN

/ rɛvəˈluʃəˌnɛri ɡruːp / (AmE), / ˌrɛvəˈluːʃənəri ɡruːp / (BrE)
a political organization formed to promote or carry out revolution against an established system or government
organization promoting or carrying out revolution

Examples

The government accused the revolutionary group of planning the attacks on the capital.
During the 20th century, many revolutionary groups emerged across Latin America.
She joined a revolutionary group dedicated to overthrowing the dictatorship.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insurgent group - a group that rises in active revolt against authority, especially in an armed rebellion
