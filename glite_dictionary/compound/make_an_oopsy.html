to make an oopsy

1. VERB

/ meɪk ən ˈuːpsi / (AmE), / meɪk ən ˈuːpsi / (BrE)
to make a small mistake or blunder, often used in a lighthearted or playful way
make a small mistake (informal)
informal

Examples

I made an oopsy and dropped my phone on the floor.
He made an oopsy during the presentation, but quickly recovered.
She quietly admitted she had made an oopsy when adding salt instead of sugar.

Synset Examples

She made an oopsy while cooking and burned the dinner.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: make a mistake - commit an error or blunder
  • Synonym: to make a blunder - make a stupid or careless mistake

Etymology

Formed from 'oopsy', a childish alteration of 'oops' (exclamation for a minor accident).
