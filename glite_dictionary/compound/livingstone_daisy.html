livingstone daisy

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪvɪŋstən ˈdeɪzi / (AmE), / ˈlɪvɪŋstən ˈdeɪzi / (BrE)
a low-growing, showy annual plant native to South Africa, with succulent leaves and brightly colored flowers, often grown as an ornamental
colorful South African ornamental plant

Examples

Clusters of livingstone daisy brightened the garden with their vivid colors.
Many gardeners choose livingstone daisy for rockeries due to its drought resistance.
The petals of the livingstone daisy sparkle in the sunlight, creating a stunning effect.

Etymology

Named after the explorer David Livingstone and 'daisy,' from Old English *dæges ēage* (day's eye).
