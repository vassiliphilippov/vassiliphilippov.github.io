pot likker

1. NOUN

/ pɑt ˈlɪkər / (AmE), / pɒt ˈlɪkə / (BrE)
the liquid left in a pot after vegetables or meat have been cooked, often used as a broth or for its nutrients
liquid from cooking vegetables or meat
american

Examples

After cooking the collard greens, she saved the pot likker to serve with cornbread.
Many Southern recipes call for drinking the pot likker because it's full of flavor and nutrients.
Don't throw away the pot likker; it's perfect for making soups or gravy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stock - the liquid prepared by simmering meat, fish, or vegetables in water
  • Synonym: broth - a clear soup made by boiling meat or vegetables

Etymology

Dialectal spelling of 'pot liquor', with 'likker' reflecting Southern American English pronunciation.
