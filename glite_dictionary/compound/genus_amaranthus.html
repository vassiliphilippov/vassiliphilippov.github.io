genus Amaranthus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˌæməˈrænθəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˌæməˈranθəs / (BrE)
A large and widely distributed genus of chiefly coarse annual herbs, including species known as amaranths.
genus of annual herbs (amaranths)

Examples

The genus Amaranthus includes several species cultivated as vegetables and ornamentals.
Botanists have classified the weeds in the garden as belonging to the genus Amaranthus.
Members of the genus Amaranthus are commonly known for their bright red or purple flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amaranth - any plant of the genus Amaranthus

Etymology

From New Latin, from Greek 'amaranthos', a mythical unfading flower.
