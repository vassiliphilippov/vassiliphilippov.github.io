Mater Turrita

1. PROPN

/ ˌmeɪtər tʊˈriːtə / (AmE), / ˌmeɪtə tʊˈriːtə / (BrE)
an ancient Phrygian mother goddess, often depicted with a turreted crown; considered the counterpart of the Greek Rhea and Roman Ops
Phrygian mother goddess with a turreted crown

Examples

The temple was dedicated to Mater Turrita, revered as the protectress of the city.
Ancient artworks often portray Mater Turrita with a crown of towers upon her head.
Roman religion incorporated Mater Turrita alongside other mother goddesses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Rhea - the Greek mother goddess, wife of Cronus
  • Synonym: Ops - the Roman earth mother goddess

Etymology

From Latin, meaning 'Mother with a turreted crown'.
