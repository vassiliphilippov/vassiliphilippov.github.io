matrimonial law

1. NOUN

/ ˌmætrɪˈmoʊniəl lɔ / (AmE), / ˌmætrɪˈməʊniəl lɔː / (BrE)
the branch of law dealing with marriage, divorce, and related domestic relations
law relating to marriage and divorce
legal

Examples

He decided to specialize in matrimonial law after witnessing several complex divorce cases.
The firm has a dedicated team for handling matrimonial law matters, such as prenuptial agreements and custody disputes.
Recent reforms in matrimonial law have changed the way property is divided during divorce.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: family law - legal area concerning family relationships, including divorce and child custody

Etymology

From Latin 'matrimonium' (marriage) and 'law'.
