starting point

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɑrtɪŋ pɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˈstɑːtɪŋ pɔɪnt / (BrE)
the place, time, or condition from which something begins or is initiated
the place or time where something begins

Examples

The negotiations used previous agreements as a starting point for discussion.
Her research provided an excellent starting point for future studies.
Before setting out on the hike, they marked the starting point on the map.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beginning - the point in time or space at which something starts
  • Antonym: endpoint - the final point or end of something

2. NOUN

/ ˈstɑrtɪŋ ˌpɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˈstɑːtɪŋ ˌpɔɪnt / (BrE)
the place, situation, or stage where something begins or is initiated, often as a basis for further activity or development
initial stage or basis for development

Examples

The proposal should serve as a starting point for negotiations between the two companies.
We need a starting point before we can develop a more detailed strategy.
For many artists, copying masterpieces is a valuable starting point in learning new techniques.

Synset Examples

This project is just a starting point for our research.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: end point - the final stage or conclusion of something
  • Synonym: origin - the place or point where something begins
