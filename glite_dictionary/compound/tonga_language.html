Tonga language

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɒŋɡə ˌlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈtɒŋɡə ˌlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Bantu language spoken by the Tonga people of southern Zambia and northern Zimbabwe
the Bantu language of the Tonga people in Zambia and Zimbabwe

Examples

She is fluent in the Tonga language and speaks it at home with her family.
The Tonga language is taught in some schools in Zambia as part of the curriculum.
Researchers are documenting the Tonga language to help preserve the cultural heritage of the region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Bantu - a group of African languages to which Tonga belongs

Etymology

Tonga, the name of the ethnic group, plus English 'language'.
