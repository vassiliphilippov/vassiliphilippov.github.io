comfort zone

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌmfɚt zoʊn / (AmE), / ˈkʌmfət zəʊn / (BrE)
The range of temperatures, typically between 28 and 30 degrees Celsius, in which the naked human body can maintain heat balance without shivering or sweating.
temperature range for thermal comfort

Examples

Most laboratories maintain a comfort zone to ensure accurate results in physiological studies.
Babies are especially sensitive to temperatures outside their comfort zone.
Engineers design climate control systems to keep buildings within the comfort zone for occupants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thermal comfort - state of mind expressing satisfaction with the surrounding environment

2. NOUN

/ ˈkʌmfərt zoʊn / (AmE), / ˈkʌmfət zəʊn / (BrE)
a situation or environment in which a person feels at ease and in control, experiencing little stress or risk
situation where one feels safe or at ease

Examples

Trying new activities often means leaving your comfort zone behind.
He found himself far outside his comfort zone during the team's challenging expedition.
You won't grow if you stay in your comfort zone all the time.

Synset Examples

Stepping out of her comfort zone was challenging but rewarding.
You should get outside of your comfort zone and try new things.
He pushed himself out of his comfort zone in that class.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: risk zone - a situation involving uncertainty or difficulty
