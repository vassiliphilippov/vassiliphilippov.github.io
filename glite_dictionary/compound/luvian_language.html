Luvian language

1. NOUN

/ ˈluːvi.ən ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈluːvi.ən ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
an extinct Anatolian language closely related to Lycian, once spoken in ancient Asia Minor
extinct Anatolian language related to Lycian

Examples

The discovery of clay tablets written in the Luvian language shed new light on Anatolian history.
He specialized in the phonology of the Luvian language for his dissertation.
Few people today are able to read texts composed in the ancient Luvian language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Lycian language - another Anatolian language closely related to Luvian
  • Degree Higher: Hittite language - the best-known Anatolian language, ancestor of Luvian

Etymology

From Latin Lūvius, referring to the ancient Luwians of Asia Minor.
