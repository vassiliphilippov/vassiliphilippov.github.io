procursive epilepsy

1. NOUN

/ proʊˈkɝːsɪv ˌɛpɪˈlɛpsi / (AmE), / prəˈkɜːsɪv ˌɛpɪˈlɛpsi / (BrE)
A form of epilepsy in which seizures are triggered by whirling or running.
epilepsy triggered by running or whirling
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with procursive epilepsy after experiencing seizures while running.
Children with procursive epilepsy might need to avoid intense physical activities such as whirling or racing.
Studies have shown that procursive epilepsy is a rare but distinct type of reflex seizure disorder.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: epilepsy - a neurological disorder marked by recurrent seizures

Etymology

From Latin 'procursus' (a running forward) and Greek 'epilēpsia' (seizure).
