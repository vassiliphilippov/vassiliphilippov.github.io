azido group

1. NOUN

/ əˈziːdoʊ ɡruːp / (AmE), / əˈziːdəʊ ɡruːp / (BrE)
a univalent chemical group with the formula N₃⁻, derived from hydrazoic acid, found in organic and inorganic compounds
the N₃⁻ univalent chemical group
medical

Examples

The azido group is commonly used in click chemistry reactions.
Introduction of the azido group into the molecule enables further functionalization.
Researchers studied the reactivity of compounds containing an azido group.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: azide - a compound containing the N₃ group

Etymology

From 'azido', relating to azide (from French azote, 'nitrogen'), and 'group' (chemical grouping).
