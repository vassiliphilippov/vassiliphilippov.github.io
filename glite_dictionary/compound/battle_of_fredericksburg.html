Battle of Fredericksburg

1. NOUN

/ ˈbætəl əv ˌfrɛdərɪksˌbɜːrɡ / (AmE), / ˈbætəl əv ˌfrɛdərɪksˌbɜːɡ / (BrE)
a major battle fought in December 1862 during the American Civil War, in which Union forces led by A. E. Burnside were defeated by Confederate troops commanded by Robert E. Lee in Fredericksburg, Virginia
Civil War battle, December 1862

Examples

Historians often analyze the Battle of Fredericksburg to understand military tactics of the Civil War era.
Many soldiers lost their lives during the Battle of Fredericksburg in 1862.
The Union's defeat at the Battle of Fredericksburg had a significant impact on morale.
