Scandinavian language

1. NOUN

/ ˌskændəˈneɪviən ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌskændɪˈneɪviən ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
any of the northern Germanic languages spoken in Scandinavia and Iceland, including Danish, Swedish, Norwegian, Icelandic, and Faroese
North Germanic language family spoken in Scandinavia

Examples

Norwegian is one example of a Scandinavian language widely spoken in Norway.
Students can choose to learn a Scandinavian language such as Swedish or Danish.
Icelandic is a Scandinavian language that has changed little over the centuries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: North Germanic language - the branch of Germanic languages to which Scandinavian languages belong

Etymology

Scandinavian from Scandinavia (region of northern Europe); language from Latin 'lingua' (tongue, speech).
