genus Haliotis

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˌhæliˈoʊtɪs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˌhæliˈəʊtɪs / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Haliotidae, comprising various species of abalones.
type genus of abalones

Examples

Many edible sea snails belong to the genus Haliotis, commonly known as abalones.
Researchers discovered a new species within the genus Haliotis along the Pacific coast.
The shell of the genus Haliotis is highly valued for its iridescent inner layer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Haliotis - type genus of abalones

Etymology

From Latin 'genus' (kind) and 'Haliotis' (type genus name from Greek 'halios' (of the sea) and 'ōtos' (ear), referring to the ear-shaped shell).
