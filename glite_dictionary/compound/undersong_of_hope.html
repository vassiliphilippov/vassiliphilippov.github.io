undersong of hope

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌndɚˌsɔŋ ʌv hoʊp / (AmE), / ˈʌndəˌsɒŋ əv həʊp / (BrE)
a subtle or underlying feeling of optimism that persists beneath the surface
a subtle feeling of optimism

Examples

Although times were tough, an undersong of hope ran through the community.
The poem expresses sorrow, yet an undersong of hope can be sensed throughout its verses.
Even in his darkest moments, he was carried by an undersong of hope.

Synset Examples

His undersong of hope kept the team motivated.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: undercurrent of hope - an underlying feeling of optimism
  • Antonym: despair - complete lack of hope

Etymology

From 'undersong' meaning an underlying melody or refrain, combined with 'hope.'
