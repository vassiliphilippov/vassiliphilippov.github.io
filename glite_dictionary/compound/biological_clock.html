biological clock

1. NOUN

/ baɪəˈlɑdʒɪkəl klɑk / (AmE), / ˌbaɪəˈlɒdʒɪkəl klɒk / (BrE)
an innate mechanism in living organisms that regulates the timing of recurring physiological processes, such as sleep and hormone production
innate mechanism regulating physiological cycles

Examples

Our biological clock helps regulate sleep cycles and other daily routines.
Traveling across time zones can disrupt your biological clock and cause jet lag.
Scientists study the biological clock to better understand sleep disorders and body rhythms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: circadian rhythm - a 24-hour cycle in the physiological processes of living beings
