genus Struthio

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˈstruːθioʊ / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈstruːθiəʊ / (BrE)
The genus of African ostriches, constituting the type genus of the family Struthionidae.
genus of African ostriches

Examples

The genus Struthio is well known for its only living species, the common ostrich.
Fossil records indicate that the genus Struthio has existed for millions of years.
Researchers are studying the evolutionary history of the genus Struthio to better understand large flightless birds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ostrich - a large flightless bird native to Africa

Etymology

From Latin 'Struthio', meaning ostrich, from Ancient Greek 'strouthiōn'.
