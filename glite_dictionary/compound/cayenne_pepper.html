cayenne pepper

1. NOUN

/ kaɪˈɛn ˌpɛpər / (AmE), / kaɪˈen ˌpɛpə / (BrE)
A type of long, thin, and often curved hot red pepper, commonly dried and ground for use as a spice.
a hot, thin red pepper used as a spice

Examples

The recipe calls for a pinch of cayenne pepper to add some heat.
She sprinkled cayenne pepper on her eggs for extra flavor.
Dried cayenne pepper is commonly used in spicy sauces and marinades.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chili pepper - a hot-tasting pod of various capsicum plants, used as a spice

Etymology

Named after Cayenne, the capital of French Guiana, where it was exported from.

2. NOUN

/ kaɪˈɛn ˈpɛpər / (AmE), / kaɪˈen ˈpɛpə / (BrE)
A variety of hot chili pepper with long, thin, red pods, and the plant that bears them.
a type of hot chili pepper plant

Examples

The chef sprinkled cayenne pepper over the dish to add some heat.
Cayenne pepper is commonly grown in warm, tropical climates.
I planted a cayenne pepper in my garden last spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chili pepper - any of several varieties of hot peppers

Etymology

Named after the city of Cayenne in French Guiana.

3. NOUN

/ kaɪˈɛn ˈpepər / (AmE), / kaɪˈen ˈpɛpə / (BrE)
a hot, red powder made from ground dried pods and seeds of pungent chili peppers of the genus Capsicum, used to add heat to food
hot red chili powder used as a spice

Examples

Sprinkle a little cayenne pepper on top if you like spicy food.
She added a dash of cayenne pepper to the stew for extra heat.
Cayenne pepper is a key ingredient in many hot sauces.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chili powder - a powder made from ground hot chili peppers and other spices
  • Degree Lower: paprika - a mild red powder made from sweet peppers

Etymology

From Cayenne, the capital of French Guiana, where such peppers were traded.
