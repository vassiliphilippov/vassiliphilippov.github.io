hyaline cartilage

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪ.əˌlaɪn ˈkɑːr.tɪlɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈhaɪ.əˌlaɪn ˈkɑː.tɪ.lɪdʒ / (BrE)
A translucent type of cartilage found commonly in joints and the respiratory passages, forming most of the fetal skeleton.
translucent cartilage in joints and airways
medical

Examples

The most common type of cartilage in the body is hyaline cartilage, found at the ends of long bones.
Damage to the hyaline cartilage in the knee can lead to joint pain and immobility.
During fetal development, much of the skeleton is initially formed as hyaline cartilage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cartilage - a firm, flexible connective tissue found in various forms in the larynx and respiratory tract, and at articulating surfaces of joints

Etymology

From Greek 'hualos' (glass) and 'cartilago' (Latin for cartilage).
