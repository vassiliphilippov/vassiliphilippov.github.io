widow's walk

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪdoʊz wɑk / (AmE), / ˈwɪdəʊz wɔːk / (BrE)
A railed platform on the roof of a coastal house, traditionally used as a lookout to watch for ships at sea.
railed rooftop lookout on coastal houses

Examples

The old mansion by the harbor has a widow's walk overlooking the sea.
On stormy nights, she would climb up to the widow's walk and watch the waves crash below.
Many historic homes along the coast feature a widow's walk as part of their traditional design.

Etymology

Originating in 19th-century New England, from the idea that wives would watch for returning sailors, sometimes in vain.
