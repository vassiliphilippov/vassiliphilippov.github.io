soft-shell crab

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɔft ˌʃɛl kræb / (AmE), / ˈsɒft ˌʃɛl kræb / (BrE)
a crab that has recently shed its old shell and has a new, tender shell that is still soft and flexible, often eaten whole as a delicacy
recently molted edible crab with a soft shell

Examples

Many restaurants serve soft-shell crab fried and eaten whole during the summer season.
The chef prepared a delicious sandwich filled with crispy soft-shell crab and fresh vegetables.
In coastal areas, fishermen eagerly await the arrival of soft-shell crab season each year.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɔft ʃɛl kræb / (AmE), / ˈsɒft ʃɛl krab / (BrE)
An edible crab that has recently shed its old shell and has not yet developed a new, hard shell, making its body tender and suitable for eating whole.
recently molted edible crab

Examples

The restaurant's specialty tonight is soft-shell crab served with lemon butter sauce.
She learned how to clean and cook soft-shell crab during her culinary training.
Fishermen harvest soft-shell crabs shortly after the molting process, when their shells are still soft.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hard-shell crab - crab with a hardened shell, as opposed to soft-shell
