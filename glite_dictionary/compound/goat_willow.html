goat willow

1. NOUN

/ ɡoʊt ˈwɪloʊ / (AmE), / ɡəʊt ˈwɪləʊ / (BrE)
a much-branched Old World willow tree with large catkins and relatively broad leaves, native to Europe and Asia (Salix caprea).
a broad-leaved European willow tree

Examples

The goat willow produces soft, silvery catkins in early spring.
In the damp woodland, several goat willow trees stood alongside the stream.
Gardeners often plant goat willow for its resilience and attractive foliage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: willow - a tree or shrub of the genus Salix, known for slender branches and catkins
