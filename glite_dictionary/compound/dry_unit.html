dry unit

1. NOUN

/ ˈdraɪ ˌjuː.nɪt / (AmE), / ˈdraɪ ˌjuː.nɪt / (BrE)
a unit of measurement for the capacity of dry commodities, such as fruit or grain
unit for measuring dry goods volume

Examples

A dry unit like a bushel is traditionally used for measuring wheat.
In the United States, a peck is another common dry unit.
Farmers usually use a dry unit rather than a liquid measure for grain.
