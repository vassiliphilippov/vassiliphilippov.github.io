skipjack tuna

1. NOUN

/ ˈskɪpˌdʒæk ˈtuːnə / (AmE), / ˈskɪpˌdʒak ˈtjuːnə / (BrE)
a small, oceanic species of tuna found in warm waters worldwide, known for forming large schools and commonly used in commercial fishing and canning
a species of oceanic tuna

Examples

Fishermen caught a large skipjack tuna near the coast this morning.
Canned skipjack tuna is a popular ingredient in many salad recipes.
The skipjack tuna is known for its speed and tendency to form large schools.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bonito - a marine fish similar to tuna, sometimes called skipjack
  • Degree Higher: bluefin tuna - a large, commercially valuable species of tuna

Etymology

From 'skipjack' (possibly from Dutch 'scheepsjager', a kind of fast boat) + 'tuna'.
