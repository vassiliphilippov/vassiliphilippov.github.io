wind exposure

1. NOUN

/ wɪnd ɪkˈspoʊʒɚ / (AmE), / wɪnd ɪkˈspəʊʒə / (BrE)
the state or condition of being exposed to the wind, especially as it affects resistance or vulnerability, such as the part of a vessel or structure facing the wind
state of being exposed to wind

Examples

The tall building's wind exposure required reinforced windows on the top floors.
Sailors must account for wind exposure when navigating through open waters.
The architect selected materials that could withstand high wind exposure in coastal areas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wind resistance - opposition to motion through air caused by wind on a surface
