equinoctial point

1. NOUN

/ ˌiːkwɪˈnɑːkʃəl pɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˌiːkwɪˈnɒkʃəl pɔɪnt / (BrE)
Either of the two points on the celestial sphere where the celestial equator crosses the ecliptic, corresponding to the positions of the sun at the spring and autumn equinoxes.
one of two celestial crossing points of equator and ecliptic

Examples

The vernal equinoctial point marks the position of the sun at the start of spring.
Astronomers track the slow shift of each equinoctial point due to the precession of the equinoxes.
The intersection of the celestial equator and the ecliptic at each equinoctial point is key to mapping the heavens.

Etymology

From Latin aequinoctialis (of the equinox) + point, from Old French point, from Latin punctum.
