sphenoidal fontanelle

1. NOUN

/ sfɪˈnɔɪdəl fɑnˈtænəl / (AmE), / sfɪˈnɔɪdɪəl fɒnˈtænəl / (BrE)
the soft, irregularly shaped area on each side of an infant's skull where the frontal, parietal, temporal, and sphenoid bones meet; later becomes the pterion after ossification
soft area in infant skull where several bones meet
medical

Examples

The sphenoidal fontanelle is one of the areas on a newborn's skull that eventually hardens as the child grows.
During the examination, the doctor checked the infant's sphenoidal fontanelle for any abnormalities.
A clear understanding of the sphenoidal fontanelle is important for pediatricians assessing head development.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fontanelle - a soft spot on an infant's skull
  • Degree Higher: pterion - the region on the skull where several bones join after ossification

Etymology

From Greek 'sphenoeidēs' (wedge-shaped) and Late Latin 'fontanella' (little fountain, referring to the soft spots on a baby's skull).
