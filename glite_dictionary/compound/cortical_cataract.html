cortical cataract

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔrtɪkəl ˈkætəˌrækt / (AmE), / ˈkɔːtɪkəl ˈkætərækt / (BrE)
a cataract characterized by clouding or opacity in the lens cortex of the eye
cataract in the lens cortex
medical

Examples

The surgeon explained that my mother had a cortical cataract affecting her vision in the left eye.
A cortical cataract often causes glare and problems with night driving before other symptoms appear.
Researchers are studying the risk factors that contribute to the development of a cortical cataract.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: nuclear cataract - a cataract affecting the central nucleus of the lens
  • Degree Higher: posterior subcapsular cataract - a cataract occurring at the back of the lens capsule
  • Synonym: cataract - clouding of the eye's lens leading to vision impairment

Etymology

From cortex (Latin for 'bark' or 'rind') referring to the outer part, and cataract (from Latin 'cataracta', meaning 'waterfall').
