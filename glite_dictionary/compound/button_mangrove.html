button mangrove

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌtn ˈmæŋˌɡroʊv / (AmE), / ˈbʌtn ˈmæŋɡrəʊv / (BrE)
an evergreen tree or shrub found in coastal regions, with fruit resembling buttons and yielding heavy, hard wood
coastal tree or shrub with button-like fruit

Examples

The button mangrove is commonly found in tidal swamps along tropical coasts.
Local communities use wood from the button mangrove for making tools and crafts.
The distinctive round fruit of the button mangrove attracts many birds and marine animals.
