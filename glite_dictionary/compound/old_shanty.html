old shanty

1. NOUN

/ oʊld ˈʃænti / (AmE), / əʊld ˈʃænti / (BrE)
A small, often dilapidated dwelling, typically in poor condition.
run-down small dwelling

Examples

He grew up in an old shanty on the outskirts of town.
After the storm, only the old shanty remained standing in the field.
Tourists were surprised to see people still living in an old shanty by the river.

Synset Examples

His childhood memories are filled with that old shanty.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hut - a small, simple shelter or dwelling
  • Antonym: mansion - a large, impressive house

Etymology

From 'shanty' (mid-19th century), possibly from Canadian French 'chantier' (lumberjack's hut).
