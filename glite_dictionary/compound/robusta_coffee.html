robusta coffee

1. NOUN

/ roʊˈbʌstə ˈkɔfi / (AmE), / rəˈbʌstə ˈkɒfi / (BrE)
a species of coffee plant native to West Africa, widely cultivated for its beans, which are resistant to disease and often used in commercial coffee blends
disease-resistant coffee plant species

Examples

Many instant coffee brands rely heavily on robusta coffee for its strong flavor and high caffeine content.
Farmers in Vietnam have turned to robusta coffee because it is less susceptible to disease than arabica varieties.
The bitter taste of some espresso blends comes from the inclusion of robusta coffee beans.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: arabica coffee - another major species of coffee known for milder flavor

Etymology

From Latin robustus (strong, hardy), referring to the plant's resistance to disease.
