Arctic char

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑrktɪk tʃɑr / (AmE), / ˈɑːktɪk tʃɑː / (BrE)
a cold-water fish of the salmon family found in Arctic and subarctic lakes and rivers, known for its pink flesh and silvery appearance
a cold-water fish of the Arctic

Examples

The pristine lakes of northern Canada are home to the elusive Arctic char.
Many chefs prize Arctic char for its delicate flavor and rich color.
Researchers studied landlocked populations of Arctic char in Quebec for signs of environmental change.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: salmon - an edible fish related to Arctic char
  • Synonym: trout - a freshwater fish related to char

Etymology

From 'Arctic' (the northern polar region) + 'char' (from Old Norse 'kertr', a kind of fish).
