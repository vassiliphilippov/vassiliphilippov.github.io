carnivorous plant

1. NOUN

/ kɑrˈnɪvərəs plænt / (AmE), / kɑːˈnɪvərəs plɑːnt / (BrE)
a plant that attracts, captures, and digests insects and other small animals as a source of nutrients
plant that eats insects or small animals

Examples

The Venus flytrap is a well-known example of a carnivorous plant native to North America.
Some carnivorous plants use sticky traps to ensnare their prey.
Botanists studied the digestive enzymes produced by the carnivorous plant to better understand its adaptations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insectivorous plant - plant that feeds on insects

Etymology

From Latin 'caro, carnis' (flesh) and 'vorare' (to devour), plus 'plant'.
