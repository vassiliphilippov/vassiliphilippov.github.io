cave dweller

1. NOUN

/ ˈkeɪv ˌdwɛlɚ / (AmE), / ˈkeɪv ˌdwɛlə / (BrE)
a person or animal that lives in a cave, especially one from prehistoric times
person or animal living in a cave

Examples

Archaeologists discovered tools and paintings left by ancient cave dwellers.
Some animals, like bats, are natural cave dwellers and spend their entire lives underground.
The exhibit showed what life was like for cave dwellers thousands of years ago.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: troglodyte - a person who lived in a cave in prehistoric times

2. NOUN

/ ˈkeɪv ˌdwɛlɚ / (AmE), / ˈkeɪv ˌdwɛlə / (BrE)
a person who lives in solitude or prefers to avoid social contact; a recluse
a solitary or reclusive person

Examples

During the pandemic, she joked that she had become a cave dweller and barely talked to anyone.
After moving to the remote village, he lived as a cave dweller, rarely visiting the town.
My friends tease me for being a cave dweller because I enjoy spending weekends alone at home.

Synset Examples

He often joked about being a cave dweller during winter.
The artist lived like a cave dweller in the mountains.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recluse - a person who lives a solitary life

Etymology

From literal 'cave dweller' (someone who lives in a cave), used figuratively for a reclusive person.
