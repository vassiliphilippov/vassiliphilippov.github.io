final curtain

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪ.nəl ˈkɝː.tən / (AmE), / ˈfaɪ.nəl ˈkɜː.tən / (BrE)
the end of a performance or event, especially in the theatre
end of a performance or event

Examples

The audience stood and applauded as the final curtain fell.
After decades on stage, she gave her last bow at the final curtain.
There was a sense of sadness when the final curtain came down on the festival.

Synset Examples

With the final curtain, the audience erupted in applause.
He reflected on his career as the final curtain fell.
The play ended with a dramatic final curtain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curtain call - appearance by performers at end of a performance to receive applause
