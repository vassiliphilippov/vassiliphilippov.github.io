visual aphasia

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪʒuəl əˈfeɪziə / (AmE), / ˈvɪʒjʊəl əˈfeɪzɪə / (BrE)
a medical condition characterized by the inability to understand written words despite normal vision.
inability to understand written words
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with visual aphasia after he could no longer understand written instructions.
In cases of visual aphasia, reading comprehension is severely impaired even though vision remains intact.
Her doctor explained that her visual aphasia was the result of specific brain damage.

Etymology

From Latin 'visualis' (of sight) and Greek 'aphasia' (speechlessness or loss of ability to speak).
