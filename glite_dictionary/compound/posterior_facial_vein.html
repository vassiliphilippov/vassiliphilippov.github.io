posterior facial vein

1. NOUN

/ poʊˈstɪriər ˈfeɪʃəl veɪn / (AmE), / pɒˈstɪəriə ˈfeɪʃəl veɪn / (BrE)
the posterior branch of the facial vein, formed by temporal veins in front of the ear
posterior branch of the facial vein
medical

Examples

During surgery, the posterior facial vein was carefully preserved to prevent excessive bleeding.
The posterior facial vein drains blood from areas in front of the ear into the internal jugular vein.
An injury near the jawline may damage the posterior facial vein and cause significant swelling.
