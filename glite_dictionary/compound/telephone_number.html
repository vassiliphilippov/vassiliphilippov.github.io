telephone number

1. NOUN

/ təˈloʊˌfoʊn ˈnʌmbɚ / (AmE), / təˈləʊ.fəʊn ˈnʌmbə / (BrE)
a sequence of digits assigned to a particular telephone for making or receiving calls
number used to call a telephone

Examples

Please write down your telephone number so I can contact you later.
She changed her telephone number after moving to a new city.
I accidentally dialed the wrong telephone number and reached a stranger.

Synset Examples

he has an unlisted number

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phone number - alternative, often used, form for telephone number

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɛlɪˌfoʊn ˈnʌmbɚ / (AmE), / ˈtɛlɪfəʊn ˈnʌmbə / (BrE)
a sequence of numbers assigned to a telephone line, used to identify and connect with a particular subscriber or device
a sequence of digits for a telephone line

Examples

Could you please write down your telephone number so I can reach you later?
After moving to a new city, she had to memorize her new telephone number.
Businesses often print their telephone number on flyers and advertisements for customers to contact them.

Synset Examples

She forgot her telephone number during the interview.
Please give me your telephone number for future contact.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phone number - alternative term for telephone number

Etymology

From 'telephone' (from Greek 'tele-' meaning afar + 'phone' meaning sound) + 'number' (from Old French 'nombre', from Latin 'numerus').
