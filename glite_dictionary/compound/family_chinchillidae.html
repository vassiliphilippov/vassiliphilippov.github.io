family Chinchillidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli tʃɪnˈtʃɪlɪˌdeɪ / (AmE), / ˈfæməli tʃɪnˈtʃɪlɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of small, bushy-tailed, burrowing rodents native to South America, including chinchillas and their relatives
family of South American bushy-tailed rodents

Examples

The family Chinchillidae includes both the common chinchilla and the lesser-known viscacha.
Scientists studying the family Chinchillidae have discovered new behaviors in these burrowing rodents.
Members of the family Chinchillidae are prized for their exceptionally soft fur.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chinchilla - a small South American rodent with soft grey fur

Etymology

From New Latin, from 'Chinchilla' (the type genus) + '-idae' (standard suffix for animal families in taxonomy).
