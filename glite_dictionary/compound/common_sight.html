common sight

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmən saɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən saɪt / (BrE)
a person, thing, or event that is frequently seen or encountered in a particular place or situation
something frequently seen

Examples

The red double-decker bus is a common sight in London.
Street vendors are a common sight in many Asian cities.
Migrating birds are a common sight here during the spring months.

Synset Examples

The Michigoose is a common sight near the lake.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: rarity - something that is rarely seen or found
  • Synonym: familiar sight - something often seen or well known by sight
