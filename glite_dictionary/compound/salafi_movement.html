Salafi movement

1. NOUN

/ səˈlɑːfi ˈmuːvmənt / (AmE), / səˈlɑːfi ˈmuːvmənt / (BrE)
A Sunni Islamic reform movement advocating a return to the practices of the earliest generations of Muslims (the salaf), often characterized by strict adherence to Islamic law and opposition to religious innovations.
Sunni Islamic reform movement

Examples

The Salafi movement emphasizes the practices of the earliest generations of Muslims as the most authentic form of Islam.
Many scholars have written about the influence of the Salafi movement in modern Islamic thought.
Some governments have expressed concern over the rapid growth of the Salafi movement in their countries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Wahhabism - A puritanical Islamic movement often associated with Saudi Arabia, sometimes considered a subset of Salafism

Etymology

From Arabic 'salaf' (predecessors, ancestors), referring to the earliest Muslims, combined with 'movement.'
