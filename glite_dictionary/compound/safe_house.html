safe house

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪf haʊs / (AmE), / ˈseɪf haʊs / (BrE)
a house used as a hiding place or refuge, especially by members of secret organizations or those in danger
a hiding place or refuge house

Examples

The undercover agent was taken to a safe house until it was safe for him to leave the country.
During the war, the resistance maintained several safe houses throughout the city.
Witnesses were relocated to a safe house to protect them from potential threats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hideout - a place where someone hides, especially from the law or enemies
  • Synonym: refuge - a place providing shelter or protection from danger or distress
