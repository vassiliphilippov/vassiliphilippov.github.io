family Plasmodiidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ˌplæzˌmoʊˈdiːaɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli ˌplæzməˈdɪːɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of protozoan parasites that includes species causing malaria in humans and other animals.
malaria parasite family
medical

Examples

The family Plasmodiidae includes the organisms responsible for causing malaria in humans.
Scientists study the family Plasmodiidae to understand how malaria spreads and how to prevent infection.
Members of the family Plasmodiidae are transmitted to humans by the bite of infected mosquitoes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Plasmodium - a genus of parasitic protozoa causing malaria

Etymology

From New Latin, 'Plasmodiidae' from genus name 'Plasmodium' + suffix '-idae' used for animal families.
