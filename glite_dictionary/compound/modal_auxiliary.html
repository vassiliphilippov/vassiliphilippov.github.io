modal auxiliary

1. NOUN

/ ˈmoʊdəl ɔːɡˈzɪl.iˌɛri / (AmE), / ˈməʊdəl ɔːɡˈzɪl.i.əri / (BrE)
an auxiliary verb, such as 'can', 'may', or 'will', used with the base form of a verb to express mood, possibility, necessity, or other modalities
auxiliary verb expressing modality

Examples

'Can' and 'will' are examples of modal auxiliaries used to express ability or future intent.
A modal auxiliary like 'must' often indicates necessity or obligation in a sentence.
Teachers often highlight the use of modal auxiliaries when teaching English grammar.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: auxiliary verb - a verb used in forming the tenses, moods, and voices of other verbs
  • Synonym: modal verb - a verb (such as can, may, must) expressing necessity or possibility

Etymology

Formed from 'modal' (relating to mood or mode) and 'auxiliary' (helping verb).
