the Holy See

1. NOUN

/ ði ˌhoʊli ˈsi / (AmE), / ði ˌhəʊli ˈsiː / (BrE)
the jurisdiction of the Pope as the Bishop of Rome and the central government of the Roman Catholic Church, recognized as a sovereign entity
the Pope's jurisdiction and Vatican's sovereign entity

Examples

He visited the Holy See to meet with officials of the Catholic Church.
Diplomatic relations with the Holy See are handled separately from those with Italy.
The Holy See plays an important role in international humanitarian efforts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: the Vatican - the independent city-state and residence of the Pope

Etymology

From Latin Sancta Sedes, meaning 'Holy Chair' or 'Holy See'.
