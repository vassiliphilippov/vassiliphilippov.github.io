lift pump

1. NOUN

/ lɪft pʌmp / (AmE), / lɪft pʌmp / (BrE)
a pump designed to raise a liquid, especially water, from a lower to a higher level, typically by suction or atmospheric pressure rather than by forcing it upward
pump for raising liquids to a higher level

Examples

The old well relied on a lift pump to draw water up for the farmhouse.
They installed a new lift pump to help supply the irrigation system.
If the lift pump fails, water will not reach the upper reservoir.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: force pump - a pump that forces a liquid to a higher level by pressure
