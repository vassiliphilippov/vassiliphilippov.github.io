order Taxales

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdər tækˈseɪliːz / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə tækˈseɪliːz / (BrE)
A former taxonomic order of coniferous trees comprising the yews, coextensive with the family Taxaceae.
taxonomic order of yew trees

Examples

The order Taxales was once widely used in botanical classification for yews.
Advances in plant genetics led to reclassifying many members of the order Taxales.
The order Taxales is now generally considered synonymous with the family Taxaceae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Taxaceae - the yew family of coniferous trees

Etymology

From Latin 'taxus' (yew) with New Latin order-naming suffix '-ales'.
