spin doctor

1. NOUN

/ spɪn ˈdɑktɚ / (AmE), / spɪn ˈdɒktə / (BrE)
a person, especially in politics or public relations, who tries to control the way events are described to the public in order to influence opinion, typically by presenting them in a favorable light
one who manipulates public perception, esp. in politics

Examples

The candidate's spin doctor explained the controversial statement to calm public outrage.
Every major political party employs at least one skilled spin doctor to handle the media.
After the scandal, a team of spin doctors worked tirelessly to protect the company's reputation.

Synset Examples

his title is Director of Communications but he is just a spin doctor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: publicist - a person responsible for public relations or publicity
  • Synonym: press agent - a person employed to generate favorable publicity

Etymology

First used in the U.S. in the 1980s, from 'spin' (interpretation or slant) + 'doctor' (expert or fixer).

2. NOUN

/ spɪn ˈdɑktɚ / (AmE), / spɪn ˈdɒktə / (BrE)
A person, especially in politics or public relations, who manipulates information or presents news in a way that is intended to influence public opinion.
person manipulating information to influence perception
informal

Examples

The candidate's spin doctor crafted a statement to downplay the controversy.
After the scandal broke, a spin doctor was called in to handle the press.
The company's spin doctor managed to turn the bad news into a positive story.

Synset Examples

Her role as a spin doctor was crucial during the crisis.
The media relied on the spin doctor for clarifications.
The politician hired a spin doctor to manage his image.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: publicist - person responsible for public relations and media representation
  • Synonym: propagandist - person who spreads information, often biased, to promote a cause
  • Antonym: truth-teller - one who tells the truth or presents facts honestly

Etymology

Formed from 'spin' (presenting information with a bias) and 'doctor' (one who does something skillfully); first cited in 1980s US political usage.
