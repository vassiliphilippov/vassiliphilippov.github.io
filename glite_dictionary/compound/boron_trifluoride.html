boron trifluoride

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɔːrɑn traɪˈfluːraɪd / (AmE), / ˈbɔːrɒn traɪˈflʊəraɪd / (BrE)
a pungent, colorless, poisonous gas (BF₃) used as a catalyst in organic synthesis and in the production of other chemicals
a pungent colorless gas (BF₃); chemical compound

Examples

The laboratory was equipped with cylinders of boron trifluoride for their organic synthesis experiments.
Boron trifluoride is highly toxic and must be handled with extreme care in a fume hood.
When boron trifluoride reacts with water, it produces boric acid and hydrofluoric acid.

Etymology

From 'boron' (element name) and 'trifluoride' (indicating three fluorine atoms).
