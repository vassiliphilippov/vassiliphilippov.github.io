to march for justice

1. VERB

/ mɑrtʃ fər ˈdʒʌstɪs / (AmE), / mɑːtʃ fə ˈdʒʌstɪs / (BrE)
to participate in a public demonstration or protest to demand justice, typically related to civil rights or social causes
protest demonstration for justice

Examples

Thousands gathered downtown to march for justice after the court verdict was announced.
Communities across the country will march for justice in solidarity with those affected.
Every year, activists march for justice to commemorate the historic civil rights movement.

Synset Examples

They will march for justice this weekend.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: protest march - organized walk to express opposition or support for a cause
