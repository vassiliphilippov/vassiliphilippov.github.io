Gulf of Lepanto

1. PROPN

/ ɡʌlf əv ləˈpæntoʊ / (AmE), / ɡʌlf əv lɪˈpantoʊ / (BrE)
an inlet of the Ionian Sea situated between central Greece and the Peloponnesus
inlet of the Ionian Sea, Greece

Examples

The Gulf of Lepanto is known for its scenic coastline and historical significance.
Sailing across the Gulf of Lepanto, we admired the mountainous backdrop of central Greece.
The Battle of Lepanto in 1571 took place in the waters of the Gulf of Lepanto.

Etymology

From Italian 'Lepanto', the Italian name for the Greek town of Nafpaktos, near the gulf.
