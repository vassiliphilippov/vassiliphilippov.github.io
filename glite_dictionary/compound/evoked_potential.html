evoked potential

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈvoʊkt pəˈtɛnʃəl / (AmE), / ɪˈvəʊkt pəˈtɛnʃl / (BrE)
an electrical response produced by the central nervous system when stimulated by an external source, especially as measured by electrodes
electrical brain response to stimulation
medical

Examples

The neurologist recorded the evoked potential to assess the patient's sensory function.
Auditory evoked potentials help diagnose hearing disorders in infants.
Researchers studied visual evoked potentials in response to flashing lights.

Synset Examples

he measured evoked potentials with an electroencephalogram

Related Terms

  • Synonym: event-related potential - an electrical brain response tied specifically to an event or stimulus

Etymology

From 'evoke' (Latin evocare, to call forth) and 'potential' (from Latin potentialis, relating to power).
