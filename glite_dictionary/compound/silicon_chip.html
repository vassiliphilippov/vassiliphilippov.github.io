silicon chip

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪlɪkən tʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈsɪlɪkən tʃɪp / (BrE)
A small, thin piece of silicon containing an integrated circuit used to perform electronic functions in computers and other devices.
thin silicon wafer with integrated circuit

Examples

The computer's speed increased dramatically after installing a faster silicon chip.
Modern smartphones rely on highly advanced silicon chips to function smoothly.
Engineers are constantly developing new silicon chips to handle more complex tasks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: microchip - a very small silicon chip containing integrated circuits
  • Synonym: integrated circuit - an electronic circuit formed on a small piece of semiconductor material

Etymology

From silicon, the chemical element used as a semiconductor, and chip, meaning a small piece cut off from a material.
