oleaster family

1. NOUN

/ oʊˈliːˌæstər ˈfæməli / (AmE), / əʊˈliːˌæstə ˈfæməli / (BrE)
A family of shrubs or small trees, often with spines, scientifically known as Elaeagnaceae.
family of shrubs and small trees (Elaeagnaceae)

Examples

The Russian olive belongs to the oleaster family and is commonly found along riverbanks.
Plants in the oleaster family are known for their silvery leaves and spiny branches.
Botanists studied the unique fruiting patterns within the oleaster family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oleaster - a type of shrub or small tree belonging to the oleaster family
  • Synonym: Elaeagnaceae - the scientific name of the oleaster family

Etymology

From 'oleaster', a type of plant, ultimately from Latin 'oleaster' (wild olive); 'family' in the botanical sense.
