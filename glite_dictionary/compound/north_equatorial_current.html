North Equatorial Current

1. PROPN

/ nɔrθ ɪˌkwɛtʃəriəl ˈkɝːənt / (AmE), / nɔːθ ɪˌkwɛtʃəriəl ˈkʌrənt / (BrE)
a warm ocean current that flows westward across the Pacific Ocean, just north of the equator
ocean current north of the equator

Examples

The North Equatorial Current plays a major role in the movement of warm water across the Pacific Ocean.
Fishermen along the Philippines are affected by changes in the North Equatorial Current.
Scientists monitor the North Equatorial Current to better understand weather patterns.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: South Equatorial Current - an ocean current that flows east to west just south of the equator
