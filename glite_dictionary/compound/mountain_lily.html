mountain lily

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaʊntən ˌlɪli / (AmE), / ˈmaʊntɪn ˌlɪli / (BrE)
a showy white-flowered perennial plant native to New Zealand, grown for its decorative flowers
New Zealand perennial with white flowers

Examples

The mountain lily blooms in the early spring, adding beauty to alpine gardens.
Gardeners in cooler climates often grow the mountain lily for its striking white flowers.
Hiking through New Zealand, we spotted several rare mountain lilies along the trail.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmaʊntən ˈlɪli / (AmE), / ˈmaʊntɪn ˈlɪli / (BrE)
A Japanese lily (Lilium auratum) with large white flowers marked by golden rays.
Japanese lily with golden rays

Examples

The mountain lily is prized for its beautiful golden markings and pleasant fragrance.
She planted a mountain lily in her garden to add a touch of elegance.
The florist recommended a bouquet featuring the striking mountain lily for the wedding.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Lilium auratum - scientific name for the Japanese lily or 'mountain lily'
