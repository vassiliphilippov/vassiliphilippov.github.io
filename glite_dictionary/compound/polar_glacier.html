polar glacier

1. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊlɚ ˈɡleɪʃər / (AmE), / ˈpəʊlə ˈɡleɪsiə / (BrE)
a glacier located near the Arctic or Antarctic poles
glacier near Earth's poles

Examples

Scientists took ice core samples from the polar glacier to study ancient climate patterns.
The melting of a polar glacier can have global consequences for sea level rise.
A polar glacier is typically colder and more stable than temperate glaciers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glacier - large mass of ice moving slowly over land
