genus Pterocarpus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌptɛr.oʊˈkɑːr.pəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌptɛr.əˈkɑː.pəs / (BrE)
a genus of tropical trees or climbers noted for usually having broadly winged pods, including species such as padauk and bloodwood
genus of tropical trees with winged pods

Examples

Many species in the genus Pterocarpus are valued for their durable timber.
The brightly colored wood from the genus Pterocarpus is often used for making furniture and musical instruments.
Researchers discovered a new member of the genus Pterocarpus in the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

Etymology

From Greek pteron (wing) + karpos (fruit), indicating the winged fruit.
