diva attitude

1. NOUN

/ ˈdiːvə ˈætɪˌtud / (AmE), / ˈdiːvə ˈætɪˌtjuːd / (BrE)
behavior that is self-important, demanding, or difficult, often likened to that of a celebrated performer with a sense of entitlement.
self-important or demanding behavior
informal

Examples

Her diva attitude made working with her a daily challenge for the team.
The singer's diva attitude became legendary backstage at the awards show.
If you bring that diva attitude to the meeting, people won't take you seriously.

Synset Examples

Her diva attitude reminded everyone of Miss Piggy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prima donna - a temperamental and self-important person

Etymology

From 'diva' (Italian, 'goddess', used for celebrated women performers) and 'attitude'.
