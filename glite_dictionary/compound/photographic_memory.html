photographic memory

1. NOUN

/ ˌfoʊ.t̬əˈɡræf.ɪk ˈmem.ə.ri / (AmE), / ˌfəʊ.təˈɡræf.ɪk ˈmem.ər.i / (BrE)
an exceptional ability to remember images, facts, or scenes in great detail, as if having taken a photograph
ability to remember visual information vividly

Examples

She astonished her colleagues with her photographic memory for names and faces.
Because of his photographic memory, he could recite entire pages from textbooks after one reading.
Many people believe a photographic memory means someone never forgets anything they see.

Synset Examples

Her photographic memory helps her ace exams.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eidetic memory - the ability to recall images, sounds, or objects with high precision
