sulphur butterfly

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌlfər ˈbʌtərˌflaɪ / (AmE), / ˈsʌlfə ˈbʌtəflaɪ / (BrE)
Any of various butterflies, especially of the subfamily Coliadinae, that are typically yellow or orange in color.
yellow or orange butterfly species

Examples

We spotted a vibrant sulphur butterfly resting on the wildflowers in the meadow.
The sulphur butterfly is easily recognized by its bright yellow wings.
Several sulphur butterflies migrated across the fields during the late summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: butterfly - an insect with two pairs of large, typically brightly colored wings

Etymology

From 'sulphur', referencing the yellow coloration, and 'butterfly'.
