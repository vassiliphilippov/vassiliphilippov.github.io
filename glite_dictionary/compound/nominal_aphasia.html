nominal aphasia

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɑːmɪnəl əˈfeɪziə / (AmE), / ˈnɒmɪnəl əˈfeɪziə / (BrE)
A medical condition characterized by the inability to name objects or recognize their names, either spoken or written.
inability to name or recognize objects (medical)
medical

Examples

After his stroke, the patient developed nominal aphasia and struggled to recall the names of everyday items.
The neurologist diagnosed her with nominal aphasia after repeated difficulty in naming common objects during the assessment.
People with nominal aphasia may understand an object's function but cannot remember or say its name.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anomia - difficulty naming objects; synonym for nominal aphasia

Etymology

From Latin 'nomen' (name) and Greek 'aphasia' (speechlessness or inability to speak).
