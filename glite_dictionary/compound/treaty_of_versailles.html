Treaty of Versailles

1. PROPN

/ ˈtrit̬i əv vɛrˈsaɪ / (AmE), / ˈtriːti əv vɛˈsaɪ / (BrE)
the peace treaty signed in 1919 that ended World War I and imposed heavy reparations and territorial losses on Germany
peace treaty ending World War I

Examples

The Treaty of Versailles was signed in the Hall of Mirrors at the Palace of Versailles.
Many historians believe the harsh terms of the Treaty of Versailles contributed to the rise of tensions leading to World War II.
Germany was required to accept responsibility for the war and pay reparations under the Treaty of Versailles.

Etymology

Named after Versailles, the French city where the treaty was signed.
