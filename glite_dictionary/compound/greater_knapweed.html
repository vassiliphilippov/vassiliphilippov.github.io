greater knapweed

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡreɪtər ˈnæpwid / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪtə ˈnapwiːd / (BrE)
a tall European perennial plant with purple flower heads, commonly found in grasslands and meadows
a tall European plant with purple flowers

Examples

The greater knapweed blooms in late summer, attracting numerous butterflies to the field.
Researchers studied how greater knapweed contributes to the diversity of wildflower meadows.
Gardeners appreciate greater knapweed for its vivid, thistle-like purple blossoms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: knapweed - any plant of the genus Centaurea, especially with thistle-like heads
