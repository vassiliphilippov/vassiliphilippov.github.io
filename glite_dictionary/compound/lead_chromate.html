lead chromate

1. NOUN

/ lɛd ˈkroʊmeɪt / (AmE), / lɛd ˈkrəʊmeɪt / (BrE)
a yellow, poisonous chemical compound (PbCrO4) used as a pigment in paints
yellow poisonous pigment used in paint

Examples

Objects painted with lead chromate were often prized for their bright yellow color.
The use of lead chromate in children's toys has been banned due to its toxicity.
Artists once favored lead chromate as a pigment, despite its hazardous properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chrome yellow - a vivid yellow pigment, often made from lead chromate
