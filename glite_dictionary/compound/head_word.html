head word

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛd wɝd / (AmE), / ˈhɛd wɜːd / (BrE)
(Grammar) The word within a phrase or grammatical constituent that determines its grammatical category and plays the same syntactic role as the whole constituent.
main word in a phrase (grammar)

Examples

In the noun phrase "the big red ball," the head word is "ball."
The head word of a verb phrase typically determines the tense of the phrase.
Linguists can identify the head word to analyze sentence structure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: head - the main or most important word of a phrase (grammar)

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɛd ˌwɝd / (AmE), / ˈhɛd ˌwɜːd / (BrE)
A word in a phrase that is modified or qualified by other words, especially in grammar or linguistics.
main word in a phrase (grammar)

Examples

In the noun phrase 'the big house', 'house' is the head word.
Modifiers add detail to the head word in a sentence.
Every phrase must contain a head word that determines its grammatical function.
