rock-bottom price

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɑk ˈbɑtəm ˈpraɪs / (AmE), / ˌrɒk ˈbɒtəm ˈpraɪs / (BrE)
the lowest possible price; a price as low as it can be
lowest possible price

Examples

The store is advertising a rock-bottom price on last year's model.
After months of negotiation, the buyer secured a rock-bottom price for the house.
Online retailers often attract customers by offering a rock-bottom price on electronics.

Synset Examples

They offered a rock-bottom price for the used car.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bargain - something bought or offered for sale more cheaply than is usual or expected
  • Antonym: exorbitant price - an extremely high or excessive price

Etymology

Derived from 'rock-bottom', meaning the lowest point, combined with 'price' to denote minimal cost.
