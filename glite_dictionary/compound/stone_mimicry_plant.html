stone mimicry plant

1. NOUN

/ stoʊn ˈmɪməkri plænt / (AmE), / stəʊn ˈmɪməkri plɑːnt / (BrE)
a highly succulent, stemless, clump-forming plant with grey-green leaves that closely resemble lumps of granite, native to South Africa
succulent plant resembling stones

Examples

The stone mimicry plant is popular among collectors for its unusual appearance and low maintenance needs.
In its natural habitat, the stone mimicry plant blends in perfectly with surrounding rocks to avoid being eaten.
I bought a stone mimicry plant from the nursery to add a unique touch to my succulent garden.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Lithops - genus of 'stone mimicry plants' native to southern Africa
  • Synonym: succulent - plant with thick, fleshy tissues for water storage
