yellow jasmine

1. NOUN

/ ˈjɛl.oʊ ˈdʒæz.miːn / (AmE), / ˈjɛl.əʊ ˈdʒæz.mɪn / (BrE)
a poisonous woody evergreen vine of the southeastern United States with fragrant yellow, funnel-shaped flowers
poisonous vine with yellow flowers

Examples

The scent of yellow jasmine drifted through the garden, filling the air with a sweet aroma.
You should be careful when handling yellow jasmine because all parts of the plant are toxic.
In spring, the yellow jasmine blooms cover the fence in a cascade of golden flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Carolina jessamine - another common name for yellow jasmine (Gelsemium sempervirens)
