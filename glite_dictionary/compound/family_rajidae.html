family Rajidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli rɑːˈd͡ʒi.aɪd.i / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli ræˈd͡ʒiːdiː / (BrE)
A family of bottom-dwelling cartilaginous fish known as skates, found in tropical and temperate marine waters.
a family of skates (cartilaginous fish)

Examples

The family Rajidae includes many species of skates found worldwide.
Researchers discovered a new member of the family Rajidae off the coast of Australia.
Unlike true rays, members of the family Rajidae generally lay eggs in protective cases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skate - a type of cartilaginous fish related to rays
  • Degree Higher: ray - a broad, flat cartilaginous fish, related group

Etymology

Rajidae from Latin 'raia' (ray, skate) + '-idae,' a zoological family suffix.
