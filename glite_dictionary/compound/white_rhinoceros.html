white rhinoceros

1. NOUN

/ waɪt raɪˈnɑːsərəs / (AmE), / waɪt raɪˈnɒsərəs / (BrE)
a large, light-grey African rhinoceros with two horns, found in grassland and savanna, and considered endangered
large African rhinoceros with two horns

Examples

The population of the white rhinoceros has declined drastically due to poaching.
We saw a white rhinoceros grazing peacefully in the South African savanna.
Conservationists are working hard to protect the endangered white rhinoceros.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: black rhinoceros - smaller African rhino species with two horns, more aggressive
