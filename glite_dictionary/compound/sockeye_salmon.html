sockeye salmon

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɑːkaɪ ˈsæmən / (AmE), / ˈsɒkaɪ ˈsæmən / (BrE)
a species of salmon (Oncorhynchus nerka) native to northern Pacific rivers, known for its red flesh and valued as food; adults die after spawning
a Pacific salmon with red flesh

Examples

The grilled sockeye salmon was the highlight of our dinner last night.
Fishermen in Alaska rely on the annual sockeye salmon run for their livelihoods.
After spawning, adult sockeye salmon die, completing their life cycle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: salmon - a large edible fish of the Salmonidae family

Etymology

From North American English; 'sockeye' is an anglicization of the Halkomelem word 'suk-kegh'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɑːkaɪ ˈsæmən / (AmE), / ˈsɒkaɪ ˈsæmən / (BrE)
the fatty red flesh of a salmon species native to the Pacific coast and rivers, valued as food
fatty red flesh of a Pacific salmon

Examples

Grilled sockeye salmon is a popular dish at many seafood restaurants along the Pacific Northwest.
The rich, red color of sockeye salmon makes it a favorite among sushi chefs.
We bought fresh sockeye salmon at the market to prepare for dinner.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: salmon - a common fish found in both Atlantic and Pacific oceans, valued for its flesh

Etymology

From North American Chinook Jargon, from Salish word for the fish, possibly from Halkomelem 'sθə́qəy̓.'
