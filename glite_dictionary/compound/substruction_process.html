substruction process

1. NOUN

/ sʌbˈstrʌkʃən ˈprɑːsɛs / (AmE), / sʌbˈstrʌkʃən ˈprəʊsɛs / (BrE)
the process of constructing the foundation of a building or structure
foundation construction process

Examples

The civil engineer explained every step of the substruction process during the site visit.
Budget overruns were caused by unexpected issues in the substruction process.
Detailed documentation of the substruction process is required for building certification.

Synset Examples

The architect oversaw the substruction process closely.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foundation - the lowest load-bearing part of a building, typically below ground level

Etymology

From Latin 'substructio' (a building under), referring to underlying construction.
