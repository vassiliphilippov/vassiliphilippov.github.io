booster station

1. NOUN

/ ˈbuːstər ˌsteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈbuːstə ˌsteɪʃən / (BrE)
a station containing equipment that amplifies and restores the strength of a transmitted signal along a communication line
a station that strengthens transmitted signals

Examples

The telephone company installed a booster station along the route to maintain signal quality.
Technicians checked the booster station to ensure it was correctly amplifying the weak signals.
Each booster station on the network plays a crucial role in keeping communications clear over long distances.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amplifier - device that increases the power of an electrical signal
