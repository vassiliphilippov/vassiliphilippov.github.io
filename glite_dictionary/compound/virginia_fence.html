Virginia fence

1. NOUN

/ vərˈdʒɪniə fɛns / (AmE), / vəˈdʒɪniə fɛns / (BrE)
a fence made of rails laid in a zigzag pattern, with the rails interlocking to form a stable structure without the need for posts
zigzag rail fence; interlocking rails
american

Examples

The old farmhouse was surrounded by a Virginia fence that had stood for generations.
They built a Virginia fence to mark the boundary of the property without using nails or posts.
The zigzag pattern of a Virginia fence makes it stable even on uneven ground.

Etymology

Named after the U.S. state of Virginia, where this type of fence was commonly used.

2. NOUN

/ vərˈdʒɪn.jə fɛns / (AmE), / vəˈdʒɪn.i.ə fɛns / (BrE)
A traditional type of fence made from horizontal rails stacked in a zigzag pattern, commonly used for gardens and properties for privacy and decorative purposes.
type of zigzag rail fence for privacy/aesthetics

Examples

They decided to build a Virginia fence around their backyard for extra privacy.
The rustic charm of a Virginia fence makes it a popular choice for rural gardens.
Maintaining a Virginia fence requires occasional replacement of wooden rails.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: split-rail fence - a fence made from wooden rails, often in a zigzag formation

Etymology

Named after the U.S. state of Virginia, where this fence style was widely used.
