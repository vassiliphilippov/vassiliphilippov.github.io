poverty level

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑvərti ˌlɛvəl / (AmE), / ˈpɒvəti ˌlɛvəl / (BrE)
The minimum income threshold below which a person or family is considered to be living in poverty, as defined by government or official standards.
minimum income defining poverty

Examples

Many families in the area live below the poverty level and struggle to meet basic needs.
Raising the minimum wage could help more people rise above the poverty level.
Policies are often evaluated by how effectively they reduce the number of citizens under the poverty level.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poverty line - an officially determined minimum income needed for basic needs
