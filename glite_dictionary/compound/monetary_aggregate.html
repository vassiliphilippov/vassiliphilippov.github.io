monetary aggregate

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌnətɛri ˈæɡrɪɡət / (AmE), / ˈmʌnɪtəri ˈæɡrɪɡət / (BrE)
a measurement of the total amount of money in circulation within an economy, including cash and various types of deposits
measure of a country's total money supply

Examples

Central banks regularly review different monetary aggregates to assess economic growth and inflation risk.
M2 is one example of a monetary aggregate used by economists.
Changes in monetary aggregate levels can influence government fiscal and monetary policy decisions.

Synset Examples

There is no single ‘correct’ measure of the money supply; instead, there are several measures, classified along a spectrum or continuum between narrow and broad monetary aggregates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: money supply - the total amount of money available in an economy
