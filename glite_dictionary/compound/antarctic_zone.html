Antarctic Zone

1. NOUN

/ ænˈtɑːrktɪk zoʊn / (AmE), / ænˈtɑːktɪk zəʊn / (BrE)
the region surrounding the South Pole, including Antarctica and its adjacent waters
region around the South Pole

Examples

Few animals can survive the extreme cold of the Antarctic Zone.
Climate change is having noticeable effects in the Antarctic Zone.
Scientists travel to the Antarctic Zone to study its unique ecosystem.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Arctic Zone - the region around the North Pole, including the Arctic Ocean and surrounding land areas

Etymology

From Greek 'antarktikos' (opposite to the Arctic) and English 'zone'.
