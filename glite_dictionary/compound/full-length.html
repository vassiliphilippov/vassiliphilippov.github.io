full-length

1. ADJ

/ fʊl lɛŋθ / (AmE), / fʊl lɛŋθ / (BrE)
(of a work, garment, or recording) of the standard or complete length; not shortened or abridged.
of standard or complete length

Examples

The director released a full-length version of the documentary last year.
She wore a beautiful full-length gown to the gala.
This is the full-length novel, not the condensed one.

Synset Examples

the full-length play

Related Terms

  • Antonym: abridged - shortened version of a text or work
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00005106-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ fʊl lɛŋkθ / (AmE), / fʊl lɛŋ(k)θ / (BrE)
measuring or showing the entire length of something, especially a person's body; not shortened or cut.
measuring the entire length; not shortened

Examples

She hung a full-length mirror on the back of her bedroom door.
The museum displayed a full-length portrait of the queen in the main hall.
He wore a full-length coat to keep warm during the winter.

Synset Examples

a full-length portrait

Related Terms

  • Antonym: shortened - made shorter; not full length
