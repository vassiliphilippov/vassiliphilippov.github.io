lacrimal vein

1. NOUN

/ ˈlækrəməl veɪn / (AmE), / ˈlækrɪməl veɪn / (BrE)
A vein that drains blood from the lacrimal gland and empties into the superior ophthalmic vein.
vein draining the lacrimal gland
medical

Examples

The lacrimal vein is responsible for draining blood from the area around the tear gland.
During the surgery, the doctor carefully avoided damaging the lacrimal vein.
Obstruction of the lacrimal vein may lead to swelling near the eye.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: superior ophthalmic vein - vein into which the lacrimal vein empties
  • Synonym: lacrimal gland - gland that produces tears, drained by lacrimal vein

Etymology

From Latin 'lacrima' meaning 'tear', referring to its association with the tear gland.
