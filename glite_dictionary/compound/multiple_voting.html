multiple voting

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌltəpəl ˈvoʊtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmʌltɪpl ˈvəʊtɪŋ / (BrE)
The act of casting more than one vote at the same election by the same person, which is illegal in many jurisdictions.
illegal act of voting more than once
legal

Examples

The electoral commission warned that multiple voting could undermine public trust in the election results.
Strict identification checks are used to prevent multiple voting during national elections.
He was charged with multiple voting, a serious offense under election law.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ballot stuffing - illegal act of casting additional votes in an election
  • Synonym: voter fraud - illegal interference with the election process by voters
