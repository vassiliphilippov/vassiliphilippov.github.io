digital audiotape

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɪdʒɪtəl ˈɔdiˌteɪp / (AmE), / ˈdɪdʒɪtəl ˈɔːdiəʊteɪp / (BrE)
A magnetic tape used for recording and playing back audio in a digital format, commonly abbreviated as DAT.
digital recording tape for audio (DAT)

Examples

The studio archived all old sessions on digital audiotape for higher fidelity.
Many musicians in the 1990s recorded their albums on digital audiotape.
Despite its quality, digital audiotape eventually lost popularity to other digital recording technologies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: DAT - Abbreviation for digital audiotape, a digital sound recording format
