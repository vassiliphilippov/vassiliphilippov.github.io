hybrid car

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪ.brɪd kɑɹ / (AmE), / ˈhaɪ.brɪd kɑː / (BrE)
a car powered by two different energy sources, typically an internal combustion engine and an electric motor, to improve fuel efficiency and reduce emissions
car with both electric and fuel power

Examples

She bought a hybrid car to save money on gasoline and help the environment.
The city encourages the use of hybrid cars by offering tax deductions on their purchase.
Driving a hybrid car can reduce your carbon footprint compared to conventional vehicles.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: electric car - a car powered solely by electricity
  • Degree Lower: gasoline car - a car powered solely by an internal combustion engine
