silver quandong tree

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪlvɚ ˈkwɑnˌdɔŋ ˌtri / (AmE), / ˈsɪlvə ˈkwɒn.dɒŋ ˌtriː / (BrE)
An Australian tree with hard white timber, glossy green leaves, white flowers, and one-seeded glossy blue fruit.
Australian tree with blue fruit and white timber

Examples

The silver quandong tree produces striking blue fruit that attract many birds in the Australian bush.
Carpenters prize the hard white timber of the silver quandong tree for furniture making.
During spring, the silver quandong tree is covered in clusters of tiny white flowers.

Etymology

From 'silver' (color) and Wiradjuri 'quandong' (fruit tree).
