first offender

1. NOUN

/ ˌfɝst əˈfɛndɚ / (AmE), / ˌfɜːst əˈfɛndə / (BrE)
a person who has been convicted of a crime for the first time
person convicted for the first time
legal

Examples

As a first offender, she received a lighter sentence from the judge.
The program is designed to help first offenders reintegrate into society.
Being a first offender may influence the outcome of a criminal trial.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: recidivist - a person who continues to commit crimes after being punished
