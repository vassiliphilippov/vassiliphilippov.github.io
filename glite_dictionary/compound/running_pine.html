running pine

1. NOUN

/ ˈrʌnɪŋ paɪn / (AmE), / ˈrʌnɪŋ paɪn / (BrE)
A creeping evergreen club moss (Lycopodium clavatum) with long, trailing stems, found in temperate and arctic regions.
a creeping club moss (Lycopodium clavatum)

Examples

In the forest, the running pine spreads across the ground with its distinctive trailing stems.
Some traditional herbal remedies use running pine as an ingredient for their preparations.
The delicate foliage of running pine resembles tiny pine needles, though it is not a true pine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ground pine - another common name for Lycopodium clavatum
