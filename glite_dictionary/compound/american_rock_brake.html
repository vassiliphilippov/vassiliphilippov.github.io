American rock brake

1. NOUN

/ əˈmɛr.ɪ.kən rɑk breɪk / (AmE), / əˈmɛr.ɪ.kən rɒk breɪk / (BrE)
A rock-inhabiting fern native to northern North America, growing in dense tufts and having fronds that resemble parsley.
a fern native to northern North America

Examples

The shady hillside was covered in clusters of American rock brake.
Botanists identified the unusual fern as an American rock brake during their field study.
The delicate fronds of the American rock brake look remarkably similar to parsley.
