soft spot

1. NOUN

/ sɔft spɑt / (AmE), / sɒft spɒt / (BrE)
a membranous gap between the bones of an infant's or fetus's skull, also known as a fontanelle
membranous gap in infant's skull

Examples

The doctor gently examined the baby's soft spot to check for normal development.
You should be careful not to press too hard on the soft spot on a newborn's head.
The pediatrician explained that the soft spot will gradually close as the child grows.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fontanelle - the anatomical term for the soft spot in a baby's skull

2. NOUN

/ sɑft spɑt / (AmE), / sɒft spɒt / (BrE)
a strong feeling of affection or fondness for someone or something, often to a sentimental degree
a sentimental fondness for someone or something

Examples

He admits to having a soft spot for classic cars.
She always had a soft spot for stray animals and often took them in.
Despite his strictness, the teacher has a soft spot for students who try hard.

Synset Examples

she had a soft spot for her youngest son

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weakness - a particular fondness or taste for something, often seen as a flaw or indulgence

3. NOUN

/ sɔft spɑt / (AmE), / sɒft spɒt / (BrE)
a particular place or aspect where someone or something is most vulnerable or easily hurt
point of vulnerability

Examples

That old knee injury is still a soft spot for him.
Cybersecurity is a soft spot for many companies these days.
Her trust in people is her soft spot, which others sometimes take advantage of.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weakness - a weak point or disadvantage
  • Antonym: strong point - an aspect in which someone or something is especially strong or successful
