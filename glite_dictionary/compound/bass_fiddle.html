bass fiddle

1. NOUN

/ beɪs ˈfɪd.əl / (AmE), / beɪs ˈfɪd.əl / (BrE)
the largest and lowest-pitched instrument of the violin family, also known as the double bass or contrabass
large low-pitched string instrument

Examples

The orchestra featured a talented musician playing the bass fiddle in the back row.
He carried his bass fiddle carefully through the crowded backstage hallway.
She learned to play the bass fiddle to join her school's jazz band.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: double bass - the largest and lowest-pitched bowed string instrument
  • Degree Lower: violin - smallest and highest-pitched instrument in the violin family
  • Degree Lower: cello - the bass instrument of the violin family, higher than the double bass
