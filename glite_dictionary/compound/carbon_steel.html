carbon steel

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑrbən stiːl / (AmE), / ˈkɑːbən stiːl / (BrE)
A strong, hard steel whose main properties are determined by the amount of carbon it contains, commonly used for tools, knives, and building structures.
a type of steel with high carbon content

Examples

The chef prefers carbon steel knives because they are easy to sharpen and hold a fine edge.
Pipes made from carbon steel are commonly used in the construction industry.
Blacksmiths often work with carbon steel to create durable tools and blades.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: stainless steel - a steel alloy with added chromium for corrosion resistance
  • Degree Higher: alloy steel - steel combined with other elements for enhanced properties
