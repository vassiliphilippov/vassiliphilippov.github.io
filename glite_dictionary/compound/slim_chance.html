slim chance

1. NOUN

/ slɪm tʃæns / (AmE), / slɪm tʃɑːns / (BrE)
a very small possibility that something will happen or succeed
very small possibility

Examples

There is only a slim chance that the weather will improve before the weekend.
She knew her application had a slim chance of being accepted, but she applied anyway.
Despite his efforts, he realized he had just a slim chance to make it to the finals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: outside chance - a small possibility that something will happen
  • Antonym: good chance - a high probability or likelihood of something happening
