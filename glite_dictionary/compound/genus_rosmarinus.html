genus Rosmarinus

1. NOUN

/ ˈʤiː.nəs ˌroʊz.məˈraɪ.nəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌrɒz.məˈraɪ.nəs / (BrE)
A genus of aromatic evergreen shrubs in the mint family, formerly including rosemary.
botanical genus of rosemary plant

Examples

Many culinary herbs, such as genus Rosmarinus, have medicinal benefits as well.
The genus Rosmarinus was historically used to classify several aromatic shrubs in the mint family.
Botanists have reclassified species from genus Rosmarinus to the genus Salvia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rosemary - an aromatic evergreen shrub used as a culinary herb

Etymology

From Latin 'ros marinus' meaning 'dew of the sea'.
