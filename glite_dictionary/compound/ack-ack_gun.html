ack-ack gun

1. NOUN

/ ˈæk ˌæk ˌɡʌn / (AmE), / ˈæk ˌæk ˌɡʌn / (BrE)
A large gun or piece of artillery designed to shoot at enemy aircraft from the ground.
anti-aircraft artillery gun

Examples

The museum displays a restored ack-ack gun used during World War II.
Soldiers were trained to operate the ack-ack gun in defense of the airfield.
Enemy planes encountered heavy fire from the ack-ack gun batteries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anti-aircraft gun - a gun designed to attack aircraft from the ground

Etymology

From 'ack-ack', a World War I-era phonetic spelling of 'AA' (anti-aircraft).
