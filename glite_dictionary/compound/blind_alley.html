blind alley

1. NOUN

/ blaɪnd ˈæli / (AmE), / blaɪnd ˈæli / (BrE)
a street or passage closed at one end, having only one way in or out; a dead end
a street closed at one end; dead end

Examples

The children played at the end of the blind alley with no fear of traffic.
Living on a blind alley means you don’t get much through traffic.
When he saw the sign, he realized he had driven into a blind alley.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dead end - a street with no exit; passage closed at one end

2. NOUN

/ blaɪnd ˈæli / (AmE), / blaɪnd ˈæli / (BrE)
a course of action or line of thought that leads to no satisfactory conclusion or result; a dead end
unproductive course of action; dead end

Examples

After months of research, the scientists realized they had gone down a blind alley.
Negotiating with that company turned out to be a blind alley and wasted our resources.
Sometimes, pursuing every lead can lead you into a blind alley rather than closer to a solution.

Synset Examples

all the clues led the police into blind alleys
so far every road that we've been down has turned out to be a blind alley

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dead end - a situation or course with no possibility of progress

3. NOUN

/ blaɪnd ˈæli / (AmE), / blaɪnd ˈæli / (BrE)
a situation or course of action that leads nowhere or offers no possibility of progress or success
situation with no way forward

Examples

After months of investigation, the police realized they were pursuing a blind alley.
Negotiations with the committee turned out to be a blind alley and made no progress.
She warned that arguing further would only lead them into a blind alley.

Synset Examples

We realized this was a blind alley with no solutions.
We ended up in a blind alley during our search.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dead end - a road or situation that leads nowhere; no possibility of progress
