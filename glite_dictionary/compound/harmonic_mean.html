harmonic mean

1. NOUN

/ hɑrˈmɑnɪk min / (AmE), / hɑːˈmɒnɪk miːn / (BrE)
A type of average calculated as the reciprocal of the arithmetic mean of the reciprocals of a set of numbers.
statistical/mathematical type of average

Examples

To calculate the harmonic mean of two speeds, you must first take their reciprocals.
The harmonic mean is especially useful when averaging rates or ratios.
In this exam, you will be asked to compute the harmonic mean of several numbers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arithmetic mean - the usual average of a set of numbers

Etymology

From 'harmonic,' relating to harmony, and 'mean,' from Latin 'medianus' (middle).
