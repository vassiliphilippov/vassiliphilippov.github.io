family Plethodontidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli plɛθˌoʊdɒnˈtɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæməli pliːθˌɒdəntiːˈdiː / (BrE)
A family of small, mostly terrestrial New World salamanders that lack lungs and gills as adults.
family of lungless New World salamanders

Examples

Many species in the family Plethodontidae are found in the forests of North America.
Unlike other salamanders, members of the family Plethodontidae rely on their skin for respiration.
The family Plethodontidae is notable for its wide diversity and lungless adaptation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lungless salamander - salamanders in the family Plethodontidae, lacking lungs as adults
  • Degree Higher: salamander - an amphibian of the order Caudata, typically lizard-like in appearance

Etymology

From modern Latin Plethodontidae, based on Plethodon (a genus of salamanders) + -idae (zoological family suffix).
