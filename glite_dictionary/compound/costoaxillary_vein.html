costoaxillary vein

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːstoʊˈæksɪˌlɛri veɪn / (AmE), / ˌkɒstəʊˈæksɪləri veɪn / (BrE)
one of several veins that connect the intercostal veins with the lateral thoracic vein or the thoracoepigastric vein.
vein connecting intercostal and lateral thoracic/ thoracoepigastric veins
medical

Examples

The surgeon identified the costoaxillary vein during the dissection to prevent accidental injury.
Rare variants of the costoaxillary vein may be encountered in patients undergoing chest wall surgery.
Knowledge of the costoaxillary vein is important for radiologists interpreting thoracic imaging studies.

Etymology

From Latin 'costa' (rib) and 'axilla' (armpit), indicating location between rib and armpit area.
