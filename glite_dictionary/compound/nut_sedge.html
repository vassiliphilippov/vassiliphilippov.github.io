nut sedge

1. NOUN

/ nʌt sɛdʒ / (AmE), / nʌt sɛdʒ / (BrE)
a perennial sedge widely distributed in many regions, known for its small edible nutlike tubers
perennial sedge with edible nutlike tubers

Examples

The farmer struggled to control the nut sedge invading his vegetable garden.
In some cultures, the tubers of nut sedge are harvested and eaten as a delicacy.
The rapid spread of nut sedge makes it a troublesome weed in many fields.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yellow nutsedge - a common species of nut sedge (Cyperus esculentus)
  • Synonym: purple nutsedge - a related species of nut sedge (Cyperus rotundus)
