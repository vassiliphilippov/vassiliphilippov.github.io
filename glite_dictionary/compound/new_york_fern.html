New York fern

1. NOUN

/ nu ˈjɔrk fɜrn / (AmE), / njuː ˈjɔːk fɜːn / (BrE)
a slender fern native to moist woods of eastern North America, known for its tapering fronds; scientific name Thelypteris novaboracensis
a fern species native to eastern North America

Examples

We spotted a New York fern along the shady trail in upstate New York.
The New York fern is known for its fronds that taper at both ends.
Gardeners often choose New York fern for its ability to thrive in moist, shady environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shield fern - common name for several ferns in the family Dryopteridaceae
