one-way light time

1. NOUN

/ ˌwʌnˈweɪ ˈlaɪt taɪm / (AmE), / ˌwʌnˈweɪ ˈlaɪt taɪm / (BrE)
the time required for light or a signal to travel from Earth to a celestial object, measured in one direction only
time for light to travel from Earth to a celestial object

Examples

Scientists calculate the one-way light time to determine when signals from distant spacecraft will reach Earth.
Data from Mars is delayed by the one-way light time of several minutes.
Understanding one-way light time is essential for deep-space mission planning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: light-time - the time taken for light to travel a given distance
