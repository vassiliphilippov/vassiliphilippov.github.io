squash rackets

1. NOUN

/ skwɑʃ ˈræk.ɪts / (AmE), / skwɒʃ ˈræk.ɪts / (BrE)
A game played in an enclosed court by two or four players who strike a small rubber ball with long-handled rackets against the walls.
indoor court game using rackets and a ball

Examples

He has been playing squash rackets competitively since college.
The health club has several courts for squash rackets available to members.
They arranged a squash rackets match after work every Friday.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: squash - alternative name for the same sport

Etymology

From 'squash', referring to the soft, squishable ball, and 'rackets', the equipment used to strike the ball.
