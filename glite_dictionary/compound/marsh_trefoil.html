marsh trefoil

1. NOUN

/ mɑrʃ ˈtri.fɔɪl / (AmE), / mɑːʃ ˈtriː.fɔɪl / (BrE)
a perennial plant native to Europe and America, with racemes of white or purplish flowers and intensely bitter, trifoliate leaves, often found rooting at the margins of water and spreading across surfaces
a perennial aquatic plant with trifoliate leaves

Examples

The pond was ringed with marsh trefoil, its pale flowers reflected in the still water.
Botanists noted the spread of marsh trefoil in the wetland reserve.
He avoided the bitter taste of marsh trefoil leaves while foraging along the lake's edge.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: buckbean - another name for marsh trefoil, a perennial aquatic plant
