Scots heather

1. NOUN

/ skɑts ˈhɛð.ɚ / (AmE), / skɒts ˈhɛð.ə / (BrE)
A low, evergreen shrub (Calluna vulgaris) with small purple or pink flowers, widely found in the Northern Hemisphere, especially in Scotland.
evergreen shrub with purple or pink flowers

Examples

The hills were covered in Scots heather, creating a stunning purple landscape.
Wildlife thrives in the dense Scots heather of the moors.
She picked some Scots heather to decorate the cottage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heather - a low-growing shrub with small pink or purple flowers, especially Calluna vulgaris
