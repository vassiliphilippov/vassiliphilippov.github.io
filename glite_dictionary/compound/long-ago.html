long-ago

1. ADJ

/ lɔŋ əˈgoʊ / (AmE), / lɒŋ əˈgəʊ / (BrE)
belonging to a time far in the past; occurring or existing a long time ago
from a time far in the past

Examples

She often reminisced about her long-ago childhood adventures in the countryside.
In a long-ago era, knights and castles dominated the landscape.
The long-ago quarrel still influenced their families' relationship today.

Synset Examples

those long-ago dresses that swished along the floor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ancient - belonging to the very distant past
