British Empire

1. PROPN

/ ˈbrɪt̬.ɪʃ ˈɛm.paɪr / (AmE), / ˈbrɪt.ɪʃ ˈɛm.paɪə / (BrE)
the former empire consisting of Great Britain and its dominions, colonies, protectorates, and other territories that reached its peak in the early 20th century
former empire of Great Britain

Examples

At its height, the British Empire controlled nearly a quarter of the world's landmass.
The legacy of the British Empire can still be seen in countries like India, Canada, and Australia.
Historians debate the many impacts, both positive and negative, of the British Empire on global history.
