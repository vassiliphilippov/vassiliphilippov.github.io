Sixteen Personality Factor Questionnaire

1. NOUN

/ sɪkˈstiːn pɚˌsɒnˈælɪti ˈfæktɚ ˌkwɛstʃəˈnɛr / (AmE), / sɪkˈstiːn pɜːˌsɒnˈælɪti ˈfæktə ˌkwɛstʃəˈneə / (BrE)
a standardized self-report personality test developed by Raymond B. Cattell to assess 16 primary personality traits using factor analysis
psychometric test for 16 personality traits

Examples

The Sixteen Personality Factor Questionnaire is frequently used in psychological research to assess individual differences.
She completed the Sixteen Personality Factor Questionnaire before her first appointment with the therapist.
Many employers use the Sixteen Personality Factor Questionnaire as part of their recruitment process to better understand applicants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: personality test - a test designed to measure aspects of a person's character

Etymology

Named for its 16 measured personality factors; questionnaire designed by psychologist Raymond B. Cattell.
