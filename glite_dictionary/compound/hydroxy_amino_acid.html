hydroxy amino acid

1. NOUN

/ haɪˈdrɑksi əˈminoʊ ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / haɪˈdrɒksi əˈmiːnəʊ ˈæsɪd / (BrE)
an amino acid containing a hydroxy group in its side chain, such as serine or threonine
an amino acid with a hydroxy group
medical

Examples

Serine is a hydroxy amino acid found in many proteins.
Threonine is another example of a hydroxy amino acid common in metabolism.
Researchers studied the reactions involving hydroxy amino acids in enzyme activity.

Synset Examples

The hydroxy group appears on the second carbon atom of the hydroxy amino acids serine and threonine.

Etymology

From hydroxy-, meaning 'containing an –OH group', and amino acid.
