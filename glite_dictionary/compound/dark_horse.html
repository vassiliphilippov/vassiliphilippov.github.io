dark horse

1. NOUN

/ dɑrk hɔrs / (AmE), / dɑːk hɔːs / (BrE)
A competitor, especially in a race or contest, about whom little is known and who is therefore unexpected to win but may surprise by succeeding.
unexpected or little-known competitor

Examples

No one expected the dark horse to win the championship, but she surprised everyone with her performance.
He was considered a dark horse in the election because voters knew little about his background.
Sometimes a dark horse emerges and takes the prize against all odds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: underdog - a competitor thought to have little chance of winning
