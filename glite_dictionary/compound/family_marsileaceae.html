family Marsileaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ˌmɑrˌsɪliːˈeɪsiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli ˌmɑːsɪliːˈeɪsiː / (BrE)
a family of aquatic and semi-aquatic ferns, commonly called the pepperwort or water-clover family
family of aquatic and semi-aquatic ferns

Examples

The family Marsileaceae includes several species found in wetlands across the world.
Botanists often study the unique reproductive structures of the family Marsileaceae.
Plants belonging to the family Marsileaceae are commonly referred to as water clovers.

Etymology

From the genus name Marsilea, after the Italian naturalist Luigi Ferdinando Marsili (1658–1730), plus the standard botanical family suffix -aceae.
