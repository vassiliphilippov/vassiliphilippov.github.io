tube-nosed bat

1. NOUN

/ tuːb noʊzd bæt / (AmE), / tjuːb nəʊzd bæt / (BrE)
A fruit bat of the genus Nyctimene, distinguished by nostrils that are elongated into diverging tubes.
fruit bat with tubular nostrils

Examples

The tube-nosed bat is easily recognized by its unusual nostrils, which protrude like small tubes from its snout.
Researchers in Papua New Guinea discovered a new species of tube-nosed bat during their expedition.
Unlike many bats, the tube-nosed bat feeds primarily on fruit and is important for pollination.
