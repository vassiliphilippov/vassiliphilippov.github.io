calico crab

1. NOUN

/ ˈkælɪˌkoʊ kræb / (AmE), / ˈkælɪkəʊ kræb / (BrE)
a brightly spotted crab found on sandy beaches along the Atlantic coast of the United States, known for its mottled shell
spotted crab of Atlantic US beaches

Examples

Walking along the shore, we found a calico crab burrowing in the sand near the waterline.
The calico crab is known for its distinctive, colorful spots on its shell.
Children watched as a calico crab scuttled quickly across the beach.

Etymology

From 'calico', referring to the spotted pattern on the shell, likened to calico fabric.
