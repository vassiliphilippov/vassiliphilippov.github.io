suborder Tyranni

1. NOUN

/ sʌbˌɔrdər tɪˈræn.aɪ / (AmE), / sʌbˌɔːdə tɪˈran.ɪ / (BrE)
A major suborder of passerine birds, including New World flycatchers, antbirds, ovenbirds, and related families.
a suborder of passerine birds

Examples

The suborder Tyranni includes many species unique to Central and South America.
Researchers are studying the evolution of song within the suborder Tyranni.
Many rainforest birds belong to the suborder Tyranni, displaying a remarkable diversity.

Etymology

From Latin 'tyrannus' (tyrant), used in taxonomy for this bird group.
