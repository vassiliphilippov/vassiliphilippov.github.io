reserve account

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈzɜrv əˈkaʊnt / (AmE), / rɪˈzɜːv əˈkaʊnt / (BrE)
an account in which funds are set aside from earnings to cover anticipated future payments or liabilities
account for future financial obligations

Examples

The company maintains a reserve account to cover warranty claims on its products.
Banks are required by law to keep a portion of their assets in a reserve account.
An auditor will review the reserve account balance to ensure it meets regulatory requirements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: provision - an amount set aside to cover future liabilities
