Inbus key

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪnbʌs kiː / (AmE), / ˈɪnbʌs kiː / (BrE)
A type of wrench with a hexagonal cross-section, designed for turning Allen screws.
hex wrench for Allen screws

Examples

I searched my toolbox for an Inbus key to assemble the furniture.
The instruction manual suggests using an Inbus key to tighten the screws.
He accidentally stripped the bolt by using the wrong size Inbus key.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Allen key - a wrench for Allen screws; synonym of Inbus key

Etymology

From German 'INBUS,' an acronym for 'Innensechskantschraube Bauer und Schaurte' (internal hexagon socket screw from Bauer and Schaurte, a manufacturer).
