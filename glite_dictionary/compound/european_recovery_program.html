European Recovery Program

1. NOUN

/ jʊˌroʊpiən rɪˈkʌvəri ˈproʊɡræm / (AmE), / jʊˌrəʊpiən rɪˈkʌvəri ˈprəʊɡram / (BrE)
A United States program (1948–1952) providing economic aid for the reconstruction of Europe after World War II; also known as the Marshall Plan.
US economic aid plan for postwar Europe

Examples

The European Recovery Program was crucial in rebuilding the economies of Western Europe after the war.
Historians often cite the European Recovery Program as a turning point in postwar international relations.
Many countries benefited from the funds provided by the European Recovery Program between 1948 and 1952.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Marshall Plan - United States initiative to aid Western Europe's economic recovery after World War II
