naval forces

1. NOUN

/ ˈneɪ.vəl ˈfɔr.sɪz / (AmE), / ˈneɪ.vəl ˈfɔː.sɪz / (BrE)
the military ships and supporting units of a country, organized for sea warfare
a country's organized warships and naval units

Examples

The country's naval forces were deployed to protect its coastline during the crisis.
Modern naval forces include aircraft carriers, submarines, and destroyers.
International exercises allowed the allied naval forces to practice coordination and communication.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: navy - the branch of a country's armed forces that conducts military operations at sea
