black art

1. NOUN

/ blæk ɑrt / (AmE), / blæk ɑːt / (BrE)
the use of magic, especially that which is believed to involve the invocation of evil spirits or supernatural powers for malevolent purposes
use of magic or occult power

Examples

Many people in the village feared her, believing she practiced the black art.
The movie portrays the dangers of dabbling in the black art and summoning dark forces.
Historians have debated whether the accused really engaged in the black art or were victims of superstition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: black magic - the use of supernatural powers for evil or selfish purposes
  • Antonym: white magic - magic used for benevolent purposes

2. NOUN

/ blæk ɑrt / (AmE), / blæk ɑːt / (BrE)
a skill that is mysterious or difficult to master, often requiring special knowledge or expertise
skill that is difficult to master

Examples

For many, accounting is a black art understood only by professionals.
He viewed the creation of soufflés as a black art that takes years to learn.
Some consider investing in the stock market to be a black art full of hidden complexities.

Synset Examples

Cooking is a black art that few can master.
He considers negotiation a black art.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mystery - something that is difficult to understand or explain
  • Synonym: craft - a skill, especially one requiring special skill or knowledge

Etymology

Originally referred to the practice of magic or sorcery, later used figuratively for difficult or arcane skills.
