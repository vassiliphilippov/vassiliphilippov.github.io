ABO system

1. NOUN

/ ˌeɪ.biːˈoʊ ˈsɪstəm / (AmE), / ˌeɪ.biːˈəʊ ˈsɪstəm / (BrE)
a system for classifying human blood based on the presence or absence of A and B antigens, resulting in four main blood groups: A, B, AB, and O
blood group classification system
medical

Examples

Doctors must always consider the ABO system when transfusing blood to patients.
The ABO system divides human blood into four main groups based on antigens.
Incompatible matches in the ABO system can lead to dangerous transfusion reactions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blood group - a category of blood based on antigens and antibodies

Etymology

Named after the A, B, and O blood groups first identified in early 20th-century medicine.
