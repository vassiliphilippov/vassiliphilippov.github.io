prison cell

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɪzən sɛl / (AmE), / ˈprɪzən sɛl / (BrE)
a small room in a prison where a prisoner is kept
room where prisoners are held

Examples

The guards checked each prison cell before turning off the lights for the night.
He spent three months in a prison cell awaiting his trial.
The prison cell was cold, bare, and barely large enough for a single bed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cell - a small room, especially in a prison or monastery
