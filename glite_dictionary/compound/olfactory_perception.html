olfactory perception

1. NOUN

/ ɑlˈfæktəri pɚˈsɛpʃən / (AmE), / ɒlˈfæktəri pəˈsɛpʃən / (BrE)
the process or sensation of detecting and recognizing odors through the stimulation of smell receptors in the nose
detection or experience of smells

Examples

Our olfactory perception can distinguish thousands of different scents in the environment.
Damage to the nasal cavity can impair olfactory perception and reduce the ability to enjoy food.
The scientist studied how aging affects olfactory perception in mammals.

Synset Examples

she loved the smell of roses

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smell - the sense or sensation of odors
  • Synonym: taste - the sense that detects flavor, closely related to smell

Etymology

From Latin olfactus (smell) + perception, from Latin percipere (to seize, understand).
