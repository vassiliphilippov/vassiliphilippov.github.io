short-staple cotton

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃɔrt ˌsteɪpəl ˈkɑtən / (AmE), / ˈʃɔːt ˌsteɪpəl ˈkɒtən / (BrE)
Cotton that has relatively short fibers, typically considered lower in quality and used for coarser textiles.
cotton with short fibers

Examples

During the 19th century, short-staple cotton became the backbone of the Southern United States' economy.
Even though short-staple cotton is easier to grow in some climates, it produces lower quality textiles than long-staple varieties.
The new processing machines made it possible to efficiently separate seeds from short-staple cotton.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: long-staple cotton - cotton with relatively long fibers used for finer textiles
