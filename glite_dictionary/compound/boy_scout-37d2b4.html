boy scout

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɔɪ skaʊt / (AmE), / ˈbɔɪ skaʊt / (BrE)
A man who is regarded as naive or overly innocent, especially in matters of experience or the ways of the world.
a naive or overly innocent man

Examples

He acted like such a boy scout that he didn't see her deception coming.
Don't be a boy scout; not everyone follows the rules in this business.
His boy scout attitude made him an easy target for more cunning negotiators.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: innocent - a naive or inexperienced person
  • Synonym: naive - lacking experience, wisdom, or judgment

Etymology

From the organization 'Boy Scouts', originally referring to boys in the Scouting movement known for their innocence, helpfulness, and adherence to rules.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɔɪ ˌskaʊt / (AmE), / ˈbɔɪ skaʊt / (BrE)
A member of a youth organization dedicated to developing good citizenship, practical skills, and personal responsibility, known for being helpful and prepared.
member of youth organization (Scouts)

Examples

The boy scout earned a badge for helping his community.
After joining as a boy scout, he quickly learned first aid and camping skills.
She thanked the boy scout for guiding her through the unfamiliar trail.

Synset Examples

The boy scout helped an elderly person cross the street.
He was a boy scout, always prepared for anything.
Her brother was a boy scout, earning badges every year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Girl Scout - a girl member of the corresponding youth organization
  • Synonym: Scout - a member of the Scouts organization, regardless of gender

Etymology

From Boy Scouts, the international youth movement founded in 1907 by Robert Baden-Powell.
