ampere-hour

1. NOUN

/ ˈæmˌpɪr ɑːr / (AmE), / ˈæmpɪə hɑː / (BrE)
A unit of electric charge equal to the amount transferred by a steady current of one ampere flowing for one hour (equivalent to 3600 coulombs).
unit of electric charge (3600 coulombs)

Examples

The battery has a capacity of 100 ampere-hours, allowing it to run for several days.
A typical car battery is rated at 48 ampere-hours under standard conditions.
When choosing a power source, it is important to note the ampere-hour rating.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coulomb - SI unit of electric charge

Etymology

Formed from 'ampere' (unit of electric current, after André-Marie Ampère) and 'hour'.
