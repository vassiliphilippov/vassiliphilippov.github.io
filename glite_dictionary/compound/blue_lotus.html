blue lotus

1. NOUN

/ blu ˈloʊ.tɪs / (AmE), / bluː ˈləʊ.tɪs / (BrE)
an aquatic plant, Nymphaea caerulea, with bluish flowers, held sacred by ancient Egyptians and often depicted in their art and rituals.
aquatic plant sacred to ancient Egyptians

Examples

The ancient Egyptians revered the blue lotus for its beauty and symbolic significance.
A pond in the garden was filled with blooming blue lotus flowers.
Researchers are studying the medicinal properties of the blue lotus species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Egyptian lotus - an aquatic plant sacred in Egyptian culture

2. NOUN

/ blu ˈloʊ.təs / (AmE), / blu ˈləʊ.təs / (BrE)
A water lily (Nymphaea caerulea or Nymphaea nouchali) native to India and southeastern Asia, noted for its blue flowers and significance in art and religion.
a blue-flowered water lily of Asia

Examples

The ancient Egyptians often depicted the blue lotus in their art and religious texts.
She admired the blue lotus blooming on the surface of the pond.
Extracts from the blue lotus have been used in traditional medicine for centuries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: water lily - an aquatic plant with broad floating leaves and large flowers
