trichopterous insect

1. NOUN

/ traɪˈkɑptərəs ˈɪnˌsɛkt / (AmE), / trɪˈkɒptərəz ˈɪn.sɛkt / (BrE)
An insect of the order Trichoptera, commonly known as a caddis fly, characterized by hairy wings and aquatic larvae.
insect of the order Trichoptera (caddis fly)

Examples

Scientists collected several trichopterous insects near the riverbank to analyze water quality.
The larvae of the trichopterous insect construct protective cases from sand and plant material.
A trichopterous insect emerged from the stream, its hairy wings catching the sunlight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caddis fly - insect of the order Trichoptera

Etymology

From Greek tricho- (hair) and pteron (wing), referring to the hairy wings of these insects.
