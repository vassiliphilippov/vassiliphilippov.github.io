Indian python

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪndiən ˈpaɪθən / (AmE), / ˈɪndiən ˈpʌɪθən / (BrE)
a very large nonvenomous python native to Southeast Asia, known for its considerable size
large python native to Southeast Asia

Examples

The Indian python can grow up to six meters in length, making it one of the world's largest snakes.
Conservationists are working to protect the habitat of the Indian python in the wild.
I saw an Indian python at the reptile house during our trip to the zoo.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: python - a large, nonvenomous constrictor snake
