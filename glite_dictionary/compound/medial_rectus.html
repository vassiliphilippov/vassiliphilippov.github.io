medial rectus

1. NOUN

/ ˈmidiəl ˈrɛktəs / (AmE), / ˈmiːdiəl ˈrɛktəs / (BrE)
the eye muscle that, when contracted, moves the eyeball toward the nose (medially)
eye muscle turning eyeball medially
medical

Examples

The medial rectus muscle is responsible for moving the eye inward toward the nose.
Damage to the medial rectus can cause problems with focusing on nearby objects.
During the eye exam, the doctor checked the strength of her medial rectus muscles.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lateral rectus - the eye muscle that turns the eyeball outward (laterally)

Etymology

From Latin 'medialis' (middle) and 'rectus' (straight), referring to its position and shape.
