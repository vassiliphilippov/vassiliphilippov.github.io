Norman Conquest

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɔrmən ˈkɑŋkwəst / (AmE), / ˈnɔːmən ˈkɒŋkwəst / (BrE)
the invasion and subsequent settlement of England by the Normans after the Battle of Hastings in 1066
the Norman invasion of England in 1066

Examples

The Norman Conquest dramatically transformed English society, language, and governance.
Many castles throughout England were built soon after the Norman Conquest.
Historians often debate the long-term effects of the Norman Conquest on English law and culture.

Etymology

From 'Norman', referring to the Normans of Normandy, France, and 'conquest', from Old French 'conqueste'.
