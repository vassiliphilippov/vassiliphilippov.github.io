Wake Island

1. PROPN

/ weɪk ˈaɪ.lənd / (AmE), / weɪk ˈaɪ.lənd / (BrE)
a small island in the western Pacific Ocean, located between Guam and Hawaii, administered by the United States
island in the western Pacific Ocean

Examples

The U.S. maintains a military base on Wake Island for strategic purposes.
During World War II, fierce battles were fought over Wake Island.
Flights over the Pacific often use Wake Island as a refueling stop.

Etymology

Named after Captain William Wake, who is believed to have discovered the island in 1796.
