bishop's cap

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɪʃəps kæp / (AmE), / ˈbɪʃəps kap / (BrE)
any of several perennial herbs of the genus Mitella, having a capsule shaped like a bishop's miter
a perennial herb of the genus Mitella

Examples

The woodland was dotted with delicate bishop's cap plants blooming in the shade.
We identified several wildflowers, including a cluster of bishop's cap near the stream.
Gardeners often enjoy the subtle beauty that bishop's cap brings to shaded borders.

Etymology

Named for the resemblance of the seed capsule to a bishop's miter (a ceremonial hat).
