saddled-shaped false morel

1. NOUN

/ ˈsædld ˌʃeɪpt fɔːls məˈrɛl / (AmE), / ˈsædld ˌʃeɪpt fɔːls məˈrɛl / (BrE)
a poisonous fungus with a saddle-shaped, dull yellow to brown cap, whose fertile part is relatively even
a poisonous fungus with a saddle-shaped cap

Examples

The hiker was warned not to pick the saddled-shaped false morel because it is highly toxic.
Experts can distinguish a saddled-shaped false morel from edible mushrooms by its uneven yet saddle-shaped cap.
Consuming a saddled-shaped false morel can lead to severe poisoning and should be strictly avoided.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: false morel - a group of poisonous fungi resembling true morels
  • Antonym: morel - an edible fungus with a honeycomb appearance
