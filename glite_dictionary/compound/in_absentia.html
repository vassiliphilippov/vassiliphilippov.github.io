in absentia

1. ADV

/ ɪn æbˈsɛnʃə / (AmE), / ɪn abˈsɛnʃɪə / (BrE)
while not present; in the absence of the person concerned, often used especially when a decision or sentence is passed without their presence.
while not present; in one's absence

Examples

The defendant was convicted in absentia after failing to appear in court.
The university awarded her degree in absentia since she could not attend the ceremony.
He was sentenced in absentia because he had fled the country.

Synset Examples

he was sentenced in absentia

Etymology

From Latin, literally 'in absence.'
