scorpaenoid fish

1. NOUN

/ skɔrˈpiː.nɔɪd fɪʃ / (AmE), / skɔːˈpiː.nɔɪd fɪʃ / (BrE)
any of various fishes characterized by a head armored with bony plates, typically belonging to the family Scorpaenidae or related groups
fish with a bony-armored head

Examples

The scorpaenoid fish is well known for its venomous spines and armored head.
Many marine biologists study scorpaenoid fish to understand their unique defensive adaptations.
The diver encountered a brightly colored scorpaenoid fish near the coral reef.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scorpaenid - any fish of the family Scorpaenidae, related to scorpaenoid fishes

Etymology

From modern Latin Scorpaenidae, derived from Greek 'skorpaina' (a kind of fish), with the suffix '-oid' meaning 'resembling'.
