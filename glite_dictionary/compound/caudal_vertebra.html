caudal vertebra

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔdəl vɚˈtɛbrə / (AmE), / ˈkɔːdəl ˈvɜːtɪbrə / (BrE)
any of the vertebrae at the lower end of the vertebral column, especially one of four vertebrae forming the human coccyx.
vertebra in the tail or coccyx
medical

Examples

The human coccyx consists of four fused caudal vertebrae.
During evolution, the number of caudal vertebrae in humans was greatly reduced.
In some animals, injuries to the caudal vertebrae can affect tail movement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coccyx - the small, triangular bone at the base of the spinal column, formed of fused caudal vertebrae in humans

Etymology

From Latin caudalis (pertaining to the tail) + vertebra (joint or bone of the spine).
