external respiration

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˈstɜrnəl ˌrɛspəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪkˈstɜːnəl ˌrɛspɪˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the process by which oxygen is taken into the body from inhaled air and carbon dioxide is expelled through exhalation, typically occurring in the lungs
the exchange of gases between air and the lungs
medical

Examples

In medical school, students learn the mechanics of external respiration and its importance for survival.
Diseases like emphysema can impair external respiration by damaging the lungs.
Proper external respiration is crucial for maintaining blood oxygen levels.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: internal respiration - the exchange of gases between the blood and body tissues

Etymology

From Latin 'externus' (outside) and 'respiratio' (breathing).
