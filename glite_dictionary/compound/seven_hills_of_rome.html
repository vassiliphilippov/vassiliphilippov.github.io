Seven Hills of Rome

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛvən hɪlz əv roʊm / (AmE), / ˈsɛv.ən hɪlz əv rəʊm / (BrE)
the seven hills on which the ancient city of Rome was built: Aventine, Caelian, Capitoline, Esquiline, Palatine, Quirinal, and Viminal
the hills forming the foundation of ancient Rome

Examples

Tourists often visit the Seven Hills of Rome to explore the origins of the ancient city.
The legend of Romulus and Remus is closely tied to the Seven Hills of Rome.
Modern Rome still recognizes the historic significance of the Seven Hills of Rome.
