suction lipectomy

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌkʃən laɪˈpɛktəmi / (AmE), / ˈsʌkʃən lɪˈpɛktəmi / (BrE)
A medical procedure in which excess fatty tissue is removed from under the skin by suction, usually for cosmetic purposes.
removal of fat by suction (cosmetic surgery)
medical

Examples

The surgeon recommended a suction lipectomy to remove stubborn fat deposits from her abdomen.
After undergoing suction lipectomy, patients are advised to follow a healthy diet and exercise.
Medical professionals warn that suction lipectomy carries risks and should only be considered after other weight loss methods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: liposuction - Cosmetic surgery to remove fat via suction

Etymology

Formed from 'suction' (from Latin 'suctio', act of sucking) and 'lipectomy' (from Greek 'lipos' meaning fat and 'ektomē' meaning excision).
