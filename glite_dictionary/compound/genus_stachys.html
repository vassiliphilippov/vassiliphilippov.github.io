genus Stachys

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈstæ.kɪs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈstaː.kɪs / (BrE)
a large genus of usually woolly or hairy herbs, subshrubs, or shrubs native to the temperate eastern hemisphere and tropical Australasia
a genus of woolly or hairy herbs and shrubs

Examples

The genus Stachys includes a variety of woolly-leaved herbs found in temperate regions.
Botanists classify wood betony and lamb's ear under the genus Stachys.
Many species in the genus Stachys are cultivated as ornamental plants for their distinctive foliage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Stachys - type genus of the mint family Lamiaceae

Etymology

From Greek stakhys (ear of grain), the type genus name in botanical Latin.
