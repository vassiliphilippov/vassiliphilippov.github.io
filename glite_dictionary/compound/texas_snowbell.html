Texas snowbell

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛk.səs ˈsnoʊˌbɛl / (AmE), / ˈtɛk.səs ˈsnəʊˌbɛl / (BrE)
a rare, threatened shrub or small tree (Styrax platanifolius var. texanus) native to the southwestern United States, with white bell-shaped flowers
rare, threatened flowering shrub of Texas

Examples

The Texas snowbell is considered endangered due to habitat loss.
Botanists are working to protect remaining populations of the Texas snowbell in the wild.
The white flowers of the Texas snowbell bloom in early spring along riverbanks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snowbell - a shrub or small tree with bell-shaped white flowers
