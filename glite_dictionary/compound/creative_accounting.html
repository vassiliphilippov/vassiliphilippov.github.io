creative accounting

1. NOUN

/ kriˈeɪtɪv əˈkaʊntɪŋ / (AmE), / kriˈeɪtɪv əˈkaʊntɪŋ / (BrE)
the use of accounting methods that are technically legal but intended to present a falsely favorable view of a company's financial position
manipulative or deceptive accounting practices

Examples

Many scandals were uncovered that involved creative accounting to boost reported profits.
Auditors are trained to recognize signs of creative accounting during financial reviews.
The government introduced new laws to crack down on creative accounting in large corporations.

Synset Examples

Investors were wary of the firm's creative accounting tactics.
The company used creative accounting to hide its debts.
He got in trouble for his creative accounting practices.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: fraudulent accounting - illegal falsification of financial records
  • Degree Lower: accounting - the process of keeping financial accounts
