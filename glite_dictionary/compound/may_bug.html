May bug

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪ bʌɡ / (AmE), / ˈmeɪ bʌɡ / (BrE)
A large, usually brown beetle found in North America, common in late spring, whose larvae feed on the roots of grasses and other plants.
large North American spring beetle

Examples

Every spring, May bugs swarm around the garden lights in the evening.
The larvae of the May bug can damage lawns by feeding on grass roots.
A single May bug flew into the room through the open window.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cockchafer - another common name for the same or similar beetle species

2. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪ bʌɡ / (AmE), / ˈmeɪ bʌɡ / (BrE)
A large European beetle, especially the cockchafer, that is destructive to vegetation as both larva and adult.
large European beetle harmful to plants

Examples

Many gardens suffer damage in spring when May bugs emerge and feed on young plants.
Children used to collect May bugs in jars during the early summer months.
The larvae of the May bug can destroy the roots of crops if left uncontrolled.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cockchafer - another name for the same beetle species, Melolontha melolontha

Etymology

Named for its tendency to appear in May; 'bug' from Middle English 'bugge' (insect).
