Cape hunting dog

1. NOUN

/ keɪp ˈhʌntɪŋ dɔg / (AmE), / keɪp ˈhʌntɪŋ dɒg / (BrE)
A large, social wild canine (Lycaon pictus) of southern and eastern Africa, known for hunting in packs and now rare in inhabited areas.
African wild hunting dog species

Examples

The Cape hunting dog is highly endangered due to habitat loss and human conflict.
Researchers observed a pack of Cape hunting dogs coordinating to catch an impala.
Unlike other canines, the Cape hunting dog communicates with a distinctive series of vocalizations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: African wild dog - large, social wild canine native to Africa

Etymology

From 'Cape' (referring to the Cape of Good Hope in South Africa) and 'hunting dog'.
