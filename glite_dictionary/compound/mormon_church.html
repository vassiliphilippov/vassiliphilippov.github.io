Mormon Church

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɔrmən tʃɝːtʃ / (AmE), / ˈmɔːmən tʃɜːtʃ / (BrE)
the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints, a Christian denomination founded by Joseph Smith in 1830, headquartered in Salt Lake City, Utah
Christian denomination founded by Joseph Smith

Examples

The Mormon Church has its world headquarters in Salt Lake City, Utah.
Many families in the area are active members of the Mormon Church.
The Mormon Church was founded in the early 19th century by Joseph Smith.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Latter-day Saints - members of the Mormon Church

Etymology

The term 'Mormon' comes from the Book of Mormon, a sacred text in the church.
