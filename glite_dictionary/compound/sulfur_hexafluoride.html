sulfur hexafluoride

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌlfɚ ˌhɛksəˈfluːraɪd / (AmE), / ˈsʌlfə ˌhɛksəˈflʊəraɪd / (BrE)
a colorless, odorless, non-flammable gas (SF6) used mainly as an electrical insulator and a potent greenhouse gas
a colorless insulating gas (SF6)

Examples

The electrical industry relies on sulfur hexafluoride for its superior insulating properties.
Because sulfur hexafluoride is a strong greenhouse gas, its use is carefully regulated.
Researchers are developing alternatives to sulfur hexafluoride to reduce environmental impact.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: greenhouse gas - a gas that traps heat in the atmosphere and contributes to global warming

Etymology

From sulfur and hexafluoride, indicating six fluorine atoms bonded to sulfur.
