moving-coil galvanometer

1. NOUN

/ ˈmuːvɪŋ ˌkɔɪl ˌɡælvəˈnɑːmɪtər / (AmE), / ˈmuːvɪŋ ˌkɔɪl ˌɡælvəˈnɒmɪtə / (BrE)
A galvanometer in which a magnetic field causes a movable coil carrying electric current to deflect, allowing measurement of electrical current.
instrument measuring electric current via a moving coil
medical

Examples

The moving-coil galvanometer is known for its high sensitivity and accuracy in measuring tiny electric currents.
Before digital meters, a moving-coil galvanometer was often used in laboratory experiments to detect electrical flow.
To calibrate the instrument, the physicist checked the readings on the moving-coil galvanometer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: galvanometer - instrument for detecting and measuring electric current

Etymology

From 'galvanometer' (from Luigi Galvani, Italian physicist) and 'moving-coil' specifying the mechanism.
