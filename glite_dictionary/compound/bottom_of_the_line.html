bottom of the line

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑt̬əm əv ðə laɪn / (AmE), / ˈbɒtəm əv ðə laɪn / (BrE)
the lowest quality or least expensive item in a range of products
lowest quality or cheapest option

Examples

We couldn't afford the latest gadget, so we settled for the bottom of the line model.
Even the bottom of the line computer can handle basic tasks efficiently.
He's not flashy; he always buys the bottom of the line version to save money.

Synset Examples

That model is the bottom of the line in their series.
It’s the bottom of the line product, but it works.
I bought the bottom of the line phone for emergencies.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: top of the line - the highest quality or most expensive item in a product range
