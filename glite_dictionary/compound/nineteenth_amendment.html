Nineteenth Amendment

1. NOUN

/ ˌnaɪnˈtiːnθ əˈmɛndmənt / (AmE), / ˌnaɪnˈtiːnθ əˈmɛndmənt / (BrE)
an amendment to the U.S. Constitution, adopted in 1920, that guarantees women the right to vote by prohibiting states from denying suffrage on the basis of sex
U.S. constitutional amendment granting women's suffrage
legal american

Examples

The Nineteenth Amendment marked a significant victory for the women's suffrage movement in the United States.
She wrote her thesis on the impact of the Nineteenth Amendment on American politics.
Many activists campaigned tirelessly for the ratification of the Nineteenth Amendment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suffrage - the right to vote in political elections
  • Degree Lower: Fifteenth Amendment - U.S. constitutional amendment granting African American men the right to vote

Etymology

Refers to the nineteenth amendment made to the U.S. Constitution, from the ordinal number nineteenth and amendment.
