manbashing rhetoric

1. NOUN

/ ˈmænˌbæʃɪŋ ˈrɛtərɪk / (AmE), / ˈmænˌbæʃɪŋ ˈrɛtərɪk / (BrE)
language or expressions that are hostile or disparaging towards men
hostile language against men

Examples

Some critics accused the politician of using manbashing rhetoric during the debate.
The article was filled with manbashing rhetoric that many readers found offensive.
He objected strongly to what he saw as manbashing rhetoric in the television series.

Synset Examples

They criticized her for her manbashing rhetoric.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: misandry - dislike of, contempt for, or ingrained prejudice against men
  • Synonym: man-hating - showing intense dislike or hostility toward men

Etymology

From 'manbashing' (man + bashing, meaning attacking men verbally) and 'rhetoric' (from Greek rhētorikē, the art of rhetoric).
