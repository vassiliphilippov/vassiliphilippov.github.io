blastodermic vesicle

1. NOUN

/ ˌblæstəˌdɜrmɪk ˈvɛsɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌblæstəˌdɜːmɪk ˈvɛsɪkl̩ / (BrE)
the early stage of mammalian embryo development, corresponding to the blastula, in which some cell differentiation has occurred
early stage of mammalian embryo development
medical

Examples

The blastodermic vesicle forms during the early stages of mammalian embryonic development.
Researchers observed cellular differentiation within the blastodermic vesicle under the microscope.
Proper implantation depends on the healthy formation of the blastodermic vesicle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blastula - an early stage of embryonic development in animals, typically a hollow ball of cells

Etymology

From 'blastoderm' (Greek blastos meaning 'germ, sprout' + derma meaning 'skin') and Latin vesicula (diminutive of vesica, meaning 'bladder, vesicle').
