POW camp

1. NOUN

/ pi oʊ ˈdʌbəlju ˌkæmp / (AmE), / piː əʊ ˈdʌbəljuː ˌkamp / (BrE)
a camp where prisoners of war are detained, typically during a conflict or war
camp for prisoners of war

Examples

Many soldiers spent years in a POW camp after being captured during the war.
The historian discovered letters written from a German POW camp in World War II.
After the conflict ended, the government shut down the last remaining POW camp in the country.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prison camp - a place for detaining prisoners, especially during wartime

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'prisoner of war' (POW) plus 'camp'.
