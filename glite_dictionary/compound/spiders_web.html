spider's web

1. NOUN

/ ˈspaɪdərz wɛb / (AmE), / ˈspaɪdəz wɛb / (BrE)
a structure of fine threads spun by a spider, usually for catching insects
web spun by a spider

Examples

The dew glistened on the spider's web stretched across the garden path.
She watched a fly become trapped in the spider's web near the window.
After cleaning the attic, he found a large spider's web in the corner.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cobweb - a spider's web, especially when old and covered with dust
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04282482-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈspaɪdɚz wɛb / (AmE), / ˈspaɪdəz wɛb / (BrE)
a fine, silken net spun by a spider, or something that closely resembles such a web
web spun by a spider or similar structure

Examples

The morning dew made the spider's web glisten in the sunlight.
A thick spider's web stretched across the empty doorway.
She brushed away a spider's web that was clinging to her hair.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cobweb - a spider web, especially an old or dusty one
  • Synonym: web - a network of fine threads made by a spider
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04282667-n
Synset POS: n
