immaculate condition

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈmækjələt kənˈdɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪˈmakjʊlət kənˈdɪʃən / (BrE)
a state of being perfectly clean, tidy, and well-maintained; flawless condition
perfectly clean or flawless state

Examples

She sold her old car because it was still in immaculate condition.
The landlord expects the apartment to be returned in immaculate condition.
Despite its age, the antique desk remains in immaculate condition.

Synset Examples

The garden was kept in immaculate condition all year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mint condition - in perfect or as-new condition
  • Synonym: pristine condition - in original, unspoiled condition
