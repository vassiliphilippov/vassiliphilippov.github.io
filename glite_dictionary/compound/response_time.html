response time

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈspɑns taɪm / (AmE), / rɪˈspɒns taɪm / (BrE)
the amount of time that passes between receiving a stimulus and making a response, often used in contexts such as psychology or computing
interval between stimulus and response

Examples

The athlete's response time to the starting pistol is critical in competitive racing.
Researchers measured the response time of participants after they saw each visual cue.
The new software update improved the computer's response time considerably.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: latency - delay before a transfer of data begins following an instruction
