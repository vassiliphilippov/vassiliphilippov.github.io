profit and loss

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɑːfɪt ənd lɔs / (AmE), / ˈprɒfɪt ənd lɒs / (BrE)
a financial statement prepared at the end of an accounting period to show a company's revenues, expenses, and the resulting net profit or loss
financial statement listing profit or loss

Examples

The company's profit and loss for the year showed a significant increase in net income.
Analysts reviewed the profit and loss statement to assess the firm's financial health.
It's important to reconcile the profit and loss account before filing taxes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: income statement - an alternative term for profit and loss statement, showing a company's revenues and expenses
