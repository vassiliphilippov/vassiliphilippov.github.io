solan goose

1. NOUN

/ ˈsoʊ.lən ɡus / (AmE), / ˈsəʊ.lən ɡuːs / (BrE)
a large white seabird with black-tipped wings, also known as the northern gannet, found in North Atlantic regions
large white seabird, northern gannet

Examples

We watched a solan goose diving for fish off the rocky coast.
The cliffs were home to hundreds of nesting solan geese each spring.
A solitary solan goose soared above the crashing waves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gannet - a large white sea bird with black wingtips, northern gannet

Etymology

From Scottish and Northern English, solan (from Old Norse sula, 'gannet') + goose.
