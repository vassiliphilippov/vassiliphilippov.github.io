venae interlobulares hepatis

1. NOUN

/ ˌviː.naɪ ˌɪn.tər.loʊˈbjul.jʊˌlɛərɪz hɛˈpætɪs / (AmE), / ˌviː.naɪ ˌɪn.tə.ləʊˈbjuː.ljʊˌlɛərɪz hɛˈpætɪs / (BrE)
the small veins located between the lobules of the liver, responsible for draining blood from the portal tracts into the liver sinusoids
small veins between liver lobules
medical

Examples

The venae interlobulares hepatis play a crucial role in distributing blood through the liver.
Damage to the venae interlobulares hepatis can impair the liver's filtering functions.
Microscopic examination revealed dilation of the venae interlobulares hepatis in the liver tissue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: portal vein - major vein carrying blood from the gastrointestinal tract and spleen to the liver

Etymology

From Latin: 'venae' (veins), 'interlobulares' (between lobules), 'hepatis' (of the liver).
