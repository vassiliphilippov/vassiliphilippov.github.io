index of refraction

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪndɛks əv rɪˈfrækʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪndɛks əv rɪˈfrækʃən / (BrE)
The ratio of the speed of light in a vacuum to its speed in a given medium.
ratio of light speed in vacuum to medium

Examples

The index of refraction of water is approximately 1.33.
Different materials have a different index of refraction, which affects how light bends.
To calculate the index of refraction, divide the speed of light in a vacuum by the speed in the medium.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: refractive index - alternative term for index of refraction

Etymology

From Latin 'index' (indicator) and 'refraction', related to Latin 'refringere' (to break up).
