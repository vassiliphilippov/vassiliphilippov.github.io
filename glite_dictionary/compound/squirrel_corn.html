squirrel corn

1. NOUN

/ ˈskwɝːəl kɔrn / (AmE), / ˈskwɪrəl kɔːn / (BrE)
A North American woodland plant (Dicentra canadensis) with creamy-white flowers and small, yellow tuber-like roots that resemble kernels of corn.
North American plant with tuberous roots

Examples

The forest floor was dotted with blooming squirrel corn in early spring.
She studied the curious roots of the squirrel corn during her botany class.
Hikers often admire the delicate flowers of squirrel corn alongside other wildflowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Dutchman's breeches - A related North American plant with similar foliage and flowers

Etymology

Named for its tuberous roots, which resemble kernels of corn, and because it is thought that squirrels may eat them.
