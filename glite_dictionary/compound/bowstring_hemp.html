bowstring hemp

1. NOUN

/ ˈboʊˌstrɪŋ hɛmp / (AmE), / ˈbəʊˌstrɪŋ hɛmp / (BrE)
a strong fiber resembling hemp, obtained from the sansevieria plant and used for making cordage such as ropes and bowstrings
strong fiber from the sansevieria plant

Examples

The fibers of bowstring hemp are prized for their strength in making durable ropes.
Traditional archers sometimes use bowstring hemp to create reliable bowstrings for their bows.
We studied the process of extracting bowstring hemp from sansevieria leaves in our botany class.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hemp - a plant fiber used for making rope and fabric

2. NOUN

/ ˈboʊˌstrɪŋ hɛmp / (AmE), / ˈbəʊˌstrɪŋ hɛmp / (BrE)
a tropical African plant (Sansevieria trifasciata), cultivated as a houseplant for its long, mottled, sword-shaped leaves or grown for its strong fiber
houseplant with sword-shaped leaves; fiber source

Examples

The bowstring hemp thrives indoors with little water or sunlight.
She placed a potted bowstring hemp on her office desk for decoration.
Fibers from bowstring hemp have traditionally been used to make cords and ropes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snake plant - another common name for Sansevieria trifasciata
  • Degree Higher: hemp - a plant (Cannabis sativa) grown for its fiber

Etymology

Named for the use of its strong fibers in making bowstrings and its similarity in appearance to hemp.

3. NOUN

/ ˈboʊˌstrɪŋ hɛmp / (AmE), / ˈbəʊˌstrɪŋ hɛmp / (BrE)
a strong fiber obtained from the leaves of the sansevieria plant, used for making ropes and cords
fiber from sansevieria plant for ropes

Examples

The locals weave bowstring hemp into durable mats and baskets.
Ropes made from bowstring hemp are highly valued for their strength and flexibility.
Gardeners often grow sansevieria for both its looks and its use as bowstring hemp.

Etymology

From the use of its fiber for making bowstrings and 'hemp' meaning fiber obtained from various plants.
