killing field

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɪlɪŋ fiːld / (AmE), / ˈkɪlɪŋ fiːld / (BrE)
an area where large numbers of people have been killed, especially by massacre or genocide during war or violent conflict
area of mass killings, especially in war

Examples

The documentary explored the horrors of the killing fields during the Cambodian genocide.
Hundreds of victims were discovered in the killing fields outside the village after the conflict ended.
Historians continue to study the killing fields to understand the extent of the atrocity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: battlefield - area where a battle is fought
