stovepipe iron

1. NOUN

/ ˈstoʊvˌpaɪp ˈaɪərn / (AmE), / ˈstəʊvˌpaɪp ˈaɪən / (BrE)
Plate iron that is thinner than tank iron, used especially in making stovepipes and similar items.
thin plate iron for stovepipes

Examples

The craftsman selected a sheet of stovepipe iron to fabricate the new chimney flue.
Historically, stovepipe iron was widely used for domestic heating systems.
They ordered several tons of stovepipe iron for their metalworking shop.
