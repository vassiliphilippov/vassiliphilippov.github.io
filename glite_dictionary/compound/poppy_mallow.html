poppy mallow

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑpi ˌmæloʊ / (AmE), / ˈpɒpi ˌmæləʊ / (BrE)
A plant of the genus Callirhoe, having palmately cleft leaves and white, red, or purple flowers that bloom throughout the summer.
flowering plant of the genus Callirhoe

Examples

The poppy mallow blooms can add a vivid splash of color to any summer garden.
Gardeners value the poppy mallow for its drought tolerance and long flowering period.
We identified several poppy mallow plants growing along the roadside.

Etymology

From 'poppy,' after the resemblance of its flowers to poppies, and 'mallow' for its family (Malvaceae) association.
