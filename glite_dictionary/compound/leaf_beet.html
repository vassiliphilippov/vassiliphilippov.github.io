leaf beet

1. NOUN

/ lif bit / (AmE), / liːf biːt / (BrE)
A variety of beet grown as a vegetable for its edible leaves and stalks, rather than its root.
a beet grown for edible leaves and stalks

Examples

The chef sautéed leaf beet with garlic for a flavorful side dish.
In the garden, leaf beet thrives in cooler weather and produces abundant greens.
Unlike beets grown for their roots, leaf beet is valued for its nutritious leaves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chard - a leafy green vegetable also known as Swiss chard, a type of leaf beet

2. NOUN

/ lif bit / (AmE), / liːf ˈbiːt / (BrE)
A plant with long, succulent whitish stalks and large green leaves, grown as a vegetable; also called Swiss chard.
vegetable with thick stalks and broad leaves

Examples

We grow leaf beet in our garden for its nutritious leaves and stalks.
The chef added chopped leaf beet to the salad for extra flavor and color.
You can sauté leaf beet with garlic for a quick and healthy side dish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Swiss chard - a variety of beet grown for its edible leaves and stalks
