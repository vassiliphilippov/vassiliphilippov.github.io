Katmai National Park and Preserve

1. PROPN

/ ˈkæt.maɪ ˌnæʃ.ə.nəl ˌpɑrk ənd prɪˈzɜrv / (AmE), / ˈkat.maɪ ˌnæʃ.ən.əl ˌpɑːk ənd prɪˈzɜːv / (BrE)
A national park and protected area in southern Alaska, known for its volcanic landscapes, mountains, and abundant wildlife.
national park and preserve in Alaska

Examples

Many tourists visit Katmai National Park and Preserve each summer to watch brown bears catch salmon.
The volcanic eruption of 1912 dramatically shaped the landscape of Katmai National Park and Preserve.
Researchers study the unique ecosystem of Katmai National Park and Preserve to learn more about Alaskan wildlife.

Etymology

Named after Mount Katmai within the park; Katmai is derived from a local Alaskan indigenous name.
