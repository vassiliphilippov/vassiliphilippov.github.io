widow bird

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪdoʊ bɜrd / (AmE), / ˈwɪdəʊ bɜːd / (BrE)
A mostly black African weaverbird, known for the long tail feathers of the male.
African weaverbird with long tail feathers

Examples

The male widow bird displays his long tail feathers during mating season.
We spotted a widow bird perched on a branch in the African savanna.
The remarkable appearance of the widow bird makes it popular among birdwatchers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: widowbird - another spelling of widow bird

Etymology

From 'widow' (in reference to the bird's black color, reminiscent of widow's mourning dress) + 'bird'.
