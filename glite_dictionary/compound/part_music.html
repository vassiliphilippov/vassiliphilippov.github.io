part music

1. NOUN

/ pɑrt ˈmjuːzɪk / (AmE), / pɑːt ˈmjuːzɪk / (BrE)
Vocal music composed for several voices with independent parts, typically performed without instrumental accompaniment.
vocal music for several independent voices

Examples

The choir performed a beautiful example of part music during the service.
She specializes in Renaissance part music and often sings in small ensembles.
Many early English composers wrote part music for church and court choirs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: a cappella - singing without instrumental accompaniment
