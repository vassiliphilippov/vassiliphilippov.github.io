inhalation anesthetic

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˌheɪlˈeɪʃən ˌænəsˈθɛtɪk / (AmE), / ɪnˌheɪlˈeɪʃən ˌanɪsˈθɛtɪk / (BrE)
a substance in the form of a gas or vapor that induces general anesthesia when breathed in
gas or vapor used for anesthesia
medical

Examples

The patient was given an inhalation anesthetic before the surgery began.
Modern inhalation anesthetics are safer and more effective than those used decades ago.
The choice of inhalation anesthetic can affect a patient's recovery time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anesthetic - a substance that induces insensitivity to pain
  • Antonym: intravenous anesthetic - an anesthetic administered by injection into the veins
