capitation payment model

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæpɪˈteɪʃən ˈpeɪmənt ˈmɑdəl / (AmE), / ˌkæpɪˈteɪʃən ˈpeɪmənt ˈmɒdəl / (BrE)
A healthcare payment system in which providers are paid a fixed amount per patient for a specified period, regardless of the amount of care provided.
healthcare payment method based on patient count
medical

Examples

Many managed care organizations use the capitation payment model to control healthcare costs.
Switching to a capitation payment model can shift the focus toward preventive care rather than volume of services.
Doctors may have concerns about the impact of a capitation payment model on patient outcomes.

Synset Examples

Capitation payment models can incentivize preventive care.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fee-for-service - payment model where services are paid for individually

Etymology

From 'capitation', originally Latin 'capitatio' (a poll tax), relating to 'caput' meaning 'head', indicating payment per person.
