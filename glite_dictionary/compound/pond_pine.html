pond pine

1. NOUN

/ pɑnd paɪn / (AmE), / pɒnd paɪn / (BrE)
a large pine tree (Pinus serotina) native to sandy swamps of the southeastern United States, having long, three-needled leaves
large pine tree of southeastern U.S. swamps

Examples

The pond pine thrives in wet, sandy soils found throughout the southeastern United States.
Unlike the pitch pine, the pond pine has significantly longer needles.
Several wildlife species find shelter among the branches of the pond pine.
