Renaissance man

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈneɪsəns mæn / (AmE), / rɪˈneɪsəns mæn / (BrE)
A person with broad knowledge or skill in a wide range of fields, especially in the arts and sciences, like those scholars during the Renaissance period who were adept in many disciplines.
a person talented in many fields

Examples

Leonardo da Vinci is the epitome of a Renaissance man, excelling in art, science, and engineering.
Many admire him for being a modern Renaissance man with expertise in music, languages, and philosophy.
The company is seeking a Renaissance man to lead their interdisciplinary research team.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polymath - person with wide-ranging knowledge or learning

Etymology

From the French 'Renaissance' (rebirth), referring to the European cultural period; 'man' meaning a person.

2. NOUN

/ rəˈneɪsəns mæn / (AmE), / rɪˈneɪsəns mæn / (BrE)
A person who has broad knowledge and skills in many different fields, especially in both the arts and the sciences.
person with wide-ranging knowledge and skills

Examples

Leonardo da Vinci is often considered the quintessential Renaissance man for his accomplishments in art, science, and engineering.
In today's world, being a Renaissance man can give you a unique edge in problem-solving and creativity.
He was praised as a Renaissance man because he excelled in mathematics, literature, and music.

Synset Examples

a statistician has to be something of a generalist

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polymath - a person of wide-ranging knowledge or learning
  • Antonym: specialist - a person who focuses on one area of knowledge or skill

Etymology

From the European Renaissance period, during which it was common for accomplished men to excel in multiple disciplines.
