sea-lettuce family

1. NOUN

/ siː ˈlɛtɪs ˈfæmɪli / (AmE), / siː ˈlɛtɪs ˈfamɪli / (BrE)
A family of thin, flat, or tubular green algae found mainly in marine environments, including the genus Ulva (sea lettuce).
family of green algae (Ulvaceae)

Examples

The sea-lettuce family includes several species commonly found along rocky shorelines.
Biologists study the sea-lettuce family to understand marine ecosystems and nutrient cycles.
Members of the sea-lettuce family are sometimes eaten by people or used in animal feed.
