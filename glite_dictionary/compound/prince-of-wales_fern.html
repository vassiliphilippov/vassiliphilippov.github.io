Prince-of-Wales fern

1. NOUN

/ ˌprɪns əv ˈweɪlz fɜrn / (AmE), / ˌprɪns əv ˈweɪlz fɜːn / (BrE)
a fern native to New Zealand, having pinnate fronds and a densely woolly stalk; sometimes classified in the genus Todea
New Zealand fern with woolly stalks

Examples

The Prince-of-Wales fern thrives in damp, shaded forests throughout New Zealand.
Many gardeners prize the Prince-of-Wales fern for its delicate, feathery fronds and unique texture.
A striking feature of the Prince-of-Wales fern is its densely woolly stalk that distinguishes it from other ferns.
