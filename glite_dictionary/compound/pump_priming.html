pump priming

1. NOUN

/ pʌmp ˈpraɪmɪŋ / (AmE), / pʌmp ˈpraɪmɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of introducing water into a pump to improve its seal and initiate water flow
starting flow in a pump by adding water

Examples

The engineers performed pump priming before starting the irrigation system.
Regular pump priming ensures that the equipment operates efficiently.
Without proper pump priming, the device can be damaged by running dry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: priming - the act of preparing something for use or action

2. NOUN

/ pʌmp ˈpraɪmɪŋ / (AmE), / pʌmp ˈpraɪmɪŋ / (BrE)
the introduction of government money into the economy, especially through spending or investment, to encourage economic activity and growth
government spending to stimulate the economy

Examples

The new administration enacted pump priming measures to counter the effects of the recession.
Some economists argue that pump priming can help economies recover from downturns more quickly.
Government pump priming is often used during periods of high unemployment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stimulus - government action designed to encourage economic growth
  • Antonym: austerity - government policy of reducing spending to control deficit

Etymology

From the practice of priming a pump with water to start its operation, used figuratively in economics since the early 20th century.
