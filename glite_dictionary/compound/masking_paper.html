masking paper

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæskɪŋ ˌpeɪpɚ / (AmE), / ˈmɑːskɪŋ ˌpeɪpə / (BrE)
a paper, often with an adhesive edge, used to cover parts of a surface to protect them from paint or finish during painting or similar work
paper for protecting areas from paint

Examples

The painter applied masking paper along the window edges to keep them clean.
Before spraying the walls, she carefully put masking paper on the skirting boards.
Always remove the masking paper slowly to avoid peeling off fresh paint.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: masking tape - adhesive tape used to cover areas during painting
