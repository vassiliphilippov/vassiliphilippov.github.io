landfill indie

1. NOUN

/ ˈlændˌfɪl ˈɪndi / (AmE), / ˈlænd.fɪl ˈɪn.di / (BrE)
A subgenre of indie music, especially from the 2000s, characterized by DIY ethics and often seen as formulaic or low-profile.
subgenre of indie music; low-profile or obscure

Examples

Many critics argue that landfill indie bands dominated the UK charts in the mid-2000s.
His playlist was full of obscure landfill indie tracks from local garage bands.
Although some dismiss landfill indie as generic, fans appreciate its raw, unpolished sound.

Synset Examples

I love this landfill indie band; their sound is unique.
She prefers landfill indie music over mainstream hits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indie rock - a genre of rock music that originated from independent music scenes

Etymology

Formed from 'landfill' (waste site) and 'indie' (independent music), originally used derogatorily for a glut of similar-sounding bands in early 2000s UK.
