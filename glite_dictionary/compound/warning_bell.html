warning bell

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔrnɪŋ bɛl / (AmE), / ˈwɔːnɪŋ bɛl / (BrE)
a bell that is rung to give an alarm of danger or to signal a warning
a bell used to signal danger or warning

Examples

The sudden blare of the warning bell sent everyone rushing to the emergency exit.
When the warning bell sounded, workers evacuated the building immediately.
A warning bell is installed in the tower to alert the village of a fire.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alarm bell - a bell that warns of danger

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɔrnɪŋ bɛl / (AmE), / ˈwɔːnɪŋ bɛl / (BrE)
an event or signal that alerts people to a potential problem, danger, or issue
signal of potential problem or danger

Examples

The drop in sales rang a warning bell for the management team.
His irregular heartbeat was a warning bell for the doctor to investigate further.
When deadlines were repeatedly missed, it set off a warning bell for the project's sponsors.

Synset Examples

Her sudden absence was a warning bell for the team.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alarm bell - a bell or signal warning of danger or requiring attention
