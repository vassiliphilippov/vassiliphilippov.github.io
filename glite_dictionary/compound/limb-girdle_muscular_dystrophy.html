limb-girdle muscular dystrophy

1. NOUN

/ lɪm ˈɡɝːdəl ˌmʌskjələr dɪsˈtrɒfi / (AmE), / lɪm ˈɡɜːdəl ˌmʌskjʊlə dɪsˈtrɒfi / (BrE)
a type of muscular dystrophy that causes progressive muscle weakness and wasting, typically beginning in the shoulders or hips and often progressing slowly
a form of muscular dystrophy affecting shoulders or hips
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with limb-girdle muscular dystrophy after experiencing progressive weakness in his hip muscles.
Researchers are working to develop new treatments for limb-girdle muscular dystrophy.
Symptoms of limb-girdle muscular dystrophy often begin in adolescence or early adulthood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Duchenne muscular dystrophy - a severe form of muscular dystrophy with early onset
  • Synonym: Becker muscular dystrophy - a milder, later-onset form of muscular dystrophy

Etymology

From 'limb-girdle' referring to the shoulder and pelvic girdles, and 'muscular dystrophy', from Greek 'dys-' (bad) + 'trophē' (nourishment).
