genus Artocarpus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌɑːr.toʊˈkɑːr.pəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌɑː.təˈkɑː.pəs / (BrE)
A genus of evergreen trees native to Asia, including the breadfruit and jackfruit, now cultivated in tropical regions worldwide.
a tree genus including breadfruit and jackfruit

Examples

The breadfruit is a member of the genus Artocarpus, which is prized throughout tropical regions.
Many species within the genus Artocarpus are important food sources, especially the jackfruit.
Researchers are studying the genetic diversity of the genus Artocarpus to improve crop resilience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: breadfruit - large, starchy fruit from Artocarpus altilis
  • Synonym: jackfruit - large, tropical fruit from Artocarpus heterophyllus

Etymology

From Latin 'artocarpus', from Greek 'artos' (bread) + 'karpos' (fruit).
