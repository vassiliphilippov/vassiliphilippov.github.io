just a game

1. NOUN

/ dʒʌst ə ɡeɪm / (AmE), / dʒʌst ə ɡeɪm / (BrE)
used to say that something should not be taken too seriously or personally, as it is only an activity or matter of little consequence
not to be taken seriously

Examples

Try not to get upset—it's just a game after all.
He keeps reminding himself that the argument was just a game.
Whenever tempers flare, the coach says, 'Remember, it's just a game.'

Synset Examples

It’s all just a game; don’t take it too seriously.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: serious business - something that must be treated with importance or gravity
