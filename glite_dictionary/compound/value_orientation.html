value orientation

1. NOUN

/ ˈvæl.ju ˌɔːr.i.enˈteɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˈvæl.juː ˌɔːr.i.enˈteɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
a set of moral principles or values accepted by an individual or a group, guiding their attitudes and behaviors
accepted moral principles or values

Examples

Different cultures may have a distinct value orientation that influences their decision-making.
Her value orientation shaped the way she approached ethical dilemmas at work.
Sociologists study how value orientation affects group behavior and social norms.

Synset Examples

the Puritan ethic
a person with old-fashioned values

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ethic - a set of moral principles
  • Synonym: morality - principles concerning right and wrong behavior
