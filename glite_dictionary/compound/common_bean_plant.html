common bean plant

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmən ˈbin plænt / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən ˈbiːn plɑːnt / (BrE)
an annual climbing or bushy plant (Phaseolus vulgaris) grown for its edible seeds or pods, commonly known as beans
bean-producing plant (Phaseolus vulgaris)

Examples

The common bean plant is a staple crop in many parts of the world.
She grows several varieties of common bean plant in her backyard garden every summer.
Diseases can affect the yield of the common bean plant if not properly managed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bean - the edible seed of the common bean plant

Etymology

From 'common' (widespread) + 'bean' (edible seed) + 'plant' (organism), in reference to Phaseolus vulgaris.
