yellow nutgrass

1. NOUN

/ ˈjɛloʊ ˈnʌtˌɡræs / (AmE), / ˈjɛləʊ ˈnʌtˌɡrɑːs / (BrE)
A European sedge plant (Cyperus esculentus) with small, edible, nutlike tubers.
European sedge with edible tubers

Examples

The farmer discovered yellow nutgrass growing in the corner of his field.
Children once dug up yellow nutgrass tubers to eat as a snack.
Gardeners often struggle to control yellow nutgrass because of its persistent roots.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chufa - another name for Cyperus esculentus, the same plant species
