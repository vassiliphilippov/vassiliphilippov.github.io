sewer system

1. NOUN

/ ˈsuːɚ ˌsɪstəm / (AmE), / ˈsjʊə ˌsɪstəm / (BrE)
A network of pipes and channels designed to carry off wastewater and sewage from buildings and streets.
network of pipes for sewage removal

Examples

The city's sewer system was upgraded to prevent flooding during heavy rains.
A broken sewer system can lead to serious health hazards in urban areas.
Modern sewer systems are designed to handle both stormwater and wastewater efficiently.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drainage system - system for removing excess water
