bank gravel

1. NOUN

/ bæŋk ˈɡrævəl / (AmE), / bæŋk ˈɡrævəl / (BrE)
Gravel found in natural deposits, typically in or near riverbanks, used especially in construction or road building.
naturally deposited gravel

Examples

The construction crew used bank gravel as a base for the new road.
Because bank gravel is naturally deposited, it is often well sorted and easy to work with.
Supplies of bank gravel were taken from the riverbed nearby for the foundation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pit gravel - gravel dug from pits, often less sorted than bank gravel
