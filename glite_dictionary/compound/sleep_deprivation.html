sleep deprivation

1. NOUN

/ ˈsliːp ˌdɛprəˈveɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈsliːp ˌdɛprɪˈveɪʃən / (BrE)
A form of psychological torture or abuse in which a person is deliberately prevented from sleeping.
deliberate prevention of sleep as torture
legal

Examples

The prisoner was subjected to sleep deprivation as a method of extracting information.
Prolonged sleep deprivation can have severe psychological consequences on a person's health.
Activists have condemned sleep deprivation as an inhumane method of interrogation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: torture - the act of inflicting severe pain as punishment or to force information
