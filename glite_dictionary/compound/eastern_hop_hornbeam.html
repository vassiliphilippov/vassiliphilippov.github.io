Eastern hop hornbeam

1. NOUN

/ ˈiːstɚn hɑp ˈhɔrnˌbim / (AmE), / ˈiːstən hɒp ˈhɔːnˌbiːm / (BrE)
a medium-sized deciduous tree (Ostrya virginiana) native to eastern North America, with thin, flaky bark and hop-like fruit clusters
a North American deciduous tree

Examples

The Eastern hop hornbeam is commonly found in the understory of eastern North American forests.
I recognized the tree by its distinctive flaky bark and hop-like fruit clusters as an Eastern hop hornbeam.
Several birds feed on the seeds of the Eastern hop hornbeam during the winter months.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hop hornbeam - any tree of the genus Ostrya; small deciduous trees with hop-like fruits
