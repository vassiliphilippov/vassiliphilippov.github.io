Taconic Mountains

1. PROPN

/ təˈkɑnɪk ˈmaʊntənz / (AmE), / təˈkɒnɪk ˈmaʊntɪnz / (BrE)
a range of the Appalachian Mountains located along the eastern border of New York with Connecticut, Massachusetts, and Vermont
mountain range in northeastern United States

Examples

The Taconic Mountains offer excellent hiking trails and scenic views in every season.
Many rare plants can be found throughout the Taconic Mountains region.
During their drive, they admired the autumn foliage of the Taconic Mountains.

Etymology

From the Native American (Mahican) word possibly meaning 'forest' or 'in the trees'.
