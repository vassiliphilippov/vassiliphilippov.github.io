clarke value

1. NOUN

/ ˈklɑrk ˌvælju / (AmE), / ˈklɑːk ˌvaljuː / (BrE)
the average concentration of a chemical element in the Earth's crust, used in geochemistry.
average concentration of an element in the Earth's crust

Examples

The clarke value for iron is significantly higher than that of gold in the Earth's crust.
Researchers compared the clarke values of different elements to understand distribution patterns.
Knowing the clarke value helps geologists assess the rarity of certain minerals.

Etymology

Named after Frank Wigglesworth Clarke, American geochemist who pioneered studies of elemental concentrations.
