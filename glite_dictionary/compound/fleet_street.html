Fleet Street

1. PROPN

/ fliːt strit / (AmE), / fliːt striːt / (BrE)
A street in central London historically known as the center of British newspaper publishing; often used to refer to the British press as a whole.
historic center of British newspaper publishing, London
british

Examples

Many journalists began their careers on Fleet Street in the 1970s.
The power of Fleet Street has shaped public opinion in Britain for decades.
After the major newspapers left Fleet Street, the area changed its character.

Etymology

Named after the River Fleet, a tributary of the Thames that runs underground in London.

2. NOUN

/ flit strit / (AmE), / fliːt striːt / (BrE)
The British national press and journalists collectively, especially as a powerful or influential group; named after the London street formerly home to many newspaper offices.
the British newspaper industry
british

Examples

Many scandals were exposed by Fleet Street reporters during the 1990s.
She always dreamed of working at one of the big Fleet Street newspapers.
Fleet Street has long been a symbol of British journalism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: the press - newspapers and journalists collectively
  • Synonym: media - mass communication, especially newspapers, television, and radio

Etymology

Named after Fleet Street in London, where many newspaper offices were once located.
