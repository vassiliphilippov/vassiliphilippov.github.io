hair space

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛr ˌspeɪs / (AmE), / ˈheə ˌspeɪs / (BrE)
In printing, the narrowest space used to separate words or letters, thinner than a thin space.
very narrow space in printing

Examples

For precise alignment, a hair space was inserted between each letter.
The typographer adjusted the text to include a hair space for better readability.
Old printing presses required manual placement of a hair space by the compositor.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: thin space - a space in typesetting narrower than a normal space but wider than a hair space

Etymology

Combines 'hair' (thinness) and 'space' (typographical separation), from printing terminology.
