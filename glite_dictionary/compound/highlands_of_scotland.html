Highlands of Scotland

1. PROPN

/ ˈhaɪlændz əv ˈskɑtlənd / (AmE), / ˈhaɪləndz əv ˈskɒtlənd / (BrE)
a mountainous region in northern Scotland famous for its rugged landscape, traditional tartan dress, and historical clan system
mountainous region of northern Scotland

Examples

Many tourists visit the Highlands of Scotland to enjoy the breathtaking views and rich cultural heritage.
Legends about the ancient clans of the Highlands of Scotland are still told in local communities.
The unique tartans and kilts originated in the Highlands of Scotland centuries ago.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Lowlands of Scotland - the southern, less mountainous region of Scotland
