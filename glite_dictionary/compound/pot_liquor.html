pot liquor

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑt ˌlɪkər / (AmE), / ˈpɒt ˌlɪkə / (BrE)
the liquid left in a pot after cooking vegetables or meat, often used as a flavorful broth or base for soups and sauces
liquid left after cooking food in a pot

Examples

Many Southern dishes use pot liquor from collard greens as a flavorful base.
Grandma always saved the pot liquor to make hearty soups.
After boiling the vegetables, she drank the pot liquor for its nutrients.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: broth - the liquid resulting from cooking meat, fish, or vegetables in water
  • Synonym: stock - a savory liquid base made by simmering meat, fish, or vegetables
