terra incognita

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɛrə ɪnˈkɑɡnɪtə / (AmE), / ˌtɛrə ɪnˈkɒɡnɪtə / (BrE)
an area or subject that is unknown or unexplored
unknown or unexplored area

Examples

Much of the deep ocean remains terra incognita to modern science.
When it comes to quantum computing, it's still terra incognita for most researchers.
Exploring a new creative field was a journey into terra incognita for her.

Synset Examples

they came like angels out of the unknown

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unknown - not known or familiar
  • Antonym: terra firma - dry land, as opposed to sea or air

Etymology

From Latin, meaning 'unknown land'.
