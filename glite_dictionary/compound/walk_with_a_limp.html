to walk with a limp

1. VERB

/ wɑk wɪð ə lɪmp / (AmE), / wɔːk wɪð ə lɪmp / (BrE)
to walk in an uneven or unsteady way, typically because of injury or pain in a leg or foot
walk unevenly due to injury

Examples

After her surgery, she began to walk with a limp for several weeks.
He tried to hide his pain but continued to walk with a limp.
She refused to let her injury stop her from walking with a limp to the market each day.

Synset Examples

After the injury, he had to walk with a limp.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: limp - to walk with difficulty, especially because of a damaged leg or foot
