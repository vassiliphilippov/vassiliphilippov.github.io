stem canker

1. NOUN

/ stɛm ˈkæŋkər / (AmE), / stɛm ˈkæŋkə / (BrE)
A plant disease, especially affecting potatoes, characterized by sunken lesions on the stems, caused by certain fungi such as Rhizoctonia.
fungal disease causing stem lesions
medical

Examples

Potato crops can be severely affected by stem canker, leading to reduced yields.
Signs of stem canker include black, sunken spots on the lower stems of the plants.
Farmers use resistant varieties to minimize the risk of stem canker in their fields.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: canker - a plant disease causing sores or lesions

Etymology

From Latin 'cancer', meaning sore or ulcer, via Old French 'chancre', referring to a plant disease causing lesions.
