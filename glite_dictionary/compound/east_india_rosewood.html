East India rosewood

1. NOUN

/ ist ˈɪndiə ˈroʊzˌwʊd / (AmE), / iːst ˈɪndiə ˈrəʊzwʊd / (BrE)
a large tree native to India, valued for its hard, dark purple-brown wood used in fine furniture and musical instruments
tree with valuable dark wood from India

Examples

Many guitars are crafted from East India rosewood due to its superior tonal quality.
The deep color and rich grain of East India rosewood make it popular among cabinetmakers.
Export of East India rosewood is regulated to protect the species from overharvesting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Dalbergia latifolia - the scientific name of East India rosewood
  • Degree Lower: rosewood - wood from rosewood trees, generally used for furniture
