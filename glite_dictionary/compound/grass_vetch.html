grass vetch

1. NOUN

/ ɡræs vɛtʃ / (AmE), / ɡrɑːs vɛtʃ / (BrE)
an annual European vetch plant with red flowers, often found in grassy areas
a European vetch with red flowers

Examples

The field was scattered with blooming grass vetch, adding a splash of red to the landscape.
Farmers sometimes plant grass vetch as a cover crop to improve soil quality.
Botanists identified the rare grass vetch during their survey of the meadow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vetch - a climbing plant of the pea family
