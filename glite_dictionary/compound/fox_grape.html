fox grape

1. NOUN

/ fɑks ɡreɪp / (AmE), / fɒks ɡreɪp / (BrE)
A species of wild grape, native to the eastern United States, with purplish-black fruit and tough skins that slip easily from the flesh; grown in many cultivated varieties.
wild grape native to eastern US

Examples

Many jams and juices are made from fox grape due to its distinctive flavor.
Birds are often attracted to the ripe fox grapes found along forest edges in autumn.
The fox grape is well known for its tough skin and aromatic qualities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Concord grape - a popular cultivated variety descended from the fox grape

2. NOUN

/ fɑks ɡreɪp / (AmE), / fɒks ɡreɪp / (BrE)
A wild grape native to the northeastern United States, Vitis labrusca, and the origin of many cultivated grape varieties such as the Concord grape.
wild grape species of northeastern U.S.

Examples

The fox grape is often found growing wild along the edges of forests in New England.
Many American grape juices are made from the fox grape due to its distinctive flavor.
The fox grape gave rise to the well-known Concord grape variety.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Concord grape - a popular cultivated variety derived from the fox grape
