acorn cup

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪˌkɔrn ˌkʌp / (AmE), / ˈeɪˌkɔːn ˌkʌp / (BrE)
the cup-shaped, hardened base of an acorn formed by bracts, usually partially enclosing the nut
the cup-shaped base of an acorn

Examples

The squirrel clutched the acorn by its acorn cup and nibbled eagerly.
Botanists explained how the acorn cup protects the seed during development.
Children collected acorn cups from the forest floor for their craft project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acorn - the nut of the oak tree
