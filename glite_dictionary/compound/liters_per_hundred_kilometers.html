liters per hundred kilometers

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪtɚz pɚ ˈhʌndrəd kɪˈlɑːmɪtɚz / (AmE), / ˈliːtəz pə ˈhʌndrəd kɪˈlɒmɪtəz / (BrE)
a measure of fuel consumption for vehicles, indicating the amount of fuel in liters used to travel one hundred kilometers
vehicle fuel consumption rate (liters/100 km)

Examples

European cars are usually rated by liters per hundred kilometers, not by miles per gallon.
This model achieves a fuel efficiency of 6 liters per hundred kilometers on the highway.
Drivers often compare liters per hundred kilometers figures when choosing a new car.

Synset Examples

Liters per one hundred kilometers is considered to be a more accurate measure of a vehicle's performance because it is a linear relationship while fuel economy leads to distortions in efficiency improvements.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: miles per gallon - measure of fuel consumption using miles and gallons
