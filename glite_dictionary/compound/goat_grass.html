goat grass

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡoʊt ɡræs / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊt ɡrɑːs / (BrE)
A European grass naturalized as a weed in North America, noted for its sharp-pointed seeds that can injure livestock when eaten.
European weed grass harmful to livestock

Examples

The pasture was overrun with goat grass, making it unsafe for grazing calves.
Some farmers struggle to control the spread of goat grass in their fields.
Because of its sharp seeds, goat grass can cause mouth injuries to sheep and cattle.
