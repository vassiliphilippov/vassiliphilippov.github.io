American pulsatilla

1. NOUN

/ əˌmɛr.ɪ.kən ˌpʌl.səˈtɪl.ə / (AmE), / əˌmɛr.ɪ.kən ˌpʌl.səˈtɪl.ə / (BrE)
A short, hairy perennial plant with early spring blue-violet or lilac flowers, native to North America and Siberia.
North American perennial plant with blue-violet flowers

Examples

The American pulsatilla blooms each year just as the snow begins to melt.
Gardeners prize the American pulsatilla for its delicate spring flowers and feathery foliage.
Hiking in Alaska, we spotted clusters of American pulsatilla growing along the trail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pasqueflower - Another name for Pulsatilla plants with similar blue-violet flowers

Etymology

Pulsatilla from Modern Latin, diminutive of Latin pulsare (to beat), referring to moving in the wind.
