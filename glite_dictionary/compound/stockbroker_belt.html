stockbroker belt

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɑkˌbroʊkər bɛlt / (AmE), / ˈstɒkˌbrəʊkə bɛlt / (BrE)
An affluent residential area, typically in the south-east of England, where many wealthy commuters live.
affluent residential suburb or commuter area
british

Examples

Property prices in the stockbroker belt have soared in recent years due to high demand from London commuters.
They moved to the stockbroker belt for its excellent schools and green spaces.
The stockbroker belt is known for its exclusive golf clubs and large detached houses.

Etymology

From 'stockbroker', referring to the profession associated with wealth, and 'belt', meaning an area or region.
