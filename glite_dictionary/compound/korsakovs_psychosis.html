Korsakov's psychosis

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔrsəˌkɑvz saɪˈkoʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˈkɔːsəkɒvz saɪˈkəʊsɪs / (BrE)
A chronic mental disorder seen mainly in people with severe alcohol dependence, characterized by memory loss for recent events while long-term memory remains relatively intact.
dementia with memory loss due to alcoholism
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with Korsakov's psychosis after years of heavy drinking had impaired his memory.
Despite his ability to recall childhood memories, his Korsakov's psychosis prevented him from remembering events just hours ago.
Medical staff monitor patients with Korsakov's psychosis for other symptoms associated with chronic alcoholism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Korsakoff's syndrome - another term for Korsakov's psychosis

Etymology

Named after Russian neuropsychiatrist Sergei S. Korsakov, who first described the condition in the late 19th century.
