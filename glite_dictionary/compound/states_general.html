States General

1. NOUN

/ steɪts ˈdʒɛnərəl / (AmE), / steɪts ˈdʒɛnərəl / (BrE)
the legislative assembly representing the estates of an entire country, especially the sovereign body of the Dutch Republic from the 16th to 18th centuries
Dutch national assembly (16th–18th c.)

Examples

The States General played a crucial role in the governance of the Dutch Republic during the Golden Age.
Delegates gathered at the States General to discuss matters of national importance.
Historians often compare the French Estates-General and the Dutch States General in their analyses of European political systems.

Etymology

From French États généraux, meaning 'general estates'; originally referring to representative assemblies in France and the Netherlands.
