calcium ion

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæl.si.əm aɪ.ɑn / (AmE), / ˈkælsiəm aɪ.ɒn / (BrE)
A positively charged ion (Ca²⁺) of calcium, important in biological processes such as blood clotting and muscle contraction.
positively charged calcium atom
medical

Examples

The level of calcium ion in the blood is tightly regulated by the body.
A calcium ion plays a crucial role in nerve signal transmission.
Many enzymes require a calcium ion as a cofactor for proper function.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: magnesium ion - positively charged magnesium atom (Mg²⁺)
  • Synonym: sodium ion - positively charged sodium atom (Na⁺)

Etymology

From calcium (from Latin 'calx', meaning lime) and ion (from Greek 'ion', going).
