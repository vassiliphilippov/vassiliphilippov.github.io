direct dye

1. NOUN

/ dəˈrɛkt daɪ / (AmE), / daɪˈrɛkt daɪ / (BrE)
a dye with a high affinity for cellulose fibers, such as cotton or rayon, which can be applied directly without a mordant
dye for cellulose fibers applied directly

Examples

Cotton towels were colored using a direct dye for a vibrant finish.
The textile mill switched from reactive dyes to direct dyes to simplify the coloring process.
A direct dye does not require the use of an additional chemical mordant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acid dye - dye used mainly on wool, silk, and nylon
  • Antonym: mordant dye - dye that requires a mordant to fix to fibers
