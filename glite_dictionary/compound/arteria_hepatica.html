arteria hepatica

1. NOUN

/ ɑːrˈtɪəriə hɛˈpætɪkə / (AmE), / ɑːˈtɪə.ri.ə hɛˈpætɪkə / (BrE)
An artery or arteries that supply blood to the liver.
liver-supplying artery
medical

Examples

The surgeon identified the arteria hepatica before beginning the liver transplant procedure.
Damage to the arteria hepatica can lead to severe complications in hepatic blood flow.
An abnormal branching pattern of the arteria hepatica was noted during the operation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hepatic artery - main artery supplying the liver

Etymology

From Latin: 'arteria' (artery) + 'hepatica' (of the liver), from Greek 'hēpar' (liver).
