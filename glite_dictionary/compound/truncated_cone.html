truncated cone

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrʌn.keɪ.tɪd koʊn / (AmE), / ˈtrʌŋ.kjʊ.eɪ.tɪd kəʊn / (BrE)
A solid figure obtained by cutting a cone parallel to its base and removing the top part, resulting in a frustum with circular bases of different sizes.
cone with the top cut off (frustum)

Examples

The lampshade has the shape of a truncated cone to help diffuse the light evenly.
In geometry class, we learned to calculate the volume of a truncated cone.
A funnel often resembles a truncated cone to allow substances to flow smoothly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frustum - the part of a solid (normally a cone or pyramid) that lies between two parallel planes cutting it

Etymology

From Latin 'truncatus' (cut off, maimed) and Greek 'konos' (cone).
