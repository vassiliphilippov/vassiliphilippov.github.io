degree day

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈɡri deɪ / (AmE), / dɪˈɡri deɪ / (BrE)
the day on which university degrees are officially awarded to graduates, typically during a formal ceremony
university graduation ceremony day

Examples

The entire family attended Emily's degree day to celebrate her graduation from university.
On his degree day, James wore his gown and cap with pride.
Guests are invited to the campus for the degree day ceremony next Friday.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: graduation day - the day when students officially graduate from an institution

2. NOUN

/ dɪˈɡri ˌdeɪ / (AmE), / dɪˈɡriː ˌdeɪ / (BrE)
a unit for measuring the extent to which the outside temperature falls below (or rises above) a standard level, used to estimate heating or cooling requirements in buildings
unit estimating heating or cooling needs

Examples

The building manager calculated the number of degree days to estimate fuel usage for the winter.
Utility companies track degree days to help predict energy demand.
A colder-than-average month led to a higher total of degree days for the region.
