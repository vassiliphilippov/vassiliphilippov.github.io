zip fastener

1. NOUN

/ zɪp ˈfæstənər / (AmE), / zɪp ˈfɑːstənə / (BrE)
a fastening device consisting of two rows of metal or plastic teeth that are brought together by a sliding tab to close an opening in clothing, bags, etc.
device for fastening with interlocking teeth
british

Examples

My jacket’s zip fastener got stuck halfway up, so I couldn’t close it fully.
The bag has a sturdy zip fastener that keeps everything secure inside.
She prefers dresses with a zip fastener instead of buttons for convenience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zipper - a fastening device with two rows of teeth and a sliding mechanism
  • Antonym: button - a small round object sewn onto clothing for fastening

Etymology

From 'zip' (imitative of the sound) and 'fastener' (something that fastens).
