methyl radical

1. NOUN

/ ˈmiːθɪl ˈrædɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈmiːθɪl ˈrædɪkəl / (BrE)
a univalent chemical group with the formula CH3, derived from methane by the removal of one hydrogen atom; a highly reactive intermediate in many chemical reactions
CH3 univalent group from methane

Examples

During combustion, the methyl radical is an important intermediate species.
The reaction proceeds via the transfer of a methyl radical to the aromatic ring.
Scientists detected a free methyl radical using electron spin resonance spectroscopy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: methyl group - CH3 group attached in organic molecules

Etymology

From 'methyl', itself from French 'méthyle', and 'radical', Latin 'radix' meaning root.
