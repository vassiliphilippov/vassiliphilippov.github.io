rosid dicot family

1. NOUN

/ ˈroʊsɪd daɪˈkɑt ˈfæməli / (AmE), / ˈrəʊsɪd daɪˈkɒt ˈfæmɪli / (BrE)
any of several families of flowering plants that belong to the rosid group of dicotyledons, characterized by specific floral and structural features
family of rosid dicotyledonous plants

Examples

The legume family is a well-known rosid dicot family that includes beans and peas.
Botanists studied the unique features of this rosid dicot family to understand its evolution.
Many economically important crops belong to a rosid dicot family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rosid - a large clade of dicotyledonous flowering plants

Etymology

From 'rosid', referring to a major clade within dicotyledons, and 'family', a taxonomic rank.
