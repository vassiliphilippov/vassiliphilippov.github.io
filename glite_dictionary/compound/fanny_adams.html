Fanny Adams

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæni ˈædəmz / (AmE), / ˈfæni ˈædəmz / (BrE)
(British, informal) Used to mean nothing at all, or very little; often in the phrase 'sweet Fanny Adams'.
nothing at all; very little
informal british

Examples

He worked for hours but ended up with Fanny Adams to show for it.
I know Fanny Adams about cars, so I can't help you with that.
They promised us a bonus, but in the end we got Fanny Adams.

Synset Examples

I asked for a raise and they gave me bugger-all
I know sweet Fanny Adams about surgery

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bugger-all - nothing at all (British slang)
  • Synonym: zilch - nothing; zero (informal)

Etymology

British slang, originally referring to a murder victim (Fanny Adams, 1867), later euphemistically used to mean 'nothing at all'.
