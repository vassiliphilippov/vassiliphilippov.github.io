knee-jerk reflex

1. NOUN

/ ˈniː ˌdʒɜrk rɪˈflɛks / (AmE), / ˈniː ˌdʒɜːk rɪˈflɛks / (BrE)
a sudden, automatic extension of the lower leg caused by striking the patellar tendon just below the kneecap
automatic leg-jerk on striking the knee
medical

Examples

The doctor tested my knee-jerk reflex by tapping just below my kneecap.
Children often find it amusing to watch their knee-jerk reflex in action during checkups.
Loss of the knee-jerk reflex can be a sign of nerve damage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: patellar reflex - the same reflex as the knee-jerk reflex, triggered at the knee
