blue elder

1. NOUN

/ bluː ˈɛldər / (AmE), / bluː ˈɛldə / (BrE)
A shrub or small tree native to the western United States, with white flowers and blue berries, the fruit of which is used in wines and jellies.
shrub/tree with blue berries, western US

Examples

We found a blue elder growing by the stream, heavy with ripe berries.
Local wineries use blue elder fruit to produce unique berry wines.
The blue elder blooms in early summer with clusters of fragrant white flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elderberry - the berry of the elder tree, used in food and drink
