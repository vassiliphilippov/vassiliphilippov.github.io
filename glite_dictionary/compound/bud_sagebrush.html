bud sagebrush

1. NOUN

/ bʌd ˈseɪdʒˌbrʌʃ / (AmE), / bʌd ˈseɪdʒˌbrʌʃ / (BrE)
A perennial shrub native to the western United States, valued as forage for sheep.
shrubby plant used as sheep forage

Examples

The sheep grazed among the bud sagebrush in the open rangeland.
Bud sagebrush is an important plant for sustaining livestock during dry months.
Scientists are studying how bud sagebrush adapts to arid environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sagebrush - common bushy plants of the genus Artemisia
