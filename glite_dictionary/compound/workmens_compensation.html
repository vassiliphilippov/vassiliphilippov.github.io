workmen's compensation

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɝːkmənz ˌkɑːmpənˈseɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈwɜːkmənz ˌkɒmpənˈseɪʃən / (BrE)
money paid to a worker or their family for injury or death caused by an accident at work as required by law
compensation for work-related injury or death
legal

Examples

Due to her injury at the factory, she filed a workmen's compensation claim.
In most states, workmen's compensation provides benefits regardless of who was at fault.
He received workmen's compensation after the workplace accident left him unable to work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: workers' compensation - legal compensation for employees injured at work
