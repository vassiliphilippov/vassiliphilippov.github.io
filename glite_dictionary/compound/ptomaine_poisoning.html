ptomaine poisoning

1. NOUN

/ toʊˌmeɪn ˈpɔɪzənɪŋ / (AmE), / təʊˌmeɪn ˈpɔɪzənɪŋ / (BrE)
(archaic) Food poisoning, especially as formerly believed to be caused by the ingestion of ptomaines.
archaic term for food poisoning
archaic medical

Examples

In the early twentieth century, many cases of foodborne illness were incorrectly attributed to ptomaine poisoning.
The term ptomaine poisoning has largely disappeared from medical literature.
Some older cookbooks warn about the dangers of ptomaine poisoning from improperly stored food.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: food poisoning - illness caused by eating contaminated food

Etymology

From 'ptomaine', coined from Italian 'ptomaina' (corpse), from Greek 'ptōma' (fall, corpse), plus 'poisoning'.
