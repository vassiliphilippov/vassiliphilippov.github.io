barium protoxide

1. NOUN

/ ˈber.i.əm ˈproʊ.tɑk.saɪd / (AmE), / ˈbær.i.əm ˈprəʊ.tɒk.saɪd / (BrE)
a whitish, toxic powder composed of barium and oxygen; an oxide of barium used in chemical processes
a whitish toxic oxide of barium
medical

Examples

The laboratory handles barium protoxide only under strict safety procedures due to its toxicity.
Barium protoxide can react with water to form barium hydroxide.
The chemist measured the amount of barium protoxide produced in the reaction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barium oxide - an oxide of barium; another name for barium protoxide

Etymology

From barium (a chemical element) and protoxide ('proto-' meaning first or primary; an oxide with the lowest proportion of oxygen).
