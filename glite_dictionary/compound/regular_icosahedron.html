regular icosahedron

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛɡjələr aɪˌkoʊsəˈhiːdrən / (AmE), / ˈrɛɡjʊlə aɪˌkɒsəˈhiːdrən / (BrE)
A three-dimensional solid with twenty identical equilateral triangle faces.
3D solid with 20 equilateral triangle faces

Examples

The regular icosahedron is one of the five Platonic solids in geometry.
Each face of a regular icosahedron is an equilateral triangle, making it highly symmetrical.
Architects sometimes draw inspiration from the regular icosahedron for futuristic building designs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: icosahedron - a polyhedron with twenty faces

Etymology

From Greek eikosi (twenty) + hedra (seat, base), describing a figure with twenty faces.
