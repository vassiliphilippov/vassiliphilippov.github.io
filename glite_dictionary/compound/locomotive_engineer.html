locomotive engineer

1. NOUN

/ ˌloʊ.kəˈmoʊ.t̬ɪv ˌɛn.dʒəˈnɪr / (AmE), / ˌləʊ.kəˈməʊ.tɪv ˌɛn.dʒɪˈnɪə / (BrE)
a person who operates a railway locomotive; a train driver
operator of a railway locomotive

Examples

The locomotive engineer checked the controls before starting the morning express.
After twenty years on the job, he became the senior locomotive engineer at the rail company.
Safety is always a top priority for a locomotive engineer operating long-distance trains.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: engine driver - person who drives a railway engine (chiefly British)

Etymology

From 'locomotive' (from Latin 'locus' (place) + 'motivus' (moving)) and 'engineer' (from Old French 'engigneor', from 'engine').
