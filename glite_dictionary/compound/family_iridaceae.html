family Iridaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ˌaɪrɪˈdeɪsiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli ˌɪrɪˈdeɪsiː / (BrE)
a large family of perennial herbaceous plants, usually with rhizomes, corms, or bulbs, including irises and related genera
plant family including irises and relatives

Examples

Several ornamental plants in the family Iridaceae are popular in gardens worldwide.
Crocuses and irises both belong to the family Iridaceae.
The family Iridaceae includes many species with brightly colored flowers.

Etymology

From modern Latin 'Iridaceae', based on 'Iris', the type genus, and the suffix '-aceae' meaning 'family'.
