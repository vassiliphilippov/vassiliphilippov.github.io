dwarf chinkapin oak

1. NOUN

/ dwɔrf ˈtʃɪŋkəpɪn oʊk / (AmE), / dwɔːf ˈtʃɪŋkəpɪn əʊk / (BrE)
a deciduous shrubby tree native to northeastern and central United States, producing a sweet edible nut and often forming dense thickets
deciduous shrubby oak tree of U.S., edible nut

Examples

The dwarf chinkapin oak produces nuts that are sweet and edible.
Dense thickets of dwarf chinkapin oak provide shelter for wildlife.
Gardeners value the dwarf chinkapin oak for its adaptability and attractive appearance.

Related Terms
