white knight

1. NOUN

/ waɪt naɪt / (AmE), / waɪt naɪt / (BrE)
a company or individual that acquires another company facing a hostile takeover, offering a more favorable alternative to current management or shareholders
friendly acquirer in hostile takeover

Examples

The struggling firm welcomed a white knight to protect it from being taken over by its rival.
When the hostile bid was announced, the board quickly sought a white knight to make a counteroffer.
A white knight stepped in at the last moment, saving the company from an aggressive takeover.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: black knight - a hostile or unfriendly acquirer in a business takeover

Etymology

From the image of a heroic knight in shining armor, popularized by medieval romance literature and later extended metaphorically to business in the late 20th century.

2. NOUN

/ waɪt naɪt / (AmE), / waɪt naɪt / (BrE)
A person who rescues or defends someone in difficulty, especially a company at risk of takeover or collapse; a benefactor or savior.
a savior or rescuer, especially in business

Examples

The struggling company was saved when a white knight investor stepped in at the last moment.
She acted as a white knight, offering support to the local charity when it was running out of funds.
In the face of a hostile takeover, the board searched desperately for a white knight to protect the firm.

Synset Examples

She played the white knight, defending the underdog.
He acted as a white knight by rescuing the failing business.
The investor was seen as a white knight for the startup.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: savior - a person who saves someone or something from danger or difficulty
  • Synonym: rescuer - a person who saves someone from a dangerous or difficult situation
  • Antonym: black knight - in business, a hostile party or corporate raider, as opposed to a white knight

Etymology

Metaphorically from the image of a chivalrous knight in shining white armor; 'knight' from Old English 'cniht' (boy, servant).
