detachment of the retina

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈtætʃmənt əv ðə rɪˈtaɪnə / (AmE), / dɪˈtætʃmənt əv ði rɪˈtiːnə / (BrE)
a medical condition in which the retina separates from the layer of blood vessels at the back of the eye, potentially causing vision loss
medical: retina separates from back of eye
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with a detachment of the retina and scheduled for urgent surgery.
Early symptoms of detachment of the retina can include flashes of light and sudden vision loss.
If left untreated, detachment of the retina can lead to permanent blindness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: retinal detachment - medical condition where the retina peels away from its underlying layer
  • Degree Lower: cataract - clouding of the lens in the eye
