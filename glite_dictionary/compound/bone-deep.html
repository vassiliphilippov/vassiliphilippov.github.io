bone-deep

1. ADJ

/ boʊnˈdiːp / (AmE), / bəʊnˈdiːp / (BrE)
Extremely deep or intense; affecting one to the core or to the innermost level.
extremely deep or intense

Examples

The cold was so bone-deep that no amount of clothing helped.
Her sadness was bone-deep after hearing the news.
He felt a bone-deep exhaustion after working for twelve hours straight.

Synset Examples

The cold wind felt bone-deep as we walked outside.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: profound - very great or intense
