square kilometre

1. NOUN

/ skwɛr kəˈlɑməˌtɚ / (AmE), / skwɛː kɪˈlɒmɪtə / (BrE)
a unit of area equal to the area of a square with sides 1 kilometre in length (1,000,000 square metres)
unit of area (1,000,000 sq m)

Examples

The national park covers over 5,000 square kilometres of pristine wilderness.
Only one square kilometre of forest remains after years of logging.
The city expanded rapidly, growing from 12 to 30 square kilometres in just two decades.

Synset Examples

The area enclosed by the walls of many European medieval cities were about one square kilometre.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: square meter - a unit of area equal to a square with sides of one meter

Etymology

From square, referring to a square shape, and kilometre, from French "kilomètre" (see kilo- and metre).
