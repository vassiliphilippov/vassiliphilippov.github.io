family Electrophoridae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ɪˌlɛktrəˈfɔːrɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli ɪˌlɛktrəˈfɔːrɪdiː / (BrE)
A small family of fishes comprising the electric eels, classified in scientific taxonomy.
taxonomic family including electric eels

Examples

The family Electrophoridae is known for its ability to generate strong electric currents.
Researchers studied members of the family Electrophoridae in the rivers of South America.
All species in the family Electrophoridae are adapted to life in murky freshwater environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: electric eel - a large, freshwater fish capable of generating electric shocks

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, with 'Electrophorus' (genus name) from Greek 'ēlektron' (amber, electric) + 'phoros' (bearing), and '-idae' (family suffix).
