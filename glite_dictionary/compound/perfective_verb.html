perfective verb

1. NOUN

/ pərˈfɛktɪv vɜrb / (AmE), / pəˈfɛktɪv vɜːb / (BrE)
a verb form or aspect that expresses an action as completed or fully accomplished, often emphasizing the result or conclusion of the action
verb form showing completed action

Examples

In Russian, the choice between an perfective verb and an imperfective verb changes the meaning of the sentence.
Learners of Polish often struggle to use the correct perfective verb in context.
The perfective verb form marks the action as finished or fully achieved.

Synset Examples

Perfective verbs are commonly formed from imperfective ones by the addition of a prefix, or else the imperfective verb is formed from the perfective one by modification of the stem or ending.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: imperfective verb - a verb form or aspect expressing an ongoing, habitual, or incomplete action

Etymology

From Latin perfectivus (effected, completed), from perfectus (completed, finished), from perficere (to finish, accomplish) + verb.
