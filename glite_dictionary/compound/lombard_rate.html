Lombard rate

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɑːmbɑrd reɪt / (AmE), / ˈlɒmbɑːd reɪt / (BrE)
the interest rate charged by a central bank for very short-term loans to other banks, secured by approved collateral
central bank interest rate for secured short-term loans

Examples

The Lombard rate is typically set above the central bank's main refinancing rate.
Due to market instability, the central bank decided to increase the Lombard rate by half a percentage point.
Many banks rely on short-term funding at the Lombard rate in times of liquidity shortage.

Synset Examples

Lending is via central banks, in particular the securities ‘eligible for collateral’ which are registered on lists; as a general rule, the Lombard rate (interest rate) is more or less one per cent above discount rate.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: discount rate - interest rate charged by central banks for loans to commercial banks without collateral
  • Synonym: interest rate - the percentage charged for borrowing money

Etymology

From Lombard, after the Lombard bankers of medieval Italy known for lending on security.
