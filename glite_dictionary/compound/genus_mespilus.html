genus Mespilus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˈmɛspɪləs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈmɛspɪləs / (BrE)
A small genus of flowering plants in the rose family, including the medlar.
genus of flowering plants (medlars)

Examples

The genus Mespilus is best known for the common medlar, a fruit-bearing tree.
Botanists classify the medlar under the genus Mespilus, though its classification has sometimes been debated.
There is only one widely recognized species in the genus Mespilus, making it a monotypic genus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: medlar - a small tree known for its edible fruit; member of genus Mespilus

Etymology

From Latin 'Mespilus' (medlar tree).
