foramen magnum

1. NOUN

/ fəˈreɪmən ˈmæɡnəm / (AmE), / fəˈreɪmən ˈmaɡnəm / (BrE)
the large opening at the base of the skull through which the spinal cord passes
large opening at base of skull
medical

Examples

The spinal cord connects to the brainstem by passing through the foramen magnum.
A fracture extending to the foramen magnum can be extremely serious.
During an autopsy, the pathologist examined the foramen magnum for signs of trauma.

Etymology

From Latin 'foramen' (an opening) and 'magnum' (large).
