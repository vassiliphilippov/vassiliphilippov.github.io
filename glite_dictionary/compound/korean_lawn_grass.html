Korean lawn grass

1. NOUN

/ kəˈriːən lɔn ɡræs / (AmE), / kəˈriːən lɔːn ɡrɑːs / (BrE)
a species of turfgrass native to East Asia and often used for lawns, also cultivated in the United States
species of turfgrass used for lawns

Examples

Many homeowners in southern states choose Korean lawn grass for its drought tolerance.
Korean lawn grass requires less mowing compared to other types of turf.
The groundskeeper recommended seeding the field with Korean lawn grass to achieve a lush, green surface.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Zoysia japonica - the botanical name for Korean lawn grass
