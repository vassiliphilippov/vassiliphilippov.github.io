white thistle

1. NOUN

/ waɪt ˈθɪsəl / (AmE), / waɪt ˈθɪsəl / (BrE)
a spiny shrub with silvery, scurfy foliage, native to the alkaline plains of the southwestern United States and Mexico
spiny shrub with silvery foliage

Examples

The arid landscape was dotted with white thistle thriving in the alkaline soil.
Botanists studied the unique characteristics of the white thistle during their field trip in New Mexico.
The silvery leaves of the white thistle make it easy to spot among other desert plants.

2. NOUN

/ waɪt ˈθɪsəl / (AmE), / waɪt ˈθɪsəl / (BrE)
A plant of the genus Argemone, chiefly found in tropical America, having large white or yellow flowers and prickly leaves, stems, and pods.
prickly plant of genus Argemone

Examples

The garden was overrun with white thistle, its prickly leaves making it hard to clear.
Local farmers often consider white thistle a troublesome weed because of its rapid growth.
The large white flowers of the white thistle brightened the edge of the tropical field.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prickly poppy - common name for Argemone species with prickly parts and poppy-like flowers
  • Degree Higher: thistle - plant with prickly leaves or stems, especially in the family Asteraceae
