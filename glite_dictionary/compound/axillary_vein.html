axillary vein

1. NOUN

/ ˈæksəˌleri veɪn / (AmE), / ˈæksɪləri veɪn / (BrE)
a large vein in the armpit region that is formed by the union of the basilic and brachial veins and continues as the subclavian vein
major vein in the armpit area
medical

Examples

The surgeon carefully avoided the axillary vein during the procedure.
An injury to the axillary vein can cause significant bleeding and complications.
The axillary vein drains blood from the upper limb back toward the heart.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: subclavian vein - vein that the axillary vein becomes as it passes the first rib
  • Degree Lower: basilic vein - vein that contributes to the axillary vein
  • Degree Lower: brachial vein - vein that combines to form the axillary vein

Etymology

From Latin 'axilla' (armpit) + 'vein'.
