pineal eye

1. NOUN

/ paɪˈniːəl aɪ / (AmE), / pɪˈniːəl aɪ / (BrE)
a light-sensitive organ located on the top of the head in certain reptiles and other animals, functioning as a primitive eye
light-sensitive organ in some reptiles

Examples

Some lizards have a pineal eye on top of their heads that helps regulate their circadian rhythms.
The pineal eye is capable of detecting changes in light intensity, aiding some reptiles in thermal regulation.
Scientists have studied the pineal eye to understand its role in the evolution of vision.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parietal eye - a light-sensitive organ similar to the pineal eye in some vertebrates

Etymology

From 'pineal', relating to the pineal gland, from Latin 'pīnea' (pine cone), and 'eye'.
