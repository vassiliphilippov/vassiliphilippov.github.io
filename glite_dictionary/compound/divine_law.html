divine law

1. NOUN

/ dəˈvaɪn lɔ / (AmE), / dɪˈvaɪn lɔː / (BrE)
a law regarded as coming directly from God, especially as distinct from human-made laws
law believed to come from God

Examples

Many people base their ethical beliefs on divine law rather than secular legislation.
The concept of divine law plays a central role in several world religions.
Debates often arise where divine law appears to conflict with human law.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: natural law - a philosophy asserting that certain rights or values are inherent by virtue of human nature and universally cognizable through human reason
