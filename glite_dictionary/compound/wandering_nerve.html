wandering nerve

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɑndərɪŋ nɜrv / (AmE), / ˈwɒndərɪŋ nɜːv / (BrE)
another term for the vagus nerve; a mixed nerve that supplies the pharynx, larynx, lungs, heart, esophagus, stomach, and most abdominal organs
another name for the vagus nerve
medical

Examples

In anatomy class, we learned that the wandering nerve plays a crucial role in controlling the heart rate.
Damage to the wandering nerve can affect swallowing, speech, and breathing functions.
The surgeon carefully avoided the wandering nerve during the operation to prevent complications.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vagus nerve - the tenth cranial nerve that interfaces with parasympathetic control of the heart, lungs, and digestive tract
