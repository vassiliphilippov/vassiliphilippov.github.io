chaparral cock

1. NOUN

/ tʃəˌpærəl kɑk / (AmE), / tʃəˌpærəl kɒk / (BrE)
a fast-moving, ground-dwelling bird native to the southwestern United States and Mexico, also known as the roadrunner
another name for the roadrunner bird

Examples

The chaparral cock darted across the dusty road, barely visible in a blur of feathers.
Locals often refer to the roadrunner as a chaparral cock due to its preferred habitat.
We spotted a chaparral cock hunting for insects near the cacti during our hike.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: roadrunner - a bird noted for its speed and distinctive appearance, also called chaparral cock

Etymology

From Spanish 'chaparral' (thicket) and 'cock' (male bird), referencing its habitat and appearance.
