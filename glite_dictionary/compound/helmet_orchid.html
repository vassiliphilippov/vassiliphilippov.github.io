helmet orchid

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛlmɪt ˈɔrˌkɪd / (AmE), / ˈhɛlmɪt ˈɔːkɪd / (BrE)
Any of several orchids of the genus Coryanthes, noted for having waxy, musky-scented flowers with a helmet-shaped lip.
orchid genus with helmet-shaped flowers

Examples

The rare helmet orchid thrives in humid, tropical environments across South America.
Botanists were excited to find a blooming helmet orchid during their field study.
The distinctive lip of the helmet orchid sets it apart from other species in the genus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orchid - a plant with complex, showy flowers of the family Orchidaceae

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɛlmɪt ˌɔrkɪd / (AmE), / ˈhɛlmɪt ˌɔːkɪd / (BrE)
Any of various terrestrial orchids of the genus Pterostylis, having a basal rosette of leaves and hood-shaped green flowers often striped with purple, brown, or red.
a terrestrial orchid of the genus Pterostylis

Examples

The helmet orchid thrives in damp, shaded forests in Australia and New Zealand.
Botanists discovered a rare species of helmet orchid during their field trip.
The distinctive hooded flowers make the helmet orchid a favorite among native wildflower enthusiasts.
