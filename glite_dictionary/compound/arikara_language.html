Arikara language

1. NOUN

/ əˈrɪkərə ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / əˈrɪkərə ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Caddoan language historically spoken by the Arikara people of North America
language of the Arikara people

Examples

Few people today are fluent in the Arikara language, making preservation efforts vital.
The Arikara language is taught in some schools to help younger generations connect with their heritage.
Linguists have documented the Arikara language as part of studies on Caddoan languages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Caddoan language - a family of Native American languages of the central United States

Etymology

Arikara, a Native American people; language name derived from the ethnonym.
