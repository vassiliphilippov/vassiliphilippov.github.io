brownie point

1. NOUN

/ ˈbraʊni pɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˈbraʊni pɔɪnt / (BrE)
an informal mark of approval or credit given for doing something helpful or considerate
credit earned for a good deed
informal

Examples

She hoped to earn a brownie point by helping her boss finish the task.
Bringing in coffee for the team scored him a brownie point with his coworkers.
Doing extra chores around the house can win you some serious brownie points.

Synset Examples

He earned a brownie point for helping his coworker.
She got a brownie point for finishing the project early.
Volunteering earned him a few brownie points at work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: credit - recognition received for work or good deeds

Etymology

Origin uncertain; likely from the merit system used by the Brownies (junior Girl Scouts) or possibly from 1950s American slang.
