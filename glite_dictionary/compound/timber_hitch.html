timber hitch

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɪmbər hɪtʃ / (AmE), / ˈtɪmbə hɪtʃ / (BrE)
a knot used to attach a rope to a log or spar, typically for hauling or lifting
a knot for securing rope to logs or spars

Examples

He used a timber hitch to secure the rope around the log before dragging it away.
When felling trees, a timber hitch is often used to attach ropes to trunks for removal.
Make sure your timber hitch is tied correctly, or the log may slip loose.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hitch - a type of knot for fastening a rope to an object
