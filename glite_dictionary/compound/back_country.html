back country

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæk ˌkʌntri / (AmE), / ˈbæk ˌkʌntri / (BrE)
an area that is remote, sparsely populated, and largely undeveloped, typically wild or wilderness land away from towns and roads
remote undeveloped area; wilderness

Examples

We spent a week hiking through the back country, far from any signs of civilization.
Access to clean water can be difficult when traveling in the back country.
The park ranger warned us about bears in the back country during the summer months.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wilderness - an uncultivated, uninhabited, and inhospitable region
  • Synonym: outback - remote, sparsely inhabited inland regions, especially in Australia
  • Antonym: settled area - an area that has been developed and populated
