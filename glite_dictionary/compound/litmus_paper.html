litmus paper

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪtməs ˌpeɪpər / (AmE), / ˈlɪtməs ˌpeɪpə / (BrE)
thin paper treated with litmus, used to test whether a substance is acidic or alkaline by changing color
indicator paper for acidity or alkalinity

Examples

The scientist used litmus paper to determine if the solution was acidic.
If you dip blue litmus paper into vinegar, it will turn red.
Please hand me some litmus paper for the chemistry experiment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indicator paper - paper used to indicate pH or specific substances

Etymology

From 'litmus', from Middle English, via Old Norse 'litmosi' (dye moss); 'paper' of Latin origin.
