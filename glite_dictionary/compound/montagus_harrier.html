Montagu's harrier

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑn.təˌɡyuz ˈhɛr.i.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈmɒn.təˌɡjuːz ˈhæɹ.i.ə / (BrE)
a medium-sized, slender European harrier (Circus pygargus) with brownish plumage, found in open country and marshes
a European species of harrier bird

Examples

We spotted a Montagu's harrier gliding gracefully over the fields during our birdwatching trip.
The male Montagu's harrier is easily recognized by its slimmer build and grey plumage.
Conservation efforts are underway to protect the nesting sites of the Montagu's harrier in southern Europe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hen harrier - a similar species of harrier found in Europe and Asia
  • Degree Higher: marsh harrier - a related species of harrier inhabiting wetlands

Etymology

Named after British naturalist George Montagu (1753–1815) who described the species.
