Santa Claus

1. PROPN

/ ˈsæn.tə klɔz / (AmE), / ˈsantə klɔːz / (BrE)
A legendary figure said to bring gifts to children on Christmas Eve; the patron saint of Christmas cheer.
legendary figure who brings Christmas gifts

Examples

Every Christmas Eve, children eagerly await a visit from Santa Claus.
Many families leave milk and cookies out for Santa Claus as part of their holiday tradition.
My little brother wrote a letter to Santa Claus asking for his favorite toy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Father Christmas - British term for the person who delivers presents at Christmas
  • Synonym: Saint Nicholas - The historical figure and origin of the Santa Claus legend

Etymology

From Dutch 'Sinterklaas', a contraction of 'Sint Nikolaas' (Saint Nicholas), via American English.

2. PROPN

/ ˈsæn.tə klɔz / (AmE), / ˈsæn.tə klɔːz / (BrE)
a legendary figure, depicted as a jolly old man in red, who delivers gifts to children at Christmas
legendary Christmas gift-giver

Examples

Many children write letters to Santa Claus every December.
The tradition says that Santa Claus travels in a sleigh pulled by reindeer.
When I was young, I believed Santa Claus left presents under my tree.

Synset Examples

Yes, Virginia, there is a Santa Claus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Father Christmas - the British equivalent of Santa Claus
  • Synonym: Saint Nicholas - the historical figure on which Santa Claus is based

Etymology

From Dutch 'Sinterklaas,' a colloquial form of 'Sint Nikolaas' (Saint Nicholas).
