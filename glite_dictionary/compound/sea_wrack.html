sea wrack

1. NOUN

/ si ˈræk / (AmE), / siː ˈræk / (BrE)
Large marine vegetation, such as kelp and rockweed, especially when washed up on shore.
marine vegetation like kelp and rockweed

Examples

After the storm, the beach was covered in sea wrack and driftwood.
Fishermen use sea wrack as fertilizer for their fields.
The tide left piles of sea wrack along the coastline.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kelp - large brown seaweed
  • Synonym: rockweed - a type of brown seaweed often found on rocky shores

Etymology

From 'sea' + 'wrack', with 'wrack' meaning seaweed, from Old English 'wræc' (seaweed, wreck).

2. NOUN

/ si ˈræk / (AmE), / si ˈræk / (BrE)
a type of marine plant, especially any of several kinds of seaweed with very long, narrow leaves, commonly found washed ashore along North Atlantic coasts
marine plant or seaweed found on shores

Examples

The storm left piles of sea wrack along the beach, attracting seabirds searching for food.
Children collected colorful shells and sea wrack to decorate their sandcastles.
After high tide, the smell of decaying sea wrack drifted across the coastal village.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seaweed - general term for marine algae or aquatic plants found in the sea

Etymology

From 'sea' + Old Norse 'vrak' (wreckage, seaweed).
