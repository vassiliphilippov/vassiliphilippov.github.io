to broach the subject

1. VERB

/ broʊtʃ ðə ˈsʌbdʒɪkt / (AmE), / brəʊtʃ ðə ˈsʌbdʒɪkt / (BrE)
to bring up or introduce a topic for discussion, especially one that may be sensitive or difficult
introduce a topic for discussion

Examples

She decided to broach the subject of moving to another city during dinner.
It's always awkward to broach the subject of salary with a potential employer.
Before we start, I need to broach the subject of next week's deadlines.

Synset Examples

It's hard to broach the subject of money with her.
We need to broach the topic carefully during the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to raise the issue - to bring up a topic for discussion

Etymology

From Middle English 'brochen' (to pierce, tap a cask), later extended to 'bring up for discussion'.
