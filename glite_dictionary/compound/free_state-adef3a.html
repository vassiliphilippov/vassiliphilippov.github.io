free state

1. NOUN

/ fri ˈsteɪt / (AmE), / friː ˈsteɪt / (BrE)
(in U.S. history) a state where slavery was prohibited before the American Civil War.
U.S. historical state without slavery

Examples

Before the Civil War, Missouri was a slave state while Illinois was a free state.
Abolitionists often helped enslaved people escape to a free state in the North.
The divide between slave states and free states influenced national politics for decades.
