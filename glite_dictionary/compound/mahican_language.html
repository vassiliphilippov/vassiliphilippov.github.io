Mahican language

1. NOUN

/ məˈhiːkən ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / məˈhiːkən ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the extinct Algonquian language formerly spoken by the Mohican people of the northeastern United States
the language of the Mohican people

Examples

Linguists have studied the Mahican language to learn more about Northeast native cultures.
The last fluent speakers of the Mahican language lived in the early 20th century.
Efforts are underway to revive some words from the Mahican language for educational projects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mohican - a member of an Algonquian-speaking Native American people, historically from the Hudson River Valley

Etymology

From Mahican, the name of the people, of uncertain origin; see also Algonquian.
