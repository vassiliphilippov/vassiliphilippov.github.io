game of pong

1. NOUN

/ ɡeɪm əv pɑŋ / (AmE), / ɡeɪm əv pɒŋ / (BrE)
a simple video game in which players use paddles to hit a ball back and forth across the screen
simple video game with bouncing ball and paddles

Examples

Every Friday evening, we challenge each other to a game of pong in the basement.
The old computer still works, and we can load a game of pong on it.
After dinner, the kids played a game of pong while the adults talked.

Synset Examples

We played a quick game of pong before dinner.

Etymology

From the name of the original 1972 arcade video game 'Pong', imitating the sound of a ball hitting a surface.
