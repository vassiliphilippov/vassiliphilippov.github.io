Temple of Solomon

1. PROPN

/ ˈtɛmpəl əv ˈsɑləmən / (AmE), / ˈtɛmpəl əv ˈsɒləmən / (BrE)
the first Jewish temple in Jerusalem, traditionally built by King Solomon in the 10th century BC, and regarded as a central place of worship until its destruction; also used to refer to successive temples rebuilt on the same site.
ancient Jewish temple in Jerusalem

Examples

Many biblical scholars study the history of the Temple of Solomon to better understand ancient Judaism.
Pilgrims visited the Temple of Solomon for centuries before it was destroyed.
Legends state that immense treasures were hidden within the Temple of Solomon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Wailing Wall - the remaining western wall of the Temple complex
