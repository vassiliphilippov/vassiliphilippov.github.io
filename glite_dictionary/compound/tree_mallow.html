tree mallow

1. NOUN

/ tri ˈmæloʊ / (AmE), / tri ˈmaləʊ / (BrE)
A perennial shrub with palmately lobed furry leaves and showy red-purple flowers, native to the southwestern United States.
a shrub with lobed leaves and showy flowers

Examples

The tree mallow produces vibrant red-purple flowers each spring.
Gardeners in arid regions often choose tree mallow for its drought resistance.
A row of tree mallows lined the edge of the property, attracting bees and butterflies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mallow - any of various plants of the genus Malva
