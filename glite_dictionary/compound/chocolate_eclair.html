chocolate eclair

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɑklət ɪˈklɛr / (AmE), / ˈtʃɒklət eɪˈklɛə / (BrE)
A long, thin pastry made with choux dough, filled with cream, and topped with chocolate icing.
pastry filled with cream and topped with chocolate

Examples

She ordered a chocolate eclair from the bakery for her dessert.
The chocolate eclair was filled with rich vanilla cream and topped with glossy icing.
At the café, he enjoyed a coffee and a fresh chocolate eclair.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eclair - a long pastry filled with cream

Etymology

From French 'éclair' (lightning), named for being eaten quickly; 'chocolate' refers to the topping.
