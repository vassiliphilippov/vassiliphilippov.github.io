purple-staining Cortinarius

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɝːpəl ˌsteɪnɪŋ kɔːrˈtɪnəriəs / (AmE), / ˈpɜːpl ˌsteɪnɪŋ kɔːˈtɪnəriəs / (BrE)
a species of mushroom with a smooth, slimy, reddish-purple cap, close violet gills, and all parts staining dark purple when bruised
a purple-staining species of mushroom

Examples

The purple-staining Cortinarius is easily recognized by its deep violet cap and gills.
Mushroom hunters should be cautious when handling purple-staining Cortinarius, as all parts bruise dark purple.
Experts recommend identifying the purple-staining Cortinarius by its slimy surface and color changes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cortinarius - a large genus of mushrooms distinguished by a web-like veil
