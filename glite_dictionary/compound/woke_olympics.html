woke Olympics

1. NOUN

/ woʊk əˈlɪmpɪks / (AmE), / wəʊk əˈlɪmpɪks / (BrE)
Used, often satirically, to describe an event, competition, or showcase where participants display or promote social justice and progressive values, sometimes criticized for prioritizing messaging over substance or entertainment.
event overly focused on social justice messaging
informal slang

Examples

Some commentators mocked the award show as a woke Olympics, saying it cared more about activism than artistry.
After watching the opening ceremony, viewers joked that the woke Olympics had replaced the real event.
The festival was dubbed the woke Olympics because every performance focused on social justice messages.

Synset Examples

She referred to the event as the woke Olympics, pointing out its agenda.
The woke Olympics showcased performances highlighting social justice.
Critics claimed the woke Olympics prioritized messaging over entertainment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: virtue signalling - The action or practice of publicly expressing opinions to demonstrate one's good character or moral correctness on a particular issue
  • Synonym: political correctness - The avoidance of language or behavior that could offend particular groups of people, often used critically

Etymology

From 'woke', 20th-century African-American slang for social awareness, combined with 'Olympics' to evoke competition or performative exhibition.
