trifoliolate leaf

1. NOUN

/ traɪˈfoʊliəˌleɪt liːf / (AmE), / traɪˈfəʊliəˌleɪt liːf / (BrE)
A leaf divided into three distinct leaflets.
leaf with three leaflets

Examples

The clover is a common plant with a trifoliolate leaf.
Botanists easily identified the species by its trifoliolate leaf structure.
Each trifoliolate leaf consists of three distinct leaflets attached to a single stem.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: compound leaf - a leaf composed of multiple leaflets

Etymology

From Latin 'tri-' (three) + 'foliolum' (a small leaf).
