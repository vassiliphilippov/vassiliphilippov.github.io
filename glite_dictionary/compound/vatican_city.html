Vatican City

1. PROPN

/ ˌvætɪkən ˈsɪti / (AmE), / ˌvætɪkən ˈsɪti / (BrE)
an independent city-state enclaved within Rome, serving as the headquarters of the Roman Catholic Church and the residence of the Pope
independent city-state, headquarters of the Catholic Church

Examples

Many tourists visit Vatican City each year to see St. Peter's Basilica and the Sistine Chapel.
The Pope lives and works in Vatican City, a tiny sovereign state within Rome.
Diplomatic relations with Vatican City are maintained by many countries around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Holy See - the jurisdiction of the Pope as leader of the Catholic Church

Etymology

From Latin 'Vaticanus' (Vatican Hill) combined with English 'City'.
