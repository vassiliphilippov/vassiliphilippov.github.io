elevated railway

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛləˌveɪtɪd ˈrɛlˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˌɛlɪˌveɪtɪd ˈreɪl.weɪ / (BrE)
a railway that is powered by electricity and runs on a track raised above street level, typically in urban areas
railway system raised above the street

Examples

The city rebuilt its old elevated railway to reduce street-level congestion.
Tourists enjoyed the view from the elevated railway as it passed through downtown.
Noise from the elevated railway can be heard several blocks away.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: subway - an underground railway system, especially in a city
  • Synonym: monorail - a railway in which the track consists of a single rail
