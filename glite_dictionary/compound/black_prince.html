Black Prince

1. PROPN

/ blæk ˈprɪns / (AmE), / blak ˈprɪns / (BrE)
the eldest son of Edward III of England (1330–1376), famed for his victories over the French at the battles of Crécy and Poitiers during the Hundred Years' War
son of Edward III, English military leader

Examples

The Black Prince was celebrated for his military prowess during the Hundred Years' War.
Many historians debate why Edward acquired the nickname Black Prince.
The tomb of the Black Prince can still be seen in Canterbury Cathedral.

Etymology

Nickname believed to refer to his distinctive black armor or coat worn in battle.
