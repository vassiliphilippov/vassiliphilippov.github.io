mineral wool

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪnərəl wʊl / (AmE), / ˈmɪnərəl wʊl / (BrE)
A light, fibrous material made from mineral substances, used primarily as thermal or acoustic insulation.
fibrous insulating material

Examples

The attic was packed with mineral wool to keep the house warm in winter.
Builders often choose mineral wool for its excellent fire resistance and soundproofing qualities.
Before installing drywall, contractors placed mineral wool in the wall cavities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fiberglass - a fibrous glass material used similarly for insulation
  • Synonym: insulation - material used to prevent heat, sound, or electricity from escaping
