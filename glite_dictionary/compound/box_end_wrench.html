box end wrench

1. NOUN

/ bɑks ɛnd rɛntʃ / (AmE), / bɒks end rɛntʃ / (BrE)
a wrench with a closed, ring-shaped end that fits over the head of a nut or bolt
wrench with a closed, ring-shaped end

Examples

To loosen the bolt, you may need a box end wrench for better grip.
He bought a new set of box end wrenches for his garage workshop.
A box end wrench is often preferred for tightening hexagonal nuts securely.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: open end wrench - a wrench with a U-shaped opening that grips two opposite faces of the bolt or nut
  • Synonym: spanner - British term for a wrench
