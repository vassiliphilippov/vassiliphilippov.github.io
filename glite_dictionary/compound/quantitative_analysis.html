quantitative analysis

1. NOUN

/ ˌkwɑːn.t̬ə.teɪ.t̬ɪv əˈnæl.ə.sɪs / (AmE), / ˌkwɒn.tɪ.tə.tɪv əˈnæl.ɪ.sɪs / (BrE)
The determination of the amount or proportion of each element or compound present in a substance, especially by chemical means.
chemical measurement of substance components
medical

Examples

The laboratory performed a quantitative analysis to measure the concentration of lead in the water sample.
Accurate quantitative analysis is essential for determining the purity of pharmaceutical drugs.
He specialized in quantitative analysis of trace elements in geological samples.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: qualitative analysis - analysis to determine what substances are present, not how much

Etymology

From Latin 'quantitativus' (relating to quantity) and Greek 'analusis' (loosening, analysis).
