tympanic cavity

1. NOUN

/ tɪmˈpænɪk ˈkævəti / (AmE), / tɪmˈpænɪk ˈkævəti / (BrE)
the main cavity in the middle ear, located between the eardrum and the inner ear, containing the auditory ossicles
middle ear cavity between eardrum and inner ear
medical

Examples

The tympanic cavity houses the tiny bones essential for hearing.
Infections can sometimes spread to the tympanic cavity, leading to serious complications.
During surgery, doctors examined the tympanic cavity for signs of damage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: middle ear - the part of the ear between the eardrum and the inner ear

Etymology

From Greek 'tympanon' (drum) and Latin 'cavitas' (cavity).
