field intensity

1. NOUN

/ ˈfiːld ɪnˌtɛnsəti / (AmE), / ˈfiːld ɪnˌtɛnsəti / (BrE)
the strength and direction of an electric or magnetic field at a specific point, usually measured as the force exerted per unit mass, charge, or magnetic pole
strength of an electric or magnetic field at a point

Examples

The field intensity near the magnet was measured with precise instruments.
A higher field intensity can cause electrical breakdown in insulating materials.
Engineers calculated the field intensity at different points in the circuit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: field strength - the magnitude of a field, often used synonymously with field intensity
