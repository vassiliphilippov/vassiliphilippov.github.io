commercial art

1. NOUN

/ kəˈmɜrʃəl ɑrt / (AmE), / kəˈmɜːʃəl ɑːt / (BrE)
art created chiefly for commercial purposes, such as advertising, packaging, or promotional materials.
art for commercial purposes

Examples

She studied commercial art in college to prepare for a career in advertising.
Many graphic designers start out in commercial art before moving on to other fields.
The exhibition featured striking examples of twentieth-century commercial art.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fine art - visual art created primarily for aesthetic or intellectual purposes
