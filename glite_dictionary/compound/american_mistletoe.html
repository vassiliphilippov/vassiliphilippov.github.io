American mistletoe

1. NOUN

/ əˈmɛrɪkən ˈmɪsəlˌtoʊ / (AmE), / əˈmɛrɪkən ˈmɪsl̩təʊ / (BrE)
a parasitic plant (Phoradendron leucarpum) native to North America, commonly used as Christmas decoration and known for growing on deciduous trees and weakening its host
a North American parasitic plant used at Christmas
american

Examples

American mistletoe is often hung in doorways during the holiday season.
The branches of the oak were infested with American mistletoe, weakening the tree.
She explained that American mistletoe can be harmful to its host plants despite its festive reputation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mistletoe - a parasitic evergreen plant used as a Christmas decoration

2. NOUN

/ əˈmɛr.ɪ.kən ˈmɪs.əlˌtoʊ / (AmE), / əˈmɛr.ɪ.kən ˈmɪs.əl.təʊ / (BrE)
A small, parasitic herb with scalelike leaves, reddish-brown stems, and berrylike fruits, commonly found on spruce and larch trees in North America.
parasitic plant on spruce or larch

Examples

In some forests, American mistletoe can be seen growing high up on larch trees.
The reddish stems of American mistletoe stand out among the green foliage in winter.
Botanists studied how American mistletoe affects the health of spruce trees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mistletoe - A group of parasitic plants with white berries traditionally used as decoration
