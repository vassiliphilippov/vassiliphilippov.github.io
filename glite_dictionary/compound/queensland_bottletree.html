Queensland bottletree

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwiːnzˌlænd ˈbɑtlˌtri / (AmE), / ˈkwiːnzˌlænd ˈbɒtlˌtriː / (BrE)
a large tree native to Queensland, Australia, with cream-colored flowers blotched with red inside, known for its bulbous trunk; sometimes placed in the genus Sterculia
Australian tree with swollen trunk and cream flowers

Examples

The Queensland bottletree is famous for its thick, bottle-shaped trunk that stores water.
Many parks in Australia feature a Queensland bottletree as a centerpiece.
The Queensland bottletree produces striking flowers that attract birds and insects.
