prevailing wind

1. NOUN

/ prɪˈveɪlɪŋ wɪnd / (AmE), / prɪˈveɪlɪŋ wɪnd / (BrE)
The direction from which the wind most frequently blows in a particular area or during a specific period.
most common wind direction

Examples

The prevailing wind in this region comes from the northwest.
Farmers often plant trees to act as barriers against the prevailing wind.
Architects designed the house to take full advantage of the prevailing wind for natural ventilation.

Synset Examples

the prevailing wind is from the southwest

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trade wind - a wind blowing steadily toward the equator, especially at sea
