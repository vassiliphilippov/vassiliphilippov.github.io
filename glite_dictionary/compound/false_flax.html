false flax

1. NOUN

/ fɔls flæks / (AmE), / fɔːls flæks / (BrE)
a plant of the genus Camelina, especially Camelina sativa, grown for its oil-rich seeds and native to Europe and central Asia
a plant of the genus Camelina

Examples

The oil produced from false flax seeds is used in cooking and cosmetics.
Farmers in the region have started cultivating false flax as an alternative oilseed crop.
False flax is valued for its ability to grow in poor soils and dry conditions.
