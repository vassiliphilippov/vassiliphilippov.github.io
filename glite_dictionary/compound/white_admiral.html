white admiral

1. NOUN

/ waɪt ˈædmərəl / (AmE), / waɪt ˈædmɪrəl / (BrE)
a Eurasian butterfly with brown wings marked by white bands or patches, commonly found in woodlands
a Eurasian butterfly with white-marked wings

Examples

We spotted a white admiral gracefully gliding among the trees in the forest.
The white admiral is known for its distinctive white bands across its brown wings.
Many wildlife photographers hope to capture a close-up of a white admiral during the summer months.

2. NOUN

/ waɪt ˈædmɪrəl / (AmE), / waɪt ˈædmɪrəl / (BrE)
A North American butterfly with blue-black wings marked by a broad white band, scientifically known as Limenitis arthemis.
North American butterfly with white-banded wings

Examples

We spotted a white admiral in the forest, its white band contrasting with the dark wings.
The white admiral is commonly seen gliding around woodland clearings during summer.
Children chased a white admiral as it fluttered through the garden.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: red admiral - A butterfly with red-orange bands on dark wings, related species
