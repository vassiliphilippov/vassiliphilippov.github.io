going-over

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡoʊɪŋˈoʊvər / (AmE), / ˌɡəʊɪŋˈəʊvə / (BrE)
a careful and thorough inspection or examination of something
thorough inspection or examination

Examples

After the renovation, the inspector gave the house a going-over to ensure everything met standards.
Before submitting her report, she did a final going-over to check for any errors.
The car was given a thorough going-over before the long road trip.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inspection - careful examination of something
  • Synonym: scrutiny - close and critical examination

2. NOUN

/ ˌɡoʊɪŋ ˈoʊvər / (AmE), / ˌɡəʊɪŋ ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
a severe or thorough scolding or reprimand.
a severe scolding

Examples

After missing the deadline, he got a real going-over from his boss.
The team received a going-over in the locker room after their poor performance.
She dreaded facing her mother, knowing a going-over was waiting for her.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: telling-off - an act of reproving or rebuking someone sharply
  • Synonym: reprimand - a formal expression of disapproval
