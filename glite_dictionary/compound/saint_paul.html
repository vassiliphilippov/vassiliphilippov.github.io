Saint Paul

1. PROPN

/ seɪnt pɔl / (AmE), / seɪnt pɔːl / (BrE)
A Christian missionary and apostle, originally known as Saul, who spread Christianity among the Gentiles and authored several Epistles in the New Testament.
Christian apostle and missionary

Examples

Saint Paul traveled extensively to spread the teachings of Christianity throughout the Roman Empire.
Many of the Epistles in the New Testament are attributed to Saint Paul.
Before his conversion, Saint Paul was known as Saul and persecuted Christians.

Synset Examples

Paul's name was Saul prior to his conversion to Christianity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: St. Paul - Alternate spelling/abbreviation for 'Saint Paul', the Christian apostle
  • Synonym: Saul of Tarsus - Birth name of Saint Paul before his conversion
  • Synonym: apostle - Early Christian missionary and leader, especially one of the Twelve or Paul

Etymology

From Latin Paulus, the name adopted by the apostle after his conversion.
