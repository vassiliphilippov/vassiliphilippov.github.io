salt-working

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɔlt ˌwɜrkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsɔːlt ˌwɜːkɪŋ / (BrE)
the process or industry of producing salt, especially by extracting it from sea water or rock deposits
production or extraction of salt

Examples

The museum includes artifacts related to traditional salt-working in the region.
Archaeological evidence reveals that salt-working was an important industry along ancient coastlines.
Modern salt-working techniques are more efficient than historical methods.

Synset Examples

From the north coast of Brittany to the Vendee region there is evidence of salt-working, salt being extracted from sea brine in ovens equipped with pans and then made into blocks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: salt production - the making of salt for use
  • Synonym: salt mining - the process of extracting salt from underground deposits
