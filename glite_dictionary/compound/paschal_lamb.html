Paschal Lamb

1. NOUN

/ ˈpæskəl læm / (AmE), / ˈpæskəl læm / (BrE)
A lamb that is regarded as symbolic of Christ, especially recalling the lamb sacrificed at Passover and in Christian tradition viewed as a representation of Jesus as the sacrificial savior.
lamb symbolic of Christ in Christian tradition

Examples

The altar cloth was embroidered with the image of the Paschal Lamb as a symbol of salvation.
During Easter, Christians often reflect on the meaning of the Paschal Lamb in their faith.
The Paschal Lamb is central to the liturgy and iconography of many Christian churches.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Agnus Dei - a lamb as a symbol of Christ, or a liturgical prayer to the Lamb of God

Etymology

From Latin paschalis (of Passover) and lamb; referring to the Passover sacrifice and its Christian symbolism.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpæskəl læm / (AmE), / ˈpæskəl læm / (BrE)
a symbol of Jesus Christ's sacrifice, especially as commemorated during Easter in Christian tradition
symbol of Christ's sacrifice in Easter

Examples

On Easter Sunday, the sermon focused on the Paschal Lamb and its significance to the congregation.
Many Christian hymns reference the Paschal Lamb as a symbol of redemption.
The artwork in the chapel depicts the Paschal Lamb carrying a banner.

Synset Examples

They celebrated Easter by honoring the Paschal Lamb.
The church service included prayers for the Paschal Lamb.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Agnus Dei - the Latin term for 'Lamb of God', a liturgical name for Christ as a sacrificial lamb in Christian theology

Etymology

From Latin paschalis (of Passover or Easter), via Ecclesiastical Greek and ultimately from Hebrew pesach (Passover).
