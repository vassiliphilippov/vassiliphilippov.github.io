family Alopiidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ˌæˌloʊˈpaɪiːdi / (AmE), / ˈfæməli ˌaləʊˈpɪɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of sharks commonly known as thresher sharks, characterized by their extremely long tail fins.
family of thresher sharks

Examples

The family Alopiidae includes all species of thresher sharks found worldwide.
Researchers are studying the migration patterns of the family Alopiidae in the Pacific Ocean.
Distinctive for their long tail fins, sharks of the family Alopiidae are easily recognizable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thresher shark - a large shark with a long, scythe-like tail fin

Etymology

From New Latin Alopiidae, based on genus Alopias (thresher shark) from Greek alōpēx (fox, alluding to the animal's cunning).
