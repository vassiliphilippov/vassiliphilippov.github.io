corpus luteum

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔrpəs luˈtiəm / (AmE), / ˈkɔːpəs luːˈtiːəm / (BrE)
a yellow hormone-producing tissue that forms in an ovary after an egg has been released, which degenerates unless pregnancy occurs
yellow hormone tissue in ovary after ovulation
medical

Examples

The corpus luteum produces progesterone to prepare the uterus for possible pregnancy.
After ovulation, the ruptured follicle develops into a corpus luteum.
If fertilization does not occur, the corpus luteum degenerates within about two weeks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: follicle - a small secretory cavity, sac, or gland, especially in an ovary

Etymology

From Latin corpus (body) + luteum (yellow).
