bud brush

1. NOUN

/ bʌd brʌʃ / (AmE), / bʌd brʌʃ / (BrE)
A perennial plant species considered valuable as forage for sheep, especially in the United States.
a perennial plant used as sheep forage

Examples

The rancher rotated his flock to graze on the bud brush during the dry season.
Because of its resilience, bud brush thrives in arid regions where other forage is scarce.
Sheep prefer bud brush in spring when its leaves are most tender.
