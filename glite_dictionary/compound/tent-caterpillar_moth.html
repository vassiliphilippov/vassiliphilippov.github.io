tent-caterpillar moth

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛnt ˌkætərˌpɪlər mɑθ / (AmE), / ˈtɛnt ˌkætəpɪlə mɒθ / (BrE)
A moth belonging to the genus Malacosoma, whose larvae are known as tent caterpillars for their habit of spinning large tent-like webs on trees.
moth whose larvae are tent caterpillars

Examples

The tent-caterpillar moth is known for the communal silken tents its larvae construct in trees.
In spring, the appearance of the tent-caterpillar moth signals the beginning of the caterpillar infestation.
Gardeners often remove nests to prevent damage caused by the larvae of the tent-caterpillar moth.

2. NOUN

/ tɛnt ˈkæɾɚˌpɪlər mɑθ / (AmE), / tɛnt ˈkatəpɪlə mɒθ / (BrE)
A moth whose larvae are known for spinning large, communal silk webs that resemble tents or carpets.
moth with larvae that spin silk webs

Examples

The tent-caterpillar moth is often considered a pest because of the damage its larvae cause to trees.
In early spring, the webs created by tent-caterpillar moth larvae can be seen in the branches of cherry trees.
A single tent-caterpillar moth can lay hundreds of eggs, leading to large clusters of tent-like webs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moth - a nocturnal insect with feathery or thick antennae
  • Degree Lower: caterpillar - larval stage of butterflies and moths
