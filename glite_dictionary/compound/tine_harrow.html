tine harrow

1. NOUN

/ taɪn ˈhæroʊ / (AmE), / taɪn ˈharəʊ / (BrE)
an agricultural implement with spikes or tines used to refine seed-bed conditions, remove small weeds, and loosen inter-row soil before or after planting
agricultural tool with tines for soil preparation

Examples

The farmer attached the tine harrow to his tractor before preparing the field for sowing.
Using a tine harrow can help control weeds without damaging young crops.
After plowing, they passed over the soil with a tine harrow to create a fine seedbed.

Synset Examples

Tine and chain harrows are often only supported by a rigid towing-bar at the front of the set.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chain harrow - harrow made with chains for soil smoothing

Etymology

From 'tine' (prong or spike) and 'harrow' (an agricultural tool); 'harrow' from Old English 'hergian', 'tine' from Old English 'tīn'.
