sodium sulphate

1. NOUN

/ ˌsoʊdiəm ˈsʌl.feɪt / (AmE), / ˌsəʊdiəm ˈsʌl.feɪt / (BrE)
a white, bitter-tasting salt used in manufacturing glass, paper, dyes, and pharmaceuticals
a salt used in industry and manufacturing

Examples

The factory uses sodium sulphate in the production of glass bottles.
Researchers studied the effects of sodium sulphate on paper strength.
Sodium sulphate is sometimes added during the dyeing process to improve color uptake.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sodium sulfate - alternative spelling (American English) of 'sodium sulphate'

Etymology

From sodium + sulphate, where sulphate refers to a salt or ester of sulfuric acid.
