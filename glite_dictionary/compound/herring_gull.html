herring gull

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛrɪŋ ɡʌl / (AmE), / ˈhɛrɪŋ ɡʌl / (BrE)
a large, gray-and-white gull of the Northern Hemisphere, known for its loud call and commonly found near coasts and inland waters
a large gray-and-white gull species

Examples

A herring gull swooped down to snatch a piece of bread from the water.
We spotted a herring gull standing on the dock, calling loudly.
Many herring gulls nest on rocky islands along the coast.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seagull - a general term for any species of gull, especially those found near the sea
