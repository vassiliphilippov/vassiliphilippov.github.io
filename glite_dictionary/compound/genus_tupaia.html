genus Tupaia

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs tuːˈpaɪə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs tjuːˈpaɪə / (BrE)
The principal genus of tree shrews, comprising several species found in Southeast Asia.
chief genus of tree shrews

Examples

Many species within the genus Tupaia are studied for their unique behaviors and biology.
Researchers discovered a new member of the genus Tupaia in the forests of Borneo.
The genus Tupaia serves as a model for evolutionary studies among small mammals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tree shrew - small, squirrel-like mammals native to Southeast Asia

Etymology

From Latin 'genus' (kind, type) and modern Latin 'Tupaia', the genus name assigned by zoologists.
