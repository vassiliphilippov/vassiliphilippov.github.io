camera obscura

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæmərə əbˈskjʊrə / (AmE), / ˌkæmərə ɒbˈskjʊərə / (BrE)
A darkened room or box with a small hole or lens at one side through which an image is projected onto an opposite surface; an early device for projecting an external scene.
dark room/image projection device

Examples

Artists during the Renaissance often used a camera obscura to aid in creating realistic drawings.
The exhibition included a working camera obscura that projected the park outside onto the wall.
Curious children entered the camera obscura and watched the upside-down image appear in the darkness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pinhole camera - a simple camera without a lens but with a tiny aperture

Etymology

From Latin, meaning 'dark chamber'.
