polished rice

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑlɪʃt ɹaɪs / (AmE), / ˈpɒlɪʃt ɹaɪs / (BrE)
rice from which the husk and outer brown layers have been removed, leaving mainly the starchy endosperm
rice with husk and outer layers removed

Examples

In many Asian countries, polished rice is a dietary staple.
Polished rice is preferred by some consumers for its appearance and texture.
Because it lacks certain nutrients, polished rice may lead to deficiencies if not supplemented.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: white rice - milled rice with husk, bran, and germ removed
  • Antonym: brown rice - whole grain rice with only the husk removed
