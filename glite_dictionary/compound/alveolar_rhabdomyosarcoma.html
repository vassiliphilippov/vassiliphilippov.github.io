alveolar rhabdomyosarcoma

1. NOUN

/ ælˌviːələr ˌræbdəmaɪoʊsɑrˈkoʊmə / (AmE), / ælˌviːələ ˌræbdəmʌɪəʊsɑːˈkəʊmə / (BrE)
A rare, aggressive form of cancer that primarily affects adolescents and young adults, characterized by cancerous cells resembling the structure of small sacs or alveoli.
rare cancer mainly in young people
medical

Examples

Recent studies have improved treatment options for alveolar rhabdomyosarcoma in teenagers.
The prognosis of alveolar rhabdomyosarcoma depends on early detection and aggressive therapy.
He was diagnosed with alveolar rhabdomyosarcoma at the age of seventeen.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: rhabdomyosarcoma - a malignant tumor involving striated muscle tissue

Etymology

From Latin 'alveolus' (small cavity) and Greek 'rhabdos' (rod) + 'myos' (muscle) + 'sarcoma' (flesh tumor).
