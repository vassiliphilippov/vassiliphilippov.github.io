shot metal

1. NOUN

/ ʃɑt ˈmɛt̬l̩ / (AmE), / ʃɒt ˈmɛtl̩ / (BrE)
an alloy consisting of approximately 98% lead and 2% arsenic, used in the manufacture of small pellets for shotgun cartridges.
alloy of lead and arsenic for making shot

Examples

The factory produces shot metal for use in hunting ammunition.
Due to health concerns, alternatives to shot metal are being explored.
Traditional shotgun shells often contain pellets made from shot metal.
