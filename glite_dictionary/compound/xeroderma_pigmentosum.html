xeroderma pigmentosum

1. NOUN

/ zɪr.oʊˈdɜr.mə ˌpɪg.mənˈtoʊ.səm / (AmE), / ˌzɪərəʊˈdɜː.mə ˌpɪg.mənˈtəʊ.səm / (BrE)
A rare inherited medical disorder causing extreme sensitivity to ultraviolet light, leading to skin damage and a greatly increased risk of skin cancer.
genetic disorder causing UV sensitivity and skin cancer risk
medical

Examples

Children with xeroderma pigmentosum must avoid sunlight to prevent severe skin damage.
The genetic diagnosis confirmed that he had xeroderma pigmentosum after multiple unusual sunburns.
Researchers are studying new treatments for xeroderma pigmentosum to reduce the risk of skin cancer.

Etymology

From Greek 'xērós' (dry), 'derma' (skin), and Latin 'pigmentosus' (colored, pigmented).
