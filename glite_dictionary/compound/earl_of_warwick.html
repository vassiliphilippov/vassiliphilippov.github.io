Earl of Warwick

1. PROPN

/ ɝl əv ˈwɔrwɪk / (AmE), / ɜːl əv ˈwɒrɪk / (BrE)
a prominent English noble title; especially Richard Neville (1428–1471), influential statesman known for his role in the Wars of the Roses, switching allegiances between the houses of York and Lancaster.
English noble; key figure in the Wars of the Roses

Examples

During the turbulent Wars of the Roses, the Earl of Warwick played a decisive role in shifting political alliances.
The history textbook devoted a chapter to the influence of the Earl of Warwick on the English monarchy.
Known as the 'Kingmaker', the Earl of Warwick was both feared and respected by his contemporaries.

Etymology

From Old English 'eorl' (nobleman) and 'Warwick', a town name from Old English 'wæring' (dam, weir) + 'wic' (settlement).
