Doctor of Humane Letters

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɑktɚ əv hjuˈmeɪn ˈlɛtɚz / (AmE), / ˈdɒktə əv hjuːˈmeɪn ˈlɛtəz / (BrE)
an honorary degree awarded in recognition of distinguished achievement in the humanities, literature, or related fields
honorary degree for achievement in humanities

Examples

She was awarded a Doctor of Humane Letters for her lifelong contributions to literature.
During the ceremony, the university conferred the title of Doctor of Humane Letters on the guest speaker.
Several notable alumni have received the Doctor of Humane Letters degree in recognition of their humanitarian work.

Etymology

From Latin *litterae humaniores* (the more humane letters), referring to studies in the humanities.
