Tombigbee River

1. PROPN

/ tɒmˈbɪɡbi ˈrɪvər / (AmE), / tɒmˈbɪɡbi ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
a river in the southeastern United States that rises in northeastern Mississippi, flows southward through western Alabama, and joins the Alabama River to form the Mobile River
river in Mississippi and Alabama, USA

Examples

The Tombigbee River provides an important navigational route for commercial vessels in the Southeast.
Heavy rainfall caused the Tombigbee River to overflow, flooding several nearby communities.
We spent the afternoon fishing along the banks of the Tombigbee River.

Etymology

From Choctaw, possibly meaning 'coffin makers' or 'box makers'.
