giant salamander

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒaɪənt səˈlændər / (AmE), / ˈdʒaɪənt səˈlæməndə / (BrE)
a very large salamander native to Asia, reaching lengths of up to more than three feet; noted for its size and sometimes used as food
very large Asian salamander species

Examples

The Chinese giant salamander is one of the largest amphibians in the world.
Researchers were amazed to discover a massive giant salamander near the riverbank.
Some people in Asia consider the giant salamander a delicacy and use it in traditional cuisine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: salamander - a type of amphibian typically having a lizard-like appearance
