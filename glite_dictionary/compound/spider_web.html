spider web

1. NOUN

/ ˈspaɪdɚ wɛb / (AmE), / ˈspaɪdə wɛb / (BrE)
a fine, silken network spun by a spider to catch prey, or anything closely resembling such a structure
web spun by a spider or similar network

Examples

The morning dew made the spider web shimmer in the sunlight.
She brushed away a spider web hanging from the doorway.
The crystal chandelier was covered in spider webs after years of neglect.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cobweb - a spider's web, often old and covered with dust
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04282667-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈspaɪdər wɛb / (AmE), / ˈspaɪdə wɛb / (BrE)
a structure made of silk threads spun by a spider, typically used to catch insects for food
web spun by a spider to catch insects

Examples

A delicate spider web glistened with morning dew in the corner of the garden.
He accidentally walked through a spider web hanging between two trees.
The attic was covered in thick spider webs that hadn't been cleaned in years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cobweb - another term for a spider web, especially an old or dusty one
