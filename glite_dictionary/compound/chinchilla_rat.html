chinchilla rat

1. NOUN

/ tʃɪnˈtʃɪlə ræt / (AmE), / tʃɪnˈtʃɪlə ræt / (BrE)
a ratlike rodent with soft fur and large ears, native to the Andes mountains in South America
Andean rodent with soft fur and large ears

Examples

The chinchilla rat is adapted to life in rocky Andean habitats.
Researchers have studied the social behavior of the chinchilla rat in the wild.
The thick, soft fur of the chinchilla rat provides protection against cold mountain temperatures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chinchilla - small South American rodent with soft grey fur
