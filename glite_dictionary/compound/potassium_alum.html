potassium alum

1. NOUN

/ pəˈteɪsiəm ˈæləm / (AmE), / pəˈtasɪəm ˈaləm / (BrE)
a white crystalline double sulfate composed of potassium and aluminum, commonly used in water purification, dyeing, and as an astringent
a white crystalline double sulfate of potassium and aluminum

Examples

In traditional water purification, potassium alum is added to help clarify the liquid.
The chemist measured out the potassium alum crystals for the experiment.
Potassium alum is also used as an astringent in some aftershave products.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alum - a group of double sulfates containing aluminum

Etymology

From potassium (element name) and alum (from Latin alumen, alum; a name for several double sulfates).
