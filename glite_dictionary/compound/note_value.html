note value

1. NOUN

/ ˈnoʊt ˌvæl.ju / (AmE), / ˈnəʊt ˌvæl.juː / (BrE)
(Music) The relative duration of a musical note, indicated by its symbol, such as whole note, half note, quarter note, etc.
duration of a musical note

Examples

The composer used longer note values in the slow movement to create a sense of calm.
Understanding the difference between note values is essential for reading music.
The rhythm of the piece was complicated by unusual note values in the melody.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: note - a single musical sound or its written symbol
