genus Alisma

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs əˈlɪz.mə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs əˈlɪz.mə / (BrE)
a small genus of aquatic or semiaquatic flowering plants found in wetlands and shallow water, known commonly as water-plantains
genus of aquatic plants (water-plantains)

Examples

The genus Alisma includes several species commonly found in marshes and ponds.
Botanists are studying the distribution of the genus Alisma across North America.
Members of the genus Alisma are often used in wetland restoration projects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Alisma - a genus of aquatic plants also known as water-plantains

Etymology

From Latin 'Alisma', the original name of the type species; 'genus' from Latin meaning 'kind' or 'type'.
