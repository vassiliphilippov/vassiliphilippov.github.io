high explosive

1. NOUN

/ haɪ ɪkˈsploʊsɪv / (AmE), / haɪ ɪkˈspləʊsɪv / (BrE)
a powerful chemical explosive that detonates at a very high rate, producing a sudden, violent release of gas and energy
powerful, fast-acting chemical explosive

Examples

The demolition team used a high explosive to bring down the old building safely.
Dynamite is a well-known type of high explosive used in mining operations.
The military stores high explosives in secured bunkers to prevent accidental detonation.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: low explosive - an explosive that deflagrates rather than detonates, producing a slower reaction
  • Degree Lower: explosive - a substance that can cause an explosion
