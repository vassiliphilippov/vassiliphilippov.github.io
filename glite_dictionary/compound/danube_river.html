Danube River

1. PROPN

/ ˈdæn.juːb ˈrɪv.ər / (AmE), / ˈdæn.juːb ˈrɪv.ə / (BrE)
the second longest river in Europe, flowing from southwestern Germany southeastward to the Black Sea, passing through or bordering several Central and Eastern European countries
major European river from Germany to the Black Sea

Examples

The Danube River flows through ten different countries, making it a vital waterway in Europe.
Many historic cities, such as Budapest and Vienna, are located along the Danube River.
Environmental organizations are working to preserve the ecosystem of the Danube River.

Synset Examples

Vienna, Budapest, and Belgrade are on the banks of the Danube

Etymology

From Latin 'Danubius', from an earlier Celtic or Thracian name for the river.
