Cape York

1. PROPN

/ keɪp ˈjɔrk / (AmE), / keɪp ˈjɔːk / (BrE)
the northern tip of Cape York Peninsula at Torres Strait, the northernmost point of the Australian mainland
northernmost point of mainland Australia

Examples

Many adventurous travelers dream of reaching Cape York, the remote northern tip of Australia.
The road to Cape York is famous for its challenging conditions and natural beauty.
Wildlife enthusiasts visit Cape York to see unique species found nowhere else on the continent.

Etymology

Named by British explorer James Cook in 1770 after Prince Edward, Duke of York and Albany.
