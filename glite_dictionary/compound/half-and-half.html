half-and-half

1. ADV

/ ˌhæf ən ˈhæf / (AmE), / ˌhɑːf ən ˈhɑːf / (BrE)
Equally divided between two parts or groups; in equal proportions.
in equal proportions

Examples

The bill was split half-and-half between the two roommates.
They decided to divide the chores half-and-half to keep things fair.
Ownership of the property was shared half-and-half by both partners.

Synset Examples

it was divided half-and-half

Related Terms

  • Synonym: equally - to the same degree or in the same amounts

2. NOUN

/ ˈhæf ən ˌhæf / (AmE), / ˌhɑːf ən ˈhɑːf / (BrE)
A mixture of equal parts milk and light cream, typically containing 10% to 18% butterfat, used in coffee, recipes, and cooking.
dairy mixture of milk and cream

Examples

He always adds half-and-half to his morning coffee instead of milk.
The recipe calls for a cup of half-and-half to make the soup creamier.
You can substitute half-and-half for heavy cream to reduce the fat content in desserts.

3. ADJ

/ ˈhæf ən ˈhæf / (AmE), / ˈhɑːf ən ˈhɑːf / (BrE)
Consisting of equal parts of two different things.
equal parts of two things

Examples

Pour a half-and-half mix of milk and cream into your coffee for a richer taste.
They decided on a half-and-half agreement, splitting the profit equally.
The paint is a half-and-half blend of blue and green.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fifty-fifty - equal division or share between two parties
  • Antonym: unequal - not equal in size, amount, or value
