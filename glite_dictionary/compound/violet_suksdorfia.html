violet suksdorfia

1. NOUN

/ ˈvaɪə.lət sʌksˈdɔːr.fi.ə / (AmE), / ˈvaɪələt sʌksˈdɔːfɪə / (BrE)
a slender, delicate plant with broad, round, deeply lobed leaves and deep pink to violet funnel-shaped flowers, native to parts of western North America
a western North American flowering plant

Examples

The violet suksdorfia flourishes in moist mountain meadows throughout Idaho and Montana.
Hikers often admire the beautiful funnel-shaped flowers of the violet suksdorfia in early summer.
Botanists documented several patches of violet suksdorfia along the forest trail.

Etymology

Named after Wilhelm Nikolaus Suksdorf, a German-American botanist.
