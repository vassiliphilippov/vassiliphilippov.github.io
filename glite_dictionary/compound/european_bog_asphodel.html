European bog asphodel

1. NOUN

/ jʊˈroʊpiən bɑɡ ˈæsfəˌdɛl / (AmE), / jʊəˈrəʊpiən bɒɡ ˈæsfədɛl / (BrE)
A yellow-flowered perennial plant (Narthecium ossifragum) of wet boggy areas in western Europe, from Scandinavia to northern Spain and Portugal.
yellow bog plant of western Europe

Examples

The European bog asphodel blooms spectacularly in peat bogs during midsummer across the north of England.
Botanists studied the habitat preferences of the European bog asphodel in Scottish wetlands.
The bright yellow flowers of the European bog asphodel are a notable feature of upland moorland landscapes.

Etymology

From the plant genus Narthecium and 'asphodel,' a name for various plants resembling lilies; 'bog' refers to its habitat.
