Articles of Confederation

1. PROPN

/ ˈɑrtɪkəlz əv kənˌfɛdəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈɑːtɪklz əv kənˌfɛdəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the first constitution of the United States, ratified in 1781, which established a loose confederation of sovereign states and a weak central government
first U.S. constitution (1781–1789)

Examples

The Articles of Confederation were replaced by the U.S. Constitution in 1789.
Under the Articles of Confederation, the federal government lacked the power to levy taxes.
Many historians debate the effectiveness of the Articles of Confederation in uniting the new states.
