quaternary ammonium compound

1. NOUN

/ kwəˈtɝː.nɛr.i əˈmɑː.ni.əm ˌkɑːmˌpaʊnd / (AmE), / kwəˈtɜː.nəri əˈmɒ.ni.əm ˌkɒm.paʊnd / (BrE)
a chemical compound derived from ammonium by replacing all four hydrogen atoms with organic groups, commonly used as disinfectants, surfactants, or in pharmaceutical preparations
type of chemical compound used as disinfectant or surfactant
medical

Examples

A quaternary ammonium compound is often added to household cleaners for its disinfectant properties.
Pharmaceutical products may contain a quaternary ammonium compound as a preservative.
The laboratory tested the effectiveness of the quaternary ammonium compound against various bacteria.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: ammonium compound - a chemical compound containing the ammonium ion (NH4+)

Etymology

From Latin 'quaternarius' (consisting of four) and 'ammonium', indicating an ammonium ion with four substituents.
