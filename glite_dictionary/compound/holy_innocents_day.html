Holy Innocents' Day

1. NOUN

/ ˈhoʊli ˌɪnəsənts deɪ / (AmE), / ˈhəʊli ˌɪnəsənts deɪ / (BrE)
December 28, a Christian feast day commemorating King Herod's massacre of the children in Bethlehem as described in the Gospel of Matthew.
Christian feast on December 28

Examples

Many churches hold a special service on Holy Innocents' Day to remember the children of Bethlehem.
On Holy Innocents' Day, some Christian traditions involve prayer for children affected by violence.
He wrote an article explaining the significance of Holy Innocents' Day in the Christian calendar.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Feast of the Holy Innocents - Alternative name for Holy Innocents' Day

Etymology

From the term 'Holy Innocents', referring to the children killed by Herod, and 'Day', marking the commemorative feast.
