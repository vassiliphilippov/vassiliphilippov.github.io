anconeous muscle

1. NOUN

/ ænˈkoʊni.əs ˈmʌsəl / (AmE), / aŋˈkəʊni.əs ˈmʌsl̩ / (BrE)
a small muscle at the elbow that helps extend the forearm and abduct the ulna during wrist pronation
muscle at the elbow aiding forearm extension
medical

Examples

In anatomical studies, the anconeous muscle is often mentioned as an accessory extensor of the elbow.
The surgeon carefully avoided injuring the anconeous muscle during the procedure.
Weakness in the anconeous muscle can contribute to difficulty in extending the forearm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: triceps brachii - the large muscle on the back of the upper arm responsible for extending the forearm
  • Antonym: biceps brachii - the muscle on the front of the upper arm that flexes the forearm

Etymology

From Latin 'ancon' meaning 'elbow'.
