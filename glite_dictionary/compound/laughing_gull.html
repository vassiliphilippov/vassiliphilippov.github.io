laughing gull

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæfɪŋ ɡʌl / (AmE), / ˈlɑːfɪŋ ɡʌl / (BrE)
a medium-sized gull of the Americas with a black head during breeding season, known for its distinctive, laughing call
a species of gull with a black head and distinctive call

Examples

A laughing gull swooped over the shoreline, its call echoing above the waves.
During migration, flocks of laughing gulls can be seen along the East Coast beaches.
The distinctive black head of the laughing gull makes it easy to identify during summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gull - a seabird of the family Laridae
