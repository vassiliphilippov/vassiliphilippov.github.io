offset lithography

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːfˌsɛt lɪˈθɑɡrəfi / (AmE), / ˌɒfˌsɛt lɪˈθɒɡrəfi / (BrE)
a printing process in which an inked image is transferred (offset) from a plate to a rubber blanket, then to the printing surface, commonly used for mass-production printing.
a mass-production printing method

Examples

Most newspapers are printed using offset lithography for high-speed production.
Advancements in offset lithography have allowed for sharper images and vibrant colors in magazines.
The print shop specializes in offset lithography for brochures and catalogs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: offset printing - printing process where the image is transferred from plate to rubber blanket to surface

Etymology

From 'offset' (to transfer) and 'lithography', from Greek lithos (stone) + -graphia (writing).
