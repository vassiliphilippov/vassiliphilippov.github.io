Virginian witch hazel

1. NOUN

/ vərˈdʒɪniən wɪtʃ ˈhæzəl / (AmE), / vəˈdʒɪniən wɪtʃ ˈhæzəl / (BrE)
A common shrub native to eastern North America, known for producing small yellow flowers after its leaves have fallen.
shrub with yellow flowers native to eastern North America

Examples

The Virginian witch hazel blooms in late autumn, brightening the forest with its yellow flowers.
Gardeners often plant Virginian witch hazel for its ornamental value and resilience.
After the leaves have dropped, the Virginian witch hazel produces clusters of fragrant yellow blossoms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: witch hazel - shrub of the genus Hamamelis, used for medicinal purposes
