Mackinaw blanket

1. NOUN

/ ˈmækɪnɔː ˈblæŋkɪt / (AmE), / ˈmakɪnɔː ˈblaŋkɪt / (BrE)
a thick, heavy woolen blanket with a plaid pattern, historically used in the northwestern United States, especially for warmth in cold climates
thick plaid woolen blanket

Examples

The trapper wrapped himself in a Mackinaw blanket to keep warm during the harsh winter night.
She found an old Mackinaw blanket at the antique shop, still vibrant with its red and black plaid.
Early settlers often relied on the Mackinaw blanket for comfort and protection against the cold.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mackinaw coat - a short, heavy, woolen coat with a plaid design, associated with the same region and era

Etymology

Named after Mackinac (formerly spelled Mackinaw), a region in Michigan where such blankets were traded.
