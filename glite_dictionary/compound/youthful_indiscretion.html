youthful indiscretion

1. NOUN

/ ˈjuθfəl ˌɪndɪˈskrɛʃən / (AmE), / ˈjuːθfʊl ˌɪndɪˈskrɛʃən / (BrE)
a mistake or misjudgment made during one's youth, often regarded as minor or excusable in hindsight
mistake made in youth

Examples

He considered his arrest for vandalism a youthful indiscretion rather than a sign of bad character.
The politician's opponents tried to use an old youthful indiscretion against her during the campaign.
Many people look back on at least one youthful indiscretion with a mix of embarrassment and fondness.

Synset Examples

We all make mistakes; it was a youthful indiscretion.
He learned from his youthful indiscretion and moved on.
That was just a youthful indiscretion, nothing serious.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mistake - an action or judgment that is wrong or misguided
