Italian clover

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈtæljən ˈkloʊvər / (AmE), / ɪˈtæliən ˈkləʊvə / (BrE)
A southern European annual plant with spiky heads of crimson flowers, cultivated widely as a forage crop.
annual plant with crimson flowers, used as forage

Examples

Farmers often plant Italian clover to improve soil fertility and provide grazing for livestock.
The brilliant red blossoms of Italian clover attract bees and other pollinators in the spring.
In many regions, Italian clover is valued for its rapid growth and high-quality forage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crimson clover - annual plant with crimson flower heads, used as forage
