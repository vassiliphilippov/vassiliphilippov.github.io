Portuguese escudo

1. NOUN

/ pɔrˈtjuːdʒiz ɛsˈkuːdoʊ / (AmE), / ˌpɔː.tʃuːˈɡiːz ɛsˈkuːdəʊ / (BrE)
the former basic monetary unit of Portugal, equal to 100 centavos, used before the adoption of the euro
former currency of Portugal

Examples

After Portugal adopted the euro, the Portuguese escudo was officially withdrawn from circulation.
You can still see old Portuguese escudo coins in some museums and private collections.
The value of the Portuguese escudo fluctuated significantly before the switch to the euro.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: euro - the currency that replaced the escudo in Portugal

Etymology

From Portuguese escudo (shield), reflecting the image on early coins; Latin *scūtum* (shield).
