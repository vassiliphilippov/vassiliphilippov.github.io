fee tail

1. NOUN

/ fi teɪl / (AmE), / fiː teɪl / (BrE)
A legal estate in land limited to a particular line of heirs, so that it cannot be sold or given away by the current holder.
hereditary legal estate restricted to certain heirs
legal

Examples

Under a fee tail, the property must pass to the owner's direct descendants.
The system of fee tail restricted the sale of large estates to outsiders.
Many medieval aristocrats held their lands in fee tail to keep them within the family.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fee simple - a legal estate in land that may be freely disposed of by the owner

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman French 'fee' (estate, property) + 'taille' (cut, limited).
