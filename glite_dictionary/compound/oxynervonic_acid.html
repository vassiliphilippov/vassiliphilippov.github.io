oxynervonic acid

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑksiˌnɜːrˈvoʊnɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / ˌɒksɪˌnɜːˈvɒnɪk ˈasɪd / (BrE)
a chemical compound that is a derivative of nervonic acid, found as a constituent in certain cerebrosides.
a derivative of nervonic acid (biochemistry)
medical

Examples

Researchers identified oxynervonic acid as a component in the brain's white matter.
The concentration of oxynervonic acid varies among different species of mammals.
Some disorders affecting nerve cells involve altered levels of oxynervonic acid.

Synset Examples

Oxynervonic acid is an important constituent of certain cerebrosides.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nervonic acid - a monounsaturated fatty acid found in nerve tissue

Etymology

From 'oxy-' (referring to oxygen) and 'nervonic acid', based on its chemical structure and relation.
