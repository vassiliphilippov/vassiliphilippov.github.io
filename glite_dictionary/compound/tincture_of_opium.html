tincture of opium

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɪŋktʃɚ əv ˈoʊpiəm / (AmE), / ˈtɪŋktʃə əv ˈəʊpiəm / (BrE)
a medicinal solution of opium dissolved in alcohol, used as a narcotic and for relieving pain or diarrhea
opium dissolved in alcohol for medicinal use
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed a small amount of tincture of opium to manage the patient's severe diarrhea.
For centuries, tincture of opium was used as a powerful painkiller and sedative.
The chemist carefully measured the tincture of opium before dispensing it as medicine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: laudanum - an alcoholic solution of opium, especially in historical contexts

Etymology

From Latin *tinctura* (a dyeing), and opium, referring to the alcoholic extraction of opium.
