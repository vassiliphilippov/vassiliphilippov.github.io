reinforced concrete

1. NOUN

/ ˌriːɪnˌfɔrst ˈkɑnkrit / (AmE), / ˌriːɪnˌfɔːst ˈkɒnkriːt / (BrE)
Concrete in which metal bars or mesh are embedded to increase its strength and durability.
concrete strengthened with metal or mesh

Examples

The bridge was constructed using reinforced concrete to ensure it would withstand heavy traffic loads.
Many modern buildings rely on reinforced concrete for their foundations and structural frameworks.
Repairs to the parking garage revealed corrosion in the reinforced concrete.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: concrete - A building material made from a mixture of cement, water, and aggregate
