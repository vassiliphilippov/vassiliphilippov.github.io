common wasp

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːmən wɑsp / (AmE), / ˈkɒm.ən wɒsp / (BrE)
a wasp (Vespula vulgaris) found in Europe and parts of Asia, known for its black and yellow stripes and tendency to nest near human habitation
a common species of wasp (Vespula vulgaris)

Examples

A common wasp built a nest under our garden shed last summer.
Unlike the hornet, the common wasp is smaller and more likely to scavenge at picnics.
He was stung by a common wasp while eating outside.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: wasp - a stinging insect of the order Hymenoptera
