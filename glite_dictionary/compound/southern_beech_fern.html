southern beech fern

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌðərn biːtʃ fɜrn / (AmE), / ˈsʌð(ə)n biːtʃ fɜːn / (BrE)
A species of beech fern found in North American woodlands, recognized by its straw-colored stripes.
North American beech fern species

Examples

The southern beech fern thrives in shaded woodland areas throughout the southeastern United States.
I identified a southern beech fern during our hike, its unique straw-colored stripes distinguishing it from other ferns.
Gardeners value the southern beech fern for its delicate appearance and adaptability to woodland gardens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beech fern - a type of fern from the genus Phegopteris, commonly found in temperate forests
