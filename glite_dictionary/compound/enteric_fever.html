enteric fever

1. NOUN

/ ɛnˈtɛrɪk ˈfiːvər / (AmE), / ɛnˈtɛrɪk ˈfiːvə / (BrE)
a serious infectious disease characterized by inflammation and ulceration of the intestines, usually caused by Salmonella typhi and acquired through contaminated food or water
infectious disease affecting the intestines
medical

Examples

The outbreak was traced to contaminated water, which resulted in several cases of enteric fever.
Vaccination can help protect travelers from diseases like enteric fever in high-risk areas.
Doctors diagnosed her with enteric fever following persistent high fever and abdominal pain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: typhoid fever - another name for enteric fever, caused by Salmonella typhi

Etymology

From Greek 'enterikos' (pertaining to the intestine) and 'fever'.
