cutaneous sensation

1. NOUN

/ kjuˈteɪniəs sɛnˈseɪʃən / (AmE), / kjuːˈteɪnɪəs sɛnˈseɪʃən / (BrE)
a sensation that is perceived on the skin, such as touch, temperature, pain, or pressure
a sensation felt through the skin
medical

Examples

The patient exhibited a complete loss of cutaneous sensation in his left hand after the accident.
Some neurological disorders can affect cutaneous sensation, leading to numbness or tingling.
Doctors evaluate cutaneous sensation to check for nerve damage during physical examinations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: touch - the sense by which physical contact is perceived
  • Synonym: tactile sensation - sensation of touch or contact with the skin

Etymology

From Latin 'cutaneus' (of the skin) and English 'sensation'.
