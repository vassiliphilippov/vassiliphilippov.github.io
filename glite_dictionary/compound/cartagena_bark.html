Cartagena bark

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːrtəˈheɪnə bɑːrk / (AmE), / ˌkɑːtɪˈheɪnə bɑːk / (BrE)
The bark of a Colombian cinchona tree, formerly used as a source of quinine for medicinal purposes.
bark of Colombian cinchona tree

Examples

Quinine was once commonly extracted from Cartagena bark to treat malaria.
The export of Cartagena bark played a significant role in Colombia's colonial economy.
He researched the medicinal properties of Cartagena bark for his thesis on tropical diseases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cinchona bark - bark from trees of the genus Cinchona, used as a source of quinine

Etymology

Named after Cartagena, a port city in Colombia, where the bark was exported.
