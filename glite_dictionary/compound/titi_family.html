titi family

1. NOUN

/ ˈtiːti ˈfæməli / (AmE), / ˈtiːti ˈfæmɪli / (BrE)
a family of evergreen shrubs and trees with leathery leaves and small white flowers in racemes, including the genera Cyrilla and Cliftonia
family of shrubs and trees; genera Cyrilla and Cliftonia

Examples

The titi family is recognized for its evergreen leaves and clusters of delicate white flowers.
Several native species in the southeastern United States belong to the titi family.
Botanists studied the ecological significance of the titi family in wetland habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cyrilla - genus of shrubs or small trees in the titi family
  • Synonym: Cliftonia - genus of shrubs in the titi family
