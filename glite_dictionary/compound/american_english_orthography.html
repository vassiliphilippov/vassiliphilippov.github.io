American English orthography

1. NOUN

/ əˈmɛrɪkən ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ ɔrˈθɑɡrəfi / (AmE), / əˈmɛrɪkən ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ ɔːˈθɒɡrəfi / (BrE)
the set of spelling conventions and rules used for English in the United States, differing in some aspects from other varieties of English
U.S. English spelling conventions
american

Examples

He is careful to use American English orthography when writing for U.S. publications.
Many school textbooks in the U.S. follow American English orthography rather than British spellings.
The word 'color' instead of 'colour' is an example of American English orthography.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: British English orthography - the set of spelling conventions used for English in the United Kingdom
