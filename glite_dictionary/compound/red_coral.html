red coral

1. NOUN

/ rɛd ˈkɔɹəl / (AmE), / rɛd ˈkɒɹəl / (BrE)
a hard, red or pink stony skeleton of a Mediterranean coral, used in jewelry and ornamentation
red or pink Mediterranean coral used in jewelry

Examples

She wore a necklace of red coral beads that gleamed in the sunlight.
Ancient artisans carved intricate figurines from red coral collected along the Mediterranean coast.
The museum displayed a rare piece of red coral jewelry dating back centuries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coral - marine invertebrate with a hard skeleton

2. NOUN

/ rɛd ˈkɔrəl / (AmE), / rɛd ˈkɒrəl / (BrE)
A species of coral, particularly from the Mediterranean, with a pink or red color, used for ornaments and jewelry.
red Mediterranean coral used in jewelry

Examples

The necklace was made from precious red coral harvested in the Mediterranean Sea.
Divers are often cautioned not to break red coral while exploring underwater reefs.
She wore antique earrings crafted from polished red coral.
