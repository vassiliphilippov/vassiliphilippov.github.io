genus Geastrum

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈʤiː.æstrəm / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈdʒiː.ɑːstrəm / (BrE)
the type genus of the family Geastraceae, consisting of fungi whose outer layer splits into star-shaped segments when dry
type genus of Geastraceae fungi

Examples

The genus Geastrum includes fungi commonly known as earthstars due to their shape.
Members of the genus Geastrum are often found in woodland areas on decaying wood.
The unique star-shaped fruiting bodies make the genus Geastrum easy to identify.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Geastrum - fungi genus whose fruiting bodies open in a star shape

Etymology

From Latin 'Geastrum' (earth star), referring to the star-shaped opening of the fungi.
