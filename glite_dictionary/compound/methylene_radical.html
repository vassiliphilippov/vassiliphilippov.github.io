methylene radical

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛθəˌlin ˈrædɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈmiːθəˌliːn ˈrædɪkəl / (BrE)
a bivalent chemical radical with the formula CH2, derived from methane and highly reactive in organic reactions
bivalent methyl (CH2) radical
medical

Examples

The methylene radical plays an important role as an intermediate in many organic reactions.
Because of its high reactivity, the methylene radical is seldom observed in pure form.
Scientists generate the methylene radical in laboratory settings to study chemical reaction mechanisms.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: methyl radical - the univalent radical CH3 derived from methane

Etymology

From 'methylene', modeled on methane plus Greek 'hylē' (wood, matter), referring to its chemical composition.
