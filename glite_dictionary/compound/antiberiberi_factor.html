antiberiberi factor

1. NOUN

/ ˌæntiˌbɛriˈbɛri ˈfæktər / (AmE), / ˌæntɪˌbɛrɪˈbɛrɪ ˈfæktə / (BrE)
a vitamin of the B group, also known as thiamine, that prevents beriberi and helps maintain appetite and growth
a B vitamin preventing beriberi
medical

Examples

Many foods contain the antiberiberi factor, which is essential for normal growth.
A deficiency in the antiberiberi factor can lead to serious health problems such as beriberi.
Enriched cereals are often fortified with the antiberiberi factor to ensure adequate nutrition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thiamine - another name for vitamin B1, preventing beriberi

Etymology

Prefix 'anti-' (against) + 'beriberi' (disease name, from Sinhalese) + 'factor' (component).
