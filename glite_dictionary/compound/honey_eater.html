honey eater

1. NOUN

/ ˈhʌniˌitər / (AmE), / ˈhʌniˌiːtə / (BrE)
an Australasian bird with a slender tongue and bill adapted for extracting nectar from flowers, belonging to the family Meliphagidae
nectar-feeding Australasian bird

Examples

The honey eater uses its specialized bill to reach deep into flowers for nectar.
Several species of honey eaters are important pollinators in Australian ecosystems.
We spotted a colorful honey eater feeding among the eucalyptus blossoms.
