perfect participle

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɜːrfɪkt ˈpɑːrtɪsɪpl̩ / (AmE), / ˈpɜːfɪkt ˈpɑːtɪsɪp(ə)l / (BrE)
a participle that indicates a completed action, typically formed with 'having' plus the past participle of a verb
participle showing completed action

Examples

The perfect participle is used in the phrase 'Having finished his work, he went home.'
Teachers often ask students to identify the perfect participle in a given sentence.
When writing essays, it is helpful to know when to use the perfect participle for clarity.

Etymology

Formed from 'perfect', meaning 'completed', and 'participle', from Latin 'participium'.
