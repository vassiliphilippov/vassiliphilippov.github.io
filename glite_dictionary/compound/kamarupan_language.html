Kamarupan language

1. NOUN

/ kəˈmɑːrʊpən ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / kəˈmɑːrʊpən ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
Any of a group of Tibeto-Burman languages spoken in northeastern India and parts of western Myanmar.
a group of Tibeto-Burman languages in northeastern India

Examples

Many dialects of the Kamarupan language are still spoken today in Assam and Arunachal Pradesh.
Linguists have documented several endangered Kamarupan languages to preserve their cultural heritage.
The Kamarupan language family exhibits significant diversity across northeastern India.

Etymology

From Kamarupa, an ancient kingdom in northeast India; used in linguistics to name language groups in that region.
