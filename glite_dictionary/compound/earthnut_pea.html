earthnut pea

1. NOUN

/ ˈɜrθ.nʌt pi / (AmE), / ˈɜːθ.nʌt piː / (BrE)
A European plant (Lathyrus tuberosus) having small edible tubers, used as food and, in Scotland, to flavor whiskey.
tuber-producing European plant

Examples

The roots of the earthnut pea have been used as a delicacy in rural European cooking.
Some Scottish whisky makers add earthnut pea to infuse a unique, nutty flavor.
She foraged for earthnut pea tubers to prepare a traditional dish.
