family Buccinidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli bʌkˈsɪnɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈfamɪli bʊkˈsɪnɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of marine gastropod molluscs commonly known as whelks.
a family of marine molluscs (whelks)

Examples

The family Buccinidae includes many species of marine snails found in cold waters.
Scientists discovered a new species belonging to the family Buccinidae off the coast of Japan.
Many members of the family Buccinidae have spiral shells and are known for their predatory habits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whelk - a common name for various marine snails in this family

Etymology

From Latin 'Buccinum', the type genus, and the suffix '-idae' denoting a family in zoological taxonomy.
