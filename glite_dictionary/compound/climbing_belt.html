climbing belt

1. NOUN

/ ˈklaɪmɪŋ bɛlt / (AmE), / ˈklaɪmɪŋ bɛlt / (BrE)
a strong belt, often made of rope or webbing and sometimes with a seat, worn around the waist to provide security and support while climbing, especially to reach high places such as trees or cliffs
supportive belt for climbing safely

Examples

Before attempting to scale the trunk, the beekeeper secured his climbing belt tightly around his waist.
The old climbing belt was frayed and in need of replacement before the next expedition.
Using a climbing belt, she was able to safely reach the hive high up in the branches.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: safety harness - a device fastening a person to an object for safety in climbing and other activities
