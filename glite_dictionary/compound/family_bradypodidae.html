family Bradypodidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ˌbrædɪˈpoʊdɪˌdi / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli ˌbrædɪˈpəʊdɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of edentate mammals comprising the true sloths, native to Central and South America
family of mammals including true sloths

Examples

The family Bradypodidae consists of several genera of tree-dwelling sloths found in Central and South America.
Scientists have studied the evolutionary adaptations of the family Bradypodidae in relation to their arboreal lifestyle.
Members of the family Bradypodidae are easily recognized by their slow movements and long, curved claws.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sloth - slow-moving tree-dwelling mammal of Central and South America

Etymology

From Neo-Latin *Bradypodidae*, based on genus Bradypus (sloth) and Greek -idae (family suffix).
