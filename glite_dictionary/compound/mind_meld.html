mind meld

1. NOUN

/ maɪnd mɛld / (AmE), / maɪnd mɛld / (BrE)
a deep and intuitive understanding or connection between people, often resulting in seamless collaboration or communication
deep intuitive connection between people

Examples

The two colleagues developed a mind meld that allowed them to anticipate each other's thoughts during meetings.
After hours of discussion, the design team finally experienced a mind meld and agreed on a creative solution.
Great writing partnerships often rely on a kind of mind meld to produce their best work.

Synset Examples

Their ideas created a mind meld that impressed everyone.
The team achieved a mind meld during the brainstorming session.
They had a mind meld about the project strategy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rapport - a close and harmonious relationship in which people understand each other's feelings or ideas

Etymology

Originally from the science fiction TV series 'Star Trek', where it referred to a telepathic link between minds.
