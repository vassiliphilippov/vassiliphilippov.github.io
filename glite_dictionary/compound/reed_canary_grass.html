reed canary grass

1. NOUN

/ ɹid kəˈnɛɹi ɡɹæs / (AmE), / ɹiːd kəˈneəɹi ɡrɑːs / (BrE)
a tall perennial grass found in marshy meadows and ditches, with broad leaves, native to Europe and North America
a tall marshy grass species

Examples

Reed canary grass is commonly found along riverbanks and in wetland restoration projects.
The rapid spread of reed canary grass can disrupt native plant communities.
Farmers sometimes plant reed canary grass in ditches to reduce soil erosion.
