dark field illumination

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɑrk fiːld ɪˌluːməˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈdɑːk fiːld ɪˌljuːmɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
a technique in microscopy in which specimens are illuminated by scattered light, causing them to appear bright against a dark background
microscopy technique using scattered light

Examples

Using dark field illumination, the bacteria were clearly visible as glowing shapes against the black background.
The laboratory technician switched to dark field illumination to enhance the visibility of the living cells.
When viewed with dark field illumination, tiny particles in the sample could be easily detected.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: bright field illumination - microscopy technique in which specimens appear dark against a bright background

Etymology

From 'dark field' (region without direct light) and 'illumination' (lighting), used in microscopy terminology.
