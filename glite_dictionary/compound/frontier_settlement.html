frontier settlement

1. NOUN

/ frʌnˈtɪr ˈsɛt.əl.mənt / (AmE), / frʌnˈtɪə ˈsɛt.əl.mənt / (BrE)
a community or settlement established on the border of settled or civilized territory, especially during periods of expansion into new regions
community on the border of civilization

Examples

Pioneers built the first frontier settlement in the Ohio Valley during the early 1800s.
Life in a frontier settlement was harsh, with limited supplies and frequent dangers.
The museum displays artifacts from a typical frontier settlement of the nineteenth century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: outpost - a small military camp or position at some distance from the main force, often in a remote or frontier region
