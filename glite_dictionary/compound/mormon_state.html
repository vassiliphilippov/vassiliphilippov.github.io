Mormon State

1. PROPN

/ ˈmɔːrmən steɪt / (AmE), / ˈmɔːmən steɪt / (BrE)
A historical nickname for the state of Utah, settled by Mormons in 1847.
nickname for Utah (US state)

Examples

Many pioneers traveled thousands of miles to settle in the Mormon State.
The Mormon State is renowned for its breathtaking national parks and unique religious history.
Salt Lake City, located in the Mormon State, is home to the headquarters of the LDS Church.
