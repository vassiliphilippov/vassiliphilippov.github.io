crown of thorns

1. NOUN

/ kraʊn əv θɔrnz / (AmE), / kraʊn əv θɔːnz / (BrE)
a bushy spurge (Euphorbia milii) native to Madagascar, with long, spiny stems, few leaves, and flowers with bright scarlet bracts
a spiny Madagascan spurge plant

Examples

The crown of thorns is popular as a houseplant because of its colorful bracts and resilience.
She pricked her finger on the spiny stem of the crown of thorns while moving the plant.
Gardeners often choose crown of thorns for its drought tolerance and striking appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Euphorbia milii - the scientific name of crown of thorns

Etymology

Allusion to the Biblical crown of thorns; from the spiny appearance of its stems.

2. NOUN

/ kraʊn əv θɔrnz / (AmE), / kraʊn əv θɔːnz / (BrE)
a source of intense pain or suffering, especially one that must be endured with patience
source of intense suffering

Examples

The constant pain in his leg was a true crown of thorns throughout his life.
For the artist, fame became a crown of thorns rather than a blessing.
Watching his friend suffer from illness was a crown of thorns he could hardly bear.

Synset Examples

that is his cross to bear
he bears his afflictions like a crown of thorns

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cross to bear - a burden or affliction that one must endure

Etymology

Biblical reference to the woven crown of thorns placed on Jesus before his crucifixion.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-14501251-n

3. NOUN

/ kraʊn əv θɔrnz / (AmE), / kraʊn əv θɔːnz / (BrE)
In Christian tradition, a mock crown made of thorn branches that was placed on the head of Jesus before the Crucifixion as a form of torture and humiliation.
mock crown of thorns put on Jesus

Examples

The crown of thorns is a powerful symbol in Christian art and iconography.
Historians continue to debate the fate of the alleged crown of thorns after it was brought to Paris.
During the reenactment, an actor wore a replica of the crown of thorns to represent the Passion of Christ.
