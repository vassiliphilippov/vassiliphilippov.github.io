Guy Fawkes Day

1. NOUN

/ ɡaɪ fɔks deɪ / (AmE), / ɡaɪ ˈfɔːks deɪ / (BrE)
A British festival held on 5 November to commemorate the foiling of the Gunpowder Plot of 1605, traditionally marked by fireworks and the burning of effigies of Guy Fawkes.
British festival on 5 November
british

Examples

Every year, children look forward to Guy Fawkes Day with fireworks and bonfires.
On Guy Fawkes Day, people across Britain remember the foiled attempt to blow up Parliament.
Our town hosts a huge celebration for Guy Fawkes Day every November.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Bonfire Night - an alternative British name for Guy Fawkes Day

Etymology

Named after Guy Fawkes, a conspirator in the failed Gunpowder Plot of 1605.
