mint family

1. NOUN

/ mɪnt ˈfæməli / (AmE), / mɪnt ˈfæmɪli / (BrE)
a family of aromatic herbs and shrubs, including mint, thyme, sage, and rosemary, with flowers resembling lips and four one-seeded nutlets
botanical family of aromatic herbs (Lamiaceae)

Examples

Many common kitchen herbs, such as basil and oregano, belong to the mint family.
The mint family is known for its aromatic leaves and square stems.
Lavender and rosemary are both members of the mint family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Lamiaceae - the scientific name for the mint family
