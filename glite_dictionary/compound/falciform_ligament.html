falciform ligament

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæl.səˌfɔrm ˈlɪɡ.ə.mənt / (AmE), / ˈfalk.sɪ.fɔːm ˈlɪɡ.ɪ.mənt / (BrE)
anatomy: a thin, sickle-shaped ligament that attaches part of the liver to the diaphragm and the anterior abdominal wall.
ligament connecting liver to diaphragm and abdomen
medical

Examples

The surgeon identified the falciform ligament before proceeding with the liver operation.
In human anatomy, the falciform ligament separates the left and right lobes of the liver.
The medical student learned to locate the falciform ligament during her anatomy class.

Etymology

From Latin 'falciformis' (sickle-shaped) + English 'ligament'.
