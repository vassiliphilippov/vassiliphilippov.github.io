escalope de veau Orloff

1. NOUN

/ ɛsˈkæləp də voʊ ɔrˈlɒf / (AmE), / ɛsˈkaləp də vəʊ ɔːˈlɒf / (BrE)
A dish of lightly sautéed veal cutlets layered with Soubise sauce and liver paste, sprinkled with grated Parmesan, and briefly baked.
French baked veal cutlet dish

Examples

For tonight's dinner party, the chef prepared escalope de veau Orloff as the main course.
I tasted escalope de veau Orloff during my trip to Paris and was impressed by its rich flavors.
Classic French menus often feature escalope de veau Orloff among their most elegant dishes.

Etymology

French, meaning 'Orloff-style veal escalope', named after Prince Orloff.
