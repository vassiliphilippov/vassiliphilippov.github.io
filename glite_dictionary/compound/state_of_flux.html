state of flux

1. NOUN

/ steɪt əv flʌks / (AmE), / steɪt əv flʌks / (BrE)
a situation in which things are changing frequently and there is uncertainty about what will happen next
condition of constant change or uncertainty

Examples

After the merger, the company found itself in a state of flux with new management and policies.
Her career was in a state of flux as she considered changing industries.
The political situation remained in a state of flux following the election results.

Synset Examples

the flux following the death of the emperor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: instability - the quality or state of being likely to change suddenly
  • Synonym: uncertainty - the state of being unsure or unknown
  • Antonym: stability - the state of being stable and unchanging

Etymology

From Latin 'fluxus' (flow, movement), used in English since the 17th century to describe continuous change.
