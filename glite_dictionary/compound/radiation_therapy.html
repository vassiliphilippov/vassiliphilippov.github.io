radiation therapy

1. NOUN

/ ˌreɪdiˈeɪʃən ˈθɛrəˌpi / (AmE), / ˌreɪdiˈeɪʃən ˈθɛrəpi / (BrE)
the treatment of disease, especially cancer, by the use of high-energy radiation such as X-rays or radioactive substances to destroy or damage cells
medical treatment using high-energy radiation
medical

Examples

After surgery, the patient underwent radiation therapy to eliminate remaining cancer cells.
Some cancers respond better to radiation therapy than to chemotherapy.
The doctor explained the possible side effects of radiation therapy before beginning the treatment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: radiotherapy - the use of radiation, typically to treat cancer
  • Antonym: chemotherapy - the treatment of disease, especially cancer, using chemical substances
