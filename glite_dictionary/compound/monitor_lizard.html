monitor lizard

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑnɪtər ˌlɪzərd / (AmE), / ˈmɒnɪtə ˌlɪzəd / (BrE)
a large tropical carnivorous lizard native to Africa, Asia, and Australia, known for its strong build and sometimes believed to warn of crocodiles
large tropical carnivorous lizard

Examples

The monitor lizard is known for its strength and agility in hunting prey in the wild.
We spotted a large monitor lizard basking on the riverbank during our trip to Australia.
Some cultures believe the monitor lizard can give warning signs if crocodiles are nearby.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: varanid - any member of the family Varanidae, to which monitor lizards belong

Etymology

From 'monitor', possibly from the belief that the lizard warns of crocodiles, and 'lizard'.
