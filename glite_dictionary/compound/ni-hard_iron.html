Ni-hard iron

1. NOUN

/ naɪ hɑrd ˈaɪ.ərn / (AmE), / naɪ hɑːd ˈaɪ.ən / (BrE)
A type of cast iron alloy to which nickel has been added to increase resistance to abrasion and wear.
nickel-alloyed abrasion-resistant cast iron

Examples

The machinery was constructed from Ni-hard iron to handle abrasive materials.
Due to its durability, Ni-hard iron is commonly used in mining equipment.
Engineers selected Ni-hard iron components to extend the lifespan of the crushers.

Etymology

From 'Ni', symbol for nickel, and 'hard', indicating hardness gained through nickel addition.
