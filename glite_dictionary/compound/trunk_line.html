trunk line

1. NOUN

/ trʌŋk laɪn / (AmE), / trʌŋk laɪn / (BrE)
a principal route or main railway line connecting important destinations; the central or main line in a transport system
main railway or transport route

Examples

The city's main trunk line carries thousands of passengers every day.
An accident on the trunk line disrupted rail services across the country.
Plans are underway to upgrade the old trunk line to accommodate faster trains.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: branch line - a secondary railway route branching off a main line

Etymology

From 'trunk', meaning main stem or central part, combined with 'line' referring to a route or track.

2. NOUN

/ trʌŋk laɪn / (AmE), / trʌŋk laɪn / (BrE)
a main telephone line that connects two telephone exchanges or switching centers directly, used for routing multiple calls.
main telephone line between exchanges

Examples

The company upgraded its trunk line to handle increased call volume between cities.
A fault in the trunk line can disrupt service across large regions.
Engineers worked overnight to repair the damaged trunk line connecting the two exchanges.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: telephone line - a wire or cable that carries telephone signals
