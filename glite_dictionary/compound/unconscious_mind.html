unconscious mind

1. NOUN

/ ʌnˈkɑnʃəs ˌmaɪnd / (AmE), / ʌnˈkɒnʃəs ˌmaɪnd / (BrE)
the part of the mind containing thoughts, feelings, and memories of which a person is not aware but which influence behavior
part of the mind not consciously accessible

Examples

Dreams are often believed to reveal hidden desires from the unconscious mind.
Many psychological theories emphasize the power of the unconscious mind in shaping behavior.
She had no idea that her unconscious mind was influencing her decisions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: subconscious - the part of the mind that is not fully conscious but influences actions
  • Antonym: conscious mind - the part of the mind aware of thoughts, feelings, and surroundings
