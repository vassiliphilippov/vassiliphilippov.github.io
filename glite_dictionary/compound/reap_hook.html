reap hook

1. NOUN

/ ˈrip hʊk / (AmE), / ˈriːp hʊk / (BrE)
A hand tool with a curved blade and a short handle, used for cutting grass or crops.
curved blade tool for cutting crops

Examples

The farmer sharpened his reap hook before heading out to the fields.
During the harvest festival, antique reap hooks were displayed as part of the exhibition.
He swung the reap hook in a practiced arc to cut the tall grass.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sickle - a similar curved-blade tool for harvesting grain or grass

Etymology

From 'reap' (to cut crops) and 'hook,' referring to the tool's shape.
