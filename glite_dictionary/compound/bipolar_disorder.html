bipolar disorder

1. NOUN

/ baɪˈpoʊlər dɪˈsɔrdər / (AmE), / baɪˈpəʊlə dɪˈsɔːdə / (BrE)
A mental health condition characterized by alternating periods of mania and depression.
mental disorder with mania and depression
medical

Examples

She was diagnosed with bipolar disorder after experiencing extreme mood swings.
Treatment for bipolar disorder often involves medication and therapy.
People with bipolar disorder can lead fulfilling lives with proper support.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mania - a mental state of elevated mood and activity
  • Synonym: depression - a mental state of low mood and energy

Etymology

From 'bipolar', meaning 'having two poles', and 'disorder'. The term reflects the alternating mood states.
