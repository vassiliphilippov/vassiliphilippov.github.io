smoke bush

1. NOUN

/ smoʊk bʊʃ / (AmE), / sməʊk bʊʃ / (BrE)
a deciduous shrub or small tree of the genus Cotinus, known for its clusters of airy, smoke-like pinkish-purple flowers
ornamental shrub with smoke-like flower clusters

Examples

The smoke bush in my garden turns a brilliant red in autumn.
Gardeners love the smoke bush for its unusual feathery blooms that resemble puffs of smoke.
We planted a smoke bush to add color and texture to the landscape.

2. NOUN

/ smoʊk bʊʃ / (AmE), / sməʊk bʊʃ / (BrE)
any of several shrubs of the genus Conospermum, typically bearing panicles of mostly white, woolly flowers native to Australia
Australian shrub with white woolly flowers

Examples

We spotted a beautiful smoke bush blooming near the edge of the woodland reserve.
This region is well known for its dense thickets of smoke bush in spring.
Botanists are studying the pollination habits of the smoke bush and its effect on local ecosystems.
