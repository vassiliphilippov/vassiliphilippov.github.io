yellow locust

1. NOUN

/ ˈjɛloʊ ˈloʊkəst / (AmE), / ˈjɛləʊ ˈləʊkəst / (BrE)
a large tree of eastern and central United States with thorny branches, pinnately compound leaves, and hanging clusters of white flowers, widely cultivated and naturalized in temperate regions
thorny tree with white flowers, native to U.S.

Examples

The yellow locust is valued for its hard, durable wood and rapid growth.
A beautiful yellow locust stood in the corner of their backyard, blooming every spring.
Many city parks have planted yellow locust trees because of their resilience in urban environments.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: black locust - a similar tree, Robinia pseudoacacia, with white flowers and dark bark
