retirement savings account

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈtaɪərmənt ˈseɪvɪŋz əˈkaʊnt / (AmE), / rɪˈtaɪəmənt ˈseɪvɪŋz əˈkaʊnt / (BrE)
an account or plan used for saving money intended to provide income after retirement
account for saving for retirement

Examples

Many people open a retirement savings account in their twenties to prepare for the future.
A retirement savings account offers tax advantages to encourage long-term saving.
You should contribute regularly to your retirement savings account to maximize its growth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pension - a regular payment made during retirement from an investment fund
