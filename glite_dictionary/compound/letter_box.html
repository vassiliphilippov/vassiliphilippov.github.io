letter box

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛtər ˌbɑks / (AmE), / ˈletə ˌbɒks / (BrE)
a numbered compartment in a post office where letters and packages are placed for collection by the person it is assigned to
post office compartment for collecting mail

Examples

He checked his letter box at the post office every morning before work.
The package was waiting for her in her letter box last Friday.
You can rent a letter box if you need a secure place to receive mail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: post office box - a locked box in a post office assigned to a person or organization for receiving mail

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɛt̬ɚ ˌbɑks / (AmE), / ˈlet.ə ˌbɒks / (BrE)
a public box in which letters and packages are placed for collection by the postal service
public box for mailing letters

Examples

I dropped my postcards in the letter box outside the station.
Make sure your letter is in the letter box before the last collection.
The letter box on the corner is painted bright red.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: postbox - a public box for mail collection
  • Synonym: mailbox - US term for a box where mail is placed for collection

3. NOUN

/ ˈlɛtər bɑks / (AmE), / ˈletə bɒks / (BrE)
A box or slot at a house or building where letters and other mail are delivered.
box for receiving delivered mail
british

Examples

She placed the package inside the letter box by the front door.
Their new house has a bright red letter box for all their mail.
I found a postcard from my friend waiting in the letter box this morning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mailbox - a box for receiving incoming mail, especially used in American English
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
