economic geology

1. NOUN

/ ˌiːkəˈnɑmɪk dʒiˈɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ˌiːkəˈnɒmɪk dʒiˈɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the branch of geology concerned with the study of earth materials of economic value, such as minerals, ores, and fossil fuels
study of economically valuable earth materials

Examples

She chose to specialize in economic geology to better understand mineral resources.
Courses in economic geology are essential for careers in mining and resource exploration.
Modern economic geology addresses both energy minerals and environmental concerns.
