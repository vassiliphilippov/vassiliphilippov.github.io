parliamentary monarchy

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑrləˌmɛntɛri məˈnɑrki / (AmE), / ˌpɑːləˈmɛntəri məˈnɑːki / (BrE)
A system of government in which a monarch acts as head of state within the parameters of a constitution and a parliament exercises legislative power.
system with monarch and parliament

Examples

The United Kingdom is a classic example of a parliamentary monarchy where the monarch has limited powers.
Unlike an absolute monarchy, a parliamentary monarchy relies on elected representatives to make laws.
Several European countries have adopted the parliamentary monarchy model for their governments.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: absolute monarchy - a system where the monarch has absolute power without a parliament
  • Synonym: constitutional monarchy - a monarchy limited by a constitution, often overlapping with parliamentary monarchy

Etymology

parliamentary from 'parliament' (from Old French) + monarchy (from Greek 'monarkhia', rule by one).
