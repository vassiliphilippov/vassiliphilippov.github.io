piping plover

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪpɪŋ ˈploʊvər / (AmE), / ˈpaɪpɪŋ ˈpləʊvə / (BrE)
a small, pale-colored shorebird (Charadrius melodus) native to eastern North America, noted for its soft whistled call
North American shorebird with whistled call

Examples

The piping plover is listed as a threatened species due to habitat loss.
Conservationists monitor piping plover nests each spring along the Atlantic coast.
We spotted a pair of piping plovers feeding quietly on the sandy beach.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plover - a wading bird of the family Charadriidae, similar in size and appearance

Etymology

From the bird's soft, pipe-like call; 'plover' from Old French 'pluvier', from Latin 'pluvia' (rain).
