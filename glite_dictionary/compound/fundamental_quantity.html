fundamental quantity

1. NOUN

/ ˌfʌndəˈmɛntəl ˈkwɑntəti / (AmE), / ˌfʌndəˈmɛntəl ˈkwɒntɪti / (BrE)
one of the basic quantities, such as length, mass, time, or electric current, that form the foundation of a system of measurement
basic measurement quantity in a system

Examples

Length is considered a fundamental quantity in the International System of Units.
In physics, every derived unit can be expressed in terms of fundamental quantities.
The selection of fundamental quantities forms the basis for all scientific measurement systems.
