Associate in Arts

1. NOUN

/ əˈsoʊʃiət ɪn ɑrts / (AmE), / əˈsəʊsɪət ɪn ɑːts / (BrE)
A two-year undergraduate degree focused on the liberal arts, typically awarded by colleges in the United States.
two-year liberal arts college degree

Examples

She earned her Associate in Arts before transferring to a four-year university.
Many community colleges offer an Associate in Arts degree in various subjects.
Holding an Associate in Arts can provide entry-level job opportunities in multiple fields.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Associate of Science - a two-year college degree focused on science or technical fields

Etymology

From 'associate' (Middle French *associé*) and 'arts' (from Latin *ars*), used in academic contexts since the late 19th century.
