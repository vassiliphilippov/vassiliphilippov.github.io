angstrom unit

1. NOUN

/ ˈæŋstrəm ˌjuː.nɪt / (AmE), / ˈæŋstrəm ˌjuː.nɪt / (BrE)
a metric unit of length equal to one ten-billionth of a meter (0.1 nanometers), commonly used to express wavelengths of electromagnetic radiation
unit of length (0.1 nanometers)

Examples

The wavelength of visible light ranges from about 4000 to 7000 angstrom units.
An X-ray has a typical wavelength measured in angstrom units.
Many atomic and molecular dimensions are often expressed using the angstrom unit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: angstrom - a unit of length equal to 0.1 nanometers
  • Degree Higher: nanometer - a unit of length equal to one billionth of a meter

Etymology

From Swedish *ångström*, after Anders Jonas Ångström (1814–1874), Swedish physicist.
