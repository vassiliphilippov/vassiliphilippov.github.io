mons pubis

1. NOUN

/ mɑnz ˈpjuːbɪs / (AmE), / mɒnz ˈpjuːbɪs / (BrE)
the rounded mound of fatty tissue lying over the pubic bone in women
mound of fatty tissue over pubic bone (female anatomy)
medical

Examples

Many anatomy textbooks include detailed diagrams of the mons pubis as part of the female reproductive system.
After puberty, hair typically starts to grow on the mons pubis.
The mons pubis can vary in size and shape from person to person.

Etymology

From Latin 'mons' meaning 'mountain' and 'pubis' meaning 'of the groin.'
