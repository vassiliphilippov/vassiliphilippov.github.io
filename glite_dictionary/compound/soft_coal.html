soft coal

1. NOUN

/ sɔft koʊl / (AmE), / sɒft kəʊl / (BrE)
A type of coal, also known as bituminous coal, that is rich in tarry hydrocarbons and burns readily with a smoky yellow flame.
bituminous coal; coal that burns with a smoky flame

Examples

Heating systems once used soft coal, which produced a lot of smoke and soot.
The region is known for its abundant deposits of soft coal.
Because soft coal contains more impurities, it is less environmentally friendly than anthracite.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bituminous coal - a type of soft coal with high tar content
