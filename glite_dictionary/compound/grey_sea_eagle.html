grey sea eagle

1. NOUN

/ ɡreɪ si ˈiːɡəl / (AmE), / ɡreɪ siː ˈiːɡl̩ / (BrE)
a large greyish-brown eagle with a short, wedge-shaped white tail, found in Europe and Greenland
a large eagle of Europe and Greenland

Examples

The grey sea eagle can often be seen soaring over coastal cliffs in Scotland.
Researchers have monitored the nesting habits of the grey sea eagle for several years.
The adult grey sea eagle is distinguished by its impressive wingspan and distinctive white tail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: white-tailed eagle - a large eagle with a white tail, also known as grey sea eagle
