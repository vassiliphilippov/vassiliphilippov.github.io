yellowfin tuna

1. NOUN

/ ˈjɛloʊfɪn ˈtuːnə / (AmE), / ˈjɛləʊfɪn ˈtjuːnə / (BrE)
a species of large tuna with yellow fins, found worldwide in tropical and subtropical oceans, often weighing up to 400 pounds
a large tropical tuna species with yellow fins

Examples

The chef prepared a delicious dish using freshly caught yellowfin tuna.
Yellowfin tuna are highly prized by fishermen for their size and strength.
Sushi restaurants often feature yellowfin tuna on their menus due to its firm texture and mild flavor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tuna - a large ocean fish of the mackerel family, valued for food
