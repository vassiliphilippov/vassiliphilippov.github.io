bastion fort

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæs.tʃən fɔrt / (AmE), / ˈbæs.tɪən fɔːt / (BrE)
A fortification with multiple outward-pointing triangular bastions, designed to allow defensive coverage of all sides; developed in Renaissance Italy.
fort with projecting triangular bastions

Examples

The city was protected by a massive bastion fort with eight projecting points to thwart attackers.
Historians studied the remains of the bastion fort built during the Renaissance.
The design of a bastion fort allowed defenders to cover all approaches with overlapping fields of fire.

Synset Examples

Star forts were employed by Michelangelo in the defensive earthworks of Florence, and refined in the sixteenth century by Baldassare Peruzzi and Scamozzi.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: star fort - a synonym for bastion fort, named for its star-shaped plan

Etymology

From French 'bastion' (a projecting part of a fortification) and English 'fort', ultimately from Latin 'fortis' (strong).
