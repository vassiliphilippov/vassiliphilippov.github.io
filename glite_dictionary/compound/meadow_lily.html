meadow lily

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛdoʊ ˈlɪli / (AmE), / ˈmɛdəʊ ˈlɪli / (BrE)
a common North American lily (Lilium canadense) with nodding yellow or reddish flowers spotted with brown, found in meadows and open woods in eastern United States
North American lily with spotted yellow/red flowers

Examples

The meadow lily blooms in early summer, brightening the fields with its vibrant colors.
We spotted a group of meadow lilies near the edge of the forest during our hike.
Botanists consider the meadow lily an important native species in eastern North America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Canada lily - another name for the meadow lily, Lilium canadense
