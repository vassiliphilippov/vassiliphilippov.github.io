falling off

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɑlɪŋ ˌɔf / (AmE), / ˈfɔːlɪŋ ˌɒf / (BrE)
a noticeable decline in performance, quality, or quantity
decline in performance or quality

Examples

There has been a significant falling off in sales over the past quarter.
The coach was concerned about the team's recent falling off in performance.
We noticed a falling off in attendance after the new policy was introduced.

Synset Examples

the team went into a slump
a gradual slack in output
a drop-off in attendance
a falloff in quality

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drop-off - a decline or decrease in level, quantity, or activity
  • Synonym: slump - a period of decline, especially in activity or performance
  • Antonym: improvement - a rise or increase in quality or performance; the opposite of a decline
