double glazing

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʌbl ˌɡleɪzɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdʌbəl ˌɡleɪzɪŋ / (BrE)
A window construction with two layers of glass separated by a space, designed to reduce heat loss and noise.
window with two panes of glass

Examples

Modern homes often use double glazing to improve energy efficiency and reduce heating costs.
The double glazing keeps the house quiet, even though it faces a busy street.
Installing double glazing can also help prevent condensation on the inside of your windows.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: triple glazing - window with three panes of glass
  • Degree Lower: single glazing - window with one pane of glass
