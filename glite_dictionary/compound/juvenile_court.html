juvenile court

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒuːvəˌnaɪl kɔrt / (AmE), / ˈdʒuːvɪnaɪl kɔːt / (BrE)
a law court that deals with cases involving children and young people who are accused of breaking the law or who are in need of care and protection
court dealing with cases involving minors
legal

Examples

The juvenile court hears cases involving minors accused of committing crimes.
She was referred to the juvenile court after repeated truancy from school.
Decisions made in juvenile courts prioritize the rehabilitation and welfare of children.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: family court - a court of law that handles cases involving family matters such as custody and divorce
