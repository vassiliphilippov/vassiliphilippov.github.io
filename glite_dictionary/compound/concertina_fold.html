concertina fold

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑnsərˈtinə foʊld / (AmE), / ˌkɒnsəˈtiːnə fəʊld / (BrE)
a pattern of folding where material is alternately folded back and forth in parallel, resembling an accordion or a zig-zag.
zig-zag accordion-like folding pattern

Examples

The invitation was cleverly designed with a concertina fold, making it fun to open.
Many tourist maps use a concertina fold so they can expand and collapse easily.
Geologists examined the concertina folds found in the rock layers.

Synset Examples

Seen from above, concertina folds resemble a Z or M or series of zigs and zags.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accordion fold - a folding pattern similar to a concertina fold

Etymology

From 'concertina', a musical instrument shaped like an accordion, reflecting the folding style's resemblance.
