Massachusetts Institute of Technology

1. PROPN

/ ˌmæsəˈtʃuːsɪts ɪnˈstɪtʃuːt əv tɛkˈnɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ˌmæsəˈtʃuːsɪts ɪnˈstɪtjuːt əv tɛkˈnɒlədʒi / (BrE)
A prestigious university in Cambridge, Massachusetts, especially known for its programs in engineering, science, and technology.
university in Cambridge, MA, USA

Examples

She was accepted to Massachusetts Institute of Technology to study computer science.
The Massachusetts Institute of Technology has produced many Nobel Prize winners in physics and chemistry.
Research at Massachusetts Institute of Technology often leads to breakthrough innovations.

Etymology

Named after the state of Massachusetts; 'Institute of Technology' is a common designation for technical institutions.
