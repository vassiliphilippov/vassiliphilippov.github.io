genus Balanus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs bəˈleɪ.nəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs bəˈleɪ.nəs / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Balanidae, comprising certain barnacle species found on rocks and ship hulls.
barnacle genus; type of Balanidae

Examples

Marine biologists studied the genus Balanus to understand how barnacles affect ship hulls.
The genus Balanus includes several species commonly found clinging to rocky shorelines.
Researchers identified a new species within the genus Balanus after examining barnacle specimens from the Pacific coast.

Etymology

From Latin 'balanus' meaning 'acorn', referring to the shape of some barnacles.
