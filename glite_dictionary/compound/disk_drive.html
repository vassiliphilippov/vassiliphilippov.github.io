disk drive

1. NOUN

/ dɪsk draɪv / (AmE), / dɪsk draɪv / (BrE)
A device in a computer that holds and spins a magnetic or optical disk, enabling information to be read from or written to it.
device for reading and writing disks

Examples

The computer's disk drive stopped working, so she couldn't access her files.
You should always eject the CD before removing it from the disk drive.
Modern laptops often replace the traditional disk drive with solid-state storage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hard drive - a high-capacity disk drive used for storing and retrieving digital information
  • Degree Higher: solid-state drive - computer storage device that uses flash memory instead of spinning disks
  • Degree Lower: floppy disk drive - a disk drive that reads and writes floppy disks
