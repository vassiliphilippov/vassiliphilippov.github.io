electrical capacity

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌlɛktrɪkəl kəˈpæsəti / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛktrɪkəl kəˈpæsəti / (BrE)
the property of an object or device that enables it to store an electric charge, measured as capacitance
ability to store electric charge

Examples

The electrical capacity of the capacitor determines how much charge it can store.
Modern electronic circuits often require components with high electrical capacity.
Engineers test the electrical capacity of materials before using them in power systems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: capacitance - the ability of a system to store an electric charge

Etymology

Derived from 'electric' (from Greek 'ēlektron', meaning amber) and 'capacity' (from Latin 'capacitas', meaning ability to hold).
