in the interstice

1. ADP

/ ɪn ði ɪnˈtɜːr.stɪs / (AmE), / ɪn ði ɪnˈtɜː.stɪs / (BrE)
in the narrow or small space between things
in the small space between things

Examples

She discovered an old coin in the interstice of the floorboards.
Dust often collects in the interstice between window panes and frames.
His keys had slipped in the interstice between the car seats.

Synset Examples

He found his phone in the interstice of the couch cushions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interstice - a small or narrow space between things or parts

Etymology

From Latin 'interstitium' (interval, space between).
