Victorian era

1. NOUN

/ vɪkˈtɔːriən ˈɛrə / (AmE), / vɪkˈtɔːrɪən ˈɪərə / (BrE)
the period in British history during the reign of Queen Victoria (1837–1901), noted for distinctive social values, culture, and moral standards
period of Queen Victoria's reign in Britain
british

Examples

Many classic novels, such as those by Charles Dickens, were written during the Victorian era.
The Victorian era was characterized by strict social codes and rapid industrial progress.
Museums often display artifacts from the Victorian era to illustrate the period's unique style.

Etymology

Named after Queen Victoria (1819–1901), monarch of the United Kingdom.
