genus Haemanthus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs hiːˈmæn.θəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs hiːˈmæn.θʌs / (BrE)
a genus of African deciduous or evergreen bulbous herbs, commonly known as blood lilies
genus of African bulbous herbs (blood lilies)

Examples

The genus Haemanthus includes several ornamental species prized for their striking red flowers.
Researchers studying bulbous plants often focus on the genus Haemanthus due to its diversity.
Many gardeners are attracted to the genus Haemanthus because of its unique flower heads.

Etymology

From Greek 'haima' meaning blood and 'anthos' meaning flower, referencing the red flowers of some species.
