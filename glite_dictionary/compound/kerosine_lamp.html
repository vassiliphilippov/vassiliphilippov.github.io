kerosine lamp

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɛrəˌsin læmp / (AmE), / ˈkɛrəˌsiːn læmp / (BrE)
A lamp that burns kerosine oil to produce light, often used before electric lighting became widespread or where electricity is unavailable.
lamp burning kerosine for light

Examples

The old farmhouse was illuminated each night by a kerosine lamp sitting on the kitchen table.
During the power outage, we relied on a kerosine lamp for light.
Collectors often seek out antique kerosine lamps because of their historical value.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oil lamp - a lamp that burns oil for light

Etymology

From 'kerosine', coined in 1850 from Greek 'keros' (wax) + '-ine'.
