head cabbage plant

1. NOUN

/ hɛd ˈkæbɪdʒ plænt / (AmE), / hɛd ˈkæbɪdʒ plɑːnt / (BrE)
A cultivated plant of the cabbage species that produces a short, thick stalk and a large, compact head of edible green leaves.
cultivated cabbage plant with edible head

Examples

The head cabbage plant is commonly used in salads and coleslaw due to its crisp leaves.
Farmers planted rows of head cabbage plants in the fertile valley.
The gardener explained how to care for a head cabbage plant from seedling to harvest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cabbage - a leafy green vegetable with a dense, round head, widely cultivated as a food plant
  • Degree Lower: kale - a cultivated form of cabbage with loose, non-head-forming leaves
