austenitic manganese steel

1. NOUN

/ ˌɔːstɪˈnɪtɪk ˈmæŋɡəniːz stiːl / (AmE), / ˌɔːstɪˈnɪtɪk ˈmæŋɡəniːz stiːl / (BrE)
A type of steel containing 10–14% manganese and characterized by high resistance to wear and shock.
manganese-rich wear-resistant steel

Examples

Because of its durability, austenitic manganese steel is often used in rock-crushing equipment.
The new machinery features parts made of austenitic manganese steel to reduce wear and maintenance costs.
Engineers selected austenitic manganese steel for the railway tracks to withstand frequent heavy impacts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: manganese steel - steel alloyed with a significant amount of manganese

Etymology

Named after Sir Robert Austen and due to its austenitic crystal structure, with 'manganese' from Latin 'magnes', meaning magnet.
