tribal sheik

1. NOUN

/ ˈtraɪbəl ʃeɪk / (AmE), / ˈtraɪbəl ʃiːk / (BrE)
the leader of an Arab tribe, village, or family, often regarded as an elder or chief in traditional society
leader of an Arab tribe or family

Examples

The tribal sheik resolved disputes among villagers and made decisions on behalf of the community.
As tribal sheik, he was highly respected for his wisdom and sense of justice.
The newly appointed tribal sheik hosted a gathering for all the elders in the region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chief - the leader of a group or tribe

Etymology

From Arabic 'shaykh', meaning elder or chief.
