leap of faith

1. NOUN

/ lip əv feɪθ / (AmE), / liːp əv feɪθ / (BrE)
an act of believing in or attempting something whose outcome is uncertain, requiring trust or courage
an act of trust despite risk or doubt

Examples

Quitting his stable job to pursue art was a real leap of faith.
Their relationship began with a leap of faith and a lot of hope.
Investing in the start-up required a huge leap of faith from everyone involved.

Synset Examples

It was a leap of faith to trust her instincts.
He took a leap of faith by moving abroad.
Starting my own business was a leap of faith.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trust - firm belief in the reliability or ability of someone or something
  • Synonym: risk - exposure to danger, harm, or loss
