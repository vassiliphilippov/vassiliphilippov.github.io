family Physeteridae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli faɪˌsiːtəˈraɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli faɪˌsiːtɪˈraɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of large marine mammals comprising the sperm whales.
taxonomic family of sperm whales

Examples

The family Physeteridae includes the largest species of toothed whales.
Scientists study the evolution of the family Physeteridae to understand deep-sea adaptations.
Members of the family Physeteridae are known for their ability to dive to great depths.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sperm whale - A species of large toothed whale, Physeter macrocephalus, belonging to family Physeteridae

Etymology

From modern Latin 'Physeteridae', based on Latin 'physeter' (sperm whale).
