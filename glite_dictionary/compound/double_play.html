double play

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʌbəl pleɪ / (AmE), / ˈdʌbəl pleɪ / (BrE)
a play in baseball in which two players are put out as the result of continuous action
a baseball play resulting in two outs

Examples

The shortstop started a double play to end the inning.
Fans cheered when their team turned a crucial double play in the ninth.
Executing a flawless double play takes skill and teamwork.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: triple play - a play in baseball in which three players are put out as a result of continuous action

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʌbəl pleɪ / (AmE), / ˈdʌbəl pleɪ / (BrE)
(in baseball) a defensive play in which two offensive players are put out as the result of continuous action.
baseball play resulting in two outs

Examples

The team turned a quick double play to end the inning.
She practiced fielding ground balls to help improve her double play skills.
It was a tight game until a spectacular double play changed the momentum.

Synset Examples

The shortstop made an incredible double play.
They executed a perfect double play in the final inning.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: triple play - a defensive play in baseball resulting in three outs
  • Degree Lower: to single out - to put one offensive player out in a play
