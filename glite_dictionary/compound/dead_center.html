dead center

1. NOUN

/ dɛd ˈsɛn.tɚ / (AmE), / dɛd ˈsɛn.tə / (BrE)
the position of a crank when it is aligned with the connecting rod and is not capable of exerting torque
position in mechanics where crank and rod align

Examples

When the piston is at dead center, the engine cannot produce any rotational force.
The mechanic explained that starting the engine at dead center can be difficult.
You should avoid stopping the flywheel at dead center to ensure smooth restarting.

2. NOUN

/ dɛd ˈsɛntɚ / (AmE), / dɛd ˈsɛntə / (BrE)
exactly in the middle of something; the precise central point
exactly in the middle

Examples

Her signature was written dead center on the page, bold and unmistakable.
During the performance, the spotlight shone dead center on the lead actor.
