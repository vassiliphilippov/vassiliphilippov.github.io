banded sand snake

1. NOUN

/ ˈbændɪd sænd sneɪk / (AmE), / ˈbændɪd sand sneɪk / (BrE)
a small snake native to the southwestern United States that swims through sand or loamy soil and has banded markings resembling those of coral snakes
a small banded sand-dwelling snake of the US Southwest

Examples

The banded sand snake is known for its ability to move quickly through loose desert soils.
Many people mistake the banded sand snake for the venomous coral snake due to its similar banding.
Researchers observed a banded sand snake burrowing beneath the surface of a sandy dune.
