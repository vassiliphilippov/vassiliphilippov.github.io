Alpine lady fern

1. NOUN

/ ˈælpaɪn ˈleɪdi fɝn / (AmE), / ˈælpaɪn ˈleɪdi fɜːn / (BrE)
A species of lady fern with deeply divided leaves, native to mountainous regions such as the Rocky Mountains.
mountainous lady fern species

Examples

The Alpine lady fern thrives in the cool, shaded forests of the Rocky Mountains.
Gardeners often plant the Alpine lady fern for its delicate, feathery appearance.
We spotted several Alpine lady ferns growing alongside the mountain stream.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lady fern - a common fern with delicate, divided fronds
