mathematical optimization

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæθəˈmætɪkəl ˌɑptɪməˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌmæθəˈmætɪkəl ˌɒptɪmaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
The process of finding the best solution or outcome, according to specific criteria, from among available alternatives using mathematical methods.
finding the best solution mathematically

Examples

Advanced control systems often rely on mathematical optimization to improve performance and efficiency.
Researchers developed a new algorithm for mathematical optimization in logistics applications.
The course covers topics such as linear algebra and mathematical optimization.

Synset Examples

High-level controllers such as Model predictive control (MPC) or Real-Time Optimization (RTO) employ mathematical optimization. These algorithms run online and repeatedly determine values for decision variables, such as choke openings in a process plant, by iteratively solving a mathematical optimization problem including constraints and a model of the system to be controlled.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: optimization - the process of making something as effective or functional as possible

Etymology

From 'mathematical', relating to mathematics, and 'optimization', from Latin 'optimum' (best).
