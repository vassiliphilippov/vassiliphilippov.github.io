Japanese radish

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˈpæniz ˈrædɪʃ / (AmE), / dʒəˈpæniːz ˈrædɪʃ / (BrE)
a large, long white radish native to Japan, often eaten raw or cooked
large white radish from Japan

Examples

The salad was topped with grated Japanese radish for added crunch and flavor.
Stir-fries in Asian cuisine often include sliced Japanese radish as a key ingredient.
She bought a fresh Japanese radish at the market to use in pickles and soups.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: daikon - the Japanese white radish, also known as mooli
