frock coat

1. NOUN

/ frɑk koʊt / (AmE), / frɒk kəʊt / (BrE)
a formal man's coat with knee-length skirts front and back, popular in the 19th century
knee-length formal man's coat (19th century)

Examples

The Victorian gentleman wore a frock coat to formal occasions and business meetings.
He found an old frock coat in his grandfather's wardrobe, complete with brass buttons.
Artists often depict 19th-century political figures in a frock coat and top hat.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: morning coat - a man's formal coat with tails, worn in the 19th century and today
  • Degree Higher: tailcoat - a formal evening coat with a tail at the back

Etymology

From 'frock' (a loose outer garment) and 'coat', with the compound arising in the 19th century to describe this style.
