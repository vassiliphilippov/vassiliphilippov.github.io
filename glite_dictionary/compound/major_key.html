major key

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪdʒɚ ki / (AmE), / ˈmeɪdʒə kiː / (BrE)
a musical key whose harmony is based on the major scale, typically producing a bright or happy sound
musical key based on major scale

Examples

The symphony was composed in a major key, giving it an uplifting feel.
Most pop songs are written in a major key to convey a happy mood.
She prefers the sound of a major key over the more somber minor keys.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: minor key - a key whose harmony is based on the minor scale
