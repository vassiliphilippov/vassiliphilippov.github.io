undercurrent of sadness

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌndɚˌkɜːr(ə)nt əv ˈsædnəs / (AmE), / ˈʌndəkʌrənt ɒv ˈsædnəs / (BrE)
a hidden or subtle feeling of sorrow that is present beneath the surface of someone's mood or a situation
subtle hidden feeling of sorrow

Examples

Despite the cheerful music, there was an undercurrent of sadness running through the film.
Her words sounded optimistic, but I sensed an undercurrent of sadness in her tone.
During the celebration, an undercurrent of sadness lingered among the guests who missed their loved ones.

Synset Examples

His laughter had an undercurrent of sadness.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: sadness - state of being sad; sorrow
  • Synonym: melancholy - a feeling of pensive sadness, often with no obvious cause
