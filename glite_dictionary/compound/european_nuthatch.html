European nuthatch

1. NOUN

/ jʊˈroʊpiən ˈnʌtˌhætʃ / (AmE), / jʊəˈrəʊpiən ˈnʌtˌhætʃ / (BrE)
a small songbird of Eurasia, Sitta europaea, with blue-grey upperparts and a buff or white underside, known for climbing tree trunks and feeding on insects and seeds
Eurasian nuthatch songbird

Examples

The European nuthatch is often seen moving headfirst down tree trunks in search of insects.
We watched a European nuthatch hammering seeds into the bark with remarkable agility.
Gardeners welcomed the European nuthatch for its role in controlling tree pests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nuthatch - a small songbird able to climb tree trunks
