dunce's cap

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʌnsɪz kæp / (AmE), / ˈdʌnsɪz kap / (BrE)
a tall, cone-shaped paper hat that was formerly placed on the head of students who were considered slow or lazy as a form of punishment and humiliation
cone-shaped hat for punishing slow pupils

Examples

In old classrooms, the slowest student was sometimes forced to wear a dunce's cap as punishment.
The dunce's cap is now seen as a symbol of outdated teaching methods.
A photo from the 1920s showed a boy sitting in the corner with a dunce's cap on his head.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dunce - a person who is slow to learn or stupid

Etymology

From 'dunce' (a slow learner or stupid person), originally from the name of the philosopher John Duns Scotus; 'cap' referring to the head covering.
