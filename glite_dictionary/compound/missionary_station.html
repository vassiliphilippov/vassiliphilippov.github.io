missionary station

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪʃəˌnɛri ˈsteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈmɪʃənəri ˈsteɪʃən / (BrE)
an establishment set up by missionaries in a foreign country to conduct religious and charitable work
religious outpost for missionary work

Examples

The church sent supplies to their missionary station in Africa.
Many children attended classes at the missionary station during the 19th century.
She spent five years working at a remote missionary station in South America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mission - group or establishment for religious or charitable work, often abroad
  • Degree Lower: outpost - a small military camp or position at some distance from the main force, or a remote settlement

Etymology

From 'missionary' (person sent to promote a religion) and 'station' (establishment), both from Latin roots.
