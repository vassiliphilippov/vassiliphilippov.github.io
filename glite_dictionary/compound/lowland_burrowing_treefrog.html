lowland burrowing treefrog

1. NOUN

/ ˈloʊˌlænd ˈbɜroʊɪŋ ˈtriˌfrɔg / (AmE), / ˈləʊˌlænd ˈbɜːrɪŋ ˈtriːˌfrɒg / (BrE)
A terrestrial, burrowing, nocturnal frog found in grassy areas and scrub forests of the southwestern United States, known for its very hard upper head surface.
burrowing frog of the US southwest

Examples

The lowland burrowing treefrog is often heard calling after summer rains in Arizona.
Biologists are studying how the lowland burrowing treefrog survives in arid environments.
We spotted two lowland burrowing treefrogs near the edge of the grassy field last night.
