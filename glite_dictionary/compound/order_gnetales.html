order Gnetales

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdər dʒiːˈniːteɪliːz / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə dʒiːˈniːteɪliːz / (BrE)
a taxonomic order of chiefly tropical or xerophytic woody plants, considered closely related to the ancestral line of angiosperms
an order of woody tropical or xerophytic plants

Examples

Plants in the order Gnetales are often adapted to dry or tropical environments.
Scientists debate the evolutionary relationship between the order Gnetales and flowering plants.
The order Gnetales includes genera such as Ephedra, Welwitschia, and Gnetum.

Etymology

From modern Latin Gnetales, based on the genus Gnetum and the standard taxonomic suffix -ales.
