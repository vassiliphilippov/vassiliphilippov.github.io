line organization

1. NOUN

/ laɪn ˌɔːr.ɡə.nəˈzeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / laɪn ˌɔː.ɡə.naɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
an organizational structure in which activities contributing directly to an organization's output are arranged in a clear line of authority from top management to the lowest level
business structure with direct chain of command

Examples

Most factories use a line organization to ensure efficient supervision and a clear command structure.
In a line organization, decisions flow directly from top management to workers on the production floor.
A line organization is often found in smaller companies, where the reporting relationships are straightforward.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: staff organization - an organizational structure where staff specialists advise line managers

2. NOUN

/ laɪn ˌɔːɹɡənəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / laɪn ˌɔːɡənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
an organizational structure in which authority flows in a direct line from top management to employees performing tasks, with each department contributing directly to the organization's output
direct-chain organizational structure

Examples

Many small businesses use a line organization due to its simple chain of command.
In a line organization, each employee reports directly to one supervisor.
The efficiency of a line organization often depends on clear roles and responsibilities.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: line-and-staff organization - an organization structure incorporating both direct authority and specialized assistance
