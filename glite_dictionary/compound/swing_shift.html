swing shift

1. NOUN

/ swɪŋ ʃɪft / (AmE), / swɪŋ ʃɪft / (BrE)
A work shift that takes place during the evening, typically from late afternoon until midnight.
evening work shift
american

Examples

She prefers working the swing shift because she can sleep in during the morning.
After a month on the swing shift, he found it difficult to adjust to a regular schedule.
Many factories offer a swing shift in addition to the day and night shifts.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: night shift - work shift that takes place during the nighttime
  • Degree Lower: day shift - work shift that takes place during the day

Etymology

From 'swing' (to move between times) and 'shift' (a period of work); originated in American labor terminology.
