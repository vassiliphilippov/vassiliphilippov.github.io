genus Phylloscopus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs fɪˌlɒsˈkoʊ.pəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs fɪˌlɒsˈkəʊ.pəs / (BrE)
A genus of small insectivorous songbirds known as warblers, found mainly in Eurasia and Africa.
a genus of warblers (songbirds)

Examples

Several species within the genus Phylloscopus migrate thousands of miles each year.
The willow warbler is a well-known example from the genus Phylloscopus.
Ornithologists study the genus Phylloscopus to understand the evolution of migratory behavior.

Etymology

From Greek 'phyllon' (leaf) and 'skopos' (seeker or watcher), referring to the birds' habit of searching for insects among leaves.
