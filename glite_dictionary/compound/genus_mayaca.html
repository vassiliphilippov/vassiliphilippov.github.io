genus Mayaca

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs maɪˈækə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs maɪˈækə / (BrE)
A small genus of delicate, moss-like plants found in bogs, with white or violet flowers, native to the Americas.
bog plant genus with moss-like appearance

Examples

The genus Mayaca is often found in wetlands and boggy areas across South America.
Botanists are studying the ecological role of the genus Mayaca in freshwater environments.
Members of the genus Mayaca have slender stems and bear delicate white or violet flowers.

Etymology

From the New Latin genus name 'Mayaca,' origin obscure; used in scientific classification.
