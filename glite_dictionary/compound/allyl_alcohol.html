allyl alcohol

1. NOUN

/ ˈælɪl ˈælˌkɑhɑl / (AmE), / ˈælɪl ˈælˌkɒhɒl / (BrE)
an unsaturated primary alcohol (C3H6O) used in the manufacture of resins, plasticizers, and pharmaceuticals
a chemical alcohol used in industry
medical

Examples

Allyl alcohol is a key intermediate in the production of several types of synthetic resins.
Laboratory workers must handle allyl alcohol with care due to its toxic properties.
The synthesis of certain pharmaceuticals relies on allyl alcohol as a starting material.

Etymology

From 'allyl', relating to the alkene functional group (from Latin 'Allium', garlic), and 'alcohol', from Arabic 'al-kuḥl'.
