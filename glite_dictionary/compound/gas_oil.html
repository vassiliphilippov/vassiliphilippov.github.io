gas oil

1. NOUN

/ ɡæs ˈɔɪl / (AmE), / ɡas ˈɔɪl / (BrE)
a heavy oil obtained from the distillation of petroleum, with a boiling range and viscosity between kerosene and lubricating oil, used as a fuel or feedstock
a heavy oil from petroleum distillation

Examples

Modern refineries produce gas oil as an important fraction during crude oil distillation.
Most industrial boilers are powered by gas oil due to its efficiency and availability.
The quality of gas oil can vary depending on the source of the crude oil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diesel oil - a heavy oil used as diesel engine fuel; similar to gas oil
  • Degree Lower: kerosene - a lighter oil fraction from petroleum distillation
