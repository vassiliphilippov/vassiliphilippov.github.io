civet cat

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪvət kæt / (AmE), / ˈsɪvɪt kæt / (BrE)
A small, cat-like mammal found in Asia and Africa, known for secreting a musky substance used in perfumes.
cat-like mammal producing musk

Examples

The civet cat is prized for the musky scent it produces, often used in high-end perfumes.
Researchers studied the habits of the civet cat in its natural forest environment.
Farmers sometimes complain that the civet cat raids their fruit crops at night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: civet - the musky substance secreted by civet cats

Etymology

From French *civette* (civet), from Arabic *zabād* (musky secretion).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɪvət ˌkæt / (AmE), / ˈsɪvɪt ˌkæt / (BrE)
A raccoon-like mammal native to Mexico and the southwestern United States, known for its long bushy tail with black and white rings; also called ringtail or bassarisk.
North American mammal with a ringed tail

Examples

The civet cat is often seen prowling around the forests at dusk.
Farmers sometimes spot a civet cat looking for food near their barns.
Children were excited to see the bushy tail of a civet cat in the zoo.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ringtail - another name for the civet cat, a North American mammal with a ringed tail
  • Synonym: raccoon - a North American mammal related to the civet cat, known for its masked face and ringed tail
