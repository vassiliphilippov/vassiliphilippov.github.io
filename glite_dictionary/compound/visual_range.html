visual range

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪʒuəl reɪndʒ / (AmE), / ˈvɪʒuəl reɪndʒ / (BrE)
the distance at which a given standard object can be seen with the unaided eye, especially as measured or specified in meteorology
distance one can see with the unaided eye

Examples

On foggy days, the visual range on the road can drop to less than one hundred meters.
Pilots rely on accurate measurements of visual range for safe takeoffs and landings.
Increasing pollution levels have significantly reduced the visual range in the city.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: visibility - the state of being able to see or be seen; a related meteorological measurement
