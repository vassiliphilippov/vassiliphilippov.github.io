executive council

1. NOUN

/ ɪɡˈzɛkjətɪv ˈkaʊnsəl / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzɛkjʊtɪv ˈkaʊnsəl / (BrE)
a council that exercises or shares supreme executive authority, often acting as an administrative or governing body
a council with executive governing power

Examples

The executive council met to discuss the new government policy.
Members of the executive council are appointed by the president.
Several countries have an executive council that aids in decision-making.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cabinet - a body of advisers to a head of state, often with executive powers
