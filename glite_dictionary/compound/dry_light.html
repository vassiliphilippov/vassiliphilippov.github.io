dry light

1. NOUN

/ draɪ laɪt / (AmE), / draɪ laɪt / (BrE)
light that is stark or harsh, or that does not cause glare or reflection
light that is stark, harsh, or non-reflective

Examples

The morning sun filtered in as a dry light, highlighting every detail of the old furniture.
Artists often favor dry light for its ability to reveal true colors without glare.
In high-altitude regions, the dry light produces sharp shadows and intense contrasts.

Synset Examples

The room's dry light gave everything a muted tone.
He prefers to work under a dry light to avoid glare.
