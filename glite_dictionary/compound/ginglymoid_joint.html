ginglymoid joint

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɪŋ.ɡlɪˌmɔɪd dʒɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˈdʒɪŋ.ɡlɪˌmɔɪd dʒɔɪnt / (BrE)
a freely movable joint in which the bones are connected so as to allow extensive movement in one plane, like a hinge
type of hinge joint in anatomy
medical

Examples

The elbow is a classic example of a ginglymoid joint in the human body.
Damage to a ginglymoid joint can severely limit movement in one direction.
Surgeons must be careful when operating on a ginglymoid joint to preserve its range of motion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hinge joint - a joint that allows movement primarily in one plane, like door hinges

Etymology

From Greek ginglymos meaning 'hinge' + -oid (resembling).
