between decks

1. ADV

/ bɪˈtwin dɛks / (AmE), / bɪˈtwiːn dɛks / (BrE)
in the space between the decks of a ship, usually referring to the area below the main deck and above the lowest deck.
in the space between a ship's decks

Examples

The sailors stored supplies between decks to keep them dry during the voyage.
Passengers were often crowded between decks on long ocean crossings in the 19th century.
Strong winds rocked the ship as crew members worked between decks to secure the cargo.
