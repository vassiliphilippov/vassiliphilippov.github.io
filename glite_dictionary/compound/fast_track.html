fast track

1. NOUN

/ fæst ˈtræk / (AmE), / fɑːst ˈtræk / (BrE)
a quick and direct route to achieving success or advancement, especially in a career, organization, or process
quick route to success or advancement

Examples

After joining the management program, she was placed on the fast track to senior leadership.
The new legislation is designed to put infrastructure projects on a fast track.
He always looked for the fast track in every aspect of his career.

Synset Examples

they saw independence as the fast track to democracy
he took a fast track to the top of the corporate ladder
the company went off the fast track when the stock market dropped

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shortcut - a route or method shorter or quicker than the usual one
  • Antonym: slow lane - a path or method involving slower progress or advancement
