monoclonal antibody

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɑːnəˈkloʊnəl ˈæntɪˌbɑːdi / (AmE), / ˌmɒnəˈkləʊnəl ˈæntɪˌbɒdi / (BrE)
an antibody produced by a single clone of cells, resulting in identical molecules, commonly used in medical and scientific research
lab-produced identical antibody molecule
medical

Examples

Researchers developed a monoclonal antibody to target the specific cancer cells.
The patient was treated with a monoclonal antibody therapy to suppress the immune response.
A single monoclonal antibody can be used to detect one particular antigen in diagnostic tests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antibody - a protein produced by the immune system to neutralize pathogens

Etymology

From 'mono-' (one), 'clone' (from Greek klōn, twig), and 'antibody'.
