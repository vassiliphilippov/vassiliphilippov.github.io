weeping love grass

1. NOUN

/ ˈwiːpɪŋ ˈlʌv ˌɡræs / (AmE), / ˈwiːpɪŋ ˈlʌv ˌɡrɑːs / (BrE)
A perennial grass native to South Africa with densely clumped, slender stems, often grown in the United States for erosion control.
a South African perennial grass used for erosion control

Examples

The field was planted with weeping love grass to help stabilize the soil.
Farmers in some regions rely on weeping love grass to prevent erosion on steep hillsides.
Weeping love grass is favored for land reclamation due to its rapid growth and dense roots.
