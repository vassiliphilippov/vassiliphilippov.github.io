umbilical vesicle

1. NOUN

/ ʌmˈbɪlɪkəl ˈvɛsɪkəl / (AmE), / ʌmˈbɪlɪkəl ˈvɛsɪkəl / (BrE)
a membranous structure in mammalian embryos that acts as the circulatory system before the heart becomes functional
embryonic circulatory structure
medical

Examples

In mammals, the umbilical vesicle plays a vital role in early embryonic development.
The contents of the umbilical vesicle are gradually absorbed as the embryo matures.
Damage to the umbilical vesicle can result in developmental abnormalities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yolk sac - membranous sac attached to embryos of vertebrates providing early nourishment

Etymology

From Latin 'umbilicus' (navel) and 'vesicula' (small bladder or sac).
