European creeper

1. NOUN

/ jʊˈroʊpiən ˈkripər / (AmE), / jʊəˈrəʊpiən ˈkriːpə / (BrE)
a small brown-and-buff tree-dwelling bird native to Europe, noted for its down-curved bill, that climbs tree trunks in search of insects
European bird with down-curved bill

Examples

The European creeper is commonly seen spiraling up oak trees searching for insects.
A European creeper clung to the bark, blending in with its brown-and-buff plumage.
Birdwatchers gathered to catch a glimpse of the elusive European creeper in the forest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: treecreeper - small songbird that creeps up tree trunks
