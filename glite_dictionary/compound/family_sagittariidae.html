family Sagittariidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli səˌdʒɪtəˈraɪɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli səˌdʒɪtəˈraɪɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of large, terrestrial birds of prey known as secretary birds, native to sub-Saharan Africa.
family of birds: secretary birds

Examples

The family Sagittariidae consists of just a single extant species, the secretary bird.
Birdwatchers in Africa are often excited to see members of the family Sagittariidae stalking through grasslands.
Unlike most raptors, the family Sagittariidae is adapted for walking long distances in search of prey.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: secretary bird - large bird of prey native to Africa, Sagittariidae family

Etymology

From Latin 'Sagittarius' (archer), referring to the bird's quill-like crests which resemble arrows.
