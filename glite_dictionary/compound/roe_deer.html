roe deer

1. NOUN

/ roʊ dɪr / (AmE), / rəʊ dɪə / (BrE)
A small, graceful deer of Eurasian woodlands, characterized by small forked antlers.
small Eurasian woodland deer

Examples

During our hike in the forest, we spotted a roe deer quietly grazing among the trees.
The roe deer is known for its agility and graceful movements in woodland habitats.
Hunters in Europe have long admired the antlers of the roe deer as prized trophies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deer - a hoofed grazing animal of the family Cervidae
