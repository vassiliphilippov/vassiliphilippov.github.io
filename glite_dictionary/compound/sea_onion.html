sea onion

1. NOUN

/ si ˈʌn.jən / (AmE), / si ˈʌn.jən / (BrE)
A European plant of the scilla genus, with small blue or purple flowers, sometimes cultivated as an ornamental.
European scilla plant with blue/purple flowers

Examples

The sea onion blooms in early spring, producing clusters of delicate blue flowers.
Gardeners often plant sea onions for their colorful appearance and easy maintenance.
I spotted a wild sea onion growing near the rocky coastline.

2. NOUN

/ si ˈʌn.jən / (AmE), / si ˈʌn.jən / (BrE)
A Mediterranean plant with dense spikes of small white flowers, known for its bulb used in traditional medicine.
Mediterranean plant with a medicinal bulb

Examples

The bulb of the sea onion has been used in herbal remedies for centuries.
Gardeners admired the tall white flower spikes of the sea onion blooming in the summer.
Scientists are studying the sea onion for its potential medicinal properties.
