native Australian

1. NOUN

/ ˈneɪtɪv ɔˈstreɪliən / (AmE), / ˈneɪtɪv ɔːˈstreɪliən / (BrE)
a member of the indigenous peoples who lived in Australia before the arrival of European settlers
member of Australia's indigenous peoples

Examples

Many native Australians maintain strong ties to their traditional lands and communities.
The arrival of Europeans had a dramatic impact on native Australians.
Efforts are being made to preserve the languages spoken by native Australians.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Aboriginal Australian - an indigenous person of Australia
