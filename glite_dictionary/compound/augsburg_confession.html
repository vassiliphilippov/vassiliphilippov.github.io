Augsburg Confession

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔɡzˌbɝɡ kənˈfɛʃən / (AmE), / ˈɔːɡzbɜːɡ kənˈfɛʃən / (BrE)
an official statement of Lutheran principles, drawn up in 1530, defending the catholicity of Lutheran doctrine and justifying reforms; still recognized in Lutheran churches today
foundational Lutheran doctrinal document (1530)

Examples

The Augsburg Confession remains a central text for Lutheran churches around the world.
Martin Luther's followers presented the Augsburg Confession to Emperor Charles V in 1530.
Many scholars study the Augsburg Confession to understand the foundations of Protestant theology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Lutheranism - the branch of Christianity associated with the teachings of Martin Luther

Etymology

Named after Augsburg, Germany, where the document was presented in 1530; 'confession' from Latin 'confessio' (acknowledgment, declaration).
