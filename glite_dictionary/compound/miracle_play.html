miracle play

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪrəkəl pleɪ / (AmE), / ˈmɪrəkəl pleɪ / (BrE)
a type of medieval drama depicting events from the life of a saint or martyr
medieval play about saints' lives

Examples

The medieval miracle play often attracted large crowds to the town square.
She studied the structure of a miracle play for her theatre history class.
Many miracle plays depicted the legendary deeds of Christian saints.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mystery play - a medieval play based on biblical stories
  • Synonym: morality play - a medieval and early Tudor theatrical entertainment intended to teach a moral lesson

Etymology

From Middle English 'miracle' (a marvel) and 'play'; refers to the depiction of religious miracles on stage.
