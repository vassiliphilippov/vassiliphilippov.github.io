prothrombin accelerator

1. NOUN

/ proʊˈθrɑm.bɪn əkˈsɛl.əˌreɪ.tər / (AmE), / prəʊˈθrɒm.bɪn əkˈsɛl.əˌreɪ.tə / (BrE)
a substance, particularly a coagulation factor, that increases the conversion of prothrombin to thrombin in the blood-clotting process
blood-clotting accelerator substance
medical

Examples

A deficiency in the prothrombin accelerator can lead to excessive bleeding.
Scientists are studying the role of the prothrombin accelerator in rare clotting disorders.
This medication works by increasing the activity of the prothrombin accelerator in the bloodstream.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prothrombin - a plasma protein important in blood clotting

Etymology

From 'prothrombin' (a blood protein) + 'accelerator' (one that speeds up a process).
