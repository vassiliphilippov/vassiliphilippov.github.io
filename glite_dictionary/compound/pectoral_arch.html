pectoral arch

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛktɚrəl ɑrtʃ / (AmE), / ˈpɛktərəl ɑːtʃ / (BrE)
the bony structure in the shoulder region formed by the collarbones (clavicles) and shoulder blades (scapulae)
shoulder girdle bones
medical

Examples

The pectoral arch supports the upper limbs and provides attachment for several muscles.
In some injuries, the pectoral arch may be fractured, affecting arm movement.
Evolutionary biologists study the pectoral arch to understand adaptations in different vertebrates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shoulder girdle - the set of bones connecting the upper limb to the trunk

Etymology

From Latin 'pectus' meaning 'chest' and 'arcus' meaning 'arch'.
