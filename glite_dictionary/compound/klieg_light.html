klieg light

1. NOUN

/ kliɡ laɪt / (AmE), / kliːɡ laɪt / (BrE)
a powerful carbon arc lamp used in film and television production to provide intense, focused lighting
intense arc lamp for film production

Examples

The director asked the crew to adjust the klieg light for a better shot of the actors.
Old Hollywood sets were often illuminated with huge klieg lights that generated intense heat.
As she stepped onto the stage, a bright klieg light followed her every move.

Etymology

Named after John and Anton Kliegl, the inventors, and their company, Kliegl Brothers Universal Electric Stage Lighting Company.
