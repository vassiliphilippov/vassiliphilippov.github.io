neck sweetbread

1. NOUN

/ nɛk ˈswiːtbɹɛd / (AmE), / nɛk ˈswiːtbɹɛd / (BrE)
the edible thymus gland of an animal, especially used as food
edible thymus gland (food)

Examples

The chef prepared the neck sweetbread with a delicate white wine sauce.
Some French dishes feature neck sweetbread as a prized delicacy.
Before cooking, the neck sweetbread should be thoroughly soaked and cleaned.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sweetbread - edible pancreas or thymus gland of an animal

Etymology

From 'neck' referring to the anatomical region and 'sweetbread,' an old term for edible glands.
