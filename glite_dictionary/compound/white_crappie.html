white crappie

1. NOUN

/ waɪt ˈkræpi / (AmE), / waɪt ˈkræpi / (BrE)
A North American freshwater fish (Pomoxis annularis) of the sunfish family, noted for its pale coloration and found in lakes and rivers.
a pale North American freshwater fish

Examples

Many anglers prefer to catch white crappie during the spring spawning season.
The lake is stocked with both white crappie and largemouth bass for recreational fishing.
Researchers studied the habitat preferences of white crappie in different river systems.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: black crappie - a similar but darker-colored freshwater fish (Pomoxis nigromaculatus)

Etymology

From 'white' (pale colour) and 'crappie', from Canadian French 'crapet', name for sunfish.
