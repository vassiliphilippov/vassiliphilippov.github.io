house party

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaʊs ˌpɑrti / (AmE), / ˈhaʊs ˌpɑːti / (BrE)
a party that lasts for one or more nights, typically held at a large house where guests often stay overnight
overnight party at a large house

Examples

We were invited to a lavish house party in the countryside last summer.
During the house party, guests slept in every room of the old mansion.
The annual house party is famous for its late-night games and festivities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: party - a social gathering of invited guests, typically for celebration or recreation

2. NOUN

/ haʊs ˈpɑːrti / (AmE), / haʊs ˈpɑːti / (BrE)
A social gathering or party held at someone's home, rather than in a public venue.
party held at a private home

Examples

We're planning a house party for Sarah's birthday next Friday night.
After the concert, everyone was invited to a massive house party nearby.
He cleaned up his apartment before throwing a house party for his friends.

Synset Examples
