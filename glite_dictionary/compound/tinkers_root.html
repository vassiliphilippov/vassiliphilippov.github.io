tinker's root

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɪŋkərz ˌruːt / (AmE), / ˈtɪŋkəz ˌruːt / (BrE)
A coarse, weedy perennial herb native to America, with large, usually perfoliate leaves and purple or dull red flowers.
American perennial herb with large leaves

Examples

The tinker's root thrives in damp meadows and open woodlands across the eastern United States.
Gardeners often find tinker's root difficult to remove due to its hardy, persistent nature.
Clusters of purple flowers made the tinker's root stand out among the other wild plants.
