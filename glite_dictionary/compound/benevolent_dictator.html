benevolent dictator

1. NOUN

/ bəˈnɛvələnt dɪkˈteɪtər / (AmE), / bɪˈnɛvələnt dɪkˈteɪtə / (BrE)
a ruler who holds absolute power but uses that power for the good of the people rather than for personal gain or oppression
absolute ruler who governs for the public good

Examples

Some people argue that a benevolent dictator can bring stability to a troubled country.
The company's founder acted as a benevolent dictator, making decisions he believed were best for everyone.
Although he was an autocrat, his citizens considered him a benevolent dictator because of his social reforms.

Synset Examples

He ruled the country like a benevolent dictator for life.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: dictator - a ruler with absolute power, often ruling oppressively
