explosive charge

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˈsploʊsɪv tʃɑrdʒ / (AmE), / ɪkˈspləʊsɪv tʃɑːdʒ / (BrE)
an amount of explosive material prepared to be detonated at one time
set amount of explosive to be detonated

Examples

The engineer set the explosive charge to demolish the old building.
Miners carefully placed each explosive charge to maximize rock removal.
He misjudged the amount of the explosive charge, causing unexpected damage.
