sea rover

1. NOUN

/ si ˈroʊvər / (AmE), / siː ˈrəʊvə / (BrE)
a person who engages in piracy by robbing at sea or plundering coastal lands without official authorization
pirate; maritime plunderer

Examples

The infamous sea rover was known for attacking merchant vessels along the coast.
Legends tell of a daring sea rover who buried his treasure on a deserted island.
Unlike a privateer, a sea rover operated without any government authority.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pirate - a person who attacks and robs ships at sea
  • Antonym: privateer - an armed ship or person authorized by a government to attack foreign ships

2. NOUN

/ si ˈroʊvər / (AmE), / si ˈrəʊvə / (BrE)
a boat or vessel designed for exploration, especially of uncharted or remote waters
exploration vessel or boat

Examples

The crew boarded the sea rover with enough supplies for a month-long expedition.
Their legendary sea rover was renowned for surviving fierce ocean storms.
Historians consider the sea rover a key vessel in the age of maritime discovery.

Synset Examples

Adventurers set sail on the sea rover to explore uncharted islands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: explorer - a person or vessel that explores unfamiliar areas
