Royal Academy of Arts

1. PROPN

/ ˈrɔɪəl əˈkædəmi əv ɑrts / (AmE), / ˈrɔɪəl əˈkædəmi əv ɑːts / (BrE)
an honorary institution in London, founded in 1768, dedicated to promoting the practice and appreciation of the visual arts in Britain, especially painting, sculpture, and architecture
British arts institution in London

Examples

She visited the Royal Academy of Arts to see the Summer Exhibition.
Many renowned British artists have been elected to the Royal Academy of Arts.
The Royal Academy of Arts plays a significant role in the development of contemporary art in the UK.
