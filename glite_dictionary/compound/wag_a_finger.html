to wag a finger

1. VERB

/ wæɡ ə ˈfɪŋɡɚ / (AmE), / wæɡ ə ˈfɪŋɡə / (BrE)
to move one's index finger side to side, typically to express disapproval or scold someone
gesture expressing disapproval or scolding

Examples

The principal wagged a finger at the students who were talking during the assembly.
Before answering, she wagged a finger at him to warn him about interrupting.
My grandmother used to wag a finger whenever I forgot my manners.

Synset Examples

He loves to wag a finger when giving advice.
The teacher wagged a finger at the late students.
She wagged a finger at him for forgetting her birthday.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scold - to reprimand or criticize angrily
  • Synonym: rebuke - to express sharp disapproval or criticism
