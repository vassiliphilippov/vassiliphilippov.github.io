blue channel catfish

1. NOUN

/ blu ˈtʃænəl ˈkætˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / bluː ˈtʃæn.əl ˈkat.fɪʃ / (BrE)
a large catfish native to the Mississippi Valley, known for its bluish coloration and widely found in rivers of central North America
a species of large North American catfish

Examples

Anglers often target the blue channel catfish for its size and fighting ability.
The blue channel catfish thrives in the slow-moving waters of the Mississippi River.
You can distinguish a blue channel catfish by its bluish-gray body and forked tail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catfish - a type of fish with whisker-like barbels around the mouth
