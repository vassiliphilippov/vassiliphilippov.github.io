common shiner

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmən ˈʃaɪnər / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən ˈʃaɪnə / (BrE)
a small, silvery freshwater fish (Luxilus cornutus) native to North America, common in streams and rivers
a North American freshwater fish

Examples

The common shiner is often found in clear, fast-moving streams across eastern North America.
Anglers sometimes use the common shiner as bait when fishing for larger game fish.
The biology class studied the habitat requirements of the common shiner during their field trip.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shiner - a small silvery fish, especially of the genus Notropis or Luxilus
