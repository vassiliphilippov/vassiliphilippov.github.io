polar opposition

1. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊlər ˌɑːpəˈzɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈpəʊlə ˌɒpəˈzɪʃən / (BrE)
A contrast or opposition between two extremes or poles, often with gradations between them.
contrast between two extremes or poles

Examples

The concept of polar opposition helps us understand the spectrum between hot and cold.
Light and darkness often serve as metaphors for polar opposition in literature.
Many moral dilemmas are presented as a polar opposition between good and evil.
