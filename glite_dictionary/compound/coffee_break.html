coffee break

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑfi ˌbreɪk / (AmE), / ˈkɒfi ˌbreɪk / (BrE)
a short rest period during the workday when people drink coffee or relax briefly
short rest period at work for coffee

Examples

We usually take a coffee break around 10:30 each morning.
During her coffee break, she liked to read the news online.
The meeting paused for a brief coffee break before continuing.

Synset Examples

a ten-minute coffee break
the British have tea breaks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tea break - a short break from work for tea, common in British culture
  • Degree Higher: break - an interruption in work or activity for rest or refreshment

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɔfi breɪk / (AmE), / ˈkɒfi breɪk / (BrE)
a short period during the working day when people stop working to have coffee or other refreshments
short break for coffee at work

Examples

Every afternoon, the staff gather in the kitchen for a coffee break.
She used her coffee break to catch up on emails.
Do you want to join us for a quick coffee break before the meeting?

Synset Examples

He’ll step out for a quick coffee break.
'bout now, I'm ready for a coffee break.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tea break - a short pause from work to have tea or refreshments
