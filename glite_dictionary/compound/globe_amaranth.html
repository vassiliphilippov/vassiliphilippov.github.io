globe amaranth

1. NOUN

/ ɡloʊb ˈæm.əˌrænθ / (AmE), / ɡləʊb ˈæm.ərænθ / (BrE)
a tropical American plant with round, brightly colored flower heads that retain their color when dried.
tropical plant with round, lasting flower heads

Examples

The globe amaranth adds vibrant color to dried floral arrangements.
Gardeners admire globe amaranth for its resilience and bright, clover-like flowers.
She planted globe amaranth to attract butterflies to her summer garden.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amaranth - any plant of the genus Amaranthus, often with vivid flower heads

Etymology

Amaranth from Greek 'amarantos' (unfading), referring to the lasting quality of the flowers.
