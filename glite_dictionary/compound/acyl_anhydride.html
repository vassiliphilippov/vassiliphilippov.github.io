acyl anhydride

1. NOUN

/ ˈæsɪl ænˈhaɪdraɪd / (AmE), / ˈeɪsɪl ænˈhaɪdraɪd / (BrE)
a type of organic compound that reacts with water to produce an acid, commonly used in chemical synthesis
organic compound reacting with water to produce acid
medical

Examples

Acyl anhydrides are widely used in organic chemistry to introduce acyl groups into other compounds.
When acyl anhydrides react with water, they break down to form two molecules of carboxylic acid.
Many pharmaceutical syntheses utilize acyl anhydrides as efficient reagents for creating complex molecules.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acid anhydride - a compound that reacts with water to form an acid, includes acyl anhydrides

Etymology

From 'acyl,' referring to a functional group derived from an acid, and 'anhydride,' from Greek 'anhydros' meaning 'without water.'
