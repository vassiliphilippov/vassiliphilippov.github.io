crow's foot

1. NOUN

/ kroʊz fʊt / (AmE), / krəʊz fʊt / (BrE)
A wrinkle at the outer corner of the eye, typically appearing as one ages.
wrinkle at eye's outer corner

Examples

She noticed the first crow's foot appear beside her eyes when she smiled.
Laughter and sunshine gave him charming crow's feet at the corners of his eyes.
A good eye cream may help reduce the appearance of a crow's foot.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wrinkle - a small line or fold in the skin, especially as a sign of aging

Etymology

So called from the resemblance of the wrinkle's pattern to a crow's foot.
