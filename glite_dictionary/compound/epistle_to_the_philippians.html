Epistle to the Philippians

1. PROPN

/ əˈpɪsəl tə ðə fɪˈlɪpiənz / (AmE), / ɪˈpɪsəl tə ðə fɪˈlɪpiənz / (BrE)
a book of the New Testament consisting of a letter from Saint Paul to the Christian community in Philippi, Macedonia
New Testament book; letter from Paul to Philippians

Examples

Many scholars study the Epistle to the Philippians to better understand early Christian theology.
He quoted from the Epistle to the Philippians during his sermon on joy and gratitude.
The Epistle to the Philippians was written by Paul while he was imprisoned.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Philippians - the Christian community in Philippi; the recipients of the epistle
  • Degree Lower: Epistle - a letter, especially a formal or didactic one

Etymology

Epistle from Greek 'epistolē' (letter); Philippians from Philippi, an ancient city in Macedonia.
