brain truster

1. NOUN

/ ˈbreɪn ˌtrʌstər / (AmE), / ˈbreɪn ˌtrʌstə / (BrE)
an expert adviser who is involved in making important decisions, particularly in politics or government, but usually without official status
informal expert adviser; unofficial consultant

Examples

He has served as a brain truster for several political campaigns over the years.
During the economic crisis, the president surrounded himself with an elite group of brain trusters.
Although she lacked any formal title, she was widely regarded as the administration's top brain truster.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adviser - a person who gives advice in a particular field
  • Synonym: consultant - an expert who gives advice professionally

Etymology

From 'brain trust', originally referring to President Franklin D. Roosevelt's group of expert advisers in the 1930s, plus the agent noun suffix '-er'.
