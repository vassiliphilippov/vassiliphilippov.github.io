genus Salmo

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈsæl.moʊ / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈsæl.məʊ / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Salmonidae, including salmon and trout species.
genus of salmon and trout

Examples

Researchers identified a new species belonging to the genus Salmo in the mountain streams.
All members of the genus Salmo are renowned for their ability to swim upstream to spawn.
The Atlantic salmon, a famous member of the genus Salmo, is highly valued by anglers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Salmonidae - the family of ray-finned fish including salmon and trout
  • Synonym: trout - a common name for fish in the genus Salmo and others

Etymology

From Latin *Salmo*, the classical name for salmon.
