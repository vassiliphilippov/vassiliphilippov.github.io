nerve agent

1. NOUN

/ nɜrv ˈeɪdʒənt / (AmE), / nɜːv ˈeɪdʒənt / (BrE)
A highly toxic chemical substance that disrupts the nervous system and respiratory functions, typically used as a weapon.
toxic chemical weapon affecting nerves

Examples

Exposure to a nerve agent can cause convulsions and respiratory failure within minutes.
The use of a nerve agent in warfare is banned under international law.
Medical teams were trained to treat victims of nerve agent poisoning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nerve gas - toxic gas affecting the nervous system
