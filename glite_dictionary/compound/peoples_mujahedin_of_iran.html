People's Mujahedin of Iran

1. PROPN

/ ˈpiːpəlz muːˈhædʒədɪn əv aɪˈræn / (AmE), / ˈpiːplz mjuːˈhædʒədɪn ɒv ɪˈrɑːn / (BrE)
a militant Iranian political organization founded in the 1960s that initially opposed the Shah's regime and later the Islamic Republic, combining elements of Marxism and Islam
Iranian militant political organization

Examples

The People's Mujahedin of Iran has been involved in several major political events since its formation in the 1960s.
Membership in the People's Mujahedin of Iran is controversial in Iran and abroad.
The government accused the People's Mujahedin of Iran of orchestrating attacks against state officials.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mujahedin-e Khalq - alternative name for the People's Mujahedin of Iran

Etymology

From Persian 'Mojahedin-e Khalq-e Iran', meaning 'Holy Warriors of the People of Iran'.
