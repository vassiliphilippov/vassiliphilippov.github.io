Party of No

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːrti əv ˈnoʊ / (AmE), / ˈpɑːti əv ˈnəʊ / (BrE)
a political group or organization characterized by consistent opposition to initiatives or proposals, often without offering alternatives
political group consistently opposing proposals

Examples

The opposition was branded the Party of No after repeatedly blocking the new legislation.
Some voters accused the council of acting like a Party of No on key reforms.
Critics say that being a Party of No is not a substitute for constructive policymaking.

Synset Examples

This initiative is stalled by the Party of No in charge.
He felt frustrated joining a Party of No at work.
The committee has become a Party of No regarding new ideas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obstructionist - a person or group who deliberately delays or prevents progress
