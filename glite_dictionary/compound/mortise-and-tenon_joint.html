mortise-and-tenon joint

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɔːr.tɪs ənd ˈtɛn.ən dʒɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˈmɔː.tɪs ənd ˈtɛn.ən dʒɔɪnt / (BrE)
a strong joint in woodworking where a projecting tenon on one piece fits into a corresponding mortise hole in another piece
woodworking joint: tenon fits into mortise

Examples

Traditional furniture often uses the mortise-and-tenon joint for added strength and durability.
The carpenter demonstrated how to cut a perfect mortise-and-tenon joint during the workshop.
Many ancient buildings have stood for centuries thanks to the mortise-and-tenon joint in their wood frames.

Etymology

From 'mortise' (Middle English, from Old French *mortaise*) + 'tenon' (Old French *tenon*, from *tenir* 'to hold').
