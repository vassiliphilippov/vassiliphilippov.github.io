developing country

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈvɛləpɪŋ ˈkʌntri / (AmE), / dɪˈvɛləpɪŋ ˈkʌntri / (BrE)
a nation with a low level of industrialization, income, and standard of living, seeking to advance socially and economically
nation with low industrial and economic development

Examples

Many organizations direct aid to a developing country to support its education and healthcare systems.
Tourism can be a vital source of income for a developing country trying to boost its economy.
Several policies were discussed to address the challenges faced by each developing country in the region.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: developed country - a nation with a high level of industrialization and standard of living
  • Degree Lower: least developed country - a nation with the lowest indicators of socioeconomic development

2. NOUN

/ dɪˈvɛləpɪŋ ˈkʌntri / (AmE), / dɪˈvɛləpɪŋ ˈkʌntri / (BrE)
A country with a relatively low level of industrialization and economic development.
country with low economic development

Examples

Many health organizations focus their efforts in developing countries where resources are scarce.
Education systems in a developing country often face significant challenges.
Tourism has become an important source of income for several developing countries.

Synset Examples

Investors are looking to expand into developing countries.

Related Terms
