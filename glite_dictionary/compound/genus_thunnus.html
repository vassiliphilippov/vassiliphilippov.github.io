genus Thunnus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈθuː.nəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈθʌn.əs / (BrE)
A genus of large, warm-blooded marine fishes commonly known as tunas.
a genus of tuna fishes

Examples

The genus Thunnus includes some of the most commercially valuable tuna species.
Bluefin tuna, a member of the genus Thunnus, is highly prized in sushi cuisine.
Researchers are studying the migratory patterns of fish in the genus Thunnus to aid conservation efforts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tuna - large, fast-swimming marine fish of the genus Thunnus

Etymology

From Latin 'Thunnus', the genus name for tuna, derived from Greek 'thynnos' (tuna).
