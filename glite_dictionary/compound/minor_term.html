minor term

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪnər tɜrm / (AmE), / ˈmaɪnə tɜːm / (BrE)
In logic, the term in a syllogism that is the subject of the conclusion.
logical subject in a syllogism's conclusion

Examples

In the syllogism, the minor term is the subject of the conclusion statement.
Logic students learned to identify the minor term in each of the exercises.
The minor term must always appear in the minor premise of a syllogism.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: major term - term in a syllogism that is the predicate of the conclusion

Etymology

From Latin 'minor' (smaller, lesser) and English 'term' (expression, word).
