family Xantusiidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli zænˌtuːsiˈɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæməli zænˌtjuːsiˈɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of small, secretive lizards known as night lizards, native to the Americas.
family of night lizards (zoological)

Examples

The family Xantusiidae encompasses several genera of lizards commonly referred to as night lizards.
Researchers studied the habitat preferences of the family Xantusiidae in the deserts of the southwestern United States.
Members of the family Xantusiidae are known for their secretive nocturnal behavior and distinctive scale patterns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: night lizard - a small, secretive lizard of the family Xantusiidae

Etymology

From New Latin *Xantusiidae*, named after the type genus *Xantusia*, itself named for John Xantus, a Hungarian zoologist.
