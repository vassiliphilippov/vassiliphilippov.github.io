genus Calamintha

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˌkæləˈmɪnθə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˌkaləˈmɪnθə / (BrE)
A genus of aromatic herbaceous plants in the mint family, commonly known as calamints, native to temperate regions.
a genus of plants (calamints) in the mint family

Examples

Researchers are studying the medicinal properties of genus Calamintha for use in herbal remedies.
Several species within the genus Calamintha are grown for their aromatic leaves and flowers.
The genus Calamintha comprises wildflowers commonly found in Europe and North America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calamint - any plant of the genus Calamintha
  • Degree Higher: mint - plants of the family Lamiaceae, including genus Calamintha

Etymology

From Latin 'calamintha', from Greek 'kalamínthē', a plant name.
