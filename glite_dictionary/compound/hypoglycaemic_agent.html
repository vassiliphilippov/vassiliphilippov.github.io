hypoglycaemic agent

1. NOUN

/ haɪ.poʊ.ɡlaɪˈsiː.mɪk ˈeɪ.dʒənt / (AmE), / haɪ.pəʊ.ɡlaɪˈsiː.mɪk ˈeɪ.dʒənt / (BrE)
a substance or drug used to lower the level of glucose in the blood, especially in the treatment of diabetes mellitus
drug that lowers blood glucose
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed a new hypoglycaemic agent to help control her blood sugar levels.
Some patients may require more than one hypoglycaemic agent to manage their diabetes effectively.
Research continues into developing safer hypoglycaemic agent options with fewer side effects.

Etymology

From 'hypoglycaemic', meaning low blood sugar, combined with 'agent'. 'Hypoglycaemic' is from Greek 'hypo-' (under) and 'glyka' (sweet).
