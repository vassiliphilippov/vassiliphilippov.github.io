Venus'-hair fern

1. NOUN

/ ˈviːnəs heɚ fɝn / (AmE), / ˈviːnəs heə fɜːn / (BrE)
A delicate maidenhair fern (Adiantum capillus-veneris) with slender, shiny black leaf stalks, found in many parts of the world.
delicate maidenhair fern species

Examples

The Venus'-hair fern thrives in moist, shaded garden corners.
Botanists admire the Venus'-hair fern for its graceful black stalks and delicate leaves.
She placed a potted Venus'-hair fern on the windowsill to brighten the room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maidenhair fern - a type of delicate fern with fan-shaped leaf segments and dark, slender stalks

Etymology

Named after Venus, the Roman goddess, reflecting the plant's delicate and attractive appearance.
