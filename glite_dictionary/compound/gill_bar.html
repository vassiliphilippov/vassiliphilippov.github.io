gill bar

1. NOUN

/ ɡɪl bɑr / (AmE), / ɡɪl bɑː / (BrE)
one of the bony or cartilaginous arches on each side of the pharynx that support the gills in fishes and aquatic amphibians
bony/cartilaginous arch supporting fish/amphibian gills

Examples

The gill bar plays a crucial role in supporting the respiratory structures of many fish species.
Scientists observed damage to the gill bar after prolonged exposure to polluted water.
Each gill bar is lined with filaments that facilitate gas exchange.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gill arch - an alternative term for a gill bar; supporting structure for fish gills
