goose grass

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡus ˌɡræs / (AmE), / ˈɡuːs ˌɡrɑːs / (BrE)
a coarse annual grass with fingerlike spikes of flowers, native to the Old World tropics and naturalized as a weed elsewhere
a type of coarse annual grass found worldwide

Examples

The fields behind the house were overrun with goose grass during the summer months.
Gardeners often struggle to control invasive goose grass in their lawns and flowerbeds.
You can identify goose grass by its distinctive fingerlike flower spikes and rough leaves.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡus ˌɡræs / (AmE), / ˈɡuːs ˌɡrɑːs / (BrE)
an annual weedy grass, often used for hay, belonging to the genus Galium or Eleusine, depending on regional usage.
annual weedy grass used for hay

Examples

Goose grass often invades pastures and is sometimes harvested for animal feed.
The farmer noticed that the goose grass was spreading rapidly across his field.
Cows and sheep can eat goose grass when other forage is scarce.

Etymology

Named for its supposed appeal to geese as food; 'goose' + 'grass'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈɡus ˌɡræs / (AmE), / ˈɡuːs ˌɡrɑːs / (BrE)
an annual plant with stems covered in curved prickles, found in North America, Europe, and Asia; also known as cleavers (Galium aparine)
a prickly annual plant; cleavers

Examples

The children found goose grass sticking to their clothes after walking through the field.
Gardeners often remove goose grass because it can easily take over flower beds.
Goose grass is also used in some herbal remedies for its mild medicinal properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cleavers - another common name for Galium aparine, a plant with clinging stems

4. NOUN

/ ˈɡus ˌɡræs / (AmE), / ˈɡuːs ˌɡrɑːs / (BrE)
A low-growing perennial plant with leaves that are silvery on the underside, found in northern United States, Europe, and Asia.
low-growing perennial plant

Examples

The field was covered with goose grass after a cool, wet spring.
Gardeners often struggle to control goose grass in their lawns and flower beds.
The silvery leaves of goose grass help distinguish it from similar weeds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cleavers - a related plant, also called 'goosegrass', Galium aparine
