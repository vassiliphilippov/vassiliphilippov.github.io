genus Octopus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ɑkˈtoʊ.pəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ɒkˈtəʊ.pəs / (BrE)
A genus of cephalopods that includes common octopuses, the type genus of the family Octopodidae.
type genus of the octopus family
medical

Examples

The genus Octopus includes many species found in oceans throughout the world.
Researchers discovered a new member of the genus Octopus off the coast of Australia.
Members of the genus Octopus are known for their intelligence and ability to camouflage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: octopus - a soft-bodied sea animal with eight limbs

Etymology

From Latin 'octopus', from Greek 'oktopous' (having eight feet).
