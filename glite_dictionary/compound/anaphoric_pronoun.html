anaphoric pronoun

1. NOUN

/ ˌænəˈfɔrɪk ˈproʊnaʊn / (AmE), / ˌænəˈfɒrɪk ˈprəʊnaʊn / (BrE)
A pronoun that refers back to another word or phrase mentioned earlier in the text or conversation.
pronoun referring to earlier word or phrase

Examples

In the sentence 'Sarah lost her keys,' the word her is an anaphoric pronoun referring to Sarah.
When reading, it is important to identify each anaphoric pronoun to understand the text clearly.
English learners often struggle with using anaphoric pronouns correctly in complex sentences.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: pronoun - word that takes the place of a noun
  • Antonym: cataphoric pronoun - a pronoun that refers forward to another word or phrase

Etymology

From Greek anaphora (a carrying back) and English pronoun.
