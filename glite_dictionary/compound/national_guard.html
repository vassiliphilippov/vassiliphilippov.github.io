National Guard

1. NOUN

/ ˈnæʃənəl ɡɑrd / (AmE), / ˈnæʃənəl ɡɑːd / (BrE)
A reserve military force organized by the individual U.S. states, equipped by the federal government, and subject to call by either state or federal authorities.
U.S. state-based reserve military force
american

Examples

The governor called in the National Guard to assist with disaster relief after the hurricane.
Members of the National Guard are often deployed during civil emergencies and large-scale events.
He joined the National Guard to serve his community while continuing his civilian career.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: militia - a military force raised from the civil population to supplement a regular army
