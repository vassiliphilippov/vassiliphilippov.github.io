white lead

1. NOUN

/ waɪt lɛd / (AmE), / waɪt lɛd / (BrE)
a poisonous white pigment made from lead carbonate, formerly used in paints
poisonous white pigment made from lead

Examples

Early artists often used white lead in their oil paintings to achieve brilliant whites.
Exposure to white lead is highly toxic and can cause severe health problems.
Modern paints no longer contain white lead due to its poisonous effects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lead carbonate - a chemical compound, the main component of white lead
  • Antonym: red lead - another lead-based pigment, different in color and composition
