moor-bird

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʊr bɜrd / (AmE), / ˈmɔː bɜːd / (BrE)
a reddish-brown grouse native to the upland moors of Great Britain, especially referring to the red grouse
reddish-brown moorland grouse (Britain)
british

Examples

During the hunting season, the moor-bird is highly prized by gamekeepers.
The call of the moor-bird echoed across the misty moors at dawn.
He spotted a flock of moor-birds feeding among the heather.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: red grouse - a reddish-brown grouse native to Britain and Ireland
