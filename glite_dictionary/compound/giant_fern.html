giant fern

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒaɪənt fɜrn / (AmE), / ˈdʒaɪənt fɜːn / (BrE)
a very large species of primitive fern native to tropical Pacific regions with high rainfall
very large fern of tropical Pacific

Examples

In the rainforest, the giant fern stretched its fronds high above the path.
Some giant ferns can reach several meters in height, dominating the undergrowth.
The leaves of the giant fern provided shelter for many small animals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tree fern - any of various ferns with a trunk elevating the fronds above ground level
