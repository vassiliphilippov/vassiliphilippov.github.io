qualitative analysis

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɑːlət̬eɪtɪv əˈnæləsɪs / (AmE), / ˈkwɒlɪtətɪv əˈnæləsɪs / (BrE)
the examination of a substance to determine its constituent elements without measuring their amounts
analytical chemistry method for identifying components

Examples

The laboratory performed a qualitative analysis to identify the metals present in the alloy.
During the qualitative analysis, the sample was tested for the presence of chloride ions.
Chemists often begin with qualitative analysis before proceeding to more precise measurements.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: quantitative analysis - chemical analysis to determine the amounts of components

Etymology

From Latin *qualitativus* (of what kind) + English *analysis*, from Greek *analusis* (a loosening).
