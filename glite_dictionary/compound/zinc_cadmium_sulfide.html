zinc cadmium sulfide

1. NOUN

/ zɪŋk kædˈmiːəm ˈsʌlfaɪd / (AmE), / zɪŋk kadˈmiːəm ˈsʌlfʌɪd / (BrE)
a chemical compound consisting of cadmium sulfide containing zinc, often used as a pigment or for scientific purposes
a chemical compound of cadmium sulfide and zinc

Examples

The laboratory synthesized zinc cadmium sulfide to study its luminescent properties.
Researchers found that zinc cadmium sulfide was suitable for use as a pigment in special paints.
Concerns were raised when zinc cadmium sulfide was released during military dispersion tests in the mid-20th century.

Etymology

From the element names zinc, cadmium, and sulfide, referring to the composition of the compound.
