hepatic artery

1. NOUN

/ hɪˈpætɪk ˈɑrtəri / (AmE), / hɪˈpætɪk ˈɑːtəri / (BrE)
an artery that supplies blood to the liver
artery supplying blood to the liver
medical

Examples

During surgery, the hepatic artery must be carefully identified to prevent damage.
A blockage in the hepatic artery can lead to serious liver complications.
The doctor explained how the hepatic artery delivers oxygenated blood to the liver.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: portal vein - the vein that carries blood from the digestive organs to the liver

Etymology

From Greek 'hēpatikos' (of the liver) and 'arteria' (artery).
