Boeing B-52 Stratofortress

1. PROPN

/ ˈboʊɪŋ ˈbi ˈfɪfti tu ˌstrætəˈfɔrtrəs / (AmE), / ˈbəʊɪŋ ˈbiː ˈfɪfti tuː ˌstrætəˈfɔːtrəs / (BrE)
A long-range, subsonic, jet-powered strategic bomber used by the United States Air Force, first introduced in the 1950s.
U.S. long-range strategic bomber aircraft

Examples

The Boeing B-52 Stratofortress has been in service with the U.S. Air Force since the 1950s.
During the Cold War, the Boeing B-52 Stratofortress played a crucial role in America's strategic deterrence.
Engineers recently upgraded the Boeing B-52 Stratofortress to extend its operational capabilities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strategic bomber - A large military aircraft designed to drop bombs on strategic targets from long range

Etymology

Boeing is the manufacturer; B-52 designates a bomber model; 'Stratofortress' refers to its strategic role.
