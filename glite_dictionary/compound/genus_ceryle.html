genus Ceryle

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs sɪˈraɪli / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs sɪˈriːli / (BrE)
A genus of birds in the kingfisher family Alcedinidae, known for their fishing habits and distinctive appearance.
genus of kingfisher birds

Examples

The genus Ceryle includes several species of kingfishers found near rivers and lakes.
Ornithologists recently discovered a new member of the genus Ceryle in South America.
The genus Ceryle is characterized by birds with strong bills adapted for catching fish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kingfisher - a brightly colored bird of the family Alcedinidae

Etymology

From Latin 'genus' (kind, type) and Neo-Latin 'Ceryle', derived from Greek 'keruleos' (a kind of kingfisher).
