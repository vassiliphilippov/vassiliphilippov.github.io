standing order

1. NOUN

/ ˈstændɪŋ ˈɔrdər / (AmE), / ˈstændɪŋ ˈɔːdə / (BrE)
a rule or instruction that remains in force until specifically changed or rescinded, particularly one governing procedures in meetings or organizations
permanent rule or instruction

Examples

The committee decided to implement a new standing order to ensure transparent voting procedures.
Under the standing order of the assembly, members must submit questions in writing two days prior to the meeting.
Changing a standing order requires a formal vote from the majority of the board.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bylaw - a rule made by an organization to govern its internal affairs
  • Synonym: regulation - an official rule or directive made by an authority
