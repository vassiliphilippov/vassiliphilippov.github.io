false belief

1. NOUN

/ fɔls bəˈlif / (AmE), / fɔːls bɪˈliːf / (BrE)
an incorrect or mistaken idea that results from faulty reasoning or misunderstanding
an incorrect idea or misconception

Examples

Many people hold the false belief that vaccines cause autism.
It is a common false belief that you must wait an hour after eating before swimming.
Her false belief about the company's profits led her to make a poor investment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: misconception - an incorrect view or opinion based on faulty thinking or understanding
  • Antonym: truth - that which is true or in accordance with fact or reality
