flotsam and jetsam

1. NOUN

/ ˈflɑtsəm ənd ˈdʒɛtsəm / (AmE), / ˈflɒtsəm ənd ˈdʒɛtsəm / (BrE)
Unwanted or discarded objects, debris, or miscellaneous items, often found washed up or scattered, used both literally and figuratively.
miscellaneous discarded items or debris

Examples

The beach was covered in flotsam and jetsam after the storm passed through.
Her attic was full of the flotsam and jetsam of a long and interesting life.
After moving house, we sorted through all the flotsam and jetsam we had accumulated over the years.

Synset Examples

He collected flotsam and jetsam for his art project.
The beach was littered with flotsam and jetsam after the storm.
Their conversation was filled with flotsam and jetsam from their week.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: debris - scattered fragments, typically of something wrecked or destroyed
  • Synonym: rubbish - waste material; refuse or litter

Etymology

From 'flotsam' (goods floating after a shipwreck) and 'jetsam' (goods jettisoned from a ship), both with origins in Anglo-Norman French and Old French.
