Manx cat

1. NOUN

/ mæŋks kæt / (AmE), / mæŋks kæt / (BrE)
A breed of domestic cat with a naturally occurring mutation that results in a shortened or absent tail, originally from the Isle of Man.
tailless cat breed from Isle of Man

Examples

The Manx cat is famous for its absence of a tail and playful personality.
She adopted a Manx cat from the animal shelter last year.
Breeders on the Isle of Man have preserved the Manx cat for generations.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: cat - domesticated feline animal kept as a pet

Etymology

From Manx, relating to the Isle of Man, where the breed originated.
