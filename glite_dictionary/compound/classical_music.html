classical music

1. NOUN

/ ˈklæsɪkəl ˈmjuzɪk / (AmE), / ˈklæsɪkəl ˈmjuːzɪk / (BrE)
A genre of music characterized by sophisticated structure and formality, originating mainly in Europe from the 18th to early 19th centuries, and typically involving orchestras or ensembles.
European art music genre

Examples

Many people find classical music relaxing and enjoy listening to it while reading or studying.
She has played the violin in a classical music orchestra since she was a teenager.
The festival features performances of classical music by world-renowned conductors and soloists.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: pop music - popular genre of music emphasizing catchiness and mass appeal
  • Antonym: jazz - music genre influenced by African American culture with improvisation
