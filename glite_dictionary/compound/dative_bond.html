dative bond

1. NOUN

/ ˈdeɪ.tɪv bɑnd / (AmE), / ˈdeɪ.tɪv bɒnd / (BrE)
A covalent bond in which both electrons in the shared pair are provided by the same atom.
type of covalent bond (chemistry)
medical

Examples

The ammonia boron trifluoride complex contains a dative bond between nitrogen and boron.
A dative bond is often represented by an arrow from the donor to the acceptor atom.
Chemistry students learned how a dative bond differs from a typical covalent bond.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coordinate bond - A covalent bond where both electrons come from one atom; synonym for dative bond
  • Degree Higher: covalent bond - A chemical bond involving the sharing of electron pairs between atoms

Etymology

From Latin 'dativus' (given), referencing the concept of electron pair donation.
