ferret badger

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɛrət ˌbædʒər / (AmE), / ˈfɛrɪt ˌbadʒə / (BrE)
a small mammal of southeast Asia, resembling a ferret, with a long body and tail, belonging to the family Mustelidae.
small, ferret-like badger of southeast Asia

Examples

The ferret badger is commonly found in forests across Southeast Asia.
Researchers observed a group of ferret badgers foraging at dusk.
Unlike other badgers, the ferret badger has a slimmer body and a longer tail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: badger - a nocturnal burrowing mammal with a grey and black coat
  • Synonym: ferret - a domesticated polecat used for hunting rabbits
