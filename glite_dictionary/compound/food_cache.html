food cache

1. NOUN

/ fuːd kæʃ / (AmE), / fuːd kæʃ / (BrE)
a supply of food stored in a secure or hidden place, often for future use or in case of emergency
hidden or stored supply of food

Examples

The hikers discovered a food cache left behind by a previous expedition.
Squirrels create a food cache to survive through the winter when resources are scarce.
The explorers relied on the food cache they had buried months earlier.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cache - a secret or hidden store of things, especially valuables or provisions

Etymology

Cache from French 'cacher' (to hide).
