sauce chausseur

1. NOUN

/ sos ʃoʊˈsɜr / (AmE), / suːs ʃəˈsɜː / (BrE)
A rich brown sauce made with tomato purée, onions, mushrooms, and dry white wine, often served with meat.
classic French brown sauce with mushrooms and wine

Examples

The chef prepared a delicious sauce chausseur to accompany the roasted chicken.
Many French recipes for game meats call for a generous helping of sauce chausseur.
After sautéing the mushrooms and onions, he finished the sauce chausseur with a splash of white wine.

Etymology

From French 'sauce chasseur', meaning 'hunter's sauce'.
