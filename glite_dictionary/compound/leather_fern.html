leather fern

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛðər fɜrn / (AmE), / ˈlɛðə fɜːn / (BrE)
a widely distributed fern of the tropical Southern Hemisphere with leathery, deeply divided fronds
fern with leathery, divided fronds

Examples

The leather fern thrives in humid, tropical environments and is known for its tough fronds.
Gardeners often choose the leather fern for its resilience and attractive foliage.
In the wild, the leather fern helps stabilize riverbanks and wetlands with its strong roots.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɛðər fɜrn / (AmE), / ˈlɛðə fɜːn / (BrE)
A stout tropical swamp fern, especially found in tropical America, with large fronds and golden yellow sporangia on the undersides.
tropical swamp fern with large fronds

Examples

The leather fern thrives in the wet, humid conditions of Florida's swamps.
Botanists examined the underside of the leather fern fronds for their distinctive yellow sporangia.
We spotted a large leather fern growing near the edge of the marsh during our nature walk.
