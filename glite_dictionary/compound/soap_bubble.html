soap bubble

1. NOUN

/ soʊp ˈbʌbəl / (AmE), / səʊp ˈbʌbəl / (BrE)
a hollow sphere made from a thin film of soap and water, often formed when blowing air through soapy water
a bubble made from soapy water

Examples

Children love to chase and pop soap bubbles in the garden.
The sunlight made the soap bubble shimmer with rainbow colors.
She blew a giant soap bubble that floated gently through the air.

2. NOUN

/ soʊp ˈbʌbəl / (AmE), / səʊp ˈbʌbəl / (BrE)
a thin, hollow sphere made from a soap solution that is filled with air
bubble formed from soap solution

Examples

The sunlight reflected many colors in the soap bubble as it drifted upwards.
She blew a soap bubble that floated across the garden before popping.
Children love chasing after a soap bubble on a breezy day.

Synset Examples

The child watched the soap bubble float away.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bubble - a thin sphere of liquid enclosing air or gas
