least effort

1. NOUN

/ list ˈɛfɚt / (AmE), / liːst ˈɛfət / (BrE)
the smallest possible amount of effort needed to achieve a result
minimum required effort

Examples

He always completes his assignments with the least effort possible.
Many people choose the route that requires the least effort to save time and energy.
The principle of least effort suggests we prefer tasks that are easier to accomplish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: minimum - the least or smallest amount possible
  • Antonym: maximum effort - the greatest possible amount of effort
