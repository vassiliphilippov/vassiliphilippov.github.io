lima bean

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪmə ˌbin / (AmE), / ˈliːmə ˌbiːn / (BrE)
A flat, pale green or white edible bean from a Central or South American plant, usually eaten cooked and never raw.
an edible flat bean, eaten cooked

Examples

She added some lima beans to the soup for extra flavor and texture.
Lima beans should always be cooked thoroughly before eating.
My grandmother serves delicious stews made with lima beans and fresh herbs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: butter bean - another name for the lima bean, especially in the southern US and Britain

Etymology

From the city of Lima, Peru, where the bean was originally exported.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪmə ˌbin / (AmE), / ˈliːmə ˌbiːn / (BrE)
a plant of the bean family with large, flat, edible seeds eaten as a vegetable; also, its seed
type of bean plant and its edible seed

Examples

She added chopped lima beans to the vegetable stew for extra flavor and texture.
Many gardeners prefer growing lima bean plants because of their rich harvest.
The recipe called for both corn and lima beans as the main ingredients.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: butter bean - another name for lima bean, especially in British English
  • Synonym: broad bean - type of edible bean, not the same but similar

Etymology

Named after Lima, the capital of Peru.

3. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪmə ˌbin / (AmE), / ˈliːmə ˌbiːn / (BrE)
A type of bean plant with flat, edible seeds, cultivated mainly in warm regions for its beans.
edible flat bean; the plant or its seed

Examples

She added cooked lima beans to the vegetable stew for extra protein.
The farmer grows both green beans and lima beans in his fields.
I always disliked lima beans as a child, but now I enjoy their buttery flavor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: butter bean - another name for a large variety of lima bean seed

Etymology

Named after Lima, the capital of Peru, where the plant was first cultivated.
