voting precinct

1. NOUN

/ ˈvoʊtɪŋ priːˌsɪŋkt / (AmE), / ˈvəʊtɪŋ priːˌsɪŋkt / (BrE)
one of the districts into which an area is divided for the purposes of voting, each having a specific polling place
district for voting in elections

Examples

The city council discussed changing the boundaries of our voting precinct before the next election.
Voters must go to their assigned voting precinct to cast their ballots on election day.
Each voting precinct is responsible for reporting its results to the county office after the polls close.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polling station - place where people go to vote during an election
  • Synonym: electoral district - area represented by an elected official or used for the purpose of elections
