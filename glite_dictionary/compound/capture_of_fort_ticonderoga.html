Capture of Fort Ticonderoga

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæp.tʃɚ əv ˌfɔrt taɪˌkɑn.dɚˈoʊ.ɡə / (AmE), / ˈkæp.tʃə əv ˌfɔːt taɪˌkɒn.dəˈrəʊ.ɡə / (BrE)
the taking of Fort Ticonderoga by American revolutionary forces from the British in 1775 during the American Revolutionary War
seizure of Fort Ticonderoga by American forces, 1775

Examples

The Capture of Fort Ticonderoga marked a significant turning point in the early stages of the American Revolution.
History classes often highlight the Capture of Fort Ticonderoga as a key achievement for the Continental Army.
Many historians believe the Capture of Fort Ticonderoga provided vital artillery for the Siege of Boston.
