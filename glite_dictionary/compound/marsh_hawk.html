marsh hawk

1. NOUN

/ mɑrʃ hɑk / (AmE), / mɑːʃ hɔːk / (BrE)
A harrier (Circus cyaneus) found in North America and Europe, known for nesting in marshes and open country.
a harrier bird found in marshes

Examples

The marsh hawk glided low over the reeds in search of prey.
We spotted a marsh hawk nesting near the pond last spring.
Unlike other hawks, the marsh hawk often hunts by flying close to the ground.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: northern harrier - another common name for the marsh hawk
