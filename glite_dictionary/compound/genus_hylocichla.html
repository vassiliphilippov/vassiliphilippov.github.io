genus Hylocichla

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs haɪləˈsɪklə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs hɪləˈsɪklə / (BrE)
A genus of American thrushes, including the wood thrush, hermit thrush, and veery.
a genus of American thrushes

Examples

The genus Hylocichla includes some of North America's most beloved songbirds.
Biologists have classified the wood thrush under the genus Hylocichla.
The veery, a member of the genus Hylocichla, is known for its distinctive song.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thrush - a type of songbird in the family Turdidae

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Greek 'hylē' (wood) and 'kikhlē' (thrush).
