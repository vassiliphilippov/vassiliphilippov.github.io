Benjamin bush

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛn.dʒə.mɪn bʊʃ / (AmE), / ˈbɛn.dʒə.mɪn bʊʃ / (BrE)
A deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States, known for its highly aromatic leaves and bark, yellow flowers, and scarlet or yellow berries.
aromatic shrub of eastern US

Examples

The Benjamin bush filled the air with its distinctive fragrance in late spring.
Gardeners often plant Benjamin bush for its bright berries and aromatic foliage.
I admired the yellow flowers blooming on the old Benjamin bush near the fence.
