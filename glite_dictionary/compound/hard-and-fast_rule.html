hard-and-fast rule

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɑrd ən ˈfæst rul / (AmE), / ˌhɑːd ən ˈfɑːst ruːl / (BrE)
a rule or principle that is fixed and cannot be changed or adapted in different situations
strict, inflexible guideline

Examples

There are no hard-and-fast rules for raising children, as each situation is unique.
In creative writing, very few hard-and-fast rules actually apply across all genres.
She believed that following hard-and-fast rules would stifle innovation in the workplace.

Synset Examples

There are no hard-and-fast rules in this situation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: absolute rule - a rule that permits no exceptions
  • Degree Lower: guideline - a general rule or principle to guide behavior
