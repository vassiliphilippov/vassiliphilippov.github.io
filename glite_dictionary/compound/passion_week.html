Passion Week

1. NOUN

/ ˈpæʃən ˌwiːk / (AmE), / ˈpæʃən ˌwiːk / (BrE)
(in Christian tradition) the week before Easter, commemorating the events leading up to the crucifixion of Jesus.
the week before Easter

Examples

Many churches hold special services on each day of Passion Week.
During Passion Week, Christians reflect on the final days of Jesus' life.
Processions and rituals are common in many countries during Passion Week.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Holy Week - the week before Easter, especially as observed in Christianity

Etymology

From 'Passion' (referring to the suffering of Christ) and 'Week', referring to the seven days leading up to Easter.
