young padawan

1. NOUN

/ jʌŋ ˈpædəˌwɑn / (AmE), / jʌŋ ˈpædəˌwæn / (BrE)
a novice or apprentice, especially one in the early stages of learning or training, originally from the Star Wars universe
novice apprentice (orig. Star Wars)

Examples

As a young padawan, he made many mistakes but learned from every one.
The Jedi Master welcomed the young padawan into the council chamber.
Outside of Star Wars, people sometimes jokingly call a new employee a young padawan.

Synset Examples

The young padawan aspired to learn from a Jedi Master.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apprentice - someone who is learning a trade from a skilled employer
  • Synonym: novice - a person new to and inexperienced in a job or situation
  • Antonym: master - an expert or teacher in a particular field

Etymology

From 'Padawan', a term coined in the Star Wars franchise for a Jedi apprentice.
