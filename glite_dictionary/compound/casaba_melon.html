casaba melon

1. NOUN

/ kəˈsɑːbə ˈmɛlən / (AmE), / kəˈsɑːbə ˈmɛlən / (BrE)
a large melon with a yellowish rind and sweet, whitish flesh
a type of melon with yellow rind and white flesh

Examples

She bought a ripe casaba melon from the farmers' market for the picnic.
The dessert was topped with slices of fresh casaba melon and strawberries.
After dinner, we enjoyed some chilled casaba melon as a refreshing treat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: melon - a large fruit with a hard rind, juicy flesh, and many seeds

Etymology

From Spanish *casaba*, named after Kasaba (now Turgutlu), a town in Turkey.
