forehand drive

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔrˌhænd draɪv / (AmE), / ˈfɔːˌhænd draɪv / (BrE)
a hard, straight shot played on the forehand side, especially in sports such as tennis, badminton, or squash.
hard straight forehand shot in racket sports

Examples

She executed a powerful forehand drive down the line to win the point.
His forehand drive is his strongest shot on the tennis court.
The coach praised her technique after watching her hit several perfect forehand drives.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forehand - a shot played on the racket-holding side of the body
  • Antonym: backhand drive - a hard, straight shot made on the backhand side in racket sports
