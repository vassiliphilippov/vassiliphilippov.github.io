sassafras oil

1. NOUN

/ ˈsæsəˌfræs ɔɪl / (AmE), / ˈsasəˌfras ɔɪl / (BrE)
an aromatic oil obtained from the root bark of sassafras trees, used chiefly in perfumery and as a disinfectant
oil from the root bark of sassafras trees

Examples

The fragrance industry uses sassafras oil to create distinctive scents for perfumes and soaps.
Some traditional remedies once included sassafras oil for its supposed healing properties.
Due to health regulations, sassafras oil is less commonly found in commercial products today.

Etymology

From American English 'sassafras' (a type of tree), itself from Spanish 'sasafrás' and ultimately from a Native American language.
