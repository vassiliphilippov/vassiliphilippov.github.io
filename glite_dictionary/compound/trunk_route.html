trunk route

1. NOUN

/ trʌŋk ruːt / (AmE), / trʌŋk ruːt / (BrE)
the main route or principal line in a railway system, usually carrying the majority of traffic or serving as a primary connection between major destinations
main line in a railway system

Examples

The Orient Express followed a trunk route that connected several major European cities.
Freight trains generally use the trunk route to ensure faster transit times across the country.
Upgrades to the trunk route will help reduce delays and increase capacity for all rail services.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: main line - the chief route of a railway system
