turnip cabbage

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɝnɪp ˈkæbɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈtɜːnɪp ˈkæbɪdʒ / (BrE)
a cruciferous plant with a thick, bulbous edible yellow or white root, grown as a vegetable
bulbous-rooted cruciferous vegetable

Examples

The farmer grew turnip cabbage alongside carrots and potatoes in his vegetable garden.
Roasted turnip cabbage makes a delicious and healthy side dish for dinner.
In the market, the turnip cabbage was displayed prominently next to other winter vegetables.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kohlrabi - a cultivar of cabbage with a swollen edible stem

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɝː.nɪp ˈkæb.ɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈtɜː.nɪp ˈkæb.ɪdʒ / (BrE)
The swollen, edible stem of the kohlrabi plant, resembling a turnip in shape and used as a vegetable.
edible stem of the kohlrabi plant

Examples

She sliced the turnip cabbage and added it to the hearty vegetable stew.
Farmers in the region commonly grow turnip cabbage for both human consumption and animal feed.
The market was selling fresh, locally grown turnip cabbages alongside carrots and potatoes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kohlrabi - a cultivated variety of cabbage with a bulbous stem eaten as a vegetable
