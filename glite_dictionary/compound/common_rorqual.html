common rorqual

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmən ˈroʊr.kwəl / (AmE), / ˈkɒm.ən ˈrɔː.kwəl / (BrE)
a large baleen whale with a flat head and deep throat grooves, found in the Atlantic and Pacific Oceans; also known as the fin whale
large baleen whale, also called fin whale

Examples

The common rorqual is the second largest whale in the world after the blue whale.
Scientists are tracking migration patterns of the common rorqual across the Pacific Ocean.
A common rorqual can reach lengths of up to 27 meters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fin whale - another common name for the common rorqual

Etymology

From New Latin *rorqualus*, from Norwegian *rorhval* ('furrow whale'), referring to grooves on the throat.
