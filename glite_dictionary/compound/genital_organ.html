genital organ

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɛnɪtəl ˈɔːrɡən / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛnɪtəl ˈɔːɡən / (BrE)
an external sex organ of a male or female animal or human
external sex organ
medical

Examples

Children should be taught the correct names for their genital organs as part of health education.
The doctor checked the patient for any abnormalities in the genital organ.
Many insects have complex genital organs that differ between species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: genitalia - the external organs of reproduction, especially of the human; the genitals

Etymology

From Latin 'genitalis' (pertaining to generation or birth) and Greek 'organon' (instrument, tool).
