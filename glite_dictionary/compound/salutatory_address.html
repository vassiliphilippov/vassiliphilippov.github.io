salutatory address

1. NOUN

/ səˈl(j)uːtəˌtɔri əˈdrɛs / (AmE), / səˈljuːtətəri əˈdrɛs / (BrE)
an opening or welcoming speech, especially one made at a graduation ceremony by a representative of the graduating class
welcome speech at graduation

Examples

The student council president delivered the salutatory address at the start of the graduation ceremony.
Her heartfelt salutatory address welcomed families, teachers, and students to the event.
As tradition dictates, the salutatory address set a warm and optimistic tone for the commencement.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: valedictory address - a farewell speech given at a graduation ceremony

Etymology

From Latin 'salutatorius' (pertaining to greeting) and 'address'.
