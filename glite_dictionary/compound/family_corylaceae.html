family Corylaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ˌkɔrɪˈleɪsiːˌiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli ˌkɒrɪˈleɪsɪˌiː / (BrE)
a family of flowering plants that includes the genus Corylus, such as hazels, in some classification systems
botanical family including hazels

Examples

The family Corylaceae includes several important species of hazel found in temperate regions.
Some botanists still classify hazels under the family Corylaceae.
The reclassification of the family Corylaceae has led to debate among plant taxonomists.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: genus Corylus - the genus including hazel trees and shrubs

Etymology

From New Latin Corylaceae, formed from Corylus (genus of hazels) + -aceae (a standard botanical family suffix).
