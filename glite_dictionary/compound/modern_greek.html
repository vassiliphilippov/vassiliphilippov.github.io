Modern Greek

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑdərn ɡrik / (AmE), / ˈmɒdən ɡriːk / (BrE)
the form of the Greek language spoken and written from the late medieval period to the present day, distinguished from Ancient and Medieval Greek
contemporary Greek language

Examples

She is fluent in both Modern Greek and English.
Many ancient texts have been translated into Modern Greek for contemporary readers.
At university, I chose to study Modern Greek literature.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Ancient Greek - the Greek language as used from the 9th century BCE to 6th century CE
  • Antonym: Medieval Greek - the Greek language as used from around 600 to 1500 CE
