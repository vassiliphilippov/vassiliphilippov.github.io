genus Rickettsia

1. NOUN

/ ˈriː.kɛt.sɪ.ə / (AmE), / rɪˈkɛts.i.ə / (BrE)
A genus of bacteria that can cause diseases such as typhus and Rocky Mountain spotted fever in humans.
bacterial genus causing typhus and other diseases
medical

Examples

The genus Rickettsia includes several species that cause significant diseases in humans.
Scientists isolated a new strain within the genus Rickettsia from the infected tissue.
Understanding the genus Rickettsia is crucial for controlling tick-borne illnesses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Rickettsia - bacteria of the genus Rickettsia, responsible for various diseases

Etymology

Named after American pathologist Howard Taylor Ricketts (1871–1910).
