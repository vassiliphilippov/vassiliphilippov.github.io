radiant flux

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪdiənt flʌks / (AmE), / ˈreɪdiənt flʌks / (BrE)
the rate at which radiant energy (electromagnetic waves) is emitted, transferred, or received, typically measured in watts
rate of flow of radiant energy

Examples

The solar panel's efficiency is affected by the amount of radiant flux it receives from the sun.
Scientists measured the radiant flux emitted by the new type of LED lamp.
Increasing the radiant flux can improve the growth rate of certain plants in greenhouses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: radiant energy - energy transmitted by electromagnetic waves

Etymology

From 'radiant' (from Latin 'radiare', to shine) + 'flux' (from Latin 'fluxus', flow).
