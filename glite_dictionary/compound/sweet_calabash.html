sweet calabash

1. NOUN

/ swit ˈkæl.əˌbæʃ / (AmE), / swiːt ˈkæl.əˌbæʃ / (BrE)
a tropical West Indian vine (Passiflora maliformis) bearing edible, apple-sized fruits with a sweet, aromatic flavor
a fruit-bearing tropical vine of the West Indies

Examples

The juice of the sweet calabash is often used to make refreshing drinks in the Caribbean.
We enjoyed the tangy flavor of the sweet calabash during our trip to Jamaica.
Many local markets featured piles of freshly harvested sweet calabash fruits.

2. NOUN

/ swit ˈkæləbæʃ / (AmE), / swiːt ˈkæləbæʃ / (BrE)

tropical passion fruit of the West Indies

Examples

We tasted the ripe sweet calabash while visiting a farm in Jamaica.
The sweet calabash is prized for its fragrant, sweet pulp.
