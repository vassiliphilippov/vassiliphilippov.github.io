gold dust

1. NOUN

/ ɡoʊld dʌst / (AmE), / ɡəʊld dʌst / (BrE)
fine particles or flakes of gold, often obtained from placer mining or naturally occurring gold deposits
fine particles or flakes of gold

Examples

The miners eagerly collected gold dust from the riverbed after a long day of panning.
During the gold rush, even a small vial of gold dust could fetch a high price.
The prospector weighed the gold dust he had sifted, hoping it would be enough to sell.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: gold nugget - a solid lump of gold, larger than gold dust
