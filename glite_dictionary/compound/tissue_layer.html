tissue layer

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɪʃu ˌleɪɚ / (AmE), / ˈtɪʃu ˌleɪə / (BrE)
a sheet of biological tissue that covers, lines, or connects organs or cells in animals or plants
sheet of biological tissue in organisms

Examples

The outer tissue layer of the leaf helps protect it from drying out.
During surgery, the doctor carefully separated each tissue layer to reach the organ.
A thin tissue layer lines the inside of the human stomach.
