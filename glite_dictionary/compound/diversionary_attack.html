diversionary attack

1. NOUN

/ dəˈvɜrʒəˌnɛri əˈtæk / (AmE), / dɪˈvɜːʃənəri əˈtæk / (BrE)
a military attack intended to distract the enemy and draw their defenses away from the main point of assault
military attack used as a distraction

Examples

The commander ordered a diversionary attack to distract the enemy from the main advance.
A well-executed diversionary attack can significantly weaken an enemy's defenses at the critical point.
During the battle, the troops launched a diversionary attack to mislead the opposition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: feint - a deceptive or pretended blow or attack

Etymology

From diversionary, from 'diversion' (Latin divertere, 'to turn aside'), plus 'attack.'
