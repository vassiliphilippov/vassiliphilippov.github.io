anionic detergent

1. NOUN

/ ænˈaɪɑnɪk dɪˈtɝdʒənt / (AmE), / ænˈaɪɒnɪk dɪˈtɜːdʒənt / (BrE)
a type of synthetic detergent whose molecules carry a negative charge in aqueous solutions, commonly used in cleaning products
synthetic detergent with negatively charged molecules

Examples

This soap contains an anionic detergent that helps break down grease and oil.
Many modern laundry powders use anionic detergents for their effective cleaning performance.
The effectiveness of an anionic detergent can be reduced by hard water.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cationic detergent - a synthetic detergent whose molecules carry a positive charge

Etymology

From 'anion', meaning a negatively charged ion, and 'detergent'.
