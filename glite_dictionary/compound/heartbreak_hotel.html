Heartbreak Hotel

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑrtˌbreɪk hoʊˈtɛl / (AmE), / ˈhɑːtˌbreɪk həʊˈtɛl / (BrE)
A metaphorical expression referring to a state or place of deep emotional distress, especially following romantic disappointment or a breakup.
state of emotional distress after heartbreak

Examples

After her divorce, Jane said she felt like she was staying at Heartbreak Hotel.
For months after the breakup, every sad song sounded like it was playing in Heartbreak Hotel.
He joked about reserving a permanent room at Heartbreak Hotel after another failed relationship.

Synset Examples

She joked about living in Heartbreak Hotel after losing love.
He checked into Heartbreak Hotel after his breakup.
This song reminds me of my time at Heartbreak Hotel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: despair - a state of complete loss of hope
  • Synonym: misery - a state of great unhappiness or emotional pain

Etymology

From the title of the 1956 Elvis Presley song 'Heartbreak Hotel', used metaphorically to describe sadness after romantic loss.
