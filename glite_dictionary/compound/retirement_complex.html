retirement complex

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈtaɪərmənt ˈkɑmplɛks / (AmE), / rɪˈtaɪəmənt ˈkɒmplɛks / (BrE)
a residential community designed for people who have retired from active working life, often offering special amenities and services
community for retired people

Examples

After turning sixty-five, they decided to move into a retirement complex closer to their family.
The retirement complex offered on-site medical care and organized activities for its residents.
Many seniors appreciate the sense of community and security found in a retirement complex.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: retirement community - housing facility or neighborhood for retired people
