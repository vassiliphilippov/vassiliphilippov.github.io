Golgi cell

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡoʊl.dʒi sɛl / (AmE), / ˈɡɒl.dʒi sɛl / (BrE)
a type of neuron found in the cerebral cortex or cerebellum, with short dendrites and either a long axon or a short axon that branches in the grey matter
a type of neuron in the brain
medical

Examples

The cerebellum contains many Golgi cells that help regulate motor coordination.
Researchers used a microscope to study the unique structure of Golgi cells in the brain.
Damage to Golgi cells can disrupt normal signaling within the central nervous system.

Etymology

Named after Camillo Golgi, Italian physician and scientist (1843–1926), who first described the cell.
