fire tree

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪɚ ˌtri / (AmE), / ˈfaɪə ˌtriː / (BrE)
a terrestrial evergreen shrub or small tree of western Australia with brilliant yellow-orange flowers, parasitic on the roots of grasses
Australian shrub or small tree with orange flowers

Examples

The fire tree blooms each spring with a spectacular display of orange flowers.
Botanists studied the parasitic habits of the fire tree in its native habitat.
In western Australia, the fire tree is admired for its vibrant blossoms and unique ecology.
