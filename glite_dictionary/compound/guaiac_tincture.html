guaiac tincture

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡwaɪ.æk ˈtɪŋk.tʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈɡwaɪ.æk ˈtɪŋk.tʃə / (BrE)
a solution of guaiac resin in alcohol, used as a reagent for detecting the presence of proteins, especially in diagnostic tests for blood.
solution of guaiac resin in alcohol, used in testing
medical

Examples

A drop of guaiac tincture was added to the solution and the color change was monitored.
Doctors often use guaiac tincture in tests to check for hidden blood in stool samples.
The guaiac tincture reacted when protein was present in the sample.

Synset Examples

Guaiac tincture can be used to detect blood in stool.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guaiacum - a resin from the Guaiacum tree, used in medical and chemical testing
  • Synonym: reagent - a substance used in a chemical reaction to detect another substance

Etymology

From 'guaiac,' from the Taíno word 'guayacán', referring to the tree yielding the resin, and 'tincture,' from Latin 'tinctura' meaning dyeing or coloring.
