mountain fetterbush

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaʊntən ˈfɛtərˌbʊʃ / (AmE), / ˈmaʊntən ˈfɛtəˌbʊʃ / (BrE)
an ornamental evergreen shrub of the southeastern United States, with small white bell-shaped flowers
a southeastern US shrub with white bell-shaped flowers

Examples

The mountain fetterbush thrives in the moist, sandy soils of southeastern forests.
Gardeners appreciate the mountain fetterbush for its delicate white flowers and glossy green leaves.
Wildlife often shelter beneath the dense foliage of the mountain fetterbush.
