major league

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪdʒər liːɡ / (AmE), / ˈmeɪdʒə liːɡ / (BrE)
the highest and most important level of professional competition in a sport, especially baseball or other team sports
top-level professional sports league

Examples

After years in the minors, she finally made it to the major league.
Sponsorships are much larger in the major league than in amateur clubs.
The player dreams of pitching in the major league one day.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: minor league - a lower-level professional sports league

2. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪ.dʒɚ liːɡ / (AmE), / ˈmeɪ.dʒə liːɡ / (BrE)
The top professional level of competition in sports, or by extension, an indication of a very high standard of achievement or professionalism.
top professional level (esp. in sports)

Examples

Only major league teams have such advanced training facilities.
Her performance was major league, impressing even the toughest critics.
After years in the minors, he finally made it to the major league.

Synset Examples

Only major league teams can afford such salaries.
Her skills are major league; she could go pro.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: minor league - a lower professional level, especially in sports

Etymology

From 'major' (greater, more important) + 'league' (sports association); originally used in American baseball.
