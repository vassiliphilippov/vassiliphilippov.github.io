boiling water

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɔɪlɪŋ ˈwɔtər / (AmE), / ˈbɔɪlɪŋ ˈwɔːtə / (BrE)
water that has been heated to or above its boiling point, at which it changes into steam.
water at boiling point

Examples

Be careful when pouring boiling water into the mug to avoid burns.
She added the pasta to a pot of boiling water and stirred gently.
The recipe says to use boiling water to dissolve the gelatin.

Synset Examples

There's a kettle of boiling water on the stove.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: hot water - water that is heated but below the boiling point
