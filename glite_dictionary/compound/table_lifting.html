table lifting

1. NOUN

/ ˈteɪbəl ˌlɪftɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈteɪbl ˌlɪftɪŋ / (BrE)
The alleged raising or movement of a table during a seance, believed by participants to be caused by spirits.
movement of table in a seance

Examples

During the Victorian era, table lifting became a popular parlor activity at spiritualist gatherings.
Skeptics tried to explain the mysterious table lifting as the result of unconscious muscle movements.
The medium claimed that the table lifting was a sign the spirits were present.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: table-turning - the phenomenon of a table rotating during a seance, attributed to spirits
