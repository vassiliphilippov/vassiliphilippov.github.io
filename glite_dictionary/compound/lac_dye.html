lac dye

1. NOUN

/ læk daɪ / (AmE), / lak daɪ / (BrE)
a scarlet dye obtained from stick lac, similar in color to cochineal and extracted using an alkali
scarlet dye from stick lac

Examples

Textiles were once colored with lac dye before synthetic dyes became common.
The vibrant red of the antique fabric came from lac dye.
Researchers studied the composition of lac dye to understand its unique color properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cochineal - a scarlet dye made from the cochineal insect

Etymology

From 'lac', from Persian 'lak' (red resinous substance), plus 'dye'.
