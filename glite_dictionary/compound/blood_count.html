blood count

1. NOUN

/ blʌd kaʊnt / (AmE), / blʌd kaʊnt / (BrE)
a medical test in which the number of red and white blood cells and platelets in a blood sample is measured
test measuring components of blood
medical

Examples

The doctor ordered a blood count to check for signs of infection.
A routine blood count can help diagnose various health conditions early.
Her blood count results indicated she needed further evaluation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: complete blood count - a detailed blood test measuring various parameters

2. NOUN

/ ˈblʌd ˌkaʊnt / (AmE), / ˈblʌd ˌkaʊnt / (BrE)
the number of red and white blood cells in a given amount of blood, measured to assess health or diagnose disease
number of blood cells in a sample
medical

Examples

The doctor ordered a blood count to check for signs of infection.
Her blood count was low after the surgery, so she needed a transfusion.
A routine blood count can help detect many medical conditions early.
