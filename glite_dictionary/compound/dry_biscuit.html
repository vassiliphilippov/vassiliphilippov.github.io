dry biscuit

1. NOUN

/ draɪ ˈbɪskɪt / (AmE), / draɪ ˈbɪskɪt / (BrE)
a biscuit that is hard, plain, and lacks moisture, often considered uninteresting or bland
plain, hard, and unmoistened biscuit

Examples

He only served dry biscuits with the tea, which disappointed the guests.
After a long hike, even a dry biscuit tasted delicious to him.
They munched on dry biscuits during the road trip, wishing for something more flavorful.

Synset Examples

The party snacks were just dry biscuits and water.
He complained about the dry biscuits at the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cracker - a thin, crisp wafer often eaten as a snack
  • Degree Higher: cookie - a sweet baked snack, often softer or more moist
