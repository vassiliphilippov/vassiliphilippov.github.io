Stokes-Adams syndrome

1. NOUN

/ ˈstoʊks ˈædəmz ˌsɪnˌdroʊm / (AmE), / ˈstəʊks ˈædəmz ˌsɪndrəʊm / (BrE)
A medical condition characterized by recurrent sudden episodes of unconsciousness caused by impaired conduction of the electrical impulse that regulates the heartbeat.
sudden unconsciousness from heartbeat conduction disorder
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with Stokes-Adams syndrome after experiencing brief losses of consciousness.
Doctors monitored his heart rhythm closely following an episode of Stokes-Adams syndrome.
Treatment for Stokes-Adams syndrome often involves the implantation of a pacemaker.

Etymology

Named after Irish physicians William Stokes (1804–1878) and Robert Adams (1791–1875).
