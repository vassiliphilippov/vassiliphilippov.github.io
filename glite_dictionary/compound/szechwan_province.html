Szechwan province

1. PROPN

/ ˈʃɛtʃˌwɑn prəˈvɪns / (AmE), / ˈsɛtʃwæn prəˈvɪns / (BrE)
a populous province in south-central China, known for its cuisine and cultural heritage
province in south-central China

Examples

The spicy dishes from Szechwan province are famous around the world.
She traveled through Szechwan province to explore its scenic mountains and rivers.
Many immigrants from Szechwan province settled in major cities across China.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sichuan province - a large province in southwest China (modern spelling)

Etymology

From Chinese 四川 (Sìchuān), rendered as Szechwan in older romanization systems.
