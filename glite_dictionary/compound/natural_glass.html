natural glass

1. NOUN

/ ˈnætʃrəl ɡlæs / (AmE), / ˈnatʃrəl ɡlɑːs / (BrE)
Glass formed in nature by the rapid cooling of molten rock (magma), such as obsidian and pumice.
glass formed naturally from cooling magma

Examples

Obsidian is a type of natural glass formed from quickly cooled lava.
Geologists study natural glass to understand volcanic processes.
The shiny, black surface of natural glass is often used for making tools.
