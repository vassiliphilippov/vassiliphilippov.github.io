friction tape

1. NOUN

/ ˈfrɪkʃən teɪp / (AmE), / ˈfrɪkʃən teɪp / (BrE)
a type of water-resistant adhesive tape, typically made of cloth coated with a sticky substance, used to insulate exposed electrical conductors
water-resistant adhesive tape for insulating electrical wires

Examples

The electrician wrapped friction tape around the exposed wires to prevent a short circuit.
You should use friction tape instead of regular tape when insulating electrical connections outdoors.
Old bundles of cables in the basement were still covered with friction tape.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: electrical tape - adhesive tape for insulating electrical wires, usually made of vinyl or plastic
