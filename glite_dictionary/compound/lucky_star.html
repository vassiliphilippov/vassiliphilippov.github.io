lucky star

1. NOUN

/ ˈlʌki stɑr / (AmE), / ˈlʌki stɑː / (BrE)
a personified source of good luck or a fortunate influence, often referred to as a guiding or protective power
a personified source of good luck

Examples

Ever since she started her new job, she believes her lucky star has been shining on her.
He thanked his lucky star for helping him escape the accident unscathed.
You should count your lucky stars that you found such a wonderful friend.

Synset Examples

Winning the contest made her feel like she had a lucky star.
He met his soulmate; he must have a lucky star.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fortune - luck, especially good luck or favorable fate
  • Antonym: jinx - someone or something that brings bad luck

Etymology

From the common belief that stars influence human destiny, with 'lucky' describing the favorable nature of such a star.
