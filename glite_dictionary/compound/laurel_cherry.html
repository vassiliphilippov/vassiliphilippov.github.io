laurel cherry

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɔrəl ˈtʃɛri / (AmE), / ˈlɒrəl ˈtʃɛri / (BrE)
a Eurasian evergreen shrub or small tree (Prunus laurocerasus) with glossy leaves, clusters of white flowers, and fruit bearing oil similar to bitter almond oil, often grown ornamentally
Eurasian evergreen shrub/tree with glossy leaves and white flowers

Examples

The laurel cherry is often used as a decorative hedge in gardens.
Gardeners appreciate the glossy leaves and fragrant flowers of the laurel cherry.
Birds are attracted to the fruit of the laurel cherry, even though it is toxic to humans.

Etymology

Named for its laurel-like leaves and cherry-like fruit, combining 'laurel' and 'cherry'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɔrəl ˈtʃɛri / (AmE), / ˈlɒrəl ˈtʃɛri / (BrE)
a small evergreen tree of the southern United States, valued for its glossy leaves and often used in landscaping
small evergreen tree of the southern US

Examples

The laurel cherry is a popular choice for ornamental hedges in the southern states.
Gardeners like to plant laurel cherry for its glossy green foliage and dense form.
The berries of the laurel cherry closely resemble those of true cherries, but are not edible.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cherry laurel - an evergreen shrub or small tree with glossy leaves and cherry-like fruit

Etymology

From 'laurel' (because of similar leaves to true laurel) and 'cherry' (due to its cherry-like fruit).
