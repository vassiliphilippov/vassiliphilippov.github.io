North Pole

1. PROPN

/ nɔrθ poʊl / (AmE), / nɔːθ pəʊl / (BrE)
the point in the Northern Hemisphere where the Earth's axis of rotation meets its surface; the northernmost point on Earth
northernmost point on Earth

Examples

Explorers have risked their lives to reach the North Pole.
The North Pole experiences continuous daylight for six months each year.
Santa Claus is often said to live at the North Pole.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: South Pole - the southernmost point of the Earth's axis
