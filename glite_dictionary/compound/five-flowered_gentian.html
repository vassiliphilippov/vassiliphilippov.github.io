five-flowered gentian

1. NOUN

/ faɪv ˈflaʊərd ˈdʒɛnʃən / (AmE), / faɪv ˈflaʊəd ˈdʒɛnʃən / (BrE)
A gentian native to eastern North America, distinguished by its clusters of bristly blue flowers.
North American gentian with bristly blue flowers

Examples

We spotted a cluster of five-flowered gentian blooming along the woodland trail.
The vibrant blue petals of the five-flowered gentian attract many photographers each spring.
Botanists study the five-flowered gentian due to its striking bristly flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gentian - a plant of the genus Gentiana, known for its intensely blue flowers
