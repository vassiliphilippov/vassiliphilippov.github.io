milk adder

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪlk ˌædər / (AmE), / ˈmɪlk ˌadə / (BrE)
A nonvenomous tan and brown kingsnake, some southeastern varieties having red stripes resembling coral snakes, known for an arrow-shaped spot on the back of its head.
nonvenomous king snake with occipital spot

Examples

The milk adder is often mistaken for the venomous coral snake due to its red stripes.
Many southeastern milk adders have an arrow-shaped spot on their heads, distinguishing them from similar species.
Although the milk adder is harmless, its striking colors serve as a warning to potential predators.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kingsnake - a nonvenomous snake of the genus Lampropeltis
