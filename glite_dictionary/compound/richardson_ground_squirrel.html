Richardson ground squirrel

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɪtʃərdsən ɡraʊnd ˈskwɝl / (AmE), / ˈrɪtʃəd­sən ɡraʊnd ˈskwɪrəl / (BrE)
a ground squirrel species (Urocitellus richardsonii) found in sagebrush and grassland areas of the western United States and Canada
North American ground squirrel species

Examples

We spotted a Richardson ground squirrel darting across the prairie in Alberta.
The presence of Richardson ground squirrels is common in open grasslands of the northern Great Plains.
Farmers often take measures to control the population of Richardson ground squirrels to protect their crops.

Etymology

Named after Scottish naturalist Sir John Richardson (1787–1865).
