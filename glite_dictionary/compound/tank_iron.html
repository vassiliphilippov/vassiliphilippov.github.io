tank iron

1. NOUN

/ tæŋk ˈaɪ.ɚn / (AmE), / tæŋk ˈaɪ.ən / (BrE)
plate iron that is thinner than boilerplate but thicker than sheet iron or stovepipe iron, used especially in making tanks and similar vessels
type of plate iron used in tanks

Examples

The engineer specified tank iron for the construction of the water vessel.
Older storage tanks were commonly built from sheets of tank iron.
Because of its thickness, tank iron provides better strength than ordinary sheet metal.
