high-stakes competition

1. NOUN

/ haɪ steɪks kəmˌpɪtɪˈʃən / (AmE), / haɪ steɪks kəmˌpɪtɪˈʃən / (BrE)
a competition in which the risks or rewards are particularly significant or consequential
competition with high risks or rewards

Examples

Qualifying for the national team is always a high-stakes competition that puts athletes under pressure.
Startups often enter high-stakes competitions to attract critical funding and partnerships.
The chess championship was a high-stakes competition that drew attention from around the world.

Synset Examples

High-stakes competitions attract the best athletes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: competition - the activity or condition of competing
