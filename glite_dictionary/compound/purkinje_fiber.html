Purkinje fiber

1. NOUN

/ pərˈkɪndʒi ˈfaɪbər / (AmE), / pəˈkɪndʒi ˈfaɪbə / (BrE)
A specialized cardiac muscle fiber forming part of the electrical conduction system of the heart, responsible for transmitting impulses within the ventricles.
specialized cardiac muscle fiber in the heart
medical

Examples

A blockage in a Purkinje fiber can seriously disrupt the heart's rhythm.
The Purkinje fibers play a crucial role in ensuring coordinated ventricular contraction.
Researchers observed abnormal electrical conduction along the Purkinje fibers in patients with heart disease.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cardiac muscle - muscular tissue of the heart

Etymology

Named after Czech physiologist Jan Evangelista Purkinje (1787–1869).
