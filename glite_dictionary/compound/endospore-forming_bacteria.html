endospore-forming bacteria

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛndoʊˌspɔr ˈfɔrmɪŋ bəˈktɪriə / (AmE), / ˈɛndəʊˌspɔː ˈfɔːmɪŋ bækˈtɪəriə / (BrE)
a group of bacteria capable of producing endospores, which are resistant, dormant structures that allow survival in harsh conditions
bacteria that form endospores
medical

Examples

Some species of endospore-forming bacteria can survive extreme heat and radiation.
Laboratory tests showed the presence of endospore-forming bacteria in the soil sample.
The resilience of endospore-forming bacteria poses a challenge for hospital sterilization procedures.

Etymology

From 'endospore' (Greek endon 'within' + spora 'seed') + 'forming' + 'bacteria' (plural of bacterium, from Greek bakterion)
