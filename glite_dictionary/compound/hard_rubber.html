hard rubber

1. NOUN

/ hɑrd ˈrʌbɚ / (AmE), / hɑːd ˈrʌbə / (BrE)
a hard, nonresilient form of rubber produced by vulcanizing natural rubber, used in manufacturing and electrical insulation
nonresilient vulcanized rubber material

Examples

Old radios were often encased in hard rubber to protect their internal components.
The handle of the tool is made from hard rubber, offering durability and grip.
Early fountain pens commonly used hard rubber for their barrels and caps.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ebonite - hard rubber made by vulcanizing rubber with a high amount of sulfur
