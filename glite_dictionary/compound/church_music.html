church music

1. NOUN

/ tʃɝtʃ ˈmjuːzɪk / (AmE), / tʃɜːtʃ ˈmjuːzɪk / (BrE)
music composed for or performed as part of religious ceremonies, especially in a Christian church setting
music for religious ceremonies

Examples

The choir specialized in singing church music from the Baroque period.
He composed several pieces of church music for the Sunday service.
Many people find comfort in listening to church music during times of reflection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sacred music - music written for religious use; not secular music
  • Antonym: secular music - music not associated with religious ceremonies
