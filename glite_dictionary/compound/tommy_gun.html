Tommy gun

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɑːmi ɡʌn / (AmE), / ˈtɒmi ɡʌn / (BrE)
A .45-caliber submachine gun, especially the Thompson submachine gun, widely used in the early to mid-20th century.
a .45-caliber submachine gun (Thompson)

Examples

Gangsters in the 1920s were notorious for using the Tommy gun during bank robberies.
The soldier carried a Tommy gun into battle during World War II.
Collectors value the classic Tommy gun for its historical significance and unique design.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: submachine gun - A lightweight automatic gun designed to be fired from the hip or shoulder

Etymology

Named after its inventor, John T. Thompson.
