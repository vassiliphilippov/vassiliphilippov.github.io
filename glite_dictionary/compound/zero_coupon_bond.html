zero coupon bond

1. NOUN

/ ˈzɪɹoʊ ˈkuːpɑn bɑnd / (AmE), / ˈzɪərəʊ ˈkuːpɒn bɒnd / (BrE)
A bond sold at a substantial discount to its face value and that does not pay interest during its term, with the full value paid at maturity.
bond sold at discount, no periodic interest

Examples

Many investors prefer zero coupon bonds for long-term savings because they accumulate interest over time.
He purchased a zero coupon bond that will mature in ten years at its full face value.
Zero coupon bonds are often used to fund future expenses like college tuition or retirement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bond - a certificate issued by a government or company promising to pay back borrowed money with interest
