wood pulp

1. NOUN

/ wʊd pʌlp / (AmE), / wʊd pʌlp / (BrE)
Wood that has been reduced to a moist, fibrous mass, used especially in making paper or other cellulose products.
fibrous mass from wood for papermaking

Examples

The factory processes wood pulp to manufacture high-quality paper products.
Most newspapers are printed on paper made from wood pulp.
Sustainable forestry practices can ensure a steady supply of wood pulp for industry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pulp - soft, moist, shapeless mass of matter
