light middleweight

1. NOUN

/ laɪt ˈmɪdəlˌweɪt / (AmE), / laɪt ˈmɪdəlweɪt / (BrE)
A weight division in combat sports for competitors who are heavier than welterweights but lighter than middleweights.
boxing or martial arts weight division

Examples

After months of training, he finally moved up to the light middleweight division.
The light middleweight title fight drew a large audience to the arena.
Several future champions began their careers in the light middleweight category.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: welterweight - a weight division below light middleweight
  • Degree Higher: middleweight - a weight division above light middleweight

Etymology

Formed from the descriptive sequence: 'light' (less heavy), 'middle', and 'weight', denoting a position between welterweight and middleweight.
