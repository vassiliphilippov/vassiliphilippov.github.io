Valenciennes lace

1. NOUN

/ vəˌlɑːnsiˈɛnz leɪs / (AmE), / vəˌlɒnsɪˈɛnz leɪs / (BrE)
a fine type of bobbin lace made with floral patterns, originally from Valenciennes, France
fine bobbin lace with floral patterns

Examples

The museum displays an exquisite collection of Valenciennes lace from the eighteenth century.
Her wedding dress was trimmed with delicate Valenciennes lace around the neckline and sleeves.
Collectors often seek out antique Valenciennes lace for its fine craftsmanship and intricate designs.

Etymology

Named after Valenciennes, a city in northern France known for lace-making.
