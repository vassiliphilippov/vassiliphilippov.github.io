easy money

1. NOUN

/ ˈizi ˈmʌni / (AmE), / ˈiːzi ˈmʌni / (BrE)
A situation where credit or loans are readily available and borrowing is simple, often due to low interest rates or relaxed lending standards.
a period of readily available credit

Examples

During the era of easy money, many people bought houses they could not afford.
The central bank's decision led to a period of easy money for borrowers.
Experts warned that easy money policies could eventually inflate asset bubbles.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: tight money - an economic condition where credit is difficult to obtain

2. NOUN

/ ˈiːzi ˈmʌni / (AmE), / ˈiːzi ˈmʌni / (BrE)
money earned with little effort or difficulty, often implying it was obtained in a simple or effortless way
money gained with little effort

Examples

He was always looking for easy money and tried his luck in online gambling.
Many people dream of making easy money without putting in too much effort.
The scheme promised easy money, but it turned out to be a scam.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: windfall - an unexpected amount of money received, often easily
  • Antonym: hard-earned money - money obtained through considerable effort or work
