life buoy

1. NOUN

/ laɪf ˈbɔɪ / (AmE), / laɪf ˈbɔɪ / (BrE)
a circular flotation device, typically made of buoyant material, used to rescue people at risk of drowning
ring-shaped flotation rescue device

Examples

The sailor threw a life buoy into the water to help the struggling swimmer.
Each cruise ship must carry enough life buoys for emergencies.
A red and white life buoy hung on the dock, ready for use.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lifesaver - a device or person that saves someone from drowning
  • Synonym: life preserver - a general flotation device for rescuing people in water

2. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪf ˌbu.i, ˈlaɪf ˌbɔɪ / (AmE), / ˈlaɪf ˌbɔɪ / (BrE)
a buoyant ring-shaped device, often made of plastic or cork, used to rescue people at risk of drowning in water
ring-shaped rescue flotation device

Examples

The crew quickly threw a life buoy to the passenger who had fallen overboard.
Regulations require every public swimming pool to have at least one life buoy visible and accessible.
He grabbed onto the life buoy until help arrived.

Synset Examples

He threw a life buoy to the drowning swimmer.
Always have a life buoy ready when out at sea.
The boat was equipped with several life buoys.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lifesaver - a device or person that saves someone from danger, especially from drowning
  • Synonym: life preserver - a device worn to keep a person afloat in water

Etymology

From 'life', referring to saving life, and 'buoy', from Middle Dutch 'boeye' (float).
