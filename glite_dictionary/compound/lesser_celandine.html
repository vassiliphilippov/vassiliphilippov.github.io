lesser celandine

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛsər səˈlændaɪn / (AmE), / ˈlɛsə sɪˈlændiːn / (BrE)
A small perennial herb native to Europe, with heart-shaped leaves and bright yellow flowers resembling buttercups; its tuberous roots have been used medicinally.
herb with yellow buttercup-like flowers

Examples

The woods were carpeted with golden lesser celandine in early spring.
Gardeners sometimes remove lesser celandine because it spreads quickly and can overtake lawns.
Herbalists historically used the roots of lesser celandine to make remedies for piles.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: greater celandine - a related plant with yellow flowers, often larger

Etymology

'Celandine' from Latin 'chelidonia', and 'lesser' denotes its smaller size compared to 'greater celandine'.
