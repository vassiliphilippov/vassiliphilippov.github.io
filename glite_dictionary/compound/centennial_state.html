Centennial State

1. PROPN

/ sɛnˈtɛnɪəl steɪt / (AmE), / sɛnˈtɛniəl steɪt / (BrE)
a nickname for the state of Colorado, United States, referring to its admission to the Union in the centennial year of 1876
nickname for Colorado, U.S.

Examples

Colorado is often called the Centennial State because it became a state in 1876.
Ski resorts in the Centennial State attract tourists from all over the world.
The Centennial State is known for its beautiful Rocky Mountain scenery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Colorado - a state in the western United States; the Centennial State

Etymology

Named because Colorado became a U.S. state in 1876, the centennial year of the Declaration of Independence.
