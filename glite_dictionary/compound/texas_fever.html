Texas fever

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛksəs ˈfiːvər / (AmE), / ˈtɛksəs ˈfiːvə / (BrE)
An infectious disease of cattle, characterized by fever and anemia, caused by protozoan parasites transmitted by the cattle tick.
cattle disease transmitted by tick

Examples

The farmer lost several cows to Texas fever before implementing strict tick control measures.
Veterinarians in the region are on alert for outbreaks of Texas fever during the summer months.
Early detection of Texas fever is crucial to prevent the spread of infection among cattle herds.
