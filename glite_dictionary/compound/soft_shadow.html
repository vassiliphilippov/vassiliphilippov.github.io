soft shadow

1. NOUN

/ sɑft ˈʃædoʊ / (AmE), / sɒft ˈʃadəʊ / (BrE)
a shadow that is gentle and diffused, lacking sharp edges due to dispersed light
gentle, diffused shadow

Examples

The lamp's wide shade created a soft shadow on the wall behind her.
Artists often prefer a soft shadow to produce more realistic and subtle lighting effects.
Cloudy days generally result in a soft shadow rather than harsh, well-defined ones.

Synset Examples

The penumbra cast a soft shadow over the garden.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hard shadow - a shadow with sharply defined edges
