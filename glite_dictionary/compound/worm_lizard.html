worm lizard

1. NOUN

/ wɜrm ˈlɪzərd / (AmE), / wɜːm ˈlɪzəd / (BrE)
A limbless, burrowing reptile of the family Amphisbaenidae, resembling a worm and native to warm or tropical regions.
limbless burrowing reptile resembling a worm

Examples

The worm lizard spends most of its life underground, searching for insects to eat.
Despite its name, the worm lizard is neither a worm nor a true lizard.
Biologists discovered a new species of worm lizard in the rainforests of South America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amphisbaena - a genus of worm lizards
