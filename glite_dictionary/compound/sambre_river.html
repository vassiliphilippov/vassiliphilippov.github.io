Sambre River

1. PROPN

/ ˈsɑːmbər ˈrɪvər / (AmE), / ˈsæmbə ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
a river in western Europe, rising in northern France and flowing east into Belgium, where it joins the Meuse at Namur
river in France and Belgium, tributary of the Meuse

Examples

The Sambre River played a crucial role in the region's industrial development during the nineteenth century.
Several picturesque towns are situated along the banks of the Sambre River.
The confluence of the Sambre River and the Meuse at Namur is a popular tourist attraction.
