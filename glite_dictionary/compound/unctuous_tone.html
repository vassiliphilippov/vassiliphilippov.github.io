unctuous tone

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌŋktʃuəs toʊn / (AmE), / ˈʌŋktjʊəs təʊn / (BrE)
a way of speaking that is excessively flattering, oily, or ingratiating, often making others uncomfortable
excessively flattering or ingratiating manner of speaking

Examples

He annoyed his colleagues with his unctuous tone during every staff meeting.
Her unctuous tone made it hard to tell if she was being genuine or manipulative.
Although he praised her efforts, his unctuous tone suggested insincerity.

Synset Examples

His unctuous tone made her uneasy during the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flattering - giving excessive or insincere praise
  • Antonym: sincere - genuine, not deceitful or hypocritical

Etymology

From Latin 'unctuosus' (oily) with 'tone' referring to a manner of speaking.
