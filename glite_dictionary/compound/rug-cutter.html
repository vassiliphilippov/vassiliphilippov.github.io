rug-cutter

1. NOUN

/ ˈrʌɡ ˌkʌt̬ɚ / (AmE), / ˈrʌɡ ˌkʌtə / (BrE)
a skilled or lively dancer, especially one who excels in energetic styles such as tap or jazz
skilled, energetic dancer
informal

Examples

He always draws a crowd at weddings because he's such a talented rug-cutter.
Back in the jazz age, being called a rug-cutter was a real compliment.
Sarah stunned everyone at the party with her moves—she's a true rug-cutter.

Synset Examples

He's a talented rug-cutter on the dance floor.
They were impressed by his skills as a rug-cutter.
At the party, she turned out to be a real rug-cutter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dancer - a person who dances

Etymology

From the phrase 'cut a rug', slang for dancing energetically, popularized in early 20th-century American jazz culture.
