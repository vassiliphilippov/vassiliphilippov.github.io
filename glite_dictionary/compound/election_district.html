election district

1. NOUN

/ əˈlɛk.ʃən ˈdɪs.trɪkt / (AmE), / ɪˈlɛk.ʃən ˈdɪs.trɪkt / (BrE)
One of several areas into which a city or town is divided for voting purposes, each containing one polling place.
voting area in a city or town

Examples

Voters in each election district must cast their ballots at the designated polling place.
The city council approved new boundaries for the election district to reflect population changes.
He was elected to represent the fourth election district on the county board.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: constituency - a body of voters in a specified area who elect a representative
  • Synonym: precinct - a district of a city or town as defined for police or election purposes
