majority leader

1. NOUN

/ məˈdʒɔrəti ˈliːdər / (AmE), / məˈdʒɒrɪti ˈliːdə / (BrE)
the leader of the political party that has the most members in a legislative body, such as the US Senate or House of Representatives
leader of majority party in a legislature

Examples

The majority leader called the meeting to order and outlined the day's legislative agenda.
After the elections, she was elected as the new majority leader of the state senate.
The majority leader and the minority leader negotiated a compromise on the bill.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: minority leader - the leader of the political party with the second-largest number of seats in a legislature
