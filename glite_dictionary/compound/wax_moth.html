wax moth

1. NOUN

/ wæks mɑθ / (AmE), / wæks mɒθ / (BrE)
A moth whose larvae live in and feed on the honeycombs of beehives, often causing damage to colonies.
moth whose larvae infest beehives

Examples

The beekeeper was concerned about a wax moth infestation in his hives.
If left unchecked, a wax moth can destroy honeycombs and harm bee colonies.
Scientists are studying the lifecycle of the wax moth to develop better pest control methods.
