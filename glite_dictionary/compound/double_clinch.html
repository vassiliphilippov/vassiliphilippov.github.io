double clinch

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʌbəl klɪntʃ / (AmE), / ˈdʌbəl klɪntʃ / (BrE)
a type of clinch knot or fastening that uses two loops for greater security
clinch knot with two loops

Examples

The fisherman demonstrated how to tie a double clinch to secure the hook.
For added strength, use a double clinch instead of a single knot.
Many anglers prefer the double clinch when fishing for larger species.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: clinch - a knot or fastening made by looping and tightening line or wire
