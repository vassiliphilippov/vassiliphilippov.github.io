entrenching tool

1. NOUN

/ ɛnˈtrɛn.tʃɪŋ tuːl / (AmE), / ɪnˈtrɛn.tʃɪŋ tuːl / (BrE)
A small folding spade or shovel carried by soldiers for digging trenches or defensive positions.
small military shovel for digging

Examples

Every soldier was issued an entrenching tool to dig foxholes for cover.
The entrenching tool folds up and fits easily into a backpack.
During training, we learned how to use the entrenching tool for both digging and self-defense.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shovel - a tool for lifting and moving material like soil or snow
  • Synonym: spade - a digging tool with a flat blade; sometimes used for the same purpose as an entrenching tool

Etymology

From 'entrench' (to dig a trench) + 'tool'.
