politically correct

1. ADJ

/ pəˌlɪtɪkli kəˈrɛkt / (AmE), / pəˌlɪtɪkli kəˈrɛkt / (BrE)
avoiding language or behavior that could offend particular groups of people, especially based on race, gender, religion, or sexual orientation
avoiding potentially offensive language or behavior

Examples

Some people feel that using politically correct language is essential to show respect to others.
The company updated its policies to be more politically correct and inclusive.
He was criticized for not being politically correct in his public statements.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: politically incorrect - not avoiding language or actions likely to offend particular groups

2. ADJ

/ pəˌlɪt̬ɪkli kəˈrɛkt / (AmE), / pəˌlɪtɪkli kəˈrɛkt / (BrE)
avoiding language or behavior that may offend or exclude particular groups of people, especially those who have been discriminated against
avoiding potentially offensive language or behavior

Examples

It is important to be politically correct when addressing a diverse group of people.
Some critics argue that being too politically correct can stifle honest conversation.
He always tries to use politically correct language in public speeches.

Synset Examples

Being politically correct is essential in today's society.
His remarks were not seen as politically correct by many.
She made a politically correct statement to avoid backlash.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: politically incorrect - not avoiding expressions or actions that might offend; insensitive to issues of discrimination or exclusion
