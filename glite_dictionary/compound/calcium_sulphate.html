calcium sulphate

1. NOUN

/ ˈkælsiəm ˈsʌlˌfeɪt / (AmE), / ˈkalsɪəm ˈsʌlfeɪt / (BrE)
a white, inorganic salt used in making plaster, cement, and as a food additive
white salt used in plaster and cement

Examples

Plaster of Paris is derived from calcium sulphate and is often used in construction.
The laboratory ordered more calcium sulphate for chemical experiments.
Food manufacturers sometimes add calcium sulphate as a firming agent.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plaster of Paris - a form of calcium sulphate used for casts and molds

Etymology

From calcium (element name) + sulphate, from Latin 'sulfur' and 'calx' (lime).
