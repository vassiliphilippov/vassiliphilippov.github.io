subfamily Nelumbonaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌsʌbˌfæməli nɪˌlʌmbəˈneɪsiː / (AmE), / ˌsʌbˌfæmɪli nɛˌlʌmbəˈniːsiː / (BrE)
A botanical taxonomic subfamily that in some classifications is considered an independent family of water lilies, comprising the single genus Nelumbo.
botanical subfamily of water lilies (Nelumbo)

Examples

The subfamily Nelumbonaceae contains only the genus Nelumbo, best known for the lotus flower.
Taxonomists sometimes elevate the subfamily Nelumbonaceae to family rank, separating it from other water lilies.
Characteristics of the subfamily Nelumbonaceae include large, showy aquatic flowers and peltate leaves.

Etymology

From Latin 'subfamilia' (subfamily) and 'Nelumbonaceae' (from genus 'Nelumbo' + '-aceae' family suffix).
