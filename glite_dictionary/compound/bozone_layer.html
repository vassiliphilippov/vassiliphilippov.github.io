bozone layer

1. NOUN

/ ˈboʊˌzoʊn ˌleɪ.ər / (AmE), / ˈbəʊzəʊn ˌleɪ.ə / (BrE)
A humorous, fictitious layer in the Earth's atmosphere said to absorb ozone, often referenced jokingly in discussions about environmental protection or human ignorance.
humorous fictitious atmospheric layer
informal slang

Examples

Many jokes about climate change reference the bozone layer to highlight ignorance.
Satirical articles describe the bozone layer as growing thicker every year.
The term bozone layer is often used to mock misguided environmental policies.

Synset Examples

The depletion of the bozone layer is a serious issue.
Efforts to restore the bozone layer are ongoing.
The bozone layer protects us from harmful UV rays.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ozone layer - the layer of ozone in Earth's atmosphere absorbing UV radiation

Etymology

Blend of 'bozo' (slang for a foolish person) and 'ozone layer.'
