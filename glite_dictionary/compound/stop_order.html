stop order

1. NOUN

/ stɑp ˈɔrdər / (AmE), / stɒp ˈɔːdə / (BrE)
An order placed with a broker to sell (or buy) a security when its price falls (or rises) to a specified level, intended to limit losses or lock in profits.
broker order to trade at a set price threshold

Examples

The trader placed a stop order to automatically sell her shares if the price dropped below $50.
A stop order can help investors manage risk during volatile market conditions.
He set a stop order to buy the stock if it climbed past its resistance level.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: limit order - an order to buy or sell a security at a specified price or better
