to make or break

1. VERB

/ meɪk ɔr breɪk / (AmE), / meɪk ɔː breɪk / (BrE)
to determine whether someone or something succeeds or fails completely
determine success or failure

Examples

This decision could make or break his career, so he considered it very carefully.
A strong debut can make or break a new product on the market.
Excellent teamwork will make or break our chances of winning the championship.

Synset Examples

Customer service can make or break a business.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to determine - to decide or influence the outcome of something
  • Antonym: ruin - to spoil or destroy completely, especially causing failure
