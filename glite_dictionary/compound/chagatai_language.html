Chagatai language

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɑːɡətaɪ ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈtʃɑːɡətaɪ ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
a Turkic literary language once widely used in Central Asia, particularly from the 15th to early 20th centuries
historical Turkic literary language

Examples

Many important works of Central Asian literature were written in Chagatai language.
The Chagatai language served as a lingua franca among diverse communities in medieval Uzbekistan.
Scholars study the Chagatai language to better understand Turkic literary heritage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Turkic language - a language belonging to the Turkic family

Etymology

Named after Chagatai, the second son of Genghis Khan.
