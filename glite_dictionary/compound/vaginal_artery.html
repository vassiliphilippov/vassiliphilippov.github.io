vaginal artery

1. NOUN

/ vəˈdʒaɪ.nəl ˈɑr.t̬ə.ri / (AmE), / vəˈdʒaɪ.nəl ˈɑː.tə.ri / (BrE)
an artery branching from the internal iliac artery that supplies blood to the vagina, base of the bladder, and rectum
artery supplying the vagina and surrounding organs
medical

Examples

The vaginal artery is crucial in supplying blood to the lower female reproductive organs.
During surgery, care must be taken to avoid damaging the vaginal artery.
The anatomy class included a detailed diagram of the vaginal artery and its branches.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: internal iliac artery - major artery in the pelvis from which the vaginal artery branches
  • Synonym: uterine artery - artery supplying blood to the uterus, related to the vaginal artery

Etymology

From Latin 'vagina' (sheath, vagina) and 'arteria' (artery).
