gorgonian coral

1. NOUN

/ ɡɔrˈɡoʊniən ˈkɔrəl / (AmE), / ɡɔːˈɡəʊniən ˈkɒrəl / (BrE)
A type of coral with a horny or calcareous branching skeleton, commonly found in warm seas and known for its tree-like forms.
coral with branching skeleton

Examples

Divers were amazed by the colorful gorgonian coral spread across the reef walls.
The branches of the gorgonian coral provide shelter for many small marine creatures.
Conservation efforts focus on protecting gorgonian coral habitats from destructive fishing methods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sea fan - a common name for certain species of gorgonian coral with fan-shaped colonies

Etymology

From 'Gorgonia', the genus name, which itself derives from the Gorgons of Greek mythology, referencing the appearance of the coral.
