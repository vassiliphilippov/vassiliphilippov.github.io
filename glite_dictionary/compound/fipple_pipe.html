fipple pipe

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪpəl paɪp / (AmE), / ˈfɪpəl paɪp / (BrE)
a tubular wind instrument, typically with eight finger holes and a fipple mouthpiece, such as a recorder or tin whistle
tubular wind instrument with fipple mouthpiece

Examples

The medieval musician played a fipple pipe during the court festivities.
Children often learn music by starting on a simple fipple pipe like the recorder.
She collects antique fipple pipes from different European countries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recorder - woodwind instrument with a fipple mouthpiece
  • Synonym: tin whistle - small fipple flute used in folk music

Etymology

From 'fipple' (a type of mouthpiece) + 'pipe' (wind instrument).
