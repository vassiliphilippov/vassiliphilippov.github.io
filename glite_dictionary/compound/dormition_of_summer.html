dormition of summer

1. NOUN

/ dɔrˈmɪʃən əv ˈsʌmər / (AmE), / dɔːˈmɪʃən əv ˈsʌmə / (BrE)
the decline or end of the summer season
end or decline of summer

Examples

With the dormition of summer, the evenings grew cooler and the days shortened.
Many festivals mark the dormition of summer with celebrations and farewell parties.
As the dormition of summer approached, she began preparing for the start of a new school year.

Synset Examples

The dormition of summer means school is starting.

Etymology

Dormition from Latin 'dormitio' (sleeping, falling asleep), used here figuratively to describe summer's ending.
