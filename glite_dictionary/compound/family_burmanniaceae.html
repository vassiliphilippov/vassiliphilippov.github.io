family Burmanniaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌbɝːməˌneɪˈeɪ.siˌiː / (AmE), / ˌbɜːmənɪˈeɪsiːˌiː / (BrE)
A family of chiefly tropical herbs with basal leaves resembling bracts and small flowers, classified in botanical taxonomy.
tropical herb family with small flowers

Examples

Many species in the family Burmanniaceae are adapted to tropical rainforest environments.
Botanists study the family Burmanniaceae for its unusual floral structures.
The family Burmanniaceae is noted for plants with basal leaves and small, pale flowers.

Etymology

From New Latin, named after the type genus *Burmannia*.
