rheumatism weed

1. NOUN

/ ˈruːmətɪzəm wiːd / (AmE), / ˈruːmətɪzəm wiːd / (BrE)
A North American perennial plant with pinkish flowers, traditionally used in folk medicine to treat joint pain or inflammation.
plant used in folk medicine for joint pain

Examples

Many herbalists believe that rheumatism weed can help reduce inflammation in sore joints.
The pinkish flowers of rheumatism weed are a common sight in summer meadows across North America.
She brewed a tea from rheumatism weed to ease her arthritis symptoms.

Etymology

Named for its traditional use in treating rheumatism (from Greek 'rheuma', meaning flow or stream, a reference to bodily humors).

2. NOUN

/ ˈruːməˌtɪzəm wiːd / (AmE), / ˈruːmətɪzəm wiːd / (BrE)
A North American plant (Canadian dogbane) known for its tough fibers used by Native Americans for making cordage and its use in folk medicine to treat joint pain or inflammation.
North American plant used for fiber and folk medicine

Examples

Some herbalists recommend rheumatism weed for relief of joint pain or swelling.
The tough fibers from rheumatism weed were once used to make strong cords and ropes.
Native American communities traditionally harvested rheumatism weed for both medicinal and practical purposes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dogbane - A plant whose fibers are used like those of rheumatism weed

Etymology

From rheumatism (a condition involving joint pain) and weed, named for its perceived medicinal use against rheumatic pain.
