divided highway

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈvaɪdɪd ˈhaɪweɪ / (AmE), / dɪˈvaɪdɪd ˈhaɪweɪ / (BrE)
A major road or highway that has a central barrier or strip separating traffic moving in opposite directions.
road with median separating directions
american

Examples

The speed limit is higher on a divided highway than on regular city streets.
After the intersection, the road widens into a divided highway with three lanes in each direction.
Drivers feel safer on a divided highway because the traffic is separated by a barrier.

Synset Examples

in Britain they call a divided highway a ‘dual carriageway’

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dual carriageway - a road with a central reservation separating traffic moving in opposite directions (British term)
