parrot disease

1. NOUN

/ ˈpærət dɪˌziz / (AmE), / ˈpærət dɪˌziːz / (BrE)
an infectious disease of birds, especially parrots, caused by the bacterium Chlamydia psittaci and potentially transmissible to humans; also known as psittacosis
infectious disease of birds, esp. parrots
medical

Examples

Parrots in the aviary were quarantined after several showed signs of parrot disease.
Humans can contract parrot disease if they are exposed to infected birds.
Veterinarians recommend regular check-ups to help prevent outbreaks of parrot disease among pet birds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: psittacosis - infectious disease transmitted from birds, especially parrots, to humans
