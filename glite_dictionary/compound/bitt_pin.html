bitt pin

1. NOUN

/ bɪt pɪn / (AmE), / bɪt pɪn / (BrE)
A horizontal pin inserted through the head of a ship's bitt to prevent mooring lines from slipping off.
nautical pin to secure mooring lines

Examples

The deckhand inserted a bitt pin to secure the mooring rope in place.
Without the bitt pin, the heavy line could easily slip off the bitt during rough weather.
After tying up at the dock, the crew checked that every bitt pin was properly fastened.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bitt - a strong post on a ship for fastening cables

Etymology

From 'bitt' (a post on the deck of a ship for fastening cables) and 'pin' (a peg or bolt).
