arctic willow

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːrktɪk ˈwɪloʊ / (AmE), / ˈɑːktɪk ˈwɪləʊ / (BrE)
a low, creeping shrub (Salix arctica) native to Arctic regions of Europe and North America
low Arctic shrub (Salix arctica)

Examples

The arctic willow is adapted to survive in harsh, cold environments above the tree line.
During their expedition, they documented the growth patterns of arctic willow across the tundra.
Many Arctic animals rely on the arctic willow as a food source during the summer months.
