two-body problem

1. NOUN

/ ˈtuˌbɑdi ˈprɑbləm / (AmE), / ˈtuːˌbɒdi ˈprɒbləm / (BrE)
a problem in physics or mathematics involving the prediction of the motion of two interacting bodies influenced by their mutual forces, such as gravity
problem involving two interacting bodies

Examples

Solving the two-body problem is a fundamental step in understanding planetary orbits.
In classical mechanics, the two-body problem can be solved exactly using differential equations.
The two-body problem serves as a basis for more complex n-body simulations in astrophysics.

Synset Examples
