normal curve

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɔrməl ˌkɝv / (AmE), / ˈnɔːməl ˌkɜːv / (BrE)
A symmetrical, bell-shaped curve representing the distribution of values, frequencies, or probabilities according to the normal distribution.
bell-shaped curve for normal distribution

Examples

The heights of students in a large class often form a normal curve when plotted on a graph.
Scientists use the normal curve to analyze data that follow a predictable pattern.
Deviations far from the mean are rare on a normal curve.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bell curve - A graphical representation of a normal distribution, named for its bell shape

Etymology

From 'normal' (standard, usual) and 'curve', referring to the curve describing the normal (Gaussian) distribution in statistics.
