sericea lespedeza

1. NOUN

/ səˈriːsiə lɛˌspiːˈdiːzə / (AmE), / səˈriːsiə lɛˌspiːˈdiːzə / (BrE)
a perennial plant (Lespedeza cuneata) widely grown for forage and as a hay crop, especially on poor or infertile land
perennial forage and hay plant

Examples

Farmers often plant sericea lespedeza to improve degraded pastures and provide nutritious feed for livestock.
The drought-resistant qualities of sericea lespedeza make it valuable for hay production on poor soils.
Many conservation programs recommend sericea lespedeza for erosion control and soil enrichment.

Etymology

From scientific Latin: 'sericea' (silky) and 'Lespedeza' (plant genus name, possibly honoring Vicente Manuel de Céspedes).
