teiid lizard

1. NOUN

/ ˈtiː.ɪd ˈlɪz.ərd / (AmE), / ˈtiː.ɪd ˈlɪz.əd / (BrE)
A tropical lizard native to the New World, characterized by a long tail and large rectangular scales on its belly.
tropical New World lizard with distinct belly scales

Examples

The rainforest was home to a colorful teiid lizard basking on a fallen log.
Scientists studied the diet of the teiid lizard in its native habitat.
She photographed several teiid lizards during her trip to Central America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whiptail lizard - a common member of the Teiidae family

Etymology

From Latin Teiidae, the lizard family name.
