Canary Island hare's foot fern

1. NOUN

/ kəˈnɛri ˈaɪlənd ˌhɛrz fʊt fɜrn / (AmE), / kəˈnæri ˈaɪlənd ˌheəz fʊt fɜːn / (BrE)
a species of fern native to the Canary Islands and Madeira, known for its soft, hairy rhizomes resembling a hare's foot
fern species from Canary Islands and Madeira

Examples

The Canary Island hare's foot fern is prized for its decorative foliage in botanical gardens.
Gardeners often grow the Canary Island hare's foot fern indoors because of its unique hairy stems.
The climate of Madeira is ideal for cultivating the Canary Island hare's foot fern.
