white water

1. NOUN

/ ˈwaɪt ˌwɔtɚ / (AmE), / ˈwaɪt ˌwɔːtə / (BrE)
Frothy, turbulent water found in rapids or at the base of waterfalls, typically characterized by its white appearance due to air bubbles.
frothy, turbulent water in rapids or waterfalls

Examples

Experienced kayakers often seek out white water rivers for a challenging adventure.
We could hear the roar of white water as we approached the base of the waterfall.
The guide warned us about the dangers of navigating through white water without proper equipment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rapids - fast-flowing section of a river with turbulent water

2. NOUN

/ ˈwaɪt ˌwɔt̬ɚ / (AmE), / ˈwaɪt ˌwɔːtə / (BrE)
fast-moving, turbulent water on the surface of a river or stream, often appearing white because of air bubbles and foam
turbulent, fast-flowing river water

Examples

The raft overturned in the white water, but everyone managed to reach the shore safely.
Tourists from around the world come here for white water rafting adventures.
After heavy rain, the river was filled with dangerous white water sections.

Synset Examples

He loved the thrill of kayaking in the white water.

Related Terms
