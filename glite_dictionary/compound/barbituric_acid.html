barbituric acid

1. NOUN

/ ˌbɑːrbɪˈtjʊrɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / ˌbɑːbɪˈtjʊərɪk ˈæsɪd / (BrE)
a white crystalline organic acid, derived from pyrimidine, used in the manufacture of barbiturate drugs
organic acid used to make barbiturates
medical

Examples

Barbituric acid is not itself a sedative, but it is the parent compound of barbiturates.
The laboratory synthesized barbituric acid as a precursor to other pharmaceutical agents.
Chemists study barbituric acid to understand the structure of related drugs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barbiturate - any of a group of drugs derived from barbituric acid

Etymology

From 'barbital' and '-uric', related to uric acid.
