order Hyracoidea

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdɚ haɪˌrækoʊˈaɪdiə / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə hʌɪˌrækəʊˈɪdɪə / (BrE)
a taxonomic group of mammals that includes hyraxes and some extinct related animals
taxonomic group including hyraxes

Examples

Scientists have classified all living hyraxes within the order Hyracoidea.
The fossil record of the order Hyracoidea suggests a much wider historical distribution.
Members of the order Hyracoidea are small, herbivorous mammals found primarily in Africa.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hyrax - small herbivorous mammal of Africa and the Middle East

Etymology

From New Latin 'Hyracoidea', from 'Hyrax' (the type genus) + '-oidea' (indicating a higher taxonomic group).
