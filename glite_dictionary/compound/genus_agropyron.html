genus Agropyron

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌæɡ.rəˈpaɪ.rɑn / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌæɡ.rəˈpaɪ.rɒn / (BrE)
A genus of perennial grasses found in temperate and cool regions, commonly known as wheatgrass or dog grass.
genus of perennial grasses (wheatgrass)

Examples

The genus Agropyron contains several species important for grazing animals.
Researchers studied the drought resistance of grasses in the genus Agropyron.
Farmers often plant members of the genus Agropyron to prevent soil erosion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wheatgrass - common name for grasses in the genus Agropyron

Etymology

From Greek 'agros' (field) and 'pyros' (wheat).
