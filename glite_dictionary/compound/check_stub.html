check stub

1. NOUN

/ tʃɛk stʌb / (AmE), / tʃɛk stʌb / (BrE)
the part of a check that is kept as a record of the transaction after the check is detached and given to the payee
part of a check kept for records

Examples

Please keep the check stub for your records in case you need to verify the payment later.
He tore off the check stub before handing the check to the landlord.
The accountant carefully filed each check stub at the end of the month.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pay stub - the part of a paycheck that lists details of payment and deductions
