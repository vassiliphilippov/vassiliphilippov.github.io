double stopping

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʌbəl ˌstɑpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdʌb(ə)l ˌstɒpɪŋ / (BrE)
the technique of playing two notes simultaneously on a stringed instrument by stopping two strings at once
playing two notes at once on a string instrument

Examples

The violinist used double stopping to produce rich, full harmonies during the performance.
Practicing double stopping can be challenging for beginner string players.
Cellists often employ double stopping in classical and contemporary music pieces.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: harmonics - technique of producing overtone pitches on string instruments
