truncation error

1. NOUN

/ trʌŋˈkeɪʃən ˈɛrɚ / (AmE), / trʌŋˈkeɪʃən ˈɛrə / (BrE)
an error that occurs when a numerical calculation is stopped before it is fully complete, usually by omitting digits or terms.
error from stopping a calculation early

Examples

When using limited decimal places in calculations, a truncation error can affect the final result.
The truncation error becomes significant when many terms are omitted in the series expansion.
Engineers must estimate the truncation error to ensure the model's accuracy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rounding error - an error caused by approximating a number by another number with fewer digits

Etymology

From Latin truncare (to lop off, cut short), related to 'truncate'.
