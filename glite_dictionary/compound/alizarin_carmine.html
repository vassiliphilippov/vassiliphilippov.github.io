alizarin carmine

1. NOUN

/ əˈlɪzərɪn ˈkɑrmiːn / (AmE), / əˈlɪzərɪn ˈkɑːmiːn / (BrE)
a red acid dye obtained from alizarin, used especially for dyeing wool a scarlet red
a red dye used in textiles

Examples

The wool was dyed a brilliant shade using alizarin carmine.
Researchers stained the specimen with alizarin carmine to observe bone growth.
In the 19th century, alizarin carmine became popular for producing vibrant reds in fabrics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alizarin - an orange-red dye originally made from madder root

Etymology

From 'alizarin', a dye derived from madder, and 'carmine', a deep red pigment.
