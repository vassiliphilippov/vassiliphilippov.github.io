antimony potassium tartrate

1. NOUN

/ ænˈtɪmoʊni pəˈteɪsiəm tɑrˌtreɪt / (AmE), / ænˈtɪməni pəˈtæsiəm tɑːˌtreɪt / (BrE)
a poisonous, colorless salt used as a mordant in dyeing and formerly in medicine as an emetic
poisonous salt used in dyeing and medicine
medical

Examples

Antimony potassium tartrate was once used to treat certain parasitic diseases, though its toxicity is now well recognized.
The dyeing process requires a mordant, and antimony potassium tartrate is sometimes employed for this purpose.
Exposure to antimony potassium tartrate must be carefully controlled due to its poisonous nature.

Etymology

From antimony (element name), potassium (element name), and tartrate (from French 'tartrate', salt of tartaric acid).
