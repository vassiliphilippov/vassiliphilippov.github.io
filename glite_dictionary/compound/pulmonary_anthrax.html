pulmonary anthrax

1. NOUN

/ ˈpʊlməˌnɛri ˈænθræks / (AmE), / ˈpʌlmənəri ˈænθræks / (BrE)
A type of anthrax infection caused by inhaling spores of Bacillus anthracis, characterized by severe respiratory symptoms and often rapid progression.
anthrax infection via inhalation; severe respiratory disease
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with pulmonary anthrax after showing severe breathing difficulties.
Bioterrorism experts monitor for signs of pulmonary anthrax outbreaks in major cities.
Prompt treatment is critical for anyone exposed to pulmonary anthrax spores through inhalation.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cutaneous anthrax - anthrax infection of the skin
  • Antonym: gastrointestinal anthrax - anthrax infection ingested through contaminated food
  • Synonym: inhalation anthrax - another term for pulmonary anthrax; anthrax from inhalation

Etymology

From Latin 'pulmonarius' (of the lungs) and Greek 'anthrax' (coal, carbuncle, or anthrax disease).
