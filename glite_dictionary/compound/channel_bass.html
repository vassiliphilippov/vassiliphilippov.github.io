channel bass

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃænəl bæs / (AmE), / ˈtʃænəl bæs / (BrE)
a large edible fish (also known as red drum or redfish) found off the coast of the United States from Massachusetts to Mexico
large edible North American fish (red drum)

Examples

The chef prepared grilled channel bass with a lemon-butter sauce for tonight's special.
Fishermen along the Gulf Coast often hope to catch a trophy-sized channel bass.
The channel bass is prized both for its fighting ability and its delicate flavor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: red drum - common alternate name for channel bass, a large North American fish
  • Synonym: redfish - another common name for channel bass, a large Atlantic fish
