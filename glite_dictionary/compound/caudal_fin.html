caudal fin

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔdəl fɪn / (AmE), / ˈkɔːdəl fɪn / (BrE)
the tail fin of fishes and some other aquatic vertebrates, used for propulsion and steering
tail fin of a fish or aquatic animal

Examples

The shark's caudal fin provides the power needed for fast swimming.
Damage to the caudal fin can affect a fish's ability to steer and accelerate.
Scientists observed how the caudal fin shape varies between different fish species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tail fin - the terminal fin of a fish, used for propulsion

Etymology

From Latin 'cauda' (tail) and English 'fin'.
