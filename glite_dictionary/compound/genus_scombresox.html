genus Scombresox

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs skɒmˈbrɛsɒks / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs skɒmˈbrɛsɒks / (BrE)
A genus of fish in the family Scomberesocidae, which includes sauries distributed in temperate seas.
a fish genus in Scomberesocidae

Examples

The genus Scombresox includes several species of sauries found in the Pacific Ocean.
Researchers have studied the migration patterns of fish in the genus Scombresox.
Members of the genus Scombresox are known for their slender bodies and elongated jaws.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'Scomber' (mackerel) and Greek 'ēxō' (to have), referring to mackerel-like fishes.
