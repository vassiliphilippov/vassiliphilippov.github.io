pocket rat

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑkɪt ræt / (AmE), / ˈpɒkɪt ræt / (BrE)
any of several rodents that have fur-lined cheek pouches used for carrying food or nesting material, such as gophers and kangaroo rats.
rodent with cheek pouches

Examples

The pocket rat gathers seeds and stores them in its cheek pouches before returning to its burrow.
Some farmers consider the pocket rat a pest because it digs extensive tunnels in their fields.
The biologist examined the teeth and pouches of the captured pocket rat for her research.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gopher - a burrowing rodent of North America with fur-lined cheek pouches
  • Synonym: kangaroo rat - a North American rodent with large hind legs and cheek pouches
