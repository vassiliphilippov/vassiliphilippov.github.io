hostile fire

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑːstaɪl ˈfaɪər / (AmE), / ˈhɒstaɪl ˈfaɪə / (BrE)
gunfire or attack directed at someone by an enemy, especially in a military context
gunfire or attack from an enemy

Examples

Several soldiers were wounded by hostile fire during the operation.
The helicopter came under hostile fire as it attempted to land.
Medals were awarded to those who braved hostile fire to rescue their comrades.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: friendly fire - gunfire mistakenly directed at one's own forces rather than the enemy
