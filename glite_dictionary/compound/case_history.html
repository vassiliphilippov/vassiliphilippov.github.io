case history

1. NOUN

/ keɪs ˈhɪstəri / (AmE), / keɪs ˈhɪstəri / (BrE)
A detailed written record of the background, medical or psychological history, and circumstances of a person or group under study or treatment.
detailed record of a person's background or treatment

Examples

The psychiatrist reviewed the patient's case history before beginning the assessment.
Her case history revealed a series of previous treatments and hospital stays.
Researchers compiled a detailed case history of the community affected by the outbreak.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: medical history - an account of a person's past medical problems and treatments
