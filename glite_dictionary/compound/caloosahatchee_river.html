Caloosahatchee River

1. PROPN

/ kəˌluːsəˈhætʃi ˈrɪvər / (AmE), / kəˌluːsəˈhætʃi ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
a river in southern Florida that flows west to the Gulf of Mexico, forming the western end of the Cross-Florida Waterway
river in southern Florida, USA

Examples

The Caloosahatchee River provides important habitat for manatees and other wildlife in Florida.
They sailed their boat up the Caloosahatchee River towards the Gulf of Mexico.
Heavy rains caused flooding along the banks of the Caloosahatchee River last summer.

Etymology

Named after the Calusa, a Native American people, and 'hatchee', from a Creek word meaning 'river'.
