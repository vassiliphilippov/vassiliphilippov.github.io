constant quantity

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑnstənt ˈkwɑntəti / (AmE), / ˈkɒnstənt ˈkwɒntəti / (BrE)
a quantity whose value does not change.
quantity that does not change

Examples

The acceleration due to gravity is often treated as a constant quantity in basic physics problems.
In this equation, the coefficient remains a constant quantity throughout the experiment.
Temperature is not always a constant quantity and can change with the environment.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: variable quantity - a quantity that can change in value
