Queckenstedt's test

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɛkənˌʃtɛdz tɛst / (AmE), / ˈkwɛkənʃtɛts tɛst / (BrE)
a medical procedure used to detect blockages in the spinal canal by observing changes in cerebrospinal fluid pressure during jugular vein compression
medical test for spinal canal blockage
medical

Examples

The neurologist performed a Queckenstedt's test to check for signs of spinal cord compression.
A positive Queckenstedt's test suggests a blockage in the spinal canal.
Queckenstedt's test is now rarely used, but it was once common in neurological assessments.

Etymology

Named after German neurologist Hans Queckenstedt (1876–1918), who developed the test.
