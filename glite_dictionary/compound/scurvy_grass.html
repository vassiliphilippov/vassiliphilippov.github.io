scurvy grass

1. NOUN

/ ˈskɝːvi ɡræs / (AmE), / ˈskɜːvi ɡrɑːs / (BrE)
a small, herbaceous plant of the cress family, found in northern regions, formerly valued for its high vitamin C content in preventing or treating scurvy
a vitamin C-rich Arctic cress plant

Examples

Sailors once relied on scurvy grass to fend off vitamin C deficiency during long sea voyages.
I found scurvy grass growing near the rocky coastline on our Arctic expedition.
Botanists study scurvy grass for its historical significance in preventing scurvy.

Etymology

Named for its historical use as a remedy for scurvy (from Latin scorbutus), combined with 'grass' as a general term for herbaceous plants.

2. NOUN

/ ˈskɝːvi ɡræs / (AmE), / ˈskɜːvi ɡrɑːs / (BrE)
a plant of the genus Barbarea, especially Barbarea verna, with yellow flowers, cultivated as a winter salad and sometimes used for its high vitamin C content
a yellow-flowered edible cress

Examples

Sailors once ate scurvy grass to ward off vitamin C deficiency on long voyages.
Fresh scurvy grass can add a peppery flavor to winter salads.
Many herbalists recommend scurvy grass for its high vitamin C content.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cress - a leafy, edible plant often used in salads

Etymology

From association with its use in preventing scurvy and the English word 'grass', although it is not a true grass.
