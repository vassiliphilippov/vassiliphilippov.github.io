thrombocytopenic purpura

1. NOUN

/ ˌθrɑːm.boʊ.saɪ.təˌpin.ɪk pɚˈpjʊr.ə / (AmE), / ˌθrɒm.bə.saɪ.təˌpiː.nɪk pəˈpjʊərə / (BrE)
A medical condition characterized by purple discolorations on the skin resulting from a decrease in blood platelets.
medical condition with purple skin spots from low platelets
medical

Examples

The child was diagnosed with thrombocytopenic purpura after developing unexplained bruises and red spots.
Treatment for thrombocytopenic purpura may involve corticosteroids to raise platelet levels.
In rare cases, thrombocytopenic purpura can lead to internal bleeding if not properly managed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: purpura - purple spots on the skin caused by small bleeding vessels

Etymology

From Greek 'thrombos' (clot), 'kytos' (cell), '-penia' (deficiency), and Latin 'purpura' (purple).
