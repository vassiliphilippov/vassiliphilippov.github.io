internal ear

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtɝːnəl ɪr / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɜːnəl ɪə / (BrE)
the innermost part of the ear, consisting of a complex system of cavities and passages that are involved in hearing and maintaining balance
innermost part of the ear for hearing and balance
medical

Examples

Problems with the internal ear can cause dizziness and loss of balance.
The doctor explained how the internal ear transmits sound signals to the brain.
Damage to the internal ear may result from infection or injury.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inner ear - the innermost part of the ear responsible for hearing and balance
