relative velocity

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛlətɪv vəˈlɑsəti / (AmE), / ˈrɛlətɪv vɪˈlɒsɪti / (BrE)
the velocity of an object as measured in relation to another object, calculated as the vector difference between their velocities
velocity of one body with respect to another

Examples

The relative velocity between two cars determines how quickly they approach each other.
Engineers must consider the relative velocity of fluids when designing turbines.
In physics problems, relative velocity helps solve questions involving moving trains or airplanes.

Synset Examples

The motion may have a different appearance as viewed from a different reference frame, but this can be explained by including the relative velocity of the reference frame in the description of the motion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: velocity - the speed and direction of a moving object

Etymology

From 'relative', meaning 'considered in relation to something else', and 'velocity', from Latin 'velocitas' (swiftness, speed).
