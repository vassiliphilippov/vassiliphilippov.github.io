pontoon plane

1. NOUN

/ pɑnˈtun pleɪn / (AmE), / pɒnˈtuːn pleɪn / (BrE)
a seaplane fitted with floats (pontoons) that allow it to land on and take off from water
seaplane with pontoons for water landings

Examples

The scientist arrived on a remote island by pontoon plane early that morning.
We saw a pontoon plane glide smoothly onto the surface of the lake.
After refueling, the pontoon plane took off from the river for its next mission.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: floatplane - a seaplane with floats instead of a hull
