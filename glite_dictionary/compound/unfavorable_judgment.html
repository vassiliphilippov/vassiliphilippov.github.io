unfavorable judgment

1. NOUN

/ ʌnˈfeɪvərəbəl ˈdʒʌdʒmənt / (AmE), / ʌnˈfeɪvərəbl ˈdʒʌdʒmənt / (BrE)
an opinion or decision expressing disapproval by highlighting faults or shortcomings
disapproval expressed by pointing out faults

Examples

The proposal was met with an unfavorable judgment from the board members.
Receiving an unfavorable judgment can be difficult, but it often helps us improve.
Her performance received an unfavorable judgment from the critics.

Synset Examples

the senator received severe criticism from his opponent

Related Terms

  • Synonym: criticism - the act of expressing disapproval and noting the problems or faults
