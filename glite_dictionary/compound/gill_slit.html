gill slit

1. NOUN

/ ɡɪl slɪt / (AmE), / ɡɪl slɪt / (BrE)
one of the paired openings in the pharynx of fishes and some amphibians, through which water passes and gas exchange occurs
opening in fish and amphibian pharynx for water passage
medical

Examples

The shark breathes by passing water over its gill slits.
Most fish have multiple gill slits along the sides of their heads.
The scientist examined the gill slit structure to determine the species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gill arch - bony or cartilaginous structure supporting the gills
