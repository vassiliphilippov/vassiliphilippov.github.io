Taber ice

1. NOUN

/ ˈteɪ.bɚ aɪs / (AmE), / ˈteɪ.bə aɪs / (BrE)
Thin layers or formations of ice developing in permafrost, typically 0.04 to 6 inches (1 to 150 millimeters) thick, formed by accumulation of water as the ground freezes.
thin ice formations in permafrost

Examples

Researchers discovered a layer of Taber ice beneath the frozen tundra.
The presence of Taber ice affects the stability of permafrost soils.
Scientists analyzed the thickness and distribution of Taber ice in subarctic regions.

Etymology

Named after geologist Morris R. Taber, who studied permafrost ice.
