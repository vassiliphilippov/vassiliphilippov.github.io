social hangover

1. NOUN

/ ˈsoʊʃəl ˈhæŋˌoʊvɚ / (AmE), / ˈsəʊʃəl ˈhæŋˌəʊvə / (BrE)
A feeling of emotional fatigue, discomfort, or exhaustion following extended or intense social interactions.
emotional fatigue after socializing
informal

Examples

After a long evening with friends, he woke up with a social hangover the next day.
She often experiences a social hangover after attending large parties or family gatherings.
Many introverts know the feeling of a social hangover all too well after a busy weekend.

Synset Examples

The weekend's drama left her with a social hangover.
He experienced a social hangover after the heated debate.
After the party, I felt the effects of a social hangover.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hangover - unpleasant aftereffects from excessive drinking
  • Synonym: social fatigue - tiredness or exhaustion from social interaction

Etymology

Formed by analogy with 'hangover' (after-effect of excess), applied to social experience.
