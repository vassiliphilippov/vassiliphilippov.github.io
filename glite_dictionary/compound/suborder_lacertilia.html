suborder Lacertilia

1. NOUN

/ sʌbˌɔrdər ləˌsɜrˈtɪliə / (AmE), / sʌbˌɔːdə ləˌsɜːˈtɪliə / (BrE)
A taxonomic suborder of reptiles comprising the true lizards, including chameleons and geckos.
taxonomic group containing true lizards

Examples

The suborder Lacertilia encompasses a wide variety of reptiles, from small geckos to large monitor lizards.
Chameleons, as members of the suborder Lacertilia, exhibit remarkable color-changing abilities.
Taxonomists continue to refine the classification within the suborder Lacertilia as new discoveries are made.

Etymology

From Latin 'lacerta' (lizard), with 'Lacertilia' as the scientific name for the suborder.
