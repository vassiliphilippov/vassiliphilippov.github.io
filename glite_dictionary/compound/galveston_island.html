Galveston Island

1. PROPN

/ ˈɡælvəstən ˈaɪlənd / (AmE), / ˈɡælvəstən ˈaɪlənd / (BrE)
an island located at the entrance of Galveston Bay, off the coast of Texas, known for its historic city and beaches
island at entrance of Galveston Bay, Texas

Examples

Every summer, tourists flock to Galveston Island for its beautiful beaches and vibrant nightlife.
The hurricane of 1900 devastated Galveston Island and remains one of the deadliest natural disasters in U.S. history.
Researchers are studying the effects of coastal erosion on Galveston Island's ecosystem.

Etymology

Named after Bernardo de Gálvez, a Spanish colonial governor, and from 'island'.
