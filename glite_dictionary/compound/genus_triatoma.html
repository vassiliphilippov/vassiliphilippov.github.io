genus Triatoma

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs traɪˈæt̬.ə.mə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs traɪˈæt.ə.mə / (BrE)
A genus of blood-sucking bugs known as conenoses or kissing bugs, which are important vectors of Chagas disease in Central and South America.
genus of blood-sucking bugs (kissing bugs)
medical

Examples

Many cases of Chagas disease have been linked to the genus Triatoma in rural areas of South America.
Researchers studied the behavior of genus Triatoma to develop better methods of controlling their spread.
Several species within the genus Triatoma are known to transmit parasites to humans.

Etymology

From Latin 'Triatoma', scientific genus name.
