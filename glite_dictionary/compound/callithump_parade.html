callithump parade

1. NOUN

/ ˈkælɪˌθʌmp pəˈreɪd / (AmE), / ˈkælɪˌθʌmp pəˈreɪd / (BrE)
a noisy and rowdy parade, often involving loud instruments, horns, and unconventional participants
noisy, boisterous parade

Examples

The annual callithump parade filled the streets with laughter, clanging pots, and outlandish costumes.
Children joined the callithump parade, banging drums and whistles to make as much noise as possible.
Residents prepared for the callithump parade by decorating their bikes and collecting noisy objects to contribute to the festivities.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: parade - procession of people or vehicles for celebration or display

Etymology

From 'callithump', possibly of US origin in the 19th century, meaning a noisy disturbance or parade.
