small slam

1. NOUN

/ smɔl slæm / (AmE), / smɔːl slæm / (BrE)
(in bridge) the achievement of winning all but one trick in a single hand.
winning all but one trick (bridge)

Examples

They needed a small slam to win the tournament, but ended up taking only eleven tricks.
If you make a small slam in spades, you score a significant bonus.
She bid confidently, hoping for a small slam despite her partner's weak hand.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: grand slam - winning all thirteen tricks in a hand of bridge
