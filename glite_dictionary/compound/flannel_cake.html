flannel cake

1. NOUN

/ ˈflænəl keɪk / (AmE), / ˈflæn(ə)l keɪk / (BrE)
A flat cake made from a thin batter and fried on both sides on a griddle; similar to a pancake, often served for breakfast.
flat cake similar to a pancake
american

Examples

For Sunday breakfast, Grandma always made a stack of flannel cakes with homemade syrup.
He flipped the flannel cake on the griddle until it was golden brown on both sides.
The old cookbook includes a recipe for traditional flannel cakes popular in nineteenth-century America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pancake - a thin, flat cake made from batter and cooked on a griddle or frying pan

Etymology

From 'flannel', possibly referring to the soft, pliable texture; used in American regional English.
