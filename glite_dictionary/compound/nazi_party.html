Nazi Party

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɑzi ˌpɑrti / (AmE), / ˈnɑːzi ˌpɑːti / (BrE)
the political party that ruled Germany under Adolf Hitler from 1933 to 1945, known formally as the National Socialist German Workers' Party
the German fascist party led by Hitler, 1933–1945

Examples

The Nazi Party seized power in Germany in 1933, leading to a dictatorship under Adolf Hitler.
Many historians study the rise and fall of the Nazi Party to understand World War II.
The policies of the Nazi Party had devastating consequences for millions of people across Europe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fascism - an authoritarian and nationalistic right-wing system of government
  • Antonym: Communist Party - a political party advocating communism as formulated by Marx and Lenin

Etymology

From German 'Nationalsozialistische Deutsche Arbeiterpartei' (National Socialist German Workers' Party); 'Nazi' is a contraction of 'Nationalsozialist'.
