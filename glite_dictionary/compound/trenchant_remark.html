trenchant remark

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrɛntʃənt rɪˈmɑrk / (AmE), / ˈtrɛntʃənt rɪˈmɑːk / (BrE)
a comment or observation that is sharply insightful and often cutting or penetrating
sharp, insightful comment

Examples

The critic's trenchant remark about the film's flaws sparked lively debate among the audience.
During the meeting, she offered a trenchant remark that revealed the core of the problem.
His trenchant remarks about corporate ethics challenged the industry to reconsider its practices.

Synset Examples

Her trenchant remarks left the audience in deep thought.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: witty remark - a clever or amusing comment
  • Degree Higher: caustic remark - a remark that is even more biting or scathing

Etymology

From Old French 'trenchant' (cutting), from 'trenchier' (to cut), referring to the incisive quality of the remark.
