Asian crocodile

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪʒən ˈkrɑːkədaɪl / (AmE), / ˈeɪʃən ˈkrɒkədaɪl / (BrE)
a large crocodile native to estuaries and rivers in eastern Asia and the Pacific islands, also known as the estuarine or saltwater crocodile
large crocodile of eastern Asia and Pacific islands

Examples

The Asian crocodile is considered the largest living reptile species in the world.
Due to habitat loss, the population of the Asian crocodile has declined in several countries.
Researchers observed an Asian crocodile basking on the riverbank at dawn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: saltwater crocodile - the world's largest living crocodile species, native to Asia and Australia
  • Degree Lower: Nile crocodile - large crocodile from Africa, related species
