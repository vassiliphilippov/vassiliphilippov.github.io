bush telegraph

1. NOUN

/ bʊʃ ˈtɛləˌɡræf / (AmE), / bʊʃ ˈtɛlɪˌɡrɑːf / (BrE)
an informal network for passing on rumors or information, especially by word of mouth
informal gossip or rumor network

Examples

He heard the news about the promotion through the bush telegraph.
Before the official memo arrived, everyone already knew via the bush telegraph.
Rumors about the company’s future spread quickly through the bush telegraph in the office.

Synset Examples

He learned about the rumors through the bush telegraph.
News spread quickly via the bush telegraph.
The bush telegraph informed us of the upcoming event.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grapevine - an informal means of circulating information or gossip

Etymology

Originally Australian English; from 'bush' (rural areas) and 'telegraph' (early communication system).
