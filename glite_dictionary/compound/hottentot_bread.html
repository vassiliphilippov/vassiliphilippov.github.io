Hottentot bread

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑtənˌtɑt brɛd / (AmE), / ˈhɒtəntɒt brɛd / (BrE)
the thick edible rootstock of the elephant's-foot plant (Dioscorea elephantipes), eaten as food in some regions of southern Africa
edible root of elephant's-foot plant

Examples

Local people would dig up Hottentot bread for its starchy, nutritious root.
Botanists have studied how Hottentot bread survives in arid environments.
Traditional recipes sometimes include the cooked root of Hottentot bread.

Etymology

From 'Hottentot' (an obsolete term for the Khoikhoi people of southern Africa) and 'bread', from the plant's use as a food.
