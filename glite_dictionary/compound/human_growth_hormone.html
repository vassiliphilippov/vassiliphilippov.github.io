human growth hormone

1. NOUN

/ ˈhjuːmən ɡroʊθ ˈhɔːrmoʊn / (AmE), / ˈhjuːmən ɡrəʊθ ˈhɔːməʊn / (BrE)
a hormone produced by the anterior pituitary gland that stimulates growth and cell reproduction in humans
pituitary hormone promoting growth
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that human growth hormone plays a crucial role in childhood development.
Some athletes have been banned for taking human growth hormone to enhance their performance.
A deficiency in human growth hormone can lead to growth disorders in children.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: growth hormone - hormone that stimulates growth, especially in animals
