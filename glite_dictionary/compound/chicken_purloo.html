chicken purloo

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɪkən pɜːrˌluː / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪkɪn pəˈluː / (BrE)
a thick stew made of rice, chicken, and sometimes small game, traditionally prepared in the southern United States
southern U.S. chicken and rice stew

Examples

Many families in South Carolina enjoy making chicken purloo for Sunday dinner.
She learned to cook chicken purloo from her grandmother's old recipe.
At the festival, they served a hearty chicken purloo full of tender meat and spicy sausage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chicken bog - a similar southern U.S. dish of chicken, rice, and sausage
  • Synonym: jambalaya - a Louisiana Creole dish of rice mixed with meat and vegetables

Etymology

Purloo is believed to be related to the word 'pilau', which comes from the Persian 'pilaw', referring to a rice dish.
