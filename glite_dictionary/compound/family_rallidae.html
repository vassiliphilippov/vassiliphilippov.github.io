family Rallidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈrælaɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈralɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of birds including rails, crakes, gallinules, and coots, found mainly in wetlands worldwide.
family of birds: rails, crakes, gallinules, coots

Examples

The family Rallidae includes several secretive species that inhabit marshes and reed beds.
Scientists have observed a steady decline in some members of the family Rallidae due to habitat loss.
Birdwatchers were excited to spot multiple representatives of the family Rallidae on their wetland tour.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rail - A bird belonging to the family Rallidae, found in marshes and wetlands.
  • Synonym: crake - A small rail-like bird in the family Rallidae.
  • Synonym: coot - An aquatic bird of the family Rallidae with distinctive white facial markings.

Etymology

From Modern Latin Rallidae, based on Latin rallus (rail, a type of bird).
