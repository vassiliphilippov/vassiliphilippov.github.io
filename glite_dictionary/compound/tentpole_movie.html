tentpole movie

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛntˌpoʊl ˈmuːvi / (AmE), / ˈtɛntpəʊl ˈmuːvi / (BrE)
A major film produced by a studio that is expected to generate substantial revenue and support the funding of other, less profitable projects.
major, highly profitable film for a studio

Examples

The studio invested heavily in its new tentpole movie hoping for a record-breaking release.
Superhero franchises are often considered the quintessential tentpole movies of modern cinema.
This summer's tentpole movie is expected to boost the company's profits significantly.

Synset Examples

The latest tentpole movie is breaking box office records.
Everyone is excited for the tentpole movie this summer.
A tentpole movie often features big-name stars.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blockbuster - A very popular and profitable film

Etymology

Metaphorical use of 'tentpole', referring to something that supports a structure; applies to films that financially support a studio's slate.
