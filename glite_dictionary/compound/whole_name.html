whole name

1. NOUN

/ hoʊl neɪm / (AmE), / həʊl neɪm / (BrE)
A word that names the whole to which a given word or part belongs; a holonym.
word for a whole of which a part is named

Examples

In the relationship between 'branch' and 'tree', 'tree' is the whole name.
'Car' is the whole name for parts like 'wheel' and 'engine'.
When discussing anatomy, 'hand' serves as the whole name for 'fingers' and 'palm'.

Synset Examples

‘hat’ is a holonym for ‘brim’ and ‘crown’

Related Terms

  • Synonym: holonym - a word denoting a whole of which a part is named
  • Antonym: part name - a word denoting a part of a given whole
