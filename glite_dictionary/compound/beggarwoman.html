beggarwoman

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛɡɚˌwʊmən / (AmE), / ˈbɛɡəˌwʊmən / (BrE)
a woman who begs for money or food in public places
female beggar

Examples

A beggarwoman sat quietly on the steps, holding out her hand for spare change.
The beggarwoman told her story to anyone who would listen in the busy marketplace.
In the novel, a beggarwoman plays a small but significant role in the plot.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beggar - someone who lives by asking for money or food

Etymology

Formed from 'beggar' + 'woman', patterning after similar compounds like 'policeman' or 'washerwoman'.
