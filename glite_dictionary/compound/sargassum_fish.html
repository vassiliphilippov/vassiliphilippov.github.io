sargassum fish

1. NOUN

/ sɑrˈɡæsəm fɪʃ / (AmE), / sɑːˈɡasəm fɪʃ / (BrE)
a small, bizarrely shaped and colored fish (Histrio histrio) found living among floating masses of sargassum seaweed in warm seas
a fish species living among sargassum seaweed

Examples

The sargassum fish is known for its remarkable camouflage among drifting seaweed.
Marine biologists discovered a sargassum fish hiding in a floating mat of algae.
Tourists were amazed to spot a colorful sargassum fish during the snorkeling trip.

Etymology

From sargassum, the type of seaweed where the fish is found, and fish.
