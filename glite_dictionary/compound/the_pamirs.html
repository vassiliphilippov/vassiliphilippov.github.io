the Pamirs

1. PROPN

/ ðə pəˈmɪrz / (AmE), / ðə paˈmɪəz / (BrE)
a mountain range in Central Asia, mainly in Tajikistan, extending into Kyrgyzstan, Afghanistan, Pakistan, and western China
mountain range in Central Asia

Examples

The explorers embarked on a challenging expedition across the Pamirs to study its unique ecosystem.
Many rivers in Central Asia have their sources in the Pamirs.
The intersection of ancient trade routes made the Pamirs an important region historically.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: the Himalayas - major mountain range in Asia, bordering the Pamirs to the south

Etymology

From Russian 'Pamir'; ultimately of Turkic/Persian origin.
