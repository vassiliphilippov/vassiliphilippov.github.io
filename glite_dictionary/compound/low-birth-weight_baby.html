low-birth-weight baby

1. NOUN

/ ˌloʊ bɜrθ ˈweɪt ˈbeɪ.bi / (AmE), / ˌləʊ bɜːθ ˈweɪt ˈbeɪ.bi / (BrE)
a baby who is born weighing less than 5.5 pounds (2,500 grams), regardless of the length of pregnancy
baby weighing less than 5.5 pounds at birth
medical

Examples

A low-birth-weight baby requires close monitoring in the hospital to prevent complications.
Premature infants are more likely to be classified as low-birth-weight babies.
Doctors are concerned when a low-birth-weight baby shows signs of difficulty breathing.

Synset Examples

a low-birth-weight infant is at risk for developing lack of oxygen during labor
