sinew of

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪnjuː əv / (AmE), / ˈsɪnjuː əv / (BrE)
the main source of strength, power, or vitality in something
main source of strength or power

Examples

Teamwork is the sinew of our company's success.
Education forms the sinew of a thriving nation.
Integrity is often regarded as the sinew of true leadership.

Synset Examples

He admired the sinew of the athlete's muscles.
The sinew of the project lies in teamwork.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: backbone of - the chief support or mainstay of something

Etymology

From the noun 'sinew', meaning tendon or muscle, used metaphorically for strength since Old English 'seonu'.
