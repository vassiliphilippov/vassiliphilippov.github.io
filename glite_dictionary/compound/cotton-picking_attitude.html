cotton-picking attitude

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑtn̩ ˌpɪkɪŋ æ.tɪˌtjud / (AmE), / ˈkɒt.n̩ ˌpɪk.ɪŋ æ.tɪˌtjuːd / (BrE)
An irritating or contemptuous manner or behavior, often provoking annoyance.
irritating or contemptuous behavior
informal american

Examples

His cotton-picking attitude during the meeting made it hard for anyone to remain calm.
I'm tired of your cotton-picking attitude every time we discuss this topic.
She apologized later for her cotton-picking attitude at the party.

Synset Examples

Her cotton-picking attitude annoyed everyone around.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attitude - a settled way of thinking or feeling

Etymology

'Cotton-picking' originated as an intensifier in American English, referencing the labor of picking cotton, and is used here informally to add emphasis.
