insertional mutagenesis

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˌsɜrʃənl mjuːˌteɪdʒəˈnɛsɪs / (AmE), / ɪnˌsɜːʃənəl mjuːˌteɪdʒəˈnesɪs / (BrE)
a mutation that occurs when foreign DNA is inserted into a genome, potentially disrupting normal gene function
mutation from insertion of foreign DNA
medical

Examples

Gene therapy carries a risk of insertional mutagenesis that can lead to unintended side effects.
Researchers observed insertional mutagenesis in mice after introducing the viral vector.
The study investigates methods to reduce insertional mutagenesis during genome editing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mutation - a permanent change in the DNA sequence

Etymology

From Latin 'insertio' (an inserting) and Greek 'mutagenesis' (origin of mutation).
