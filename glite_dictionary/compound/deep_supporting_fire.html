deep supporting fire

1. NOUN

/ diːp səˈpɔrtɪŋ faɪr / (AmE), / diːp səˈpɔːtɪŋ faɪə / (BrE)
artillery or other fire directed at targets away from friendly forces, intended to disrupt enemy reserves, command, supplies, and communications
military fire targeting enemy rear areas

Examples

The artillery unit provided deep supporting fire to disrupt enemy reinforcements before the main assault.
Commanders requested deep supporting fire against the enemy supply lines to weaken their response capabilities.
Effective deep supporting fire can significantly impair the enemy's ability to organize a counterattack.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: supporting fire - fire provided to assist advancing troops or operations
