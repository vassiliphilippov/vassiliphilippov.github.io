well point

1. NOUN

/ wɛl pɔɪnt / (AmE), / wɛl pɔɪnt / (BrE)
a perforated tube driven into the ground to collect water from the surrounding soil, typically used in dewatering operations
perforated tube for ground water collection

Examples

The construction team installed a well point system to lower the groundwater level at the site.
During the dewatering project, each well point had to be checked daily for clogs.
The engineer explained how a well point draws water from the soil to prevent flooding.
