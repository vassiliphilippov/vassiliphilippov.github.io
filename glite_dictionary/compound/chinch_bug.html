chinch bug

1. NOUN

/ tʃɪntʃ bʌɡ / (AmE), / tʃɪntʃ bʌɡ / (BrE)
a small black-and-white bug that feeds on cereal grasses and can damage crops
small bug harmful to cereal grasses

Examples

Farmers often struggle to control chinch bug infestations in their wheat fields.
The lawn turned brown due to damage caused by chinch bugs.
Researchers are studying new methods to prevent chinch bug outbreaks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bug - an insect or similar small creature

Etymology

From Spanish 'chinche' (bug, bedbug).
