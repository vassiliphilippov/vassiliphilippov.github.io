fibrous-rooted begonia

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪbrəs ˌruːtɪd bɪˈɡoʊniə / (AmE), / ˈfaɪbrəs ˌruːtɪd bɪˈɡəʊniə / (BrE)
A type of begonia plant that has fibrous roots rather than tuberous or rhizomatous roots.
begonia with fibrous roots

Examples

The fibrous-rooted begonia is well-suited as a bedding plant in shaded gardens.
Unlike its tuberous cousin, the fibrous-rooted begonia often blooms year-round indoors.
Gardeners value the fibrous-rooted begonia for its hardiness and prolific flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: begonia - a genus of flowering plants popular as ornamentals
