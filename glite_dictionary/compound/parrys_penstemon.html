Parry's penstemon

1. NOUN

/ ˈpæɹiz ˈpɛnstəmən / (AmE), / ˈpariz ˈpɛnstɪmən / (BrE)
A species of penstemon with erect stems and pinkish-lavender flowers borne in long, interrupted clusters, native to Arizona.
a flowering plant native to Arizona

Examples

In spring, the desert comes alive with the blooms of Parry's penstemon.
Gardeners in Arizona often plant Parry's penstemon to attract hummingbirds and bees.
The park ranger pointed out a patch of Parry's penstemon along the hiking trail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: penstemon - a genus of North American flowering plants

Etymology

Named after Charles Christopher Parry, an American botanist, and Greek 'pénstēmōn' (bearded mouth, referring to the staminode shape).
