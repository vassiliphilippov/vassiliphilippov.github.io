bottom power

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑːt̬əm ˌpaʊər / (AmE), / ˈbɒtəm ˌpaʊə / (BrE)
(chiefly West African) influence or support, often exerted from grassroots or community levels, sometimes referring to the use of sexual appeal—especially by women—to gain advantage or sway decisions.
grassroots influence; using sexual appeal for advantage

Examples

Many activists believe that bottom power is crucial in driving social change at the grassroots level.
Rumors spread that she used her bottom power to influence the company's hiring decisions.
Although controversial, some politicians have gained support through bottom power in their communities.

Synset Examples

They relied on bottom power to rally support for the cause.
His bottom power helped him influence the decision.
She used her bottom power to secure a promotion.

Etymology

From English 'bottom' (slang for buttocks) + 'power', originally popularized in Nigerian English.
