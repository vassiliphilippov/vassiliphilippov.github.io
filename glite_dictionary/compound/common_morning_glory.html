common morning glory

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmən ˈmɔrnɪŋ ˌɡlɔri / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən ˈmɔːnɪŋ ˌɡlɒri / (BrE)
A climbing plant native to Central America, often grown as an ornamental, with bright sky-blue flowers; the most commonly cultivated type of morning glory.
climbing plant with blue flowers

Examples

The vibrant blooms of the common morning glory make it a favorite among gardeners.
She planted common morning glory seeds along the fence to create a colorful summer display.
The common morning glory opens its flowers early in the morning, closing them by afternoon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: morning glory - any of various twining vines with trumpet-shaped flowers

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmən ˈmɔrnɪŋ ˌɡlɔri / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən ˈmɔːnɪŋ ˌɡlɔːri / (BrE)
a tropical and subtropical climbing annual plant with funnel-shaped blue, purple, pink, or white flowers, often grown as an ornamental
a climbing plant with colorful funnel-shaped flowers

Examples

The common morning glory blooms early in the day, showcasing its bright blue flowers.
Gardeners often plant common morning glory along fences to create a wall of vibrant color.
Because it grows quickly, the common morning glory can cover trellises and arbors in just a few weeks.
