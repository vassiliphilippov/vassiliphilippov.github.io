banning-order

1. NOUN

/ ˈbænɪŋ ˌɔrdɚ / (AmE), / ˈbænɪŋ ˌɔːdə / (BrE)
an official order that prohibits or forbids certain actions, behaviors, or people, often issued by legal or governmental authorities
official order prohibiting something

Examples

The government issued a banning-order to prevent the group from holding public meetings.
Anyone caught violating the banning-order could face legal consequences.
He challenged the banning-order in court, arguing that it infringed on his rights.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ban - an official or legal prohibition
