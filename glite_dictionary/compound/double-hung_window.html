double-hung window

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʌbl ˈhʌŋ ˈwɪndoʊ / (AmE), / ˌdʌbl ˈhʌŋ ˈwɪndəʊ / (BrE)
a window with two sashes that can both slide vertically past each other
window with two vertically sliding sashes

Examples

The old house still has its original double-hung window frames in the living room.
A double-hung window allows for better ventilation since both the top and bottom sashes can open.
Maintenance is easier with a double-hung window because the sashes can be tilted inward for cleaning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sash window - a window made of one or more movable panels, or sashes
