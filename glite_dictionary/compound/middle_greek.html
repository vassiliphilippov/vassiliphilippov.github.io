Middle Greek

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪdəl ɡrik / (AmE), / ˈmɪdəl ɡriːk / (BrE)
the stage of the Greek language spoken and written from approximately 600 to 1200 AD, forming a transition between Ancient and Modern Greek.
Greek language from 600 to 1200 AD

Examples

Scholars study Middle Greek to learn about the language of the Byzantine Empire.
Many religious texts from the period are written in Middle Greek.
The development of Middle Greek influenced later forms of the Greek language.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Ancient Greek - the Greek language as used from the 9th century BC to the 6th century AD
  • Degree Higher: Modern Greek - the Greek language as spoken and written since around 1453
