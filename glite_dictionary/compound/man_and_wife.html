man and wife

1. NOUN

/ mæn ənd waɪf / (AmE), / mæn ənd waɪf / (BrE)
a phrase referring to a married couple, specifically a man and his wife, traditionally used in wedding ceremonies and formal contexts
married couple (man and his wife)
archaic

Examples

The minister pronounced them man and wife at the end of the ceremony.
It was declared in court that they were legally man and wife.
In the old story, the couple were at last happily united as man and wife.

Synset Examples

his second marriage was happier than the first
a married couple without love

Related Terms

  • Synonym: husband and wife - a married couple; man and woman joined by marriage

Etymology

From the traditional English wedding phrase, using 'man' for husband and 'wife' for married woman.
