old boy

1. NOUN

/ oʊld bɔɪ / (AmE), / əʊld bɔɪ / (BrE)
an elderly man who is lively, energetic, or spirited
vivacious elderly man

Examples

Despite his age, the old boy danced all night at the party.
Everyone admired the old boy for his enthusiasm and humor.
The old boy entertained the children with his adventurous stories.

2. NOUN

/ oʊld bɔɪ / (AmE), / əʊld bɔɪ / (BrE)
a friendly or familiar term of address for a man, especially used among men who have known each other for a long time
familiar term of address for a man
british

Examples

"How have you been, old boy?" he asked cheerfully when they met again.
The two old boys reminisced about their school days over dinner.
You’re looking well, old boy!

3. NOUN

/ oʊld bɔɪ / (AmE), / əʊld bɔɪ / (BrE)
a male who formerly attended a particular school; especially used to refer to alumni of a private school in Britain
former male pupil of a school

Examples

The exclusive club was established for old boys of the prestigious boarding school.
As an old boy of Eton, he maintained close ties with former classmates.
The old boys donate generously to the school's charitable fund each year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alumnus - a former student of a school, college, or university
