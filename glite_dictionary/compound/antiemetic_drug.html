antiemetic drug

1. NOUN

/ ˌæn.ti.ɪˈmɛt.ɪk drʌɡ / (AmE), / ˌæn.ti.ɪˈmɛt.ɪk drʌɡ / (BrE)
a drug that prevents or alleviates nausea and vomiting
drug preventing nausea or vomiting
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed an antiemetic drug to help control her nausea during chemotherapy.
Some antiemetic drugs can cause drowsiness as a side effect.
Before the surgery, he was given an antiemetic drug to prevent postoperative vomiting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antiemetic - a substance that prevents vomiting

Etymology

From Greek anti- (against) + emesis (vomiting).
