to never know

1. VERB

/ ˈnɛvɚ noʊ / (AmE), / ˈnɛvə nəʊ / (BrE)
used to express uncertainty about what may happen in the future, or to suggest that anything is possible.
express uncertainty about future events

Examples

You never know what opportunities might come your way.
He always takes his umbrella because you never know when it will rain.
With new technology emerging so quickly, you never know what's going to be possible next year.

Synset Examples

(You) never know what might happen next.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: who knows - used to express doubt or uncertainty
