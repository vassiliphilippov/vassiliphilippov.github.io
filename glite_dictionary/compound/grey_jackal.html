grey jackal

1. NOUN

/ ɡreɪ ˈdʒæk.əl / (AmE), / ɡreɪ ˈdʒæk.əl / (BrE)
a jackal species native to the deserts of Northern Africa, closely related to the golden jackal and the grey wolf
a jackal species of Northern Africa

Examples

The grey jackal is well adapted to the arid landscapes of Northern Africa.
Researchers observed a pack of grey jackals hunting in the early morning.
Unlike the golden jackal, the grey jackal tends to live in more desert-like habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: golden jackal - a jackal species found in Eurasia and North Africa
  • Synonym: grey wolf - a large canine native to Eurasia and North America
