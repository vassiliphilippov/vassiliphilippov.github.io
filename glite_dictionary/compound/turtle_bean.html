turtle bean

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɝː.t̬l̩ bin / (AmE), / ˈtɜː.tl̩ biːn / (BrE)
A small, black variety of common bean, native to Central and South America, typically used dried in cooking.
a small black bean used in cooking

Examples

The recipe calls for one cup of dried turtle beans soaked overnight.
Vegetarian chili is often made with turtle beans as the main protein source.
She added cooked turtle beans to her salad for extra flavor and nutrition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: black bean - another name for turtle bean, commonly used in US English
