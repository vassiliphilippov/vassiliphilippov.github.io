working day

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɝːkɪŋ deɪ / (AmE), / ˈwɜːkɪŋ deɪ / (BrE)
the number of hours an employee is required to work in a single day for a standard wage
hours required to work in a day

Examples

His working day usually starts at 9 AM and ends at 5 PM.
The new contract reduced the working day from ten hours to eight.
Many employees prefer a flexible working day to accommodate their personal schedules.

Synset Examples

they work an 8-hour day

Related Terms

  • Synonym: workday - a day on which work is done, especially the usual hours of work

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɜrkɪŋ deɪ / (AmE), / ˈwɜːkɪŋ deɪ / (BrE)
A day on which work is usually done, especially in contrast to weekends or public holidays.
day on which work is done

Examples

Friday is considered a working day in many countries, while Saturday and Sunday are not.
My shift covers every working day from 9 to 5.
During the working day, the offices are busy with employees and visitors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: business day - a day, typically Monday to Friday, when normal business operations are conducted
  • Antonym: holiday - a day of celebration or rest when work is usually suspended
  • Synonym: weekday - any day of the week except Saturday and Sunday
