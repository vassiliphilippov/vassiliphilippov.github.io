indigo snake

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪndɪɡoʊ sneɪk / (AmE), / ˈɪndɪɡəʊ sneɪk / (BrE)
a large, dark-blue, nonvenomous snake found in southern North America and Mexico, known for invading burrows
a large, nonvenomous blue snake of North America

Examples

The indigo snake is one of the largest native snakes in the United States.
We spotted an indigo snake slithering across the path during our hike.
Because it's nonvenomous, the indigo snake is often welcomed by farmers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snake - a legless, carnivorous reptile

Etymology

Named for its deep blue (indigo) coloration; 'snake' from Old English 'snaca'.
