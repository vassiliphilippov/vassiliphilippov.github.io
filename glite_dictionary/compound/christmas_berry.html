Christmas berry

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɪsməs ˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈkrɪsməs ˌbɛri / (BrE)
A spiny evergreen shrub of the southeastern United States with spreading branches, usually blue or mauve flowers, and red berries.
evergreen shrub with red berries

Examples

The Christmas berry produces clusters of red fruit that persist through the winter.
Gardeners in the Southeast often plant Christmas berry shrubs for their attractive appearance.
Pollinators are drawn to the blue or mauve flowers of the Christmas berry each spring.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkrɪs.məs ˌbɛr.i / (AmE), / ˈkrɪs.məs ˌbɛr.i / (BrE)
an ornamental evergreen shrub or small tree of the Pacific coast of the United States, with large white flowers and red, berrylike fruits, often grown for decorative purposes
ornamental shrub with red berries

Examples

The Christmas berry is popular for holiday decorations due to its bright red fruit.
Gardeners often plant Christmas berry to add winter color to their landscapes.
Many birds are attracted to the Christmas berry for its abundant fruit in late autumn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: toyon - an evergreen shrub native to California with red berries, also known as Christmas berry
