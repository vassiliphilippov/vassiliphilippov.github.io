tincture of iodine

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɪŋktʃɚ əv ˈaɪ.oʊˌdaɪn / (AmE), / ˈtɪŋktʃə əv ˈaɪ.əʊˌdiːn / (BrE)
a solution of iodine dissolved in ethyl alcohol, used as a topical antiseptic on wounds
iodine solution used as antiseptic
medical

Examples

The nurse cleaned the cut with tincture of iodine before covering it with a bandage.
A bottle of tincture of iodine was always kept in our family’s first-aid kit.
Doctors used tincture of iodine to disinfect wounds in the early twentieth century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: iodine - a chemical element used as an antiseptic

Etymology

From Latin 'tinctura' (act of dyeing or coloring) and Greek 'iodes' (violet-colored).
