shoemaker's last

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃoʊˌmeɪkərz læst / (AmE), / ˈʃəʊˌmeɪkəz lɑːst / (BrE)
a solid form shaped like a human foot, used by shoemakers to make or repair shoes
tool shaped like a foot for making shoes

Examples

The cobbler placed the leather over the shoemaker's last to shape the new boots.
A well-made shoemaker's last ensures that the shoes will fit comfortably.
He repaired the torn shoe using a shoemaker's last he inherited from his grandfather.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: last - foot-shaped form used in making or repairing shoes

Etymology

From 'shoemaker', one who makes shoes, and 'last', from Old English 'læste' (footprint, sole).
