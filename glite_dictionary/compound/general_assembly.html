General Assembly

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒɛn(ə)rəl əˈsɛmbli / (AmE), / ˌdʒɛn(ə)rəl əˈsɛmbli / (BrE)
the main deliberative body of the United Nations, composed of representatives from all member states
main United Nations deliberative body

Examples

All member nations have one vote each in the General Assembly of the United Nations.
The annual meeting of the General Assembly addresses issues of global concern.
She represented her country at the General Assembly last September.
