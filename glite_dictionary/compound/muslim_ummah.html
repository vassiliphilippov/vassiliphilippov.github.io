Muslim Ummah

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌzlɪm ˈʊmɑː / (AmE), / ˈmʊzlɪm ˈʊmɑː / (BrE)
The worldwide community of Muslims, considered as a unified group transcending national, ethnic, or cultural boundaries.
the worldwide Muslim community

Examples

Leaders from various countries gathered to discuss issues affecting the Muslim Ummah.
The Muslim Ummah celebrates Eid al-Fitr at the end of Ramadan.
Charity and solidarity are important values within the Muslim Ummah.

Synset Examples

moderate Muslims urge the Ummah to reject the terrorism of radical Muslims

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ummah - the Muslim community as a whole

Etymology

From Arabic 'ummah' (community); 'Muslim' from Arabic 'muslim' (one who submits to God).
