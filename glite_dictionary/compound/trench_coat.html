trench coat

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrɛntʃ ˌkoʊt / (AmE), / ˈtrɛntʃ kəʊt / (BrE)
A long, double-breasted, belted raincoat with deep pockets, often of military style.
belted, military-style raincoat

Examples

He wore a classic trench coat over his suit as he walked through the city streets.
A beige trench coat is considered a timeless piece in many wardrobes.
During the rainstorm, she stayed dry thanks to her waterproof trench coat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: raincoat - waterproof or water-resistant coat worn to protect against rain

Etymology

Named after its association with soldiers in the trenches during World War I.

2. NOUN

/ trɛntʃ koʊt / (AmE), / trɛntʃ kəʊt / (BrE)
A long, waterproof coat with a belt and typically deep pockets, often associated with military style and worn in both formal and casual settings.
long, waterproof, belted coat

Examples

The detective pulled his trench coat tighter as he walked through the foggy streets.
She wore a stylish trench coat over her evening dress to the gala.
Classic films often feature mysterious figures in a trench coat and a fedora.

Synset Examples

The detective appeared in a trench coat and hat.
I saw him lurking in a trench coat by the corner.
The character in the movie wore a trench coat throughout.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: raincoat - a waterproof or water-resistant coat worn to protect against rain

Etymology

From 'trench' (referring to World War I trenches) + 'coat', because it was originally worn by soldiers in the trenches during World War I.
