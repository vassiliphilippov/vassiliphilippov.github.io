sinusoidal projection

1. NOUN

/ ˌsaɪnəˈsɔɪdəl prəˈdʒɛkʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɪnjʊˈsɔɪdəl prəˈdʒɛkʃən / (BrE)
a map projection that represents the parallels and the equator as straight lines and the other meridians as sine curves, preserving area, and commonly used for mapping tropical regions
equal-area map projection with sine curve meridians

Examples

The sinusoidal projection is often used in atlases to display regions near the equator.
Because it is an equal-area map, the sinusoidal projection preserves the true size of landmasses.
Cartographers sometimes prefer the sinusoidal projection when creating world maps focused on tropical zones.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: map projection - the method of representing the surface of a sphere on a plane

Etymology

From 'sinusoidal' (from Latin 'sinus' meaning 'curve') and 'projection', referring to the use of sine curves for the meridians.
