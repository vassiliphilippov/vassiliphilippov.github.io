rogue's gallery

1. NOUN

/ roʊɡz ˈɡæləri / (AmE), / rəʊɡz ˈɡæləri / (BrE)
a collection of photographs of known criminals kept by the police for identification purposes
collection of criminal photographs

Examples

The detective consulted the rogue's gallery to see if any suspects matched the witness's description.
Over the years, the police station filled its rogue's gallery with thousands of mugshots.
Visitors to the crime museum were fascinated by the historic rogue's gallery on display.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mug shot - a photograph taken of a person's face for police records

Etymology

From 'rogue' (a dishonest person) and 'gallery' (a display or collection); first used in the 19th century for police collections of criminal photos.

2. NOUN

/ roʊɡz ˈɡæləri / (AmE), / rəʊɡz ˈɡaləri / (BrE)
a collection or group of people considered disreputable, undesirable, or infamous, especially criminals or wrongdoers
group of disreputable or criminal people

Examples

The detective reviewed the rogue's gallery in search of a suspect for the recent burglary.
Every political scandal seems to add new faces to the country's rogue's gallery.
The book profiles a rogue's gallery of fraudsters, hackers, and scammers from around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: criminal - person who has committed a crime
  • Synonym: villain - wicked or criminal person

Etymology

Originated in 19th-century law enforcement for photographic collections of criminals; 'rogue' from Latin 'rogare' (to ask, beg).
