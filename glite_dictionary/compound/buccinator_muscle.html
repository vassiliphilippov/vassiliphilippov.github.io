buccinator muscle

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌksɪˌneɪtɚ ˈmʌsəl / (AmE), / ˈbʌksɪnətə ˈmʌsl̩ / (BrE)
a thin muscle of the cheek that flattens the cheek and pulls back the angle of the mouth
cheek muscle that flattens the cheek
medical

Examples

The buccinator muscle is crucial for blowing air and playing wind instruments.
When you chew, your buccinator muscle helps keep food between your teeth.
Weakness in the buccinator muscle can cause problems with speech and swallowing.

Etymology

From Latin bucinator (trumpeter), from buccina (trumpet), referring to the muscle's shape and function.
