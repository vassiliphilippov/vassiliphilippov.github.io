gas fixture

1. NOUN

/ ɡæs ˈfɪkstʃɚ / (AmE), / ɡæs ˈfɪkstʃə / (BrE)
a device used to convey illuminating gas from a supply pipe to a gas burner, typically as part of an indoor lighting system
device for conveying gas to a burner

Examples

The old mansion still had a gas fixture in the library, though it was no longer in use.
During renovations, workers discovered a forgotten gas fixture behind the plaster wall.
Victorian homes often featured ornate gas fixtures as part of their original lighting systems.
