New Zealand wine berry

1. NOUN

/ nuː ˈziːlənd waɪn ˈbɛri / (AmE), / njuː ˈziːlənd waɪn ˈbɛri / (BrE)
a deciduous shrub or small tree native to New Zealand, known for its attractive leaves and small red berries that turn black and are used for making wine
New Zealand shrub with berries used for wine

Examples

The New Zealand wine berry thrives in well-drained, fertile soil across the country.
Many gardeners value the New Zealand wine berry for its ornamental foliage and fruit.
The berries of the New Zealand wine berry are harvested each year to produce a unique local wine.
