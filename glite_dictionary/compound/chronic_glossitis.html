chronic glossitis

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɑː.nɪk ɡlɒˈsaɪ.tɪs / (AmE), / ˈkrɒ.nɪk ɡlɒˈsaɪ.tɪs / (BrE)
A persistent inflammation of the tongue, often leading to atrophy of tongue tissue, and sometimes associated with conditions such as pernicious anemia.
persistent inflammation of the tongue
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with chronic glossitis after several months of persistent tongue discomfort.
Vitamin deficiencies can sometimes result in chronic glossitis and require long-term management.
Pernicious anemia is often accompanied by symptoms of chronic glossitis and other oral manifestations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glossitis - inflammation of the tongue

Etymology

From Greek 'glossa' meaning 'tongue' and '-itis' indicating inflammation.
