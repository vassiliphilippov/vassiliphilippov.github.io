truss bridge

1. NOUN

/ trʌs brɪdʒ / (AmE), / trʌs brɪdʒ / (BrE)
A bridge constructed with a framework of connected elements forming triangular units to support the structure.
bridge made with a truss framework

Examples

The old railway truss bridge spans the river just outside the city.
Engineers decided to replace the wooden bridge with a steel truss bridge for greater durability.
Many historic truss bridges can still be found on country roads throughout the region.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: arch bridge - a bridge with abutments at each end shaped as a curved arch
  • Antonym: suspension bridge - a bridge in which the deck is hung below suspension cables
  • Antonym: beam bridge - a bridge supported by beams
