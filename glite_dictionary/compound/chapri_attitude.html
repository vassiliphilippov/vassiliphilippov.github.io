chapri attitude

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɑːpri æˈtɪtjud / (AmE), / ˈtʃɑːpri əˈtɪtjuːd / (BrE)
A style of flashy or showy behavior, typically regarded as immature or lacking in sophistication.
flashy, immature behavior
informal

Examples

His chapri attitude was obvious the moment he walked into the room with gold chains and flashy clothes.
She finds that kind of chapri attitude really off-putting.
Their chapri attitude often leads to them being misunderstood by others.

Synset Examples

The chapri attitude didn't impress the older crowd.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to show off - to behave in a way intended to attract attention or admiration
  • Antonym: modest - humble, not attempting to impress others

Etymology

From Hindi slang 'chapri' (a derogatory term for someone considered flashy or showy) + English 'attitude'.
