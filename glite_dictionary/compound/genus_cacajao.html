genus Cacajao

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs kəˌkɑːˈhaʊ / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs kəˌkaːˈjaʊ / (BrE)
A genus of South American monkeys known as uakaris, characterized by their short tails and often red faces.
genus of South American uakari monkeys

Examples

Scientists studying the Amazon rainforest observed several species within the genus Cacajao in the treetops.
The genus Cacajao is notable for the distinctive appearance of its uakari monkeys.
Conservationists are working to protect habitats crucial for the survival of the genus Cacajao.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: uakari - a type of monkey native to the Amazon, belonging to genus Cacajao

Etymology

From New Latin Cacajao, based on local South American names for the monkeys.
