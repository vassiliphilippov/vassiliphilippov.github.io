bog candles

1. NOUN

/ bɑɡ ˈkændlz / (AmE), / bɒɡ ˈkændlz / (BrE)
A North American orchid with spikes of many fragrant white flowers on erect leafy stems, typically found in wet or boggy ground.
a white-flowered North American orchid

Examples

The bog candles were in full bloom along the edge of the marsh.
Botanists trekked through the wetland to locate a rare patch of bog candles.
With their delicate fragrance, bog candles stand out against the lush green of the bog.
