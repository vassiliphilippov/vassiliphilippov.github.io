process printing

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɑː.ses ˌprɪn.tɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈprɒ.ses ˌprɪn.tɪŋ / (BrE)
a method of producing colored images by printing from halftone plates, typically using the four basic color inks (cyan, magenta, yellow, and black).
method of printing colored images

Examples

The magazine uses process printing to achieve vivid and detailed color photographs.
High-quality brochures are often created through process printing techniques.
Thanks to advances in process printing, reproducing artwork in full color has become more affordable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: color printing - printing in color rather than black and white

Etymology

From process (referring to the sequence of printing steps) + printing; use dates to late 19th century with the rise of color printing technology.
