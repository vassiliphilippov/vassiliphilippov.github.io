order Berycomorphi

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdər bɛrɪkoʊˈmɔrfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə bɛrɪkəˈmɔːfaɪ / (BrE)
an order of spiny-finned fish in the superorder Acanthopterygii, comprising several families of deep-sea and marine fishes
an order of spiny-finned fish

Examples

Many deep-sea fish species belong to the order Berycomorphi.
Scientists discovered a new species within the order Berycomorphi during the oceanic expedition.
The anatomy of fish in the order Berycomorphi is adapted to life in deep marine environments.

Etymology

From New Latin Berycomorphi, based on type genus Beryx plus Greek morphē (form).
