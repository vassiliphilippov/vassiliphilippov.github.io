radio theatre

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪdioʊ ˈθiətər / (AmE), / ˈreɪdiəʊ ˈθɪətə / (BrE)
A dramatized, purely acoustic performance broadcast on radio or audio media, featuring actors, sound effects, and music to tell a story.
dramatized audio performance for radio

Examples

Many families used to gather around the radio to listen to their favorite radio theatre programs each evening.
The BBC's radio theatre brought classic novels to life for listeners across the country.
He prefers radio theatre because the imaginative soundscapes create a unique storytelling experience.

Synset Examples

An important turning point in radio drama came when Schenectady, New York's WGY, after a successful tryout on August 3, 1922, began weekly studio broadcasts of full-length stage plays in September 1922, using music, sound effects and a regular troupe of actors, The WGY Players.
