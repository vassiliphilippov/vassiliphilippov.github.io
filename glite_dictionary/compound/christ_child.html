Christ Child

1. PROPN

/ kraɪst tʃaɪld / (AmE), / kraɪst tʃaɪld / (BrE)
the infant Jesus, especially as portrayed in Christian art and nativity scenes.
the infant Jesus in Christianity

Examples

Every Christmas, a figurine of the Christ Child is placed in the family nativity scene.
In many cultures, children receive gifts from the Christ Child rather than Santa Claus.
The painting depicted the Virgin Mary holding the Christ Child tenderly in her arms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Jesus - the central figure of Christianity
  • Synonym: nativity - the birth of Jesus Christ

Etymology

From Christ, meaning 'the anointed one' from Greek 'Khristos', and child, from Old English 'cild'.
