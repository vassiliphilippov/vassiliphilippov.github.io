Acheson process

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪtʃəsən ˌprɑːsɛs / (AmE), / ˈatʃɪsən ˌprəʊsɛs / (BrE)
an industrial process for producing graphite by heating a mixture of coke and clay at high temperatures
industrial process for making graphite

Examples

The Acheson process revolutionized the commercial production of synthetic graphite.
Factories still use the Acheson process to create high-quality graphite electrodes.
The discovery of the Acheson process marked a significant advancement in industrial chemistry.

Etymology

Named after Edward G. Acheson (1856–1931), American chemist who invented the process.
