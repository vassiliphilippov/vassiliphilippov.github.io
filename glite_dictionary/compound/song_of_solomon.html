Song of Solomon

1. PROPN

/ sɑŋ əv ˈsɑləmən / (AmE), / sɒŋ əv ˈsɒləmən / (BrE)
A book of the Old Testament consisting of a collection of love poems, traditionally attributed to Solomon.
book of the Old Testament; love poems

Examples

Many scholars debate the interpretation of the Song of Solomon in biblical literature.
The Song of Solomon contains some of the most poetic passages in the Old Testament.
She quoted verses from the Song of Solomon during the wedding ceremony.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Song of Songs - another name for the same biblical book

Etymology

From the traditional ascription to King Solomon in Jewish and Christian tradition.
