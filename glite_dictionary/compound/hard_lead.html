hard lead

1. NOUN

/ hɑrd lɛd / (AmE), / hɑːd lɛd / (BrE)
Lead that is hard due to the presence of impurities, especially antimony, commonly used in alloys and industrial processes.
impure, hardened form of lead

Examples

Certain bullets are made from hard lead to withstand higher pressures.
Recycling batteries often yields hard lead containing various metallic impurities.
Because of its strength, hard lead is preferred in manufacturing certain pipes and casings.

2. NOUN

/ hɑrd lɛd / (AmE), / hɑːd lɛd / (BrE)
a lead alloy containing about 5% antimony, making it harder than pure lead
a lead alloy with antimony, harder than pure lead

Examples

The batteries use hard lead to improve durability and longevity.
The type of hard lead produced here contains precisely 5% antimony.
Compared to soft lead, hard lead is more suitable for bearing surfaces.
