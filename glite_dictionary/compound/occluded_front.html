occluded front

1. NOUN

/ əˈkluːdɪd frʌnt / (AmE), / əˈkluːdɪd frʌnt / (BrE)
(Meteorology) A type of weather front formed when a cold front overtakes a warm front, forcing the warm air mass above the cold air.
weather front where cold air overtakes warm air

Examples

An occluded front often brings clouds and prolonged periods of precipitation.
Meteorologists tracked the development of the occluded front as it moved across the region.
When a cold front catches up to a warm front, an occluded front is formed.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: warm front - the boundary of an advancing mass of warm air
  • Antonym: cold front - the boundary of an advancing mass of cold air

Etymology

From 'occlude' meaning to close up or block, in reference to the closure of the warm sector.
