cabin class

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæbɪn klæs / (AmE), / ˈkæbɪn klɑːs / (BrE)
a class of accommodation on a ship, train, or plane that is less expensive than first class
mid-level travel accommodation

Examples

The cruise offered special discounts for cabin class passengers this season.
Many travelers prefer cabin class for its balance of comfort and affordability.
On the train, cabin class seats were more spacious than those in economy.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: first class - the highest standard of accommodation or service on a ship, train, or plane
  • Degree Lower: economy class - the lowest class of accommodation on a plane, ship, or train
