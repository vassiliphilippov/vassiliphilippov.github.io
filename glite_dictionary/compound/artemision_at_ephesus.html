Artemision at Ephesus

1. PROPN

/ ˌɑːr.təˈmɪʒ.i.ən æt ɪˈfiː.səs / (AmE), / ˌɑː.tɪˈmɪz.i.ɒn æt ˈef.ɪ.səs / (BrE)
the large ancient temple dedicated to the Greek goddess Artemis, built in Ephesus beginning in 541 BC and destroyed in 262 AD
ancient temple of Artemis at Ephesus

Examples

Tourists still visit the site of the Artemision at Ephesus, despite the temple's destruction centuries ago.
The Artemision at Ephesus was once considered one of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World.
Archaeologists have uncovered new artifacts at the ruins of the Artemision at Ephesus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Temple of Artemis - another name for the Artemision at Ephesus, one of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World

Etymology

From Greek 'Artemision', the sanctuary of Artemis, and 'Ephesus', an ancient Greek city in Asia Minor.
