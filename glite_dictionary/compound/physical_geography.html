physical geography

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪzɪkəl dʒiˈɑɡrəfi / (AmE), / ˈfɪzɪkəl dʒiˈɒɡrəfi / (BrE)
the branch of geography concerned with the natural features and processes of the earth's surface, such as landforms, climate, soils, and vegetation
study of earth's physical features

Examples

She chose to major in physical geography because she was fascinated by landscapes and natural processes.
The textbook covers many aspects of physical geography, including mountains, rivers, and climate zones.
Fieldwork in physical geography often involves studying soil types and measuring erosion rates.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: human geography - the study of human societies in relation to their environment
  • Synonym: geomorphology - the study of landforms and the processes that shape them

Etymology

From 'physical' (relating to nature) and 'geography' (from Greek geōgraphia 'earth description').
