soil erosion

1. NOUN

/ sɔɪl ɪˈroʊ.ʒən / (AmE), / sɔɪl ɪˈrəʊ.ʒən / (BrE)
the gradual removal of the top layer of soil by natural forces such as water or wind
loss of soil by water or wind

Examples

Farmers use terraces to prevent soil erosion on steep hillsides.
Heavy rainfall can accelerate soil erosion in deforested areas.
Planting cover crops is an effective way to control soil erosion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: erosion - the gradual destruction or removal of something by natural forces

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɔɪl ɪˈroʊʒən / (AmE), / ˈsɔɪl ɪˈrəʊʒən / (BrE)
The gradual removal of topsoil by the action of wind, water, or other natural forces.
removal of topsoil by wind or water

Examples

Heavy rainfall can accelerate soil erosion on sloped farmland.
Planting trees helps prevent soil erosion by stabilizing the soil with their roots.
Farmers use contour plowing as a method to reduce soil erosion.

Synset Examples

Soil erosion is a major concern for farmers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: erosion - the process by which soil and rock are removed from the Earth's surface by wind or water flow
