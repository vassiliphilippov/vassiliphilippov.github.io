outside loop

1. NOUN

/ ˈaʊt.saɪd ˌluːp / (AmE), / ˈaʊtsaɪd ˌluːp / (BrE)
An aerobatic maneuver in which an aircraft makes a looping movement consisting of a dive followed by inverted flight and then a climb, returning to level flight.
aerobatic flight maneuver

Examples

The pilot performed an outside loop that made the crowd gasp in awe.
Attempting an outside loop requires great skill and a strong airframe.
After mastering the inside loop, she moved on to perfecting the outside loop in her routine.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inside loop - a loop maneuver with the pilot's head on the inside of the loop
