white book

1. NOUN

/ waɪt bʊk / (AmE), / waɪt bʊk / (BrE)
an official report or authoritative publication issued by a government, often bound in white covers
official government report (white cover)

Examples

The government released a white book detailing its new policies on healthcare reform.
Several historians often refer to the official white book as a key source for early 20th-century events.
Copies of the white book were distributed to members of parliament for review.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blue book - an official government report or directory, often with a blue cover
  • Synonym: green paper - a government document of discussion proposals, typically green-covered
  • Synonym: white paper - an authoritative report or guide, commonly issued by governments

Etymology

Patterned after similar terms for government documents named for their binding color, such as 'Blue Book' and 'Green Book'.
