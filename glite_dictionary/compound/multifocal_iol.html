multifocal IOL

1. NOUN

/ ˌmʌltiˈfoʊkəl aɪ oʊ ˈɛl / (AmE), / ˌmʌltɪˈfəʊkəl aɪ əʊ ˈɛl / (BrE)
a type of intraocular lens implant with multiple zones or rings for different focal distances, allowing simultaneous near and far vision after eye surgery
intraocular lens implant for near and far vision
medical

Examples

After cataract surgery, she received a multifocal IOL so she could see both near and far objects clearly.
Many patients choose a multifocal IOL to reduce their dependence on reading glasses.
The doctor explained the benefits and drawbacks of a multifocal IOL compared to a traditional monofocal lens.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: monofocal IOL - intraocular lens implant designed for a single focal distance

Etymology

IOL is an acronym for intraocular lens; 'multifocal' from Latin 'multi-' (many) + 'focus' (point).
