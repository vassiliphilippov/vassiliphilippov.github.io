Adam's ale

1. NOUN

/ ˈædəmz ˌeɪl / (AmE), / ˈædəmz ˌeɪl / (BrE)
humorous or poetic term for water, especially drinking water
humorous term for water
informal

Examples

After a long jog, all he wanted was a glass of Adam's ale to quench his thirst.
Some old writers would praise Adam's ale instead of wine or beer.
We toasted the hike's success with pure Adam's ale from the mountain spring.

Synset Examples

He enjoyed a cold glass of Adam's ale after the hike.
On hot days, nothing beats a refreshing Adam's ale.
We sat by the river, sipping on Adam's ale.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: water - the clear, colorless liquid essential for most plant and animal life

Etymology

Alludes to the biblical Adam, who had only water to drink in the Garden of Eden.
