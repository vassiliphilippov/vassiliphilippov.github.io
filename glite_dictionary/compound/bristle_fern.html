bristle fern

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrɪsəl fɝn / (AmE), / ˈbrɪsəl fɜːn / (BrE)
Any fern of the genus Trichomanes, typically having large, finely divided, often translucent fronds, and commonly growing on trees or mossy banks.
a fern of the genus Trichomanes

Examples

The bristle fern thrives in the humid understory of rainforests, often clinging to tree branches.
Botanists discovered several rare species of bristle fern along the shaded riverbank.
The delicate, translucent fronds of the bristle fern make it a favorite among plant enthusiasts.
