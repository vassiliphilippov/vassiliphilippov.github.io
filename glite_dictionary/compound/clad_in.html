clad in

1. ADJ

/ klæd ɪn / (AmE), / klæd ɪn / (BrE)
dressed in or covered with a particular type of clothing or material.
dressed in or covered with something

Examples

The knight was clad in shining armor as he rode into battle.
The hills were clad in a thick blanket of snow during winter.
Guests arrived clad in their finest evening wear for the celebration.

Synset Examples

She was clad in a beautiful gown for the gala.
The statue is clad in bronze, shining in the sun.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dressed in - wearing particular clothes
  • Synonym: covered in - having something covering the surface

Etymology

Past participle of 'clothe', from Old English 'clāthian'.
