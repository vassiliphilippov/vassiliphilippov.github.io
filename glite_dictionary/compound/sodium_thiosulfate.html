sodium thiosulfate

1. NOUN

/ ˈsoʊdiəm ˌθaɪ.oʊˈsʌl.feɪt / (AmE), / ˈsəʊdiəm ˌθaɪ.əˈsʌl.feɪt / (BrE)
a white, crystalline compound (Na2S2O3) used chiefly as a fixing agent in photographic processing and as an antidote to cyanide poisoning
chemical compound; photographic fixer

Examples

The photographer added sodium thiosulfate to the developing solution to fix the image.
Sodium thiosulfate can also be used as an antidote in cases of cyanide poisoning.
They bought sodium thiosulfate in bulk for use in the darkroom.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fixer - chemical used to fix photographic images

Etymology

From sodium + thio- (containing sulfur) + sulfate; denotes the presence of sulfur in the sulfate group.
