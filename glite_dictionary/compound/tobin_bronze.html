Tobin bronze

1. NOUN

/ ˈtoʊbɪn ˌbroʊnz / (AmE), / ˈtəʊbɪn ˌbrɒnz / (BrE)
a strong brass alloy containing tin and zinc, known for its resistance to seawater corrosion; used especially in marine applications
a seawater-resistant brass alloy containing tin

Examples

The propeller was made from Tobin bronze due to its excellent resistance to sea water.
Marine engineers often select Tobin bronze for fittings and fasteners exposed to harsh conditions.
The shipbuilder recommended using Tobin bronze instead of regular brass for underwater components.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brass - a yellow alloy of copper and zinc

Etymology

Named after George Tobin (1842–1932), engineer and metallurgist.
