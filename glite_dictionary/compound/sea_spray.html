sea spray

1. NOUN

/ si ˈspreɪ / (AmE), / siː ˈspreɪ / (BrE)
small droplets of water blown from the surface of the sea, typically by wind or breaking waves
droplets of water blown from the sea

Examples

The wind carried sea spray onto the deck of the boat.
Salt from the sea spray can damage metal surfaces over time.
She waded along the shore, enjoying the cool sea spray on her face.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spray - small droplets of liquid in air
