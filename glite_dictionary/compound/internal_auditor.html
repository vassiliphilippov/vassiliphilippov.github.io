internal auditor

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtɜrnəl ˈɔdɪtər / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɜːnl ˈɔːdɪtə / (BrE)
an employee of a company who examines its financial records, processes, and controls, and reports findings to management and the board of directors
employee who audits a company's records

Examples

The internal auditor discovered discrepancies during the routine review of expense claims.
Each year, the internal auditor presents a detailed report to the board of directors.
A company relies on the internal auditor to help identify areas where financial controls can be improved.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: external auditor - an independent auditor who audits a company's records from outside the organization
