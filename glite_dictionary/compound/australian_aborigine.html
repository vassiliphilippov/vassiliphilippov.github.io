Australian Aborigine

1. NOUN

/ əˈstreɪliən ˌæbəˈrɪdʒəni / (AmE), / ɒˈstreɪliən ˌæbəˈrɪdʒəni / (BrE)
a member of the indigenous people of Australia who lived there before the arrival of Europeans
indigenous Australian person

Examples

Many Australian Aborigines maintain strong cultural traditions passed down through generations.
The artwork of Australian Aborigines is celebrated worldwide for its unique style and symbolism.
Anthropologists have studied the ancient lifestyles of Australian Aborigines across various regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Aboriginal Australian - a member of any of the indigenous peoples of Australia
  • Antonym: European Australian - a person of European descent born or living in Australia

Etymology

From Latin ab origine (from the beginning; original inhabitants), used in English to refer to indigenous people of Australia.
