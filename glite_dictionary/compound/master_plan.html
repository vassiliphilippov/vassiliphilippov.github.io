master plan

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæstər plæn / (AmE), / ˈmɑːstə plæn / (BrE)
a comprehensive, long-term outline for the development or management of a project, area, or organization
long-term project outline

Examples

The city unveiled its master plan to redevelop the riverfront district over the next decade.
The architect presented a master plan for the university's future expansion.
A master plan is essential for coordinating all phases of such a large-scale project.

Synset Examples

the zoning board adopted a master plan for the new development

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blueprint - a detailed plan or design

2. NOUN

/ ˈmæstɚ ˌplæn / (AmE), / ˈmɑːstə ˌplæn / (BrE)
a comprehensive and strategic plan that outlines the overall approach or direction for achieving long-term goals
comprehensive strategic plan

Examples

The city council approved a master plan for new parks and infrastructure improvements.
Her master plan involved carefully coordinating multiple teams over several months.
After several revisions, the architect presented the final master plan for the campus expansion.

Synset Examples

His master plan was to expand the business internationally.
They revealed their master plan for the project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blueprint - a detailed technical plan or design
  • Synonym: scheme - a large-scale systematic plan or arrangement
  • Antonym: improvisation - the act of creating something spontaneously without planning
