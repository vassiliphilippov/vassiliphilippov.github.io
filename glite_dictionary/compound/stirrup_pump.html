stirrup pump

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɪrəp pʌmp / (AmE), / ˈstɪrəp pʌmp / (BrE)
a portable hand-operated pump, typically mounted on a footplate, used to spray water in fighting fires
hand-operated fire-fighting pump

Examples

The old farmhouse was equipped with a stirrup pump for emergencies.
During the blackout, residents used a stirrup pump to control the small fire.
In the museum, you can see a World War II stirrup pump on display.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hand pump - a manually operated pump

Etymology

Formed from 'stirrup' (due to the footplate resembling a stirrup) and 'pump'.
