family Chermidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli kɚˈmaɪdi / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli tʃɜːˈmiːdiː / (BrE)
A family of insects, commonly known as jumping plant lice, belonging to the order Hemiptera and known for feeding on plant sap.
family of jumping plant lice

Examples

Scientists discovered a new species in the family Chermidae on the island.
The family Chermidae includes several pests that affect fruit crops worldwide.
Members of the family Chermidae are identified by their jumping ability and sap-sucking mouthparts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jumping plant louse - insect that feeds on plant sap and can jump

Etymology

From modern Latin Chermidae, based on genus Chermes.
