greater pectoral muscle

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡreɪtər pɛkˈtɔrəl ˈmʌsəl / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪtə pɛkˈtɔːrəl ˈmʌsəl / (BrE)
a large chest muscle that brings the arm toward the body and rotates it inward
major chest muscle for arm movement
medical

Examples

Physical therapy after a shoulder injury often targets the greater pectoral muscle to restore strength and mobility.
The greater pectoral muscle is heavily engaged during push-ups and bench press exercises.
In anatomical diagrams, the greater pectoral muscle is usually shown covering the front of the upper chest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pectoralis major - the main large muscle of the chest
  • Degree Lower: lesser pectoral muscle - the smaller chest muscle beneath the pectoralis major

Etymology

From Latin *pectoralis* (of the chest) and Latin *musculus* (muscle).
