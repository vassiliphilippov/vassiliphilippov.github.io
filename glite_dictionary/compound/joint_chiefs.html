Joint Chiefs

1. PROPN

/ dʒɔɪnt tʃifs / (AmE), / dʒɔɪnt tʃiːfs / (BrE)
the group of senior military leaders who advise the President of the United States on military matters, formally known as the Joint Chiefs of Staff
US senior military advisory group
american

Examples

The Joint Chiefs met with the President to discuss potential military operations.
Testifying before Congress, the Joint Chiefs emphasized the importance of defense readiness.
Decisions made by the Joint Chiefs carry significant weight in shaping U.S. military policy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Joint Chiefs of Staff - the full name of the group of US senior military leaders
