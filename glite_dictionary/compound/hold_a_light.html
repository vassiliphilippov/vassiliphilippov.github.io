to hold a light

1. VERB

/ hoʊld ə laɪt / (AmE), / həʊld ə laɪt / (BrE)
to assist someone by physically holding a source of illumination so they can perform a task more easily
assist by holding a light source

Examples

Could you hold a light over here while I change this tire?
She asked her brother to hold a light so she could read the tiny print.
During the blackout, Tom had to hold a light while his father fixed the fuse.

Synset Examples

Can you hold a light while I fix the engine?
Please hold a light so I can see better.
I need someone to hold a light for the painting.
