dray horse

1. NOUN

/ dreɪ hɔrs / (AmE), / dreɪ hɔːs / (BrE)
A large, strong horse bred or used for pulling heavy loads, especially carts or wagons.
horse for pulling heavy loads

Examples

The farmer relied on his dray horse to haul timber from the woods.
In the city’s past, dray horses were used to deliver barrels of beer to local pubs.
A dray horse needs to be strong and steady to manage such heavy loads.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: draft horse - horse bred for heavy harness work

Etymology

From 'dray' (a strong cart or wagon for heavy loads) and 'horse'.
