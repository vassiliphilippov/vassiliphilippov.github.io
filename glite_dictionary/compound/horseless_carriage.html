2. NOUN

/ ˈhɔrsləs ˈkærɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈhɔːsləs ˈkærɪdʒ / (BrE)
A term used in the late 19th and early 20th centuries to describe an early automobile, especially before the term 'car' became standard.
early term for automobile
archaic

Examples

The museum features a beautifully restored horseless carriage from 1895.
In the early 1900s, many people were skeptical of the new horseless carriage.
He dreamed of inventing a horseless carriage that would revolutionize travel.

Synset Examples

They marveled at the horseless carriage in the museum.
The horseless carriage was the start of modern vehicles.
The invention of the horseless carriage changed transportation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: automobile - a road vehicle powered by an engine, typically four-wheeled
  • Synonym: car - a road vehicle for transporting passengers

Etymology

Formed from 'horseless' (without a horse) and 'carriage' (a vehicle for people), describing the earliest motor vehicles.
