zinc vitriol

1. NOUN

/ zɪŋk ˈvɪtriəl / (AmE), / zɪŋk ˈvɪtrɪəl / (BrE)
a colorless, water-soluble powder, also known as zinc sulfate, used as a mordant, wood preservative, or for electrodeposition of zinc
zinc sulfate used in industry

Examples

The wood was treated with zinc vitriol to protect it from decay.
In textile dyeing, zinc vitriol acts as an effective mordant.
They used zinc vitriol in the electrodeposition process at the factory.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zinc sulfate - a colorless crystalline sulfate of zinc, also called white vitriol

Etymology

'Vitriol' derives from Latin 'vitriolum' (sulphuric acid), historically applied to sulfate salts.
