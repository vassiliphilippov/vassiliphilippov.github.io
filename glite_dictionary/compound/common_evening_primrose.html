common evening primrose

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmən ˈivnɪŋ ˈprɪmˌroʊz / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən ˈiːvnɪŋ ˈprɪm.rəʊz / (BrE)
a coarse biennial plant native to eastern North America, with yellow flowers that open in the evening; now also naturalized in Europe
yellow-flowered evening-blooming plant

Examples

The common evening primrose blooms at dusk, attracting moths with its fragrant yellow blossoms.
Researchers are studying the seeds of the common evening primrose for their oil, which is rich in essential fatty acids.
Gardeners appreciate the resilience of common evening primrose in poor, sandy soils.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: evening primrose - plant of the genus Oenothera with yellow evening-opening flowers
