oleic acid

1. NOUN

/ oʊˈleɪɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / əʊˈliːɪk ˈæsɪd / (BrE)
a colorless, oily unsaturated fatty acid found in olive oil and other fats, used in making soaps, cosmetics, ointments, and lubricants
an unsaturated fatty acid in olive oil and fats

Examples

Olive oil is rich in oleic acid, which is beneficial for heart health.
Manufacturers commonly use oleic acid in the production of soaps and cosmetics.
Research shows that oleic acid can help reduce inflammation in certain skin conditions.

Etymology

From Latin 'oleum' (oil), reflecting its presence in natural oils.
