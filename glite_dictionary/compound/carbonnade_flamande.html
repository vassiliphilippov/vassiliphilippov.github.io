carbonnade flamande

1. NOUN

/ kɑr.bəˌnɑd flɑːˈmɑːnd / (AmE), / kaː.bɔːˌnad flaːˈmɑːnd / (BrE)
a traditional Flemish dish of beef stewed in beer, typically seasoned with garlic and served with boiled potatoes
Flemish beef stew cooked in beer

Examples

The chef prepared a delicious carbonnade flamande using locally brewed ale.
For dinner, we enjoyed a classic carbonnade flamande with crusty bread on the side.
She ordered carbonnade flamande after reading about the famous Flemish stew in a travel guide.

Etymology

From French 'carbonnade flamande', meaning 'Flemish carbonnade'; 'carbonnade' itself from French, related to 'charcoal-grilled'.
