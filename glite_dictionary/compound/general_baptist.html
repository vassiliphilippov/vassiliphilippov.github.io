General Baptist

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɛnɚl ˈbæptɪst / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛn(ə)rəl ˈbæptɪst / (BrE)
a member of a group of Baptist congregations that follow the teachings of Jacobus Arminius, especially rejecting the doctrine of strict predestination
member of a Baptist group rejecting strict predestination

Examples

Many General Baptists emphasized free will and universal salvation in their sermons.
The church was founded in the 17th century by English General Baptists seeking religious freedom.
Unlike Particular Baptists, General Baptists believed that Christ died for all people.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Particular Baptist - member of a Baptist group believing in particular (limited) atonement and strict predestination

Etymology

From 'general' (unlimited atonement) plus 'Baptist'; refers to followers of Arminian doctrine as opposed to particular (Calvinist) Baptists.
