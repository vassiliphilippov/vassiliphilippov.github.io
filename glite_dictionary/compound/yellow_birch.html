yellow birch

1. NOUN

/ ˈjɛloʊ bɜrtʃ / (AmE), / ˈjɛləʊ bɜːtʃ / (BrE)
a North American tree with thin, lustrous yellow or gray bark, known for its hardwood and distinctive appearance
North American tree with yellowish bark

Examples

The forest was full of yellow birch and sugar maple trees.
Furniture made from yellow birch is valued for its durability and attractive grain.
In autumn, the leaves of the yellow birch turn a brilliant golden color.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: birch - a tree of the genus Betula with thin bark and slender branches
