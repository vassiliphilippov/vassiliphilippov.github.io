herb Robert

1. NOUN

/ hɜrb ˈrɑbərt / (AmE), / hɜːb ˈrɒbət / (BrE)
a low, spreading plant of the geranium family with sticky stems and small reddish-purple flowers, found throughout the Northern Hemisphere
low geranium with reddish-purple flowers

Examples

The herb Robert thrives in shady garden corners and produces bright pink flowers in early summer.
I found a patch of herb Robert growing along the path behind the old church.
Although herb Robert is sometimes considered a weed, it is valued in some herbal traditions.

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'herba Roberti', named after St. Robert of Molesme or possibly Robert, Duke of Normandy.

3. NOUN

/ hɜrb ˈrɑbɚt / (AmE), / hɜːb ˈrɒbət / (BrE)
a low, sticky herb with small reddish-purple flowers, found widely in the Northern Hemisphere; also known as Geranium robertianum.
a sticky wildflower (Geranium robertianum)

Examples

We found herb Robert growing along the edge of the woodland trail.
The distinctive reddish stems of herb Robert make it easy to identify in the garden.
Some gardeners consider herb Robert a weed, but its flowers attract pollinators.

Etymology

Named after Saint Robert of Molesme, a French abbot; possibly associated with its reputed medicinal uses.
