order Protura

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdər proʊˈtjʊrə / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə prəʊˈtjʊərə / (BrE)
A small order of minute, wingless, primitive arthropods, commonly known as telsontails, found in soil and leaf litter.
an order of tiny, wingless arthropods
medical

Examples

Scientists discovered several new species in the order Protura while examining forest soil samples.
Members of the order Protura are so small and delicate that they are often overlooked in ecological studies.
The biology textbook included a detailed section on the unique characteristics of the order Protura.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin *Protura*, with 'pro-' meaning 'first' and '-ura' meaning 'tail'.
