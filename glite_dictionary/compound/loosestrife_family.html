loosestrife family

1. NOUN

/ ˈluːsˌstraɪf ˈfæməli / (AmE), / ˈluːsˌstraɪf ˈfæmɪli / (BrE)
a family of herbs, shrubs, and small trees with pink or purple flowers, scientifically known as Lythraceae
plant family with pink or purple flowers

Examples

The loosestrife family includes many ornamental plants prized for their vibrant flowers.
Several wetland species within the loosestrife family are important for local ecosystems.
Lythrum salicaria, known as purple loosestrife, is a notorious member of the loosestrife family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Lythraceae - the botanical (scientific) name for the loosestrife family

Etymology

loosestrife from Middle English *losstrif*, family Lythraceae from the genus *Lythrum* (Greek *lythron*, gore, blood, for the color of the flowers).
