musculophrenic vein

1. NOUN

/ ˌmʌs.kjə.loʊˈfrɛn.ɪk veɪn / (AmE), / ˌmʌs.kjʊ.ləʊˈfrɛn.ɪk veɪn / (BrE)
a vein that drains blood from the upper abdominal wall and lower intercostal spaces into the internal thoracic vein
vein draining upper abdomen and lower chest wall
medical

Examples

During the surgery, the musculophrenic vein was carefully identified to prevent excessive bleeding.
The musculophrenic vein receives blood from the lower intercostal spaces before joining the internal thoracic vein.
Damage to the musculophrenic vein can compromise blood drainage in the upper abdominal wall.

Etymology

From Latin 'musculus' (muscle) and 'phrenicus' (of the diaphragm).
