hyperhygienist tendency

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪpɚhaɪˈdʒinɪst ˈtɛndənsi / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪpəhʌɪˈdʒiːnɪst ˈtɛndənsi / (BrE)
A behavioral trait characterized by excessive concern for cleanliness and hygiene.
excessive cleanliness habit

Examples

Her hyperhygienist tendency led her to sanitize every surface in her home daily.
The doctor recognized a hyperhygienist tendency in patients who washed their hands dozens of times a day.
Because of his hyperhygienist tendency, he avoided using public transportation altogether.

Synset Examples

His hyperhygienist tendencies made him avoid crowded places.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: obsessive-compulsive disorder - a mental disorder involving recurrent and persistent thoughts and repetitive behaviors
  • Degree Lower: cleanliness - the quality of being clean or keeping clean

Etymology

Formed from 'hyper-' (over, excessive) + 'hygienist' (advocate or practitioner of hygiene) + 'tendency'.
