chalk and talk

1. NOUN

/ tʃɔk ənd tɔk / (AmE), / tʃɔːk ən tɔːk / (BrE)
A teaching method characterized by formal lectures with little or no interaction or practical activity.
traditional lecture-based teaching method

Examples

The new curriculum moves away from chalk and talk towards more interactive methods.
While some students benefit from chalk and talk, others prefer hands-on activities.
The professor's chalk and talk approach left little room for discussion.

Synset Examples

The lesson consisted of mostly chalk and talk sessions.
The lecture was all chalk and talk, with no interaction.
He prefers chalk and talk to hands-on learning.
