Ionian order

1. NOUN

/ aɪˈoʊniən ˈɔrdər / (AmE), / aɪˈəʊniən ˈɔːdə / (BrE)
an architectural style of classical Greek origin, characterized by columns with capitals decorated with spiral scrolls (volutes)
classical Greek architectural style with volute capitals

Examples

The columns in the ancient temple were designed in the Ionian order, featuring elegant spiral scrolls.
Many neoclassical buildings in Europe incorporate the Ionian order in their facades.
The architect chose the Ionian order for the library's portico to create a distinguished look.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Doric order - the oldest and simplest Greek architectural order
  • Synonym: Corinthian order - the most ornate Greek architectural order

Etymology

From Ionia, an ancient region in western Asia Minor; 'order' from Latin ordo (row, rank).
