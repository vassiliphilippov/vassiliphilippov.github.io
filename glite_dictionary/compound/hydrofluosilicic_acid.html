hydrofluosilicic acid

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪdroʊˌfluːəˈsɪlɪsɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪdrəʊˌfluːəˈsɪlɪsɪk ˈasɪd / (BrE)
an unstable, poisonous, and corrosive acid known chiefly through its salts; used mainly in water fluoridation and chemical manufacturing
a poisonous and corrosive acid used mainly in industry
medical

Examples

Industrial plants use hydrofluosilicic acid during the process of fluoridating drinking water.
Because hydrofluosilicic acid is highly corrosive, workers must wear protective equipment when handling it.
The laboratory study focused on the chemical reactions of hydrofluosilicic acid with various metals.

Etymology

From hydrogen, fluoric (related to fluorine), and silicic (related to silicon) acid.
