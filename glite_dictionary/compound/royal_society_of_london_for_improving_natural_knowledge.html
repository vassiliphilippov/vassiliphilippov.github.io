Royal Society of London for Improving Natural Knowledge

1. PROPN

/ ˈrɔɪəl səˈsaɪəti əv ˈlʌndən fər ɪmˈpruvɪŋ ˈnætʃrəl ˈnɑlɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈrɔɪəl səˈsaɪəti əv ˈlʌndən fə ɪmˈpruːvɪŋ ˈnætʃrəl ˈnɒlɪdʒ / (BrE)
A prestigious English scientific society founded in 1660 and given a royal charter in 1662, dedicated to promoting scientific knowledge.
historic English scientific society

Examples

Isaac Newton was a fellow of the Royal Society of London for Improving Natural Knowledge in the late 17th century.
The Royal Society of London for Improving Natural Knowledge has played a pivotal role in the advancement of science.
Each year, the Royal Society of London for Improving Natural Knowledge elects new fellows who have made significant scientific contributions.

Etymology

Name adopted in England following its foundation in 1660, with 'Royal Society' indicating its royal charter and prestige.
