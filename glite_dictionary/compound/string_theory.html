string theory

1. NOUN

/ ˈstrɪŋ ˌθɪəri / (AmE), / ˈstrɪŋ ˌθɪəri / (BrE)
A theoretical framework in particle physics proposing that subatomic particles are actually one-dimensional strings whose vibrations determine their properties.
theory that subatomic particles are one-dimensional strings

Examples

Many physicists believe string theory may unify all the fundamental forces of nature.
The mathematics of string theory is highly complex and abstract.
He gave a lecture on how string theory could explain the existence of multiple dimensions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quantum theory - the theory describing the behavior of matter and energy on the atomic and subatomic levels
  • Synonym: particle physics - the branch of physics dealing with subatomic particles

Etymology

From 'string' (referring to the one-dimensional objects in the theory) and 'theory'.
