Sierra Madre Occidental

1. PROPN

/ siˌɛrə ˈmɑdreɪ ˌɑksɪˈdɛntəl / (AmE), / siˌɛərə ˈmɑːdreɪ ˌɒksɪˈdɛntl̩ / (BrE)
a mountain range in northwestern Mexico, running parallel to the Pacific coast from the U.S. border south through several states
mountain range in northwestern Mexico

Examples

The Sierra Madre Occidental is known for its rugged terrain and rich biodiversity.
Many rivers in Mexico originate in the Sierra Madre Occidental before flowing to the Pacific Ocean.
The copper canyon system is one of the most famous features of the Sierra Madre Occidental.

Etymology

From Spanish, literally 'Western Mother Mountain Range'.
