Himalayan lilac

1. NOUN

/ hɪˈmeɪliən ˈlaɪlæk / (AmE), / hɪˈmaljən ˈlɪlək / (BrE)
a robust upright shrub native to the mountains of northern India, with oblong-elliptic leaves and pale lilac or white malodorous flowers
shrubs native to northern India, with lilac or white flowers

Examples

The Himalayan lilac is prized in some gardens for its unusual flowers, despite their strong scent.
A Himalayan lilac can grow quite tall and provide dense cover for wildlife in mountainous areas.
The malodorous blossoms of the Himalayan lilac make it less popular as an ornamental shrub.
