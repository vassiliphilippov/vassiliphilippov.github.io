verbal agreement

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɝːbəl əˈɡriːmənt / (AmE), / ˈvɜːbəl əˈɡriːmənt / (BrE)
an agreement that is made by spoken words rather than in writing
agreement made by spoken words

Examples

The two companies reached a verbal agreement during the meeting, but nothing was signed.
We had a verbal agreement to share the profits equally, which later caused confusion.
Relying on a verbal agreement can be risky if disputes arise.

Synset Examples

They had a verbal agreement to meet every week.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: written agreement - an agreement that is set down in writing
