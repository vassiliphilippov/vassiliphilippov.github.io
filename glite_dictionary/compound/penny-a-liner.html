penny-a-liner

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛni ə ˈlaɪnər / (AmE), / ˌpɛni ə ˈlaɪnə / (BrE)
a writer who produces cheap or low-quality written work, often for little pay
writer of cheap or low-quality work
archaic

Examples

His reputation as a penny-a-liner made it difficult for him to find serious literary work.
Few respected her as more than a penny-a-liner churning out forgettable articles.
Though talented, he resorted to becoming a penny-a-liner to pay the bills.

Synset Examples

The author was known for his penny-a-liner style.
Her poetry has become a penny-a-liner in local papers.
He wrote a penny-a-liner for that cheap magazine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hack writer - a writer who produces dull, unoriginal, or low-quality work for money
  • Antonym: literary - concerning literature or writers of high quality

Etymology

From 'penny' (a small coin) + 'a liner' (per line), originally referring to writers paid a penny per line.
