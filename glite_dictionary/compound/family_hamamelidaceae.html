family Hamamelidaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌhæməˌmilɪˈdeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / ˌhæməˌmiːlɪˈdeɪsɪˌiː / (BrE)
a family of flowering plants that includes the witch-hazels and related genera such as Hamamelis, Corylopsis, Fothergilla, Liquidambar, and Parrotia
a plant family including witch-hazels and related genera

Examples

The family Hamamelidaceae includes about 27 genera of flowering plants.
Liquidambar styraciflua, also known as sweetgum, is a notable member of the family Hamamelidaceae.
Botanists continue to study the evolutionary relationships within the family Hamamelidaceae.

Etymology

From modern Latin Hamamelidaceae, from Hamamelis (the type genus) + -aceae (family suffix in botanical Latin).
