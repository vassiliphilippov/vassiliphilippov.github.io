Atlas Mountains

1. PROPN

/ ˈætləs ˈmaʊntənz / (AmE), / ˈætləs ˈmaʊntɪnz / (BrE)
a mountain range in northern Africa stretching from southwestern Morocco to northern Tunisia, between the Mediterranean Sea and the Sahara Desert
mountain range in northern Africa

Examples

The Atlas Mountains create a natural barrier between the Mediterranean coast and the Sahara Desert.
Many Berber villages are located in the foothills of the Atlas Mountains.
Snow can be seen on the peaks of the Atlas Mountains during the winter months.

Etymology

Named after Atlas, a Titan in Greek mythology, believed to support the sky.
