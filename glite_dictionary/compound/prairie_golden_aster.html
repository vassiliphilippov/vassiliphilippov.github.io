prairie golden aster

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɛri ˈɡoʊldən ˈæstər / (AmE), / ˈprɛəri ˈɡəʊldən ˈæstə / (BrE)
a perennial plant with hairy stems and branched clusters of yellow flower heads, found in dry areas across Canada and the western United States
yellow-flowered perennial plant of dry North America

Examples

In late summer, the fields were sprinkled with prairie golden aster in full bloom.
Botanists surveyed the area and identified several patches of prairie golden aster.
The prairie golden aster thrives in dry, sandy soils across the Midwest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: golden aster - any of several plants with yellow ray flowers in the aster family
