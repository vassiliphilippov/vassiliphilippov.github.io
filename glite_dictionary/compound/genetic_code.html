genetic code

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˈnɛtɪk koʊd / (AmE), / dʒɪˈnɛtɪk kəʊd / (BrE)
the sequence of nucleotides in DNA or RNA that determines the genetic information carried by living cells and directs the synthesis of proteins
order of DNA/RNA nucleotides encoding genetic information

Examples

Scientists have deciphered the genetic code of several different species.
Changes in the genetic code can lead to hereditary diseases.
Understanding the genetic code is essential for advancements in biotechnology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: DNA - molecule carrying genetic instructions in living organisms
  • Synonym: genome - the full set of genetic material in an organism

Etymology

From 'genetic' (relating to genes) and 'code' (system of symbols conveying information).
