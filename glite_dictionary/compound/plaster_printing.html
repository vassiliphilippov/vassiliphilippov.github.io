plaster printing

1. NOUN

/ ˈplæstər ˈprɪntɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈplɑːstə ˈprɪntɪŋ / (BrE)
a printmaking technique in which an intaglio plate is converted to a relief print by creating a plaster cast, making previously incised lines stand out above the surface
printmaking method using plaster casts

Examples

The artist demonstrated plaster printing by casting a mold from the intaglio plate before producing several prints.
Because plaster printing converts incised lines to relief, it allows for unique textural effects not possible with traditional methods.
Her latest exhibit features works created through plaster printing, showcasing raised surfaces that catch the light uniquely.

Synset Examples

Plaster printing has been around for quite a while, but unfortunately I don't have knowledge of the artist who actually invented the process.
