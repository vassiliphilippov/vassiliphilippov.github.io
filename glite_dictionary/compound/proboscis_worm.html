proboscis worm

1. NOUN

/ proʊˈbɑːsɪs wɜrm / (AmE), / prəˈbɒsɪs wɜːm / (BrE)
A soft, unsegmented marine worm with a long, threadlike proboscis, capable of stretching and contracting; member of the phylum Nemertea.
soft unsegmented marine worm (Nemertea)

Examples

The proboscis worm can extend its long proboscis to capture prey hidden in the sand.
Marine biologists discovered a new species of proboscis worm near the coral reef.
The proboscis worm is known for its remarkable ability to stretch many times its own body length.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nemertean - member of the phylum Nemertea, also known as proboscis worms

Etymology

From Latin 'proboscis' (trunk, snout), referring to the worm's distinctive proboscis.
