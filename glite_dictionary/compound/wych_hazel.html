wych hazel

1. NOUN

/ wɪtʃ ˈheɪzəl / (AmE), / wɪtʃ ˈheɪzəl / (BrE)
a shrub or small tree of the genus Hamamelis, whose bark yields an astringent lotion used medicinally
shrub or tree (Hamamelis); source of astringent

Examples

She applied an extract of wych hazel to soothe her irritated skin.
The garden's wych hazel bloomed with yellow flowers in early spring.
Some herbal remedies include wych hazel for its natural astringent properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: witch hazel - shrub of the genus Hamamelis, used medicinally

Etymology

From Old English *wice* (pliant, flexible) and hazel, referring to similarity with the hazel tree.

2. NOUN

/ wɪtʃ ˈheɪzəl / (AmE), / wɪtʃ ˈheɪzəl / (BrE)
a shrub or small tree of the genus Hamamelis, whose bark is used to make an astringent lotion
shrub or tree of genus Hamamelis

Examples

Traditional remedies use wych hazel extract to ease skin irritation and inflammation.
The garden features several wych hazel bushes with distinctive yellow flowers.
She applied an astringent lotion made from wych hazel to her face.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: witch hazel - another name for wych hazel, a shrub of genus Hamamelis

Etymology

From Middle English *wiche* (pliant, flexible) and 'hazel'; though not a true hazel.
