Morlett's crocodile

1. NOUN

/ mɔrˈlɛts ˈkrɑkəˌdaɪl / (AmE), / mɔːˈlɛts ˈkrɒkədaɪl / (BrE)
a medium-sized species of crocodile (Crocodylus moreletii) native to freshwater habitats in Mexico, Belize, and Guatemala
a Central American crocodile species

Examples

The Morlett's crocodile inhabits rivers and lakes across parts of Central America.
Researchers are studying the nesting habits of the Morlett's crocodile to aid conservation efforts.
A juvenile Morlett's crocodile was spotted sunning itself on the riverbank.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crocodile - large aquatic reptile of the family Crocodylidae

Etymology

Named after the French naturalist Pierre Marie Arthur Morelet, who first described the species in 1850.
