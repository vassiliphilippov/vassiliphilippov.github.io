grobian behavior

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡroʊbiən bɪˈheɪvjər / (AmE), / ˈɡrəʊbɪən bɪˈheɪvjə / (BrE)
rude or boorish behavior; conduct showing lack of manners or refinement
rude or boorish conduct

Examples

At dinner, his grobian behavior shocked the other guests and ruined the mood.
Workplaces usually do not tolerate any form of grobian behavior.
Her grobian behavior during the ceremony drew disapproving looks from everyone present.

Synset Examples

The grobian attitude in the meeting was hard to ignore.
His grobian behavior at the party embarrassed everyone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boorish - rude and insensitive
  • Antonym: polite behavior - respectful and considerate conduct

Etymology

From 'grobian', originally a mock patron saint of coarse manners in German literature, from German 'Grobian' (coarse, boor).
