Euclid's first axiom

1. NOUN

/ ˈjuːklɪdz ˈfɝst ˈæksiəm / (AmE), / ˈjuːklɪdz ˈfɜːst ˈæksiəm / (BrE)
the geometric principle stating that a straight line can be drawn between any two points
geometric principle about drawing lines

Examples

Euclid's first axiom is fundamental to the study of geometry.
Many problems in classical geometry are based on Euclid's first axiom.
Without Euclid's first axiom, our understanding of straight lines would be very different.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: axiom - a statement that is accepted as true without proof

Etymology

Named after Euclid, a Greek mathematician, from Greek 'Eukleidēs'.
