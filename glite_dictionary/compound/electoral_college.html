electoral college

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈlɛktərəl ˈkɑlɪdʒ / (AmE), / ɪˈlɛktərəl ˈkɒlɪdʒ / (BrE)
the body of electors chosen to formally elect the president and vice president of the United States.
body of US presidential electors

Examples

The electoral college ultimately decided the outcome of the presidential race.
Many Americans debate the fairness of the electoral college system.
Each state has a set number of electoral college votes based on its population.
