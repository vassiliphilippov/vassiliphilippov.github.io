to find closure

1. VERB

/ faɪnd ˈkloʊʒɚ / (AmE), / faɪnd ˈkləʊʒə / (BrE)
to achieve a sense of emotional resolution, especially after a difficult or traumatic experience
achieve emotional resolution after distress

Examples

After years of searching for answers, he was finally able to find closure.
Many people attend therapy to find closure about past trauma.
Talking with her ex-partner helped her find closure and move forward.

Synset Examples

She finally found closure after the breakup.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to get over - recover from an emotional shock or disappointment
  • Synonym: to move on - to progress emotionally after a difficult event
