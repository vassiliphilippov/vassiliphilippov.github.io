western meadowlark

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɛstərn ˈmɛd̬oʊˌlɑrk / (AmE), / ˈwɛstən ˈmɛdəʊˌlɑːk / (BrE)
a medium-sized songbird (Sturnella neglecta) native to western North America, known for its bright yellow underparts and melodic song
North American songbird species

Examples

The western meadowlark is celebrated as the state bird of several US states, including Kansas and Nebraska.
We spotted a western meadowlark perched on a fence post, singing its distinctive tune.
The bright yellow chest of the western meadowlark makes it easy to identify in open grasslands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meadowlark - a songbird of the genus Sturnella
