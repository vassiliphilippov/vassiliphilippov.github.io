elaidic acid

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌleɪɪdɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / ɪˌleɪɪdɪk ˈasɪd / (BrE)
a monounsaturated trans fatty acid, isomeric with oleic acid, commonly found in hydrogenated oils and processed foods
a trans fatty acid found in processed foods

Examples

Many processed foods contain elaidic acid due to the use of partially hydrogenated oils.
Researchers study the health effects of elaidic acid on cardiovascular disease.
Margarine is a common source of elaidic acid in modern diets.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: oleic acid - a cis monounsaturated fatty acid found in many animal and vegetable fats

Etymology

From Greek elaion (oil), with the suffix -ic to denote an acid.
