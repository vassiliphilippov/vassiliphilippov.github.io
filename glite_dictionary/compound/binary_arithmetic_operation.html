binary arithmetic operation

1. NOUN

/ ˈbaɪ.nɛri ərɪθˈmɛtɪk ˌɑpəˈreɪʃn̩ / (AmE), / ˈbʌɪ.nəri ærɪθˈmɛtɪk ˌɒpəˈreɪʃn̩ / (BrE)
a mathematical operation involving two operands, where each operand and the result can have one of two possible values, following the rules of Boolean algebra
Boolean operation with two operands

Examples

The AND gate is a classic example of a binary arithmetic operation in digital logic circuits.
In programming, a binary arithmetic operation takes two boolean inputs and produces a boolean output.
Learning about the OR binary arithmetic operation is fundamental for understanding digital electronics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Boolean operation - an operation on logical values that follows the rules of Boolean algebra
