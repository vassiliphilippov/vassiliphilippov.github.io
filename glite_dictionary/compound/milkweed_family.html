milkweed family

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪlkwid ˈfæməli / (AmE), / ˈmɪlkwiːd ˈfæməli / (BrE)
a family of flowering herbs and shrubs (Asclepiadaceae), most producing a milky sap, widely distributed and including the milkweeds
family of plants with milky sap

Examples

Many butterflies lay their eggs exclusively on plants in the milkweed family.
The unique flowers of the milkweed family attract a variety of pollinators.
Members of the milkweed family are known for their ability to thrive in disturbed soils.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Asclepiadaceae - the botanical name for the milkweed family

Etymology

Named after the genus Asclepias (milkweeds) and distinguished by the characteristic milky sap.
