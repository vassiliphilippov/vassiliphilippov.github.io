European gallinule

1. NOUN

/ jʊˈroʊpiən ˈɡælɪˌnjuːl / (AmE), / jʊəˈrəʊpiən ˈɡælɪˌnjuːl / (BrE)
A purple gallinule native to southern Europe, a water bird known for its bright plumage and long toes.
purple gallinule of southern Europe

Examples

Birdwatchers in Spain were thrilled to spot a European gallinule feeding near the reeds.
The European gallinule is admired for its striking purple and blue feathers.
A conservation program has helped restore the population of the European gallinule in wetland habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: purple gallinule - a species of brightly colored water bird in the rail family
