monosyllabic word

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɑːnoʊsɪˈlæbɪk wɝːd / (AmE), / ˌmɒnəʊsɪˈlæbɪk wɜːd / (BrE)
a word consisting of only one syllable
word with one syllable

Examples

The word cat is a common example of a monosyllabic word.
Children often learn monosyllabic words before moving on to longer ones.
In poetry, the use of monosyllabic words can create a rhythmic simplicity.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: polysyllabic word - a word with more than one syllable

Etymology

From monosyllabic (from Greek 'monos' meaning 'single' and 'syllabē' meaning 'syllable') + word.
