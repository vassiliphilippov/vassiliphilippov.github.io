genus Phellodendron

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌfɛl.oʊˈdɛn.drən / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌfɛl.əʊˈdɛn.drən / (BrE)
A small genus of aromatic deciduous trees native to East Asia, often with thick corky bark.
genus of aromatic East Asian trees

Examples

The genus Phellodendron includes trees valued for their corky bark and ornamental features.
Botanists have identified several species within the genus Phellodendron across East Asia.
Studies focus on the medicinal properties of the genus Phellodendron and its traditional uses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Phellodendron - any tree of the genus Phellodendron

Etymology

From New Latin 'Phellodendron', from Greek 'phellos' (cork) and 'dendron' (tree).
