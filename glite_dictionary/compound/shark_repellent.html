shark repellent

1. NOUN

/ ʃɑrk rɪˈpɛl.ənt / (AmE), / ʃɑːk rɪˈpel.ənt / (BrE)
a measure adopted by a company to protect itself from an unwanted takeover, especially by making such attempts more difficult or less attractive to the acquiring party
strategy to deter hostile takeover

Examples

The board adopted a shark repellent to deter aggressive takeover bids from competitors.
Implementing a shark repellent can help a company maintain independence during turbulent market conditions.
Some investors argue that a shark repellent can reduce shareholder value by discouraging potential suitors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poison pill - a defensive strategy used by companies to prevent or discourage hostile takeovers
  • Antonym: hostile takeover - the acquisition of a company against the wishes of its management

Etymology

Formed by analogy to 'shark repellent' (substance deterring sharks), applied figuratively in business to 'corporate sharks.'
