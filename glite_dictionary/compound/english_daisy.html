English daisy

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ ˈdeɪzi / (AmE), / ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ ˈdeɪzi / (BrE)
A low-growing Eurasian plant (Bellis perennis) with yellow central disc flowers and pinkish-white outer ray flowers, often found in lawns and meadows.
a small Eurasian daisy with yellow center and pinkish-white petals

Examples

A cluster of English daisy blooms brightened up the garden path.
She picked an English daisy and placed it delicately in her hair.
English daisy is commonly seen in lawns and grassy meadows throughout Europe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: daisy - a small flower with a yellow center and white petals
