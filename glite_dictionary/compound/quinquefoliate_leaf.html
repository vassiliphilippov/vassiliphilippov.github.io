quinquefoliate leaf

1. NOUN

/ kwɪŋ.kwɪˈfoʊ.li.eɪt liːf / (AmE), / kwɪŋ.kwɪˈfəʊ.li.eɪt liːf / (BrE)
a compound leaf divided into five distinct leaflets
leaf with five leaflets
medical

Examples

The rose bush in the garden has a quinquefoliate leaf structure.
Each quinquefoliate leaf on this tree is made up of five smaller leaflets.
Botanists can often identify species by observing a quinquefoliate leaf pattern.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: trifoliate leaf - a compound leaf with three leaflets
  • Degree Lower: bifoliate leaf - a compound leaf with two leaflets
  • Degree Higher: multifoliate leaf - a compound leaf with more than five leaflets

Etymology

From Latin 'quinque' (five) and 'folium' (leaf).
