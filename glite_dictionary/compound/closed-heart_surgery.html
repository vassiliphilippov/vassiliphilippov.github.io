closed-heart surgery

1. NOUN

/ ˌkloʊzd ˈhɑrt ˌsɝːdʒəri / (AmE), / ˌkləʊzd ˈhɑːt ˌsɜːdʒəri / (BrE)
A type of heart surgery performed through a small incision without opening the chest cavity.
heart surgery without opening the chest
medical

Examples

The patient required closed-heart surgery due to the complexity of the condition.
Advances in closed-heart surgery have reduced recovery times for many patients.
Unlike closed-heart surgery, open-heart procedures involve a longer hospital stay.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: open-heart surgery - heart surgery where the chest cavity is opened
