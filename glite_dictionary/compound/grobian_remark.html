grobian remark

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡroʊbiən rɪˈmɑrk / (AmE), / ˈɡrəʊbiən rɪˈmɑːk / (BrE)
a crude, boorish, or uncouth comment
crude or boorish comment

Examples

He was quickly reprimanded for making a grobian remark during the formal dinner.
Her patience was tested by his constant grobian remarks in meetings.
The politician’s grobian remark offended many in the audience.

Synset Examples

She dismissed his grobian remarks as unworthy of a response.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insult - a rude or offensive remark or action
  • Antonym: compliment - a polite expression of praise or admiration

Etymology

From Grobian, a stock character representing coarse manners, after the German satirical work 'Der Grobianus'.
