booze-up

1. NOUN

/ ˈbuːz ˌʌp / (AmE), / ˈbuːz ʌp / (BrE)
An informal social gathering where people drink alcohol together, often in a lively or merry manner.
informal drinking party
informal british

Examples

After the conference ended, the team organized a booze-up at the local pub.
His birthday turned into quite a booze-up, with laughter and plenty of drinks.
They reminisced about their university days and the occasional wild booze-up with friends.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: party - a social gathering of invited guests, typically involving eating, drinking, and entertainment

Etymology

From slang 'booze' (alcoholic drink), plus 'up' indicating an event or gathering.
