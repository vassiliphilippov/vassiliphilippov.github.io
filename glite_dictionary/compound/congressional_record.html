Congressional Record

1. NOUN

/ kənˌɡrɛʃənəl ˈrɛkərd / (AmE), / kɒnˌɡrɛʃənəl ˈrɛkɔːd / (BrE)
the official published account of the speeches, debates, and votes of the United States Congress
official record of U.S. Congress proceedings
american legal

Examples

Every speech in the Senate is entered into the Congressional Record for public review.
She researched the legislative history by reading the Congressional Record.
The Congressional Record documents the votes and debates of Congress each day.
