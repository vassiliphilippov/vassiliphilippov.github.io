table-mountain pine

1. NOUN

/ ˈteɪbəl ˌmaʊntən paɪn / (AmE), / ˈteɪbəl ˌmaʊntɪn paɪn / (BrE)
a small pine tree native to the upland regions of the eastern United States, especially the Appalachians, characterized by dark brown flaking bark and cones with thorn-tipped scales
a pine tree of the Appalachian uplands

Examples

The table-mountain pine thrives in the dry, rocky soils of the Appalachian mountains.
We identified several table-mountain pines during our hike through the nature reserve.
Because of its unique cones, the table-mountain pine can be easily distinguished from other native pines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pine - a type of evergreen tree with needle-like leaves and cones
