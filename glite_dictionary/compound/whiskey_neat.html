whiskey neat

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪski nit / (AmE), / ˈwɪski niːt / (BrE)
Whiskey served plain, without ice, water, or any mixer.
whiskey served without mixer or ice

Examples

He sat at the end of the bar, sipping a whiskey neat and reading the newspaper.
She prefers her whiskey neat to appreciate the full flavor of the spirit.
When asked how he'd like his drink, he replied, 'A whiskey neat, please.'

Synset Examples

he ordered a whiskey neat

Related Terms

  • Antonym: whiskey on the rocks - whiskey served over ice cubes
