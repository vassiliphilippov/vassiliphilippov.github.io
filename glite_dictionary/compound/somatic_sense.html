somatic sense

1. NOUN

/ soʊˈmætɪk sɛns / (AmE), / səˈmatɪk sɛns / (BrE)
the faculty of perceiving bodily sensations, including skin senses, proprioception, and sensations from internal organs
the sense of bodily sensations
medical

Examples

The somatic sense allows us to perceive touch, pressure, pain, and temperature.
Damage to certain nerves can impair a person's somatic sense.
Proprioception is a component of the somatic sense responsible for body position awareness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: proprioception - the sense of self-movement and body position

Etymology

From Greek *sōmatikos* (of the body), from *sōma* (body) + English 'sense'.
