Sistine Chapel

1. PROPN

/ ˈsɪstiːn ˈtʃæpəl / (AmE), / ˈsɪstiːn ˈtʃæpəl / (BrE)
The private chapel in Vatican City, built by order of Pope Sixtus IV in the late 15th century, famous for its Renaissance artwork, including Michelangelo's ceiling frescoes.
famous papal chapel in Vatican City

Examples

Thousands of visitors each year marvel at the ceiling of the Sistine Chapel.
Michelangelo painted some of his greatest masterpieces on the ceiling of the Sistine Chapel.
During their trip to Rome, they attended a service inside the Sistine Chapel.

Etymology

Named after Pope Sixtus IV (Italian: Sisto IV), who commissioned its construction.
