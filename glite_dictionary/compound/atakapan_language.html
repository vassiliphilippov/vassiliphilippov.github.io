Atakapan language

1. NOUN

/ ˌætəˈkɑːpən ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌætəˈkɑːpən ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
an extinct language once spoken by the Atakapa people along the Gulf Coast of Louisiana and Texas
an extinct Native American language of Louisiana and Texas

Examples

Linguists have studied the Atakapan language to understand the culture of the Atakapa people.
Few records remain of the Atakapan language due to the early loss of native speakers.
The Atakapan language differs significantly from other languages of the Gulf Coast region.

Etymology

From Atakapa, the name of the indigenous people of the region, plus 'language'.
