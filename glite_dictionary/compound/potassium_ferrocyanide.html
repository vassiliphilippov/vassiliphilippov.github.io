potassium ferrocyanide

1. NOUN

/ pəˌtæsiəm ˌfɛroʊˈsaɪəˌnaɪd / (AmE), / pəˌtasiəm ˌfɛrəʊˈsaɪənaɪd / (BrE)
a yellowish crystalline compound, K₄[Fe(CN)₆], used in the chemical industry and sometimes in gardening, prepared by reacting potassium cyanide with ferrous salts
a synthetic chemical compound used in industry and gardening

Examples

Some gardeners use potassium ferrocyanide in solutions to help plants absorb nutrients more effectively.
The industrial process involved adding potassium ferrocyanide to precipitate heavy metals from wastewater.
You should handle potassium ferrocyanide with care, as it contains cyanide ions.

Etymology

From potassium and ferrocyanide, indicating its potassium salt form of ferrocyanide.
