President Coolidge

1. PROPN

/ ˈprɛzɪdənt ˈkuːlɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈprɛzɪdənt ˈkuːlɪdʒ / (BrE)
the 30th President of the United States (1872–1933), who took office after the death of President Harding in 1923
30th President of the USA, 1923–1929

Examples

President Coolidge assumed office after President Harding died in 1923.
Many historians praise President Coolidge for his public speaking skills and quiet leadership.
As a child, I wrote a report on President Coolidge for history class.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Calvin Coolidge - the personal name of the 30th President of the United States
  • Antonym: President Harding - the 29th President of the United States, Coolidge's predecessor

Etymology

From the surname Coolidge; referring to Calvin Coolidge, U.S. President.
