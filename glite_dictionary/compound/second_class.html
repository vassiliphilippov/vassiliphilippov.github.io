second class

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛkənd ˈklæs / (AmE), / ˌsɛkənd ˈklɑːs / (BrE)
a category of something that is not of the highest quality or status in a classification
not the highest quality; lower category

Examples

She always felt she was treated as a second class citizen in the company.
The postage was cheaper if you sent the letter second class instead of first class.
Many people believe that access to education should not depend on being in the second class group.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: first class - the highest quality or status in a classification

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɛkənd klæs / (AmE), / ˈsekənd klɑːs / (BrE)
a rank, category, or standard that is the next below the highest; not of the first rank or quality.
next below the highest rank or category

Examples

The ferry offered both second class and first class cabins for passengers.
She disliked being treated as a second class citizen in her own country.
Many students purchased second class train tickets to save money on travel.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: first class - the highest rank or category
  • Degree Lower: third class - the rank or category below second class

3. ADV

/ ˌsɛk.ənd ˈklæs / (AmE), / ˌsɛk.ənd ˈklɑːs / (BrE)
using a mode of travel, transport, or postal service that is ranked as the second tier in terms of quality or priority, below first class.
in the second tier for travel or mail

Examples

We chose to travel second class to save money on the train tickets.
The letter was sent second class, so it took a few extra days to arrive.
He usually flies second class rather than paying more for first class.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: first class - in the highest category of service for travel or mail
  • Degree Lower: third class - in the third category, below first and second, for travel or mail

4. NOUN

/ ˈsɛkənd klæs / (AmE), / ˈsɛkənd klɑːs / (BrE)
the category of accommodations on a ship, train, or plane that are less expensive and offer fewer privileges than those in first class
less expensive travel accommodation

Examples

He bought a second class ticket for the overnight train to Paris.
Most of the students traveled in second class to save money.
She preferred second class because it was affordable, even if it wasn't as comfortable.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: first class - the most expensive and luxurious class of accommodation
  • Synonym: economy class - the cheapest class of airline seating; generally similar to second class but varies by context
