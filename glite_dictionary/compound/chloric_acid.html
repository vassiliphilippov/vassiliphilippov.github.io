chloric acid

1. NOUN

/ ˌklɔrɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / ˌklɒrɪk ˈasɪd / (BrE)
a strong, unstable acid (HClO₃) with an acrid odor, found in chlorate salts and used in chemical analysis
a strong, unstable acid (HClO₃)
medical

Examples

Chloric acid is rarely found in pure form due to its instability.
Laboratory experiments often involve preparing chloric acid from chlorates.
The presence of chloric acid can be detected by its strong oxidizing properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hydrochloric acid - a strong acid (HCl), also derived from chlorine

Etymology

From 'chloric', based on 'chlorine', and 'acid'.
