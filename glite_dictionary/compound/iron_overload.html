iron overload

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪɚn ˌoʊvɚˌloʊd / (AmE), / ˈaɪən ˌəʊvəˌləʊd / (BrE)
A medical condition in which excess iron accumulates in the body's tissues, potentially causing organ damage.
condition of excess iron in the body
medical

Examples

Chronic iron overload can lead to liver cirrhosis and heart problems if left untreated.
Hereditary hemochromatosis is a common cause of iron overload in adults.
Regular blood donations may be recommended to manage iron overload in certain patients.
