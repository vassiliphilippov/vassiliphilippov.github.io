to play even harder

1. VERB

/ pleɪ ˈivən ˈhɑrdər / (AmE), / pleɪ ˈiːvən ˈhɑːdə / (BrE)
to engage in enjoyable activities with even more energy, enthusiasm, or intensity, especially after working hard
enjoy activities with greater enthusiasm

Examples

After the long workweek, they like to play even harder on the weekends.
She believes in working hard and playing even harder whenever she gets the chance.
We managed to finish the project early, so we decided to play even harder at the party.

Synset Examples

He works hard - and plays even harder.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: play hard - engage in enjoyable activities with much energy or enthusiasm
