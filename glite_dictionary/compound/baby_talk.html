baby talk

1. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪ.bi ˌtɔk / (AmE), / ˈbeɪ.bi ˌtɔːk / (BrE)
a mode of speaking in which adults imitate the simplified speech characteristic of young children, often in a playful or affectionate way
imitation of a child's speech by adults

Examples

Whenever she spoke to her puppy, she used baby talk to make him feel loved.
The teacher discouraged parents from using baby talk when helping their children learn to speak.
He found baby talk charming when adults interacted with infants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: childish speech - language resembling that of a child

2. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪ.bi tɔk / (AmE), / ˈbeɪ.bi tɔːk / (BrE)
the speech or utterances of a very young child learning to talk, often marked by simplified sounds and grammar
speech of a very young child

Examples

Her daughter's baby talk was full of amusing made-up words and sounds.
He recorded his son's baby talk to remember those early learning moments.
Parents often find baby talk both adorable and fascinating as children learn to speak.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: babble - incoherent or meaningless speech, especially that of a young child
