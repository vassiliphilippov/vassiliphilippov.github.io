parhelic ring

1. NOUN

/ pɑrˈhɛlɪk rɪŋ / (AmE), / pɑːˈhiːlɪk rɪŋ / (BrE)
A luminous, horizontal halo appearing parallel to the horizon at the same altitude as the sun, caused by sunlight interacting with ice crystals in the atmosphere.
horizontal halo at sun's altitude

Examples

The rare parhelic ring stretched across the sky, creating a stunning optical illusion.
Photographers eagerly captured the parhelic ring that formed during the cold, clear morning.
Scientists explained that the appearance of a parhelic ring results from sunlight passing through ice crystals in the atmosphere.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: halo - A ring of light appearing around the sun or moon
  • Synonym: parhelion - A bright spot on a solar halo, also known as a sun dog

Etymology

From Greek 'para' (beside) and 'hēlios' (sun), referring to its position relative to the sun.
