genus Hyacinthus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs haɪˈæsɪnθəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs hʌɪˈasɪnθəs / (BrE)
A genus of bulbous flowering plants, often placed in the family Hyacinthaceae, known for the hyacinth flowers.
genus of flowering plants (hyacinths)

Examples

The genus Hyacinthus includes several species commonly cultivated for their fragrant, colorful flowers.
Plant breeders have developed many hybrids within the genus Hyacinthus for ornamental gardens.
Botanists sometimes debate whether the genus Hyacinthus should be placed in the family Asparagaceae or Hyacinthaceae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hyacinth - a fragrant spring-blooming plant of the genus Hyacinthus

Etymology

From Latin *Hyacinthus*, from Greek *Hyakinthos*, the name of a mythological figure and the flower.
