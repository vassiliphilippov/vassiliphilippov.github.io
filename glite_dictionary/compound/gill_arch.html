gill arch

1. NOUN

/ ɡɪl ɑrtʃ / (AmE), / ɡɪl ɑːtʃ / (BrE)
one of a series of bony or cartilaginous arches on each side of the pharynx that support the gills in fishes and some aquatic amphibians
bony/cartilaginous arch supporting fish gills

Examples

The anatomy textbook included a diagram showing each gill arch in detail.
Evolutionary changes in the gill arch have played a role in the development of jaws in vertebrates.
The scientist carefully examined the gill arches to assess the fish's health.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: branchial arch - another term for gill arch, used especially in technical contexts
