Candlemas Day

1. NOUN

/ ˈkændəlˌmæs deɪ / (AmE), / ˈkændlˌməs deɪ / (BrE)
A Christian festival on February 2nd commemorating the presentation of Christ in the temple; also considered a quarter day in Scotland.
Christian festival on February 2nd

Examples

Many churches hold special services on Candlemas Day to celebrate the presentation of Jesus at the temple.
In Scotland, Candlemas Day was traditionally one of the quarter days for settling rents and contracts.
Folk traditions around Candlemas Day include predictions about the weather for the coming months.

Etymology

From Old English 'candelmæsse', meaning 'festival of candles'.
