certificate of deposit

1. NOUN

/ sərˈtɪfɪkət əv dəˈpɑzɪt / (AmE), / səˈtɪfɪkət əv dɪˈpɒzɪt / (BrE)
a certificate issued by a bank to a person depositing money for a specified period, usually paying interest
interest-bearing bank deposit certificate

Examples

She invested her savings in a certificate of deposit to earn higher interest rates.
The bank offers a five-year certificate of deposit with a competitive annual percentage yield.
When the certificate of deposit matures, you can withdraw the money along with the accumulated interest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: time deposit - a bank deposit held for a fixed term, earning interest
  • Antonym: demand deposit - a bank deposit that can be withdrawn at any time without notice
