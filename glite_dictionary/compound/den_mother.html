den mother

1. NOUN

/ dɛn ˈmʌðɚ / (AmE), / dɛn ˈmʌðə / (BrE)
a woman who supervises and leads a den of Cub Scouts, typically organizing activities and providing guidance
female supervisor of Cub Scouts den

Examples

My mother volunteered to be the den mother for my younger brother's Cub Scout group.
The den mother organized a fun camping trip for her den this summer.
As a den mother, she helped young scouts earn their badges and learn new skills.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: den leader - adult who leads a Cub Scouts den, regardless of gender

Etymology

From 'den' (a group of Cub Scouts) + 'mother' (female caregiver), reflecting the nurturing and supervisory role.

2. NOUN

/ dɛn ˈmʌðɚ / (AmE), / dɛn ˈmʌðə / (BrE)
a woman who supervises or looks after a group, especially of children or young people, often acting as a caretaker or mentor
female supervisor or caretaker of a group

Examples

Sarah was the den mother for the local Cub Scout troop, organizing activities and caring for the children.
Since their parents worked late, Mrs. Lee became the unofficial den mother for the neighborhood kids after school.
In the start-up office, Jane took on the role of den mother, making sure everyone felt welcome and supported.

Synset Examples

he serves as den mother to all the freshmen in this dormitory
she's the den mother to new secretaries

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chaperone - a person who supervises young people
  • Synonym: mentor - an experienced and trusted adviser

Etymology

From 'den' (as in Cub Scouts, a group or section) and 'mother' (female caregiver); used by analogy for group leaders beyond scouting.
