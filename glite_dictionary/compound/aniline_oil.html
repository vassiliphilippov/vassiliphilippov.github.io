aniline oil

1. NOUN

/ ˈæn.ɪˌlin ɔɪl / (AmE), / ˈan.ɪˌliːn ɔɪl / (BrE)
a poisonous, oily liquid amine obtained from nitrobenzene, used in the manufacture of dyes, plastics, and medicines
poisonous oily liquid used in dyes and plastics

Examples

Aniline oil is a key ingredient in the production of many synthetic dyes and pigments.
Factory workers must handle aniline oil with care because it is highly toxic.
In the 19th century, scientists discovered how to produce aniline oil from nitrobenzene.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aniline - a toxic aromatic amine used chiefly in dyes

Etymology

Aniline from German Anilin, from anil (from Arabic an-nīl, indigo), plus 'oil'.
