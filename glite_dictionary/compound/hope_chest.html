hope chest

1. NOUN

/ hoʊp tʃɛst / (AmE), / həʊp tʃɛst / (BrE)
a chest used to store clothing, linens, and other household items collected in anticipation of marriage, especially by a young woman
chest for storing trousseau items before marriage

Examples

She filled her hope chest with handmade quilts and delicate linens for her future home.
The old hope chest in the attic held her grandmother’s wedding dress and family heirlooms.
Many young women once dreamed of placing treasured keepsakes in a hope chest before marriage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trousseau - a bride's collection of clothes and linens

Etymology

From 'hope' (anticipation) + 'chest', referring to hope for marriage; popularized in North America in the early 20th century.
