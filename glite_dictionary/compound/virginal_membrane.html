virginal membrane

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɜrdʒɪnəl ˈmɛmˌbreɪn / (AmE), / ˈvɜːdʒɪnəl ˈmɛm.breɪn / (BrE)
a fold of tissue that partly covers the entrance to the vagina, usually present at birth and sometimes referred to as the hymen
fold of tissue at entrance to vagina (hymen)
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that the virginal membrane can vary greatly in appearance among individuals.
Some cultures place importance on the presence of the virginal membrane as a sign of purity.
In some cases, the virginal membrane may tear due to physical activity, not just sexual intercourse.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hymen - membrane that partially covers the vaginal opening

Etymology

From Latin 'virginalis' (of a maiden) and 'membrana' (membrane).
