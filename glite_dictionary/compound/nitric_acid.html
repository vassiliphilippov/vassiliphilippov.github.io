nitric acid

1. NOUN

/ ˈnaɪ.trɪk ˈæ.sɪd / (AmE), / ˈnɪ.trɪk ˈæs.ɪd / (BrE)
a highly corrosive and toxic strong acid (HNO₃), used especially in the production of fertilizers, explosives, and rocket fuels
a strong, corrosive acid used in industry

Examples

The laboratory stored bottles of nitric acid in a secure chemical cabinet.
Nitric acid plays a crucial role in manufacturing fertilizers and explosives.
Always handle nitric acid with extreme caution, as it can cause severe burns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sulfuric acid - a strong mineral acid used in industry
  • Synonym: hydrochloric acid - a strong acid found in the stomach and used in industry

Etymology

From Latin 'nitrum' (natron, potassium nitrate) and 'acid.'
