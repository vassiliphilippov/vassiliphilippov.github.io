grade separation

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡreɪd ˌsɛpəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪd ˌsɛpəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
A crossing of two routes, especially a road and a railway, at different elevations by means of an underpass or overpass.
crossing at different elevations (overpass or underpass)

Examples

The city plans to build a grade separation at the busy intersection to improve traffic flow.
Installing a grade separation eliminates delays caused by trains passing through level crossings.
A highway grade separation can significantly reduce accidents at dangerous junctions.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: level crossing - A crossing where a road and railway intersect at the same level

Etymology

From 'grade' (level or slope) + 'separation' (act of separating).
