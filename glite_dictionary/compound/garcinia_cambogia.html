Garcinia cambogia

1. NOUN

/ ɡɑːrˈsɪniə kəmˈboʊdʒiə / (AmE), / ɡɑːˈsɪnɪə kəmˈbəʊdʒɪə / (BrE)
A tropical tree native to Indonesia, known for producing an orange to brown gum resin (gamboge) used as a pigment.
tropical Indonesian tree yielding gamboge resin

Examples

The fruit of the Garcinia cambogia tree is used in traditional medicine and cooking across Southeast Asia.
Artists historically valued gamboge, a pigment derived from the resin of Garcinia cambogia.
Botanists studied the spread of Garcinia cambogia in Indonesian rainforests.

Etymology

From the genus name 'Garcinia', named after botanist Laurent Garcin, and 'cambogia', referring to the Malabar region (Cambogia) in India.
