martial music

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑrʃəl ˈmjuzɪk / (AmE), / ˈmɑːʃəl ˈmjuːzɪk / (BrE)
brisk, rhythmic music composed or played to accompany marching troops or military parades
military marching music

Examples

The band played martial music as the troops paraded through the city square.
Ceremonies often begin with martial music to inspire discipline and unity among soldiers.
During the festival, traditional martial music filled the air, reminding everyone of the town's military history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: march - music with a strong, regular rhythm written for marching

Etymology

From Latin 'martialis' (of Mars, the god of war) + 'music'.
