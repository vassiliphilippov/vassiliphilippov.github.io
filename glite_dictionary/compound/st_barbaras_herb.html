St. Barbara's herb

1. NOUN

/ seɪnt ˈbɑːrbərəz hɜrb / (AmE), / sənt ˈbɑːbərəz hɜːb / (BrE)
Any plant of the genus Barbarea, a group of yellow-flowered, Eurasian cresses often cultivated for winter salads.
plant of the genus Barbarea; winter salad cress

Examples

Gardeners often grow St. Barbara's herb as a hardy winter salad green.
The bright yellow flowers of St. Barbara's herb are easy to spot in the wild.
In traditional cooking, St. Barbara's herb leaves are added to salads for a peppery flavor.

Etymology

Named after Saint Barbara, patron saint of artillerymen and miners, possibly due to the plant's common growth in disturbed ground.
