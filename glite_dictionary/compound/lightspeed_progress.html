lightspeed progress

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪtspiːd ˈprɑːɡrɛs / (AmE), / ˈlaɪtspiːd ˈprəʊɡrɛs / (BrE)
very rapid advancement or development, especially in a project or process
extremely rapid advancement

Examples

The development team made lightspeed progress on the project, finishing weeks ahead of schedule.
Thanks to new technology, their research is moving at lightspeed progress.
With everyone working overtime, we saw lightspeed progress on the construction site.

Synset Examples

They made lightspeed progress on the new app.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rapid progress - quick advancement or development
  • Antonym: slow progress - advancement at a much slower or delayed pace

Etymology

From 'lightspeed', referring to the maximum speed at which light travels, used figuratively; combined with 'progress'.
