Book of Isaiah

1. PROPN

/ ˈbʊk əv aɪˈzeɪ.ə / (AmE), / ˈbʊk əv aɪˈzaɪ.ə / (BrE)
A book of the Old Testament containing the prophecies attributed to the prophet Isaiah.
Old Testament book with Isaiah's prophecies

Examples

The Book of Isaiah contains some of the most poetic passages in the Bible.
Scholars often debate the authorship of the Book of Isaiah.
Many Christians read from the Book of Isaiah during Advent.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Isaiah - the Hebrew prophet whose prophecies appear in the Book of Isaiah

Etymology

Named after the Hebrew prophet Isaiah; 'Isaiah' from Hebrew Yəšaʿyāhu meaning 'Yahweh is salvation'.
