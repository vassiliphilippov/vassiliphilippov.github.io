French Guinea

1. PROPN

/ frɛntʃ ˈɡɪni / (AmE), / frɛntʃ ˈɡɪni / (BrE)
the former name of Guinea, a territory in western Africa that was a French colony until it gained independence in 1958
former name of Guinea (West Africa)

Examples

During the colonial era, French Guinea was administered as part of French West Africa.
The capital of French Guinea was Conakry, which remains the capital of modern Guinea.
After gaining independence in 1958, French Guinea became known simply as Guinea.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Guinea - country in West Africa, formerly called French Guinea
  • Degree Higher: French West Africa - a former federation of French colonial territories in West Africa

Etymology

From French 'Guinée française', denoting the period when Guinea was a French colony.
