neutralization reaction

1. NOUN

/ ˌnuːtrəlaɪˈzeɪʃən riˈækʃən / (AmE), / ˌnjuːtrəlaɪˈzeɪʃən riˈækʃən / (BrE)
a chemical reaction between an acid and a base that produces a salt and usually water
reaction of acid and base yielding salt and water

Examples

When hydrochloric acid is mixed with sodium hydroxide, a neutralization reaction occurs, forming salt and water.
Titration is commonly used in laboratories to measure the progress of a neutralization reaction.
A neutralization reaction can help to treat acidic wastewater by adding a base.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acid-base reaction - a general term for reactions between acids and bases

Etymology

From 'neutralization' (from Latin neuter, neither) and 'reaction'.
