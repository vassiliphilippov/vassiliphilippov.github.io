present tense

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɛzənt tɛns / (AmE), / ˈprɛzənt tɛns / (BrE)
The form of a verb that expresses an action or state occurring at the time of speaking.
verb tense for current actions or states

Examples

In English, the present tense is used to describe what is happening now.
She often forgets to use the present tense when talking about daily routines.
Teachers explained that 'eat' is the present tense form of the verb.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: past tense - verb tense indicating an action or state that happened previously

Etymology

From Latin 'praesens' (present) and 'tempus' (time, tense).
