pissed off

1. ADJ

/ pɪst ɔf / (AmE), / pɪst ɒf / (BrE)
very angry or annoyed; extremely irritated
very angry or annoyed
informal

Examples

He was absolutely pissed off when he found out someone had scratched his car.
She gets pissed off if people don't return her calls promptly.
I'm really pissed off about how they treated me at the meeting.

Synset Examples

made an irritated gesture
feeling nettled from the constant teasing
peeved about being left out
felt really pissed at her snootiness
riled no end by his lies
roiled by the delay

Related Terms

  • Synonym: angry - feeling or showing strong displeasure or annoyance
  • Antonym: calm - not showing or feeling anger, nervousness, or other strong emotions

Etymology

From adjective 'pissed' (meaning annoyed or angry), combined with preposition 'off'.
