migratory grasshopper

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪɡrəˌtɔri ˈɡræsˌhɑpər / (AmE), / ˈmɪɡrəˌtɒri ˈɡrɑːsˌhɒpə / (BrE)
a species of grasshopper (Melanoplus sanguinipes) found in central and western United States, known as a serious pest of grain crops and rangeland
a grasshopper species harmful to crops

Examples

The migratory grasshopper is one of the major threats to wheat fields in the Midwest.
Farmers set traps to reduce outbreaks of migratory grasshoppers during the growing season.
Large swarms of migratory grasshoppers can devastate pastures and crops if not controlled.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grasshopper - an insect that jumps and is known for its leaping ability
