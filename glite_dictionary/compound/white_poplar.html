white poplar

1. NOUN

/ waɪt ˈpɑplɚ / (AmE), / waɪt ˈpɒplə / (BrE)
A light, easily worked wood from the tulip tree, used in making furniture and for veneers.
light wood from tulip tree for furniture

Examples

The cabinet was crafted from white poplar, giving it a smooth finish.
Veneer sheets made of white poplar are popular for their durability and appearance.
Because of its light weight, white poplar is often chosen for making drawers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tulip tree - a large North American tree (Liriodendron tulipifera) that yields white poplar wood

2. NOUN

/ waɪt ˈpɑːplɚ / (AmE), / waɪt ˈpɒplə / (BrE)
a species of poplar tree (Populus alba) characterized by white bark and leaves with whitish undersides, widely cultivated for ornamental purposes
a poplar tree with white bark and leaves underneath

Examples

The white poplar is easily recognized by the silvery underside of its leaves.
They planted a row of white poplars along the driveway for shade and beauty.
The bark of the white poplar stands out starkly against the winter landscape.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poplar - a fast-growing tree of the genus Populus
