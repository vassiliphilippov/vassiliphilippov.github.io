purple beech

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɜr.pəl ˌbitʃ / (AmE), / ˈpɜː.pəl ˌbiːtʃ / (BrE)
a variety of European beech tree with shining purple or copper-colored leaves, often grown as an ornamental
variety of beech with purple leaves

Examples

The purple beech in the park stands out with its striking coppery foliage in spring.
Gardeners often plant purple beech as a specimen tree for its unique leaf color.
The leaves of the purple beech turn a deep, rich bronze as the season progresses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: European beech - a species of beech native to Europe, Fagus sylvatica
