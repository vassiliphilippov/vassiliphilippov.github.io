heavy syllable

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛvi ˈsɪləbəl / (AmE), / ˈhɛvi ˈsɪləbəl / (BrE)
(in linguistics) a syllable that contains a long vowel, a diphthong, or ends with one or more consonants after a short vowel, giving it extra weight in phonological terms.
syllable with extra weight in phonology

Examples

The word 'lamp' contains a heavy syllable because it ends in two consonants.
In many languages, stress tends to fall on a heavy syllable within a word.
Children often find it easier to clap out words that have at least one heavy syllable.

Synset Examples

A heavy syllable consists of a long vowel or a diphthong, or a short vowel followed by one or more consonants in the same syllable.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: light syllable - a syllable with a short vowel and no final consonant; the opposite in phonological weight to a heavy syllable
