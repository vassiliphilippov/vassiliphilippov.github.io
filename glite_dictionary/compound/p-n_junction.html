p-n junction

1. NOUN

/ piː ˈɛn ˈdʒʌŋkʃən / (AmE), / piː ˈɛn ˈdʒʌŋkʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
the boundary between a p-type semiconductor and an n-type semiconductor, forming a region with unique electrical characteristics, commonly used in diodes and other electronic devices
boundary between p-type and n-type semiconductors
medical

Examples

The p-n junction is the fundamental building block of most semiconductor devices.
When a voltage is applied across the p-n junction, it allows current to flow easily in one direction but not the other.
Light-emitting diodes (LEDs) function because of the properties of the p-n junction.

Synset Examples

a p-n junction has marked rectifying characteristics

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diode - an electronic device that allows current to flow in one direction only

Etymology

From 'p-type' (positive-type) and 'n-type' (negative-type) semiconductor, referring to the majority charge carriers in each material.
