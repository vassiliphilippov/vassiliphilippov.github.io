Mesa Verde National Park

1. PROPN

/ ˌmeɪsə ˈvɜrdi ˌnæʃənəl ˈpɑrk / (AmE), / ˌmeɪsə ˈvɜːdi ˌnæʃənəl ˈpɑːk / (BrE)
a national park in Colorado, United States, known for its well-preserved prehistoric cliff dwellings built by the Ancestral Puebloans
national park in Colorado with ancient cliff dwellings

Examples

During our road trip, we visited Mesa Verde National Park to see the famous cliff dwellings.
Archaeologists have studied the ancient structures at Mesa Verde National Park for decades.
Mesa Verde National Park is recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage site because of its cultural significance.

Etymology

From Spanish 'mesa verde', meaning 'green table', referring to the area's flat-topped mountains.
