cystic artery

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪstɪk ˈɑːr.t̬ə.ri / (AmE), / ˈsɪstɪk ˈɑː.tə.ri / (BrE)
an artery that branches from the hepatic artery and supplies blood to the gall bladder and the surface of the liver
artery supplying the gall bladder
medical

Examples

The surgeon carefully ligated the cystic artery during the gallbladder removal procedure.
An injury to the cystic artery can cause significant bleeding during abdominal surgery.
Proper identification of the cystic artery is crucial in laparoscopic cholecystectomy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hepatic artery - the main artery supplying blood to the liver

Etymology

From Greek kystis (bladder) and Latin arteria (artery).
