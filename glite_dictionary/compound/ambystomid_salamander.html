ambystomid salamander

1. NOUN

/ æmˈbɪstəˌmɪd ˈsæləˌmændɚ / (AmE), / æmˈbɪstəˌmɪd ˈsæləˌmændə / (BrE)
A small to moderate-sized terrestrial or semiaquatic salamander of the family Ambystomatidae, found in the Americas.
type of North American salamander

Examples

The ambystomid salamander is well adapted for life in both terrestrial and aquatic habitats.
Conservation efforts are underway to protect several endangered ambystomid salamander species in North America.
Identifying an ambystomid salamander requires close examination of its physical features.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mole salamander - another name for ambystomid salamander

Etymology

From New Latin Ambystoma (the genus name) + -id, indicating a family in zoological taxonomy.
