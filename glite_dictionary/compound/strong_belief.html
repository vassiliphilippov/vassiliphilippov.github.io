strong belief

1. NOUN

/ strɔŋ bɪˈlif / (AmE), / strɒŋ bɪˈliːf / (BrE)
A firm or unshakable conviction in something, held with confidence even without need for proof or evidence.
firm conviction held without proof

Examples

She has a strong belief that honesty is always the best policy.
His strong belief in his team's abilities motivated everyone to do better.
Despite the lack of evidence, they maintained a strong belief in the theory.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: faith - complete trust or confidence in someone or something
  • Antonym: doubt - a feeling of uncertainty or lack of conviction
