princess royal

1. NOUN

/ ˌprɪnsɪs ˈroʊjəl / (AmE), / ˌprɪnsɪs ˈrɔɪəl / (BrE)
the title given to the eldest daughter of a British sovereign.
title for eldest British royal daughter
british

Examples

Queen Victoria was given the title of princess royal when she was only a child.
The princess royal often represented the monarch at official events.
Historically, the title of princess royal is not automatically given but must be bestowed by the sovereign.
