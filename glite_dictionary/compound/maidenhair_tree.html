maidenhair tree

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪdənˌhɛr tri / (AmE), / ˈmeɪdənˌheə triː / (BrE)
a deciduous Chinese tree (Ginkgo biloba) with fan-shaped leaves and yellow fleshy seeds, grown mainly as an ornamental street tree
deciduous tree with fan-shaped leaves (Ginkgo biloba)

Examples

The maidenhair tree is often planted along city streets because of its resistance to pollution.
Many people admire the unique fan-shaped leaves of the maidenhair tree in autumn.
The seeds of the maidenhair tree are used in some traditional Asian cuisines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ginkgo - another common name for maidenhair tree

Etymology

Named for its leaves, which resemble those of the maidenhair fern; common name for Ginkgo biloba, originally from Chinese.
