self-torture

1. NOUN

/ sɛlf ˈtɔrtʃɚ / (AmE), / sɛlf ˈtɔːtʃə / (BrE)
The act of inflicting mental or emotional distress on oneself, often by dwelling on negative thoughts or feelings.
inflicting distress on oneself

Examples

Her constant self-torture made it impossible for her to find peace of mind.
He engaged in self-torture by replaying his mistakes over and over in his head.
The novel explores themes of guilt, regret, and self-torture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: self-punishment - the act of punishing oneself
