percussion instrument

1. NOUN

/ pərˈkʌʃ.ən ˌɪn.strəˌmɛnt / (AmE), / pəˈkʌʃ.ən ˌɪn.strə.mənt / (BrE)
A musical instrument that produces sound by being struck, shaken, or scraped.
class of instrument played by striking or shaking

Examples

The snare drum is a type of percussion instrument used in many styles of music.
Children love to play simple percussion instruments like tambourines and maracas.
The orchestra's percussion instrument section added energy to the performance.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: string instrument - instrument that produces sound from vibrating strings
  • Antonym: wind instrument - instrument that produces sound by air vibration

Etymology

From Latin 'percussio' (a striking) + instrument.
