electroshock therapy

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌlɛktroʊˈʃɑk ˈθɛrəpi / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛktrəʊˈʃɒk ˈθɛrəpi / (BrE)
A medical treatment in which a strong electric current is passed through the brain to induce convulsions, typically to treat certain psychiatric disorders.
psychiatric treatment using electric shocks
medical

Examples

Electroshock therapy was used as a last resort for patients with severe depression.
Some psychiatrists advocate the careful use of electroshock therapy in persistent mental illnesses.
The controversy surrounding electroshock therapy stems from its side effects and public misunderstanding.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: electroconvulsive therapy - formal term for electroshock therapy

Etymology

Formed from 'electro-' (relating to electricity) and 'shock therapy'.
