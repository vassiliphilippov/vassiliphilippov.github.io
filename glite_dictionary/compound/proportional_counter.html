proportional counter

1. NOUN

/ prəˈpɔrʃənəl ˈkaʊntər / (AmE), / prəˈpɔːʃənəl ˈkaʊntə / (BrE)
a device for detecting and measuring ionizing radiation, in which the electrical output is proportional to the number of ions created in the detector tube
device for measuring radiation intensity

Examples

A proportional counter is often used in laboratories to detect low levels of alpha or beta radiation.
The output signal from a proportional counter can provide information about the energy of incoming particles.
Scientists calibrated the proportional counter before starting the radiation measurement experiment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Geiger counter - radiation detector that produces pulses but without a proportional relationship
