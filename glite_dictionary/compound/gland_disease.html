gland disease

1. NOUN

/ ɡlænd dɪˈziz / (AmE), / ɡlænd dɪˈziːz / (BrE)
a disorder affecting one or more glands in the body, often disrupting their normal function
disorder of a body gland
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with a rare gland disease that affected her hormone levels.
Doctors are researching new treatments for various gland diseases such as thyroid and adrenal disorders.
Enlargement of a gland is often the first sign of a possible gland disease.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glandular disorder - disease or disorder affecting glands
