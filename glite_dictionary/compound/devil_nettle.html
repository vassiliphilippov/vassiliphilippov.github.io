devil nettle

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛvəl ˌnɛt̬l̩ / (AmE), / ˈdɛv.əl ˌnɛt.l̩ / (BrE)
a stinging herb native to tropical America, known for its irritating hairs
a stinging herb of tropical America

Examples

Be careful when walking barefoot, as devil nettle can cause a painful sting.
Local farmers warn that devil nettle grows rapidly during the rainy season.
The leaves of devil nettle are covered with tiny hairs that irritate the skin.
