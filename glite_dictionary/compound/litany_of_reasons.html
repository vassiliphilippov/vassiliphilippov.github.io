litany of reasons

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪtəni əv ˈrizənz / (AmE), / ˈlɪtəni əv ˈriːzənz / (BrE)
a long and often repetitive list of reasons, especially given as justifications or complaints
a lengthy list of reasons

Examples

She gave me a litany of reasons why she couldn't attend the party.
The manager offered a litany of reasons to explain the project's delay.
During the meeting, he read out a litany of reasons for declining the proposal.

Synset Examples

He recited a litany of reasons for his absence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: list of excuses - a series of explanations offered to justify something

Etymology

From 'litany', originally a form of prayer with a repeated response, now used figuratively for repetitive lists.
