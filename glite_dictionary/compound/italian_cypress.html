Italian cypress

1. NOUN

/ əˈtæljən ˈsaɪprəs / (AmE), / ɪˈtælɪən ˈsʌɪprəs / (BrE)
a tall, slender tree of southern Europe and western Asia, with thin grey bark and upward-growing branches, widely planted for ornament; Cupressus sempervirens
a tall slender ornamental tree

Examples

Tall Italian cypress trees lined the driveway, giving the estate a Mediterranean feel.
They planted an Italian cypress to provide a vertical accent in the garden.
The Italian cypress is popular in landscaping due to its elegant, columnar shape.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cypress - any coniferous tree of the family Cupressaceae
