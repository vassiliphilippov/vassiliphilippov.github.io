open society

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊpən səˈsaɪəti / (AmE), / ˈəʊpən səˈsaɪəti / (BrE)
a society in which members enjoy significant political and civil freedoms, such as free expression, association, and participation in democratic processes
society with significant political and civil freedoms

Examples

Many believe that an open society encourages innovation and personal growth through freedom of thought.
Threats to an open society often arise from attempts to limit free speech and dissent.
Building an open society requires respect for individual rights and transparent government processes.

Synset Examples

America's open society has made it an easy target for terrorists

Related Terms

  • Antonym: closed society - a society with restricted freedoms and little openness
