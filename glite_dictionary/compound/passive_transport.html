passive transport

1. NOUN

/ ˈpæsɪv trænˌspɔrt / (AmE), / ˈpæsɪv trænˌspɔːt / (BrE)
the movement of substances across a cell membrane by diffusion without the expenditure of cellular energy
movement of substances across a membrane without energy use
medical

Examples

Diffusion of oxygen into a cell is a form of passive transport.
Many nutrients enter the bloodstream through passive transport processes.
The distinction between passive transport and active transport is important in biology.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: active transport - movement of substances across a cell membrane using energy

Etymology

From 'passive' (not involving active response) + 'transport' (carrying across).
