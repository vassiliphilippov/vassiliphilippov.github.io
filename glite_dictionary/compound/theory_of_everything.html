theory of everything

1. NOUN

/ ˈθiːəri əv ˈɛvrɪˌθɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈθɪəri əv ˈɛvrɪθɪŋ / (BrE)
a hypothetical theoretical framework that fully explains and links together all fundamental forces and forms of matter in the universe, sought especially in physics
explanatory framework for all fundamental forces

Examples

Physicists have long searched for a theory of everything that unites all forces of nature.
The idea of a theory of everything fascinates both scientists and philosophers alike.
String theory has been proposed as a possible theory of everything in physics.

Synset Examples

The theory of everything might involve multiple types of bozon.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: grand unified theory - a model in particle physics unifying the electromagnetic, weak, and strong forces, but not gravity
