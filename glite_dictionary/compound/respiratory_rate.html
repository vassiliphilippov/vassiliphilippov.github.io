respiratory rate

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈspɪrəˌtɔri reɪt / (AmE), / rɛˈspɪrətəri reɪt / (BrE)
the number of breaths a person takes per minute, commonly used as an indicator of health
number of breaths per minute
medical

Examples

The nurse carefully counted the patient's respiratory rate before recording the results.
An increased respiratory rate can be an early sign of infection or distress.
During exercise, your respiratory rate rises to supply more oxygen to your muscles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: breathing rate - number of breaths taken per minute

Etymology

From Latin 'respirare' (to breathe) and English 'rate'.
