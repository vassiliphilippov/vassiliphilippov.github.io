Canadianese term

1. NOUN

/ kəˌneɪ.di.əˈniz tɝm / (AmE), / kəˌneɪ.di.əˈniːz tɜːm / (BrE)
a word or phrase characteristic of Canadian English
word or phrase from Canadian English

Examples

He explained that 'toonie' is a Canadianese term for a two-dollar coin.
Her essay included several Canadianese terms unfamiliar to British readers.
Linguists often collect Canadianese terms to document regional language differences.

Synset Examples

She often uses Canadianese terms like 'eh' in conversations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Canadianism - a word or expression originating in or peculiar to Canada

Etymology

Formed from 'Canadian' + '-ese' (pertaining to language), on the model of terms like 'Americanese', to indicate Canadian English usage.
