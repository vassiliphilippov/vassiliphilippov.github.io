order Therapsida

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdər θəˈræpsɪdə / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə θəˈræpsɪdə / (BrE)
an extinct order of mammal-like reptiles that lived from the mid Permian to late Triassic period, found on all continents
extinct order of mammal-like reptiles

Examples

Fossils of order Therapsida have been discovered on nearly every continent.
The extinction of order Therapsida marked a significant transition in vertebrate evolution.
Paleontologists study order Therapsida to understand the origins of mammals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: therapsid - any member of the order Therapsida
  • Degree Lower: order Reptilia - the biological order including modern reptiles

Etymology

From Greek ther- (beast) + apsis (arch), referring to the anatomical features of the group.
