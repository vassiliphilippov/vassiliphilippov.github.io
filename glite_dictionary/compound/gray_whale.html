gray whale

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡreɪ ˌweɪl / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪ ˌweɪl / (BrE)
a medium-sized greyish-black baleen whale (Eschrichtius robustus) found in the northern Pacific Ocean
medium-sized greyish whale of the northern Pacific

Examples

The annual migration of the gray whale is one of the longest of any mammal.
We spotted a gray whale breaching off the coast of California last spring.
Marine biologists study the feeding habits of the gray whale in the Bering Sea.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: baleen whale - a whale that has baleen plates for filtering food from water
  • Synonym: blue whale - the largest species of baleen whale
