bully tree

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʊl.i ˌtri / (AmE), / ˈbʊl.i ˌtri / (BrE)
A tropical hardwood tree of the genus Manilkara, found in tropical America, that yields balata gum and produces heavy, reddish timber.
tropical tree yielding balata gum and red timber

Examples

The latex from the bully tree is used to make balata, a rubber-like substance.
Furniture made from bully tree wood is prized for its durability and color.
Many bully trees were harvested for their valuable timber in the 19th century.
