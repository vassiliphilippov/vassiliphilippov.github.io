mutton tallow

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌtn ˈtæloʊ / (AmE), / ˈmʌtn ˈtaləʊ / (BrE)
fat rendered from the body of a mature sheep, used in cooking, soap-making, and candle-making.
fat from a mature sheep, used in various products

Examples

Traditional soap makers often use mutton tallow as a base ingredient for its hardening properties.
Early pioneers relied on mutton tallow for making candles and preserving leather goods.
The chef rendered mutton tallow to fry potatoes, giving them a distinctive flavor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beef tallow - tallow obtained from cattle fat
  • Synonym: lard - fat rendered from pig meat, used in cooking
