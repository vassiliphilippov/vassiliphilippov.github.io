National Climatic Data Center

1. PROPN

/ ˌnæʃənl klaɪˈmætɪk ˈdeɪtə ˈsɛntər / (AmE), / ˌnæʃənəl klaɪˈmætɪk ˈdeɪtə ˈsentə / (BrE)
a division of the National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration (NOAA) responsible for maintaining the world’s largest active archive of weather and climate data
NOAA division archiving weather and climate data

Examples

The National Climatic Data Center houses extensive records of global temperatures and precipitation patterns.
Researchers accessed the National Climatic Data Center to analyze trends in extreme weather events.
According to the National Climatic Data Center, last year was one of the warmest on record.
