genus Colocasia

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˌkoʊloʊˈkeɪʒə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˌkɒləˈkeɪzɪə / (BrE)
A small genus of perennial tuberous plants in tropical Asia, including taro.
a genus of tropical Asian tuberous plants

Examples

Many dishes in Southeast Asia are made from the starchy corms of genus Colocasia.
The taro plant, a member of genus Colocasia, is widely cultivated for food.
Botanists studied several species within genus Colocasia to understand their diversity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: taro - a tropical plant grown for its edible starchy corm

Etymology

From New Latin 'Colocasia', the genus name.
