Thracian language

1. NOUN

/ θreɪˈʃiən ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / θreɪˈʃɪən ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
an extinct Indo-European language formerly spoken by the ancient Thracian people in Southeast Europe until the early Middle Ages
extinct language of ancient Thrace

Examples

The Thracian language vanished by the early Middle Ages, leaving few written records behind.
Scholars debate the relationship between the Thracian language and other Indo-European tongues.
Inscriptions in the Thracian language have provided valuable clues about ancient Balkan cultures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Phrygian language - another ancient Indo-European language, possibly related to Thracian

Etymology

From 'Thracian', pertaining to Thrace, an ancient region in Southeast Europe.
