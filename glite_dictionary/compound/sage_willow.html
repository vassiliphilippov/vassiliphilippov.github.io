sage willow

1. NOUN

/ seɪdʒ ˈwɪloʊ / (AmE), / seɪdʒ ˈwɪləʊ / (BrE)
A North American shrub with whitish, grayish leaves, often found in moist areas.
North American shrub with whitish leaves

Examples

The sage willow thrives along riverbanks in northern regions.
Botanists studied the distinctive leaves of the sage willow during their survey.
Wildlife often uses the dense branches of the sage willow for shelter.

2. NOUN

/ seɪdʒ ˈwɪloʊ / (AmE), / seɪdʒ ˈwɪləʊ / (BrE)
A willow shrub native to dry areas of the eastern United States, noted for its long, narrow leaves that are whitish and hairy underneath.
a willow shrub of eastern US with narrow, pale leaves

Examples

The sage willow thrives in dry, sandy soils along riverbanks in the eastern United States.
Botanists studied the leaves of the sage willow to understand their unique adaptation to drought.
She planted a sage willow in her garden to attract local wildlife.
