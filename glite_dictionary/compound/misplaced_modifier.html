misplaced modifier

1. NOUN

/ mɪsˈpleɪst ˈmɑdɪˌfaɪɚ / (AmE), / mɪsˈpleɪst ˈmɒdɪˌfaɪə / (BrE)
a word or phrase whose placement in a sentence causes it to appear to modify the wrong word, often creating ambiguity or unintended meaning
modifier in the wrong place in a sentence

Examples

The sentence contains a misplaced modifier that makes its meaning unclear.
Careful writers avoid using a misplaced modifier to prevent confusion.
The classic example of a misplaced modifier is, 'She found a gold man's watch.'

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dangling modifier - modifier with no clear subject to modify, often at the beginning of a sentence
