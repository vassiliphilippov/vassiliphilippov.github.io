genus Nestor

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˈnɛstər / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈnɛstə / (BrE)
a genus of parrots comprising two extant species native to New Zealand and two extinct species from Norfolk Island and Chatham Island
genus of parrots from New Zealand and nearby islands
medical

Examples

The genus Nestor includes the well-known kea and kaka of New Zealand.
Research on the genus Nestor helps conservationists understand the threats facing these unique parrots.
Two species within the genus Nestor have sadly become extinct in recent centuries.

Etymology

From Latin 'Nestor', from Greek mythology, Nestor being a wise king; used as the genus name in taxonomy.
