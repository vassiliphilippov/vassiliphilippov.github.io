rete Malpighii

1. NOUN

/ ˌriːti mælˈfiːɡi.aɪ / (AmE), / ˌriːti mælˈpiːɡi.aɪ / (BrE)
the deepest layer of the epidermis, consisting of living cells that divide to form new skin cells
the deepest layer of the epidermis
medical

Examples

The rete Malpighii is essential for the regeneration of new skin cells.
Damage to the rete Malpighii can impair the skin’s ability to heal efficiently.
In histology, the rete Malpighii appears as the innermost layer of the epidermis.

Etymology

From Latin rete (net) and Marcello Malpighi (Italian anatomist, 1628–1694), who first described it.
