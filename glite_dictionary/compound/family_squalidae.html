family Squalidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ˈskwæliˌdi / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli ˈskwæliːdiː / (BrE)
A family of dogfishes characterized by having a spine in each dorsal fin.
a family of spiny dogfish sharks

Examples

The family Squalidae includes several species of small, spiny dogfish found in oceans worldwide.
Researchers are studying the reproductive habits of sharks within the family Squalidae.
Unlike other shark families, members of the family Squalidae have a distinct spine in both dorsal fins.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dogfish - any of several small sharks of the family Squalidae

Etymology

From Latin 'Squalus' (a kind of sea fish) plus the taxonomic suffix '-idae' used for animal families.
