super-speed

1. NOUN

/ ˈsuːpər spiːd / (AmE), / ˈsuːpə spiːd / (BrE)
The capability of moving or acting with extreme rapidity, especially beyond normal human ability, often used in science fiction and superhero contexts.
extremely rapid speed (esp. in fiction)

Examples

The hero's super-speed allowed her to travel across the city in mere seconds.
Thanks to his super-speed, he could outrun any vehicle on the highway.
Many comic book characters possess super-speed as one of their extraordinary powers.

Synset Examples

Before they can make a second attempt, however, Allen is again struck by lightning, which causes him to regain his super-speed.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: speed - the rate at which someone or something moves or operates
  • Degree Higher: supersonic - moving faster than the speed of sound

Etymology

Formed from 'super-' (above, beyond) and 'speed'.
