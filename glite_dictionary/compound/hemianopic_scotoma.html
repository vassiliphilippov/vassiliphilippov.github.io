hemianopic scotoma

1. NOUN

/ ˌhiː.mi.əˈnɑː.pɪk skoʊˈtoʊ.mə / (AmE), / ˌhiː.mi.əˈnɒ.pɪk skəʊˈtəʊ.mə / (BrE)
A blind spot affecting one half of the visual field, usually resulting from damage to the visual pathways in the brain.
blind spot in half the visual field
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with a hemianopic scotoma following a stroke.
Vision tests revealed a hemianopic scotoma affecting the left visual field.
A hemianopic scotoma can significantly impair daily activities like reading or driving.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scotoma - an area of partial or complete loss of vision surrounded by a field of normal vision

Etymology

From Greek 'hemi-' (half), 'anopsia' (blindness), and 'scotoma' (darkness, blind spot).
