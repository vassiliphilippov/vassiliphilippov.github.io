curlew sandpiper

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɜr.lu ˈsændˌpaɪ.pɚ / (AmE), / ˈkɜː.ljuː ˈsændˌpaɪ.pə / (BrE)
a small migratory sandpiper (Calidris ferruginea) of the Old World with a curved bill resembling that of a curlew
a small Old World sandpiper with a curved bill

Examples

The curlew sandpiper is often seen along coastal mudflats during migration season.
With its long, down-curved bill, the curlew sandpiper is easily identified among other waders.
Flocks of curlew sandpipers gather at wetlands to rest and feed before continuing their journey.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sandpiper - a small wading bird of the family Scolopacidae
