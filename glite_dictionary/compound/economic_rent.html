economic rent

1. NOUN

/ ˌiːkəˈnɑmɪk rɛnt / (AmE), / ˌiːkəˈnɒmɪk rɛnt / (BrE)
the excess return obtained from land or another factor of production compared to the minimum necessary to keep it in use, especially as derived from more productive land relative to the poorest cultivated land under similar conditions
excess return from land or resource use

Examples

Landowners can earn economic rent when their property is more productive than the least productive land in use.
The concept of economic rent is fundamental to classical economics and land taxation theory.
Farmers paying more for better soil are essentially paying the economic rent of the land.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rent - payment for use of property or land

Etymology

From 'economic' (pertaining to economics) + 'rent' from Old French rente, from Latin reddita (thing given back).
