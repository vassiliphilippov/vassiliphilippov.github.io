rupestrine plant

1. NOUN

/ ruˈpɛstrin plænt / (AmE), / ruːˈpɛstrɪn plɑːnt / (BrE)
a plant that grows among rocks or rocky habitats
plant growing among rocks

Examples

The botanist discovered a rare rupestrine plant thriving in the mountain crevices.
Some rupestrine plants have adapted to survive with minimal soil or water.
The garden was designed to mimic the natural habitat of rupestrine plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: epilithic plant - a plant that grows on the surface of rocks

Etymology

From Latin 'rupestris' (rocky, pertaining to rocks) + 'plant'.
