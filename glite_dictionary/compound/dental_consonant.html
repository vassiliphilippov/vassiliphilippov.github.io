dental consonant

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛnt̬l ˈkɑn.sə.nənt / (AmE), / ˈdɛntl ˈkɒn.sə.nənt / (BrE)
a consonant sound produced by placing the tip of the tongue against or near the upper front teeth or the gum ridge
consonant produced with tongue near the teeth

Examples

The sounds /t̪/ and /d̪/ in some languages are classified as dental consonants.
Linguists often compare dental consonant pronunciation across different languages.
Spanish has more dental consonants than English does.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: alveolar consonant - a consonant articulated with the tongue against the alveolar ridge
  • Degree Lower: labiodental consonant - a consonant articulated with the lower lip against the upper teeth

Etymology

From Latin dentalis (of teeth) and consonant.
