tail assembly

1. NOUN

/ ˈteɪl əˌsɛmbli / (AmE), / ˈteɪl əˌsemblɪ / (BrE)
the rear structural section of an aircraft, typically including the fin, rudder, and horizontal stabilizer, which helps control flight stability and direction
rear structural section of an aircraft

Examples

The inspector examined the tail assembly for signs of structural damage.
During the preflight check, the pilot noticed a problem with the tail assembly.
A strong wind gust can put added stress on the tail assembly during landing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: empennage - the entire tail section of an aircraft, including all stabilizing surfaces
