coddled egg

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑd.əld ɛɡ / (AmE), / ˈkɒd.əld ɛɡ / (BrE)
An egg cooked briefly in its shell in gently boiling water, so that the white is set but the yolk remains soft.
egg cooked briefly in the shell

Examples

He prefers a coddled egg for breakfast because the yolk stays soft and creamy.
To make a coddled egg, place the egg in barely simmering water for just a few minutes.
The recipe calls for a coddled egg on top of toasted bread.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: boiled egg - an egg cooked in its shell in boiling water until both white and yolk are set
  • Synonym: poached egg - an egg cooked without its shell in simmering water

Etymology

From the verb 'coddle' (to cook gently), likely from earlier English and possibly influenced by French 'caudle' (a warm drink).
