widdle wave

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪd.əl weɪv / (AmE), / ˈwɪd.əl weɪv / (BrE)
a small or playful wave, typically given in a childlike or affectionate manner
small or playful wave
informal

Examples

She gave a quick widdle wave to her friends before boarding the bus.
The toddler offered a shy widdle wave to the neighbors across the street.
He couldn't help but smile when his niece greeted him with a widdle wave.

Synset Examples

He gave her a widdle wave as he left.

Etymology

Formed from 'little', playfully altered to 'widdle', and 'wave'.
