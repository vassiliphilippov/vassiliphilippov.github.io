black-tailed deer

1. NOUN

/ ˌblæk ˈteɪld ˌdɪr / (AmE), / ˌblæk ˈteɪld ˌdɪə / (BrE)
a deer found in western North America, characterized by a black-tipped tail and considered a subspecies of the mule deer
a North American deer with a black-tipped tail

Examples

We spotted a black-tailed deer grazing near the edge of the forest at sunrise.
The black-tailed deer is often seen in the coastal areas of the Pacific Northwest.
Researchers tracked the migration patterns of the black-tailed deer over several seasons.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mule deer - a large deer of western North America, closely related to the black-tailed deer
