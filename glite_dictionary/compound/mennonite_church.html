Mennonite Church

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛnəˌnaɪt tʃɜrtʃ / (AmE), / ˈmɛnəˌnaɪt tʃɜːtʃ / (BrE)
A Christian denomination originating from the 16th-century Anabaptist movement, known for its simple lifestyle and commitment to pacifism.
Christian denomination known for simple living

Examples

The Mennonite Church emphasizes community, peace, and simple living among its members.
She grew up attending a local Mennonite Church in rural Pennsylvania.
Many humanitarian projects are organized by the Mennonite Church around the world.

Etymology

Named after Menno Simons, an early leader of the Anabaptist movement.
