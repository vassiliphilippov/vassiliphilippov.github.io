Battle of the Teutoburg Forest

1. PROPN

/ ˈbæt̬.əl əv ðə tuːˈtoʊ.bɜːrɡ ˈfɔːr.ɪst / (AmE), / ˈbæt.əl əv ðə tjuːˈtɒb.ɜːɡ ˈfɒr.ɪst / (BrE)
a decisive battle in AD 9 in which Germanic tribes under Arminius destroyed three Roman legions, halting Roman expansion into Germania
decisive 9 AD battle, Germans defeated Romans

Examples

The Battle of the Teutoburg Forest marked a turning point in Roman military history.
Historians continue to study the Battle of the Teutoburg Forest for its impact on the Roman Empire.
The defeat at the Battle of the Teutoburg Forest ended Roman attempts to conquer Germania.
