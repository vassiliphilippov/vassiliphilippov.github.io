Canada goose

1. NOUN

/ ˈkænədə ɡus / (AmE), / ˈkanədə ɡuːs / (BrE)
a large, greyish-brown wild goose native to North America, noted for its black head and neck, white cheeks, and loud, trumpeting call
large North American wild goose

Examples

A flock of Canada geese settled on the pond, honking loudly as they landed.
The distinctive black neck of the Canada goose makes it easy to identify.
Each autumn, hundreds of Canada geese migrate south for the winter.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: goose - a large waterbird with a long neck, short legs, and webbed feet
