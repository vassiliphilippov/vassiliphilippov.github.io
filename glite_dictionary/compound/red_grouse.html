red grouse

1. NOUN

/ rɛd ɡraʊs / (AmE), / rɛd ɡraʊs / (BrE)
a reddish-brown grouse found on upland moors in Great Britain, known for its distinctive coloring and importance in game shooting
a moorland game bird of Great Britain

Examples

The red grouse is a familiar sight on the Scottish moors.
Conservationists are concerned about declining red grouse populations.
Shooting red grouse is a traditional part of the hunting season in Britain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grouse - any of several gallinaceous birds of the family Tetraonidae
