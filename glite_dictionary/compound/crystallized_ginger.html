crystallized ginger

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɪstəˌlaɪzd ˈdʒɪndʒər / (AmE), / ˈkrɪstəlaɪzd ˈdʒɪndʒə / (BrE)
Gingerroot that has been cooked in sugar syrup and coated with sugar, eaten as a sweet or used in baking.
candied gingerroot pieces

Examples

My grandmother always keeps a tin of crystallized ginger on her kitchen counter for guests.
The recipe calls for two tablespoons of chopped crystallized ginger to add sweetness and spice.
After dinner, she offered us pieces of crystallized ginger as a digestive treat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: candied ginger - gingerroot preserved with sugar
