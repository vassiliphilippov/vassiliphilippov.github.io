sundew plant

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌnˌdu ˌplænt / (AmE), / ˈsʌn.djuː ˌplɑːnt / (BrE)
any of various insect-eating bog plants of the genus Drosera, characterized by sticky, hair-covered leaves that trap and digest insects
insect-eating bog plant of genus Drosera

Examples

The sundew plant uses its sticky leaves to capture unsuspecting insects.
In their natural habitat, sundew plants thrive in nutrient-poor boggy soils.
A sundew plant can curl its leaves around trapped prey to aid digestion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Drosera - the genus containing sundew plants
  • Synonym: carnivorous plant - a plant that traps and digests insects

Etymology

From 'sundew,' referring to the dew-like droplets on the plant's leaves, and 'plant.'
