hydrogen electrode

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪdrədʒən ɪˈloʊˌdroʊd / (AmE), / ˈhaɪdrədʒən ɪˈləʊdrəʊd / (BrE)
an electrode in which hydrogen gas is adsorbed onto platinum, used as a standard reference in electrochemistry to measure electrode potentials
electrode using hydrogen gas and platinum as reference

Examples

The hydrogen electrode is commonly used as a reference in electrochemical measurements.
Accurate pH readings can be obtained with the help of a hydrogen electrode.
She calibrated the device using a hydrogen electrode to ensure reliability.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: standard hydrogen electrode - the primary reference electrode based on the hydrogen electrode system
