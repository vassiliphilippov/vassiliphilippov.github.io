lunar crater

1. NOUN

/ ˈluːnɚ ˈkreɪtɚ / (AmE), / ˈluːnə ˈkreɪtə / (BrE)
A crater on the surface of the Moon, typically formed by the impact of a meteorite.
a crater on the Moon's surface

Examples

Astronomers are studying the formation of lunar craters caused by asteroid impacts.
The Apollo missions landed near a large lunar crater to collect rock samples.
From Earth, some lunar craters are visible with a simple telescope.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crater - a large, bowl-shaped cavity in the ground

Etymology

lunar from Latin 'lunaris' (of the moon) + crater from Greek 'krater' (mixing bowl, then bowl-shaped depression).

2. NOUN

/ ˈluːnɚ ˈkreɪtɚ / (AmE), / ˈluːnə ˈkreɪtə / (BrE)
a depression on the surface of the Moon, typically formed by the impact of a meteorite or asteroid
depression on the Moon's surface

Examples

The astronauts discovered an unusually large lunar crater near their landing site.
Scientists believe the lunar crater was formed by a massive meteor impact millions of years ago.
The telescope revealed the sharp edges of a lunar crater under the full moon's light.

Synset Examples

The astronauts explored the lunar crater.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crater - a large, bowl-shaped cavity in the ground or on a celestial body

Etymology

From Latin 'luna' (moon) and 'crater' (bowl, basin).
