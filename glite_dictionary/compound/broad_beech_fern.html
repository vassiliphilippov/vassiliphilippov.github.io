broad beech fern

1. NOUN

/ brɔd bitʃ fɝn / (AmE), / brɔːd biːtʃ fɜːn / (BrE)
a species of beech fern native to North American woodlands, characterized by straw-colored stripes
North American beech fern species

Examples

The broad beech fern thrives in the shade of North American forests.
We identified several broad beech ferns by their unique straw-colored stripes.
The botanist photographed a large patch of broad beech fern near the creek.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beech fern - any of several ferns of the genus Phegopteris, commonly called beech fern
