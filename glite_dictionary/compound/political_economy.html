political economy

1. NOUN

/ pəˌlɪd.ɪ.kəl ɪˈkɑː.nə.mi / (AmE), / pəˌlɪt.ɪ.kəl ɪˈkɒn.ə.mi / (BrE)
the branch of social science concerned with the production, distribution, and consumption of goods and services, and how these processes are managed and influenced by governments and policies
study of economics and government policies

Examples

She is a professor of political economy at the university, focusing on how policies affect economic growth.
The book explores the political economy of developing countries in the context of globalization.
Studying political economy helps us understand why economic decisions are often influenced by politics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: economics - the social science of production, distribution, and consumption of goods and services

Etymology

From French 'économie politique', based on Greek 'politikos' (of citizens) and 'oikonomia' (household management).
