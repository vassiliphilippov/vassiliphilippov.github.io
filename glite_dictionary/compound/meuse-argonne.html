Meuse-Argonne

1. PROPN

/ mjuːz ɑːrˈɡɒn / (AmE), / mjuz ɑːˈɡɒn / (BrE)
the final major Allied offensive of World War I, in which American forces attacked and pushed back German troops in the Meuse-Argonne region of France in 1918
major World War I American-led military offensive

Examples

Many American soldiers lost their lives during the Meuse-Argonne campaign in 1918.
The Meuse-Argonne battle was pivotal in bringing World War I to an end.
Visiting the Meuse-Argonne Cemetery, she reflected on the cost of war.

Etymology

Named after the Meuse River and the Argonne Forest, the geographic locations in France where the battle took place.
