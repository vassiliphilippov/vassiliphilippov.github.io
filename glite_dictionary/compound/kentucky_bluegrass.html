Kentucky bluegrass

1. NOUN

/ kənˈtʌki ˈbluˌɡræs / (AmE), / kɛnˈtʌki ˈbluːˌɡrɑːs / (BrE)
a species of grass native to Europe and widely grown in North America, valued for its fine texture, bright green color, and use in lawns and pastures
a fine-leaved grass used in lawns and pastures

Examples

The newly seeded lawn contains mostly Kentucky bluegrass because of its vibrant color and durability.
Cattle often graze on fields planted with Kentucky bluegrass in the central United States.
Many golf courses use Kentucky bluegrass for its fine texture and lush appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bluegrass - a type of grass with bluish-green blades

2. NOUN

/ kənˈtʌki ˈbluːˌɡræs / (AmE), / kɪnˌtʌki ˈbluːɡrɑːs / (BrE)
a valuable meadow and pasture grass native to Europe and especially central United States, known for its tall stalks and slender bright green leaves; commonly used in lawns and pasture mixtures
species of grass used in lawns and pastures

Examples

The lush lawn was seeded with Kentucky bluegrass for its rich color and durability.
Farmers often prefer Kentucky bluegrass as pasture for their livestock because of its resilience and nutrition.
Our local golf course maintains its fairways with a blend dominated by Kentucky bluegrass.

Etymology

Named after the state of Kentucky and its bluish-green leaves; scientific name Poa pratensis from Latin.
