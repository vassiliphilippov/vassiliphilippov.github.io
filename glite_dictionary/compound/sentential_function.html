sentential function

1. NOUN

/ sɛnˈtɛnʃəl ˈfʌŋkʃən / (AmE), / sɛnˈtɛnʃəl ˈfʌŋkʃən / (BrE)
A formal expression containing variables that becomes a sentence when its variables are replaced by constants, used especially in logic and mathematics.
expression with variables that becomes a sentence

Examples

The logician explained that a sentential function becomes a proposition when its variables are instantiated.
In formal logic, a sentential function may be true or false depending on the values assigned to its variables.
Students learned how to convert a sentential function into a complete sentence by replacing variables with constants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: open sentence - an equation or statement containing variables that is neither true nor false until the variables are specified

Etymology

From Latin 'sententia' (thought, opinion, sentence) and late Latin 'functio' (a performing, execution).
