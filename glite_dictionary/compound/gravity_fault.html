gravity fault

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡrævəti fɔlt / (AmE), / ˈɡravɪti fɔːlt / (BrE)
an inclined geological fault in which the hanging wall has moved downward relative to the footwall, typically caused by gravity
downward-slipping geological fault

Examples

The earthquake was caused by a major gravity fault deep beneath the Earth's surface.
Geologists mapped the area and identified several gravity faults near the river basin.
A gravity fault typically forms in regions where the Earth's crust is being extended.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: normal fault - a fault where the hanging wall slides downward relative to the footwall
