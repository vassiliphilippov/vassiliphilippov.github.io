safety-valve mechanism

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪfti vælv məˈkænɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈseɪfti vɑːlv mɪˈkanɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
a device or system designed to automatically release pressure in order to prevent excessive buildup and ensure safe operation
device for releasing excess pressure

Examples

The engineer inspected the safety-valve mechanism to ensure it functioned correctly under high pressure.
Most boilers are equipped with a safety-valve mechanism to prevent dangerous accidents.
When the pressure reached a critical level, the safety-valve mechanism released steam to stabilize the system.

Synset Examples

The safety-valve mechanism ensures pressure is controlled.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: relief valve - a valve that opens to relieve excess pressure
