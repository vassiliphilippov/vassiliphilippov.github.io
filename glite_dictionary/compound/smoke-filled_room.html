smoke-filled room

1. NOUN

/ ˌsmoʊk fɪld ˈruːm / (AmE), / ˌsməʊk fɪld ˈruːm / (BrE)
(often figurative) A room literally or figuratively filled with smoke, typically used to refer to an environment where secret or unofficial decisions are made, especially in politics.
secretive meeting place, often political

Examples

The party leader was chosen in a smoke-filled room away from the public eye.
Negotiations continued late into the night in a smoke-filled room above the restaurant.
Rumors swirled about deals struck in smoke-filled rooms rather than open meetings.

Synset Examples

A smoke-filled room greeted us as we entered.
They discussed plans in a smoke-filled room.
The meeting was held in a smoke-filled room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: backroom - A place or situation where decisions are made privately, away from public view
