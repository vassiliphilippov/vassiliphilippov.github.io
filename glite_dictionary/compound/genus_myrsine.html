genus Myrsine

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs mɪrˈsaɪni / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs mɪˈsiːni / (BrE)
A genus of evergreen trees and shrubs with aromatic foliage, native to regions including Africa and New Zealand.
genus of evergreen trees and shrubs

Examples

The genus Myrsine includes many species with glossy, aromatic leaves.
Botanists have discovered a new shrub in the genus Myrsine in New Zealand.
Members of the genus Myrsine are commonly found in subtropical forests.

Etymology

From Latin 'myrsine', from Greek 'myrsinē' meaning myrtle.
