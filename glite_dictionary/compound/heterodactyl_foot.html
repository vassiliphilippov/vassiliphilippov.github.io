heterodactyl foot

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɛt̬əroʊˈdæktaɪl fʊt / (AmE), / ˌhɛtərəʊˈdæktaɪl fʊt / (BrE)
A bird's foot in which the first and second toes point backward, and the third and fourth toes point forward.
bird's foot with two toes back, two forward

Examples

The trogon is known for its unique heterodactyl foot structure.
Few bird families exhibit a heterodactyl foot, making it a useful trait for identification.
Researchers examined the anatomy of the heterodactyl foot to understand its evolutionary advantages.

Etymology

From Greek heteros (different) + daktylos (finger, toe).
