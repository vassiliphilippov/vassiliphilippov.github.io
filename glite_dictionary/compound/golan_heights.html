Golan Heights

1. PROPN

/ ˈɡoʊlɑn ˈhaɪts / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊlæn ˈhaɪts / (BrE)
a region of highlands located between southern Lebanon and southern Syria, known for its strategic military significance and disputed control
hilly border region in the Middle East

Examples

The Golan Heights have long been a point of contention in Middle Eastern politics.
Much of the Golan Heights is controlled by Israel, though its status is disputed internationally.
Tourists visit the Golan Heights for its natural beauty, vineyards, and historical sites.

Synset Examples

artillery on the Golan Heights can dominate a large area of Israel

Etymology

From Hebrew 'Ramat Ha-Golan', meaning 'Golan Heights'.
