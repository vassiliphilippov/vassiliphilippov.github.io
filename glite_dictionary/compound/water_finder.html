water finder

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔt̬ɚ ˌfaɪndɚ / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə ˌfaɪndə / (BrE)
a forked stick or rod used in dowsing to locate underground water or oil
tool for dowsing water or oil underground

Examples

The old farmer swore by his water finder to locate new wells on his property.
Before drilling, they often employ a water finder to decide where to dig.
Skeptics doubt the effectiveness of a water finder, calling it a pseudoscience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: divining rod - a stick used for dowsing to find water or minerals underground
