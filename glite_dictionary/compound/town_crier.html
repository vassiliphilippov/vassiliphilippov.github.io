town crier

1. NOUN

/ taʊn ˈkraɪər / (AmE), / taʊn ˈkraɪə / (BrE)
an official in former times whose job was to make public announcements in the streets, often by ringing a bell
official who announced news publicly (historical)

Examples

The town crier rang his bell and shouted the latest news in the market square.
In some festivals, a town crier still appears to entertain the crowd with announcements.
Before newspapers, the town crier was relied upon to spread important information.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: herald - an official messenger, especially in former times
