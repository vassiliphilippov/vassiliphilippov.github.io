antifouling paint

1. NOUN

/ ˌæn.tiˈfaʊ.lɪŋ peɪnt / (AmE), / ˌæn.tiˈfaʊ.lɪŋ peɪnt / (BrE)
A paint applied to underwater surfaces to prevent the growth of barnacles, algae, and other marine organisms.
paint that prevents marine growth on surfaces

Examples

We applied antifouling paint to the boat’s hull before launching it for the season.
Regular use of antifouling paint can help maintain a ship’s performance and reduce maintenance costs.
Many marinas require vessels to use antifouling paint to prevent the spread of invasive species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antifouling - preventing the accumulation of unwanted organisms on submerged surfaces

Etymology

From 'anti-' (against) + 'fouling' (making dirty), referring to preventing fouling of surfaces.
