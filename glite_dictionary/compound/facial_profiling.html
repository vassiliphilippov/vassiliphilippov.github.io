facial profiling

1. NOUN

/ ˈfeɪʃəl ˈproʊfaɪlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfeɪʃəl ˈprəʊfaɪlɪŋ / (BrE)
The practice of identifying individuals, especially criminals or terrorists, by analyzing videotaped images of their faces, often for security purposes.
security identification by analyzing faces

Examples

The airport has implemented facial profiling to improve passenger security screening.
Concerns about privacy have been raised with the widespread use of facial profiling in public spaces.
Law enforcement agencies are exploring the use of facial profiling to identify suspects more efficiently.

Synset Examples

facial profiling is a new form of airport security

Related Terms

  • Synonym: facial recognition - automated process of identifying individuals by analyzing facial features
