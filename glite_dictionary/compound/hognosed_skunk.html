hognosed skunk

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑɡˌnoʊzd skʌŋk / (AmE), / ˈhɒɡˌnəʊzd skʌŋk / (BrE)
a large skunk with a bare, pig-like snout and a distinctive white back and tail, native to southwestern North America and Mexico
large skunk with bare snout, white back and tail

Examples

The hognosed skunk is easily recognized by its pig-like snout and striking coloration.
Farmers in the region sometimes spot a hognosed skunk searching for insects at night.
Unlike other skunk species, the hognosed skunk prefers open habitats and grassy plains.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skunk - a small mammal known for its ability to spray a pungent odor
