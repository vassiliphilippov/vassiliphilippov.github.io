Kodiak bear

1. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊdiˌæk bɛr / (AmE), / ˈkəʊdiˌæk beə / (BrE)
a very large subspecies of the brown bear found on the coasts of Alaska, especially on Kodiak Island, and in British Columbia
large brown bear of Alaska and British Columbia

Examples

The Kodiak bear is famous for its immense size and strength.
Scientists are studying the habitat of the Kodiak bear on Kodiak Island.
A fully grown Kodiak bear can weigh over 1,500 pounds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brown bear - a large bear species, Ursus arctos

Etymology

Named after Kodiak Island in Alaska, where this bear subspecies is native.
