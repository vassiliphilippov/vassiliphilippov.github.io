war zone

1. NOUN

/ wɔr zoʊn / (AmE), / wɔː zəʊn / (BrE)
an area where fighting between armed forces is taking place, especially a region designated for military operations during a conflict
area where military conflict occurs

Examples

Reporters risked their lives to enter the war zone and cover the ongoing conflict.
Many civilians have been displaced from the war zone in search of safety.
The city was declared a war zone after heavy fighting erupted between rival factions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: combat zone - an area where active fighting is taking place
  • Antonym: peace zone - an area free from conflict or hostilities

2. NOUN

/ wɔr zoʊn / (AmE), / wɔː zəʊn / (BrE)
an area where armed conflict is taking place or a situation marked by chaos and danger
area with active conflict or chaos

Examples

Journalists often risk their lives to report from a war zone.
After the hurricane, the city looked like a war zone with debris everywhere.
The negotiations failed, and the region quickly descended into a war zone.

Synset Examples

Traveling through a war zone requires careful planning and precautions.
The comments section was a war zone reminiscent of Douchebagistan.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: battlefield - a place where a battle is fought
