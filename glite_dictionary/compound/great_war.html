Great War

1. NOUN

/ ɡreɪt wɔr / (AmE), / ɡreɪt wɔː / (BrE)
the war fought mainly in Europe from 1914 to 1918 between the Allies and the Central Powers, later known as World War I
World War I (1914–1918)

Examples

Many historians have studied the causes and consequences of the Great War.
His grandfather served in the trenches during the Great War.
The devastation of the Great War shaped an entire generation’s outlook on life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: World War I - the First World War (1914–1918)
  • Antonym: World War II - the Second World War (1939–1945)

Etymology

Used before 'World War I' became common; 'great' means 'major' or 'vast'.
