rise of idiocracy

1. NOUN

/ raɪz əv ˌɪdiˈɑkrəsi / (AmE), / raɪz əv ˌɪdɪˈɒkrəsi / (BrE)
an increase in foolish or irrational governance or social organization, often used to criticize the perceived decline in quality of leadership or decision-making
increase in foolish or irrational governance

Examples

Some observers believe the rise of idiocracy is threatening effective governance worldwide.
The film satirized the rise of idiocracy in a future society.
Social commentators argue that the rise of idiocracy can be seen in current political trends.

Synset Examples

Critics warn against the rise of idiocracy in politics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: idiocracy - government or rule by people considered to be foolish or incompetent

Etymology

From 'idiocracy', modeled after 'bureaucracy'; popularized by the 2006 satirical film 'Idiocracy'.
