Levant morocco

1. NOUN

/ lɪˈvænt məˈroʊkoʊ / (AmE), / lɪˈvɑːnt məˈrəʊkəʊ / (BrE)
a type of heavy, high-quality morocco leather with a pronounced grain, often used in bookbinding
heavy grained morocco leather for bookbinding

Examples

The rare volume was bound in Levant morocco with gilt lettering on the spine.
Collectors value books bound in Levant morocco for their durability and beauty.
The binder recommended Levant morocco for this limited edition to preserve it for generations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: morocco - soft leather made chiefly from goatskin, especially originally from Morocco

Etymology

From 'Levant', a historical term for the eastern Mediterranean region, and 'morocco', a fine leather originally from Morocco.
