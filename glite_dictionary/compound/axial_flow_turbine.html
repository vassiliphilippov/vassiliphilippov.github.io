axial flow turbine

1. NOUN

/ ˈæk.si.əl floʊ tɝːˈbaɪn / (AmE), / ˈæk.si.əl fləʊ ˈtɜː.baɪn / (BrE)
A type of turbine in which the working fluid flows parallel to the axis of rotation, commonly used in jet engines and power generation.
turbine with axial fluid flow

Examples

The axial flow turbine is widely used in modern jet engines due to its efficiency.
Engineers chose an axial flow turbine for the power plant to accommodate the high flow rate.
Unlike a radial design, an axial flow turbine allows the fluid to pass straight through along the shaft.

Synset Examples

Whereas for an axial turbine the rotor is ‘impacted’ by the air flow, for a radial turbine, the flow is smoothly orientated at 90 degrees by the compressor towards the combustion chamber and driving the turbine in the same way water drives a watermill.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: radial flow turbine - turbine where the fluid flows perpendicular to the rotation axis

Etymology

Formed from 'axial' (relating to an axis) and 'turbine', influenced by technical engineering language.
