helmet flower

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛlmɪt ˌflaʊər / (AmE), / ˈhɛlmɪt ˌflaʊə / (BrE)
a poisonous plant native to northern Europe, with hooded blue-purple flowers; its dried leaves and roots yield aconite
poisonous European herb with hooded blue-purple flowers

Examples

The helmet flower is admired for its striking blue-purple blossoms but must be handled with care.
Botanists warn that every part of the helmet flower is highly toxic to humans and animals.
She planted a helmet flower in her garden, unaware of its poisonous properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aconite - another name for the helmet flower; a poisonous plant also known as monkshood
