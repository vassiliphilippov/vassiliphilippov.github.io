genus Coracias

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs kɔːˈreɪ.ʃi.əs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs kɒˈreɪ.si.əs / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Coraciidae, a group of brightly colored Old World birds known as rollers.
type genus of Coraciidae (rollers)

Examples

The genus Coracias includes several species of vividly colored rollers found in Africa and Eurasia.
Research on the genus Coracias has contributed valuable knowledge to ornithology.
Many naturalists are fascinated by the unique behaviors of birds within the genus Coracias.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Coraciidae - family of birds known as rollers

Etymology

From Latin 'Coracias', from Greek 'korakías', a kind of crow.
