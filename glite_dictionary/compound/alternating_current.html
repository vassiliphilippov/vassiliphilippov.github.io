alternating current

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔl.tɚˌneɪ.t̬ɪŋ ˈkɝː.rənt / (AmE), / ˈɔːl.təˌneɪ.tɪŋ ˈkʌr.ənt / (BrE)
An electric current that repeatedly reverses direction, typically in a sinusoidal pattern, as opposed to direct current.
electric current that reverses direction

Examples

Most homes are supplied with alternating current for their electrical appliances.
The generator can produce both direct and alternating current.
In physics class, we learned how alternating current operates in a circuit.

Synset Examples

In the US most household current is AC at 60 cycles per second

Related Terms

  • Antonym: direct current - an electric current flowing in one direction only
