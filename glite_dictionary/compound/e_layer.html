E layer

1. NOUN

/ iː ˈleɪɚ / (AmE), / iː ˈleɪə / (BrE)
a region of the Earth's ionosphere, located about 50 to 90 miles above the surface, that reflects medium-length radio waves
region of the ionosphere reflecting radio waves

Examples

Radio signals can travel long distances at night because they are reflected by the E layer of the ionosphere.
Scientists study changes in the E layer to improve radio communications.
During the experiment, the team measured ionization levels in the E layer.

Etymology

Named for being the fifth identified layer in the ionosphere, using the letter 'E'.
