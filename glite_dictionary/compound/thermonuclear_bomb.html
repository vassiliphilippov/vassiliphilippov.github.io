thermonuclear bomb

1. NOUN

/ ˌθɜr.moʊˈnu.kli.ɚ ˌbɑm / (AmE), / ˌθɜː.məˈnjuː.klɪ.ə ˌbɒm / (BrE)
A nuclear weapon that derives its explosive power from the fusion of light atomic nuclei, such as hydrogen, at extremely high temperatures.
nuclear weapon using fusion reactions

Examples

The thermonuclear bomb tested in 1952 was far more powerful than earlier atomic bombs.
Scientists explained the destructive mechanism of a thermonuclear bomb to the press.
The treaty aimed to prevent the spread of thermonuclear bombs to unstable regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hydrogen bomb - a nuclear bomb using fusion of hydrogen nuclei
  • Degree Lower: atomic bomb - a nuclear weapon using fission reactions

Etymology

Thermonuclear from thermo- (relating to heat) and nuclear (relating to the nucleus of an atom), referring to the high-temperature fusion reactions.
