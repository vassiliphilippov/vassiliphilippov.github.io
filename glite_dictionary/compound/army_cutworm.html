army cutworm

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːrmi ˌkʌtˌwɜrm / (AmE), / ˈɑːmi ˌkʌtˌwɜːm / (BrE)
the larva of a noctuid moth (Euxoa auxilliaris) that migrates in large groups and is destructive to grains and alfalfa crops, especially in the midwestern United States
agricultural pest caterpillar in North America

Examples

The army cutworm can devastate entire fields of wheat if not controlled early.
Farmers in the Midwest worry about army cutworm outbreaks each spring.
Researchers are developing new methods to monitor army cutworm populations in alfalfa crops.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cutworm - larvae of various moths that cut down young plants at the ground level

Etymology

So named because of their army-like group movements and their habit of cutting through crops.
