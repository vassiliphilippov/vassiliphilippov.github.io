South America

1. PROPN

/ saʊθ əˈmɛrɪkə / (AmE), / saʊθ əˈmɛrɪkə / (BrE)
the continent in the southern part of the Western Hemisphere, consisting of the countries south of Panama
continent and its countries in the Western Hemisphere

Examples

South America is home to the world's largest rainforest, the Amazon.
Many rare animal species are found only in South America.
Trade agreements between South America and Europe have increased in recent years.

Synset Examples

South America is an important market for goods from the United States

Related Terms

  • Synonym: South American - a person from South America; relating to South America

Etymology

From 'south' (direction) and 'America' (from Italian explorer Amerigo Vespucci).

2. PROPN

/ saʊθ əˈmɛrɪkə / (AmE), / saʊθ əˈmerɪkə / (BrE)
a continent in the western hemisphere, south of North America, extending from the Caribbean Sea to Cape Horn
continent in the western hemisphere

Examples

The Amazon rainforest is located in South America and is the largest tropical forest in the world.
Brazil is the largest country by area and population in South America.
Many unique species, such as llamas and capybaras, are native to South America.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: North America - continent directly north of South America

Etymology

From 'south', referring to its location relative to North America, and 'America', after Italian explorer Amerigo Vespucci.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09463304-n
Synset POS: n
