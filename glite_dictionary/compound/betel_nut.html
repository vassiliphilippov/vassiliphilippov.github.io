betel nut

1. NOUN

/ ˈbiːtəl nʌt / (AmE), / ˈbiːtəl nʌt / (BrE)
the seed of the betel palm, commonly chewed with betel leaves and lime for its stimulant and mildly narcotic effects, especially in South and Southeast Asia
seed of the betel palm chewed as a stimulant

Examples

In many parts of Southeast Asia, people chew betel nut as a traditional practice.
Chewing betel nut can stain teeth and cause long-term health issues.
The market was filled with stalls selling fresh betel nut and betel leaves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: areca nut - the seed of the areca palm, another name for betel nut

Etymology

From Portuguese 'betel', from Malayalam 'vettila' (betel leaf) and 'nut'.
