bushel of peaches

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʊʃəl əv ˈpiːtʃɪz / (AmE), / ˈbʊʃəl əv ˈpiːtʃɪz / (BrE)
a quantity of peaches equal to a standard bushel, used as a unit of measurement for fruit
a quantity of peaches equal to a standard bushel

Examples

We bought a bushel of peaches at the farmer's market for making preserves.
Each bushel of peaches weighed about fifty pounds when freshly picked.
Grandma always makes a pie whenever she gets a bushel of peaches in the summer.

Synset Examples

They filled the basket with a bushel of peaches.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bushel - a unit of dry volume used in the U.S. and Britain

Etymology

From 'bushel', a Middle English term from Old French 'boissel', meaning a dry measure of capacity.
