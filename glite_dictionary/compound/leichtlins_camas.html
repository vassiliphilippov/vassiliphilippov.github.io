Leichtlin's camas

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪtʃtlɪnz ˈkæməs / (AmE), / ˈlaɪtʃtlɪnz ˈkaməs / (BrE)
A species of camas plant (Camassia leichtlinii) native to regions west of the Cascade Mountains in North America, known for its star-shaped blue to purple flowers.
a species of camas plant from western North America

Examples

In spring, the fields west of the Cascades are dotted with the striking blue blooms of Leichtlin's camas.
Native American communities have traditionally harvested the bulbs of Leichtlin's camas as a food source.
Botanists documented how Leichtlin's camas thrives in moist meadows and open woods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: camas - A genus of plants with edible bulbs, native to North America

Etymology

Named after Max Leichtlin, a 19th-century German botanist, combined with 'camas' from the Nez Perce word 'qém’es' for the plant.
