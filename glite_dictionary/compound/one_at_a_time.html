one at a time

1. ADV

/ wʌn æt ə taɪm / (AmE), / wʌn æt ə taɪm / (BrE)
separately; each individually and not all together, in a sequence
each separately, in succession

Examples

Please enter the room one at a time so we can check your tickets.
He handled the issues one at a time rather than all at once.
Children, you may ask your questions one at a time so everyone gets a turn.

Synset Examples

the prisoners came out one by one

Related Terms

  • Synonym: one by one - each separately, one after another
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: r

2. ADV

/ wʌn ət ə taɪm / (AmE), / wʌn ət ə taɪm / (BrE)
separately or in sequence, with each instance or item dealt with before the next
separately; in sequence

Examples

The technician asked the customers to approach the counter one at a time.
Guests will be called one at a time to the registration desk.
It's safer for children to cross the stream one at a time on the narrow bridge.

Synset Examples

Please submit your assignments one at a time.
He prefers to tackle tasks one at a time.
