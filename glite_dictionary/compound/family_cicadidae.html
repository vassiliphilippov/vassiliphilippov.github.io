family Cicadidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli sɪˈkeɪdəˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli sɪˈkeɪdəˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of large, loud-voiced insects commonly known as cicadas, characterized by transparent wings and prominent eyes, found mostly in warm climates.
taxonomic family of cicadas

Examples

The family Cicadidae is well known for the loud, distinctive sounds produced by its members.
Scientists have identified over 3,000 species within the family Cicadidae worldwide.
Most of the insects in the family Cicadidae spend years underground before emerging to sing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cicada - an insect known for its loud, shrill song

Etymology

From modern Latin 'Cicadidae', from Latin 'cicada' (cicada, tree cricket).
