brown creeper

1. NOUN

/ braʊn ˈkriːpər / (AmE), / braʊn ˈkriːpə / (BrE)
a small North American songbird with a down-curved bill, known for creeping up tree trunks in search of insects
North American songbird with a curved bill

Examples

The brown creeper is often seen spiraling up tree trunks in search of insects.
Birdwatchers spotted a brown creeper during their morning walk in the woods.
Unlike woodpeckers, the brown creeper uses its tail for balance while climbing.
