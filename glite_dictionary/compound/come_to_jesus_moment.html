come to Jesus moment

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌm tə ˈdʒiːzəs ˈmoʊmənt / (AmE), / ˈkʌm tə ˈdʒiːzəs ˈməʊmənt / (BrE)
an occasion when someone realizes the truth about a situation or confronts an important issue, often resulting in a significant change in attitude or behavior
moment of major realization or reckoning

Examples

After failing two exams, he finally had a come to Jesus moment and started studying seriously.
The coach called a meeting that quickly became a come to Jesus moment for the entire team.
Her come to Jesus moment happened when she realized she needed a better work-life balance.

Synset Examples

She had a come to Jesus moment after the accident.
The meeting turned into a come-to-Jesus moment for the team.
The project needed a come-to-Jesus moment to realign goals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: epiphany - a moment of sudden and great revelation or realization
  • Synonym: reckoning - a time when one is called to account for one's actions or to pay debts

Etymology

From the phrase 'come to Jesus,' referring originally to a moment of religious conversion or confession, later broadened in figurative usage.
