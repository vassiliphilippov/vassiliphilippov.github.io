mountain pine

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaʊn.tən paɪn / (AmE), / ˈmaʊn.tɪn paɪn / (BrE)
an evergreen tree native to New Zealand, resembling the kawaka
New Zealand evergreen tree

Examples

The mountain pine thrives in the higher elevations of New Zealand’s forests.
Botanists studied the mountain pine to compare it to other native evergreens.
Hikers admired the dense foliage of the mountain pine along the trail.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmaʊntən paɪn / (AmE), / ˈmaʊntɪn paɪn / (BrE)
a tall pine tree native to western North America, with stout blue-green needles and mature grey-brown bark in rectangular plates
a tall pine tree of western North America

Examples

The forest was dominated by mountain pine, stretching high into the sky.
A mountain pine can be recognized by its blue-green needles and distinctive bark.
Wildfires can threaten vast stands of mountain pine in this region.

3. NOUN

/ ˈmaʊntn paɪn / (AmE), / ˈmaʊntɪn paɪn / (BrE)
A low, spreading pine tree (Pinus mugo) of central Europe, with short, bright green needles in bunches of two.
a shrubby pine tree of central Europe

Examples

The mountain pine is well adapted to survive in rocky Alpine soils.
Gardeners often use mountain pine for ornamental purposes in landscaping.
Unlike taller trees, the mountain pine remains low and bushy, even as it matures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Pinus mugo - scientific name for mountain pine
  • Antonym: Scots pine - a different species of pine tree common in Europe
