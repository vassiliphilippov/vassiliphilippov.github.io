superior thalamostriate vein

1. NOUN

/ suˈpɪriər θəˌlæmoʊˈstraɪ.eɪt veɪn / (AmE), / suˈpɪəriə θəˌlæməʊˈstraɪ.eɪt veɪn / (BrE)
a long vein in the brain that contributes to the formation of the internal cerebral vein
vein in the brain forming the internal cerebral vein
medical

Examples

The superior thalamostriate vein is an important structure for draining blood from the deep regions of the brain.
Damage to the superior thalamostriate vein can cause serious neurological complications.
During the procedure, the surgeon carefully avoided the superior thalamostriate vein to prevent bleeding.

Etymology

From 'thalamus' (Greek, chamber) and 'striate' (from Latin 'striatus', furrowed or striped), referring to the areas the vein drains.
