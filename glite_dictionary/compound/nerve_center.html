nerve center

1. NOUN

/ nɜrv ˈsɛntər / (AmE), / nɜːv ˈsɛntə / (BrE)
A cluster of nerve cells in the body that governs a specific process or function.
cluster of nerve cells governing a process
medical

Examples

The hypothalamus acts as a nerve center for regulating body temperature.
Damage to the nerve center in the brainstem can affect vital functions like breathing.
The speech nerve center is typically located in the left hemisphere of the brain.

Synset Examples

in most people the speech center is in the left hemisphere

2. NOUN

/ nɝv ˈsɛntɚ / (AmE), / nɜːv ˈsɛntə / (BrE)
A central place from which activities, information, or operations are directed or controlled.
central place of control or direction

Examples

The nerve center of the company's operations is located in New York City.
During the crisis, all communications were routed through the nerve center for rapid response.
The military established a secure nerve center to coordinate their activities.

Synset Examples

the nerve center of the diamond industry is in Amsterdam

Related Terms

  • Synonym: headquarters - main office or center of operations
  • Synonym: hub - center of activity or network

Etymology

From 'nerve' (inspired by the body's nervous system) and 'center'. Figurative use referring to the body's nerve centers controlling key functions.
