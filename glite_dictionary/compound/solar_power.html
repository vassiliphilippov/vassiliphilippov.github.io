solar power

1. NOUN

/ ˈsoʊlər ˈpaʊər / (AmE), / ˈsəʊlə ˈpaʊə / (BrE)
energy obtained by converting sunlight into electricity or heat, used to supply power to buildings and devices
energy from sunlight for power generation

Examples

Many homeowners are installing solar power systems to reduce their electricity bills.
The city invested in solar power as part of its environmental initiative.
During the summer, the house runs entirely on solar power generated from rooftop panels.

Synset Examples

the amount of energy falling on the earth is given by the solar constant, but very little use has been made of solar energy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: solar energy - energy from the sun, especially as used for electricity or heating

Etymology

From Latin 'solaris' (of the sun) and English 'power'.
