aniline dye

1. NOUN

/ ˈænɪlin daɪ / (AmE), / ˈænɪliːn daɪ / (BrE)
any of a group of synthetic dyes derived from aniline, used especially in textiles and industry.
synthetic dye made from aniline

Examples

Synthetic fabrics often use aniline dye for their bright and lasting colors.
In the nineteenth century, the discovery of aniline dye revolutionized the textile industry.
Proper gloves should be worn when handling aniline dye in the laboratory.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: synthetic dye - a dye made by chemical synthesis rather than extracted from natural sources

Etymology

From 'aniline', which comes from French 'aniline', from anil (indigo) from Arabic 'an-nīl', and 'dye', Old English 'dēag'.
