venae dorsales clitoridis superficiales

1. NOUN

/ ˌviː.niː dɔːrˈsælɛs klɪˈtɔːrɪdɪs ˌsuː.pɚˈfɪʃ.iˌæl.is / (AmE), / ˌviː.niː dɔːˈsælɛs klɪˈtɒrɪdɪs ˌsuː.pəˈfɪʃ.iˌæl.is / (BrE)
The superficial dorsal veins of the clitoris, running along its dorsum and draining into the external pudendal vein.
superficial dorsal veins of the clitoris
medical

Examples

The venae dorsales clitoridis superficiales are important when considering blood supply in certain surgical procedures.
An injury to the venae dorsales clitoridis superficiales can lead to localized swelling and hematoma formation.
Proper identification of the venae dorsales clitoridis superficiales is essential during reconstructive genital surgeries.

Etymology

Latin: 'venae' (veins), 'dorsales' (dorsal), 'clitoridis' (of the clitoris), 'superficiales' (superficial).
