Black Dutch

1. NOUN

/ blæk ˈdʌtʃ / (AmE), / blak ˈdʌtʃ / (BrE)
a term historically used in the United States to describe people of mixed ancestry, often of African, Native American, and European descent, with unclear or ambiguous origins
term for people of mixed ancestry (U.S. historical)
american

Examples

Many Southern families claim to have Black Dutch heritage in their family histories.
The origins of the term Black Dutch remain controversial among historians.
He told me that his grandmother always described their background as Black Dutch.

Synset Examples

The term Black Dutch is often misunderstood in history.
Many people in the area are proud to be Black Dutch.
He identified as Black Dutch due to his ancestry.

Etymology

From American English; the origin is uncertain but likely references ambiguous or concealed ancestry, with 'Dutch' sometimes used for 'Deutsch' (German).
