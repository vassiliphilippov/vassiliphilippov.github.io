to build to a crescendo

1. VERB

/ bɪld tə ə krəˈʃɛndoʊ / (AmE), / bɪld tə ə krɪˈʃendəʊ / (BrE)
to gradually increase in intensity or force, reaching a high point or climax
increase to a peak or climax

Examples

The tension in the courtroom built to a crescendo as the verdict was read.
The protesters’ chants built to a crescendo before the police arrived.
Her excitement built to a crescendo as she approached the finish line.

Synset Examples

The music built to a crescendo that left everyone breathless.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: climax - the most intense point in the development of something
  • Antonym: to subside - to decrease in intensity or become less active

Etymology

From Italian 'crescendo', meaning 'growing', used in music to indicate increasing volume.
