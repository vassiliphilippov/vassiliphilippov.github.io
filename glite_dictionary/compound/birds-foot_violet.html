bird's-foot violet

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɝdz ˌfʊt ˈvaɪələt / (AmE), / ˈbɜːdz ˌfʊt ˈvaɪələt / (BrE)
a violet of the eastern United States with large pale blue or purple flowers, resembling pansies
a North American violet flower species

Examples

The bird's-foot violet is known for the unique shape of its leaves and striking purple blooms.
In spring, clusters of bird's-foot violet add color to many eastern woodlands.
Gardeners often plant bird's-foot violet to attract butterflies and brighten shady areas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: violet - a small plant with purple, blue, or white flowers

Etymology

Named for the shape of its leaves, which resemble a bird's foot, combined with 'violet' for its flower.
