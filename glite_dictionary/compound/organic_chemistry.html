organic chemistry

1. NOUN

/ ɔrˈgænɪk ˈkɛmɪstri / (AmE), / ɔːˈgænɪk ˈkɛmɪstri / (BrE)
the branch of chemistry concerned with the study of compounds containing carbon, especially those of natural and synthetic origin
branch of chemistry studying carbon compounds

Examples

She decided to major in organic chemistry because she was fascinated by the structures of carbon compounds.
The exam covered several challenging topics in organic chemistry, including reaction mechanisms and functional groups.
Researchers in organic chemistry have developed new synthetic methods for creating complex molecules.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inorganic chemistry - the branch of chemistry dealing with inorganic compounds

Etymology

From 'organic' (relating to organisms) and 'chemistry' (from Greek 'khēmeia', art of transmuting metals).
