genus Prunus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˈpruːnəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈpruːnəs / (BrE)
A genus of shrubs and trees in the rose family (Rosaceae), including cherries, plums, peaches, apricots, and almonds, widely distributed in temperate regions.
genus of fruit trees and shrubs

Examples

The genus Prunus includes many well-known fruit trees such as cherries and plums.
Botanists study the genus Prunus to understand the diversity of stone fruits.
Almonds are actually part of the genus Prunus along with peaches and apricots.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Rosaceae - the rose family, a family of flowering plants

Etymology

From Latin 'prunus' (plum tree), scientific name assigned in botanical classification.
