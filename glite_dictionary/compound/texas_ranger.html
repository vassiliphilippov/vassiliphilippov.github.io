Texas Ranger

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛksəs ˈreɪndʒər / (AmE), / ˈtɛksəs ˈreɪndʒə / (BrE)
a member of the Texas state law enforcement agency, originally established as mounted lawmen to maintain order on the frontier
member of Texas state law enforcement
american

Examples

The Texas Ranger questioned several witnesses about the robbery.
As a Texas Ranger, she was respected for her dedication and bravery.
The legend of the Texas Ranger dates back to the early days of the American West.

Etymology

From Texas (the U.S. state) + ranger (a person who patrols a region), referring to members of the original force organized in Texas in the 19th century.
