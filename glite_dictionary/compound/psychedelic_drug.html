psychedelic drug

1. NOUN

/ ˌsaɪkəˈdɛlɪk drʌɡ / (AmE), / ˌsaɪkəˈdɛlɪk drʌɡ / (BrE)
A drug that produces hallucinations and profound changes in perception, mood, and thought processes.
hallucinogenic drug
medical

Examples

Many indigenous cultures use psychedelic drugs in religious rituals.
The use of psychedelic drugs in psychotherapy is being re-evaluated by researchers.
He described vivid visual patterns after taking a psychedelic drug.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hallucinogen - a substance that causes hallucinations

Etymology

From 'psychedelic', from Greek 'psyche' (mind) and 'deloun' (to make visible), referring to mind-altering effects.

2. NOUN

/ ˌsaɪkəˈdɛlɪk drʌɡ / (AmE), / ˌsaɪkəˈdɛlɪk drʌɡ / (BrE)
a drug that produces hallucinations or changes in perception, mood, and thought
drug that induces hallucinations

Examples

LSD is a well-known psychedelic drug used in experiments on consciousness.
The use of psychedelic drugs has influenced many artists and musicians over the decades.
Researchers are studying the effects of psychedelic drugs on depression and anxiety.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hallucinogen - a drug that causes hallucinations

Etymology

From Greek 'psyche' (mind) and 'deloun' (to make visible, reveal), coined in the 1950s.
