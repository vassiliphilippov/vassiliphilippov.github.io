Vatican Palace

1. NOUN

/ ˈvætɪkən ˈpælɪs / (AmE), / ˈvætɪkən ˈpælɪs / (BrE)
the official residence of the Pope in Vatican City, serving as the administrative center of the Roman Catholic Church
the Pope's residence in Vatican City

Examples

The Pope greeted thousands of visitors from the balcony of the Vatican Palace.
Many priceless works of art are kept within the walls of the Vatican Palace.
During their trip to Rome, they toured St. Peter’s Basilica and the Vatican Palace.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Apostolic Palace - another name for the Vatican Palace

Etymology

From Vatican, referring to Vatican City, and palace, from Latin palatium (palace).
