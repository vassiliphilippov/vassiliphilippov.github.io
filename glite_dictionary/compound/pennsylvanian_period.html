Pennsylvanian period

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛnsəlˈveɪniən ˈpɪriəd / (AmE), / ˌpɛnsᵻlˈveɪnɪən ˈpɪəriəd / (BrE)
the geological period from about 323 to 299 million years ago, marked by a warm climate and extensive swampy forests, notable for the formation of significant coal deposits
geological period in the late Carboniferous

Examples

Many coal beds in North America were formed during the Pennsylvanian period.
The warm, swampy environments of the Pennsylvanian period supported giant insects and dense forests.
Geologists study the Pennsylvanian period to understand the formation of ancient coal deposits.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Carboniferous period - the geological period encompassing the Mississippian and Pennsylvanian sub-periods
  • Degree Lower: Mississippian period - the earlier subperiod of the Carboniferous, preceding the Pennsylvanian

Etymology

Named after the U.S. state of Pennsylvania, where rocks from this period were first studied.
