California redwood

1. NOUN

/ kəˌlɪfɔrnjə ˈrɛdˌwʊd / (AmE), / kəˌlɪfɔːniə ˈrɛdwʊd / (BrE)
a very tall evergreen tree (Sequoia sempervirens) native to the coastal areas of California and southern Oregon, known for its great height and reddish-brown bark
very tall evergreen tree of coastal California and Oregon

Examples

The ancient California redwood forests attract thousands of tourists each year.
Standing under a towering California redwood makes you feel incredibly small.
The California redwood is known to live for over a thousand years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: redwood - a general term for trees with reddish timber, especially Sequoia species
