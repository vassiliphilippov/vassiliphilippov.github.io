bucket shop

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌkət ˌʃɑp / (AmE), / ˈbʌkɪt ˌʃɒp / (BrE)
(historical) A cheap saloon or bar where liquor was sold by the bucket, often of questionable quality.
cheap bar selling liquor by the bucket (historical)
archaic

Examples

In the 19th century, a bucket shop was a common sight in some city neighborhoods.
She avoided entering the bucket shop on the corner, knowing its reputation for watered-down whiskey.
Old newspaper articles detailed raids on bucket shops for selling alcohol illegally.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbʌk.ɪt ʃɑp / (AmE), / ˈbʌk.ɪt ʃɒp / (BrE)
A brokerage firm that engages in unethical, dishonest, or overly aggressive trading practices, often operating outside legal regulations.
unethical brokerage firm

Examples

The authorities shut down the bucket shop after it was found to be deceiving investors.
Many people lost money in the bucket shop scam during the stock market craze.
He later discovered that he had been dealing with a bucket shop rather than a legitimate brokerage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brokerage - a firm that arranges the buying and selling of securities
  • Synonym: investment firm - a company that invests clients' money

Etymology

Originally referred to venues that sold liquor from leftover 'buckets'; later extended in the late 19th century to fraudulent stock brokers.
