trench mortar

1. NOUN

/ trɛntʃ ˈmɔrtər / (AmE), / trɛntʃ ˈmɔːtə / (BrE)
a short-barreled, muzzle-loading gun designed to fire shells at a high angle over a short range, used especially in trench warfare
short-barreled gun for firing shells at high angles

Examples

The soldiers set up the trench mortar behind a barricade to defend their position.
During World War I, the use of the trench mortar revolutionized trench warfare with its high-angle fire.
A single round from a trench mortar could inflict serious damage on enemy fortifications.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mortar - a short smoothbore gun for firing shells (technically called mortars) at high angles

Etymology

From 'trench', referring to battlefield trenches, and 'mortar', a type of artillery.
