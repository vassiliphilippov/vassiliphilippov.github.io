Hanging Gardens of Babylon

1. PROPN

/ ˈhæŋɪŋ ˈɡɑrdənz əv ˈbæbɪˌlɑn / (AmE), / ˈhæŋɪŋ ˈɡɑːdnz əv ˈbæbɪlən / (BrE)
an ancient terraced garden in Babylon, reputedly built by Nebuchadnezzar II around 600 BC and considered one of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World.
ancient terraced garden in Babylon

Examples

Many historians question whether the Hanging Gardens of Babylon ever actually existed.
Tourists are fascinated by stories of the Hanging Gardens of Babylon and their legendary beauty.
Ancient texts describe the Hanging Gardens of Babylon as a marvel of engineering and design.
