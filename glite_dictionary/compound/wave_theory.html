wave theory

1. NOUN

/ weɪv ˈθɪəri / (AmE), / weɪv ˈθɪəri / (BrE)
The theory that light and other forms of energy are transmitted in the form of waves rather than particles.
theory that light travels as waves

Examples

The success of wave theory in explaining interference patterns was a major breakthrough in optics.
According to wave theory, light bends when passing through narrow slits due to diffraction.
Many phenomena, such as reflection and refraction, are better understood within the framework of wave theory.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: particle theory - the theory that light is transmitted as particles
