Lassa fever

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɑːsə ˌfiːvər / (AmE), / ˈlæsə ˌfiːvə / (BrE)
A severe, acute viral disease found in West Africa, marked by fever, muscle aches, inflammation, and sometimes difficulty swallowing, transmitted by rodents and potentially fatal.
acute viral disease of West Africa
medical

Examples

Doctors isolated the patient after she showed symptoms of Lassa fever following her trip to Nigeria.
Cases of Lassa fever rise during the dry season when contact with infected rodents increases.
The medical team wore protective gear to prevent the spread of Lassa fever in the hospital.

Etymology

Named after the town of Lassa in Nigeria, where the disease was first identified.
