tussock caterpillar

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʌsək ˈkætərˌpɪlər / (AmE), / ˈtʌsək ˈkætəpɪlə / (BrE)
the larva of a tussock moth, typically covered with tufts of hair or bristles
hairy caterpillar of a tussock moth

Examples

The tussock caterpillar is easily recognized by the distinctive tufts of hair along its body.
Farmers watched as tussock caterpillars devoured the leaves on their crops.
Despite their attractive appearance, tussock caterpillars can cause significant damage to trees and shrubs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tussock moth - a type of moth whose larva is the tussock caterpillar
  • Degree Higher: caterpillar - larval stage of a butterfly or moth
