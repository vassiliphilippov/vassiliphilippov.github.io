simple past

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪmpəl pæst / (AmE), / ˈsɪmpəl pɑːst / (BrE)
the form of a verb that describes an action completed in the past and not continuing into the present
past tense verb form (e.g. "walked")

Examples

The simple past of 'go' is 'went', not 'goed'.
You use the simple past to talk about finished events in the past.
In English, regular verbs take '-ed' for the simple past form.

Synset Examples

Regular verbs form the simple past in ‘-ed’; however there are a few hundred irregular verbs with different forms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: past tense - the verb tense expressing actions or states in the past
  • Antonym: present simple - the verb tense expressing habitual or general actions
