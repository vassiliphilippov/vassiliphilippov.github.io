Temple of Artemis

1. PROPN

/ ˈtɛmpəl əv ɑːrˈtɛmɪs / (AmE), / ˈtɛmpəl əv ɑːˈtɛmɪs / (BrE)
An ancient large temple dedicated to the Greek goddess Artemis, located at Ephesus, considered one of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World.
ancient Greek temple in Ephesus

Examples

The ruins of the Temple of Artemis attract tourists and historians from around the globe.
Many ancient writers described the Temple of Artemis as one of the most beautiful buildings ever constructed.
It took over two centuries to build the magnificent Temple of Artemis at Ephesus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ephesus - Ancient city in present-day Turkey where the temple stood
  • Synonym: Seven Wonders of the World - Famous list of remarkable constructions of classical antiquity

Etymology

From Artemis, the Greek goddess of the hunt, and temple, from Latin templum.
