genus Pothos

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˈpoʊθɒs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈpɒθɒs / (BrE)
a genus of evergreen climbing plants with adhesive adventitious roots, native to southeastern Asia and Brazil
a genus of evergreen climbing plants

Examples

The genus Pothos includes several popular houseplants known for their trailing vines.
Botanists have identified new species within the genus Pothos in Southeast Asia.
The adhesive roots of the genus Pothos help the plants climb trees in tropical forests.

Etymology

From Latin 'Pothos', from Greek 'pothos', the name of a plant.
