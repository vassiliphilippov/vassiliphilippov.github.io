mean value

1. NOUN

/ miːn ˈvæl.juː / (AmE), / miːn ˈvæl.juː / (BrE)
the average of a set of numbers, found by applying a specific function to the numbers and dividing by a function of the total count
average of a set of numbers

Examples

The mean value of the temperatures was calculated over the week.
To summarize the data, first find the mean value of each group.
Mathematicians often discuss the properties of a mean value in various contexts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: average - the typical value of a set, obtained by summing elements and dividing by their number

Etymology

From 'mean' (mathematical average) and 'value'. 'Mean' from Latin 'medianus' (middle), 'value' from Latin 'valere' (to be worth).
