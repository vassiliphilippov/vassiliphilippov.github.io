order Myxobacterales

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪksoʊˌbæk.təˈreɪliːz / (AmE), / ˈmɪksəʊˌbæktəˈreɪliːz / (BrE)
a taxonomic order of higher bacteria known for forming fruiting bodies and exhibiting social behavior
taxonomic order of bacteria
medical

Examples

Scientists discovered new species within the order Myxobacterales during their research expedition.
Many members of the order Myxobacterales produce antibiotics as part of their lifecycle.
The social behavior of the order Myxobacterales has fascinated microbiologists for decades.

Etymology

From Greek 'myxa' (slime) + 'bakterion' (rod, bacterium); taxonomic name.
