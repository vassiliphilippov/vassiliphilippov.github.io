Rift Valley fever

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɪft ˈvæli ˈfiːvər / (AmE), / ˈrɪft ˈvæli ˈfiːvə / (BrE)
an infectious disease of animals and humans in Africa, caused by a bunyavirus and transmitted by mosquitoes or contact with infected animals
a viral infection transmitted by mosquitoes, common in Africa
medical

Examples

Outbreaks of Rift Valley fever can have serious consequences for livestock farmers in East Africa.
People can contract Rift Valley fever by directly handling infected animal tissue.
Researchers are developing vaccines to curb the spread of Rift Valley fever.

Etymology

Named after the Rift Valley in Kenya where the disease was first identified.
