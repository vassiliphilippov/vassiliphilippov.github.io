to hang, draw and quarter

1. VERB

/ hæŋ drɔː ənd ˈkwɔːrtər / (AmE), / hæŋ drɔː ənd ˈkwɔːtə / (BrE)
to execute someone by hanging, disemboweling, and dividing the body into four parts; used figuratively to mean subjecting someone to severe punishment or criticism.
severely punish or criticize; historical execution method
archaic

Examples

In medieval England, traitors would often be hang, draw and quarter for their crimes.
His teammates threatened to hang, draw and quarter him after he missed the winning shot.
She said if I lost the documents, my boss would probably hang, draw and quarter me.

Synset Examples

The proposal was met with such disdain it felt like being hang, draw and quarter.
He joked that missing the deadline felt like being hang, draw and quarter.
In medieval times, traitors were often punished by hanging, drawing and quartering.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to execute - to put someone to death, especially as a legal penalty
  • Synonym: to punish - to subject someone to a penalty for an offense

Etymology

Refers to the medieval English practice of executing traitors by hanging, disemboweling (drawing), and quartering (dividing into four parts).
