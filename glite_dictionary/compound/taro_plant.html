taro plant

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɑːroʊ plænt / (AmE), / ˈtɑːrəʊ plɑːnt / (BrE)
a tropical plant cultivated for its starchy edible root and grown as an ornamental for its large, glossy leaves
tropical root plant; also grown ornamentally

Examples

The taro plant is a staple food in many Pacific Island cultures.
She grew a taro plant in her garden for its beautiful foliage.
Farmers harvest the roots of the taro plant to make traditional dishes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: taro - the root or corm of the taro plant, used as food

Etymology

From Polynesian languages, Tongan and Samoan *talo*; scientific name Colocasia esculenta.
