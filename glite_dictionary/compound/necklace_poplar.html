necklace poplar

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɛk.ləs ˈpɑ.p.lɚ / (AmE), / ˈnɛk.ləs ˈpɒp.lə / (BrE)
a species of poplar tree native to eastern and central United States, known for its rapid growth and luxuriant foliage, and also cultivated in Europe for timber.
a fast-growing American poplar tree

Examples

The necklace poplar is valued for its ability to provide shade in a short period of time.
Farmers in Europe often plant necklace poplar for its high-quality timber.
The leaves of the necklace poplar turn a vibrant yellow in autumn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poplar - a tall, fast-growing tree of the willow family
