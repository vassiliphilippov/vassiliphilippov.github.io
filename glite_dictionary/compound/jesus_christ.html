Jesus Christ

1. PROPN

/ ˈdʒiːzəs ˈkraɪst / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːzəs ˈkraɪst / (BrE)
The central figure of Christianity, believed by Christians to be the Son of God and whose teachings form the foundation of the Christian faith.
central figure of Christianity

Examples

Many people around the world celebrate the birth of Jesus Christ at Christmas.
The teachings of Jesus Christ have influenced Western philosophy and culture for centuries.
She grew up learning about Jesus Christ and the stories of the New Testament.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Jesus - Another name for Jesus Christ, especially in religious contexts

Etymology

Jesus from Hebrew Yēšūa‘; Christ from Greek Khristos (the anointed one).
