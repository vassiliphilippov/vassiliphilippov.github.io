personal property

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɝːsənl ˈprɑːpɚti / (AmE), / ˈpɜːsənəl ˈprɒpəti / (BrE)
property that is movable, as opposed to real estate, including possessions such as furniture, vehicles, and jewelry.
movable property, not real estate
legal

Examples

After the sale of his house, he packed up all his personal property and moved to another city.
The insurance policy covers both your home and your personal property in case of fire or theft.
Jewelry and vehicles are considered personal property rather than real estate.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: real property - land and anything attached to it, such as buildings
  • Synonym: chattel - an item of personal property; a movable possession
