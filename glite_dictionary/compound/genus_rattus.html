genus Rattus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˈrætəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈratəs / (BrE)
a group of rodents including common house rats, characterized by upper incisors with a beveled edge
group of rat species (rodents)

Examples

Many pest control studies focus on the behavioral patterns of the genus Rattus.
The genus Rattus includes species that have adapted to urban environments worldwide.
Scientists observed that members of the genus Rattus have upper incisors with a distinctive beveled edge.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rat - a medium-sized rodent, especially one of the genus Rattus

Etymology

From Latin 'genus' (kind, type) and 'Rattus' (rat).
