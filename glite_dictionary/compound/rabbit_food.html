rabbit food

1. NOUN

/ ˈræbɪt fuːd / (AmE), / ˈræbɪt fuːd / (BrE)
(informal, humorous) Uncooked vegetables, often eaten in salads.
uncooked vegetables (informal)
informal

Examples

She only ordered a salad and joked that she was eating rabbit food again.
He prefers steak over rabbit food any day of the week.
Nutritionists recommend including more rabbit food in your daily diet.

2. NOUN

/ ˈræbɪt fuːd / (AmE), / ˈræbɪt fuːd / (BrE)
(informal) food, especially raw vegetables or salads, considered unappetizing or insubstantial, like what a rabbit might eat.
humorous term for light vegetarian food
informal

Examples

She complained that lunch was just rabbit food and left still hungry.
My brother refuses to eat rabbit food and wants something more filling for dinner.
He joked that going vegan meant he'd have to survive on rabbit food forever.
