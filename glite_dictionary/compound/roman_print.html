roman print

1. NOUN

/ ˈroʊ.mən prɪnt / (AmE), / ˈrəʊ.mən prɪnt / (BrE)
a style of typeface with upright characters, originally based on lettering used in ancient Roman inscriptions and commonly used in Western printing.
typeface style with upright letters

Examples

The book was typeset in roman print to enhance readability.
He preferred roman print over italic for official documents.
Many classical inscriptions use a form of roman print letters.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: italic - style of typeface with slanted characters
  • Synonym: serif - typeface with strokes at the ends of letters

Etymology

From Latin 'Romanus' (Roman), referring to the style of letters used in ancient Rome.
