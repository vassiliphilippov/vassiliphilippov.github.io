first derivative

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɜrst dəˈrɪvətɪv / (AmE), / ˈfɜːst dɪˈrɪvətɪv / (BrE)
the result of differentiating a function once, representing the instantaneous rate of change of one quantity with respect to another
result of differentiating a function once

Examples

The first derivative of position with respect to time is velocity.
If the first derivative is positive, the function is increasing at that point.
To find where the curve is flat, set the first derivative equal to zero and solve for x.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: derivative - the general term for a function's derivative

Etymology

From Latin 'derivare' (to draw off), adapted in mathematical use for the process of derivation in calculus.
