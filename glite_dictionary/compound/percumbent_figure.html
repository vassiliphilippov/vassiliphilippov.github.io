percumbent figure

1. NOUN

/ pərˈkʌmbənt ˈfɪɡjər / (AmE), / pəˈkʌmbənt ˈfɪɡjə / (BrE)
A figure represented as lying down or reclining, especially in a work of art.
figure depicted lying or reclining

Examples

The artist painted a percumbent figure resting peacefully beneath the tree.
In the cathedral, a marble percumbent figure adorned the tomb of the nobleman.
Critics noted the emotional depth of the percumbent figure in her sculptures.

Synset Examples

The percumbent figure in the painting evoked a sense of peace.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recumbent figure - a figure lying down, especially in sculpture or art
  • Antonym: upright figure - a figure standing or sitting erect, especially in art

Etymology

From Latin 'percumbere' (to lie down), combined with 'figure'.
