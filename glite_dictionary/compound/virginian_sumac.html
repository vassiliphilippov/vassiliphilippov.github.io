Virginian sumac

1. NOUN

/ vərˈdʒɪniən ˈsuːmæk / (AmE), / vəˈdʒɪniən ˈsjuːmæk / (BrE)
A deciduous shrubby tree native to eastern North America, with compound leaves that turn brilliant red in autumn and dense clusters of greenish-yellow flowers followed by crimson, acidic berries.
deciduous shrub or small tree of eastern North America

Examples

The Virginian sumac is known for its vibrant red foliage in the fall.
Birds are attracted to the bright berries of the Virginian sumac during winter.
Gardeners often plant Virginian sumac for its ornamental value and hardiness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sumac - any of various plants of the genus Rhus
