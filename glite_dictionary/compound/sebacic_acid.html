sebacic acid

1. NOUN

/ səˈbæsɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / sɪˈbæsɪk ˈæsɪd / (BrE)
a dicarboxylic acid (C10H18O4) used chiefly in the manufacture of resins, plastics, and lubricants
a dicarboxylic acid used in resins and plastics

Examples

The company uses sebacic acid as a key ingredient in making nylon and plasticizers.
Researchers explored the properties of coatings derived from sebacic acid and its derivatives.
Sebacic acid is sometimes favored in the production of biodegradable polymers.

Etymology

From Latin 'sebum' (tallow), as it was originally obtained from animal fats.
