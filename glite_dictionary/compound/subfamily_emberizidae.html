subfamily Emberizidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌsʌbˈfæmɪli ɛmˌbɛrɪˈzaɪdiː / (AmE), / ˌsʌbˈfæmɪli ɛmˌbɛrɪˈzaɪdiː / (BrE)
A taxonomic group of birds that includes buntings and some New World sparrows.
bird subfamily: buntings, some sparrows

Examples

The subfamily Emberizidae encompasses many species known for their melodic songs and colorful plumage.
Researchers are studying migration patterns within the subfamily Emberizidae, especially among North American sparrows.
Many field guides include buntings and sparrows under the classification of subfamily Emberizidae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bunting - a small songbird of the family Emberizidae
  • Synonym: sparrow - a small, brown and gray or brown and white songbird, some of which are included in Emberizidae

Etymology

From New Latin Emberizidae, the scientific name for this group; ultimately from Emberiza, a type of bunting.
