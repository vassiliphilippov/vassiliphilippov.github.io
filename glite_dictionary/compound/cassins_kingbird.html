Cassin's kingbird

1. NOUN

/ kəˈsɪnz ˈkɪŋˌbɝd / (AmE), / kəˈsɪnz ˈkɪŋˌbɜːd / (BrE)
a kingbird species found in the southwestern United States, mostly grey with a yellow abdomen
North American kingbird with grey and yellow coloring

Examples

We watched a Cassin's kingbird perched on the fence, its yellow belly bright in the morning sun.
During migration, several Cassin's kingbirds stopped to rest in our backyard.
The birdwatcher added Cassin's kingbird to her life list after spotting one in Arizona.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kingbird - a type of large tyrant flycatcher

Etymology

Named after American ornithologist John Cassin.
