sheep tick

1. NOUN

/ ʃip tɪk / (AmE), / ʃiːp tɪk / (BrE)
A wingless parasitic fly that lives on the skin of sheep and cattle, feeding on their blood.
parasitic fly on sheep and cattle

Examples

The sheep tick is a common problem for farmers during the summer months.
If left untreated, sheep ticks can cause irritation and disease in livestock.
A veterinarian inspected the flock for sheep ticks after several animals showed signs of discomfort.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tick - A small arachnid that is an external parasite, feeding on the blood of mammals and birds

2. NOUN

/ ʃip tɪk / (AmE), / ʃiːp tɪk / (BrE)
a small parasitic tick, especially Ixodes ricinus, found on sheep and other animals, and known for transmitting diseases such as louping ill and Lyme disease
a parasitic tick on sheep and other animals
medical

Examples

The presence of the sheep tick in livestock can lead to the spread of serious diseases.
Farmers check their sheep regularly for sheep ticks to prevent infection.
The sheep tick is also capable of biting humans and transmitting Lyme disease.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tick - a small parasitic arachnid that feeds on blood
  • Synonym: deer tick - a tick species known for transmitting Lyme disease
