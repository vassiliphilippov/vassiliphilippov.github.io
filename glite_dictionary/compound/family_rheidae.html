family Rheidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli riːˈiːdiːiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli riːˈiːdiːiː / (BrE)
A family of large, flightless birds native to South America, coextensive with the order Rheiformes.
a family of large flightless South American birds

Examples

The family Rheidae consists of species commonly known as rheas in South America.
Members of the family Rheidae are similar in appearance to ostriches but are found in different regions.
Unlike many other birds, those in the family Rheidae are unable to fly but are strong runners.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Rheiformes - an order of large, flightless birds from South America

Etymology

From scientific Latin Rheidae, based on the type genus Rhea.
