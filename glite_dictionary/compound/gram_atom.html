gram atom

1. NOUN

/ ɡræm ˈætəm / (AmE), / ɡræm ˈatəm / (BrE)
the quantity of an element whose mass in grams is numerically equal to its atomic weight; a mole of atoms of an element.
quantity of element equal to its atomic weight in grams

Examples

A gram atom of oxygen weighs 16 grams, equal to its atomic weight.
The reaction involves one gram atom of magnesium combining with chlorine.
In stoichiometry, the concept of a gram atom is essential for calculating reactant quantities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mole - the SI base unit for amount of substance, especially in chemistry

Etymology

Formed from 'gram' (metre-kilogram-second system unit of mass) and 'atom', used in scientific terminology.
