primer coat

1. NOUN

/ ˈpraɪmər koʊt / (AmE), / ˈprʌɪmə kəʊt / (BrE)
a first or preparatory layer of paint or size applied to a surface to improve adhesion and durability of subsequent coats
first layer of paint or size

Examples

Before applying the main color, you should always put down a primer coat on the wall.
The wood was rough, so we gave it a thick primer coat to ensure better coverage later.
He forgot the primer coat, which caused the paint to peel off easily.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: undercoat - a layer of paint applied before the final coat

Etymology

From 'primer' (a preparatory coating) + 'coat' (a layer of paint).
