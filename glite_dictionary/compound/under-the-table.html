under-the-table

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌndɚ ðə ˈteɪbəl / (AmE), / ˌʌndə ðə ˈteɪbəl / (BrE)
secretly done or conducted in an unofficial or hidden way, often to avoid legal or ethical scrutiny.
conducted secretly or unofficially
informal

Examples

The company made several under-the-table payments to secure the contract.
She was paid under-the-table for her weekend work at the restaurant.
Officials warned that any under-the-table deals would be investigated and prosecuted.

Synset Examples

a sub-rosa report
has their under-the-table backing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sub-rosa - done in secret; secretly or confidentially
