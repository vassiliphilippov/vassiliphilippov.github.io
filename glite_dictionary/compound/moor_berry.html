moor berry

1. NOUN

/ mʊr ˈbɛri / (AmE), / mɔː ˈbɛri / (BrE)
An evergreen shrub (Empetrum nigrum) with leathery leaves, native to moorlands and bearing small, dark edible berries.
evergreen shrub with edible berries

Examples

The moor berry thrives in the acidic soils of northern heaths and moors.
She picked baskets of moor berries to make jam for the winter.
Hikers often spot the low-growing moor berry bush among the heather.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crowberry - another name for Empetrum nigrum, shrub with black berries
