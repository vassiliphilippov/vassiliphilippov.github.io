bridge circuit

1. NOUN

/ brɪdʒ ˈsɜːr.kɪt / (AmE), / brɪdʒ ˈsɜː.kɪt / (BrE)
an electrical circuit with two branches forming a diamond shape, used for measuring electrical quantities by connecting a meter across them
diamond-shaped electrical circuit for measurement

Examples

A bridge circuit can be used to measure small changes in resistance with great accuracy.
The Wheatstone bridge circuit is commonly used in laboratory experiments.
Engineers checked the bridge circuit before installing the sensor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Wheatstone bridge - a specific type of bridge circuit used to measure resistance
