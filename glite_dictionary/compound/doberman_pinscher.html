Doberman pinscher

1. NOUN

/ ˈdoʊbərmən ˈpɪnʃər / (AmE), / ˈdəʊbəmən ˈpɪnʃə / (BrE)
A medium to large dog breed of German origin, known for its glossy black and tan coat and often used as a guard dog.
medium-large German breed of dog

Examples

The Doberman pinscher is known for its loyalty and intelligence, making it a popular choice for guard work.
She entered her Doberman pinscher in the national dog show, where he impressed the judges with his sleek coat.
Many families choose a Doberman pinscher for both companionship and protection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Rottweiler - German breed of large, powerful guard dog
  • Synonym: German shepherd - large German breed often used as a police dog

Etymology

Named after Karl Friedrich Louis Dobermann, the German breeder who developed the breed.
