fisherman's knot

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪʃɚmənz nɑt / (AmE), / ˈfɪʃəmənz nɒt / (BrE)
a knot used for securely tying together the ends of two lines, especially fishing lines
knot for tying two lines together

Examples

He used a fisherman's knot to join the two pieces of fishing line together.
A fisherman's knot is known for its strength and reliability when connecting thin cords.
Before starting his trip, he practiced tying a fisherman's knot until he could do it quickly and securely.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: knot - an interlacement of parts of one or more flexible bodies, used to bind or connect
