self-report inventory

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛlf rɪˈpɔrt ɪnˌvɛn.tɔː.ri / (AmE), / ˌself rɪˈpɔːt ɪnˌvɛn.t(ə).ri / (BrE)
a psychological assessment tool in which individuals answer questions about their own traits, behaviors, or choices
psychological assessment questionnaire

Examples

The psychologist administered a self-report inventory to assess the patients’ levels of anxiety and depression.
A self-report inventory relies on the individual’s own accounts of their thoughts and behaviors.
Before therapy began, she completed a self-report inventory about her coping mechanisms.

Etymology

Formed from 'self-report' (report by oneself) and 'inventory' (list or questionnaire).
