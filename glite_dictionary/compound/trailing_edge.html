trailing edge

1. NOUN

/ ˈtreɪlɪŋ ɛdʒ / (AmE), / ˈtreɪlɪŋ ɛdʒ / (BrE)
the rear edge of an airfoil, such as a wing or blade, opposite to the leading edge
rear edge of an airfoil

Examples

The airplane's trailing edge was damaged during turbulence.
Engineers inspected the wing's trailing edge for any signs of wear.
Flaps are usually attached to the trailing edge to help control lift.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: leading edge - the front edge of an airfoil
