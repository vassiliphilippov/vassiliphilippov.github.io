klutzy behavior

1. NOUN

/ ˈklʌtzi bɪˈheɪvjər / (AmE), / ˈklʌtzi bɪˈheɪvɪə / (BrE)
awkward or clumsy actions, often resulting in accidents or mistakes
awkward or clumsy actions
informal

Examples

She apologized for her klutzy behavior after spilling coffee all over the table.
His klutzy behavior during the dance rehearsal caused several minor accidents.
Despite her klutzy behavior, everyone found her endearing and fun to be around.

Synset Examples

His klutzy behavior made everyone laugh during the game.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clumsiness - the quality of being awkward in movement or action
  • Antonym: graceful behavior - showing smooth, controlled movements

Etymology

From 'klutz', probably from Yiddish *klots* (block or clumsy person), plus 'behavior'.
