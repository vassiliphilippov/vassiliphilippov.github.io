Hydrangea arborescens

1. NOUN

/ haɪˈdreɪndʒiə ɑr.bəˈrɛsɛnz / (AmE), / hɪˈdrændʒɪə ɑː.bəˈres.enz / (BrE)
A deciduous shrub native to eastern North America, known for its large clusters of creamy white flowers.
shrub with white flower clusters

Examples

The Hydrangea arborescens blooms in early summer with striking white flower clusters.
Gardeners often plant Hydrangea arborescens for its resilience and attractive flowers.
Unlike other hydrangeas, Hydrangea arborescens thrives in a variety of soil types.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hydrangea - a genus of flowering plants with showy blooms

Etymology

From New Latin, combining the genus Hydrangea (from Greek 'hydor' meaning water and 'angeion' meaning vessel) and 'arborescens' (from Latin 'arbor' meaning tree).
