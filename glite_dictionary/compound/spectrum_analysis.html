spectrum analysis

1. NOUN

/ ˈspɛktrəm əˈnæləsɪs / (AmE), / ˈspɛktrəm əˈnæləsɪs / (BrE)
the process of analyzing the composition or structure of a substance by studying its spectrum, often using a spectroscope
analysis of a substance using its spectrum

Examples

The scientist employed spectrum analysis to determine the chemical composition of the sample.
Advances in spectrum analysis have greatly improved our understanding of distant stars.
Modern laboratories often use spectrum analysis to identify unknown substances.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spectroscopy - the study of the interaction between matter and electromagnetic radiation

Etymology

From 'spectrum', Latin for 'image, apparition', and 'analysis', from Greek 'analusis' meaning 'a breaking up'.
