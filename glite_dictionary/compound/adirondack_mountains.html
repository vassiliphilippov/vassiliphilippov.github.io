Adirondack Mountains

1. PROPN

/ ˌædɪˈrɑndæk ˈmaʊntənz / (AmE), / ˌædɪˈrɒndæk ˈmaʊntɪnz / (BrE)
a mountain range in northeastern New York State, known for its scenic beauty and popular as a resort area
mountain range in northeastern New York State

Examples

We spent our summer vacation hiking in the Adirondack Mountains of New York.
The Adirondack Mountains are famous for their fall foliage and crystal-clear lakes.
Many artists have found inspiration in the wild beauty of the Adirondack Mountains.

Etymology

From Mohawk adirodack (they eat trees), originally used for Algonquin people by the Mohawk; applied to the mountain range by early 19th-century mapmakers.
