red light

1. NOUN

/ rɛd laɪt / (AmE), / rɛd laɪt / (BrE)
a signal, especially used in traffic control, indicating that vehicles or pedestrians must stop
traffic signal meaning stop

Examples

She stopped the car as soon as she saw the red light ahead.
Ignoring a red light can result in a hefty fine and legal trouble.
The pedestrian waited patiently for the red light to change before crossing the street.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: green light - a signal to proceed in traffic
  • Degree Lower: amber light - a signal warning that the traffic light is about to change

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɛd ˌlaɪt / (AmE), / ˈrɛd ˌlaɪt / (BrE)
a warning sign of imminent danger or a signal to stop or proceed with caution; often used figuratively or literally
a warning of danger or signal to stop

Examples

Seeing the approaching train was a clear red light that made everyone back away from the tracks.
The financial instability should have been a red light for investors to reconsider their decisions.
Ignoring the obvious red light in his behavior, she continued to trust him.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: green light - a signal to proceed or indication of permission to continue
  • Synonym: warning - a statement or signal of potential danger

3. NOUN

/ ˈrɛd ˌlaɪt / (AmE), / ˈrɛd ˌlaɪt / (BrE)
a traffic signal that shows a red color to indicate that vehicles or pedestrians must stop
traffic signal indicating to stop

Examples

She stopped at the red light to wait for the signal to change.
The driver ran a red light and was fined for it.
Pedestrians should only cross when the red light turns green.

Synset Examples

The red light signaled that the meeting had ended.
She stopped at the red light, waiting patiently.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: green light - a traffic signal that indicates you may proceed
