heliocentric parallax

1. NOUN

/ ˌhiːlioʊˈsɛntrɪk ˈpærəˌlæks / (AmE), / ˌhiːliəʊˈsɛntrɪk ˈpærəlæks / (BrE)
The apparent shift in position of a celestial object when observed from two different points in Earth's orbit around the sun, used to measure the object's distance.
astronomical distance measurement by Earth's solar orbit

Examples

By measuring the heliocentric parallax of a nearby star, astronomers can calculate its distance from Earth.
Improvements in telescopes have made the detection of heliocentric parallax more precise for distant objects.
The team presented their findings on heliocentric parallax at the international astronomy conference.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parallax - the apparent shift in position of an object due to a change in the observer's point of view

Etymology

From Greek hēlios (sun) + Latin centrum (center) + Greek parallaxis (alternation), relating to measurements from the sun's center.
