submaxillary gland

1. NOUN

/ sʌbˈmæksəˌlɛri ɡlænd / (AmE), / sʌbˈmæksɪləri ɡlænd / (BrE)
a salivary gland located beneath the lower jaw on either side, producing a significant portion of saliva, especially at night, and releasing it into the mouth under the tongue
salivary gland under the lower jaw
medical

Examples

The surgeon carefully removed the submaxillary gland to treat the chronic infection.
The submaxillary gland is responsible for much of the saliva produced during sleep.
Swelling below the jaw can sometimes indicate inflammation of the submaxillary gland.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: submandibular gland - a synonym for the submaxillary gland, referring to the same salivary gland under the jaw

Etymology

From Latin sub- (under) + maxilla (jaw) + -ary (relating to) + gland.
