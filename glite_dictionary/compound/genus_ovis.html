genus Ovis

1. NOUN

/ ˈd͡ʒiːnəs ˈoʊvɪs / (AmE), / ˈd͡ʒiːnəs ˈəʊvɪs / (BrE)
A genus of mammals in the family Bovidae that includes domestic sheep and related wild species.
biological genus of sheep

Examples

The genus Ovis includes both domestic sheep and several species of wild sheep.
Taxonomists have long debated how many species belong in the genus Ovis.
Scientists studying ancient DNA discovered a new extinct member of the genus Ovis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sheep - a domesticated ruminant animal with a thick woolly coat; member of genus Ovis

Etymology

From Latin 'genus' (kind, type) and 'Ovis' (sheep).
