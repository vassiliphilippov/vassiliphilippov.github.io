yellow journalist

1. NOUN

/ ˈjɛloʊ ˈdʒɜrnəlɪst / (AmE), / ˈjɛləʊ ˈdʒɜːnəlɪst / (BrE)
a journalist who exaggerates or sensationalizes news stories to attract attention or readership
journalist who sensationalizes news

Examples

The yellow journalist wrote headlines that distorted the facts to boost newspaper sales.
Many accused him of being a yellow journalist because he focused on scandal rather than truth.
Despite his talent, he gained notoriety as a yellow journalist more interested in shock than accuracy.

Synset Examples

The yellow journalist twisted facts to attract readers.
She was labeled a yellow journalist for sensationalism.
As a yellow journalist, he prioritized headlines over truth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sensationalist - a person who presents stories in a way that is intended to provoke public interest or excitement
  • Antonym: objective journalist - a journalist who sticks to facts and avoids sensationalism

Etymology

From 'yellow journalism', a term originating circa 1890s in the U.S. from sensational newspapers with yellow-ink comic strips.
