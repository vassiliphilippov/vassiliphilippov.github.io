fire salamander

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪɚ səˌlæməndɚ / (AmE), / ˈfaɪə səˌlæməndə / (BrE)
A European salamander (Salamandra salamandra) with dark skin marked by distinctive yellow spots or patches.
European salamander with yellow spots

Examples

We spotted a fire salamander sheltering beneath a damp log in the forest.
The black body and bright yellow spots make the fire salamander easy to identify.
Researchers studied the habitat requirements of the endangered fire salamander.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: salamander - a small amphibian with a lizard-like appearance
