horse-chestnut family

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɔrs ˌtʃɛs.nʌt ˈfæm.ə.li / (AmE), / ˈhɔːs ˌtʃɛs.nʌt ˈfæm.ɪ.li / (BrE)
a family of trees with showy flowers and inedible nutlike seeds in leathery capsules, notably including the horse-chestnuts (Aesculus)
family of trees with showy flowers and inedible seeds

Examples

The horse-chestnut family includes both North American and Eurasian species of trees.
She studied the flowering patterns of several members of the horse-chestnut family for her botany thesis.
The glossy seeds from trees in the horse-chestnut family are commonly known as conkers.
