male hypogonadism

1. NOUN

/ meɪl ˌhaɪpoʊˈɡoʊnəˌdɪzəm / (AmE), / meɪl ˌhʌɪpəʊˈɡəʊnədɪzəm / (BrE)
a medical condition in which a male fails to produce sufficient levels of sex hormones due to absent, underdeveloped, or malfunctioning testicles
impaired testicular function in males
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with male hypogonadism after showing delayed puberty.
Certain genetic conditions can lead to male hypogonadism and infertility.
Testosterone therapy is sometimes prescribed to men suffering from male hypogonadism.

Etymology

From Greek hypo- (under) + gonē (seed, generation) + -ism (condition).
