prolonged interrogation

1. NOUN

/ prəˈlɔŋd ˌɪn.təɹ.əˈɡeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / prəˈlɒŋd ˌɪn.tə.rəˈɡeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of subjecting someone to questioning for an extended period, often as a form of psychological pressure or torture
extended questioning, often as psychological pressure

Examples

The suspect broke down after hours of prolonged interrogation by the authorities.
Human rights groups condemned the use of prolonged interrogation as a form of torture.
She described the prolonged interrogation as mentally exhausting and deeply distressing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interrogation - the act of formally questioning someone, especially by an authority
