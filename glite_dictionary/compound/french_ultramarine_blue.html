French ultramarine blue

1. NOUN

/ frɛntʃ ˌʌltrəˈmɛrin blu / (AmE), / frɛntʃ ˌʌltrəˈmɑːriːn bluː / (BrE)
an intense blue pigment produced artificially, used particularly in painting and industry as a substitute for natural ultramarine
a synthetic blue pigment used in art and industry

Examples

The artist preferred French ultramarine blue for its vibrant color and consistency.
Manufacturers began producing French ultramarine blue in the nineteenth century as a cost-effective alternative to natural ultramarine.
Her painting features deep shades of French ultramarine blue that create a striking effect.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ultramarine - a vivid blue pigment originally made from lapis lazuli

Etymology

'Ultramarine' comes from Medieval Latin 'ultramarinus' (beyond the sea), originally referring to the source of the pigment; 'French ultramarine' denotes the artificial pigment first manufactured in France in the 19th century.
