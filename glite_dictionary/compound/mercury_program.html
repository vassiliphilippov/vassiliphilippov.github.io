Mercury program

1. PROPN

/ ˈmɝːkjʊri ˌproʊɡræm / (AmE), / ˈmɜːkjʊri ˌprəʊɡræm / (BrE)
the United States' first human spaceflight program (1961–1963), aimed at putting a man in orbit around the Earth
first US human spaceflight program

Examples

The Mercury program marked the United States' first steps into human spaceflight.
Each mission in the Mercury program carried only a single astronaut.
Historians often credit the Mercury program with paving the way for later achievements like Apollo.

Synset Examples

under the Mercury program each flight had one astronaut

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Apollo program - the NASA spaceflight program that landed humans on the Moon
  • Degree Higher: Gemini program - the US human spaceflight program between Mercury and Apollo

Etymology

Named after the Roman god Mercury, symbolizing speed and mobility, and to match NASA's naming conventions.
