case knife

1. NOUN

/ keɪs naɪf / (AmE), / keɪs naɪf / (BrE)
a knife with a fixed blade designed to be carried in a sheath
knife with fixed blade and sheath

Examples

The hunter removed his case knife from its sheath before preparing the campsite.
He always carried a case knife for emergencies while hiking in the mountains.
The antique dealer displayed a nineteenth-century case knife with an engraved handle.

2. NOUN

/ keɪs naɪf / (AmE), / keɪs naɪf / (BrE)
A table knife with a rounded or blunt end, used for eating or spreading food.
table knife with a rounded end

Examples

He spread butter on his toast with a case knife.
The silverware set included a case knife for each guest.
She handed him a case knife to slice the soft cheese.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: table knife - a knife used at the table for eating food

Etymology

Originally referred to a knife kept in a case along with other cutlery pieces.
