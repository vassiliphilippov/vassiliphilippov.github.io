liberty chit

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪbɚti tʃɪt / (AmE), / ˈlɪbəti tʃɪt / (BrE)
an official permit allowing military personnel to leave or return to a military installation
military permit to leave a base

Examples

Each sailor needed to present his liberty chit before leaving the base for the weekend.
The guard checked her liberty chit at the gate before allowing her to exit.
If you lose your liberty chit, you must report it immediately to your commanding officer.

Synset Examples

he had to show his pass in order to get out

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pass - official document giving permission to go somewhere

Etymology

From 'liberty' meaning leave or time off in the military, and 'chit' (slip or note), from Hindi 'chiṭṭhi' (letter, note).
