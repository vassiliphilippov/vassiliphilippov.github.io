aeolian harp

1. NOUN

/ iˈoʊliən hɑrp / (AmE), / iːˈəʊliən hɑːp / (BrE)
A stringed musical instrument which produces sound as wind passes over its strings, usually tuned in unison and played by natural air movement.
harp played by the wind

Examples

The aeolian harp filled the garden with soft, ethereal music as the evening breeze blew.
He placed an aeolian harp on his windowsill to let the autumn wind create haunting melodies.
The poet compared the aeolian harp to the natural expression of the soul in harmony with nature.

Etymology

From Aeolus, the Greek god of the winds, and 'harp'.
