security interest

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈkjʊrəti ˈɪntrəst / (AmE), / sɪˈkjʊərɪti ˈɪntrəst / (BrE)
A legal claim or right on property that secures the payment or performance of a debt or obligation.
legal claim securing a debt
legal

Examples

The bank holds a security interest in the property until the loan is repaid.
Creditors with a security interest are often paid first if a company goes bankrupt.
She granted a security interest in her car as collateral for the loan.
