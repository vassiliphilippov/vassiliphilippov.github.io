nosey parker

1. NOUN

/ ˈnoʊzi ˈpɑrkər / (AmE), / ˈnəʊzi ˈpɑːkə / (BrE)
a person who is too interested in other people's private affairs or business
overly inquisitive person
informal british

Examples

My new colleague is a real nosey parker who always wants to know everyone’s business.
Don’t be such a nosey parker—let people solve their problems themselves.
Ever since she moved in, our neighbor has played the role of nosey parker on our street.

Synset Examples

Don't be such a nosey parker; mind your own business.
My neighbor is a real nosey parker, always peeking.
She’s a nosey parker who knows everyone’s secrets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: busybody - a person who meddles in others' affairs

Etymology

Origin uncertain, recorded in Britain since the late 19th century; 'nosey' from 'nose' + 'Parker' (a common surname, likely arbitrary).
