aldermanic duty

1. NOUN

/ ˌɔːldərˌmænɪk ˈduːti / (AmE), / ˌɔːldəmænɪk ˈdjuːti / (BrE)
the responsibilities or obligations of an alderman, an elected member of a local government council
duty of a local elected council member

Examples

His aldermanic duty requires him to attend weekly council meetings and listen to constituents' concerns.
She took her aldermanic duties seriously, working to improve the local parks and schools.
Balancing personal life and aldermanic duty can be a challenge for those committed to public service.

Synset Examples

His aldermanic duties kept him busy every evening.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alderman - an elected member of a municipal council
  • Degree Higher: civic duty - the responsibilities of a citizen towards their community

Etymology

From 'alderman', from Old English 'ealdorman' (elder or senior man), combined with 'duty'.
