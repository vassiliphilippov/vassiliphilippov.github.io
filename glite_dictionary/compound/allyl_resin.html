allyl resin

1. NOUN

/ ˈælɪl ˈrɛzən / (AmE), / ˈælɪl ˈrɛzɪn / (BrE)
a synthetic resin derived from allyl alcohol that hardens when cured, used especially as an adhesive
synthetic resin adhesive

Examples

The aircraft parts are bonded together using a special allyl resin adhesive.
When allyl resin is cured, it forms a hard, durable layer.
Researchers are exploring new uses for allyl resin in medical devices due to its strength.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resin - a solid or semi-solid organic substance, usually secreted by plants or made synthetically

Etymology

From 'allyl', referring to the organic radical derived from propylene, and 'resin'.
