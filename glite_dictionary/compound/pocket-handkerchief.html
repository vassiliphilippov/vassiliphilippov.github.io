pocket-handkerchief

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑkɪt ˌhæŋkərˌtʃɪf / (AmE), / ˈpɒkɪt ˌhæŋkətʃɪf / (BrE)
a piece of cloth for wiping the face or hands, typically carried in a pocket
handkerchief carried in a pocket

Examples

He pulled a clean pocket-handkerchief from his jacket before sneezing.
She offered him a pocket-handkerchief when he spilled his drink.
It was customary for gentlemen to carry a white pocket-handkerchief at formal events.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: handkerchief - a small piece of cloth for wiping the face or hands

Etymology

From 'pocket' and 'handkerchief', indicating a handkerchief carried in one's pocket.
