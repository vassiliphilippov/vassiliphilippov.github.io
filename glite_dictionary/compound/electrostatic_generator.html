electrostatic generator

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌlɛktroʊˈstætɪk ˌdʒɛnəˈreɪtər / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛktrəʊˈstætɪk ˌdʒɛnəˈreɪtə / (BrE)
an electrical device that generates high voltage by accumulating static electric charge
device generating static electric charge

Examples

The electrostatic generator produced a spark over several centimeters long.
In the science lab, students observed the effects of an electrostatic generator on different materials.
A Van de Graaff machine is a common type of electrostatic generator used for physics experiments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: van de graaff generator - type of electrostatic generator using a moving belt to accumulate charge

Etymology

From 'electrostatic', relating to stationary electric charges, and 'generator', from Latin 'generare' (to produce).
