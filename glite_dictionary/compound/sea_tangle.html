sea tangle

1. NOUN

/ si ˈtæŋɡəl / (AmE), / siː ˈtæŋɡl̩ / (BrE)
a large brown seaweed, especially of the genus Laminaria, commonly found in cold coastal waters; kelp
large brown seaweed (kelp)

Examples

Many coastal communities use sea tangle as fertilizer for their crops.
The biologist collected samples of sea tangle for her marine research.
Dried sea tangle is often used as an ingredient in traditional Asian cuisine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kelp - large brown seaweed of the order Laminariales
