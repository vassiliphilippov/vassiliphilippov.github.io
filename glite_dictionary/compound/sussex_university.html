Sussex University

1. PROPN

/ ˈsʌsɪks juːnəˌvɝːsəti / (AmE), / ˈsʌsɪks juːnɪˌvɜːsɪti / (BrE)
a university located in Brighton, England, founded in the 1960s, known as one of the 'red-brick' universities
university in Brighton, England
british

Examples

She studied physics at Sussex University before working at a major research institute.
The new arts building at Sussex University has received numerous architectural awards.
Many international students choose Sussex University for its diverse programs and vibrant campus life.

Etymology

Named after the historic county of Sussex; 'university' from Latin 'universitas'.
