battle-axe

1. NOUN

/ ˈbætlˌæks / (AmE), / ˈbætl ˌæks / (BrE)
A large, broad-bladed axe used as a weapon in warfare, especially in the past.
large axe used in battle

Examples

The knight wielded a heavy battle-axe during the fierce combat.
Museum displays often feature the battle-axe as an example of medieval weaponry.
He admired the ornate designs carved into the handle of the ancient battle-axe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: broadax - a large axe with a broad cutting blade

Etymology

From battle (combat) + axe (cutting weapon); in use since Middle English.
