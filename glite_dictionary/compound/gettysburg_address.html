Gettysburg Address

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɛtiˌsbɜːrɡ əˈdrɛs / (AmE), / ˈɡɛtɪzbɜːɡ əˈdrɛs / (BrE)
a famous speech delivered by Abraham Lincoln on November 19, 1863, during the American Civil War at the dedication of the national cemetery at Gettysburg
Lincoln's 1863 Civil War speech at Gettysburg

Examples

Many students are asked to memorize the Gettysburg Address in school history classes.
The Gettysburg Address is often cited as one of the greatest speeches in American history.
At the ceremony, the speaker recited the words of the Gettysburg Address to honor the fallen soldiers.
