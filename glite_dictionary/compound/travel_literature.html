travel literature

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrævəl ˌlɪt̬ɚˌeɪtʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈtrævəl ˌlɪtərətʃə / (BrE)
A literary genre comprising descriptive accounts or narratives of journeys, often focusing on experiences of new places, peoples, and cultures.
genre of writing about journeys

Examples

He specialized in travel literature, writing vividly about his adventures in Asia and Africa.
The library has an extensive collection of travel literature from the nineteenth century.
Many readers are drawn to travel literature for its exploration of distant cultures and landscapes.

Synset Examples

The first wave of travel literature in the century following Columbus recounted heroic tales of crusades, conquests and pilgrimages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: travelogue - a film, book, or illustrated lecture about travel
  • Synonym: itinerary - a detailed plan or account of a journey
