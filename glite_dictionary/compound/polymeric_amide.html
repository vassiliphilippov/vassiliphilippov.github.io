polymeric amide

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑlɪˈmɛrɪk ˈæmaɪd / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈmɛrɪk ˈæmɪd / (BrE)
a polymer made up of repeating amide groups in its molecular structure
polymer with repeated amide groups
medical

Examples

Nylon is a well-known example of a polymeric amide used in textiles.
Researchers developed a new polymeric amide for use in medical implants.
The backbone of a polymeric amide consists of recurring amide groups linking its subunits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polyamide - a polymer containing recurring amide groups; often used for synthetic fibers like nylon

Etymology

From 'polymeric', relating to polymers, and 'amide', from French 'amide', based on ammonia.
