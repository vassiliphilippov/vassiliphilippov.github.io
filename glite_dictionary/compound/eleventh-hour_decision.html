eleventh-hour decision

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈlɛvənθ aʊər dɪˈsɪʒən / (AmE), / ɪˈlɛvənθ aʊə dɪˈsɪʒən / (BrE)
a decision or choice made at the last possible moment, often just before a deadline or critical point
last-minute decision or action

Examples

The team made an eleventh-hour decision to change the presentation topic.
Thanks to an eleventh-hour decision, the flight was saved from cancellation.
Her eleventh-hour decision to apply changed the course of her career.

Synset Examples

She always leaves things for an eleventh-hour decision.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: last-minute decision - a decision made just before it is necessary or required
  • Degree Lower: snap decision - a decision made quickly and without much thought

Etymology

From the phrase 'eleventh hour', meaning late or last moment, referencing the Biblical parable of the workers in the vineyard (Matthew 20:1–16), where laborers are hired at the eleventh hour of the day.
