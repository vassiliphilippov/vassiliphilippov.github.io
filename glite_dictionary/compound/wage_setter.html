wage setter

1. NOUN

/ weɪdʒ ˈsɛtɚ / (AmE), / weɪdʒ ˈsɛtə / (BrE)
an economic condition or variable that determines or influences wage rates
factor determining wage rates (economics)

Examples

Central banks can act as a wage setter by influencing labor market expectations.
In some countries, collective bargaining agreements serve as the main wage setter.
The minimum wage law is often viewed as a key wage setter for low-income workers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wage determinant - a factor that determines wage levels
