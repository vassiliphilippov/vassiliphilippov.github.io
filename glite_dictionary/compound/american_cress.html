American cress

1. NOUN

/ əˈmɛr.ɪ.kən krɛs / (AmE), / əˈmɛr.ɪ.kən krɛs / (BrE)
An herbaceous plant of southwestern European origin, cultivated in various regions for its edible leaves.
edible leafy plant

Examples

I added chopped American cress to the salad for a peppery kick.
The farmer specializes in growing American cress alongside other leafy greens.
Young leaves of American cress are often used as a garnish or in sandwiches.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: winter cress - another name for the plant, Barbarea vulgaris
