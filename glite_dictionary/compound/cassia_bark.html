cassia bark

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæsiə bɑːrk / (AmE), / ˈkæsiə bɑːk / (BrE)
the aromatic bark of the cassia tree, used as a spice and considered less desirable than true cinnamon
aromatic bark used as a spice

Examples

Many commercial ground cinnamons contain cassia bark instead of true cinnamon.
The chef preferred using Ceylon cinnamon over cassia bark for its milder flavor.
In traditional Chinese medicine, cassia bark is used for its warming properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cinnamon - the aromatic spice from the bark of Cinnamomum verum (Ceylon cinnamon)

Etymology

From Latin 'cassia', from Greek 'kassia', referring to the aromatic plant.
