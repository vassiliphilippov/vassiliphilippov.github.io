Egyptian water lily

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈdʒɪp.ʃən ˈwɔː.tər ˌlɪl.i / (AmE), / ɪˈdʒɪp.ʃən ˈwɔː.tə ˌlɪl.i / (BrE)
a species of white water lily (Nymphaea lotus), native to Egypt and southeastern Africa, held sacred by ancient Egyptians
species of white water lily from Egypt

Examples

The Egyptian water lily was highly revered in ancient Egyptian culture and featured in many temple carvings.
We spotted a beautiful Egyptian water lily blooming along the banks of the Nile.
Botanists study the Egyptian water lily for its unique adaptation to aquatic environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lotus - another term for aquatic plants often confused with Egyptian water lily
