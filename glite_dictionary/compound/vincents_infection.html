Vincent's infection

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪnsənts ɪnˈfɛkʃən / (AmE), / ˈvɪnsənts ɪnˈfɛkʃən / (BrE)
an acute, contagious infection of the mouth and throat characterized by ulceration of the mucous membranes
acute infection of mouth and throat
medical

Examples

The dentist diagnosed the patient with Vincent's infection after examining the oral ulcers.
Poor oral hygiene and stress can increase the risk of developing Vincent's infection.
Treatment for Vincent's infection usually involves antibiotics and improved oral care.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trench mouth - a synonym for Vincent's infection

Etymology

Named after French physician Jean Hyacinthe Vincent, who identified the condition.
