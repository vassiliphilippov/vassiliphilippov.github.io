audio forensics

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔdiˌoʊ fəˈrɛnsɪks / (AmE), / ˈɔːdiəʊ fəˈrensɪks / (BrE)
the scientific field concerned with the acquisition, analysis, and evaluation of sound recordings for use as evidence in legal or official investigations
forensic analysis of audio recordings
legal

Examples

The police turned to audio forensics to clarify the voices on a crucial recording.
A team specializing in audio forensics uncovered evidence of tampering in the tape.
Recent advances in audio forensics have made it easier to authenticate digital recordings.

Synset Examples

Emergency calls can be analyzed using audio forensics if the context and situation of the caller has to be identified.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forensic science - the application of scientific methods to investigate crimes

Etymology

From 'audio', relating to sound, and 'forensics', from Latin *forensis* (of the forum or public discussion).
