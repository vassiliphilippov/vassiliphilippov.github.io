Mohawk River

1. PROPN

/ ˈmoʊhɑk ˈrɪvər / (AmE), / ˈməʊhɑːk ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
a river in central New York State, USA, that flows southeast to join the Hudson River.
river in central New York, USA

Examples

The Mohawk River played a crucial role in the development of the Erie Canal.
Many picturesque towns are located along the banks of the Mohawk River.
Flooding from the Mohawk River caused significant damage to local communities last spring.

Etymology

Named after the Mohawk people, an indigenous tribe of North America.
