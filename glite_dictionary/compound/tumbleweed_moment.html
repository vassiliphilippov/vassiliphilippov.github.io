tumbleweed moment

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʌm.bəl.wid ˈmoʊ.mənt / (AmE), / ˈtʌm.bəl.wiːd ˈməʊ.mənt / (BrE)
A period of awkward or uncomfortable silence during a conversation or situation, often following something inappropriate or embarrassing.
awkward silence in conversation
informal

Examples

There was a tumbleweed moment after he mentioned the failed project.
Her comment was followed by a tumbleweed moment as everyone looked away uncomfortably.
Every joke that falls flat causes yet another tumbleweed moment in the meeting.

Synset Examples

His confession created a tumbleweed moment at the party.
There was a tumbleweed moment when no one spoke.
After the awkward joke, we had a tumbleweed moment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: awkward silence - a period of uncomfortable silence in conversation

Etymology

From the image of a tumbleweed rolling through a deserted scene in Western films, symbolizing emptiness or silence.
