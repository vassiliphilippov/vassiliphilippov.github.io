Farley maidenhair

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɑrli ˈmeɪdənˌhɛr / (AmE), / ˈfɑːli ˈmeɪdənˌheə / (BrE)
a species of maidenhair fern (Adiantum farleyense) named after Farley Hill in Barbados, where it was first discovered. It is known for its delicate, decorative fronds.
a species of maidenhair fern

Examples

The Farley maidenhair is a popular choice for ornamental gardens due to its elegant foliage.
Botanists discovered a rare specimen of Farley maidenhair growing near the estate.
She placed a potted Farley maidenhair by the window to brighten the room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maidenhair fern - a group of delicate ferns of the genus Adiantum

Etymology

Named after Farley Hill, a country house estate in Barbados, where the plant was discovered.
