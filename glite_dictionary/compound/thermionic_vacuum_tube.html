thermionic vacuum tube

1. NOUN

/ θɜrˈmaɪ.ɑː.nɪk ˈvæk.juːm tuːb / (AmE), / θɜːˈmʌɪ.ɒn.ɪk ˈvak.juːm tjuːb / (BrE)
an electronic device with electrodes sealed in an evacuated glass or metal envelope, allowing current to flow by thermionic emission
electronic device using thermionic emission in a vacuum

Examples

Early radios relied on the thermionic vacuum tube to amplify weak electrical signals.
Many audio enthusiasts believe that thermionic vacuum tubes produce a warmer sound than modern electronics.
The invention of the thermionic vacuum tube revolutionized long-distance telephone communication.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vacuum tube - electronic device relying on electron flow in a vacuum
  • Antonym: transistor - semiconductor device used to amplify or switch electronic signals

Etymology

From 'thermionic', meaning related to electrons emitted by heat, and 'vacuum tube', referring to the device's evacuated enclosure.
