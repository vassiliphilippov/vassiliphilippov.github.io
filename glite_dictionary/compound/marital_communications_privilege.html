marital communications privilege

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛrɪtl kəˌmjunɪˈkeɪʃənz ˈprɪvəlɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈmærɪtl kəˌmjuːnɪˈkeɪʃənz ˈprɪvɪlɪdʒ / (BrE)
The legal right that prevents spouses from disclosing confidential communications made between them during their marriage.
legal rule protecting confidential spousal communications
legal

Examples

The marital communications privilege prevented her from testifying about private conversations with her husband.
Lawyers often cite the marital communications privilege when discussing evidence obtained during marriage.
Due to the marital communications privilege, the court excluded certain statements exchanged between the spouses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attorney-client privilege - the right of clients to keep communications with their attorneys confidential
  • Synonym: spousal immunity - the legal principle that a spouse cannot be compelled to testify against the other
