genus Arisaema

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ær.ɪˈsiː.mə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ær.ɪˈsiː.mə / (BrE)
A genus of tuberous or rhizomatous herbaceous perennial plants in the family Araceae, including species such as Jack-in-the-pulpit.
genus of perennial plants in Araceae

Examples

Many species in the genus Arisaema are known for their unusual hooded flowers.
Botanists recently discovered a new member of the genus Arisaema in the Himalayan region.
Gardeners prize certain plants from the genus Arisaema for their unique appearance and shade tolerance.

Etymology

From New Latin Arisaema, based on Greek 'aron' (arum) and 'haima' (blood), referring to characteristics of the plants.
