hepatitis C

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɛpəˈtaɪtɪs si / (AmE), / ˌhɛpəˈtaɪtɪs siː / (BrE)
A form of viral hepatitis caused by the hepatitis C virus, primarily transmitted through blood-to-blood contact, leading to liver inflammation.
a type of viral hepatitis caused by hepatitis C virus
medical

Examples

People who share needles are at a higher risk of contracting hepatitis C.
The new medication has shown promise in treating chronic hepatitis C infections.
Many cases of hepatitis C go undiagnosed because symptoms can be mild or absent for years.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: hepatitis B - a form of viral hepatitis caused by the hepatitis B virus
  • Degree Lower: hepatitis A - a form of viral hepatitis caused by the hepatitis A virus
  • Degree Higher: cirrhosis - chronic liver damage from a variety of causes leading to scarring and liver failure

Etymology

From 'hepatitis' (inflammation of the liver) and the letter 'C' indicating the specific virus type.
