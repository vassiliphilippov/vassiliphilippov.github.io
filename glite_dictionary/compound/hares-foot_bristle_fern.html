hare's-foot bristle fern

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛrz ˌfʊt ˈbrɪsəl fɜrn / (AmE), / ˈheəz ˌfʊt ˈbrɪsəl fɜːn / (BrE)
a species of bristle fern with soft, hairy rhizomes resembling a hare's foot, belonging to the genus Trichomanes or Davallia.
fern with hairy rhizomes

Examples

The hare's-foot bristle fern thrives in humid, shaded environments of tropical forests.
Gardeners often cultivate hare's-foot bristle fern for its unusual hairy rhizomes.
You can easily identify a hare's-foot bristle fern by the soft, brown hairs on its creeping stems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bristle fern - any fern of the family Hymenophyllaceae or related genera

Etymology

Named for the fern's rhizomes, which resemble the foot of a hare.
