mood swing

1. NOUN

/ mud swɪŋ / (AmE), / muːd swɪŋ / (BrE)
a sudden or dramatic change in a person's emotional state, often occurring unpredictably
sudden change in emotional state

Examples

She experienced a severe mood swing after hearing the unexpected news.
During puberty, teenagers are known to have frequent mood swings.
His mood swing from joy to anger surprised everyone in the room.

Synset Examples

His hormonal mood swings were hard to predict.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: emotional instability - tendency to experience frequent changes in mood
