strategic warning

1. NOUN

/ strəˈtiːdʒɪk ˈwɔrnɪŋ / (AmE), / strəˈtiːdʒɪk ˈwɔːnɪŋ / (BrE)
a warning given in advance of a potential threat or hostile act, particularly to allow time for preparation or response, especially in military contexts
advance notice of a potential threat

Examples

The intelligence agency issued a strategic warning of possible missile attacks in the region.
Prior to the invasion, government officials failed to act on a strategic warning from military advisers.
A strategic warning is essential for national leaders to prepare a coordinated defense response.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: tactical warning - warning issued immediately before or during a hostile act
