Lamaze method

1. NOUN

/ ləˈmɑz ˌmɛθ.əd / (AmE), / ləˈmɑːz ˌmɛθ.əd / (BrE)
a method of childbirth preparation involving instruction in relaxation, breathing, and concentration techniques, often supported by a partner or coach
childbirth preparation technique
medical

Examples

Many couples attend Lamaze method classes to prepare for a natural birth experience.
The Lamaze method emphasizes breathing techniques to help manage labor pain.
Her doctor recommended the Lamaze method as a way to cope with childbirth without medication.

Etymology

Named after French obstetrician Dr. Fernand Lamaze (1891–1957), who developed the technique.

2. NOUN

/ ləˈmɑz ˌmɛθ.əd / (AmE), / ləˈmɑːz ˌmɛθ.əd / (BrE)
a method of preparing women for natural childbirth through classes that teach relaxation, concentration, and breathing techniques, often with the aid of a coach
childbirth preparation method using relaxation and breathing
medical

Examples

Many parents choose the Lamaze method to help manage pain during childbirth.
The hospital offers Lamaze method classes for expectant mothers and their partners.
She practiced breathing techniques from the Lamaze method throughout her pregnancy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: natural childbirth - childbirth without medical intervention or drugs
  • Degree Higher: childbirth - the act of giving birth to a baby

Etymology

Named after French obstetrician Fernand Lamaze (1891–1957), who devised the method.
