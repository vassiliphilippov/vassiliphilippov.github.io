chromosomal aberration

1. NOUN

/ kɹoʊˌmoʊˈsoʊməl əˌbɛɹˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / kɹəʊˌməʊˈsəʊməl əˌbɛˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
any change in the normal structure or number of chromosomes, often resulting in physical or mental abnormalities
structural or numerical change in chromosomes
medical

Examples

A chromosomal aberration can be detected through genetic testing.
Down syndrome is caused by a specific type of chromosomal aberration.
Exposure to radiation may increase the risk of developing chromosomal aberrations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chromosomal abnormality - an abnormal condition involving chromosomes
  • Synonym: mutation - a change in the DNA sequence or genetic structure

Etymology

From 'chromosome', from Greek 'khrōma' (color) + 'sōma' (body), and 'aberration', from Latin 'aberrare' (to stray).
