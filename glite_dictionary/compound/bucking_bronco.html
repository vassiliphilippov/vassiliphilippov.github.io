bucking bronco

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌkɪŋ ˈbrɑŋkoʊ / (AmE), / ˈbʌkɪŋ ˈbrɒŋkəʊ / (BrE)
a wild or unbroken horse that bucks, often used in rodeos to test a rider's skill
a wild horse that bucks in rodeos

Examples

The cowboy managed to ride the bucking bronco for a full eight seconds.
At the rodeo, she watched a bucking bronco throw its rider with incredible force.
He dreamed of one day taming a bucking bronco in front of a cheering crowd.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bronco - an unbroken or partially trained horse

Etymology

From American English 'bronco', from Spanish 'bronco' (rough, unbroken), and 'bucking', describing the action of the horse.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbʌkɪŋ ˈbrɑŋkoʊ / (AmE), / ˈbʌkɪŋ ˈbrɒŋkəʊ / (BrE)
a horse, especially in rodeos, that bucks violently in an attempt to throw off its rider
a horse that bucks off riders

Examples

The cowboy managed to ride the bucking bronco for the full eight seconds.
She compared her hectic day to staying on a bucking bronco.
At the rodeo, the most exciting event was watching the bucking bronco try to throw its rider.

Synset Examples

The horse was a real bucking bronco at the rodeo.
She felt like a bucking bronco trying to control the situation.
He rode that bucking bronco like a pro.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bronco - an untrained or wild horse

Etymology

Bronco is from Spanish 'bronco', meaning rough or untrained.
