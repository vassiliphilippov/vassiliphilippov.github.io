alveolar ectasia

1. NOUN

/ ælˌvioʊlər ɛkˈteɪziə / (AmE), / ælˌviːələ ɛkˈteɪzɪə / (BrE)
abnormal enlargement of the alveoli (air sacs) in the lungs, often associated with lung diseases such as emphysema
abnormal enlargement of lung air sacs
medical

Examples

The doctor suspected alveolar ectasia after reviewing the patient's lung function tests.
Early diagnosis of alveolar ectasia can help prevent progression to severe respiratory problems.
Alveolar ectasia is often seen in chronic smokers with long-term lung damage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: emphysema - a chronic lung condition characterized by damage to the alveoli

Etymology

From Latin 'alveolus' (small cavity) and Greek 'ektasis' (extension, dilation).
