highball glass

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪˌbɔl ɡlæs / (AmE), / ˈhaɪˌbɔːl ɡlɑːs / (BrE)
a tall, straight-sided glass typically used for serving highball cocktails and similar mixed drinks
tall glass for mixed drinks

Examples

He ordered a gin and tonic in a highball glass at the bar.
Please make sure all the highball glasses are clean before the party begins.
The bartender filled the highball glass with ice before pouring the whiskey and soda.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tumbler - a flat-bottomed glass with no handle or stem

Etymology

From 'highball' (a type of cocktail) and 'glass'; 'highball' possibly from railroad terminology indicating 'all clear'.
