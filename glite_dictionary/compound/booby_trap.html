booby trap

1. NOUN

/ ˈbuːbi træp / (AmE), / ˈbuːbi træp / (BrE)
An explosive device or mine concealed to detonate when touched, stepped on, or otherwise triggered, often intended to cause injury or damage.
hidden explosive device

Examples

The soldiers moved cautiously to avoid setting off a hidden booby trap in the field.
He lost his leg after stepping on a booby trap during the war.
Clearing the building took hours because every room contained a potential booby trap.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: land mine - an explosive device placed on or just under the ground, detonated by pressure or proximity

Etymology

From 'booby' (fool) + 'trap,' first recorded in 19th century military use.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbuːbi ˌtræp / (AmE), / ˈbuːbi ˌtræp / (BrE)
a hidden or unexpected problem or difficulty that is intended to catch someone by surprise
unexpected difficulty or hidden danger

Examples

The contract was full of booby traps that caught many inexperienced parties off guard.
Negotiating the new regulations presented several booby traps for unwary businesses.
Learning a new language can be straightforward, but there are always some booby traps along the way.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pitfall - a hidden or unsuspected danger or difficulty
  • Synonym: snag - an unexpected or hidden obstacle or drawback

Etymology

From 'booby' (simpleton, fool) and 'trap'; originally military slang for hidden explosive devices, later generalized.
