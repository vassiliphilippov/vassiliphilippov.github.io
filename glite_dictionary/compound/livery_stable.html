livery stable

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪvəri ˌsteɪbəl / (AmE), / ˈlɪvəri ˌsteɪbl / (BrE)
a stable where horses and vehicles are kept for hire
stable for hiring horses and vehicles

Examples

The old town still has a livery stable where you can rent horses for a day trip.
In the 19th century, travelers would leave their carriages at the local livery stable.
My grandfather worked at a livery stable caring for the horses used by visitors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stable - a building for the lodging and feeding of horses or other livestock

Etymology

From 'livery' (from Old French livrée, meaning 'delivered', referring to provision of horses or vehicles) + 'stable'.
