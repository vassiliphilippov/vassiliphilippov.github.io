Chinese checkers

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃaɪ.niz ˈtʃɛk.ərz / (AmE), / ˈtʃaɪ.niːz ˈtʃɛk.əz / (BrE)
a board game played on a six-pointed star-shaped board in which each player tries to move a set of marbles from one point of the star to the opposite point
board game with marbles on a star-shaped board

Examples

We spent the rainy afternoon playing Chinese checkers by the fireplace.
His grandparents taught him the rules of Chinese checkers when he was a child.
At the party, we set up a Chinese checkers tournament to entertain the guests.

Etymology

The name is a marketing invention from the United States in the 1920s; the game is not of Chinese origin.
