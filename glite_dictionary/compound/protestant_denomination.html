Protestant denomination

1. NOUN

/ ˈproʊt̬əstənt ˌdɛnəˈmɪneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈprɒtɪstənt ˌdɛnəˈmɪneɪʃən / (BrE)
a recognized group of Protestant congregations sharing common beliefs, traditions, and practices within Christianity
group of Protestant congregations

Examples

The Methodist Church is one of the largest Protestant denominations in the United States.
Many Protestant denominations have their own unique traditions and practices.
The conference brought together leaders from several Protestant denominations to discuss religious freedom.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: denomination - a recognized autonomous branch within a religion
