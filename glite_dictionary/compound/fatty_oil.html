fatty oil

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæti ɔɪl / (AmE), / ˈfæti ɔɪl / (BrE)
An oil of animal or plant origin that is nonvolatile and composed mainly of triglycerides, as opposed to essential oils.
nonvolatile animal or plant oil

Examples

Olive oil is a type of fatty oil commonly used in cooking.
Some cosmetics use natural fatty oil to moisturize the skin.
Unlike essential oils, a fatty oil does not evaporate quickly or have a strong aroma.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: essential oil - a volatile oil obtained from plants, having the characteristic odor or flavor of the plant
  • Synonym: fixed oil - another term for fatty oil, a nonvolatile oil from plants or animals
