aerobic exercise

1. NOUN

/ ɛˈroʊbɪk ˈɛksaɪrsɪz / (AmE), / ɛəˈrəʊbɪk ˈɛksəsaɪz / (BrE)
physical activity that increases the body's demand for oxygen and improves cardiovascular endurance
oxygen-demanding physical activity

Examples

Many people incorporate aerobic exercise into their daily routine to improve heart health.
Running, swimming, and cycling are all forms of aerobic exercise.
Doctors often recommend aerobic exercise for patients who want to lose weight or increase stamina.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: anaerobic exercise - physical activity that does not require increased oxygen intake

Etymology

From 'aerobic' (from Greek 'aēr' meaning air + 'bios' meaning life) and 'exercise' (from Latin 'exercitium').
