orange marmalade

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔːrɪndʒ ˈmɑːrməˌleɪd / (AmE), / ˈɒrɪndʒ ˈmɑːməˌleɪd / (BrE)
A type of sweet preserve made from oranges, including pieces of rind, typically used as a spread for bread or toast.
a sweet preserve made from oranges

Examples

She spread orange marmalade on her toast every morning for breakfast.
The recipe calls for a spoonful of orange marmalade to add a citrusy flavor.
We bought a jar of homemade orange marmalade at the farmers' market.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marmalade - a preserve made from citrus fruit and sugar
  • Degree Lower: jam - a sweet spread made from boiled fruit and sugar

Etymology

Marmalade: from Portuguese 'marmelada' (quince jam); 'orange' from Old French 'orenge', from Arabic 'nāranj'.
