solid propellant rocket

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɑːlɪd prəˈpɛl·ənt ˈrɑːk·ɪt / (AmE), / ˈsɒlɪd prəˈpɛl·ənt ˈrɒk·ɪt / (BrE)
A rocket that uses a chemical propellant in solid form to generate thrust.
rocket using solid chemical fuel

Examples

The Space Shuttle used booster rockets based on solid propellant rocket technology.
A solid propellant rocket is often chosen for its simplicity and reliability.
During the demonstration, the solid propellant rocket ignited with a powerful burst of flame.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: liquid propellant rocket - a rocket using liquid propellant
