genus Hylobates

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs haɪˈloʊ.bəˌtiːz / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs hɪˈlɒ.bəˌtiːz / (BrE)
A genus of small apes known as gibbons, native to Southeast Asia and characterized by long arms and agile movement through trees.
genus of gibbons (apes)

Examples

The genus Hylobates includes several species of gibbons found in Indonesia and Malaysia.
Researchers study the genus Hylobates to learn more about primate evolution and behavior.
Due to deforestation, some members of the genus Hylobates are considered endangered.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gibbon - a small, agile, tree-dwelling ape

Etymology

From New Latin 'Hylobates', from Greek 'hylē' (forest) + 'baínō' (to go).
