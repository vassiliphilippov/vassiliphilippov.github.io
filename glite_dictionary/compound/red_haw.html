red haw

1. NOUN

/ rɛd hɔ / (AmE), / rɛd hɔː / (BrE)
A North American hawthorn tree (Crataegus species) that bears red fruit and dense clusters of pink-red flowers.
North American hawthorn with red fruit

Examples

The red haw in the garden is covered with bright red fruit every autumn.
Birds are attracted to the red haw because of its dense clusters of berries.
Spring brought a burst of pink-red flowers to the old red haw tree beside the house.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hawthorn - a thorny shrub or tree bearing small bitter red fruit

2. NOUN

/ rɛd hɔ / (AmE), / rɛd hɔː / (BrE)
An American red-fruited hawthorn with stems and leaves densely covered with short woolly hairs.
a red-fruited American hawthorn shrub

Examples

The field was bordered by a thick stand of red haw in full fruit.
Wildlife often feed on the bright berries of the red haw during autumn.
Gardeners appreciate the red haw for its decorative red berries and woolly stems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hawthorn - a type of shrub or small tree with thorny branches and edible red fruit
