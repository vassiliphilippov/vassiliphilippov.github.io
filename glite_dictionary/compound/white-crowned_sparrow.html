white-crowned sparrow

1. NOUN

/ waɪt kraʊnd ˈspæroʊ / (AmE), / waɪt kraʊnd ˈspærəʊ / (BrE)
A North American songbird with a black-and-white striped crown, belonging to the finch family.
North American songbird with black-and-white striped head

Examples

The white-crowned sparrow is known for its distinctive black-and-white striped head.
Every spring, the song of the white-crowned sparrow can be heard in the local park.
Birdwatchers often look for the white-crowned sparrow during migration season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: finch - a small to medium-sized songbird of the family Fringillidae
