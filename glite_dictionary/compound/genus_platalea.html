genus Platalea

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs pləˈteɪ.li.ə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs pləˈtiː.liː.ə / (BrE)
A genus of large, long-legged wading birds known as spoonbills, type genus of the family Plataleidae.
genus of spoonbill birds
medical

Examples

The genus Platalea includes several species of spoonbills found across the world.
Biologists recently observed breeding behavior in birds belonging to the genus Platalea.
The distinctive bill shape is characteristic of all members of the genus Platalea.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spoonbill - a wading bird with a broad, flat bill

Etymology

From Latin 'Platalea', the original name of the spoonbill genus.
