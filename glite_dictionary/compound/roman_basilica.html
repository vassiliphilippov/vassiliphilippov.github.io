Roman basilica

1. NOUN

/ ˈroʊmən bəˈsɪlɪkə / (AmE), / ˈrəʊmən bəˈsɪlɪkə / (BrE)
an ancient Roman building used for public administration, law courts, and other civic functions
ancient Roman public building

Examples

The ruins of the Roman basilica at Pompeii reveal its importance in civic life.
Architects studied the Roman basilica as a model for later church designs.
Many legal proceedings were held in a Roman basilica during the height of the empire.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: basilica - a large oblong hall or building with double colonnades, often used for law courts

Etymology

From Latin 'basilica', itself from Greek 'basilikē' (royal [house]).
