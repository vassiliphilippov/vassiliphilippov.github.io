elderberry bush

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛldɚˌbɛri bʊʃ / (AmE), / ˈɛldəˌbɛri bʊʃ / (BrE)
A shrub or small tree of the genus Sambucus, typically found in temperate and subtropical regions, bearing white flowers and small dark berries.
shrub/tree with white flowers and dark berries

Examples

The elderberry bush in our garden is covered with clusters of white flowers every spring.
Birds love to eat the ripe fruit from the elderberry bush in late summer.
She planted an elderberry bush near the fence to attract pollinators and enjoy homemade syrup.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elderberry - small dark fruit of the elderberry bush

Etymology

From 'elderberry,' combining 'elder' (tree of genus Sambucus) and 'berry.'
