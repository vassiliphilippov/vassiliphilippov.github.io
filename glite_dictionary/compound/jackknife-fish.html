jackknife-fish

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒækˌnaɪf fɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈdʒækˌnaɪf fɪʃ / (BrE)
A black-and-white drumfish with an erect elongated dorsal fin, found in the western Atlantic Ocean.
a drumfish with a long dorsal fin

Examples

The jackknife-fish is popular among aquarium enthusiasts for its striking appearance.
Divers spotted a school of jackknife-fish near the coral reef.
The distinctive dorsal fin of the jackknife-fish makes it easy to identify in the wild.
