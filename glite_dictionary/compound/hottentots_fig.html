Hottentot's fig

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑː.tənˌtɑts fɪɡ / (AmE), / ˈhɒt.ənˌtɒts fɪɡ / (BrE)
A low-growing South African succulent plant with fleshy leaves and a capsular fruit containing edible pulp; commonly found along the coast.
South African succulent plant with edible fruit

Examples

The coastal dunes were carpeted with bright flowers from the Hottentot's fig.
She tasted the tangy pulp of a ripe Hottentot's fig during her trip to South Africa.
Gardeners often plant Hottentot's fig to stabilize sandy soils near the ocean.

Etymology

From 'Hottentot', an old term for the Khoikhoi people, and 'fig' for the edible fruit. Now sometimes regarded as offensive.
