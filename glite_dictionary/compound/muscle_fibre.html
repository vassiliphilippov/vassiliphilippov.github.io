muscle fibre

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌsəl ˌfaɪbɚ / (AmE), / ˈmʌsl ˌfaɪbə / (BrE)
An elongated contractile cell that composes the muscles of the body and is responsible for movement.
elongated cell in muscle tissue

Examples

Each muscle fibre contracts in response to signals from the nervous system.
Athletes require a higher proportion of fast-twitch muscle fibres for explosive movements.
An injury can cause damage to the muscle fibres in the affected area.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: myocyte - muscle cell
