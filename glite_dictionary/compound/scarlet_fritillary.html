scarlet fritillary

1. NOUN

/ ˈskɑːrlət frɪˈtɪləri / (AmE), / ˈskɑːlɪt frɪˈtɪləri / (BrE)
a western United States plant with scarlet and yellow narrow bell-shaped flowers, belonging to the lily family
western US plant with scarlet-yellow flowers

Examples

The scarlet fritillary blooms in early spring across the foothills of California.
We spotted several scarlet fritillaries during our hike in the Sierra Nevada mountains.
Gardeners admire the scarlet fritillary for its vibrant, bell-shaped flowers and unique coloration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fritillary - plant of the lily family with bell-shaped flowers

Etymology

From 'scarlet' (bright red color) and 'fritillary' (from Latin 'fritillaria', a type of flower).
