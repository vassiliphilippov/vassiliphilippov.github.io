abducent nerve

1. NOUN

/ æbˈdusənt nɜrv / (AmE), / æbˈdjuːsənt nɜːv / (BrE)
the sixth cranial nerve, responsible for controlling the lateral rectus muscle that moves the eye outward
sixth cranial nerve controlling lateral eye movement
medical

Examples

Damage to the abducent nerve can result in an inability to move the eye outward.
The physician suspected an abducent nerve palsy based on the patient's symptoms.
An MRI was ordered to check for lesions affecting the abducent nerve.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cranial nerve - any of the nerves that arise directly from the brain
  • Degree Lower: oculomotor nerve - the third cranial nerve controlling most eye movements
  • Degree Lower: ocular motor nerve - another name for oculomotor nerve, controlling most eye movements
  • Degree Lower: trochlear nerve - the fourth cranial nerve, involved in eye movement

Etymology

From Latin 'abducens' (leading away).
