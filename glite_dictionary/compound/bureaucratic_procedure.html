bureaucratic procedure

1. NOUN

/ bjʊˌɹɑː.kɹəˈtɪk pɹəˈsiː.dʒɚ / (AmE), / bjʊəˌɹɒk.rəˈtɪk prəˈsiː.dʒə / (BrE)
an official process or set of rules that is often unnecessarily complicated and time-consuming, typically associated with bureaucracy
needlessly complicated official process

Examples

Many complained that the bureaucratic procedure delayed the approval of building permits for months.
She grew frustrated with the endless bureaucratic procedure required to change her residency status.
Getting a simple document approved became a complex bureaucratic procedure involving multiple offices.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: red tape - excessive bureaucracy or adherence to official rules and formalities

Etymology

From French 'bureaucratie' (bureaucracy) + English 'procedure'.
