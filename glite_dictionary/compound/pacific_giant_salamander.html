Pacific giant salamander

1. NOUN

/ pəˈsɪfɪk ˈdʒaɪənt ˈsæləˌmændər / (AmE), / pəˈsɪfɪk ˈdʒaɪənt ˈsæləˌmændə / (BrE)
a large species of salamander native to the forests of western North America, noted for its size and robust build
large salamander of western North America

Examples

The Pacific giant salamander can reach lengths of up to seven inches in the wild.
Researchers observed a Pacific giant salamander hiding under a log near the stream.
Many hikers are surprised by the size of the Pacific giant salamander in the forests of Oregon.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: salamander - an amphibian of the order Caudata with a lizard-like appearance
