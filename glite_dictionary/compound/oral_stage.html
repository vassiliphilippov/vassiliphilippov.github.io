oral stage

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrəl steɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈɒːrəl steɪdʒ / (BrE)
(in psychoanalysis) the first stage of infant development, during which pleasure is centered in the mouth and satisfaction comes from activities like sucking, chewing, and biting.
first psychosexual stage (psychoanalysis)
medical

Examples

According to Freud, a fixation at the oral stage may lead to issues with dependency in adulthood.
The oral stage is characterized by infants deriving pleasure from sucking and eating.
Many psychoanalysts consider the oral stage crucial in shaping early personality traits.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: anal stage - second psychosexual stage (focus on the anus, toilet training)
