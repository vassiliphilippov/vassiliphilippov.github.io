wild rosemary

1. NOUN

/ waɪld ˈroʊzmɛri / (AmE), / waɪld ˈrəʊzməri / (BrE)
a bog shrub native to northern and central Europe and eastern Siberia to Korea and Japan, known for its aromatic leaves but unrelated to true rosemary
bog shrub native to Eurasia

Examples

The marsh was dotted with patches of wild rosemary among the moss and grasses.
Scientists are studying the medicinal properties of wild rosemary in traditional remedies.
We identified the fragrant leaves of wild rosemary during our botany field trip.
