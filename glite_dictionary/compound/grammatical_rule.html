grammatical rule

1. NOUN

/ ɡrəˈmætɪkəl ruːl / (AmE), / ɡrəˈmætɪkəl ruːl / (BrE)
a rule that governs the structure and formation of sentences and phrases in a language
rule for sentence structure in a language

Examples

Every language has its own set of grammatical rules that learners must master.
Violating a basic grammatical rule can make a sentence difficult to understand.
Teachers often explain grammatical rules to help students improve their writing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: syntax - the arrangement of words and phrases to create well-formed sentences
