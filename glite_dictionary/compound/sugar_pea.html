sugar pea

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃʊɡɚ pi / (AmE), / ˈʃʊɡə pi / (BrE)
a variety of pea plant with thin, flat edible pods, commonly eaten whole
variety of edible pea with flat pods

Examples

I added fresh sugar peas to the stir-fry for extra crunch.
Sugar peas are often used in Asian cuisine because their pods are tender and sweet.
She prefers sugar peas over regular peas because you can eat the whole pod.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snow pea - a similar variety of pea with edible flat pods, often called sugar pea

2. NOUN

/ ˈʃʊɡɚ pi / (AmE), / ˈʃʊɡə piː / (BrE)
A variety of pea with flat, edible pods that are eaten whole while still unripe.
pea with flat, edible pods

Examples

She added fresh sugar peas to the stir-fry for a crisp, sweet flavor.
At the farmer's market, I bought a basket of organic sugar peas.
The recipe calls for trimming the ends off the sugar peas before cooking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snap pea - a related variety of pea with edible pods but rounder and crisper than sugar pea
  • Synonym: snow pea - a type of edible-podded pea similar to sugar pea
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07741765-n
Synset POS: n
