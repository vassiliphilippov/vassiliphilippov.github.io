Nubian Desert

1. PROPN

/ ˈnuːbiən ˈdɛzɚt / (AmE), / ˈnjuːbɪən ˈdɛzət / (BrE)
an arid sandstone plateau in northeastern Sudan, lying between the Nile and the Red Sea
desert region in northeastern Sudan

Examples

The Nubian Desert is known for its harsh climate and sparse vegetation.
Explorers crossed the Nubian Desert during their research expeditions in Sudan.
Annual rainfall in the Nubian Desert is extremely low, making it one of the driest places on Earth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sahara Desert - large desert in North Africa

Etymology

Named after the Nubian people, indigenous to the region.
