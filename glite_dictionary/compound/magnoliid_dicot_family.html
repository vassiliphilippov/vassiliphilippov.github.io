magnoliid dicot family

1. NOUN

/ mæɡˈnoʊliɪd ˈdaɪkɒt ˈfæmɪli / (AmE), / mæɡˈnəʊlɪɪd ˈdɪkɒt ˈfamɪli / (BrE)
A family of dicotyledonous flowering plants considered among the most primitive living angiosperms.
primitive dicot flowering plant family

Examples

The magnoliid dicot family includes many plants that are considered living fossils by botanists.
Scientists study the magnoliid dicot family to understand the early evolution of flowering plants.
Some spices, such as nutmeg and bay laurel, belong to a magnoliid dicot family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: magnoliid - primitive group of angiosperms
  • Antonym: monocot family - a family of flowering plants with single cotyledon

Etymology

From 'Magnolia' (genus name) + '-id' (forming adjectives) and 'dicot' (from 'dicotyledon').
