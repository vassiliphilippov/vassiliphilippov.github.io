whorled milkweed

1. NOUN

/ wɜrld ˈmɪlkwid / (AmE), / wɜːld ˈmɪlkwiːd / (BrE)
A species of milkweed native to eastern North America, characterized by narrow leaves arranged in whorls and greenish-white flowers.
a North American milkweed with whorled leaves

Examples

The prairie was dotted with whorled milkweed blooming among the tall grasses.
Monarch caterpillars feed on whorled milkweed as a vital part of their life cycle.
Gardeners appreciate the delicate blooms of whorled milkweed in naturalized landscapes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: milkweed - any of various plants of the genus Asclepias with milky sap
