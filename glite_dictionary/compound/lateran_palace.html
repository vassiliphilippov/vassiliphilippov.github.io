Lateran Palace

1. PROPN

/ ˈleɪ.tə.rən ˈpæl.ɪs / (AmE), / ˈleɪ.tər.ən ˈpæl.ɪs / (BrE)
the former principal residence of the popes in Rome until the 14th century, now part of the Lateran complex
historic papal residence in Rome

Examples

Papal councils were frequently held at the Lateran Palace during the Middle Ages.
After the 14th century, the popes moved from the Lateran Palace to the Vatican.
The architecture of the Lateran Palace reflects many centuries of renovations and historical events.

Etymology

From Latin 'Lateranum', the name of the district and palace in Rome.
