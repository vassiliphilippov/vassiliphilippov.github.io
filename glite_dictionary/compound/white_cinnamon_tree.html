white cinnamon tree

1. NOUN

/ waɪt ˈsɪn.ə.mən triː / (AmE), / waɪt ˈsɪn.əm.ɪn ˌtriː / (BrE)
a large evergreen shrub or small tree with white aromatic bark, leathery leaves, and small purple to red flowers in terminal clusters
tree with white aromatic bark and purple-red flowers

Examples

The white cinnamon tree is prized for its fragrant bark used in traditional medicine.
Gardeners admired the leathery leaves and colorful flowers of the white cinnamon tree.
Researchers studied the aromatic compounds found in the bark of the white cinnamon tree.
