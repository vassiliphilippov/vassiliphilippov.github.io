embryonic membrane

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛm.briˈɑ.nɪk ˈmɛm.breɪn / (AmE), / ˌɛm.brɪˈɒn.ɪk ˈmɛm.breɪn / (BrE)
an inner membrane surrounding the embryo in higher vertebrates, particularly when covering the head at birth
inner membrane around embryo
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that the embryonic membrane sometimes remains intact during childbirth.
Researchers are studying how the embryonic membrane contributes to the development of organs.
In rare cases, a baby may be born with the embryonic membrane covering its head.

Etymology

From 'embryonic' (relating to an embryo) and 'membrane' (thin layer of tissue).
