euhemerization of myths

1. NOUN

/ juˌhimərəˈzeɪʃən əv mɪθs / (AmE), / juːˌhiːmərəˈzeɪʃən əv mɪθs / (BrE)
the interpretation of myths as accounts of actual historical events or persons
interpreting myths as historical events

Examples

The euhemerization of myths was popular among historians who sought factual origins for legendary tales.
Some scholars argue that the euhemerization of myths can oversimplify complex cultural narratives.
Through the euhemerization of myths, heroes like Hercules were viewed as real historical figures.

Synset Examples

The euhemerization of myths helps us understand ancient beliefs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rationalization of myths - explaining myths as symbolic or psychological phenomena rather than literal truth

Etymology

From the name of the ancient Greek writer Euhemerus, who argued myths originated in real history.
