Galapagos Islands

1. PROPN

/ ɡəˈlɑːpəˌɡoʊs ˈaɪləndz / (AmE), / ˌɡæləˈpæɡəs ˈaɪləndz / (BrE)
a group of volcanic islands in the Pacific Ocean, belonging to Ecuador, famous for their unique wildlife and natural significance
volcanic islands in the Pacific Ocean, noted for unique wildlife

Examples

Charles Darwin visited the Galapagos Islands in 1835, inspiring his theory of evolution.
The Galapagos Islands are home to many species found nowhere else on Earth.
Tourists travel from around the world to explore the natural wonders of the Galapagos Islands.

Etymology

Spanish *Islas Galápagos* (Islands of the Tortoises), from 'galápago' meaning 'tortoise'.
