common European earwig

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːmən jʊˌrəˈpiːən ˈɪrˌwɪɡ / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən jʊˌərəˈpiːən ˈɪəˌwɪɡ / (BrE)
a species of earwig (Forficula auricularia) native to Europe, sometimes harmful to cultivated bulbs
a species of European earwig insect

Examples

The common European earwig is often found hiding under flowerpots or stones in gardens.
Gardeners sometimes worry that the common European earwig will damage their flower bulbs.
Despite its fearsome pincers, the common European earwig rarely bites humans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: earwig - a small elongated insect with pincers on the abdomen
