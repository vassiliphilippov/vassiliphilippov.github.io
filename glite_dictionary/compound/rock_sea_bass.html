rock sea bass

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɑk si ˌbæs / (AmE), / ˈrɒk si ˌbæs / (BrE)
a small species of sea bass found in rocky coastal waters of the western Atlantic, known for its robust body and dark coloration
a species of sea bass inhabiting rocky coasts

Examples

The diver spotted a rock sea bass hiding among the stones on the ocean floor.
Fishermen often try to catch rock sea bass along the rocky Atlantic shoreline.
The aquarium features several species, including the colorful rock sea bass.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sea bass - a common name for various species of marine fish in the family Serranidae
