information superhighway

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪnfɚˈmeɪʃən ˌsuːpɚˈhaɪˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˌɪnfəˈmeɪʃən ˌsuːpəˈhaɪˌweɪ / (BrE)
An extensive electronic communications network, especially the internet, used for rapid transfer of digital data including sound, video, and graphics.
the internet and related digital communication networks

Examples

The information superhighway has revolutionized how people access and share knowledge around the world.
During the 1990s, politicians often spoke about investing in the information superhighway to promote economic growth.
Artists are finding new ways to distribute their work thanks to the information superhighway.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: internet - the global system of interconnected computer networks

Etymology

First popularized in the early 1990s, combining 'information' and 'superhighway' as a metaphor for fast, broad digital communication; 'superhighway' refers to a major road for rapid travel.
