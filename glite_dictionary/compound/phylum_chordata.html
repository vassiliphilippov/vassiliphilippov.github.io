phylum Chordata

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪləm kɔrˈdeɪtə / (AmE), / ˈfʌɪləm kɔːˈdeɪtə / (BrE)
A major group in animal classification that includes all vertebrates and animals possessing a notochord at some stage of development.
animal group with a notochord or backbone

Examples

All mammals, birds, reptiles, amphibians, and fish belong to the phylum Chordata.
The phylum Chordata is characterized by animals that have a notochord at some stage of development.
Tunicates and lancelets are less familiar members of the phylum Chordata.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: vertebrate - animal with a backbone
  • Antonym: invertebrate - animal without a backbone

Etymology

From Greek khordē (string) and New Latin Chordata, referring to the notochord characteristic.
