English sonnet

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ ˈsɑnɪt / (AmE), / ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ ˈsɒnɪt / (BrE)
a sonnet consisting of three quatrains and a concluding couplet, usually in iambic pentameter and following the rhyme pattern abab cdcd efef gg
a sonnet with three quatrains and a couplet (abab cdcd efef gg)

Examples

Many of Shakespeare's poems are written as English sonnets, marked by their distinctive rhyme scheme.
The English sonnet form is often used to express deep emotion within a structured pattern.
Students analyzed how each English sonnet concludes with a powerful rhyming couplet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Shakespearean sonnet - a sonnet with the same form as the English sonnet
  • Antonym: Petrarchan sonnet - a sonnet form with an octave and sestet, typical rhyme abba abba cde cde

Etymology

Also known as Shakespearean sonnet, named for its use by William Shakespeare.
