common duckweed

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmən ˈdʌkˌwid / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən ˈdʌkˌwiːd / (BrE)
a small free-floating aquatic plant (Lemna minor) found in temperate regions outside eastern Asia and Australia, often covering the surface of still or slow-moving water
a widespread small aquatic plant

Examples

The surface of the pond was covered in a thick layer of common duckweed.
Gardeners often remove common duckweed from ornamental ponds to keep the water clear.
The rapid growth of common duckweed can sometimes block sunlight for underwater plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: duckweed - any of several small floating aquatic plants of the family Lemnaceae
