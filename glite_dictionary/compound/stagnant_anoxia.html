stagnant anoxia

1. NOUN

/ ˈstæɡnənt əˈnɑksiə / (AmE), / ˈstæɡnənt əˈnɒksiə / (BrE)
A type of lack of oxygen in body tissues caused by slow peripheral circulation, often seen in cases of congestive heart failure.
lack of oxygen from poor blood circulation
medical

Examples

Patients with congestive heart failure are at increased risk of stagnant anoxia.
Stagnant anoxia can occur when blood circulation becomes extremely slow.
Doctors monitor oxygen levels in cases where stagnant anoxia is suspected.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: anoxia - absence of oxygen in body tissues
  • Degree Lower: hypoxia - deficiency in the amount of oxygen reaching tissues

Etymology

From 'stagnant' (not flowing) and 'anoxia' (lack of oxygen), the latter from Greek 'an-' (without) + 'oxys' (sharp, oxygen).
