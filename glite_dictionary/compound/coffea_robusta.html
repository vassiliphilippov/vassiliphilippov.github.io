Coffea robusta

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔfi ə roʊˈbʌstə / (AmE), / ˈkɒfi ə rəʊˈbʌstə / (BrE)
A species of coffee plant, native to West Africa, widely grown for its beans, which are resistant to coffee rust and used in commercial coffee production.
species of coffee plant

Examples

Many instant coffees are made from Coffea robusta beans due to their strong flavor.
The farm switched to growing Coffea robusta because it is less susceptible to disease.
Researchers are studying Coffea robusta for its ability to thrive in harsher climates.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Coffea arabica - the most commonly cultivated coffee species with milder flavor

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'Coffea' (the coffee genus) with Latin 'robusta' (strong, sturdy).
