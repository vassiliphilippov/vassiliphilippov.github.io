bullace grape

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʊleɪs ɡreɪp / (AmE), / ˈbʊlɪs ɡreɪp / (BrE)
a dull-purple grape native to the southern United States
dull-purple grape of the southern US

Examples

The vineyard specializes in bullace grape, prized for its unique flavor.
Jams made from the bullace grape are a traditional treat in southern cuisine.
She planted a few bullace grape vines alongside the house.

Etymology

From 'bullace', a type of wild plum, and 'grape'. 'Bullace' comes from Middle English, from Old French 'beloce'.
