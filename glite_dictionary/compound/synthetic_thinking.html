synthetic thinking

1. NOUN

/ sɪnˈθɛtɪk ˈθɪŋkɪŋ / (AmE), / sɪnˈθɛtɪk ˈθɪŋkɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of combining separate ideas or elements to form a unified and comprehensive whole
combining ideas into a whole

Examples

Effective leaders often use synthetic thinking to develop strategies that integrate diverse opinions.
His synthetic thinking enabled him to create solutions by combining concepts from different fields.
The course encourages synthetic thinking, pushing students to connect knowledge from various disciplines.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: analytical thinking - breaking down complex ideas into components

Etymology

From 'synthetic', from Greek 'synthesis' (putting together) and 'thinking'.
