Mongolian race

1. NOUN

/ mɑŋˈɡoʊliən reɪs / (AmE), / mɒŋˈɡəʊliən reɪs / (BrE)
an outdated anthropological classification referring to peoples of East Asia, Southeast Asia, and other regions, now considered inaccurate and offensive
outdated anthropological racial classification
archaic offensive

Examples

The term Mongolian race was used in 19th-century anthropology to classify people of East Asian origin.
The concept of the Mongolian race is now recognized as scientifically invalid and offensive.
Older texts may refer to East Asian peoples as belonging to the Mongolian race.

Etymology

From 'Mongolian', relating to Mongolia or its people + 'race', originally denoting groups divided by physical traits.
