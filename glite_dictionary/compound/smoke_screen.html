smoke screen

1. NOUN

/ smoʊk skriːn / (AmE), / sməʊk skriːn / (BrE)
a cloud of smoke created to conceal military movements or activities from the enemy's view
cloud of smoke concealing movement

Examples

The army released a smoke screen to hide their tanks as they advanced across the field.
During the exercise, the ship set up a smoke screen to obscure its position from observers.
A well-timed smoke screen allowed the troops to retreat without being seen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: camouflage - the use of materials or coloration to hide or disguise people or objects from view

2. NOUN

/ smoʊk skrin / (AmE), / sməʊk skriːn / (BrE)
An action or statement used to conceal the truth, confuse others, or obscure the real purpose or situation.
action used to conceal or confuse

Examples

The elaborate investigation was just a smoke screen to distract the media from the real issue.
Politicians often use a smoke screen of complicated language to avoid answering difficult questions.
Her apology turned out to be a smoke screen for her actual intentions.

Synset Examples

requesting new powers of surveillance is just a smokescreen to hide their failures

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cover - something that hides or protects the real thing
  • Synonym: disguise - to give a different appearance in order to conceal identity
  • Synonym: pretext - a false reason given to hide the real one

Etymology

Originally referring to a cloud of smoke used on battlefields to obscure troop movements.
