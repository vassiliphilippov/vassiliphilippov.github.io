Pindaric ode

1. NOUN

/ pɪnˈdɛrɪk oʊd / (AmE), / pɪnˈdarɪk əʊd / (BrE)
a type of ode characterized by a formal structure with groups of three stanzas, modeled after the odes of the ancient Greek poet Pindar
a formal ode with a triadic structure

Examples

The poet composed a Pindaric ode to commemorate the nation's bicentennial.
Many English writers in the 17th century experimented with the Pindaric ode format.
The Pindaric ode requires careful attention to its complex stanzaic structure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ode - a lyric poem expressing praise or emotion

Etymology

Named after Pindar, an ancient Greek lyric poet, whose odes inspired the form.
