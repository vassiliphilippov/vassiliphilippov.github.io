family Resedaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌfæməli rɛzəˈdeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / ˌfæmɪli rɛzᵻˈdeɪsɪˌiː / (BrE)
A family of mainly Mediterranean herbs, commonly known as mignonette, including various species of aromatic plants.
a family of Mediterranean herbs (mignonette)

Examples

The family Resedaceae consists of several herbaceous plants native to Mediterranean regions.
Mignonette, a popular garden plant, belongs to the family Resedaceae.
Botanists study the characteristics that distinguish the family Resedaceae from other plant families.

Etymology

From Latin 'Reseda' (a type of mignonette) plus the botanical suffix '-aceae' indicating a plant family.
