gag order

1. NOUN

/ ɡæɡ ˈɔrdər / (AmE), / ɡæɡ ˈɔːdə / (BrE)
a legal order by a court restricting the disclosure of information or public comment about a particular case
legal order limiting case publicity
legal american

Examples

The judge issued a gag order to prevent lawyers from discussing the case with the media.
After the gag order was announced, reporters could no longer report on the trial's details.
Her attorney warned that violating the gag order could lead to contempt of court charges.

Synset Examples

imposing a gag order on members of the press violates the First Amendment

Related Terms

  • Synonym: injunction - a court order requiring someone to do or cease doing a specific action
  • Synonym: broadcast ban - a prohibition on broadcasting certain information

Etymology

From 'gag' (to silence) + 'order' (official directive); legal use dates from 20th century U.S. courts.
