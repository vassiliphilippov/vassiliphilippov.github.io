ascorbic acid

1. NOUN

/ əˈskɔrbɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / əˌskɔːbɪk ˈæsɪd / (BrE)
a naturally occurring vitamin (vitamin C) found in many fruits and vegetables, essential for the prevention of scurvy and maintenance of good health
vitamin C in fruits and vegetables
medical

Examples

Fresh oranges are a rich source of ascorbic acid, which helps strengthen the immune system.
Doctors recommend getting enough ascorbic acid in your diet to prevent scurvy.
This supplement contains a high concentration of ascorbic acid for added nutritional benefits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vitamin C - another name for ascorbic acid
  • Antonym: scurvy - a disease caused by vitamin C deficiency

Etymology

From Latin 'scorbutus' (scurvy) plus 'a-' (without), indicating prevention of scurvy.
