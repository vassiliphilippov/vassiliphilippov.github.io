Divine Comedy

1. PROPN

/ dəˈvaɪn ˈkɑːmədi / (AmE), / dɪˈvaɪn ˈkɒmədi / (BrE)
a narrative epic poem written by Dante Alighieri in the early 14th century, describing the journey of the soul through Hell, Purgatory, and Paradise
epic poem by Dante Alighieri

Examples

Many scholars consider the Divine Comedy one of the greatest works of world literature.
In his essay, he explored the symbolism of the Divine Comedy in relation to medieval theology.
The Divine Comedy was originally written in the Tuscan dialect of Italian.

Etymology

From Italian 'Divina Commedia', title given by later editors; 'divina' meaning 'divine', 'commedia' meaning 'comedy'.
