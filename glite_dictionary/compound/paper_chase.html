paper chase

1. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪpər tʃeɪs / (AmE), / ˈpeɪpə tʃeɪs / (BrE)
an outdoor game in which one group of players (the hares) run ahead, leaving a trail of bits of paper, while another group (the hounds) try to follow the trail and catch them before they reach the finish.
an outdoor chasing game using paper trails

Examples

As children, we would often organize a paper chase in the local park during summer.
The school's sports day included a traditional paper chase that excited all the participants.
Setting up a paper chase requires plenty of paper scraps and some adventurous runners.

Etymology

Refers to the actual use of paper as a trail in the game's origins in 19th-century England.
