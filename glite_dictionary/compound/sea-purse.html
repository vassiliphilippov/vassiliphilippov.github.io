sea-purse

1. NOUN

/ ˈsiˌpɝs / (AmE), / ˈsiːˌpɜːs / (BrE)
the seaward undercurrent formed after waves break on the shore, pulling water back towards the sea
seaward undercurrent after waves break

Examples

Swimmers should be cautious of the sea-purse, as it can pull them away from the shore.
The sea-purse was especially strong after the storm, making it dangerous to enter the water.
Lifeguards often warn about the risks associated with the sea-purse at this beach.
