Roman coriander

1. NOUN

/ ˈroʊ.mən kəˈræn.dɚ / (AmE), / ˈrəʊ.mən kəˈræn.də / (BrE)
an herb of the Mediterranean region (Nigella sativa) with pungent seeds used as a spice, similar to caraway
Mediterranean herb with spice seeds

Examples

Many Indian recipes call for Roman coriander seeds to enhance flavor.
You can find Roman coriander in specialty spice shops or international markets.
The pungency of Roman coriander makes it a valued spice in Mediterranean cuisine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: black cumin - an alternative name for Roman coriander (Nigella sativa)
  • Degree Lower: caraway - a plant with similar-tasting seeds used as a spice
