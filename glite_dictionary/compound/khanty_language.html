Khanty language

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑnti ˌlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈkanti ˌlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Ugric language spoken by the Khanty people (Ostyaks) of western Siberia, related to Hungarian and Mansi
Ugric language of the Khanty people

Examples

The Khanty language is endangered, with fewer than 10,000 speakers remaining today.
He is researching traditional songs preserved in the Khanty language.
Linguists study the Khanty language to understand the history of the Ugric branch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hungarian - Finno-Ugric language spoken in Hungary
  • Synonym: Mansi language - another Ugric language closely related to Khanty

Etymology

Khanty: from the self-designation of the people; language: from Latin lingua, meaning 'tongue, speech'.
