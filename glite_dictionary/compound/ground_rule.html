ground rule

1. NOUN

/ ɡraʊnd ruːl / (AmE), / ɡraʊnd ruːl / (BrE)
a rule, especially in baseball, established to deal with situations caused by the particular features of the playing field; more generally, a fundamental rule that applies in a specific context
special rule for specific situation, esp. in baseball

Examples

The stadium has a unique ground rule for balls that hit the scoreboard.
Before we start, let's review the basic ground rules for this discussion.
Each ballpark may have its own ground rule that affects how certain plays are judged.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rule - a prescribed guide for conduct or action

2. NOUN

/ ɡraʊnd ruːl / (AmE), / ɡraʊnd ruːl / (BrE)
a basic rule that applies to a particular situation, especially established at the beginning to ensure order and fairness
basic rule for a specific situation

Examples

Before we begin the project, let's agree on a ground rule for communication.
One important ground rule of this classroom is to respect everyone's ideas.
The committee established a ground rule that all members must attend at least half the meetings.

Synset Examples

The umpire explained the ground rule to the confused players.
They need to follow the ground rule for fair play.
Every game starts with a clear ground rule to ensure fairness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rule - a statement telling what is or is not allowed in a particular situation

Etymology

From the phrase 'ground rules' originally used in sports (late 19th c.) for rules specific to a particular playing field.
