doctor's degree

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɑktɚz dɪˈɡri / (AmE), / ˈdɒktəz dɪˈɡriː / (BrE)
an academic degree awarded by a university or college as the highest level of achievement in a particular field of study
highest university academic degree

Examples

After many years of research, she finally earned her doctor's degree in chemistry.
A doctor's degree is often required for advanced positions in academia.
He received an honorary doctor's degree from the university for his contributions to science.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: doctorate - an academic degree of the highest rank
  • Degree Lower: master's degree - an academic degree lower than a doctor's degree
