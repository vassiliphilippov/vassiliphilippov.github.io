net ton

1. NOUN

/ nɛt tʌn / (AmE), / nɛt tɒn / (BrE)
A United States unit of weight equal to 2,000 pounds, also known as a short ton.
U.S. weight unit of 2,000 pounds
american

Examples

The shipment weighed over fifty net tons and required special handling.
Steel production is often measured in net tons for reporting purposes in the United States.
The contract specifies a price per net ton of coal delivered.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: short ton - A U.S. ton equivalent to 2,000 pounds
  • Antonym: long ton - A British unit of weight equivalent to 2,240 pounds
