combination in restraint of trade

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːmbəˈneɪʃən ɪn rɪˈstreɪnt əv treɪd / (AmE), / ˌkɒmbɪˈneɪʃən ɪn rɪˈstreɪnt əv treɪd / (BrE)
A monopoly, contract, or agreement among parties designed to limit competition or restrict free trade, considered illegal under United States antitrust laws.
illegal agreement restricting competition (US law)
legal american

Examples

The court found that the firms had engaged in a combination in restraint of trade by fixing prices.
Any combination in restraint of trade is prohibited under the Sherman Antitrust Act.
His legal team argued that their agreement did not constitute a combination in restraint of trade.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antitrust - relating to legislation preventing or controlling monopolies and promoting competition
  • Synonym: monopoly - exclusive control by one company over an entire market
