so far

1. ADV

/ soʊ ˈfɑr / (AmE), / səʊ ˈfɑː / (BrE)
up to this point in time; until now, especially following a superlative to indicate the best, worst, or most so far achieved
up to this point in time

Examples

That was his most impressive speech so far this year.
The company has raised the highest amount of money so far among all competitors.
This is the best result we've seen so far in the experiment.

Synset Examples

this is the best so far
the largest drug bust yet

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yet - up to now; until the present or a specified or implied time
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00028594-r
Synset POS: r

2. ADV

/ soʊ ˈfɑr / (AmE), / səʊ ˈfɑː / (BrE)
up to this time; until now; used especially with negative statements or questions to refer to what has happened up to the present moment.
up to now; until this point

Examples

So far, we have not received any responses to our invitation.
She has completed five chapters so far, and plans to finish the book next month.
So far, everything is going according to plan.

Synset Examples

So far he hasn't called
the sun isn't up yet

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thus far - up to this point; similar meaning to 'so far'

Synset POS: r
