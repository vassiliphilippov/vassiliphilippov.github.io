summer squash

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌmɚ skwɑʃ / (AmE), / ˈsʌmə skwɒʃ / (BrE)
A type of squash plant with bushy growth, producing edible fruit that is harvested and eaten while still immature with soft rind and seeds.
a type of edible immature squash

Examples

We picked a fresh summer squash from the garden for tonight's dinner.
This recipe calls for thinly sliced summer squash sautéed with garlic and herbs.
Unlike winter varieties, summer squash are best eaten while their skin is still tender.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zucchini - a type of summer squash with long, green fruit
  • Antonym: winter squash - type of squash harvested at maturity, with hard rind and seeds

2. NOUN

/ ˈsʌmɚ skwɑʃ / (AmE), / ˈsʌmə skwɒʃ / (BrE)
A type of squash harvested while still immature and with a tender rind, typically eaten as a vegetable; includes varieties such as zucchini and crookneck.
type of tender, immature squash

Examples

We sautéed fresh summer squash from the market with olive oil and garlic.
Zucchini is a popular variety of summer squash found in many recipes.
The garden was filled with different kinds of summer squash, all ready to be picked in July.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zucchini - a variety of summer squash with a smooth, dark green skin
  • Antonym: winter squash - squash harvested when mature with a hard rind

Etymology

Named for being harvested in summer and distinguished from winter squash.
