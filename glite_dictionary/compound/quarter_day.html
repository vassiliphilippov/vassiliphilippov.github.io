quarter day

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɔrdɚ deɪ / (AmE), / ˈkwɔːtə deɪ / (BrE)
One of four traditional days in the year, often associated with Christian holy days, when rents or other payments are due.
traditional day for periodic payments

Examples

Rents were traditionally collected on quarter day in many parts of Britain.
On each quarter day, tenants were expected to settle their dues to the landlord.
Historically, quarter day was also a time for hiring fairs and settling contracts.

Etymology

From the practice of dividing the year into quarters for legal and financial purposes in Britain and Ireland.
