foetal distress

1. NOUN

/ ˈfiːtəl dɪˈstrɛs / (AmE), / ˈfiːtəl dɪˈstrɛs / (BrE)
A medical condition in which a fetus shows signs of abnormality, commonly detected by an irregular heart rate or rhythm during pregnancy or labor, indicating potential risk to the fetus's well-being.
dangerous fetal condition in pregnancy
medical british

Examples

The doctor expressed concern about foetal distress during the final stage of labour.
Continuous monitoring is necessary to detect any signs of foetal distress in high-risk pregnancies.
Emergency intervention was required after foetal distress was identified on the monitor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fetal distress - An abnormal condition of a fetus; American spelling variant

Etymology

From Latin 'fetus' (offspring, bringing forth) and English 'distress' (extreme anxiety, sorrow, pain).
