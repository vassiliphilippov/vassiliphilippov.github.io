shuttlecock fern

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃʌt̬əlˌkɑk fɜrn / (AmE), / ˈʃʌtəlkɒk fɜːn / (BrE)
a tall fern of northern temperate regions with gracefully arched fronds and fertile fronds resembling ostrich plumes, especially Matteuccia struthiopteris
tall northern fern with plume-like fronds

Examples

The shuttlecock fern adds elegance to shady garden borders with its tall, arching fronds.
In spring, the distinctive shape of the shuttlecock fern makes it easy to identify in the wild.
Many gardeners grow the shuttlecock fern for its ornamental foliage and hardy nature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ostrich fern - common name for Matteuccia struthiopteris, a similar or identical species

Etymology

Named for the resemblance of its fronds to a shuttlecock (the object used in badminton).
