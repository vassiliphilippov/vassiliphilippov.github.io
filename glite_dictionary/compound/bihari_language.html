Bihari language

1. NOUN

/ bɪˈhɑri ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / bɪˈhɑːri ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
a group of Indic languages spoken mainly in Bihar, India, and also in parts of Pakistan and Bangladesh
Indic language(s) of Bihar, India

Examples

Many people in Bihar still speak Bihari language at home despite using Hindi at school.
The government launched new programs to preserve the Bihari language and its dialects.
Some migrants in Bangladesh continue to use Bihari language as part of their cultural identity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hindi - official language of India, closely related Indo-Aryan language
  • Synonym: Bengali - Eastern Indo-Aryan language of Bangladesh and India

Etymology

From Bihar, the name of an Indian state, plus 'language'.
