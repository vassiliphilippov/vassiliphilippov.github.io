suspension system

1. NOUN

/ səˈspɛnʃən ˌsɪstəm / (AmE), / səˈspɛnʃən ˌsɪstəm / (BrE)
A mechanical arrangement of springs, shock absorbers, and other components that connects the wheels and axles to a vehicle's chassis, designed to absorb shock and ensure smooth ride quality.
vehicle system for absorbing road shocks

Examples

The car's suspension system was upgraded for a smoother ride over rough roads.
A faulty suspension system can cause uneven tire wear and poor handling.
Off-road vehicles need a robust suspension system to absorb shock from bumps and jumps.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shock absorber - a device for absorbing jolts and vibrations in a vehicle's suspension system
