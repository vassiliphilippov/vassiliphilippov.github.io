lacrimal gland

1. NOUN

/ ˈlækrɪməl ɡlænd / (AmE), / ˈlæk.rɪ.məl ɡlænd / (BrE)
a gland in the upper outer region of each eye that produces tears
tear-secreting gland in the eye
medical

Examples

The lacrimal gland is responsible for producing the tears that keep your eyes moist.
An infection of the lacrimal gland can cause swelling and discomfort above the eye.
Doctors examined her lacrimal gland to determine the cause of her excessive tearing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tear gland - another term for the lacrimal gland

Etymology

From Latin 'lacrima' (tear) + 'gland', from Latin 'glandula' (gland).
