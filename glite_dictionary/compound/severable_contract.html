severable contract

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛvərəbəl ˈkɑːntrækt / (AmE), / ˈsɛvərəbəl ˈkɒntrækt / (BrE)
A contract that can be divided into separate agreements so that a breach by one party of part of the contract does not void the entire agreement.
contract divisible into separate agreements
legal

Examples

The court determined that the severable contract allowed the remaining provisions to stay in force despite the breach.
Because the agreement was a severable contract, only the disputed section was affected by the violation.
Their business dealings relied on a severable contract to minimize risk if either party defaulted on part of the agreement.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: entire contract - a contract that must be performed in its entirety, not divisible into separate parts

Etymology

From Latin 'separare' (to separate) via 'severable' + 'contract'.
