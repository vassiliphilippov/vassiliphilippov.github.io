Hatteras Island

1. PROPN

/ ˈhæt̬ərəs ˈaɪlænd / (AmE), / ˈhætərəs ˈaɪlənd / (BrE)
a barrier island along the coast of North Carolina, United States, known for its beaches and role as part of the Outer Banks
barrier island off North Carolina coast

Examples

Many tourists visit Hatteras Island each year to enjoy its scenic beaches and historic lighthouse.
The residents of Hatteras Island prepared for the approaching hurricane by boarding up their homes.
Fishing is a popular activity on Hatteras Island thanks to its access to both the ocean and the sound.

Etymology

Named after the Hatteras, a Native American tribe formerly inhabiting the area.
