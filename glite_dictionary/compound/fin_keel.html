fin keel

1. NOUN

/ fɪn kil / (AmE), / fɪn kiːl / (BrE)
A metal plate or projection attached vertically to the bottom of a boat's hull, providing additional lateral stability and helping to prevent sideways drift.
vertical keel for stability on boats

Examples

The yacht's fin keel gives it exceptional stability in choppy waters.
Many modern sailboats use a fin keel instead of a full-length one for improved maneuverability.
He inspected the fin keel for any damage before launching the vessel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: keel - the main structural element running along the bottom of a boat or ship
