arithmetic operation

1. NOUN

/ əˈrɪθmətɪk ˌɑpəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌærɪθˈmetɪk ˌɒpəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
a mathematical calculation such as addition, subtraction, multiplication, or division, performed with numbers
mathematical calculation with numbers

Examples

Addition is the most basic arithmetic operation learned in elementary school.
When you use a calculator, each arithmetic operation is performed instantly.
Multiplication is a more advanced arithmetic operation than addition or subtraction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: addition - the operation of adding numbers
  • Synonym: subtraction - the operation of taking away one number from another
  • Synonym: multiplication - the operation of multiplying numbers
  • Synonym: division - the operation of dividing one number by another

Etymology

From arithmetic, from Greek arithmētikḗ (art of counting); operation from Latin operātiō (activity, work).
