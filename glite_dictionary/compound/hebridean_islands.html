Hebridean Islands

1. PROPN

/ ˌhɛb.rɪˈdi.ən ˈaɪ.ləndz / (AmE), / ˌhɛb.rɪˈdiː.ən ˈaɪ.ləndz / (BrE)
a group of more than 500 islands off the western coast of Scotland, known for their scenic landscapes and rich cultural history
islands off western Scotland

Examples

The Hebridean Islands are renowned for their dramatic coastlines and wildlife.
She spent her summer exploring the remote Hebridean Islands by ferry and bicycle.
Archaeological sites in the Hebridean Islands reveal a rich and ancient history.

Etymology

From the name 'Hebrides', ultimately from Old Norse 'Hebríðir'.
