egg-and-anchor

1. NOUN

/ ɛɡ ənd ˈæŋkɚ / (AmE), / ɛɡ ənd ˈæŋkə / (BrE)
a decorative molding pattern featuring alternating egg-shaped figures and anchor-like shapes, commonly used in classical architecture.
decorative molding with egg and anchor shapes

Examples

The antique mirror frame features an ornate egg-and-anchor molding along its border.
Ancient Greek temples sometimes display the egg-and-anchor motif in their friezes.
Restorers replaced the damaged section with a new egg-and-anchor trim to match the original design.
