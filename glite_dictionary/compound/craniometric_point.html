craniometric point

1. NOUN

/ ˌkreɪnioʊˈmɛtrɪk pɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˌkræniəˈmɛtrɪk pɔɪnt / (BrE)
a specific landmark on the skull used as a reference for taking craniometric measurements
skull landmark for measurements

Examples

The scientists measured several craniometric points to compare skull shapes among species.
Each craniometric point must be precisely identified to ensure accurate data.
One commonly used craniometric point is the nasion, located at the intersection of the frontal and nasal bones.

Etymology

From Greek kranion (skull) + metron (measure) + English 'point'.
