herring hog

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛrɪŋ hɑɡ / (AmE), / ˈhɛrɪŋ hɒɡ / (BrE)
a regional or colloquial name for the common porpoise found in the northern Atlantic and Pacific Oceans
colloquial name for a porpoise

Examples

Old fishermen would spot a herring hog and warn the others to watch their herring nets.
The herring hog is known for its playful leaps near the coast.
In some coastal communities, the herring hog was once believed to compete with humans for fish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: porpoise - a small toothed whale with a blunt snout, related to dolphins

Etymology

Likely from the animal's habit of feeding on herring and its hog-like appearance when surfacing.
