very softly

1. ADV

/ ˈvɛri ˈsɔftli / (AmE), / ˈvɛri ˈsɒftli / (BrE)
(in music) to be played at a very low volume; extremely quietly.
(music) at a very low volume

Examples

The pianist was instructed to play the final notes very softly to create a delicate ending.
In this passage, the violins enter very softly, barely audible to the audience.
The conductor gestured for the choir to sing very softly during the lullaby section.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: softly - at low volume
