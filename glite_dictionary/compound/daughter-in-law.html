daughter-in-law

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɔtər ɪn lɔ / (AmE), / ˈdɔːtə ɪn lɔː / (BrE)
the wife of someone's son
wife of one's son

Examples

My daughter-in-law is coming to visit us next weekend.
She welcomed her daughter-in-law into the family with open arms.
They bought a gift for their new daughter-in-law after the wedding.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: son-in-law - the husband of someone's daughter

Etymology

From 'daughter' + 'in-law', indicating relationship acquired by marriage, not by blood.
