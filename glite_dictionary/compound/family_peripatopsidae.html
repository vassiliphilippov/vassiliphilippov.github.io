family Peripatopsidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌfæməli pɛrəˌpætɑpˈsɪdiː / (AmE), / ˌfæmɪli pɛrəˌpætɒpˈsɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of velvet worms, comprising various species within the phylum Onychophora, found primarily in the Southern Hemisphere.
family of velvet worms (Onychophora)

Examples

The family Peripatopsidae includes several genera of velvet worms distributed across Australasia and South Africa.
Researchers are studying the morphological diversity within the family Peripatopsidae.
Unlike members of other velvet worm families, the family Peripatopsidae is mainly terrestrial and adapted to temperate forests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Onychophora - phylum of velvet worms

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'Peripatopsis' (the type genus) and '-idae' (a zoological family suffix).
