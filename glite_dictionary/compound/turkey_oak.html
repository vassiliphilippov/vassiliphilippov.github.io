turkey oak

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɝːki oʊk / (AmE), / ˈtɜːki əʊk / (BrE)
a large deciduous tree of central and southern Europe and Asia Minor, with lance-shaped leaves with spiked lobes
deciduous European oak tree

Examples

The leaves of the turkey oak are deeply lobed and quite distinctive.
A row of turkey oak trees lines the edge of the park.
The botanist identified the specimen as a mature turkey oak.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oak - a tree of the genus Quercus, bearing acorns and lobed leaves

Etymology

Named for its native range in Turkey and surrounding regions; 'oak' from Old English 'āc'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɝki oʊk / (AmE), / ˈtɜːki əʊk / (BrE)
a large deciduous tree of North America, with rounded crown, spreading branches, and narrow, deeply lobed leaves, valued for its wood; Quercus laevis.
species of North American oak tree

Examples

The turkey oak is common in sandy soils throughout the southeastern United States.
After the storm, branches from the large turkey oak were scattered across the yard.
Scientists studied the acorns produced by the turkey oak to understand local wildlife diets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oak - tree or shrub of the genus Quercus, bearing acorns as fruit

Etymology

The name refers to the resemblance of its leaves to those of the European Turkey oak (Quercus cerris).

3. NOUN

/ ˈtɝːki oʊk / (AmE), / ˈtɜːki əʊk / (BrE)
a small, slow-growing deciduous tree (Quercus laevis) of the southeastern United States, with bristle-tipped leaves resembling a turkey’s toes, commonly found in dry, sandy areas
deciduous tree of the southeastern US

Examples

The leaves of the turkey oak are deeply lobed, resembling a turkey's foot.
Turkey oak is often found in the dry, sandy soils of Georgia and Florida.
The wildlife reserve is home to several rare turkey oaks.

/ ˈtɝki ˌoʊk / (AmE), / ˈtɜːki ˌəʊk / (BrE)

Examples

We planted a turkey oak in our backyard to attract local wildlife.
