boatswain bird

1. NOUN

/ ˈboʊtsən bɜrd / (AmE), / ˈbəʊtsən bɜːd / (BrE)
a mostly white web-footed tropical seabird, often found far from land; commonly refers to a tropicbird
tropical seabird (tropicbird)

Examples

The graceful boatswain bird soared effortlessly above the ship's mast.
Researchers spotted a flock of boatswain birds nesting on the remote island.
The distinctive tail feathers make the boatswain bird easy to recognize at sea.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tropicbird - a tropical seabird with long tail streamers

Etymology

From 'boatswain,' referencing the bird’s call, reminiscent of a boatswain’s whistle.
