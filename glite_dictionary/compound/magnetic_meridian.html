magnetic meridian

1. NOUN

/ mæɡˈnɛtɪk məˈrɪdiən / (AmE), / mæɡˈnɛtɪk məˈrɪdiən / (BrE)
An imaginary line on the Earth's surface that joins the magnetic north and south poles and indicates the direction of the Earth's magnetic field at a particular location.
imaginary line through Earth's magnetic poles

Examples

The compass needle aligns itself with the magnetic meridian at that location.
Surveyors must consider the angle between the geographic and magnetic meridian when mapping land.
The magnetic meridian changes slightly over time due to shifts in Earth's magnetic field.
