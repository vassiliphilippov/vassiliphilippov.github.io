truck farming

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrʌk ˌfɑrmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtrʌk ˌfɑːmɪŋ / (BrE)
the practice of growing vegetables or other crops for sale at markets, rather than for personal consumption
growing vegetables for sale

Examples

The family made a living from truck farming and supplied vegetables to nearby towns.
Changes in climate have made truck farming more challenging in this region.
He learned the basics of truck farming from his grandfather's farm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: market gardening - small-scale production of fruits, vegetables, and flowers for sale

Etymology

From the obsolete use of 'truck' meaning 'barter, exchange of goods', from French 'troquer' (to barter).
