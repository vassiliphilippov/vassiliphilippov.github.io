master key

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæstɚ ki / (AmE), / ˈmɑːstə kiː / (BrE)
a key that is designed to open several different locks, especially in a building with many rooms or units
key that opens multiple locks

Examples

Only the manager has the master key to the building.
The janitor used a master key to unlock several doors during his rounds.
If you lose your master key, it poses a significant security risk.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: passkey - a key capable of opening multiple locks, especially for emergency access

2. NOUN

/ ˈmæstɚ kiː / (AmE), / ˈmɑːstə kiː / (BrE)
a key designed to open several different locks, especially within the same system
key that opens multiple locks

Examples

The hotel manager kept the master key in a secure drawer.
Only authorized personnel are allowed to use the master key.
After losing the master key, they had to replace every lock in the office.

Synset Examples

She used the master key to unlock all the doors.
The janitor carried a master key for the entire building.
They lost the master key and had to change all the locks.

Related Terms
