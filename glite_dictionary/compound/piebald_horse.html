piebald horse

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪˌbɔld hɔrs / (AmE), / ˈpaɪˌbɔːld hɔːs / (BrE)
A horse that has a coat with irregular patches of two different colors, typically black and white.
horse with black-and-white patches

Examples

The piebald horse stood out in the field with its unique coat.
She rode a piebald horse during the parade, drawing everyone's attention.
Among the group, only one piebald horse had such striking black and white markings.

Synset Examples

The piebald horse stood out in the field with its unique coat.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: skewbald horse - horse with irregular patches of white and any color other than black
  • Synonym: paint horse - breed of horse known for large patches of white and another color

Etymology

From 'piebald', from 'pie' (magpie, for its black-and-white coloring) and 'bald' (marked with white).
