Thames River

1. PROPN

/ tɛmz ˈrɪvɚ / (AmE), / tɛmz ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
the longest river in England, flowing eastward through London to the North Sea
major river in England

Examples

The Thames River is an iconic part of the London landscape.
Boats of all sizes travel along the Thames River every day.
Many historic events have taken place on the banks of the Thames River.

Etymology

Named after the Old English 'Temes', from Latin 'Tamesis', likely of Celtic origin.
