subscription right

1. NOUN

/ səbˈskrɪpʃən raɪt / (AmE), / səbˈskrɪpʃən raɪt / (BrE)
the right of a shareholder to purchase shares from a new issue of common stock before it is offered to the public
right to buy new shares before public
legal

Examples

Each existing shareholder exercised their subscription right to buy additional shares during the new issue.
The company granted a subscription right to shareholders, allowing them to purchase common stock at a set price.
By using his subscription right, he maintained his percentage of ownership in the firm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: preemptive right - the right of existing shareholders to buy new shares before the public
