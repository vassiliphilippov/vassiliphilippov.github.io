sodium hydroxide

1. NOUN

/ ˌsoʊdiəm haɪˈdrɑksaɪd / (AmE), / ˌsəʊdiəm haɪˈdrɒksaɪd / (BrE)
a white, caustic, strongly alkaline compound (NaOH) used in making soap, paper, and many industrial chemicals
caustic alkaline compound (NaOH) used in industry
medical

Examples

When handling sodium hydroxide, always wear protective gloves and goggles.
The factory uses large amounts of sodium hydroxide to manufacture paper.
Sodium hydroxide can cause severe chemical burns if it comes into contact with skin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caustic soda - another name for sodium hydroxide, especially in industrial contexts

Etymology

From 'sodium' (named after soda, from Medieval Latin 'sodanum') and 'hydroxide' (hydro- + oxide).
