stag's-horn coral

1. NOUN

/ stæɡz hɔrn ˈkɔɹ.əl / (AmE), / stæɡz hɔːn ˈkɒr.əl / (BrE)
a large branching coral whose shape resembles the antlers of a stag, commonly found in shallow tropical reefs
a branching coral resembling stag antlers

Examples

Divers marveled at the size of the stag's-horn coral in the shallow lagoon.
Many reef fish find shelter among the branches of stag's-horn coral.
Rising sea temperatures pose a threat to stag's-horn coral populations worldwide.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elkhorn coral - a coral species with thick, antler-like branches
