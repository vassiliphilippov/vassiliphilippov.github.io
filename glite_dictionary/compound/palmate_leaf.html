palmate leaf

1. NOUN

/ ˈpæl.meɪt liːf / (AmE), / ˈpæl.meɪt liːf / (BrE)
A leaf with lobes or leaflets radiating from a single point, resembling the shape of an open hand.
leaf with lobes radiating from one point

Examples

The horse chestnut has a distinctive palmate leaf with several leaflets spreading from a single point.
Maple trees are often recognized by their palmate leaves that resemble hands with outstretched fingers.
The botanist explained that a palmate leaf can help plants capture more sunlight effectively.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: pinnate leaf - a leaf with leaflets arranged on either side of a central axis

Etymology

From Latin 'palma' (palm of the hand), referring to its hand-like shape.
