electronics intelligence

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌlɛkˈtrɑnɪks ɪnˈtɛlədʒəns / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛkˈtrɒnɪks ɪnˈtelɪdʒəns / (BrE)
technical intelligence obtained by analyzing electromagnetic emissions from foreign sources, excluding radioactive sources.
gathering intelligence from electromagnetic signals

Examples

The military unit specializes in electronics intelligence to monitor potential threats.
Advancements in technology have made electronics intelligence more effective at detecting enemy communications.
Training in electronics intelligence involves learning to interpret various electromagnetic signals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: signals intelligence - intelligence gathered from interception of signals
