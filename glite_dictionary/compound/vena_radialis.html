vena radialis

1. NOUN

/ ˌviːnə rəˈdeɪlɪs / (AmE), / ˌviːnə rəˈdeɪlɪs / (BrE)
a superficial vein running up the radial side of the forearm, which joins the ulnar veins to form the brachial vein
superficial vein of the radial forearm
medical

Examples

The vena radialis is often used as a reference point in forearm surgeries.
Blood from the hand drains through both the ulnar vein and the vena radialis.
Damage to the vena radialis may cause complications in blood flow from the lower arm.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: brachial vein - the vein formed by the union of the radial and ulnar veins
  • Synonym: ulnar vein - one of the veins of the forearm that joins with the radial vein

Etymology

From Latin *vena* (vein) and *radialis* (of the radius).
