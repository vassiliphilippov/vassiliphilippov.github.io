day of remembrance

1. NOUN

/ deɪ əv rɪˈmɛmbɚns / (AmE), / deɪ əv rɪˈmɛmbərəns / (BrE)
a day set aside to remember and honor a past event, usually its anniversary or a commemorative occasion
commemorative day for a past event

Examples

Each year, the country observes a day of remembrance for those who lost their lives in the war.
June 6th has become a significant day of remembrance for many communities worldwide.
Schools held a special assembly on the day of remembrance to honor the victims of the tragedy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anniversary - the date on which an event occurred in a previous year
