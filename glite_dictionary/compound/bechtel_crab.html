Bechtel crab

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛk.təl kræb / (AmE), / ˈbɛk.təl kræb / (BrE)
A cultivated variety of crabapple tree derived from the Iowa crab, known for its large double pink blossoms.
cultivated crabapple tree with double pink flowers

Examples

A Bechtel crab in full bloom adds a splash of color to the garden each spring.
They planted a Bechtel crab near the entrance to attract bees and butterflies.
The nursery specializes in rare ornamental trees, including the Bechtel crab.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crabapple - a small, sour apple; also the tree that bears it

Etymology

Named after E. A. Bechtel, a 19th-century American nurseryman.
