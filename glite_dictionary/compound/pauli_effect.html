Pauli effect

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaʊli ɪˌfɛkt / (AmE), / ˈpaʊli ɪˌfɛkt / (BrE)
A humorous phenomenon in which the mere presence of a particular person is said to cause unexpected technical failures or malfunctions.
joking phenomenon where someone's presence causes failures

Examples

Whenever the projector broke during meetings, colleagues would laugh and blame the Pauli effect.
Some scientists jokingly attribute frequent lab mishaps to the Pauli effect.
The Pauli effect was invoked after several computers crashed during his visit.

Synset Examples

The Pauli effect struck again when he lost his lucky pen.
He joked about the Pauli effect causing chaos at every event.

Etymology

Named after Austrian physicist Wolfgang Pauli, believed by colleagues to inadvertently cause experiments to fail merely by being present.
