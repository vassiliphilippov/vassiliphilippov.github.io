transient ischemic attack

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrænziənt ɪˈskiːmɪk əˈtæk / (AmE), / ˈtrænziənt ɪˈsiːmɪk əˈtæk / (BrE)
A brief episode of neurological dysfunction caused by a temporary interruption in the blood supply to the brain, usually without lasting damage.
brief episode of reduced brain blood supply
medical

Examples

After her transient ischemic attack, the doctors monitored her closely for signs of a stroke.
A transient ischemic attack can serve as a warning sign of more serious circulatory problems.
The patient described vision loss and dizziness during his transient ischemic attack.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: stroke - an interruption of blood supply to the brain causing lasting damage

Etymology

From Latin 'transiens' (passing), Greek 'ischaimos' (stanching blood), and English 'attack'.
