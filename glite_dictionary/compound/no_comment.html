no comment

1. NOUN

/ noʊ ˈkɑːmɛnt / (AmE), / nəʊ ˈkɒmɛnt / (BrE)
used to say that you do not wish to answer questions or provide information, especially to journalists or the public
refusal to answer questions or give information

Examples

When reporters asked about the allegations, the spokesperson replied simply, no comment.
The company gave no comment when questioned on the merger rumors.
During the interview, the actor responded with no comment to several personal questions.

Synset Examples

The politician issued a no comment on the scandal.
She offered no comment regarding the rumors.
When asked about the incident, he said no comment.
