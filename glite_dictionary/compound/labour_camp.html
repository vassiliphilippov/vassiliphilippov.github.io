labour camp

1. NOUN

/ ˈleɪbɚ kæmp / (AmE), / ˈleɪbə kæmp / (BrE)
a place where prisoners, especially political prisoners, are confined and forced to perform hard physical labor as punishment or for political repression
prison where prisoners are forced to work

Examples

Many political dissidents were sent to a labour camp during the regime’s rule.
Survivors of the labour camp spoke about the harsh conditions and relentless work.
The memoir describes her years of forced labor in a Siberian labour camp.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: concentration camp - a place where large numbers of people are detained, especially for forced labor or extermination
  • Synonym: prison - a building where people are legally held as punishment for crimes

Etymology

From 'labour' (British spelling of 'labor') + 'camp'. The concept became widely known in English through reference to Soviet and Nazi forced labor camps.
