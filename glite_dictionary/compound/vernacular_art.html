vernacular art

1. NOUN

/ vərˈnækjələr ɑrt / (AmE), / vəˈnakjʊlə ɑːt / (BrE)
art created by self-taught or untrained artists, typically reflecting local traditions and not formally recognized as art by its creators
art by self-taught, untrained creators

Examples

The museum's new exhibit features stunning examples of vernacular art from rural communities.
Many pieces of vernacular art use found materials and everyday objects in creative ways.
Collectors are growing more interested in vernacular art because of its authenticity and unique perspective.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: outsider art - art by self-taught artists outside the mainstream art scene

Etymology

From 'vernacular', meaning native or local, and 'art'; 'vernacular' from Latin 'vernaculus' (native, domestic).
