grammar school

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡræmər skuːl / (AmE), / ˈɡræmə skuːl / (BrE)
a secondary school, especially formerly, that emphasized Latin and Greek in preparation for university entry
secondary school focusing on classical languages
british

Examples

He attended a grammar school where the curriculum focused on Latin and Greek.
In the past, only students who passed an entrance exam could enter a grammar school.
Many famous scholars received their education at a grammar school in their youth.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: comprehensive school - a British secondary school not based on academic selection

Etymology

From Latin 'grammatica schola', referring to schools teaching grammar, especially Latin; adopted in English since the Middle Ages.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡræm.ɚ skuːl / (AmE), / ˈɡræm.ə skuːl / (BrE)
A school for young children, typically covering the elementary grades; in the US, usually the first 6 or 8 grades.
elementary school for young children

Examples

Her children attended the local grammar school until they moved to the city.
The old brick building served as a grammar school for nearly one hundred years.
He began teaching at a grammar school right after finishing college.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elementary school - school for the first years of a child's education (US)
  • Synonym: primary school - school for young children (mainly UK/Australia)

Etymology

From 'grammar' (referring to the study of Latin grammar) + 'school'; originally in Britain, schools that taught classical languages.
