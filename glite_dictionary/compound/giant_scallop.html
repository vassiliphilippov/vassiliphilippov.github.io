giant scallop

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒaɪənt ˈskæləp / (AmE), / ˈdʒaɪənt ˈskæl.əp / (BrE)
a large edible saltwater clam found in deep waters along the Atlantic coast of North America
large edible Atlantic coast scallop

Examples

The giant scallop is prized for its sweet and tender meat among seafood lovers.
Fishermen in Nova Scotia harvest giant scallops from the cold Atlantic waters every year.
A single giant scallop shell can measure more than six inches across.
