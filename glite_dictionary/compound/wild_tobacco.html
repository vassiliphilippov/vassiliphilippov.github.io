wild tobacco

1. NOUN

/ waɪld təˈbækoʊ / (AmE), / waɪld təˈbækəʊ / (BrE)
a species of tobacco plant native to South America and Mexico, often distinguished from cultivated tobacco
species of tobacco plant from South America and Mexico

Examples

Wild tobacco grows abundantly in the arid regions of northern Mexico.
Researchers studied wild tobacco to understand its resistance to certain pests.
Unlike its cultivated relative, wild tobacco is rarely used for commercial tobacco products.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tobacco - plant cultivated for its leaves, processed for smoking or chewing
