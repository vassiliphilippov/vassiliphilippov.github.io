modern-day Frankenstein

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɑdərn deɪ ˌfrænkənˈstaɪn / (AmE), / ˌmɒdən deɪ ˌfræŋkənˈstaɪn / (BrE)
a person who creates or experiments with life, especially in ways seen as audacious, risky, or morally questionable
one who creates or experiments with life (figurative)

Examples

Some critics have called the controversial genetic engineer a modern-day Frankenstein.
He was accused of playing God, like a modern-day Frankenstein in the laboratory.
The film's villain is portrayed as a modern-day Frankenstein, obsessed with creating new forms of life.

Synset Examples

She felt like a modern-day Frankenstein in the lab.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Frankenstein - the creator in Mary Shelley's novel, or something monstrous out of control

Etymology

Alludes to Dr. Frankenstein, the scientist in Mary Shelley's 1818 novel 'Frankenstein.'
