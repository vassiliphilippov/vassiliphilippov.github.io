flight path

1. NOUN

/ ˈflaɪt ˌpæθ / (AmE), / ˈflaɪt ˌpɑːθ / (BrE)
the route or trajectory followed by an aircraft, rocket, or projectile as it moves through the air
trajectory of an aircraft or projectile in the air

Examples

The pilot adjusted the flight path to avoid turbulence over the mountains.
Mission control monitored the rocket's flight path for any unexpected changes.
Birds sometimes cross the flight path of approaching airplanes, posing a risk to aviation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trajectory - the path followed by a projectile moving under given forces

2. NOUN

/ ˈflaɪt ˌpæθ / (AmE), / ˈflaɪt ˌpɑːθ / (BrE)
the route that an aircraft follows when flying from one place to another, especially one that is officially designated
route followed by an aircraft in flight

Examples

The storm forced the pilot to alter the plane's flight path for safety.
Air traffic controllers monitor each aircraft's flight path to avoid collisions.
Birds can sometimes interfere with a jet's flight path near the airport.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: route - a way or course taken in getting from a starting point to a destination
