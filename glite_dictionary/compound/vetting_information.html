vetting information

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɛtɪŋ ˌɪnfərˈmeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈvɛtɪŋ ˌɪnfəˈmeɪʃən / (BrE)
The process of carefully examining information to ensure its accuracy and reliability.
checking information for accuracy

Examples

The research team is responsible for vetting information before it is included in the final report.
Editors spend a great deal of time vetting information to avoid publishing errors.
She made it clear that vetting information was crucial for maintaining trust with their clients.

Synset Examples

She spent hours vetting all the information for accuracy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to verify - to make sure or demonstrate that something is true or accurate
  • Antonym: misinformation - false or inaccurate information

Etymology

From the verb 'vet', originally shortened from 'veterinarian', meaning to scrutinize or examine closely, combined with 'information'.
