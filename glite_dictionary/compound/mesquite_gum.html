mesquite gum

1. NOUN

/ mɛˈskit ɡʌm / (AmE), / mɛˈskiːt ɡʌm / (BrE)
a natural gum obtained from the pods of mesquite trees, similar to gum arabic and used in food and industrial products
gum from mesquite tree pods

Examples

The thick mesquite gum was used by indigenous peoples as a traditional sweetener.
Scientists studied the properties of mesquite gum as a potential food stabilizer.
Historically, traders collected mesquite gum for use in making adhesives and inks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gum arabic - a natural gum from Acacia trees; widely used as a stabilizer in food

Etymology

From 'mesquite' (from Spanish 'mezquite', from Nahuatl 'mizquitl') + 'gum'.
