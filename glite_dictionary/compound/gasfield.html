gasfield

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡæsˌfiːld / (AmE), / ˈɡasˌfiːld / (BrE)
an area of land where large amounts of natural gas are found underground and can be extracted
region with underground natural gas

Examples

The company invested millions to develop the new gasfield near the coast.
Scientists estimate that the gasfield holds enough reserves for twenty years of production.
Local communities benefited economically after the discovery of a major gasfield in the region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oilfield - an area where crude oil is extracted from underground
