Antonine Wall

1. NOUN

/ æn.toʊˌnaɪn ˈwɑl / (AmE), / æn.təˌnaɪn ˈwɔːl / (BrE)
a Roman defensive wall built around 140 AD, spanning approximately 37 miles across central Scotland, marking the northern frontier of Roman Britain.
Roman wall in Scotland, ancient frontier

Examples

The Antonine Wall was constructed by the Romans to protect their northern frontier in Britain.
Archaeologists have uncovered several forts along the route of the Antonine Wall.
Many tourists visit the remains of the Antonine Wall each year to learn about Roman history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hadrian's Wall - an earlier Roman defensive wall in northern England

Etymology

Named after the Roman emperor Antoninus Pius, under whose reign the wall was constructed.
