alveolar resorption

1. NOUN

/ ælˌviːələr rɪˈzɔrpʃən / (AmE), / ælˌviːələ rɪˈzɔːpʃən / (BrE)
the loss or breakdown of the bone surrounding the sockets of the teeth, often occurring after tooth loss or due to disease
breakdown of bone around tooth sockets
medical

Examples

After tooth extraction, alveolar resorption can make it difficult to fit dental implants.
Periodontal disease often leads to alveolar resorption if left untreated.
Dentists monitor patients for signs of alveolar resorption during routine check-ups.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bone loss - loss of bone tissue
  • Degree Lower: alveolar bone - the bone that contains the tooth sockets

Etymology

From Latin 'alveolus' (small cavity) and 'resorptio' (process of being absorbed again).
