giro account

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒaɪroʊ əˌkaʊnt / (AmE), / ˈdʒɪərəʊ əˌkaʊnt / (BrE)
an account, typically at a post office or financial institution, used for making payments and deposits as with a bank account, especially in some European countries.
account at post office or bank for payments

Examples

She opened a giro account at the post office for her everyday transactions.
Money is transferred directly into his giro account every month.
Many people in Europe use a giro account instead of a traditional bank account.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bank account - an arrangement made with a bank whereby one may deposit and withdraw money

Etymology

From Italian 'giro' (turn, circulation), used in European banking terminology.
