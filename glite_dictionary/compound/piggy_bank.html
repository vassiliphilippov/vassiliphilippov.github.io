piggy bank

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪɡi bæŋk / (AmE), / ˈpɪɡi baŋk / (BrE)
a container, often shaped like a pig, used by children for saving coins
a child's coin bank, often pig-shaped

Examples

She dropped her spare change into her piggy bank every night before bed.
After a year of saving, his piggy bank was finally full.
The pink piggy bank on Emma's shelf was a gift from her grandmother.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coin bank - a container for saving coins
  • Degree Higher: safe - a strong container for storing money or valuables

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɪɡi bæŋk / (AmE), / ˈpɪɡi baŋk / (BrE)
A small container, often shaped like a pig, used by children for saving coins and money.
container for saving money (often pig-shaped)

Examples

After a year of saving, Tom's piggy bank was finally filled to the top with coins.
Sarah shook her piggy bank to see how much spare change it still held.
When he needed money for a comic book, he decided to break open his piggy bank.

Synset Examples

He brought his piggy to the bank.
I broke my piggy bank to buy a new game.
She filled her piggy bank with spare change.
The children loved their colorful piggy banks.
He counted his savings in the piggy bank.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: money box - a container for saving coins, often with a slot at the top
  • Degree Higher: safe - a strong container for valuables like money and jewelry

Etymology

From 'pig' (the animal) and 'bank' (repository for money), with the pig shape becoming popular in the 19th century.
