Faroe Islands

1. PROPN

/ ˈfɛroʊ ˈaɪləndz / (AmE), / ˈfɛərəʊ ˈaɪləndz / (BrE)
a group of volcanic islands in the North Atlantic Ocean, situated between Iceland and the Shetland Islands, forming a self-governing territory within the Kingdom of Denmark
North Atlantic island group, Danish territory

Examples

The Faroe Islands are famous for their dramatic cliffs and sweeping ocean views.
He spent a summer studying seabirds in the Faroe Islands.
Fishing is a major industry in the Faroe Islands.

Etymology

From Danish Færøerne, meaning 'sheep islands'.

2. PROPN

/ ˈfɛroʊ ˈaɪ.ləndz / (AmE), / ˈfeərəʊ ˈaɪ.ləndz / (BrE)
a group of self-governing islands in the North Atlantic, officially part of the Kingdom of Denmark
autonomous North Atlantic islands; part of Denmark

Examples

The Faroe Islands are known for their dramatic landscapes and rugged cliffs.
She plans to visit the Faroe Islands during her trip to Scandinavia next summer.
The fishing industry is a major part of the economy in the Faroe Islands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Denmark - country in northern Europe; sovereign state over the Faroe Islands

Etymology

From Faroese 'Føroyar' (Sheep Islands), related to Old Norse 'Færeyjar.'
