molal concentration

1. NOUN

/ moʊˈlæl kənˌsɛn.treɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / məʊˈlæl kənˌsɛn.treɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the concentration of a solution measured as the number of moles of solute per kilogram of solvent
solution concentration in moles per kg solvent
medical

Examples

The molal concentration of the solution was used to calculate the boiling point elevation.
Adjust the molal concentration to 1 mol/kg for accurate comparison between solutions.
In chemistry, molal concentration is often preferred over molarity for temperature-dependent studies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: molarity - concentration measured in moles per liter of solution

Etymology

From 'molal' (relating to moles, from Latin 'moles' meaning mass) and 'concentration'.
