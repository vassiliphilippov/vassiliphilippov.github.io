conservation of parity

1. NOUN

/ kənˌsɝːˈveɪʃən əv ˈpær.ə.ti / (AmE), / kənˌsɜːˈveɪʃən əv ˈpær.ə.ti / (BrE)
(Physics) The principle that the laws of physics remain unchanged if the spatial coordinates are inverted, so physical processes behave the same in a left-handed or right-handed coordinate system.
physics: symmetry principle in physical laws

Examples

The discovery that the conservation of parity is violated in weak interactions was a major breakthrough in physics.
For many years, physicists believed in the universal conservation of parity until experiments proved otherwise.
The conservation of parity holds in electromagnetic interactions but not in certain radioactive decays.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parity - symmetry in physical systems; equal measure or status
  • Antonym: parity violation - the phenomenon where parity conservation does not hold, especially in weak interactions
