Colorado River hemp

1. NOUN

/ kəˌlɑːrɑːdoʊ ˈrɪvər hɛmp / (AmE), / kəˌlɒrədəʊ ˈrɪvə hɛmp / (BrE)
A tall annual plant native to the southwestern United States, widely grown as green manure and formerly used for its strong bast fiber by Indigenous peoples.
tall annual plant native to southwestern US

Examples

The farmers planted Colorado River hemp as a cover crop to improve the soil.
Historically, Colorado River hemp was valued for its strong fibers used in making cordage.
You can spot Colorado River hemp growing along riverbanks in the southwestern United States.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hemp - plant used for its fiber, seeds, or oil
