family Corydalidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli kɔɹiˌdæləˌdi / (AmE), / ˈfamɪli kɒrɪˌdalɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of large aquatic insects in the order Megaloptera, commonly known as dobsonflies and fishflies.
family of insects (dobsonflies, fishflies)

Examples

The family Corydalidae includes some of the largest aquatic insects in North America.
Entomologists often study larvae from the family Corydalidae to monitor freshwater quality.
Species in the family Corydalidae are important predators in stream ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dobsonfly - a large aquatic insect of the family Corydalidae

Etymology

From Corydalus, the type genus, plus Latin -idae (indicating an animal family).
