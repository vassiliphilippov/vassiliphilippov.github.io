wire recorder

1. NOUN

/ ˈwaɪɚ rɪˌkoʊrdɚ / (AmE), / ˈwaɪə rɪˌkɔːdə / (BrE)
an early type of magnetic audio recorder that used thin steel wire to record and play back sound
early magnetic audio recorder using wire

Examples

The museum's audio section displays a rare wire recorder from the 1940s.
Before tape recorders became common, journalists sometimes used a wire recorder to capture interviews.
He restored an old wire recorder and played back his grandfather's recordings.
