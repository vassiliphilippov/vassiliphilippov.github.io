shoe-leather effort

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃuˌlɛðɚ ˈɛfɚt / (AmE), / ˈʃuːˌlɛðə ˈɛfət / (BrE)
Hard and persistent work, especially involving significant physical activity such as walking or manual labor.
hard work involving physical effort

Examples

The volunteers' shoe-leather effort helped deliver supplies to every house in the village.
It took a lot of shoe-leather effort for the journalist to track down all the witnesses.
Political canvassing still relies on old-fashioned shoe-leather effort to reach undecided voters.

Synset Examples

He put in shoe-leather efforts to gather signatures.
The campaign required a lot of shoe-leather work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hard work - activity involving much effort and perseverance

Etymology

From the idea of wearing out shoe leather by extensive walking or laborious effort.
