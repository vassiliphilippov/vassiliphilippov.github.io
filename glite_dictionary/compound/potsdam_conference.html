Potsdam Conference

1. PROPN

/ ˈpɑts.dæm ˈkɑn.fɚ.əns / (AmE), / ˈpɒts.dæm ˈkɒn.fər.əns / (BrE)
a meeting held in Potsdam in July–August 1945 where the leaders of the United States, the Soviet Union, and the United Kingdom planned the administration of postwar Germany and Poland
conference of Allied leaders in 1945

Examples

The Potsdam Conference marked a pivotal moment in shaping postwar Europe.
Discussions at the Potsdam Conference determined the fate of Germany after World War II.
Harry Truman represented the United States at the Potsdam Conference in 1945.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Yalta Conference - a meeting of Allied leaders in February 1945 to discuss postwar reorganization of Europe

Etymology

Named after Potsdam, Germany, where the conference took place.
