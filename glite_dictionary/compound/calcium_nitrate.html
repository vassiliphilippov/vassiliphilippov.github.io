calcium nitrate

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæl.si.əm ˈnaɪ.treɪt / (AmE), / ˈkalsɪəm ˈnʌɪtreɪt / (BrE)
a white, deliquescent salt, soluble in water, used as a fertilizer and in various industrial processes
a white, water-soluble chemical salt

Examples

The farmer applied calcium nitrate to his crops to improve their growth.
Due to its high solubility, calcium nitrate is often chosen for hydroponic systems.
Laboratory technicians stored the calcium nitrate in airtight containers to prevent it from absorbing moisture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: salt - a chemical compound formed from the reaction of an acid and a base

Etymology

From Latin *calx* (lime) and modern Latin *nitras* (nitrate).
