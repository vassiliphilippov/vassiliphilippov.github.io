white pine

1. NOUN

/ waɪt paɪn / (AmE), / waɪt paɪn / (BrE)
the soft, pale wood obtained from white pine trees, used especially in construction and carpentry
wood from white pine trees

Examples

The shelves were crafted from white pine for a smooth, clean finish.
Builders often choose white pine because it is easy to work with and lightweight.
Aromatic white pine is valued for both its beauty and versatility in woodworking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: white pine - a North American pine tree with soft, pale wood

2. NOUN

/ waɪt paɪn / (AmE), / waɪt paɪn / (BrE)
Any of several pine trees with five needles per cluster, pale wood, and smooth, light grey bark when young; especially the eastern white pine (Pinus strobus).
a group of pale-wooded, five-needled pine trees

Examples

The tall white pine dominated the landscape with its graceful shape and silvery-grey bark.
Builders often prefer white pine for its soft, workable wood in interior projects.
Several white pines grew along the lake's edge, providing shade and habitat for wildlife.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eastern white pine - a species of white pine native to eastern North America (Pinus strobus)
  • Synonym: western white pine - a species of white pine native to western North America (Pinus monticola)
