genus Grias

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈɡraɪ.əs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈɡraɪ.əs / (BrE)
A genus of tropical American trees in the family Lecythidaceae, which includes the anchovy pear tree.
genus of tropical American trees

Examples

Botanists have identified several species within the genus Grias in the Amazon rainforest.
The genus Grias includes the anchovy pear, a tree valued for its edible fruit.
Several members of the genus Grias are cultivated for ornamental and ecological purposes.

Etymology

From New Latin 'Grias', the genus name in botanical nomenclature.
