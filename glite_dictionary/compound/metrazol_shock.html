metrazol shock

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛtrəzɑl ʃɑk / (AmE), / ˈmɛtrəzɒl ʃɒk / (BrE)
a medical procedure in which Metrazol is administered in sufficient quantity to induce convulsions and coma, formerly used in psychiatric treatment
convulsive therapy using Metrazol
medical

Examples

The patient underwent metrazol shock as part of his treatment for severe depression.
Historical records show that metrazol shock was once a common intervention in psychiatric hospitals.
Many doctors abandoned metrazol shock in favor of safer and more effective therapies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: electroconvulsive therapy - a psychiatric treatment in which seizures are electrically induced
  • Synonym: insulin shock therapy - a psychiatric treatment using insulin to induce coma or convulsions

Etymology

From Metrazol (a brand name for pentylenetetrazol) and shock (in reference to convulsive shock therapy).
