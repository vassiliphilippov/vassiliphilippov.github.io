maturity-onset diabetes

1. NOUN

/ məˈtʃʊr.ə.ti ˈɒn.sɛt daɪˈæb.i.tiz / (AmE), / məˈtʃʊə.rɪ.ti ˈɒn.sɛt daɪˈiː.biː.tiːz / (BrE)
A mild form of diabetes mellitus that develops gradually in adults, often controlled by diet and oral medication rather than insulin injections.
adult-onset form of diabetes mellitus
medical

Examples

Doctors diagnosed her with maturity-onset diabetes after she reported persistent fatigue and increased thirst.
Obesity is a major risk factor in developing maturity-onset diabetes in later life.
Many cases of maturity-onset diabetes can be managed with dietary changes and oral medications.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: type 2 diabetes - common form of diabetes mellitus developing typically in adults
