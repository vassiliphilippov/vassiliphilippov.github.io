genus Paranthropus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs pəˈrænθrəpəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs pəˈrænθrəpəs / (BrE)
a former genus of extinct hominins, including Australopithecus robustus, used in past classifications of early human ancestors
extinct hominin genus (Australopithecus robustus)

Examples

Fossils classified as genus Paranthropus provide important evidence for understanding human evolution.
Many paleoanthropologists now prefer to refer to Australopithecus robustus rather than genus Paranthropus.
Research suggests that members of the genus Paranthropus had powerful jaws adapted for chewing tough vegetation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Australopithecus robustus - an extinct hominin species formerly included in Paranthropus

Etymology

From Greek 'para' (beside) + 'anthrōpos' (man), indicating a genus related to humans.
