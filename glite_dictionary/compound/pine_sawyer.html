pine sawyer

1. NOUN

/ paɪn ˈsɔɪ.ər / (AmE), / paɪn ˈsɔː.jə / (BrE)
a large beetle, especially of the genus Monochamus, whose larvae bore holes in pine trees
beetle whose larvae bore into pine trees

Examples

The forest service warned of an infestation of pine sawyers damaging the local pine stands.
After examining the tree, she found the telltale holes left by pine sawyer larvae.
The wood from infested trees showed extensive tunneling by pine sawyers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: longhorn beetle - a beetle with long antennae, family Cerambycidae

Etymology

From 'pine' (the tree) + 'sawyer' (a term for a type of wood-boring beetle).
