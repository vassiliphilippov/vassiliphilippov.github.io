aggressive behavior

1. NOUN

/ əˈɡrɛsɪv bəˈheɪvjər / (AmE), / əˈɡrɛsɪv bɪˈheɪvjə / (BrE)
hostile or violent actions intended to harm or assert dominance over others
hostile or violent actions

Examples

Some children may display aggressive behavior when they feel threatened or frustrated.
The teacher addressed the student's aggressive behavior during class to maintain a safe environment.
Therapists work with patients to manage or reduce aggressive behavior in social situations.

Synset Examples

Castration can help reduce aggressive behavior in dogs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hostility - hostile or antagonistic behavior
  • Antonym: passive behavior - behavior that is non-confrontational and lacks aggression
