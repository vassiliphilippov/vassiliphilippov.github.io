common spoonbill

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmən ˈspunˌbɪl / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən ˈspuːnbɪl / (BrE)
a large white wading bird (Platalea leucorodia) with a distinctive spoon-shaped bill, found in southern Eurasia and northeastern Africa
large white wading bird with a spoon-shaped bill

Examples

We spotted a common spoonbill feeding in the shallow waters of the wetland reserve.
The common spoonbill is easily identified by its white plumage and unusual bill shape.
Conservationists are monitoring the breeding sites of the common spoonbill in the region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spoonbill - any bird with a long, flat, spoon-shaped bill
