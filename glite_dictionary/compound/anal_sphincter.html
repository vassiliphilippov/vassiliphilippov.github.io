anal sphincter

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪnəl ˈsfɪŋktər / (AmE), / ˈeɪnəl ˈsfɪŋktə / (BrE)
the ring of muscle surrounding and controlling the opening of the anus
ring-shaped muscle of the anus
medical

Examples

Damage to the anal sphincter can cause problems with bowel control.
Exercises are sometimes prescribed to strengthen the anal sphincter after surgery.
The anal sphincter contracts involuntarily to prevent accidental loss of stool.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sphincter - a ring of muscle that constricts a passage or closes a natural orifice

Etymology

From Latin 'anus' (ring, anus) and Greek 'sphinktēr' (binder, tightener).
