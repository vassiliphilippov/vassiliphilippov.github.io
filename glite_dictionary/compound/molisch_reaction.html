Molisch reaction

1. NOUN

/ ˈmoʊlɪʃ riˈækʃən / (AmE), / ˈmɒlɪʃ rɪˈakʃən / (BrE)
a chemical test used to detect the presence of carbohydrates in a solution, producing a violet ring when carbohydrates react with alpha-naphthol and sulfuric acid
biochemical test for carbohydrates
medical

Examples

The Molisch reaction is commonly used in laboratories to identify the presence of carbohydrates in food samples.
A positive Molisch reaction resulted in the appearance of a violet ring in the test tube.
Chemistry students performed the Molisch reaction as part of their experiment on biomolecule detection.

Etymology

Named after Austrian botanist Hans Molisch (1856–1937), who developed the test.
