Russo-Japanese War

1. NOUN

/ ˌruːsoʊ dʒəˈpæniz wɔɹ / (AmE), / ˌrʊsəʊ dʒəˈpæniːz wɔː / (BrE)
the war fought between Russia and Japan from 1904 to 1905, resulting in a Japanese victory and giving Japan control over Korea and Manchuria
war between Russia and Japan (1904–05)

Examples

The Russo-Japanese War marked the first major military victory of an Asian power over a European one in modern history.
During the Russo-Japanese War, Japan gained international recognition for its growing military strength.
Many historians consider the outcome of the Russo-Japanese War a turning point in early 20th-century East Asian politics.

Etymology

From the names of the combatant nations, Russia and Japan, combined with 'war'.
