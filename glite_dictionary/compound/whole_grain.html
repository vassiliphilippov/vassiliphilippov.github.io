whole grain

1. NOUN

/ ˈhoʊl ɡreɪn / (AmE), / ˈhəʊl ɡreɪn / (BrE)
a grain that contains all its naturally occurring parts—the bran, germ, and endosperm—having not been refined or milled to remove any of these components
grain with all seed parts intact

Examples

Eating whole grain bread is a healthier option than white bread.
The cereal is made with 100% whole grain oats.
Nutritionists recommend including more whole grain foods in your daily diet.

Synset Examples

Whole grains are a great source of fiber.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: refined grain - grain that has been milled to remove the bran and germ
