firing mechanism

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪɹɪŋ ˈmɛk.əˌnɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈfaɪə.rɪŋ ˈmɛk.ə.nɪz.əm / (BrE)
the part of a firearm that initiates the discharge of a projectile by igniting the charge
device that ignites a firearm's charge

Examples

The revolver's firing mechanism failed, preventing the gun from discharging.
Engineers improved the firing mechanism to make the weapon more reliable.
Careful maintenance of the firing mechanism is essential for safe firearm operation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trigger - part used to activate a firearm's firing mechanism
