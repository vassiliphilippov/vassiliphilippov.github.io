Korean Strait

1. NOUN

/ kəˈriːən streɪt / (AmE), / kəˈriːən streɪt / (BrE)
the narrow body of water between Korea and Japan, connecting the East China Sea and the Sea of Japan
strait between Korea and Japan

Examples

Many ships pass through the Korean Strait on their way to ports in East Asia.
The Korean Strait is a vital route for maritime trade between Japan and Korea.
The waters of the Korean Strait are known for their strong currents.
