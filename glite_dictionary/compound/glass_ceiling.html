glass ceiling

1. NOUN

/ ɡlæs ˈsiːlɪŋ / (AmE), / ɡlɑːs ˈsiːlɪŋ / (BrE)
an unacknowledged barrier within an organization that prevents minorities and women from advancing to upper-level positions, despite their qualifications or achievements
invisible barrier to advancement for minorities and women

Examples

Despite her qualifications, she hit a glass ceiling in the company and couldn't rise any higher.
The organization is working to break the glass ceiling that keeps talented women from executive positions.
Many people believe that the glass ceiling still exists, especially in traditionally male-dominated fields.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barrier - an obstacle that prevents progress

Etymology

Metaphorical use of 'glass' to indicate something transparent but real; first used in the 1980s in the US.

2. NOUN

/ ɡlæs ˈsiːlɪŋ / (AmE), / ɡlɑːs ˈsiːlɪŋ / (BrE)
an unofficial barrier to advancement in a profession, especially affecting women and members of minorities, that prevents them from achieving upper-level positions
invisible barrier to career advancement

Examples

She encountered the glass ceiling when she was passed over for promotion despite excellent qualifications.
Efforts to break the glass ceiling have led to greater diversity in leadership positions.
Although progress has been made, the glass ceiling still exists in many industries.

Synset Examples

Many women still face a glass ceiling in the workplace.
As a womanager, she broke many glass ceilings in the industry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barrier - an obstacle that prevents progress
  • Antonym: equal opportunity - the principle of treating all people the same, especially at work

Etymology

Metaphoric use of 'glass' (invisible) + 'ceiling' (limit), first recorded in the 1980s in the context of workplace inequality.
