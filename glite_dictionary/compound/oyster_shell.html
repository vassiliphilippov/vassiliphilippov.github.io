oyster shell

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔɪstər ʃɛl / (AmE), / ˈɔɪstə ʃɛl / (BrE)
the hard, calcareous external shell of an oyster, often used as a source of calcium or for decorative purposes
the hard shell of an oyster

Examples

She found an oyster shell on the beach and added it to her collection.
Crushed oyster shell is often used as a natural source of calcium for garden soil.
The artist used oyster shell fragments to create a unique mosaic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shell - the hard outer covering of some animals, such as mollusks and crustaceans
