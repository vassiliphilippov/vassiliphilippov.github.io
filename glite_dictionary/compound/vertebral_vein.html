vertebral vein

1. NOUN

/ vərˈtiːbrəl veɪn / (AmE), / vəˈtiːbrəl veɪn / (BrE)
a vein that passes through the openings in the cervical vertebrae, forming a network around the vertebral artery and draining into the brachiocephalic vein
vein in the neck spine region
medical

Examples

The vertebral vein plays a crucial role in draining blood from the spinal cord and neck structures.
During the surgery, the doctor carefully avoided injuring the vertebral vein.
Blockage in the vertebral vein may cause circulation problems in the cervical region.

Etymology

From Latin 'vertebralis' (of the vertebra) + 'vein'.
