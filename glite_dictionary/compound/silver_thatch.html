silver thatch

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪlvər ˌθætʃ / (AmE), / ˈsɪlvə ˌθætʃ / (BrE)
a small, stocky fan palm native to southern Florida and Cuba, known for its silvery leaves
a fan palm of southern Florida and Cuba

Examples

The silver thatch thrives in coastal regions with sandy soil.
Gardeners value the silver thatch for its drought resistance and striking foliage.
Many birds nest among the leaves of the silver thatch palm.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɪlvər θætʃ / (AmE), / ˈsɪlvə θatʃ / (BrE)
a small palm found in southern Florida and the West Indies, closely resembling the silvertop palmetto
a palm tree of Florida and the West Indies

Examples

The island was dotted with silver thatch swaying gently in the breeze.
They used the leaves of the silver thatch to weave baskets and hats.
A rare bird built its nest in a silver thatch near the beach.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silvertop palmetto - a similar palm species of the southeastern US
