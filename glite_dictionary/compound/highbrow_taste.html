highbrow taste

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪˌbraʊ teɪst / (AmE), / ˈhaɪˌbraʊ teɪst / (BrE)
a preference for things considered refined, intellectual, or culturally sophisticated
preference for refined or intellectual things

Examples

Her highbrow taste in art made the gallery's modernist collection her favorite.
He was known for his highbrow taste in literature, often reading obscure classics.
The restaurant caters to those with a highbrow taste for gourmet cuisine.

Synset Examples

Her highbrow tastes often alienate her friends.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lowbrow taste - preference for things considered less cultured or unrefined
  • Synonym: highbrow - a person with intellectual or cultured tastes

Etymology

From 'highbrow' (intellectual, cultured) + 'taste' (preference).
