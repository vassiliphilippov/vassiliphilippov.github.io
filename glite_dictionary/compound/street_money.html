street money

1. NOUN

/ ˈstrit ˌmʌni / (AmE), / ˈstriːt ˌmʌni / (BrE)
informal income earned from small jobs or unregulated sources, often outside formal employment.
informal income from odd jobs
informal

Examples

She earned some street money by running errands for her neighbors.
Many teenagers make street money doing odd jobs over the summer.
He relied on street money from busking to pay his bills.

Synset Examples

She earned street money by washing cars on weekends.
He made some quick street money selling homemade bracelets.
