invertebrate foot

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈvɝtəbrət fʊt / (AmE), / ɪnˈvɜːtɪbrət fʊt / (BrE)
an organ of locomotion or attachment found in invertebrate animals, such as the muscular foot of a mollusk
organ for movement or attachment in invertebrates

Examples

The invertebrate foot of a snail contracts rhythmically to propel it forward.
Scientists examined the structure of the invertebrate foot in various mollusks to understand its function.
In octopuses, the invertebrate foot has evolved into arms for grasping and movement.
