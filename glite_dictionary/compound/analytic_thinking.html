analytic thinking

1. NOUN

/ əˈnælɪtɪk ˈθɪŋkɪŋ / (AmE), / əˈnælɪtɪk ˈθɪŋkɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of breaking down a complex whole into its constituent parts to better understand their relationships
process of breaking down and analyzing parts

Examples

Many scientists rely on analytic thinking to solve complex problems in their research.
Schools are increasingly encouraging analytic thinking in students to improve problem-solving skills.
Her analytic thinking helped the team identify and fix the root cause of the issue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: analysis - detailed examination of the elements or structure of something
  • Antonym: holistic thinking - thinking that emphasizes the whole rather than its parts
