chock-a-block

1. ADV

/ ˌtʃɑk ə ˈblɑk / (AmE), / ˌtʃɒk ə ˈblɒk / (BrE)
Packed very tightly; completely full or crowded.
completely full or crowded
informal british

Examples

The train was chock-a-block with commuters during rush hour.
Shelves in the little shop were chock-a-block with goods from floor to ceiling.
After the festival, the streets were chock-a-block and difficult to navigate.

Synset Examples

it was chock-a-block full

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jam-packed - completely full or crowded
  • Antonym: empty - containing nothing; not full

Etymology

Originally nautical, probably from sailing terminology: 'chock-a-block' described blocks (pulleys) so tight together no further movement was possible.
