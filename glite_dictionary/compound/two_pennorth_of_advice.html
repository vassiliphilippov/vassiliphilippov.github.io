two penn'orth of advice

1. NOUN

/ tuː ˈpɛnɚθ əv ədˈvaɪs / (AmE), / tuː ˈpɛnəθ əv ədˈvaɪs / (BrE)
a small or modest amount of advice, typically offered informally or with humility
a small amount of advice
informal british

Examples

She couldn’t resist giving her two penn'orth of advice during the meeting.
Before you decide, let me offer my two penn'orth of advice.
Everyone at the table seemed eager to contribute their two penn'orth of advice.

Synset Examples

I only need a two penn'orth of advice on this.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: two cents' worth - an American idiom meaning a small or modest opinion or piece of advice

Etymology

From 'pennyworth', meaning the value of a penny; 'penn'orth' is a British contraction.
