Hooker's orchid

1. NOUN

/ ˈhʊkərz ˌɔrkɪd / (AmE), / ˈhʊkəz ˌɔːkɪd / (BrE)
a species of orchid distinguished by its long spur, basal leaves, and petals that meet beneath the upper sepal
a species of long-spurred orchid

Examples

The botanical garden had a rare specimen of Hooker's orchid in its alpine section.
Researchers discovered new growth of Hooker's orchid in the mountainous region last spring.
Enthusiasts travel from afar to photograph Hooker's orchid when it blooms in the wild.

Etymology

Named after British botanist Sir Joseph Dalton Hooker (1817–1911).
