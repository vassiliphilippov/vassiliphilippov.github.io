Aegean civilisation

1. NOUN

/ əˈdʒiː.ən ˌsɪ.vɪ.ləˈzeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ɪˈdʒiː.ən ˌsɪ.vɪ.laɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the prehistoric culture that developed on the islands of the Aegean Sea and nearby lands, especially noted for the Minoan and Mycenaean civilizations
prehistoric culture of the Aegean region

Examples

The ancient Aegean civilisation left behind magnificent palaces and intricate pottery on Crete and other islands.
Archaeologists have uncovered new evidence about the collapse of the Aegean civilisation in the late Bronze Age.
Art and architecture from the Aegean civilisation greatly influenced later Greek culture.

Synset Examples

by 800 BC the entire Aegean culture had adopted this style of pottery

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Minoan civilisation - Bronze Age civilisation centered on Crete, an important part of the Aegean civilisation
  • Synonym: Mycenaean civilisation - Late Bronze Age civilisation on mainland Greece, considered part of Aegean civilisation

Etymology

Aegean from the name of the Aegean Sea, itself from ancient Greek Aigaion Pelagos.
