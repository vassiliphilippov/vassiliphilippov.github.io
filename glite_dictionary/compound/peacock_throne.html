Peacock Throne

1. NOUN

/ ˈpiˌkɑk θroʊn / (AmE), / ˈpiːkɒk θrəʊn / (BrE)
the famous golden throne of the Mughal emperors of India, renowned for its lavish decoration; later seized by the Persians in 1739 and lost, it became a symbol of royal authority in Iran
golden throne of Mughal emperors, later Persian symbol

Examples

The Peacock Throne was renowned for its beauty and immense value.
After its capture in 1739, the Peacock Throne became a symbol of Persian imperial power.
Many historians have speculated about the fate of the Peacock Throne after it disappeared.

Etymology

Named for its lavish decoration featuring peacock motifs; associated with Mughal and Persian royalty.
