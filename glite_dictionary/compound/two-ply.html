two-ply

1. ADJ

/ tuːˌplaɪ / (AmE), / tuːˌplaɪ / (BrE)
consisting of two layers or strands; having a thickness made up of two parts
made of two layers or strands

Examples

She prefers using two-ply toilet paper because it is stronger and softer than the single-ply variety.
This scarf is knitted from two-ply yarn for extra warmth.
The manufacturer recommends two-ply cardboard for better protection during shipping.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: single-ply - made of a single layer or strand
  • Degree Higher: multi-ply - made of more than two layers or strands
