genus Orycteropus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ɔːrˌɪk.təˈroʊ.pəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ɒˌrɪk.təˈrəʊ.pəs / (BrE)
A genus of mammals that includes the aardvark, and is coextensive with the family Orycteropodidae.
genus of mammals including the aardvark

Examples

The genus Orycteropus contains only a single extant species, the aardvark.
Researchers studied fossils of the genus Orycteropus to learn more about its evolutionary history.
All members of the genus Orycteropus share distinctive digging adaptations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aardvark - nocturnal mammal native to Africa

Etymology

From Greek oryktēr (digger) + pous (foot).
