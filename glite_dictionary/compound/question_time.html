question time

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɛs.tʃən taɪm / (AmE), / ˈkwɛs.tʃən taɪm / (BrE)
a period during a parliamentary session when members, especially in the British Parliament, may ask questions of government ministers
parliamentary session for questioning ministers
british

Examples

Many important issues were raised during question time in the House of Commons today.
Televised question time helps make the government more accountable to the public.
Cabinet ministers must be prepared for challenging inquiries at question time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Prime Minister's Questions - weekly session in the UK Parliament where the Prime Minister answers questions
