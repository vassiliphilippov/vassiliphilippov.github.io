stone-cold

1. ADJ

/ stoʊn koʊld / (AmE), / stəʊn kəʊld / (BrE)
completely cold; having lost all warmth.
completely cold; without warmth

Examples

The soup was stone-cold by the time she finished her phone call.
He handed me a stone-cold fish for breakfast.
After being left outside, the tea had turned stone-cold.

Synset Examples

by the time he got back to his coffee it was stone-cold

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: cold - having a low temperature, especially when compared to the temperature of the human body
  • Degree Higher: freezing - extremely cold; at or below the freezing point

Etymology

Formed from the nouns 'stone' and adjective 'cold', using the idea that a stone is typically very cold to the touch.
