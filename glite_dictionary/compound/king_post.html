king post

1. NOUN

/ kɪŋ poʊst / (AmE), / kɪŋ pəʊst / (BrE)
the central vertical post in a triangular truss, connecting the crossbeam to the apex and supporting the structure
central post in a triangular truss

Examples

The roof was reinforced with a sturdy king post to improve its stability.
Carpenters installed a king post at the center of the truss to support the crossbeam.
A damaged king post can compromise the integrity of the entire structure.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: queen post - either of a pair of upright posts on either side of a triangular truss, typically used when the span is longer than that suited for a king post
