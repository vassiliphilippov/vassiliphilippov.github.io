family Squillidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli skwɪˈlɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli skwɪˈlɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of crustaceans that burrow in mud or under stones in shallow waters along seashores.
family of burrowing seashore crustaceans

Examples

Species in the family Squillidae are commonly known as mantis shrimps.
Researchers discovered a new member of the family Squillidae in coastal mudflats.
The characteristic burrowing behavior of the family Squillidae helps them avoid predators.

Etymology

From the genus name Squilla (type genus of the family) + taxonomic suffix -idae, used in zoological family names.
