radio detection and ranging

1. NOUN

/ ˌreɪdioʊ dɪˈtɛkʃən ənd ˈreɪndʒɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌreɪdiəʊ dɪˈtɛkʃən ənd ˈreɪndʒɪŋ / (BrE)
A system or instrument that uses the echo of pulses of microwave radiation to detect and locate distant objects, such as aircraft or ships.
system using microwaves to locate distant objects

Examples

The development of radio detection and ranging transformed military and civilian navigation systems.
Meteorologists rely on radio detection and ranging to monitor storms and predict weather patterns.
Early airport security depended heavily on advances in radio detection and ranging technology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: radar - system using radio waves to detect and locate objects

Etymology

A phrase formed from the initial letters RAdio Detection And Ranging (origin of 'radar').
