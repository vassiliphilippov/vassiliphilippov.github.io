collective security

1. NOUN

/ kəˈlɛktɪv səˈkjʊrəti / (AmE), / kəˈlɛktɪv sɪˈkjʊərɪti / (BrE)
A system in which a group of nations agree to act together to maintain international peace and security, often by promising to defend each other against aggression.
system of joint international peacekeeping

Examples

Many countries see collective security as essential for preventing large-scale wars.
The United Nations was established to promote collective security among its members.
After World War I, there were several attempts to establish collective security arrangements in Europe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mutual defense - the policy of defending each other by mutual agreement
