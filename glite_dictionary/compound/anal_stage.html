anal stage

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪnl steɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈanəl steɪdʒ / (BrE)
In psychoanalytic theory, the second stage of a child's psychosexual development, during which pleasure focuses on bowel and bladder control.
stage in child development (psychoanalysis)
medical

Examples

According to Freud, conflicts during the anal stage can influence personality later in life.
The therapist explained that toilet training is a central issue of the anal stage of development.
Disruptions in the anal stage are believed to contribute to certain adult behaviors.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: oral stage - the first psychosexual stage in Freudian theory
  • Degree Higher: phallic stage - the third psychosexual stage in Freudian theory

Etymology

From Latin 'anus' (ring, anus) and 'stage', in reference to Freud's psychosexual development theory.
