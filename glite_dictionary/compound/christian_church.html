Christian Church

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɪstʃən tʃɝːtʃ / (AmE), / ˈkrɪstʃən tʃɜːtʃ / (BrE)
A Protestant church that regards the Bible as the sole authority of faith and practices baptism by immersion.
Protestant denomination emphasizing Bible and immersion baptism

Examples

The Christian Church in our town has been active in mission work for over a century.
She was baptized by immersion in the local Christian Church last summer.
Members of the Christian Church consider the Bible the highest authority in matters of faith.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Disciples of Christ - A Protestant religious group with similar beliefs, also known as the Christian Church (Disciples of Christ)
