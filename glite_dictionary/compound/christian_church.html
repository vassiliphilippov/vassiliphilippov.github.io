Christian church

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɪstʃən tʃɝːtʃ / (AmE), / ˈkrɪstʃən tʃɜːtʃ / (BrE)
a body or group of Christians with its own distinctive doctrines, traditions, and forms of worship, often identified as a denomination within Christianity
a distinct group or denomination within Christianity

Examples

The Christian church in her town is known for its community outreach programs.
Various branches of the Christian church hold different views on certain theological matters.
He decided to join a Christian church that emphasizes traditional liturgy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: denomination - a recognized autonomous branch of the Christian Church

Etymology

Christian from Greek 'Christianos' (follower of Christ) and church from Old English 'cirice', from Greek 'kyriakē' (of the Lord).
