broadcast programming

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrɔːdˌkæst ˌproʊˌgræmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbrɔːdkɑːst ˌprəʊˌgræmɪŋ / (BrE)
the practice of arranging broadcast media content, such as television or radio shows, into a planned schedule over a set period
arrangement of broadcast media schedules

Examples

The network's broadcast programming offered a new lineup of dramas for the fall season.
Changing audience habits have influenced the evolution of broadcast programming strategies over the years.
She studied broadcast programming as part of her media and communications degree.

Synset Examples

In Broadcast programming, counterprogramming is the practice of offering television programs to attract an audience from another television station airing a major event.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scheduling - the act of arranging or planning events or tasks at particular times
