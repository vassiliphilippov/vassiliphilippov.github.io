genetic abnormality

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˈnɛtɪk æbˌnɔrmæləti / (AmE), / dʒəˈnɛtɪk æbˈnɔːməlɪti / (BrE)
a disease or disorder resulting from inherited genetic factors
inherited disease or disorder
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that the child's condition was due to a genetic abnormality.
Many genetic abnormalities can be detected through prenatal screening.
Research is ongoing to find cures for rare genetic abnormality-related diseases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: genetic disorder - a disorder caused by abnormalities in genes or chromosomes

Etymology

From 'genetic', relating to genes, and 'abnormality', from Latin 'abnormalis' (irregular).
