genus Polybotrya

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˌpɒliˈbɒtriə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˌpɒlɪˈbɒtrɪə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical American ferns, typically terrestrial in youth but often climbing as they mature.
genus of tropical American ferns

Examples

The genus Polybotrya includes several attractive ornamental ferns popular in botanical gardens.
Scientists discovered a new species within the genus Polybotrya growing in the rainforests of Brazil.
Members of the genus Polybotrya start as terrestrial plants but often become climbing as they mature.

Etymology

From Greek 'polys' (many) + 'botrys' (bunch of grapes), referencing the plant's frond structure.
