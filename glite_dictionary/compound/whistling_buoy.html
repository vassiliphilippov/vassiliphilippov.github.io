whistling buoy

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪs.lɪŋ ˌbuː.i / (AmE), / ˈwɪs.lɪŋ ˌbɔɪ / (BrE)
a floating navigational marker that emits a whistling sound, typically to warn vessels of hazards or mark channels
buoy making a whistling sound for navigation

Examples

The captain navigated carefully past the whistling buoy marking the entrance to the harbor.
As the fog rolled in, the sound of the whistling buoy grew louder, guiding the sailors safely.
Several whistling buoys are placed along the coastline to help ships avoid shallow waters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: buoy - a floating device used to mark locations or warn of dangers in water
