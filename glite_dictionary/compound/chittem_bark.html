chittem bark

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɪtəm bɑrk / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪtəm bɑːk / (BrE)
the dried bark of the cascara buckthorn, used as a laxative
dried cascara buckthorn bark used as a laxative
medical

Examples

Traditional healers often prescribed chittem bark to relieve chronic constipation.
The pharmacy sells chittem bark as a natural remedy for digestive issues.
Because of its potent laxative effect, chittem bark should be used with caution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cascara - the dried bark of Rhamnus purshiana, used as a laxative

Etymology

Chittem is an alteration of 'chittam', associated with the plant Rhamnus purshiana in North America.
