to run amain

1. VERB

/ rʌn əˈmeɪn / (AmE), / rʌn əˈmeɪn / (BrE)
To run quickly or at full speed; to dash or hurry.
to run at full speed (archaic)
archaic

Examples

In the old tale, the villagers ran amain to warn their neighbors of the coming storm.
Hearing the dinner bell, the children ran amain across the fields.
Startled by the sudden noise, the soldiers ran amain for cover.

Synset Examples

They ran amain to catch the last bus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: run swiftly - to move quickly on foot
  • Antonym: run slowly - to move at a slow speed on foot

Etymology

From archaic adverb 'amain', from Middle English 'amain', from Old French 'amain' (with great force).
