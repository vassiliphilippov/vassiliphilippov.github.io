red laver

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛd ˈleɪvər / (AmE), / ˈrɛd ˈleɪvə / (BrE)
an edible seaweed of a reddish color, especially species of the genus Porphyra, used in Asian cuisine and as a source of food
edible red seaweed

Examples

The sushi chef wrapped the rice and fish in sheets of red laver.
Dried red laver is commonly used in soups and salads in East Asian dishes.
You can find red laver at most Asian grocery stores, usually sold in thin, crispy sheets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nori - edible seaweed sheets used in Japanese cuisine
