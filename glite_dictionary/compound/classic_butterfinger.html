classic butterfinger

1. NOUN

/ ˈklæsɪk ˈbʌtərˌfɪŋɡər / (AmE), / ˈklæsɪk ˈbʌtəˌfɪŋɡə / (BrE)
a person who is habitually clumsy, especially prone to dropping things
habitually clumsy person
informal

Examples

After spilling coffee on the floor, Mark was teased as the office's classic butterfinger.
You can always count on Julia to drop the bouquet—she's a real classic butterfinger.
He fumbled the keys again, proving he's the classic butterfinger of the family.

Synset Examples

He couldn’t catch the ball—classic butterfingers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: butterfingers - a person who often drops things

Etymology

From 'classic' (exemplary) + 'butterfinger', a term for someone who drops things, originally from the idea of greasy or slippery fingers.
