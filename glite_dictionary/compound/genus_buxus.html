genus Buxus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈbʌk.səs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈbʌk.səs / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Buxaceae, consisting of evergreen shrubs and small trees commonly known as boxwoods.
type genus of Buxaceae (boxwood plants)

Examples

The genus Buxus contains several species valued for their dense evergreen foliage.
Gardeners often plant members of the genus Buxus as ornamental hedges.
Scientific studies have classified many boxwood species under the genus Buxus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boxwood - an evergreen shrub or small tree of the genus Buxus

Etymology

From Latin 'Buxus', the name for boxwood.
