coriander seed

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɔːriˈændər siːd / (AmE), / ˌkɒriˈændə siːd / (BrE)
the dried seeds of the coriander plant, used whole or ground as a spice in cooking
dried seeds of the coriander plant used as spice

Examples

Many Indian curries include coriander seed as a key spice for its citrusy flavor.
Ground coriander seed is often sprinkled over roasted vegetables for added aroma.
She toasted some coriander seed before grinding it to use in her bread recipe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coriander - an aromatic herb used in cooking, especially its leaves and seeds

Etymology

From Latin coriandrum, the name of the plant, and Old English sed meaning seed.
