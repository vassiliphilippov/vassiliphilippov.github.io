personal equation

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɜrsənl ɪˈkweɪʒən / (AmE), / ˈpɜːsənl ɪˈkweɪʒən / (BrE)
the variation in observations or judgments resulting from the individual characteristics or biases of the person making them, particularly in scientific or psychological measurements
variation due to individual differences

Examples

In psychological experiments, the personal equation can affect the interpretation of results.
The astronomer accounted for his personal equation when timing star transits.
Every judge brings a unique personal equation to their assessments of talent.
