mass rapid transit

1. NOUN

/ mæs ˈræpɪd ˈtræn.sɪt / (AmE), / mæs ˈrapɪd ˈtræn.zɪt / (BrE)
an urban public transportation system using underground or elevated trains designed to move large numbers of passengers quickly.
urban train-based public transport system

Examples

The city recently invested in a mass rapid transit system to address traffic congestion.
Many commuters rely on mass rapid transit to travel efficiently across the metropolitan area.
Expanding the mass rapid transit network has significantly reduced air pollution in the urban core.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: subway - underground electric railway system in a city
  • Synonym: metro - an urban electric train system, usually underground
  • Degree Lower: light rail - a passenger train system, usually at street level and less extensive than mass rapid transit
