Queen Anne's lace

1. NOUN

/ kwiːn ænz leɪs / (AmE), / kwiːn ænz leɪs / (BrE)
a wild Eurasian plant with finely divided leaves and clusters of small white or yellowish flowers, often found in fields and roadsides; also known as wild carrot
wild plant with clusters of small white flowers

Examples

Every summer, the fields behind our house are full of Queen Anne's lace in bloom.
She gathered a bouquet of Queen Anne's lace and daisies for the kitchen table.
Farmers sometimes consider Queen Anne's lace an invasive weed, but many appreciate its delicate beauty.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wild carrot - another common name for Queen Anne's lace

Etymology

Named after Queen Anne of Great Britain, referencing the lace-like appearance of the flowers.
