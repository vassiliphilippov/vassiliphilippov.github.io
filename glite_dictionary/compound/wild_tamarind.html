wild tamarind

1. NOUN

/ waɪld təˈmærɪnd / (AmE), / waɪld təˈmærɪnd / (BrE)
a tree native to the West Indies, Florida, and Mexico, with long flat pods resembling those of the tamarind
tree with long flat pods, resembling tamarind

Examples

The wild tamarind grows abundantly in southern Florida’s sandy soils.
Locals often use the wood of the wild tamarind for crafting small items.
The leaves of the wild tamarind close at night, a feature known as nyctinasty.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tamarind - a tropical tree with edible fruit pods

2. NOUN

/ waɪld tæˈmærɪnd / (AmE), / waɪld təˈmærɪnd / (BrE)
a common thorny tropical American tree with yellow flowers, edible curved pods, useful timber, yellow dye, and mucilaginous gum
tropical American tree with yellow flowers and edible pods

Examples

The wild tamarind provides both edible pods and durable timber in tropical regions.
Local villagers harvest gum from the wild tamarind for use in traditional medicines.
The leaves of the wild tamarind create dense shade, making it popular for agroforestry.
