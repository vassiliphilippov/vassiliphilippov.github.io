small civet

1. NOUN

/ smɔl ˈsɪvət / (AmE), / smɔːl ˈsɪvɪt / (BrE)
a common species of civet native to Southeast Asia, known for its slender body and nocturnal habits
a Southeast Asian species of civet

Examples

The small civet is active mainly at night, searching for insects and fruit.
Local farmers often spot a small civet near the forest edge.
Because it adapts well to different habitats, the small civet is widespread across Southeast Asia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: civet - a small, lean, mostly nocturnal mammal found in the tropics of Africa and Asia
