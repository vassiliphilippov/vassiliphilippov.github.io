genus Anodonta

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs æˈnɑːdɒntə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs əˈnɒdəntə / (BrE)
A genus of thin-shelled freshwater mussels found in rivers and lakes, belonging to the family Unionidae.
a genus of freshwater mussels

Examples

The genus Anodonta is known for its thin, fragile shells that distinguish it from other freshwater mussels.
Several species within the genus Anodonta inhabit lakes and slow-moving rivers in North America and Europe.
Researchers recently discovered a new member of the genus Anodonta in the river delta ecosystem.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Anodonta - a genus of freshwater mussels with thin shells

Etymology

From Greek 'anōdontos', meaning 'toothless', referring to the shell structure.
