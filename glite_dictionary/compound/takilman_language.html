Takilman language

1. NOUN

/ təˈkɪlmən ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / təˈkɪlmən ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
The language of the Takelma people; a Penutian language formerly spoken in southwestern Oregon.
a language spoken by the Takelma people

Examples

Linguists have documented the Takilman language using early 20th-century field notes.
The Takilman language is an important subject of study in Native American linguistics.
Few people today can speak the Takilman language fluently.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Penutian - a proposed family of Native American languages
  • Synonym: Takelma - an indigenous people of southwestern Oregon

Etymology

From 'Takelma', the name of the indigenous people, plus 'language'.
