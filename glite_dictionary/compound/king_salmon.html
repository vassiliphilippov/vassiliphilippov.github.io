king salmon

1. NOUN

/ kɪŋ ˈsæmən / (AmE), / kɪŋ ˈsæmən / (BrE)
A large species of Pacific salmon highly valued as food, with adults dying after spawning.
a large Pacific salmon species

Examples

The chef prepared grilled king salmon with a lemon-dill sauce for the special dinner.
Anglers travel to Alaska every year hoping to catch a massive king salmon.
After spawning in the river, the king salmon naturally dies, completing its life cycle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chinook salmon - another name for king salmon
  • Degree Lower: sockeye salmon - a different species of Pacific salmon

2. NOUN

/ kɪŋ ˈsæmən / (AmE), / kɪŋ ˈsæmən / (BrE)
The pink or white flesh of a large Pacific salmon, valued as food.
flesh of a large Pacific salmon (as food)

Examples

The grilled king salmon was perfectly seasoned and flaky.
Sushi chefs often prefer king salmon for its rich flavor and buttery texture.
At the market, she bought a fillet of fresh king salmon for dinner.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Chinook salmon - the species of large Pacific salmon from which king salmon meat comes
