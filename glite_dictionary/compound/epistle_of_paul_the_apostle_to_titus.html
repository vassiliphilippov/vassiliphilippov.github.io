Epistle of Paul the Apostle to Titus

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈpɪsəl əv pɔl ði əˈpɑsəl tu ˈtaɪtəs / (AmE), / ɪˈpɪsəl əv pɔːl ði əˈpɒsl tuː ˈtaɪtəs / (BrE)
a book of the New Testament containing a letter from Saint Paul to Titus, offering advice on church leadership and pastoral responsibilities
New Testament book; letter from Paul to Titus

Examples

The Epistle of Paul the Apostle to Titus is often studied for its guidance on church organization.
In his sermon, the pastor quoted from the Epistle of Paul the Apostle to Titus.
Scholars debate the authorship of the Epistle of Paul the Apostle to Titus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Titus - recipient of the epistle, an early Christian leader
  • Synonym: Epistle of Paul the Apostle to Timothy - another Pauline epistle offering pastoral advice

Etymology

Epistle from Latin 'epistola' (letter), from Greek 'epistolē', and Paul from Latin 'Paulus'; Titus from Greek 'Titos'.
