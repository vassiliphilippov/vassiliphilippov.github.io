metric unit

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛtrɪk ˈjunɪt / (AmE), / ˈmɛtrɪk ˈjuːnɪt / (BrE)
a unit of measurement in the metric system, such as the meter, kilogram, or second, based on powers of ten
unit of measurement in the metric system

Examples

It is common for scientists to report data using metric units for consistency.
The length of the table was given in both inches and metric units.
Most countries have officially adopted metric units for everyday measurements.

Synset Examples

convert all the measurements to metric units
it is easier to work in metric

Related Terms

  • Antonym: imperial unit - a unit of measurement in the system formerly used in the UK and still used in the US

Etymology

From French 'métrique', from Greek 'metron' (measure), and Latin 'unitas' (oneness, unity).
