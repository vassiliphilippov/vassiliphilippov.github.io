Peasant's Revolt

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛzənts rɪˌvoʊlt / (AmE), / ˈpɛzənts rɪˌvəʊlt / (BrE)
a major uprising of peasants and lower-class people in England in 1381, protesting taxes and social injustice, which was eventually suppressed by the authorities
English uprising of 1381 against taxes and injustice

Examples

The Peasant's Revolt was a turning point in English medieval history.
Historians still debate the lasting effects of the Peasant's Revolt on English society.
Richard II played a central role in suppressing the Peasant's Revolt.
