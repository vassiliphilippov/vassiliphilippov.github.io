synapsid reptile

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈnæp.sɪd ˈrɛp.taɪl / (AmE), / sɪˈnap.sɪd ˈrɛp.taɪl / (BrE)
an extinct reptile distinguished by a single pair of openings in the skull behind the eyes, regarded as ancestral to mammals
extinct reptile with single skull opening; mammal ancestor

Examples

Paleontologists discovered the fossilized remains of a synapsid reptile in the ancient rock formation.
The synapsid reptile is notable for having a skull structure more similar to mammals than modern reptiles.
Early synapsid reptiles eventually gave rise to mammals during the course of evolution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: synapsid - member of a group including mammals and their extinct relatives with a single skull opening
  • Antonym: diapsid - reptile with two pairs of temporal openings in the skull

Etymology

From Greek 'synapsis' (union, conjunction) + 'reptile', due to the characteristic joined opening in the skull.
