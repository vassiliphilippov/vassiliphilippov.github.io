vinyl ether

1. NOUN

/ ˈvaɪnəl ˈiːθər / (AmE), / ˈvaɪnɪl ˈiːθə / (BrE)
a colorless, volatile, highly flammable liquid formerly used as an inhalation anesthetic
a volatile liquid anesthetic (chemical)
medical

Examples

The use of vinyl ether as an anesthetic has declined due to safety concerns.
Chemists handled vinyl ether with care because of its flammable nature.
In the past, vinyl ether was chosen for certain surgical procedures requiring inhalation anesthesia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ether - a class of organic compounds used as anesthetics and solvents

Etymology

From 'vinyl', referring to the ethenyl group, and 'ether', from Greek aither (upper air).
