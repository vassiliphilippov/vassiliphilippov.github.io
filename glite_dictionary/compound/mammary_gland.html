mammary gland

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæməri ɡlænd / (AmE), / ˈmaməri ɡlænd / (BrE)
The milk-secreting organ in female mammals that produces and delivers milk to feed offspring.
milk-secreting organ in female mammals
medical

Examples

The mammary gland develops during puberty and enlarges during pregnancy.
Lactation is the process by which the mammary gland produces milk for newborns.
A swollen mammary gland can be a sign of infection in some animals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: breast - the chest or front part of the body, also a milk-secreting organ in humans

Etymology

From Latin 'mamma' (breast) and 'glandula' (gland).
