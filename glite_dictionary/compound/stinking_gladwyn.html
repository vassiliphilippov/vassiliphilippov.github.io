stinking gladwyn

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɪŋkɪŋ ˈɡlædwɪn / (AmE), / ˈstɪŋkɪŋ ˈɡlædwɪn / (BrE)
a type of iris with purple flowers and foul-smelling leaves, native to southern and western Europe and North Africa
iris species with foul-smelling leaves

Examples

The stinking gladwyn brightens shady gardens with its distinctive purple flowers.
When crushed, the leaves of the stinking gladwyn release an unpleasant odor.
Many gardeners grow stinking gladwyn for its resilience and striking orange seeds.
