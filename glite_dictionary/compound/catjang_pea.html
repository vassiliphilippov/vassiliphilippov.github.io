catjang pea

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæt.dʒæŋ pi / (AmE), / ˈkæt.dʒæŋ piː / (BrE)
A tropical woody plant with showy yellow flowers and flat pods, widely cultivated in tropical regions for its edible seeds.
tropical leguminous plant with edible pods

Examples

The catjang pea is valued both as a food crop and for improving soil fertility.
Farmers in tropical regions often plant catjang peas to enrich their fields.
A dish made from catjang pea seeds is rich in protein and nutrients.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cowpea - another leguminous plant cultivated in the tropics

Etymology

From Hindi 'kachang' or Malay 'kacang' (bean), with 'pea' from Old English 'pise'.
