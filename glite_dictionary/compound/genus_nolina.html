genus Nolina

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs nəˈliːnə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs nəˈliːnə / (BrE)
A genus of perennial plants resembling yucca, native to the southern United States and Mexico.
a plant genus similar to yucca

Examples

The desert garden featured several species from the genus Nolina.
Many plants in the genus Nolina are used for landscaping in arid regions.
Researchers found that members of the genus Nolina are drought-resistant and hardy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Nolina - any plant of the genus Nolina

Etymology

From Latin 'genus' (kind, type) and 'Nolina', named after French botanist Abbé Nolin.
