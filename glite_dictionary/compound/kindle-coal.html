kindle-coal

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɪndl ˌkoʊl / (AmE), / ˈkɪndl ˌkəʊl / (BrE)
a type of coal specially used for starting fires
coal for starting fires

Examples

We stacked some kindle-coal under the logs to help the fire catch quickly.
Early settlers often relied on kindle-coal in winter to ignite their stoves.
Without kindle-coal, it would have taken much longer to get the fireplace going.

Synset Examples

Adding kindle-coal helped the flames rise quickly.

Etymology

Formed from 'kindle' (to start a fire) and 'coal'.
