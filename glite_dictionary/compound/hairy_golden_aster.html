hairy golden aster

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛri ˈɡoʊldən ˈæstər / (AmE), / ˈheəri ˈɡəʊldən ˈæstə / (BrE)
A perennial plant with yellow flower heads in branched clusters, covered with fine hairs, found in dry areas across North America.
species of North American yellow-flowered perennial plant

Examples

The hairy golden aster brightens the dry meadows every summer with its vivid yellow blooms.
Botanists discovered a large patch of hairy golden aster thriving in the arid region of Colorado.
Many wildflowers, including the hairy golden aster, attract pollinators to the prairie ecosystem.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: golden aster - plant with yellow, daisy-like flowers in the aster family
