chloride of lime

1. NOUN

/ ˈklɔːr.aɪd əv laɪm / (AmE), / ˈklɔː.rɪd əv laɪm / (BrE)
a white powder consisting mainly of calcium hypochlorite, used as a bleaching agent and disinfectant
chemical bleaching agent and disinfectant

Examples

The public swimming pool is cleaned regularly with chloride of lime to ensure proper sanitation.
Workers handled barrels of chloride of lime during the disinfection process.
Historically, chloride of lime was a common household product for whitening laundry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calcium hypochlorite - a chemical compound used as a disinfectant and bleaching agent

Etymology

Formed from 'chloride' (from chlorine) and 'lime' (from Old English 'lim', calcium oxide).
