sugar loaf

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃuɡɚ ˌloʊf / (AmE), / ˈʃʊɡə ˌləʊf / (BrE)
a large, solid, conical mass of refined sugar, traditionally sold in this form before the widespread use of granulated sugar
conical mass of refined sugar

Examples

In the eighteenth century, households would buy a sugar loaf and break it into pieces for use.
The museum displayed an old sugar loaf along with antique kitchen tools.
Unlike modern granulated sugar, a sugar loaf was shaped like a tall cone and wrapped in blue paper.
