set of pipes

1. NOUN

/ sɛt əv paɪps / (AmE), / sɛt əv paɪps / (BrE)
a group of connected pipes or tubes used for transporting fluids, such as water or gas, within a building or system
group of connected pipes for fluid transport

Examples

The engineer inspected the set of pipes for leaks before turning the water back on.
A new set of pipes was installed during the renovation of the kitchen.
They had to replace the entire set of pipes after discovering corrosion in the basement.

Synset Examples

The old house has a rusty set of pipes.
The plumber replaced the broken set of pipes quickly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pipework - the system of pipes in a building or machine
  • Synonym: plumbing - the system of pipes, tanks, fittings, and other apparatus required for the water supply, heating, and sanitation in a building
