street arab

1. NOUN

/ ˈstrit ˌær.əb / (AmE), / ˈstriːt ˌær.əb / (BrE)
(sometimes offensive) a homeless child, especially a boy, living or roaming the streets, often abandoned or lacking stable shelter.
homeless or abandoned street child
offensive

Examples

The novel describes the harsh life of a street arab surviving in Victorian London.
He was mistaken for a street arab by the police due to his ragged appearance and wandering.
Many works of the era depict the street arab as both a victim and a symbol of society's neglect.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: street urchin - a poor, often homeless child who lives or hangs about in the streets

Etymology

The term 'Arab' here was used in the 19th century to evoke nomadic or wandering qualities, reflecting historical stereotypes.
