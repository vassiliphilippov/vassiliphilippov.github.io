standard anatomical position

1. NOUN

/ ˈstændɚd əˌnæt̬əmɪkəl pəˈzɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈstændəd əˌnætəmɪkəl pəˈzɪʃən / (BrE)
the position of the human body standing upright, facing forward, arms at the sides with palms facing forward, and feet pointing ahead; used as the reference for anatomical descriptions
body reference position in anatomy
medical

Examples

In the standard anatomical position, the palms of the hands face forward and the feet point straight ahead.
Doctors use the standard anatomical position to describe the locations of injuries or organs.
Before beginning the dissection, students should orient the specimen in the standard anatomical position.

Synset Examples

For the purpose of universal comparison, there is a standard position — the anatomical position — that the patient or cadaver theoretically has assumed.

Etymology

From anatomical terminology; 'anatomical' from Greek anatomē (dissection).
