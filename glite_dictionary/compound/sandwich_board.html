sandwich board

1. NOUN

/ ˈsændwɪtʃ bɔrd / (AmE), / ˈsænwɪdʒ bɔːd / (BrE)
a pair of advertising boards, hinged at the top, worn over the shoulders so that one board hangs in front and one in back, used to display advertisements in public places
double-sided advertising board worn by a person

Examples

He wore a sandwich board outside the shop to advertise the grand opening.
The street was filled with people handing out flyers and carrying sandwich boards.
A man in a sandwich board marched down the avenue, promoting the new pizza restaurant.

Etymology

From the practice of a person wearing advertisements between two boards, resembling the filling between slices of bread in a sandwich.
