intestinal fortitude

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtɛstɪnəl ˈfɔrtɪˌtud / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɛstɪnəl ˈfɔːtɪˌtjuːd / (BrE)
courage and determination in facing adversity or difficult situations
courage and determination; stamina

Examples

She showed great intestinal fortitude by continuing her studies during a family crisis.
The job interview tested his intestinal fortitude as much as his skills.
You need a lot of intestinal fortitude to stand up for what you believe in.

Synset Examples

It takes intestinal fortitude to face such challenges.
He showed remarkable intestinal fortitude during the crisis.
Running a marathon requires a lot of intestinal fortitude.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: courage - the ability to do something that frightens one
  • Synonym: determination - firmness of purpose

Etymology

Figuratively combines 'intestinal' (relating to the intestines) and 'fortitude' (from Latin fortitudo, meaning strength or courage) to imply inner strength.
