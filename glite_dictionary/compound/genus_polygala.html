genus Polygala

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs pəˈlɪɡ.ə.lə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs pɒˈlɪɡ.ə.lə / (BrE)
the type genus of the family Polygalaceae, including milkworts, senega, and snakeroot plants
type genus of Polygalaceae (milkworts)

Examples

The genus Polygala comprises over 500 species of flowering plants found mainly in temperate regions.
Many herbalists value certain species within the genus Polygala for their medicinal properties.
New discoveries of species related to the genus Polygala are occasionally reported in botanical journals.

Etymology

From Latin 'Polygala' meaning 'much milk', referring to the belief that the plants increased milk production in cattle.
