to pencil whip

1. VERB

/ ˈpɛnsəl wɪp / (AmE), / ˈpɛnsəl wɪp / (BrE)
to complete something, especially paperwork or a report, quickly and carelessly, often by falsifying information or failing to check details
complete something inaccurately or by falsifying

Examples

The auditor discovered that the supervisor had pencil whipped several safety checklists.
Management warned employees not to pencil whip their time sheets or risk disciplinary action.
Rather than pencil whipping the inspection, she took her time to do it thoroughly.

Synset Examples

He didn’t want to pencil whip the project just to finish it.
They tried to pencil whip the report to meet the deadline.
Pencil whipping the figures won’t help in the long run.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to falsify - to change or misrepresent information to deceive
  • Synonym: to fabricate - to invent or concoct, typically with deceitful intent
  • Antonym: to verify - to make sure or demonstrate that something is true

Etymology

Probably from 'pencil' (writing tool) + 'whip' (to do quickly), originally U.S. office slang.
