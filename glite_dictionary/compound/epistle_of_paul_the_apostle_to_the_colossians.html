Epistle of Paul the Apostle to the Colossians

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈpɪsəl əv pɔl ði əˈpɑsəl tu ðə kəˈlɑʃənz / (AmE), / ɪˈpɪsəl ɒv pɔːl ði əˈpɒsl tuː ðə kəˈlɒʃənz / (BrE)
a book of the New Testament, presented as a letter written by Saint Paul to the Christians in Colossae (in ancient Phrygia)
a New Testament letter to the Colossians

Examples

Many Christians study the Epistle of Paul the Apostle to the Colossians to better understand Paul's teachings.
The Epistle of Paul the Apostle to the Colossians addresses themes of faith and spiritual maturity.
In church, the pastor read a passage from the Epistle of Paul the Apostle to the Colossians.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Colossians - the Christians in Colossae to whom the epistle is addressed
  • Synonym: Epistle of Paul the Apostle to the Ephesians - a New Testament letter to the Ephesians, similar type of Pauline epistle
