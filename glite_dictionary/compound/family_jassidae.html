family Jassidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ˈʤæsɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli ˈjasɪˌdiː / (BrE)
a family of small leafhoppers, often considered identical with Cicadellidae in some classifications
leafhopper insect family (Jassidae)

Examples

The family Jassidae includes many species of small, jumping insects commonly known as leafhoppers.
Some entomologists treat the family Jassidae as synonymous with Cicadellidae, combining both under one name.
Research on the family Jassidae has revealed a wide diversity of forms and habitats among leafhoppers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cicadellidae - the family of common leafhoppers, sometimes considered synonymous with Jassidae

Etymology

From New Latin 'Jassidae', based on the type genus 'Jassus'.
