cerebrospinal fluid

1. NOUN

/ səˌriːbroʊˈspaɪnəl ˈfluɪd / (AmE), / səˌriːbrəʊˈspaɪnəl ˈfluːɪd / (BrE)
the clear liquid produced in the ventricles of the brain that surrounds and protects the brain and spinal cord
clear fluid around the brain and spinal cord
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that cerebrospinal fluid plays a vital role in cushioning the brain against injury.
A sample of cerebrospinal fluid was taken to test for signs of infection.
Swelling in the brain can affect the flow of cerebrospinal fluid and increase pressure inside the skull.

Etymology

From Latin 'cerebrum' (brain) and 'spinalis' (of the spine), indicating its location.
