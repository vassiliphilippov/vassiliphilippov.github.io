thermic fever

1. NOUN

/ ˈθɝː.mɪk ˈfiː.vɚ / (AmE), / ˈθɜː.mɪk ˈfiː.vər / (BrE)
A medical condition marked by sudden collapse or exhaustion due to extreme heat or sun exposure.
collapse from excessive heat or sun
medical

Examples

Workers are at risk of thermic fever during long hours in the summer sun.
Doctors warned that athletes could suffer thermic fever if they did not stay hydrated and cool.
Early signs of thermic fever include dizziness, confusion, and rapid heartbeat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heatstroke - a condition caused by overheating of the body, similar to thermic fever
  • Synonym: sunstroke - a condition caused by excessive exposure to the sun's heat

Etymology

From Greek *thermē* (heat) and English *fever*.
