shot glass

1. NOUN

/ ʃɑt ɡlæs / (AmE), / ʃɒt ɡlɑːs / (BrE)
a small glass designed to hold a single serving of liquor, typically a shot of whiskey or other spirits
small glass for a single shot of liquor

Examples

He poured a measure of tequila into a shot glass and handed it to me.
The bartender placed three shot glasses on the counter for the group.
After the toast, everyone downed their shot glass in one quick gulp.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: glass - a drinking vessel made of glass
