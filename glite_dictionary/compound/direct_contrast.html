direct contrast

1. NOUN

/ dəˈrɛkt ˈkɑntræst / (AmE), / dɪˈrɛkt ˈkɒntrɑːst / (BrE)
a clear and obvious opposition or difference between two things when they are compared
clear opposition or difference

Examples

There is a direct contrast between her cheerful manner and his somber mood.
The bright, modern furniture is in direct contrast to the old-fashioned décor.
The film's ending stands in direct contrast to its optimistic beginning.

Synset Examples

in contrast to
by contrast

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contrast - the state of being strikingly different from something else
