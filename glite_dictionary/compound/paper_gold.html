paper gold

1. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪpər ɡoʊld / (AmE), / ˈpeɪpə ɡəʊld / (BrE)
reserve assets created by the International Monetary Fund to supplement actual gold and convertible currency reserves in the international monetary system
IMF reserve assets; non-physical gold

Examples

The central bank decided to increase its holdings of paper gold to bolster its foreign exchange reserves.
Paper gold serves as an important tool for the IMF in maintaining currency stability among member states.
Unlike physical gold, paper gold exists only as a financial record on the balance sheets of central banks.

Etymology

Refers to financial assets that function like gold reserves but exist only on paper, especially Special Drawing Rights (SDRs) created by the IMF.
