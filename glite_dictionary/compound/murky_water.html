murky water

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɝːki ˈwɔtɚ / (AmE), / ˈmɜːki ˈwɔːtə / (BrE)
water that is dark, cloudy, or difficult to see through, often because it contains dirt, mud, or other substances
dark, cloudy water

Examples

After the storm, the basement was flooded with murky water that smelled unpleasant.
The diver could hardly see anything in the murky water of the lake.
The rescue operation was slowed by the murky water and low visibility.

Synset Examples

The old sheugh was filled with murky water after the rain.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: clear water - water that is transparent and easy to see through
  • Synonym: turbid water - water that is cloudy or opaque with suspended matter
