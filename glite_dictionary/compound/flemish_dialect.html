Flemish dialect

1. NOUN

/ ˈflɛmɪʃ ˈdaɪˌalɛkt / (AmE), / ˈflɛmɪʃ ˈdaɪəˌlɛkt / (BrE)
the variety of Dutch spoken in northern Belgium, recognized as one of the official languages of Belgium
Dutch dialect spoken in Belgium

Examples

Many people in northern Belgium speak the Flemish dialect at home and in schools.
The Flemish dialect differs in vocabulary and pronunciation from standard Dutch.
She learned the Flemish dialect after moving to Antwerp for work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Dutch - the West Germanic language spoken in the Netherlands and parts of Belgium

Etymology

From 'Flemish', referring to Flanders—the northern part of Belgium—plus 'dialect', from Greek 'dialektos' (discourse, language).
