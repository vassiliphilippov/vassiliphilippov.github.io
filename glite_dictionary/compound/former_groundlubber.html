former groundlubber

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔrmɚ ˈɡraʊndlʌbɚ / (AmE), / ˈfɔːmə ˈɡraʊndlʌbə / (BrE)
a person who was previously inexperienced in sailing
one formerly inexperienced in sailing

Examples

As a former groundlubber, she quickly adapted to life at sea.
He proudly called himself a former groundlubber after finishing his first solo voyage.
Many members of the crew were former groundlubbers before joining the navy.

Synset Examples

As a former groundlubber, she learned sailing quickly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: groundlubber - a person inexperienced in sailing or nautical matters
  • Synonym: landlubber - an inexperienced or unskilled sailor; someone unfamiliar with the sea

Etymology

From 'groundlubber' (inexperienced sailor; landlubber) with 'former' meaning previous, attached.
