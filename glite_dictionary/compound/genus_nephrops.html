genus Nephrops

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs nɪˈfrɒps / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs nɪˈfrɒps / (BrE)
A genus of marine crustaceans in the family Nephropsidae, which includes the Norway lobster.
genus of marine crustaceans (Nephropsidae)

Examples

The genus Nephrops includes several species, most notably the commercially valuable Norway lobster.
Scientists discovered a new species within the genus Nephrops during their deep-sea expedition.
Fisheries regulations often focus on sustainable harvests of crustaceans from the genus Nephrops.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Norway lobster - a species of Nephrops (Nephrops norvegicus), an edible lobster

Etymology

From New Latin, Nephrops, the type genus, from Greek nephros (kidney) and ops (appearance).
