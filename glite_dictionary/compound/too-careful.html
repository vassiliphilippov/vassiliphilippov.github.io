too-careful

1. ADJ

/ tuː ˈkɛr.fəl / (AmE), / tuː ˈkeə.fəl / (BrE)
Excessively or unnecessarily careful; taking more care than is needed or appropriate.
excessively careful

Examples

You shouldn't be too-careful when giving feedback; sometimes honesty is more helpful.
Her too-careful approach to driving made traffic back up behind her.
Managers who are too-careful may miss valuable opportunities by overanalyzing risks.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: careful - giving attention and thought to avoiding harm or errors
  • Synonym: overcautious - too cautious or wary
