network bridge

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɛtˌwɝk brɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈnɛtˌwɜːk brɪdʒ / (BrE)
A device used in computer networks to connect separate network segments, allowing them to communicate as a single network.
device connecting network segments

Examples

The IT team installed a network bridge to connect the two office subnets.
A network bridge can improve communication between different network segments.
By configuring a network bridge, you can easily combine wired and wireless networks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: switch - a network device for connecting multiple devices within a network
  • Synonym: router - a device that forwards data packets between computer networks

Etymology

Formed from 'network' (computing term for interconnected systems) and 'bridge' (something that connects or joins).
