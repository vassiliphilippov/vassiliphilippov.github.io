immunizing agent

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈmjunəˌzaɪzɪŋ ˈeɪdʒənt / (AmE), / ɪˈmjuːnʌɪzɪŋ ˈeɪdʒənt / (BrE)
a substance or organism that stimulates an immune response, producing immunity when introduced into the body
substance that induces immunity
medical

Examples

The new immunizing agent showed promising results in early clinical trials.
Polio was eradicated in many countries due to the widespread use of an effective immunizing agent.
Researchers are working to develop a safer immunizing agent for children.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vaccine - a preparation to stimulate the body's immune response to a disease
  • Synonym: antigen - a substance that induces an immune response
