Aesculapian tradition

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛskjəˈleɪpiən trəˈdɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌiːskjʊˈleɪpɪən trəˈdɪʃən / (BrE)
the system or practice of medicine and healing, especially as derived from or associated with classical traditions or the school of Asclepius
tradition in medicine and healing

Examples

The hospital maintained an Aesculapian tradition that dated back more than a century.
She wrote a thesis on the influence of the Aesculapian tradition on modern healthcare systems.
Many ancient remedies owe their origins to the Aesculapian tradition.

Synset Examples

Aesculapian traditions are important in holistic medicine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: medical tradition - established systems or customs in the practice of medicine

Etymology

From Latin 'Aesculapius', the Roman name for the Greek god of medicine, Asclepius, combined with 'tradition'.
