domino effect

1. NOUN

/ ˈdoʊmɪˌnoʊ ɪˌfɛkt / (AmE), / ˈdɒmɪnəʊ ɪˌfɛkt / (BrE)
A situation in which one event causes a series of similar events to happen one after another.
one event triggering a chain of events

Examples

The manager warned that a single factory closure could set off a domino effect across the entire industry.
After the power outage, there was a domino effect as more and more neighborhoods lost electricity.
One mistake in the system led to a domino effect of failures throughout the network.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chain reaction - a series of events, each caused by the previous one

Etymology

Metaphorical use based on the way toppling one domino causes an entire row to fall.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdoʊ.mɪˌnoʊ ɪˈfɛkt / (AmE), / ˈdɒm.ɪ.nəʊ ɪˈfɛkt / (BrE)
A situation in which one event causes a series of similar or related events to happen one after another.
series of events triggered by one incident

Examples

The announcement triggered a domino effect of changes across the industry.
When one supplier stopped delivering, it set off a domino effect that impacted every department.
Raising the price of fuel had a domino effect on transportation and the cost of goods.

Synset Examples

The policy changes created a domino effect in the market.
One domino fell, causing the rest to topple.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chain reaction - a sequence of events each caused by the previous one
  • Synonym: ripple effect - the continuing and spreading results of an event or action

Etymology

Metaphor from the falling of dominoes in a line, each knocking over the next.
