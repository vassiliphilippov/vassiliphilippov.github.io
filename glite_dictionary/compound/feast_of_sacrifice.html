Feast of Sacrifice

1. PROPN

/ fiːst əv səˈkrɪfaɪs / (AmE), / fiːst əv səˈkrɪfɪs / (BrE)
an important Islamic festival commemorating Abraham's willingness to sacrifice his son, observed by ritual animal sacrifice and giving to charity
major Islamic festival involving ritual sacrifice

Examples

Muslims around the world celebrate the Feast of Sacrifice each year to commemorate Abraham's devotion.
During the Feast of Sacrifice, families often share meat with the needy in their communities.
The Feast of Sacrifice is considered one of the two holiest days in the Islamic calendar.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Eid al-Adha - the Arabic name for the Feast of Sacrifice, the major Islamic festival of sacrifice
