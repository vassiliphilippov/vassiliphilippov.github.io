cinnamon vine

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪnəmən vaɪn / (AmE), / ˈsɪnəmən vaɪn / (BrE)
a perennial climbing plant (Dioscorea polystachya) native to China, grown for its glossy heart-shaped, cinnamon-scented leaves and edible tubers
a climbing plant with cinnamon-scented leaves and edible tubers

Examples

We planted a cinnamon vine along the fence to enjoy its fragrant leaves and edible roots.
The cinnamon vine is prized for both its ornamental qualities and its nutritious tubers.
Gardeners often choose the cinnamon vine for its ability to quickly cover walls with lush, heart-shaped foliage.
