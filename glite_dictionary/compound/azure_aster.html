azure aster

1. NOUN

/ ˈæʒər ˈæstər / (AmE), / ˈaʒə ˈɑːstə / (BrE)
a North American perennial flowering plant of the aster family, with purplish-blue or bluish-lavender ray florets (Symphyotrichum oolentangiense)
a blue-flowered North American aster

Examples

The azure aster brightens prairies with its vivid blue flowers in late summer.
We planted an azure aster in our garden to attract butterflies and bees.
A cluster of azure asters bloomed along the woodland trail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aster - a plant of the genus Aster, with star-shaped flowers

Etymology

Named for its azure (blue) flowers and aster, from Greek 'aster' meaning 'star', due to the star-like shape of the flowers.
