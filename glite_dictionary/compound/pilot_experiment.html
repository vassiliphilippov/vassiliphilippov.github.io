pilot experiment

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪlət ɪkˈspɛrɪmənt / (AmE), / ˈpaɪlət ɪkˈspɛrɪmənt / (BrE)
a small-scale preliminary study conducted to evaluate feasibility, time, cost, and risks before a full-scale research project
preliminary test study before main experiment

Examples

The team conducted a pilot experiment to determine whether their procedure was feasible.
Based on the results of the pilot experiment, the researchers adjusted their data collection methods.
Sometimes, a carefully designed pilot experiment can save significant time and resources later in the main study.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pilot study - a small-scale preliminary study conducted before main research

Etymology

From 'pilot' (trial, test) and 'experiment', modeled after 'pilot study'.
