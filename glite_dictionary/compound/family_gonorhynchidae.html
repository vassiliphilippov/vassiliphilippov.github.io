family Gonorhynchidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌfæməli ɡoʊnəˈrɪŋkɪdiː / (AmE), / ˌfæmɪli ɡɒnəˈrɪŋkɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of fishes containing only the genus Gonorhynchus, known as beaked sandfishes, found in marine environments.
taxonomic family of beaked sandfishes

Examples

The family Gonorhynchidae consists of only a few species of marine fish known as beaked sandfishes.
Ichthyologists have debated the classification of the family Gonorhynchidae due to its unique skeletal features.
The family Gonorhynchidae is distinguished by the beak-like shape of the fishes' snouts.

Etymology

From modern Latin 'Gonorhynchidae', based on the genus name 'Gonorhynchus'.
