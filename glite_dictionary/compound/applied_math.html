applied math

1. NOUN

/ əˈplaɪd mæθ / (AmE), / əˈplaɪd mæθ / (BrE)
the branch of mathematics concerned with mathematical methods that are used in practical fields such as science, engineering, business, or industry
mathematics used in practical fields

Examples

In college, I decided to major in applied math because I enjoyed solving real-world problems.
Her research in applied math led to new algorithms for analyzing biological data.
The company hired graduates with strong applied math backgrounds to improve their financial models.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: pure mathematics - mathematics studied for its theoretical development rather than practical application
