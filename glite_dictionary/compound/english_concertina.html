English concertina

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ kənˌsɚˈtinə / (AmE), / ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ kənˌsɜːˈtiːnə / (BrE)
a type of concertina musical instrument developed in England, distinguished by its hexagonal shape and button arrangement
English-developed concertina instrument

Examples

She bought an English concertina to play traditional folk tunes at the festival.
The English concertina is known for its distinctive button layout and mellow sound.
Several musicians at the session played the English concertina alongside fiddles and guitars.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: concertina - a free-reed musical instrument with buttons, similar to an accordion
