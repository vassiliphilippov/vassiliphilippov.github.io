arcus senilis

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑrkəs səˈnɪlɪs / (AmE), / ˈɑːkəs sɛˈnɪlɪs / (BrE)
A grayish or whitish arc-shaped deposit visible in the cornea, often seen in older adults and usually benign.
corneal arc-shaped deposit in older adults
medical

Examples

The ophthalmologist identified an arcus senilis in the patient's left eye during the examination.
Although arcus senilis is common in elderly individuals, it does not typically affect vision.
The presence of arcus senilis can sometimes indicate high cholesterol levels in younger patients.

Etymology

From Latin arcus (arch, arc) + senilis (of old age).
