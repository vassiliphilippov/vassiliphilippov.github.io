walleyed pike

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɑːlˌaɪd paɪk / (AmE), / ˈwɒl.aɪd paɪk / (BrE)
A freshwater fish found in North America, also known as the walleye, noted for its silvery eyes and pike-like appearance.
freshwater fish also called walleye

Examples

The walleyed pike is a popular catch among anglers in the Great Lakes region.
Many restaurants in Minnesota serve dishes featuring walleyed pike as a local delicacy.
Fishermen use special lures to attract walleyed pike during early spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: walleye - North American freshwater fish with silvery eyes
  • Synonym: pike - Large predatory freshwater fish

Etymology

Named for its prominent, cloudy ('walleyed') reflective eyes and its pike-like body shape.
