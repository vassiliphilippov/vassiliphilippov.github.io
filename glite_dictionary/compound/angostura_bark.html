angostura bark

1. NOUN

/ ˌæŋɡəˈstʊrə bɑrk / (AmE), / ˌæŋɡəˈstjʊərə bɑːk / (BrE)
the bitter bark of a South American tree (Galipea officinalis) used in medicines, liqueurs, and bitters
bitter bark used in medicines and liqueurs

Examples

The bartender added a pinch of angostura bark to the cocktail for its distinctive flavor.
Traditional healers have used angostura bark in remedies for centuries.
Genuine bitters are often made by infusing angostura bark with alcohol and spices.

Etymology

From the town of Angostura (now Ciudad Bolívar) in Venezuela, near where the tree was first described.
