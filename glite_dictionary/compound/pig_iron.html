pig iron

1. NOUN

/ pɪɡ ˈaɪərn / (AmE), / pɪɡ ˈaɪən / (BrE)
Crude iron that is tapped from a blast furnace and cast into blocks or ingots for further refining.
crude iron from a blast furnace

Examples

The foundry produced several tons of pig iron before refining it into steel.
Historically, pig iron was poured into sand molds to form bars for easy transport.
Modern steelmaking still begins with pig iron as an essential raw material.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cast iron - a hard alloy of iron suitable for casting
  • Antonym: wrought iron - iron with a low carbon content, tough and malleable, used before steel

Etymology

Named for the traditional casting process, reminiscent of piglets suckling a sow, where the branching molds resembled a litter of pigs.
