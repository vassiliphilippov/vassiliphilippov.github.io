genus Contopus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs kɒnˈtoʊ.pəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs kɒnˈtəʊ.pəs / (BrE)
A genus of small insect-eating birds in the tyrant flycatcher family, commonly known as pewees.
genus of birds called pewees

Examples

The genus Contopus comprises several species of pewees found in the Americas.
Birdwatchers often try to distinguish members of the genus Contopus by their distinctive calls.
Research on the genus Contopus has helped ornithologists understand migration patterns in flycatchers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pewee - a small American bird of the tyrant flycatcher family

Etymology

From Latin 'contopus,' used in scientific classification for this genus.
