Senna auriculata

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛnə ɔˌrɪkjəˈleɪtə / (AmE), / ˈsɛnə ɔːˌrɪkjʊˈleɪtə / (BrE)
an evergreen Indian shrub with bright yellow flowers, whose bark is used in tanning; sometimes classified in the genus Cassia
evergreen Indian shrub with yellow flowers

Examples

The bark of Senna auriculata is traditionally used in tanning and herbal medicine in India.
Botanists sometimes refer to Senna auriculata by its older name, Cassia auriculata.
During the monsoon, Senna auriculata produces a profusion of vivid yellow flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cassia auriculata - another scientific name for Senna auriculata, used in older classifications

Etymology

From the genus name 'Senna', derived from Arabic 'sanā', and the species name 'auriculata', from Latin meaning 'having ear-shaped appendages'.
