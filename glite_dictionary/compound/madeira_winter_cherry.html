Madeira winter cherry

1. NOUN

/ məˈdɛr.ə ˈwɪn.tɚ ˈtʃɛr.i / (AmE), / məˈdɪə.rə ˈwɪn.tə ˈtʃɛr.i / (BrE)
A small South American shrub grown as a houseplant for its abundant, ornamental but poisonous red or yellow cherry-sized fruit.
shrub with ornamental, poisonous fruit

Examples

The Madeira winter cherry is often displayed in homes despite its toxic fruit.
Gardeners admire the Madeira winter cherry for its bright, cherry-sized berries.
Because the Madeira winter cherry is poisonous, keep it away from children and pets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Jerusalem cherry - a related plant with similar ornamental fruit
