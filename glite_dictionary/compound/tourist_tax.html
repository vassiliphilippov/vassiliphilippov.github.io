tourist tax

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʊr.ɪst tæks / (AmE), / ˈtʊə.rɪst tæks / (BrE)
a tax imposed by local authorities on people staying in tourist accommodation, usually charged per night or per stay
tax charged to visitors in tourist accommodation

Examples

Many European cities have introduced a tourist tax to help fund local infrastructure.
The hotel receptionist explained that the tourist tax was not included in the room rate.
Visitors staying longer than one night must pay a daily tourist tax as required by law.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: occupancy tax - a tax levied on the occupancy of hotel or short-term rental rooms
  • Synonym: resort fee - an additional charge hotels or resorts may levy for amenities
