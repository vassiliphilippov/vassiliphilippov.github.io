reverse lightning

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈvɜrs ˈlaɪtnɪŋ / (AmE), / rɪˈvɜːs ˈlaɪtnɪŋ / (BrE)
a rare atmospheric phenomenon involving discharges lasting about 10 milliseconds, bursting from the tops of storm clouds in blue cones that widen as they flash upward
upward blue lightning from storm tops

Examples

Scientists observed reverse lightning shooting upward from the thunderstorm during the experiment.
Photographs captured the moment when reverse lightning illuminated the night sky above the city.
Researchers study reverse lightning to understand its role in the Earth's electrical system.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blue jet - a type of upward lightning from thunderstorm tops
