due west

1. NOUN

/ du ˈwɛst / (AmE), / djuː ˈwɛst / (BrE)
the direction corresponding to 270 degrees on a compass; directly west.
the direction directly west

Examples

We set out at dawn, keeping the boat headed due west across the lake.
The compass needle pointed due west as they walked along the desert trail.
The hikers followed the path due west until they reached the old lighthouse.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: due east - the direction corresponding to 90 degrees on a compass; directly east
  • Synonym: west - the direction toward the point of the horizon where the sun sets; 270 degrees

Etymology

From 'due' (completely, exactly) and 'west', indicating the precise direction.
