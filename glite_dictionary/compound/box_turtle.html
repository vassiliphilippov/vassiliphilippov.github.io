box turtle

1. NOUN

/ bɑks ˈtɝt̬əl / (AmE), / bɒks ˈtɜːtl̩ / (BrE)
A chiefly terrestrial North American turtle with a domed shell that can be closed tightly for protection.
North American turtle with closable shell

Examples

A box turtle can retract into its shell to protect itself from predators.
She found a box turtle crossing the hiking trail after the rain.
Many people keep box turtles as pets because of their gentle nature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: turtle - Any reptile of the order Testudines with a bony or leathery shell
