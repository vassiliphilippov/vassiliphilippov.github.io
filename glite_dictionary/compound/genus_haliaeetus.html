genus Haliaeetus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌhæliˈiːtəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌhæliˈiːtəs / (BrE)
A genus of large birds of prey in the family Accipitridae, known as sea eagles, found near large bodies of water.
genus of sea eagles (birds of prey)

Examples

The genus Haliaeetus includes several species of sea eagles found across the world.
Bald eagles and white-tailed eagles are both part of the genus Haliaeetus.
Ornithologists study the genus Haliaeetus to understand the evolution of large predatory birds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sea eagle - a large bird of prey that lives near water

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'haliaetos' ('sea eagle'): 'hali-' (sea) + 'aetos' (eagle).
