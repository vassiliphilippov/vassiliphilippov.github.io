putting iron

1. NOUN

/ ˈpʌtɪŋ ˌaɪrn / (AmE), / ˈpʌtɪŋ ˌaɪən / (BrE)
a type of golf club, similar to an iron, formerly used for putting on the green before the modern putter became standard
historical golf club for putting

Examples

In the early days of golf, players used a putting iron instead of the modern putter.
His grandfather's old putting iron is now a collector's item displayed in the clubhouse.
She learned about the putting iron while studying the history of golf clubs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: putter - a club used to make short, gentle strokes on the putting green

Etymology

From 'putting', the act of striking a golf ball gently on the green, and 'iron', denoting a type of golf club.
