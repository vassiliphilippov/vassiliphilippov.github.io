Parker House roll

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑrkər haʊs roʊl / (AmE), / ˈpɑːkə haʊs rəʊl / (BrE)
A soft, buttery dinner roll made from yeast dough, traditionally shaped by folding a round of dough in half before baking.
soft, folded yeast dinner roll

Examples

She served warm Parker House rolls with butter during Thanksgiving dinner.
The Parker House roll is known for its distinctive folded shape and soft texture.
He learned to bake Parker House rolls from his grandmother's old cookbook.

Etymology

Named after the Parker House Hotel in Boston, where the roll was created in the 19th century.
