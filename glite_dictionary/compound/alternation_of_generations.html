alternation of generations

1. NOUN

/ ˌɔltɚˈneɪʃən əv ˌdʒɛnɚˈeɪʃənz / (AmE), / ˌɔːltəˈneɪʃən əv ˌdʒɛnəˈreɪʃənz / (BrE)
the succession of two or more distinct forms, such as sexual and asexual phases, in the life cycle of a plant or animal
succession of different life cycle forms
medical

Examples

The alternation of generations is a key feature of the life cycles of many algae and plants.
Ferns exhibit alternation of generations, alternating between sporophyte and gametophyte stages.
Understanding the alternation of generations helps explain how mosses reproduce.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: metagenesis - alternation of sexual and asexual generations in animals or plants

Etymology

Based on Latin alternare (to alternate) and generatio (generation, production).
