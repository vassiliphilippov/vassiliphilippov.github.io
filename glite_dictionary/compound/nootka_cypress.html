Nootka cypress

1. NOUN

/ ˈnuːtkə ˌsaɪprəs / (AmE), / ˈnuːtkə ˌsɪprəs / (BrE)
a tall evergreen tree native to the Pacific coast of North America, often grown as an ornamental plant
tall evergreen tree of Pacific North America

Examples

The Nootka cypress is highly valued for its attractive foliage and durability.
Gardeners often plant Nootka cypress for its unique appearance and resilience to coastal winds.
The wood of the Nootka cypress is prized for building boats and outdoor furniture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cypress - any tree of the genus Cupressus, evergreen conifer with flattened sprays of scale-like leaves

Etymology

Named after Nootka Sound, a region on the Pacific coast of Canada, where the tree is native; 'cypress' from Old French 'cipres', from Latin 'cupressus'.
