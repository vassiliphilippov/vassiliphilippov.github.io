to take up the running

1. VERB

/ teɪk ʌp ðə ˈrʌnɪŋ / (AmE), / teɪk ʌp ðə ˈrʌnɪŋ / (BrE)
to assume responsibility for managing a task, project, or organization, especially after someone else has previously managed it.
assume responsibility for managing
british

Examples

When the project manager resigned, Julia agreed to take up the running of the team.
After his father's illness, he had to take up the running of the family restaurant.
If no one else volunteers, I suppose I'll have to take up the running myself.

Synset Examples

He decided to take up the running of the family business.
As the oldest sibling, I took up the running for our parents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to take over - to assume control or responsibility for something from someone else
  • Antonym: to hand over - to transfer responsibility or control to someone else
