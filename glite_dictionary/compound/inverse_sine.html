inverse sine

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪnˌvɝs ˈsaɪn / (AmE), / ˌɪnˌvɜːs ˈsaɪn / (BrE)
the function that gives the angle whose sine is a given number, usually denoted as arcsin
the angle whose sine is a given number (arcsin)

Examples

The inverse sine of 0.5 is 30 degrees, or π/6 radians.
To solve for the angle, take the inverse sine of the value you calculated.
Many calculators have a button for inverse sine, often labeled as 'arcsin'.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arcsine - the function that returns the angle whose sine is a given number
  • Antonym: sine - the trigonometric function relating a right triangle's angle to the ratio of the opposite side to the hypotenuse

Etymology

From Latin 'inversus' (turned upside down) and 'sine' (from Latin 'sinus', curve or fold), referring to the reversal of the sine function.
