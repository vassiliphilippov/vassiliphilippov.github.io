vaginal discharge

1. NOUN

/ vəˈdʒaɪ.nəl dɪsˈtʃɑrʤ / (AmE), / vəˈdʒaɪ.nəl dɪsˈtʃɑːdʒ / (BrE)
the secretion of fluid from the vagina, typically clear or white, produced by the cervical glands
fluid secreted from the vagina
medical

Examples

Many women experience changes in vaginal discharge throughout their menstrual cycle.
If you notice unusual odor or color in your vaginal discharge, consult a healthcare provider.
Normal vaginal discharge is typically clear or white and does not have a strong smell.
