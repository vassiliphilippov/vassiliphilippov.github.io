genus Trigonella

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌtrɪ.ɡəˈnɛl.ə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌtrɪ.ɡəˈnɛl.ə / (BrE)
A genus of aromatic herbs native to the Old World, including species such as fenugreek.
a genus of aromatic Old World herbs

Examples

The genus Trigonella includes fenugreek, an herb widely used in cooking and medicine.
Researchers are studying various species within the genus Trigonella for their potential health benefits.
Many plants in the genus Trigonella are valued for their aromatic properties and agricultural uses.

Etymology

From Latin 'trigonum' (triangle), referring to the triangular shape of some seed pods.
