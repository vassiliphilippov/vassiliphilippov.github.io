ready-to-wear

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɛdi tə ˈwɛr / (AmE), / ˌrɛdi tə ˈweə / (BrE)
clothing that is mass-produced in standard sizes for sale to the general public, rather than made to order
mass-produced, off-the-rack clothing

Examples

She prefers to buy ready-to-wear rather than having her dresses custom made.
Most department stores offer a wide variety of ready-to-wear for every occasion.
The fashion show featured the designer's latest ready-to-wear collection.

Synset Examples

she couldn't find anything in ready-to-wear that she liked

Related Terms

  • Synonym: off-the-rack - clothing that is ready-made and available in stores
  • Antonym: tailor-made - clothing made according to an individual's measurements

2. ADJ

/ ˌrɛdi tə ˈwɛr / (AmE), / ˌrɛdi tə ˈwɛə / (BrE)
(especially of clothing) made in standard sizes and available from merchandise in stock, rather than being custom-made.
clothing made in standard sizes, not custom-made

Examples

He prefers to buy ready-to-wear suits rather than ordering custom-tailored ones.
Many department stores carry a large selection of ready-to-wear dresses for every occasion.
The designer launched a new ready-to-wear collection at Fashion Week.

Synset Examples

ready-to-wear clothes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: off-the-rack - (of clothes) made in standard sizes and not tailored to fit a particular person
  • Antonym: custom-made - (of clothes) made specifically for an individual customer

Etymology

From 'ready' + 'to wear', indicating clothing immediately available for wearing.
