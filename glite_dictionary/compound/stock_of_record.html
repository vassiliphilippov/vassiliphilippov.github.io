stock of record

1. NOUN

/ stɑk əv ˈrɛkərd / (AmE), / stɒk əv ˈrɛkɔːd / (BrE)
Stock owned by shareholders whose names are officially registered on a company's records on a specific date, often to determine eligibility for dividends or other rights.
stock registered to shareholders on a given date

Examples

Only holders of stock of record on March 31st will receive the declared dividend.
It is important to become a stock of record holder before the record date to qualify for voting rights.
When the company announced its merger, only those with stock of record were eligible for the special meeting.
