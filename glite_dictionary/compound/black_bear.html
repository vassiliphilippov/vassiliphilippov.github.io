black bear

1. NOUN

/ ˈblæk bɛr / (AmE), / ˈblæk beə / (BrE)
A medium-sized North American bear (Ursus americanus) with fur that is usually black or dark brown; less aggressive and smaller than the brown bear.
North American bear species, usually black

Examples

On our camping trip, we spotted a black bear rummaging near the edge of the forest.
The black bear is often found in wooded areas across North America.
Park rangers warn visitors to secure their food to avoid attracting black bears.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: brown bear - a large bear species (Ursus arctos) found in North America and Eurasia

2. NOUN

/ blæk bɛr / (AmE), / blæk beə / (BrE)
a bear with a black coat found in central and eastern Asia, known for its relatively small size and distinctive color
Asian bear species with black coat

Examples

The black bear is native to the forests of central and eastern Asia.
Unlike the American black bear, the Asian black bear often has a light patch on its chest.
Wildlife researchers observed a group of black bears feeding near the river.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: American black bear - a species of black bear native to North America
