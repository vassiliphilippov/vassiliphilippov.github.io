creeping spike rush

1. NOUN

/ ˈkripɪŋ spaɪk rʌʃ / (AmE), / ˈkriːpɪŋ spaɪk rʌʃ / (BrE)
A sedge with cylindrical stems, commonly found in wetlands and known for its spreading habit.
wetland sedge with cylindrical stems

Examples

The pond was fringed with creeping spike rush and other aquatic plants.
Restoration projects often include planting creeping spike rush to prevent soil erosion.
Botanists identified the plant as creeping spike rush during the survey.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sedge - grass-like plant of the family Cyperaceae, often found in wetlands
