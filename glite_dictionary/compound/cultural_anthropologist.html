cultural anthropologist

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌltʃɚəl ˌænθrəˈpɑlədʒɪst / (AmE), / ˈkʌltʃərəl ˌænθrəˈpɒlədʒɪst / (BrE)
a specialist in anthropology who studies the cultural aspects of human societies, such as beliefs, practices, kinship systems, and customs
specialist who studies cultural aspects of societies

Examples

As a cultural anthropologist, she spent years researching wedding rituals in rural villages.
Many cultural anthropologists study the ways in which communities adapt to globalization.
The university invited a cultural anthropologist to lecture on kinship systems in Southeast Asia.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: anthropologist - a person who studies human societies and cultures
  • Antonym: archaeologist - a specialist in archaeology, the study of ancient societies through material remains

Etymology

From 'culture' (Latin 'cultura', cultivation) and 'anthropologist' (from Greek 'anthrōpos', human, and 'logos', study).
