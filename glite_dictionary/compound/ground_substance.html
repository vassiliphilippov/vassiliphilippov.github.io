ground substance

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡraʊnd ˌsʌbstəns / (AmE), / ˈɡraʊnd ˌsʌbstəns / (BrE)
the amorphous material in which cells and fibers are embedded in connective tissue
material embedding tissue cells
medical

Examples

The connective tissue matrix consists of cells, fibers, and ground substance.
The ground substance in cartilage is rich in proteoglycans.
Abnormalities in the ground substance can interfere with tissue repair.

Etymology

From 'ground' (foundation) + 'substance', referring to its role as the matrix in tissues.

2. NOUN

/ ɡraʊnd ˈsʌbstəns / (AmE), / ɡraʊnd ˈsʌbstəns / (BrE)
the clear, non-granular material in the cytoplasm of a cell, in which other cell components are suspended
clear material in cell cytoplasm
medical

Examples

The ground substance in the cytoplasm helps maintain the cell's internal environment.
Microscopic analysis revealed that the ground substance was abundant in the tissue sample.
Changes in the composition of ground substance can affect cell function and structure.
