broomcorn millet

1. NOUN

/ ˈbruːmˌkɔrn ˈmɪlɪt / (AmE), / ˈbruːmkɔːn ˈmɪlɪt / (BrE)
a species of millet cultivated mainly in Europe and Asia for its grain, and sometimes in the US for forage
a species of millet grown for grain or forage

Examples

Farmers in northern China often plant broomcorn millet because it matures quickly in dry conditions.
The grains of broomcorn millet are used in traditional European porridge dishes.
In some regions, broomcorn millet is grown as a forage crop for livestock.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: millet - any of various small-seeded grasses grown for food
