Spanish lime

1. NOUN

/ ˈspænɪʃ laɪm / (AmE), / ˈspænɪʃ laɪm / (BrE)
a small Caribbean fruit with green leathery skin and sweet, juicy translucent pulp, eaten like grapes
a small Caribbean fruit with green skin and juicy pulp

Examples

I had never tasted a Spanish lime until my trip to Puerto Rico.
Vendors were selling bags of fresh Spanish limes on the beach.
The sweet pulp of the Spanish lime is delicious and refreshing.

2. NOUN

/ ˈspænɪʃ laɪm / (AmE), / ˈspænɪʃ laɪm / (BrE)
A tropical American tree (Melicoccus bijugatus) that bears a small edible fruit with green leathery skin and sweet, juicy translucent pulp.
tropical fruit tree with edible green fruit

Examples

We bought a bag of Spanish limes from the market and enjoyed their sweet, tangy flavor.
The Spanish lime is popular in many Caribbean countries as a summer snack.
She showed us how to peel the Spanish lime and eat the juicy pulp inside.
