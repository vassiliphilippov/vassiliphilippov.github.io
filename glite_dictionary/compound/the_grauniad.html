the Grauniad

1. PROPN

/ ði ˈɡrɑːniæd / (AmE), / ði ˈɡrɑːniæd / (BrE)
A humorous, informal nickname for The Guardian newspaper, originally referencing its reputation for typographical errors.
jocular nickname for The Guardian
informal british

Examples

Many journalists joke about getting published in the Grauniad at least once in their careers.
He buys the Grauniad every Saturday for the cultural supplement.
Social media users often refer to the Grauniad when discussing UK media bias.

Synset Examples

He reads the Grauniad for its unique perspective on news.
She prefers the Grauniad for its investigative journalism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: The Guardian - British national daily newspaper

Etymology

From a deliberate misspelling of 'The Guardian', popularized in Private Eye magazine, mocking the newspaper's history of typographical errors.
