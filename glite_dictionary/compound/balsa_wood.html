balsa wood

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑl.sə wʊd / (AmE), / ˈbæl.sə wʊd / (BrE)
a strong and very lightweight wood from the balsa tree, often used for model making, floats, and insulation
very lightweight wood from the balsa tree

Examples

The model airplane is constructed entirely from balsa wood because of its light weight.
Many life jackets contain balsa wood to help them float in water.
He carved the figure from a block of balsa wood with ease.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: balsa - the balsa tree, Ochroma pyramidale, source of balsa wood

Etymology

From Spanish 'balsa' meaning 'raft', referencing the wood's use in rafts.
