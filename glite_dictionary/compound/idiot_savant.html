idiot savant

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪdiət sæˈvɑnt / (AmE), / ˌɪdɪət sæˈvɒ̃ / (BrE)
an outdated term for a person with severe intellectual disability who shows extraordinary ability in a specific area, such as memory or calculation
person with intellectual disability showing special skill
archaic offensive

Examples

The film depicted a character who was an idiot savant with extraordinary mathematical abilities.
Although he was labeled an idiot savant, his art astonished all who saw it.
The term idiot savant is now considered outdated and offensive by many professionals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: savant syndrome - condition in which a person with a developmental disorder has exceptional skills
  • Synonym: autistic savant - person with autism who demonstrates profound capability in a specific area

Etymology

From French, 'idiot' (uneducated person) + 'savant' (learned person); now considered outdated and offensive.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪdiət səˈvɑnt / (AmE), / ˌɪdiət sæˈvɑ̃ / (BrE)
a person who, despite significant mental or developmental disabilities, shows extraordinary skill or brilliance in a specific area, such as mathematics or music
person with remarkable skill in one area despite disabilities
offensive archaic

Examples

The young man was considered an idiot savant for his ability to play complex piano pieces from memory.
Although he struggled with daily tasks, his photographic memory marked him as an idiot savant among his peers.
In the film, the main character is portrayed as an idiot savant with extraordinary calculation skills.

Synset Examples

Her skills in math make her an idiot savant in the classroom.
Despite his social awkwardness, he’s an idiot savant in coding.
He’s an idiot savant when it comes to memorizing facts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: savant - a learned person, especially one distinguished in a particular field

Etymology

From French 'savant' (learned) and English 'idiot'; term first used in the late 19th century.
