chain pickerel

1. NOUN

/ tʃeɪn ˈpɪkərəl / (AmE), / tʃeɪn ˈpɪkərəl / (BrE)
a freshwater fish of the pike family, native to eastern North America, noted for its elongated body and chain-like markings
a North American freshwater pike

Examples

The chain pickerel is prized by anglers for its spirited fight.
We spotted a large chain pickerel swimming near the reeds.
Because of its distinctive markings, the chain pickerel is easy to identify.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pickerel - a young or small pike; also any of several pike species
