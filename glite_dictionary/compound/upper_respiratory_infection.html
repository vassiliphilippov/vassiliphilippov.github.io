upper respiratory infection

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌpɚ rɪˈspɪrəˌtɔri ɪnˈfɛkʃən / (AmE), / ˈʌpə rɪˈspɪrəˌtəri ɪnˈfɛkʃən / (BrE)
an infection affecting the upper part of the respiratory tract, such as the nose, throat, or sinuses
infection of the nose, throat, or upper airway
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed her with an upper respiratory infection after she complained of a sore throat and congestion.
Children are especially susceptible to getting an upper respiratory infection during the winter months.
Common symptoms of an upper respiratory infection include sneezing, coughing, and a runny nose.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cold - a common viral infection of the upper respiratory tract
