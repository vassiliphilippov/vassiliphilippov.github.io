Barents Sea

1. PROPN

/ ˈbɑːrənts si / (AmE), / ˈbærənts siː / (BrE)
the part of the Arctic Ocean located north of Norway and Russia, known for its cold climate and rich marine life
sea north of Norway and Russia

Examples

The Barents Sea is an important area for commercial fishing and marine research.
Icebreakers travel through the Barents Sea during Arctic expeditions.
Oil and gas exploration has increased dramatically in the Barents Sea region.

Etymology

Named after Dutch navigator Willem Barentsz (c. 1550–1597).
