musculus teres minor

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌs.kjə.ləs ˌtɛr.iːz ˈmaɪ.nɚ / (AmE), / ˈmʌs.kjʊ.ləs ˌtɛər.iːz ˈmɪ.nə / (BrE)
A small, rounded muscle of the shoulder that helps adduct the arm and rotate it laterally.
shoulder muscle for arm adduction and lateral rotation
medical

Examples

A tear in the musculus teres minor can lead to pain and limited shoulder movement.
Physical therapy exercises can help strengthen the musculus teres minor after an injury.
The musculus teres minor works alongside other muscles to stabilize the shoulder joint.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rotator cuff - group of muscles and tendons stabilizing the shoulder
  • Degree Higher: musculus teres major - larger rounded muscle of the shoulder

Etymology

From Latin, 'musculus' meaning 'muscle' and 'teres minor' meaning 'smaller rounded'.
