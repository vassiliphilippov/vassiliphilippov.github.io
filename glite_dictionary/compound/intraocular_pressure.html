intraocular pressure

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.trəˈɑː.kjə.lɚ ˈprɛʃ.ɚ / (AmE), / ˌɪn.trəˈɒk.jʊ.lə ˈprɛʃ.ə / (BrE)
the pressure exerted by the fluids within the eyeball, maintained by the balance of aqueous humor production and drainage
pressure inside the eyeball
medical

Examples

Elevated intraocular pressure can increase the risk of developing glaucoma.
Regular eye exams can help monitor intraocular pressure and detect problems early.
The doctor measured the patient's intraocular pressure during the eye checkup.

Synset Examples

glaucoma can result from increased intraocular pressure

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ocular pressure - pressure within the eyeball
  • Degree Higher: glaucoma - a disease linked to increased intraocular pressure

Etymology

From Latin 'intra' (within) + 'ocular' (of the eye) + 'pressure'.
