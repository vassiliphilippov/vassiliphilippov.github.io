dark chapter

1. NOUN

/ dɑrk ˈʧæp.tɚ / (AmE), / dɑːk ˈtʃæp.tə / (BrE)
a period in the past marked by troubling or painful events, often referred to in the context of history or personal experience
troubling or painful period in history or life

Examples

Many consider the economic depression of the 1930s to be a dark chapter in American history.
The scandal marked a dark chapter in the company's otherwise successful record.
Her struggle with illness was a dark chapter in an otherwise happy childhood.

Synset Examples

Slavery is a dark chapter in human history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unhappy time - a time of sadness or distress; similar meaning
  • Antonym: golden age - a period of great happiness, prosperity, or achievement; opposite meaning
