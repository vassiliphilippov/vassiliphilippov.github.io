Kussmaul's coma

1. NOUN

/ ˈkuːsmaʊlz ˈkoʊmə / (AmE), / ˈkʊsmaʊlz ˈkəʊmə / (BrE)
A type of coma that occurs as a serious complication in cases of inadequately treated diabetes mellitus.
diabetic coma caused by untreated diabetes
medical

Examples

Ketoacidosis can progress to Kussmaul's coma if left untreated in diabetic patients.
The doctor explained the dangers of Kussmaul's coma to the patient's family.
Prompt medical intervention is needed to prevent Kussmaul's coma in severe diabetes cases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diabetic coma - a coma resulting from severe diabetic complications

Etymology

Named after German physician Adolf Kussmaul (1822–1902), who described the condition.
