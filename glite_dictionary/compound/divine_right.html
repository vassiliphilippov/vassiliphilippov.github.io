divine right

1. NOUN

/ dəˈvaɪn raɪt / (AmE), / dɪˈvaɪn raɪt / (BrE)
the doctrine that monarchs derive their authority to rule directly from God and are not answerable to their subjects
doctrine of monarchs' authority from God

Examples

The concept of divine right was invoked by rulers to legitimize their reign.
Many philosophers in the Enlightenment challenged the idea of divine right.
The Stuart kings in England famously claimed divine right as the basis for their authority.

Synset Examples

the doctrine of the divine right of kings was enunciated by the Stuarts in Britain in the 16th century

Related Terms

  • Synonym: absolute monarchy - system of government where the monarch has unrestricted power
  • Antonym: constitutional monarchy - system where a monarch's powers are limited by a constitution
