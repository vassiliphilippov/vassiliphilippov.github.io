doctoral dissertation

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɑktɚəl ˌdɪsɚˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈdɒktərəl ˌdɪsəˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
a lengthy, formal piece of written research submitted by a candidate for a doctoral degree at a university
written research for a doctoral degree

Examples

She spent three years conducting research for her doctoral dissertation on climate change policies.
The library archives hold hundreds of doctoral dissertations from former graduate students.
His doctoral dissertation was later published as a book on political philosophy.

Synset Examples

The term graduate thesis is sometimes used to refer to both master's theses and doctoral dissertations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thesis - a long essay or dissertation involving personal research, typically for a university degree

Etymology

From Latin 'doctor' (teacher, scholar) and Latin 'dissertatio' (discussion, discourse).
