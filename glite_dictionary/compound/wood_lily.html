wood lily

1. NOUN

/ wʊd ˈlɪli / (AmE), / wʊd ˈlɪli / (BrE)
A lily native to eastern North America, having orange to orange-red flowers with purple spots.
North American orange-red spotted lily

Examples

The wood lily brightens up forest clearings with its vibrant orange-red petals.
During our hike, we spotted a rare wood lily growing near the trail.
Gardeners value the wood lily for its striking, purple-speckled flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lily - any plant of the genus Lilium with large prominent flowers

2. NOUN

/ wʊd ˈlɪli / (AmE), / wʊd ˈlɪli / (BrE)
a perennial plant of the genus Trillium, having a whorl of three leaves at the top of the stem and a single three-petaled flower, found in woodland areas
a woodland Trillium plant with three-petaled flower

Examples

We found a beautiful wood lily blooming near the forest edge during our hike.
The wood lily is admired for its striking three-petaled flower and elegant appearance.
Gardeners often look for the wood lily to add to their shade-loving plant collections.
