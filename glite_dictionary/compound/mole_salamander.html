mole salamander

1. NOUN

/ moʊl ˈsæləˌmændɚ / (AmE), / məʊl ˈsæləˌmændə / (BrE)
A brownish-black burrowing salamander native to the southeastern United States.
a burrowing salamander species

Examples

The mole salamander spends much of its life underground, emerging mainly to breed.
Researchers tracked the movements of a mole salamander population in Georgia.
Unlike other salamanders, the mole salamander prefers moist environments under leaf litter or logs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: salamander - an amphibian with a lizard-like appearance, belonging to the order Urodela
