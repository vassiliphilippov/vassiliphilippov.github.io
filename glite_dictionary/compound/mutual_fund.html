mutual fund

1. NOUN

/ ˈmjuːtʃuəl fʌnd / (AmE), / ˈmjuːtʃuəl fʌnd / (BrE)
an investment fund that pools money from many investors to purchase a diversified portfolio of securities
investment fund pooling investors' money

Examples

She invested her savings in a mutual fund to diversify her portfolio.
The mutual fund saw steady growth over the past five years.
Many people choose mutual funds for their retirement accounts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: investment fund - a fund that collects investors' money to invest in various assets
  • Degree Higher: hedge fund - a private investment fund with advanced strategies

Etymology

From 'mutual' (shared by all) and 'fund' (sum of money), first used in the US in the 20th century for pooled investment vehicles.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmjuː.tʃu.əl fʌnd / (AmE), / ˈmjuː.tju.əl fʌnd / (BrE)
an investment vehicle consisting of a pool of funds collected from many investors, managed by professionals, that invests in stocks, bonds, or other securities and regularly sells and redeems shares to investors
pooled investment fund managed by professionals

Examples

She invested most of her savings in a mutual fund to diversify her portfolio.
Many people choose a mutual fund for its professional management and lower risk.
The value of a mutual fund can fluctuate depending on the market performance of its assets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: investment fund - a fund that pools money to invest in securities
