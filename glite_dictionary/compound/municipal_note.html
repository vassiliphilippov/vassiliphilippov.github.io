municipal note

1. NOUN

/ məˈnɪs.ə.pəl noʊt / (AmE), / mjuːˈnɪs.ɪ.pəl nəʊt / (BrE)
a short-term debt security issued by a local government, typically maturing in less than two years
short-term municipal debt security

Examples

The city issued a municipal note to finance urgent infrastructure repairs.
Investors often consider a municipal note for its short maturity and relatively low risk.
That municipal note will mature in just eighteen months, providing quick access to capital.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: municipal bond - a debt security issued by a local government, typically longer-term
