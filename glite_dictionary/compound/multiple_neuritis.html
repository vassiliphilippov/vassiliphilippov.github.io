multiple neuritis

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌltəpəl nʊˈraɪtəs / (AmE), / ˈmʌltɪpl njuːˈraɪtɪs / (BrE)
inflammation of many or all of the peripheral nerves in the body, often resulting in muscle weakness and sensory disturbances
inflammation of multiple peripheral nerves
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with multiple neuritis caused by a severe vitamin deficiency.
Symptoms of multiple neuritis may include numbness, tingling, and loss of muscle control.
Leprosy can sometimes lead to multiple neuritis, resulting in widespread nerve damage.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: neuritis - inflammation of a single nerve or group of nerves

Etymology

From Latin 'neuri-' (nerve) and '-itis' (inflammation), with 'multiple' from Latin 'multiplex'.
