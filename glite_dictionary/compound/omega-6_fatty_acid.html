omega-6 fatty acid

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊməɡə ˈsɪks ˈfæti ˌæsɪd / (AmE), / ˌəʊmɪɡə ˈsɪks ˈfæti ˌæsɪd / (BrE)
a type of polyunsaturated fatty acid in which the first double bond occurs at the sixth carbon atom from the end of the carbon chain, important for human nutrition but must be obtained from the diet
type of essential polyunsaturated fatty acid
medical

Examples

Consuming foods rich in omega-6 fatty acid is important for maintaining healthy cell membranes.
Sunflower oil is known for its high omega-6 fatty acid content.
An imbalance between omega-6 fatty acid and omega-3 fatty acid intake may affect inflammation in the body.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: omega-3 fatty acid - a polyunsaturated fatty acid with the first double bond at the third carbon atom

Etymology

From omega (the last letter of the Greek alphabet, used to indicate the position of the double bond) and 6 (the carbon position), plus 'fatty acid'.
