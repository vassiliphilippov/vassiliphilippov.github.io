elkhorn fern

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛlkˌhɔrn ˌfɜrn / (AmE), / ˈɛlkˌhɔːn ˌfɜːn / (BrE)
a large epiphytic fern of Australia, southeastern Asia, and Polynesia, with fronds shaped like elk antlers, often cultivated as an ornamental plant
ornamental fern with antler-shaped fronds

Examples

The elkhorn fern thrives in humid, shaded environments and is popular in tropical gardens.
She mounted an elkhorn fern on a piece of driftwood for a striking display in her living room.
Botanical gardens often feature the elkhorn fern among their collection of exotic plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: staghorn fern - a similar epiphytic fern with fronds resembling a stag's antlers
