European water shrew

1. NOUN

/ jʊˈɹoʊpiən ˈwɔtər ʃru / (AmE), / jʊəˈɹəʊpiən ˈwɔːtə ʃruː / (BrE)
a species of shrew (Neomys fodiens) found widely across Europe, typically inhabiting wetlands and water margins
a European aquatic shrew species

Examples

The European water shrew is recognizable by the fringe of stiff hairs on its feet.
Researchers found a European water shrew near the riverbank during their survey.
Unlike other shrews, the European water shrew spends much of its time in or near freshwater.
