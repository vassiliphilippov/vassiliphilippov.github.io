pheasant coucal

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɛzənt ˈkuːkəl / (AmE), / ˈfɛzənt ˈkuːkəl / (BrE)
A large Australian bird of the cuckoo family, with a long tail resembling that of a pheasant.
Australian bird with a long, pheasant-like tail

Examples

The pheasant coucal is often seen walking on the ground rather than flying.
In the early morning, a pheasant coucal called from the tall grass near the creek.
Unlike typical cuckoos, the pheasant coucal builds its own nest and raises its own young.
