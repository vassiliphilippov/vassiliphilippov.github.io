family Cyrilliaceae

1. NOUN

/ sɪˌrɪliˈeɪsiːˌiː / (AmE), / sɪˌrɪlɪˈeɪsɪˌiː / (BrE)
a family of shrubs and trees with leathery leaves and small white flowers in racemes, including the genera Cyrilla and Cliftonia
botanical family of shrubs and trees

Examples

The family Cyrilliaceae includes several evergreen shrubs native to the southeastern United States.
Botanists have reclassified some species, moving them into the family Cyrilliaceae.
Plants in the family Cyrilliaceae often thrive in wetland environments.

Etymology

From the genus name 'Cyrilla', combined with the standard botanical family suffix '-aceae'.
