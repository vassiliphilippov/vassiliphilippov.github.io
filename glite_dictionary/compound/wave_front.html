wave front

1. NOUN

/ weɪv frʌnt / (AmE), / weɪv frʌnt / (BrE)
A surface composed of all points just reached by a propagating wave at a given instant.
surface just reached by a wave

Examples

The wave front of the earthquake reached the city within seconds.
Scientists mapped the wave front to understand the spread of the sound in water.
As the light passed through the lens, its wave front was altered.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wave - a disturbance that transfers energy through matter or space

2. NOUN

/ ˈweɪv frʌnt / (AmE), / ˈweɪv frʌnt / (BrE)
an imaginary surface in a medium joining all points reached simultaneously by a propagating wave, such as sound or light
surface reached at same time by a wave

Examples

The light wave front was distorted as it passed through the turbulent atmosphere.
A sound wave front spreads out in all directions from the explosion.
Engineers analyzed the wave front to determine the speed of the signal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wave - an oscillation or disturbance that transfers energy

Etymology

From 'wave' (oscillation in medium) + 'front' (foremost part); used in physics since the 19th century.
