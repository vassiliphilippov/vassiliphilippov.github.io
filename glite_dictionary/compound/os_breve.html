os breve

1. NOUN

/ oʊs ˈbriːvi / (AmE), / ɒs ˈbreːviː / (BrE)
A bone that is approximately equal in length, width, and thickness; a short bone, such as those found in the wrist and ankle.
a bone of roughly equal dimensions
medical

Examples

The carpal bones of the wrist are classified as os breve in human anatomy.
Medical students must learn the differences between os breve and other types of bones.
The term os breve is often used when discussing skeletal structure in vertebrates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: short bone - a bone of approximately equal dimension in all directions

Etymology

From Latin 'os' (bone) and 'breve' (short).
