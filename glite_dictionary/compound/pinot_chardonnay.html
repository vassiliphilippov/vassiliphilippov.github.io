Pinot Chardonnay

1. NOUN

/ pɪˌnoʊ ʃɑːr.dəˈneɪ / (AmE), / ˈpiː.nəʊ ʃɑː.də.neɪ / (BrE)
a dry white table wine similar to Chablis, made from Chardonnay grapes
dry white wine from Chardonnay grapes

Examples

Many sommeliers recommend a Pinot Chardonnay as a refreshing accompaniment to seafood dishes.
The restaurant boasts an impressive selection of Pinot Chardonnay from various vineyards.
We enjoyed a crisp glass of Pinot Chardonnay on the terrace overlooking the lake.

Etymology

From French 'Pinot' (a grape variety name) and 'Chardonnay' (another grape variety).
