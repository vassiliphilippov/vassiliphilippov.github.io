fringed pink

1. NOUN

/ frɪndʒd pɪŋk / (AmE), / frɪndʒd pɪŋk / (BrE)
a low, wiry-stemmed plant native to southern California, characterized by its fringed pink flowers
plant with fringed pink flowers

Examples

The fringed pink blooms beautifully in spring along the Californian coast.
Gardeners prize the fringed pink for its delicate, lacy petals.
We identified the wildflower as a rare fringed pink growing near the trail.

2. NOUN

/ frɪndʒd pɪŋk / (AmE), / frɪn(d)ʒd pɪŋk / (BrE)
a Eurasian perennial plant of the pink family (Dianthus superbus), bearing fragrant lilac or rose-coloured flowers with deeply fringed petals
a Eurasian pink-flowered perennial plant

Examples

The fringed pink is cherished for its beautifully fringed petals and sweet fragrance.
Gardeners often plant fringed pink in borders to add a splash of color and scent.
The delicate blooms of the fringed pink attract butterflies and bees in the summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Dianthus - the genus including pinks and carnations
