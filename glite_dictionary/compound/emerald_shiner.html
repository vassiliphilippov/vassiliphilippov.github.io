emerald shiner

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛmərəld ˈʃaɪnər / (AmE), / ˈɛmərəld ˈʃaɪnə / (BrE)
a small North American freshwater fish with a blunt nose and distinctive greenish sheen, found in the Great Lakes and Mississippi Valley
a small greenish North American fish

Examples

Fishermen often use emerald shiners as bait when fishing in the lakes.
A school of emerald shiners flashed beneath the surface of the river.
The emerald shiner is easily identified by its bright, greenish appearance.
