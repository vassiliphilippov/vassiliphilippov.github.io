Medieval Greek

1. NOUN

/ məˈdiːviəl ˈɡrik / (AmE), / mɪˌdiːvɪəl ˈɡriːk / (BrE)
The stage of the Greek language spoken and written approximately from 600 to 1200 AD, bridging the period between Ancient and Modern Greek.
historical stage of the Greek language (c. 600–1200 AD)

Examples

Scholars study Medieval Greek to understand the evolution of the language between ancient and modern times.
Many important Byzantine texts were written in Medieval Greek.
Her research paper analyzes religious manuscripts composed in Medieval Greek.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Ancient Greek - Greek language used from circa 9th century BC to 6th century AD
  • Degree Higher: Modern Greek - The form of Greek used from the 15th century AD to the present

Etymology

From 'medieval,' meaning Middle Ages, and 'Greek.'
