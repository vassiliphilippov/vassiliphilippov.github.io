commander in chief

1. NOUN

/ kəˈmændər ɪn ˈtʃif / (AmE), / kəˈmɑːndə ɪn ˈtʃiːf / (BrE)
the person who holds the highest authority and supreme command over a nation's armed forces
supreme military commander

Examples

The President acts as commander in chief of the armed forces during times of war.
As commander in chief, she has the final say on all military operations.
Under the constitution, only the commander in chief can authorize such actions.

Synset Examples

in the U.S. the president is the commander in chief

Related Terms

  • Synonym: generalissimo - military commander with supreme authority

Etymology

From 'commander' (one who commands) and 'chief' (leader), dating to the 17th century for military use.
