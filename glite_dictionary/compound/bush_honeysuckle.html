bush honeysuckle

1. NOUN

/ bʊʃ ˈhʌniˌsʌkəl / (AmE), / bʊʃ ˈhʌniˌsʌkl̩ / (BrE)
A shrub native to the southeastern United States, characterized by large, dense clusters of sulfur-yellow flowers.
shrub with yellow flowers, southeastern US

Examples

The garden was bordered by bush honeysuckle, adding bright yellow color in spring.
Wildlife are often attracted to the bush honeysuckle for its fragrant blooms.
After planting a bush honeysuckle, she noticed more butterflies in her yard.

3. NOUN

/ bʊʃ ˈhʌniˌsʌkəl / (AmE), / bʊʃ ˈhʌniˌsʌkl̩ / (BrE)
A shrub of the honeysuckle family (genus Lonicera), native to southern Russia to central Asia, known for its bushy growth habit.
a honeysuckle shrub native to Eurasia

Examples

The garden was bordered by a dense row of bush honeysuckle, providing both shade and privacy.
Invasive bush honeysuckle species can quickly overtake native plants in woodlands.
We planted a bush honeysuckle for its fragrant blossoms and hardiness in colder climates.
