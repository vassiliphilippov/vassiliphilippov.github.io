present simple

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɛz.ənt ˈsɪm.pəl / (AmE), / ˈprɛz.ənt ˈsɪm.pəl / (BrE)
the verb tense used to express actions, events, or conditions that regularly happen, are facts, or exist now
verb tense for regular actions or current facts

Examples

Many English learners find the present simple tense easier to use than other forms.
You use the present simple for things like routines and general truths.
In the present simple, the verb doesn't change for all subjects except the third person singular.

Synset Examples

The sentences ‘I live in Madrid’, ‘She doesn't like cheese’, and ‘I think you're wrong’ are all in the present simple.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: present continuous - verb tense for actions happening right now
  • Antonym: past simple - verb tense for completed past actions

Etymology

Formed from 'present' (as in present tense) and 'simple', distinguishing it from other present tenses in English grammar.
