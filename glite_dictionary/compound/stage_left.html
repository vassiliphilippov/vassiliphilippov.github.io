stage left

1. NOUN

/ steɪdʒ lɛft / (AmE), / steɪdʒ lɛft / (BrE)
the part of a stage that is to the actor's left when facing the audience
left side of stage (actor's perspective)

Examples

The actor made her entrance from stage left, drawing the audience's attention.
Props were arranged neatly stage left before the performance began.
During rehearsal, he missed his cue and entered from stage left instead of stage right.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: stage right - the part of the stage on the actor's right as the actor faces the audience
