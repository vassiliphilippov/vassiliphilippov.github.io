right whale

1. NOUN

/ ˈraɪt weɪl / (AmE), / ˈraɪt weɪl / (BrE)
a large baleen whale of cold coastal waters, historically considered the ‘right’ whale to hunt for its valuable baleen and oil, and now endangered
a large baleen whale formerly prized by whalers

Examples

The North Atlantic right whale is one of the most endangered large whale species in the world.
Centuries of hunting severely reduced right whale populations in both hemispheres.
Scientists track the migration patterns of the right whale to aid in its conservation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bowhead whale - a related Arctic baleen whale species
  • Synonym: humpback whale - another species of baleen whale

Etymology

The name refers to whalers considering it the ‘right’ whale to hunt, as it floated when killed and provided valuable resources.
