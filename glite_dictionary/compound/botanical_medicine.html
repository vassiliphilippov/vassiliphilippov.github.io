botanical medicine

1. NOUN

/ bəˈtænɪkəl ˈmɛdəsən / (AmE), / bəˈtænɪk(ə)l ˈmɛdɪsɪn / (BrE)
the use of plants or plant extracts as remedies or treatments for illness and to promote health, especially when not part of the regular diet
use of plants for medicinal purposes

Examples

Many cultures around the world have relied on botanical medicine for centuries to treat various ailments.
She decided to study botanical medicine after seeing the benefits of herbal remedies in her community.
Recent research supports the effectiveness of certain botanical medicine in managing chronic pain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: herbal medicine - the use of herbs for therapeutic purposes

Etymology

From 'botanical' (relating to plants) and 'medicine' (from Latin 'medicina', the art of healing).
