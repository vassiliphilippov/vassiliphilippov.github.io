gold digger

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡoʊld ˌdɪɡər / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊld ˌdɪɡə / (BrE)
a person who dig or pans for gold, typically in a gold field
person who digs or pans for gold

Examples

During the gold rush, thousands of gold diggers flocked to California in search of fortune.
The veteran gold digger taught newcomers how to pan the river for nuggets.
Many gold diggers lived in makeshift camps near the mining sites.

Etymology

From gold + digger, meaning one who digs for gold.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡoʊld ˌdɪɡɚ / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊld ˌdɪɡə / (BrE)
a woman who forms relationships with men solely to obtain money or material benefits from them
woman seeking wealth through relationships

Examples

The tabloids accused her of being a gold digger after she married the billionaire.
Many movies portray the stereotypical gold digger pursuing wealthy men for their fortunes.
He was wary of gold diggers who seemed more interested in his bank account than his personality.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fortune hunter - someone who pursues wealth by marrying a rich person
  • Synonym: sugar baby - a young person who receives money or gifts from an older partner

Etymology

From 'gold' (precious metal) + 'digger', originally used for those who mine for gold; figuratively applied to people seeking wealth through relationships since the early 20th century.
