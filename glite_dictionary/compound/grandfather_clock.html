grandfather clock

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡræn(d)ˌfɑðɚ klɑk / (AmE), / ˈɡrɑːn(d)ˌfɑːðə klɒk / (BrE)
A tall, freestanding, weight-driven pendulum clock with the movement and pendulum enclosed in a tall, narrow case.
tall freestanding pendulum clock

Examples

The antique grandfather clock in the hallway chimed every hour.
He inherited a beautiful grandfather clock from his grandparents.
The rhythmic tick-tock of the grandfather clock echoed through the quiet house.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pendulum clock - a clock that uses a swinging pendulum to keep time

Etymology

The name is derived from the 1876 song 'My Grandfather’s Clock' by Henry Clay Work, which popularized the term.
