jousting armor

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒaʊstɪŋ ˈɑrmər / (AmE), / ˈdʒaʊstɪŋ ˈɑːmə / (BrE)
A type of heavy plate armor, specially designed for use in jousting tournaments, not intended for free combat, and typically produced in the late 15th to 16th century.
heavy armor for jousting tournaments

Examples

The museum displays an authentic jousting armor used by a 16th-century knight.
It took several assistants to help the competitor put on his jousting armor before the tournament began.
Unlike battlefield gear, jousting armor prioritized protection against the lance over mobility.

Synset Examples

The jousting armour could weigh as much as 50 kg (100 pounds).

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plate armor - body armor made from large metal plates
