First Epistle of John

1. PROPN

/ ˈfɜrst ɪˈpɪsəl ʌv dʒɑn / (AmE), / ˈfɜːst ɪˈpɪsl ɒv dʒɒn / (BrE)
the first of three New Testament letters traditionally attributed to Saint John the Apostle, addressing themes of love and faith
a New Testament letter attributed to John

Examples

The First Epistle of John is often cited for its emphasis on the importance of love within Christian communities.
Scholars debate the authorship of the First Epistle of John and its relation to the other Johannine letters.
During Bible study, we examined the themes present in the First Epistle of John.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Epistle - a letter, especially a formal or didactic one; a book of the New Testament
