loving cup

1. NOUN

/ ˈlʌvɪŋ kʌp / (AmE), / ˈlʌvɪŋ kʌp / (BrE)
a large decorative drinking vessel with two handles, often awarded as a trophy to the winner of a competition
two-handled trophy cup

Examples

The championship loving cup was displayed on the winner's mantelpiece.
She accepted the loving cup on behalf of her team at the awards ceremony.
The silver loving cup had the names of all previous champions engraved on it.

Synset Examples

the school kept the cups in a special glass case

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trophy - an object such as a cup or medal awarded as a prize for victory or success

Etymology

The term dates to the 19th century, from the practice of shared ceremonial drinking vessels at celebratory gatherings.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlʌvɪŋ ˌkʌp / (AmE), / ˈlʌvɪŋ ˌkʌp / (BrE)
A large drinking vessel, typically with two handles, used at banquets for people to drink from in turn as part of a ceremony or celebration.
large ceremonial two-handled drinking vessel

Examples

The newlyweds shared wine from a loving cup during the reception.
The silver loving cup was passed around the table at the awards dinner.
She inherited an antique loving cup from her grandparents' wedding.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: goblet - a drinking glass with a foot and a stem
  • Synonym: chalice - a large cup or goblet, typically for ceremonial purposes
