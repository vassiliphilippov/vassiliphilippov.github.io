conduction anesthesia

1. NOUN

/ kənˈdʌk.ʃən ænˈiːs.θi.ə / (AmE), / kənˈdʌk.ʃən ænˈiːs.θi.ə / (BrE)
a form of anesthesia in which sensation in an area is blocked by applying an anesthetic agent to the nerve supplying that area
anesthesia by blocking nerve conduction
medical

Examples

The dentist opted for conduction anesthesia to numb the patient's jaw before the procedure.
During minor surgical operations, conduction anesthesia can be used to avoid general anesthesia.
The effectiveness of conduction anesthesia depends on correctly targeting the relevant nerve.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: local anesthesia - anesthesia affecting a limited area of the body

Etymology

From Latin 'conduction' (leading) and Greek 'anesthesia' (lack of sensation).
