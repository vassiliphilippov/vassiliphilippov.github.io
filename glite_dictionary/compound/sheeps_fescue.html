sheep's fescue

1. NOUN

/ ʃips ˈfɛskju / (AmE), / ʃiːps ˈfɛskjuː / (BrE)
a type of fine-leaved grass (Festuca ovina) cultivated for sheep pasturage or used as lawn grass, especially in upland regions
a grass cultivated for sheep pastures or lawns

Examples

The hillside was covered with sheep's fescue, providing excellent grazing for the flock.
Gardeners often plant sheep's fescue for its resilience and attractive appearance in lawns.
Sheep's fescue thrives in poor, rocky soils where other grasses struggle to grow.
