Dafla language

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɑːflə ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈdɑːflə ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the language or group of languages spoken by the Dafla people, classified among the Kamarupan languages of northeastern India
language spoken by the Dafla people of India

Examples

The Dafla language is endangered, with only a few thousand speakers remaining.
Linguists have documented several dialects within the Dafla language.
Efforts are being made to preserve the ancient stories told in the Dafla language.

Etymology

From 'Dafla', the name of an indigenous people of Arunachal Pradesh, India, and 'language' from Latin 'lingua'.
