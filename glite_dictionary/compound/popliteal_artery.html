popliteal artery

1. NOUN

/ pɑːˈplɪtiəl ˈɑːrtəri / (AmE), / pɒˈplɪtɪəl ˈɑːtəri / (BrE)
the continuation of the femoral artery that passes through the knee region and branches to supply blood to the lower leg and foot
artery behind the knee
medical

Examples

A blockage in the popliteal artery can cause severe pain and impaired circulation in the lower leg.
The surgeon carefully repaired the damaged popliteal artery during the operation.
Ultrasound imaging is commonly used to assess the condition of the popliteal artery.

Etymology

From Latin popliteus (of the ham, back of the knee) + artery.
