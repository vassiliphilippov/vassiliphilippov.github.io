Central American strap fern

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛntrəl əˈmɛrɪkən stræp fɜrn / (AmE), / ˌsɛntrəl əˈmɛrɪkən stræp fɜːn / (BrE)
A species of fern with shorter and narrower leaves than the Florida strap fern, found from Florida to the West Indies, Mexico, and south to Argentina.
a fern species native to the Americas

Examples

The Central American strap fern thrives in humid forests from Mexico to Argentina.
Some gardeners prefer the Central American strap fern for its delicate, narrow fronds.
Field researchers identified several specimens of the Central American strap fern along the riverbank.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Florida strap fern - a related fern species with broader leaves
