embryonic stage

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛm.briˈɑn.ɪk steɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌɛm.brɪˈɒn.ɪk steɪdʒ / (BrE)
the early phase in the development of an organism, during which it is in the form of an embryo
early development phase as an embryo

Examples

The embryonic stage of a frog lasts only a few days before it turns into a tadpole.
Many birth defects can occur if the embryonic stage of development is disrupted.
Research focuses on how cells differentiate during the embryonic stage.

Synset Examples

The embryonic stage is critical for growth.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: fetal stage - the developmental stage following the embryonic stage in mammals

Etymology

From Greek embruon (young one) + Latin stage (a step).
