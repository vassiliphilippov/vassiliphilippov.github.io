digital subscriber line

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɪdʒɪtəl səbˈskraɪbɚ laɪn / (AmE), / ˈdɪdʒɪtəl səbˈskraɪbə laɪn / (BrE)
a high-speed digital telecommunications line provided by telephone companies, enabling fast data transmission over standard telephone lines
high-speed digital telephone line

Examples

Many people switched to a digital subscriber line for faster internet access than dial-up connections.
The technician came to install a digital subscriber line in our house yesterday.
Businesses often rely on a digital subscriber line for reliable online communication.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: DSL - abbreviation for digital subscriber line

Etymology

Formed from 'digital', 'subscriber', and 'line'; often abbreviated as DSL.
