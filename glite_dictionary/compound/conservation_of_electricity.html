conservation of electricity

1. NOUN

/ kənˌsɜːrˈveɪʃən əv ɪˌlɛkˈtrɪsəti / (AmE), / kənˌsɜːvˈeɪʃən əv ɪˌlɛkˈtrɪsəti / (BrE)
the principle that the total electric charge in a closed system remains constant, regardless of changes within the system
principle that electric charge remains constant

Examples

According to the conservation of electricity, the total charge must remain the same even if charges move between objects.
Scientists rely on the conservation of electricity to explain why electric charge is neither created nor destroyed.
The conservation of electricity is a fundamental concept in physics classes worldwide.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conservation of energy - the principle that energy in a closed system remains constant
  • Synonym: law of conservation of charge - another name for the conservation of electric charge
