Ku Klux Klan

1. PROPN

/ ku klʌks klæn / (AmE), / kuː klʌks klæn / (BrE)
A secret society of white supremacists founded in the 19th-century United States, known for using terror and violence against Black people and other groups.
U.S. white supremacist secret society

Examples

The Ku Klux Klan was responsible for numerous acts of violence during the Reconstruction era.
Several documentaries have examined the rise and decline of the Ku Klux Klan in American history.
Membership in the Ku Klux Klan is widely condemned for its racist ideology and actions.

Etymology

Name is a fanciful formation; 'Ku Klux' is said to derive from Greek 'kyklos' (circle), imitating the sound of a rifle being cocked, combined with 'Klan.'
