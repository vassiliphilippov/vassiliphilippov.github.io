blasting gelatin

1. NOUN

/ ˈblæstɪŋ ˈdʒɛləˌtin / (AmE), / ˈblɑːstɪŋ ˈdʒɛlətɪn / (BrE)
a type of explosive made by combining guncotton (nitrocellulose) with nitroglycerin, used especially in mining and demolition
explosive mixture of guncotton and nitroglycerin

Examples

The quarry workers handled the blasting gelatin with great caution due to its sensitivity.
Blasting gelatin was once widely used in mining before safer alternatives were developed.
The explosive force of blasting gelatin made it effective for breaking up rock formations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gelignite - a similar high explosive based on nitroglycerin and nitrocellulose
