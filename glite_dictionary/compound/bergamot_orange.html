bergamot orange

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɝːɡəmət ˈɔːrɪndʒ / (AmE), / ˈbɜːɡəmɒt ˈɒrɪndʒ / (BrE)
a small citrus tree native to Italy, known for its pear-shaped fruit whose aromatic oil is used in perfumery and flavoring
small citrus tree; fruit used for oil

Examples

The essential oil of the bergamot orange is a key ingredient in many perfumes.
Earl Grey tea is flavored with the oil of the bergamot orange.
Farmers in southern Italy cultivate the bergamot orange for both its fruit and fragrance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: citrus - genus of fruit trees including lemons, oranges, and limes
  • Synonym: orange - the fruit or tree related to the bergamot orange

Etymology

From Italian bergamotto, possibly from Turkish bey armudu (prince's pear).
