sigmatic aorist

1. NOUN

/ sɪɡˈmætɪk ˈɛərɪst / (AmE), / sɪɡˈmatɪk ˈɛːrɪst / (BrE)
A form of the aorist tense in certain languages, created by adding sigma or '-s' to the verb stem.
aorist tense formed by adding '-s' or sigma

Examples

The sigmatic aorist is a key feature in the morphology of Ancient Greek and Sanskrit verbs.
Scholars debate whether the sigmatic aorist developed independently in Indo-European languages.
Because the sigmatic aorist is rare in some branches, its historical development is difficult to trace.

Synset Examples

The sigmatic aorist has disappeared in Baltic, so our information on this category is limited to the Slavic data.

Etymology

From Greek 'sigma' (the letter 's') and 'aorist' (a grammatical tense).
