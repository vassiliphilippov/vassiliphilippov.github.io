rank and file

1. NOUN

/ ræŋk ənd faɪl / (AmE), / ræŋk ənd faɪl / (BrE)
the ordinary members of an organization or group, especially as opposed to its leaders or officers
ordinary members (not leaders) of a group

Examples

The rank and file were not consulted before the new policies were announced.
Changes in management rarely affect the rank and file as much as the higher-ups.
The union's rank and file showed strong support for the strike.

Synset Examples

the strike was supported by the union rank and file
he rose from the ranks to become a colonel

Related Terms

  • Antonym: leadership - the leaders or people in charge of a group or organization

Etymology

Originally a military term referring to rows (ranks) and columns (files) of soldiers.

2. NOUN

/ ˌræŋk ən ˈfaɪl / (AmE), / ˌræŋk ən ˈfaɪl / (BrE)
the ordinary members of an organization, group, or society, as distinguished from its leaders or officers
ordinary members of a group

Examples

The company's rank and file were dissatisfied with the new management policies.
Unlike the executives, the rank and file employees did not receive bonuses this year.
She has always fought for the rights of the rank and file within the union.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: leadership - the leaders of a group or organization

Etymology

From military terminology referring to soldiers who form ranks and files, indicating the common troops as opposed to officers.
