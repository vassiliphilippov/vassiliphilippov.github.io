to call someone a maggot

1. VERB

/ kɔl ˈsʌmwʌn ə ˈmæɡət / (AmE), / kɔːl ˈsʌmwən ə ˈmæɡət / (BrE)
to insult someone by calling them 'maggot', implying they are worthless or lazy
insulting term implying worthlessness
offensive

Examples

In a fit of anger, he called Tom a maggot and stormed out of the room.
She regrets having called her colleague a maggot during their argument.
No manager should ever call someone a maggot at work or anywhere else.

Synset Examples

She called him a maggot for his lazy attitude.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maggot - a fly larva, also used as a derogatory insult
