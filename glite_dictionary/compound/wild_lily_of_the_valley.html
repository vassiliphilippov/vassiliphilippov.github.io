wild lily of the valley

1. NOUN

/ waɪld ˈlɪli əv ðə vəˈli / (AmE), / waɪld ˈlɪli əv ðə vəˈli / (BrE)
an evergreen plant with rounded leaves and strongly scented creamy-white flowers, found widely in northern regions of both the Old and New Worlds
evergreen plant with fragrant white flowers

Examples

The wild lily of the valley blooms early in the spring, filling the forest with its sweet fragrance.
Hikers in northern forests often come across patches of wild lily of the valley along shaded paths.
She planted wild lily of the valley in her garden to enjoy its delicate scent every year.

2. NOUN

/ waɪld ˈlɪli əv ðə vəˈli / (AmE), / waɪld ˈlɪli əv ðə vəˈli / (BrE)
A North American evergreen plant with oblong leaves and small pinkish bell-shaped flowers, formerly used for making shinplasters.
a North American evergreen plant

Examples

The wild lily of the valley thrives in shady North American forests and is known for its delicate, bell-shaped flowers.
Early settlers sometimes used the leaves of wild lily of the valley as a remedy for bruises.
Unlike true lilies, the wild lily of the valley blooms with pinkish bells in the spring.
