spoon food

1. NOUN

/ spuːn fuːd / (AmE), / spuːn fuːd / (BrE)
food prepared in a soft, easily swallowed form that does not require chewing, recommended especially for people with digestive difficulties or after surgery
soft diet not requiring chewing
medical

Examples

After dental surgery, the doctor recommended a spoon food diet to ease chewing and swallowing.
Patients with severe intestinal disorders often require spoon food to avoid further irritation.
The hospital menu included several spoon food options for patients recovering from gastrointestinal procedures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soft diet - a diet consisting of soft foods for ease of digestion or swallowing
