cash crop

1. NOUN

/ kæʃ krɑp / (AmE), / kæʃ krɒp / (BrE)
A crop grown specifically for sale in the market, rather than for personal use or consumption by the grower.
crop grown for sale, not personal use

Examples

Cotton was once the most important cash crop in the southern United States.
Farmers often plant corn as a cash crop to supplement their incomes.
Global demand for coffee helps make it a valuable cash crop in many developing countries.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: staple crop - a crop that is grown for regular food consumption, not primarily for sale
