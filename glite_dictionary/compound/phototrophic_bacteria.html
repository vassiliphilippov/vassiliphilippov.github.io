phototrophic bacteria

1. NOUN

/ ˌfoʊtoʊˈtroʊfɪk bəˈktɪriə / (AmE), / ˌfəʊtəʊˈtrəʊfɪk bækˈtɪəriə / (BrE)
bacteria that obtain energy for growth from sunlight and derive their carbon from carbon dioxide or organic compounds; includes green and purple bacteria.
bacteria using sunlight for energy
medical

Examples

Many phototrophic bacteria thrive in environments with ample sunlight, such as shallow lakes.
The study focused on the role of phototrophic bacteria in recycling nutrients in the ecosystem.
Some phototrophic bacteria can adapt to low-light conditions by altering their photosynthetic pigments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cyanobacteria - photosynthetic bacteria commonly known as blue-green algae
  • Antonym: heterotrophic bacteria - bacteria that obtain energy from organic compounds rather than sunlight

Etymology

From Greek phōs (light) + trophē (nourishment); 'bacteria' from Greek bakterion (small staff).
