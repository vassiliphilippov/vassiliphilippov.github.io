Senegal gum

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛnɪɡəl ɡʌm / (AmE), / ˈsɛnɪɡəl ɡʌm / (BrE)
a type of gum arabic obtained from acacia trees growing near the Senegal River, used in food, pharmaceuticals, and industry as a stabilizer and thickener
gum arabic from Senegal region

Examples

The artist mixed his colors with Senegal gum to achieve a smoother texture.
Exports of Senegal gum have been vital to the local economy for centuries.
Pharmaceutical companies use Senegal gum as a stabilizer in certain medications.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gum arabic - natural gum from acacia trees used as a stabilizer

Etymology

Named for the Senegal River region in West Africa, where the gum is traditionally harvested.
