top onion

1. NOUN

/ tɑp ˈʌnjən / (AmE), / tɒp ˈʌnjən / (BrE)
a type of perennial onion (Allium species) grown mainly for novelty or for early salad use, having small bulbs (bulbils) in place of flowers at the top of the stem
a perennial onion with bulbils replacing flowers

Examples

The gardener planted several top onions for their unusual appearance and early harvest.
Unlike regular onions, the top onion produces bulbils instead of flowers.
You can use the young shoots of the top onion in salads for a mild flavor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Egyptian onion - another name for top onion or tree onion
  • Degree Lower: shallot - a related allium used for salad and culinary purposes
