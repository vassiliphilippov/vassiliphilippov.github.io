large-leaved cucumber tree

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɑrdʒ ˌlivd ˈkjuːkʌmbər triː / (AmE), / ˈlɑːdʒ ˌliːvd ˈkjuːkʌmbə triː / (BrE)
a deciduous shrub or tree of the southeastern United States with very large leaves and large creamy flowers tinged with purple near the base
tree with huge leaves and large creamy flowers

Examples

The leaves of the large-leaved cucumber tree can grow up to two feet long, making it easy to spot in woodlands.
In the spring, the large-leaved cucumber tree produces striking creamy flowers with purple bases.
Botanists are studying how the large-leaved cucumber tree adapts to changes in its native habitat.
