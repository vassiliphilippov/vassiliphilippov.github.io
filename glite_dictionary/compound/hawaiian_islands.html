Hawaiian Islands

1. PROPN

/ həˈwaɪ.ən ˈaɪ.ləndz / (AmE), / həˈwaɪ.i.ən ˈaɪ.ləndz / (BrE)
a group of volcanic and coral islands in the central Pacific Ocean, constituting the U.S. state of Hawaii
group of islands in the central Pacific Ocean

Examples

The Hawaiian Islands are popular with tourists for their beautiful beaches and unique culture.
Endemic species found only on the Hawaiian Islands are of great interest to biologists.
The Pacific Ocean surrounds the Hawaiian Islands, making them an important location for marine research.

Etymology

From Hawaiian, the native language of Hawaii, and 'islands' referring to the group; 'Hawaii' ultimately from Proto-Polynesian *Sawaiki.
