respiratory organ

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛspəˌrɛˌtɔri ˈɔrɡən / (AmE), / rɪˈspɪrətəri ˈɔːɡən / (BrE)
an organ in an animal or human body responsible for exchanging gases, such as oxygen and carbon dioxide, during breathing
organ for breathing (e.g. lungs, gills)

Examples

Fish use their respiratory organs, known as gills, to extract oxygen from water.
Damage to the respiratory organs can severely impact an animal's ability to survive.
The lungs are the main respiratory organ in mammals, enabling efficient gas exchange.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lung - main respiratory organ in humans and many animals
  • Synonym: gill - respiratory organ in fish and some amphibians

Etymology

From Latin respirare (to breathe) and organum (organ).
