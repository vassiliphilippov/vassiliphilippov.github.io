Commonwealth spelling

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑm.ənˌwɛlθ ˈspɛl.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkɒm.ənˌwɛlθ ˈspɛl.ɪŋ / (BrE)
the system of English spelling conventions used in the United Kingdom, Ireland, and most Commonwealth countries, as distinguished from American spelling
British and Commonwealth English spelling conventions

Examples

Many textbooks use Commonwealth spelling rather than American spelling.
He prefers Commonwealth spelling for words like 'colour' and 'theatre'.
English exams in Australia typically accept only Commonwealth spelling.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: American spelling - the system of English spelling conventions used in the United States
