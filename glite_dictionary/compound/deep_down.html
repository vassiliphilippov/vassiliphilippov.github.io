deep down

1. ADV

/ diːp daʊn / (AmE), / diːp daʊn / (BrE)
used to refer to what a person truly feels or believes, even if it is not shown or admitted openly.
in reality; at heart

Examples

Deep down, I knew he was right, even though I disagreed at first.
She seems tough, but deep down she's very sensitive.
Deep down, everyone wants to feel accepted and loved.

Synset Examples

she is very kind at heart

Related Terms

  • Synonym: at heart - used to say what someone is really like or what they really feel
