vantage point

1. NOUN

/ ˈvæn.tɪdʒ pɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˈvɑːn.tɪdʒ pɔɪnt / (BrE)
a position or place that provides a clear or advantageous view of something
a place offering a good view

Examples

We climbed the hill to reach a vantage point overlooking the valley.
From this vantage point, you can clearly see the river winding through the town.
Photographers searched for the perfect vantage point to capture the sunset.

Synset Examples

from that vantage point he could survey the whole valley

Related Terms

  • Synonym: viewpoint - a particular position from which something is observed or considered
  • Synonym: perspective - a particular attitude or view; also a way of regarding something

Etymology

From 'vantage', meaning advantage (from Old French 'avantage'), combined with 'point' (from Old French 'point', from Latin 'punctum').
