genus Eruca

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ɪˈruːkə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ɪˈruːkə / (BrE)
A genus of annual to perennial herbs in the Mediterranean region, including arugula (rocket).
plant genus including arugula

Examples

The leaves of genus Eruca are commonly used in Mediterranean salads.
Scientists have studied the growth patterns of plants belonging to the genus Eruca.
Wild populations of genus Eruca thrive in sunny, well-drained soils.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arugula - an edible leafy green plant in cuisine, also called rocket

Etymology

From Latin 'Eruca' meaning 'caterpillar' or a kind of plant (arugula).
