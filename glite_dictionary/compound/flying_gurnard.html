flying gurnard

1. NOUN

/ ˈflaɪɪŋ ˈɡɜrnərd / (AmE), / ˈflaɪɪŋ ˈɡɜːnɑːd / (BrE)
a tropical fish with large, fan-shaped pectoral fins used for underwater gliding; it is not related to searobins.
tropical fish with large fanlike fins

Examples

The flying gurnard spread its enormous fins as it glided gracefully along the seabed.
While snorkeling in the Caribbean, we spotted a colorful flying gurnard resting on the sand.
Unlike searobins, the flying gurnard uses its fanlike fins to move elegantly underwater.

Etymology

From 'flying' (referring to its gliding movement) and 'gurnard' (from Old French 'gournart', a kind of fish).
