povertician rhetoric

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑːvərˈtɪʃən ˈrɛtərɪk / (AmE), / ˌpɒvətˈɪʃən ˈrɛtərɪk / (BrE)
political speech or statements focused on addressing or highlighting poverty and related issues
political speech focused on poverty

Examples

The candidate's povertician rhetoric appealed to the working class voters in the city.
Critics accused her of using povertician rhetoric without proposing real solutions.
Media attention grew due to his frequent use of povertician rhetoric during the campaign.

Synset Examples

His povertician rhetoric gained him many supporters.

Etymology

Blend of 'poverty' and 'politician', with 'rhetoric' from Greek 'rhetorikē' (art of oratory).
