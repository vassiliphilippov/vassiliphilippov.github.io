anxiety neurosis

1. NOUN

/ ænˈzaɪəti nuˈroʊsɪs / (AmE), / ænˈzaɪəti njʊəˈrəʊsɪs / (BrE)
A psychological disorder marked by persistent, excessive anxiety and often accompanied by physical symptoms of fear, such as palpitations and sweating.
psychological disorder with persistent anxiety
medical

Examples

He was diagnosed with anxiety neurosis after experiencing frequent panic attacks and insomnia.
The symptoms of anxiety neurosis can include rapid heartbeat, sweating, and constant worry.
Many people suffering from anxiety neurosis find it difficult to cope with everyday stress.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: generalized anxiety disorder - A disorder characterized by excessive, uncontrollable worry about various aspects of life.

Etymology

From 'anxiety' (Latin anxietas) + 'neurosis' (from Greek neuron, nerve).
