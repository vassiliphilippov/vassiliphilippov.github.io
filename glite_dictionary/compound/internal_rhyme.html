internal rhyme

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtɜrnəl raɪm / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɜːnəl raɪm / (BrE)
a rhyme created by two or more words within the same line of poetry
rhyme within one line of poetry

Examples

Poe’s use of internal rhyme in 'The Raven' helps create a haunting musical effect.
Some rappers skillfully employ internal rhyme to add complexity to their lyrics.
You can find a clever internal rhyme in the second line of this poem.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: end rhyme - rhyme at the ends of lines in poetry
