genus Pseudomonas

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˌsjuːdəˈmoʊnəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˌsjuːdəˈməʊnəs / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Pseudomonadaceae, comprising various species of Gram-negative bacteria found in soil and water, some of which cause diseases in plants and animals.
type genus of Pseudomonadaceae bacteria
medical

Examples

Several species within the genus Pseudomonas are known for their resistance to antibiotics.
The genus Pseudomonas includes bacteria commonly found in soil and fresh water.
Researchers are studying the genus Pseudomonas because of its environmental and medical importance.

Etymology

Genus name from Greek pseudo- (false) + monas (unit).
