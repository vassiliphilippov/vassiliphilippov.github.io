supply-side economics

1. NOUN

/ səˈplaɪ saɪd ˌɛkəˈnɑmɪks / (AmE), / səˈplaɪ saɪd ˌiːkəˈnɒmɪks / (BrE)
an economic theory that emphasizes boosting production and investment as the key to economic growth, often through tax cuts and deregulation
economic theory focusing on increasing production

Examples

Many politicians promoted supply-side economics in the 1980s, arguing that tax cuts would boost growth.
Critics of supply-side economics claim it disproportionately benefits the rich.
Her research examines the effects of supply-side economics on employment rates.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Keynesian economics - the school of economics emphasizing demand-side management
  • Synonym: trickle-down economics - economic theory linked with supply-side ideas, focusing on benefits to the wealthy 'trickling down'
