family Equidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ˈɛkwɪˌdeɪ / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli ˈɛkwɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of mammals including horses, asses, zebras, and their extinct relatives.
mammal family: horses, asses, zebras, etc.

Examples

The family Equidae includes both modern horses and their extinct ancestors.
Zebras, which belong to the family Equidae, are native to Africa.
Scientists study fossils from the family Equidae to understand the evolution of horses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: equid - any member of the family Equidae, e.g., horse or zebra

Etymology

From Latin 'Equidae', derived from 'equus' meaning horse.
