Melba toast

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛlbə toʊst / (AmE), / ˈmɛlbə təʊst / (BrE)
Very thin, crisp slices of toasted bread, often served with soup or salad or as a base for toppings.
thin, crisp slice of toasted bread

Examples

She served chicken liver pâté on Melba toast at the party.
I like to eat Melba toast with a bit of butter and jam for breakfast.
The salad came with a side of Melba toast instead of croutons.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: toast - sliced bread browned by heat

Etymology

Named after Dame Nellie Melba, the Australian soprano, for whom the toast was created in the early 20th century.
