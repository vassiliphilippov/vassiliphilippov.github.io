Sabine River

1. PROPN

/ səˈbin ˈrɪvɚ / (AmE), / səˈbiːn ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
a river in eastern Texas that flows south into the Gulf of Mexico, forming part of the border between Texas and Louisiana
river between Texas and Louisiana, USA

Examples

The Sabine River serves as a natural boundary between Texas and Louisiana.
Many species of fish can be found in the Sabine River system.
Heavy rain caused flooding along the banks of the Sabine River.

Etymology

Named after the Sabine people or the cypress trees (from Spanish 'sabinas') along the river.
