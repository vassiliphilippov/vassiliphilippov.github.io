perfectly elastic demand

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɝːfɪktli ɪˈlæstɪk dɪˈmænd / (AmE), / ˈpɜːfɪktli ɪˈlæstɪk dɪˈmɑːnd / (BrE)
A situation in which consumers will only buy at a specific price, so any increase makes demand drop to zero, and any decrease leads to infinite demand.
demand that changes drastically with small price changes

Examples

In a market with perfectly elastic demand, suppliers must sell at the prevailing price or risk losing all customers.
If wheat exhibited perfectly elastic demand, even a minor price increase would eliminate all sales instantly.
The concept of perfectly elastic demand is essential for understanding certain idealized economic models.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: perfectly inelastic demand - demand that does not change regardless of price
  • Degree Lower: elastic demand - demand that changes in response to price but not infinitely

Etymology

From 'elastic', used in economics for responsive changes; 'perfectly' meaning absolute.
