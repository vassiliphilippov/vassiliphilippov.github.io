Arapaho language

1. NOUN

/ əˈræpəˌhoʊ ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / əˈrapəhəʊ ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Native American Algonquian language spoken by the Arapaho people of the Great Plains
Algonquian language of the Arapaho people

Examples

Few people today are fluent speakers of the Arapaho language.
Efforts are underway to teach the Arapaho language in local schools.
The dictionary project documents traditional words in the Arapaho language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Algonquian - a family of Native American languages

Etymology

From Arapaho, the name of the indigenous people; 'language' from Latin 'lingua' (tongue, language).
