involutional depression

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪnˌvɑːluːˈʃənəl dɪˈprɛʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪnˌvɒljuːˈʃənəl dɪˈprɛʃən / (BrE)
a major depressive episode occurring during or after the climacteric, particularly associated with menopause or aging.
depression related to menopause or aging
medical

Examples

Many women experience involutional depression during the years surrounding menopause.
The psychiatrist diagnosed him with involutional depression as he reached late middle age.
Support groups may help those suffering from involutional depression cope with their symptoms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: depression - persistent feeling of sadness and loss of interest
  • Degree Lower: postpartum depression - depression occurring after childbirth

Etymology

From 'involution' (Latin involutio, folding in) and 'depression'; originally coined in early 20th-century psychiatry.
