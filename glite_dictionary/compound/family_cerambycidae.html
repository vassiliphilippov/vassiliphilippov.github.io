family Cerambycidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli səˌræmbɪˈsaɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli səˌræmbɪˈsaɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of beetles commonly known as long-horned beetles, characterized by their long antennae.
family of long-horned beetles

Examples

The family Cerambycidae includes thousands of species of long-horned beetles found worldwide.
Entomologists study the family Cerambycidae to learn more about forest ecosystems.
Several members of the family Cerambycidae are important pests of timber and wooden structures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: longhorn beetle - a beetle of the family Cerambycidae

Etymology

From New Latin Cerambycidae, based on Cerambyx (a genus of beetles) and -idae (denoting animal families).
