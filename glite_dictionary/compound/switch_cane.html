switch cane

1. NOUN

/ swɪtʃ keɪn / (AmE), / swɪtʃ keɪn / (BrE)
a small cane (Arundinaria tecta) found in wet or moist areas in the southern United States
small cane of moist southern US areas

Examples

Dense patches of switch cane covered the swampy ground along the riverbank.
Early settlers used switch cane to make baskets and fishing rods.
The wetland restoration project included planting native switch cane to prevent soil erosion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cane - plant with jointed stems, often used to make furniture or baskets
  • Synonym: reed - tall, slender-leaved plant of wet places
