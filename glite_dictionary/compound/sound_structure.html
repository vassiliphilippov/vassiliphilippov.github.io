sound structure

1. NOUN

/ saʊnd ˈstrʌktʃɚ / (AmE), / saʊnd ˈstrʌktʃə / (BrE)
the permissible arrangement and organization of speech sounds in words, especially as studied in linguistics or phonology.
arrangement of sounds in words

Examples

The sound structure of English allows certain consonant clusters that are rare in other languages.
Linguists analyze the sound structure of words to understand pronunciation rules.
Children learn the sound structure of their native language during early speech development.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phonotactics - rules governing the possible sound sequences in a language
  • Degree Lower: syllable structure - the organization of sounds within a syllable
