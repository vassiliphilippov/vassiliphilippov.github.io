conservation of mass

1. NOUN

/ kənˌsɜrvəˈʃən əv mæs / (AmE), / kɒnˌsɜːvəˈveɪʃən əv mæs / (BrE)
the principle that matter cannot be created or destroyed in an isolated system; the total mass remains constant over time
principle that mass is neither created nor destroyed

Examples

The conservation of mass explains why the total weight in a chemical reaction remains the same.
Teachers often demonstrate the conservation of mass using simple experiments in the classroom.
Understanding the conservation of mass is fundamental to studying chemistry and physics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: law of conservation of matter - alternative phrasing of conservation of mass
  • Synonym: conservation of energy - scientific principle stating energy cannot be created or destroyed
