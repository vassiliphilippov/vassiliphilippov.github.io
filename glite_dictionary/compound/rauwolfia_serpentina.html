Rauwolfia serpentina

1. NOUN

/ raʊˈwʊlfiə sərˈpɛntɪnə / (AmE), / raʊˈwʊlfiə səˈpɛntɪnə / (BrE)
An East Indian climbing shrub with twisted limbs and roots that resemble serpents, known for its medicinal properties.
East Indian shrub with serpentine roots
medical

Examples

The roots of Rauwolfia serpentina are used in traditional Indian medicine for treating hypertension.
Botanists identified the specimen as Rauwolfia serpentina due to its twisted, snake-like roots.
Many pharmaceutical drugs are derived from the alkaloids found in Rauwolfia serpentina.

Etymology

From the genus name 'Rauwolfia', after German physician Leonhard Rauwolf, and Latin 'serpentina', meaning 'serpentine', referring to the root shape.
