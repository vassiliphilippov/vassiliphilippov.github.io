Classical Latin

1. NOUN

/ ˈklæsɪkəl ˈlætɪn / (AmE), / ˈklasɪkəl ˈlatɪn / (BrE)
the form of the Latin language used by educated people in ancient Rome, especially in literature and formal writing
standard literary Latin used in ancient Rome

Examples

Many ancient Roman poets wrote in Classical Latin, which is admired for its elegance and precision.
Scholars often compare Classical Latin to later forms to understand changes in pronunciation and grammar.
A working knowledge of Classical Latin is still required for some advanced studies in classics and philology.

Synset Examples

There's a distinct difference in pronunciation between Classical Latin and Ecclesiastical Latin

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Ecclesiastical Latin - the form of Latin used in the Roman Catholic Church

Etymology

From Latin 'classicus' (of the highest class) + 'Latin'.
