American white birch

1. NOUN

/ əˈmɛrɪkən waɪt bɝtʃ / (AmE), / əˈmɛrɪkən waɪt bɜːtʃ / (BrE)
a small North American birch tree with peeling white bark, often used for making baskets or toy canoes
North American birch tree with white bark

Examples

The American white birch is well known for its striking white bark that peels away in thin layers.
Baskets made from American white birch have been a tradition in Native American cultures.
A grove of American white birch trees added beauty to the landscape.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paper birch - North American birch with white papery bark (synonym)
