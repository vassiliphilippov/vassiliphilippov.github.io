Reform Judaism

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈfɔrm ˈdʒuːdiɪzəm / (AmE), / rɪˈfɔːm ˈdʒuːdiɪzəm / (BrE)
A branch of Judaism that seeks to adapt traditional Jewish beliefs and practices to fit modern values and life, often interpreting Jewish laws more flexibly.
liberal branch of Judaism

Examples

Many American Jews practice Reform Judaism, finding that its values align with modern life.
The synagogue is affiliated with Reform Judaism, emphasizing inclusivity and progressive beliefs.
He converted to Reform Judaism because he appreciated its flexible approach to tradition.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Orthodox Judaism - branch of Judaism strictly observing traditional beliefs and laws
  • Degree Lower: Conservative Judaism - branch of Judaism between Reform and Orthodox in practice

2. NOUN

/ rɪˈfɔrm ˈdʒuːdiˌɪzəm / (AmE), / rɪˈfɔːm ˈdʒuːdiˌɪzəm / (BrE)
a major movement within Judaism that advocates for a modern, liberal interpretation of Jewish beliefs, rituals, and laws
modern liberal movement in Judaism

Examples

Many Jewish families in the United States practice Reform Judaism and value its emphasis on individual choice.
The beliefs of Reform Judaism often adapt traditional rituals to fit modern life.
She grew up in a household where Reform Judaism shaped their religious and cultural identity.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Conservative Judaism - a branch of Judaism seeking a middle ground between traditional and reform movements
  • Degree Lower: Orthodox Judaism - a branch of Judaism adhering strictly to traditional teachings and practices
