honeycomb tripe

1. NOUN

/ ˈhʌniˌkoʊm traɪp / (AmE), / ˈhʌnɪkəʊm traɪp / (BrE)
the edible lining of the reticulum (second stomach) of a ruminant, characterized by its honeycomb-like texture, used as food
edible lining of a ruminant's second stomach

Examples

Many traditional dishes in Italy and China feature honeycomb tripe as a main ingredient.
The texture of honeycomb tripe makes it ideal for absorbing flavorful broths in soups and stews.
He cleaned and boiled the honeycomb tripe before adding it to the spicy stew.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tripe - the edible stomach lining of a ruminant, used as food

Etymology

Named for its honeycomb-like texture and 'tripe' from Old French 'tripe' (guts, entrails).
