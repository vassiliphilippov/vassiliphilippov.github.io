hydraulic transmission

1. NOUN

/ haɪˈdrɑdrɪk trænzˈmɪʃən / (AmE), / haɪˈdrɒdrɪk trænzˈmɪʃən / (BrE)
a system of transmitting power in a machine, vehicle, or device using fluids under pressure instead of mechanical gears or shafts
power transmission using hydraulic fluid

Examples

The new tractor uses a hydraulic transmission instead of a standard gear system for smoother operation.
A malfunction in the hydraulic transmission can lead to significant performance issues in heavy machinery.
Technicians inspected the hydraulic transmission to ensure there were no leaks in the fluid lines.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: mechanical transmission - system of transmitting power using physical components like gears and shafts

Etymology

hydraulic from Greek *hydor* (water) + *aulos* (pipe); transmission from Latin *transmittere* (send across).
