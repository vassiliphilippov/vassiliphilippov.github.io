existential philosopher

1. NOUN

/ ɛɡˌzɪˈstɛn.ʃəl fəˈlɑː.sə.fər / (AmE), / ɛɡˌzɪˈstɛn.ʃəl fəˈlɒ.sə.fə / (BrE)
a philosopher who focuses on individual freedom, responsibility, and the unexplainable nature of human existence, especially within existentialism
philosopher emphasizing existentialism and personal choice

Examples

Jean-Paul Sartre is often considered the most influential existential philosopher of the twentieth century.
As an existential philosopher, she believed that individuals must create their own meaning in life.
Many students are drawn to the works of an existential philosopher because of their focus on personal freedom and responsibility.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: existentialism - a philosophical theory emphasizing individual existence, freedom, and choice

Etymology

From 'existential' (relating to existence) and 'philosopher,' ultimately from Greek *philosophos* (lover of wisdom).
