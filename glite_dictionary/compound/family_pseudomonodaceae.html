family Pseudomonodaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ˌsjuːdəʊmɒnoʊˈdeɪsiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli ˌsjuːdəʊmɒnəˈdiːsaɪ / (BrE)
A family of rod-shaped, Gram-negative bacteria, including species that are important pathogens of plants and animals.
bacterial family with significant pathogens

Examples

Several members of the family Pseudomonodaceae are notorious for causing diseases in agricultural crops.
The family Pseudomonodaceae includes bacteria that can infect both plants and animals.
Researchers are studying antibiotic resistance within the family Pseudomonodaceae to find new treatments.

Etymology

From Greek 'pseudo-' (false) and 'monas' (unit), referencing the genus Pseudomonas within the family.
