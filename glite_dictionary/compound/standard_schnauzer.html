standard schnauzer

1. NOUN

/ ˈstændɚd ˈʃnaʊzɚ / (AmE), / ˈstandəd ˈʃnaʊzə / (BrE)
a medium-sized breed of schnauzer dog, known for its wiry coat and distinct whiskers, originally bred in Germany as a working and guard dog.
medium-sized schnauzer breed of dog

Examples

The standard schnauzer is known for its intelligence and alert temperament.
We adopted a standard schnauzer from the animal shelter last year.
Her standard schnauzer loves to accompany her on long walks in the park.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: miniature schnauzer - a small-sized schnauzer breed
  • Degree Higher: giant schnauzer - a large-sized schnauzer breed

Etymology

From German *Schnauzer* (moustached), combined with 'standard' to denote breed size.
