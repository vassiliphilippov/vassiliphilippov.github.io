back forty

1. NOUN

/ bæk ˈfɔɹ.ti / (AmE), / bæk ˈfɔː.ti / (BrE)
an area of land, especially the remote or less accessible part of a farm, often measuring forty acres
remote section of farmland
american

Examples

The old barn sits way out on the back forty, far from the main house.
They let the cattle graze on the back forty during the summer months.
He spent Saturday clearing brush from his back forty to prepare for planting.

Synset Examples

We often camp on the back forty during summer.

They plan to plough the back forty for the new crop.
He spent the weekend ploughing the back forty by hand.
The farmer planted corn in the back forty this year.
He bought a cabin on the back forty of his property.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acreage - an area of land measured in acres

Etymology

Originally from American English; referring to a typical forty-acre parcel in agricultural land division.
