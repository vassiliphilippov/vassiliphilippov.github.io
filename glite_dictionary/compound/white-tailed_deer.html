white-tailed deer

1. NOUN

/ ˌwaɪt ˈteɪld ˌdɪr / (AmE), / ˌwaɪt ˈteɪld ˌdɪə / (BrE)
a common North American deer (Odocoileus virginianus) characterized by a white underside on its tail, which it raises as a signal when alarmed
North American deer with a white tail underside

Examples

A white-tailed deer darted across the road as we were driving through the forest.
Hunters often look for the flicker of a white-tailed deer's tail when tracking them in the woods.
The white-tailed deer population has increased significantly in many suburban areas.
