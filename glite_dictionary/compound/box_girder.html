box girder

1. NOUN

/ bɑks ˈɡɪrdər / (AmE), / bɒks ˈɡɜːdə / (BrE)
a beam with a hollow, rectangular cross section, often constructed from boards or metal, used especially in bridges and large structures
hollow rectangular structural beam

Examples

The new bridge was designed with a box girder to provide greater strength and stability.
Engineers often choose a box girder for long-span highway overpasses due to its structural efficiency.
During the inspection, they discovered a crack in the steel box girder supporting the railway bridge.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: I-beam - beam with an I-shaped cross section
  • Synonym: girder - a large beam supporting structures such as bridges
