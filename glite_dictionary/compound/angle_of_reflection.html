angle of reflection

1. NOUN

/ ˈæŋɡəl əv rəˈflɛkʃən / (AmE), / ˈæŋɡl əv rɪˈflɛkʃən / (BrE)
the angle formed between a reflected ray and a line perpendicular (normal) to the surface at the point where the ray reflects
angle between reflected ray and surface normal

Examples

According to the law of reflection, the angle of reflection equals the angle of incidence.
The mirror's tilt altered the angle of reflection and changed where the light beam landed.
Students measured the angle of reflection using a protractor and recorded their results.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: angle of incidence - angle between the incoming ray and the normal to the reflecting surface
