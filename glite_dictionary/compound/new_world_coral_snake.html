New World coral snake

1. NOUN

/ nu ˈwɝld ˈkɔɹəl sneɪk / (AmE), / njuː ˈwɜːld ˈkɒrəl sneɪk / (BrE)
Any of several brightly colored, venomous snakes native to the Americas, characterized by red, black, and yellow or white bands.
venomous banded snake native to the Americas

Examples

The New World coral snake can be identified by its distinct red, yellow, and black banding pattern.
Hikers in Central America are advised to be cautious of the New World coral snake.
Unlike their mimics, the New World coral snake is highly venomous and should not be handled.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coral snake - venomous snake with brightly colored bands
