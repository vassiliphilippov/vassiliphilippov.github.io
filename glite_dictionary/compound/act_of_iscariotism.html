act of Iscariotism

1. NOUN

/ ˈækt əv ɪˈskæriətɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈækt əv ɪˈskærɪətɪzəm / (BrE)
an act of treachery or betrayal, especially one committed for monetary gain
treacherous betrayal for money

Examples

His decision to reveal confidential information was an act of Iscariotism that shocked the entire company.
History remembers the act of Iscariotism as one of the greatest betrayals ever committed.
She could never forgive him for that single act of Iscariotism, no matter what explanation he gave.

Synset Examples

His betrayal felt like an act of Iscariotism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: betrayal - the act of betraying someone's trust
  • Synonym: treachery - betrayal of trust or confidence

Etymology

From Judas Iscariot, the biblical apostle who betrayed Jesus for thirty pieces of silver, plus the suffix '-ism.'
