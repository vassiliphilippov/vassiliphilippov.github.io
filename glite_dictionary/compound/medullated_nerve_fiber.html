medullated nerve fiber

1. NOUN

/ məˈdʌlˌeɪtɪd nɜːrv ˈfaɪbər / (AmE), / mɪˈdʌl.jeɪ.tɪd nɜːv ˈfaɪ.bə / (BrE)
a nerve fiber that is covered by a sheath of myelin, which increases the speed of nerve impulse conduction
nerve fiber with myelin sheath
medical

Examples

A medullated nerve fiber conducts signals much faster than one without a myelin sheath.
Damage to a medullated nerve fiber can slow down nerve impulse transmission.
During development, the formation of the medullated nerve fiber is crucial for proper nervous system function.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: myelinated nerve fiber - nerve fiber with a myelin sheath
  • Antonym: unmyelinated nerve fiber - nerve fiber without a myelin sheath

Etymology

From Latin 'medulla' meaning 'marrow', referring to the inner, soft part; 'medullated' meaning 'having a medulla or marrow-like sheath'.
