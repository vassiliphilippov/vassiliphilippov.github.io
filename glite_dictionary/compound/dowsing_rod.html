dowsing rod

1. NOUN

/ ˈdaʊzɪŋ rɑd / (AmE), / ˈdaʊzɪŋ rɒd / (BrE)
a forked stick or rod, traditionally used in attempts to locate underground water, minerals, or oil by dowsing
rod used in dowsing for water or minerals

Examples

The old farmer claimed his dowsing rod could find underground streams with amazing accuracy.
Skeptics doubt that a dowsing rod is effective for locating oil deposits.
She watched the dowsing rod slowly dip, convinced they were about to find water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: divining rod - rod used to detect water, also called dowsing rod

Etymology

From 'dowsing', of uncertain origin, plus 'rod'.
