shove-ha'penny

1. NOUN

/ ʃʌv ˈheɪpəni / (AmE), / ʃʌv ˈheɪpəni / (BrE)
A traditional British game in which coins or discs are slid by hand across a board toward marked areas to score points.
British tabletop game sliding coins on a board
british

Examples

Every Friday night, the locals gathered at the pub to play shove-ha'penny together.
He learned the rules of shove-ha'penny from his grandfather, who had played the game as a child.
A worn shove-ha'penny board was proudly displayed above the fireplace.

Etymology

From the verb 'shove' (to push) and 'ha'penny', a contraction of 'half penny', referring to the coins originally used in the game.
