genus Trillium

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs trɪˈliːəm / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs trɪˈlɪəm / (BrE)
A genus of deciduous perennial herbs commonly found in temperate regions, sometimes classified within the family Liliaceae.
genus of perennial herbaceous plants

Examples

The woodland was carpeted with blooming genus Trillium in the spring.
Botanists debated whether the genus Trillium belongs in the Liliaceae or Melanthiaceae family.
Some species in the genus Trillium are prized in shade gardens for their distinctive flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trillium - a plant of the genus Trillium

Etymology

Trillium from New Latin, based on Swedish botanist Linnaeus's taxonomic naming; 'genus' from Latin genus (kind, type).
