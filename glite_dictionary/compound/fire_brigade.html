fire brigade

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪɚ brɪˌɡeɪd / (AmE), / ˈfaɪə brɪˌɡeɪd / (BrE)
a group of people, especially in Britain, who are trained and organized to deal with fires and other emergencies
British fire department
british

Examples

The fire brigade responded within minutes to the emergency call.
She works as a firefighter for the local fire brigade.
When the alarm sounded, the fire brigade rushed to the burning building.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fire department - an organization for fighting fires, chiefly American

2. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪɚ brɪˌɡeɪd / (AmE), / ˈfaɪə brɪˌɡeɪd / (BrE)
a group of people, often organized professionally or temporarily, who are trained and equipped to put out fires
group trained to fight fires

Examples

The village organized a fire brigade after a series of small fires broke out.
When the warehouse caught fire, a fire brigade was quickly assembled from local volunteers.
The town relies on a volunteer fire brigade rather than a professional fire department.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fire department - professional public organization for fighting fires
