heavy particle

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛvi ˈpɑrtɪkl / (AmE), / ˈhɛvi ˈpɑːtɪkl / (BrE)
an elementary particle, such as a baryon or hadron, with mass equal to or greater than a proton, participating in strong interactions
elementary particle with mass ≥ proton's

Examples

The proton is an example of a heavy particle in atomic physics.
Researchers studied the behavior of each heavy particle produced in the collision.
A neutron, being a heavy particle, also participates in strong nuclear forces.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: baryon - a type of heavy particle made of three quarks
  • Antonym: lepton - an elementary particle that does not participate in strong interactions
