celery root

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛləri ruːt / (AmE), / ˈsɛləri ruːt / (BrE)
the thick, edible, aromatic root of a variety of celery, used as a vegetable, also known as celeriac
edible root of a celery variety (celeriac)

Examples

Cut the celery root into cubes before roasting it with olive oil.
Celery root can be grated raw into salads or cooked in soups and stews.
The chef made a creamy celery root purée to serve with the fish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: celeriac - the same plant as celery root; another common name

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɛləri ruːt / (AmE), / ˈsɛlɚi ruːt / (BrE)
an edible, knobbly root vegetable with a mild, celery-like flavor, cultivated for its swollen underground stem
edible aromatic root vegetable

Examples

Roasted celery root makes a delicious side dish for winter meals.
She chopped the celery root into cubes before adding it to the soup.
You can mash celery root with potatoes for a creamy, flavorful purée.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: celeriac - another term for celery root, an edible root vegetable
