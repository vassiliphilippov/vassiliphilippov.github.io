sea island cotton

1. NOUN

/ siː ˈaɪ.lənd ˈkɑː.tən / (AmE), / siː ˈaɪ.lənd ˈkɒt.ən / (BrE)
a variety of cotton plant grown mainly on islands along the Caribbean and the Atlantic coast of the southern United States, known for its exceptionally long, silky fibers
a cotton plant with long, silky fibers

Examples

Manufacturers prize sea island cotton for its strength and softness in luxury garments.
The unique climate of the coastal islands produces the finest sea island cotton fibers.
She purchased a shirt made from pure sea island cotton, renowned for its quality.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cotton - soft, white fibrous substance from the cotton plant, used in textiles
