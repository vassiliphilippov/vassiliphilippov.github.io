parasol mushroom

1. NOUN

/ ˈpærəˌsɑl ˈmʌʃˌrum / (AmE), / ˈpærəsɒl ˈmʌʃrʊm / (BrE)
an edible mushroom with a long stalk, white flesh, white gills, and spores, typically found in open woodlands during autumn
an edible long-stalked woodland mushroom

Examples

We found a beautiful parasol mushroom growing at the edge of the field.
The parasol mushroom is popular with foragers for its delicate flavor.
Always take care when identifying wild fungi like the parasol mushroom before eating them.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: shaggy parasol - a similar species of edible mushroom
  • Synonym: agaric - any mushroom with gills on the underside of the cap

Etymology

From 'parasol' (sunshade) and 'mushroom', referring to its umbrella-like cap.
