toga virilis

1. NOUN

/ ˈtoʊɡə vəˈrɪlɪs / (AmE), / ˈtəʊɡə vɪˈrɪlɪs / (BrE)
(in ancient Rome) a toga worn by a young man to mark his coming of age and status as a citizen.
coming-of-age Roman toga

Examples

When he turned sixteen, Marcus was presented with the toga virilis during a special ceremony.
The toga virilis symbolized a Roman youth's transition to adulthood and full citizenship.
Historians often mention the toga virilis in accounts of ancient Roman rites of passage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: toga - loose outer garment worn in ancient Rome

Etymology

Latin, literally 'toga of manhood'.
