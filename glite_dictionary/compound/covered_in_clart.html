covered in clart

1. ADJ

/ ˈkʌvɚd ɪn klɑrt / (AmE), / ˈkʌvəd ɪn klɑːt / (BrE)
coated or smeared with mud or dirt, especially after exposure to wet or messy conditions
covered in mud or dirt
informal british

Examples

After helping on the farm, his boots were covered in clart.
The dogs came back from their walk absolutely covered in clart.
My trousers were covered in clart after the football match in the rain.

Synset Examples

The road was covered in clart after the heavy rain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: muddy - covered with or full of mud
  • Antonym: clean - free from dirt, marks, or stains

Etymology

From 'clart', a northern English and Scots word for mud or dirt, of uncertain origin.
