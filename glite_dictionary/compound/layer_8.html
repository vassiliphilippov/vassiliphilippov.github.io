layer 8

1. NOUN

/ ˈleɪ.ɚ eɪt / (AmE), / ˈleɪ.ə eɪt / (BrE)
(informal, often humorous) In computing and IT, a joking reference to problems or errors caused by users, as opposed to technical faults; user error.
IT slang for user-caused problems
informal slang

Examples

Some network issues are actually just layer 8 problems caused by user mistakes.
The technician blamed the glitch on layer 8 instead of the hardware.
They rolled their eyes, realizing it was a classic layer 8 situation.

Synset Examples

In IT, the term layer 8 refers to user errors.
He joked that the layer 8 problem was the toughest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: user error - A mistake made by the computer user, rather than a technical fault

Etymology

From a mock extension of the OSI (Open Systems Interconnection) model, which has seven layers; 'layer 8' humorously refers to the 'user' as an extra layer.
