family Fregatidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli friːˈɡætɪˌdeɪ / (AmE), / ˈfæməli friːˈɡætɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of large, tropical seabirds known as frigate birds, characterized by long wings, forked tails, and a distinctive throat pouch in males.
family of seabirds called frigate birds

Examples

The family Fregatidae includes several species of frigate birds found in tropical oceans.
Males of the family Fregatidae are known for their bright red throat pouches used in displays.
Researchers studied migration patterns within the family Fregatidae to understand their long-distance flights.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Fregata - the type genus of the family Fregatidae
  • Synonym: frigate bird - common name for birds of the family Fregatidae

Etymology

From New Latin 'Fregatidae', based on the type genus 'Fregata' and the family suffix '-idae'.
