king's evil

1. NOUN

/ kɪŋz ˈivəl / (AmE), / kɪŋz ˈiːvəl / (BrE)
a form of tuberculosis characterized by swelling of the lymph glands, especially in the neck (historically known as scrofula)
tubercular swelling of lymph glands (scrofula)
archaic medical

Examples

In medieval England, people believed that the monarch's touch could heal the king's evil.
Many historical accounts describe sufferers of the king's evil seeking royal intervention.
Treatment for the king's evil often involved special ceremonies performed by the king.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scrofula - tuberculosis of the lymph nodes, especially of the neck

Etymology

Originates from the medieval belief that the English and French monarchs could cure the disease by touch.
