out-of-the-box thinking

1. NOUN

/ ˌaʊt əv ðə ˈbɑks ˈθɪŋkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌaʊt əv ðə ˈbɒks ˈθɪŋkɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or process of approaching problems or situations in an unconventional or creative way, leading to new and innovative ideas or solutions
creative or unconventional thinking

Examples

The company encourages out-of-the-box thinking to stay ahead of its competitors.
Her out-of-the-box thinking led to an innovative marketing campaign.
We need out-of-the-box thinking to solve these complex problems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: creative thinking - the process of thinking in new and original ways
  • Antonym: conventional thinking - adhering to traditional ideas or methods

Etymology

From the idiom 'think outside the box', originating in the 20th century, referring to solving the 'nine dots' puzzle by extending lines beyond the perceived boundary.
