enigmatical canon

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛnɪɡˈmætɪkəl ˈkænən / (AmE), / ˌɛnɪɡˈmætɪkəl ˈkanən / (BrE)
A type of musical canon in which the entries of successive voices are indicated by cryptic symbols or riddles, used especially in the 15th and 16th centuries.
musical canon using cryptic notation

Examples

The Renaissance composer created an enigmatical canon whose meaning challenged even the most skilled musicians.
Scholars studied the enigmatical canon to decipher the hidden instructions in its cryptic notation.
Many 16th-century works feature the enigmatical canon, blending musical artistry with intellectual puzzles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: canon - A contrapuntal musical composition in which a melody is imitated after a delay.

Etymology

From 'enigmatical', meaning mysterious or puzzling, and 'canon', a contrapuntal composition technique in music.
