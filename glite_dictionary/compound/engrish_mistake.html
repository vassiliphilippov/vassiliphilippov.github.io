Engrish mistake

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪŋɡrɪʃ mɪˈsteɪk / (AmE), / ˈɪŋɡrɪʃ mɪsˈteɪk / (BrE)
a humorous or awkward error in English, often occurring in translations or signage by non-native speakers
humorous error in English, often in translation
informal

Examples

The sign outside the restaurant had a classic Engrish mistake that made everyone laugh.
Her website is dedicated to collecting amusing Engrish mistakes from around the world.
He snapped a photo of the package because of the hilarious Engrish mistake in its instructions.

Synset Examples

Her blog humorously highlighted common Engrish mistakes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blooper - an embarrassing mistake, especially in speech or writing
  • Synonym: solecism - a grammatical mistake in speech or writing
  • Antonym: correct usage - use of English that is grammatically and idiomatically proper

Etymology

From 'Engrish', a term popularized in Japan to describe flawed English as used by non-native speakers, blending 'English' and Japanese/Asian pronunciation or spelling errors.
