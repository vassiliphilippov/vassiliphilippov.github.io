pectoral muscle

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛk.tɚ.əl ˈmʌs.əl / (AmE), / ˈpɛk.tər.əl ˈmʌs.əl / (BrE)
either of the two large muscles on the front of the chest that move the shoulder and upper arm
large muscle of the chest
medical

Examples

She injured her pectoral muscle while lifting heavy weights at the gym.
The bodybuilder's well-defined pectoral muscle attracted much attention at the competition.
Physical therapy can help recover strength in a damaged pectoral muscle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chest muscle - any muscle located in the chest region

Etymology

From Latin pectoralis (of the chest), from pectus (chest, breast).
