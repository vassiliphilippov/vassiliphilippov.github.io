bad medicine

1. NOUN

/ bæd ˈmɛd.ɪ.sən / (AmE), / bæd ˈmɛd.ɪ.sɪn / (BrE)
treatment that is ineffective or unpleasant, but sometimes considered necessary
ineffective or unpleasant treatment

Examples

Sometimes bad medicine is necessary to achieve a full recovery.
He knew it was bad medicine, but he followed the doctor's orders.
Swallowing the truth was bad medicine for her, but it was for the best.

Synset Examples

That treatment felt like bad medicine but worked.
Sometimes bad medicine is necessary for healing.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: good medicine - treatment that is effective or beneficial
