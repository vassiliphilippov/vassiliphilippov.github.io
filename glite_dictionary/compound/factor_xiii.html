factor XIII

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæk.tɚ ˌθɜr.tin / (AmE), / ˈfæk.tə ˌθɜː.tiːn / (BrE)
a protein in the blood that, when activated by thrombin, helps stabilize blood clots by forming cross-links in fibrin
protein involved in blood clot stabilization
medical

Examples

When a person lacks factor XIII, their blood clots are weak and prone to breaking down.
Doctors may test for factor XIII deficiency in patients with unexplained bleeding.
The activation of factor XIII is a critical step in the final stage of blood coagulation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fibrinase - the activated form of factor XIII that stabilizes fibrin clots
  • Synonym: thrombin - an enzyme that activates clotting factors in the blood

Etymology

Named by numbering as one of the blood clotting factors, using Roman numeral XIII.
