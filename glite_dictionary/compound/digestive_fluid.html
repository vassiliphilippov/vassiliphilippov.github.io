digestive fluid

1. NOUN

/ daɪˈdʒɛstɪv ˈfluɪd / (AmE), / dɪˈdʒɛstɪv ˈfluːɪd / (BrE)
a liquid produced by the body that helps to break down food in the digestive system
liquid aiding food digestion

Examples

The pancreas secretes a digestive fluid containing enzymes that break down proteins.
Bile is an important digestive fluid produced by the liver to help digest fats.
Doctors analyzed the patient's digestive fluid to diagnose his stomach problems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: digestive juice - another term for a liquid aiding digestion
