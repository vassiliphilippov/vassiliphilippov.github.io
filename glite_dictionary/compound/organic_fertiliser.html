organic fertiliser

1. NOUN

/ ɔːrˈgænɪk ˈfɜːrtəˌlaɪzər / (AmE), / ɔːˈɡanɪk ˈfɜːtɪlaɪzə / (BrE)
a fertilizer made from natural animal or plant materials, rather than synthetic chemicals
fertilizer from natural sources

Examples

Many gardeners prefer to use organic fertiliser to enrich their soil naturally.
Tom switched from chemical to organic fertiliser for his vegetable patch last year.
The use of organic fertiliser can help improve soil health over time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fertilizer - a substance added to soil to increase fertility
  • Antonym: chemical fertiliser - a fertilizer made from synthetic chemicals
