day of reckoning

1. NOUN

/ ˈdeɪ əv ˈrɛkənɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdeɪ əv ˈrɛkənɪŋ / (BrE)
(in Christian belief) the day at the end of time when God judges everyone according to their actions; any moment of final judgment or accountability.
final judgment; time of ultimate accountability

Examples

For many believers, the day of reckoning is a time to account for one's actions.
After years of corruption, the day of reckoning finally arrived for the dishonest officials.
Some view environmental crises as humanity's day of reckoning for misusing natural resources.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Judgment Day - the day of God's final judgment of all people

2. NOUN

/ ˌdeɪ əv ˈrɛkənɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌdeɪ əv ˈrekənɪŋ / (BrE)
The time when one must face the consequences or judgement for past actions, often used in the context of a decisive or fateful event.
time of judgment or consequences

Examples

Many fear the day of reckoning when their past decisions finally catch up with them.
The company ignored the warnings, but a financial day of reckoning inevitably arrived.
For years he escaped justice, but his day of reckoning finally came in court.

Synset Examples

everyone was aware of the approaching doom but was helpless to avoid it
that's unfortunate but it isn't the end of the world

Related Terms

  • Synonym: judgment day - the time of God's final judgment of humankind
  • Synonym: doomsday - a time or event of final judgment or destruction

Etymology

From the use of 'reckoning' meaning 'judgment' or 'settling of accounts', with biblical roots referring to judgment day.
