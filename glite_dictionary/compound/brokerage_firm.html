brokerage firm

1. NOUN

/ ˈbroʊkərɪdʒ fɝm / (AmE), / ˈbrəʊkərɪdʒ fɜːm / (BrE)
A company that acts as an intermediary between buyers and sellers of financial securities, typically charging a fee for its services.
company acting as intermediary for securities trades

Examples

She opened an account at a well-known brokerage firm to start investing in stocks.
The brokerage firm charges a commission on every trade executed for its clients.
Many people rely on a brokerage firm for advice and investment management.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brokerage - the business or service of acting as a broker

Etymology

From 'brokerage' (from 'broker' + -age) and 'firm' (from Latin 'firmus', strong).
