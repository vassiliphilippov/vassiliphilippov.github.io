beard lichen

1. NOUN

/ bɪrd ˈlaɪ.kən / (AmE), / bɪəd ˈlaɪ.kən / (BrE)
A type of greenish-grey, hanging lichen that typically grows on trees, resembling a beard in appearance.
greenish-grey hanging lichen

Examples

The branches were covered in beard lichen that swayed gently in the breeze.
Scientists often use beard lichen as an indicator of air quality in forests.
Hikers recognized the tree by the dense growth of beard lichen hanging from its limbs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: usnea - a genus of beard lichens, often referred to as old man's beard
