short vowel

1. NOUN

/ ʃɔrt ˈvaʊəl / (AmE), / ʃɔːt ˈvaʊəl / (BrE)
a vowel sound in a language that is pronounced for a shorter duration than a long vowel
vowel pronounced for a short duration

Examples

In the word 'cat', the short vowel sound is represented by the letter 'a'.
Teachers often emphasize the difference between long and short vowels to help students with pronunciation.
The short vowel sounds in English can be difficult for learners to distinguish from long vowels.

Synset Examples

When a vowel is followed by a consonant, the vowel is short.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: long vowel - a vowel sound pronounced for a longer duration
