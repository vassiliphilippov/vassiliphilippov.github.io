lily of the valley

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪli əv ðə ˈvæli / (AmE), / ˈlɪli əv ðə ˈvæli / (BrE)
a low-growing perennial plant (Convallaria majalis) with two large, broad leaves, fragrant nodding white bell-shaped flowers, and red berries, commonly cultivated as a garden plant
a plant with fragrant white bell-shaped flowers

Examples

The scent of lily of the valley filled the garden in early spring.
She planted lily of the valley along the shady border of her yard.
Lilies of the valley are often used in bridal bouquets for their delicate appearance and fragrance.
