time signal

1. NOUN

/ taɪm ˈsɪɡnəl / (AmE), / taɪm ˈsɪɡnəl / (BrE)
an electronic or radio signal that indicates the precise correct time, often used for synchronizing clocks or broadcasting the official time
signal indicating the exact time

Examples

The radio station broadcasts a time signal every hour so listeners can set their clocks accurately.
The lab relies on a time signal from an atomic clock for synchronizing equipment.
International time signals are crucial for coordinating global telecommunications networks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: timestamp - a digital record of the time something occurred
