chronic eczema

1. NOUN

/ ˌkrɑːnɪk ɪgˈziːmə / (AmE), / ˌkrɒnɪk ɪkˈsiːmə / (BrE)
A persistent form of eczema characterized by thickened skin and more prominent skin lines, often due to ongoing irritation or scratching.
persistent eczema with thickened skin
medical

Examples

The patient suffered from chronic eczema on his hands, which caused constant discomfort.
After years of scratching, her chronic eczema had led to visibly thickened patches of skin.
Effective management can help control the symptoms of chronic eczema and prevent flare-ups.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eczema - an inflammatory skin condition causing itching and redness
