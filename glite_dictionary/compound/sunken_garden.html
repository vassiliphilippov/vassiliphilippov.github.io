sunken garden

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌŋkən ˈɡɑrdn̩ / (AmE), / ˈsʌŋkən ˈɡɑːdn̩ / (BrE)
a garden constructed lower than the surrounding ground, often set into a pit or depression for visual effect
garden set below ground level

Examples

The estate featured a beautiful sunken garden surrounded by stone walls and terraces.
After restoring the property, they planted roses and shrubs in the old sunken garden.
Many historic homes have a sunken garden as a central landscaping feature.
