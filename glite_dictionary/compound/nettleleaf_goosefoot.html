nettleleaf goosefoot

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɛt̬əlˌlif ˈɡusˌfʊt / (AmE), / ˈnɛtəlˌliːf ˈɡuːsfʊt / (BrE)
A European annual plant with coarsely toothed leaves, now widespread in the United States and southern Canada.
a European annual plant with toothed leaves

Examples

The nettleleaf goosefoot is considered a weed in some North American gardens.
You can identify nettleleaf goosefoot by its coarsely serrated leaves.
Researchers studied the spread of nettleleaf goosefoot across the Midwest.
