Irish Gaelic

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪrɪʃ ˈɡeɪlɪk / (AmE), / ˈaɪərɪʃ ˈɡeɪlɪk / (BrE)
the Celtic language spoken in Ireland, also known as Irish
the Celtic language of Ireland

Examples

He studied Irish Gaelic at university to reconnect with his heritage.
Many road signs in Ireland are written in both English and Irish Gaelic.
The government encourages the teaching of Irish Gaelic in schools across the country.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Irish - the Goidelic Celtic language of Ireland
  • Degree Lower: Scottish Gaelic - the Celtic language spoken in Scotland

Etymology

'Gaelic' from Scottish Gaelic 'Gàidhlig', relating to Goidelic languages; 'Irish' specifies the variant spoken in Ireland.

2. NOUN

/ ˌaɪrɪʃ ˈgeɪlɪk / (AmE), / ˌaɪərɪʃ ˈgeɪlɪk / (BrE)
the Celtic language native to Ireland, also known as Irish
Celtic language of Ireland

Examples

She studied Irish Gaelic at university to connect with her heritage.
Many road signs in Ireland are written in both English and Irish Gaelic.
He is fluent in Irish Gaelic and often speaks it with his family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Irish - the Goidelic language of Ireland, also known as Irish Gaelic
  • Degree Lower: Scottish Gaelic - the Celtic language of Scotland closely related to Irish Gaelic

Etymology

From Irish 'Gaeilge' (the Irish language), with 'Gaelic' from Scottish Gaelic 'Gàidhlig'.
