sand viper

1. NOUN

/ sænd ˈvaɪpər / (AmE), / sænd ˈvaɪpə / (BrE)
a harmless North American snake with an upturned nose, known for spreading its head and neck or playing dead when disturbed
harmless North American snake with upturned nose

Examples

The sand viper is often mistaken for venomous snakes because of its appearance.
When threatened, the sand viper may flatten its head to appear larger.
Despite its name, the sand viper poses no danger to humans.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsænd ˌvaɪpər / (AmE), / ˈsænd ˌvaɪpə / (BrE)
a highly venomous viper found in northern Africa and southwestern Asia, distinguished by a horny spine above each eye
a venomous viper of North Africa and Southwest Asia

Examples

A sand viper can be identified by the distinctive horns above its eyes.
Researchers in the desert encountered a sand viper while studying local wildlife.
The bite of a sand viper can be dangerous and requires immediate medical attention.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horned viper - a viper with horn-like structures above the eyes
