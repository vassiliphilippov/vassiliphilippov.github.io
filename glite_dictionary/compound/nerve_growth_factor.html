nerve growth factor

1. NOUN

/ nɜrv ɡroʊθ ˈfæktər / (AmE), / nɜːv ɡrəʊθ ˈfæktə / (BrE)
a protein that promotes the growth, maintenance, and survival of peripheral nerve cells
protein promoting nerve cell growth
medical

Examples

Researchers discovered that nerve growth factor plays a crucial role in the development of the nervous system.
A lack of nerve growth factor can lead to the degeneration of certain types of neurons.
Scientists injected nerve growth factor into damaged tissue to stimulate nerve repair.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neurotrophin - a family of proteins that promote the survival and development of neurons
