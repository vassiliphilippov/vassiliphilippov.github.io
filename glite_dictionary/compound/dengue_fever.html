dengue fever

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛŋɡi ˈfiːvər / (AmE), / ˈdɛŋɡeɪ ˈfiːvə / (BrE)
A tropical infectious disease transmitted by mosquitoes, causing fever, rash, and severe pain in the head and joints.
mosquito-borne tropical disease
medical

Examples

Several outbreaks of dengue fever have occurred in tropical countries in recent years.
She was hospitalized after experiencing severe symptoms of dengue fever.
Mosquito control programs play a crucial role in preventing the spread of dengue fever.

Etymology

From Spanish *dengue*, possibly of African origin, referring to the disease.
