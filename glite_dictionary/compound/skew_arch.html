skew arch

1. NOUN

/ skju ˈɑrtʃ / (AmE), / skjuː ˈɑːtʃ / (BrE)
An arch whose sides, or jambs, are not at right angles to its face, often used in bridges or tunnels where the structure crosses an obstacle at an oblique angle.
arch with sides not at right angles to face

Examples

The engineer designed a skew arch to allow the railway bridge to cross the road at an angle.
Constructing a skew arch requires precise calculations to ensure structural stability.
The old stone skew arch is admired for its unique angled construction.
