gustatory organ

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡʌstəˌtɔri ˈɔrɡən / (AmE), / ˈɡʌstətəri ˈɔːɡən / (BrE)
a sensory structure on the tongue responsible for detecting taste, such as a taste bud
sensory structure for tasting on the tongue
medical

Examples

Scientists studied the gustatory organ to better understand how people perceive flavors.
The function of a gustatory organ is critical for detecting sweet, salty, sour, and bitter tastes.
Damage to a gustatory organ can result in a loss of sense of taste.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: taste bud - a small organ on the tongue that senses taste

Etymology

From Latin gustare (to taste) and organum (organ).
