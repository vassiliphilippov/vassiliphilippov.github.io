structural iron

1. NOUN

/ ˈstrʌktʃərəl ˈaɪərn / (AmE), / ˈstrʌktʃərəl ˈaɪən / (BrE)
Iron that has been cast or worked into shapes used for building and construction purposes.
iron shaped for building structures

Examples

The building's frame was constructed from structural iron for maximum strength.
Early skyscrapers relied heavily on structural iron before steel became more common.
Repairs required replacing several beams made of structural iron that had weakened over time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: structural steel - steel shaped for use in building structures
