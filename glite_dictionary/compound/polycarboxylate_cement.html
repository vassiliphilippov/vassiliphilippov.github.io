polycarboxylate cement

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑliˌkɑrˈbɑksəˌleɪt ˈsɛmɛnt / (AmE), / ˌpɒliˌkɑːˈbɒksəleɪt ˈsɛmɛnt / (BrE)
a dental cement used as a luting agent for securing restorations and as a lining for cavities in teeth
dental cement for cementing restorations
medical

Examples

The dentist chose polycarboxylate cement to fix the crown securely in place.
Because of its mild acidity, polycarboxylate cement is often preferred for lining deep cavities.
Proper mixing is essential to achieve the best strength with polycarboxylate cement.

Synset Examples

Polycarboxylate cement has the potential to bond to calcium contained in tooth structure as well as to any base metals contained in casting.

Etymology

Formed from 'polycarboxylate' (a polymer containing multiple carboxylate groups) and 'cement' (from Latin caementum, stone chips for making mortar).
