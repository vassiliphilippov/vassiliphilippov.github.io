cerebral death

1. NOUN

/ səˈribrəl dɛθ / (AmE), / sɛˈriːbrəl dɛθ / (BrE)
A medical condition in which brain activity has irreversibly ceased, consciousness is lost, and reflexes are absent, although other body functions may continue temporarily.
irreversible loss of brain function
medical

Examples

The doctors concluded that the patient had suffered cerebral death after extensive neurological testing.
Organ donation is sometimes possible following cerebral death, before vital functions fully cease.
Guidelines for diagnosing cerebral death vary between different countries and medical institutions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brain death - irreversible cessation of all brain activity

Etymology

From Latin 'cerebrum' meaning brain, combined with 'death'.
