out of the way

1. ADV

/ aʊt əv ðə weɪ / (AmE), / aʊt əv ðə weɪ / (BrE)
in a place that is remote or not on the usual route; at a distance from where people usually go
in a remote or inconvenient place

Examples

Their cabin was so out of the way that few people ever visited.
We stopped at an out of the way café that locals recommended.
He enjoys hiking in out of the way places far from the city.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: remote - far from the centers of population; distant
  • Synonym: inconvenient - causing trouble, difficulties, or discomfort
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00041525-r
Synset POS: r

2. ADJ

/ aʊt əv ði weɪ / (AmE), / aʊt əv ðə weɪ / (BrE)
improper, unusual, or likely to cause offense; not conforming to accepted standards of behavior or speech.
improper or offensive (of remarks, behavior)

Examples

His comment at dinner was completely out of the way and shocked the guests.
Please try not to make any out of the way remarks during the meeting.
Her out of the way behavior often offends people around her.

Synset Examples

out-of-the-way remarks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: improper - not conforming to accepted standards; inappropriate
  • Synonym: offensive - causing someone to feel deeply upset, insulted, or annoyed
  • Antonym: appropriate - suitable or proper in the circumstances
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01887447-s
Synset POS: s
