genus Daucus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒen.əs ˈdaʊ.kəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈdaʊ.kəs / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the parsley family (Apiaceae), including the carrot and related species.
genus of plants including the carrot

Examples

The genus Daucus includes both wild and cultivated species such as the common carrot.
Researchers are studying the genetic diversity within the genus Daucus.
Several weeds in the genus Daucus can be found in meadows across Europe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carrot - an orange root vegetable; Daucus carota

Etymology

From Latin 'Daucus', the name for the carrot, and 'genus', meaning 'kind' or 'type'.
