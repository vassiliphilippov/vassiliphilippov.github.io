Huntingdon willow

1. NOUN

/ ˈhʌntɪŋdən ˈwɪloʊ / (AmE), / ˈhʌntɪŋdən ˈwɪləʊ / (BrE)
A large willow tree of Eurasia and North Africa with greyish, silvery leaves and grey bark, commonly grown as an ornamental.
type of large Eurasian willow tree

Examples

The park was lined with magnificent Huntingdon willow trees along the riverbank.
Gardeners often choose the Huntingdon willow for its graceful appearance and rapid growth.
Botanists noted the distinctive grey leaves of the Huntingdon willow during their survey.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: willow - a tree or shrub of the genus Salix, found in moist soils

Etymology

Named after Huntingdon, a town in England, where the hybrid originated.
