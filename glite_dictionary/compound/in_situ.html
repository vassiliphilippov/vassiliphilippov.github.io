in situ

1. ADV

/ ɪn ˈsiːtu / (AmE), / ɪn ˈsɪtjuː / (BrE)
in the original or natural place or position; not moved from where it was found or formed
in the original place or position

Examples

The fossils were preserved in situ by the research team to avoid damage.
Doctors identified a tumor in situ, meaning it had not yet spread.
The mosaic remains in situ in the ancient villa where it was discovered.

Synset Examples

carcinoma in situ
the archeologists left the pottery in place

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ex situ - outside the original or natural place

Etymology

From Latin, literally 'in the place'.

Examples
