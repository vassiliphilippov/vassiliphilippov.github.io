genus Thujopsis

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒiːnəs θuːˈdʒɒpsɪs / (AmE), / ˌdʒiːnəs θjuːˈdʒɒpsɪs / (BrE)
A genus of coniferous trees closely related to genus Thuja, containing only one species, native to Japan.
a genus of coniferous trees (Thujopsis)

Examples

The genus Thujopsis consists of only a single species native to Japan.
Botanists compared specimens of genus Thujopsis with those from genus Thuja.
Many gardeners appreciate plants from the genus Thujopsis for their ornamental value.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: genus Thuja - a related genus of coniferous trees known as arborvitae

Etymology

From scientific Latin, formed from Greek 'thuja' (a kind of tree) and 'opsis' (appearance).
