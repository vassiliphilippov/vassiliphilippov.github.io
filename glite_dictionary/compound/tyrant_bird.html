tyrant bird

1. NOUN

/ ˈtaɪrənt bɝd / (AmE), / ˈtaɪrənt bɜːd / (BrE)
a large American bird of the tyrant flycatcher family, typically characterized by catching insects in flight
large American insect-catching bird

Examples

The tyrant bird swooped down to catch a dragonfly on the wing.
Naturalists observed several tyrant birds feeding in the forest canopy at dusk.
Many species of tyrant bird are known for their impressive aerial hunting skills.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flycatcher - a bird that catches insects in flight, especially of the tyrant family

Etymology

Named for their aggressive behavior, from 'tyrant' + 'bird'.
