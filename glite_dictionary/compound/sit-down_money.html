sit-down money

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪt daʊn ˈmʌni / (AmE), / ˈsɪt daʊn ˈmʌni / (BrE)
money set aside for a specific purpose, especially as initial funding for a project or venture
initial funding or seed money

Examples

She was relieved to have enough sit-down money to sign the rental contract.
Many entrepreneurs struggle to find sit-down money for their startups.
His parents gave him some sit-down money when he moved out on his own.

Synset Examples
