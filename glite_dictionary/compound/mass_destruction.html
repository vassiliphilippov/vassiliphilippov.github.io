mass destruction

1. NOUN

/ mæs dɪˈstrʌkʃən / (AmE), / mæs dɪˈstrʌkʃən / (BrE)
widespread and extensive damage or destruction affecting a large area or number of things, often caused by war, disasters, or powerful weapons.
widespread and extensive destruction

Examples

The hurricane left a trail of mass destruction along the coast.
International agreements aim to prevent the use of weapons of mass destruction.
The massive explosion caused mass destruction in the city center.

Synset Examples

The war led to mass destruction across the country.
Nuclear weapons can cause mass destruction rapidly.
The earthquake resulted in mass destruction of buildings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: devastation - severe and overwhelming damage or destruction
  • Synonym: annihilation - complete destruction or obliteration
