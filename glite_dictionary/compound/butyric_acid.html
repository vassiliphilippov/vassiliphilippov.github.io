butyric acid

1. NOUN

/ bjuˈtɪrɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / bjuːˈtɪrɪk ˈæsɪd / (BrE)
a fatty acid with a strong, unpleasant smell, occurring especially in rancid butter and some other animal fats
a foul-smelling fatty acid found in butter

Examples

Rancid butter contains high levels of butyric acid, responsible for its strong odor.
Researchers analyzed the concentration of butyric acid in various dairy products.
The smell of butyric acid can be quite overwhelming even in small amounts.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: caproic acid - a saturated fatty acid with a similar structure and properties

Etymology

From Latin butyrum (butter) + -ic, referring to its discovery in butter.
