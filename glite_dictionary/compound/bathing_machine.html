bathing machine

1. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪðɪŋ məˈʃin / (AmE), / ˈbeɪðɪŋ məˈʃiːn / (BrE)
a small hut on wheels used at beaches in the 18th and 19th centuries to allow people to change clothes and enter the sea in privacy
historical beach changing hut on wheels
archaic

Examples

On Victorian beaches, a bathing machine offered privacy for swimmers to change into their bathing suits.
The old photograph shows a row of bathing machines lined up along the shore.
Historians often mention the role of the bathing machine in the evolution of beachgoing customs.
