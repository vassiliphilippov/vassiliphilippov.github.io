rocket science

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɑːkɪt ˌsaɪəns / (AmE), / ˈrɒkɪt ˌsaɪəns / (BrE)
used to describe something that is very complicated or difficult to understand, often in negative phrases to suggest that something is actually simple
something very complicated or difficult

Examples

Fixing a leaking faucet isn't rocket science, you just need the right tools.
Learning to ride a bike is not rocket science—anyone can do it with practice.
Although it sounds complex, programming this app isn't exactly rocket science.

Synset Examples

Don't worry, it's not rocket science to fix this.
Building a website is easier than rocket science.
Understanding basic math isn't rocket science.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: child's play - something very easy to do
  • Synonym: not brain surgery - something not difficult or complex, similar in meaning to 'not rocket science'

Etymology

Refers to the complexity of actual rocket science (the science of designing and launching rockets), used metaphorically since the late 20th century.
