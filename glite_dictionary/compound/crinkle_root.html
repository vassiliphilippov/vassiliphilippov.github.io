crinkle root

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɪŋkəl ruːt / (AmE), / ˈkrɪŋkl ruːt / (BrE)
A North American herb with pungent, scaly or toothed roots, used sometimes as a condiment or for medicinal purposes.
North American herb with pungent roots

Examples

The chef added grated crinkle root to the salad for an extra kick.
Native Americans traditionally used crinkle root for its medicinal properties.
You'll find crinkle root growing wild in damp, wooded areas across North America.
