blow tube

1. NOUN

/ bloʊ tuːb / (AmE), / bləʊ tjuːb / (BrE)
a tube through which air or gas is blown into a flame to increase its heat or intensity, especially in scientific or glassworking applications
tube for directing air/gas into a flame

Examples

The glassblower carefully inserted the blow tube into the furnace to heat the molten glass.
A blow tube is essential in some chemistry experiments to obtain a hotter flame.
She used a blow tube to direct oxygen into the flame and shape the delicate ornament.

2. NOUN

/ bloʊ tuːb / (AmE), / bləʊ tjuːb / (BrE)
A tube used to shoot darts or other projectiles by blowing air through it.
tube for shooting darts by blowing

Examples

He made a blow tube from a plastic straw to shoot paper darts.
The indigenous tribe demonstrated how to hunt with a blow tube.
Kids used a homemade blow tube to launch tiny projectiles across the room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blowgun - a tube for shooting darts by blowing through it
