Libyan Islamic Fighting Group

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪbiən aɪˈslæmɪk ˈfaɪtɪŋ ɡruːp / (AmE), / ˈlɪbiən aɪˈslæmɪk ˈfaɪtɪŋ ɡruːp / (BrE)
a militant Islamist group formed in Libya in 1995, known for its opposition to the Libyan government and alignment with al-Qaeda
Libyan Islamist militant group (est. 1995)

Examples

The Libyan Islamic Fighting Group was accused of plotting attacks against the government in the late 1990s.
Several members of the Libyan Islamic Fighting Group later joined other militant organizations.
Reports linked the Libyan Islamic Fighting Group to al-Qaeda after secret meetings in Afghanistan.
