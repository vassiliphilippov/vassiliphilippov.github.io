Shoshonean language

1. NOUN

/ ʃoʊˈʃoʊniən ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ʃəˈʃəʊniːən ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
A subfamily of Uto-Aztecan languages spoken mainly in the southwestern United States.
Uto-Aztecan language subfamily

Examples

Many tribes in the Great Basin historically spoke a Shoshonean language.
Linguists study the similarities between each Shoshonean language in the Uto-Aztecan family.
The preservation of a Shoshonean language is important for maintaining cultural heritage.

Etymology

From 'Shoshone', the name of an indigenous people, plus 'an', and 'language'.
