genus Limosa

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs laɪˈmoʊsə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs lɪˈməʊsə / (BrE)
A genus of large, long-billed wading birds known as godwits, found in wetlands and coastal regions.
a genus of wading birds (godwits)

Examples

Several species within the genus Limosa migrate thousands of miles each year.
The scientist observed members of the genus Limosa feeding along the mudflats.
Identification of the genus Limosa is based on their long, upturned bills and long legs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: godwit - a large wading bird of the genus Limosa

Etymology

From Latin 'Limosa', possibly from 'limosus' meaning 'muddy', referring to the bird's habitat.
