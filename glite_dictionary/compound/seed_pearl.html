seed pearl

1. NOUN

/ siːd pɜrl / (AmE), / siːd pɜːl / (BrE)
a very small, usually imperfect pearl, often used in clusters for decoration in jewelry.
a very small, imperfect pearl

Examples

The antique necklace was adorned with delicate seed pearls along its chain.
She preferred earrings that featured a single seed pearl for a subtle touch of elegance.
Tiny seed pearls were sewn onto the bridal veil as intricate ornaments.
