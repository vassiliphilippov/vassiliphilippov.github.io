indefinite pronoun

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈdɛfɪnət proʊˈnaʊn / (AmE), / ɪnˈdɛfɪnət prəˈnaʊn / (BrE)
a pronoun that does not refer to a specific person, thing, or amount, such as 'someone', 'anybody', or 'nothing'
pronoun with no specific referent

Examples

In the sentence, 'Is there anyone at home?', 'anyone' is an indefinite pronoun.
Words like 'everything' and 'nobody' are often used as indefinite pronouns in English.
When you say, 'Somebody left their bag,' 'somebody' functions as an indefinite pronoun.

Synset Examples

In ‘many disagree with his views’ the word ‘many’ functions as an indefinite pronoun, while in ‘many people disagree with his views’ it functions as a quantifier.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pronoun - a word that replaces a noun

Etymology

From Latin prefix 'in-' (not) + 'definitus' (defined) + English 'pronoun'.
