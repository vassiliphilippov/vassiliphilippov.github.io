tomato paste

1. NOUN

/ təˈmeɪɾoʊ peɪst / (AmE), / təˈmɑːtəʊ peɪst / (BrE)
a thick, concentrated paste made from cooked and strained tomatoes, used as a base or ingredient in cooking
thick concentrated tomato puree

Examples

Stir two tablespoons of tomato paste into the soup for extra richness.
The recipe called for both diced tomatoes and tomato paste to intensify the flavor.
We always keep a can of tomato paste in the pantry for making sauces.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: tomato purée - smooth, liquid mixture of cooked tomatoes
  • Degree Lower: tomato sauce - sauce made from tomatoes, often seasoned
