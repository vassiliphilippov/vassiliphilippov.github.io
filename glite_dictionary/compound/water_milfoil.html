water milfoil

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔtər ˈmɪlˌfɔɪl / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə ˈmɪl.fɔɪl / (BrE)
an aquatic plant of the genus Myriophyllum, characterized by feathery submerged leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers
aquatic plant with feathery submerged leaves

Examples

Invasive water milfoil can quickly overtake lakes, disrupting native aquatic ecosystems.
Divers pulled strands of water milfoil from the marina to prevent it from spreading further.
The feathery leaves of water milfoil are easily distinguished from other underwater plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: milfoil - a plant of the genus Achillea or Myriophyllum, with finely divided leaves

Etymology

From 'milfoil' (yarrow), itself from Latin 'millefolium', meaning 'thousand leaves', here referring to the plant's finely divided leaves; 'water' indicates its aquatic habitat.
