central vein of liver

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛntrəl veɪn əv ˈlɪvər / (AmE), / ˈsɛntrəl veɪn ɒv ˈlɪvə / (BrE)
a small vein located at the center of each hepatic lobule, receiving blood from the liver sinusoids and draining into the hepatic veins
small vein in liver lobule
medical

Examples

The central vein of liver is crucial for draining blood from each hepatic lobule.
Damage to the central vein of liver can affect liver function significantly.
Each hepatic lobule contains a single central vein of liver at its core.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hepatic vein - a blood vessel that carries blood from the liver to the heart
  • Degree Lower: liver sinusoid - a type of capillary found in the liver lobules
