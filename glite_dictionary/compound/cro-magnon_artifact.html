Cro-Magnon artifact

1. NOUN

/ kroʊ ˈmæn.jən ˈɑːr.tə.fækt / (AmE), / krəʊ ˈmæɡ.nɒn ˈɑː.tɪ.fækt / (BrE)
an object made or used by Cro-Magnon humans, representing the culture and technology of early modern humans in Europe
object from Cro-Magnon humans

Examples

The discovery of a new Cro-Magnon artifact shed light on prehistoric craftsmanship.
Many Cro-Magnon artifacts display sophisticated techniques for their time.
Museums often feature Cro-Magnon artifact exhibits to educate visitors about early human history.

Synset Examples

Researchers study Cro-Magnon artifacts for cultural understanding.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: artifact - object made or modified by humans, typically of historical interest

Etymology

Cro-Magnon: From the name of a site in France where remains of early modern humans were discovered; artifact: from Latin arte factum, meaning 'something made with skill.'
