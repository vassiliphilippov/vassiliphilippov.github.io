yellow mombin

1. NOUN

/ ˈjɛloʊ ˈmɒmbɪn / (AmE), / ˈjɛləʊ ˈmɒmbɪn / (BrE)
a tropical American tree (Spondias mombin) bearing edible yellow fruit, grown for its fruit and ornamental value
a tropical American fruit tree

Examples

The yellow mombin produces small, tangy fruits that are popular in local markets.
She made a refreshing juice from ripe yellow mombin harvested in her backyard.
Farmers in Central America cultivate the yellow mombin for both its fruit and shade.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hog plum - another common name for yellow mombin

2. NOUN

/ ˈjɛloʊ ˈmɑmbɪn / (AmE), / ˈjɛləʊ ˈmɒmbɪn / (BrE)
A small, yellow, oval-shaped tropical fruit native to the Americas, often used in jams, beverages, or eaten fresh.
a yellow, oval tropical fruit

Examples

The market had a basket full of fresh yellow mombin for sale.
She made a delicious jam using ripe yellow mombin from her garden.
Many birds are attracted to the sweet flesh of the yellow mombin.

Etymology

From Portuguese 'mombim', from Tupi 'mõbi'.
