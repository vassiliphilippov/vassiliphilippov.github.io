lycaenid butterfly

1. NOUN

/ laɪˈsiːnɪd ˈbʌt̬ɚˌflaɪ / (AmE), / laɪˈsiːnɪd ˈbʌtəflaɪ / (BrE)
Any butterfly belonging to the family Lycaenidae, typically small and brightly colored.
butterfly of the Lycaenidae family

Examples

The lycaenid butterfly is known for its iridescent blue wings and delicate appearance.
Several lycaenid butterflies were spotted near the wildflowers in early summer.
The garden attracts a variety of species, including the rare lycaenid butterfly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: butterfly - an insect of the order Lepidoptera with large, colorful wings

Etymology

From New Latin Lycaenidae, the scientific family name, ultimately from Greek Lycaena (a genus name).
