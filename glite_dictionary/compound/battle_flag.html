battle flag

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæɾl ˌflæɡ / (AmE), / ˈbatəl ˌflaɡ / (BrE)
A flag carried at the front of a military formation to serve as a rallying point or symbol during battle.
flag carried into battle by troops

Examples

The regiment advanced behind their battle flag, lifting the soldiers' spirits.
During the ceremony, the old battle flag was displayed with pride.
The enemy managed to capture the battle flag, a significant blow to the army's morale.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: standard - military or ceremonial flag, especially one used as a symbol by an army
