police matron

1. NOUN

/ pəˈlis ˈmeɪtrən / (AmE), / pəˈliːs ˈmeɪtrən / (BrE)
a female officer, often responsible for supervising women or children in police custody
female police officer supervising women or children

Examples

The police matron escorted the female suspect to the holding cell.
At the turn of the century, a police matron was stationed at every major city jail.
The police matron ensured that the children in custody were safe and comfortable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: matron - a woman in charge of domestic and medical arrangements

Etymology

From 'police' + 'matron', where 'matron' comes from Latin 'matrona' (married woman).
