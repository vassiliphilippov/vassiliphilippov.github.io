ship canal

1. NOUN

/ ʃɪp kəˈnæl / (AmE), / ʃɪp kəˈnal / (BrE)
A canal wide and deep enough to allow seagoing ships to pass through.
canal for large seagoing vessels

Examples

The Panama ship canal is vital for international trade.
Engineers debated the construction of a new ship canal to connect the rivers.
A major ship canal can drastically shorten shipping routes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: canal - an artificial waterway for navigation or irrigation
