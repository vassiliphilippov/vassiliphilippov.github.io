connotative of

1. ADJ

/ ˈkɑː.nə.teɪ.tɪv əv / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.ə.tə.tɪv əv / (BrE)
having the quality of suggesting or implying a secondary meaning or association beyond the literal definition, especially as related to connotation.
suggesting an additional or implied meaning

Examples

The phrase is connotative of wealth and privilege.
Her smile was connotative of approval, though she said nothing.
That imagery is connotative of both hope and nostalgia.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: denotative of - having the quality of indicating the literal, primary meaning of a word

Etymology

From 'connotative' (from Latin 'connotare' to mark in addition) + 'of'.
