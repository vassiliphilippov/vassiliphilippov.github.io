swinging chad

1. NOUN

/ ˈswɪŋɪŋ tʃæd / (AmE), / ˈswɪŋɪŋ tʃad / (BrE)
A piece of paper on a punched card ballot that is incompletely removed and remains attached at two corners, potentially causing uncertainty in vote tabulation.
partially detached ballot fragment

Examples

The recount revealed several ballots with swinging chads that complicated the final results.
Election officials carefully inspected each swinging chad to determine if a vote had been properly cast.
The controversy centered around whether ballots with swinging chads should be counted as valid votes.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: hanging chad - a chad still attached to a ballot at one corner
  • Degree Lower: pregnant chad - a chad indented but not detached from the ballot
  • Degree Lower: dimpled chad - a chad marked but not perforated
