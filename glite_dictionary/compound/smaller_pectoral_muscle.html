smaller pectoral muscle

1. NOUN

/ smɔːlər pɛkˈtɔːrəl ˈmʌsəl / (AmE), / smɔːlə pɛkˈtɔːrəl ˈmʌsl̩ / (BrE)
a thin, triangular skeletal muscle located beneath the pectoralis major that draws the scapula downward or raises the ribs
chest muscle under the pectoralis major
medical

Examples

The smaller pectoral muscle helps move your shoulder blade forward and down.
Injuries to the smaller pectoral muscle can cause shoulder pain and limit mobility.
During deep inhalation, the smaller pectoral muscle assists in lifting the ribs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pectoralis minor - the anatomical name for the smaller pectoral muscle
  • Degree Higher: pectoralis major - the larger pectoral muscle covering the upper chest
