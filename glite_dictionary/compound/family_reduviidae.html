family Reduviidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli rɪˌduːviˈaɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæməli rɪˌdjuːvɪˈiːdaɪ / (BrE)
a family of predatory insects known as assassin bugs, characterized by their elongated bodies and piercing mouthparts
a family of assassin bugs (insects)

Examples

The family Reduviidae includes many species commonly referred to as assassin bugs.
Researchers studying the family Reduviidae are interested in their predatory behaviors and roles in ecosystems.
Some members of the family Reduviidae are known to transmit Chagas disease in Central and South America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assassin bug - a predatory bug of the family Reduviidae

Etymology

From the New Latin 'Reduviidae', based on the type genus 'Reduvius' plus the family suffix '-idae'.
