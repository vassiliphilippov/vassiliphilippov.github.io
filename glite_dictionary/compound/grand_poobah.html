grand poobah

1. NOUN

/ ɡrænd ˈpuːbɑː / (AmE), / ɡrænd ˈpuːbɑː / (BrE)
a person who holds great authority or importance, often used humorously for someone who has an exaggerated sense of their own importance
person of great (often self-important) authority
informal

Examples

He was appointed the grand poobah of the committee, much to everyone's amusement.
At the annual dinner, the grand poobah gave a long-winded speech.
Everyone deferred to the grand poobah, although some thought the title was a bit silly.

Synset Examples

He likes to think of himself as the grand poobah of the project.
The grand poobah made all the final decisions.
As the grand poobah, she enjoys the spotlight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bigwig - an important or influential person
  • Synonym: VIP - a very important person

Etymology

Pseudonymous title originating from the character 'Pooh-Bah' in the Gilbert and Sullivan operetta The Mikado (1885).
