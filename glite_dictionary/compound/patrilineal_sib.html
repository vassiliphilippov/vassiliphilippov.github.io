patrilineal sib

1. NOUN

/ ˌpætrɪˈlɪniəl sɪb / (AmE), / ˌpatrɪˈlɪnɪəl sɪb / (BrE)
a member of a kinship group related through descent from the father's side of the family
relative through father's lineage

Examples

In traditional societies, inheritance often passes from one patrilineal sib to another through the father's lineage.
Anthropologists studied how each patrilineal sib traced their ancestry to a common male ancestor.
She was considered part of her father's patrilineal sib but not her mother's clan.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: matrilineal sib - a member of a kinship group related through the mother's side

Etymology

From Latin 'pater' (father) + 'linea' (line) and 'sib', short for sibling or kin member.
