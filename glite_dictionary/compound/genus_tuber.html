genus Tuber

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈtuː.bər / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈtjuː.bə / (BrE)
The genus of fungi in the family Tuberaceae, which includes species commonly known as truffles.
genus of fungi including truffles

Examples

The genus Tuber is famous for producing highly prized edible truffles.
Botanists studied several new species within the genus Tuber discovered in France.
Many chefs seek fungi from the genus Tuber for their rich flavor in gourmet cuisine.

Etymology

From Latin 'tuber', meaning 'swelling' or 'lump', referring to the shape of truffles.
