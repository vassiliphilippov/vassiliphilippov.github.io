creeping fern

1. NOUN

/ ˈkripɪŋ fɝn / (AmE), / ˈkriːpɪŋ fɜːn / (BrE)
a delicate fern of the eastern United States with a twining stem, palmately-lobed sterile fronds, and forked fertile fronds
delicate eastern U.S. fern with twining stem

Examples

The forest floor was covered with creeping ferns winding around the tree trunks.
Gardeners often plant creeping fern as an attractive ground cover in shaded areas.
The creeping fern thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial shade.
