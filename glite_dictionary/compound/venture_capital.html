venture capital

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɛn.tʃɚ ˌkæp.ɪ.təl / (AmE), / ˈvɛn.tʃə ˌkæp.ɪ.təl / (BrE)
money invested in new or emerging companies, especially those involving risk or innovation, in exchange for potential financial returns
investment in start-up or risky businesses

Examples

The startup was able to grow rapidly thanks to significant funding from venture capital.
Many high-tech companies rely on venture capital to develop their products and enter the market.
Without access to venture capital, most entrepreneurs would struggle to launch innovative businesses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: private equity - investment in private companies not listed on public exchanges

Etymology

From 'venture' (risk, enterprise) and 'capital' (financial assets), used together since the mid-20th century in business.
