testicular vein

1. NOUN

/ tɛˈstɪkjələr veɪn / (AmE), / tɛˈstɪkjʊlə veɪn / (BrE)
A vein that carries blood away from the testicles toward the heart.
vein that drains blood from the testicles
medical

Examples

A blockage in the testicular vein can lead to swelling and discomfort.
The surgeon examined the patient's testicular vein during the procedure.
Ultrasound imaging revealed an abnormality in his left testicular vein.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spermatic vein - vein accompanying the spermatic cord in males (synonym for testicular vein)

Etymology

From Latin 'testiculus' (testicle) and 'vena' (vein).
