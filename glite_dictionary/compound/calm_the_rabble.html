to calm the rabble

1. VERB

/ kɑm ðə ˈræbəl / (AmE), / kɑːm ðə ˈræbəl / (BrE)
to pacify or quieten a noisy or unruly crowd, especially one that is disorderly or difficult to control
pacify an unruly crowd

Examples

After the protest turned chaotic, police tried to calm the rabble with a firm but peaceful approach.
The coach did his best to calm the rabble when the fans started booing the referee's decision.
It took several announcements to calm the rabble and restore order in the packed auditorium.

Synset Examples

She tried to calm the rabble after the announcement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to pacify - to bring peace or calm to; to placate
