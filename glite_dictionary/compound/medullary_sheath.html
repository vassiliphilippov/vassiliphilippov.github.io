medullary sheath

1. NOUN

/ məˈdʌləri ʃiθ / (AmE), / mɪˈdʌl.əri ʃiːθ / (BrE)
a layer of myelin that encloses and insulates the axons of certain nerve fibers
myelin layer around nerve axons
medical

Examples

The medullary sheath plays a crucial role in speeding up nerve impulse conduction.
Damage to the medullary sheath can result in neurological disorders such as multiple sclerosis.
During development, the medullary sheath gradually forms around the axons in the central nervous system.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: myelin sheath - the insulating layer around many nerve fibers

Etymology

From Latin 'medullaris' (pertaining to marrow) and 'sheath'.
