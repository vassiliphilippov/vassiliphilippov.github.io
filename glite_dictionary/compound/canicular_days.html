canicular days

1. NOUN

/ kəˈnɪkjələr deɪz / (AmE), / kəˈnɪkjʊlə deɪz / (BrE)
The hottest period of the year, typically between early July and early September, often associated with summer inactivity.
hottest period of the year

Examples

During the canicular days, many people retreat to air-conditioned spaces to escape the heat.
The city seemed to slow down in the canicular days of August, with streets nearly empty by noon.
Farm work was postponed until evening during the canicular days when the sun was less intense.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dog days - the hottest period of summer

Etymology

From Latin 'canicula' (little dog), referring to the constellation Sirius, whose rising marked this period.
