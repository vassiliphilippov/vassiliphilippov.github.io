ideal solid

1. NOUN

/ aɪˈdiːəl ˈsɑːlɪd / (AmE), / aɪˈdɪəl ˈsɒlɪd / (BrE)
any of the five three-dimensional shapes whose faces are congruent regular polygons and whose angles are all congruent, known as the Platonic solids
a three-dimensional Platonic solid

Examples

The cube is an example of an ideal solid composed of six square faces.
Mathematicians have studied each ideal solid since ancient times for its symmetrical properties.
The regularity of an ideal solid makes it unique among polyhedra.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Platonic solid - any one of five regular convex polyhedra with congruent faces and angles
