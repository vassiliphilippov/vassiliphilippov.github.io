sprig tail

1. NOUN

/ sprɪɡ teɪl / (AmE), / sprɪɡ teɪl / (BrE)
A large grouse found in prairies and open forests of western North America, known for its distinctively long, pointed tail feathers.
large North American grouse

Examples

A sprig tail was spotted dancing in the early morning light on the prairie.
Local hunters often seek the elusive sprig tail during the fall season.
The sharp-tailed grouse, commonly called a sprig tail, thrives in open grasslands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sharp-tailed grouse - a prairie grouse of North America with a pointed tail
