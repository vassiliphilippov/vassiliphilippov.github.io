Kaposi's varicelliform eruption

1. NOUN

/ kəˈpoʊsiz vəˌrɪsəˈlɪfɔrm ɪˈrʌpʃən / (AmE), / kəˈpəʊsiz vəˌrɪsɪˈlɪfɔːm ɪˈrʌpʃən / (BrE)
a rare, severe skin infection marked by high fever and widespread blisters, typically occurring as a complication of a viral infection in people with atopic dermatitis
rare blistering skin complication of viral infection
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with Kaposi's varicelliform eruption after developing widespread vesicular lesions and a high fever.
Prompt treatment is essential when Kaposi's varicelliform eruption develops in children with atopic dermatitis.
Some medical textbooks describe Kaposi's varicelliform eruption as a rare but serious complication of viral infections in the skin.

Etymology

Named after Moritz Kaposi, Hungarian dermatologist; 'varicelliform' indicates resemblance to varicella (chickenpox).
