Alaska Peninsula

1. PROPN

/ əˈlæskə pəˈnɪnsjələ / (AmE), / əˈlæskə pəˈnɪnsjʊlə / (BrE)
a peninsula in southwestern Alaska extending southwest from the mainland, forming the northeastern limit of the Aleutian Islands chain
peninsula in southwestern Alaska

Examples

The bears on the Alaska Peninsula are renowned for their size and strength.
Salmon runs on the Alaska Peninsula attract both wildlife and fishermen each summer.
Scientists traveled to the Alaska Peninsula to study its unique volcanic landscape.
