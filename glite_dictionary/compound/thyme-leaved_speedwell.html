thyme-leaved speedwell

1. NOUN

/ ˈtaɪm liːvd ˈspiːdˌwɛl / (AmE), / ˈtaɪm liːvd ˈspiːdwɛl / (BrE)
A low-growing perennial herb with small, opposite leaves and racemes of blue flowers, found throughout Eurasia and the Americas.
perennial herb with small leaves and blue flowers

Examples

The thyme-leaved speedwell spread quickly across the meadow with its delicate blue flowers.
Gardeners admire the thyme-leaved speedwell for its attractive foliage and subtle beauty.
Botanists collected samples of the thyme-leaved speedwell to study its adaptation to different soils.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: speedwell - any of various plants of the genus Veronica
