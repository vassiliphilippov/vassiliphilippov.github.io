sliding seat

1. NOUN

/ ˈslaɪdɪŋ siːt / (AmE), / ˈslaɪdɪŋ siːt / (BrE)
a seat in a rowing boat that moves backwards and forwards on runners, allowing the rower to use their legs for added power
movable seat in a rowing boat

Examples

The racing shell was equipped with a sliding seat to maximize the rower's power.
With the invention of the sliding seat, rowing became more efficient and faster.
She adjusted her sliding seat before the regatta to ensure optimal performance.
