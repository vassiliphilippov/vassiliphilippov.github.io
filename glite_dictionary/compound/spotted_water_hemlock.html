spotted water hemlock

1. NOUN

/ ˈspɑːt̬ɪd ˈwɔːt̬ɚ ˈhɛmˌlɑk / (AmE), / ˈspɒtɪd ˈwɔːtə ˈhɛmlɒk / (BrE)
a tall, poisonous North American plant with purple-spotted stems and clusters of tuberous roots, highly toxic if ingested
poisonous North American plant with purple-spotted stems

Examples

Children should be taught to recognize spotted water hemlock, as its roots are extremely toxic and can be deadly if eaten.
The purple-spotted stems make spotted water hemlock easy to identify in wet meadows and stream banks.
Despite its resemblance to edible plants, spotted water hemlock is one of the most poisonous plants native to North America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hemlock - a poisonous plant with divided leaves and small white flowers
  • Synonym: water hemlock - a group of extremely poisonous plants of the genus Cicuta
