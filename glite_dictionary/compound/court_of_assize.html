court of assize

1. NOUN

/ kɔrt əv əˈsaɪz / (AmE), / kɔːt əv əˈsaɪz / (BrE)
a county court in England and Wales, historically responsible for serious criminal cases, replaced in 1971 by Crown courts
historic English county criminal court
legal british archaic

Examples

The last court of assize in the county was held in 1971 before the system was replaced.
Cases of murder and other serious crimes were brought before the court of assize.
Historically, judges travelled the country to preside over the court of assize sessions.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Crown court - the main criminal court in England and Wales, successor to court of assize

Etymology

From Old French 'assise' (session, sitting), from 'asseoir' (to sit), relating to the session of judges.
