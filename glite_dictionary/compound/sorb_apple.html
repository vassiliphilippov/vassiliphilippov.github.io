sorb apple

1. NOUN

/ sɔrb ˈæp.əl / (AmE), / sɔːb ˈæp.əl / (BrE)
A medium-sized European tree of the species Sorbus domestica, resembling the rowan and bearing edible fruit.
European tree with edible fruit

Examples

The sorb apple tree produces small, edible fruits that are harvested in autumn.
He planted a sorb apple in his garden, hoping to try the rare fruit.
Local farmers sometimes use sorb apples to make traditional jams and liqueurs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rowan - a small deciduous tree with orange-red berries

Etymology

From Latin 'sorbus' (sorb tree) and 'apple'.

2. NOUN

/ sɔrb ˈæpəl / (AmE), / sɔːb ˈæpəl / (BrE)
A small, acidic fruit with a gritty texture, produced by the sorb tree (Sorbus domestica).
acidic fruit of the sorb tree

Examples

The children picked a ripe sorb apple from the old tree in the orchard.
Because of its tart flavor, the sorb apple is sometimes used to make preserves and jams.
The gritty texture of a fresh sorb apple makes it quite distinct from other fruits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: service tree - a deciduous tree that produces the sorb apple

Etymology

‘Sorb’ from Latin *sorbum* (fruit of the service tree), *apple* from Old English *æppel*.
