thorium-228

1. NOUN

/ ˈθɔriəm tu ˈtwɛnti eɪt / (AmE), / ˈθɔːriəm tu ˈtwenti eɪt / (BrE)
a radioactive isotope of thorium with a mass number of 228, used in scientific research and nuclear studies
radioactive thorium isotope (mass 228)
medical

Examples

Thorium-228 is often used in experiments related to radiation detection.
Exposure to thorium-228 can pose a significant health risk due to its radioactivity.
Scientists measured the decay rate of thorium-228 in the laboratory.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: thorium - a chemical element, symbol Th, atomic number 90

Etymology

Element name 'thorium' from Norse god Thor; isotope notation denotes mass number 228.
