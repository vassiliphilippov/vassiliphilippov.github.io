structural member

1. NOUN

/ ˈstrʌktʃərəl ˈmɛmbər / (AmE), / ˈstrʌktʃərəl ˈmɛmbə / (BrE)
a component of a structure or building that provides support and helps bear loads
supporting part of a structure

Examples

The structural member was reinforced to ensure the building could withstand strong winds.
Steel beams are commonly used as structural members in modern construction.
If a structural member fails, it can compromise the stability of the entire structure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beam - a long, sturdy piece of wood or metal used to support weight
  • Synonym: girder - a large iron or steel beam or compound structure used for building bridges and the framework of large buildings
