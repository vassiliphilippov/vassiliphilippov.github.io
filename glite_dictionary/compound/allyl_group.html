allyl group

1. NOUN

/ ˈæl.i.əl ɡruːp / (AmE), / ˈæl.ɪl ɡruːp / (BrE)
an unsaturated organic radical with the formula C3H5, derived from propylene, commonly found as a substituent in organic compounds
univalent organic radical C3H5
medical

Examples

The allyl group is highly reactive and found in many organic compounds.
Adding an allyl group to the molecule changes its properties significantly.
The chemist discussed the importance of the allyl group in synthesizing new drugs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vinyl group - organic radical –CH=CH2 found in ethylene derivatives

Etymology

From Latin 'allium' (garlic), as the group was first isolated from garlic oil.
