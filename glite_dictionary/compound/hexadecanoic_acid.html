hexadecanoic acid

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɛksəˌdiːkəˈnoʊɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / ˌhɛksədɪˌkænəʊɪk ˈasɪd / (BrE)
A saturated fatty acid, commonly known as palmitic acid, found as a major component in meat and dairy fats.
a saturated fatty acid found in animal fats
medical

Examples

Hexadecanoic acid is one of the most common saturated fatty acids in the human diet.
The laboratory analysis detected high levels of hexadecanoic acid in the milk sample.
Researchers studied the effects of hexadecanoic acid on cellular metabolism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: palmitic acid - another name for hexadecanoic acid, found in palm oil and animal fats

Etymology

From hexadecane (C16 hydrocarbon) plus -oic (acid suffix), denoting a 16-carbon saturated fatty acid.
