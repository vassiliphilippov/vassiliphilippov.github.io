biohazard suit

1. NOUN

/ ˈbaɪ.oʊˌhæz.ɚd suːt / (AmE), / ˈbaɪ.əʊˌhæz.əd suːt / (BrE)
a one-piece protective garment designed to shield the wearer from hazardous biological or chemical agents
protective garment against biological/chemical hazards

Examples

The scientist wore a biohazard suit while working in the high-security laboratory.
Emergency responders donned biohazard suits before entering the contaminated area.
A biohazard suit is essential equipment when handling infectious samples.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hazmat suit - protective suit for hazardous materials

Etymology

From 'biohazard' (biological hazard) + 'suit', first appearing in the late 20th century with the development of advanced protective gear.
