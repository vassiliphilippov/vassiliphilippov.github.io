spark transmitter

1. NOUN

/ spɑrk trænzˈmɪtər / (AmE), / spɑːk trænzˈmɪtə / (BrE)
an early type of radio transmitter that generates electromagnetic waves using electric sparks across a gap as the power source
early radio transmitter using electric sparks

Examples

The invention of the spark transmitter marked a major advance in early wireless communication.
Before the advent of vacuum tubes, many ships relied on a spark transmitter for distress calls.
The museum's collection includes a fully restored spark transmitter from the early twentieth century.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: radio transmitter - device that sends out radio signals
