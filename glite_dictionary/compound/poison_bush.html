poison bush

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɔɪzən bʊʃ / (AmE), / ˈpɔɪzən bʊʃ / (BrE)
any of several Australian evergreen shrubs of the genus Gastrolobium, whose leaves are poisonous to livestock and which bear showy yellow to reddish-orange flowers and two-seeded pods
Australian shrub poisonous to livestock

Examples

Many farmers had to fence their cattle away from the poison bush to prevent fatal poisoning.
The bright flowers of the poison bush belie the danger hidden in its leaves.
Scientists study the toxins in poison bush to find safe ways to manage grazing land.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɔɪzən bʊʃ / (AmE), / ˈpɔɪzən bʊʃ / (BrE)
either of two Australian plants of the genus Swainsona that are poisonous to sheep
Australian poisonous plant

Examples

Farmers were warned to keep livestock away from the poison bush during the summer months.
The poison bush is notorious for causing illness in grazing sheep across Australia.
Botanists studied the poison bush to understand its toxic effects on animals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swainsona - a genus of Australian flowering plants
