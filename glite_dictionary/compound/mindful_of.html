mindful of

1. ADJ

/ ˈmaɪnd.fəl əv / (AmE), / ˈmaɪnd.fəl ɒv / (BrE)
conscious of and attentive to something, especially in a way that shows care or consideration.
aware and considerate of

Examples

When planning the menu, the chef was mindful of the guests' dietary restrictions.
You should always be mindful of your tone when giving feedback to colleagues.
The company is mindful of its impact on the environment and looks for sustainable solutions.

Synset Examples

She remained mineful of others' feelings during the discussion.
He was mineful of the team's workload while assigning tasks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aware of - having knowledge of something
  • Antonym: oblivious to - not aware or conscious of something

Etymology

From "mindful" + preposition "of"; "mindful" from mind + -ful, meaning 'full of mind/care'.
