genus Scaphiopus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs skəˈfaɪ.oʊ.pəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs skæˈfiː.ə.pəs / (BrE)
A genus of North American spadefoot toads distinguished by their vertical pupils and digging adaptations.
a genus of North American spadefoot toads

Examples

The genus Scaphiopus is known for its unique vertical pupils and spade-like feet.
Members of the genus Scaphiopus are adapted to burrowing in sandy soils.
Amphibian researchers often study the genus Scaphiopus due to its distinctive breeding behaviors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spadefoot toad - a type of toad with a spade-shaped digging structure on each hind foot

Etymology

From Greek skaphion (little boat, spade) and pous (foot); scientific Latin formation.
