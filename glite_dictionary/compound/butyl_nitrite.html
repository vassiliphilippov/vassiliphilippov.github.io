butyl nitrite

1. NOUN

/ ˈbjuːtəl ˈnaɪtraɪt / (AmE), / ˈbjuːtɪl ˈnaɪtraɪt / (BrE)
a colorless liquid chemical compound, sometimes inhaled as a stimulant or recreational drug
a stimulant chemical compound
medical

Examples

Some people inhale butyl nitrite to experience brief feelings of euphoria.
The possession and sale of butyl nitrite are regulated in several countries.
He explained the chemical similarities between butyl nitrite and other alkyl nitrites.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amyl nitrite - a chemically similar compound used medically and recreationally

Etymology

From 'butyl', relating to the butyl group in chemistry (from butane), and 'nitrite', from Latin 'nitrum' (potash) and chemical suffix '-ite'.
