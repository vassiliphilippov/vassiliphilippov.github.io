Hail Mary

1. NOUN

/ heɪl ˈmɛri / (AmE), / heɪl ˈmeəri / (BrE)
a traditional prayer or salutation to the Virgin Mary, used especially in Roman Catholic devotion
a prayer to the Virgin Mary

Examples

He knelt in the chapel to recite a Hail Mary before his exam.
The choir softly sang a Hail Mary at the end of the service.
She whispered a quick Hail Mary under her breath for strength.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ave Maria - musical or spoken setting of the Hail Mary prayer

Etymology

From the opening words of the prayer, 'Hail Mary', based on Latin 'Ave Maria'.

2. NOUN

/ heɪl ˈmɛri / (AmE), / heɪl ˈmeəri / (BrE)
a final, desperate attempt to achieve something, typically made when all else has failed
a last-ditch effort

Examples

With time running out, the quarterback threw a Hail Mary into the end zone.
It was a Hail Mary application, but she actually got the scholarship.
The negotiations were a Hail Mary to save the failing company.

Synset Examples

The team executed a Hail Mary to win the game.
She considered it a Hail Mary to apply for that job.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: last-ditch effort - a final and often desperate attempt to achieve something

Etymology

Originally referring to a long, hopeful forward pass in American football, named after the Catholic prayer 'Hail Mary', invoking divine help.
