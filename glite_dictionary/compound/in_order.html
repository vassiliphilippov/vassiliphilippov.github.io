in order

1. ADJ

/ ɪn ˈɔrdɚ / (AmE), / ɪn ˈɔːdə / (BrE)
arranged or prepared correctly and ready for use or action; in a proper state.
properly arranged or ready

Examples

Please make sure everything is in order before the meeting starts.
Her desk was always in order, with every item neatly arranged.
We checked the house and found all the rooms in order for the guests.

Synset Examples

everything is in order for their arrival

Related Terms

  • Synonym: in place - in the correct position or arrangement
  • Antonym: out of order - not in proper arrangement or not working correctly
