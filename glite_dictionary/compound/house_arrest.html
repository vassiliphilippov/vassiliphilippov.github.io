house arrest

1. NOUN

/ haʊs əˈrɛst / (AmE), / haʊs əˈrɛst / (BrE)
The state of being legally required to remain in one's own home instead of in prison as a form of punishment or while awaiting trial.
legal confinement to one's home
legal

Examples

After being convicted of fraud, she was sentenced to house arrest for six months.
During his house arrest, he was prohibited from leaving his property except for medical emergencies.
The judge granted house arrest instead of jail time for the non-violent offender.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imprisonment - the state of being confined in prison
