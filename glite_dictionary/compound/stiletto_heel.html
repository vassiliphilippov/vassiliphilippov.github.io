stiletto heel

1. NOUN

/ stəˈlɛtoʊ hil / (AmE), / stɪˈletəʊ hiːl / (BrE)
a very high, narrow heel on a woman's shoe, tapering to a small point at the ground
very high, narrow heel on a woman's shoe

Examples

She stepped out in red pumps with a stiletto heel that added several inches to her height.
Wearing a stiletto heel for long hours can be uncomfortable and challenging for many women.
The designer's new collection features shoes with a signature black stiletto heel.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: kitten heel - a short, slender heel on women's shoes
  • Antonym: block heel - a thick, chunky heel on women's shoes

Etymology

Stiletto from Italian 'stiletto' (dagger), referring to the heel's resemblance to a slender dagger.
