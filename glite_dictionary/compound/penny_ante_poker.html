penny ante poker

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛni ˈænti ˈpoʊkər / (AmE), / ˌpɛni ˈænti ˈpəʊkə / (BrE)
Poker played for very small stakes, often just for fun or minimal betting amounts.
poker with low-stakes betting

Examples

We gathered on Friday nights to play penny ante poker and catch up with friends.
He lost only a few dollars during the penny ante poker game, so it didn't really matter.
Their tradition of penny ante poker goes back to their college days.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: high-stakes poker - poker played for large amounts of money

Etymology

From 'penny ante,' meaning a game played for very low stakes, originally referring to the ante of a penny in poker games.
