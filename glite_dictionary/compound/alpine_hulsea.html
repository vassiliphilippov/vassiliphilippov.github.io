alpine hulsea

1. NOUN

/ ˈælpaɪn hʌlˈsiːə / (AmE), / ˈælpaɪn hʌlˈsiːə / (BrE)
a low, tufted plant native to the northwestern United States, featuring hairy stems topped by flower heads with short, narrow yellow rays
a yellow-flowered plant of the northwestern US

Examples

During our hike in the mountains, we came across a patch of alpine hulsea in full bloom.
The alpine hulsea thrives in the rocky, high-altitude regions of the northwestern United States.
Botanists are fascinated by the resilience of alpine hulsea to harsh alpine environments.

Etymology

From 'alpine' (relating to high mountains) and Latinized genus name Hulsea, after Thomas Hulme, an American botanist.
