life-of-man

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪf əv ˈmæn / (AmE), / ˈlaɪf əv ˈman / (BrE)
A perennial woody plant (Aralia racemosa), native to southeastern North America to Mexico, noted for its aromatic roots and panicled umbels.
a North American aromatic perennial plant

Examples

The roots of life-of-man have long been used in traditional remedies.
In the wild, life-of-man is easily recognized by its large, panicled umbels and aromatic smell.
Botanists found several clusters of life-of-man growing along the forest edge.

  • Degree Lower: wild sarsaparilla - a related perennial plant with aromatic root, less so than life-of-man
