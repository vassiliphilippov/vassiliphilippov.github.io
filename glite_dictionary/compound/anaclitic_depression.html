anaclitic depression

1. NOUN

/ ˌænəˈklɪtɪk dɪˈprɛʃən / (AmE), / ˌanəˈklɪtɪk dɪˈprɛʃən / (BrE)
A severe and progressive form of depression observed in infants who lose their mother or primary caregiver and do not receive an adequate substitute, often leading to developmental and emotional problems.
infant depression from loss of caregiver
medical

Examples

The orphanage study showed that many children developed anaclitic depression when separated from their mothers.
Psychologists observed symptoms of anaclitic depression in infants who lacked consistent emotional care.
Early intervention is crucial to prevent anaclitic depression in vulnerable children.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: depression - A mental health disorder characterized by persistent sadness and loss of interest.

Etymology

From Greek 'anaklinein' (to lean upon) + 'depression'.
