Italic language

1. NOUN

/ əˈtælɪk ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ɪˈtælɪk ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
A branch of the Indo-European language family that includes Latin and its related languages spoken in ancient Italy.
branch of Indo-European languages including Latin

Examples

Scholars often study Italic languages to understand the origins of Latin grammar and vocabulary.
Oscan and Umbrian are examples of ancient Italic languages spoken in Italy.
The evolution of the Romance languages can be traced back to their roots in the Italic language family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Latin - the ancient language of Rome, chief representative of the Italic languages

Etymology

From Latin 'Italicus' (Italian), referring to ancient peoples of Italy.
