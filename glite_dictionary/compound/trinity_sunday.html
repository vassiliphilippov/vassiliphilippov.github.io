Trinity Sunday

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrɪnəti ˈsʌn.deɪ / (AmE), / ˈtrɪnɪti ˈsʌn.deɪ / (BrE)
The first Sunday after Pentecost in the Christian calendar, dedicated to the doctrine of the Trinity.
first Sunday after Pentecost in Christianity

Examples

Many churches hold special services on Trinity Sunday to celebrate the doctrine of the Holy Trinity.
The choir prepared a beautiful anthem for Trinity Sunday this year.
On Trinity Sunday, the sermon focused on the unity of Father, Son, and Holy Spirit.

Etymology

From 'Trinity', referring to the Christian doctrine of God as three persons, and 'Sunday', the day of observance.
