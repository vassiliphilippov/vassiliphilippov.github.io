booster rocket

1. NOUN

/ ˈbuːstər ˌrɑːkɪt / (AmE), / ˈbuːstə ˌrɒkɪt / (BrE)
a rocket providing the initial thrust in a multistage launch vehicle, after which it is jettisoned when its fuel is spent
first stage of a multistage rocket

Examples

The space shuttle used a pair of solid-fuel booster rockets to gain enough speed for orbit.
After its fuel was spent, the booster rocket was jettisoned from the main spacecraft.
Every successful launch depends on reliable booster rockets to provide the initial thrust.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: booster - rocket engine used to increase thrust at launch
