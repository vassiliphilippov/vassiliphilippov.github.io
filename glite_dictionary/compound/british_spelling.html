British spelling

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrɪtɪʃ ˈspɛlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbrɪtɪʃ ˈspɛlɪŋ / (BrE)
The system of English spelling conventions used in the United Kingdom, Ireland, and most Commonwealth countries, differing from American spelling in certain word endings and patterns.
standard UK/Commonwealth spelling conventions
british

Examples

Some words are written differently in British spelling, such as 'colour' instead of 'color'.
The textbook provides both British spelling and American spelling for key vocabulary.
He was taught British spelling at school because he grew up in London.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: American spelling - spelling conventions used in American English
