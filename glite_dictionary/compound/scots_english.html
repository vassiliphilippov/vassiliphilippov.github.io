Scots English

1. NOUN

/ skɑts ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ / (AmE), / skɒts ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ / (BrE)
the variety of English spoken in Scotland, characterized by distinctive pronunciation, grammar, and vocabulary
dialect of English used in Scotland

Examples

She grew up speaking Scots English at home with her family in Edinburgh.
Many literary works from Scotland are written in Scots English to reflect authentic dialogue.
The unique expressions of Scots English can sometimes be difficult for outsiders to understand.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Standard English - the form of the English language widely accepted as the usual correct form
  • Antonym: Scottish Gaelic - Celtic language traditionally spoken in Scotland, distinct from Scots English
