cartridge paper

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑrtɹɪdʒ ˌpeɪpɚ / (AmE), / ˈkɑːtɹɪdʒ ˌpeɪpə / (BrE)
a thick, high-quality white paper used for pencil and ink drawings or printing
thick white paper for drawing

Examples

She bought a pad of cartridge paper for her art class.
Architects often prefer cartridge paper for their detailed ink sketches.
The drawing was done in pencil on thick cartridge paper.

Etymology

From its original use in making cartridges for firearms.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːr.trɪdʒ ˌpeɪ.pɚ / (AmE), / ˈkɑː.trɪdʒ ˌpeɪ.pə / (BrE)
a thick, high-quality paper originally made for making cartridge cases, now commonly used for drawing or printing
thick, high-quality drawing paper

Examples

She bought a pad of cartridge paper for her art class.
High-quality cartridge paper is ideal for sketching and watercolor painting.
Early printers often used cartridge paper for important documents due to its strength.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drawing paper - paper used for drawing
  • Degree Lower: newsprint - cheaper paper used for printing newspapers

Etymology

Originally used to make paper cartridges for firearms; later adapted for artistic and general use.
