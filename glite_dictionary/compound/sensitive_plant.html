sensitive plant

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛnsətɪv plænt / (AmE), / ˈsɛnsətɪv plɑːnt / (BrE)
a tropical plant (Mimosa pudica) whose compound leaves fold or droop when touched or exposed to light.
tropical plant with touch-sensitive leaves

Examples

Children are often fascinated when the sensitive plant folds its leaves at the lightest touch.
The sensitive plant is a popular curiosity in botanical gardens because of its rapid leaf movements.
Researchers study the sensitive plant to better understand plant responses to environmental stimuli.

Etymology

Named for its unusual sensitivity to touch and light.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɛnsətɪv plænt / (AmE), / ˈsɛnsətɪv plɑːnt / (BrE)
a tropical plant (Mimosa pudica) whose leaflets fold and droop when touched or at night
tropical plant whose leaves fold when touched

Examples

Children love to see the sensitive plant fold its leaves when they touch it.
The sensitive plant thrives in warm, humid environments and should be handled gently.
When the temperature drops, the leaves of the sensitive plant droop until morning.
