corn syrup

1. NOUN

/ kɔrn ˈsɪɹ.əp / (AmE), / kɔːn ˈsɪr.əp / (BrE)
A sweet, thick syrup made from cornstarch, used chiefly in foods as a sweetener and thickener.
sweet syrup from cornstarch

Examples

The recipe calls for a cup of corn syrup to add sweetness and thickness to the sauce.
Many processed foods contain corn syrup as a common ingredient.
To make homemade caramel, you often need corn syrup to prevent crystallization.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glucose syrup - a syrup made from the hydrolysis of starch, similar to corn syrup
