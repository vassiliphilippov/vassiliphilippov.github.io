nerve fibre

1. NOUN

/ nɝv ˈfaɪbɚ / (AmE), / nɜːv ˈfaɪbə / (BrE)
A long, slender extension of a nerve cell (neuron) that conducts electrical impulses away from the cell body.
extension of a nerve cell
medical

Examples

A single nerve fibre carries signals from your finger to your brain.
When the nerve fibre is damaged, muscle weakness may result.
Scientists used microscopes to observe the structure of each nerve fibre.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: axon - long projection of a nerve cell that transmits impulses
  • Antonym: dendrite - branch-like extension of a nerve cell that receives impulses
