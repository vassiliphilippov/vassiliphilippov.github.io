family Treponemataceae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli trɛˌpənoʊmiˈteɪsiːˌiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli trɛˌpɒnɪmiˈteɪsɪˌiː / (BrE)
A family of small, spiral-shaped bacteria (spirochetes), some of which are parasitic or pathogenic.
family of spirochete bacteria, some pathogenic
medical

Examples

Some bacteria in the family Treponemataceae are known to cause serious human diseases.
The family Treponemataceae includes the agent responsible for syphilis.
Researchers studied the role of the family Treponemataceae in animal infections.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Treponema - genus of spirochete bacteria within this family

Etymology

From New Latin: 'Treponema', the type genus, and '-aceae', a standard suffix for bacterial family names.
