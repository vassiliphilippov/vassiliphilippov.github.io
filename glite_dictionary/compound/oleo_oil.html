oleo oil

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊlioʊ ɔɪl / (AmE), / ˈəʊlɪəʊ ɔɪl / (BrE)
a yellowish oil extracted from beef fat, used in making margarine, soap, and for lubrication
oil from beef fat used in margarine and soap

Examples

The factory specializes in producing oleo oil for use in the food industry.
Margarine manufacturers often rely on oleo oil as a key ingredient.
The process of refining oleo oil makes it suitable for use in soaps and lubricants.

Etymology

From the Latin 'oleum' meaning 'oil', combined with 'oil'.
