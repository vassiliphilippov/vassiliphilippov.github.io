half-moon bastion

1. NOUN

/ ˈhæfˌmun ˈbæstiən / (AmE), / ˈhɑːfˌmuːn ˈbæstɪən / (BrE)
a type of curved bastion used in 16th-century fortifications, shaped like a half-moon and designed to provide defensive fire but later abandoned due to strategic disadvantages
curved bastion used in 16th-century fortifications

Examples

The castle's half-moon bastion offered a unique vantage point over the valley.
In the 16th century, engineers experimented with the half-moon bastion before better designs replaced it.
Restoration plans revealed the original foundations of a half-moon bastion beneath the modern walls.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bastion - a projecting part of a fortification
