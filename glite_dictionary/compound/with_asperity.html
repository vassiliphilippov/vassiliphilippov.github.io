with asperity

1. ADV

/ wɪð əˈspɛrəti / (AmE), / wɪð əˈspɛrɪti / (BrE)
in a harsh or severe manner; with sharpness or harshness of tone or manner
in a harsh or severe manner

Examples

She criticized his report with asperity during the meeting, leaving him embarrassed.
The manager replied with asperity to the repeated complaints from staff.
He addressed the group with asperity, making his dissatisfaction very clear.

Synset Examples

The teacher spoke with asperity when addressing the class.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: harshly - in a harsh manner
  • Antonym: gently - in a gentle manner

Etymology

From the noun 'asperity', which originates from Latin asperitas (roughness, harshness), from asper (rough).
