poverty line

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːvɚti laɪn / (AmE), / ˈpɒvəti laɪn / (BrE)
the minimum level of income considered adequate in a particular country, below which a person or family is regarded as being in poverty
minimum income threshold for poverty

Examples

Many families in the region live below the poverty line and struggle to meet basic needs.
Government programs aim to help those whose income falls under the poverty line.
The poverty line varies from country to country, depending on local economic conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poverty threshold - another term for poverty line, the minimum income level
  • Antonym: wealth - abundance of valuable possessions or money; opposite end of poverty spectrum
