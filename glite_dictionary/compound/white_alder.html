white alder

1. NOUN

/ waɪt ˈældɚ / (AmE), / waɪt ˈældə / (BrE)
A shrub or small tree native to the eastern and southern coastal United States, noted for its racemes of fragrant white flowers.
a North American shrub or small tree with white flowers

Examples

The white alder blooms in late spring, filling the air with a sweet, spicy fragrance.
Gardeners appreciate the white alder for its attractive racemes of flowers and rapid growth.
A grove of white alders lines the banks of the creek, providing shade and beauty.

2. NOUN

/ waɪt ˈældɚ / (AmE), / waɪt ˈældə / (BrE)
a deciduous tree (Alnus rhombifolia) native to the western United States, with smooth gray bark and serrated leaves, often found along streams
tree native to western U.S.

Examples

The white alder is commonly found along swift-moving streams in California.
Gardeners appreciate the white alder for its ability to stabilize riverbanks and provide shade.
In autumn, the leaves of the white alder turn a golden yellow, brightening up the landscape.

Related Terms
