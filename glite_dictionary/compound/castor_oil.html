castor oil

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæstər ɔɪl / (AmE), / ˈkɑːstə ɔɪl / (BrE)
a pale yellow vegetable oil obtained from the seeds of the castor plant, used as a laxative and in manufacturing paints and varnishes
vegetable oil used as laxative and in industry

Examples

As a child, he dreaded taking castor oil whenever he was ill.
Castor oil is sometimes used as a natural remedy for constipation.
The painter mixed castor oil with pigments to create a glossy finish.

Etymology

From castor (the plant, Ricinus communis) + oil.
