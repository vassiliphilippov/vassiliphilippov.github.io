hole-and-corner

1. ADJ

/ ˌhoʊl ən ˈkɔːrnər / (AmE), / ˌhəʊl ən ˈkɔːnə / (BrE)
Involving or relating to obscure, insignificant, or unimportant matters or activities, often kept secret or hidden from the wider world.
relating to obscure or insignificant matters

Examples

They led a hole-and-corner existence, rarely participating in the life of the wider community.
The scandal was largely the result of hole-and-corner arrangements and secret meetings.
His hole-and-corner activities went unnoticed for years in the quiet town.

Synset Examples

a hole-and-corner life in some obscure community

Related Terms

  • Synonym: secret - not known or seen or not meant to be known or seen by others
  • Synonym: clandestine - kept secret or done secretively, especially because illicit

Etymology

Formed by compounding 'hole' and 'corner', referring figuratively to hidden or obscure places.
