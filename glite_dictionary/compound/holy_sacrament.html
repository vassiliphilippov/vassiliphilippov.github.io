Holy Sacrament

1. NOUN

/ ˈhoʊli ˈsækrəmənt / (AmE), / ˈhəʊli ˈsækrəmənt / (BrE)
the Christian rite commemorating the Last Supper, involving the consecration of bread and wine
Christian rite with bread and wine

Examples

The priest prepared the altar for the Holy Sacrament before the service began.
Many Christians receive the Holy Sacrament each Sunday as a sign of their faith.
She taught her children about the meaning of the Holy Sacrament during catechism classes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Eucharist - the Christian ceremony commemorating the Last Supper
  • Synonym: Communion - the sharing or receiving of the Holy Sacrament

Etymology

From Latin 'sacramentum' (a solemn oath or mystery), later used for Christian rites.
