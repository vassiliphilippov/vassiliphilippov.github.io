barium dioxide

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛriəm daɪˈɑksaɪd / (AmE), / ˈbæriəm daɪˈɒksaɪd / (BrE)
a white, toxic powder used as an oxidizing agent, obtained by heating barium oxide in air
white toxic chemical compound (BaO₂)

Examples

Industrially, barium dioxide is used to prepare hydrogen peroxide.
Always handle barium dioxide with care, as it is highly toxic.
The laboratory stored barium dioxide in tightly sealed containers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barium oxide - a white solid oxide of barium (BaO)

Etymology

From 'barium', a chemical element (from New Latin *barium*), and 'dioxide', meaning a compound with two oxygen atoms (from Greek *di-* 'two' and *oxide*).
