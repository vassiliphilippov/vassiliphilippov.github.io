possum oak

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːsəm oʊk / (AmE), / ˈpɒsəm əʊk / (BrE)
A relatively tall deciduous oak tree of the southeastern United States, often grown as a shade tree, especially in wet soil.
deciduous oak tree of southeastern US

Examples

The possum oak provides ample shade in the park during the hot summer months.
Wetland restoration projects in the South often include planting possum oaks to stabilize the soil.
Many homeowners in Georgia prefer the possum oak for its tolerance of damp ground and attractive foliage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: water oak - A deciduous oak tree native to the southeastern US, found in wet soils
