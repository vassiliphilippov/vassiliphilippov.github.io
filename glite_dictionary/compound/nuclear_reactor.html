nuclear reactor

1. NOUN

/ ˈnuː.kli.ɚ riˈæk.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈnjuː.klɪ.ə rɪˈæk.tə / (BrE)
A device in which a nuclear chain reaction is initiated, controlled, and sustained at a steady rate to produce energy or artificial elements.
apparatus to control nuclear reactions

Examples

The power generated by a nuclear reactor can supply electricity to an entire city.
Safety measures are crucial when operating a nuclear reactor to prevent accidents.
Scientists developed a new type of nuclear reactor designed to minimize radioactive waste.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reactor - device for maintaining and controlling a nuclear reaction
