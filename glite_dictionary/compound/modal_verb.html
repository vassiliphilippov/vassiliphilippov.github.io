modal verb

1. NOUN

/ ˈmoʊdəl vɝːb / (AmE), / ˈməʊdəl vɜːb / (BrE)
an auxiliary verb, such as 'can', 'could', 'may', 'might', 'will', or 'must', used to express possibility, necessity, permission, or ability
auxiliary verb expressing modality

Examples

You should use a modal verb like 'can' or 'must' to express necessity.
In English, the modal verb 'may' is often used to indicate permission.
She taught her students how to use each modal verb correctly in different contexts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: auxiliary verb - a verb used in forming the tenses, moods, and voices of other verbs

Etymology

From 'modal' (from Latin 'modus', manner) and 'verb' (from Latin 'verbum', word).
