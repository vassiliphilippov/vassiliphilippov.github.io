Middle Irish

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪdəl ˈaɪrɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈmɪdəl ˈaɪərɪʃ / (BrE)
the form of the Irish Gaelic language spoken and written from about 1100 to 1500
historical stage of Irish (1100–1500)

Examples

Many ancient texts were translated from Latin into Middle Irish.
Linguists study Middle Irish to better understand the history of Celtic languages.
Some epic poems survive only in manuscripts written in Middle Irish.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Old Irish - the Irish language as used from the 6th to the 10th century
  • Degree Higher: Modern Irish - the Irish language from about 1600 to the present

Etymology

Formed from 'middle' (between earlier and later) + 'Irish'.
