cacodyl radical

1. NOUN

/ ˈkækəˌdɪl ˈrædɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈkakədɪl ˈrædɪkəl / (BrE)
a univalent chemical group derived from arsine, with the formula (CH3)2As–
univalent group from arsine in chemistry
medical

Examples

The cacodyl radical plays a significant role in organoarsenic chemistry.
Scientists isolated the cacodyl radical during experiments with arsenic compounds.
The toxic properties of the cacodyl radical were studied extensively in the nineteenth century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cacodyl - a foul-smelling organic compound containing arsenic

Etymology

From cacodyl (from Greek κακώδης, kakōdēs, 'foul-smelling') and 'radical' (Latin radix 'root').
