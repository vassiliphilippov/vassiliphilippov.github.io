pineapple plant

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪnˌæpəl plænt / (AmE), / ˈpaɪnˌæpəl plɑːnt / (BrE)
a tropical American plant (Ananas comosus) that bears a large fleshy edible fruit with a tuft of stiff leaves at the top; widely cultivated in tropical regions
tropical plant that produces pineapples

Examples

The pineapple plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils in tropical climates.
It takes about two years for a pineapple plant to produce its first fruit.
Gardeners often grow the pineapple plant indoors as an ornamental houseplant.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: pineapple - the large fleshy edible fruit produced by the pineapple plant
