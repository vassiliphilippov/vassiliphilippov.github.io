autonomic nervous system

1. NOUN

/ ˌɔtəˈnɑmɪk ˈnɜrvəs ˈsɪstəm / (AmE), / ˌɔːtəˈnɒmɪk ˈnɜːvəs ˈsɪstəm / (BrE)
the part of the nervous system that controls involuntary functions such as heart rate, digestion, and respiration
part of nervous system controlling involuntary functions
medical

Examples

The autonomic nervous system regulates the heartbeat and controls digestion without conscious effort.
Stress can trigger a response in the autonomic nervous system, leading to increased blood pressure.
Doctors study the autonomic nervous system to understand disorders that affect basic bodily functions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sympathetic nervous system - part of the autonomic nervous system that prepares the body for activity
  • Synonym: parasympathetic nervous system - part of the autonomic nervous system that conserves energy and calms the body

Etymology

From Greek 'auto-' (self) + 'nomos' (law), relating to self-regulation.
