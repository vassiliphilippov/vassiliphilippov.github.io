family Raphidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ræˈfaɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæməli ræˈfɪdiː / (BrE)
A biological family of extinct birds, including dodos and solitaires.
family of extinct birds (dodos, solitaires)

Examples

The family Raphidae is best known for containing the famous dodo.
Extensive fossil records provide insight into the extinction of birds in the family Raphidae.
Members of the family Raphidae were native to islands in the Indian Ocean.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dodo - an extinct flightless bird of the family Raphidae
  • Synonym: solitaire - any of several extinct flightless birds of the family Raphidae

Etymology

From scientific Latin 'Raphidae', based on the type genus 'Raphus'.
