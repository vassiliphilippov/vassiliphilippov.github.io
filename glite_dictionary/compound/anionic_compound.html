anionic compound

1. NOUN

/ ˌænaɪˈɑnɪk kəmˈpaʊnd / (AmE), / ˌanɪˈɒnɪk kəmˈpaʊnd / (BrE)
a chemical compound in which the primary active component is an anion, or negatively charged ion.
compound with an active anion
medical

Examples

Sodium dodecyl sulfate is a common anionic compound used in detergents and cleaning products.
Researchers analyzed the properties of the anionic compound under different temperatures.
The presence of an anionic compound can increase the solubility of some metal ions in water.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cationic compound - compound characterized by an active cation

Etymology

From 'anion' (Greek anion, from anienai 'to go up') and 'compound.'
