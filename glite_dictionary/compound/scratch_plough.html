scratch plough

1. NOUN

/ skrætʃ plaʊ / (AmE), / skrætʃ plaʊ / (BrE)
a simple, light plough without a mouldboard, used to break up the surface of the soil without turning it over
simple light plough without a mouldboard

Examples

Farmers in ancient times often used a scratch plough to prepare their fields for sowing.
The scratch plough is well-suited to light soils and minimal farming.
Unlike modern ploughs, a scratch plough does not turn the soil but simply loosens its surface.

Synset Examples

Rather than cutting and turning the soil to produce ridged furrows, the ard breaks up a narrow strip of soil and cuts a shallow furrow (or drill), leaving intervening strips undisturbed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ard - an ancient simple plough resembling a scratch plough
