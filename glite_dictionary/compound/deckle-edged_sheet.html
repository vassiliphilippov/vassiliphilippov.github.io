deckle-edged sheet

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛkəl ˌɛdʒd ʃit / (AmE), / ˈdɛkəl ˌɛdʒd ʃiːt / (BrE)
A sheet of paper with rough, uneven edges, typically resulting from being made by hand or using a deckle.
paper with rough, uneven edges

Examples

She prefers to write her letters on deckle-edged sheets because of their unique texture.
The invitation was printed on a deckle-edged sheet to give it a handcrafted feel.
Art collectors value prints made on deckle-edged sheets for their authenticity and charm.

Synset Examples

The artist used deckle-edged sheets for her watercolor paintings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deckle edge - rough, irregular edge on hand-made paper

Etymology

From 'deckle' (a frame used in papermaking, from German 'Deckel' meaning lid) + 'edge'.
