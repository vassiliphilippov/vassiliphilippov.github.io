Yekke family

1. NOUN

/ ˈjɛk.i ˈfæm.ə.li / (AmE), / ˈjɛki ˈfæm.ɪ.li / (BrE)
A family of German-speaking Jewish descent, especially one originating from the Yekke community.
German-speaking Jewish family; Yekke community

Examples

The annual reunion brought together dozens of Yekke families from across the country.
Many members of the Yekke family maintained German traditions even after emigrating.
A local museum features exhibits on Yekke families and their contributions to the community.

Synset Examples

Many Yekke families gather for cultural festivals annually.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Yekke - A German-speaking Jew, especially of the diaspora

Etymology

From 'Yekke', a term referring to German-speaking Jews, likely from Yiddish, possibly relating to the German word 'Jacke' (jacket), referencing their formal dress style.
