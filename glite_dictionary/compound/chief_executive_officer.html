chief executive officer

1. NOUN

/ tʃiːf ɪɡˈzɛkjətɪv ˈɑːfɪsər / (AmE), / tʃiːf ɪɡˈzɛkjʊtɪv ˈɒfɪsə / (BrE)
the highest-ranking executive in a company, responsible for managing the overall operations and making major corporate decisions
most senior company executive

Examples

The chief executive officer addressed the company's employees at the annual meeting.
After the merger, she was appointed chief executive officer of the newly formed organization.
Many investors judge a company's prospects based on the reputation of its chief executive officer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: CEO - abbreviation for 'chief executive officer'
  • Synonym: president - leader of a corporation, sometimes interchangeable with chief executive officer
  • Synonym: managing director - equivalent position to chief executive officer in some countries

Etymology

Formed from 'chief' (leader) + 'executive officer' (manager responsible for operations).
