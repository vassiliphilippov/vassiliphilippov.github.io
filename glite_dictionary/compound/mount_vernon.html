Mount Vernon

1. PROPN

/ maʊnt ˈvɝːnən / (AmE), / maʊnt ˈvɜːnən / (BrE)
the former plantation home of George Washington in northeastern Virginia, overlooking the Potomac River, now preserved as a historic site
George Washington's historic estate in Virginia

Examples

Many tourists visit Mount Vernon each year to learn about George Washington's life.
After touring Mount Vernon, we enjoyed a picnic overlooking the Potomac River.
Mount Vernon has been designated a National Historic Landmark due to its historical significance.
