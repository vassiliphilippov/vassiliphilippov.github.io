benzene nucleus

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛnˌziːn ˈnuːkliəs / (AmE), / ˈbɛnziːn ˈnjuːkliəs / (BrE)
the structural core of benzene, consisting of a closed ring of six carbon atoms, each bonded to a hydrogen atom
core structure of benzene (six-carbon ring)

Examples

The benzene nucleus forms the basis for many aromatic compounds in organic chemistry.
Substituents can attach to the benzene nucleus at different positions, creating various derivatives.
Understanding the structure of the benzene nucleus is essential for studying aromatic reactions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aromatic ring - a planar ring structure with conjugated bonds typical in aromatic compounds

Etymology

From 'benzene', a chemical compound, and 'nucleus', Latin for 'kernel' or 'core'.
