pigeon toes

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪdʒən toʊz / (AmE), / ˈpɪdʒɪn təʊz / (BrE)
A condition in which the toes point inward, often resulting in a walking gait with the feet turned toward each other.
condition where toes point inward

Examples

The doctor noticed that her pigeon toes were causing her to trip frequently.
Children with pigeon toes often grow out of the condition as they age.
Special shoes were prescribed to help correct his pigeon toes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: in-toeing - the medical term for toes pointing inward
