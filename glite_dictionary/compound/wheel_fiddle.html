wheel fiddle

1. NOUN

/ wil ˈfɪdəl / (AmE), / wiːl ˈfɪdl̩ / (BrE)
a stringed musical instrument that produces sound by means of a rotating wheel rubbing against the strings, also known as a hurdy-gurdy
stringed instrument played with a wheel

Examples

The musician played a wheel fiddle at the medieval festival, enchanting the crowd with its unique sound.
She bought a rare wheel fiddle from an antique shop while traveling in Europe.
Learning to play the wheel fiddle can be challenging because of its crank mechanism and keyboard.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hurdy-gurdy - a musical instrument that produces sound by a crank-turned, rosined wheel rubbing against the strings
