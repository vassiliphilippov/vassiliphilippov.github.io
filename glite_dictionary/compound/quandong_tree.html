quandong tree

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwænˌdɔŋ tri / (AmE), / ˈkwɒn.dɒŋ triː / (BrE)
An Australian tree with hard white timber, glossy green leaves, white flowers, and one-seeded glossy blue fruit.
Australian tree with glossy blue fruit

Examples

The quandong tree is valued for both its timber and its edible fruit.
Farmers planted several quandong trees at the edge of their property.
The glossy blue fruit of the quandong tree attracts many birds during summer.

Etymology

From Wiradjuri (an Aboriginal Australian language) *quandong* (the tree or its fruit).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkwɑːn.dɑːŋ triː / (AmE), / ˈkwɒn.dɒŋ triː / (BrE)
An Australian tree (Santalum acuminatum) producing edible fruit with a fleshy outer layer and a nutlike seed.
Australian tree with edible fruit and seed

Examples

The quandong tree is highly valued for its nutritious, tangy fruit by Indigenous Australians.
Farmers recently started cultivating quandong trees for use in gourmet jams and desserts.
After a long walk, we rested in the shade of a large quandong tree.

Etymology

From Wiradjuri (Australian Aboriginal language) 'quandong', referring to the fruit.
