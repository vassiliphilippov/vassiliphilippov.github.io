fair use

1. NOUN

/ fɛr jus / (AmE), / feə juːs / (BrE)
The legal right to use portions of copyrighted material without permission or payment, under certain circumstances such as criticism, commentary, news reporting, teaching, scholarship, or research.
permitted use of copyrighted material without payment
legal american

Examples

The documentary included short clips under fair use to analyze the original film.
Teachers often rely on fair use to share excerpts from books and articles in class.
He argued that sharing the image online fell within the scope of fair use.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: copyright infringement - the unauthorized use of copyrighted material in violation of copyright owner's rights
