monster-in-law

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑnstər ɪn lɔ / (AmE), / ˈmɒnstə ɪn lɔː / (BrE)
a mother-in-law who is extremely difficult to deal with, often in a humorous or exaggerating way
difficult or unpleasant mother-in-law (humorous)
informal

Examples

My monster-in-law insists on criticizing everything I do in the kitchen.
He survived the holidays with his monster-in-law by keeping a good sense of humor.
Whenever her monster-in-law visits, she finds herself stressed and exhausted.

Synset Examples

Her monster-in-law made the wedding planning a nightmare.
He joked about his monster-in-law before the family gathering.
Dealing with a monster-in-law requires patience and humor.

Etymology

Formed by blending 'monster' and 'mother-in-law,' popularized by the 2005 film 'Monster-in-Law.'
