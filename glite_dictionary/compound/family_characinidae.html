family Characinidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ˌkærəˈsɪnɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfamɪli ˌkarəˈsɪnɪdiː / (BrE)
a former scientific name for the family of freshwater fishes now known as Characidae, which includes tetras and related species
former name for Characidae (fish family)

Examples

The species formerly classified under family Characinidae are now included in the Characidae.
Ichthyologists often refer to the family Characinidae in older scientific literature.
Many aquarium enthusiasts are familiar with the tetra fishes once known as part of the family Characinidae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Characidae - the current scientific family name for tetras and related fishes

Etymology

From modern Latin Characinidae, based on Characinus (a genus of fish) + -idae (family suffix).
