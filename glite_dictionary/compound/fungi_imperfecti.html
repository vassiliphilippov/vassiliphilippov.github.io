Fungi imperfecti

1. NOUN

/ ˈfʌndʒaɪ ɪmˌpɝːˈfɛktaɪ / (AmE), / ˈfʌndʒi ɪmˌpɜːˈfɛkti / (BrE)
a group of fungi comprising species for which no sexually reproductive stage has been observed, classified together due to this trait
fungal group lacking known sexual stage

Examples

Many plant diseases are caused by Fungi imperfecti, which lack a known sexual stage.
The classification of Fungi imperfecti has changed as molecular techniques have revealed their relationships.
Researchers study Fungi imperfecti to better understand asexual reproduction in fungi.

Etymology

From Latin 'fungi' (fungus) and 'imperfecti' (imperfect), denoting lacking a sexual stage.
