tree swallow

1. NOUN

/ tri ˈswɑloʊ / (AmE), / tri ˈswɒləʊ / (BrE)
A small North American swallow (Tachycineta bicolor) with bluish-green upperparts and white underparts, often nesting in tree cavities.
North American swallow species

Examples

A pair of tree swallows built their nest in the birdhouse beside the pond.
The iridescent feathers of the tree swallow glistened in the morning sun.
In spring, tree swallows can often be seen darting over open fields in search of insects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swallow - small migratory bird with slender body and long pointed wings

2. NOUN

/ ˈtri ˌswɑːloʊ / (AmE), / ˈtriː ˌswɒləʊ / (BrE)
A small migratory songbird, often nesting in tree cavities, found in North America and other regions.
species of small migratory songbird

Examples

A flock of tree swallows darted above the lake at sunset.
The tree swallow builds its nest in abandoned woodpecker holes.
We watched a tree swallow catch insects over the meadow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swallow - a small migratory songbird with pointed wings and a forked tail
