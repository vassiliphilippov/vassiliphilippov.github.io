western kingbird

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɛstərn ˈkɪŋˌbɜrd / (AmE), / ˈwɛstən ˈkɪŋˌbɜːd / (BrE)
a large flycatcher bird found in western North America, with pale grey head and back, yellowish underparts, and a black tail
North American flycatcher with yellowish breast

Examples

We spotted a western kingbird perched on the ranch fence, hunting for insects.
The western kingbird is known for aggressively defending its territory.
During migration, flocks of western kingbirds can sometimes be seen across open fields.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kingbird - a group of large, aggressive North American flycatchers
