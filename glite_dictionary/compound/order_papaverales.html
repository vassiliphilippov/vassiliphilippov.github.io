order Papaverales

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdər pəˌpævəˈreɪliːz / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə pəˌpævəˈreɪliːz / (BrE)
a former taxonomic order of dicotyledonous flowering plants, including poppies and related families such as Papaveraceae and Cruciferae.
a former order of flowering plants (poppies, etc.)
medical

Examples

The order Papaverales includes well-known plants like poppies and mustards.
Botanists no longer widely use the order Papaverales in modern plant classifications.
Research on the order Papaverales helps scientists understand the evolution of flowering plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Papaveraceae - the poppy family, a principal family of Papaverales
  • Synonym: order Rhoeadales - alternative classification closely related to Papaverales

Etymology

From Latin 'Papaver' (poppy) and taxonomic suffix '-ales' (order).
