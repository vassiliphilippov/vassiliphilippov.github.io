Australian nettle

1. NOUN

/ əˈstreɪliən ˈnɛtəl / (AmE), / ɒˈstreɪliən ˈnɛtəl / (BrE)
Any of several tall Australian trees of the genus Laportea, known for their stinging hairs.
Australian trees of genus Laportea

Examples

The leaves of the Australian nettle can cause severe irritation if touched.
Scientists studied the distribution of Australian nettle in the rainforest.
Hikers are advised to avoid brushing against the Australian nettle while exploring nature trails.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nettle - a herbaceous plant with stinging hairs
