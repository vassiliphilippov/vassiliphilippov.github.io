apical placentation

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪpɪkəl ˌplæsənˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈeɪpɪkəl ˌplæsənˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
a type of placentation in plants where one or a few ovules develop at the top of a simple or compound ovary
ovule arrangement at top of ovary in plants
medical

Examples

In primrose flowers, apical placentation can be observed under careful microscopic examination.
Botanists distinguish apical placentation from other types based on the ovule position in the ovary.
Understanding apical placentation helps in identifying certain plant families and species.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: basal placentation - ovules attached at the base of the ovary
  • Antonym: axile placentation - ovules attached to a central column in the ovary

Etymology

From Latin apex (tip, summit) and placenta (cake, used for botanical term 'placentation').
