cobalt bloom

1. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊˌbɔlt bluːm / (AmE), / ˈkəʊ.bɒlt bluːm / (BrE)
a reddish mineral, hydrated cobalt arsenate, found in monoclinic crystals and used to color glass, usually found in cobalt- and arsenic-bearing veins
hydrated cobalt arsenate mineral

Examples

The striking color of cobalt bloom makes it a popular specimen for mineral collectors.
Glassmakers sought cobalt bloom as a pigment for producing rich blue glass.
Geologists discovered veins rich in cobalt bloom while surveying the old mine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: erythrite - a mineral consisting of cobalt(II) arsenate, also called cobalt bloom
