sphecoid wasp

1. NOUN

/ ˈsfiːkɔɪd wɑsp / (AmE), / ˈsfiːkɔɪd wɒsp / (BrE)
any of various solitary wasps of the superfamily Sphecoidea, typically non-social and known for their burrowing or hunting behavior
a solitary wasp of the Sphecoidea superfamily

Examples

A sphecoid wasp can often be seen digging a burrow in sandy soil to lay its eggs.
Many gardeners appreciate the presence of the sphecoid wasp for controlling caterpillar populations.
I found a sphecoid wasp dragging its prey back to its underground nest.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: wasp - an insect of the order Hymenoptera, with a slender body and narrow waist

Etymology

From Greek *sphēk-*, meaning 'wasp', with '-oid' meaning 'resembling'.
