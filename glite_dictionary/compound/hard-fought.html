hard-fought

1. ADJ

/ hɑrd ˈfɔt / (AmE), / hɑːd ˈfɔːt / (BrE)
requiring a lot of effort, determination, or struggle to achieve, usually in the face of opposition
achieved with great effort or struggle

Examples

After a hard-fought battle, the team finally emerged victorious.
The politician won the hard-fought election by a narrow margin.
Negotiators reached a hard-fought agreement after several days of intense discussions.

Synset Examples

a hard-fought primary

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hard-won - achieved only with great difficulty or after a struggle
  • Antonym: easy - not needing much effort
