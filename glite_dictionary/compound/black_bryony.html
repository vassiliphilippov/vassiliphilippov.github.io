black bryony

1. NOUN

/ blæk ˈbraɪ.əni / (AmE), / blak ˈbraɪ.əni / (BrE)
a common European climbing plant with tuberous roots, heart-shaped leaves, and clusters of bright red berries, belonging to the genus Tamus.
a European twining vine with red berries

Examples

The hedgerow was covered in black bryony with its vivid red berries glistening in the sun.
Gardeners should be cautious, as black bryony berries are toxic if ingested.
During the hike, we spotted black bryony winding around an old wooden fence.
