teres major

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɛr.iːz ˈmeɪ.dʒɚ / (AmE), / ˌtɛəriːz ˈmeɪ.dʒə / (BrE)
A thick, rounded muscle of the upper back and shoulder that helps move and rotate the arm inward.
muscle of the upper back/shoulder
medical

Examples

The teres major plays an important role in stabilizing the shoulder during arm movements.
Injury to the teres major can result in difficulty rotating the arm inward.
The physical therapist recommended stretches to strengthen the teres major after the shoulder injury.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: teres minor - a smaller teres muscle near the teres major

Etymology

From Latin 'teres' (rounded, smooth) and 'major' (greater).
