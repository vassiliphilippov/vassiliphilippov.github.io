single-leaf pinyon

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪŋɡəl liːf ˈpɪniˌɑn / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋɡəl liːf ˈpɪnjɒn / (BrE)
A pinyon pine (Pinus monophylla) native to the southwestern United States, characterized by solitary needles and often several stems, valued for its edible nuts.
pinyon tree with single needles, edible nuts

Examples

The single-leaf pinyon is well adapted to the arid climate of Nevada and eastern California.
Locals gather pine nuts from the single-leaf pinyon every autumn for traditional dishes.
Unlike other pines, the single-leaf pinyon has only one needle per fascicle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pinyon pine - any of several North American pines that bear edible seeds
  • Synonym: nut pine - a pine tree that produces edible nuts
