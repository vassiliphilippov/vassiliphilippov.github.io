Helen of Troy

1. PROPN

/ ˈhɛlən əv ˈtrɔɪ / (AmE), / ˈhɛlən əv ˈtrɔɪ / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, the beautiful daughter of Zeus and Leda whose abduction by Paris sparked the Trojan War.
mythical Greek woman, cause of Trojan War

Examples

The legend of Helen of Troy has inspired countless works of literature and art.
Many poets describe Helen of Troy as the face that launched a thousand ships.
The story of Helen of Troy is central to the epic poem the Iliad.

Etymology

From ancient Greek Ἑλένη (Helenē), legendary for her beauty.
