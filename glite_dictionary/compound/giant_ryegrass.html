giant ryegrass

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒaɪənt ˈraɪˌɡræs / (AmE), / ˈdʒaɪənt ˈraɪɡrɑːs / (BrE)
A stout, perennial grass native to western North America, known for its tall growth and robust appearance.
tall grass of western North America

Examples

Farmers often plant giant ryegrass to improve soil stability in erosion-prone areas.
The tall stalks of giant ryegrass can provide shelter for wildlife in open fields.
Researchers are studying giant ryegrass as a potential bioenergy crop due to its rapid growth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ryegrass - a grass of the genus Lolium, commonly planted for pasture and lawns
