temporary expedient

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛmpəˌrɛri ɪkˈspiːdiənt / (AmE), / ˈtɛmpərəri ɪkˈspiːdiənt / (BrE)
a quick or improvised solution used to address a problem for a short period, often without long-term effectiveness or planning
quick, improvised short-term solution

Examples

The government introduced a temporary expedient to address the immediate housing crisis.
Using a tarp was only a temporary expedient until the roof could be repaired properly.
Many businesses turned to remote work as a temporary expedient during the pandemic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stopgap - a temporary way of dealing with a problem or satisfying a need
  • Antonym: permanent solution - a long-term or enduring answer to a problem
