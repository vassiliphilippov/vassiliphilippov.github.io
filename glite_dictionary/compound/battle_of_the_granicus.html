Battle of the Granicus

1. PROPN

/ ˈbæt̬əl əv ðə ˈɡrænɪkəs / (AmE), / ˈbætəl əv ðə ˈɡranɪkəs / (BrE)
the first major battle won by Alexander the Great against the Persian Empire in 334 BC, near the Granicus River in Asia Minor
first major Alexander-Persian battle, 334 BC

Examples

The Battle of the Granicus marked the beginning of Alexander's campaign to conquer the Persian Empire.
Many historians consider the Battle of the Granicus a turning point in ancient military history.
At the Battle of the Granicus, Alexander demonstrated his strategic brilliance and bold leadership.

Etymology

Granicus from the name of a river in northwestern Asia Minor; 'Battle of' as per naming of historical military engagements.
