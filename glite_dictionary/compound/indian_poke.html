Indian poke

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪndiən poʊk / (AmE), / ˈɪndiən pəʊk / (BrE)
A North American plant (usually Veratrum viride) with large leaves and clusters of yellowish-green flowers, known for yielding a toxic alkaloid used in traditional medicine.
North American plant with toxic medicinal alkaloid

Examples

The roots of Indian poke were once used in traditional remedies by indigenous peoples.
Despite its poisonous properties, Indian poke has a long history in herbal medicine.
You can recognize Indian poke by its thick stem and clusters of greenish-yellow flowers in the summer.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɪndiən poʊk / (AmE), / ˈɪndiən pəʊk / (BrE)
A perennial plant native to southeastern Asia and China, belonging to the pokeweed family.
pokeweed of Asia and China

Examples

The Indian poke thrives in moist, shady woodlands.
Researchers are studying the medicinal properties of Indian poke roots.
Gardeners should be cautious, as Indian poke berries can be toxic if ingested.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pokeweed - a group of perennial plants with racemose flowers and poisonous berries
