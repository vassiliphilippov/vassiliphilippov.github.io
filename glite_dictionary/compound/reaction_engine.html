reaction engine

1. NOUN

/ riˈækʃən ˌɛn.dʒɪn / (AmE), / riˈækʃən ˌɛn.dʒɪn / (BrE)
an engine, such as a jet or rocket engine, that propels a vehicle by expelling a high-speed stream of gas or fluid in the opposite direction
engine using jet or rocket propulsion

Examples

The spacecraft was powered by a reaction engine capable of generating immense thrust.
Modern jet aircraft use a type of reaction engine to achieve efficient, high-speed flight.
During the demonstration, the new reaction engine produced a visible stream of exhaust.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jet engine - an engine that propels an aircraft by ejecting a jet of gas
  • Synonym: rocket engine - an engine that produces thrust by expelling exhaust in one direction

Etymology

From 'reaction' (from Latin 'reactio', meaning 'a moving back') and 'engine'.
