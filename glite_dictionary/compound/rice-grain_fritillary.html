rice-grain fritillary

1. NOUN

/ ˈraɪs ɡreɪn frɪˈtɪləri / (AmE), / ˈraɪs ɡreɪn frɪˈtɪləri / (BrE)
a herb native to northwestern North America, with green-and-purple bell-shaped flowers
herb with purple-green bell-shaped flowers

Examples

The rice-grain fritillary blooms early in the spring along the Pacific Northwest.
Botanists are fascinated by the unique coloration of the rice-grain fritillary.
We spotted several rice-grain fritillaries growing near the forest edge.
