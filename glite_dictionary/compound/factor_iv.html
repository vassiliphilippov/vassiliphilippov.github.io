factor IV

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæktɚ fɔr / (AmE), / ˈfæktə fɔː / (BrE)
the calcium ion, recognized as a component necessary for blood clotting
calcium ion in blood clotting
medical

Examples

Factor IV is essential for several steps in the blood coagulation process.
The absence of sufficient factor IV can lead to problems with clotting.
Doctors measured the patient's factor IV levels during treatment for the bleeding disorder.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calcium - a chemical element, symbol Ca, essential mineral in the body
  • Degree Higher: factor VIII - a protein involved in blood clotting, also known as antihemophilic factor
  • Degree Lower: factor II - prothrombin, a protein involved in blood coagulation
