sacral plexus

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪ.krəl ˈplɛk.səs / (AmE), / ˈseɪ.krəl ˈplɛk.səs / (BrE)
a network of nerves formed by the fourth and fifth lumbar nerves and the first three sacral nerves, which supplies nerves to the pelvis and lower limbs
network of nerves in the pelvis
medical

Examples

The sacral plexus is essential for providing nerve supply to the lower limbs.
Injury to the sacral plexus can cause serious mobility problems.
The surgeon carefully avoided the sacral plexus during the procedure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lumbar plexus - nerve plexus in the lower back formed by lumbar nerves

Etymology

From Latin 'sacer' (sacred, relating to the sacrum) and 'plexus' (network).
