association cortex

1. NOUN

/ əˌsoʊsiˈeɪʃn ˈkɔːrtɛks / (AmE), / əˌsəʊsiˈeɪʃn ˈkɔːtɛks / (BrE)
the regions of the cerebral cortex that are not primarily involved in sensory or motor functions, but are responsible for higher cognitive processes such as thinking, planning, and interpreting information
part of brain for higher cognitive functions
medical

Examples

Damage to the association cortex can result in impairments in complex language and reasoning abilities.
Researchers found increased activity in the association cortex during problem-solving tasks.
The association cortex integrates information from different sensory inputs to help us interpret the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cerebral cortex - the outer layer of the brain, involved in many important neural functions

Etymology

From 'association' (linking together), referring to integrative function, and Latin 'cortex' (bark, rind), referring to the brain's outer layer.
