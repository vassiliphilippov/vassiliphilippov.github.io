distal muscular dystrophy

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɪstl ˈmʌskjələr dɪsˈtrəfi / (AmE), / ˈdɪstəl ˈmʌskjʊlə dɪsˈtrɒfi / (BrE)
A rare form of muscular dystrophy that develops between ages 40 and 60, marked by progressive weakness and wasting of muscles in the hands, forearms, and lower legs, typically inherited in an autosomal dominant manner.
form of muscular dystrophy affecting distal muscles
medical

Examples

People diagnosed with distal muscular dystrophy often first notice weakness in their hands.
Genetic testing confirmed that his symptoms were caused by distal muscular dystrophy.
Effective management of distal muscular dystrophy can help preserve mobility and independence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: muscular dystrophy - a group of inherited disorders causing muscle weakness and wasting
