kid glove

1. NOUN

/ kɪd ɡlʌv / (AmE), / kɪd ɡlʌv / (BrE)
A glove made from fine, soft leather, especially that of young goats (kids).
glove made of kidskin leather

Examples

In the 19th century, wearing a kid glove was a sign of elegance and wealth.
She carefully chose a pair of white kid gloves to match her evening gown.
The shop specializes in leather accessories, including kid gloves in various colors.

Etymology

From 'kid' meaning a young goat, referring to the leather used, and 'glove'.
