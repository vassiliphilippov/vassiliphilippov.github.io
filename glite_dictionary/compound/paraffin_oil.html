paraffin oil

1. NOUN

/ ˈpærəˌfɪn ɔɪl / (AmE), / ˈparəfɪn ɔɪl / (BrE)
(British) Kerosene; a thin, clear liquid used as a fuel, especially for heating and lighting.
kerosene (especially British usage)
british

Examples

In the countryside, many homes still use paraffin oil heaters during winter.
She refilled the old lamp with paraffin oil before lighting it.
The shed contained several bottles of paraffin oil for emergencies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kerosene - a flammable oil used as a fuel
