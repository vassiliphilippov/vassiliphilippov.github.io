Minoan civilisation

1. NOUN

/ mɪˈnoʊ.ən ˌsɪv.əl.əˈzeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / mɪˈnəʊ.ən ˌsɪv.ɪ.laɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the Bronze Age culture that developed on Crete and flourished from about 3000 to 1100 BC, known for its advanced art, architecture, and writing systems
Bronze Age culture of ancient Crete

Examples

The palace at Knossos was at the heart of the Minoan civilisation.
Archaeologists unearthed pottery and frescoes from the Minoan civilisation on Crete.
The decline of the Minoan civilisation is still debated among historians.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Mycenaean civilization - Late Bronze Age Greek civilization, following the Minoan civilization

Etymology

From Minos, legendary king of Crete, combined with civilisation (from Latin 'civis', meaning citizen).
