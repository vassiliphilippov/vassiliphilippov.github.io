plant organ

1. NOUN

/ plænt ˈɔrɡən / (AmE), / plɑːnt ˈɔːɡən / (BrE)
a distinct structural and functional part of a plant or fungus, such as a leaf, root, stem, or flower
major part of a plant or fungus

Examples

The leaf is a type of plant organ specialized for photosynthesis.
Roots are essential plant organs that absorb water and nutrients from the soil.
During development, each plant organ grows according to genetically determined patterns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: organ - a part of an organism that performs a specific function
