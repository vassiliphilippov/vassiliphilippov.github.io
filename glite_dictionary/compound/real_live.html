real live

1. ADJ

/ ˈriːəl laɪv / (AmE), / ˈrɪəl laɪv / (BrE)
used to emphasize that someone or something is genuine or actually exists, especially as opposed to being imagined or part of a story
genuine, actually existing (emphatic phrase)

Examples

The children were amazed when a real live magician came to their party.
I've never met a real live astronaut before!
Seeing a real live tiger at the zoo was thrilling.

Synset Examples

He finally got to see a real live magician perform.

2. ADJ

/ riːəl laɪv / (AmE), / riːəl laɪv / (BrE)
Used to emphasize that someone or something is genuine or authentic and not fictional, imaginary, or artificial.
genuine; not fictional or imaginary

Examples

Meeting a real live astronaut was the highlight of her year.
The children were amazed to see a real live elephant at the zoo.
It felt strange to watch a real live concert after so many months online.

Synset Examples

She wants to meet a real live author.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: genuine - truly what something is said to be; authentic
  • Synonym: authentic - of undisputed origin; genuine
  • Antonym: fake - not genuine; counterfeit
