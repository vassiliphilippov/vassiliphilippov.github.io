harvest mouse

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑrvɪst maʊs / (AmE), / ˈhɑːvɪst maʊs / (BrE)
a small reddish-brown mouse native to Eurasia, typically found in cornfields and other grassy habitats
small reddish-brown Eurasian field mouse

Examples

We spotted a harvest mouse building a nest among the wheat stalks.
The harvest mouse is known for its prehensile tail, which helps it climb tall grasses.
Conservationists are working to protect the habitat of the endangered harvest mouse.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɑrvəst maʊs / (AmE), / ˈhɑːvɪst maʊs / (BrE)
A small mouse species found in fields or grasslands, particularly in grain crops, native to the Americas or Eurasia.
small mouse found in fields or crops

Examples

The harvest mouse built a tiny nest among the wheat stalks.
Researchers tracked the movements of several harvest mice in the field.
A harvest mouse is rarely seen except during harvest season, when the crops are cut.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: field mouse - a small mouse found in fields or meadows

Etymology

From 'harvest' (referring to grain crops) + 'mouse', indicating its typical habitat.
