visual sense

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪʒuəl sɛns / (AmE), / ˈvɪʒjʊəl sɛns / (BrE)
the ability to perceive the world by sight; the sense of seeing
sense of sight; ability to see

Examples

Owls have a highly developed visual sense that allows them to hunt at night.
After the accident, his visual sense was partially impaired, making reading difficult.
The scientist studied how the brain processes information from the visual sense.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sight - the faculty or power of seeing
  • Antonym: blindness - the condition of lacking visual sense
