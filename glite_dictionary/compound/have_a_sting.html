to have a sting

1. VERB

/ hæv ə stɪŋ / (AmE), / hæv ə stɪŋ / (BrE)
(of words, actions, etc.) to cause emotional pain or hurt; to leave a sharp, lasting sense of discomfort.
cause emotional pain or hurt

Examples

Her words had a sting that took me by surprise.
Even a polite rejection can have a sting if you're feeling vulnerable.
His joke had a sting that made everyone uncomfortable.

Synset Examples

The criticism had a sting that lingered for days.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sting - a sharp, painful effect or feeling
