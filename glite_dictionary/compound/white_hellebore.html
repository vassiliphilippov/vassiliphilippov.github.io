white hellebore

1. NOUN

/ waɪt ˈhɛlɪˌbɔr / (AmE), / waɪt ˈhɛlɪˌbɔː / (BrE)
A North American plant with large leaves and yellowish-green flowers in racemes, which yields a toxic alkaloid used medicinally.
North American plant with toxic medicinal alkaloid

Examples

The roots of white hellebore have been used in traditional medicine, despite their toxicity.
Gardeners should handle white hellebore with care because of its poisonous alkaloids.
The distinctive yellow-green flowers make white hellebore easy to identify in the wild.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hellebore - any plant of the genus Helleborus, some of which are poisonous
