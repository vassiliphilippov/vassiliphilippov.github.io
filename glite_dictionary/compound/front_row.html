front row

1. NOUN

/ frʌnt roʊ / (AmE), / frʌnt rəʊ / (BrE)
the first row of seats closest to the stage, screen, or area where the main action takes place
first row of seats near the stage or action

Examples

We managed to get seats in the front row at the basketball game.
Tickets for the front row usually sell out quickly at popular concerts.
Sitting in the front row, she felt almost part of the performance.

Synset Examples

She always wanted to be in the front row of the fashion show.
She always wants to be in the front row for movies.
We sat in the front row at the concert.
They cheered loudly while sitting in the front row.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: back row - the last row of seats furthest from the stage or action
