United States government

1. NOUN

/ ˌjuː.naɪ.t̬ɪd ˈsteɪts ˌɡʌv.ɚn.mənt / (AmE), / ˌjuː.naɪ.tɪd ˈsteɪts ˌɡʌv.ən.mənt / (BrE)
the executive, legislative, and judicial branches that collectively govern the United States at the federal level
federal governing system of the U.S.

Examples

The United States government passed new legislation to address climate change.
He has worked for the United States government for over twenty years.
International relations are often shaped by the policies set by the United States government.
