to burnish public image

1. VERB

/ ˈbɝːnɪʃ ˌpʌblɪk ˈɪmɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈbɜːnɪʃ ˌpʌblɪk ˈɪmɪdʒ / (BrE)
to improve or enhance the reputation of a person, company, or organization in the eyes of the public
improve reputation or public standing

Examples

The politician hired a media consultant to burnish public image before the election.
The corporation launched a charity initiative to burnish public image after recent scandals.
Regular community outreach helped the firm burnish public image and attract new customers.

Synset Examples

They worked to burnish the company's public image.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polish one's image - improve the way one or one's organization is seen by the public
  • Antonym: tarnish public image - damage or harm one's reputation or standing

Etymology

From 'burnish' meaning 'to polish', figuratively extended to refer to reputation.
