Old Saxon

1. NOUN

/ oʊld ˈsæksən / (AmE), / əʊld ˈsæksən / (BrE)
the West Germanic language spoken in northern Germany before 1200, predecessor of Low German
historic West Germanic language (pre-1200)

Examples

Old Saxon was primarily spoken in what is now northwestern Germany before the 12th century.
Epic poetry like the Heliand was composed in Old Saxon.
Many features of modern Low German can be traced back to Old Saxon.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Low German - the modern language descended from Old Saxon

Etymology

Saxon refers to the Saxons, a Germanic people; 'Old' denotes its historic stage.
