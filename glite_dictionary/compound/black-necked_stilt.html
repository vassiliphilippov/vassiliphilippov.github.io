black-necked stilt

1. NOUN

/ ˌblæk ˈnɛkt ˈstɪlt / (AmE), / ˌblæk ˈnɛkt ˈstɪlt / (BrE)
A long-legged wading bird (Himantopus mexicanus) of the Americas, with black plumage extending from the head down the back of the neck.
long-legged wading bird with black neck

Examples

We watched a black-necked stilt feeding gracefully in the shallow marsh.
The black-necked stilt is easily recognized by its slender legs and striking markings.
Several black-necked stilts migrate through this wetland every spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stilt - a long-legged wading bird of the genus Himantopus
