polyurethane foam

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑliˈjʊrəˌθeɪn foʊm / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈjʊərəˌθeɪn fəʊm / (BrE)
A light, spongy material made from polyurethane plastics by adding water during manufacturing; commonly used for insulation, cushioning, and packaging.
spongy plastic material for insulation or cushions

Examples

The sofa was stuffed with polyurethane foam for extra comfort.
Soundproof rooms often use panels made of polyurethane foam.
Spray polyurethane foam is applied for thermal insulation in buildings.

Etymology

From 'polyurethane', which combines 'poly-' (many) and 'urethane', referring to a type of polymer.
