beard moss

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɪrd mɑs / (AmE), / ˈbɪəd mɒs / (BrE)
a greenish-grey, hanging lichen that grows on trees, resembling a long beard
a pendent lichen growing on trees

Examples

The old oaks were draped with beard moss, giving the forest a mysterious appearance.
Scientists studied beard moss to learn more about the air quality in the region.
Birds often use beard moss to line their nests high up in the branches.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lichen - a composite organism arising from algae or cyanobacteria living among filaments of fungi
