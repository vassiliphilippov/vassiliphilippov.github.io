Transvaal Province

1. PROPN

/ trænzˈvɑl ˈprɑvɪns / (AmE), / tranzˈvɑːl ˈprɒvɪns / (BrE)
a former province in northeastern South Africa, originally inhabited by Bantu-speaking Africans and later colonized by the Boers
former South African province

Examples

Many gold mines were established in Transvaal Province during the late nineteenth century.
The history of the Transvaal Province is closely linked with the rise of the Boer republics.
Following the end of apartheid, Transvaal Province was divided into several new provinces.

Etymology

From Afrikaans 'Transvaal', meaning 'across the Vaal River'.
