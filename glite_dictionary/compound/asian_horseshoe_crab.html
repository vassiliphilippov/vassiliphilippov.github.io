Asian horseshoe crab

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪʒən ˈhɔrsˌʃuː kræb / (AmE), / ˈeɪʃ(ə)n ˈhɔːsʃuː kræb / (BrE)
a species of horseshoe crab found along the coasts of eastern Asia, known for its hard shell and distinctive tail spine
horseshoe crab native to eastern Asia

Examples

The Asian horseshoe crab is commonly found in shallow waters along the coast of China and Japan.
Researchers are studying the population decline of the Asian horseshoe crab due to habitat loss.
Children were fascinated by the ancient-looking Asian horseshoe crab they discovered on the beach.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horseshoe crab - marine arthropod with a hard carapace and long tail spine
