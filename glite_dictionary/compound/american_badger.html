American badger

1. NOUN

/ əˌmɛrɪkən ˈbædʒɚ / (AmE), / əˌmɛrɪkən ˈbadʒə / (BrE)
a species of badger native to North America, recognized by its distinct facial markings and burrowing habits
a badger species native to North America

Examples

The American badger is well adapted to life in open grasslands and prairies.
Farmers sometimes spot an American badger digging for rodents in their fields.
Unlike its European cousin, the American badger prefers solitary living.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: badger - a burrowing mammal of the mustelid family
