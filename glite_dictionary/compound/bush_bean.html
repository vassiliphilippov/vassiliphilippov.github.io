bush bean

1. NOUN

/ bʊʃ biːn / (AmE), / bʊʃ biːn / (BrE)
A type of bean plant that grows in a bushy form and does not require staking or supports.
type of bean plant with bushy growth

Examples

We planted several bush bean varieties in our vegetable garden this year.
Unlike pole beans, bush bean plants do not need a trellis for support.
The bush bean produced a heavy crop despite its compact size.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: pole bean - a bean plant with climbing vines that requires supports
