Japanese snowbell

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˈpæniz ˈsnoʊˌbɛl / (AmE), / dʒəˈpæniːz ˈsnəʊˌbɛl / (BrE)
A deciduous shrub or small tree native to China and Japan, known for its fragrant, white, bell-shaped flowers.
deciduous Asian tree with white flowers

Examples

The Japanese snowbell blooms in late spring with clusters of delicate white flowers.
She planted a Japanese snowbell in her garden to attract pollinators.
A row of Japanese snowbells lines the walkway, creating a stunning floral display each year.
