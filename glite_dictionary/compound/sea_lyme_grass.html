sea lyme grass

1. NOUN

/ siː laɪm ɡræs / (AmE), / siː laɪm ɡrɑːs / (BrE)
A type of dune grass found along the Pacific coast, used for stabilizing sand dunes.
dune grass used as sand binder

Examples

The sea lyme grass plays a crucial role in preventing coastal erosion by stabilizing the dunes.
Botanists planted sea lyme grass to help restore the damaged shoreline habitat.
You can recognize sea lyme grass by its tough, bluish-green leaves and tall stems.
