earnings before interest, taxes, depreciation and amortization

1. NOUN

/ ˈɜrnɪŋz bɪˈfɔr ˈɪntrəst ˈtæksɪz dɪˌpriʃiˈeɪʃən ənd əˌmɔrtəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈɜːnɪŋz bɪˈfɔː ˈɪntrɪst ˈtæksɪz dɪˌpriːʃɪˈeɪʃən ənd əˌmɔːtɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
a measure of a company's profitability calculated as net income before deducting interest, taxes, depreciation, and amortization expenses, commonly abbreviated as EBITDA
profit measure before interest, taxes, depreciation, and amortization

Examples

Many investors look at earnings before interest, taxes, depreciation and amortization to assess a company's core profitability.
The company's earnings before interest, taxes, depreciation and amortization increased significantly over the last fiscal year.
Analysts often compare earnings before interest, taxes, depreciation and amortization across companies to evaluate operational performance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: EBITDA - abbreviation for 'earnings before interest, taxes, depreciation and amortization'
  • Degree Lower: net income - company's total earnings after all expenses

Etymology

A compound term formed from the key accounting concepts: earnings, interest, taxes, depreciation, and amortization; commonly abbreviated as EBITDA.
