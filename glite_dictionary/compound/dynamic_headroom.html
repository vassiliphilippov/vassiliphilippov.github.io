dynamic headroom

1. NOUN

/ daɪˈnæmɪk ˈhɛdˌrum / (AmE), / daɪˈnæmɪk ˈhɛdˌruːm / (BrE)
the capacity of an audio or electronic system to accommodate brief peaks in sound level without distortion
audio system's capacity for loud sound peaks

Examples

The amplifier's dynamic headroom ensures that even sudden loud passages are reproduced clearly.
Audio engineers often measure dynamic headroom when designing high-fidelity equipment.
Without enough dynamic headroom, music can sound distorted during peaks in volume.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: headroom - the amount of space between the highest level a system can handle and its normal operation

Etymology

From 'dynamic' (relating to power or force) + 'headroom' (space or margin), both from Greek and English roots.
