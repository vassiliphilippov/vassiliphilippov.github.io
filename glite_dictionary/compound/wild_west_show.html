Wild West show

1. NOUN

/ waɪld wɛst ʃoʊ / (AmE), / waɪld wɛst ʃəʊ / (BrE)
a form of popular entertainment, especially in the late 19th and early 20th centuries, featuring displays of cowboy skills, horsemanship, and re-enactments of frontier life, often associated with Buffalo Bill.
entertainment show depicting the American frontier

Examples

Buffalo Bill's Wild West show thrilled audiences across the United States and Europe.
Many myths about cowboys originated from the performances in the Wild West show.
The Wild West show combined horseback stunts, sharpshooting, and dramatic re-enactments of frontier life.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: rodeo - a competitive sport based on skills from cattle ranching

Etymology

The term comes from shows organized by Buffalo Bill (William F. Cody) in the late 19th century to depict scenes from the American frontier.
