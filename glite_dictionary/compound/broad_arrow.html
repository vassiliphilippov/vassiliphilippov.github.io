broad arrow

1. NOUN

/ brɔd ˈæroʊ / (AmE), / brɔːd ˈærəʊ / (BrE)
an arrow with a wide, typically barbed, head, often used historically for marking or in warfare
arrow with a wide barbed head

Examples

The museum displayed a broad arrow used during medieval battles.
A broad arrow was often stamped on government property in Britain.
He recognized the broad arrow design etched into the old chest's wood.

2. NOUN

/ brɔd ˈæroʊ / (AmE), / brɔːd ˈarəʊ / (BrE)
a mark in the shape of an arrowhead, used to identify property belonging to the British government
arrowhead mark on British government property
british

Examples

The crates were stamped with a broad arrow to indicate government ownership.
Old muskets marked with the broad arrow became valuable collector's items.
Only goods bearing the broad arrow were allowed on military transport.

Etymology

Refers to the broad, stylised arrow symbol used as an official mark since the 16th century in England.

3. NOUN

/ brɔd ˈæroʊ / (AmE), / brɔːd ˈærəʊ / (BrE)
a mark shaped like an arrowhead, used especially in the past to indicate government property or to mark convicts' clothing
arrowhead-shaped mark, esp. for official use

Examples

The uniforms of prisoners were stamped with a broad arrow to indicate their status.
Many old government tools bore the broad arrow as a sign of official property.
He noticed a faint broad arrow mark etched into the ancient stone.
