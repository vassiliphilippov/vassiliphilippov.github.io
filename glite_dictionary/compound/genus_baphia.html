genus Baphia

1. NOUN

/ ˈd͡ʒiːnəs bəˈfaɪə / (AmE), / ˈd͡ʒiːnəs bəˈfaɪə / (BrE)
a small genus of shrubs, lianas, and trees native to Africa and Madagascar, belonging to the family Fabaceae.
African genus of shrubs and trees (Fabaceae)

Examples

The genus Baphia includes several species renowned for their rich dyes and useful wood.
Researchers discovered a new member of the genus Baphia in Madagascar's rainforests.
Traditional healers sometimes use plants from the genus Baphia for medicinal purposes.

Etymology

From New Latin 'Baphia', genus name formed using Greek roots.
