Irish whiskey

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪrɪʃ ˈwɪski / (AmE), / ˈaɪərɪʃ ˈwɪski / (BrE)
A type of whiskey distilled and matured in Ireland, typically made chiefly from barley.
whiskey made in Ireland, mainly from barley

Examples

He ordered a glass of Irish whiskey at the bar to celebrate his promotion.
Many connoisseurs appreciate the smooth flavor of traditional Irish whiskey.
The recipe calls for a splash of Irish whiskey to enhance the dessert.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Scotch whisky - whisky distilled in Scotland, mainly from malted barley
  • Degree Lower: whiskey - a distilled alcoholic beverage made from fermented grain mash
