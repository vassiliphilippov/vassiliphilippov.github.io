infinite sequence

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪn.fə.nət ˈsiː.kwəns / (AmE), / ˈɪn.fɪ.nɪt ˈsiː.kwəns / (BrE)
A sequence with an endless succession of elements, continuing indefinitely according to a specified order.
mathematical sequence with no end

Examples

In mathematics, an infinite sequence is a list of elements that goes on without end.
The decimal expansion of pi can be represented as an infinite sequence of digits.
Each natural number can be placed uniquely within the infinite sequence of counting numbers.

Synset Examples

Normally, the term infinite sequence refers to a sequence which is infinite in one direction, and finite in the other — the sequence has a first element, but no final element (a singly infinite sequence).

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sequence - an ordered list of elements
