fractional monetary unit

1. NOUN

/ ˈfrækʃənl ˈmɑnəˌtɛri ˈjunɪt / (AmE), / ˈfrækʃnəl ˈmɒnɪtri ˈjuːnɪt / (BrE)
A monetary unit that is valued at a fraction, usually one hundredth, of the principal monetary unit of a country.
subunit of main currency

Examples

The euro cent is the fractional monetary unit of the euro.
In many countries, the fractional monetary unit is used for small transactions.
A penny is the fractional monetary unit of the British pound.

Etymology

fractional from Latin fractio (a breaking), monetary from Latin monetarius (of money), unit from Latin unitas (oneness).
