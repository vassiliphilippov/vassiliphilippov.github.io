gray matter

1. NOUN

/ ɡreɪ ˈmætər / (AmE), / ɡreɪ ˈmætə / (BrE)
The darker tissue of the brain and spinal cord, consisting mainly of nerve cell bodies and branching fibers, forming the cerebral cortex and other nerve centers.
brain tissue composed of nerve cell bodies
medical

Examples

Scientists observed an increase in gray matter in the brains of the participants after training.
Damage to the gray matter of the spinal cord can cause loss of movement and sensation.
Meditation has been linked to changes in gray matter density in certain brain regions.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: white matter - pale-colored nervous tissue composed mainly of myelinated fibers in the brain and spinal cord

Etymology

Refers to the grayish color of this type of brain tissue, in contrast to white matter.
