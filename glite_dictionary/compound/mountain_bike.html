mountain bike

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaʊn.tn̩ ˌbaɪk / (AmE), / ˈmaʊn.tɪn ˌbaɪk / (BrE)
a bicycle with a strong frame and wide tires, originally designed for riding over rough terrain such as hills and mountains
bicycle for rough terrain

Examples

He bought a new mountain bike to explore the trails near his house.
Many people use a mountain bike for both off-road adventures and daily commuting.
Her old mountain bike was perfect for the rocky paths in the national park.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bicycle - a vehicle with two wheels powered by pedaling
  • Antonym: road bike - a lightweight bicycle designed for speed on paved roads
