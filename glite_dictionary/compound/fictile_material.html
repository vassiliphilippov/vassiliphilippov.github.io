fictile material

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪktəl məˈtɪriəl / (AmE), / ˈfɪktaɪl məˈtɪəriəl / (BrE)
A substance, especially clay or a similar material, that can be easily shaped or molded, commonly used in art and sculpture.
clay-like material for shaping

Examples

The sculptor preferred to work with fictile material because it allowed for intricate detail.
Many ancient artifacts were made from fictile material found locally.
Art students practiced by shaping figures out of fictile material in class.

Synset Examples

Fictile materials are perfect for crafting detailed models.
The artist created a fictile sculpture from clay.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clay - a stiff, sticky fine-grained earth used in making pottery and bricks

Etymology

From Latin fictilis (made of clay), from fingere (to shape, form).
