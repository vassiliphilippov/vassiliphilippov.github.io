emphysematous phlegmon

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛm.fɪˈsi.mə.təs ˈflɛɡ.mɑn / (AmE), / ˌɛm.fɪˈsiː.mə.təs ˈflɛɡ.mɒn / (BrE)
A rare and deadly bacterial infection characterized by rapidly spreading tissue death and gas formation in tissues, often caused by Clostridium bacteria.
severe, gas-producing bacterial infection
medical

Examples

The surgeon suspected emphysematous phlegmon in the patient after noticing gas in the soft tissues on imaging.
Prompt treatment with antibiotics and surgery is critical for those diagnosed with emphysematous phlegmon.
Clostridial infection led to emphysematous phlegmon, necessitating immediate intervention to prevent systemic complications.

Etymology

From Greek emphysema (inflation, from emphysan 'to blow in') and phlegmon (inflammation, from phlegma 'inflammation').
