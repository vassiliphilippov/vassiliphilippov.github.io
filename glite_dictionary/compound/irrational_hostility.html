irrational hostility

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈræʃənəl hɑˈstɪləti / (AmE), / ɪˈræʃənəl hɒˈstɪlɪti / (BrE)
extreme antagonism or prejudice that lacks a reasonable basis or justification.
unjustified extreme antagonism

Examples

His irrational hostility toward his new coworker made teamwork nearly impossible.
The nation's irrational hostility towards outsiders led to restrictive immigration policies.
Sometimes, irrational hostility can stem from personal insecurities or past experiences.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prejudice - preconceived opinion not based on reason
  • Synonym: animosity - strong hostility or dislike
  • Antonym: tolerance - acceptance of different opinions or behaviors
