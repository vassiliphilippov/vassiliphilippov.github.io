Epistle of Paul the Apostle to the Ephesians

1. PROPN

/ ɪˈpɪsəl əv pɔl ði əˈpɑsəl tu ði ɪˈfiʒənz / (AmE), / ɪˈpɪsəl əv pɔːl ði əˈpɑːsl tuː ði ɪˈfiːʒənz / (BrE)
a book of the New Testament, written as a letter from Saint Paul to the Ephesians, outlining the divine plan for salvation through Christ
New Testament book; Paul's letter to the Ephesians

Examples

Many scholars have studied the theology of the Epistle of Paul the Apostle to the Ephesians.
During Bible study, we discussed passages from the Epistle of Paul the Apostle to the Ephesians.
The Epistle of Paul the Apostle to the Ephesians addresses themes of unity and redemption.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ephesians - another term for the Epistle of Paul to the Ephesians
  • Degree Higher: New Testament - the second major part of the Christian biblical canon

Etymology

Epistle from Greek 'epistolē' (letter); Ephesians refers to people of Ephesus, an ancient Greek city.
