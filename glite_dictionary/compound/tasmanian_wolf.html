Tasmanian wolf

1. NOUN

/ tæzˈmeɪniən wʊlf / (AmE), / tazˈmeɪniən wʊlf / (BrE)
a rare doglike carnivorous marsupial of Tasmania, with stripes on its back, likely extinct; also known as the thylacine
extinct doglike marsupial of Tasmania

Examples

The Tasmanian wolf was once the largest carnivorous marsupial in Tasmania.
Researchers believe the Tasmanian wolf became extinct in the early twentieth century.
Many people have reported unconfirmed sightings of the Tasmanian wolf in the wild.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thylacine - another name for the Tasmanian wolf

Etymology

Named after Tasmania + wolf, describing its resemblance to a wolf; also called 'thylacine' from Greek 'thylakos' (pouch).
