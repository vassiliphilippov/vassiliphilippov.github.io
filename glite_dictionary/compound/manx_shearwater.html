Manx shearwater

1. NOUN

/ mæŋks ˈʃɛrˌwɔtɚ / (AmE), / mæŋks ˈʃɪəˌwɔːtə / (BrE)
a small black-and-white seabird of the petrel family, common in the northeastern Atlantic, known for its long migratory flights
Atlantic black-and-white seabird

Examples

The Manx shearwater is famous for its long-distance migrations between the North Atlantic and South America.
During the summer, thousands of Manx shearwaters return to breed on remote island colonies.
I spotted a Manx shearwater gliding effortlessly above the waves during our boat trip.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shearwater - a long-winged seabird in the petrel family

Etymology

Manx refers to the Isle of Man, where the bird breeds, and shearwater describes its gliding flight close to water.
