American ivy

1. NOUN

/ əˈmɛrɪkən ˈaɪvi / (AmE), / əˈmɛrɪkən ˈaɪvi / (BrE)
A common North American climbing vine, noted for its compound leaves and bluish-black berrylike fruit.
North American climbing vine

Examples

The fence was covered in American ivy, making the garden look lush and green.
Some birds are attracted to the berries of American ivy during the fall.
He planted American ivy along the house to help keep the walls cool in summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Virginia creeper - a common North American vine with similar leaves and fruit
