prosodic system

1. NOUN

/ prəˈzɑdɪk ˈsɪstəm / (AmE), / prəˈzɒdɪk ˈsɪstəm / (BrE)
the system of rules governing stress, rhythm, and intonation patterns in a particular language
system of stress and intonation in a language

Examples

The English prosodic system relies heavily on stress to convey meaning and grammatical relationships.
Researchers compare the prosodic system of Chinese with that of English to identify differences in intonation.
Learning the prosodic system of a new language can be challenging for adult learners.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prosody - the patterns of rhythm and sound used in poetry or language

Etymology

From Greek 'prosodikos' (relating to accent or song), from 'prosodos' (song, ode) + 'system'.
