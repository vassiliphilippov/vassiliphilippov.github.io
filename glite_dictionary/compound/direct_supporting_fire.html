direct supporting fire

1. NOUN

/ dəˈrɛkt səˈpɔrtɪŋ faɪr / (AmE), / dɪˈrɛkt səˈpɔːtɪŋ faɪə / (BrE)
fire delivered to assist a specific part of a military force in action, rather than the entire force.
fire to support a specific military unit

Examples

The artillery provided direct supporting fire to the advancing infantry during the assault.
He called for direct supporting fire to break through the enemy's defensive line.
Proper coordination is crucial when delivering direct supporting fire to avoid friendly casualties.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: general supporting fire - fire delivered in support of the entire force, not a specific part
