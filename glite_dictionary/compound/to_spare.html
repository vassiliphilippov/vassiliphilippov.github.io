to spare

1. X

/ tə spɛr / (AmE), / tə speə / (BrE)
used after a noun to indicate that there is more than is needed or available in excess
more than is needed; in excess

Examples

With only five minutes to spare, they caught the last train home.
They have money to spare after their big win.
We packed enough food to spare for the entire trip.

Synset Examples

She offered advice to spare during the meeting.
There were resources to spare for the project.
