September 11 attacks

1. NOUN

/ sɛpˈtɛmbɚ ɪˈlɛvən əˈtæks / (AmE), / sɛpˈtɛmbə ɪˈlɛvən əˈtæks / (BrE)
the terrorist attacks on September 11, 2001, in which hijacked airplanes were used to strike targets in the United States, including the World Trade Center and the Pentagon
terrorist attacks in the U.S. on September 11, 2001

Examples

The September 11 attacks changed the course of world history and led to major changes in U.S. security policy.
Every year, ceremonies are held in memory of those lost in the September 11 attacks.
Books and movies have documented the events of the September 11 attacks in detail.
