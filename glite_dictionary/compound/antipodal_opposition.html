antipodal opposition

1. NOUN

/ ænˈtɪp.ə.dl ˌɑː.pəˈzɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / ænˈtɪp.ə.dl ˌɒp.əˈzɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
opposition or relation between points that are directly opposite each other on a diameter, especially on a circle or sphere
opposition across a diameter (antipodes)

Examples

The concept of antipodal opposition is important when studying the properties of spheres in geometry.
In astronomy, certain planets exhibit antipodal opposition during their orbits.
The scientist described the antipodal opposition between two points on the globe to illustrate global distance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antipode - a point directly opposite another on a sphere

Etymology

From Greek 'antipous' (opposite the foot) and Latin 'oppositio' (opposition).
