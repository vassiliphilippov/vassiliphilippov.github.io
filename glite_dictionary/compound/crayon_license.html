crayon license

1. NOUN

/ ˈkreɪˌɒn ˈlaɪsəns / (AmE), / ˈkreɪən ˈlaɪsəns / (BrE)
a humorous or mock certificate given to children, especially preschoolers, to signify their 'official' ability to use crayons or as a playful mark of graduation from an early childhood class.
mock certificate for preschoolers' crayon use
informal

Examples

After completing preschool, Emma proudly showed her crayon license to her grandparents.
The teacher handed out a crayon license to every child in the art club.
Getting his crayon license made Oliver feel like a real artist.

Synset Examples

He received his crayon license after finishing preschool.
Every child gets a crayon license at the art class.
