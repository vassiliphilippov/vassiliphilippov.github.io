brass family

1. NOUN

/ bræs ˈfæməli / (AmE), / brɑːs ˈfæməli / (BrE)
A group of musical instruments, such as trumpets and trombones, that produce sound by the vibration of the player's lips against a mouthpiece and are typically made of brass.
group of brass musical instruments

Examples

The trumpet, tuba, and French horn all belong to the brass family of instruments.
She chose to play an instrument from the brass family in the school band.
The brass family creates a powerful and resonant sound in orchestras.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: woodwind family - group of woodwind musical instruments
