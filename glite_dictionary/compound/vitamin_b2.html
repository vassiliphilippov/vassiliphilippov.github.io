vitamin B2

1. NOUN

/ vaɪtəˌmɪn ˈbi tu / (AmE), / ˈvɪtəmɪn ˈbiː tuː / (BrE)
a vitamin of the B complex essential for human health, which prevents skin lesions and weight loss; also known as riboflavin
a B vitamin, also called riboflavin
medical

Examples

Many breakfast cereals are fortified with vitamin B2 to help prevent deficiencies.
Doctors recommend including foods rich in vitamin B2 in your diet.
A lack of vitamin B2 can cause cracked lips and a sore throat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: riboflavin - the chemical name for vitamin B2
  • Degree Lower: vitamin B1 - another B vitamin, also called thiamine
  • Degree Higher: vitamin B12 - another B vitamin, also called cobalamin, important for nerve function
