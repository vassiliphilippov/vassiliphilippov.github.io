backhand drive

1. NOUN

/ ˈbækˌhænd draɪv / (AmE), / ˈbakhænd draɪv / (BrE)
A hard, straight shot made with a backhand stroke, especially in racquet sports such as tennis.
hard straight shot using backhand stroke

Examples

She perfected her backhand drive to win more points during the tournament.
His powerful backhand drive left his opponent unable to return the ball.
The coach advised her to practice the backhand drive for greater consistency.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: forehand drive - hard straight shot using a forehand stroke
