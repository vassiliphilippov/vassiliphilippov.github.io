orthogonal opposition

1. NOUN

/ ɔrˈθɑɡənl əˈpɑzɪʃən / (AmE), / ɔːˈθɒɡənl əˈpɒzɪʃən / (BrE)
the relationship of being at right angles to each other, especially in geometry or abstract frameworks
relation of things at right angles

Examples

In mathematics, vectors in orthogonal opposition have a dot product of zero.
The architect used lines in orthogonal opposition to create a striking geometric pattern.
These molecular axes are in orthogonal opposition, maximizing their spatial separation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perpendicularity - the state of being perpendicular or at right angles

Etymology

From Greek orthogonios (right-angled) and Latin oppositio (opposition).
