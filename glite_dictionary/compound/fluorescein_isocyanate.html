fluorescein isocyanate

1. NOUN

/ fluˈɹɛsiːn ˌaɪsoʊsaɪˈəneɪt / (AmE), / fluˈɒrəsiːn ˌaɪsɪəˈneɪt / (BrE)
a fluorescent compound often attached to antibodies to visualize cells or proteins in immunofluorescence microscopy
fluorochrome used in immunofluorescence
medical

Examples

The laboratory used fluorescein isocyanate to label the antibodies in their experiment.
When excited by ultraviolet light, fluorescein isocyanate emits a bright green fluorescence.
Researchers rely on fluorescein isocyanate to make target proteins visible under the fluorescence microscope.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fluorescein - a synthetic organic compound that exhibits fluorescence

Etymology

From 'fluorescein', a synthetic organic compound, and 'isocyanate', from the chemical group isocyanate (NCO).
