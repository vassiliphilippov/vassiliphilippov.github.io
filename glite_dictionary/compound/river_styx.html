River Styx

1. PROPN

/ ˈrɪvər ˈstɪks / (AmE), / ˈrɪvə ˈstɪks / (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) a river in the underworld, Hades, across which the ferryman Charon carried the souls of the dead.
mythical river of the underworld in Greek mythology

Examples

In ancient Greek myths, souls were ferried across the River Styx to the realm of the dead.
She referenced the River Styx when describing her fear of the unknown after death.
The oath sworn on the River Styx was considered unbreakable by the gods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hades - the underworld in Greek mythology
  • Synonym: Charon - the ferryman who carried souls across the River Styx

Etymology

From Greek 'Styx', derived from the word 'stygos', meaning hate or detestation.
