Tacoma Narrows Bridge

1. PROPN

/ təˈkoʊmə ˈnæroʊz brɪdʒ / (AmE), / təˈkəʊmə ˈnærəʊz brɪdʒ / (BrE)
a suspension bridge spanning Puget Sound at Tacoma, Washington, known for its catastrophic collapse in 1940 and subsequent reconstruction
suspension bridge in Tacoma, Washington

Examples

The Tacoma Narrows Bridge famously collapsed in 1940 due to wind-induced vibrations.
Thousands of cars cross the Tacoma Narrows Bridge every day, connecting Tacoma with the Kitsap Peninsula.
Engineers studied the failure of the Tacoma Narrows Bridge to improve suspension bridge design worldwide.
