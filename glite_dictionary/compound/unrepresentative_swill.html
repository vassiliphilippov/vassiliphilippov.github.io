unrepresentative swill

1. NOUN

/ ˌʌnrɛprɪˈzɛntətɪv swɪl / (AmE), / ˌʌnrɪˌprɛzənˈteɪtɪv swɪl / (BrE)
used to refer to remarks, information, or reports that are irrelevant, misleading, or fail to accurately reflect reality
misleading or irrelevant comments or reports

Examples

The politician dismissed the criticism as mere unrepresentative swill spread by his opponents.
Much of what you read online these days amounts to unrepresentative swill rather than factual reporting.
Analysts warned that the statistics were unrepresentative swill and should not be taken at face value.

Synset Examples

His comments about the event were simply unrepresentative swill, failing to capture reality.
The survey results were just unrepresentative swill, not reflective of the real opinions.
Critics dismissed the report as unrepresentative swill, lacking credible sources.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nonsense - language or ideas that are foolish or absurd
  • Synonym: drivel - silly or senseless talk

Etymology

Formed from 'unrepresentative' (not accurately reflecting or typical) and 'swill' (worthless rubbish in figurative use).
