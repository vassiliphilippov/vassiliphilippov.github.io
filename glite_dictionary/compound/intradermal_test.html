intradermal test

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.trəˈdɝː.məl tɛst / (AmE), / ˌɪn.trəˈdɜː.məl tɛst / (BrE)
a medical test in which a small amount of a suspected allergen is injected into the skin to determine an allergic reaction
test where allergen is injected into skin
medical

Examples

The doctor recommended an intradermal test to identify the specific allergy causing his symptoms.
An intradermal test can sometimes detect allergies that do not show up with other skin tests.
Nurses are trained to administer the intradermal test safely and interpret its results.

Etymology

From Latin 'intra-' (within) + 'derma' (skin) + 'al' (relating to).
