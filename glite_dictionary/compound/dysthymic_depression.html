dysthymic depression

1. NOUN

/ dɪsˈθaɪmɪk dɪˈprɛʃən / (AmE), / dɪsˈθʌɪmɪk dɪˈprɛʃən / (BrE)
A mild but long-lasting form of depression characterized by a persistently low mood lasting for at least two years.
long-term mild depression
medical

Examples

After years of feeling persistently low, she was diagnosed with dysthymic depression by her psychiatrist.
People with dysthymic depression may find it hard to remember a time when they felt truly happy.
Treatment options for dysthymic depression include therapy and sometimes medication.

Synset Examples

I thought she had just been in a bad mood for thirty years, but the doctor called it dysthymia

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dysthymia - persistent mild depression
  • Antonym: major depressive disorder - severe depression with significant impairment

Etymology

From Greek ‘dys-’ (bad) + ‘thymos’ (spirit, mind), plus ‘depression’.
