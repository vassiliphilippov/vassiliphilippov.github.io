gruesome twosome

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡruːsəm ˈtuːsəm / (AmE), / ˈɡruːsəm ˈtuːsəm / (BrE)
A pair of people who are known together for causing trouble or engaging in mischievous or problematic behavior.
a troublesome or notorious pair

Examples

The gruesome twosome were at it again, playing practical jokes on their classmates.
Everyone avoided the gruesome twosome at the party because of their history of causing chaos.
Those two have built a reputation as the gruesome twosome in the office.

Synset Examples

The gruesome twosome plotted their next prank together.
Everyone knows they are the gruesome twosome of trouble.
Those two are the gruesome twosome of the neighborhood.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: dynamic duo - two people working well together, often in a positive sense
  • Synonym: partners in crime - two people who habitually get into trouble together
