hemorrhage of cash flow

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛmərɪdʒ əv kæʃ floʊ / (AmE), / ˈhɛmərɪdʒ əv kæʃ fləʊ / (BrE)
a rapid and excessive outflow of money from an organization, often threatening its financial stability
rapid loss of money from an organization

Examples

The company struggled to halt the hemorrhage of cash flow during the economic downturn.
Investors were deeply concerned by the ongoing hemorrhage of cash flow at the startup.
New management introduced cost-cutting measures to address the hemorrhage of cash flow.

Synset Examples

They needed to stop the hemorrhage of cash flow immediately.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bleeding cash - losing money quickly

Etymology

Figurative use of 'hemorrhage,' from Greek 'haimorrhagia' (loss of blood), applied to financial contexts.
