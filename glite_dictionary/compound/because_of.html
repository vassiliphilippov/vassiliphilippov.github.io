because of

1. ADP

/ bɪˈkʌz ʌv / (AmE), / bɪˈkɒz ɒv / (BrE)
used to give the reason for something; due to or on account of.
on account of; due to

Examples

Flights were canceled because of heavy snow in the city.
She decided to leave early because of a sudden headache.
Many businesses closed because of the economic downturn.

Synset Examples

She couldn't sleep because of the noise outside.
He was late for dinner because of traffic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: due to - caused by; as a result of
  • Synonym: on account of - because of; for the reason of
