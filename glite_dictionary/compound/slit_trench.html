slit trench

1. NOUN

/ slɪt trɛntʃ / (AmE), / slɪt trɛntʃ / (BrE)
a narrow excavation in the ground, typically used as a shelter for soldiers during battle
narrow battlefield shelter trench

Examples

The soldiers quickly dug a slit trench to protect themselves from enemy fire.
During heavy shelling, the unit took cover in a slit trench near the front line.
Maintenance of each slit trench was essential for the troops’ safety.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foxhole - a small pit dug for protection from enemy fire
