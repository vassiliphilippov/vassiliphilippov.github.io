double eagle

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʌbəl ˈiɡəl / (AmE), / ˈdʌbəl ˈiːɡl̩ / (BrE)
(in golf) a score of three strokes under par on a single hole, also called an albatross.
score of three under par on a hole (golf)

Examples

Scoring a double eagle is one of the rarest feats in professional golf.
She was thrilled when her third shot landed for a double eagle on the par-five.
Only a few players have ever made a double eagle in major tournaments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: albatross - score of three under par on a hole (golf); synonym for double eagle

2. NOUN

/ ˌdʌb.əl ˈiː.ɡəl / (AmE), / ˌdʌb.əl ˈiː.ɡəl / (BrE)
a former gold coin of the United States worth twenty dollars, minted chiefly in the 19th and early 20th centuries
former $20 US gold coin
american

Examples

The rare 1933 double eagle fetched a record price at auction.
Collectors prize the double eagle for its historical and monetary value.
The double eagle was discontinued after 1933, making it a sought-after coin among numismatists.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: eagle - a former gold coin of the United States worth ten dollars

Etymology

From the coin's value being double that of the eagle ($10 gold coin) issued by the U.S. Mint.
