Bergen-Belsen concentration camp

1. PROPN

/ ˈbɜrɡən ˈbɛlsən ˌkɑn.sənˈtreɪ.ʃən ˌkæmp / (AmE), / ˈbɜːɡən ˈbɛlsən ˌkɒn.sənˈtreɪ.ʃən ˌkæmp / (BrE)
A Nazi concentration camp for Jews established in northwestern Germany during World War II.
Nazi concentration camp in WWII Germany

Examples

Anne Frank died in the Bergen-Belsen concentration camp just weeks before it was liberated.
Many survivors of the Bergen-Belsen concentration camp later shared testimonies of the horrors they endured.
The British army liberated the Bergen-Belsen concentration camp in April 1945, revealing the extent of Nazi atrocities.

Etymology

From the villages of Bergen and Belsen in Lower Saxony, Germany, near which the camp was located.
