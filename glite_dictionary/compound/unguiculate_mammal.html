unguiculate mammal

1. NOUN

/ ʌnˈɡwɪkjələt ˈmæməl / (AmE), / ʌŋˈɡwɪkjʊlət ˈmaməl / (BrE)
a mammal with nails or claws, as opposed to hoofs
mammal with nails or claws

Examples

Sloths are an example of an unguiculate mammal because they possess prominent claws.
The distinction between unguiculate mammals and ungulates is fundamental in mammalian classification.
Primates, as unguiculate mammals, generally have flattened nails instead of claws.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ungulate - a mammal with hooves

Etymology

From Latin 'unguiculatus' (having claws or nails), from 'unguis' (nail, claw).
