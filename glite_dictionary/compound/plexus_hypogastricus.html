plexus hypogastricus

1. NOUN

/ ˈplɛksəs ˌhaɪpoʊɡæsˈtrɪkəs / (AmE), / ˈplɛksəs ˌhɪpəʊɡæsˈtrɪkəs / (BrE)
A network of nerves located in the pelvis that serves the pelvic organs.
nerve network in the pelvis
medical

Examples

The plexus hypogastricus plays a crucial role in regulating the functions of pelvic organs.
Damage to the plexus hypogastricus can lead to a variety of pelvic disorders.
Surgeons must be careful to avoid the plexus hypogastricus during certain pelvic procedures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pelvic plexus - A network of nerves in the pelvis, synonymous with plexus hypogastricus

Etymology

From Latin 'plexus' (network or braid) and 'hypogastricus' (relating to the lower abdomen).
