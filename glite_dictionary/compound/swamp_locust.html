swamp locust

1. NOUN

/ swɑmp ˈloʊkəst / (AmE), / swɒmp ˈləʊkəst / (BrE)
A species of honey locust tree found in swamps and bottomlands of the southern United States, known for its short oval pods and heavy dark wood.
honey locust tree of southern U.S. swamps

Examples

The wood from the swamp locust is prized for its strength and durability.
In the southern wetlands, you can often find the distinctive short pods of the swamp locust.
Botanists studied the swamp locust to learn more about native swamp ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: honey locust - a North American tree with spiny branches and fragrant flowers
