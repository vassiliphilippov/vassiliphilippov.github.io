country music

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌntri ˌmjuːzɪk / (AmE), / ˈkʌntri ˌmjuːzɪk / (BrE)
A genre of popular music with origins in the rural Southern United States, featuring simple melodies and lyrics, often played on stringed instruments such as guitars and fiddles.
genre of American folk-style music

Examples

She grew up listening to country music on the radio every morning.
The festival features several popular country music artists from Nashville.
He prefers country music to pop or rock for its storytelling lyrics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: folk music - traditional music of the people; passed orally from generation to generation

Etymology

From 'country' (referring to rural areas) and 'music'; term popularized in the 20th century to distinguish this style from urban styles.
