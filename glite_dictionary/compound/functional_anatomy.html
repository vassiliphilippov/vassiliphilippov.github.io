functional anatomy

1. NOUN

/ ˈfʌŋkʃənl əˈnætəmi / (AmE), / ˈfʌŋkʃənəl əˈnætəmi / (BrE)
the branch of anatomy that focuses on the relationship between anatomical structure and function
study of anatomy in relation to function
medical

Examples

The course on functional anatomy helped her understand how muscles create movement in the human body.
Researchers in functional anatomy study how the shape of bones relates to their role in locomotion.
Surgeons rely on knowledge of functional anatomy when planning complex operations.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: anatomy - the scientific study of the structure of living things
  • Degree Higher: physiology - the study of how living organisms function
