Colorado Plateau

1. PROPN

/ ˌkɑːləˌreɪdoʊ pləˈtoʊ / (AmE), / ˌkɒlərˌɑːdəʊ plæˈtəʊ / (BrE)
a large elevated region in the southwestern United States, situated south and west of the Rocky Mountains and known for its dramatic canyons, mesas, and the Grand Canyon
elevated region in the southwestern US

Examples

The Colorado Plateau is famous for its stunning red rock formations and vast canyons.
Many national parks, including Zion and Arches, are located on the Colorado Plateau.
Geologists often study the Colorado Plateau to understand the region's unique landforms and history.
