disassortative mating

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪsəˈsɔrtətɪv ˈmeɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌdɪsəˈsɔːtətɪv ˈmeɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
The mating of individuals with traits that are more dissimilar from each other than would be expected from random mating, often promoting genetic diversity.
mating between dissimilar individuals
medical

Examples

In some species, disassortative mating helps maintain genetic variation in the population.
Scientists observed disassortative mating patterns, where individuals chose partners with different immune system genes.
Unlike assortative mating, disassortative mating can increase the frequency of rare traits within a group.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: assortative mating - mating between individuals with similar traits

Etymology

From dis- (denoting negation) + assortative (from 'assort', to arrange) + mating.
