pile dwelling

1. NOUN

/ paɪl ˈdwɛlɪŋ / (AmE), / paɪl ˈdwɛlɪŋ / (BrE)
A house or building constructed on wooden or stone piles over or near a body of water, especially common in some prehistoric settlements.
dwelling built on piles over water

Examples

Archaeologists discovered the remains of a pile dwelling on the edge of the ancient lake.
Many prehistoric communities in Europe were known to live in pile dwellings for protection and access to water.
The museum features a full-scale replica of a traditional pile dwelling from the Bronze Age.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stilt house - a house raised on posts or stilts above the ground or water
