logical relation

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɑːdʒɪkəl rɪˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈlɒdʒɪkəl rɪˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
a relation between two or more propositions, such as implication, equivalence, or contradiction, that is determined by logic
relation between propositions in logic

Examples

The logical relation between these two statements is one of contradiction.
Understanding the logical relation of equivalence helps in constructing valid arguments.
If there is no logical relation between the claims, the conclusion does not follow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: implication - a logical relation where one proposition implies another
  • Synonym: contradiction - an opposition between two propositions such that both cannot be true

Etymology

From logic (from Greek logikē) + relation (from Latin relatio)
