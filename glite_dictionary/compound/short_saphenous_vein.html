short saphenous vein

1. NOUN

/ ʃɔrt səˈfiːnəs veɪn / (AmE), / ʃɔːt səˈfiːnəs veɪn / (BrE)
a vein running from the foot up the back of the leg to the knee, responsible for draining blood from the lateral side of the foot and leg
vein running up the back of the lower leg
medical

Examples

During the operation, the surgeon carefully identified the short saphenous vein.
Varicose veins commonly affect the short saphenous vein in some patients.
The short saphenous vein can be visualized by ultrasound for medical procedures.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: great saphenous vein - the largest vein in the leg running along the length of the lower limb

Etymology

From Latin 'saphena', meaning 'clearly visible'.
