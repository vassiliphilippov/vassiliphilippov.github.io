serial music

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪriəl ˈmjuːzɪk / (AmE), / ˈsɪəriəl ˈmjuːzɪk / (BrE)
A 20th-century style of music in which a fixed sequence of notes or elements is used as the structural basis for a composition.
music using ordered sequences of notes

Examples

The composer experimented with serial music to explore new tonal possibilities.
Many listeners find serial music challenging because of its abstract structures.
Some 20th-century works of serial music avoid traditional harmony and melody.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: twelve-tone music - music based on using all twelve notes in a chromatic scale in a fixed order

Etymology

From 'serial', relating to series, and 'music'; the method was pioneered by Arnold Schoenberg and others in the early 20th century.
