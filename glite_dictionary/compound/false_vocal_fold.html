false vocal fold

1. NOUN

/ fɔls ˈvoʊkəl foʊld / (AmE), / fɔːls ˈvəʊkəl fəʊld / (BrE)
either of the upper two folds of tissue in the larynx that are not directly involved in producing voice
upper laryngeal tissue, not for vocalization
medical

Examples

During surgery, the doctor was careful not to damage the false vocal fold.
Unlike the true vocal cords, the false vocal fold does not vibrate to produce sound.
Swelling in the false vocal fold can sometimes cause discomfort or a sensation of throat fullness.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: vocal cord - each of the folds of tissue in the larynx responsible for sound production
  • Antonym: true vocal fold - the lower pair of the vocal cords, which produce sound
