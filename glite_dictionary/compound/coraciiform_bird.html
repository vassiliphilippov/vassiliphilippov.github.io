coraciiform bird

1. NOUN

/ kɔːˈræs.i.ɪ.fɔːrm bɜrd / (AmE), / kɒˈræs.ɪ.ɪ.fɔːm bɜːd / (BrE)
a mainly short-legged, tree-dwelling nonpasserine bird that typically nests in holes, belonging to the order Coraciiformes
arboreal nonpasserine bird of the order Coraciiformes

Examples

Many coraciiform birds are known for their vibrant plumage and elaborate courtship displays.
The nest of a coraciiform bird is usually found in tree hollows or riverbanks.
Scientists conducted a field study on the feeding habits of several coraciiform birds in the rainforest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: roller - a brightly colored bird of the family Coraciidae
  • Synonym: kingfisher - a brightly colored bird of the family Alcedinidae

Etymology

From scientific Latin Coraciiformes, based on Latin coracius (a type of bird), from Greek korakias, related to korax (raven).
