silent majority

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪlənt məˈdʒɔrəti / (AmE), / ˈsaɪlənt məˈdʒɔːrəti / (BrE)
the large group of people in a country or group who do not publicly express their opinions, especially those believed to hold views different from those expressed by the loudest voices
large group that does not express opinions publicly

Examples

Many politicians claim to represent the silent majority rather than the most vocal activists.
The silent majority often goes unnoticed until an election brings their views to light.
Despite loud protests, the government believed the silent majority supported its decision.

Synset Examples

The silent majority finally spoke up.
Politicians often ignore the silent majority.
The silent majority supports the new policy.

Etymology

Popularized in the United States by President Richard Nixon in 1969.
