nuclear-powered submarine

1. NOUN

/ ˈnuːkliɚ ˌpaʊɚd ˌsʌbməˈriːn / (AmE), / ˈnjuːklɪə ˌpaʊəd ˌsʌbməˈriːn / (BrE)
A submarine that is propelled by nuclear power, allowing it to operate underwater for extended periods without surfacing.
submarine powered by nuclear energy

Examples

A nuclear-powered submarine can remain submerged for months without needing to refuel.
The navy commissioned a new nuclear-powered submarine equipped with advanced stealth technology.
Unlike diesel submarines, nuclear-powered submarines have virtually unlimited range under the sea.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: submarine - a vessel designed for underwater operation
