analytical geometry

1. NOUN

/ əˌnæl.ɪˈtɪk.əl dʒiˈɑː.mə.tri / (AmE), / əˌnæl.ɪˈtɪk.əl dʒiˈɒm.ɪ.tri / (BrE)
a branch of mathematics that uses algebraic methods and coordinates to study the properties and relations of geometric figures
geometry using algebra and coordinates

Examples

In analytical geometry, we use equations to represent lines and curves.
She excelled at analytical geometry because of her strong algebra skills.
The introduction of analytical geometry revolutionized mathematics in the seventeenth century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coordinate geometry - the study of geometry using a coordinate system

Etymology

From analysis (related to breaking down problems) and geometry (from Greek 'geo-' meaning earth and 'metron' meaning measure).
