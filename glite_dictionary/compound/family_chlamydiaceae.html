family Chlamydiaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli klæmˌɪdiˈeɪsiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli klæmˌɪdɪˈeɪsiː / (BrE)
A family of Gram-negative bacteria that are obligate parasites in warm-blooded vertebrates.
family of Gram-negative parasitic bacteria

Examples

The family Chlamydiaceae includes bacteria responsible for diseases in both humans and animals.
Researchers studied the genetic diversity within the family Chlamydiaceae to understand disease transmission.
Members of the family Chlamydiaceae are known for their unique intracellular lifestyle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Chlamydia - genus of bacteria in the family Chlamydiaceae

Etymology

From scientific Latin 'Chlamydiaceae', based on 'Chlamydia', the type genus, and '-aceae', a standard family suffix in taxonomy.
