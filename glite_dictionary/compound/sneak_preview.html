sneak preview

1. NOUN

/ sniːk priːˈvjuː / (AmE), / sniːk priːˈvjuː / (BrE)
an advance showing of a film, product, or event, often to test audience reactions before its general release
an advance showing to test reactions

Examples

The studio invited critics to a sneak preview of the highly anticipated movie.
We got tickets to a sneak preview of the new smartphone before its official launch.
Attendees were impressed by the sneak preview and shared their reactions on social media.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: preview - an advance showing or viewing of something

2. NOUN

/ snik ˈpriːvju / (AmE), / sniːk ˈpriːvjuː / (BrE)
an advance showing or viewing of something, especially a movie, before its official release
advance showing or viewing

Examples

We were invited to a sneak preview of the highly anticipated film.
The museum offered a sneak preview of its new exhibition to select donors.
Fans lined up early to catch a sneak preview of the next season's fashion line.

Synset Examples

We got a sneak preview of the new movie tonight.
