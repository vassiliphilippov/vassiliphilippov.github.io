to stay in character

1. VERB

/ steɪ ɪn ˈkæɹɪktɚ / (AmE), / steɪ ɪn ˈkærɪktə / (BrE)
to continue behaving or speaking as the character one is portraying, without reverting to one's real self
to keep acting as a character

Examples

Despite distractions during the performance, the actor managed to stay in character at all times.
She found it challenging to stay in character during the emotional scene.
Even when the director gave notes, he would stay in character and respond as his role.

Synset Examples

The actor stayed in character even off stage.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: break character - to cease acting as a character, usually unintentionally
