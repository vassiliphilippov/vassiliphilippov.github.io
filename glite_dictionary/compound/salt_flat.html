salt flat

1. NOUN

/ sɔlt flæt / (AmE), / sɒlt flæt / (BrE)
A level area of land covered with salt left after the evaporation of salt water.
level area covered with salt deposits

Examples

The Bonneville salt flat in Utah is famous for land speed record attempts.
Photographers are drawn to the stunning reflections on the salt flat after a rainfall.
Many migratory birds stop at the salt flats during their long journeys.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: salt pan - a natural basin or depression where salt collects after water evaporates
