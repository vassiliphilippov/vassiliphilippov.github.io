crowfoot family

1. NOUN

/ ˈkroʊˌfʊt ˈfæməli / (AmE), / ˈkrəʊˌfʊt ˈfæmɪli / (BrE)
a large family of flowering plants, Ranunculaceae, which includes buttercups and related species
plant family including buttercups

Examples

Many wildflowers in the meadow belong to the crowfoot family, including buttercups and anemones.
The crowfoot family contains many species that are valued for their ornamental flowers.
Some members of the crowfoot family are poisonous if ingested by livestock.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ranunculaceae - the botanical (Latin) name for the crowfoot family

Etymology

Named from the resemblance of some members’ leaves to a crow’s foot; 'crowfoot' from Old English 'crāwa' (crow) + 'fōt' (foot).
