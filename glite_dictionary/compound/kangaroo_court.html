kangaroo court

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæŋɡəˈru kɔrt / (AmE), / ˌkæŋɡəˈruː kɔːt / (BrE)
An unofficial court or tribunal that disregards standard legal procedures and delivers a predetermined or unjust verdict.
unofficial or sham court

Examples

Many saw the disciplinary hearing as a kangaroo court lacking fairness and due process.
The accused was convicted in a kangaroo court and denied the right to proper defense.
Accusations were decided by a kangaroo court rather than through legitimate legal proceedings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: show trial - a public trial where the verdict is predetermined
  • Antonym: court of law - a court that follows the legal system and official procedures

Etymology

Originates from 19th-century American slang, metaphorically likening the irregular 'leaps' of a kangaroo to irregular legal proceedings.

2. NOUN

/ ˌkæŋɡəˈru ˌkɔrt / (AmE), / ˌkæŋɡəˈruː ˌkɔːt / (BrE)
An unofficial tribunal or proceeding that ignores recognized standards of law or justice in order to reach a predetermined or biased outcome.
unofficial, unfair tribunal or trial

Examples

The protestors claimed that the disciplinary hearing was a kangaroo court with a foregone conclusion.
After the abrupt verdict, many believed they had witnessed a kangaroo court rather than a proper trial.
His case was decided so swiftly and unfairly that it resembled a kangaroo court.

Synset Examples

The accusations against him felt like a kangaroo court.
She refused to participate in what she deemed a kangaroo court.
Everyone knew the trial was a kangaroo court from the start.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: show trial - a trial staged for propaganda purposes, where the verdict is predetermined

Etymology

Originated in 19th-century America, possibly referring to the rapid, 'jumping' proceedings, or as a metaphor for deceptive or illegitimate judicial practices.
