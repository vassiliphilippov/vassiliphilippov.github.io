hooded skunk

1. NOUN

/ ˈhʊdɪd skʌŋk / (AmE), / ˈhʊdɪd skʌŋk / (BrE)
a species of skunk (Mephitis macroura) found in Mexico and the southernmost parts of the southwestern United States, known for the prominent white ruff or hood on its neck and shoulders
species of skunk with a distinctive white hood

Examples

We spotted a hooded skunk while hiking in southern Arizona last summer.
The distinctive white fur on a hooded skunk makes it easy to identify at night.
Researchers are studying the habitat range of the hooded skunk across Mexico.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skunk - a small North American mammal known for its ability to spray a foul-smelling liquid
