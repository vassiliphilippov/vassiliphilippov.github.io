megawatt hour

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛɡəˌwɑt ˈaʊɚ / (AmE), / ˌmɛɡəwɒt ˈaʊə / (BrE)
a unit of energy equal to one million watts of power used for one hour
unit of energy (one million watts for one hour)

Examples

The power plant generates over 50,000 megawatt hours of electricity annually.
The contract specified an energy price per megawatt hour delivered to the grid.
They reported a record-high consumption exceeding one million megawatt hours last month.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: kilowatt hour - a unit of energy equal to one thousand watts of power used for one hour

Etymology

From 'mega-' (meaning million), 'watt' (after James Watt) + 'hour'.
