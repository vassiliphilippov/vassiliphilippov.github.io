gluteus maximus

1. NOUN

/ ɡluːˌtiː.əs ˈmæk.sɪ.məs / (AmE), / ɡluːˌtɪː.əs ˈmæk.sɪ.məs / (BrE)
the largest and outermost muscle of the buttocks, responsible for movement of the hip and thigh
large muscle of the buttocks
medical

Examples

The gluteus maximus is responsible for the shape of the buttocks and powerful hip movements.
In strength training, squats are excellent for working the gluteus maximus.
He experienced pain in his gluteus maximus after running a marathon.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: gluteus medius - the middle of the three gluteal muscles
  • Degree Lower: gluteus minimus - the smallest of the three gluteal muscles

Etymology

From Latin 'gluteus' (of the buttocks) and 'maximus' (greatest, largest).
