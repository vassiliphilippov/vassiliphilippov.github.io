compression ratio

1. NOUN

/ kəmˈprɛʃən ˈreɪʃioʊ / (AmE), / kəmˈprɛʃən ˈreɪʃɪəʊ / (BrE)
the ratio of the final (discharge) pressure to the initial (suction) pressure in a gas compressor or engine cylinder
ratio of final to initial pressure in a compressor

Examples

The engine's compression ratio determines its efficiency and power output.
A higher compression ratio can lead to more fuel-efficient performance in modern cars.
Engineers adjusted the compression ratio to optimize the compressor's performance.

Synset Examples

High flow, low compression ratio applications are best served by axial flow compressors.

Etymology

From 'compression' + 'ratio', referring to how much a volume is reduced by pressure.
