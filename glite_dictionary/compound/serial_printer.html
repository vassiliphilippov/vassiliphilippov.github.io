serial printer

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪriəl ˈprɪntər / (AmE), / ˈsɪəriəl ˈprɪntə / (BrE)
A type of printer that prints one character at a time, typically using a serial interface.
printer that prints one character at a time

Examples

The old office computer was attached to a serial printer, which printed reports slowly.
A serial printer can be connected to computers using a serial port interface.
Engineers often used a serial printer to generate hard copies of data logs in the 1980s.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dot matrix printer - a printer that creates images with small dots

Etymology

From 'serial' (relating to transmitting data in sequence) and 'printer'.
