bound morpheme

1. NOUN

/ baʊnd ˈmɔrˌfim / (AmE), / baʊnd ˈmɔːfiːm / (BrE)
a morpheme that cannot stand alone and only appears as part of a larger word or structure, such as prefixes, suffixes, or inflectional endings
a morpheme that must attach to another

Examples

The plural '-s' in 'dogs' is a classic example of a bound morpheme.
Prefixes and suffixes are typically types of bound morphemes in English.
A bound morpheme never appears as an independent word.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: free morpheme - a morpheme that can stand alone as a word

Etymology

Formed from 'bound', meaning constrained, and 'morpheme', from French 'morphème' (unit of meaning).
