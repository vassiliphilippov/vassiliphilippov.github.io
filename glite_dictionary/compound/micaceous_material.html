micaceous material

1. NOUN

/ maɪˈkeɪʃəs məˈtɪriəl / (AmE), / mɪˈkeɪʃəs məˈtɪəriəl / (BrE)
A material characterized by a flaky or layered texture similar to that of mica.
material with a flaky or layered texture

Examples

The geologist examined the micaceous material under a microscope to study its distinct layers.
Potters often incorporate micaceous material into their clay mixtures for added texture and strength.
She found that the micaceous material sparkled slightly when caught by sunlight.

Synset Examples

The artist preferred micaceous materials for texture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mica - a shiny silicate mineral with a layered structure

Etymology

From 'micaceous', derived from Latin 'mica' (a crumb, grain); referring to similarity to mica mineral.
