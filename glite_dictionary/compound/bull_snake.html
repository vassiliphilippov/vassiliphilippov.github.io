bull snake

1. NOUN

/ bʊl sneɪk / (AmE), / bʊl sneɪk / (BrE)
a large, harmless North American snake that feeds on rodents and burrows underground; a subspecies of gopher snake
large harmless North American burrowing snake

Examples

A bull snake slithered across the dusty path as we hiked through the grasslands.
Farmers appreciate the presence of bull snakes in their fields because they eat rodents.
Although a bull snake can look intimidating, it is not venomous and is generally harmless to humans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gopher snake - a large, nonvenomous North American snake, of which the bull snake is a subspecies
