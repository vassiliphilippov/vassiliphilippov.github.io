cerebral thrombosis

1. NOUN

/ səˈribɹəl θrɑmˈboʊsɪs / (AmE), / sɪˈriː.brəl θrɒmˈbəʊ.sɪs / (BrE)
A blood clot that forms in a cerebral artery or vein, which can obstruct blood flow to the brain and may cause a stroke.
blood clot in a brain vessel
medical

Examples

She suffered a cerebral thrombosis that left her partially paralyzed on one side.
Prompt treatment after a cerebral thrombosis can significantly improve recovery chances.
The MRI scan revealed a cerebral thrombosis blocking one of his major arteries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stroke - sudden loss of brain function due to interrupted blood supply
  • Synonym: embolism - obstruction of a blood vessel by a clot or foreign material

Etymology

From Latin 'cerebrum' (brain) and Greek 'thrombosis' (clotting of blood).
