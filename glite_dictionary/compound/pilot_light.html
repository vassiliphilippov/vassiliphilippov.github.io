pilot light

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪlət laɪt / (AmE), / ˈpaɪlət laɪt / (BrE)
a small gas burner that remains continuously ignited to light a larger burner when needed
small flame that ignites a larger gas burner

Examples

After the storm, the pilot light on our stove went out and needed to be relit.
The technician checked the pilot light before servicing the water heater.
If the furnace isn't working, it could be due to an extinguished pilot light.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪlət laɪt / (AmE), / ˈpaɪlət laɪt / (BrE)
a small light on a device or control panel that indicates whether power is on or a motor is operating
indicator light showing power is on

Examples

The pilot light on the front panel glowed steadily to show the machine was running.
If the pilot light goes out, you may not realize the appliance is still off.
She checked the pilot light before starting the motor to make sure everything was functioning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indicator light - a light that shows whether a machine or system is working

Etymology

From 'pilot' (guide) and 'light', indicating its role as a guiding signal.
