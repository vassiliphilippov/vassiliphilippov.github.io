anchor chain

1. NOUN

/ ˈæŋ.kər tʃeɪn / (AmE), / ˈaŋ.kə tʃeɪn / (BrE)
a heavy chain or strong rope used to connect an anchor to a vessel
chain or rope attaching anchor to vessel

Examples

The crew checked the anchor chain before lowering the anchor into the water.
A rusty anchor chain can be a major safety hazard for any vessel.
As the storm approached, they secured extra lengths of anchor chain to keep the boat stable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anchor - a heavy object attached to a rope or chain and used to moor a vessel
