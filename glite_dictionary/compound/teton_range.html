Teton Range

1. PROPN

/ ˈtiːtoʊn reɪndʒ / (AmE), / ˈtiːtɒn reɪndʒ / (BrE)
a mountain range in northwest Wyoming, USA, noted for its dramatic peaks and containing the Grand Teton
mountain range in Wyoming, USA

Examples

The Teton Range rises sharply above the Jackson Hole valley, offering breathtaking scenery.
Grand Teton National Park preserves much of the Teton Range and its surrounding wilderness.
Many hikers visit Wyoming each year to explore trails in the Teton Range.

Etymology

Named after the French word 'téton' meaning 'nipple' or 'teat', given by early French trappers.
