U.S. House of Representatives

1. PROPN

/ ju ɛs ˈhaʊs əv ˌrɛprɪˌzɛntəˈtɪvz / (AmE), / juː ɛs ˈhaʊs əv ˌrɛprɪˌzɛntətɪvz / (BrE)
the lower legislative chamber of the United States Congress, consisting of elected representatives from each state, responsible for making and passing federal laws
lower chamber of the U.S. Congress
american

Examples

After the bill passed the U.S. House of Representatives, it was sent to the Senate for approval.
She was elected to the U.S. House of Representatives at the age of thirty-two.
Debates in the U.S. House of Representatives are broadcast live on television and online.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Senate - the upper legislative chamber of the United States Congress
  • Synonym: Congress - the legislative branch of the U.S. government, composed of the House of Representatives and the Senate
