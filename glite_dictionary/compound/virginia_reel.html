Virginia reel

1. NOUN

/ vərˈdʒɪn.jə riːl / (AmE), / vəˈdʒɪn.i.ə riːl / (BrE)
An American country dance in which couples form two facing lines and perform a series of lively figures.
American country dance with two facing lines

Examples

At the community festival, everyone joined hands for a lively Virginia reel in the barn.
Learning the steps of the Virginia reel was a highlight of dance class.
The band played traditional tunes as the dancers performed the Virginia reel with enthusiasm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contra dance - a folk dance with lines of couples

Etymology

Named after the state of Virginia, United States, where the dance was popularized; 'reel' from the Scottish folk dance.
