agitated depression

1. NOUN

/ ˈædʒɪˌteɪtəd dɪˈprɛʃən / (AmE), / ˈædʒɪˌteɪtɪd dɪˈprɛʃən / (BrE)
a form of clinical depression characterized by irritability, restlessness, and increased motor activity
a type of depression marked by restlessness
medical

Examples

During her bout of agitated depression, she was constantly pacing the room and snapping at friends.
Doctors noted that agitated depression often involves both sadness and noticeable physical restlessness.
His symptoms of agitated depression included irritability, anxiety, and an inability to sit still.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: major depressive disorder - a mood disorder causing a persistent feeling of sadness and loss of interest
  • Antonym: mania - a state of abnormally elevated arousal, affect, and energy level
