gastrointestinal system

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡæstroʊɪnˌtɛstɪˈnuəl ˈsɪstəm / (AmE), / ˌɡæstrəʊɪnˌtɛstɪˈnjuːəl ˈsɪstəm / (BrE)
the organ system in the body responsible for the digestion and absorption of food, consisting mainly of the stomach and intestines
organ system for digestion and absorption
medical

Examples

Damage to the gastrointestinal system can cause serious complications with digestion and nutrient absorption.
Doctors studied the effects of the medication on the gastrointestinal system.
The gastrointestinal system includes organs such as the stomach, intestines, and esophagus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: digestive system - the organ system responsible for breaking down food and absorbing nutrients

Etymology

From 'gastro-' (relating to the stomach) and 'intestinal' (relating to the intestine), both from Greek and Latin roots.
