fruit of the union

1. NOUN

/ fruːt əv ðə ˈjuːniən / (AmE), / fruːt əv ðə ˈjuːniən / (BrE)
a child or children produced as the result of a union or relationship; the outcome of a partnership, especially referring to offspring
children resulting from a relationship

Examples

After years of waiting, the fruit of the union finally arrived to the joy of both families.
The couple was grateful for the fruit of the union that brought new purpose to their lives.
Photos of the fruit of the union were proudly displayed on the living room mantel.

Synset Examples

The fruit of the union was a beautiful daughter.
The fruit of their union has brought them much joy.
They celebrated the fruit of their union with a family gathering.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: offspring - a person's child or children
  • Synonym: progeny - descendants or the next generation

Etymology

Figurative use of 'fruit' meaning 'result' or 'product', from Old French and Latin *fructus* (enjoyment, produce).
