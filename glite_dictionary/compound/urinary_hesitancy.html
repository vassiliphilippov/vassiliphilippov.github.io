urinary hesitancy

1. NOUN

/ ˈjʊrɪˌnɛri həˈsɪtənsi / (AmE), / ˈjʊərɪnəri hɪˈsɪtənsi / (BrE)
difficulty or delay in starting the flow of urine, often due to obstruction or other medical conditions
difficulty in starting urination
medical

Examples

The patient reported urinary hesitancy after starting the new medication.
Older men often experience urinary hesitancy due to prostate enlargement.
Stress can sometimes cause urinary hesitancy, even in otherwise healthy individuals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: urinary retention - inability to completely or partially empty the bladder
  • Antonym: incontinence - inability to control urination
