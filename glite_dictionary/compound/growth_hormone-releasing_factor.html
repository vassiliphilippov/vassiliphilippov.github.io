growth hormone-releasing factor

1. NOUN

/ ɡroʊθ ˌhɔːrmoʊn rɪˈlisɪŋ ˈfæktər / (AmE), / ɡrəʊθ ˌhɔːməʊn rɪˈliːsɪŋ ˈfæktə / (BrE)
a hormone produced in the hypothalamus that stimulates the release of growth hormone from the anterior pituitary gland
hormone stimulating growth hormone release
medical

Examples

The hypothalamus secretes growth hormone-releasing factor to regulate growth in children.
A deficiency in growth hormone-releasing factor can lead to reduced growth hormone levels.
Scientists studied the effects of synthetic growth hormone-releasing factor on pituitary function.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: growth hormone - a hormone that stimulates growth, cell reproduction, and regeneration
