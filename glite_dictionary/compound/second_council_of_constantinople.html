Second Council of Constantinople

1. PROPN

/ ˈsɛkənd ˈkaʊnsəl əv ˌkɑnstænˈtɪnəˌnoʊpəl / (AmE), / ˈsɛkənd ˈkaʊnsəl əv ˌkɒnstænˈtɪnəˌnəʊpəl / (BrE)
the fifth ecumenical council of the Christian Church, held in 553, which condemned certain teachings as heresy, including those of Origen
fifth ecumenical council, 553 AD

Examples

The Second Council of Constantinople played a crucial role in shaping Christian doctrine during the 6th century.
Many theological disputes were addressed at the Second Council of Constantinople in 553.
Origen's writings were condemned as heretical by the Second Council of Constantinople.
