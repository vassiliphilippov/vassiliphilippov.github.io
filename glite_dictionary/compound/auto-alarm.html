auto-alarm

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔːtoʊ əˌlɑrm / (AmE), / ˈɔːtəʊ əˌlɑːm / (BrE)
a radio receiver on ships that automatically sounds an alarm bell when a distress signal is received.
automatic maritime distress signal receiver

Examples

The ship's auto-alarm was triggered when it received the SOS radio signal late at night.
Every vessel is required to have an auto-alarm for detecting distress calls at sea.
During the routine inspection, the captain checked the auto-alarm to ensure it was functioning properly.

Etymology

From 'auto-', meaning automatic, and 'alarm', from French 'alarme'.
