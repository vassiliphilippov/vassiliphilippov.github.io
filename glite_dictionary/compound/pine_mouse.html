pine mouse

1. NOUN

/ paɪn maʊs / (AmE), / paɪn maʊs / (BrE)
A small, short-tailed vole with glossy fur, native to the eastern United States, known for burrowing.
small burrowing vole of eastern US

Examples

The pine mouse is commonly found in deciduous forests and feeds on roots and tubers.
After weeks of research, the scientist finally spotted a pine mouse near its burrow.
Gardeners sometimes struggle with damage from the pine mouse eating underground plant roots.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vole - a small burrowing rodent related to mice
