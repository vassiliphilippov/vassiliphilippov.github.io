to reinvest dividend

1. VERB

/ ˌriːɪnˈvɛst ˈdɪvəˌdɛnd / (AmE), / ˌriːɪnˈvɛst ˈdɪvɪˌdɛnd / (BrE)
To use dividends received from an investment to buy more shares or units of the same or another investment, rather than taking the dividends as cash.
use dividends to buy more investments

Examples

Many investors choose to reinvest dividend payments to take advantage of compound growth over time.
Instead of cashing out, she decided to reinvest dividend income automatically through a dividend reinvestment plan.
He always prefers to reinvest dividend earnings back into his portfolio rather than spending them.

Synset Examples

He reinvested his dividend for better returns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dividend - sum of money paid regularly by a company to its shareholders
