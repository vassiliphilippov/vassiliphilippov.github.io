genetic profiling

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˈnɛtɪk ˈproʊfaɪlɪŋ / (AmE), / dʒɪˈnɛtɪk ˈprəʊfaɪlɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of analyzing a person's DNA from tissue or body fluid samples to identify an individual or determine genetic relationships
analysis of DNA for identification
medical

Examples

The police used genetic profiling to link the suspect to the crime scene.
Advances in genetic profiling have revolutionized forensic science and criminal investigations.
Privacy advocates are concerned about the use and storage of information obtained through genetic profiling.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: DNA fingerprinting - the identification of individuals by analysis of DNA sequences
