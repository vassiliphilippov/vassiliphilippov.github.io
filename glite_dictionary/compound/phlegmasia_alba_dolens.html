phlegmasia alba dolens

1. NOUN

/ flɛɡˈmeɪziə ˈæl.bə ˈdoʊ.lɛnz / (AmE), / flɛɡˈmeɪziə ˈal.bə ˈdəʊ.lɛnz / (BrE)
a painful condition characterized by thrombosis of the femoral vein in the leg, often occurring after childbirth, leading to swelling and whiteness of the limb
painful femoral vein thrombosis after childbirth
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed her sudden leg swelling as phlegmasia alba dolens following childbirth.
Prompt recognition and treatment of phlegmasia alba dolens are crucial to prevent complications.
His left leg became swollen and pale due to phlegmasia alba dolens.

Etymology

From Latin and Greek: 'phlegmasia' (inflammation), 'alba' (white), 'dolens' (painful).
