too abstract

1. ADJ

/ tu ˈæbstrækt / (AmE), / tuː ˈabstrakt / (BrE)
(of ideas or explanations) excessively abstract; difficult to understand or grasp because of lacking concrete details.
so abstract as to be hard to understand

Examples

The professor's lecture was too abstract for most students to follow easily.
I find modern art too abstract to appreciate fully.
Her philosophy essay was too abstract, making her argument hard to follow.

Synset Examples

His explanation was too abstract for me to understand.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abstract - existing in thought or as an idea but not having a physical reality
  • Antonym: concrete - existing in a material or physical form; not abstract
