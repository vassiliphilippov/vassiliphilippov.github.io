limber pine

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪmbər paɪn / (AmE), / ˈlɪmbə paɪn / (BrE)
A North American pine tree (Pinus flexilis) with long needles, flexible branches, and dark-grey furrowed bark, found in mountainous regions in the west of the continent.
western North American pine tree

Examples

The limber pine thrives in harsh, rocky soils at high elevations.
Researchers studied how limber pine forests adapt to changing climates.
We spotted a centuries-old limber pine on our hike through the mountain pass.
