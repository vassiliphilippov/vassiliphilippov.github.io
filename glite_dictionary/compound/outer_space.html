outer space

1. NOUN

/ ˈaʊtɚ speɪs / (AmE), / ˈaʊtə speɪs / (BrE)
the physical universe beyond Earth's atmosphere, especially the region where there is very little air and objects such as stars, planets, and satellites are found
region beyond Earth's atmosphere

Examples

Many scientists dream of traveling to outer space to explore other planets.
The satellite orbited quietly in outer space, transmitting data back to Earth.
Movies often depict outer space as a place full of mystery and danger.

Synset Examples

the astronauts walked in outer space without a tether
the first major milestone in space exploration was in 1957, when the USSR's Sputnik 1 orbited the Earth

Related Terms

  • Synonym: space - the expanse beyond Earth's atmosphere
