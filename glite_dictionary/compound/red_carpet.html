red carpet

1. NOUN

/ rɛd ˈkɑrpət / (AmE), / rɛd ˈkɑːpɪt / (BrE)
a long, narrow strip of red carpet, especially one laid down for an important visitor to walk on at a ceremonial event
strip of red carpet for dignitaries

Examples

The celebrities walked down the red carpet before entering the theater.
The organizers laid out a red carpet to greet the foreign delegates.
Reporters lined the red carpet hoping for a glimpse of the famous guests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: welcome mat - a mat placed in front of a door to welcome visitors

2. NOUN

/ rɛd ˈkɑːrpɪt / (AmE), / rɛd ˈkɑːpɪt / (BrE)
special attention or hospitality given to an important guest; the treatment of someone as an honored visitor
special treatment or hospitality

Examples

The actress received the red carpet treatment at the film premiere.
We rolled out the red carpet for our overseas guests.
He was given the red carpet welcome wherever he went.

Synset Examples

the maitre d' gave them the red-carpet treatment

Related Terms

  • Synonym: VIP treatment - special privileges or attention for important people

Etymology

Refers to the literal practice of rolling out a red carpet for honored guests, a tradition dating back to at least the 19th century.

The organization rolled out the red carpet for its visiting dignitaries.
