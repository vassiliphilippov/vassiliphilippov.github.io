leaf bud

1. NOUN

/ lif bʌd / (AmE), / liːf bʌd / (BrE)
a bud on a plant from which leaves, but not flowers, develop
a plant bud that produces leaves

Examples

In early spring, you can see the leaf buds beginning to swell on the branches.
The gardener pointed out the difference between a leaf bud and a flower bud.
After a cold winter, the tree's leaf buds burst open, covering the branches in green.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: flower bud - a bud from which a flower develops
