Middle Irish language

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɪdəl ˈaɪrɪʃ ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌmɪdəl ˈaɪərɪʃ ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
The stage of the Irish Gaelic language spoken and written from approximately 1100 to 1500.
historical stage of Irish Gaelic (1100–1500)

Examples

The poem was originally composed in Middle Irish language during the twelfth century.
Scholars study texts in the Middle Irish language to learn about medieval Ireland.
Her research focuses on how the Middle Irish language evolved into modern Irish.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Old Irish language - earlier stage of Irish Gaelic, circa 600–900
  • Degree Higher: Modern Irish language - later stage of Irish Gaelic, from the 17th century onwards

Etymology

From 'Middle' (denoting the intermediate period) + 'Irish' (referring to Ireland) + 'language'.
