gum kino

1. NOUN

/ ɡʌm ˈkiːnoʊ / (AmE), / ɡʌm ˈkiːnəʊ / (BrE)
A reddish gum obtained from certain tropical trees, used as an astringent and in tanning leather.
reddish gum from tropical trees, used in tanning

Examples

The tanner used gum kino to prepare the leather for dyeing.
In traditional medicine, gum kino was valued for its astringent properties.
They imported barrels of gum kino from India for use in the factory.

Etymology

From Hindi 'kīno', the native name for the tree yielding the gum.
