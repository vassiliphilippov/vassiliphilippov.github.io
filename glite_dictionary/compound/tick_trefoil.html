tick trefoil

1. NOUN

/ tɪk ˈtriːfɔɪl / (AmE), / tɪk ˈtriːfɔɪl / (BrE)
Any of various plants of the genus Desmodium, native to tropical and subtropical regions, characterized by trifoliate leaves and sticky segmented pods.
plant with trifoliate leaves, sticky pods

Examples

The tick trefoil clings to clothing with its barbed seed pods during hikes.
We found several varieties of tick trefoil growing along the forest edge.
Gardeners often remove tick trefoil because its sticky pods stick to pets and people.

Etymology

Named for its sticky pods (like 'ticks') and trefoil leaves (from Latin 'trifolium', meaning three-leaved).
