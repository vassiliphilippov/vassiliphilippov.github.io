sulphur bacteria

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌlfər bækˈtɪəriə / (AmE), / ˈsʌlfə bækˈtɪəriə / (BrE)
Any bacteria of the genus Thiobacillus or other bacteria that oxidize or reduce sulfur compounds as part of their metabolism.
bacteria involved in sulfur metabolism

Examples

Certain sulphur bacteria can convert hydrogen sulfide into sulfur in natural environments.
Scientists discovered a new species of sulphur bacteria thriving in the hot springs.
Sulphur bacteria are important for their role in the global sulfur cycle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Thiobacillus - A genus of bacteria that oxidize sulfur compounds

Etymology

From sulfur (British spelling: sulphur) and modern Latin bacterium, reflecting their role in sulfur metabolism.
