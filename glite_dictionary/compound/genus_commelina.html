genus Commelina

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌkɒ.məˈlaɪ.nə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌkɒ.məˈliː.nə / (BrE)
A genus of herbaceous plants in the family Commelinaceae, including the dayflower and widow's tears, known for their branching or creeping habit.
a plant genus including dayflower

Examples

The genus Commelina is well known for its brightly colored dayflowers that bloom each morning.
Botanists have identified dozens of species within the genus Commelina across tropical and subtropical regions.
Gardeners sometimes cultivate plants from the genus Commelina for their attractive blue flowers.

Etymology

From Latin *Commelina*, named after Dutch botanists Jan and Caspar Commelijn.
