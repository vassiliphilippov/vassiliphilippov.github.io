lazy Susan

1. NOUN

/ ˈleɪzi ˈsuːzən / (AmE), / ˈleɪzi ˈsuːzən / (BrE)
a revolving tray placed on a table, used for easily sharing food or condiments among diners
revolving tray for serving food

Examples

We placed the dips on the lazy Susan so everyone could reach them easily.
Her grandmother always kept jams and honey on a lazy Susan in the middle of the table.
The restaurant's large round table featured a glass lazy Susan for sharing dishes.

Etymology

American English, attested early 20th century; origin of 'Susan' in the phrase is unclear.
