powder metallurgy

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaʊdɚ ˌmɛtəlˌɜrdʒi / (AmE), / ˈpaʊdə ˌmɛtələːdʒi / (BrE)
A branch of metallurgy that deals with producing solid metal objects from powdered metals by compressing and heating them without melting.
production of metals from powder by compaction and heating

Examples

Many car components are manufactured using powder metallurgy techniques to achieve complex shapes and precise tolerances.
The advantages of powder metallurgy include reduced waste and the possibility of creating unique alloy compositions.
She is conducting research on new materials for powder metallurgy applications in aerospace engineering.

Etymology

From 'powder' (fine particles) and 'metallurgy' (from Greek metallourgos, worker in metal).
