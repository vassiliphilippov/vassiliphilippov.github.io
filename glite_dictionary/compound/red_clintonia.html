red Clintonia

1. NOUN

/ rɛd klɪnˈtoʊniə / (AmE), / rɛd klɪnˈtəʊniə / (BrE)
A plant with nearly leafless stalks topped by clusters of red or reddish lavender flowers, native to regions from California to Oregon.
Northwestern U.S. plant with red or lavender flowers

Examples

We saw a red Clintonia blooming along the hiking trail in northern California.
The red Clintonia is easily identified by its striking stalk of reddish flowers.
Gardeners in Oregon often admire the unique appearance of red Clintonia in the wild.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bluebead lily - A related plant species with blue berries, also known as Clintonia

Etymology

Named after DeWitt Clinton, American politician and naturalist.
