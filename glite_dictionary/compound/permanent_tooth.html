permanent tooth

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɝːmənənt tuθ / (AmE), / ˈpɜːmənənt tuːθ / (BrE)
any of the 32 adult teeth that replace baby (deciduous) teeth and are meant to last for most of a person's life
adult tooth that replaces a baby tooth
medical

Examples

The dentist explained that a permanent tooth had erupted where the baby tooth had fallen out.
Children typically get their first permanent tooth around the age of six.
If a permanent tooth is lost, it will not grow back like a baby tooth.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: deciduous tooth - a baby tooth that is eventually replaced by a permanent tooth
