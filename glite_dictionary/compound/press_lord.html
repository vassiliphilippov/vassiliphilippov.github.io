press lord

1. NOUN

/ prɛs lɔrd / (AmE), / prɛs lɔːd / (BrE)
A person who owns or controls a large and influential newspaper or group of newspapers.
powerful newspaper proprietor

Examples

He was often referred to as a press lord due to his extensive control over several national newspapers.
Several press lords influenced public opinion during the early 20th century.
The rise of digital media has reduced the power once held by the traditional press lord.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: media mogul - a powerful or influential person in the media industry
