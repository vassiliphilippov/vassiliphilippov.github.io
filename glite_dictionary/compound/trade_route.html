trade route

1. NOUN

/ treɪd ruːt / (AmE), / treɪd ruːt / (BrE)
a regularly used route, especially at sea, followed by commercial vessels carrying goods
regularly used route for commercial shipping

Examples

Ancient civilizations thrived along the trade route that connected Asia and Europe.
Many pirates attacked ships sailing on the main trade route through the Caribbean.
Modern shipping companies monitor weather patterns to avoid delays along their busiest trade routes.

2. NOUN

/ treɪd ruːt / (AmE), / treɪd ruːt / (BrE)
A route regularly used by traders for the transport and exchange of goods, especially across regions or countries.
regular path used by traders

Examples

The ancient trade route connected the Mediterranean with Asia, bringing silk and spices to Europe.
Merchants traveled for months along the trade route through the Sahara Desert.
The decline of the city began when the main trade route shifted to the sea.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silk road - historic network of trade routes connecting East Asia and Europe

3. NOUN

/ treɪd ruːt / (AmE), / treɪd ruːt / (BrE)
a route regularly used for the transport and exchange of goods by merchants or traders
pathway for commercial transport

Examples

The ancient city thrived because it was situated along a major trade route.
Disruption of the sea trade route caused significant delays in deliveries.
Many explorers searched for new trade routes to increase wealth and influence.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shipping lane - a regular route used by ships for commercial transport
