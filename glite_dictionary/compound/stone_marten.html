stone marten

1. NOUN

/ stoʊn ˈmɑːrtn̩ / (AmE), / stəʊn ˈmɑːtn̩ / (BrE)
A Eurasian marten (Martes foina) with a brown coat and a pale breast and throat, native to Europe and parts of Asia.
Eurasian marten with brown coat and pale breast

Examples

The stone marten is often seen near human settlements in central Europe.
Unlike the pine marten, the stone marten has a distinctive pale throat patch.
Farmers sometimes consider the stone marten a pest because it nests in barns and attics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beech marten - another name for the stone marten, especially in some regions
  • Degree Higher: pine marten - a different marten species with a darker coat
