jackfruit tree

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒækˌfruːt triː / (AmE), / ˈdʒækˌfruːt triː / (BrE)
a tropical Asian tree (Artocarpus heterophyllus) cultivated for its very large, edible fruit and seeds
tree yielding large edible fruit

Examples

The jackfruit tree can grow up to 20 meters tall in its native environment.
Farmers rely on the jackfruit tree for both its nutritious fruit and timber.
A mature jackfruit tree can produce hundreds of fruits in a single season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jackfruit - the large, edible fruit produced by the jackfruit tree

Etymology

From 'jackfruit', ultimately from Portuguese 'jaca', from Malayalam 'chakka'; combined with 'tree'.
