John the Baptist

1. PROPN

/ dʒɑn ðə ˈbæptɪst / (AmE), / dʒɒn ðə ˈbæptɪst / (BrE)
(in the New Testament) a Jewish preacher regarded as the forerunner of Jesus, known for baptizing Jesus and later being executed by Herod.
New Testament preacher; forerunner of Jesus

Examples

Many artists have painted scenes featuring John the Baptist baptizing Jesus in the Jordan River.
John the Baptist is often depicted wearing simple clothes made of camel's hair in religious art.
The story of John the Baptist is frequently referenced in sermons about faith and sacrifice.

2. PROPN

/ dʒɑn ðə bæp.tɪst / (AmE), / dʒɒn ðə ˈbæp.tɪst / (BrE)
A biblical prophet who baptized Jesus and preached repentance in the wilderness.
biblical prophet who baptized Jesus

Examples

Many artists have painted scenes depicting John the Baptist baptizing Jesus in the Jordan River.
In his sermon, the pastor spoke about the humility of John the Baptist.
Some people fast during the feast of John the Baptist in June.

Synset Examples

He was known for his wisdom, much like John the Baptist.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Jesus Christ - central figure of Christianity; baptized by John the Baptist

Etymology

From John (from Hebrew Yohanan, 'God is gracious') and 'the Baptist', denoting his role as baptizer in the New Testament.
