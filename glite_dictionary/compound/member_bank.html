member bank

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛmbɚ bæŋk / (AmE), / ˈmɛmbə bæŋk / (BrE)
A bank that is a member of the Federal Reserve System in the United States.
bank in Federal Reserve System

Examples

The Federal Reserve regulates every member bank to ensure compliance with monetary policies.
Any member bank can borrow funds from the Federal Reserve's discount window.
A member bank must meet specific reserve requirements imposed by law.
