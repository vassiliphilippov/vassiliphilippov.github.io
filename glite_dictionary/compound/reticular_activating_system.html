reticular activating system

1. NOUN

/ rəˈtɪkjələr ˈæktɪˌveɪtɪŋ ˈsɪstəm / (AmE), / rɪˈtɪkjʊlə ˈæktɪveɪtɪŋ ˈsɪstəm / (BrE)
the part of the brainstem's reticular formation that regulates alertness and arousal in the central nervous system
network in brainstem regulating alertness and arousal
medical

Examples

Damage to the reticular activating system can result in severe disturbances of consciousness.
The reticular activating system plays a key role in maintaining wakefulness throughout the day.
Researchers are studying how the reticular activating system contributes to attention and sleep cycles.

Etymology

From Latin 'reticulatus' (net-like) + 'activating' (causing action) + 'system'.
