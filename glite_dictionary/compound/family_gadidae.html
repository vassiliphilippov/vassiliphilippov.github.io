family Gadidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæmɪli ˈɡædəˌdi / (AmE), / ˈfamɪli ˈɡadɪdiː / (BrE)
A large family of mostly marine food fishes, including the cods, haddocks, and their relatives.
cod and related marine food fishes

Examples

Many commercially valuable fish, such as cod and haddock, belong to the family Gadidae.
Researchers studied the evolutionary relationships within the family Gadidae to understand species migration patterns.
The family Gadidae plays a critical role in the fishing industry worldwide.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cod - a large marine fish, important as food, of the family Gadidae
  • Synonym: haddock - a type of marine food fish closely related to cod, within the family Gadidae

Etymology

Modern Latin *Gadidae*, from genus *Gadus* (cod).
