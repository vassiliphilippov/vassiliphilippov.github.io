K particle

1. NOUN

/ ˈkeɪ ˌpɑrtɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈkeɪ ˌpɑːtɪkəl / (BrE)
an unstable subatomic particle (a type of meson) produced during high-energy particle collisions, known for decaying into other particles
unstable subatomic meson

Examples

Scientists observed a K particle decaying rapidly in their experiment.
The presence of a K particle confirms the high-energy conditions of the collision.
Physicists study K particles to learn more about the fundamental forces in nature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kaon - another name for the K particle, a type of meson
  • Degree Higher: meson - a type of subatomic particle composed of one quark and one antiquark

Etymology

The 'K' comes from 'kaon', named from the Greek letter 'kappa'.
