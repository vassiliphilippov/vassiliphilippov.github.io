necrobiosis lipoidica

1. NOUN

/ ˌnɛkɹoʊˌbaɪˈoʊsɪs lɪˈpɔɪdɪkə / (AmE), / ˌnɛkɹəʊˌbaɪˈəʊsɪs lɪˈpɔɪdɪkə / (BrE)
A rare skin disorder characterized by thin, shiny, reddish patches, usually on the legs, often associated with diabetes mellitus.
skin disease with shiny patches, linked to diabetes
medical

Examples

The dermatologist diagnosed the shiny patches on his legs as necrobiosis lipoidica.
Patients with diabetes are more likely to develop necrobiosis lipoidica than those without the condition.
Although rare, necrobiosis lipoidica can sometimes lead to ulcers if left untreated.

Etymology

From Greek nekros (dead) + biosis (way of life) and lipoid (fat-like) + -ica (pertaining to).
