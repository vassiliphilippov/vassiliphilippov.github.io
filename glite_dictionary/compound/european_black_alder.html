European black alder

1. NOUN

/ jʊˈroʊpiən blæk ˈɔldər / (AmE), / jʊəˈrəʊpiən blæk ˈɔːldə / (BrE)
a medium-sized deciduous tree (Alnus glutinosa) native to Europe and western Asia, with dark bark, rounded leaves hairy beneath, and woody fruiting catkins
European deciduous tree with dark bark and woody catkins

Examples

The European black alder is commonly found near rivers and wetlands throughout the continent.
Wood from the European black alder is valued for its resistance to decay in water.
Her research focused on the growth patterns of European black alder saplings in restored marshlands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alder - tree or shrub of the birch family, genus Alnus
