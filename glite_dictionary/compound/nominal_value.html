nominal value

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɑːmənl ˈvælju / (AmE), / ˈnɒmɪnəl ˈvaljuː / (BrE)
the value of a security as stated by the issuing company, not reflecting its market value
face value of a security

Examples

The nominal value of the bond was $1,000, but it traded above that on the exchange.
Shareholders received certificates stating the nominal value of their shares.
A stock’s nominal value may differ significantly from its current market value.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: market value - the current price at which an asset can be bought or sold
