whole wheat flour

1. NOUN

/ ˌhoʊl ˈwiːt ˌflaʊɚ / (AmE), / ˌhəʊl ˈwiːt ˌflaʊə / (BrE)
flour made by grinding the entire wheat kernel, including the bran, germ, and endosperm; used in baking and cooking for its higher nutritional value compared to refined flour.
flour from the entire wheat grain

Examples

Many health-conscious bakers prefer using whole wheat flour instead of white flour for extra nutrition.
The recipe calls for two cups of whole wheat flour and a tablespoon of honey.
Breads made with whole wheat flour are darker and have a richer, nuttier flavor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wholemeal flour - British term for flour made from the entire wheat grain
  • Antonym: white flour - flour made only from the endosperm of wheat, finer and lighter than whole wheat
