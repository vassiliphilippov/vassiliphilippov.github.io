refractory pot

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈfræktəri pɑt / (AmE), / rɪˈfræktəri pɒt / (BrE)
a small, porous bowl made of bone ash, used in assaying to separate precious metals from other substances such as lead.
porous bowl for assaying metals

Examples

The laboratory technician placed the sample in a refractory pot for the assaying process.
Each refractory pot is designed to withstand extremely high temperatures during metal separation.
After multiple uses, the refractory pot showed signs of wear from repeated heating and cooling.

Etymology

From refractory (resistant to heat) and pot; 'refractory' from Latin 'refractarius', meaning stubborn or resisting.
