phase II clinical trial

1. NOUN

/ feɪz tu ˈklɪnɪkəl ˈtraɪəl / (AmE), / feɪz tuː ˈklɪnɪkəl ˈtraɪəl / (BrE)
a clinical trial conducted with more participants than phase I, designed to evaluate the effectiveness of a treatment and monitor possible side effects
clinical trial for treatment efficacy and side effects
medical

Examples

The new cancer drug successfully passed its phase II clinical trial and is now moving to phase III.
Researchers hope the phase II clinical trial will provide clearer evidence of the drug's effectiveness.
During the phase II clinical trial, several unexpected side effects were carefully documented and analyzed.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: phase I clinical trial - earlier clinical trial phase to assess safety and dosage
  • Degree Higher: phase III clinical trial - subsequent clinical trial phase involving more participants to confirm effectiveness and monitor adverse reactions
