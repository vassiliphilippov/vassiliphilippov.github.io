genus Pithecanthropus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs pɪˌθikænˈθroʊpəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs pɪˌθikænˈθrəʊpəs / (BrE)
a former genus of primitive, apelike early humans now classified as Homo erectus.
former human genus, now Homo erectus

Examples

The discovery of fossils attributed to genus Pithecanthropus helped shape our understanding of human evolution.
Many textbooks once classified Java Man under the genus Pithecanthropus before the revision to Homo erectus.
Although genus Pithecanthropus is now outdated, it remains a term used in historical anthropology literature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Homo erectus - an extinct species of early human

Etymology

From Greek 'pithēkos' (ape) + 'anthrōpos' (man).
