decanedioic acid

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɛkəˌniːdioʊˈɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / ˌdɛkəniːdiˈəʊɪk ˈasɪd / (BrE)
A dicarboxylic acid with ten carbon atoms, used in the manufacture of resins.
dicarboxylic acid used in resins

Examples

Decanedioic acid is often used as a monomer in the production of certain nylons and resins.
Researchers studied the reactivity of decanedioic acid under various conditions.
Manufacturers select decanedioic acid for its stability and performance in high-strength plastic materials.

Etymology

From 'decane' (a ten-carbon alkane) + 'dioic acid' (compound with two carboxylic acid groups).
