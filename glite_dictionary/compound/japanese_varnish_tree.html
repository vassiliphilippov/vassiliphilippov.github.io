Japanese varnish tree

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˈpæniz ˈvɑrnɪʃ tri / (AmE), / dʒəˈpæniːz ˈvɑːnɪʃ triː / (BrE)
A deciduous ornamental tree, native to East Asia and widely grown in the southern United States, with maple-like leaves and racemes of yellow-green flowers followed by unusual leaf-like pods.
deciduous ornamental tree with maple-like leaves

Examples

The Japanese varnish tree is prized for its attractive foliage and unique seed pods.
Many gardeners plant the Japanese varnish tree to add a touch of elegance to their landscapes.
The Japanese varnish tree blooms in early summer, displaying delicate yellow-green flowers.

The sap of the Japanese varnish tree is collected to make traditional lacquerware.
