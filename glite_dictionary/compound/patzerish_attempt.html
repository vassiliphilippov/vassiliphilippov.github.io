patzerish attempt

1. NOUN

/ ˈpætsərɪʃ əˈtɛmpt / (AmE), / ˈpætsərɪʃ əˈtɛmpt / (BrE)
an attempt or effort that is clumsy, inept, or amateurish, especially in chess or similar games
inept or clumsy effort (esp. in chess)

Examples

Her patzerish attempt at solving the puzzle only made things worse.
The coach shook his head at the team's patzerish attempt to execute the new play.
Despite his enthusiasm, every patzerish attempt at chess ended in a quick defeat.

Synset Examples

She laughed at her patzerish attempts to play the game.
His patzerish moves gave his opponent an easy win.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blunder - a careless or stupid mistake, especially in chess or games
  • Antonym: masterstroke - an extremely skillful move or achievement

Etymology

From 'patzer', a chess term for a weak or poor player (borrowed from German), with the English suffix '-ish' forming an adjective, and 'attempt'.
