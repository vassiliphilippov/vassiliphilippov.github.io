butter-and-eggs

1. NOUN

/ ˌbʌt̬ər ənd ˈɛɡz / (AmE), / ˌbʌtə ənd ˈɛɡz / (BrE)
A common European perennial plant (Linaria vulgaris) with showy yellow and orange flowers, often considered a weed in North America.
yellow-flowered perennial plant (Linaria vulgaris)

Examples

The meadow was full of butter-and-eggs blooming among the wild grasses.
Gardeners often find butter-and-eggs difficult to remove because it spreads quickly.
The bright yellow and orange flowers of butter-and-eggs attract many pollinators in summer.
