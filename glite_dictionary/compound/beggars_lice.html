beggar's lice

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛɡərz laɪs / (AmE), / ˈbɛɡəz laɪs / (BrE)
Any of several tropical or subtropical plants with trifoliate leaves and sticky seed pods that cling to animals or clothing.
plant with sticky pods

Examples

The beggar's lice stuck to my socks after our walk through the field.
Children found beggar's lice clinging to their clothes during their hike in the woods.
Some farmers consider beggar's lice a nuisance because it spreads so easily on animals.

Etymology

From 'beggar' (a person who begs) and 'lice' (parasitic insects), describing the way the seed pods cling to passersby like lice.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɛɡərz ˌlaɪs / (AmE), / ˈbɛɡəz ˌlaɪs / (BrE)
A common name for several Eurasian and North American plants whose small, prickly seeds attach easily to clothing or animal fur.
plant with prickly seeds that stick to clothing

Examples

After walking through the woods, my pants were covered in beggar's lice.
Beggar's lice sticks stubbornly to socks and animal fur when you hike in the countryside.
In late summer, the seeds of beggar's lice are notorious for clinging to your clothes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stickseed - another common name for plants with sticky seeds
  • Synonym: tick-trefoil - related plant also known for sticky seeds
