buffalo clover

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌfəloʊ ˈkloʊvər / (AmE), / ˈbʌfələʊ ˈkləʊvə / (BrE)
a type of clover plant native to the western United States, often found in prairies and grasslands
a clover native to western U.S.

Examples

We spotted several patches of buffalo clover growing along the trail.
The prairie was dotted with wildflowers, including buffalo clover and bluebonnets.
Researchers are studying how buffalo clover supports local pollinator populations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clover - a small plant with usually three-lobed leaves and dense flower heads

2. NOUN

/ ˈbʌfəloʊ ˈkloʊvɚ / (AmE), / ˈbʌfələʊ ˈkləʊvə / (BrE)
a low-growing annual herb native to the southwestern United States, particularly Texas, with silky leaves and blue flowers; noted as a leading cause of livestock poisoning in the region
poisonous southern US herb with blue flowers

Examples

Cattle grazing on buffalo clover may become seriously ill due to its toxic properties.
The fields were dotted with patches of buffalo clover in early spring.
Ranchers in Texas routinely inspect their land for buffalo clover to protect their livestock.
