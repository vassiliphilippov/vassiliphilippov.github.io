spiny lobster

1. NOUN

/ ˈspaɪni ˈlɑbstɚ / (AmE), / ˈspaɪni ˈlɒbstə / (BrE)
a warm-water lobster lacking large claws, found in tropical and subtropical seas, often harvested for its tail meat
warm-water lobster species without large claws

Examples

The chef prepared grilled spiny lobster tails for the guests at the beachfront restaurant.
Unlike the common lobster, the spiny lobster does not have large front claws.
Diving for spiny lobster is a popular activity along the coasts of Florida and California.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lobster - a marine crustacean with large claws, commonly eaten as food

2. NOUN

/ ˈspaɪni ˈlɑːbstər / (AmE), / ˈspaɪni ˈlɒbstə / (BrE)
a large edible marine crustacean with a spiny shell and no large pincers, found in warm seas
edible marine crustacean with spiny shell

Examples

The fisherman hauled a huge spiny lobster out of the trap.
Grilled spiny lobster is a delicacy in many coastal regions.
Unlike true lobsters, the spiny lobster lacks large claws but has a tough, spiny shell.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lobster - a large marine crustacean with a hard shell and large pincers
  • Synonym: crayfish - a freshwater crustacean resembling a small lobster
