soft-shelled turtle

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɔft ʃɛld ˈtɝtl̩ / (AmE), / ˈsɒft ʃɛld ˈtɜːtəl / (BrE)
a species of aquatic turtle with a flat, flexible shell covered by leathery skin, known for its voracious appetite and ability to bite painfully
aquatic turtle with a flexible, leathery shell

Examples

The soft-shelled turtle is well adapted to life in rivers and lakes, where it hunts for food.
When threatened, a soft-shelled turtle can inflict a surprisingly painful bite.
Conservation efforts are underway to protect several endangered species of soft-shelled turtle around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: turtle - a reptile with a hard shell living in or near water
  • Antonym: hard-shelled turtle - a turtle with a rigid, hard shell
