attributive genitive case

1. NOUN

/ əˈtrɪbjətɪv ˈdʒɛnətɪv keɪs / (AmE), / əˈtrɪbjətɪv ˈdʒɛnətɪv keɪs / (BrE)
A genitive case word used as an attributive adjective, especially to indicate possession or close association, as in 'John's mother'.
genitive word used attributively, e.g. 'John's mother'

Examples

The attributive genitive case often expresses a possessive relationship, as in ‘Sarah’s book’.
In the phrase ‘the teacher’s desk’, ‘teacher’s’ is an example of the attributive genitive case.
Understanding the attributive genitive case is important for students studying English grammar.

Synset Examples

an example of the attributive genitive is ‘John's’ in ‘John's mother’

Related Terms

  • Synonym: possessive case - Case indicating possession, closely related concept in English grammar

Etymology

From Latin 'genitivus' (of generation or possession); 'attributive' from Latin 'attribuere' (to assign).
