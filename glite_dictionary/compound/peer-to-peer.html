peer-to-peer

1. ADJ

/ ˌpɪr tə ˈpɪr / (AmE), / ˌpɪə tə ˈpɪə / (BrE)
(of a network or system) involving or denoting direct interaction or transactions between participants of equal status, without a central authority or intermediary.
direct interaction between equal participants

Examples

The company offers a peer-to-peer payment service that lets users send money instantly.
Many file-sharing programs use peer-to-peer technology to distribute content efficiently.
A peer-to-peer lending platform connects borrowers and investors directly, bypassing traditional banks.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: client-server - network model with dedicated service provider and receiver

Etymology

Formed from 'peer' (equal) and 'to', indicating direct connection between equals.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-90009191-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˌpɪr tə ˈpɪr / (AmE), / ˌpɪə tə ˈpɪə / (BrE)
(of a computer network) consisting of two or more computers connected as equal partners, able to share processing, control, and access to data and peripherals without a central server.
computer network with equal partners

Examples

Many file-sharing applications use a peer-to-peer network architecture to distribute data efficiently.
Unlike traditional client-server models, peer-to-peer networks allow all computers to act as both clients and servers.
Setting up a peer-to-peer connection can enable users to share files directly without a central authority.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: client-server - a network in which a central server provides resources to clients

Etymology

Formed from 'peer' (equal) + 'to' + 'peer', referring to networks where all devices are equals.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-90009192-a
Synset POS: a
