silk oak

1. NOUN

/ sɪlk oʊk / (AmE), / sɪlk əʊk / (BrE)
Any of several Australian timber trees, especially Grevillea robusta, with fernlike foliage and mottled wood often used in cabinetry and veneering.
Australian timber tree with fernlike leaves

Examples

The cabinet was made from the richly colored wood of silk oak.
Silk oak trees are commonly planted as ornamentals in warm climates.
The distinctive fernlike leaves of the silk oak set it apart from other Australian trees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Grevillea robusta - The most common species referred to as silk oak
