foxhole

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɑksˌhoʊl / (AmE), / ˈfɒksˌhəʊl / (BrE)
A small pit or dugout used as a shelter by a soldier from enemy fire.
military shelter dug in the ground

Examples

The soldier quickly jumped into a foxhole to avoid the incoming gunfire.
During the night, they took turns guarding the foxhole against a possible attack.
After digging their foxhole, the unit felt somewhat safer on the exposed hillside.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dugout - a shelter dug in the ground, especially for military use
  • Synonym: trench - a long, deep ditch used as a shelter from enemy fire in warfare

Etymology

Originally used by U.S. troops in World War I, likening the dugout to the burrow of a fox.
