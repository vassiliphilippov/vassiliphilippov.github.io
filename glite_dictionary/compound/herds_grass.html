herd's grass

1. NOUN

/ hɜrdz ɡræs / (AmE), / hɜːdz ɡrɑːs / (BrE)
A type of grass with long cylindrical spikes, grown in northern United States and Europe for use as hay.
hay grass with long spikes

Examples

Farmers often rely on herd's grass because it produces excellent hay for livestock feed.
The field was covered in tall herd's grass, ready to be harvested for the winter months.
Researchers are developing new varieties of herd's grass to improve yield in colder climates.
