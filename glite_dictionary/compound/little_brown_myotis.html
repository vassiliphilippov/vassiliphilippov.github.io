little brown myotis

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪt̬l ˈbraʊn maɪˈoʊtɪs / (AmE), / ˈlɪtl ˈbraʊn mʌɪˈəʊtɪs / (BrE)
A small, common North American bat (Myotis lucifugus) that is widely distributed across the continent.
common North American bat species

Examples

The little brown myotis is one of the most widespread bats in North America.
Researchers are studying the habits of the little brown myotis to monitor population changes.
When dusk falls, you can often spot a little brown myotis hunting for insects near water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bat - a nocturnal flying mammal

Etymology

From Greek 'myos' (mouse) + 'ous' (ear), referencing the bat's mouse-like ears; 'myotis' is the genus name.
