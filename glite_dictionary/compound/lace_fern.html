lace fern

1. NOUN

/ leɪs fɝːn / (AmE), / leɪs fɜːn / (BrE)
a small tufted fern native to northwestern America, known for its delicate, lace-like fronds
small tufted fern of northwestern America

Examples

The lace fern thrives in moist, shaded forests throughout the Pacific Northwest.
Gardeners often choose the lace fern for its fine, attractive foliage.
As we hiked, I spotted a patch of lace fern growing beside the trail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fern - a non-flowering vascular plant with feathery or leafy fronds
