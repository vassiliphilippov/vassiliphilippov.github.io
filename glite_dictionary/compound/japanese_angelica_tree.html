Japanese angelica tree

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˈpænɪs ˈændʒəlɪkə tri / (AmE), / dʒəˈpænɪs ˈændʒəlɪkə triː / (BrE)
a deciduous Asian shrub or small tree (Aralia elata) with prickly stems and large leaves, cultivated for its ornamental appearance and adventive in parts of eastern North America
deciduous Asian shrub or small tree (Aralia elata)

Examples

The Japanese angelica tree is popular in gardens for its striking appearance and upright growth.
Gardeners often prune the Japanese angelica tree to maintain its shape and encourage new shoots.
In the wild, the Japanese angelica tree can form dense thickets that crowd out native plants.
