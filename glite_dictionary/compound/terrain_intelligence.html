terrain intelligence

1. NOUN

/ təˈreɪn ɪnˈtɛlədʒəns / (AmE), / təˈreɪn ɪnˈtɛlɪdʒəns / (BrE)
tactical intelligence relating to the natural and artificial features of an area, such as landforms, vegetation, roads, and buildings, used in planning military or strategic operations.
tactical information about an area's features

Examples

The military unit relied on terrain intelligence to plan their approach through the mountains.
Accurate terrain intelligence was essential for the engineers building the new supply route.
Without proper terrain intelligence, the army could not anticipate obstacles in the city.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reconnaissance - military observation to gather information about an area
