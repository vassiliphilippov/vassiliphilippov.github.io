mass medium

1. NOUN

/ mæs ˈmiːdiəm / (AmE), / mæs ˈmiːdiəm / (BrE)
A means of communication, such as television, radio, or newspapers, that reaches a large audience.
means of wide public communication

Examples

Television is often considered the most influential mass medium in shaping public opinion.
With the rise of the internet, a new mass medium emerged, changing how people receive information.
Advertising across every mass medium is crucial for launching a nationwide product.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mass media - means of communication reaching large audiences collectively

Etymology

From Latin 'massa' (mass) + Latin 'medium' (means, middle).
