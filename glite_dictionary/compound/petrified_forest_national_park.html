Petrified Forest National Park

1. PROPN

/ ˈpɛtrɪˌfaɪd ˈfɔrɪst ˈnæʃənəl pɑrk / (AmE), / ˈpɛtrɪfaɪd ˈfɒrɪst ˈnæʃənəl pɑːk / (BrE)
A national park in northeastern Arizona, United States, renowned for its large deposits of petrified coniferous trees.
national park in Arizona with petrified trees

Examples

Many tourists visit Petrified Forest National Park to see the colorful fossilized logs scattered across the desert.
The unique geology and ancient trees make Petrified Forest National Park a popular destination for scientists and sightseers alike.
We photographed incredible views of prehistoric wood during our hike through Petrified Forest National Park.
