genus Quiscalus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs kwɪˈskæl.əs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs kwɪˈskæl.əs / (BrE)
A genus of birds in the family Icteridae that includes the grackles, native to the Americas.
a genus of birds including grackles

Examples

The genus Quiscalus contains several species of grackles found throughout the Americas.
Many ornithologists study the behavior of birds in the genus Quiscalus.
Members of the genus Quiscalus are known for their iridescent plumage and loud calls.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grackle - a common name for birds in the genus Quiscalus

Etymology

From Latin 'Quiscalus', the name for the grackle genus given by Carl Linnaeus.
