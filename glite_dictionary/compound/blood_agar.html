blood agar

1. NOUN

/ blʌd ˈæɡ.ər / (AmE), / blʌd ˈɑː.ɡɑː / (BrE)
a culture medium made by adding whole blood to nutrient agar, used to grow and identify bacteria
agar medium with blood for culturing bacteria
medical

Examples

Laboratory technicians often use blood agar to grow bacterial cultures from patient samples.
Hemolysis patterns on blood agar help identify different types of bacteria.
The presence of certain bacteria can be detected by their appearance on blood agar plates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agar - a gelatinous substance obtained from seaweed, used for culturing microorganisms
