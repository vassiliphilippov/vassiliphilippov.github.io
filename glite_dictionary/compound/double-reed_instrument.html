double-reed instrument

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʌbəl ˈrid ˈɪn.strə.mənt / (AmE), / ˌdʌb.əl ˈriːd ˈɪn.strə.mənt / (BrE)
a woodwind instrument that produces sound by means of two joined reeds vibrating together, such as the oboe or bassoon
a woodwind instrument with two joined reeds

Examples

The oboe is a well-known double-reed instrument often featured in orchestras.
She learned to play several double-reed instruments during her conservatory studies.
The unique timbre of a double-reed instrument distinguishes it from other woodwinds.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: single-reed instrument - a woodwind instrument that uses a single reed to produce sound, such as a clarinet
  • Synonym: woodwind - a family of musical instruments that produce sound by blowing air through a reed or across an opening
