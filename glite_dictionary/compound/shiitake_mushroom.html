shiitake mushroom

1. NOUN

/ ʃiˈtɑːki ˈmʌʃˌruːm / (AmE), / ʃɪˈɑːkeɪ ˈmʌʃrʊm / (BrE)
An edible mushroom from East Asia, with a golden to dark brown cap and a tough, typically inedible stem, widely used in cooking.
an edible East Asian mushroom

Examples

The chef added sliced shiitake mushrooms to the stir-fry for extra flavor.
I bought some fresh shiitake mushrooms at the Asian market yesterday.
Vegetarian ramen often features shiitake mushrooms as a main ingredient.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: mushroom - a fungal growth with a stem and a cap, often edible

Etymology

From Japanese 'shiitake', from 'shii' (oak) and 'take' (mushroom).
