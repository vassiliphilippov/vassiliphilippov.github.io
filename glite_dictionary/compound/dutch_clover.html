Dutch clover

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʌtʃ ˈkloʊvɚ / (AmE), / ˈdʌtʃ ˈkləʊvə / (BrE)
A creeping European clover (Trifolium repens) with white to pink flowers and bright green leaves, commonly grown for forage and naturalized in North America.
a creeping clover with white to pink flowers

Examples

The field was covered with Dutch clover, creating a lush green carpet dotted with white flowers.
Farmers often plant Dutch clover to improve soil fertility and provide forage for livestock.
You can recognize Dutch clover by its distinctive trifoliate leaves and creeping stems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: white clover - another common name for Dutch clover (Trifolium repens)
