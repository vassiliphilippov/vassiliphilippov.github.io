bog myrtle

1. NOUN

/ bɑɡ ˈmɝːtəl / (AmE), / bɒɡ ˈmɜːtəl / (BrE)
a perennial shrub found in wet areas of Europe and North America, with aromatic, bitter leaves and small white or purplish flowers
aromatic shrub of wetlands

Examples

The bog myrtle gives off a strong scent when its leaves are crushed.
Local people once used bog myrtle to flavor traditional dishes and beverages.
You can find bog myrtle growing in abundance along the edges of the marsh.
