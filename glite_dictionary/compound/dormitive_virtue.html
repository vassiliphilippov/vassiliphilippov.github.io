dormitive virtue

1. NOUN

/ dɔrˈmɪtɪv ˈvɜrtʃu / (AmE), / dɔːˈmɪtɪv ˈvɜːtʃuː / (BrE)
a property or quality that causes sleep or induces dormancy
quality that induces sleep

Examples

The doctor mentioned the dormitive virtue of the new medication during his lecture.
Some herbs are prized for their dormitive virtue and are used as natural sleep aids.
Philosophers sometimes mock explanations that rely solely on the term dormitive virtue.

Synset Examples

His favorite book discusses the dormitive virtue of plants.
The tea has a dormitive virtue that helps with sleep.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sedative - a substance that calms or induces sleep
  • Antonym: stimulant - a substance that raises physiological or nervous activity

Etymology

From Latin 'dormitīvus' (sleep-inducing) + English 'virtue' (power or quality).
