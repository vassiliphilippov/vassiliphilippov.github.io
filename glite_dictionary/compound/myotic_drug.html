myotic drug

1. NOUN

/ maɪˈɑːtɪk drʌɡ / (AmE), / maɪˈɒtɪk drʌɡ / (BrE)
A drug that causes constriction of the pupil of the eye (miosis).
drug that constricts the pupil
medical

Examples

The ophthalmologist prescribed a myotic drug to help manage the patient's glaucoma.
Pilocarpine is a commonly used myotic drug in eye treatments.
After applying the myotic drug, the patient's pupils became noticeably smaller.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: mydriatic drug - a drug that dilates the pupil of the eye

Etymology

From Greek *muōtikos* (causing contraction), from *muein* (to close).
