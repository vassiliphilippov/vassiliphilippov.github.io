Deccan hemp

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛkən hɛmp / (AmE), / ˈdɛkən hɛmp / (BrE)
a valuable fiber plant of the East Indies, now widely cultivated in many regions
fiber plant originally from East Indies

Examples

The strong fibers of Deccan hemp are used to make ropes and twine.
Farmers in tropical regions increasingly cultivate Deccan hemp for export.
Research has shown that Deccan hemp thrives in well-drained soils.

Etymology

From Deccan, a region in southern India, and 'hemp' referring to fiber plants.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɛkən hɛmp / (AmE), / ˈdɛkən hɛmp / (BrE)
A fiber obtained from the East Indian plant Hibiscus cannabinus, used for making cordage and textiles.
fiber from Hibiscus cannabinus plant

Examples

The factory imported Deccan hemp to produce durable ropes for the shipping industry.
Research has shown that Deccan hemp is both strong and resistant to mildew.
Historically, Deccan hemp was used in the production of twine and coarse textiles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kenaf - fiber plant Hibiscus cannabinus from which Deccan hemp is obtained

Etymology

Named after the Deccan region of India and 'hemp', by analogy with hemp fibers.
