yellow ochre

1. NOUN

/ ˈjɛloʊ ˈoʊkər / (AmE), / ˈjɛləʊ ˈəʊkə / (BrE)
a natural earth pigment containing hydrated iron oxide, clay, and silica, used especially in painting and coloring materials
natural yellow earth pigment

Examples

Artists often use yellow ochre to add warm, earthy tones to their paintings.
The cliff revealed layers of clay streaked with yellow ochre and red hues.
Historic buildings were sometimes painted with a yellow ochre wash for a natural look.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ochre - an earth pigment containing ferric oxide, used as a colorant

Etymology

From 'yellow' and 'ochre', the latter from Greek 'ōkhra' (pale yellow).
