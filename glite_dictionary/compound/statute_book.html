statute book

1. NOUN

/ ˈstætʃuːt bʊk / (AmE), / ˈstætjuːt bʊk / (BrE)
an official record or published collection of all the laws enacted by a government or authority in a particular jurisdiction
official collection of a jurisdiction's laws

Examples

The new laws will soon be added to the statute book.
Many out-of-date regulations remain on the statute book long after they've stopped being enforced.
The statute book is updated regularly to reflect changes in legislation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: legislation - laws collectively passed by a legislative body
