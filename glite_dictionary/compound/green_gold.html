green gold

1. NOUN

/ ɡrin ɡoʊld / (AmE), / ɡriːn ɡəʊld / (BrE)
a gold alloy, typically of at least 14 karats, combined with silver or silver and cadmium, that has a greenish appearance
gold alloy with a greenish hue

Examples

This ring is made from green gold, giving it a unique, subtle greenish tint.
Jewelers often use green gold for crafting elegant, nature-inspired pieces.
The necklace's unusual color comes from its high proportion of silver in the green gold alloy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alloy - a metal made by combining two or more metallic elements
