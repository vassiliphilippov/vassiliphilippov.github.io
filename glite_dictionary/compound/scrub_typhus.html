scrub typhus

1. NOUN

/ skɹʌb ˈtaɪfəs / (AmE), / skɹʌb ˈtɪfəs / (BrE)
an infectious disease caused by the bacterium Orientia tsutsugamushi, transmitted by the bite of larval mites, especially in Asia, and characterized by fever, headache, and rash
infectious disease caused by mite-borne bacteria
medical

Examples

Doctors diagnosed the patient with scrub typhus after she developed a high fever and rash following a hike in rural India.
Cases of scrub typhus are most frequently reported in Asia, where mite-infested scrublands are common.
The laboratory confirmed scrub typhus as the cause of his sudden illness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: typhus - any of several infectious diseases caused by rickettsial bacteria, marked by fever and rash

Etymology

From 'scrub' (referring to low vegetation habitats where infection occurs) and 'typhus' (from Greek 'tuphos', fever).
