brass farthing

1. NOUN

/ bræs ˈfɑrðɪŋ / (AmE), / brɑːs ˈfɑːðɪŋ / (BrE)
A very small or worthless sum of money; something considered to have no value.
worthless or insignificant amount
british

Examples

She wouldn’t give a brass farthing for their opinion.
He claims he doesn’t care a brass farthing about criticism.
Don’t waste a brass farthing on things you don’t need.

Synset Examples

She doesn’t care a brass farthing for gossip.
He wouldn't spend a brass farthing on that.
Don't waste a brass farthing on useless items.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: red cent - A very small or worthless amount of money
  • Synonym: tuppence - A trivial amount of money, very little

Etymology

From the British 'farthing', a former small coin, with 'brass' implying it is of less value than even the original copper coin.
