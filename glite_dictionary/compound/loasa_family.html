loasa family

1. NOUN

/ loʊˈɑːsə ˈfæməli / (AmE), / ləʊˈɑːsə ˈfæməli / (BrE)
a family of bristly, often climbing plants found in America, Africa, and southern Arabia, known botanically as Loasaceae
plant family (Loasaceae)

Examples

Many species in the loasa family produce strikingly beautiful flowers.
The loasa family is known for its bristly stems and leaves that can irritate the skin.
Botanists have discovered new members of the loasa family in remote areas of South America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Loasaceae - botanical family comprising the loasa family

Etymology

From New Latin Loasaceae, based on the type genus Loasa.
