conjunctival vein

1. NOUN

/ kənˌdʒʌŋk.tɪ.vəl veɪn / (AmE), / kənˌdʒʌŋk.tɪ.vəl veɪn / (BrE)
a vein that drains blood from the conjunctiva of the eye
vein draining the eye's conjunctiva
medical

Examples

The doctor examined her conjunctival veins for signs of inflammation.
Reddening of the conjunctival veins may indicate irritation or infection.
Dilated conjunctival veins are commonly observed in patients with conjunctivitis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vein - a blood vessel that carries blood toward the heart

Etymology

From Latin 'conjunctiva' (joining together) and Latin 'vena' (vein).
