to search for prey

1. VERB

/ sɝtʃ fər preɪ / (AmE), / sɜːtʃ fə preɪ / (BrE)
to look for animals to hunt and eat, especially used of predators in the wild
hunt for animals to eat

Examples

At dawn, the lion began to search for prey along the riverbank.
Several sharks can be seen searching for prey near the coral reef.
Owls typically search for prey at night when their sharp vision gives them an advantage.

Synset Examples

The hawk tuah circled above, searching for prey.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hunt - to chase or search for animals to kill for food or sport
