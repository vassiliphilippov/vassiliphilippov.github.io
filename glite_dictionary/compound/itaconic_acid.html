itaconic acid

1. NOUN

/ ˌaɪ.təˈkɑ.nɪk ˈæs.ɪd / (AmE), / ˌaɪ.təˈkɒ.nɪk ˈæs.ɪd / (BrE)
a white, crystalline carboxylic acid produced by the fermentation of sugars, used in the manufacture of resins and plastics
a carboxylic acid used in manufacturing
medical

Examples

The fermentation of certain sugars can produce itaconic acid, which is important in industrial chemistry.
Researchers have developed new methods to synthesize itaconic acid from renewable sources.
Polymers made from itaconic acid are being explored for use in biodegradable plastics.

Etymology

From 'itacon-', altered from aconitic acid based on its relation to aconitic acid, originally from aconite; named in 19th century chemical literature.
