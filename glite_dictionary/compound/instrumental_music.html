instrumental music

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪnstrəˈmɛntl̩ ˈmjuːzɪk / (AmE), / ˌɪnstrəˈmɛntəl ˈmjuːzɪk / (BrE)
music composed or performed mainly or exclusively using musical instruments, without singing or vocals
music performed without vocals

Examples

The orchestra performed a beautiful piece of instrumental music at the concert.
Many people find instrumental music helps them concentrate while studying.
He prefers instrumental music over songs with lyrics when he's relaxing.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: vocal music - music performed with singing or vocal parts

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪnstrəˈmɛntəl ˈmjuːzɪk / (AmE), / ˌɪnstrʊˈmɛntəl ˈmjuːzɪk / (BrE)
music composed or performed primarily by musical instruments, typically without vocal accompaniment.
music performed by instruments, not vocals

Examples

She prefers listening to instrumental music when she studies because it helps her concentrate.
The concert featured both choral works and instrumental music composed in the Baroque period.
Many film soundtracks are made up mostly of instrumental music to set the mood without distracting from the dialogue.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: vocal music - music performed by voices, often with lyrics
