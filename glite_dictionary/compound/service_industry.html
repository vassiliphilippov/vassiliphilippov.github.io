service industry

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɝːvɪs ˌɪndəstri / (AmE), / ˈsɜːvɪs ˌɪndəstri / (BrE)
An industry that provides services rather than goods; the sector of the economy concerned with offering non-tangible products such as hospitality, retail, healthcare, or education.
sector providing services, not goods

Examples

Tourism is a major part of the service industry in many countries.
After working in retail for years, she developed a deep understanding of the service industry.
The service industry includes fields like healthcare, banking, and education.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: services sector - the part of the economy that provides services instead of goods
  • Antonym: manufacturing industry - an industry that produces goods from raw materials

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɝːvɪs ˌɪndəstri / (AmE), / ˈsɜːvɪs ˌɪndəstri / (BrE)
the sector of the economy that provides services rather than producing physical goods, including areas such as retail, healthcare, education, and hospitality
sector providing services, not goods

Examples

Many people find jobs in the service industry after graduating from college.
Tourism is a major part of the service industry in this country.
Technological advances are rapidly changing the nature of the service industry.

Synset Examples

She works in the service industry and loves it.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: manufacturing industry - industry involved in producing physical goods
  • Synonym: tertiary sector - another term for the service industry in economics
