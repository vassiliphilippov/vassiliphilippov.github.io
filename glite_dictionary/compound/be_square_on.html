to be square on

1. VERB

/ biː skwɛr ɑn / (AmE), / biː skweə ɒn / (BrE)
to be honest or straightforward about something, especially concerning one's responsibilities or obligations
be honest or straightforward

Examples

It's important to be square on your finances if you want to avoid trouble.
He always tried to be square on every deal he made.
If you are square on your intentions, people will trust you more.

Synset Examples

She always tries to be square on her responsibilities.
It's crucial to be square on your commitments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: be frank - to be open and direct in speech
  • Antonym: to deceive - to mislead or lie to someone

Etymology

From 'square' meaning honest or fair, originally American slang of early 20th century.
