red osier dogwood

1. NOUN

/ rɛd ˈoʊʒər ˈdɔgˌwʊd / (AmE), / rɛd ˈəʊzɪə ˈdɒgˌwʊd / (BrE)
a North American shrub (Cornus sericea) with reddish-purple twigs and clusters of small white flowers, often found along streams and wetlands
North American shrub with red twigs and white flowers

Examples

The bright stems of the red osier dogwood stand out beautifully against the winter snow.
Along the lakeshore, clusters of red osier dogwood provide shelter for birds and wildlife.
In early summer, the red osier dogwood blooms with delicate white flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dogwood - shrubs and trees in the Cornus genus

Etymology

From 'osier' (a type of willow with flexible stems) and 'dogwood', referring to the Cornus genus.
