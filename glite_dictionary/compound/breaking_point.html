breaking point

1. NOUN

/ ˈbreɪkɪŋ pɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˈbreɪkɪŋ pɔɪnt / (BrE)
the moment or level at which a person can no longer cope with stress, or when a situation becomes critical or can no longer be sustained
threshold where coping or endurance fails

Examples

The constant workload pushed her close to her breaking point.
After hours of negotiations, tempers reached the breaking point.
The bridge was tested until it finally hit its breaking point.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tipping point - the critical point at which a significant change occurs
  • Synonym: crisis - a time of intense difficulty or danger

2. NOUN

/ ˈbreɪkɪŋ ˌpɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˈbreɪkɪŋ ˌpɔɪnt / (BrE)
the moment or stage at which a person or thing gives way under stress or pressure
critical moment of collapse under stress

Examples

After working nonstop for weeks, she finally reached her breaking point and had to take a break.
The old bridge was strained almost to its breaking point during the flood.
Negotiations continued until both sides were pushed to the breaking point.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: limit - the greatest amount, number, or level possible or allowed
  • Synonym: threshold - the point or level at which something begins or changes
