Dead Sea Scrolls

1. PROPN

/ dɛd si ˈskroʊlz / (AmE), / dɛd si ˈskrəʊlz / (BrE)
a collection of ancient Hebrew and Aramaic manuscripts, including texts from the Old Testament, discovered in caves near the Dead Sea between 1947 and 1956
ancient biblical manuscripts found near Dead Sea

Examples

Scholars continue to study the Dead Sea Scrolls for insights into ancient Judaism.
The discovery of the Dead Sea Scrolls was one of the greatest archaeological finds of the 20th century.
Exhibitions featuring the Dead Sea Scrolls attract visitors from around the world.

Synset Examples

the Dead Sea Scrolls provide information about Judaism and the Bible around the time of Jesus

Etymology

Named after the Dead Sea, near whose shores the scrolls were found. 'Scroll' from Old French 'escroe', Latin 'scrofa' (strip, roll).
