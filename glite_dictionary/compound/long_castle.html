long castle

1. NOUN

/ lɔŋ ˈkæsəl / (AmE), / lɒŋ ˈkɑːsəl / (BrE)
In chess, castling on the queenside, moving the king two squares towards the queenside rook and the rook to the square next to the king.
chess move: queenside castling

Examples

After he played long castle, his rook immediately controlled the open file.
Choosing to long castle can offer greater attacking chances on the kingside.
Some players avoid long castle because it often exposes the queen's rook pawn.

Synset Examples

This is in part because the a file can be weak after long castling.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: short castle - castling on the kingside in chess
