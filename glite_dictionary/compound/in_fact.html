in fact

1. ADV

/ ɪn fækt / (AmE), / ɪn fakt / (BrE)
used to emphasize the truth of a statement, especially when it contrasts with or adds to what was previously said
actually; in reality

Examples

Many thought the plan was impossible, but in fact it succeeded beyond expectations.
He looks angry, but in fact he's just tired.
She said she was busy; in fact, she had forgotten about the meeting.

Synset Examples

in fact, it was a wonder anyone survived
painters who are in fact anything but unsophisticated
as a matter of fact, he is several inches taller than his father

Related Terms

  • Synonym: actually - used to emphasize the real truth of a situation
  • Synonym: in reality - in actual fact, rather than what people might think
  • Antonym: in theory - used to contrast with what happens in practice
