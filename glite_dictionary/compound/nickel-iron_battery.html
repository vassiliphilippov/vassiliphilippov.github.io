nickel-iron battery

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɪkəl ˈaɪərn ˈbætəri / (AmE), / ˈnɪkəl ˈaɪən ˈbætri / (BrE)
a rechargeable storage battery with a nickel oxide cathode and an iron anode, using potassium hydroxide as the electrolyte, typically producing about 1.2 volts per cell
type of rechargeable storage battery

Examples

The nickel-iron battery is known for its durability and long lifespan, making it suitable for off-grid applications.
Early electric vehicles often used the nickel-iron battery because of its resistance to overcharging and rough handling.
Unlike lead-acid batteries, a nickel-iron battery can withstand frequent deep discharges without significant degradation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nickel-cadmium battery - rechargeable battery with a nickel oxide cathode and a cadmium anode
  • Synonym: lead-acid battery - storage battery with lead electrodes and sulfuric acid electrolyte
