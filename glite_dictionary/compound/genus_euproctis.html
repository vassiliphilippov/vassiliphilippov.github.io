genus Euproctis

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs juːˈprɒktɪs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs juːˈprɒktɪs / (BrE)
A genus of moths in the family Lymantriidae, known for species whose caterpillars often have stinging hairs.
genus of moths in Lymantriidae family

Examples

Several pest species affecting crops belong to the genus Euproctis.
Scientists discovered a new species within the genus Euproctis in Southeast Asia.
Caterpillars from the genus Euproctis can cause skin irritation in humans.

Etymology

From New Latin 'Euproctis', used in scientific classification of moths.
