genus Sticherus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˈstɪkərəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈstɪkərəs / (BrE)
A genus of ferns known as umbrella ferns, native to warm regions of Australia and New Zealand.
genus of umbrella ferns

Examples

Scientists discovered a new species within the genus Sticherus during their expedition in New Zealand.
Members of the genus Sticherus are often called umbrella ferns due to the shape of their fronds.
Botanists are studying how the genus Sticherus adapts to different climates across Australia.

Etymology

Genus name from Greek 'stichos' (row or line), referring to the arrangement of sporangia on the frond.
