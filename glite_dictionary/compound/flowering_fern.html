flowering fern

1. NOUN

/ ˈflaʊ.ɚ.ɪŋ fɝːn / (AmE), / ˈflaʊ.ər.ɪŋ fɜːn / (BrE)
an Australasian fern with clusters of sporangia on the stems of its fertile fronds
Australasian fern with sporangia in clusters

Examples

Many gardeners appreciate the unique appearance of the flowering fern in shaded areas.
The spores of the flowering fern are produced in visible clusters along its stems.
During the hike in New Zealand, we spotted several flowering ferns near the stream.

2. NOUN

/ ˈflaʊərɪŋ fɜrn / (AmE), / ˈflaʊərɪŋ fɜːn / (BrE)
A large fern of the genus Osmunda, notable for its creeping rhizomes and fronds bearing spore clusters that resemble flowers.
a fern of the genus Osmunda

Examples

The flowering fern is often found in moist woodlands and along riverbanks.
Botanists were fascinated by the unique spore clusters of the flowering fern.
Despite its name, the flowering fern does not actually produce flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Osmunda - a genus of ferns with sporangia on modified fronds
