declarative sentence

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈklærətɪv ˈsɛn.təns / (AmE), / dɪˈklær.ə.tɪv ˈsɛn.təns / (BrE)
A sentence in the indicative mood that makes a statement or declares something.
statement-making sentence (indicative mood)

Examples

"The sun rises in the east" is a classic example of a declarative sentence.
Students learned how to distinguish a declarative sentence from a question or command.
Every essay should begin with a strong declarative sentence to set the topic.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: interrogative sentence - a sentence that asks a question
  • Antonym: imperative sentence - a sentence that gives a command or request
  • Antonym: exclamatory sentence - a sentence that expresses strong emotion or surprise
