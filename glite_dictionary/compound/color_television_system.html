color television system

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌlər ˌtɛləˈvɪʒən ˈsɪstəm / (AmE), / ˈkʌlə ˌtɛlɪˈvɪʒən ˈsɪstəm / (BrE)
a system for broadcasting and displaying television images in color, rather than in black and white.
television system that transmits images in color

Examples

The introduction of the color television system revolutionized home entertainment in the 1960s.
Engineers spent years perfecting the color television system to ensure accurate color reproduction.
Many countries transitioned from black-and-white to a color television system during the late twentieth century.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: black-and-white television - a television system transmitting only monochrome images
