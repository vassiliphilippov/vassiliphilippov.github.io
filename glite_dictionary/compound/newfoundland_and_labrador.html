Newfoundland and Labrador

1. PROPN

/ ˌnuːfənˈlænd ənd ˈlæbrəˌdɔr / (AmE), / ˈnjuːfən(l)ənd ənd ˈlæbrədɔː / (BrE)
a province of Canada consisting of the island of Newfoundland and part of the mainland, bordering the Labrador Sea; admitted as Canada's 10th province in 1949
Canadian province on Newfoundland island and mainland Labrador

Examples

She spent her summer vacation exploring Newfoundland and Labrador's rugged coastline and picturesque towns.
Newfoundland and Labrador became the tenth province of Canada in 1949.
The wildlife in Newfoundland and Labrador attracts photographers and nature lovers from all over the world.

Etymology

Newfoundland: from English 'new found land'; Labrador: from Portuguese 'lavrador' (farmer), after João Fernandes Lavrador, explorer.
