Zambezi River

1. PROPN

/ zæmˈbiːzi ˈrɪvər / (AmE), / zæmˈbiːzi ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
the fourth-longest river in Africa, flowing eastward across southern Africa to the Indian Ocean
major river in southern Africa

Examples

The Zambezi River is famous for Victoria Falls, one of the world's largest waterfalls.
Many communities depend on the Zambezi River for fishing and agriculture.
We took a boat trip along the Zambezi River to watch the sunset over the water.

Etymology

From local Tonga name 'Kasambabezi' (regarded as 'river of God').
