Napier's rods

1. NOUN

/ ˈneɪpiərz rɑdz / (AmE), / ˈneɪpɪəz rɒdz / (BrE)
a set of numbered rods invented by John Napier, formerly used as a manual aid for multiplication and division
mathematical calculation rods invented by John Napier

Examples

Before the widespread use of calculators, mathematicians often relied on Napier's rods to simplify complex multiplications.
A set of Napier's rods can demonstrate how multiplication algorithms were performed in the 17th century.
John showed his students how to calculate square roots with Napier's rods during the math history class.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Napier's bones - variation of Napier's rods used for calculation

Etymology

Named after John Napier (1550–1617), Scottish mathematician, who invented the device.
