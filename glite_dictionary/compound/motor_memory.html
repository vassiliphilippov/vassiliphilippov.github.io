motor memory

1. NOUN

/ ˈmoʊtər ˈmɛməri / (AmE), / ˈməʊtə ˈmɛməri / (BrE)
the ability to remember and perform previously learned motor skills, such as riding a bicycle or typing
memory for motor skills
medical

Examples

Years of playing piano have contributed to her excellent motor memory.
Athletes rely on motor memory to execute complex movements without conscious thought.
Rehabilitation after injury often involves retraining the brain's motor memory.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: procedural memory - memory for performing skills and tasks
