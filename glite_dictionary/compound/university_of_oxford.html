University of Oxford

1. PROPN

/ junɪˈvɝːsəti əv ˈɑks fərd / (AmE), / juːnɪˈvɜːsəti əv ˈɒksfəd / (BrE)
A prestigious university located in Oxford, England, renowned for its academic excellence and history.
prestigious university in Oxford, England

Examples

He graduated from the University of Oxford with a degree in history.
The University of Oxford is known for its world-class research and teaching.
Many prime ministers and scientists have studied at the University of Oxford.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Oxford - The city in England where the university is located

Etymology

University: from Latin universitas (whole, totality); Oxford: from Old English Oxnaford (ford of the oxen).
