in perpetuity

1. ADV

/ ɪn pɚˈpɛtʃuəti / (AmE), / ɪn pəˈpɛtjʊɪti / (BrE)
for an indefinitely long time; forever, without limit or end
for an indefinitely long time

Examples

The land was donated to the local park service in perpetuity.
This scholarship fund will provide financial support in perpetuity.
The artist's work will be displayed in the museum in perpetuity.

Synset Examples

bequeathed to the nation in perpetuity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forever - for all time; always
  • Antonym: temporarily - for a limited period of time

Etymology

From Latin perpetuitas (continuity, permanence), via perpetuus (continuous, everlasting).

2. ADV

/ ɪn pɚˈpɛtʃuːəti / (AmE), / ɪn pəˈpɛtjʊɪti / (BrE)
for all time; forever; without end
forever; without end

Examples

The land was donated to the wildlife trust in perpetuity to ensure its preservation.
The copyright for the novel is held in perpetuity by the author's estate.
Some scholarships are funded in perpetuity so that students can benefit for generations.

Synset Examples

desire happiness in perpetuity
an annuity paid in perpetuity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forever - for all future time; continually
  • Antonym: temporary - lasting for only a limited period; not permanent

Etymology

From Latin 'perpetuitas' (continuity, endless duration).
