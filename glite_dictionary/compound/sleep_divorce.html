sleep divorce

1. NOUN

/ slip dɪˈvɔrs / (AmE), / sliːp dɪˈvɔːs / (BrE)
an arrangement in which a couple chooses to sleep in separate beds or rooms because of conflicting sleep habits or schedules
when couples sleep separately due to sleep issues

Examples

After struggling with her husband's snoring, they agreed on a sleep divorce to get better rest.
Many couples find that a sleep divorce helps their relationship and improves their health.
A recent survey showed that more people are considering a sleep divorce due to differing sleep routines.

Synset Examples

They faced a sleep divorce after years of different schedules.
Her sleep divorce made family life quite challenging.
He joked about their sleep divorce during dinner.

Etymology

Modelled after 'divorce', combining 'sleep' and 'divorce' to suggest separation while remaining together.
