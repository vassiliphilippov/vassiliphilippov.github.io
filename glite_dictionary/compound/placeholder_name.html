placeholder name

1. NOUN

/ ˈpleɪsˌhoʊldər neɪm / (AmE), / ˈpleɪshəʊldə neɪm / (BrE)
a name used temporarily in examples or models to stand for an unspecified or hypothetical person or thing
temporary name used in examples

Examples

The legal document used 'Richard Roe' as a placeholder name for the unknown defendant.
In programming tutorials, 'foo' and 'bar' are often used as placeholder names.
Teachers frequently use 'Jane Doe' as a placeholder name in class examples.

Synset Examples

Richard Roe is a common placeholder name in legal cases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dummy name - a false or made-up name used in examples or to conceal real identity
  • Synonym: placeholder - something that serves as a substitute until the real thing is available
