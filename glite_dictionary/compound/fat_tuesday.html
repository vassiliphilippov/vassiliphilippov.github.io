Fat Tuesday

1. NOUN

/ ˌfæt ˈtuːzdeɪ / (AmE), / ˌfæt ˈtjuːzdeɪ / (BrE)
The day before Ash Wednesday, traditionally celebrated with feasting and festivities as the last day before Lent, especially noted for carnival celebrations in New Orleans.
the day before Lent marked by festivities

Examples

Many people travel to New Orleans to celebrate Fat Tuesday with parades and music.
After indulging in sweets and rich foods on Fat Tuesday, many begin fasting for Lent.
The city streets were packed with revelers enjoying the festivities of Fat Tuesday.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mardi Gras - The French name for Fat Tuesday; a carnival celebrated before Lent, especially in New Orleans

Etymology

Translation of the French 'Mardi Gras', meaning 'Fat Tuesday', referring to the practice of using up rich foods before Lent.

2. NOUN

/ fæt ˈtuːzdeɪ / (AmE), / fæt ˈtjuːzdeɪ / (BrE)
The day before Ash Wednesday, marked by feasting and revelry before the fasting period of Lent begins, also known as Mardi Gras.
the day before Lent; Mardi Gras

Examples

In New Orleans, Fat Tuesday is celebrated with lively parades and music.
Many people indulge in rich foods on Fat Tuesday before beginning the Lenten fast.
The school organized a pancake breakfast for students on Fat Tuesday.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mardi Gras - the day before Ash Wednesday, also known as Fat Tuesday

Etymology

Translation of French 'Mardi Gras' (literally 'Fat Tuesday').
