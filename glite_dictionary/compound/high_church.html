High Church

1. NOUN

/ haɪ tʃɝːtʃ / (AmE), / haɪ tʃɜːtʃ / (BrE)
A tradition within the Anglican Church that emphasizes Catholic practices, beliefs, and rituals, including adherence to sacraments and church authority.
Anglican tradition stressing Catholic practices and rituals

Examples

He was known as a staunch supporter of the High Church within the Anglican community.
The High Church tradition places great importance on sacraments and formal rituals.
Debates between High Church and Low Church members shaped the history of Anglican worship.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Low Church - Anglican tradition emphasizing simplicity in worship and less ritual

Etymology

From 'high' (emphasizing formality or ceremony) + 'church', used since the 17th century in Anglicanism.
