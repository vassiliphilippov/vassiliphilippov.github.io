family Troglodytidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli trəˌɡlɑdəˌtaɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli trɒˌɡlɒdɪˌtaɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of small, mainly New World songbirds known as wrens.
the wren family of birds

Examples

The family Troglodytidae includes around 80 species of wrens found mostly in the Americas.
Birdwatchers enjoy observing members of the family Troglodytidae for their lively songs and energetic behavior.
Research on the family Troglodytidae has provided insights into adaptation and speciation in songbirds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wren - a small brown passerine bird of the family Troglodytidae

Etymology

From New Latin Troglodytidae, based on Genus Troglodytes, meaning 'cave-dweller' from Greek.
