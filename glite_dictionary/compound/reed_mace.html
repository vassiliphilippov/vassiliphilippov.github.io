reed mace

1. NOUN

/ ˈrid ˌmeɪs / (AmE), / ˈriːd ˌmeɪs / (BrE)
a tall marsh plant with long flat leaves and cylindrical seed heads, often used for making mats and chair seats, found in North America, Europe, Asia, and North Africa
tall marsh plant with cylindrical seed heads

Examples

In the wetlands, reed mace grows abundantly along the banks.
The long leaves of reed mace are often woven into traditional mats and chair seats.
Each summer, the brown seed heads of reed mace become covered with fluffy down.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cattail - another name for reed mace, especially in American English
