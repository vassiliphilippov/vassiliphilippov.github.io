Polynesian tattler

1. NOUN

/ pəˌliːniˈʒən ˈtætlər / (AmE), / pɒˌliːniˈziːən ˈtætlə / (BrE)
A small wading bird (Tringa brevipes) found in coastal regions of the Pacific, known for its migratory behavior.
small Pacific coastal wading bird

Examples

The Polynesian tattler is often seen foraging along rocky shores during migration season.
Birdwatchers were excited to spot a rare Polynesian tattler in the bay this morning.
Unlike the wandering tattler, the Polynesian tattler breeds in Alaska and migrates to islands across the Pacific.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wandering tattler - a closely related species of tattler found in Pacific regions

Etymology

'Polynesian' from Polynesia (a region in the Pacific), 'tattler' from its call; scientific name Tringa brevipes.
