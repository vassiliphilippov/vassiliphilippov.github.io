crunch time

1. NOUN

/ krʌntʃ taɪm / (AmE), / krʌntʃ taɪm / (BrE)
a critical period when decisive action or intense effort is required, especially to meet a deadline or achieve a goal
critical period for urgent action
informal american

Examples

The team pulled several all-nighters during crunch time to finish the report.
It's crunch time now—the exam starts in less than an hour!
Coaches often say that crunch time is when true champions rise to the occasion.

Synset Examples

It's crunch time before the project deadline.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deadline - a time or date by which something must be done
  • Synonym: eleventh hour - the last possible moment before something must be completed

Etymology

Metaphorical use of "crunch" (decisive moment) combined with "time"; popularized in American English from the late 20th century.
