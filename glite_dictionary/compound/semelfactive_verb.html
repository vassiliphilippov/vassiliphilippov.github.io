semelfactive verb

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛm.əlˈfæk.tɪv vɜrb / (AmE), / ˌsiː.məlˈfæk.tɪv ˈvɜːb / (BrE)
a verb form that expresses an action occurring once, as opposed to being repeated or continuous
verb for a single, non-repeated action

Examples

The verb 'knock' is often considered a semelfactive verb because it refers to a single act.
Linguists classify 'sneeze' as a semelfactive verb since it represents one event rather than a repeated action.
In language studies, a semelfactive verb is contrasted with one that shows habitual or continuous action.

Synset Examples

Semelfactive verbs include ‘blink’, ‘sneeze’, and ‘knock’.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: iterative verb - verb form expressing repeated or habitual actions

Etymology

From Latin semel (once) + facere (to do) via linguistic terminology.
