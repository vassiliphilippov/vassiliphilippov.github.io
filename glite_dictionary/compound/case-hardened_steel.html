case-hardened steel

1. NOUN

/ ˈkeɪs ˌhɑrdnd stil / (AmE), / ˈkeɪs ˌhɑːdnd stiːl / (BrE)
steel with a hard, wear-resistant surface and a softer, tough interior, produced by hardening only the outer layer
steel with hardened outer surface

Examples

The gears were made of case-hardened steel to increase their durability.
Engineers selected case-hardened steel for the component to resist surface wear.
Most locks use case-hardened steel bolts to prevent tampering.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carburized steel - steel hardened by introducing carbon to the surface
