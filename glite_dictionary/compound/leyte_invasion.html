Leyte invasion

1. NOUN

/ ˈleɪti ɪnˈveɪʒən / (AmE), / ˈleɪti ɪnˈveɪʒən / (BrE)
the Allied landing on Leyte Island in the Philippines in October 1944, marking the return of US forces and the beginning of the liberation of the Philippines during World War II; notable as the first use of kamikaze attacks by Japan.
WWII Allied landing on Leyte Island, Philippines, 1944

Examples

The Leyte invasion marked a turning point in the Pacific War.
General MacArthur famously declared, "I have returned," during the Leyte invasion.
The Japanese first used kamikaze tactics against Allied ships during the Leyte invasion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Battle of Leyte Gulf - major naval battle associated with Leyte invasion in 1944
