genus Aegiceras

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌiː.dʒɪˈsɛr.əs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌiː.dʒɪˈsɛː.ræs / (BrE)
A genus of tropical shrubs or small trees in the family Primulaceae, native to mangrove habitats in Southeast Asia and Australia.
genus of tropical mangrove shrubs/trees

Examples

The genus Aegiceras is adapted to the brackish environments of coastal mangroves.
Scientists discovered a new species within the genus Aegiceras along the northern coast of Australia.
The flowers of the genus Aegiceras are small and fragrant, attracting many kinds of pollinators.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Aegiceras - a genus of tropical trees and shrubs found in mangroves

Etymology

From Greek aigíkēras, meaning 'goat's horn', referring to the shape of the fruit.
