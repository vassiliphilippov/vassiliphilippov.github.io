American cranberry

1. NOUN

/ əˈmɛrɪkən ˈkrænˌbɛri / (AmE), / əˈmɛrɪkən ˈkrænˌbəri / (BrE)
A low-growing, trailing plant native to North America, with small, red, edible berries used in cooking and juices.
North American berry-producing plant

Examples

Wild American cranberry bushes grow in the bogs of New England and Canada.
The tart flavor of the American cranberry makes it perfect for sauces and juices.
Farmers harvest American cranberries in autumn using special wetland techniques.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cranberry - the edible red fruit of several related plants
  • Antonym: European cranberry - a related cranberry species native to Europe

Etymology

From 'cranberry,' after the bird 'crane' (from Dutch 'kraanbes'), and 'American' to indicate origin.
