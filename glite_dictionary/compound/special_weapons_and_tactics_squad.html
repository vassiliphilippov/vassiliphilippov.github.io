Special Weapons and Tactics squad

1. NOUN

/ ˌspɛʃəl ˈwɛpənz ənd ˈtæk.tɪks skwɑd / (AmE), / ˌspɛʃəl ˈwɛpənz ənd ˈtæk.tɪks skwɒd / (BrE)
a police unit specially trained to handle violent and dangerous situations, such as hostage crises or armed standoffs
police unit for dangerous situations

Examples

The city deployed a Special Weapons and Tactics squad to respond to the armed robbery.
A Special Weapons and Tactics squad was called in when negotiations with the suspect failed.
Several members of the Special Weapons and Tactics squad received medals for their bravery during the rescue operation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: SWAT team - a team of police officers trained for high-risk situations

Etymology

Acronym SWAT, from 'Special Weapons and Tactics', first used by U.S. police in the 1960s.
