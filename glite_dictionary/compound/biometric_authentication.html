biometric authentication

1. NOUN

/ ˌbaɪ.oʊˈmɛ.trɪk ɔˌθɛn.tɪ.kəˈʃeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌbaɪ.əʊˈmɛ.trɪk ɔˌθɛn.tɪ.kəˈʃeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the process of automatically identifying individuals by analyzing their unique physiological or behavioral characteristics, such as fingerprints or voice patterns
automatic individual identification using bodily traits

Examples

Many smartphones now use biometric authentication for unlocking the device, such as fingerprint or facial recognition.
Banks have started implementing biometric authentication to increase the security of online transactions.
He prefers biometric authentication over traditional passwords because it is more convenient and secure.

Synset Examples

negative identification can only be accomplished through biometric identification
if a pin or password is lost or forgotten, it can be changed and reissued but a biometric identification cannot

Related Terms

  • Synonym: biometric identification - identifying a person using biometric traits
  • Antonym: password authentication - identifying users by requiring a password

Etymology

biometric from Greek bios (life) + metron (measure) and authentication from Greek authentikos (genuine).
