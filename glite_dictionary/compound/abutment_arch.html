abutment arch

1. NOUN

/ əˈbʌtmənt ɑrtʃ / (AmE), / əˈbʌtmənt ɑːtʃ / (BrE)
An arch that is supported at one or both ends by an abutment, often used to bear the load and transfer it to a supporting structure.
arch supported by an abutment

Examples

The bridge was reinforced with a massive abutment arch on either side to support increased traffic.
Engineers recommended a new abutment arch to stabilize the old masonry structure.
The integrity of the abutment arch was carefully inspected after the earthquake.

Etymology

From 'abutment' (structure supporting the ends of a bridge or arch), from Old French 'abuter' (to join end to end), and 'arch.'
