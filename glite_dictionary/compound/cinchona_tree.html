cinchona tree

1. NOUN

/ sɪnˈtʃoʊnə tri / (AmE), / sɪŋˈkəʊnə triː / (BrE)
A small tree native to Ecuador and Peru, known for its large glossy leaves, fragrant pink flowers, and medicinal bark used to produce quinine.
tree with medicinal bark native to South America

Examples

The bark of the cinchona tree is famous as a natural source of quinine.
Botanists are working to conserve endangered cinchona trees in South America.
The cinchona tree produces fragrant pink flowers and striking glossy leaves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cinchona - the bark or genus from which quinine is obtained

Etymology

From Spanish 'cinchona,' named after the Countess of Chinchón; the genus name given by Linnaeus.
