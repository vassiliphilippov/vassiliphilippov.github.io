Order of the Purple Heart

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdɚ əv ðə ˈpɝːpəl hɑrt / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə əv ðə ˈpɜːpl hɑːt / (BrE)
a United States military decoration awarded to members of the armed forces who are wounded or killed in action against an enemy
U.S. military decoration for wounds in action
american

Examples

He was awarded the Order of the Purple Heart after being injured in combat.
The ceremony honored several veterans who received the Order of the Purple Heart for their bravery.
His Order of the Purple Heart is displayed proudly in his family’s living room.

Etymology

Named for the purple heart-shaped badge introduced by George Washington in 1782; adopted as a military decoration by the U.S. in 1932.
