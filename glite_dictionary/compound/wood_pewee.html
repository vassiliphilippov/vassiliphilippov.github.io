wood pewee

1. NOUN

/ wʊd ˈpiˌwi / (AmE), / wʊd ˈpiːwiː / (BrE)
a small olive-colored flycatcher bird (Contopus virens) of eastern North American woodlands, known for its soft, whistled call
a North American woodland flycatcher bird

Examples

A wood pewee perched on a branch, calling softly in the early morning light.
Many birdwatchers hope to spot a wood pewee during the spring migration.
The olive-brown feathers help the wood pewee blend into its woodland surroundings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pewee - a type of small North American flycatcher

Etymology

From 'wood', referring to its woodland habitat, and 'pewee', imitating its characteristic call.
