California yew

1. NOUN

/ kəˌlɪfɔrniə ju / (AmE), / kəˌlɪfəˈnɪə juː / (BrE)
A small or medium-sized tree of the Pacific coast of North America, known for its fine, hard, close-grained wood.
North American tree with fine, hard wood

Examples

The California yew grows in moist forests along the Pacific coast.
Wood from the California yew is valued for its strength and fine grain.
Botanists are studying the distribution of the California yew in Oregon and Washington.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yew - evergreen tree or shrub with red berries and poisonous foliage
