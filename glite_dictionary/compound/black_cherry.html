black cherry

1. NOUN

/ blæk ˈtʃɛr.i / (AmE), / blak ˈtʃɛr.i / (BrE)
a dark-colored, sweet cherry fruit, especially from certain cultivated cherry trees
a type of sweet dark cherry

Examples

She topped her cheesecake with fresh black cherries for extra sweetness.
The jam was made from ripe black cherries picked in midsummer.
I prefer black cherry flavors in soda and desserts over traditional cherry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cherry - the small, round, typically red or black fruit of various trees of the genus Prunus

2. NOUN

/ blæk ˈtʃɛri / (AmE), / blak ˈtʃɛri / (BrE)
a large North American wild cherry tree (Prunus serotina) with dark, sour, edible fruit; also, the fruit of this tree
North American cherry tree and its dark fruit

Examples

The black cherry is prized for both its dark wood and its tart fruit.
Birds love to eat the ripe black cherry that grows wild in the forest.
She baked a pie using fresh black cherries picked from the tree behind her house.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wild cherry - a cherry tree that is not cultivated, especially Prunus avium or Prunus serotina
