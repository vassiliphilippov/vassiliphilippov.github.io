infinitesimal calculus

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪnfɪnɪˈtɛsəməl ˈkælkjələs / (AmE), / ˌɪnfɪnɪˈtɛsɪməl ˈkælkjʊləs / (BrE)
the branch of mathematics dealing with limits, differentiation, and integration of functions
branch of mathematics (limits, differentiation, integration)

Examples

He studied infinitesimal calculus to better understand how functions change.
The foundations of infinitesimal calculus were developed in the 17th century.
A deep knowledge of infinitesimal calculus is essential for advanced physics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calculus - mathematical study of change (short for infinitesimal calculus)

Etymology

From Latin infinitesimus (infinitely small) and calculus (small stone, reckoning).
