solitary vireo

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɑːlɪˌtɛri ˈvaɪrioʊ / (AmE), / ˈsɒlɪˌtɛəri ˈvɪəriəʊ / (BrE)
A small songbird of eastern North America, with a bluish-grey head and mostly green body, formerly considered a single species but now split into several.
a North American songbird with bluish-grey head

Examples

The solitary vireo is known for its clear, whistled song in the spring forests.
We spotted a solitary vireo during our hike in the Appalachian Mountains.
Unlike other vireos, the solitary vireo often forages alone rather than in flocks.
