state capitalism

1. NOUN

/ steɪt ˈkæpɪˌtælɪzəm / (AmE), / steɪt ˈkapɪtəlɪzəm / (BrE)
an economic system in which the government has significant control of or ownership in business enterprises within a primarily capitalist market.
capitalism with government ownership or control

Examples

China's economy is often described as an example of state capitalism in the modern world.
Critics argue that state capitalism limits true market competition by allowing the government to pick winners and losers.
Some believe state capitalism can combine the strengths of private enterprise with government oversight.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: capitalism - an economic system based on private ownership of capital
  • Degree Higher: socialism - an economic system where the means of production are owned and regulated by the community or state

Etymology

Formed from 'state' (government) + 'capitalism' (private ownership economic system).
