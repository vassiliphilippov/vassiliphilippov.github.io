American maidenhair fern

1. NOUN

/ əˈmɛrɪkən ˈmeɪdnˌhɛr ˌfɜrn / (AmE), / əˈmɛrɪkən ˈmeɪdnheə ˌfɜːn / (BrE)
a hardy North American fern with palmately branched, divergent, and recurved fronds borne on shiny, dark reddish stalks
North American fern with shiny dark stalks

Examples

The delicate fronds of the American maidenhair fern create a lush, green carpet in shaded woodland gardens.
She added an American maidenhair fern to her collection because of its beautiful, shiny reddish stems.
Botanists study the American maidenhair fern to better understand its adaptations to North American climates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maidenhair fern - a type of fern with delicate, divided fronds
