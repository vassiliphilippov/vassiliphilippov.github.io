to terminate the contract

1. VERB

/ ˈtɜːrməˌneɪt ðə ˈkɑːntrækt / (AmE), / ˈtɜːmɪneɪt ðə ˈkɒntrækt / (BrE)
to formally end an agreement or contract before its intended completion
formally end a contract

Examples

After months of disputes, the company decided to terminate the contract with its supplier.
You may be required to pay a penalty if you terminate the contract early.
Both parties agreed to terminate the contract by mutual consent.

Synset Examples

They decided to terminate the contract early.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cancel the contract - to officially make a contract no longer valid or effective
  • Antonym: renew the contract - to extend the duration of a contract
