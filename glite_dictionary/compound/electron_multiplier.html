electron multiplier

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈlɛktrɑn ˈmʌltəˌplaɪər / (AmE), / ɪˈlɛktrɒn ˈmʌltɪˌplaɪə / (BrE)
a device, typically a vacuum tube, that increases the number of electrons in an electric current by means of secondary emission, amplifying the electron flow
vacuum tube that amplifies electron flow

Examples

The electron multiplier detected even the faintest signals during the experiment.
Scientists used an electron multiplier to amplify the output of the photomultiplier tube.
Repairing the electron multiplier improved the sensitivity of the analytical instrument.
