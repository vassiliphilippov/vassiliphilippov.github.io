California black oak

1. NOUN

/ kæləˈfɔrniə blæk oʊk / (AmE), / kælɪˈfɔːniə blæk əʊk / (BrE)
a large deciduous tree (Quercus kelloggii) native to the Pacific coast of North America, known for its deeply lobed, bristle-tipped leaves and dark bark
large Pacific coast oak tree

Examples

The California black oak is a vital habitat for many wildlife species in the Sierra Nevada.
We admired the colorful autumn leaves of the California black oak on our hike.
Logging regulations protect old-growth California black oak forests in this region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: black oak - a species of oak tree with dark bark, typically Quercus velutina or Quercus kelloggii
