Epistle of Paul the Apostle to the Galatians

1. PROPN

/ ɪˈpɪsəl əv ˈpɔl ði əˈpɑsəl tu ðə ɡəˈleɪʃənz / (AmE), / ɪˈpɪsəl əv ˈpɔːl ði əˈpɒsl̩ tuː ðə ɡəˈleɪʃənz / (BrE)
a book of the New Testament containing a letter written by Saint Paul to the Christian communities in Galatia.
New Testament letter by Saint Paul to the Galatians

Examples

Many scholars have studied the Epistle of Paul the Apostle to the Galatians for its theological insights.
The Epistle of Paul the Apostle to the Galatians addresses the issue of faith versus law.
She referenced the Epistle of Paul the Apostle to the Galatians during her Bible study.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Galatians - the biblical book also known as the Epistle to the Galatians

Etymology

Epistle from Greek *epistolē* (letter); Galatians refers to the people of Galatia, an ancient region in Anatolia.
