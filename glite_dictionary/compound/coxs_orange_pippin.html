Cox's Orange Pippin

1. NOUN

/ kɑks ɔːrɪndʒ pɪpɪn / (AmE), / kɒks ɒrɪndʒ pɪpɪn / (BrE)
a famous variety of eating apple with yellow to orange skin and a distinctive, richly aromatic flavor
a variety of eating apple

Examples

The Cox's Orange Pippin is often celebrated for its complex, aromatic flavor among apple enthusiasts.
She bought a basket of Cox's Orange Pippin apples at the farmer's market to make a pie.
Gardeners recommend Cox's Orange Pippin trees for those seeking high-quality dessert apples.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apple - the round fruit of a tree of the rose family, with crisp flesh

Etymology

Named after Richard Cox, the 19th-century English horticulturist who bred it.
