bottlenose whale

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑtəlˌnoʊz weɪl / (AmE), / ˈbɒt.əl.nəʊz weɪl / (BrE)
A large beaked whale of the northern Atlantic, having a bulbous forehead and a prominent beak.
species of beaked whale with bulbous forehead

Examples

The bottlenose whale can be identified by its distinctive bulbous forehead.
Scientists observed a group of bottlenose whales migrating through the cold Atlantic waters.
The bottlenose whale feeds mainly on squid and deep-sea fish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beaked whale - any whale of the family Ziphiidae with a long narrow beak
