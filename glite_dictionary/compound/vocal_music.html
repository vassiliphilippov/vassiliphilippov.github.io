vocal music

1. NOUN

/ ˈvoʊkəl ˈmjuːzɪk / (AmE), / ˈvəʊkəl ˈmjuːzɪk / (BrE)
Music performed by the human voice, with or without instrumental accompaniment, as opposed to purely instrumental music.
music performed by voice

Examples

The choir specializes in vocal music from the Renaissance period.
Opera is a form of vocal music that often tells dramatic stories.
The album features both instrumental tracks and vocal music with lyrics.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: instrumental music - music performed without vocalization; only by musical instruments

2. NOUN

/ ˈvoʊ.kəl ˈmju.zɪk / (AmE), / ˈvəʊ.kəl ˈmjuː.zɪk / (BrE)
music intended to be performed by singers, often with instrumental accompaniment
music performed by singers

Examples

She prefers listening to vocal music rather than instrumental pieces.
The choir performed a moving piece of vocal music at the concert.
Much of classical vocal music features accompaniment by piano or orchestra.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: instrumental music - music performed primarily with instruments, without singing
