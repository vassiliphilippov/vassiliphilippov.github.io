common seal

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑm.ən sil / (AmE), / ˈkɒm.ən siːl / (BrE)
a small, spotted seal (Phoca vitulina) inhabiting the coastal waters of the Northern Hemisphere.
small spotted seal of the Northern Hemisphere

Examples

The common seal is often seen resting on sandbanks along the British coastline.
Researchers are studying the migration patterns of the common seal in the North Atlantic.
A group of common seals basked on the rocks as the tide came in.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: harbour seal - alternative name for the common seal (Phoca vitulina)
