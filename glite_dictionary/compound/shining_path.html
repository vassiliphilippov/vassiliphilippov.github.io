Shining Path

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃaɪnɪŋ pæθ / (AmE), / ˈʃaɪnɪŋ pɑːθ / (BrE)
a Maoist insurgent group in Peru, known for acts of terrorism and guerrilla warfare, founded in the late 1960s and infamous for its brutality
Peruvian Maoist terrorist group

Examples

The Peruvian government launched a major offensive against the Shining Path in the 1980s.
International reports highlighted the brutal tactics used by the Shining Path.
Despite major losses, some Shining Path members continued to operate in remote regions.

Synset Examples

Shining Path has been responsible for 30,000 deaths

Etymology

Translation of Spanish 'Sendero Luminoso', the official name of the group.
