sedimentary clay

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛd.əˈmɛn.tə.ri kleɪ / (AmE), / ˌsɛd.ɪˈmɛn.təri kleɪ / (BrE)
Clay that has been formed by the accumulation and compaction of mineral or organic particles deposited by water, wind, or ice.
clay formed by sedimentary deposits

Examples

The archaeologist discovered a pot made from sedimentary clay along the river bank.
Farmers in the region struggle to grow certain crops because the soil contains a high proportion of sedimentary clay.
When wet, sedimentary clay becomes smooth and sticky, making it useful for ceramics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clay - a stiff, sticky fine-grained earth

Etymology

From sedimentary (from Latin 'sedimentum', settling) + clay.
