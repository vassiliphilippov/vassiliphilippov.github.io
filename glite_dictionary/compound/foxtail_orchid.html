foxtail orchid

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɑks.teɪl ˈɔr.kɪd / (AmE), / ˈfɒks.teɪl ˈɔː.kɪd / (BrE)
A tropical orchid of the genus Rhynchostylis, especially Rhynchostylis gigantea, characterized by dense cylindrical clusters of pink or white flowers often marked with purple.
orchid with dense foxtail-like flower clusters

Examples

The foxtail orchid is prized for its long, fragrant flower clusters.
Gardeners often cultivate foxtail orchids for their vibrant display during the blooming season.
The foxtail orchid thrives in warm, humid climates with filtered sunlight.
