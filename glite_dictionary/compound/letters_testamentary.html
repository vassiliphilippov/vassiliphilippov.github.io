letters testamentary

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛtərz tɛstəˈmɛnˌtɛri / (AmE), / ˈlɛtəz tɛstəˈmɛntri / (BrE)
an official legal document issued by a probate court authorizing a person to act as the executor of a deceased person's will
document authorizing executor of a will
legal

Examples

The executor presented the letters testamentary to the bank to access the deceased's accounts.
Without letters testamentary, she was unable to fulfill her father's last wishes legally.
The court issued letters testamentary after confirming the validity of the will.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: probate - the official proving of a will

Etymology

From Latin 'testamentarius' (of a will); 'letters' in legal sense meaning 'official documents'.
