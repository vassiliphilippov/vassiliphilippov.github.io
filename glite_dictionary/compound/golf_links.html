golf links

1. NOUN

/ ɡɑlf lɪŋks / (AmE), / ɡɒlf lɪŋks / (BrE)
A golf course situated on sandy, coastal ground, often with natural undulating terrain and little tree cover.
coastal golf course on sandy ground

Examples

The championship was held at the historic golf links overlooking the North Sea.
She enjoyed walking along the windswept golf links even when she wasn't playing golf.
Many traditional Scottish golf links are famous for their challenging natural terrain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: golf course - a large area of land designed for playing golf

Etymology

From 'golf' and 'links', the latter from Scottish, meaning sandy, undulating ground near the sea.
