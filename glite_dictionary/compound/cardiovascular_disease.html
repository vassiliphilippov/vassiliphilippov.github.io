cardiovascular disease

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːrdioʊˈvæskjələr dɪˈziːz / (AmE), / ˌkɑːdiəʊˈvæskjʊlə dɪˈziːz / (BrE)
A disease affecting the heart or blood vessels.
disease of the heart or blood vessels
medical

Examples

People with cardiovascular disease are at higher risk for heart attacks and strokes.
Regular exercise and a healthy diet can help prevent cardiovascular disease.
Smoking is a major risk factor for developing cardiovascular disease.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heart disease - any disorder that affects the heart's function
  • Synonym: stroke - a sudden interruption in the blood supply to the brain
  • Synonym: hypertension - high blood pressure, a common cardiovascular condition

Etymology

From Latin 'cardia' (heart) and vasculum (small vessel) + disease.
