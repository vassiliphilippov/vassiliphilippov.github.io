parol evidence rule

1. NOUN

/ pəˈroʊl ˈɛvɪdəns ruːl / (AmE), / pəˈrəʊl ˈɛvɪdəns ruːl / (BrE)
A legal rule stating that oral statements cannot be used to contradict, vary, or add to the terms of a written contract.
legal rule about oral evidence and contracts
legal

Examples

The parol evidence rule prevented the court from considering the witness's oral statements about the agreement.
According to the parol evidence rule, only the written contract terms are legally binding.
Lawyers often cite the parol evidence rule when disputes arise over ambiguous contract clauses.

Etymology

From Anglo-French 'parol' (word, speech) and 'evidence' (testimony); legal usage since the 18th century.
