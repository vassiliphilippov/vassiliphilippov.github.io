Justinian code

1. NOUN

/ dʒʌˈstɪniən koʊd / (AmE), / dʒʌˈstɪniən kəʊd / (BrE)
the body of Roman civil law codified under Emperor Justinian I in the 6th century, forming the foundation of many modern civil law systems
Roman civil law code under Justinian I
legal

Examples

The influence of the Justinian code can still be seen in legal systems throughout Europe.
Law students studied the Justinian code to understand the roots of modern civil law.
The compilation of the Justinian code marked a turning point in the history of jurisprudence.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: civil law - legal system derived from Roman law and codified statutes

Etymology

Named after Emperor Justinian I of Byzantium (6th century).
