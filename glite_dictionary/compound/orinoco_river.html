Orinoco River

1. PROPN

/ ɔːrɪˈnoʊkoʊ ˈrɪvər / (AmE), / ɒrɪˈnəʊkəʊ ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
a major river in northern South America, flowing approximately 1,500 miles through Venezuela and Colombia into the Atlantic Ocean
major river in northern South America

Examples

The Orinoco River is one of South America's longest and most important waterways.
Many unique species can be found in the basin of the Orinoco River.
The expedition followed the Orinoco River upstream to explore remote regions of Venezuela.

Etymology

From Spanish Orinoco, likely from an indigenous language.
