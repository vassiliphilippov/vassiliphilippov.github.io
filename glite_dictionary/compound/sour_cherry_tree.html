sour cherry tree

1. NOUN

/ saʊɚ ˈtʃɛri tri / (AmE), / saʊə ˈtʃɛri triː / (BrE)
a small tree of Eurasian origin that produces tart, edible red to black fruit commonly used in cooking and preserves
tree bearing tart red to black cherries

Examples

The sour cherry tree in our backyard yields fruit perfect for jam making each summer.
Farmers often cultivate sour cherry trees for their distinctive, tangy fruit used in pies and desserts.
Botanists have identified several varieties of sour cherry tree native to Eurasia.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sweet cherry tree - tree producing sweet edible cherries
