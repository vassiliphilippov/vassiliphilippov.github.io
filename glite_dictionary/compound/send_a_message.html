to send a message

1. VERB

/ sɛnd ə ˈmɛsɪdʒ / (AmE), / sɛnd ə ˈmɛsɪdʒ / (BrE)
to convey a particular idea, intention, or attitude, usually indirectly or through actions rather than explicit words
convey an idea or intention

Examples

Her resignation was meant to send a message to the company's management.
Wearing casual clothes to the interview might send a message that you’re not serious about the job.
The government’s new policy is designed to send a message to potential offenders.

Synset Examples

Her outfit was meant to send a message.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: signal - an action or gesture that conveys a message
  • Synonym: to communicate - to impart or convey information or ideas
