butanoic acid

1. NOUN

/ bjuːˈteɪnoʊɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / bjuːˈteɪnəʊɪk ˈasɪd / (BrE)
a fatty acid with an unpleasant odor, found in butter and some other fats
a fatty acid found in butter

Examples

Rancid butter contains high amounts of butanoic acid, which gives it an unpleasant odor.
Cheddar cheese owes some of its distinctive flavor to butanoic acid.
During fermentation, butanoic acid can be produced by certain bacteria.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: butyric acid - another name for butanoic acid

Etymology

From 'butane' (a four-carbon alkane) and '-oic acid' suffix used in organic acids.
