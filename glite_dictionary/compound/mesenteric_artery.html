mesenteric artery

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛsənˈtɛrɪk ˈɑːrtɛri / (AmE), / ˌmɛsənˈtɛrɪk ˈɑːtəri / (BrE)
either of two major arteries branching from the aorta and supplying blood to the intestines through the mesentery
major artery supplying blood to the intestines
medical

Examples

The surgeon identified the mesenteric artery to prevent accidental injury during the operation.
A blockage in the mesenteric artery can lead to serious intestinal problems.
Doctors were concerned about reduced blood flow through the mesenteric artery.

Etymology

From 'mesentery', from Greek 'mesenterion' (middle intestine), and 'artery', from Greek 'artēria'.
