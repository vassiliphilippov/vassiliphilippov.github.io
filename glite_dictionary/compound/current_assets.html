current assets

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɝː.ənt ˈæs.ɛts / (AmE), / ˈkʌr.ənt ˈæs.ɛts / (BrE)
assets consisting of cash or other resources that can be easily converted to cash within one year, such as inventory or accounts receivable
assets easily converted to cash within a year

Examples

The company's current assets include cash, inventory, and accounts receivable.
To qualify for the loan, they had to provide a list of their current assets.
Strong current assets are essential for maintaining good liquidity.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fixed assets - assets not easily converted into cash, such as property or equipment
  • Synonym: liquid assets - assets readily convertible to cash
