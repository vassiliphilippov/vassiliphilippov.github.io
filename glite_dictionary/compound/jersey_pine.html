Jersey pine

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɝzi paɪn / (AmE), / ˈdʒɜːzi paɪn / (BrE)
A small, shrubby pine tree of the eastern United States with twisted branches and short needles in clusters of two.
a shrubby eastern US pine tree

Examples

The Jersey pine thrives in sandy soils and open woodlands of New Jersey.
Some gardeners plant Jersey pine as a windbreak because of its hardy nature.
We identified several Jersey pines along the edge of the forest trail.
