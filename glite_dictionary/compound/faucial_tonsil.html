faucial tonsil

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔʃəl ˈtɑn.səl / (AmE), / ˈfɔː.ʃi.əl ˈtɒn.sɪl / (BrE)
either of two masses of lymphatic tissue, one on each side of the oral pharynx, commonly known as the palatine tonsils
mass of lymphatic tissue in the throat
medical

Examples

The doctor examined her faucial tonsil for signs of infection.
Swelling of the faucial tonsil can cause a sore throat and difficulty swallowing.
Surgery to remove a faucial tonsil is known as a tonsillectomy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: palatine tonsil - either of a pair of oval masses of lymphoid tissue on either side of the pharynx

Etymology

From Latin 'faux, faucis' (throat) and 'tonsilla' (tonsil).
