fertile soil

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɜrtəl ˈsɔɪl / (AmE), / ˈfɜːtaɪl ˈsɔɪl / (BrE)
soil that is rich in nutrients and able to support abundant plant growth
nutrient-rich, productive soil

Examples

Farmers prefer to cultivate crops in fertile soil because it yields better harvests.
The scientist tested the fertile soil for its mineral content.
Centuries of volcanic ash have created fertile soil in this region.

Synset Examples

The region is known for its fertile soil.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: barren soil - soil with few nutrients, unable to support plant growth
