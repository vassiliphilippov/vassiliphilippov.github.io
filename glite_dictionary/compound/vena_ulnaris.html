vena ulnaris

1. NOUN

/ ˌviːnə ʌlˈnerɪs / (AmE), / ˌviːnə ʌlˈnɛːrɪs / (BrE)
any of several veins in the forearm that accompany the ulnar artery; part of the venous system draining blood from the forearm.
a vein of the forearm
medical

Examples

The surgeon located the vena ulnaris before beginning the procedure.
Damage to the vena ulnaris can lead to complications when drawing blood from the forearm.
Understanding the position of the vena ulnaris is important in medical anatomy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ulnar vein - any vein that accompanies the ulnar artery in the forearm

Etymology

From Latin 'vena' (vein) and 'ulnaris' (of the ulna, the forearm bone).
