family Pygopodidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli paɪˌɡɑpəˈdɪdi / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli paɪˌɡɒpəˈdɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of legless lizards native to Australia and Tasmania, known for their snake-like appearance and belonging to the order Squamata.
Australian and Tasmanian legless lizard family

Examples

The family Pygopodidae includes about 45 species of legless lizards native to Australia.
Scientists are studying the evolutionary adaptations of the family Pygopodidae to understand their snake-like features.
Unlike true snakes, members of the family Pygopodidae retain vestigial hind limbs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: legless lizard - a lizard with no limbs or only rudimentary limbs
  • Degree Lower: gecko - a type of small lizard related to the family Pygopodidae

Etymology

From scientific Latin 'Pygopodidae', based on Greek 'pyg-' (rump) and 'pous, podos' (foot), alluding to the lizards’ vestigial limbs.
