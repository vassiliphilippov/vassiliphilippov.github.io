nautch dance

1. NOUN

/ nɔtʃ dæns / (AmE), / nɔːtʃ dɑːns / (BrE)
an intricate traditional dance from India, typically performed by professional female dancers at social and festive occasions
traditional Indian dance by professional female dancers

Examples

The graceful moves of the nautch dance captivated everyone at the royal gathering.
In colonial India, British officials were often entertained by performances of nautch dance.
She spent years perfecting her skills to excel in the art of nautch dance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: devadasi - female temple dancer in India

Etymology

From Hindi 'nāc' (dance), ultimately from Sanskrit 'nṛtya' (dance).
