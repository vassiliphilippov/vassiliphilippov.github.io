Bush administration

1. NOUN

/ bʊʃ ˌædmɪnɪˈstreɪʃən / (AmE), / bʊʃ ədˌmɪnɪˈstreɪʃən / (BrE)
the executive branch of the United States government during the presidency of George W. Bush (2001–2009)
US government under President George W. Bush

Examples

The Bush administration implemented significant changes to US foreign policy after the events of September 11, 2001.
Critics often debate the environmental policies of the Bush administration in comparison to other recent presidencies.
The Bush administration faced challenges managing the aftermath of Hurricane Katrina.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Obama administration - the US executive under President Barack Obama
  • Antonym: Clinton administration - the US executive under President Bill Clinton

2. NOUN

/ bʊʃ ædˌmɪnəˈstreɪʃən / (AmE), / bʊʃ ədˌmɪnɪˈstreɪʃən / (BrE)
the executive branch of the United States government under President George H. W. Bush, who served from 1989 to 1993
U.S. presidency of George H. W. Bush (1989–93)

Examples

Many foreign policy decisions made by the Bush administration had long-term effects on international relations.
Environmental regulation shifted significantly during the Bush administration.
Historians often debate the economic legacy of the Bush administration.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Clinton administration - the executive branch under President Bill Clinton
  • Antonym: Reagan administration - the executive branch under President Ronald Reagan
