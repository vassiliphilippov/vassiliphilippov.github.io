evergreen grass

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛv.ɚˌɡrin ˌɡræs / (AmE), / ˈɛvəˌɡriːn ˌɡrɑːs / (BrE)
A coarse, perennial grass native to Eurasia, resembling oat, commonly found on roadside verges, rough grassland, and hay meadows; introduced to North America for forage.
coarse perennial grass, Eurasian origin

Examples

The field was covered in evergreen grass that provided lush grazing for livestock year-round.
Farmers in North America sometimes plant evergreen grass as a forage crop due to its hardiness.
Roadside verges in Eurasia are often dominated by evergreen grass, forming dense green patches even in winter.
