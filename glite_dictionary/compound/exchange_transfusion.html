exchange transfusion

1. NOUN

/ ɪksˈtʃeɪndʒ trænzˈfjuːʒən / (AmE), / ɪksˈtʃeɪndʒ trænsˈfjuːʒ(ə)n / (BrE)
a medical procedure in which a person's blood is slowly removed and replaced with an equal amount of donor blood
blood replacement procedure
medical

Examples

The infant underwent an exchange transfusion to treat severe jaundice.
After complications arose, the doctors opted for an exchange transfusion procedure.
Exchange transfusion is sometimes necessary in cases of acute blood poisoning.

Etymology

From 'exchange' (to give and receive in return) and 'transfusion' (from Latin transfusio, pouring from one to another).
