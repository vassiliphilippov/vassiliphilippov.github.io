sodium tripolyphosphate

1. NOUN

/ ˈsoʊdiəm traɪˌpɑliˈfɑsfeɪt / (AmE), / ˈsəʊdiəm traɪˌpɒlɪˈfɒsfeɪt / (BrE)
a sodium salt of triphosphoric acid, used as a builder in soaps and detergents to enhance cleaning efficiency
chemical used in detergents
medical

Examples

The ingredient list showed that sodium tripolyphosphate was added to improve the detergent's cleaning power.
Environmental regulations often limit the amount of sodium tripolyphosphate in wastewater due to its potential to cause algal blooms.
Researchers analyzed the effectiveness of sodium tripolyphosphate in various soap formulations.

Etymology

Formed from sodium + tri- (three) + polyphosphate (salt of polyphosphoric acid).
