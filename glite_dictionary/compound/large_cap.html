large cap

1. NOUN

/ lɑrdʒ kæp / (AmE), / lɑːdʒ kæp / (BrE)
a company with a large market capitalization, typically valued at over $10 billion
company with large market capitalization

Examples

Many investors prefer to put their money into large cap companies for stability.
The fund is heavily weighted towards large cap stocks like Apple and Microsoft.
After the merger, the company quickly became a large cap in the industry.

Synset Examples

he works for a large cap

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: mid cap - a company with a medium market capitalization
  • Degree Lower: small cap - a company with a small market capitalization

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'large capitalization', where capitalization refers to market value of a company's outstanding shares.
