religious movement

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈlɪdʒəs ˈmuːvmənt / (AmE), / rɪˈlɪdʒəs ˈmuːvmənt / (BrE)
a group or organized effort seeking to bring about reforms or significant changes in religious beliefs or practices
organized effort for religious reform

Examples

The Protestant Reformation was a significant religious movement that changed the course of European history.
Many young people joined the new religious movement seeking spiritual renewal and social change.
The rise of the environmental religious movement reflects modern concerns about faith and ecology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: revival - renewed religious interest or awakening within a faith community
  • Synonym: sect - a subgroup of a religion with distinct beliefs or practices, often emerging from a movement
