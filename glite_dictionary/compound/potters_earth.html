potter's earth

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑtərz ɝθ / (AmE), / ˈpɒtəz ɜːθ / (BrE)
A type of clay that contains no iron, used in making pottery or for modeling.
iron-free clay for pottery or modeling

Examples

The sculptor preferred to use potter's earth because it was easy to shape and did not stain.
Many ancient vessels were crafted from finely purified potter's earth.
She bought a large bag of potter's earth for her ceramics class.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clay - fine-grained natural soil material containing clay minerals
