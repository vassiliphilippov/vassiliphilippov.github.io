deep end

1. NOUN

/ dip ɛnd / (AmE), / diːp end / (BrE)
A situation that is difficult or challenging, often one in which someone is inexperienced or unprepared.
a difficult or challenging situation

Examples

When she started her new job, she was immediately in the deep end with several big projects.
He likes being in the deep end because it pushes him to grow.
New teachers often find themselves in the deep end during their first week.

Synset Examples

In the deep end, you learn to adapt quickly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thrown in at the deep end - put into a challenging situation without preparation
