Old Irish language

1. NOUN

/ oʊld ˈaɪrɪʃ ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / əʊld ˈaɪrɪʃ ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
The version of the Irish Gaelic language spoken and written in Ireland up to about 1100 AD.
early form of Irish Gaelic (to c. 1100)

Examples

Manuscripts written in Old Irish language give us insights into early Irish culture.
She is studying the grammar of the Old Irish language for her thesis.
Many place names in Ireland have their roots in the Old Irish language.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Middle Irish language - Form of Irish Gaelic spoken from 1100 to 1200
  • Degree Higher: Irish language - Modern-day Irish Gaelic language
  • Synonym: Gaelic - Celtic languages spoken in Ireland and Scotland

Etymology

Old Irish from 'Goídelc', the native term for the language, with 'Old' marking its earliest documented period.
