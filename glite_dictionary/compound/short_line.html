short line

1. NOUN

/ ʃɔrt laɪn / (AmE), / ʃɔːt laɪn / (BrE)
a railroad or transportation route, usually privately owned, that operates over a relatively small or limited distance, often connecting with main lines
small railroad or transit route

Examples

The company purchased a short line to serve local industries.
Many short lines connect rural towns to major rail networks.
After the main railroad was abandoned, a short line took over freight service.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: branch line - a secondary railway line branching off a main line
