genus Marmota

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs mɑːrˈmoʊtə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs mɑːˈməʊtə / (BrE)
A genus of large ground squirrels known as marmots, found in North America, Europe, and Asia.
genus of marmots (large ground squirrels)

Examples

The genus Marmota includes several species adapted to cold mountainous regions.
Biologists have studied the hibernation patterns of animals within the genus Marmota.
Alpine marmots, part of the genus Marmota, are well-known for their loud warning whistles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marmot - any burrowing rodent of the genus Marmota

Etymology

From Latin 'marmota', meaning marmot.
