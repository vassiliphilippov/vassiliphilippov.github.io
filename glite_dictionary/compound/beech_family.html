beech family

1. NOUN

/ biːtʃ ˈfæməli / (AmE), / biːtʃ ˈfæmɪli / (BrE)
a family of mainly monoecious trees and shrubs, including beeches, chestnuts, and oaks (family Fagaceae)
family of trees including beeches, chestnuts, and oaks

Examples

The beech family includes many important hardwood trees found across the Northern Hemisphere.
Chestnuts, part of the beech family, produce edible nuts that are popular in many cuisines.
Oaks are perhaps the most familiar members of the beech family in temperate regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Fagaceae - the botanical name for the beech family
  • Degree Lower: oak - a common member of the beech family
  • Degree Lower: beech - a genus within the beech family
  • Degree Lower: chestnut - a genus within the beech family
