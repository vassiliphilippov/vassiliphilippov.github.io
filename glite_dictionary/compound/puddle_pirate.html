puddle pirate

1. NOUN

/ ˈpʌd.əl ˌpaɪ.rət / (AmE), / ˈpʌd.əl ˌpaɪ.rət / (BrE)
a humorous or playful term for a child pretending to be a treasure hunter or playing adventurously in puddles
child playing adventurously in puddles

Examples

On rainy afternoons, the children loved being puddle pirates in their bright rubber boots.
She watched her little brother run through the water, declaring himself a puddle pirate.
After the storm passed, the street was full of happy puddle pirates splashing and laughing.

Synset Examples

He jokingly called himself a puddle pirate on rainy days.
The kids pretended to be puddle pirates in the backyard.
The puddle pirates stole our toys by the river!
