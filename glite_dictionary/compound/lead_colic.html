lead colic

1. NOUN

/ lɛd ˈkɑlɪk / (AmE), / lɛd ˈkɒlɪk / (BrE)
Persistent abdominal pain and constipation resulting from chronic lead poisoning.
abdominal pain from lead poisoning
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with lead colic after reporting severe abdominal pain and constipation.
Historically, factory workers were at risk of developing lead colic due to chronic exposure to lead.
Doctors recognized lead colic as a key symptom of prolonged lead poisoning in the 19th century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lead poisoning - toxicity caused by absorption of lead into the body

Etymology

From 'lead', the heavy metal, and 'colic' from Greek 'kōlikos' (pertaining to the colon).
