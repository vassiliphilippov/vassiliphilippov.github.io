hard of hearing

1. ADJ

/ hɑrd əv ˈhɪrɪŋ / (AmE), / hɑːd əv ˈhɪərɪŋ / (BrE)
having partial loss of hearing; unable to hear well but not completely deaf
partially unable to hear

Examples

My uncle is hard of hearing, so he often turns the television volume up very high.
Special devices can help students who are hard of hearing participate in the classroom.
Being hard of hearing can sometimes make it difficult to follow conversations in crowded places.

Synset Examples

He speaks loudly because his friend is hard of hearing.
Being hard of hearing makes group discussions challenging.
My grandmother is hard of hearing but still enjoys conversations.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: deaf - unable to hear at all
  • Degree Lower: hearing - having normal hearing ability
