muscle spasm

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌsəl ˌspæzəm / (AmE), / ˈmʌsəl ˌspæzəm / (BrE)
a sudden, involuntary contraction of a muscle, often causing pain or discomfort
involuntary, painful muscle contraction
medical

Examples

After a long run, he experienced a painful muscle spasm in his calf.
Overuse of a particular muscle can sometimes lead to a sudden muscle spasm.
The muscle spasm in her back made it difficult to stand upright.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cramp - a painful involuntary muscle contraction
