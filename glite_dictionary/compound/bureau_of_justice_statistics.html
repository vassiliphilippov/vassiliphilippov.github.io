Bureau of Justice Statistics

1. NOUN

/ bjʊˈɹoʊ əv ˈdʒʌstɪs stəˈtɪstɪks / (AmE), / bjʊəˈrəʊ əv ˈdʒʌstɪs stəˈtɪstɪks / (BrE)
the agency within the U.S. Department of Justice responsible for collecting and analyzing criminal justice statistics for federal and local policy makers
U.S. agency for criminal justice statistics

Examples

The Bureau of Justice Statistics publishes annual reports on crime rates across the United States.
According to the Bureau of Justice Statistics, the overall incarceration rate has declined in recent years.
Researchers often rely on data from the Bureau of Justice Statistics for comprehensive information on criminal justice trends.
