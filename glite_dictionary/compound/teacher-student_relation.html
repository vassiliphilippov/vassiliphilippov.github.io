teacher-student relation

1. NOUN

/ ˈtiː.tʃər ˈstuː.dənt rɪˈleɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˈtiː.tʃə ˈstjuː.dənt rɪˈleɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the formal academic relationship between teachers and their students, typically involving instruction, supervision, and learning
academic relationship between teacher and student

Examples

The teacher-student relation plays a crucial role in academic success and personal development.
Maintaining a professional boundary in the teacher-student relation is essential for ethical education.
Studies have shown that a positive teacher-student relation can improve classroom engagement and motivation.
