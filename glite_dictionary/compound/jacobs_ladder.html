Jacob's ladder

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒeɪkəbz ˌlædər / (AmE), / ˈdʒeɪkəbz ˌlædə / (BrE)
a European perennial plant with pinnate leaves and clusters of bright blue or white flowers, especially of the genus Polemonium
a plant with blue or white flowers (Polemonium)

Examples

The Jacob's ladder in my garden produces delicate blue flowers every spring.
She identified the plant as a Jacob's ladder because of its distinctive leaf pattern.
Gardeners often grow Jacob's ladder for its attractive foliage and blossoms.

Etymology

Named for the ladder-like arrangement of its leaves, alluding to the biblical story of Jacob's dream (Genesis 28:12).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʒeɪkəbz ˈlædər / (AmE), / ˈdʒeɪkəbz ˈladə / (BrE)
a hanging ladder made of ropes or chains with wooden or metal rungs, used especially on ships
nautical rope or chain ladder

Examples

The sailors quickly climbed the Jacob's ladder to reach the ship's deck.
For safety drills, the crew practiced descending the Jacob's ladder into the lifeboat.
A Jacob's ladder was lowered over the side to help the inspectors board the vessel.

Etymology

Named after the biblical ladder seen by Jacob in the Book of Genesis.
