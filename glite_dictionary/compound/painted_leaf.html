painted leaf

1. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪntɪd lif / (AmE), / ˈpeɪntɪd liːf / (BrE)
a tropical American plant with poisonous milky sap and bright, usually scarlet, petal-like leaves around small yellow flowers
tropical plant with scarlet petal-like leaves

Examples

The painted leaf adds a vibrant splash of color to the garden with its striking red bracts.
Gardeners should handle the painted leaf carefully, as its milky sap is toxic.
In tropical regions, the painted leaf is often grown as an ornamental plant.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪntɪd liːf / (AmE), / ˈpeɪntɪd liːf / (BrE)
A common name for the poinsettia, a plant native to the United States and eastern Mexico, known for its brightly colored leaves.
poinsettia plant with colorful leaves

Examples

The painted leaf adds a vibrant splash of color to winter gardens.
Many people use the painted leaf as a festive decoration during the holidays.
In its natural habitat, the painted leaf blooms in late autumn.
