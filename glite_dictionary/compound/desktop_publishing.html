desktop publishing

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɛskˌtɑp ˈpʌblɪʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌdɛsktɒp ˈpʌblɪʃɪŋ / (BrE)
the creation of printed materials by using personal computers equipped with graphics capabilities and specialized software
creation of printed materials using computers

Examples

Advances in desktop publishing have made it easier for small businesses to create brochures and newsletters.
She used desktop publishing software to design the organization's annual report.
Before desktop publishing, producing professional-looking documents required much more specialized equipment.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: publishing - the business or activity of preparing and issuing books, journals, or other material for sale

Etymology

Formed from 'desktop' (referring to desktop computers) and 'publishing' (from publish).
