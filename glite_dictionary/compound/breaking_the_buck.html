breaking the buck

1. NOUN

/ ˈbreɪkɪŋ ðə bʌk / (AmE), / ˈbreɪkɪŋ ðə bʌk / (BrE)
(of a money market fund) to fall below a net asset value of one dollar per share, indicating a loss in principal for investors.
fall below $1 in money market fund value

Examples

During the 2008 financial crisis, several funds feared breaking the buck and took emergency action.
Money market funds are heavily regulated to prevent breaking the buck and protect investors.
If a fund starts to approach breaking the buck, investors may rush to withdraw their money.

Synset Examples

Some funds aim to avoid breaking the buck under pressure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: default - failure to fulfill a financial obligation
