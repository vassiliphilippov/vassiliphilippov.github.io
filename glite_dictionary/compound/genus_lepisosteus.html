genus Lepisosteus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒinəs lɛpɪsˈɑstɪəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs lɛpɪsˈɒstiːəs / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Lepisosteidae, comprising freshwater gar fishes found in North and Central America.
type genus of freshwater gars

Examples

The genus Lepisosteus includes several species of gars native to North America.
Members of the genus Lepisosteus are known for their elongated jaws and prehistoric appearance.
Researchers studied the distribution of the genus Lepisosteus in freshwater lakes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gar - a predatory freshwater fish with a long body and snout

Etymology

From Latin 'genus' (kind) and 'Lepisosteus', from Greek 'lepis' (scale) and 'osteon' (bone).
