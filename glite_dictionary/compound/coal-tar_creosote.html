coal-tar creosote

1. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊl tɑr kriˈoʊsoʊt / (AmE), / ˈkəʊl tɑː ˈkriːəsəʊt / (BrE)
A dark, oily liquid obtained by distillation of coal tar, used especially as a preservative for wood.
dark oily wood preservative from coal tar

Examples

Old railroad ties were treated with coal-tar creosote to prevent rot and insect damage.
Workers handling coal-tar creosote must wear protective gear due to its toxic properties.
Traditionally, coal-tar creosote was applied to utility poles to prolong their lifespan.

Etymology

Creosote from Greek *kreas* (flesh) + *sōtēr* (preserver); coal-tar indicates its source.
