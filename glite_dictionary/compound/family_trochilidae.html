family Trochilidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli troʊˈkɪlɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli trəʊˈkɪlɪˌdiː / (BrE)
The taxonomic family of small, vibrant, fast-flying birds commonly known as hummingbirds, found throughout the Americas.
the hummingbird family (taxonomy)

Examples

The family Trochilidae includes over 300 species of hummingbirds across the Americas.
Scientists have studied the rapid wing movements unique to the family Trochilidae.
The vibrant plumage of birds in the family Trochilidae makes them popular with wildlife observers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hummingbird - small, fast-flying bird of the Trochilidae family

Etymology

From Latin *Trochilus* (type genus), with Greek *trochilos* meaning 'small bird.'
