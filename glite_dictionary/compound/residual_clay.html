residual clay

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈzɪdʒuəl kleɪ / (AmE), / rɪˈzɪdjʊəl kleɪ / (BrE)
clay soil that remains in place after soluble elements have been dissolved and removed, typically by weathering processes
clay left after soluble elements are dissolved

Examples

The hillside is covered in residual clay that resulted from centuries of rock weathering.
Farmers found that crops grew poorly in areas with thick deposits of residual clay.
Geologists collected samples of residual clay to study mineral leaching in the region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clay - a natural, earthy material that is plastic when wet and hardens when heated
