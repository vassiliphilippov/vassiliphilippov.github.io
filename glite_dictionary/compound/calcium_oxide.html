calcium oxide

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæl.si.əm ˈɑːk.saɪd / (AmE), / ˈkalsɪəm ˈɒksaɪd / (BrE)
A white, caustic, crystalline solid used chiefly in the manufacture of calcium hydroxide and as a component in construction and chemical industries.
white, caustic chemical compound (CaO)

Examples

Industrial furnaces often use calcium oxide as a flux to remove impurities from metals.
Exposure to calcium oxide dust can cause irritation to the eyes and respiratory tract.
When water is added to calcium oxide, it produces calcium hydroxide, releasing considerable heat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quicklime - another name for calcium oxide
  • Degree Higher: calcium hydroxide - a compound formed by adding water to calcium oxide

Etymology

From 'calcium', named for Latin calx (lime) and 'oxide', from Greek oxys (sharp, acid) + -ide.
