second gear

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛkənd ɡɪr / (AmE), / ˈsɛkənd ɡɪə / (BrE)
the gear in a vehicle's gearbox providing the second lowest forward gear ratio, used after first gear for moderate speeds and increased power
second lowest forward gear in a vehicle's gearbox

Examples

Once the car started moving, she smoothly shifted into second gear.
Driving uphill, he kept the engine in second gear for better power.
After slowing down for the turn, you should change to second gear before accelerating.

Synset Examples

he had to shift down into second to make the hill

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: first gear - the gear with the lowest forward ratio in a gearbox
  • Degree Higher: third gear - the gear with the third lowest forward ratio in a gearbox

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɛkənd ɡɪr / (AmE), / ˈsɛkənd ɡɪə / (BrE)
the gear in a vehicle's transmission that is used for moderate speed, providing more power than first gear but less than higher gears
gear for moderate speed in a vehicle

Examples

The car struggled to climb the hill in second gear.
Change to second gear as you approach the sharp bend.
He shifted smoothly from first to second gear after starting the engine.

Synset Examples

The car struggled to move in second gear on the hill.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: first gear - gear in a vehicle used for starting and very low speeds
  • Degree Higher: third gear - gear in a vehicle used for higher speeds than second gear
