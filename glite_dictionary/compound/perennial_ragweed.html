perennial ragweed

1. NOUN

/ pəˈrɛniəl ˈræɡˌwid / (AmE), / pəˈrɛniəl ˈræɡwiːd / (BrE)
a coarse, perennial plant with creeping roots found in dry, barren lands of the southwestern United States and Mexico
a perennial plant native to southwestern U.S. and Mexico

Examples

Perennial ragweed thrives in dry, disturbed soils across the southwestern United States.
Allergic reactions are sometimes triggered by the pollen from perennial ragweed.
Farmers often struggle to control the spread of perennial ragweed in their fields.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ragweed - a type of plant whose pollen is a common allergen
