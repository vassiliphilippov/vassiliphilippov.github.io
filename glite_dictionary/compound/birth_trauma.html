birth trauma

1. NOUN

/ bɝːθ ˈtrɔmə / (AmE), / bɜːθ ˈtrɔːmə / (BrE)
physical injury sustained by a newborn during the process of birth.
physical injury to a newborn during birth
medical

Examples

The doctors took extra precautions to prevent birth trauma during the delivery.
Many cases of birth trauma are mild and resolve without long-term effects.
Proper obstetric techniques can significantly reduce the risk of birth trauma.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: birth injury - injury sustained by an infant during birth
  • Degree Higher: neonatal injury - injury occurring to a newborn

2. NOUN

/ bɝθ ˈtrɔmə / (AmE), / bɜːθ ˈtrɔːmə / (BrE)
psychological or emotional injury said to be caused to an infant by distressing events during birth, sometimes cited as influencing later mental health
emotional injury caused by events during birth
medical

Examples

Some psychologists believe that birth trauma can leave lasting psychological effects on an individual.
Therapists sometimes explore early experiences of birth trauma to help patients understand their mental health issues.
The concept of birth trauma is controversial within the psychiatric community.
