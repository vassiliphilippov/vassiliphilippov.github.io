sublunary existence

1. NOUN

/ sʌbˈluːnɛri ɪɡˈzɪstəns / (AmE), / sʌbˈluːnəri ɪɡˈzɪstəns / (BrE)
Life or existence pertaining to the earthly or mundane realm, in contrast to celestial or spiritual matters.
earthly or mundane existence

Examples

During his meditation, he reflected on the transience of sublunary existence and the quest for meaning.
Poets often contrast the fleeting nature of sublunary existence with the idea of eternal life.
In ancient cosmology, sublunary existence was thought to be full of change, unlike the perfect heavens.

Synset Examples

Philosophers often pondered the nature of sublunary existence.
They believed the sublunary realm was governed by different laws.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mundane - lacking interest or excitement; relating to the earthly world
  • Antonym: celestial - relating to the sky or heavens; heavenly

Etymology

From Latin sublunaris (beneath the moon) + existence.
