preconceived notion

1. NOUN

/ ˌpriːkənˈsiːvd ˈnoʊʃn̩ / (AmE), / ˌpriːkənˈsiːvd ˈnəʊʃn̩ / (BrE)
an opinion or idea formed before having enough evidence or knowledge, often without considering all the facts
an opinion formed without full evidence

Examples

She struggled to overcome her preconceived notion about the new technology.
The study challenges the preconceived notion that older workers are less productive.
It's important to approach each situation without a preconceived notion of the outcome.

Synset Examples

he did not even try to confirm his preconceptions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prejudice - preconceived opinion not based on reason or experience
  • Synonym: bias - inclination or prejudice for or against something

Etymology

From 'preconceived' (formed before actual knowledge, from Latin 'prae-' meaning before + 'concipere' meaning to take in or conceive) and 'notion' (from Latin 'notio', meaning knowledge or idea).
