special-interest group

1. NOUN

/ ˈspɛʃəl ˈɪntrəst ɡruːp / (AmE), / ˈspɛʃəl ˈɪntrəst ɡruːp / (BrE)
a group of people with shared interests who try to influence public officials and government decisions, often through lobbying
group seeking to influence policy

Examples

The environmental special-interest group lobbied lawmakers to pass stricter pollution regulations.
Many special-interest groups donate money to political campaigns during election seasons.
A special-interest group representing teachers called for increased funding in public education.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lobby - to try to influence a politician or government official
