viper's bugloss

1. NOUN

/ ˈvaɪpɚz ˈbʌɡˌlɔs / (AmE), / ˈvaɪpəz ˈbʌɡ.lɒs / (BrE)
a rough, prickly European plant (Echium vulgare) with spikes of bright blue flowers, now naturalized in North America
a European plant with blue flower spikes

Examples

The meadow was dotted with the bright blue flowers of viper's bugloss.
Gardeners often plant viper's bugloss to attract bees and butterflies.
Despite its weeds status, viper's bugloss adds vibrant color to roadsides in summer.

Etymology

The name refers to the plant's supposed resemblance to a viper's head and its former use as a remedy for snakebites. 'Bugloss' comes from Greek 'bouglōssos' meaning 'ox tongue', describing the rough leaves.
