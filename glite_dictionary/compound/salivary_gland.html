salivary gland

1. NOUN

/ ˈsæləˌvɛri ɡlænd / (AmE), / ˈsælɪvəri ɡlænd / (BrE)
Any of three pairs of glands in the mouth and digestive system that secrete saliva to aid in digestion.
gland in the mouth that produces saliva
medical

Examples

Inflammation of a salivary gland can cause swelling and discomfort in the mouth.
The doctor explained how the salivary glands help start the digestive process by producing saliva.
Surgery was required to remove a stone from her salivary gland.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parotid gland - largest salivary gland located near the ear
  • Synonym: submandibular gland - salivary gland located beneath the lower jaw
  • Synonym: sublingual gland - salivary gland located under the tongue

Etymology

From Latin 'saliva' (spittle) and 'glandula' (gland).
