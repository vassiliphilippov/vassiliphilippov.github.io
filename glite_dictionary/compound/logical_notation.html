logical notation

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɑːdʒɪkəl noʊˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈlɒdʒɪkəl nəʊˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
a system of symbols and signs used to represent concepts and relationships in logic
system of symbols used in logic

Examples

Students often struggle to understand logical notation in introductory logic courses.
Computer scientists rely on logical notation to formalize algorithms and proofs.
The philosopher explained the argument using standard logical notation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: symbolic logic - use of symbols to represent logical forms and operations
