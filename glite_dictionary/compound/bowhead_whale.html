bowhead whale

1. NOUN

/ ˈboʊˌhɛd weɪl / (AmE), / ˈbəʊˌhɛd weɪl / (BrE)
A large baleen whale of Arctic and sub-Arctic waters, characterized by its bow-shaped mouth and massive body.
large Arctic baleen whale

Examples

The bowhead whale migrates annually through the icy waters of the Arctic Ocean.
Scientists have noted that the bowhead whale can live for over 200 years.
Native Alaskan communities have long relied on the bowhead whale for subsistence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: right whale - a closely related baleen whale species

Etymology

Named for the distinctive arching, or bow-shaped, head of the species.
