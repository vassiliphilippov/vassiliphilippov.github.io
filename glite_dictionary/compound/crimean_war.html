Crimean War

1. PROPN

/ kraɪˈmiː.ən wɔr / (AmE), / kraɪˈmiː.ən wɔː / (BrE)
a war fought from 1853 to 1856 in Crimea between Russia and an alliance of Britain, France, the Ottoman Empire, and Sardinia.
war in Crimea (1853–1856) between Russia and a European alliance

Examples

The Crimean War was a significant conflict that highlighted the decline of the Ottoman Empire.
Many soldiers suffered from disease and poor conditions during the Crimean War.
Florence Nightingale became famous for her work with wounded soldiers in the Crimean War.

Etymology

Named after Crimea, the region where much of the fighting took place; 'Crimean' from the Crimean Peninsula.
