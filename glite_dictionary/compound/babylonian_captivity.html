Babylonian Captivity

1. NOUN

/ ˌbæbəˈloʊniən kəpˈtɪvəti / (AmE), / ˌbæbəˈləʊniən kəpˈtɪvɪti / (BrE)
the period when a large number of Jews were deported from ancient Israel to Babylonia by order of Nebuchadnezzar II, beginning in 586 BC
Jewish exile in Babylonia (586 BC)

Examples

The Babylonian Captivity was a turning point in Jewish history and culture.
Many biblical prophecies reference the events surrounding the Babylonian Captivity.
Scholars study the Babylonian Captivity to understand the origins of Judaism in exile.
