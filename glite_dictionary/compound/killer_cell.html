killer cell

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɪlɚ sɛl / (AmE), / ˈkɪlə sɛl / (BrE)
A type of immune cell, specifically a T cell with a CD8 receptor, that recognizes and destroys virus-infected cells.
immune cell that destroys infected cells
medical

Examples

The body's killer cells play a crucial role in targeting and eliminating virus-infected cells.
When activated, a killer cell binds to an infected cell and destroys it.
Scientists study how killer cells respond to different pathogens during an immune response.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cytotoxic T cell - A T lymphocyte that kills infected cells; synonym for killer cell
  • Synonym: natural killer cell - A type of lymphocyte that can kill tumor cells and virus-infected cells without antigen specificity
