green in the field

1. ADJ

/ ɡrin ɪn ðə fild / (AmE), / ɡriːn ɪn ðə fiːld / (BrE)
having little or no experience in a particular area or activity; inexperienced.
inexperienced in a specific area

Examples

She was still green in the field but eager to learn.
Most new hires are green in the field and require training.
Even though he's green in the field, he's already making valuable contributions.

Synset Examples

She was still green in the field but eager to learn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inexperienced - lacking experience or practice in a particular activity or field
  • Antonym: seasoned - having a lot of experience in a particular area

Etymology

The adjective 'green' has been used for 'inexperienced' since Middle English, associating greenness with youth and immaturity.
