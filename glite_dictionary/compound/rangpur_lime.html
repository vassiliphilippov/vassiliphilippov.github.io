Rangpur lime

1. NOUN

/ ˈræŋpʊr laɪm / (AmE), / ˈræŋpʊə laɪm / (BrE)
a hybrid citrus fruit, a cross between mandarin orange and lemon, with highly acidic flesh and orange-colored peel
acidic citrus fruit hybrid

Examples

The bartender used a Rangpur lime to give the cocktail a unique tart flavor.
Rangpur limes are prized for their sharp acidity and vibrant orange rind.
In Southeast Asia, the Rangpur lime is often used in traditional cooking.

Etymology

Named after Rangpur, a region in Bangladesh where the fruit is commonly cultivated.
