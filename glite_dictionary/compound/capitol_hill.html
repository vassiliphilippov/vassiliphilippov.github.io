Capitol Hill

1. PROPN

/ ˌkæpɪtəl ˈhɪl / (AmE), / ˌkapɪtəl ˈhɪl / (BrE)
The area in Washington, D.C., where the United States Capitol is located and where Congress meets; often used to refer to the U.S. Congress or its activities.
site of U.S. Capitol and Congress
american

Examples

Many important legislative decisions are made on Capitol Hill every year.
Reporters gathered on Capitol Hill to cover the budget negotiations.
The senator gave a speech on Capitol Hill addressing health care reform.

Synset Examples

they are debating the budget today on Capitol Hill

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Congress - the legislative branch of the United States government

Etymology

From 'Capitol', the building where the U.S. Congress meets, and 'Hill', referring to its location on a raised area in Washington, D.C.
