muscle fiber

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌsəl ˌfaɪbɚ / (AmE), / ˈmʌsl̩ ˌfaɪbə / (BrE)
An elongated, contractile cell that makes up the muscles of the body.
a cell that forms muscle tissue

Examples

Each muscle fiber contracts in response to signals from the nervous system.
Damage to the muscle fibers can result in weakness or pain.
Athletes often train to increase the strength and size of their muscle fibers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: myocyte - a type of cell found in muscle tissue
