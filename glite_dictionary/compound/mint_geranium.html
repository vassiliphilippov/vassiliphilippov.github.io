mint geranium

1. NOUN

/ mɪnt dʒəˈreɪniəm / (AmE), / mɪnt dʒəˈreɪniəm / (BrE)
a tansy-scented Eurasian perennial herb with buttonlike yellow flowers, used as a potherb, salad green, and for potpourri, tea, or flavoring; sometimes classified in the genus Chrysanthemum
a fragrant herb used in cooking and potpourri

Examples

The mint geranium adds a refreshing aroma to homemade potpourri.
She sprinkled chopped mint geranium leaves over her salad for a hint of flavor.
Some herbal teas include mint geranium for its subtle, complex scent.
