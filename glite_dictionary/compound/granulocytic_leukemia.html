granulocytic leukemia

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡrænjəloʊˈsɪtɪk lɪˈkiːmiə / (AmE), / ˌɡranjʊləˈsɪtɪk lɪˈkiːmiə / (BrE)
a malignant cancer of blood-forming tissues characterized by an excessive increase of myelocytes in the blood
type of blood cancer with excess myelocytes
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed the patient with granulocytic leukemia after observing high levels of myelocytes in the blood.
Advances in treatment have improved survival rates for those with granulocytic leukemia.
Family members attended educational sessions about granulocytic leukemia to better understand the disease.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leukemia - cancer of blood-forming tissues, including bone marrow

Etymology

From 'granulocytic' (relating to granulocytes) and 'leukemia' (from Greek leukos 'white' + haima 'blood').
