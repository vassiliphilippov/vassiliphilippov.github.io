the brush-off

1. NOUN

/ ðə ˈbrʌʃ ˌɔf / (AmE), / ðə ˈbrʌʃ ɒf / (BrE)
an act or instance of dismissing or rejecting someone in a curt or unfriendly manner
a blunt or unfriendly rejection
informal

Examples

I tried to join the conversation, but she gave me the brush-off.
After waiting weeks for a reply, his inquiry got the brush-off.
Whenever I suggest new ideas, the boss just gives me the brush-off.

Synset Examples

When I asked about the promotion, she gave me the brush-off.
The manager gave us the brush-off when we raised concerns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rebuff - an abrupt or ungracious refusal or rejection
  • Antonym: welcome - an instance of greeting or accepting warmly
