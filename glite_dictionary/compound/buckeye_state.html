Buckeye State

1. PROPN

/ ˈbʌkˌaɪ steɪt / (AmE), / ˈbʌk.aɪ steɪt / (BrE)
A nickname for the US state of Ohio, located in the Great Lakes region with capital Columbus.
nickname for the US state of Ohio

Examples

Many people refer to Ohio as the Buckeye State due to the prevalence of buckeye trees.
The Buckeye State is known for its rich manufacturing history and its vibrant cities.
Tourists from all over visit the Buckeye State to see landmarks in Columbus, Cleveland, and Cincinnati.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ohio - a state in north central United States, with capital Columbus

Etymology

The nickname refers to the abundance of buckeye trees (genus Aesculus) in Ohio.
