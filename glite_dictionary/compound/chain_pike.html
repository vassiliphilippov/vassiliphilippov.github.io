chain pike

1. NOUN

/ tʃeɪn paɪk / (AmE), / tʃeɪn paɪk / (BrE)
A small predatory freshwater fish of the pike family, found in quiet waters of eastern United States; also known as the chain pickerel (Esox niger).
freshwater fish of eastern US

Examples

He caught a large chain pike in the shallow waters near the dock.
The chain pike is easily recognized by its chain-like markings along its sides.
Many anglers in the region hope to catch a chain pike during the spring fishing season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pickerel - a small North American pike
