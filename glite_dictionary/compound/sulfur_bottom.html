sulfur bottom

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌlfər ˌbɑtəm / (AmE), / ˈsʌlfə ˌbɒtəm / (BrE)
An alternative name for the blue whale, the largest mammal ever known, recognized by its bluish-grey color and migratory habits, mainly found in the Southern Hemisphere.
alternative name for the blue whale

Examples

The enormous sulfur bottom is seldom seen close to shore.
Early whalers told tales of the elusive sulfur bottom they encountered in southern waters.
Marine biologists studied the migration patterns of the sulfur bottom to better understand ocean ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blue whale - the largest animal ever known, a species of baleen whale

Etymology

Refers to the pale yellowish coloring sometimes observed on the underside of some blue whales.
