aortic aneurysm

1. NOUN

/ eɪˈɔrtɪk ˈænjəˌrɪzəm / (AmE), / eɪˈɔːtɪk ˈanjʊərɪzm / (BrE)
A localized, abnormal dilation or bulge in the wall of the aorta, the main artery carrying blood from the heart to the rest of the body.
bulge in the wall of the aorta
medical

Examples

The surgeon explained that the patient needed urgent repair of an aortic aneurysm.
Because of the size of his aortic aneurysm, regular monitoring was necessary.
Smoking and high blood pressure are risk factors for an aortic aneurysm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aneurysm - a localized, blood-filled bulge in a blood vessel

Etymology

From 'aorta', the main artery, and 'aneurysm', from Greek 'aneurysma' (a widening).
