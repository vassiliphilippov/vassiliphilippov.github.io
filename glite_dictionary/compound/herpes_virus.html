herpes virus

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɜrpiːz ˈvaɪrəs / (AmE), / ˈhɜːpiːz ˈvaɪərəs / (BrE)
any of a group of viruses (family Herpesviridae) that cause diseases characterized by the formation of painful blisters on the skin or mucous membranes
virus causing skin blisters
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed him with a herpes virus infection after noticing clusters of blisters on his lips.
A herpes virus can remain dormant in the body and reactivate later in life.
Researchers are developing vaccines to prevent herpes virus outbreaks in at-risk populations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: herpes simplex - a type of herpes virus causing oral or genital lesions
  • Synonym: varicella-zoster virus - a herpesvirus causing chickenpox and shingles

Etymology

From Greek 'herpēs' (creeping) and Latin 'virus' (poison, slime).
