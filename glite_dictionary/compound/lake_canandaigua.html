Lake Canandaigua

1. PROPN

/ ˌleɪk kənənˈdeɪɡwə / (AmE), / ˌleɪk kənənˈdeɪɡwə / (BrE)
a glacial lake in central New York State, one of the Finger Lakes
glacial lake in New York, Finger Lake

Examples

Every summer, tourists flock to Lake Canandaigua for boating and relaxation.
The scenic views around Lake Canandaigua make it a popular destination for photographers.
Residents enjoy swimming and fishing in Lake Canandaigua during the warmer months.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Finger Lakes - a group of long, narrow lakes in central New York State

Etymology

From an Iroquoian (Seneca) term meaning 'the chosen spot' or 'place selected for settlement'.
