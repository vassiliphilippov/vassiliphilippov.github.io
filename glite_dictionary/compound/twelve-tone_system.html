twelve-tone system

1. NOUN

/ ˈtwɛlv ˌtoʊn ˈsɪstəm / (AmE), / ˈtwɛlv ˌtəʊn ˈsɪstəm / (BrE)
A method of musical composition in which all twelve semitones of the chromatic scale are arranged in a particular sequence (tone row) and used systematically, often with variations such as inversion or retrograde; introduced by Arnold Schoenberg.
serial musical technique using twelve tones

Examples

Many twentieth-century composers experimented with the twelve-tone system to break free from traditional tonality.
The twelve-tone system was pioneered by composer Arnold Schoenberg in the 1920s.
Learning the twelve-tone system can be challenging for students accustomed to tonal harmonies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: serialism - the compositional technique of using series for structuring music

Etymology

Coined in the early 20th century, combining 'twelve-tone' (referring to the use of all twelve semitones) and 'system'.
