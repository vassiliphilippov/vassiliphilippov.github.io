British shilling

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrɪtɪʃ ˈʃɪlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbrɪtɪʃ ˈʃɪlɪŋ / (BrE)
a former unit of currency in Great Britain, equal to one twentieth of a pound sterling or twelve pence, used before decimalization in 1971.
former British monetary unit (1/20 pound)
british archaic

Examples

My grandfather collected old British shillings as a hobby.
The British shilling was replaced by the decimal system in 1971.
She found a British shilling at the bottom of an antique box.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shilling - a former British coin and monetary unit equal to one twentieth of a pound

Etymology

From Old English 'scilling', a former English coin; 'British' denotes its use in Great Britain.
