long-tailed weasel

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɔŋ ˌteɪld ˈwiːzəl / (AmE), / ˈlɒŋ ˌteɪld ˈwiːzəl / (BrE)
a common American weasel, notable for its relatively large size and black-tipped long tail
a North American weasel with a long, black-tipped tail

Examples

The long-tailed weasel is often found in grasslands and open forests throughout North America.
Farmers appreciate the long-tailed weasel because it helps control rodent populations.
With its black-tipped tail and slender body, the long-tailed weasel is easy to recognize in the wild.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weasel - small carnivorous mammal with a slender body and typically brown fur
