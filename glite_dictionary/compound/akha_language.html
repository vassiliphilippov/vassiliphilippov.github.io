Akha language

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːkɑ ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈɑːkə ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
a Loloish language spoken by the Akha people in parts of South Yunnan Province and nearby regions of Southeast Asia
language of the Akha people, Southeast Asia

Examples

The Akha language is primarily spoken in villages along the southern Yunnan mountains.
There are significant efforts to preserve the Akha language in Thailand and Laos.
He published a dictionary to help document the Akha language for future generations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Loloish - a branch of the Sino-Tibetan language family

Etymology

Akha from the ethnonym of the Akha people, a branch of the Loloish family of languages.
