white bedstraw

1. NOUN

/ waɪt ˈbɛdˌstrɔ / (AmE), / waɪt ˈbɛdstrɔː / (BrE)
A Eurasian herb (Galium mollugo) with clusters of small white flowers, often found in meadows and naturalized in North America.
a Eurasian herb with small white flowers

Examples

The field was covered in white bedstraw blooming among the tall grasses.
Gardeners often find white bedstraw spreading quickly in wildflower meadows.
White bedstraw can be recognized by its delicate panicles of tiny white flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bedstraw - any plant of the genus Galium, with whorled leaves and small flowers
