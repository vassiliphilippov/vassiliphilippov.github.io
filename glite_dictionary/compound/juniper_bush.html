juniper bush

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒuː.nə.pɚ bʊʃ / (AmE), / ˈdʒuː.nɪ.pə bʊʃ / (BrE)
A shrub found in arid regions of Syria and Arabia, known for its small white flowers, and identified as the 'juniper' mentioned in the Old Testament; sometimes classified in the genus Genista.
desert shrub of Syria and Arabia

Examples

The shepherds sought shelter under a juniper bush during the midday heat.
Biblical texts refer to the juniper bush as a place of refuge in the desert.
A juniper bush can survive in poor soil and arid climates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: juniper - evergreen shrub or tree of the genus Juniperus
