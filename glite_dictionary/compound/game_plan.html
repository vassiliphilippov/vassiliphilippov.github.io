game plan

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡeɪm plæn / (AmE), / ˈɡeɪm plæn / (BrE)
a carefully thought-out strategy for achieving a specific goal, especially in sports or business
strategy for achieving a goal

Examples

The coach outlined the team's game plan before the championship match.
We need a solid game plan if we're going to win this client.
After halftime, they decided to change their game plan to be more aggressive.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strategy - a plan of action designed to achieve a long-term goal
  • Synonym: tactic - a specific action or maneuver to achieve a short-term goal

Etymology

From 'game' (contest) + 'plan' (scheme or method), first used in American sports contexts in the mid-20th century.

a carefully considered strategy or plan for achieving a particular objective, especially in business, politics, or personal matters
strategy or plan to achieve a goal

Examples

She outlined her game plan for launching the new product line next quarter.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strategy - a plan of action designed to achieve a long-term or overall aim
  • Synonym: tactic - an action or strategy carefully planned to achieve a specific end
