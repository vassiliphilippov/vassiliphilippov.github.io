monetary system

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑːnəˌtɛri ˈsɪstəm / (AmE), / ˈmɒnɪtəri ˈsɪstəm / (BrE)
the system by which money is supplied and managed in a country or region, including the standards by which its value is measured
system for supplying and managing money

Examples

The country reformed its monetary system to stabilize the economy.
A gold standard is one example of a monetary system.
Changing the monetary system affected both inflation and exchange rates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: currency - system of money in general use in a country
  • Antonym: barter system - system of exchange where goods or services are traded without money

Etymology

From 'monetary', from Latin 'monetarius' (of money), and 'system', from Greek 'systēma' (organized whole).
