plantain family

1. NOUN

/ ˈplæn.tɪn ˈfæm.ə.li / (AmE), / ˈplæn.tɪn ˈfæm.ɪ.li / (BrE)
a family of chiefly small herbs and a few shrubs, most commonly known for including weedy species in the genus Plantago
family of small weedy herbs (Plantaginaceae)

Examples

The plantain family includes a variety of herbaceous plants commonly found worldwide.
Many members of the plantain family are considered persistent weeds in gardens and lawns.
Researchers studied the ecological roles of several species within the plantain family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Plantaginaceae - the scientific name for the plantain family of flowering plants
  • Synonym: plantain - any plant of the genus Plantago, especially those with a rosette of leaves and spike of tiny flowers

Etymology

From the genus Plantago, type genus of the family; ultimately from Latin plantago (plantain).
