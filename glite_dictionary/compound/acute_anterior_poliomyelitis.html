acute anterior poliomyelitis

1. NOUN

/ əˈkjuːt ænˈtɪriər ˌpoʊlioʊmaɪˈɛlɪtɪs / (AmE), / əˈkjuːt ænˈtɪəriə ˌpəʊlɪəʊmaɪˈɛlɪtɪs / (BrE)
an acute viral disease characterized by inflammation of nerve cells in the brain stem and spinal cord, leading to rapid onset of paralysis; commonly known as a form of polio
a form of polio affecting nerve cells in the spinal cord
medical

Examples

The outbreak of acute anterior poliomyelitis led to widespread concern in the affected communities.
Vaccines have greatly reduced the incidence of acute anterior poliomyelitis worldwide.
Doctors treated several cases of acute anterior poliomyelitis during the epidemic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polio - an infectious viral disease that affects the nervous system
  • Synonym: poliomyelitis - a viral disease affecting the nerve cells of the spinal cord

Etymology

From Greek 'polios' (gray), 'myelos' (marrow, referring to the spinal cord), and '-itis' (inflammation).
