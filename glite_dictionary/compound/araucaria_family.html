araucaria family

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛrəˈkɛriə ˈfæməli / (AmE), / ˌarəˈkɛːrɪə ˈfamɪli / (BrE)
A family of tall evergreen, cone-bearing trees native to South America and Australia, with broad, leathery leaves; also known as Araucariaceae.
family of tall evergreen trees (Araucariaceae)

Examples

The monkey puzzle tree, a member of the araucaria family, is native to Chile and Argentina.
Several species in the araucaria family are popular ornamental trees in parks and gardens.
Botanists classified the ancient conifer fossils as part of the araucaria family.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Pinaceae - family of coniferous trees including pines, to which Araucariaceae is sometimes related

Etymology

From the genus name Araucaria, which derives from Arauco, a region in Chile.
