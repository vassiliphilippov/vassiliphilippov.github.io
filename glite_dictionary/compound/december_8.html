December 8

1. PROPN

/ dɪˈsɛmbər eɪtθ / (AmE), / dɪˈsɛmbə eɪtθ / (BrE)
A Roman Catholic holy day observed on this date as the Feast of the Immaculate Conception, commemorating the belief in the Virgin Mary's freedom from original sin.
Roman Catholic holy day on December 8

Examples

Many Catholics attend Mass on December 8 to honor the Immaculate Conception.
Schools in some countries close for the holy day celebrated on December 8.
The significance of December 8 stems from its association with the Virgin Mary in Catholic tradition.
