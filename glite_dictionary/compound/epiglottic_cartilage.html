epiglottic cartilage

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛpɪˈɡlɑtɪk ˈkɑrtɪlɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈɡlɒtɪk ˈkɑːtɪlɪdʒ / (BrE)
a thin, flexible plate of elastic cartilage that forms the main part of the epiglottis in the larynx
elastic cartilage in the epiglottis
medical

Examples

The epiglottic cartilage helps the epiglottis maintain its shape during swallowing.
Damage to the epiglottic cartilage can affect normal breathing and swallowing functions.
During surgery, the surgeon carefully identified the epiglottic cartilage to avoid injury.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: epiglottis - the flap of tissue at the base of the tongue that covers the windpipe during swallowing

Etymology

From 'epiglottic', relating to the epiglottis (from Greek 'epi-' meaning 'upon' and 'glotta' meaning 'tongue'), and 'cartilage' (from Latin 'cartilago').
