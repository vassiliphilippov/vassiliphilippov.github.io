common chickweed

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmən ˈtʃɪkˌwid / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən ˈtʃɪkwɪd / (BrE)
a widespread low-growing annual weed with small white flowers, often found in gardens and eaten by chickens
a widespread annual garden weed with small white flowers

Examples

The vegetable patch was overrun with common chickweed after the spring rains.
Many birds, especially chickens, feed on the leaves of common chickweed.
Gardeners often struggle to control common chickweed because of its rapid growth and spreading habit.

Etymology

From 'chick' + 'weed', so named because chickens commonly eat it.
