Japanese oak

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˈpæniz oʊk / (AmE), / dʒəˈpæniːz əʊk / (BrE)
a small evergreen tree native to China and Japan, belonging to the oak genus Quercus
a small evergreen Asian oak tree

Examples

The Japanese oak is valued for its strong wood and attractive foliage.
Many gardens feature a Japanese oak as an ornamental tree.
The leaves of the Japanese oak remain green throughout the year.

2. NOUN

/ dʒəˈpæniz oʊk / (AmE), / dʒəˈpæniːz əʊk / (BrE)
an oak tree native to Japan, known for its moderately light, fine-grained wood
oak tree native to Japan

Examples

The furniture was made from solid Japanese oak, prized for its durability and beauty.
Several species of Japanese oak are used in traditional Japanese carpentry.
Experts can distinguish Japanese oak from other varieties by its unique grain pattern.
