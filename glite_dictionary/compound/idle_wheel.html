idle wheel

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪdəl wil / (AmE), / ˈaɪdl wiːl / (BrE)
a wheel or pulley mounted on a shaft that presses against a belt to guide or tighten it, typically without transmitting power itself
a pulley that guides or tightens a belt

Examples

The idle wheel keeps the timing belt properly tensioned in the engine.
If the idle wheel wears out, the whole pulley system may develop slack.
Adjusting the idle wheel can help eliminate unwanted belt noise in machinery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: idler pulley - a pulley that does not transmit power but serves to guide or tension a belt
