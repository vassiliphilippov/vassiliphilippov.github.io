golden marguerite

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡoʊl.dən mɑːrˈɡjʊr.ɪt / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊl.dən məˈɡjʊə.rɪt / (BrE)
A Eurasian perennial herb (Anthemis tinctoria) with hairy, divided leaves and bright yellow, daisy-like flowers, often grown as an ornamental plant.
a yellow-flowered Eurasian perennial herb

Examples

The golden marguerite is often planted in gardens for its vibrant yellow blooms.
Many wildflower meadows in North America now include the golden marguerite among other species.
She picked a bouquet of golden marguerites to brighten the kitchen table.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Anthemis tinctoria - the scientific name of the golden marguerite
