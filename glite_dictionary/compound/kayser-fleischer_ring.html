Kayser-Fleischer ring

1. NOUN

/ ˈkaɪsər ˈflaɪʃər rɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkaɪzə ˈflaɪʃə rɪŋ / (BrE)
a golden brown or greenish ring of pigment at the outer edge of the cornea, characteristic of Wilson's disease
pigmented corneal ring (Wilson's disease symptom)
medical

Examples

The presence of a Kayser-Fleischer ring is a key diagnostic feature in Wilson's disease.
During the eye examination, the doctor noticed the characteristic Kayser-Fleischer ring around the patient's cornea.
Kayser-Fleischer rings are often detected by slit-lamp examination in people with hepatic copper accumulation.

Etymology

Named after German physicians Bernhard Kayser and Bruno Fleischer who first described the sign in the early 20th century.
