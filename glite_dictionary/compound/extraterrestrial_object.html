extraterrestrial object

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛk.strə.təˈrɛs.tri.əl ˈɑb.dʒɪkt / (AmE), / ˌɛk.strə.təˈrɛs.tri.əl ˈɒb.dʒɪkt / (BrE)
a natural object that exists outside the Earth and its atmosphere
object outside Earth and its atmosphere

Examples

Scientists study extraterrestrial objects to understand the origins of the solar system.
Meteorites are examples of extraterrestrial objects that have reached the Earth's surface.
The telescope detected an unknown extraterrestrial object moving across the night sky.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: celestial body - a natural object in the sky, such as a star, planet, or comet

Etymology

From Latin extra (outside) + terrestris (of the earth) + English object.
