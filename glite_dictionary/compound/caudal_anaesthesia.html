caudal anaesthesia

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔdəl əˌniːsθiə / (AmE), / ˈkɔːdəl əˌniːsθiːə / (BrE)
a type of regional anesthesia in which an anesthetic is injected into the lower end of the spinal canal, mainly used for procedures in the lower body; now largely replaced by epidural anesthesia
anesthesia via injection into lower spinal canal
medical british

Examples

Caudal anaesthesia is often used in pediatric surgery to numb the lower part of the body.
The surgeon opted for caudal anaesthesia instead of general anesthesia for the procedure.
Advances in epidural techniques have made caudal anaesthesia less common in adults.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: epidural anesthesia - anesthesia produced by the injection of anesthetic into the epidural space of the spinal cord
  • Synonym: spinal anesthesia - anesthesia resulting from injection into the subarachnoid space of the spine

Etymology

caudal from Latin 'cauda' (tail), referring to the tail end of the spine; anaesthesia from Greek 'anaisthēsia' (insensibility).
