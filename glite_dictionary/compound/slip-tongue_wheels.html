slip-tongue wheels

1. NOUN

/ ˈslɪp ˌtʌŋ ˈwiːlz / (AmE), / ˈslɪp ˌtʌŋ ˈwiːlz / (BrE)
a type of logging skidder consisting of wheels with dangling tongs that lift the end of a log off the ground for easier transport, used in the 19th and early 20th centuries
historical logging skidder with tongs and wheels

Examples

In the late 1800s, loggers often transported timber using slip-tongue wheels to keep the logs from dragging.
The museum displayed a set of restored slip-tongue wheels as part of its logging history exhibit.
Before modern machinery, crews relied on slip-tongue wheels to haul heavy logs out of dense forests.
