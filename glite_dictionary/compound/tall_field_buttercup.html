tall field buttercup

1. NOUN

/ tɔl fild ˈbʌtərkʌp / (AmE), / tɔːl fiːld ˈbʌtəkʌp / (BrE)
a perennial European buttercup (Ranunculus acris) with yellow spring flowers, widely naturalized in eastern North America
a perennial yellow-flowered buttercup

Examples

The tall field buttercup blooms in meadows and pastures each spring.
Local farmers often remove tall field buttercup because it can be toxic to livestock.
She identified the wildflower as a tall field buttercup thanks to its bright yellow petals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: buttercup - any of various plants of the genus Ranunculus with yellow flowers
