Rome Beauty

1. NOUN

/ roʊm bjuːti / (AmE), / rəʊm ˈbjuːti / (BrE)
A large, round, red apple variety, noted for its firm flesh and primarily used for baking.
variety of baking apple

Examples

The recipe calls for a Rome Beauty because it holds its shape well when baked.
She bought several Rome Beauty apples at the farmer's market for her famous pie.
A crisp Rome Beauty is ideal for making baked apples due to its firm texture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apple - a round fruit with red, green, or yellow skin and firm white flesh

Etymology

Named after Rome Township, Ohio, USA, where the variety originated in the early 19th century.
