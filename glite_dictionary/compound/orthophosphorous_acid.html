orthophosphorous acid

1. NOUN

/ ˌɔrθoʊfəˈsfɔrəs ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / ˌɔːθəʊfəˈsfɒrəs ˈæsɪd / (BrE)
a clear or yellow monobasic acid (H3PO2) used as a reducing agent and in chemical synthesis
a monobasic acid, H3PO2

Examples

The chemist prepared orthophosphorous acid in the laboratory for use in a reduction reaction.
Handling orthophosphorous acid requires caution due to its reactivity and potential health effects.
Unlike orthophosphoric acid, orthophosphorous acid contains only one replaceable hydrogen atom.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phosphorous acid - a related acid (H3PO3) of phosphorus
  • Degree Higher: phosphoric acid - a higher oxidation acid of phosphorus (H3PO4)

Etymology

From 'ortho-' (indicating the normal form) + 'phosphorous' (from phosphorus) + 'acid'.
