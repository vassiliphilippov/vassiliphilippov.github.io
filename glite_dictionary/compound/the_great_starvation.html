the Great Starvation

1. PROPN

/ ðə ɡreɪt stɑrˈveɪʃən / (AmE), / ðə ɡreɪt stɑːˈveɪʃən / (BrE)
the period of mass famine in Ireland between 1846 and 1851, caused by potato blight, leading to widespread starvation and emigration
famine in Ireland (1846–1851) caused by potato blight

Examples

Historians often refer to the Great Starvation as a turning point in Irish history.
During the Great Starvation, countless families were forced to leave their homeland in search of food and opportunity.
Many memorials commemorate the victims of the Great Starvation throughout Ireland and the Irish diaspora.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: the Great Famine - another name for the Great Starvation in Ireland
