HMS Bounty

1. PROPN

/ ˌeɪtʃ ɛm ɛs ˈbaʊnti / (AmE), / ˌeɪtʃ ɛm ɛs ˈbaʊnti / (BrE)
a British naval ship famous for the 1789 mutiny led by part of its crew against Captain William Bligh
famous British ship of the Bounty mutiny
british

Examples

The HMS Bounty is best known for the mutiny that occurred in 1789.
Several films and books have been inspired by the story of the HMS Bounty.
Captain Bligh survived after being set adrift by the HMS Bounty mutineers.

Etymology

HMS stands for 'His/Her Majesty's Ship'; Bounty was the ship's given name.
