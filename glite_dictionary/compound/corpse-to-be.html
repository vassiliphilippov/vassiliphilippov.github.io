corpse-to-be

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔrps tə ˈbi / (AmE), / ˈkɔːps tə ˈbiː / (BrE)
a humorous term for someone extremely fatigued or appearing likely to die soon; a person who seems almost dead from tiredness or poor health
someone extremely tired or near death (humorous)
informal

Examples

After the overnight shift, he looked and felt like a corpse-to-be.
Marathon runners often become corpses-to-be by the end of the race.
She called herself a corpse-to-be after pulling three all-nighters in a row.

Synset Examples

She felt like a corpse-to-be after the exhausting marathon.
The doctor warned that he was a corpse-to-be without treatment.
He joked about being a corpse-to-be after the party.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: walking dead - a person in a very bad or exhausted state
