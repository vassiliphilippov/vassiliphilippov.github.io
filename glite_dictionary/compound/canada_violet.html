Canada violet

1. NOUN

/ kəˈnædə ˈvaɪələt / (AmE), / kəˈnædə ˈvaɪələt / (BrE)
a tall North American perennial plant with heart-shaped leaves and white flowers marked with purple streaks, found in woodlands
North American violet with white, purple-streaked flowers

Examples

The Canada violet blooms in late spring along shaded forest trails.
Botanists easily distinguish the Canada violet by its white petals veined with purple.
She planted several Canada violets to brighten up the woodland garden.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: violet - herbaceous plant with purple or white flowers of the genus Viola
