vat color

1. NOUN

/ væt ˈkʌlɚ / (AmE), / væt ˈkʌlə / (BrE)
A water-insoluble dye that is applied to fabric by first reducing it to a soluble alkaline form and then regenerating it to its insoluble state through oxidation; commonly used for dyeing cotton.
water-insoluble dye used in textile dyeing

Examples

The fabric was dyed using a vat color to achieve a deep, vibrant blue.
Due to their durability, vat colors are preferred for coloring cotton yarns.
Indigo is a well-known vat color, especially popular in denim production.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indigo - A natural blue vat dye used especially for coloring cotton and denim

Etymology

From 'vat' (a tank or tub for holding liquids) and 'color' (dye); refers to the process using vats for dyeing.
