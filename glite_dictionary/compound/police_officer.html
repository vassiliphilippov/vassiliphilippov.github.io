police officer

1. NOUN

/ pəˈlis ˌɑfɪsər / (AmE), / pəˈliːs ˌɒfɪsə / (BrE)
a member of a police force, responsible for enforcing laws and maintaining public order
member of a police force

Examples

The police officer directed traffic at the busy intersection during rush hour.
Two police officers arrived at the scene within minutes of receiving the emergency call.
As a police officer, she is trained to handle a wide range of situations calmly and professionally.
