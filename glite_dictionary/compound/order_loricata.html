order Loricata

1. NOUN

/ ˌɔrdər lɔːrɪˈkeɪtə / (AmE), / ˌɔːdə ˌlɒrɪˈkeɪtə / (BrE)
A former zoological order that included crocodilians, now more commonly referred to as Crocodylia.
former name for order Crocodylia (crocodilians)

Examples

The family of crocodiles was once classified under order Loricata in older zoological texts.
Scientists no longer use the term order Loricata, preferring Crocodylia.
You may encounter order Loricata in nineteenth-century natural history literature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Crocodylia - the order of reptiles that includes crocodiles, alligators, caimans, and gharials

Etymology

From Latin 'loricatus' meaning 'armored', referring to the armored skin of crocodilians.
