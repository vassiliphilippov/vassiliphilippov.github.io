field-sequential color TV system

1. NOUN

/ ˈfiːld səˈkwenʃəl ˈkʌlər ˌtiːˈviː ˈsɪstəm / (AmE), / ˈfiːld sɪˈkwenʃəl ˈkʌlə ˌtiːˈviː ˈsɪstəm / (BrE)
an early system of color television in which images are transmitted by scanning successive fields in the three primary colors, typically red, green, and blue, in sequence.
early color TV system using sequential primary color fields

Examples

The field-sequential color TV system was an important step in the development of modern color broadcasting.
Although the field-sequential color TV system offered vibrant colors, it was eventually replaced by more advanced methods.
Engineers demonstrated the field-sequential color TV system at several technology exhibitions in the 1950s.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: color television - the transmission and reception of television signals in color
