genus Coriandrum

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌkɔːr.iˈæn.drəm / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌkɒr.iˈæn.drəm / (BrE)
A small genus of annual herbs native to the Mediterranean region, including the common coriander.
botanical genus including coriander

Examples

The genus Coriandrum is best known for the familiar herb coriander.
Botanists classify coriander as a member of the genus Coriandrum.
There are only two recognized species in the genus Coriandrum.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coriander - an aromatic herb of the genus Coriandrum

Etymology

From Latin 'Coriandrum', meaning coriander.
