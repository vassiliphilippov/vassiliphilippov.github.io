pier mirror

1. NOUN

/ pɪr ˈmɪrɚ / (AmE), / pɪə ˈmɪrə / (BrE)
a tall, narrow mirror placed against or between windows or columns, commonly used as a decorative element in rooms
tall mirror between windows or columns

Examples

A pier mirror hung elegantly between the tall windows facing the garden.
Antique collectors often seek out ornate pier mirrors from the 19th century.
The foyer was decorated with a large gilded pier mirror above a marble-topped table.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wall mirror - a mirror designed for hanging on a wall

Etymology

From 'pier' in architecture, meaning a section of wall between openings, and 'mirror.'
