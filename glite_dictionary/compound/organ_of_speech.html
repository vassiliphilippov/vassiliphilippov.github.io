organ of speech

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrɡən əv spiːtʃ / (AmE), / ˈɔːɡən əv spiːtʃ / (BrE)
any of the anatomical structures involved in the production of speech, such as the tongue, lips, vocal cords, or larynx.
anatomical structure used in speech

Examples

The lips and tongue are important organs of speech that help shape sounds into words.
Damage to any organ of speech can affect a person's ability to communicate clearly.
Linguists study how each organ of speech contributes to the production of different sounds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: speech organ - another term for organ of speech; anatomical structure involved in speech
