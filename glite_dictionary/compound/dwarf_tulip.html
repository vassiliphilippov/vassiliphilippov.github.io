dwarf tulip

1. NOUN

/ dwɔrf ˈtuːlɪp / (AmE), / dwɔːf ˈtjuːlɪp / (BrE)
A small, early-blooming variety of tulip, often cultivated for its compact size and vibrant flowers.
small early-blooming tulip variety

Examples

The dwarf tulip is perfect for borders and rock gardens due to its compact size.
In early spring, her garden was filled with colorful dwarf tulips.
Many gardeners choose the dwarf tulip for container planting because it flowers earlier than taller varieties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tulip - a bulbous spring-flowering plant of the lily family
