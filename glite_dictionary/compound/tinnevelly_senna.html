tinnevelly senna

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɪnəˈvɛli ˈsɛnə / (AmE), / ˌtɪnɪˈvɛli ˈsɛnə / (BrE)
an erect shrub (Senna alexandrina) with racemes of tawny yellow flowers, whose dried leaves are used medicinally as a cathartic
a medicinal shrub, source of senna leaves

Examples

The dried leaves of tinnevelly senna are commonly used as a natural laxative in herbal medicine.
Botanists study the characteristics and medicinal uses of tinnevelly senna in South India.
Herbal preparations containing tinnevelly senna are available in many health food stores.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: senna - any plant of the genus Senna, known for its medicinal leaves and pods

Etymology

Named after Tinnevelly (now Tirunelveli), a city in southern India, from where the senna was historically exported.
