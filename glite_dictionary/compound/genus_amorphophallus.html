genus Amorphophallus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs əˌmɔrfəˈfæləs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs əˌmɒfəˈfæləs / (BrE)
A genus of large tropical east Asian plants in the arum family, known for their unusual flowers and tuberous roots, including the devil's tongue and snake palm.
genus of tropical arum plants

Examples

The genus Amorphophallus includes the famous corpse flower known for its pungent odor.
Botanists are studying several species in the genus Amorphophallus to understand their pollination strategies.
Gardeners cultivate some members of the genus Amorphophallus for their striking and unusual flowers.

Etymology

From Greek 'amorphos' (shapeless) and 'phallos' (phallus), referring to the bizarre shape of the flower structure.
