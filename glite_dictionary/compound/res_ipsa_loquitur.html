res ipsa loquitur

1. NOUN

/ reɪs ˈɪpsə loʊˈkwɪtər / (AmE), / reɪs ˈɪpsə ləʊˈkwɪtjʊə / (BrE)
A legal principle stating that negligence can be inferred from the mere occurrence of certain types of accidents, without direct evidence of fault.
legal principle inferring negligence from accident itself
legal

Examples

The doctrine of res ipsa loquitur applied because the instrument was left inside the patient during surgery.
In this case, res ipsa loquitur allows the court to infer negligence even without direct evidence.
The judge explained that res ipsa loquitur was appropriate when the cause of the accident is within the defendant’s control.

Etymology

From Latin, meaning 'the thing speaks for itself'.
