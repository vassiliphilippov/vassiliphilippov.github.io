Maltese cross

1. NOUN

/ mɔlˈtis krɔs / (AmE), / mɔːlˈtiːz krɒs / (BrE)
a cross with four equal arms, each in the shape of a broad arrowhead, with the narrow points meeting at the center
cross with arrow-shaped arms

Examples

The firefighter proudly wore a badge featuring the Maltese cross on his uniform.
The Maltese cross is a prominent symbol in the insignia of the Knights of Malta.
Artists often use the Maltese cross in designing medals and emblems.

Etymology

Named after the Knights of Malta, who adopted it as their symbol.
