to causing trouble

1. VERB

/ ˈkɔzɪŋ ˈtrʌbəl / (AmE), / ˈkɔːzɪŋ ˈtrʌbəl / (BrE)
acting in a way that creates problems, disturbances, or difficulties
creating problems or disturbances

Examples

The group of teenagers was causing trouble in the park late at night.
He was warned not to cause trouble during the meeting.
If you keep causing trouble, you'll be asked to leave.

Synset Examples

That yob was causing trouble at the bar.
That hooer has a reputation for causing trouble.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: making mischief - deliberately causing trouble or annoyance; similar meaning
  • Antonym: behaving - acting in a socially acceptable manner; opposite sense
