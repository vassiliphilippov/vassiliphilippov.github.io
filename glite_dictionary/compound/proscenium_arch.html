proscenium arch

1. NOUN

/ proʊˈsiːniəm ɑːrtʃ / (AmE), / prəˈsiːniəm ɑːtʃ / (BrE)
the arch that forms the frame separating the stage from the auditorium in a theater, usually above the stage opening
theater arch framing stage opening

Examples

The actors took their bows in front of the proscenium arch at the end of the play.
A traditional theater often features a large proscenium arch separating the audience from the stage.
The ornate decorations on the proscenium arch caught everyone's attention as the curtain rose.

Etymology

From Latin 'proscenium' (fore-stage, from Greek 'proskēnion') + 'arch'.
