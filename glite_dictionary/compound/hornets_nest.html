hornets' nest

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɔrnəts nɛst / (AmE), / ˈhɔːnɪts nɛst / (BrE)
a situation full of trouble, danger, or strong controversy
contentious or dangerous situation

Examples

The new policy created a hornets' nest of complaints from staff across the company.
The mayor's decision to raise taxes has stirred up a political hornets' nest.
Publishing the report opened a legal hornets' nest for the organization.

Synset Examples

talk of invading Iraq stirred up a political hornets' nest

Related Terms

  • Synonym: can of worms - a complicated problem or situation likely to cause more trouble
  • Synonym: powder keg - a situation that could easily become very dangerous

Etymology

Refers metaphorically to the literal nest of hornets, known for being dangerous if disturbed.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɔrnəts ˌnɛst / (AmE), / ˈhɔːnɪts ˌnɛst / (BrE)
a structure built by hornets or wasps as a place to live and rear their young
structure built by hornets or wasps

Examples

A large hornets' nest hung dangerously close to the front door.
The children discovered a hornets' nest in the hollow tree trunk.
The gardener wore protective clothing to remove the hornets' nest from the attic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wasps' nest - habitation for wasps
