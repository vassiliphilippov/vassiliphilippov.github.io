free kick

1. NOUN

/ fri kɪk / (AmE), / friː kɪk / (BrE)
In soccer, a kick awarded to a team following a foul or rule infringement by the opposition, taken without interference from opponents except as allowed by the rules.
kick awarded after a foul in soccer

Examples

The referee awarded a free kick after the defender tripped the striker.
He expertly curled the free kick into the top corner of the net.
Their team practiced free kicks all week in preparation for the big match.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: penalty kick - a kick awarded for certain fouls inside the penalty area
  • Degree Lower: goal kick - a kick taken from within the goal area to restart play
