wattle and daub

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɑt̬l ənd dɔb / (AmE), / ˈwɒtl ənd dɔːb / (BrE)
A building material and construction method using a lattice of interwoven rods and twigs, which is then covered with clay or mud.
traditional wall-building material of sticks and clay

Examples

Many medieval houses were built using wattle and daub for their walls.
The restoration team carefully repaired the old wattle and daub structure using traditional methods.
When examined closely, the texture of wattle and daub becomes visible underneath the whitewash.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: half-timbered - a style of building with timber framing and spaces filled with other materials

Etymology

From Old English *watol* (framework of rods) and *daub* (to smear with plaster).
