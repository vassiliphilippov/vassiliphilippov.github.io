antiarrhythmic medication

1. NOUN

/ ˌænti.əˈrɪð.mɪk ˌmɛd.ɪˈkeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌænti.əˈrɪð.mɪk ˌmɛd.ɪˈkeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
a drug used to prevent or correct abnormal heart rhythm (arrhythmia)
drug for irregular heart rhythm
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed an antiarrhythmic medication to stabilize the patient's heartbeat.
Some antiarrhythmic medications can cause side effects and require careful monitoring by a cardiologist.
Research is ongoing to develop new antiarrhythmic medications with fewer adverse effects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arrhythmia - irregular heartbeat, medical condition

Etymology

From Greek 'anti-' (against) + 'arrhythmia' (irregular heartbeat) + English 'medication'.
