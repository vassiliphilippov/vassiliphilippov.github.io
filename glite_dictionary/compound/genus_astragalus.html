genus Astragalus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˌæstrəˈɡeɪləs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˌæstrəˈɡeɪləs / (BrE)
a large genus of annual or perennial herbs or shrubs found in north temperate regions, and the largest genus in the family Leguminosae (Fabaceae)
large plant genus in temperate regions (Leguminosae)

Examples

Many species within the genus Astragalus are used in traditional medicine around the world.
The genus Astragalus is easily recognized by its unique seed pods and feathery leaves.
Researchers discovered several new plants belonging to the genus Astragalus during their expedition.

Etymology

From Greek astrágalos (ankle bone, a plant name) used in scientific Latin.
