deuced shame

1. NOUN

/ ˈduːst ʃeɪm / (AmE), / ˈdjuːst ʃeɪm / (BrE)
A situation or event that causes great disappointment or regret.
great disappointment or regret
archaic british

Examples

It was a deuced shame that he missed the concert after all his preparation.
Everyone agreed it was a deuced shame the team lost despite playing so well.
Losing that opportunity truly felt like a deuced shame to her.

Synset Examples

It was a deuced shame that he missed the concert.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bitter disappointment - a very strong sense of disappointment
  • Degree Higher: tragedy - an event causing great suffering or disappointment

Etymology

From "deuced" (an old-fashioned intensifier, derived from "deuce," meaning 'the devil' in 17th-century English) + "shame."
