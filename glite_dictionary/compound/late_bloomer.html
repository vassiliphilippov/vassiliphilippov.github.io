late bloomer

1. NOUN

/ leɪt ˈbluːmər / (AmE), / leɪt ˈbluːmə / (BrE)
a person who develops skills or shows their talents or maturity later than is typical or expected
person who matures or succeeds later than usual

Examples

He was always considered a late bloomer, only finding his passion for music in his thirties.
Many athletes are late bloomers, reaching their peak performance after most have retired.
Despite being a late bloomer, she eventually became a respected leader in her field.

Synset Examples

He was a late bloomer, discovering his passion in college.
As a late bloomer, she just started her career at 30.
He's a late bloomer, but he's finally finding his passion.
She became a late bloomer in her artistic career.
Many late bloomers find success later in life.
Don't worry, many are late bloomers in their field.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: prodigy - a young person with exceptional abilities or talents
  • Antonym: child prodigy - a child who is exceptionally talented or precocious
  • Synonym: blossom - to develop or come into one's own potential

Etymology

From 'late' (not early) + 'bloomer' (one who blooms or flourishes); 'bloom' derives from Old Norse 'blóm'.
