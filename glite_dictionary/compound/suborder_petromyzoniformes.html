suborder Petromyzoniformes

1. NOUN

/ ˌsʌbˌɔrdər pɛˌtrəmɪzəˈnɪfərˌmiːz / (AmE), / ˌsʌbˌɔːdə ˌpiːtrɒmɪˌzɒnɪˈfɔːmiːz / (BrE)
A taxonomic suborder comprising lampreys, distinguished from hagfishes and other jawless fishes.
taxonomic group of lampreys

Examples

Lampreys belong to the suborder Petromyzoniformes and are distinct from other jawless fish like hagfishes.
Biologists study the evolutionary traits of the suborder Petromyzoniformes to understand the history of vertebrates.
The suborder Petromyzoniformes includes species with unique suction-cup mouths used for feeding.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lamprey - eel-like jawless fish of the family Petromyzontidae
  • Antonym: hagfish - primitive jawless fish related to lampreys, but not classified in Petromyzoniformes

Etymology

From Neo-Latin: 'Petromyzon' (genus name for lamprey) + 'iformes' (having the form of).
