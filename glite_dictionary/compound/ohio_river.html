Ohio River

1. PROPN

/ oʊˈhaɪ.oʊ ˈrɪv.ɚ / (AmE), / əʊˈhaɪ.əʊ ˈrɪv.ə / (BrE)
a major river in the eastern United States, formed in western Pennsylvania and flowing west to join the Mississippi River
major river in the eastern United States

Examples

The city of Cincinnati is located on the banks of the Ohio River.
In the spring, heavy rains often cause the Ohio River to flood.
Many steamboats traveled along the Ohio River during the 19th century.

Etymology

Named after the Iroquoian word "Ohi:yo'" meaning "good river" or "large creek".
