half-dozen

1. NOUN

/ ˌhæf ˈdʌz.ən / (AmE), / ˌhɑːf ˈdʌz.ən / (BrE)
a group or set of six; six items or people considered together
a group of six

Examples

She bought a half-dozen eggs from the market this morning.
There were only a half-dozen people at the meeting.
I’ve read a half-dozen books by that author.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: dozen - a group of twelve
  • Synonym: six - the number 6; one more than five

Etymology

Formed from 'half' and 'dozen' (from Old French 'douzaine', from Latin 'duodecim', twelve), originally meaning half of twelve (i.e., six).
