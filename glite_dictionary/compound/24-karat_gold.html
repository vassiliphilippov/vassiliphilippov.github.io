24-karat gold

1. NOUN

/ ˌtwɛnti ˈfɔr ˈkærət ɡoʊld / (AmE), / ˌtwɛnti ˈfɔː ˈkærət ɡəʊld / (BrE)
Gold that is completely pure, containing no other metals; 100 percent gold.
pure gold (100 percent)

Examples

She wore a necklace made of 24-karat gold that gleamed in the sunlight.
The wedding rings were crafted from 24-karat gold for maximum purity and value.
Because 24-karat gold is so soft, it is often mixed with other metals for durability.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gold - a yellow precious metal, the chemical element with symbol Au

Etymology

From the carat standard, where 24 carats represents pure gold; 'karat' from Arabic 'qīrāṭ', via Italian 'carato'.
