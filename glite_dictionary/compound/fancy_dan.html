fancy Dan

1. NOUN

/ ˈfænsi dæn / (AmE), / ˈfænsi dæn / (BrE)
a man who is ostentatious or showy in appearance or manner
a flashy, showy man
informal

Examples

The new manager came in dressed like a fancy Dan and tried to impress everyone.
Some people thought his style was too much for the office, calling him a fancy Dan.
You don't have to be a fancy Dan to make a good impression.

Synset Examples

The new guy acts like a fancy Dan at work.
He's such a fancy Dan, always showing off.
Don't be a fancy Dan, just keep it simple.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: show-off - a person who acts in a pretentious way to attract attention
  • Synonym: braggart - a person who boasts about achievements or possessions
  • Antonym: modest - not showing excessive pride or ostentation
