rail-splitter

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪlˌsplɪtər / (AmE), / ˈreɪlˌsplɪtə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to split logs to make rails for fences, especially split-rail fences
person who splits logs for fence rails

Examples

Abraham Lincoln was known as a rail-splitter in his early years.
The rail-splitter worked tirelessly to provide enough fencing for the farm.
In the 1800s, a rail-splitter was a common sight in rural America.
