moving in

1. NOUN

/ ˈmuːvɪŋ ɪn / (AmE), / ˈmuːvɪŋ ɪn / (BrE)
the act of taking possession of and starting to live in a building or residence
the act of occupying a building

Examples

The moving in process was exhausting, but finally everything was unpacked by the evening.
They plan to have a small party to celebrate their moving in next week.
Her parents helped her with the moving in after she signed the lease for her first apartment.

Synset Examples

occupation of a building without a certificate of occupancy is illegal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: move-in - the act or process of entering and beginning to occupy a place
  • Antonym: moving out - the act of leaving and ceasing to occupy a place or residence
