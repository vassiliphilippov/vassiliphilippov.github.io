grand scheme

1. NOUN

/ ɡrænd skiːm / (AmE), / ɡrænd skiːm / (BrE)
a large and ambitious plan, often involving complex or far-reaching aims
large and ambitious plan

Examples

He unveiled his grand scheme for developing the company's international presence.
In the grand scheme of things, her mistake was relatively minor.
The mayor's grand scheme to transform public transport was met with skepticism.

Synset Examples

They devised a grand scheme to increase sales.
Her grand scheme included a nationwide marketing campaign.
The grand scheme fell apart due to budget cuts.
She felt like a puppet in his grand scheme.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plan - a set of intended actions
  • Synonym: plot - a secret plan; a scheme, often with negative connotations
