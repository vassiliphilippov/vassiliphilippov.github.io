flower head

1. NOUN

/ ˈflaʊ.ɚ hɛd / (AmE), / ˈflaʊ.ə hɛd / (BrE)
a dense, compact cluster of small flowers arranged so closely together that they appear as a single flower, as seen in plants like clover or daisies.
dense cluster of flowers resembling a single bloom

Examples

The clover's flower head is actually made up of many tiny blossoms packed closely together.
A sunflower's large flower head can hold hundreds of seeds.
Gardeners admired the vibrant flower heads of the daisies blooming in the field.
