germinal concept

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɜrmɪnəl ˈkɑnsɛpt / (AmE), / ˈdʒɜːmɪnəl ˈkɒnsɛpt / (BrE)
a basic or original idea that serves as the foundation for further development
foundational idea for further development

Examples

The researcher's theory was built upon several germinal concepts introduced decades earlier.
Innovation often begins with a single germinal concept that grows into a full-fledged project.
They revisited their germinal concept to inspire the next phase of design.

Synset Examples

They discussed the germinal concepts at the brainstorming session.
The concept of germinal ideas often sparks new inventions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: core idea - central or main idea from which others develop
  • Synonym: preliminary idea - initial idea considered at the start of a process

Etymology

From Latin 'germen' (seed, sprout), via 'germinal' (related to beginnings); 'concept' from Latin 'conceptus' (something conceived).
