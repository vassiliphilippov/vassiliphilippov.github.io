pigeon pea

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪdʒən pi / (AmE), / ˈpɪdʒ.ɪn piː / (BrE)
the small, highly nutritious seed of a tropical leguminous plant (Cajanus cajan), often used as food
seed of the pigeon-pea plant

Examples

We cooked a spicy stew with pigeon peas and rice for dinner.
Farmers in India cultivate pigeon pea as a major source of protein.
The nutritionist recommended adding pigeon pea to the diet for extra fiber.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɪdʒən pi / (AmE), / ˈpɪdʒɪn piː / (BrE)
a tropical shrub (Cajanus cajan) with yellow flowers and edible seeds, widely grown for food and as a forage crop in warm regions
tropical shrub with edible seeds

Examples

Farmers in India often plant pigeon pea as a drought-resistant crop.
The seeds of the pigeon pea are used in many traditional stews and curries.
Intercropping with pigeon pea helps enrich the soil with nitrogen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cajan pea - alternative name for pigeon pea
  • Synonym: red gram - alternative name for pigeon pea
