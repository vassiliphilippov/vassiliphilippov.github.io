sedge wren

1. NOUN

/ sɛdʒ rɛn / (AmE), / sɛdʒ rɛn / (BrE)
A small American wren (Cistothorus stellaris) that inhabits wet, sedgy meadows.
a small American wren of wet meadows

Examples

We spotted a sedge wren singing among the tall grasses near the marsh.
The sedge wren prefers habitats with dense, wet sedges and rarely ventures into dry fields.
Conservationists are monitoring sedge wren populations due to habitat loss in many areas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wren - a small, active songbird of the family Troglodytidae

2. NOUN

/ sɛdʒ rɛn / (AmE), / sɛdʒ rɛn / (BrE)
a small songbird found in North America, typically inhabiting grasslands and marshes with abundant sedges.
small grassland songbird

Examples

We spotted a sedge wren singing in the marsh early this morning.
The sedge wren prefers habitats with tall grasses and plenty of cover.
During migration, sedge wrens can be seen in wetlands across the Midwest.
