liquid unit

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪkwɪd ˈjunɪt / (AmE), / ˈlɪkwɪd ˈjuːnɪt / (BrE)
a unit used to measure the volume or capacity of liquids
unit for measuring volume of liquids

Examples

A liter is a common liquid unit used in most countries around the world.
To convert a liquid unit to milliliters, multiply by 1,000.
The recipe called for three different liquid units to measure the various ingredients.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dry unit - a unit of capacity for measuring dry substances
