vacuum gauge

1. NOUN

/ ˈvæk.juːm ɡeɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈvæk.juːm ɡeɪdʒ / (BrE)
an instrument used to measure pressure below atmospheric pressure, indicating the degree of vacuum in a system
instrument for measuring low (sub-atmospheric) pressure

Examples

The engineer used a vacuum gauge to ensure the pressure in the chamber was sufficiently low.
Regular calibration of the vacuum gauge is necessary for accurate readings in the laboratory.
A faulty vacuum gauge can lead to incorrect assumptions about system performance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pressure gauge - instrument for measuring the pressure of fluids

Etymology

From Latin 'vacuus' (empty) and English 'gauge' (measuring instrument).
