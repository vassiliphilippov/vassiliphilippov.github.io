woo-monger

1. NOUN

/ ˈwuːˌmʌŋɡər / (AmE), / ˈwuːˌmʌŋɡə / (BrE)
A person who promotes or peddles romance, charm, or often dubious promises related to relationships.
person promoting or selling romance or charm

Examples

Some critics dismiss her as a woo-monger who spreads empty hopes.
He was called a woo-monger after promising unrealistic love advice online.
The magazine exposed several self-styled woo-mongers preying on lonely hearts.

Synset Examples

They call her a woo-monger for her extravagant claims.
As a woo-monger, he thrives on people's hope.
He's such a woo-monger, always selling dreams.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: matchmaker - a person who arranges romantic relationships or marriages
  • Degree Higher: warmonger - a person who promotes or tries to incite war

Etymology

Formed by combining 'woo', meaning to seek the affection of someone, especially romantically, with the suffix '-monger', meaning a person who promotes or deals in something.
