love-in

1. NOUN

/ ˈlʌv ɪn / (AmE), / ˈlʌv ɪn / (BrE)
a gathering or event where people celebrate love, peace, and goodwill, especially popular in the 1960s and 1970s
gathering focused on love and peace

Examples

After the concert, people gathered in the park for a love-in that lasted all night.
The university students organized a love-in to promote kindness and acceptance.
During the summer of 1967, a huge love-in took place in San Francisco's Golden Gate Park.

Synset Examples

The park was filled with couples enjoying a love-in.
The love-in attracted many romantic souls.
They organized a love-in to celebrate their anniversary.

Etymology

Formed from 'love' + 'in', modeled after 'sit-in' and similar protest event terms.
