genus Thyrsopteris

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs θɜrˌsɒptəˈrɪs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs θəːˌsɒptəˈrɪs / (BrE)
A genus of tree ferns containing the species Thyrsopteris elegans, native to certain regions of South America.
a genus of tree ferns

Examples

The genus Thyrsopteris is notable for its single surviving species, Thyrsopteris elegans.
Botanists studied the unique leaf structure of the genus Thyrsopteris during their fieldwork in Chile.
Conservation efforts are underway to protect the rare genus Thyrsopteris from habitat loss.

Etymology

From Greek 'thyrsos' (thyrsus, a type of plant stem) and 'pteris' (fern).
