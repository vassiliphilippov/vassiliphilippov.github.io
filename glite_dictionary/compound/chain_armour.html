chain armour

1. NOUN

/ tʃeɪn ˈɑrmər / (AmE), / tʃeɪn ˈɑːmə / (BrE)
A type of flexible body armour made of small interlinked metal rings, worn as protection in the Middle Ages.
flexible armour of interlinked metal rings

Examples

The knight wore chain armour beneath his surcoat for protection in battle.
Medieval chain armour provided flexibility as well as defense against slashing weapons.
Archaeologists discovered fragments of chain armour at the ancient battlefield site.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mail - armour made of interlinked metal rings
  • Antonym: plate armour - armour made from large metal plates
