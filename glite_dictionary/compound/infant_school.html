infant school

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪnfənt skuːl / (AmE), / ˈɪnfənt skuːl / (BrE)
(in the UK) a school for children aged between five and seven years old, usually the first stage of primary education.
British primary school for ages 5–7
british

Examples

She started at the local infant school when she was five years old.
After finishing infant school, children usually move on to junior school.
The infant school held a special assembly for the new pupils.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: junior school - school for children aged 7–11 in the UK
  • Synonym: primary school - school for children aged 5–11 in the UK
