polygonal shape

1. NOUN

/ pɑːˈlɪɡənl ʃeɪp / (AmE), / pɒˈlɪɡənl ʃeɪp / (BrE)
a closed plane figure with straight sides, typically having three or more sides and angles
closed figure with straight sides

Examples

The artist used a polygonal shape to design the new company logo.
Children drew every polygonal shape they could think of, from triangles to octagons.
The pattern on the floor tiles is formed from repeating polygonal shapes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polygon - a closed plane figure bounded by straight lines

Etymology

From Greek 'polygonon' (many-angled) + English 'shape'.
