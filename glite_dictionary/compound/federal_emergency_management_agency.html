Federal Emergency Management Agency

1. PROPN

/ ˌfɛdərəl ɪˈmɜrdʒənsi ˈmænɪdʒmənt ˈeɪdʒənsi / (AmE), / ˌfɛdərəl ɪˈmɜːdʒənsi ˈmænɪdʒmənt ˈeɪdʒənsi / (BrE)
an independent agency of the US government responsible for coordinating response and support during federal emergencies, such as natural disasters
US agency for emergency management
american

Examples

After the hurricane, the Federal Emergency Management Agency sent teams to provide disaster relief to affected communities.
The Federal Emergency Management Agency plays a crucial role in coordinating emergency response efforts across the United States.
Many residents applied for assistance from the Federal Emergency Management Agency after their homes were damaged by flooding.
