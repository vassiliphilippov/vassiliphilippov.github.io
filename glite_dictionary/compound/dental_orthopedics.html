dental orthopedics

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛntəl ˌɔrθəˈpidɪks / (AmE), / ˈdɛntəl ˌɔːθəʊˈpiːdɪks / (BrE)
the branch of dentistry concerned with the prevention or correction of irregularities of the teeth and jaws
branch of dentistry correcting dental irregularities
medical

Examples

Advancements in dental orthopedics have made it easier to treat misaligned teeth in young children.
After her consultation, the dentist suggested she visit a specialist in dental orthopedics.
The field of dental orthopedics often overlaps with orthodontics when addressing jaw development.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orthodontics - the branch of dentistry dealing specifically with the correction of irregularities of the teeth

Etymology

From Greek 'orthos' (straight) + 'paideia' (rearing of children), via 'orthopedics', applied to dental context.
