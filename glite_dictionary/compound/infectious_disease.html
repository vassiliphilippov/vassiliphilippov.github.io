infectious disease

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈfɛkʃəs dɪˈziz / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɛkʃəs dɪˈziːz / (BrE)
A disease caused by pathogenic microorganisms and capable of being transmitted from one person or organism to another.
disease transmissible between people or organisms
medical

Examples

Measles is an example of an infectious disease that can quickly spread through a community.
Proper handwashing is important to prevent the transmission of infectious diseases.
The doctor specialized in the treatment of rare infectious diseases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contagious disease - a disease that is easily transmitted by contact with an infected person or surface
