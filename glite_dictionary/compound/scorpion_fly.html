scorpion fly

1. NOUN

/ ˈskɔrpiən flaɪ / (AmE), / ˈskɔːpiən flaɪ / (BrE)
a predatory insect of the family Panorpidae, with a long beak and antennae; the male has a curved tail resembling a scorpion, though it is not venomous
a long-beaked insect, male tail resembles a scorpion

Examples

We spotted a scorpion fly perched on a leaf during our hike.
The male scorpion fly's tail looks dangerous, but it's harmless to humans.
Entomologists study the unique mating behaviours of the scorpion fly.

Etymology

Named for the male’s tail, which resembles a scorpion’s stinger, though it poses no danger.
