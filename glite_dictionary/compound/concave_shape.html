concave shape

1. NOUN

/ kɑnˈkeɪv ʃeɪp / (AmE), / kɒnˈkeɪv ʃeɪp / (BrE)
a shape that curves or bends inward, having an interior angle greater than 180 degrees
shape that curves inward

Examples

A satellite dish is a classic example of a concave shape designed to focus signals.
The mirror's concave shape allowed it to concentrate sunlight onto a single point.
Artists sometimes use the illusion of a concave shape to create depth in their paintings.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: convex shape - a shape that curves or bulges outward

Etymology

From Latin 'concavus', from 'con-' (together) + 'cavus' (hollow).
