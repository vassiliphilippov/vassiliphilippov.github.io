dragon's blood

1. NOUN

/ ˈdræɡənz blʌd / (AmE), / ˈdræɡənz blʌd / (BrE)
a dark red resin obtained from certain tropical trees, used in varnishes, medicines, and photoengraving
dark red resin from certain trees

Examples

The artisan used dragon's blood to create a deep red pigment for the painting.
Historically, dragon's blood has been used as a varnish for violins and other stringed instruments.
The lab technician handled the dragon's blood resin with care during the photoengraving process.

Etymology

Named for its intense red color, reminiscent of the mythical blood of dragons; the term dates to the Middle Ages.
