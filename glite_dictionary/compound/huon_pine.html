Huon pine

1. NOUN

/ ˈhjuː.ɒn paɪn / (AmE), / ˈhjuː.ɒn paɪn / (BrE)
a large, slow-growing Tasmanian tree (Lagarostrobos franklinii) valued for its durable, yellowish, aromatic wood used in carving and shipbuilding
Tasmanian timber tree with aromatic yellow wood

Examples

The furniture maker crafted an elegant desk from Huon pine to showcase its unique grain.
Many historic Tasmanian boats were built using the rot-resistant timber of the Huon pine.
Conservationists work to protect the ancient Huon pine forests in southwestern Tasmania.

Etymology

Named for the Huon River in Tasmania, Australia.
