argillaceous rock

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːrdʒɪˈleɪʃəs rɑk / (AmE), / ˌɑːdʒɪˈleɪʃəs rɒk / (BrE)
A sedimentary rock composed primarily of clay minerals.
sedimentary rock made of clay

Examples

The hillside consists mainly of argillaceous rock interbedded with thin layers of sandstone.
Geologists collected samples from the argillaceous rock formation to study its mineral content.
Water percolates slowly through argillaceous rocks, making them nearly impermeable compared to sandy layers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shale - a fine-grained sedimentary rock formed by the compaction of silt and clay

Etymology

From Latin 'argilla' meaning 'clay', and Latin-derived English 'aceous' meaning 'of the nature of'.
