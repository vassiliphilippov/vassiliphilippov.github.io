genus Caiman

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˈkaɪmən / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈkeɪmən / (BrE)
A genus of Central and South American reptiles in the alligator family, known as caimans.
genus of caimans (reptiles)

Examples

Species in the genus Caiman are commonly found in wetlands of South America.
The spectacled caiman belongs to the genus Caiman and is widespread throughout its range.
Biologists studied the behavior of young reptiles in the genus Caiman.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caiman - any of several species of Central and South American reptiles related to alligators

Etymology

From Latin 'genus' (type, kind) and New Latin 'Caiman', from Spanish 'caimán' (caiman, a type of crocodilian).
