strike pay

1. NOUN

/ straɪk peɪ / (AmE), / straɪk peɪ / (BrE)
money paid by a trade union to its members during a strike to help cover living expenses
money given to strikers by a union

Examples

The union provided strike pay to help workers during the long industrial action.
While on strike, employees relied on strike pay to meet their basic needs.
Without strike pay, many workers would struggle financially during a work stoppage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strike fund - money set aside by a union to provide financial support during strikes
