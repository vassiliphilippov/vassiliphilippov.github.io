ion-exchange resin

1. NOUN

/ aɪ.ɑn ɪksˌtʃeɪndʒ ˈrɛz.ɪn / (AmE), / aɪ.ɒn ɪksˌtʃeɪndʒ ˈrɛz.ɪn / (BrE)
a synthetic, insoluble material in the form of small beads that exchanges ions with surrounding solutions, often used in water purification and chemical processing
synthetic material that exchanges ions
medical

Examples

Many water treatment plants use ion-exchange resin to remove unwanted minerals from the supply.
An ion-exchange resin can be regenerated after use by washing it with a concentrated solution of the appropriate ions.
The chemist selected an ion-exchange resin suitable for separating metal ions from the mixture.

Synset Examples

Sodium polystyrene sulfonate is a strongly acidic ion-exchange resin and is used to treat hyperkalemia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resin - a solid or highly viscous substance produced by some plants or synthetically

Etymology

From 'ion' (charged particle) + 'exchange' (to swap) + 'resin' (solid or semi-solid organic substance).
