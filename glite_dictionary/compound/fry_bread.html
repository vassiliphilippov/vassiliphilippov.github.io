fry bread

1. NOUN

/ ˈfraɪ ˌbrɛd / (AmE), / ˈfraɪ ˌbrɛd / (BrE)
A type of flat bread, usually deep-fried or skillet-cooked, often made from wheat flour, cornmeal, or a combination, and sometimes including ham bits; commonly associated with Native American cuisine.
deep-fried or skillet-cooked flat bread

Examples

The vendor at the festival was selling warm fry bread with honey drizzled on top.
Many Native American communities consider fry bread to be a staple at gatherings and ceremonies.
She made a batch of fry bread in a skillet over the campfire for dinner.
