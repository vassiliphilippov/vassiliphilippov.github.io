troy weight

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrɔɪ ˌweɪt / (AmE), / ˈtrɔɪ ˌweɪt / (BrE)
a system of weights used for measuring precious metals and gemstones, based on a pound of 12 ounces and an ounce of 480 grains
system of weights for precious metals and gems

Examples

Gold and silver are traditionally measured using troy weight rather than the standard avoirdupois system.
The jeweler calculated the value of the ring by its troy weight.
One troy weight ounce is heavier than a standard ounce.

Etymology

From Old French *troi* (Troyes, a city in France), where this system was first used.
