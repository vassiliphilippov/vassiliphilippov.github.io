lean-to

1. NOUN

/ ˈliːn tuː / (AmE), / ˈliːn tuː / (BrE)
a simple structure or shelter with a single sloping roof, typically attached to another building or standing alone
simple shelter with a sloping roof

Examples

The hikers spent the night in a lean-to they built from branches.
A small lean-to behind the house was used to store firewood.
We took shelter from the rain under a makeshift lean-to in the woods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shed - a simple structure used for storage or shelter
  • Synonym: hut - a small, simple shelter or dwelling

Etymology

From the idea that the structure 'leans to' another, serving as an attachment.
