bishop pine

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɪʃəp paɪn / (AmE), / ˈbɪʃəp paɪn / (BrE)
A pine tree species (Pinus muricata) native to the northwestern coast of California, identified by its two or three needles per bundle.
Californian coastal pine tree species

Examples

The bishop pine is commonly found along the foggy coasts of northern California.
Ecologists are studying the bishop pine forest to understand its unique adaptation to wildfire.
A mature bishop pine can reach heights of over 20 meters in its native habitat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pine - Evergreen tree with needle-shaped leaves and woody cones
