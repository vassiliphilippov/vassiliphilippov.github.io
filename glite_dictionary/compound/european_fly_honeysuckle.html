European fly honeysuckle

1. NOUN

/ jʊˌroʊpiən flaɪ ˈhʌniˌsʌkəl / (AmE), / jʊəˌrəʊpiən flaɪ ˈhʌniˌsʌkəl / (BrE)
a Eurasian shrub (Lonicera xylosteum) with pairs of yellowish-white flowers and bright scarlet fruit, often cultivated as an ornamental plant
Eurasian shrub with yellowish-white flowers and red fruit

Examples

The European fly honeysuckle is often grown in gardens for its attractive berries.
Birds are drawn to the scarlet fruit of the European fly honeysuckle in late summer.
Some landscapes use European fly honeysuckle as a hedge for its ornamental value.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fly honeysuckle - any of several honeysuckle shrubs with paired flowers and berries
