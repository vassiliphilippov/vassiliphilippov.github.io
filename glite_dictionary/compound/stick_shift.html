stick shift

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɪk ˌʃɪft / (AmE), / ˈstɪk ˌʃɪft / (BrE)
A type of motor vehicle transmission that is operated manually using a gear lever and clutch pedal, rather than automatically.
manual vehicle transmission with gear lever
american

Examples

He prefers driving cars with a stick shift rather than an automatic transmission.
Learning to use a stick shift can be challenging for some new drivers.
The rental agency only had vehicles with a stick shift available.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: manual transmission - motor vehicle transmission operated by hand with a clutch and gear lever
  • Antonym: automatic transmission - vehicle transmission that shifts gears automatically

Etymology

From 'stick' meaning gear lever, and 'shift' from the action of changing gears; chiefly North American usage.

2. NOUN

/ stɪk ʃɪft / (AmE), / stɪk ʃɪft / (BrE)
a manual transmission in a car, or a car equipped with a manual transmission, operated by shifting a stick lever
manual transmission vehicle
american

Examples

She prefers driving a stick shift because it gives her more control over the car.
Many young drivers today have never learned how to operate a stick shift.
His first car was an old stick shift that he bought from his uncle.

Synset Examples

Do you know how to drive stick shift?

Related Terms

  • Antonym: automatic - a car with automatic transmission
  • Synonym: manual transmission - a system in which the driver must manually shift gears in a vehicle

Etymology

From 'stick' (gear lever) and 'shift' (to change gears), referring to changing gears by moving a gear stick.
