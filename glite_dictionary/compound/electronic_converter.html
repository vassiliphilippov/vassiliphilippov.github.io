electronic converter

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌlɛkˈtrɑnɪk kənˈvɝːtɚ / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛkˈtrɒnɪk kənˈvɜːtə / (BrE)
A device that converts an electrical signal from one frequency to another, especially in telecommunications.
device for changing signal frequency

Examples

The cable TV received input through an electronic converter installed behind the set.
An electronic converter can adapt signals for a range of communication devices.
Older radios often require an electronic converter to receive modern frequencies.
