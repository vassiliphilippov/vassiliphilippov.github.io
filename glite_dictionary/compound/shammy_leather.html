shammy leather

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃæmi ˈlɛðər / (AmE), / ˈʃæmi ˈlɛðə / (BrE)
a soft and absorbent leather, originally made from chamois skin but now typically made from sheepskin, used for cleaning and polishing surfaces
soft, absorbent cleaning leather

Examples

He wiped the car with a shammy leather to remove the water spots.
A clean shammy leather is perfect for polishing windows.
Always store your shammy leather damp to keep it soft and pliable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chamois leather - soft leather made from the skin of a chamois or sheep

Etymology

From 'shammy', an alteration of 'chamois', from French 'chamois' (the animal).
