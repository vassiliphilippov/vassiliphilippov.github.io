olive drab

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑlɪv dræb / (AmE), / ˈɒlɪv drab / (BrE)
a dull greyish-green to yellowish or light olive-brown color, often used for military uniforms and equipment
a dull olive-green color

Examples

The soldiers wore uniforms in olive drab to blend in with the landscape.
She painted her walls olive drab to create a calming, earthy atmosphere.
The designer chose olive drab for the jacket, giving it a rugged look.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: khaki - a light brownish-yellow color used especially for military uniforms

3. NOUN

/ ˈɑːlɪv dræb / (AmE), / ˈɒlɪv drab / (BrE)
A dull olive-green color, especially used for military uniforms and equipment, particularly by the United States Army.
dull olive-green military color/fabric

Examples

The soldiers wore olive drab uniforms during the training exercise.
His backpack was an old olive drab issued by the army.
The jeep was painted in olive drab to blend in with the surroundings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: khaki - a light brownish-yellow color; used for military uniforms

Etymology

From 'olive' (the color of an olive) and 'drab' (a dull, light brown color, from Old French 'drabe', undyed cloth).
