amicus curiae

1. NOUN

/ əˌmikəs ˈkjʊriˌaɪ / (AmE), / əˌmiːkəs ˈkjʊərɪˌiː / (BrE)
A person or organization that offers information or advice to a court in a legal case to which they are not a party, usually in order to influence the decision on matters of public interest.
independent legal adviser to a court
legal

Examples

The Supreme Court accepted several amicus curiae briefs from interested organizations.
As an amicus curiae, the advocacy group submitted its expert legal opinion to the court.
Several professors acted as amicus curiae to provide independent analysis in the high-profile lawsuit.

Etymology

From Latin, literally 'friend of the court'.
