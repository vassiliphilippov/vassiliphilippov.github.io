tundra soil

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʌn.drə ˌsɔɪl / (AmE), / ˈtʌn.drə ˌsɔɪl / (BrE)
a dark, muck-like soil with a permanently frozen subsoil, typically found in Arctic and subarctic regions
soil in Arctic regions with frozen subsoil

Examples

The plants in the Arctic have adapted to grow in tundra soil despite its difficult conditions.
Building structures on tundra soil can be challenging due to the underlying frozen layer.
Scientists are studying how climate change affects the stability of tundra soil in northern regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: permafrost - permanently frozen layer of soil in polar regions
