mynah bird

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪnə bɜrd / (AmE), / ˈmaɪnə bɜːd / (BrE)
a tropical Asian bird of the starling family, often noted for its ability to mimic human speech
tropical Asian bird known for mimicking

Examples

The mynah bird in the cage can imitate almost any word you say.
She was fascinated by the talkative mynah bird at the zoo.
Many people keep the mynah bird as a pet because of its remarkable mimicry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: starling - a family of small to medium-sized passerine birds

Etymology

From Hindi 'mainā', via Sanskrit 'madana' (a bird species).
