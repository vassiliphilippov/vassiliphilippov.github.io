to feel ouchy

1. VERB

/ fil ˈaʊtʃi / (AmE), / fiːl ˈaʊtʃi / (BrE)
to experience pain or soreness, especially in a mild or childlike way
to feel pain or soreness in a childlike way
informal

Examples

After a long walk, my feet feel ouchy from wearing new shoes.
Does your arm feel ouchy after getting the vaccine?
He complained that his finger felt ouchy after accidentally closing the door on it.

Synset Examples

I bumped my knee and it feels ouchy now.
