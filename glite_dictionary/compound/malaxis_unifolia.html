Malaxis unifolia

1. NOUN

/ məˈlæksɪs ˌjuːnɪˈfoʊliə / (AmE), / məˈlæksɪs ˌjuːnɪˈfəʊliə / (BrE)
A North American orchid species with a single leaf and small flowers with threadlike petals.
species of North American orchid

Examples

The rare Malaxis unifolia was discovered growing in the damp understory of the forest.
Botanists admire the delicate flowers of Malaxis unifolia for their threadlike appearance.
Conservation efforts are underway to protect Malaxis unifolia and its shrinking natural habitat.

Etymology

Malaxis from Greek 'malaxis' (softening); unifolia from Latin 'uni-' (one) + 'folia' (leaf).
