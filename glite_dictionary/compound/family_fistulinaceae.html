family Fistulinaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli fɪsˌtjuːlɪˈneɪsiˌiː / (AmE), / ˈfæməli fɪsˌtjuːlɪˈneɪsiː / (BrE)
A family of fungi distinct from the Polyporaceae, characterized by having tubes on the underside of the cap that are separate from each other.
a family of fungi with separate tubes under the cap

Examples

The family Fistulinaceae includes several unique species of fungi found in temperate forests.
Researchers recently discovered a new species belonging to the family Fistulinaceae in South America.
Unlike other fungi, members of the family Fistulinaceae have tubes that do not join together on the cap's underside.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'Fistulina' (the type genus) and the suffix '-aceae' denoting a family.
