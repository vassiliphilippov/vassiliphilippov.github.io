Wild Bill Hickok

1. PROPN

/ waɪld bɪl ˈhɪkək / (AmE), / waɪld bɪl ˈhɪkɒk / (BrE)
(1837–1876) A famous American frontier marshal and gunfighter known for his exploits in the Old West.
noted American frontier marshal (1837–1876)

Examples

The legendary gunfighter Wild Bill Hickok was known for his quick draw and sense of justice.
Many movies have portrayed Wild Bill Hickok as a symbol of the American frontier.
Tourists still visit Deadwood to see the place where Wild Bill Hickok met his untimely end.

Etymology

Nickname of James Butler Hickok, with 'Wild Bill' alluding to his reputation.

2. PROPN

/ waɪld bɪl ˈhɪkɑk / (AmE), / waɪld bɪl ˈhɪkɒk / (BrE)
(1837–1876) An American frontier lawman, marksman, and gambler, whose exploits in the Old West became legendary.
legendary American frontier lawman

Examples

Stories about Wild Bill Hickok have inspired countless Western movies and novels.
Some historians debate whether Wild Bill Hickok's exploits were exaggerated by the press of his day.
The notorious poker game that ended Wild Bill Hickok's life is still discussed among card players.
