calvary of heartbreak

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæl.və.ri əv ˈhɑrtˌbreɪk / (AmE), / ˈkæl.vəri əv ˈhɑːtˌbreɪk / (BrE)
an experience of intense emotional suffering or distress, likened to a painful ordeal
intense emotional suffering

Examples

Her divorce turned into a calvary of heartbreak that lasted for years.
Each betrayal felt like a new calvary of heartbreak to him.
Support from friends helped her endure the calvary of heartbreak after losing her job.

Synset Examples

They supported him through his calvary of heartbreak.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ordeal - a difficult or painful experience
  • Synonym: torment - severe physical or mental suffering

Etymology

From 'Calvary,' the hill near Jerusalem where Jesus was crucified; used metaphorically to denote suffering, combined with 'heartbreak.'
