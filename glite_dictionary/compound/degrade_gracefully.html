to degrade gracefully

1. VERB

/ dɪˈɡreɪd ˈɡreɪsfəli / (AmE), / dɪˈɡreɪd ˈɡreɪsfəli / (BrE)
to deteriorate in a controlled or dignified way, without sudden or undignified failure
to deteriorate in a controlled, dignified way

Examples

The old museum is allowed to degrade gracefully rather than being demolished outright.
Engineers designed the software to degrade gracefully if the system was overloaded.
Even as the historic painting ages, it continues to degrade gracefully without losing its charm.

Synset Examples

Her beauty may degrade gracefully, but her spirit remains strong.
The fabric will degrade gracefully in the sunlight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decay - to deteriorate or decline, usually gradually
  • Antonym: collapse - to fail or break down suddenly, often in an uncontrolled way
