test case

1. NOUN

/ tɛst keɪs / (AmE), / tɛst keɪs / (BrE)
A legal action brought as a representative case, the outcome of which is expected to establish a precedent for similar cases.
legal action expected to set a precedent
legal

Examples

The judge said this lawsuit was a test case that could affect thousands of workers.
Lawyers carefully selected the facts for their test case to maximize its potential to set a precedent.
This ruling marks the first test case concerning digital privacy in the country.
