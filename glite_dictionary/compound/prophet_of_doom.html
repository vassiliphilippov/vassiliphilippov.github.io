prophet of doom

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɑːfɪt əv duːm / (AmE), / ˈprɒfɪt əv duːm / (BrE)
a person who consistently predicts unpleasant or disastrous events, often with pessimism
person who predicts disaster or misfortune

Examples

After the recession began, many economists were labeled prophets of doom by the media.
While she warned of potential dangers, her colleagues called her a prophet of doom.
Not wanting to sound like a prophet of doom, he carefully explained the risks.

Synset Examples

Her constant warnings made her seem like a prophet of doom.
Don't be a prophet of doom; things will get better.
He was labeled a prophet of doom for predicting the crisis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: doomsayer - a person who predicts disastrous events
  • Antonym: optimist - a person who tends to be hopeful and confident about the future

Etymology

Combines 'prophet' (from Greek prophētēs, 'inspired speaker') and 'doom' (from Old English dōm, 'judgment').
