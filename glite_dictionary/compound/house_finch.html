house finch

1. NOUN

/ haʊs fɪntʃ / (AmE), / haʊs fɪntʃ / (BrE)
a small North American finch, originally found in the western United States and Mexico, with brownish or grayish plumage and, in males, red markings on the head and breast
a small North American finch species

Examples

A brightly colored male house finch perched on the garden fence.
House finch populations have expanded into eastern North America.
We spotted a nest of house finches in the eaves of our porch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: finch - a small songbird with a stout bill
