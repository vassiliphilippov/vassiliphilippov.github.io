wild ginger

1. NOUN

/ waɪld ˈdʒɪndʒər / (AmE), / waɪld ˈdʒɪndʒə / (BrE)
a low-growing perennial herb with pungent, gingery leaves and rhizomes, often found in woodland areas and used as a flavoring or for medicinal purposes
woodland herb with gingery rhizomes

Examples

The forest floor was covered in wild ginger, its heart-shaped leaves giving off a spicy scent.
Native Americans traditionally used wild ginger as a natural remedy and flavoring.
You can spot wild ginger along shady woodland trails in early spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ginger - tropical plant with a spicy rhizome, commonly used as a spice
