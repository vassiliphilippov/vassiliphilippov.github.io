cogent evidence

1. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊdʒənt ˈɛvɪdəns / (AmE), / ˈkəʊdʒənt ˈɛvɪdəns / (BrE)
evidence that is clear, logical, and strongly persuasive in establishing the truth of something.
clear and convincing evidence

Examples

The prosecutor presented cogent evidence that strongly supported the defendant's guilt.
Without cogent evidence, the committee could not approve the new policy proposal.
Her arguments were well-structured, but lacked cogent evidence to persuade the audience.

Synset Examples

if you have any proof for what you say, now is the time to produce it

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: evidence - Information or objects that help to prove something
  • Degree Higher: proof - Conclusive evidence; something which establishes a fact beyond doubt

Etymology

From Latin *cogere* (to drive together, to compel) influencing 'cogent' and Latin *evidentia* (clearness, evidence).
