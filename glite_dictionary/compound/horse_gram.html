horse gram

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɔrs ˌɡræm / (AmE), / ˈhɔːs ˌɡræm / (BrE)
a twining herb (Macrotyloma uniflorum) of the Old World tropics, especially cultivated in India for its edible seeds and as fodder for livestock
a tropical leguminous plant grown for food and fodder

Examples

Farmers in southern India often grow horse gram as it is tolerant of dry conditions.
The high protein content of horse gram makes it a valuable dietary staple in some rural communities.
She prepared a traditional curry using soaked horse gram seeds.
