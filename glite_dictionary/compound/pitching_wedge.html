pitching wedge

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪtʃɪŋ wɛdʒ / (AmE), / ˈpɪtʃɪŋ wɛdʒ / (BrE)
a golf club with a high loft, used to hit the ball over obstacles or for short approach shots onto the green
golf club for lofted shots

Examples

He used his pitching wedge to clear the bunker and land the ball on the green.
My pitching wedge is my favorite club for short approach shots.
She selected a pitching wedge to loft the ball over the water hazard.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sand wedge - a golf club designed for shots out of sand bunkers
