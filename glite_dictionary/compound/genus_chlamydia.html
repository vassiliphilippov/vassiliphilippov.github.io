genus Chlamydia

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs kləˈmɪdiə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs klæˈmɪdiə / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Chlamydiaceae, comprising disease-causing, obligate intracellular parasitic bacteria.
type genus of Chlamydiaceae (bacteria)
medical

Examples

The genus Chlamydia contains bacteria responsible for serious human and animal diseases.
Research on the genus Chlamydia has led to better treatments for sexually transmitted infections.
Members of the genus Chlamydia are known for their unique intracellular lifestyle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Chlamydia - A genus of bacteria causing various infectious diseases

Etymology

From Greek 'chlamys' (cloak), referring to the cloak-like envelope around the bacteria.
