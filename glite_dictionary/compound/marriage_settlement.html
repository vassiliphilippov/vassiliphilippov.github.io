marriage settlement

1. NOUN

/ ˈmærɪdʒ ˈsɛtəlmənt / (AmE), / ˈmarɪdʒ ˈsɛtəlmənt / (BrE)
a formal agreement made before marriage, especially one detailing the financial arrangements between the parties
prenuptial agreement or contract
legal

Examples

The couple signed a marriage settlement to clarify their financial rights before the wedding.
Historically, a marriage settlement protected the interests of both families involved.
Their marriage settlement outlined the distribution of property in case of divorce or death.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prenuptial agreement - an agreement made by a couple before they marry concerning the ownership of their respective assets
  • Degree Higher: divorce settlement - an arrangement regarding property or money after a divorce
