winter cherry

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪn.tɚ ˌtʃɛr.i / (AmE), / ˈwɪn.tə ˌtʃɛr.i / (BrE)
a small South American shrub grown as a houseplant, valued for its abundant ornamental but poisonous red or yellow cherry-sized fruit
ornamental shrub with cherry-like fruit

Examples

Many people keep a winter cherry on their windowsill for decoration, unaware that its berries are toxic.
The bright red fruit of the winter cherry stands out against its dark green leaves.
Although the winter cherry is attractive, you should keep it away from pets and children.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Jerusalem cherry - another name for the winter cherry shrub

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɪntər ˌtʃɛri / (AmE), / ˈwɪntə ˌtʃɛri / (BrE)
A perennial plant of the nightshade family, cultivated for its decorative orange-red, papery calyx that encloses the fruit.
ornamental plant with papery orange-red calyx

Examples

The winter cherry is often used in floral arrangements for its bright, lantern-like pods.
She grows winter cherry in her garden for the striking orange-red color in autumn.
I bought a bunch of winter cherry stems to decorate the living room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: physalis - any plant of the genus Physalis, such as the ground cherry or cape gooseberry
