order Casuarinales

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdɚ kæˌzjuːəˈraɪnəˌliːz / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə kæˌzjuːəˈraɪnəˌliːz / (BrE)
A taxonomic order of chiefly Australian trees and shrubs, including the casuarinas, comprising the family Casuarinaceae.
taxonomic order of casuarinas and relatives

Examples

The order Casuarinales is primarily found in Australia, where its members are adapted to nutrient-poor soils.
Botanists recently published a study on the evolutionary history of the order Casuarinales.
Species in the order Casuarinales often resemble pine trees, although they are not closely related.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Casuarinaceae - family of plants within the order Casuarinales, including casuarinas
  • Synonym: casuarina - tree of the genus Casuarina, member of Casuarinales

Etymology

From Latin 'casuarina' (genus name) + '-ales' (a suffix used in naming plant orders).
