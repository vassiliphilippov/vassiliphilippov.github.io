leafnose bat

1. NOUN

/ ˈlifˌnoʊz bæt / (AmE), / ˈliːfnəʊz bæt / (BrE)
A bat with a leaf-shaped flap at the end of its nose, especially those in the families Phyllostomatidae, Rhinolophidae, and Hipposideridae.
bat with a leaf-shaped flap on its nose

Examples

The leafnose bat uses its distinctive nose flap to help with echolocation while hunting insects.
Researchers found a new species of leafnose bat in the tropical rainforest.
The families Phyllostomatidae and Hipposideridae include several types of leafnose bats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horseshoe bat - bat with a horseshoe-shaped flap on its nose
