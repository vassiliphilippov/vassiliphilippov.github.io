Greek key

1. NOUN

/ ɡriːk kiː / (AmE), / ɡriːk kiː / (BrE)
an ornamental pattern made up of a continuous line shaped into repeated right angles, forming a decorative border or design
decorative border pattern of repeated right angles

Examples

The mosaic floor was edged with a stunning Greek key pattern.
She chose curtains with a bold Greek key border that matched the room’s décor.
Ancient Greek temples often featured the Greek key motif in their friezes.

Synset Examples

there was a simple fret at the top of the walls

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meander - an ornamental pattern of winding or continuous lines, similar to the Greek key
  • Synonym: fret - a decorative design of repeated geometric shapes, often found on architectural moldings

Etymology

Named after its frequent use in ancient Greek art and architecture, also called 'meander' after the Meander River in Turkey.
