Lake Albert Nyanza

1. PROPN

/ leɪk ˈæl.bɚt niˈæn.zə / (AmE), / leɪk ˈæl.bət nɪˈæn.zə / (BrE)
a shallow lake located on the border between Uganda and the Democratic Republic of the Congo, in the Great Rift Valley of East-Central Africa
lake on the Uganda–Congo border in Africa

Examples

The expedition set up camp near the shores of Lake Albert Nyanza before continuing their journey.
Early explorers mapped Lake Albert Nyanza as one of the key lakes of the Great Rift Valley.
Fishermen from both Uganda and Congo rely on the resources of Lake Albert Nyanza.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Lake Albert - another name for Lake Albert Nyanza

Etymology

'Nyanza' is an East African term for 'lake,' from Bantu languages.
