to bullet vote

1. VERB

/ ˈbʊlət voʊt / (AmE), / ˈbʊlɪt vəʊt / (BrE)
to cast multiple votes for a single candidate rather than distributing them among several candidates, especially in cumulative voting systems
to concentrate votes on one candidate

Examples

Strategic voters may choose to bullet vote to maximize their preferred candidate's chances of winning.
She bullet voted rather than spreading her votes among several candidates on the ballot.
In this election, many participants are expected to bullet vote for their top choice only.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plump - to give all your votes to one candidate

Etymology

From 'bullet', originally referring to a single ballot or vote; reflects the analogy of using all one's force on a single point.
