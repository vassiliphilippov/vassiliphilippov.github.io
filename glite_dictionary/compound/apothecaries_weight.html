apothecaries' weight

1. NOUN

/ əˈpɑːθɪˌkeriz weɪt / (AmE), / əˈpɒθɪkəriz weɪt / (BrE)
a system of weights formerly used in pharmacy, in which an ounce is equal to 480 grains and a pound is 12 ounces
pharmacy weight measurement system
medical

Examples

Pharmacists once used the apothecaries' weight system to measure out medicines precisely.
The recipe called for one ounce of an ingredient, referring specifically to apothecaries' weight.
Modern pharmacies no longer use apothecaries' weight for compounding drugs.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: avoirdupois weight - weight system used for general goods, not pharmacy

Etymology

From apothecary (pharmacist; from Old French *apotecaire*, from Late Latin *apothecarius*) + weight.
