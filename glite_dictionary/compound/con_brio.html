con brio

1. ADV

/ kɑn ˈbriː.oʊ / (AmE), / kɒn ˈbriː.əʊ / (BrE)
(in music) with vigor and energy; played in a lively, spirited manner.
with vigor and energy (music)

Examples

The orchestra played the overture con brio, filling the hall with excitement.
Her interpretation of the sonata was marked by a con brio tempo throughout the quicker movements.
The conductor instructed the musicians to perform the allegro section con brio.

Synset Examples

allegro con brio

Etymology

From Italian con brio, meaning 'with spirit' or 'with vigor'.

2. ADV

/ kɑn ˈbri.oʊ / (AmE), / kɒn ˈbriː.əʊ / (BrE)
(in music) with vigor or spirit; to be performed with energy and enthusiasm.
performed with energy (music)

Examples

The pianist played the final movement con brio, impressing the audience with her energy.
The conductor instructed the orchestra to perform the piece con brio to capture its lively character.
She always interprets Beethoven’s faster passages con brio, bringing excitement to the performance.

Etymology

From Italian, literally 'with spirit' or 'with vigor'; 'con' means 'with', 'brio' means 'vigor, spirit'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02287272-s
Synset POS: s
