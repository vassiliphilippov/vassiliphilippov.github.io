stony coral

1. NOUN

/ ˈstoʊni ˈkɔrəl / (AmE), / ˈstəʊni ˈkɒrəl / (BrE)
A type of coral that produces a hard, calcareous skeleton, forming reefs and islands through the aggregation of its colonies.
reef-building coral with hard skeleton

Examples

The Great Barrier Reef is primarily composed of stony corals.
Climate change poses a threat to stony coral populations worldwide.
Scientists study stony coral to understand the health of marine ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coral - marine invertebrate with a hard skeleton
