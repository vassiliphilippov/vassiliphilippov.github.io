Hall's honeysuckle

1. NOUN

/ hɔlz ˈhʌniˌsʌkəl / (AmE), / hɔːlz ˈhʌniˌsʌkl̩ / (BrE)
A vigorous variety of Japanese honeysuckle (Lonicera japonica), widely cultivated and known for its fast-growing, vine-like habit.
a fast-growing variety of honeysuckle vine

Examples

Hall's honeysuckle quickly covered the fence with its dense green foliage and sweet-scented flowers.
Gardeners often plant Hall's honeysuckle for its ability to attract pollinators and birds.
Be careful when growing Hall's honeysuckle, as it can become invasive in some regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Japanese honeysuckle - a species of honeysuckle native to East Asia with fragrant white or yellow flowers

Etymology

Named after George Hall, an American who introduced the species to the United States.
