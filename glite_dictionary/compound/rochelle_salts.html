Rochelle salts

1. NOUN

/ roʊˈʃɛl sɔlts / (AmE), / rəʊˈʃɛl sɒlts / (BrE)
A double salt of potassium sodium tartrate, used as a mild laxative and in certain chemical applications.
a double salt; potassium sodium tartrate
medical

Examples

The chemist recommended Rochelle salts to relieve mild constipation.
In older laboratories, Rochelle salts were often used in chemical reactions.
Seidlitz powders contain Rochelle salts as the active ingredient.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Seidlitz powder - effervescent powder containing Rochelle salts; used as a laxative

Etymology

Named after the city of La Rochelle, France, where it was first prepared.
