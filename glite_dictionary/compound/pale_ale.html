pale ale

1. NOUN

/ peɪl eɪl / (AmE), / peɪl eɪl / (BrE)
A type of amber-colored beer brewed with pale malts, typically lighter and drier than traditional bitter ales.
an amber-colored beer brewed with pale malts

Examples

I'd like a pint of pale ale, please.
She prefers pale ale over lagers because of its light, hoppy flavor.
This brewery is famous for its award-winning pale ale.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bitter - a type of British ale with a stronger hop flavor and often darker than pale ale
