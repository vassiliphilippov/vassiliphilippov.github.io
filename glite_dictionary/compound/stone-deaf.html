stone-deaf

1. ADJ

/ stoʊn ˈdɛf / (AmE), / stəʊn ˈdɛf / (BrE)
Completely unable to hear; totally deaf.
completely deaf

Examples

After the accident, he was left stone-deaf in both ears.
Despite being stone-deaf, she learned to communicate using sign language.
The explosion rendered the old miner stone-deaf.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: deaf - partially or completely unable to hear

Etymology

Formed from 'stone' (emphasizing totality) and 'deaf.'
