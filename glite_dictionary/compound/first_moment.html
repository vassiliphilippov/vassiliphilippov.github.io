first moment

1. NOUN

/ ˌfɝst ˈmoʊmənt / (AmE), / ˌfɜːst ˈməʊmənt / (BrE)
the mean of a set of values; the sum of the values divided by their number, especially in statistics and mathematics.
mean value in statistics or mathematics

Examples

The first moment of a distribution is also known as its mean.
When analyzing data, calculating the first moment is often the first step.
The first moment provides crucial information about the central tendency of values.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mean - average value of a set of numbers
  • Antonym: median - the middle value in a set of ordered numbers

Etymology

From mathematical usage: 'moment' from Latin *momentum* (movement, influence).
