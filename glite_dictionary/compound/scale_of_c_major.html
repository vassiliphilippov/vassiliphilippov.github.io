scale of C major

1. NOUN

/ skeɪl əv si ˈmeɪdʒər / (AmE), / skeɪl əv si ˈmeɪdʒə / (BrE)
the major diatonic scale in music that has no sharps or flats, consisting of the notes C, D, E, F, G, A, and B
major scale with no sharps or flats

Examples

The scale of C major is often taught to beginners because it contains no sharps or flats.
To play the scale of C major, start with the note C and proceed stepwise up to B.
She mastered the scale of C major before moving on to more complex keys.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: C major - the key or scale of music with no sharps or flats, based on C
  • Degree Higher: scale - a series of musical notes ordered by pitch
  • Degree Higher: chromatic scale - a scale including all twelve pitches of the octave
