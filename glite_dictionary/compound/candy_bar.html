candy bar

1. NOUN

/ ˈkændi bɑr / (AmE), / ˈkændi bɑː / (BrE)
A sweet food made of chocolate, sugar, or both, molded into a rectangular bar shape and intended to be eaten as a snack.
sweet bar-shaped snack
american

Examples

She reached into her bag and pulled out a candy bar for a quick snack.
After lunch, Tom bought a candy bar from the vending machine.
The children shared a candy bar while watching the movie.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chocolate bar - a chocolate confection shaped as a bar
  • Degree Lower: candy - sweet confectionery, especially made of sugar
  • Degree Higher: dessert - sweet course eaten at the end of a meal

2. NOUN

/ ˈkændi bɑr / (AmE), / ˈkændi bɑː / (BrE)
A sweet snack consisting of chocolate, often with additional ingredients such as nuts, caramel, or nougat, shaped into a bar.
chocolate-covered sweet bar

Examples

He grabbed a candy bar from the vending machine for a quick snack.
Would you like to try this new candy bar with caramel inside?
She packed a candy bar in her lunch as a treat.

Synset Examples

You can buy a candy bar for just a nickel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chocolate bar - a bar of sweetened chocolate, sometimes with added ingredients
