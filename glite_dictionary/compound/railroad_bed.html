railroad bed

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪlˌrɑd bɛd / (AmE), / ˈreɪlrəʊd bɛd / (BrE)
the layer of material on which railway tracks are laid, providing support and stability
base layer for railway tracks

Examples

The construction crew spent weeks preparing the railroad bed before laying the new tracks.
Heavy rains washed away part of the railroad bed, causing delays in train service.
A sturdy railroad bed is essential to ensure safe and smooth rail travel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: track bed - the foundation on which railroad tracks are laid
