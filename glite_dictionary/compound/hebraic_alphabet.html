Hebraic alphabet

1. NOUN

/ hɪˈbreɪɪk ˈælfəbɛt / (AmE), / hɪˈbreɪɪk ˈælfəbɛt / (BrE)
the alphabet used for writing Hebrew and, later, Yiddish and Ladino, originating in the 5th century BC
alphabet used for Hebrew, Yiddish, and Ladino

Examples

Scholars have traced the origins of the Hebraic alphabet to ancient Phoenician scripts.
Children in the diaspora learn to read the Hebraic alphabet before studying Hebrew texts.
The Hebraic alphabet has been adapted to write languages such as Yiddish and Ladino.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hebrew alphabet - the set of letters used in the modern Hebrew script
  • Antonym: Cyrillic alphabet - the writing system for Russian and some other Slavic languages

Etymology

From Latin Hebraicus (Hebrew) and Greek alphabetos, ultimately from the first two Hebrew letters, aleph and bet.
