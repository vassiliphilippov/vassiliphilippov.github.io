pulmonary trunk

1. NOUN

/ ˈpʌlməˌnɛri trʌŋk / (AmE), / ˈpʌlmənəri trʌŋk / (BrE)
the large artery that carries deoxygenated blood from the right ventricle of the heart to the lungs, where it divides into the right and left pulmonary arteries
major artery from the right ventricle to the lungs
medical

Examples

The surgeon carefully examined the pulmonary trunk before proceeding with the heart operation.
A blockage in the pulmonary trunk can lead to life-threatening complications.
The anatomy class studied the structure and function of the pulmonary trunk.

Etymology

From Latin 'pulmonarius' (pertaining to the lungs) and 'truncus' (trunk, stem).
