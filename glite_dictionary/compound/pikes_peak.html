Pikes Peak

1. PROPN

/ paɪks piːk / (AmE), / paɪks piːk / (BrE)
a mountain peak in the Rocky Mountains of central Colorado, United States, standing at 14,109 feet (4,301 meters)
mountain in Colorado, USA

Examples

The view from the top of Pikes Peak is absolutely breathtaking on a clear day.
Many hikers dream of reaching the summit of Pikes Peak every summer.
Pikes Peak has become a symbol of the American West and adventure.

Etymology

Named after Zebulon Pike, an American explorer who led an expedition to the area in 1806.
