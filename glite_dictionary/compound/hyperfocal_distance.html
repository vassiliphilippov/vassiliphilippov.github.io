hyperfocal distance

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪpərˈfoʊkəl ˈdɪstəns / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪpəˈfəʊkəl ˈdɪstəns / (BrE)
The distance from a camera lens to the closest point that is acceptably sharp when the lens is focused at infinity, such that everything beyond this point appears in focus.
distance ensuring maximum depth of field in focus

Examples

By setting the lens to the hyperfocal distance, the photographer ensured both the foreground and background were in focus.
Understanding your lens's hyperfocal distance is essential for capturing sharp landscape shots.
He consulted a chart to determine the correct hyperfocal distance for his chosen aperture and focal length.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: depth of field - the range within a photo that appears acceptably sharp

Etymology

From 'hyper-' (beyond) and 'focal' (relating to focus); used in photography since the late 19th century.
