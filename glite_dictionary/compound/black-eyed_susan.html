black-eyed Susan

1. NOUN

/ ˌblæk.aɪd ˈsuːzən / (AmE), / ˌblæk.aɪd ˈsjuːzən / (BrE)
A tropical African climbing plant with yellow flowers that have a dark purple center, commonly grown as an ornamental.
climbing plant with yellow, dark-centered flowers

Examples

The garden was brightened by the cheerful blooms of the black-eyed Susan climbing the fence.
She planted a variety of black-eyed Susans to attract butterflies to her yard.
A single black-eyed Susan flower stood out among the greenery due to its striking dark center.

Etymology

Named for the distinctive dark center ('eye') of its flowers and a traditional English ballad character, Susan.

3. NOUN

/ ˌblækˌaɪd ˈsuːzən / (AmE), / ˌblæk.aɪd ˈsuː.zən / (BrE)
a North American plant with yellow or orange petals and a dark brown or black center, often grown as a garden flower and also known as the state flower of Maryland
a flower with yellow petals and dark center

Examples

Several black-eyed Susans brightened the edge of the country road.
She planted black-eyed Susans in her garden for their vibrant color and resilience.
The black-eyed Susan is cherished as Maryland's official state flower.
