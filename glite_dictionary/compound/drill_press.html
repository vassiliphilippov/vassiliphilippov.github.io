drill press

1. NOUN

/ drɪl prɛs / (AmE), / drɪl prɛs / (BrE)
A machine tool with a vertical stand, used for making precise holes in materials, typically operated with an electric motor and a lever.
upright machine for drilling holes

Examples

The machinist used the drill press to create evenly spaced holes in the metal plate.
A drill press allows for more accurate drilling than a handheld drill.
Safety goggles should always be worn when operating the drill press in the workshop.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drill - a tool or machine with a rotating cutting tip for making holes
