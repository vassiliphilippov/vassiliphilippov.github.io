Texas storksbill

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛksəs ˈstɔrksˌbɪl / (AmE), / ˈtɛksəs ˈstɔːksˌbɪl / (BrE)
A plant of the genus Erodium, found in prairies and desert regions of the southwestern United States and Mexico, noted for its pink or lavender flowers and beak-like fruit.
prairie and desert plant of the US Southwest and Mexico

Examples

The Texas storksbill blooms in early spring across the arid landscapes of New Mexico.
Botanists discovered several patches of Texas storksbill along the nature trail.
Gardeners appreciate the resilience of Texas storksbill in drought-prone areas.
