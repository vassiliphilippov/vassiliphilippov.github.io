Piltdown hoax

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪlˌdaʊn hoʊks / (AmE), / ˈpɪlˌdaʊn həʊks / (BrE)
a famous scientific fraud in which supposed fossil remains of a primitive human were later proved to have been fabricated
notorious paleontological forgery

Examples

The Piltdown hoax misled the scientific community for decades before it was exposed.
Many textbooks once included the Piltdown hoax as genuine evidence for human evolution.
The exposure of the Piltdown hoax changed the way scientists verify ancient discoveries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fraud - deception intended to result in financial or personal gain
  • Synonym: forgery - the action of forging or producing a copy of something fraudulently

Etymology

Named after Piltdown, a village in England where the remains were purportedly found.
