acerate leaf

1. NOUN

/ ˈæs.əˌreɪt liːf / (AmE), / ˈæs.ər.eɪt liːf / (BrE)
A needle-shaped leaf, especially found in coniferous trees.
needle-shaped leaf (conifers)

Examples

Pine trees are known for their acerate leaves, which reduce water loss.
The acerate leaves of spruce trees help them survive cold climates.
Botanists often study the acerate leaf structure to understand conifer adaptation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: needle leaf - A leaf that is long and thin, like a needle

Etymology

From Latin 'acer' meaning 'sharp' or 'pointed'.
