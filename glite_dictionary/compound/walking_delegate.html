walking delegate

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔkɪŋ ˈdɛləˌɡeɪt / (AmE), / ˈwɔːkɪŋ ˈdɛlɪɡət / (BrE)
a union representative who visits workers at their workplaces to ensure that agreements with employers are being observed
union official who checks workplace agreements

Examples

The walking delegate inspected several construction sites to verify the workers' conditions were as agreed.
Many disputes were avoided because the walking delegate addressed concerns on the job site promptly.
Each walking delegate is responsible for ensuring the union contract is upheld in their assigned area.

Etymology

From 'walking' (moving from site to site) and 'delegate' (representative). The term originated in US labor unions in the late 19th century.
