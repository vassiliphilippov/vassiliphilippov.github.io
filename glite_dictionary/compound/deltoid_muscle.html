deltoid muscle

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛl.tɔɪd ˈmʌs.əl / (AmE), / ˈdɛl.tɔɪd ˈmʌs.əl / (BrE)
a large triangular muscle covering the shoulder, responsible for lifting and rotating the arm
shoulder muscle for arm movement
medical

Examples

The deltoid muscle is essential for raising your arm above your head.
After the injury, he struggled to move his deltoid muscle without pain.
Weightlifters often focus on exercises that strengthen the deltoid muscle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shoulder muscle - muscle located in the shoulder region
  • Degree Higher: biceps - muscle at the front of the upper arm

Etymology

From Greek 'deltoeidēs' (shaped like the letter delta, Δ) + 'muscle'.
