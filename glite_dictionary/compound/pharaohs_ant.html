pharaoh's ant

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɛroʊz ænt / (AmE), / ˈfeərəʊz ænt / (BrE)
a small yellow or red ant (Monomorium pharaonis), common in warm regions and known as a persistent household pest
a small household pest ant

Examples

The kitchen was infested with pharaoh's ants that seemed impossible to eliminate.
Pharaoh's ants are particularly difficult to control because they build nests in hidden places.
A pest control specialist identified the insects as pharaoh's ants after inspecting the apartment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ant - a small insect usually living in colonies

Etymology

Named for the mistaken belief that this ant species was one of the plagues of ancient Egypt.
