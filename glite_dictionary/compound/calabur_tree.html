calabur tree

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæləbɚ ˌtri / (AmE), / ˈkæləbə ˌtriː / (BrE)
A fast-growing tropical American evergreen tree with white flowers and fleshy edible fruit; its bark yields silky fiber used for cordage and its wood is valued for making staves.
tropical tree with edible fruit and useful fiber

Examples

The calabur tree is prized for its edible fruit and strong, silky fiber.
Local villagers use the bark of the calabur tree to make durable rope.
Several calabur trees grow near the river, providing shade and seasonal fruit.
