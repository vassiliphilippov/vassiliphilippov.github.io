to hold the ring

1. VERB

/ hoʊld ðə rɪŋ / (AmE), / həʊld ðə rɪŋ / (BrE)
to keep or look after the wedding ring temporarily, especially until it is needed during the ceremony
temporarily keep the wedding ring at a ceremony

Examples

During the wedding, her brother was asked to hold the ring until the exchange of vows.
Could you hold the ring for me while I get ready for the ceremony?
The best man forgot he was supposed to hold the ring until the officiant asked for it.

Synset Examples

During the wedding, she was chosen to hold the ring.
They decided to hold the ring until the right moment.
He nervously asked his friend to hold the ring for him.
