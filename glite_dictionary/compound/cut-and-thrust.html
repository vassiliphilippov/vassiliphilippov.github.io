cut-and-thrust

1. NOUN

/ ˌkʌt ən ˈθrʌst / (AmE), / ˌkʌt ən ˈθrʌst / (BrE)
Fighting or combat involving the use of knives, swords, or similar weapons, specifically employing both cutting and thrusting movements.
fighting style using both cutting and thrusting

Examples

The duel quickly turned into a vicious cut-and-thrust as both men fought for their lives.
Medieval knights mastered the techniques of cut-and-thrust combat on the battlefield.
The play depicted the deadly cut-and-thrust between rival gangs in the city.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fencing - the sport or activity of fighting with swords

Etymology

From 'cut' and 'thrust', referring to the two main types of sword attacks.
