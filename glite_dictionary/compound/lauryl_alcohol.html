lauryl alcohol

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɔriːl ˈælkəhɑl / (AmE), / ˈlɔːrɪl ˈælkəhɒl / (BrE)
A colorless, insoluble solid alcohol (C12H26O) used in the manufacture of detergents and pharmaceuticals.
solid alcohol used in detergents

Examples

Many shampoos contain lauryl alcohol to help create a rich lather.
The production process relies on lauryl alcohol as a key raw material.
Because of its surfactant properties, lauryl alcohol is widely used in the cosmetics industry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dodecanol - another name for lauryl alcohol, an alcohol with a 12-carbon chain
  • Degree Lower: octyl alcohol - an alcohol with an 8-carbon chain, related compound

Etymology

From 'lauryl', referring to lauric acid from which it is derived, and 'alcohol'.
