fenestra of the vestibule

1. NOUN

/ fəˈnɛstrə əv ðə ˈvɛstəˌbjul / (AmE), / fəˈnɛstrə əv ðə ˈvɛstɪbjuːl / (BrE)
The oval opening in the wall of the inner ear to which the base of the stapes is attached, allowing sound vibrations to enter the inner ear.
oval opening in the inner ear for the stapes
medical

Examples

The surgeon carefully examined the fenestra of the vestibule during the ear operation.
Hearing loss can occur if the fenestra of the vestibule is blocked or damaged.
Vibrations from the stapes are transmitted into the inner ear through the fenestra of the vestibule.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oval window - another name for the fenestra of the vestibule

Etymology

From Latin fenestra (window) and vestibule (entrance hall).
