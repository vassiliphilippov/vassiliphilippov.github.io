Parthian language

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːrθiən ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈpɑːθɪən ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
An extinct Northwestern Iranian language once spoken in the Parthian kingdom between 250 BC and AD 226.
extinct language of the Parthian kingdom

Examples

The Parthian language was used in official inscriptions of the Parthian Empire.
Several ancient texts have been preserved in the Parthian language.
Studying the Parthian language offers insights into Iranian linguistic history.

Etymology

From 'Parthian', referring to Parthia, an ancient region of north-eastern Iran.
