straight sinus

1. NOUN

/ streɪt ˈsaɪnəs / (AmE), / streɪt ˈsaɪnəs / (BrE)
an unpaired venous channel in the dura mater of the brain that receives blood from the inferior sagittal sinus and the great cerebral vein
venous channel in brain's dura mater
medical

Examples

The neurosurgeon carefully identified the straight sinus during the procedure to avoid damaging vital blood vessels.
A blockage in the straight sinus can lead to increased intracranial pressure and significant neurological symptoms.
Anatomy students used detailed diagrams to locate the straight sinus among the cranial sinuses.
