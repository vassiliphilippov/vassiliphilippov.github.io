daylight-savings time

1. NOUN

/ ˈdeɪˌlaɪt ˈseɪvɪŋz taɪm / (AmE), / ˈdeɪ.laɪt ˈseɪ.vɪŋz taɪm / (BrE)
the practice of setting clocks forward by one hour during the warmer months to extend evening daylight
practice of adjusting clocks for more daylight

Examples

Remember to set your clock forward an hour for daylight-savings time this weekend.
Some regions do not observe daylight-savings time and stick to standard time year-round.
The switch to daylight-savings time can disrupt people's sleeping patterns temporarily.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: standard time - the official local time of a region before any adjustment for daylight savings
