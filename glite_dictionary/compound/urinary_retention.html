urinary retention

1. NOUN

/ ˈjʊrəˌnɛri rɪˈtɛnʃən / (AmE), / ˈjʊərɪnəri rɪˈtɛnʃən / (BrE)
the inability to completely or partially empty the bladder, resulting in the retention of urine
inability to empty the bladder
medical

Examples

The patient was admitted to hospital due to urinary retention and discomfort.
Some medications can cause urinary retention as a side effect, especially in older adults.
Frequent urination and urinary retention can both be symptoms of an enlarged prostate.

Synset Examples

he has a problem with urinary retention

Etymology

From Latin "urina" (urine) and Late Latin "retentionem" (a keeping back).
