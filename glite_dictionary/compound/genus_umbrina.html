genus Umbrina

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ʌmˈbriːnə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ʌmˈbriːnə / (BrE)
A genus of fish in the family Sciaenidae, containing 16 species commonly known as drums.
a genus of fish (drums)

Examples

The genus Umbrina includes several species found in coastal waters worldwide.
Biologists recently discovered a new member of the genus Umbrina in the Mediterranean Sea.
Many species in the genus Umbrina are economically important due to their popularity as food fish.

Etymology

From Latin 'Umbrina', a diminutive of 'umbra', meaning a type of fish.
