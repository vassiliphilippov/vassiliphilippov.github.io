rattail fish

1. NOUN

/ ˈræt.teɪl fɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈræt.teɪl fɪʃ / (BrE)
a deep-sea fish with a large head and body and a long, tapering tail, found in various oceans
deep-sea fish with tapering tail

Examples

The marine biologist discovered a rattail fish at a depth of 1,000 meters below the ocean surface.
Several specimens of the rattail fish were displayed in the aquarium's deep-sea exhibit.
With its long, slender tail, the rattail fish is easily distinguished from other deep-sea creatures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grenadier - another name for rattail fish, especially in scientific contexts

Etymology

Named for its tail resembling that of a rat; 'rattail' + 'fish'.
