base metal

1. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪs ˌmɛt̬l̩ / (AmE), / ˈbeɪs ˌmɛtəl / (BrE)
a metal that is common and not considered precious, such as iron, copper, or zinc
common, non-precious metal

Examples

Many coins are made from base metal rather than silver or gold.
Corrosion is a major concern when working with base metal components in engineering projects.
Copper, a base metal, is widely used in electrical wiring due to its conductivity.

Synset Examples

lead, iron, copper, tin, and zinc are base metals

Related Terms

  • Antonym: precious metal - a rare, highly valued metal such as gold or silver

Etymology

Formed from 'base' (in the sense of 'not noble' or 'not precious') + 'metal'.
