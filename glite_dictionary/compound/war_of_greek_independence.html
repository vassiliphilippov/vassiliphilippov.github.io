War of Greek Independence

1. NOUN

/ wɔr əv ɡrik ɪnˌdɛpənˈdəns / (AmE), / wɔː əv ɡriːk ɪnˌdɛpənˈdəns / (BrE)
the war (1821–1829) in which the Greeks fought to gain independence from Ottoman Turkish rule, resulting in the establishment of an independent Greek state with the support of Britain, France, and Russia
Greek war for independence from Turkey, 1821–1829

Examples

The War of Greek Independence inspired European supporters of revolutionary movements.
During the War of Greek Independence, many Philhellenes from abroad volunteered to fight alongside Greek forces.
The outcome of the War of Greek Independence led to the creation of the modern Greek state.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Greek War of Independence - alternative name for the War of Greek Independence
