Santa Fe Trail

1. PROPN

/ ˌsæn.tə ˈfeɪ ˌtreɪl / (AmE), / ˌsæntə ˈfeɪ ˌtreɪl / (BrE)
a historic route from Missouri to New Mexico that was an important pathway for settlers moving westward in the 19th century
historic route from Missouri to New Mexico, USA

Examples

Many pioneers followed the Santa Fe Trail in search of new opportunities in the American West.
The Santa Fe Trail played a vital role in the expansion of the United States during the 1800s.
Historic landmarks along the Santa Fe Trail attract tourists interested in America's frontier history.

Etymology

Named after Santa Fe, the capital of New Mexico, and the English word 'trail'.
