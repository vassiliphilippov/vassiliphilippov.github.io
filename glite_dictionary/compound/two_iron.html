two iron

1. NOUN

/ ˈtuː ˌaɪ.ɚn / (AmE), / ˈtuː ˌaɪ.ən / (BrE)
A golf club with a nearly vertical face, typically used for long-distance shots.
golf club with nearly vertical face

Examples

He managed to hit the ball 200 yards with a two iron.
The two iron is often considered one of the hardest clubs to use.
She decided to use her two iron for the long fairway shot.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: three iron - golf club with a slightly less vertical face than a two iron
  • Degree Higher: one iron - golf club with a more vertical face than a two iron
