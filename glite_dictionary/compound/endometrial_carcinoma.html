endometrial carcinoma

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛndoʊˈmitriəl ˌkɑrsiˈnoʊmə / (AmE), / ˌɛndəʊˈmiːtrɪəl ˌkɑːsɪˈnəʊmə / (BrE)
A malignant tumor arising from the lining of the uterus (endometrium); a type of uterine cancer.
cancer of the uterus lining
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with endometrial carcinoma after experiencing abnormal uterine bleeding.
Treatment options for endometrial carcinoma may include surgery, radiation, and chemotherapy.
Early detection of endometrial carcinoma often leads to a better prognosis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: uterine cancer - cancer of the uterus

Etymology

From 'endometrial' (pertaining to the endometrium) and 'carcinoma' (from Greek karkinōma, meaning cancerous ulcer).
