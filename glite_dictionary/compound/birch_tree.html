birch tree

1. NOUN

/ bɝːtʃ triː / (AmE), / bɜːtʃ triː / (BrE)
A deciduous tree or shrub of the genus Betula, typically having thin, peeling bark and slender branches.
deciduous tree of genus Betula

Examples

A birch tree stood alone at the edge of the meadow, its bark shining in the sun.
The wood from a birch tree is often used for furniture and plywood.
In autumn, the leaves of the birch tree turn a bright yellow before falling.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: birch - tree of the genus Betula

Etymology

From Old English 'birce' (birch) and 'tree'.
