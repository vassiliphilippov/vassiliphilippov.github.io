silver dollar

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪlvɚ ˈdɑlɚ / (AmE), / ˈsɪlvə ˈdɒlə / (BrE)
A southeastern European plant (Lunaria annua) cultivated for its fragrant purplish flowers and distinctive round, flat, silver-white seedpods often used in indoor decoration.
plant with silver-white decorative seedpods

Examples

The dried seedpods of the silver dollar look beautiful in floral arrangements.
She planted silver dollar seeds in her garden for their unique decorative appeal.
A vase filled with silver dollar stems brightened up the living room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: honesty - another common name for Lunaria annua, the 'silver dollar' plant

Etymology

Named for the resemblance of its round seedpods to silver coins called dollars.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɪlvɚ ˈdɑlər / (AmE), / ˈsɪlvə ˈdɒlə / (BrE)
a coin worth one dollar made chiefly of silver, especially one formerly issued in the United States
a one-dollar coin made of silver

Examples

My grandfather gave me a rare silver dollar for my coin collection.
The silver dollar is highly valued by collectors due to its historical significance.
During the nineteenth century, silver dollars were commonly used in everyday transactions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dollar - the basic monetary unit in the US and some other countries
