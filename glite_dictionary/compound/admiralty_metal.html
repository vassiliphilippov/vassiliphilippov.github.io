Admiralty Metal

1. NOUN

/ ˈædmɪrælti ˈmɛt̬l̩ / (AmE), / ˈædmɪrəlti ˈmɛtəl / (BrE)
A type of alpha-beta brass alloy containing tin, designed to resist corrosion in seawater; used especially in marine engineering.
tin-containing brass resistant to seawater

Examples

The ship's condenser tubes were made from Admiralty Metal for its corrosion resistance.
Some marine fittings require the use of Admiralty Metal to withstand harsh ocean conditions.
Engineers selected Admiralty Metal because of its proven durability in saltwater environments.

Etymology

Named after the British Admiralty, which specified its use in naval engineering.
