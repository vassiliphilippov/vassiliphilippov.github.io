Euxine Sea

1. PROPN

/ ˈjuːksaɪn siː / (AmE), / ˈjuːksaɪn siː / (BrE)
an old or literary name for the Black Sea, a large inland sea between southeastern Europe and Asia Minor
old name for the Black Sea

Examples

In ancient texts, the Euxine Sea is frequently mentioned as a route for Greek colonies.
The Euxine Sea forms a natural boundary between Europe and Asia Minor.
During their travels, they sailed along the northern coast of the Euxine Sea.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Black Sea - large inland sea between southeastern Europe and Asia Minor

Etymology

From Latin Euxinus (from Greek Euxeinos Pontos, meaning 'hospitable sea').
