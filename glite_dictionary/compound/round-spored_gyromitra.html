round-spored gyromitra

1. NOUN

/ raʊnd spɔrd ˌdʒaɪrəˈmaɪtrə / (AmE), / raʊnd spɔːd ˌdʒɪərəˈmiːtrə / (BrE)
A species of fungus with a brown, puffed-up fertile part, a rosy pink fluted stalk, and smooth round spores, typically found on hardwood debris east of the Great Plains.
a fungus with round spores and pink stalk

Examples

The round-spored gyromitra can easily be mistaken for a true morel by novice mushroom hunters.
Researchers collected several specimens of round-spored gyromitra from a woodland area after heavy rain.
You should use caution when handling the round-spored gyromitra, as some related species can be toxic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: false morel - a term for various species of Gyromitra, related false morels

Etymology

From Greek 'gyros' (round) and 'mitra' (headband, turban), referring to the mushroom's appearance.
