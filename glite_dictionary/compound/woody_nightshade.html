woody nightshade

1. NOUN

/ ˈwʊdi ˈnaɪtˌʃeɪd / (AmE), / ˈwʊdi ˈnaɪtʃeɪd / (BrE)
A poisonous perennial climbing plant of the Old World, with violet flowers and oval coral-red berries, also widespread as a weed in North America; also called bittersweet or Solanum dulcamara.
poisonous perennial vine with violet flowers and red berries

Examples

The bright red berries of woody nightshade are attractive but highly toxic if ingested.
Gardeners are advised to remove woody nightshade from areas where children play.
We found woody nightshade growing along the edge of the woods near the riverbank.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bittersweet - another common name for woody nightshade (Solanum dulcamara)
