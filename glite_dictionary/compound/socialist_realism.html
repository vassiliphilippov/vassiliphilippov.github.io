Socialist realism

1. NOUN

/ ˌsoʊʃəlɪst ˈriːəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌsəʊʃəlɪst ˈrɪəˌlɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
a style of realistic art that was developed in the Soviet Union and became the official artistic method in other socialist countries, characterized by the glorified depiction of communist values and everyday life.
official Soviet art style

Examples

Many Soviet artists were required to adopt Socialist realism in their paintings and sculptures during the 20th century.
The ideals of Socialist realism influenced literature, film, and visual arts throughout the Eastern Bloc.
Critics have often debated the artistic value of Socialist realism compared to other modern art movements.

Synset Examples

Socialist realism of the 1930s was part of a larger system of authoritative discourse developed through an interactive exchange between leaders and their supporters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: realism - art style focusing on lifelike representation

Etymology

From Russian социалистический реализм (sotsialisticheskiy realizm), meaning 'socialist realism'.
