on average

1. ADV

/ ɑn ˈæv.ɚ.ɪdʒ / (AmE), / ɒn ˈæv.ər.ɪdʒ / (BrE)
typically or usually; taking all cases into account and calculating the mean result
typically; as a mean or average

Examples

On average, students spend about two hours a day on homework.
Temperatures on average are warmer in July than in January.
House prices in this area on average exceed the national median.

Synset Examples

on average he watches three movies a week

Related Terms

  • Synonym: typically - in a way that shows what is usual or expected
  • Synonym: generally - in most cases; usually

2. ADV

/ ɑn ˈævɚɪdʒ / (AmE), / ɒn ˈævərɪdʒ / (BrE)
when the typical value is considered across a group or range of cases; usually or generally.
typically, when considered generally

Examples

On average, Americans spend less time commuting than Europeans.
The restaurant receives, on average, 200 customers each night.
Children in this school, on average, score above the national reading level.

Synset Examples

On average, it takes 30 minutes to complete the task.
The team scores, on average, three goals per game.
On average, she reads two books a month.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: typically - in a way that shows the most usual qualities of something
  • Synonym: generally - in most cases; usually
