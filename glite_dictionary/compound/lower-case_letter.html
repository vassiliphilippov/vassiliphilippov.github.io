lower-case letter

1. NOUN

/ ˈloʊɚ keɪs ˈlɛtɚ / (AmE), / ˈləʊə keɪs ˈlɛtə / (BrE)
a letter of the alphabet written in the smaller form, as opposed to a capital letter
a letter in its small (non-capital) form

Examples

Children are taught to write both upper-case and lower-case letters in kindergarten.
Please use only lower-case letters when creating your username.
The password must include at least one lower-case letter and one number.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: upper-case letter - a letter in its capital form

Etymology

Refers to the days of printing when small letters were kept in the lower case of a printer's type case.
