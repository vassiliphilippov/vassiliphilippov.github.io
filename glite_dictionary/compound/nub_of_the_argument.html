nub of the argument

1. NOUN

/ nʌb əv ði ˈɑːrɡjəmənt / (AmE), / nʌb əv ði ˈɑːɡjʊmənt / (BrE)
the central or most important point of an argument or discussion
central point in an argument

Examples

The nub of the argument was that both parties wanted the same outcome.
She quickly got to the nub of the argument and proposed a compromise.
Let's not get distracted—what is the nub of the argument here?

Synset Examples

The nub of her argument was surprisingly strong.
He found the nub of the issue after much discussion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crux - the decisive or most important point at issue
  • Synonym: essence - the intrinsic nature or indispensable quality of something

Etymology

From 'nub', meaning a small lump or protuberance; by extension, the core or heart of something.
