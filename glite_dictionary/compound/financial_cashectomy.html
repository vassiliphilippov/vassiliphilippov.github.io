financial cashectomy

1. NOUN

/ fəˈnænʃəl kæʃˈɛktəmi / (AmE), / faɪˈnænʃəl kæʃˈɛktəmi / (BrE)
a severe or drastic loss of money, especially as the result of spending, investment, or financial misfortune (often used humorously)
drastic loss of money (humorous)

Examples

After paying for the unexpected car repairs, I suffered a financial cashectomy that emptied my savings account.
Investing in that failed startup turned into a complete financial cashectomy for the Smiths.
The medical bills resulted in a massive financial cashectomy that took years to recover from.

Synset Examples

Her impulsive shopping habits led to a financial cashectomy.

Etymology

Formed from 'cash' (money) and '-ectomy' (surgical removal, from Greek 'ektomē'), suggesting the removal of cash.
