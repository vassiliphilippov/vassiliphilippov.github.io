preclinical trial

1. NOUN

/ priˈklɪnɪkəl ˈtraɪəl / (AmE), / priːˈklɪnɪkəl ˈtraɪəl / (BrE)
a laboratory study of a new drug or medical device on animals to gather evidence before testing in humans
laboratory study on animals before human trials
medical

Examples

The new cancer drug must undergo a preclinical trial before it can be tested in humans.
Results from the preclinical trial demonstrated promising effects on laboratory mice.
A successful preclinical trial is required before a pharmaceutical company seeks approval for human testing.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: clinical trial - a research study testing drugs or treatments in humans

Etymology

From 'pre-' meaning before and 'clinical trial', indicating the stage before testing in humans.
