to play pretend

1. VERB

/ pleɪ prɪˈtɛnd / (AmE), / pleɪ prɪˈtɛnd / (BrE)
to engage in imaginative or make-believe play, especially as a child
engage in imaginative play

Examples

The children love to play pretend and create stories about princesses and dragons.
Sometimes my little brother asks me to play pretend with his action figures.
At preschool, they play pretend in the kitchen area, cooking imaginary meals.

Synset Examples

The children played pretend in their cardboard castle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: make-believe - imaginative or pretend play
