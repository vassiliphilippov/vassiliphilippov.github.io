Petroselinum crispum tuberosum

1. NOUN

/ pɛtɹəˈsiːlɪnəm ˈkrɪspəm tuːˈbɛɹəsəm / (AmE), / pɛtɹəˈsiːlɪnəm ˈkrɪspəm tjuːˈbɛrəsəm / (BrE)
A variety of parsley with smooth leaves and an enlarged edible taproot, resembling a savory parsnip; also known as Hamburg parsley or root parsley.
root parsley; parsley with edible taproot

Examples

The chef added diced Petroselinum crispum tuberosum to the stew for its delicate flavor and texture.
Gardeners recommend planting Petroselinum crispum tuberosum in rich, well-drained soil for best root development.
Unlike regular parsley, Petroselinum crispum tuberosum is prized as much for its edible root as for its leaves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hamburg parsley - another name for Petroselinum crispum tuberosum
  • Synonym: parsnip - a root vegetable with a similar appearance and use
  • Synonym: parsley - common name for Petroselinum crispum; a leafy herb

Etymology

From New Latin: genus name 'Petroselinum' (parsley) + species 'crispum' (curly) + varietal 'tuberosum' (tuberous, having a tuber/root).
