gasoline engine

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡæsəˌliːn ˈɛn.dʒɪn / (AmE), / ˈɡæsəliːn ˈɛn.dʒɪn / (BrE)
An internal combustion engine that runs on gasoline, commonly used to power automobiles.
engine that runs on gasoline

Examples

The new car is powered by a highly efficient gasoline engine that delivers excellent mileage.
Many lawn mowers use a small gasoline engine for their operation.
Compared to a gasoline engine, electric motors are quieter and produce no emissions while running.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: diesel engine - an internal-combustion engine that runs on diesel fuel

Etymology

From 'gasoline', derived from 'gas' and the suffix '-oline', plus 'engine' from Old French 'engin'.
