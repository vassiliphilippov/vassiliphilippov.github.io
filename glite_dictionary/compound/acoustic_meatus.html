acoustic meatus

1. NOUN

/ əˈkuːstɪk miˈeɪtəs / (AmE), / əˈkjuːstɪk miˈeɪtəs / (BrE)
either of the passages in the outer ear that extend from the auricle to the tympanic membrane (eardrum)
ear passage from auricle to eardrum
medical

Examples

The doctor examined the acoustic meatus for signs of infection.
Excessive earwax can sometimes block the acoustic meatus and affect hearing.
Damage to the acoustic meatus may cause discomfort or ear pain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: auditory canal - the passage leading from the outer ear to the eardrum

Etymology

From Latin 'acusticus' (of hearing) and 'meatus' (passage, channel).
