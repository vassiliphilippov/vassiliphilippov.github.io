true bug

1. NOUN

/ tru bʌɡ / (AmE), / truː bʌɡ / (BrE)
an insect of the order Hemiptera, especially the suborder Heteroptera, characterized by piercing mouthparts and often referred to as a 'bug' in a strict scientific sense
insect of order Hemiptera (scientific sense)

Examples

The scientist explained that only insects in the order Hemiptera are considered true bugs.
Some people call any insect a bug, but only certain species are classified as true bugs.
The water strider is a well-known example of a true bug found on ponds and streams.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bug - an insect or similar small crawling animal

Etymology

Formed from 'true' + 'bug' to distinguish these insects from other so-called 'bugs'.
