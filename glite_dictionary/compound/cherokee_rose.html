Cherokee rose

1. NOUN

/ tʃəˈroʊki roʊz / (AmE), / tʃəˈrəʊki rəʊz / (BrE)
A climbing rose native to China with fragrant white blossoms, commonly grown as an ornamental plant.
climbing rose with white fragrant flowers

Examples

The Cherokee rose blooms every spring, covering the fence with delicate white flowers.
Gardeners admire the Cherokee rose for its beauty and sweet fragrance.
A single Cherokee rose was planted near the entrance of the old farmhouse.

Etymology

Named after the Cherokee people in the United States, but originally from China (Rosa laevigata).
