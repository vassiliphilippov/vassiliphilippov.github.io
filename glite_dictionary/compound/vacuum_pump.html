vacuum pump

1. NOUN

/ ˈvæk.juːm ˌpʌmp / (AmE), / ˈvæk.juːm ˌpʌmp / (BrE)
a device that removes air or gas from an enclosed space to create a vacuum
device for creating a vacuum

Examples

The laboratory technician turned on the vacuum pump to evacuate air from the chamber.
Our coffee maker uses a vacuum pump to brew espresso under low pressure.
A faulty vacuum pump can seriously affect the performance of a car's braking system.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: compressor - device that increases the pressure of a gas

Etymology

From Latin 'vacuum' (empty space) and English 'pump'.
