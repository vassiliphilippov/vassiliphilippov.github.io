graduated table

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡrædʒuˌeɪtɪd ˈteɪbəl / (AmE), / ˈɡrædjʊeɪtɪd ˈteɪbəl / (BrE)
an ordered scale or standard used for reference, typically divided into intervals or degrees for measurement or comparison
ordered reference scale or standard

Examples

The doctor used a graduated table to assess the severity of symptoms.
A graduated table helps rate performance consistently across different categories.
Researchers compared the data using a graduated table with defined scoring intervals.

Synset Examples

judging on a scale of 1 to 10

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scale - a system of ordered marks at fixed intervals used as a reference standard in measurement
  • Synonym: gauge - a standard measure used to check or compare accuracy

Etymology

From 'graduate' (to divide into steps or degrees) + 'table' (a systematic list or arrangement).
