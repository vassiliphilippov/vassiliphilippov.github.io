electrolytic capacitor

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌlɛktroʊˈlɪtɪk kəˈpæsɪtər / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛktrəˈlɪtɪk kəˈpæsɪtə / (BrE)
A type of fixed capacitor in which two electrodes are separated by an electrolyte, often used for storing electrical energy in electronic circuits.
capacitor with electrodes and electrolyte

Examples

The broken radio needed a new electrolytic capacitor to function properly.
Electrolytic capacitors are widely used for smoothing and filtering in power supplies.
Before touching any electrolytic capacitor, make sure it has fully discharged to avoid electric shock.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: capacitor - an electronic component storing electric charge

Etymology

From 'electrolytic', relating to electrolysis, and 'capacitor', from Latin 'capacitas' (capacity).
