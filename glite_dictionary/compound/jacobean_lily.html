Jacobean lily

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˈkoʊbiən ˈlɪli / (AmE), / dʒəˈkəʊbiən ˈlɪli / (BrE)
a Mexican bulbous plant (Sprekelia formosissima) cultivated for its showy, bright red solitary flower
Mexican bulbous plant with bright red flower

Examples

Every spring, the Jacobean lily adds a burst of red color to the garden.
The vibrant bloom of the Jacobean lily is highly prized by flower enthusiasts.
She planted a Jacobean lily near the pond to create a striking focal point.

Etymology

Named for King James I (Jacobus in Latin); possibly referencing the period in which it was introduced to Europe.
