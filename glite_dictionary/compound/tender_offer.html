tender offer

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛn.dɚ ˈɑː.fɚ / (AmE), / ˈtɛn.də ˈɒ.fə / (BrE)
an offer to purchase shares in a corporation, typically at a premium over the market price, for cash, securities, or both
offer to buy corporate shares at a premium

Examples

The company announced a tender offer to acquire all outstanding shares at $40 per share.
After the tender offer, the board of directors advised shareholders not to sell their stock.
Several investors accepted the tender offer because it was above the current market value.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: takeover bid - an attempt to gain control of a company by buying its shares
