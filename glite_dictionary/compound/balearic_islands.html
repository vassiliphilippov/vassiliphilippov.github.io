Balearic Islands

1. PROPN

/ bəˌlɛrɪk ˈaɪləndz / (AmE), / bəˌlɪərɪk ˈaɪləndz / (BrE)
an archipelago in the western Mediterranean Sea, off the eastern coast of Spain, comprising Mallorca, Menorca, Ibiza, and several smaller islands
Spanish Mediterranean archipelago

Examples

Many tourists visit the Balearic Islands every year for their beautiful beaches and vibrant nightlife.
The Balearic Islands are known for their unique culture and Mediterranean cuisine.
Ibiza is one of the most famous of the Balearic Islands.

Etymology

From Latin 'Baleares Insulae', the Roman name for the islands.
