cause célèbre

1. NOUN

/ kɔz səˈlɛbrə / (AmE), / kɔːz sɛˈlɛbrə / (BrE)
an event or incident that attracts a great deal of public attention, especially one involving controversy or scandal
an incident attracting public attention

Examples

The trial quickly became a cause célèbre and was covered by newspapers worldwide.
Environmental groups turned the local pollution dispute into a cause célèbre.
The disappearance of the journalist soon emerged as a cause célèbre, sparking protests across the country.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scandal - an action or event causing public outrage or censure

Etymology

From French 'cause célèbre' meaning 'famous case.'
