Niger franc

1. NOUN

/ ˈnaɪdʒər fræŋk / (AmE), / ˈniːʒə fræŋk / (BrE)
the basic monetary unit formerly used in Niger before the adoption of the CFA franc
former monetary unit of Niger

Examples

Before 1945, the Niger franc was the official currency used by the country.
The old coins issued in Niger francs are now collectors’ items.
With the adoption of the CFA franc, the Niger franc was discontinued.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: CFA franc - the currency currently used in Niger and other West African countries

Etymology

From French 'franc', formerly the basic monetary unit, and 'Niger', the name of the country.
