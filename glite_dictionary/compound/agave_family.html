agave family

1. NOUN

/ əˈɡɑːveɪ ˈfæməli / (AmE), / əˈɡɑːvi ˈfæməli / (BrE)
a family of chiefly tropical and xerophytic plants, including agaves and related genera, sometimes divided among the Amaryllidaceae or Liliaceae in some classifications
family of tropical plants, including agaves

Examples

The agave family is known for species that thrive in arid environments.
Century plants, a member of the agave family, are popular in desert landscaping.
Tequila is produced from plants belonging to the agave family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Agavaceae - the botanical family commonly called the agave family

Etymology

From the genus name Agave, derived from Greek 'agauos' meaning 'noble' or 'illustrious'.
