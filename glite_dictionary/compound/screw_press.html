screw press

1. NOUN

/ skruː prɛs / (AmE), / skruː prɛs / (BrE)
a machine in which pressure is applied by means of a screw mechanism, often used for pressing liquids from solids or shaping materials
machine that applies pressure using a screw

Examples

The ancient Romans used a screw press to extract olive oil from the fruit.
After harvesting grapes, the juice was separated from the skins using a screw press.
Modern recycling plants sometimes employ a screw press to dewater pulp and fibrous materials.

Synset Examples

The screw press was first invented and used by the Romans in the first century AD, primarily in wine and olive oil production.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hydraulic press - a machine using hydraulic force to exert pressure
