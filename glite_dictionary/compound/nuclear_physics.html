nuclear physics

1. NOUN

/ ˈnuːkliɚ ˈfɪzɪks / (AmE), / ˈnjuːkliə ˈfɪzɪks / (BrE)
The branch of physics that deals with the study of the structure and behavior of atomic nuclei.
study of atomic nuclei in physics

Examples

She decided to specialize in nuclear physics during her graduate studies.
Several advances in medicine have been made possible by research in nuclear physics.
The university offers a highly respected program in nuclear physics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: atomic physics - the branch of physics dealing with atoms as a system

Etymology

From Latin nucleus (kernel) and Greek physikē (science of nature).
