liquid detergent

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪkwɪd dɪˈtɝdʒənt / (AmE), / ˈlɪkwɪd dɪˈtɜːdʒənt / (BrE)
A cleaning substance in liquid form, used especially for washing clothes, dishes, or surfaces.
cleaning agent in liquid form

Examples

I always use liquid detergent when doing my laundry because it dissolves easily in water.
Some people prefer liquid detergent for washing dishes, as it creates more foam.
The instructions say to add two tablespoons of liquid detergent to the washing machine.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: powder detergent - detergent in powder form
