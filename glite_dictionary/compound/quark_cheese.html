quark cheese

1. NOUN

/ kwɑrk tʃiːz / (AmE), / kwɑːk tʃiːz / (BrE)
a fresh, soft, and smooth cheese made from pasteurized milk with a starter and rennet, commonly used in European cuisines
soft fresh cheese made from pasteurized milk

Examples

Many traditional German recipes call for quark cheese as the primary ingredient.
She spread a layer of quark cheese on her toast for breakfast.
The cheesecake was lighter than usual because it used quark cheese instead of cream cheese.

Etymology

From German 'Quark' (curd, soft cheese).
