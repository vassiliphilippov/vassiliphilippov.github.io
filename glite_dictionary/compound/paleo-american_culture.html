Paleo-American culture

1. NOUN

/ ˌpeɪli.oʊ əˈmɛr.ɪ.kən ˈkʌl.tʃər / (AmE), / ˌpeɪlɪəʊ əˈmɛrɪkən ˈkʌltʃə / (BrE)
the prehistoric culture of the earliest human inhabitants of North and South America
prehistoric culture of earliest Americans

Examples

Archaeologists discovered new artifacts that shed light on Paleo-American culture in the southwestern United States.
The study of Paleo-American culture helps us understand the migration patterns of early humans into the Americas.
Many sites associated with Paleo-American culture contain distinctive stone tools and hunting implements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Paleo-Indian - relating to the earliest humans in the Americas

Etymology

From 'paleo-', from Greek 'palaios' (ancient), and 'American'; referring to earliest periods of human presence in the Americas.
