genus Oryx

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˈɔːrɪks / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈɒrɪks / (BrE)
A genus of large antelopes native to arid regions of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula, characterized by long straight horns and distinct markings.
genus of large antelopes (Oryx)

Examples

The genus Oryx includes species well adapted to desert life.
Scientists are conducting population surveys of the genus Oryx throughout North Africa.
Members of the genus Oryx are known for their impressive horns and striking coat patterns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oryx - a large antelope with long straight horns

Etymology

From New Latin Oryx, from Greek orux (an antelope or gazelle).
