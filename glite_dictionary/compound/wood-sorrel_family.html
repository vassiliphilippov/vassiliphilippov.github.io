wood-sorrel family

1. NOUN

/ wʊd ˈsɔrəl ˈfæməli / (AmE), / wʊd ˈsɒrəl ˈfæmɪli / (BrE)
a family of flowering plants (Oxalidaceae), widely distributed herbs with compound leaves and five-parted flowers, including various species of wood-sorrel
plant family Oxalidaceae (wood-sorrel group)

Examples

The wood-sorrel family includes plants found in both temperate and tropical regions.
Many garden flowers, such as the common yellow oxalis, belong to the wood-sorrel family.
Researchers discovered a new genus within the wood-sorrel family in South America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Oxalidaceae - the botanical family name for the wood-sorrel family
