Belmont Stakes

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛl.mɑnt steɪks / (AmE), / ˈbɛl.mɒnt steɪks / (BrE)
An annual horse race for three-year-old Thoroughbreds held in Elmont, New York, and the third and final leg of the U.S. Triple Crown series.
annual prestigious American horse race

Examples

The Belmont Stakes draws thousands of spectators to Elmont each June.
Winning the Belmont Stakes completed the horse's quest for the Triple Crown.
He placed a bet on the Belmont Stakes hoping for a long-shot victory.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Kentucky Derby - the first leg of the U.S. Triple Crown horse races
  • Synonym: Preakness Stakes - the second leg of the U.S. Triple Crown horse races

Etymology

Named after August Belmont Sr., a 19th-century financier and racing enthusiast.
