family Caproidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ˌkæpɹɔɪˈdeɪ / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli ˌkæprɔɪˈdiː / (BrE)
A family of marine fishes known as boarfishes, recognized for their deep bodies and spiny fins.
a family of marine boarfishes

Examples

The family Caproidae includes several species commonly called boarfishes.
Marine biologists study the family Caproidae to understand their unique adaptations.
Fossils of the family Caproidae have been discovered in ancient seabeds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boarfish - a fish of the Caproidae family with a laterally compressed body

Etymology

From New Latin 'Caproidae', based on the type genus 'Capros'.
