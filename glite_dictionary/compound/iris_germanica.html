Iris germanica

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪ.rɪs dʒɚˈmæn.ɪ.kə / (AmE), / ˈaɪ.rɪs dʒəˈmæn.ɪ.kə / (BrE)
A large species of iris with showy purple or white flowers, native to central and southern Europe, often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
species of iris with purple or white flowers

Examples

The vibrant blooms of Iris germanica make it a popular choice for gardeners in Europe and North America.
In spring, the Iris germanica in our backyard garden produce spectacular white and purple flowers.
Botanists study Iris germanica for its hardiness and beautiful blossoms.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'Iris' (the genus name) and 'germanica' (meaning 'German'), introduced in scientific nomenclature.
