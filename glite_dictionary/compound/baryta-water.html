baryta-water

1. NOUN

/ bəˌraɪtə ˈwɔtɚ / (AmE), / bəˌraɪtə ˈwɔːtə / (BrE)
an aqueous solution of barium hydroxide, used mainly as a chemical reagent
aqueous solution of barium hydroxide
medical

Examples

Baryta-water is commonly used in laboratories to test for the presence of carbon dioxide.
The experiment required the addition of fresh baryta-water to observe any chemical reactions.
Always handle baryta-water with care, as it contains barium hydroxide which is toxic.

Synset Examples

The monohydrate (x =1) is known as baryta, or baryta-water, it is one of the principal compounds of barium.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: baryta - barium oxide or barium compounds used in chemistry

Etymology

From 'baryta' (heavy spar, barium compounds) and 'water.'
