baryon number

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæriˌɑn ˈnʌmbɚ / (AmE), / ˈbæriɒn ˈnʌmbə / (BrE)
the difference between the number of baryons and antibaryons in a system, a value conserved in particle interactions
difference between number of baryons and antibaryons

Examples

The baryon number is preserved in all known physical reactions.
In this process, the total baryon number remains unchanged.
Physicists track changes in baryon number when studying particle collisions.

Etymology

From 'baryon', a subatomic particle, and 'number', referring to its quantity in a system.
