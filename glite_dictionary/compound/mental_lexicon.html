mental lexicon

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛn.tl ˈlɛk.sɪ.kɑn / (AmE), / ˈmɛn.tl ˈlɛk.sɪ.kɒn / (BrE)
the mental store of words and their meanings possessed by an individual language user
a person's internal store of words and meanings

Examples

Researchers study how the mental lexicon is organized in the brain.
A large mental lexicon can make language learning much easier.
Damage to certain brain areas can affect a person's mental lexicon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vocabulary - the body of words used in a particular language or by a particular person

Etymology

From Latin 'lexicon' (wordbook, dictionary), used in linguistics to refer to the mind's word store.
