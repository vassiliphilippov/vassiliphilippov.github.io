white violet

1. NOUN

/ waɪt ˈvaɪ.əlɪt / (AmE), / waɪt ˈvaɪ.əlɪt / (BrE)
A short-stemmed violet native to eastern North America, with fragrant white flowers often veined with purple.
species of violet with white flowers

Examples

The white violet blooms early in the spring along woodland paths.
She picked a bouquet of white violets for her grandmother from the garden.
Unlike other varieties, the white violet has delicate purple veins on its petals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: violet - any plant of the genus Viola, typically with violet-colored flowers

2. NOUN

/ waɪt ˈvaɪ.ə.lət / (AmE), / waɪt ˈvaɪ.ə.lət / (BrE)
A tall North American perennial plant with heart-shaped leaves and white flowers marked with purple streaks.
North American plant with white, purple-streaked flowers

Examples

The white violet blooms each spring in the shaded woodlands of the Northeast.
We spotted several white violets growing near the edge of the creek.
Her bouquet included a single delicate white violet among the greenery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: violet - a small plant with purple, blue, or white flowers
