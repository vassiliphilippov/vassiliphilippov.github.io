swamp bay

1. NOUN

/ swɑmp beɪ / (AmE), / swɒmp beɪ / (BrE)
a shrub or small tree with fragrant white flowers, native to the southeastern United States, commonly found in swamps and wet areas
shrubs or trees of SE US wetlands

Examples

The swamp bay is well adapted to the wet soils of southern marshlands.
Many birds nest in the branches of the swamp bay during the spring.
We identified a swamp bay by its small, fragrant white flowers in the nature reserve.
