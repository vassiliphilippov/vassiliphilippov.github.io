Linotype machine

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪnəˌtaɪp məˈʃin / (AmE), / ˈlʌɪnətʌɪp məˈʃiːn / (BrE)
a machine for typesetting text operated by keyboard, which casts an entire line of type as a single piece of metal
keyboard-operated typesetting machine

Examples

The Linotype machine revolutionized newspaper printing in the late nineteenth century.
Many operators trained for years to master the Linotype machine in busy print shops.
The museum displays a restored Linotype machine alongside other historic printing equipment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: typesetter - person or machine that arranges type for printing

Etymology

From Linotype, a brand name formed from 'line of type' (referring to its function).
