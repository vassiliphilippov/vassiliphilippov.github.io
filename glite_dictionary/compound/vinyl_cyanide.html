vinyl cyanide

1. NOUN

/ ˌvaɪnəl saɪˈænaɪd / (AmE), / ˌvaɪ.nɪl saɪˈæ.naɪd / (BrE)
a colorless, volatile liquid compound (C3H3N) used chiefly in the manufacture of plastics and synthetic fibers; also known as acrylonitrile
a colorless liquid chemical compound (acrylonitrile)

Examples

Vinyl cyanide is commonly used in the production of acrylic fibers and plastics.
Exposure to vinyl cyanide should be minimized due to its toxic properties.
Researchers found traces of vinyl cyanide in the atmosphere of Saturn's moon Titan.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acrylonitrile - a colorless liquid used in plastics and fibers, also known as vinyl cyanide

Etymology

From 'vinyl', referring to the ethylene group, and 'cyanide', from Greek 'kyanos' (dark blue).
