genus Taenia

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs tiˈniːə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs tiˈniːə / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Taeniidae, consisting of tapeworms that are parasitic in the intestines of vertebrates, including humans.
genus of parasitic tapeworms
medical

Examples

The genus Taenia includes tapeworms that can cause diseases in humans and animals.
Research on the genus Taenia has led to a better understanding of parasitic infections.
Veterinarians often study the life cycle of the genus Taenia to help prevent livestock diseases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tapeworm - a parasitic flatworm from the class Cestoda

Etymology

From Latin 'taenia' meaning 'ribbon' or 'tape', referencing the shape of the worms.
