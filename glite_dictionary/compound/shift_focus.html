to shift focus

1. VERB

/ ʃɪft ˈfoʊkəs / (AmE), / ʃɪft ˈfəʊkəs / (BrE)
to change the direction of one's attention or concentration to something different
change attention to something different

Examples

After the merger, the company decided to shift focus toward sustainable technologies.
It's sometimes necessary to shift focus when current strategies aren't effective.
The teacher asked the class to shift focus to the next topic on the syllabus.

Synset Examples

It's time to shift our focus to new projects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: redirect attention - to direct attention towards a new target
  • Degree Lower: concentrate - to focus all attention on a specific thing
