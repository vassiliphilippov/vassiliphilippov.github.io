Model T

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɑː.dl ˈtiː / (AmE), / ˌmɒd.l ˈtiː / (BrE)
an early, mass-produced automobile introduced by Ford Motor Company in 1908, regarded as the first affordable car for many Americans
early mass-produced Ford automobile

Examples

The Model T revolutionized transportation and made cars accessible to the average person.
Collectors sometimes restore an original Model T to working condition for classic car shows.
Henry Ford's assembly line allowed the Model T to be produced quickly and sold at a lower price.

Etymology

The name comes from Ford's system of sequential model lettering; 'T' indicates its place in the sequence.
