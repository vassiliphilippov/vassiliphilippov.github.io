tidal basin

1. NOUN

/ ˈtaɪdəl ˈbeɪsən / (AmE), / ˈtaɪdəl ˈbeɪsən / (BrE)
A pool or enclosed area near a coast that fills with water at high tide, often used for docks or flood control.
enclosed area filled at high tide

Examples

Boats enter the tidal basin when the water level rises during high tide.
The city constructed a tidal basin to help control flooding in the harbor area.
Many visitors enjoy walking around the tidal basin when the cherry blossoms are in bloom.

Etymology

From 'tidal' (relating to tides) + 'basin' (bowl-shaped area).
