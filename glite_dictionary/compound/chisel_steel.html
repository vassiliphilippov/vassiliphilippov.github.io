chisel steel

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɪzəl stiːl / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪzl stiːl / (BrE)
a hard, strong type of steel used in making chisels and cutting tools
steel used for making chisels

Examples

The blade was made from high-quality chisel steel for lasting sharpness.
Artisans prefer chisel steel because it holds an edge even after repeated use.
Some chisel steel is specially alloyed to improve resistance to wear and deformation.
