common zebra

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmən ˈziːbrə / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən ˈziːbrə / (BrE)
a species of zebra (Equus quagga) found in the plains of central and eastern Africa, known for its distinctive black and white stripes
zebra species of central/eastern African plains

Examples

The common zebra is often seen grazing in the grasslands of Kenya.
Unlike the more elusive Grevy’s zebra, the common zebra lives in large herds.
Tourists love photographing the striking stripes of the common zebra during safaris.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zebra - an African wild horse with black-and-white stripes
