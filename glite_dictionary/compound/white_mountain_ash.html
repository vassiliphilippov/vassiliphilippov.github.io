white mountain ash

1. NOUN

/ waɪt ˈmaʊntən æʃ / (AmE), / waɪt ˈmaʊntɪn æʃ / (BrE)
A large tree with dark, compact bark on the lower trunk and smooth, white bark above, valued for its lumber similar to that of European or American ashes.
a tree with white upper bark, used for lumber

Examples

The white mountain ash is prized for its straight, strong timber.
Above the dark bark, the white mountain ash shows off its characteristic pale trunk.
Several white mountain ashes grow along the forest's upper slopes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mountain ash - a tree of the genus Sorbus, known for its ash-like leaves and orange-red berries
