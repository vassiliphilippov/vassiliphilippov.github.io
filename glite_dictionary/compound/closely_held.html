closely held

1. ADJ

/ ˈkloʊsli hɛld / (AmE), / ˈkləʊsli hɛld / (BrE)
(of a company) owned by a small number of shareholders, with shares not publicly traded.
owned by a few shareholders (business)
legal

Examples

The company remained a closely held business after the founder's retirement.
Because it is closely held, the firm does not release its finances publicly.
A closely held corporation often makes decisions more efficiently than a public one.

Synset Examples

a closely-held corporation

Related Terms

  • Antonym: publicly traded - having shares that are bought and sold on the open stock market
