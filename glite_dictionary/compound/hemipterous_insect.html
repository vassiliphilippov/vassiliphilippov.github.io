hemipterous insect

1. NOUN

/ hɛˈmɪptɚəs ˈɪnsɛkt / (AmE), / hɛˈmɪptərəs ˈɪnsɛkt / (BrE)
an insect characterized by having sucking mouthparts and forewings that are thickened and leathery at the base, usually undergoing incomplete metamorphosis; member of the order Hemiptera
insect with sucking mouthparts (Hemiptera)
medical

Examples

The aphid is a well-known hemipterous insect that damages many garden plants.
A hemipterous insect can often be recognized by the unique structure of its wings and mouthparts.
Scientists studied the feeding habits of a rare hemipterous insect found in the rainforest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: true bug - common name for insects of the order Hemiptera

Etymology

From Greek 'hēmi-' (half) + 'pteron' (wing), describing the partly thickened forewings.
