preliminary prospectus

1. NOUN

/ prɪˈlɪmɪˌnɛri prəˈspɛktəs / (AmE), / prɪˈlɪmɪnəri prəˈspɛktəs / (BrE)
a first draft of a prospectus for securities, issued to potential investors before the final version and marked to indicate that its terms may change
first draft of a securities prospectus
legal

Examples

The company filed its preliminary prospectus with the financial regulator ahead of the initial public offering.
Potential investors were given a preliminary prospectus outlining the terms of the new bond issue.
The preliminary prospectus contained several disclaimers noting that important details were subject to change.

Synset Examples

because some portions of the cover page are printed in red ink a preliminary prospectus is sometimes called a red herring

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prospectus - document offering details about a security or investment
  • Antonym: final prospectus - the completed version of a securities prospectus given to investors
