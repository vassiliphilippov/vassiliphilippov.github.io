corner man

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔrnər mæn / (AmE), / ˈkɔːnə mæn / (BrE)
(historical) In a minstrel show, a man seated at one end of the line of performers who engages in humorous dialogue with the interlocutor.
performer at end of minstrel show line
archaic

Examples

During the performance, the corner man exchanged witty remarks with the interlocutor, making the audience laugh.
In traditional minstrel shows, a corner man often played an exaggerated comedic character.
The corner men would sit at each end of the line, ready to banter throughout the act.
