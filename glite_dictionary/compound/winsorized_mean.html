Winsorized mean

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪnsəˌraɪzd min / (AmE), / ˈwɪnzəˌraɪzd miːn / (BrE)
A statistical average calculated by replacing the extreme values in a data set with the closest remaining values before computing the mean.
statistical mean after limiting extreme values

Examples

After removing the most extreme values, the Winsorized mean provided a more robust estimate of central tendency.
Researchers often use the Winsorized mean to minimize the effect of outliers in data analysis.
The Winsorized mean can be particularly useful when the data contains several unusually high or low observations.

Synset Examples

The main results include a necessary and sufficient condition for asymptotic normality of the Winsorized mean under the assumption that rn → ∞, sn → ∞, rnn−1 → 0, snn−1 → 0 and F is convex at infinity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mean - the numerical average of a set of values
  • Synonym: median - the value separating the higher half from the lower half of a data sample

Etymology

From the name of Charles P. Winsor, an American statistician who proposed the method.
