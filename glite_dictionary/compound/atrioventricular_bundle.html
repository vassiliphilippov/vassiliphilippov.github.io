atrioventricular bundle

1. NOUN

/ ˌeɪtrioʊvɛnˈtrɪkjələr ˈbʌndəl / (AmE), / ˌeɪtrɪəʊvɛnˈtrɪkjʊlə ˈbʌndəl / (BrE)
a bundle of specialized muscle fibers in the heart that transmits impulses from the atrioventricular node to the ventricles, causing them to contract
bundle of fibers carrying heart impulses
medical

Examples

The atrioventricular bundle ensures that the ventricles contract after the atria.
A blockage in the atrioventricular bundle can lead to serious heart rhythm problems.
The cardiologist explained the role of the atrioventricular bundle in conducting electrical impulses in the heart.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bundle of His - another name for the atrioventricular bundle in the heart

Etymology

From 'atrioventricular', meaning relating to the atria and ventricles of the heart, and 'bundle'.
