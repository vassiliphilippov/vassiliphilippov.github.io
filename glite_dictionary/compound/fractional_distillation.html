fractional distillation

1. NOUN

/ ˈfrækʃənl dɪˌstɪləˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈfrækʃənəl dɪˌstɪlˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of separating a mixture of liquids into fractions by distillation, typically by taking advantage of differences in boiling points
separation of liquids by distillation into fractions

Examples

Fractional distillation is commonly used to separate crude oil into its various components.
In the laboratory, fractional distillation helps isolate ethanol from a fermented mixture.
The chemical plant relies on fractional distillation columns to purify solvents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: distillation - the action of purifying a liquid by successive evaporation and condensation

Etymology

fractional: from Latin *fractio* (a breaking), distillation: from Latin *distillare* (to drip down)
