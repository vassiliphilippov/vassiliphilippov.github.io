Yuma language

1. NOUN

/ ˈjuːmə ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈjuːmə ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the language of the Yuma people, belonging to the Yuman language family and spoken in parts of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico
language of the Yuma people

Examples

Linguists have documented the grammar and vocabulary of the Yuma language for research and preservation.
She grew up listening to traditional stories told in the Yuma language by her grandparents.
Revitalization efforts aim to teach the Yuma language to younger generations in local schools.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Yuman language - a group of related languages spoken by various Native American peoples of the southwestern U.S. and Mexico

Etymology

From the name Yuma, referring to the indigenous people of the lower Colorado River region.
