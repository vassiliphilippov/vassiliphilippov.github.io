fringed orchis

1. NOUN

/ frɪndʒd ˈɔr.kɪs / (AmE), / frɪndʒd ˈɔː.kɪs / (BrE)
Any of several summer-flowering North American orchids distinguished by having a fringed or deeply cut lip.
a North American orchid with a fringed lip

Examples

The fringed orchis blooms in wet meadows during the summer months.
Botanists were excited to discover a rare fringed orchis along the woodland trail.
The delicate petals of the fringed orchis are easily recognized by their deeply divided fringe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orchid - a plant of a diverse and widespread family with showy flowers

Etymology

Formed from 'fringed', meaning decorated with a fringe or border, and 'orchis', from Greek *orkhis* (orchid).
