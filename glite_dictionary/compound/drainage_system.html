drainage system

1. NOUN

/ ˈdreɪnɪdʒ ˌsɪstəm / (AmE), / ˈdreɪnɪdʒ ˌsɪstəm / (BrE)
A network of artificial or natural channels, pipes, or ditches designed to remove excess water from an area.
system for removing excess water

Examples

During heavy rains, the city's drainage system prevents flooding in residential areas.
The farm installed a new drainage system to protect crops from standing water.
Engineers are inspecting the drainage system to ensure it can handle increased rainfall.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sewerage system - a network of pipes for carrying sewage
