stock epithet

1. NOUN

/ stɑk ˈɛpɪˌθɛt / (AmE), / stɒk ˈɛpɪˌθɛt / (BrE)
a descriptive word or phrase, often repeated, that is used by an author to characterize a person or thing, especially in epic poetry or oral storytelling
repeated descriptive phrase in literature

Examples

The poet often used a stock epithet to describe heroes, such as 'swift-footed Achilles.'
Ancient storytellers relied on the stock epithet to maintain the rhythm and memory of their tales.
In Homeric poetry, the phrase 'rosy-fingered Dawn' is a classic example of a stock epithet.

Synset Examples

Stock epithets are most commonly used in literature which is based on a strong oral tradition, for example, some types of epic poetry and ballad.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: epithet - a word or phrase used to characterize someone or something

Etymology

From 'epithet', from Greek 'epitheton' (added), and 'stock' meaning standard or conventional.
