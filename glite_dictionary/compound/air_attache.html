air attache

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛr ˌætəˈʃeɪ / (AmE), / ˈeə ˌatəˈʃeɪ / (BrE)
a military officer attached to a diplomatic mission who serves as an expert on air force matters
military air force official at an embassy

Examples

The embassy's air attache coordinated joint air defense exercises with the host country.
As air attache, she advised the ambassador on all matters relating to aviation security.
Several foreign air attaches attended the international air show as official observers.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: military attache - a commissioned officer in the armed forces attached to a diplomatic mission
  • Degree Lower: naval attache - a naval officer attached to a diplomatic mission representing naval interests
  • Degree Higher: attache - a specialist assigned to a diplomatic mission

Etymology

From French 'attaché' (attached), referring to a specialist assigned to a diplomatic post.
