meadow fescue

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛdoʊ ˈfɛskjuː / (AmE), / ˈmɛdəʊ ˈfɛskjuː / (BrE)
A perennial grass with wide, flat leaves, cultivated in Europe and America for permanent pasture, hay, and lawns.
a grass used for pasture and hay

Examples

The farmer planted meadow fescue to improve the quality of his grazing fields.
Lawns sown with meadow fescue are known for their lush green appearance.
Researchers are studying meadow fescue for its resilience to different climates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fescue - a type of grass from the genus Festuca
