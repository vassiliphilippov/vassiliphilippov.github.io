the ould one

1. NOUN

/ ði oʊld wʌn / (AmE), / ði əʊld wʌn / (BrE)
an elderly person, often regarded as wise or respectable, especially in Irish or regional dialects
elderly person (often wise; dialect)

Examples

Every village in the area had the ould one whom people visited for advice.
After dinner, the children gathered around the ould one for stories.
Many respect the ould one due to her years of experience and kindness.

Synset Examples

She often visits the ould one for wisdom.

Etymology

From Irish English dialect; 'ould' is an altered form of 'old'.
