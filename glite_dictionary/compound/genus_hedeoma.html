genus Hedeoma

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒinəs hɪˈdiːəmə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs hɪˈdiːəmə / (BrE)
A small genus of aromatic American herbs in the mint family, including American pennyroyal.
a genus of American mint herbs

Examples

The genus Hedeoma is commonly known for species like the American pennyroyal.
Several species within the genus Hedeoma have been used in traditional herbal remedies.
Researchers studied the aromatic compounds present in the genus Hedeoma for their medicinal properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pennyroyal - an aromatic herb of the mint family, common species in Hedeoma

Etymology

From Latin 'genus' (kind, type) and New Latin 'Hedeoma', from Greek 'hēdēos' (sweet) and 'osmē' (smell).
