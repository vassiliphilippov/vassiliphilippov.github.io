plasmatic energy

1. NOUN

/ plæzˈmætɪk ˈɛnɚdʒi / (AmE), / plæzˈmætɪk ˈɛnədʒi / (BrE)
a dynamic or vibrant form of energy, often used metaphorically to describe lively, electrifying vitality
dynamic, vibrant energy

Examples

The artist infused her performance with plasmatic energy that captivated the audience.
You could feel his plasmatic energy light up the entire room during the celebration.
The novel's hero was known for his relentless drive and plasmatic energy.

Synset Examples

His plasmatic energy made the event unforgettable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vitality - the state of being strong and active; energy
  • Antonym: lethargy - the state of being sluggish or lacking energy

Etymology

From 'plasmatic,' relating to plasma, plus 'energy,' from Greek energeia (activity, operation).
