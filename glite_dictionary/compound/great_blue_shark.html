great blue shark

1. NOUN

/ ɡreɪt blu ʃɑrk / (AmE), / ɡreɪt bluː ʃɑːk / (BrE)
a large, slender, pelagic shark with a blue back and white belly, found in oceans worldwide and considered dangerous to humans, especially after shipwrecks
a large pelagic shark with blue coloring

Examples

The great blue shark is often seen near the ocean's surface, searching for prey.
Divers sometimes encounter the great blue shark in the open water, especially in temperate zones.
Historically, shipwreck survivors feared the presence of the great blue shark in the aftermath of maritime disasters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blue shark - a species of shark known for its blue coloration
