phonetic alphabet

1. NOUN

/ fəˈnɛɾɪk ˈæl.fəˌbɛt / (AmE), / fəˈnɛtɪk ˈæl.fə.bɛt / (BrE)
an alphabet in which each symbol represents a distinct sound of speech, used for accurate representation of pronunciation
alphabet representing speech sounds

Examples

Linguists use the phonetic alphabet to transcribe speech sounds from different languages.
The International Phonetic Alphabet is a widely used system in language studies.
Learning the phonetic alphabet helped her improve her pronunciation in English.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alphabet - a set of letters used in writing a language

Etymology

From Greek phōnētikos (vocal, relating to sound) and Late Latin alphabetum.
