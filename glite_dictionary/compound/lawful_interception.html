lawful interception

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɔfəl ˌɪntərˈsɛpʃən / (AmE), / ˈlɔːfʊl ˌɪntəˈsɛpʃən / (BrE)
the legally authorized monitoring or surveillance of electronic communications, typically by government agencies for security or law enforcement purposes
legally approved communications surveillance
legal

Examples

The new regulations require telephone companies to enable lawful interception by security services.
Banking systems must ensure that lawful interception can be performed in accordance with national laws.
During investigations, lawful interception is used to collect evidence from suspects' communications.

Synset Examples

Lawful interception helps prevent criminal activities.
The agency employed lawful interception for security purposes.
They discussed lawful interception in the cybersecurity meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: surveillance - the close observation of a person or group, especially one under suspicion
  • Synonym: wiretap - the act of secretly listening to or recording telephone or internet conversations
