walled garden

1. NOUN

/ wɔld ˈɡɑrdn̩ / (AmE), / wɔːld ˈɡɑːdn̩ / (BrE)
a digital environment or platform where access is restricted and content or data is controlled by the provider, limiting interactions with outside systems or websites
restricted online environment

Examples

Many tech companies create a walled garden to keep users within their ecosystem.
The walled garden approach can make it difficult to share data with other platforms.
Some people find the restrictions of a walled garden frustrating, while others appreciate the security.

Synset Examples

He prefers the security of a walled garden for his data.
Their website feels like a walled garden, limiting access.
The app operates as a walled garden, keeping users contained.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: open platform - a digital environment with minimal restrictions and external access allowed

Etymology

From the literal 'walled garden' (a garden enclosed by walls), figuratively applied in the 20th century to digital platforms.
