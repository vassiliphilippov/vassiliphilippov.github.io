full radiator

1. NOUN

/ fʊl ˌreɪdiˈeɪɾɚ / (AmE), / fʊl ˌreɪdiˈeɪtə / (BrE)
a hypothetical object that absorbs all electromagnetic radiation that falls on it, used especially in physics to describe idealized energy absorption and emission.
object absorbing all electromagnetic radiation

Examples

A full radiator is a useful model in the study of thermal radiation.
Physicists often compare real objects to a full radiator when calculating energy emission.
At a given temperature, a full radiator emits the maximum possible amount of radiation.

Synset Examples

a black body maintained at a constant temperature is a full radiator at that temperature because the radiation reaching and leaving it must be in equilibrium

Related Terms

  • Synonym: black body - an idealized physical body that absorbs all incident electromagnetic radiation
