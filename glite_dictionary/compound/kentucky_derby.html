Kentucky Derby

1. NOUN

/ kɛnˈtʌki ˈdɝbi / (AmE), / kɪnˈtʌki ˈdɜːbi / (BrE)
an annual horse race for three-year-old Thoroughbreds, held at Churchill Downs in Louisville, Kentucky
famous American horse race
american

Examples

The Kentucky Derby is often called 'The Most Exciting Two Minutes in Sports.'
She wore a wide-brimmed hat to the Kentucky Derby last year.
Winning the Kentucky Derby is the dream of every horse owner and jockey in America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Derby - a horse race, especially an important one

Etymology

Named after the state of Kentucky and inspired by the British Derby at Epsom.
