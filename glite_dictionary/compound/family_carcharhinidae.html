family Carcharhinidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli kɑr.kəˈraɪ.nɪ.diː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli kɑː.kəˈraɪ.nɪ.diː / (BrE)
A large family of living sharks, found worldwide, especially in tropical waters, characterized by the absence of spines in the dorsal fin; includes requiem sharks such as tiger sharks and soupfin sharks.
family of requiem sharks (including tiger and soupfin sharks)

Examples

The family Carcharhinidae contains many well-known sharks, such as the bull shark and tiger shark.
Marine biologists study the family Carcharhinidae to understand shark diversity and adaptations.
Members of the family Carcharhinidae are commonly found in warm, tropical seas around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: requiem shark - a shark of the family Carcharhinidae
  • Synonym: tiger shark - a large requiem shark (Carcharhinus leucas)

Etymology

From Greek 'karcharos' (sharp or jagged) and 'rhinos' (nose), with '-idae' as a zoological family suffix.
