lobbying expense

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɑː.bi.ɪŋ ɪkˈspɛns / (AmE), / ˈlɒb.i.ɪŋ ɪkˈspens / (BrE)
money spent to promote or influence legislation, often in the context of business or political advocacy
money spent on promoting legislation

Examples

Companies must report all lobbying expenses to comply with tax regulations.
The organization allocated a significant portion of its budget to lobbying expenses last year.
New restrictions limit which lobbying expenses can be claimed as deductions.

Synset Examples

many lobbying expenses are deductible by a taxpayer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lobbying - attempting to influence legislation or government policy
  • Degree Lower: campaign expense - money spent to support a political campaign
