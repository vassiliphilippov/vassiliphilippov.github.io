ripping chisel

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɪpɪŋ ˌtʃɪzəl / (AmE), / ˈrɪpɪŋ ˌtʃɪzl / (BrE)
a long, sturdy chisel with a slightly bent cutting edge, used for heavy prying or cleaning out mortises in woodwork
sturdy bent-ended chisel for heavy prying

Examples

He used a ripping chisel to pry out the old wooden beams.
A ripping chisel is ideal for cleaning out deep mortises in timber framing.
Before replacing the joint, the carpenter cleared away debris with a ripping chisel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chisel - a hand tool with a flat, sharp edge for carving or cutting hard materials
