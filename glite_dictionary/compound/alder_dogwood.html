alder dogwood

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔldɚ ˈdɔɡˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈɔːldə ˈdɒɡˌwʊd / (BrE)
A small tree native to Europe, known for its reddish stems and white flowers, belonging to the dogwood family.
European small tree in the dogwood family

Examples

The alder dogwood produces clusters of small white flowers in early summer.
Gardeners often plant alder dogwood for its striking red stems in winter.
The leaves of the alder dogwood turn a beautiful purple-red in autumn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dogwood - any shrub or tree of the genus Cornus, known for showy bracts and colorful stems
