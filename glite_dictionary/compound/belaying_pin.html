belaying pin

1. NOUN

/ bəˈleɪɪŋ pɪn / (AmE), / bɪˈleɪɪŋ pɪn / (BrE)
a wood or metal bar used on ships or in climbing to secure a rope by looping it around the pin
bar for securing ropes (nautical/climbing)

Examples

The sailor quickly wrapped the rope around the belaying pin to hold the sail in place.
During the tour of the old ship, we saw several wooden belaying pins along the railing.
Mountain climbers sometimes use a modern equivalent of a belaying pin to secure their ropes.

Etymology

From the verb 'belay' (to secure a rope) and 'pin' (a fastening device).
