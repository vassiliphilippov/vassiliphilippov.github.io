family Sterculiaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌfæməli stərˌkjuːliˈeɪsiˌaɪ / (AmE), / ˌfamɪli stɜːˌkjuːlɪˈeɪsɪˌiː / (BrE)
A large family of flowering plants in the order Malvales, previously recognized as containing several genera including cacao and kola.
family of flowering plants (formerly Malvales)

Examples

The family Sterculiaceae includes notable species such as the cacao tree and the kola nut.
Botanists once classified several tropical trees under the family Sterculiaceae, though recent studies have revised this.
Members of the family Sterculiaceae are mostly found in warm, tropical regions.

Etymology

From the genus name Sterculia, after the Roman god Sterculius, with Latin suffix -aceae denoting a plant family.
