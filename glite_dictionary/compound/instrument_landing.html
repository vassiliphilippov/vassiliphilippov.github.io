instrument landing

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪnstrəmənt ˈlændɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɪnstrəmənt ˈlændɪŋ / (BrE)
an aircraft landing carried out solely by using the aircraft's instruments, without visual reference to the ground or runway
aircraft landing using only instruments

Examples

The pilot performed an instrument landing due to dense fog at the airport.
Safety procedures demand strict adherence to protocols during an instrument landing.
Thanks to training, she was confident in executing an instrument landing in poor weather conditions.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: visual landing - an aircraft landing made using visual references
