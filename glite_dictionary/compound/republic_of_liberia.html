Republic of Liberia

1. PROPN

/ rɪˈpʌblɪk əv laɪˈbɪriə / (AmE), / rɪˈpʌblɪk əv laɪˈbɪəriə / (BrE)
A country in West Africa, founded in 1822 by formerly enslaved people from the United States and proclaimed a republic in 1847.
country in West Africa

Examples

The Republic of Liberia declared its independence in 1847, making it Africa's first modern republic.
She traveled to the Republic of Liberia for a research project on post-colonial African states.
The flag of the Republic of Liberia closely resembles that of the United States due to its historical origins.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Liberia - country in West Africa

Etymology

Liberia is named from Latin 'liber' (free), reflecting its founding by freed people.
