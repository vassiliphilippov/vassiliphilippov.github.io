dovish comment

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʌvɪʃ ˈkɑːmɛnt / (AmE), / ˈdʌvɪʃ ˈkɒmɛnt / (BrE)
a statement or remark expressing a preference for peace, compromise, or less aggressive action, especially in politics or economics
peace-seeking or conciliatory remark

Examples

The official's dovish comment reassured investors that interest rates would remain low.
Her dovish comment suggested support for diplomatic negotiations rather than military action.
Analysts interpreted the central banker's dovish comment as a sign of a softer monetary policy.

Synset Examples

Her dovish comments eased the concerns about inflation.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hawkish comment - remark favoring aggression or firmness, especially in policy

Etymology

From 'dovish' (derived from 'dove', symbolizing peace) + 'comment'.
