Siouan language

1. NOUN

/ ˈsuː.ən ˈlæŋ.ɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈsuː.ən ˈlaŋ.ɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
a family of Native American languages originally spoken by the Sioux and related peoples of central North America
Native American language family

Examples

Linguists have documented several Siouan languages that were once spoken across the Great Plains.
The revival of Siouan languages is important to the cultural heritage of many Native American tribes.
Some scholars specialize in the grammar and history of Siouan languages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sioux - the people primarily associated with the Siouan language family

Etymology

From Sioux, the name of the primary people associated with the language family, plus the English suffix '-an'.
