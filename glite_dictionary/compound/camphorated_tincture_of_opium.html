camphorated tincture of opium

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæm.fəˌreɪ.tɪd ˈtɪŋk.tʃɚ əv ˈoʊ.pi.əm / (AmE), / ˈkæm.fər.eɪ.tɪd ˈtɪŋk.tʃə əv ˈəʊ.pi.əm / (BrE)
a medicinal preparation of opium and camphor, historically used to treat diarrhea and relieve pain
medicinal preparation of opium for diarrhea
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed camphorated tincture of opium to help control the patient's severe diarrhea.
Historically, camphorated tincture of opium was a common remedy for intestinal distress in both adults and children.
The pharmacy no longer stocks camphorated tincture of opium due to concerns about its opiate content.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paregoric - another name for camphorated tincture of opium

Etymology

From camphor (from Malay *kapur*) and tincture (from Latin *tinctura*), plus opium (from Latin *opium*).
