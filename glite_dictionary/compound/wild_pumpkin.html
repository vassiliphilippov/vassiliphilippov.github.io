wild pumpkin

1. NOUN

/ waɪld ˈpʌmpkɪn / (AmE), / waɪld ˈpʌmpkɪn / (BrE)
a perennial vine native to dry regions of the central and southwestern United States and Mexico, producing small, hard, green mottled, inedible fruit
a vine with small inedible fruit

Examples

The wild pumpkin thrives in arid landscapes, where its tough fruits go uneaten by animals.
Botanists studied the resilient wild pumpkin as a model for drought-tolerant plants.
Despite its name, the fruit of the wild pumpkin is not suitable for human consumption.
