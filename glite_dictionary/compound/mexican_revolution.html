Mexican Revolution

1. PROPN

/ ˌmɛksɪkən ˌrɛvəˈluːʃən / (AmE), / ˌmɛksɪkən ˌrɛvəˈluːʃən / (BrE)
the period of social and political upheaval in Mexico from 1910 to 1920, marked by agrarian reforms and major leadership by figures such as Pancho Villa and Emiliano Zapata
Mexican social-political revolution (1910–1920)

Examples

The Mexican Revolution dramatically changed the country's political and social landscape in the early twentieth century.
Pancho Villa and Emiliano Zapata were prominent leaders during the Mexican Revolution.
Many land reforms implemented after the Mexican Revolution shaped modern Mexican society.
