sweet vetch

1. NOUN

/ swit vɛtʃ / (AmE), / swiːt vɛtʃ / (BrE)
a perennial plant of the western United States, with pink to purple flowers and flat pods that separate into nearly round segments
a North American perennial plant with pink-purple flowers

Examples

Farmers sometimes plant sweet vetch to improve soil nitrogen content.
In the foothills, the sweet vetch displays clusters of purple blooms each spring.
Botanists collected samples of sweet vetch for study in their native habitat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vetch - any plant of the genus Vicia, commonly grown as forage
