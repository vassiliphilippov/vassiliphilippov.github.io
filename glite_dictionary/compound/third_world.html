Third World

1. NOUN

/ θɝd wɝld / (AmE), / θɜːd wɜːld / (BrE)
the group of underdeveloped and developing countries of Asia, Africa, and Latin America, especially as a political or economic bloc; sometimes considered outdated or pejorative.
developing nations of Asia, Africa, and Latin America

Examples

The report highlighted poverty and education challenges facing the Third World today.
During the Cold War, many nations in the Third World remained non-aligned with the major powers.
Aid organizations work to improve healthcare in Third World regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: developing world - countries with less developed industrial bases and lower Human Development Index
  • Synonym: Global South - a modern term for the regions of Latin America, Asia, Africa, and Oceania

Etymology

Coined in the mid-20th century by analogy to the 'third estate' in pre-revolutionary France, referring to countries not aligned with the Western or Eastern blocs during the Cold War.

2. NOUN

/ θɝd wɝld / (AmE), / θɜːd wɜːld / (BrE)
The group of countries with low levels of industrialization, limited economic development, and often significant poverty, especially in Africa, Asia, and Latin America; now sometimes considered offensive or dated, with 'developing countries' preferred.
developing countries with economic challenges

Examples

Many international organizations focus their aid efforts on the Third World.
He has spent years working on education projects in the Third World.
Concerns about clean water access are still major issues in the Third World.

Synset Examples

The documentary highlighted issues in the Third World.
He carried a Third World briefcase filled with supplies.
They traveled to the Third World for volunteer work.
Her Third World briefcase was packed for the charity trip.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: developing country - a country with low industrialization and low human development
  • Antonym: First World - highly developed, industrialized countries

Etymology

Coined during the Cold War (French 'tiers monde'), referring to countries not aligned with NATO (First World) or the Communist Bloc (Second World).
