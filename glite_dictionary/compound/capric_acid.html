capric acid

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæprɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / ˈkæprɪk ˈasɪd / (BrE)
a fatty acid found in animal fats and oils, with an unpleasant odor resembling that of goats
a fatty acid with a goat-like smell
medical

Examples

Goat milk contains a significant amount of capric acid, which contributes to its distinctive flavor.
Researchers studied the effects of capric acid on human metabolism.
The unpleasant smell from the animal oils was due to the presence of capric acid.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decanoic acid - another name for capric acid, a 10-carbon saturated fatty acid

Etymology

From Latin 'capra' (goat), due to its odor.
