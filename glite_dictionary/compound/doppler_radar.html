Doppler radar

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɑplɚ ˌreɪˌdɑr / (AmE), / ˈdɒplə ˌreɪˌdɑː / (BrE)
A radar system that measures the velocity of objects using the Doppler effect to detect changes in frequency.
radar measuring velocity using Doppler effect

Examples

The weather service used a Doppler radar to track the approaching storm.
Airports rely on Doppler radar systems to monitor wind patterns near runways.
Technicians checked the Doppler radar for signs of tornado development in the area.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: radar - a system for detecting objects using radio waves

Etymology

Named after Christian Doppler, Austrian physicist (1803–1853), whose work described the Doppler effect.
