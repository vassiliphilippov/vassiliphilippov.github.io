Angara River

1. PROPN

/ ænˈɡɑːrə ˈrɪvər / (AmE), / ænˈɡɑːrə ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
a river in southeastern Siberia, Russia, flowing northwest from Lake Baikal to join the Yenisei River
river in southeastern Siberia, Russia

Examples

The Angara River is the only river that flows out of Lake Baikal.
Hydroelectric plants along the Angara River provide power to surrounding regions.
During their expedition, they followed the course of the Angara River through Siberia.

Etymology

From Russian Ангара (Angará), the local name for the river.
