genus Asarum

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs əˈsɛə.rəm / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs əˈsɑː.rəm / (BrE)
A genus of herbaceous perennial plants, commonly known as wild ginger, native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.
genus of plants known as wild ginger

Examples

Botanists classify wild ginger under the genus Asarum for its unique rhizomes and heart-shaped leaves.
The genus Asarum includes several species that thrive in woodland gardens.
Some cultures use plants from the genus Asarum in traditional herbal remedies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wild ginger - common name for species in the genus Asarum

Etymology

From Latin 'Asarum,' the classical name for this group of plants; 'genus' is Latin for 'kind' or 'group.'
