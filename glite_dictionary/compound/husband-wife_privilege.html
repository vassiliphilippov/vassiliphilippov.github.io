husband-wife privilege

1. NOUN

/ ˈhʌzbənd ˈwaɪf ˈprɪvəlɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈhʌzbənd ˈwaɪf ˈprɪvəlɪdʒ / (BrE)
the legal right that prevents a spouse from being compelled to disclose confidential communications made between spouses during their marriage
legal right protecting spousal communications
legal

Examples

The court ruled that the husband-wife privilege prevented her from testifying about their private conversation.
Lawyers often rely on husband-wife privilege to protect sensitive marital communications in court.
Because of husband-wife privilege, confidential discussions between spouses are safeguarded from legal disclosure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spousal privilege - legal protection preventing compelled testimony between spouses
