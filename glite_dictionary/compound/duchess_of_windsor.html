Duchess of Windsor

1. PROPN

/ ˈdʌtʃ.əs əv ˈwɪn.zər / (AmE), / ˈdʌtʃ.ɪs əv ˈwɪn.zə / (BrE)
(1896–1986) The title held by Wallis Simpson, an American divorcee whose marriage to Edward VIII in 1937 prompted his abdication from the British throne.
title of Wallis Simpson, wife of Edward VIII

Examples

Wallis Simpson became the Duchess of Windsor after marrying Edward VIII.
The Duchess of Windsor was a controversial figure in British history.
Many books detail the love story between the Duke and Duchess of Windsor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Edward VIII - King of the United Kingdom who abdicated to marry Wallis Simpson
