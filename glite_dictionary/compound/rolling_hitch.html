rolling hitch

1. NOUN

/ ˈroʊlɪŋ hɪtʃ / (AmE), / ˈrəʊlɪŋ hɪtʃ / (BrE)
a knot used to fasten a rope to a spar, post, or to another rope so that it resists movement in one direction
type of fastening knot for ropes

Examples

To secure the line to the pole, she tied a rolling hitch.
Sailors often use a rolling hitch to prevent slipping along another rope.
Learning the rolling hitch is essential for anyone interested in sailing knots.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clove hitch - a type of knot for fastening rope to a post
  • Synonym: half hitch - a simple knot used as part of other hitches

Etymology

From 'rolling' (because it resists rolling or slipping) and 'hitch' (a type of knot).
