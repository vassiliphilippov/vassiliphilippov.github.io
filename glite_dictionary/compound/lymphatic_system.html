lymphatic system

1. NOUN

/ lɪmˈfætɪk ˈsɪstəm / (AmE), / lɪmˈfætɪk ˈsɪstəm / (BrE)
the network of vessels, tissues, and organs that helps protect the body against infection and maintains fluid balance by transporting lymph.
body's network for lymph circulation and immune defense
medical

Examples

The lymphatic system plays a crucial role in defending the body against infections.
Blockages in the lymphatic system can lead to swelling called lymphedema.
Doctors studied the patient's lymphatic system to track the spread of the disease.

Etymology

From 'lymphatic', relating to lymph ('lymph' from Latin 'lympha', water), plus 'system'.
