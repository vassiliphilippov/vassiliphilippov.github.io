skin tumor

1. NOUN

/ skɪn ˈtumɚ / (AmE), / skɪn ˈtjuːmə / (BrE)
A growth or neoplasm that originates in the skin, particularly the epidermis, which can be benign or malignant.
growth or neoplasm of the skin
medical

Examples

The doctor examined the skin tumor to determine if it was benign or malignant.
She had a small skin tumor removed from her shoulder last year.
Early detection of a skin tumor increases the chances of successful treatment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tumor - an abnormal mass of tissue
  • Degree Higher: carcinoma - a malignant tumor of epithelial origin
