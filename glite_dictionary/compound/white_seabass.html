white seabass

1. NOUN

/ waɪt ˈsiːˌbæs / (AmE), / waɪt ˈsiːˌbæs / (BrE)
a large species of fish (Atractoscion nobilis) found along the Pacific coast of North America, valued for both sport and eating
a large Pacific coastal fish, prized for food and sport

Examples

Anglers come from far away hoping to catch a white seabass along the California coast.
The chef prepared a delicious dish featuring grilled white seabass with lemon and herbs.
Due to overfishing, there are regulations on the minimum size of white seabass you can keep.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: croaker - a type of fish within the same family, known for making croaking sounds
