ethmoidal artery

1. NOUN

/ ɛθˈmɔɪdəl ˈɑrˌtɛri / (AmE), / ɛθˈmɔɪdəl ˈɑːtəri / (BrE)
an artery that branches from the ophthalmic artery and supplies blood to structures in and around the nasal cavity and ethmoid bone
artery supplying the nasal cavity and ethmoid region
medical

Examples

The ethmoidal artery is vulnerable during surgery involving the nasal region.
Inflammation in the sinuses may sometimes affect the ethmoidal artery and lead to bleeding.
Damage to the ethmoidal artery can result in severe nosebleeds during sinus operations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ophthalmic artery - main artery supplying the eye and its orbit

Etymology

From 'ethmoid', referring to the ethmoid bone, and 'artery'.
