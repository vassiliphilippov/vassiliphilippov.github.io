quite an

1. ADV

/ kwaɪt æn / (AmE), / kwaɪt ən / (BrE)
used to emphasize that something or someone is remarkable, impressive, or exceptional in some way
emphasizer for something remarkable or impressive

Examples

The hike turned out to be quite an adventure, far more challenging than we had expected.
That was quite an explanation—very thorough and insightful.
Today has been quite an unusual day at work; so much happened!

Synset Examples

her victory was quite something
she's quite a girl
quite a film
quite a walk
we've had quite an afternoon

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quite a - emphasizer for something remarkable or impressive
  • Synonym: rather - to a certain or significant extent; somewhat (used for emphasis)
  • Degree Lower: somewhat - to some degree (used as a comparative, less strong)
