mourning dove

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɔrnɪŋ ˌdoʊv / (AmE), / ˈmɔːnɪŋ ˌdʌv / (BrE)
A North American wild dove (Zenaida macroura) known for its soft, mournful cooing call.
a type of North American wild dove

Examples

A mourning dove perched on the fence, its soft call echoing through the morning air.
We spotted several mourning doves feeding quietly under the oak tree.
The distinctive coo of the mourning dove is a familiar sound in many American backyards.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dove - a bird in the pigeon family, typically with a soft cooing call

Etymology

Named for the bird's plaintive, mournful call; derives from 'mourning' (grieving) and 'dove'.
