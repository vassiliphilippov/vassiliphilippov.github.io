capillary vein

1. NOUN

/ kəˈpɪləri veɪn / (AmE), / kəˈpɪləri veɪn / (BrE)
a very small vein that is directly continuous with a capillary, involved in the transition of blood from capillaries to larger veins
minute vein continuous with a capillary
medical

Examples

Oxygen-depleted blood passes from the capillaries into the capillary vein before reaching larger veins.
A blockage in a capillary vein can disrupt blood flow at a very local level.
The researchers observed the dilation of each capillary vein under the microscope.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: capillary - a very fine blood vessel
  • Synonym: venule - a small vein that collects blood from capillaries

Etymology

From 'capillary', from Latin 'capillaris' (relating to hair), describing the hair-like thinness, and 'vein', from Latin 'vena'.
