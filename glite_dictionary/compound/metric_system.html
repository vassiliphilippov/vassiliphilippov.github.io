metric system

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛtrɪk ˌsɪstəm / (AmE), / ˈmɛtrɪk ˌsɪstəm / (BrE)
A decimal system of measurement based on the meter, kilogram, and second, used worldwide.
decimal system of measurement

Examples

Most countries have adopted the metric system for their official measurements.
The United States still relies largely on customary units rather than the metric system.
Learning the metric system made science class much easier.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: imperial system - traditional system of weights and measures, mainly used in the UK and US

Etymology

From French 'système métrique', introduced during the French Revolution in the late 18th century.
