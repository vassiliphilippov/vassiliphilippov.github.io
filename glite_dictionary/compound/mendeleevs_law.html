Mendeleev's law

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛn.dəˈleɪ.əvz lɔ / (AmE), / ˌmɛn.dɪˈleɪ.ɪvz lɔː / (BrE)
the principle that the chemical properties of elements are periodic functions of their atomic numbers
principle of periodicity in element properties
medical

Examples

Many introductory chemistry textbooks explain Mendeleev's law as the basis for the periodic table.
Scientists observed that Mendeleev's law helped predict undiscovered elements and their properties.
Because of Mendeleev's law, elements with similar properties appear at regular intervals on the periodic table.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: periodic law - the modern principle that element properties are periodic with atomic number

Etymology

Named after Russian chemist Dmitri Mendeleev, who first formulated the law in the 19th century.
