Manilla hemp

1. NOUN

/ məˈnɪlə hɛmp / (AmE), / məˈnɪlə hɛmp / (BrE)
A strong, durable fiber obtained from the abaca plant, native to the Philippines, used chiefly for making ropes and various products.
fiber from the abaca plant, used in rope-making

Examples

Ropes made of Manilla hemp are prized for their strength and resistance to saltwater.
The ship was equipped with sturdy cables woven from Manilla hemp.
Farmers in the Philippines cultivate abaca to produce Manilla hemp for export.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abaca - a species of banana native to the Philippines whose stalks yield strong fibers
  • Degree Lower: hemp - plant used to produce fiber for rope and fabric

Etymology

Named after Manila, the capital of the Philippines, where it was first exported; 'hemp' is used for its fibrous quality.
