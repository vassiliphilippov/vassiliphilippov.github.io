caramelized sugar

1. NOUN

/ ˈkærəˌmɛlaɪzd ˈʃuɡər / (AmE), / ˈkarəmɛlaɪzd ˈʃuːɡə / (BrE)
Sugar that has been heated until it melts and turns brown, used to color and flavor food.
sugar heated until brown, for flavoring

Examples

To make flan, you start by pouring caramelized sugar into the bottom of the mold.
The chef drizzled caramelized sugar over the dessert for a rich, sweet topping.
Some sauces gain their distinctive color from the addition of caramelized sugar.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caramel - sugar or syrup heated until it turns brown, used for flavoring
