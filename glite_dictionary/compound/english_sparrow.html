English sparrow

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ ˈspæroʊ / (AmE), / ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ ˈspærəʊ / (BrE)
a small, hardy brown-and-grey bird native to Europe, commonly found in urban areas worldwide; also known as the house sparrow (Passer domesticus)
a small European bird; house sparrow

Examples

An English sparrow perched on the windowsill, chirping loudly every morning.
The park was full of English sparrows searching for crumbs left by visitors.
Despite its name, the English sparrow can be found in cities all over the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: house sparrow - common small brown bird found worldwide, especially in cities
