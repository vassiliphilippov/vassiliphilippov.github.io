court of assize and nisi prius

1. NOUN

/ kɔrt əv əˈsaɪz ənd ˌnaɪsɪ ˈpraɪəs / (AmE), / kɔːt əv əˈsaɪz ənd ˌnaɪsɪ ˈpraɪəs / (BrE)
a historical county court in England responsible for hearing serious criminal and civil cases, replaced in 1971 by Crown Courts.
historic English county court (pre-1971)
legal british archaic

Examples

Before 1971, the court of assize and nisi prius handled the most serious criminal trials outside London.
Legal historians often reference the court of assize and nisi prius when studying the evolution of English law.
The court of assize and nisi prius was abolished with the introduction of the Crown Court system.

Etymology

From Old French 'assise' (session, legal sitting) and Latin 'nisi prius' (unless before), reflecting Anglo-Norman legal tradition.
