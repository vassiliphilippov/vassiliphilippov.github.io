order Crocodilia

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdər ˌkrɑkəˈdɪliə / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə ˌkrɒkəˈdɪlɪə / (BrE)
a taxonomic group of large, predatory, semi-aquatic reptiles including crocodiles, alligators, caimans, and gharials
taxonomic order of crocodilians

Examples

Members of the order Crocodilia are found in tropical regions around the world.
Scientists study the evolution of the order Crocodilia to understand ancient reptiles.
The order Crocodilia includes both alligators and gharials as well as crocodiles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crocodile - large aquatic reptile of the Crocodylidae family
  • Synonym: alligator - large aquatic reptile distinct from the true crocodile

Etymology

From Latin 'crocodilus' (crocodile), with standard zoological order prefix 'order'.
