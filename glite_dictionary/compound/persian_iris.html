Persian iris

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɜːrʒən ˈaɪrɪs / (AmE), / ˈpɜːʃən ˈaɪrɪs / (BrE)
a bulbous iris native to Asia Minor, cultivated for its pale lilac-colored flowers
bulbous iris plant with pale lilac flowers

Examples

The Persian iris is prized among gardeners for its delicate lilac blooms in the spring.
He planted several Persian irises along the border of the walkway to add color to the garden.
In its native range, the Persian iris grows well in rocky, well-drained soils.
