wicket gate

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪkɪt ˌɡeɪt / (AmE), / ˈwɪkɪt ˌɡeɪt / (BrE)
A small gate or door, especially one built into or next to a larger gate or door, allowing passage without opening the main entrance.
small gate within a larger door

Examples

The old castle had a wicket gate that allowed guards to enter unnoticed at night.
You can use the wicket gate to access the garden without opening the main gate.
The servant slipped quietly through the wicket gate, careful not to attract attention.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: postern - a small secondary gate or door, especially at the rear of a fort or castle

Etymology

From Middle English 'wiket' (small door or gate), possibly from Old Northern French 'wiket'.
