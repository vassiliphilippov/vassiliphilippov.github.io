dominical calendar

1. NOUN

/ dəˈmɪnɪkəl ˈkælənˌdər / (AmE), / dəˈmɪnɪkəl ˈkælɪndə / (BrE)
a calendar that specifically indicates or marks Sundays
calendar marking Sundays

Examples

The dominical calendar helps churches plan their liturgical schedules.
Historians used a dominical calendar to track religious observances in old manuscripts.
He referenced the dominical calendar to determine the dates for each Sabbath.

Synset Examples

The dominical calendar marks the day of rest.

Etymology

From Latin 'dominicus' (of the Lord), referring to Sunday as the Lord's Day.
