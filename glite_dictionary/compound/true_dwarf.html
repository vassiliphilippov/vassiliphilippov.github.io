true dwarf

1. NOUN

/ tru dɔrf / (AmE), / truː dwɔːf / (BrE)
A person with achondroplasia whose small size is caused by a genetic defect, with normal body proportions and mental and sexual development.
person with achondroplasia and normal development
medical

Examples

The physician explained that the child was a true dwarf with normal mental development.
Unlike other forms of dwarfism, a true dwarf has proportional body parts.
Medical researchers are studying the genetic causes of true dwarf conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: achondroplasia - a genetic disorder resulting in dwarfism
