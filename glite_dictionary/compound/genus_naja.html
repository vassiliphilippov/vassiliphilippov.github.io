genus Naja

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈnɑː.dʒə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈnɑː.dʒə / (BrE)
A genus of venomous snakes in the family Elapidae, commonly known as cobras, native to Africa and Asia.
genus of cobras (snakes)

Examples

The genus Naja includes some of the world's most dangerous snakes.
Researchers discovered a new species within the genus Naja in Southeast Asia.
Unlike other elapids, cobras in the genus Naja are known for their ability to flare their neck ribs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cobra - a large, poisonous snake of the genus Naja

Etymology

From Latin 'Naja', derived from Sanskrit 'nāga' meaning 'snake' or 'serpent'.
