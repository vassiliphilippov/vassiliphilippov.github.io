New World

1. NOUN

/ ˈnu wɝld / (AmE), / ˈnjuː wɜːld / (BrE)
the continents of North and South America, especially regarded as lands discovered and colonized by Europeans from the late 15th century onwards
the Americas as discovered by Europeans

Examples

Many species of plants and animals were introduced from the New World to Europe.
The explorers set sail in hopes of finding riches in the New World.
European colonization of the New World began in the late 15th century.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Old World - Europe, Asia, and Africa, especially before the discovery of the Americas
