theatre curtain

1. NOUN

/ ˈθiət̬ər ˈkɜrtən / (AmE), / ˈθɪətə ˈkɜːtn̩ / (BrE)
a large hanging cloth in a theatre that separates the stage from the audience, rising or parting at the start and lowering or closing at the end or during pauses in performances
curtain separating stage and audience in a theatre

Examples

The theatre curtain slowly rose to reveal the first scene of the play.
As the theatre curtain closed, the audience applauded enthusiastically.
She waited nervously behind the theatre curtain before making her entrance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curtain - a piece of cloth that hangs to cover a window, stage, or doorway
