to shed blood

1. VERB

/ ʃɛd blʌd / (AmE), / ʃɛd blʌd / (BrE)
to lose blood from the body, especially as a result of injury or violence
to lose blood from the body

Examples

Many soldiers shed blood during the long and brutal conflict.
He was willing to shed blood for his beliefs if necessary.
No one should have to shed blood over a simple disagreement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to bleed - to lose blood from the body, typically through a wound

2. VERB

/ ʃɛd blʌd / (AmE), / ʃɛd blʌd / (BrE)
to engage in violence, especially by causing injury or death; to sacrifice one's life in conflict or battle
to engage in violence or sacrifice life

Examples

The soldiers were ready to shed blood to protect their homeland.
Many revolutions have shed blood in the name of freedom.
He would never shed blood unnecessarily, valuing peace over conflict.
