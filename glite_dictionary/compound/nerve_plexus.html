nerve plexus

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɝːv ˈplɛksəs / (AmE), / ˈnɜːv ˈplɛksəs / (BrE)
a network of intersecting nerves forming a complex structure in the body, especially in the peripheral nervous system
network of intersecting nerves
medical

Examples

The brachial nerve plexus controls movement and sensation in the arm.
A damaged nerve plexus can result in weakness or loss of function in the affected area.
Doctors examined the lumbar nerve plexus to determine the source of the patient's pain.

Etymology

From Latin 'plexus' (braid, network), used in anatomical terminology.
