bar sinister

1. NOUN

/ bɑːr ˈsɪnɪstər / (AmE), / bɑː ˈsɪnɪstə / (BrE)
(in heraldry) a mark consisting of a band running from the top right to the bottom left of a shield, traditionally used to indicate illegitimacy.
heraldic mark denoting illegitimacy

Examples

The knight’s shield was defaced with a bar sinister to indicate his illegitimate birth.
In heraldry, a bar sinister runs from the upper right to the lower left of the shield.
The presence of the bar sinister fueled rumors about the nobleman’s lineage.

Etymology

From French 'barre' (bar) and Latin 'sinister' (left).

2. NOUN

/ bɑɹ ˈsɪnɪstɚ / (AmE), / bɑː ˈsɪnɪstə / (BrE)
the status of being born to parents who were not married; illegitimacy by birth
illegitimacy of birth
archaic

Examples

For generations, the family was unfairly stigmatized because of the bar sinister.
He bore the bar sinister bravely, despite the whispers from those around him.
Many old novels refer to the bar sinister as a mark of shame.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: illegitimacy - being born to unmarried parents

Etymology

From heraldic terminology: 'bar' (a horizontal stripe on a shield) and 'sinister' (Latin for 'left'), traditionally interpreted (incorrectly) as a sign of illegitimate descent.

3. NOUN

/ bɑɹ ˈsɪnəstɚ / (AmE), / bɑː ˈsɪnɪstə / (BrE)
a heraldic symbol, typically a diagonal band on a coat of arms, historically believed to indicate illegitimacy of birth
heraldic symbol denoting illegitimacy

Examples

The old family crest bore a bar sinister across its center.
Rumors spread quickly when the public saw the bar sinister on the new lord's shield.
Some believe the bar sinister marks a shameful family secret in the past.

Synset Examples

The noble family displayed a bar sinister on their shield.
She learned that the bar sinister represented an illegitimate birth.

Etymology

From bar (heraldic term for a horizontal band) and sinister (Latin for 'left'), referring to the left side of a shield (as viewed by the bearer).
