breach of the covenant of warranty

1. NOUN

/ briːtʃ əv ðə ˈkʌvənənt əv ˈwɔːrənti / (AmE), / briːtʃ əv ðə ˈkʌvənənt ɒv ˈwɒrənti / (BrE)
The failure by a seller to uphold the legal guarantee that the title to property is valid and unencumbered.
failure to stand by title guarantee (legal)
legal

Examples

The buyer sued for damages after a breach of the covenant of warranty was discovered.
If there is a breach of the covenant of warranty, the seller may be liable to compensate the purchaser.
A breach of the covenant of warranty can arise if the property is found to have existing liens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: covenant of warranty - a promise in a deed that the title is good and unencumbered
  • Synonym: breach of contract - violation of a legal agreement
