verse form

1. NOUN

/ vɜrs fɔrm / (AmE), / vɜːs fɔːm / (BrE)
A particular structure or type of composition in poetry, characterized by a specific metrical arrangement and pattern of rhythmical lines.
a structure or type of poetic composition

Examples

The sonnet is a classic verse form that has been used for centuries in English poetry.
He experimented with different verse forms to express his ideas more vividly.
Haiku is a Japanese verse form known for its brevity and strict syllable count.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poetic form - a structure or pattern of poetry
