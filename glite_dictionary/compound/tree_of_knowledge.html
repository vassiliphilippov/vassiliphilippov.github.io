tree of knowledge

1. NOUN

/ triː əv ˈnɑlɪdʒ / (AmE), / triː əv ˈnɒlɪdʒ / (BrE)
the biblical tree in the Garden of Eden whose fruit, when eaten by Adam and Eve, brought knowledge of good and evil
biblical tree whose fruit gave knowledge of good and evil

Examples

According to Genesis, Adam and Eve ate fruit from the tree of knowledge and were cast out of Eden.
Many scholars interpret the tree of knowledge as a symbol of human curiosity and free will.
The story of the tree of knowledge has influenced theology and philosophy for centuries.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: tree of life - the other major biblical tree in the Garden of Eden, associated with immortality
