Ottawa River

1. PROPN

/ ˈɑtəwə ˈrɪvər / (AmE), / ˈɒtəwə ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
a major river in southeastern Canada forming part of the boundary between Quebec and Ontario and flowing into the Saint Lawrence River near Montreal
major river in southeastern Canada

Examples

The Ottawa River is known for its picturesque scenery and recreational activities.
During the spring melt, the Ottawa River often experiences high water levels.
Early explorers used the Ottawa River as a key transportation route.

Etymology

Named after the Ottawa people, whose traditional territory is in the area.
