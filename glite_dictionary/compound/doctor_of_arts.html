Doctor of Arts

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɑktɚ əv ɑrts / (AmE), / ˈdɒktə əv ɑːts / (BrE)
a doctoral degree focusing on a specific field within the arts or humanities, typically emphasizing creative or scholarly practice
advanced academic degree in the arts

Examples

After years of study, she earned her Doctor of Arts in literature.
The university's Doctor of Arts program emphasizes both research and creative practice.
He was the first in his department to hold a Doctor of Arts degree.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Doctor of Philosophy - a research-based doctoral degree (PhD)
  • Degree Lower: Master of Arts - a postgraduate degree in the arts (MA)

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɑktər əv ɑrts / (AmE), / ˈdɒktə əv ɑːts / (BrE)
An advanced academic or honorary degree in the arts, typically awarded for distinguished achievements in artistic or scholarly work.
advanced or honorary arts degree

Examples

He was awarded a Doctor of Arts in recognition of his contributions to literature.
After decades as a celebrated painter, she received an honorary Doctor of Arts from the university.
The ceremony included several prominent figures who were granted a Doctor of Arts degree.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Doctor of Philosophy - advanced academic degree usually known as PhD
