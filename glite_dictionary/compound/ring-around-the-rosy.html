ring-around-the-rosy

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɪŋ əˈraʊnd ðə ˈroʊzi / (AmE), / ˌrɪŋ əˈraʊnd ðə ˈrəʊzi / (BrE)
A children's game in which players hold hands and dance in a circle, squatting or falling down at a specific cue, often while singing a rhyme.
children's circle game with song and actions

Examples

The children played ring-around-the-rosy in the backyard until sunset.
She remembers singing ring-around-the-rosy with her friends at school.
During recess, a group of kids started ring-around-the-rosy, laughing as they all fell down.
