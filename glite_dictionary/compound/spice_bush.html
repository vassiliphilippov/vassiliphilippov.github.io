spice bush

1. NOUN

/ spaɪs bʊʃ / (AmE), / spaɪs bʊʃ / (BrE)
A deciduous shrub native to eastern North America, with aromatic leaves and bark, yellow flowers, and scarlet or yellow berries.
aromatic North American shrub

Examples

The spice bush releases a pleasant fragrance when its leaves are crushed.
Hikers in the Appalachian Mountains often spot spice bush growing along forest trails.
In the fall, the spice bush is covered with clusters of bright red berries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Lindera benzoin - the botanical name for spice bush
