labial pipe

1. NOUN

/ ˈleɪ.bi.əl paɪp / (AmE), / ˈleɪ.bɪ.əl paɪp / (BrE)
an organ pipe that produces sound by air passing across a sharp edge or lip, rather than by vibrating a reed
organ pipe using a lip to create sound

Examples

The cathedral's organ features a beautifully restored labial pipe section.
Labial pipes produce a mellower sound compared to reed pipes in an organ.
He specialized in tuning the labial pipes to achieve the perfect tone.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: reed pipe - organ pipe that produces sound by means of a vibrating reed

Etymology

From Latin 'labium' (lip), referring to the pipe's lip edge.
