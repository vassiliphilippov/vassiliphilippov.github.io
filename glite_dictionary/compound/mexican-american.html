Mexican-American

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛksɪkən əˈmɛrɪkən / (AmE), / ˌmɛksɪkən əˈmɛrɪkən / (BrE)
a person living in the United States who is of Mexican descent or was born in Mexico
US resident of Mexican descent

Examples

Many Mexican-American families celebrate both Mexican and American traditions throughout the year.
As a proud Mexican-American, she speaks both Spanish and English at home.
The Mexican-American community has made significant contributions to the culture and economy of California.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Chicano - a US citizen of Mexican descent, especially one who emphasizes political and cultural identity

Etymology

Formed from 'Mexican' + 'American', denoting people of Mexican heritage in the US.
