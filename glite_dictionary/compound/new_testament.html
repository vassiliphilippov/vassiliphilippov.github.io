New Testament

1. PROPN

/ ˌnuː ˈtɛstəˌmənt / (AmE), / ˌnjuː ˈtɛstəmənt / (BrE)
the collection of books forming the second part of the Christian Bible, including the Gospels, Acts, epistles, and Revelation, composed after the life of Jesus Christ
second part of the Christian Bible

Examples

Many Christians read passages from the New Testament during their Sunday worship services.
Scholars have debated the historical context of the New Testament books for centuries.
Her favorite verse is found in the New Testament, in the Gospel of John.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Old Testament - the first part of the Christian Bible, comprising texts originating before Jesus Christ

Etymology

From Latin 'Novum Testamentum', translating Greek 'hē kainē diathēkē' (the new covenant).
