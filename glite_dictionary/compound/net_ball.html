net ball

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɛt bɔl / (AmE), / ˈnɛt bɔːl / (BrE)
(in tennis) a served ball that touches the net but still lands in the service box, requiring the serve to be taken again.
serve touching the net in tennis

Examples

The umpire called a net ball, so Emily had to serve again.
Whenever a net ball occurs, the point is replayed from the start of the serve.
He was frustrated because he hit a net ball on his first serve.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: let - a serve in tennis that hits the net but lands in the correct area, requiring a replay
