Chilean rimu

1. NOUN

/ tʃɪˈliən ˈraɪmu / (AmE), / tʃɪˈliːən ˈriːmuː / (BrE)
a hardy, prostrate, spreading shrub of the Podocarpaceae family, resembling the mountain rimu and native to the mountains of southern Chile
a hardy shrub from southern Chile

Examples

The Chilean rimu is known for thriving in the harsh, cold climates of the southern Andes.
Botanists have studied the Chilean rimu for insights into plant adaptation to extreme environments.
Unlike other members of its family, the Chilean rimu spreads prostrate along the rocky ground.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mountain rimu - a related shrub species in the Podocarpaceae family
