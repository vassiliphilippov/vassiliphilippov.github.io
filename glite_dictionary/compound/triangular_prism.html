triangular prism

1. NOUN

/ traɪˈæŋɡjələr ˈprɪzəm / (AmE), / traɪˈaŋɡjʊlə ˈprɪzəm / (BrE)
a solid geometric figure with two parallel, congruent triangular bases and three rectangular faces connecting them
solid with triangular bases

Examples

The mathematics teacher used a triangular prism to illustrate how to calculate surface area and volume.
Light passing through a triangular prism can form a spectrum of colors.
The artist built a sculpture based on the shape of a triangular prism.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: prism - a solid with two parallel faces that are congruent polygons and other faces that are parallelograms

Etymology

From Latin *trigonus* (triangular) and Greek *prisma* (something sawed or shaped).
