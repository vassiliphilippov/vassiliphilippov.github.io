child neglect

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃaɪld nəˌɡlɛkt / (AmE), / ˈtʃaɪld nɪˌɡlɛkt / (BrE)
failure of a parent or caregiver to provide adequate emotional, physical, or supervisory care for a child, resulting in harm or risk of harm
failure to care for a child properly

Examples

The authorities intervened after reports of child neglect surfaced in the community.
Child neglect can have long-lasting effects on mental and physical health.
Schools are often required to report any suspected cases of child neglect to social services.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neglect - failure to give proper care or attention
  • Synonym: child abuse - physical, sexual, or emotional mistreatment of a child
