Sino-Tibetan language

1. NOUN

/ ˌsaɪnoʊ tɪˈbeɪtən ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌsaɪnəʊ tɪˈbiːtən ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
a member of the family of tonal languages spoken in eastern Asia, including Chinese, Tibetan, and Burmese
language family of East Asia (Chinese, Tibetan, etc.)

Examples

Mandarin is the most widely spoken Sino-Tibetan language in the world.
Many ethnic groups in Southeast Asia speak a Sino-Tibetan language as their native tongue.
The Sino-Tibetan language family includes both Chinese and Tibetan dialects.

Etymology

From 'Sino-', referring to China, and 'Tibetan', relating to Tibet; the term denotes languages associated with these regions.
