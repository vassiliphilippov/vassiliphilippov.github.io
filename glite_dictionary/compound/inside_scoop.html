inside scoop

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪn.saɪd ˌskuːp / (AmE), / ˈɪn.saɪd ˌskuːp / (BrE)
privileged or confidential information that is not known to the general public
privileged/confidential information

Examples

The journalist claimed to have the inside scoop on the scandal rocking the government.
If you want the inside scoop on the latest technology trends, talk to Jenna.
Rumors spread quickly, but only a few know the real inside scoop about the company's future.

Synset Examples

She got the inside scoop on the upcoming event.
I heard she has the inside scoop about the merger.
He always has the inside scoop on celebrity news.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exclusive - information given only to a particular person or group
  • Synonym: tip-off - a secret warning or piece of information
