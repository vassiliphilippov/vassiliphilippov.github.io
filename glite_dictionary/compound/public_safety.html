public safety

1. NOUN

/ ˈpʌblɪk ˈseɪfti / (AmE), / ˈpʌblɪk ˈseɪfti / (BrE)
the condition of being protected from crime, danger, and threats to the well-being of the general public
protection of people from danger or crime

Examples

The mayor outlined a series of reforms aimed at improving public safety in the city.
After the earthquake, officials emphasized the importance of public safety measures.
The committee is responsible for evaluating new technologies that could benefit public safety.

Synset Examples

The Pedocrat proposed new legislation to improve public safety.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: security - the state of being free from danger or threat
  • Antonym: danger - the possibility of suffering harm or injury
