National Association of Securities Dealers Automated Quotations

1. NOUN

/ ˌnæʃəˈnæl əˌsoʊsiˌeɪʃən əv sɪˈkjʊrətiz dɪˈlɚz ˌɔːtəˈmeɪtəd ˈkwoʊteɪʃənz / (AmE), / ˌnaʃəˈnal əˌsəʊsiˈeɪʃən ɒv sɪˈkjʊərɪtiz ˈdiːləz ˌɔːtəˈmeɪtɪd ˈkwəʊteɪʃənz / (BrE)
a computerized system that provides brokers with price quotations for securities traded over the counter, known as NASDAQ
computerized over-the-counter securities quotation system (NASDAQ)

Examples

The National Association of Securities Dealers Automated Quotations system revolutionized the way stocks were traded over the counter.
Many technology companies are listed on the National Association of Securities Dealers Automated Quotations exchange.
Brokers rely on the National Association of Securities Dealers Automated Quotations platform for real-time price information.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: NASDAQ - abbreviation for the same computerized securities quotation system

Etymology

Acronym from full name ‘National Association of Securities Dealers Automated Quotations’; launched in 1971.
