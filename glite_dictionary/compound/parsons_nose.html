parson's nose

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑrsənz ˌnoʊz / (AmE), / ˈpɑːsənz nəʊz / (BrE)
the fatty tail of a cooked or prepared bird, especially a chicken or turkey, considered edible
the tail end of a cooked bird

Examples

My grandfather always claimed the parson's nose was the tastiest part of the roast chicken.
She left the parson's nose for her brother, knowing it's his favorite bit.
In some families, eating the parson's nose is considered a treat reserved for the head of the table.

Etymology

Said to be a humorous reference to the prominent rear of Anglican clergymen (parsons), dating from the 18th century.
