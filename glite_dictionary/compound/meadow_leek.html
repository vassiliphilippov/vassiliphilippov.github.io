meadow leek

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛdoʊ lik / (AmE), / ˈmɛdəʊ liːk / (BrE)
A North American plant of the lily family, with an edible bulb and slender leaves, also known as wild leek or ramps.
North American wild onion plant

Examples

The chef added chopped meadow leek to the omelette for extra flavor.
In spring, meadow leeks are often foraged from forest floors in the eastern United States.
Native Americans traditionally used meadow leek both as food and for medicinal purposes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ramp - A North American wild onion (Allium tricoccum), also called wild leek
  • Degree Lower: leek - A cultivated plant (Allium ampeloprasum) with a mild onion-like taste
