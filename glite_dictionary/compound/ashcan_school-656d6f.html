Ashcan school

1. NOUN

/ ˈæʃˌkæn skuːl / (AmE), / ˈæʃˌkan skuːl / (BrE)
a group of early 20th-century American artists whose work depicted realistic and often gritty scenes of urban life
early 20th-century American art movement

Examples

The Ashcan school artists were known for their depictions of everyday urban scenes.
Many critics praised the Ashcan school for its commitment to realism over romanticism.
Paintings from the Ashcan school often portray the struggles of lower-class city dwellers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Social realism - an artistic movement depicting social and political realities, often focusing on working-class life

Etymology

Named after trash cans (‘ashcans’) to reflect the artists' focus on the harsh realities of urban life; originated in early 20th-century New York.
