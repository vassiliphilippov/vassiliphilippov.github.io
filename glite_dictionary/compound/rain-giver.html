Rain-giver

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪnˌɡɪvər / (AmE), / ˈreɪnˌɡɪvə / (BrE)
An epithet for Jupiter, referring to his power or role in bringing rain; used especially in classical mythology or literature.
epithet for Jupiter as bringer of rain

Examples

The ancient Romans often prayed to the Rain-giver during times of drought.
In poetry, Jupiter is sometimes invoked as the mighty Rain-giver.
The temple was dedicated to the Rain-giver, seeking his favor for fertile fields.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Jupiter - Roman chief god, associated with the sky and weather
