little owl

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪt̬l aʊl / (AmE), / ˈlɪtl aʊl / (BrE)
a small species of owl (Athene noctua) native to Europe and parts of Asia and North Africa, known for its rounded head and yellow eyes
small European owl species

Examples

The little owl is often seen perched on fence posts at dusk.
Farmers welcome the little owl because it helps control rodent populations.
We spotted a little owl nesting in the hollow of an old tree.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: owl - a nocturnal bird of prey with large eyes and a flat face
