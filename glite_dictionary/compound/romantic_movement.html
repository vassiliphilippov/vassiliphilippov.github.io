Romantic Movement

1. NOUN

/ roʊˈmæntɪk ˈmuːvmənt / (AmE), / rəʊˈmæntɪk ˈmuːvmənt / (BrE)
A literary and artistic movement of the late 18th and early 19th centuries that emphasized emotion, imagination, and the beauty of nature over the order and rationality of civilization.
art and literature movement celebrating nature and emotion

Examples

Many poets of the Romantic Movement drew inspiration from the beauty and power of the natural world.
The Romantic Movement marked a shift away from strict rationalism toward expressing deep emotion in art and literature.
William Wordsworth is considered a central figure in the Romantic Movement of English poetry.

Synset Examples

Romanticism valued imagination and emotion over rationality

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Romanticism - an artistic and intellectual movement emphasizing inspiration and emotion
