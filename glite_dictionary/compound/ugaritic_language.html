Ugaritic language

1. NOUN

/ juːɡəˈrɪtɪk ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / juːɡəˈrɪtɪk ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
an extinct Semitic language that was spoken in northern Syria in ancient times
extinct Semitic language of northern Syria

Examples

Many ancient texts written in Ugaritic language have expanded our understanding of Semitic cultures.
Scholars study the Ugaritic language to compare its vocabulary with Hebrew and Phoenician.
The Ugaritic language is primarily known from clay tablets found in the city of Ugarit.

Etymology

From Ugarit, an ancient city in northern Syria where the language was discovered.
