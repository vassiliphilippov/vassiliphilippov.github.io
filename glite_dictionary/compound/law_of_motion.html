law of motion

1. NOUN

/ lɔ əv ˈmoʊʃən / (AmE), / lɔː əv ˈməʊʃən / (BrE)
any of the three fundamental laws of classical mechanics formulated by Isaac Newton, describing the relationship between a body and the forces acting upon it, and the motion in response to those forces.
fundamental law in classical mechanics

Examples

Isaac Newton's laws of motion revolutionized our understanding of physics.
The first law of motion states that an object at rest stays at rest unless acted on by a force.
Students learned about each law of motion in their introductory science class.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Newton's laws of motion - the three specific laws of classical mechanics formulated by Isaac Newton
  • Degree Lower: law of inertia - Newton's first law of motion
  • Degree Higher: law of acceleration - Newton's second law of motion
