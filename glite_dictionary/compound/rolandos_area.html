Rolando's area

1. NOUN

/ roʊˈlændoʊz ˈɛəriə / (AmE), / rəʊˈlandəʊz ˈeəriə / (BrE)
the region of the cerebral cortex responsible for controlling voluntary motor movements
region of cortex for motor control
medical

Examples

Damage to the Rolando's area can result in weakness or paralysis of certain muscles.
Modern imaging techniques have helped scientists to better understand the function of the Rolando's area.
Lesions found in the Rolando's area often lead to impaired voluntary movement.

Etymology

Named after Luigi Rolando (1773–1831), Italian anatomist, who studied the brain's structure.
