quinnat salmon

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɪnæt ˈsæmən / (AmE), / ˈkwɪnæt ˈsæmən / (BrE)
a large species of Pacific salmon prized as food, with adults dying after spawning
a large Pacific salmon species

Examples

The quinnat salmon migrates upstream to spawn before dying in the river.
Fishermen prize the quinnat salmon for its rich flavor and large size.
This restaurant features wild-caught quinnat salmon on its seasonal menu.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Chinook salmon - another common name for quinnat salmon
  • Degree Lower: sockeye salmon - another species of Pacific salmon

Etymology

From Maori 'kēnate', an indigenous New Zealand name for this salmon species.
