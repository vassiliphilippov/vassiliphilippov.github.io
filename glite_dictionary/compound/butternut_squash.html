butternut squash

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌt̬ɚˌnʌt skwɑʃ / (AmE), / ˈbʌtənʌt skwɒʃ / (BrE)
A buff-colored winter squash with a long, usually straight neck and sweet, orange flesh, commonly used in cooking.
buff-colored winter squash; sweet orange flesh

Examples

She roasted the butternut squash and served it with a drizzle of olive oil for dinner.
A bowl of butternut squash soup is perfect on a chilly autumn day.
He learned how to peel and dice butternut squash for his favorite casserole recipe.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: squash - a family of vegetables with a hard or soft rind

2. NOUN

/ ˈbʌt̬ɚˌnʌt skwɑʃ / (AmE), / ˈbʌtənʌt ˌskwɒʃ / (BrE)
A variety of winter squash with a smooth, buff-colored skin, orange flesh, and a slightly sweet flavor, shaped somewhat like a bottle.
a type of winter squash with buff skin

Examples

She roasted a butternut squash with rosemary for dinner.
The soup was made from pureed butternut squash and carrots.
I bought two butternut squash at the farmers' market yesterday.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: squash - a vegetable or its plant of the gourd family, cultivated for its edible fruit
  • Synonym: pumpkin - a large, round, orange fruit related to squashes and gourds
