bell metal

1. NOUN

/ bɛl ˈmɛt̬l̩ / (AmE), / bɛl ˈmɛtl̩ / (BrE)
A hard alloy of bronze consisting of about 3 or 4 parts copper to 1 part tin, used chiefly for making bells.
hard bronze alloy for bells

Examples

The church's bell metal bells rang out across the valley every morning.
Traditional craftsmen still use bell metal to cast temple bells in many parts of Asia.
Because of its durability and resonance, bell metal is prized in the making of musical instruments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bronze - an alloy of copper and tin, commonly used for statues and medals
