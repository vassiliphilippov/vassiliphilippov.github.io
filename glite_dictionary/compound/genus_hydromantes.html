genus Hydromantes

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌhaɪ.drəˈmæntiːz / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌhɪ.drəˈmæntiːz / (BrE)
A genus of web-toed salamanders found in North America and Europe, known for their climbing abilities and projectile tongues.
genus of web-toed salamanders

Examples

The genus Hydromantes is unique among salamanders for its ability to launch its tongue to catch prey.
Researchers studied several species within the genus Hydromantes in California's mountain caves.
Members of the genus Hydromantes are known for their remarkable climbing skills.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'hydro-' (water) and 'mantis' (prophet, perhaps for its climbing stance).
