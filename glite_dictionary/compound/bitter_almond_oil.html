bitter almond oil

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɪtər ˈɑːmənd ɔɪl / (AmE), / ˈbɪtə ˈɑːmənd ɔɪl / (BrE)
a pale yellow essential oil obtained by distilling bitter almonds, commonly used in flavorings and perfumery
essential oil from bitter almonds

Examples

Bitter almond oil is widely used to add a distinctive aroma to certain liqueurs.
Because it naturally contains benzaldehyde, bitter almond oil must be carefully refined for food use.
Some traditional marzipan recipes call for a few drops of bitter almond oil to achieve authentic flavor.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: almond oil - oil extracted from almonds, especially sweet varieties
