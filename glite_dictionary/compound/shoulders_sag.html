to shoulders sag

1. VERB

/ ˈʃoʊldərz sæɡ / (AmE), / ˈʃəʊldəz sæɡ / (BrE)
(of a person's shoulders) to droop or slump as a sign of disappointment, fatigue, or dejection.
shoulders droop with disappointment or fatigue

Examples

At the end of the long day, her shoulders sagged with exhaustion.
His shoulders sag every time he hears disappointing news.
You could see the relief on his face as his shoulders sagged and he took a deep breath.

Synset Examples

His shoulders sagged after hearing the bad news.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slump - to sit or fall heavily and limply
  • Synonym: stoop - to bend one's head or body forward or downward
