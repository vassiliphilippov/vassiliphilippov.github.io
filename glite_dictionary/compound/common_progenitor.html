common progenitor

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmən pɹoʊˈdʒɛnətɚ / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən pɹəˈdʒɛnɪtə / (BrE)
an individual or organism from which two or more groups or species are descended; a shared ancestor
shared ancestor; origin of two or more lines

Examples

Scientists believe that humans and chimpanzees share a common progenitor from millions of years ago.
All blood cells are derived from a common progenitor in the bone marrow.
Researchers traced the origins of the language families to a common progenitor.

Synset Examples

The scientist traced the lineage back to a common progenitor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ancestor - a person, typically one more remote than a grandparent, from whom one is descended

Etymology

Progenitor from Latin progenitor (ancestor, forebear), from progignere (to beget).
