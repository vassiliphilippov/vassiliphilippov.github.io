Frankenstein's monster

1. NOUN

/ ˌfræŋkənˌstaɪnz ˈmɑnstɚ / (AmE), / ˌfræŋkənˌstaɪnz ˈmɒnstə / (BrE)
the creature created by Victor Frankenstein in Mary Shelley's 1818 novel, commonly referred to as 'Frankenstein' though this is the name of his creator
the creature created by Victor Frankenstein

Examples

Many people mistakenly call Frankenstein's monster 'Frankenstein', confusing the creation with its creator.
The appearance of Frankenstein's monster has become iconic in horror films and popular culture.
In Mary Shelley's novel, Frankenstein's monster seeks acceptance but is rejected by society.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Frankenstein - the scientist who creates the monster in Mary Shelley's novel

Etymology

From the name of Victor Frankenstein, the scientist in Mary Shelley's 1818 novel, and 'monster'.
