farce comedy

1. NOUN

/ fɑrs ˈkɑmədi / (AmE), / fɑːs ˈkɒmədi / (BrE)
A type of comedy characterized by exaggerated satire, absurd situations, and improbable events.
comedy with exaggerated, improbable situations

Examples

The theater group performed a hilarious farce comedy that had the audience in stitches.
She enjoys watching classic farce comedies with slapstick humor and outrageous plots.
That movie is a good example of a modern farce comedy, filled with mistaken identities and physical gags.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: farce - a comedy with exaggerated, improbable situations
