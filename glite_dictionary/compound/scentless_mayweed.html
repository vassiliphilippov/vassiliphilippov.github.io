scentless mayweed

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛnt.ləs ˈmeɪˌwid / (AmE), / ˈsɛntləs ˈmeɪwiːd / (BrE)
a common European annual weed with white flowers and finely divided leaves, naturalized in eastern North America, often found in fields and wasteland
a European annual weed with white flowers

Examples

Scentless mayweed can quickly spread in unmanaged fields and often invades newly planted lawns.
Farmers sometimes struggle to control scentless mayweed in their crops, as it grows rapidly.
The delicate white petals of the scentless mayweed add a simple beauty to roadside verges.
