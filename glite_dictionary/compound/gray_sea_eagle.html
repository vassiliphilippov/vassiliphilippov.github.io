gray sea eagle

1. NOUN

/ ɡreɪ si ˈiːɡəl / (AmE), / ɡreɪ si ˈiːɡl / (BrE)
a large, greyish-brown eagle with a short wedge-shaped white tail, found in Europe and Greenland
a large eagle of Europe and Greenland

Examples

The gray sea eagle soared majestically above the rugged coastline.
Several tourists spotted a gray sea eagle hunting for fish near the fjord.
The conservation group is working to protect the gray sea eagle population in the region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: white-tailed eagle - large eagle with a white tail, native to Europe and northern Asia
