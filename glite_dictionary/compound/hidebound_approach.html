hidebound approach

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪdˌbaʊnd əˈproʊtʃ / (AmE), / ˈhaɪdˌbaʊnd əˈprəʊtʃ / (BrE)
a rigid or conservative method that resists change or innovation
rigid, conservative method

Examples

The company suffered from a hidebound approach that prevented it from adapting to new technologies.
Critics argue that the government's hidebound approach is slowing much-needed reform.
Teachers who rely on a hidebound approach may fail to engage students effectively.

Synset Examples

Her hidebound approach to teaching stifled creativity in the classroom.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conservative approach - a method that avoids change and innovation
  • Antonym: progressive approach - a method that embraces change and innovation

Etymology

From 'hidebound' meaning narrow-minded or inflexible, originally describing cattle with tough, inelastic hide.
