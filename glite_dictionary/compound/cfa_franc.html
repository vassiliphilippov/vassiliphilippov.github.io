CFA franc

1. NOUN

/ si ɛf eɪ fræŋk / (AmE), / siː ef eɪ fræŋk / (BrE)
The name of two currencies used in several African countries, both guaranteed by the French treasury.
African currency guaranteed by France

Examples

The economic policies of the region are closely tied to the CFA franc.
Many West African countries use the CFA franc as their official currency.
Discussions about currency reform often mention the stability of the CFA franc.

Synset Examples

The value of the CFA franc has been widely criticized as being too high, which many economists believe favours the urban elite of the African countries, which can buy imported manufactured goods cheaply at the expense of farmers who cannot easily export agricultural products.

Etymology

French acronym for 'Communauté Financière Africaine' (African Financial Community).
