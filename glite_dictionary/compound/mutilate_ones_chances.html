to mutilate one's chances

1. VERB

/ ˈmjuːtəˌleɪt wʌnz ˈtʃænsɪz / (AmE), / ˈmjuːtɪleɪt wʌnz ˈtʃɑːnsɪz / (BrE)
to severely damage or diminish one's opportunities for success or progress
seriously damage one's opportunities

Examples

Late submission could mutilate one's chances of getting the grant.
His nervous answer nearly mutilated his chances of passing the interview.
Spreading that rumor might mutilate your chances for a promotion.

Synset Examples

He feared the surgery would mutilate his chances of recovery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ruin one's chances - to destroy one's possibility of success
  • Antonym: improve one's chances - to increase one's opportunity for success
