rhyme royal

1. NOUN

/ ˈraɪm ˈrɔɪ.əl / (AmE), / ˈraɪm ˈrɔɪ.əl / (BrE)
A stanza form consisting of seven lines of iambic pentameter, usually rhyming ababbcc; introduced into English by Chaucer.
seven-line iambic pentameter stanza

Examples

Geoffrey Chaucer was the first English poet to use rhyme royal extensively in his works.
The poem's formal structure is based on rhyme royal, giving it a stately and measured rhythm.
Aspiring poets are often challenged by mastering the intricate pattern of rhyme royal.

Etymology

From the word 'rhyme' and 'royal', associated with use by royal poets and court verse.
