sperm count

1. NOUN

/ spɜrm kaʊnt / (AmE), / spɜːm kaʊnt / (BrE)
the number of sperm present in a given sample of semen, typically measured to assess male fertility
number of sperm in semen
medical

Examples

The doctor recommended testing his sperm count to evaluate his fertility.
A low sperm count can decrease the chances of conceiving a child.
Diet and lifestyle choices may significantly affect a man's sperm count.

Synset Examples

the sperm count is used as an indicator of male fertility

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fertility - ability to conceive children or produce offspring

2. NOUN

/ spɝm kaʊnt / (AmE), / spɜːm kaʊnt / (BrE)
a measurement of the concentration of sperm cells in a sample of semen, usually to assess fertility
measurement of sperm cells in semen
medical

Examples

The doctor recommended a sperm count to determine the cause of the couple's infertility.
A low sperm count can make it more difficult for a man to father a child.
His sperm count had improved after several months of treatment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sperm concentration - amount of sperm per milliliter of semen
  • Degree Higher: fertility test - a test to assess reproductive potential
