Hebridean Isles

1. PROPN

/ ˌhɛbrɪˈdiən aɪlz / (AmE), / ˌhɛbrɪˈdiːən aɪlz / (BrE)
a group of more than 500 islands off the western coast of Scotland, known as the Hebrides
islands off western Scotland

Examples

My grandparents grew up on one of the Hebridean Isles off the Scottish coast.
The wildlife of the Hebridean Isles attracts nature lovers from around the world.
Ferries regularly connect the mainland to several of the Hebridean Isles throughout the year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hebrides - another name for the Hebridean Isles

Etymology

From 'Hebridean,' relating to the Hebrides islands; 'Hebrides' from Latin 'Hebudes' or 'Ebudae'.
