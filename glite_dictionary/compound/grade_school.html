grade school

1. NOUN

/ ɡreɪd skuːl / (AmE), / ɡreɪd skuːl / (BrE)
A school for young children, usually covering the first six to eight years of formal education.
elementary school for young children
american

Examples

My son started first grade at the local grade school last fall.
After moving to a new city, she made friends quickly at her new grade school.
The annual grade school science fair attracts students from all over the district.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elementary school - A school for children in the first stages of their education, typically ages 5 to 11

Etymology

From 'grade' (as in levels or years of schooling) + 'school'.
