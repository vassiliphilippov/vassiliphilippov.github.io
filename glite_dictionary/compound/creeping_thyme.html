creeping thyme

1. NOUN

/ ˈkripɪŋ ˌθaɪm / (AmE), / ˈkriːpɪŋ ˌtaɪm / (BrE)
a low-growing aromatic shrub (Thymus serpyllum) with small purple flowers, commonly found on banks and hillsides in Europe and widely grown as ground cover
low-growing aromatic shrub

Examples

Creeping thyme makes an excellent ground cover for sunny spots in your garden.
Gardeners often choose creeping thyme for its aromatic foliage and attractive flowers.
The hillside was dotted with blooming creeping thyme, filling the air with a pleasant scent.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thyme - an aromatic herb of the genus Thymus, used in cooking and medicine
