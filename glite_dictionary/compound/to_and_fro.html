to and fro

1. ADV

/ tu ən froʊ / (AmE), / tə ən frəʊ / (BrE)
in a constant movement backward and forward, or from one place to another and back again
back and forth motion

Examples

The clock's pendulum swung to and fro in perfect rhythm.
Children were running to and fro in the playground during recess.
She paced to and fro across the room, deep in thought.

Synset Examples

he traveled back and forth between Los Angeles and New York
the treetops whipped to and fro in a frightening manner
the old man just sat on the porch and rocked back and forth all day

Related Terms

  • Synonym: back and forth - moving first in one direction and then in the opposite; alternating motion

Etymology

From Middle English phrase 'to and fro' meaning 'to and from'; 'fro' is an archaic word for 'from'.
