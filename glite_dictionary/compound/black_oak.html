black oak

1. NOUN

/ blæk oʊk / (AmE), / blæk əʊk / (BrE)
A medium to large deciduous tree of eastern North America, with dark outer bark and yellow inner bark used for tanning, having broad, bristle-tipped, five-lobed leaves.
North American deciduous tree with dark bark

Examples

The forest was dominated by towering black oak and maple trees.
Craftsmen historically used the yellow bark of the black oak for tanning leather.
A mature black oak can provide deep shade in summer with its broad leaves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: red oak - North American oak tree with reddish wood and bristle-tipped leaves
