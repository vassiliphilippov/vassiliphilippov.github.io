esophagogastric junction

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌsɑfəgoʊˈɡæstrɪk ˈdʒʌŋkʃən / (AmE), / ɪˌsɒfəɡəʊˈɡæstrɪk ˈdʒʌŋkʃən / (BrE)
the area where the esophagus joins the stomach, marking the transition between esophageal and gastric tissue
junction between esophagus and stomach
medical

Examples

The surgeon carefully examined the esophagogastric junction for signs of abnormal tissue.
Acid reflux often affects the esophagogastric junction, potentially causing inflammation or damage.
A biopsy was taken from the esophagogastric junction to check for the presence of Barrett's esophagus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gastroesophageal junction - junction between esophagus and stomach; alternate term

Etymology

From Greek 'oisophagos' (esophagus) and 'gaster' (stomach), plus 'junction' (joining).
