domestic prelate

1. NOUN

/ dəˈmɛstɪk ˈprɛlət / (AmE), / dəˈmɛstɪk ˈprɛlət / (BrE)
(in the Roman Catholic Church) a priest who is an honorary member of the papal household, often granted certain privileges and honors.
honorary papal household priest

Examples

The bishop was named a domestic prelate by the Pope in recognition of his service.
Several domestic prelates attended the Vatican ceremony, wearing distinctive attire.
As a domestic prelate, he enjoyed certain privileges within the papal household.

Etymology

From Latin 'domesticus' (of the household) and 'praelatus' (preferred, prelate).
