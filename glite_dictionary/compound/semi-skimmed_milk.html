semi-skimmed milk

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛmi ˌskɪmd ˈmɪlk / (AmE), / ˌsɛmi ˌskɪmd ˈmɪlk / (BrE)
milk from which some of the cream (fat) has been removed, typically containing about 1.5–1.8% fat.
milk with some cream removed

Examples

Many people in the UK prefer semi-skimmed milk with their tea or cereal.
I switched to semi-skimmed milk to reduce my daily fat intake.
The recipe calls for either semi-skimmed milk or whole milk, depending on your preference.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: whole milk - milk with all its natural fat content
  • Degree Lower: skimmed milk - milk from which almost all the cream has been removed
