physical topology

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪzɪkəl təˈpɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ˈfɪzɪkəl təˈpɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the arrangement and layout of devices and cables in a computer network, as it appears physically
actual layout of devices in a network

Examples

The physical topology of the office network is a star configuration.
Network administrators must plan the physical topology before installing cables and devices.
Different physical topologies can affect network performance and reliability.

Synset Examples

the physical topologies of local area networks include the bus, the ring and the star

Related Terms

  • Antonym: logical topology - the way data flows within a network, regardless of its physical layout

Etymology

From 'physical' (relating to material existence) and 'topology' (from Greek 'topos' meaning place).
