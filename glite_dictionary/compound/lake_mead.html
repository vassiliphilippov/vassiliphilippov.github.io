Lake Mead

1. PROPN

/ leɪk mid / (AmE), / leɪk miːd / (BrE)
the largest reservoir in the United States, located on the border of Nevada and Arizona, formed by the Hoover Dam on the Colorado River
largest US reservoir in Nevada and Arizona

Examples

The drought has significantly lowered the water levels in Lake Mead over the past decade.
Many tourists visit Lake Mead each year for boating and fishing activities.
Lake Mead was created after the construction of the Hoover Dam in the 1930s.
