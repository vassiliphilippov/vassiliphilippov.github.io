family Lemuridae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli lɛˈmjʊəraɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæməli lɛˈmjʊərɪdiː / (BrE)
A biological family of primates, commonly known as typical lemurs, native to Madagascar.
biological family of typical lemurs

Examples

The family Lemuridae includes several species known for their long, bushy tails and large eyes.
Conservation efforts in Madagascar focus on protecting the endangered family Lemuridae.
Biologists study the social behavior of the family Lemuridae to learn more about primate evolution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lemur - a small primate native to Madagascar, member of family Lemuridae

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'Lemuridae' (from 'Lemur', genus name + -idae, a taxonomic suffix for animal families).
