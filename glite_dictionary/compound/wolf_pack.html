wolf pack

1. NOUN

/ wʊlf pæk / (AmE), / wʊlf pæk / (BrE)
a group of wolves that live and hunt together as a social unit
group of wolves hunting together

Examples

The wolf pack moved silently through the forest, tracking its prey.
Researchers observed how the wolf pack coordinated their hunting strategies.
Each member of the wolf pack plays an important role in the group's survival.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pack - a group of animals, especially predatory ones, living and hunting together

2. NOUN

/ wʊlf pæk / (AmE), / wʊlf pæk / (BrE)
A group of submarines operating together to attack enemy convoys, especially used during World War II.
group of submarines operating together

Examples

During the Battle of the Atlantic, a wolf pack coordinated a devastating attack on an Allied convoy.
The German navy perfected the use of wolf packs to intercept and sink merchant ships.
Intelligence reports revealed that a wolf pack was patrolling the North Atlantic shipping lanes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: submarine - a naval vessel designed to operate underwater
  • Synonym: convoy - a group of ships or vehicles traveling together, often for protection

Etymology

Derived from the zoological term 'wolf pack', referring to wolves hunting in groups, metaphorically applied to coordinated submarine operations.
