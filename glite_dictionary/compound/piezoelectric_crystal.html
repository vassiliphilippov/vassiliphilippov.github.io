piezoelectric crystal

1. NOUN

/ ˌpiːzoʊɪˈlɛktrɪk ˈkrɪstəl / (AmE), / ˌpiːɛtsəʊɪˈlɛktrɪk ˈkrɪstəl / (BrE)
A crystal that produces an electric charge when subjected to mechanical stress and can be used as a transducer in electronic devices.
crystal producing electricity under stress

Examples

Quartz is a common example of a piezoelectric crystal used in many electronic circuits.
A piezoelectric crystal can generate a voltage when it is compressed or bent.
Ultrasound machines often rely on piezoelectric crystals to generate and receive sound waves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transducer - a device that converts variations in a physical quantity, such as pressure or brightness, into an electrical signal or vice versa

Etymology

From Greek 'piezein' (to press) and 'electric'; relating to the piezoelectric effect discovered in the 19th century.
