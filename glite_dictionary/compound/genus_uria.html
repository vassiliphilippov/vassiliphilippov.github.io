genus Uria

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˈjʊriə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈjʊərɪə / (BrE)
A genus of seabirds in the family Alcidae, commonly known as murres, which inhabit northern coastal regions.
genus of seabirds called murres
medical

Examples

The genus Uria includes two species of murres found in the northern oceans.
Scientists observed differences in breeding behavior between members of the genus Uria and closely related seabirds.
The eggs of the genus Uria are known for their unique pear shape, which helps prevent them from rolling off cliffs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: murre - a type of northern seabird of the genus Uria

Etymology

From Neo-Latin 'Uria', the scientific genus name, possibly from Greek 'ouria', a kind of water bird.
