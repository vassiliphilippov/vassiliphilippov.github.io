elaeostearic acid

1. NOUN

/ əˌleɪ.oʊˈstiː.ərɪk ˈæs.ɪd / (AmE), / ɪˌleɪ.əʊˈstɪə.rɪk ˈæs.ɪd / (BrE)
a crystalline unsaturated fatty acid found especially in tung oil, used industrially in varnishes and paints
a fatty acid found in tung oil

Examples

The drying properties of tung oil are mainly due to its high content of elaeostearic acid.
Chemists analyzed the sample and identified elaeostearic acid as the primary component.
Manufacturers use elaeostearic acid to produce quick-drying varnishes and paints.

Etymology

From Greek elaion (oil) + stear (fat, tallow) + -ic, indicating an acid.
