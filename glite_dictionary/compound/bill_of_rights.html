Bill of Rights

1. NOUN

/ bɪl əv ˈraɪts / (AmE), / bɪl əv ˈraɪts / (BrE)
A formal statement of the fundamental rights and liberties guaranteed to the people, especially referring to the first ten amendments of the United States Constitution, or similar documents in other countries.
formal statement of fundamental rights and liberties
legal

Examples

The Bill of Rights protects freedoms such as speech, religion, and the press.
Many countries have adopted their own versions of a Bill of Rights to ensure civil liberties.
The Supreme Court upheld the provisions outlined in the Bill of Rights.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: constitution - fundamental principles or established precedents by which a state is governed
  • Synonym: declaration of rights - another formal proclamation of legal rights and liberties

Etymology

From the phrase 'bill of rights', with 'bill' meaning a formal statement, and 'rights' referring to legal entitlements.
