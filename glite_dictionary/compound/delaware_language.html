Delaware language

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛləˌwɛr ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈdɛləwɛə ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Algonquian language historically spoken by the Delaware (Lenape) people of the northeastern United States.
Native American Algonquian language

Examples

Few people today speak the Delaware language fluently, but revitalization efforts are ongoing.
He devoted years to documenting the grammar of the Delaware language.
The children learned basic greetings in the Delaware language at the cultural center.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Lenape - the people who are native speakers of the Delaware language

Etymology

Named after the Delaware (Lenape) people, who in turn were named after the Delaware River.
