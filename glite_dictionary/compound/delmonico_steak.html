Delmonico steak

1. NOUN

/ dɛlˈmɑnɪkoʊ steɪk / (AmE), / dɛlˈmɒnɪkəʊ steɪk / (BrE)
A small steak cut from the front part of the short loin of beef, traditionally served as a luxury dish.
a steak cut from the front short loin of beef

Examples

The chef recommended the Delmonico steak because of its tender texture and rich flavor.
He ordered a perfectly cooked Delmonico steak at the famous steakhouse downtown.
Many food historians credit Delmonico's restaurant for popularizing the Delmonico steak in America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ribeye steak - a tender cut of beef from the rib section

Etymology

Named after Delmonico's, a famous 19th-century New York restaurant where the steak was popularized.
