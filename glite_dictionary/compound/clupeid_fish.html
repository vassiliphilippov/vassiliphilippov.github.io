clupeid fish

1. NOUN

/ ˈkluːpiɪd fɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈkluːpɪɪd fɪʃ / (BrE)
Any of a family of soft-finned schooling food fishes, such as herrings and sardines, typically found in the shallow waters of northern seas.
family of schooling food fishes (herrings, sardines, etc.)

Examples

The North Atlantic is home to several species of clupeid fish, including herring and sprat.
Fishermen rely on annual migrations of clupeid fish for a significant portion of their catch.
Sardines, a type of clupeid fish, are a staple in the diet of many coastal communities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: herring - a small, silvery fish of the Clupeidae family
  • Synonym: sardine - a small, oily fish of the Clupeidae family

Etymology

From New Latin Clupeidae, from Latin clupea (a kind of fish, herring).
