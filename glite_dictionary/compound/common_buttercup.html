common buttercup

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmən ˈbʌt̬ɚˌkʌp / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən ˈbʌtəkʌp / (BrE)
a perennial plant with golden to sulphur-yellow flowers, native to Europe and Asia and widely naturalized in North America, typically flowering in late spring and early summer
perennial yellow-flowered plant of temperate regions

Examples

In the meadow, bright common buttercup blossoms stretched as far as the eye could see.
She bent down to pick a common buttercup for her wildflower bouquet.
Common buttercup is sometimes mistaken for other yellow wildflowers, but its tall stems set it apart.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: buttercup - any plant of the genus Ranunculus, typically with bright yellow flowers
