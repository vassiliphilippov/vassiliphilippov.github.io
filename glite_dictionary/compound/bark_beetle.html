bark beetle

1. NOUN

/ bɑrk ˈbitl̩ / (AmE), / bɑːk ˈbiːtl̩ / (BrE)
A small beetle that bores tunnels in the bark and wood of trees, often causing damage; related to weevils.
small beetle that bores into tree bark

Examples

The forest was devastated by an outbreak of bark beetle infestations last year.
Many pine trees died because bark beetles burrowed through their bark, disrupting nutrient flow.
Scientists are studying how climate change affects the spread of the bark beetle.
