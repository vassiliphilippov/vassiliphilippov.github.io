tonic solfa

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɑːnɪk ˈsəʊlfɑː / (AmE), / ˈtɒnɪk ˈsɒlfɑː / (BrE)
a system of musical notation using syllables (do, re, mi, fa, sol, la, ti) to indicate the scale degrees
musical notation system using syllables

Examples

Many children learn to sing using the tonic solfa system in school music classes.
The choir practiced sight-reading by following the tonic solfa notation of the piece.
She quickly identified the melody in tonic solfa, moving from do to sol.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: solfège - a system of teaching pitch and sight singing using syllables

Etymology

From 'tonic' (relating to the keynote) + Italian 'solfa' (from solfeggio, a singing exercise using syllables).
