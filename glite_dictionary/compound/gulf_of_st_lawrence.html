Gulf of St. Lawrence

1. PROPN

/ ˈɡʌlf əv seɪnt ˈlɔːrəns / (AmE), / ˈɡʌlf əv sənt ˈlɒrəns / (BrE)
an extensive inlet of the Atlantic Ocean bordering southeastern Canada, forming the outlet of the St. Lawrence River
large gulf off southeastern Canada

Examples

The Gulf of St. Lawrence is home to a wide variety of marine life, including whales and seals.
Many fishing villages are scattered along the coast of the Gulf of St. Lawrence.
Icebergs occasionally drift down into the Gulf of St. Lawrence during the spring months.
