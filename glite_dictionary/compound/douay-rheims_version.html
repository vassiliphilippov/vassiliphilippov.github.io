Douay-Rheims Version

1. PROPN

/ ˌduː.eɪ ˈriːmz ˌvɝː.ʒən / (AmE), / ˌduː.eɪ ˈriːmz ˌvɜː.ʃən / (BrE)
an English translation of the Bible from the Latin Vulgate, produced by Roman Catholic scholars in the late 16th and early 17th centuries
English translation of the Bible from the Vulgate

Examples

Many Roman Catholics continue to read the Douay-Rheims Version for its literary style and faithfulness to the Vulgate.
The Douay-Rheims Version was often used in England before newer translations became popular.
Scholars compare the language of the Douay-Rheims Version with that of the King James Bible.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: King James Version - an English translation of the Bible authorized in 1611 for the Church of England

Etymology

Douay and Rheims are French towns where the translation was produced and published.
