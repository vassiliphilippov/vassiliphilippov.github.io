venereal wart

1. NOUN

/ vəˈnɪəri.əl wɔrt / (AmE), / vɪˈnɪəri.əl wɔːt / (BrE)
a small, benign growth appearing on or around the genitals or anus, typically caused by certain types of human papillomavirus (HPV)
wart on or near the genitals or anus, often from HPV
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed him with a venereal wart after a routine health screening.
Using protection can help prevent sexually transmitted infections, including venereal warts.
Removal of a venereal wart may require a minor surgical procedure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: genital wart - wart on the genital area caused by HPV

Etymology

From Latin 'venereus' (of sexual love), referencing Venus, the Roman goddess of love; 'wart' from Old English 'wearte'.
