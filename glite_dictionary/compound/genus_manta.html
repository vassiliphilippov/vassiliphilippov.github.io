genus Manta

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈmæn.tə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈmæn.tə / (BrE)
A genus of large rays in the family Mobulidae, known as manta rays, found in tropical and subtropical waters.
genus of large rays (manta rays)

Examples

Scientists have identified several species within the genus Manta based on genetic analysis.
The genus Manta includes some of the largest living rays in the ocean.
Protection efforts for the genus Manta are increasing due to declining populations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: manta ray - a large, flat-bodied fish in the genus Manta

Etymology

From Latin 'manta', meaning 'cloak' or 'blanket', describing the shape of the rays.
