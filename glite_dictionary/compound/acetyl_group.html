acetyl group

1. NOUN

/ əˈsiːtɪl ɡruːp / (AmE), / əˈsiːtɪl ɡruːp / (BrE)
a functional group with the formula CH₃CO–, derived from acetic acid and found in many organic compounds
organic chemical group CH₃CO–
medical

Examples

The acetyl group is commonly found in many biological molecules.
Adding an acetyl group to the compound changes its properties.
Enzymes often transfer an acetyl group during metabolic reactions.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: methyl group - the organic group –CH₃, a related simple alkyl group
  • Synonym: acetate - the ion or salt containing the acetyl group bonded to oxygen

Etymology

From 'acetyl,' formed from Latin 'acetum' (vinegar) + '-yl' (chemical suffix).
