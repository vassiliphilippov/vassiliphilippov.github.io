canary bird

1. NOUN

/ kəˈnɛri bɝd / (AmE), / kəˈneəri bɜːd / (BrE)
a small, usually yellow songbird native to the Canary Islands, often kept as a pet for its singing
a small yellow singing bird, kept as a pet

Examples

The bright yellow canary bird filled the room with its cheerful song.
She bought a canary bird for her grandmother as a birthday gift.
Many people enjoy watching their canary bird hop around in its cage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: canary - another term for the canary bird, especially as a pet or songbird

Etymology

From the Canary Islands, named after the Latin 'Canariae Insulae' (Islands of Dogs).
