dirty work

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɝti wɝk / (AmE), / ˈdɜːti wɜːk / (BrE)
unpleasant, difficult, or morally distasteful tasks that others may not want to do
unpleasant or undesirable tasks

Examples

He always gets stuck with the dirty work at the office.
Someone has to do the dirty work, even if it's thankless.
She hired someone else to handle the dirty work for her.

Synset Examples

He always gets stuck with the dirty work around here.
She didn't mind doing the dirty work if it helped the team.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grunt work - routine or physically demanding tasks regarded as boring or menial
  • Synonym: chores - everyday tasks, especially household ones, often considered boring or tedious
