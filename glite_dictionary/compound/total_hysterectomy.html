total hysterectomy

1. NOUN

/ ˈtoʊ.t̬əl hɪˌstɛr.əˈktə.mi / (AmE), / ˈtəʊ.təl hɪˌstɛr.əˈktə.mi / (BrE)
a surgical procedure in which the uterus and cervix are completely removed
removal of the uterus and cervix
medical

Examples

The doctor recommended a total hysterectomy due to the severity of her symptoms.
After her total hysterectomy, she received ongoing support during her recovery.
A total hysterectomy is performed when both the uterus and the cervix must be removed.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: partial hysterectomy - surgical removal of the uterus, leaving the cervix intact
  • Degree Lower: hysterectomy - surgical removal of the uterus

Etymology

From Greek 'hystera' (uterus) and '-ectomy' (removal).
