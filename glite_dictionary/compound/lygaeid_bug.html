lygaeid bug

1. NOUN

/ laɪˈɡiːɪd bʌɡ / (AmE), / laɪˈɡiːɪd bʌɡ / (BrE)
A brightly colored true bug of the family Lygaeidae, often a pest of cultivated crops and some fruit trees.
a brightly colored agricultural pest bug

Examples

The cotton fields were infested with lygaeid bugs, threatening this year's harvest.
Farmers often try new methods to control lygaeid bug populations in fruit orchards.
The brightly marked lygaeid bug is easily spotted on the leaves of tomato plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: true bug - insect of the order Hemiptera, characterized by piercing-sucking mouthparts

Etymology

From modern Latin Lygaeidae, the family name, based on the type genus Lygaeus.
