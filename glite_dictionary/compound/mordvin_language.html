Mordvin language

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɔrdvɪn ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈmɔːdvɪn ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Finno-Ugric language or group of languages spoken by the Mordvinian people in Russia
Finnic language of Mordvinians

Examples

The Mordvin language has two primary dialects: Erzya and Moksha.
There are efforts to revive the Mordvin language among younger Mordvinians.
Some ancient texts are preserved in the Mordvin language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Erzya - one of the two main branches of the Mordvin language
  • Synonym: Moksha - one of the two main branches of the Mordvin language
  • Degree Higher: Finnic - relating to the Finnic group of Uralic languages

Etymology

From the name 'Mordvin,' referring to the Mordvinian people of Russia.
