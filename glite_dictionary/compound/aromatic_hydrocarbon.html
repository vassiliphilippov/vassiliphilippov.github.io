aromatic hydrocarbon

1. NOUN

/ əˌrɑːmɛtɪk ˈhaɪˌdrɑːkɑːrbən / (AmE), / əˌræmətɪk ˈhaɪdrəʊˌkɑːbən / (BrE)
A hydrocarbon containing one or more benzene rings, characteristic of the benzene series of organic compounds.
hydrocarbon with benzene ring(s)

Examples

Benzene is a common example of an aromatic hydrocarbon found in many chemical products.
The laboratory tests confirmed the presence of several aromatic hydrocarbons in the contaminated groundwater.
Exposure to aromatic hydrocarbons should be minimized due to their potential health effects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: benzene - an aromatic hydrocarbon with the formula C6H6

Etymology

Aromatic from Greek 'arōma' (spice), relating to the compounds' distinctive odors; 'hydrocarbon' from hydrogen and carbon.
