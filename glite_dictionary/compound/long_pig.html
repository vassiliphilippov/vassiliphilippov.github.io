long pig

1. NOUN

/ lɔŋ pɪɡ / (AmE), / lɒŋ pɪɡ / (BrE)
slang term for human flesh, especially when regarded as food by cannibals.
slang for human flesh (cannibalism)
slang

Examples

The explorers had heard rumors that the tribe ate long pig as part of their rituals.
Anthropological studies document the use of the term long pig in reference to cannibal practices.
Some sailors feared shipwreck on certain islands because of local legends about long pig.

Synset Examples

In some cultures, they refer to human meat as long pig.
The term long pig has historical roots in cannibalism.

Etymology

Said to be a translation of a phrase used by indigenous peoples of the South Pacific, comparing human flesh to pork.
