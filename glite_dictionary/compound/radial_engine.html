radial engine

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪdiəl ˈɛndʒɪn / (AmE), / ˈreɪdiəl ˈɛndʒɪn / (BrE)
An internal combustion engine with cylinders arranged in a circle around a central crankcase, commonly used in aircraft.
engine with cylinders arranged in a circle

Examples

During World War II, many fighter planes were powered by a radial engine for reliability and performance.
The mechanic carefully reassembled the radial engine before the vintage aircraft's test flight.
Radial engines are known for their unique appearance, with cylinders arranged like spokes on a wheel.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inline engine - an engine with cylinders arranged in a single straight line
  • Synonym: rotary engine - an internal combustion engine in which the crankshaft remains stationary and the entire cylinder block rotates
