Twinkie defence

1. NOUN

/ ˈtwɪŋki dɪˌfɛns / (AmE), / ˈtwɪŋki dɪˌfɛns / (BrE)
a legal defense in which a defendant claims that an unusual circumstance, such as consumption of junk food or emotional distress, diminished their responsibility for a crime
legal defense citing diminished responsibility
legal american

Examples

The lawyer argued the Twinkie defence to explain his client's actions during the trial.
Many people criticized the use of the Twinkie defence as an excuse for serious crimes.
He referenced the Twinkie defence when discussing unusual legal strategies.

Synset Examples

The lawyer used the Twinkie defence in a strange case.
The Twinkie defence became a topic of debate nationwide.
He jokingly referenced the Twinkie defence during his trial.

Etymology

From the 1979 trial of Dan White in San Francisco, where his consumption of Twinkies was cited as evidence of depression.
