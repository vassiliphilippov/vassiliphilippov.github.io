baby mama

1. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪ.bi ˌmɑ.mə / (AmE), / ˈbeɪ.bi ˌmɑː.mə / (BrE)
the mother of a man's child, especially when the man is not married to or living with her
mother of a man's child (not married to him)
informal american

Examples

After their breakup, he still supports his baby mama and visits their daughter every weekend.
She invited her baby mama to the child's birthday party to keep things friendly for their son.
Rumors spread quickly when the rapper mentioned his baby mama in the new song.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: baby daddy - the father of a woman's child (not her husband or partner)

Etymology

Originally African-American Vernacular English, from 'baby' + 'mama' (mother); popularized in US music and culture since the late 20th century.
