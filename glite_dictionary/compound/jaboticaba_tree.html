jaboticaba tree

1. NOUN

/ ˌʒɑːbəˈtiːkəˌbɑː triː / (AmE), / ˌʒabəˈtiːkəbə triː / (BrE)
A small evergreen tropical tree native to Brazil and the West Indies, grown in Brazil for its edible, tough-skinned, purple, grapelike fruit that grows directly on the trunk and branches; introduced to the southern United States.
tropical fruit tree native to Brazil

Examples

The jaboticaba tree produces fruit that resembles grapes but grows directly on its trunk and branches.
After planting a jaboticaba tree in her backyard, Maria eagerly awaited the first harvest of its unique fruit.
Botanists studied the jaboticaba tree to learn more about its adaptations to tropical climates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jaboticaba - the edible fruit produced by the jaboticaba tree

Etymology

From Portuguese jabuticaba, of Tupi origin, referring to the fruit of the tree.
