Ewing's tumour

1. NOUN

/ ˈjuː.ɪŋz ˈtuː.mɚ / (AmE), / ˈjuː.ɪŋz ˈtjuː.mə / (BrE)
a rare malignant tumor that forms in the bone marrow, most often affecting the pelvis or long bones, especially in children and young adults
rare malignant bone marrow tumor
medical

Examples

The young patient was diagnosed with Ewing's tumour after persistent pain in his leg.
Treatment for Ewing's tumour usually involves chemotherapy and surgery.
Researchers are studying new therapies to improve outcomes for those with Ewing's tumour.

Etymology

Named after James Ewing, the American pathologist who first described it in 1921.
