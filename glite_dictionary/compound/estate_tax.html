estate tax

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈsteɪt tæks / (AmE), / ɪˈsteɪt tæks / (BrE)
a tax levied on the total value of a deceased person's money and property before it is distributed to heirs
tax on the estate of a deceased person

Examples

After her grandfather died, the family had to pay a significant estate tax on his assets.
The new law increased the threshold for the estate tax, reducing the number of affected families.
Financial planners often offer advice on minimizing potential estate taxes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inheritance tax - a tax on money or property inherited from someone who has died
