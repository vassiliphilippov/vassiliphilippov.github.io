fluorocarbon plastic

1. NOUN

/ flʊˌɔrəˌkɑrbən ˈplæstɪk / (AmE), / flʊə.rəˌkɑː.bən ˈplæs.tɪk / (BrE)
a type of plastic made from fluorocarbons, known for its chemical resistance and stability
plastic made from fluorocarbons

Examples

Pipes made from fluorocarbon plastic are highly resistant to corrosive chemicals.
Some laboratory equipment is coated in fluorocarbon plastic to prevent contamination.
Engineers selected fluorocarbon plastic for its durability and low friction properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: PTFE - polytetrafluoroethylene, a common fluorocarbon plastic also known as Teflon
