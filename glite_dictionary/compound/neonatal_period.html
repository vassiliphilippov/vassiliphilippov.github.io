neonatal period

1. NOUN

/ ˌniː.oʊˈneɪ.tl̩ ˈpɪr.i.əd / (AmE), / ˌniː.əʊˈneɪ.tl̩ ˈpɪə.ri.əd / (BrE)
the period comprising the first 28 days after birth in a newborn's life
the first 28 days of a newborn's life
medical

Examples

Modern hospitals provide specialized care for infants during the neonatal period.
The risk of certain infections is highest in the neonatal period.
Premature babies require intensive monitoring throughout the entire neonatal period.

Etymology

From neo- (new) + Latin natalis (of birth) + period (time interval).
