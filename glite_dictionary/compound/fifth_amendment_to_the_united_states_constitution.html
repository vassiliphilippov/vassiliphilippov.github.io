Fifth Amendment (to the United States Constitution)

1. NOUN

/ fɪfθ əˈmɛndmənt / (AmE), / fɪfθ əˈmɛndmənt / (BrE)
an amendment to the U.S. Constitution that sets restrictions on government prosecution of criminal cases, mandates due process, and prohibits self-incrimination and double jeopardy
constitutional amendment on due process and rights of the accused
legal american

Examples

He invoked the Fifth Amendment to avoid testifying in court.
The Fifth Amendment protects against double jeopardy, ensuring no person is tried twice for the same crime.
Property owners can demand compensation under the Fifth Amendment when land is taken for public use.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: due process - fair treatment through the normal judicial system
  • Synonym: self-incrimination - act of implicating oneself in a crime
