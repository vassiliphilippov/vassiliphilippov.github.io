pine vole

1. NOUN

/ paɪn voʊl / (AmE), / paɪn vəʊl / (BrE)
A small, short-tailed, glossy-furred vole found in the eastern United States, known for its burrowing habits.
burrowing vole of eastern US

Examples

The pine vole spends most of its life in underground tunnels.
Farmers are often concerned about the damage caused by pine voles to fruit tree roots.
We spotted a pine vole in the garden while digging up old roots.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vole - a small rodent resembling a mouse or rat
