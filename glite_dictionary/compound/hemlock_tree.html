hemlock tree

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛm.lɑk ˌtri / (AmE), / ˈhɛmlɒk ˌtriː / (BrE)
An evergreen coniferous tree of the genus Tsuga, native to North America and Asia, characterized by flat needles and small cones.
evergreen coniferous tree (genus Tsuga)

Examples

The hemlock tree is a common sight in the forests of the northeastern United States.
She admired the graceful branches of a hemlock tree by the riverbank.
Many birds build their nests in the dense foliage of the hemlock tree.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hemlock - a highly poisonous plant (Conium maculatum)

Etymology

From the resemblance in smell of crushed foliage to the poisonous plant hemlock (Conium).
