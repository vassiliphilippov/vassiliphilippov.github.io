genus Echinacea

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌɛk.ɪˈneɪ.ʃə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌɛk.ɪˈneɪ.si.ə / (BrE)
A small genus of coarse perennial herbs native to North America, known for their daisy-like flowers and medicinal uses.
a genus of North American flowering herbs

Examples

Scientists are studying the medicinal properties of genus Echinacea to understand its effects on the immune system.
Many gardeners plant members of the genus Echinacea for their attractive flowers and hardiness.
The genus Echinacea is well known for its use in herbal remedies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coneflower - common name for plants in the genus Echinacea

Etymology

From Latin 'echinacea', derived from Greek 'ekhinos' meaning 'hedgehog,' referring to the spiny central disc of the flower.
