tappet wrench

1. NOUN

/ ˈtæpɪt rɛntʃ / (AmE), / ˈtæpɪt rɛntʃ / (BrE)
a spanner with parallel jaws at a fixed width, typically used to adjust tappets or other parts in machinery
spanner with fixed-width parallel jaws

Examples

The mechanic reached for a tappet wrench to adjust the engine's valves.
He keeps a set of tappet wrenches in his toolbox for precise work.
This tappet wrench fits perfectly on the narrow nuts under the cover.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: open-end wrench - spanner with open, fixed-width jaws used for gripping nuts and bolts

Etymology

From 'tappet' (part of a machine, especially in engines) + 'wrench'.
