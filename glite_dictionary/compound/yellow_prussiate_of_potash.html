yellow prussiate of potash

1. NOUN

/ ˈjɛloʊ ˌprʌsiˌeɪt əv ˈpɑːtæʃ / (AmE), / ˈjɛləʊ ˌprʌsɪeɪt əv ˈpɒtæʃ / (BrE)
a yellow crystalline salt, potassium ferrocyanide, used chiefly in the preparation of pigments and as an anticaking agent
potassium ferrocyanide; a yellow chemical compound

Examples

Gardeners sometimes add yellow prussiate of potash to fertilizers as an anticaking agent.
Historically, yellow prussiate of potash was used in the production of pigments and dyes.
Be careful when handling yellow prussiate of potash, as it is a chemical compound.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: potassium ferrocyanide - a yellow crystalline salt used in industry

Etymology

From 'prussiate,' relating to Prussian blue pigments, and 'potash,' an old term for potassium compounds.
