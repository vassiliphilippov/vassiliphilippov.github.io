mountain chinchilla

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaʊn.tən tʃɪnˈtʃɪl.ə / (AmE), / ˈmaʊn.tɪn tʃɪnˈtʃɪl.ə / (BrE)
a South American rodent native to the mountains of Chile and Peru, now also bred in captivity for its fur and as a pet
South American rodent native to Andes

Examples

The mountain chinchilla is known for its exceptionally soft, dense fur.
Many people are surprised to learn that the mountain chinchilla is now bred in captivity as a pet.
Deforestation in the Andes poses a serious threat to the wild population of the mountain chinchilla.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chinchilla - a small South American rodent with soft gray fur

Etymology

From Spanish 'chinchilla', possibly from Aymara or Quechua.
