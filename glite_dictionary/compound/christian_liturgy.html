Christian liturgy

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɪstʃən ˈlɪtərdʒi / (AmE), / ˈkrɪstʃən ˈlɪtədʒi / (BrE)
the prescribed form or set of Christian worship services, especially as conducted in churches
prescribed form of Christian worship services

Examples

The Christian liturgy is central to the worship practices of many denominations.
During the Easter season, the Christian liturgy includes special prayers and hymns.
Scholars study the history and development of the Christian liturgy across different cultures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: liturgy - a prescribed form of public worship
  • Synonym: service - an act of public worship in a church

Etymology

From Greek *leitourgia* (public service, worship), via Latin *līturgia*; 'Christian' from Greek *Christianos* (follower of Christ).
