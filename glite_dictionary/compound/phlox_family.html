phlox family

1. NOUN

/ flɑks ˈfæməli / (AmE), / flɒks ˈfæməli / (BrE)
a family (Polemoniaceae) of mostly herbaceous plants with often showy flowers, widely distributed in temperate regions
family of showy-flowered herbaceous plants

Examples

The phlox family includes many popular garden flowers, such as the bluebonnet and Jacob's ladder.
Plants of the phlox family are admired for their vibrant, showy blossoms in spring and summer.
Botanists classify the species under the phlox family due to their distinctive floral structures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Polemoniaceae - the scientific name for the phlox family
