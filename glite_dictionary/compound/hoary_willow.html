hoary willow

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɔri ˈwɪloʊ / (AmE), / ˈhɔːri ˈwɪləʊ / (BrE)
A North American shrub of the willow family with whitish, grayish leaves.
North American shrub with whitish leaves

Examples

The hoary willow thrives in moist soils along riverbanks in North America.
Botanists studied the distinctive leaves of the hoary willow to understand its adaptation to the environment.
A cluster of hoary willow bushes bordered the edge of the meadow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: willow - A tree or shrub of the genus Salix, typically with narrow leaves and catkins
