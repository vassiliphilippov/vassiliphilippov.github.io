franquist belief

1. NOUN

/ frænˌkɪst bəˈlif / (AmE), / fræŋˌkwɪst bɪˈliːf / (BrE)
A political viewpoint or ideology associated with the regime of Francisco Franco, especially support for authoritarian, nationalist principles established during his rule in Spain.
support for Franco's political ideology

Examples

Her franquist belief influenced her stance on government authority and social order.
Some older citizens still hold strong franquist beliefs despite Spain's modern democracy.
The historian wrote extensively about the impact of franquist belief on Spanish society.

Synset Examples

Her franquist beliefs shaped her political views.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fascist belief - support for fascist political ideology
  • Antonym: democratic belief - support for democratic political ideology

Etymology

From Spanish 'franquista' (supporter of Franco), after Francisco Franco (1892–1975), Spanish dictator + English 'belief'.
