investment letter

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈvɛstmənt ˈlɛtər / (AmE), / ɪnˈvɛstmənt ˈlɛtə / (BrE)
a letter stating that a security is being purchased for investment purposes rather than resale, typically used to avoid the need for SEC registration
letter about buying securities for investment
legal

Examples

The purchaser provided an investment letter to the broker to complete the private placement.
Without an investment letter, the securities would have required SEC registration.
He signed an investment letter affirming that he intended to hold the shares long-term.
