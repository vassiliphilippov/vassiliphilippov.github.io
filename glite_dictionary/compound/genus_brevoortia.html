genus Brevoortia

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs brɛˈvɔrt.i.ə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs briːˈvɔː.tɪ.ə / (BrE)
A genus of fish in the herring family, commonly known as menhaden, found along the Atlantic coast of North and South America.
genus of fish (menhaden)

Examples

Marine biologists often study the genus Brevoortia to understand population dynamics in coastal ecosystems.
Several species within the genus Brevoortia are harvested commercially for their oil and fish meal.
The distribution of the genus Brevoortia extends along both Atlantic and Gulf coasts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: menhaden - an oily fish of the genus Brevoortia

Etymology

Genus name from the surname Brevoort, honoring James Carson Brevoort, American ichthyologist.
