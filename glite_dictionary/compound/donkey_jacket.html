donkey jacket

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɑŋki ˌdʒækɪt / (AmE), / ˈdɒŋki ˌdʒækɪt / (BrE)
A short, thick jacket made of heavy material, typically worn by manual laborers, especially in Britain.
a thick workman's jacket
british

Examples

He wore his old donkey jacket to keep warm on the construction site.
Many British workers traditionally wore a donkey jacket during the winter months.
The museum has a display featuring a classic donkey jacket from the 1970s.

Etymology

Originally associated with British laborers; the name may derive from 'donkey engines' (small steam engines used by workers).
