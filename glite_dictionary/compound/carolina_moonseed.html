Carolina moonseed

1. NOUN

/ kəˈraɪlɪnə ˈmuːnˌsiːd / (AmE), / kəˈraɪlɪnə ˈmuːnsiːd / (BrE)
A woody vine native to the southeastern United States, resembling the common moonseed but bearing red fruits.
woody vine with red fruits, southeastern US

Examples

The dense foliage of the Carolina moonseed provided excellent cover for birds in the garden.
Botanists identified the vine as a Carolina moonseed due to its distinctive red fruit.
Many southern landscapes feature the Carolina moonseed climbing along fences and trees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moonseed - woody climbing plant with rounded leaves and berries
