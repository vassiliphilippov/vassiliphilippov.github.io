losing streak

1. NOUN

/ ˈluːzɪŋ strik / (AmE), / ˈluːzɪŋ striːk / (BrE)
A series of consecutive defeats or losses, especially in sports, games, or competitions.
a run of consecutive losses

Examples

After three losses in a row, the team found themselves on a losing streak.
His confidence dropped during the month-long losing streak at the poker table.
They finally ended their losing streak with a dramatic victory on Saturday.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: winning streak - a run of consecutive wins or successes
