pinon nut

1. NOUN

/ ˈpiːnjɒn nʌt / (AmE), / ˈpiːnjɒn nʌt / (BrE)
the edible seed of certain pine trees, especially those native to southwestern North America, commonly eaten as a snack or used in cooking
edible pine seed, esp. from southwestern US

Examples

The chef sprinkled pinon nuts over the salad for added flavor and crunch.
Harvesting pinon nuts is an important tradition in some Native American communities.
I bought a bag of roasted pinon nuts at the local farmers' market.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pine nut - the seed of a pine tree, often eaten as food

Etymology

From Spanish 'piñón' (pine nut), ultimately from Latin 'pinus' (pine).
