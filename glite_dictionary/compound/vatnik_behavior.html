vatnik behavior

1. NOUN

/ ˈvæt.nɪk bɪˈheɪ.vjɚ / (AmE), / ˈvæt.nɪk bɪˈheɪ.vjə / (BrE)
Behavior characterized by extreme nationalism, especially associated with uncritical support for state propaganda, often used in political discussions about individuals from Russia or former Soviet countries.
extreme nationalist behavior, esp. pro-Russian
slang

Examples

The forum moderators warned users that any display of vatnik behavior would lead to a ban.
His posts were full of vatnik behavior, openly supporting government actions without question.
Journalists often debate the influence of vatnik behavior on social networks during times of conflict.

Synset Examples

Many online discussions devolve into arguments about vatnik behavior.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: nationalist behavior - behavior demonstrating strong support for one's nation and its interests
  • Degree Lower: patriotic - having or expressing devotion to and vigorous support for one's country
  • Degree Higher: jingoism - extreme patriotism, especially in the form of aggressive or warlike foreign policy

Etymology

From Russian internet slang 'vatnik', originally referring to a padded cotton jacket but later used disparagingly for blindly nationalistic individuals.
