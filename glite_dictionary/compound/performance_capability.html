performance capability

1. NOUN

/ pərˈfɔrməns kəˌpeɪəbɪlɪti / (AmE), / pəˈfɔːməns kəˌpeɪəbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the ability of a technological or mechanical system to function or operate as intended or specified
ability of a system to function as intended

Examples

The new engine's performance capability far exceeds that of previous models.
Engineers regularly test the performance capability of safety systems under extreme conditions.
Customers often demand higher performance capability in the latest smartphones.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: capacity - the maximum amount that something can contain or produce
  • Antonym: limitation - an aspect in which performance is restricted or constrained
