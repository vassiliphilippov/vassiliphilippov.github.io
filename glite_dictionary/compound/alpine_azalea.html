alpine azalea

1. NOUN

/ ˈælpaɪn əˈzeɪliə / (AmE), / ˈalpʌɪn əˈzeɪliə / (BrE)
A creeping mat-forming evergreen shrub found in high mountain regions of the Northern Hemisphere, cultivated for its rose-pink flowers.
creeping shrub with rose-pink flowers

Examples

The alpine azalea thrives in cold, mountainous areas and produces beautiful rose-pink blossoms.
Gardeners often choose alpine azalea to add color to rock gardens or alpine beds.
In the wild, alpine azalea can form dense mats across rocky slopes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: azalea - a type of flowering shrub in the genus Rhododendron
