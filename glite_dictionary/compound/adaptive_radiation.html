adaptive radiation

1. NOUN

/ əˈdæptɪv ˌreɪdiˈeɪʃn̩ / (AmE), / əˈdaptɪv ˌreɪdɪˈeɪʃn̩ / (BrE)
the evolution of diverse species from a common ancestor when organisms adapt to different ecological niches
evolution of varied species from one ancestor

Examples

Darwin's finches are a classic example of adaptive radiation in the Galápagos Islands.
The mammals underwent adaptive radiation after the extinction of the dinosaurs, occupying many ecological roles.
Coral reefs provide an environment where fish species experience rapid adaptive radiation.

Etymology

From 'adaptive' (from Latin adaptare, to fit) and 'radiation' (from Latin radiatio, a spreading out in rays).
