genus Carcharhinus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌkɑːr.kəˈraɪ.nəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌkɑː.kəˈraɪ.nəs / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Carcharhinidae, comprising various species of requiem sharks found in warm seas.
type genus of Carcharhinidae (requiem sharks)

Examples

The genus Carcharhinus includes many of the most commonly recognized sharks, such as the bull shark and the blacktip shark.
Marine biologists are studying how certain species within the genus Carcharhinus adapt to different coastal habitats.
Members of the genus Carcharhinus are often distinguished by their streamlined bodies and prominent dorsal fins.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Carcharhinidae - the family of requiem sharks

Etymology

From Greek 'karcharos' (sharpened, jagged) and 'rhinos' (nose), referring to the shark's snout.
