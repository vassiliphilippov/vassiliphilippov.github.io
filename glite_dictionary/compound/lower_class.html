lower class

1. NOUN

/ ˈloʊər klæs / (AmE), / ˈləʊə klɑːs / (BrE)
the social group that is considered to have the lowest status in a society, typically with the least wealth, education, or power
the social group of lowest status

Examples

Many people born into the lower class face significant barriers to social mobility.
The politician promised new programs to support members of the lower class.
She wrote a novel highlighting the struggles of the lower class in Victorian England.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: working class - the social group consisting of people employed for wages, especially in manual or industrial work
  • Degree Higher: middle class - the social group between the upper and working classes, including professional and business people and their families
