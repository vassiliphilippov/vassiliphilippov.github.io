Castile soap

1. NOUN

/ kæˈstil soʊp / (AmE), / kaˈstiːl səʊp / (BrE)
a hard, mild soap made with olive oil and sodium hydroxide, originally from the Castile region of Spain
mild hard soap made from olive oil

Examples

Many people use Castile soap as a gentle cleanser for sensitive skin.
You can make your own household cleaner by diluting Castile soap with water.
The recipe calls for a bar of pure Castile soap grated into flakes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soap - a substance used for washing and cleaning

Etymology

From Castile, a region in Spain, where this type of soap originated.
