single entendre

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪŋɡəl ɑ̃ˈtɑːndrə / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋɡəl ɒ̃ˈtɒndrə / (BrE)
a statement or joke that has only one clear meaning, as opposed to a double entendre
phrase or joke with one clear meaning

Examples

The comedian's act was full of single entendres that everyone could understand.
Her speech avoided subtlety, relying instead on single entendre jokes.
He prefers the simplicity of a single entendre over complex wordplay.

Synset Examples

The comedian's act relies on clever single entendres.
Her jokes are mostly single entendres, not double.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: double entendre - a phrase or figure of speech that has two meanings, one of which is usually risqué

Etymology

Modelled after the French-derived 'double entendre,' with 'single' added in English.
