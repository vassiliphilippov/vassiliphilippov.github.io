scombroid fish

1. NOUN

/ ˈskɑm.brɔɪd fɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈskɒm.brɔɪd fɪʃ / (BrE)
Any of a group of important marine fishes, including tunas and mackerels, found in tropical and temperate seas, some of which are partially endothermic and can thrive in colder waters.
group of marine fishes including tunas and mackerels

Examples

Scombroid fish such as tuna are highly valued for their rich, flavorful meat.
Scombroid fish can maintain a higher body temperature than most other fish, allowing scombroid fish to hunt in cooler waters.
Many of the world’s largest commercial fisheries harvest scombroid fish for both food and sport.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tuna - a large and fast-swimming scombroid fish
  • Synonym: mackerel - a common type of scombroid fish

Etymology

From Greek skombros (a kind of fish, mackerel) + -oid.
