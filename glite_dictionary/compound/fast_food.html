fast food

1. NOUN

/ fæst ˈfuːd / (AmE), / fɑːst ˈfuːd / (BrE)
Inexpensive food, such as hamburgers or fried chicken, prepared and served quickly, typically by restaurants specializing in quick service.
quickly prepared inexpensive food

Examples

He grabbed some fast food on his way home from work.
Many people eat fast food because it is convenient and cheap.
Excessive consumption of fast food can lead to health problems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: junk food - food that is high in calories but low in nutritional value
