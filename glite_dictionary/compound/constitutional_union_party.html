Constitutional Union Party

1. PROPN

/ kənˌstɪtuˈʃənl ˈjunjən ˌpɑrti / (AmE), / kənˌstɪtjʊˈʃuːnəl ˈjuːnjən ˌpɑːti / (BrE)
a former United States political party, formed in 1859 by former Whigs to preserve the Union before the Civil War
U.S. political party (1859–1860s)

Examples

The Constitutional Union Party nominated John Bell for president in the 1860 election.
Though short-lived, the Constitutional Union Party played a notable role in pre-Civil War American politics.
Many former Whigs joined the Constitutional Union Party hoping to prevent the breakup of the United States.
