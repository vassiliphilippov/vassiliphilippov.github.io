king nut

1. NOUN

/ kɪŋ nʌt / (AmE), / kɪŋ nʌt / (BrE)
A type of hickory tree native to the eastern United States, similar to the shagbark hickory but producing a larger nut.
a hickory tree with a large nut

Examples

The king nut tree produces one of the largest edible nuts found in North American forests.
Unlike the shagbark hickory, the king nut has a much thicker shell.
Hikers often search for king nuts as a source of food in the wild.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shagbark hickory - a type of hickory tree with shaggy bark and edible nuts
