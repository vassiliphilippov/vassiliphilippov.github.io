desensitization procedure

1. NOUN

/ dɪˌsɛnsɪˌtaɪˈzeɪʃən prəˈsiːdʒər / (AmE), / dɪˌsɛnsɪtaɪˈzeɪʃən prəˈsiːdʒə / (BrE)
a technique in behavior therapy for treating phobias and anxiety problems, involving exposure to feared situations in a controlled, relaxed way until the anxiety lessens
behavior therapy technique to reduce anxiety
medical

Examples

The therapist suggested a desensitization procedure to help Sarah overcome her fear of dogs.
During the desensitization procedure, patients are gradually exposed to their source of anxiety.
Many psychologists use a desensitization procedure as part of their treatment for specific phobias.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: systematic desensitization - a specific form of desensitization therapy for phobias
