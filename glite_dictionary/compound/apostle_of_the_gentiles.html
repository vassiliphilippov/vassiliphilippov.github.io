Apostle of the Gentiles

1. PROPN

/ əˈpɑsəl əv ðə ˈdʒɛnˌtaɪlz / (AmE), / əˈpɒsl̩ əv ðə ˈdʒɛn.taɪlz / (BrE)
a title for Saint Paul, who was a principal Christian missionary to non-Jews and author of several New Testament Epistles
Saint Paul as missionary to non-Jews

Examples

Saint Paul is often called the Apostle of the Gentiles because of his missionary work among non-Jews.
The title Apostle of the Gentiles highlights Paul's role in spreading Christianity beyond Jewish communities.
In his letters, the Apostle of the Gentiles addresses early Christian churches across the Roman Empire.

Synset Examples

Paul's name was Saul prior to his conversion to Christianity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Paul - Christian apostle and author, also known as Saul of Tarsus
  • Degree Higher: Gentile - a person who is not Jewish

Etymology

From Greek 'apostolos' (messenger, one who is sent forth) and Latin 'gentilis' (of a clan, foreign, non-Jewish).
