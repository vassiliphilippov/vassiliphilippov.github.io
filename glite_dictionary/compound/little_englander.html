Little Englander

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɪtəl ˈɪŋɡləndər / (AmE), / ˌlɪtl ˈɪŋləndə / (BrE)
a person with narrow-minded or insular views, especially about foreign affairs and England's role in the world
person with narrow, insular English views
british

Examples

He accused her of being a Little Englander because she opposed international cooperation.
Many politicians avoid the Little Englander label by supporting global partnerships.
In discussions about Brexit, the term Little Englander was frequently used.

Synset Examples

He has a little Englander mentality when it comes to foreign policy.
The debate revealed his little Englander views on immigration.
Being a little Englander limits his understanding of global issues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nationalist - a person who advocates for the interests of their own nation, sometimes seen as a synonym in this context
  • Antonym: cosmopolitan - a person who is comfortable with and open to different cultures, an antonym in sociopolitical attitude

Etymology

Originally referred to 19th-century opponents of British imperial expansion, now broader.
