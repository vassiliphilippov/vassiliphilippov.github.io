Seminole bread

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛmɪˌnoʊl brɛd / (AmE), / ˈsɛmɪnəʊl brɛd / (BrE)
A small, tough, woody plant (Zamia integrifolia) native to Florida, the West Indies, and Cuba, whose roots and stems yield an edible arrowroot starch.
woody plant yielding arrowroot starch

Examples

The roots of Seminole bread were used by the indigenous people to make a nourishing starch.
We learned about Seminole bread while studying traditional foods of Florida.
Some local dishes incorporate starch extracted from Seminole bread.

Etymology

Named after the Seminole people; 'bread' refers to the edible starch made from its roots.
