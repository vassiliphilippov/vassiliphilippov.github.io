zoot suit

1. NOUN

/ zut sut / (AmE), / zuːt sjuːt / (BrE)
A man's suit with a long jacket, wide padded shoulders, and high-waisted, wide-legged, tight-cuffed trousers, typically worn as a flamboyant fashion in the 1930s and 1940s.
flashy men's suit from the 1930s–40s

Examples

He strutted into the club wearing his favorite zoot suit and a wide-brimmed hat.
The zoot suit became a symbol of rebellion among minority youth in the 1940s.
Museum exhibits highlighted the vibrant history of the zoot suit in American culture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suit - a set of outer clothes made of the same fabric

Etymology

Originated in American English during the late 1930s; the term 'zoot' is likely a reduplication of 'suit,' influenced by jazz slang.
