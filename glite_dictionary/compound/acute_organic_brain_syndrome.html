acute organic brain syndrome

1. NOUN

/ əˈkjut ɔrˈɡænɪk breɪn sɛnˌdroʊm / (AmE), / əˈkjuːt ɔːˈɡænɪk breɪn ˈsɪn.drəʊm / (BrE)
A temporary mental disorder, such as confusion or disorientation, in an otherwise healthy person caused by reversible brain tissue impairment from factors like head injury, drugs, or infection.
temporary mental disorder due to reversible brain impairment
medical

Examples

The patient developed acute organic brain syndrome after suffering a severe infection.
Doctors ruled out stroke and determined her confusion was due to acute organic brain syndrome.
Recovery from acute organic brain syndrome is likely if the underlying cause is treated quickly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: delirium - A disturbed state of mind with confusion and reduced awareness, often reversible
