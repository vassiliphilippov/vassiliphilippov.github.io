quartermaster general

1. NOUN

/ ˌkwɔːrtərˌmæstər ˈdʒɛnərəl / (AmE), / ˌkwɔːtəmɑːstə ˈdʒɛnərəl / (BrE)
a senior military officer responsible for the supply and provision of equipment, food, and transportation for an entire army.
senior officer in charge of army supplies

Examples

The quartermaster general coordinated the distribution of food and equipment to every division.
As quartermaster general, she was responsible for ensuring the troops had adequate winter clothing.
During the war, the quartermaster general faced challenges in transporting supplies across the difficult terrain.

Etymology

From 'quartermaster' (from Middle Dutch 'meester' meaning 'master') and 'general', indicating the head of army supplies.
