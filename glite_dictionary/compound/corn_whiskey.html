corn whiskey

1. NOUN

/ kɔrn ˈwɪski / (AmE), / kɔːn ˈwɪski / (BrE)
a type of whiskey distilled from a mash containing at least 80 percent corn, traditionally made in the United States
American whiskey made mostly from corn

Examples

The distillery is famous for its corn whiskey, which uses locally grown maize.
He poured himself a glass of corn whiskey after a long day at work.
Unlike bourbon, corn whiskey is rarely aged in new charred oak barrels.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bourbon - a type of American whiskey made from at least 51% corn
  • Antonym: rye whiskey - whiskey made primarily from rye grain
