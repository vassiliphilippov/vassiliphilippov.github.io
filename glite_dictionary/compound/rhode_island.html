Rhode Island

1. PROPN

/ ˌroʊd ˈaɪ.lənd / (AmE), / ˌrəʊd ˈaɪ.lənd / (BrE)
A state in the northeastern United States, one of the original thirteen colonies, with Providence as its capital.
U.S. state in New England

Examples

She grew up in Rhode Island before moving to New York City.
Rhode Island is known for its beautiful coastline and historic mansions.
The university in Rhode Island attracts students from across the country.

Etymology

Named after the Greek island of Rhodes, possibly by early explorer Giovanni da Verrazzano.
