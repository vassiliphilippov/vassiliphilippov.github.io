field-pea plant

1. NOUN

/ ˈfiːld piː plænt / (AmE), / ˈfiːld piː plɑːnt / (BrE)
A type of pea plant native to the Mediterranean region and North Africa, widely grown for use as forage and for its seeds.
variety of pea plant for forage/seed

Examples

Farmers often plant field-pea plants as a valuable source of animal fodder.
The field-pea plant thrives in regions with mild, Mediterranean climates.
Researchers are studying the nutritional benefits of seeds from the field-pea plant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pea plant - the leguminous plant producing peas
