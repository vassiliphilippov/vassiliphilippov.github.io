magnus hitch

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæɡnəs hɪtʃ / (AmE), / ˈmæɡnəs hɪtʃ / (BrE)
A type of knot similar to a clove hitch but with greater security, used to secure a rope to a pole or another rope.
secure knot similar to a clove hitch

Examples

To secure the line to the post, the sailor tied a magnus hitch.
The magnus hitch holds much better under a heavy load than a clove hitch.
For greater security, use a magnus hitch when fastening your rope to a pole.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rolling hitch - a knot used to attach a rope to a rod, pole, or another rope
  • Synonym: clove hitch - a knot for temporarily securing a rope to an object
