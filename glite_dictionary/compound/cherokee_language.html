Cherokee language

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɛrəki ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈtʃɛrəki ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Iroquoian language spoken by the Cherokee people, primarily in the southeastern United States
the language of the Cherokee people

Examples

She is studying the Cherokee language to connect with her heritage.
The Cherokee language has its own unique syllabary writing system.
Efforts are underway to preserve and teach the Cherokee language to younger generations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Iroquoian - relating to the Iroquoian language family

Etymology

Cherokee from the name of the indigenous people, possible Muscogee origin; 'language' from Latin lingua.
