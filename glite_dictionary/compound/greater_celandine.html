greater celandine

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡreɪtər səˈlænˌdaɪn / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪtə sɪˈlændiːn / (BrE)
a perennial herb with branched woody stems and bright yellow flowers, native to Europe and western Asia
a yellow-flowered perennial herb

Examples

The garden was dotted with patches of greater celandine blooming beneath the old trees.
Traditional herbalists sometimes used greater celandine in remedies to support liver health.
We found a cluster of greater celandine growing wild at the edge of the forest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: celandine - a plant name used for two unrelated species with yellow flowers

Etymology

Celandine from Latin chelidonia, from Greek khelidonion (from khelidōn, 'swallow' bird); 'greater' distinguishes it from lesser celandine.
