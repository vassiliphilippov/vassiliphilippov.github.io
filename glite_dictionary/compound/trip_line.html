trip line

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrɪp laɪn / (AmE), / ˈtrɪp laɪn / (BrE)
a light rope used in logging to release a dog hook from a log at a distance
rope for releasing a dog hook (logging)

Examples

The logger carefully pulled the trip line to release the dog hook from a safe distance.
Always check the trip line for knots before using it in the logging operation.
A broken trip line can make removing the dog hook much more dangerous.
