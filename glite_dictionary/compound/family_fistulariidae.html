family Fistulariidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli fɪstjʊˌlɛriˈaɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli fɪstjʊˌlæriˈiːdiː / (BrE)
A family of elongated marine fishes known as cornetfishes, characterized by their tubular snouts and long bodies.
a family of fishes (cornetfishes)

Examples

The family Fistulariidae consists of only a few species, all commonly known as cornetfishes.
Members of the family Fistulariidae can be found in tropical and subtropical oceans worldwide.
Distinctive for their long bodies and tubular snouts, the family Fistulariidae is fascinating to marine biologists.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cornetfish - a long, slender marine fish with a tubular snout

Etymology

From modern Latin 'Fistulariidae,' based on the genus Fistularia, from Latin 'fistula' (pipe, tube), referring to the fishes' tubular snout.
