relative majority

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛlətɪv məˈdʒɔrəti / (AmE), / ˈrɛlətɪv məˈdʒɒrɪti / (BrE)
(in an election) the largest number of votes received by any candidate or option, though less than half of the total votes cast; a plurality.
largest number of votes, less than half (plurality)

Examples

The candidate won by a relative majority, receiving more votes than any opponent but still less than half.
No party obtained an absolute majority; the largest party held a relative majority.
In some electoral systems, a relative majority is sufficient to claim victory even without surpassing the 50% threshold.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plurality - the number of votes cast for a candidate who receives more than any other but does not receive an absolute majority
  • Antonym: absolute majority - more than half of the total votes cast in an election

Etymology

From Latin 'relativus' (having relation) and Latin 'majoritas' (greater number).
