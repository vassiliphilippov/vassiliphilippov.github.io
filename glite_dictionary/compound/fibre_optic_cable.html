fibre optic cable

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪbɚ ˈɑptɪk ˈkeɪbəl / (AmE), / ˈfaɪbə ˈɒptɪk ˈkeɪbəl / (BrE)
A cable made of optical fibers designed to transmit large amounts of data using pulses of light.
cable using optical fibers to transmit data

Examples

The new data center will rely on a fibre optic cable network for fast internet speeds.
Engineers installed a fibre optic cable under the ocean to connect the two continents.
Many homes today have access to high-speed internet through a fibre optic cable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fiber optic cable - Alternative spelling (American English)

Etymology

From 'fibre' (British spelling of 'fiber'), Latin 'fibra' (fiber, filament), and 'optic', Greek 'optikos' (of sight), referring to light-based transmission.
