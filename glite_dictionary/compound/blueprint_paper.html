blueprint paper

1. NOUN

/ ˈbluːˌprɪnt ˈpeɪpər / (AmE), / ˈbluːˌprɪnt ˈpeɪpə / (BrE)
specialized sensitized paper used to produce blueprints by chemical processes, producing white lines on a blue background
sensitized paper for making blueprints

Examples

The architect unrolled a sheet of blueprint paper to reveal the building plans.
Traditional blueprint paper has largely been replaced by digital methods in modern engineering.
He purchased several rolls of blueprint paper for his advanced drafting class.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blueprint - a photographic print with white lines on a blue background, used especially for plans and technical drawings
