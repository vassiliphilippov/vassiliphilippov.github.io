Douay Version

1. NOUN

/ ˌduː.eɪ ˈvɜr.ʒən / (AmE), / ˌduː.eɪ ˈvɜː.ʒən / (BrE)
an English translation of the Latin Vulgate Bible, produced by Roman Catholic scholars at Douay and Rheims in the late 16th and early 17th centuries
English Catholic translation of the Vulgate Bible

Examples

Many Catholic families in England owned a Douay Version of the Bible.
The Douay Version was used as the standard English Catholic Bible for centuries.
She compared passages from the Douay Version and the King James Version to study their differences.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: King James Version - a major English Protestant translation of the Bible

Etymology

Named after the town of Douay (now Douai) in France, where the translation was produced.
