topgallant mast

1. NOUN

/ tɑpˌɡæl.ənt mæst / (AmE), / tɒpˌɡæl.ənt mɑːst / (BrE)
the mast that is fixed to the top of the topmast, supporting the topgallant sail on a square-rigged vessel
mast above the topmast on a ship

Examples

The lookout climbed the topgallant mast to improve his view of the horizon.
High winds snapped the topgallant mast during the storm, forcing the crew to make urgent repairs.
The old sailing ship was restored, complete with a new topgallant mast crafted from sturdy timber.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: topmast - the second section of a mast above the lower mast in a square-rigged ship
  • Degree Higher: royal mast - a mast above the topgallant mast, supporting the royal sail

Etymology

From 'topgallant' (the section above the topmast) and 'mast'; 'topgallant' ultimately from 'top' + 'gallant', originally referring to sails at the highest part of the mast.
