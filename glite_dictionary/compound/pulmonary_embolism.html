pulmonary embolism

1. NOUN

/ ˈpʌlməˌnɛri ɛmˈboʊlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈpʌlmənəri ɪmˈbəʊlɪzəm / (BrE)
A blockage of the pulmonary artery or one of its branches, usually caused by a blood clot that has traveled from elsewhere in the body.
blockage of pulmonary artery
medical

Examples

The patient suffered a pulmonary embolism after surgery, requiring immediate treatment.
Shortness of breath can be a warning sign of a pulmonary embolism.
Doctors used imaging tests to confirm the diagnosis of pulmonary embolism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deep vein thrombosis - a condition in which a blood clot forms in a deep vein, often leading to pulmonary embolism if the clot travels to the lungs

Etymology

From Latin 'pulmonarius' (of the lungs) and Greek 'embolismos' (insertion, obstruction).
