Sunyavadi texts

1. NOUN

/ ˌsuːnjəˈvɑːdi tɛksts / (AmE), / ˌsuːnjəˈvɑːdi tɛksts / (BrE)
Texts associated with or expounding the Sunyavada philosophy of emptiness, especially in Buddhist or Hindu traditions.
texts on philosophy of emptiness

Examples

She studied the Sunyavadi texts for wisdom.
Many of the Sunyavadi texts challenge the notion of inherent existence.
Scholars often debate the interpretation of key passages in the Sunyavadi texts.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Madhyamaka - a school of Buddhist philosophy central to the doctrine of emptiness

Etymology

From Sanskrit śūnyavāda (doctrine of emptiness) + English texts.
