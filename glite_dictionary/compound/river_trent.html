River Trent

1. PROPN

/ ˈrɪvɚ trɛnt / (AmE), / ˈrɪvə trɛnt / (BrE)
a river in central England that flows northeast to join the Ouse and form the Humber Estuary
major river in central England

Examples

The River Trent has historically been important for trade and transport across the Midlands.
Several towns and cities, including Nottingham and Stoke-on-Trent, are situated along the River Trent.
The confluence of the River Trent and the Ouse marks the beginning of the Humber Estuary.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: River Thames - major river running through southern England, including London
  • Synonym: Ouse - river in northern England that joins the River Trent to form the Humber
