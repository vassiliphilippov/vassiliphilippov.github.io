great-uncle

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡreɪt ˈʌŋkəl / (AmE), / ˌɡreɪt ˈʌŋkl̩ / (BrE)
The brother of one’s grandparent; an uncle of one’s father or mother.
the brother of one's grandparent

Examples

My great-uncle fought in World War II and often shared his stories with us.
At the family reunion, I finally met my grandmother's brother, my great-uncle.
After my great-uncle passed away, he left a collection of letters to my mother.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: great-aunt - the sister of one's grandparent
  • Degree Lower: uncle - the brother of one's parent
