Golgi's cell

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡoʊldʒiz sɛl / (AmE), / ˈɡɒldʒiz sɛl / (BrE)
a neuron in the cerebral cortex characterized by short dendrites and an axon that may be long or short, often found branching in the grey matter
type of cerebral cortex neuron
medical

Examples

Researchers identified several Golgi's cells in the layers of the cerebral cortex.
Damage to Golgi's cells can affect neural processing in the brain.
The unique structure of a Golgi's cell differentiates it from other cortical neurons.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neuron - a nerve cell; the fundamental unit of the nervous system

Etymology

Named after Camillo Golgi, Italian physician and scientist who discovered this type of cell.
