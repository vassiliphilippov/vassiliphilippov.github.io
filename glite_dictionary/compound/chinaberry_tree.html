chinaberry tree

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃaɪ.nəˌbɛr.i tri / (AmE), / ˈtʃaɪ.nəˌbɛr.i triː / (BrE)
A deciduous tree native to northern India and China, with purple blossoms and small inedible yellow fruits, often planted for shade in the southern United States.
Asian tree with purple flowers, shade tree

Examples

The chinaberry tree in their backyard provided welcome shade during the hot summer days.
Clusters of yellow fruit hung from the chinaberry tree throughout autumn.
Many southern towns have streets lined with chinaberry trees planted decades ago.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Melia azedarach - the scientific name for chinaberry tree
