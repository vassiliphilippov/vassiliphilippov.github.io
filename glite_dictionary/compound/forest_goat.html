forest goat

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔrɪst ɡoʊt / (AmE), / ˈfɒrɪst ɡəʊt / (BrE)
a large, cow-like animal with the glossy coat of a horse, the agility of a goat, and long antelope-like horns, typically living in wooded areas
a large agile animal with long horns, found in forests

Examples

The forest goat is known for its ability to move swiftly through dense woodland.
Researchers observed a forest goat grazing alongside deer near the riverbank.
With its long horns and muscular build, the forest goat is well adapted to its habitat.
