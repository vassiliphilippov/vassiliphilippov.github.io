paleocontact hypothesis

1. NOUN

/ ˌpeɪlioʊˈkɑːntækt haɪˈpɑːθəsɪs / (AmE), / ˌpeɪlɪəʊˈkɒntækt haɪˈpɒθəsɪs / (BrE)
The hypothesis that intelligent extraterrestrial beings visited Earth and made contact with humans in antiquity and prehistory, often considered pseudo-scientific.
hypothesis of ancient alien contact

Examples

Some researchers have dedicated years to studying the paleocontact hypothesis and its implications for human history.
Critics argue that the paleocontact hypothesis lacks credible scientific evidence.
Popular television shows have helped bring the paleocontact hypothesis to mainstream audiences.

Synset Examples

Sagan argued that while many legends, artifacts and purported out-of-place artifacts were cited in support of ancient astronaut theories, ‘very few require more than passing mention’ and could be easily explained with more conventional theories.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ancient astronaut theory - The idea that extraterrestrials visited Earth in ancient times.

Etymology

From Greek 'palaio-' (ancient) and 'contact', with 'hypothesis' from Greek 'hypothesis' (base, supposition).
