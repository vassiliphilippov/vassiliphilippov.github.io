Doctor of Laws

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɑktɚ əv lɔz / (AmE), / ˈdɒktə əv lɔːz / (BrE)
an honorary or academic degree in law, typically awarded for outstanding achievements in the field
honorary or academic law degree

Examples

She received an honorary Doctor of Laws degree from Yale University.
The university awarded him a Doctor of Laws for his contributions to legal scholarship.
Many distinguished jurists have been named Doctor of Laws in recognition of their service.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: LL.D. - abbreviation for Doctor of Laws

Etymology

From Latin 'doctor' (teacher) and 'legum' (of laws); plural of 'lex' (law) in Latin.
