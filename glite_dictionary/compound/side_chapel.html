side chapel

1. NOUN

/ saɪd ˈtʃæpəl / (AmE), / saɪd ˈtʃæpəl / (BrE)
a small chapel located off the side aisle of a church, often used for private prayer or special services
small chapel off the side aisle in a church

Examples

Many visitors light candles in the side chapel before the main service begins.
The historic cathedral features a beautifully decorated side chapel dedicated to Saint Mary.
She found a moment of quiet reflection in the side chapel away from the bustling congregation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chapel - a small building or room used for worship
