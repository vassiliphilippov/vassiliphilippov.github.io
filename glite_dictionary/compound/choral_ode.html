choral ode

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔrəl oʊd / (AmE), / ˈkɔːrəl əʊd / (BrE)
an ode sung by the chorus in classical Greek drama, often commenting on or reacting to the main action of the play
ode sung by chorus in Greek drama

Examples

The choral ode provided emotional commentary on the events unfolding in the play.
Scholars have studied the structure and themes of the choral ode in ancient Greek tragedies.
During the performance, the choral ode captured the collective response of the citizens to the hero's fate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strophe - the first section of a choral ode in Greek tragedy
  • Synonym: antistrophe - the second section of a choral ode in Greek tragedy
  • Synonym: epode - the third section of a choral ode in Greek tragedy

Etymology

From 'choral', relating to a chorus, and 'ode', from Greek 'ōdē' (song).
