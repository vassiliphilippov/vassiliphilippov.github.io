heavy torpedo

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛvi tɔrˈpiːdoʊ / (AmE), / ˈhɛvi tɔːˈpiːdəʊ / (BrE)
a large torpedo, typically carrying a warhead of at least 450 kg, used mainly as a standoff weapon by submarines
large submarine-launched torpedo

Examples

The submarine was equipped with heavy torpedoes designed to sink large enemy ships.
Handling a heavy torpedo in the cramped space of a submarine requires special equipment and training.
Modern navies rely on heavy torpedoes for their increased range and devastating warheads.

Synset Examples

Although lightweight torpedoes are fairly easily handled, the transport and handling of heavyweight torpedoes is difficult, especially in the small space of a submarine.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lightweight torpedo - a torpedo that is smaller and lighter than a heavy torpedo
