Virginia snakeroot

1. NOUN

/ vərˈdʒɪn.i ˈsneɪkˌruːt / (AmE), / vəˈdʒɪn.i ˈsneɪkˌruːt / (BrE)
A North American woodland herb (Aristolochia serpentaria) with heart-shaped leaves and small, brownish flowers, formerly used medicinally.
North American woodland herb

Examples

Early settlers believed that Virginia snakeroot had medicinal properties to treat snakebites.
The botanist discovered a cluster of Virginia snakeroot in the woodland understory.
Herbalists once used Virginia snakeroot as an ingredient in traditional remedies.

Etymology

Named for the U.S. state of Virginia and its historical use as a remedy for snakebites.
