maidenhair berry

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪdənˌhɛr ˈbɛri / (AmE), / ˈmeɪd(ə)nhɛː ˈbɛri / (BrE)
A slow-growing, procumbent evergreen shrub of northern North America and Japan, with white flowers and numerous white, fleshy, rough, and hairy seeds.
an evergreen shrub with white flowers and hairy seeds

Examples

The maidenhair berry is often found in shaded forest areas of northern Japan.
Gardeners admire the delicate appearance of the maidenhair berry throughout the year.
Despite their rough seeds, maidenhair berries attract many birds in the summer.
