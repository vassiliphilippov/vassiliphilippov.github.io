parsley haw

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑrsli hɔ / (AmE), / ˈpɑːsli hɔː / (BrE)
A species of hawthorn native to the southern United States, characterized by its pinnately lobed leaves resembling parsley.
a southern U.S. species of hawthorn

Examples

The parsley haw blooms in late spring, brightening the southern woodlands with its white flowers.
Gardeners often plant the parsley haw for its attractive foliage and wildlife-friendly berries.
We spotted a parsley haw near the riverbank, its leaves distinctively cut like parsley.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hawthorn - a thorny shrub or small tree with white, pink, or red flowers and red fruit
