posse comitatus

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑsi ˌkɑməˈteɪtəs / (AmE), / ˌpɒsi ˌkɒmɪˈteɪtəs / (BrE)
A group of people mobilized by the authorities, especially a sheriff, to assist in maintaining law and order or pursuing lawbreakers; a temporary police force.
temporary police force called by authority
legal

Examples

When the sheriff formed a posse comitatus, local citizens volunteered to join the search for the fugitives.
The law allowed him to summon a posse comitatus in times of emergency.
Historically, a posse comitatus could be assembled to enforce court orders or capture criminals.

Etymology

From Medieval Latin posse comitatus (power of the county), from Latin posse (to be able) + comitatus (county).

A law limiting the use of the federal military in domestic law enforcement and civilian affairs.
law restricting military involvement in civilian affairs
legal american

Examples

The posse comitatus limits how the military can act within the United States.
Some legal scholars debate the scope of posse comitatus in national emergencies.
Congress amended posse comitatus to specify exceptions involving certain federal agencies.
