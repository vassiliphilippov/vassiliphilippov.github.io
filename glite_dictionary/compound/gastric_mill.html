gastric mill

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡæstrɪk mɪl / (AmE), / ˈɡæstrɪk mɪl / (BrE)
a thick-walled muscular pouch in the digestive tract of some animals, especially birds and reptiles, used for grinding food
muscular pouch for grinding food in animals

Examples

The gastric mill in birds helps to mechanically break down seeds and grains.
Lobsters use their gastric mill to crush and grind their food before digestion.
During dissection, the biologist pointed out the gastric mill in the crab's stomach.

Etymology

From Latin 'gastricus' (of the stomach) and 'mill' (grinding device).
