celestial horizon

1. NOUN

/ səˈlɛstʃəl həˈraɪzn̩ / (AmE), / sɪˈlɛstɪəl həˈraɪzn / (BrE)
The great circle on the celestial sphere whose plane passes through the observer's sensible horizon and the center of the Earth.
the astronomical horizon circle

Examples

The celestial horizon is crucial when determining a star's altitude in navigational astronomy.
Early astronomers mapped the stars in relation to the celestial horizon to aid in navigation.
When observing the sky, the celestial horizon differs slightly from the visible, or sensible, horizon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horizon - the line where the sky and Earth appear to meet

Etymology

From Latin 'caelestis' (heavenly) + Greek 'horizōn' (bounding circle).
