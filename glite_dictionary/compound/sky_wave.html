sky wave

1. NOUN

/ skaɪ weɪv / (AmE), / skaɪ weɪv / (BrE)
A radio wave that is reflected back to the Earth's surface by the ionosphere or a communications satellite, enabling long-distance transmission beyond the horizon.
radio wave reflected by ionosphere or satellite

Examples

Ham radio operators use the sky wave to communicate over vast distances.
The effectiveness of a sky wave depends on the conditions within the ionosphere.
Weather and solar activity can significantly impact the quality of sky wave transmissions.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ground wave - a radio wave that travels directly along the ground rather than being reflected
