trigeminal neuralgia

1. NOUN

/ traɪˈdʒɛmɪnəl nʊˈrældʒə / (AmE), / traɪˈdʒɛmɪnəl njʊˈraldʒiə / (BrE)
A chronic pain condition characterized by sudden, severe, stabbing pain along the trigeminal nerve in the face.
intense facial nerve pain disorder
medical

Examples

She was diagnosed with trigeminal neuralgia after experiencing severe pain on one side of her face.
Patients with trigeminal neuralgia often describe their discomfort as electric shock-like bursts.
Effective management of trigeminal neuralgia may require medication or surgical intervention.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neuralgia - intense nerve pain disorder

Etymology

From Latin 'trigeminus' (threefold) + Greek 'neuralgia' (pain of the nerves).
