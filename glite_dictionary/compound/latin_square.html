Latin square

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæt̬.ɪn skwɛr / (AmE), / ˈlæt.ɪn skweə / (BrE)
a square array with n rows and n columns filled with n different symbols, arranged so that no symbol repeats in any row or column
square matrix with unique symbols per row/column

Examples

The researcher used a Latin square to design the experiment and eliminate potential biases.
In combinatorics, a Latin square can be used to study various properties of arrangements.
Constructing a Latin square of order five can be a challenging mathematical exercise.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sudoku - number puzzle based on Latin squares

Etymology

From Latin, referencing the historical use of Latin letters for the symbols.
