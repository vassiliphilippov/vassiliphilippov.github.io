Carolina pond fern

1. NOUN

/ kəˈraɪlɪnə pɑnd fɜrn / (AmE), / kəˈrælɪnə pɒnd fɜːn / (BrE)
a small, free-floating aquatic fern native to the eastern United States and tropical America, now naturalized in parts of Europe
floating aquatic fern species

Examples

The Carolina pond fern is often used in aquariums for its attractive floating leaves.
Efforts to control the spread of Carolina pond fern in European waterways have increased recently.
Scientists are studying the Carolina pond fern for its potential role in water purification.
