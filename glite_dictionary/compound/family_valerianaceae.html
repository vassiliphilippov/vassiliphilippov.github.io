family Valerianaceae

1. NOUN

/ fəˈmɪli vælɪˌɛrəˈneɪsiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli ˌvælɪˌɛərəˈneɪsiː / (BrE)
a family of mainly herbaceous plants, typically with a strong, often unpleasant odor, including the genus Valeriana.
herbaceous plant family with pungent odor

Examples

Many traditional medicines use plants found in the family Valerianaceae.
The characteristic smell of some members of the family Valerianaceae makes them easy to identify.
Botanists have reclassified certain species formerly in the family Valerianaceae into different families.

Etymology

From Latin *Valeriana* (type genus) and botanical suffix *-aceae* indicating family.
