liege lord

1. NOUN

/ lidʒ lɔrd / (AmE), / liːdʒ lɔːd / (BrE)
a feudal lord to whom allegiance and service are owed, especially in the context of medieval European society
feudal lord entitled to allegiance
archaic

Examples

The knight pledged loyalty to his liege lord above all others.
In exchange for land, the peasants served their liege lord faithfully throughout their lives.
During the ceremony, each vassal swore fealty to his liege lord.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: vassal - a person in the feudal system who owed allegiance and service to a lord
  • Synonym: overlord - a person of great power or authority, especially a feudal lord over other lords

Etymology

From Old French *lige* (free, sovereign) and *seigneur* (lord); 'liege' traces back to Medieval Latin *ligius*.
