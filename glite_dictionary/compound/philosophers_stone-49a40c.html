philosophers' stone

1. NOUN

/ fəˈlɑsəfərz stoʊn / (AmE), / fəˈlɒsəfəz stəʊn / (BrE)
a hypothetical substance that alchemists believed could transform base metals into gold or grant immortality
mythical alchemical substance

Examples

The quest for the philosophers' stone inspired centuries of alchemical experiments.
Legends claim the philosophers' stone could grant eternal life as well as riches.
Many famous alchemists devoted their lives to finding the philosophers' stone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elixir of life - a mythical substance believed to grant immortality

Etymology

From medieval Latin *philosophi lapis*, meaning 'stone of the philosophers.'
