Zen Buddhism

1. NOUN

/ zɛn ˈbuːdɪzəm / (AmE), / zɛn ˈbʊdɪzəm / (BrE)
A school of Buddhism emphasizing meditation and direct intuitive insight as the path to enlightenment.
school of Buddhism focused on meditation and insight

Examples

Many Westerners are attracted to Zen Buddhism because of its practical approach to meditation.
She spent several years studying Zen Buddhism in Japan.
The principles of Zen Buddhism encourage living in the present moment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Buddhism - a major world religion founded on the teachings of Buddha

Etymology

Zen from Japanese 禅 (Zen), from Chinese 禪 (Chán), ultimately from Sanskrit ध्यान (dhyāna, meditation); Buddhism from Buddha + -ism.

2. NOUN

/ zɛn ˈbʊdɪzəm / (AmE), / zɛn ˈbʊdɪzəm / (BrE)
A school of Mahayana Buddhism emphasizing the attainment of enlightenment through meditation and intuitive insight rather than reliance on faith or ritual, primarily practiced in China and Japan.
school of Mahayana Buddhism based on meditation

Examples

Many Westerners are drawn to Zen Buddhism because of its focus on meditation and simplicity.
He spent several years in Japan studying the principles of Zen Buddhism.
Zen Buddhism teaches that enlightenment can be achieved through direct experience rather than scripture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Buddhism - Religion based on the teachings of the Buddha
  • Degree Higher: Mahayana - Branch of Buddhism emphasizing the potential for all beings to achieve enlightenment

Etymology

From Japanese 'Zen', from Chinese 'Chán', from Sanskrit 'dhyāna' (meditation), and 'Buddhism' from 'Buddha'.
