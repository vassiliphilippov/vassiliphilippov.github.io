cassia-bark tree

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæsiə ˌbɑrk tri / (AmE), / ˈkæsiə ˌbɑːk triː / (BrE)
A Chinese tree with aromatic bark that produces a type of cinnamon considered less desirable than Ceylon cinnamon.
Chinese tree used for cinnamon bark

Examples

The factory imports large quantities of cassia-bark trees for spice production.
Unlike Ceylon cinnamon, the bark of the cassia-bark tree has a stronger, more pungent flavor.
Chinese herbalists sometimes use extracts from the cassia-bark tree in traditional remedies.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: cinnamon - Spice made from the dried inner bark of trees, especially Ceylon cinnamon
  • Synonym: cassia - Spice from the bark of the cassia-bark tree, similar to cinnamon
