bum chum

1. NOUN

/ bʌm tʃʌm / (AmE), / bʌm tʃʌm / (BrE)
an informal or humorous term for a very close friend or companion; often used in British English.
close friend or companion (British, informal)
informal british

Examples

We've been bum chums since our primary school days.
He always goes on holiday with his bum chum.
Everyone thought the two of them were just bum chums who did everything together.

Synset Examples

He was my bum chum since we were kids.
Every weekend, my bum chum and I hang out.
We went to the concert together, just like bum chums.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: close friend - a person with whom one has a close and trusting relationship
  • Antonym: acquaintance - a person one knows slightly, but who is not a close friend
