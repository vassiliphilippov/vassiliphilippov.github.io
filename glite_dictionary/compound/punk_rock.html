punk rock

1. NOUN

/ pʌŋk rɑk / (AmE), / pʌŋk rɒk / (BrE)
A style of rock music characterized by fast tempos, aggressive melodies, and often political or anti-establishment lyrics, emerging in the 1970s.
a genre of aggressive rock music

Examples

Many bands in the 1970s helped define the raw, energetic sound of punk rock.
She has a vast collection of punk rock albums from both American and British groups.
The rebellious spirit of punk rock resonated with disaffected youth around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: punk - a member of a countercultural youth movement or the associated music/style
  • Antonym: progressive rock - a style of rock music with complex compositions and instrumentation
