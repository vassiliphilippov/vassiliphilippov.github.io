Loyalist Volunteer Force

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɔɪ.əl.ɪst vəˈlʌn.tɪr fɔrs / (AmE), / ˈlɔɪ.ə.lɪst vəˈlʌn.tɪə fɔːs / (BrE)
a paramilitary organization formed in 1996 in Northern Ireland, opposed to the peace process and known for violent actions against Catholics and moderate Protestants
paramilitary group in Northern Ireland (est. 1996)

Examples

The Loyalist Volunteer Force claimed responsibility for the attack in Northern Ireland.
Several leaders of the Loyalist Volunteer Force were arrested during police raids.
The peace process was repeatedly threatened by actions of the Loyalist Volunteer Force.
