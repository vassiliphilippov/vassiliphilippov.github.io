out back

1. ADV

/ aʊt bæk / (AmE), / aʊt bæk / (BrE)
At the rear part of a building or house, usually outside.
at the rear of a building

Examples

Let's meet out back after work for a quick chat.
They keep the recycling bins out back near the fence.
If you go out back, you'll see the garden and patio.

Synset Examples

The coach said he'd take him out back for a chat.
He told him to take it outside and out back.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: backyard - area behind a house, often a garden or yard
  • Antonym: front - the area at the front of a building
