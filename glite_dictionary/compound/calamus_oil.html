calamus oil

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæləˌməs ɔɪl / (AmE), / ˈkæləməs ɔɪl / (BrE)
an aromatic oil extracted from the root of the calamus plant, used in perfumes and sometimes considered carcinogenic
aromatic oil from calamus root

Examples

The perfumer added a few drops of calamus oil to enhance the fragrance.
Due to health concerns, some countries restrict the use of calamus oil in food products.
The distinctive aroma of calamus oil makes it a rare but valued ingredient in niche perfumery.

Etymology

From Latin 'calamus' (reed) and 'oil', referring to the source plant and substance.
