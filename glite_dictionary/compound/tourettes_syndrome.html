Tourette's syndrome

1. NOUN

/ tuˈrɛts ˌsɪnˈdroʊm / (AmE), / tʊəˈrɛts ˈsɪndrəʊm / (BrE)
A neurological disorder characterized by repetitive involuntary movements and vocalizations called tics, sometimes including grunts, shouts, or coprolalia.
neurological disorder with involuntary tics
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that Tourette's syndrome can result in frequent involuntary tics and vocalizations.
Her son was recently diagnosed with Tourette's syndrome after months of noticing unusual movements and sounds.
People with Tourette's syndrome may experience symptoms that change over time or fluctuate in severity.

Etymology

Named after French neurologist Georges Gilles de la Tourette (1857–1904).
