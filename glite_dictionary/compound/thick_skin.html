thick skin

1. NOUN

/ θɪk skɪn / (AmE), / θɪk skɪn / (BrE)
skin that is physically very thick, as found in animals like elephants or rhinoceroses
very thick skin (literal)

Examples

The elephant's thick skin protects it from thorns and insect bites.
Rhinoceroses are known for their tough, thick skin that acts like armor.
You can see the animal's thick skin up close at the wildlife sanctuary.
