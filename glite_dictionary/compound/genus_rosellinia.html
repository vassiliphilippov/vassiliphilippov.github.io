genus Rosellinia

1. NOUN

/ ˈroʊsɛˌlɪniə / (AmE), / ˌrɒsɛˈlɪnɪə / (BrE)
A genus of fungi characterized by smooth perithecia and dark, one-celled ascospores.
a genus of fungi

Examples

Scientists discovered a new species within the genus Rosellinia during their rainforest expedition.
The genus Rosellinia includes fungi that can be harmful to certain crops.
Mycologists study the genus Rosellinia to understand plant diseases caused by its species.

Etymology

From Italian botanist Ferdinando Pio Rosellini (1814–1872).
