somatic cell

1. NOUN

/ səˈmæt̬ɪk sɛl / (AmE), / səˈmatɪk sɛl / (BrE)
any cell of a plant or animal that is not involved in reproduction; specifically, a body cell excluding the gametes.
body cell, non-reproductive cell
medical

Examples

The mutation was found in a somatic cell rather than in a gamete.
Cancer develops when a somatic cell begins to grow uncontrollably.
Most tissues in your body are made up of somatic cells instead of reproductive cells.

Synset Examples

somatic cells are produced from preexisting cells

Related Terms

  • Antonym: gamete - a reproductive cell (sperm or egg) that fuses with another during fertilization

Etymology

From Greek sōma meaning 'body' + -ic, referring to cells of the body.
