Central African CFA franc

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛntrəl ˈæfrɪkən ˌsiː ɛf ˈeɪ ˈfræŋk / (AmE), / ˌsɛntrəl ˈæfrɪkən ˌsiː ɛf ˈɑː ˈfræŋk / (BrE)
the official currency used by Cameroon, Central African Republic, Chad, Republic of the Congo, Equatorial Guinea, and Gabon
currency for six Central African countries

Examples

The price of imported goods is often quoted in Central African CFA francs in Cameroon.
The Central African CFA franc is guaranteed by the French treasury, ensuring its stable exchange rate.
Tourists can easily exchange euros for Central African CFA francs upon arrival in Gabon.

Synset Examples

In several west African states, the West African CFA franc, which is of equal value to the Central African CFA franc, is in circulation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: West African CFA franc - the currency of eight West African states, equal in value to Central African CFA franc

Etymology

From French 'franc' (the name of the currency), where CFA stands for 'Communauté Financière Africaine' (African Financial Community).
