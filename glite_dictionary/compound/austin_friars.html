Austin Friars

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔstɪn ˈfraɪərz / (AmE), / ˈɔːstɪn ˈfraɪəz / (BrE)
Members of a monastic order following the rule of St. Augustine, also known as Augustinian friars.
member of the Augustinian monastic order

Examples

The Austin Friars established several important houses across medieval Europe.
During the Reformation, the Austin Friars were forced to disband in England.
Some old churches in London were once run by Austin Friars.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Augustinian - Relating to the order following the rule of St. Augustine

Etymology

From Austin, a contracted form of Augustine (St. Augustine), and friar, from Old French frere, from Latin frater (brother).
