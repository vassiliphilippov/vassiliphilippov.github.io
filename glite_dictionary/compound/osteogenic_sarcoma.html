osteogenic sarcoma

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːstiəˈdʒɛnɪk sɑːrˈkoʊmə / (AmE), / ˌɒstiəˈdʒɛnɪk sɑːˈkəʊmə / (BrE)
a malignant tumor of bone, most common in children and young adults, often affecting the femur
malignant bone tumor, common in youth
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with osteogenic sarcoma after complaining of persistent knee pain.
Early detection of osteogenic sarcoma improves the chances of successful treatment.
Researchers are studying new therapies to treat osteogenic sarcoma in children.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: osteosarcoma - another term for osteogenic sarcoma

Etymology

From Greek osteon (bone) + -genic (producing) + sarcoma (from Greek sarkōma, fleshy tumor).
