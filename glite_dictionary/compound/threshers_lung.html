thresher's lung

1. NOUN

/ ˈθrɛʃərz lʌŋ / (AmE), / ˈθrɛʃəz lʌŋ / (BrE)
A respiratory disease (alveolitis) caused by an allergic reaction to fungal spores in dust inhaled from moldy hay, often affecting farm workers.
lung disease from inhaling moldy hay dust
medical

Examples

After years of working in the fields, he developed thresher's lung from inhaling spores in moldy hay.
The doctor diagnosed her with thresher's lung after she experienced persistent coughing and difficulty breathing.
Wearing a mask during threshing reduced the risk of developing thresher's lung among agricultural workers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: farmer's lung - lung disease caused by inhaling dust from moldy hay or crops
