Chancellor of the Exchequer

1. NOUN

/ ˌtʃæn.sə.lɚ əv ði ɪkˈtʃɛk.ɚ / (AmE), / ˌtʃɑːn.səl.ər əv ði ɪkˈtʃɛk.ər / (BrE)
the British government minister who is responsible for the country's economic and financial matters, especially the Treasury
British finance minister
british

Examples

The Chancellor of the Exchequer announced a new budget to address the economic downturn.
After years in Parliament, she was appointed Chancellor of the Exchequer by the Prime Minister.
Debate over tax policy was intense following remarks from the current Chancellor of the Exchequer.

Etymology

Exchequer from Old French 'eschequier' (chessboard), referring to the checkered cloth used in medieval bookkeeping.
