Albright's disease

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔlˌbraɪts dɪˌziz / (AmE), / ˈɔːlbraɪts dɪˌziːz / (BrE)
A rare disorder characterized by fibrous dysplasia affecting multiple bones, causing bone deformities and fractures.
rare bone disorder affecting multiple bones
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with Albright's disease after multiple bone deformities were found.
Researchers are studying new treatments for Albright's disease to improve bone strength.
People with Albright's disease often experience recurrent fractures during childhood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fibrous dysplasia - abnormal bone growth where normal bone is replaced with fibrous tissue

Etymology

Named after American physician Fuller Albright (1900–1969), who first described the condition.
