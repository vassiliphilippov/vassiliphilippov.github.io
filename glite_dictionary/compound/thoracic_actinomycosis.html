thoracic actinomycosis

1. NOUN

/ θəˈræsɪk ˌæktɪnoʊmaɪˈkoʊsɪs / (AmE), / θəˈræsɪk ˌæktɪnəʊmaɪˈkəʊsɪs / (BrE)
a serious infectious disease caused by Actinomyces bacteria, affecting the chest and lungs, often resulting in abscesses and tissue damage
actinomycosis infection of the chest
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with thoracic actinomycosis after a series of chest X-rays revealed lung abscesses.
Treatment of thoracic actinomycosis often requires long-term antibiotics and sometimes surgical intervention.
Delayed diagnosis of thoracic actinomycosis can lead to severe complications, including chest wall involvement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: actinomycosis - a chronic bacterial infection causing abscesses

Etymology

From 'thoracic' (relating to the chest, from Greek 'thōraks') and 'actinomycosis' (from Greek 'aktis' meaning 'ray' and 'mykēs' meaning 'fungus').
