supervisory routine

1. NOUN

/ suːpərˈvaɪzəri ruːˈtiːn / (AmE), / suːpəˈvaɪzəri ruːˈtiːn / (BrE)
a routine in a computer program that manages or coordinates the operation of other subroutines
routine that manages subroutines in computing

Examples

The supervisory routine handles all task scheduling in the operating system.
Whenever a subroutine completes, the supervisory routine determines which one runs next.
Because of a flaw in the supervisory routine, some processes were not coordinated properly.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: subroutine - a set of instructions in a program that performs a specific task, used within another routine
