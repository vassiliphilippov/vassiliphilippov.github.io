boot camp

1. NOUN

/ ˈbuːt kæmp / (AmE), / ˈbuːt kæmp / (BrE)
a camp where new members of the armed forces receive intensive basic training
military training camp for recruits

Examples

After graduating from high school, he enlisted in the Marines and went straight to boot camp.
The recruits found boot camp both physically and mentally challenging.
She wrote letters home every week describing her experiences at boot camp.

Etymology

American English; originally U.S. military slang from World War I, referring to the boots (shoes) issued to new recruits.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbuːt kæmp / (AmE), / ˈbuːt kæmp / (BrE)
a short, intensive training program designed to improve fitness, skills, or discipline, often in a military or fitness setting
intensive training program (often military or fitness)

Examples

After college graduation, he attended a coding boot camp to jumpstart his career in tech.
The boot camp lasted six weeks and included rigorous daily workouts and strict discipline.
Many people join a fitness boot camp to quickly improve their stamina and lose weight.

Synset Examples

She signed up for a boot camp to get fit.
The military boot camp was physically demanding.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: training camp - place for organized instruction or practice

Etymology

Originally U.S. military slang, referring to training camps for new recruits (with 'boot' meaning a new recruit).
