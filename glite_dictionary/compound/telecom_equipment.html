telecom equipment

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛləˌkɑm ɪˈkwɪpmənt / (AmE), / ˈtɛlɪkɒm ɪˈkwɪpmənt / (BrE)
the hardware and devices used for transmitting or receiving communications over a distance, such as telephones, switches, and routers
devices for telecommunications

Examples

The company invested heavily in telecom equipment to improve its communication infrastructure.
Modern telecom equipment includes fiber-optic cables, wireless transmitters, and data switches.
Without reliable telecom equipment, global businesses would struggle to stay connected with their clients.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: telecommunications - communication over a distance by cable, telegraph, telephone, or broadcasting
  • Degree Lower: network equipment - hardware used to connect computers and networks

Etymology

Shortened from 'telecommunication equipment'; 'telecommunication' from Greek 'tēle' (far) + Latin 'communicare' (to share).
