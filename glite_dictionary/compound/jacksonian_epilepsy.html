Jacksonian epilepsy

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒækˈsoʊniən ɪˈplɛp.si / (AmE), / ˌdʒækˈsəʊniən ɪˈlep.si / (BrE)
A type of focal epilepsy characterized by seizures that typically progress from the distal to the proximal muscles of a limb on the same side of the body.
type of focal epilepsy with sequential limb involvement
medical

Examples

The neurologist diagnosed the patient with Jacksonian epilepsy after observing the progression of muscle spasms.
A classic feature of Jacksonian epilepsy is the spread of convulsions from the fingers to the upper arm.
Treatment for Jacksonian epilepsy often involves medication to control the localized seizures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: focal epilepsy - epilepsy localized to a specific brain region
  • Antonym: generalized epilepsy - epilepsy involving the entire brain

Etymology

Named after English neurologist John Hughlings Jackson (1835–1911), who first described this type of seizure.
