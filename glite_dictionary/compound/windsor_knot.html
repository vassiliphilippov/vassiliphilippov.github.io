Windsor knot

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪnzər nɑt / (AmE), / ˈwɪnzə nɒt / (BrE)
a wide, symmetrical triangular knot for tying a necktie, often used for formal occasions
a way of tying a necktie

Examples

He tied his tie in a perfect Windsor knot for the interview.
The Windsor knot adds a formal look to any suit and shirt combination.
Many professionals prefer the Windsor knot because of its wide, balanced appearance.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: four-in-hand knot - a simple and narrow knot for tying a necktie

Etymology

Named after the Duke of Windsor (Edward VIII), who popularized the style.
