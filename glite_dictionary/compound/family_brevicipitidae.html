family Brevicipitidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli brɛvɪˌsɪpɪˈtaɪdi / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli brɛvɪˌsɪpɪˈtiːdiː / (BrE)
A family of narrow-mouthed toads and sheep frogs, some of which burrow and some are arboreal, distributed worldwide.
a family of narrow-mouthed toads and sheep frogs

Examples

The family Brevicipitidae encompasses both burrowing and arboreal frog species found in tropical regions.
Scientists recently discovered a new species within the family Brevicipitidae in Madagascar.
Some members of the family Brevicipitidae are notable for their distinctive, narrow mouths and unique vocalizations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Brevicipitidae - Scientific family name for narrow-mouthed frogs

Etymology

From New Latin Brevicipitidae, based on the genus Breviceps (from Latin brevis, 'short' + caput, 'head').
