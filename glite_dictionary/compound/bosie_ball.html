bosie ball

1. NOUN

/ ˈboʊzi bɔl / (AmE), / ˈbəʊzi bɔːl / (BrE)
a cricket ball bowled to appear as if it will break one way, but which actually turns in the opposite direction
deceptive cricket delivery

Examples

The bowler surprised the batsman with a bosie ball that spun sharply the other way.
Some cricket fans still call the googly a bosie ball in honor of its inventor.
It's difficult for new players to spot a bosie ball when it's delivered well.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: googly - a deceptive spinning delivery in cricket, bowled with the same intent as a bosie ball

Etymology

From 'Bosie,' nickname of Bernard Bosanquet, the English cricketer who invented the delivery, plus 'ball.'
