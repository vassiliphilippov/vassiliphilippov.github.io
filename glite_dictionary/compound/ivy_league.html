Ivy League

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪvi liːɡ / (AmE), / ˈaɪvi liːɡ / (BrE)
a group of eight prestigious universities in the northeastern United States known for their academic excellence and social status
group of top U.S. universities

Examples

Getting accepted into an Ivy League university is highly competitive.
Many leaders in politics and business are Ivy League graduates.
She dreams of studying at an Ivy League college someday.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ivy League school - a university or college that is a member of the Ivy League

Etymology

Originates from the names of the group’s member schools and their association as a sports league, first referred to collectively in the 1930s.
