Lake Geneva

1. PROPN

/ leɪk dʒəˈniːvə / (AmE), / leɪk dʒɪˈniːvə / (BrE)
A large lake situated between southwestern Switzerland and France, crossed from east to west by the Rhone River.
lake between Switzerland and France

Examples

The Rhône River flows into and out of Lake Geneva, giving it a unique landscape.
Many tourists visit Lake Geneva each year to enjoy its scenic beauty and water activities.
The towns surrounding Lake Geneva are known for their picturesque views and charming atmosphere.

Etymology

Named after the nearby Swiss city of Geneva.
