aggregate demand

1. NOUN

/ ˈæɡrɪˌɡeɪt dɪˈmænd / (AmE), / ˈaɡrɪɡət dɪˈmɑːnd / (BrE)
the total demand for final goods and services in an economy at a particular time and price level, especially as a key concept in macroeconomics
total demand for goods and services in an economy

Examples

During a recession, governments may increase spending to boost aggregate demand.
Falling consumer confidence can result in lower aggregate demand across the country.
Economists use models to predict changes in aggregate demand and their impact on employment.

Synset Examples

The decline in Japan has been sharper than in Europe or North America, mostly because global demand for the country's products, such as automobiles and electronics, has fallen.
Schools of economics agree that during a time of economic downturn, government should cut taxes to increase aggregate demand.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: aggregate supply - the total supply of goods and services produced in an economy at a given price level and time

Etymology

From 'aggregate', from Latin aggregare (to collect), and 'demand', from Latin demandare (to entrust, to demand).
