apple canker

1. NOUN

/ ˈæp.əl ˈkæŋ.kər / (AmE), / ˈap.əl ˈkæŋ.kə / (BrE)
a disease of apple trees characterized by sunken, dead areas on the bark, often caused by fungi or bacteria
disease of apple trees

Examples

The orchard suffered severe losses due to apple canker spreading among the trees.
Pruning infected branches can help control apple canker in apple orchards.
Experts recommend regular inspections to detect early signs of apple canker.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: canker - a plant disease causing lesions or dead areas on bark or stems
