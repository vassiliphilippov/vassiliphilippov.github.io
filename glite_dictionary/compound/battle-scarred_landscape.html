battle-scarred landscape

1. NOUN

/ ˈbætl skɑrd ˈlændˌskeɪp / (AmE), / ˈbætl skɑːd ˈlændskeɪp / (BrE)
an area of land visibly marked or damaged as a result of war or conflict
land damaged by war

Examples

The battle-scarred landscape stretched for miles, with craters and broken trees as far as the eye could see.
Historians visited the battle-scarred landscape to study the effects of the decades-long conflict.
After years of fighting, villages were restored but the battle-scarred landscape still bore the wounds of war.

Synset Examples

The battle-scarred landscape was a reminder of the conflict.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: war zone - an area where fighting is taking place
