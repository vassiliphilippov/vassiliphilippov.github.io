safety arch

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪfti ɑrtʃ / (AmE), / ˈseɪfti ɑːtʃ / (BrE)
an undecorated arch built within a structure to provide additional strength or support
supportive, undecorated arch in construction

Examples

The engineer recommended adding a safety arch to stabilize the old stone bridge.
A safety arch can prevent the collapse of large interior spaces under heavy loads.
Modern renovations sometimes expose the original safety arch that supported the roof.
