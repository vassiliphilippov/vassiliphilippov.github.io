organic structure

1. NOUN

/ ɔrˈɡænɪk ˈstrʌktʃɚ / (AmE), / ɔːˈɡanɪk ˈstrʌktʃə / (BrE)
the complete physical body of an organism, such as an animal, plant, or human being
the whole body of an organism

Examples

The biologist studied the organic structure of the rare plant to understand its unique adaptations.
In severe trauma, damage to the organic structure can threaten the survival of the entire organism.
Technological advances now allow us to observe the organic structure of microscopic organisms in detail.

Synset Examples

he felt as if his whole body were on fire

Etymology

From organic, relating to living matter (Greek organikos), and structure, from Latin structura (a fitting together, building).
