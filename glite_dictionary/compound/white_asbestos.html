white asbestos

1. NOUN

/ waɪt əˈsbɛstoʊs / (AmE), / waɪt æsˈbɛstɒs / (BrE)
a fibrous mineral, usually grey or green, widely used commercially as a source of asbestos
a common type of commercial asbestos mineral

Examples

Many older buildings contain white asbestos in their insulation materials.
Although less dangerous than other forms, white asbestos can still pose serious health risks.
Regulations now restrict the use of white asbestos in most construction projects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chrysotile - the mineralogical name for white asbestos
  • Antonym: blue asbestos - a different, hazardous type of asbestos mineral (crocidolite)
