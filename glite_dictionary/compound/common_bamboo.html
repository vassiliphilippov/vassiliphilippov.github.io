common bamboo

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmən bæmˈbu / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən bæmˈbuː / (BrE)
A tall, fast-growing species of bamboo with thin-walled culms that are striped green and yellow, widely cultivated around the world.
widely cultivated bamboo species

Examples

The gardener planted several common bamboo shoots along the edge of the pond.
Common bamboo grows rapidly and provides excellent privacy screens for gardens and parks.
Many cultures use common bamboo for building materials and crafts due to its strength and flexibility.
