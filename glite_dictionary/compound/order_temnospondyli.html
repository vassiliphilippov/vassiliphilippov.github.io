order Temnospondyli

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdər tɛmnəˈspɒndɪlaɪ / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə tɛmˌnɒspɒnˈdɪlaɪ / (BrE)
a group of large, extinct amphibians that lived during the Carboniferous and Permian periods, characterized by vertebrae with some unfused elements
extinct order of prehistoric amphibians

Examples

Scientists discovered fossils belonging to the order Temnospondyli in the Permian rock formations.
Many members of the order Temnospondyli were aquatic or semi-aquatic predators.
The vertebral structure of the order Temnospondyli sets them apart from other ancient amphibians.

Etymology

From Greek 'temno' (to cut) + 'spondylos' (vertebra), referring to the structure of their vertebrae.
