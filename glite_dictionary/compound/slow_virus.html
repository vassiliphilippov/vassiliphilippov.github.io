slow virus

1. NOUN

/ sloʊ ˈvaɪrəs / (AmE), / sləʊ ˈvaɪərəs / (BrE)
a virus that remains inactive in the body for an extended period before symptoms develop
virus dormant before symptoms
medical

Examples

Some neurological diseases in sheep are caused by a slow virus that incubates for years.
The research focused on how a slow virus can evade the immune system for such a long period.
Doctors suspect a slow virus may be responsible for certain degenerative conditions.

Synset Examples

kuru is caused by a slow virus
