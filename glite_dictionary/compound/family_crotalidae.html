family Crotalidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli kroʊˈtæləˌdi / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli krəʊˈtælɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of venomous snakes, commonly known as pit vipers, characterized by heat-sensing pits between the eyes and nostrils.
family of pit vipers (venomous snakes)

Examples

Rattlesnakes and copperheads are both members of the family Crotalidae.
The family Crotalidae includes species with heat-sensing pits for hunting prey.
Scientists studied several venomous species within the family Crotalidae to understand their evolution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pit viper - a common name for snakes in the family Crotalidae

Etymology

From Neo-Latin Crotalidae, after the genus name Crotalus (rattlesnake), ultimately from Greek krotalon (a rattle).
