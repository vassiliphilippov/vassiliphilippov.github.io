next of kin

1. NOUN

/ nɛkst əv kɪn / (AmE), / nɛkst əv kɪn / (BrE)
the person or persons most closely related to someone, especially as their legal representative if they die or are incapacitated
person's closest living relative(s)

Examples

The hospital needed to contact his next of kin after the accident.
She listed her mother as her next of kin on the form.
In the event of his death, his sister would be considered his next of kin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: relative - a person connected by blood or marriage
  • Synonym: family - a group of people related by blood or marriage

Etymology

From the phrase meaning 'nearest of family', combining 'next' and 'kin' (family, relatives).
