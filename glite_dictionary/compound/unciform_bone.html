unciform bone

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌn.səˌfɔrm ˌboʊn / (AmE), / ˈʌnsɪˌfɔːm bəʊn / (BrE)
a carpal bone in the wrist aligned with the fourth and fifth fingers, also known as the hamate bone
carpal bone in the wrist (hamate)
medical

Examples

A fracture of the unciform bone is rare but can occur in athletes who use racquets.
The unciform bone is located on the ulnar side of the wrist, near the little finger.
During the surgery, the doctor carefully examined the unciform bone for any signs of damage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hamate bone - wrist bone with a hook-shaped projection (same bone as unciform)

Etymology

From Latin 'unciformis' (hook-shaped), referring to the bone's hook-like projection.
