soft-shelled crab

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɑft ˌʃɛld kræb / (AmE), / ˈsɒft ˌʃɛld krab / (BrE)
An edible crab that has recently molted and not yet formed its new hard shell, commonly consumed whole in various dishes.
edible crab recently molted, with a soft shell

Examples

The restaurant's signature dish features a delicious soft-shelled crab fried in a light batter.
She ordered a soft-shelled crab sandwich for lunch, savoring the delicate flavor of the whole crab.
Soft-shelled crabs are a delicacy popular in coastal regions during the spring molting season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blue crab - a species of crab often prepared as soft-shelled crab

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɔft ʃɛld kræb / (AmE), / ˈsɒft ʃɛld kræb / (BrE)
a crab that has recently shed its old shell and has a new shell that is still soft and flexible, often considered a delicacy in cooking
crab with a soft, newly molted shell

Examples

The restaurant is famous for its fried soft-shelled crab served with spicy sauce.
During its molting season, the soft-shelled crab is especially sought after by seafood lovers.
We ordered a fresh soft-shelled crab sandwich for lunch at the beach cafe.

Related Terms
