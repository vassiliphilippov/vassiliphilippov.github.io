short subject

1. NOUN

/ ʃɔrt ˈsʌbdʒɪkt / (AmE), / ʃɔːt ˈsʌbdʒɪkt / (BrE)
a short film, typically screening before the main feature in a cinema.
brief film shown before a feature

Examples

The theater played a short subject before the main movie began.
Classic cartoons were often produced as short subjects for cinemas.
Viewers enjoyed a comedy short subject prior to the feature presentation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: short film - a motion picture that is not long enough to be considered a feature film
