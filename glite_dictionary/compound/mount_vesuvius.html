Mount Vesuvius

1. PROPN

/ maʊnt vəˈsuːviəs / (AmE), / maʊnt vɪˈsuːvɪəs / (BrE)
a volcano in southwestern Italy, famous for its eruption in AD 79 that destroyed Pompeii and Herculaneum
volcano in southwestern Italy, famous for AD 79 eruption

Examples

Many tourists visit Mount Vesuvius to learn about the ancient eruption that destroyed Pompeii.
The fertile soils around Mount Vesuvius are ideal for growing grapes and other crops.
Scientists closely monitor Mount Vesuvius because it remains an active threat to the surrounding region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: volcano - an opening in Earth's crust through which lava erupts

Etymology

From Latin Vesuvius, of uncertain origin, possibly pre-Latin.
