flowering quince

1. NOUN

/ ˈflaʊɚɪŋ kwɪns / (AmE), / ˈflaʊərɪŋ kwɪns / (BrE)
An Asiatic ornamental shrub (genus Chaenomeles) with spiny branches and pink or red blossoms, often grown for its decorative flowers.
ornamental shrub with spiny branches and colorful flowers

Examples

The flowering quince brightens up the garden each spring with its vivid red blossoms.
Gardeners often use flowering quince as a hedge due to its dense, spiny branches.
A vase filled with cut branches of flowering quince adds a touch of color indoors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quince - a hard, acid, pear-shaped fruit and the tree it grows on (Cydonia oblonga)
