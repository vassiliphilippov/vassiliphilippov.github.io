wont to

1. ADJ

/ wɑnt tu, wɔnt tu / (AmE), / wɒnt tə / (BrE)
in the habit of doing something; accustomed to
in the habit of doing; accustomed

Examples

As she was wont to do, she took a walk each morning before breakfast.
He is wont to arrive late to meetings, much to the frustration of his colleagues.
The old professor was wont to share stories from his travels at the start of every class.

Synset Examples

… was wont to complain that this is a cold world

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accustomed to - habituated or familiar with something
  • Antonym: unaccustomed to - not used to or unfamiliar with something

Etymology

From Old English 'gewunod', past participle of 'gewunian', meaning 'to be accustomed'.

2. ADJ

/ wɑnt tu, wənt tu / (AmE), / wɒnt tuː, wənt tuː / (BrE)
likely or in the habit of doing something; accustomed or inclined to do something, especially as a repeated or characteristic activity.
in the habit of doing; inclined to do
archaic

Examples

He is wont to rise at dawn and take a brisk walk before breakfast.
She was wont to lose herself in thought during long, quiet evenings by the fire.
The manager is wont to praise employees for even the smallest achievements.

Synset Examples

She was wont to read before bed each night.
He was wont to take long walks in the evening.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accustomed to - in the habit of; familiar with something through repeated experience
  • Synonym: apt to - having a tendency to do something
  • Antonym: unaccustomed to - not familiar with something; not in the habit of doing something

Etymology

From Middle English 'wonte', past participle of 'wonen' (to dwell, be used to), of Germanic origin.
