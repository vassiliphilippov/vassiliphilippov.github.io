Netherlands Guiana

1. PROPN

/ ˈnɛðərləndz ɡiˈaːnə / (AmE), / ˈnɛðələndz ɡɪˈɑːnə / (BrE)
the former name for Suriname, a country in northeastern South America that gained independence from the Netherlands in 1975
former name of Suriname, South America

Examples

The region once known as Netherlands Guiana became Suriname after gaining independence in 1975.
Many historical maps still refer to Suriname as Netherlands Guiana.
Dutch influence is still evident in the culture of what was formerly Netherlands Guiana.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Suriname - a country in northeastern South America, formerly known as Netherlands Guiana

Etymology

From the Netherlands (the former colonial power) and Guiana (a region in northeastern South America).
