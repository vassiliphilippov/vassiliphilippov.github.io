Moorish arch

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʊrɪʃ ɑrtʃ / (AmE), / ˈmʊərɪʃ ɑːtʃ / (BrE)
an architectural arch that widens at the base and curves to form a rounded top, characteristic of Moorish or Islamic architecture
arch with widened base, rounded top

Examples

The palace featured a beautiful Moorish arch at its entrance, showcasing intricate tile work.
Architects often incorporate a Moorish arch to evoke the grandeur of Islamic design.
The distinctive shape of the Moorish arch sets it apart from traditional Roman arches.

Etymology

From 'Moorish', relating to the Moors of North Africa and Spain, referencing the style of Islamic architecture.
