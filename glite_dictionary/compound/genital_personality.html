genital personality

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɛnɪtəl pɚˌsɒnəˈæləti / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛnɪtəl pəˌsɒnəˈalɪti / (BrE)
(psychoanalysis) a mature personality that is not dominated by infantile pleasure drives, typically associated with healthy adult development in Freudian theory.
mature personality in psychoanalytic theory

Examples

According to Freudian theory, a genital personality represents the healthy outcome of psychosexual development.
She believed that achieving a genital personality allowed individuals to form mature, balanced relationships.
Unlike the earlier stages, the genital personality is characterized by a focus on love and productivity.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: oral personality - a personality type dominated by the oral stage in psychoanalytic theory
  • Degree Lower: anal personality - a personality type associated with the anal stage in psychoanalytic theory

Etymology

From 'genital' (relating to sexual organs or reproduction) and 'personality,' popularized in psychoanalysis by Sigmund Freud.
