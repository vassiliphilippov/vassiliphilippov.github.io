at the same time

1. ADV

/ ət ðə seɪm taɪm / (AmE), / ət ðə seɪm taɪm / (BrE)
during the same period; simultaneously or concurrently.
simultaneously; concurrently

Examples

She managed to work full-time and study at the same time.
He tried to answer emails and take phone calls at the same time.
It’s hard to watch two movies at the same time.

Synset Examples

concurrently with the conference an exhibition of things associated with Rutherford was held
going to school and holding a job at the same time

Related Terms

  • Synonym: simultaneously - occurring at the same time
  • Synonym: concurrently - happening at the same time

2. ADV

/ æt ðə seɪm taɪm / (AmE), / ət ðə seɪm taɪm / (BrE)
at the exact moment; simultaneously.
simultaneously; at once

Examples

Both engines failed at the same time, causing a serious emergency.
She tried to talk and eat at the same time, but it proved difficult.
All the lights in the building went out at the same time.

Synset Examples

they spoke simultaneously

Related Terms

  • Synonym: simultaneously - at the same instant as something else

3. ADV

/ ət ðə seɪm taɪm / (AmE), / ət ðə seɪm taɪm / (BrE)
used to indicate a contrast or reservation; nevertheless; despite what has just been said.
nevertheless; despite this

Examples

She wanted to go out, but at the same time, she knew she needed to rest.
It's expensive; at the same time, it's the best option available.
I understand your position, at the same time I hope you'll consider mine.

Synset Examples

although I'm a little afraid, however I'd like to try it

he was a stern yet fair master
granted that it is dangerous, all the same I still want to go

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nevertheless - in spite of that; notwithstanding
  • Synonym: nonetheless - in spite of that; nevertheless
  • Synonym: however - used to introduce a statement that contrasts with or seems to contradict something that has been said previously
