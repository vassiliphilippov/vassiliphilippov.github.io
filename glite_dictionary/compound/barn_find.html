barn find

1. NOUN

/ bɑrn faɪnd / (AmE), / bɑːn faɪnd / (BrE)
a valuable collectible, especially a classic vehicle, that is unexpectedly discovered in a barn or similar storage, often in original condition
unexpectedly discovered valuable collectible, typically a vehicle

Examples

His grandfather's garage contained a barn find—an untouched 1950s convertible.
Collectors often dream of stumbling upon a barn find in rural areas.
The auction featured several rare barn finds that attracted a lot of attention.

Synset Examples

He was thrilled to discover a barn find from the 1960s.
She shared her latest barn find with the car club.
That classic car was a real barn find.
