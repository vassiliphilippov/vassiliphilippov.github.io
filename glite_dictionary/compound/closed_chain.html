closed chain

1. NOUN

/ kloʊzd tʃeɪn / (AmE), / kləʊzd tʃeɪn / (BrE)
(Chemistry) a sequence of atoms in a molecule connected so that they form a closed loop or ring, rather than an open chain.
loop of atoms in a molecule

Examples

Benzene is a classic example of a compound with a closed chain structure.
Many sugars can form both open and closed chain configurations in solution.
The presence of a closed chain in a molecule often alters its chemical reactivity.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: open chain - a molecular chain that does not form a closed loop
