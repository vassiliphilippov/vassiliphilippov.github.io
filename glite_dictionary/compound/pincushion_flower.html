pincushion flower

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪnˌkʊʃən ˌflaʊər / (AmE), / ˈpɪnˌkʊʃ.ən ˌflaʊ.ə / (BrE)
an annual plant of the teasel family, native to the Old World, with fragrant purple to deep crimson flower heads; commonly grown as an ornamental and naturalized in the United States.
ornamental annual with purple or crimson flowers

Examples

The pincushion flower adds a pop of color to garden borders with its bright blooms.
She planted several pincushion flowers to attract butterflies to her backyard.
The delicate scent of the pincushion flower filled the air in early summer.

Etymology

Named for the shape of the flower, which resembles a pincushion.
