canebrake rattlesnake

1. NOUN

/ ˈkeɪnˌbreɪk ˈrætəlˌsneɪk / (AmE), / ˈkeɪnbrɛɪk ˈrætəlsneɪk / (BrE)
a large venomous rattlesnake (Crotalus horridus) found in the southeastern United States, typically inhabiting wooded areas and canebrakes
a venomous rattlesnake of the southeastern US

Examples

The canebrake rattlesnake is commonly found in wooded river bottoms in the southeastern United States.
Researchers are studying the habitat preferences of the canebrake rattlesnake in Georgia forests.
It's important to watch your step when hiking in areas known to be home to the canebrake rattlesnake.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: timber rattlesnake - another common name for the same species (Crotalus horridus)

Etymology

Named for its habitat among canebrakes (dense thickets of cane grass) and its rattling tail; 'canebrake' from 'cane' and 'brake' (a thicket).
