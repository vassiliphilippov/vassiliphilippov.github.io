oyster-fish

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔɪstər fɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈɔɪstə fɪʃ / (BrE)
A type of toadfish found in coastal waters, known for its stout body and often inhabiting oyster beds.
a type of toadfish

Examples

The oyster-fish often hides among rocks and shells in shallow waters.
Fishermen sometimes catch oyster-fish by accident while looking for oysters.
The oyster-fish is recognized by its broad head and strong jaws.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: toadfish - a family of bottom-dwelling fishes known for their croaking sounds
