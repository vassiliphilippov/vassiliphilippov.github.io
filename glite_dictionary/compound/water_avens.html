water avens

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔtɚ ˌævənz / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə ˌævənz / (BrE)
a perennial plant (Geum rivale) of the north temperate zone, with pinnate leaves and nodding flowers that have a brown-purple calyx and orange-pink petals, typically found in moist habitats
perennial plant with nodding flowers

Examples

In the wet meadows, water avens bloom in late spring with their nodding, delicate flowers.
The gardener recommended planting water avens near the pond for a splash of color.
I identified the orange-pink blossom as water avens during our nature walk.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: avens - a plant of the Geum genus, with usually yellow, white, or reddish flowers
