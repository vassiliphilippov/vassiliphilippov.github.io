Bradley's spleenwort

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrædliːz ˈspliːnwɝt / (AmE), / ˈbrædliːz ˈspliːnwɜːt / (BrE)
a species of spleenwort fern (Asplenium bradleyi) native to the eastern and southern United States, typically found in rocky habitats
a fern native to eastern and southern US

Examples

The rocky cliffs were covered in Bradley's spleenwort, thriving in the limestone crevices.
Botanists were excited to discover a patch of Bradley's spleenwort during their field survey in Alabama.
Because of habitat loss, Bradley's spleenwort is considered rare in several states.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spleenwort - any fern of the genus Asplenium

Etymology

Named after Frank Howe Bradley (1838–1879), an American geologist, and from 'spleenwort', a traditional name for ferns in the genus Asplenium.
