Sundanese language

1. NOUN

/ ˌsʌn.dəˈniːz ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌsʌn.dəˈniːz ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Austronesian language spoken by the Sundanese people of West Java, Indonesia.
Austronesian language of West Java, Indonesia

Examples

Many people in Bandung speak the Sundanese language fluently alongside Indonesian.
The Sundanese language has its own traditional script, called Aksara Sunda.
Several universities offer courses in the Sundanese language and literature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Javanese language - A language spoken in central and eastern Java, Indonesia

Etymology

From 'Sundanese', referring to the Sunda region and people of West Java, Indonesia.
