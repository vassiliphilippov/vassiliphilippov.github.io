p-type semiconductor

1. NOUN

/ ˈpi taɪp ˌsɛmɪkənˈdʌktɚ / (AmE), / ˈpiː taɪp ˌsɛmɪkənˈdʌktə / (BrE)
a type of semiconductor in which electrical conduction is mainly due to the movement of positively charged holes rather than electrons
semiconductor dominated by positive holes

Examples

The p-type semiconductor was created by doping silicon with boron atoms.
Current in a p-type semiconductor is primarily conducted by holes moving through the lattice.
When a p-type semiconductor is joined with an n-type, a diode is formed.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: n-type semiconductor - semiconductor in which conduction is due mainly to electrons

Etymology

From 'p-type' (positive-type), indicating the positive charge of the majority carriers, and 'semiconductor'.
