gasoline bomb

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡæsəˌliːn ˈbɑm / (AmE), / ˌɡæsəliːn ˈbɒm / (BrE)
a crude incendiary weapon made from a bottle filled with gasoline or another flammable liquid, equipped with a rag wick
crude firebomb made from a bottle and gasoline

Examples

The protestors hurled a gasoline bomb at the abandoned building, starting a fire.
Police found several gasoline bombs hidden in the suspect's car.
He was arrested for attempting to ignite a gasoline bomb during the riot.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Molotov cocktail - an improvised incendiary bomb, usually a bottle filled with flammable liquid and a wick
