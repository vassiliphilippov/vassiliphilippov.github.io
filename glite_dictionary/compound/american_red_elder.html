American red elder

1. NOUN

/ əˈmɛrɪkən rɛd ˈɛldər / (AmE), / əˈmɛrɪkən rɛd ˈɛldə / (BrE)
A common North American shrub or small tree (Sambucus racemosa) with clusters of red berries.
North American shrub or tree with red berries

Examples

The American red elder produces clusters of bright red berries in early summer.
Birds are often attracted to the fruits of the American red elder in the wild.
Gardeners appreciate the American red elder for its hardiness and ornamental value.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: red elderberry - Another name for Sambucus racemosa, the American red elder shrub
