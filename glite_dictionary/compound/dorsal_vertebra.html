dorsal vertebra

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɔrsəl vərˈtibreɪ / (AmE), / ˈdɔːsəl vəˈtiːbrə / (BrE)
any of the twelve vertebrae of the human spine located in the thoracic region, between the cervical and lumbar vertebrae
vertebra in the thoracic (middle back) region
medical

Examples

The surgeon carefully examined the dorsal vertebra for signs of injury.
X-rays revealed a fracture in the fifth dorsal vertebra.
Pain between the shoulders can sometimes indicate issues with a dorsal vertebra.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thoracic vertebra - a vertebra in the thoracic (chest) region of the spine
  • Degree Lower: lumbar vertebra - a vertebra in the lower back region of the spine
  • Degree Higher: cervical vertebra - a vertebra in the neck region of the spine

Etymology

From Latin 'dorsalis' (of the back) and 'vertebra' (joint or bone of the spine).
