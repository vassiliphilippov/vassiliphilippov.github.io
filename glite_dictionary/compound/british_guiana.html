British Guiana

1. PROPN

/ ˌbrɪt̬ɪʃ ɡiˈɑnə / (AmE), / ˌbrɪtɪʃ ɡiˈɑːnə / (BrE)
the former name of the country now known as Guyana, a territory in northeastern South America that was a British colony until gaining independence in 1966.
former name of Guyana, South America
british

Examples

Before independence, Guyana was known as British Guiana under colonial rule.
The first postage stamps of British Guiana are extremely rare and valuable to collectors.
My grandfather emigrated from British Guiana in the early 20th century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Guyana - a country on the northern mainland of South America

Etymology

Named as the British-controlled part of the Guiana region in South America.
