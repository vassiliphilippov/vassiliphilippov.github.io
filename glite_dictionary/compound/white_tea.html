white tea

1. NOUN

/ waɪt tiː / (AmE), / waɪt tiː / (BrE)
a type of tea made from young, minimally processed tea leaves that are lightly oxidized, resulting in a delicate flavor and pale color.
a delicate tea made from lightly oxidized leaves

Examples

I prefer drinking white tea in the afternoon because of its light flavor.
This shop imports premium white tea directly from China.
She offered her guests a choice between white tea and green tea.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: green tea - tea made from unoxidized leaves, producing a lighter flavor
  • Degree Higher: black tea - tea made from fully oxidized leaves, producing a stronger flavor
