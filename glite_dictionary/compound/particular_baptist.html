Particular Baptist

1. NOUN

/ pərˈtɪkjələr ˈbæptɪst / (AmE), / pəˈtɪkjʊlə ˈbæptɪst / (BrE)
a member of a branch of Baptists who adhere to Calvinistic doctrines, especially the belief in predestination and particular atonement
Calvinistic Baptist denomination member

Examples

The early Particular Baptist churches were known for their strict adherence to Calvinism.
He studied the differences between General Baptists and Particular Baptists.
Many English-speaking Particular Baptist congregations trace their origins to the 17th century.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: General Baptist - member of a branch of Baptists who believe in general atonement

Etymology

From 'particular' (referring to particular atonement) and 'Baptist' (Christian denomination).
