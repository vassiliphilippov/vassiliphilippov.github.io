cold front

1. NOUN

/ koʊld frʌnt / (AmE), / kəʊld frʌnt / (BrE)
the forward edge of an advancing mass of colder air, especially as it moves to replace warmer air in the atmosphere
meteorological boundary of advancing cold air

Examples

A cold front brought heavy rain and strong winds to the region overnight.
Meteorologists predict that the cold front will reach the city by late afternoon.
When a cold front passes, temperatures often drop suddenly and skies may clear.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: warm front - the forward edge of an advancing mass of warm air
