red buckeye

1. NOUN

/ rɛd ˈbʌkˌaɪ / (AmE), / rɛd ˈbʌk.aɪ / (BrE)
a shrub native to the southern United States with red flowers, belonging to the horse chestnut family
southern U.S. shrub with red flowers

Examples

The red buckeye adds a splash of color to southern gardens each spring.
Gardeners often plant red buckeye for its vibrant flowers and ability to attract hummingbirds.
The leaves of the red buckeye turn yellow before dropping in the autumn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: buckeye - a type of tree or shrub in the horse chestnut family
