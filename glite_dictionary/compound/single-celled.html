single-celled

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɪŋɡəl sɛld / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋɡl sɛld / (BrE)
(of an organism) consisting of only one cell; not divided into multiple cells.
consisting of one cell

Examples

Amoebas are single-celled organisms found in water and soil.
Under the microscope, the scientist observed a single-celled bacterium moving rapidly.
Yeast is a type of single-celled fungus used in baking and brewing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unicellular - composed of a single cell
  • Antonym: multicellular - composed of many cells
