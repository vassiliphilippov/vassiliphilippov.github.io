orchidaceous plant

1. NOUN

/ ˌɔr.kɪˈdeɪ.ʃəs ˈplænt / (AmE), / ˌɔː.kɪˈdeɪ.ʃəs ˈplɑːnt / (BrE)
Any plant belonging to the orchid family (Orchidaceae), typically known for having flowers with unusual shapes and beautiful colors.
plant of the orchid family

Examples

The greenhouse had a stunning collection of orchidaceous plants from around the world.
Many orchidaceous plants require specific conditions to thrive and produce their colorful flowers.
She specializes in the care and cultivation of rare orchidaceous plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orchid - a plant of the large family Orchidaceae

Etymology

From modern Latin Orchidaceae (the orchid family) plus English -aceous (having the nature of) and plant.
