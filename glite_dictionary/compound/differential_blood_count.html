differential blood count

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪfəˈrɛnʃəl blʌd kaʊnt / (AmE), / ˌdɪfəˈrɛnʃəl blʌd kaʊnt / (BrE)
a laboratory test that determines the number of each type of white blood cell in a specific volume of blood
blood test for white cell types
medical

Examples

The doctor ordered a differential blood count to check for signs of infection.
A differential blood count revealed an increased number of lymphocytes.
The laboratory technician completed a differential blood count as part of the routine examination.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: complete blood count - a test measuring various components of the blood
