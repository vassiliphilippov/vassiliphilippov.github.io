diabetic coma

1. NOUN

/ daɪəˈbɛtɪk ˈkoʊmə / (AmE), / daɪəˈbiːtɪk ˈkəʊmə / (BrE)
A state of unconsciousness caused by severely uncontrolled diabetes, often due to very high or very low blood sugar levels.
coma resulting from severe diabetes
medical

Examples

The patient was rushed to the emergency department after slipping into a diabetic coma.
Uncontrolled blood sugar levels can lead to conditions as serious as a diabetic coma.
Doctors worked quickly to treat her diabetic coma and stabilize her glucose levels.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: coma - a state of deep unconsciousness
