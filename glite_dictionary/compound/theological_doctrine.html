theological doctrine

1. NOUN

/ ˌθi.əˈlɑ.dʒɪ.kəl ˈdɑk.trɪn / (AmE), / ˌθiː.əˈlɒ.dʒɪ.kəl ˈdɒk.trɪn / (BrE)
A belief or set of principles held and taught by a religious group, especially as part of its theology.
doctrine or belief taught in theology

Examples

Many universities offer courses exploring the development of theological doctrine throughout history.
The council convened to resolve disputes over matters of theological doctrine.
Understanding this theological doctrine is crucial for interpreting the church's position on salvation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dogma - a principle or set of principles laid down by an authority as incontrovertibly true

Etymology

From Greek *theologia* (study of God) and Latin *doctrina* (teaching, learning).
