Scotch terrier

1. NOUN

/ skɑtʃ ˈtɛriər / (AmE), / skɒtʃ ˈtɛriə / (BrE)
an old Scottish breed of small, long-haired terrier, typically black, with erect tail and ears
a small Scottish breed of terrier dog
archaic

Examples

The Scotch terrier is known for its wiry black coat and sturdy build.
Her family keeps a friendly Scotch terrier that loves to go for walks in the park.
In Victorian times, the Scotch terrier gained popularity as a companion animal in Scotland.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Scottish Terrier - a modern small, wiry-haired Scottish breed of terrier

Etymology

From 'Scotch', an archaic term for 'Scottish', referring to its origin in Scotland, and 'terrier', from Old French *chien terrier* ('earth dog').
