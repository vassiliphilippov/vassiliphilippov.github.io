genus Chamaeleo

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs kæˈmiː.li.oʊ / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs kəˈmiː.lɪəʊ / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Chamaeleontidae, comprising typical chameleons.
type genus of chameleons

Examples

The genus Chamaeleo includes several well-known species of chameleons found in Africa and Asia.
Researchers discovered a new species within the genus Chamaeleo during their expedition.
Most pet chameleons sold in captivity belong to the genus Chamaeleo.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Chamaeleon - a genus of lizards commonly known as chameleons

Etymology

From Latin 'Chamaeleo', from Greek 'khamaileōn', meaning 'ground lion' (chameleon).
