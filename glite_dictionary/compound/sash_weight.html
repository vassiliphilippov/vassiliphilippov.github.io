sash weight

1. NOUN

/ sæʃ weɪt / (AmE), / sæʃ weɪt / (BrE)
a heavy bar or cylinder, usually made of metal, used as a counterweight to balance a sliding sash window
counterweight for a sliding sash window

Examples

The old house still has its original sash weights hidden in the window frames.
When one sash weight broke, the window became difficult to open and close.
Replacing the missing sash weight restored smooth movement to the sash window.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: counterweight - a weight used to balance another weight
