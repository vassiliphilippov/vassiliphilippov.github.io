House of Lords

1. PROPN

/ haʊs əv lɔrdz / (AmE), / haʊs əv lɔːdz / (BrE)
the upper chamber of the Parliament of the United Kingdom, consisting of appointed and hereditary members
upper chamber of UK Parliament
british

Examples

The House of Lords debated the new legislation for several hours before reaching a decision.
Appointment to the House of Lords is often considered a great honor in British society.
Unlike the elected House of Commons, the House of Lords is composed mainly of appointed members.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: House of Commons - the lower chamber of the UK Parliament, composed of elected representatives

2. NOUN

/ ˌhaʊs əv ˈlɔrdz / (AmE), / ˌhaʊs əv ˈlɔːdz / (BrE)
the upper chamber of the British Parliament, composed of appointed, hereditary, and clergy members, responsible for reviewing and revising legislation passed by the House of Commons
upper chamber of UK Parliament
british

Examples

Several important bills were debated in the House of Lords last week.
The House of Lords reviews legislation passed by the House of Commons before it becomes law.
Many people argue about the future role of the House of Lords in modern British politics.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: House of Commons - the lower chamber of the British Parliament
