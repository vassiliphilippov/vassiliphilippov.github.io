grass family

1. NOUN

/ ɡræs ˈfæməli / (AmE), / ɡrɑːs ˈfæməli / (BrE)
a large family of mostly herbaceous, sometimes woody, plants including grasses, cereals, bamboo, reeds, and sugar cane (family Poaceae)
family of grasses and related plants

Examples

Rice and wheat are members of the grass family, providing staple food for much of the world.
The grass family includes both tiny lawn grasses and massive bamboo species.
Sugar cane, although not a true tree, belongs to the grass family as well.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Poaceae - the botanical family including all true grasses
