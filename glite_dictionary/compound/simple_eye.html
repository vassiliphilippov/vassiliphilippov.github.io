simple eye

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪmpəl aɪ / (AmE), / ˈsɪmpəl aɪ / (BrE)
an eye with a single lens, as found in some insects and other invertebrates, distinct from a compound eye.
an eye with a single lens

Examples

Many insects have both simple eyes and compound eyes to help them see their surroundings.
The spider's primary simple eyes provide it with acute vision for hunting.
Researchers studied the light sensitivity of the simple eye in different species of flies.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: compound eye - an eye consisting of multiple small visual units (ommatidia), typical of many insects and crustaceans
