Stanley Steamer

1. NOUN

/ ˈstænli ˈstiːmɚ / (AmE), / ˈstænli ˈstiːmə / (BrE)
A steam-powered automobile produced by the Stanley Motor Carriage Company in the early 20th century.
steam-powered car from Stanley company

Examples

The museum displays a well-preserved Stanley Steamer from 1905.
Driving a Stanley Steamer required careful attention to the water and fuel levels.
Enthusiasts restored an old Stanley Steamer for the annual vintage car parade.

Etymology

Named after the Stanley brothers, founders of the Stanley Motor Carriage Company, and from 'steamer', meaning a steam-powered vehicle.
