family Sarcoscyphaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli sɑːrˌkɒsɪˈfeɪsiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli sɑːkɒsˌsaɪfəˈsiː / (BrE)
A family of fungi belonging to the order Pezizales, known for their brightly colored, cup-shaped fruiting bodies.
family of fungi in order Pezizales

Examples

The family Sarcoscyphaceae includes fungi often recognized by their vivid scarlet or orange cups.
Mycology students studied the family Sarcoscyphaceae as part of their course on fungal biodiversity.
Many species in the family Sarcoscyphaceae are found on decaying wood in temperate forests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sarcoscypha - genus of fungi within Sarcoscyphaceae with cup-shaped fruiting bodies

Etymology

Sarcoscyphaceae from genus Sarcoscypha (from Greek 'sarx' meaning flesh and 'skyphos' meaning cup) + Latin family suffix '-aceae.'
