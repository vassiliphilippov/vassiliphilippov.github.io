Dewey, Cheatem and Howe

1. PROPN

/ ˈdjuːi ˈtʃiːtəm ənd ˈhaʊ / (AmE), / ˈdjuːi ˈtʃiːtəm ənd ˈhaʊ / (BrE)
a humorous fictional law firm name, used as a joke to suggest dishonesty or trickery by lawyers
fictional joke law firm name
informal american

Examples

The comedian joked that he got all his legal advice from Dewey, Cheatem and Howe.
Whenever someone mentioned hiring a lawyer, he would suggest Dewey, Cheatem and Howe.
Their sketch featured the infamous law firm of Dewey, Cheatem and Howe representing a client.

Synset Examples

Everyone laughed when he mentioned Dewey, Cheatem and Howe.
He joked about hiring Dewey, Cheatem and Howe for legal advice.

Etymology

A pun on the phrase 'Do we cheat 'em and how?', mimicking the style of legal firm names.
