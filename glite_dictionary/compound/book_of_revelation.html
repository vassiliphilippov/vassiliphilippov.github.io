Book of Revelation

1. PROPN

/ bʊk əv ˌrɛvəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / bʊk əv ˌrɛvəˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the last book of the New Testament, featuring symbolic visions and prophecies about the end of the world, attributed to Saint John the Apostle
last book of the New Testament

Examples

Many people have tried to interpret the symbolism in the Book of Revelation.
The Book of Revelation describes the final battle between good and evil.
In Christian eschatology, the Book of Revelation is considered a key prophetic text.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Apocalypse - Greek name for the Book of Revelation

Etymology

From Latin 'revelatio' (an unveiling, a revelation), referring to the title of the biblical book.
