big leaguer

1. NOUN

/ bɪɡ ˈliɡər / (AmE), / bɪɡ ˈliːɡə / (BrE)
A player who is a member of a major-league baseball team.
major-league baseball player
american

Examples

After years in the minors, he finally became a big leaguer last season.
The rookie acted like a true big leaguer both on and off the field.
She interviewed several big leaguers for her article on baseball careers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: major leaguer - a member of a major-league sports team, especially in baseball
  • Antonym: minor leaguer - a player in the minor leagues of baseball

Etymology

From the phrase 'big league', meaning the major leagues of professional baseball, plus '-er' denoting a person associated with it.
