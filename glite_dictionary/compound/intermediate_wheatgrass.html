intermediate wheatgrass

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪntərˈmiːdiət ˈwiːtˌɡræs / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈmiːdiət ˈwiːtɡrɑːs / (BrE)
A species of Asiatic grass introduced to North America and used as pasture and fodder for livestock.
a grass species used for pasture and fodder

Examples

Intermediate wheatgrass is commonly planted by farmers to provide sustainable forage for cattle.
Researchers are developing intermediate wheatgrass as a perennial grain crop.
Because of its deep roots, intermediate wheatgrass helps prevent soil erosion.
