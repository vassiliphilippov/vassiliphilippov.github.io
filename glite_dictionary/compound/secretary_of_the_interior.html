Secretary of the Interior

1. NOUN

/ sɛk.rəˌtɛɹ.i əv ði ɪnˈtɛɹ.i.ɚ / (AmE), / ˌsɛk.rəˈteə.ri əv ði ˌɪnˈtɪə.ri.ə / (BrE)
the member of a government cabinet who leads the Department of the Interior, responsible for managing public lands, natural resources, and other related affairs.
head of Department of the Interior

Examples

The Secretary of the Interior announced new policies for national park conservation.
He was appointed Secretary of the Interior in the new administration.
Debates about wildlife management often involve the Secretary of the Interior.

Synset Examples

the position of Interior Secretary was created in 1849

2. NOUN

/ sɛk.rəˌtɛr.i əv ði ɪnˈtɛr.i.ɚ / (AmE), / ˌsɛk.rəˈtɛə.ri əv ði ɪnˈtɪə.ri.ə / (BrE)
the US government official who is the head of the Department of the Interior, responsible for the management and conservation of most federal land and natural resources
US government cabinet post: head of the Interior Department
american

Examples

President Roosevelt nominated Harold Ickes as Secretary of the Interior in 1933.
The Secretary of the Interior plays a crucial role in managing national parks and public lands.
After leaving office, she became the first female Secretary of the Interior in U.S. history.
