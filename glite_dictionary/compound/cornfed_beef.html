cornfed beef

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔrnˌfɛd bif / (AmE), / ˈkɔːnfɛd biːf / (BrE)
Beef from cattle that have been raised primarily on a corn-based diet, often noted for its tenderness and marbling.
beef from corn-fed cattle

Examples

Many restaurants serve cornfed beef because of its consistent tenderness and flavor.
The farm prides itself on producing high-quality cornfed beef for local markets.
Some consumers believe that cornfed beef is juicier than grass-fed alternatives.

Synset Examples

She prefers cornfed beef for its rich flavor.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: grass-fed beef - beef from cattle raised by grazing on grass rather than grains
