genus Phaseolus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs fəˈziː.oʊləs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs fəˈziː.ələs / (BrE)
A group of herbaceous plants native to warm regions, including most American beans, belonging to the botanical genus Phaseolus.
genus of plants including most American beans

Examples

The genus Phaseolus includes common beans such as kidney and pinto beans.
Many varieties within the genus Phaseolus are cultivated for food worldwide.
Botanists study the genus Phaseolus to better understand the origins of domesticated beans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bean - the edible seed of various plants in the family Fabaceae

Etymology

From Latin 'phaseolus', meaning 'bean'.
