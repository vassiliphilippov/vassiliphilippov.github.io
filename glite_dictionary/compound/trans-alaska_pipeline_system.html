Trans-Alaska Pipeline System

1. NOUN

/ trænz əˈlæskə ˈpaɪplaɪn ˈsɪstəm / (AmE), / tranz əˈlæskə ˈpaɪplaɪn ˈsɪstəm / (BrE)
a major oil pipeline running about 800 miles (1,287 km) across Alaska, from Prudhoe Bay to the port of Valdez
major oil pipeline across Alaska

Examples

The Trans-Alaska Pipeline System transports crude oil across some of the most remote terrains in the United States.
Environmental groups closely monitor the Trans-Alaska Pipeline System for any potential impacts on wildlife and ecosystem.
Construction of the Trans-Alaska Pipeline System was a major engineering achievement in the 1970s.
