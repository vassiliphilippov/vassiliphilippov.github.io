adder's tongue fern

1. NOUN

/ ˈædərz ˈtʌŋ fɜrn / (AmE), / ˈædəz ˈtʌŋ fɜːn / (BrE)
A type of fern with a distinctive fertile spike resembling a snake's tongue, belonging to the genus Ophioglossum.
fern with a snake-tongue-shaped fertile spike

Examples

The adder's tongue fern can be found growing in moist meadows and woodlands.
Botanists were excited to discover an unusually large specimen of adder's tongue fern in the field.
She uses illustrations of the adder's tongue fern in her botanical journal.

Etymology

So named for the snake-tongue-like shape of its fertile spike and the Old English word for 'adder' (a type of snake).
