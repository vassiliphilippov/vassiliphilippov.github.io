red-berried elder

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɛd ˈbɛrid ˈɛldɚ / (AmE), / ˌrɛd ˈbɛrid ˈɛldə / (BrE)
A Eurasian shrub (Sambucus racemosa) with clusters of small red berries and pinnate leaves.
Eurasian shrub with red berries

Examples

The red-berried elder produces bright clusters of berries in late spring.
Experts warn that the berries of the red-berried elder are toxic if eaten raw.
We spotted a red-berried elder growing at the edge of the forest during our hike.

2. NOUN

/ ˌrɛd ˈbɛrid ˈɛldɚ / (AmE), / ˌrɛd ˈbɛrid ˈɛldə / (BrE)
A North American shrub or small tree (Sambucus racemosa), typically bearing clusters of red berries.
shrub or tree with red berries (Sambucus racemosa)

Examples

In the forest, we spotted a red-berried elder growing near the stream.
The bright red fruits of the red-berried elder attract many birds in spring.
Gardeners should be cautious, as the berries of the red-berried elder are toxic to humans.
