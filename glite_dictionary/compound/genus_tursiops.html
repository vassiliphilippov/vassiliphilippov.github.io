genus Tursiops

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs tərˈsaɪ.ɑps / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs tɜːˈsaɪ.ɒps / (BrE)
A genus of marine mammals within the dolphin family (Delphinidae), including species such as the common bottlenose dolphin.
a dolphin genus including bottlenose dolphins

Examples

The genus Tursiops is well-known for including the highly intelligent bottlenose dolphins.
Scientists have studied social behavior in several members of the genus Tursiops.
The genus Tursiops is widely distributed in warm and temperate seas around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bottlenose dolphin - A common species within the genus Tursiops, known for its bottle-shaped snout

Etymology

Tursiops from Latin 'tursio' (dolphin) and Greek 'ōps' (appearance, face).
