colloidal gel

1. NOUN

/ kəˈlɔɪdiəl dʒɛl / (AmE), / kəˈlɔɪdiəl dʒɛl / (BrE)
A semi-solid substance in which microscopic particles are dispersed throughout a solid network, forming a gel-like structure.
semi-solid colloid with particles dispersed in a network

Examples

The mixture thickened overnight and eventually formed a colloidal gel in the beaker.
Researchers studied the properties of the colloidal gel to develop new drug delivery systems.
A colloidal gel can trap liquid in its structure, giving it a unique texture.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: sol - a colloidal suspension in a fluid form
  • Synonym: gel - a jelly-like substance

Etymology

From 'colloid,' from Greek kolla (glue) + 'gel,' short for 'gelatin'.
