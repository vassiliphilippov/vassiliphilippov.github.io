crystallized honey

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɪstəˌlaɪzd ˈhʌni / (AmE), / ˈkrɪstəlaɪzd ˈhʌni / (BrE)
Honey in which part of the glucose has formed crystals, giving it a grainy or solid texture rather than remaining liquid.
honey with glucose crystals; grainy or solid texture

Examples

I spread crystallized honey on my toast for a different texture and flavor.
Some people prefer crystallized honey because it's less messy to use than liquid honey.
If your crystallized honey has hardened, you can gently warm it to return it to a liquid state.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: liquid honey - honey that has not undergone crystallization and remains fluid
