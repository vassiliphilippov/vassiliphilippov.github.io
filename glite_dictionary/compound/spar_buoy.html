spar buoy

1. NOUN

/ spɑr ˈbuː.i / (AmE), / spɑː ˈbɔɪ / (BrE)
a tall, cylindrical buoy that floats upright in the water, resembling a vertical log, used for navigation or marking locations at sea.
upright cylindrical buoy for navigation

Examples

The lighthouse keeper checked the spar buoy every week to ensure it was properly anchored.
A spar buoy is often used to mark underwater hazards near shipping lanes.
Unlike can buoys, a spar buoy floats vertically in the water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: buoy - a floating marker used for navigation or warning

Etymology

From 'spar' (a long, round pole) and 'buoy' (floating marker).
