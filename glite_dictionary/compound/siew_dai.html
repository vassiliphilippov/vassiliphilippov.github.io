siew dai

1. ADV

/ siu daɪ / (AmE), / siu daɪ / (BrE)
(in Singaporean and Malaysian English) Used when ordering a drink, especially tea or coffee, to indicate it should be made with less sugar than usual.
with less sugar (in drinks, esp. Singapore/Malaysia)
informal

Examples

Could I have my coffee siew dai today?
When he orders teh, he always says siew dai to avoid too much sugar.
She prefers bubble tea siew dai because it's not overly sweet.

Synset Examples

We ordered siew dai with our dim sum for a treat.
He loves siew dai, especially with a spicy dipping sauce.

Etymology

From Cantonese 少甜 (siu tim), literally 'less sweet'.
