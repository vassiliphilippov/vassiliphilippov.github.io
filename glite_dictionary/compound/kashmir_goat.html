Kashmir goat

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæʃmɪr ɡoʊt / (AmE), / ˈkæʃmɪə ɡəʊt / (BrE)
A domesticated goat from the Himalayan region, valued for its soft undercoat used to make cashmere wool.
Himalayan goat producing cashmere wool

Examples

The Kashmir goat is renowned for producing some of the world's finest cashmere fiber.
Farmers in the Himalayas often raise Kashmir goats for their valuable wool.
A single Kashmir goat can yield only a few ounces of soft undercoat each year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cashmere - fine, soft wool obtained from Kashmir goats

Etymology

Named after Kashmir, a region in the Himalayas where the goat originates.
