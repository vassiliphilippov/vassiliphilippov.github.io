Age of Fishes

1. NOUN

/ eɪdʒ əv ˈfɪʃɪz / (AmE), / eɪdʒ əv ˈfɪʃɪz / (BrE)
the geological period from about 405 million to 345 million years ago, during which fishes were the dominant form of life and amphibians and ammonites first appeared
Devonian geological period; era when fishes predominated

Examples

The Age of Fishes saw the rise and diversification of many ancient fish species.
Scientists have found fossils from the Age of Fishes in sedimentary rocks around the world.
During the Age of Fishes, early amphibians evolved from lobe-finned fishes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Devonian - the geological period also known as the Age of Fishes
