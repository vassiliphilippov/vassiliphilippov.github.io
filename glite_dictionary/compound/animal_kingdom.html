animal kingdom

1. NOUN

/ ˈænɪməl ˈkɪŋdəm / (AmE), / ˈænɪməl ˈkɪŋdəm / (BrE)
the taxonomic group that comprises all living and extinct animals as distinct from plants, fungi, and other life forms
taxonomic group including all animals

Examples

Children are often fascinated by the diversity of species in the animal kingdom.
Biologists classify humans as part of the animal kingdom.
The discovery of new creatures expanded our understanding of the animal kingdom.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: plant kingdom - the taxonomic group that includes all plants
  • Antonym: fungi kingdom - the taxonomic group that includes all fungi

Etymology

From Latin 'animal' (living being) and Old English 'cyningdom' (kingdom, domain).
