Bull Run

1. PROPN

/ bʊl rʌn / (AmE), / bʊl rʌn / (BrE)
either of two major battles fought near a stream called Bull Run in Virginia during the American Civil War, both resulting in Confederate victories over Federal forces (1861 and 1862).
Civil War battles in Virginia
american

Examples

Historians debate the significance of the Union's defeat at Bull Run for the rest of the Civil War.
The reenactment of the Battle of Bull Run attracted hundreds of Civil War enthusiasts.
Many soldiers believed that victory at Bull Run would end the conflict quickly, but they were mistaken.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: First Battle of Bull Run - the first of the two battles fought at Bull Run in 1861
  • Synonym: Second Battle of Bull Run - the second of the two battles fought at Bull Run in 1862

Etymology

Named after Bull Run, a stream in Virginia, United States, near the site of the battles.
