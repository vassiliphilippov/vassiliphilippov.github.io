chaparral sage

1. NOUN

/ təˈpærəl seɪdʒ / (AmE), / ʃəˈpærəl seɪdʒ / (BrE)
a silvery-leaved herb native to California, known for its purple flowers; also called Salvia clevelandii
a Californian herb with purple flowers

Examples

The chaparral sage in her garden released a pleasant fragrance after the rain.
Botanists often study chaparral sage for its drought-resistant properties.
We saw fields of chaparral sage blooming along the California hillside.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sage - an aromatic herb of the genus Salvia

Etymology

From 'chaparral' (dense growth of shrubs, Spanish origin) and 'sage' (from Latin 'salvia').
