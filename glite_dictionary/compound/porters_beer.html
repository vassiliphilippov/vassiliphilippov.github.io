porter's beer

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɔrtərz ˌbɪr / (AmE), / ˈpɔːtəz ˌbɪə / (BrE)
a very dark, sweet style of ale brewed from roasted unmalted barley, known for its rich flavor
a dark, sweet ale brewed from roasted barley

Examples

He ordered a pint of porter's beer to enjoy with his dinner.
The brewery is famous for its traditional porter's beer recipe.
With its roasted flavors and dark color, porter's beer is popular among craft beer enthusiasts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stout - a strong, dark beer similar to porter

Etymology

From 'porter', named for its popularity among street and river porters in 18th-century London.
