act of darkness

1. NOUN

/ ækt əv ˈdɑːrk.nəs / (AmE), / ækt əv ˈdɑːk.nəs / (BrE)
a morally wrong or evil action; a deed considered wicked or immoral, often used in a literary or formal context
an evil or immoral deed (literary)
archaic

Examples

Many saw the betrayal as an act of darkness that shattered their trust.
The novel tells the story of an act of darkness that haunts the village for generations.
He confessed his act of darkness in a letter before leaving town.

Synset Examples

The crime scene was an act of darkness against the community.
The sudden storm felt like an act of darkness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crime - an act that is against the law; an illegal act
  • Antonym: good deed - an action that is morally right or kind
