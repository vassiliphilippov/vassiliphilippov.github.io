G-series agent

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒi ˌsɪriːz ˈeɪdʒənt / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː ˌsɪəriːz ˈeɪdʒənt / (BrE)
any of a group of highly toxic, non-persistent nerve agents, discovered and first synthesized before or during World War II, including agents such as tabun, sarin, and soman
type of nerve agent used in chemical warfare

Examples

The military trained to handle exposure to G-series agents during chemical attack drills.
Sarin, also known as GB, is perhaps the most notorious G-series agent used in chemical warfare.
Research into detecting G-series agents remains a high priority for defense organizations.

Synset Examples

GB was the only G agent that was fielded by the US as a munition, specifically in rockets, aerial bombs, and artillery shells.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nerve agent - a chemical agent that disrupts the nervous system

Etymology

Named from 'G' for 'German,' as first synthesized in Germany; 'agent' from the Latin 'agens' (doing, acting).
