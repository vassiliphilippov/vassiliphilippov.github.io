genus Eriosoma

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˌɪəriˈoʊsəmə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˌɪəriˈəʊsəmə / (BrE)
A genus of woolly aphids, a group of small, sap-sucking insects covered with a waxy, white, wool-like substance.
a genus of woolly aphids

Examples

The genus Eriosoma includes several species known for producing a woolly secretion.
Entomologists study the genus Eriosoma to understand its role in agricultural pest control.
Members of the genus Eriosoma are often found on elm and alder trees.

Etymology

From Latin 'genus' (kind) and 'Eriosoma' from Greek 'erion' (wool) + 'soma' (body), referring to their woolly appearance.
