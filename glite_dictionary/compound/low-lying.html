low-lying

1. ADJ

/ ˈloʊˌlaɪɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈləʊˌlaɪɪŋ / (BrE)
Situated at a low elevation, often making an area more likely to experience flooding.
at a low elevation; prone to flooding

Examples

The farm is located in a low-lying area that frequently floods in spring.
Many wetlands are found in low-lying coastal regions vulnerable to rising sea levels.
After heavy rains, the low-lying roads were quickly covered with water.

Synset Examples

Birds often nest in the low-lying shrubs around the garden.
They built new houses in the low-lying fields by the river.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: high-lying - situated at a high elevation
