energy level

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛnɚdʒi ˌlɛvəl / (AmE), / ˈɛnədʒi ˌlɛvəl / (BrE)
a specific, stable amount of energy that a physical system, especially an electron in an atom or molecule, can have
quantum state with a specific energy

Examples

Electrons in an atom occupy fixed energy levels rather than a continuous range of energies.
When an electron drops to a lower energy level, it emits a photon.
Scientists measured the difference between the two energy levels in the experiment.

Synset Examples

according to quantum theory only certain energy levels are possible

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛnɚdʒi ˌlɛvəl / (AmE), / ˈɛnədʒi ˌlɛvəl / (BrE)
the amount of physical or mental energy that a person has at a particular time
amount of physical or mental energy

Examples

After a good night's sleep, my energy level was much higher in the morning.
Coffee helps boost her energy level during long afternoons at work.
He was concerned that his low energy level was affecting his productivity.

Synset Examples

His energy levels were exhausted after the workout.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vitality - the state of being strong and full of energy
  • Antonym: fatigue - extreme tiredness resulting from mental or physical exertion
