main-topmast

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪnˌtɑp.mæst / (AmE), / ˈmeɪnˌtɒp.mɑːst / (BrE)
the section of a ship's mast immediately above the mainmast; the second stage of the mainmast in square-rigged ships
the mast section above the mainmast (nautical)

Examples

The lookout climbed the main-topmast to get a better view of the approaching coastline.
During the storm, the main-topmast snapped, making the ship difficult to control.
The sails attached to the main-topmast helped propel the ship faster when the wind was strong.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: mainmast - the principal mast of a ship
  • Degree Higher: main-topgallant mast - the mast above the main-topmast on a sailing ship

Etymology

From 'mainmast' + 'topmast', referring to the placement above the mainmast.
