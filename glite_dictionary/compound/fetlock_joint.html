fetlock joint

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɛt.lɑk dʒɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˈfɛt.lɒk dʒɔɪnt / (BrE)
the joint between the cannon bone and the pastern in the leg of a horse or other hoofed mammals
joint between the cannon bone and pastern

Examples

The veterinarian carefully examined the horse's fetlock joint for signs of swelling or injury.
Pain in the fetlock joint can indicate excessive strain from training or racing.
After the fall, the mare developed stiffness in her right fetlock joint.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fetlock - the tuft of hair or region at the joint above the hoof of a horse

Etymology

From 'fetlock', a Middle English term for a tuft of hair on a horse's leg, and 'joint', from Old French 'joindre' (to join).
