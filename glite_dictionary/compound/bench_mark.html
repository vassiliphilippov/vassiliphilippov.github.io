bench mark

1. NOUN

/ bɛntʃ mɑrk / (AmE), / bɛntʃ mɑːk / (BrE)
A permanent reference point marked by surveyors, indicating a known position and elevation, used as a basis for measuring other locations.
surveying reference point

Examples

The surveyors placed a bench mark near the construction site to establish accurate elevation measurements.
The old stone post serves as a bench mark for all local land surveys in the region.
Before beginning the project, the engineers verified the location of the official bench mark.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: datum - a fixed starting point of a scale or operation used in surveying and mapping
