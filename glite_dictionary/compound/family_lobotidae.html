family Lobotidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli loʊˈbɑːtɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli ləʊˈbɒtɪdiː / (BrE)
A small family of marine fish, known as tripletails, found in warm seas.
a family of marine tripletail fish

Examples

The family Lobotidae includes only a few species of tripletail fish found in tropical waters.
Ichthyologists studied the distinctive fins of the family Lobotidae for their unique adaptations.
Fishermen sometimes catch members of the family Lobotidae off the coasts of Florida and the Gulf of Mexico.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tripletail - a type of marine fish with three rounded fins

Etymology

From New Latin 'Lobotidae', based on the genus 'Lobotes'.
