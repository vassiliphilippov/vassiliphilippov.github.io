picture tube

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪktʃɚ tuːb / (AmE), / ˈpɪktʃə tjuːb / (BrE)
a cathode-ray tube in a television that displays images by converting electrical signals into visible pictures on a screen
cathode-ray tube in a television

Examples

The old television stopped working because its picture tube burned out.
Many people replaced their bulky picture tube TVs with flat-screen models.
Technicians used to repair the picture tube when the display became blurry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cathode-ray tube - an electronic display device that uses electron beams to create images on a phosphorescent screen
