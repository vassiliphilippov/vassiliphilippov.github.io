aurora borealis

1. NOUN

/ əˌrɔrə bɔˈriælɪs / (AmE), / ɔːˌrɔːrə bɒˈriːælɪs / (BrE)
a natural display of bright and colorful lights in the night sky, seen mainly in high-latitude regions of the Northern Hemisphere
northern lights atmospheric phenomenon

Examples

We were lucky enough to see the aurora borealis on our trip to Norway.
Photographers travel great distances to capture the beauty of the aurora borealis.
The aurora borealis appeared as shimmering green and pink lights across the Arctic sky.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: aurora australis - the aurora of the Southern Hemisphere

Etymology

From Latin: 'aurora' (dawn) + 'borealis' (northern), meaning 'northern dawn'.
