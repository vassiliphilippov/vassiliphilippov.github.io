paraffin series

1. NOUN

/ ˈpærəfɪn ˈsɪriːz / (AmE), / ˈpærəfɪn ˈsɪəriːz / (BrE)
a series of non-aromatic saturated hydrocarbons (alkanes) with the general formula CnH2n+2, including methane, ethane, and propane.
series of saturated hydrocarbons (alkanes)

Examples

The paraffin series includes methane, ethane, and propane as its simplest members.
Chemists study the paraffin series to understand the properties of alkanes.
All compounds in the paraffin series share the general molecular formula CnH2n+2.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alkane - type of saturated hydrocarbon with single bonds

Etymology

From 'paraffin' (a kind of alkane hydrocarbon), ultimately from Latin 'parum' (little) + 'affinis' (affinity).
