family Lampridae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli læmˈpraɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli læmˈprɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of large, deep-bodied marine fishes known as opahs, found in warm and temperate oceans.
a family of opah fish

Examples

The family Lampridae includes only a few living species of opahs.
Studies of the family Lampridae have revealed unique adaptations for deep-sea living.
Biologists recently identified a new member of the family Lampridae in Pacific waters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Lampris - a genus of opahs within family Lampridae

Etymology

From Modern Latin, after genus name Lampris, combined with standard zoological family suffix -idae.
