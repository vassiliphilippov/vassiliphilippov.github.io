thin-shelled

1. ADJ

/ θɪn ˈʃɛld / (AmE), / θɪn ˈʃɛld / (BrE)
Having a thin shell; used to describe animals or plants whose shell is notably slender or delicate.
having a thin shell

Examples

The thin-shelled eggs of these birds make them vulnerable to predation.
Oysters from polluted waters often have thin-shelled or brittle shells.
Some snails have a thin-shelled structure, which can make them more sensitive to environmental changes.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: thick-shelled - having a thick shell
