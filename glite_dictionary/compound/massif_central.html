Massif Central

1. PROPN

/ mæˌsiːf sɛnˈtræl / (AmE), / masˌiːf sɑ̃ˈtʁal / (BrE)
a mountainous plateau region in southern France, covering almost one sixth of the country
mountainous plateau region in southern France

Examples

The Massif Central is known for its dramatic volcanic landscapes and rugged beauty.
Many ancient towns in the Massif Central offer a glimpse into France's rural history.
Hiking enthusiasts often travel to the Massif Central for its challenging trails and breathtaking views.

Etymology

From French 'Massif Central', meaning 'Central Massif'.
