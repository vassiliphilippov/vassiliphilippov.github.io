phylum Porifera

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪləm pəˈrɪfərə / (AmE), / ˈfʌɪləm pɒˈrɪfərə / (BrE)
A major group (phylum) of simple aquatic animals, commonly called sponges, characterized by porous bodies and a lack of true tissues.
biological phylum of sponges

Examples

Scientists believe that phylum Porifera represents one of the earliest branches of animal evolution.
Members of the phylum Porifera are found in marine and freshwater habitats worldwide.
The unique filtering ability of phylum Porifera helps to maintain healthy aquatic ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sponge - simple aquatic animal in phylum Porifera

Etymology

From Latin 'porus' (pore) and '-fera' (bearing), referring to pore-bearing animals.
