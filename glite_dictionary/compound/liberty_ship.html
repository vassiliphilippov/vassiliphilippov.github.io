Liberty ship

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪbɚti ʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈlɪbəti ʃɪp / (BrE)
a type of large, slow cargo ship mass-produced in the United States during World War II to transport supplies and troops
type of WWII cargo ship

Examples

During the war, the United States built thousands of Liberty ships to support the Allied forces.
My grandfather served on a Liberty ship crossing the Atlantic in 1944.
Several Liberty ships still exist today as museum vessels.

Etymology

Named for the first ship of the class, the SS Patrick Henry, whose launch speech referred to 'Give me liberty or give me death.'
