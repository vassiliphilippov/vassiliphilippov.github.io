Comptroller General

1. NOUN

/ kəmˌtroʊlɚ dʒəˈnɛrəl / (AmE), / kəmˌtrəʊlə dʒəˈnɛrəl / (BrE)
the chief United States federal official responsible for supervising government expenditures and settling claims against the government
chief official overseeing U.S. federal expenditures

Examples

The Comptroller General testified before Congress about the accuracy of government financial reports.
As Comptroller General, she was responsible for ensuring federal funds were spent appropriately.
The report was reviewed by the Comptroller General before being made public.

Etymology

From 'comptroller' (variant of 'controller', influenced by French 'compte') and 'general' (from Latin 'generalis').
