cultivated rice

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌltɪˌveɪtɪd raɪs / (AmE), / ˈkʌltɪˌveɪtɪd raɪs / (BrE)
A species of rice (Oryza sativa) grown as a staple crop and a major source of food for much of the world's population.
domesticated rice plant for food

Examples

Cultivated rice is a staple food for billions of people across Asia and Africa.
Farmers have developed many varieties of cultivated rice to adapt to different climates.
The genetic diversity of cultivated rice is crucial for food security.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: wild rice - rice species that grow naturally and are not domesticated
