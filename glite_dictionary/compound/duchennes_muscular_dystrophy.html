Duchenne's muscular dystrophy

1. NOUN

/ duːˈʃɛnz ˈmʌskjələr dɪsˈtrəfi / (AmE), / djʊˈʃɛnz ˈmʌskjʊlə dɪsˈtrəfi / (BrE)
a severe inherited type of muscular dystrophy that primarily affects boys, causing progressive muscle weakness; it is carried by females but affects only males due to X-linked recessive inheritance
a severe inherited muscle disease affecting boys
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that Duchenne's muscular dystrophy is a genetic disorder usually diagnosed in early childhood.
Families affected by Duchenne's muscular dystrophy often seek support to manage the progressive symptoms.
Researchers are working to find better treatments for Duchenne's muscular dystrophy to improve quality of life for patients.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Becker muscular dystrophy - a related but milder form of muscular dystrophy

Etymology

Named after French neurologist Guillaume Duchenne (1806–1875), who first described the condition.
