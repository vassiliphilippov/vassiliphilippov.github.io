propionic acid

1. NOUN

/ proʊˌpiˈɑnɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / prəʊˌpɪˈɒnɪk ˈæsɪd / (BrE)
a colorless, pungent liquid fatty acid (C3H6O2) found in milk, sweat, and some fuels, used as a preservative and in chemical manufacturing
a fatty acid found in milk and sweat

Examples

The preservative in the bread contains propionic acid to prevent mold growth.
Scientists detected the presence of propionic acid in the sweat samples.
Industrial production of propionic acid is commonly done through fermentation.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: butyric acid - a related fatty acid found in butter, also with a strong odor
  • Degree Lower: acetic acid - a related, less complex fatty acid found in vinegar

Etymology

From Latin 'pro-' (forward) + Greek 'piōn' (fat), referring to its relation to fats.
