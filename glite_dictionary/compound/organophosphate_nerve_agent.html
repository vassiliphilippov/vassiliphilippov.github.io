organophosphate nerve agent

1. NOUN

/ ˌɔːrɡənoʊˌfɑsfəˌteɪt nɜːrv ˈeɪdʒənt / (AmE), / ˌɔːɡənəʊˌfɒsfəˌteɪt nɜːv ˈeɪdʒənt / (BrE)
a highly toxic chemical weapon from a group of nerve agents based on organophosphate compounds, causing severe disruption to the nervous system
chemical nerve agent weapon

Examples

Exposure to an organophosphate nerve agent can be fatal within minutes without immediate treatment.
During the conflict, reports indicated the possible use of organophosphate nerve agents against civilians.
Scientists developed the first organophosphate nerve agent in the 1930s for use as a weapon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nerve agent - a chemical weapon that interferes with normal function of the nervous system

Etymology

From 'organophosphate', a group of organic compounds containing phosphorus, and 'nerve agent', a term for chemical weapons disrupting nervous system function.
