cone clutch

1. NOUN

/ koʊn klʌtʃ / (AmE), / kəʊn klʌtʃ / (BrE)
a type of friction clutch with cone-shaped friction surfaces, used to transmit power between shafts
mechanical clutch with cone-shaped surfaces

Examples

The vintage car was equipped with a cone clutch to provide smoother gear changes.
Mechanics replaced the worn-out cone clutch to restore the vehicle's transmission efficiency.
A cone clutch offers more frictional surface area compared to a simple disk clutch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: friction clutch - a clutch using friction to transmit power
