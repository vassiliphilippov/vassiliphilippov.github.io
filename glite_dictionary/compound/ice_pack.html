ice pack

1. NOUN

/ aɪs pæk / (AmE), / aɪs pæk / (BrE)
a waterproof bag or container filled with ice or a cold substance, used to reduce swelling, pain, or fever by applying it to the body
cold compress using ice for injuries

Examples

She placed an ice pack on her sprained ankle to reduce the swelling.
After hitting his head, the nurse gave him an ice pack to help with the bump.
It’s a good idea to keep an ice pack in your freezer in case of minor injuries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cold pack - a compress of cold material for injuries

2. NOUN

/ ˈaɪs ˌpæk / (AmE), / ˈaɪs ˌpak / (BrE)
A large area of floating ice masses that have become pressed together, typically found in polar or subpolar seas.
large area of floating sea ice

Examples

Satellite images showed the ice pack extending hundreds of miles from the Antarctic coast.
The ship became trapped in a thick ice pack north of Greenland.
During summer, the ice pack melts and breaks into smaller floes, opening up new areas of ocean.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pack ice - a large expanse of floating sea ice
