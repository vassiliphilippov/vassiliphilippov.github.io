yard bird

1. NOUN

/ ˈjɑrd ˌbɝd / (AmE), / ˈjɑːd ˌbɜːd / (BrE)
A military recruit who is assigned only menial tasks, especially as a form of punishment or discipline.
military recruit assigned menial tasks
slang

Examples

The sergeant ordered the yard bird to clean the latrines again.
Most of the yard birds spent their first weeks doing chores around the base.
He dreaded being called a yard bird because of all the tedious tasks involved.

2. NOUN

/ jɑrd bɝd / (AmE), / jɑːd bɜːd / (BrE)
(slang) A person serving a sentence in jail or prison; a prison inmate.
prison inmate (slang)
slang

Examples

After years as a yard bird, he finally got parole and tried to start a new life.
The guards caught two yard birds trying to sneak contraband during recreation time.
As a young yard bird, he learned quickly whom to trust and whom to avoid.
