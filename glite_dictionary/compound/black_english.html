Black English

1. NOUN

/ ˌblæk ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ / (AmE), / ˌblæk ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ / (BrE)
A variety of American English, often nonstandard, characteristically spoken by African Americans in the United States.
variety of American English spoken by African Americans

Examples

Many linguists study Black English because of its unique grammatical structures.
Some expressions in Black English differ greatly from Standard American English.
Educators are increasingly recognizing the value of Black English in the classroom.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: African American Vernacular English - AAVE; the most commonly used linguistic term for this dialect
  • Antonym: Standard American English - the standard form of American English
