male erectile dysfunction

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪl ɪˈrɛktaɪl dɪsˈfʌŋkʃən / (AmE), / ˈmeɪl ɪˈrɛktaɪl dɪsˈfʌŋkʃən / (BrE)
A medical condition in which a man is unable to achieve or maintain an erection of the penis suitable for sexual activity.
man's inability to have or maintain an erection
medical

Examples

Some men may experience male erectile dysfunction due to stress or underlying health conditions.
Doctors often discuss treatment options for male erectile dysfunction with their patients.
Advancements in medication have improved outcomes for those affected by male erectile dysfunction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impotence - inability to achieve or maintain an erection or to have an orgasm

Etymology

From Latin 'erectus' (upright) and Latin 'dysfunction' from Greek 'dus-' (bad, difficult) + 'function'.
