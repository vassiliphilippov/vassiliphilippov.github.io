carbon tetrachloride

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑrbən ˌtɛtrəˈklɔraɪd / (AmE), / ˌkɑːbən ˌtɛtrəˈklɔːraɪd / (BrE)
a colorless, toxic, nonflammable liquid formerly used as a solvent and in fire extinguishers, now largely discontinued due to health risks
toxic, colorless chemical compound

Examples

The use of carbon tetrachloride in fire extinguishers has been discontinued due to safety concerns.
Laboratory technicians handle carbon tetrachloride with care because of its toxic effects on the liver.
Decades ago, carbon tetrachloride was a common ingredient in household cleaning agents.

Etymology

Formed from 'carbon' and 'tetrachloride,' referring to its four chlorine atoms bonded to carbon.
