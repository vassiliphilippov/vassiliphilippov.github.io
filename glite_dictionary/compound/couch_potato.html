couch potato

1. NOUN

/ kaʊtʃ pəˈteɪtoʊ / (AmE), / kaʊtʃ pəˈteɪtəʊ / (BrE)
a person who spends a lot of time sitting and watching television, without taking much physical exercise
person who idly watches TV for long periods
informal american

Examples

After retiring, he became a total couch potato and hardly left the house.
Her doctor warned her not to live like a couch potato if she wanted to stay healthy.
Don't spend your weekend being a couch potato—let's go for a hike instead!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: idler - a lazy person who avoids work or activity
  • Antonym: active person - someone who is physically active or energetic

Etymology

Popularized in the United States in the late 1970s, formed from 'couch' (sofa) and 'potato' (suggesting inactivity).

2. NOUN

/ kaʊtʃ pəˈteɪɾoʊ / (AmE), / kaʊtʃ pəˈteɪtəʊ / (BrE)
a person who spends a lot of time sitting or lying down, usually watching television, and does not take much physical exercise
inactive person who watches TV a lot
informal

Examples

My brother is such a couch potato that he spends hours every evening watching TV.
Instead of being a couch potato on weekends, try going for a walk or playing a sport.
Mary decided to stop being a couch potato and joined a local gym.

Synset Examples

She was a couch potato all weekend, binge-watching shows.
After work, I turn into a couch potato and binge-watch series.
Don't be such a potato, get up and dance!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slacker - a person who avoids work or effort
  • Antonym: active - a person who is energetic and involved in physical activities

Etymology

Originated in U.S. slang in the 1970s, modeled humorously after food terms like 'couch tomato'.
