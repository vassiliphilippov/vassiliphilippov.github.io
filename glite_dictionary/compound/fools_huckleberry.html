fool's huckleberry

1. NOUN

/ fulz ˈhʌkəlˌbɛri / (AmE), / fuːlz ˈhʌkəlˌbɛri / (BrE)
A straggling shrub native to northwestern North America, noted for its bluish-tinged foliage and umbels of small, bell-shaped flowers.
Northwestern North American shrub with bluish foliage

Examples

In the dense understory, we spotted a fool's huckleberry covered in delicate pink blossoms.
The bluish leaves of the fool's huckleberry make it easy to identify among other shrubs.
Hikers are warned not to confuse fool's huckleberry with edible wild berries.
