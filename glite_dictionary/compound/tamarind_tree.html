tamarind tree

1. NOUN

/ ˈtæmərɪnd triː / (AmE), / ˈtæmərɪnd triː / (BrE)
A tropical evergreen tree with feathery leaves and fragrant flowers, producing long pods with edible acidic pulp.
tropical tree with edible acidic pods

Examples

The tamarind tree in the backyard provides welcome shade during hot afternoons.
Locals gather the pods from the tamarind tree to make delicious chutneys and drinks.
A giant tamarind tree stood at the entrance of the old village temple.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tamarind - the edible, acidic fruit of the tamarind tree

Etymology

From Arabic 'tamr hindī' (Indian date), referring to the tree's pod; 'tree' from Old English 'trēow'.
