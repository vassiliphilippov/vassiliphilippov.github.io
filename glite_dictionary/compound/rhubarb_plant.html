rhubarb plant

1. NOUN

/ ˈruˌbɑrb plænt / (AmE), / ˈruːˌbɑːb plɑːnt / (BrE)
a perennial plant with long green or reddish leafstalks, edible when cooked, while the leaves are poisonous
plant with edible stalks and poisonous leaves

Examples

The rhubarb plant thrives in cool climates and can be harvested every spring.
She grew a healthy rhubarb plant in her backyard for homemade pies.
Never eat the leaves of a rhubarb plant, as they contain toxic compounds.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: rhubarb - the edible stalks of the rhubarb plant, used in cooking
  • Synonym: dock - a plant in the same family (Polygonaceae) with similar leaf shape

Etymology

From Greek *rha* (rhubarb) and *barbaron* (foreign), influenced by Middle English and Latin 'rheubarbarum'.
