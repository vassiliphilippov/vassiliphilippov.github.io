Tarawa-Makin

1. PROPN

/ təˈrɑːwə ˈmɑːkɪn / (AmE), / təˈrɑːwə ˈmakɪn / (BrE)
the battles in World War II fought in November 1943 in the Pacific, where United States Marines captured the Tarawa and Makin islands from Japanese forces after intense fighting.
WWII Pacific battles (Tarawa, Makin, 1943)

Examples

The fierce combat at Tarawa-Makin marked a pivotal moment in the Pacific campaign.
Historians often study the tactics used during the Tarawa-Makin operations to understand amphibious assaults.
Memorial services are held annually to honor those who fought at Tarawa-Makin.

Etymology

Named after the Tarawa and Makin atolls in the Gilbert Islands, central Pacific.
