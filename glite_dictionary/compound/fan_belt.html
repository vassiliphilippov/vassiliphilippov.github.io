fan belt

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæn ˌbɛlt / (AmE), / ˈfan ˌbɛlt / (BrE)
A belt in a car engine that is driven by the crankshaft and turns a fan to pull air through the radiator, helping to cool the engine.
belt in a car engine that drives the fan

Examples

The mechanic said the fan belt needed to be replaced because it was worn out.
If your fan belt breaks while driving, your engine may overheat quickly.
I heard a squealing noise and realized the fan belt was slipping.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: serpentine belt - a single, continuous belt used to drive multiple devices in an engine
