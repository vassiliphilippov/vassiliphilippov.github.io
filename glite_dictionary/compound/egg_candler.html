egg candler

1. NOUN

/ ɛɡ ˈkændlər / (AmE), / ɛɡ ˈkændlə / (BrE)
A device with a light and shield that lets only a small spot of light shine through, used to inspect the interior quality of eggs.
device for inspecting eggs with light

Examples

The farmer used an egg candler to check for cracks and imperfections in the shells.
Before packing, each egg was examined with an egg candler to ensure its quality.
An egg candler helps determine if an egg is fertilized without breaking the shell.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: candler - a device for shining light to inspect objects
