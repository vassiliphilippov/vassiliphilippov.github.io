spread-eagle

1. ADJ

/ ˈsprɛd ˌiɡəl / (AmE), / ˈsprɛd ˌiːɡl / (BrE)
(of a person or animal) with arms and legs stretched out wide apart, especially when lying down or in the air.
position with limbs spread out wide

Examples

The hiker lay spread-eagle on the grass, gazing up at the sky.
After leaping off the diving board, she hovered spread-eagle before hitting the water.
The actor collapsed spread-eagle on the stage during the dramatic finale.

Synset Examples

The gymnast executed a perfect spread-eagle in mid-air.
He fell spread-eagle into the snow, creating a snow angel.

Etymology

From 'spread' + 'eagle', likening the limb position to an eagle with wings outstretched.
