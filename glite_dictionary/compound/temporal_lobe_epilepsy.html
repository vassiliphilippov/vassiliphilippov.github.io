temporal lobe epilepsy

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛmpərəl loʊb ɛˈlɛpsi / (AmE), / ˈtɛmpərəl ləʊb ɪˈlɛpsi / (BrE)
A form of epilepsy originating in the temporal lobe of the brain, typically marked by impaired consciousness, memory lapses, and sometimes unusual automatic movements or hallucinations.
epileptic disorder of the temporal lobe
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed her with temporal lobe epilepsy after several episodes of memory loss and confusion.
Unusual sensations and repetitive movements are classic symptoms of temporal lobe epilepsy.
Certain medications are effective in managing temporal lobe epilepsy and reducing the frequency of seizures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: epilepsy - A neurological disorder marked by sudden seizures

Etymology

From 'temporal lobe' (a region of the brain) + 'epilepsy' (from Greek 'epilēpsia', a seizure).
