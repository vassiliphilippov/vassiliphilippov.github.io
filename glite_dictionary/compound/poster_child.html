poster child

1. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊstər tʃaɪld / (AmE), / ˈpəʊstə tʃaɪld / (BrE)
A child who represents a cause, especially one affected by a disease or condition and featured in fundraising campaigns to generate public support or donations.
representative child for a cause or charity

Examples

The young girl became the poster child for the charity's cancer awareness campaign.
He was once seen as the poster child for overcoming adversity in the community.
Their son became a poster child for the national diabetes association.

Synset Examples

she was the poster child for muscular dystrophy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: symbol - something that represents or stands for something else, especially a concept

Etymology

From 'poster' (a public display sign) and 'child'; originally referring to children featured on posters for causes from the mid-20th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊstər tʃaɪld / (AmE), / ˈpəʊstə tʃaɪld / (BrE)
A person or thing that is seen as a typical or prominent example of a particular cause, movement, or ideal.
typical example of a cause or movement

Examples

After his speech, she became the poster child for mental health awareness.
The company is now a poster child for innovation in the tech sector.
He's often cited as the poster child of success in the face of adversity.

Synset Examples

The initiative has become a poster child for community service.
He's the poster child for the environmental movement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exemplar - a person or thing serving as a typical example or excellent model
  • Synonym: icon - a person or thing regarded as a representative symbol or as worthy of veneration

Etymology

From the use of children on posters to promote charitable causes, especially medical or social campaigns, in the 20th century.
