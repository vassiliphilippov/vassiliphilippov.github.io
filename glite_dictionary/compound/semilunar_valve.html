semilunar valve

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛməˌlunɚ ˈvælv / (AmE), / ˌsɛmiˌljuːnɑː ˈvælv / (BrE)
Either of two valves in the heart, each with crescent-shaped cusps, which prevent blood from flowing backward into the heart.
crescent-shaped heart valve
medical

Examples

The semilunar valve closes to prevent blood from re-entering the heart after contraction.
A defect in a semilunar valve can lead to significant cardiovascular problems.
During each heartbeat, the semilunar valve ensures one-way blood flow from the ventricles.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: atrioventricular valve - a valve located between the atrium and ventricle of the heart; prevents backflow into atria
  • Synonym: aortic valve - a semilunar valve between the left ventricle and the aorta
  • Synonym: pulmonary valve - a semilunar valve between the right ventricle and the pulmonary artery

Etymology

From Latin 'semilunaris' (crescent-shaped); 'semi' (half) + 'luna' (moon).
