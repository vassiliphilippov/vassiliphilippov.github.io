adhesive friction

1. NOUN

/ ədˈhɪsɪv ˈfrɪkʃən / (AmE), / ədˈhiːsɪv ˈfrɪkʃən / (BrE)
the frictional force that resists the movement of a body across the surface it rests on, due to adhesion between the surfaces
friction from adhesion between surfaces

Examples

The adhesive friction between the tires and the road prevents the car from skidding.
Scientists study adhesive friction to develop better materials for brake pads.
Good adhesive friction is essential for athletes running on synthetic tracks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sliding friction - friction that occurs when two surfaces slide over each other

Etymology

From 'adhesive' (from Latin 'adhaesivus', tending to stick) + 'friction' (from Latin 'frictio', a rubbing).
