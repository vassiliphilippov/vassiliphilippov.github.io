bamboo palm

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæmˌbu ˌpɑm / (AmE), / ˈbæmbuː ˌpɑːm / (BrE)
a tropical palm of the genus Raffia, known for its slender, bamboo-like stems, often grown as a houseplant
tropical palm with bamboo-like stems

Examples

Many people grow the bamboo palm indoors because it thrives in low light.
The slender, cane-like stems of the bamboo palm add a tropical feel to any room.
Botanists classify the bamboo palm under the genus Raffia due to its unique appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: raffia palm - a palm of the genus Raffia, source of raffia fiber

2. NOUN

/ bæmˈbu pɑm / (AmE), / bamˈbuː pɑːm / (BrE)
A small, graceful palm with reedlike stems and loose, coarse fibers at the base of its leaves, often grown as a houseplant.
small palm plant with reedlike stems

Examples

The bamboo palm is a popular choice for adding greenery to offices and homes.
She placed a bamboo palm in the corner to brighten up her living room.
Unlike other palms, the bamboo palm thrives in low-light indoor environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parlor palm - a popular small indoor palm plant
