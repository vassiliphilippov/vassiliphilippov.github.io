contact lens

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑntækt lɛnz / (AmE), / ˈkɒntækt lɛnz / (BrE)
A thin, curved lens made of glass or plastic that is placed directly on the surface of the eye to correct vision or deliver medication.
lens worn on eye to correct vision

Examples

I find that wearing a contact lens is more comfortable than glasses.
She lost her contact lens while swimming in the pool.
The doctor prescribed a new type of contact lens for my astigmatism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spectacles - devices consisting of lenses mounted in a frame and worn on the nose and ears to correct or protect eyesight

Etymology

From 'contact', meaning direct touch, and 'lens', from Latin 'lens' (lentil, lens).
