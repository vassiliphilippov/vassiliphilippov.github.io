genus Polygonia

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs pəˌlɪɡˈoʊ.ni.ə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs pəˌlɪɡˈəʊ.nɪ.ə / (BrE)
A genus of butterflies known as comma butterflies, characterized by angular wing shapes and distinctive comma-shaped markings.
genus of comma butterflies

Examples

The genus Polygonia includes several species with wings that have ragged, angular edges.
Scientists studied the mating habits of butterflies within the genus Polygonia.
The distinctive comma-shaped marking is a key feature in identifying members of the genus Polygonia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: comma butterfly - a common name for butterflies of the genus Polygonia, noted for comma-shaped marks

Etymology

From New Latin 'Polygonia', from Greek 'poly-' (many) and 'gonia' (angle), referring to the angular wings.
