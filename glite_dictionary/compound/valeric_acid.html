valeric acid

1. NOUN

/ vəˈlɛrɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / vəˈlɛrɪk ˈasɪd / (BrE)
a clear, oily carboxylic acid with a strong odor, used in the manufacture of perfumes and pharmaceuticals
a carboxylic acid used in perfumes and drugs

Examples

The laboratory synthesized valeric acid for use in creating a new fragrance.
Perfume manufacturers value valeric acid for its strong and distinctive aroma.
Researchers studied the effects of valeric acid on human cells in medical trials.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pentanoic acid - another chemical name for valeric acid

Etymology

From Latin 'valeriana' (the plant valerian) where it was first obtained + 'acid'.
