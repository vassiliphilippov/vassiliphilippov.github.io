spur blight

1. NOUN

/ spɝː blaɪt / (AmE), / spɜː blaɪt / (BrE)
A fungal disease affecting raspberries, causing wilting and dark lesions on the canes, primarily near the leaf base.
raspberry disease causing cane lesions

Examples

Raspberry growers fear spur blight because it can significantly reduce crop yield.
Symptoms of spur blight include brownish lesions on the stems near the leaf joints.
Controlling spur blight requires removing infected canes and improving air circulation in the patch.
