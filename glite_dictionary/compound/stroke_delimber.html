stroke delimber

1. NOUN

/ stroʊk dɪˈlɪmbər / (AmE), / strəʊk dɪˈlɪmbə / (BrE)
A machine that removes limbs from felled trees using a saw or flailing chains, commonly used in forestry operations.
machine for removing branches from trees

Examples

The logging company invested in a new stroke delimber to speed up their tree processing operation.
A stroke delimber can efficiently remove branches from trees right at the harvesting site.
After felling, the operator used a stroke delimber to prepare the logs for transport.

Etymology

From 'stroke', relating to the back-and-forth motion of the cutting head, and 'delimber', a forestry tool for removing limbs.
