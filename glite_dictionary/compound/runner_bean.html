runner bean

1. NOUN

/ ˈrʌnɚ ˌbin / (AmE), / ˈrʌnə ˌbiːn / (BrE)
a climbing tropical American bean plant (Phaseolus coccineus) with red flowers, grown for its edible pods and seeds, especially popular in Britain
a climbing bean plant with edible pods
british

Examples

She added fresh runner beans from the garden to the salad.
In the UK, runner beans are a staple vegetable during the summer months.
The bright red flowers of the runner bean plant attract bees and butterflies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: green bean - a common bean with edible green pods

2. NOUN

/ ˈrʌnər ˌbin / (AmE), / ˈrʌnə ˌbiːn / (BrE)
A type of climbing bean, especially popular in Britain, with long green pods typically sliced and cooked as a vegetable.
type of long green bean, often eaten in Britain
british

Examples

She picked a bowlful of fresh runner beans from the garden for dinner.
Runner beans are often served sliced and lightly steamed with a knob of butter.
The allotment was full of healthy runner bean plants climbing up their supports.
