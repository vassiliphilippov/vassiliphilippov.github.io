interstitial cell-stimulating hormone

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪntɚˈstɪʃəl sɛl stɪˈmjuleɪtɪŋ ˈhɔrmoʊn / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈstɪʃəl sɛl stɪˈmjuːleɪtɪŋ ˈhɔːməʊn / (BrE)
a hormone secreted by the anterior pituitary gland that stimulates ovulation in females and the release of androgens in males
pituitary hormone affecting ovulation and androgen release
medical

Examples

The interstitial cell-stimulating hormone plays a crucial role in regulating the reproductive systems of mammals.
Medical researchers study interstitial cell-stimulating hormone levels to understand fertility issues.
An imbalance of interstitial cell-stimulating hormone can impact ovulation and testosterone production.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: luteinizing hormone - another name for the same hormone

Etymology

From 'interstitial cell' (cells located between other structures) and 'stimulating hormone', describing its function.
