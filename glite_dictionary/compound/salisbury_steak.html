Salisbury steak

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɔlz.bɚi steɪk, ˈsælz.bə.ri steɪk / (AmE), / ˈsɒlz.bəri steɪk, ˈsɔːlz.bəri steɪk / (BrE)
a dish consisting of a seasoned ground beef patty, typically served with a sauce or gravy
ground beef patty with sauce

Examples

I ordered the Salisbury steak with mashed potatoes for dinner last night.
The cafeteria often serves Salisbury steak covered in rich brown gravy.
Her homemade Salisbury steak tastes just like the classic comfort food from childhood.

Etymology

Named after Dr. James H. Salisbury (1823–1905), American physician who promoted this style of meat dish.
