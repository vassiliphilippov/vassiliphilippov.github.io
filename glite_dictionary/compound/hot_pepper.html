hot pepper

1. NOUN

/ hɑt ˈpɛpər / (AmE), / hɒt ˈpɛpə / (BrE)
A plant that produces medium-sized, very spicy oblong red peppers, chiefly grown for use in hot sauce.
a spicy pepper-producing plant

Examples

She added finely chopped hot pepper to give the salsa a fiery kick.
The farm specializes in growing hot pepper varieties used for making hot sauce.
He warned me not to touch my eyes after handling a hot pepper.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chili - a hot-tasting pod of a variety of capsicum, used in sauces or as a spice
  • Antonym: sweet pepper - a mild variety of pepper with a sweet flavor, not hot

2. NOUN

/ hɑt ˈpɛpər / (AmE), / hɒt ˈpɛpə / (BrE)
any of various types of pungent chili peppers belonging to the Capsicum genus, known for their spicy flavor
spicy chili pepper variety

Examples

She added some chopped hot pepper to the sauce to give it a kick.
Beware of touching your eyes after slicing a hot pepper without gloves.
Different varieties of hot peppers can be found at the local farmers' market.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chili pepper - another term for a spicy pepper from the Capsicum genus
  • Antonym: sweet pepper - a mild, non-pungent variety of Capsicum fruit
