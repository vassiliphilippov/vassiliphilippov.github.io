grunt work

1. NOUN

/ ɡrʌnt wɝːk / (AmE), / ɡrʌnt wɜːk / (BrE)
tedious, menial, or physically demanding tasks that are considered uninteresting or routine
tedious or menial tasks
informal

Examples

After being hired as an intern, she spent most of her time doing grunt work for the department.
Although he disliked the grunt work, he knew it was necessary to gain experience.
Volunteers are needed to handle the grunt work of setting up the event.

Synset Examples

He was tired of the grunt work at his job.
I spent the afternoon doing grunt work in the garden.
She believes everyone should do their share of grunt work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drudgery - hard, menial, or tedious work
  • Synonym: chores - routine household tasks
  • Degree Higher: office work - work done in an office, often considered routine

Etymology

From 'grunt' (imitative of low animal sounds, also meaning low-ranking worker) + 'work'.
