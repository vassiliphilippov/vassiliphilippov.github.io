buyer's market

1. NOUN

/ ˈbaɪ.ɚz ˌmɑrkɪt / (AmE), / ˈbaɪ.əz ˌmɑːkɪt / (BrE)
A market situation in which supply exceeds demand, giving purchasers an advantage over sellers in price negotiations.
market with more sellers than buyers

Examples

Ever since the recession, it's been a buyer's market in real estate, with prices falling steadily.
Thanks to the surplus of cars for sale, shoppers are now enjoying a buyer's market.
When job openings decline and candidate numbers rise, the employment field can turn into a buyer's market.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: seller's market - a market in which demand exceeds supply, favoring sellers
