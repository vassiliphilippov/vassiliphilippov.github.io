pudding berry

1. NOUN

/ ˈpʊdɪŋ ˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈpʊdɪŋ ˌbɛri / (BrE)
a creeping perennial herb with red berries and clustered leaf whorls at the tips of its shoots, native to regions from Greenland to Alaska
creeping herb with red berries

Examples

The pudding berry grows abundantly in the cold tundra regions of Alaska.
Researchers are studying how the pudding berry adapts to harsh Arctic climates.
Bears are known to eat the bright red pudding berry during the short northern summer.
