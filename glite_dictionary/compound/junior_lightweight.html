junior lightweight

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒun.jɚ ˈlaɪtˌweɪt / (AmE), / ˈdʒuː.njə ˈlaɪt.weɪt / (BrE)
A weight class in boxing for competitors who weigh no more than 130 pounds (59 kilograms).
boxing weight class under 130 pounds

Examples

He won the junior lightweight title after defeating his opponent in the final round.
The junior lightweight division is known for its fast-paced and technical boxing matches.
Several champions have successfully moved up from junior lightweight to higher weight classes during their careers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lightweight - a weight class in boxing above featherweight and below welterweight, limit varies
