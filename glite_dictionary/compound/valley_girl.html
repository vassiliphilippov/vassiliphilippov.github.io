valley girl

1. NOUN

/ ˈvæli ɡɜrl / (AmE), / ˈvæli ɡɜːl / (BrE)
a girl or young woman, especially from the San Fernando Valley in California, stereotyped as being fashionable, materialistic, and speaking in a distinctive manner
stereotyped young woman from San Fernando Valley
informal american

Examples

Many people associate the valley girl accent with uptalk and the use of the word 'like' as a filler.
In the 1980s, the valley girl stereotype became popular thanks to movies and pop culture.
Her speech and fashion sense made her seem like a typical valley girl to her classmates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: preppy - a young person associated with a conventional, upper-middle-class lifestyle
  • Degree Higher: hipster - a person who follows the latest trends and fashions, especially those regarded as outside the cultural mainstream

Etymology

From San Fernando Valley, California + girl; popularised in the 1980s.

2. NOUN

/ ˈvæli ɡɜrl / (AmE), / ˈvæli ɡɜːl / (BrE)
(informal) A stereotype of a young woman from California, especially the San Fernando Valley, noted for an affected manner of speech, social preoccupations, and a distinctive accent.
stereotypical young Californian woman, distinctive speech
informal american

Examples

She talks like a total valley girl whenever she's excited.
The main character was portrayed as a typical valley girl in the film.
People often mimic the speech of a valley girl for comedic effect.

Synset Examples

The movie featured a classic valley girl character.
Her valley girl accent was hard to ignore.
She always used 'like' in her valley girl speech.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: preppy - a stereotype of a young, affluent person with a fashion-conscious and conventional style

Etymology

From the San Fernando Valley area of California, referring to young women from that region.
