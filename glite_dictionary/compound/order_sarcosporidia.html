order Sarcosporidia

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdɚ sɑrˌkoʊspəˈrɪdiə / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə ˌsɑːkəʊspəˈrɪdiə / (BrE)
a taxonomic order of imperfectly known protozoan parasites that infect the muscles of vertebrates
order of parasitic protozoans infecting muscles

Examples

The muscle tissue of the deer was found to be infected with parasites from the order Sarcosporidia.
Research on the order Sarcosporidia has revealed new insights into their life cycle.
Veterinarians monitor outbreaks of diseases caused by members of the order Sarcosporidia.

Etymology

From Greek 'sarx' meaning 'flesh' and 'sporos' meaning 'seed'.
