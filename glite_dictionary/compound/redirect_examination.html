redirect examination

1. NOUN

/ ˌridɪˈrɛkt ˌɛɡzæməˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːdɪˈrɛkt ˌɛɡzæmɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
(law) The questioning of a witness by the party that called them, after cross-examination by the opposing party.
witness questioning after cross-examination (law)
legal

Examples

The attorney began the redirect examination to clarify points raised during the cross-examination.
On redirect examination, the witness explained her previous statement in greater detail.
After cross-examination, the prosecution opted not to conduct a redirect examination.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cross-examination - the questioning of a witness by the opposing party in a court case
  • Degree Lower: direct examination - the initial questioning of a witness by the party who called them
