to becloud someone's judgment

1. VERB

/ bɪˈklaʊd ˈsʌmwʌnz ˈdʒʌdʒmənt / (AmE), / bɪˈklaʊd ˈsʌmwʌnz ˈdʒʌdʒmənt / (BrE)
to make someone's ability to judge or decide clearly less effective or more confused
to obscure someone's judgment

Examples

She feared that personal feelings might becloud her judgment during the negotiation.
Grief can easily becloud someone's judgment in a difficult situation.
Ambition sometimes beclouds people's judgment when making important choices.

Synset Examples

His emotions began to becloud his judgment.
Don't let doubt becloud your decision-making.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cloud one's judgment - to make someone's judgment less clear or objective

Etymology

From 'becloud' (to obscure with clouds), based on 'cloud' + 'be-' (Old English intensifying prefix).
