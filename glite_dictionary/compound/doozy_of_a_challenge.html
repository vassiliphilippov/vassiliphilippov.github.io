doozy of a challenge

1. NOUN

/ ˈduzi ʌv ə ˈtʃælɪndʒ / (AmE), / ˈduːzi ɒv ə ˈtʃælɪndʒ / (BrE)
an extremely difficult or exceptional challenge or task
an unusually difficult task
informal american

Examples

Completing the marathon in such hot weather was a doozy of a challenge for all the runners.
The final exam turned out to be a real doozy of a challenge, stumping even the best students.
Rebuilding the old car was a doozy of a challenge, but she managed to finish it.

Synset Examples

He faced a doozy of a challenge at work today.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: difficult task - a task that is hard to accomplish
  • Antonym: easy task - a task that is simple or straightforward

Etymology

From American English slang 'doozy' (something extraordinary), probably from 'daisy' as a term for excellence.
