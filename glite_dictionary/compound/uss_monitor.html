USS Monitor

1. PROPN

/ ˌjuː ɛs ɛs ˈmɑnɪtər / (AmE), / ˌjuː ɛs ɛs ˈmɒnɪtə / (BrE)
the first ironclad warship commissioned by the United States Navy, famous for its battle with the CSS Virginia during the American Civil War
first US ironclad warship in Civil War
american

Examples

The USS Monitor played a crucial role in the Battle of Hampton Roads in 1862.
Many historians consider the USS Monitor to be a turning point in naval warfare due to its ironclad design.
After its historic confrontation, the USS Monitor became an icon of Civil War innovation.

Etymology

USS stands for 'United States Ship'; 'Monitor' was chosen to indicate the vessel's purpose as a warning to the Confederacy.
