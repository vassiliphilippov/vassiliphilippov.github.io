hyaline membrane disease

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪəˌliːn məˈmbreɪn dɪˈziːz / (AmE), / ˈhaɪəliːn mɛmˈbreɪn dɪˈziːz / (BrE)
A serious lung condition in newborns, especially premature infants, where the lungs do not expand properly due to a lack of surfactant, leading to difficulty breathing and the formation of a glassy membrane over the alveoli.
lung disorder in newborns, esp. premature infants
medical

Examples

Premature babies are at a high risk of developing hyaline membrane disease due to their underdeveloped lungs.
Doctors administered surfactant therapy to treat the infant's hyaline membrane disease.
Advancements in neonatal care have greatly reduced deaths from hyaline membrane disease.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: respiratory distress syndrome - acute lung condition in newborns caused by insufficient surfactant

Etymology

From 'hyaline' (glassy, transparent), referring to the appearance of the membrane, and 'membrane disease'.
