Guadalupe Island

1. PROPN

/ ˌɡwɑdəˈluːpeɪ ˈaɪlənd / (AmE), / ˌɡwɑːdəˈluːpeɪ ˈaɪlənd / (BrE)
a Mexican island in the Pacific Ocean, located west of Baja California, known for its unique wildlife and rugged landscape
island off the west coast of Baja California, Mexico

Examples

Scientists traveled to Guadalupe Island to study its population of great white sharks.
Guadalupe Island is famous for its stunning volcanic cliffs and rare plant species.
The boat departed early in the morning, heading for Guadalupe Island in the Pacific Ocean.

Etymology

From Spanish 'Isla Guadalupe', named after Our Lady of Guadalupe.
