shelf life

1. NOUN

/ ʃɛlf laɪf / (AmE), / ʃɛlf laɪf / (BrE)
The length of time that a product, especially food or medicine, remains suitable for use, sale, or consumption.
period a product remains usable

Examples

Most canned foods have a shelf life of two to five years if stored properly.
The shelf life of this medicine is indicated on the packaging.
Proper storage conditions can extend the shelf life of fresh produce.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expiration date - the date after which a product should not be used
  • Synonym: best before - date indicating when food is at its best quality

Etymology

From 'shelf' (place where goods are stored) and 'life' (duration of existence); first used in the 1920s relating to food products.

2. NOUN

/ ʃɛlf laɪf / (AmE), / ʃɛlf laɪf / (BrE)
the length of time that a product, especially food or medicine, remains suitable for use or sale
period product remains usable

Examples

Always check the shelf life before purchasing medications or packaged foods.
This particular paint has a shelf life of two years if stored properly.
Freezing can significantly extend the shelf life of many food products.

Synset Examples

Check the shelf life before buying canned goods.

This product has a long shelf life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expiry date - the date after which a product should not be used
  • Synonym: use-by date - the recommended last date for using a product

Etymology

Formed from 'shelf' (a flat storage surface) and 'life'; first recorded use in the 20th century related to product storage.
