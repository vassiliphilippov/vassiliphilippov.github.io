article of faith

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːr.tɪ.kəl əv feɪθ / (AmE), / ˈɑː.tɪ.kəl əv feɪθ / (BrE)
a statement or principle, often from a creed or doctrine, regarded as an essential belief, especially in Christianity; one of the distinct sections into which such a creed is divided
a principle or section of a creed or doctrine

Examples

The Nicene Creed contains several articles of faith that are central to Christian doctrine.
Belief in the resurrection of Jesus is an article of faith for many Christians.
Accepting certain teachings as articles of faith is essential in belonging to the church.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: creed - a formal statement of religious beliefs

Etymology

From Latin 'articulus fidei', meaning a point or part of faith.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɑːr.t̬ə.kəl əv feɪθ / (AmE), / ˈɑː.tɪ.kəl əv feɪθ / (BrE)
a belief or doctrine that is accepted without question or proof, especially within a particular group or religion
unquestioned belief, often religious

Examples

It is an article of faith among her followers that she can do no wrong.
The idea that the market always corrects itself is almost an article of faith in economics.
For many, the right to free speech is an article of faith not to be compromised.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dogma - a principle or set of principles laid down by an authority as incontrovertibly true
  • Synonym: tenet - a principle or belief, especially one of the main principles of a religion or philosophy
