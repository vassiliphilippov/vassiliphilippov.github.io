chromosomal abnormality

1. NOUN

/ kroʊˈmoʊ.səməl æbˌnɔrˈmæləti / (AmE), / krəˈməʊ.sə.məl æbˌnɔːˈmaləti / (BrE)
a change in the normal structure or number of chromosomes, often resulting in physical or mental disorders
change in chromosomes causing disorders
medical

Examples

Down syndrome is a common chromosomal abnormality resulting from an extra copy of chromosome 21.
Doctors detected a chromosomal abnormality during prenatal testing.
Some forms of infertility are linked to a subtle chromosomal abnormality present in one of the parents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: genetic disorder - a disease caused by abnormalities in an individual's genetic material
  • Antonym: normal karyotype - the standard chromosomal constitution of a species

Etymology

From chromosome + abnormality; 'chromosome' from Greek 'khrōma' (color) + 'sōma' (body).
