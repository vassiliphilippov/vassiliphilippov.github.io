genus Staphylococcus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌstæf.ɪ.ləˈkɑː.kəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌstæf.ɪ.ləˈkɒk.əs / (BrE)
A taxonomic group of spherical, Gram-positive bacteria, many species of which are pathogenic, including those causing boils, pneumonia, and food poisoning.
group of pathogenic spherical bacteria
medical

Examples

The genus Staphylococcus includes several species responsible for common skin infections.
Research into the genus Staphylococcus has led to better treatments for bacterial diseases.
Some members of the genus Staphylococcus are resistant to antibiotics, making infections difficult to treat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Staphylococcus - Any bacterium of the genus Staphylococcus; spherical bacteria forming clusters

Etymology

From New Latin 'Staphylococcus', based on Greek 'staphylē' (bunch of grapes) and 'kokkos' (berry), referring to the bacteria's clustered appearance.
