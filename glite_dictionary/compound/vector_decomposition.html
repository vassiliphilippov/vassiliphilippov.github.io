vector decomposition

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɛktər ˌdiːˌkɑmpəˈzɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈvɛktə ˌdiːˌkɒmpəˈzɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of breaking a vector or vector field into component parts, usually along specified directions or axes
breaking a vector into components

Examples

Engineers often use vector decomposition to analyze the forces acting on a bridge.
Understanding vector decomposition is essential for solving problems in electromagnetism.
By applying vector decomposition, the wind’s effect was divided into north-south and east-west components.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vector analysis - the branch of mathematics dealing with vectors and operations on them

Etymology

From Latin 'vector' (carrier) and Latin 'decomponere' (to separate into parts).
