sergeant first class

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɝː.dʒənt ˌfɝːst ˈklæs / (AmE), / ˌsɑː.dʒənt ˌfɜːst ˈklɑːs / (BrE)
a non-commissioned officer in the US Army ranked above staff sergeant and below master sergeant
US Army non-commissioned officer rank

Examples

After twenty years of service, she was promoted to sergeant first class in the Army.
A sergeant first class typically supervises a platoon or serves as a senior advisor.
The responsibilities of a sergeant first class involve both leadership and training duties.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: staff sergeant - a non-commissioned officer rank just below sergeant first class
  • Degree Higher: master sergeant - the non-commissioned officer rank just above sergeant first class
