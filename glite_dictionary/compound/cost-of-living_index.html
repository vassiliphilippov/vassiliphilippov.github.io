cost-of-living index

1. NOUN

/ kɑst əv ˈlɪvɪŋ ˈɪndɛks / (AmE), / kɒst əv ˈlɪvɪŋ ˈɪndɛks / (BrE)
An index that measures the average cost of goods and services required by a typical consumer, used to track changes in the cost of maintaining a certain standard of living over time.
measure of average consumer expenses

Examples

The government uses the cost-of-living index to adjust pensions for inflation.
Rising prices pushed the cost-of-living index to its highest point in a decade.
Many wage contracts are tied to changes in the cost-of-living index.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: consumer price index - an index measuring the average change in prices paid by consumers for goods and services
