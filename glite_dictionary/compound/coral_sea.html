Coral Sea

1. PROPN

/ ˈkɔrəl si / (AmE), / ˈkɒrəl siː / (BrE)
the first naval battle in history fought entirely by aircraft based on carriers, taking place in May 1942 during World War II between Japanese and Allied forces, resulting in a Japanese defeat
naval battle in World War II (May 1942)

Examples

The Battle of the Coral Sea marked a turning point in the Pacific War.
Historians often analyze the strategies employed during the Coral Sea engagement.
The outcome of the Coral Sea battle helped prevent further Japanese expansion in the region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Battle of Midway - major naval battle in the Pacific Theater of World War II

2. PROPN

/ ˈkɔrəl ˌsi / (AmE), / ˈkɒrəl ˌsiː / (BrE)
a marginal sea of the South Pacific Ocean, located northeast of Australia, bordered by the Great Barrier Reef.
sea northeast of Australia

Examples

The Coral Sea is famous for its stunning marine biodiversity, especially around the Great Barrier Reef.
During World War II, the Battle of the Coral Sea was a crucial turning point in the Pacific theater.
Many tourists visit Australia to go diving in the Coral Sea and explore its vibrant coral reefs.
