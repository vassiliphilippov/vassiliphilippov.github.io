special drawing rights

1. NOUN

/ ˈspɛʃəl ˈdrɔɪɪŋ raɪts / (AmE), / ˈspɛʃəl ˈdrɔːɪŋ raɪts / (BrE)
reserve assets created by the International Monetary Fund (IMF) to supplement member countries' official reserves and support stability in the foreign exchange market
IMF reserve assets supplementing official reserves

Examples

The IMF allocated additional special drawing rights to help countries cope with the economic crisis.
Many developing nations depend on special drawing rights to bolster their foreign currency reserves.
At the meeting, officials discussed the benefits of increasing special drawing rights for member countries.
