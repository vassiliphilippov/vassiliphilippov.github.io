staircase wit

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɛrˌkeɪs wɪt / (AmE), / ˈstɛəkɛɪs wɪt / (BrE)
the occurrence of thinking of a clever or witty remark too late, especially after the situation has passed
thinking of a clever reply too late

Examples

He only thought of the perfect comeback as staircase wit hours after the argument was over.
Samantha often suffers from staircase wit, wishing she had spoken up in meetings sooner.
That hilarious comment is just staircase wit now, since the conversation already moved on.

Synset Examples

He realized his staircase wit too late after the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: afterwit - the realization of a witty remark too late

Etymology

Translation of French 'esprit d'escalier' (wit of the staircase), referring to thinking of a retort on the stairs after leaving a gathering.
