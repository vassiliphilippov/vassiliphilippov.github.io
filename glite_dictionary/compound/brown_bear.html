brown bear

1. NOUN

/ ˈbraʊn ˌbɛr / (AmE), / ˈbraʊn ˌbeə / (BrE)
a large, powerful bear found in Eurasia and North America, known for its brown fur and formidable size
large Eurasian and North American bear

Examples

We watched a brown bear fishing for salmon in the river.
The brown bear can weigh up to 700 kilograms in the wild.
Researchers tracked the movements of several brown bears across the forest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grizzly bear - a subspecies of brown bear found in North America
  • Antonym: polar bear - a large white bear native to the Arctic
