inborn error of metabolism

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪnˌbɔrn ˈɛrər əv məˈtæbəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌɪnˌbɔːn ˈɛrə əv məˈtæbəlɪzəm / (BrE)
a disease caused by an inherited defect, usually a missing or insufficient enzyme, leading to abnormal metabolism
genetic metabolic disorder
medical

Examples

Phenylketonuria is a classic example of an inborn error of metabolism.
Early diagnosis of an inborn error of metabolism can be crucial for effective treatment.
Doctors screened the newborn for various inborn errors of metabolism after noticing unusual symptoms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: metabolic disorder - a disorder that disrupts normal metabolism

Etymology

Coined in 1908 by British physician Archibald Garrod to describe inherited metabolic disorders.
