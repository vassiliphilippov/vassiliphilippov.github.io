Newtonian telescope

1. NOUN

/ nuːˈtoʊniən ˈtɛlɪˌskoʊp / (AmE), / njuːˈtəʊniən ˈtelɪˌskəʊp / (BrE)
a type of reflecting telescope in which the image is viewed through an eyepiece mounted at right angles to the main axis
type of reflecting telescope

Examples

The Newtonian telescope uses a simple arrangement of mirrors to produce clear images of distant stars.
He built his own Newtonian telescope for observing the planets and the Moon.
Many amateur astronomers prefer the Newtonian telescope because of its affordability and ease of use.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reflecting telescope - a telescope that uses mirrors to form an image

Etymology

Named after Sir Isaac Newton, who invented the design in the 17th century.
