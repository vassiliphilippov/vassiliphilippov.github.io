adjutant stork

1. NOUN

/ ˈædʒuˌtænt stɔrk / (AmE), / ˈadjʊt(ə)nt stɔːk / (BrE)
a large stork native to India, noted for its upright, military-like gait
large Indian stork with military gait

Examples

The adjutant stork is often seen near wetlands in India.
With its imposing stature, the adjutant stork resembles a soldier on parade.
Local villagers respect the adjutant stork for its role in controlling pests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stork - large wading bird with long legs and neck

Etymology

Named for its stiff, military-like walk, resembling an adjutant (a military officer).
