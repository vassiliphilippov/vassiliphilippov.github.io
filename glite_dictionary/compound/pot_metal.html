pot metal

1. NOUN

/ pɑt ˈmɛtəl / (AmE), / pɒt ˈmɛtəl / (BrE)
a type of cast iron used for making cooking utensils and wares
cast iron for cooking wares

Examples

The antique teapot was made from pot metal and showed signs of rust.
Many old kitchen utensils were crafted from pot metal, valued for its durability and low cost.
Collectors often seek out pot metal cookware due to its historical significance.

Etymology

From 'pot', referring to cooking pots, and 'metal', indicating its material nature.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɑt ˌmɛt̬l̩ / (AmE), / ˈpɒt ˌmɛtl̩ / (BrE)
an alloy of copper and lead, sometimes with other metals, used especially for making large pots and inexpensive items
copper-lead alloy, used for pots

Examples

The old teapot was made from pot metal, giving it a dull greyish appearance.
Jewelry made of pot metal tends to tarnish and break easily over time.
Early cookware was often cast from pot metal because it was inexpensive and easy to shape.
