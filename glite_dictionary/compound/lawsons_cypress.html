Lawson's cypress

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɔsənz ˈsaɪprəs / (AmE), / ˈlɔːsənz ˈsɪprəs / (BrE)
a large evergreen coniferous tree native to western North America, known for its valuable timber and tall, straight trunk
large coniferous tree of western North America

Examples

The wood of the Lawson's cypress is prized for its durability and resistance to decay.
Many ornamental gardens in Europe feature Lawson's cypress because of its elegant shape.
Several Lawson's cypress trees towered above the forest floor, their trunks stretching high into the sky.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Port Orford cedar - another common name for Lawson's cypress
  • Degree Higher: cypress - any tree or shrub of the coniferous genus Cupressus and related genera

Etymology

Named after Scottish botanist Charles Lawson (1795–1873).
