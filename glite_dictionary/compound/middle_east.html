Middle East

1. PROPN

/ ˌmɪd.əl ˈist / (AmE), / ˌmɪd.əl ˈiːst / (BrE)
A region surrounding the eastern Mediterranean, typically comprising countries from Egypt and Turkey to Iran, notable for its historical and cultural significance.
region around eastern Mediterranean

Examples

She spent two years conducting research in the Middle East.
Conflicts in the Middle East often have global repercussions.
Many ancient civilizations began in the Middle East, including Mesopotamia and Egypt.

Synset Examples

the Middle East is the cradle of Western civilization

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Near East - region comprising the easternmost Mediterranean countries; sometimes overlaps with Middle East

Etymology

First used in the early 20th century, modeled after 'Far East', to denote the region east of Europe and west of South Asia.
