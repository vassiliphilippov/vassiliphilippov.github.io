firm stance

1. NOUN

/ fɝːm stæns / (AmE), / fɜːm stɑːns / (BrE)
an unwavering and strong position or opinion on an issue, especially when refusing to change one's mind
strong and unwavering position

Examples

The government took a firm stance against corruption to restore public trust.
She maintained a firm stance throughout the debate, despite criticism from the opposition.
Their firm stance on environmental protection inspired other organizations to make changes.

Synset Examples

The CEO took a firm stance on the new policy.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: soft stance - a less strict or lenient position
  • Degree Higher: tough stance - an even more uncompromising or strict position
