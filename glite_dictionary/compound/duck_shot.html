duck shot

1. NOUN

/ dʌk ʃɑt / (AmE), / dʌk ʃɒt / (BrE)
small lead shot used in shotgun shells, typically for hunting ducks or other waterfowl.
small lead shot for hunting ducks

Examples

The hunters loaded their shotguns with duck shot before heading to the marsh.
It's important to use the appropriate size of duck shot to avoid injuring game unnecessarily.
Regulations sometimes restrict the use of lead duck shot to protect wetland environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: birdshot - small-diameter shot for shooting birds
  • Degree Higher: buckshot - large lead shot for shooting larger game
