double reverse

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʌbəl rɪˈvɜrs / (AmE), / ˈdʌbəl rɪˈvɜːs / (BrE)
(in American football) a running play in which the ball is handed off twice, each time reversing direction.
American football play with two directional reversals
american

Examples

The team surprised their opponents with a perfectly executed double reverse that resulted in a touchdown.
During the game, the coach called a double reverse to catch the defense off guard.
Fans cheered as the running back completed a successful double reverse late in the fourth quarter.
