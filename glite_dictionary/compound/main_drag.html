main drag

1. NOUN

/ meɪn dræɡ / (AmE), / meɪn dræɡ / (BrE)
the chief street or principal thoroughfare in a town or city, often where most businesses are located
the main street of a town or city
informal american

Examples

The parade marched down the main drag, drawing a big crowd from the neighborhood.
On Friday nights, teenagers love to cruise up and down the main drag.
All the best restaurants in town are located on the main drag.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: main street - the principal street of a town, especially in the US
  • Antonym: side street - a smaller street off the main street

Etymology

‘Drag’ as a noun for a street dates to 19th-century American English, likely from ‘drag’ meaning a place where people promenade or move slowly.
