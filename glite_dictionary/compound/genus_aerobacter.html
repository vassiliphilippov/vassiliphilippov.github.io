genus Aerobacter

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˌɛəroʊˈbæktər / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˌɛərəʊˈbæktə / (BrE)
A genus of aerobic bacteria widely distributed in nature, formerly used for several species now reclassified under other genera.
genus of aerobic bacteria

Examples

Many soil samples contain bacteria from the genus Aerobacter, which play a role in nitrogen fixation.
Researchers studied the prevalence of genus Aerobacter in hospital environments.
Some species previously classified in the genus Aerobacter are now assigned to Klebsiella and Enterobacter.

Etymology

From Greek 'aēr' (air) + 'baktērion' (small rod), reflecting aerobic nature; 'Aerobacter' is a Neo-Latin taxonomic creation.
