cyma reversa

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪmə rəˈvɜrsə / (AmE), / ˈsɪmə rɪˈvɜːsə / (BrE)
a molding with an S-shaped profile, where the convex curve is above the concave curve
S-shaped molding profile (concave below, convex above)

Examples

The console table featured a beautifully carved cyma reversa along its edge.
In classical architecture, the cyma reversa is often used for cornice moldings.
The craftsman demonstrated how to sculpt a cyma reversa for the restoration project.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cyma recta - a molding with an S-shaped profile, convex below and concave above

Etymology

From Latin 'cyma' (wave, molding) and 'reversa' (reversed).
