Irish wolfhound

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪrɪʃ ˈwʊlfˌhaʊnd / (AmE), / ˈaɪərɪʃ ˈwʊlfˌhaʊnd / (BrE)
a large breed of hound, originally from Ireland, with a rough, thick coat; among the tallest of all dog breeds, traditionally used for hunting wolves
large Irish breed of hound

Examples

The Irish wolfhound is known for its impressive height and gentle temperament.
In the show ring, the Irish wolfhound moved with surprising grace despite its size.
They adopted an Irish wolfhound to join their family of dogs last year.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: hound - a type of dog bred for hunting

Etymology

From Irish + wolf + hound, referring to its Irish origin and use in hunting wolves.
