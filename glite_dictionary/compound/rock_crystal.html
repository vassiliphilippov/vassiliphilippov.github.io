rock crystal

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɑk ˌkrɪstl̩ / (AmE), / ˈrɒk ˌkrɪstl̩ / (BrE)
A transparent, colorless variety of quartz, often used in electronic and optical equipment.
transparent, colorless quartz

Examples

The lens was finely crafted from rock crystal to ensure perfect clarity.
Many ancient carvings were made out of rock crystal for both beauty and durability.
Technicians prefer rock crystal for certain radio frequency applications due to its purity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quartz - a hard mineral composed of silicon dioxide
