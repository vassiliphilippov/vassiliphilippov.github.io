pure imaginary number

1. NOUN

/ pjʊr ɪˌmædʒɪˈnɛri ˈnʌmbər / (AmE), / pjʊə ɪˌmædʒɪˈnəri ˈnʌmbə / (BrE)
a complex number whose real part is zero and whose value is of the form bi, where b is a nonzero real number and i is the imaginary unit.
complex number with zero real part

Examples

The number 3i is a pure imaginary number because its real part is zero.
In mathematics, a pure imaginary number is represented by a real multiple of i.
She explained that multiplying two pure imaginary numbers yields a negative real number.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: complex number - a number of the form a + bi, with real and imaginary parts
  • Antonym: real number - a number with no imaginary part; lies on the real number line

Etymology

Formed from 'pure' (meaning unmixed) and 'imaginary number' (introduced by René Descartes in 1637).
