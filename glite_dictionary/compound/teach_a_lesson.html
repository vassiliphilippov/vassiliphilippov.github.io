to teach a lesson

1. VERB

/ titʃ ə ˈlɛs.ən / (AmE), / tiːtʃ ə ˈlɛs.ən / (BrE)
to provide someone with a valuable experience, often one that corrects behavior or imparts important knowledge or insight
provide valuable or corrective experience

Examples

The coach used the loss to teach a lesson about perseverance to the team.
Her harsh words were meant to teach a lesson after his repeated mistakes.
Sometimes, failure can really teach a lesson that success never could.

Synset Examples

They hoped to teach the kids a lesson about teamwork.
He wanted to teach him a lesson about respect.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to instruct - to teach or give instructions to someone
  • Synonym: to punish - to discipline or impose a penalty on someone for wrongdoings
