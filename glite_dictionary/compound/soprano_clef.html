soprano clef

1. NOUN

/ səˈprɑːnoʊ klɛf / (AmE), / səˈprɑːnəʊ klɛf / (BrE)
a musical clef that places middle C on the bottom line of the staff, used chiefly for notating soprano vocal parts
musical clef placing middle C on the bottom line

Examples

The old choral score was written using the soprano clef, which is unfamiliar to most modern singers.
You can identify the soprano clef because it places middle C on the lowest staff line.
Music students rarely encounter the soprano clef outside of specialized historical pieces.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alto clef - clef placing middle C on the center line of the staff
  • Synonym: tenor clef - clef placing middle C on the second line from the top of the staff
  • Synonym: treble clef - clef placing G above middle C on the second line of the staff

Etymology

From Italian 'soprano' (highest range voice) and French 'clef' (key).
