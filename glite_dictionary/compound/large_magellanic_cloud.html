Large Magellanic Cloud

1. PROPN

/ lɑrdʒ məˈɡɛlənɪk klaʊd / (AmE), / lɑːdʒ məˈɡɛlənɪk klaʊd / (BrE)
the larger of the two irregular galaxies, known as the Magellanic Clouds, visible from the Southern Hemisphere, which orbits the Milky Way
satellite galaxy of the Milky Way

Examples

The Large Magellanic Cloud contains one of the brightest star-forming regions known, the Tarantula Nebula.
Astronomers use the Large Magellanic Cloud as a reference point for studying stellar evolution.
You can see the Large Magellanic Cloud with the naked eye from the Southern Hemisphere on a clear night.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Small Magellanic Cloud - the smaller of the two Magellanic Clouds, another companion galaxy of the Milky Way

Etymology

Named after Ferdinand Magellan, Portuguese explorer, whose expedition first recorded the galaxies for Europeans.
