wide-body aircraft

1. NOUN

/ ˈwaɪd ˌbɑdi ˈɛrˌkræft / (AmE), / ˈwaɪd ˌbɒdi ˈeəkrɑːft / (BrE)
a large commercial airliner with two passenger aisles, designed to carry a high number of passengers.
large twin-aisle airliner

Examples

The airline recently added a new wide-body aircraft to its international fleet.
Passengers appreciate the extra space available on a wide-body aircraft.
It takes longer to board a wide-body aircraft due to the high number of seats.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: narrow-body aircraft - a commercial airliner with a single aisle
