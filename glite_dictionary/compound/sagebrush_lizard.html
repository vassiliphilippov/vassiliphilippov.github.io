sagebrush lizard

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪdʒˌbrʌʃ ˈlɪzərd / (AmE), / ˈseɪdʒˌbrʌʃ ˈlɪzəd / (BrE)
a small ground-dwelling lizard (Sceloporus graciosus) native to the western United States, typically found in open areas with sagebrush and low bushes
a small western US ground-dwelling lizard

Examples

While hiking in Nevada, we spotted a sagebrush lizard basking on a rock.
The sagebrush lizard relies on camouflage to avoid predators in its desert habitat.
Children enjoyed looking for sagebrush lizards among the low bushes during the nature walk.
