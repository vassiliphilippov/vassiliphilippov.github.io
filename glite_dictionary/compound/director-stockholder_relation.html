director-stockholder relation

1. NOUN

/ dəˈrɛktər ˈstɑkˌhoʊldər rɪˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈrɛktə ˈstɒkhəʊldə rɪˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the legal and ethical responsibility of company directors to act in the best interests of the company's stockholders
directors' obligations to stockholders
legal

Examples

The director-stockholder relation requires directors to prioritize shareholders' interests when making major decisions.
Lawsuits often arise when the director-stockholder relation is perceived to be violated by board members.
Experts debated how the director-stockholder relation affects long-term company strategy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fiduciary duty - the obligation to act in the best interest of another party
