siege of Vicksburg

1. NOUN

/ sidʒ əv ˈvɪksbɜːrɡ / (AmE), / siːdʒ əv ˈvɪksbɜːɡ / (BrE)
the decisive military campaign during the American Civil War in 1863 in which Union forces besieged and captured the city of Vicksburg, leading to the surrender of Confederate troops after nearly seven weeks
major 1863 Civil War battle and siege

Examples

The siege of Vicksburg marked a turning point in the American Civil War.
Many historians consider the siege of Vicksburg to be as important as the Battle of Gettysburg.
During the siege of Vicksburg, residents of the city were forced to live under extremely harsh conditions.
