wild calla

1. NOUN

/ waɪld ˈkælə / (AmE), / waɪld ˈkælə / (BrE)
A plant of wetlands and bogs in temperate regions, with small greenish flowers partly enclosed in a white spathe and bearing red berries.
wetland plant with white spathe and red berries

Examples

The wild calla grows abundantly in northern marshes each spring.
She collected samples of wild calla for her botany research project.
In the boggy area, clusters of wild calla stood out with their white spathes.
