Navy SEAL

1. NOUN

/ ˈneɪ.vi siːl / (AmE), / ˈneɪ.vi siːl / (BrE)
A member of an elite United States Navy unit trained for special operations in sea, air, and land environments.
elite US Navy special operations member
american

Examples

After years of intense training, he finally became a Navy SEAL and was deployed overseas.
The operation was conducted by a team of highly skilled Navy SEALs.
Movies often portray Navy SEALs as fearless warriors capable of handling any mission.

Synset Examples

SEAL is an acronym for Sea Air and Land

Related Terms

  • Synonym: commando - a soldier trained for special missions
  • Synonym: green beret - member of the US Army Special Forces
  • Antonym: civilian - person not in the armed forces

Etymology

Acronym for Sea, Air, and Land; SEAL teams established by the United States Navy in the 1960s.
