field bindweed

1. NOUN

/ ˈfild ˈbaɪndˌwid / (AmE), / ˈfiːld ˈbaɪndwiːd / (BrE)
a weakly climbing perennial plant, native to Europe, with white or pink flowers, commonly considered an invasive weed in North America
a climbing perennial weed with white or pink flowers

Examples

The rapid spread of field bindweed poses a major problem for local farmers.
Gardeners often struggle to remove field bindweed from their vegetable patches.
The pink and white flowers of field bindweed are deceptively attractive despite its invasive nature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bindweed - a plant of the convolvulus family known for twining stems
