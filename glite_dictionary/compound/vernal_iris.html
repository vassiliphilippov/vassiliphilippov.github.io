vernal iris

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɜrnəl ˈaɪ.rɪs / (AmE), / ˈvɜː.nəl ˈaɪ.rɪs / (BrE)
A low-growing American iris that flowers in spring, bearing bright blue-lilac flowers.
spring-flowering American iris plant

Examples

The vernal iris brightens up the garden with its blue-lilac flowers each spring.
Botanists studied the natural habitat of the vernal iris in the southeastern United States.
We planted vernal irises along the pathway for a splash of spring color.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: iris - a plant genus with showy flowers, including garden and wild species

Etymology

From Latin 'vernalis' meaning 'of the spring' and 'iris', the genus name.
