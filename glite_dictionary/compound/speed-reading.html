speed-reading

1. NOUN

/ spiːd ˈriːdɪŋ / (AmE), / spiːd ˈriːdɪŋ / (BrE)
The practice of reading printed or written material at speeds significantly faster than average, often with techniques to improve comprehension.
rapid reading method

Examples

She took a speed-reading course to prepare for her law exams.
Many executives use speed-reading to keep up with industry news.
Improving your speed-reading skills can save you time when studying large volumes of text.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skimming - reading rapidly to get a general overview
  • Synonym: scanning - reading quickly to find specific information
