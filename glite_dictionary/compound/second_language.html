second language

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛkənd ˈlæŋgwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈsɛkənd ˈlaŋgwɪdʒ / (BrE)
A language that a person learns in addition to their native language, especially one acquired after early childhood.
language learned in addition to native language

Examples

Many immigrants speak English as a second language after moving to a new country.
The school offers courses to help students improve their second language skills.
She found it challenging to master French as her second language.

Synset Examples

Research on adult-learned second language (L2) has provided considerable insight into the neurocognitive mechanisms underlying the learning and processing of L2 grammar
A period of limited exposure generally leads to attrition (loss) of L2 performance or knowledge
Crucially, it is also desirable to retain them, even in the absence of continued practice or exposure to the L2

Related Terms

  • Antonym: native language - the first language a person learns from birth
  • Synonym: foreign language - a language not commonly spoken in the country of the speaker
