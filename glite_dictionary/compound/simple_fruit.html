simple fruit

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪmpəl frut / (AmE), / ˈsɪmpəl fruːt / (BrE)
A type of fruit developed from a single ovary and consisting of one or more seeds within a fleshy or dry pericarp, such as a grape or tomato.
fruit from a single ovary

Examples

A grape is classified as a simple fruit because it develops from a single ovary.
Many common foods, like tomatoes and cherries, are actually simple fruits in botanical terms.
Unlike an aggregate fruit, a simple fruit arises from just one ovary of a flower.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: aggregate fruit - fruit formed from several ovaries of one flower
  • Antonym: multiple fruit - fruit formed from ovaries of multiple flowers

Etymology

simple from Latin 'simplex' (single) + fruit from Latin 'fructus' (produce, fruit).
