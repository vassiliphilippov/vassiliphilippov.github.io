suborder Menurae

1. NOUN

/ sʌbˌɔrdər məˈnjuːri / (AmE), / sʌbˌɔːdə mɪˈnjʊəriː / (BrE)
a taxonomic suborder of birds comprising the lyrebirds and scrubbirds, native to Australia
taxonomic suborder of lyrebirds and scrubbirds

Examples

The suborder Menurae includes both the famous lyrebirds and the elusive scrubbirds of Australia.
Researchers debate the evolutionary relationships within the suborder Menurae.
Field guides to Australian birds often highlight members of the suborder Menurae for their unique vocalizations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Menura - the genus comprising lyrebirds

Etymology

From modern Latin 'Menura', the type genus of the suborder, and Latin 'suborder.'
