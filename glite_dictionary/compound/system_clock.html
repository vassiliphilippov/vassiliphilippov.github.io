system clock

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪstəm klɑk / (AmE), / ˈsɪstəm klɒk / (BrE)
A time-of-day clock in a computer system that keeps track of the current time and date.
clock that tracks time in computers

Examples

When you reset the system clock, it can affect how programs schedule tasks.
The system clock needs to be accurate to ensure security certificates are valid.
If your computer's system clock is incorrect, you may have trouble connecting to online services.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɪstəm klɑk / (AmE), / ˈsɪstəm klɒk / (BrE)
an electronic component in a computer that generates a regular high-frequency signal to synchronize the operations of internal components
computer component that synchronizes operations

Examples

Without a functioning system clock, your computer will not be able to coordinate its processes efficiently.
Upgrading the system clock can sometimes increase the overall performance of your device.
The system clock sends signals that regulate how quickly instructions are executed by the CPU.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clock - a device for measuring or registering time; base for 'system clock'
