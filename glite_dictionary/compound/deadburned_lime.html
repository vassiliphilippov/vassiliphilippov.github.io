deadburned lime

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛd.bɝnd laɪm / (AmE), / ˈdɛd.bɜːnd laɪm / (BrE)
Lime that has been heated to such a high temperature that it loses reactivity and is unsuitable for most binding purposes.
lime heated until it loses reactivity

Examples

The use of deadburned lime in the mixture resulted in poor strength and weak bonding.
Refractory bricks are sometimes made with deadburned lime to increase their resistance to heat.
Due to its lack of reactivity, deadburned lime is not suitable for most construction applications.

Synset Examples

Overburned lime will not hold up to to freeze-thaw weather cycles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quicklime - calcium oxide formed by heating limestone
  • Synonym: overburned lime - lime that has been excessively heated, becoming less reactive

Etymology

Formed from 'dead' (meaning inert or inactive) and 'burned,' referring to the process of intense firing.
