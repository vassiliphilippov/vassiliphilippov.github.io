Navaho language

1. NOUN

/ ˈnævəˌhoʊ ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈnavəhəʊ ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
The Athabaskan language traditionally spoken by the Navajo people of the southwestern United States.
language of the Navajo people

Examples

The Navaho language has a complex system of verb conjugations.
Many elders in the community still speak the Navaho language fluently.
Efforts are underway to preserve and revitalize the Navaho language among younger generations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Navajo language - alternative spelling of Navaho language, the Athabaskan language of the Navajo people

Etymology

Navaho, variant of Navajo, from Spanish adaptation of the native Diné term for themselves; Athabaskan from a North American language family.
