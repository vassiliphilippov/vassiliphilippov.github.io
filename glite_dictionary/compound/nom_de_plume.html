nom de plume

1. NOUN

/ nɑm də ˈplum / (AmE), / nɒm də ˈpluːm / (BrE)
a pseudonym adopted by an author instead of their real name
an author's pseudonym

Examples

Many famous authors, such as Mark Twain, wrote under a nom de plume.
She published her first novel using a nom de plume to protect her privacy.
The writer's real identity was unknown for years because he always used a nom de plume.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pseudonym - a fictitious name, especially one used by an author

Etymology

From French, literally 'name of feather', meaning 'pen name'.
