graphics driver

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡræfɪks ˌdraɪvər / (AmE), / ˈɡrafɪks ˌdrʌɪvə / (BrE)
A computer program or software component that enables communication between the operating system or applications and a graphics device, such as a display screen or graphics card.
software enabling use of graphics devices

Examples

You need to update your graphics driver to play the latest video games.
Without a proper graphics driver, your computer screen may not display correctly.
The installer automatically detects and installs the appropriate graphics driver for your hardware.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: device driver - software that controls a particular hardware device attached to a computer
