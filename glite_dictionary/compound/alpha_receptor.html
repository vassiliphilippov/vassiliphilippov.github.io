alpha receptor

1. NOUN

/ ˈælfə rɪˈsɛptər / (AmE), / ˈalfə rɪˈsɛptə / (BrE)
a type of receptor found on nerve cell membranes in the sympathetic nervous system, involved in responses such as vasoconstriction and contraction of smooth muscles
receptor in sympathetic nervous system
medical

Examples

The drug acts mainly on alpha receptors, leading to an increase in blood pressure.
Certain nasal decongestants stimulate alpha receptors to constrict blood vessels.
Researchers are studying how alpha receptor antagonists may help treat hypertension.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: beta receptor - a different type of adrenergic receptor affecting other sympathetic responses

Etymology

From 'alpha', the first letter of the Greek alphabet, used to classify this subgroup of adrenergic receptors.
