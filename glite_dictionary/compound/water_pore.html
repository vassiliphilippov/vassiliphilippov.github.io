water pore

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔtɚ pɔr / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə pɔː / (BrE)
a small opening on the surface or edge of a leaf in higher plants, through which water is exuded
a pore in plants that releases water

Examples

The tip of the leaf had a visible water pore, allowing droplets to form in the early morning.
Some tropical plants rely on a water pore to expel excess moisture during humid nights.
Upon close examination, the botanist identified several water pores along the leaf margin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hydathode - a pore in plants that exudes water; technical synonym
