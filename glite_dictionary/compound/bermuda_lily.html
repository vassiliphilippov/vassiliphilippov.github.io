Bermuda lily

1. NOUN

/ bərˈmjudə ˈlɪli / (AmE), / bɜːˈmjuːdə ˈlɪli / (BrE)
a tall lily with large, fragrant, white trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring, often grown as an ornamental plant
a tall lily with white trumpet-shaped flowers

Examples

The Bermuda lily is often used in floral arrangements for weddings and Easter celebrations.
She planted a row of Bermuda lilies in her garden to add a touch of elegance each spring.
The fragrant white flowers of the Bermuda lily bloomed just in time for the festival.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Easter lily - a lily commonly associated with Easter, closely related to the Bermuda lily

Etymology

Named after Bermuda, where it was formerly cultivated for export, and 'lily' from Old English 'lilie'.
