in common

1. ADV

/ ɪn ˈkɑː.mən / (AmE), / ɪn ˈkɒm.ən / (BrE)
shared by or pertaining to two or more people, groups, or things together.
shared or mutual

Examples

They soon realized they had very little in common despite being classmates.
The two companies have a lot in common in terms of corporate culture.
What do these paintings have in common?

Synset Examples

we have several things in common
when an audience is responding to a play they are identifying what they have in common with the human beings on stage

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shared - used or done by more than one person
  • Antonym: individual - belonging to or relating to one person

2. ADV

/ ɪn ˈkɑːmən / (AmE), / ɪn ˈkɒmən / (BrE)
shared by two or more people, groups, or things; held or experienced jointly
shared or held jointly

Examples

The two companies have several interests in common.
The neighbors hold the garden in common and share its upkeep.
Despite their differences, the siblings found much in common during their conversations.

Synset Examples

mites are arachnids, but they have a lot in common with insects
property held in common

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jointly - together with one or more people; shared
  • Antonym: individually - separately; not shared with others
