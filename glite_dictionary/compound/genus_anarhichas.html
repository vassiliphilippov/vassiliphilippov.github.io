genus Anarhichas

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ænəˈrɪkəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs anaˈrɪkəs / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Anarhichadidae, which includes marine fish commonly known as wolffish.
type genus of the wolffish family

Examples

The genus Anarhichas includes several species of wolffish found in cold northern waters.
Biologists have studied the dental structure of the genus Anarhichas to understand their feeding habits.
Members of the genus Anarhichas are known for their powerful jaws and teeth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Anarhichadidae - the family including wolffish

Etymology

From New Latin, with 'genus' meaning 'kind,' and 'Anarhichas' from Greek 'anarrhikhos', a type of fish.
