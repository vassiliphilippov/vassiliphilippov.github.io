in full

1. ADV

/ ɪn fʊl / (AmE), / ɪn fʊl / (BrE)
completely and without anything missing; to the full amount or extent.
completely; to the full amount or extent

Examples

The invoice must be paid in full by the end of the month.
She accepted the blame in full for the mistake.
The concert was broadcast in full on national television.

Synset Examples

the amount was paid in full

Related Terms

  • Synonym: completely - totally; fully
  • Antonym: partially - not completely; in part
