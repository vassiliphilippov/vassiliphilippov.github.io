wrist-slap

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɪstˌslæp / (AmE), / ˈrɪstˌslap / (BrE)
a mild or insignificant punishment or reprimand, especially one considered too lenient
minor punishment or reprimand

Examples

The CEO received only a wrist-slap for violating company policy.
Critics argued that the fine was just a wrist-slap compared to the seriousness of the crime.
Instead of being suspended, she got a wrist-slap and returned to work the next day.

Synset Examples

The penalty was just a wrist-slap, nothing serious.
He received a wrist-slap for being late.
Her wrist-slap punishment didn't deter her behavior.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reprimand - an official expression of disapproval
  • Synonym: punishment - the infliction or imposition of a penalty

Etymology

From the figurative sense of a light slap on the wrist as a mild punishment.
