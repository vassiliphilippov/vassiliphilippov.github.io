do-or-die moment

1. NOUN

/ ˌduː ɔr ˈdaɪ ˌmoʊmənt / (AmE), / ˌduː ɔː ˈdaɪ ˌməʊmənt / (BrE)
a time or situation in which someone must succeed or risk failure, often under great pressure
critical and decisive moment

Examples

The final debate was a do-or-die moment for the candidate’s campaign.
It’s a do-or-die moment for the company as they try to secure funding.
Reaching the summit before sunset was a do-or-die moment for the climbers.

Synset Examples

This is a do-or-die moment for our team.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crunch time - period of intense pressure to succeed
  • Synonym: make-or-break - situation where success or failure is decisive

Etymology

From the idiom 'do or die,' meaning to act with determination or face defeat, combined with 'moment.'
