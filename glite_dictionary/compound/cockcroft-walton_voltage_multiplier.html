Cockcroft-Walton voltage multiplier

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑk.krɑft ˈwɔl.tən ˈvoʊl.tɪdʒ məlˌtɪ.plaɪ.ər / (AmE), / ˈkɒk.krɒft ˈwɔl.tən ˈvəʊl.tɪdʒ ˌmʌl.tɪˈplaɪ.ə / (BrE)
a device that uses a network of capacitors and rectifiers to generate high voltages, commonly used to accelerate charged particles in physics experiments
device that multiplies voltage using capacitors and rectifiers

Examples

The Cockcroft-Walton voltage multiplier enabled scientists to achieve particle energies high enough for nuclear experiments.
Many early particle accelerators relied on a Cockcroft-Walton voltage multiplier to supply the necessary high voltage.
A technician inspected the Cockcroft-Walton voltage multiplier for signs of electrical discharge.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: voltage multiplier - an electrical circuit that increases voltage

Etymology

Named after British physicists John Cockcroft and Ernest Walton, who developed the circuit in the 1930s.
