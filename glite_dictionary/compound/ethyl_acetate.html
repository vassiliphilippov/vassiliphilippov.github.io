ethyl acetate

1. NOUN

/ ˈiːθɪl ˈæsəˌteɪt / (AmE), / ˈiːθɪl ˈæsɪteɪt / (BrE)
a colorless, flammable liquid with a fruity odor, used as a solvent and in flavorings and perfumes
solvent and flavoring agent

Examples

Nail polish removers often contain ethyl acetate as a primary solvent.
The laboratory used ethyl acetate to extract organic compounds from the mixture.
Many artificial fruit flavors are created with ethyl acetate for its pleasant, sweet aroma.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acetone - a colorless, volatile, flammable solvent used in cleaning and as a chemical intermediate

Etymology

Formed from ethyl (from ethanol) and acetate (from acetic acid), describing its chemical composition.
