Friedman unit

1. NOUN

/ ˈfriːdmən ˈjuːnɪt / (AmE), / ˈfriːdmən ˈjuːnɪt / (BrE)
a facetious term for an undefined, recurring period of time, especially in political or project contexts, often promising imminent change or progress that is repeatedly delayed.
joking term for indefinite time span
informal

Examples

Every year, the manager assures us that the product launch is just one Friedman unit away.
Debates about progress were measured in several Friedman units rather than actual dates.
He quipped that peace would come in a Friedman unit, so nobody held their breath.

Synset Examples

He joked that every project was a Friedman unit away from completion.
They discussed the timeline using Friedman units at the conference.
The report estimated timelines in Friedman units for clarity.

Etymology

Refers to journalist Thomas Friedman, who often predicted events would change in the next six months, coining the phrase as a humorous unit of time.
