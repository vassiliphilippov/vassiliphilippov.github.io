armorial bearing

1. NOUN

/ ɑrˈmɔriəl ˈbɛrɪŋ / (AmE), / ɑːˈmɔːriəl ˈbeərɪŋ / (BrE)
a design or symbol depicted on a shield, used in heraldry to identify a person, family, or organization
heraldic symbol on a shield

Examples

The knight's armorial bearing was displayed proudly on his shield during the ceremony.
Many families in Europe still possess their original armorial bearing from centuries ago.
Each armorial bearing is unique, designed to represent the history and values of its owner.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coat of arms - the distinctive heraldic design on a shield or escutcheon

Etymology

From Latin arma (arms) and Medieval Latin armorial, relating to heraldry; bearing from Old English beran (to bear).
