Cheyenne language

1. NOUN

/ ʃaɪˈæn ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ʃaɪˈæn ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Algonquian language spoken by the Cheyenne people, primarily in Montana and Oklahoma
language of the Cheyenne people

Examples

Many elders are working hard to preserve the Cheyenne language for future generations.
The Cheyenne language uses a unique system of syllabic writing.
She is fluent in English and the Cheyenne language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Algonquian language - languages of the Algonquian family

Etymology

Cheyenne from Dakota *šahíyena* (the name of the people); language is Algonquian in origin.
