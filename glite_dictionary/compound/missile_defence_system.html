missile defence system

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪsəl dɪˈfɛns ˈsɪstəm / (AmE), / ˈmɪsaɪl dɪˈfɛns ˈsɪstəm / (BrE)
A system of weapons and technology designed to detect, intercept, and destroy incoming missiles, especially as part of naval or military defense.
system to protect against missiles

Examples

The ship was equipped with a state-of-the-art missile defence system to protect against enemy attacks.
NATO members are investing heavily in joint missile defence systems to bolster security.
The failure of the missile defence system raised concerns about the fleet's vulnerability.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: missile defense - the action or systems used to intercept attacking missiles
