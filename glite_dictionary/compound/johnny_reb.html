Johnny Reb

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɑni rɛb / (AmE), / ˈdʒɒni rɛb / (BrE)
a nickname for a Confederate soldier in the American Civil War, especially as used by Union soldiers
Confederate soldier (nickname, Civil War)

Examples

The legend of Johnny Reb has persisted in Southern folklore for generations.
During the Civil War, Union soldiers would often refer to their enemies as Johnny Reb.
He dressed as Johnny Reb for the reenactment of the famous battle.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Billy Yank - a nickname for a Union soldier in the American Civil War

Etymology

Formed from the given name 'Johnny' and the abbreviation 'Reb' for 'rebel'; 'Johnny' was used by Union soldiers to refer to Confederate troops during the Civil War.
