dining table

1. NOUN

/ ˈdaɪnɪŋ ˌteɪbəl / (AmE), / ˈdaɪnɪŋ ˌteɪbəl / (BrE)
a table designed for people to sit around and eat meals together
table at which meals are eaten

Examples

We placed the dishes on the dining table before everyone sat down.
The large dining table can seat up to twelve guests for holiday meals.
After dinner, they gathered around the dining table to play board games.

Synset Examples

he helped her clear the dining table
a feast was spread upon the board

Related Terms

  • Synonym: table - a piece of furniture with a flat top and one or more legs

Etymology

From 'dining' (from Old French 'disner', to dine) + 'table' (from Latin 'tabula', a board).
