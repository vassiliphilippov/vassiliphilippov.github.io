ovarian pregnancy

1. NOUN

/ oʊˈvɛriən ˈprɛɡnənsi / (AmE), / əʊˈvɛəriən ˈprɛɡnənsi / (BrE)
A rare type of ectopic pregnancy in which the fertilized egg implants and grows within the ovary instead of the uterus.
ectopic pregnancy in the ovary
medical

Examples

An ovarian pregnancy is extremely rare compared to other forms of ectopic pregnancy.
Doctors confirmed the symptoms were caused by an ovarian pregnancy rather than a tubal one.
Surgical intervention is often required to manage an ovarian pregnancy safely.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ectopic pregnancy - pregnancy occurring outside the uterus

Etymology

From ovary + pregnancy; 'ovary' from Latin 'ovarium' (egg receptacle).
