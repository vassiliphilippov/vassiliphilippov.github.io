May wine

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪ ˌwaɪn / (AmE), / ˈmeɪ ˌwaɪn / (BrE)
A traditional punch made from Moselle wine, sugar, sparkling water or champagne, and flavored with sweet woodruff, typically served in spring.
a punch with Moselle wine and sweet woodruff

Examples

Traditionally, May wine is enjoyed at spring festivals in Germany.
We prepared a refreshing bowl of May wine for our garden party last weekend.
Sweet woodruff gives May wine its distinctive aroma and flavor.

Etymology

From German 'Maiwein', literally 'May wine', as it is traditionally served during May.
