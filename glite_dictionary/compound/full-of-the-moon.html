full-of-the-moon

1. NOUN

/ ˌfʊl əv ðə ˈmun / (AmE), / ˌfʊl əv ðə ˈmuːn / (BrE)
the time when the Moon is fully illuminated; the phase of the Moon when its whole disk is visible from Earth
time when the Moon is fully illuminated
archaic

Examples

Many ancient festivals were celebrated at the full-of-the-moon each month.
The poet wrote about the beauty of the night at the full-of-the-moon.
Some traditions believe that wishes made during the full-of-the-moon are more likely to come true.

Synset Examples

the moon is at the full

Related Terms

  • Synonym: full moon - the phase of the Moon when it is fully illuminated as seen from Earth
