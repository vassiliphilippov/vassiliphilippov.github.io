consent decree

1. NOUN

/ kənˈsɛnt dɪˈkri / (AmE), / kənˈsɛnt dɪˈkriː / (BrE)
a formal agreement or settlement between parties that is approved and enforced by a court, often without admission of guilt by the defendant
court-approved legal agreement or settlement
legal

Examples

The company entered into a consent decree to resolve allegations of unfair business practices.
Under the terms of the consent decree, the manufacturer agreed to change its advertising methods.
The court issued a consent decree after both parties reached an agreement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: settlement - an official agreement ending a dispute
  • Synonym: judgment - a formal decision given by a court

Etymology

From 'consent' (Latin consentire, to agree) and 'decree' (Latin decretum, something decided).
