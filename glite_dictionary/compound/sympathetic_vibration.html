sympathetic vibration

1. NOUN

/ sɪm.pəˈθɛt̬.ɪk vaɪˈbreɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / sɪm.pəˈθet.ɪk vaɪˈbreɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
a vibration in one object caused by the resonance from another object vibrating at the same frequency
vibration caused by resonance from another object

Examples

When you play a note on one piano, sympathetic vibration can cause strings in a nearby piano to sound as well.
The glass shattered due to the sympathetic vibration induced by the singer's powerful note.
Engineers must consider sympathetic vibration when designing bridges to avoid structural resonance.
