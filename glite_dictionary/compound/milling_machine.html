milling machine

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪlɪŋ məˌʃin / (AmE), / ˈmɪlɪŋ məˌʃiːn / (BrE)
a machine tool used to shape metal or other solid materials by moving a secured workpiece against rotating cutting tools
machine tool for shaping materials

Examples

To manufacture precision parts, the engineers used a highly advanced milling machine.
Each milling machine in the workshop needed routine maintenance to ensure consistent performance.
Learning to operate a milling machine is essential for students in mechanical engineering.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lathe - machine tool for shaping materials by rotating the workpiece
  • Synonym: drill press - machine tool for drilling holes in materials

Etymology

From 'mill' (to grind or shape) + 'machine'; technical term from late 19th century engineering.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɪlɪŋ məˌʃin / (AmE), / ˈmɪlɪŋ məˌʃiːn / (BrE)
a machine tool used for cutting, shaping, or finishing metal or other materials by means of a rotating cutter
machine for shaping materials with a rotating cutter

Examples

The factory installed a new milling machine last week.
With the milling machine, precise grooves were cut into the metal plates.
Learning to operate a milling machine is an important skill for many machinists.

Related Terms
