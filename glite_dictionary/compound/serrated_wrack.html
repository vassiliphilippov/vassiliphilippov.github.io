serrated wrack

1. NOUN

/ səˈreɪtɪd ræk / (AmE), / səˈreɪtɪd ræk / (BrE)
a species of brown seaweed with serrated (saw-toothed) edges, commonly found on rocky seashores
type of brown seaweed with jagged edges

Examples

The shoreline was covered in serrated wrack after the storm.
You can often find mussels clinging to serrated wrack at low tide.
Marine biologists study serrated wrack to understand coastal ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seaweed - a general term for marine algae
  • Degree Lower: bladder wrack - a related type of brown seaweed with air bladders
