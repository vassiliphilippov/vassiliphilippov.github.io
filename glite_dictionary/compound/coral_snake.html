coral snake

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔrəl ˌsneɪk / (AmE), / ˈkɒrəl ˌsneɪk / (BrE)
A small, brightly colored venomous snake of the Americas, banded in red, black, and yellow or white, noted for its potent neurotoxic venom.
a brightly banded venomous snake (Americas)

Examples

The bite of a coral snake can be fatal without prompt medical attention.
Many people mistake a harmless king snake for a coral snake because of their similar colors.
During their trip to Costa Rica, they spotted a coral snake slithering through the underbrush.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: king snake - a harmless snake with similar banded patterns, often mimicking the coral snake

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɔr.əl ˌsneɪk / (AmE), / ˈkɒr.əl ˌsneɪk / (BrE)
any of several brightly colored, venomous snakes of the family Elapidae, found in Asia, Africa, and Australia, characterized by bands of red, yellow, and black or white.
venomous, banded snake (Asia, Africa, Australia)

Examples

The distinctive red, yellow, and black bands of a coral snake help to warn potential predators.
Hikers in northern Australia are advised to be cautious of coral snakes in the undergrowth.
A bite from a coral snake can be extremely dangerous without prompt medical attention.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cobra - a highly venomous snake of the family Elapidae
