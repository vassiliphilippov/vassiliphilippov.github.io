Alpine scurvy

1. NOUN

/ ˈælpaɪn ˈskɜrvi / (AmE), / ˈælpiːn ˈskɜːvi / (BrE)
A disease caused by a deficiency of niacin or tryptophan, or by a defect in converting tryptophan to niacin, characterized by gastrointestinal problems, skin redness (erythema), and neurological or mental disorders; often linked to malnutrition or alcoholism.
disease from niacin/tryptophan deficiency
medical

Examples

The mountaineering team suffered from Alpine scurvy due to inadequate nutrition during their expedition.
Chronic alcoholism is one of the risk factors for developing Alpine scurvy.
Symptoms of Alpine scurvy include skin redness, digestive issues, and sometimes mental changes.
