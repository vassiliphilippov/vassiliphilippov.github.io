Joan of Arc

1. PROPN

/ dʒoʊn əv ɑrk / (AmE), / ˌdʒəʊn əv ˈɑːk / (BrE)
(c. 1412–1431) A French heroine and military leader who, inspired by religious visions, led French resistance against the English during the Hundred Years' War, and was later canonized as a saint.
French heroine and saint, led resistance to the English

Examples

The story of Joan of Arc has inspired countless works of art and literature.
Many people view Joan of Arc as a symbol of courage and faith.
She read a biography of Joan of Arc for her history class.

Etymology

From French Jeanne d'Arc, her name in French.
