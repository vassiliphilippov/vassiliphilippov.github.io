to defuse tension

1. VERB

/ dɪˈfjuz ˈtɛnʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈfjuːz ˈtɛnʃən / (BrE)
to make a situation less tense or likely to erupt into conflict by calming or reducing hostility.
make a situation less tense

Examples

The manager tried to defuse tension between the two employees before it got out of hand.
Her calm words helped to defuse tension after the heated argument.
Diplomats worked late into the night to defuse tension in the border region.

Synset Examples

The negotiator worked to defuse tensions during the meeting.
They needed to defuse the situation before it escalated.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to escalate - to increase the severity or intensity of a situation

Etymology

From 'defuse' (to remove the fuse from an explosive device) and 'tension'.
