adipic acid

1. NOUN

/ əˈdɪpɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / əˈdɪpɪk ˈæsɪd / (BrE)
a white, crystalline dicarboxylic acid used chiefly in the manufacture of nylon and other synthetic polymers
a chemical used to make nylon

Examples

The production of nylon relies heavily on adipic acid as a key ingredient.
Researchers studied the environmental impact of adipic acid in industrial wastewater.
Pure adipic acid appears as white crystals that dissolve easily in water.

Etymology

From Latin adipem (fat) + -ic, reflecting its origin from fatty substances.
