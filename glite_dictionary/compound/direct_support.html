direct support

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈrɛkt səˈpɔrt / (AmE), / dɪˈrɛkt səˈpɔːt / (BrE)
a military mission in which one force supports another specific force and is allowed to respond directly to that unit's requests for assistance
military mission supporting a specific unit

Examples

The artillery battalion was placed in direct support of the infantry brigade during the operation.
Helicopters were assigned direct support to quickly respond to troops' requests on the ground.
The commander requested direct support from the engineering unit to repair the damaged bridge.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: general support - support provided to a broader or nonspecific group rather than a particular unit
