amphetamine sulfate

1. NOUN

/ æmˈfɛtəˌmin ˈsʌlˌfeɪt / (AmE), / amˈfɛtəmiːn ˈsʌlfeɪt / (BrE)
a white, crystalline sulfate salt of amphetamine used as a central nervous system stimulant, particularly in the treatment of attention deficit hyperactivity disorder (ADHD) and narcolepsy
a stimulant drug; a salt of amphetamine
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed amphetamine sulfate to help manage her ADHD symptoms.
As a medication, amphetamine sulfate is closely monitored due to its potential for abuse.
Laboratory analysis confirmed the presence of amphetamine sulfate in the seized substances.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amphetamine - a stimulant drug of the phenethylamine class
  • Degree Higher: dextroamphetamine - the dextrorotatory isomer of amphetamine, a central nervous system stimulant

Etymology

From 'amphetamine' and 'sulfate'; 'amphetamine' from alpha-methylphenethylamine and 'sulfate' from Latin 'sulphur' (sulfur) plus '-ate'.
