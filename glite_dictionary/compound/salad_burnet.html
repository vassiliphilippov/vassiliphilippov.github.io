salad burnet

1. NOUN

/ ˈsæləd ˈbɜrnɪt / (AmE), / ˈsæləd ˈbɜːnɪt / (BrE)
a European herb (Sanguisorba minor) with purple-tinged flowers and leaves that are sometimes used in salads
edible European herb with purple-tinged flowers

Examples

Fresh salad burnet added a subtle cucumber flavor to the summer salad.
She grows salad burnet in her herb garden for its delicate leaves.
The recipe calls for a handful of chopped salad burnet to garnish the dish.

Etymology

From the use of its leaves in salads and Middle English 'burnet', from Old French 'bouronete', a plant name.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsæləd ˈbɜrnɪt / (AmE), / ˈsæləd ˈbɜːnɪt / (BrE)
a perennial herb (Sanguisorba minor) with edible leaves often used in salads for their cucumber-like flavor
herb with edible leaves used in salads

Examples

She sprinkled chopped salad burnet over the fresh greens for a hint of cucumber flavor.
Growing salad burnet in your garden is easy and rewarding for anyone who enjoys fresh herbs.
The chef recommended using salad burnet to add a unique taste to summer salads.

Etymology

From 'salad' and 'burnet', the latter from French 'burnette', a diminutive of 'brune' (brown), referring to the flower's color.
