Second Epistle of Peter

1. PROPN

/ ˈsɛkənd ɪˈpɪsəl əv ˈpiːtər / (AmE), / ˈsɛkənd ɪˈpɪsəl əv ˈpiːtə / (BrE)
the second book of the New Testament traditionally attributed to Saint Peter the Apostle, consisting of a pastoral letter addressing early Christian communities
New Testament book attributed to Saint Peter

Examples

The Second Epistle of Peter addresses themes of faith and perseverance amid persecution.
He cited passages from the Second Epistle of Peter during his sermon on Christian conduct.
Scholars debate whether the Second Epistle of Peter was actually written by the Apostle Peter.
