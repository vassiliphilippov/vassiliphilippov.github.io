burlesque show

1. NOUN

/ bɜːrˈlɛsk ʃoʊ / (AmE), / bɜːˈlɛsk ʃəʊ / (BrE)
A theatrical performance featuring comic and often provocative acts, typically including striptease and parody.
theatrical entertainment with comic and provocative acts

Examples

She starred in a burlesque show that drew large crowds every weekend.
The city is famous for its lively burlesque shows and vibrant nightlife.
They attended a vintage burlesque show as part of their bachelorette party.

Synset Examples

They organized a burlesque show for charity this weekend.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vaudeville - a type of light, theatrical variety entertainment popular in the early 20th century

Etymology

From Italian 'burlesco' (mockery, joke) and English 'show'.
