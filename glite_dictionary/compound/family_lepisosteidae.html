family Lepisosteidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli lɛpɪsɒˈstiːɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli lɛpɪsɒˈstiːɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of freshwater fish that comprises the genus Lepisosteus, commonly known as gars.
a family of freshwater gars

Examples

The prehistoric look of the family Lepisosteidae setsgars apart from other freshwater fish.
Researchers study the family Lepisosteidae to learn more about evolutionary adaptations in fish.
Members of the family Lepisosteidae are notable for their elongated jaws and sharp teeth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gar - a long, slender freshwater fish with bony scales, belonging to Lepisosteidae

Etymology

From New Latin 'Lepisosteidae', derived from the genus name 'Lepisosteus' and the suffix '-idae' denoting a family in zoological taxonomy.
