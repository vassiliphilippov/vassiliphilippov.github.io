patibulary expression

1. NOUN

/ pəˈtɪbjəˌlɛri ɪkˈsprɛʃən / (AmE), / pəˈtɪbjʊləri ɪkˈsprɛʃən / (BrE)
a facial expression that suggests fear of punishment, doom, or impending death
expression suggesting punishment or death

Examples

As he awaited his sentence, a patibulary expression settled over his features.
The accused wore a patibulary expression throughout the trial, fearing the worst outcome.
She tried to hide her patibulary expression, but everyone sensed her dread.

Synset Examples

His face showed a patibulary expression as he received the news.

Etymology

From Latin patibulum (gallows) + English 'expression'.
