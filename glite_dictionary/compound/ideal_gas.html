ideal gas

1. NOUN

/ aɪˈdiːəl ɡæs / (AmE), / aɪˈdɪəl ɡas / (BrE)
A hypothetical gas consisting of molecules that have no volume and do not interact, used in scientific models to simplify the study of gases.
hypothetical gas with no intermolecular forces

Examples

The behavior of an ideal gas is described by the ideal gas law.
In physics, we often assume an ideal gas to simplify calculations.
At high temperatures and low pressures, real gases behave more like an ideal gas.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: real gas - a gas that does not behave as an ideal gas due to intermolecular forces or volume

Etymology

From 'ideal', meaning perfect or theoretical, and 'gas', from Greek 'khaos' (chaos).
