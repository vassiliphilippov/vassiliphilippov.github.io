cumin seed

1. NOUN

/ ˈkjuːmɪn siːd / (AmE), / ˈkʌmɪn siːd / (BrE)
The aromatic seed of the cumin plant, used as a spice in cooking.
aromatic spice seed from cumin plant

Examples

She sprinkled ground cumin seed over the roasted vegetables to add flavor.
Store your cumin seed in an airtight container to keep it fresh.
Many Indian dishes call for whole cumin seed to be toasted in oil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cumin - the cumin plant, whose seeds are used as a spice

Etymology

From Latin 'cuminum', from Greek 'kyminon', referring to the cumin plant and its seeds.
