spotted cranesbill

1. NOUN

/ ˈspɑtɪd ˈkreɪnzˌbɪl / (AmE), / ˈspɒtɪd ˈkreɪnzˌbɪl / (BrE)
a common wild geranium of eastern North America, with deeply parted leaves and rose-purple flowers
wild geranium with rose-purple flowers

Examples

The spotted cranesbill blooms in early spring across many eastern forests.
Gardeners appreciate the spotted cranesbill for its delicate foliage and bright flowers.
We found several patches of spotted cranesbill during our nature walk.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wild geranium - any of several geranium species growing wild

Etymology

From 'crane' and 'bill', referring to the beak-like shape of the seed capsule; pattern described as 'spotted'.
