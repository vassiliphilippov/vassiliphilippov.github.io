genus Sialis

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs saɪˈæ.lɪs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs sʌɪˈæ.lɪs / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Sialidae, comprising aquatic insects known as alderflies.
type genus of Sialidae (alderflies)

Examples

Several species within the genus Sialis are found near ponds and slow-moving streams.
Entomologists often study the life cycle of insects in the genus Sialis to learn more about aquatic ecosystems.
The genus Sialis is easily recognized by the net-veined wings of its adult members.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sialidae - a family of insects known as alderflies

Etymology

From Latin 'Sialis', the name given to the type species.
