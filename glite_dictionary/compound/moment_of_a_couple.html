moment of a couple

1. NOUN

/ ˈmoʊmənt əv ə ˈkʌpəl / (AmE), / ˈməʊmənt əv ə ˈkʌpəl / (BrE)
The product of the magnitude of either of two equal and opposite forces and the distance between their lines of action; a measure of the turning effect produced by the couple.
turning effect from two opposing forces

Examples

The engineer calculated the moment of a couple to determine the required torque for the system.
A high moment of a couple can cause significant rotation in mechanical structures.
In statics problems, understanding the moment of a couple is crucial for analysis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: torque - rotational force or the tendency of a force to rotate an object
