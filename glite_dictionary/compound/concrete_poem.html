concrete poem

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːn.krit poʊm / (AmE), / ˈkɒŋ.kriːt pəʊm / (BrE)
A poem in which the arrangement of words or letters on the page conveys meaning, often forming a picture related to the subject of the poem.
poem with meaning through visual layout

Examples

Lewis Carroll's 'The Mouse's Tale' is a famous concrete poem shaped like a winding tail.
The poetry collection features a striking concrete poem that forms the outline of a tree.
Students were asked to create a concrete poem using the shape of their favorite animal.

Synset Examples

‘The Mouse's Tale’ is a concrete poem by Lewis Carroll which appears in his novel Alice's Adventures in Wonderland.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: visual poem - poem whose visual appearance is integral to its effect

Etymology

From Latin 'concretus' (condensed, hardened), referring to the poem's physical, visual form.
