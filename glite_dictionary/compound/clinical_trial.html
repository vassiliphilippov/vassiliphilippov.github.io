clinical trial

1. NOUN

/ ˈklɪnɪkəl ˈtraɪəl / (AmE), / ˈklɪnɪkəl ˈtraɪəl / (BrE)
a scientifically controlled study in which people are tested to assess the safety and effectiveness of new drugs or medical treatments
controlled medical study on people
medical

Examples

The company launched a clinical trial to evaluate the vaccine’s effectiveness.
Participants in the clinical trial received either the new drug or a placebo.
Before approval, every medication must undergo a clinical trial to ensure it is safe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: experiment - a scientific procedure to test a hypothesis
