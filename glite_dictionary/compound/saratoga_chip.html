Saratoga chip

1. NOUN

/ ˌsærəˈtoʊɡə tʃɪp / (AmE), / ˌsærəˈtəʊɡə tʃɪp / (BrE)
a thin, crisp slice of potato fried in deep fat, an early name for what is now called a potato chip
thin, crisp fried potato slice

Examples

The chef served a bowl of Saratoga chips with the club sandwiches.
At the fair, we sampled homemade Saratoga chips sprinkled with salt and herbs.
The original Saratoga chip recipe dates back to the 1850s in New York.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: potato chip - a thin slice of potato, deep-fried and crisped; a common snack food

Etymology

Named after Saratoga Springs, New York, where the snack was first prepared in the 19th century.
