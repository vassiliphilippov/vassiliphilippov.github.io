journal box

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɝrnəl bɑks / (AmE), / ˈdʒɜːnəl bɒks / (BrE)
a metal housing that encloses and supports a journal bearing, typically found in railway vehicles or machinery
metal housing for a journal bearing

Examples

The mechanic inspected the journal box for signs of excessive wear and replaced the damaged parts.
Proper lubrication of the journal box is essential to prevent overheating of the bearing.
The old locomotive suffered a failure after the journal box ran dry.
