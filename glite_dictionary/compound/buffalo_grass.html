buffalo grass

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌfəloʊ ɡræs / (AmE), / ˈbʌfələʊ ɡrɑːs / (BrE)
a low, mat-forming perennial grass native to the southern United States and tropical America, commonly used as lawn grass
mat-forming lawn grass

Examples

Many homeowners in Texas choose buffalo grass because it requires less water than other lawn types.
The buffalo grass in our backyard thrives even during hot summers.
Researchers are studying how buffalo grass can be used for sustainable landscaping.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbʌfəˌloʊ ɡræs / (AmE), / ˈbʌfələʊ ɡrɑːs / (BrE)
a short, hardy grass native to the dry plains of central North America, often found where bison (buffalo) once roamed
a hardy grass of central North American plains

Examples

The lawn was planted with buffalo grass to reduce water usage during the hot summer.
Herds of bison once grazed on the buffalo grass that still grows across the prairie.
Buffalo grass is popular in landscaping for its drought resistance and low maintenance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blue grama - a similar prairie grass of North America

Etymology

Named after the American buffalo (bison), which once grazed on the grass.
