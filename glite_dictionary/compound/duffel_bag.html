duffel bag

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʌfəl bæɡ / (AmE), / ˈdʌfəl baɡ / (BrE)
a large cylindrical bag made of sturdy cloth, typically used for carrying personal belongings, especially when traveling or camping
large cylindrical travel bag

Examples

He packed his clothes into a sturdy duffel bag for the weekend trip.
The soldiers slung their duffel bags over their shoulders as they boarded the bus.
After practice, she tossed her gym shoes and uniform into her duffel bag.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: holdall - a large bag used for carrying clothes and other personal belongings
  • Degree Lower: backpack - a bag carried on the back, often by campers or travelers
  • Degree Higher: trunk - a large, sturdy container for transporting personal belongings

Etymology

From Duffel, a town in Belgium known for producing the heavy cloth originally used to make such bags.
