water parsnip

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔt̬ɚ ˌpɑr.snɪp / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə ˌpɑː.snɪp / (BrE)
a stout, white-flowered perennial plant that grows in shallow fresh water, native to the northern United States and Asia
a white-flowered aquatic perennial plant

Examples

The water parsnip thrives in marshy areas and along the edges of slow-moving streams.
Botanists collected samples of water parsnip to study its adaptation to aquatic environments.
Caution is advised, as water parsnip can easily be confused with the poisonous water hemlock.
