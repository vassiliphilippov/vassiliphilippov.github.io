side of pork

1. NOUN

/ saɪd əv pɔrk / (AmE), / saɪd əv pɔːk / (BrE)
one half of a dressed hog carcass, typically before it is divided into cuts of meat
one half of a dressed hog carcass

Examples

The butcher displayed a fresh side of pork in the window of his shop.
After purchasing a side of pork, they decided to cure some of it into bacon.
Each side of pork was carefully inspected before being cut into smaller portions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pork - meat from a pig
  • Degree Lower: bacon - cured meat from the sides and back of a pig
