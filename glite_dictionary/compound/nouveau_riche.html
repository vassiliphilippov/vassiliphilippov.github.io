nouveau riche

1. NOUN

/ nuˌvoʊ ˈriʃ / (AmE), / ˌnuːvəʊ ˈriːʃ / (BrE)
People who have recently acquired wealth, typically regarded as lacking the experience, taste, or refinement of those born to wealth.
newly wealthy individuals (often lacking refinement)

Examples

The party was full of nouveau riche individuals eager to show off their expensive jewelry.
After making his fortune in tech, he was labeled nouveau riche by old money families.
Some people criticize the nouveau riche for their extravagant and sometimes ostentatious taste.

Synset Examples

Her style screamed nouveau riche rather than refined elegance.
He embraced his title as a new nouveau riche with pride.
The party was filled with nouveau riche eager to impress.
They were considered the new nouveau riche for their extravagant spending.
The party was filled with the new nouveau riche, showing off their wealth.
The nouveau riche often envy the vieux riche's sophistication.
He was a nouveau riche, flaunting his wealth at every opportunity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parvenu - a person of obscure origin who has gained wealth or status

Etymology

French, literally meaning 'new rich'.
