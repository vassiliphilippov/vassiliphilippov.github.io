Mediterranean Sea

1. PROPN

/ ˌmɛdɪtəˌreɪniən ˈsiː / (AmE), / ˌmɛdɪtəˈreɪniən ˈsiː / (BrE)
the large sea almost completely enclosed by Europe, Africa, and Asia, connecting to the Atlantic Ocean through the Strait of Gibraltar.
large inland sea between Europe, Africa, and Asia

Examples

The Mediterranean Sea is known for its warm climate and beautiful coastlines.
Shipping routes through the Mediterranean Sea have been vital since ancient times.
They took a cruise that sailed across the Mediterranean Sea last summer.

Etymology

From Latin 'Mediterraneus' (inland, in the middle of land) + 'Sea'.
