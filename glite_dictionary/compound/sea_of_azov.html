Sea of Azov

1. PROPN

/ si əv ˈæzˌɑv / (AmE), / siː əv ˈæzɒv / (BrE)
an inland sea in Eastern Europe, linked to the Black Sea and located between Russia and Ukraine
inland sea between Russia and Ukraine

Examples

The Sea of Azov freezes over in some winters due to its shallow depth.
Fishing is an important industry along the coast of the Sea of Azov.
Due to its strategic location, the Sea of Azov has been the focus of several historical conflicts.

2. PROPN

/ si əv əˈzɑv / (AmE), / si əv əˈzɒv / (BrE)
a shallow bay of the Black Sea located between Russia and Ukraine, connected to the Black Sea by the Kerch Strait
shallow bay of the Black Sea between Russia and Ukraine

Examples

The Sea of Azov is known for its shallow waters and abundant fish populations.
Major ports along the Sea of Azov are vital for regional shipping and trade.
The Kerch Strait connects the Black Sea to the Sea of Azov.
