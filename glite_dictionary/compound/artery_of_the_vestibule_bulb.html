artery of the vestibule bulb

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːr.t̬ə.ri əv ðə vɛs.tə.bjuːl bʌlb / (AmE), / ˈɑː.tə.ri əv ðə vɛs.tɪ.bjuːl bʌlb / (BrE)
a branch of the internal pudendal artery in females that supplies blood to the bulb of the vestibule
branch of the internal pudendal artery in females
medical

Examples

The artery of the vestibule bulb plays an important role in the blood supply to the vulvar region.
During gynecological surgery, care must be taken to avoid damaging the artery of the vestibule bulb.
The pulsation of the artery of the vestibule bulb can be observed in detailed anatomical dissections.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: internal pudendal artery - major artery supplying the perineum and external genitalia

Etymology

From Latin 'arteria' (artery), 'bulbus vestibuli' (bulb of the vestibule) in anatomical nomenclature.
