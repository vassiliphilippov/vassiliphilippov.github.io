Nobel disease

1. NOUN

/ ˈnoʊbəl dɪˌziz / (AmE), / ˈnəʊbəl dɪˌziːz / (BrE)
a humorous term for the tendency of highly successful individuals, especially Nobel Prize winners, to become overconfident or adopt unorthodox and controversial ideas following their fame
overconfidence or eccentricity after major success

Examples

After winning the prestigious award, he worried he might start showing symptoms of Nobel disease.
Some scientists argue that Nobel disease affects even the brightest minds in academia.
His outlandish theories were jokingly attributed to a case of Nobel disease.

Synset Examples

She joked about catching Nobel disease after her success.
His obsession with fame felt like Nobel disease.
Many artists fear the side effects of Nobel disease.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: megalomania - delusion about one's own importance or power
  • Antonym: humility - modesty about one's own importance

Etymology

Modelled after Nobel Prize and 'disease' to humorously characterize the phenomenon; originated in scientific circles.
