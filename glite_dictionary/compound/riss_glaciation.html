Riss glaciation

1. NOUN

/ rɪs ɡleɪsiˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / rɪs ɡlasɪˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
the next-to-last Pleistocene glaciation in the Alps, characterized by extensive glaciers and the deposits formed during that period
next-to-last Pleistocene glaciation in the Alps

Examples

Geologists study the Riss glaciation to understand the formation of the Alpine landscape.
Many valleys in the Alps were shaped during the Riss glaciation.
Sediments from the Riss glaciation provide evidence of ancient climatic changes.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Würm glaciation - the last Pleistocene glaciation in the Alps
  • Degree Lower: Mindel glaciation - the third Pleistocene glaciation in the Alps, before the Riss

Etymology

Named after the River Riss in Germany, near where deposits from this glaciation were first described.
