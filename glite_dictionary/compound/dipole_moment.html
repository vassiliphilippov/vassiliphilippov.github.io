dipole moment

1. NOUN

/ ˈdaɪˌpoʊl ˈmoʊmənt / (AmE), / ˈdaɪpəʊl ˈməʊmənt / (BrE)
a measure of the separation of positive and negative charges in a system, indicating the strength and direction of its dipole
measure of charge separation in a system

Examples

The water molecule has a significant dipole moment due to its polar covalent bonds.
Measuring the dipole moment of a molecule can provide insights into its structure.
In the presence of an electric field, a dipole moment will tend to align with the field.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: electric dipole moment - the dipole moment related specifically to electric charge distribution

Etymology

Formed from 'dipole' (two poles) and 'moment' (from Latin 'momentum').
