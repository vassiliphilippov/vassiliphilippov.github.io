tenement district

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛnəmənt ˈdɪstrɪkt / (AmE), / ˈtɛnəmənt ˈdɪstrɪkt / (BrE)
An urban area where most residences are tenement houses, often associated with overcrowding and poor living conditions.
urban area of mainly tenement houses

Examples

The city council proposed redevelopment plans for the old tenement district near the river.
Many immigrants settled in the crowded tenement district during the late nineteenth century.
She conducted research on the living conditions prevalent in urban tenement districts across the country.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slum - an area of a city marked by poverty and substandard living conditions
