funnel shape

1. NOUN

/ ˈfʌnəl ʃeɪp / (AmE), / ˈfʌnəl ʃeɪp / (BrE)
A conical shape that is wide at one end and narrow at the other, resembling a funnel.
conical shape, wide at one end, narrow at the other

Examples

The tornado had a distinct funnel shape as it touched down in the field.
This vase has a narrow base and widens toward the top, giving it a funnel shape.
Many laboratory devices use tubes with a funnel shape to direct liquids into containers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cone - A solid or hollow object with a circular base and a single vertex; often used in geometry.
