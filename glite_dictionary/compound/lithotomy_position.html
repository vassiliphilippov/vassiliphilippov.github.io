lithotomy position

1. NOUN

/ lɪˈθɑtəmi pəˈzɪʃən / (AmE), / lɪˈθɒtəmi pəˈzɪʃən / (BrE)
a position in which a person lies on their back with knees bent and thighs apart, typically assumed for vaginal or rectal examinations
back-lying position for medical exams
medical

Examples

For the procedure, the patient was placed in the lithotomy position to improve access.
The gynecologist asked me to assume the lithotomy position for the examination.
Some patients may find the lithotomy position uncomfortable during lengthy surgeries.

Etymology

From Greek 'lithotomia' (stone cutting) + position; originally named for surgical procedures involving bladder stones.
