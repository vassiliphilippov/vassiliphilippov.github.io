ascending colon

1. NOUN

/ əˈsɛndɪŋ ˈkoʊlən / (AmE), / əˈsɛndɪŋ ˈkəʊlən / (BrE)
the section of the large intestine that runs upward from the cecum to the transverse colon
section of large intestine running upward
medical

Examples

The ascending colon is responsible for absorbing water and salts from undigested food.
A blockage in the ascending colon may cause abdominal pain and digestive issues.
During surgery, the doctor examined the patient's ascending colon for signs of disease.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: cecum - a pouch connected to the junction of the small and large intestines
  • Degree Higher: transverse colon - the part of the large intestine that passes across the abdomen from right to left

Etymology

From Latin ascendere (to rise) + colon (large intestine), from Greek kolon.
