relation back

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈleɪʃən ˈbæk / (AmE), / rɪˈleɪʃən ˈbæk / (BrE)
(law) the principle that an act, especially the filing of a legal document, is considered by law to have occurred at an earlier date than when it actually took place.
legal principle of deeming an act to have occurred earlier
legal

Examples

The court granted relation back to the original filing date of the lawsuit.
His attorney argued for relation back so the amended claim would not be time-barred.
Under the rule of relation back, the new evidence was treated as if it had been submitted earlier.

Synset Examples

his attorney argued for the relation back of the amended complaint to the time the initial complaint was filed
