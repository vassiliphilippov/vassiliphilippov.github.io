executive session

1. NOUN

/ ɪɡˈzɛkjətɪv ˈsɛʃən / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzɛkjʊtɪv ˈsɛʃən / (BrE)
a meeting or session of a legislative body or other organization that is closed to the public, typically to discuss confidential matters
confidential closed meeting (usually legislative)
legal

Examples

The board met in an executive session to discuss sensitive personnel issues.
During the executive session, all media and spectators were asked to leave the room.
The Senate moved into executive session to consider matters of national security.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: open session - a meeting that is open to the public
