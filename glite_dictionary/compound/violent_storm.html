violent storm

1. NOUN

/ ˈvaɪələnt stɔrm / (AmE), / ˈvaɪələnt stɔːm / (BrE)
a severe weather condition characterized by sustained winds of 64–72 knots, often accompanied by heavy precipitation, thunder, and lightning; classified as force 11 on the Beaufort scale
a wind force 11 storm with severe weather

Examples

The coastal city was battered by a violent storm that lasted through the night.
Ships remained in port to avoid the dangers of a violent storm at sea.
The forecast warned of a possible violent storm approaching from the southwest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: storm - a violent disturbance of the atmosphere
  • Degree Higher: hurricane - a tropical cyclone with extremely strong winds
  • Degree Lower: gale - a strong wind, weaker than a storm
