cardiac sphincter

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːrdiˌæk ˈsfɪŋktər / (AmE), / ˈkɑːdiˌæk ˈsfɪŋktə / (BrE)
the ring of muscle located at the junction between the esophagus and the stomach, acting as a valve to prevent backflow of stomach contents
muscle valve at esophagus-stomach junction
medical

Examples

The cardiac sphincter prevents acidic stomach contents from moving back up into the esophagus.
When the cardiac sphincter does not function properly, it can result in acid reflux or heartburn.
Surgeons sometimes examine the cardiac sphincter in patients with persistent digestive issues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lower esophageal sphincter - the physiological sphincter at the lower end of the esophagus

Etymology

From Latin 'cardia' (upper orifice of the stomach) and 'sphincter' (a band, tightener).
