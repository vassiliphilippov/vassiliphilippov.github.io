skull session

1. NOUN

/ skʌl ˈsɛʃ.ən / (AmE), / skʌl ˈsɛʃ.ən / (BrE)
an informal meeting or session, especially among members of an athletic team, to discuss strategy or review plays
strategy meeting, especially in sports
american informal

Examples

Before the big game, the coach held a skull session to go over the opponent's tactics.
The team meets every Friday for a skull session to study game footage and discuss strategies.
During the skull session, players are encouraged to ask questions about the new plays.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strategy session - a meeting to discuss plans or tactics

Etymology

American colloquialism, possibly from World War II military slang, using 'skull' for 'head' (brainstorming).

2. NOUN

/ skʌl ˈsɛʃən / (AmE), / skʌl ˈsɛʃən / (BrE)
a meeting to discuss ideas, solve problems, or plan strategies, especially among executives or advisors
strategy or brainstorming meeting

Examples

The team held a skull session to develop new marketing strategies for the product launch.
Every Monday morning, executives participate in a skull session to review company goals and challenges.
Before the big game, the coaches gathered for a skull session to finalize their tactics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brainstorming - a group discussion to produce ideas
  • Synonym: meeting - an assembly of people for a particular purpose

Etymology

Probably from the notion of using one's skull (head/brain) to think intensively.
