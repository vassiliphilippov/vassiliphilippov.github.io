Lake St. Clair

1. PROPN

/ leɪk sənt ˈklɛr / (AmE), / leɪk sənt ˈklɛː / (BrE)
a lake situated between Ontario, Canada, and Michigan, USA, connecting Lake Huron with Lake Erie
lake between Ontario and Michigan

Examples

Many migratory birds stop at Lake St. Clair during their journey across North America.
Fishing is a popular activity on Lake St. Clair because of its abundant fish population.
The freighter sailed from Lake Huron, passed through Lake St. Clair, and continued to Lake Erie.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Great Lakes - group of five large lakes in North America

Etymology

Named by French explorers after Saint Clare of Assisi in 1679.
