Eurocreep attitude

1. NOUN

/ ˈjʊroʊˌkriːp ˈætɪˌtjud / (AmE), / ˈjʊərəʊˌkriːp ˈatɪtjuːd / (BrE)
a negative or suspicious perception of increasing European influence, especially as unwelcome or intrusive
negative view of European influence

Examples

Political analysts discussed the rise of the Eurocreep attitude in certain regions.
The spokesperson dismissed accusations of a Eurocreep attitude within the government.
Some media outlets are accused of promoting a Eurocreep attitude against EU policies.

Synset Examples

The article criticized the Eurocreep attitude prevalent in politics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Europhobia - fear or hostility toward European integration or the EU

Etymology

Blend of 'Europe' and 'creep', with 'attitude' referring to a stance or viewpoint.
