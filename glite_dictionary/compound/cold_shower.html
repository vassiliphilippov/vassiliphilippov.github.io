cold shower

1. NOUN

/ koʊld ˈʃaʊ.ɚ / (AmE), / kəʊld ˈʃaʊ.ə / (BrE)
an event or experience that suddenly shocks or disappoints someone, much like an abrupt awakening
shock or disappointment; sudden awakening

Examples

Her rejection of his proposal was a real cold shower after his hopeful anticipation.
The disappointing results of the survey came as a cold shower to the management team.
His arrogant attitude received a cold shower from the harsh public criticism.

Synset Examples

He took a cold shower to wake himself up.
The unexpected news felt like a cold shower.
Her criticism was a cold shower after my enthusiasm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wake-up call - an event that alerts someone to a problem or reality
  • Synonym: reality check - a moment of insight that forces one to face the facts
