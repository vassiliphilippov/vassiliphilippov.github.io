rotary converter

1. NOUN

/ ˈroʊˌtɛri kənˈvɝːtɚ / (AmE), / ˈrəʊtəri kənˈvɜːtə / (BrE)
an electrical device that uses a rotating synchronous machine to convert alternating current (AC) to direct current (DC), or vice versa
electrical machine converting AC to DC or vice versa

Examples

The old power station still houses a rotary converter used for converting AC to DC.
Engineers replaced the rotary converter with a modern solid-state rectifier.
A rotary converter was essential in early electrified rail systems for supplying direct current.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: converter - a device that changes something from one form to another
