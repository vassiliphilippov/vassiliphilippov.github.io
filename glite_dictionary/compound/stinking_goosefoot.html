stinking goosefoot

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɪŋkɪŋ ˈɡuːsfʊt / (AmE), / ˈstɪŋkɪŋ ˈɡuːsfʊt / (BrE)
a European species of goosefoot (Chenopodium vulvaria) with strong-smelling leaves, introduced to eastern North America
a strongly scented European goosefoot plant

Examples

The stinking goosefoot gives off a pungent odor when its leaves are crushed.
Botanists found stinking goosefoot growing wild along the abandoned railway tracks.
Although rare, stinking goosefoot sometimes appears in disturbed soils in North America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: goosefoot - a plant of the genus Chenopodium, with triangular-shaped leaves
