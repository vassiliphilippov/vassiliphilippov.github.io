transsexual surgery

1. NOUN

/ trænˈsɛkʃuəl ˈsɝdʒəri / (AmE), / trænˈsɛkʃuəl ˈsɜːdʒəri / (BrE)
surgical and hormonal procedures intended to alter a person's sexual characteristics to resemble those of the opposite sex, typically as part of gender transition.
surgery to alter sexual characteristics
medical

Examples

After years of deliberation, she decided to undergo transsexual surgery to align her body with her gender identity.
Medical advances have improved the safety and effectiveness of transsexual surgery over the past decades.
Some people travel abroad to access transsexual surgery because of lower costs or shorter waiting times.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gender reassignment surgery - surgical procedure to change a person's sex characteristics to match their gender identity

Etymology

From 'transsexual' (from Latin trans- ‘across’ + sexual) + 'surgery'.
