needle cast

1. NOUN

/ ˈnidəl kæst / (AmE), / ˈniːdəl kɑːst / (BrE)
a disease of coniferous trees in which the needles turn brown and fall off prematurely, typically caused by fungal infection
disease causing premature needle loss in conifers

Examples

The pine plantation was severely affected by needle cast this year, leading to visible thinning of the trees.
Foresters routinely monitor for signs of needle cast to prevent large-scale losses in coniferous forests.
Spruce trees showing symptoms of needle cast may require treatment to control the spread of the disease.
