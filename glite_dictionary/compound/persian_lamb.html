Persian lamb

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɜrʒən læm / (AmE), / ˈpɜːʃən læm / (BrE)
the curly, glossy fur obtained from a young karakul lamb, often used for making coats and hats
curly fur from a young karakul lamb

Examples

She wore a luxurious Persian lamb coat to the opera.
The hat was made of genuine Persian lamb and felt incredibly soft.
Demand for Persian lamb has declined as synthetic materials have become popular.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: astrakhan - curly fur from a young lamb, similar to Persian lamb

Etymology

Persian, from Persia (modern-day Iran), reflecting the geographic origin of the karakul lamb.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɜrʒən læm / (AmE), / ˈpɜːʃən læm / (BrE)
the young of a karakul sheep, especially valued for its soft, curly fleece, often used for making fur garments
a karakul lamb, often used for its fur

Examples

The coat was made of luxurious Persian lamb that shone softly in the light.
Vintage hats trimmed with Persian lamb remain popular among collectors.
Demand for Persian lamb pelts increased in the fashion industry during the early 20th century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: karakul - a breed of sheep valued for its pelt

Etymology

From 'Persian,' relating to Persia (modern-day Iran), and 'lamb.' Refers to the origin of the karakul breed.

Source: WordNet
