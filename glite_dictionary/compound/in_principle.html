in principle

1. ADV

/ ɪn ˈprɪnsəpəl / (AmE), / ɪn ˈprɪnsɪpəl / (BrE)
used to indicate that something is accepted or agreed to in a general or fundamental sense, though specific details or practical aspects may differ or remain undecided.
agreed in general, not in detail

Examples

While in principle we support your proposal, we need to discuss the specifics further.
She agreed in principle to join the committee, but has not made a final commitment yet.
The law was accepted in principle, but implementation was delayed due to technical concerns.

Synset Examples

in principle, we agree

Related Terms

  • Antonym: in practice - in actual operation or experience, as opposed to theoretical or general agreement
