case shot

1. NOUN

/ ˈkeɪs ʃɑt / (AmE), / ˈkeɪs ʃɒt / (BrE)
A type of ammunition consisting of a metallic cylinder filled with small balls or shot, used in firearms or cannons to disperse projectiles over a wide area.
ammunition packed with small shot

Examples

The artillery crew loaded a case shot to repel the advancing soldiers.
During the battle, case shot was fired to cause maximum damage to enemy infantry.
Historical accounts describe the devastating effect of case shot on tightly packed troops.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: canister shot - a similar type of artillery ammunition using a canister to hold projectiles
  • Synonym: grapeshot - a cluster of small iron balls used as anti-personnel ammunition in cannons

Etymology

From 'case' (container) and 'shot' (projectile); used from the 17th century in artillery.
