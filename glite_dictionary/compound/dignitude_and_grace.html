dignitude and grace

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɪɡnɪˌtuːd ənd ɡreɪs / (AmE), / ˈdɪɡnɪˌtjuːd ənd ɡreɪs / (BrE)
composure and elegance in response to situations, especially when under scrutiny or challenge
composure and elegance in response

Examples

Despite the harsh questions at the press conference, she answered with dignitude and grace.
He accepted the unexpected outcome with dignitude and grace, impressing everyone in the room.
The way they handled public scrutiny showed true dignitude and grace.

Synset Examples

They handled the criticism with dignitude and grace.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poise - graceful and elegant bearing in a person
  • Antonym: awkwardness - lack of grace or elegance in manner
