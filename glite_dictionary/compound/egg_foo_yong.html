egg foo yong

1. NOUN

/ ɛɡ fuː ˈjɔŋ / (AmE), / ɛɡ fuː ˈjɒŋ / (BrE)
a Chinese-style omelette containing eggs, onions, celery, and chopped meat or fish, often served with sauce
Chinese-style omelette dish

Examples

I ordered the egg foo yong with shrimp at the new Chinese restaurant.
Her recipe for egg foo yong includes bean sprouts and mushrooms.
The egg foo yong came with a savory brown gravy on the side.

Etymology

From Cantonese 芙蓉蛋 (*fu4 jung4 daan6*), meaning 'hibiscus egg'.
