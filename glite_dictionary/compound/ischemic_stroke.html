ischemic stroke

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈskimɪk stroʊk / (AmE), / ɪˈskɛmɪk strəʊk / (BrE)
A type of stroke caused by a blockage in a blood vessel supplying blood to the brain, resulting in reduced blood flow and potential brain damage.
stroke caused by blocked blood flow to the brain
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with an ischemic stroke after experiencing sudden weakness on one side of his body.
Immediate treatment is crucial to minimize brain damage during an ischemic stroke.
Doctors explained that controlling high blood pressure can help reduce the risk of an ischemic stroke.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hemorrhagic stroke - a type of stroke caused by bleeding in the brain

Etymology

From Greek ischaimos (stanching blood) and Latin stroke (blow or attack); 'ischemic' refers to lack of blood supply.
