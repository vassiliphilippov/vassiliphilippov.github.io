class Ginkgopsida

1. NOUN

/ klæs ˌɡɪŋkoʊˈpsaɪdə / (AmE), / klɑːs ˌɡɪŋkɡəʊˈpsɪdə / (BrE)
A botanical class that includes ginkgo trees, sometimes treated as a class or subdivision in plant classification systems.
botanical class including ginkgo trees
medical

Examples

The fossil record provides evidence of the ancient origins of class Ginkgopsida.
Botanists debate whether class Ginkgopsida should be grouped as a class or a subdivision.
Only the Ginkgo biloba remains as a living representative of class Ginkgopsida.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ginkgoales - the order containing ginkgo trees
  • Degree Higher: Gymnospermophyta - the division of gymnosperms in plant taxonomy

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'Ginkgo' (the genus) and Greek 'opsis' (appearance/form), used in botanical classification.
