unleaded gasoline

1. NOUN

/ ʌnˌlɛdɪd ˈɡæsəliːn / (AmE), / ʌnˌlɛdɪd ˈɡæsəliːn / (BrE)
gasoline that does not contain added lead compounds, used as a fuel for vehicles.
gasoline without added lead

Examples

Most cars today run on unleaded gasoline to reduce air pollution.
The mechanic warned me never to use anything but unleaded gasoline in my new car.
Sales of unleaded gasoline increased after the government banned leaded fuels.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: leaded gasoline - gasoline containing lead additives used to improve engine performance
