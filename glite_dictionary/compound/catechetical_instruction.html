catechetical instruction

1. NOUN

/ kætəˈkɛtɪkəl ɪnˈstrʌkʃən / (AmE), / katɪˈkɛtɪkəl ɪnˈstrʌkʃən / (BrE)
the teaching of religious principles or doctrine using a question-and-answer format, often in preparation for confirmation or baptism
religious teaching by questions and answers

Examples

The children attended weekly catechetical instruction before their confirmation ceremony.
Many churches offer catechetical instruction to new members to familiarize them with core beliefs.
His role included leading catechetical instruction and preparing candidates for baptism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catechism - a summary of religious doctrine often in question-and-answer form

Etymology

From Late Latin catecheticus (relating to instruction by word of mouth), ultimately from Greek katēkhein (to instruct orally).
