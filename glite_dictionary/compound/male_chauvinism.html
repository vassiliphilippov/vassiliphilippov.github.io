male chauvinism

1. NOUN

/ meɪl ˈʃoʊvəˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / meɪl ˈʃəʊvɪnɪzəm / (BrE)
the belief or attitude that men are superior to women; prejudice or discrimination in favor of men over women
belief in men's superiority over women

Examples

Her outspoken criticism challenged the male chauvinism that dominated the company culture.
The article exposed widespread male chauvinism in the advertising industry.
He refused to tolerate any form of male chauvinism in his household.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sexism - prejudice or discrimination based on a person's sex or gender, typically against women

Etymology

From 'male' + 'chauvinism' (French Chauvinisme, after the surname Chauvin, reputedly an excessively patriotic Napoleonic soldier); the term adapted to gender attitudes in the 20th century.
