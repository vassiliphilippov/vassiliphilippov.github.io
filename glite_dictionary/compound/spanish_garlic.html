Spanish garlic

1. NOUN

/ ˌspænɪʃ ˈɡɑrlɪk / (AmE), / ˌspænɪʃ ˈɡɑːlɪk / (BrE)
A European variety of leek that is cultivated and used in the same way as leeks.
European leek used like leeks

Examples

Many Mediterranean dishes call for Spanish garlic because of its mild, sweet flavor.
The farmer is growing Spanish garlic alongside onions and shallots this season.
When sautéed, Spanish garlic adds a subtle aroma to soups and stews.
