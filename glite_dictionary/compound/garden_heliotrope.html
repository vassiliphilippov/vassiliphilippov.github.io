garden heliotrope

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɑrdən ˈhɛliəˌtroʊp / (AmE), / ˈɡɑːdn ˈhiːliəˌtrəʊp / (BrE)
a tall perennial plant (Valeriana officinalis) with highly fragrant pink or white flowers, whose root is used in herbal medicine
fragrant flowering medicinal plant

Examples

The dried roots of the garden heliotrope are often used in traditional remedies.
A border of garden heliotrope added a sweet fragrance to the cottage garden.
She prefers to plant garden heliotrope for its beautiful clusters of pink flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Valeriana officinalis - scientific name for garden heliotrope
  • Degree Lower: heliotrope - any plant of the genus Heliotropium, typically with fragrant flowers

Etymology

Heliotrope from Greek 'heliotropion' (turning toward the sun); here refers to a plant noted for its scent, not sun-following habit.
