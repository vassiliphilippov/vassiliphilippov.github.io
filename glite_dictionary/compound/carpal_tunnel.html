carpal tunnel

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑr.pəl ˈtʌn.əl / (AmE), / ˈkɑː.pəl ˈtʌn.əl / (BrE)
the narrow passageway on the palm side of the wrist through which nerves and tendons pass
narrow passage in the wrist
medical

Examples

The median nerve passes through the carpal tunnel in your wrist.
Swelling in the carpal tunnel can cause numbness and tingling in the fingers.
Doctors examined her carpal tunnel for signs of compression.

Etymology

From Latin 'carpus' (wrist) and Middle English 'tunnel' (underground passage).
