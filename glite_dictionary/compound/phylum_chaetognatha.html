phylum Chaetognatha

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪləm kiːtɒɡˈneɪθə / (AmE), / ˈfaɪləm kiːtɒɡˈneɪθə / (BrE)
A taxonomic phylum of small, transparent, active marine worms known as arrowworms, found in oceans worldwide.
marine worm phylum (arrowworms)
medical

Examples

The phylum Chaetognatha includes about 120 species of arrowworms found in marine environments worldwide.
Scientists study the phylum Chaetognatha to understand planktonic food webs in the ocean.
Members of the phylum Chaetognatha play an important role as predators of zooplankton.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arrowworm - A common name for small, transparent planktonic marine worms of the phylum Chaetognatha

Etymology

From Greek 'chaite' (hair) + 'gnathos' (jaw), referring to their bristled jaws.
