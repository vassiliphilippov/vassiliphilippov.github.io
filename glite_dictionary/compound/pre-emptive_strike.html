pre-emptive strike

1. NOUN

/ priˈɛmptɪv straɪk / (AmE), / priˈɛmptɪv straɪk / (BrE)
a surprise attack launched to prevent an enemy from carrying out a similar attack against oneself
surprise attack to prevent enemy action

Examples

The military launched a pre-emptive strike to neutralize the threat before any attack could take place.
Some leaders justified the pre-emptive strike by claiming there was an imminent risk of aggression.
Debate continues over whether a pre-emptive strike can ever be morally or legally justified.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: first strike - the initial attack in a conflict, especially with nuclear weapons
  • Antonym: retaliatory strike - an attack carried out in response to another attack

Etymology

From the prefix 'pre-' (before) and 'emptive' (from Latin 'emere', to buy), used here in the sense of 'preventive'; 'strike' meaning an attack.
