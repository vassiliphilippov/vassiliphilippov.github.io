civil rights leader

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪvəl raɪts ˈliːdər / (AmE), / ˈsɪvəl raɪts ˈliːdə / (BrE)
a person who leads or is prominent in efforts to secure equal rights and opportunities for minority groups
leader in equality and justice movements

Examples

Martin Luther King Jr. was a prominent civil rights leader who inspired millions.
The city unveiled a statue honoring a local civil rights leader from the 1960s.
She became a dedicated civil rights leader after witnessing discrimination in her community.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: activist - a person who campaigns for social or political change
