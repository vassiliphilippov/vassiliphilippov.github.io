Indian pink

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪndiən pɪŋk / (AmE), / ˈɪndiən pɪŋk / (BrE)
a tropical American annual climbing plant with red or sometimes white flowers and finely divided leaves, often grown as an ornamental and naturalized in parts of the United States
a flowering climbing plant with red or white flowers

Examples

The vibrant Indian pink brightened the corner of the garden with its red blossoms.
Gardeners often plant Indian pink to attract hummingbirds and butterflies.
We admired the delicate leaves and striking flowers of the Indian pink during our botanical walk.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɪndiən pɪŋk / (AmE), / ˈɪndiən pɪŋk / (BrE)
A North American perennial plant (Spigelia marilandica) with bright red tubular flowers, cultivated as an ornamental.
North American plant with bright red flowers

Examples

The brilliant red flowers of the Indian pink attract hummingbirds to the garden.
She planted Indian pink along the shaded border to add a splash of color.
Botanists recommend Indian pink for woodland gardens because of its hardiness and beauty.
