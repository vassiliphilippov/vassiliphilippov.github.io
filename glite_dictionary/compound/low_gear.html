low gear

1. NOUN

/ loʊ ɡɪr / (AmE), / ləʊ ɡɪə / (BrE)
the lowest forward gear in a vehicle's gearbox, used to provide maximum power and torque at low speeds, especially when starting or climbing
lowest forward gear in a vehicle

Examples

When driving up a steep hill, it's best to shift into low gear.
Starting in low gear helps the car move smoothly from a stop.
The truck remained in low gear while navigating the narrow, winding roads.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: high gear - the highest forward gear in a vehicle's gearbox

2. NOUN

/ loʊ ɡɪr / (AmE), / ləʊ ɡɪə / (BrE)
a gear position in a vehicle that provides more power and less speed, typically used for going up steep inclines or driving in difficult conditions
a vehicle gear for slow speed and high power

Examples

To climb the mountain road safely, he put the truck into low gear.
When approaching the muddy path, it's best to use low gear for better traction.
Descending the steep slope, the driver stayed in low gear to control the speed.

Synset Examples

Driving in low gear helped navigate the rough terrain.
He shifted into low gear to handle the steep hill.
She kept the car in low gear while going downhill.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: high gear - a gear position providing more speed and less power
