work of art

1. NOUN

/ wɝːk əv ɑrt / (AmE), / wɜːk əv ɑːt / (BrE)
an object produced by an artist, especially a painting or sculpture, that is considered to have artistic value or merit
an object of artistic value, especially painting or sculpture

Examples

The museum acquired a new work of art by a renowned sculptor.
She considers every painting she creates to be a work of art.
That building is so beautifully designed—it's truly a work of art.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: masterpiece - a work of outstanding artistry, skill, or workmanship
  • Synonym: artwork - a piece of creative visual art

2. NOUN

/ wɝːk əv ɑːrt / (AmE), / wɜːk əv ɑːt / (BrE)
a creation, such as a painting, sculpture, or performance, that is considered to have artistic merit or significant expressive value
a creation with artistic value

Examples

Her new novel is considered a true work of art by critics worldwide.
The museum displays several works of art from the Renaissance period.
He calls his carefully crafted garden a living work of art.

Synset Examples
