fast-paced

1. ADJ

/ fæst peɪst / (AmE), / fɑːst peɪst / (BrE)
moving or progressing quickly; full of energy or activity
moving or progressing quickly

Examples

The new job is very fast-paced and requires quick decision-making.
We watched a fast-paced thriller that kept us on the edge of our seats.
The company's fast-paced environment attracts ambitious young professionals.

Synset Examples

The luge track was thrilling and fast-paced.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: slow-paced - moving or progressing slowly
