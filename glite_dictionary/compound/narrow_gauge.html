narrow gauge

1. NOUN

/ ˈnæroʊ ˌɡeɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈnærəʊ ˌɡeɪdʒ / (BrE)
A railway track where the distance between the rails is less than the standard 56.5 inches (1,435 mm).
railroad track with rails closer than standard gauge

Examples

The historic railway still operates on a narrow gauge track through the mountains.
Engineers chose a narrow gauge for the new mining railway to reduce construction costs.
Unlike standard railways, narrow gauge lines can navigate tighter curves and steeper gradients.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: standard gauge - the standard width of railway track (56.5 inches or 1,435 mm)
