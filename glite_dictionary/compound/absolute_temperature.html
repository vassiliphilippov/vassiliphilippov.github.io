absolute temperature

1. NOUN

/ ˈæbsəˌluːt ˈtɛmpərətʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈæbsəluːt ˈtɛmpərətʃə / (BrE)
temperature measured from absolute zero, expressed on the Kelvin scale or Rankine scale
temperature measured from absolute zero

Examples

The scientist recorded the absolute temperature in Kelvin for each experiment.
Physics students learn that absolute temperature cannot be negative by definition.
The absolute temperature of the gas was 300 K, according to the laboratory measurements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kelvin - the SI base unit of absolute temperature
  • Degree Lower: absolute zero - the lowest possible temperature, zero on the Kelvin scale

Etymology

From Latin 'absolutus' (unrestricted) and 'temperature'.
