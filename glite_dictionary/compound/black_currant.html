black currant

1. NOUN

/ blæk ˈkɝː.ənt / (AmE), / blæk ˈkʌr.ənt / (BrE)
A small, dark purple to black berry from a widely cultivated shrub (Ribes nigrum), known for its aromatic flavor and used in food and drink.
edible aromatic black berry

Examples

I added some black currant jam to my toast for a tart, fruity flavor.
The garden has a row of black currant bushes that ripen in mid-summer.
Many herbal teas feature black currant for its distinctive taste and color.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: red currant - a similar berry of a related shrub, red in color

2. NOUN

/ blæk ˈkɝː.ənt / (AmE), / blæk ˈkʌr.ənt / (BrE)
A small, dark purple to black berry of a shrub (Ribes nigrum), commonly used in jams, jellies, and desserts.
small black berry used in jams

Examples

She spread homemade jam made from black currant on her toast.
A tart filled with black currant is a popular dessert in many European countries.
The flavor of black currant is both sweet and slightly tart.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: currant - small edible berry from shrubs of the genus Ribes
