giant schnauzer

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒaɪənt ˈʃnaʊzər / (AmE), / ˈdʒaɪənt ˈʃnaʊzə / (BrE)
a large, robust breed of schnauzer originally bred for working purposes, known for its thick, wiry coat and distinctive beard and eyebrows.
large breed of schnauzer dog

Examples

The giant schnauzer is known for its intelligence and protective nature.
At the dog show, her giant schnauzer impressed the judges with its obedience.
They chose a giant schnauzer because of its reputation as a loyal family companion.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: schnauzer - medium-sized dog breed with a wiry coat and beard
  • Degree Lower: miniature schnauzer - smallest breed variety of schnauzer
  • Degree Lower: standard schnauzer - medium-sized schnauzer breed

Etymology

From German *Schnauzer* (from 'Schnauze', meaning 'snout'), with 'giant' indicating its size compared to other schnauzers.
