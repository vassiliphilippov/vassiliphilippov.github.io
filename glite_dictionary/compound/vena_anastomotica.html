vena anastomotica

1. NOUN

/ ˈveɪ.nə ˌæn.ə.stəˈmɑ.tɪ.kə / (AmE), / ˈviː.nə ˌæn.ə.stəˈmɒ.tɪ.kə / (BrE)
either of two veins that connect different parts of the venous system in the brain, allowing communication between veins.
communicating brain vein
medical

Examples

The vena anastomotica plays a key role in draining blood from the brain's cortex.
During the operation, the surgeon identified the vena anastomotica to prevent accidental damage.
Imaging studies revealed that the patient's vena anastomotica was unusually large.

Etymology

From Latin: 'vena' meaning vein and 'anastomotica' from Greek 'anastomosis' (a joining or connection).
