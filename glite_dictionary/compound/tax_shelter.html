tax shelter

1. NOUN

/ tæks ˈʃɛltɚ / (AmE), / tæks ˈʃɛltə / (BrE)
An arrangement or investment made to reduce or avoid paying taxes on income or profits, often by exploiting tax laws.
arrangement to reduce taxes legally

Examples

The company set up an offshore tax shelter to reduce its annual tax bill.
Many wealthy individuals use legal tax shelters to minimize the amount of tax they owe.
The government closed several loopholes to discourage the abuse of tax shelters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tax avoidance - the legal use of strategies to minimize tax liability
  • Antonym: tax evasion - the illegal act of not paying taxes owed
