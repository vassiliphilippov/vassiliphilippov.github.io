Purkinje cell

1. NOUN

/ pɚˈkɪn.dʒi ˌsɛl / (AmE), / pɜːˈkɪn.dʒi ˌsɛl / (BrE)
a large neuron with many branching dendrites found in the cerebellar cortex, responsible for transmitting signals within the brain
type of neuron in the cerebellum
medical

Examples

The unique structure of the Purkinje cell allows it to receive thousands of synaptic inputs.
Damage to Purkinje cells can result in loss of motor coordination.
Researchers studied the activity of a single Purkinje cell during movement.

Etymology

Named after Czech physiologist Jan Evangelista Purkyně (1787–1869), who discovered these cells.
