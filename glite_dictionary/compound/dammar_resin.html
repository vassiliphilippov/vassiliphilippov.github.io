dammar resin

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɑːmɑr ˈrɛzɪn / (AmE), / ˈdæmə ˈrɛzɪn / (BrE)
a hard, pale resin obtained from trees of the family Dipterocarpaceae (especially the amboyna pine) and genus Agathis, used in varnishes, lacquers, and incense
a type of hard, pale resin from certain trees

Examples

Traditional varnishes are often made with dammar resin as a key ingredient.
Artists sometimes use dammar resin to produce a glossy finish on oil paintings.
Many tree species in Southeast Asia are tapped for dammar resin.

Etymology

From Malay 'damar' (resin).
