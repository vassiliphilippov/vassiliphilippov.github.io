chemical weapon

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɛmɪkəl ˈwɛpən / (AmE), / ˈkɛmɪkəl ˈwɛpən / (BrE)
a weapon that uses toxic chemical substances to inflict harm or death on people, animals, or plants
weapon using toxic chemicals

Examples

The government signed an agreement to ban the use of chemical weapons in warfare.
Exposure to chemical weapons can have devastating long-term effects on survivors.
International law strictly prohibits the production and stockpiling of chemical weapons.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: biological weapon - a weapon that uses biological toxins or infectious agents
  • Antonym: conventional weapon - a weapon that is not nuclear, chemical, or biological
