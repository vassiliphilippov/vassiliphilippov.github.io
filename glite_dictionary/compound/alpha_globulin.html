alpha globulin

1. NOUN

/ ˈælfə ˈɡlɑːbjəlɪn / (AmE), / ˈælfə ˈɡlɒbjʊlɪn / (BrE)
A group of globulin proteins in blood plasma or serum that are alkaline and move rapidly during electrophoresis.
a type of blood plasma protein
medical

Examples

Doctors often measure alpha globulin levels to assess liver function.
A deficiency in alpha globulin can indicate certain health problems.
During the test, the patient's alpha globulin fraction appeared elevated.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: globulin - a group of simple proteins found in blood serum and milk

Etymology

From 'alpha', denoting the first in a series, and 'globulin', from Latin 'globulus' (small sphere).
