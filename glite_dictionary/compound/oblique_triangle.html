oblique triangle

1. NOUN

/ əˈbliːk ˈtraɪæŋɡəl / (AmE), / əˈbliːk ˈtraɪæŋɡl̩ / (BrE)
a triangle with no right angle
triangle without a right angle

Examples

An oblique triangle has no ninety-degree angle among its sides.
To solve an oblique triangle, you can use the Law of Sines or Cosines.
Geometry students learned how to distinguish a oblique triangle from a right triangle.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: right triangle - a triangle with one right angle

Etymology

From Latin obliquus (slanting).
