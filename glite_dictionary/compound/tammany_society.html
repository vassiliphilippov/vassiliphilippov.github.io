Tammany Society

1. NOUN

/ ˈtæm.ə.ni səˈsaɪ.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˈtæm.əni səˈsaɪ.əti / (BrE)
a political organization within the Democratic Party in New York City (active mainly in the late 1800s and early 1900s), known for seeking political control through corruption and patronage
New York City political organization noted for corruption

Examples

The Tammany Society wielded immense political power in New York City for decades.
Many reformers fought against the Tammany Society and its tradition of corruption.
Boss Tweed was famously associated with the Tammany Society during its most notorious period.

Etymology

Named after Tamanend, a Native American leader, and also known as Tammany Hall.
