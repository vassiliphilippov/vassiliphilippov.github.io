ugly duckling

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌɡli ˈdʌklɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈʌɡli ˈdʌklɪŋ / (BrE)
a person or thing that appears unpromising or unattractive at first but develops into someone or something beautiful or admirable
person or thing that becomes attractive or successful

Examples

As a child, she was considered an ugly duckling, but she grew up to be a celebrated actress.
The old building was an ugly duckling until it was renovated into a stunning art gallery.
Many teenagers feel like an ugly duckling during adolescence, only to gain confidence later in life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: late bloomer - a person whose talents or capabilities become apparent later than usual

Etymology

From Hans Christian Andersen's fairy tale 'The Ugly Duckling' (1843).
