ethyl radical

1. NOUN

/ ˈiːθɪl ˌrædɪkl̩ / (AmE), / ˈiːθɪl ˌrædɪkl̩ / (BrE)
a reactive group of atoms with the formula C2H5, derived from ethane by the removal of one hydrogen atom
unstable C2H5 group in chemistry
medical

Examples

The ethyl radical plays an important role in many organic reactions.
During combustion, the formation of the ethyl radical can initiate chain reactions.
Scientists detected the presence of an ethyl radical in the reaction flask.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: methyl radical - the univalent hydrocarbon radical CH3 derived from methane
  • Synonym: ethyl group - the C2H5 group as a substituent in organic chemistry

Etymology

From 'ethyl', coined from ether + -yl (chemical suffix), and 'radical', from Latin radix (root).
