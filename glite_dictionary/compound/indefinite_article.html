indefinite article

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈdɛfɪnət ˈɑrtɪkəl / (AmE), / ɪnˈdɛfɪnət ˈɑːtɪkl̩ / (BrE)
a determiner such as 'a' or 'an' that is used to refer to a non-specific person or thing
non-specific determiner ('a' or 'an')

Examples

In the sentence 'I saw a dog,' the word 'a' is an indefinite article.
Learners should understand when to use indefinite articles versus definite articles in English.
The indefinite article is not used with plural nouns in most cases.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: definite article - a determiner such as 'the' used to refer to something specific

Etymology

From Latin 'indefinitus' (not defined) + 'article' from Latin 'articulus' (a joint, a part).
