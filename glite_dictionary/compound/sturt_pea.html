Sturt pea

1. NOUN

/ stɝːt piː / (AmE), / stɜːt piː / (BrE)
a sprawling perennial plant native to Australia, noted for its vivid scarlet flowers marked with black, commonly found in arid regions
Australian plant with scarlet black-marked flowers

Examples

The vibrant flowers of the Sturt pea are a symbol of the Australian outback.
Botanists collected several Sturt peas while studying the desert flora.
Many gardens in Australia feature the distinctive Sturt pea for its striking appearance.

Etymology

Named after the explorer Charles Sturt; 'pea' for its legume family appearance.
