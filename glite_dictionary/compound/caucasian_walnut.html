Caucasian walnut

1. NOUN

/ kɔːˈkeɪʒən ˈwɔlnʌt / (AmE), / kɔːˈkeɪziən ˈwɔːlnʌt / (BrE)
a medium-sized tree native to the Caucasus region, distinguished from other walnut trees by its much-branched form and winged fruit
tree species with winged fruit, native to Caucasus

Examples

The Caucasian walnut is prized for its distinctively winged fruit and attractive wood grain.
Botanists studied the Caucasian walnut in its natural habitat in the forests of Georgia.
Unlike common walnuts, the Caucasian walnut produces fruit with prominent wings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: walnut - a tree producing edible nuts in a hard shell
