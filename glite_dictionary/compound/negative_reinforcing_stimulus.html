negative reinforcing stimulus

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɛɡətɪv rɪˈfɔrsɪŋ ˈstɪmjələs / (AmE), / ˈnɛɡətɪv rɪˈfɔːsɪŋ ˈstɪmjʊləs / (BrE)
a stimulus whose removal serves to decrease the likelihood of the behavior that caused its removal; used in behavioral psychology.
stimulus removed to decrease a behavior

Examples

The removal of a loud noise can serve as a negative reinforcing stimulus for a particular behavior.
If a child stops crying after a parent leaves the room, the parent's departure may become a negative reinforcing stimulus.
Therapists sometimes identify a negative reinforcing stimulus to help modify unwanted behaviors.
