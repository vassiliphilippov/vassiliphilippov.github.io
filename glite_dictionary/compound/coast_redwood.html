coast redwood

1. NOUN

/ koʊst ˈrɛdˌwʊd / (AmE), / kəʊst ˈrɛdˌwʊd / (BrE)
a tall evergreen tree (Sequoia sempervirens) native to the coastal regions of northern California and southern Oregon, known for being the tallest tree species in the world
tall evergreen tree native to US west coast

Examples

The ancient coast redwood stands proudly among the tallest trees in the world.
Many tourists visit California every year to see the majestic coast redwood forests.
Some areas along the Pacific coast are famous for their dense coast redwood groves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: redwood - a large evergreen tree with reddish wood
