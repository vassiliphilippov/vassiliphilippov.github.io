cleavage cavity

1. NOUN

/ ˈkliːvɪdʒ ˈkævəti / (AmE), / ˈkliːvɪdʒ ˈkævəti / (BrE)
the fluid-filled space that forms within a blastula during early embryonic development
fluid-filled cavity in a blastula
medical

Examples

The cleavage cavity plays a crucial role in the early stages of embryonic development.
Scientists observed the formation of a cleavage cavity within the developing embryo.
Disruption of the cleavage cavity can lead to abnormalities in blastula formation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blastocoel - the cavity of a blastula, equivalent term
