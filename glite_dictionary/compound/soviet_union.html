Soviet Union

1. PROPN

/ ˈsoʊ.vi.ət ˈjun.jən / (AmE), / ˈsəʊ.vɪət ˈjuː.njən / (BrE)
a former federation of communist republics in eastern Europe and northern Asia, established in 1922 and dissolved in 1991, which included Russia and 14 other republics
former federation of communist republics (1922–1991)

Examples

The Soviet Union was officially dissolved in December 1991, ending decades of communist rule.
During the Cold War, the Soviet Union competed fiercely with the United States.
Many athletes from the Soviet Union won medals at the Olympic Games.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Russia - largest republic within the Soviet Union

Etymology

From Russian ‘Sovyetskiy Soyuz’, meaning ‘Soviet Union’.
