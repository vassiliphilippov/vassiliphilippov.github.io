basal ganglion

1. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪsəl ˈɡæŋɡliən / (AmE), / ˈbeɪsəl ˈɡæŋɡlɪən / (BrE)
any of several clusters of gray matter located deep within each cerebral hemisphere, involved in the control of voluntary movement
brain structure involved in movement control
medical

Examples

Damage to the basal ganglion can result in movement disorders like Parkinson's disease.
Researchers are studying how the basal ganglion contributes to habit formation and motor learning.
The surgeon carefully avoided the basal ganglion during the operation to prevent complications.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: basal ganglia - clusters of neurons in the brain that regulate movement (plural form)

Etymology

From Latin 'basis' (base) and Greek 'ganglion' (tumor, bundle of nerve fibers).
