Christian sharia

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɪs.tʃən ʃəˈri.ə / (AmE), / ˈkrɪs.tʃən ʃəˈrɪə / (BrE)
A legal or moral system within Christianity modeled on or compared to Islamic sharia, often referring to religiously based laws or codes promoted by Christian groups.
Christian legal/moral system modeled on sharia

Examples

The rise of certain political movements has sparked debate about the possible establishment of Christian sharia in some Western countries.
Critics warn against imposing Christian sharia on secular legal systems.
The term Christian sharia is often used in discussions about religious influence on public policy.

Synset Examples

Some argue that Christian sharia promotes moral guidelines.
The concept of Christian sharia is controversial among scholars.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sharia - the religious law forming part of the Islamic tradition

Etymology

Combines 'Christian' (relating to Christianity) with 'sharia', from Arabic 'šarīʿa' (Islamic law).
