ridge rope

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɪdʒ ˌroʊp / (AmE), / ˈrɪdʒ rəʊp / (BrE)
either of a pair of ropes running along each side of a ship's bowsprit to serve as lifelines for crew members working there
lifeline rope along a ship's bowsprit

Examples

The sailor carefully grasped the ridge rope as he moved out onto the bowsprit in rough seas.
Both ridge ropes need to be secure to ensure the crew’s safety while they work forward.
She tied her safety harness to the ridge rope before proceeding with the repairs at the ship’s bow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lifeline - a safety line for preventing falls on boats or ships
