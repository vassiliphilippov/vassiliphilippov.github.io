apatetic coloration

1. NOUN

/ əˌpæˈtɛtɪk ˌkʌləˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / əˌpəˈtɛtɪk ˌkʌləˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
Coloration in animals that serves as natural camouflage, allowing them to blend with their surroundings to avoid detection.
animal camouflage coloration

Examples

The stick insect's apatetic coloration makes it nearly invisible among tree branches.
Many animals rely on apatetic coloration as a primary defense against predators.
Researchers studied the effectiveness of apatetic coloration in various lizard species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: camouflage - coloration or patterns aiding concealment
  • Synonym: cryptic coloration - coloration providing concealment from predators

Etymology

From Greek 'apatētos' (deceptive) and Latin 'coloratio' (coloring); used in zoology.
