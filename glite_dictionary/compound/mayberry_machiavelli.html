Mayberry Machiavelli

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪˌbɛri ˌmɑːkiəˈvɛli / (AmE), / ˈmeɪˌbɛri ˌmakɪəˈvɛli / (BrE)
A clever schemer or manipulator, especially one who appears outwardly folksy or unassuming.
cunning or manipulative schemer

Examples

His charming smile hid the fact that he was a true Mayberry Machiavelli in the office.
Behind his small-town manners, the Mayberry Machiavelli orchestrated every decision in city council.
She called her rival a Mayberry Machiavelli for always finding clever ways to gain the upper hand.

Synset Examples

Everyone knew him as Mayberry Machiavelli for his clever schemes.
His cunning plans earned him the nickname Mayberry Machiavelli.
In the small town, he was the infamous Mayberry Machiavelli.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: schemer - a person who devises secret or underhanded plans
  • Synonym: manipulator - a person who controls or influences others in a clever or unscrupulous way
  • Degree Higher: Machiavellian - cunning, scheming, and unscrupulous

Etymology

'Mayberry' refers to the fictional small town from the TV show 'The Andy Griffith Show,' suggesting innocence, combined with 'Machiavelli,' referencing the Italian political philosopher known for cunning and scheming.
