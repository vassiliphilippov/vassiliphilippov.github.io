face-to-face

1. ADJ

/ ˌfeɪs tə ˈfeɪs / (AmE), / ˌfeɪs tə ˈfeɪs / (BrE)
(of people) in each other's physical presence; meeting or interacting with someone directly rather than remotely or indirectly.
in each other's presence; directly meeting

Examples

We finally had a face-to-face meeting after months of video calls.
The interview was conducted face-to-face rather than over the phone.
She prefers face-to-face discussions to written communication.

Synset Examples

a face-to-face encounter

Related Terms

  • Synonym: in-person - taking place with people physically present
  • Antonym: remote - taking place at a distance, not physically present
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01772487-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADV

/ ˌfeɪs tə ˈfeɪs / (AmE), / ˌfeɪs tə ˈfeɪs / (BrE)
with two people or things positioned so they are directly facing each other
directly facing each other

Examples

The two paintings were placed face-to-face on the easels.
Neighbors lived face-to-face in houses across the narrow street.
During the negotiation, the leaders sat face-to-face at the table.

Synset Examples

the two photographs lay face-to-face on the table

they sat opposite at the table

Related Terms

  • Synonym: opposite - situated on the other side or facing

3. ADV

/ ˌfeɪs tə ˈfeɪs / (AmE), / ˌfeɪs tə ˈfeɪs / (BrE)
in the presence of each other; physically together rather than at a distance or via communication devices
in person; in each other's presence

Examples

After months of video calls, the team finally met face-to-face for a conference.
Negotiations were easier once the two leaders spoke face-to-face.
Many people prefer doing business face-to-face rather than through emails.

Synset Examples

she met the president face-to-face

Related Terms

  • Synonym: in person - physically present, not remote
