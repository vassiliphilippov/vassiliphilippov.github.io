charley horse

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɑr.li hɔrs / (AmE), / ˈtʃɑː.li hɔːs / (BrE)
A sudden, painful muscle cramp or stiffness, typically in the thigh or calf, often following exercise.
painful muscle cramp, esp. after exercise
american

Examples

She had to stop running when she got a sudden charley horse in her calf.
After the game, he was limping from a charley horse that wouldn't go away.
Stretching before exercise can help prevent a painful charley horse.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cramp - a sudden, involuntary muscle contraction causing pain

Etymology

American English, origin uncertain; first recorded in late 19th-century U.S. baseball slang.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɑrli hɔrs / (AmE), / ˈtʃɑːli hɔːs / (BrE)
A painful muscular cramp or stiffness, especially in the thigh or calf, often occurring after vigorous exercise.
painful muscle cramp after exercise
informal american

Examples

After running a marathon, Lisa suffered a charley horse in her right calf.
He stopped playing basketball because a charley horse made it hard to walk.
Waking up with a charley horse in the middle of the night can be very painful.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cramp - a sudden, involuntary muscle contraction causing pain

Etymology

Origin uncertain; first attested late 19th century U.S. baseball slang, possibly from a racehorse named Charley Horse.
