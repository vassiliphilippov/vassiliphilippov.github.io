yellow chestnut oak

1. NOUN

/ ˈjɛloʊ ˈtʃɛsnʌt oʊk / (AmE), / ˈjɛləʊ ˈtʃɛsnʌt əʊk / (BrE)
a medium-sized deciduous tree native to the eastern United States, known for its strong, durable wood
medium-sized oak tree of eastern US

Examples

The yellow chestnut oak is valued by furniture makers for its tough timber.
Several yellow chestnut oaks flourish along the Appalachian trails, providing habitat for wildlife.
Botanists studied the distinctive leaves of the yellow chestnut oak during their field research.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oak - a type of tree in the genus Quercus
