hydraulic brake cylinder

1. NOUN

/ haɪˈdrɑlɪk breɪk ˈsɪlɪndər / (AmE), / haɪˈdrɒlɪk breɪk ˈsɪlɪndə / (BrE)
a cylinder in a hydraulic braking system containing brake fluid that is compressed by a piston to apply braking force
cylinder part in hydraulic brake system

Examples

The hydraulic brake cylinder must be checked regularly for leaks or damage.
When the mechanic replaced the hydraulic brake cylinder, the brakes functioned properly again.
A faulty hydraulic brake cylinder can cause reduced braking power and unsafe driving conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: master cylinder - main cylinder in a hydraulic brake system that supplies pressure to the brakes
