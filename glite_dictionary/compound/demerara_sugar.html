demerara sugar

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɛm.əˈɹɛr.ə ˈʃʊɡ.ɚ / (AmE), / ˌdɛm.əˈrɑː.rə ˈʃʊɡ.ə / (BrE)
a coarse, light brown, partially refined cane sugar with large crystals, originally produced in Guyana
coarse light brown cane sugar

Examples

Sprinkle a little demerara sugar on top for a sweet, crunchy finish.
She always uses demerara sugar in her coffee for its rich flavor.
The recipe calls for brown or demerara sugar to enhance the taste.

Etymology

Named after the Demerara region in Guyana, where it was first produced.
