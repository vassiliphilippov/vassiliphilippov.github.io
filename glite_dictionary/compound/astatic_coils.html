astatic coils

1. NOUN

/ əˈstætɪk kɔɪlz / (AmE), / əˈstatɪk kɔɪlz / (BrE)
an arrangement of electrical coils designed to produce zero external magnetic field when current flows through them, used in sensitive measuring instruments to minimize interference from external magnetic fields
arrangement of coils minimizing magnetic interference

Examples

The engineer adjusted the astatic coils to improve the sensitivity of the galvanometer.
By using astatic coils, the instrument became less affected by surrounding magnetic fields.
A precise measurement required the installation of new astatic coils in the device.

Etymology

From Greek 'a-' (not) + 'statikos' (causing to stand), referring to lack of static (stationary) magnetic field.
