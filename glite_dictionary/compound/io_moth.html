io moth

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪ.oʊ mɑθ / (AmE), / ˈaɪ.əʊ mɒθ / (BrE)
A large yellow North American moth (Automeris io) noted for the prominent eyelike spot on each hind wing; its larvae have stinging spines.
North American moth with eyelike wing spots

Examples

The io moth is easily recognized by the striking eyespots on its hind wings.
When threatened, the io moth reveals its hind wings to deter predators.
Gardeners should be cautious, as the larvae of the io moth have stinging spines.

Etymology

Named after Io, a figure from Greek mythology, due to its eye-like spots.
