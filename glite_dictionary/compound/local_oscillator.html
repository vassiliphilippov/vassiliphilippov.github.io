local oscillator

1. NOUN

/ ˈloʊkəl ɑːsɪˌleɪtər / (AmE), / ˈləʊkəl ɒsɪˌleɪtə / (BrE)
an electronic oscillator that produces a signal which mixes with an incoming signal in a radio receiver to generate new frequencies for processing
oscillator used in radio receivers

Examples

The local oscillator frequency determines the tuning range of the radio receiver.
Malfunctions in the local oscillator can cause interference and poor reception quality.
Engineers adjusted the local oscillator to match the incoming signal precisely.
