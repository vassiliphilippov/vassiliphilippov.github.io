rock outcrop

1. NOUN

/ rɑk ˈaʊtˌkrɑp / (AmE), / rɒk ˈaʊtkrɒp / (BrE)
a visible portion of bedrock or rock formation that protrudes above the surface of the surrounding land
exposed section of bedrock

Examples

The hikers stopped to rest near a rock outcrop overlooking the valley.
A small rock outcrop jutted out of the grassy hillside, sheltering several wildflowers.
Geologists studied the rock outcrop to better understand the area's ancient history.
