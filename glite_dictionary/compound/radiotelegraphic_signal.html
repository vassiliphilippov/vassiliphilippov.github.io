radiotelegraphic signal

1. NOUN

/ ˌreɪdioʊˌtɛləˈɡræfɪk ˈsɪɡnəl / (AmE), / ˌreɪdiəʊˌtelɪˈɡræfɪk ˈsɪɡnəl / (BrE)
a signal transmitted by telegraphy using radio waves
signal sent by radio telegraph

Examples

The ship sent out a radiotelegraphic signal to request assistance during the storm.
Early wireless communication relied heavily on the radiotelegraphic signal for transmitting messages over long distances.
Interference can sometimes disrupt the clarity of a radiotelegraphic signal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: radiotelegraphy - transmission of messages using telegraphy by radio waves

Etymology

From radiotelegraph (radio + telegraph) + -ic, indicating a signal transmitted via radio-based telegraphy.
