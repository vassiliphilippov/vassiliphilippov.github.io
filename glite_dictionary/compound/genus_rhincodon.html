genus Rhincodon

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs rɪŋˈkoʊ.dən / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs rɪŋˈkəʊ.dɒn / (BrE)
A genus of large sharks containing only the whale shark, the largest living fish species.
genus of sharks, includes whale shark

Examples

The genus Rhincodon includes only the whale shark, the largest fish in the world.
Scientists study the evolutionary history of the genus Rhincodon to understand its unique adaptations.
The discovery of a new fossil closely related to the genus Rhincodon provided insights into shark evolution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whale shark - the largest living fish, member of genus Rhincodon

Etymology

From Greek 'rhine' (rasp) + 'odous' (tooth), referencing the shark's rasp-like teeth.
