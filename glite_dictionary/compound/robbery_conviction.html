robbery conviction

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɑːbəri kənˈvɪkʃən / (AmE), / ˈrɒbəri kənˈvɪkʃən / (BrE)
a formal declaration by a court that someone is guilty of the crime of robbery
finding of guilt for robbery
legal

Examples

The court handed down a robbery conviction after hearing all the evidence.
He appealed his robbery conviction, claiming that the trial was unfair.
Her criminal record includes a robbery conviction from several years ago.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: robbery - the crime of taking property unlawfully from a person or place by force or threat
  • Antonym: acquittal - a judgment that a person is not guilty of the crime with which they have been charged
