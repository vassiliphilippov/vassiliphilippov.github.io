Plymouth Rock

1. PROPN

/ ˈplɪməθ rɑk / (AmE), / ˈplɪməθ rɒk / (BrE)
a large boulder on the shore of Plymouth, Massachusetts, traditionally regarded as the landing place of the Pilgrims in 1620
historic boulder in Plymouth, Massachusetts

Examples

Every year, thousands of tourists visit Plymouth Rock to see the legendary Pilgrims' landing site.
The story of Plymouth Rock is taught in American schools as part of early colonial history.
Historians debate whether the Pilgrims actually set foot on Plymouth Rock, but its symbolism remains strong.

Etymology

Named after Plymouth, Massachusetts, the site of settlement by the Mayflower Pilgrims.

2. NOUN

/ ˈplɪməθ ˌrɑk / (AmE), / ˈplɪməθ ˌrɒk / (BrE)
an American breed of domestic chicken, known for its hardiness and barred plumage
American breed of domestic chicken

Examples

The Plymouth Rock is favored by farmers for its reliable egg production and calm temperament.
Our backyard flock includes a beautiful Plymouth Rock hen with striking black and white stripes.
Since childhood, she has raised Plymouth Rocks for both their meat and their eggs.

Etymology

Named after Plymouth, Massachusetts, where the breed was developed in the 19th century.
