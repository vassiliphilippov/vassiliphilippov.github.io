Texas horned lizard

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛksəs hɔrnd ˈlɪzərd / (AmE), / ˈtɛksəs hɔːnd ˈlɪzəd / (BrE)
A species of horned lizard native to arid and semiarid open regions of the southwestern United States, known for its distinct horns and flat body.
species of horned lizard in southwestern US

Examples

The Texas horned lizard is often seen basking on rocks in the summer sun.
Biologists have studied the Texas horned lizard to understand its unique defense mechanisms.
My niece was fascinated by the Texas horned lizard during our nature walk in West Texas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horned lizard - any lizard of the genus Phrynosoma, known for horns and spines
