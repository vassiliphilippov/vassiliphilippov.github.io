king nut hickory

1. NOUN

/ kɪŋ nʌt ˈhɪkəri / (AmE), / kɪŋ nʌt ˈhɪkəri / (BrE)
A species of hickory tree native to the eastern United States, resembling the shagbark hickory but producing much larger nuts.
tree producing large nuts, eastern US

Examples

The king nut hickory is prized for its exceptionally large and sweet nuts.
In the eastern forests, you can distinguish a king nut hickory by its bark and massive fruit.
This grove features several mature king nut hickory trees along the trail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shagbark hickory - a North American hickory tree with shaggy bark and edible nuts
