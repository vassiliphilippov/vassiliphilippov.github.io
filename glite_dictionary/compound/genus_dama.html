genus Dama

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˈdeɪmə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈdɑːmə / (BrE)
A genus of deer that includes the fallow deer, native to Europe and parts of Asia.
genus of deer (fallow deer)

Examples

The genus Dama is best known for the fallow deer, a species common in European parks.
Unlike the red deer, members of the genus Dama have palmate antlers.
Scientists studied several fossils to trace the evolution of the genus Dama.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fallow deer - A medium-sized deer species (Dama dama) with palmate antlers

Etymology

From Latin 'dama' meaning 'fallow deer'.
