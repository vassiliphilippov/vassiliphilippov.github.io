end-plate

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛnd pleɪt / (AmE), / ˈɛnd pleɪt / (BrE)
the flattened terminal region of a motor neuron where it transmits neural impulses to a muscle
motor neuron terminal region
medical

Examples

The end-plate facilitates the transmission of nerve signals to muscle fibers during movement.
Damage to the end-plate can lead to muscle weakness or paralysis.
Researchers studied changes in the end-plate in response to different types of physical activity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neuromuscular junction - the synapse between a motor neuron and muscle fiber
