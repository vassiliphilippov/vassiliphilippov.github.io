Eternal City

1. PROPN

/ ɪˈtɝnəl ˈsɪti / (AmE), / ɪˈtɜːnəl ˈsɪti / (BrE)
a poetic name for Rome, the capital of Italy, renowned for its long history and central role in Western civilization and the Roman Catholic Church
poetic name for Rome, Italy

Examples

Many tourists dream of visiting the Eternal City to see its ancient ruins and famous art.
The Pope addressed thousands of pilgrims gathered in the heart of the Eternal City.
She studied architecture in the Eternal City, inspired by its classical heritage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Rome - the capital city of Italy

Etymology

A calque of Latin 'urbs aeterna', a traditional honorific for the city of Rome.
