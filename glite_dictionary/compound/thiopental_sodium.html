thiopental sodium

1. NOUN

/ ˌθaɪ.oʊˌpɛnˈtɔl ˈsoʊdiəm / (AmE), / ˌθaɪ.əʊˌpɛnˈtɒl ˈsəʊdiəm / (BrE)
a barbiturate used as a fast-acting general anesthetic, often administered intravenously for induction of anesthesia
fast-acting general anesthetic (barbiturate)
medical

Examples

Before the surgery, the patient was given thiopental sodium to induce anesthesia.
The rapid onset of thiopental sodium makes it useful for short medical procedures.
Doctors discontinued the use of thiopental sodium for executions in several states.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barbiturate - a class of drugs used as sedatives and anesthetics

Etymology

From 'thio-' (sulfur-containing), 'pent' (five), and 'al' (barbiturate derivative), with sodium indicating the salt form.
