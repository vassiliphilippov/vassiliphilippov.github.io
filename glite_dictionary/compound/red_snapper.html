red snapper

1. NOUN

/ rɛd ˈsnæpɚ / (AmE), / rɛd ˈsnapə / (BrE)
a species of snapper with reddish flesh, found chiefly off the Atlantic coast and Gulf of Mexico, highly valued for eating
a reddish fish prized for its flesh

Examples

The chef recommended the grilled red snapper with lemon and herbs for tonight's special.
Many restaurants along the Gulf Coast serve fresh red snapper caught that very day.
She ordered a whole red snapper, impressed by its delicate flavor and texture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snapper - a type of fish from the family Lutjanidae

2. NOUN

/ ˌrɛd ˈsnæpər / (AmE), / ˌrɛd ˈsnæpə / (BrE)
a highly valued food fish with a pinkish-red head and body, found in Atlantic coastal waters of North America and the Gulf of Mexico
a type of pinkish-red food fish

Examples

The chef prepared a delicious dish using freshly caught red snapper.
Overfishing has led to declining populations of red snapper in some regions.
At the market, I chose a red snapper for tonight's dinner.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snapper - a marine fish of the family Lutjanidae, especially valued as food
