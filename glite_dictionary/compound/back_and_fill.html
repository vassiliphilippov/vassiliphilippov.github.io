to back and fill

1. VERB

/ bæk ənd fɪl / (AmE), / bæk ənd fɪl / (BrE)
to make small adjustments or changes to adapt to circumstances, especially in construction; to refill or repair trenches or excavations
make adjustments or refill trenches

Examples

The builders had to back and fill to finish the foundation on time.
After the inspection, workers needed to back and fill the open trenches before paving resumed.
In order to meet the changing requirements, the engineering team constantly had to back and fill.

Synset Examples

They had to back and fill to meet the deadline.
