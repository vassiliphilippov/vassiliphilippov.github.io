prairie soil

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɛri sɔɪl / (AmE), / ˈprɛəri sɔɪl / (BrE)
A type of fertile soil found under grasslands in temperate regions, characterized by high organic matter content.
fertile soil under grasslands

Examples

Much of the Midwest is covered in rich prairie soil, ideal for growing crops.
Centuries of grass growth and decay have contributed to the deep prairie soil in this region.
The conversion of prairie soil to farmland led to the agricultural success of the plains.
