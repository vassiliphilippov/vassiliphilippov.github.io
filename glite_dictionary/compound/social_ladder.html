social ladder

1. NOUN

/ ˈsoʊʃəl ˈlædər / (AmE), / ˈsəʊʃəl ˈladə / (BrE)
a metaphorical hierarchy of social status or class, through which people may move up or down
metaphorical hierarchy of social status

Examples

She hoped that a college degree would help her climb the social ladder.
After inheriting a fortune, he suddenly found himself higher on the social ladder.
Some people spend their entire lives worrying about their place on the social ladder.

Synset Examples

Climbing the social ladder requires networking and effort.
He quickly ascended the social ladder after landing a high-profile job.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pecking order - an informal hierarchy of status among members of a group
