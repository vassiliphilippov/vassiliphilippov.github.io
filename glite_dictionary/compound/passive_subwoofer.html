passive subwoofer

1. NOUN

/ ˈpæsɪv səˈbwʊfər / (AmE), / ˈpæsɪv səˈbʊfə / (BrE)
A subwoofer that requires an external amplifier for power, typically consisting of just the driver and enclosure.
subwoofer needing external amplifier

Examples

The new home theater system includes a passive subwoofer that connects to an external amplifier.
Many audiophiles prefer a passive subwoofer for greater customization of their audio setup.
To use a passive subwoofer, you'll need a separate amplifier capable of powering it.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: active subwoofer - a subwoofer with a built-in amplifier
