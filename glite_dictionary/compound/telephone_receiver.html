telephone receiver

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛləˌfoʊn rɪˈsiːvər / (AmE), / ˈtɛlɪfəʊn rɪˈsiːvə / (BrE)
the part of a telephone that you hold to your ear and mouth to listen and speak during a call
part of a telephone for listening and speaking

Examples

She picked up the telephone receiver and dialed her friend's number.
The old-fashioned telephone receiver was heavy and smooth in his hand.
He heard a strange sound coming from the telephone receiver before the line went dead.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: handset - the part of a telephone containing the receiver and transmitter that is held in the hand
