billion floating point operations per second

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɪljən ˈfloʊtɪŋ pɔɪnt ˌɑpəˈreɪʃənz pər ˈsɛkənd / (AmE), / ˈbɪljən ˈfləʊtɪŋ pɔɪnt ˌɒpəˈreɪʃənz pə ˈsɛkənd / (BrE)
A unit for measuring a computer's speed, equal to one billion floating point operations performed in one second.
unit measuring computer speed (billion FLOPS)

Examples

This new processor can reach ten billion floating point operations per second, making it one of the fastest available.
Scientists use computers capable of several billion floating point operations per second to simulate complex physical phenomena.
The benchmark results showed the system achieved over one billion floating point operations per second during the test.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gigaflop - a unit of computing speed equal to one billion floating point operations per second

Etymology

Derived from the computing term 'floating point operation' and the SI prefix 'billion', often abbreviated as 'gigaflop(s)'.
