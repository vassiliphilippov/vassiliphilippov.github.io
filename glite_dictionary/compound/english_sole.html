English sole

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ soʊl / (AmE), / ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ səʊl / (BrE)
a flatfish found along the Pacific coast of North America, valued for its delicate, almost pure white flesh
a Pacific flatfish prized for its white flesh

Examples

The chef prepared the English sole with a light lemon butter sauce.
English sole is often sought after for its mild taste and tender texture.
Fresh English sole fillets can be found in many seafood markets along the West Coast.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sole - a type of flatfish, especially of the family Soleidae

2. NOUN

/ ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ soʊl / (AmE), / ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ səʊl / (BrE)
a pale brown flatfish (Parophrys vetulus) of the Pacific coast of North America, valued as a food fish
edible flatfish of Pacific North America

Examples

The chef prepared the English sole with a lemon butter sauce for the evening's special.
Fishing for English sole is regulated along the Pacific Northwest to preserve their population.
Grilled English sole pairs well with fresh vegetables and white wine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Dover sole - a common European flatfish eaten as food
