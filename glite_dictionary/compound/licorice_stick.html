licorice stick

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪkərɪs stɪk / (AmE), / ˈlɪkərɪʃ stɪk / (BrE)
an informal term for a clarinet, especially referring to one with a standard middle range
clarinet (musical instrument; informal term)
informal

Examples

The jazz musician played a soulful solo on his licorice stick during the concert.
She learned to play the licorice stick in middle school and has loved it ever since.
Many classic jazz bands featured a licorice stick as part of their ensemble sound.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clarinet - a woodwind instrument with a single reed mouthpiece; main orchestral clarinet

Etymology

Refers to the clarinet's resemblance in color and shape to sticks of black licorice candy.
