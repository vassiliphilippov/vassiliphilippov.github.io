mole rat

1. NOUN

/ moʊl ræt / (AmE), / məʊl ræt / (BrE)
an African burrowing rodent with mole-like habits and appearance, known for living underground in colonies
African burrowing rodent

Examples

The mole rat spends most of its life underground, rarely coming to the surface.
Scientists study the mole rat because of its unusual social structure and longevity.
A colony of mole rats can dig complex tunnel systems stretching for kilometers.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: naked mole rat - a species of mole rat lacking fur, native to East Africa

2. NOUN

/ moʊl ræt / (AmE), / məʊl ræt / (BrE)
a burrowing rodent with a scaly tail, native to India and Sri Lanka
burrowing rodent of India and Sri Lanka

Examples

The mole rat is well adapted to life underground, thanks to its powerful digging claws.
Scientists studied the mole rat to understand how animals survive in low-oxygen environments.
Unlike other rodents, the mole rat has a scaly tail and nearly hairless skin.

Related Terms

3. NOUN

/ moʊl ræt / (AmE), / məʊl ræt / (BrE)
a short-limbed, tailless rodent that resembles a true mole in appearance and habits, found in eastern Europe and the Middle East.
a burrowing rodent similar to a mole

Examples

The mole rat spends most of its life underground, digging intricate tunnels.
Scientists are fascinated by the social behavior of the naked mole rat.
Farmers sometimes consider the mole rat a pest because it damages crops.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mole - a small, burrowing mammal with very small eyes and velvety fur
  • Synonym: rodent - a small, gnawing mammal such as a rat or mouse
