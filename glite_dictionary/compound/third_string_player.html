third string player

1. NOUN

/ θɝd strɪŋ ˈpleɪɚ / (AmE), / θɜːd strɪŋ ˈpleɪə / (BrE)
a player who is third in the order of substitutes, typically less likely to play in games unless others are unavailable
sports team's third-choice substitute player

Examples

As the third string player, he rarely got to play.
After two injuries, the coach was forced to use the third string player in the final quarter.
She worked hard all season to move up from third string player to the main lineup.

Synset Examples

As the third string player, he rarely got to play.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: first string player - a team's top-choice player at a position
  • Degree Higher: second string player - a team's second-choice substitute player

Etymology

From the phrase 'first string' in American English sports, where 'string' refers to a lineup of players (e.g., first team, second team), dating to early 20th century.
