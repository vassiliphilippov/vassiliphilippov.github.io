Hualapai language

1. NOUN

/ ˌwɑːləˈpaɪ ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌwɑːləˈpaɪ ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
The Yuman language spoken by the Hualapai people of northwestern Arizona.
Yuman language of the Hualapai people

Examples

She is learning to speak Hualapai language to connect with her ancestors.
Linguists have documented the grammar of the Hualapai language.
Efforts are underway to preserve the Hualapai language for future generations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Yuman - language family in the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico

Etymology

From the name 'Hualapai,' the indigenous people of Arizona, plus 'language.'
