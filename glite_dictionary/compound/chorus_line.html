chorus line

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔɹ.əs ˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈkɔː.rəs ˌlaɪn / (BrE)
A group of dancers or singers who perform together as a unit in a theatrical production, especially in musical theatre.
group of performers in a show

Examples

The dancers in the chorus line practiced their routines for hours before the big performance.
She always dreamed of joining a Broadway chorus line and dancing on stage every night.
The director expanded the chorus line to add more energy to the final musical number.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ensemble - a group of musicians, actors, or dancers who perform together

Etymology

From 'chorus' meaning a group of performers, and 'line' referring to their usual arrangement.
