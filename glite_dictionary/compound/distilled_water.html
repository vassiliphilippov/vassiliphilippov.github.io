distilled water

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈstɪld ˈwɔtər / (AmE), / dɪˈstɪld ˈwɔːtə / (BrE)
water that has been purified by boiling it and condensing the steam, removing impurities and dissolved salts.
purified water by distillation

Examples

Many laboratories use distilled water to ensure experiments are not affected by impurities.
He filled the iron with distilled water to prevent mineral buildup.
Some car batteries require topping up with distilled water instead of tap water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: purified water - water that has been mechanically filtered or processed to remove impurities

Etymology

From Latin 'destillare' (to drop, trickle) and 'water.'
