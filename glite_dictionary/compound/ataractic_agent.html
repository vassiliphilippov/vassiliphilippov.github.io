ataractic agent

1. NOUN

/ ˌætəˈræktɪk ˈeɪdʒənt / (AmE), / ˌætəˈræktɪk ˈeɪdʒənt / (BrE)
a drug used to relieve stress or tension without impairing mental clarity
drug reducing stress without affecting mental clarity
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed an ataractic agent to help the patient manage stress without drowsiness.
Despite her anxiety, the ataractic agent allowed her to remain calm and focused during the interview.
Researchers are developing new ataractic agents with fewer side effects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tranquilizer - a drug used to reduce anxiety or tension
  • Synonym: anxiolytic - a drug that reduces anxiety

Etymology

From Greek 'ataraktos' (not disturbed, calm) + 'agent'.
