false dragonhead

1. NOUN

/ fɔls ˈdræɡənˌhɛd / (AmE), / fɔːls ˈdræɡənˌhɛd / (BrE)
A North American plant with a spike of two-lipped pink or white flowers, commonly grown as an ornamental.
North American flowering plant

Examples

The false dragonhead adds vibrant color to summer gardens with its pink blossoms.
Gardeners often plant false dragonhead alongside other perennials for their long blooming period.
The two-lipped flowers of the false dragonhead attract bees and butterflies.
