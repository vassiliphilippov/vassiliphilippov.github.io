owner's equity

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊnɚz ˈɛkwəti / (AmE), / ˈəʊnəz ˈɛkwɪti / (BrE)
the amount of money or value that an owner has invested in a business, calculated as the net assets of the entity after deducting liabilities
owner's investment in a business

Examples

The accountant explained how owner's equity is affected by profits and losses each year.
After paying off all liabilities, the remaining value is considered the company's owner's equity.
Their owner's equity increased when they invested more funds into the business.

Synset Examples

The types of accounts and their description that comprise the owner's equity depend on the nature of the entity and may include: common stock, capital surplus, retained earnings, and reserve.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: capital - money or assets invested in a business
  • Antonym: liability - the financial debts or obligations of a business
