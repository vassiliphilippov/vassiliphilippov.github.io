death spiral

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛθ ˌspaɪɹəl / (AmE), / ˈdɛθ ˌspaɪərəl / (BrE)
A situation or process that deteriorates quickly and irreversibly, leading to inevitable failure or collapse.
rapid, irreversible decline to failure

Examples

After losing its main client, the small business entered a death spiral it couldn't escape.
Many observers feared that rising prices and falling sales would create a death spiral for the industry.
The coach resigned in the middle of the season, sending the team's morale into a death spiral.

Synset Examples

The company's finances were in a death spiral after the scandal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: downward spiral - a continuous worsening situation or process
