corpus callosum

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɔːrpəs kəˈloʊsəm / (AmE), / ˌkɔːpəs kəˈləʊsəm / (BrE)
a broad band of nerve fibers joining the two hemispheres of the brain
band of nerve fibers connecting brain hemispheres
medical

Examples

Damage to the corpus callosum can disrupt communication between the left and right sides of the brain.
The corpus callosum is especially prominent in the brains of mammals.
An MRI scan revealed thinning of the corpus callosum in the patient.

Etymology

From Latin corpus (body) + callosum (hard, thickened skin).
