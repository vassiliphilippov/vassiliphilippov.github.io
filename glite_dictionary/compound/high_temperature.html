high temperature

1. NOUN

/ haɪ ˈtɛmpɚtʃɚ / (AmE), / haɪ ˈtɛmpərətʃə / (BrE)
a state or condition in which the degree of heat present is above normal or average; an elevated temperature
an elevated degree of heat

Examples

The city experienced unusually high temperatures during the summer heatwave.
Bacteria often multiply rapidly at high temperatures.
A high temperature can be a sign of infection in children.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fever - an abnormally high body temperature
  • Antonym: low temperature - a condition of having little heat or being cold

2. NOUN

/ haɪ ˈtɛmpɚətʃɚ / (AmE), / haɪ ˈtɛmpərətʃə / (BrE)
a body temperature that is above the normal range, usually indicating fever or illness
elevated body temperature; fever

Examples

If your child has a high temperature for more than three days, contact a doctor.
She stayed home from work because her high temperature made her feel weak and dizzy.
Taking acetaminophen can help reduce a high temperature during a cold or flu.

Synset Examples

Her temperature was high, indicating she was sick.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fever - an abnormally high body temperature, usually due to illness
