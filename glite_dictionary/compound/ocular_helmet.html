ocular helmet

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑkjələr ˈhɛlmɪt / (AmE), / ˈɒkjʊlə ˈhɛlmɪt / (BrE)
A type of leather or steel medieval helmet, similar to a nasal helmet, with a convex nasal piece and frontal head stripe forming an eye opening, used in medieval Scandinavia.
medieval Scandinavian helmet with eye opening

Examples

The Viking warrior wore an ocular helmet adorned with intricate engravings into battle.
Museum curators carefully restored the rare ocular helmet found during the Scandinavian excavation.
The reenactor's costume included a replica ocular helmet to match historical references.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nasal helmet - a helmet with a nose guard, used in medieval Europe

Etymology

ocular from Latin 'oculus' (eye); helmet from Old French 'helm', from Germanic origin.
