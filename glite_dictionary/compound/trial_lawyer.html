trial lawyer

1. NOUN

/ ˈtraɪəl ˌlɔjər / (AmE), / ˈtraɪəl ˌlɔːjə / (BrE)
a lawyer who specializes in representing clients before a court of law, particularly in trials
lawyer specializing in courtroom trials

Examples

She hired a well-known trial lawyer to represent her in court.
As a trial lawyer, he spends most of his time preparing for and attending trials.
The trial lawyer delivered a persuasive closing argument to the jury.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barrister - a type of lawyer specializing in court advocacy, especially in the UK
