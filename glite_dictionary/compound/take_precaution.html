to take precaution

1. VERB

/ teɪk prɪˈkɑːʃən / (AmE), / teɪk prɪˈkɔːʃən / (BrE)
to act in order to prevent harm or danger; to take steps to ensure safety or avoid risk
act to prevent harm or risk

Examples

It is important to take precaution when working with hazardous chemicals.
Always take precaution against theft when traveling in unfamiliar places.
Before the storm hit, the family decided to take precaution and secure all their windows.

Synset Examples

Farmers took precautions to prevent bluetongue outbreaks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: take measures - to do something to achieve a result, especially to prevent harm
  • Antonym: be careless - to not pay attention to risks or safety
