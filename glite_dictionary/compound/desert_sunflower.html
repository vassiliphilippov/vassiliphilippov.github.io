desert sunflower

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛzɚt ˈsʌnˌflaʊɚ / (AmE), / ˈdezət ˈsʌnˌflaʊə / (BrE)
A slender, hairy plant with few leaves and golden-yellow flower heads, found in sandy desert areas of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.
desert plant with golden-yellow flower heads

Examples

The desert sunflower flourishes in sandy soils under the intense sun of Arizona deserts.
Hikers were delighted to spot a blooming desert sunflower during their spring expedition.
Many photographers are drawn to the striking gold of the desert sunflower against the arid landscape.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sunflower - tall plant with a large yellow flower head
