European lemming

1. NOUN

/ jʊˈroʊpiən ˈlɛmɪŋ / (AmE), / jʊəˈrəʊpiən ˈlɛmɪŋ / (BrE)
a small rodent (Lemmus lemmus) native to northern Europe, known for its periodic mass migrations, sometimes resulting in many drowning in water bodies
a migratory rodent species of northern Europe

Examples

The population of the European lemming can fluctuate widely from year to year due to mass migrations.
Scientists tracked a herd of European lemmings as they crossed rivers in search of food.
Legends about the European lemming jumping into the sea have intrigued naturalists for centuries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lemming - a small, short-tailed rodent found in arctic tundra
