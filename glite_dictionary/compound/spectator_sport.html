spectator sport

1. NOUN

/ spɛkˈteɪtɚ spɔrt / (AmE), / spɛkˈteɪtə spɔːt / (BrE)
a sport that is entertaining to watch and attracts many viewers or fans
a sport popular to watch

Examples

Football is considered a classic spectator sport because it draws huge crowds to stadiums.
Tennis has become a global spectator sport thanks to televised tournaments like Wimbledon.
Some argue that chess is not a spectator sport because it lacks physical action to engage large audiences.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: participation sport - a sport in which many people actively play rather than primarily watch

2. NOUN

/ ˈspɛk.teɪ.tɚ spɔrt / (AmE), / ˈspɛk.teɪ.tə spɔːt / (BrE)
A sport or activity that is mainly performed for observers and attracts many viewers rather than participants.
sport for observers or viewers

Examples

For many people, soccer is the ultimate spectator sport and draws massive crowds.
Tennis has grown from an elite pastime to a global spectator sport watched by millions.
Basketball is a highly competitive spectator sport with enthusiastic fans worldwide.

Synset Examples

Hiking is more of a personal adventure than a spectator sport.
The championship became a major spectator sport this year.
Football is a popular spectator sport around the world.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: participatory sport - A sport in which people commonly take part rather than just watch
