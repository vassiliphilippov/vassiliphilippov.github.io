2. NOUN

/ ˈbætʃələrz ˌbʌtən / (AmE), / ˈbatʃələz ˌbʌtən / (BrE)
an annual Eurasian plant, cultivated for its showy blue, purple, pink, or white flowers, also known as cornflower
a showy flowering plant (cornflower)

Examples

A bouquet of bachelor's buttons added a splash of color to the kitchen table.
The field was full of wildflowers, especially bright blue bachelor's button.
She grew pink and white bachelor's buttons in her garden every summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cornflower - another common name for Centaurea cyanus

Etymology

Refers to the plant's use as a bachelor's boutonnière (buttonhole flower); the term dates to the 16th century.
