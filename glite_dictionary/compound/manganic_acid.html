manganic acid

1. NOUN

/ mænˈɡænɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / manˈɡanɪk ˈasɪd / (BrE)
A dibasic acid (H2MnO4) known only in solution and in manganate salts, used in chemistry.
a dibasic acid (H2MnO4); chemistry term
medical

Examples

When potassium permanganate reacts with an acid, it can produce manganic acid in solution.
The chemist explained the structure of manganic acid during the inorganic compounds lecture.
Because manganic acid is unstable, it cannot be isolated in pure form.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: manganate - an ion or salt containing manganese in the +6 oxidation state
  • Degree Higher: permanganic acid - an acid containing manganese in a higher oxidation state (HMO4)

Etymology

From 'manganic', formed from manganese plus the chemical suffix -ic (indicating higher oxidation state), and 'acid'.
