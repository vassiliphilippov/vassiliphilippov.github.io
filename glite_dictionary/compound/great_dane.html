Great Dane

1. NOUN

/ ɡreɪt deɪn / (AmE), / ɡreɪt deɪn / (BrE)
a very large breed of dog with a smooth coat, known for its strength and stature
a giant breed of dog

Examples

The Great Dane towered over the other dogs in the park, drawing admiring glances from everyone.
Despite his size, a Great Dane can be surprisingly gentle and affectionate with children.
Their family adopted two Great Danes, and now their house feels even more lively.

Etymology

Named after the French 'grand Danois' (great Danish dog), though the breed's origins are German.
