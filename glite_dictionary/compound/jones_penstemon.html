Jones' penstemon

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒoʊnz ˈpɛnstəˌmɒn / (AmE), / ˈdʒəʊnz ˈpɛnstəmən / (BrE)
A low-growing plant of the western United States, having light blue and violet flowers in short clusters near the tips of its stems.
plant with blue-violet flower clusters (Nevada–Utah)

Examples

We spotted several Jones' penstemon blooming along the rocky hillside in Utah.
The conservation group works to protect Jones' penstemon from habitat loss.
Botanists identified the plant as Jones' penstemon due to its distinctive blue-violet flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: penstemon - a genus of plants with tubular flowers, mainly found in North America

Etymology

Named after American botanist Marcus E. Jones and the genus Penstemon.
