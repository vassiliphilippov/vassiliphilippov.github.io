common facial vein

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmən ˈfeɪʃəl veɪn / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən ˈfeɪʃəl veɪn / (BrE)
a vein formed by the union of the facial vein and the retromandibular vein, which empties into the jugular vein
a vein in the head formed from facial and retromandibular veins
medical

Examples

The common facial vein is visible during detailed anatomical dissection of the neck.
Injury to the common facial vein can cause significant bleeding in surgical procedures.
Blood from the face drains into the internal jugular vein through the common facial vein.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: facial vein - vein in the face that forms part of the common facial vein
  • Synonym: retromandibular vein - vein that joins with the facial vein to form the common facial vein
  • Synonym: jugular vein - large vein in the neck into which the common facial vein empties
