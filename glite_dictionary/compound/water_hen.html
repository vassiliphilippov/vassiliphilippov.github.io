water hen

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔt̬ɚ hɛn / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə hɛn / (BrE)
any of several small aquatic birds of the genus Gallinula, having a frontal shield and a resemblance to domestic hens; also called moorhen.
aquatic bird of the genus Gallinula

Examples

We watched a water hen gliding gracefully across the pond near the reeds.
The distinctive red frontal shield makes the water hen easy to identify among other water birds.
A family of water hens built their nest on the riverbank last spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moorhen - an aquatic bird of the genus Gallinula, synonym for water hen

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɔtər hɛn / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə hɛn / (BrE)
a coot found in North America; a waterbird with a lobed bill and feet, related to the moorhen.
a North American coot (waterbird)

Examples

A flock of water hens gathered at the edge of the lake.
We often see water hens paddling through the reeds in early spring.
The distinctive call of the water hen echoed across the marsh.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coot - an aquatic bird with a white bill and lobed toes
  • Synonym: moorhen - a similar waterbird, closely related to the coot
