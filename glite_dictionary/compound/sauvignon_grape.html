Sauvignon grape

1. NOUN

/ soʊˈviːnjən ɡreɪp / (AmE), / səʊˈviːnjən ɡreɪp / (BrE)
A variety of small blue-black grape from the Médoc region of France, highly prized for use in winemaking, especially in wines such as Sauvignon Blanc and Cabernet Sauvignon.
grape variety used in winemaking

Examples

The Sauvignon grape is a key ingredient in many renowned French wines.
Wine producers select the best Sauvignon grapes to ensure high-quality vintages.
Some of the world's most famous white wines are made from the Sauvignon grape.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cabernet Sauvignon - a type of red wine grape and associated wine
  • Synonym: Sauvignon Blanc - a type of white wine grape and associated wine

Etymology

From French 'sauvignon', possibly from 'sauvage' (wild), due to the grape's wild origins.
