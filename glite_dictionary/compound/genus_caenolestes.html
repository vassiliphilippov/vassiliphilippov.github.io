genus Caenolestes

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs siːˌnɒˈlɛstiz / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs siːˌnɒˈlɛstiːz / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Caenolestidae, comprising several species of shrew opossums native to South America.
type genus of Caenolestidae (shrew opossums)

Examples

The genus Caenolestes includes several rare marsupials found in South American forests.
Scientists discovered a new species belonging to the genus Caenolestes in the Andes.
The dental characteristics of the genus Caenolestes set it apart from other marsupials.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Caenolestidae - family of small South American marsupials

Etymology

From New Latin Caenolestes, from Greek kainos (new) + lestes (robber).
