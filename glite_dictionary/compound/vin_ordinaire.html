vin ordinaire

1. NOUN

/ vɛ̃ ɔːr.dɪˈnɛr / (AmE), / vɛ̃ ɔː.dɪˈnɛər / (BrE)
an inexpensive French table wine of ordinary quality and unspecified origin, typically served as a daily drink rather than chosen for distinction
inexpensive French table wine

Examples

At the bistro, we ordered a carafe of vin ordinaire to accompany our meal.
Instead of a fine vintage, the host served a simple vin ordinaire with lunch.
She prefers to drink vin ordinaire with cheese rather than more expensive wines.

Etymology

From French 'vin ordinaire' meaning 'ordinary wine'.
