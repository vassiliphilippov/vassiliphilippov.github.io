retention basin

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈtɛnʃən ˈbeɪsən / (AmE), / rɪˈtɛnʃən ˈbeɪsɪn / (BrE)
An artificial pond or reservoir designed to permanently impound stormwater to prevent downstream flooding and improve water quality.
pond that permanently stores stormwater

Examples

The city built a retention basin to control runoff during heavy rains.
Wildlife has begun to thrive around the new retention basin in the park.
The retention basin helped prevent flooding in the neighborhood after the storm.
