earth-nut pea

1. NOUN

/ ˈɜrθ.nʌt pi / (AmE), / ˈɜːθ.nʌt piː / (BrE)
A European herb (Lathyrus tuberosus) bearing small edible tubers, sometimes used in Scotland to flavor whiskey.
European plant with edible tubers

Examples

They foraged for earth-nut pea during their hike in the Scottish countryside.
The chef added earth-nut pea to the traditional dish for a unique flavor.
Farmers sometimes grow earth-nut peas as a specialty crop for local markets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tuberous pea - another name for earth-nut pea, a plant with edible tubers
