mashie niblick

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæʃi ˈnɪblɪk / (AmE), / ˈmaʃi ˈnɪblɪk / (BrE)
an iron golf club with a lofted face, used for hitting high shots onto the green; now obsolete
archaic golf club for high-to-green shots
archaic

Examples

The old golfer reached for his trusty mashie niblick to loft the ball onto the green.
Back in the day, players often carried a mashie niblick for tricky approach shots.
He found a vintage mashie niblick at the antique sports shop.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: iron - a type of golf club with a flat, angled face

Etymology

From Scottish 'mashie' (a type of golf club) and 'niblick' (another club), reflecting a transitional club between the two.
