Yana language

1. NOUN

/ ˈjɑːnə ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈjɑːnə ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
an extinct Native American language formerly spoken by the Yana people in Northern California
extinct Native American language of the Yana people

Examples

Linguists have studied the Yana language to learn more about California's indigenous cultures.
Only a handful of recordings of the Yana language exist today.
The last native speaker of the Yana language died in the early 20th century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Yana - the indigenous people associated with the Yana language
  • Degree Higher: Yuman language - another Native American language family

Etymology

From Yana, the name of the indigenous people, plus 'language'.
