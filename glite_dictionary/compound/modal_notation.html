modal notation

1. NOUN

/ ˈmoʊdəl noʊˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈməʊdəl nəʊˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
a system of musical notation used in the medieval period, based on rhythmic modes, which established patterns for organizing rhythm in polyphonic music
medieval system of rhythmic musical notation

Examples

The development of modal notation allowed composers to write music with more precise rhythmic structures.
Scholars often study modal notation to understand the evolution of Western musical rhythm.
Before the invention of modal notation, medieval music lacked clear rhythmic organization.

Synset Examples

Modal notation was developed by the composers of the Notre Dame school from 1170 to 1250, replacing the even and unmeasured rhythm of early polyphony and plainchant with patterns based on the metric feet of classical poetry, and was the first step towards the development of modern mensural notation.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: mensural notation - system of musical notation that succeeded modal notation, providing precise rhythmic values
