genus Peperomia

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˌpɛpəˈroʊmiə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˌpiːpəˈrəʊmɪə / (BrE)
a large genus of small, mostly tropical, usually succulent herbs belonging to the family Piperaceae
genus of tropical succulent herbs

Examples

The genus Peperomia contains over 1,000 species of small, ornamental plants.
Many horticulturists appreciate the diversity found within the genus Peperomia.
Leaves from the genus Peperomia are often thick and succulent, making them popular as houseplants.

Etymology

From New Latin 'Peperomia', based on Greek 'peperi' (pepper) and 'homoios' (similar), referring to similarity to pepper plants.
