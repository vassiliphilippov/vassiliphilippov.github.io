hasty defence

1. NOUN

/ ˈheɪsti dɪˈfɛns / (AmE), / ˈheɪsti dɪˈfɛns / (BrE)
a defensive military position or response that is organized quickly, while under pressure from the enemy or with little preparation time
rapidly organized military defense

Examples

Under enemy fire, the unit established a hasty defence to hold their position until reinforcements arrived.
A hasty defence can be effective when time is too short for a detailed defensive plan.
The soldiers’ ability to form a hasty defence was crucial for the survival of their outpost.
