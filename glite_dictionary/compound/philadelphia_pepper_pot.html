Philadelphia pepper pot

1. NOUN

/ ˌfɪləˌdɛlfiə ˈpɛpər pɒt / (AmE), / ˌfɪləˌdɛlfiə ˈpɛpə pɒt / (BrE)
A spicy soup made with vegetables, tripe, and peppercorns, often containing dumplings, traditionally associated with Philadelphia.
traditional spicy soup from Philadelphia

Examples

The chef prepared a hearty Philadelphia pepper pot for the winter festival.
I had never tasted Philadelphia pepper pot until I visited Pennsylvania.
The recipe for Philadelphia pepper pot has been passed down in her family for generations.

Etymology

Named after Philadelphia, Pennsylvania, where the dish became popular in the 19th century.
