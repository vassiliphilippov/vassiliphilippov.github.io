genus Anas

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˈænəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈænəs / (BrE)
The type genus of the Anatidae family, comprising many species of freshwater ducks.
type genus of ducks (Anatidae)

Examples

The mallard, one of the best-known species, belongs to the genus Anas.
Biologists study the evolutionary relationships within the genus Anas to understand duck diversity.
Many freshwater ducks, including the pintail and teal, are classified under the genus Anas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: duck - a waterbird with a broad blunt bill, short legs, and a waddling gait

Etymology

From Latin 'anas' meaning 'duck'.
