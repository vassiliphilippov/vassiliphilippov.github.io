aculeated edge

1. NOUN

/ əˈkjuːliˌeɪtɪd ɛdʒ / (AmE), / əˈkjuːliˌeɪtɪd ɛdʒ / (BrE)
a sharp, pointed edge, often on leaves or surfaces, that serves to repel or deter animals
sharp-pointed edge that deters animals

Examples

The aculeated edge of the plant leaf prevents most animals from grazing on it.
Some insects develop shells with an aculeated edge to ward off predators.
Scientists observed that the aculeated edge on the seed pod effectively limited its consumption by birds.

Synset Examples

The aculeated edges of the leaf deter herbivores.

Etymology

From Latin aculeatus (having a spine or prickle), from aculeus (a prickle, sting).
