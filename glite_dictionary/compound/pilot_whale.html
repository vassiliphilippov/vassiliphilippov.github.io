pilot whale

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪlət weɪl / (AmE), / ˈpaɪlət weɪl / (BrE)
a small, dark-colored whale found mainly in the Atlantic, known for its social behavior and for the largest male acting as leader of the group
a species of small, dark whale led by a dominant male

Examples

The pilot whale is often seen swimming in large groups along the Atlantic coast.
A stranded pilot whale was rescued by marine biologists last weekend.
Unlike other cetaceans, the pilot whale can be identified by its rounded forehead and robust body.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: whale - a large marine mammal belonging to the order Cetacea

Etymology

Named for the belief that the largest male acts as the 'pilot' or leader of the group.
