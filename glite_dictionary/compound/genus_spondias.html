genus Spondias

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs spɒnˈdaɪ.əs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈspɒn.dɪ.as / (BrE)
A genus of tropical trees producing one-seeded fruit, known for species like the hog plum and ambarella.
genus of tropical fruit trees

Examples

The genus Spondias includes several trees prized for their edible fruits in tropical regions.
Botanists often study the genus Spondias for its diverse adaptations to different tropical environments.
Mangoes are unrelated, but some fruits from the genus Spondias are locally called wild plums.

Etymology

From Latin 'Spondias', the Latinized form of the Greek 'Spondias', a type of plum tree.
