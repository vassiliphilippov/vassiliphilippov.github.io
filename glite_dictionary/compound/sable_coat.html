sable coat

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪbəl koʊt / (AmE), / ˈseɪbəl kəʊt / (BrE)
a luxurious fur coat made from the pelts of sable, a small carnivorous mammal known for its soft, dark fur
coat made of sable fur

Examples

She wore a stunning sable coat to the winter gala, drawing admiring glances from everyone.
During the auction, a vintage sable coat was sold for thousands of dollars.
He inherited his mother's prized sable coat, a symbol of elegance and luxury.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fur coat - a coat made from animal fur

Etymology

From Middle French sable (the animal), ultimately from Slavic origin; coat from Old French cote (outer garment).
