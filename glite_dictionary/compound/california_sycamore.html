California sycamore

1. NOUN

/ kəˌlɪfɔːrniə ˈsɪkəmɔr / (AmE), / kəˌlɪfəniə ˈsɪkəmɔː / (BrE)
a tall tree native to Baja California, with deciduous bark, large, palmately lobed leaves, and round clusters of flowers
tree native to Baja California with lobed leaves

Examples

The California sycamore is commonly found near streams and rivers in its native habitat.
Botanists studied the unique bark patterns of the California sycamore during their field research.
Many parks in Southern California feature the shade of a large California sycamore.
