Japanese barnyard millet

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˈpænɪz ˈbɑrnˌjɑrd ˈmɪlɪt / (AmE), / dʒəˈpænɪz ˈbɑːnˌjɑːd ˈmɪlɪt / (BrE)
a coarse annual grass cultivated in Japan and southeastern Asia for its edible seeds and as animal forage, also grown in the US as wildlife food
grain grass grown for food and forage

Examples

Farmers in Japan often cultivate Japanese barnyard millet for both grain and livestock feed.
Wild ducks feed on Japanese barnyard millet seeds in wetland areas across the southern United States.
Researchers are studying Japanese barnyard millet as a drought-resistant alternative crop.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: millet - a group of small-seeded grasses grown as cereal crops
