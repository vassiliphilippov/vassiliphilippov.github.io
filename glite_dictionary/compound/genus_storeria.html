genus Storeria

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs stɔːˈrɛr.i.ə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs stɔːˈrɪə / (BrE)
A genus of snakes in the Colubridae family, including several species of brown snake and the redbelly snake.
genus of small North American snakes

Examples

The genus Storeria includes several small, harmless snake species native to North America.
Herpetologists are studying the distribution of the genus Storeria in wetland habitats.
One well-known member of the genus Storeria is the redbelly snake.

Etymology

Storeria named after American zoologist David Humphreys Storer; 'genus' from Latin genus (kind, type).
