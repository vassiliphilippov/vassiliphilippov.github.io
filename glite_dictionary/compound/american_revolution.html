American Revolution

1. PROPN

/ əˈmɛrɪkən ˌrɛvəˈluʃən / (AmE), / əˈmɛrɪkən ˌrɛvəˈluːʃən / (BrE)
the war of independence fought by the thirteen American colonies against Great Britain from 1775 to 1783, resulting in the formation of the United States
the 1775–1783 war for U.S. independence

Examples

The American Revolution marked a significant turning point in world history.
Many books explore the causes and outcomes of the American Revolution.
The ideals of the American Revolution influenced democratic movements around the globe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Revolutionary War - another common name for the American Revolution
  • Synonym: War of Independence - alternative term for the American Revolution
