Khoisan language

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔɪˌsɑn ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈkɔɪsɑːn ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
a group of indigenous languages spoken primarily in southern Africa, notable for their use of click consonants
language family of southern Africa

Examples

Linguists have studied the Khoisan language family to understand the origins of click sounds.
Many speakers of Khoisan language communities now also use Bantu languages in daily life.
The Khoisan language is under threat as fewer young people learn it.

Etymology

From Khoikhoi and San, names of indigenous peoples of southern Africa.
