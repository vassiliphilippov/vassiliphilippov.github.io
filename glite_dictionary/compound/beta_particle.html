beta particle

1. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪtə ˈpɑrtɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈbiːtə ˈpɑːtɪkl̩ / (BrE)
a high-speed electron or positron emitted during the decay of a radioactive isotope
electron/positron from radioactive decay

Examples

During nuclear decay, a beta particle can be emitted from the nucleus of an atom.
Researchers measured the energy of each beta particle using a special detector.
Exposure to a beta particle can be harmful without proper shielding.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: alpha particle - a positively charged particle emitted from some radioactive nuclei
  • Synonym: positron - the antiparticle of the electron, with a positive charge
  • Synonym: electron - a subatomic particle with a negative charge

Etymology

From Greek 'beta', the second letter of the Greek alphabet, used in scientific naming.
