withdrawing room

1. NOUN

/ wɪðˈdrɔɪɪŋ ˌruːm / (AmE), / wɪðˈdrɔːɪŋ ˌruːm / (BrE)
A formal room in a large house where visitors can be received and entertained, traditionally used for private gatherings away from the main hall.
formal room for entertaining visitors

Examples

The guests gathered in the withdrawing room for tea after dinner.
Their historic home still has a beautifully furnished withdrawing room overlooking the gardens.
In Victorian novels, the withdrawing room is often the setting for important conversations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drawing room - formal reception room in a large house

Etymology

From 'withdrawing' (retiring, going away) + 'room', originally meaning a room to withdraw to.
