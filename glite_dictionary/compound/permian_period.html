Permian period

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɜr.mi.ən ˈpɪr.i.əd / (AmE), / ˈpɜː.mi.ən ˈpɪə.ri.əd / (BrE)
the last period of the Paleozoic Era, lasting from about 299 to 251 million years ago, notable for the diversification of reptiles and ending with a major mass extinction
a geologic period in the Paleozoic Era

Examples

Many fossils found in the Permian period rocks provide evidence of early reptilian evolution.
The Permian period ended with the largest mass extinction in Earth's history.
Geologists discovered new insights about climate change during the Permian period.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Carboniferous period - the geologic period before the Permian
  • Degree Higher: Triassic period - the geologic period after the Permian

Etymology

Named after the Russian region of Perm, where rocks from this period were first studied.
