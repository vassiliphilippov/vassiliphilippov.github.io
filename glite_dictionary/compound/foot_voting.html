foot voting

1. NOUN

/ fʊt ˈvoʊtɪŋ / (AmE), / fʊt ˈvəʊtɪŋ / (BrE)
The act of expressing political or personal preference by moving to a different location or by taking action, rather than by casting a formal vote.
expressing preference by moving or acting

Examples

Many families participate in foot voting by moving to communities with better schools.
Some economists argue that foot voting is a powerful form of democratic expression.
The decline in city population was partly due to foot voting in response to high taxes.

Synset Examples

Foot voting is crucial in determining community priorities.
He believed foot voting was the best way to express opinions.
The election results reflected foot voting from the local residents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exit - the act of leaving, especially to demonstrate opinion or preference
  • Antonym: voice - expressing preference through direct communication, as opposed to leaving
