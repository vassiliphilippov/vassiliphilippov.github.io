Angas language

1. NOUN

/ ˈæŋɡəs ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈæŋɡəs ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
a Chadic language spoken in northern Nigeria, closely related to Hausa and including dialects such as Hill Angas and Plain Angas
Chadic language of northern Nigeria

Examples

Linguists have studied the Angas language for its unique tonal patterns.
The Angas language is taught in some schools in Plateau State, Nigeria.
Several books have been published to preserve the Angas language and its dialects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hausa - a major Chadic language of West Africa

Etymology

Named after the Angas people of Nigeria.
