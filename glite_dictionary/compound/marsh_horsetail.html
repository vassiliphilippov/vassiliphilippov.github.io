marsh horsetail

1. NOUN

/ mɑrʃ ˈhɔrˌstɛɪl / (AmE), / mɑːʃ ˈhɔːsteɪl / (BrE)
a species of horsetail (Equisetum palustre) commonly found in wet or boggy areas of the Northern Hemisphere
species of horsetail in wet areas

Examples

The marsh horsetail grows abundantly in the soggy fields near the riverbank.
Scientists studied the ecological impact of marsh horsetail in northern wetlands.
Many gardeners struggle to remove marsh horsetail from waterlogged soil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horsetail - a plant of the genus Equisetum, with jointed stems and brush-like branches
