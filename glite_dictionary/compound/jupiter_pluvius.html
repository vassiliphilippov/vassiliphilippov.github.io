Jupiter Pluvius

1. PROPN

/ ˈdʒuːpɪtɚ ˈpluːviəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒuːpɪtə ˈpluːvɪəs / (BrE)
An epithet of the Roman god Jupiter, signifying his role as the bringer of rain.
epithet of Jupiter as bringer of rain

Examples

In ancient Rome, people prayed to Jupiter Pluvius during periods of drought.
References to Jupiter Pluvius are common in classical literature discussing weather deities.
The temple dedicated to Jupiter Pluvius was believed to protect the city from dry spells.

Etymology

From Latin 'Jupiter' (Roman god) and 'pluvius' (rain-bringing).
