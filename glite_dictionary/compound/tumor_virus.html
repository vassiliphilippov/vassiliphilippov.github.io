tumor virus

1. NOUN

/ ˈtuːmər ˈvaɪrəs / (AmE), / ˈtjuːmə ˈvaɪərəs / (BrE)
a virus that is capable of causing the formation of tumors in animals or humans
virus that causes tumors
medical

Examples

Scientists have identified a tumor virus linked to certain types of skin cancer.
The study focused on how a tumor virus alters the host cell's DNA.
Some vaccines target specific tumor viruses to prevent infection and reduce cancer risk.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oncovirus - a virus that can cause cancer
