Northern Rhodesia

1. PROPN

/ ˈnɔrðərn roʊˈdiːʒə / (AmE), / ˈnɔːðən rəʊˈdiːzɪə / (BrE)
the former name of Zambia when it was a British protectorate in south-central Africa, prior to independence in 1964
former British colony; now Zambia

Examples

In 1964, Northern Rhodesia gained independence and became known as Zambia.
Many historical documents from the colonial era refer to the area as Northern Rhodesia.
My grandfather worked in Northern Rhodesia during the late 1950s.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Zambia - a landlocked country in south-central Africa; formerly Northern Rhodesia
  • Synonym: Southern Rhodesia - historical region in southern Africa; now Zimbabwe

Etymology

Named after British imperialist Cecil Rhodes; 'Northern' to distinguish it from Southern Rhodesia (now Zimbabwe).
