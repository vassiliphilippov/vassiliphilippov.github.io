exterior angle

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˈstɪriɚ ˈæŋɡəl / (AmE), / ɪkˈstɪəriə ˈæŋɡəl / (BrE)
An angle formed outside a polygon by one side and the extension of an adjacent side, equal to the supplement of the interior angle.
angle outside a polygon

Examples

The sum of the exterior angles of any polygon is always 360 degrees.
To find an exterior angle of a regular hexagon, simply divide 360 degrees by six.
Each exterior angle of a regular pentagon measures 72 degrees.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: interior angle - an angle inside a polygon between two adjacent sides

Etymology

From Latin 'exterior' (outer) + 'angle', from Latin 'angulus' (angle).
