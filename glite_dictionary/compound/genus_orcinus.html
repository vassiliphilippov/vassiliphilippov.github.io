genus Orcinus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ɔːrˈsaɪ.nəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ɔːˈsɪn.əs / (BrE)
A genus of marine mammals that includes the killer whale (Orcinus orca).
genus of killer whales

Examples

The genus Orcinus consists of only one living species, the killer whale, known scientifically as Orcinus orca.
Scientists have studied the evolutionary history of the genus Orcinus through fossil records.
Within the family Delphinidae, the genus Orcinus is well-known for its apex predator, the killer whale.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Orcinus orca - the killer whale, the only currently recognized species in the genus
  • Degree Higher: Delphinidae - the family of marine mammals to which genus Orcinus belongs

Etymology

From Latin 'Orcinus' derived from 'Orcus,' the Roman god of the underworld, referencing the predatory nature of the killer whale.
