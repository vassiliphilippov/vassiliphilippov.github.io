Kansas City

1. PROPN

/ ˈkænzəs ˌsɪti / (AmE), / ˈkænzəs ˌsɪti / (BrE)
a major city in western Missouri at the confluence of the Kansas River and the Missouri River, adjacent to Kansas City, Kansas
city in western Missouri, U.S.

Examples

Kansas City is famous for its jazz history and vibrant barbecue scene.
They moved to Kansas City for a new job opportunity last year.
The two airports near Kansas City make it easy for visitors to travel there.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Kansas City, Kansas - city adjacent to Kansas City, Missouri, across the state line

Etymology

Named after the Kansas River, which in turn is named after the Kansa Native American people.

2. PROPN

/ ˈkænzəs ˈsɪti / (AmE), / ˈkænzəs ˈsɪti / (BrE)
a city in northeast Kansas, located on the Missouri River, adjacent to Kansas City, Missouri
city in northeast Kansas, US

Examples

She grew up in Kansas City before moving to New York for college.
The jazz scene in Kansas City has a rich and vibrant history.
We plan to visit Kansas City next summer for a barbecue festival.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Kansas City, Missouri - a large city in western Missouri adjacent to Kansas City, Kansas

Etymology

Named after the Kansas River, itself from the Kansas (Kansa) Native American tribe.
