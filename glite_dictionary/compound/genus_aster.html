genus Aster

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈæs.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈæs.tə / (BrE)
A large genus of herbaceous plants widely grown for their daisylike flowers, including many popular garden varieties.
genus of daisylike flowering herbs

Examples

Many gardeners choose genus Aster for their vibrant autumn blooms and easy care.
The genus Aster includes species native to both North America and Eurasia.
Botanists have reclassified some species once belonging to genus Aster into other genera.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aster - a plant of the genus Aster, with star-shaped flowers

Etymology

From Latin 'aster', meaning 'star', referring to the star-shaped appearance of the flowers.
