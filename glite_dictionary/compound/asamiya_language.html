Asamiya language

1. NOUN

/ əˈsɑːmiˌjɑː ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / əˈsɑːmiˌjɑː ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Indo-Aryan language spoken primarily by the people of Assam in northeastern India, closely related to Bengali.
Indo-Aryan language of Assam, India

Examples

The Asamiya language is one of the official languages of Assam, India.
She is fluent in both Bengali and Asamiya language.
Many regional newspapers are published in the Asamiya language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Assamese - the language spoken in Assam, India

Etymology

From Assamese 'Asamiya' (relating to Assam), plus 'language'.
