order Thecodontia

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdər θiːˈkoʊdɒntiə / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə θiːˈkəʊdɒntɪə / (BrE)
an extinct order of terrestrial reptiles with teeth set in sockets, living from the late Permian to Triassic periods
extinct order of reptiles (Permian–Triassic)

Examples

Many paleontologists once classified early archosaurs within the order Thecodontia.
The order Thecodontia is now considered paraphyletic, but was important to our understanding of reptile evolution.
Fossils attributed to the order Thecodontia have been found across several continents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: archosaur - a group of diapsid reptiles including all dinosaurs, pterosaurs, and modern birds and crocodiles

Etymology

From Greek 'theke' (case, socket) and 'odous' (tooth); taxonomic name.
