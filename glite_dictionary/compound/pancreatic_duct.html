pancreatic duct

1. NOUN

/ ˌpænkriˈæt̬ɪk dʌkt / (AmE), / ˌpæŋkriˈætɪk dʌkt / (BrE)
the duct that connects the pancreas to the intestine, allowing pancreatic juices to flow into the digestive tract
duct connecting pancreas and intestine
medical

Examples

The pancreatic duct carries digestive enzymes from the pancreas into the small intestine.
A blockage in the pancreatic duct can cause pancreatitis and severe abdominal pain.
Doctors use imaging techniques to check for stones or tumors in the pancreatic duct.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bile duct - the duct that conveys bile from the liver and gallbladder to the duodenum

Etymology

From 'pancreatic' (relating to the pancreas) and 'duct' (channel or tube).
