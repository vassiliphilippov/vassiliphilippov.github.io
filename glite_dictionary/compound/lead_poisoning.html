lead poisoning

1. NOUN

/ lɛd ˈpɔɪzənɪŋ / (AmE), / lɛd ˈpɔɪzənɪŋ / (BrE)
A medical condition caused by the absorption of excessive amounts of lead into the body, resulting in toxic effects.
illness from absorbing too much lead
medical

Examples

Lead poisoning in children can cause serious developmental delays and learning difficulties.
Authorities issued a warning after several cases of lead poisoning were traced to contaminated drinking water.
If untreated, lead poisoning may lead to organ damage and other long-term health complications.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poisoning - the condition of being harmed by a toxic substance
