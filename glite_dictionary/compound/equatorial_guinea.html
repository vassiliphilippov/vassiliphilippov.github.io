Equatorial Guinea

1. PROPN

/ ˌekwəˈtɔriəl ˈɡɪni / (AmE), / ˌekwətɔːrɪəl ˈɡɪni / (BrE)
a country in west central Africa, including islands in the Gulf of Guinea, which became independent from Spain in 1968
country in west central Africa

Examples

She spent several years living in Equatorial Guinea as part of a humanitarian project.
The president of Equatorial Guinea delivered a speech at the United Nations.
Oil production is a major part of Equatorial Guinea's economy.

Etymology

Named for its proximity to the Equator and the Gulf of Guinea.
