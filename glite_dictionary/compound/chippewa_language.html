Chippewa language

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɪpəwɑː ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪpɪwə ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Algonquian language traditionally spoken by the Ojibwa (Chippewa) people of North America
Algonquian language of the Ojibwa people

Examples

He is studying the Chippewa language to better understand his ancestors' culture.
Many words from the Chippewa language have been adopted into regional English dialects.
The community center offers classes in the Chippewa language for young people.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ojibwa language - the language spoken by the Ojibwa people, also called Chippewa

Etymology

Chippewa is an anglicized form of 'Ojibwa', the name of the indigenous people.
