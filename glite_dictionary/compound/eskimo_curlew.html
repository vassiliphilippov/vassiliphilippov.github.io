Eskimo curlew

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛskɪˌmoʊ ˈkɜr.lu / (AmE), / ˈɛskɪməʊ ˈkɜː.ljuː / (BrE)
a migratory shorebird (Numenius borealis) from North America, formerly abundant, now extremely rare or possibly extinct
a rare North American migratory shorebird

Examples

The sighting of an Eskimo curlew in the wild is considered extraordinarily rare today.
Many ornithologists believe the Eskimo curlew may already be extinct.
Conservationists still search northern Canada for evidence of the Eskimo curlew.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curlew - a wading bird with a long curved bill
