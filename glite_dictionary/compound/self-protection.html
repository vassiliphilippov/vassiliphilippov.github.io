self-protection

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛlf prəˈtɛkʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɛlf prəˈtɛkʃən / (BrE)
The act of defending oneself from harm or danger.
defending oneself from harm

Examples

She learned martial arts for self-protection and confidence.
Wearing a helmet when cycling is a simple form of self-protection.
Some animals have evolved unique mechanisms for self-protection against predators.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: self-defense - the act of defending oneself, especially with force, against attack
  • Antonym: vulnerability - the quality or state of being exposed to harm

Etymology

Formed from 'self' + 'protection', with 'self' from Old English 'self, sylf' and 'protection' from Latin 'protectio'.
