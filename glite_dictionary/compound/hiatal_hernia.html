hiatal hernia

1. NOUN

/ haɪˈeɪt̬l ˈhɜrniə / (AmE), / haɪˈeɪtəl ˈhɜːniə / (BrE)
A medical condition in which part of the stomach pushes up through the diaphragm into the chest cavity.
stomach protrusion through diaphragm
medical

Examples

After being diagnosed with a hiatal hernia, he experienced frequent heartburn and discomfort.
A hiatal hernia may not always cause symptoms, but it can sometimes lead to acid reflux.
Doctors often recommend lifestyle changes to manage a hiatal hernia before considering surgery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hernia - protrusion of an organ through the structure that normally contains it

Etymology

From 'hiatal', relating to a hiatus (Latin 'hiatus', opening), and 'hernia' (Latin 'hernia', rupture).
