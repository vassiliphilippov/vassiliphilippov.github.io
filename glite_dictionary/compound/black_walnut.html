black walnut

1. NOUN

/ blæk ˈwɔlnət / (AmE), / blak ˈwɔːlnʌt / (BrE)
A North American walnut tree (Juglans nigra) valued for its hard, dark wood and edible nut with a tough shell.
North American walnut tree and its nut

Examples

The wood of the black walnut is prized for making fine furniture and cabinetry.
She picked up a black walnut from the ground and struggled to crack its tough shell.
Black walnut trees provide both nutritious nuts and attractive shade in large gardens.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: English walnut - European walnut tree with a milder flavor and thinner shell
