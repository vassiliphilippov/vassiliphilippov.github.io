order Phallales

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdər fæˈleɪliːz / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə fæˈleɪliːz / (BrE)
a taxonomic order of fungi, including the stinkhorns and similar species, whose mature fruiting bodies are slimy and have a foul odor
taxonomic order of fungi including stinkhorns
medical

Examples

Members of the order Phallales are known for their distinctive, often unpleasant odor.
The scientist published a study on several rare species in the order Phallales.
Fungi of the order Phallales can be commonly found in decaying wood in some forests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stinkhorn - a type of foul-smelling fungus in the Phallales order

Etymology

From New Latin 'Phallus' (the type genus) plus the taxonomic suffix '-ales'.
