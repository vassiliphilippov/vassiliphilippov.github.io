Arabian Peninsula

1. PROPN

/ əˈreɪ.bi.ən pəˈnɪn.sjə.lə / (AmE), / əˈreɪ.bi.ən pəˈnɪn.sjʊ.lə / (BrE)
a large peninsula in Southwest Asia, situated between the Red Sea and the Persian Gulf, notable for its vast desert and significant oil resources
peninsula in Southwest Asia, between Red Sea and Persian Gulf

Examples

The Arabian Peninsula is one of the largest peninsulas in the world, known for its deserts.
Oil exports from the Arabian Peninsula have significant influence on global markets.
The climate of the Arabian Peninsula is extremely arid, with vast stretches of sand dunes.
