steam engine

1. NOUN

/ stiːm ˈɛn.dʒɪn / (AmE), / stiːm ˈɛn.dʒɪn / (BrE)
An external-combustion engine in which steam produced by heating water is used to move a piston or turn a turbine.
engine powered by steam

Examples

The steam engine was a crucial invention during the Industrial Revolution.
Many old trains were powered by a steam engine that chugged along the tracks.
James Watt made major improvements to the steam engine in the late 18th century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: locomotive - a powered rail vehicle for pulling trains, often powered by a steam engine
  • Antonym: internal combustion engine - an engine in which fuel is burned inside the engine itself

Etymology

Formed from 'steam' and 'engine', first used in the early 18th century to describe machines driven by steam power.
