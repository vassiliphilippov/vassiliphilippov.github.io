genus Stenocarpus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌstɛ.nəˈkɑːr.pəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌstiː.nəˈkɑː.pəs / (BrE)
A small genus of timber trees native to Australia and Malaysia, known for their hard wood.
genus of timber trees (Australia, Malaysia)

Examples

The genus Stenocarpus includes several species valued for their timber.
Botanists have documented the genus Stenocarpus in both Australia and Southeast Asia.
Furniture manufacturers sometimes use wood from the genus Stenocarpus for its durability.

Etymology

From Greek 'stenos' (narrow) + 'karpos' (fruit), referring to the narrow fruit of the type species.
