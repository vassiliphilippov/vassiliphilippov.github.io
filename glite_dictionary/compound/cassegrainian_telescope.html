Cassegrainian telescope

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæs.ɪˈɡreɪ.ni.ən ˈtɛl.əˌskoʊp / (AmE), / ˌkæs.ɪˈɡreɪ.ni.ən ˈtɛl.ɪˌskəʊp / (BrE)
a reflecting telescope with a paraboloidal primary mirror and a hyperboloidal secondary mirror, where light is focused through an opening in the center of the primary mirror
type of reflecting telescope

Examples

The Cassegrainian telescope design allows for a much more compact instrument compared to traditional refractors.
Astronomers prefer a Cassegrainian telescope for its clear central field and ease of use.
A Cassegrainian telescope is ideal for both amateur and professional astronomical observations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reflecting telescope - a telescope that forms images by reflecting light with mirrors

Etymology

Named after Laurent Cassegrain, 17th-century French priest and inventor.
