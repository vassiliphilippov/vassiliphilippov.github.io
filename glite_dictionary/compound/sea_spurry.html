sea spurry

1. NOUN

/ si ˈspɜri / (AmE), / si ˈspʌri / (BrE)
a low-growing weedy plant with small pink flowers, native to Europe and Asia and found on sand dunes, heaths, and coastal cliffs, now naturalized in eastern North America
a weedy pink-flowered plant of coasts and dunes

Examples

The sea spurry thrives on sandy dunes along the northern coastline.
Botanists observed patches of sea spurry near the heath, adding to the area's floral diversity.
Gardeners sometimes struggle to control sea spurry in coastal lawns due to its rapid spread.
