western blackberry

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɛstɚn ˈblækˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈwɛstən ˈblækˌbəri / (BrE)
A species of blackberry native to western North America, known for its oblong black fruit.
a blackberry species with oblong black fruit

Examples

The western blackberry thrives along the Pacific coast and produces sweet, juicy fruit.
We picked baskets full of western blackberries during our summer hike.
The pie was filled with fresh western blackberry compote, making it a regional favorite.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blackberry - a small dark purple or black fruit; the plant bearing it
