Caudine Forks

1. PROPN

/ ˌkɔːdaɪn ˈfɔrks / (AmE), / ˌkɔːdaɪn ˈfɔːks / (BrE)
the site of a battle in the Apennines in 321 BC where the Samnites decisively defeated the Roman army
site of a 321 BC Samnite victory over Rome

Examples

The defeat at the Caudine Forks was a humiliating setback for the Roman Republic.
Historians often cite the Caudine Forks as an example of a turning point in the Second Samnite War.
After the disaster at the Caudine Forks, Roman military tactics underwent significant changes.

Etymology

From Latin 'Furculae Caudinae', meaning 'Caudine Forks', named after the mountain passes near Caudium.
