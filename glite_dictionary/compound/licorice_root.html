licorice root

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪk.ərɪʃ ˌruːt / (AmE), / ˈlɪk.ərɪʃ ˌruːt / (BrE)
the root of the licorice plant, used as a flavoring in candy, liqueurs, and traditional medicines
root used for flavoring and medicine

Examples

Traditional Chinese medicine often uses licorice root to relieve coughs and sore throats.
The flavor of licorice root is distinctive and popular in both candies and herbal teas.
She bought dried licorice root to make a natural sweetener for her homemade drinks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: licorice - a sweet, chewy, black substance made from the root of the licorice plant

Etymology

From Middle English 'licores', via Old French from Late Latin 'liquiritia', referring to the sweet root of the plant.
