price control

1. NOUN

/ praɪs kənˈtroʊl / (AmE), / praɪs kənˈtrəʊl / (BrE)
A government-imposed restriction on the maximum price that can be charged for goods or services, typically enacted during times of inflation or emergency.
government-set limit on prices

Examples

The government imposed price controls on essential goods during the economic crisis.
Economists often debate the long-term effects of price control policies.
Many countries lift price controls once the market stabilizes after a period of inflation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: price ceiling - an upper limit set by the government on the price of a product or service
