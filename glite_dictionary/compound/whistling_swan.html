whistling swan

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪs·lɪŋ swɑn / (AmE), / ˈwɪs.lɪŋ swɒn / (BrE)
a North American subspecies of tundra swan (Cygnus columbianus columbianus) noted for its soft whistling call
North American subspecies of tundra swan

Examples

Each spring, thousands of whistling swans migrate along the Pacific Flyway to their breeding grounds in Alaska.
The whistling swan is easily recognized by its soft, high-pitched call as it flies overhead.
Researchers observed a flock of whistling swans resting on the tundra near the lake.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tundra swan - a bird species of the northern hemisphere known for white plumage and migratory habits
