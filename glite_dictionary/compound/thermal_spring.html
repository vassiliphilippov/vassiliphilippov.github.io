thermal spring

1. NOUN

/ ˈθɜrməl sprɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈθɜːməl sprɪŋ / (BrE)
a natural spring where water emerges at a temperature significantly higher than the surrounding air temperature, typically 70°F (21°C) or above
hot or warm natural spring

Examples

The resort is famous for its thermal spring that attracts visitors year-round.
Researchers studied the unique bacteria thriving in the thermal spring ecosystem.
We relaxed in the soothing waters of a natural thermal spring after a long hike.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hot spring - a natural spring producing water that is hot or very warm

Etymology

From Greek 'thermos' (warm, hot) + English 'spring' (source of water).
