pygmy cypress

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪɡmi ˈsaɪprəs / (AmE), / ˈpɪɡmi ˈsɪprəs / (BrE)
a rare, small cypress tree native to northern California, sometimes regarded as the same species as the Gowen cypress
rare small cypress tree of northern California

Examples

The pygmy cypress is found in only a few isolated groves in northern California.
Scientists are studying the ecology of the pygmy cypress to ensure its preservation.
Unlike larger relatives, the pygmy cypress grows just a few feet tall even at maturity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cypress - a coniferous tree of the genus Cupressus
  • Synonym: Gowen cypress - a rare species of cypress native to California

Etymology

From English 'pygmy' (diminutive) + 'cypress' (tree name from Latin 'cupressus').
