goa bean

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡoʊ.ə ˌbin / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊ.ə ˌbiːn / (BrE)
a twining annual vine of the Old World tropics, bearing purplish flowers and four-winged pods, cultivated for food.
tropical vine with four-winged pods

Examples

The farmer decided to grow goa beans because their pods are highly nutritious and versatile.
You can stir-fry goa beans with garlic and spices for a delicious side dish.
The goa bean thrives in warm, humid climates and is valued for both its leaves and pods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: winged bean - another name for the goa bean, a tropical legume with winged pods

Etymology

Named after Goa, a region in India, where the plant is found.

2. NOUN

/ ˈgoʊ.ə ˌbiːn / (AmE), / ˈgəʊ.ə ˌbiːn / (BrE)
an edible tropical plant (Psophocarpus tetragonolobus), cultivated in the Old World, noted for its winged pods and use as a vegetable
a tropical bean with winged pods

Examples

The goa bean is prized in Southeast Asian cuisine for its crunchy pods.
Farmers report that the goa bean thrives in humid, tropical climates.
You can stir-fry goa beans with garlic for a delicious side dish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: winged bean - a tropical legume known for its edible pods, synonym for goa bean
