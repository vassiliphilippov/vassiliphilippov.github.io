Third Council of Constantinople

1. PROPN

/ θɜrd ˈkaʊnsəl əv ˌkɑnstæn.tɪˈnoʊ.pəl / (AmE), / θɜːd ˈkaʊnsəl əv ˌkɒnstæn.tɪˈnəʊ.pl̩ / (BrE)
the sixth ecumenical council held in 680-681 that condemned Monothelitism by affirming Christ's divine and human wills
6th ecumenical council (680-681) condemning Monothelitism

Examples

The Third Council of Constantinople defined key doctrines on Christ's divine and human wills.
Many theologians still discuss the impact of the Third Council of Constantinople on church doctrine.
The decisions made at the Third Council of Constantinople influenced later ecumenical councils.
