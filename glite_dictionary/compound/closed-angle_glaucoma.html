closed-angle glaucoma

1. NOUN

/ kloʊzd ˈæŋɡəl ɡloʊˈkoʊmə / (AmE), / kləʊzd ˈæŋɡl ɡləʊˈkəʊmə / (BrE)
a type of glaucoma in which the iris blocks the outflow of aqueous humor, causing a rapid increase in intraocular pressure
glaucoma where iris blocks fluid, raising pressure
medical

Examples

Closed-angle glaucoma usually requires urgent medical attention to prevent permanent vision loss.
Symptoms of closed-angle glaucoma can develop suddenly and include severe eye pain and blurred vision.
If untreated, closed-angle glaucoma may lead to irreversible blindness in the affected eye.

Synset Examples

closed-angle glaucoma can cause a rapid buildup of high intraocular pressure that results in permanent visual damage in a couple of days

Related Terms

  • Antonym: open-angle glaucoma - a type of glaucoma in which the drainage angle remains open
  • Synonym: glaucoma - a group of eye conditions that damage the optic nerve
