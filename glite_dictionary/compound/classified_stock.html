classified stock

1. NOUN

/ ˈklæsɪˌfaɪd stɑk / (AmE), / ˈklasɪˌfaɪd stɒk / (BrE)
A type of common stock that is divided into classes, such as A or B, with different rights or privileges, such as voting power.
common stock divided into classes with different rights

Examples

The company issued classified stock to give founders greater control over corporate decisions.
Investors should understand the differences between Class A and Class B classified stock before buying shares.
Holders of Class A classified stock often enjoy more voting rights than holders of Class B shares.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: common stock - ordinary shares representing ownership in a corporation
