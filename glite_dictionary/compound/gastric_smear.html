gastric smear

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡæstrɪk smɪr / (AmE), / ˈɡæstrɪk smɪə / (BrE)
a laboratory test in which material obtained from the stomach is smeared on a microscope slide for examination, usually to detect infection or disease
laboratory test using stomach material
medical

Examples

The doctor ordered a gastric smear to check for the presence of bacteria.
A gastric smear can sometimes help diagnose infections causing chronic stomach inflammation.
After collecting the sample, the technician prepared a gastric smear for microscopic analysis.

Etymology

From 'gastric' (relating to the stomach) and 'smear' (a thin layer spread for examination); 'gastric' from Greek 'gaster' (stomach).
