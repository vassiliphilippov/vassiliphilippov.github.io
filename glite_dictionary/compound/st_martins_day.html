St Martin's Day

1. PROPN

/ ˌseɪnt ˈmɑrtɪnz deɪ / (AmE), / ˌseɪnt ˈmɑːtɪnz deɪ / (BrE)
the feast day celebrating Saint Martin, traditionally observed on November 11, especially in parts of Europe; a quarter day in Scotland
feast day of Saint Martin (Nov 11)

Examples

Local churches host special events on St Martin's Day to honor the saint's generosity.
In Scotland, St Martin's Day marks one of the traditional quarter days for settling rents and contracts.
Many European countries hold lantern processions to celebrate St Martin's Day every November.
