ratchet wheel

1. NOUN

/ ˈrætʃət wiːl / (AmE), / ˈratʃɪt wiːl / (BrE)
a wheel with teeth that is held in place by a pawl or detent and is turned in one direction by a lever
toothed wheel used with a pawl

Examples

The clock's mechanism relies on a ratchet wheel and pawl to move forward incrementally.
He replaced the worn ratchet wheel to restore the tool’s function.
The bicycle uses a ratchet wheel to allow the pedals to spin freely in one direction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ratchet - a device consisting of a toothed wheel or bar and a pawl that permits motion in only one direction

Etymology

From 'ratchet', of uncertain origin, possibly from French *rochet* (a blunt lance head), and 'wheel'.
