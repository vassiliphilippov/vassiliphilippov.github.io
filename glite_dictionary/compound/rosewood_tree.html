rosewood tree

1. NOUN

/ ˈroʊzˌwʊd triː / (AmE), / ˈrəʊzwʊd triː / (BrE)
A tropical hardwood tree of the genus Dalbergia, valued for its hard, dark reddish or purplish wood streaked with black, used especially in fine furniture and musical instruments.
tree yielding valuable dark, streaked hardwood

Examples

The furniture maker preferred to use rosewood trees because of their beautiful, richly colored grain.
Many rosewood tree species are now protected due to overharvesting for luxury goods.
The lumber from a rosewood tree is prized for crafting high-quality guitars and pianos.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rosewood - the wood from a rosewood tree, dark and streaked, used for furniture
