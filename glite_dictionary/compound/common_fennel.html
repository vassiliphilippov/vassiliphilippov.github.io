common fennel

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɒmən ˈfɛnəl / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən ˈfɛnəl / (BrE)
A strongly aromatic plant with feathery leaves and seeds that have an aniseed smell and are used for seasoning.
aromatic herb used for seasoning

Examples

She added fresh common fennel leaves to the salad for extra flavor.
The seeds of common fennel are used in many Mediterranean dishes.
I planted common fennel in my herb garden to attract butterflies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fennel - an aromatic plant with feathery leaves and aniseed flavor used in cooking

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmən ˈfɛnəl / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən ˈfɛnəl / (BrE)
The leaves of a Mediterranean plant, used as a herb in cooking for their aromatic flavor.
aromatic herb used in cooking

Examples

She sprinkled fresh common fennel over the roasted fish for a delicate, anise-like flavor.
The herbalist recommends using common fennel leaves in salads and soups.
At the market, I picked up a bunch of common fennel for tonight's recipe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fennel - a Mediterranean plant with feathery leaves and yellow flowers, used as a herb

Etymology

Fennel from Latin *feniculum*, diminutive of *fēnum* or *faenum* (hay).
