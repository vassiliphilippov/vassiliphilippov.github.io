Amphitheatrum Flavium

1. PROPN

/ ˌæm.fɪ.θiˈæ.trʊm ˈflɑː.vi.əm / (AmE), / ˌam.fɪ.θɪˈaː.trʊm ˈflaː.vi.ʊm / (BrE)
the formal Latin name for the Colosseum, a large ancient amphitheater in Rome built under the Flavian emperors, beginning with Vespasian around AD 75–80
Latin name for the Colosseum in Rome

Examples

The Amphitheatrum Flavium was the largest arena of its kind in the Roman world.
Historians often use the term Amphitheatrum Flavium when referring to the origins of the Colosseum.
Archaeologists have uncovered new artifacts near the Amphitheatrum Flavium during recent excavations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Colosseum - the common name for the large Roman amphitheater known historically as the Amphitheatrum Flavium

Etymology

Latin, meaning 'Flavian amphitheater', from the Flavian dynasty of Roman emperors.
