family Tarsiidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌfæməli tɑrˈsaɪɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˌfæməli tɑːˈsɪɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of primates coextensive with the genus Tarsius, comprising the tarsiers.
family of primates including tarsiers

Examples

The family Tarsiidae is known for its members' large eyes and unique mode of locomotion.
Scientists debate the evolutionary relationships within the family Tarsiidae based on genetic studies.
Conservation efforts are underway to protect endangered species within the family Tarsiidae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tarsier - a small nocturnal primate of Southeast Asia

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'Tarsiidae' (the family name, based on 'Tarsius', the genus of tarsiers) with the standard zoological suffix '-idae' for animal families.
