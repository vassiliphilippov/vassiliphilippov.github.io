electronic surveillance

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌlɛkˈtrɑnɪk sərˈveɪləns / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛkˈtrɒnɪk səˈveɪləns / (BrE)
the monitoring of people or places using electronic equipment such as cameras, microphones, or tracking devices
monitoring with electronic devices

Examples

The government increased electronic surveillance to prevent terrorist activities.
Many businesses rely on electronic surveillance to protect their property.
Concerns about privacy have grown with the rise of electronic surveillance in public spaces.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: surveillance - close observation, especially of a suspected person
