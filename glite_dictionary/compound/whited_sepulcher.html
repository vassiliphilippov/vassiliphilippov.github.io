whited sepulcher

1. NOUN

/ ˈwaɪtɪd ˈsɛpəlkɚ / (AmE), / ˈwaɪtɪd ˈsɛp(ə)lkə / (BrE)
A person who pretends to be virtuous or morally upright but is actually corrupt or wicked inside; a hypocrite.
hypocrite; outwardly good, inwardly corrupt person
archaic

Examples

He was revealed as a whited sepulcher, hiding his dishonest business dealings behind a charitable reputation.
In the novel, the mayor is portrayed as a whited sepulcher, respected by many but secretly cruel.
They called her a whited sepulcher because her words preached kindness while her actions were spiteful.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hypocrite - A person who pretends to have virtues or moral beliefs they do not actually possess

Etymology

From the biblical phrase in Matthew 23:27, where Jesus compares hypocrites to 'whited sepulchers'—tombs made beautiful on the outside but full of decay within.
