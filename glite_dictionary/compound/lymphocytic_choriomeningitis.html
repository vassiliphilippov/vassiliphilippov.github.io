lymphocytic choriomeningitis

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɪmfəˈsɪtɪk ˌkɔrioʊˌmɛnɪnˈdʒaɪtɪs / (AmE), / ˌlɪmfəˈsɪtɪk ˌkɔːriəʊmɛnɪnˈdʒaɪtɪs / (BrE)
a viral infection of the meninges caused by a virus carried by house mice, leading to symptoms similar to meningitis
viral infection of meninges from mice
medical

Examples

The doctor suspected lymphocytic choriomeningitis after the patient presented with headache and fever.
People can contract lymphocytic choriomeningitis through exposure to rodent urine or droppings.
Researchers are studying new treatments for lymphocytic choriomeningitis due to its neurological complications.

Etymology

From 'lymphocytic' (relating to lymphocytes) + 'choriomeningitis' (inflammation of the choroid and meninges), from Greek 'chorion' (membrane) and 'meninx' (membrane).
