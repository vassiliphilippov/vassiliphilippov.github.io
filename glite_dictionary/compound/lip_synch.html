lip synch

1. NOUN

/ lɪp sɪŋk / (AmE), / lɪp sɪŋk / (BrE)
the technique of matching the movement of a speaker's lips with pre-recorded spoken or sung audio in a film, video, or live performance
matching lip movements to prerecorded audio

Examples

The actor had to lip synch to the song during the performance.
The music video was shot in one take, but required perfect lip synch.
She practiced her lip synch to make sure it matched the recorded dialogue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lip sync - alternative spelling of 'lip synch' used in American English

Etymology

Blend of 'lip' and 'synch,' a shortening of 'synchronize.'
