functional genomics

1. NOUN

/ ˈfʌŋkʃənl dʒəˈnoʊmɪks / (AmE), / ˈfʌŋkʃənl dʒɪˈnəʊmɪks / (BrE)
the branch of genomics that studies the roles and interactions of genes and their products to determine their biological functions
study of gene functions within genomics
medical

Examples

Functional genomics helps scientists understand how genes contribute to complex diseases.
Recent advances in functional genomics have accelerated drug discovery and development.
Many research institutions now have dedicated functional genomics labs to study gene expression.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: genomics - the study of genomes, including their structure, function, evolution, and mapping

Etymology

From 'functional' (relating to function) and 'genomics' (the study of genomes, from 'gene' and '-omics').
