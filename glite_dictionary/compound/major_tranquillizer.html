major tranquillizer

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪdʒɚ trænˈkwɪlaɪzɚ / (AmE), / ˈmeɪdʒə træŋˈkwɪlaɪzə / (BrE)
a powerful tranquilizer used to treat psychotic conditions, especially to produce a calming effect; an antipsychotic drug
antipsychotic drug; strong tranquilizer
medical

Examples

After his diagnosis, the doctor prescribed a major tranquillizer to help manage his symptoms.
Some patients respond well to a major tranquillizer in cases of severe agitation.
The use of a major tranquillizer requires careful monitoring by medical professionals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antipsychotic - drug used to treat psychotic disorders; synonym for major tranquillizer
  • Degree Lower: minor tranquillizer - a weaker type of tranquillizer, often used to treat anxiety; degree lower than major tranquillizer
