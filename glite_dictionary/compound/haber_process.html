Haber process

1. NOUN

/ ˈheɪbər ˌprɑːses / (AmE), / ˈhɑːbə ˌprəʊses / (BrE)
an industrial method for producing ammonia by reacting nitrogen and hydrogen gases under high pressure and temperature in the presence of an iron catalyst
industrial process for making ammonia

Examples

The Haber process revolutionized agriculture by making large-scale fertilizer production possible.
Many chemical plants worldwide rely on the Haber process to produce ammonia efficiently.
The invention of the Haber process had a major impact on food production and population growth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ammonia synthesis - the production of ammonia by chemical means

Etymology

Named after German chemist Fritz Haber (1868–1934) who developed the process.
