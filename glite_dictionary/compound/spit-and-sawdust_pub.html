spit-and-sawdust pub

1. NOUN

/ spɪt ənd ˈsɔ dʌst pʌb / (AmE), / spɪt ənd ˈsɔːdəst pʌb / (BrE)
A simple, old-fashioned, and often shabby pub, typically with a basic, unpretentious atmosphere.
plain, old-fashioned, basic pub
british

Examples

His friends preferred the charm of a spit-and-sawdust pub to a modern cocktail lounge.
We ducked into a spit-and-sawdust pub after the rain started, seeking warmth and cheap drinks.
She avoided the trendy bars and chose a spit-and-sawdust pub where the locals gathered.

Synset Examples

We stopped at a spit-and-sawdust pub for a quick drink.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dive bar - a disreputable or shabby bar

Etymology

Refers to the former practice of spreading sawdust on the floor of pubs, with patrons sometimes spitting on the floor.
