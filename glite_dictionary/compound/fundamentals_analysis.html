fundamentals analysis

1. NOUN

/ ˌfʌndəˈmɛntəlz əˈnæləsɪs / (AmE), / ˌfʌndəˈmɛntəlz əˈnæləsɪs / (BrE)
An investment strategy involving the examination of a company's fundamental financial and economic factors to assess its value.
evaluation of basic financial data for investing

Examples

Many investors rely on fundamentals analysis to determine which stocks to buy for the long term.
A thorough fundamentals analysis of the company revealed strong potential for future growth.
She prefers fundamentals analysis over technical indicators when making investment decisions.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: technical analysis - Analysis based on market data and price charts rather than fundamentals

Etymology

From 'fundamentals' (basic elements) + 'analysis' (detailed examination).
