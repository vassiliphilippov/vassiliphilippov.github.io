beggar-my-neighbour strategy

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛɡɚ maɪ ˈneɪbɚ ˈstrætədʒi / (AmE), / ˈbɛɡə maɪ ˈneɪbə ˈstrætədʒi / (BrE)
an economic or political policy that benefits one country or party while harming others, often by shifting problems or costs onto them
self-serving policy harming others

Examples

The government adopted a beggar-my-neighbour strategy during the global financial crisis.
Trade wars are often viewed as a classic example of a beggar-my-neighbour strategy.
Many economists warn against the long-term effects of a beggar-my-neighbour strategy.

Synset Examples

the United States has pursued a beggar-my-neighbor policy

Etymology

From the phrase 'beggar my neighbour' (to impoverish another to one's own advantage), likely referencing the card game of the same name.
