precious metal

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɛʃəs ˈmɛt̬l̩ / (AmE), / ˈprɛʃəs ˈmɛtl / (BrE)
a rare, naturally occurring metal of high economic value, often used in jewelry, coins, and investment, such as gold, silver, or platinum.
valuable rare metal (e.g. gold, silver)

Examples

Gold is the most well-known precious metal and has been valued for centuries.
The price of a precious metal like platinum can fluctuate on the global market.
She inherited a collection of coins made from different precious metals.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: base metal - a common and inexpensive metal, not a precious metal
