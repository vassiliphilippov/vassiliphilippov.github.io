Chinese cork oak

1. NOUN

/ t͡ʃaɪˈniːz kɔːrk oʊk / (AmE), / t͡ʃʌɪˈniːz kɔːk əʊk / (BrE)
a medium to large deciduous tree native to China, Japan, and Korea, notable for its thick corky bark
species of oak tree native to East Asia

Examples

The Chinese cork oak is cultivated for its thick, corky bark used in production.
A mature Chinese cork oak can reach impressive heights in the wild.
Botanists study the Chinese cork oak for its unique ecological and economic value.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: oak - a tree of the genus Quercus, bearing acorns
  • Synonym: cork oak - oak tree species whose bark is harvested for cork
