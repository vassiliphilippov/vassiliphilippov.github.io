conjure man

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌn.dʒər mæn / (AmE), / ˈkʌn.dʒə mæn / (BrE)
a man who practices folk magic or witchcraft, especially as a healer or diviner in certain cultures
male folk magician; witch doctor

Examples

The locals believed the conjure man could cure any illness with his herbs and spells.
Stories about the mysterious conjure man were passed down through generations.
Seeking help, she visited the conjure man for protection against bad luck.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: witch doctor - a traditional healer using folk magic or rituals
  • Synonym: conjurer - person who practices magic or illusions

Etymology

From 'conjure' (to cast magic) + 'man'; 'conjure' from Old French 'conjurer', from Latin 'conjurare' (to swear together, to conspire or invoke).
