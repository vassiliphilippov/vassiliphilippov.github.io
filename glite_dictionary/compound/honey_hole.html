honey hole

1. NOUN

/ ˈhʌni hoʊl / (AmE), / ˈhʌni həʊl / (BrE)
A place especially rich in resources or opportunities, often used to describe a favored fishing spot where catches are plentiful.
productive or favored spot, esp. for fishing

Examples

After hours of searching, they finally stumbled upon a honey hole teeming with trout.
This thrift store is my secret honey hole for vintage finds.
Locals guard the location of the old mining honey hole closely.

Synset Examples

He found a fishing honey hole last summer.
They discovered a honey hole for rare plants.
This spot is a honey hole for treasure hunters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hot spot - a place of intense activity or resources

Etymology

Based on 'honey' as something desirable and 'hole', a place; origin in fishing jargon.
