inert gas

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈnɜrt ɡæs / (AmE), / ɪˈnɜːt ɡas / (BrE)
any of the gaseous elements in group 18 of the periodic table (such as helium, neon, argon, krypton, xenon, and radon) that are chemically unreactive under most conditions.
chemically unreactive gaseous element

Examples

Helium is an inert gas commonly used to fill balloons because it does not react with other substances.
The laboratory was filled with an inert gas to prevent unwanted chemical reactions during the experiment.
Argon, an inert gas, is often used in welding to protect the metal from oxidation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: noble gas - chemically unreactive gaseous element in group 18 of the periodic table

Etymology

Formed from 'inert' (unreactive) + 'gas', referring to their lack of chemical reactivity.
