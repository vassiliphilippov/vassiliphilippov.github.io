shallow-draught

1. ADJ

/ ˈʃæloʊ dræft / (AmE), / ˈʃæləʊ drɑːft / (BrE)
(of a vessel) having a keel that does not extend far below the waterline, allowing it to navigate shallow waters.
having a keel close to the waterline (of vessels)

Examples

The new ferry is a shallow-draught vessel designed to operate on narrow rivers.
Due to its shallow-draught, the boat can safely cross over sandbanks at low tide.
Engineers recommended a shallow-draught design for better access to remote harbors.

Synset Examples

a shallow-draft river boat

Related Terms

  • Antonym: deep-draught - (of a vessel) having a keel that extends deep below the waterline
  • Synonym: shallow-draft - alternative form (especially US) meaning the same as 'shallow-draught'

Etymology

draught from Old English 'drægth', linked to the verb 'draw'; shallow from Old English 'sceald'.
