Chabad Hasidism

1. NOUN

/ xəˈbɑd həˈsɪdɪzəm / (AmE), / xəˈbad həˈsɪdɪzəm / (BrE)
A branch of Hasidic Judaism originating in Eastern Europe, known for its intellectual approach and associated with the Lubavitch movement.
a branch of Hasidic Judaism

Examples

The teachings of Chabad Hasidism emphasize the use of intellect in spiritual life.
Many families in Crown Heights are followers of Chabad Hasidism.
Under Soviet rule, the adherents of Chabad Hasidism maintained their traditions despite difficulties.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Hasidism - mystical Jewish religious movement originating in Eastern Europe in the 18th century
  • Synonym: Lubavitch - another name for the Chabad movement, the largest Hasidic group

Etymology

Chabad from Hebrew חב"ד (ḤabaD), acronym for ḥokhmah (wisdom), binah (understanding), da'at (knowledge), originally a movement founded by Rabbi Schneur Zalman in 18th-century Russia.
