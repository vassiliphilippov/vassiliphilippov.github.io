surf fish

1. NOUN

/ sɝf fɪʃ / (AmE), / sɜːf fɪʃ / (BrE)
a small to medium-sized shallow-water fish found along the Pacific coast of North America, belonging to the family Embiotocidae
Pacific coast shallow-water fish

Examples

Many anglers on the West Coast enjoy catching surf fish from sandy beaches.
The diet of surf fish consists mainly of small crustaceans and mollusks.
During spawning season, surf fish can be seen gathering in the shallow waters near the shore.

2. NOUN

/ sɝf fɪʃ / (AmE), / sɜːf fɪʃ / (BrE)
a fish of the Pacific coast of North America, commonly found in surf zones
fish found in Pacific coastal surf zones

Examples

Anglers often catch surf fish using bait in shallow waters close to the shore.
The surf fish is well adapted to surviving turbulent waves along the Pacific beaches.
Many coastal communities rely on surf fish as a local food source.
