suborder Theropoda

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌbˌɔrdɚ ˌθɛˈrɑpədə / (AmE), / ˈsʌbˌɔːdə ˌθɪˈrɒpədə / (BrE)
a group of carnivorous saurischian dinosaurs characterized by short forelimbs, mainly from the Jurassic and Cretaceous periods
group of carnivorous dinosaurs

Examples

Fossil evidence shows that suborder Theropoda included species like Allosaurus and Tyrannosaurus rex.
Many paleontologists specialize in the study of suborder Theropoda fossils.
The short forelimbs and sharp teeth were distinctive features of dinosaurs in suborder Theropoda.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Saurischia - the order of lizard-hipped dinosaurs including Theropoda
  • Synonym: Tyrannosaurus - a large theropod dinosaur

Etymology

From Greek 'ther' (beast) + 'pous, podos' (foot); scientific naming convention.
