fine-tooth comb

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪn tuθ koʊm / (AmE), / ˈfaɪn tuːθ kəʊm / (BrE)
A method or tool used to examine something very closely and thoroughly, often to find small details or errors.
method of close, detailed examination

Examples

The report was checked with a fine-tooth comb to avoid any mistakes before submission.
She went through his emails with a fine-tooth comb, searching for inconsistencies.
Auditors examined the company's financial records with a fine-tooth comb to ensure everything was accurate.

Synset Examples

he went over the contract with a fine-tooth comb looking for loopholes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scrutiny - close and careful examination
  • Synonym: scan - to look at all parts of (something) carefully

Etymology

From the literal tool 'fine-tooth comb', used for careful grooming, extended figuratively to mean thorough examination.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪn tuθ koʊm / (AmE), / ˈfaɪn tuːθ kəʊm / (BrE)
a comb with teeth that are set very close together, used for precise grooming or removal of lice and other debris from hair
comb with closely spaced teeth

Examples

You should use a fine-tooth comb to check your child’s hair for lice.
The police went over the crime scene with a fine-tooth comb to find every clue.
She examined the document with a fine-tooth comb for any errors or inconsistencies.
