concrete representation

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑnˌkrit ˌrɛprɪzɛnˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈkɒŋkriːt ˌrɛprɪzɛnˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
a clear or tangible example that expresses an abstract idea in specific, understandable terms
tangible example of an abstract idea

Examples

A diagram can provide a concrete representation of a difficult mathematical concept.
Artists use images as a concrete representation of their ideas and emotions.
The teacher showed a model as a concrete representation to help students understand the topic.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: abstraction - a general idea or quality rather than a specific object or instance

Etymology

From Latin 'concretus' (grown together, solidified) and Latin 'repraesentatio' (representation).
