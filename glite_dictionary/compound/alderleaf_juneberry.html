alderleaf Juneberry

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔldərˌlif ˈdʒunˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈɔːldəˌliːf ˈdʒuːnˌbɛri / (BrE)
A shrub or small tree native to northwestern North America, noted for its fragrant creamy white flowers and small waxy purple-red fruits.
shrub with white flowers and purple-red fruits

Examples

The alderleaf Juneberry blooms in early spring, filling the garden with a sweet fragrance.
We collected the ripe fruits from the alderleaf Juneberry tree for making jam.
Birds are often attracted to the alderleaf Juneberry because of its colorful and nutritious berries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: serviceberry - another name for trees or shrubs of the genus Amelanchier
