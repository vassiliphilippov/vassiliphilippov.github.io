baker's half dozen

1. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪkərz hæf ˈdʌzən / (AmE), / ˈbeɪkəz hɑːf ˈdʌzn / (BrE)
A group of seven items, especially baked goods.
group of seven items, esp. baked goods

Examples

He asked for a baker's half dozen bagels from the local bakery.
The recipe made exactly a baker's half dozen chocolate chip cookies.
For the school bake sale, she brought in a baker's half dozen cupcakes.

Synset Examples

He bought a baker's half dozen of croissants.
I only need a baker's half dozen of rolls.
She brought a baker's half dozen of muffins.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: baker's dozen - a group of thirteen items, especially baked goods

Etymology

Formed by analogy with 'baker's dozen' (13); 'half dozen' meaning six, with baker's extra added.
