sweet buckeye

1. NOUN

/ swit ˈbʌkˌaɪ / (AmE), / swiːt ˈbʌk.aɪ / (BrE)
a tall tree of the central United States, often cultivated, with smooth bark and yellow flowers, also known as yellow buckeye
a species of buckeye tree in central US

Examples

The sweet buckeye is a striking addition to parks because of its impressive height and yellow flowers.
Farmers often plant sweet buckeye trees for shade and decoration along property lines.
The leaves of the sweet buckeye turn golden in autumn, attracting many photographers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yellow buckeye - another common name for the same species of tree
