Siouan-speaking

1. ADJ

/ ˈsuː.ən ˈspiː.kɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsuː.ən ˈspiː.kɪŋ / (BrE)
able to communicate in a Siouan language or languages; relating to someone who speaks a Siouan language
able to speak a Siouan language

Examples

The tribe is predominantly Siouan-speaking, preserving much of its linguistic heritage.
Several Siouan-speaking communities still exist in the Midwest and Plains regions.
Researchers interviewed Siouan-speaking elders to document traditional stories.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Siouan - a family of North American indigenous languages

Etymology

From 'Siouan', referring to a family of North American indigenous languages, plus 'speaking'.
