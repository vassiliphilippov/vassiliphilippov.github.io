Ancient Greek

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪnʃənt ˈɡrik / (AmE), / ˈeɪnʃənt ˈɡriːk / (BrE)
the form of the Greek language used before and during classical antiquity, prior to the Roman Empire
Greek language of ancient times

Examples

He specialized in translating Ancient Greek texts into modern English.
Many philosophical works were originally written in Ancient Greek.
She learned Ancient Greek in order to read Homer in the original.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Modern Greek - the contemporary form of the Greek language

Etymology

From Latin 'Graecus' (Greek) and English 'ancient'.
