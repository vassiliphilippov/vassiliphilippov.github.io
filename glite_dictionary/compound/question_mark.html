question mark

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɛs.tʃən ˌmɑrk / (AmE), / ˈkwɛs.tʃən ˌmɑːk / (BrE)
the punctuation mark (?) used at the end of a sentence to indicate a direct question
punctuation mark indicating a question

Examples

Remember to put a question mark at the end of your sentence if you are asking something.
He drew a large question mark on the board to get our attention.
The document was full of errors, including missing question marks after questions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interrogation point - another term for question mark, chiefly historical or technical
  • Antonym: exclamation mark - punctuation mark (!) used for exclamations
  • Antonym: period - punctuation mark (.) used to end statements
