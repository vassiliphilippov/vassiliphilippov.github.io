curve ball

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɝːv bɔːl / (AmE), / ˈkɜːv bɔːl / (BrE)
A pitch in baseball thrown with a spin that causes it to curve as it approaches the batter.
baseball pitch with a curving path

Examples

The pitcher threw a wicked curve ball that struck the batter out.
He didn't expect the curve ball, and missed completely.
Her curve ball is the best in the league.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: slider - a baseball pitch that slides laterally with less pronounced curve
  • Antonym: fastball - a straight, high-speed pitch in baseball
  • Synonym: breaking ball - any baseball pitch that breaks, including a curve ball
