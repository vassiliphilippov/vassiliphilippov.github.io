antidiarrheal drug

1. NOUN

/ ˌæntiˌdaɪəˈriəl drʌɡ / (AmE), / ˌæntiˌdaɪəˈriːəl drʌɡ / (BrE)
a drug used to control or stop diarrhea
drug that treats diarrhea
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed an antidiarrheal drug to help manage the patient's symptoms.
You should not use an antidiarrheal drug for more than two days without medical advice.
Some people keep an antidiarrheal drug in their travel kit for emergencies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antidiarrheal - a substance or agent that relieves diarrhea

Etymology

From 'anti-' (against) + 'diarrheal' (relating to diarrhea) + 'drug'.
