knight of the green baize

1. NOUN

/ naɪt əv ðə ɡrin beɪz / (AmE), / naɪt əv ðə ɡriːn beɪz / (BrE)
a highly skilled and respected player of billiards or snooker, often admired for their expertise and style
expert billiards or snooker player

Examples

After years of practice, he finally earned the title of knight of the green baize among his peers.
The championship match attracted several renowned knights of the green baize from across the country.
Legendary players are often referred to as knights of the green baize in snooker literature.

Synset Examples

In the local pub, he's known as the knight of the green baize.
He played for hours as a knight of the green baize.
The tournament featured several knights of the green baize.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cueist - a person who plays billiards or snooker
  • Antonym: amateur - a person who is not skilled or professional in billiards or snooker

Etymology

Refers metaphorically to 'knight' as a chivalric figure and 'green baize,' the cloth covering billiards and snooker tables.
