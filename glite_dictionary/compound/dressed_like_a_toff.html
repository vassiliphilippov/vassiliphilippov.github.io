dressed like a toff

1. ADJ

/ drɛst laɪk ə tɑf / (AmE), / drɛst laɪk ə tɒf / (BrE)
Wearing clothing that is elegant and typical of the upper class; smartly or ostentatiously dressed, usually to imitate the style of the wealthy.
elegantly or upper-class dressed
british

Examples

She arrived at the party dressed like a toff, turning heads with her stylish outfit.
Even though he grew up modestly, he enjoys going out dressed like a toff from time to time.
During the festival, many tourists are seen dressed like a toff to blend in with the high society crowd.

Synset Examples

He always dressed like a toff at the gala.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: toff - an upper-class or wealthy person

Etymology

From British slang 'toff' (an upper-class person), of uncertain origin, possibly from 'tuft'.
