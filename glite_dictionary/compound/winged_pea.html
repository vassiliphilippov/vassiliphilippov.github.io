winged pea

1. NOUN

/ wɪŋd pi / (AmE), / wɪŋd piː / (BrE)
a European annual plant with sprawling habit, producing edible pods that have four distinctive wings
European plant with 4-winged edible pods

Examples

The winged pea thrives in well-drained soil throughout much of Europe.
Gardeners appreciate the unusual appearance of the winged pea's four-edged pods.
She added fresh winged peas to the salad for a unique crunch.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɪŋd pi / (AmE), / ˈwɪŋd piː / (BrE)
a tropical climbing plant (Psophocarpus tetragonolobus) with tuberous roots, clusters of purplish flowers, and edible pods edged with four jagged wings; native to the Old World tropics
tropical vine with winged pods

Examples

The winged pea is valued for its edible pods, seeds, and tubers.
Farmers in Southeast Asia often grow winged pea as a source of protein and nutrition.
You can recognize the winged pea by the four jagged wings along its pods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: winged bean - a tropical plant with edible pods edged with wings; another name for winged pea
