magnetic flux density

1. NOUN

/ mægˈnɛtɪk flʌks dənˈsɪti / (AmE), / mæɡˈnɛtɪk flʌks dɛnˈsɪti / (BrE)
The amount of magnetic flux passing through a unit area perpendicular to the direction of the magnetic field, usually measured in teslas (T).
measure of magnetic flux per unit area
medical

Examples

The magnetic flux density at the center of the coil was measured using a tesla meter.
Changes in magnetic flux density can affect the performance of sensitive electronic devices.
A strong magnet can produce a higher magnetic flux density than a weak magnet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: magnetic field - region around a magnetic material or a moving electric charge within which the force of magnetism acts
  • Degree Higher: magnetic flux - the total magnetic field passing through a given area

Etymology

Composed of 'magnetic' (from Greek magnetis, referring to magnet) and 'flux density' (from Latin fluxus, meaning flow, and density).
