single tax

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪŋɡəl tæks / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋɡl̩ tæks / (BrE)
a system of taxation in which only one tax is levied, typically on land or land value
tax system based on a single levy, usually on land

Examples

Henry George was a strong advocate of the single tax as a solution to poverty.
Some economists argue that a single tax on land would promote fairness and efficiency.
Debates about implementing a single tax have appeared throughout modern economic history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: land value tax - a tax on the value of land
