tiger salamander

1. NOUN

/ ˈtaɪɡɚ ˈsæləˌmændɚ / (AmE), / ˈtaɪɡə ˈsæləˌmændə / (BrE)
a large North American salamander, typically brown or black with vertical yellowish blotches, found in a variety of habitats
a North American salamander with yellow blotches

Examples

The tiger salamander is known for its distinctive yellow blotches on a dark background.
We saw a tiger salamander while hiking near the pond last weekend.
Tiger salamanders are often found in underground burrows during dry weather.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: salamander - an amphibian with a lizard-like appearance

Etymology

Named for its striped or blotched pattern reminiscent of a tiger; 'salamander' from Greek 'salamandra'.
