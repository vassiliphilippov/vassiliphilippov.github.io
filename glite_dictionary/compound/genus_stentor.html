genus Stentor

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˈstɛntɔr / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈstɛntɔː / (BrE)
A genus of trumpet-shaped, ciliated protozoa characterized by a spiral feeding funnel, commonly found in freshwater environments.
genus of trumpet-shaped protozoa

Examples

The genus Stentor includes several species of single-celled organisms notable for their size and shape.
Researchers observed the feeding process of the genus Stentor under the microscope.
Members of the genus Stentor use their cilia to create currents that draw food particles into their funnels.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Stentor - a large, trumpet-shaped single-celled organism

Etymology

From Latin 'stentor', from Greek 'Stentōr', a mythical Greek herald known for his loud voice, referencing the protozoan's trumpet shape.
