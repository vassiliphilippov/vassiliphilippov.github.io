von Recklinghausen's disease

1. NOUN

/ vɑn ˈrɛklɪnˌhaʊzənz dɪˈziz / (AmE), / vɒn ˈrɛklɪŋˌhaʊzənz dɪˈziːz / (BrE)
an inherited disorder characterized by numerous neurofibromas, skin spots, and often developmental abnormalities; also known as neurofibromatosis type 1
hereditary disorder with skin spots and neurofibromas
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with von Recklinghausen's disease after multiple neurofibromas appeared on her skin.
He inherited von Recklinghausen's disease from his mother, who also had café-au-lait spots.
Genetic testing confirmed the presence of von Recklinghausen's disease in the family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neurofibromatosis - a genetic disorder causing tumors on nerve tissue

Etymology

Named after German pathologist Friedrich Daniel von Recklinghausen (1833–1910), who first described the disease.
