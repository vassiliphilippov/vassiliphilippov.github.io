tree sloth

1. NOUN

/ tri sloʊθ / (AmE), / triː sləʊθ / (BrE)
a slow-moving mammal native to South and Central America, known for living in trees, hanging upside down, and feeding on leaves and fruits
slow-moving arboreal mammal

Examples

We saw a tree sloth hanging lazily from a branch in the rainforest.
The tree sloth moves so slowly that algae can grow on its fur.
Many tourists are eager to spot a tree sloth during their trip to Costa Rica.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sloth - arboreal mammal known for its slow movement
