para-aminobenzoic acid

1. NOUN

/ ˌpærə əˌminoʊbɛnˈzoʊɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / ˌpærə əˌmiːnəʊbɛnˈzəʊɪk ˈasid / (BrE)
a metabolic compound found in yeast and liver cells, used in the manufacture of dyes, drugs, and sunscreen products
chemical used in dyes, drugs, and sunscreens
medical

Examples

Many sunscreens contain para-aminobenzoic acid to protect the skin from ultraviolet rays.
Researchers measured the level of para-aminobenzoic acid in yeast samples.
Allergic reactions to para-aminobenzoic acid are relatively rare but have been reported.

Etymology

From 'para-' (chemical prefix indicating position on a benzene ring), 'amino' (from ammonia), and 'benzoic acid' (a simple aromatic carboxylic acid).
