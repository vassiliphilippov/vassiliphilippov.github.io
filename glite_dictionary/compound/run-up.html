run-up

1. NOUN

/ ˈrʌnˌʌp / (AmE), / ˈrʌn.ʌp / (BrE)
an approach or running start during which an athlete gathers speed before an action, such as taking a jump or delivering a throw
approach run to gain speed (sports)

Examples

The long run-up helped the high jumper clear the bar with ease.
He stumbled during his run-up and missed the shot put mark.
In cricket, the bowler's run-up is crucial for generating speed and accuracy.

2. NOUN

/ ˈrʌn ʌp / (AmE), / ˈrʌn ʌp / (BrE)
A rapid or substantial increase in amount, price, or level over a short period, especially in financial or economic contexts.
sudden increase (esp. in finance)

Examples

Analysts attributed the run-up in oil prices to geopolitical tensions.
There was a dramatic run-up in housing values during the early 2000s.
After a significant run-up, technology stocks began to decline in value.

Synset Examples

a runup in interest rates
market runups are followed by corrections

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rise - an upward movement or increase
  • Antonym: fall - a decrease or drop in amount or level

Etymology

From the verb 'run up', meaning to increase or accumulate quickly; first attested in this sense in the 20th century.
