ghost bullet

1. NOUN

/ ɡoʊst ˈbʊlɪt / (AmE), / ɡəʊst ˈbʊlɪt / (BrE)
a bullet that is difficult to detect, identify, or trace, often used to describe ammunition that leaves little to no ballistic evidence
bullet difficult to detect or trace

Examples

The forensics team struggled to recover any evidence of the ghost bullet used in the crime.
Rumors spread about a ghost bullet capable of causing deadly wounds without leaving a trace.
He claimed he had invented a ghost bullet that no one could ever track back to him.

Synset Examples

They were shocked by how the ghost bullet struck without warning.
The sniper's shot was a ghost bullet, hitting its mark unseen.
He joked about his aim being a ghost bullet, always missing.

Etymology

Formed from 'ghost' (suggesting invisibility or elusiveness) and 'bullet'.
