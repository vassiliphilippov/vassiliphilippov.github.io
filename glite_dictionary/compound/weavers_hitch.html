weaver's hitch

1. NOUN

/ ˈwiːvɚz hɪtʃ / (AmE), / ˈwiːvəz hɪtʃ / (BrE)
a knot used to temporarily tie a rope to the middle of another rope or to an eye
knot for temporarily tying ropes

Examples

The weaver's hitch is commonly used in net making and other crafts.
He secured the tarp to the rope using a weaver's hitch for easy removal.
After practicing several times, she could tie a weaver's hitch without looking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sheet bend - a knot for joining two ropes
