anaconda mortgage

1. NOUN

/ ˌæn.əˈkɑn.də ˈmɔr.ɡɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌæn.əˈkɒn.də ˈmɔː.ɡɪdʒ / (BrE)
A very large and burdensome mortgage that severely restricts the borrower's financial flexibility, often likened to being tightly constricted.
very large, burdensome mortgage

Examples

Many homeowners struggle under an anaconda mortgage that leaves little room for other expenses.
After refinancing, they hoped to escape their anaconda mortgage and regain financial freedom.
Taking out an anaconda mortgage can be risky if your income is unstable.

Synset Examples

He felt trapped by his anaconda mortgage after buying the house.
She regretted taking on the anaconda mortgage; it suffocated her finances.
The bank offered him an anaconda mortgage with a hefty interest rate.

Etymology

From the name of the anaconda snake, metaphorically suggesting constriction by debt.
