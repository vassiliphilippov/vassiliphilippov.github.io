psychoactive substance

1. NOUN

/ ˌsaɪkoʊˈæktɪv ˈsʌbstəns / (AmE), / ˌsaɪkəʊˈæktɪv ˈsʌbstəns / (BrE)
a drug or chemical that affects the mind, producing changes in mood, perception, or behavior
a drug altering mood or perception
medical

Examples

The government strictly regulates the sale of psychoactive substances to protect public health.
Some plants produce psychoactive substances that have been used for centuries in traditional ceremonies.
She was studying the effects of a new psychoactive substance on memory and mood in clinical trials.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hallucinogen - a drug causing hallucinations or perceptual distortions
  • Degree Lower: stimulant - a substance that increases physiological or nervous activity
  • Degree Lower: depressant - a substance that reduces nervous or physiological activity

Etymology

From 'psycho-' (mind) and 'active', referring to substances that act on mental processes.
