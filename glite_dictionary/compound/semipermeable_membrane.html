semipermeable membrane

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛmiˌpɜːrmiˈeɪbəl ˈmɛmˌbreɪn / (AmE), / ˌsɛmiˌpɜːmɪˈeɪbəl ˈmɛmˌbreɪn / (BrE)
a membrane that allows certain molecules or ions to pass through it by diffusion but not others
membrane permitting selective passage of molecules

Examples

The cell uses a semipermeable membrane to control which substances can enter or leave.
Osmosis only occurs properly when a semipermeable membrane is present.
Scientists designed an artificial semipermeable membrane for water purification.

Etymology

From Latin semi- (half, partial) + permeable (from Latin permeabilis, able to be passed through) + membrane (from Latin membrana, skin).
