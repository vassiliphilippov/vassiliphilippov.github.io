genus Cydonia

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs saɪˈdoʊ.ni.ə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs sɪˈdəʊ.nɪ.ə / (BrE)
a genus of small deciduous trees in the rose family, best known for producing the quince fruit (Cydonia oblonga)
the botanical genus of the quince

Examples

The genus Cydonia contains only one recognized species, the quince.
Botanists classify the quince tree under the genus Cydonia in the family Rosaceae.
Fruit from the genus Cydonia has been cultivated for centuries in the Mediterranean region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quince - the fruit produced by trees of the genus Cydonia

Etymology

From Latin Cydonia, referring to Cydonia in Crete, where the quince was cultivated.
