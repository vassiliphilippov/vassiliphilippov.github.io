family Muscicapidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ˌmʌsɪkəˈpeɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli ˌmʌsɪkəˈpeɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of passerine birds known as the Old World or true flycatchers, found mainly in Europe, Asia, and Africa.
family of Old World flycatchers (birds)

Examples

Several species in the family Muscicapidae are renowned for their aerial hunting skills.
The garden is visited by migratory birds from the family Muscicapidae every spring.
Recent taxonomic studies have clarified which birds rightly belong to the family Muscicapidae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flycatcher - a bird that catches flying insects, especially of the family Muscicapidae

Etymology

From New Latin *Muscicapidae*, based on Latin *muscicapa* ('flycatcher'): *musca* ('fly') + *capere* ('to catch').
