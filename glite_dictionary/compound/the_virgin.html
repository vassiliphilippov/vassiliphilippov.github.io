the Virgin

1. PROPN

/ ðə ˈvɝːdʒɪn / (AmE), / ði ˈvɜːdʒɪn / (BrE)
(in Christianity) the mother of Jesus Christ, especially venerated by Roman Catholics as the Virgin Mary.
the Virgin Mary, mother of Jesus

Examples

Pilgrims traveled great distances to visit the shrine dedicated to the Virgin.
Many classical paintings depict the Virgin holding the infant Jesus.
Devotion to the Virgin remains an important part of Catholic worship in many countries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Virgin Mary - the mother of Jesus in Christian theology

Etymology

From Latin 'virgo' (maiden, virgin), referring to Mary, the mother of Jesus, in Christian tradition.
