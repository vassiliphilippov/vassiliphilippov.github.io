vernix caseosa

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɜrnɪks kəˈsiːoʊsə / (AmE), / ˈvɜːnɪks kaˈsiːəʊsə / (BrE)
a white, cheesy protective substance that coats and protects the skin of a fetus in the womb
white protective substance on a fetus's skin
medical

Examples

The newborn was still covered in vernix caseosa immediately after delivery.
During the final weeks of pregnancy, the amount of vernix caseosa on the baby's skin increases.
The doctor explained that vernix caseosa helps protect the baby's skin from amniotic fluid.

Etymology

From Latin 'vernix' (varnish) + 'caseosa' (cheesy, from 'caseus' meaning cheese).
