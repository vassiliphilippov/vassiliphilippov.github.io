Jewish religion

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒu ɪʃ rɪˈlɪdʒən / (AmE), / ˈdʒuːɪʃ rɪˈlɪdʒən / (BrE)
The religion practiced by Jews, based on the teachings of the Torah and the Talmud.
religion of Jews; Judaism

Examples

Many aspects of the Jewish religion revolve around family and community traditions.
She began studying the Jewish religion after discovering her family's heritage.
The laws of the Jewish religion are rooted in ancient texts like the Torah.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Judaism - the monotheistic religion of Jews, based on the Torah, Talmud, and related texts
