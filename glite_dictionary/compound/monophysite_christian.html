Monophysite Christian

1. NOUN

/ məˈnɑːfɪˌsaɪt ˈkrɪstʃən / (AmE), / məˈnɒfɪˌsaɪt ˈkrɪstʃən / (BrE)
a member of a Christian sect that believes in the single, divine nature of Christ rather than a dual nature, human and divine
member of Christian sect believing in single nature of Christ

Examples

During the early centuries, Monophysite Christian beliefs provoked heated theological debates within the Church.
The emperor's edict led to the exile of many Monophysite Christians.
Scholars still study the impact of the Monophysite Christian tradition on Eastern churches.

Synset Examples

Monophysite Christians faced persecution in various regions.
The Monophysite doctrine was controversial in early Christianity.
He studied the beliefs of the Monophysite sect extensively.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Nestorian Christian - a member of a Christian sect believing in the dual nature of Christ

Etymology

From Greek monos (single) + physis (nature); 'Christian' from Greek christianos, follower of Christ.
