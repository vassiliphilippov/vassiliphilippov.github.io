line double

1. NOUN

/ laɪn ˈdʌbl̩ / (AmE), / laɪn ˈdʌbl / (BrE)
(Baseball) A double resulting from a line drive; a hit in which the batter safely reaches second base after striking the ball hard and low in a straight trajectory.
baseball double from a line drive

Examples

He hit a line double down the right field line in the seventh inning.
The crowd cheered as the batter's line double brought in two runs.
Her line double was one of the hardest-hit balls of the game.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: double - a two-base hit in baseball
