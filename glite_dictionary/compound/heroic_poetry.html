heroic poetry

1. NOUN

/ hɪˈroʊɪk ˈpoʊətri / (AmE), / hɪˈrəʊɪk ˈpəʊɪtri / (BrE)
Poetry that recounts and celebrates the deeds and adventures of a hero, often from ancient legends or national epics.
poetry about heroes' deeds

Examples

Homer’s Iliad and Odyssey are classic examples of heroic poetry.
The tradition of heroic poetry preserves the myths of ancient warriors and their epic battles.
Students analyzed the structure and themes of heroic poetry in their literature class.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: epic poetry - a long narrative poem about heroic deeds
