pet name

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛt ˌneɪm / (AmE), / ˈpɛt ˌneɪm / (BrE)
a name used as a term of endearment or a familiar form of someone's proper name, especially one with a diminutive ending
affectionate or diminutive name

Examples

His grandmother always called him by his pet name, even as an adult.
Among friends, she was known by the pet name 'Maggie'.
He whispered sweet pet names to his partner during their conversation.

Synset Examples

‘Billy’ is a hypocorism for ‘William’

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hypocorism - a pet name or term of endearment, often a diminutive form of a name

Etymology

Formed from 'pet' (term of affection) and 'name'.
