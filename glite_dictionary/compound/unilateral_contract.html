unilateral contract

1. NOUN

/ ˌjuː.nəˈlæt.ər.əl ˈkɑn.trækt / (AmE), / ˌjuː.nɪˈlæt.ər.əl ˈkɒn.trækt / (BrE)
A contract in which only one party makes a promise, and the other party can accept only by performing a specified act.
a contract where only one party promises
legal

Examples

In a unilateral contract, only one party is legally obligated once the other party performs.
The insurance policy was an example of a unilateral contract offered to the customer.
A reward offer is typically a unilateral contract because it is accepted by performing the requested act.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: bilateral contract - a contract in which both parties make promises to each other

Etymology

From Latin 'unus' (one) + 'latus' (side) + 'contractus' (agreement).
