rural area

1. NOUN

/ ˈrʊrəl ˈɛriə / (AmE), / ˈrʊərəl ˈeəriə / (BrE)
an area of countryside that is outside cities and towns, typically characterized by low population density and open land
countryside outside towns or cities

Examples

Many families in the rural area rely on agriculture as their main source of income.
Access to public transportation is limited in most rural areas.
The school district serves both urban and rural area communities.

Synset Examples

his poetry celebrated the slower pace of life in the country

Related Terms

  • Antonym: urban area - an area within a city or town, characterized by higher population density and developed infrastructure

2. NOUN

/ ˈrʊrəl ˈɛriə / (AmE), / ˈrʊərəl ˈeəriə / (BrE)
An area of land that is located outside cities and towns, typically characterized by low population density and large open spaces used for agriculture or natural habitats.
region outside cities and towns

Examples

Many families in this country live in a rural area and make a living through farming.
Access to healthcare can be limited for people who reside in a rural area.
She moved from a busy city to a peaceful rural area to enjoy a slower pace of life.

Synset Examples

He grew up in a rural area, often called that term.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: urban area - a region within a city or town
