Jacquemier's sign

1. NOUN

/ ʒækˈmjeɪərz saɪn / (AmE), / ʒakˈmɪə(z) saɪn / (BrE)
a purplish discoloration of the vaginal mucous membrane, observed as an early sign of pregnancy
early pregnancy sign; vaginal discoloration
medical

Examples

The doctor noted the presence of Jacquemier's sign during the early examination of the patient.
Detection of Jacquemier's sign can help confirm a suspected pregnancy.
Medical students are taught to recognize Jacquemier's sign as a physiological change in early pregnancy.

Etymology

Named after Édouard Jacquemier, French obstetrician (1806–1875).
