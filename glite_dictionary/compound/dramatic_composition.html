dramatic composition

1. NOUN

/ drəˈmætɪk kəmˌpɑːzɪˈʃən / (AmE), / drəˈmatɪk kəmˌpɒzɪˈʃən / (BrE)
A literary work, such as a play, written to be performed by actors on stage, television, or in film.
a play or work meant for performance

Examples

The festival featured a new dramatic composition that explored themes of justice and forgiveness.
She won an award for her dramatic composition presented on national television.
Students were asked to analyze a dramatic composition by Shakespeare in their literature class.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: play - a literary work written for performance by actors
  • Synonym: script - the written text of a play or broadcast

Etymology

From 'dramatic', relating to drama (from Greek 'drama', action), and 'composition', meaning something composed or created.
