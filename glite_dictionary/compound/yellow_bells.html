yellow bells

1. NOUN

/ ˈjɛloʊ ˈbɛlz / (AmE), / ˈjɛləʊ ˈbɛlz / (BrE)
a viscid herb native to arid regions of the southwestern United States, known for its drooping yellow flowers
a desert plant with yellow flowers

Examples

The yellow bells in her desert garden bloom every spring.
Botanists admire the striking color of yellow bells against the barren landscape.
We stopped to photograph the yellow bells swaying gently in the wind.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Tecoma stans - the scientific name for yellow bells
