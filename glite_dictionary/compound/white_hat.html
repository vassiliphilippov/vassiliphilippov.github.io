white hat

1. NOUN

/ ˈwaɪt ˌhæt / (AmE), / ˈwaɪt ˌhæt / (BrE)
A person who represents honesty or good intentions, especially an ethical computer hacker who helps protect systems from malicious attacks.
ethical person or hacker; good character

Examples

In the realm of cybersecurity, a white hat helps organizations find vulnerabilities before criminals do.
He made a name for himself as a white hat by exposing security flaws ethically.
The old Westerns made it easy to tell who the white hat was.

Synset Examples

He always plays the role of a white hat in movies.
She is known as a white hat in the cybersecurity community.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: black hat - someone who acts with bad intentions, especially a malicious hacker

Etymology

Derived from the tradition in Western films where heroes wore white hats, contrasted with black hats worn by villains.
