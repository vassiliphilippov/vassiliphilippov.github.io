plantation owner

1. NOUN

/ plænˈteɪʃən ˈoʊnər / (AmE), / plænˈteɪʃən ˈəʊnə / (BrE)
a person who owns or manages a large estate (plantation), especially for agricultural production, such as crops like sugar, cotton, or tobacco
owner or manager of a plantation

Examples

The plantation owner oversaw hundreds of workers during the harvest season.
Many plantation owners became wealthy from crops like sugar and cotton.
The old mansion was once the home of a prominent plantation owner.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: planter - owner or manager of a plantation
