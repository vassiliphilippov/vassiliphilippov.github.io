pineapple guava

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪˌnæpəl ˈɡwɑvə / (AmE), / ˈpaɪˌnæpl ˈɡwɑːvə / (BrE)
A small, oval, dark-green tropical fruit with sweet, aromatic white flesh, chiefly used for jellies and preserves.
tropical fruit with sweet white flesh

Examples

She made delicious jam from the ripe pineapple guava she picked from her garden.
A basket of fresh pineapple guava was displayed at the farmer's market.
The flavor of a perfectly ripe pineapple guava is both sweet and slightly tangy.

Etymology

From 'pineapple' (reference to the fruit's flavor) + 'guava' (resemblance to common guava in form).
