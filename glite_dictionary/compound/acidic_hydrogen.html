acidic hydrogen

1. NOUN

/ əˈsɪdɪk ˈhaɪdrədʒən / (AmE), / əˈsɪdɪk ˈhʌɪdrɪdʒən / (BrE)
A hydrogen atom in an acid that can be released as a positive ion (proton) when the acid dissociates.
hydrogen atom that can dissociate from an acid
medical

Examples

When hydrochloric acid dissolves in water, its acidic hydrogen is released as H+ ions.
In organic compounds, the presence of an acidic hydrogen can facilitate certain chemical reactions.
Weak acids do not completely dissociate, so not every acidic hydrogen is released in solution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: proton - a subatomic particle with a positive charge, released from acids as hydrogen ions

Etymology

From 'acidic' (relating to acid) and 'hydrogen' (element name).
