Riggs' disease

1. NOUN

/ rɪɡz dɪˈziːz / (AmE), / rɪɡz dɪˈziːz / (BrE)
An old term for chronic periodontitis, a purulent inflammation of the sockets of the teeth leading to loosening and loss of teeth.
old term for chronic periodontitis
medical archaic

Examples

Dentists used to call chronic gum inflammation Riggs' disease in the nineteenth century.
Modern textbooks rarely refer to Riggs' disease, preferring the term periodontitis.
The patient was diagnosed with Riggs' disease after presenting with severe gum recession and loose teeth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: periodontitis - serious gum infection that damages gums and can destroy the jawbone

Etymology

Named after American dentist John M. Riggs (1811–1885), who described the disease.
