ash gray

1. NOUN

/ æʃ ˈɡreɪ / (AmE), / æʃ ˈɡreɪ / (BrE)
a pale, light shade of gray resembling the color of ashes
a light gray color

Examples

She painted the walls a soft ash gray to create a calming atmosphere.
The kitten’s fur was an unusual shade of ash gray, almost silvery in the sunlight.
He wore an ash gray suit to the wedding, looking both modern and stylish.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: gray - the color between black and white; neutral color
  • Degree Higher: charcoal - a dark gray color like that of burned wood
  • Synonym: silver - a metallic, light gray color

Etymology

Formed from 'ash' (the powdery residue after burning) and 'gray'.
