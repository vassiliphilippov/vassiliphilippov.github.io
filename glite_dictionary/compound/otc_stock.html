OTC stock

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊ ti ˈsi stɑk / (AmE), / ˌəʊ tiː ˈsiː stɒk / (BrE)
A stock that is traded over the counter (OTC), rather than on an organized exchange.
stock traded outside exchanges

Examples

Many technology startups begin as OTC stocks before qualifying for a major exchange.
Because OTC stock trading is less regulated, investors face higher risks.
She researched the financials carefully before buying shares in an OTC stock.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: listed stock - stock traded on an official exchange
  • Synonym: over-the-counter - not traded on an organized exchange

Etymology

OTC stands for 'over the counter.'
