Old World flycatcher

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊld ˈwɝːld ˈflaɪˌkætʃɚ / (AmE), / ˌəʊld ˈwɜːld ˈflaɪˌkætʃə / (BrE)
Any of a large family of small songbirds native to Europe, Asia, and Africa that catch insects in flight.
small insect-eating songbird of Europe, Asia, or Africa

Examples

The spotted Old World flycatcher perched quietly on a branch, scanning for insects to catch mid-air.
Many Old World flycatchers migrate long distances each year between Africa and Europe.
Unlike the New World species, the Old World flycatcher builds its nest in tree holes.
