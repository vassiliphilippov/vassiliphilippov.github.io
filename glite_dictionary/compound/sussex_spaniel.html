Sussex spaniel

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌsɪks ˈspæniəl / (AmE), / ˈsʌsɪks ˈspænɪəl / (BrE)
a breed of spaniel originating in England, having short legs and a golden liver-colored coat
English dog breed with golden liver coat

Examples

The Sussex spaniel is known for its gentle temperament and loyalty.
They adopted a Sussex spaniel from a rescue center last year.
With its distinctive golden liver coat, the Sussex spaniel stands out at dog shows.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spaniel - a type of dog originally bred as a gun dog, typically with long ears and a silky coat

Etymology

Named after Sussex, a county in southern England, where the breed was developed.
