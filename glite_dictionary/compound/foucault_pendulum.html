Foucault pendulum

1. NOUN

/ fuˈkoʊ ˈpɛn.dʒə.ləm / (AmE), / fuːˈkəʊ ˈpɛn.djʊ.ləm / (BrE)
A large pendulum suspended so it can swing freely in any direction; the changing orientation of its swing demonstrates the Earth's rotation.
pendulum demonstrating Earth's rotation

Examples

The Foucault pendulum in the science museum attracted a crowd of curious visitors.
With a Foucault pendulum, you can directly observe the rotation of the Earth.
Many universities display a Foucault pendulum as an educational exhibit in their physics departments.

Etymology

Named after French physicist Léon Foucault (1819–1868), who invented the pendulum in 1851.
