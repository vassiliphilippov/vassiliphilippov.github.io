crazy quilt

1. NOUN

/ ˈkreɪzi kwɪlt / (AmE), / ˈkreɪzi kwɪlt / (BrE)
a patchwork quilt made of irregular pieces of fabric sewn together without following a regular design or pattern
patchwork quilt with no set design

Examples

Her grandmother made a beautiful crazy quilt from scraps of old dresses.
The crazy quilt on the bed caught everyone's attention with its vibrant mix of fabrics.
Learning to sew a crazy quilt requires creativity rather than following patterns.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkreɪzi kwɪlt / (AmE), / ˈkreɪzi kwɪlt / (BrE)
A patchwork made up of irregularly shaped pieces of fabric in varied colors and patterns, often used metaphorically to describe something composed of diverse or mismatched elements.
patchwork of irregular, varied elements

Examples

The artist filled the canvas with a crazy quilt of vibrant colors and shapes.
Her memories of childhood formed a crazy quilt of images and emotions.
The city's architecture is a crazy quilt of historic and modern styles.

Synset Examples

Her thoughts were a crazy quilt of ideas.
The design looked like a crazy quilt of colors.
He wore a crazy quilt jacket to the festival.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: patchwork - needlework made by sewing together pieces of fabric in different designs

Etymology

From 'crazy' (irregular, unpredictable) + 'quilt' (bedcover made by sewing together layers of fabric), describing the erratic pattern of this type of quilt.
