elk nut

1. NOUN

/ ɛlk nʌt / (AmE), / ɛlk nʌt / (BrE)
the oily, drupaceous fruit of the rabbitwood shrub, sometimes eaten by wildlife such as elk
fruit of the rabbitwood shrub

Examples

While hiking, we found an elk nut on the ground beneath a rabbitwood bush.
Local wildlife, including elk, frequently feed on the elk nuts in late summer.
The elk nut is known for its oily texture and is an important food source in arid regions.
