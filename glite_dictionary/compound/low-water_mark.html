low-water mark

1. NOUN

/ ˌloʊ ˈwɔtər ˌmɑrk / (AmE), / ˌləʊ ˈwɔːtə ˌmɑːk / (BrE)
a line or mark that shows the lowest level reached by water or, figuratively, the lowest point or level attained by something
lowest level reached by water or something else

Examples

After the drought, the rocks showed a distinct low-water mark on their surface.
The scandal represented the low-water mark of his political career.
Each spring, the river rises well above its usual low-water mark.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: high-water mark - the highest point that water rises to

2. NOUN

/ ˈloʊ ˌwɔtər ˈmɑrk / (AmE), / ˈləʊ ˌwɔːtə ˈmɑːk / (BrE)
the lowest or worst point of a situation or condition
the lowest point or level

Examples

The company's sales hit a low-water mark during the recession.
His confidence reached its low-water mark after repeated failures.
That period was the low-water mark of her political career.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: high-water mark - the highest point or level achieved

Etymology

Originally refers to the lowest level reached by a body of water; used figuratively since the 18th century.
