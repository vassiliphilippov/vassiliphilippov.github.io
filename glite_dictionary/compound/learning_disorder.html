learning disorder

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɝː.nɪŋ dɪsˌɔɹ.dɚ / (AmE), / ˈlɜː.nɪŋ dɪsˌɔː.də / (BrE)
a condition affecting children of normal intelligence who have difficulty learning specific academic skills, such as reading, writing, or mathematics
difficulty learning specific skills
medical

Examples

Early intervention can help children with a learning disorder reach their full academic potential.
The school provides resources for students diagnosed with a learning disorder.
Dyslexia is a common type of learning disorder that affects reading skills.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: learning disability - difficulty in learning due to cognitive or neurological reasons
  • Synonym: learning difficulty - a problem that affects the ability to learn at a typical rate
