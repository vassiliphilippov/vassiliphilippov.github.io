Attacapa language

1. NOUN

/ əˈtækəpə ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / əˈtækəpə ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
an extinct language spoken by the Atakapa people of the Gulf Coast region in Louisiana and Texas
extinct language of the Atakapa people

Examples

Linguists have studied the Attacapa language to learn more about indigenous cultures of the Gulf Coast.
Few written records survive of the Attacapa language, making its reconstruction difficult.
The Attacapa language has no known close relatives among other Native American languages.

Etymology

Named after the Atakapa people; the ethnonym is thought to derive from Choctaw.
