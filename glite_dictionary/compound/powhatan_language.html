Powhatan language

1. NOUN

/ ˌpaʊ.həˈtæn ˈlæŋ.ɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌpaʊ.həˈtæn ˈlæŋ.ɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
an extinct Algonquian language once spoken by the Powhatan people of eastern Virginia
extinct Algonquian language of the Powhatan people

Examples

Historical documents contain only a few recorded words from the Powhatan language.
Linguists study the Powhatan language to learn about early Indigenous peoples in Virginia.
The Powhatan language became extinct in the late 18th century, but efforts to revive it are ongoing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Algonquian - a family of Native American languages to which Powhatan belongs

Etymology

Named after the Powhatan people, from Virginia Algonquian (Algonquian language family).
