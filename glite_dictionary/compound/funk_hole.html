funk hole

1. NOUN

/ ˈfʌŋk hoʊl / (AmE), / ˈfʌŋk həʊl / (BrE)
A place, especially a dugout or shelter, used as a safe retreat during times of danger or anxiety.
a safe retreat or shelter
british

Examples

After the first air raid, the soldiers scrambled into a funk hole for cover.
During the bombardment, he stayed hidden in his funk hole for hours.
The men constructed a makeshift funk hole beneath the trenches as a precaution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dugout - a shelter dug into the ground, especially for protection in warfare
  • Synonym: shelter - a place giving protection from danger

Etymology

funk (state of fear) + hole, originally British slang from military contexts.
