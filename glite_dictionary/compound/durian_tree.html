durian tree

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʊriən triː / (AmE), / ˈdjʊəriən triː / (BrE)
a tree native to Southeast Asia, known for producing large, spiny, edible fruits with a distinctive smell
Southeast Asian tree with spiny, edible fruit

Examples

The durian tree thrives in the tropical climates of Malaysia and Indonesia.
Many farmers in Southeast Asia cultivate the durian tree for its prized fruit.
A single durian tree can produce dozens of the notorious, strong-smelling fruits each year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: durian - the large, spiny fruit of the durian tree

Etymology

From Malay 'duri' meaning 'thorn', referring to the fruit's spiny covering.
