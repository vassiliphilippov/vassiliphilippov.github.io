pound sterling

1. NOUN

/ paʊnd ˈstɝːlɪŋ / (AmE), / paʊnd ˈstɜːlɪŋ / (BrE)
the official basic unit of currency in the United Kingdom, equal to 100 pence
currency unit of the United Kingdom

Examples

He converted his dollars to pound sterling before traveling to London.
The contract price is quoted in pounds sterling, not euros or dollars.
Due to the exchange rate, one pound sterling is worth more than one U.S. dollar.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pound - basic unit of currency in several countries, including the UK
  • Degree Lower: penny - a unit of currency, one hundredth of a pound sterling

Etymology

From the use of the word 'sterling' to denote English silver coinage, possibly from Old French 'esterlin' or Middle English 'sterlinge'.
