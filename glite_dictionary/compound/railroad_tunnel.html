railroad tunnel

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪlˌrɑd ˈtʌnəl / (AmE), / ˈreɪlˌrɒd ˈtʌnəl / (BrE)
a tunnel constructed for trains to travel through, typically passing through hills, mountains, or under cities
tunnel for trains to pass through

Examples

The train disappeared into the railroad tunnel, emerging on the other side of the mountain minutes later.
Workers spent months reinforcing the old railroad tunnel to meet new safety standards.
We could hear the echo of the whistle as the locomotive entered the railroad tunnel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: railway tunnel - a tunnel for railway tracks (mainly British term)
