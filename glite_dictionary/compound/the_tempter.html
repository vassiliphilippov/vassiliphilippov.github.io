the Tempter

1. NOUN

/ ðə ˈtɛmptər / (AmE), / ðə ˈtɛmptə / (BrE)
A name for Satan or the Devil, regarded as the chief spirit of evil and adversary of God, especially as one who tempts or leads humans into sin.
Satan; one who tempts to evil

Examples

In the story, the Tempter tried to persuade the hero to abandon his quest.
Many sermons warn against listening to the Tempter and turning away from righteousness.
Milton depicted the Tempter as a cunning and persuasive figure in his epic poem.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Satan - The chief evil spirit and adversary in Christian, Islamic, and Jewish belief
  • Synonym: the Devil - A name for the supreme spirit of evil in Christian and Jewish belief
  • Antonym: God - The supreme being and creator in theistic religions; the adversary of the Tempter

Etymology

From Middle English tempter, from Latin temptator (one who tempts); used as a title for Satan in Christian theology.
