Tibetan mastiff

1. NOUN

/ təˈbɛt̬.ən ˈmæs.tɪf / (AmE), / tɪˈbɛt.ən ˈmæs.tɪf / (BrE)
a very large, powerful dog breed with a thick rough coat, originally from Tibet and central Asia, traditionally used as a guard dog
large, powerful dog breed from Tibet

Examples

The Tibetan mastiff is known for its loyalty and protective instincts.
Because of their size, Tibetan mastiffs need plenty of exercise and space.
Her family has raised Tibetan mastiffs for generations in the mountains.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mastiff - a large and powerful dog breed

Etymology

From Tibetan (region of origin) and mastiff (Middle English 'mastif', from Old French).
