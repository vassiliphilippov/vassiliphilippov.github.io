quite some difference

1. NOUN

/ kwaɪt sʌm ˈdɪf.ɚ.əns / (AmE), / kwaɪt sʌm ˈdɪf.ər.əns / (BrE)
A significant or notable difference; a difference large enough to be clearly recognized or important.
significant or notable difference

Examples

There's quite some difference between your plan and mine.
After the repairs, there was quite some difference in how the car handled.
You'll notice quite some difference when you use the new software version.

Synset Examples

There’s quite some difference between the two proposals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: big difference - a large or important difference
  • Antonym: little difference - a small or minor difference
