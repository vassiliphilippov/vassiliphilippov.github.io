Abnaki language

1. NOUN

/ æbˈnɑːki ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / æbˈnɑːki ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Algonquian language traditionally spoken by the Abnaki and Penobscot peoples of northeastern North America.
Algonquian language of the Abnaki and Penobscot

Examples

The Abnaki language is considered critically endangered, with only a few fluent speakers remaining.
He studied the phonetic structure of the Abnaki language for his anthropology thesis.
Cultural preservation efforts include teaching children the Abnaki language in school programs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Algonquian - a family of North American Indian languages

Etymology

Abnaki from Eastern Abenaki 'Wabanaki', meaning 'people of the dawn land'.
