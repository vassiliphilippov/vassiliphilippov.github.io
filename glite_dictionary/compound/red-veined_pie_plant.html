red-veined pie plant

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɛd ˈveɪnd paɪ plænt / (AmE), / ˌrɛd ˈveɪnd paɪ plɑːnt / (BrE)
An Asian herb native to the Himalayas, known for its reddish-veined leaves; commonly used as a pie ingredient.
Asian herb with red-veined leaves

Examples

The gardener pointed out the red-veined pie plant growing along the edge of the path.
Many traditional Himalayan recipes feature the tart stems of the red-veined pie plant.
She used freshly picked red-veined pie plant in her homemade dessert.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rhubarb - an edible plant with thick red stalks, often used in pies
