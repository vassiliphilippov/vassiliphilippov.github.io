mail fraud

1. NOUN

/ meɪl frɔd / (AmE), / meɪl frɔːd / (BrE)
the crime of intentionally deceiving someone out of money or property through the use of postal mail
fraud committed through postal mail
legal

Examples

He was convicted of mail fraud for sending out fake invoices to hundreds of people.
The company owner is facing charges of mail fraud after a lengthy investigation by federal authorities.
Anyone caught committing mail fraud could receive a lengthy prison sentence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fraud - wrongful or criminal deception intended to result in financial or personal gain
