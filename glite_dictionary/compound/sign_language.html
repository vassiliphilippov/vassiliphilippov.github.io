sign language

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪn ˌlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈsaɪn ˌlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
A language that uses visual hand signs, gestures, and facial expressions as its primary means of communication, especially used by people who are deaf or hard of hearing.
language using hand signs and gestures

Examples

Many people who are deaf communicate using sign language.
She decided to learn sign language to talk with her cousin.
There are several different types of sign language used around the world.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: spoken language - language expressed through speech
  • Synonym: gesture - movement of hands or body to express an idea

2. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪn ˌlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈsaɪn ˌlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
a system of communication using visual gestures and signs, commonly used by deaf people
visual language of gestures, used by the deaf

Examples

Learning sign language opened up new ways to communicate with my deaf friends.
Many schools now offer sign language courses for students and staff.
The interpreter translated the speech into sign language for the audience.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: spoken language - language communicated through speech
  • Synonym: gesture - a movement of part of the body, especially a hand or the head, to express an idea or meaning

Etymology

From 'sign' meaning visual gesture or symbol, and 'language', from Latin 'lingua'.
