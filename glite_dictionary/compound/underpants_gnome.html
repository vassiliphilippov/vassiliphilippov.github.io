underpants gnome

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌndɚˌpænts noʊm / (AmE), / ˈʌndəˌpænts nəʊm / (BrE)
a humorous reference to a character from the television show South Park, used to represent absurdity or a flawed scheme that lacks a crucial step for success
humorous symbol for flawed or absurd plan
informal

Examples

After reading the proposal, he joked that it sounded like an underpants gnome scheme.
The team worried they might become underpants gnomes by skipping important planning steps.
Her business pitch fell apart at step two, just like the underpants gnome strategy.

Synset Examples

We joked about being like the underpants gnome in our project.
The company's profits vanished, like the underpants gnome's plan.

Etymology

From the TV show South Park, where 'underpants gnome' characters execute a nonsensical, illogical profit plan.
