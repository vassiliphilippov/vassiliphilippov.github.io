non-insulin-dependent diabetes

1. NOUN

/ ˌnɑn ˈɪn.sjə.lɪn dɪˈpɛn.dənt daɪˈəˌbi.tiz / (AmE), / ˌnɒn ˈɪn.sjʊ.lɪn dɪˈpɛn.dənt daɪəˈbiː.tiːz / (BrE)
A mild form of diabetes mellitus that develops gradually in adults, often controlled without insulin injections, and commonly associated with obesity or stress; also known as type 2 diabetes.
type of diabetes controllable without insulin
medical

Examples

After being diagnosed with non-insulin-dependent diabetes, she managed the condition through careful diet and exercise.
Obesity is a significant risk factor for developing non-insulin-dependent diabetes in adulthood.
Many people with non-insulin-dependent diabetes can avoid insulin therapy by making lifestyle changes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: type 2 diabetes - common form of diabetes developing in adulthood, usually not needing insulin
  • Antonym: insulin-dependent diabetes - form of diabetes that requires insulin injections (type 1 diabetes)
