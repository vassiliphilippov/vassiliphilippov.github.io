deep-rooted value

1. NOUN

/ diːp ˈruːtɪd ˈvæljuː / (AmE), / diːp ˈruːtɪd ˈvæljuː / (BrE)
a belief or principle that is firmly established and difficult to change, often held by an individual or group over a long period
firmly established belief or principle

Examples

Honesty is a deep-rooted value in many families across generations.
The company prides itself on its deep-rooted values of trust and respect.
Changing a deep-rooted value often requires patience and understanding.

Synset Examples

The community's deep-rooted values are hard to change.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: core value - a fundamental, central belief or principle
