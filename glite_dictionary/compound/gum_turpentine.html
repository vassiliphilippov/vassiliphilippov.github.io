gum turpentine

1. NOUN

/ ɡʌm ˈtɝːpənˌtaɪn / (AmE), / ɡʌm ˈtɜːpənˌtaɪn / (BrE)
A fluid oleoresin obtained from coniferous trees, especially pines, used in solvents, varnishes, and in the production of turpentine oil.
oleoresin from pines used in varnishes and solvents

Examples

The painters used gum turpentine to thin their oil paints and clean their brushes.
Extraction of gum turpentine is a major industry in some forested regions.
Some varnishes contain gum turpentine to improve their flow and drying properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: turpentine - volatile oil distilled from oleoresin of pines

Etymology

From 'gum' (resin) and 'turpentine' (from Medieval Latin 'terebinthina', meaning resin from the terebinth tree).
