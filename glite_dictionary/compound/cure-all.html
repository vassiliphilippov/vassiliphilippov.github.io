cure-all

1. NOUN

/ ˈkjʊr ˌɔl / (AmE), / ˈkjʊə ˌɔːl / (BrE)
A remedy claimed to cure all diseases or problems; a universal solution, often regarded with skepticism.
remedy said to cure all ailments

Examples

Many people believed the new tonic was a cure-all for every ailment.
There is no cure-all for the complex issues facing society today.
The alchemists famously searched for a cure-all that could grant eternal health.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: panacea - a solution or remedy for all problems or diseases
