leaf lettuce

1. NOUN

/ lif ˈlɛt̬.ɪs / (AmE), / liːf ˈlɛt.ɪs / (BrE)
A type of lettuce with loosely curled leaves that do not form a compact head, often used in salads.
lettuce with loose, curly leaves

Examples

The salad was made with fresh leaf lettuce, cherry tomatoes, and cucumbers.
I prefer leaf lettuce to iceberg because it's more flavorful and tender.
We planted several types of leaf lettuce in our garden this spring.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: head lettuce - lettuce forming a compact, round head
  • Synonym: romaine - a variety of lettuce with long, crisp leaves
  • Degree Higher: butterhead lettuce - lettuce with soft, butter-textured leaves forming a head

2. NOUN

/ lif ˈlɛt.ɪs / (AmE), / liːf ˈlɛt.ɪs / (BrE)
A variety of lettuce with loose, curled or incised leaves that do not form a compact head.
lettuce with loose, curly leaves

Examples

I added freshly washed leaf lettuce to the salad for extra crunch.
Unlike iceberg, leaf lettuce has open, loose leaves and a milder flavor.
She prefers making sandwiches with leaf lettuce instead of head lettuce.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: head lettuce - lettuce that forms a compact, round head
  • Degree Higher: romaine - a variety of lettuce with long, crisp leaves and a firm rib
  • Synonym: butterhead lettuce - a type of lettuce with soft, round leaves that form a loose head

Synset POS: n
