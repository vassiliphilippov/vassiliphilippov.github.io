golden maidenhair

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡoʊldən ˈmeɪdənˌhɛr / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊldən ˈmeɪdənheə / (BrE)
a species of mat-forming fern with creeping rootstocks and large pinnatifid fronds, found in North America, Europe, Africa, and East Asia
a species of fern with pinnatifid fronds

Examples

The shady woodland slope was covered in golden maidenhair, giving a soft green shimmer to the ground.
Gardeners often plant golden maidenhair in rock gardens for its attractive, delicate fronds.
Researchers documented the presence of golden maidenhair populations along the riverbanks of eastern Asia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maidenhair fern - any fern of the genus Adiantum, typically with delicate divided fronds
