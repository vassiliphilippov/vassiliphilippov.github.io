wild card

1. NOUN

/ waɪld kɑrd / (AmE), / waɪld kɑːd / (BrE)
A playing card that can represent any card chosen by the player holding it.
a card in games representing any chosen value

Examples

In poker, the wild card can dramatically change the outcome of the game.
He drew a wild card and used it as an ace to win the hand.
When a wild card is in play, you need to be extra careful with your strategy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: joker - a playing card sometimes used as a wild card in card games

2. NOUN

/ ˈwaɪld ˌkɑrd / (AmE), / ˈwaɪld ˌkɑːd / (BrE)
A person or thing whose influence is unpredictable or whose characteristics are uncertain.
unpredictable factor; element of uncertainty

Examples

The weather was a real wild card that affected the outcome of the event.
Her unpredictable arrival added a new wild card to the meeting.
In this year's tournament, the new team is seen as a wild card by many experts.

Synset Examples

the weather was a wild card

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unknown quantity - a person or thing whose nature or impact is not yet known
  • Antonym: predictable - something whose outcome or behavior can be anticipated

Etymology

Originally from the use of special cards with flexible value in card games.
