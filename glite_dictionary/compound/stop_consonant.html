stop consonant

1. NOUN

/ stɑp ˈkɑn.sə.nənt / (AmE), / stɒp ˈkɒn.sə.nənt / (BrE)
a consonant sound produced by completely blocking the flow of air and then suddenly releasing it
consonant made by blocking and releasing air

Examples

The teacher explained that the sound 'p' is a stop consonant in English.
Children often have trouble pronouncing stop consonants clearly at first.
Voiced and voiceless stop consonants are distinguished by timing in speech.

Synset Examples

his stop consonants are too aspirated

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plosive - a consonant produced by stopping airflow and releasing it; synonym for stop consonant

Etymology

From 'stop' (block) + 'consonant' (speech sound); used in linguistics since the 19th century.
