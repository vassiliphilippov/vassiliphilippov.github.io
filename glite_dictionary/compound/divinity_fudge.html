divinity fudge

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈvɪnəti fʌdʒ / (AmE), / dɪˈvɪnɪti fʌdʒ / (BrE)
A white, creamy fudge made with egg whites, sugar, and often nuts or vanilla, known for its light, airy texture.
white, creamy egg-white fudge

Examples

She brought a plate of homemade divinity fudge to the holiday party.
The recipe for divinity fudge calls for egg whites, sugar, and a pinch of salt.
My grandmother used to make divinity fudge every Christmas.

Etymology

The name 'divinity' may refer to the candy's delicate and 'heavenly' texture; popular in early 20th-century America.
