price level

1. NOUN

/ ˈpraɪs ˌlɛvəl / (AmE), / ˈpraɪs ˌlɛvəl / (BrE)
an economic index measuring the average prices of goods and services over time or comparing changes in the value of money
average measure of prices over time

Examples

The central bank monitors the price level to assess inflation trends in the economy.
Rapid changes in the price level can affect consumers' purchasing power.
Economists use the consumer price index to measure changes in the price level over time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inflation - a general increase in prices and fall in the purchasing value of money
  • Antonym: deflation - a general decrease in prices, or the increase in the value of money
