Alaska Purchase

1. NOUN

/ əˈlæskə ˈpɝː.tʃəs / (AmE), / əˈlæskə ˈpɜː.tʃəs / (BrE)
the 1867 transaction in which the United States bought Alaska from Russia, arranged by Secretary of State William H. Seward
purchase of Alaska by the US from Russia in 1867

Examples

The Alaska Purchase expanded the territory of the United States significantly in the 19th century.
Many Americans initially mocked the Alaska Purchase as "Seward's Folly."
The Alaska Purchase was negotiated between U.S. Secretary of State Seward and the Russian Empire in 1867.
