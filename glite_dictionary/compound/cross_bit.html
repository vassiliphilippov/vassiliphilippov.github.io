cross bit

1. NOUN

/ krɑs bɪt / (AmE), / krɒs bɪt / (BrE)
a rock drill bit with cruciform (cross-shaped) cutting edges, typically used in mining and drilling operations
rock drill bit with cross-shaped edges

Examples

The mining crew replaced the worn cross bit before starting the next shift underground.
A cross bit is ideal for drilling into hard, rocky surfaces because of its cruciform design.
Over time, the cutting edges of a cross bit can become blunt and require sharpening.
