tax policy

1. NOUN

/ tæks ˈpɑləsi / (AmE), / tæks ˈpɒləsi / (BrE)
the set of principles or measures a government uses to decide how taxes are levied, collected, and managed
government principles for setting taxes

Examples

The government announced changes to its tax policy aimed at stimulating economic growth.
A country's tax policy can significantly impact business investment decisions.
Public debates about tax policy often focus on fairness and efficiency.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fiscal policy - government policy on revenue, spending, and debt management
