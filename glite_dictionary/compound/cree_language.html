Cree language

1. NOUN

/ kri ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / kri ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Algonquian language spoken by the Cree people, primarily in Canada
Indigenous Algonquian language of the Cree

Examples

She is studying the Cree language at university to better understand her heritage.
Many communities in northern Canada continue to speak the Cree language today.
The Cree language includes several dialects spread across a large region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Algonquian - a family of North American indigenous languages

Etymology

From Cree, the name of the indigenous people, plus 'language'.
