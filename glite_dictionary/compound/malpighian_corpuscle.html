malpighian corpuscle

1. NOUN

/ mælˈpɪgiən ˈkɔrpəsəl / (AmE), / malˈpɪɡiən ˈkɔːpʌsl / (BrE)
a structure in the kidney consisting of Bowman's capsule and a glomerulus at the end of a nephron, where filtration of blood occurs
kidney structure with Bowman's capsule and glomerulus
medical

Examples

The malpighian corpuscle plays a crucial role in the filtration process within the kidney.
Scientists examined the structure of the malpighian corpuscle under a microscope to better understand kidney function.
Diseases affecting the malpighian corpuscle can lead to serious renal complications.

Etymology

Named after Marcello Malpighi, Italian biologist (1628–1694), who first described the structure.
