patent infringement

1. NOUN

/ ˈpætənt ɪnˈfrɪndʒmənt / (AmE), / ˈpeɪtənt ɪnˈfrɪndʒmənt / (BrE)
the act of violating the rights granted by a patent, typically by making, using, or selling a patented invention without permission
violation of a patent holder's rights
legal

Examples

The company faced a lawsuit over patent infringement after releasing its new smartphone.
If you manufacture products that use protected technology without permission, you risk committing patent infringement.
They settled out of court to avoid a lengthy patent infringement case.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: copyright infringement - unauthorized use of copyrighted material
  • Synonym: trademark infringement - unauthorized use of a trademark
