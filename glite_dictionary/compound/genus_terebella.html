genus Terebella

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˌtɛrəˈbɛlə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˌtɛrəˈbɛlə / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Terebellidae, consisting of tube-forming marine polychaete worms with numerous filamentous tentacles.
type genus of Terebellidae polychaete worms

Examples

The genus Terebella is distinguished by its many long, filamentous tentacles.
Marine biologists often study the genus Terebella to better understand polychaete behavior.
Species within the genus Terebella construct tubes using secreted mucus and sand particles.

Etymology

From New Latin, Terebella, a genus name; Latin 'terebella' meaning 'little borer'.
