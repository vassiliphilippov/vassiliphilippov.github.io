out-of-town

1. ADJ

/ aʊt əv taʊn / (AmE), / aʊt əv taʊn / (BrE)
happening in or coming from another town or city; not local.
from or in another town or city

Examples

We decided to hold the conference at an out-of-town hotel to avoid distractions.
The theater often hosts out-of-town performances before they open on Broadway.
She found an out-of-town school that matched her interests.

Synset Examples

an out-of-town tryout
an out-of-town school

Related Terms

  • Antonym: local - relating to the particular area you are in
