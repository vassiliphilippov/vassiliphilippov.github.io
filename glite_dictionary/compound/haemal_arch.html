haemal arch

1. NOUN

/ ˈhiːməl ɑːrtʃ / (AmE), / ˈhiːməl ɑːtʃ / (BrE)
a bony or cartilaginous structure projecting from the underside of a vertebral centrum, enclosing the caudal blood vessels, especially in the tails of vertebrates.
arch below vertebrae enclosing tail blood vessels
medical

Examples

In fish, the haemal arch protects the major blood vessels running through the tail.
Researchers carefully examined the haemal arch in several reptile skeletons to study their evolution.
Damage to the haemal arch can compromise blood flow in the lower body of some animals.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: neural arch - the dorsal arch of a vertebra enclosing the spinal cord

Etymology

From Greek haima (blood) + arch (arch, bow).
