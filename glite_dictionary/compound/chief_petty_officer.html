chief petty officer

1. NOUN

/ tʃif ˈpɛti ˌɑfɪsər / (AmE), / tʃiːf ˈpeti ˌɒfɪsə / (BrE)
a senior noncommissioned officer in the navy, typically ranking above a petty officer and below a warrant officer
senior noncommissioned naval rank

Examples

He was promoted to chief petty officer after twenty years of service in the Navy.
A chief petty officer is responsible for training junior sailors on the ship.
Three chief petty officers attended the leadership conference last week.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: petty officer - a noncommissioned officer in the navy below chief petty officer
  • Degree Higher: warrant officer - a naval officer holding a rank above chief petty officer
