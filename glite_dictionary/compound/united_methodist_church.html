United Methodist Church

1. PROPN

/ ˌjuːnaɪtɪd ˈmɛθədɪst tʃɜrtʃ / (AmE), / ˌjuːnaɪtɪd ˈmɛθədɪst tʃɜːtʃ / (BrE)
a denomination of the Christian Church formed by the merger of the Wesleyan Methodist Church and the Evangelical United Brethren Church, prominent especially in the United States
Christian denomination formed by a merger

Examples

The United Methodist Church is one of the largest Protestant denominations in the United States.
After moving to a new city, she joined the local United Methodist Church to find a sense of community.
The United Methodist Church was established in 1968 after the historic merger.
