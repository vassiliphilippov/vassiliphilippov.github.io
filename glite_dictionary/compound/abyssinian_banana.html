Abyssinian banana

1. NOUN

/ əˌbɪsɪˈniən bəˈnænə / (AmE), / əˌbɪsɪˈniːən bəˈnænə / (BrE)
a large evergreen plant with huge paddle-shaped leaves, related to the banana and bearing inedible banana-like fruit and edible young flower shoots, native to East Africa
tropical African plant related to the banana

Examples

The Abyssinian banana is grown mainly for ornamental purposes in tropical and subtropical gardens.
The broad leaves of the Abyssinian banana can create a dramatic focal point in any landscape.
Although its fruit is inedible, the flower shoots of the Abyssinian banana may be cooked and eaten in some cultures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: banana - a long, curved fruit with a yellow skin and soft, sweet flesh

Etymology

Abyssinian refers to Abyssinia, the historical name for Ethiopia, where the plant originates.
