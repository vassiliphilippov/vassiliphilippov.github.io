associate professor

1. NOUN

/ əˈsoʊsiət prəˈfɛsər / (AmE), / əˈsəʊsɪət prəˈfɛsə / (BrE)
A university or college teacher ranking above an assistant professor and below a full professor.
a mid-level academic rank

Examples

She was promoted to associate professor after several years of research and teaching.
He applied for tenure as an associate professor in the biology department.
The university is hiring an associate professor of mathematics this semester.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: assistant professor - a university teacher ranking below associate professor
  • Degree Higher: professor - a senior university teacher; the highest academic rank
  • Degree Lower: lecturer - a university or college teacher, often junior rank
