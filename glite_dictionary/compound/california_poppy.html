California poppy

1. NOUN

/ kəˌlɪfɔrniə ˈpɑpi / (AmE), / kəˌlɪfəˈnɪə ˈpɒpi / (BrE)
a plant native to the Pacific coast of North America, widely cultivated for its bright yellow to orange-red flowers
a North American flowering plant

Examples

The vibrant California poppy blooms each spring, covering hillsides with brilliant color.
She planted several California poppies in her garden to attract butterflies.
California poppy is the state flower of California and is beloved for its resilience and beauty.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poppy - a plant of the genus Papaver with showy flowers, often red
