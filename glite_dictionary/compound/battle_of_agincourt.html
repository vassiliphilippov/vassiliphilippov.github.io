Battle of Agincourt

1. PROPN

/ ˈbætl əv ˈædʒɪnˌkɔrt / (AmE), / ˈbætl əv ˈædʒɪnˌkɔːt / (BrE)
a battle fought in northern France in 1415, where English forces under Henry V decisively defeated a larger French army during the Hundred Years' War
decisive 1415 English victory over French army

Examples

The Battle of Agincourt is remembered for the effectiveness of English longbowmen.
Many historians regard the Battle of Agincourt as a turning point in the Hundred Years' War.
Shakespeare dramatized the Battle of Agincourt in his play 'Henry V'.

Etymology

Agincourt from the French place name Agincourt.
