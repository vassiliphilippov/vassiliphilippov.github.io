Empty Quiver approach

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛmpti ˈkwɪvɚ əˈproʊtʃ / (AmE), / ˈɛmpti ˈkwɪvə əˈprəʊtʃ / (BrE)
A strategy or policy that involves using all available resources or options, leaving nothing in reserve.
strategy of using all available resources

Examples

The team adopted an Empty Quiver approach for the finals, using every player in the squad.
In times of crisis, an Empty Quiver approach may be necessary to address urgent needs.
The CEO’s Empty Quiver approach left the company with no additional resources for emergencies.

Synset Examples

After the last game, the coach declared an Empty Quiver approach.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scorched earth policy - military strategy of destroying resources to prevent enemy use
  • Antonym: hold in reserve - to keep something back for future use

Etymology

From 'empty the quiver', a metaphor alluding to using up all arrows (resources) in a quiver.
