flagellated cell

1. NOUN

/ fləˈdʒɛl.eɪ.tɪd sɛl / (AmE), / fləˈdʒɛl.ɪ.eɪ.tɪd sɛl / (BrE)
a cell or unicellular organism that possesses one or more flagella
cell with a flagellum

Examples

A sperm is a type of flagellated cell that uses its tail to move toward the egg.
Certain algae have a flagellated cell stage during their life cycle.
Researchers studied the movement patterns of the flagellated cell under a microscope.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flagellum - a long, whip-like appendage used for movement in some cells

Etymology

From 'flagellated', from Latin 'flagellum' (whip), referring to the whip-like structure.
