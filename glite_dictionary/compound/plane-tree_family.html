plane-tree family

1. NOUN

/ ˈpleɪnˌtri ˈfæməli / (AmE), / ˈpleɪnˌtri ˈfæmɪli / (BrE)
A family of deciduous trees, coextensive with the genus Platanus, commonly known as plane trees, characterized by their broad leaves and patchy bark.
botanical family of plane trees

Examples

The plane-tree family includes several species cherished for their shade and ornamental value.
Many parks feature trees from the plane-tree family because of their resistance to pollution.
Leaves of the plane-tree family are often large and palmately lobed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Platanus - the genus comprising all plane trees
