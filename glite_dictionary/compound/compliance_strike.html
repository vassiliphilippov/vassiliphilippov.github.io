compliance strike

1. NOUN

/ kəmˈplaɪəns straɪk / (AmE), / kəmˈplaɪəns straɪk / (BrE)
a strike carried out by workers to demand adherence to regulations or resolve issues of compliance, especially regarding workplace rules and safety standards
strike for adherence to regulations

Examples

The union called a compliance strike to enforce health and safety standards in the factory.
After failed negotiations, a compliance strike halted production until management agreed to follow safety protocols.
Many companies face losses when workers launch a compliance strike over regulatory violations.

Synset Examples

The workers initiated a compliance strike for better safety standards.
A compliance strike can disrupt operations significantly.
Management responded quickly to avoid a compliance strike.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strike - an organized refusal to work by employees as a form of protest
