brewer's grain

1. NOUN

/ ˈbruːɚz ɡreɪn / (AmE), / ˈbruːəz ɡreɪn / (BrE)
the solid residue left after malt and grain have been mashed and lautered in the brewing process, often used as animal feed
residue from brewing process

Examples

Dairy farms often supplement their cattle feed with brewer's grain for added nutrition.
After the beer was brewed, the leftover brewer's grain was delivered to a local farm.
Pigs and chickens can both benefit from the protein in brewer's grain.

Synset Examples

As it mainly consists of carbohydrates and proteins, and is readily consumed by animals, spent grain is used in animal feed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spent grain - the solid remains of malted grain after the brewing process
