dirty tricks

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɝːti trɪks / (AmE), / ˈdɜːti trɪks / (BrE)
underhand or dishonest actions, especially in politics or business, designed to harm or discredit an opponent
underhand actions to discredit an opponent

Examples

The candidate accused his rival of using dirty tricks to sway the election.
Their company was known for resorting to dirty tricks against competitors.
We must win this contract fairly, without any dirty tricks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smear campaign - an effort to damage someone’s reputation with false accusations or misleading information
  • Synonym: sabotage - deliberate actions to damage or hinder someone or something
  • Antonym: fair play - honest and straightforward behavior
