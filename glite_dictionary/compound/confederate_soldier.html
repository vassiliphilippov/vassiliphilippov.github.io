Confederate soldier

1. NOUN

/ kənˈfɛdərət ˈsoʊldʒər / (AmE), / kənˈfɛdərət ˈsəʊldʒə / (BrE)
a soldier who served in the army of the Confederate States of America during the American Civil War (1861–1865)
soldier of the Confederacy in the Civil War

Examples

The museum displayed a uniform worn by a Confederate soldier during the American Civil War.
Many letters from a young Confederate soldier survived and provide a glimpse into life during the conflict.
He traced his ancestry back to a Confederate soldier who fought at Gettysburg.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Union soldier - a soldier in the army of the Union (United States) during the American Civil War

Etymology

From 'Confederate', referring to the Confederate States of America, and 'soldier', from Latin 'solidarius' (one who serves for pay).
