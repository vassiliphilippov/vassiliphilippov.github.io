sulphur oxide

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌlfɚ ˈɑkˌsaɪd / (AmE), / ˈsʌlfə ˈɒksaɪd / (BrE)
any of several chemical compounds consisting of sulphur and oxygen, notably sulphur dioxide and sulphur trioxide, which are important in chemistry and environmental science
chemical compound of sulphur and oxygen

Examples

Industrial processes release sulphur oxide into the atmosphere, contributing to air pollution.
The environmental impact of sulphur oxides is a major concern for regulatory agencies.
Desulfurization technologies aim to reduce sulphur oxide emissions from power plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sulphur dioxide - a compound of sulphur and oxygen, SO₂
  • Synonym: sulphur trioxide - a compound of sulphur and oxygen, SO₃

Etymology

From 'sulphur', from Latin 'sulphur', and 'oxide', from Greek 'oxys' (sharp, acidic) + '-ide', used in chemistry for binary compounds.
