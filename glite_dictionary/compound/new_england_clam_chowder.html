New England clam chowder

1. NOUN

/ nu ˈɪŋɡlənd klæm ˈtʃaʊdər / (AmE), / njuː ˈɪŋɡlənd klæm ˈtʃaʊdə / (BrE)
a thick, creamy soup made with clams, potatoes, onions, salt pork, and milk, typically associated with New England
a creamy clam soup from New England

Examples

The restaurant's signature dish is New England clam chowder, served hot with oyster crackers.
She ordered a bowl of New England clam chowder to warm up on the cold day.
Many tourists try authentic New England clam chowder when visiting Boston.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clam chowder - a soup containing clams and other ingredients
  • Antonym: Manhattan clam chowder - a tomato-based clam chowder originating from New York

Etymology

Named after New England, a region in the northeastern United States, and from 'chowder', possibly from French 'chaudière' (stew pot).
