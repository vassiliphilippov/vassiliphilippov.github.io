litres per hundred kilometres

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪtərz pɚ ˈhʌndrəd kɪˈlɑmɪtɚz / (AmE), / ˈliːtəz pə ˈhʌndrəd kɪˈlɒmɪtəz / (BrE)
a measure of a vehicle's fuel consumption, equal to the number of litres of fuel used to travel one hundred kilometres
fuel consumption measure (litres/100 km)

Examples

The car's fuel efficiency is rated at 6 litres per hundred kilometres on the highway.
Many European countries report fuel consumption in litres per hundred kilometres rather than miles per gallon.
New models boast lower litres per hundred kilometres, making them more economical for drivers.

Synset Examples

Liters per one hundred kilometers is considered to be a more accurate measure of a vehicle's performance because it is a linear relationship while fuel economy leads to distortions in efficiency improvements.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: miles per gallon - a measure of fuel efficiency indicating miles traveled per gallon of fuel
