giant foxtail

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒaɪənt ˈfɑkˌsteɪl / (AmE), / ˈdʒaɪənt ˈfɒksteɪl / (BrE)
a coarse annual grass of the foxtail genus (Setaria faberi), considered a weed and commonly found in the United States
a weed grass (Setaria faberi) widespread in the US

Examples

The field was overrun with giant foxtail, making it difficult for crops to grow.
Farmers struggle each year to control the spread of giant foxtail in their soybean fields.
Her research focuses on the ecological impact of giant foxtail as an invasive weed species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foxtail - a type of grass with bushy, spike-like seed heads resembling a fox's tail
