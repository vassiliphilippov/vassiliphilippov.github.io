elephantiasis neuromatosa

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛlɪfænˈtaɪəsɪs nʊˌroʊməˈtoʊsə / (AmE), / ˌɛlɪfænˈtaɪəsɪs njuːˌrəʊməˈtəʊsə / (BrE)
Abnormal enlargement and thickening of a limb, typically due to neurofibromatosis.
enlargement of a limb due to neurofibromatosis
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with elephantiasis neuromatosa after presenting with a massively enlarged leg.
Elephantiasis neuromatosa is commonly associated with advanced cases of neurofibromatosis type 1.
Treatment options for elephantiasis neuromatosa may include surgery to reduce limb size.

Etymology

From Greek 'elephas' (elephant) and 'neuromatosis' (a tumor condition involving nerves).
