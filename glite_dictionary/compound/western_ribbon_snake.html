Western ribbon snake

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɛstərn ˈrɪbən sneɪk / (AmE), / ˈwɛstən ˈrɪbən sneɪk / (BrE)
a slender, nonvenomous North American snake with yellow or reddish stripes, found in woodlands, grasslands, and tropical areas
striped North American snake species

Examples

The Western ribbon snake is often found near streams and ponds.
I spotted a Western ribbon snake slithering through the tall grass in Texas.
Unlike other snakes in the region, the Western ribbon snake is harmless to humans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ribbon snake - a species of snake characterized by long stripes along its body
