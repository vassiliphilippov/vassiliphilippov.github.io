saurian creature

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɔriən ˈkritʃər / (AmE), / ˈsɔːrɪən ˈkriːtʃə / (BrE)
a lizard-like reptile, typically used to refer to members of the order Sauria or similar extinct reptiles
lizard-like reptile

Examples

The fossils revealed that the region was once inhabited by giant saurian creatures.
Many children are fascinated by dinosaurs and other saurian creatures from prehistoric times.
Scientists debate how some saurian creatures of the Mesozoic era moved and hunted.

Synset Examples

He read about the saurian creatures of the Jurassic period.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lizard - a small reptile with a long body and tail
  • Synonym: reptile - a cold-blooded, scaly vertebrate animal

Etymology

From Latin 'saurus' (lizard) via New Latin 'Sauria' (the reptile suborder).
