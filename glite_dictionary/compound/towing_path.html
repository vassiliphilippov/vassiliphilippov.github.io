towing path

1. NOUN

/ ˈtoʊɪŋ pæθ / (AmE), / ˈtəʊɪŋ pɑːθ / (BrE)
a path alongside a canal or river, used by animals or people towing boats from the waterway's bank
path beside a waterway for towing boats

Examples

Horses once pulled barges along the towing path beside this old canal.
We walked along the towing path, enjoying the peaceful river views.
Maintenance crews keep the towing path clear for cyclists and walkers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: towpath - a path beside a river or canal for towing boats
