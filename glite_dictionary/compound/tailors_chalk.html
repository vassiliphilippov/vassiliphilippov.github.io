tailor's chalk

1. NOUN

/ ˈteɪlərz tʃɔk / (AmE), / ˈteɪləz tʃɔːk / (BrE)
a thin, flat piece of chalk used by tailors to make temporary marks on fabric when cutting and fitting garments
chalk used for marking fabric in tailoring

Examples

She marked the hemline with tailor's chalk before starting to sew.
A piece of tailor's chalk is essential for accurate dressmaking.
The white marks from the tailor's chalk disappear when the fabric is pressed.
