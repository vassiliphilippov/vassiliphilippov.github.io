creep hole

1. NOUN

/ krip hoʊl / (AmE), / kriːp həʊl / (BrE)
A small opening or passage, often used to sneak or spy through.
small opening for sneaking or spying

Examples

The children found a creep hole under the fence and crawled into the neighbor's yard.
A narrow creep hole behind the fireplace led to a secret chamber.
He peered through the creep hole to watch the intruders without being seen.

Synset Examples

The creephole led to a hidden garden behind the house.
They discovered a creephole that opened into the old barn.
