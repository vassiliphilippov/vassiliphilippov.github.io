red bearberry

1. NOUN

/ rɛd ˈbɛrˌbɛri / (AmE), / rɛd ˈbɛːˌbɛri / (BrE)
an evergreen mat-forming shrub found in North America and northern Eurasia, with small white flowers, red berries, and leaves that turn red in autumn
evergreen shrub with red berries

Examples

In the tundra, red bearberry provides vital food for wildlife during the harsh winters.
Many gardeners value red bearberry for its colorful foliage and ground-covering habit.
The leaves of the red bearberry turn a brilliant red as autumn approaches.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bearberry - a creeping shrub with edible red berries, found in northern regions
