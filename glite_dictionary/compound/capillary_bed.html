capillary bed

1. NOUN

/ kəˈpɪləri bɛd / (AmE), / kəˈpɪləri bɛd / (BrE)
a network of tiny blood vessels (capillaries) densely packed in a tissue, allowing the exchange of substances between blood and surrounding cells
network of tiny blood vessels in tissue
medical

Examples

Oxygen and nutrients pass from the blood into tissues through the capillary bed.
Damage to the capillary bed can result in poor tissue perfusion.
The lungs contain an extensive capillary bed for gas exchange.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: capillary - a very small blood vessel

Etymology

From Latin 'capillaris' (of hair, hair-like) and English 'bed' (layer or area).
