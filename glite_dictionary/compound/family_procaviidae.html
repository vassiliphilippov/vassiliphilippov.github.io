family Procaviidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli proʊˌkeɪviˈaɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli prəʊˌkeɪvɪˈiːdiː / (BrE)
A family of small herbivorous mammals, commonly known as hyraxes, encompassing all recent members of the order Hyracoidea.
a family of mammals including hyraxes

Examples

The family Procaviidae is native to Africa and the Middle East, where hyraxes can be found in rocky terrain.
Members of the family Procaviidae are distinguished by their unique foot structure and incisors.
Researchers studied the evolutionary relationships within the family Procaviidae to understand their adaptation to diverse habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hyrax - small, thickset, herbivorous mammal of Africa and the Middle East

Etymology

From New Latin Procaviidae, based on Procavia (genus of hyraxes) + '-idae' (zoological family suffix).
