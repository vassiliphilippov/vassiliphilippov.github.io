adverse opinion

1. NOUN

/ ædˈvɝːs əˈpɪnjən / (AmE), / ədˈvɜːs əˈpɪnjən / (BrE)
an official statement by an auditor indicating that a company's financial statements do not fairly represent its financial position or comply with accepted accounting standards
audit statement that financial reports are not fairly presented

Examples

The auditor issued an adverse opinion after finding major discrepancies in the company's financial records.
Receiving an adverse opinion can harm a corporation's reputation and access to credit.
Because the statements contained significant errors, the accountant concluded with an adverse opinion.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: qualified opinion - an auditor's opinion that financial statements are mostly fairly presented, with some exceptions
  • Degree Lower: unqualified opinion - an auditor's opinion that financial statements are fairly presented and comply with standards
