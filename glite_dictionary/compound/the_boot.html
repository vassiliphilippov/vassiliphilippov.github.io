the boot

1. NOUN

/ ðə buːt / (AmE), / ðə buːt / (BrE)
An instrument of torture used historically to crush or heat a person's foot and leg.
instrument of torture for foot and leg

Examples

During the inquisition, prisoners feared the boot more than any other torture device.
Historical records describe how the boot was used to extract confessions.
In the museum's torture exhibit, the boot is displayed next to other gruesome devices.
