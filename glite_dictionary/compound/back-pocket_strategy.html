back-pocket strategy

1. NOUN

/ ˌbæk ˈpɑːkɪt ˈstrætədʒi / (AmE), / ˌbæk ˈpɒkɪt ˈstrætədʒi / (BrE)
a secret or reserved plan kept for use in unexpected situations or emergencies
secret plan for emergencies

Examples

It's wise to develop a back-pocket strategy in case negotiations break down.
She always has a back-pocket strategy for handling last-minute problems at work.
The coach used his back-pocket strategy when the original game plan failed.

Synset Examples

I always keep a few back-pocket strategies ready for unexpected challenges.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contingency plan - a plan designed to take account of a possible future event or circumstance

Etymology

Metaphorically from the idea of keeping something hidden or in reserve in one's back pocket.
