nonverbal intelligence

1. NOUN

/ nɑnˈvɝːbəl ɪnˈtɛlɪdʒəns / (AmE), / nɒnˈvɜːbəl ɪnˈtɛlɪdʒəns / (BrE)
the ability to understand and solve problems or tasks without using spoken or written language
intelligence shown without language use

Examples

Children with high nonverbal intelligence may excel at puzzles or visual-spatial tasks.
Some standardized tests are designed to measure nonverbal intelligence exclusively.
Her nonverbal intelligence helped her solve complex problems, even though she struggled with reading.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: verbal intelligence - intelligence shown through the use of language
