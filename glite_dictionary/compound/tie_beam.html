tie beam

1. NOUN

/ ˈtaɪ ˌbiːm / (AmE), / ˈtaɪ ˌbiːm / (BrE)
a horizontal beam in a structure that prevents two other structural members from spreading apart or separating
horizontal beam preventing spread in structures

Examples

The architect specified an extra tie beam to support the old roof.
Without a tie beam, the stone walls might shift apart over time.
A carpenter explained how a tie beam keeps the rafters stable in a wooden frame.

Synset Examples

he nailed the rafters together with a tie beam
