Patwin language

1. NOUN

/ ˈpæt.wɪn ˈlæŋ.ɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈpætwɪn ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Native American language traditionally spoken by the Patwin people of northern California
Native American language of the Patwin

Examples

The Patwin language is critically endangered, with only a few fluent speakers remaining today.
Linguists have documented oral histories in the Patwin language for cultural preservation.
She is learning the Patwin language to reconnect with her ancestral roots.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Wintun language - another language in the Wintuan family of northern California

Etymology

Named after the Patwin people, indigenous to northern California.
