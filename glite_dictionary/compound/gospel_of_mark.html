Gospel of Mark

1. PROPN

/ ˈɡɑspəl əv mɑrk / (AmE), / ˈɡɒspəl əv mɑːk / (BrE)
the shortest of the four Gospels in the New Testament, recounting the life and teachings of Jesus Christ
one of the New Testament Gospels

Examples

The Gospel of Mark provides a fast-paced account of Jesus’s life and ministry.
Scholars often compare the Gospel of Mark with the other Synoptic Gospels.
She found a passage in the Gospel of Mark that inspired her.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Gospel of Matthew - the first book of the New Testament Gospels
  • Synonym: Gospel of Luke - one of the four Gospels in the New Testament
  • Synonym: Gospel of John - one of the four Gospels in the New Testament

Etymology

From Old English 'gōdspel' (good news) and Mark, after the traditional author, John Mark.
