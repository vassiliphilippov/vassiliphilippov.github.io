weasel word

1. NOUN

/ ˈwizəl wɝd / (AmE), / ˈwiːzəl wɜːd / (BrE)
a word or phrase used to avoid making a direct or clear statement, often to mislead or evade responsibility
evasive or ambiguous word/phrase

Examples

Politicians often use weasel words to avoid answering tough questions directly.
The advertisement was full of weasel words that made the product sound better than it really is.
She criticized the report for relying on weasel words instead of providing concrete facts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: equivocation - the use of ambiguous language to conceal the truth
  • Antonym: direct statement - an explicit or unambiguous assertion

Etymology

From the animal 'weasel', alluding to the idea of sucking the meaning out of a statement, as a weasel supposedly sucks out the contents of an egg.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwiːzəl wɝːd / (AmE), / ˈwiːzəl wɜːd / (BrE)
a word or phrase used to avoid stating something explicitly or to create ambiguity, often to mislead or evade responsibility
ambiguous or evasive language

Examples

His statement was filled with weasel words that made his message unclear.
Avoiding direct answers, the report used multiple weasel words throughout the summary.
She called out the spokesperson for relying on weasel words instead of giving real answers.

Synset Examples

She recognized his weasel words and demanded clarity.
Politicians often use weasel words to dodge accountability.
His answer was full of weasel words and avoided the question.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: euphemism - a mild or indirect word substituted for one considered too harsh

Etymology

From the reputation of weasels as sneaky animals, plus 'word.' First recorded in the early 20th century.
