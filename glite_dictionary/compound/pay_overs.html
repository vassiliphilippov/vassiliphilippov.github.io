to pay overs

1. VERB

/ peɪ ˈoʊvərz / (AmE), / peɪ ˈəʊvəz / (BrE)
to pay more than the usual or expected price, often due to extra costs or scarcity
pay more than usual (esp. due to extra costs)

Examples

Due to high demand, they had to pay overs for the concert tickets.
When the shipment was delayed, the company ended up paying overs to secure materials from another supplier.
We refused to pay overs for a product we could find cheaper elsewhere.

Synset Examples

They were forced to pay overs for the supplies they needed.
The company had to pay overs due to unexpected costs.
Budget cuts meant they had to pay overs on their next project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to overpay - to pay more than is necessary or reasonable
  • Antonym: to underpay - to pay less than is due, expected, or usual
