dissociation energy

1. NOUN

/ dɪˌsoʊsieɪʃən ˈɛnɚdʒi / (AmE), / dɪˌsəʊsiˈeɪʃən ˈɛnədʒi / (BrE)
the amount of energy required to break all chemical bonds in a molecule, separating it into individual atoms
energy to separate all atoms in a molecule
medical

Examples

The dissociation energy of hydrogen molecules is a fundamental constant in chemistry.
Understanding the dissociation energy helps scientists predict the stability of chemical compounds.
High dissociation energy values mean that the molecule's bonds are particularly strong.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bond energy - energy required to break a specific chemical bond in a molecule

Etymology

From 'dissociation', from Latin dissociare (to separate), and 'energy', from Greek energeia (activity, operation).
