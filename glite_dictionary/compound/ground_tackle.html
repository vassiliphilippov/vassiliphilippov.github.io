ground tackle

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡraʊnd ˌtækəl / (AmE), / ˈɡraʊnd ˌtakəl / (BrE)
the anchor, cables, and related equipment used to secure a vessel in place
equipment to anchor a vessel

Examples

The ship's ground tackle was inspected before setting sail.
Good ground tackle is essential to keep your boat secure during a storm.
They replaced the old ground tackle to improve safety.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anchor - a heavy object attached to a cable or chain and used to moor a vessel to the sea bottom
  • Synonym: moorings - devices or equipment used to secure a vessel in position
