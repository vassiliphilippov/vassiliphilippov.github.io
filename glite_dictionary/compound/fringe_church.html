fringe church

1. NOUN

/ frɪndʒ tʃɝtʃ / (AmE), / frɪndʒ tʃɜːtʃ / (BrE)
a religious group or organization that operates outside mainstream denominations, often characterized by unconventional practices and beliefs
non-mainstream religious group

Examples

After years of dissatisfaction, he decided to join a fringe church with a small but dedicated following.
The documentary explored how a fringe church challenged traditional religious doctrines in the community.
Local authorities monitored the fringe church due to concerns about its secretive ceremonies.

Synset Examples

She left the fringe church for a more traditional congregation.
The fringe church attracted followers with unorthodox beliefs.
Many people were skeptical about the fringe church's teachings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cult - a religious group often seen as unorthodox or spurious
  • Antonym: mainstream church - a church belonging to widely accepted denominations

Etymology

From 'fringe' (meaning on the periphery, marginal) + 'church'.
