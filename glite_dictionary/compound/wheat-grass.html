wheat-grass

1. NOUN

/ ˈwiːtˌɡɹæs / (AmE), / ˈwiːtˌɡrɑːs / (BrE)
a perennial grass of the genus Agropyron, native to temperate regions and often cultivated as forage or for soil stabilization
grass of the genus Agropyron

Examples

The farmer planted wheat-grass to prevent soil erosion along the hillside.
Many health drinks now include extracts from wheat-grass because of its nutritional benefits.
Wild wheat-grass covers large areas of the Great Plains.

Etymology

Formed from 'wheat' and 'grass' due to its wheat-like appearance.
