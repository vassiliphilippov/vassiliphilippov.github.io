bastion of history

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæstiən əv ˈhɪstəri / (AmE), / ˈbæstɪən əv ˈhɪstəri / (BrE)
a place, building, or institution that powerfully represents or preserves historical significance
place symbolizing historical significance

Examples

The museum has long been regarded as a bastion of history in the city.
Many consider the ancient cathedral to be a bastion of history and culture.
The university library is a bastion of history, filled with rare books and manuscripts.

Synset Examples

The old castle stood as a bastion of history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: landmark - a building or place that is easily recognized, especially one that is historically important
