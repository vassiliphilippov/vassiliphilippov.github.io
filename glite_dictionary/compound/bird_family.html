bird family

1. NOUN

/ bɝd ˈfæməli / (AmE), / bɜːd ˈfæməli / (BrE)
A taxonomic group of birds ranking above genus and below order, whose members share common characteristics and ancestry.
taxonomic group of birds above genus

Examples

The owl bird family includes several nocturnal species found worldwide.
Scientists discovered a new species belonging to the tanager bird family in South America.
Each bird family can be distinguished by unique features such as beak shape or feeding habits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: family - taxonomic rank in biological classification
