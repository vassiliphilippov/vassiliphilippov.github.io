Guiana Highlands

1. PROPN

/ giˈænə ˈhaɪləndz / (AmE), / giˈɑːnə ˈhaɪləndz / (BrE)
a mountainous plateau region in northern South America, extending from Venezuela into Guyana and northern Brazil
mountainous plateau in northern South America

Examples

The Guiana Highlands are known for their dramatic table-top mountains called tepuis.
Many unique plant and animal species live in the Guiana Highlands.
Angel Falls, the world's highest uninterrupted waterfall, is located in the Guiana Highlands.

Etymology

Named after Guiana, a historical region of South America; 'highlands' from English 'high' + 'lands'.
