pitcher sage

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪtʃɚ seɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈpɪtʃə seɪdʒ / (BrE)
an erect perennial plant native to California, with aromatic leaves and pale blue or violet flowers, belonging to the genus Lepechinia
a Californian perennial herb with aromatic leaves

Examples

The pitcher sage is prized in native gardens for its fragrant foliage and showy flowers.
We saw several clusters of pitcher sage growing along the hillside trail.
Gardeners appreciate pitcher sage because it attracts hummingbirds and bees.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɪtʃɚ seɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈpɪtʃə seɪdʒ / (BrE)
A plant native to California, characterized by woolly stems and leaves and large white flowers; commonly known as Salvia spathacea or other related species.
a California plant with woolly stems and white flowers

Examples

The pitcher sage brightened the garden with its beautiful white blooms.
Botanists often study native plants like pitcher sage in California chaparral habitats.
The woolly leaves of the pitcher sage distinguish it from other sages.
