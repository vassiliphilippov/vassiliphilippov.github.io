broad interpretation

1. NOUN

/ brɔd ˌɪntərprəˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / brɔːd ˌɪntəprɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
an approach to interpreting the U.S. Constitution in which judges consider not only the text but also the spirit of the times and the needs of the nation
liberal approach to constitutional interpretation
legal american

Examples

The judge's decision reflected a broad interpretation of constitutional rights.
Some legal scholars support a broad interpretation to adapt to modern challenges.
The Supreme Court has at times favored a broad interpretation of federal powers.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: strict interpretation - a legal doctrine advocating a literal, narrow reading of the Constitution
