channel catfish

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃænəl ˈkætˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈtʃæn.əl ˈkæt.fɪʃ / (BrE)
A North American freshwater catfish (Ictalurus punctatus) commonly found in central United States rivers and lakes, often farmed for food.
a common North American freshwater catfish

Examples

The river is home to a large population of channel catfish prized by local anglers.
Grilled channel catfish is a popular dish in many Southern restaurants.
Researchers tagged several channel catfish to study their migration patterns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catfish - a fish with whisker-like barbels around the mouth
