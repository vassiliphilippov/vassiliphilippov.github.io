rag week

1. NOUN

/ ræɡ wiːk / (AmE), / ræɡ wiːk / (BrE)
A week at some British universities during which events and entertainments are organized, often to raise money for charity.
charity fundraising week at some British universities
british

Examples

During rag week, students organized a fancy dress parade through the city center.
The university’s rag week raised thousands for local charities this year.
Some of the most memorable events on campus happen during rag week.

Etymology

'Rag' in this context dates from the late 19th century, originally referring to student activities or pranks.
