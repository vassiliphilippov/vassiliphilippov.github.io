white gold

1. NOUN

/ waɪt ɡoʊld / (AmE), / waɪt ɡəʊld / (BrE)
a pale, silvery alloy of gold mixed with platinum, palladium, or nickel, often used in jewellery
pale gold alloy for jewellery

Examples

She wore a beautiful ring made of white gold set with diamonds.
Many couples prefer white gold wedding bands for their modern appearance.
This necklace combines white gold and rose gold for a unique effect.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: yellow gold - traditional alloy of gold with a yellow hue
