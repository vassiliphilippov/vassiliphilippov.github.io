Vistula River

1. PROPN

/ ˈvɪstjələ ˈrɪvər / (AmE), / ˈvɪstjʊlə ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
the longest river in Poland, flowing northward into the Baltic Sea
main river of Poland

Examples

The Vistula River passes through several major Polish cities, including Warsaw and Kraków.
A boat tour on the Vistula River offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside.
Many historical battles were fought near the Vistula River during World War II.

Etymology

From Polish Wisła, Latin Vistula; the English form follows the Latin name.
