garden rhubarb

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɑrdn ˈruːbɑrb / (AmE), / ˈɡɑːdn ˈruːbɑːb / (BrE)
A plant with thick, edible red or green stalks, often cooked and used in pies, sauces, or preserves.
edible-stalked plant used in cooking

Examples

The tart flavor of garden rhubarb makes it a favorite ingredient in spring desserts.
We planted garden rhubarb in the backyard to use in homemade jams.
Many cooks combine strawberries with garden rhubarb for a delicious pie filling.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rhubarb - the general term for the plant genus Rheum, especially the edible garden variety
