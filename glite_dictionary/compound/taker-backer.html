taker-backer

1. NOUN

/ ˈteɪkər ˈbækər / (AmE), / ˈteɪkə ˈbakə / (BrE)
a person who takes something without giving back, often withdrawing support or failing to reciprocate contributions
person who takes without giving back
informal

Examples

She called her colleague a taker-backer because he always took credit but rarely helped others.
It's frustrating to work with a taker-backer who never does their share of the work.
The team struggled because too many members were taker-backers instead of active participants.

Synset Examples

As a taker-backer, she rarely contributed her own ideas.
He's always been a taker-backer in our projects.
We need more supporters, not just taker-backers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: freeloader - a person who takes advantage of others' generosity without giving anything in return
  • Antonym: giver - a person who gives something freely rather than takes

Etymology

Formed from 'taker' + 'backer', indicating one who takes and pulls back support.
