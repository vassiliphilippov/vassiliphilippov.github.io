epsilon toxin

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛpsɪˌlɑn ˈtɑksɪn / (AmE), / ˈɛpsɪˌlɒn ˈtɒksɪn / (BrE)
a potent bacterial toxin produced by Clostridium perfringens, which can cause severe abdominal cramps and diarrhea after ingestion of contaminated food
a toxin from Clostridium perfringens bacteria
medical

Examples

The presence of epsilon toxin in contaminated food can lead to severe foodborne illness.
Researchers are studying how epsilon toxin damages intestinal cells in humans and animals.
After eating undercooked meat, several people experienced symptoms linked to epsilon toxin exposure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enterotoxin - a toxin produced in or affecting the intestines
