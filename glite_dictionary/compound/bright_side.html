bright side

1. NOUN

/ braɪt saɪd / (AmE), / braɪt saɪd / (BrE)
an aspect of a difficult or unpleasant situation that provides some comfort or hope
encouraging aspect of bad situation

Examples

Even when things go wrong, it's important to look on the bright side.
After losing his job, he tried to find the bright side by spending more time with family.
Despite the rain ruining our plans, the bright side was that we had a cozy day indoors.

Synset Examples

every cloud has a silver lining
look on the bright side of it

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silver lining - a hopeful or positive aspect in a bad situation

2. NOUN

/ braɪt saɪd / (AmE), / braɪt saɪd / (BrE)
the positive or hopeful aspect of a situation, especially one that is otherwise negative or difficult
positive aspect of a situation

Examples

Even though I missed my train, I tried to see the bright side and enjoyed a walk in the park.
There's always a bright side to every problem if you look hard enough.
Despite their loss, the coach encouraged the team to focus on the bright side and learn from the experience.

Synset Examples

Let's focus on the bright side of this challenge.
She found the bright side in every tough situation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silver lining - a hopeful or positive aspect in a bad situation
