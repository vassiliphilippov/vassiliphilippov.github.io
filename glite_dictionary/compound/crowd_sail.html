to crowd sail

1. VERB

/ kraʊd seɪl / (AmE), / kraʊd seɪl / (BrE)
To set all available sails to catch as much wind as possible, usually to maximize a vessel's speed.
set all sails on a vessel

Examples

With the storm approaching, the captain decided not to crowd sail and kept some canvas down.
They hoped to win the race by choosing to crowd sail during the final stretch.
To escape their pursuers, the crew had to crowd sail despite the risk of damage.

Synset Examples

The sailors decided to crowd sail to maximize speed.
Crowd sail helped them catch the wind perfectly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: make full sail - to set all sails available, maximizing sail area
