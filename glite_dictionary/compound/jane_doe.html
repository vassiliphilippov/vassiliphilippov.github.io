Jane Doe

1. NOUN

/ dʒeɪn doʊ / (AmE), / dʒeɪn dəʊ / (BrE)
a name used for an unknown or unidentified woman, especially one involved in legal proceedings
unidentified or anonymous woman
legal

Examples

The police could not identify the victim, so they listed her as Jane Doe.
A Jane Doe filed a lawsuit to protect her identity in court.
Hospitals often use Jane Doe for unidentified female patients.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: John Doe - an unknown or fictitious man who is a party to legal proceedings

Etymology

Patterned after the generic use of 'John Doe' for unidentified men; 'Jane' as the feminine equivalent.

2. PROPN

/ dʒeɪn doʊ / (AmE), / dʒeɪn dəʊ / (BrE)
a name used to refer to an unidentified or anonymous woman, especially in legal contexts
anonymous woman (placeholder name)

Examples

The victim was identified only as Jane Doe in the court documents.
The hospital admitted a Jane Doe late last night after a car accident.
Police are asking for help in identifying the woman known as Jane Doe.

Synset Examples

In this case, Jane Doe represents all unidentified persons.
The report mentioned a Jane Doe in the investigation.
They used Jane Doe as a placeholder in the document.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: John Doe - name used for an unidentified or anonymous man

Etymology

Patterned after 'John Doe'; 'Jane' as the feminine equivalent.
