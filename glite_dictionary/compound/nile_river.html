Nile River

1. PROPN

/ ˈnaɪl ˌrɪvər / (AmE), / ˈnaɪl ˌrɪvə / (BrE)
the longest river in the world, flowing north through eastern Africa into the Mediterranean Sea
the longest river in the world, in Africa

Examples

Ancient Egyptian civilization flourished along the Nile River.
Tourists often take cruises down the Nile River to see its historic sites.
The Nile River is famous for its annual flooding, which enriched the soil.

Etymology

From Latin 'Nilus', from Greek 'Neilos', from ancient Egyptian 'iteru' (river).
