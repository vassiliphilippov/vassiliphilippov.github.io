Arctic Ocean

1. PROPN

/ ˈɑːrktɪk ˈoʊʃən / (AmE), / ˈɑːktɪk ˈəʊʃən / (BrE)
the ocean surrounding the North Pole, characterized by its ice-covered waters and extreme northern location
ocean near the North Pole

Examples

The Arctic Ocean is shrinking as global temperatures continue to rise each year.
Several countries have research stations near or on the ice of the Arctic Ocean.
Polar bears are native to the icy region surrounding the Arctic Ocean.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Atlantic Ocean - large ocean between the Americas and Europe/Africa
  • Antonym: Antarctic Ocean - ocean surrounding Antarctica

Etymology

From Latin 'arcticus' (northern, of the bear constellation) and 'oceanus' (ocean).
