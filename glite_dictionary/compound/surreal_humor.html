surreal humor

1. NOUN

/ səˈriəl ˈhjumər / (AmE), / səˈriːəl ˈhjuːmə / (BrE)
A form of humour characterized by bizarre, illogical events and situations that violate normal expectations of reasoning.
humour using bizarre, illogical elements

Examples

Classic examples of surreal humor include sketches by Monty Python that involve talking animals and nonsensical scenarios.
The play used surreal humor to highlight the unpredictability of life and the absurdity of routine.
Her taste in comedy leans toward surreal humor rather than traditional jokes or slapstick.

Synset Examples

Surreal humour is also found frequently in avant-garde theatre such as Waiting for Godot and Rosencrantz & Guildenstern Are Dead.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: absurd humor - humor based on absurdity and nonsense
  • Degree Lower: black humor - humor with dark or morbid themes

Etymology

From "surreal," meaning beyond reality (from French surréaliste, influenced by Latin super 'above' + real), and "humor," from Latin humor 'moisture, fluid' (later 'frame of mind').
