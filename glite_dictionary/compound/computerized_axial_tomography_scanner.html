computerized axial tomography scanner

1. NOUN

/ kəmˌpjuːtəraɪzd ˌæksiəl təˌmɑːɡrəfi ˈskænər / (AmE), / kəmˌpjuːtəraɪzd ˌæksiəl təˈmɒɡrəfi ˈskænə / (BrE)
a machine that creates detailed three-dimensional images of the inside of the body by combining X-ray images taken from different angles
medical imaging machine (CAT scanner)
medical

Examples

The doctor recommended a computerized axial tomography scanner to get a clearer picture of the patient's head injury.
Newer hospitals are often equipped with the latest computerized axial tomography scanner technology for advanced diagnostics.
The technician explained how the computerized axial tomography scanner works before beginning the procedure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: CT scanner - alternative term for computerized axial tomography scanner

Etymology

From computerized + axial + Greek tomos (slice) + -graphy (writing); refers to the imaging technique first developed in the 1970s.
