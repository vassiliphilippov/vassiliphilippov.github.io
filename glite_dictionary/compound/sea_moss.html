sea moss

1. NOUN

/ si mɑs / (AmE), / siː mɒs / (BrE)
a sessile aquatic animal that forms moss-like colonies of small polyps, each with a curved or circular ridge bearing tentacles, typically attached to stones or seaweed and reproducing by budding
aquatic animal forming moss-like colonies

Examples

Divers discovered a colony of sea moss attached to the rocky seabed.
The delicate structure of sea moss colonies resembles underwater gardens.
Marine biologists study how sea moss reproduces through budding and forms new colonies.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsi ˌmɔs / (AmE), / ˈsiː ˌmɒs / (BrE)
any of various red algae, especially those with rose to purple fronds, such as dulse or carrageen, often used as food or in traditional remedies.
a type of edible red algae

Examples

Many people add sea moss to their smoothies for its nutrient content.
In coastal regions, sea moss is harvested and dried for culinary use.
Traditional Irish recipes often include sea moss as a thickening agent.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carrageen - an edible red seaweed used to make carrageenan
  • Synonym: dulse - an edible red seaweed with leathery fronds
