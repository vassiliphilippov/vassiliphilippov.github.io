sweet cicely

1. NOUN

/ swit ˈsɪsəli / (AmE), / swiːt ˈsɪsɪli / (BrE)
a European perennial herb (Myrrhis odorata) with soft, fern-like leaves and white flowers, often grown for its aromatic qualities
a European aromatic herb with fern-like leaves

Examples

The leaves of sweet cicely are often used to sweeten fruit dishes naturally.
Gardeners value sweet cicely for its pleasant aniseed scent and delicate foliage.
In the spring, sweet cicely produces clusters of small white flowers that attract pollinators.

Etymology

From 'sweet' referring to its aroma, and 'cicely,' a diminutive of the given name Cecilia, possibly referencing the plant's delicate nature.

3. NOUN

/ swit ˈsɪsəli / (AmE), / swiːt ˈsɪsɪli / (BrE)
a herb with fern-like leaves and sweet-tasting seeds used as a garnish, flavoring, and in confectionery
herb with sweet seeds, used as garnish or flavoring

Examples

Finely chopped sweet cicely gives salads a mild aniseed flavor.
The chef sprinkled sweet cicely leaves over the roasted vegetables as a garnish.
You can use dried sweet cicely seeds to flavor homemade liqueurs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: myrrh - aromatic gum resin from trees, also called sweet cicely in some regions
