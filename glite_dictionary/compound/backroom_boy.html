backroom boy

1. NOUN

/ ˈbækˌruːm bɔɪ / (AmE), / ˈbækˌruːm bɔɪ / (BrE)
a man who works as an expert adviser or technical specialist behind the scenes, often making important decisions without being publicly recognized or holding official status
male expert adviser working behind the scenes

Examples

Though rarely seen in public, the backroom boy played a crucial role in every campaign victory.
Many of the company's innovations were the work of its backroom boys rather than its executives.
He found greater satisfaction as a backroom boy than being in the spotlight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: behind the scenes - working out of public view
  • Antonym: frontman - a person who is the visible leader or representative of a group

Etymology

From 'backroom' (a room at the back of a building, often where secret or less visible work takes place) and 'boy' as an informal term for a man; first used in mid-20th century Britain.
