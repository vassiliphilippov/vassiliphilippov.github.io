posterior meningeal artery

1. NOUN

/ pəˈstɪriɚ məˌnɪndʒiəl ˈɑrtəri / (AmE), / pɒˈstɪəriə mɛˈnɪndʒɪəl ˈɑːtəri / (BrE)
a branch of the ascending pharyngeal artery that supplies blood to the dura mater of the posterior cranial fossa
artery supplying part of the brain's covering
medical

Examples

A rupture of the posterior meningeal artery can cause serious bleeding in the cranial cavity.
During surgery, the posterior meningeal artery must be carefully preserved to prevent complications.
An abnormal enlargement of the posterior meningeal artery was detected in the patient’s MRI scan.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: meningeal artery - an artery supplying the meninges of the brain
  • Degree Higher: ascending pharyngeal artery - an artery that supplies parts of the pharynx and adjacent structures

Etymology

From Latin: 'posterior' (behind, later) + 'meninx' (membrane) + 'arteria' (artery).
