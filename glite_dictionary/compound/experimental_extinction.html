experimental extinction

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˌspɛrəməntəl ɪkˈstɪŋkʃən / (AmE), / ɪkˌspɛrɪˈmɛntl ɪkˈstɪŋkʃən / (BrE)
In psychology, the process in conditioning where the reinforcement is removed, causing the conditioned response to weaken or disappear.
psychological process in conditioning

Examples

The researchers observed experimental extinction when the animals stopped responding once the food was no longer given.
After repeated trials without a reward, experimental extinction led to a significant drop in the conditioned behavior.
Therapists sometimes use techniques similar to experimental extinction to modify unwanted habits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: extinction - disappearance of a conditioned response
