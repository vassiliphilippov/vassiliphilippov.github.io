seborrheic dermatitis

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛbəˈriɪk dɜːrməˈtaɪtəs / (AmE), / ˌsɛb.əˈriː.ɪk ˌdɜː.məˈtaɪ.tɪs / (BrE)
a chronic skin disorder causing scaly, greasy patches, especially on the scalp, face, or other areas of the skin
chronic skin disorder with scaly, greasy patches
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed her with seborrheic dermatitis after examining the scaly patches on her scalp.
Many people with seborrheic dermatitis experience flaking and redness around the nose and eyebrows.
Medicated shampoos can help manage seborrheic dermatitis symptoms effectively.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eczema - a group of medical conditions that cause the skin to become inflamed or irritated

Etymology

From Greek 'seborrhea' (excessive sebum flow) and 'dermatitis' (skin inflammation) via medical Latin.
