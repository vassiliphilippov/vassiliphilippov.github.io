writer's name

1. NOUN

/ ˈraɪtərz neɪm / (AmE), / ˈraɪtəz neɪm / (BrE)
the name that appears as the author of a written work, especially as shown in a by-line
name of the author on a by-line

Examples

The writer's name was printed in bold at the top of the article.
She used a pseudonym instead of her real writer's name to keep her identity private.
Readers often judge a story by the writer's name before even reading the first sentence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: byline - a line in a newspaper or magazine naming the writer of an article
  • Synonym: pen name - a name used by writers instead of their real name
