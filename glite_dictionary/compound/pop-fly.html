pop-fly

1. NOUN

/ pɑp flaɪ / (AmE), / pɒp flaɪ / (BrE)
(in baseball) a ball that is hit high into the air but does not travel far from home plate, making it easy to catch.
short high fly ball in baseball

Examples

The batter hit a pop-fly to shortstop, resulting in an easy out.
With two outs, a pop-fly ended the inning for the visiting team.
The outfielder tracked the pop-fly and caught it without difficulty.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fly ball - a baseball that is hit high into the air
  • Antonym: grounder - a baseball hit that bounces along the ground

Etymology

From 'pop' (as in 'pop up', meaning to rise suddenly) + 'fly' (a ball hit high into the air).
