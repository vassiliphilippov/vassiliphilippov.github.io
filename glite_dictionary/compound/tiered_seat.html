tiered seat

1. NOUN

/ tɪrd sit / (AmE), / tɪəd siːt / (BrE)
a seat in an arrangement where rows are placed at progressively higher levels so spectators in the back can see over those in front
a seat in progressively raised rows

Examples

We chose tiered seats in the auditorium to get a better view of the stage.
The stadium's tiered seats ensure everyone can see the field clearly.
In modern theaters, tiered seats have become standard to enhance the audience experience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bleacher - an outdoor bench seat arranged in tiers, usually at sports venues
  • Synonym: grandstand - a roofed spectator stand with tiered seating, often at a stadium or racetrack
