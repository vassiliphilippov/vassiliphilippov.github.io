family Coprinaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli koʊˌprɪˈneɪsiːˌiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli kɒˌprɪˈneɪsiːˌiː / (BrE)
a family of fungi, formerly including the genus Coprinus, in some taxonomic classifications
a family of fungi, includes some 'inky cap' mushrooms

Examples

Many inky cap mushrooms were once classified under the family Coprinaceae.
Molecular studies have led to changes in the definition of family Coprinaceae over time.
The family Coprinaceae is no longer recognized in some modern taxonomies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Coprinus - a genus of fungi known as inky caps

Etymology

From Latin 'Coprinus' (a genus of mushrooms) + '-aceae' (a suffix for family names in taxonomy).
