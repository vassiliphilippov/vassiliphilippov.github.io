labia majora

1. NOUN

/ ˌleɪ.bi.ə məˈdʒɔr.ə / (AmE), / ˌleɪ.bi.ə məˈdʒɔː.rə / (BrE)
the two outer rounded folds of skin on either side of the vulva, forming part of the external female genitalia
outer folds of the vulva
medical

Examples

During the examination, the doctor observed that the labia majora appeared healthy and normal.
The labia majora protect the more delicate structures of the vulva.
Some individuals may notice natural differences in the size or shape of their labia majora.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: labia minora - the two inner folds of the vulva
  • Synonym: vulva - the external female genital organs

Etymology

From Latin 'labia' meaning 'lips' and 'majora' meaning 'greater'.
