surreal humour

1. NOUN

/ səˈriəl ˈhjuːmɚ / (AmE), / səˈrɪəl ˈhjuːmə / (BrE)
a type of comedy that relies on bizarre, illogical or absurd situations and events, often disregarding conventional logic
absurd, illogical type of comedy

Examples

The film relied heavily on surreal humour to create a sense of dreamlike absurdity.
Many sketches on that TV show are famous for their surreal humour and unpredictable punchlines.
Audiences were divided over the play’s surreal humour, with some finding it hilarious and others perplexing.

Synset Examples

Surreal humour is also found frequently in avant-garde theatre such as Waiting for Godot and Rosencrantz & Guildenstern Are Dead.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: absurd humour - comedy based on ridiculous or nonsensical situations
  • Degree Lower: slapstick - comedy characterized by physical humour and visual gags

Etymology

From 'surreal' (from French 'surréaliste', beyond real) + 'humour' (from Old French 'humor', fluid/temper).
