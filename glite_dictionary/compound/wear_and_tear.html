wear and tear

1. NOUN

/ wɛr ənd tɛr / (AmE), / wɛə ənd teə / (BrE)
the gradual damage, diminution in value, or deterioration of something as a result of ordinary use or aging
gradual damage or value loss from normal use

Examples

Because of regular wear and tear, the car lost much of its original value.
The landlord is responsible for repairs beyond normal wear and tear.
Furniture in a busy office often shows signs of wear and tear after just a few years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: depreciation - the reduction in the value of an asset over time
