genus Diomedea

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs daɪ.əˈmiː.di.ə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs daɪ.əˈmiː.di.ə / (BrE)
A genus of large seabirds in the family Diomedeidae, commonly known as the great albatrosses.
genus of large albatrosses

Examples

The genus Diomedea includes some of the world’s largest flying birds, such as the wandering albatross.
Researchers studied breeding habits unique to the genus Diomedea in the Southern Ocean.
Most species within the genus Diomedea are characterized by long wingspans and excellent gliding ability.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Diomedeidae - family of albatrosses

Etymology

From New Latin, after Diomedea (named after Diomedes, a hero in Greek mythology, whose companions were supposedly turned into birds).
