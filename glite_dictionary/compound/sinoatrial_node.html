sinoatrial node

1. NOUN

/ saɪnoʊˈeɪtriəl noʊd / (AmE), / saɪnəʊˈeɪtrɪəl nəʊd / (BrE)
a specialized group of cells in the right atrium of the heart that acts as the natural pacemaker, initiating and regulating the heartbeat.
heart's natural pacemaker
medical

Examples

The sinoatrial node is often called the heart's natural pacemaker.
An irregular heartbeat can occur if the sinoatrial node is not functioning properly.
Doctors may implant an artificial pacemaker if the sinoatrial node fails.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: atrioventricular node - a cluster of cells that relays heart impulses from atria to ventricles

Etymology

From Latin 'sino-' (relating to a sinus, especially the sinus venosus of the heart) + 'atrial' (of the atrium) + 'node' (knot or lump).
