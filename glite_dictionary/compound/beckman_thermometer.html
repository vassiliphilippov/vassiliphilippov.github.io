Beckman thermometer

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛkmən θəˈmɑːrmətər / (AmE), / ˈbɛkmən θəˈmɒmɪtə / (BrE)
A mercury thermometer designed to measure very small differences or changes in temperature, often used in scientific experiments.
precise mercury thermometer for small temperature changes

Examples

The researcher used a Beckman thermometer to record minute temperature changes in the solution.
A Beckman thermometer is essential for highly accurate calorimetry experiments in the laboratory.
Because it detects differences as small as 0.01°C, the Beckman thermometer is valued in precise scientific work.

Etymology

Named after American chemist Isaac Beckman (1900–1989), who developed the device.
