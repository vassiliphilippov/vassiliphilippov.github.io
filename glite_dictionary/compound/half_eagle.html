half eagle

1. NOUN

/ hæf ˈiːɡəl / (AmE), / hɑːf ˈiːɡl̩ / (BrE)
a former gold coin of the United States with a face value of five dollars, issued from the late 18th to the early 20th century
former U.S. $5 gold coin

Examples

The rare 1861 half eagle sold for a record price at the auction.
Collectors often seek out half eagles because of their historical significance and gold content.
She inherited a half eagle from her grandfather's coin collection.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: eagle - a former U.S. gold coin worth ten dollars

Etymology

Named after the 'eagle,' a U.S. ten-dollar gold coin; 'half eagle' denotes its value as half of an eagle.
