Sahaptin language

1. NOUN

/ səˈhæptɪn ˌlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / səˈhæptɪn ˌlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
a Native American language of the Penutian family spoken by the Sahaptin peoples of the Pacific Northwest.
a Native American language of the Pacific Northwest

Examples

The Sahaptin language is still spoken by some members of the Yakama Nation today.
Linguists have documented several dialects of the Sahaptin language in Washington and Oregon.
He is helping to preserve the Sahaptin language by recording traditional stories from elders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Nez Perce language - related Penutian language of the Pacific Northwest

Etymology

From 'Sahaptin', the name of the people, likely from local indigenous terms.
