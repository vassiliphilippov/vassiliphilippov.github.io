on hand

1. ADJ

/ ɑn hænd / (AmE), / ɒn hænd / (BrE)
available or present, especially for use or to help when needed
available or present when needed

Examples

The staff will be on hand to assist you throughout the event.
It's important to have extra supplies on hand in case of emergencies.
Do you have the financial records on hand for the meeting?

Synset Examples

there will be a wealth of information on hand from the lawyers
emergency police were on hand in case of trouble
the cash on hand is adequate for current needs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: available - able to be used or obtained; at someone's disposal
  • Antonym: absent - not present at a place or event

2. ADV

/ ɑn hænd / (AmE), / ɒn hænd / (BrE)
(of people or things) available or present and ready for immediate use or service.
available; ready for immediate use

Examples

We always have extra batteries on hand in case the power goes out.
Is there a doctor on hand during the event?
Keep some snacks on hand for unexpected guests.

Synset Examples

They will have a technician on hand to assist.
She had all the documents on hand for the meeting.
I always keep extra supplies on hand for emergencies.
He left the mess on her hands to clean.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: available - able to be used or obtained; at someone's disposal
  • Antonym: absent - not present or available
