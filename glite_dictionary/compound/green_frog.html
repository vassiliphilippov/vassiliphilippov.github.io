green frog

1. NOUN

/ ɡrin frɑɡ / (AmE), / ɡriːn frɒɡ / (BrE)
a common North American frog (Lithobates clamitans) found near marshes and ponds, similar in appearance to the bullfrog
North American frog species

Examples

We spotted a green frog sitting quietly on a lily pad near the edge of the pond.
The call of the green frog can often be heard on warm summer nights in the marsh.
Unlike the bullfrog, the green frog is slightly smaller and less aggressive.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bullfrog - a large North American frog (Lithobates catesbeianus), known for its loud call
