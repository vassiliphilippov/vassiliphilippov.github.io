black music

1. NOUN

/ blæk ˈmjuːzɪk / (AmE), / blæk ˈmjuːzɪk / (BrE)
Music created by African-American musicians, often characterized by distinctive rhythms and melodic lines; includes genres such as blues, jazz, gospel, and hip hop.
music by African-American musicians

Examples

The history of black music in America includes jazz, blues, gospel, and hip hop.
Many modern artists draw inspiration from the rhythms and styles of black music.
This exhibition explores the cultural impact of black music on society.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blues - a genre of music with roots in African-American communities
  • Synonym: jazz - a genre of music characterized by swing and improvisation
  • Synonym: gospel music - a genre of Christian music rooted in black religious experience
