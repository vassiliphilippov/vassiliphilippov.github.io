archosaurian reptile

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːrkoʊˈsɔːriən ˈrɛptaɪl / (AmE), / ˌɑːkəˈsɔːrɪən ˈrɛptaɪl / (BrE)
An extinct group of reptiles that includes dinosaurs, plesiosaurs, pterosaurs, ichthyosaurs, and thecodonts.
extinct group of ancient reptiles

Examples

The fossil record provides valuable information about archosaurian reptiles and their evolution.
Paleontologists discovered a new species of archosaurian reptile that lived millions of years ago.
Many archosaurian reptiles developed unique adaptations that allowed them to dominate prehistoric ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: archosaur - any of a group of diapsid reptiles that includes all extinct dinosaurs and living crocodilians and birds
  • Degree Lower: dinosaur - a member of the extinct group of reptiles dominant in the Mesozoic era

Etymology

From Greek 'archos' (chief) + 'sauros' (lizard), referring to the major clade 'Archosauria'.
