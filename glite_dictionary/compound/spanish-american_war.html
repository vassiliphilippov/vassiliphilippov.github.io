Spanish-American War

1. PROPN

/ ˌspænɪʃ əˈmɛrɪkən wɔr / (AmE), / ˌspænɪʃ əˈmɛrɪkən wɔː / (BrE)
a war fought in 1898 between the United States and Spain, resulting in the U.S. acquiring territories such as Puerto Rico, Guam, and the Philippines.
war between the U.S. and Spain, 1898

Examples

The Spanish-American War marked a turning point in U.S. foreign policy.
Many historians have studied the causes of the Spanish-American War.
The treaty ending the Spanish-American War was signed in Paris.
