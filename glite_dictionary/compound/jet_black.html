jet black

1. NOUN

/ dʒɛt ˈblæk / (AmE), / dʒɛt ˈblak / (BrE)
A deep, intense shade of black, as dark as the mineral jet.
a very deep shade of black

Examples

Her hair was jet black and shimmered in the sunlight.
He wore a jet black suit to the formal event.
The night sky was so jet black that not a single star was visible.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pitch black - completely dark; without any light

Etymology

The term comes from 'jet', a lignite mineral used as a gemstone, valued for its intense black colour.
