common pepper

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmən ˈpɛpər / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən ˈpɛpə / (BrE)
A tropical climbing plant (Piper nigrum), native to South Asia, whose dried berries are used as the spice black pepper.
plant producing black pepper spice

Examples

The berries of the common pepper plant are harvested and dried to produce black pepper.
Farmers in southern India cultivate common pepper as a major export crop.
She added a pinch of ground common pepper to enhance the flavor of her dish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: black pepper - the spice made from the dried berries of Piper nigrum
