neural net

1. NOUN

/ ˈnʊrəl nɛt / (AmE), / ˈnjʊərəl nɛt / (BrE)
A computer architecture modeled after the network of neurons in the brain, composed of interconnected processing units and capable of learning through experience.
artificial network modeled on brain neurons

Examples

Researchers used a neural net to recognize faces in photographs with high accuracy.
Training a neural net requires a large amount of data and computational power.
The neural net gradually improved its predictions as it processed more examples.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: artificial neural network - a computing system inspired by the biological neural networks of animal brains

Etymology

Short for 'neural network', from 'neural' (relating to nerves/neurons) and 'net', an abbreviation of 'network'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈnʊrəl nɛt / (AmE), / ˈnjʊərəl nɛt / (BrE)
A network of interconnected neurons or nerve cells that work together to perform a specific function in the body.
network of interconnected neurons

Examples

The brain's visual cortex can be considered a specialized neural net for processing images.
Scientists studied the neural net responsible for controlling limb movement in frogs.
Damage to a neural net can result in the loss of specific bodily functions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neural network - a network of artificial or biological neurons

Etymology

From 'neural', relating to nerves or the nervous system, and 'net', short for 'network'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
