pudendal block

1. NOUN

/ pjuˈdɛndəl blɑk / (AmE), / pjuˈdɛndəl blɒk / (BrE)
a regional anesthesia in which a local anesthetic is used to numb the pudendal nerves, typically to reduce pain during childbirth
anesthesia for childbirth pain relief
medical

Examples

The doctor administered a pudendal block to help reduce the pain of delivery.
A pudendal block is commonly used when an episiotomy is anticipated during childbirth.
The effectiveness of a pudendal block can vary depending on the precise location of the injection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: regional anesthesia - loss of sensation in a region of the body by blocking nerve supply
  • Degree Higher: epidural - a type of anesthesia used during childbirth, injected into the epidural space

Etymology

From Latin 'pudendus' (the external genitals) and English 'block' (obstruction or interruption).
