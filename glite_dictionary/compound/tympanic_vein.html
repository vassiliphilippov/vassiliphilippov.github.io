tympanic vein

1. NOUN

/ tɪmˈpænɪk veɪn / (AmE), / tɪmˈpænɪk veɪn / (BrE)
A vein draining blood from the tympanic cavity and emptying into the retromandibular vein.
vein from tympanic cavity (ear)
medical

Examples

The tympanic vein plays an important role in draining blood from the middle ear.
Infections in the ear can sometimes affect the tympanic vein and neighboring vessels.
Surgeons must be careful not to damage the tympanic vein during middle ear procedures.

Etymology

From Greek *tympanon* (drum), referring to the eardrum, and Latin *vena* (vein).
