architectural style

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːrkəˈtɛktʃərəl staɪl / (AmE), / ˌɑːkɪˈtɛktʃərəl staɪl / (BrE)
a distinctive design or method of construction that characterizes a particular period, place, or movement in architecture
distinctive form in architecture

Examples

The Gothic architectural style is characterized by pointed arches and ribbed vaults.
Many modern buildings are influenced by the Bauhaus architectural style.
Her research focuses on how function and tradition shape architectural style in urban environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: style - a particular procedure by which something is done, a manner or mode
