life-support system

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪf səˌpɔrt ˌsɪstəm / (AmE), / ˈlaɪf səˌpɔːt ˌsɪstəm / (BrE)
medical equipment that assists or replaces vital bodily functions to keep a patient alive when natural functions are failing
machine aiding vital bodily functions
medical

Examples

After the accident, she was kept alive by a life-support system in the intensive care unit.
Doctors debated whether to continue treatment with the life-support system.
New advancements in life-support systems have improved survival rates for critically ill patients.

Synset Examples

the patient is on life support

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ventilator - a machine that helps a patient breathe

2. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪf səˌpɔrt ˌsɪstəm / (AmE), / ˈlaɪf səˌpɔːt ˌsɪstəm / (BrE)
equipment that provides the necessary conditions for life in environments that would otherwise be fatal, such as space, underwater, or in medical situations.
equipment maintaining life in hostile conditions

Examples

Astronauts depend on their life-support systems to survive in the vacuum of space.
The intensive care unit is equipped with advanced life-support systems for critically ill patients.
Submarines are fitted with life-support systems to sustain the crew during long underwater missions.

Synset Examples

the astronauts relied on their life-support systems

Related Terms

  • Synonym: respirator - device that helps a person breathe
  • Synonym: ventilator - machine for moving air into and out of the lungs
