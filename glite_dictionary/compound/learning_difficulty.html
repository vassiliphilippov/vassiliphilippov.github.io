learning difficulty

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɝːnɪŋ ˈdɪfəkəlti / (AmE), / ˈlɜːnɪŋ ˈdɪfɪkəlti / (BrE)
a condition that makes it harder for someone to acquire knowledge or skills at the typical rate expected for their age
condition affecting ability to learn

Examples

Children with a learning difficulty may need extra support at school.
Her learning difficulty does not affect her intelligence or creativity.
The school offers resources for students with any type of learning difficulty.

Synset Examples

Teachers must adapt to students with learning difficulties.
He has a learning difficulty that makes reading challenging.
Her learning difficulty requires special educational support.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: learning disability - a condition that makes it difficult to learn in a typical manner
