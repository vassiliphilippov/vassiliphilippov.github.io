total eclipse

1. NOUN

/ ˈtoʊt̬l ɪˈklɪps / (AmE), / ˈtəʊtl ɪˈklɪps / (BrE)
an eclipse during which the eclipsed body is completely covered and no part of it is visible from a particular location
eclipse with full obscuration of the body

Examples

The city was plunged into darkness during the total eclipse of the Sun.
A total eclipse is a rare event that attracts scientists and tourists from around the world.
We wore special glasses to safely watch the total eclipse as the Moon completely covered the Sun.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: partial eclipse - an eclipse in which only part of the eclipsed body is obscured
