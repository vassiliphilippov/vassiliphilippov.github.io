serious music

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪriəs ˈmjuːzɪk / (AmE), / ˈsɪəriəs ˈmjuːzɪk / (BrE)
Music belonging to a traditional or classical genre, characterized by formal structure and intended for attentive or critical listening, rather than entertainment or background.
traditional or classical music genre

Examples

He has a deep love for serious music and often attends orchestral concerts.
The radio station features mostly serious music in the evenings, such as symphonies and operas.
Many people find it easier to relax to pop songs than to serious music.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: classical music - traditional Western art music from the classical period or genre
  • Antonym: light music - music intended for entertainment, less serious in tone
