snowplow parent

1. NOUN

/ ˈsnoʊˌplaʊ ˈpɛrənt / (AmE), / ˈsnəʊˌplaʊ ˈpeərənt / (BrE)
a parent who attempts to remove all obstacles or challenges from their child's path, often resulting in the child becoming overly dependent and less resilient
overprotective parent who removes obstacles for child

Examples

Her snowplow parent attitude meant she tried to solve every problem for her son.
Some educators warn that being a snowplow parent can prevent kids from learning resilience.
He grew frustrated with his snowplow parent constantly stepping in to negotiate on his behalf.

Synset Examples

Her snowplow parent approach often backfires.
Being a snowplow parent can hinder a child's growth.
Snowplow parenting restricts children from facing challenges.
Snowplow parenting can lead to dependency issues.
She criticized snowplow parenting for stifling independence.
Snowplow parents tend to overprotect their kids.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: helicopter parent - a parent who pays extremely close attention to a child, often overprotective
  • Antonym: free-range parent - a parent who allows children greater freedom and independence

Etymology

Formed by analogy to 'helicopter parent,' from 'snowplow' (a vehicle that clears snow), referring to 'clearing' obstacles from a child's path.
