present-tense morpheme

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɛzənt tɛns ˈmɔrfiːm / (AmE), / ˈprɛznt tɛns ˈmɔːfiːm / (BrE)
a linguistic morpheme added to a verb to indicate present tense
morpheme marking present tense

Examples

In English, the -s in 'runs' is the third-person singular present-tense morpheme.
Many languages use a specific present-tense morpheme to distinguish habitual actions.
The absence of a present-tense morpheme in some verbs can make tense ambiguous.

Synset Examples

The third-person singular present-tense morpheme is a survival from the time that English was an inflected language with several suffixes to distinguish person and number.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: past-tense morpheme - a morpheme that signifies the past tense of a verb
  • Degree Lower: morpheme - the smallest grammatical unit in a language

Etymology

Formed from 'present tense', indicating the time of action, and 'morpheme', from French 'morphème', modeled after 'phonème'.
