caterpillar track

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæt̬ɚˌpɪlɚ træk / (AmE), / ˈkætəpɪlə trak / (BrE)
a continuous metal or rubber belt fitted around the wheels of a vehicle, allowing it to move easily over rough terrain
belt system for tracked vehicles

Examples

The tank uses a caterpillar track to traverse muddy and rocky ground with ease.
Construction vehicles equipped with a caterpillar track can operate in areas where wheeled vehicles cannot go.
After hours of use, the caterpillar track needed to be thoroughly cleaned and oiled.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: track - a continuous band of treads, as on tanks or bulldozers, allowing movement over rough ground

Etymology

From the Caterpillar Tractor Company, which popularized the design, and 'track' (path or course).
