genus Cicer

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈsɪk.ər / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈsɪk.ə / (BrE)
A group of Asiatic herbs in the pea family, including the chickpea plant.
genus of plants including chickpea

Examples

The genus Cicer is best known for including the chickpea, a staple in many cuisines.
Botanists have identified several wild species within the genus Cicer.
Research into the genus Cicer focuses on improving crop yields for global agriculture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chickpea - an edible round, yellowish seed, or the plant it comes from

Etymology

From Latin 'Cicer', meaning chickpea.
