vestibular gland

1. NOUN

/ vɛˈstɪbjələr ɡlænd / (AmE), / vɛˈstɪbjʊlə ɡlænd / (BrE)
either of two small glands on each side of the vaginal opening that secrete fluid to lubricate the vagina during sexual activity
gland near vaginal opening; produces lubrication
medical

Examples

During a pelvic examination, the doctor checked the vestibular gland for any signs of swelling.
An infection in the vestibular gland can cause discomfort and requires prompt medical attention.
The secretions from the vestibular gland help maintain vaginal moisture during intercourse.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Bartholin's gland - the more common name for the vestibular gland

Etymology

From Latin 'vestibulum' (entrance hall) + gland, referring to its location near the entrance of the vagina.
