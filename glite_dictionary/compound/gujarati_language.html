Gujarati language

1. NOUN

/ ɡʊˌdʒɑːˈrɑːti ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ɡʊˌdʒɑːˈrɑːti ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Indic language spoken primarily by people in Gujarat, a state in western India.
Indic language of Gujarat, India

Examples

Many families in Mumbai use Gujarati language at home as well as Hindi and English.
She is fluent in the Gujarati language, having grown up in Ahmedabad.
The Gujarati language shares roots with other Indo-Aryan languages but has its unique script and vocabulary.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hindi language - the Indo-Aryan language spoken mainly in northern India
  • Synonym: Marathi language - an Indo-Aryan language spoken in Maharashtra, India
  • Synonym: language - a system of spoken or written communication

Etymology

From Gujarati 'ગુજરાતી' (Gujarati), the name of the language and the people.
