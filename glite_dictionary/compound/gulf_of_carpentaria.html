Gulf of Carpentaria

1. PROPN

/ ˈɡʌlf əv ˌkɑːr.pənˈtɛəri.ə / (AmE), / ˈɡʌlf əv ˌkɑː.pənˈteə.ri.ə / (BrE)
a large, shallow gulf of the Arafura Sea situated in northern Australia
wide gulf in northern Australia

Examples

The Gulf of Carpentaria has extensive tidal flats and mangrove forests along its coastline.
During the wet season, many rivers flow into the Gulf of Carpentaria, causing seasonal flooding.
Scientists study marine life diversity in the Gulf of Carpentaria due to its rich ecosystem.

Etymology

Named after Pieter de Carpentier, a Dutch colonial administrator.
