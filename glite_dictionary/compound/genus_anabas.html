genus Anabas

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs əˈneɪbəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈanəbəs / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Anabantidae, comprising small freshwater fish that resemble perch.
type genus of Anabantidae; perch-like fish

Examples

The genus Anabas is well known for species capable of surviving out of water for short periods.
Biologists studying the genus Anabas have documented their unique ability to move across land.
Fish belonging to the genus Anabas are often called climbing perch due to their remarkable behavior.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Anabas - a genus of climbing perches in Anabantidae

Etymology

From New Latin 'Anabas', based on Greek 'anabainein' (to walk up), referring to the fish's ability to move on land.
