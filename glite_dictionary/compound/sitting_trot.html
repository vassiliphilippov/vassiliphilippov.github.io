sitting trot

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪtɪŋ trɑt / (AmE), / ˈsɪtɪŋ trɒt / (BrE)
A gait in horse riding in which the rider remains seated in the saddle while the horse trots, without rising to the trot's rhythm.
riding a trot without rising from the saddle

Examples

During the lesson, the instructor asked everyone to switch to sitting trot for better balance.
It takes practice to stay comfortable in sitting trot during a dressage test.
He struggled with the sitting trot on his energetic young horse.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: rising trot - A gait where the rider rises and sits in rhythm with the trot
