east wind

1. NOUN

/ ist wɪnd / (AmE), / iːst wɪnd / (BrE)
A wind that blows from the east.
wind blowing from the east

Examples

An east wind blew across the fields, carrying a chill through the early morning air.
Sailors dreaded the arrival of the east wind during winter storms.
The old house creaked as the strong east wind battered its walls.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: west wind - wind that blows from the west
  • Degree Lower: north wind - wind that blows from the north
  • Degree Higher: south wind - wind that blows from the south
