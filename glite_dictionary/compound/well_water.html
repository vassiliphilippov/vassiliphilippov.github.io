well water

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɛl ˌwɑt̬ɚ / (AmE), / ˈwɛl ˌwɔːtə / (BrE)
Water obtained from an underground source, typically extracted through a well.
water from a well

Examples

Many rural homes rely on well water for their daily needs.
The taste of well water can vary depending on the minerals in the ground.
After the drought, their well water levels dropped significantly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spring water - water from a natural underground spring
