to ang kong siao

1. VERB

/ ɑŋ kɔŋ siaʊ / (AmE), / ɑŋ kɒŋ siaʊ / (BrE)
to become extremely upset, agitated, or to act irrationally or crazily, often in response to bad news or frustration.
act irrationally; become extremely upset
informal

Examples

She went ang kong siao when she heard the news.
Don't go ang kong siao; it's just a game.
He gets ang kong siao over the smallest things.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: freak out - to lose control or become very upset or excited
  • Synonym: to go ballistic - to become extremely angry or irrational

Etymology

From Hokkien and/or Teochew, common in Singaporean English ('ang kong' meaning 'crazy', 'siao' meaning 'mad/crazy').
