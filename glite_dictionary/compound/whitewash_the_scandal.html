to whitewash the scandal

1. VERB

/ ˈwaɪtˌwɑʃ ðə ˈskændl̩ / (AmE), / ˈwaɪtwɒʃ ðə ˈskændl / (BrE)
to try to conceal or cover up a scandal, wrongdoing, or embarrassing incident, often by presenting misleading information
conceal a scandal or wrongdoing

Examples

The company tried to whitewash the scandal by releasing carefully worded statements to the press.
She accused the committee of attempting to whitewash the scandal rather than address the facts.
There was public outrage when they learned officials had tried to whitewash the scandal instead of accepting responsibility.

Synset Examples

He attempted to whitewash the scandal with a public apology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to cover up - to hide the truth about something, especially a crime or mistake
  • Antonym: expose the scandal - to reveal a scandal or wrongdoing to the public

Etymology

Originally from the use of 'whitewash' meaning to literally paint a surface white to cover imperfections; figuratively used to mean concealing faults or misdeeds.
