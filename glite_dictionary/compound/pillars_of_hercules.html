Pillars of Hercules

1. PROPN

/ ˈpɪlərz əv hɜrˈkjuliːz / (AmE), / ˈpɪləs əv ˈhɜːkjʊliːz / (BrE)
the two promontories on either side of the Strait of Gibraltar, traditionally identified as the Rock of Gibraltar in Europe and Jebel Musa in Africa, which according to legend were formed by Hercules
mythical promontories at the Strait of Gibraltar

Examples

Ancient mariners feared sailing beyond the Pillars of Hercules into the unknown Atlantic Ocean.
According to Greek mythology, the Pillars of Hercules were created when Hercules split a mountain in two.
The Rock of Gibraltar is often considered one of the Pillars of Hercules.

Etymology

From Latin Columnae Herculis, referring to the legendary feats of Hercules in Greek and Roman mythology.
