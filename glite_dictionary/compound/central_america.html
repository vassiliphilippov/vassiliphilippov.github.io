Central America

1. PROPN

/ ˌsɛntrəl əˈmɛrɪkə / (AmE), / ˌsɛntrəl əˈmɛrɪkə / (BrE)
The region consisting of countries between Mexico and South America, including Guatemala, Belize, Honduras, El Salvador, Nicaragua, Costa Rica, and Panama.
region of countries between Mexico and South America

Examples

Many ancient civilizations flourished in Central America long before European explorers arrived.
Climate change has had a significant impact on agriculture in Central America.
Several species of birds native to Central America migrate north for the summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Latin America - regions of the Americas where Romance languages are spoken
  • Degree Higher: South America - continent south of Central America

2. PROPN

/ ˌsɛntrəl əˈmɛrɪkə / (AmE), / ˌsɛntrəl əˈmɛrɪkə / (BrE)
the narrow region of land connecting North America and South America, extending from the southern border of Mexico to the northern border of Colombia; includes countries such as Guatemala, Honduras, Nicaragua, and Panama
region joining North and South America

Examples

Many migratory birds stop in Central America during their seasonal journeys between North and South America.
She spent her summer backpacking through several countries in Central America.
Earthquakes occasionally occur in Central America because of its complex geological structure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Latin America - regions of the Americas where Romance languages are spoken
  • Antonym: North America - continent north of Central America
  • Antonym: South America - continent south of Central America
