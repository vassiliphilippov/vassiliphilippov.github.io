double-blind experiment

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʌbəlˈblaɪnd ɪkˈspɛrəmənt / (AmE), / ˌdʌbəlˈblaɪnd ɪkˈspɛrɪmənt / (BrE)
an experiment in which neither the participants nor the experimenters know which group the participants belong to, used to prevent bias
experiment where participants and experimenters are unaware of group assignments

Examples

The clinical trial was a double-blind experiment to ensure unbiased results.
Researchers conducted a double-blind experiment to test the effectiveness of the new drug.
In a double-blind experiment, neither the patients nor the doctors knew who received the placebo.

Synset Examples

a double-blind procedure is used to guard against both experimenter bias and placebo effects

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: single-blind experiment - an experiment where only the subjects are unaware of critical information
