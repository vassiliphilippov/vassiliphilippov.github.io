family Apodidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ˌæpəˈdaɪdi / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli ˌapəˈdɪːdiː / (BrE)
a family of birds comprising the swifts, characterized by their slender bodies and long, narrow wings; formerly included in the order Coraciiformes.
taxonomic family of swifts (birds)

Examples

The family Apodidae includes over a hundred species of fast-flying birds known as swifts.
Many ornithologists study the migratory patterns within the family Apodidae.
Some members of the family Apodidae are famous for spending most of their lives in flight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swifts - small, fast-flying birds known for their aerial agility

Etymology

From New Latin Apodidae, based on Greek 'apous' (without feet), referencing the birds' small, weak legs.
