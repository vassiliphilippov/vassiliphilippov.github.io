starting gate

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɑrtɪŋ ɡeɪt / (AmE), / ˈstɑːtɪŋ ɡeɪt / (BrE)
a movable barrier at the starting line of a racecourse that is raised or removed to start the race
movable barrier at a race's start

Examples

The horses waited nervously behind the starting gate before the race began.
As soon as the starting gate opened, the greyhound shot forward ahead of the others.
Officials checked the starting gate several times to ensure it would open properly.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: finish line - the line marking the end of a race
