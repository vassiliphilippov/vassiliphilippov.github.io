plumb level

1. NOUN

/ plʌm ˈlɛvəl / (AmE), / plʌm ˈlɛvəl / (BrE)
a tool consisting of a level with an attached plumb line at right angles, used by carpenters to check vertical and horizontal alignment
carpenter's leveling tool with plumb line

Examples

The carpenter used a plumb level to ensure the wall was perfectly vertical.
Before securing the frame, she double-checked its position with a plumb level.
A good plumb level is essential for precise construction work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spirit level - a tool for determining horizontal levels using a bubble in liquid

Etymology

From 'plumb', referring to a lead weight used to establish a vertical line, and 'level', meaning an instrument to determine a horizontal plane.
