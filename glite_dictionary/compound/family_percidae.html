family Percidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli pərˈsaɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli pəˈsɪːdiː / (BrE)
A family of active freshwater fishes that includes the true perches and pike perches.
taxonomic family of perches and pike perches

Examples

The family Percidae comprises species commonly found in rivers and lakes across the Northern Hemisphere.
Members of the family Percidae are sought after by anglers for their fighting ability.
The characteristics of the family Percidae make them important in freshwater ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perch - a type of freshwater fish in the family Percidae
  • Synonym: pike perch - another freshwater fish in the family Percidae

Etymology

From New Latin 'Percidae', based on Latin 'perca' (perch), with the family suffix '-idae' used in zoological nomenclature.
