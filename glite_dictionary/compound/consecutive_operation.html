consecutive operation

1. NOUN

/ kənˈsɛkjəˌtɪv ˌɑpəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / kənˈsɛkjʊtɪv ˌɒpəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the execution of operations in sequence, where each operation follows immediately after the previous one without overlap
execution of operations one after another

Examples

The system is designed to perform each consecutive operation reliably without user intervention.
During the experiment, the robot executed a series of consecutive operations to assemble the device.
To minimize errors, the tasks must follow a strict consecutive operation order.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: simultaneous operation - execution of operations at the same time
