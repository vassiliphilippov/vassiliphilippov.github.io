divinyl ether

1. NOUN

/ daɪˈvaɪnɪl ˈiːθər / (AmE), / daɪˈvɪnɪl ˈiːθə / (BrE)
a colorless, volatile, highly flammable liquid formerly used as an inhalation anesthetic
volatile liquid; former inhalation anesthetic
medical

Examples

The anesthesiologist administered divinyl ether to induce unconsciousness before the operation.
Due to its flammability, divinyl ether is no longer commonly used in modern medicine.
Researchers studied the effects of divinyl ether as an alternative to other anesthesia agents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ether - a class of organic compounds; also a common anesthetic

Etymology

From 'di-' (two) + 'vinyl' (ethenyl group) + 'ether' (organic compound class); reflects its chemical structure.
