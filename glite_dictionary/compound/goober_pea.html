goober pea

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡuːbər piː / (AmE), / ˈɡuːbə piː / (BrE)
a pod of the peanut plant, typically containing two edible seeds; used especially in American English
peanut pod, especially in US English
american

Examples

My grandmother used to roast goober peas over the open fire for a snack.
The children gathered goober peas fresh from the field after the harvest.
In the South, songs about goober peas are still remembered from Civil War days.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peanut - the edible seed of the peanut plant
  • Synonym: groundnut - British term for peanut
  • Synonym: monkey nut - British term for peanut pod in its shell

Etymology

From African languages (Kikongo and Kimbundu 'nguba', meaning peanut), adopted into American English.
