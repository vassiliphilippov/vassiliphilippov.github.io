engagement ring

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈɡeɪdʒmənt rɪŋ / (AmE), / ɪnˈɡeɪdʒmənt rɪŋ / (BrE)
a ring, typically set with a precious stone, given by one person to another when they become engaged to marry
ring given to signify engagement

Examples

She admired the sparkling diamond engagement ring on her finger.
He surprised her with an engagement ring during their vacation in Paris.
Their families celebrated after she accepted his gift of an engagement ring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wedding ring - a ring given or worn at a marriage ceremony

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈɡeɪdʒmənt rɪŋ / (AmE), / ɪnˈɡeɪdʒmənt rɪŋ / (BrE)
A ring, typically set with a gemstone, given by one person to another to signify a marriage proposal and engagement to be married.
ring given for marriage proposal

Examples

She showed her friends the stunning engagement ring she had just received.
He searched for weeks before choosing the perfect engagement ring for his fiancée.
The jeweler offered advice on how to select a diamond for an engagement ring.

Synset Examples

He proposed with a beautiful engagement ring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wedding ring - a ring given during the marriage ceremony as a symbol of marriage

Etymology

From 'engagement' (pledge to marry) + 'ring'. Use dates to 19th century Western customs.
