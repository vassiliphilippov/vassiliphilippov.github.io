Narragansett Bay

1. PROPN

/ ˌnærəˈɡænsɪt beɪ / (AmE), / ˌnærəˈɡænsɪt beɪ / (BrE)
a large, deep inlet of the Atlantic Ocean in Rhode Island, noted for its harbors and islands
deep inlet of the Atlantic Ocean in Rhode Island

Examples

We went sailing on Narragansett Bay during our summer vacation.
The polluted waters of Narragansett Bay have improved greatly over the past decade.
Many small islands dot Narragansett Bay, attracting tourists and boaters alike.

Etymology

Named after the Narragansett people, an Algonquian Native American tribe from the region.
