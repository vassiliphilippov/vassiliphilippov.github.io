vacant lot

1. NOUN

/ ˈveɪkənt lɑt / (AmE), / ˈveɪkənt lɒt / (BrE)
a piece of land in a town or city that has no permanent buildings on it
a piece of land without buildings

Examples

The old house was torn down, leaving a vacant lot in the middle of the block.
Children love to play baseball in the vacant lot behind the store.
Developers are interested in buying the vacant lot for a new apartment building.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: empty lot - a piece of land without any buildings, especially in an urban area
