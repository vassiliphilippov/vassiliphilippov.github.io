acaroid resin

1. NOUN

/ ˈækəˌrɔɪd ˈrɛz.ən / (AmE), / ˈækərɔɪd ˈrɛz.ɪn / (BrE)
an alcohol-soluble resin obtained from certain Australian trees, used in making varnishes and in manufacturing paper
resin from Australian trees used in varnish and paper

Examples

A small amount of acaroid resin was added to the varnish to improve its durability.
Paper manufacturers sometimes use acaroid resin to enhance the strength of their products.
The unique properties of acaroid resin make it valuable in both industrial and artistic applications.

Etymology

From 'acaroid,' a reference to the Xanthorrhoea genus, plus 'resin'.
