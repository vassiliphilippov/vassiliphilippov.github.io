earth-closet

1. NOUN

/ ˈɜrθ ˌklɑzət / (AmE), / ˈɜːθ ˌklɒzɪt / (BrE)
A type of toilet, typically in a small outbuilding, consisting of a bench with holes through which users defecate, where waste is covered with earth instead of being flushed away.
a historical dry toilet using earth to cover waste

Examples

The old farmhouse still had an earth-closet at the back, rather than a modern bathroom.
Many Victorian houses were originally equipped with an earth-closet instead of a flushing toilet.
He studied the mechanics of the earth-closet as part of his research into historical sanitation methods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: latrine - a simple toilet, often in a pit or trench, used especially in camps or rural areas
  • Synonym: outhouse - a small outdoor building used as a toilet, typically with a seat over a pit

Etymology

Formed from 'earth' (soil) + 'closet' (toilet, from Old French 'clos' meaning 'enclosure').
