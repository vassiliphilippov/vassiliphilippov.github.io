arctiid moth

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːrk.ti.ɪd mɔθ / (AmE), / ˈɑːk.tɪ.ɪd mɒθ / (BrE)
a stout-bodied, broad-winged moth typically having conspicuously striped or spotted wings; its larvae are hairy caterpillars
a moth family with striped wings and hairy larvae

Examples

The arctiid moth displays vivid patterns that help warn predators of its toxicity.
Researchers discovered a new species of arctiid moth in the Amazon rainforest.
The larvae of the arctiid moth are covered in dense, bristly hairs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tiger moth - common name for many arctiid moths with bright color patterns

Etymology

From New Latin Arctiidae, the scientific family name, from Greek arktos (bear), referring to the hairy caterpillars.
