tap dance

1. NOUN

/ tæp dæns / (AmE), / tæp dɑːns / (BrE)
A dance in which rhythmic sounds are created by the movements of the feet, usually with special shoes fitted with metal plates.
rhythmic dance using foot taps

Examples

She learned to tap dance as a child and performed at local shows.
The sound of tap dance echoed through the theater during rehearsal.
He practices tap dance every evening to improve his rhythm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tapdancing - the act or art of performing tap dance

2. NOUN

/ tæp dæns / (AmE), / tæp dɑːns / (BrE)
A style of dance characterized by rhythmic tapping sounds made with the feet, typically wearing special shoes with metal plates.
dance with rhythmic tapping sounds

Examples

She impressed the audience with her tap dance routine during the talent show.
The school offers classes in ballet, jazz, and tap dance for children of all ages.
He wore special shoes to create the distinctive sounds of tap dance on the wooden stage.

Synset Examples

She performed a stunning tap dance at the recital.
The children learned tap dance during their dance class.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ballet - a classical dance form with precise steps and gestures
  • Synonym: jazz dance - a lively style of dance with improvisation and energetic movements
