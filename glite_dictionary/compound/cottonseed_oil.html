cottonseed oil

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːtnˌsid ɔɪl / (AmE), / ˈkɒtnˌsiːd ɔɪl / (BrE)
an edible oil extracted from the seeds of the cotton plant, used in cooking and food products
edible oil from cotton seeds

Examples

Many food manufacturers use cottonseed oil as an ingredient in salad dressings and mayonnaise.
The cook chose cottonseed oil for frying because of its mild flavor and high smoke point.
Some people avoid cottonseed oil due to concerns about allergens and refining processes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vegetable oil - edible oil derived from plants, often used in cooking
  • Degree Higher: olive oil - edible oil made from olives, used in cooking and dressings
