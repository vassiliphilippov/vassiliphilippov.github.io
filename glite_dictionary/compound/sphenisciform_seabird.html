sphenisciform seabird

1. NOUN

/ sfɪˈnɪsɪfɔrm ˈsiːbɝd / (AmE), / sfɪˈnɪsɪfɔːm ˈsiːbɜːd / (BrE)
A flightless seabird of the order Sphenisciformes, commonly known as a penguin, found mainly in cold southern oceans.
flightless seabird order, penguin

Examples

The sphenisciform seabird is uniquely adapted to life in the icy waters of the Antarctic.
Researchers studied a colony of sphenisciform seabirds to better understand their breeding habits.
Unlike most birds, the sphenisciform seabird is an excellent swimmer but cannot fly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: penguin - a flightless seabird of the southern hemisphere

Etymology

From modern Latin Sphenisciformes, from Greek sphēniskos (little wedge, referring to a penguin's shape) + -form (shape).
