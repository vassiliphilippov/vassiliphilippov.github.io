ushering in

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌʃərɪŋ ɪn / (AmE), / ˈʌʃərɪŋ ɪn / (BrE)
the act of introducing or beginning something new, especially a significant change or period
the start or introduction of something new

Examples

The collapse of the regime marked the ushering in of a new political order.
Advancements in digital technology led to the ushering in of the information age.
Many historians view this invention as the ushering in of the modern era.

Synset Examples

it signalled the ushering in of a new era

Related Terms

  • Synonym: introduction - the act of bringing something into use or existence for the first time
  • Antonym: end - the point in time at which something concludes

Etymology

From the verb 'usher' (to guide) + 'in' (preposition, indicating introduction).
