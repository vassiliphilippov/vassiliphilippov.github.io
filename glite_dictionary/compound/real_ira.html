Real IRA

1. PROPN

/ riəl aɪ ɑːr eɪ / (AmE), / rɪəl aɪ ɑː r eɪ / (BrE)
a radical Irish republican paramilitary group formed in 1997 by members opposed to the Provisional IRA's ceasefire, responsible for violent attacks in Northern Ireland
dissident Irish republican paramilitary group (since 1997)

Examples

The Real IRA claimed responsibility for several bombings in Northern Ireland.
Several members of the Real IRA were arrested after a failed attack on a police station.
Unlike the mainstream IRA, the Real IRA rejected the peace process.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Provisional IRA - mainstream Irish republican paramilitary group active during the Troubles
  • Synonym: Continuity IRA - another splinter group from the Irish Republican Army

Etymology

Formed from 'Real' (meaning genuine) and 'IRA', the acronym for Irish Republican Army.
