sickle alfalfa

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪkəl ælˈfælfə / (AmE), / ˈsɪkəl ælˈfælfə / (BrE)
A European plant naturalized in North America, with yellow flowers and sickle-shaped seed pods, also called sickle medic.
a plant with yellow flowers and sickle-shaped pods

Examples

The field was full of sickle alfalfa blooming under the summer sun.
Scientists are studying sickle alfalfa for its drought resistance and soil-enriching properties.
Farmers sometimes plant sickle alfalfa as a forage crop for livestock in dry regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sickle medic - another name for sickle alfalfa, Medicago falcata
