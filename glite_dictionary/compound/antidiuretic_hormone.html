antidiuretic hormone

1. NOUN

/ ˌæntidaɪˈjʊrɛtɪk ˈhɔrmoʊn / (AmE), / ˌæntidaɪˈjʊərɛtɪk ˈhɔːməʊn / (BrE)
a hormone produced by the posterior pituitary gland that increases water reabsorption in the kidneys and helps regulate blood pressure
hormone that reduces urine production
medical

Examples

The levels of antidiuretic hormone increase when the body is dehydrated.
Doctors measured the patient's antidiuretic hormone to diagnose the cause of excessive thirst.
A deficiency of antidiuretic hormone can lead to frequent urination and dehydration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vasopressin - another name for antidiuretic hormone

Etymology

From anti- (against) + diuretic (promoting urine production) + hormone; refers to its function of inhibiting urine output.
