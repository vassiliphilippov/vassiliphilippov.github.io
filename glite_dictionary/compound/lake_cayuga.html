Lake Cayuga

1. PROPN

/ keɪˈjuːɡə / (AmE), / keɪˈjuːɡə / (BrE)
a glacial lake in central New York State, the longest of the Finger Lakes
the longest Finger Lake in New York State

Examples

The scenic beauty of Lake Cayuga attracts visitors from all over the region.
Several colleges, including Cornell University, are located near Lake Cayuga.
Boating and fishing are popular activities on Lake Cayuga during the summer months.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Lake Seneca - the largest of the Finger Lakes in New York State

Etymology

Named after the Cayuga people, a Native American tribe of the Iroquois Confederacy.
