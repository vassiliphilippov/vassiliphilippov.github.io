carpet bombing

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːr.pɪt ˌbɑː.mɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkɑː.pɪt ˌbɒm.ɪŋ / (BrE)
The systematic and extensive bombing of an area in order to devastate a large target, often without regard for specific targets within it.
systematic, widespread bombing of a large area

Examples

The city suffered heavy damage as a result of carpet bombing during the war.
Some condemned the use of carpet bombing as indiscriminate and unnecessarily destructive.
Historians still debate the strategic effectiveness of carpet bombing in modern warfare.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: precision bombing - bombing aimed at a specific target

Etymology

From 'carpet' (referring to coverage as thorough as a carpet) + 'bombing.'
