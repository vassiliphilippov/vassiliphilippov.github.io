trial balloon

1. NOUN

/ ˈtraɪ.əl bəˈluːn / (AmE), / ˈtraɪ.əl bəˈluːn / (BrE)
a balloon released to test air currents, often used to gather information before making a decision
balloon released to test air currents

Examples

Before launching the new aircraft, engineers released a trial balloon to study the wind direction.
The weather team sent up a trial balloon early in the morning to gather data on the upper atmosphere.
Years ago, explorers often used a trial balloon before attempting a journey by air.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtraɪəl bəˈluːn / (AmE), / ˈtraɪəl bəˈluːn / (BrE)
an idea or proposal suggested to test the reaction of others, especially the public, before a final decision is made
an attempt to test public opinion

Examples

The government released the policy as a trial balloon to see how the public would react.
His comments about restructuring the company were just a trial balloon to gauge employees' opinions.
Sometimes politicians float a trial balloon before committing to a controversial decision.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: test case - an instance used to test or evaluate something

Etymology

Originally a metaphor from launching a balloon to check wind conditions before sending up a manned flight.

3. NOUN

/ ˈtraɪ.əl bəˈluːn / (AmE), / ˈtraɪ.əl bəˈluːn / (BrE)
an idea or proposal put forward tentatively to observe how it will be received by others
tentative proposal to test reactions

Examples

The company floated a trial balloon to see if the public would accept the new product.
Her announcement about cutting taxes was widely seen as a trial balloon to judge voter response.
Launching a trial balloon can minimize risk by revealing potential objections early.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: feeler - a suggestion or hint put out to test people's reactions
