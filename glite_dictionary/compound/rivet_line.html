rivet line

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɪvət laɪn / (AmE), / ˈrɪvɪt laɪn / (BrE)
a row of rivets placed along a seam to join or reinforce materials, especially in metalworking or construction
row of rivets at a seam

Examples

The welders inspected the rivet line for any signs of weakness or corrosion.
During restoration, several bolts along the rivet line had to be replaced due to fatigue.
A visible crack formed parallel to the rivet line after years of stress on the structure.

Synset Examples

the fuselage cracked along the rivet line

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seam - a line where two pieces of material are joined
