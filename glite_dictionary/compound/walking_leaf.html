walking leaf

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔkɪŋ liːf / (AmE), / ˈwɔːkɪŋ liːf / (BrE)
A tropical fern with long, narrow fronds that root at the tip, allowing the plant to spread as if 'walking.'
fern with fronds that root at the tip

Examples

The walking leaf is remarkable for its fronds, which root at the tips and help the plant spread.
In tropical gardens, the walking leaf can create a lush, spreading ground cover.
Botanists are fascinated by how the walking leaf propagates so effectively.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɔkɪŋ lif / (AmE), / ˈwɔːkɪŋ liːf / (BrE)
A tropical insect that resembles a flattened leaf, found commonly in southern Asia and the East Indies.
tropical insect mimicking a leaf

Examples

The walking leaf is a master of disguise, blending seamlessly among real leaves.
In the wild, a walking leaf can be incredibly difficult to spot due to its camouflage.
Scientists study the walking leaf to learn more about evolutionary adaptations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leaf insect - insect resembling a leaf, of the family Phylliidae
