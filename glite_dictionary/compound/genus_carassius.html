genus Carassius

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒinəs kəˈræsiəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs kəˈrasɪəs / (BrE)
A genus of freshwater fish in the family Cyprinidae, which includes the goldfish and its relatives.
a fish genus including goldfish

Examples

The genus Carassius includes several species commonly kept in home aquariums.
Researchers studied the evolutionary history of the genus Carassius across Europe and Asia.
The goldfish, perhaps the most famous member of the genus Carassius, has been bred for centuries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: goldfish - a small domesticated fish, Carassius auratus

Etymology

From Latin 'genus' (kind) and 'Carassius', a Latinized form of a former German name for carp.
