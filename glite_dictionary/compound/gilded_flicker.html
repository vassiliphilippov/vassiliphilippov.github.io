gilded flicker

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɪldəd ˈflɪkər / (AmE), / ˈɡɪldɪd ˈflɪkə / (BrE)
a large woodpecker native to the southwestern United States, similar to the yellow-shafted flicker but without the red neck.
southwestern U.S. woodpecker species

Examples

The gilded flicker nests in saguaro cacti throughout the Sonoran Desert.
We spotted a gilded flicker perched on a telephone pole during our Arizona hike.
Unlike its eastern relatives, the gilded flicker has no red patch on its neck.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: yellow-shafted flicker - a closely related species of flicker with yellow feather shafts and a red neck patch
  • Synonym: woodpecker - a bird with a strong bill that pecks at wood to find insects or create nesting sites
