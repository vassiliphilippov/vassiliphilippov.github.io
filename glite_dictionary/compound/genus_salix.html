genus Salix

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈseɪ.lɪks / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈsæ.lɪks / (BrE)
the genus of plants comprising the willows, a large and widespread group of shrubs and trees
botanical genus including willows

Examples

Several species within the genus Salix are prized for their ornamental and environmental value.
The genus Salix includes both towering trees and small, slender shrubs.
Scientists are studying the genus Salix for its ability to absorb pollutants from wetlands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: willow - a tree or shrub of the genus Salix, with narrow leaves and flexible branches

Etymology

From Latin *Salix* (willow), used in scientific classification.
