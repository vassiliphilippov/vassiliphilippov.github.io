Civil Rights movement

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɪvəl ˈraɪts ˌmuːvmənt / (AmE), / ˌsɪvəl ˈraɪts ˌmuːvmənt / (BrE)
a mass social and political movement in the United States during the 1950s and 1960s aimed at securing equal rights for Black Americans and ending racial segregation and discrimination
US movement for Black civil rights (1950s–60s)

Examples

Dr. Martin Luther King Jr. played a central role in the Civil Rights movement.
The Civil Rights movement led to major legal reforms in American society.
Many students studied the Civil Rights movement in their history classes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: civil rights - rights protecting individuals' freedom and equality
