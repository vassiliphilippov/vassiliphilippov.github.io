Maine lobster

1. NOUN

/ meɪn ˈlɑːb.stɚ / (AmE), / meɪn ˈlɒb.stə / (BrE)
a large lobster species (Homarus americanus) occurring along the Atlantic coast of North America, especially harvested in Maine, known for its sweet, tender meat
large North Atlantic lobster species

Examples

The restaurant is famous for its fresh Maine lobster dishes.
Every summer, tourists come to the coast to sample Maine lobster.
She ordered a whole Maine lobster at the seafood market.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lobster - a large marine crustacean with a muscular tail and claws, used as food

2. NOUN

/ meɪn ˈlɑbstɚ / (AmE), / meɪn ˈlɒbstə / (BrE)
The edible flesh of the North Atlantic lobster, especially as caught off the coast of Maine, known for its large tender claws.
edible North Atlantic lobster, especially from Maine

Examples

She ordered a Maine lobster for dinner at the seaside restaurant.
The Maine lobster is prized for its sweet, tender meat.
They served fresh Maine lobster at the wedding reception.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lobster - a large marine crustacean with a long body and claws
