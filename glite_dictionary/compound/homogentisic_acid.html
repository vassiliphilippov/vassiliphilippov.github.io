homogentisic acid

1. NOUN

/ ˌhoʊmoʊˌdʒɛnˈtɪsɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / ˌhəʊməʊˌdʒɛnˈtɪsɪk ˈæsɪd / (BrE)
an intermediate acid produced during the metabolism of the amino acids tyrosine and phenylalanine
acid formed during amino acid metabolism
medical

Examples

Alkaptonuria is caused by a buildup of homogentisic acid in the body.
During tyrosine metabolism, homogentisic acid acts as a key intermediate.
Researchers detected high levels of homogentisic acid in the patient's urine sample.

Etymology

From 'homo-' (same) + 'gentisic' (from gentisic acid) + 'acid'.
