water vole

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔtər voʊl / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə vəʊl / (BrE)
a large aquatic vole of western North America (Microtus richardsoni), found in wetland habitats
large aquatic vole native to western North America

Examples

The biologist spotted a water vole near the stream during her field research.
Habitat loss has led to a decline in the water vole population in some regions.
A water vole can often be seen swimming across ponds in the early morning.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɔtər voʊl / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə vəʊl / (BrE)
a large semiaquatic rodent, native to Europe and Asia, typically found near water and resembling a rat; also called a water rat
large aquatic rodent found in Eurasia

Examples

The water vole is often mistaken for a rat due to its appearance and habitat.
Conservationists are working to protect water voles from habitat loss and predators.
We spotted a water vole swimming across the stream during our nature walk.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vole - a small rodent resembling a mouse or rat
