Siamese twin

1. NOUN

/ ˌsaɪəˈmiz ˈtwɪn / (AmE), / ˌsaɪəˈmiːz ˈtwɪn / (BrE)
one of a pair of identical twins whose bodies are joined at birth at some point
conjoined identical twin
medical

Examples

The surgery to separate the Siamese twins was considered a major medical breakthrough.
Doctors explained the unique challenges in treating Siamese twins due to their shared organs.
The parents decided to consult specialists before making decisions about their Siamese twin daughters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conjoined twin - a twin physically joined to its sibling at birth; preferred modern medical term

Etymology

From the famous case of Chang and Eng Bunker, conjoined twins from Siam (now Thailand) born in the 19th century.
