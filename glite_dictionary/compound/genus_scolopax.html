genus Scolopax

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs skəˈloʊ.pæks / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs skəˈləʊ.paks / (BrE)
A genus of birds in the family Scolopacidae, comprising the Old World woodcocks.
genus of Old World woodcocks

Examples

The genus Scolopax includes several species of woodcocks found across Europe and Asia.
Biologists studied the migration patterns of birds in the genus Scolopax.
Members of the genus Scolopax are known for their cryptic plumage and elusive behavior.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: woodcock - a type of bird, member of genus Scolopax
  • Degree Higher: Scolopacidae - the family of wading birds including genus Scolopax

Etymology

From Latin *Scolopax* (woodcock), used in scientific taxonomy.
