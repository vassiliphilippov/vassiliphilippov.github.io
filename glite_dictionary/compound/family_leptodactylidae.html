family Leptodactylidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ˌlɛptəˌdæktɪˈlaɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfamɪli ˌlɛptədaktɪˈlaɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of New World frogs, sometimes considered nearly equivalent to the family Bufonidae in certain classifications.
a family of New World frogs

Examples

The family Leptodactylidae includes many species of frogs native to Central and South America.
Some taxonomists debate whether certain genera should be classified within the family Leptodactylidae.
Members of the family Leptodactylidae are often characterized by their terrestrial breeding habits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Bufonidae - the family of true toads

Etymology

From Neo-Latin Leptodactylidae, from Greek leptos (slender) + dactylos (finger), referring to the frogs' slender toes.
