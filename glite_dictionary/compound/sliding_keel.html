sliding keel

1. NOUN

/ ˈslaɪdɪŋ ˌkil / (AmE), / ˈslaɪdɪŋ ˌkiːl / (BrE)
a retractable fin fitted to the bottom of a sailboat to reduce sideways drift and improve stability
retractable sailboat fin to prevent drift

Examples

The yacht is equipped with a sliding keel to navigate shallow waters safely.
When facing strong crosswinds, the sailor lowered the sliding keel to maintain course.
Early 19th-century boat designs often featured a sliding keel for enhanced stability.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: centerboard - a pivoted lifting keel in the hull of a sailboat
