dawn redwood

1. NOUN

/ dɔn ˈrɛdˌwʊd / (AmE), / dɔːn ˈrɛdˌwʊd / (BrE)
a tall, fast-growing deciduous tree native to China with flat bright-green leaves and small round cones, widely cultivated as an ornamental and once known only from fossils
Chinese deciduous tree; ornamental; once thought extinct

Examples

A dawn redwood can grow over 100 feet tall in cultivation under ideal conditions.
Many gardens feature the dawn redwood for its unique foliage and fast growth.
The dawn redwood was considered extinct until living specimens were discovered in China in the 1940s.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: redwood - very tall evergreen trees native to western North America (genus Sequoia or Sequoiadendron); closely related name and appearance

Etymology

Named for its resemblance to redwoods and its recent 'dawn' of rediscovery in the 20th century, after being known from fossils.
