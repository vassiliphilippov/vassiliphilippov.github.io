livery company

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪvəri ˌkʌmpəni / (AmE), / ˈlɪvəri ˌkʌmpəni / (BrE)
one of the historic trade or craft associations in the City of London, some of which date back to medieval guilds, often with ceremonial and charitable functions
historic guild in London

Examples

He joined a livery company to continue his family's tradition in the City of London.
Each livery company has its own ancient hall and distinctive coat of arms.
The Worshipful Company of Goldsmiths is a prominent livery company involved in charity and education.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guild - an association of artisans or merchants formed for mutual aid or protection

Etymology

From the use of 'livery' to denote the distinctive dress worn by members; 'company' from Old French 'compaignie'.
