blood serum

1. NOUN

/ blʌd ˈsɪrəm / (AmE), / blʌd ˈsɪərəm / (BrE)
the clear, yellowish fluid that remains after blood has clotted, minus blood cells and clotting proteins, rich in proteins and used in diagnostics and research
clear fluid from clotted blood
medical

Examples

After the sample was centrifuged, the blood serum was separated for testing.
Doctors often analyze blood serum to detect infections or monitor organ function.
Changes in protein levels in blood serum can indicate various diseases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: serum - the clear fluid part of any animal or plant fluid, especially blood, after clotting
