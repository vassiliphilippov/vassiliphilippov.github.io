merry andrew

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛri ˌændru / (AmE), / ˈmɛri ˌandruː / (BrE)
a person who entertains others by behaving in a silly or ridiculous way; a clown or buffoon
comical person; buffoon
archaic

Examples

The court jester was known as the merry andrew who kept the king amused.
Instead of helping, he acted the merry andrew and made everyone laugh at his antics.
The children loved watching the merry andrew perform tricks at the village fair.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clown - a comic entertainer; a buffoon
  • Synonym: buffoon - a ridiculous but amusing person; a clown

Etymology

Possibly from the popular name Andrew for clowns or jesters in 17th-century England.
