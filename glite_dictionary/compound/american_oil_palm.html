American oil palm

1. NOUN

/ əˈmɛrɪkən ɔɪl pɑm / (AmE), / əˈmɛrɪkən ɔɪl pɑːm / (BrE)
A species of palm tree native to Central and South America, cultivated for its oil-rich fruit.
palm tree producing oil-rich fruit

Examples

The American oil palm is valued for the high yield of oil extracted from its fruit.
Farmers in Colombia have started growing American oil palms to boost local oil production.
Unlike its African counterpart, the American oil palm thrives in humid tropical forests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oil palm - a palm tree cultivated for oil-producing fruit, especially Elaeis guineensis
