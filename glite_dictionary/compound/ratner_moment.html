Ratner moment

1. NOUN

/ ˈræt.nɚ ˈmoʊ.mənt / (AmE), / ˈræt.nə ˈməʊ.mənt / (BrE)
an embarrassing or damaging public relations incident, especially one caused by a careless or tactless remark
damaging PR blunder or gaffe

Examples

The CEO's comments about his customers led to a disastrous Ratner moment for the company.
Journalists were quick to report every Ratner moment during the heated debate.
Fearing a Ratner moment, the politician rehearsed her speech multiple times.

Synset Examples

Avoiding a Ratner moment is crucial for public figures.
His careless comments were a classic Ratner moment.
She had a Ratner moment during the presentation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gaffe - an unintentional act or remark causing embarrassment
  • Synonym: faux pas - an embarrassing or tactless act or remark in a social situation

Etymology

Alludes to Gerald Ratner, a British businessman whose public remarks in 1991 negatively impacted his company's reputation.
