two-by-four

1. NOUN

/ ˌtuː baɪ ˈfɔr / (AmE), / ˌtuː baɪ ˈfɔː / (BrE)
a plank of wood with a cross section nominally measuring two inches by four inches, commonly used in construction
standard-sized plank of wood

Examples

He built the frame using two-by-fours from the hardware store.
A two-by-four is often used for wall studs in residential buildings.
Don't forget to measure the length of the two-by-four before you cut it.

Etymology

Named for its nominal dimensions: two inches by four inches.
