Indian giving

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪndiən ˌɡɪvɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɪndiən ˌɡɪvɪŋ / (BrE)
The act of taking back a gift after having given it, or expecting a gift to be returned; considered offensive due to its negative stereotypical connotations.
taking back a previously given gift (offensive idiom)
offensive american

Examples

Many found his Indian giving behavior rude and inconsiderate at the birthday party.
She accused him of Indian giving after he asked for the gift back.
Please avoid Indian giving; if you give something, let it stay given.

Synset Examples

His Indian giving nature made it hard to accept gifts.
She experienced Indian giving when her friend took back the favor.
We joked about his Indian giving when he wanted his book back.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to take back - to regain possession after having given something away

Etymology

Offensive term based on a negative stereotype about Native Americans in U.S. English, first recorded in the 19th century.
