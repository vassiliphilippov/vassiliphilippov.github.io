class Equisetatae

1. NOUN

/ klæs ˌɛkwɪˈsiːtətiː / (AmE), / klɑːs ˌɛkwɪˈsiːtətiː / (BrE)
A former class of vascular plants comprising horsetails and related extinct forms, recognized in some botanical classification systems.
a class of plants including horsetails
medical

Examples

The fossil record shows that plants of the class Equisetatae were once much more diverse.
Many textbooks once classified horsetails under the class Equisetatae before taxonomy was revised.
Researchers debated whether certain Paleozoic fossils belonged within the class Equisetatae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horsetail - a plant of the genus Equisetum

Etymology

From Latin 'equisetum' (horsetail), with '-atae' denoting a class in botanical Latin.
