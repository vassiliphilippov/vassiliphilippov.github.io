blueback salmon

1. NOUN

/ ˈbluˌbæk ˈsæmən / (AmE), / ˈbluːbæk ˈsæmən / (BrE)
A small salmon of the northern Pacific, with red flesh, valued as food; also known as the sockeye salmon. Adults die after spawning.
species of small, edible Pacific salmon

Examples

Fishermen in Alaska rely on the annual blueback salmon run to support their livelihoods.
The bright red flesh of the blueback salmon is prized by chefs around the world.
After spawning in freshwater streams, every adult blueback salmon dies as part of its natural lifecycle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sockeye salmon - another name for blueback salmon
