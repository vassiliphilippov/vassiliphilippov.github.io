compact disk

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑm.pækt dɪsk / (AmE), / ˈkɒm.pækt dɪsk / (BrE)
a small, round optical disc used to store digital audio, video, or data, typically played back using a laser
small optical disc for digital data

Examples

My favorite album was reissued on compact disk with bonus tracks.
Many computers no longer include a compact disk drive as most software is now downloaded online.
She backed up her wedding photos on a compact disk to keep them safe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compact disc - alternative standard spelling for the same digital optical disk
  • Synonym: CD - abbreviation for compact disc
  • Antonym: vinyl record - analog sound storage medium, predecessor to the compact disk

Etymology

From 'compact' meaning 'small; closely packed' and 'disk', with spelling modeled on earlier magnetic disks; introduced by Philips and Sony in the 1980s.
