Rio de la Plata

1. PROPN

/ ˌrioʊ deɪ lɑ ˈplɑtə / (AmE), / ˌriːəʊ deɪ lə ˈplɑːtə / (BrE)
an estuary on the southeastern coastline of South America, forming part of the border between Argentina and Uruguay, where the Paraná and Uruguay rivers meet the Atlantic Ocean
estuary between Argentina and Uruguay

Examples

Many ships cross the Rio de la Plata to travel between Buenos Aires and Montevideo.
The Rio de la Plata is an important waterway for commerce in South America.
You can see the wide expanse of the Rio de la Plata from the coast of Uruguay.

Etymology

From Spanish, meaning 'River of Silver'.
