scheduled territories

1. NOUN

/ ˈskɛdʒuˌəld ˈtɛrəˌtɔriz / (AmE), / ˈʃɛdjuːld ˈtɛrɪtəriz / (BrE)
the group of countries whose currencies were officially tied to the British pound sterling, especially during the mid-20th century
countries with currencies tied to the British pound

Examples

After World War II, several nations were classified as scheduled territories by the British government.
Laws regarding currency exchange often referenced the list of scheduled territories.
The regulations affected trade between the United Kingdom and the scheduled territories.
