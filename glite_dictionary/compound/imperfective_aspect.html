imperfective aspect

1. NOUN

/ ɪmˌpɝːˈfɛktɪv ˈæspɛkt / (AmE), / ɪmˌpɜːˈfɛktɪv ˈæspekt / (BrE)
a grammatical aspect that expresses an action or state without specifying its beginning or end, focusing on its ongoing, habitual, or repeated nature
grammatical aspect of ongoing or habitual action

Examples

In Russian, the imperfective aspect is used to describe actions that are ongoing or repeated.
The teacher explained how to form the imperfective aspect in Spanish verbs.
Some languages mark the imperfective aspect with special verb endings.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: perfective aspect - grammatical aspect expressing a completed action

Etymology

From Latin 'im-' (not) + 'perfectus' (completed) + 'aspectus' (appearance, view).
