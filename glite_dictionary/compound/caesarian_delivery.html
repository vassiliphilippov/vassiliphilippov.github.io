Caesarian delivery

1. NOUN

/ siˈzɛriən dɪˈlɪvəri / (AmE), / siˈzɛːriən dɪˈlɪvəri / (BrE)
The delivery of a baby by surgical incision through the abdominal wall and uterus.
surgical childbirth procedure
medical

Examples

After a long labor, the doctors decided a Caesarian delivery was safest for both mother and child.
Many women recover quickly after a Caesarian delivery, though it is still considered major surgery.
The hospital has seen an increase in scheduled Caesarian deliveries in recent years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cesarean section - surgical procedure to deliver a baby

Etymology

From Latin 'caesarea' (cutting), related to the belief that Julius Caesar was born in this way.
