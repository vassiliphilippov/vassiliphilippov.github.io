straight furrow

1. NOUN

/ streɪt ˈfɜroʊ / (AmE), / streɪt ˈfʌrəʊ / (BrE)
A furrow in the soil that is made in a straight line, typically for planting seeds.
straight line in soil for planting

Examples

The farmer guided the plow to create a straight furrow across the field.
It is important to plant seeds in a straight furrow for easier cultivation.
After the rain, the straight furrow was still clearly visible in the vegetable patch.

Synset Examples

Farmers need to draw a straight furrow for planting seeds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: furrow - a long narrow trench made in the ground by a plow, especially for planting seeds or irrigation
