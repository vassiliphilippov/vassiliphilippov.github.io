family Cichlidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli sɪkˈlɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli sɪkˈlɪdiː / (BrE)
A large family of freshwater fish, commonly known as cichlids, native to mainly tropical regions of Africa, Central and South America, and widely kept in aquariums.
a family of freshwater fish (cichlids)

Examples

Many popular aquarium fish belong to the family Cichlidae due to their vivid colors and interesting behaviors.
The family Cichlidae includes over 1,600 species found primarily in Africa and the Americas.
Biologists often study the family Cichlidae to understand rapid speciation and adaptive radiation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cichlid - any fish of the family Cichlidae

Etymology

From Latin 'Cichlidae', the family name based on 'Cichla', a genus of cichlid.
