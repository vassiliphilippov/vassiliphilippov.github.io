general anesthetic

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒɛnərəl ænəsˈθɛtɪk / (AmE), / ˌdʒɛnərəl ænɪsˈθɛtɪk / (BrE)
a drug or agent that causes loss of sensation and consciousness throughout the entire body, used especially during surgical procedures
drug causing total unconsciousness
medical

Examples

The surgeon administered a general anesthetic before beginning the operation.
Because the procedure was complex, a general anesthetic was required instead of a local one.
Some patients feel dizzy or nauseous after receiving a general anesthetic.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: local anesthetic - an anesthetic causing loss of sensation in a specific area of the body

Etymology

From 'anesthetic' (from Greek 'anaisthēsia', meaning 'without sensation') combined with 'general' indicating whole-body effect.
