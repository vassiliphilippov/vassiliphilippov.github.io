cobalt ultramarine

1. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊˌbɔlt ˌʌltrəˈmɛrin / (AmE), / ˈkəʊˌbɒlt ˌʌltrəˈmɑːriːn / (BrE)
a greenish-blue pigment made from cobalt oxide and alumina, used especially in painting and ceramics
a greenish-blue pigment from cobalt compounds

Examples

The vivid color in her artwork was achieved using cobalt ultramarine for the sky.
Restorers applied cobalt ultramarine to match the original tones of the painting.
Ceramic vases were decorated with a unique shade of blue from cobalt ultramarine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ultramarine - a vivid blue pigment originally made from lapis lazuli

Etymology

From cobalt (the metallic element, from German 'Kobalt') + ultramarine (from Latin 'ultra' + 'mare', meaning 'beyond the sea').
