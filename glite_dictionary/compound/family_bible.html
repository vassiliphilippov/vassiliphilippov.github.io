family Bible

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ˈbaɪbəl / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli ˈbaɪbəl / (BrE)
A large Bible kept in a family home, often with blank pages for recording marriages, births, and deaths of family members.
large Bible for recording family events

Examples

The old family Bible contained handwritten records of their ancestors’ births and marriages.
After the wedding, they carefully inscribed the date in their family Bible.
Many families treasure a family Bible as a historical keepsake passed down through generations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Bible - the holy book of Christianity
