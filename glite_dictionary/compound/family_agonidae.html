family Agonidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ˌæɡəˈnaɪdi / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli ˌæɡəˈnɪːdiː / (BrE)
A family of small, armored marine fishes commonly known as poachers, found in cold northern seas.
family of armored marine fishes (poachers)

Examples

The family Agonidae includes several species known for their distinctive bony armor.
Scientists collected specimens from the family Agonidae during their Arctic expedition.
Members of the family Agonidae are adapted to life on the cold sea floor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poacher - a fish of the family Agonidae, with bony plates

Etymology

From New Latin, combining the genus name Agonus and the suffix -idae denoting a family in zoological classification.
