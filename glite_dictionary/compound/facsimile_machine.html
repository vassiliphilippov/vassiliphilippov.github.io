facsimile machine

1. NOUN

/ fækˈsɪməli məˈʃin / (AmE), / fækˈsɪmɪli məˈʃiːn / (BrE)
a device that copies documents and transmits them electronically over a telephone line or radio
device for sending copies of documents electronically

Examples

The office still uses a facsimile machine to send important documents overseas.
With the rise of email, the facsimile machine has become less common in businesses.
She waited by the facsimile machine for the signed contract to arrive.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fax machine - a device for sending and receiving copies of documents electronically

Etymology

From Latin 'facsimile' (make similar), originally meaning 'an exact copy'; 'machine' from Latin 'machina'.
