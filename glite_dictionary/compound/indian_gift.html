Indian gift

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪn.di.ən ɡɪft / (AmE), / ˈɪn.di.ən ɡɪft / (BrE)
A gift that is given to someone and later taken back by the giver.
a gift taken back by the giver
offensive

Examples

She laughed when he realized the necklace was an Indian gift and asked for it back.
Some people find it upsetting to receive an Indian gift because it is later taken away.
Calling someone's present an Indian gift is now widely considered offensive.

Synset Examples

She laughed when he said it was an Indian gift.
He considered it an Indian gift, given and then taken back.
Receiving an Indian gift can be confusing sometimes.

Etymology

From a stereotype in 18th-19th century American English, now considered offensive.
