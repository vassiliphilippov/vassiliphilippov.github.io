explosive compound

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˈsploʊsɪv ˈkɑmpaʊnd / (AmE), / ɪkˈspləʊsɪv ˈkɒmpaʊnd / (BrE)
a chemical compound that can undergo a rapid and violent reaction, producing an explosion
chemical compound causing explosions

Examples

The laboratory strictly regulates the storage of explosive compounds to prevent accidents.
Chemists are developing safer alternatives to traditional explosive compounds for industrial use.
Nitroglycerin is an example of an explosive compound widely used in mining.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: explosive - a substance that causes a sudden release of energy and explosion
