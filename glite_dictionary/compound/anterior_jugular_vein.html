anterior jugular vein

1. NOUN

/ ænˈtɪriər ˈdʒʌɡjələr veɪn / (AmE), / ænˈtɪəriə ˈdʒʌɡjʊlə veɪn / (BrE)
a vein located in the front of the neck that arises below the chin from veins draining the lower face and joins the external jugular vein
vein at the front of the neck
medical

Examples

The surgeon carefully identified the anterior jugular vein before making the incision.
Swelling in the neck can sometimes put pressure on the anterior jugular vein.
In some procedures, the anterior jugular vein may be used for venous access.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: external jugular vein - a vein in the neck that receives blood from the outside of the skull and deep parts of the face

Etymology

From Latin 'ante-' meaning 'before' and 'jugularis' meaning 'of the throat'.
