velvet sumac

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɛlvət ˈsuːmæk / (AmE), / ˈvɛlvɪt ˈsjuːmæk / (BrE)
a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America, with velvety branches, compound leaves that turn bright red in autumn, and clusters of greenish-yellow flowers followed by crimson, acidic berries
a North American shrub or tree with velvety branches and red autumn leaves

Examples

The leaves of the velvet sumac turn a stunning red in autumn, making it a popular ornamental plant.
Clusters of crimson berries on the velvet sumac attract many types of birds.
You can identify velvet sumac by its dense, velvety branches and brilliant fall foliage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sumac - any of various shrubs or small trees of the genus Rhus
