political hack

1. NOUN

/ pəˈlɪdɪkəl hæk / (AmE), / pəˈlɪtɪkəl hæk / (BrE)
a politician, especially one considered unscrupulous or self-serving, who supports a party or clique for personal gain rather than public interest
unscrupulous or self-serving politician

Examples

Many people saw the senator as just another political hack working for special interests.
The appointment was criticized as the work of a political hack rather than a professional.
She vowed to clean up government and get rid of partisan political hacks from the system.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hack - an unprincipled or mercenary person, especially in politics or journalism
