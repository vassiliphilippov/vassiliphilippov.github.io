white birch

1. NOUN

/ waɪt bɝːtʃ / (AmE), / waɪt bɜːtʃ / (BrE)
A birch tree of Europe, especially Betula pendula, with dull white to pale brown bark and slightly drooping, hairy branches.
a European birch tree with pale bark

Examples

The white birch stood tall at the edge of the forest, its pale bark gleaming in the sunlight.
Gardeners often choose white birch for its delicate appearance and graceful branches.
Many birds build their nests in the branches of the white birch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silver birch - a related birch tree, Betula pendula, known for its silvery bark
