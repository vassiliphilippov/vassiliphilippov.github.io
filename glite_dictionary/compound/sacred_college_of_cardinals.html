Sacred College of Cardinals

1. PROPN

/ ˈseɪkrɪd ˈkɑlɪdʒ əv ˈkɑrdn̩lz / (AmE), / ˈseɪkrɪd ˈkɒlɪdʒ əv ˈkɑːdɪnəlz / (BrE)
The collective body of all cardinals in the Roman Catholic Church, responsible for electing and advising the pope.
body of cardinals in the Catholic Church

Examples

The Sacred College of Cardinals convened in Rome to discuss important matters of doctrine.
Members of the Sacred College of Cardinals are responsible for electing the new pope.
He studied the influence of the Sacred College of Cardinals in Church history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: College of Cardinals - the collective body of cardinals in the Catholic Church; synonym for Sacred College of Cardinals

Etymology

From Latin 'sacrum collegium cardinalium', meaning 'holy college of cardinals'.
