European Russia

1. NOUN

/ jʊˈroʊpiən ˈrʌʃə / (AmE), / jʊəˈrəʊpiən ˈrʌʃə / (BrE)
the region of Russia that lies within the geographical boundaries of Europe, west of the Ural Mountains
region of Russia in Europe

Examples

Most of Russia's population lives in European Russia, west of the Ural Mountains.
Moscow and St. Petersburg are major cities located in European Russia.
The climate in European Russia is generally milder than in the eastern part of the country.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Asian Russia - the part of Russia that is in Asia, east of the Urals
