family Coregonidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli kɔːˌrɛɡoʊˈniːdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli kɒˌrɛɡəʊˈniːdiː / (BrE)
a family of soft-finned freshwater fishes, including the whitefishes, formerly classified under Salmonidae
family of freshwater whitefishes

Examples

Scientists recently discovered a new species within the family Coregonidae in European freshwater lakes.
Many commercially important whitefish belong to the family Coregonidae.
Taxonomists now treat the family Coregonidae as distinct from the salmon family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whitefish - a common name for species within Coregonidae
  • Degree Higher: family Salmonidae - the family of salmon and trout; previously included Coregonidae

Etymology

From New Latin Coregonidae, named after the type genus Coregonus.
