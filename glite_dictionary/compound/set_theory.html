set theory

1. NOUN

/ sɛt ˈθɪəri / (AmE), / sɛt ˈθɪəri / (BrE)
The branch of mathematics that studies the properties and relationships of sets.
branch of mathematics about sets

Examples

Set theory forms the foundation for much of modern mathematics.
Students find set theory useful for understanding the basics of mathematical logic.
The famous mathematician Georg Cantor is credited with developing set theory.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mathematics - the abstract science of number, quantity, and space
