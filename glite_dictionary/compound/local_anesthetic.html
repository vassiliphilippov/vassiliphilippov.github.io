local anesthetic

1. NOUN

/ ˈloʊkəl ænəsˈθɛtɪk / (AmE), / ˈləʊkəl anɪsˈθɛtɪk / (BrE)
A drug or agent that causes loss of sensation in a specific area of the body, usually to prevent pain during a medical procedure.
drug causing numbness in one area
medical

Examples

The dentist applied a local anesthetic before filling my cavity.
After receiving a local anesthetic, I didn't feel any pain during the minor surgery.
Some people are allergic to a particular type of local anesthetic.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: general anesthetic - anesthetic that produces loss of consciousness
