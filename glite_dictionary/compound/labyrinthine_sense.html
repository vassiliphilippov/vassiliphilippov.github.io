labyrinthine sense

1. NOUN

/ ˌlæbəˈrɪnθin sɛns / (AmE), / ˌlæbɪˈrɪnθiːn sɛns / (BrE)
the sense that detects the position and movement of the head, provided by sensory organs within the inner ear
sense detecting head orientation and movement
medical

Examples

Our labyrinthine sense helps us maintain our balance while walking or running.
Disorders affecting the labyrinthine sense can result in vertigo and dizziness.
The labyrinthine sense is crucial for coordinating head and eye movements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vestibular sense - the sense of balance related to the inner ear
  • Synonym: equilibrium - the state of physical balance; bodily balance

Etymology

From 'labyrinth' (referring to the inner ear's labyrinth) and 'sense'.
