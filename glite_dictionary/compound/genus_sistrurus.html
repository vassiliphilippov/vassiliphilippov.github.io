genus Sistrurus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs sɪsˈtruːr.əs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs sɪsˈtruːr.əs / (BrE)
A genus of small, venomous pit vipers commonly known as pygmy rattlesnakes, native to North America.
genus of pygmy rattlesnakes

Examples

The genus Sistrurus includes several species of small rattlesnakes found in North America.
Researchers studied the venom yields of snakes within the genus Sistrurus.
The massasauga is a well-known representative of the genus Sistrurus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rattlesnake - a venomous American snake with a segmented rattle at the end of its tail

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'Sistrurus' (coined genus name) with Latin 'genus' (kind, type).
