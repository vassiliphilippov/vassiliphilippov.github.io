green foxtail

1. NOUN

/ ɡrin ˈfɑkˌsteɪl / (AmE), / ɡriːn ˈfɒksteɪl / (BrE)
a type of annual grass (Setaria viridis), originally European but now common in North America as a weed, characterized by its bristly, green, foxtail-like flower heads
a weedy grass species with green bristly heads

Examples

Green foxtail often invades cornfields and competes with crops for nutrients.
The rapid growth of green foxtail makes it a persistent weed in gardens across the Midwest.
Farmers frequently battle green foxtail because it spreads quickly in disturbed soils.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foxtail - a grass with bushy, tail-like flower heads
