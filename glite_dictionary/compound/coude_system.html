coude system

1. NOUN

/ kuːˈdeɪ ˈsɪstəm / (AmE), / kuːˈdeɪ ˈsɪstəm / (BrE)
A reflecting telescope arrangement in which light is directed, usually by mirrors, to a fixed focus for attachment of a plate holder or spectrograph.
telescope system with light directed to a fixed focus

Examples

The observatory recently installed a new coude system to enhance their spectrographic observations.
Astronomers prefer the coude system when they need a stationary instrument attached to a telescope.
A coude system is ideal for collecting light at a fixed location for detailed analysis.

Etymology

From French 'coudé' meaning 'elbowed', referring to the bent light path.
