knee breeches

1. NOUN

/ niː ˈbrɪtʃɪz / (AmE), / niː ˈbriːtʃɪz / (BrE)
short trousers fastened just below the knee, worn especially by men in the 17th–18th centuries
short trousers ending above the knee

Examples

In the portrait, the nobleman is elegantly dressed in knee breeches and a satin coat.
During the festival, actors wore authentic knee breeches to recreate the eighteenth-century atmosphere.
He found a pair of knee breeches in the theater's costume department for the period drama.
