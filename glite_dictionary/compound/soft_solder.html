soft solder

1. NOUN

/ sɒft ˈsɑːdər / (AmE), / sɒft ˈsɒldə / (BrE)
a type of solder that melts at a relatively low temperature, typically used for joining metals in plumbing and electronics
solder with a low melting point

Examples

The plumber used soft solder to join the copper pipes in the bathroom.
For delicate electronic components, soft solder is preferred because it melts at a lower temperature.
Always choose soft solder when working with metals that might be damaged by high heat.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hard solder - solder that melts at a higher temperature

Etymology

From 'soft' (not hard) + 'solder' (from Old French soudure, Latin solidare 'to make solid').
