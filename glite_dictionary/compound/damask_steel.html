Damask steel

1. NOUN

/ dəˈmæsk stil / (AmE), / dəˈmɑːsk stiːl / (BrE)
a hard, resilient steel with a characteristic patterned surface, traditionally used for sword blades and often highly decorated
patterned steel used for sword blades

Examples

The swordsmith was famous for crafting blades from Damask steel with intricate swirling patterns.
Collectors prize antique swords made of Damask steel for their strength and beauty.
The Damask steel blade retained its sharp edge even after years of use.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Damascus steel - pattern-welded steel known for its wavy surface pattern

Etymology

Named after Damascus, the capital of Syria, where such steel was historically produced or traded.
