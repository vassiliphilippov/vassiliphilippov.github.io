religious mysticism

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈlɪdʒəs ˈmɪstɪˌsɪzəm / (AmE), / rɪˈlɪdʒəs ˈmɪstɪsɪzm / (BrE)
A form of religion or spiritual practice centered on achieving a direct, personal communion with an ultimate reality or divine presence through mystical experiences.
mystical approach to religion

Examples

The ancient philosopher wrote about religious mysticism as a means of reaching true enlightenment.
Many traditions of religious mysticism emphasize meditative practices and spiritual contemplation.
He became interested in religious mysticism after reading about the lives of famous mystics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mysticism - belief in the possibility of attaining insight or direct communication with ultimate reality through intuition or spiritual experience
  • Degree Lower: asceticism - practice of severe self-discipline and abstention from all forms of indulgence for religious reasons
