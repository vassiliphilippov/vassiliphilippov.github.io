Secretary of the Navy

1. NOUN

/ səˈkrɛt̬.ɚ.i əv ðə nəˈvi / (AmE), / sɪˈkrɛt.ər.i əv ðə nəˈvi / (BrE)
the head of the United States Department of the Navy, an executive position that oversaw Navy operations before being merged under the Secretary of Defense in 1947
former head of the U.S. Navy Department
american

Examples

The Secretary of the Navy played a key role in shaping naval policy during World War II.
After 1947, the Secretary of the Navy became subordinate to the Secretary of Defense.
Throughout history, many distinguished individuals have served as Secretary of the Navy.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Secretary of Defense - the head of the U.S. Department of Defense, which absorbed the role of Secretary of the Navy in 1947
  • Synonym: Secretary of War - the head of the former War Department, another position combined into the Secretary of Defense
