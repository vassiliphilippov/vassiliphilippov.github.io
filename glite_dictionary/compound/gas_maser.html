gas maser

1. NOUN

/ ɡæs ˈmeɪzɚ / (AmE), / ɡæs ˈmeɪzə / (BrE)
A type of maser in which microwave radiation is amplified by stimulated emission from gas molecules.
maser using gas molecules

Examples

The discovery of the gas maser in star-forming regions advanced our understanding of interstellar chemistry.
Researchers used a gas maser to study the behavior of microwave amplification in rarefied gases.
The gas maser operates effectively at specific frequencies determined by the gaseous medium used.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maser - a device that produces or amplifies microwaves by stimulated emission of radiation

Etymology

Maser from 'Microwave Amplification by Stimulated Emission of Radiation'; 'gas' from Old English 'gās'.
