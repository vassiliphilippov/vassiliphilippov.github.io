Gibson Desert

1. PROPN

/ ˈgɪbsən ˈdɛzɚt / (AmE), / ˈgɪbsən ˈdɛzət / (BrE)
a large desert region in western Australia, known for its arid environment and sparse population
large desert in western Australia

Examples

The Gibson Desert covers a vast area of sand dunes and gravel plains.
Travelers should prepare carefully before crossing the Gibson Desert due to its harsh conditions.
Scientists study the plants and animals that have adapted to life in the Gibson Desert.

Etymology

Named after Alfred Gibson, an Australian explorer.
