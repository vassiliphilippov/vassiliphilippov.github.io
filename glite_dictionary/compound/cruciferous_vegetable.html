cruciferous vegetable

1. NOUN

/ kruˈsɪfərəs ˈvɛdʒtəbl / (AmE), / kruːˈsɪfərəs ˈvɛdʒtəbl / (BrE)
any edible vegetable belonging to the mustard family, including cabbage, broccoli, cauliflower, brussels sprouts, and related plants
vegetable in the mustard family (e.g., broccoli, cabbage)

Examples

Many nutritionists recommend eating at least one cruciferous vegetable per day for their health benefits.
Broccoli is perhaps the most popular cruciferous vegetable among children and adults alike.
Cooking methods can affect the nutrient content of a cruciferous vegetable like cauliflower.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brassicaceae - the mustard family of plants

Etymology

From Latin 'crucifer' (cross-bearing), referring to the cross-shaped flowers of plants in this family.
