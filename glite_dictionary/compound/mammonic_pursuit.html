mammonic pursuit

1. NOUN

/ mæˈmɑnɪk pərˈsuːt / (AmE), / mæˈmɒnɪk pəˈsjuːt / (BrE)
a relentless or single-minded pursuit of wealth or material gain
relentless pursuit of wealth

Examples

The mammonic pursuit of some corporations can lead to ethical compromises.
He sacrificed family and health to his mammonic pursuit of fortune.
Many people tire of the constant mammonic pursuit in the modern world.

Synset Examples

The mammonic pursuit of wealth can be quite exhausting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: greed - intense and selfish desire for wealth or power
  • Synonym: avarice - extreme greed for wealth

Etymology

From 'Mammon', used in the Bible and later English as a personification of wealth or greed, plus the suffix '-ic' and 'pursuit'.
