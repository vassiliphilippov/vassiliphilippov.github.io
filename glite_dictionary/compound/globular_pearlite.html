globular pearlite

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡlɑbjəlɚ ˈpɪrlaɪt / (AmE), / ˈɡlɒbjʊlə ˈpɜːlaɪt / (BrE)
a form of pearlite in steel or iron where cementite appears in spherical globules instead of the usual layered structure, typically formed when cooling occurs very slowly
pearlite with cementite in globules, not layers
medical

Examples

When iron cools extremely slowly, globular pearlite may develop instead of the usual lamellar structure.
Metallurgists study globular pearlite to understand how cooling rates affect steel properties.
The presence of globular pearlite in a sample indicates it underwent slow cooling.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: pearlite - a layered microstructure in steel of ferrite and cementite

Etymology

From 'globular', referring to a spherical shape, and 'pearlite', a microstructure in steel named for its iridescent appearance.
