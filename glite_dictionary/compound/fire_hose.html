fire hose

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪɚ ˌhoʊz / (AmE), / ˈfaɪə ˌhəʊz / (BrE)
a large, strong hose used by firefighters for carrying water from a hydrant to the site of a fire
large hose for firefighting

Examples

The firefighters quickly unrolled the fire hose and connected it to the hydrant.
After the drill, we had to carefully dry and store the fire hose.
A powerful stream of water shot out of the fire hose, dousing the flames.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hose - flexible tube for conveying liquid

2. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪɚ hoʊz / (AmE), / ˈfaɪə həʊz / (BrE)
A strong, flexible hose used to convey water to a fire for extinguishing it.
hose for delivering water in firefighting

Examples

The firefighter quickly unrolled the fire hose to tackle the burning building.
Every hallway in the building is equipped with a fire hose in case of emergency.
They checked the fire hose connections during the routine safety inspection.

Synset Examples

A fire hose is essential during emergencies.
The firefighters quickly grabbed the fire hose.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hose - a flexible tube conveying fluids
