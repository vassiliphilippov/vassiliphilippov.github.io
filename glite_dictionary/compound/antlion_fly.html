antlion fly

1. NOUN

/ ˈæntˌlaɪ.ən flaɪ / (AmE), / ˈæntˌlaɪ.ən flaɪ / (BrE)
A winged insect resembling a dragonfly, whose larvae (called doodlebugs) dig conical pits to trap ants and other small insects.
insect like a dragonfly; larvae are called doodlebugs

Examples

The antlion fly is fascinating for its predatory larvae, which dig traps in sandy soil.
While hiking in the desert, we watched an antlion fly dart through the air near a dry creek bed.
Entomologists often study the unique life cycle of the antlion fly in their field research.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antlion - predatory insect; larvae trap ants in pits
