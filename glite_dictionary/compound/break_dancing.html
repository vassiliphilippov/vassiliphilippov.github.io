break dancing

1. NOUN

/ breɪk ˈdænsɪŋ / (AmE), / breɪk ˈdɑːnsɪŋ / (BrE)
A style of solo street dance featuring fast, acrobatic movements and ground work, typically performed to hip-hop or rap music.
acrobatic street dance style

Examples

The crowd cheered as he performed an impressive break dancing routine on the city square.
She has been practicing break dancing for five years and often enters competitions.
Many music videos from the 1980s feature energetic break dancing scenes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: b-boying - another term for break dancing or a practitioner of it
  • Synonym: hip-hop - a culture and style of music associated with break dancing

Etymology

From 'break', referring to the percussion breaks in music, and 'dancing'. Became popular in the 1970s in the US.
