genus Amaryllis

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌæməˈrɪlɪs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌæməˈrɪlɪs / (BrE)
The type genus of the Amaryllidaceae family, comprising bulbous flowering plants native to southern Africa.
genus of bulbous plants from southern Africa

Examples

The genus Amaryllis includes several popular ornamental bulbs prized for their large, showy flowers.
Botanists originally classified many species under the genus Amaryllis, but some have since been reassigned.
Most members of the genus Amaryllis are native to the western regions of southern Africa.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Amaryllidaceae - The botanical family including the genus Amaryllis and related plants

Etymology

From Latin 'Amaryllis', the plant name, from Greek 'Amarullis', a shepherdess in poetry.
