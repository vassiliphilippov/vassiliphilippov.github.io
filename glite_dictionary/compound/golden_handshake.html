golden handshake

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡoʊldən ˈhændʃeɪk / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊldən ˈhændʃeɪk / (BrE)
a large sum of money or generous financial compensation given to an employee when they leave a job, especially as an incentive to retire early or as part of a severance agreement
generous retirement or severance payment

Examples

The CEO received a golden handshake worth several million dollars when he retired.
Rumors circulated that senior managers would be offered a golden handshake to encourage early retirement.
She was reluctant to leave until the company promised her a substantial golden handshake.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: severance package - an arrangement of pay and benefits offered to an employee after leaving a company
  • Synonym: redundancy payment - compensation given to an employee who is dismissed, especially in the UK

Etymology

From the notion of a 'handshake' as a symbol of agreement, combined with 'golden' to suggest large financial value (20th century).
