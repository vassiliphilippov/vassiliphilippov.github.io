civil liberty

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪvəl ˈlɪbərti / (AmE), / ˈsɪvəl ˈlɪbəti / (BrE)
the right of individuals to enjoy freedom of action and speech as protected by law, especially from unjust governmental interference
individual rights protected by law

Examples

The Constitution guarantees every citizen the right to civil liberty.
The protesters were advocating for greater civil liberty and government transparency.
Many believe that national security laws should not infringe on civil liberty.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: freedom - the power or right to act, speak, or think without hindrance or restraint
  • Synonym: civil rights - the rights of citizens to political and social freedom and equality

Etymology

From Latin 'civilis' (relating to citizens) and 'libertas' (freedom).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɪvəl ˈlɪbərti / (AmE), / ˈsɪvəl ˈlɪbəti / (BrE)
A fundamental individual right, such as freedom of speech or religion, protected by law against unwarranted governmental interference.
basic individual right protected by law

Examples

Many people moved to the country in search of greater civil liberty and freedom from oppression.
The constitution guarantees every citizen certain civil liberties that the government cannot infringe upon.
Freedom of speech is considered a basic civil liberty in many democratic societies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: human right - a right believed to belong to every person
  • Synonym: civil rights - the rights of citizens to political and social freedom and equality
  • Antonym: oppression - prolonged cruel or unjust treatment or control

Etymology

From Latin civilis (civil) and Middle English liberté (freedom), from Latin libertas.
