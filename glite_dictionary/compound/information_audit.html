information audit

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪnfərˈmeɪʃən ˈɔdɪt / (AmE), / ˌɪnfəˈmeɪʃən ˈɔːdɪt / (BrE)
a systematic examination and assessment of how information is used, managed, and flows within an organization, often to improve its quality and effectiveness
systematic examination of organizational information use

Examples

The company conducted an information audit to identify inefficiencies in the documentation process.
An information audit can help organizations understand how data is stored and accessed.
Before implementing the new software, they performed an information audit to map existing information flows.

Synset Examples

As regards the applicability of this method, it should be noted that the costing stage was not included in either study and that this might suggest it is not required in an information audit.
