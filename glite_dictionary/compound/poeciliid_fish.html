poeciliid fish

1. NOUN

/ piːˈsɪliɪd fɪʃ / (AmE), / piːˈsɪliɪd fɪʃ / (BrE)
A small, usually brightly colored, livebearing fish of the family Poeciliidae, found in warm fresh or brackish waters, often used for mosquito control.
small livebearing tropical fish

Examples

Many aquarium enthusiasts keep poeciliid fish like guppies and mollies because of their bright colors.
Poeciliid fish are frequently introduced to ponds for mosquito control due to their appetite for larvae.
The study examined the reproductive habits of several species of poeciliid fish in brackish environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guppy - a small, colorful poeciliid fish popular in aquariums
  • Synonym: molly - a type of poeciliid fish commonly kept as a pet

Etymology

From New Latin Poeciliidae (the fish family name), from Greek poikilos (variegated).
