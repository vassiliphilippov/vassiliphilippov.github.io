adverbial of condition

1. NOUN

/ ædˈvɝːbiəl əv kənˈdɪʃən / (AmE), / ədˈvɜːbiəl əv kənˈdɪʃən / (BrE)
An adverbial phrase or clause that expresses a possible or counterfactual situation and its consequences, often beginning with 'if' or similar conjunctions.
adverbial expressing condition

Examples

In the sentence 'You can go outside if it stops raining', the words in bold form an adverbial of condition.
The teacher asked us to identify the adverbial of condition in each sentence.
'Unless you hurry' is an example of an adverbial of condition at the beginning of a clause.

Synset Examples

In ‘old furniture will last forever if you polish it regularly’, the clause beginning with ‘if’ is an adverbial of condition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adverbial clause - a clause functioning as an adverbial modifier
