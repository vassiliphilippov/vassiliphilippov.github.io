photochromic eyewear

1. NOUN

/ ˌfoʊ.t̬oʊˈkroʊ.mɪk ˈaɪˌwɛr / (AmE), / ˌfəʊ.təʊˈkrəʊ.mɪk ˈaɪˌwɛə / (BrE)
Eyeglasses fitted with lenses that darken on exposure to sunlight and become clear indoors.
eyeglasses with lenses that darken in sunlight

Examples

She prefers photochromic eyewear because it adapts automatically to changing light conditions.
Photochromic eyewear is useful for people who spend a lot of time moving between indoors and outdoors.
Many athletes choose photochromic eyewear to protect their eyes from sunlight without needing separate sunglasses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: photochromic lenses - optical lenses that darken on exposure to ultraviolet light

Etymology

Photochromic from Greek 'phōs' (light) + 'chrōma' (color); eyewear from 'eye' + 'wear'.
