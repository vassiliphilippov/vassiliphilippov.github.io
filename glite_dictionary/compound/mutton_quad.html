mutton quad

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌtən kwɑd / (AmE), / ˈmʌtən kwɒd / (BrE)
In printing, a metal spacing block with a square body, typically as wide as it is high, used to create space in letterpress composition.
square spacing block in printing

Examples

The compositor reached for a mutton quad to add space at the end of the line.
A traditional mutton quad is the same width as the type size being used.
Distinguishing between an en quad and a mutton quad can be difficult for beginners in letterpress printing.

Synset Examples

since ‘em quad’ is hard to distinguish from ‘en quad’, printers sometimes called it a ‘mutton quad’

Related Terms

  • Synonym: em quad - a printer's space of the width of an uppercase 'M' or the point size of the type
  • Degree Lower: en quad - a printer's space half the width of an em quad

Etymology

‘Mutton’ is a printer’s slang alteration of ‘em’, referring to the ‘em quad’, with 'quad' shortened from 'quadrat'.
