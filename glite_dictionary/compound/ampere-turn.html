ampere-turn

1. NOUN

/ ˈæmˌpɛr tɝn / (AmE), / ˈæmˌpɪə tɜːn / (BrE)
A unit of magnetomotive force equal to that produced by one ampere of current flowing through a single turn of a coil.
unit of magnetomotive force

Examples

The strength of the electromagnet was specified as 500 ampere-turns.
Increasing the number of ampere-turns enhances the magnetomotive force in the coil.
The calculation required converting gilberts into ampere-turns for comparison.

Etymology

From 'ampere' (the unit of electric current, after André-Marie Ampère) + 'turn' (a loop of the coil).
