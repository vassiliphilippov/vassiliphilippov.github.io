middle distance

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪdəl ˈdɪstəns / (AmE), / ˈmɪdəl ˈdɪstəns / (BrE)
the part of a scene or landscape that lies between the foreground and the background
space between foreground and background

Examples

In this painting, the middle distance is filled with rolling green hills.
The artist added a small cottage in the middle distance to create depth in the composition.
Our view of the mountains in the middle distance was partly obscured by morning mist.
