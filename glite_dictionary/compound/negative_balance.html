negative balance

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɛɡətɪv ˈbæləns / (AmE), / ˈnɛɡətɪv ˈbæləns / (BrE)
an amount in a bank account or other financial account when debits exceed credits, meaning the account holder owes money to the bank or institution
bank account or account owing money

Examples

If your account has a negative balance, you may be charged an overdraft fee.
The company discovered a negative balance in its operational account after all expenses were paid.
You must deposit funds immediately to correct the negative balance in your savings account.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overdraft - a deficit in a bank account caused by drawing more money than the account holds
