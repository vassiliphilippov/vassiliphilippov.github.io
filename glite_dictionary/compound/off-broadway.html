off-Broadway

1. NOUN

/ ɑf ˈbrɔdˌweɪ / (AmE), / ɒf ˈbrɔːdˌweɪ / (BrE)
(in New York City) relating to or denoting professional theaters, typically with fewer than 500 seats, located outside the main Broadway theater district and generally featuring less commercial, lower-budget productions.
small professional theaters outside Broadway in NYC

Examples

She performed in an off-Broadway play before moving on to bigger productions.
Many innovative shows start in off-Broadway theaters before transferring to Broadway.
The off-Broadway scene is known for its experimental and daring performances.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Broadway - the main theatre district in New York City known for large commercial productions
  • Degree Lower: off-off-Broadway - theaters in New York City with very small audience capacity, even less commercial than off-Broadway

Etymology

From the phrase 'off Broadway', meaning 'not on Broadway', Broadway being the main theatre district of New York City.
