active placebo

1. NOUN

/ ˈæktɪv pləˈsiboʊ / (AmE), / ˈæktɪv pləˈsiːbəʊ / (BrE)
A placebo used in clinical trials that produces noticeable side effects to mimic those of the drug being tested.
placebo producing side effects in trials
medical

Examples

The researchers used an active placebo to ensure that participants experienced similar side effects as those taking the actual drug.
Administering an active placebo helps to maintain the blinding of clinical trial participants.
Some studies compare a new medication not just to an inactive placebo but to an active placebo for more accurate results.

Synset Examples

an active placebo mimics the side effects of the experimental drug

Related Terms

  • Synonym: placebo - a substance with no therapeutic effect, used as a control in testing new drugs
