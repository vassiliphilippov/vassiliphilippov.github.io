English hawthorn

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ ˈhɔːˌθɔrn / (AmE), / ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ ˈhɔːθɔːn / (BrE)
a species of hawthorn (Crataegus laevigata) native to Europe, with deeply cleft leaves and bright red fruits, commonly grown as a hedge plant
European species of hawthorn tree

Examples

The English hawthorn produces clusters of bright red berries in late summer.
Gardeners often use English hawthorn to form dense, thorny hedges.
In some regions, the English hawthorn has become naturalized outside its native range.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hawthorn - a small tree with thorny branches and white or pink flowers

2. NOUN

/ ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ ˈhɔːˌθɔrn / (AmE), / ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ ˈhɔːˌθɔːn / (BrE)
a thorny Eurasian shrub or small tree with clusters of white to scarlet flowers and deep red berries, often cultivated and naturalized in North America
species of hawthorn shrub or tree with showy flowers

Examples

The English hawthorn blooms with clusters of white flowers each spring.
Gardeners often plant English hawthorn for its showy appearance and hardy nature.
Birds are attracted to the deep red berries of the English hawthorn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hawthorn - a shrub or small tree with thorny branches and clusters of white or pink flowers
