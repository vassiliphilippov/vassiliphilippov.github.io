local anesthesia

1. NOUN

/ ˈloʊkəl ˌænəsˈθiʒə / (AmE), / ˈləʊkəl ˌanəsˈθiːziə / (BrE)
a loss of sensation in a specific, limited area of the body, usually caused by the application or injection of a local anesthetic agent
loss of sensation in a local area
medical

Examples

The dentist used local anesthesia to numb the area before removing the wisdom tooth.
Patients receiving local anesthesia remain awake and aware but do not feel pain in the treated region.
Minor surgical procedures are often performed under local anesthesia to minimize recovery time.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: general anesthesia - loss of sensation over the entire body, typically involving unconsciousness

Etymology

From 'local' (relating to a particular area) and 'anesthesia', from Greek anaisthēsia (insensibility).
