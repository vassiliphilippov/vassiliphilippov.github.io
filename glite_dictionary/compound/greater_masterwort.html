greater masterwort

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡreɪtər ˈmæstərˌwɜrt / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪtə ˈmɑːstəwɜːt / (BrE)
A European herb (Astrantia major) with aromatic roots, basal leaves, and large clusters of white or rosy flowers.
European herb with showy flower clusters

Examples

The greater masterwort thrives in moist meadows and is prized for its ornamental flowers.
Gardeners often plant greater masterwort for its clusters of delicate, star-shaped blossoms.
Herbalists in Europe have used the roots of greater masterwort in traditional remedies for centuries.

  • Degree Lower: lesser masterwort - a related species of masterwort
