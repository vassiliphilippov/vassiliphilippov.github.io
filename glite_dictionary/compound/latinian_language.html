Latinian language

1. NOUN

/ ˌleɪˈtɪniən ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌleɪˈtɪniən ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
A group of languages descended from Latin, including French, Spanish, Italian, Portuguese, and Romanian.
language family descended from Latin

Examples

Italian is a Latinian language spoken by millions in Europe and around the world.
French, Spanish, and Portuguese are all considered Latinian languages.
The structure of Romanian shows its roots as a Latinian language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Romance language - any language that evolved from Latin, such as French, Spanish, or Italian

Etymology

From Latin Latinus (‘Latin’) + English suffix -ian, referring to origin from the Latin language.
