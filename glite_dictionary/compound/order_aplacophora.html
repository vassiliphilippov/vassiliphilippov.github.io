order Aplacophora

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdər æpləˈkɑfərə / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə ˌapləkɒˈfəːrə / (BrE)
A taxonomic order of shell-less, worm-like mollusks within the class Amphineura.
order of shell-less marine mollusks
medical

Examples

Researchers discovered several new species within the order Aplacophora in deep-sea habitats.
The order Aplacophora is characterized by its worm-like, shell-less marine mollusks.
Taxonomists debated the classification of the order Aplacophora due to its unique anatomical features.

Etymology

From Greek 'a-' (without) + 'plax' (plate) + 'phoros' (bearing), referencing the lack of a shell.
