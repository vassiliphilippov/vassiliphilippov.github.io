Philadelphia fleabane

1. NOUN

/ fɪˌlædəlˈfiə ˈfliːˌbeɪn / (AmE), / fɪˌlædəlˈfɪə ˈfliːˌbeɪn / (BrE)
a North American wildflower with small daisy-like flower heads, each surrounded by a delicate fringe of very slender white or pink rays
North American wildflower with fringed white or pink rays

Examples

Philadelphia fleabane brightens meadows and roadsides throughout the spring and early summer.
She photographed the delicate pink blooms of the Philadelphia fleabane along the hiking trail.
Gardeners often admire the feathery appearance of Philadelphia fleabane in native plant beds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fleabane - a plant of the daisy family, formerly thought to repel fleas
