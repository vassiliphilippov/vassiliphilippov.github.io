activated carbon

1. NOUN

/ ˈæk.tɪ.veɪ.tɪd ˈkɑːr.bən / (AmE), / ˈæk.tɪ.veɪ.tɪd ˈkɑː.bən / (BrE)
a form of carbon processed to have small, low-volume pores that increase its surface area, used for adsorption in purification and as a medical antidote for certain poisons
porous carbon used for adsorption and purification
medical

Examples

The water treatment plant uses activated carbon filters to remove impurities and improve taste.
Doctors administered activated carbon to the patient after the accidental ingestion of poison.
Many air purifiers rely on activated carbon to adsorb odors and toxic gases from the environment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: charcoal - a black, porous form of carbon, often used as fuel
  • Synonym: antidote - a substance that counteracts the effects of poison

Etymology

From 'activate' + 'carbon', referring to the process that increases the surface area of the carbon.
