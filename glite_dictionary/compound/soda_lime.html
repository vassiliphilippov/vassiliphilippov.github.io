soda lime

1. NOUN

/ ˈsoʊ.də ˌlaɪm / (AmE), / ˈsəʊ.də ˌlaɪm / (BrE)
a mixture of sodium hydroxide and calcium hydroxide used to absorb moisture, carbon dioxide, and other gases, especially in medical and laboratory settings
chemical mixture that absorbs gases and liquids

Examples

The anesthesia machine uses soda lime to remove carbon dioxide from exhaled air.
Laboratory technicians replaced the old soda lime in the absorption chamber.
Breathing circuits in submarines sometimes rely on soda lime to ensure air quality.

Etymology

From 'soda', referring to sodium compounds, and 'lime', referring to calcium compounds.
