prairie wake-robin

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɛri ˈweɪk ˌrɑbɪn / (AmE), / ˈprɛəri ˈweɪk ˌrɒbɪn / (BrE)
A trillium native to the central United States, characterized by dark purple, stalkless flowers.
a North American trillium with dark purple flowers

Examples

The prairie wake-robin blooms early in the spring among the tall grasses of the Midwest.
Botanists were excited to discover a rare prairie wake-robin during their field study.
Because of habitat loss, the prairie wake-robin has become less common in some areas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trillium - a genus of perennial flowering plants known as wake-robins
