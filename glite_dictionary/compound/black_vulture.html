black vulture

1. NOUN

/ blæk ˈvʌltʃər / (AmE), / blæk ˈvʌltʃə / (BrE)
A large black bird of prey (Coragyps atratus) found in the Americas, smaller than the turkey vulture, with short tail and white patches on the wing undersides.
New World vulture species of the Americas

Examples

The black vulture soared gracefully over the open fields in search of food.
Unlike the turkey vulture, the black vulture flaps its wings more frequently when flying.
A group of black vultures gathered near the edge of the forest to feed.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: turkey vulture - Large New World vulture with a red head

2. NOUN

/ blæk ˈvʌltʃɚ / (AmE), / blæk ˈvʌltʃə / (BrE)
a large dark-plumaged vulture (Aegypius monachus) native to southern Eurasia and northern Africa, noted for its robust build and scavenging habits.
large Eurasian-African vulture species

Examples

The black vulture is one of the largest birds of prey found in Eurasia.
Researchers observed a black vulture circling over the savanna in northern Africa.
Unlike the New World species, the black vulture of Eurasia has a distinctive mane of feathers around its neck.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cinereous vulture - another common name for Aegypius monachus
