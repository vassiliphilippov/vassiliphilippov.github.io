Thompson submachine gun

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɑmpsən ˌsʌbməˌʃin ˌɡʌn / (AmE), / ˈtɒmpsən ˌsʌbməˌʃiːn ˌɡʌn / (BrE)
a .45-caliber submachine gun, famously used during the 20th century by military, law enforcement, and criminals
a .45-caliber submachine gun

Examples

The gangster carried a Thompson submachine gun during the heist, making it an iconic image of the era.
During World War II, the Thompson submachine gun became standard equipment for many Allied soldiers.
Collectors now prize original Thompson submachine guns for their historical value and unique design.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tommy gun - slang term for the Thompson submachine gun

Etymology

Named after its inventor, John T. Thompson.
