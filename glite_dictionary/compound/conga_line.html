conga line

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑŋɡə laɪn / (AmE), / ˈkɒŋɡə laɪn / (BrE)
a line of people, one behind another, performing the conga dance, typically moving around a room or space
line of dancers performing the conga

Examples

The wedding guests formed a conga line that wound around the whole ballroom.
Everyone laughed as the conga line grew longer and wove through the party.
At midnight, the DJ encouraged everyone to join the conga line on the dance floor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conga - a lively Latin American dance or the music for it

Etymology

From 'conga', a Latin American dance, and 'line'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːŋɡə laɪn / (AmE), / ˈkɒŋɡə laɪn / (BrE)
a line dance in which participants form a line, usually placing their hands on the hips or shoulders of the person in front, and follow a repetitive, rhythmic movement to music
a line dance where people follow each other

Examples

At the party, everyone joined the conga line as the band began to play lively music.
The conga line snaked through the room, with laughter and singing filling the air.
She started a conga line at her birthday celebration, and soon almost every guest was dancing.

Synset Examples

The kids danced in a conga line around the room.
We formed a conga line at the wedding reception.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conga - a lively Cuban dance or music style with a characteristic rhythm

Etymology

From Spanish 'conga', referring to a style of Cuban dance and music, combined with 'line'.
