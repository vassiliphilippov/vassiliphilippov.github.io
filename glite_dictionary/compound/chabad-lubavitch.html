Chabad-Lubavitch

1. NOUN

/ xɑːˈbɑd luˈbɑvɪtʃ / (AmE), / xəˈbɑːd ljuːˈbævɪtʃ / (BrE)
a major Hasidic Jewish movement known for outreach, hospitality, and emphasis on religious study and community involvement
major Hasidic Jewish movement

Examples

Chabad-Lubavitch centers can be found in cities around the world, welcoming Jews of all backgrounds.
She learned more about her heritage through classes at the Chabad-Lubavitch community center.
The Chabad-Lubavitch movement is renowned for its emphasis on outreach and education.

Etymology

From Hebrew חב"ד (Chabad), an acronym for 'wisdom, understanding, knowledge', and Lubavitch, the town in Russia where the movement was based.
