Canton flannel

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæn.tən ˈflæn.əl / (AmE), / ˈkæn.tən ˈflæn.əl / (BrE)
a heavy cotton fabric that is brushed to have a nap on one side, often used for sleepwear and bedding
heavy cotton fabric with nap on one side

Examples

The pajamas were made of Canton flannel, keeping her warm through the chilly night.
He preferred Canton flannel sheets because they felt soft against his skin.
She bought several yards of Canton flannel to sew cozy winter nightgowns.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: flannel - a soft woven fabric; general class of soft fabrics

Etymology

Named after Canton (now Guangzhou), China, where the fabric was originally made.
