economic crisis

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈkɑnəmɪk ˈkraɪsɪs / (AmE), / ˌiːkəˈnɒmɪk ˈkraɪsɪs / (BrE)
A period of severe economic downturn marked by widespread unemployment, low prices, and reduced levels of trade and investment.
period of severe economic downturn

Examples

After the collapse of several major banks, the country entered a prolonged economic crisis.
Government intervention was required to stabilize the market during the economic crisis of 2008.
Unemployment soared as the economic crisis deepened, affecting millions of families.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recession - a period of temporary economic decline
  • Degree Higher: depression - a prolonged and severe downturn in the economy
