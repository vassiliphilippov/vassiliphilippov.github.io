antidepressant drug

1. NOUN

/ ˌæn.t̬i.dɪˈprɛs.ənt drʌɡ / (AmE), / ˌæn.ti.dɪˈpres.ənt drʌɡ / (BrE)
a drug or medication used to treat depression and mood disorders, often by altering chemical activity in the brain
drug for treating depression
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed an antidepressant drug to help improve her mood.
Many antidepressant drugs require several weeks to show their full effects.
He experienced mild side effects after starting his antidepressant drug.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antidepressant - a medicine or substance that is used to treat depression

Etymology

From Latin prefix 'anti-' (against) and 'depress' (press down) plus scientific '-ant.'
