Hartford fern

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑrt.fərd fɝn / (AmE), / ˈhɑːt.fəd fɜːn / (BrE)
A delicate fern native to the eastern United States, with a twining stem, palmately-lobed sterile fronds, and forked fertile fronds.
a delicate eastern U.S. fern

Examples

The Hartford fern thrives in moist, shady woodlands throughout much of the eastern United States.
Botanists admired the unique forked fertile fronds of the Hartford fern during their field study.
She included a detailed drawing of the Hartford fern in her collection of native plants.

Etymology

Named for Hartford, a city in Connecticut, U.S., near one of the fern's native locations; 'fern' from Old English 'fearn'.
