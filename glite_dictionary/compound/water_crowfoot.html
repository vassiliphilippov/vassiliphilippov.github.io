water crowfoot

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔtər ˈkroʊˌfʊt / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə ˈkrəʊˌfʊt / (BrE)
an aquatic plant of ponds and slow streams, with submerged and floating leaves and white flowers, found in Europe and North America
aquatic plant with white flowers

Examples

In spring, the water crowfoot covers the pond with delicate white flowers.
The stream's ecosystem is supported by clusters of water crowfoot along the banks.
Floating leaves made it easy to identify the water crowfoot near the shallow edge.

Etymology

From 'water' + 'crowfoot', so named due to the shape of its leaves resembling a crow's foot.
