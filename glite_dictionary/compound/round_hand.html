round hand

1. NOUN

/ raʊnd hænd / (AmE), / raʊnd hand / (BrE)
a style of clear handwriting featuring large, round curves, commonly used in the 18th and 19th centuries
handwriting style with large round curves

Examples

The invitation was addressed in beautiful round hand script.
Many historical documents from the 18th century were written in round hand.
Learning round hand can help improve the legibility of your cursive writing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: copperplate - a handwriting style characterized by elegant, flowing lines, often used in formal documents

Etymology

From 'round' (shape) and 'hand' (handwriting style); popularized in the 17th and 18th centuries.
