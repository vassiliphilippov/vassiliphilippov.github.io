tectonic shift

1. NOUN

/ tɛkˈtɑnɪk ʃɪft / (AmE), / tɛkˈtɒnɪk ʃɪft / (BrE)
a major and significant change in a particular field, industry, or situation
major, significant change

Examples

The company underwent a tectonic shift when it adopted new digital strategies.
There has been a tectonic shift in public opinion on climate change over the past decade.
The emergence of streaming services caused a tectonic shift in the music industry.

Synset Examples

The tectonic shifts in the industry are hard to ignore.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paradigm shift - a fundamental change in approach or underlying assumptions

Etymology

From 'tectonic' (relating to structure of Earth's crust) and 'shift', used metaphorically since the late 20th century.
