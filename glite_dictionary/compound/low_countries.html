Low Countries

1. PROPN

/ loʊ ˈkʌntriz / (AmE), / ləʊ ˈkʌntriz / (BrE)
the lowland region of western Europe bordering the North Sea, comprising Belgium, Luxembourg, and the Netherlands
region in western Europe (Belgium, Luxembourg, Netherlands)

Examples

The Low Countries experienced rapid economic growth during the 16th century.
Artists from the Low Countries played a key role in the development of Renaissance painting.
Much of the Low Countries is vulnerable to flooding due to its low elevation.
