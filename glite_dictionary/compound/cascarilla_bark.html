cascarilla bark

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæskəˈrɪlə bɑrk / (AmE), / ˌkaskəˈrɪlə bɑːk / (BrE)
The aromatic bark of the cascarilla shrub, used as a tonic and in making incense.
aromatic bark used as tonic/incense

Examples

Many traditional healers use cascarilla bark for its aromatic and medicinal properties.
The distillery adds a small amount of cascarilla bark to enhance the flavor of certain spirits.
Burning cascarilla bark is a common practice in some religious ceremonies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cascarilla - The shrub or its bark used in flavoring and medicine

Etymology

From Spanish 'cascarilla', meaning 'little bark', referring to bark of the croton shrub (Croton eluteria).
