Irish Republic

1. PROPN

/ ˈaɪrɪʃ rɪˈpʌblɪk / (AmE), / ˈaɪ.rɪʃ rɪˈpʌb.lɪk / (BrE)
the state consisting of 26 of the 32 counties of Ireland, which achieved independence from the United Kingdom in 1921; also called the Republic of Ireland
the independent state of 26 Irish counties

Examples

The Irish Republic was formally declared independent in 1921.
Tourists from around the world visit the Irish Republic to explore its rich cultural history.
The political landscape of the Irish Republic changed dramatically after it joined the European Union.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Republic of Ireland - the official name for the independent Irish state since 1949
  • Antonym: Northern Ireland - the part of the island of Ireland that remains part of the United Kingdom
