line roulette

1. NOUN

/ laɪn ruːˈlɛt / (AmE), / laɪn ruːˈlɛt / (BrE)
A curve formed by the path of a point on a figure as it rolls without slipping around another fixed figure.
curve traced by rolling figure in geometry

Examples

The cycloid is a classic example of a line roulette in mathematics.
When constructing certain gears, engineers use the principles of line roulette to determine contact curves.
Studying the properties of a line roulette helps in understanding complex rolling motions in geometry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: roulette - a curve traced by a point fixed to a curve as it rolls along another curve

Etymology

From French 'roulette' (little wheel), referring to the rolling motion in geometry.
