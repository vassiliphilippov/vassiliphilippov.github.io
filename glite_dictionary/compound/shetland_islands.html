Shetland Islands

1. PROPN

/ ˈʃɛtlənd ˈaɪləndz / (AmE), / ˈʃɛtlənd ˈaɪləndz / (BrE)
a group of about 100 islands in the North Atlantic Ocean, situated off the north coast of Scotland, known for their unique wildlife, culture, and landscape
archipelago off the north coast of Scotland

Examples

Tourists travel to the Shetland Islands to experience their dramatic coastline and rich birdlife.
The Shetland Islands have a unique blend of Scottish and Norse cultural influences.
Ferries regularly connect mainland Scotland with the Shetland Islands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Orkney Islands - an archipelago to the southwest of the Shetland Islands, also north of Scotland

Etymology

From Old Norse 'Hjaltland', the Norse name for the islands.
