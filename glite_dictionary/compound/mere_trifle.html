mere trifle

1. NOUN

/ mɪr ˈtraɪfəl / (AmE), / mɪə ˈtraɪfəl / (BrE)
something trivial or unimportant; a thing of little value or significance
something unimportant or trivial

Examples

He dismissed the complaint as a mere trifle and moved on to more pressing matters.
Compared to their other problems, the late delivery was a mere trifle.
Don't be offended—it was only a mere trifle, nothing worth worrying about.

Synset Examples

He considered the issue a mere trifle, not worth his time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trifle - something of little value or importance
