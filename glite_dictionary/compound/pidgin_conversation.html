pidgin conversation

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪdʒɪn ˌkɑnvərˈseɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈpɪdʒɪn ˌkɒnvəˈseɪʃən / (BrE)
a conversation conducted in a simplified language that develops as a means of communication between speakers of different native languages
conversation in a simplified mixed language

Examples

At the busy port, traders engaged in pidgin conversation to make deals despite language barriers.
The children learned to hold a pidgin conversation with their classmates from different countries.
Their pidgin conversation often mixed English words with local dialect phrases.

Synset Examples

The market was lively, filled with pidgin conversations.
They communicated in pidgin English during their travels.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pidgin - a simplified language that develops between groups without a common language

Etymology

From 'pidgin', a simplified language, originally from 'business' as pronounced by Chinese speakers in English; 'conversation' meaning spoken exchange.
