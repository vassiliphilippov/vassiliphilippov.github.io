bell curve

1. NOUN

/ bɛl kɜrv / (AmE), / bɛl kɜːv / (BrE)
a graph or line that shows a normal distribution, with most occurrences clustering around the average and tapering off symmetrically toward the extremes
graph of a normal distribution

Examples

The grades in the class were distributed along a bell curve, with most students scoring near the middle.
IQ scores in the population typically follow a bell curve.
The company uses a bell curve to evaluate employee performance each year.

Synset Examples

In statistics class, the professor was a bell curve god.
She excelled at explaining the bell curve like a true bell curve god.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: normal distribution - a probability distribution that is symmetric about the mean

Etymology

Named for its bell-like shape; 'curve' from Latin 'curvus' (bent).
