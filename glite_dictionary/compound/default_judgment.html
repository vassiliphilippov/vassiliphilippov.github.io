default judgment

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈfɔlt ˈdʒʌdʒmənt / (AmE), / dɪˈfɔːlt ˈdʒʌdʒmənt / (BrE)
a judgment entered by a court in favor of the plaintiff when the defendant fails to respond or appear in court
judgment for plaintiff when defendant fails to appear
legal

Examples

The court awarded a default judgment after the defendant failed to respond to the lawsuit.
If you do not appear in court, the plaintiff may obtain a default judgment against you.
To avoid a default judgment, the company hired a lawyer to file an answer in time.
