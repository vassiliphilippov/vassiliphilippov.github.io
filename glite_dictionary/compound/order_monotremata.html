order Monotremata

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdər ˌmɑnəˈtrimətə / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə ˌmɒnəˈtriːmətə / (BrE)
a taxonomic order of egg-laying mammals, including the platypus and echidnas, coextensive with the subclass Prototheria
taxonomic order of egg-laying mammals

Examples

The platypus and the echidnas are the only living members of order Monotremata.
Scientists study order Monotremata to understand the evolution of mammals.
Order Monotremata is unique because its members lay eggs rather than give birth to live young.

Etymology

From Greek 'monos' (single) and 'trēma' (hole), referring to the single opening for excretion and reproduction.
