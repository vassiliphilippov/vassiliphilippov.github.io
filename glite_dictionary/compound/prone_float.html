prone float

1. NOUN

/ proʊn floʊt / (AmE), / prəʊn fləʊt / (BrE)
A swimming position where the body is kept face down in the water with arms stretched forward, usually for floating or gliding.
face-down floating position in swimming

Examples

The instructor demonstrated the prone float before asking the students to try it themselves.
Learning to maintain a steady prone float is an important basic swimming skill.
She practiced the prone float to improve her confidence in deep water.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: supine float - a floating position on the back, face up
