bourgie tastes

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʊrʒi teɪsts / (AmE), / ˈbʊːʒi teɪsts / (BrE)
Pretentious or upper-class preferences, especially in matters of style, food, or culture, often implying an affectation of wealth or sophistication.
pretentious or upper-class preferences
informal slang

Examples

Her bourgie tastes were evident in her love of designer handbags and gourmet coffee.
He was teased for his bourgie tastes in wine and artisanal cheese.
Despite growing up in a small town, she developed bourgie tastes after moving to the city.

Synset Examples

Her bourgie tastes were evident in her fashion choices.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bourgeois - conventional, middle-class, or materialistic attitude or values
  • Synonym: snobbish - characteristic of a snob; displaying condescension toward those considered lower in status
  • Antonym: down-to-earth - practical, realistic, or not pretentious

Etymology

From 'bourgie', a clipped form of 'bourgeois' (from French bourgeois, middle class), plus 'tastes'.
