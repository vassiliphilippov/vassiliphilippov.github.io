to feel jelak

1. VERB

/ fiːl dʒəˈlɑk / (AmE), / fiːl dʒəˈlæk / (BrE)
To experience a sense of fatigue, weariness, or annoyance, often after overindulgence or as a result of frustration.
feel fatigued or annoyed
informal

Examples

After eating too much cake, she began to feel jelak and lost her appetite.
He didn't want to join the discussion anymore because he started to feel jelak.
Whenever deadlines pile up, many of us feel jelak from the constant pressure.

Synset Examples

After the long hike, we were all feeling quite jelak.
His mood darkened as he realized how jelak he felt.

Etymology

From Malay 'jelak' (fed up, satiated or annoyed).
