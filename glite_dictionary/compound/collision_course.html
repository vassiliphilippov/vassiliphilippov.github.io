collision course

1. NOUN

/ kəˈlɪʒ.ən kɔrs / (AmE), / kəˈlɪʒ.ən kɔːs / (BrE)
A course of action or situation that is likely to result in conflict or confrontation if it continues unchanged.
likely path leading to conflict

Examples

The two companies are on a collision course over the patent dispute.
Her refusal to compromise put her on a collision course with management.
Environmental regulations and economic growth often seem to be set on a collision course.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conflict - serious disagreement or argument, typically a protracted one

3. NOUN

If they do not compromise soon, both nations could be on a collision course.
