sweet little time

1. NOUN

/ swit ˈlɪt̬əl taɪm / (AmE), / swiːt ˈlɪtl taɪm / (BrE)
a leisurely or deliberately slow amount of time taken to do something, especially when causing annoyance or impatience
leisurely or slow passage of time
informal

Examples

She took her sweet little time finishing dinner while everyone else waited.
He always seems to use his sweet little time before turning in his assignments.
Don't take your sweet little time—we need to leave soon!

Synset Examples

He always takes his sweet little time getting ready.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to take one's time - to do something without hurrying
