third cranial nerve

1. NOUN

/ ˌθɜrd ˈkreɪniəl nɜrv / (AmE), / ˌθɜːd ˈkreɪnɪəl nɜːv / (BrE)
the third pair of cranial nerves, also called the oculomotor nerves, which supply most of the muscles that move the eyeball and control the size of the pupil
the oculomotor nerve; nerve supplying eye muscles
medical

Examples

Damage to the third cranial nerve can cause double vision and drooping of the eyelid.
The third cranial nerve plays a crucial role in moving the eye upward, downward, and inward.
A neurological exam may test the function of the third cranial nerve by checking pupil response.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oculomotor nerve - the third cranial nerve; moves most of the eye muscles

Etymology

From 'cranial', relating to the cranium (skull), and 'nerve'; the third in the standard cranial nerve numbering.
