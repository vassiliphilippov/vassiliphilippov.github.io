sour orange

1. NOUN

/ saʊɚ ˈɔrɪndʒ / (AmE), / saʊə ˈɒrɪndʒ / (BrE)
an orange tree that produces bitter or acidic fruit, commonly used as rootstock in grafting or for its fruit in cooking and marmalade
orange tree with bitter or sour fruit

Examples

The tart flavor of sour orange juice is often used in marinades for meat dishes.
Many gardeners use sour orange trees as rootstock for grafting sweet orange varieties.
The marmalade was made from sour orange peel, giving it a tangy taste.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sweet orange - a type of orange tree producing sweet fruit

2. NOUN

/ ˌsaʊɚ ˈɔːrɪndʒ / (AmE), / ˌsaʊə ˈɒrɪndʒ / (BrE)
A highly acidic variety of orange, often used in marmalade and cooking for its tart flavor.
a highly acidic orange used in cooking

Examples

The recipe calls for two sour oranges to give the sauce its characteristic tang.
In Spain, sour oranges are commonly used to make traditional marmalade.
She prefers using sour orange juice instead of lemon in her marinade.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bitter orange - a variety of orange with a tart, bitter flavor used in cooking and marmalade
