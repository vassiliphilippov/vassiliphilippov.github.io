gomuti palm

1. NOUN

/ ɡoʊˈmuːti pɑm / (AmE), / ɡəˈmuːti pɑːm / (BrE)
A Malaysian feather palm whose base is densely covered with fibers; it yields a sweet sap used to make wine, and its trunk pith is a source of sago.
Malaysian palm yielding sap and sago

Examples

The gomuti palm is widely cultivated in Southeast Asia for its sweet sap and strong fibers.
Harvesters collect sago from the trunk of the gomuti palm for use in local cuisine.
Many villages rely on the gomuti palm as an important source of building materials and traditional wine.

Etymology

From Malay 'gemuti' or 'gomuti', the local name for the palm (Arenga pinnata).
