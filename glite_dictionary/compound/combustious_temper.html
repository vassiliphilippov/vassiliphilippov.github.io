combustious temper

1. NOUN

/ kəmˈbʌs.tʃəs ˈtɛm.pɚ / (AmE), / kəmˈbʌs.tʃəs ˈtɛm.pə / (BrE)
A temperament or disposition that is easily provoked to anger; a tendency to lose one's temper quickly.
prone to anger; easily angered temperament

Examples

Despite his combustious temper, he was respected for his honesty and work ethic.
She feared his combustious temper might ruin the negotiations if he wasn't careful.
The actor's combustious temper was infamous among the film crew.

Synset Examples

His combustious temper often led to arguments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fiery temper - a temperament that becomes angry or passionate quickly
  • Antonym: placid disposition - a temperament that is calm and not easily angered

Etymology

From 'combustious', derived from 'combust', from Latin 'combustus' (burned).
