crown glass

1. NOUN

/ kraʊn ɡlæs / (AmE), / kraʊn ɡlɑːs / (BrE)
A type of optical glass with low dispersion and a low refractive index, used especially in lenses.
optical glass with low dispersion and refractive index

Examples

The camera lens contains elements made from crown glass to minimize distortion.
Optical engineers often choose crown glass for its low refractive index in precision lenses.
Early telescope makers relied on crown glass before advanced materials were available.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: flint glass - optical glass with high dispersion and refractive index

Etymology

The term 'crown' may originate from the circular 'crown' shape formed during traditional glass-blowing methods.

2. NOUN

/ kraʊn ɡlæs / (AmE), / kraʊn ɡlɑːs / (BrE)
A type of glass made by blowing molten glass into a globe and spinning it into a flat disk, formerly used for windows.
type of flat spun window glass

Examples

Historic buildings often feature crown glass in their original window panes.
The unique swirling patterns of crown glass make it highly prized by conservators.
Unlike modern plate glass, crown glass has small variations in thickness and clarity.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: window glass - glass used for windows

Etymology

From 'crown', referring to the circular shape of the spun glass disk.
