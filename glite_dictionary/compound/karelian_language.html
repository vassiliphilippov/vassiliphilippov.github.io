Karelian language

1. NOUN

/ kəˈriːliən ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / kəˈriːlɪən ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
A Finnic language spoken by the people of Karelia, mainly in Russia and Finland.
Finnic language of Karelia

Examples

Few young people today are fluent in the Karelian language outside rural communities.
The preservation of the Karelian language is considered important for cultural identity in Karelia.
Courses in the Karelian language are offered at several universities in Finland.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Finnish - the Finnic language of Finland
  • Synonym: Estonian - the Finnic language of Estonia

Etymology

From Karelia, the region where the language is spoken, combined with 'language'.
