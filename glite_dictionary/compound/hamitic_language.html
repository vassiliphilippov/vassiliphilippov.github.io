Hamitic language

1. NOUN

/ hæˈmɪtɪk ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / hæˈmɪtɪk ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
an obsolete classification for a group of northern African languages once thought to be related to Semitic languages
obsolete northern African language group
archaic

Examples

The term Hamitic language is rarely used in modern linguistic studies.
Early explorers often grouped Berber and Cushitic as Hamitic language families.
Scholars have criticized the outdated concept of the Hamitic language group.

Etymology

From Hamitic, after Ham (a biblical figure), based on dated theories of language classification.
