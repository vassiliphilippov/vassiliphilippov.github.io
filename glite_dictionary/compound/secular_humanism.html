secular humanism

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛkjələr ˈhjuːmənɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈsɛkjʊlə ˈhjuːmənɪzəm / (BrE)
A doctrine or philosophy that emphasizes human reason, ethics, and justice, and specifically rejects religious beliefs and the supernatural as the basis of morality and decision-making.
philosophy emphasizing human reason, rejecting religion

Examples

Many supporters of secular humanism believe that ethical decisions should be based on reason and evidence, not religious doctrine.
The principles of secular humanism became influential in academic and political circles during the twentieth century.
Critics of secular humanism argue that it undermines traditional values rooted in religion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: humanism - philosophical stance focused on human values and concerns

Etymology

From 'secular' (not religious) + 'humanism' (emphasis on human values and concerns).
