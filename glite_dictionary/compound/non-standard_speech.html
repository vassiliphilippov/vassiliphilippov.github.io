non-standard speech

1. NOUN

/ nɑn ˈstændɚd spiːtʃ / (AmE), / nɒn ˈstændəd spiːtʃ / (BrE)
Speech that differs from the widely accepted and recognized forms used by native adult speakers in a particular community, often not conforming to the standard language norms.
speech deviating from standard language norms

Examples

Linguists often study non-standard speech to better understand language variation within a community.
Her use of non-standard speech sometimes made her stand out among her more formal classmates.
The teacher encouraged acceptance of non-standard speech as part of the region's cultural diversity.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: standard speech - speech conforming to widely accepted norms
