Malcolm stock

1. NOUN

/ ˈmælkəm stɑk / (AmE), / ˈmælkəm stɒk / (BrE)
any of various ornamental flowering plants of the genus Malcolmia, grown for their attractive flowers
ornamental plant of the Malcolmia genus

Examples

The garden features a bed of fragrant Malcolm stock in full bloom.
Horticulturists often recommend Malcolm stock for its hardiness and attractive flowers.
She planted several Malcolm stocks along the garden path to add color and scent.

Etymology

Named after the botanist William Malcolm; 'stock' refers to plants similar to those in the Matthiola genus, commonly called 'stock'.
