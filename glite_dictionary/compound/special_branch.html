Special Branch

1. NOUN

/ ˈspɛʃəl bræntʃ / (AmE), / ˈspɛʃəl brɑːn(t)ʃ / (BrE)
a division of the police force concerned with matters of national security and political crimes, especially involving terrorism or espionage
police department for political security
british

Examples

He was questioned by officers from the Special Branch about his travels abroad.
The Special Branch played a key role in investigating the espionage case.
Several members of Special Branch attended the high-profile security briefing.

Etymology

From the English words 'special' and 'branch', originally designating a separate section within the Metropolitan Police, UK, established in 1883.
