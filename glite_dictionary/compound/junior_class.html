junior class

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒuːniɚ klæs / (AmE), / ˈdʒuːniə klɑːs / (BrE)
the penultimate year or group of students in a high school or college, typically the third of four years in the US education system
second-to-last year in school or college (US)
american

Examples

She was elected president of the junior class at her college.
Every year, the junior class organizes the spring dance for the school.
Students in the junior class often begin preparing for college applications.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: senior class - final year/group of students in high school or college
  • Degree Lower: sophomore class - second year/group of students in high school or college
