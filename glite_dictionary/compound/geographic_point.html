geographic point

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒioʊˈɡræfɪk pɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˌdʒiː.əˈɡræf.ɪk pɔɪnt / (BrE)
a specific location or position on the surface of the Earth, usually defined by coordinates such as latitude and longitude
point or position on Earth's surface

Examples

Using GPS technology, you can pinpoint any geographic point on the globe with great accuracy.
Every geographic point can be given a unique set of latitude and longitude coordinates.
Researchers traveled to a remote geographic point in the Arctic to collect climate data.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: location - a particular place or position

Etymology

From Greek geōgraphia (earth description) + point (from Latin punctum, a dot or mark).
