Carolina jasmine

1. NOUN

/ kəˈraɪ.lə.nə ˈdʒæz.ə.min / (AmE), / kəˈrɛl.ɪ.nə ˈdʒæz.ɪ.miːn / (BrE)
a poisonous evergreen vine native to the southeastern United States, with fragrant yellow, funnel-shaped flowers
poisonous vine with yellow flowers

Examples

The garden was filled with the sweet scent of Carolina jasmine in the spring.
Be careful when planting Carolina jasmine, as it is poisonous to pets and humans.
The vibrant yellow flowers of Carolina jasmine add a bright splash of color to southern landscapes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yellow jessamine - another common name for Carolina jasmine
