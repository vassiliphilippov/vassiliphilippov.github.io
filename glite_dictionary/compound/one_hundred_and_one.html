one hundred and one

1. NUM

/ wʌn ˈhʌndrəd ənd wʌn / (AmE), / wʌn ˈhʌndrəd ənd wʌn / (BrE)
used to indicate a very large number or quantity; many
a large number; many

Examples

She had one hundred and one things to do before noon.
There are one hundred and one reasons to visit this city.
His explanation included one hundred and one excuses for being late.

Synset Examples

She has one hundred and one reasons to be happy.
This book covers one hundred and one ways to cook.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hundreds - a large but unspecified number of something
