bountiful crop

1. NOUN

/ ˈbaʊntɪfəl krɑp / (AmE), / ˈbaʊntɪfəl krɒp / (BrE)
a harvest that is especially large and plentiful
a large and plentiful harvest

Examples

After months of hard work, they finally enjoyed a bountiful crop of tomatoes in the summer.
The favorable weather this year resulted in a bountiful crop for local farmers.
Experts predict that the wheat fields will yield a particularly bountiful crop compared to last year.

Synset Examples

The farmer's crop was bountiful this year.
He found a seedful fruit that promised a bountiful harvest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bumper crop - an unusually large harvest
  • Antonym: poor crop - a harvest with little yield
