dovetail plane

1. NOUN

/ ˈdoʊvˌteɪl pleɪn / (AmE), / ˈdəʊvˌteɪl pleɪn / (BrE)
a woodworking tool used to cut the grooves or angles needed for dovetail joints
tool for making dovetail joint grooves

Examples

He used a dovetail plane to cut precise grooves for the new drawer.
A well-made dovetail plane can significantly speed up joinery work.
Traditional furniture makers often prefer a dovetail plane for crafting strong, interlocking joints.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dovetail joint - a woodworking joint shaped like a dove's tail, interlocking two boards

Etymology

From 'dovetail', referring to the type of joint, and 'plane', the tool for shaping wood.
