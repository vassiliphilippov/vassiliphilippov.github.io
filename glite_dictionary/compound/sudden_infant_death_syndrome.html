sudden infant death syndrome

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌdən ˈɪnfənt dɛθ ˌsɪnˌdroʊm / (AmE), / ˈsʌdn ˈɪnfənt dɛθ ˌsɪndrəʊm / (BrE)
the sudden and unexpected death of an apparently healthy baby during sleep, typically affecting infants less than one year old, for which no cause can be found, also known as cot death or SIDS.
unexpected infant death during sleep
medical

Examples

Doctors continue to research the causes of sudden infant death syndrome to help prevent future cases.
Educational campaigns have helped reduce the incidence of sudden infant death syndrome in many countries.
She became an advocate for safe sleep practices after losing her child to sudden infant death syndrome.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: SIDS - abbreviation for sudden infant death syndrome
  • Synonym: cot death - British term for sudden infant death syndrome
