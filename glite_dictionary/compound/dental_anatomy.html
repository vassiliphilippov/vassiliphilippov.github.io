dental anatomy

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛntəl əˈnætəmi / (AmE), / ˈdɛntəl əˈnætəmi / (BrE)
the branch of anatomy that studies the structure and form of teeth
study of the structure of teeth
medical

Examples

She is taking a course in dental anatomy to understand the structure of teeth in detail.
A solid grasp of dental anatomy is essential for students entering the field of dentistry.
The textbook provides detailed diagrams of dental anatomy for each type of tooth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: odontology - the scientific study of the structure and diseases of teeth

Etymology

From Latin 'dens, dentis' (tooth) and Greek 'anatomē' (dissection).
