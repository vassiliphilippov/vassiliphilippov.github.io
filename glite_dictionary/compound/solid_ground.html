solid ground

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɑlɪd ɡraʊnd / (AmE), / ˈsɒlɪd ɡraʊnd / (BrE)
the firm surface of the earth, especially as opposed to water or air
firm surface of the earth

Examples

After a long boat ride, he was relieved to set foot on solid ground again.
The hikers finally reached solid ground after crossing the shaky bridge.
Pilots prefer to have their aircraft on solid ground during storms.
