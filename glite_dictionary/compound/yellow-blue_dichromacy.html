yellow-blue dichromacy

1. NOUN

/ ˈjɛloʊ bluː daɪˈkroʊməsi / (AmE), / ˈjɛləʊ bluː daɪˈkrɒməsi / (BrE)
A type of color blindness characterized by difficulty distinguishing between yellow and blue hues.
form of color blindness affecting yellow and blue
medical

Examples

People with yellow-blue dichromacy often have trouble distinguishing between blue and green objects.
Doctors can diagnose yellow-blue dichromacy through specialized vision tests.
Yellow-blue dichromacy is rarer than red-green color blindness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tritanopia - a form of color blindness impacting blue-yellow discrimination

Etymology

From 'yellow' + 'blue', the colors affected, and 'dichromacy,' from Greek 'di-' (two) + 'khrōma' (color).
