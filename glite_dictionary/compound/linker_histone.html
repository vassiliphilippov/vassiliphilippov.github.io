linker histone

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪŋkər ˈhɪstoʊn / (AmE), / ˈlɪŋkə ˈhɪstəʊn / (BrE)
a simple protein (such as histone H1 or H5) that binds to nucleosomes, helping to package DNA into chromatin
protein that binds nucleosomes (H1/H5)
medical

Examples

The linker histone H1 helps organize DNA structure within the cell nucleus.
Researchers studied how linker histones influence chromatin compaction in different cell types.
Mutations in the linker histone gene can disrupt normal patterns of gene expression.

Etymology

From 'linker', meaning something that connects, and 'histone', from Greek 'histos' (web, tissue).
