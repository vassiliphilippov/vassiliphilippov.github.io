American magpie

1. NOUN

/ əˈmɛɹɪkən ˈmægˌpaɪ / (AmE), / əˈmɛɹɪkən ˈmægpʌɪ / (BrE)
a species of magpie found in the Rocky Mountains of North America, known for its black and white plumage and long tail.
a magpie species native to North America
american

Examples

We spotted an American magpie perched on a fence near the hiking trail.
The black-and-white feathers of the American magpie glistened in the morning sun.
Unlike other magpies, the American magpie is primarily found west of the Great Plains.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: magpie - a long-tailed bird of the crow family, known for its black and white plumage
