Himalayish language

1. NOUN

/ hɪˈmæləjɪʃ ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / hɪˈmaləjɪʃ ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
A branch of the Tibeto-Burman language family spoken in regions such as Tibet, Nepal, Bhutan, and Sikkim.
Tibeto-Burman language group of the Himalayas

Examples

Some scholars specialize in the study of Himalayish languages found across the mountain regions.
Many Himalayish languages are endangered as speakers shift to national languages like Nepali.
The diversity of Himalayish languages reflects the complex history and culture of the Himalayas.

Etymology

From Himalaya, the name of the mountain range, + -ish (forming adjectives) + language.
