job-control language

1. NOUN

/ dʒɑb kənˈtroʊl ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / dʒɒb kənˈtrəʊl ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
A specialized computer language used for describing job requirements and commands to an operating system.
computer language for operating system job control

Examples

Many mainframe computers require the use of a job-control language to schedule and execute tasks.
The manual provided detailed examples of job-control language statements for batch processing.
Learning the job-control language was essential for setting up automated jobs on the old system.
