surgical gown

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɝdʒɪkəl ɡaʊn / (AmE), / ˈsɜːdʒɪkəl ɡaʊn / (BrE)
A protective garment worn by surgeons and other operating room staff during medical procedures to maintain a sterile environment.
protective garment worn during surgery
medical

Examples

The surgeon put on a fresh surgical gown before beginning the operation.
All staff entering the operating room must wear a sterile surgical gown.
The hospital ordered new surgical gowns to ensure sufficient protection for its medical team.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scrubs - sanitary clothing worn by medical staff
