genus Anhinga

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ænˈhɪŋ.ɡə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ænˈhɪŋ.ɡə / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Anhingidae, consisting of water birds known as darters or snakebirds.
type genus of Anhingidae (darters)

Examples

The genus Anhinga contains several species distributed in tropical regions worldwide.
Researchers studied the feeding habits of birds in the genus Anhinga.
Fossil records indicate the presence of extinct species within the genus Anhinga.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Anhinga - a large aquatic bird of warm regions, also called snakebird
  • Degree Higher: Anhingidae - the family of birds including the anhingas or darters

Etymology

From Latin 'anhinga', used as the genus name after the Portuguese name for the bird, originally from Tupi-Guarani.
