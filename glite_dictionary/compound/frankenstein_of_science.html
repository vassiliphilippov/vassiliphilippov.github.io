Frankenstein of science

1. NOUN

/ ˈfræŋkənˌstaɪn əv ˈsaɪəns / (AmE), / ˈfraŋkənˌstaɪn əv ˈsaɪəns / (BrE)
a person in the scientific community whose work or ideas are regarded as dangerously radical or ethically questionable
controversial scientist or theorist

Examples

His theories were so unorthodox that some peers dubbed him the Frankenstein of science.
The media called her the Frankenstein of science after her controversial genetic experiments.
He wore the label Frankenstein of science as a badge of honor, challenging established norms.

Synset Examples

His ideas were so radical, he was called the Frankenstein of science.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Frankenstein - a person who creates something that brings disaster to its creator

Etymology

From 'Frankenstein,' the title character of Mary Shelley's 1818 novel, representing a scientist whose creation goes beyond control.
