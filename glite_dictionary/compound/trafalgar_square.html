Trafalgar Square

1. PROPN

/ trəˈfælɡər ˈskwɛr / (AmE), / trəˈfæləɡə ˈskweə / (BrE)
A large public square in central London known for its memorial to Admiral Nelson and frequent public events.
famous public square in London

Examples

Thousands of tourists gather at Trafalgar Square each year to admire its famous landmarks.
The New Year celebration in Trafalgar Square attracts large crowds from all over the world.
We met by the lion statues in Trafalgar Square before heading to the theater.

Etymology

Named after the Battle of Trafalgar (1805), where Admiral Nelson defeated the French and Spanish fleets.
