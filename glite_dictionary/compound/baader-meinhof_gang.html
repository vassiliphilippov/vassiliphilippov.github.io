Baader-Meinhof Gang

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑːdər ˈmaɪnhɔːf ɡæŋ / (AmE), / ˈbɑːdə ˈmaɪnhɒf ɡæŋ / (BrE)
a radical left-wing terrorist group active in Germany from 1968 to 1977, also known as the Red Army Faction
German radical left-wing terrorist group (1968–1977)

Examples

The Baader-Meinhof Gang orchestrated a series of bombings in West Germany during the 1970s.
Police launched a nationwide search to apprehend members of the Baader-Meinhof Gang.
The Baader-Meinhof Gang gained international notoriety for their acts of political violence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Red Army Faction - alternative name for the Baader-Meinhof Gang

Etymology

Named after founding members Andreas Baader and Ulrike Meinhof.
