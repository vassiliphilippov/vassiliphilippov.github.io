king fern

1. NOUN

/ kɪŋ fɜrn / (AmE), / kɪŋ fɜːn / (BrE)
a large fern of worldwide distribution with deep roots and upright, doubly compound tufted fronds
large widely distributed fern with tufted fronds

Examples

The king fern can grow particularly large in rainforests, reaching impressive heights.
Botanists often study the structure of the king fern due to its unique fronds.
Some species of king fern are popular in large gardens for their dramatic appearance.

2. NOUN

/ kɪŋ fɝn / (AmE), / kɪŋ fɜːn / (BrE)
a large fern native to the rainforests of tropical Australia, New Zealand, and South Africa, known for its impressive size
a large tropical fern of Australia, NZ, and South Africa

Examples

The king fern can grow fronds that reach up to five meters in length.
We admired a massive king fern while hiking through the New Zealand rainforest.
Botanists consider the king fern to be one of the oldest surviving plant species.
