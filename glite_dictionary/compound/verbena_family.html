verbena family

1. NOUN

/ vərˈbiːnə ˈfæməli / (AmE), / vəˈbiːnə ˈfæməli / (BrE)
a family of tropical and subtropical herbs, shrubs, and trees native mainly to the New World, known scientifically as Verbenaceae
family of tropical and subtropical plants (Verbenaceae)

Examples

The verbena family includes many ornamental plants popular in gardens worldwide.
Several medicinal herbs belong to the verbena family due to their beneficial properties.
Scientists recently discovered new species within the verbena family in South America.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: mint family - family of aromatic herbs (Lamiaceae), sometimes mistaken for Verbenaceae

Etymology

From Latin 'verbena' (sacred bough); used for the family's type genus.
