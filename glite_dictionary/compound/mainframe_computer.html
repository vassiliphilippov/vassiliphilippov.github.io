mainframe computer

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪnˌfreɪm kəmˈpjuːtər / (AmE), / ˈmeɪnfreɪm kəmˈpjuːtə / (BrE)
a large, powerful computer used primarily by large organizations for bulk data processing, typically serving many users simultaneously.
large, powerful server computer

Examples

The bank relies on a mainframe computer to process millions of transactions per day.
Many universities used a mainframe computer for research in the 1970s.
Although personal computers have become more common, some industries still use a mainframe computer for critical operations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: server - a computer or system that provides resources, data, or services to other computers

Etymology

From 'mainframe' (main structural frame) referring to early large-scale computer housings.
