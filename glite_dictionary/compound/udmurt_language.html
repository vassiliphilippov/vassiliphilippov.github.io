Udmurt language

1. NOUN

/ udˈmʊrt ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ʊdˈmʊːt ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Finno-Ugric language spoken by the Udmurt people, mainly in the Udmurt Republic of Russia
language of the Udmurt people

Examples

The Udmurt language is still spoken by around half a million people in Russia.
She is fluent in both Russian and Udmurt language.
Efforts to preserve the Udmurt language include publishing children's books and newspapers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Finno-Ugric - branch of the Uralic language family including Finnish, Hungarian, and Udmurt

Etymology

Udmurt is from the self-designation 'Udmurt', whose origin is uncertain; 'language' from Latin 'lingua'.
