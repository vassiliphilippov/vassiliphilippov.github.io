diapensia family

1. NOUN

/ ˌdaɪəˈpɛnsiə ˈfæməli / (AmE), / ˌdaɪəˈpensiə ˈfamɪli / (BrE)
A family of small, evergreen plants found in northern temperate regions, sometimes classified in its own order, Diapensiales.
family of low evergreen plants

Examples

The diapensia family includes small evergreen shrubs adapted to cold, rocky soils.
Botanists sometimes place the diapensia family in its own order, Diapensiales.
Some species in the diapensia family are cultivated for their resilience in alpine gardens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Diapensiaceae - the scientific name for the diapensia family

Etymology

From Diapensia (genus name), of uncertain origin, used in botanical Latin.
