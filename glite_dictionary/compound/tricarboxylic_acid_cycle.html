tricarboxylic acid cycle

1. NOUN

/ traɪˌkɑrˌbɒkˈsɪlɪk ˈæsɪd ˈsaɪkəl / (AmE), / traɪˌkɑːbɒkˈsɪlɪk ˈæsɪd ˈsaɪkl̩ / (BrE)
a series of chemical reactions in cells, especially in mitochondria, that oxidize acetyl compounds to produce energy, also known as the Krebs cycle or citric acid cycle
cellular process for energy production
medical

Examples

The tricarboxylic acid cycle is essential for energy production in almost all aerobic organisms.
Students learned how the tricarboxylic acid cycle connects to other metabolic pathways.
Disruptions in the tricarboxylic acid cycle can lead to various metabolic disorders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Krebs cycle - another name for the tricarboxylic acid cycle
  • Synonym: citric acid cycle - alternative name for the same metabolic cycle

Etymology

tricarboxylic from 'tri-' (three) + 'carboxylic', indicating three carboxyl groups; cycle refers to the series of reactions.
