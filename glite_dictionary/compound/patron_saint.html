patron saint

1. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪtrən ˌseɪnt / (AmE), / ˈpeɪtrən ˌseɪnt / (BrE)
a saint regarded as the special guardian or protector of a particular group, place, or activity
saint protector of a group or place

Examples

Saint George is the patron saint of England.
Many sailors pray to their patron saint before long voyages.
St. Patrick is widely celebrated as the patron saint of Ireland.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: protector - one who protects someone or something

Etymology

From Latin 'patronus' (protector, defender) and 'sanctus' (holy, saint).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪ.trən ˌseɪnt / (AmE), / ˈpeɪ.trən ˌseɪnt / (BrE)
A saint regarded as the protective guardian of a particular place, group, profession, or cause.
protective saint of a group or cause

Examples

Saint George is the patron saint of England.
Many hospitals were named after their patron saint.
She joked that her cat was the patron saint of lazy afternoons.

Synset Examples

Saint Jude is the patron saint of lost causes.
He considers his mother the patron saint of his success.
She feels her mentor is her patron saint in her career.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guardian angel - an angel believed to guide and protect a particular person

Etymology

From Latin 'patronus' (protector) + 'saint' (from Latin 'sanctus').
