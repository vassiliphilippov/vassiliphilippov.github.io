right of election

1. NOUN

/ raɪt əv ɪˈlɛkʃən / (AmE), / raɪt əv ɪˈlɛkʃən / (BrE)
in probate law: the legal right of a surviving spouse to choose between taking what is provided to them under the will or the share of the estate granted by law.
legal right to choose inheritance share
legal

Examples

The surviving spouse decided to exercise her right of election after reading the terms of the will.
If you are unsure about the right of election, consult a probate lawyer for advice.
Under the law, the right of election ensures that a spouse cannot be disinherited entirely.
