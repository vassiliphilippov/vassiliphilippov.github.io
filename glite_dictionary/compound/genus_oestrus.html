genus Oestrus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈiː.strəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈiː.strəs / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Oestridae, comprising sheep botflies, whose larvae are parasitic in sheep.
type genus of Oestridae (sheep botflies)

Examples

The larvae of genus Oestrus infest sheep and can cause significant health problems.
Research on genus Oestrus has improved our understanding of parasitic infections in livestock.
Farmers often monitor sheep for signs of infestation by members of the genus Oestrus.

Etymology

From Latin 'oestrus' (gadfly, frenzy), used in taxonomy for sheep botflies.
