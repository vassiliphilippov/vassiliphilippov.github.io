Conservative Jew

1. NOUN

/ kənˈsɝːvətɪv dʒuː / (AmE), / kənˈsɜːvətɪv dʒuː / (BrE)
A member of a branch of Judaism who maintains some traditional Jewish laws while adapting others to fit modern circumstances.
member of Conservative Judaism

Examples

My neighbor is a Conservative Jew who attends synagogue every Saturday.
As a Conservative Jew, Rachel observes kosher dietary laws but uses technology on the Sabbath.
The community center offers programs for Orthodox, Reform, and Conservative Jew members.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Reform Jew - Jew who adheres to the beliefs and practices of Reform Judaism
  • Degree Higher: Orthodox Jew - Jew who strictly observes traditional Jewish law and customs
