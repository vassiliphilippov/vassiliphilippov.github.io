Karakalpak language

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑrəˈkɑlpæk ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌkærəkælˈpæk ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Turkic language spoken by the Karakalpak people of Central Asia, primarily in Uzbekistan
Turkic language of the Karakalpak people

Examples

She is fluent in both Russian and Karakalpak language.
The Karakalpak language uses the Cyrillic script in Uzbekistan.
Researchers are working to preserve the Karakalpak language for future generations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Kazakh language - a closely related Turkic language spoken in Kazakhstan

Etymology

From the name Karakalpak (Turkic: 'black hat') + English 'language'.
