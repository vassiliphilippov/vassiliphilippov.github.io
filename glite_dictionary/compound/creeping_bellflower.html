creeping bellflower

1. NOUN

/ ˈkripɪŋ ˈbɛlˌflaʊɚ / (AmE), / ˈkriːpɪŋ ˈbɛlˌflaʊə / (BrE)
A European perennial plant with creeping rootstocks and nodding spikelike racemes of blue to violet, bell-shaped flowers (Campanula rapunculoides).
European perennial with blue-violet bell-shaped flowers

Examples

The creeping bellflower quickly spreads through gardens because of its underground stems.
Clusters of creeping bellflower brightened the edge of the woodland trail.
She planted creeping bellflower for its attractive blue blossoms, unaware how invasive it could become.
