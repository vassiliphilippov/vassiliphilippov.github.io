Mariana Islands

1. PROPN

/ ˌmær.iˈæn.ə ˈaɪ.ləndz / (AmE), / ˌmær.iˈæn.ɪ ˈaɪ.ləndz / (BrE)
a chain of coral and volcanic islands in Micronesia, including Guam and the Northern Mariana Islands, located in the western Pacific Ocean.
island chain in Micronesia, Pacific Ocean

Examples

Many tourists visit the Mariana Islands for their beautiful beaches and clear blue waters.
The United States administers Guam and the Northern Mariana Islands as territories.
The Mariana Islands were discovered by European explorers in the 16th century.

Etymology

Named after Mariana of Austria, widow of Philip IV of Spain.
