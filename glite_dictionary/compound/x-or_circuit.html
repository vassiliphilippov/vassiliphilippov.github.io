X-OR circuit

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛks ɔr ˌsɝː.kɪt / (AmE), / ˈɛks ɔː ˌsɜː.kɪt / (BrE)
an electronic circuit that produces an output only when its inputs are different, implementing the exclusive OR (XOR) logical operation
exclusive OR logic gate circuit

Examples

The X-OR circuit is used to detect when two input bits are different.
In digital electronics, an X-OR circuit is essential for building adders and comparators.
Engineers often refer to the exclusive OR gate as the X-OR circuit in technical manuals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: XOR gate - a logic gate that performs exclusive OR operation
  • Antonym: AND gate - a logic gate that outputs true only if all inputs are true
  • Antonym: OR gate - a logic gate that outputs true if any input is true

Etymology

Combination of 'X', an abbreviation for 'exclusive', and 'OR', referring to the logical OR operation.
