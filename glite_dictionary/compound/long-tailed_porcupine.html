long-tailed porcupine

1. NOUN

/ lɔŋ teɪld ˈpɔrkjupaɪn / (AmE), / lɒŋ teɪld ˈpɔːkjʊpaɪn / (BrE)
a species of porcupine native to Borneo and Sumatra, characterized by short spines and a long tail
porcupine species with a long tail

Examples

The long-tailed porcupine is known for its unusually long tail compared to other members of its family.
Researchers in Borneo observed a long-tailed porcupine foraging for fruit in the rainforest at night.
Unlike most porcupines, the long-tailed porcupine has relatively short spines and is an agile climber.
