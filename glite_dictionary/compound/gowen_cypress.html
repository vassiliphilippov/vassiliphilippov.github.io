Gowen cypress

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡoʊ.ən ˈsaɪ.prəs / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊ.ən ˈsɪ.prəs / (BrE)
a small or sometimes shrubby evergreen tree (Cupressus goveniana) native to California, often used as an ornamental plant
ornamental Californian evergreen tree

Examples

The Gowen cypress is prized for its attractive foliage in California gardens.
Wild populations of Gowen cypress are now quite rare and protected.
Botanists study how soil conditions affect the growth of Gowen cypress.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Monterey cypress - a related cypress species native to coastal California

Etymology

Named after H. N. Gowen, the discoverer, and from Latinized form of 'cypress.'
