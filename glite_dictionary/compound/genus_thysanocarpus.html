genus Thysanocarpus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌθaɪ.sə.noʊˈkɑːr.pəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌθaɪ.sə.nəˈkɑː.pəs / (BrE)
A small genus of herbaceous plants native to upland regions of the Pacific coast of North America, belonging to the mustard family (Brassicaceae).
genus of Pacific coast herbs

Examples

The genus Thysanocarpus includes several species commonly known as fringepods.
Botanists have long studied the distribution of genus Thysanocarpus across California's upland regions.
Seeds from genus Thysanocarpus were collected for conservation purposes.

Etymology

From Greek thysanos (tassel, fringe) and karpos (fruit), referring to the fringed fruit of the plant.
