uranyl oxalate

1. NOUN

/ jʊˈreɪnɪl ɑkˈsæleɪt / (AmE), / jʊˈreɪnɪl ɒkˈsæleɪt / (BrE)
a yellow crystalline salt formed by the reaction of uranyl compounds with oxalic acid, typically used in chemical analysis
a uranium-containing salt used in chemistry

Examples

The laboratory prepared a sample of uranyl oxalate for the analysis of uranium content.
When heated, uranyl oxalate decomposes to produce uranium oxide and carbon dioxide.
The yellow color of uranyl oxalate is a characteristic feature used in its identification.

Etymology

From uranyl (uranium oxide ion) + oxalate (from oxalic acid).
