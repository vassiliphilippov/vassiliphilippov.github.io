genus Draba

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˈdreɪbə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈdrɑːbə / (BrE)
a large genus of low-growing, tufted herbs found in temperate and Arctic regions, commonly known as whitlow-grasses
genus of tufted temperate herbs (whitlow-grasses)

Examples

The genus Draba comprises many species that thrive in Alpine and Arctic environments.
Botanists studied the genus Draba to understand how plants adapt to cold climates.
Several members of the genus Draba are commonly called whitlow-grasses due to their appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Draba - the plant genus commonly called whitlow-grass

Etymology

From Draba, the New Latin genus name, derived from Greek drabē (sharp, acrid plant), used in scientific naming.
