Spanish onion

1. NOUN

/ ˈspænɪʃ ˈʌnjən / (AmE), / ˈspænɪʃ ˈʌnjən / (BrE)
A large, mild, and juicy variety of onion, often eaten raw in salads or on sandwiches.
large, mild, juicy onion variety

Examples

The chef used a thinly sliced Spanish onion to add sweetness to the salad.
You can serve burgers with rings of raw Spanish onion for extra flavor and crunch.
At the market, she chose a big, juicy Spanish onion for tonight's dinner.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: onion - a round vegetable with a strong taste and smell, composed of many layers

Etymology

Named for its association with onions grown in Spain or similar varieties.
