black bass

1. NOUN

/ blæk ˈbæs / (AmE), / blæk ˈbɑːs / (BrE)
Any of various American freshwater game fish of the sunfish family, widely distributed and highly valued by anglers, especially species such as the largemouth bass and smallmouth bass.
American freshwater game fish (sunfish family)

Examples

The black bass is a favorite target for many freshwater anglers across North America.
Some lakes are stocked regularly with black bass to support recreational fishing.
The black bass puts up a strong fight, making it exciting to catch.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: largemouth bass - a species of black bass known for its large mouth
  • Degree Lower: smallmouth bass - a species of black bass with a smaller mouth and more slender body

a type of North American freshwater fish

Examples
