bandicoot rat

1. NOUN

/ ˈbændɪˌkut ræt / (AmE), / ˈbændɪˌkuːt ræt / (BrE)
a large, burrowing rat with a scaly tail, found in India and Sri Lanka
large burrowing rat of South Asia

Examples

The fields were damaged by a family of bandicoot rats digging extensive tunnels.
Farmers in rural India struggle to control the bandicoot rat population.
Unlike smaller rodents, the bandicoot rat can cause significant damage to crops.

Etymology

From Telugu 'pandikokku' meaning 'pig-rat'.
