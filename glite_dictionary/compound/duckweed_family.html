duckweed family

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʌkˌwid ˈfæməli / (AmE), / ˈdʌkwiːd ˈfæmɪli / (BrE)
a family of small, free-floating aquatic plants, typically found in ponds and still waters, known for their simple, thalloid bodies
family of small floating aquatic plants

Examples

The duckweed family includes several species that are important for aquatic ecosystems.
Scientists study the duckweed family to understand water pollution and nutrient cycles.
Members of the duckweed family can reproduce rapidly and cover entire ponds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Lemnaceae - the scientific name for the duckweed family
