genus Haematoxylum

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒiːnəs ˌhiːməˈtɒksɪləm / (AmE), / ˌdʒiːnəs ˌhiːməˈtɒksɪləm / (BrE)
A small genus of tropical American spiny, bushy shrubs or trees, known for species such as the logwood tree.
genus of tropical American shrubs or trees

Examples

The genus Haematoxylum includes the logwood tree, which is valued for its dye-producing wood.
Many explorers collected specimens from the genus Haematoxylum during their expeditions in Central America.
Botanists have identified several species within the genus Haematoxylum that thrive in tropical climates.

Etymology

From Greek 'haima' meaning 'blood' and 'xylon' meaning 'wood', referring to the red dye derived from some species.
