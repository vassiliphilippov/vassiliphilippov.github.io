intravenous injection

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.trəˈvinəs ɪnˈdʒɛk.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪn.trəˈviː.nəs ɪnˈdʒek.ʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of injecting a substance directly into a vein, typically by means of a syringe or needle
injection directly into a vein
medical

Examples

The nurse administered the antibiotic through an intravenous injection to ensure rapid absorption.
He was nervous about receiving an intravenous injection before the procedure, but it was over quickly.
Some chemotherapy treatments are delivered by intravenous injection in the hospital.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: injection - the act of putting a liquid, especially a drug, into someone’s body using a needle

Etymology

intravenous from Latin 'intra-' (within) + 'vena' (vein) + -ous; injection from Latin 'injectio', from 'injicere' (to throw in).
