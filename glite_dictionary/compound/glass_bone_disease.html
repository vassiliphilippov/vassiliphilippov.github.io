glass bone disease

1. NOUN

/ ɡlæs boʊn dɪˈziz / (AmE), / ɡlɑːs bəʊn dɪˈziːz / (BrE)
a rare medical condition in which the bones are extremely fragile and easily broken, often causing frequent fractures and limited physical activity
medical condition causing brittle bones
medical

Examples

Despite having glass bone disease, she has learned to adapt and enjoy daily life.
Children with glass bone disease are especially prone to frequent fractures.
Doctors recommend physical therapy for patients with glass bone disease to strengthen muscles and protect the bones.

Synset Examples

Her glass bone disease limits her physical activities.
He was diagnosed with glass bone disease at a young age.
Living with glass bone disease requires careful movement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: osteogenesis imperfecta - medical term for glass bone disease, genetic disorder causing brittle bones
