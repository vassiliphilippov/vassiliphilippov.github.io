over-the-counter

1. ADJ

/ ˌoʊvɚ ðə ˈkaʊntɚ / (AmE), / ˌəʊvə ðə ˈkaʊntə / (BrE)
(of securities) not listed or traded on a stock exchange but bought and sold through a network of dealers.
(of securities) not traded on a stock exchange

Examples

The company raised capital by issuing over-the-counter shares instead of listing on a major exchange.
Many small technology firms are traded over-the-counter due to their size and limited reporting requirements.
Some investors prefer buying over-the-counter stocks because of the potential for higher returns, despite the risks.

Synset Examples

over-the-counter stocks

Related Terms

  • Antonym: listed - (of securities) traded on a stock exchange

2. ADJ

/ ˌoʊvɚ ðə ˈkaʊntɚ / (AmE), / ˌəʊvə ðə ˈkaʊntə / (BrE)
(of a medicine) available for purchase without a prescription from a doctor.
available without prescription

Examples

Many pain relievers are sold over-the-counter in pharmacies and supermarkets.
You should check if the allergy medicine is over-the-counter or needs a prescription.
The pharmacist recommended an over-the-counter remedy for my cold symptoms.

Synset Examples

nonprescription drugs
an over-the-counter cold remedy

Related Terms

  • Antonym: prescription-only - (of a medicine) requiring a prescription from a doctor
