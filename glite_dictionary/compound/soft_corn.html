soft corn

1. NOUN

/ sɑft kɔrn / (AmE), / sɒft kɔːn / (BrE)
A type of corn with kernels composed almost entirely of soft starch rather than hard or flinty starch.
corn with mostly soft starch kernels

Examples

The farmer planted soft corn because it grinds easily into flour.
Researchers are studying how soft corn performs in different climates.
Some tortilla recipes specifically call for soft corn due to its texture.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: flint corn - a type of corn with hard, flinty kernels
