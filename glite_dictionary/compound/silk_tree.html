silk tree

1. NOUN

/ sɪlk triː / (AmE), / sɪlk triː / (BrE)
an attractive tree native to Asia with bipinnate leaves and flowers having long, silky stamens, often cultivated for ornament.
ornamental Asiatic tree with silky flowers

Examples

The silk tree in their backyard blooms with delicate pink flowers each summer.
Gardeners often plant the silk tree for its ornamental value and shade.
A row of silk trees lines the entrance to the botanical garden.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mimosa - another name for the silk tree, especially Albizia julibrissin
