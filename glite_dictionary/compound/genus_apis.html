genus Apis

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˈeɪpɪs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈeɪpɪs / (BrE)
The genus in the family Apidae that comprises the honeybees, including the European honeybee (Apis mellifera).
genus of honeybees

Examples

The genus Apis includes several species, most notably those that produce honey.
Scientists study the genus Apis to understand bee behavior and pollination.
Members of the genus Apis are crucial pollinators in many ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: honeybee - a bee of the genus Apis, known for producing honey

Etymology

From Latin 'apis' meaning 'bee', used in scientific taxonomy.
