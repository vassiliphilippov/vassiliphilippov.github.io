New York State Barge Canal

1. PROPN

/ nuː jɔrk steɪt bɑrdʒ kəˈnæl / (AmE), / njuː jɔːk steɪt bɑːdʒ kəˈnæl / (BrE)
a system of canals in New York State connecting the Great Lakes with the Hudson River and Lake Champlain, historically significant for transportation and commerce
canal system connecting Great Lakes and Hudson River

Examples

Freight was shipped across the New York State Barge Canal to reach eastern ports.
The New York State Barge Canal played a vital role in the region's early 20th-century development.
Many towns grew along the New York State Barge Canal due to increased commercial activity.
