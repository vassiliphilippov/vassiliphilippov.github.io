genus Homo

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˈhoʊmoʊ / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈhəʊməʊ / (BrE)
The genus of hominids that includes modern humans and their close extinct relatives, such as Homo erectus and Homo habilis.
genus including humans and close relatives

Examples

The genus Homo includes modern humans as well as several extinct species.
Scientists debate the exact number of extinct species in the genus Homo.
Fossils attributed to the genus Homo have been found throughout Africa and Eurasia.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Homo sapiens - the species name for modern humans
  • Degree Lower: Homo erectus - an extinct species of the genus Homo

Etymology

From Latin 'Homo' meaning 'man' or 'human being'.
