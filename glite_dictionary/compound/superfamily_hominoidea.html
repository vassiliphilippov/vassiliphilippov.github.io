superfamily Hominoidea

1. NOUN

/ ˌsuː.pɚˌfæm.əˈliː hɒˌmɪnˈɔɪdi.ə / (AmE), / ˌsuː.pəˌfæm.ɪˈliː hɒˌmɪnˈɔɪdi.ə / (BrE)
A taxonomic group in biological classification that includes the anthropoid apes and humans.
taxonomic group of apes and humans

Examples

The superfamily Hominoidea encompasses both modern humans and our closest ape relatives.
Scientists debate the evolutionary relationships within the superfamily Hominoidea.
Fossil evidence sheds light on how the superfamily Hominoidea diversified over millions of years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hominoidea - The superfamily including anthropoid apes and humans

Etymology

From New Latin: 'superfamily' indicating a rank above family, and 'Hominoidea' (from Latin 'homo' meaning man, and '-oidea' for resemblance).
