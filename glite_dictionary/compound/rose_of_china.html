Rose of China

1. NOUN

/ roʊz əv ˈtʃaɪnə / (AmE), / rəʊz əv ˈtʃaɪnə / (BrE)
A large, showy shrub or small tree native to Asia, especially valued for its large single or double red to deep-red flowers.
shrubby Asian plant with large red flowers

Examples

The Rose of China is a popular choice for gardens in warm climates.
Her front yard boasted a magnificent Rose of China in full bloom.
Botanists admire the Rose of China for its striking, deep-red blossoms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hibiscus - a genus of flowering plants including the Rose of China
