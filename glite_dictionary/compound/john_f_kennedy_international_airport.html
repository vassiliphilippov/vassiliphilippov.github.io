John F. Kennedy International Airport

1. PROPN

/ dʒɑn ɛf ˈkɛnədi ˌɪntərˈnæʃənəl ˈɛrˌpɔrt / (AmE), / dʒɒn ɛf ˈkɛnədi ˌɪntəˈnæʃnəl ˈeəpɔːt / (BrE)
A major airport located in southeastern Queens, New York City, serving as one of the primary airports for international and domestic flights to and from New York City.
major airport in New York City

Examples

Our flight to London departs from John F. Kennedy International Airport early tomorrow morning.
After landing at John F. Kennedy International Airport, we took a taxi into Manhattan.
Many international travelers pass through John F. Kennedy International Airport each year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: airport - a place where aircraft regularly take off and land, with buildings for passengers

Etymology

Named after John F. Kennedy, the 35th President of the United States, in 1963.
