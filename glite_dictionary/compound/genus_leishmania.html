genus Leishmania

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs liʃˈmeɪniə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs liʃˈmeɪniə / (BrE)
A genus of flagellate protozoa that are responsible for causing leishmaniasis in humans and animals.
parasitic protozoan genus causing leishmaniasis
medical

Examples

Scientists are studying the genus Leishmania to develop better treatments for leishmaniasis.
Infected sandflies transmit the genus Leishmania to animals and humans through their bites.
Identification of the genus Leishmania in blood samples is crucial for accurate diagnosis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leishmaniasis - disease caused by Leishmania parasites
  • Synonym: Leishmania - a genus of protozoan parasites

Etymology

From New Latin; named after Scottish pathologist William Boog Leishman (1865–1926).
