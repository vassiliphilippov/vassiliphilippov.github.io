22-karat gold

1. NOUN

/ ˌtwɛntiˈtuː ˈkærət goʊld / (AmE), / ˌtwɛntiˈtjuː ˈkærət gəʊld / (BrE)
An alloy consisting of approximately 87 percent gold, commonly used in jewelry.
gold alloy with 87 percent purity

Examples

She bought a beautiful necklace made of 22-karat gold for her wedding.
The ring's value increased because it was crafted from 22-karat gold.
Many traditional ornaments in India are made with 22-karat gold due to its strength and luster.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: 24-karat gold - pure gold (99.9% gold content)
  • Degree Lower: 18-karat gold - gold alloy containing about 75% gold

Etymology

From 'karat,' a term for gold purity from Italian 'carato,' ultimately from Arabic 'qirat.'
