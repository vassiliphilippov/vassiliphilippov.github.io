shore leave

1. NOUN

/ ʃɔr liv / (AmE), / ʃɔː liːv / (BrE)
official permission for a sailor or naval officer to go ashore while a ship is in port
sailor's official leave when in port

Examples

After months at sea, the crew finally enjoyed their shore leave in Singapore.
He spent most of his shore leave exploring the city with his fellow sailors.
Regulations required all sailors to return to the ship at the end of their shore leave.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leave - official permission to be away from duties
