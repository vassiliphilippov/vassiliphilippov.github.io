atopic eczema

1. NOUN

/ əˈtoʊpɪk ˈɛksɪmə / (AmE), / əˈtɒpɪk ˈɛksɪmə / (BrE)
A chronic skin condition characterized by inflammation, redness, and itching, often associated with allergies and atopy.
chronic allergic skin inflammation
medical

Examples

Many children with atopic eczema experience intense itching and discomfort, especially at night.
The doctor recommended moisturizing creams to help manage her atopic eczema.
Research shows a genetic link in families prone to allergies and atopic eczema.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dermatitis - inflammation of the skin
  • Synonym: eczema - non-specific term for an inflammatory skin condition

Etymology

From Greek 'atopos' (out of place, unusual) and Latin 'exzyma' (boiling out, referring to skin eruption).
