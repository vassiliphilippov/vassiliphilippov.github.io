Kaffir pox

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæfɚ ˌpɑks / (AmE), / ˈkæfə ˌpɒks / (BrE)
A mild form of smallpox caused by a less virulent strain of the virus, historically reported in southern Africa.
a mild smallpox variant
offensive medical

Examples

During the nineteenth century, Kaffir pox was occasionally reported in rural communities.
The symptoms of Kaffir pox were less severe than those of classical smallpox.
Some historical texts describe Kaffir pox as having a lower fatality rate compared to typical smallpox.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smallpox - an infectious disease caused by the variola virus

Etymology

From 'Kaffir,' a now-offensive term used in colonial-era southern Africa, and 'pox.'
