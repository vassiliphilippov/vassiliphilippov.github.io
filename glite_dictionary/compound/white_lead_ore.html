white lead ore

1. NOUN

/ waɪt liːd ɔɹ / (AmE), / waɪt liːd ɔː / (BrE)
a mineral composed of lead carbonate (cerussite), serving as a significant source of lead
lead carbonate mineral; source of lead

Examples

The miners extracted white lead ore from the hillside for use in various industries.
Early painters valued white lead ore because it could be processed into pigments for white paint.
Many deposits of white lead ore were discovered throughout the 18th century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cerussite - a mineral consisting of lead carbonate
