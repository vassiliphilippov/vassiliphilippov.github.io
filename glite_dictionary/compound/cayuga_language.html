Cayuga language

1. NOUN

/ kaɪˈjuːɡə ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / kaɪˈjuːɡə ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Iroquoian language spoken by the Cayuga people, indigenous to regions of Canada and the United States
Iroquoian language of the Cayuga people

Examples

She is learning the Cayuga language to help preserve her family's cultural heritage.
The Cayuga language is taught in some schools in Ontario and New York state.
Linguists have documented traditional stories spoken in Cayuga language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Iroquoian - family of indigenous languages of North America

Etymology

From the name Cayuga, referring to one of the original five nations of the Iroquois Confederacy.
