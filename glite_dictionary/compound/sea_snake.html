sea snake

1. NOUN

/ siː sneɪk / (AmE), / siː sneɪk / (BrE)
A highly venomous aquatic snake of warm coastal seas, distinguished by a flattened, paddle-like tail and viviparous birth, feeding mainly on fish.
venomous aquatic snake of warm seas

Examples

Divers should be cautious when encountering a sea snake in tropical waters.
The sea snake uses its quick-acting venom to immobilize fish before eating them.
Scientists study the unique adaptations that allow the sea snake to thrive in marine environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snake - a long, limbless reptile
