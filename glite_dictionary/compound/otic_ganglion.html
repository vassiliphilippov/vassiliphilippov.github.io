otic ganglion

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊtɪk ɡæŋˌɡliən / (AmE), / ˈəʊtɪk ˈɡaŋɡlɪən / (BrE)
an autonomic ganglion located just below the foramen ovale of the skull, whose postganglionic fibers supply the parotid gland
autonomic ganglion supplying the parotid gland
medical

Examples

Damage to the otic ganglion can disrupt saliva production in the parotid gland.
The surgeon carefully avoided the otic ganglion during the procedure near the base of the skull.
Stimulation of the otic ganglion increases secretion from the parotid gland.

Etymology

From 'otic' (relating to the ear), from Greek 'ous, ōtos' (ear), and 'ganglion' from Greek 'ganglion' (tumor, nerve bundle).
