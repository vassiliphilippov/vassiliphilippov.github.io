free nerve ending

1. NOUN

/ fri nɜrv ˈɛndɪŋ / (AmE), / friː nɜːv ˈɛndɪŋ / (BrE)
a microscopic sensory nerve ending in the skin that is not connected to any specific sensory receptor and is involved in detecting pain and temperature
type of sensory nerve ending in skin
medical

Examples

The sensation of pain from a burn is transmitted by free nerve endings in the skin.
Unlike other sensory structures, free nerve endings are not associated with any specific receptor cells.
Damage to free nerve endings can lead to a loss of pain or temperature sensitivity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nociceptor - a sensory receptor for painful stimuli
