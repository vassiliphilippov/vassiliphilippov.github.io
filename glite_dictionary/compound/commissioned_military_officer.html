commissioned military officer

1. NOUN

/ kəˈmɪʃənd ˈmɪlɪˌtɛri ˈɒfɪsər / (AmE), / kəˈmɪʃənd ˈmɪlɪt(ə)ri ˈɒfɪsə / (BrE)
an officer in the armed forces who has earned a commission, typically holding authority by virtue of a formal appointment
military officer holding a formal commission

Examples

He rose through the ranks to become a commissioned military officer in the Air Force.
As a commissioned military officer, she was entrusted with significant leadership responsibilities.
The ceremony marked his transition from cadet to commissioned military officer.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: non-commissioned officer - a military officer who has not earned a commission, such as a sergeant or corporal
