Old World chat

1. NOUN

/ oʊld wɜrld tʃæt / (AmE), / əʊld wɜːld tʃæt / (BrE)
any of several small Old World songbirds of the thrush family, known for their chattering calls.
small Old World songbird with a chattering call

Examples

The Old World chat is admired for its lively, melodic calls during the spring mating season.
Many birdwatchers visit the area to catch a glimpse of rare Old World chat species.
Unlike robins, the Old World chat prefers open woodland habitats and bushes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: songbird - a bird that sings musical songs, especially a passerine
  • Antonym: New World chat - related group of American songbirds called 'chats'
