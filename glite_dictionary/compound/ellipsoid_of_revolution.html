ellipsoid of revolution

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈlɪp.sɔɪd əv rɪˌvɑː.luˈʃən / (AmE), / ɪˈlɪp.sɔɪd əv rɪˌvjuː.luˈʃən / (BrE)
A three-dimensional shape formed by rotating an ellipse about one of its principal axes, resulting in a spheroidal surface.
shape from rotating an ellipse around an axis

Examples

The Earth is often modeled as an ellipsoid of revolution rather than a perfect sphere.
By rotating an ellipse around its major axis, you create an ellipsoid of revolution.
Many astronomical bodies have the shape of an ellipsoid of revolution due to their rotation.

Synset Examples

it looked like a sphere but on closer examination I saw it was really a spheroid

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spheroid - a shape resembling a sphere, especially one that is an ellipsoid of revolution
  • Degree Lower: sphere - a perfectly round three-dimensional shape, the special case when both axes are equal

Etymology

From 'ellipse' (Greek elleipsis, 'a falling short') + '-oid' (resembling) and Latin 'revolutio' (a revolving).
