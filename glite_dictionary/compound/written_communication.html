written communication

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɪtən kəˌmjunɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈrɪt.ən kəˌmjuː.nɪˈkeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the process of conveying information or ideas through printed or handwritten symbols, such as letters, emails, or reports
exchange of information in writing

Examples

Business relies heavily on written communication to convey contracts and agreements.
Effective written communication helps prevent misunderstandings in the workplace.
She preferred written communication over phone calls to ensure clarity and accuracy.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: verbal communication - the exchange of information using spoken words
  • Antonym: nonverbal communication - conveying information without words, e.g. through gestures
