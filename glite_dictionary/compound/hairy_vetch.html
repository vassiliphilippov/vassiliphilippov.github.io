hairy vetch

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛri ˌvɛtʃ / (AmE), / ˈheəri ˌvɛtʃ / (BrE)
a European plant of the pea family, cultivated for forage and as a cover crop
European plant grown for forage and cover

Examples

Farmers often plant hairy vetch to improve soil fertility and control weeds.
Cover crops such as hairy vetch can fix nitrogen and enrich the soil without synthetic fertilizers.
After harvesting the hairy vetch, the field was ready for the next crop.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vetch - climbing plant of the pea family, often used as forage
