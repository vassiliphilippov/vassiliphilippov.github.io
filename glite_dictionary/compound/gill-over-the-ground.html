gill-over-the-ground

1. NOUN

/ ɡɪl oʊvər ðə ɡraʊnd / (AmE), / ɡɪl əʊvə ðə ɡraʊnd / (BrE)
A trailing European aromatic plant of the mint family, with rounded leaves and small purplish flowers, often grown in hanging baskets and naturalized in North America.
a trailing aromatic plant of the mint family

Examples

The gill-over-the-ground is often used in hanging baskets for its attractive foliage.
As a child, she would collect gill-over-the-ground in the woods near her home.
Gardeners sometimes struggle to control gill-over-the-ground because it spreads quickly across lawns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ground ivy - another common name for this plant (Glechoma hederacea)

Etymology

From 'gill' (a former name for ground ivy) + 'over-the-ground', referring to its creeping growth.
