quote unquote

1. X

/ ˈkwoʊt ənˈkwoʊt / (AmE), / ˈkwəʊt ənˈkwəʊt / (BrE)
used to indicate that the following word or phrase is a quotation or should be regarded with irony or skepticism, especially in speech
used to highlight or question a quoted word or phrase

Examples

He called her his, quote unquote, 'best friend'—but they barely know each other.
The report was written by an employee, quote unquote, 'at the scene of the accident'.
He's a quote unquote expert, but has very little real experience.

Synset Examples

He was, quote unquote, the best player on the team.
He’s a, quote unquote, expert in his field.
She said, quote unquote, that it was her last chance.
