family Phaethontidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli fiːəˈθɒn.tɪˌdeɪ / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli fiːəˈθɒn.tɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of seabirds known as tropicbirds, characterized by long tail feathers, found mainly in tropical oceans.
family of seabirds: tropicbirds
medical

Examples

The family Phaethontidae is easily recognized by the birds' long, slender tail feathers.
Scientists study the family Phaethontidae to understand seabird adaptation to tropical environments.
All tropicbird species belong to the avian family Phaethontidae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tropicbird - a seabird of the family Phaethontidae, known for its long tail streamers

Etymology

From Modern Latin Phaethontidae, based on genus Phaethon, from Greek mythology (Phaethon, son of Helios).
