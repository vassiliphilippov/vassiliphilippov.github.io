common reed

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmən ˌrid / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən ˌriːd / (BrE)
a tall, widespread grass with broad leaves and large feathery flower heads, typically found in moist areas and used for making mats, screens, and arrow shafts
tall wetland grass with broad leaves

Examples

The common reed can grow very tall along the edges of lakes and rivers.
Local artisans weave common reed into baskets and mats for sale at markets.
Many birds and insects make their homes among the dense growth of common reed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reed - tall, slender-leaved grass that grows in water or marshy ground
