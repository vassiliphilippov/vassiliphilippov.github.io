cyanuric acid

1. NOUN

/ saɪ.əˈnjʊr.ɪk ˈæs.ɪd / (AmE), / saɪ.əˈnjʊər.ɪk ˈæs.ɪd / (BrE)
A white, crystalline organic compound used chiefly as a stabilizer in swimming pool water and in industrial processes.
chemical used in pool stabilizers

Examples

The technician measured the cyanuric acid levels in the pool to ensure proper chlorine stabilization.
Cyanuric acid is commonly added to outdoor pools to protect chlorine from sunlight degradation.
Excessive cyanuric acid can interfere with effective pool sanitization.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: isocyanuric acid - a related compound with a similar structure and uses

Etymology

From cyan, referring to the cyano group, and uric, relating to uric acid.
