genus Calycophyllum

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˌkælɪkoʊˈfɪləm / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˌkælɪkəˈfɪləm / (BrE)
A genus of medium to large tropical American trees with shiny, reddish-brown, shredding bark.
a genus of tropical American trees

Examples

The genus Calycophyllum is known for its distinctive bark that peels away in strips.
Biologists discovered a new species within the genus Calycophyllum in the Amazon rainforest.
Many species in the genus Calycophyllum are important sources of timber in South America.

Etymology

From Greek 'kalyx' (cup) and 'phyllon' (leaf), referring to the structure of the calyx and leaves.
