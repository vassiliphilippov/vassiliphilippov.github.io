corn sugar

1. NOUN

/ kɔrn ˈʃʌɡɚ / (AmE), / kɔːn ˈʃʌɡə / (BrE)
Dextrose obtained by hydrolyzing cornstarch, commonly used as a sweetener in food products.
dextrose from cornstarch

Examples

Many processed foods contain corn sugar as a sweetener.
The production of corn sugar involves the hydrolysis of cornstarch.
Some people with corn allergies may react to products made with corn sugar.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dextrose - a simple sugar (glucose) often obtained from starch

2. NOUN

/ kɔrn ˈʃʊɡɚ / (AmE), / kɔːn ˈʃʊɡə / (BrE)
a form of dextrose obtained from cornstarch, used as a sweetening agent in foods and beverages
dextrose from cornstarch used as sweetener

Examples

Many processed foods list corn sugar as an ingredient on their labels.
Some people prefer corn sugar when brewing beer at home for its clean fermentation.
The company switched from cane sugar to corn sugar due to lower costs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dextrose - a simple sugar also known as glucose
  • Synonym: glucose - a simple sugar and important energy source
