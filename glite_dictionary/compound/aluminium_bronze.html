aluminium bronze

1. NOUN

/ əˌluːmɪniəm ˈbroʊnz / (AmE), / ˌæljuːˈmɪniəm ˈbrəʊnz / (BrE)
a hard, corrosion-resistant alloy of copper with aluminium, valued for its strength and durability
a copper-aluminium alloy

Examples

Many marine components are made from aluminium bronze because of its resistance to seawater corrosion.
The statue was cast in aluminium bronze to achieve both strength and an attractive finish.
Engineers selected aluminium bronze for the valve due to its excellent tensile properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bronze - an alloy consisting primarily of copper, usually with tin

Etymology

From 'aluminium' and 'bronze', denoting an alloy of copper with aluminium.
