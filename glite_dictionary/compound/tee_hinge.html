tee hinge

1. NOUN

/ tiː hɪndʒ / (AmE), / tiː hɪndʒ / (BrE)
a type of hinge shaped like the letter T when opened, used in doors or gates, where one side is a long strap and the other is a short plate fitted into the frame
T-shaped metal hinge used in carpentry

Examples

He installed a tee hinge on the garden gate for extra support.
The carpenter prefers using tee hinges for heavy wooden doors.
A rusty tee hinge made it difficult to open the old shed door.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strap hinge - a hinge with long, narrow leaves used for gates or doors
  • Synonym: butt hinge - a hinge with two rectangular leaves joined by a pin, commonly recessed into doors and frames

Etymology

From the letter 'T', describing the hinge's shape, and 'hinge' (from Middle English 'henge').
