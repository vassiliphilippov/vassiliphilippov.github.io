dentate nucleus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛn.teɪt ˈnuː.kli.əs / (AmE), / ˈdɛn.teɪt ˈnjuː.kli.əs / (BrE)
a large, deeply folded mass of grey matter located within the white matter of each cerebral hemisphere, forming part of the cerebellum
fused grey matter mass in the cerebellum
medical

Examples

Damage to the dentate nucleus can result in problems with coordination and movement.
MRI scans revealed an unusual pattern in the patient's dentate nucleus.
Researchers are studying the role of the dentate nucleus in cognitive processing.

Etymology

From Latin 'dentatus' (toothed) and 'nucleus' (kernel, core), referring to its toothed appearance.
