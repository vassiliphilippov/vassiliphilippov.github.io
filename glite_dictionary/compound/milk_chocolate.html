milk chocolate

1. NOUN

/ mɪlk ˈtʃɑklət / (AmE), / mɪlk ˈtʃɒklət / (BrE)
a sweet chocolate made from cocoa solids, cocoa butter, sugar, and milk powder, commonly used in confectionery and baking
sweet chocolate with milk powder

Examples

She handed me a bar of milk chocolate as a treat after dinner.
Many people prefer milk chocolate over dark chocolate because of its creamy sweetness.
Baking cookies with milk chocolate chips gives them a smooth, rich flavor.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dark chocolate - chocolate that contains little or no milk solids
  • Antonym: white chocolate - a confection made with cocoa butter, sugar, and milk solids but no cocoa solids
