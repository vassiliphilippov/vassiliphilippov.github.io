temporal arteritis

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛmpərəl ɑːrˈtɛrɪtɪs / (AmE), / ˈtɛmpərəl ɑːˈtɛrɪtɪs / (BrE)
A condition characterized by inflammation of the temporal arteries, often causing headaches, jaw pain, and sometimes visual problems.
inflammation of the temporal arteries
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed her with temporal arteritis after she described her severe headache and jaw pain.
Untreated temporal arteritis can sometimes lead to vision loss in elderly patients.
A biopsy of the artery confirmed the presence of temporal arteritis in the patient.

Etymology

From Latin 'temporalis' (of the temples) and 'arteritis' (artery inflammation).
