Paris green

1. NOUN

/ ˌpærɪs ˈɡriːn / (AmE), / ˌpærɪs ˈɡriːn / (BrE)
a bright green color with a slight yellowish tint
a vivid yellowish-green color

Examples

The painter chose Paris green for the leaves in his landscape.
She wore a dress of Paris green that stood out at the party.
The decorator recommended accents of Paris green to freshen the room.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: emerald green - a bright green color resembling that of an emerald
  • Degree Higher: chartreuse - a color halfway between yellow and green

Etymology

Named after the pigment first used in Paris, France, in the 19th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˌpɛrɪs ˈɡriːn / (AmE), / ˌparɪs ˈɡriːn / (BrE)
a highly toxic, bright green chemical compound, formerly used as a pigment and insecticide, consisting of copper(II) acetoarsenite
a toxic green chemical pigment/insecticide

Examples

The artists of the nineteenth century often used Paris green in their paintings, despite its toxicity.
Paris green was once a popular insecticide before safer alternatives became available.
The vibrant shade of Paris green made it a favorite, though it was hazardous to human health.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: copper arsenate - a toxic chemical compound used as an insecticide
  • Synonym: copper acetate - a chemical compound related to Paris green

Etymology

Named after Paris, the city where it was first used extensively, and its green color.
