lapis lazuli

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæpɪs ˈlæzjʊˌlaɪ / (AmE), / ˈlæpɪs ˈlæzjuːlaɪ / (BrE)
a deep blue semiprecious stone prized for its intense color, used in jewelry and decoration
deep blue semiprecious stone

Examples

The mosaic was inlaid with lapis lazuli and gold.
Painters once ground lapis lazuli to create vibrant blue pigments for their works.
She wore a necklace adorned with polished lapis lazuli beads.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: turquoise - an opaque blue-to-green mineral used as a gemstone

Etymology

From Medieval Latin *lapis lazuli*, literally 'stone of Lazuli' (Lazuli from Persian *lāzhward*, a place known for the stone).
