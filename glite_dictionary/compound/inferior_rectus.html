inferior rectus

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈfɪriɚ ˈrɛktəs / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɪəriə ˈrɛktəs / (BrE)
the muscle in the eye that moves the eyeball downward and toward the nose
eye muscle that moves eyeball down and in
medical

Examples

Damage to the inferior rectus can make it difficult to look downward effectively.
The surgeon carefully avoided the inferior rectus during the procedure.
Contraction of the inferior rectus pulls the eye downward and slightly inward.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: superior rectus - the muscle in the eye that moves the eyeball upward
  • Synonym: lateral rectus - the muscle that moves the eyeball outward (laterally)

Etymology

From Latin 'inferior' (lower) and 'rectus' (straight), referring to its position and fiber alignment.
