bluejack oak

1. NOUN

/ ˈbluːˌdʒæk oʊk / (AmE), / ˈbluːˌdʒæk əʊk / (BrE)
a small, semi-evergreen shrubby tree native to the southeastern United States, with hairy young twigs and leaves that narrow to a slender, bristly point
a shrubby oak tree of the southeastern US

Examples

The bluejack oak is common in sandy soils throughout the southeastern United States.
Many birds use the bluejack oak as shelter due to its dense foliage.
A botanist identified the shrub as a bluejack oak by its slender, bristly leaves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oak - a type of tree or shrub in the genus Quercus
