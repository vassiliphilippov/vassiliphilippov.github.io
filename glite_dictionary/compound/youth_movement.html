youth movement

1. NOUN

/ juːθ ˈmuːvmənt / (AmE), / juːθ ˈmuːvmənt / (BrE)
a political, religious, or social reform movement or agitation consisting mainly of young people
political or social movement of young people

Examples

The civil rights era was marked by the rise of a powerful youth movement across the country.
A youth movement emerged to demand climate action from world leaders.
Many believe that the impact of a youth movement can transform society for generations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: student movement - organized movement of students for social or political change
