talcum powder

1. NOUN

/ ˈtæl.kəm ˌpaʊ.dɚ / (AmE), / ˈtæl.kəm ˌpaʊ.də / (BrE)
a fine, scented toilet powder made from purified talc, used to absorb moisture and reduce friction on the skin
toilet powder made from talc

Examples

She always applies talcum powder after her shower to keep her skin dry.
The doctor recommended using talcum powder to prevent chafing during hot weather.
He noticed a faint scent of lavender from the talcum powder in the nursery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: baby powder - powder for infants, often made from talc or cornstarch

Etymology

From talcum (from Latin 'talcum', referring to talc) and powder.
