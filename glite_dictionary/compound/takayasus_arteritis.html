Takayasu's arteritis

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɑːkaɪˈjɑːsuːz ɑːrˈtɛrɪtɪs / (AmE), / ˌtækəˈjɑːsuːz ɑːˈtɛrɪtɪs / (BrE)
a rare inflammatory disease of the large blood vessels, especially the aorta and its main branches, often resulting in absence of pulse in the arms and carotid arteries
rare inflammatory disease of large arteries
medical

Examples

The diagnosis of Takayasu's arteritis can be challenging due to its nonspecific early symptoms.
Patients with Takayasu's arteritis often present with absent pulses in their upper limbs.
Current treatments for Takayasu's arteritis aim to reduce vascular inflammation and prevent complications.

Etymology

Named after Japanese physician Mikito Takayasu, who first described the condition in 1908.
