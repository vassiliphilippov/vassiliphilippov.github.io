opossum shrimp

1. NOUN

/ əˈpɑsəm ˌʃrɪmp / (AmE), / əˈpɒsəm ˌʃrɪmp / (BrE)
a small shrimp-like crustacean whose females carry eggs and young in a pouch between their legs
shrimp-like crustacean with brood pouch

Examples

The scientist studied the behavior of the opossum shrimp in its natural habitat.
Female opossum shrimp carry their eggs in a brood pouch beneath their legs.
Several species of opossum shrimp play a crucial role in freshwater ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mysid - another name for opossum shrimp; member of the family Mysidae

Etymology

Named for the similarity to opossums, which also carry young in a pouch.
