family Caryophyllaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli kærioʊˌfɪleɪˈeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli kærɪə(ʊ)ˌfɪlɪˈeɪsɪˌiː / (BrE)
A large family of flowering herbs and subshrubs, usually with stems swollen at the nodes, commonly known as the pink or carnation family.
the pink/carnation family of flowering plants

Examples

Many members of the family Caryophyllaceae are popular in gardens for their ornamental flowers.
The family Caryophyllaceae includes well-known plants such as carnations and pinks.
Botanists study the diversity of the family Caryophyllaceae to understand plant evolution.

Etymology

From New Latin 'Caryophyllaceae', based on the genus 'Caryophyllus'.
