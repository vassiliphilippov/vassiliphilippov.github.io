blue wall of silence

1. NOUN

/ blu wɔl əv səˈlaɪnɪs / (AmE), / bluː wɔːl əv sɪˈlaɪnɪs / (BrE)
an unwritten code among police officers to remain silent or provide false testimony to protect colleagues from investigation or punishment
unwritten police code of secrecy

Examples

The blue wall of silence has been blamed for enabling corruption within some police departments.
Several officers broke the blue wall of silence by testifying against their colleagues in court.
Documentaries have explored how the blue wall of silence hampers efforts to hold police accountable.

Synset Examples

the blue wall cracked when some officers refused to take part in the cover-up

Related Terms

  • Synonym: code of silence - a general agreement to not speak or testify about wrongdoing

Etymology

From 'blue' referring to police uniforms and 'wall of silence' signifying a barrier of secrecy.
