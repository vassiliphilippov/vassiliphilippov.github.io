common currency

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmən ˈkɝrənsi / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən ˈkʌrənsi / (BrE)
something that is widely accepted, used, or valued, especially in a particular group or context
widely accepted or valued thing

Examples

In the world of academia, published research is common currency.
Trust has become the common currency of successful teams.
Shared interests serve as common currency in building friendships.

Synset Examples

In this community, respect is common currency among members.
Shared experiences became the common currency of their friendship.
Kindness is the common currency that unites us all.
