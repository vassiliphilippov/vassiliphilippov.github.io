horizontal line

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɔːrɪˈzɑːntl ˈlaɪn / (AmE), / ˌhɒrɪˈzɒntl ˈlaɪn / (BrE)
a straight line that runs at a constant level, parallel to the horizon or baseline
a line parallel to the horizon

Examples

Please draw a horizontal line across the middle of your page.
Engineers often use a horizontal line as a reference point in technical drawings.
The sunrise created a perfect horizontal line where the sea met the sky.

Synset Examples

He drew a horizontal line across the graph.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: vertical line - a straight line that goes up and down, at right angles to the horizon
