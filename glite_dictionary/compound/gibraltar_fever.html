Gibraltar fever

1. NOUN

/ dʒɪˈbrɔltər ˈfiːvər / (AmE), / dʒɪˈbrɒltə ˈfiːvə / (BrE)
an infectious bacterial disease in humans, transmitted by contact with infected animals or consumption of contaminated meat or milk, causing fever and headache; also known as brucellosis.
infectious bacterial disease, also called brucellosis
medical

Examples

Many cases of Gibraltar fever occurred after people drank unpasteurized milk in the region.
Veterinarians are at increased risk of contracting Gibraltar fever through contact with infected livestock.
The doctor suspected Gibraltar fever when the patient presented with persistent fever and joint pain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brucellosis - another term for Gibraltar fever; bacterial infection from animals
