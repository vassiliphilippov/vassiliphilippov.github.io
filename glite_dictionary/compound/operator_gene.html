operator gene

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑː.pəˌreɪ.tɚ ˌdʒin / (AmE), / ˈɒp.ər.eɪ.tə ˌdʒiːn / (BrE)
a segment of DNA that regulates the activity of adjacent structural genes by controlling the production of messenger RNA
gene regulating adjacent gene expression
medical

Examples

The operator gene plays a crucial role in the regulation of certain enzyme-producing genes.
Mutations in the operator gene can disrupt normal gene expression.
Scientists discovered the function of the operator gene while studying bacterial DNA.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: regulatory gene - a gene involved in controlling the expression of one or more other genes
