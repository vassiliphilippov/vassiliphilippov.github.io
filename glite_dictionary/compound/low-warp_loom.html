low-warp loom

1. NOUN

/ loʊ wɑrp luːm / (AmE), / ləʊ wɑːp luːm / (BrE)
a hand-operated loom in which the warp threads are arranged horizontally, used for weaving tapestry.
horizontal handloom for tapestry weaving

Examples

The artist created intricate designs on the low-warp loom in her studio.
Traditional tapestry workshops often feature a low-warp loom as their primary tool.
Using a low-warp loom allows for greater control over the tapestry's details.
