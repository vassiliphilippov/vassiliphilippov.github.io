Ramses the Great

1. PROPN

/ ˈræm.siːz ðə ɡreɪt / (AmE), / ˈræm.siːz ðə ɡreɪt / (BrE)
the third pharaoh of Egypt's Nineteenth Dynasty, renowned for his extensive building projects and long reign (c. 1303–1213 BC)
notable ancient Egyptian pharaoh

Examples

The massive temples at Abu Simbel were constructed during the reign of Ramses the Great.
Historians consider Ramses the Great one of the most successful pharaohs in Egypt's history.
Many statues and monuments bearing the name Ramses the Great still fascinate archaeologists today.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pharaoh - ancient Egyptian ruler

Etymology

Ramses, from Egyptian Ra-mesesu ('Ra has fashioned him'); 'the Great' denotes his prominence.
