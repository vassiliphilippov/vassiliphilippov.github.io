genetic fingerprinting

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˈnɛtɪk ˈfɪŋɡərˌprɪntɪŋ / (AmE), / dʒɪˈnɛtɪk ˈfɪŋɡəˌprɪntɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of analyzing a person's DNA from body tissue or fluid samples in order to identify them
process of analyzing DNA for identification
medical

Examples

The police used genetic fingerprinting to match the suspect to evidence found at the scene.
Advances in genetic fingerprinting have revolutionized forensic science and criminal investigations.
Hospitals now use genetic fingerprinting to confirm the identities of newborns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: DNA profiling - technique for identifying individuals by their DNA features
