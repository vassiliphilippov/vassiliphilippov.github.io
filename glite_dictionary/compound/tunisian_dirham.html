Tunisian dirham

1. NOUN

/ tuːˈnɪʒən ˈdɪrˌhæm / (AmE), / tjuːˈnɪziən ˈdɪəˌræm / (BrE)
A former monetary unit of Tunisia, equal to one hundredth of a Tunisian dinar.
former monetary unit of Tunisia

Examples

In the early twentieth century, the Tunisian dirham was a commonly used unit of currency.
Historians often reference the Tunisian dirham when discussing Tunisia's economic past.
A hundred Tunisian dirhams were equal to one dinar under the old monetary system.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dinar - the basic monetary unit of Tunisia

Etymology

From Arabic 'dirham', ultimately from Greek 'drachma'.
