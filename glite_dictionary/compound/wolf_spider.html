wolf spider

1. NOUN

/ wʊlf ˈspaɪdər / (AmE), / wʊlf ˈspaɪdə / (BrE)
A ground-dwelling spider of the family Lycosidae that hunts its prey actively rather than using a web to catch it.
a ground-hunting spider species

Examples

A wolf spider can often be seen carrying its egg sac attached to its abdomen.
Unlike many other spiders, the wolf spider does not spin webs to catch its prey.
The biologist identified the species as a wolf spider based on its hunting behavior and eye arrangement.
