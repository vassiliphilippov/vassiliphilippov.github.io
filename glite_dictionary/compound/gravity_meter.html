gravity meter

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡrævəti ˌmiːtər / (AmE), / ˈɡravəti ˌmiːtə / (BrE)
an instrument used to measure variations in the Earth's gravitational field
instrument for measuring gravity variations

Examples

The geologists used a gravity meter to detect underground mineral deposits.
A portable gravity meter is essential for field surveys in remote areas.
By analyzing data from the gravity meter, scientists identified subtle changes in Earth's gravity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gravimeter - an instrument for measuring gravitational acceleration
