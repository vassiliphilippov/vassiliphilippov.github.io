poetic justice

1. NOUN

/ poʊˈɛtɪk ˈdʒʌstɪs / (AmE), / pəʊˈɛtɪk ˈdʒʌstɪs / (BrE)
an outcome in which virtue is rewarded and vice is punished, especially in a way that is particularly or ironically appropriate.
deserved, fitting outcome or punishment

Examples

It was poetic justice when the dishonest manager was caught and fired by the company.
The ending of the novel provided poetic justice for the main villain.
Many viewers felt that the twist ending was pure poetic justice for all the wrongs committed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: justice - the quality of being fair and reasonable

Etymology

Coined in the 17th century; 'poetic' from Greek 'poiētikos' (creative), combined with 'justice'.
