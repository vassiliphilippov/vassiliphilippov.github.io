genus Chimaera

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs kaɪˈmɪərə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs kɪˈmɪərə / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Chimaeridae, a group of cartilaginous fishes known as chimaeras.
type genus of Chimaeridae (cartilaginous fishes)

Examples

The genus Chimaera includes several rare species of deep-sea fish.
Scientists studied members of the genus Chimaera to better understand the evolution of cartilaginous fishes.
A new species within the genus Chimaera was discovered off the coast of New Zealand.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Chimaeridae - family of cartilaginous fishes including chimaeras

Etymology

From Latin 'chimaera', from Greek 'khimaira' (a mythological monster), used for its fanciful appearance.
