witch hazel

1. NOUN

/ wɪtʃ ˈheɪzəl / (AmE), / wɪtʃ ˈheɪzəl / (BrE)
a shrub or small tree of the genus Hamamelis, whose bark and leaves are used to make an astringent lotion
shrub/tree used for astringent lotion

Examples

Many people use witch hazel lotion to soothe minor skin irritations.
The garden includes a beautiful witch hazel that blooms in late winter.
Extract from the bark of witch hazel is valued for its medicinal properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hamamelis - the genus of witch hazel shrubs and small trees

Etymology

'Witch' from Old English 'wice' (pliant, bendable) and 'hazel' referencing the hazel-like leaves.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɪtʃ ˌheɪzəl / (AmE), / ˈwɪtʃ ˌheɪzəl / (BrE)
A shrub or small tree of the genus Hamamelis, especially Hamamelis virginiana, whose bark and leaves are used to make an astringent lotion.
shrub of the genus Hamamelis; source of astringent

Examples

Many people use witch hazel lotion to soothe minor skin irritations.
The witch hazel in the garden blooms late in the autumn.
Some commercial toners list witch hazel as a main ingredient for its astringent properties.

Etymology

Named after the resemblance of its leaves to those of the hazel and from 'witch' in the sense of 'pliant' from Old English 'wice'.
