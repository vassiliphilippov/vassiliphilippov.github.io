elastic potential energy

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈlæstɪk pəˈtɛnʃəl ˈɛnərdʒi / (AmE), / ɪˈlæstɪk pəˈtɛnʃəl ˈɛnədʒi / (BrE)
the potential energy stored in an object when it is deformed, such as when a spring is stretched or compressed
energy stored in a deformed object

Examples

A stretched rubber band contains elastic potential energy that is released when it snaps back.
Engineers calculate elastic potential energy to design effective shock absorbers.
When you compress a spring, you increase its elastic potential energy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: potential energy - energy possessed by a body due to its position or state
  • Antonym: kinetic energy - energy possessed by a body due to its motion

Etymology

From elastic (from Greek 'elastikos', meaning ductile) + potential energy (physics term for stored energy).
