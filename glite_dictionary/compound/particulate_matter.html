particulate matter

1. NOUN

/ pɑrˈtɪkjələt ˈmætər / (AmE), / pɑːˈtɪkjʊlət ˈmætə / (BrE)
very small solid or liquid particles suspended in air or another gas, especially as pollutants that can be harmful to health
tiny solid or liquid airborne particles (pollutant)

Examples

The city issued a warning when particulate matter levels reached hazardous concentrations.
Exposure to fine particulate matter is linked to respiratory and cardiovascular diseases.
Air purifiers are often used to reduce particulate matter indoors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aerosol - tiny solid or liquid particles suspended in air or another gas

Etymology

From 'particulate' (from Latin particula, 'particle') and 'matter' (from Latin materia, 'substance').
