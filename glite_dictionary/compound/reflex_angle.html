reflex angle

1. NOUN

/ ˈriː.flɛks ˈæŋ.ɡəl / (AmE), / ˈriː.flɛks ˈæŋ.ɡəl / (BrE)
an angle that measures more than 180 degrees but less than 360 degrees
angle greater than 180° but less than 360°

Examples

The exterior angle of a regular pentagon is a reflex angle.
When you open a book so the covers form a reflex angle, it is nearly flat.
A reflex angle can be found by subtracting the given angle from 360 degrees.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: acute angle - an angle less than 90 degrees
  • Degree Lower: obtuse angle - an angle more than 90 but less than 180 degrees

Etymology

From Latin 'reflexus' meaning 'bent back'.
