interstellar medium

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪntərˈstɛlɚ ˈmiːdiəm / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈstelə ˈmiːdiəm / (BrE)
the matter, including gas and dust, that exists in the space between stars in a galaxy
matter between stars in a galaxy

Examples

Scientists study the interstellar medium to learn how stars and planets form.
The density of the interstellar medium varies throughout our galaxy.
Dust in the interstellar medium can obscure the light from distant stars.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interplanetary medium - matter and energy existing in the space between planets

Etymology

From 'interstellar' (between stars) and Latin 'medium' (middle, means).
