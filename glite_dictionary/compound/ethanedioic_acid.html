ethanedioic acid

1. NOUN

/ iˌθæniˈdaɪɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / iˌθanɪˈdʌɪɪk ˈasɪd / (BrE)
a toxic, colorless crystalline organic acid found in some plants and used as a bleach, rust remover, and in chemical analysis
a toxic organic acid used in industry and analysis
medical

Examples

Ethanedioic acid is commonly used in laboratories for chemical analysis and cleaning purposes.
Even small amounts of ethanedioic acid can be toxic if ingested, so it must be handled with care.
Rhubarb leaves contain ethanedioic acid, which makes them poisonous to humans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oxalic acid - another name for ethanedioic acid

Etymology

From systematic chemical nomenclature: ethane (root) + dioic (indicating two carboxylic acid groups).
