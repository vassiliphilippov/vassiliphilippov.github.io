ground level

1. NOUN

/ ɡraʊnd ˈlɛvəl / (AmE), / ɡraʊnd ˈlɛvəl / (BrE)
the height or surface of the ground on which something stands; the level of the ground outside a building or structure.
height or surface of the ground

Examples

During the renovation, the entire entrance was lowered to ground level for easier access.
The apartment is situated at ground level, making it perfect for those who dislike stairs.
Floodwaters reached well above ground level after the heavy rains.

Synset Examples

the base of the tower was below grade

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grade - the level or slope of the ground around a building

2. NOUN

/ ɡraʊnd ˈlɛvəl / (AmE), / ɡraʊnd ˈlɛvəl / (BrE)
the floor of a building that is at or nearest to the level of the ground outside
floor at or nearest ground outside building

Examples

Our apartment is on the ground level, so it's easy to step outside into the garden.
The lobby is located at the ground level of the hotel, just beyond the main entrance.
Shops at ground level tend to have the highest rents in this city center.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: first floor - the floor of a building at street or ground level (especially in American English)
  • Antonym: basement - floor of a building that is partly or entirely below ground level
