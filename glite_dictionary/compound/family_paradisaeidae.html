family Paradisaeidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli pəˌrædəˈsiːɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli pəˌrædɪˈsiːɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of birds native to Australasia, known as birds of paradise, noted for their spectacular plumage and intricate courtship displays.
family of birds of paradise

Examples

The family Paradisaeidae is celebrated for the incredible diversity of plumage among its male members.
Researchers studied the mating rituals unique to the family Paradisaeidae in New Guinea.
Many species within the family Paradisaeidae use elaborate dances during courtship.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: birds of paradise - colorful birds native to Australasia known for elaborate feathers and dances

Etymology

From modern Latin 'Paradisaeidae', the family name for birds of paradise, based on Greek 'paradeisos' (paradise).
