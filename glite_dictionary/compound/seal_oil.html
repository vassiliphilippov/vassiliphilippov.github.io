seal oil

1. NOUN

/ ˈsiːl ˌɔɪl / (AmE), / ˈsiːl ˌɔɪl / (BrE)
a pale yellow to red-brown oil obtained from the blubber of seals, used in making soap, dressing leather, and as a lubricant
oil derived from the blubber of seals

Examples

The indigenous people traditionally used seal oil as a vital source of nutrition and lamp fuel.
Leather workers sometimes treat hides with seal oil to keep them supple and water-resistant.
Seal oil was once a common ingredient in the production of soap and lubricants.
