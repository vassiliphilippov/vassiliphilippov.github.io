genus Berberis

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs bərˈbɛr.ɪs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs bəˈbɛː.rɪs / (BrE)
A large genus of shrubs found in temperate regions of both the New and Old Worlds, commonly known as barberries.
a genus of shrubs (barberries)

Examples

Many species within the genus Berberis are used as ornamental plants in gardens.
The genus Berberis includes shrubs that produce sharp spines and brightly colored berries.
Several plants in the genus Berberis are valued for their medicinal properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barberry - common name for shrubs in the genus Berberis

Etymology

From Latin 'Berberis' (barberry), from Arabic 'barbārīs'.
