mountain sandwort

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaʊn.tɪn ˈsændˌwɝt / (AmE), / ˈmaʊn.tɪn ˈsændˌwɜːt / (BrE)
A low, tufted plant of northern or alpine regions, belonging to the genus Arenaria, with small white flowers.
boreal or alpine flowering plant

Examples

Mountain sandwort thrives in rocky alpine environments where few other plants can survive.
Botanists discovered a rare variety of mountain sandwort growing near the glacier.
The delicate white flowers of mountain sandwort stand out against the harsh, stony ground.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sandwort - a plant of the genus Arenaria, with small flowers and leaves

Etymology

From 'mountain' referring to its habitat and 'sandwort,' from Old English 'wurt' (plant or root).
