genus Bucephala

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌbjuːˈsɛf.ələ / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌbjuːˈsɛf.ələ / (BrE)
A genus of ducks that includes buffleheads and goldeneyes, characterized by their diving habits and distinctive markings.
genus of ducks, including buffleheads and goldeneyes

Examples

The genus Bucephala comprises several species of diving ducks found in North America and Eurasia.
Both the bufflehead and the common goldeneye belong to the genus Bucephala.
Ornithologists often study the genus Bucephala to understand adaptations in diving waterfowl.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bufflehead - a small North American diving duck
  • Synonym: goldeneye - a type of diving duck with golden eyes

Etymology

From New Latin 'Bucephala', based on Greek 'boukephalos' (bull-headed) and Latin 'genus' (kind, type).
