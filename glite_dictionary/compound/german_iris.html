German iris

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɝːmən ˈaɪrɪs / (AmE), / ˈdʒɜːmən ˈaɪrɪs / (BrE)
a perennial plant with large, showy deep blue-purple flowers, commonly grown in gardens; a cultivated species of iris native to Europe, especially Italy.
a species of iris with blue-purple flowers

Examples

The German iris adds a burst of color to the spring garden.
Many gardeners prefer the German iris for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
She planted several German irises along the walkway to brighten the entrance.

Etymology

Named for Germany, though native to the Mediterranean; scientific name Iris germanica.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɝːmən ˈaɪrɪs / (AmE), / ˈdʒɜːmən ˈaɪrɪs / (BrE)
a species of large iris plant (Iris germanica) with purple or white flowers, native to central and southern Europe, commonly grown in gardens
a species of iris plant with purple or white flowers

Examples

The German iris blooms in late spring, displaying its striking purple flowers in the garden.
Many gardeners prefer the German iris for its hardiness and vibrant colors.
After the rain, drops of water sparkled on the petals of the German iris.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: iris - a genus of flowering plants with showy flowers
