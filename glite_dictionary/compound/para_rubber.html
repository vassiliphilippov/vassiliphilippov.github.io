Para rubber

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːrə ˈrʌbər / (AmE), / ˈpɑːrə ˈrʌbə / (BrE)
a natural rubber derived from the latex of the Brazilian rubber tree (Hevea brasiliensis), widely used in manufacturing
natural rubber from Brazilian rubber tree

Examples

The company sources Para rubber for use in the production of tires.
Early waterproof clothing often relied on Para rubber as the main material.
Harvesting Para rubber requires carefully tapping the trees to collect the latex.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: natural rubber - rubber obtained naturally from latex of certain trees

Etymology

From Pará, a state in northern Brazil, where the rubber tree is native.
