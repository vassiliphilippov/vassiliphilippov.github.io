water birch

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔt̬ɚ bɝːtʃ / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə bɜːtʃ / (BrE)
A birch tree native to the western United States, resembling the paper birch but with brownish bark.
birch tree with brownish bark, western US

Examples

The water birch thrives along streams in the Rocky Mountains.
In spring, clusters of catkins appear on the branches of the water birch.
Unlike the paper birch, the water birch has darker, reddish-brown bark.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paper birch - a birch tree with white peeling bark
