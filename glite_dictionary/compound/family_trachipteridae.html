family Trachipteridae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ˌtrækɪptəˈraɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli ˌtrakɪptəˈraɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of elongated, ribbon-like marine fishes known as ribbonfishes, characterized by their laterally compressed bodies and long dorsal fins.
a family of ribbonfishes (Trachipteridae)

Examples

The family Trachipteridae includes several species of unusual, ribbon-shaped fishes found in deep ocean waters.
Scientists recently discovered a new member of the family Trachipteridae off the coast of Japan.
Members of the family Trachipteridae are rarely seen by divers due to their deep-sea habitat.

Etymology

From New Latin 'Trachipteridae', based on 'Trachipterus', the type genus, from Greek 'trachys' (rough) and 'pteron' (fin, wing).
