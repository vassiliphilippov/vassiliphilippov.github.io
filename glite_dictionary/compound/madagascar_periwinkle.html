Madagascar periwinkle

1. NOUN

/ ˌmædəˈɡæskər ˈpɛriˌwɪŋkəl / (AmE), / ˌmædəˈɡæskə ˈpɛriˌwɪŋkl̩ / (BrE)
a woody flowering plant native to Madagascar, commonly grown for its large pink or red flowers
flowering plant with pink/red flowers

Examples

The Madagascar periwinkle is known for its vibrant, glossy leaves and striking pink flowers.
Gardeners often plant Madagascar periwinkle as a decorative border in warm climates.
Extracts from the Madagascar periwinkle are used in traditional medicine and pharmaceuticals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: periwinkle - a category of flowering plants; related genus

Etymology

Named for Madagascar, its native origin, and periwinkle, a common name for plants in the genus Catharanthus.
