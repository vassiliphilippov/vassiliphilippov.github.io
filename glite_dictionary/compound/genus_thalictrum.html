genus Thalictrum

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs θəˈlɪk.trəm / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs θəˈlɪk.trəm / (BrE)
A genus of widely distributed perennial herbs, commonly known as meadow rues, found in temperate regions.
genus of perennial herbs (meadow rue)

Examples

Botanists classify the meadow rue under the genus Thalictrum, which contains several species.
The genus Thalictrum is known for its delicate flowers and divided leaves in temperate meadows.
Many garden varieties come from the genus Thalictrum, valued for their ornamental foliage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meadow rue - common name for plants in the genus Thalictrum

Etymology

From New Latin 'Thalictrum', the genus name, from Greek 'thaliktron' for a type of plant.
