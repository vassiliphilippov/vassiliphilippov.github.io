genus Loxia

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˈlɑksiə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈlɒksiə / (BrE)
A genus of finches known as crossbills, characterized by their distinctive crossed bills, found mainly in the Northern Hemisphere.
genus of crossbill finches

Examples

The genus Loxia includes several species of crossbills with specialized beaks for feeding on conifer seeds.
Many birdwatchers are fascinated by the unique bill shape of birds from the genus Loxia.
Studies of the genus Loxia help scientists understand adaptation to specific food sources.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crossbill - a type of finch with a crossed bill

Etymology

From New Latin Loxia, the genus name assigned by early taxonomists.
