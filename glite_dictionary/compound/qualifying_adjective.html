qualifying adjective

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɑlɪˌfaɪɪŋ ˈædʒɪktɪv / (AmE), / ˈkwɒlɪfaɪɪŋ ˈadʒɪktɪv / (BrE)
an adjective that expresses a characteristic or attribute of the noun it modifies, such as 'nervous' in 'a nervous person'
an adjective expressing a noun's attribute

Examples

In the phrase 'a cold morning', the word cold is a qualifying adjective describing the noun.
A qualifying adjective such as 'happy' can be used before a noun to provide more information about it.
Teachers often highlight qualifying adjectives in sentences to help students understand how description works.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: descriptive adjective - an adjective that describes quality or state of a noun

Etymology

From 'qualify' (to attribute a quality) and 'adjective', ultimately from Latin 'adjectivum'.
