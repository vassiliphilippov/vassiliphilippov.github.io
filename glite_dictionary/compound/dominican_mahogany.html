Dominican mahogany

1. NOUN

/ dəˈmɪnɪkən məˈhɑɡəni / (AmE), / dəˈmɪnɪkən məˈhɒɡəni / (BrE)
a species of mahogany tree native to the West Indies, valued for its hard, reddish-brown wood
mahogany tree of the West Indies

Examples

Furniture made from Dominican mahogany is both beautiful and durable.
The carpenter prefers using Dominican mahogany for high-end cabinetry.
Conservation efforts have helped protect the remaining Dominican mahogany trees in the region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mahogany - hard, reddish-brown wood from mahogany trees
