constant hebetation

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑnstənt ˌhɛbəˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈkɒnstənt ˌhɛbɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
A persistent state of mental dullness or lethargy.
persistent mental dullness

Examples

Her constant hebetation at work worried her colleagues, who noticed her declining engagement.
After weeks of sleepless nights, he struggled with constant hebetation that made remembering simple facts difficult.
Doctors tried to determine the cause of his constant hebetation and lack of motivation.

Synset Examples

His constant hebetation made it hard to focus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lethargy - A lack of energy and enthusiasm; sluggishness.
  • Antonym: alertness - The quality of being alert and attentive.

Etymology

From Latin 'hebetatio' (making dull), based on 'hebes' (dull, blunt) plus English 'constant'.
