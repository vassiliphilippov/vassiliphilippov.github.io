Kernig's sign

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɜrnɪɡz saɪn / (AmE), / ˈkɜːnɪɡz saɪn / (BrE)
a clinical sign in which the patient, with the thigh flexed, cannot fully extend the knee due to hamstring stiffness, indicating possible meningitis
inability to extend knee due to hamstring stiffness; sign of meningitis
medical

Examples

The doctor checked for Kernig's sign during the neurological examination.
A positive Kernig's sign can be an important clue in diagnosing meningitis.
Absence of Kernig's sign does not completely rule out a central nervous system infection.

Etymology

Named after Vladimir Kernig (1840–1917), Russian neurologist.
