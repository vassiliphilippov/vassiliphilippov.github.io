Group 14 element

1. NOUN

/ ɡruːp ˈfɔrˈtin ˈɛləmənt / (AmE), / ɡruːp ˈfɔːˈtin ˈɛlɪmənt / (BrE)
any of the chemical elements in group 14 of the periodic table, including carbon, silicon, germanium, tin, lead, and flerovium.
element from periodic table group 14

Examples

Carbon is the best-known Group 14 element due to its essential role in organic chemistry.
Silicon, a Group 14 element, is widely used in computer chips and solar panels.
All Group 14 elements share similar valence electron configurations.

Synset Examples

Carbon, the lightest carbon group element, sublimates at 3825 °C.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carbon group - the group of elements consisting of carbon, silicon, germanium, tin, lead, and flerovium

Etymology

From 'group' (classification) and the group 14 designation in the periodic table of elements.
