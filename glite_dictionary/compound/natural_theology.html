natural theology

1. NOUN

/ ˈnætʃrəl θiˈɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ˈnætʃərəl θɪˈɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the branch of theology that argues knowledge of God can be attained through human reason and observation of the natural world, independent of revealed or scriptural sources.
theology about knowing God through reason alone

Examples

The arguments of natural theology seek to prove the existence of God by reason and observation alone.
Throughout history, philosophers have debated the merits of natural theology versus revealed religion.
His book offers a comprehensive overview of natural theology and its impact on Western thought.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: revelation - knowledge of God disclosed through divine means

Etymology

From Latin 'natura' (nature) and Greek 'theologia' (study of God).
