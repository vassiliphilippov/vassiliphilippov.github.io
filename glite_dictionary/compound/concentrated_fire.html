concentrated fire

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑn.sənˌtreɪ.tɪd ˈfaɪɚ / (AmE), / ˌkɒn.sənˌtreɪ.tɪd ˈfaɪə / (BrE)
gunfire from two or more weapons aimed simultaneously at a single target or area, especially in military operations
simultaneous gunfire at a single target

Examples

The platoon called in concentrated fire to suppress the enemy's machine gun nest.
Artillery units delivered concentrated fire on the bridge to prevent enemy reinforcements from crossing.
Naval commanders coordinated concentrated fire from several warships onto the shore defenses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barrage - a rapid, continuous discharge of artillery or gunfire
