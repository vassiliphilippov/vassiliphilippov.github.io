monkey puzzle

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌŋki ˌpʌzəl / (AmE), / ˈmʌŋki ˌpʌzl / (BrE)
a large South American evergreen coniferous tree (Araucaria araucana) with spreading, spiny branches and edible seeds, native to Chile and Argentina and often grown as an ornamental
a South American evergreen coniferous tree

Examples

A monkey puzzle can live for over one thousand years in its native habitat.
They planted a monkey puzzle in the corner of the botanical garden.
The sharp foliage of the monkey puzzle tree makes it difficult for animals to climb.

Etymology

The name is said to have arisen in the 1800s when someone stated that its strange, spiky branches would puzzle even a monkey.
