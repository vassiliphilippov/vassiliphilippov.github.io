chemical defence

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɛmɪkəl dɪˈfɛns / (AmE), / ˈkɛmɪkəl dɪˈfɛns / (BrE)
the actions or systems designed to protect people or equipment from harmful chemicals, especially during attacks or accidents involving chemical agents
protective measures against chemical agents

Examples

The army trained extensively in chemical defence to prepare for potential threats from hostile forces.
During the drill, all personnel were required to wear chemical defence gear.
Advances in chemical defence technology have improved safety for first responders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: biological defence - protective measures against biological agents
  • Antonym: chemical attack - an attack using toxic chemical agents
