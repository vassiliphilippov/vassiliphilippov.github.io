sympathy strike

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪmpəθi straɪk / (AmE), / ˈsɪmpəθi straɪk / (BrE)
a strike by workers to support other workers who are already on strike, rather than due to their own direct grievances
strike to support other workers

Examples

The union called a sympathy strike to show solidarity with workers in another city.
Many employees joined the sympathy strike even though they had no direct grievances against their own employer.
Management argued that the sympathy strike was illegal under the current labor laws.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strike - refusal to work as a form of protest
