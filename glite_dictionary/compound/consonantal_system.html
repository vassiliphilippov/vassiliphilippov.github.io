consonantal system

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑn.səˈnæn.t̬l̩ ˈsɪs.təm / (AmE), / ˌkɒn.səˈnæn.tl̩ ˈsɪs.təm / (BrE)
the system of consonants and their organization used in a particular language
system of consonants in a language

Examples

Linguists study the consonantal system of a language to understand its phonetic structure.
Arabic has a particularly rich consonantal system with sounds not found in English.
The consonantal system of English includes both voiced and voiceless sounds.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: vowel system - the system of vowels used in a particular language

Etymology

From Latin 'consonans' (sounding together) and Greek 'systema' (organized whole).
