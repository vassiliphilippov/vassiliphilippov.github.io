Australian spelling

1. NOUN

/ əˈstreɪliən ˈspɛlɪŋ / (AmE), / ɒˈstreɪliən ˈspɛlɪŋ / (BrE)
The system of English spelling conventions used in Australia, differing in some respects from British and American spelling.
Australian English orthography

Examples

Australian newspapers generally follow Australian spelling rather than British or American forms.
The word 'colour' is an example of Australian spelling differing from American English spelling.
When writing for an Australian audience, it's best to use Australian spelling throughout your document.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: British spelling - the system of spelling used in British English
  • Antonym: American spelling - the system of spelling used in American English
