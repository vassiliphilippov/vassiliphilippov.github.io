tissue plasminogen activator

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɪʃu ˌplæzməˌnoʊdʒən ˈæktəˌveɪtər / (AmE), / ˈtɪs.juː ˌplæz.mɪ.nəʊ.dʒən ˈæk.tɪ.veɪ.tə / (BrE)
a protein or drug that promotes the breakdown of blood clots, used especially to treat heart attacks and strokes
protein/drug that breaks down blood clots
medical

Examples

The doctors administered tissue plasminogen activator to dissolve the blood clot quickly.
Early use of tissue plasminogen activator can significantly improve stroke outcomes.
Researchers are studying new ways to deliver tissue plasminogen activator to patients more effectively.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thrombolytic - drug that dissolves blood clots
  • Antonym: anticoagulant - drug that prevents blood clotting

Etymology

Formed from 'tissue,' 'plasminogen' (from plasma + -in + -ogen), and 'activator.'
