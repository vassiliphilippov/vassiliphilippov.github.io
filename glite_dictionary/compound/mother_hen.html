mother hen

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌðɚ hɛn / (AmE), / ˈmʌðə hɛn / (BrE)
A hen that has chicks and looks after them.
a hen with chicks

Examples

The mother hen clucked softly as her chicks gathered around her.
Farmers watched the mother hen lead her brood across the yard every morning.
A mother hen will fiercely protect her young from any sign of danger.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hen - an adult female chicken

2. NOUN

/ ˈmʌðər hɛn / (AmE), / ˈmʌðə hɛn / (BrE)
a person who cares for the needs of others, often in an overly protective or interfering way
overprotective or caring person

Examples

Ever since the new intern started, Carla has acted like a mother hen, checking on her constantly.
He tends to be a mother hen to his younger siblings, making sure they have everything they need.
Sometimes, being a mother hen can make people feel smothered rather than supported.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overprotective - excessively protective of someone

3. NOUN

/ ˈmʌðɚ hɛn / (AmE), / ˈmʌðə hɛn / (BrE)
a person who is excessively protective or caring towards others, often fussing over their well-being
overly protective or nurturing person

Examples

As the team's mother hen, she made sure everyone had lunch and enough rest.
Try not to be such a mother hen—your kids need to learn independence.
At the office, Mark is a real mother hen, always checking that his colleagues are okay.

Synset Examples
