square flipper

1. NOUN

/ ˈskwɛr ˌflɪpɚ / (AmE), / ˈskwɛə ˌflɪpə / (BrE)
A medium-sized, greyish to yellow seal of the Arctic Ocean, having bristles on each side of its muzzle; also known as the bearded seal.
Arctic seal with bristled muzzle

Examples

The square flipper is often found resting on drifting ice in the Arctic region.
Researchers studied the whiskers of the square flipper to understand how it senses movement in the water.
Hunters in northern communities sometimes rely on the square flipper as a traditional food source.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bearded seal - A large Arctic seal with long whiskers, also called square flipper
