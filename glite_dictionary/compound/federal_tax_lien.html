federal tax lien

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɛdərəl tɑks lin / (AmE), / ˈfɛdərəl tæks liːn / (BrE)
A legal claim by the federal government on a taxpayer's property when they fail to pay taxes owed.
legal claim on property for unpaid federal taxes
legal american

Examples

The IRS imposed a federal tax lien on his property after years of unpaid taxes.
You cannot sell the house until the federal tax lien has been resolved.
A federal tax lien can affect your credit score and ability to get loans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lien - a legal right to keep possession of property until a debt is paid
