blood feud

1. NOUN

/ blʌd fjud / (AmE), / blʌd fjuːd / (BrE)
A prolonged and bitter conflict between families or groups, in which members of each side seek vengeance by killing members of the other.
prolonged cycle of retaliatory killings

Examples

The two families have been locked in a blood feud for generations, each seeking revenge for past killings.
In some regions, a blood feud can last decades and involve multiple extended families.
The novel is set against the backdrop of a violent blood feud in a remote mountain village.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vendetta - a prolonged bitter feud or campaign for revenge, especially one involving murder
