passive immunity

1. NOUN

/ ˈpæsɪv ɪˈmjunəti / (AmE), / ˈpæsɪv ɪˈmjuːnəti / (BrE)
a temporary form of immunity to a disease obtained by the introduction of antibodies from another individual, either naturally or by injection
temporary immunity from antibodies acquired externally
medical

Examples

Newborn babies receive passive immunity from their mothers through antibodies passed via the placenta.
A patient can be given passive immunity by injecting them with antibodies to fight a specific infection.
While passive immunity offers immediate protection, it does not last as long as active immunity.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: active immunity - immunity produced by the body's own immune system after exposure to a pathogen or vaccine

Etymology

From 'passive' meaning not produced by one's own activity, and 'immunity', Latin 'immunitas' (exemption).
