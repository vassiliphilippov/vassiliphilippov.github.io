umbrella sedge

1. NOUN

/ ʌmˈbrɛl.ə sɛdʒ / (AmE), / ʌmˈbrɛl.ə sɛdʒ / (BrE)
An African sedge widely cultivated as an ornamental water plant, known for its terminal umbrellalike cluster of slender, grasslike leaves.
ornamental African sedge with umbrella-shaped leaves

Examples

The umbrella sedge is often planted around garden ponds for its striking appearance.
Botanists admire the umbrella sedge for its unique cluster of radiating leaves.
Many nurseries recommend the umbrella sedge as a low-maintenance aquatic plant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cyperus alternifolius - scientific name for umbrella sedge
