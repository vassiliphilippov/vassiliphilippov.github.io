virgin's bower

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɜrdʒɪnz ˈbaʊ.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈvɜːdʒɪnz ˈbaʊ.ə / (BrE)
a climbing plant of eastern North America, noted for sprawling over other vegetation and bearing clusters of small creamy white flowers
climbing plant with white flowers (eastern North America)

Examples

The virgin's bower climbed the old wooden fence, filling the garden with delicate white blooms each summer.
Many gardeners appreciate virgin's bower for its rapid growth and attractive flowers.
Wild virgin's bower can often be seen sprawling over hedgerows and shrubs in rural landscapes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clematis - a genus of climbing ornamental plants to which virgin's bower belongs
