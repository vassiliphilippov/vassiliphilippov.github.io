stem vowel

1. NOUN

/ stɛm ˈvaʊəl / (AmE), / stɛm ˈvaʊəl / (BrE)
a vowel that appears at the end of a stem and immediately before an inflectional ending, especially in inflected languages
vowel ending a stem before inflection

Examples

In Latin verbs, the stem vowel often changes to indicate tense or mood.
The addition of the stem vowel makes the verb easier to conjugate.
Some languages use a stem vowel to separate the stem from the suffix.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: theme vowel - a vowel inserted in a word stem to connect to inflectional endings; often synonymous in linguistics

Etymology

From 'stem' (main part of a word) and 'vowel' (speech sound), used in linguistics.
