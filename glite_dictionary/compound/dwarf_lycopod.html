dwarf lycopod

1. NOUN

/ dwɔrf ˈlaɪkəˌpɒd / (AmE), / dwɔːf ˈlʌɪkəpɒd / (BrE)
A tufted spikemoss that forms loose, spreading mats, found in eastern North America.
a tufted spikemoss of eastern North America

Examples

The dwarf lycopod can be found carpeting the forest floor in moist, shaded areas.
Botanists recorded the presence of dwarf lycopod in several locations across eastern North America.
Many gardeners appreciate dwarf lycopod for its low-growing, mat-forming habit in rock gardens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spikemoss - a genus of clubmoss-like plants (Selaginella)
