Carpathian Mountains

1. PROPN

/ kɑrˈpeɪθiən ˈmaʊntənz / (AmE), / kɑːˈpeɪθiən ˈmaʊntənz / (BrE)
a mountain range in central and eastern Europe, stretching from Slovakia and southern Poland through western Ukraine to northeastern Romania
a mountain range in central and eastern Europe

Examples

Many tourists visit the Carpathian Mountains for hiking and breathtaking views.
The Carpathian Mountains form a natural border between several Eastern European countries.
Wildlife, such as brown bears and wolves, still roam the forests of the Carpathian Mountains.

Etymology

From Latin 'Carpates Montes' (Carpathian Mountains), likely named after the Dacian tribe Carpi.
