line of flight

1. NOUN

/ laɪn əv flaɪt / (AmE), / laɪn ɒv flaɪt / (BrE)
the path taken by a moving object as it travels through the air
path of an object through the air

Examples

The arrow followed a graceful line of flight before striking the target.
Engineers studied the line of flight of the drone to optimize its performance.
From the stands, the fans could trace the line of flight of the baseball as it soared over the fence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trajectory - the curved path that an object follows as it moves through space
