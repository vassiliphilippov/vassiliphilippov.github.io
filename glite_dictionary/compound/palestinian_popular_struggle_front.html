Palestinian Popular Struggle Front

1. PROPN

/ ˌpæləsˈtɪniən ˈpɑpjələr ˈstrʌgəl frʌnt / (AmE), / ˌpaləsˈtɪːnɪən ˈpɒpjʊlə ˈstrʌɡəl frʌnt / (BrE)
a Palestinian militant organization established in 1967, originally split from al-Fatah, known for attacks against Israel and later close relations with al-Fatah.
Palestinian militant group formed in 1967

Examples

The Palestinian Popular Struggle Front was involved in several cross-border attacks during the 1970s.
Discussions included representatives from both al-Fatah and the Palestinian Popular Struggle Front.
Members of the Palestinian Popular Struggle Front have shifted their focus toward political negotiations in recent years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: al-Fatah - a major Palestinian political and military organization
