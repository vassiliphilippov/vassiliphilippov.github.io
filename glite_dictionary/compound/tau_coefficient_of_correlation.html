tau coefficient of correlation

1. NOUN

/ taʊ koʊˌɪfɪʃənt əv kəˌrɛˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / taʊ kəʊˌɪfɪʃənt əv kəˌrɛˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
a nonparametric statistic used to measure the degree of agreement between two rankings, indicating the strength and direction of their association
statistic for rank correlation
medical

Examples

The tau coefficient of correlation can reveal the degree of association between judges' rankings of contest entries.
Researchers used the tau coefficient of correlation to analyze the consistency of survey responses.
A high tau coefficient of correlation suggests strong agreement between two rating methods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rank correlation coefficient - a statistic used to measure ordinal association between two variables
  • Degree Lower: Pearson correlation coefficient - a statistic measuring linear correlation between two variables

Etymology

From Greek letter tau (τ), used as a symbol for this correlation measure.
