old days

1. NOUN

/ oʊld deɪz / (AmE), / əʊld deɪz / (BrE)
A time in the past, especially one remembered with nostalgia.
a nostalgic reference to the past

Examples

My grandparents love to talk about the old days when life seemed simpler.
In the old days, people wrote letters instead of sending emails.
He often reminisced about the old days when he was growing up in the countryside.

Synset Examples

In the old days, kids played games for two ha'pennies for a penny.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bygone days - days in the past; former times
  • Antonym: present day - the current time; the present
