minimal art

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪnəməl ɑrt / (AmE), / ˈmɪnɪməl ɑːt / (BrE)
an art movement, originating in the 1950s, characterized by extreme simplification of form and color in sculpture and painting
art movement characterized by simplicity

Examples

Many museums have dedicated galleries to minimal art from the 1960s and 1970s.
Her collection focuses mainly on minimal art, with works by Donald Judd and Dan Flavin.
Critics have praised the serene beauty found in minimal art pieces.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: minimalism - an art movement emphasizing simplicity and minimal design
  • Antonym: abstract expressionism - an art movement characterized by expressive, non-representational forms
