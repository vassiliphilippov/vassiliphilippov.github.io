civil officer

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪvəl ˈɑfəsər / (AmE), / ˈsɪvəl ˈɒfɪsə / (BrE)
a person who holds an official position in government or administration, especially one dealing with civilian affairs rather than military matters
official in civilian government affairs

Examples

The president appointed a new civil officer to oversee the city's public health services.
As a civil officer, her duties included issuing marriage licenses and supervising local elections.
Only a civil officer has the authority to enforce zoning regulations within this district.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: official - a person holding office or authority, especially in an organization or government

Etymology

From 'civil', relating to citizens or civilian life, and 'officer', from Old French 'officier', meaning person with official duties.
