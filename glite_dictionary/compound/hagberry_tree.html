hagberry tree

1. NOUN

/ ˈhæɡˌbɛri triː / (AmE), / ˈhæɡbəri triː / (BrE)
a small European cherry tree (Prunus padus) closely resembling the American chokecherry, with clusters of small, dark berries
European cherry tree with small dark berries

Examples

The hagberry tree is valued for its ornamental flowers and clusters of dark berries.
Along the old country lane, a single hagberry tree stood in bloom each spring.
Birds flocked to the hagberry tree to feed on its ripening fruit in late summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chokecherry - American cherry tree closely resembling the hagberry

Etymology

From 'hagberry', of uncertain origin, possibly related to Old Norse 'hegg' (bird cherry), and 'tree'.
