marine engineer

1. NOUN

/ məˈrin ɛn.dʒəˌnɪr / (AmE), / məˈriːn ˌɛn.dʒɪˈnɪə / (BrE)
a naval officer or professional responsible for the operation and maintenance of a ship's engines and mechanical systems
ship engine and systems officer/professional

Examples

The marine engineer conducted a routine inspection of the ship's engine room.
As a marine engineer, she ensures all mechanical systems on board are in excellent condition.
After the breakdown, a marine engineer was called to repair the main engine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: engineer - a person who designs, builds, or maintains engines or machines

Etymology

From Latin 'marinus' (of the sea) and 'engineer' (from Old French 'engigneor').
