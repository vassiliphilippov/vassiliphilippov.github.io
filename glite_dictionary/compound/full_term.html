full term

1. NOUN

/ fʊl tɝm / (AmE), / fʊl tɜːm / (BrE)
the point in pregnancy when gestation is complete and birth is imminent or due
completion of pregnancy, ready for birth
medical

Examples

The baby was delivered at full term with no complications.
Most pregnancies last about forty weeks, reaching full term before childbirth.
She was relieved when her doctor confirmed the pregnancy had reached full term.

Synset Examples

a healthy baby born at full term

Related Terms

  • Antonym: premature - born before the full term of gestation
