potassium iodide

1. NOUN

/ pəˌtæsiəm ˈaɪəˌdaɪd / (AmE), / pəˌtasiəm ˈʌɪədaɪd / (BrE)
a white crystalline salt used in medicine, organic synthesis, photographic processes, and as an additive in iodized salt
a white crystalline salt (KI) used in various fields

Examples

The pharmacist supplied potassium iodide tablets to protect against radiation.
Potassium iodide is commonly added to table salt to help prevent iodine deficiency.
Chemists use potassium iodide in the preparation of certain photographic materials.

Etymology

From 'potassium' (from Modern Latin 'potassa' + '-ium') and 'iodide' (from 'iodine' + '-ide', indicating a compound with iodine).
