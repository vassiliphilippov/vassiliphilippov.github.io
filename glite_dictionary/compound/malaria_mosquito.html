malaria mosquito

1. NOUN

/ məˈlɛriə məˈskiːtoʊ / (AmE), / məˈleəriə məˈskiːtəʊ / (BrE)
a mosquito of the genus Anopheles, especially those species that transmit the parasite responsible for malaria in humans
mosquito that transmits malaria

Examples

The malaria mosquito is responsible for spreading the deadly disease in tropical regions.
Researchers are studying ways to control the population of the malaria mosquito.
Wearing insect repellent can help protect you from the bites of the malaria mosquito.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Anopheles - genus of mosquitoes that includes malaria vectors
  • Synonym: mosquito - small flying insect that bites and feeds on blood
