Midwestern United States

1. NOUN

/ mɪdˈwɛstərn ˌjuːnaɪtɪd ˈsteɪts / (AmE), / mɪdˈwɛstən ˌjuːnaɪtɪd ˈsteɪts / (BrE)
the north-central region of the United States, often referred to as the heartland or breadbasket of America
region in north-central United States
american

Examples

She grew up in the Midwestern United States and remembers long, snowy winters.
Agriculture plays a significant role in the economy of the Midwestern United States.
Many major rivers flow through the Midwestern United States, including the Mississippi and the Missouri.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: American Midwest - central-northern region of the United States
  • Antonym: Southern United States - region comprising the southern portion of the United States
  • Antonym: Northeastern United States - region comprising the northeastern portion of the United States
