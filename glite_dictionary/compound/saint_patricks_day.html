Saint Patrick's Day

1. NOUN

/ seɪnt ˈpætrɪks deɪ / (AmE), / seɪnt ˈpætrɪks deɪ / (BrE)
the annual holiday on March 17th commemorating Saint Patrick, the patron saint of Ireland, traditionally marked by celebrations of Irish culture
Irish holiday celebrated on March 17th

Examples

Many cities hold large parades to celebrate Saint Patrick's Day every year.
People often wear green on Saint Patrick's Day to honor Irish heritage.
On Saint Patrick's Day, Irish pubs are usually filled with music and laughter.

Etymology

Named after Saint Patrick (5th century), the patron saint of Ireland, whose feast day is March 17th.
