silver wattle

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪlvɚ ˈwɑːtəl / (AmE), / ˈsɪlvə ˈwɒtəl / (BrE)
an evergreen tree native to Australasia, with white or silvery bark, young silvery leaves, and yellow flowers, commonly known as Acacia dealbata.
Australasian evergreen tree with silvery bark and yellow flowers

Examples

The silver wattle blooms with vibrant yellow flowers in late winter.
Gardeners in Australia often plant silver wattle for its ornamental beauty and rapid growth.
The bark of the silver wattle is smooth and has a distinctive silvery appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Acacia dealbata - the botanical name for silver wattle
