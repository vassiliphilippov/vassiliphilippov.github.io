excursion rate

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˈskɝːʒən reɪt / (AmE), / ɪkˈskɜːʃən reɪt / (BrE)
a reduced fare offered for a round-trip journey, typically for travel within a set period or under specific conditions
discounted round-trip fare

Examples

If you purchase your ticket in advance, you may qualify for the excursion rate on the return journey.
Many travelers opt for the excursion rate because it offers significant savings over standard fares.
The airline advertised an excursion rate for round-trip flights during the holiday season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: discount fare - a fare offered at a lower price than the standard rate
  • Antonym: full fare - the standard or regular price for a ticket

Etymology

From 'excursion' (from Latin 'excursio', meaning 'an expedition') and 'rate' (from Old French 'rate', from Latin 'rata').
