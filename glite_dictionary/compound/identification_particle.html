identification particle

1. NOUN

/ aɪˌdɛntəfɪˈkeɪʃən ˈpɑːrtɪkl̩ / (AmE), / aɪˌdɛntɪfɪˈkeɪʃən ˈpɑːtɪkəl / (BrE)
a microscopic particle added to a product to identify or trace the source of manufacture
tiny traceable marker in products

Examples

Manufacturers use identification particles to track the origins of their products.
A single identification particle can be enough to prove authenticity in forensic investigations.
Advanced packaging often contains identification particles invisible to the naked eye.
