musical memory

1. NOUN

/ ˈmjuː.zɪ.kəl ˈmɛm.ə.ri / (AmE), / ˈmjuː.zɪ.kəl ˈmem.əri / (BrE)
the ability to remember and recognize music, such as melodies, tones, or other musical elements
the ability to remember music

Examples

Her musical memory allowed her to play complex pieces after hearing them just once.
Even after years away from the piano, his musical memory remained strong.
A good musical memory is essential for composers and conductors.

Synset Examples

Musical memory is considered to be partly independent from other memory systems.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: memory - the faculty by which the mind stores and remembers information
