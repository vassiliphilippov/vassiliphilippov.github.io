Oxford movement

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑksfərd ˈmuvmənt / (AmE), / ˈɒksfəd ˈmuːvmənt / (BrE)
a 19th-century movement within the Church of England that sought to revive Catholic traditions and resist liberal reforms
19th-century Anglican religious revival movement

Examples

The Oxford movement influenced Anglican worship practices in the nineteenth century.
Many clergymen associated with the Oxford movement published influential religious tracts.
Historians trace the origins of modern Anglo-Catholicism to the Oxford movement.

Etymology

Named after Oxford University, where the movement originated in the 1830s.
