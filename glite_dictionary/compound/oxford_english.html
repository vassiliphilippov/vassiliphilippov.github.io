Oxford English

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑksfərd ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈɒksfəd ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ / (BrE)
the variety of English traditionally spoken at Oxford University, often perceived as affected or pretentious.
English dialect associated with Oxford University

Examples

Many people perceive Oxford English as a mark of social status.
She adopted Oxford English to fit in at the university.
Some critics find Oxford English affected and pretentious.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Received Pronunciation - the standard accent of Standard English in the UK
