southeastern pocket gopher

1. NOUN

/ ˌsaʊθˈiːstɚn ˈpɑkɪt ˈɡoʊfɚ / (AmE), / ˌsaʊθˈiːstən ˈpɒkɪt ˈɡəʊfə / (BrE)
A species of pocket gopher native to Alabama, Georgia, and Florida, known for burrowing habits.
a gopher species found in southeastern U.S.

Examples

The southeastern pocket gopher digs extensive tunnels in the sandy soils of Florida.
Unlike other species, the southeastern pocket gopher is rarely seen above ground during the day.
Farmers sometimes find the southeastern pocket gopher a nuisance because of its burrowing under fields.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gopher - a burrowing rodent native to North and Central America
