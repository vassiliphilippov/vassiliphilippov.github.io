tube foot

1. NOUN

/ tuːb fʊt / (AmE), / tjuːb fʊt / (BrE)
A small, flexible, tubular appendage found on echinoderms, such as starfish and sea urchins, typically with a sucker at the end, used for movement, feeding, and respiration.
appendage of echinoderms used for movement and feeding

Examples

A starfish moves slowly across the ocean floor by extending its tube feet.
The tube feet of a sea urchin help it cling to rocks and gather food.
Scientists observed how damaged tube feet of the sea cucumber regrew over time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: podium - a scientific term for a foot or foot-like structure in animals

Etymology

From 'tube', referring to the shape, and 'foot', indicating its appendage function.
