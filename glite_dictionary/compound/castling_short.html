castling short

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæs.lɪŋ ʃɔrt / (AmE), / ˈkɑːs.lɪŋ ʃɔːt / (BrE)
In chess, castling on the kingside, moving the king two squares towards the rook on its original square and then placing the rook next to the king.
chess move, kingside castling

Examples

In many opening positions, castling short is considered the safest option for the king.
After castling short, White quickly began an attack on the opposite side of the board.
Some players prefer castling short, while others take risks with queenside castling.

Synset Examples

Since a positional player will always keep his options open and his king safe, castling short would be a good option, and is played most of the time at top level.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: castling long - In chess, castling on the queenside

Etymology

From 'castle' (the chess move) + 'short', referring to the shorter distance the king moves when castling on the kingside.
