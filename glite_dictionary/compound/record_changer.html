record changer

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛkərd ˈtʃeɪndʒər / (AmE), / ˈrɛkɔːd ˈtʃeɪndʒə / (BrE)
an automatic device on a record player that selects and plays successive records without manual intervention
automatic record-playing device

Examples

The old stereo system in the basement includes a record changer that can play six albums automatically.
He repaired the vintage record changer so it would switch discs smoothly from one to the next.
Collectors often seek out turntables with a functioning record changer for added convenience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: turntable - a rotating platform for playing phonograph records
