ordinal number

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdɪnəl ˈnʌmbər / (AmE), / ˈɔːdɪnəl ˈnʌmbə / (BrE)
a number that indicates position or order in a sequence, such as first, second, or third
number indicating position in a sequence

Examples

"Fourth" is an example of an ordinal number in English.
Children learn ordinal numbers like "first," "second," and "third" in early grades.
Please list the steps using ordinal numbers to show the correct order.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cardinal number - a number that represents quantity (one, two, three, etc.)

Etymology

From Latin ordinis (order) + English number.
