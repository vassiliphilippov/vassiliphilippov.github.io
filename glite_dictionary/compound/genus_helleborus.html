genus Helleborus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs hɛˈlɛbərəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs hɛlɪˈbɔːrəs / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the buttercup family (Ranunculaceae), commonly known as hellebores, which includes several species of perennial herbs.
a genus of perennial herbs (hellebores)

Examples

Many gardens feature genus Helleborus for their early spring blooms and attractive foliage.
Members of the genus Helleborus are prized for their resilience in shaded parts of the garden.
Botanists classify hellebores under the genus Helleborus, part of the Ranunculaceae family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: helleborus - a plant of the genus Helleborus

Etymology

From Latin 'genus' (kind, type) and 'Helleborus', the classical Latin name for the plant.
