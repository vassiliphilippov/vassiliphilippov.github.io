-proof

1. ADJ

/ pruːf / (AmE), / pruːf / (BrE)
(in combination) resistant or protected against a specified thing or able to withstand it; not affected by it.
resistant to a specified thing (as a suffix)

Examples

The windows in the laboratory are shatterproof to ensure safety.
She bought a childproof bottle so her son couldn't open it.
This jacket is completely waterproof, so you'll stay dry in the rain.

Synset Examples

temptation-proof
childproof locks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resistant - not affected by something; able to withstand

Etymology

From Middle English *proef*, from Old French *proeve* (test, proof), ultimately from Latin *probare* (to test, to prove). Used as a productive suffix since the 19th century.
