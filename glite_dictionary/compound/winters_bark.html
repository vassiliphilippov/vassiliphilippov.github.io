winter's bark

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪntɚz bɑrk / (AmE), / ˈwɪntəz bɑːk / (BrE)
the aromatic bark of certain South American trees, formerly used as a remedy and spice for its tonic and stimulant properties
aromatic medicinal bark

Examples

Early explorers used winter's bark to prevent scurvy during long sea voyages.
The spicy taste of winter's bark made it popular in traditional medicine.
An herbalist recommended adding powdered winter's bark to tea for its stimulating effect.

Etymology

Named for William Winter, a 16th-century English explorer associated with its introduction to Europe.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɪntərz bɑrk / (AmE), / ˈwɪntəz bɑːk / (BrE)
A South American evergreen tree (Drimys winteri) whose aromatic bark was once used as a spice and remedy for scurvy.
aromatic bark of a South American tree

Examples

Sailors once relied on winter's bark as a source of vitamin C during long voyages.
The aromatic winter's bark was often used in traditional medicine in South America.
Botanists studied winter's bark for its potential culinary and medicinal uses.

Etymology

Named after Captain John Winter, who used the bark to prevent scurvy during voyages in the 16th century.
