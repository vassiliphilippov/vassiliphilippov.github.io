anemone fish

1. NOUN

/ əˈnɛməni fɪʃ / (AmE), / əˈnɛməni fɪʃ / (BrE)
a small brightly colored tropical marine fish that lives among the tentacles of sea anemones, with which it has a symbiotic relationship
a tropical fish living among sea anemones

Examples

The anemone fish is often seen darting among the stinging tentacles of sea anemones.
Many aquarium enthusiasts keep anemone fish because of their vibrant colors and interesting behavior.
The anemone fish avoids predators by seeking refuge within the poisonous arms of its host.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clownfish - a type of anemone fish especially known for its orange and white stripes

Etymology

From 'anemone' (a type of sea creature) and 'fish'.
