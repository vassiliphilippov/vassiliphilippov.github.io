suborder Microchiroptera

1. NOUN

/ ˌsʌbˌɔrdər ˌmaɪkroʊˌkaɪˈrɒptərə / (AmE), / ˌsʌbˌɔːdə ˌmaɪkrəʊkaɪˈrɒptərə / (BrE)
A suborder of bats that includes most species except fruit bats; commonly known as insectivorous bats.
suborder of insectivorous bats

Examples

The suborder Microchiroptera comprises the majority of bat species found worldwide.
Most insect-eating bats belong to the suborder Microchiroptera, unlike fruit bats.
Researchers study the echolocation abilities unique to the suborder Microchiroptera.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Megachiroptera - suborder of bats known as fruit bats
  • Degree Higher: Chiroptera - order that includes all bats

Etymology

From New Latin: 'micro-' (small) + 'Chiroptera' (order of bats), from Greek 'cheir' (hand) and 'pteron' (wing).
