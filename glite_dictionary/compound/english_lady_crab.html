English lady crab

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ ˈleɪdi kræb / (AmE), / ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ ˈleɪdi kræb / (BrE)
A species of crab native to the coasts of England, known for its distinctive appearance.
a crab species native to English coasts

Examples

The English lady crab is recognized by spots on its shell.
During our seaside trip, we spotted an English lady crab among the rocks.
Fishermen along the English coast often encounter the English lady crab in shallow waters.
