Communist Party

1. NOUN

/ kəˈmjunɪst ˈpɑrti / (AmE), / kəˈmjuːnɪst ˈpɑːti / (BrE)
A political party that advocates for and seeks to implement a communist form of government; in some countries, it is the only legal political party.
political party advocating communism

Examples

The Communist Party rose to power after the revolution in 1949.
He was a lifelong member of the Communist Party and supported its ideology.
In some countries, the Communist Party controls all branches of government.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: communism - a political and economic system advocating common ownership of production
  • Degree Lower: socialist party - a political party supporting socialism, often distinct from Communist Parties
