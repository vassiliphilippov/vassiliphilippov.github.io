running noose

1. NOUN

/ ˈrʌnɪŋ nus / (AmE), / ˈrʌnɪŋ nuːs / (BrE)
a loop formed in a rope or cord by a slipknot, which tightens as the rope is pulled
slipknot loop that tightens when pulled

Examples

The trap was set using a running noose to capture the animal safely.
He made a running noose at the end of the rope for the rescue operation.
A simple running noose can be used when tying a quick-release knot.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slipknot - a knot that slips along the rope or cord and tightens under strain
