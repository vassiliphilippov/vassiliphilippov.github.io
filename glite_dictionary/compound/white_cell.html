white cell

1. NOUN

/ ˈwaɪt ˌsɛl / (AmE), / ˈwaɪt ˌsɛl / (BrE)
a type of blood cell that helps protect the body by engulfing and destroying bacteria, fungi, and other harmful organisms
blood cell involved in immune defense

Examples

The doctor said my white cell count was higher than normal due to the infection.
A low white cell level can make you more vulnerable to illness.
Researchers study how white cells respond to different bacteria in the lab.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: white blood cell - another term for white cell; immune system blood cell
  • Antonym: red cell - blood cell that carries oxygen (erythrocyte)
