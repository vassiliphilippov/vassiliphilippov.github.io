forward market

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔrwɚd ˌmɑrkɪt / (AmE), / ˈfɔːwəd ˌmɑːkɪt / (BrE)
a financial market in which forward or futures contracts for commodities or securities are bought and sold for delivery at a future date
market for trading forward or futures contracts

Examples

Many companies use the forward market to hedge against price fluctuations in raw materials.
Currency rates can be locked in through contracts in the forward market.
The forward market for oil experienced increased activity ahead of the winter season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: futures market - market in which futures contracts are bought and sold

Etymology

From 'forward' (ahead in time) + 'market'.
