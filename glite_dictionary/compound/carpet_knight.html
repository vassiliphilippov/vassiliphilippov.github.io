carpet knight

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːrpɪt naɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɑːpɪt naɪt / (BrE)
A knight who enjoys a life of luxury and idleness, often having been knighted at court rather than for valor in battle.
idle knight honored at court, not in battle
archaic

Examples

He was dismissed by his peers as merely a carpet knight untested by war.
In times of peace, many a carpet knight found favor at court through flattery and charm.
Unlike the carpet knights of the royal halls, she sought glory on the battlefield.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: knight - A man awarded a non-hereditary title by a monarch for merit or service, especially in battle.

Etymology

From 'carpet' (symbolizing courtly luxury) and 'knight'; refers to those knighted on the palace carpet, not the battlefield.
