tidal current

1. NOUN

/ ˈtaɪdəl ˈkɜrənt / (AmE), / ˈtaɪdəl ˈkʌrənt / (BrE)
a water current generated by the movement of tides
water current caused by tides

Examples

Boats in the harbor must account for the strength of the tidal current.
The tidal current changed direction as the tide began to rise.
Marine animals often migrate along the path of the tidal current.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ocean current - a continuous, directed movement of seawater
