Alaskan pipeline

1. NOUN

/ əˈlæskən ˈpaɪplaɪn / (AmE), / əˈlæskən ˈpaɪplaɪn / (BrE)
an oil pipeline in Alaska that runs about 800 miles from Prudhoe Bay to the port of Valdez
Major oil pipeline in Alaska

Examples

The Alaskan pipeline was constructed in the 1970s to transport crude oil across the state.
Environmental groups have raised concerns about the impact of the Alaskan pipeline on local wildlife.
Thousands of workers were involved in building the Alaskan pipeline, one of the largest construction projects in U.S. history.
