subfamily Malaconotinae

1. NOUN

/ sʌbˌfæməli ˌmæləkəˈnoʊtɪniː / (AmE), / sʌbˌfæməli ˌmaləkəˈnəʊtɪniː / (BrE)
A subfamily of African bush shrikes in the family Malaconotidae, consisting of passerine birds native to Africa.
subfamily of African bush shrikes

Examples

The subfamily Malaconotinae includes several striking bird species found throughout sub-Saharan Africa.
Researchers studied the vocalizations unique to the subfamily Malaconotinae to better understand their evolution.
Conservation efforts are underway to protect members of the subfamily Malaconotinae from habitat loss.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Malaconotidae - family of African bush shrikes
  • Degree Lower: Malaconotus - a genus within Malaconotinae

Etymology

From New Latin 'Malaconotinae', based on the genus Malaconotus and the suffix '-inae' used for animal subfamilies.
