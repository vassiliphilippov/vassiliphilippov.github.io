all-or-none

1. ADJ

/ ˌɔl əɹ ˈnʌn / (AmE), / ˌɔːl ɔː ˈnʌn / (BrE)
(Of a process or response) occurring fully or not occurring at all; allowing no partial action or effect.
happening completely or not at all

Examples

The nerve's response to stimuli is all-or-none, meaning it either fires completely or not at all.
The company implemented an all-or-none approach to the policy change, refusing to allow exceptions.
Her commitment to projects tends to be all-or-none; she either dedicates herself fully or not at all.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: partial - involving only part, not the whole
