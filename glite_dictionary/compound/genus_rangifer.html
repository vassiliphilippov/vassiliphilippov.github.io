genus Rangifer

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈræn.dʒɪ.fər / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈran.dʒɪ.fə / (BrE)
A genus of deer that includes the reindeer and caribou, found in Arctic and sub-Arctic regions.
genus of deer including reindeer and caribou

Examples

The genus Rangifer comprises animals commonly known as reindeer or caribou.
Biologists study the distribution of genus Rangifer across the Arctic tundra.
Within the genus Rangifer, the best-known species is the reindeer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reindeer - A species of deer within the genus Rangifer, also known as caribou in North America
  • Synonym: caribou - The North American name for reindeer, a species within this genus

Etymology

From Latin 'genus' (kind, type) and scientific 'Rangifer', the genus name for reindeer and caribou.
