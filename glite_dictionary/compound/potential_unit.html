potential unit

1. NOUN

/ pəˈtɛnʃəl ˈjuːnɪt / (AmE), / pəˈtɛnʃəl ˈjuːnɪt / (BrE)
a unit of measurement for electric potential energy per unit charge at a point in a circuit compared with a reference point, usually ground
unit of electrical potential energy per charge

Examples

The potential unit used in most electrical calculations is the volt.
Engineers measure electric potential differences in potential units to ensure circuit safety.
A one-volt potential unit means one joule of energy per coulomb of charge.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: volt - the SI unit of electric potential
