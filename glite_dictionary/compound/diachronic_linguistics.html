diachronic linguistics

1. NOUN

/ ˌdaɪ.əˈkrɑː.nɪk lɪŋˈɡwɪs.tɪks / (AmE), / ˌdaɪ.əˈkrɒ.nɪk lɪŋˈɡwɪs.tɪks / (BrE)
the branch of linguistics concerned with the historical development and evolution of languages over time
study of language change over time

Examples

She specialized in diachronic linguistics, focusing on how English has evolved through the centuries.
A key question in diachronic linguistics is how sound changes spread across different language communities.
The book offers a comprehensive introduction to diachronic linguistics for undergraduate students.

Synset Examples

the synchrony and diachrony of language

Related Terms

  • Antonym: synchronic linguistics - study of a language at a specific point in time

Etymology

From Greek dia ('through') + chronos ('time') + linguistics.
