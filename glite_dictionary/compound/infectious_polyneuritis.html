infectious polyneuritis

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈfɛkʃəs ˌpɒlɪn(j)uˈraɪtəs / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɛkʃəs ˌpɒlɪnjuːˈraɪtɪs / (BrE)
a medical condition in which inflammation of multiple peripheral nerves causes pain, weakness, and sometimes paralysis, often with an unknown cause
inflammation of multiple peripheral nerves
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with infectious polyneuritis after experiencing rapid muscle weakness and numbness in his limbs.
Early treatment of infectious polyneuritis can sometimes improve the chances of full recovery.
Researchers continue to investigate the possible causes of infectious polyneuritis, as its origins remain unclear.

Etymology

From 'poly-' meaning many, and 'neuritis' meaning inflammation of nerves.
