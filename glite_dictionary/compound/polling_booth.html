polling booth

1. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊlɪŋ buːθ / (AmE), / ˈpəʊlɪŋ buːθ / (BrE)
A small, enclosed space in a polling place where voters can mark their ballots in private.
private voting place at an election

Examples

Each voter entered the polling booth to mark their ballot in complete privacy.
Large lines formed outside as people waited for their turn in the polling booth.
Election officials checked that each polling booth was properly set up before voting began.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: voting booth - a small private area for voters to mark ballots
  • Degree Higher: polling station - the building where voting takes place
