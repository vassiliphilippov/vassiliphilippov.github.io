welter of information

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɛltər əv ˌɪnfərˈmeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈwɛltə əv ˌɪnfəˈmeɪʃən / (BrE)
a confused or jumbled mass of information or data; an overwhelming mixture of details
a chaotic mix of information

Examples

The welter of information online makes it hard to know what is accurate.
Faced with a welter of information, the team struggled to find the relevant facts.
She tried to make sense of the welter of information presented at the meeting.

Synset Examples

The welter of information was overwhelming.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flood of information - an overwhelming quantity of information

Etymology

From 'welter' meaning a confused mass, which comes from Middle English 'welteren' (to roll).
