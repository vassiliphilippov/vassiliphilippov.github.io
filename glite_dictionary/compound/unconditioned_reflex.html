unconditioned reflex

1. NOUN

/ ˌʌn.kənˈdɪʃ.ənd ˈriː.flɛks / (AmE), / ˌʌn.kənˈdɪʃ.ənd ˈriː.flɛks / (BrE)
a reflex response that occurs automatically in reaction to a stimulus, without prior learning or conditioning
automatic, unlearned reflex response
medical

Examples

The knee-jerk reaction at the doctor is an example of an unconditioned reflex.
An unconditioned reflex does not require any prior training or learning to appear.
Babies are born with several unconditioned reflexes, such as sucking and grasping.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: conditioned reflex - a learned response to a stimulus after repeated association

Etymology

From 'unconditioned' (not learned by conditioning) and 'reflex' (from Latin reflexus, bent back).
