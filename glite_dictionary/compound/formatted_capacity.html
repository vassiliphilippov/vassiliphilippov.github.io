formatted capacity

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔrˌmætɪd kəˈpæsəti / (AmE), / ˈfɔːˌmætɪd kəˈpæsəti / (BrE)
(in computing) the usable storage space available on a disk after it has been formatted, excluding space taken up by system information and disk structure.
usable space on a formatted disk

Examples

After formatting the drive, the formatted capacity was noticeably less than the advertised size.
Manufacturers often list the raw capacity, but users should check the formatted capacity for actual usable space.
Software utilities can show you the formatted capacity of your hard disk in megabytes or gigabytes.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: raw capacity - the total physical storage of a disk before formatting
