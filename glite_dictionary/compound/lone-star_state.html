Lone-Star State

1. PROPN

/ ˈloʊn stɑr steɪt / (AmE), / ˈləʊn stɑː steɪt / (BrE)
a nickname for Texas, the second largest state in the United States, located in the southwest and bordering the Gulf of Mexico
nickname for the state of Texas

Examples

Many people dream of visiting the Lone-Star State because of its rich culture and history.
The Lone-Star State is famous for its barbecue and live music scenes.
She moved from California to the Lone-Star State in search of new career opportunities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Texas - a state in the southern United States

Etymology

Refers to the single star on the Texas state flag, symbolizing its former status as an independent republic.
