striated muscle fiber

1. NOUN

/ straɪˈeɪtɪd ˈmʌsəl ˈfaɪbɚ / (AmE), / straɪˈeɪtɪd ˈmʌsəl ˈfaɪbə / (BrE)
a long, slender cell found in striated muscle tissue that is capable of contraction
contractile cell in striated muscle tissue
medical

Examples

Each striated muscle fiber is made up of repeating units called sarcomeres.
Damage to a striated muscle fiber can impair muscle function and cause weakness.
A single striated muscle fiber can contract quickly and powerfully during exercise.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: smooth muscle fiber - an elongated contractile cell in smooth muscle

Etymology

From Latin 'stria' (furrow, groove) and 'fiber' (thread, filament).
