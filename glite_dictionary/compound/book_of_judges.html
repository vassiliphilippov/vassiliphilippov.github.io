Book of Judges

1. PROPN

/ bʊk əv ˈdʒʌdʒɪz / (AmE), / bʊk əv ˈdʒʌdʒɪz / (BrE)
a book of the Old Testament that recounts the history of the Israelites under the rule of judges before the establishment of monarchy
Old Testament book about Israel under the judges

Examples

The Book of Judges describes the turbulent period after the Israelites entered Canaan.
He gave a sermon based on a story from the Book of Judges.
Scholars study the Book of Judges to understand ancient Israelite society.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Old Testament - the first part of the Christian Bible, based on Hebrew Scriptures
  • Degree Lower: Book of Joshua - the Old Testament book preceding Judges, telling the story of Joshua
  • Degree Higher: Book of Ruth - the Old Testament book following Judges
