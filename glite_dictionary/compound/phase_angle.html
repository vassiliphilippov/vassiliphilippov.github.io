phase angle

1. NOUN

/ ˈfeɪz ˌæŋɡəl / (AmE), / ˈfeɪz ˌaŋɡl̩ / (BrE)
the position of a point in time on a waveform cycle, measured as an angle from a reference point
angle describing position in a cycle

Examples

The voltage and current have a phase angle of 90 degrees in this circuit.
Understanding the phase angle is crucial for analyzing alternating current signals.
As the wheel rotates, the phase angle increases steadily over time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phase - A distinct stage in a cycle or process

Etymology

From 'phase', meaning a stage in a cycle, and 'angle', from Latin 'angulus' (corner).
