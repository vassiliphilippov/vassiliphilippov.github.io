Methodist denomination

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛθ.ə.dɪst ˌdɛn.əˈmeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˈmɛθ.əd.ɪst ˌdɛn.əˈmeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
a group of congregations that share the beliefs, practices, and organizational structure of Methodism
a grouping of Methodist churches

Examples

The Methodist denomination places a strong emphasis on social justice and community service.
She grew up attending a church that belonged to a Methodist denomination.
There are several branches within the Methodist denomination, each with unique traditions.

Etymology

From Methodist, referring to Methodism (a Protestant Christian movement founded by John Wesley), and denomination, meaning a recognized autonomous branch of the Christian Church.
