plane table

1. NOUN

/ pleɪn ˈteɪ.bəl / (AmE), / pleɪn ˈtæb.əl / (BrE)
a surveying instrument consisting of a drawing board and a ruler mounted on a tripod, used for sighting and mapping topographical details
surveying instrument for mapping topography

Examples

The surveyors set up the plane table on the hillside to begin their mapping work.
Using a plane table, she accurately recorded the positions of various features in the field.
The plane table allows engineers to create detailed site plans without complex calculations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: theodolite - an instrument for measuring horizontal and vertical angles
