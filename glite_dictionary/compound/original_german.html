original German

1. NOUN

/ əˈrɪdʒənəl ˈdʒɜrmən / (AmE), / əˈrɪdʒɪnəl ˈdʒɜːmən / (BrE)
the first or authentic version of a text or work in the German language, rather than in translation
work in its first German-language version

Examples

Many scholars recommend reading Kafka in the original German to fully appreciate his language.
The subtitles were helpful, but she wanted to listen to the opera in the original German.
Collectors value first editions, especially those printed in the original German.

Synset Examples

This book is best understood in the original German.
He prefers to watch films in the original German.
I read the poem in the original German.
