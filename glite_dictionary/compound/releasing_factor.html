releasing factor

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈlisɪŋ ˈfæktɚ / (AmE), / rɪˈliːsɪŋ ˈfæktə / (BrE)
any of several hormones produced in the hypothalamus that stimulate the release of specific hormones from the anterior pituitary gland
hormone stimulating pituitary secretion
medical

Examples

The hypothalamus secretes a releasing factor to prompt the pituitary gland to produce growth hormone.
Scientists are studying how each releasing factor controls various endocrine responses.
An abnormality in a releasing factor can disrupt the balance of hormones in the body.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: releasing hormone - hormone produced by the hypothalamus that triggers pituitary hormone release

2. NOUN

/ rɪˈlisɪŋ ˈfæktɚ / (AmE), / rɪˈliːsɪŋ ˈfæktə / (BrE)
a hormone-like substance secreted by the hypothalamus that stimulates the anterior pituitary gland to release a specific hormone
hypothalamic substance that triggers pituitary hormone release
medical

Examples

The hypothalamus secretes a releasing factor to stimulate hormone production in the pituitary gland.
Scientists discovered a new releasing factor involved in growth regulation.
A deficiency in a specific releasing factor can lead to hormonal imbalances.
