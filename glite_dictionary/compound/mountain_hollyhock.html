mountain hollyhock

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaʊntn ˈhɑliˌhɑk / (AmE), / ˈmaʊntɪn ˈhɒlɪˌhɒk / (BrE)
a perennial plant of the northwestern United States and western Canada, resembling a hollyhock with white or pink flowers
a perennial plant resembling hollyhock

Examples

The mountain hollyhock thrives in alpine meadows during the summer months.
We spotted a group of mountain hollyhocks alongside the hiking trail, their pink flowers swaying in the breeze.
Gardeners in cooler climates often plant mountain hollyhock for its resilience and attractive blooms.
