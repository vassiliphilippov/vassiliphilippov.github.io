order Scandentia

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdɚ skænˈdɛnʃiə / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə skænˈdɛnʃɪə / (BrE)
a taxonomic order of small, tree-dwelling mammals known as tree shrews, sometimes classified with primates or insectivores
mammalian order of tree shrews

Examples

Scientists recently discovered a new species within the order Scandentia in Borneo.
The order Scandentia is sometimes grouped with primates due to anatomical similarities.
Members of the order Scandentia have keen eyesight and are active during the day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tree shrew - a small, squirrel-like mammal of Southeast Asia

Etymology

From New Latin, Scandentia meaning 'climbers', from Latin scandere 'to climb'.
