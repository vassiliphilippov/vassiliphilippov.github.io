dead air

1. NOUN

/ dɛd ɛr / (AmE), / dɛd eə / (BrE)
an unintentional period of silence during a broadcast when no audio is transmitted
a period of silence on air

Examples

The radio station experienced several seconds of dead air before the announcer spoke again.
Technicians scrambled to fix the problem when dead air disrupted the live broadcast.
Broadcasters are trained to avoid dead air to keep listeners engaged.

2. NOUN

/ dɛd ɛr / (AmE), / dɛd eə / (BrE)
an unintended period of silence during a radio or television broadcast, often regarded as a technical error or lapse
unintentional silence in broadcasting

Examples

The radio station quickly played a song to fill the dead air after a technical issue.
There was an awkward moment of dead air during the broadcast when the guest's call dropped.
Producers try to avoid dead air because it makes listeners think something went wrong.

Synset Examples

There was dead air during the radio show after the host left.
The presentation suffered from dead air and awkward silences.
You could hear the dead air after the power outage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silence - the absence of sound
