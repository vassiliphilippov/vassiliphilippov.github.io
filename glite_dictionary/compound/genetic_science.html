genetic science

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˈnɛtɪk ˈsaɪəns / (AmE), / dʒɪˈnɛtɪk ˈsaɪəns / (BrE)
The branch of biology concerned with the study of heredity and variation in organisms.
branch of biology studying heredity

Examples

Advancements in genetic science have made it possible to map the human genome.
Many ethical debates have emerged as genetic science pushes the boundaries of what can be achieved in medicine.
She decided to pursue a career in genetic science to help fight hereditary diseases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: genetics - the study of heredity and variation in living organisms

Etymology

From 'genetic' (from Greek *genetikos*, relating to origin) and 'science' (from Latin *scientia*, knowledge).
