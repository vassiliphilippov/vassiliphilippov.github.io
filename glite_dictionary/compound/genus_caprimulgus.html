genus Caprimulgus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs kæˈprɪm.ʌl.ɡəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs kæˈprɪm.ʌl.ɡəs / (BrE)
A genus of birds in the family Caprimulgidae, commonly known as nightjars, serving as the type genus for this family.
genus of nightjar birds

Examples

The genus Caprimulgus includes several species of nightjars found across Europe and Asia.
Researchers have discovered a new species within the genus Caprimulgus in Southeast Asia.
The genus Caprimulgus is characterized by cryptic plumage and nocturnal habits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nightjar - a nocturnal bird of the Caprimulgidae family

Etymology

From Latin 'caprimulgus' meaning 'goat-milker', due to a mistaken belief about their habits.
