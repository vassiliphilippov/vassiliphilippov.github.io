genus Mantis

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈmæn.tɪs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈmæn.tɪs / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Mantidae, including several species of mantises.
type genus of Mantidae (mantises)

Examples

Scientists discovered a new species within the genus Mantis in Southeast Asia.
Many entomologists study the genus Mantis to understand predatory insect behavior.
The praying mantis commonly seen in gardens belongs to the genus Mantis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mantis - an insect of the order Mantodea, especially those in the genus Mantis

Etymology

From Latin 'mantis', meaning seer or prophet, referring to the mantis's praying posture.
