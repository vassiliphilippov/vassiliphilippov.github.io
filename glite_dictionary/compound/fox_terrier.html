fox terrier

1. NOUN

/ fɑks tɛˈrɪɚ / (AmE), / fɒks ˈtɛr.i.ə / (BrE)
a small, energetic breed of terrier, usually black-and-white, originally bred to dig out foxes during hunts
small, energetic terrier breed

Examples

The fox terrier quickly dug through the loose soil, searching for the scent of a fox.
Growing up, her family always had a lively fox terrier in the house.
At the dog show, a well-groomed fox terrier won first prize for its breed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: terrier - a breed of dog originally bred to hunt small animals

Etymology

From 'fox' (the animal) and 'terrier' (from Middle French terrier, meaning 'earth-dog', from Latin terra, 'earth').
