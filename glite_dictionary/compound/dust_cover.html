dust cover

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʌst ˌkʌvɚ / (AmE), / ˈdʌst ˌkʌvə / (BrE)
A large protective cloth placed over furniture to prevent dust from accumulating when the furniture is not in use.
protective cloth for furniture

Examples

The old sofa was hidden under a dust cover in the attic.
Before going on vacation, she draped a dust cover over the chairs to keep them clean.
He pulled off the dust cover to reveal an antique desk beneath.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dust sheet - a cloth used to protect furniture or floors from dust, especially during periods of non-use or decoration (primarily British)

2. NOUN

/ dʌst ˈkʌvɚ / (AmE), / dʌst ˈkʌvə / (BrE)
A removable paper jacket that protects a book and usually contains information such as the title, author, and promotional material.
protective paper jacket for books

Examples

The first edition of the novel still has its original dust cover, making it a valuable collector's item.
She carefully removed the dust cover before reading the book so she wouldn't damage it.
The dust cover featured stunning artwork that caught my attention in the bookstore.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dust jacket - a removable paper cover for a book, often printed with promotional material

Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ dʌst ˈkʌvɚ / (AmE), / dʌst ˈkʌvə / (BrE)
a removable protective cover, usually made of plastic or cloth, used to shield equipment from dust and damage when not in use
removable cover for equipment protection

Examples

Please remember to put the dust cover back on the printer after using it.
The record player came with a clear plastic dust cover to protect the turntable.
He lifted the dust cover and started cleaning the keyboard.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cover - a thing that lies on, over, or around something, especially in order to protect or conceal it
