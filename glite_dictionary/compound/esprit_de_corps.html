esprit de corps

1. NOUN

/ ɛˌsprit də ˈkɔr / (AmE), / ɛˌspriː də ˈkɔː / (BrE)
A feeling of pride, loyalty, and unity shared by members of a group that makes them want to achieve something together.
group unity and shared pride

Examples

The company's annual retreat is intended to strengthen esprit de corps among employees.
Military units rely heavily on esprit de corps to operate effectively in challenging situations.
A strong esprit de corps kept the volunteers motivated throughout the difficult project.

Synset Examples

The coach emphasized esprit de corps to improve performance.
The team’s esprit de corps was evident during the match.
They built esprit de corps through team-building activities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: morale - the confidence, enthusiasm, and discipline of a person or group at a particular time

Etymology

From French, literally 'spirit of the body'.
