playing field

1. NOUN

/ ˈpleɪɪŋ fiːld / (AmE), / ˈpleɪɪŋ fiːld / (BrE)
an area of land specially prepared and marked for playing sports or games
area of land for playing sports

Examples

The school invested in a new playing field for its students.
The rain made the playing field slippery and difficult to use.
Several teams share the same playing field throughout the season.

Synset Examples

the home crowd cheered when Princeton took the field

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pitch - British term for a sports field or area for games
  • Synonym: sports field - field or ground used for athletic events and games
  • Synonym: court - flat area marked for playing ball games like tennis, basketball

2. NOUN

/ ˈpleɪɪŋ fiːld / (AmE), / ˈpleɪɪŋ fiːld / (BrE)
the conditions or circumstances in which competitors operate, especially regarding fairness or equality
conditions for competition, esp. fairness

Examples

The new trade regulations are designed to create a playing field where all companies can compete fairly.
Smaller firms often struggle when the playing field is tilted in favor of large corporations.
Reforms are necessary to ensure a level playing field in access to government contracts.

Synset Examples

the government's objective is to insure a genuinely level playing field for American industry and commerce in Europe

Related Terms

  • Synonym: level playing field - a situation in which everyone has the same opportunity

Etymology

From 'playing field' (sports ground), used figuratively since the late 20th century.
