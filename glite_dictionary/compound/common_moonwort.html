common moonwort

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmən ˈmunˌwɜrt / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən ˈmuːnwɜːt / (BrE)
A small fern (Botrychium lunaria) native to America, Eurasia, and Australia, with distinctive crescent-shaped leaflets.
a small fern with crescent-shaped leaflets

Examples

The common moonwort can be found growing in meadows and grassy hillsides across Europe.
Botanists are fascinated by the unique leaf structure of the common moonwort.
During their hike, they discovered several common moonwort ferns near the edge of the forest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moonwort - Any fern of the genus Botrychium, known for moon-shaped leaflets

Etymology

From 'moonwort', referring to the crescent (moon-shaped) leaflets, and 'common' meaning widespread.
