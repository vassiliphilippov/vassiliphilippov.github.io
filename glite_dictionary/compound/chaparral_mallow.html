chaparral mallow

1. NOUN

/ ˌtʃæpəˈræl ˈmæloʊ / (AmE), / ˌtʃapəˈral ˈmaləʊ / (BrE)
a shrub native to the coastal ranges of California and Baja California, with hairy branches and spikes of many mauve flowers
a shrub with mauve flowers native to California and Baja California

Examples

The chaparral mallow blooms in spring, adding vibrant color to the hillside.
Gardeners prize the chaparral mallow for its drought tolerance and attractive mauve flowers.
Pollinators such as bees are drawn to the chaparral mallow when it is in full bloom.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mallow - a plant with hairy stems and showy flowers

Etymology

From 'chaparral', Spanish for 'thicket', and 'mallow', a type of flowering plant.
