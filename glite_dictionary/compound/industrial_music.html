industrial music

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈdʌstriəl ˈmjuːzɪk / (AmE), / ɪnˈdʌstriəl ˈmjuːzɪk / (BrE)
A style of experimental music that emerged in the 1970s, characterized by harsh, mechanical sounds and transgressive or provocative themes.
experimental music genre with mechanical sounds

Examples

Many early bands in the industrial music scene used homemade instruments and unconventional sound sources.
The festival showcased the evolution of industrial music from its 1970s roots to more modern electronic styles.
She became interested in industrial music after hearing its unique blend of noise and rhythm in a documentary.

Synset Examples

Industrial music was created originally by using mechanical and electric machinery, and later advanced synthesizers, samplers and electronic percussion as the technology developed.
