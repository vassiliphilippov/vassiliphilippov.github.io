bay window

1. NOUN

/ beɪ ˈwɪndoʊ / (AmE), / beɪ ˈwɪndəʊ / (BrE)
a window that projects outward from the exterior wall of a building, forming a recess inside
window projecting outward from a wall

Examples

The living room's bay window lets in plenty of natural light.
We put a cozy bench in the bay window to create a reading nook.
From the bay window, you can see the entire garden.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bow window - a curved window projecting from a building

Etymology

From 'bay' meaning a recess or compartment and 'window.'

2. NOUN

/ beɪ ˈwɪndoʊ / (AmE), / beɪ ˈwɪndəʊ / (BrE)
(slang) A large, protruding belly; a paunch.
slang for a large belly or paunch
slang

Examples

After years of enjoying good food, he developed quite a bay window.
My uncle joked about his bay window after the holidays.
The old man's bay window made it hard for him to button his coat.
