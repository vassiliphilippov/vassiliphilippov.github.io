Muntz metal

1. NOUN

/ mʌnts ˈmɛt̬l̩ / (AmE), / mʌnts ˈmɛtl̩ / (BrE)
A type of brass alloy containing more zinc than alpha brass, making it stronger; commonly used for castings and hot-worked products.
strong brass alloy with high zinc content

Examples

The shipbuilder specified Muntz metal for the hull fittings due to its corrosion resistance.
Manufacturers use Muntz metal to produce durable bolts and marine hardware.
Because of its strength and workability, Muntz metal is ideal for hot-rolling and casting applications.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brass - a metal alloy of copper and zinc

Etymology

Named after George Fredrick Muntz, the 19th-century English metallurgist who developed the alloy.
