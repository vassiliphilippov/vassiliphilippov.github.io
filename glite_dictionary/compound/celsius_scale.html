Celsius scale

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛl.si.əs skeɪl / (AmE), / ˈsɛl.si.əs skeɪl / (BrE)
A temperature scale in which 0 degrees marks the freezing point and 100 degrees the boiling point of water under standard conditions.
temperature scale with 0°C as freezing, 100°C as boiling point

Examples

The water temperature was measured using the Celsius scale for scientific accuracy.
In most countries, weather forecasts use the Celsius scale rather than Fahrenheit.
At sea level, water boils at 100 degrees on the Celsius scale.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Fahrenheit scale - temperature scale with freezing point at 32°F, boiling at 212°F

Etymology

Named after Swedish astronomer Anders Celsius who devised the scale in 1742.
