light second

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪt ˌsɛkənd / (AmE), / ˈlaɪt ˌsɛkənd / (BrE)
the distance that light travels in a vacuum in one second, approximately 300,000 kilometers (186,000 miles)
distance light travels in one second

Examples

The nearest spacecraft is just a few light seconds away from Earth.
Astronomers use the light second to measure distances between objects in space.
A radio message would take several light seconds to reach the moon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: light-year - distance light travels in one year
  • Synonym: astronomical unit - mean distance from Earth to Sun

Etymology

Formed from 'light' (referring to electromagnetic radiation) and 'second' (unit of time); patterned after scientific units like 'light-year'.
