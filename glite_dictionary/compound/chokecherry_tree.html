chokecherry tree

1. NOUN

/ ˈt͡ʃoʊkˌt͡ʃɛri tri / (AmE), / ˈt͡ʃəʊkˌt͡ʃɛri triː / (BrE)
a North American wild cherry tree (Prunus virginiana) bearing clusters of small, astringent dark berries favored by birds
North American wild cherry tree

Examples

Every spring, the chokecherry tree in our backyard bursts into fragrant white blossoms.
Birds flock to the chokecherry tree to eat its ripe, dark berries.
Pioneers once used fruit from the chokecherry tree to make jams and preserves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chokecherry - the fruit of the chokecherry tree

Etymology

From 'choke', referencing the astringent taste, + 'cherry'.
