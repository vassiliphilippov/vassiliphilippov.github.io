Audubon's caracara

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔdəbənz kəˈrɑːkərə / (AmE), / ˈɔːdəbənz kəˈrɑːkərə / (BrE)
a bird of prey found from the southern United States to Central America, characterized by rusty black plumage with a black-and-white breast and tail
a bird of prey of the southern US and Central America

Examples

We spotted an Audubon's caracara perched elegantly on a fence post near the marsh.
The distinct markings on the Audubon's caracara make it easy to recognize among other birds of prey.
Conservationists are studying the habitats of the Audubon's caracara to ensure their populations remain stable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caracara - a type of bird of prey in the falcon family

Etymology

Named after John James Audubon (1785–1851), American ornithologist and painter.
