caspase-mediated cell death

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæˌspeɪs ˈmiːdiˌeɪtɪd sɛl dɛθ / (AmE), / ˈkæˌspeɪz ˈmiːdiˌeɪtɪd sɛl dɛθ / (BrE)
a form of programmed cell death in which caspase enzymes play a central role in orchestrating the process of cellular self-destruction, typically as part of an organism's mechanism to remove unnecessary or dangerous cells
cell death driven by caspase enzymes
medical

Examples

Cancers sometimes evade caspase-mediated cell death, leading to uncontrolled proliferation.
Many neurodegenerative diseases involve excessive caspase-mediated cell death of neurons.
Researchers developed drugs to block caspase-mediated cell death after a stroke to protect brain tissue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apoptosis - programmed cell death, often mediated by caspases

Etymology

From 'caspase,' derived from 'cysteine-aspartic protease,' referring to the family of enzymes involved; 'mediated' and 'cell death' are self-explanatory.
