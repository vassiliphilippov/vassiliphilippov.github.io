window dressing

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪndoʊ ˌdrɛsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwɪndəʊ ˌdrɛsɪŋ / (BrE)
The arrangement and decoration of items in the windows of a shop to attract customers.
decoration of shop windows

Examples

The store hired a professional team for its window dressing during the holiday season.
She has a keen eye for window dressing, creating displays that stop people in their tracks.
Those beautiful window dressing arrangements boosted sales significantly last month.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɪndoʊ ˌdrɛsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwɪndəʊ ˌdrɛsɪŋ / (BrE)
An action or arrangement intended to create a favourable impression or to disguise something unpleasant or unsatisfactory.
action to make something look better than it is

Examples

The company's impressive financial statements were just window dressing to conceal its mounting debts.
He accused the government of using reforms as window dressing rather than addressing the real issues.
Much of the new policy is little more than window dressing to improve the administration's image.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: facade - a deceptive outward appearance
  • Synonym: camouflage - something intended to hide or disguise
  • Antonym: transparency - the quality of being open and honest; nothing hidden

Etymology

From the literal practice of arranging displays in shop windows to attract customers, transferred to metaphorical use in the early 20th century.

3. NOUN

/ ˈwɪndoʊ ˌdrɛsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwɪndəʊ ˌdrɛsɪŋ / (BrE)
an action or arrangement intended to create a favorable impression but lacking real substance or value
superficial arrangement to impress

Examples

The management's financial report was mostly window dressing to please investors.
His apology was nothing but window dressing and didn't address the real issue.
Many reforms announced before the election turned out to be just window dressing.

Synset Examples

Her report was just window dressing with no real substance.
The company used window dressing to attract more customers.
The politician's promises felt like mere window dressing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: façade - a deceptive outward appearance
  • Synonym: pretense - an attempt to make something appear true that is not
  • Antonym: substance - the real content or value of something

Etymology

Originally referred to the practice of arranging goods in shop windows to attract customers; later used figuratively.
