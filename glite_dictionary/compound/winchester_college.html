Winchester College

1. PROPN

/ ˈwɪntʃəstər ˈkɑːlɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈwɪntʃɪstə ˈkɒlɪdʒ / (BrE)
A famous independent public school for boys, founded in 1382 and located in Winchester, England.
historic British public school
british

Examples

He attended Winchester College before going on to study at Oxford.
Many traditions at Winchester College date back hundreds of years.
Admission to Winchester College is highly competitive and prestigious.

Etymology

Named after the city of Winchester and from Latin 'collegium' (society, association).
