long haul

1. NOUN

/ lɔŋ hɔl / (AmE), / lɒŋ hɔːl / (BrE)
a journey that covers a great distance, especially by road, rail, or air
journey over a long distance

Examples

The flight from London to Sydney is definitely a long haul for any traveler.
Truck drivers often have to prepare for a long haul across several states.
After such a long haul, the team was glad to finally reach their destination.

Synset Examples

it's a long haul from New York to Los Angeles

Related Terms

  • Antonym: short haul - a journey over a short distance

2. NOUN

/ lɔŋ hɔl / (AmE), / lɒŋ hɔːl / (BrE)
a long period of time over which developments or efforts gradually have results; the long term.
extended duration; long period of time

Examples

Finishing a degree is definitely a long haul, but it's worth the effort in the end.
The company's recovery will take a long haul after the recent financial losses.
Their friendship has lasted through the long haul of life's changes.

Synset Examples

in the long run we will win
in the long run we will all be dead
he performed well over the long haul

Related Terms

  • Antonym: short haul - a short period of time compared to a long haul
  • Synonym: long run - the long term; a period of time sufficient for results to become apparent

3. NOUN

/ lɔŋ hɔl / (AmE), / lɒŋ hɔːl / (BrE)
a long and difficult process or period of work requiring considerable effort and persistence
long, difficult effort or period

Examples

Training for law school is definitely going to be a long haul for anyone.
We're in this business for the long haul, not just a quick profit.
Recovering from the storm will be a long haul for the entire region.

Synset Examples

Training for a marathon is a long haul.
She knew the long haul would require persistence.
They are committed to the long haul of this project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marathon - a long and difficult task or activity
