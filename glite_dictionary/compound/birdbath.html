birdbath

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɝːdˌbæθ / (AmE), / ˈbɜːdˌbɑːθ / (BrE)
a shallow basin, typically placed outdoors in a garden, for birds to bathe and drink in
shallow outdoor basin for birds

Examples

The robins gathered around the birdbath each morning to splash in the cool water.
She placed a ceramic birdbath in the middle of her flower garden.
After the rain, the birdbath overflowed and sparkled in the sunlight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fountain - a decorative structure that sends water into the air, often found in gardens

Etymology

Formed from 'bird' and 'bath'; widely used since the late 19th century.
