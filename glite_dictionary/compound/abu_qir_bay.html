Abu Qir Bay

1. PROPN

/ ˌɑːbuː ˈkɪr ˌbeɪ / (AmE), / ˌɑːbuː ˈkɪə ˌbeɪ / (BrE)
a bay on the Mediterranean Sea in northern Egypt, near Alexandria
bay in northern Egypt, near Alexandria

Examples

The British fleet defeated the French at Abu Qir Bay in 1798.
Many fishermen rely on the waters of Abu Qir Bay for their livelihood.
Tourists often visit Abu Qir Bay to explore its historical significance and natural beauty.

Etymology

From Arabic 'Abū Qīr', named after the nearby town of Abu Qir.
