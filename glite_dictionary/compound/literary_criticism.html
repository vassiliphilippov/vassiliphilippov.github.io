literary criticism

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪt̬əˌrɛri ˈkrɪt̬ɪˌsɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈlɪt.ər.əri ˈkrɪt.ɪ.sɪ.zəm / (BrE)
the analysis, interpretation, or evaluation of works of literature, especially in written form
analysis or evaluation of literature

Examples

Her essay offers a compelling piece of literary criticism on Shakespeare's tragedies.
Contemporary literary criticism often examines themes of identity and power.
He published several volumes of literary criticism during his career as a professor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: critique - a detailed analysis and assessment, especially of a literary or artistic work
  • Synonym: review - a critical appraisal of a book, play, film, etc.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɪt̬əˌrɛri ˈkrɪt̬əˌsɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈlɪtərəri ˈkrɪtɪsɪzəm / (BrE)
the study, analysis, and evaluation of literary works, often using specific theories or methods
analysis and evaluation of literary works

Examples

She has a deep interest in literary criticism and reads essays about classic novels.
The professor's course focused on different schools of literary criticism, including feminist and psychoanalytic approaches.
His collection of literary criticism offers new insights into 20th-century poetry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: critique - a detailed analysis and assessment of something, especially a literary, philosophical, or political theory
  • Synonym: literary theory - the body of ideas and methods used in the practical reading of literature
