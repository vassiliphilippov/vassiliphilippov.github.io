nonrapid eye movement sleep

1. NOUN

/ ˌnɑnˈræpɪd ˈaɪ ˌmuːvmənt sliːp / (AmE), / ˌnɒnˈræpɪd ˈaɪ ˌmuːvmənt sliːp / (BrE)
A recurring stage of sleep characterized by the absence of rapid eye movements and dreaming, accounting for about 75% of normal sleep time.
stage of sleep without rapid eye movements
medical

Examples

During nonrapid eye movement sleep, the body repairs tissues and strengthens the immune system.
Infants spend more time in nonrapid eye movement sleep as their brains develop.
Disruptions in nonrapid eye movement sleep may contribute to various health issues.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: REM sleep - a stage of sleep with rapid eye movements, associated with dreaming

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'non-' with 'rapid eye movement sleep', a term from sleep research in the mid-20th century.
