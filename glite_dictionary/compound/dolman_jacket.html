dolman jacket

1. NOUN

/ ˈdoʊlmən ˈdʒækɪt / (AmE), / ˈdɒlmən ˈdʒækɪt / (BrE)
a type of military jacket, originally worn by hussars, designed to be worn over the shoulders
a hussar's military jacket

Examples

The museum displayed an ornate dolman jacket from the Napoleonic era.
He wore a dolman jacket with elaborate braiding for the reenactment.
Many 19th-century cavalry uniforms included the stylish dolman jacket.

Etymology

From Turkish 'dolaman,' meaning a long robe; adopted into European military fashion.
