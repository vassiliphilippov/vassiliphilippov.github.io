vertebrate paleontology

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɜrtɪbrət ˌpeɪliənˈtɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ˈvɜːtɪbrət ˌpaliːɒnˈtɒlədʒi / (BrE)
The branch of paleontology concerned with the study of fossils of animals with backbones.
study of fossil vertebrates
medical

Examples

He specializes in vertebrate paleontology, focusing on ancient mammals and reptiles.
The museum's collection is widely used for vertebrate paleontology research.
Students interested in vertebrate paleontology often participate in fossil digs during the summer.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: paleontology - the scientific study of fossils
  • Degree Lower: invertebrate paleontology - the study of fossils of animals without backbones

Etymology

From Latin 'vertebratus' (jointed, having vertebrae) and Greek 'palaios' (ancient) + 'ontos' (being) + 'logia' (study).
