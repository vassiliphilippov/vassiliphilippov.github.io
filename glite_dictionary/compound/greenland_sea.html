Greenland Sea

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡriːnˌlænd siː / (AmE), / ˈɡriːnlənd siː / (BrE)
the part of the Arctic Ocean situated north of Iceland, between Greenland and the Svalbard archipelago
sea area north of Iceland in the Arctic Ocean

Examples

The Greenland Sea is known for its icy waters and drifting sea ice year-round.
Many species of whales migrate through the Greenland Sea during the summer months.
Researchers are studying climate change effects in the Greenland Sea ecosystem.
