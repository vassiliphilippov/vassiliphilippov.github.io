rock elm

1. NOUN

/ rɑk ɛlm / (AmE), / rɒk elm / (BrE)
A large elm tree, native to eastern North America, with graceful, gradually spreading branches and valued as an ornamental species.
large North American elm tree

Examples

The rock elm can live for over 200 years in the wild.
Many parks in the Midwest feature the distinctive silhouette of the rock elm.
Carpenters value the wood of the rock elm for its strength.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ulmus thomasii - scientific name for rock elm
  • Degree Lower: elm - general term for trees of the genus Ulmus

2. NOUN

/ rɑk ɛlm / (AmE), / rɒk elm / (BrE)
a tall, widely distributed species of elm tree native to eastern North America, known for its hard, heavy wood
a North American elm tree species

Examples

The rock elm is valued for its extremely tough wood, often used in flooring and furniture.
Many rock elm trees can be found growing in the forests of the northeastern United States.
Damage from Dutch elm disease has significantly reduced the number of rock elm trees in some areas.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: elm - a type of deciduous tree belonging to the genus Ulmus
