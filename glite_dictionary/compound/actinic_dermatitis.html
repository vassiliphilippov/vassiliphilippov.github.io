actinic dermatitis

1. NOUN

/ ækˈtɪnɪk ˌdɜːrməˈtaɪtɪs / (AmE), / ækˈtɪnɪk ˌdɜːmɪˈtaɪtɪs / (BrE)
A skin inflammation caused by exposure to sunlight or ultraviolet radiation.
skin inflammation from sunlight exposure
medical

Examples

After a few hours in the sun, she developed a rash diagnosed as actinic dermatitis.
Farmers and outdoor workers are prone to actinic dermatitis if they do not protect their skin adequately.
Sunscreens and protective clothing can help prevent actinic dermatitis in sensitive individuals.

Etymology

From Greek aktinos (ray) and New Latin dermatitis (inflammation of the skin).
