blood kinship

1. NOUN

/ blʌd ˈkɪn.ʃɪp / (AmE), / blʌd ˈkɪn.ʃɪp / (BrE)
the relationship between people who are related by descent from a common ancestor; relation by blood rather than marriage or adoption
relationship by blood

Examples

He felt a strong sense of blood kinship with his cousins despite rarely seeing them.
The law recognizes blood kinship when determining inheritance rights.
Anthropologists study blood kinship to understand how family groups are structured.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kinship - the state of being related to others
  • Antonym: affinity - relationship by marriage, not by blood
