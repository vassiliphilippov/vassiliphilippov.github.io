antiprofessional attitude

1. NOUN

/ ˌæn.taɪ.prəˈfɛʃ.ə.nəl ˈæt.əˌtud / (AmE), / ˌæn.ti.prəˈfeʃ.ən.əl ˈæt.ɪ.tjuːd / (BrE)
an attitude or behavior that is opposed to or undermines professionalism, often characterized by disregard for standards, ethics, or conduct expected in a professional setting
opposition to professionalism in attitude or behavior

Examples

An antiprofessional attitude can cause conflicts within a team and reduce overall productivity.
The manager addressed issues of antiprofessional attitude during the staff meeting.
A persistent antiprofessional attitude may lead to disciplinary action in many organizations.

Synset Examples

An antiprofessional attitude can harm workplace culture.
She was criticized for her antiprofessional conduct.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: professionalism - the competence or skill expected of a professional

Etymology

Formed from 'anti-' (against) and 'professional,' plus 'attitude.'
