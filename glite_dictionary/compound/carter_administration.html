Carter administration

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑrtər ˌædmɪnɪˈstreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈkɑːtə ˌædmɪnɪˈstreɪʃᵊn / (BrE)
The executive branch of the United States government during the presidency of Jimmy Carter (1977–1981).
U.S. government under President Carter

Examples

During the Carter administration, the United States faced a major energy crisis.
The diplomatic approach to foreign policy shifted significantly in the Carter administration.
Many remember the Carter administration for its emphasis on human rights.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Reagan administration - the executive under President Reagan
  • Antonym: Nixon administration - the executive under President Nixon
  • Antonym: Ford administration - the executive under President Ford
