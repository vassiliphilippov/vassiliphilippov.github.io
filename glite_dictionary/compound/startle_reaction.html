startle reaction

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɑːrtl riˈækʃən / (AmE), / ˈstɑːtl rɪˈækʃən / (BrE)
a reflexive, automatic response of the body and mind to a sudden unexpected stimulus, often involving muscle flexing and other physical changes
reflexive response to a sudden stimulus
medical

Examples

The doctor observed the infant's startle reaction when a loud noise was made in the room.
A sudden flash of lightning triggered my startle reaction despite knowing the storm was coming.
Researchers measured the startle reaction to assess the patients' nervous system responses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: startle reflex - the automatic movement in response to a sudden stimulus
