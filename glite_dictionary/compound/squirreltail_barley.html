squirreltail barley

1. NOUN

/ ˈskwɝːlˌteɪl ˈbɑːrli / (AmE), / ˈskwɪəlˌteɪl ˈbɑːli / (BrE)
A type of barley with highly ornamental flower heads featuring long, silky awns, native to North America and northeastern Asia.
ornamental barley with silky awns

Examples

The squirreltail barley swayed gently in the summer breeze beside the trail.
Gardeners often plant squirreltail barley for its attractive feathery flower heads.
Birds are drawn to fields of squirreltail barley during the late summer months.

Etymology

From 'squirrel tail' (referring to the bushy shape of the awns) and 'barley'.
