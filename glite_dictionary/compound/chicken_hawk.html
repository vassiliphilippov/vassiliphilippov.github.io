chicken hawk

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɪkən ˌhɔk / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪkɪn ˌhɔːk / (BrE)
A nontechnical term for any of several hawks that are alleged to prey on poultry.
hawk believed to prey on chickens

Examples

Farmers often worry that a chicken hawk will attack their poultry.
The presence of a chicken hawk caused the hens to scatter in fright.
Many people mistakenly call all large raptors chicken hawks even if they don't eat chickens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hawk - a bird of prey with broad rounded wings
