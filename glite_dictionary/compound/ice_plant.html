ice plant

1. NOUN

/ aɪs plænt / (AmE), / aɪs plɑːnt / (BrE)
A plant with fleshy leaves and stems covered in fine, glistening hairs, giving the appearance of being encrusted with ice; especially referring to several species in the genus Mesembryanthemum, native to the Old World and often grown as ornamentals or naturalized in warm regions.
a succulent plant with glistening foliage

Examples

The ice plant grows in dry, sunny areas and is prized for its sparkling foliage.
Gardeners often use ice plant as ground cover due to its drought resistance.
After rainfall, the leaves of the ice plant shimmer as if they're covered in frost.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mesembryanthemum - genus of plants known as ice plants
