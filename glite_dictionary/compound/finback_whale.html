finback whale

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪnbæk weɪl / (AmE), / ˈfɪnbæk weɪl / (BrE)
A large baleen whale with a flat head and distinctive ridges along its throat, found in the Atlantic and Pacific Oceans.
large baleen whale, flat-headed, ridged throat

Examples

The finback whale is known for its speed and graceful swimming style.
Scientists observed a pod of finback whales feeding in the North Atlantic Ocean.
Conservation groups are working to protect the endangered finback whale from hunting and habitat loss.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blue whale - the largest animal known, another species of baleen whale
