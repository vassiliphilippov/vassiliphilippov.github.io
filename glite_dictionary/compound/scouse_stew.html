Scouse stew

1. NOUN

/ skaʊs stu / (AmE), / skaʊs stjuː / (BrE)
a traditional meat and vegetable stew originating from Liverpool, England
traditional Liverpool meat and vegetable stew
british

Examples

My mother makes a delicious Scouse stew every winter to warm us up.
The pub near the station is famous for its traditional Scouse stew.
After a long day, all he wanted was a hot plate of Scouse stew.

Synset Examples

He enjoyed the hearty Scouse stew his grandmother made.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lobscouse - a sailor's stew, root word of 'Scouse'

Etymology

"Scouse" is a shortening of "lobscouse," a stew eaten by sailors, with roots in Northern European languages.
