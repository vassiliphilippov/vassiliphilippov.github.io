back pocket

1. NOUN

/ bæk ˈpɑːkɪt / (AmE), / bæk ˈpɒkɪt / (BrE)
a strategy, resource, or plan kept in reserve for future use or in case of emergency
a reserve strategy or resource

Examples

She always had a backup plan in her back pocket just in case things went wrong.
The negotiator kept several options in his back pocket during the discussion.
It's wise to have a few arguments in your back pocket before entering the debate.

Synset Examples

He always keeps a plan in his back pocket.
You should keep some cash in your back pocket.
She kept her best ideas in her back-pocket for the final presentation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ace up one's sleeve - a secret advantage or resource kept in reserve
