radio brightness

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪdiˌoʊ ˈbraɪtnəs / (AmE), / ˈreɪdiəʊ ˈbraɪtnəs / (BrE)
the strength or intensity of a radio wave as detected by a radio telescope, used to measure radio emissions from astronomical sources
intensity of a radio wave detected by a telescope

Examples

The radio brightness of the distant galaxy was measured accurately by the observatory.
An increase in radio brightness indicated a burst of energy from the pulsar.
Astronomers compare the radio brightness of different regions to map activity in deep space.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: radio intensity - measurement of the strength of radio waves
