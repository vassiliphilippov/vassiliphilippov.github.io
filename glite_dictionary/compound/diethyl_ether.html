diethyl ether

1. NOUN

/ daɪˌɪθəl ˈiːθər / (AmE), / daɪˌiːθɛl ˈiːθə / (BrE)
a colorless, volatile, highly flammable liquid formerly used as an inhalation anesthetic and as a solvent
flammable liquid, former anesthetic
medical

Examples

The laboratory stored diethyl ether in a special flammable materials cabinet.
Before the advent of modern anesthetics, surgeons often used diethyl ether for surgical procedures.
Because of its high volatility, diethyl ether can form explosive mixtures with air.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ether - a class of organic compounds with an oxygen atom linked to two alkyl or aryl groups

Etymology

From 'di-' meaning two, 'ethyl' (from ethyl group), and 'ether', related to its chemical structure.
