biologic attack

1. NOUN

/ baɪ.əˈlɑː.dʒɪk əˈtæk / (AmE), / baɪ.əˈlɒ.dʒɪk əˈtæk / (BrE)
An attack that uses bacteria, viruses, or toxins to harm or kill people, animals, or contaminate food supplies.
attack using biological agents

Examples

The government has increased funding to protect against a potential biologic attack.
A biologic attack could cause widespread panic and severe health crises.
Experts warn that cities are not fully prepared to respond to a biologic attack.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: biological warfare - the use of biological toxins or infectious agents as weapons
