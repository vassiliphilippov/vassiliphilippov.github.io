washer-upper

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɑʃər ˈʌpər / (AmE), / ˈwɒʃə ˈʌpə / (BrE)
a person whose job or chore is to wash dishes, especially in a kitchen
person who washes dishes
informal british

Examples

After the big family dinner, I ended up as the washer-upper while everyone else relaxed.
At the restaurant, he worked evenings as a washer-upper in the busy kitchen.
No one volunteered to be the washer-upper after our party, so the dishes piled up.

Synset Examples

The washer-upper cleaned the dishes after dinner.
As a washer-upper, she kept the kitchen tidy.
Being the washer-upper is part of my chores.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dishwasher - person or machine that washes dishes

Etymology

Formed from the verb 'wash up' (British English: to wash dishes) plus the agent noun suffix '-er.'
