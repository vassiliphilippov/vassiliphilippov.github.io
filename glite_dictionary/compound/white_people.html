White people

1. NOUN

/ ˈwaɪt ˌpiːpəl / (AmE), / ˈwaɪt ˌpiːpəl / (BrE)
people who are identified as having light skin, especially of European origin, considered as a distinct racial group in some social and historical contexts
people of light European descent

Examples

Some White people have lived in South Africa for generations and consider it their home.
The magazine featured stories about the experiences of White people in multicultural societies.
She grew up in a predominantly White people neighborhood in the suburbs.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Black people - people identified as having dark skin, especially of African descent
