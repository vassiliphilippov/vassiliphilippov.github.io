Toowomba canary grass

1. NOUN

/ təˈwʊmbə ˈkænəˌri ɡræs / (AmE), / təˈwʊmbə ˈkanəri ɡrɑːs / (BrE)
A perennial grass native to Australia and South Africa, introduced to North America as a forage crop.
a perennial forage grass species

Examples

Farmers began planting Toowomba canary grass to improve pasture quality for their livestock.
The rapid growth of Toowomba canary grass makes it a valuable crop in both Australia and North America.
Researchers found that Toowomba canary grass thrives in a variety of soil types across different climates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: canary grass - a type of grass grown for forage or as a source of seed
