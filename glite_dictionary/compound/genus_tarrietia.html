genus Tarrietia

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs tɑːriˈiːtiə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs tɑːriˈiːtiə / (BrE)
a small genus of timber trees native to East Asia and Australia
genus of East Asian and Australian timber trees

Examples

Several species belonging to the genus Tarrietia are prized for their durable timber.
Botanists discovered a new member of the genus Tarrietia in northern Australia.
The genus Tarrietia includes trees that thrive in both tropical and subtropical climates.

Etymology

From modern Latin; Tarrietia is the Latinized name for the genus, used in botanical classification.
