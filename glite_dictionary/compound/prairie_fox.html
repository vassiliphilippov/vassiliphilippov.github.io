prairie fox

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɛri ˌfɑks / (AmE), / ˈprɛːri ˌfɒks / (BrE)
a small, grey fox native to the plains of western North America, also known as the swift fox
small plains fox of western North America

Examples

The prairie fox is well adapted to the open grasslands of the Great Plains.
Researchers observed a family of prairie foxes near their den at dusk.
Conservation efforts have helped increase the prairie fox population in recent years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swift fox - small fox native to North American prairies, also called prairie fox
