genus Fagopyrum

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs fəˈɡoʊ.pɪˌrʌm / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs fəˈɡəʊ.pɪˌrʌm / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the family Polygonaceae, comprising buckwheat and related species.
genus of plants including buckwheat

Examples

Buckwheat is the best-known member of the genus Fagopyrum.
The genus Fagopyrum comprises several species valued for their seeds.
Botanists have debated whether to classify buckwheat under the genus Fagopyrum or Polygonum.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: buckwheat - a plant cultivated for its grain-like seeds, belonging to genus Fagopyrum

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Latin 'genus' (race, kind) and 'Fagopyrum' (buckwheat genus name, from Latin 'fagus' (beech) and Greek 'pyros' (wheat)).
