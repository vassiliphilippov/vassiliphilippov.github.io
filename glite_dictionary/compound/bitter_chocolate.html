bitter chocolate

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɪt̬ɚ ˈtʃɑk.lət / (AmE), / ˈbɪtə ˈtʃɒk.lət / (BrE)
a type of pure, unsweetened or very dark chocolate, often used in baking, icings, sauces, and candy making
pure unsweetened or very dark chocolate for baking

Examples

She prefers to use bitter chocolate when baking rich, decadent cakes.
The dessert was topped with shavings of bitter chocolate for a sophisticated flavor.
Many professional chefs choose bitter chocolate for making authentic French mousse.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: dark chocolate - chocolate with a high cocoa content and little or no milk solids
