sleeper cell

1. NOUN

/ ˈsliːpər sɛl / (AmE), / ˈsliːpə sɛl / (BrE)
a secret group of agents who remain inactive within a community until instructed to carry out undercover operations, often for espionage or terrorism
secret group of inactive agents

Examples

Authorities uncovered a sleeper cell that had been operating undetected for years.
Intelligence agencies are constantly on the lookout for potential sleeper cells within the country.
The plot was orchestrated by a sleeper cell activated after years of inactivity.

Synset Examples

an al-Qaeda sleeper cell may have used Arizona as its base

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cell - a small group of people acting as a unit, especially in espionage or terrorism
