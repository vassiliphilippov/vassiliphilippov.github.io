Newfoundland dwarf birch

1. NOUN

/ ˌnjuːfənˌlænd dwɔrf bɜrtʃ / (AmE), / ˌnjuːfənˌlænd dwɔːf bɜːtʃ / (BrE)
a small shrubby birch native to cold regions of North America and Greenland
a small birch shrub from North America and Greenland

Examples

The Newfoundland dwarf birch is commonly found in the tundra regions of Labrador.
Botanists studied the Newfoundland dwarf birch to understand how plants survive harsh climates.
We spotted a Newfoundland dwarf birch while hiking through the northern forests of Canada.

Related Terms
