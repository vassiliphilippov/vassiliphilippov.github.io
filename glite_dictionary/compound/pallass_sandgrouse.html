Pallas's sandgrouse

1. NOUN

/ ˈpæləsɪz ˈsændˌɡraʊs / (AmE), / ˈpæləsɪz ˈsændˌɡraʊs / (BrE)
a species of sandgrouse native to Eurasia, noted for having a black patch on its belly
Eurasiatic sandgrouse species with black belly patch

Examples

The sudden arrival of Pallas's sandgrouse in western Europe surprised ornithologists.
A male Pallas's sandgrouse has a distinctive black patch on its belly and long pointed tail feathers.
During migration, large flocks of Pallas's sandgrouse can sometimes be seen crossing arid landscapes.

Etymology

Named after the German naturalist Peter Simon Pallas (1741–1811), who described the species.
