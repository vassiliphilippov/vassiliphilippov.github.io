ECTS-credit point

1. NOUN

/ ˌiː ˌsiː ˌtiː ˈɛs ˈkrɛdɪt pɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˌiː ˌsiː ˌtiː ˈɛs ˈkrɛdɪt pɔɪnt / (BrE)
a unit used in European higher education to measure the workload and learning outcomes required to complete a course, seminar, or module, as part of the European Credit Transfer and Accumulation System (ECTS)
unit of academic credit in Europe

Examples

Each master's course is worth 5 ECTS-credit points, reflecting the study required.
To graduate, students must accumulate 180 ECTS-credit points over three years.
The ECTS-credit point system makes it easier for students to transfer between European universities.

Synset Examples

One academic year corresponds to 60 ECTS credits that are equivalent to 1500-1800 hours of study in all countries respective of standard or qualification type and is used to facilitate transfer and progression throughout the Union.

Etymology

Acronym from European Credit Transfer and Accumulation System (ECTS) + 'credit point'.
