railroad siding

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪlˌrɑd ˈsaɪdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈreɪlˌrəʊd ˈsaɪdɪŋ / (BrE)
a short length of railway track connected to the main track, used for storing trains or letting other trains pass
a rail track for storage or passing

Examples

The train waited on the railroad siding while the express passed by on the main line.
Old freight cars were stored on a remote railroad siding outside the city.
The engineer pulled the locomotive into a railroad siding to allow another train to overtake.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: siding - a short railway track connected to a main track
