Plato's Academy

1. PROPN

/ ˈpleɪtoʊz əˈkædəmi / (AmE), / ˈpleɪtəʊz əˈkædəmi / (BrE)
the school of philosophy founded by Plato in ancient Athens, regarded as the prototype of later academies
school of philosophy founded by Plato

Examples

Many famous philosophers studied at Plato's Academy in Athens.
The teachings at Plato's Academy greatly influenced Western thought.
After Plato's death, Plato's Academy continued to flourish for centuries.

Synset Examples

Plato's Academy continued for several hundred years after Plato died

Related Terms

  • Synonym: academy - a place of study or training in a special field

Etymology

Named after the Greek philosopher Plato, who founded the school, and the site Akadēmeia in Athens.
