respiratory tract infection

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈspɪrəˌtɔri trækt ɪnˈfɛkʃən / (AmE), / rɪˈspɪrətəri trækt ɪnˈfɛkʃən / (BrE)
an infection affecting any part of the respiratory tract, including the sinuses, throat, airways, or lungs
infection of the respiratory tract
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed her with a respiratory tract infection after she complained of persistent coughing and a sore throat.
Children are especially susceptible to respiratory tract infections during the winter months.
Antibiotics are sometimes prescribed for severe cases of respiratory tract infection, but many are caused by viruses.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: upper respiratory tract infection - an infection limited to the upper parts of the respiratory tract, such as the nose and throat
  • Degree Higher: lower respiratory tract infection - an infection affecting the lower parts of the respiratory tract, such as the bronchi or lungs
