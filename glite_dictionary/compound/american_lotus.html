American lotus

1. NOUN

/ əˈmɛrɪkən ˈloʊtəs / (AmE), / əˈmɛrɪkən ˈləʊtəs / (BrE)
a water lily native to eastern North America, with pale yellow flowers and edible, nutlike seeds
North American water lily with yellow flowers

Examples

The American lotus can be found growing in shallow ponds and lakes throughout the Midwest.
Many people harvest the seeds of the American lotus for food.
Large leaves and striking yellow blossoms make the American lotus easily recognizable in wetlands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lotus - aquatic plant with large, showy flowers
