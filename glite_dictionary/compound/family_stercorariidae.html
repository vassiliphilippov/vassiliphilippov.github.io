family Stercorariidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli stɜːrˌkɔːrəˈraɪɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli stɜːˌkɒərəˈraɪɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of predatory seabirds known as jaegers and skuas, found mainly in polar and subpolar regions.
family of predatory seabirds (jaegers and skuas)

Examples

The family Stercorariidae includes both skuas and jaegers, which are known for their aggressive feeding habits.
Research on the migratory patterns of the family Stercorariidae helps us understand changes in marine ecosystems.
Birdwatchers were thrilled to spot several members of the family Stercorariidae during the coastal expedition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skua - a large, aggressive seabird in the family Stercorariidae
  • Synonym: jaeger - a predatory seabird of the same family, known for stealing other birds' food

Etymology

From scientific Latin 'Stercorariidae', based on 'Stercorarius' (type genus) meaning 'of dung', from Latin 'stercus' (dung), referring to their feeding habits.
