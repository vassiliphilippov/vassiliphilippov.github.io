anchor ring

1. NOUN

/ ˈæŋkər rɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈaŋkə rɪŋ / (BrE)
a ring-shaped surface or solid with a circular cross-section and a hole in the center; a torus
toroidal (ring-shaped) figure or surface

Examples

The mathematician explained the properties of an anchor ring using a three-dimensional model.
Some bacteria have flagella arranged in an anchor ring at one end of the cell.
The shape of an anchor ring can be seen in many inflatable pool toys.

Synset Examples

a ring of ships in the harbor
a halo of smoke

Related Terms

  • Synonym: torus - a surface or solid formed by rotating a circle around an axis in its plane

Etymology

From 'anchor' + 'ring', referring to metal rings used in anchoring, later used in geometry for a torus shape.
