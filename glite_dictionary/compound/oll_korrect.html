oll korrect

1. X

/ ɑl kəˈrɛkt / (AmE), / ɒl kəˈrɛkt / (BrE)
(archaic, humorous) An alternative spelling of 'all correct,' used as a playful or deliberately incorrect way of indicating agreement or correctness; an early form of 'OK'.
humorous/archaic form of 'OK'
archaic

Examples

In old newspapers, you might find the phrase oll korrect instead of 'OK'.
He ended the note with a cheerful 'oll korrect' to amuse his friend.
The president used oll korrect as a catchphrase during his campaign in the 1840s.

Synset Examples

The term 'oll korrect' eventually evolved into the word 'OK'.
He used 'oll korrect' in his letter to make it more fun.
The phrase 'oll korrect' was a humorous way to say okay.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: OK - expressing agreement or acceptance; fine
  • Synonym: all correct - fully correct; without mistakes

Etymology

Intentionally humorous misspelling of 'all correct', popularized in 19th-century American English and a source of 'OK'.
