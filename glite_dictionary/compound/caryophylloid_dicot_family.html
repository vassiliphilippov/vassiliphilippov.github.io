caryophylloid dicot family

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɛr.i.əˈfɪl.ɔɪd ˈdaɪ.kɑt ˈfæm.ə.li / (AmE), / ˌkær.i.əˈfɪl.ɔɪd ˈdaɪ.kət ˈfæm.ɪ.li / (BrE)
A family of relatively early dicotyledonous plants, mostly consisting of flowering species.
family of dicot flowering plants

Examples

The caryophylloid dicot family includes many well-known ornamental flowers like carnations and pinks.
Botanists have identified unique characteristics that distinguish the caryophylloid dicot family from other early dicot plant groups.
Recent studies focus on the evolutionary history of the caryophylloid dicot family within flowering plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Caryophyllaceae - the botanical family also known as the pink or carnation family

Etymology

From New Latin Caryophyllaceae (type genus Caryophyllus) + Greek -oid ('like') + dicot (short for dicotyledon).
