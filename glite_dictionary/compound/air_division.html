air division

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛr dɪˌvɪʒən / (AmE), / ˈeə dɪˌvɪʒən / (BrE)
a unit of the United States Air Force that usually consists of two or more wings and forms an intermediate level of command.
US Air Force unit (two or more wings)

Examples

The new air division commander addressed his staff at the morning meeting.
During the Cold War, each air division had its own specific strategic objectives.
After reorganizing, the United States Air Force created several new air divisions to improve coordination.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: wing - an operational unit in the Air Force, smaller than a division
