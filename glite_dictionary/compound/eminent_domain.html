eminent domain

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛmɪnənt doʊˈmeɪn / (AmE), / ˈɛmɪnənt dəʊˈmeɪn / (BrE)
the right of a government to take private property for public use, with payment of compensation to the owner
government right to take private property
legal american

Examples

The government exercised eminent domain to acquire land for the new highway.
Homeowners protested when their properties were threatened by eminent domain claims.
Local authorities must offer fair compensation when invoking eminent domain for public projects.

Etymology

From Latin 'eminentia' (distinction, prominence) and Old French 'domaine', from Latin 'dominium' (ownership).
