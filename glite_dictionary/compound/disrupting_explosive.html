disrupting explosive

1. NOUN

/ dɪsˈrʌptɪŋ ɪkˈsploʊsɪv / (AmE), / dɪsˈrʌptɪŋ ɪkˈspləʊsɪv / (BrE)
a type of high explosive used to damage or destroy a targeted object, especially in military or demolition operations.
high explosive used to damage a target

Examples

The bomb disposal unit used a disrupting explosive to safely neutralize the suspicious package.
Engineers selected a disrupting explosive to breach the concrete barrier.
A disrupting explosive is often employed for controlled demolitions in urban areas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: explosive - substance that causes an explosion
