Montezuma cypress

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɒn.təˈzuː.mə ˈsaɪ.prəs / (AmE), / ˌmɒn.tɪˈzjuː.mə ˈsaɪ.prəs / (BrE)
the wood obtained from cypress trees (Taxodium mucronatum) native to river valleys of the Mexican highlands
wood of Mexican river cypress tree

Examples

The benches were crafted from Montezuma cypress to showcase the beauty of Mexican hardwood.
Due to its durability, Montezuma cypress is often used in outdoor construction in Mexico.
The rich color of Montezuma cypress makes it popular for artisanal woodwork and sculptures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bald cypress - wood from Taxodium distichum, a related North American species

Etymology

Named after Montezuma II, Aztec emperor, and cypress from Greek kuparissos.

2. NOUN

/ ˌmɑːnteɪˈzuːmə ˈsaɪprəs / (AmE), / ˌmɒntɪˈzuːmə ˈsaɪprəs / (BrE)
a large cypress tree (Taxodium mucronatum) native to river valleys in the highlands of Mexico, known for its broad trunk and longevity
Mexican highland cypress tree species

Examples

The Montezuma cypress is considered the national tree of Mexico.
Tourists admire the massive trunk of the ancient Montezuma cypress in Oaxaca.
The Montezuma cypress thrives in moist river valleys throughout the Mexican highlands.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: bald cypress - a closely related species of cypress found in the southern United States

Etymology

Named after Montezuma II, Aztec emperor, and the Latinized word for cypress.
