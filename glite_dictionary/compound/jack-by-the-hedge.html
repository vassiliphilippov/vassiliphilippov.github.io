jack-by-the-hedge

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒæk baɪ ðə ˈhɛdʒ / (AmE), / ˌdʒak baɪ ðə ˈhɛdʒ / (BrE)
A European plant, also called garlic mustard, with leaves that smell of garlic, often found growing by hedges and in woodlands.
European herb smelling of garlic

Examples

You can easily identify jack-by-the-hedge by its strong garlic scent when you crush the leaves.
Foragers often collect jack-by-the-hedge in the spring to add to salads and sauces.
The footpath was lined with clusters of jack-by-the-hedge, filling the air with a faint garlic aroma.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: garlic mustard - another common name for Alliaria petiolata

Etymology

From the common British practice of naming plants 'Jack' plus a description of their location; 'hedge' referring to its typical habitat.
