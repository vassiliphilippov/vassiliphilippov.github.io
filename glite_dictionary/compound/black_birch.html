black birch

1. NOUN

/ blæk bɝtʃ / (AmE), / blæk bɜːtʃ / (BrE)
A species of birch tree native to swamps and river bottoms in the eastern United States, noted for its reddish-brown bark.
birch tree with reddish-brown bark

Examples

The black birch thrives in wet soils along the riverbanks of the eastern United States.
Honey made from the nectar of black birch trees has a unique, spicy flavor.
Botanists conducted a study on the effects of climate change on black birch populations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: birch - any of various trees or shrubs of the genus Betula

2. NOUN

/ blæk bɝtʃ / (AmE), / blak bɜːtʃ / (BrE)
a birch tree native to eastern North America, with dark bark that produces a volatile oil and hard wood used for furniture
North American birch tree with dark bark

Examples

The dense wood of the black birch is prized by furniture makers.
Aromatic oil can be distilled from the bark of the black birch.
In the Appalachian forests, black birch often grows alongside maple and oak.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sweet birch - another name for the same tree, Betula lenta
  • Degree Lower: yellow birch - a different species of birch with yellowish bark, Betula alleghaniensis
