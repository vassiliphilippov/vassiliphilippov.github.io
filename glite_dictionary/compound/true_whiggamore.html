true Whiggamore

1. NOUN

/ tru ˈwɪg.ə.mɔr / (AmE), / truː ˈwɪɡ.əmɔː / (BrE)
A person who is steadfastly loyal to a particular party or cause, especially in the context of the Whiggamores, a historical political faction.
staunch supporter of a party or cause
archaic

Examples

Despite the shifting political winds, he remained a true Whiggamore devoted to his party's ideals.
Many historians describe her as a true Whiggamore for her unwavering commitment to reform.
He always voted along party lines, earning a reputation as a true Whiggamore.

Synset Examples

He joked that his friend was a true Whiggamore at heart.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: partisan - a strong supporter of a party, cause, or person
  • Antonym: opponent - someone who opposes or competes against another

Etymology

From 'Whiggamore', a Scottish term originally referring to Covenanters who marched on Edinburgh in 1648, later applied to members of the Whig party.
