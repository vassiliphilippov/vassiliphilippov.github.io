lion-jaw forceps

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪ.ən dʒɔ ˈfɔr.sɛps / (AmE), / ˈlaɪ.ən dʒɔː ˈfɔː.seps / (BrE)
a type of surgical forceps with strong, sharp teeth in the jaws, used for holding bone fragments during procedures
surgical forceps for holding bone fragments
medical

Examples

The surgeon used lion-jaw forceps to firmly grasp the fractured bone during the operation.
After sterilizing the lion-jaw forceps, the nurse handed them to the orthopedic team.
Lion-jaw forceps are preferred when strong gripping of bone fragments is required.
