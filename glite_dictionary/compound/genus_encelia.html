genus Encelia

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ɛnˈsiː.li.ə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ɛnˈsiː.li.ə / (BrE)
A genus of shrubs native to the southwestern United States and Mexico, commonly known as brittlebush.
genus of shrubs (brittlebush)

Examples

The genus Encelia includes several species of drought-tolerant shrubs found in arid regions.
Scientists studied the adaptations of plants in the genus Encelia to understand desert ecology.
The genus Encelia, known for brittlebush, provides important habitat for desert wildlife.

Etymology

Genus name from the Swedish botanist Christoph Entzelt (Encelius).
