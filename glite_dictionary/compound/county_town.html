county town

1. NOUN

/ ˈkaʊnti taʊn / (AmE), / ˈkaʊnti taʊn / (BrE)
the town or city that serves as the administrative center of a county, typically where the county government is located
administrative center of a county
british

Examples

Norwich is the historic county town of Norfolk.
The records were transferred to the county town after the new council offices opened.
Each county town typically has a courthouse and administrative buildings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: county seat - the town or city that is the seat of government for a county, chiefly in the US
