magnetic energy

1. NOUN

/ mægˈnɛtɪk ˈɛnɚdʒi / (AmE), / mæɡˈnɛtɪk ˈɛnədʒi / (BrE)
the potential energy stored in a magnetic field or possessed by a system due to its magnetization.
potential energy of a magnetic field

Examples

The coil stores magnetic energy when an electric current passes through it.
In an MRI machine, large amounts of magnetic energy are used to create detailed images of the body.
Physicists study the conversion of magnetic energy into electrical power in generators.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: potential energy - energy stored in an object due to its position or condition
