minor premise

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪnər ˈprɛmɪs / (AmE), / ˈmaɪnə ˈprɛmɪs / (BrE)
the premise in a syllogism that contains the minor term, which becomes the subject of the conclusion
the premise containing the minor term in a syllogism

Examples

In the syllogism 'All men are mortal; Socrates is a man; therefore, Socrates is mortal,' the minor premise is 'Socrates is a man.'
She explained that the minor premise must always mention the subject of the conclusion.
Understanding both the major and minor premise is essential for analyzing logical arguments.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: major premise - the premise of a syllogism containing the major term (the predicate of the conclusion)

Etymology

From Latin 'minor' (lesser) and 'praemissa' (things sent before).
