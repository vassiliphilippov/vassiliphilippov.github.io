arch doxy

1. NOUN

/ ɑrtʃ ˈdɑksi / (AmE), / ɑːtʃ ˈdɒksi / (BrE)
(Archaic) A leading woman, especially the mistress of a prominent man or a notable female camp follower; sometimes used for a notable female figure in a religious or social context.
leading woman, mistress (archaic)
archaic

Examples

The arch doxy held sway over the congregation, commanding attention with her presence.
Centuries ago, being called an arch doxy could imply both admiration and scandal.
In the old ballad, the arch doxy led the procession with songs and laughter.

Synset Examples

The arch doxy delivered a powerful sermon that moved many.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: doxy - mistress or prostitute (archaic slang)

Etymology

From 'arch-' (chief, principal) + 'doxy' (slang for a mistress, from early English cant).
