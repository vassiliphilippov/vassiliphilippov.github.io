deficiency disease

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈfɪʃənsi dɪˈziz / (AmE), / dɪˈfɪʃənsi dɪˈziːz / (BrE)
A disease caused by the lack of an essential nutrient, such as a vitamin or mineral, in the diet.
disease caused by lack of nutrients
medical

Examples

The most common deficiency disease caused by lack of vitamin C is scurvy.
Children in impoverished communities are at higher risk of developing deficiency diseases.
Rickets is a deficiency disease resulting from insufficient vitamin D.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nutritional deficiency - lack of essential nutrients in the body
