plains pocket gopher

1. NOUN

/ pleɪnz ˈpɑkɪt ˈɡoʊfər / (AmE), / pleɪnz ˈpɒkɪt ˈɡəʊfə / (BrE)
a burrowing rodent native to the grasslands of central North America, known for fur-lined cheek pouches used to carry food
North American burrowing rodent species

Examples

The plains pocket gopher spends much of its life underground, digging intricate tunnel systems.
Farmers often notice mounds of soil, a sign that a plains pocket gopher is active in the area.
Unlike other species, the plains pocket gopher prefers the loose, sandy soils of grassland regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pocket gopher - a burrowing rodent with fur-lined cheek pouches
