visual modality

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪʒuəl məˈdæləti / (AmE), / ˈvɪʒuəl məˈdalɪti / (BrE)
the sensory system or process related to seeing; the faculty of vision as distinguished from other senses
the sense or faculty of vision

Examples

The brain integrates information from the visual modality and the auditory modality to create perception.
Damage to the visual modality can result in partial or total blindness.
Researchers studied how quickly children respond to cues presented through the visual modality.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: auditory modality - the sense or faculty of hearing
  • Degree Higher: modality - a particular mode in which something exists or is experienced, especially a sensory experience

Etymology

From Latin 'visus' (sight) and Medieval Latin 'modalitas' (mode, manner).
