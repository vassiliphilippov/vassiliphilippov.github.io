bank failure

1. NOUN

/ bæŋk ˈfeɪljər / (AmE), / bæŋk ˈfeɪljə / (BrE)
the situation in which a bank is unable to meet its financial obligations and ceases operations
collapse of a bank due to insolvency

Examples

The central bank intervened to prevent a bank failure that could have affected the entire economy.
After the bank failure, many customers lost their life savings.
Government regulations were tightened in response to a recent bank failure in the region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bankruptcy - legal status of being unable to repay debts
  • Antonym: solvency - the ability to meet long-term financial obligations
