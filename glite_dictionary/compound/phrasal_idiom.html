phrasal idiom

1. NOUN

/ ˈfreɪzəl ˈɪdiəm / (AmE), / ˈfreɪzəl ˈɪdiəm / (BrE)
an idiomatic expression whose meaning cannot be deduced from the meanings of its individual words
type of idiom; meaning not literal

Examples

Many English learners struggle to understand phrasal idioms such as 'break the ice.'
The book contains a list of common phrasal idioms used in daily conversation.
She explained that 'kick the bucket' is a phrasal idiom meaning 'to die.'

Related Terms

  • Synonym: idiom - a group of words whose meaning is different from the meanings of the words individually
