Palau Islands

1. PROPN

/ pəˈlaʊ ˈaɪ.ləndz / (AmE), / pəˈlaʊ ˈaɪ.ləndz / (BrE)
a chain of more than 200 islands, about 400 miles long, located in the western central Pacific Ocean, forming part of the country of Palau
chain of islands in the western Pacific Ocean

Examples

The Palau Islands are renowned for their pristine coral reefs and rich marine biodiversity.
During World War II, fierce battles were fought on several of the Palau Islands.
Tourists flock to the Palau Islands for diving and snorkeling adventures.

Etymology

Named after the native term 'Belau', later adapted as 'Palau' by English speakers.
