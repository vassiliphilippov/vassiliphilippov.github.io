prairie white-fringed orchid

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɛr.i waɪt ˈfrɪndʒd ˈɔr.kɪd / (AmE), / ˈpreə.ri waɪt ˈfrɪn(d)ʒd ˈɔː.kɪd / (BrE)
a rare orchid species (Platanthera leucophaea) native to central North America, characterized by white, fringed flowers and often found in prairies; considered threatened in its natural habitat
a threatened North American orchid species

Examples

The prairie white-fringed orchid is protected under federal law due to its declining numbers.
Botanists discovered several prairie white-fringed orchid specimens during their field survey of the tallgrass prairie.
Habitat loss poses a serious threat to the prairie white-fringed orchid and other rare wildflowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orchid - a plant of the family Orchidaceae, known for complex and beautiful flowers
