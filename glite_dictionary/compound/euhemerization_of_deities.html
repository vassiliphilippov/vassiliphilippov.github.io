euhemerization of deities

1. NOUN

/ juˌhimərəˈzeɪʃən əv ˈdiːɪtiz / (AmE), / juːˌhiːmərəˈzeɪʃ(ə)n əv ˈdiːɪtiːz / (BrE)
the process of interpreting gods as real historical figures rather than as supernatural beings
interpreting gods as historical figures

Examples

The euhemerization of deities can be observed in the transformation of ancient gods into legendary kings.
Many academic texts explore the euhemerization of deities in early mythologies.
The euhemerization of deities has shaped modern interpretations of several mythological figures.

Synset Examples

Scholars often discuss the euhemerization of deities in literature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: euhemerism - the theory that gods originated as real historical persons

Etymology

From 'euhemerize', named after the Greek writer Euhemerus, combined with the plural of 'deity'.
