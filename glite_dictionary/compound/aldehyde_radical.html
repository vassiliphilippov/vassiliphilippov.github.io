aldehyde radical

1. NOUN

/ ˈældɪˌhaɪd ˈrædɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈældɪˌhaɪd ˈrædɪkəl / (BrE)
the functional group -CHO found in organic compounds, characteristic of aldehydes
the -CHO chemical group

Examples

The presence of an aldehyde radical distinguishes aldehydes from other organic compounds.
In the structure of formaldehyde, there is a single aldehyde radical bonded to a hydrogen atom.
Chemists often test for the aldehyde radical to identify aldehydes in a sample.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aldehyde - an organic compound containing a -CHO group

Etymology

From 'aldehyde', which is from German 'Aldehyd', altered from New Latin 'alcohol dehydrogenatum' (dehydrogenated alcohol), and 'radical', from Latin 'radix' meaning root.
