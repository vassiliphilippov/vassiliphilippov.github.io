Euclid's fourth axiom

1. NOUN

/ ˈjuːklɪdz ˈfɔrθ ˈæksiˌɑm / (AmE), / ˈjuːklɪdz ˈfɔːθ ˈæksiɒm / (BrE)
the axiom in Euclidean geometry stating that all right angles are equal to each other
Euclidean axiom: all right angles are equal

Examples

According to Euclid's fourth axiom, all right angles are congruent.
The teacher explained how Euclid's fourth axiom underpins the logic of geometric proofs.
Understanding Euclid's fourth axiom is essential for studying classical geometry.

Etymology

Euclid: from Ancient Greek Εὐκλείδης (Eukleidēs), Greek mathematician; axiom: from Greek axiōma (worthy).
