tale of Job

1. NOUN

/ teɪl əv dʒoʊb / (AmE), / teɪl əv dʒəʊb / (BrE)
A story or reference to Job from the Bible, illustrating patience and endurance in the face of suffering.
biblical story symbolizing patience and endurance

Examples

Many people are reminded of the tale of Job when dealing with prolonged hardships.
He referenced the tale of Job to encourage his friend to persevere.
The sermon used the tale of Job to illustrate faith during adversity.

Synset Examples

The tale of Job teaches us about resilience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: patience of Job - extreme patience and endurance in the face of suffering, as exemplified by Job

Etymology

From the Book of Job in the Hebrew Bible, which tells the story of Job's trials.
