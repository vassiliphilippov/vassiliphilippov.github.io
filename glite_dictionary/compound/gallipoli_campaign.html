Gallipoli Campaign

1. NOUN

/ ɡəˈlɪpəli kæmˈpeɪn / (AmE), / ɡəˈlɪpəli kəmˈpeɪn / (BrE)
the unsuccessful military campaign by Allied forces in World War I (1915) aimed at capturing the Gallipoli peninsula from the Ottoman Empire to secure a sea route to Russia; the attack was repelled by Turkish forces
Allied World War I campaign at Gallipoli (1915)

Examples

The Gallipoli Campaign is remembered as one of World War I’s most significant failures for the Allies.
Many soldiers lost their lives during the brutal Gallipoli Campaign of 1915.
Historians often debate the strategic mistakes made in the Gallipoli Campaign and their long-term consequences.

Etymology

Gallipoli: from Italian *Gallipoli*, from Greek *Kallipolis* ('beautiful city'); Campaign: from French *campagne*, from Italian *campagna* ('field').
