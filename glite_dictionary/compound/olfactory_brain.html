olfactory brain

1. NOUN

/ ɑlˈfæktəɹi breɪn / (AmE), / ɒlˈfæktəri breɪn / (BrE)
the part of the brain, particularly in the cerebral hemispheres, that controls the sense of smell; in humans, it is associated with mediating complex emotional behavior
brain region governing sense of smell
medical

Examples

Damage to the olfactory brain can result in a diminished sense of smell.
The evolution of the olfactory brain is more pronounced in animals that rely on scent for survival.
Researchers studied how the human olfactory brain processes emotional responses to odors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: olfactory bulb - the neural structure involved in the sense of smell

Etymology

From Latin 'olfactorius' (relating to smelling) and 'brain'.
