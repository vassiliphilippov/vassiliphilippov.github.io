suborder Limicolae

1. NOUN

/ sʌbˌɔrdər ˌlɪmɪˈkoʊliː / (AmE), / sʌbˌɔːdə ˌlɪmɪˈkəʊliː / (BrE)
a taxonomic group used in some classifications for migratory shorebirds, coextensive with the Charadrii
a group of migratory shorebirds (taxonomic)

Examples

The suborder Limicolae includes many species of migratory shorebirds found on every continent.
Taxonomies may differ, but some still recognize the suborder Limicolae as a distinct grouping within birds.
Researchers study the migration patterns of birds classified under the suborder Limicolae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Charadrii - taxonomic order that includes waders/shorebirds

Etymology

From Latin 'Limicolae', meaning 'dweller in mud', from 'limus' (mud) + 'cola' (dweller).
