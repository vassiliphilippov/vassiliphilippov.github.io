dumb cane

1. NOUN

/ dʌm keɪn / (AmE), / dʌm keɪn / (BrE)
a tropical plant with large, attractive dark green leaves, containing toxins that can swell the tongue and throat if ingested
a tropical poisonous houseplant

Examples

The dumb cane in her living room needs to be kept out of reach of children and pets.
He accidentally touched his mouth after trimming his dumb cane and felt a tingling sensation.
Houseplant enthusiasts should be cautious, as dumb cane contains hazardous sap.

Etymology

So named because ingestion of its sap may temporarily render someone unable to speak ('dumb').
