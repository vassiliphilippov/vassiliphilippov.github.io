spackling compound

1. NOUN

/ ˈspæk.lɪŋ ˌkɑm.paʊnd / (AmE), / ˈspæk.lɪŋ ˌkɒm.paʊnd / (BrE)
a powder, usually containing gypsum plaster and glue, that forms a plastic paste when mixed with water and is used to fill cracks and holes in plaster or wallboard
paste filler for cracks/holes in walls

Examples

Before painting the wall, he filled the cracks with spackling compound.
After sanding down the spackling compound, the surface was smooth and ready for primer.
Most home improvement stores carry various types of spackling compound for repairing drywall.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: filler - material for filling holes or gaps
  • Synonym: plaster - material used for coating walls and ceilings

Etymology

From the brand name 'Spackle', with 'compound' indicating a mixture or substance.
