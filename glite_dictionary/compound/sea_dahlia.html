sea dahlia

1. NOUN

/ si ˈdæliə / (AmE), / siː ˈdalɪə / (BrE)
A stout herbaceous plant with a single large flower per stalk, cultivated as an ornamental and originally developed from a Mexican wildflower.
ornamental plant with solitary flowers

Examples

The gardener planted a row of sea dahlias along the edge of the flowerbed for a splash of color.
Botanists admire the unique structure of the sea dahlia and its large, vibrant blooms.
Each sea dahlia in the garden stood tall, displaying a single impressive flower on its stalk.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dahlia - a genus of bushy, tuberous, herbaceous perennial plants native to Mexico and Central America, cultivated for their brightly colored flowers
