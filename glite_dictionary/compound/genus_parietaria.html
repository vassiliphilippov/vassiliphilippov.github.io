genus Parietaria

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs pəˌraɪ.əˈtɛr.i.ə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs pəˌraɪ.ɪˈtɛə.ri.ə / (BrE)
A small genus of stingless herbaceous plants in the family Urticaceae, commonly known as Parietaria.
genus of stingless herbs (Parietaria)

Examples

The genus Parietaria includes several plants commonly found growing in temperate regions.
Researchers studied pollen from the genus Parietaria to understand allergy patterns.
Members of the genus Parietaria are often referred to as pellitory in botanical literature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Parietaria - any herb of the genus Parietaria

Etymology

From New Latin 'Parietaria', from Latin 'paries' (wall), referring to the habit of some species growing on walls.
