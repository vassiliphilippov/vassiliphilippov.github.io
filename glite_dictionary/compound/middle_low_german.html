Middle Low German

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪdəl loʊ dʒɜrmən / (AmE), / ˈmɪdəl ləʊ ˈdʒɜːmən / (BrE)
A historical variety of the Low German language spoken and written from about 1100 to 1500, notable as a lingua franca in northern Europe during the Hanseatic League period.
historical Low German language (1100–1500)

Examples

The Hanseatic League conducted much of its trade negotiations in Middle Low German.
Many historical documents from northern Germany are written in Middle Low German.
The grammar of Middle Low German differs significantly from that of modern Standard German.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Low German - West Germanic language spoken mainly in northern Germany and the eastern Netherlands
  • Synonym: Middle High German - historical German language spoken from 1050 to 1350

Etymology

Formed from 'Middle' (referring to the medieval period) + 'Low German' (from the German 'Niederdeutsch').
