jack oak

1. NOUN

/ dʒæk oʊk / (AmE), / dʒæk əʊk / (BrE)
a common deciduous oak tree of central and southeastern United States with dark bark and broad, club-shaped three-lobed leaves, often forming dense thickets
a scrubby oak tree of the U.S. with lobed leaves

Examples

Dense thickets of jack oak made the land difficult to clear for farming.
The jack oak is easily recognized by its dark bark and club-shaped leaves.
Wildlife is often found sheltering among the jack oak trees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: black oak - a related species of oak tree in North America

2. NOUN

/ dʒæk oʊk / (AmE), / dʒæk əʊk / (BrE)
A small to medium-sized deciduous oak tree native to east central North America, with leaves having sharply pointed lobes.
North American oak tree species

Examples

A mature jack oak can provide excellent shade in a backyard garden.
Due to its tolerance for poor soils, jack oak often thrives where other trees cannot.
The distinctive leaves of the jack oak make it easy to identify among other oaks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oak - deciduous or evergreen tree of the genus Quercus
