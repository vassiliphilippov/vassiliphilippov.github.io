ultramarine blue

1. NOUN

/ ˌʌltrə məˈrin bluː / (AmE), / ˌʌltrəməˈriːn bluː / (BrE)
a deep blue pigment made from powdered lapis lazuli, or a synthetic substance of similar color
deep blue pigment from lapis lazuli

Examples

The artist preferred to use ultramarine blue in her seascapes for its brilliant and enduring hue.
Historically, ultramarine blue was one of the most expensive pigments available to painters.
A hint of ultramarine blue gave the sky in the painting its vivid, almost otherworldly color.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cobalt blue - a bright blue pigment made from cobalt salts

Etymology

From Latin *ultramarinus* (beyond the sea), originally referring to the importation of lapis lazuli from Asia.
