pediculosis corporis

1. NOUN

/ pɛˌdɪkjəˌloʊsɪs ˈkɔrpərəs / (AmE), / pɛˌdɪkjʊˌləʊsɪs ˈkɔːpərɪs / (BrE)
infestation of the skin of the body caused by body lice, characterized by itching and irritation
infestation of body with lice
medical

Examples

Pediculosis corporis is more common in crowded or unhygienic living conditions.
The patient was diagnosed with pediculosis corporis after presenting with persistent itching and a rash.
Proper hygiene and washing clothes in hot water are essential to prevent pediculosis corporis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pediculosis capitis - infestation of the scalp with head lice
  • Synonym: scabies - skin infestation caused by mites leading to itching

Etymology

From Latin 'pediculus' (louse) and 'corporis' (of the body).
