Dianthus chinensis heddewigii

1. NOUN

/ daɪˈænθəs tʃɪˈnɛnsɪs ˌhɛdəˈwiːɡiː / (AmE), / daɪˈænθəs tʃɪˈnɛnsɪs ˌhɛdəˈwiːɡiː / (BrE)
A flowering cultivar of China pink, noted for its jagged-edged petals, often grown as an ornamental garden plant.
flowering ornamental plant; jagged-edged China pink

Examples

Many gardeners appreciate the bold colors of Dianthus chinensis heddewigii in summer borders.
I planted several Dianthus chinensis heddewigii along the walkway for their showy, fringed petals.
Dianthus chinensis heddewigii thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: China pink - A species of Dianthus with colorful flowers, native to East Asia

Etymology

Dianthus and chinensis from Latin botanical nomenclature; 'heddewigii' commemorates the 19th-century German gardener Heddewig.
