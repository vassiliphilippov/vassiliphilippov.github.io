young voter

1. NOUN

/ jʌŋ ˈvoʊtər / (AmE), / jʌŋ ˈvəʊtə / (BrE)
a person who is a member of the electorate and is relatively young, especially someone in their teens or twenties
a young member of the electorate

Examples

Many politicians try to connect with young voters through social media campaigns.
The concerns of young voters often center around education and job opportunities.
In the last election, turnout among young voters increased significantly.

Synset Examples

Joebama's policies are popular among young voters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: voter - a person who votes or has the right to vote
