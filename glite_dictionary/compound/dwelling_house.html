dwelling house

1. NOUN

/ ˈdwɛlɪŋ ˌhaʊs / (AmE), / ˈdwɛlɪŋ ˌhaʊs / (BrE)
a house or building in which people live, especially as distinct from a commercial or industrial building
residential house; place where people live

Examples

The town council had strict regulations about what constituted a dwelling house in the residential district.
The insurance policy only covers damage to the main dwelling house, not to detached garages or sheds.
In some countries, it is illegal to run a business out of a dwelling house without special permission.

Synset Examples

he built a modest dwelling near the pond
they raise money to provide homes for the homeless

Related Terms

  • Synonym: residence - a place where someone lives
  • Synonym: house - a building for human habitation
  • Antonym: commercial building - a building intended for business use

Etymology

From 'dwelling' (from Old English *dwellan*, to linger) and 'house' (from Old English *hūs*).
