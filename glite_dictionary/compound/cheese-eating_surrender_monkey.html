cheese-eating surrender monkey

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃiːz ˌiːtɪŋ səˈrɛndɚ ˈmʌŋki / (AmE), / ˈtʃiːz ˌiːtɪŋ səˈrɛndə ˈmʌŋki / (BrE)
(offensive, informal) A derogatory term for someone, especially of French origin, who is seen as cowardly or willing to surrender easily.
derogatory term for a cowardly person, esp. French
offensive informal

Examples

He called the opposing team a cheese-eating surrender monkey after their lackluster performance.
Some critics dismissed the diplomat as a cheese-eating surrender monkey for not standing firm in negotiations.
The phrase cheese-eating surrender monkeys was used in a satirical editorial about political appeasement.

Synset Examples

He called the opposing team a cheese-eating surrender monkey.
That attitude makes you a cheese-eating surrender monkey.
They labeled the politician a cheese-eating surrender monkey.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coward - a person who lacks courage
  • Antonym: hero - a person noted for courageous acts

Etymology

Popularized by the television show 'The Simpsons' (1995) as a satirical insult towards the French.
