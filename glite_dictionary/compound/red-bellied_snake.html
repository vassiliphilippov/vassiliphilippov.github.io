red-bellied snake

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɛd ˈbɛlid ˌsneɪk / (AmE), / ˌrɛd ˈbɛlɪd ˌsneɪk / (BrE)
a small, harmless woodland snake found in the southeastern United States, known for its distinctive reddish belly
small harmless snake of southeastern US

Examples

The red-bellied snake is often found under logs or leaf litter in wooded areas.
Although shy and elusive, the red-bellied snake plays an important role in controlling insect populations.
Many people are surprised to learn that the red-bellied snake is completely harmless to humans.
