fraud in fact

1. NOUN

/ frɔd ɪn fækt / (AmE), / frɔːd ɪn fakt / (BrE)
Actual deception involving concealment or false representation made with the intent to harm another party; distinguished from constructive fraud which does not require intent to deceive.
legal term for intentional deceit
legal

Examples

The court found that the company had committed fraud in fact by hiding financial information from investors.
To prove fraud in fact, the plaintiff must show deliberate deception and intent to cause harm.
Unlike negligence, fraud in fact involves an intentional act to mislead another party.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: constructive fraud - deception without intent to deceive, implied by law

Etymology

From Latin 'fraus' (deceit) and English 'in fact' (in reality; actual).
