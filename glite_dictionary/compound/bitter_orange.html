bitter orange

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɪt̬ɚ ˈɔːrɪndʒ / (AmE), / ˈbɪtə ˈɒrɪndʒ / (BrE)
a small, highly acidic orange with a bitter taste, often used in marmalade and flavorings
a type of sour, acidic orange fruit

Examples

The traditional recipe for English marmalade uses bitter orange for its distinctive flavor.
Some herbal supplements contain extracts from bitter orange peel.
Farmers harvested a large crop of bitter oranges this season, destined for preserves and liqueurs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Seville orange - another name for the bitter orange, especially in culinary contexts
  • Antonym: sweet orange - the more commonly eaten orange, less sour than bitter orange

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɪtər ˈɔːrɪndʒ / (AmE), / ˈbɪtə ˈɒrɪndʒ / (BrE)
a citrus tree (Citrus × aurantium) bearing sour or bitter fruit, often used as grafting stock or for making marmalade
citrus tree with sour or bitter fruit

Examples

The bitter orange is commonly used to make traditional English marmalade.
Gardeners often use bitter orange as a rootstock for grafting other citrus varieties.
The peel of the bitter orange is valued in some herbal medicines.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sweet orange - citrus fruit with sweet, edible flesh
