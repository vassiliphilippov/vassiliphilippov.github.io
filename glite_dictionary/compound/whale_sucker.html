whale sucker

1. NOUN

/ ˈweɪl ˌsʌkər / (AmE), / ˈweɪl ˌsʌkə / (BrE)
a large Pacific remora (Remora australis) that attaches itself to whales and dolphins by means of a suction disk on its head
a remora fish that attaches to whales and dolphins

Examples

The whale sucker uses its suction disc to cling to passing whales.
A group of whale suckers were seen attached to a dolphin during the marine research trip.
Biologists studied how the whale sucker benefits from riding on large marine mammals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: remora - a fish that attaches itself to larger animals using a suction disc
