Mother Carey's hen

1. NOUN

/ ˌmʌðər ˈkɛriz hɛn / (AmE), / ˌmʌðə ˈkɛːriz hɛn / (BrE)
a medium-sized seabird, specifically a type of storm petrel, often encountered in the North Atlantic; also called a storm petrel.
a type of storm petrel (seabird)

Examples

The sailors spotted a Mother Carey's hen skimming just above the stormy waves.
Superstitious mariners believed seeing a Mother Carey's hen foretold rough weather at sea.
Ornithologists observed several Mother Carey's hens nesting on the rocky island cliffs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: storm petrel - a small seabird often called Mother Carey's chicken/hen

Etymology

Probably from the folklore figure Mother Carey, symbolizing the sea’s dangers; name used by sailors for storm petrels.
