Provisional Irish Republican Army

1. PROPN

/ prəˈvɪʒənəl ˈaɪrɪʃ rɪˈpʌblɪkən ˈɑːrmi / (AmE), / prəˈvɪʒənəl ˈaɪərɪʃ rɪˈpʌblɪkən ˈɑːmi / (BrE)
a paramilitary organization of Irish nationalists, active mainly in the late 20th century, that used violence and guerrilla tactics to seek a united, independent Ireland and the end of British rule in Northern Ireland
militant Irish nationalist organization (late 20th century)

Examples

The Provisional Irish Republican Army claimed responsibility for several bombings during the conflict in Northern Ireland.
In the 1990s, peace talks included representatives from the Provisional Irish Republican Army.
The activities of the Provisional Irish Republican Army had a significant impact on British and Irish politics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: IRA - abbreviation for Irish Republican Army, broader group including Provisionals
