Cocopa language

1. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊkoʊpə ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈkəʊkəpə ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Yuman language spoken by the Cocopa people of the lower Colorado River region in the United States and Mexico.
Yuman language of the Cocopa people

Examples

The Cocopa language is considered endangered, with only a few fluent speakers remaining today.
Researchers have compiled dictionaries to help preserve the Cocopa language for future generations.
Many traditional stories were originally told in the Cocopa language, reflecting the culture’s rich oral history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Yuman language - a family of Native American languages spoken in the southwestern United States and northern Mexico

Etymology

From Cocopa, the name of the indigenous people, plus 'language'.
