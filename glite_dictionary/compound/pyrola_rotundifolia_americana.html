Pyrola rotundifolia americana

1. NOUN

/ paɪˌroʊlə roʊˌtʌndɪˈfoʊliə əˌmɛrɪˈkænə / (AmE), / paɪˌrɔːlə rəʊˌtʌndɪˈfəʊlɪə əˌmɛrɪˈkɑːnə / (BrE)
A subspecies of an evergreen plant native to eastern North America, with leathery leaves and numerous white flowers.
North American evergreen plant subspecies

Examples

The Pyrola rotundifolia americana thrives in the shaded forests of eastern Canada.
Botanists documented the white flowers of the Pyrola rotundifolia americana during their summer field study.
Gardeners appreciate the leathery leaves of Pyrola rotundifolia americana for their year-round greenery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Pyrola rotundifolia - the round-leaved wintergreen, a closely related species

Etymology

From Latin: 'Pyrola' meaning 'little pear', 'rotundifolia' meaning 'round-leaved', 'americana' denoting American origin.
