sumac family

1. NOUN

/ ˈsuː.mæk ˈfæm.ə.li / (AmE), / ˈsjuː.mæk ˈfæm.ɪ.li / (BrE)
a family of flowering plants, including trees, shrubs, and vines, some of which have resinous or poisonous sap, such as cashew, mango, pistachio, poison ivy, and sumac
plant family including sumac, cashew, and poison ivy

Examples

Many members of the sumac family produce resin that can be toxic to humans.
The mango tree belongs to the sumac family, along with cashew and pistachio.
Poison ivy is a notorious member of the sumac family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Anacardiaceae - the botanical name for the sumac family
