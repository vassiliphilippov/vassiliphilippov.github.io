Marburg disease

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑr.bɜrɡ dɪˌziz / (AmE), / ˈmɑː.bɜːɡ dɪˌziːz / (BrE)
A rare and severe infectious disease caused by the Marburg virus, characterized by fever, bleeding, and organ failure, often fatal in humans.
severe viral hemorrhagic fever
medical

Examples

Several patients infected with Marburg disease were treated in strict isolation units.
An outbreak of Marburg disease caused panic due to its high fatality rate.
Scientists are researching vaccines to prevent the spread of Marburg disease.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ebola - a similar viral hemorrhagic fever caused by the Ebola virus

Etymology

Named after Marburg, Germany, where the disease was first identified in 1967.
