special effect

1. NOUN

/ ˈspɛʃ.əl ɪˈfɛkt / (AmE), / ˈspɛʃ.əl ɪˈfɛkt / (BrE)
an image or sound created artificially in film, television, or theater to represent something that cannot be achieved by standard methods
artificial visual or audio effect in media

Examples

The movie relies heavily on special effects to create its breathtaking action scenes.
Old science fiction films used practical special effects like miniatures and stop motion.
The explosion in the final scene was a special effect designed by a talented team.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: visual effect - an effect created visually, often with computer graphics, in films or TV
