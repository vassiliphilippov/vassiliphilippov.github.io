permanent magnet

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɚmənənt ˈmægnət / (AmE), / ˈpɜːmənənt ˈmægnət / (BrE)
a magnet that retains its magnetic properties after being removed from a magnetic field
magnet that retains magnetism

Examples

A permanent magnet can attract iron objects even after it is taken out of a magnetic field.
The speaker's design uses a large permanent magnet to create clear, powerful sound.
Unlike an electromagnet, a permanent magnet does not require electricity to maintain its magnetism.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: electromagnet - a magnet created by an electric current
