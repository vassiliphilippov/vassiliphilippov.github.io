to elucidate someone's reasoning

1. VERB

/ ɪˈluːsɪˌdeɪt ˈsʌmˌwʌnz ˈriːzənɪŋ / (AmE), / ɪˈluːsɪˌdeɪt ˈsʌmwʌnz ˈriːz(ə)nɪŋ / (BrE)
to make someone's thought process or reasoning clear by explaining it in detail
clarify someone's thought process

Examples

The professor encouraged the student to elucidate his reasoning behind each answer during the oral exam.
Could you elucidate your reasoning for choosing this particular method over others?
In her report, she attempted to elucidate the committee's reasoning for rejecting the proposal.

Synset Examples

The teacher asked him to elucidate his reasoning.
He tried to elucidate the complex theory in simple terms.
Can you elucidate your point for better understanding?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to explain - to make something clear or easy to understand
  • Synonym: to clarify - to make a statement or situation less confused and more comprehensible

Etymology

From Latin 'elucidare' (to make clear), from 'lux' (light).
