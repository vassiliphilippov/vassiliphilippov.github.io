block heel

1. NOUN

/ blɑk hil / (AmE), / blɒk hiːl / (BrE)
A wide, usually square-shaped heel on a shoe, providing stability and comfort and available in various heights.
wide, usually square shoe heel

Examples

She prefers wearing block heels because they are more comfortable than stilettos.
The designer's new collection features sandals with colorful block heels for the summer.
I bought a pair of boots with a chunky block heel for extra support.

Synset Examples

Shoes with block heels will give you a few additional inches while still being fairly comfortable.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: stiletto heel - a long, thin, high heel on women's shoes
