literary hack

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪt̬əˌrɛri hæk / (AmE), / ˈlɪtərəri hæk / (BrE)
A writer who produces mediocre or uninspired work, often for financial gain and with little regard for quality.
a mediocre or uninspired writer

Examples

Many critics dismissed the novelist as a literary hack with no real talent.
Despite being a literary hack, he managed to publish dozens of books each year.
She refused to become a literary hack and instead devoted herself to original, creative writing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hack writer - A writer who produces dull or low-quality work for money
  • Degree Higher: author - A person who writes books or other works

Etymology

From 'hack', meaning a writer who works for hire; 'hack' originally from 'hackney', indicating something hired out cheaply or used up.
