law of equal areas

1. NOUN

/ lɔ əv ˈikwəl ˈɛriəz / (AmE), / lɔː əv ˈiːkwəl ˈeəriəz / (BrE)
Kepler's second law stating that a line joining a planet and the sun sweeps out equal areas during equal intervals of time.
Kepler's second law in planetary motion

Examples

According to the law of equal areas, planets move faster when they are closer to the sun.
Astronomers use the law of equal areas to predict a planet's changing speed along its orbit.
Kepler's law of equal areas was a breakthrough in understanding planetary motion.

Synset Examples

Kepler's second law means that a planet's orbital speed changes with its distance from the sun

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Kepler's second law - Kepler's law describing planetary motion: equal areas are swept in equal times
  • Degree Lower: law of ellipses - Kepler's first law: planets orbit the sun in ellipses
  • Degree Higher: law of harmonies - Kepler's third law: the square of a planet's orbital period relates to the cube of its average distance from the sun

Etymology

From scientific terminology: the law as formulated by Johannes Kepler in the early 17th century.
