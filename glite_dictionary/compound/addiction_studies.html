addiction studies

1. NOUN

/ əˈdɪk.ʃən ˈstʌd.iz / (AmE), / əˈdɪk.ʃən ˈstʌd.iːz / (BrE)
the academic study of addiction and addictive behaviors from multidisciplinary perspectives, including psychology, sociology, and medicine
academic field on addiction

Examples

She decided to pursue a master's degree in addiction studies to better understand substance abuse.
Recent research in addiction studies examines the role of genetics in addictive behaviors.
Universities now offer specialized programs focusing on addiction studies for counselors and healthcare professionals.

Synset Examples

Experiments psychologists Mark Sobell and Linda Sobell provided powerful momentum to the anti-abstinence movement in addiction studies in the 1970s by showing that controlled drinking was not only possible but demonstrated success in recovery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: addiction - the condition of being habitually or compulsively occupied with or involved in something, especially a harmful substance or behavior
