fire escape

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪɚ ɪˌskeɪp / (AmE), / ˈfaɪə ɪˌskeɪp / (BrE)
a staircase or structure, usually on the outside of a building, used for escaping in case of fire or other emergency
emergency escape stairway on a building

Examples

The fire escape was blocked, making it unsafe for residents during the emergency.
She climbed down the fire escape to get away from the burning building.
Many old New York apartments have a fire escape attached to their windows.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: escape route - a way out of a place in case of danger
