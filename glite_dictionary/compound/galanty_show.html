galanty show

1. NOUN

/ ɡəˈlænti ˌʃoʊ / (AmE), / ɡəˈlænti ˌʃəʊ / (BrE)
a dramatic performance in which figures are shown as moving shadows cast on a screen or wall
shadow play performance
archaic

Examples

As children, we often entertained ourselves by staging a galanty show with paper cutouts and candles.
The magician's galanty show filled the walls with fantastic, flickering creatures that seemed to come to life.
Victorian parlors sometimes featured a galanty show as an evening amusement for guests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shadow play - a performance where moving shadows of figures are cast on a screen

Etymology

From Italian *galanti* (gallant or elegant), possibly influenced by French *galant*, referring to the refined, elegant style of such entertainments.
