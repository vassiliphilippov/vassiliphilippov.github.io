sea otter

1. NOUN

/ si ˈɑt̬ɚ / (AmE), / si ˈɒtə / (BrE)
a large marine mammal of the northern Pacific coasts with very thick, dark brown fur, belonging to the species Enhydra lutris
marine mammal with thick fur

Examples

The sea otter is known for using rocks to break open shellfish.
Conservation efforts have helped the sea otter population recover along some Pacific coastlines.
A mother sea otter floats on her back while grooming her pup.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: otter - a semiaquatic mammal with a long body and tail, dense fur, and webbed feet
