clinker brick

1. NOUN

/ ˈklɪŋkɚ brɪk / (AmE), / ˈklɪŋkə brɪk / (BrE)
A very hard, dense brick, often irregular in shape, typically used as a paving stone or in construction for its durability.
very hard brick used for paving

Examples

The pathway was laid with clinker bricks that gave it a rustic charm.
Due to their durability, clinker bricks are often chosen for garden edging and driveways.
He admired the textured surface of the old clinker brick patio.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brick - a rectangular block of clay used for building

Etymology

From 'clinker', referring to bricks that are partly vitrified by excessive heat in the kiln, making them extremely hard.
