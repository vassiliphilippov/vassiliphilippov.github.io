SWOT matrix

1. NOUN

/ ˈswɑt ˌmeɪ.trɪks / (AmE), / ˈswɒt ˌmeɪ.trɪks / (BrE)
a strategic planning tool used to identify and analyze the strengths, weaknesses, opportunities, and threats related to a business or project
business analysis tool for strengths, weaknesses, opportunities, threats

Examples

When starting a new company, it's essential to create a SWOT matrix to assess internal and external factors.
The marketing team presented their SWOT matrix during the quarterly strategy meeting.
By reviewing their SWOT matrix, the managers identified potential risks and areas for improvement.

Synset Examples

All businesses benefit from a SWOT analysis, and all businesses benefit from completing a SWOT analysis of their main competitors, which interestingly can then provide useful points back into the economic aspects of the PEST analysis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: SWOT analysis - analytical process using strengths, weaknesses, opportunities, and threats framework

Etymology

Acronym from the first letters of Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, and Threats; 'matrix' from Latin 'matrix' (womb, source).
