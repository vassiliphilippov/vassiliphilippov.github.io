rock 'n' roll

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɑk ən ˈroʊl / (AmE), / ˌrɒk ən ˈrəʊl / (BrE)
A genre of popular music that originated in the 1950s, characterized by a blend of rhythm and blues and country and western influences, typically with a strong beat and simple chord progressions.
1950s-origin popular music genre

Examples

Elvis Presley is often called the king of rock 'n' roll, thanks to his energetic performances and influence.
They played classic rock 'n' roll tunes that filled the dance floor all night.
The rise of rock 'n' roll in the 1950s transformed popular music for generations.

Synset Examples

rock is a generic term for the range of styles that evolved out of rock'n'roll.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: rock - A broad genre of popular music that developed from rock 'n' roll.

Etymology

From 'rock and roll,' an African American slang phrase, repurposed for the music style in the early 1950s.
