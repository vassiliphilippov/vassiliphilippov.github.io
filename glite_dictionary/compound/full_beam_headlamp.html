full beam headlamp

1. NOUN

/ fʊl bim ˈhɛd.læmp / (AmE), / fʊl biːm ˈhɛd.læmp / (BrE)
A car headlamp providing a bright, focused light with no control to avoid dazzling other drivers, used only when no other vehicles are present on the road.
car headlamp with undirected, bright light

Examples

You should only use the full beam headlamp when there's no one coming toward you on the road.
The driver's full beam headlamp dazzled oncoming vehicles on the narrow country lane.
Remember to switch off the full beam headlamp when approaching another car at night.

Synset Examples

International ECE Regulations permit higher-intensity high-beam headlamps than are allowed under North American regulations.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dipped headlamp - a car headlamp providing a lower, angled beam to avoid dazzling other drivers
  • Synonym: high-beam headlight - another term for a headlamp providing a bright, centre-weighted light
