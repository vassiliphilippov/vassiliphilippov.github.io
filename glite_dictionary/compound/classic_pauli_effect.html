classic Pauli effect

1. NOUN

/ ˈklæs.ɪk ˈpaʊli ɪˈfɛkt / (AmE), / ˈklæs.ɪk ˈpaʊli ɪˈfɛkt / (BrE)
a humorous phenomenon in scientific circles where a person's presence is believed to cause experimental equipment to fail or malfunction
humorous jinxing of lab equipment by presence

Examples

Whenever Dr. Kaufman entered the lab, the classic Pauli effect seemed to strike, and the computers crashed.
The students blamed the experiment's failure on the classic Pauli effect after their professor walked in.
She joked that her bad luck with lab equipment was just the classic Pauli effect in action.

Synset Examples

Every time she entered the lab, the equipment malfunctioned; classic Pauli effect.

Etymology

Named after the physicist Wolfgang Pauli, who was jokingly said to cause apparatus to fail simply by being nearby.
