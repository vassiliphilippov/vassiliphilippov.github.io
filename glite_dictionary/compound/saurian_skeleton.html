saurian skeleton

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɔriən ˈskɛlətən / (AmE), / ˈsɔːrɪən ˈskɛlɪtən / (BrE)
the preserved or fossilized bones forming the skeleton of a reptile or dinosaur
skeleton of a reptile or dinosaur

Examples

The museum displayed an impressive saurian skeleton from the late Jurassic period.
Scientists carefully reconstructed the saurian skeleton found in the remote desert site.
Children gathered around the gigantic saurian skeleton on their school field trip.

Synset Examples

The museum displayed a fascinating saurian skeleton.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fossil - the preserved remains or impression of a prehistoric organism

Etymology

From 'saurian', based on Latin 'saurus' (lizard) from Greek 'sauros', and 'skeleton' from Greek 'skeletos' (dried up, mummy).
