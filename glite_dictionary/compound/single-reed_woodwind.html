single-reed woodwind

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪŋɡəl riːd ˈwʊdˌwɪnd / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋɡl riːd ˈwʊdwɪnd / (BrE)
A woodwind instrument that produces sound with one vibrating reed, such as a clarinet or saxophone.
woodwind using a single reed

Examples

The clarinet is a well-known single-reed woodwind used in orchestras and bands.
She preferred playing single-reed woodwind instruments over brass ones.
Saxophones, as single-reed woodwind instruments, are common in jazz music.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: double-reed woodwind - woodwind instrument using two reeds, such as the oboe or bassoon
