gamboge tree

1. NOUN

/ ɡæmˈboʊdʒ triː / (AmE), / ɡamˈbəʊdʒ triː / (BrE)
A low, spreading tree of Indonesia that produces an orange to brown gum resin (gamboge) used as a pigment.
tree yielding gamboge resin for pigment

Examples

The gamboge tree is prized for its resin, which artists use to create vibrant yellow pigments.
Several gamboge trees grow near the village, their trunks exuding the distinctive orange gum.
Harvesting resin from the gamboge tree is a traditional practice in parts of Indonesia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gamboge - orange to brown gum resin used as a pigment

Etymology

From 'gamboge' (the resin), which comes from Latin 'gambogium', itself from Cambodia, the region where the tree is found.
