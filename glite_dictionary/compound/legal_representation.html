legal representation

1. NOUN

/ ˈliɡəl ˌrɛprɪˌzɛnˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈliːɡəl ˌrɛprɪˈzɛnˈteɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
the provision of professional representation by a lawyer or authorized person in legal matters or proceedings
professional legal advocacy or counsel
legal

Examples

The defendant was granted legal representation by the court to ensure a fair trial.
Without legal representation, many people struggle to understand their rights in complex legal situations.
She sought legal representation after receiving notice of the lawsuit.

Synset Examples

an person who has been declared incompetent should have legal representation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: counsel - lawyer or group of lawyers representing a client in court
  • Degree Lower: defendant - a person accused or sued in a court of law

Etymology

legal from Latin *legalis* (of the law); representation from Latin *repraesentare* (to bring before one).
