business deduction

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɪznəs dɪˈdʌkʃən / (AmE), / ˈbɪznəs dɪˈdʌkʃən / (BrE)
an amount that a business is allowed to subtract from its total income before calculating tax, for expenses incurred in the course of business
tax write-off for business expenses
legal

Examples

The company claimed a business deduction for its travel expenses last year.
Office supplies are a common type of business deduction on tax returns.
Failing to document purchases can result in the denial of a business deduction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tax deduction - an expense subtracted from total income to reduce taxable income
