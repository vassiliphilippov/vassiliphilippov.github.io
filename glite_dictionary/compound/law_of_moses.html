Law of Moses

1. NOUN

/ lɔ əv ˈmoʊzɪz / (AmE), / lɔː əv ˈməʊzɪz / (BrE)
the body of laws given by God to the Israelites through Moses, including the Ten Commandments and other religious and ethical rules found in the Torah or first five books of the Old Testament
biblical laws given through Moses

Examples

The dietary regulations outlined in the Law of Moses are still observed by many Jews today.
According to the Law of Moses, certain rituals were to be performed in the temple.
He studied the history of the Law of Moses to better understand ancient legal traditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Torah - the first five books of the Hebrew Bible

Etymology

From biblical figure Moses, who, according to tradition, received these laws from God; reflects their origin in the Torah.
