inorganic phosphate

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˌɔːrˈɡænɪk ˈfoʊsfeɪt / (AmE), / ɪnˌɔːˈɡænɪk ˈfɒsfeɪt / (BrE)
a chemical compound consisting of a salt of phosphoric acid, usually not bonded to carbon and commonly found in biological and industrial contexts
a salt of phosphoric acid (not bonded to carbon)
medical

Examples

Elevated levels of inorganic phosphate were detected in the patient's blood sample.
Many industrial fertilizers contain inorganic phosphate to promote plant growth.
Researchers measured inorganic phosphate to study kidney function in the laboratory animals.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: organic phosphate - a phosphate group bonded to a carbon atom, as in biological molecules
