family Sarcoptidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli sɑːrˈkɒptɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli sɑːˈkɒptɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of small whitish mites, some of which are parasitic on mammals and birds, including the causative agents of mange and scabies
a family of small parasitic mites
medical

Examples

The family Sarcoptidae includes mites that can infest a variety of mammals.
Veterinarians study members of the family Sarcoptidae to understand and treat mange in animals.
Mites from the family Sarcoptidae are responsible for causing scabies in humans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mite - tiny arthropod belonging to the subclass Acari

Etymology

From New Latin Sarcoptidae, based on the genus Sarcoptes (from Greek sark-, sarx 'flesh' + optēs 'looker, watcher') and -idae (taxonomic family suffix).
