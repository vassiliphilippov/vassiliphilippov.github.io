Karok language

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæɹoʊk ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈkærɒk ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the native language spoken by the Karok people of northwestern California, belonging to the Quoratean language family
language of the Karok people (California)

Examples

Fewer than one hundred people are fluent in the Karok language today.
Efforts are underway to revive and teach the Karok language in local schools.
She is studying the unique grammar of the Karok language as part of her linguistics research.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Karok - a member of a Native American people of northwestern California

Etymology

Karok, from the Karok people's own name (meaning 'upriver people').
