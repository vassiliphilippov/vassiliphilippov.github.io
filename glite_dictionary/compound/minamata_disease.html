Minamata disease

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɪnəˈmɑːtə dɪˈziz / (AmE), / ˌmɪnəˈmɑːtə dɪˈziːz / (BrE)
A severe neurological disorder caused by mercury poisoning, first identified in people who ate contaminated fish from Minamata Bay, Japan, in the 1950s.
mercury poisoning disorder from Minamata, Japan
medical

Examples

Hundreds of people suffered from Minamata disease after consuming fish contaminated with mercury.
The tragic history of Minamata disease raised worldwide awareness about industrial pollution.
Researchers have studied the long-term effects of Minamata disease on neurological health.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mercury poisoning - poisoning caused by exposure to mercury or its compounds

Etymology

Named after Minamata Bay, Japan, where the disease was first identified in the 1950s.
