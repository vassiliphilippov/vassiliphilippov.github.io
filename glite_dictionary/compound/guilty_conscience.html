guilty conscience

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɪlti ˈkɑnʃəns / (AmE), / ˈɡɪlti ˈkɒnʃəns / (BrE)
a feeling of remorse or responsibility resulting from knowing one has done something wrong
remorse from knowing you've done wrong

Examples

She could not sleep because of her guilty conscience after lying to her friend.
His guilty conscience made him confess to the mistake before anyone found out.
The guilty conscience weighed heavily on her mind, affecting her mood all day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: remorse - deep regret or guilt for a wrong committed
  • Antonym: innocent conscience - a clear conscience with no feelings of guilt or wrongdoing
