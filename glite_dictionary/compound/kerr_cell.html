Kerr cell

1. NOUN

/ kɜr sɛl / (AmE), / kɜː sɛl / (BrE)
an optical device consisting of a transparent cell with electrodes, placed between polarizing media, that passes light only when the planes of polarization are parallel, used as a high-speed shutter or for modulating laser beams
optical device for modulating light

Examples

The Kerr cell allowed the scientists to control the passage of light with incredible speed.
A Kerr cell shutter is often used in high-speed photography to freeze motion.
By adjusting the voltage across the Kerr cell, the intensity of the laser beam was precisely modulated.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Pockels cell - an optical device similar to a Kerr cell, but operating on a different principle

Etymology

Named after John Kerr, Scottish physicist (1824–1907), who discovered the Kerr effect.
