Mach number

1. NOUN

/ mɑk ˈnʌmbɚ / (AmE), / mɑːk ˈnʌmbə / (BrE)
the ratio of the speed of a moving object to the speed of sound in the surrounding medium
ratio of object speed to speed of sound

Examples

The aircraft reached a Mach number of 2, meaning it was flying twice the speed of sound.
Engineers calculate the Mach number to determine aerodynamic forces on high-speed jets.
As the Mach number increases, shock waves become more pronounced around the vehicle.

Etymology

Named after Austrian physicist Ernst Mach (1838–1916), who studied supersonic motion.
