yellow fever

1. NOUN

/ ˈjɛloʊ ˈfiːvər / (AmE), / ˈjɛləʊ ˈfiːvə / (BrE)
a serious infectious disease transmitted by mosquitoes, characterized by fever and jaundice, caused by a flavivirus, and occurring mainly in tropical regions
infectious mosquito-borne disease
medical

Examples

The epidemic of yellow fever in the 19th century devastated entire communities.
Vaccines are recommended for travelers to areas where yellow fever is common.
Doctors quickly diagnosed the patient with yellow fever after observing his symptoms.

2. NOUN

/ ˈjɛl.oʊ ˌfi.vɚ / (AmE), / ˈjɛl.əʊ ˌfiː.və / (BrE)
a serious viral disease transmitted by mosquitoes, causing fever and jaundice, and common in parts of Africa and South America
mosquito-borne viral disease
medical

Examples

Many countries require proof of yellow fever vaccination for travelers from at-risk areas.
An outbreak of yellow fever prompted urgent public health measures.
The symptoms of yellow fever include high fever and yellowing of the skin.

Synset Examples

Yellow fever is a serious illness in some regions.
Vaccination against yellow fever is necessary for travelers.
He was diagnosed with yellow fever during his trip.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: malaria - a mosquito-borne infectious disease caused by parasites

Etymology

From the characteristic yellowing (jaundice) of the skin due to the disease; 'fever' from Latin 'febris'.
