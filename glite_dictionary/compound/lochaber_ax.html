Lochaber ax

1. NOUN

/ loʊˈkɑːbər æks / (AmE), / ləʊˈkɑːbə æks / (BrE)
a type of long-handled battle-ax formerly used by Scottish Highlanders
historical Scottish battle-ax

Examples

The Lochaber ax was a formidable weapon in the hands of the Highland warriors.
Museums in Scotland often display the Lochaber ax alongside other traditional arms.
In the battle reenactment, the soldier carried an impressive replica of the Lochaber ax.

Etymology

Named after Lochaber, a region in the Scottish Highlands, where the weapon originated.
