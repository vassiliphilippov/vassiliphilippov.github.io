cypriniform fish

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɪprəˌnɪfɔrm ˈfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˌsɪprɪnɪfɔːm ˈfɪʃ / (BrE)
A soft-finned fish belonging to the order Cypriniformes, which includes carps, minnows, and their relatives.
soft-finned fish of order Cypriniformes

Examples

The lake is known for its diverse population of cypriniform fish, including several species of carp.
Researchers studied how pollution affects the behavior of local cypriniform fish.
Many cypriniform fish are popular in aquarium hobbies due to their vibrant colors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carp - a large freshwater fish from the family Cyprinidae
  • Synonym: minnow - a small freshwater fish of the family Cyprinidae

Etymology

From Latin cyprinus (carp) + -form (having the form of).
