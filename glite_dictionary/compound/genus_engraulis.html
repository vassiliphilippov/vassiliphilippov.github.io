genus Engraulis

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ɛnˈɡrɔːlɪs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ɛŋˈɡrɔːlɪs / (BrE)
the genus of small, schooling fish commonly known as anchovies, which is the type genus of the family Engraulidae
genus of anchovy fish

Examples

The genus Engraulis includes several species of anchovies found in both the Atlantic and Pacific Oceans.
Scientists studied the migration habits of fish within the genus Engraulis.
Commercial fishing often targets species belonging to the genus Engraulis due to their economic value.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anchovy - a small, oily fish in the family Engraulidae

Etymology

From Latin 'Engraulis', used in scientific classification for anchovies.
