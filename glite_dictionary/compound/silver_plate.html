silver plate

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪlvɚ pleɪt / (AmE), / ˈsɪlvə pleɪt / (BrE)
a thin layer of silver applied to the surface of another material, usually for decorative or protective purposes
thin layer of silver on another material

Examples

The antique teapot was covered in a delicate silver plate that has now begun to wear away.
Many affordable cutlery sets use silver plate instead of solid silver.
Polishing removes tarnish but can slowly thin the silver plate on decorative items.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silver plating - the process of coating an object with a thin layer of silver

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɪlvɚ ˌpleɪt / (AmE), / ˈsɪlvə ˌpleɪt / (BrE)
tableware made of a base metal coated with a thin layer of silver, usually by electroplating, used for dining and serving.
tableware coated with a layer of silver

Examples

The antique silver plate was carefully polished before the dinner party.
They prefer to use silver plate rather than solid silver for everyday meals.
He inherited a set of ornate Victorian silver plate from his grandmother.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silverware - utensils or objects made of or plated with silver, especially for table use
  • Degree Higher: solid silver - tableware or objects made entirely of silver
