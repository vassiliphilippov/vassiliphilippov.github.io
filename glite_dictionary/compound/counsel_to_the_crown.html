Counsel to the Crown

1. NOUN

/ ˈkaʊn.səl tə ðə kraʊn / (AmE), / ˈkaʊn.səl tə ðə kraʊn / (BrE)
a barrister appointed to serve as legal counsel to the British monarch
barrister advising the British monarch
legal british

Examples

After years of dedicated work, she was appointed as Counsel to the Crown in 2010.
The role of Counsel to the Crown carries significant prestige within the legal community.
Only the most experienced barristers are considered for Counsel to the Crown positions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Queen's Counsel - a senior barrister appointed on the recommendation of the British monarch's counsel
  • Synonym: barrister - a lawyer entitled to practice as an advocate, particularly in the higher courts
