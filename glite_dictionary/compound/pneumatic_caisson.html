pneumatic caisson

1. NOUN

/ nuˈmæt̬ɪk ˈkeɪsən / (AmE), / njuːˈmatɪk ˈkeɪsən / (BrE)
a large, watertight chamber filled with compressed air, used in underwater construction to keep water and mud out while work is done
watertight underwater chamber used in construction

Examples

The workers entered the pneumatic caisson to begin building the bridge's underwater foundation.
A pneumatic caisson was used to construct the tunnel beneath the river safely.
Engineers monitored air pressure carefully inside the pneumatic caisson during construction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caisson - a watertight chamber used in construction work under water or as a foundation

Etymology

From pneumatic, meaning 'operated by air or gas under pressure,' and caisson, from French 'caisson' (large chest or box).
