real walloper

1. NOUN

/ ˈriːəl ˈwɑːləpər / (AmE), / ˈrɪəl ˈwɒləpə / (BrE)
An informal term for a person who is highly effective or impressive in a particular area or situation.
very effective or impressive person (informal)
informal

Examples

Everyone in the office knows Susan is a real walloper when it comes to closing tough sales.
That new chef is a real walloper in the kitchen—her dishes are always outstanding.
If you need results fast, hire Tom—he’s a real walloper at solving complex problems.

Synset Examples

He’s a real walloper when it comes to negotiating deals.

Etymology

From 'real' (meaning very or genuinely) and 'walloper' (slang for someone who hits hard or makes a strong impact).
