money supply

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌni səˌplaɪ / (AmE), / ˈmʌni səˌplaɪ / (BrE)
the total amount of money circulating in an economy, including cash and money held in bank accounts
total amount of money in an economy

Examples

Central banks often adjust the money supply to influence inflation and economic growth.
An increase in the money supply can lead to higher consumer spending.
Economists closely watch changes in the money supply to understand market trends.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: money stock - the total supply of money available in an economy at a particular time
