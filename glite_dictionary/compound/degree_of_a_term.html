degree of a term

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈɡri əv ə tɜrm / (AmE), / dɪˈɡriː əv ə tɜːm / (BrE)
in mathematics, the sum of the exponents of the variables in a single term of an expression or polynomial
sum of exponents in a math term

Examples

In the expression 3x²y, the degree of a term is three, because x is squared and y is to the first power.
You determine the degree of a term by adding up all the exponents on its variables.
For the term -5abc², the degree of a term is four, since a and b have exponent one and c has exponent two.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: degree of a polynomial - the highest degree among the terms of a polynomial
