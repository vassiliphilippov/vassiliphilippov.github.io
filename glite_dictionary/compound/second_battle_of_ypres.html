Second Battle of Ypres

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛkənd ˈbætəl əv ˈiːprə / (AmE), / ˈsɛkənd ˈbætl əv ˈiːprə / (BrE)
a major battle during World War I in 1915, in which German forces first used chlorine gas as a weapon, gaining territory from the Allies near Ypres, Belgium.
World War I battle where poison gas was first used

Examples

The Second Battle of Ypres marked the first large-scale use of chemical weapons in warfare.
Many soldiers suffered severe injuries during the Second Battle of Ypres due to chlorine gas attacks.
The Second Battle of Ypres was a turning point in the use of modern weaponry on the Western Front.
