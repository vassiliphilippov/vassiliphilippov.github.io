champion lode

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃæm.pi.ən loʊd / (AmE), / ˈtʃæm.pi.ən ləʊd / (BrE)
the principal vein of ore in a mineral deposit, especially the main productive vein in a mine.
main ore vein in a deposit

Examples

The miners struck the champion lode after months of fruitless digging.
Geologists surveyed the site to determine whether a champion lode was present beneath the surface.
Throughout the region's history, the discovery of a champion lode often led to the rapid growth of mining towns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mother lode - the principal vein or zone of veins of gold or silver ore
