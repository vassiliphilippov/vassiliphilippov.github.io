Commonwealth Day

1. PROPN

/ ˈkɑm.ənˌwɛlθ deɪ / (AmE), / ˈkɒm.ənˌwɛlθ deɪ / (BrE)
An annual celebration observed by members of the Commonwealth of Nations, originally marking the anniversary of Queen Victoria's birth but now serving to celebrate the unity of the Commonwealth.
annual celebration of the Commonwealth of Nations
british

Examples

Schools across the United Kingdom hold special assemblies to mark Commonwealth Day every March.
On Commonwealth Day, the Queen delivers a message to all the nations in the Commonwealth.
Commonwealth Day is an opportunity to reflect on the shared values of the member countries.

Etymology

Named after the Commonwealth of Nations and originally aligned with Queen Victoria's birthday.
