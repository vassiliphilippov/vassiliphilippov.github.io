jury box

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɜːri ˌbɑks / (AmE), / ˈdʒʊəri ˌbɒks / (BrE)
an enclosure in a courtroom where the jury sits during a trial
courtroom enclosure for jury

Examples

The judge instructed the members of the jury box before the trial began.
As the lawyer spoke, all eyes in the jury box were fixed on her.
After the recess, the jurors quietly returned to the jury box.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jury - a group of people sworn to deliver a verdict in a legal case
