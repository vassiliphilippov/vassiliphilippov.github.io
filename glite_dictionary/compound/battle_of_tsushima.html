Battle of Tsushima

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæt̬l əv t͡suːˈʃiːmə / (AmE), / ˈbætl əv tsʊˈʃiːmə / (BrE)
a decisive naval battle fought in 1905 during the Russo-Japanese War, in which the Japanese fleet defeated the Russian fleet in the Korean Strait
naval battle in the 1905 Russo-Japanese War

Examples

The Battle of Tsushima marked a turning point in naval warfare between Japan and Russia.
Historians often cite the Battle of Tsushima as the moment Japan emerged as a major world power.
Many Russian ships were sunk during the Battle of Tsushima, leading to a decisive Japanese victory.

Etymology

Named after Tsushima Strait, off the coast of Japan, where the battle took place.
