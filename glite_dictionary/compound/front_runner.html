front runner

1. NOUN

/ frʌnt ˈrʌnɚ / (AmE), / frʌnt ˈrʌnə / (BrE)
the person or thing that is currently leading in a race, competition, or contest
leading competitor or candidate

Examples

She emerged as the front runner after winning three consecutive matches.
Many analysts predict he will remain the front runner in the presidential race.
Despite injuries, the team stayed the clear front runner throughout the season.

Synset Examples

The team’s star player is the front runner for MVP.
She is the front runner for the scholarship.
As a front runner, he has the best chance to win.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contender - person competing to win something
  • Antonym: underdog - competitor thought unlikely to win
