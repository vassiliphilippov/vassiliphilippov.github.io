family Coccinellidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ˌkɑːksɪˈnɛlɪˌdi / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli ˌkɒksɪˈnɛlɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of small beetles commonly known as ladybugs or ladybird beetles.
the family of ladybugs

Examples

The family Coccinellidae includes over 5,000 species of beetles commonly known as ladybugs.
Scientists studied members of the family Coccinellidae to learn more about their role in pest control.
Ladybird beetles, part of the family Coccinellidae, are popular among gardeners for their ability to eat aphids.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ladybug - a small round beetle, often brightly colored, belonging to the family Coccinellidae

Etymology

From New Latin: genus name Coccinella, from Latin *coccinus* (scarlet-colored), referring to the typical coloring of many species, plus -idae (family suffix).
