rock solid

1. ADJ

/ rɑk ˈsɑlɪd / (AmE), / rɒk ˈsɒlɪd / (BrE)
extremely strong, reliable, or trustworthy; not likely to fail or weaken
extremely strong or reliable

Examples

Her trust in him remained rock solid despite the rumors.
They have a rock solid investment strategy that has paid off for years.
The evidence against the suspect was rock solid, making conviction almost certain.

Synset Examples

His alibi was rock solid during the investigation.

She has a rock solid reputation in her field.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: firm - strong and steady; not likely to move or change
  • Antonym: fragile - easily broken or damaged; not strong
