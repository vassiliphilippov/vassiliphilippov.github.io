paper profit

1. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪpɚ ˈprɑːfɪt / (AmE), / ˈpeɪpə ˈprɒfɪt / (BrE)
an increase in the value of an investment that has not yet been realized through a sale; an unrealized gain reflected only on paper
unrealized gain on an investment

Examples

The company reported a large paper profit as its stock price soared, but no actual cash was received.
Although she holds a significant paper profit in her shares, she hasn't sold them yet.
A fall in the market could quickly turn a paper profit into a loss.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unrealized gain - a gain not yet secured by a transaction
  • Antonym: paper loss - an unrealized loss on an investment
