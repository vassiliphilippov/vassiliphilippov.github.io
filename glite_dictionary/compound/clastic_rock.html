clastic rock

1. NOUN

/ ˈklæs.tɪk rɑk / (AmE), / ˈklæs.tɪk rɒk / (BrE)
A type of rock formed from fragments or grains of pre-existing rocks that have been transported, deposited, and cemented together.
rock made of fragments of older rocks

Examples

Sandstone is a common type of clastic rock formed from compacted sand grains.
Geologists study clastic rocks to understand sedimentary processes and the history of Earth's surface.
A clastic rock can contain fragments of many different minerals or older rocks.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: sedimentary rock - rock formed by the accumulation of mineral or organic particles
  • Synonym: conglomerate - a coarse-grained clastic sedimentary rock

Etymology

From Greek klastos (broken) and rock; used in geological sciences.
