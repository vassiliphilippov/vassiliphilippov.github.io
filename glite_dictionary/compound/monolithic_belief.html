monolithic belief

1. NOUN

/ mɑːnəˈlɪθɪk bəˈliːf / (AmE), / mɒnəˈlɪθɪk bɪˈliːf / (BrE)
A belief system or viewpoint that is rigid, uniform, and resistant to change or differing perspectives.
rigid and uniform belief

Examples

Her monolithic belief in tradition made her resistant to any suggestions for reform.
Many organizations discourage monolithic beliefs in favor of diverse perspectives.
It is dangerous when a society adopts a monolithic belief that ignores all dissent.

Synset Examples

His monolithic beliefs left no room for compromise.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dogma - a principle or set of principles laid down by an authority as incontrovertibly true
  • Antonym: open-mindedness - willingness to consider new and different ideas or opinions

Etymology

From 'monolithic', from Greek 'monolithos' (single stone) + 'belief'; the phrase likens beliefs to being as unyielding and indivisible as a single stone.
