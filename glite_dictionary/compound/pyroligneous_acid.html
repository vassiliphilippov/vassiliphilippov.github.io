pyroligneous acid

1. NOUN

/ ˌpaɪrəˈlɪdʒniəs ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / ˌpɪrəˈlɪdʒnɪəs ˈasɪd / (BrE)
a reddish-brown liquid obtained by the destructive distillation of wood, containing acetic acid, methanol, acetone, wood oils, and tars
liquid from wood distillation

Examples

Historically, pyroligneous acid was used as a source of acetic acid in industry.
After the distillation process, pyroligneous acid is collected for further chemical separation.
The smoky taste in some foods comes from flavoring with pyroligneous acid.

Etymology

From Greek pyro- (fire) + Latin ligneus (woody), referring to its origin from wood by fire.
