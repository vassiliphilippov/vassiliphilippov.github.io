monoamine oxidase

1. NOUN

/ ˌmoʊnoʊˈæmin ˈɑksɪˌdeɪs / (AmE), / ˌmɒnəʊˈæmiːn ˈɒksɪdeɪz / (BrE)
an enzyme in the body that breaks down monoamines, such as serotonin, norepinephrine, and dopamine, by catalyzing their oxidation
an enzyme that breaks down monoamines
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that monoamine oxidase helps regulate levels of neurotransmitters in the brain.
Some antidepressants work by inhibiting monoamine oxidase to increase the availability of certain chemicals.
Research has shown genetic differences in monoamine oxidase activity among individuals.

Etymology

From monoamine (mono- + amine) and oxidase (from Latin 'oxy-' + 'ase', enzyme suffix).
