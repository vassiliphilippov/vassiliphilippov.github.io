round clam

1. NOUN

/ raʊnd klæm / (AmE), / raʊnd klam / (BrE)
A large Atlantic coast clam with a hard, rounded shell, often used in chowders or other clam dishes.
large Atlantic coast clam with hard shell

Examples

The chef added chopped round clam to the seafood stew for extra flavor.
Fishermen along the Atlantic coast often harvest round clams for commercial use.
A hearty bowl of chowder usually features tender pieces of round clam.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quahog - another term for round clam, particularly in the US Northeast

2. NOUN

/ raʊnd klæm / (AmE), / raʊnd klam / (BrE)
An edible American clam with a thick, heavy shell, once used as money by some Native American peoples.
an edible American clam with heavy shell

Examples

Fishermen collected dozens of round clams along the New England coast.
The shells of the round clam were once used as wampum by Native Americans.
We made a delicious chowder with fresh round clams from the market.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quahog - an edible American clam also known as the hard clam, commonly used in chowders
