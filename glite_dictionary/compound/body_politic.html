body politic

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑːdi pəˈlɪtɪk / (AmE), / ˈbɒdi pəˈlɪtɪk / (BrE)
an organized group of people considered as a single entity governed by a common authority, especially a state or nation
organized society under one government

Examples

The constitution was created to protect the rights and liberties of the body politic.
Many believe that the health of the body politic depends on an informed and active citizenry.
When faced with a crisis, a united body politic can overcome tremendous challenges.

Synset Examples

the state has elected a new president
African nations
students who had come to the nation's capitol
the country's largest manufacturer
an industrialized land

Related Terms

  • Synonym: state - an organized political community under one government
  • Synonym: nation - a large body of people united by common descent, history, culture, or language, inhabiting a particular state or territory

Etymology

From Middle English, translating Latin corpus politicum, meaning 'political body.'

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɑdi pɑˈlɪtɪk / (AmE), / ˈbɒdi pɒˈlɪtɪk / (BrE)
The people of a nation considered collectively as an organized group under a single government; the political organization of a society or nation.
the collective citizens of a nation

Examples

Debate over taxation policy affects the entire body politic and not just special interest groups.
Corruption threatens the health of the body politic in any democracy.
Social movements can lead to dramatic shifts within the body politic.

Synset Examples

Every citizen is a part of the body politic.
The body politic is influenced by public opinion.
Understanding the body politic is essential for voters.

Etymology

From Middle English, translation of Medieval Latin corpus politicum (political body).
