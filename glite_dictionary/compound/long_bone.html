long bone

1. NOUN

/ lɔŋ boʊn / (AmE), / lɒŋ bəʊn / (BrE)
A bone in the limbs of vertebrate animals that is long, cylindrical in shape, and contains marrow, such as the femur or humerus.
type of cylindrical limb bone
medical

Examples

The femur is the largest long bone in the human body.
Damage to a long bone often requires careful medical treatment to ensure proper healing.
Growth occurs at the ends of a long bone during childhood and adolescence.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: short bone - a bone that is as wide as it is long, found typically in wrists and ankles
  • Antonym: flat bone - a bone that is thin and flattened, such as those in the skull
