podocarpus family

1. NOUN

/ ˌpoʊdoʊˈkɑrpəs ˈfæməli / (AmE), / ˌpəʊdəʊˈkɑːpəs ˈfæmɪli / (BrE)
A family of evergreen gymnosperm trees and shrubs with simple, persistent needlelike or scalelike leaves, known scientifically as Podocarpaceae.
family of evergreen gymnosperms (Podocarpaceae)

Examples

The podocarpus family includes many evergreen trees found in southern hemisphere forests.
Members of the podocarpus family are often used as ornamental plants in landscaping.
Scientists study the podocarpus family to understand gymnosperm evolution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Podocarpaceae - the scientific (Latin) name for the podocarpus family

Etymology

From scientific Latin Podocarpaceae, after Podocarpus, the type genus, from Greek podos (foot) and karpos (fruit).
