ring containment

1. NOUN

/ rɪŋ kənˈteɪnmənt / (AmE), / rɪŋ kənˈteɪnmənt / (BrE)
a method of controlling infectious disease outbreaks by vaccinating only those who have been exposed and their immediate contacts
disease control by targeted vaccination
medical

Examples

Ring containment was credited with stopping several smallpox outbreaks in the 20th century.
The public health team quickly implemented ring containment after the case of suspected exposure was discovered.
Modern strategies against bioterrorism often rely on ring containment to prevent widespread infection.

Synset Examples

ring containment is a proven method of halting a smallpox epidemic
