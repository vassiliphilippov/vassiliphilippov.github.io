true flapper

1. NOUN

/ tru ˈflæpər / (AmE), / truː ˈflæpə / (BrE)
A genuinely fashionable young woman in the 1920s, embodying the typical flapper style and attitude.
genuinely fashionable young woman of the 1920s

Examples

She wore a beaded dress and danced like a true flapper at the party.
In her photos, my grandmother looked every bit the true flapper of the Roaring Twenties.
Aspiring to be a true flapper, she cut her hair short and wore bold makeup.

Synset Examples

She wore a stunning dress like a true flapper.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flapper - a fashionable young woman, especially in the 1920s

Etymology

From 'flapper,' an early 20th-century term for a fashionable young woman, especially in the 1920s.
