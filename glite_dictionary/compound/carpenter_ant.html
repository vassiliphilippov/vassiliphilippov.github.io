carpenter ant

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːrpəntər ænt / (AmE), / ˈkɑːpəntə ænt / (BrE)
a large ant that makes nests by boring tunnels in decaying wood, where it lays its eggs
ant that bores tunnels in wood

Examples

We found a carpenter ant nest in the rotting fence post.
A carpenter ant infestation can damage wooden structures over time.
Unlike termites, the carpenter ant does not eat wood but excavates it for nesting.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: ant - a small insect in the family Formicidae, typically living in colonies
