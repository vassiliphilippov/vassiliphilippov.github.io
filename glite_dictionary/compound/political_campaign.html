political campaign

1. NOUN

/ pəˈlɪt̬ɪkəl kæmˈpeɪn / (AmE), / pəˈlɪtɪkəl kæmˈpeɪn / (BrE)
an organized effort by a candidate or political party to win an election for public office
organized effort to win an election

Examples

The political campaign for mayor attracted national attention due to its intensity.
She volunteered for a political campaign during the last election cycle.
Television advertisements played a crucial role in the governor's political campaign.

Synset Examples

I managed his campaign for governor
he is raising money for a Senate run

Related Terms

  • Synonym: election - a formal process of choosing representatives
  • Synonym: political race - competition between candidates for office

2. NOUN

/ pəˈlɪdɪkəl kæmˈpeɪn / (AmE), / pəˈlɪtɪkəl kæmˈpeɪn / (BrE)
an organized effort by a candidate or political party to win an election
effort to win an election

Examples

The senator launched his political campaign with a rally in the city square.
Media coverage can greatly influence the outcome of a political campaign.
Social media now plays a crucial role in every major political campaign.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: election campaign - a campaign specifically to influence voters in an election
  • Degree Higher: political movement - an organized effort to achieve political goals, broader than a campaign
