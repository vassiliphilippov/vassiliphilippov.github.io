innominate vein

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈnɑmɪnət veɪn / (AmE), / ɪˈnɒmɪnət veɪn / (BrE)
either of the two large veins in the chest formed by the union of the internal jugular and subclavian veins, which join to form the superior vena cava
large vein formed by jugular and subclavian veins
medical

Examples

The surgeon carefully exposed the innominate vein during the procedure.
A blockage in the innominate vein can lead to swelling of the neck and arm.
Radiologists look for abnormalities in the innominate vein on chest CT scans.

Etymology

From Latin 'innominatus' meaning 'unnamed', as it was not given a specific name in early anatomy.
