Chateau Migraine

1. NOUN

/ ʃæˌtoʊ maɪˈɡreɪn / (AmE), / ʃæˌtəʊ mɪˈɡreɪn / (BrE)
a humorous term for a severe headache or bad hangover, especially one caused by drinking too much alcohol
humorous term for a severe headache or hangover
informal

Examples

After three glasses of cheap wine, I woke up with a Chateau Migraine the next morning.
The party was fun, but my Chateau Migraine the day after was not worth it.
He joked that the wine was so bad it would guarantee a full-blown Chateau Migraine by morning.

Synset Examples

After the party, my head is a total Chateau Migraine.
She described her hangover as a true Chateau Migraine.
Too much noise in here feels like a Chateau Migraine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hangover - unpleasant physical effects after drinking too much alcohol
  • Synonym: migraine - a severe recurring headache

Etymology

From French 'château' (castle, often used on wine labels) and 'migraine.' Used jokingly to suggest bad wine causes a headache.
