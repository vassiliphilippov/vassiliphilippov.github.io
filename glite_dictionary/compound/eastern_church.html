Eastern Church

1. NOUN

/ ˈiːstərn tʃɜːrtʃ / (AmE), / ˈiːstən tʃɜːtʃ / (BrE)
The Christian churches which developed from the Byzantine Church and follow its rites and traditions, especially the Eastern Orthodox Church.
Christian churches derived from the Byzantine tradition

Examples

The Eastern Church celebrates many of its major feasts on different dates than the Western Church.
The influence of Byzantine art can still be seen in the traditions of the Eastern Church.
She studied the liturgical music of the Eastern Church for her thesis.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Western Church - The Christian church of Western Europe, especially the Roman Catholic Church

2. NOUN

/ ˈistərn tʃɝːtʃ / (AmE), / ˈiːstən tʃɜːtʃ / (BrE)
the Christian church as it existed in the Byzantine Empire, especially before the division from the Western Church; now commonly referring to the Eastern Orthodox Church
Byzantine Christian church; precursor to Eastern Orthodoxy

Examples

The influence of the Eastern Church can still be seen in modern Orthodox traditions.
After the schism, the Eastern Church and Western Church developed distinct liturgies.
Art from the Eastern Church featured elaborate mosaics and iconic imagery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Eastern Orthodox Church - branch of Christianity arising from the Byzantine tradition
  • Antonym: Western Church - the Christian church of Western Europe, especially after the split with the East
