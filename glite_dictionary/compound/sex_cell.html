sex cell

1. NOUN

/ sɛks sɛl / (AmE), / sɛks sɛl / (BrE)
A cell involved in sexual reproduction, such as a sperm or egg, responsible for transmitting genetic material to the next generation.
cell involved in sexual reproduction

Examples

The human body produces sex cells in the reproductive organs.
Fertilization occurs when two sex cells unite to form a new organism.
Mutations in sex cells can be passed on to offspring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gamete - a reproductive cell (sperm or egg) that unites with another to form a new organism
