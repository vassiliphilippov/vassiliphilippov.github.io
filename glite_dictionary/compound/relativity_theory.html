relativity theory

1. NOUN

/ rɛˈlæt̬.əˌvɪ.ti ˈθi.ə.ri / (AmE), / rɪˈlæt.ɪ.və.ti ˈθɪə.ri / (BrE)
a scientific theory in physics stating that space and time are relative concepts, not absolute, most notably developed by Albert Einstein
a physics theory about relativity of space and time

Examples

Einstein's relativity theory revolutionized modern physics in the early twentieth century.
Many experiments have confirmed the predictions made by relativity theory.
Students found the mathematical aspects of relativity theory quite challenging at first.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: special relativity - the theory dealing with inertial frames and the speed of light
  • Degree Higher: general relativity - the theory dealing with gravitation and curved spacetime
