Allegheny plum

1. NOUN

/ ˌælɪˈɡeɪni plʌm / (AmE), / ˌælɪˈɡeɪni plʌm / (BrE)
a wild plum tree native to the northeastern United States, producing dark purple fruits with yellow flesh
wild plum tree of northeastern U.S.

Examples

The Allegheny plum is rarely found outside its native region due to its limited habitat requirements.
Botanists are working to conserve the Allegheny plum, which is considered increasingly rare in the wild.
The fruit of the Allegheny plum is dark purple on the outside with a sweet yellow flesh inside.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plum - a sweet, edible stone fruit

Etymology

Named after the Allegheny region in the United States.
