high acidity

1. NOUN

/ haɪ əˈsɪdəti / (AmE), / haɪ əˈsɪdəti / (BrE)
a condition in which a substance, especially soil or food, has a low pH level or a high concentration of acid
state of having a high acid level

Examples

The oranges from this region are known for their high acidity and tangy flavor.
High acidity in wine can create a crisp, refreshing taste.
Some plants struggle to grow in areas with high acidity in the soil.

Synset Examples

High acidity in the soil can affect plant growth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acidity - the quality of being acid; the amount of acid in a substance
