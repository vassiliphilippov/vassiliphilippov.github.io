Tweedledee and Tweedledum

1. NOUN

/ ˌtwidlˈdi ən ˌtwidlˈdʌm / (AmE), / ˌtwiːdlˈdiː ən ˌtwiːdlˈdʌm / (BrE)
two people who are very similar in appearance or behavior and are hard to tell apart
two people who are hard to tell apart

Examples

The two candidates were like Tweedledee and Tweedledum, offering almost identical policies.
My twin nephews are Tweedledee and Tweedledum; even their parents can barely tell them apart.
In their matching outfits, the sisters resembled Tweedledee and Tweedledum at the party.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lookalike - someone or something that looks very similar to another

Etymology

From the names of two identical characters in Lewis Carroll's 'Through the Looking-Glass', originally from a nursery rhyme.
