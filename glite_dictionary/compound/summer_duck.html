summer duck

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌmɚ dʌk / (AmE), / ˈsʌmə dʌk / (BrE)
A showy North American duck (the wood duck, Aix sponsa) that nests in hollow trees, noted for its colorful plumage.
a showy North American duck species

Examples

A pair of summer ducks made their nest in the old oak tree near the river.
The male summer duck is known for its striking, colorful feathers.
We occasionally spotted a summer duck gliding across the calm waters in spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wood duck - North American duck species with colorful plumage, Aix sponsa
