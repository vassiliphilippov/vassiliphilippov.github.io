phobic disorder

1. NOUN

/ ˈfoʊbɪk dɪsˈɔrdər / (AmE), / ˈfəʊbɪk dɪsˈɔːdə / (BrE)
A mental health condition involving an extreme and irrational fear of specific objects, situations, or social interactions.
an anxiety disorder marked by irrational fears
medical

Examples

The psychologist diagnosed her with a phobic disorder after learning about her severe fear of elevators.
Children with a phobic disorder may avoid school for fear of social situations.
Treatment for a phobic disorder often includes therapy and gradual exposure to the source of fear.

Synset Examples

phobic disorder is a general term for all phobias

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phobia - an extreme or irrational fear of or aversion to something
  • Synonym: anxiety disorder - a category of mental disorders marked by significant feelings of anxiety and fear

Etymology

From Greek phobos (fear) and English disorder; used in psychiatry since the 20th century.
