genus Ruscus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈrʌs.kəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈrʌs.kəs / (BrE)
A genus of European evergreen shrubs, sometimes classified in the family Asparagaceae, known for its small, tough leaves and red berries.
genus of European evergreen shrubs

Examples

The genus Ruscus is well known for species commonly called butcher's broom.
Botanists debated whether the genus Ruscus should be placed in the family Asparagaceae.
Several members of the genus Ruscus are cultivated for their ornamental foliage and berries.

Etymology

From Latin 'Ruscus', a plant name in classical Latin.
