electron gun

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈlɛktrɑn ɡʌn / (AmE), / ɪˈlɛktrɒn ɡʌn / (BrE)
a device in a cathode-ray tube or electron microscope that emits and focuses a stream of electrons
device emitting and focusing electrons

Examples

The television's display relies on an electron gun to produce its image.
Scientists adjusted the electron gun to focus the electron beam more precisely.
In electron microscopes, the electron gun is crucial for generating the necessary high-energy beam.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cathode ray - a stream of electrons emitted from the cathode in a vacuum tube

Etymology

From 'electron' (subatomic particle) + 'gun' (device that fires a stream).
