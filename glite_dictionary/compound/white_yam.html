white yam

1. NOUN

/ waɪt jæm / (AmE), / waɪt jam / (BrE)
A type of yam grown in Australasia and Polynesia, known for its large root with fine, edible white flesh.
yam with large edible white flesh root

Examples

The farmer harvested several white yam roots from his field after the rainy season.
In Polynesian cuisine, white yam is often used in traditional dishes for its smooth texture.
She boiled slices of white yam as a starchy side dish for dinner.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yam - an edible starchy tuber from various climbing plants
