sulfur mustard

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌlfɚ ˈmʌstɚd / (AmE), / ˈsʌlfə ˈmʌstəd / (BrE)
a toxic chemical compound used as a warfare agent, causing severe blisters and damage to the eyes and lungs; there is no known antidote
toxic blistering chemical warfare agent

Examples

The army was trained to recognize the distinct smell of sulfur mustard during chemical warfare drills.
Exposure to sulfur mustard can result in severe blistering and long-term respiratory problems.
Despite international bans, some reports suggest that sulfur mustard has been used in recent conflicts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mustard gas - blistering chemical warfare agent

Etymology

From 'sulfur' (element) and 'mustard' (referring to its mustard-like smell).
