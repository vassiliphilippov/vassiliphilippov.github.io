sewer water

1. NOUN

/ ˈsuːər ˌwɔtər / (AmE), / ˈsjuːə ˌwɔːtə / (BrE)
water that is mixed with waste matter, typically found in or coming from a sewer system
water mixed with waste in sewers

Examples

The city was concerned about sewer water leaking into the river after heavy rains.
Exposure to sewer water can lead to serious health problems due to harmful bacteria.
Engineers tested the sewer water for contaminants before repairing the broken pipes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sewage - waste water and excrement conveyed in sewers
