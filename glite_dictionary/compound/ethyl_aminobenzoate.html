ethyl aminobenzoate

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛθɪl əˌmaɪnoʊ bɛnˈzoʊeɪt / (AmE), / ˈɛθɪl əˌmiːnəʊ bɛnˈzəʊeɪt / (BrE)
A white crystalline chemical compound used as a local anesthetic.
chemical used as local anesthetic
medical

Examples

The dentist applied ethyl aminobenzoate to numb the patient's gums before the procedure.
Researchers studied the effectiveness of ethyl aminobenzoate as a topical anesthetic.
Allergic reactions to ethyl aminobenzoate are rare but can occur in sensitive individuals.

Etymology

From 'ethyl' (the ethyl group), 'amino' (containing an amino group), and 'benzoate' (derived from benzoic acid).
