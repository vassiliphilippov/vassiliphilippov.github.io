magnetic resonance imaging

1. NOUN

/ mæɡˌnɛtɪk rɪˈzɒnəns ˈɪmɪdʒɪŋ / (AmE), / mæɡˌnɛtɪk rɪˈzɒnəns ˈɪmɪdʒɪŋ / (BrE)
a medical imaging technique that uses the nuclear magnetic resonance of atoms within the body to produce detailed images of organs and tissues
medical imaging technique using magnetic fields
medical

Examples

The doctor recommended a magnetic resonance imaging scan to get a clearer view of the patient's brain.
Recent advances in magnetic resonance imaging have improved the detection of tumors.
She was nervous before her magnetic resonance imaging appointment, but the test was quick and painless.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: MRI - abbreviation of 'magnetic resonance imaging'
  • Degree Lower: computed tomography - medical imaging technique using X-rays for cross-sectional images
  • Degree Higher: positron emission tomography - medical imaging technique using positron-emitting radiotracers

Etymology

From 'magnetic', 'resonance', and 'imaging'; the term refers to producing images using nuclear magnetic resonance technology.
