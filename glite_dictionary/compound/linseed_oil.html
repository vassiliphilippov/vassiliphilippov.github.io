linseed oil

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪnˌsid ˌɔɪl / (AmE), / ˈlɪnsiːd ˌɔɪl / (BrE)
a yellowish oil obtained from the seeds of the flax plant, used especially in making paints, varnishes, and linoleum
oil from flax seeds used in paints

Examples

The artist mixes his pigments with linseed oil to achieve a glossy finish.
Traditional woodworkers often treat furniture with linseed oil to protect the surface.
She bought a bottle of linseed oil at the hardware store for her painting project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flaxseed oil - oil extracted from flax seeds, used for consumption and industry
  • Degree Higher: drying oil - oil that hardens to a tough film after exposure to air

Etymology

From 'linseed' (flax seed), ultimately from Latin 'linum' (flax) + 'seed'.
