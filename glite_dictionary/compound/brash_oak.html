brash oak

1. NOUN

/ bræʃ oʊk / (AmE), / bræʃ əʊk / (BrE)
A small deciduous tree native to eastern and central United States, known for its dark green, deeply lobed leaves and tough, moisture-resistant wood often used for fence posts.
a small, tough-wooded US oak tree

Examples

Local farmers often use brash oak wood for durable fence posts due to its moisture resistance.
The leaves of the brash oak are shaped distinctively, making the tree easy to identify in the wild.
After the storm, they found several brash oak saplings uprooted near the fence line.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oak - a tree or shrub of the genus Quercus, known for hard wood
