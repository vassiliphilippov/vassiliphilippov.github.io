laying waste

1. NOUN

/ ˈleɪɪŋ weɪst / (AmE), / ˈleɪɪŋ weɪst / (BrE)
the act of causing extensive destruction or devastation to an area, object, or group
the act of widespread destruction

Examples

The invading army marched through the valley, laying waste to every village in its path.
Centuries ago, warlords were infamous for laying waste to entire regions in pursuit of power.
The hurricane left a path of destruction, effectively laying waste to the coastal town.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: devastation - great destruction or damage
  • Synonym: ruin - severe damage causing destruction or collapse

Etymology

From the verb phrase 'lay waste', meaning 'to devastate' or 'destroy', with 'waste' from Old French 'waste' (desolate, ruined).
