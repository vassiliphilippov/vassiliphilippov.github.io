railway locomotive

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪl.weɪ ˌloʊ.kəˈmoʊ.tɪv / (AmE), / ˈreɪl.weɪ ˌləʊ.kəˈməʊ.tɪv / (BrE)
a self-propelled vehicle on wheels that provides the motive power for a train along railway tracks
engine-powered train vehicle

Examples

The old steam railway locomotive was displayed in the museum as a piece of history.
Engineers prepared the railway locomotive for a long journey across the country.
Without a functioning railway locomotive, the train could not leave the station.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: locomotive - a powered rail vehicle for pulling trains

Etymology

From 'railway' + 'locomotive', with 'locomotive' from Latin 'locus' (place) and 'motivus' (causing motion).
