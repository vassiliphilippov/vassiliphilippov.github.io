collage film

1. NOUN

/ kəˈlɑːʒ fɪlm / (AmE), / kəˈlɑːʒ fɪlm / (BrE)
a film composed by assembling and juxtaposing different types of footage, often from varied sources, to create a new artistic work
film made from diverse assembled footage

Examples

The director's latest collage film incorporates newsreels and home movies to portray the era.
She appreciates the experimental nature of a collage film over traditional narrative cinema.
Critics praised the collage film for its inventive use of archival footage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: montage - a technique in film editing that combines separate shots into a sequence

Etymology

From French 'collage' (pasting, gluing), originally an art term for glued assembly.
