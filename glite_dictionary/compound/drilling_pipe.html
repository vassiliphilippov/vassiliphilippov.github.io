drilling pipe

1. NOUN

/ ˈdrɪlɪŋ paɪp / (AmE), / ˈdrɪlɪŋ pʌɪp / (BrE)
a series of connected tubes used to transmit mechanical force and drilling mud from a drilling platform to a drilling bit in oil or gas exploration
tube system for drilling rigs

Examples

The drilling pipe was carefully assembled to ensure a secure connection with the drilling bit.
Engineers monitored the flow of mud through the drilling pipe during the deep-water operation.
A damaged drilling pipe can halt progress and cause significant safety risks on the rig.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drill pipe - a tubular component used to transmit drilling fluid and torque in a drill string
