white mulberry

1. NOUN

/ waɪt ˈmʌlˌbɛri / (AmE), / waɪt ˈmʌlbəri / (BrE)
an Asian species of mulberry tree (Morus alba) known for its leaves, which are used to feed silkworms, and its fruit, which ranges from white to pale red
Asian mulberry tree; silkworm food source

Examples

The white mulberry tree is widely cultivated in Asia as food for silkworms.
She picked a handful of white mulberries from the tree in her garden.
Botanists study the white mulberry for its importance in the silk industry.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: black mulberry - a species of mulberry tree with dark purple-black fruit (Morus nigra)
