genus Cyprinus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs sɪˈpraɪ.nəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs sɪˈprɪ.nəs / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Cyprinidae, comprising carp and related freshwater fish species.
type genus of Cyprinidae (carps)

Examples

The genus Cyprinus includes the well-known common carp found in many freshwater habitats.
Scientists have studied several evolutionary traits unique to the genus Cyprinus.
Specimens from the genus Cyprinus are often used in ecological research on freshwater fish diversity.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Cyprinus carpio - a species of Cyprinus; the common carp

Etymology

From Latin 'Cyprinus' (carp), used in scientific Latin classification.
