Philippine mahogany

1. NOUN

/ fɪˈlɪpɪn məˈhɑːɡəni / (AmE), / fɪˈlɪpɪn məˈhɒɡəni / (BrE)
A red hardwood from trees native to the Philippines, used especially for cigar boxes, furniture, and interior finishing.
red hardwood from Philippine trees

Examples

The boat was constructed from solid Philippine mahogany, giving it both strength and beauty.
They used Philippine mahogany to panel the luxurious hotel lobby.
Cigar boxes made of Philippine mahogany are renowned for their delicate aroma and finish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mahogany - hard reddish-brown wood from several tropical trees

2. NOUN

/ fɪˈlɪpɪn məˈhɑːni / (AmE), / fɪˈlɪpɪn məˈhɒni / (BrE)
a tropical timber tree from the Philippines, known for its hard, reddish, fragrant wood often used in furniture and construction
hardwood tree from the Philippines

Examples

The table was made from fine Philippine mahogany, giving it a deep, rich color.
Carpenters often choose Philippine mahogany for its durability and elegant appearance.
Many boats were traditionally built using Philippine mahogany due to its resistance to water.
