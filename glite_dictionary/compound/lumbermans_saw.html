lumberman's saw

1. NOUN

/ ˈlʌmbɚˌmænz sɔ / (AmE), / ˈlʌmbəmænz sɔː / (BrE)
a large saw with handles at both ends, designed to be operated by two people for cutting wood or logs
large two-handled saw for cutting logs

Examples

The old loggers used a lumberman's saw to fell massive trees in the forest.
We needed two people to operate the lumberman's saw efficiently when cutting through thick logs.
A traditional lumberman's saw requires teamwork and rhythm for smooth cutting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crosscut saw - a saw used to cut across the grain of wood, often similar in design to a lumberman's saw
