white willow

1. NOUN

/ waɪt ˈwɪloʊ / (AmE), / waɪt ˈwɪləʊ / (BrE)
a large willow tree (Salix alba) native to Eurasia and North Africa, with greyish, silky leaves and grey bark
a species of willow tree with greyish leaves

Examples

The bark of the white willow has traditionally been used as a remedy for pain and inflammation.
We planted a white willow by the riverbank to prevent soil erosion.
The white willow stood tall, its silver-grey leaves shimmering in the morning light.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: willow - a tree or shrub of the genus Salix, with slender branches and narrow leaves
