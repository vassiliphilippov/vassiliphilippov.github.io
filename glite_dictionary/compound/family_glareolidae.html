family Glareolidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ɡlɛəriˈɒlɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli ˌɡleərɪəˈlɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of birds that includes the pratincoles and coursers, found mainly in the Old World tropics and subtropics.
family of birds: pratincoles and coursers

Examples

The family Glareolidae consists of medium-sized wading birds known for their graceful flight and long, pointed wings.
Researchers are studying the migratory patterns of species within the family Glareolidae.
Both pratincoles and coursers belong to the avian family Glareolidae, which inhabits various regions across Africa and Eurasia.

Etymology

From Modern Latin Glareolidae, based on the type genus Glareola.
