normal school

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɔrməl skuːl / (AmE), / ˈnɔːməl skuːl / (BrE)
A college or institution that trained individuals to become elementary or primary school teachers, typically lasting two years.
school for training teachers

Examples

She graduated from the normal school and began teaching in a rural district.
The first normal school in the state opened in the late nineteenth century.
Many educators at the time attended a normal school before pursuing university degrees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: teacher training college - a college that trains people to become teachers

Etymology

From French 'école normale', intended as a model school with teaching 'the norm'.
