sex gland

1. NOUN

/ sɛks ɡlænd / (AmE), / sɛks ɡlænd / (BrE)
a gland in which sex cells (gametes) are produced, such as an ovary or testis
gland producing sex cells (gonads)
medical

Examples

The hormones released by the sex gland are essential for reproductive health.
Damage to the sex gland can result in infertility in some cases.
Both the ovary and the testis are types of sex gland found in animals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gonad - an organ that produces gametes; a testis or ovary
