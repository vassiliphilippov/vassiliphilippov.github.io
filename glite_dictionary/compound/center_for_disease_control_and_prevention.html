Center for Disease Control and Prevention

1. PROPN

/ ˈsɛn.tɚ fɔr dɪˈziz kənˈtroʊl ənd prɪˈvɛn.ʃən / (AmE), / ˈsɛn.tə fə dɪˈziːz kənˈtrəʊl ənd prɪˈvɛn.ʃən / (BrE)
A U.S. federal agency located in Atlanta, responsible for investigating, diagnosing, and controlling or preventing diseases, especially new or unusual ones.
U.S. federal agency for disease control and prevention

Examples

The Center for Disease Control and Prevention issued new guidelines for disease prevention last week.
Many health professionals rely on data from the Center for Disease Control and Prevention to track outbreaks.
The Center for Disease Control and Prevention conducts research on infectious diseases and public health threats.
