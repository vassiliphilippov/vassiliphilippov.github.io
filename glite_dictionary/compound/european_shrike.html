European shrike

1. NOUN

/ jʊˈroʊpiən ʃraɪk / (AmE), / jʊəˈrəʊpiən ʃraɪk / (BrE)
a small predatory songbird native to Europe, known for impaling its prey on thorns; also called a butcherbird
small predatory European songbird

Examples

The European shrike is often spotted perched on fences searching for insects and small mammals.
It is fascinating to observe how the European shrike impales its prey on sharp branches.
Birdwatchers from across the continent travel to see different species of European shrike in their natural habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shrike - a predatory songbird of the family Laniidae
  • Synonym: butcherbird - another name for shrike, referencing its habit of impaling prey
