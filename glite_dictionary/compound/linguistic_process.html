linguistic process

1. NOUN

/ lɪŋˈɡwɪstɪk ˈprɑː.ses / (AmE), / lɪŋˈɡwɪstɪk ˈprəʊ.ses / (BrE)
a process involved in the use, comprehension, or production of human language
process in human language

Examples

Reading aloud is a linguistic process that involves both recognition and production of words.
Memory plays an important role in every linguistic process within the brain.
Language acquisition is considered a complex linguistic process studied by researchers.

2. NOUN

/ lɪŋˈɡwɪstɪk ˈprɑː.sɛs / (AmE), / lɪŋˈɡwɪstɪk ˈprəʊ.ses / (BrE)
a mental operation involved in the production or comprehension of language
mental operation in language use

Examples

Language acquisition is considered a complex linguistic process involving several stages.
Understanding sarcasm requires a distinct linguistic process from understanding literal statements.
Researchers use brain imaging to study the linguistic processes involved in speech comprehension.

Synset Examples

he didn't have the language to express his feelings

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cognitive process - a general mental operation of acquiring knowledge and understanding

Etymology

From 'linguistic', relating to language (from Latin lingua), and 'process', from Latin processus (advance, progression).
