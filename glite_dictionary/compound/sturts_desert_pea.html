Sturt's desert pea

1. NOUN

/ stɝts ˈdɛzɚt pi / (AmE), / stɜːts ˈdezət piː / (BrE)
a sprawling perennial plant native to arid regions of Australia, recognized for its bright scarlet flowers with distinctive black markings
Australian perennial with scarlet black-marked flowers

Examples

The brilliant flowers of the Sturt's desert pea make it one of Australia's most iconic plants.
We saw several Sturt's desert pea specimens thriving in the harsh outback conditions.
Gardeners often struggle to grow Sturt's desert pea outside its native environment.

Etymology

Named after Charles Sturt, a British explorer who discovered the plant; 'pea' refers to its family Fabaceae.
