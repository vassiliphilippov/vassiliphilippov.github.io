cerebral mantle

1. NOUN

/ səˈribɹəl ˈmæntl̩ / (AmE), / səˈriːbrəl ˈmæntl̩ / (BrE)
the layer of unmyelinated neurons (grey matter) that forms the cortex of the cerebrum in the brain
the grey matter forming the brain's cortex
medical

Examples

The cerebral mantle plays a key role in processing sensory information and initiating voluntary movement.
Damage to the cerebral mantle can result in significant cognitive and behavioral changes.
Neuroscientists are studying the connections within the cerebral mantle to better understand brain function.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cerebral cortex - the outer layer of the cerebrum responsible for higher brain functions
  • Synonym: grey matter - regions of the brain and spinal cord rich in neuronal cell bodies

Etymology

From Latin 'cerebrum' (brain) and 'mantle' (cloak, covering).
