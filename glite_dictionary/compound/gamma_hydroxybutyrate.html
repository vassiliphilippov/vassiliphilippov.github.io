gamma hydroxybutyrate

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡæmə haɪˌdrɒksiˌbjuːtəˈreɪt / (AmE), / ˈɡæmə hʌɪˌdrɒksɪˌbjuːtəˈreɪt / (BrE)
A colorless, odorless substance used as a recreational drug and a sedative; commonly known as GHB, it can cause loss of consciousness and is sometimes misused to facilitate sexual assault.
sedative and recreational drug (GHB)
medical

Examples

The effects of gamma hydroxybutyrate can include drowsiness, dizziness, and even unconsciousness.
Police warned partygoers about the dangers of unknowingly ingesting gamma hydroxybutyrate in their drinks.
Doctors strictly monitor patients using gamma hydroxybutyrate for medical treatment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: GHB - Abbreviation for gamma hydroxybutyrate, the same substance

Etymology

From Greek letter 'gamma' + hydroxy- (relating to a hydroxyl group) + butyrate (salt or ester of butyric acid).
