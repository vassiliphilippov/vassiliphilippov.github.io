zeroth law of thermodynamics

1. NOUN

/ ˈzɪroʊθ lɔː əv ˌθɜrməˌdaɪˈnæmɪks / (AmE), / ˈzɪərəʊθ lɔː əv ˌθɜːmə(ʊ)daɪˈnæmɪks / (BrE)
the principle stating that if two systems are each in thermal equilibrium with a third system, then they are in thermal equilibrium with each other
principle of thermal equilibrium in physics

Examples

The zeroth law of thermodynamics allows scientists to define temperature in a consistent way.
Understanding the zeroth law of thermodynamics is essential for studying how heat flows between objects.
In his lecture, the professor explained the significance of the zeroth law of thermodynamics in establishing the concept of thermal equilibrium.

Etymology

Formed from 'zeroth' (from zero + -th), coined to indicate its logical precedence to the first law, and 'thermodynamics' (from Greek thermē ‘heat’ + dynamis ‘power’).
