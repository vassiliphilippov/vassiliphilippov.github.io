electron optics

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈlɛk.trɑn ˈɑp.tɪks / (AmE), / ɪˈlɛk.trɒn ˈɒp.tɪks / (BrE)
the branch of physics and electronics concerned with the behavior and manipulation of electron beams, especially their focusing and deflection by electric and magnetic fields
study of electron beam focusing and deflection

Examples

Advances in electron optics have greatly improved the resolution of electron microscopes.
She specialized in electron optics during her graduate studies in physics.
Understanding electron optics is crucial for designing efficient cathode ray tubes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: optics - the branch of physics involving the study of light and vision

Etymology

From 'electron' (coined in 1894 from the Greek 'ēlektron' meaning amber) and 'optics' (from Greek 'optikē', relating to sight).
