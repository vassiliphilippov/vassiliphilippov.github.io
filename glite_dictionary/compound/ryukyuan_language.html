Ryukyuan language

1. NOUN

/ ɹaɪˈjuːkiu.ən ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / raɪˈjuːkiːʊən ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
Any of the distinct languages related to Japanese that are spoken by the people of the Ryukyu Islands, including Okinawan, Amami, and others.
language(s) of the Ryukyu Islands

Examples

Linguists study the Ryukyuan language to understand its connection to Japanese and its unique dialects.
Many elders in Okinawa still speak the Ryukyuan language fluently, while younger generations often prefer Japanese.
Preserving the Ryukyuan language is important for maintaining the cultural heritage of the Ryukyu Islands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Japanese - the main language spoken in Japan and related to Ryukyuan

Etymology

From Ryukyu, the name of the islands, plus 'an' denoting relation, and 'language'.
