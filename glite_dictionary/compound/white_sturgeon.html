white sturgeon

1. NOUN

/ waɪt ˈstɝdʒən / (AmE), / waɪt ˈstɜːdʒən / (BrE)
a large species of sturgeon (Acipenser transmontanus) found in the marine and fresh waters of the northwestern coast of North America, valued as both a food and game fish
large sturgeon of northwest North America

Examples

The white sturgeon is considered the largest freshwater fish in North America.
Fishing for white sturgeon is strictly regulated to protect the species.
Caviar harvested from the white sturgeon is highly prized among gourmet chefs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sturgeon - large primitive fish of the family Acipenseridae

2. NOUN

/ waɪt ˈstɝːdʒən / (AmE), / waɪt ˈstɜːdʒən / (BrE)
a large sturgeon (Acipenser transmontanus) native to western North America, valued for its caviar and isinglass
a large North American sturgeon

Examples

The white sturgeon is the largest freshwater fish in North America.
Caviar produced from white sturgeon is highly prized by gourmets worldwide.
Researchers have been working to restore the white sturgeon population in several rivers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sturgeon - a large primitive fish prized for caviar and flesh
