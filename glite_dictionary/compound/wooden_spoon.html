wooden spoon

1. NOUN

/ ˈwʊdən ˌspun / (AmE), / ˈwʊdən ˌspuːn / (BrE)
a spoon made from wood, commonly used for stirring and mixing in cooking
a spoon made of wood, used for cooking

Examples

She used a wooden spoon to stir the soup on the stove.
Please pass me the wooden spoon so I can mix the batter.
After years of use, the wooden spoon had developed a smooth, worn handle.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: spoon - an eating or cooking utensil with a shallow bowl and a handle

2. NOUN

/ ˈwʊdən ˌspun / (AmE), / ˈwʊd.ən ˌspuːn / (BrE)
an informal prize, often a literal wooden spoon, awarded to the person or team coming last in a competition
prize given to last-place finisher

Examples

The team received the wooden spoon after finishing at the bottom of the league.
No one wants to go home with the wooden spoon at the end of the tournament.
He joked about proudly displaying his wooden spoon from the quiz night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: booby prize - a prize given as a joke to the least successful competitor
  • Antonym: first prize - an award given to the winner of a competition

Etymology

Originating from the practice at Cambridge University in the 19th century of awarding an actual wooden spoon to the lowest-ranked mathematics student.
