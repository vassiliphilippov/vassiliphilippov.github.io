Tennessee River

1. PROPN

/ ˌtɛnɪˈsiː ˈrɪvər / (AmE), / ˌtɛnɪˈsiː ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
a major river in the southeastern United States, formed near Knoxville and joining the Ohio River in western Kentucky
major river in the southeastern US

Examples

The Tennessee River curves gracefully through several states before joining the Ohio River.
Many cities along the Tennessee River depend on it for water and recreation.
The fisherman spent the weekend boating on the Tennessee River with his family.

Etymology

Named after the state of Tennessee, itself likely from Cherokee *Tanasi*, the name of a Cherokee town.

2. PROPN

/ ˌtɛnəˈsi ˈrɪvər / (AmE), / ˌtɛnəˈsi ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
a major river in the southeastern United States, flowing in a U-shaped course from eastern Tennessee to the Ohio River in western Kentucky
major river in southeastern United States

Examples

The Tennessee River forms part of the border between Alabama and Mississippi.
Many cities along the Tennessee River depend on it for transportation and water supply.
She enjoyed fishing trips on the Tennessee River during her childhood.

Etymology

Named after the state of Tennessee, which is believed to be derived from a Cherokee village name.
