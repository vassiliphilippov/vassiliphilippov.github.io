coquilla nut

1. NOUN

/ koʊˈkiːjə nʌt / (AmE), / kəʊˈkɪlɪə nʌt / (BrE)
the hard, brown nut of a South American palm, used in carving and as a substitute for ivory
hard brown nut used like vegetable ivory

Examples

The craftsman carved a delicate figure from a coquilla nut.
Buttons made from coquilla nuts have a smooth, polished appearance.
Collectors value items made with the unique shell of the coquilla nut.

Etymology

From Spanish 'coquilla', diminutive of 'coco' (nut, coconut).
