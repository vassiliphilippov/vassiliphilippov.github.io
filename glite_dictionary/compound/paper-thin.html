paper-thin

1. ADJ

/ ˌpeɪpər ˈθɪn / (AmE), / ˌpeɪpə ˈθɪn / (BrE)
Extremely thin; as thin as a sheet of paper.
extremely thin

Examples

His patience was paper-thin after a long day of delays.
The hotel walls were so paper-thin that I could hear every conversation next door.
She wore a paper-thin dress that offered little protection from the cold.

Synset Examples

her blouse was paper thin

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: thin - having little extent from one surface to its opposite
  • Degree Higher: transparent - allowing light to pass through so that objects behind can be distinctly seen

2. ADJ

/ ˌpeɪ.pɚ ˈθɪn / (AmE), / ˌpeɪ.pə ˈθɪn / (BrE)
Extremely thin or delicate, like a sheet of paper.
extremely thin or delicate

Examples

The artist created a sculpture from paper-thin sheets of metal.
Her patience was paper-thin after hours of waiting in line.
The old book's paper-thin pages tore easily when handled.

Synset Examples

The paper-thin layer of frosting melted in the sun.
She wore a paper-thin dress that fluttered in the wind.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: thin - having little thickness; not wide from one side to the other
  • Synonym: wafer-thin - extremely thin, like a wafer
