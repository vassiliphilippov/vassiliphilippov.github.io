gutter press

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡʌt̬ər prɛs / (AmE), / ˈɡʌtə prɛs / (BrE)
Newspapers and magazines that publish sensational stories, often about the private lives of famous people, with little regard for accuracy or ethics.
sensationalist newspapers and magazines

Examples

Many celebrities complain about how the gutter press invades their privacy for scandalous stories.
The politician vowed not to respond to rumors spread by the gutter press.
Headlines in the gutter press are often exaggerated or misleading to attract attention.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tabloid - A newspaper with a small page size, often associated with sensational journalism.

Etymology

From the figurative use of 'gutter' (suggesting low moral standards) + 'press' (printing and publishing).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡʌtər prɛs / (AmE), / ˈɡʌtə prɛs / (BrE)
Newspapers or journalists who publish sensational or low-quality news, often focusing on scandal and sensationalism rather than accuracy or integrity.
sensationalist or low-quality newspapers

Examples

Many celebrities have taken legal action against the gutter press for invading their privacy.
He accused the gutter press of distorting the facts to sell more papers.
Despite public outrage, the gutter press continues to publish sensational stories.

Synset Examples

The politician scolded the gutter press for spreading lies.
He refused to read the gutter press, preferring reputable news.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tabloid - a newspaper with short articles and lots of photographs, often focusing on sensational stories

Etymology

From 'gutter' (meaning something low or disreputable) + 'press' (printing or journalism).
