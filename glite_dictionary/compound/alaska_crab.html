Alaska crab

1. NOUN

/ əˈlæskə kræb / (AmE), / əˈlæskə kræb / (BrE)
a large edible crab found in the northern Pacific, especially along the coasts of Alaska and Japan
a large edible northern Pacific crab

Examples

The restaurant is known for its fresh Alaska crab dishes every season.
Fishermen in the Bering Sea risk their lives catching Alaska crab each winter.
She ordered a plate of steamed Alaska crab during her trip to Juneau.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: king crab - a large edible crab of the northern Pacific, especially of the genus Paralithodes

2. NOUN

/ əˈlæskə kræb / (AmE), / əˈlæskə kræb / (BrE)
The edible meat, especially from the legs, of large cold-water crabs found in Alaskan waters, valued as a delicacy.
meat of large Alaskan cold-water crab

Examples

The restaurant serves fresh Alaska crab with melted butter as a specialty.
She ordered a salad topped with tender pieces of Alaska crab.
Harvesting Alaska crab is vital to the region's fishing industry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: king crab - large crab species from cold northern waters, especially valued for its meat
