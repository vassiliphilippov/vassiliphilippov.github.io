order Cycadales

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdər saɪˈkædeɪliːz / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə saɪˈkædeɪliːz / (BrE)
a taxonomic order of primitive tropical gymnosperms, once abundant in the Mesozoic era and now represented by a few scattered tropical species
taxonomic order of tropical gymnosperms

Examples

The order Cycadales was much more widespread during the age of dinosaurs.
Modern species belonging to the order Cycadales are primarily found in tropical regions.
Many fossils of the order Cycadales have helped paleobotanists understand prehistoric flora.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cycad - any plant of the order Cycadales, a group of primitive tropical gymnosperms

Etymology

From New Latin Cycadales, from Cycas (type genus) + -ales (taxonomic order suffix).
