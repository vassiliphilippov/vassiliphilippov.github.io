division Eubacteria

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈvɪʒən juːˈbæktɪriə / (AmE), / dɪˈvɪʒən juːˈbæktɪərɪə / (BrE)
A group of unicellular organisms classified as monerans, characterized by simple cells with rigid walls and, in motile types, flagella; considered a major division of bacteria in older taxonomies.
major bacterial division, simple one-celled organisms

Examples

Scientists have studied the division Eubacteria to understand antibiotic resistance in pathogens.
Many species within the division Eubacteria can survive in extreme environments such as hot springs.
Photosynthetic members of the division Eubacteria play a key role in producing oxygen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bacteria - Microscopic single-celled organisms, including Eubacteria
  • Antonym: Archaebacteria - A separate group of ancient bacteria differing biochemically and genetically from Eubacteria

Etymology

From New Latin 'Eubacteria', combining 'eu-' meaning 'true' (from Greek) and 'bacteria', indicating 'true bacteria' in contrast to Archaebacteria.
