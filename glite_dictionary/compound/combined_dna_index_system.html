Combined DNA Index System

1. NOUN

/ kəmˈbaɪnd ˌdiːɛnˈeɪ ˈɪndɛks ˈsɪstəm / (AmE), / kəmˈbaɪnd ˌdiːɛnˈeɪ ˈɪndɛks ˈsɪstəm / (BrE)
a computerized DNA database maintained by the Federal Bureau of Investigation for storing and comparing DNA profiles in criminal investigations
FBI-maintained DNA database for forensics

Examples

The Combined DNA Index System has revolutionized the way law enforcement agencies solve crimes.
Investigators entered the suspect's genetic profile into the Combined DNA Index System to search for a match.
Thanks to the Combined DNA Index System, authorities were able to link crimes that occurred in different states.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: CODIS - abbreviation for the Combined DNA Index System
