altitude sickness

1. NOUN

/ ˈæl.tɪˌtjud ˌsɪk.nəs / (AmE), / ˈæl.tɪ.tjuːd ˌsɪk.nəs / (BrE)
A condition caused by lack of oxygen at high altitudes, resulting in symptoms such as headache, nausea, and dizziness.
illness from high-altitude oxygen deficiency
medical

Examples

She began to feel altitude sickness after climbing to 3,800 meters above sea level.
Hikers are advised to ascend slowly to avoid altitude sickness.
The symptoms of altitude sickness can include headache, fatigue, and shortness of breath.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acute mountain sickness - mild form of altitude sickness with similar symptoms
