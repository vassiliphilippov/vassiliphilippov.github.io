riding light

1. NOUN

/ ˈraɪdɪŋ laɪt / (AmE), / ˈraɪdɪŋ laɪt / (BrE)
A light displayed by a ship at anchor, usually placed in the rigging, to indicate its presence at night or in low visibility.
anchor light on a ship

Examples

The crew made sure the riding light was visible throughout the night while the ship was anchored.
A vessel at anchor is required to display a riding light for safety.
As darkness fell, the captain ordered the riding light to be switched on.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anchor light - a light displayed by a vessel at anchor to signal its presence

Etymology

From the noun 'riding' (from 'ride at anchor') and 'light'.
