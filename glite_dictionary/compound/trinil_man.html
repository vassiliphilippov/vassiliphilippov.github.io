Trinil man

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrɪnɪl mæn / (AmE), / ˈtrɪnɪl mæn / (BrE)
the fossil remains of an early human (Homo erectus) discovered in Java, Indonesia, originally named Pithecanthropus erectus
early human fossil from Java

Examples

The discovery of Trinil man in 1891 marked a milestone in paleoanthropology.
Scientists believe Trinil man belonged to the species now called Homo erectus.
Excavations at Trinil unearthed the skullcap and femur of Trinil man.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Java man - fossil hominid discovered in Java, Indonesia, same species as Trinil man
  • Synonym: Homo erectus - extinct species of archaic humans

Etymology

Named after Trinil, the site in Java, Indonesia, where the fossils were discovered in 1891.
