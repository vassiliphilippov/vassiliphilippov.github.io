Piedmont glacier

1. NOUN

/ ˈpiːdˌmɑnt ˈɡleɪʃər / (AmE), / ˈpiːdmɒnt ˈɡlæsiər / (BrE)
a glacier that spreads out as a broad lobe at the base of mountains where valley glaciers merge, forming an almost stagnant sheet of ice
type of glacier formed by merging valley glaciers

Examples

The Malaspina Glacier in Alaska is a classic example of a Piedmont glacier.
When several valley glaciers converge, they can form a massive Piedmont glacier at the mountain's base.
Researchers study the movement of the Piedmont glacier to understand glacial dynamics in the region.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: valley glacier - a glacier confined within a valley
  • Degree Higher: ice sheet - a large mass of glacial ice covering a vast area

Etymology

From Italian 'piemonte' meaning 'foot of the mountain'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpiːd.mɑnt ˈɡleɪ.ʃər / (AmE), / ˈpiːdmɒnt ˈɡleɪ.si.ə / (BrE)
A glacier formed when one or more valley glaciers spread out onto a flat plain at the base of mountains, merging into a broad lobe of ice.
broad glacier formed where valley glaciers meet on a plain

Examples

The Malaspina is the largest Piedmont glacier in North America.
A Piedmont glacier forms when several valley glaciers converge on a flat plain.
Scientists studied the melt patterns of the Piedmont glacier at the mountain's base.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: valley glacier - a glacier that flows down a valley

Etymology

From Italian 'piedemonte' meaning 'at the foot of a mountain' and 'glacier'.
