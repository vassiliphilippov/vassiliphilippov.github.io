rivet counter

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɪvət ˌkaʊntər / (AmE), / ˈrɪvɪt ˌkaʊntə / (BrE)
a person who is excessively concerned with minor details or trivial points, especially in a hobby or technical context
person obsessed with trivial details

Examples

Some model train enthusiasts can be real rivet counters, focusing on every tiny inaccuracy.
The director told the rivet counters to relax and enjoy the movie, not to analyze every prop.
Don't be a rivet counter; sometimes it's best to appreciate the big picture.

Synset Examples

Don't be a rivet counter and just enjoy the show.
As a rivet counter, she often fixes minor errors others overlook.
He's such a rivet counter when it comes to details.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pedant - a person excessively concerned with minor details and rules

Etymology

Originally from the model railroad hobby, where counting rivets referred to obsessing over minute details of models' accuracy.
