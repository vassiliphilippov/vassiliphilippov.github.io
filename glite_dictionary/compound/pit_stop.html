pit stop

1. NOUN

/ pɪt stɑp / (AmE), / pɪt stɒp / (BrE)
A brief stop during a motor race, typically to refuel, change tires, or service the car.
brief stop in car racing for service

Examples

The leading car made a quick pit stop to change its tires and refuel.
Unexpected issues during the pit stop cost the team several crucial seconds.
Every race strategy must account for at least one pit stop under high-speed conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pit crew - the team responsible for servicing a car during a pit stop

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɪt ˌstɑp / (AmE), / ˈpɪt ˌstɒp / (BrE)
a short stop made during a journey by car or other vehicle, typically to rest, get fuel, or refresh oneself
brief stop for rest or refreshment during a trip

Examples

We made a quick pit stop at the gas station before continuing our journey.
After three hours on the road, everyone was ready for a pit stop to grab snacks.
He took a pit stop to stretch his legs and use the restroom.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: break - a short period of rest during an activity
  • Synonym: rest stop - a designated stop along a highway for resting or refreshment

Etymology

From motorsports, where 'pit stop' refers to a quick stop in the pit area for service; figurative general use developed in the 1980s.
