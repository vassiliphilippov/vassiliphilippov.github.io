Japanese privet

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˈpæniz ˈprɪvɪt / (AmE), / dʒəˈpæniːz ˈprɪvɪt / (BrE)
an evergreen shrub native to Japan and Korea, with small dark leaves and flowers in loose clusters, related to but smaller than the Chinese privet
evergreen shrub native to Japan and Korea

Examples

The Japanese privet is popular for use in hedges due to its dense foliage.
A row of Japanese privet bushes lined the garden path, adding year-round greenery.
Unlike the Chinese variety, Japanese privet tends to stay more compact and manageable.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Chinese privet - a related but larger species of privet shrub from China
