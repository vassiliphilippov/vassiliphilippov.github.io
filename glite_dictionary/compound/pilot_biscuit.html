pilot biscuit

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪlət ˈbɪskɪt / (AmE), / ˈpaɪlət ˈbɪskɪt / (BrE)
a very hard, unsalted biscuit once commonly eaten by sailors as a durable ship's staple
hard, unsalted sailor's biscuit

Examples

During the long voyage, the crew survived on salted meat and pilot biscuit.
The museum displayed a 19th-century pilot biscuit preserved from an old shipwreck.
Sailors often soaked their pilot biscuit in water or soup to make it edible.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hardtack - very hard, dry biscuit or bread used as ship's provisions

Etymology

From 'pilot' (nautical term for a ship's navigator) and 'biscuit', reflecting its use as a staple for crews.
