deverbal noun

1. NOUN

/ diˈvɝːbəl naʊn / (AmE), / diˈvɜːbəl naʊn / (BrE)
A noun that is formed from a verb, often by adding a suffix.
noun derived from a verb

Examples

In the sentence 'His movement was graceful,' the word movement is a deverbal noun.
The English word 'decision' is a deverbal noun derived from the verb 'decide'.
Many scientific terms are deverbal nouns created by adding suffixes to verbs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: verbal noun - a noun derived from a verb, often showing ongoing action

Etymology

From Latin de- (down, away) + Latin verbalis (pertaining to a verb).
