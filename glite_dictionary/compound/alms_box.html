alms box

1. NOUN

/ ɑːlmz bɑks / (AmE), / ɑːlmz bɒks / (BrE)
A box, especially in a church, used for collecting money or gifts given as charity.
box for collecting alms in a church

Examples

The church placed an alms box near the entrance for parishioners to donate to the needy.
Coins clinked as the children dropped them into the old wooden alms box.
Tourists admired the ornate alms box that had stood in the chapel for centuries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poor box - a box in a church for collecting money for the poor

Etymology

From Old English 'ælmysse' (alms) and 'box', influenced by Latin 'eleemosyna' (alms).
