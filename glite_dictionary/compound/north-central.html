north-central

1. ADJ

/ ˌnɔrθ ˈsɛntrəl / (AmE), / ˌnɔːθ ˈsɛntrəl / (BrE)
Situated in or relating to the northern and central parts of a region, especially the United States area covering the upper Mississippi Valley and Great Lakes region.
of the northern central region (esp. US)

Examples

He grew up in a small north-central town known for its beautiful lakes.
The north-central states often experience harsh winters and mild summers.
Many major rivers run through the north-central part of the United States.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: midwestern - relating to the Midwest region of the United States

2. ADJ

/ nɔrθ ˈsɛntrəl / (AmE), / nɔːθ ˈsɛntrəl / (BrE)
Located in the northern part of the central area of a region or country.
northern part of a central region

Examples

Heavy snowfall is common in north-central Minnesota during the winter months.
The north-central region of the country is known for its fertile farmland.
Many migratory birds stop in the north-central United States on their journey south.

Synset Examples

the north-central part of Russia

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: northwest - located in or toward the northwest
  • Degree Higher: north - situated toward or in the direction of the north
  • Degree Lower: central - in, at, of, or forming the center
