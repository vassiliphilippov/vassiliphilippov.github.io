genus Giraffa

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs dʒɪˈræfə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs dʒɪˈræfə / (BrE)
The genus comprising the giraffes, the only extant genus in the family Giraffidae.
zoological genus of giraffes

Examples

The genus Giraffa includes only the modern giraffe species.
Scientists have debated the classification of subspecies within the genus Giraffa.
Fossils attributed to the genus Giraffa have been found across several parts of Africa.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: giraffe - a large African mammal with a very long neck and legs

Etymology

From Latin *genus* (kind, type) and *Giraffa* (giraffe), the scientific name for the giraffe genus.
