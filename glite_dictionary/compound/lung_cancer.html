lung cancer

1. NOUN

/ lʌŋ ˈkæn.sɚ / (AmE), / lʌŋ ˈkæn.sə / (BrE)
A malignant tumor or growth in the lungs, usually characterized by uncontrolled cell division and often linked to smoking.
malignant tumor of the lungs
medical

Examples

Early detection of lung cancer greatly increases a patient's chances of survival.
His father developed lung cancer after years of heavy smoking.
Scientists are researching new treatments to improve outcomes for those diagnosed with lung cancer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cancer - a malignant growth or tumor caused by abnormal cell division
