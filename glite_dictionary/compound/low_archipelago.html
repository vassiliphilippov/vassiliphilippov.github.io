Low Archipelago

1. PROPN

/ loʊ ˌɑːrtʃəˈpɛlɪˌɡoʊ / (AmE), / ləʊ ˌɑːtʃɪˈpelɪɡəʊ / (BrE)
a group of about 80 coral islands in French Polynesia, part of the Tuamotu Islands
group of coral islands in French Polynesia

Examples

The Low Archipelago is renowned for its stunning coral atolls and turquoise lagoons.
Researchers visited several islands in the Low Archipelago to study marine biodiversity.
Tourists are often drawn to the remote beauty of the Low Archipelago for snorkeling and diving.

Etymology

From 'low' (possibly contrasting with 'High Archipelago' or high islands) and 'archipelago' (from Italian 'arcipelago').
