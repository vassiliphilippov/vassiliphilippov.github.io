good will

1. NOUN

/ ɡʊd wɪl / (AmE), / ɡʊd wɪl / (BrE)
A friendly or kindly feeling of support, approval, or hope that something will succeed.
friendly hope or support for success

Examples

We wish them nothing but good will on their new venture.
The project was launched in a spirit of good will and cooperation.
Her good will towards her competitors was appreciated by everyone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: support - help or approval for someone or something
  • Antonym: ill will - hostile or unfriendly feeling toward someone or something

2. NOUN

/ ɡʊd wɪl / (AmE), / ɡʊd wɪl / (BrE)
a friendly or helpful attitude towards others; a disposition to be kind, compassionate, or cooperative
kind and friendly attitude

Examples

The volunteers acted out of pure good will to help the community.
His good will towards his colleagues made him well liked at work.
A little good will can go a long way in resolving conflicts.

Synset Examples

the victor's grace in treating the vanquished

Related Terms

  • Synonym: benevolence - the quality of being well-meaning and kindly
  • Antonym: ill will - hostile or unfriendly feeling

Etymology

From Middle English 'good' (virtuous, benevolent) and 'will' (wish, desire).
