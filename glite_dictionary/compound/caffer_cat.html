caffer cat

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæfər kæt / (AmE), / ˈkæfə kæt / (BrE)
a widely distributed wildcat found in Africa and Asia Minor, also known as the African wildcat
African wildcat of Africa and Asia Minor

Examples

The caffer cat is considered the ancestor of the domestic cat.
Researchers found tracks believed to belong to a caffer cat in the savanna.
Unlike other wildcats, the caffer cat adapts well to a variety of environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: African wildcat - a wildcat species native to Africa and parts of Asia

Etymology

From the former scientific name Felis caffer, based on obsolete colonial terminology and scientific Latin.
