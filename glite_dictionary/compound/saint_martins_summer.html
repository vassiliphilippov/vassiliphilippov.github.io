Saint Martin's summer

1. NOUN

/ seɪnt ˈmɑrtɪnz ˈsʌmər / (AmE), / seɪnt ˈmɑːtɪnz ˈsʌmə / (BrE)
a period of unusually warm weather occurring in the autumn, especially around the feast of Saint Martin (November 11)
period of warm autumn weather

Examples

Many poets have written about the beauty of Saint Martin's summer in the countryside.
We were surprised by a brief Saint Martin's summer before winter truly arrived.
In some parts of Europe, people look forward to Saint Martin's summer each November.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Indian summer - a period of unseasonably warm, dry weather in late autumn

Etymology

Named after Saint Martin's Day, celebrated on November 11, when such weather often occurs in parts of Europe.
