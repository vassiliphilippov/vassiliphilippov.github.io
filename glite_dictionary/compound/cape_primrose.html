Cape primrose

1. NOUN

/ keɪp ˈpraɪmˌroʊz / (AmE), / keɪp ˈpraɪmˌrəʊz / (BrE)
Any of several African plants of the genus Streptocarpus, popular as houseplants for their showy blue or purple flowers.
African houseplant with showy flowers

Examples

The Cape primrose is prized for its vibrant violet blooms and ease of care indoors.
She placed a pot of Cape primrose on her sunny windowsill to brighten the room.
Gardeners appreciate Cape primroses for their long-lasting, colorful flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Streptocarpus - the botanical genus of Cape primrose

Etymology

Named after the Cape of Good Hope in South Africa and 'primrose' for its flower's resemblance to true primroses.
