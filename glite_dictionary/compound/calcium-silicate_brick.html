calcium-silicate brick

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæl.si.əm ˈsɪl.ɪ.keɪt brɪk / (AmE), / ˌkæl.si.əm ˈsɪl.ɪ.keɪt brɪk / (BrE)
A type of brick made from a mixture of sand and slaked lime, rather than clay, usually light gray or off-white in color, used in construction.
brick made from sand and lime

Examples

The construction company selected calcium-silicate brick for the building because of its uniform appearance.
Unlike traditional clay bricks, calcium-silicate bricks are lighter in color and have a smoother finish.
When choosing materials for the facade, the architect preferred calcium-silicate brick for its durability and modern look.

Synset Examples

The finished calcium-silicate bricks are very accurate and uniform, although the sharp arrises need careful handling to avoid damage to brick and bricklayer.
