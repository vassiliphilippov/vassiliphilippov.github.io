Grey Friar

1. NOUN

/ ɡreɪ ˈfraɪər / (AmE), / ɡreɪ ˈfraɪə / (BrE)
A Roman Catholic friar belonging to the Franciscan order, distinguished by the wearing of a grey habit.
Franciscan friar in grey habit

Examples

The old church was once managed by Grey Friars devoted to a life of poverty.
A Grey Friar greeted us at the monastery gate with a gentle smile.
Medieval manuscripts often depict a Grey Friar in a simple, grey robe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Franciscan - A member of the Roman Catholic religious order founded by St Francis of Assisi.

Etymology

From the color (grey) of the habit traditionally worn by Franciscan friars.
