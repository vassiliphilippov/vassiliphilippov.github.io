writer's cramp

1. NOUN

/ ˈraɪtərz kræmp / (AmE), / ˈraɪtəz kræmp / (BrE)
a condition causing involuntary muscular spasms in the fingers or hand, typically brought on by prolonged writing
spasm in hand or fingers from writing
medical

Examples

After several hours of taking notes, she developed writer's cramp in her hand.
Many musicians suffer from a form of writer's cramp affecting their ability to play.
Doctors recommend frequent breaks to avoid writer's cramp when writing by hand.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hand cramp - muscular spasms in the hand
  • Synonym: dystonia - a neurological movement disorder causing muscle contractions

Etymology

From 'writer' (one who writes) + 'cramp' (painful muscle spasm), referring to its occurrence during writing.
