sulfur oxide

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌlfɚ ˈɑːksaɪd / (AmE), / ˈsʌlfə ˈɒksaɪd / (BrE)
any of several chemical compounds consisting of sulfur and oxygen, especially sulfur dioxide (SO₂) and sulfur trioxide (SO₃)
oxide compound of sulfur and oxygen

Examples

The combustion of coal can release large amounts of sulfur oxide into the atmosphere.
Reducing sulfur oxide emissions is essential for improving air quality in urban areas.
Chemists studied the reaction rates of different sulfur oxide compounds under varying conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sulfur dioxide - a common oxide of sulfur, SO₂
  • Synonym: sulfur trioxide - a common oxide of sulfur, SO₃

Etymology

From sulfur (Latin: sulphur) + oxide, referring to compounds containing oxygen.
