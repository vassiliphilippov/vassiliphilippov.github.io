green corn

1. NOUN

/ ɡrin kɔrn / (AmE), / ɡriːn kɔːn / (BrE)
Corn that is eaten as a vegetable while it is still young, soft, and unripe.
corn eaten while young and soft

Examples

In late summer, farmers sell freshly picked green corn at the market.
The festival featured traditional dishes made with green corn.
She prefers to eat green corn grilled with butter and herbs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sweetcorn - corn eaten as a vegetable, especially in the form of kernels

2. NOUN

/ ɡrin kɔrn / (AmE), / ɡriːn kɔːn / (BrE)
corn harvested when the ears are young, tender, and sweet, typically before full maturity and suitable for eating as a vegetable.
young, tender corn suitable for eating

Examples

The festival celebrates the first harvest of green corn every summer.
She prefers to cook green corn because of its sweet flavor and tender kernels.
Farmers sell green corn at the market before it matures and dries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sweetcorn - corn eaten as a vegetable, harvested while immature and sweet
