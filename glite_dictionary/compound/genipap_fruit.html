genipap fruit

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒɛn.ɪˈpæp frut / (AmE), / ˌdʒɛn.ɪˈpap fruːt / (BrE)
A succulent, orange-sized tropical fruit with a thick rind, native to Central and South America.
tropical fruit with thick rind

Examples

The market had a selection of fresh genipap fruit imported from Brazil.
She used the juice of genipap fruit to make a traditional drink.
When ripe, the thick rind of the genipap fruit turns yellowish and is easy to peel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: genipap - the tree bearing the genipap fruit

Etymology

From Spanish 'genipa', from indigenous South American languages.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɛnɪˌpæp frut / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛnɪpap fruːt / (BrE)
the fruit of a tropical tree native to the West Indies and northern South America, which is succulent and about the size of an orange
tropical edible fruit from the West Indies and South America

Examples

The genipap fruit has a thick, bitter rind but a sweet, edible pulp inside.
Farmers in Brazil often harvest genipap fruit during the rainy season for local markets.
Juices made from genipap fruit are popular in Caribbean cuisine for their unique flavor.

Etymology

From Carib or Spanish 'genipapo,' referring to the tree and its fruit.
