Chinese overtime

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃaɪˌniz ˈoʊvərˌtaɪm / (AmE), / ˈtʃaɪˌniːz ˈəʊvətaɪm / (BrE)
the practice of working extra hours beyond the normal schedule without receiving additional pay, especially as a routine or expected part of the job
unpaid extra work hours

Examples

Management expected us to put in Chinese overtime to finish the project on schedule.
He was tired of the constant Chinese overtime and started looking for a new job.
We joked about earning nothing for all the Chinese overtime we did last month.

Synset Examples

He worked Chinese overtime last week, burning the midnight oil.
They called it Chinese overtime, but it felt like regular hours to me.
She joked about doing Chinese overtime to meet the deadline.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overtime - time worked beyond the usual working hours, often paid at a higher rate

Etymology

Refers to a dated and potentially insensitive term describing an exploitative overtime practice; 'Chinese' used here is not related to China but reflects a historical stereotype.
