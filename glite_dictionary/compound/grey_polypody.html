grey polypody

1. NOUN

/ ɡreɪ ˈpɑːlɪˌpoʊdi / (AmE), / ɡreɪ ˈpɒlɪˌpəʊdi / (BrE)
a fern that grows on rocks or tree trunks, with fronds that are greyish and scurfy on the underside; found in the Americas and South Africa
a fern species with greyish, scurfy fronds

Examples

The grey polypody thrives in moist, shaded forests throughout South Africa.
She identified the grey polypody growing along the rocky outcrop during her hike.
Many botanists study the unique adaptations of the grey polypody to its environment.
