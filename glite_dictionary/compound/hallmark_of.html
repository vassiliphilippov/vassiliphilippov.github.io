hallmark of

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɔlˌmɑrk əv / (AmE), / ˈhɔːlmɑːk ɒv / (BrE)
a distinguishing characteristic or feature that is typical of someone or something
distinguishing characteristic or feature

Examples

Innovation is the hallmark of great technology companies.
Patience is a hallmark of skilled teachers.
Detailed craftsmanship has long been a hallmark of this furniture maker.

Synset Examples

The hallmark of this brand is quality and durability.
Honesty is a hallmark of a good leader.
Her kindness is the hallmark of her character.
The ever-changing weather is a hallmark of Minnesomalia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trait - a distinguishing quality or characteristic
  • Synonym: feature - an important or distinctive aspect of something
  • Degree Lower: quirk - a peculiar or unexpected trait or aspect

Etymology

From 'hallmark,' originally referring to marks stamped on articles of gold, silver, or platinum at London’s Goldsmiths’ Hall for authentication.
