white light

1. NOUN

/ waɪt laɪt / (AmE), / waɪt laɪt / (BrE)
light that contains all colors of the visible spectrum and appears white to the human eye
light containing all visible colors

Examples

The scientist passed white light through a prism to reveal a spectrum of colors.
Fluorescent bulbs are designed to emit white light for bright, even illumination.
Natural sunlight appears as white light because it contains all the visible wavelengths.

Synset Examples

The artist used white light to enhance the painting's details.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: visible light - the portion of the electromagnetic spectrum visible to the human eye
  • Antonym: monochromatic light - light consisting of a single wavelength and color
