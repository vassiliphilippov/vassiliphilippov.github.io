family Suidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ˈsuːɪ.diː / (AmE), / ˈfæməli ˈsjuːɪ.diː / (BrE)
A family of mammals including pigs, hogs, and boars, characterized by stout bodies, short legs, and omnivorous diets.
taxonomic family of pigs and relatives

Examples

The family Suidae includes both wild and domesticated species found around the world.
Scientists have studied the evolutionary history of the family Suidae for decades.
Members of the family Suidae are known for their keen sense of smell and omnivorous feeding habits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pig - common domesticated animal in the family Suidae
  • Synonym: hog - large domesticated pig, member of Suidae
  • Synonym: boar - wild pig, member of family Suidae

Etymology

From Latin 'Sus' (pig), the type genus, and '-idae' (family suffix used in scientific classification).
