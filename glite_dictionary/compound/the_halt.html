the halt

1. NOUN

/ ðə hɔlt / (AmE), / ðə hɔːlt / (BrE)
(archaic) People who are lame, collectively.
(archaic) lame people collectively
archaic

Examples

In ancient times, the halt were often marginalized by society.
The parable mentioned helping the halt and the blind.
Charity was extended to the halt as well as to the poor and orphaned.

Synset Examples

the poor, and the maimed, and the halt, and the blind

Etymology

From Old English 'healt' meaning 'lame'.
