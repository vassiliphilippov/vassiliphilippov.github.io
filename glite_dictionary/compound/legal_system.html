legal system

1. NOUN

/ ˈliːɡəl ˈsɪstəm / (AmE), / ˈliːɡəl ˈsɪstəm / (BrE)
The system of rules, institutions, and procedures of a country or region for interpreting and enforcing the law.
system for interpreting and enforcing the law

Examples

Every country has its own legal system that governs how laws are created and enforced.
The legal system ensures that disputes are resolved fairly and justly.
She studied the differences between the British and American legal system during her law degree.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: judiciary - the branch of government responsible for interpreting laws and administering justice
