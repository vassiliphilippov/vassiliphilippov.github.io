silent war

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪlənt wɔɚ / (AmE), / ˈsaɪlənt wɔː / (BrE)
a conflict or struggle that occurs without open acknowledgement or direct confrontation, often remaining unnoticed by others
unacknowledged conflict or struggle

Examples

The two departments have been engaged in a silent war for resources for years.
There is a silent war being waged against misinformation on social media platforms.
Many feel that a silent war exists between tradition and innovation in the company.

Synset Examples

Many argue that there's a silent war on women in the workplace.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cold war - a state of hostility without open warfare, especially between countries
  • Antonym: open conflict - a conflict or struggle that is openly acknowledged
