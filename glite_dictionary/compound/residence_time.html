residence time

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛzɪdəns taɪm / (AmE), / ˈrɛzɪdəns taɪm / (BrE)
the period during which something remains in a particular place, especially before moving on or being replaced, often used in technical or scientific contexts.
period something stays in a place

Examples

The residence time of water in the reservoir is approximately six months.
Air pollution models often account for the residence time of particles in the atmosphere.
Longer residence time can lead to greater exposure to toxic substances.
