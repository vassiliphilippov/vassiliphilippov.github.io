Chinese mustard

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃaɪˌniz ˈmʌstərd / (AmE), / ˈtʃaɪniːz ˈmʌstəd / (BrE)
a type of very hot mustard made from ground mustard seeds, commonly used in Chinese cuisine as a condiment
very hot mustard used in Chinese cuisine

Examples

She dipped her egg roll into the Chinese mustard for an extra kick of heat.
The chef added a spoonful of Chinese mustard to the sauce for bold flavor.
Be careful with Chinese mustard; it's spicier than you might expect.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mustard - a condiment made from the seeds of the mustard plant

2. NOUN

/ ˈtʃaɪˌniz ˈmʌstərd / (AmE), / ˈtʃaɪniːz ˈmʌstəd / (BrE)
A leafy plant of Asian origin, grown for its pungent leaves and used as a vegetable or seasoning in cooking.
pungent leafy vegetable used in Asian cuisine

Examples

You can stir-fry Chinese mustard with garlic for a quick and healthy side dish.
The spicy flavor of Chinese mustard enhances many traditional Asian soups and stews.
At the market, she chose fresh Chinese mustard to add to her noodle recipe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mustard greens - leafy greens from various mustard plants, used as a vegetable
