whole number

1. NOUN

/ hoʊl ˈnʌmbɚ / (AmE), / həʊl ˈnʌmbə / (BrE)
a number without fractions; an integer, which can be positive, negative, or zero
a number with no fractions (an integer)

Examples

Five is a whole number, not a fraction or decimal.
When you round 4.7 down, it becomes the whole number four.
Negative numbers like -3 are also considered whole numbers in mathematics.

Synset Examples

an integer is a number that is not a fraction

Related Terms

  • Synonym: integer - a whole number; a number with no fractional part
