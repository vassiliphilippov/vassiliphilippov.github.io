crocodilian reptile

1. NOUN

/ ˌkrɑːkəˈdɪliən ˈrɛptaɪl / (AmE), / ˌkrɒkəˈdɪliən ˈrɛptaɪl / (BrE)
any large, predatory, semi-aquatic reptile of the order Crocodylia, such as crocodiles, alligators, caimans, and gharials.
large predatory semi-aquatic reptile of Crocodylia

Examples

The Nile is home to several species of crocodilian reptile, including the fearsome Nile crocodile.
A crocodilian reptile can live both in water and on land, stalking its prey with patience.
Scientists are studying the evolution of the hearing organs in crocodilian reptiles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crocodile - large predatory reptile of tropical regions
  • Synonym: alligator - large reptile similar to a crocodile, native to the Americas and China
  • Antonym: lizard - any reptile of the order Squamata, typically with four legs

Etymology

From modern Latin Crocodylia (order name), based on Greek krokodilos (crocodile) + Latin reptilis (creeping).
