board of appeals

1. NOUN

/ bɔrd əv əˈpilz / (AmE), / bɔːd əv əˈpiːlz / (BrE)
A group of appointed officials, not part of the judiciary, who are responsible for hearing and deciding appeals against decisions made by authorities or organizations.
panel that hears appeals (non-judicial)

Examples

The city's board of appeals overturned the zoning decision after a lengthy public hearing.
Property owners can request a review from the board of appeals if their building permit is denied.
The new policy will be subject to review by the board of appeals before being finalized.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: appeals board - a group that hears appeals in administrative settings
