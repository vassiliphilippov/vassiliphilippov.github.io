grey area

1. NOUN

/ ɡreɪ ˈɛriə / (AmE), / ɡreɪ ˈeəriə / (BrE)
an ill-defined or intermediate area that is not clearly one thing or another, often referring to uncertainty or ambiguity in rules or categories
unclear or ambiguous area

Examples

The law regarding online privacy is still a grey area in many countries.
Ethical issues in artificial intelligence are a grey area for many researchers.
Many of the company's policies fall into a grey area and are open to interpretation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gray area - American English spelling of 'grey area'
  • Antonym: black and white - clear and unambiguous, with no shades of ambiguity

2. NOUN

/ ɡreɪ ˈɛriə / (AmE), / ɡreɪ ˈeəriə / (BrE)
a situation or topic that is not clearly defined, making it difficult to determine what is right or acceptable
ambiguous or unclear situation

Examples

Navigating the grey area between personal freedom and public safety can be challenging for lawmakers.
The contract has some grey areas that led to confusion between the parties.
Ethical decisions often fall into a grey area where right and wrong aren't easily distinguished.

Synset Examples

He navigated the grey area of ethics in his research.
There's a grey area in the law regarding online privacy.
The policy has some grey areas that need clarification.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gray area - American spelling of 'grey area'
  • Antonym: black-and-white - clearly defined or categorized situation, without ambiguity
