core histone

1. NOUN

/ kɔr ˈhɪstoʊn / (AmE), / kɔː ˈhɪstəʊn / (BrE)
any of a group of basic proteins (H2A, H2B, H3, and H4) that form the central part of the nucleosome in chromatin structure by binding to DNA
basic protein forming nucleosome core in DNA
medical

Examples

The core histone proteins play a crucial role in the organization of chromatin.
Scientists identified modifications on each core histone during their experiments.
Mutations in a core histone can disrupt normal gene expression.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: linker histone - histone H1, involved in packaging nucleosomes
  • Degree Higher: histone - any of a group of basic proteins found in chromatin

Etymology

From 'core' meaning central, and 'histone', from German 'Histon', coined by Albrecht Kossel.
