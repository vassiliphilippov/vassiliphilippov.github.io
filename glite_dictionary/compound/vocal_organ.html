vocal organ

1. NOUN

/ ˈvoʊkəl ˈɔrɡən / (AmE), / ˈvəʊkəl ˈɔːɡən / (BrE)
any of the organs, such as the larynx, tongue, lips, or palate, involved in producing speech sounds.
organ involved in speech production

Examples

The larynx is a major vocal organ responsible for producing sound.
Damage to any vocal organ can affect a person's ability to speak clearly.
Speech therapists study the function of each vocal organ to help patients recover their voices.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: larynx - voice box in the throat involved in speech
