brush-tailed phalanger

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrʌʃ teɪld fəˈlæŋɡər / (AmE), / ˈbrʌʃ teɪld fəˈlæŋɡə / (BrE)
A marsupial mammal from Australia and New Guinea, known for its bushy tail; also called a brushtail possum.
Australian/New Guinean marsupial with a bushy tail

Examples

The brush-tailed phalanger is a familiar sight in urban gardens across Australia.
Conservationists are studying the habits of the brush-tailed phalanger to protect native forests.
At night, the brush-tailed phalanger searches for leaves and fruit in the treetops.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brushtail possum - Common name for brush-tailed phalanger, Australian marsupial
