Turk's head

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɜrks hɛd / (AmE), / ˈtɜːks hɛd / (BrE)
an ornamental knot that forms a braided, cylindrical pattern resembling a small turban, often used for decoration or grip on handles and ropes
decorative braided knot resembling a turban

Examples

The sailor tied a Turk's head around his knife handle for a better grip.
She decorated the walking stick with a neat Turk's head at the top.
Making a Turk's head knot requires patience and careful attention to detail.

Etymology

Named for its resemblance to a traditional Turkish turban.
