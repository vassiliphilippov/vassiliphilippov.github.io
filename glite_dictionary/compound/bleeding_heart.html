bleeding heart

1. NOUN

/ ˈbliːdɪŋ hɑːrt / (AmE), / ˈbliːdɪŋ hɑːt / (BrE)
A garden plant with arching stems bearing deep-pink, drooping, heart-shaped flowers, commonly grown for ornamental purposes.
garden plant with heart-shaped pink flowers

Examples

My grandmother planted a row of bleeding hearts along the fence in her backyard.
In early spring, the bleeding heart produces stunning arching sprays of pink blossoms.
Because of its delicate flowers, the bleeding heart is a favorite in cottage gardens.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbliːdɪŋ hɑrt / (AmE), / ˈbliːdɪŋ hɑːt / (BrE)
a person who shows excessive sympathy for people who are viewed as exploited or underprivileged, often with a tone of criticism or disapproval
overly sympathetic or compassionate person

Examples

The columnist dismissed the activists as just another bleeding heart trying to save the world.
He was called a bleeding heart by critics who thought he cared too much about social issues.
Some politicians use the term bleeding heart to criticize those who support welfare programs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sentimentalist - a person who is excessively prone to feelings of tenderness, sadness, or nostalgia
  • Antonym: hard-hearted - lacking sympathy or compassion

Etymology

Figurative use of 'heart' to suggest emotion, with 'bleeding' intensifying the sentiment; originally used in the 20th century as a critical term.
