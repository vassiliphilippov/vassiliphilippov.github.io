corkwood tree

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔɹkwʊd triː / (AmE), / ˈkɔːkwʊd triː / (BrE)
A small deciduous tree or shrub of damp areas in the southeastern United States, noted for its very light wood.
tree or shrub with very light wood (southeastern US)

Examples

The corkwood tree grows in wetlands across the southeastern states, thriving in marshy conditions.
Due to its extremely light wood, the corkwood tree is not widely used for construction.
In some regions, the corkwood tree is also cultivated as an ornamental shrub.

Etymology

From 'corkwood', referring to the lightness of the wood, and 'tree'.
