sweet gum

1. NOUN

/ swit ɡʌm / (AmE), / swiːt ɡʌm / (BrE)
an aromatic resin obtained from the sweet gum tree, used in medicine, perfumes, and as a chewing gum base
aromatic resin from sweet gum tree

Examples

The indigenous people often used sweet gum as a natural remedy for wounds.
Collectors harvested sweet gum for use in early perfumes and medicines.
Chewing sweet gum was common before commercial chewing gum was invented.

2. NOUN

/ swit ɡʌm / (AmE), / swiːt ɡʌm / (BrE)
a North American deciduous tree (genus Liquidambar) with prickly, spherical fruit clusters and fragrant sap, valued for its wood and ornamental use
North American tree with prickly fruit and fragrant sap

Examples

A row of sweet gum trees lines the path, their star-shaped leaves turning bright red in autumn.
The prickly balls from the sweet gum littered the ground after a windy night.
Gardeners appreciate the sweet gum for its colorful foliage and distinctive fruit clusters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: liquidambar - genus of trees including sweet gum
