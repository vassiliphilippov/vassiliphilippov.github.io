benzene formula

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛnˌziːn ˈfɔr·mjə·lə / (AmE), / ˈbɛn.ziːn ˈfɔː.mjʊ.lə / (BrE)
the chemical structure representing benzene as a closed ring of six carbon atoms, each attached to a hydrogen atom
chemical structure of benzene

Examples

The benzene formula shows a six-membered carbon ring with alternating double bonds.
August Kekulé first proposed the cyclic benzene formula in the nineteenth century.
Chemists often use the hexagon with a circle inside to represent the benzene formula.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: benzene - an aromatic hydrocarbon with the formula C6H6
