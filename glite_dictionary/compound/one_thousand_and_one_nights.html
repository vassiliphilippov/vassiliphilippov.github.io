One Thousand and One Nights

1. PROPN

/ wʌn ˈθaʊzənd ənd wʌn ˈnaɪts / (AmE), / wʌn ˈθaʊzənd ənd wʌn ˈnaɪts / (BrE)
A collection of Middle Eastern folktales in Arabic, dating from the 10th century, also known as the Arabian Nights.
collection of Middle Eastern folktales

Examples

My grandmother used to read me stories from the One Thousand and One Nights before bedtime.
Many modern tales have been inspired by the legends in the One Thousand and One Nights.
The One Thousand and One Nights features famous characters like Aladdin and Sinbad.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Arabian Nights - Alternative title for 'One Thousand and One Nights'

Etymology

English title calqued from Arabic 'Alf laylah wa-laylah' (ألف ليلة وليلة, 'a thousand nights and a night').
