cadmium sulfide

1. NOUN

/ ˈkædmiəm ˈsʌlfaɪd / (AmE), / ˈkadmɪəm ˈsʌlfaɪd / (BrE)
a yellow inorganic compound (CdS) used mainly as a pigment in paints and plastics
yellow pigment compound (CdS)

Examples

The artist chose cadmium sulfide to give the painting a vibrant yellow color.
Industrial chemists use cadmium sulfide in the manufacture of certain types of solar cells.
Prolonged exposure to cadmium sulfide dust can be hazardous to health.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cadmium yellow - a bright yellow pigment containing cadmium sulfide

Etymology

Cadmium from Latin *cadmia* (calamine, zinc carbonate); sulfide from Latin *sulphur* (sulfur).
