ring thrush

1. NOUN

/ rɪŋ θrʌʃ / (AmE), / rɪŋ θrʌʃ / (BrE)
A European thrush (Turdus torquatus), commonly found in rocky areas, the male of which is distinguished by blackish plumage and a white band around the neck.
European thrush with a white neck band

Examples

The ring thrush is often spotted in upland areas of northern Europe.
Male ring thrushes can be recognized by their distinctive white neck band.
During migration, birdwatchers sometimes see a ring thrush among flocks of other thrushes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ring ouzel - a European thrush with a conspicuous white crescent on the breast
