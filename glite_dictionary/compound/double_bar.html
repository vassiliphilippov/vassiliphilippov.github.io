double bar

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʌbəl bɑr / (AmE), / ˈdʌbəl bɑː / (BrE)
A pair of adjacent vertical lines in musical notation used to mark the end of a principal section or the entire musical piece.
music: two adjacent bar lines marking section end

Examples

The song ended with a double bar at the bottom of the last page.
Please mark the section break with a double bar in your score.
He drew a double bar to indicate the conclusion of the piece.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bar line - a single vertical line in musical notation separating measures
