family Pristidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæmli prɪˈstɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfamli prɪˈstɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of large, primitive rays, known as sawfishes, distinguished by their long, flattened, saw-like snouts
a family of rays with saw-like snouts
medical

Examples

The family Pristidae includes several species of sawfishes found in tropical and subtropical waters.
Members of the family Pristidae are easily recognized by their elongated, toothed snouts.
Conservation efforts are underway to protect endangered species within the family Pristidae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sawfish - a type of large marine ray with a long, saw-like snout

Etymology

From New Latin 'Pristidae', based on Greek 'pristis' (sawfish).
