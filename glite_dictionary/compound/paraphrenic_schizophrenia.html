paraphrenic schizophrenia

1. NOUN

/ ˌpærəˈfrɛnɪk skɪtsəˈfriːniə / (AmE), / ˌparəˈfrɛnɪk skɪtsəˈfriːnɪə / (BrE)
a form of schizophrenia marked by persistent delusions, often of persecution, grandeur, or jealousy, where affected individuals typically remain socially presentable and capable of functioning in daily life if their delusions are not acted upon
a type of schizophrenia with persistent delusions but preserved social functioning
medical

Examples

Despite experiencing paraphrenic schizophrenia, he was able to maintain steady employment and social relationships.
Doctors noted that her paraphrenic schizophrenia was characterized by elaborate delusions but little impairment in outward appearance.
The treatment approach for paraphrenic schizophrenia often differs due to patients' relatively preserved social functioning.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: schizophrenia - a severe mental disorder characterized by distortions of reality and thought

Etymology

From Greek 'para-' (beside, abnormal) and 'phren' (mind), and 'schizophrenia', coined in medical terminology.
