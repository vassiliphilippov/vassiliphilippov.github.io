iron fist approach

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪərn fɪst əˈproʊtʃ / (AmE), / ˈaɪən fɪst əˈprəʊtʃ / (BrE)
a strict, harsh, or uncompromising method of handling situations or enforcing rules
strict or harsh method of enforcement

Examples

The government decided to use an iron fist approach to tackle rising crime rates.
His iron fist approach alienated many employees who preferred a more flexible management style.
While some praised her iron fist approach, others felt it was unnecessarily severe.

Synset Examples

He dealt with conflicts using an iron fist approach.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: firm hand - controlling or managing something with strength and determination
  • Antonym: velvet glove - a gentle or diplomatic way of handling things, sometimes masking firmness

Etymology

From the figurative use of 'iron fist' (meaning uncompromising enforcement), combined with 'approach.'
