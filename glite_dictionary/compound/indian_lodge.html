Indian lodge

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪndiən lɑdʒ / (AmE), / ˈɪndɪən lɒdʒ / (BrE)
a traditional dwelling used by certain Native American peoples, such as a tepee, wigwam, or other structure
traditional Native American dwelling

Examples

The tribe built an Indian lodge near the river to shelter themselves during the winter months.
Visitors to the museum admired the intricate structure of the reconstructed Indian lodge.
Anthropologists studied the different designs and materials used in each Indian lodge.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tepee - a cone-shaped tent used by some Native American peoples
  • Synonym: wigwam - a domed or round shelter used by certain Native American groups
