ribonucleic acid

1. NOUN

/ ˌraɪboʊnjuːˌkleɪɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / ˌraɪbəʊnjuːˌkliːɪk ˈæsɪd / (BrE)
A nucleic acid present in all living cells, playing a vital role in transmitting genetic information and in various cellular processes.
nucleic acid molecule in cells
medical

Examples

Ribonucleic acid plays a central role in translating genetic instructions into cellular functions.
Scientists discovered that some viruses use ribonucleic acid as their genetic material instead of DNA.
The process of protein synthesis relies on multiple types of ribonucleic acid molecules.

Synset Examples

ribonucleic acid is the genetic material of some viruses

Related Terms

  • Synonym: RNA - abbreviation for ribonucleic acid
  • Antonym: deoxyribonucleic acid - nucleic acid carrying genetic instructions (DNA)

Etymology

From ribose (a sugar), nucleic, and acid; named for its chemical structure and relation to nucleic acids.
