death panel

1. NOUN

/ dɛθ ˈpænl / (AmE), / dɛθ ˈpanəl / (BrE)
a supposedly authoritative group that decides whether individuals will be granted or denied medical care, especially concerning end-of-life treatment.
group alleged to decide end-of-life care
american

Examples

Critics of the policy warned that it could lead to the creation of so-called death panels.
The term death panel emerged during debates over healthcare reform in the United States.
Many experts argued that the idea of a death panel misrepresents how end-of-life decisions are actually made.

Synset Examples

The controversial bill included provisions for a death panel.
