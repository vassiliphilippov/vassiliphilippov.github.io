spinach mustard

1. NOUN

/ ˈspɪnɪtʃ ˈmʌstərd / (AmE), / ˈspɪnɪtʃ ˈmʌstəd / (BrE)
An Asiatic plant grown for its edible swollen root crown and foliage, often used in salads and cooking.
Asian plant with edible leaves and root crown

Examples

Stir-fried spinach mustard is a popular dish in many Asian cuisines.
She bought fresh spinach mustard at the farmer's market for tonight's dinner.
The garden's new addition, spinach mustard, thrives best in cool weather.
