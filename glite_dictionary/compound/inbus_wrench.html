Inbus wrench

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪnbəs rɛntʃ / (AmE), / ˈɪnbʌs rɛntʃ / (BrE)
a type of wrench with a hexagonal cross-section used for turning Allen screws
hexagonal wrench for Allen screws

Examples

The bike mechanic used an Inbus wrench to adjust the seat height.
Be sure to bring the correct size Inbus wrench when you assemble the furniture.
I couldn't find my Inbus wrench, so I borrowed one from my neighbor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Allen wrench - a wrench with a hexagonal tip for turning Allen screws

Etymology

From German 'INBUS' (acronym for 'Innensechskantschraube Bauer und Schaurte'), a brand name that became genericized in some regions.
