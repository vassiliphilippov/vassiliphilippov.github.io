loony antics

1. NOUN

/ ˈluni ˈæn.tɪks / (AmE), / ˈluːni ˈæn.tɪks / (BrE)
crazy or silly behavior, especially actions that are wild or foolish and attract attention
crazy or silly behavior
informal

Examples

Everyone was entertained by his loony antics at the talent show.
The comedian's loony antics brought laughter to the audience all night.
Parents sometimes get embarrassed by their children's loony antics in public.

Synset Examples

Her loony antics always make the party fun.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shenanigans - mischievous or silly behavior
  • Synonym: pranks - playful or mischievous tricks or actions

Etymology

'Loony' is derived from 'lunatic', originally from Latin 'luna' (moon), once thought to influence madness; 'antics' comes from Italian 'antico' (antique), referring to grotesque or fanciful gestures.
