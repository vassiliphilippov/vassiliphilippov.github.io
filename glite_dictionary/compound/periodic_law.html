periodic law

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɪriˈɑdɪk lɔ / (AmE), / ˌpɪəriˈɒdɪk lɔː / (BrE)
the principle in chemistry stating that the properties of elements recur periodically when they are arranged by increasing atomic number
principle that element properties repeat periodically

Examples

The periodic law explains why elements in the same group have similar chemical properties.
As new elements were discovered, the periodic law helped scientists predict their behavior.
Understanding the periodic law is essential for mastering basic chemistry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: periodic table - an arrangement of chemical elements by increasing atomic number

Etymology

From 'periodic' (recurring at intervals) and 'law' (principle), referring to recurrence of properties in the periodic table.
