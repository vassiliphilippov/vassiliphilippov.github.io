French Oceania

1. PROPN

/ frɛntʃ oʊˈʃiːniə / (AmE), / frentʃ əʊˈsiːnɪə / (BrE)
the former name for a French overseas territory in the South Pacific, now known as French Polynesia
former name for French Polynesia

Examples

During the early 20th century, French Oceania was an important stopover for explorers in the Pacific.
The islands that made up French Oceania are now collectively called French Polynesia.
The governor of French Oceania introduced policies that affected trade in the region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: French Polynesia - modern name for the former French Oceania

Etymology

From French 'Océanie française', referring to the French colonial possession in Oceania.
