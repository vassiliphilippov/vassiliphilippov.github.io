imperfect tense

1. NOUN

/ ɪmˈpɚfɪkt tɛns / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɜːfɪkt tɛns / (BrE)
a verb tense used to describe an ongoing or repeated action in the past
verb tense for ongoing past action

Examples

In Latin, the imperfect tense is used to indicate actions that were ongoing in the past.
Spanish verbs have a distinct imperfect tense for describing habitual past actions.
When teaching French, she explained how the imperfect tense is different from the simple past.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: past tense - verb tense expressing action that has already happened

Etymology

From Latin 'imperfectum' (unfinished, incomplete) + 'tense' from Latin 'tempus' (time).
