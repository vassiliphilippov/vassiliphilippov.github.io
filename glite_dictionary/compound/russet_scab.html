russet scab

1. NOUN

/ ˈrʌsɪt skæb / (AmE), / ˈrʌsɪt skæb / (BrE)
a disease of potatoes caused by fungi of the genus Rhizoctonia, resulting in rough, brownish patches on the tuber surface
potato disease causing brown patches

Examples

The farmer discovered russet scab developing on several rows of potatoes after a wet spring.
Careful crop rotation can help prevent russet scab from affecting your potato yield.
Signs of russet scab include superficial, corky patches on potato skins.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: potato scab - another disease causing similar scabbing on potatoes
