big science

1. NOUN

/ bɪɡ ˈsaɪəns / (AmE), / bɪɡ ˈsaɪəns / (BrE)
scientific research involving large-scale projects, significant funding, and collaboration, typically aimed at achieving major breakthroughs or results
large-scale, heavily funded scientific research

Examples

The Human Genome Project is a prime example of big science in action.
Government funding is often essential for big science initiatives like particle accelerators.
Some critics argue that big science can overshadow smaller, innovative research efforts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: megaproject - a large-scale project requiring substantial resources

Etymology

Originated in the mid-20th century, describing the shift to large, collaborative scientific projects, notably those during and after World War II.
