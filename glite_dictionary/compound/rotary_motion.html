rotary motion

1. NOUN

/ ˈroʊˌtɛri ˈmoʊʃən / (AmE), / ˈrəʊtəri ˈməʊʃən / (BrE)
movement in which an object rotates around an axis
movement revolving around an axis

Examples

The engine converts fuel into rotary motion to drive the wheels.
A ceiling fan produces a steady rotary motion as it spins above the room.
The magician's trick relied on the smooth rotary motion of the concealed platform.

Synset Examples

the rotation of the dancer kept time with the music

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rotation - the act of rotating as if on an axis

Etymology

From Latin 'rota' (wheel) and 'motion', influenced by the scientific vocabulary of the 17th century.
