malignant influence

1. NOUN

/ məˈlɪɡnənt ˈɪnfluəns / (AmE), / məˈlɪɡnənt ˈɪnflʊəns / (BrE)
a harmful or evil effect or power that corrupts or damages individuals or situations
harmful or evil effect; corrupting power

Examples

There was a malignant influence at work that slowly undermined the team's morale.
Some believe that social media can exert a malignant influence on young people.
The detective suspected a malignant influence behind the town's recent troubles.

Synset Examples

The community feared the malignant influence spreading through the area.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: benign influence - a gentle or beneficial effect or power
