paracentral scotoma

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛr.əˈsɛn.trəl skəˈtoʊ.mə / (AmE), / ˌpar.əˈsɛn.trəl skəˈtəʊ.mə / (BrE)
A blind spot in the visual field that is located near, but not at, the center of vision (the fixation point).
blind spot near central vision
medical

Examples

The patient presented with a paracentral scotoma that made reading difficult.
Early glaucoma can cause a paracentral scotoma in the visual field.
Her visual test revealed a small paracentral scotoma near her line of sight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scotoma - an area of partial or complete loss of vision surrounded by a field of normal vision

Etymology

From Greek 'para' (beside) + Latin 'centralis' (central) + Greek 'skotoma' (darkness, dimness of vision).
