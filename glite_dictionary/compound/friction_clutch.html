friction clutch

1. NOUN

/ ˈfrɪkʃən klʌtʃ / (AmE), / ˈfrɪkʃən klʌtʃ / (BrE)
a type of clutch in which one component transmits motion to another through frictional contact between their surfaces
clutch using friction to transmit motion

Examples

The car's friction clutch allows the driver to change gears smoothly while driving.
A worn-out friction clutch can cause slipping and loss of power transmission.
Technicians inspected the friction clutch for signs of overheating and excessive wear.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clutch - mechanical device that connects and disconnects power transmission

Etymology

From 'friction', denoting rubbing, and 'clutch', a mechanical device for connecting and disconnecting a driving and a driven part.
