chain reaction

1. NOUN

/ tʃeɪn riˈækʃən / (AmE), / tʃeɪn riˈækʃən / (BrE)
A self-sustaining nuclear reaction in which the products of one event cause further similar events, especially where neutrons released by nuclear fission trigger more fissions.
self-sustaining nuclear reaction process

Examples

The explosion was caused by a chain reaction set off in the nuclear reactor.
A single neutron can start a chain reaction that releases enormous amounts of energy.
Scientists carefully monitor the chain reaction to prevent it from getting out of control.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nuclear fission - the splitting of a nucleus into smaller parts accompanied by energy release

Etymology

From 'chain', meaning a connected series, and 'reaction', from Latin 'reactio' (return, reaction).

2. NOUN

/ tʃeɪn riˈækʃən / (AmE), / tʃeɪn riˈækʃən / (BrE)
A series of chemical or nuclear reactions in which the product of one reaction initiates the next in the sequence.
series of linked chemical or nuclear reactions

Examples

The nuclear explosion was the result of a self-sustaining chain reaction.
Polymerization often proceeds through a chain reaction mechanism in which active sites propagate the process.
Scientists carefully control conditions in reactors to maintain a safe chain reaction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: domino effect - a sequence of events where one event triggers the next

Etymology

From 'chain' (series of linked elements) and 'reaction' (chemical process); term popularized in the early 20th century in physics and chemistry.
