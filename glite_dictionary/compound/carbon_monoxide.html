carbon monoxide

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑrbən məˈnɑksaɪd / (AmE), / ˌkɑːbən ˌmɒˈnɒksaɪd / (BrE)
A colorless, odorless, and highly poisonous gas (CO) produced by the incomplete combustion of carbon-containing materials.
poisonous, colorless gas (CO)

Examples

Leaving your car running in a closed garage produces dangerous levels of carbon monoxide.
Faulty furnaces can leak carbon monoxide, which is hazardous to your health.
Detectors are required in many homes to alert residents to carbon monoxide poisoning risks.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: carbon dioxide - a colorless, odorless gas (CO₂) produced by burning carbon and organic compounds

Etymology

From 'carbon' and 'monoxide' (mono- 'one' + oxide), indicating it is an oxide of carbon with one oxygen atom.
