linear measure

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪniɚ ˈmɛʒɚ / (AmE), / ˈlɪniə ˈmɛʒə / (BrE)
a unit used for measuring length or distance, such as the meter, inch, or mile
unit for measuring length or distance

Examples

The meter is a linear measure used in the metric system.
When sewing, a tape measure provides an accurate linear measure of fabric.
In the United States, the inch and foot are commonly used as units of linear measure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: length - the measurement or extent of something from end to end
  • Antonym: area - a measure of a surface; a unit expressing the size of a surface

Etymology

From 'linear' (relating to length) and 'measure' (a unit or standard of measurement).
