blacktip shark

1. NOUN

/ ˈblæk.tɪp ʃɑrk / (AmE), / ˈblæk.tɪp ʃɑːk / (BrE)
a species of small, widely distributed shark found in shallow waters, noted for the black tips on its fins
a small shark with black-tipped fins

Examples

Snorkelers often admire the graceful movements of the blacktip shark swimming near coral reefs.
The blacktip shark gets its name from the dark markings on the tips of its fins.
Despite being common in shallow waters, the blacktip shark rarely poses a threat to humans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shark - large group of cartilaginous fish with a streamlined body and sharp teeth
