gutta-percha tree

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡʌtəˈpɝːtʃə triː / (AmE), / ˌɡʌtəˈpɜːtʃə triː / (BrE)
an East Indian tree (Palaquium gutta) that produces gutta-percha, a latex used in insulation and dentistry
tree yielding gutta-percha latex

Examples

The latex extracted from the gutta-percha tree is used in dental procedures.
During the colonial era, the gutta-percha tree became an important export in Southeast Asia.
Botanists studied the gutta-percha tree for its unique medicinal properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Palaquium gutta - scientific name for the gutta-percha tree

Etymology

‘Gutta-percha’ from Malay ‘getah perca’ meaning 'percha sap'; scientific name from Latin and local names.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɡʌtəˈpɜrtʃə tri / (AmE), / ˌɡʌtəˈpɜːtʃə triː / (BrE)
an East Indian tree, especially those of the genus Palaquium, that yields gutta-percha, a rubbery substance used in industry
tree yielding gutta-percha (East Indies)

Examples

The gutta-percha tree is valued for its latex, which is used in various industrial applications.
Early telegraph wires were insulated with a material extracted from the gutta-percha tree.
Botanists study the gutta-percha tree for its unique properties and environmental significance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Palaquium - genus of tropical trees yielding gutta-percha

Etymology

From Malay 'getah perca' (gutta-percha), originally applied to the resin produced by these trees.
