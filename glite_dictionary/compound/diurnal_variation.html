diurnal variation

1. NOUN

/ daɪˈɝnəl ˌvɛriˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / daɪˈɜːnəl ˌveərɪˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
regular changes or fluctuations that occur during each day, especially over a 24-hour period
daily fluctuations or changes

Examples

The temperature in deserts often shows a marked diurnal variation, with hot days and cold nights.
Doctors measure diurnal variation in blood pressure to assess certain health conditions.
Many plants open and close their flowers in response to diurnal variation in sunlight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: circadian rhythm - the physical, mental, and behavioral changes that occur on a 24-hour cycle

Etymology

From Latin 'diurnus' (daily) and 'variation' (change, difference).
