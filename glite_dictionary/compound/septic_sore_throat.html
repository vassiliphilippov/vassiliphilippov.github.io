septic sore throat

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛptɪk sɔr θroʊt / (AmE), / ˈsɛptɪk sɔː θrəʊt / (BrE)
A severe infection of the throat and tonsils caused by streptococcal bacteria, often accompanied by fever and inflammation.
streptococcal infection of throat and tonsils
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed him with a septic sore throat after examining his swollen tonsils.
Untreated septic sore throat can lead to serious complications such as rheumatic fever.
She was admitted to the hospital because her septic sore throat had caused a high fever and difficulty swallowing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strep throat - throat infection caused by Streptococcus bacteria

Etymology

From 'septic' (relating to infection by microorganisms) and 'sore throat.' 'Septic' from Greek 'sēptikos' (putrefactive).
