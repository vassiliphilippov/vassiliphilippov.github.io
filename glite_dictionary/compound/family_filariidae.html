family Filariidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli fɪˌlɛriˈaɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli fɪˌlɛərɪˈaɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of threadlike parasitic roundworms that can infect humans and other vertebrates, some species causing diseases such as filariasis
a family of threadlike parasitic roundworms
medical

Examples

The family Filariidae includes several species known to cause dangerous diseases in humans.
Scientists discovered a new species within the family Filariidae during their expedition.
Members of the family Filariidae are transmitted to hosts by various types of insects.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the genus name 'Filaria' and the family suffix '-idae'.
