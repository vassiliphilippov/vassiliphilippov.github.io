tethered to

1. ADJ

/ ˈtɛðərd tu / (AmE), / ˈtɛðəd tʊ / (BrE)
secured or attached to something by means of a tether or similar restraint
secured or attached to something

Examples

The dog was tethered to a post in the backyard so it wouldn't run away.
During the storm, the boat remained tethered to the dock for safety.
She felt tethered to her responsibilities and unable to travel freely.

Synset Examples

The astronaut was tethered to the spacecraft during the spacewalk.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: untethered - not tied or secured to anything

Etymology

From 'tether', from Old Norse 'tjōðr', meaning a rope or chain for tying.
