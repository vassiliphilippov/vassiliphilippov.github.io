anterior cardinal vein

1. NOUN

/ ænˈtɪriər ˈkɑːrdɪnəl veɪn / (AmE), / ænˈtɪəriə ˈkɑːdɪnəl veɪn / (BrE)
a major vein in vertebrate embryos that drains blood from the head region toward the heart
major embryonic vein draining the head
medical

Examples

The anterior cardinal vein develops early in the embryo to drain the cranial region.
Blood from the embryonic head is transported by the anterior cardinal vein.
Researchers studied the formation of the anterior cardinal vein in developing zebrafish.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: posterior cardinal vein - a vein carrying blood from the posterior part of the body in vertebrate embryos

Etymology

From Latin: 'anterior' (before, in front), 'cardinal' (principal), and 'vein'.
