articles of incorporation

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːr.tɪ.kəlz əv ɪnˌkɔːr.pəˈreɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˈɑː.tɪ.klz əv ɪnˌkɔː.pəˈreɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
A legal document that establishes a corporation by setting out its basic information and is filed with a state government according to law.
legal document forming a corporation
legal

Examples

The company filed its articles of incorporation with the state before beginning business operations.
According to the articles of incorporation, three directors are required for the board.
Changes to the articles of incorporation must be approved by a majority of shareholders.

Etymology

From the word 'article' (from Latin articulus, 'a separate part or division'), and 'incorporation' (from Late Latin incorporatio, 'embodiment').
