chicory escarole

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɪkəri ɛˈskɑːroʊl / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪkəri ɪˈskɑːrəʊl / (BrE)
A variety of endive with broad, slightly bitter leaves with irregular frilled edges, used in salads and cooking.
variety of endive with frilled leaves

Examples

The salad was topped with fresh chicory escarole and a light vinaigrette.
Many chefs favor chicory escarole for its mild bitterness in mixed greens.
The market had bundles of crisp chicory escarole beside other leafy vegetables.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: endive - a leafy plant used in salads, related to chicory

Etymology

From French 'chicorée' (chicory) and Italian 'scarola' (escarole).
