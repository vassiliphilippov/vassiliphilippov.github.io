whizz-kid

1. NOUN

/ wɪz kɪd / (AmE), / wɪz kɪd / (BrE)
a young person who is very successful or skillful, especially in a career or particular field; someone whose career progresses rapidly
talented and successful young person
informal british

Examples

The young engineer was labeled a whizz-kid after inventing a groundbreaking new app.
As a whizz-kid in the finance world, she made partner at just 27.
The company hired a whizz-kid to modernize their outdated IT systems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prodigy - a person, especially a young one, endowed with exceptional abilities

Etymology

From 'whizz' (suggesting speed or cleverness) and 'kid' (informal for 'child' or 'young person').
