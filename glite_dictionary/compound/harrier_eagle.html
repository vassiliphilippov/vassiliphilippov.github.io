harrier eagle

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛriər ˈiːɡəl / (AmE), / ˈhæriə ˈiːɡl̩ / (BrE)
a large Old World bird of prey in the hawk family, intermediate in some features between typical hawks and eagles
large Old World bird of prey

Examples

The harrier eagle is found in parts of Africa, where it preys on small mammals and birds.
Unlike true eagles, the harrier eagle displays some features typical of hawks as well.
Birdwatchers were excited to spot a rare harrier eagle soaring above the plain.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: hawk - bird of prey with sharp vision and hooked beak
  • Degree Higher: eagle - large bird of prey with a massive hooked bill and long broad wings
