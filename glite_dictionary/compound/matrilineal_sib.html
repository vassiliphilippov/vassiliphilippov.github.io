matrilineal sib

1. NOUN

/ ˌmætrəˈlɪniəl sɪb / (AmE), / ˌmætrɪˈlɪniəl sɪb / (BrE)
a member of a group whose kinship is traced through the mother's line; one related on the mother's side
relative through mother's line

Examples

Anthropologists studied how property was passed down among the matrilineal sib members of the tribe.
In some cultures, only a matrilineal sib could inherit certain ceremonial duties.
She found out that her closest matrilineal sib lived in another village.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: patrilineal sib - a relative through the father's line

Etymology

From Latin mater (mother) and linea (line), plus sib (short for sibling).
