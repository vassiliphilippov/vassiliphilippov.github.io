bio-fertilizer

1. NOUN

/ ˌbaɪoʊˈfɜrtəˌlaɪzər / (AmE), / ˌbaɪəʊˈfəːtɪlaɪzə / (BrE)
A substance containing living microorganisms that, when applied to seeds, plants, or soil, enhances plant growth by increasing the supply or availability of essential nutrients.
organic substance with growth-promoting microorganisms

Examples

Organic farmers increasingly use bio-fertilizer to improve soil quality and crop yields.
The use of bio-fertilizer reduces the need for chemical fertilizers in sustainable agriculture.
Scientists are developing new formulations of bio-fertilizer to support plant growth under challenging conditions.

Synset Examples

Bio-fertilizers add nutrients through the natural processes of nitrogen fixation, solubilizing phosphorus, and stimulating plant growth through the synthesis of growth-promoting substances.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fertilizer - a substance added to soil to increase its fertility

Etymology

Formed from 'bio-' (relating to life) and 'fertilizer'.
