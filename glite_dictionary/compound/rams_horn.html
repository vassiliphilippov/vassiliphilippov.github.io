ram's horn

1. NOUN

/ ræmz hɔrn / (AmE), / ræmz hɔːn / (BrE)
A flowering plant native to the southern United States and Mexico, known for its large whitish or yellowish flowers mottled with purple and a long, curved beak.
a flowering plant with curved beak-like flowers

Examples

The ram's horn blooms each spring, displaying its distinctive curved flowers across the fields.
Gardeners in Texas often cultivate ram's horn for its striking appearance and resilience.
I spotted a ram's horn growing along the roadside, easily recognized by its long, beak-like blossom.
