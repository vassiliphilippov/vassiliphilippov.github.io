charm quark

1. NOUN

/ tʃɑrm kwɑrk / (AmE), / tʃɑːm kwɑːk / (BrE)
a type of elementary particle, one of the six flavors of quark, with an electric charge of +2/3 and a mass much greater than that of an electron
type of elementary quark particle

Examples

The discovery of the charm quark helped physicists explain certain particle interactions in the Standard Model.
Researchers at the accelerator detected evidence of a charm quark in the collision data.
Without the charm quark, our understanding of subatomic particles would be incomplete.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: strange quark - a type of quark with a negative strange quantum number
  • Degree Higher: bottom quark - a heavier quark also known as beauty quark
  • Degree Lower: up quark - a lightest type of quark with positive charge

Etymology

From 'charm,' referring to a property (charm quantum number) introduced in particle physics in the 1970s; 'quark' coined by Murray Gell-Mann from James Joyce's 'Finnegans Wake.'
