to Zionize the narrative

1. VERB

/ ˈzaɪ.əˌnaɪz ðə nəˈreɪ.tɪv / (AmE), / ˈzaɪ.əˌnaɪz ðə nəˈrəʊ.tɪv / (BrE)
to present or alter information, events, or history in a way that supports or promotes Zionist ideology
reshape accounts to fit Zionist ideology

Examples

Some historians argue that certain textbooks Zionize the narrative by omitting Palestinian perspectives.
Documentaries that Zionize the narrative may downplay controversies surrounding the creation of Israel.
Critics accuse the publication of consistently Zionizing the narrative in its coverage of Middle Eastern events.

Synset Examples

Efforts to Zionize the narrative are evident in media.

Etymology

From 'Zionize', formed from 'Zionism' + '-ize'. 'Zionism' from Hebrew 'Tziyyon' (Zion).
