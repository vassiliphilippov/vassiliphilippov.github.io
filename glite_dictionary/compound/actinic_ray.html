actinic ray

1. NOUN

/ ækˈtɪnɪk reɪ / (AmE), / ækˈtɪnɪk reɪ / (BrE)
Electromagnetic radiation, especially ultraviolet light, capable of inducing photochemical reactions.
radiation causing photochemical change

Examples

Prolonged exposure to actinic rays can cause materials to degrade over time.
The experiment required a source of actinic rays to initiate the chemical reaction.
Photography film is sensitive to actinic rays in the ultraviolet spectrum.

Etymology

From Greek aktis (ray, beam) and -ic, meaning 'pertaining to rays'.
