two-grain spelt

1. NOUN

/ ˌtuː ˈgreɪn ˈspɛlt / (AmE), / ˌtuː ˈɡreɪn ˈspɛlt / (BrE)
a hard red wheat (Triticum dicoccum) cultivated mainly in Russia and Germany, used chiefly as livestock feed in the United States
a type of hard red wheat

Examples

Farmers in eastern Europe often cultivate two-grain spelt because of its resilience to harsh climates.
The mill processes two-grain spelt into feed for livestock throughout the winter.
While rare in American diets, two-grain spelt is valued as a heritage grain in parts of Russia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: emmer - an ancient wheat (Triticum dicoccum) grown for food and fodder
