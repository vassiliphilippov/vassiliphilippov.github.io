labyrinthine vein

1. NOUN

/ ˌlæbəˈrɪnθin veɪn / (AmE), / ˌlæb.əˈrɪn.θiːn veɪn / (BrE)
a vein that drains blood from the inner ear
vein that drains inner ear
medical

Examples

The labyrinthine vein is essential for draining blood from the structures of the inner ear.
Damage to the labyrinthine vein can result in hearing and balance problems.
During surgery on the temporal bone, the labyrinthine vein must be carefully preserved.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vein - blood vessel carrying blood toward the heart

Etymology

From 'labyrinth' (inner ear structure) + '-ine' (relating to) + 'vein'.
