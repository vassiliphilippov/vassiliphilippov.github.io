kingdom Animalia

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɪŋdəm ˌænɪˈeɪliə / (AmE), / ˈkɪŋdəm ˌænɪˈeɪlɪə / (BrE)
the taxonomic kingdom that comprises all living and extinct animals
taxonomic group including all animals

Examples

Biologists classify humans, dogs, and birds as members of the kingdom Animalia.
Unlike plants, all animals belong to the kingdom Animalia and are multicellular.
Sponges are simple organisms, but they are still part of the kingdom Animalia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: animal - a living organism that feeds on organic matter, typically with specialized sense organs and nervous system

Etymology

From Latin 'animalia' (animals), used in biological classification.
