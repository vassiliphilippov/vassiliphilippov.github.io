Gospel of Matthew

1. PROPN

/ ˈɡɑspəl əv ˈmæθju / (AmE), / ˈɡɒspəl əv ˈmæθjuː / (BrE)
the first book of the New Testament in the Christian Bible, presenting the life and teachings of Jesus; includes the Sermon on the Mount
the first book of the New Testament

Examples

Many scholars study the Gospel of Matthew to understand the historical context of Christianity.
The Sermon on the Mount is a famous passage found in the Gospel of Matthew.
In church last Sunday, the priest read from the Gospel of Matthew about compassion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Gospel of Mark - the second book of the New Testament presenting another account of Jesus's life
  • Synonym: Gospel of Luke - the third book of the New Testament offering a distinct narrative of Jesus's life
  • Synonym: Gospel of John - the fourth book of the New Testament focusing on theological themes in Jesus's life

Etymology

Gospel from Old English 'gōdspel' (good news); Matthew from the Latin 'Matthaeus', from Greek 'Matthaios'.
