brooder pneumonia

1. NOUN

/ ˈbruːdər pəˈmoʊniə / (AmE), / ˈbruːdə pəˈməʊniə / (BrE)
a severe respiratory disease occurring as an acute, rapidly fatal pneumonia in young chickens and turkeys
acute respiratory disease in young poultry
medical

Examples

The veterinarian diagnosed the sudden deaths as brooder pneumonia among the young chicks.
Effective ventilation and hygiene are crucial to preventing outbreaks of brooder pneumonia in poultry farms.
Several turkey poults developed respiratory distress and were found to have brooder pneumonia.

Etymology

From 'brooder' (a heated enclosure for young poultry) and 'pneumonia' (from Latin and Greek, lung inflammation).
