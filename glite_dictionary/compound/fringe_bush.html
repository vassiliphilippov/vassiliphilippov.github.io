fringe bush

1. NOUN

/ frɪndʒ bʊʃ / (AmE), / frɪndʒ bʊʃ / (BrE)
A small, bushy tree native to the southeastern United States, known for its abundant clusters of white flowers.
tree with clusters of white flowers (US Southeast)

Examples

The fringe bush blooms beautifully in early spring, covering its branches with delicate white flowers.
Gardeners in Georgia often plant the fringe bush for its ornamental appeal and easy maintenance.
Several fringe bushes lined the edge of the property, attracting butterflies and bees.
