parula warbler

1. NOUN

/ pəˈruːlə ˈwɔːrb(ə)lɚ / (AmE), / pəˈruːlə ˈwɔːb(ə)lə / (BrE)
A small gray-blue wood warbler with a yellow throat and breast, native to eastern North America.
small North American warbler bird

Examples

The parula warbler is easily identified by its blue-gray plumage and bright yellow throat.
Birdwatchers travel to eastern forests in spring to spot the elusive parula warbler.
A parula warbler was observed building its nest high in the tangled branches.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: warbler - a small songbird, typically with a trilling song

Etymology

From the genus name Parula in New Latin, ultimately from Latin 'parulus', diminutive of 'parus' (titmouse, a type of small bird).
