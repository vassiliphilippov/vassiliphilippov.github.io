Cumberland Gap

1. PROPN

/ ˈkʌmbərlənd ˌɡæp / (AmE), / ˈkʌmbələnd ˌɡap / (BrE)
a mountain pass in the Cumberland Mountains between Kentucky, Virginia, and Tennessee, historically used by early settlers moving west
mountain pass between Kentucky, Virginia, and Tennessee

Examples

Early pioneers traveled through the Cumberland Gap to settle in Kentucky.
The Cumberland Gap played a crucial role in the westward expansion of the United States.
We visited the Cumberland Gap National Historical Park on our summer road trip.

Etymology

Named after the Duke of Cumberland; 'gap' refers to a mountain pass.
