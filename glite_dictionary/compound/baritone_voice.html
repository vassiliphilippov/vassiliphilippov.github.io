baritone voice

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛrɪˌtoʊn vɔɪs / (AmE), / ˈbærɪtəʊn vɔɪs / (BrE)
the second lowest adult male singing voice, lying between bass and tenor
second lowest adult male singing voice

Examples

The soloist sang in a rich baritone voice that filled the entire concert hall.
He was chosen for the lead role because of his deep baritone voice.
Many opera composers write parts specifically for singers with a baritone voice.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: tenor - highest adult male singing voice
  • Degree Lower: bass voice - lowest adult male singing voice
  • Synonym: baritone - adult male singing voice between bass and tenor

Etymology

From Italian 'baritono', from Greek 'barytonos' (deep-sounding), combining 'barys' (deep) and 'tonos' (tone).
