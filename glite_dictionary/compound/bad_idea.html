bad idea

1. NOUN

/ bæd aɪˈdiə / (AmE), / bad aɪˈdɪə / (BrE)
A choice, plan, or suggestion that is likely to have negative results or is considered unwise.
an unwise or poor choice

Examples

He realized it was a bad idea to skip breakfast before the exam.
Investing all your money in one stock is usually a bad idea.
Trusting her with the secret turned out to be a bad idea.

Synset Examples

Buying another pair of shoes felt like a bad idea.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: good idea - a wise or sensible choice
  • Synonym: mistake - an action or judgment that is wrong or misguided
