champagne flute

1. NOUN

/ ʃæmˈpeɪn fluːt / (AmE), / ʃæmˈpeɪn fluːt / (BrE)
a tall, narrow wineglass, typically used for serving champagne
tall narrow glass for champagne

Examples

She raised her champagne flute for a toast at the wedding.
The waiter brought a tray of champagne flutes filled with bubbly.
Each guest was handed a delicate champagne flute at the reception.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wineglass - a glass for drinking wine

Etymology

From French 'champagne' (a type of sparkling wine) and 'flute' (a slender glass).
