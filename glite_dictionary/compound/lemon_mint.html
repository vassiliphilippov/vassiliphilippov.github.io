lemon mint

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛ.mən mɪnt / (AmE), / ˈlɛm.ən mɪnt / (BrE)
an annual herb of central and western North America with lemon-scented leaves and purple terminal flower clusters, also called horsemint (Monarda citriodora)
lemon-scented annual herb (Monarda citriodora)

Examples

The garden was filled with the fragrance of lemon mint blooming in the summer sun.
Many herbal teas use lemon mint leaves for their refreshing citrus flavor.
Wild lemon mint attracts bees and butterflies to prairie landscapes.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɛmən mɪnt / (AmE), / ˈlɛmən mɪnt / (BrE)
a type of mint plant with leaves that have a citrus-like scent, similar to bergamot orange
a mint plant with a citrus scent

Examples

The refreshing scent of lemon mint filled the herb garden on a summer afternoon.
She added a few leaves of lemon mint to the salad for a subtle citrus flavor.
Lemon mint is often used in teas and drinks because of its fragrant aroma.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bergamot - an aromatic herb or its oil with a citrus scent
  • Degree Lower: mint - an aromatic herb of the family Lamiaceae
