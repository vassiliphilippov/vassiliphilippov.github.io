haemolytic anaemia

1. NOUN

/ ˌhiːməˈlɪtɪk əˈniːmiə / (AmE), / ˌhiːməˈlɪtɪk əˈniːmiə / (BrE)
A type of anaemia caused by the abnormal destruction of red blood cells (erythrocytes).
anaemia from destruction of red blood cells
medical british

Examples

The doctor diagnosed the patient with haemolytic anaemia after observing a rapid loss of red blood cells.
Certain medications can sometimes trigger haemolytic anaemia in susceptible individuals.
People with haemolytic anaemia may experience fatigue, jaundice, and shortness of breath.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hemolytic anemia - Anemia resulting from destruction of erythrocytes (US spelling)

Etymology

From Greek haima (blood) + lysis (loosening, dissolving) and anaemia, from Greek anaímos (without blood).
