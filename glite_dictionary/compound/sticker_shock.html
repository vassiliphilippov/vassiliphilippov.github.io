sticker shock

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɪkər ʃɑk / (AmE), / ˈstɪkə ʃɒk / (BrE)
A feeling of surprise or dismay at learning that something costs much more than expected.
surprise at very high prices

Examples

When she opened the utility bill this month, the sticker shock left her speechless.
Buyers often get sticker shock when shopping for new cars these days.
After seeing the rent for downtown apartments, his sticker shock was obvious.

Synset Examples

She felt sticker shock after checking the hotel rates.
The restaurant menu gave me sticker shock today.
I experienced sticker shock when I saw the car's price.
He walked out sticker-shocked from the electronics store.
They left the dealership sticker-shocked and reconsidering.
After the bill arrived, we were sticker-shocked and silent.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: price gouging - unfairly high pricing by sellers
  • Antonym: bargain - something bought for less than the usual price

Etymology

From 'sticker' (referring to a price label) and 'shock' (sudden surprise).
