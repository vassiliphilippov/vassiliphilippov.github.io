Italian vermouth

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈtæliən vərˈmuθ / (AmE), / ɪˈtælɪən vəˈmuːθ / (BrE)
a sweet, dark amber variety of vermouth, originally produced in Italy, commonly used in cocktails
sweet, dark Italian variety of vermouth

Examples

Many classic cocktails, such as the Negroni, call for Italian vermouth as a key ingredient.
He prefers Italian vermouth over the French variety because of its richer, sweeter flavor.
The bar ran out of Italian vermouth, so they substituted it with a dry version.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: French vermouth - a dry, pale variety of vermouth from France

Etymology

From Italian, denoting origin in Italy, and French *vermouth*, from German *Wermut* (wormwood).
