deepwater squirrelfish

1. NOUN

/ ˈdiːpˌwɔːtər ˈskwɪrəlˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈdiːpwɔːtə ˈskwɪrəlˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
a species of squirrelfish found in the western Atlantic, especially from South Carolina to Bermuda and the Gulf of Mexico
a fish species found in the western Atlantic

Examples

The deepwater squirrelfish is often seen hiding in rocky crevices during the day.
Marine biologists studied the habitat preferences of the deepwater squirrelfish along the Gulf Coast.
Anglers sometimes catch deepwater squirrelfish while fishing off the coast of Bermuda.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: squirrelfish - a type of brightly colored fish in the family Holocentridae
