order Psilophytales

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdər ˌsaɪloʊˈfaɪteɪlz / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə ˌsaɪləˈfaɪtiːlz / (BrE)
an extinct order of Paleozoic plants with simple dichotomously branched stems, found in Europe and eastern Canada, including some of the oldest vascular land plants
extinct group of ancient vascular plants

Examples

Fossils of order Psilophytales provide key evidence about the earliest vascular plants on Earth.
Botanists study order Psilophytales to understand the evolution of land plants.
Many specimens of order Psilophytales have been discovered in Silurian and Devonian rock formations.

Etymology

From New Latin: Psilo- (bare) + phyton (plant) + -ales (order).
