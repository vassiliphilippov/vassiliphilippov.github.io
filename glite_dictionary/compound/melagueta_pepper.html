melagueta pepper

1. NOUN

/ mɛləˈɡɛtə ˈpɛpər / (AmE), / mɛləˈɡiːtə ˈpɛpə / (BrE)
A West African plant (Aframomum melegueta) of the ginger family, valued for its pungent, peppery seeds used as a spice.
West African plant yielding pungent seeds

Examples

The chef added ground melagueta pepper to the stew for a spicy flavor.
In West Africa, melagueta pepper is commonly used in traditional medicines and cuisines.
The seeds of melagueta pepper are prized for their sharp, peppery taste.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grains of paradise - Spice made from the seeds of Aframomum melegueta

Etymology

From Portuguese 'malagueta', originally referring to various spicy plants, from Latin American Spanish and possibly related to Spanish 'malagueta' (allspice).
