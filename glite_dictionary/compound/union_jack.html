Union Jack

1. NOUN

/ ˈjuːniən ˈdʒæk / (AmE), / ˈjuːnjən ˈdʒæk / (BrE)
the national flag of the United Kingdom, consisting of the crosses of St George, St Andrew, and St Patrick combined.
the national flag of the United Kingdom

Examples

The Union Jack was proudly displayed during the celebration of the Queen's birthday.
Tourists often buy souvenirs featuring the Union Jack when they visit London.
He wrapped himself in the Union Jack after his victory at the Olympic Games.

Etymology

From 'union' (referring to the union of England, Scotland, and Ireland) and 'Jack' (a flag flown at the bow of a ship).
