disputed state

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈspjutɪd steɪt / (AmE), / dɪˈspjuːtɪd steɪt / (BrE)
a territory that possesses de facto sovereignty but is not widely recognized as an independent state by the international community
territory claiming sovereignty without international recognition

Examples

The region declared itself a disputed state after the collapse of the central government.
Several disputed states have their own governments and military forces, despite lacking international recognition.
Negotiations stalled because one side refused to acknowledge the existence of the disputed state.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unrecognized state - a territory claiming independence but lacking international recognition
  • Degree Higher: sovereign state - a fully recognized independent country
