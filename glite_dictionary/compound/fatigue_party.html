fatigue party

1. NOUN

/ fəˈtiɡ ˈpɑrti / (AmE), / fəˈtiːɡ ˈpɑːti / (BrE)
a group of soldiers assigned to perform fatigue duty, such as cleaning, maintenance, or other non-combat tasks
group of soldiers performing non-combat duty

Examples

The sergeant assigned a fatigue party to clean the barracks after inspection.
A fatigue party was formed to construct defensive positions near the front line.
After breakfast, the fatigue party started clearing debris from the motor pool.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fatigue duty - non-combat military tasks or labor

Etymology

From 'fatigue' (from French 'fatigue', meaning labor, weariness) and 'party' meaning group.
