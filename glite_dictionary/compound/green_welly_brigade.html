green welly brigade

1. NOUN

/ ɡrin ˈwɛli brəˈɡeɪd / (AmE), / ɡriːn ˈwɛli brɪˈɡeɪd / (BrE)
(British, often humorous or slightly disparaging) people, especially from affluent rural backgrounds, who are enthusiastic about country pursuits and environmental matters, often identified by their outdoor clothing such as green wellington boots.
affluent environmental enthusiasts, often rural (UK)
british informal

Examples

The green welly brigade were out in force at the charity walk in the countryside.
Locals sometimes mock the green welly brigade for their enthusiasm for rural traditions.
A member of the green welly brigade spoke about the importance of preserving local wildlife habitats.

Synset Examples

The green welly brigade is known for their dedication to nature.
Members of the green welly brigade met to discuss conservation efforts.
The green welly brigade organized a cleanup of the local park.

Etymology

From the association of green Wellington boots with British countryside enthusiasts; brigade used humorously for a distinct group.
