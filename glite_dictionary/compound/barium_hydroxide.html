barium hydroxide

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛəriəm haɪˈdrɑksaɪd / (AmE), / ˈbɛəriəm haɪˈdrɒksaɪd / (BrE)
a white, poisonous, water-soluble compound used in analytical chemistry and the manufacture of lubricating oils and other chemicals
white poisonous chemical compound

Examples

For the experiment, we need to handle barium hydroxide with care due to its toxicity.
They used barium hydroxide to prepare a solution in the laboratory.
As a waste product, barium hydroxide must be disposed of according to hazardous materials guidelines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barium - a soft, silvery-white metallic element
  • Degree Lower: calcium hydroxide - a white powdery substance used in mortars and cements

Etymology

Barium from New Latin 'barium', from Greek 'barys' (heavy); hydroxide from hydro- (water) and oxide.
