principle of superposition

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɪnsəpəl əv ˌsuːpərpəˈzɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈprɪnsɪpl əv ˌsuːpəzɪˈʃən / (BrE)
(Geology) The principle that in any sequence of undisturbed sedimentary rock layers, the lowest layer is the oldest and each successive layer above is younger.
geological law about oldest and youngest rock layers

Examples

The principle of superposition helps geologists determine the relative ages of sedimentary rock layers.
By applying the principle of superposition, scientists can reconstruct the sequence of geologic events.
The discovery of the principle of superposition was crucial for understanding Earth's history.

2. NOUN

/ ˈprɪnsəpəl əv ˌsuːpərpəˈzɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈprɪnsɪpl əv ˌsuːpəzɪˈʃɒn / (BrE)
a scientific law stating that the displacement at any point, resulting from the sum of overlapping wave systems, is equal to the sum of the displacements of the individual waves at that point
physics law on overlapping waves

Examples

The principle of superposition explains how wave patterns interfere constructively and destructively.
In quantum mechanics, the principle of superposition underlies the concept of a particle being in multiple states at once.
Students learned to apply the principle of superposition to solve complex wave problems in physics.

Synset Examples

the principle of superposition is the basis of the wave theory of light

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interference - the process in which two or more waves overlap and combine

Etymology

Formed from 'principle' and 'superposition', the latter from Latin superpositio(n-), from superponere ‘place over’.
