mother lode

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌðɚ loʊd / (AmE), / ˈmʌðə ləʊd / (BrE)
The principal or richest vein of ore in a mine or deposit, often used figuratively for a principal source of something valuable.
the principal ore vein in a deposit

Examples

The old miners believed the mother lode was still hidden in the mountains.
After months of research, the scientist finally found the mother lode of data she needed.
The region's mother lode produced enough gold to make several families wealthy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bonanza - a situation or event that creates a sudden increase in wealth or good fortune

Etymology

From American English, originally used during the California Gold Rush to denote the main gold-carrying vein.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmʌðɚ loʊd / (AmE), / ˈmʌðə ləʊd / (BrE)
the principal or richest source of something, especially a valuable resource or supply
rich, principal source (especially of wealth or resources)

Examples

After hours of research, she finally stumbled upon the mother lode of primary sources for her thesis.
The company's latest oil well hit the mother lode, making them an industry leader overnight.
Collectors considered the attic a mother lode of rare comic books and memorabilia.

Synset Examples

She found the mother lode of information for her project.
The new discovery was the mother lode of fossil finds.
They struck the mother lode in the gold mine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bonanza - a situation or event that creates a sudden increase in wealth or good fortune

Etymology

From mining terminology; originally from US California Gold Rush era, referring to a main vein of gold ore; 'lode' from Old English 'lād' (way, course).
