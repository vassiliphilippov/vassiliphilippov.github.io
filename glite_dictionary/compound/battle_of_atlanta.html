Battle of Atlanta

1. PROPN

/ ˈbæɾl əv ætˈlæntə / (AmE), / ˈbætəl əv ətˈlæntə / (BrE)
A major battle of the American Civil War in 1864, where Union forces under General Sherman besieged and captured Atlanta, cutting off its supply lines before burning much of the city.
American Civil War battle in Atlanta, 1864

Examples

The Battle of Atlanta was a turning point in the Civil War, bringing the Union closer to victory.
Sherman's tactics during the Battle of Atlanta are still studied by military historians today.
Many historical reenactments commemorate the Battle of Atlanta each year.
