karaya gum

1. NOUN

/ kəˈraɪ.ə ɡʌm / (AmE), / kəˈraɪ.ə ɡʌm / (BrE)
a natural gum obtained from an Asian tree, used to finish textiles and as a thickener in food and cosmetics
natural gum from an Asian tree, thickener

Examples

A small amount of karaya gum is added to processed foods to improve their texture.
The textile industry uses karaya gum to give fabrics a smooth finish.
Her cosmetic moisturizer lists karaya gum as a natural thickening ingredient.

Etymology

From Hindi 'karāya' + English 'gum.'
