chronic myelocytic leukemia

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɑnɪk ˌmaɪəˌlɪtɪk ləˈkiːmiə / (AmE), / ˈkrɒnɪk ˌmaɪəˌlɪtɪk ljuːˈkiːmiə / (BrE)
A type of chronic leukemia characterized by an increased number of granular white blood cells, more common in older adults.
a type of chronic leukemia with too many granular white blood cells
medical

Examples

After being diagnosed with chronic myelocytic leukemia, he started targeted therapy prescribed by his hematologist.
Chronic myelocytic leukemia is most commonly found in adults over the age of 60.
Patients with chronic myelocytic leukemia often show elevated white blood cell counts in blood tests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leukemia - a malignant disease of the bone marrow and blood
  • Degree Higher: acute myelocytic leukemia - a rapidly progressing leukemia involving myeloid lineage of white blood cells

Etymology

From Greek 'muelos' (marrow) and 'leukos' (white), referring to the white blood cells produced in the bone marrow.
