brittle bush

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrɪtl ˌbʊʃ / (AmE), / ˈbrɪtl ˌbʊʃ / (BrE)
a fragrant shrub of the southwestern United States and Mexico with brittle stems, blue-green leaves, and yellow flowers, producing a resin used in incense, varnish, and folk medicine
fragrant southwestern U.S. shrub with yellow flowers

Examples

In the desert landscape, brittle bush dots the hillsides with its bright yellow blooms.
The resin from the brittle bush has been traditionally used in making incense.
After heavy rainfalls, brittle bush plants come alive with a stunning display of flowers.
