buttoned-up demeanor

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌtnd ʌp dɪˈmiːnər / (AmE), / ˈbʌtnd ʌp dɪˈmiːnə / (BrE)
A reserved, formal, or emotionally restrained appearance or manner; not showing one's feelings openly.
reserved or formal appearance or manner

Examples

Despite her buttoned-up demeanor, she laughed easily among close friends.
His buttoned-up demeanor made it difficult for colleagues to get to know him.
In interviews, the CEO maintains a buttoned-up demeanor that commands respect.

Synset Examples

His buttoned-up demeanor hid his fun personality.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reserved - showing self-restraint or keeping one's emotions and thoughts to oneself
  • Synonym: withdrawn - not wanting to communicate with other people

Etymology

From 'buttoned up' (meaning tightly fastened or closed) + 'demeanor' (manner, appearance).
