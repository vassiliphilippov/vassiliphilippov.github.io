pilot cloth

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪlət klɔθ / (AmE), / ˈpaɪlət klɒθ / (BrE)
a thick, durable, blue woolen cloth used to make overcoats, particularly for sailors and similar uses
thick blue fabric for coats, especially sailors'

Examples

The navy issued new overcoats made from pilot cloth to all its sailors.
His vintage jacket is crafted from traditional pilot cloth, known for its warmth and durability.
Museum exhibits often include uniforms constructed with pilot cloth to demonstrate historical clothing techniques.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: melton - a thick, heavy woolen cloth often used for coats

Etymology

From 'pilot' (meaning sailor or ship's guide) and 'cloth', referring to its origin in seafaring garments.
