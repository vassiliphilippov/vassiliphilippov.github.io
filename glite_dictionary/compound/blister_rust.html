blister rust

1. NOUN

/ ˈblɪstɚ ˌrʌst / (AmE), / ˈblɪstə ˌrʌst / (BrE)
A disease of pine trees caused by rust fungi of the genus Cronartium, characterized by the formation of blisters on the bark and destruction of bark and sapwood.
a fungal disease affecting pines

Examples

White pine forests have suffered significant losses due to blister rust infestations over the last century.
Foresters monitor trees closely for signs of blister rust, which include characteristic swellings and cankers.
The spread of blister rust is often facilitated by cool, moist weather conditions.

2. NOUN

/ ˈblɪstər rʌst / (AmE), / ˈblɪstə rʌst / (BrE)
A disease of pines caused by a fungus of the genus Cronartium, characterized by cankers and blisters on bark, and requiring a currant or gooseberry plant as an alternate host.
disease of pines caused by a fungus

Examples

White pine forests have been severely affected by blister rust in recent decades.
Efforts to control blister rust often involve removing nearby currant bushes.
The presence of blister rust can be identified by orange blisters on the pine's bark.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: white pine blister rust - specific form of blister rust affecting white pine
  • Synonym: rust - plant disease caused by parasitic fungi, producing reddish spots

Etymology

From 'blister' (swelling on skin or bark) + 'rust' (fungal disease name referring to reddish spores).
