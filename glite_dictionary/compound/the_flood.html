the Flood

1. PROPN

/ ðə flʌd / (AmE), / ðə flʌd / (BrE)
(in the Bible) the great deluge described in the Book of Genesis, said to have been sent by God to destroy all life on earth except for Noah, his family, and the animals in the ark.
the biblical deluge in Noah's time

Examples

Many scholars debate the historical basis for the Flood described in Genesis.
During Sunday school, the children learned about Noah and the Flood.
The story of the Flood has parallels in various ancient cultures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deluge - a great flood, especially one regarded as divine punishment
