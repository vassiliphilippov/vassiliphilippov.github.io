Zaire River

1. PROPN

/ zaɪˈɪr ˈrɪvər / (AmE), / zaɪˈɪə ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
the former name of the Congo River, a major river in central Africa that flows through the Democratic Republic of the Congo into the Atlantic Ocean
former name of the Congo River, central Africa

Examples

The Zaire River is one of Africa’s major waterways and supports a vast ecosystem.
During the 1970s and 1980s, the Zaire River was commonly used in maps and textbooks.
Many explorers followed the course of the Zaire River to reach the heart of Central Africa.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Congo River - the current name of the major African river formerly known as Zaire River

Etymology

From Portuguese 'Zaire', a misreading of the Kikongo word 'nzadi' meaning 'river'.
