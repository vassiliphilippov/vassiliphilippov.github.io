National Guard Bureau

1. NOUN

/ ˈnæʃənəl ɡɑrd bjuˈroʊ / (AmE), / ˈnæʃənəl ɡɑːd bjuəˈrəʊ / (BrE)
the agency of the U.S. Department of Defense that administers the Army National Guard and Air National Guard and coordinates their activities with the Army and Air Force
U.S. agency managing the National Guard services
american

Examples

The National Guard Bureau announced new measures to improve disaster response coordination.
Her position at the National Guard Bureau involves overseeing recruitment and training programs.
Members of Congress met with officials from the National Guard Bureau to discuss national security concerns.
