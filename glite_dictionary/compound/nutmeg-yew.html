nutmeg-yew

1. NOUN

/ ˈnʌtˌmɛɡ.ju / (AmE), / ˈnʌtˌmɛɡ.juː / (BrE)
A California evergreen tree whose fruit resembles a nutmeg but has a strong turpentine flavor.
California evergreen tree with nutmeg-like fruit

Examples

The nutmeg-yew is native to California's coastal forests.
Botanists studied the unusual properties of the nutmeg-yew's fruit.
Although it looks similar to nutmeg, the fruit of the nutmeg-yew is not edible.

Etymology

From 'nutmeg', for the resemblance of the fruit, and 'yew', for its similarity in appearance to the yew tree.
