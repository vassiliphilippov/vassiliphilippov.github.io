genus Juncus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈdʒʌŋ.kəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈdʒʌŋ.kəs / (BrE)
the type genus of the family Juncaceae; perennial tufted, smooth (glabrous) marsh plants, commonly known as rushes, found in temperate regions
type genus of rushes (Juncaceae family)

Examples

The genus Juncus includes many species commonly referred to as rushes in wetlands.
Botanists studied the distribution of genus Juncus across different temperate marshes.
Many ornamental grasses are related to the genus Juncus and thrive in garden ponds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rush - common name for plants of the genus Juncus

Etymology

From Latin 'Juncus' meaning 'rush', used in botanical naming.
