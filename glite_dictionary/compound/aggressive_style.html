aggressive style

1. NOUN

/ əˈɡrɛsɪv staɪl / (AmE), / əˈɡrɛsɪv staɪl / (BrE)
a way of behaving, playing, or performing that is forceful, intense, and competitive
forceful, intense, and competitive approach

Examples

The coach admired her aggressive style during the championship game.
He brought an aggressive style to the negotiations, quickly taking control of the situation.
Their new advertising campaign has a noticeably more aggressive style than their previous ones.

Synset Examples

The player was known for his aggressive style on the field.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: defensive style - a cautious or protective manner of behaving or performing
