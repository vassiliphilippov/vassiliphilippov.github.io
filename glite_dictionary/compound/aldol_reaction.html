aldol reaction

1. NOUN

/ ˈældɔl riˌæk.ʃən / (AmE), / ˈældɒl riˈæk.ʃən / (BrE)
a chemical reaction in which aldehydes or ketones combine to form an aldol, typically in the presence of a base or acid catalyst.
chemical reaction forming an aldol

Examples

The aldol reaction is widely used in organic synthesis to build complex molecules.
Our laboratory experiment involved performing an aldol reaction between two different aldehydes.
A basic catalyst can accelerate the aldol reaction by generating enolate ions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aldol condensation - reaction involving the formation and subsequent dehydration of aldol products

Etymology

From 'ald' (aldehyde) + 'ol' (alcohol), reflecting the structure of the product.
