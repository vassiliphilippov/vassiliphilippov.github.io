sacral nerve

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪ.krəl nɜrv / (AmE), / ˈseɪ.krəl nɜːv / (BrE)
any of the pairs of spinal nerves that emerge from the sacral region of the spinal cord, usually five in number
spinal nerve in the sacral region
medical

Examples

Damage to a sacral nerve can result in loss of bladder control.
The sacral nerves carry signals to the muscles and organs of the pelvis.
After her injury, doctors checked her sacral nerve function to assess movement in her legs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spinal nerve - any nerve arising from the spinal cord

Etymology

From Latin 'sacer' (sacred), referring to the sacrum bone.
