six-pack

1. NOUN

/ sɪks pæk / (AmE), / sɪks pæk / (BrE)
A package containing six bottles or cans of a beverage, especially beer or soft drinks, sold as a unit.
a package of six bottles or cans

Examples

He picked up a six-pack of soda on his way home from work.
The refrigerator was stocked with a six-pack of cold beer.
She grabbed a six-pack for the picnic this afternoon.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɪksˌpæk / (AmE), / ˈsɪks.pæk / (BrE)
A package or carton that holds six bottles or cans, typically of beer or a soft drink.
a carton holding six bottles or cans

Examples

I picked up a six-pack of soda for the party.
He bought a six-pack of beer from the store on his way home.
The convenience store is having a sale on six-packs this weekend.

Etymology

From 'six' (the number) and 'pack' (bundle or package), referring to the number of items in the package.
