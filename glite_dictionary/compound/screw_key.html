screw key

1. NOUN

/ skruː kiː / (AmE), / skruː kiː / (BrE)
a tool, typically a type of wrench, designed for turning screws by fitting into their heads or slots
tool for turning screws

Examples

He reached into his toolbox and pulled out a screw key to tighten the hinge.
Old pianos often require a special screw key to adjust their internal components.
The mechanic replaced the lost screw key before continuing his work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: screwdriver - a hand tool with a tip that fits into the head of a screw to turn it
