horse cassia

1. NOUN

/ hɔrs ˈkæsiə / (AmE), / hɔːs ˈkæsiə / (BrE)
A tropical American semi-evergreen tree with erect racemes of pink or rose-colored flowers, cultivated as an ornamental.
tropical tree with pink or rose flowers

Examples

The garden's focal point is a blooming horse cassia that attracts many butterflies.
We planted a horse cassia in our yard for its beautiful pink flowers.
The landscaper recommended horse cassia for its ornamental value and hardiness.

2. NOUN

/ hɔrs ˈkæsiə / (AmE), / hɔːs ˈkæsiə / (BrE)
an East Indian tree (Cassia fistula) with long pods containing a black purgative pulp, traditionally used as a medicine for horses
East Indian medicinal tree for horses

Examples

The bark and pods of the horse cassia are valued for their medicinal properties in rural India.
Farmers traditionally give horse cassia pulp to treat intestinal issues in animals.
The lush, yellow flowers of horse cassia brighten gardens as well as serve practical uses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cassia - a genus of tropical trees and shrubs with fragrant flowers and medicinal properties

Etymology

Named for its historical use as a horse medicine; 'cassia' from Latin, referring to plants of the genus Cassia.
