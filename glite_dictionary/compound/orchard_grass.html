orchard grass

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrtʃərd ɡræs / (AmE), / ˈɔːtʃəd ɡrɑːs / (BrE)
a stout perennial grass, originally from Europe, widely cultivated for hay and pasture
a grass used for hay and pasture

Examples

The farmer planted orchard grass to provide nutritious hay for the livestock.
Orchard grass grows well in temperate climates and is valued for its high yield.
Our horses prefer eating orchard grass over other types of hay.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: timothy - a widely grown hay grass similar to orchard grass
