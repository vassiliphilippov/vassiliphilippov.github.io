to miss an opportunity

1. VERB

/ mɪs ən ˌɑːpərˈtunəti / (AmE), / mɪs ən ˌɒpəˈtjuːnɪti / (BrE)
to fail to take advantage of a chance to do or achieve something
fail to take a chance

Examples

He didn't want to miss an opportunity to meet the famous author.
Many investors regret when they miss an opportunity to buy shares early.
If we leave now, we might miss an opportunity for a great adventure.

Synset Examples

He claims he never misses an opportunity to travel.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: seize an opportunity - to take advantage of a chance
  • Synonym: to pass up - to decline or not take advantage of (an opportunity)
