Christian Scientist

1. NOUN

/ ˌkrɪstʃən ˈsaɪəntɪst / (AmE), / ˌkrɪstʃən ˈsaɪəntɪst / (BrE)
a member of the Christian Science church, a Protestant denomination founded by Mary Baker Eddy in the United States
member of the Christian Science church

Examples

She was raised as a Christian Scientist and attended church every week.
Several Christian Scientists gathered for the annual convention in Boston.
As a Christian Scientist, he relied on prayer instead of conventional medicine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Christian Science - a religious movement emphasizing spiritual healing, founded by Mary Baker Eddy
