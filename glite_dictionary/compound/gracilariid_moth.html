gracilariid moth

1. NOUN

/ ɡrəˌsɪl.əˈraɪ.ɪd mɑθ / (AmE), / ɡrəˌsɪl.əˈraɪ.ɪd mɒθ / (BrE)
a small, dull or metallic-colored tineoid moth whose larvae mine in plant leaves, belonging to the family Gracillariidae
tiny leaf-mining moth of family Gracillariidae

Examples

The gracilariid moth is known for its larvae that create winding mines inside leaves.
Biologists discovered a new species of gracilariid moth in the rainforest canopy.
Gardeners often notice the damage caused by gracilariid moth larvae on oak and birch leaves.

Etymology

From New Latin Gracillariidae, the scientific name for this family of moths.
