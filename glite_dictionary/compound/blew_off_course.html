to blew off course

1. VERB

/ blu ɑf kɔrs / (AmE), / blu ɒf kɔːs / (BrE)
(of a vehicle, plan, or course of action) to be deviated from the intended direction or plan, especially unexpectedly or due to external forces.
deviated from the intended course or plan

Examples

The plane was blew off course by a sudden storm over the ocean.
Their project blew off course after they received new instructions from management.
Many ships have blew off course in these unpredictable waters.

Synset Examples

The ship blew off course due to the strong winds.
His plans blew off course after the unexpected delay.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to veer - to change direction suddenly
  • Antonym: stay on course - to remain on the planned or intended path or plan
