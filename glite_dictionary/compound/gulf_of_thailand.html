Gulf of Thailand

1. PROPN

/ ɡʌlf əv taɪˈlænd / (AmE), / ɡʌlf əv taɪˈlænd / (BrE)
a large arm of the South China Sea bordered by Thailand, Cambodia, and Vietnam, located between the Indochina peninsula and the Malay Peninsula
an arm of the South China Sea in Southeast Asia

Examples

Hundreds of fishing boats can be seen every morning in the Gulf of Thailand.
The Gulf of Thailand is famous for its beautiful coral reefs and clear waters.
Oil exploration is an important industry in the Gulf of Thailand.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: South China Sea - a marginal sea to the south of China, part of the Pacific Ocean
