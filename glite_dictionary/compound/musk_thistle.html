musk thistle

1. NOUN

/ mʌsk ˈθɪsəl / (AmE), / mʌsk ˈθɪsl̩ / (BrE)
A Eurasian perennial plant with spiny, cottony leaves and nodding crimson, musky-scented flower heads, often found in North America and valued for its nectar.
a spiny Eurasian plant with musky crimson flowers

Examples

The field was covered with musk thistle, its crimson heads nodding in the breeze.
Beekeepers appreciate musk thistle for the abundant nectar it provides.
Conservationists are concerned about the spread of musk thistle in local grasslands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thistle - a group of plants with prickly stems and leaves and typically purple flowers

Etymology

From 'musk' (suggesting a scented aroma) and 'thistle,' originally Old English 'þistel'.
