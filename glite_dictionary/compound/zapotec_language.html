Zapotec language

1. NOUN

/ ˈzæpəˌtɛk ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈzæpətɛk ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the indigenous language or group of languages spoken by the Zapotec people of southern Mexico, primarily in Oaxaca.
indigenous language of southern Mexico

Examples

Many students enrolled in the university course to learn the Zapotec language as part of their anthropological studies.
Efforts to preserve the Zapotec language are supported by local communities in Oaxaca.
He grew up in a village where the Zapotec language was spoken daily by most residents.

Etymology

From Spanish 'zapoteca', from Nahuatl 'tzapotēcah', referring to the Zapotec people.
