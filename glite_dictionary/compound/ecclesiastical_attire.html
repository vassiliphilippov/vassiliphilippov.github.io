ecclesiastical attire

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌkliːziˈæstɪkəl əˈtaɪr / (AmE), / ɪˌkliːziˈæstɪkəl əˈtaɪə / (BrE)
clothing that is appropriate or formal for wear in church or by members of the clergy
clothing appropriate for church

Examples

The priest carefully chose his ecclesiastical attire before the Sunday service.
Certain colors of ecclesiastical attire are reserved for specific church holidays.
She was fascinated by the ornate ecclesiastical attire on display in the cathedral museum.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vestments - ceremonial robes worn by clergy

Etymology

From Latin 'ecclesiasticus' (of the church), from Greek 'ekklēsiastikos', combined with 'attire' (clothing).
