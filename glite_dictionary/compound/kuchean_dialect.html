Kuchean dialect

1. NOUN

/ kuˈtʃiːən ˈdaɪə.lɛkt / (AmE), / kuˈtʃiːən ˈdaɪə.lɛkt / (BrE)
an extinct dialect of the Tocharian language, formerly spoken in Kucha, Qarašähär, and Turpan in Central Asia
an extinct Tocharian dialect of Central Asia

Examples

Several ancient manuscripts are written in the Kuchean dialect of Tocharian.
Linguists study the Kuchean dialect to trace the history of Indo-European languages in Central Asia.
The Kuchean dialect developed unique linguistic features distinct from other Tocharian varieties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Tocharian B - one of the two main dialects of Tocharian, closely related to Kuchean dialect
  • Antonym: Tocharian A - the other main Tocharian dialect, distinct from Kuchean

Etymology

From Kucha, the region in present-day Xinjiang, China, where the dialect was spoken.
