family Chrysomelidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ˌkraɪsəˈmɛlɪdi / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli ˌkraɪsəʊˈmɛlɪdi / (BrE)
A large family of beetles commonly known as leaf beetles, which feed on plants and are found worldwide.
family of leaf beetles

Examples

The family Chrysomelidae includes thousands of species of plant-feeding beetles.
Scientists study the family Chrysomelidae for their impact on agriculture and ecosystems.
Many members of the family Chrysomelidae are known for their vibrant metallic colors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leaf beetle - common name for beetles of the family Chrysomelidae

Etymology

From Greek 'chrysos' (gold) and 'melē' (limb), referring to the often metallic colors of some species.
