leading rein

1. NOUN

/ ˈliːdɪŋ reɪn / (AmE), / ˈliːdɪŋ reɪn / (BrE)
A rein used to direct a horse's head to the left or right, typically employed to guide the horse's movement.
rein to guide a horse's head

Examples

The instructor showed her how to use the leading rein to turn the horse left.
Applying the leading rein gently helped keep the horse calm during the lesson.
A common mistake is pulling too hard on the leading rein when asking for a turn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rein - a long, narrow strap attached to a horse's bit, used to control it
