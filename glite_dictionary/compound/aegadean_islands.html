Aegadean Islands

1. PROPN

/ ɛɡəˌdiːən ˈaɪləndz / (AmE), / iːɡəˌdiːən ˈaɪləndz / (BrE)
a group of islands off the west coast of Sicily in the Mediterranean Sea
island group near Sicily, Italy

Examples

The Aegadean Islands are famous for the naval battle that ended the First Punic War.
Many tourists visit the Aegadean Islands for their beautiful scenery and clear waters.
Fishing remains an important tradition among the communities of the Aegadean Islands.

Etymology

From Latin 'Aegates Insulae', the ancient name for the islands.
