cataract surgery

1. NOUN

/ ˈkætəˌrækt ˈsɝdʒəri / (AmE), / ˈkætərækt ˈsɜːdʒəri / (BrE)
A surgical procedure in which the cloudy lens of the eye is removed and usually replaced with an artificial intraocular lens to restore vision.
surgical removal and replacement of the eye's lens
medical

Examples

After her cataract surgery, she was amazed by how clear her vision became.
Doctors explained that cataract surgery is one of the most commonly performed procedures worldwide.
The patient recovered quickly following cataract surgery on his left eye.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lens replacement - surgical procedure replacing the lens of the eye
  • Degree Lower: cataract - clouding of the lens in the eye
