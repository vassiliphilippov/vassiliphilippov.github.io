old hovel

1. NOUN

/ oʊld ˈhʌvəl / (AmE), / əʊld ˈhʌvəl / (BrE)
A small, dilapidated house, often in poor condition and showing signs of age and neglect.
dilapidated small house

Examples

After decades of abandonment, the old hovel was nearly swallowed by weeds and vines.
They found shelter in an old hovel at the edge of the abandoned village.
The old hovel had a collapsed roof and shattered windows, bearing witness to many hard winters.

Synset Examples

The old hovel was falling apart after years of neglect.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hovel - a small, squalid, or simply constructed dwelling
