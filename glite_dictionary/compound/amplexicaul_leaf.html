amplexicaul leaf

1. NOUN

/ ˌæmplɪkˈsɪkəl lif / (AmE), / ˌamplɪkˈsɪːkɔːl liːf / (BrE)
a leaf whose base clasps or encircles the stem
leaf with base clasping the stem

Examples

The stem of the plant is surrounded by an amplexicaul leaf at every node.
Some species of wildflowers are easily identified by their amplexicaul leaves.
In this botanical illustration, notice how the amplexicaul leaf clasps the central stem.

Etymology

From Latin amplexus (embraced, encircled) + caulis (stem).
