Oedipus Rex

1. PROPN

/ ˈɛdɪpəs ˈrɛks / (AmE), / ˈiːdɪpəs ˈreks / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, a tragic king of Thebes who unknowingly killed his father and married his mother; also the subject of the famous tragedy by Sophocles.
tragic king in Greek mythology; play by Sophocles

Examples

Many scholars regard Oedipus Rex as the greatest example of Greek tragedy.
The plot of Oedipus Rex revolves around fate, prophecy, and personal responsibility.
After reading Oedipus Rex, we discussed the role of fate in human life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Oedipus - tragic king of Thebes in Greek mythology

Etymology

From Latin, meaning 'Oedipus the King'; title of Sophocles’ tragedy.
