bitter pignut

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɪt.ɚ ˈpɪɡ.nʌt / (AmE), / ˈbɪt.ə ˈpɪɡ.nʌt / (BrE)
A hickory tree of the eastern United States with leaves of 7 or 9 leaflets and thin-shelled, very bitter nuts.
species of hickory tree with bitter nuts

Examples

The bitter pignut is commonly found in forests throughout the eastern United States.
Although the nuts of the bitter pignut are too bitter to eat, they are important for wildlife.
We identified the tree by its thin-shelled nuts and the distinctive leaves of the bitter pignut.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hickory - a type of North American tree with tough wood and edible nuts
