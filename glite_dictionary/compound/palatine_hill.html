Palatine Hill

1. PROPN

/ ˈpæl.ə.taɪn hɪl / (AmE), / ˈpæl.ə.taɪn hɪl / (BrE)
the most central of the Seven Hills of Rome, regarded as the site of ancient Roman settlements and many imperial palaces
central hill of ancient Rome; site of palaces

Examples

Archaeologists recently uncovered ancient ruins atop the Palatine Hill.
The emperors of Rome built their grand palaces on the Palatine Hill.
Visitors to Rome are often captivated by the views from the Palatine Hill.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Capitoline Hill - one of the Seven Hills of Rome, historically significant

Etymology

From Latin 'Palatium', referring to the hill in Rome, source of the word 'palace'.
