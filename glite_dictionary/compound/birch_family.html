birch family

1. NOUN

/ bɝtʃ ˈfæməli / (AmE), / bɜːtʃ ˈfæmɪli / (BrE)
A family of monoecious trees and shrubs including birches, alders, hornbeams, hazels, and related plants (family Betulaceae).
botanical family including birches and alders

Examples

The birch family includes many species found in temperate forests across the world.
Hazelnut trees, which belong to the birch family, produce edible nuts.
Alder, a member of the birch family, is often used for making furniture.
