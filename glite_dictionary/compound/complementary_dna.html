complementary DNA

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːmpləˈmɛn.tə.ri ˌdiː.enˈeɪ / (AmE), / ˌkɒmplɪˈmɛn.təri ˌdiː.enˈeɪ / (BrE)
single-stranded DNA synthesized from a messenger RNA template by the enzyme reverse transcriptase, used in genetics and molecular biology
DNA strand synthesized from mRNA template
medical

Examples

Scientists frequently use complementary DNA to study gene expression in different tissues.
The process of creating complementary DNA involves reverse transcription of messenger RNA.
Researchers synthesized complementary DNA to clone and analyze specific genes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cDNA - abbreviation for complementary DNA
  • Antonym: messenger RNA - nucleic acid that carries genetic information from DNA to the ribosome

Etymology

Formed from 'complementary' (from Latin 'complementum', meaning completion) and the initialism 'DNA' (deoxyribonucleic acid).
