pearl-weed

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɜrlˌwid / (AmE), / ˈpɜːlˌwiːd / (BrE)
any of several low-growing plants of the genus Sagina, with small spherical white flowers resembling pearls
a low-growing plant with pearl-like flowers

Examples

The garden was dotted with clusters of pearl-weed growing between the stones.
Some wild meadows are covered in pearl-weed during early summer, lending a delicate appearance to the ground.
Botanists value pearl-weed for its small, pearl-like flowers and mat-forming habit.
