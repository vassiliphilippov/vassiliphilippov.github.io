Neanderthal man

1. NOUN

/ niˈæn.dərˌtɑl mæn / (AmE), / niˈɑːn.dəˌtɑːl mæn / (BrE)
an extinct species of robust human (Homo neanderthalensis) that lived during the Middle Paleolithic period in Europe and western Asia
extinct robust human species

Examples

The discovery of Neanderthal man remains provided valuable insight into human evolution.
Some scientists believe that Neanderthal man and modern humans may have interbred.
Fossils of Neanderthal man have been unearthed in both Europe and western Asia.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Homo sapiens - the modern human species
  • Synonym: Neanderthal - the extinct species or genus to which Neanderthal man belongs

Etymology

From Neanderthal, the valley in Germany where remains were first found, plus man.
