vena gluteus

1. NOUN

/ ˈviːnə ˈɡluːtiəs / (AmE), / ˈveɪnə ˈɡluːtɪəs / (BrE)
A vein that drains blood from the gluteal (buttock) muscles on either side of the body.
vein serving gluteal muscles
medical

Examples

The vena gluteus plays a vital role in draining blood from the buttock region.
Damage to the vena gluteus during surgery can lead to significant bleeding.
Anatomy students often study the vena gluteus when learning about the circulatory system of the lower body.

Etymology

From Latin 'vena' (vein) and 'gluteus' (relating to the buttocks).
