point man

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɔɪnt mæn / (AmE), / ˈpɔɪnt mæn / (BrE)
a person who leads or represents an important project, initiative, or enterprise, especially as its main spokesperson or coordinator
main leader or spokesperson of a project or initiative

Examples

He was appointed as the point man for the company's expansion into Asia.
The senator served as the administration's point man on healthcare reform.
During the crisis, she acted as the point man communicating with the public.

Synset Examples

he is the president's point man on economic issues

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spokesperson - a person who speaks on behalf of an organization
  • Synonym: leader - the person who leads a group or organization

Etymology

Originally U.S. military slang for a soldier who walks at the front of a patrol (the 'point'), later generalized to leadership roles.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɔɪnt mæn / (AmE), / ˈpɔɪnt mæn / (BrE)
A soldier who goes ahead of a patrol to scout and detect possible danger.
lead scout in a military patrol

Examples

The point man signaled for the rest of the squad to stop when he spotted movement up ahead.
During the operation, the point man was responsible for detecting any ambushes.
They chose their most experienced soldier to act as point man during the night patrol.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scout - a soldier sent ahead to gather information

3. NOUN

/ ˈpɔɪnt mæn / (AmE), / ˈpɔɪnt mæn / (BrE)
the person who is chiefly responsible for a particular project or task; a leader or main representative in an activity.
main person responsible for a task

Examples

After years of experience, Sarah became the point man for all major negotiations.
When the crisis hit, he stepped in as the point man to organize the company's response.
The team relied on their point man to communicate with upper management.

Synset Examples

He was the point man for the project presentation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leader - a person who leads or commands a group, organization, or country
  • Synonym: representative - a person chosen or appointed to act or speak for others

Etymology

Originally U.S. military slang for the soldier who leads a patrol; now used more generally for a leader or chief representative.
