turbo-propeller plane

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɝːboʊ prəˈpɛlər pleɪn / (AmE), / ˌtɜːbəʊ prəˈpɛlə pleɪn / (BrE)
An airplane powered by a turbojet engine that drives an external propeller.
airplane with a turbojet-driven propeller

Examples

The turbo-propeller plane made a smooth landing on the small runway.
Regional airlines often use turbo-propeller planes for short-distance flights.
He was fascinated by the design of the new turbo-propeller plane unveiled at the airshow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: turboprop - airplane with a turbine engine and propeller

Etymology

Formed from 'turbo-' (from Latin 'turbo', relating to spinning) and 'propeller' plus 'plane'.
