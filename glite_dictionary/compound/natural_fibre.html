natural fibre

1. NOUN

/ ˈnætʃrəl ˈfaɪbɚ / (AmE), / ˈnætʃrəl ˈfaɪbə / (BrE)
A fiber obtained from plants or animals, as opposed to one produced synthetically.
fiber from plants or animals

Examples

Cotton is a common natural fibre used in clothing around the world.
Wool is a natural fibre that comes from sheep.
The company promotes the use of natural fibre products for environmental sustainability.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: synthetic fibre - fiber made by chemical synthesis rather than from natural sources
