quartic polynomial

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɔrtɪk pəˈlɪnoʊmiəl / (AmE), / ˈkwɔːtɪk pəˈlɪnəmiəl / (BrE)
A polynomial of degree four, having the highest exponent of the variable as four.
polynomial of the fourth degree

Examples

The equation forms a quartic polynomial that needs to be solved for x.
Graphing a quartic polynomial can reveal up to four real roots and three turning points.
He found that the best-fit curve was a quartic polynomial rather than a simple quadratic.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: cubic polynomial - a polynomial of the third degree
  • Degree Higher: quintic polynomial - a polynomial of the fifth degree

Etymology

From Latin 'quartus' meaning 'fourth' and 'polynomial' from Greek 'poly-' (many) + 'nomial' (term).
