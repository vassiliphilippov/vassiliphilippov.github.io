American Dream

1. NOUN

/ əˌmɛrɪkən ˈdriːm / (AmE), / əˌmɛrɪkən ˈdriːm / (BrE)
the ideal that every US citizen should have an equal opportunity to achieve success and prosperity through hard work, determination, and initiative
ideal of opportunity and success in the US
american

Examples

Many immigrants move to the United States seeking the American Dream for themselves and their families.
Her parents worked tirelessly so that she could have a chance at the American Dream.
The novel explores the cost and possibility of achieving the American Dream in modern society.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: American nightmare - a negative or disappointing outcome contrasting the American Dream
  • Synonym: success - the accomplishment of an aim or purpose

Etymology

Coined in the early 20th century; popularized by historian James Truslow Adams in 1931.
