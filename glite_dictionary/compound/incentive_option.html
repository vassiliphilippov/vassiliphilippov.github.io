incentive option

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈsɛntɪv ˈɑpʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˈsɛntɪv ˈɒpʃən / (BrE)
an option granted to a corporate executive, allowing the purchase of company stock at a fixed price if certain financial performance goals are met
stock option given as executive incentive

Examples

The company awarded its CEO an incentive option after surpassing its annual revenue targets.
Many executives receive incentive options as part of their compensation packages.
The board linked the incentive option grants to measurable company performance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stock option - a contract giving the right to buy or sell stock at a set price
