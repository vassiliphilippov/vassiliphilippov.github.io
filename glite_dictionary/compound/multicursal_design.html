multicursal design

1. NOUN

/ ˌmʌltiˈkɜrsəl dɪˈzaɪn / (AmE), / ˌmʌltɪˈkɜːsəl dɪˈzaɪn / (BrE)
a layout or structure featuring multiple pathways or routes, often used in maze and landscape design
design with multiple pathways or routes

Examples

The multicursal design of the hedge maze made it a real challenge to find the exit.
Urban planners appreciated the multicursal design for encouraging exploration within the park.
Unlike the simple maze, the garden's multicursal design offers visitors many choices at every turn.

Synset Examples

He enjoyed the multicursal design of the new park.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unicursal design - design with only a single path or route

Etymology

From Latin multi- (many) + cursa (course, path).
