iron disulfide

1. NOUN

/ ˌaɪɚn daɪˈsʌlfaɪd / (AmE), / ˌaɪən daɪˈsʌlfaɪd / (BrE)
a chemical compound made up of one iron atom and two sulfur atoms, commonly found as the mineral pyrite
a compound of iron and sulfur
medical

Examples

The mineral pyrite is actually iron disulfide and is often called fool’s gold.
Scientists analyzed the sample to confirm it contained iron disulfide rather than another iron compound.
Iron disulfide can sometimes generate sparks when struck against metal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pyrite - common mineral form of iron disulfide
  • Degree Lower: iron sulfide - compound with iron and sulfur, may have different numbers of sulfur atoms

Etymology

Formed from 'iron' and 'disulfide,' with 'di-' meaning two and 'sulfide' indicating a compound with sulfur.
