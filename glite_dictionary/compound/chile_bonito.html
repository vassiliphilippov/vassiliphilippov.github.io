Chile bonito

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃiːleɪ bəˈniːtoʊ / (AmE), / ˈtʃiːleɪ bəˈniːtəʊ / (BrE)
A species of bonito fish found along the Pacific coast of the Americas, known for its dark, oily flesh that is suitable for canning.
Pacific coast bonito fish species

Examples

Fishermen caught a large Chile bonito off the coast of Peru this morning.
The dark, oily flesh of the Chile bonito makes it ideal for canning and export.
While visiting the fish market, I spotted several fresh Chile bonitos on display.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bonito - a type of medium-sized predatory fish related to tuna
