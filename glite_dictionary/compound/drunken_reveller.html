drunken reveller

1. NOUN

/ ˈdrʌŋkən rɪˈvɛlər / (AmE), / ˈdrʌŋkən rɪˈvɛlə / (BrE)
A person who takes part in lively festivities while intoxicated with alcohol.
person celebrating while intoxicated

Examples

The streets were crowded with drunken revellers after the big football match.
Police struggled to control the noise created by a group of drunken revellers outside the club.
During the festival, many drunken revellers spilled out into the city square.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: reveller - a person who takes part in lively festivities

Etymology

From 'drunken', the past participle of 'drink', and 'reveller', from Old French 'reveler' (to make merry).
