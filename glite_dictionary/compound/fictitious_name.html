fictitious name

1. NOUN

/ fɪkˈtɪʃəs neɪm / (AmE), / fɪkˈtɪʃəs neɪm / (BrE)
(law) a name under which a corporation or business operates that is not its legal, registered name.
legal/business name used instead of official name
legal

Examples

The bakery operates under a fictitious name rather than its corporate name.
To open the account, the business had to register its fictitious name with the county clerk.
Using a fictitious name allows companies to brand themselves differently from their legal entity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: doing business as - the practice of operating under a name different from the legal or registered name
  • Synonym: assumed name - another term for 'fictitious name' used in law and business
