spiked loosestrife

1. NOUN

/ spaɪkt ˈluːsstraɪf / (AmE), / spaɪkt ˈluːsstraɪf / (BrE)
a marsh plant with a tall spike of purple flowers, native to Europe and now widespread in eastern North America
marsh plant with purple flower spikes

Examples

The wetlands were covered with spiked loosestrife, adding splashes of purple to the landscape.
Gardeners sometimes plant spiked loosestrife for its tall, showy flower clusters.
Spiked loosestrife has become invasive in parts of North America, threatening native marsh plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loosestrife - any plant of the genus Lythrum, including purple loosestrife
