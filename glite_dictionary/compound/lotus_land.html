lotus land

1. NOUN

/ ˈloʊtəs lænd / (AmE), / ˈləʊtəs lænd / (BrE)
an imaginary place of idyllic contentment, pleasure, and self-indulgence, often implying escapism or detachment from reality
place of dreamy pleasure and escapism

Examples

After years in the hectic city, he longed for a lotus land where worries seemed far away.
Vacationers often seek a lotus land to escape the demands of everyday life.
Her new lifestyle felt like a lotus land of endless pampering and peace.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paradise - a place of ideal beauty, delight, or happiness
  • Synonym: utopia - an imagined perfect place or state of things

Etymology

From the mythological 'Land of the Lotus-Eaters' in Homer’s Odyssey, whose inhabitants lived in dreamy forgetfulness after eating the lotus fruit.
