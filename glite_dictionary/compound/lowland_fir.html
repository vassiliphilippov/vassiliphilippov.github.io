lowland fir

1. NOUN

/ ˈloʊˌlænd ˌfɜr / (AmE), / ˈləʊˌlænd ˌfɜː / (BrE)
A tall fir tree native to the Pacific coast of northwestern America, known for its long curved branches and deep green leaves.
tall fir tree of the Pacific Northwest

Examples

The lowland fir thrives in the moist forests along the Pacific coastline.
We identified a lowland fir by its deep green needles and gracefully curving branches.
Foresters are studying how climate change affects the growth of lowland firs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Douglas fir - a large coniferous tree native to western North America
