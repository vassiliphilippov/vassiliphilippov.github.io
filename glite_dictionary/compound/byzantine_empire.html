Byzantine Empire

1. PROPN

/ bəˈzɪnˌtaɪn ˈɛmpaɪr / (AmE), / bɪˈzæntaɪn ˈɛmpʌɪə / (BrE)
the eastern part of the Roman Empire, which survived after the division of the Roman Empire in 395 AD, lasting until the fall of Constantinople in 1453
eastern Roman Empire (395–1453)

Examples

The Byzantine Empire preserved much of Greek and Roman knowledge through the Middle Ages.
Historians often study the Byzantine Empire to understand the development of Eastern Orthodox Christianity.
After the fall of Rome, the Byzantine Empire continued to thrive for nearly a thousand years.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Roman Empire - ancient empire that split into eastern and western parts

Etymology

From Byzantium, the ancient name of Constantinople (modern Istanbul), its capital.
