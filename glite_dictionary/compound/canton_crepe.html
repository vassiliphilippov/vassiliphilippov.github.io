Canton crepe

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæn.tən kreɪp / (AmE), / ˈkæn.tən kreɪp / (BrE)
a soft, thick, crinkled fabric used for dresses, heavier than crepe de Chine
a heavy, crinkled dress fabric

Examples

The bride's dress was made from luxurious Canton crepe that draped beautifully.
She inherited a vintage Canton crepe gown from her grandmother.
Designers often choose Canton crepe for its elegant texture and substantial feel.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: crepe de Chine - a lightweight, smooth dress crepe fabric

Etymology

Named after Canton (now Guangzhou), China, reflecting the fabric's origin or style.
