class Psilotatae

1. NOUN

/ klæs saɪˌloʊtəˈtiː / (AmE), / klɑːs saɪˌlɒtəˈtiː / (BrE)
A botanical class comprising whisk ferns of the family Psilotaceae, characterized by vascular plants with no true roots, little or no leaf differentiation, and rudimentary spore sacs.
botanical class of primitive whisk ferns
medical

Examples

The class Psilotatae is distinguished by vascular plants lacking true roots and with simple spore-producing organs.
Botanists study the unique characteristics found in the class Psilotatae to understand plant evolution.
Fossil records indicate that members of the class Psilotatae are among the most primitive vascular plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Psilotaceae - the botanical family containing whisk ferns

Etymology

From New Latin 'Psilotatae', based on the genus 'Psilotum' and Latin classification terms.
