first harmonic

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɜrst hɑrˈmɑnɪk / (AmE), / ˈfɜːst hɑːˈmɒnɪk / (BrE)
the basic tone in a harmonic series, corresponding to the fundamental frequency of a musical note
lowest tone in a harmonic series

Examples

The first harmonic is essential in defining the pitch we perceive.
A guitar string's first harmonic vibrates at its fundamental frequency.
If you dampen the first harmonic, higher overtones become more prominent.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fundamental - the principal or lowest frequency of a musical note
  • Degree Higher: second harmonic - the second tone in the harmonic series (first overtone)
