Y chromosome

1. NOUN

/ waɪ kɹoʊməˌsoʊm / (AmE), / waɪ ˈkrəʊməsəʊm / (BrE)
the sex chromosome found in males of many species, including humans, that determines male biological characteristics
sex chromosome determining male traits
medical

Examples

Only males inherit a Y chromosome from their fathers.
Genetic disorders can be linked to mutations on the Y chromosome.
Scientists study the Y chromosome to trace paternal ancestry.

Synset Examples

human males normally have one X chromosome and one Y chromosome

Related Terms

  • Antonym: X chromosome - the other human sex chromosome, paired with Y in males and with another X in females

Etymology

Named after the Y shape it appears as under a microscope; 'chromosome' from Greek 'chroma' (color) + 'soma' (body).
