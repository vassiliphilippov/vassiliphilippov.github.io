by virtue of

1. ADP

/ baɪ ˈvɝtʃu əv / (AmE), / baɪ ˈvɜːtjuː əv / (BrE)
because of or as a result of (something); due to
because of; as a result of

Examples

She was granted access by virtue of her credentials.
The bill was passed by virtue of a unanimous vote.
The company succeeded by virtue of its innovative approach.

Synset Examples

She got the promotion by virtue of her dedication.
The team won by virtue of their teamwork.
He was invited by virtue of his experience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: because of - due to; for the reason of
  • Synonym: on account of - because of; owing to

Etymology

From the use of 'virtue' in Middle English and Old French meaning 'power' or 'influence'.
