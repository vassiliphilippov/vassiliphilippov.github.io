false hellebore

1. NOUN

/ fɔls ˈhɛlɪˌbɔr / (AmE), / fɔːls ˈhɛlɪˌbɔː / (BrE)
a perennial herb of the lily family with thick, toxic rhizomes, especially of the genus Veratrum
toxic perennial herb of the lily family

Examples

The leaves of the false hellebore should be handled with care due to their toxicity.
Many hikers mistake false hellebore for edible wild plants, which can lead to poisoning.
In early spring, the broad shoots of false hellebore are easily seen in wet meadows.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: hellebore - a plant of the genus Helleborus, often poisonous
