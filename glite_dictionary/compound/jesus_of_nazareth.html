Jesus of Nazareth

1. PROPN

/ ˈdʒiːzəs əv ˈnæzərəθ / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːzəs əv ˈnazərəθ / (BrE)
(c. 4 BC – AD 29) The teacher and prophet from Bethlehem, active in Nazareth, whose teachings form the foundation of Christianity.
central figure of Christianity

Examples

Many historians study the life and teachings of Jesus of Nazareth.
Christians worldwide celebrate the birth of Jesus of Nazareth every December 25th.
The parables told by Jesus of Nazareth continue to inspire people today.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Christ - another name for Jesus, especially in his role as the Messiah
  • Synonym: prophet - one who proclaims or interprets divine will, as Jesus is considered

Etymology

Jesus from Latin *Iesus*, from Greek *Iēsous*, from Hebrew *Yēšūa‘* (Joshua); Nazareth is a town in Galilee, Israel.
