New Caledonian pine

1. NOUN

/ nuː ˌkælɪˈdoʊniən paɪn / (AmE), / njuː ˌkælɪˈdəʊniən paɪn / (BrE)
a very tall evergreen tree native to New Caledonia and the New Hebrides, similar in appearance to the Norfolk Island pine
tall evergreen tree from New Caledonia

Examples

The New Caledonian pine is a striking feature of the island's landscape.
Botanists studied the unique growth patterns of the New Caledonian pine.
Unlike most pines, the New Caledonian pine thrives in tropical climates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Norfolk Island pine - tall evergreen tree native to Norfolk Island, similar in appearance
