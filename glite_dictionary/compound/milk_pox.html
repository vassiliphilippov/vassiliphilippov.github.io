milk pox

1. NOUN

/ mɪlk pɑks / (AmE), / mɪlk pɒks / (BrE)
A mild form of smallpox caused by a less virulent strain of the virus.
mild form of smallpox
medical

Examples

Before vaccination, milk pox was sometimes considered a less dangerous alternative to smallpox.
Doctors of the era noted that patients with milk pox often recovered quickly.
Some people believed that catching milk pox offered lifelong immunity from smallpox.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smallpox - an acute contagious viral disease, with fever and pustules, that usually leaves permanent scars
