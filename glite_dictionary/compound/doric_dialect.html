Doric dialect

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɔrɪk ˈdaɪəˌlɛkt / (AmE), / ˈdɒrɪk ˈdaɪəlɛkt / (BrE)
the dialect of Ancient Greek that was spoken in the region of Doris and by related peoples in ancient Greece.
Ancient Greek dialect from Doris

Examples

The Doric dialect was one of several branches of the Ancient Greek language.
Many classical Greek poems were written in the Doric dialect.
The use of the Doric dialect can be seen in inscriptions from the Peloponnese.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ionic dialect - the dialect of Ancient Greek spoken in Ionia
  • Synonym: Attic dialect - the dialect of Ancient Greek spoken in Attica and Athens
  • Synonym: Aeolic dialect - the dialect of Ancient Greek spoken in Aeolis

Etymology

From Latin 'Doricus' (Dorian, of Doris) and English 'dialect' from Greek 'dialektos' (discourse, language).
