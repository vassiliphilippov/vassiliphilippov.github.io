ancient history

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪnʃənt ˈhɪstəri / (AmE), / ˈeɪnʃənt ˈhɪstəri / (BrE)
something that was once significant but is now so well known or outdated that it has lost its original relevance or importance
something once important but now common or outdated

Examples

To her, their past argument is ancient history and not worth discussing anymore.
That scandal is ancient history—everyone’s moved on by now.
Don't worry, your mistake is ancient history and nobody cares about it today.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: old news - information that is no longer new or interesting

2. NOUN

/ ˈeɪnʃənt ˈhɪstəri / (AmE), / ˈeɪnʃənt ˈhɪstəri / (BrE)
the history of the ancient world, typically from the earliest recorded times until the early Middle Ages
the history of the ancient world

Examples

She has always been fascinated by ancient history, especially Greek and Roman civilizations.
The museum's collection includes many artifacts from ancient history.
For some people, the lives of the pharaohs belong to ancient history.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: modern history - the history of the modern period, from the end of the Middle Ages to the present
  • Degree Higher: medieval history - the history of the Middle Ages, between ancient and modern times

/ ˈeɪn.tʃənt ˈhɪs.tɚ.i / (AmE), / ˈæn.tʃənt ˈhɪs.tər.i / (BrE)
the study of the civilizations, peoples, and events of the distant past, especially before the end of the Western Roman Empire
the study of past civilizations

Examples

Many students choose to specialize in ancient history at university.
The museum has an impressive collection of artifacts from ancient history.
Her passion for ancient history inspired her to visit archaeological sites in Greece.
