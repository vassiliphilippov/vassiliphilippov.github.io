imperial mammoth

1. NOUN

/ ɪmˈpɪriəl ˈmæməθ / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɪəriəl ˈmæməθ / (BrE)
an extinct species of large mammoth (Mammuthus imperator) that lived in North America during the Pleistocene epoch
extinct giant North American mammoth species

Examples

The imperial mammoth roamed the plains of North America during the Ice Age.
Fossils of the imperial mammoth have been uncovered in several parts of California.
Scientists believe the imperial mammoth was one of the largest species of mammoths ever discovered.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mammoth - large extinct elephant-like animal with long curved tusks
