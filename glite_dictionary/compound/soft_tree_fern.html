soft tree fern

1. NOUN

/ sɔft tri fɜrn / (AmE), / sɒft triː fɜːn / (BrE)
a species of fern native to Australia and Tasmania, often cultivated for its tall trunk and feathery fronds, and known for its hardiness in cool climates
Australian and Tasmanian fern species

Examples

The soft tree fern is popular among gardeners for its graceful, arching fronds.
In Tasmania, the soft tree fern can be found growing in damp, shaded forests.
Many cool-climate gardens feature the soft tree fern as a striking focal point.
