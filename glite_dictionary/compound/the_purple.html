the purple

1. NOUN

/ ðə ˈpɝːpəl / (AmE), / ðə ˈpɜːpəl / (BrE)
Imperial rank or status, especially that of a Roman emperor.
imperial or royal status

Examples

He was born to the purple and destined to rule from a young age.
The general aspired to the purple, plotting his rise to supreme power.
Many risked their lives to seize the purple throughout Roman history.

Synset Examples

he was born to the purple

Related Terms

  • Synonym: throne - the position and dignity of a sovereign

Etymology

From the association of the color purple with imperial robes in ancient Rome and Byzantium.

2. NOUN

/ ðə ˈpɝpəl / (AmE), / ðə ˈpɜːpl / (BrE)
the official dress worn by a cardinal in the Roman Catholic Church, traditionally colored a rich purple
cardinal's official dress in the Catholic Church

Examples

After his appointment, the archbishop was vested in the purple during the grand ceremony.
Many admired the rich hue and intricate embroidery of the purple worn by the cardinal.
Receiving the purple is considered one of the highest honors within the Church hierarchy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cardinal - a senior official of the Roman Catholic Church
  • Degree Higher: purple - a color intermediate between red and blue

Etymology

From the association with Tyrian purple, a prestigious dye used in ancient times, and the Roman Catholic tradition.
