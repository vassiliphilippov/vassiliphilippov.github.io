stipendiary magistrate

1. NOUN

/ stɪˈpɛndiˌɛri ˈmædʒɪstreɪt / (AmE), / stɪˈpɛndɪəri ˈmædʒɪstreɪt / (BrE)
(historical, UK) a paid, professional magistrate appointed by the Home Secretary to preside over certain courts, especially in police and minor criminal cases.
historical UK paid magistrate
legal british

Examples

Before becoming a circuit judge, he served as a stipendiary magistrate in the London courts.
The stipendiary magistrate presided over the trial with professionalism and efficiency.
Historically, only a stipendiary magistrate would handle such complex cases in metropolitan areas.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lay magistrate - an unpaid, usually non-professional magistrate in the UK
  • Synonym: district judge - the modern UK equivalent to the stipendiary magistrate

Etymology

From Latin stipendium (payment, wage) + magistrate.
