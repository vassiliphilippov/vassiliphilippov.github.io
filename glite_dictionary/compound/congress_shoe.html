congress shoe

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑŋɡrəs ʃu / (AmE), / ˈkɒŋɡrəs ʃuː / (BrE)
an ankle-high shoe with elastic gussets at the sides, making it easy to slip on and off
ankle-high shoe with elastic sides

Examples

He slipped on his congress shoes before leaving for the meeting.
The classic design of the congress shoe became popular in the 19th century.
With its elastic sides, the congress shoe is both stylish and convenient.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Chelsea boot - a close-fitting, ankle-high boot with elastic side panels

Etymology

Named after the U.S. Congress, where it was popular in the mid-19th century.
