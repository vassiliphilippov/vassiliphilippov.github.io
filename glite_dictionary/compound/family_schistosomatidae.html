family Schistosomatidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ʃɪˌstoʊsəˈmætɪdi / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli ʃɪˌstɒsəˈmætɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of parasitic trematode flatworms that includes the schistosomes, some of which cause disease in humans and animals.
family of parasitic flatworms (schistosomes)
medical

Examples

Many species in the family Schistosomatidae are known to cause schistosomiasis in humans.
Research on the family Schistosomatidae has led to better treatments for parasitic infections.
Parasitologists often study the life cycles of worms in the family Schistosomatidae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: schistosome - any worm of the genus Schistosoma, a member of this family

Etymology

From modern Latin 'Schistosomatidae', based on the genus name 'Schistosoma' and the standard zoological family ending '-idae'.
