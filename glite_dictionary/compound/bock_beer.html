bock beer

1. NOUN

/ bɑk bɪr / (AmE), / bɒk bɪə / (BrE)
a strong, dark lager beer of German origin, traditionally brewed in autumn and matured over winter for spring consumption
strong, dark German lager beer

Examples

Many German breweries produce bock beer as a seasonal specialty in the spring.
The festival featured a variety of bock beer from both local and international brewers.
He prefers the rich, malty flavor of bock beer over lighter lagers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lager - type of pale, bottom-fermented beer

Etymology

From German 'Bockbier', with 'Bock' referring to a male goat, possibly from the town of Einbeck, where the style originated.
