reef whitetip shark

1. NOUN

/ riːf ˈwaɪtˌtɪp ʃɑrk / (AmE), / riːf ˈwaɪtˌtɪp ʃɑːk / (BrE)
a species of shark (Triaenodon obesus) found in the Pacific and Indian Oceans and the Red Sea, noted for the white-tipped dorsal and caudal fins
shark with white-tipped fins found in reefs

Examples

Divers commonly encounter the reef whitetip shark resting in coral caves during the day.
The reef whitetip shark is known for its slender body and distinctive white-tipped fins.
Researchers are studying the behavior of the reef whitetip shark in its natural habitat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shark - large fish with a cartilaginous skeleton
