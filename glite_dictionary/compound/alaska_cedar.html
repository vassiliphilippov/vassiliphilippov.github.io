Alaska cedar

1. NOUN

/ əˈlæskə ˈsɛdər / (AmE), / əˈlɑːskə ˈsiːdə / (BrE)
a tall evergreen tree native to the Pacific coast of North America, often grown for ornamental purposes
evergreen tree of Pacific North America

Examples

The Alaska cedar is prized for its durable, fragrant wood.
Several Alaska cedar trees were planted around the park for shade and beauty.
They studied the unique adaptation of the Alaska cedar to coastal climates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cedar - a type of large evergreen tree with aromatic wood
