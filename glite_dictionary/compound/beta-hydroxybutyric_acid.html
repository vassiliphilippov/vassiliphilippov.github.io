beta-hydroxybutyric acid

1. NOUN

/ ˌbeɪ.tə haɪˌdrɒk.si.bjuːˈtɪr.ɪk ˈæs.ɪd / (AmE), / ˌbiː.tə haɪˌdrɒk.si.bjuːˈtɪr.ɪk ˈæs.ɪd / (BrE)
A beta derivative of hydroxybutyric acid that is produced during the metabolism of fats and is found in the blood and urine, especially in cases of impaired metabolism such as diabetes or fasting.
a ketone body found in blood and urine in metabolic disorders
medical

Examples

During prolonged fasting, levels of beta-hydroxybutyric acid in the blood can increase significantly.
Doctors may measure beta-hydroxybutyric acid in urine to help diagnose metabolic conditions.
Elevated beta-hydroxybutyric acid is a hallmark of diabetic ketoacidosis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ketone body - one of a group of three compounds produced during fat metabolism

Etymology

From 'beta' (position of the hydroxy group) + 'hydroxybutyric acid' (a type of organic acid).
