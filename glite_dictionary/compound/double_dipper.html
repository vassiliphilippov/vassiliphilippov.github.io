double dipper

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʌb.əl ˈdɪp.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈdʌb.əl ˈdɪp.ə / (BrE)
a person who receives two incomes from the government, typically a salary and a pension, at the same time
person receiving two government incomes

Examples

Many critics argue that a double dipper unfairly benefits from both a government job and a pension.
The new law aims to prevent retired officials from becoming double dippers in the public sector.
He was exposed as a double dipper after it was revealed he was drawing a teacher's pension and a public salary.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: double dip - the act of receiving two benefits or payments from the same source

Etymology

From 'double dip', an American English idiom dating from the mid-20th century, meaning to receive two payments or benefits.
