true glottis

1. NOUN

/ tru ˈɡlɑtɪs / (AmE), / truː ˈɡlɒtɪs / (BrE)
the space between the two true vocal folds in the larynx
space between true vocal folds
medical

Examples

A tumor located at the true glottis may cause changes in a person's voice.
The surgeon carefully examined the true glottis before proceeding with the operation.
Swelling of the true glottis can lead to difficulties in breathing and speaking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glottis - the part of the larynx consisting of the vocal cords and the opening between them
