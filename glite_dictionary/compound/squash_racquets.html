squash racquets

1. NOUN

/ skwɑʃ ˈræk.ɪts / (AmE), / skwɒʃ ˈræk.ɪts / (BrE)
a game played in an enclosed court by two or four players who strike a small ball with long-handled rackets against the walls
a racquet sport played in an enclosed court

Examples

He plays squash racquets every Saturday at the local sports club.
The rules of squash racquets require players to hit the ball above the tin and below the outline.
Many schools offer squash racquets as part of their athletic programs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: squash - a fast-paced racquet sport played in an enclosed court

Etymology

From 'squash' (the ball) and 'racquet'; 'squash' refers to the ball's ability to be squashed against the wall.
