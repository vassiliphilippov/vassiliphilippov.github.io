common box

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmən bɑks / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən bɒks / (BrE)
a large, slow-growing evergreen shrub or small tree (Buxus sempervirens), often used for hedges, borders, and topiary figures
an evergreen shrub or tree used in hedges and topiary

Examples

The common box is a popular choice for shaping into decorative garden features.
They trimmed the common box hedges every spring to keep them tidy.
Many historical gardens are bordered by thick common box shrubs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boxwood - an evergreen shrub or tree of the genus Buxus, often used in hedges or topiary
