Archimedean drill

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑrkəˈmiːdiən drɪl / (AmE), / ˌɑːkɪˈmiːdiən drɪl / (BrE)
a small hand-operated drill with a spiral shaft, used for making precise holes in miniatures and crafts
small spiral hand drill for fine work

Examples

She used an Archimedean drill to create tiny holes in the wooden model ship.
An Archimedean drill allows precise control when working with fragile miniature components.
Before fitting the jewelry, he made a pilot hole with his Archimedean drill.

Etymology

Named after Archimedes, reflecting the spiral mechanism inspired by his screw invention.
