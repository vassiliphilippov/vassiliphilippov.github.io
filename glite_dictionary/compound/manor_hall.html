manor hall

1. NOUN

/ ˈmænɚ hɔl / (AmE), / ˈmanə hɔːl / (BrE)
the large principal room in a manor house or castle, often used for gatherings and ceremonies
large main room in a manor or castle

Examples

The banquet was held in the grand manor hall of the estate.
Medieval lords would gather their guests in the manor hall for feasts and celebrations.
The impressive fireplace dominated one end of the manor hall.
