Pilgrim's Progress

1. PROPN

/ ˈpɪlɡɹəmz ˈprɑːɡrɛs / (AmE), / ˈpɪlɡɹɪmz ˈprəʊɡrəs / (BrE)
an allegorical novel written by John Bunyan in 1678, regarded as one of the most significant works in English literature
allegorical novel by John Bunyan (1678)

Examples

Many scholars consider Pilgrim's Progress to be one of the greatest works of religious English literature.
In her essay, she compared her life's journey to the spiritual quest in Pilgrim's Progress.
The library owns an illustrated edition of Pilgrim's Progress that dates back to the nineteenth century.

Etymology

Title of the 1678 allegorical work by John Bunyan, referencing the journey of a Christian pilgrim.
