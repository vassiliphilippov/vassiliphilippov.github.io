satellite television

1. NOUN

/ ˈsætəˌlaɪt ˌtɛləˈvɪʒən / (AmE), / ˈsætəlaɪt ˌtɛlɪˈvɪʒən / (BrE)
a television broadcasting system in which signals are transmitted to and received from orbiting satellites
TV system using signals from satellites

Examples

Many rural areas rely on satellite television because cable service is not available.
The hotel room was equipped with satellite television offering hundreds of channels.
Advancements in satellite television have improved picture quality and signal reliability.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cable television - television system where signals are transmitted via coaxial or fiber-optic cables
