Southern Carpathians

1. PROPN

/ ˈsʌðɚn kɑːrˈpeɪθiənz / (AmE), / ˈsʌðən kɑːˈpɛːθɪənz / (BrE)
a mountain range in the southern part of the Carpathians, extending across central Romania
mountain range in central Romania

Examples

Many hiking trails in the Southern Carpathians offer stunning views of Romania’s valleys and peaks.
Wolves and bears are still found in the forests of the Southern Carpathians.
The Southern Carpathians separate the region of Transylvania from Wallachia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Carpathians - the Carpathian Mountain range in Central and Eastern Europe

Etymology

Carpathians from Latin Carpathus, the name of a mountain region; 'Southern' indicates the southern section of this range.
