unit of time

1. NOUN

/ ˈjuːnɪt əv taɪm / (AmE), / ˈjuːnɪt əv taɪm / (BrE)
a standard quantity used to measure intervals or durations of time, such as a second, minute, or hour
a standard measure of time duration

Examples

The second is the most commonly used unit of time in scientific experiments.
Hours and minutes are familiar units of time for scheduling daily activities.
When converting from one unit of time to another, you often multiply or divide by sixty.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: time unit - an alternative term referring to the same concept
