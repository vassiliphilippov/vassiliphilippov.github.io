maxillary palpus

1. NOUN

/ mækˈsɪləri ˈpælpəs / (AmE), / mækˈsɪləri ˈpalpəs / (BrE)
A small, segmented appendage on the outer side of each maxilla in an insect, believed to have a sensory function.
sensory appendage on insect maxilla
medical

Examples

The scientist examined the maxillary palpus under a microscope to study its structure.
Certain butterflies use their maxillary palpi to sense food sources nearby.
Damage to the maxillary palpus can affect an insect's ability to detect environmental cues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: palpus - a sensory appendage of an arthropod
  • Synonym: antenna - sensory appendage on the heads of insects and crustaceans

Etymology

From Latin 'maxilla' (jawbone) and 'palpus' (touch, feeler).
