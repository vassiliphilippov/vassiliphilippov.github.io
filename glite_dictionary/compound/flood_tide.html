flood tide

1. NOUN

/ flʌd taɪd / (AmE), / flʌd taɪd / (BrE)
the period during which the tide is rising and water flows towards the shore, between low tide and the next high tide
rising period of the tide

Examples

Several fishing boats set out to sea at flood tide to take advantage of the rising water.
The riverbanks were barely visible during the flood tide.
Shellfish gatherers waited for the flood tide before beginning their work.

Synset Examples

a tide in the affairs of men which, taken at the flood, leads on to fortune

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ebb tide - the period when the tide is falling and water flows away from the shore

2. NOUN

/ flʌd taɪd / (AmE), / flʌd tʌɪd / (BrE)
The highest or most successful point of something as it develops or unfolds.
peak or highest point of development

Examples

The company reached the flood tide of its prosperity before the market crash.
At the flood tide of public interest, the campaign was more successful than ever.
She felt she was riding the flood tide of her career after the award.

Synset Examples

the climax of the artist's career
in the flood tide of his success

Related Terms

  • Synonym: climax - the most intense, exciting, or important point of something
  • Synonym: culmination - the highest or climactic point of something
  • Antonym: low tide - the lowest or least successful point of something

Etymology

Originally a nautical term referring to the incoming or rising tide reaching its peak.
