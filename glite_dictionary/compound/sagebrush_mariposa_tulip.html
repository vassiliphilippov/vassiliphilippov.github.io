sagebrush mariposa tulip

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪdʒˌbrʌʃ ˈmærɪˌpoʊsə ˈtuːlɪp / (AmE), / ˈseɪdʒˌbrʌʃ ˈmærɪˌpəʊsə ˈtjuːlɪp / (BrE)
A mariposa lily (Calochortus macrocarpus) native to arid regions of northwestern North America, distinguished by loose clusters of one to three lilac flowers on tall, erect stems.
lilac-flowered mariposa lily of NW North America

Examples

The sagebrush mariposa tulip blooms in May, adding washes of pale purple to the grasslands.
We spotted a cluster of sagebrush mariposa tulips during our hike east of the Cascade Mountains.
Botanists consider the sagebrush mariposa tulip an indicator species for the region's dry hillsides.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mariposa lily - a genus of North American flowering plants with tulip-like flowers
