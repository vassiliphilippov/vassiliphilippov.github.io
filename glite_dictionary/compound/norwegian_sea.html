Norwegian Sea

1. PROPN

/ nɔrˈwi.dʒən si / (AmE), / nɔːˈwiː.dʒən siː / (BrE)
the part of the North Atlantic Ocean lying off the coast of Norway and to the north of the North Sea
sea off the coast of Norway

Examples

The Norwegian Sea is known for its rich marine biodiversity, attracting researchers from around the world.
Strong currents in the Norwegian Sea influence the climate of northern Europe.
During their expedition, the crew encountered rough weather in the Norwegian Sea.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: North Sea - sea to the south of the Norwegian Sea, between Great Britain, Scandinavia, Germany, the Netherlands, Belgium, and France
