discount rate

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɪsˌkaʊnt reɪt / (AmE), / ˈdɪskaʊnt reɪt / (BrE)
the interest rate set by a central bank that is charged to commercial banks for borrowing funds from the central bank
central bank interest rate for banks

Examples

The Federal Reserve decided to lower the discount rate to stimulate the economy.
Banks can borrow money from the central bank at the discount rate when they face short-term liquidity needs.
A change in the discount rate often signals the central bank's monetary policy intentions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interest rate - the proportion of a loan that is charged as interest to the borrower

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɪskaʊnt ˌreɪt / (AmE), / ˈdɪskaʊnt ˌreɪt / (BrE)
the rate of interest charged on a loan, with the interest amount deducted in advance for a set period, usually expressed as an annual percentage
interest rate deducted in advance on a loan

Examples

The bank charges a discount rate of 5% on short-term commercial loans.
Understanding the discount rate is crucial when calculating the true cost of borrowing.
At a lower discount rate, businesses can save more money on advance loans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interest rate - the percentage charged on a loan or paid on deposits
