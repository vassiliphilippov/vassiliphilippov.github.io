valvular incompetence

1. NOUN

/ ˈvælvjələr ɪnˈkɑmpɪtəns / (AmE), / ˈvalvjʊlə ɪnˈkɒmpɪtəns / (BrE)
a medical condition in which a bodily valve is unable to close completely, leading to abnormal blood flow or leakage
inability of a bodily valve to close completely
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with valvular incompetence in the mitral valve, resulting in blood leakage.
Chronic valvular incompetence can lead to heart failure if left untreated.
Doctors used an ultrasound to confirm the presence of valvular incompetence in the affected vein.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: valvular insufficiency - incomplete closure of a valve, resulting in regurgitation

Etymology

From Latin 'valvula' (diminutive of 'valva', leaf of a door) and 'incompetentia' (inability).
