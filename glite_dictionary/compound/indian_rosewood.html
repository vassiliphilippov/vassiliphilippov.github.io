Indian rosewood

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪndiən ˈroʊzˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈɪndiən ˈrəʊzˌwʊd / (BrE)
A large tree native to the Indian subcontinent, valued for its hard, dark-colored wood used especially in furniture and musical instruments.
tree yielding dark hardwood, native to India

Examples

The furniture was crafted from Indian rosewood and had a rich, deep hue.
Many guitars feature backs and sides made of Indian rosewood for their tonal quality.
Demand for Indian rosewood has increased due to its durability and beauty in fine woodworking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Dalbergia latifolia - scientific name for Indian rosewood
  • Degree Higher: rosewood - hardwood from various tropical trees
