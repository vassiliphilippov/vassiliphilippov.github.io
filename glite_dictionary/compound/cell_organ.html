cell organ

1. NOUN

/ sɛl ˈɔrɡən / (AmE), / sɛl ˈɔːɡən / (BrE)
a specialized part within a cell that performs a specific function, similar to an organ in the body
specialized part of a cell

Examples

The mitochondrion is a type of cell organ responsible for producing energy.
Under the microscope, scientists studied the structure of each cell organ.
Damage to a single cell organ can impact the entire cell's function.

Synset Examples

the first organelle to be identified was the nucleus

Related Terms

  • Synonym: organelle - a specialized subunit within a cell that has a specific function
