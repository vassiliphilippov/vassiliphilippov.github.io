organized labor

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔːrɡəˌnaɪzd ˈleɪbɚ / (AmE), / ˈɔːɡənaɪzd ˈleɪbə / (BrE)
employees collectively represented by a labor union or unions, especially in negotiations with employers
workers represented by a trade union

Examples

Organized labor played a key role in securing better wages and working conditions for employees.
The new government policies were opposed by organized labor across the country.
Negotiations between management and organized labor broke down late last night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trade union - an organized association of workers formed to protect and further their rights and interests
