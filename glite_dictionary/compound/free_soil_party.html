Free Soil Party

1. PROPN

/ fri sɔɪl ˈpɑrti / (AmE), / fri sɔɪl ˈpɑːti / (BrE)
a former political party in the United States, founded in 1848, that opposed the expansion of slavery into western territories
U.S. political party (1848–1854) against slavery expansion

Examples

The Free Soil Party attracted voters who opposed the spread of slavery into new U.S. territories.
Members of the Free Soil Party later joined the emerging Republican Party in the 1850s.
The platform of the Free Soil Party was influential in shaping antislavery sentiment in the North.

Etymology

Named from its opposition to the expansion of slavery into the 'free soil' of western territories.
