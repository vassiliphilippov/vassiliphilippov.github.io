acoustic radiation pressure

1. NOUN

/ əˈkuːstɪk ˌreɪdiˈeɪʃən ˈprɛʃər / (AmE), / əˈkuːstɪk ˌreɪdiˈeɪʃən ˈprɛʃə / (BrE)
the pressure exerted on a surface perpendicular to the direction of propagation of a sound wave
pressure caused by sound waves on a surface

Examples

Researchers measured the acoustic radiation pressure on the membrane to study the effects of high-intensity ultrasound.
The acoustic radiation pressure can move tiny particles suspended in a liquid when subjected to focused sound waves.
Understanding acoustic radiation pressure is crucial in designing medical ultrasound equipment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sound pressure - the pressure variation caused by a sound wave

Etymology

From 'acoustic,' relating to sound, and 'radiation pressure,' a term in physics for pressure exerted by energy waves.
