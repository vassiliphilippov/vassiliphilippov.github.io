miotic drug

1. NOUN

/ maɪˈɑtɪk drʌɡ / (AmE), / maɪˈɒtɪk drʌɡ / (BrE)
A drug that causes the pupil of the eye to constrict.
drug that constricts pupil of eye
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed a miotic drug to help reduce the pressure in her eyes.
Pilocarpine is a common miotic drug used to treat glaucoma.
After using the miotic drug, the patient's pupils became noticeably smaller.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: mydriatic drug - a drug that causes dilation of the pupil

Etymology

From Greek 'meiōsis' (diminution) and 'drug'.
