swamp cypress

1. NOUN

/ swɑmp ˈsaɪprəs / (AmE), / swɒmp ˈsaɪprəs / (BrE)
A coniferous tree of the southeastern United States with a trunk that is swollen at the base, commonly found in coastal swamps and flood-prone river areas.
coniferous tree of southeastern US swamps

Examples

The old swamp cypress towered over the edge of the bayou, its roots half-submerged.
Many swamp cypresses can live for hundreds of years in the wetlands of the Mississippi Delta.
We saw turtles basking on the knees of a swamp cypress during our canoe trip.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bald cypress - deciduous coniferous tree found in swamps of the southern United States

2. NOUN

/ swɑmp ˈsaɪprəs / (AmE), / swɒmp ˈsaɪprəs / (BrE)
the wood of a cypress tree native to the southeastern United States, especially from trees with trunks expanded at the base, typically found in coastal swamps and river floodplains
wood of a southeastern US cypress tree

Examples

The furniture was crafted from swamp cypress, prized for its durability and distinctive grain.
In some regions, old cabins are still built using swamp cypress wood for its resistance to decay.
During our canoe trip, we passed through forests of swamp cypress towering above the water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bald cypress - a common name for the tree Taxodium distichum, native to swamps of the southeastern US
