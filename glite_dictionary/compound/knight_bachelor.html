knight bachelor

1. NOUN

/ naɪt ˈbætʃələr / (AmE), / naɪt ˈbætʃələ / (BrE)
A knight of the lowest rank who is not a member of one of the organized orders of chivalry.
lowest rank of knight not in an order

Examples

He was made a knight bachelor for his services to the crown.
As a knight bachelor, he was not part of any chivalric order.
The ceremony honored new knights bachelor alongside other decorated individuals.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: knight - a man awarded a non-hereditary title by a monarch or leader

Etymology

Middle English 'bacheler', from Old French, from Medieval Latin 'baccalarius', meaning a young man or vassal.
