execution speed

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛksɪˈkjuːʃən spiːd / (AmE), / ˌɛksɪˈkjuːʃən spiːd / (BrE)
(computer science) the rate at which a computer or processor executes instructions, typically measured in instructions per second.
rate at which a computer executes instructions

Examples

The new CPU significantly improves execution speed compared to previous models.
Optimizing your code can greatly enhance the execution speed of your application.
Some algorithms are designed specifically to maximize execution speed on modern hardware.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: processing speed - the rate at which a computer processes data
  • Synonym: clock speed - the speed at which a processor's clock operates
