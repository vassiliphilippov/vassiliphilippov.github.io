topological space

1. NOUN

/ təˈpɑːlədʒɪkəl speɪs / (AmE), / təˈpɒlədʒɪkəl speɪs / (BrE)
A set of points together with a collection of subsets (called open sets) that satisfies specific axioms, forming the fundamental object of study in topology.
mathematical set with a topology
medical

Examples

A compact topological space has many interesting properties in analysis.
Every metric space is also a topological space but not vice versa.
The notion of continuity depends fundamentally on the definition of a topological space.

Synset Examples

assume that the topological space is finite dimensional

Etymology

Formed from 'topology' (from Greek 'topos' meaning 'place') and 'space'.
