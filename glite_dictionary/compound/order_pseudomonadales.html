order Pseudomonadales

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdər ˌsjuːdoʊˌmænəˈdeɪliːz / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə ˌsjuːdəʊˌmænəˈdeɪliːz / (BrE)
A taxonomic order of Gram-negative bacteria, typically spiral, spherical, or rod-shaped, many of which are motile by polar flagella; includes some species with photosynthetic pigments.
order of Gram-negative bacteria
medical

Examples

Scientists discovered a new species within the order Pseudomonadales in tropical soils.
Many members of the order Pseudomonadales are important in the study of plant diseases.
The order Pseudomonadales includes bacteria that can survive in various environments.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: order Bacillales - another order of bacteria, Gram-positive

Etymology

From Greek 'pseudo-' (false) and 'monas' (unit), referring to the type genus Pseudomonas.
