drepanocytic anaemia

1. NOUN

/ ˌdrɛpəˈnɑsɪtɪk əˈniːmiə / (AmE), / ˌdrɛpənəˈsɪtɪk əˈniːmiə / (BrE)
A congenital form of anemia, mainly affecting people of African descent, characterized by red blood cells that have a crescent or sickle shape instead of the normal disc shape.
anemia with crescent-shaped red blood cells
medical british

Examples

Drepanocytic anaemia is particularly prevalent in regions where malaria is or was common.
Patients with drepanocytic anaemia often experience episodes of severe pain due to blocked blood vessels.
Genetic counseling is recommended for families affected by drepanocytic anaemia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sickle-cell anemia - A congenital anemia with sickle-shaped red blood cells, common in people of African descent

Etymology

From Greek 'drepanon' (sickle) and 'kytos' (cell), with 'anaemia' from Greek 'anaimia'.
