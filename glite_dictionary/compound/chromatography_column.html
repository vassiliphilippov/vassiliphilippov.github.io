chromatography column

1. NOUN

/ kroʊˌmætəˈɡræfi ˈkɑləm / (AmE), / krəʊˌmætəˈɡræfi ˈkɒləm / (BrE)
A vertical tube, usually made of glass, used in column chromatography in which a mixture is separated by passing it through a stationary material that adsorbs the components selectively.
vertical tube for column chromatography

Examples

The scientist loaded the mixture onto the chromatography column to begin the separation process.
After several hours, colored bands appeared inside the chromatography column, indicating successful separation of the compounds.
Proper maintenance of a chromatography column is essential for accurate and reproducible results in laboratory experiments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: column chromatography - Separation technique using a vertical column

Etymology

From 'chromatography', which comes from Greek *khrōma* (color) and *graphein* (to write), and 'column'.
