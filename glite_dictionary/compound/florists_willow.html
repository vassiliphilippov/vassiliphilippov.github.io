florist's willow

1. NOUN

/ flɔːrɪsts ˈwɪloʊ / (AmE), / flɒrɪsts ˈwɪləʊ / (BrE)
a willow tree with many branches, large catkins, and broad leaves, used especially in floral arrangements
a willow tree used in floristry

Examples

The florist's willow is prized for its attractive broad leaves and flexible stems.
She arranged branches of florist's willow in the vase to add height to the bouquet.
Many gardens feature florist's willow for its ornamental catkins in early spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: willow - a tree or shrub of the genus Salix, with slender branches and narrow leaves
