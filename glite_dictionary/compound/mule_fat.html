mule fat

1. NOUN

/ ˈmjuːl fæt / (AmE), / ˈmjuːl fæt / (BrE)
a Californian shrub (Baccharis salicifolia) with slender leafy shoots, important as browse for mule deer
Californian browse shrub (Baccharis salicifolia)

Examples

The mule fat growing along the riverbank provides food for wildlife throughout the year.
Biologists studied how mule fat contributes to the habitat of native animals in California.
After the fire, patches of mule fat quickly began to regrow in the burned area.
