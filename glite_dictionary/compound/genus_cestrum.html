genus Cestrum

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈsɛs.trəm / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈsɛs.trəm / (BrE)
A genus of fragrant, tropical American shrubs belonging to the nightshade family, often cultivated for their attractive flowers.
genus of fragrant tropical American shrubs

Examples

Several species in the genus Cestrum are prized for their fragrant evening blooms.
The genus Cestrum includes both ornamental and invasive shrubs in many warm regions.
Botanists studied the distribution of the genus Cestrum across South America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cestrum - A genus of tropical shrubs in the nightshade family

Etymology

From Latin 'cestrum', a kind of plant, from Greek 'kestron' (a plant name).
