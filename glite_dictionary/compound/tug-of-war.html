tug-of-war

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʌɡ əv ˌwɔr / (AmE), / ˌtʌɡ əv ˈwɔː / (BrE)
a contest in which two teams pull on opposite ends of a rope, with the aim of dragging the other team across a central line
team sport using a rope

Examples

Every summer, our village holds a tug-of-war during the festival to test the teams' strength.
The tug-of-war ended quickly when one team slipped on the muddy ground.
As a child, she loved participating in the annual tug-of-war at school sports day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contest - an event in which people compete
  • Synonym: game - an activity that one engages in for amusement or competition

Etymology

From the verb 'tug' (to pull with force) and 'war', reflecting the competitive pulling struggle.

2. NOUN

/ ˌtʌɡ əv ˈwɔɹ / (AmE), / ˌtʌɡ əv ˈwɔː / (BrE)
a hard struggle between two evenly matched people or groups, often over power, control, or an important outcome
struggle between evenly matched groups

Examples

There was a constant tug-of-war between management and staff over working hours.
The debate turned into a political tug-of-war that lasted for months.
Negotiations became a mental tug-of-war as neither side wanted to compromise.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: struggle - a determined effort or fight to overcome difficulty or opposition
  • Synonym: conflict - a serious disagreement or argument

Etymology

From the literal contest involving teams pulling on opposite ends of a rope.
