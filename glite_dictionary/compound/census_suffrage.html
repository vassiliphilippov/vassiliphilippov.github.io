census suffrage

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛnsəs ˈsʌfrɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈsɛnsəs ˈsʌfrɪdʒ / (BrE)
a system of voting in which the value of each person's vote varies according to their rank or status as determined by the census, such as wealth or income level
voting system weighted by social or economic status

Examples

Under census suffrage, wealthy citizens exerted far greater influence over election outcomes.
The reform movement campaigned to replace census suffrage with universal suffrage.
Many 19th-century European countries used systems of census suffrage before adopting more democratic voting rights.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: universal suffrage - the right of all adult citizens to vote regardless of status
  • Synonym: plural voting - a system where some people have more than one vote

Etymology

From 'census', Latin for 'assessment', and 'suffrage', Latin 'suffragium' meaning 'vote'.
