family Anhimidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌænəˈmaɪdiː / (AmE), / ˌænɪˈmɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of South American birds known as screamers, characterized by their loud calls and unique skeletal features.
family of South American screamers (birds)

Examples

The family Anhimidae contains only three extant species, all native to South America.
Members of the family Anhimidae are notable for their powerful vocalizations.
Ornithologists studied the skeletal structure of birds in the family Anhimidae to better understand their evolution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: screamer - any of several South American birds of the family Anhimidae

Etymology

From New Latin Anhimidae, based on the type genus Anhima.
