knock knock

1. NOUN

/ nɑk nɑk / (AmE), / nɒk nɒk / (BrE)
A simple joke or riddle format, typically starting with the phrase 'Knock knock!' and involving a call-and-response interaction.
type of simple joke or riddle

Examples

My little brother loves to tell knock knock jokes at the dinner table.
The teacher opened her lesson with a classic knock knock to make the class laugh.
We took turns inventing new knock knock jokes during the long car ride.

Synset Examples

Knock knock! Who's there?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: joke - something said or done to provoke laughter
