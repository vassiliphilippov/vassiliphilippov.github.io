bohunk stereotype

1. NOUN

/ boʊˌhʌŋk ˈstɛriəˌtaɪp / (AmE), / bəʊˌhʌŋk ˈstɛriəˌtaɪp / (BrE)
A derogatory stereotype or perception about Eastern European laborers, especially immigrants, often seen as unskilled or coarse.
derogatory stereotype of Eastern European laborers
offensive

Examples

The bohunk stereotype was commonly invoked in early 20th-century American newspapers to describe immigrant workers.
His views unfortunately reflected the outdated bohunk stereotype of Eastern European laborers.
Sociologists have studied how the bohunk stereotype influenced public perceptions of immigrant communities.

Synset Examples

The bohunk stereotype often depicts laborers from Europe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bohunk - offensive term for an Eastern European immigrant laborer
  • Synonym: stereotype - a widely held but oversimplified idea of a particular type of person

Etymology

From 'bohunk', itself a blend of 'Bohemian' and 'Hungarian', referring to immigrants from Central and Eastern Europe.
