blood bank

1. NOUN

/ blʌd bæŋk / (AmE), / blʌd bæŋk / (BrE)
an organization or facility that collects, stores, and supplies blood or blood plasma, typically for medical use
organization or place for storing blood

Examples

After the accident, the hospital relied on the blood bank to provide enough blood for surgeries.
Many people donate blood at their local blood bank to help patients in need.
The city opened a new blood bank to ensure an adequate supply during emergencies.

Synset Examples

the Red Cross created a blood bank for emergencies

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blood transfusion - the process of transferring blood or blood products into one's circulation intravenously
  • Synonym: organ bank - a place where organs are preserved for future transplantation
