scare quote

1. NOUN

/ skɛr kwoʊt / (AmE), / skɛə kwəʊt / (BrE)
a quotation mark or pair of quotation marks placed around a word or phrase to indicate that it is not the writer's preferred term, often to suggest irony, skepticism, or disagreement
quotation marks used to express skepticism or distance

Examples

Some journalists use scare quotes to signal skepticism about certain political terms.
She put the word 'natural' in scare quotes to question the product's authenticity.
The article relied heavily on scare quotes around the word 'expert' to undermine their credibility.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quotation mark - punctuation mark used to indicate speech or quotations
