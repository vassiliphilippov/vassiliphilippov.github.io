genus Menziesia

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs mɛnˈziːziə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs mɛnˈziːziə / (BrE)
A genus of deciduous shrubs native to North America and eastern Asia, formerly recognized in the heath family (Ericaceae).
genus of deciduous shrubs

Examples

Some species in the genus Menziesia produce clusters of pink or purple flowers in spring.
The genus Menziesia was once considered separate but is now often included in the genus Rhododendron.
Botanists studied the distribution of the genus Menziesia across various mountain ranges.

Etymology

Named after Archibald Menzies, Scottish naturalist (1754–1842).
