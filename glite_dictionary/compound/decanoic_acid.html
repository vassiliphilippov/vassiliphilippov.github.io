decanoic acid

1. NOUN

/ dɛˈkeɪniɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / dɛˈkeɪnɪk ˈæsɪd / (BrE)
a saturated fatty acid found in animal fats and oils, with an unpleasant odor similar to that of goats
a saturated fatty acid in animal oils
medical

Examples

Decanoic acid is sometimes called capric acid in scientific literature.
Researchers studied the effects of decanoic acid on metabolic pathways in animals.
The distinctive odor of goats is partly due to the presence of decanoic acid in their fat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: capric acid - another name for decanoic acid

Etymology

From deca- (prefix meaning 'ten') and -anoic (relating to carboxylic acids), referring to its 10-carbon structure.
