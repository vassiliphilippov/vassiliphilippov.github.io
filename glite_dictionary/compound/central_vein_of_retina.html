central vein of retina

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛntrəl veɪn əv rəˈtaɪnə / (AmE), / ˈsɛntrəl veɪn əv rɪˈtiːnə / (BrE)
the vein formed by the union of the retinal veins, accompanying the central arteries of the retina within the optic nerve
major vein draining blood from the retina
medical

Examples

A blockage in the central vein of retina can result in sudden vision loss.
Ophthalmologists often examine the central vein of retina when diagnosing retinal diseases.
The central vein of retina exits the eye through the optic nerve.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: central artery of retina - the artery that supplies blood to the retina
