Orly Group

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrli ɡruːp / (AmE), / ˈɔːli ɡruːp / (BrE)
a Marxist-Leninist militant organization formed in 1975 seeking to compel Turkey to acknowledge the Armenian genocide, pay reparations, and cede territory to Armenia; known for orchestrating attacks including the 1983 Orly Airport bombing
Armenian militant group active 1975–1980s

Examples

The Orly Group claimed responsibility for the bombing at Orly Airport in 1983.
Authorities increased security measures across Europe following Orly Group attacks targeting Turkish interests.
Few had heard of the Orly Group before its violent campaign made international headlines.

Synset Examples

ASALA bombing at Orly Airport in Paris in 1983 killed 8 and wounded 55 people
