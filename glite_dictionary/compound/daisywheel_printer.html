daisywheel printer

1. NOUN

/ ˈdeɪziˌwiːl ˈprɪntər / (AmE), / ˈdeɪziˌwiːl ˈprɪntə / (BrE)
a type of printer that forms characters by striking a wheel with petal-like extensions, each carrying a letter or symbol, against an ink ribbon
printer using a rotating daisy-shaped wheel

Examples

The office used a daisywheel printer to produce documents with high-quality text.
In the 1980s, many writers relied on a daisywheel printer for their manuscripts.
Replacing the ink ribbon on a daisywheel printer was a common maintenance task.

Etymology

From 'daisywheel', so named for its resemblance to a daisy flower, plus 'printer'.
