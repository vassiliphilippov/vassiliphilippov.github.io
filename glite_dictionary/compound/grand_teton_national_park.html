Grand Teton National Park

1. PROPN

/ ɡrænd ˈtiːtɑn ˌnæʃənl ˈpɑrk / (AmE), / ɡrænd ˈtiːtɒn ˌnæʃənəl ˈpɑːk / (BrE)
A national park in northwestern Wyoming, USA, noted for its rugged mountains and scenic beauty.
national park in Wyoming, USA

Examples

We spent a week hiking in Grand Teton National Park last summer.
The scenery in Grand Teton National Park rivals that of nearby Yellowstone.
Many wildlife photographers visit Grand Teton National Park to capture images of elk and bears.

Etymology

Named after the Grand Teton mountain, from French 'les trois tétons' (the three nipples), referring to the shape of the peaks.
