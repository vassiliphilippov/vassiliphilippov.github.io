unit cell

1. NOUN

/ ˈjuːnɪt sɛl / (AmE), / ˈjuːnɪt sɛl / (BrE)
the smallest structural unit in a crystal lattice, whose repetition in three dimensions forms the entire crystal structure
smallest structural unit in a crystal lattice

Examples

The dimensions of the unit cell determine many of the crystal's physical properties.
Each unit cell in the lattice contains two sodium and two chloride ions.
Scientists use X-ray diffraction to measure the size and shape of the unit cell.

Etymology

Formed from 'unit' (single, indivisible) and 'cell' (small chamber or compartment).
