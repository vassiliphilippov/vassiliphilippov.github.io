creature comforts

1. NOUN

/ ˈkriːtʃɚ ˌkʌmfɚts / (AmE), / ˈkriːtʃə ˌkʌmfəts / (BrE)
material things or conveniences that make life comfortable and pleasant
things that provide physical comfort

Examples

After a long hike, she was happy to return to the creature comforts of her home.
He can camp for weeks in the mountains, never missing the creature comforts most people require.
Traveling light means giving up some creature comforts for the sake of adventure.

Synset Examples

all the comforts of home

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: luxury - great comfort or elegance, especially when involving great expense
  • Degree Lower: necessity - something essential or indispensable

Etymology

From 'creature' (living being) and 'comforts', originally referring to comforts enjoyed by living beings.
