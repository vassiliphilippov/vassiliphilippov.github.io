Treasury Secretary

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrɛʒəri səˌkrɛtɛri / (AmE), / ˈtrɛʒəri səˈkrɛtəri / (BrE)
the government official who heads the Treasury Department, especially in the United States
government official in charge of the Treasury

Examples

Janet Yellen became the first female Treasury Secretary of the United States in 2021.
The Treasury Secretary is responsible for formulating economic policy and overseeing the nation's finances.
President Biden met with his Treasury Secretary to discuss the upcoming budget proposals.

Synset Examples

Alexander Hamilton was the first Secretary of the Treasury

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Secretary of the Treasury - official title for the head of the U.S. Treasury Department

2. NOUN

/ ˈtrɛʒəri səˌkrɛtɛri / (AmE), / ˈtrɛʒəri səˌkrɛtəri / (BrE)
the official in charge of a government's Treasury Department, especially in the United States; responsible for managing government finances, economic policy, and financial regulation
head of a government's Treasury Department

Examples

Janet Yellen became the first woman to serve as Treasury Secretary of the United States.
The Treasury Secretary plays a crucial role in crafting fiscal policy and managing national debt.
After the financial crisis, the new Treasury Secretary implemented measures to stabilize the economy.

Synset Examples

the position of Treasury Secretary was created in 1789

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Chancellor of the Exchequer - British government official in charge of economic and financial matters
