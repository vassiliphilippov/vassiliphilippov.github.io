justice of the peace

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒʌstɪs əv ðə pis / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌstɪs əv ðə piːs / (BrE)
a magistrate appointed to keep the peace and perform minor legal duties such as witnessing documents, performing marriages, or hearing minor cases
local magistrate with minor legal powers
legal

Examples

The couple was married by a justice of the peace in a small ceremony.
If you have a minor legal dispute, you might appear before a justice of the peace.
As a justice of the peace, she can witness legal documents for community members.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: magistrate - a civil officer who administers the law, especially in minor cases

Etymology

From Middle English, meaning ‘judge for keeping the peace’.
