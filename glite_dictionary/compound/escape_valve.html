escape valve

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈskeɪp vælv / (AmE), / ɪˈskeɪp vælv / (BrE)
a valve in a container or system designed to release excess pressure automatically when it becomes dangerously high
valve for releasing excess pressure

Examples

The engineer checked the escape valve to make sure it was functioning properly before starting the boiler.
Without a working escape valve, pressure could build up dangerously inside the tank.
During the test, steam was released through the escape valve to prevent an explosion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: safety valve - a valve that automatically releases substances from a boiler, pressure vessel, or other system when pressure exceeds safe levels

2. NOUN

/ ɪˈskeɪp vælv / (AmE), / ɪˈskeɪp vælv / (BrE)
a device fitted to a container or system to release excess pressure for safety purposes
device to release excess pressure for safety

Examples

The escape valve activates when pressure inside the vessel becomes too high.
It is important to check that the escape valve is functioning properly before using the machine.
Factory boilers are fitted with an escape valve to prevent dangerous explosions.

Synset Examples
