real dambuster

1. NOUN

/ ˈriːəl ˈdæmˌbʌstər / (AmE), / ˈrɪəl ˈdæmˌbʌstə / (BrE)
A person who achieves a notable or significant success, especially in a challenging situation.
person who achieves a significant success
british

Examples

After their dramatic comeback, the coach called his team a group of real dambusters.
She proved herself a real dambuster by solving the problem no one else could tackle.
The journalist became known as a real dambuster for his ground-breaking investigative work.

Synset Examples

He was hailed as a real dambuster after the successful mission.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hero - a person admired for courage or outstanding achievements
  • Synonym: winner - a person who wins a competition or achieves success

Etymology

From the RAF 617 Squadron ('Dambusters'), famed for destroying German dams during WWII; used figuratively for great achievement.
