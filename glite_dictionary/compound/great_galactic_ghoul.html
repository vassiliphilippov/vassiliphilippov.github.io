Great Galactic Ghoul

1. PROPN

/ ɡreɪt ɡəˈlæk.tɪk ɡuːl / (AmE), / ɡreɪt ɡəˈlæk.tɪk ɡuːl / (BrE)
a mythical creature or fictional character said to devour spacecraft or stars in science fiction stories, particularly relating to Mars exploration
mythical creature from science fiction

Examples

Many engineers jokingly blame the Great Galactic Ghoul for failed Mars missions.
According to lore, the Great Galactic Ghoul lurks near the Red Planet.
Children love imagining stories featuring the mischievous Great Galactic Ghoul devouring lost spaceships.

Synset Examples

Children tell stories about the Great Galactic Ghoul during campfires.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ghoul - an evil spirit or phantom, especially one said to rob graves

Etymology

Coined as a humorous term in space science, blending 'ghoul' with 'galactic' to name a mythical being blamed for failed missions to Mars.
