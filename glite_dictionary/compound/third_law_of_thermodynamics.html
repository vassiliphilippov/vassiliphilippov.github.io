third law of thermodynamics

1. NOUN

/ θɝd lɔ əv ˌθɜrməˈdaɪnəmɪks / (AmE), / θɜːd lɔː əv ˌθɜːməʊˈdaɪnəmɪks / (BrE)
the law stating that the entropy of a perfect crystal approaches zero as its temperature approaches absolute zero
law about entropy at absolute zero

Examples

According to the third law of thermodynamics, it is impossible to reach absolute zero in a finite number of steps.
The third law of thermodynamics explains why entropy becomes minimal at extremely low temperatures.
Scientists rely on the third law of thermodynamics to understand behavior of matter close to absolute zero.
