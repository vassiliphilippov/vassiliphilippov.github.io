Windward Passage

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪndwɚd ˈpæsɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈwɪndwəd ˈpæsɪdʒ / (BrE)
a strait between eastern Cuba and western Haiti, connecting the Atlantic Ocean with the Caribbean Sea
strait between Cuba and Haiti

Examples

Cargo ships often travel through the Windward Passage on their way to and from the Caribbean Sea.
Hurricanes sometimes pass near the Windward Passage, causing rough seas and dangerous conditions for navigation.
The Windward Passage played a strategic role in maritime history due to its important location.
