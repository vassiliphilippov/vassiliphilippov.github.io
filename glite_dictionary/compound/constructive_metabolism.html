constructive metabolism

1. NOUN

/ kənˈstrʌktɪv məˈtæbəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / kənˈstrʌktɪv məˈtæbəlɪzəm / (BrE)
the process in living organisms by which simpler substances are converted into more complex compounds, such as tissues, often with the storage of energy; also called anabolism
the synthesis of complex substances from simpler ones in organisms
medical

Examples

During childhood, constructive metabolism is particularly active as tissues and organs develop rapidly.
Protein intake is essential for constructive metabolism to build muscles.
Hormonal imbalances can disrupt the normal process of constructive metabolism in the body.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anabolism - the process of building up complex substances from simpler ones in living organisms
  • Antonym: catabolism - the breakdown of complex substances into simpler ones in living organisms, often releasing energy

Etymology

From 'constructive' (Latin constructivus) and 'metabolism' (from Greek metabolē, 'change').
