grizzly bear

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡrɪz.li bɛr / (AmE), / ˈɡrɪz.li beə / (BrE)
a large, powerful brownish-yellow subspecies of bear native to the uplands of western North America
large brown bear of western North America

Examples

We spotted a grizzly bear near the edge of the forest during our hike.
The grizzly bear is known for its great strength and imposing presence in the wild.
Park rangers warn visitors to store food safely to avoid attracting grizzly bears.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bear - a large heavy mammal with thick fur and a very short tail

Etymology

From 'grizzly' (having grey or silver-tipped hair) + 'bear'.
