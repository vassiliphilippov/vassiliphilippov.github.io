scented fern

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛntɪd fɝn / (AmE), / ˈsɛntɪd fɜːn / (BrE)
a fern native to eastern North America with pale green fronds that give off a scent similar to hay
fern with pale green, hay-scented fronds

Examples

The scented fern gives off a pleasant aroma when its leaves are crushed.
She planted a scented fern in the shady corner of her garden.
Wild scented ferns grew abundantly along the hiking trail.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɛntɪd fɜrn / (AmE), / ˈsɛntɪd fɜːn / (BrE)
A common perennial aromatic herb native to Eurasia, with yellow button-like flower heads and bitter-tasting pinnate leaves, sometimes used medicinally.
aromatic Eurasian perennial herb

Examples

Medicinal tea can be made from scented fern leaves despite their bitter taste.
The garden's border was lined with scented fern to repel insects naturally.
In traditional remedies, extracts of scented fern were once used to treat digestive issues.
