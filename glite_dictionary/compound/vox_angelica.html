vox angelica

1. NOUN

/ vɑks ænˈdʒɛlɪkə / (AmE), / vɒks ænˈdʒɛlɪkə / (BrE)
an organ stop producing a gentle tremolo effect, imitating the sound of a human voice
organ stop with a soft tremolo effect

Examples

The organist selected the vox angelica stop to add a gentle, ethereal quality to the music.
Her favorite part of the recital was when the vox angelica created a soft, trembling melody.
Many church organs include a vox angelica for pieces requiring a delicate, voice-like effect.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: celeste - an organ stop producing an undulating or wavering sound

Etymology

From Latin, meaning ‘voice of an angel’.
