posterior serratus muscle

1. NOUN

/ pəˈstɪriɚ səˈreɪtəs ˈmʌsəl / (AmE), / pɒˈstɪəri.əs səˈreɪtəs ˈmʌsl̩ / (BrE)
a muscle of the back that pulls the rib cage backward and downward
back muscle that lowers rib cage
medical

Examples

The posterior serratus muscle helps in expanding the chest during deep breathing.
Injuries to the posterior serratus muscle can affect proper movement of the rib cage.
Physical therapists may target the posterior serratus muscle during rehabilitation exercises for upper back pain.

Etymology

From Latin 'posterior' (behind) and 'serratus' (saw-shaped).
