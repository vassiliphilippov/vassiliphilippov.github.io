Cahita language

1. NOUN

/ kɑːˈhiːtə ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / kəˈhiːtə ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Uto-Aztecan language spoken by the Cahita people of northwestern Mexico.
Uto-Aztecan language of the Cahita people

Examples

Linguists have studied the Cahita language to understand the cultural history of the region.
Many words in Spanish spoken in Sinaloa are borrowed from the Cahita language.
The preservation of the Cahita language is important for the identity of the Cahita people.

Etymology

From the name 'Cahita,' an indigenous group of northwestern Mexico.
