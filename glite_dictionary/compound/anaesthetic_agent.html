anaesthetic agent

1. NOUN

/ ˌænəsˈθɛtɪk ˈeɪdʒənt / (AmE), / ˌanɪsˈθɛtɪk ˈeɪdʒənt / (BrE)
a drug that induces temporary loss of sensation in the body, particularly to prevent pain during medical procedures
drug causing loss of sensation
medical british

Examples

The surgeon administered an anaesthetic agent before beginning the operation.
Different types of anaesthetic agents are chosen depending on the procedure and patient.
Modern anaesthetic agents are designed to be effective while minimizing side effects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anaesthetic - a substance that causes loss of sensation
  • Antonym: analgesic - a drug that relieves pain without loss of sensation

Etymology

From Greek 'anaisthēsia' (insensibility) + Latin 'agens' (doing, acting).
