elastic tissue

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈlæstɪk ˈtɪʃu / (AmE), / ɪˈlæstɪk ˈtɪsjuː / (BrE)
a type of connective tissue composed mainly of elastic fibers, found in the skin, walls of arteries and veins, and some tendons and ligaments
connective tissue with elastic fibers
medical

Examples

The aorta contains a large amount of elastic tissue to withstand high blood pressure.
Damage to elastic tissue in the skin can lead to reduced flexibility as we age.
Certain hereditary disorders can affect the elastic tissue in blood vessel walls.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: connective tissue - tissue that supports, connects, or separates different types of tissues or organs
