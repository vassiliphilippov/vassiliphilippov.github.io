financial gain

1. NOUN

/ fəˈnænʃəl ɡeɪn / (AmE), / faɪˈnænʃəl ɡeɪn / (BrE)
an increase in money or assets resulting from an investment, transaction, or business activity
increase in money or assets

Examples

The company invested in new technology for financial gain rather than for better service.
He made a considerable financial gain from the sale of his house.
They were motivated primarily by the prospect of financial gain, not by charity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: profit - money earned after all expenses are paid
  • Antonym: loss - money lost rather than earned
