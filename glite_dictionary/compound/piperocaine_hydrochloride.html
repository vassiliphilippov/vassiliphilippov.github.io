piperocaine hydrochloride

1. NOUN

/ paɪpərəˌkoʊin haɪˌdrɒklaɪd / (AmE), / paɪpərəˌkəʊiːn haɪˌdrɒklaɪd / (BrE)
a compound used as a local or spinal anesthetic, typically in medical procedures
local or spinal anesthetic compound
medical

Examples

Piperocaine hydrochloride is administered to numb the lower part of the body during surgery.
Some dentists may use piperocaine hydrochloride as a local anesthetic for minor procedures.
The doctor explained the potential side effects of piperocaine hydrochloride before starting the spinal anesthesia.

Etymology

From 'piperocaine', based on 'piperidine' (a chemical compound) and the chemical term 'hydrochloride'.
