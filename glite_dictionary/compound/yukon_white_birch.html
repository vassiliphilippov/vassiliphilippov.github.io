Yukon white birch

1. NOUN

/ ˈjuːkən waɪt bɜrtʃ / (AmE), / ˈjuːkɒn waɪt bɜːtʃ / (BrE)
A species of birch tree native to Alaska and northwestern Canada, characterized by its white to pale brown bark.
birch tree native to Alaska and the Yukon

Examples

The Yukon white birch thrives in the forests of Alaska and northern Canada.
We admired the pale bark of the Yukon white birch during our hike.
Researchers are studying how the Yukon white birch adapts to colder climates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: birch - a tree with thin, smooth bark and slender branches
