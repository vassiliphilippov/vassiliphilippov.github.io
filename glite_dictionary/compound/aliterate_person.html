aliterate person

1. NOUN

/ əˈlɪt̬ərət ˈpɝːsən / (AmE), / əˈlɪtərət ˈpɜːsən / (BrE)
a person who is able to read but chooses not to read or is unwilling to gain information from written material
person able to read but chooses not to

Examples

Despite having access to books, Tom became an aliterate person who rarely read anything beyond social media.
Many educators are concerned about the increasing number of aliterate persons in today's society.
An aliterate person may have the skill to read but lacks the motivation to do so regularly.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: illiterate person - a person who cannot read or write

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'a-' (not, without) + 'literate' (able to read) + 'person'.
