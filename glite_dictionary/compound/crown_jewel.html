crown jewel

1. NOUN

/ kraʊn ˈdʒuːəl / (AmE), / kraʊn ˈdʒuːəl / (BrE)
a precious stone that forms an important and valuable part of a monarch's ceremonial regalia
precious stone in a monarch's regalia

Examples

The Koh-i-Noor diamond is considered a crown jewel of the British monarchy.
The theft of a crown jewel from the royal palace caused a major scandal.
Many tourists visit the tower to catch a glimpse of the crown jewels on display.

2. NOUN

/ kraʊn ˈdʒuː.əl / (AmE), / kraʊn ˈdʒuː.əl / (BrE)
the most valuable or prized asset or possession, especially the most important part of a business or organization
the most valuable asset

Examples

During the merger, the company's crown jewel was its popular software division.
The new CEO decided to sell off all but the crown jewels of the business.
Analysts agreed that the airline's routes in Asia were its true crown jewels.

Synset Examples

their crown jewels figured prominently in the takeover attempt

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prize asset - a valuable and desirable asset, often targeted in acquisitions
  • Antonym: liability - something that acts as a disadvantage or burden, especially in business

Etymology

Originally refers to the actual jewels in a monarch's crown; figurative use dates from the late 20th century.
