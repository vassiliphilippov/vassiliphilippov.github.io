river horse

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɪvɚ hɔrs / (AmE), / ˈrɪvə hɔːs / (BrE)
An old-fashioned term for the hippopotamus, a large thick-skinned herbivorous mammal living in or near African rivers.
hippopotamus; large African river mammal
archaic

Examples

In the story, the explorers spotted a river horse basking on the muddy riverbank.
Long ago, people believed the river horse to be the most dangerous creature in Africa.
The guide explained that the river horse is known today as the hippopotamus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hippopotamus - Large thick-skinned herbivorous African mammal

Etymology

From the literal translation of Greek hippopotamos ('hippos' meaning horse and 'potamos' meaning river).
