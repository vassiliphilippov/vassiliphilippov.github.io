civil engineering

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪvəl ˌɛn.dʒɪˈnɪr.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsɪv.əl ˌɛn.dʒɪˈnɪə.rɪŋ / (BrE)
The branch of engineering concerned with the design, construction, and maintenance of public works such as roads, bridges, and dams.
branch of engineering for public works

Examples

He studied civil engineering at university and specialized in building bridges.
The field of civil engineering is essential for constructing safe roads and infrastructure.
Many civil engineering projects focus on water supply and management systems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: structural engineering - the branch of engineering concerned with the design and analysis of structures
  • Antonym: mechanical engineering - the branch of engineering dealing with machinery and mechanical systems

Etymology

From Latin 'civīlis' (relating to citizens) and 'engineering' from Latin 'ingenium' (talent, device).
