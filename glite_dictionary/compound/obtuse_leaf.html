obtuse leaf

1. NOUN

/ əbˈtus liːf / (AmE), / əbˈtjuːs liːf / (BrE)
A simple leaf with a rounded or blunt tip, rather than a pointed one.
leaf with a blunt, rounded tip

Examples

The obtuse leaf of this plant distinguishes it from other species with pointed foliage.
In the diagram, the obtuse leaf is labeled to show its rounded tip.
The botanist collected several specimens with an obtuse leaf shape for her research.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: acute leaf - a leaf with a sharp, pointed tip

Etymology

From Latin 'obtusus' (blunted, dull) + 'leaf'.
