oyster plant

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔɪ.stɚ ˌplænt / (AmE), / ˈɔɪ.stə ˌplɑːnt / (BrE)
The edible root of the salsify plant, often used as a vegetable and noted for its oyster-like flavor.
edible root of the salsify plant

Examples

The chef prepared a delicious soup using fresh oyster plant from the market.
When cooked, the oyster plant has a taste that many compare to oysters.
She grew oyster plant in her garden for its unique flavor and nutritional value.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: salsify - a plant with an edible root, sometimes called oyster plant

a plant with long white roots and edible leaves, especially salsify (Tragopogon porrifolius), whose root has an oyster-like flavor
a plant, especially salsify, with edible white roots

Examples

I roasted the oyster plant with garlic for a unique, slightly seafood-like taste.
The chef recommended using oyster plant as a substitute for oysters in vegetarian dishes.
Her grandmother used to grow oyster plant in the garden and serve the roots every winter.

3. NOUN

/ ˈɔɪstər ˌplænt / (AmE), / ˈɔɪstə ˌplɑːnt / (BrE)
A Mediterranean biennial herb (Tragopogon porrifolius) with long-stemmed purple flowers and an edible root, cultivated as a vegetable and naturalized in parts of the United States.
Mediterranean herb with edible root

Examples

She roasted oyster plant and served it with a garlic sauce for dinner.
The roots of the oyster plant are prized for their delicate, oyster-like flavor.
Gardeners often grow oyster plant for its unique taste and attractive purple flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: salsify - another name for oyster plant, especially the cultivated variety

Etymology

Named for the oyster-like flavor of its root.
