prairie vole

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɛri voʊl / (AmE), / ˈpreəri vəʊl / (BrE)
a small rodent species (Microtus ochrogaster) found in the prairie regions of central United States and southern Canada
small rodent of North American prairies

Examples

The prairie vole is known for forming long-term pair bonds with its mate.
Scientists study the prairie vole to understand social behavior in mammals.
We spotted a prairie vole scurrying through the tall grass during our field trip.
