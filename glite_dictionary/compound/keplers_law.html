Kepler's law

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɛplɚz lɔ / (AmE), / ˈkɛpləz lɔː / (BrE)
any of the three empirical laws of planetary motion formulated by Johannes Kepler, describing the movement of planets around the sun
law of planetary motion in astronomy

Examples

According to Kepler's law, the planets follow elliptical orbits around the Sun.
The discovery of Kepler's law was a pivotal moment in the history of astronomy.
We studied Kepler's law in our physics class to understand planetary motion.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Kepler's first law - the law stating planets move in elliptical orbits with the Sun at one focus
  • Degree Higher: Kepler's second law - the law stating a line joining a planet and the Sun sweeps out equal areas in equal times
  • Degree Higher: Kepler's third law - the law stating the square of a planet's orbital period is proportional to the cube of its orbit's semi-major axis

Etymology

Named after German astronomer Johannes Kepler (1571–1630).
