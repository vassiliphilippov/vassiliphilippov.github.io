capillary tubing

1. NOUN

/ kəˈpɪləri ˈtuːbɪŋ / (AmE), / kəˈpɪləri ˈtjuːbɪŋ / (BrE)
a very thin tube with a small internal diameter, used especially to hold or transfer liquid by capillary action
very thin tube for capillary action

Examples

The laboratory technician used capillary tubing to measure small amounts of liquid precisely.
Refrigerators often use capillary tubing as part of their cooling systems.
Scientists observed the liquid rising inside the capillary tubing during the experiment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: capillary tube - a tube with a very small diameter used for capillary action

Etymology

From Latin capillaris (hair-like) + tubing (from tube).
