bell-type annealing furnace

1. NOUN

/ bɛl taɪp əˈnilɪŋ ˈfɜrnɪs / (AmE), / bɛl taɪp əˈniːlɪŋ ˈfɜːnɪs / (BrE)
a type of furnace in which steel strips are heated to remove strain hardening, as an alternative to continuous annealing
furnace for annealing steel strips

Examples

The new steel plant installed a bell-type annealing furnace to improve the quality of its products.
A bell-type annealing furnace is often used when controlled atmosphere processing is required.
Regular maintenance is essential for the efficient operation of a bell-type annealing furnace.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: continuous annealing furnace - a furnace where steel is annealed in a continuous process
