benzoyl group

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛnzoʊɪl ˌɡruːp / (AmE), / ˈbɛnzɔɪl ˌɡruːp / (BrE)
the univalent radical derived from benzoic acid, with the structure C6H5CO–, used in organic chemistry
univalent radical from benzoic acid (C6H5CO–)

Examples

The benzoyl group is commonly found in many organic compounds used in pharmaceuticals.
When a hydrogen atom is replaced by a benzoyl group, the compound exhibits new chemical properties.
Researchers studied how the benzoyl group affects the stability of certain esters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phenyl group - aromatic radical derived from benzene, C6H5–

Etymology

From benzoic (acid) + -yl, following chemical nomenclature for radicals.
