rack of lamb

1. NOUN

/ ræk əv læm / (AmE), / ræk əv læm / (BrE)
a cut of lamb comprising the rib section, often roasted and served as a single piece or divided into chops
roast rib section of lamb

Examples

The restaurant is famous for its perfectly cooked rack of lamb with rosemary sauce.
She prepared a rack of lamb for the holiday dinner, impressing all her guests.
He ordered the rack of lamb, which was served garnished with fresh herbs and roasted potatoes.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: lamb chop - a single chop cut from a rack of lamb or another part of the lamb
