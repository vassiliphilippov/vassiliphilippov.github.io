Blue Ridge

1. PROPN

/ bluː rɪdʒ / (AmE), / bluː rɪdʒ / (BrE)
a range of the Appalachians extending from southern Pennsylvania to northern Georgia, known for its bluish color when seen from a distance
mountain range in eastern United States

Examples

The Blue Ridge is famous for its scenic beauty and hiking trails along the parkway.
From the summit, we could see the misty outline of the Blue Ridge stretching into the horizon.
Many artists have been inspired by the stunning vistas of the Blue Ridge mountains.

Etymology

Named for the bluish haze often seen over the mountains, which comes from trees releasing isoprene into the atmosphere.
