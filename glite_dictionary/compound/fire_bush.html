fire bush

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪɚ ˌbʊʃ / (AmE), / ˈfaɪə ˌbʊʃ / (BrE)
A densely branched Eurasian shrub known for its foliage that turns purple-red in autumn.
ornamental shrub; foliage turns red in autumn

Examples

The leaves of the fire bush turn a vibrant red when autumn arrives.
Gardeners often plant fire bushes to add color to their landscapes in the fall.
In the park, a row of fire bush shrubs brightened the walkway with their brilliant foliage.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪɚ bʊʃ / (AmE), / ˈfaɪə bʊʃ / (BrE)
An evergreen South American shrub with showy trumpet-shaped orange flowers, cultivated as an ornamental or houseplant.
South American ornamental shrub

Examples

The fire bush added a splash of vibrant color to the conservatory.
Gardeners often choose the fire bush for its striking orange flowers and easy maintenance.
In the winter garden, the fire bush remains lush and green when many other plants have shed their leaves.
