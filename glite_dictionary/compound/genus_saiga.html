genus Saiga

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈsaɪ.gə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈsaɪ.gə / (BrE)
A genus of antelopes containing the saiga, a distinctive species native to Eurasian steppes.
genus of antelopes (saigas)

Examples

The genus Saiga contains only one living species, the saiga antelope.
Conservationists are concerned about the survival of animals in the genus Saiga.
Members of the genus Saiga are well adapted to harsh steppe environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: saiga - an antelope species (Saiga tatarica) native to Eurasian steppes

Etymology

From scientific Latin 'Saiga', derived from Russian 'saiga', the name for the animal.
