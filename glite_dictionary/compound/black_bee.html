black bee

1. NOUN

/ blæk biː / (AmE), / blæk biː / (BrE)
a dark-colored, often ill-tempered honeybee (Apis mellifera mellifera), originally from northern Europe, especially Germany
a dark, northern European honeybee

Examples

The black bee is known for its resilience in colder climates.
Unlike the Italian honeybee, the black bee can be more defensive around its hive.
Beekeepers sometimes prefer the black bee for its ability to survive harsh winters.
