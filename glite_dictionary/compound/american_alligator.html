American alligator

1. NOUN

/ əˈmɛrɪkən ˈælɪˌɡeɪtər / (AmE), / əˈmɛrɪkən ˈælɪɡeɪtə / (BrE)
a large species of alligator native to the southeastern United States, with a broad snout and dark coloration
large alligator species of southeastern U.S.

Examples

The American alligator is commonly found in freshwater swamps and marshes throughout Florida and Louisiana.
Many tourists visit the Everglades to catch a glimpse of a wild American alligator.
At the zoo, the children were amazed by the massive size of the American alligator in its enclosure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alligator - large reptile with a broad snout, native to the Americas and China
