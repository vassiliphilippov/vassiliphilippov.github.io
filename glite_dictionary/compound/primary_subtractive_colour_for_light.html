primary subtractive colour for light

1. NOUN

/ ˈpraɪˌmɛri səbˈtræktɪv ˈkʌlər fər laɪt / (AmE), / ˈpraɪˌməri səbˈtraktɪv ˈkʌlə fə laɪt / (BrE)
any of the three colors—magenta, cyan, and yellow—that are primary for the subtractive color system and are formed by subtracting certain wavelengths from white light
color primary in subtractive light mixing

Examples

An artist explained that primary subtractive colour for light includes cyan, magenta, and yellow, which are used in printing.
When mixing paints, understanding the primary subtractive colour for light helps achieve vibrant secondary hues.
Printers apply the theory of primary subtractive colour for light to produce a wide gamut of colors.

Synset Examples

the primary subtractive colors for light are magenta, cyan, and yellow

Related Terms

  • Antonym: additive primary colour - one of the colors (red, green, blue) used in additive color mixing for light
