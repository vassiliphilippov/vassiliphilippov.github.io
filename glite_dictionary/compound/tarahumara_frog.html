Tarahumara frog

1. NOUN

/ təˌrɑːhuˈmɑːrə frɑːɡ / (AmE), / təˌrɑːhʊˈmɑːrə frɒɡ / (BrE)
a large Mexican frog (Lithobates tarahumarae) typically found near water in southwestern Mexico and adjacent areas
a species of Mexican frog

Examples

The Tarahumara frog is native to streams and ponds in the Sierra Madre Occidental of Mexico.
Researchers are studying the habitat requirements of the endangered Tarahumara frog.
Because of habitat loss, the population of the Tarahumara frog has declined in recent decades.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frog - a tailless amphibian with long hind legs for leaping

Etymology

Named after the Tarahumara people of northern Mexico, where the species is found.
