transcendental philosophy

1. NOUN

/ ˌtrænˌsɛnˈdɛn.tl̩ fəˈlɑː.sə.fi / (AmE), / ˌtrænˌsɛnˈdɛn.tl fəˈlɒs.ə.fi / (BrE)
a branch of philosophy that emphasizes intuition and spirituality over empirical observation and material reality
philosophy prioritizing intuition over empirical evidence

Examples

Immanuel Kant's transcendental philosophy profoundly shaped modern thought.
She wrote her dissertation on the influence of transcendental philosophy in 19th-century Europe.
Debates on transcendental philosophy often contrast intuition with scientific evidence.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: empiricism - the theory that all knowledge is derived from sense-experience
  • Synonym: transcendentalism - a system of philosophy emphasizing the spiritual over the material

Etymology

From Latin transcendere (to climb over) and Greek philosophia (love of wisdom).
