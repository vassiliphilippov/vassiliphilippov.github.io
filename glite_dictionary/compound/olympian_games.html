Olympian Games

1. NOUN

/ əˈlɪmpiən ˈɡeɪmz / (AmE), / əˈlɪmpiən ˈɡeɪmz / (BrE)
An ancient Panhellenic festival of athletic and religious events held every four years at Olympia in honor of Zeus, beginning in 776 BC.
ancient Greek athletic festival in Olympia

Examples

The ancient Olympian Games were held to honor Zeus and attracted athletes from all over Greece.
Scholars have studied the origins of the Olympian Games to understand ancient sporting traditions.
Statues of victors in the Olympian Games once lined the pathways of Olympia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Olympic Games - the modern international athletic competition held every four years

Etymology

From Olympia, the site in Greece, and Games, indicating contests; linked to the worship of Zeus in ancient Greece.
