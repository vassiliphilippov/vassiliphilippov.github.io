leaf soil

1. NOUN

/ lif sɔɪl / (AmE), / liːf sɔɪl / (BrE)
soil formed mainly from decayed leaves, often used to improve the quality of garden soil or potting mixes
soil made from decayed leaves

Examples

Gardeners often add leaf soil to flower beds to enrich the earth and encourage healthy plant growth.
The potting mix contained a large amount of leaf soil for extra nutrients.
After a year of composting, the pile had turned into rich, dark leaf soil ready for the garden.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leaf mould - a substance consisting of decayed leaves, used as a soil improver
