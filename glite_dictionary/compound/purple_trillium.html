purple trillium

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɝːpəl ˈtrɪliəm / (AmE), / ˈpɜːpl ˈtrɪliəm / (BrE)
a perennial plant of eastern North America with malodorous pink to purple flowers and an astringent root traditionally used in folk medicine to ease childbirth
North American plant with purple flowers

Examples

The forest floor was covered with blooming purple trillium in early spring.
Native Americans used the root of purple trillium for medicinal purposes.
We spotted a rare purple trillium along the hiking trail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trillium - a genus of perennial flowering plants with three-leaf arrangements and showy flowers
