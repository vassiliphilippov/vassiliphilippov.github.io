juvenile rheumatoid arthritis

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒuːvəˌnaɪl ˌruːˌmætɔɪd ɑːrˈθraɪtɪs / (AmE), / ˈdʒuːvɪnaɪl ˌruːˌmætɔɪd ɑːˈθraɪtɪs / (BrE)
a type of rheumatoid arthritis affecting children, causing inflammation of large joints and potentially impairing bone growth
type of rheumatoid arthritis in children
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed the child with juvenile rheumatoid arthritis after observing persistent joint swelling and pain.
Advances in treatment have improved outcomes for patients with juvenile rheumatoid arthritis.
Support groups help families cope with the challenges of managing juvenile rheumatoid arthritis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rheumatoid arthritis - chronic autoimmune disorder causing joint inflammation
