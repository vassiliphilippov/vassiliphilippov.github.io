geometrical regularity

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒioʊˈmɛtrɪkəl ˌrɛɡjəˈlɛrɪti / (AmE), / ˌdʒiːəˈmɛtrɪkəl ˌrɛɡjʊˈlærɪti / (BrE)
the property of a geometric shape, especially a polygon, having all sides and all angles equal
having all sides and angles equal (geometry)

Examples

The architect admired the geometrical regularity of the ancient temple's columns.
A square is the simplest example of geometrical regularity among polygons.
The artist broke the geometrical regularity of the design by adding a single asymmetrical line.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: irregularity - the state of not being regular or equal in shape or measure

Etymology

From geometry (Greek *geōmetriā*) + regularity (Latin *regulāritās*, from *regula*, rule).
