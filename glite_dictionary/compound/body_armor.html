body armor

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑdi ˌɑrmər / (AmE), / ˈbɒdi ˌɑːmə / (BrE)
protective clothing made from strong materials, designed to absorb or deflect physical attacks and safeguard the wearer's body
protective clothing for the body

Examples

Police officers wore body armor during the dangerous raid to protect themselves from gunfire.
Modern body armor is lightweight yet strong enough to stop most pistol bullets.
The soldier credited his body armor with saving his life on the battlefield.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: armor - protective covering, often metal, worn to prevent injury
  • Degree Lower: bulletproof vest - protective vest worn to stop bullets
