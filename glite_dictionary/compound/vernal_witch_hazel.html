vernal witch hazel

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɜrnəl wɪtʃ ˈheɪzəl / (AmE), / ˈvɜːnəl wɪtʃ ˈheɪzəl / (BrE)
a fragrant shrub (Hamamelis vernalis) found in the lower Mississippi valley, known for its very small flowers blooming from midwinter to spring
Mississippi valley shrub with early-blooming flowers

Examples

The vernal witch hazel blooms when most other plants are still dormant during winter.
Gardeners appreciate vernal witch hazel for its delicate, fragrant flowers in early spring.
Native to the lower Mississippi valley, vernal witch hazel thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: witch hazel - shrub of the genus Hamamelis, known for its medicinal bark and yellow flowers
