mental faculty

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛntl ˈfækəlti / (AmE), / ˈmɛntl ˈfækəlti / (BrE)
an inherent cognitive or perceptual ability of the mind, such as reasoning, memory, or perception
inherent mental ability

Examples

Critical thinking is considered a mental faculty essential for problem solving.
Age can affect various mental faculties, including memory and attention.
Meditation is said to sharpen one's mental faculties over time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: faculty - an inherent mental or physical power
  • Synonym: mental capacity - the ability of the mind to process information or perform cognitive functions
