standard cell

1. NOUN

/ ˈstændɚd sɛl / (AmE), / ˈstændəd sɛl / (BrE)
a primary electrochemical cell used as a reference standard for electromotive force measurements
primary cell for voltage reference

Examples

The laboratory uses a standard cell to calibrate their voltmeters regularly.
A Weston standard cell is commonly used because of its stable voltage output.
Measurement accuracy depends in part on the condition of the standard cell used for reference.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: primary cell - an electrochemical cell that is not rechargeable
