Battle of Teutoburger Wald

1. PROPN

/ ˈbæt̬əl əv ˌtɔɪtoʊˈbʊrɡər ˈvalt / (AmE), / ˈbætl əv ˌtɔɪtəbʊˈɡɜː wald / (BrE)
a battle in 9 AD in which Germanic tribes led by Arminius destroyed three Roman legions, halting Roman expansion into Germania
famous AD 9 battle between Romans and Germanic tribes

Examples

The Battle of Teutoburger Wald marked a turning point in Roman history.
Historians still debate the exact location of the Battle of Teutoburger Wald.
The defeat at the Battle of Teutoburger Wald greatly affected the Roman Empire's expansion plans.

Etymology

From German 'Teutoburger Wald,' meaning 'Teutoburg Forest.'
