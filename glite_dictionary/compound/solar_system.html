solar system

1. NOUN

/ ˈsoʊlər ˌsɪstəm / (AmE), / ˈsəʊlə ˌsɪstəm / (BrE)
the sun and all the planets, moons, and other celestial objects that travel around it as a system bound by gravity
sun and its orbiting celestial bodies

Examples

The solar system consists of the sun, eight planets, and many smaller objects like asteroids and comets.
Scientists are studying the outer edges of our solar system for signs of distant planets.
Children often learn about the solar system in elementary school science classes.

Etymology

From Latin 'solaris' (of the sun) + 'systema' (system).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsoʊlər ˌsɪstəm / (AmE), / ˈsəʊlə ˌsɪstəm / (BrE)
the Sun and all the celestial bodies, such as planets, asteroids, and comets, that are bound to it by gravity and orbit around it
the Sun and its orbiting celestial bodies

Examples

There are eight planets in our solar system, including Earth.
Scientists search for life on other planets within our solar system.
The telescope provides a detailed view of objects in the solar system.

Synset Examples

NASA's orbiter is crucial for studying our solar system.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: planetary system - a star and all the celestial bodies bound to it by gravity

Etymology

From Latin 'solaris' (of the sun) + 'systema' (system).
