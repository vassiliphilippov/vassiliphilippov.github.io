to advocate for the rights

1. VERB

/ ˈæd.vəˌkeɪt fɔr ðə raɪts / (AmE), / ˈæd.vəˌkeɪt fə ðə raɪts / (BrE)
to actively support or promote the rights of a particular group or individual, especially in a social or legal context.
actively support rights of a group

Examples

As a lawyer, she often advocates for the rights of refugees in court.
Many organizations advocate for the rights of children to have access to quality education.
The activist has spent her career advocating for the rights of people with disabilities.

Synset Examples

She advocates for the rights of drug users in society.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: champion - to support or defend a cause or person
  • Antonym: to oppose - to act against or resist something
