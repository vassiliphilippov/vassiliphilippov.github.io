histiocytic leukaemia

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɪstioʊˌsɪtɪk luˈkiːmiə / (AmE), / ˌhɪstɪəˌsɪtɪk ljuːˈkiːmiə / (BrE)
A type of leukemia characterized by excessive proliferation of monocytes and monoblasts in the blood.
leukemia with monocyte proliferation
medical british

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with histiocytic leukaemia after tests showed increased monocyte levels.
Treatment options for histiocytic leukaemia are still under clinical research.
Doctors monitor blood counts closely in cases of histiocytic leukaemia to track disease progression.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leukemia - a group of cancers affecting the blood and bone marrow

Etymology

From Greek 'histion' (tissue) and 'kytos' (cell), and 'leukaemia' derived from Greek 'leukos' (white) and 'haima' (blood).
