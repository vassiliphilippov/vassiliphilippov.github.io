electronic counter-countermeasures

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌlɛkˈtrɑnɪk ˈkaʊntər ˌkaʊntər məˈʒɚz / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛkˈtrɒnɪk ˈkaʊntə ˌkaʊntə məˈʒəːz / (BrE)
actions or techniques in electronic warfare intended to ensure the effective use of the electromagnetic spectrum by friendly forces, despite enemy interference or countermeasures.
techniques to protect friendly electromagnetic operations from enemy electronic attack

Examples

The military implemented new electronic counter-countermeasures to protect communications during the conflict.
Advancements in electronic counter-countermeasures have made it more difficult for enemy forces to jam radar systems.
Training focused on deploying electronic counter-countermeasures against evolving threats to secure battlefield communications.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: electronic countermeasures - actions to impede enemy use of the electromagnetic spectrum
