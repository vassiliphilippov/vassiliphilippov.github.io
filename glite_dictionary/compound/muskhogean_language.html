Muskhogean language

1. NOUN

/ mʌsˈkoʊgiən ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / mʌsˈkəʊɡiən ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
a family of indigenous languages spoken in the southeastern United States, including Creek, Choctaw, and Seminole
North American indigenous language family

Examples

The Muskhogean language family includes Creek, Seminole, and Choctaw among others.
Many words in the Muskhogean language family are still in use by Native American communities today.
Linguists study the connections between each Muskhogean language to trace their history and development.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Creek - a major language of the Muskhogean family
  • Synonym: Choctaw - a language belonging to the Muskhogean family

Etymology

From Creek *maskóki*, the language family name.
