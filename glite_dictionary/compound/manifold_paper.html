manifold paper

1. NOUN

/ ˈmænəˌfoʊld ˈpeɪpər / (AmE), / ˈmænɪfəʊld ˈpeɪpə / (BrE)
A thin, lightweight paper used with carbon paper for making multiple copies of documents.
lightweight paper for making multiple copies

Examples

The secretary placed a sheet of manifold paper beneath the original to create duplicates.
For legal forms, manifold paper is often used with carbon copies.
He tore out the manifold paper after finishing the receipt book.

Synset Examples

an original and two manifolds

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carbon paper - a thin paper coated with carbon used for making copies

Etymology

From 'manifold' (meaning 'many times' or 'many parts'), used in printing to refer to making several copies at once.
