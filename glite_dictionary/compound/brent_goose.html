brent goose

1. NOUN

/ brɛnt ɡus / (AmE), / brɛnt ɡuːs / (BrE)
a small, dark species of goose (Branta bernicla) that breeds in the Arctic and migrates south for the winter
species of small dark migratory goose

Examples

Flocks of brent geese can often be seen feeding along the coastline in winter.
Each spring, the brent goose journeys thousands of miles from its Arctic breeding grounds.
The estuary is a vital stopover for migratory brent geese.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barnacle goose - a similar migratory goose species (Branta leucopsis)

Etymology

From the Old Norse 'brandgás', meaning 'burnt (black) goose'.
