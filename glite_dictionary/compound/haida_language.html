Haida language

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪdə ˌlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈhaɪdə ˌlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the language spoken by the Haida people, belonging to the Na-Dene language family, traditionally spoken in parts of British Columbia and Alaska.
language of the Haida people

Examples

She is studying the Haida language to help preserve her cultural heritage.
Linguists have published several dictionaries of the Haida language.
The Haida language has only a few fluent speakers left today.

Etymology

From Haida, the name of the indigenous people of the Haida Gwaii archipelago.
