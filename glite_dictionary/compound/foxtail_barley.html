foxtail barley

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɑːksˌteɪl ˈbɑːrli / (AmE), / ˈfɒksteɪl ˈbɑːli / (BrE)
a species of barley (Hordeum jubatum) grown for its distinctive, ornamental flower heads with long, silky awns, native to North America and northeastern Asia
an ornamental grass species

Examples

The garden featured a border of foxtail barley swaying in the wind.
Farmers sometimes regard foxtail barley as a troublesome weed in their fields.
The silky awns of foxtail barley created a shimmering effect under the summer sun.
