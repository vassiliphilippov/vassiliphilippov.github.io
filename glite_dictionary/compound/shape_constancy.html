shape constancy

1. NOUN

/ ʃeɪp ˈkɑn.stən.si / (AmE), / ʃeɪp ˈkɒn.stən.si / (BrE)
the tendency to perceive the shape of a rigid object as remaining unchanged despite changes in the angle or perspective from which it is viewed
perceptual stability of object shape

Examples

Thanks to shape constancy, we recognize a door as rectangular even when it appears trapezoidal from an angle.
Children develop an understanding of shape constancy as their perception matures.
Photographs that distort perspective can challenge our normal experience of shape constancy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: size constancy - perceptual phenomenon of seeing size as constant despite distance changes
