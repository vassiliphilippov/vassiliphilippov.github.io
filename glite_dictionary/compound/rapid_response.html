rapid response

1. NOUN

/ ˈræpɪd rɪˈspɑns / (AmE), / ˈrapɪd rɪˈspɒns / (BrE)
A quick or immediate reaction to a situation, especially an emergency or unexpected event.
quick reaction to a situation

Examples

The company's rapid response to customer complaints improved its reputation.
Paramedics arrived with a rapid response that saved the child's life.
Effective leadership often requires a rapid response to changing circumstances.

Synset Examples

The rapid response from the team saved the event from disaster.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: immediate response - a reaction with no delay to a situation
  • Antonym: delayed response - a reaction that occurs after some time has passed
