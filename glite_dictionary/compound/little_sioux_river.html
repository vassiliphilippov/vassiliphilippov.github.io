Little Sioux River

1. PROPN

/ ˌlɪt̬.əl ˈsuː ˌrɪv.ɚ / (AmE), / ˌlɪt.əl ˈsuː ˌrɪv.ə / (BrE)
a river that originates in southwestern Minnesota and flows southwest to join the Missouri River in western Iowa
river in Minnesota and Iowa, US

Examples

The Little Sioux River is popular for canoeing and fishing in the summer months.
Heavy rains caused the Little Sioux River to swell beyond its banks last year.
The conservation program aims to protect the unique wildlife along the Little Sioux River.

Etymology

Named after the Sioux people, with 'Little' distinguishing it from the Big Sioux River.
