Bristol Channel

1. PROPN

/ ˈbrɪstl̩ ˈtʃænəl / (AmE), / ˈbrɪstl ˈtʃænəl / (BrE)
an inlet of the Atlantic Ocean located between southern Wales and southwestern England
inlet between Wales and England

Examples

The Bristol Channel is known for having one of the highest tidal ranges in the world.
Many coastal towns along the Bristol Channel attract tourists during the summer months.
Ships traveling to ports in southern Wales often cross the Bristol Channel.

Etymology

Named after the city of Bristol, England, on its northeastern shore.
