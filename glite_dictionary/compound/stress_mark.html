stress mark

1. NOUN

/ ˈstrɛs ˌmɑrk / (AmE), / ˈstrɛs ˌmɑːk / (BrE)
a symbol placed over or near a syllable in a word to indicate which syllable is stressed when speaking
mark showing syllable stress

Examples

The dictionary entry includes a stress mark to show which syllable to emphasize.
In many languages, a stress mark is used in written form to clarify pronunciation.
Students learned how to place the stress mark correctly in phonetic transcription.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diacritic - a mark added to a letter to alter its pronunciation or to distinguish it
