American aloe

1. NOUN

/ əˈmɛr.ɪ.kən ˈæloʊ / (AmE), / əˈmɛr.ɪ.kən ˈaləʊ / (BrE)
A tropical American plant, chiefly of the genus Agave, with a basal rosette of fibrous sword-shaped leaves and tall flowering spikes, sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes or fiber.
tropical American plant with fibrous leaves and tall flower spikes

Examples

The American aloe can live for decades before sending up its towering flower spike.
Gardeners often admire the dramatic appearance of the American aloe in landscaping projects.
Fibers from the leaves of the American aloe have been traditionally used for making rope and textiles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agave - a genus of plants including American aloe
  • Synonym: century plant - another common name for American aloe (Agave americana)
