water strider

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɑtɚ ˌstraɪdɚ / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə ˌstraɪdə / (BrE)
a long-legged insect that is able to move quickly across the surface of water without sinking
insect that runs on water

Examples

A water strider glided effortlessly across the calm pond, barely making a ripple.
Children watched as the water strider darted back and forth on the lake's surface.
The water strider's long legs allow it to stay above water without sinking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pond skater - another name for the water strider
