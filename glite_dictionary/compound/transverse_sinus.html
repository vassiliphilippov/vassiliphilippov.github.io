transverse sinus

1. NOUN

/ trænzˈvɜrs ˈsaɪnəs / (AmE), / trɑːnzˈvɜːs ˈsaɪnəs / (BrE)
either of two channels in the dura mater of the brain that carry venous blood from the back of the head to the sigmoid sinus.
dural venous channel in the brain
medical

Examples

The transverse sinus helps drain blood from the brain's posterior regions.
Blockage in the transverse sinus can lead to increased intracranial pressure.
During the surgery, the neurosurgeon carefully avoided injuring the transverse sinus.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: sigmoid sinus - venous sinus that receives blood from the transverse sinus in the brain

Etymology

From Latin 'transversus' (turned across) and 'sinus' (curve, cavity).
