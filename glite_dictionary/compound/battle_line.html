battle line

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæɾəl laɪn / (AmE), / ˈbætl laɪn / (BrE)
the line along which opposing armies or troops are positioned and engage in combat
line where opposing armies meet

Examples

The soldiers waited anxiously at the battle line for the order to advance.
Maps showed the shifting battle line after each day of the campaign.
The general inspected the battle line before the fighting began.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: front line - the foremost line of an army or conflict, closest to the enemy

2. NOUN

/ ˈbæɾl̩ ˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈbætl ˌlaɪn / (BrE)
a clear division or position taken by opposing groups or individuals during a conflict or dispute, especially in a figurative sense
figurative division or stance in conflict

Examples

During the debate, each party drew a battle line over the proposed budget cuts.
Teachers and parents found themselves on opposite sides of the battle line over the new curriculum.
Environmental issues have created a new battle line in local politics.

Synset Examples

The team drew a battle line over the project deadline.
They established a battle line against the new regulations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: front line - the foremost position in a conflict or struggle
  • Antonym: common ground - a basis of mutual interest or agreement in a dispute

Etymology

From military terminology, where 'battle line' referred to troops arrayed for combat.
