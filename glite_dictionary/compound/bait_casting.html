bait casting

1. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪt ˌkæstɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbeɪt ˌkɑːstɪŋ / (BrE)
A method of fishing that uses a single-handed rod to cast relatively heavy artificial bait.
fishing method with a single-handed rod and heavy bait

Examples

He prefers bait casting when targeting larger freshwater fish like bass and pike.
Many anglers consider bait casting to offer better accuracy than spinning techniques.
Learning the basics of bait casting can help avoid backlashes and improve casting distance.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fly fishing - fishing using a lightweight artificial fly
  • Antonym: spinning - fishing using a spinning reel and lighter lures
