mechanic's lien

1. NOUN

/ məˈkænɪks liːn / (AmE), / mɪˈkænɪks liːn / (BrE)
a legal claim against a property by a contractor, laborer, or supplier who has supplied work or materials for building or repairing it, to secure payment for their services
legal claim on property for unpaid work or materials
legal

Examples

The contractor filed a mechanic's lien against the homeowner for unpaid renovation work.
Before selling the property, she had to satisfy the mechanic's lien on the house.
If you don't pay the builder, they may place a mechanic's lien on your land.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lien - a legal right to keep or sell property as security for a debt

Etymology

From 'mechanic' (worker) and 'lien' (from Old French, a legal claim to property as security).
