Sea of Okhotsk

1. PROPN

/ si əv ɑkˈhɑtsk / (AmE), / si əv ɒkˈhɒtsk / (BrE)
a marginal sea of the northwest Pacific Ocean, located east of Russia and north of Japan
a sea in the northwest Pacific, east of Russia

Examples

The Sea of Okhotsk is known for its seasonal ice cover and rich marine life.
Fishing fleets often operate in the Sea of Okhotsk during the summer months.
Strong storms occasionally sweep across the Sea of Okhotsk, affecting shipping routes.

Etymology

From Russian 'Okhotskoye more', named after the port town of Okhotsk.
