battle damage

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæt̬l ˌdæmɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈbætl ˌdæmɪdʒ / (BrE)
loss, destruction, or impairment of military equipment or vehicles as a result of combat engagement
military equipment lost or damaged in combat

Examples

The aircraft sustained significant battle damage during the mission, but managed to return to base.
Engineers quickly repaired the tank's battle damage so it could rejoin the fight.
Inspection teams were sent to assess the battle damage after the ceasefire.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: casualty - person killed or injured in a war or accident
  • Synonym: battle wound - physical injury received during battle
