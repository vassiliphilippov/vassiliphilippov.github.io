loose-leaf lettuce

1. NOUN

/ ˈlus ˌlif ˈlɛt̬ɪs / (AmE), / ˈluːs ˌliːf ˈlɛtɪs / (BrE)
A type of lettuce with loosely curled leaves that do not form a compact head.
lettuce with loose, curled leaves

Examples

Many salads feature loose-leaf lettuce for its tender texture and mild flavor.
She prefers to grow loose-leaf lettuce in her garden instead of iceberg varieties.
Farmers' markets often offer several types of loose-leaf lettuce during the spring.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: romaine lettuce - lettuce variety with long leaves forming an elongated head
  • Antonym: crisphead lettuce - lettuce with a crisp, compact head, such as iceberg lettuce
