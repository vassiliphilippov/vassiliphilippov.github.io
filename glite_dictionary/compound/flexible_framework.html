flexible framework

1. NOUN

/ ˈflɛksəbəl ˈfreɪmˌwɝk / (AmE), / ˈflɛksəbəl ˈfreɪmˌwɜːk / (BrE)
A structure or system that can be readily adapted or adjusted to different circumstances or requirements, especially in development or planning.
adaptable structure or system

Examples

The new regulations require a flexible framework so businesses can easily adapt to market changes.
Our team used a flexible framework to design the website, allowing future updates.
A flexible framework makes innovation easier by not restricting development to fixed rules.

Synset Examples

The new software provides a flexible framework for developers.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: rigid framework - a structure or system not easily adaptable to change
