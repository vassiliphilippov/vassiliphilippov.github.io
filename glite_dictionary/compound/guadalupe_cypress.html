Guadalupe cypress

1. NOUN

/ ɡwɑːdəˌlup saɪˌprɛs / (AmE), / ɡwɑːdəˌl(j)uːp saɪˌprɛs / (BrE)
A species of cypress tree, native to Guadalupe Island off the west coast of Mexico, known for its bluish foliage and spreading form.
tree species native to Guadalupe Island

Examples

The Guadalupe cypress is prized for its attractive blue-green foliage in ornamental gardens.
Conservationists are working to protect the last wild stands of Guadalupe cypress on its native island.
A mature Guadalupe cypress can develop a wide-spreading canopy, providing welcome shade in arid climates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cypress - any evergreen coniferous tree of the genus Cupressus

Etymology

Named after Guadalupe Island, off the coast of Mexico, where it is endemic.
