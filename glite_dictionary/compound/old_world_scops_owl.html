Old World scops owl

1. NOUN

/ oʊld wɜːrld skɑps aʊl / (AmE), / əʊld wɜːld skɒps aʊl / (BrE)
A species of small owl (Otus scops) native to Europe, known for its distinctive call and cryptic plumage.
small European owl (Otus scops)

Examples

The Old World scops owl is famous for its camouflaged feathers that blend into tree bark.
Birdwatchers in Spain often hope to spot an Old World scops owl during the spring migration.
Unlike its relatives in Asia, the Old World scops owl is rarely seen outside its European range.

Etymology

From 'Old World' (referring to Europe, Asia, Africa) and 'scops owl' (from Greek skōps, a type of owl).
