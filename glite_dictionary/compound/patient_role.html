patient role

1. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪʃənt roʊl / (AmE), / ˈpeɪʃənt rəʊl / (BrE)
(Linguistics) The semantic role of an entity that is affected by or undergoes the action denoted by the verb in a clause, but is not the agent.
semantic role affected by a verb's action

Examples

In the sentence 'The ball was kicked', 'the ball' fills the patient role.
Linguists often distinguish between the agent role and the patient role in sentence analysis.
Identifying the patient role helps clarify who or what is affected by the action.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: agent - the semantic role of the doer or initiator of the action

Etymology

From 'patient' (from Latin 'patiens', undergoing) in semantic role sense, and 'role'.
