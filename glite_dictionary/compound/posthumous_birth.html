posthumous birth

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑstʃəməs bɝθ / (AmE), / ˈpɒstjʊməs bɜːθ / (BrE)
The birth of a child after the death of their biological father.
birth after father's death

Examples

The author was a posthumous birth, arriving months after his father's untimely passing.
Legal complications sometimes arise in cases of posthumous birth regarding inheritance rights.
She discovered that her grandfather had been a posthumous birth during a family genealogy project.

Etymology

From Latin posthumus (after burial, born after father's death) + English birth.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːstʃəməs bɜrθ / (AmE), / ˈpɒstjʊməs bɜːθ / (BrE)
the birth of a child after the death of the mother, sometimes occurring by Caesarean section
birth of a child after mother's death
medical

Examples

The doctor performed a Caesarean section to deliver the posthumous birth after the tragic accident.
Legal debates surrounded the child's rights following the posthumous birth of the heir to the estate.
Stories of posthumous births are rare but often make headlines due to their extraordinary circumstances.

Etymology

From Latin posthumus (born after the death of the father or mother), from post- (after) + humus (ground, earth).
