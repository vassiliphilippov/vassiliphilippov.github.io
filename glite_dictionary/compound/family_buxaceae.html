family Buxaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli bʌkˈseɪsiaɪ / (AmE), / ˈfæməli bʌkˈsiːsɪiː / (BrE)
A family of widely distributed evergreen shrubs and trees, known as the box family, including boxwoods and related genera.
the box family of evergreen shrubs and trees

Examples

The park features several mature family Buxaceae specimens near the entrance.
Many evergreen hedges are cultivated from species in the family Buxaceae.
Botanists often study the varied leaf shapes within the family Buxaceae.

Etymology

From Latin *Buxus* (box tree), the type genus, plus the family-forming suffix -aceae.
