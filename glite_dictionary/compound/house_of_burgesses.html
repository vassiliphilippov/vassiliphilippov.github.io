House of Burgesses

1. PROPN

/ ˈhaʊs əv ˈbɜrdʒəsɪz / (AmE), / ˈhaʊs əv ˈbɜːdʒɪsɪz / (BrE)
the lower house of the colonial Virginia legislature, established in the early 17th century as the first representative assembly in America
lower house of colonial Virginia legislature

Examples

The House of Burgesses set a precedent for self-government in the American colonies.
Many influential leaders, including George Washington and Thomas Jefferson, served in the House of Burgesses.
The Virginia assembly, known as the House of Burgesses, first convened in Jamestown in 1619.

Etymology

From 'burgess' (English for a representative or citizen, especially in English parliamentary towns), ultimately from Old French 'burgeis'.
