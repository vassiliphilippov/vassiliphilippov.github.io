zombie formalism

1. NOUN

/ ˈzɑːmbi ˈfɔːrməˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈzɒmbi ˈfɔːməlɪzəm / (BrE)
A movement in contemporary art characterized by superficial aesthetics and often criticized for lacking originality and depth.
contemporary art style seen as superficial

Examples

Many critics view zombie formalism as a sign of declining creativity in the art world.
The gallery was dominated by works that perfectly embodied zombie formalism.
Her paintings were dismissed as zombie formalism by several leading reviewers.

Synset Examples

The exhibit was filled with pieces reflecting zombie formalism.
His latest book tackles the problem of zombie formalism.
Critics accused the artist of zombie formalism in his work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: formalism - Emphasis on style or form over content or meaning in art or literature

Etymology

Formed from 'zombie' (a reanimated corpse) and 'formalism' (emphasis on formal qualities over content), popularized in art criticism in the 2010s.
