collard greens

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːlərd ˌɡrinz / (AmE), / ˈkɒləd ˌɡriːnz / (BrE)
A type of kale with large, smooth, dark green leaves, often cooked and eaten as a vegetable.
a leafy green vegetable, variety of kale

Examples

She served a delicious side of collard greens with the roast chicken.
Many Southern recipes feature collard greens simmered with ham hocks for flavor.
Fresh collard greens are available at the farmer's market this season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kale - a hardy cabbage with curled or wrinkled leaves

Etymology

From 'collard', an alteration of 'colewort', influenced by 'collar'; greens referring to the edible leaves.
