oligochaete worm

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊlɪɡoʊˈkiːt wɜrm / (AmE), / ˌɒlɪɡəˈkiːt wɜːm / (BrE)
A type of hermaphroditic annelid worm, found on land and in water, with bristles arranged singly along its body.
hermaphroditic annelid worm with single bristles

Examples

The soil in the garden was rich with oligochaete worms that aerated the earth.
Researchers observed several aquatic oligochaete worms in the pond's sediment sample.
Unlike leeches, oligochaete worms have bristles called setae along their bodies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: earthworm - common terrestrial oligochaete worm

Etymology

From Greek 'oligos' (few) + 'chaite' (hair), referring to the sparse bristles.
