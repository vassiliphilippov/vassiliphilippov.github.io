semantic relation

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈmæntɪk ɹɪˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / sɪˈmæntɪk rɪˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
a relationship between the meanings of words, phrases, or sentences
relation between linguistic meanings

Examples

A synonymy is a type of semantic relation where two words share similar meanings.
Understanding the semantic relation between sentences can improve machine translation accuracy.
The dictionary explains each semantic relation to help learners distinguish between similar terms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: semantic connection - another term for a relationship between meanings
  • Antonym: syntactic relation - relation based on grammar or sentence structure, not meaning

Etymology

From 'semantic', relating to meaning, and 'relation', meaning connection or association.
