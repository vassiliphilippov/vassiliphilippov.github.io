sex chromatin

1. NOUN

/ sɛks kroʊˈmætɪn / (AmE), / sɛks krəʊˈmatɪn / (BrE)
a mass of chromatin found only in female cells, used as a cytological marker of the presence of two X chromosomes
chromatin body found in female cells
medical

Examples

The laboratory technician identified the sex chromatin in the cell sample.
Presence of sex chromatin is often used for gender determination in forensic analysis.
In females, one X chromosome forms the sex chromatin in somatic cell nuclei.

Synset Examples

the presence or absence of sex chromatin in cells obtained by amniocentesis makes it possible to determine the sex of a fetus

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Barr body - a dense body of X-chromatin found in female cell nuclei

Etymology

From 'sex' referring to biological sex, and 'chromatin' from Greek 'chroma' (color) + '-in'.
