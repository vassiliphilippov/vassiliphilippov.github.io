patent right

1. NOUN

/ ˈpætənt raɪt / (AmE), / ˈpeɪtənt raɪt / (BrE)
the exclusive right granted by a government to an inventor to make, use, or sell an invention for a specified period of time
exclusive legal right to an invention
legal

Examples

The company enforced its patent right to prevent competitors from copying its technology.
Obtaining a patent right is crucial for protecting new inventions in the marketplace.
His research led to a breakthrough and the subsequent grant of a patent right.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: copyright - exclusive legal right to reproduce and distribute a creative work
  • Synonym: trademark - a distinct sign or symbol legally registered for a company or product

Etymology

From 'patent', from Latin 'patere' (to be open), and 'right', from Old English 'riht'.
