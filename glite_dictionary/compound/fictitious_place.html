fictitious place

1. NOUN

/ fɪkˈtɪʃəs pleɪs / (AmE), / fɪkˈtɪʃəs pleɪs / (BrE)
A place that exists only in imagination or in fictional or religious writings.
imaginary or invented place

Examples

Many legends describe a fictitious place hidden deep in the mountains.
The story is set in a fictitious place resembling medieval Europe.
Atlantis is often cited as a classic example of a fictitious place.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imaginary place - a place created by the imagination
  • Antonym: real place - a place that actually exists; not imagined
