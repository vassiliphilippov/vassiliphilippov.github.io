imaginary lat syndrome

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈmædʒəˌnɛri læt ˈsɪnˌdroʊm / (AmE), / ɪˈmadʒɪnəri læt ˈsɪndrəʊm / (BrE)
A humorous term describing the sensation of muscle pain or fatigue that is perceived rather than real, especially after exercise.
humorous term for perceived muscle fatigue
informal

Examples

After completing a single set of pull-ups, he claimed he had imaginary lat syndrome.
She laughed about her imaginary lat syndrome after just looking at the weights.
Whenever someone skips arm day, they blame imaginary lat syndrome.

Synset Examples
