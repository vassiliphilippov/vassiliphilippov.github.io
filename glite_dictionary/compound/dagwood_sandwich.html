Dagwood sandwich

1. NOUN

/ ˈdæɡwʊd ˌsænwɪtʃ / (AmE), / ˈdæɡwʊd ˌsanwɪtʃ / (BrE)
A very large sandwich made with several layers of fillings such as meats, cheese, and vegetables.
large, multi-layered sandwich

Examples

For lunch, he made himself a huge Dagwood sandwich packed with turkey, ham, cheese, and lettuce.
The Dagwood sandwich quickly became my favorite item on the diner’s menu.
She challenged her friends to see who could finish a Dagwood sandwich the fastest.

Synset Examples

He made a Dagwood sandwich stacked high with ingredients.
I ordered a Dagwood sandwich for lunch yesterday.
The café is famous for its giant Dagwood sandwiches.

Etymology

Named after Dagwood Bumstead, a character in the comic strip 'Blondie' known for making tall sandwiches.
