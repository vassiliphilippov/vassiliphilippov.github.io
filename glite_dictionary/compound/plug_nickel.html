plug nickel

1. NOUN

/ plʌɡ ˈnɪkəl / (AmE), / plʌɡ ˈnɪkəl / (BrE)
A very small amount of money; something of no value or worth.
something worthless; a trivial amount of money
american

Examples

I wouldn't pay a plug nickel for that broken chair at the yard sale.
She insisted that his apology wasn't worth a plug nickel.
The old washing machine isn't worth a plug nickel now that it's stopped working.

Synset Examples

He didn't care to lose a plug nickel over the argument.
I wouldn't spend a plug nickel on that old car.
She wouldn't give a plug nickel for his opinion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: red cent - a small amount of money; something of little value
  • Degree Higher: penny - the smallest unit of currency; something of little value

Etymology

Originally referred to a nickel coin in which the plug (center) had been removed, thus making it worthless.
