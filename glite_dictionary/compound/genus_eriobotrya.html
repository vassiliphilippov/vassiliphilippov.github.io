genus Eriobotrya

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒinəs ɛriˌoʊˈbɒtriə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ɛrɪəʊˈbɒtrɪə / (BrE)
A genus of Asiatic evergreen fruit trees, including the loquat, belonging to the rose family (Rosaceae).
genus of Asiatic evergreen fruit trees

Examples

The genus Eriobotrya includes trees that are prized for their edible fruit.
Botanists often study the genus Eriobotrya for its unique evergreen leaves and fragrant flowers.
The loquat is the most well-known member of the genus Eriobotrya.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loquat - a small, edible fruit from Eriobotrya japonica

Etymology

From Neo-Latin 'Eriobotrya', from Greek 'erion' meaning wool and 'botrys' meaning bunch (of grapes), describing the woolly fruit clusters.
