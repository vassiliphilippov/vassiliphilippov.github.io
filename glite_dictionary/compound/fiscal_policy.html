fiscal policy

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪskəl ˈpɑːləsi / (AmE), / ˈfɪskəl ˈpɒləsi / (BrE)
A government policy relating to its budget, particularly its use of taxation and public spending to influence the economy.
government policy on taxation and spending

Examples

The government's fiscal policy aims to reduce the budget deficit through spending cuts and tax increases.
Changes in fiscal policy can have significant effects on economic growth and employment.
Debates over fiscal policy often center on how much the government should intervene in the economy.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: monetary policy - a policy dealing with the control of the money supply and interest rates

Etymology

From Latin 'fiscus' (treasury) and 'policy' from Old French 'policie', meaning governance.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɪskəl ˈpɑːləsi / (AmE), / ˈfɪskəl ˈpɒləsi / (BrE)
the government's policy concerning taxation, public spending, and borrowing, used to influence the economy
government policy on taxation and spending

Examples

The government's new fiscal policy aims to stimulate economic growth by increasing public spending.
Experts debated whether a tighter fiscal policy would help control inflation.
Effective fiscal policy can help a country recover from recession.

Synset Examples

His thesis focused on hydraulic Keynesianism and fiscal policy.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: monetary policy - government or central bank policy regarding the money supply and interest rates

Etymology

From Latin 'fiscus' (treasury) and 'policy', via Old French and Middle English.
