Oscan language

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːskən ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈɒskən ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
an extinct Italic language formerly spoken in ancient southern Italy by tribes such as the Samnites, Aurunci, and Sidicini
extinct Italic language of ancient Italy

Examples

The Oscan language was spoken by several tribes in southern Italy before the rise of Rome.
Many ancient inscriptions in the Oscan language have been discovered by archaeologists.
Linguists study the Oscan language to understand the development of Italic dialects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Latin - the Italic language of ancient Rome

Etymology

From Latin Oscus (relating to the Oscans), the ancient people who spoke the language.
