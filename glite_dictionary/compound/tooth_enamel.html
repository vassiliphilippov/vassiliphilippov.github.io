tooth enamel

1. NOUN

/ tuːθ ɪˈnæməl / (AmE), / tuːθ ɪˈnaməl / (BrE)
the hard, white, outer layer that covers the crown of a tooth
hard outer covering of a tooth
medical

Examples

Proper brushing helps protect your tooth enamel from decay and keeps your teeth healthy.
Many whitening toothpastes can be abrasive and may wear down tooth enamel over time.
Excessive consumption of acidic drinks can erode tooth enamel, leading to sensitivity and cavities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enamel - hard, glossy substance covering teeth
  • Degree Lower: dentin - the tissue beneath tooth enamel
