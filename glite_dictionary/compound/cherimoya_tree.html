cherimoya tree

1. NOUN

/ ˌtʃɛrɪˈmɔɪə tri / (AmE), / ˌtʃɛrɪˈmɔɪə triː / (BrE)
a small tropical American tree (Annona cherimola) that bears round or oblong edible fruit with creamy, sweet flesh
tropical fruit-bearing tree

Examples

The cherimoya tree thrives in subtropical climates with mild winters.
Gardeners often prize the cherimoya tree for its delicious, custard-like fruit.
He planted a cherimoya tree in his backyard to enjoy homegrown fruit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cherimoya - the edible fruit of the cherimoya tree

Etymology

From Spanish 'cherimoya', probably from Quechua 'chirimuya' (cold seeds).
