family Santalaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli sænˌtæˈleɪsiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli sænˌtæˈleɪsiː / (BrE)
a family of chiefly tropical herbs, shrubs, or trees, typically bearing nuts or one-seeded fruit, known as Santalaceae
a family of tropical plants (Santalaceae)

Examples

The family Santalaceae includes a variety of tropical plants with single-seeded fruit.
Mistletoe is a well-known member of the family Santalaceae.
Researchers identified several new species belonging to the family Santalaceae in the rainforest.

Etymology

From Latin 'Santalum' (sandalwood) + botanical family suffix '-aceae'.
