domestic relations court

1. NOUN

/ dəˈmɛstɪk rɪˈleɪʃənz kɔrt / (AmE), / dəˈmɛstɪk rɪˈleɪʃənz kɔːt / (BrE)
A court in some U.S. states that deals with family law matters, particularly disputes involving children and domestic issues.
US court for family and child-related cases
legal american

Examples

The judge in the domestic relations court ruled in favor of shared custody after a lengthy hearing.
Cases involving child support are often heard by a domestic relations court in the United States.
She sought legal assistance before filing for divorce in the domestic relations court.
