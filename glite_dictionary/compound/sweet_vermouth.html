sweet vermouth

1. NOUN

/ swit vərˈmuθ / (AmE), / swiːt vəˈmuːθ / (BrE)
a type of dark, sweet, fortified wine flavored with herbs and spices, typically amber or red in color and often used in cocktails
sweet, dark, aromatic fortified wine

Examples

A Manhattan is traditionally made with rye whiskey and sweet vermouth.
Many bartenders recommend storing sweet vermouth in the refrigerator after opening.
She prefers her Negroni with a splash of sweet vermouth and extra orange peel.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dry vermouth - a pale, less sweet variety of vermouth

Etymology

From French 'vermouth', from German 'Wermut' (wormwood), referring to the herbal ingredient.
