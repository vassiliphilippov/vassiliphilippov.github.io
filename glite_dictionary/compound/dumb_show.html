dumb show

1. NOUN

/ dʌm ʃoʊ / (AmE), / dʌm ʃəʊ / (BrE)
a performance or part of a play presented exclusively through gestures and body movement, without spoken words
performance using gestures, no spoken words

Examples

The actors used dumb show to portray the tragic events before the play began.
In traditional Elizabethan theatre, a dumb show often summarized the plot for the audience.
Their creative use of dumb show brought the silent scene to life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pantomime - a performance using gestures instead of words

Etymology

From 'dumb' (silent, unable to speak) + 'show' (presentation).
