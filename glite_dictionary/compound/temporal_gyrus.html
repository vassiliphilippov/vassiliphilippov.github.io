temporal gyrus

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛmpərəl ˈdʒaɪrəs / (AmE), / ˈtɛmpərəl ˈdʒɪərəs / (BrE)
any of the convolutions on the outer surface of the temporal lobe of the brain's cerebrum
ridge on the surface of the temporal lobe
medical

Examples

The superior temporal gyrus is essential for processing auditory information.
Damage to the middle temporal gyrus can affect language comprehension.
Researchers studied the activation of the temporal gyrus during memory tasks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gyrus - a ridge or fold between two clefts on the cerebral surface in the brain

Etymology

From Latin 'temporalis' (of the temple) and Greek 'gyros' (circle, ring).
