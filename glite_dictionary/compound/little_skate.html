little skate

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪt̬l skeɪt / (AmE), / ˈlɪtl skeɪt / (BrE)
a small species of skate (Leucoraja erinacea) found in shallow, inshore waters of North America, growing up to 21 inches long
a small North American marine skate

Examples

The little skate is often seen by divers in shallow coastal waters during the summer.
Many marine biologists study the little skate because of its abundance and manageable size.
A fisherman caught a little skate while trawling near the shore.
