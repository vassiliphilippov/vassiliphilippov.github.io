editor in chief

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛdɪtɚ ɪn ˈtʃif / (AmE), / ˌɛdɪtə ɪn ˈtʃiːf / (BrE)
the person with overall responsibility for the editorial content of a publication, such as a newspaper, magazine, or journal
chief editorial leader of a publication

Examples

The editor in chief is responsible for approving every article before publication.
As editor in chief, she led the magazine to win several national awards.
He was promoted to editor in chief after a decade of dedicated work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: editor - person who prepares texts for publication

2. NOUN

/ ˌɛdɪtər ɪn ˈtʃiːf / (AmE), / ˌɛdɪtə ɪn ˈtʃiːf / (BrE)
the person with the highest editorial authority in a publication, responsible for its content and direction
chief editorial authority in a publication

Examples

After ten years at the magazine, she was finally named editor in chief.
The editor in chief oversees all major decisions regarding editorial content.
As editor in chief, he sets the tone for the entire newspaper.

Synset Examples

The editor in chief approved the final draft.
