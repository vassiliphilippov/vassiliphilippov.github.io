crystal detector

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɪstl dɪˈtɛktər / (AmE), / ˈkrɪstl dɪˈtɛktə / (BrE)
an early electronic detector used in radios, consisting of a fine wire in contact with a crystal (often galena), which acts as a rectifier of radio signals
early radio signal detector using a crystal

Examples

Early radios often used a crystal detector to convert radio signals into sound.
The crystal detector relies on a thin wire adjusted to touch the crystal at the right spot.
Repairing the old radio required replacing its original crystal detector.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cat's-whisker detector - type of crystal detector using a fine wire whisker

Etymology

Crystal from Greek 'krustallos' (ice), detector from Latin 'detegere' (to uncover); named for the use of a crystal in the detecting apparatus.
