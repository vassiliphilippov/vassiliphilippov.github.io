iron mould

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪərn moʊld / (AmE), / ˈaɪən məʊld / (BrE)
A discolored spot or stain, especially on cloth or paper, caused by rust or ink.
stain caused by rust or ink

Examples

Old books are often damaged by iron mould on their pages.
She attempted to remove the iron mould from the antique linen.
The document was nearly illegible due to iron mould and age.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stain - a mark that is difficult to remove from a surface, especially one caused by a liquid

Etymology

From 'iron' referring to the metal, and 'mould' meaning stain, with origins in Old French 'molde' (pulpy, soft).
