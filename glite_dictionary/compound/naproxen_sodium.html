naproxen sodium

1. NOUN

/ nəˈproʊksɛn ˈsoʊdiəm / (AmE), / nəˈprɒksɛn ˈsəʊdiəm / (BrE)
a nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drug used to relieve pain and reduce inflammation
nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drug
medical

Examples

My doctor recommended naproxen sodium to help manage my arthritis pain.
Over-the-counter naproxen sodium is sold under several brand names, including Aleve.
If you experience stomach upset, take naproxen sodium with food.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ibuprofen - a widely used nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drug (NSAID)
  • Antonym: acetaminophen - a common pain reliever not classified as an NSAID

Etymology

From 'naproxen', the active compound, and 'sodium', the salt form it is combined with for faster absorption.
