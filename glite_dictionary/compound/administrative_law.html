administrative law

1. NOUN

/ ədˌmɪnɪˈstreɪtɪv lɔ / (AmE), / ədˌmɪnɪsˈtreɪtɪv lɔː / (BrE)
the area of law concerned with the rules, regulations, and decisions made by administrative agencies of government
law concerning government agencies' rules and decisions
legal

Examples

He specialized in administrative law after graduating from law school.
Administrative law regulates how government agencies operate and make decisions.
The case became a landmark in the development of administrative law in the country.

Etymology

From 'administrative' (relating to administration) and 'law'; term used since the 19th century in legal contexts.
