Coats Land

1. PROPN

/ koʊts lænd / (AmE), / kəʊts lænd / (BrE)
a region of western Antarctica situated along the southeastern shore of the Weddell Sea
region in western Antarctica

Examples

Explorers first charted Coats Land in the early twentieth century.
Research teams set up a temporary camp in Coats Land to study the ice sheets.
The edge of the Weddell Sea is bordered by Coats Land to the southeast.

Etymology

Named after the Coats family, sponsors of the Scottish National Antarctic Expedition (1902–04) led by William Speirs Bruce.
