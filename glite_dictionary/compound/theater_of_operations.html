theater of operations

1. NOUN

/ ˈθiːətər əv ˌɑːpəˈreɪʃənz / (AmE), / ˈθɪətə əv ˌɒpəˈreɪʃənz / (BrE)
a region or area where active military action takes place during a conflict
military area of active operations

Examples

During World War II, North Africa was a major theater of operations for Allied forces.
The general oversaw logistics across the entire theater of operations.
Medical teams were stationed just outside the theater of operations to treat wounded soldiers.

Synset Examples

the army was in the field awaiting action
he served in the Vietnam theater for three years

Related Terms

  • Synonym: battlefield - the area where a battle takes place
  • Antonym: home front - the civilian population and activities of a nation at war away from the war zone

Etymology

From military usage; 'theater' meaning a place of action, combined with 'operations' in the sense of organized military activity.
