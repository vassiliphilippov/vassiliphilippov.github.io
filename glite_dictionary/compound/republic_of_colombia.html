Republic of Colombia

1. PROPN

/ rɪˈpʌblɪk əv kəˈlʌmbiə / (AmE), / rɪˈpʌblɪk əv kəˈlɒmbiə / (BrE)
The official name of Colombia, a country in northwestern South America with coastlines on the Pacific Ocean and the Caribbean Sea, independent from Spain since 1821.
country in northwestern South America

Examples

The Republic of Colombia is known for its rich biodiversity and vibrant culture.
After traveling through Ecuador, she crossed the border into the Republic of Colombia.
Simon Bolivar played a crucial role in the independence of the Republic of Colombia from Spain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Colombia - A country in northwestern South America

Etymology

From Latin 'republica' (state, commonwealth) and the name 'Colombia', derived from Christopher Columbus.
