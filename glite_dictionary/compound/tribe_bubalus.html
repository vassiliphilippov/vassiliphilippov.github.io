tribe Bubalus

1. NOUN

/ traɪb ˈbjuːbələs / (AmE), / traɪb ˈbjuːbələs / (BrE)
A taxonomic grouping of water buffalo and closely related animals, sometimes placed in the genus Bos in some classification systems.
taxonomic group of water buffalo

Examples

The tribe Bubalus includes the domesticated water buffalo often seen in Asian agriculture.
Some taxonomists consider the tribe Bubalus distinct from the genus Bos.
The evolutionary history of the tribe Bubalus is a subject of ongoing zoological research.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: water buffalo - large domesticated bovid native to Asia, also known as Bubalus bubalis
  • Synonym: genus Bos - biological genus that includes cattle and related species

Etymology

From Latin 'bubalus' (buffalo or wild ox).
