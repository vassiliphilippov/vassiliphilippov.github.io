magnetic resonance

1. NOUN

/ məgˈnɛtɪk ˈrɛzənəns / (AmE), / mæɡˈnɛtɪk ˈrɛzənəns / (BrE)
a phenomenon in which atomic nuclei or electrons absorb and re-emit electromagnetic radiation when placed in a magnetic field, due to their quantized energy states
absorption/emission of radiation by particles in a magnetic field

Examples

Doctors use magnetic resonance to study the structure and function of internal organs.
The principles of magnetic resonance are widely applied in chemistry and medicine.
Understanding magnetic resonance requires knowledge of quantum mechanics and electromagnetic fields.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: magnetic resonance imaging - a technique that uses magnetic resonance to produce images of organs and structures
