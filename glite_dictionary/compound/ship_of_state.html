ship of state

1. NOUN

/ ʃɪp əv steɪt / (AmE), / ʃɪp əv steɪt / (BrE)
a country’s government or political leadership, often seen as something that needs wise guidance to stay stable
the government or national leadership

Examples

In challenging times, a steady hand is needed at the helm of the ship of state.
Debate continues over who is truly steering the ship of state in the right direction.
The prime minister called for unity to prevent the ship of state from capsizing.

Synset Examples

In times of crisis, the ship of state needs steady hands.
Navigating the ship of state requires strong leadership.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: government - the group of people with the authority to govern a country
  • Synonym: administration - the officials in charge of managing government policy and affairs

Etymology

Metaphorical use based on the image of a ship requiring skilled navigation, popularized since ancient Greece (Plato's 'Republic').
