enlarged heart

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈlɑrdʒd hɑrt / (AmE), / ɪnˈlɑːdʒd hɑːt / (BrE)
a heart that is larger than normal, usually due to disease or medical condition
abnormally large heart
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that an enlarged heart can result from high blood pressure or heart disease.
During the check-up, tests revealed that he had an enlarged heart.
Athletes sometimes show signs of an enlarged heart due to intense physical training.

Synset Examples

mild cardiomegaly is common in athletes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cardiomegaly - medical term for enlargement of the heart
