surgical vacuum pump

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɜrdʒɪkəl ˈvækjuːm pʌmp / (AmE), / ˈsɜːdʒɪkəl ˈvækjuːm pʌmp / (BrE)
A medical device used during and after surgical procedures to remove fluids, tissues, or gases from a patient's body.
device for extracting fluids or gases in surgery
medical

Examples

The surgical vacuum pump was activated to clear excess blood from the wound site.
Proper sterilization of the surgical vacuum pump is critical to prevent infection after surgery.
Modern procedures often rely on a surgical vacuum pump to enhance visibility during operations.

Synset Examples

Surgical vacuum pumps are being used not only for general surgery, but also for liposuction and endoscopy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vacuum pump - a device that removes gas to create a partial vacuum
