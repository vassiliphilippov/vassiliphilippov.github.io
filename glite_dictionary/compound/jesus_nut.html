Jesus nut

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːzəs nʌt / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːzəs nʌt / (BrE)
a vital fastener, especially in helicopters, that secures the main rotor to the mast and whose failure would result in catastrophic loss of control
critical aviation fastener securing the rotor

Examples

The pilot checked the Jesus nut before taking off to ensure everything was secure.
A loose Jesus nut can cause catastrophic failure in a helicopter's rotor system.
During maintenance, the engineer carefully inspected the Jesus nut for any signs of wear.

Synset Examples

He tightened the Jesus nut for safety during the flight.
The mechanic explained the Jesus nut keeps the rotor on.
Don't forget to check the Jesus nut before flying.

Etymology

Informal American aviation slang; from the exclamation 'Jesus!' invoked at the potential consequences of its failure.
