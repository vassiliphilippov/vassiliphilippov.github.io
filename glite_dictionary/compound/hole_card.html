hole card

1. NOUN

/ ˈhoʊl kɑrd / (AmE), / ˈhəʊl kɑːd / (BrE)
(in poker) a playing card dealt face down to a player, not revealed until the showdown.
card dealt face down in poker

Examples

Each player receives one hole card and one card face up.
She won the hand with an ace as her hole card.
You should never reveal your hole card before the showdown.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: community card - a card dealt face up in the center of the table, shared by all players

2. NOUN

/ hoʊl kɑrd / (AmE), / həʊl kɑːd / (BrE)
A hidden asset, resource, or advantage kept in reserve to be revealed when most beneficial.
hidden advantage used strategically

Examples

She kept her experience as a hole card until the final interview round.
The company revealed a new patent as their hole card during negotiations.
He always seems to have a hole card when the odds are against him.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trump card - an advantage kept in reserve for use at a strategic moment
  • Antonym: disadvantage - an unfavorable or less advantageous position

Etymology

From poker, where a 'hole card' is a face-down card known only to the player.
