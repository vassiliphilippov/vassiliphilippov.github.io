family Gruidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæm.ə.li ɡruːˈaɪ.di / (AmE), / ˈfæm.ɪ.li ɡruːˈiː.daɪ / (BrE)
A family of large, long-legged and long-necked birds known as cranes, found in temperate and tropical regions worldwide.
the biological family of cranes

Examples

The family Gruidae includes several species of cranes found across many continents.
Researchers are studying the migration patterns of birds in the family Gruidae.
Cranes belonging to the family Gruidae are known for their elaborate mating dances.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crane - a large wading bird with long legs and neck

Etymology

From scientific Latin 'Gruidae', from 'Grus' (genus name for cranes), ultimately from Latin 'grus' (crane).
