nucleolar organizer

1. NOUN

/ nuːˈkliːəˌlɑːr ˈɔːrɡənaɪzər / (AmE), / njuːˈkliːələ ˈɔːɡənaɪzə / (BrE)
a region of a chromosome involved in the formation of the nucleolus after cell division
chromosome region forming nucleolus
medical

Examples

The nucleolar organizer plays a crucial role in nucleolus formation during cell division.
Scientists identified abnormalities in the nucleolar organizer region of certain chromosomes.
The presence of a nucleolar organizer can be detected through specialized staining techniques.

Etymology

From 'nucleolar', relating to the nucleolus, and 'organizer', from organize + -er.
