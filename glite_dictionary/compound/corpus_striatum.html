corpus striatum

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɔːrpəs straɪˈeɪtəm / (AmE), / ˌkɔːpəs strɪˈeɪtəm / (BrE)
a mass of white and grey matter in the brain, located in front of the thalamus and consisting of the caudate nucleus and the lenticular nucleus
region of the brain with striped appearance
medical

Examples

The corpus striatum plays an important role in controlling movement.
Damage to the corpus striatum can result in movement disorders such as Parkinson's disease.
Researchers studied how the corpus striatum communicates with other parts of the brain.

Etymology

From Latin 'corpus' (body) and 'striatus' (striped), referring to its striated appearance.
