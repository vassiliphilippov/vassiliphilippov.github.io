rack and pinion

1. NOUN

/ ræk ənd pɪn.jən / (AmE), / ræk ənd ˈpɪn.i.ən / (BrE)
A mechanical device consisting of a round gear (the pinion) engaging with a flat, toothed bar (the rack), used to convert rotary motion into linear motion or vice versa.
mechanism converting rotary to linear motion

Examples

The steering system in many cars uses a rack and pinion mechanism for precise control.
The engineer explained how a rack and pinion converts the motor's rotation into straight-line motion.
Our workshop project involved building a model of a rack and pinion to demonstrate linear movement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gear - a rotating machine part having cut teeth

Etymology

From 'rack' (toothed bar) and 'pinion' (small cogwheel); both terms date to the 17th century in mechanical contexts.
