Latin alphabet

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæt̬.ɪn ˈæl.fə.bɛt / (AmE), / ˈlæt.ɪn ˈæl.fə.bɛt / (BrE)
the alphabet developed by the ancient Romans, used today for writing English and many other western European languages
alphabet originating with the ancient Romans

Examples

The English language is written using the Latin alphabet, unlike Russian, which uses Cyrillic.
Many languages in Africa and Asia have adapted the Latin alphabet for their writing systems.
Learning the Latin alphabet is an essential step in reading and writing many European languages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Roman alphabet - another term for the Latin alphabet

Etymology

'Latin' from Latin 'Latinus', pertaining to Latium; 'alphabet' from Greek 'alpha' and 'beta'.
