pin-up art

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪn ʌp ɑrt / (AmE), / ˈpɪn ʌp ɑːt / (BrE)
art featuring models, typically women, posed in a glamorous or provocative manner, often evoking a nostalgic style from the mid-20th century
art with models in suggestive, nostalgic poses

Examples

He collected vintage pin-up art from the 1940s and 1950s.
Many magazines featured pin-up art to boost morale during wartime.
The exhibition showcases the evolution of pin-up art throughout the decades.

Synset Examples

He admired the vintage pin-up art at the gallery.
She has a collection of classic pin-up posters in her room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pin-up - a model, typically portrayed in a glamorous or provocative pose
  • Synonym: glamour photography - photography emphasizing the allure and beauty of the subject

Etymology

From 'pin-up' (images designed to be pinned on a wall) and 'art'.
