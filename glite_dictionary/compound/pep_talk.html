pep talk

1. NOUN

/ pɛp tɑk / (AmE), / pɛp tɔːk / (BrE)
a speech or talk intended to encourage and motivate someone or a group to perform better or with more enthusiasm
motivational talk to boost morale

Examples

Before the game, the coach gave the team a pep talk to boost their confidence.
I needed a pep talk from my manager before making the big presentation.
She always knows how to give a great pep talk when we are feeling down.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: motivational speech - a speech intended to inspire or encourage an audience

Etymology

From 'pep' (short for 'pepper', meaning energy or vigor) + 'talk'.

/ pɛp tɑk / (AmE), / pɛp tɔːk / (BrE)
a speech or conversation intended to encourage someone and inspire confidence or enthusiasm
motivational speech or conversation

Examples

Sometimes a simple pep talk from a friend can make a huge difference in your mood.
The teacher gave her students a quick pep talk before the final exam.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

Etymology

Formed from 'pep', meaning energy or spirit (early 20th century slang abbreviation of 'pepper'), and 'talk'.
