eastern spruce

1. NOUN

/ ˈiːstɚn sprus / (AmE), / ˈiːstən spruːs / (BrE)
A medium-sized species of spruce tree native to eastern North America, valued for lumber and pulpwood.
spruce tree of eastern North America

Examples

Many loggers in Canada harvest eastern spruce for its high-quality lumber.
The paper mill uses eastern spruce as a main source of pulpwood.
The dense forests of Maine are rich in eastern spruce trees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: white spruce - a closely related species of spruce tree in North America
