bird's-eye view

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɝdz aɪ ˌvju / (AmE), / ˈbɜːdz aɪ ˌvjuː / (BrE)
A view of something from a high position, giving a broad and comprehensive perspective of the whole area.
view from above showing broad area

Examples

We climbed to the top of the building for a spectacular bird's-eye view of the entire city.
The brochure included a bird's-eye view map to help visitors navigate the park.
With satellite images, scientists can study a bird's-eye view of environmental changes over time.

Synset Examples

From the mountain's peak, we enjoyed a bird's-eye perspective of the valley.
The map offered a bird's-eye rendition of the city's streets.
The drone gave us a bird's-eye view of the festival.
He explained the plan with a bird's-eye view to ensure everyone understood.
The architect provided a bird's-eye view of the entire project layout.
From the tower, we had a stunning bird's-eye view of the ocean.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aerial view - A view from the air, typically taken from an aircraft or drone

Etymology

Refers to the perspective a bird might have when flying high above an area.
