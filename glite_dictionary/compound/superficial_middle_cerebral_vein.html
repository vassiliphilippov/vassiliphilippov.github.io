superficial middle cerebral vein

1. NOUN

/ suː.pɚˈfɪʃ.əl ˈmɪd.əl sɪˈriː.brəl veɪn / (AmE), / suː.pəˈfɪʃ.əl ˈmɪd.l̩ səˈriː.brəl veɪn / (BrE)
a large vein running along the lateral sulcus (Sylvian fissure) of the brain, draining into the cavernous sinus
a vein in the lateral sulcus of the brain
medical

Examples

The superficial middle cerebral vein can be seen during neurosurgical procedures involving the lateral surface of the brain.
Damage to the superficial middle cerebral vein may result in complications during brain surgery.
On an MRI scan, the superficial middle cerebral vein is often visible near the Sylvian fissure.
