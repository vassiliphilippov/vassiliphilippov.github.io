colonial architecture

1. NOUN

/ kəˈloʊniəl ˈɑːrkɪˌtɛktʃɚ / (AmE), / kəˈləʊniəl ˈɑːkɪˌtɛktʃə / (BrE)
a style of architecture characteristic of buildings constructed during a region’s colonial period, often reflecting the styles of the colonizing country
architecture from colonial times

Examples

The old government building is a fine example of colonial architecture.
Many Caribbean cities are known for their well-preserved colonial architecture.
Tourists flock to see the city's historic colonial architecture on guided walking tours.

Synset Examples

The Raffles hotel is famous for its colonial architecture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: colonial - relating to colonies, especially in reference to the period when a country was under colonial rule
