equine distemper

1. NOUN

/ ˈiːˌkwaɪn dɪˈstɛmpɚ / (AmE), / ˈiːˌkwaɪn dɪˈstɛmpə / (BrE)
An acute bacterial disease affecting horses, marked by inflammation of the mucous membranes, especially in the nose and throat.
bacterial disease affecting horses
medical

Examples

The veterinarian diagnosed the horse with equine distemper after noting its swollen lymph nodes.
Prevention of equine distemper includes isolating infected animals and maintaining good stable hygiene.
The stable was quarantined when several horses showed symptoms of equine distemper.

Etymology

From Latin 'equīnus' (of a horse) and Middle English 'distemper' (disease, disorder).
