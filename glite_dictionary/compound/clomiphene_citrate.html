clomiphene citrate

1. NOUN

/ kloʊˈmɪfiːn ˈsaɪtreɪt / (AmE), / kləʊˈmɪfiːn ˈsɪtreɪt / (BrE)
a synthetic drug used to stimulate ovulation in women with fertility problems, sometimes resulting in multiple births
fertility drug for stimulating ovulation
medical

Examples

After three cycles of clomiphene citrate therapy, she finally became pregnant.
The doctor prescribed clomiphene citrate to help induce ovulation in patients with infertility.
Side effects of clomiphene citrate may include hot flashes and mood swings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Clomid - trade name for clomiphene citrate

Etymology

From clomiphene, a synthetic compound, plus citrate (a salt or ester of citric acid).
