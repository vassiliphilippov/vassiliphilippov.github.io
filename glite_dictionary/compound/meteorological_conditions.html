meteorological conditions

1. NOUN

/ ˌmit̬iəˈrɑlədʒɪkəl kənˈdɪʃənz / (AmE), / ˌmiːtiərəˈlɒdʒɪkəl kənˈdɪʃənz / (BrE)
the state of the atmosphere at a particular time, especially as it affects weather forecasting or climate analysis
prevailing state of the atmosphere (weather)

Examples

The flight was delayed because the meteorological conditions were deemed unsafe for takeoff.
Scientists monitor meteorological conditions to predict severe storms and issue warnings.
Changes in meteorological conditions can have a significant impact on agricultural productivity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weather conditions - the atmospheric conditions at a certain time and place

Etymology

From Greek 'meteōrologia' (study of atmospheric phenomena) + Latin 'conditio' (state, situation).
