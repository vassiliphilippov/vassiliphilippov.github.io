tree hugger

1. NOUN

/ ˈtri ˌhʌɡɚ / (AmE), / ˈtriː ˌhʌɡə / (BrE)
A derogatory term for an environmentalist, especially one who opposes logging or supports the protection of forests.
(slang) derogatory term for environmentalist
slang

Examples

Some people dismiss climate activists as tree huggers, but their concerns are becoming mainstream.
The protesters, labeled tree huggers by critics, chained themselves to the old oaks.
While he used to mock tree huggers, he now volunteers for several environmental organizations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: environmentalist - a person concerned with protecting the environment

Etymology

From the literal act of hugging trees, popularized as a symbol of environmental activism, especially in protests.

2. NOUN

/ tri ˈhʌɡər / (AmE), / tri ˈhʌɡə / (BrE)
An informal term for an environmentalist or conservationist, especially one strongly dedicated to protecting trees and nature.
informal term for an environmentalist
informal slang

Examples

Despite being called a tree hugger, he continued his fight to save the local forest.
Many tree huggers volunteer for organizations that plant trees in urban areas.
She wore her tree hugger badge with pride at the environmental conference.

Synset Examples

The community labeled her a tree hugger for her passion.
As a tree hugger, she advocates for environmental protection.
He proudly identifies as a tree hugger at every rally.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: environmentalist - a person who is concerned with or advocates the protection of the environment

Etymology

From the literal idea of hugging trees, associated with environmental protests.
