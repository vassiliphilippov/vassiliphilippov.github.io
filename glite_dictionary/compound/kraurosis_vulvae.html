kraurosis vulvae

1. NOUN

/ kraʊˈroʊsɪs ˈvʌlvi / (AmE), / kraʊˈrəʊsɪs ˈvʌlviː / (BrE)
A chronic atrophic condition affecting the vulva, often considered a precancerous lesion.
atrophic vulvar condition, sometimes precancerous
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with kraurosis vulvae after presenting with itching and dryness.
Some studies suggest that kraurosis vulvae can be a precursor to certain vulvar cancers if left untreated.
Topical steroid creams are sometimes prescribed to manage the symptoms of kraurosis vulvae.

Etymology

From Greek 'krauros' (dry, shriveled) and Latin 'vulva' (womb, covering).
