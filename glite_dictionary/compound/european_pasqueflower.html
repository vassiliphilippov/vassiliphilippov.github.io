European pasqueflower

1. NOUN

/ jʊˈroʊpiən ˈpæskˌflaʊər / (AmE), / jʊəˈrəʊpiən ˈpaskˌflaʊə / (BrE)
a perennial plant native to Europe, having violet or white flowers that bloom in spring
European perennial plant with spring flowers

Examples

The European pasqueflower is among the first blooms to appear in alpine meadows each spring.
Gardeners prize the delicate beauty of the European pasqueflower for early-season color.
After the snow melts, clusters of European pasqueflower brighten the rocky slopes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pasqueflower - a plant of the genus Pulsatilla with showy flowers

Etymology

From 'pasque', from French 'pâque' (Easter), referring to the time of flowering, and 'flower'.
