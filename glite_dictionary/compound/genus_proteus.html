genus Proteus

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒiːnəs ˈproʊtiəs / (AmE), / ˌdʒiːnəs ˈprəʊtiəs / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Proteidae, comprising aquatic, eel-like amphibians such as the olm.
genus of aquatic amphibians (Proteidae)

Examples

The olm, a rare cave-dwelling amphibian, belongs to the genus Proteus.
Scientists have long studied the unusual characteristics of the genus Proteus.
Members of the genus Proteus are known for their elongated, eel-like bodies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Proteidae - family of aquatic, eel-like amphibians

Etymology

From Latin 'Proteus', a sea god in Greek mythology, used in taxonomy; 'genus' from Latin for kind or type.
