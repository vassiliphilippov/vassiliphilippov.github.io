subclass Cnidosporidia

1. NOUN

/ ˌsʌbˌklæs ˌnaɪdoʊspɔːˈrɪdiə / (AmE), / ˌsʌbˌklɑːs ˌnaɪdəʊspɔːˈrɪdiə / (BrE)
A group of single-host parasitic protozoans that infect lower vertebrates and invertebrates.
group of single-host parasitic protozoans
medical

Examples

Scientists have discovered new species within the subclass Cnidosporidia affecting freshwater fish.
The subclass Cnidosporidia is known for producing resistant spores in its host organisms.
Research on the subclass Cnidosporidia has advanced our understanding of parasitic infections in aquatic environments.

Etymology

From New Latin 'Cnidosporidia', based on Greek 'knidē' (nettle) + 'spora' (seed, spore), referencing the spore structure.
