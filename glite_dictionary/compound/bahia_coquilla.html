bahia coquilla

1. NOUN

/ bəˈhiː.ə koʊˈkiː.jə / (AmE), / bəˈhiː.ə kəʊˈkɪl.jə / (BrE)
A Brazilian palm tree whose fibers are used in making ropes, mats, and brushes.
Brazilian palm with fibrous material

Examples

The fibers from the bahia coquilla are prized for their durability in brush-making.
Many traditional ropes in Brazil are crafted from bahia coquilla fibers.
Eco-friendly mats made from bahia coquilla are becoming more popular in local markets.

Etymology

From Bahia, a state in Brazil, and Portuguese 'coquilla' (a kind of palm).
