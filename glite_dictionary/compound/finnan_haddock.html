finnan haddock

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪnən ˈhædək / (AmE), / ˈfɪnən ˈhædək / (BrE)
A smoked haddock, typically from Scotland, often baked or broiled with butter; a traditional dish.
smoked haddock dish (Scotland)

Examples

We had finnan haddock for breakfast during our trip to Scotland.
The chef recommended grilling the finnan haddock with a touch of butter.
Traditional Scottish restaurants often feature finnan haddock on their menus.

Etymology

From 'Finnan', a shortening of Findon, a village near Aberdeen, Scotland, where the dish was originally prepared, and 'haddock', a species of fish.
