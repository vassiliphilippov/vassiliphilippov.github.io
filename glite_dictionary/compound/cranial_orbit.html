cranial orbit

1. NOUN

/ ˈkreɪniəl ˈɔrbɪt / (AmE), / ˈkreɪnɪəl ˈɔːbɪt / (BrE)
the bony cavity in the skull that contains and protects the eyeball
bony eye socket in the skull
medical

Examples

The surgeon carefully examined the cranial orbit for any signs of fracture after the accident.
In some animals, the cranial orbit is positioned differently to give them a wider field of vision.
The CT scan revealed a small tumor within the patient's left cranial orbit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orbit - the bony socket in the skull containing the eye

Etymology

From Latin 'cranium' (skull) and 'orbita' (track, orbit, eye socket).
