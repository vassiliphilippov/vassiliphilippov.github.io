genus Stenotomus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒinəs stɛˈnɑːtəməs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs stɛˈnɒtəməs / (BrE)
A genus of marine fish in the family Sparidae, commonly known as scups, found in the western Atlantic Ocean.
a genus of marine fish (scups)

Examples

The genus Stenotomus includes several species commonly known as scups.
Researchers studied population trends within the genus Stenotomus along the Atlantic coast.
Fish in the genus Stenotomus are important both ecologically and commercially.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scup - a common marine fish of the species Stenotomus chrysops

Etymology

From New Latin: 'Stenotomus' is derived from Greek 'stenos' (narrow) and 'tomos' (cut, section).
