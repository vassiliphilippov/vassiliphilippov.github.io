English language

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
The West Germanic language spoken as a first language in the United Kingdom, the United States, and many other countries; the main global language of international communication.
global West Germanic language

Examples

The English language continues to evolve, absorbing new words from other tongues.
He is fluent in several languages, but the English language is his native tongue.
Studying the history of the English language reveals how it spread around the world.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: German language - The West Germanic language spoken in Germany and Austria
  • Degree Lower: French language - The Romance language spoken chiefly in France and worldwide

Etymology

From Old English 'Englisc' (English) + 'language' from Latin 'lingua' (tongue, speech).
