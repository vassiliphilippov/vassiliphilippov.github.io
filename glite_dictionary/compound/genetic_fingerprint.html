genetic fingerprint

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˈnɛtɪk ˈfɪŋɡərˌprɪnt / (AmE), / dʒɪˈnɛtɪk ˈfɪŋɡəˌprɪnt / (BrE)
A pattern unique to an individual, obtained by analyzing a person's DNA sequence, used especially for identification purposes in forensic science.
unique DNA pattern for identification

Examples

The suspect was identified after police matched his genetic fingerprint with the evidence found at the scene.
Every individual has a unique genetic fingerprint, making DNA analysis a powerful tool in criminal investigations.
Researchers used the genetic fingerprint technique to confirm the biological relationship between the two individuals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: DNA fingerprint - analysis of DNA sequences to identify individuals

Etymology

Compound of 'genetic' (from genes/DNA) and 'fingerprint', referring to the uniqueness of the DNA profile, analogous to a fingerprint.
