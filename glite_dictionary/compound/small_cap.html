small cap

1. NOUN

/ smɔl kæp / (AmE), / smɔːl kæp / (BrE)
a publicly traded company with a relatively small market capitalization, typically below a certain threshold defined by stock exchanges.
company with small market capitalization

Examples

Many investors diversify their portfolios by including small cap stocks for higher growth potential.
The small cap sector outperformed the large cap stocks this quarter.
Analysts believe small cap companies are more vulnerable to market fluctuations.

Synset Examples

this annual conference is a showcase for ambitious small caps

Related Terms

  • Antonym: large cap - company with large market capitalization
  • Degree Higher: mid cap - company with medium market capitalization

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'small capitalization', used in financial contexts.
