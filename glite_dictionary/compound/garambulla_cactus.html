garambulla cactus

1. NOUN

/ ɡəˌrɑːmˈbuːjə ˈkæktəs / (AmE), / ɡəˌræmˈbʊljə ˈkæktəs / (BrE)
A tall, tree-like cactus native to western Mexico, known for its small, oblong, edible berrylike fruit.
arborescent cactus of western Mexico with edible fruit

Examples

The garambulla cactus produces small, sweet fruits that are enjoyed by local communities in Mexico.
Researchers are studying how the garambulla cactus adapts to arid climates.
A traditional Mexican dessert is sometimes made using the fruit of the garambulla cactus.

Etymology

From Spanish 'garambullo', used in Mexico for this species and its fruit.
