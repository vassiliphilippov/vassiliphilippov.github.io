air search radar

1. NOUN

/ ɛr sɝtʃ ˈreɪˌdɑr / (AmE), / eə sɜːtʃ ˈreɪˌdɑː / (BrE)
a shipboard radar system designed to detect and track aircraft in the surrounding airspace.
radar system for detecting aircraft

Examples

The air search radar scanned the sky continuously for incoming enemy aircraft.
Modern warships are equipped with advanced air search radar to enhance their defense systems.
During the exercise, the air search radar detected several planes approaching from the east.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: radar - a system for detecting the presence, direction, distance, and speed of objects
