cotter pin

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːt̬ɚ pɪn / (AmE), / ˈkɒtə pɪn / (BrE)
A metal fastener consisting of a split pin that, after being inserted through a hole, is secured by bending its ends apart.
split pin fastener

Examples

Make sure to insert a cotter pin through the hole to lock the castle nut in place.
He replaced the old cotter pin with a new one to ensure the machinery was secure.
Always bend the ends of the cotter pin outward to prevent it from slipping out.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: split pin - a metal fastener consisting of a pin split at one end

Etymology

From 'cotter' (a metal pin) and 'pin'; 'cotter' from Middle English, perhaps from Old French 'coter' (to wedge).
