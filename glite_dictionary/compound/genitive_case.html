genitive case

1. NOUN

/ ˈʤɛnətɪv keɪs / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛnɪtɪv keɪs / (BrE)
the grammatical case indicating possession or a close relationship, usually marked in English by the addition of 's or an apostrophe
possessive grammatical case

Examples

In English, the genitive case is shown by adding 's to a noun, as in 'the dog's leash'.
Many languages use the genitive case to indicate familial relationships.
The teacher explained how the genitive case works in German grammar.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: possessive case - grammatical case marking possession (in English, same as genitive)

Etymology

From Latin 'genitivus' (of or belonging to birth or kind), via Old French and late Middle English.
