beech fern

1. NOUN

/ bitʃ fɝn / (AmE), / biːtʃ fɜːn / (BrE)
A type of fern of the genus Phegopteris, characterized by deeply cut triangular fronds.
a fern of the genus Phegopteris

Examples

The beech fern grows abundantly in moist woodland areas.
Gardeners appreciate beech ferns for their delicate, lacy appearance.
A cluster of beech fern provided lush ground cover beneath the shade of tall trees.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: fern - a nonflowering, vascular plant with feathery fronds
