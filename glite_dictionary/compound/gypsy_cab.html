gypsy cab

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɪpsi kæb / (AmE), / ˈdʒɪpsi kæb / (BrE)
A taxi that picks up passengers without proper authorization to do so, often while only licensed to respond to phone calls and not to pick up passengers from the street.
illegally cruising taxicab
american

Examples

We accidentally hailed a gypsy cab instead of an official taxi at the airport.
The city has cracked down on gypsy cabs that pick up passengers without permits.
You should avoid taking a gypsy cab late at night for your own safety.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hack taxi - A taxi that picks up passengers without a proper license, similar in meaning

Etymology

The term 'gypsy' here refers metaphorically to roaming or wandering, likening the unauthorized cab’s operation to the nomadic lifestyle stereotypically associated with the Romani people.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɪpsi kæb / (AmE), / ˈdʒɪpsi kab / (BrE)
An unofficial taxi, often unlicensed and unmetered, that is hired directly from the street rather than through a regulated taxi service.
unofficial, unlicensed taxi

Examples

After the concert, we flagged down a gypsy cab to get home faster.
Some cities try to crack down on gypsy cabs for safety reasons.
The gypsy cab offered a cheaper fare than the official taxis.

Synset Examples

The gypsy cab driver knew all the shortcuts.
We took a gypsy cab to avoid the long wait.
He prefers gypsy cabs for their quick service.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: taxi - a car licensed to transport passengers in return for payment of a fare
  • Synonym: hackney carriage - a taxicab, especially a traditional one in Britain
  • Antonym: licensed taxi - a legally regulated taxi that follows official standards
