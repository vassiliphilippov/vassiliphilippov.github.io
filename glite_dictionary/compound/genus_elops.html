genus Elops

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈiː.lɑps / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈiː.lɒps / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Elopidae, comprising fish commonly known as tenpounders.
type genus of Elopidae (tenpounder fish)

Examples

The genus Elops includes several species of marine fish found in coastal waters.
Biologists studied the migratory patterns of fish from the genus Elops.
Many anglers are familiar with the tenpounder, a member of the genus Elops.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Elopidae - a family of fishes including the tenpounder and ladyfish

Etymology

From Greek elops (a kind of fish); used in zoological taxonomy.
