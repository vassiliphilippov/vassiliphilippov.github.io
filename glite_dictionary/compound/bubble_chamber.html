bubble chamber

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌb.əl ˌtʃæm.bɚ / (AmE), / ˈbʌb.əl ˌtʃæm.bə / (BrE)
a device used in physics to detect and track the paths of ionizing particles by observing the bubbles formed in a superheated liquid
physics device for tracking particles

Examples

The scientists analyzed the photographs taken in the bubble chamber to study subatomic particle interactions.
A bubble chamber allows researchers to visualize the movements of charged particles as they leave trails of bubbles.
Before more advanced detectors were developed, the bubble chamber was a crucial tool in experimental physics.
