operations research

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑpəˈreɪʃənz rɪˈsɜrtʃ / (AmE), / ˌɒpəˈreɪʃənz rɪˈsɜːtʃ / (BrE)
the study of mathematical methods used to analyze complex systems and to determine the most efficient way to achieve a desired outcome or solve organizational problems
field of applying math to optimize decisions

Examples

Large corporations use operations research to improve their supply chain management.
During World War II, operations research played a key role in military planning.
A master's degree in operations research can open doors to careers in analytics and consulting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: management science - discipline that uses analytical methods to help make business decisions
  • Synonym: optimization - process of making something as effective as possible

Etymology

The term emerged in the 1940s, combining 'operation' and 'research' to describe systematic analysis for military and organizational efficiency.
