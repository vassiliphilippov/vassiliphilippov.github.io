Austrian schilling

1. NOUN

/ ˈaʊstriən ʃɪˈlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɒstriən ʃɪˈlɪŋ / (BrE)
the former basic unit of currency in Austria, replaced by the euro in 2002
former currency of Austria

Examples

Before the introduction of the euro, the Austrian schilling was used for all transactions in Austria.
He exchanged his remaining Austrian schillings for euros at the bank.
Collectors seek old coins from the Austrian schilling era.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: euro - the official currency used in most countries of the European Union
