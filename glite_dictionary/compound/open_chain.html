open chain

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊpən tʃeɪn / (AmE), / ˈəʊpən tʃeɪn / (BrE)
a sequence of atoms in a molecule linked in a chain whose ends are not connected to form a ring
chain of atoms not forming a ring

Examples

Alkanes are examples of molecules with an open chain structure.
Some sugars can exist in both a ring and an open chain form.
The open chain arrangement affects the chemical properties of the compound.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ring - a cyclic arrangement of atoms in a molecule
