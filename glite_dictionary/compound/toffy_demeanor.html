toffy demeanor

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɑfi dɪˈmiːnər / (AmE), / ˈtɒfi dɪˈmiːnə / (BrE)
An affectedly posh or snobbish manner, as if emulating the style of an upper-class person.
affectedly snobbish or posh manner
informal british

Examples

Despite his toffy demeanor, he was quite approachable once you got to know him.
Her toffy demeanor was just a facade to hide her insecurities.
They laughed at his toffy demeanor and exaggerated accent.

Synset Examples

Her toffy demeanor didn't impress the crowd.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snobbish - having or showing the attitude of people who think they are better than others
  • Antonym: humble - having or showing a modest or low view of one's importance

Etymology

From the informal British term 'toff' (an upper-class person) + 'demeanor'.
