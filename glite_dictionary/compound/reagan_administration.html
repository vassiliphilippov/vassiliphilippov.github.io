Reagan administration

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪɡən ədˌmɪnɪˈstreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈreɪɡən ədˌmɪnɪˈstreɪʃən / (BrE)
The executive branch of the United States government under President Ronald Reagan, from 1981 to 1989.
U.S. government under President Reagan (1981–1989)

Examples

The Reagan administration implemented significant tax cuts and increased defense spending during the 1980s.
Environmental policies were greatly debated under the Reagan administration.
Historians continue to assess the impact of the Reagan administration on American foreign relations.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Carter administration - the executive under President Carter
  • Degree Higher: Bush administration - the executive under President George H. W. Bush or George W. Bush
