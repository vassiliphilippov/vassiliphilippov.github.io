lady's slipper

1. NOUN

/ ˈleɪdiz ˌslɪpər / (AmE), / ˈleɪdiz ˌslɪpə / (BrE)
any of several North American wildflowers of the orchid family, having an inflated, slipper-shaped lip on the flower
wild orchid with slipper-shaped flower

Examples

The rare lady's slipper was spotted blooming in the forest this spring.
Gardeners admire the beauty of the lady's slipper, though it is hard to grow in captivity.
We photographed several lady's slippers along the nature trail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cypripedium - genus of lady's slipper orchids

Etymology

Named for the flower's resemblance to a slipper and traditionally associated with a 'lady'; use dates from the 16th century.
