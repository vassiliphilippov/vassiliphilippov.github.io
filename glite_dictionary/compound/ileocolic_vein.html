ileocolic vein

1. NOUN

/ ˌaɪli.oʊˈkɑlɪk veɪn / (AmE), / ˌaɪli.əʊˈkɒlɪk veɪn / (BrE)
a vein that carries blood from the end of the ileum, the appendix, the cecum, and the lower part of the ascending colon
vein draining parts of the lower right abdomen
medical

Examples

The surgeon located the ileocolic vein before removing the diseased section of the intestine.
Inflammation in the lower abdomen can sometimes affect the ileocolic vein.
During the operation, bleeding from the ileocolic vein had to be carefully controlled.

Etymology

From 'ileum' (last part of the small intestine), 'colon' (large intestine), and Latin 'vena' (vein).
