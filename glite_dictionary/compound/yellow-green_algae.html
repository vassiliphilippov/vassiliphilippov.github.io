yellow-green algae

1. NOUN

/ ˌjɛloʊˈɡrin ˈældʒi / (AmE), / ˌjɛləʊˈɡriːn ˈaldʒiː / (BrE)
Any algae of the division Chrysophyta in which the chlorophyll is masked by yellow pigment.
algae with yellow pigment, division Chrysophyta

Examples

Many freshwater ponds are home to yellow-green algae that thrive in nutrient-rich water.
Under the microscope, yellow-green algae display their characteristic pigment clearly.
Scientists are studying yellow-green algae for their potential role in aquatic ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chrysophyte - a member of the division Chrysophyta, often called golden algae
