anatomical structure

1. NOUN

/ ˌæn.əˈtɑ.mɪ.kəl ˈstrʌk.tʃɚ / (AmE), / ˌæn.əˈtɒm.ɪ.kəl ˈstrʌk.tʃə / (BrE)
a part of a living organism that has a specific form and function, formed by the arrangement and construction of tissues and organs
part of a living organism with specific form and function

Examples

The heart is an anatomical structure responsible for pumping blood throughout the body.
Scientists discovered a new anatomical structure in the frog's digestive system.
Knowledge of each anatomical structure is essential for understanding how the human body works.

Synset Examples

he has good bone structure

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: tissue - group of similar cells performing a function
  • Degree Higher: organ - distinct body part with specific function

Etymology

From Greek anatome (dissection) + Latin structura (a fitting together, building).
