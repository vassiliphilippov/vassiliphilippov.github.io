social anxiety

1. NOUN

/ ˈsoʊʃl æŋˈzaɪəti / (AmE), / ˈsəʊʃl æŋˈzaɪəti / (BrE)
A feeling of unease or fear during social interactions, often due to worry about being judged or evaluated by others.
fear or discomfort in social situations

Examples

Her social anxiety made it difficult for her to attend large gatherings.
People with social anxiety often worry excessively about being judged by others.
Therapy can help individuals manage the symptoms of social anxiety.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stage fright - fear or anxiety felt before or while performing in front of an audience
  • Antonym: confidence - a feeling or belief that one can rely on oneself; self-assurance

Etymology

From 'social' (relating to society or interaction) + 'anxiety' (from Latin anxietas, 'uneasiness').
