debt ratio

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛt ˈreɪʃiˌoʊ / (AmE), / ˈdɛt ˈreɪʃɪəʊ / (BrE)
A financial ratio that measures the proportion of a company's total debt to its total assets, indicating financial leverage and risk.
proportion of total debt to total assets

Examples

The company's debt ratio increased after it took out several large loans last year.
Investors often look at the debt ratio to assess a firm's financial health before buying shares.
A lower debt ratio generally suggests a company is less risky for creditors.

Synset Examples

A debt ratio indicates how risky it would be for a bank to extend a loan to a company, with a higher ratio indicating great risk.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: debt-equity ratio - ratio of total liabilities to shareholders' equity
