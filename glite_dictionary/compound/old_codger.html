old codger

1. NOUN

/ oʊld ˈkɑdʒər / (AmE), / əʊld ˈkɒdʒə / (BrE)
an eccentric but likeable old man, used affectionately or humorously
eccentric but likeable old man
informal

Examples

Everyone in the village knew the old codger who told wild stories at the pub.
That old codger next door still shovels his own driveway every winter.
My uncle is a real old codger, always cracking jokes and wearing silly hats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: geezer - an old man, often eccentric or odd

2. NOUN

/ oʊld ˈkɑdʒɚ / (AmE), / əʊld ˈkɒdʒə / (BrE)
an old man, especially one who is eccentric, grumpy, or has quirky behavior
eccentric or grumpy old man
informal

Examples

The old codger next door spends hours tinkering with his antique radio collection.
Everyone in town has heard a story about that old codger at the general store.
Despite being a bit of an old codger, he always remembers everyone's birthday.

Synset Examples

Don't mind the old codger; he's harmless.
That old codger knows everyone in the neighborhood.
The old codger down the street has great stories.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: geezer - an old man, often eccentric (British informal)
  • Synonym: old-timer - an elderly person, especially a man

Etymology

From 'codger,' of uncertain origin, possibly from the dialect term 'cadger' (beggar or peddler) in English.
