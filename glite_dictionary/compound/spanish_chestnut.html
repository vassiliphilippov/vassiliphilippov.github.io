Spanish chestnut

1. NOUN

/ ˈspænɪʃ ˈtʃɛs.nʌt / (AmE), / ˈspænɪʃ ˈtʃɛs.nʌt / (BrE)
a large tree, Castanea sativa, native to southern Europe, northwestern Africa, and southwestern Asia, grown for its edible nuts; also, the nut itself
tree and edible nut of southern Europe

Examples

The Spanish chestnut is prized for its sweet, edible nuts sold in many European markets.
They planted a Spanish chestnut in the garden for its beautiful foliage and shade.
Roasted Spanish chestnuts are a traditional treat during the winter holidays in Italy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chestnut - a tree producing edible nuts; the nut of the chestnut tree
