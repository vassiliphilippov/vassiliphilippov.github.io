East Germanic

1. NOUN

/ ist ˈdʒɜrmənɪk / (AmE), / iːst ˈdʒɜːmənɪk / (BrE)
an extinct group of Germanic languages formerly spoken in Eastern Europe, including Gothic, Vandalic, and others
extinct Germanic language branch

Examples

The East Germanic languages, such as Gothic, are no longer spoken today.
Scholars study East Germanic texts to better understand the migration period.
Unlike West Germanic tongues, East Germanic left few written records.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: West Germanic - the branch of Germanic languages including English, German, and Dutch
  • Antonym: North Germanic - the branch of Germanic languages including Scandinavian languages

Etymology

From the position in eastern Europe and the Germanic roots; 'Germanic' from Latin 'Germanicus', referring to the people and their languages.
