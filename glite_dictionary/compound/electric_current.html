electric current

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈlɛktrɪk ˈkɜrənt / (AmE), / ɪˈlɛktrɪk ˈkʌrənt / (BrE)
A flow of electric charge through a conductor, such as a wire, usually measured in amperes.
flow of electric charge

Examples

The electrician checked the electric current flowing through the wires to ensure safety.
A sudden surge in electric current can damage electronic equipment.
Understanding how electric current works is fundamental to studying physics.

Synset Examples

the current was measured in amperes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: current - flow of something, such as water or electricity

Etymology

From 'electric' (from Greek 'ēlektron', amber) and 'current' (from Latin 'currere', to run).
