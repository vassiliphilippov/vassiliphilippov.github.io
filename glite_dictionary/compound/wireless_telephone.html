wireless telephone

1. NOUN

/ ˈwaɪrləs tɛləˌfoʊn / (AmE), / ˈwaɪələs ˈtɛlɪfəʊn / (BrE)
A telephone that transmits and receives voice signals by radio waves, without the need for a wired connection.
telephone using radio waves, not cables

Examples

The company introduced a new wireless telephone model that allows users to walk freely while talking.
Early wireless telephones relied on analog radio signals for communication over short distances.
She replaced her old landline with a modern wireless telephone to avoid dealing with tangled cords.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cordless phone - a telephone with a portable handset communicating over radio waves to a base unit
  • Degree Higher: mobile phone - a portable telephone that uses a cellular network

2. NOUN

/ ˈwaɪrləs təˈlɛfoʊn / (AmE), / ˈwaɪələs tɪˈlɛfəʊn / (BrE)
a telephone system or device that transmits voice signals using radio waves instead of wires
telephone using radio waves, not wires

Examples

The invention of the wireless telephone revolutionized long-distance communication in the early twentieth century.
She spoke to her friend on a wireless telephone from her backyard, far from any phone jack.
Before mobile phones became popular, some homes had a wireless telephone with a range limited to their property.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cordless phone - a telephone with a wireless handset communicating with a base station connected to a telephone line
  • Degree Higher: cell phone - a portable telephone using cellular network for communication
  • Antonym: landline - a telephone system that uses physical wires for signal transmission

Etymology

From 'wireless' (lacking wires) and 'telephone', following late 19th–early 20th century naming conventions for radio-based technology.
