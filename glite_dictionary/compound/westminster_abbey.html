Westminster Abbey

1. PROPN

/ ˌwɛstmɪnstər ˈæbi / (AmE), / ˌwɛstmɪnstə ˈæbi / (BrE)
a famous Gothic church in Westminster, London, known for hosting British royal coronations and burials of notable figures
historic church in London, site of royal events

Examples

Many kings and queens have been crowned at Westminster Abbey over the centuries.
Tourists from around the world visit Westminster Abbey to see its remarkable architecture and tombs.
Poets' Corner in Westminster Abbey commemorates many of England's most famous writers.

Synset Examples

Westminster Abbey is the scene of the coronations of almost all English monarchs
Distinguished English subjects are buried in Westminster Abbey

Etymology

Named after its location in Westminster and the historical term 'abbey' for a monastery or church run by an abbot or abbess.
