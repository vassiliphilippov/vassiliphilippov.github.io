Roman numeral

1. NOUN

/ ˈroʊ.mən ˈnuː.mə.rəl / (AmE), / ˈrəʊ.mən ˈnjuː.mə.rəl / (BrE)
a symbol or letter from the system used in ancient Rome for writing numbers, in which letters such as I, V, X, L, C, D, and M represent values like 1, 5, 10, 50, 100, 500, and 1000 respectively
symbol for numbers in ancient Roman system

Examples

The clock on the church tower used Roman numerals instead of Arabic numbers.
Super Bowl events are often labeled with Roman numerals, such as Super Bowl XLV.
She struggled to read the chapter numbers because they were written in Roman numerals.

Etymology

From Latin Romanus (Roman) and numeralis (of a number).
