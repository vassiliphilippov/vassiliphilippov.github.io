order Rodentia

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdɚ roʊˈdɛnʃiə / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə rəʊˈdɛnʃiə / (BrE)
the taxonomic group of mammals that comprises the rodents, including rats, mice, squirrels, beavers, and others
taxonomic group comprising rodents

Examples

The order Rodentia includes more species than any other order of mammals.
Scientists have discovered a new species belonging to the order Rodentia in South America.
All members of the order Rodentia share a distinctive pair of continuously growing incisors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rodent - a small gnawing mammal of the order Rodentia

Etymology

From Latin 'rodere' (to gnaw); 'Rodentia' is the order name in scientific taxonomy.
