quiche Lorraine

1. NOUN

/ kiʃ lɔˈreɪn / (AmE), / kiːʃ lɒˈreɪn / (BrE)
A rich, open pie of pastry filled with a savory custard containing cheese and bacon.
savory pie with cheese and bacon

Examples

For lunch, I ordered a slice of quiche Lorraine with a side salad.
The classic quiche Lorraine is made with crispy bacon and melted cheese.
She brought a homemade quiche Lorraine to the picnic, and it was finished in minutes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quiche - a savory open tart with a custard filling

Etymology

French, named after the Lorraine region of France.
