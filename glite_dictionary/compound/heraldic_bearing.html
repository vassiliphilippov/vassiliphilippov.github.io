heraldic bearing

1. NOUN

/ hɛrˈældɪk ˈbɛrɪŋ / (AmE), / həˈrældɪk ˈbeərɪŋ / (BrE)
a design or image depicted on a shield as part of heraldry
design or emblem on a shield

Examples

The family's heraldic bearing featured a golden lion on a red background.
Each knight in the tournament displayed his heraldic bearing proudly on his shield.
Scholars studied the ancient heraldic bearing to trace the lineage of the noble house.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coat of arms - a unique heraldic design on a shield or escutcheon or on a surcoat or tabard used to identify the wearer

Etymology

From 'heraldic' (relating to heraldry) and 'bearing' (something carried or displayed); 'bearing' in this sense means a device or emblem borne on a shield.
