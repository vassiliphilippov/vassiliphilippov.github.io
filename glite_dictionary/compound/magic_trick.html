magic trick

1. NOUN

/ ˈmædʒɪk trɪk / (AmE), / ˈmædʒɪk trɪk / (BrE)
an act in which an illusion of seemingly impossible or supernatural feats is performed, especially for entertainment
illusion or feat performed for entertainment

Examples

The magician amazed the children with his latest magic trick.
No one could figure out how the magic trick was done.
She practiced the magic trick for hours before performing it on stage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: illusion - an instance of a deceptive appearance or impression, often in magic
  • Synonym: conjuring trick - a trick performed by a magician, especially involving sleight of hand

2. NOUN

/ ˈmædʒɪk trɪk / (AmE), / ˈmædʒɪk trɪk / (BrE)
an act or performance that uses illusion or sleight of hand to entertain or deceive an audience
performance of illusion or sleight of hand

Examples

The children were amazed when the magician performed a dazzling magic trick at the party.
He spent hours practicing his new magic trick until it looked perfect.
Most people couldn't figure out how the magic trick was done.

Synset Examples

The magician used a trick to trapan his assistant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: illusion - something that deceives by producing a false or misleading impression
  • Synonym: sleight of hand - manual dexterity used to perform magic tricks
