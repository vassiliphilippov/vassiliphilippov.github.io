bobbery pack

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑːbəri pæk / (AmE), / ˈbɒbəri pæk / (BrE)
a group of noisy and playful children
group of playful, noisy children

Examples

A bobbery pack raced through the playground, laughing and shouting joyfully.
The teacher struggled to keep the bobbery pack under control during recess.
During the party, a bobbery pack invaded the living room with games and giggles.

Synset Examples

The kids were excited to see the bobbery pack during the parade.

Etymology

Possibly from Anglo-Indian English 'bobbery' meaning an uproar or noisy disturbance, from Hindi 'bābari'.
