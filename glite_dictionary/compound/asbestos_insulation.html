asbestos insulation

1. NOUN

/ æsˈbɛstəs ɪnˌsuːˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / æsˈbɛstəs ˌɪnsjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
a type of insulation material made from asbestos, once widely used for its heat-resistant properties but now recognized as hazardous to health
insulation material made from asbestos

Examples

Many older homes still contain asbestos insulation in the walls and ceilings.
Due to health risks, contractors must wear protective gear when removing asbestos insulation.
Regulations now prohibit the use of asbestos insulation in new construction projects.

Synset Examples

The old building was full of asbestos insulation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fiberglass insulation - insulation material made from fine glass fibers

Etymology

From 'asbestos', from Greek 'asbestos' (inextinguishable), and 'insulation', from Latin 'insulatus'.
