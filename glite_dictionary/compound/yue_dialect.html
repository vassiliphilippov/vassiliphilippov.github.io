Yue dialect

1. NOUN

/ juː ˈdaɪ.əˌlɛkt / (AmE), / juː ˈdaɪ.əˌlɛkt / (BrE)
a variety of Chinese, including Cantonese, spoken in Guangdong (Canton) province, Hong Kong, and other areas outside mainland China
Chinese dialect group including Cantonese

Examples

Many Chinese immigrants in North America speak the Yue dialect at home with their families.
The Yue dialect is widely used in Hong Kong media and entertainment.
Linguists have studied the historical development of the Yue dialect in southern China.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cantonese - the most widely spoken variety of the Yue dialect group

Etymology

From Chinese 粤 (Yuè), referring to Guangdong province.
