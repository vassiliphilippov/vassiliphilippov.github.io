Fourth Council of Constantinople

1. PROPN

/ ˈfɔrθ ˈkaʊnsəl əv ˌkɒnstænˈtɪnəpoʊl / (AmE), / ˈfɔːθ ˈkaʊnsəl əv ˌkɒnstænˈtɪnəpəl / (BrE)
an ecumenical council held in 869–870 that condemned Photius as patriarch of Constantinople and contributed to the schism between the eastern and western Christian churches
ecumenical council of 869–870

Examples

The Fourth Council of Constantinople marked a turning point in the history of the Christian church.
Historians often reference the Fourth Council of Constantinople when discussing the roots of the East–West Schism.
At the Fourth Council of Constantinople, the legitimacy of Photius's position as patriarch was debated intensely.
