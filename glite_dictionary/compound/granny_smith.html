Granny Smith

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡræn.i ˈsmɪθ / (AmE), / ˌɡran.i ˈsmɪθ / (BrE)
a variety of apple with green skin and crisp, tart flesh
a tart green variety of apple

Examples

She packed a Granny Smith apple in her lunch for a refreshing, tart snack.
The recipe calls for two Granny Smith apples to add a sharp flavor to the pie.
I prefer Granny Smith apples because they remain crisp in salads.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apple - a round fruit with red, yellow, or green skin and firm white flesh

Etymology

Named after Maria Ann ("Granny") Smith, who first cultivated the variety in Australia in the 19th century.
