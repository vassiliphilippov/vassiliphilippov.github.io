lateral thinking

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæt̬.ɚ.əl ˈθɪŋ.kɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlæt.ər.əl ˈθɪŋ.kɪŋ / (BrE)
a way of solving problems by approaching them from new or different angles rather than using traditional step-by-step logic
solving problems by creative, nontraditional thinking

Examples

The company encourages lateral thinking to develop innovative solutions for its clients.
When faced with the puzzle, her lateral thinking led her to an unexpected answer.
Workshops on lateral thinking help employees break free from conventional problem-solving methods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: creative thinking - thinking that involves imagination and innovation
  • Antonym: vertical thinking - traditional, logical, and sequential problem-solving

Etymology

Popularized by Edward de Bono (British psychologist) in the 1960s; from 'lateral' (sideways) + 'thinking'.
