snail bean

1. NOUN

/ sneɪl biːn / (AmE), / sneɪl biːn / (BrE)
a perennial tropical American vine cultivated for its showy yellow and purple flowers with a coiled keel resembling a snail shell; sometimes placed in the genus Phaseolus
tropical vine with coiled, snail-like flowers

Examples

The snail bean produces vibrant flowers that attract bees and butterflies to the garden.
Gardeners often plant the snail bean for its ornamental value and unique coiled petals.
In some regions, the snail bean is used to create natural fences due to its vigorous growth.
