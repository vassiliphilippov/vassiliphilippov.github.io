air castle

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛr ˌkæsəl / (AmE), / ˈeə ˌkɑːsəl / (BrE)
A fanciful or unrealistic hope or dream; an unattainable or imaginary aspiration.
an unrealistic dream or aspiration
archaic

Examples

As a child, she was always building air castles about becoming a famous singer.
It's good to have goals, but don't waste your time on air castles.
His plans for early retirement were just air castles with no basis in reality.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: daydream - a series of pleasant thoughts that distract one's attention from the present
  • Synonym: pipe dream - an unattainable or fanciful hope or scheme

Etymology

Modeled after the phrase 'castles in the air', dating to the late Middle Ages and referencing impossible ambitions.
