Ming dynasty

1. NOUN

/ mɪŋ ˈdaɪ.nə.sti / (AmE), / mɪŋ ˈdɪn.ə.sti / (BrE)
the imperial dynasty that ruled China from 1368 to 1644, known for its restoration of Han Chinese rule and flourishing of arts and culture
Chinese imperial dynasty (1368–1644)

Examples

The Forbidden City was constructed during the Ming dynasty and remains a symbol of imperial China.
Many pieces of porcelain from the Ming dynasty are highly prized by collectors.
During the Ming dynasty, international trade and the arts both flourished in China.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Qing dynasty - the imperial dynasty that succeeded the Ming in China (1644–1912)
  • Antonym: Yuan dynasty - the Mongol-led imperial dynasty that preceded the Ming in China (1271–1368)

Etymology

From Chinese 明 (Míng, meaning 'bright' or 'brilliant').
