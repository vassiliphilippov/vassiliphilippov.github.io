American oriole

1. NOUN

/ əˈmɛrɪkən ˈɔriˌoʊl / (AmE), / əˈmɛrɪkən ˈɔːrɪəʊl / (BrE)
a North American songbird, typically with black and orange or yellow plumage in the male
North American songbird with bright plumage

Examples

We spotted a beautiful American oriole perched high in the maple tree.
The bright colors of the male American oriole make it easy to recognize in the spring.
Many birdwatchers hope to catch a glimpse of the elusive American oriole during migration season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oriole - Old World and New World songbird with bright plumage
