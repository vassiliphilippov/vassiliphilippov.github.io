smilo grass

1. NOUN

/ ˈsmaɪloʊ ˌɡɹæs / (AmE), / ˈsmaɪləʊ ˌɡrɑːs / (BrE)
a perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region, cultivated as forage and introduced into North America
Mediterranean perennial grass used for forage

Examples

Smilo grass is often planted in dry regions because of its drought-tolerant properties.
Farmers in California use smilo grass as a reliable source of forage for livestock.
The rapid spread of smilo grass in some areas can threaten native plant species.
