cattle guard

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæt̬l̩ ɡɑrd / (AmE), / ˈkætl ɡɑːd / (BrE)
A set of parallel metal bars placed over a ditch or depression in a road or path, allowing vehicles and pedestrians to pass but preventing cattle from crossing.
set of metal bars to block cattle

Examples

The rancher installed a cattle guard at the entrance to keep his herd from wandering onto the highway.
We drove slowly over the cattle guard as we entered the property.
A cattle guard is often used instead of a gate on many rural roads.
