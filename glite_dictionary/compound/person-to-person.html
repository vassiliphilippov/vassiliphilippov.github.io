person-to-person

1. ADV

/ ˌpɝːsən tə pɝːsən / (AmE), / ˌpɜːsən tə pɜːsən / (BrE)
involving direct interaction or communication between two people with no intermediary; face to face.
directly between two people

Examples

The manager wanted to resolve the conflict person-to-person rather than through email.
He prefers to negotiate person-to-person when making important decisions.
They settled their disagreement person-to-person instead of in a group meeting.

Synset Examples

preferred to settle the matter one-on-one
interviewed her person-to-person

Related Terms

  • Synonym: one-on-one - between two people, especially in a direct encounter
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00044989-r
Synset POS: r

2. ADJ

/ ˌpɝːsən tə ˈpɝːsən / (AmE), / ˌpɜːsən tə ˈpɜːsən / (BrE)
involving direct communication or contact between individuals, without intermediaries
direct communication or contact between people

Examples

The bank offers person-to-person payment services that let you send money directly to friends.
A person-to-person meeting can resolve misunderstandings faster than emails or calls.
Many prefer person-to-person counseling for sensitive issues rather than online support.

Synset Examples

a person-to-person interview
person-to-person telephone calls

Related Terms

  • Synonym: face-to-face - involving direct, physical presence or interaction
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01773691-s
Synset POS: s
