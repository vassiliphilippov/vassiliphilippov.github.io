Sierra Nevada

1. PROPN

/ siˌɛrə nəˈvɑdə / (AmE), / siˌɛərə nəˈvɑːdə / (BrE)
a mountain range in southern Spain, extending along the Mediterranean coast east of Granada and renowned for its high peaks, including Mulhacén.
mountain range in southern Spain

Examples

The Sierra Nevada is famous for its stunning alpine scenery and winter sports resorts.
Many rare plant species can be found in the Sierra Nevada region of Spain.
We hiked across the Sierra Nevada during our trip to Andalusia.

Etymology

From Spanish, meaning 'snowy range', from 'sierra' (mountain range) and 'nevada' (snow-covered).

2. PROPN

/ siˌɛrə nəˈvædə / (AmE), / siˌɛərə nɪˈvɑːdə / (BrE)
a mountain range in eastern California, USA, known for its dramatic peaks and scenic beauty, including Mount Whitney.
mountain range in eastern California

Examples

The Sierra Nevada attracts thousands of hikers and climbers each year.
Snowmelt from the Sierra Nevada feeds many of California’s major rivers.
We could see the towering peaks of the Sierra Nevada in the distance.

Etymology

From Spanish 'sierra' (mountain range) and 'nevada' (snowy), meaning 'snowy mountains'.
