Podkamennaya Tunguska River

1. PROPN

/ ˌpɒd.kəˌmɛn.jə təŋˈɡuːs.kə ˈrɪv.ɚ / (AmE), / ˌpɒd.kəˌmɛn.jə tʊŋˈɡuːs.kə ˈrɪv.ə / (BrE)
a river in central Siberia, Russia, that flows northwest to join the Yenisei River
river in central Siberia, Russia

Examples

The Podkamennaya Tunguska River was the site of the famous 1908 meteor explosion.
Researchers sailed up the Podkamennaya Tunguska River to study the remote Siberian landscape.
The Podkamennaya Tunguska River eventually flows into the great Yenisei River.

Etymology

From Russian, meaning 'Stony Tunguska,' referring to its stony riverbed.
