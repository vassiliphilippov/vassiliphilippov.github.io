bay-rum tree

1. NOUN

/ beɪ ˈrʌm tri / (AmE), / beɪ ˈrʌm triː / (BrE)
A tropical West Indian tree (Pimenta racemosa) whose leaves are the source of the aromatic liquid known as bay rum.
West Indian tree used to make bay rum

Examples

The leaves of the bay-rum tree are distilled to make a fragrant aftershave.
Traditional Caribbean gardens often include a bay-rum tree for its aromatic properties.
She recognized the spicy scent coming from the bay-rum tree near the porch.
