white lie

1. NOUN

/ waɪt laɪ / (AmE), / waɪt laɪ / (BrE)
A harmless or trivial lie, often told to avoid hurting someone's feelings or to be polite.
a harmless or trivial lie

Examples

She told a white lie to avoid hurting his feelings about the dinner.
Sometimes a white lie is better than brutal honesty.
He admitted that it was just a white lie and didn't mean to deceive anyone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fib - an unimportant lie, especially about something small

2. NOUN

/ waɪt laɪ / (AmE), / waɪt laɪ / (BrE)
a harmless or unimportant lie, especially one told to avoid hurting someone’s feelings
a harmless or trivial lie

Examples

Telling a white lie to avoid hurting a friend is sometimes considered acceptable.
He claimed he had forgotten the appointment, but it was just a white lie.
She didn’t want to offend her coworker, so she told a white lie about loving the gift.

Synset Examples

He told a white lie to spare her feelings.
Sometimes a white lie is better than the truth.
She made a white lie about being busy to avoid the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fib - a small or harmless lie, often told to avoid hurting someone's feelings

Etymology

The term 'white lie' contrasts the color white (symbolizing innocence) with 'black lie' (a serious or malicious lie); attested since the 18th century.
