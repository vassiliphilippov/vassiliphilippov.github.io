fast pace

1. NOUN

/ fæst peɪs / (AmE), / fɑːst peɪs / (BrE)
a speed or rate that is relatively quick, especially compared to what is normal or expected in a situation
relatively quick speed or rate

Examples

The runners maintained a fast pace throughout the marathon.
The meeting moved at such a fast pace that I could barely keep up with the discussion.
She likes to work in environments where a fast pace is the norm.

Synset Examples

He runs a fastish pace during his morning jogs.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: slow pace - a speed or rate that is relatively slow
  • Degree Higher: rapid pace - a speed or rate even greater than 'fast pace'
