Haitian Creole

1. NOUN

/ ˌheɪʃən ˈkriːoʊl / (AmE), / ˌheɪʃən ˈkriːəʊl / (BrE)
a creole language spoken in Haiti, derived mainly from French and West African languages
the main language spoken in Haiti

Examples

Many people in Haiti speak Haitian Creole as their first language.
She is fluent in both French and Haitian Creole.
The song was performed in Haitian Creole during the festival.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: creole - a stable natural language developed from a mixture of different languages

Etymology

‘Creole’ from Portuguese *crioulo* (person native to a locality); ‘Haitian’ relating to Haiti.

2. NOUN

/ ˌheɪʃən ˈkriːoʊl / (AmE), / ˌheɪʃən ˈkriːəʊl / (BrE)
the creole language spoken by most residents of Haiti, based primarily on French and influenced by various African languages
creole language spoken in Haiti

Examples

She speaks both French and Haitian Creole fluently.
Many children in Haiti grow up with Haitian Creole as their first language.
The document was translated from English into Haitian Creole for wider accessibility.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Creole - a stable natural language that has developed from a mixture of different languages

Etymology

From French créole haïtien, denoting the local language of Haiti.
