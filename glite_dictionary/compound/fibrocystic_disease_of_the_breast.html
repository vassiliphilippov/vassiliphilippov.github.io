fibrocystic disease of the breast

1. NOUN

/ ˌfaɪbroʊˈsɪstɪk dɪˈziz əv ðə brɛst / (AmE), / ˌfʌɪbrə(ʊ)ˈsɪstɪk dɪˈziːz əv ðə brɛst / (BrE)
a benign condition of the breast characterized by the presence of one or more cysts and fibrous tissue, often causing lumpiness or discomfort
benign breast condition with cysts and fibrous tissue
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that fibrocystic disease of the breast is a benign condition that often causes lumpiness.
Women with fibrocystic disease of the breast may experience tenderness before menstruation.
Mammograms can help differentiate fibrocystic disease of the breast from other abnormalities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mastopathy - general term for diseases of the breast, especially noncancerous
  • Antonym: breast cancer - malignant tumor of the breast, a different condition from fibrocystic disease

Etymology

From fibro- (relating to fibers) and cystic (relating to cysts), describing the tissue changes in the breast.
