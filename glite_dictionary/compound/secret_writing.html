secret writing

1. NOUN

/ ˈsiːkrət ˈraɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsiːkrɪt ˈraɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
the practice or process of writing in code or cipher to keep messages hidden from unauthorized readers
writing in code or cipher

Examples

During wartime, spies often relied on secret writing to transmit critical information securely.
The museum displayed examples of secret writing used by diplomats in the 18th century.
Learning the basics of secret writing fascinated the students in the cryptography class.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cryptography - the art of writing or solving codes
  • Synonym: cipher - a coded message or method of secret writing
  • Antonym: plaintext - ordinary writing that is not in code or cipher

2. NOUN

/ ˈsiːkrət ˈraɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsiːkrət ˈraɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
writing that uses codes or ciphers to conceal its meaning
writing in code or cipher

Examples

The spy sent a message in secret writing to avoid detection by enemy forces.
During World War II, secret writing played a key role in military communication.
She studied ancient forms of secret writing and tried to decipher their hidden messages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cipher - a secret or disguised way of writing; a code
  • Synonym: code - a system of words, letters, figures, or symbols used to represent others, especially for secrecy
