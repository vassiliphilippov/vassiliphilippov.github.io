kangaroo's paw

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæŋɡəˌɹuːz ˈpɔ / (AmE), / ˌkaŋɡəˌɹuːz ˈpɔː / (BrE)
A spring-flowering Australian herb of the genus Anigozanthos, having clusters of woolly, tubular flowers often used as ornamentals.
Australian herb with woolly flowers

Examples

The kangaroo's paw adds striking color to Australian native gardens.
After visiting the botanical garden, Mia planted a kangaroo's paw in her backyard.
Many florists use kangaroo's paw in bouquets for its unique shape and vibrant hues.

Etymology

Named for the shape of its flowers, which resemble the paw of a kangaroo.
