to break the record

1. VERB

/ breɪk ðə ˈrɛkərd / (AmE), / breɪk ðə ˈrɛkɔːd / (BrE)
to achieve a better result, time, or score than anyone has previously achieved in a particular activity or sport
achieve a new best result or score

Examples

After years of training, she finally broke the record in the long jump.
The swimmer broke the record by finishing the race in under two minutes.
If he breaks the record tomorrow, he will become a national hero.

Synset Examples

The Spanish athlete broke the record in the 100m sprint.
She broke the world record, no kidding!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: set a record - to achieve something that is better or greater than anything done before
  • Synonym: break one's own record - to surpass your prior best achievement
