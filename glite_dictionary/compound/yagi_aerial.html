Yagi aerial

1. NOUN

/ ˈjɑːɡi ˈɛriəl / (AmE), / ˈjæɡi ˈɪəriəl / (BrE)
A type of sharply directional antenna consisting of a driven element and several parasitic elements, commonly used in television and radio reception.
type of directional antenna

Examples

The technician adjusted the Yagi aerial on the roof to improve the TV signal.
Many ham radio operators rely on a Yagi aerial for long-distance communication.
To receive the broadcast from that station, you'll need a high-gain Yagi aerial.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Yagi antenna - another name for Yagi aerial, a directional antenna

Etymology

Named after Hidetsugu Yagi, a Japanese engineer who co-invented the antenna.
