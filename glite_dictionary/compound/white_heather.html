white heather

1. NOUN

/ waɪt ˈhɛðɚ / (AmE), / waɪt ˈhɛðə / (BrE)
a type of heather found in mountainous regions of the western United States, characterized by its bell-shaped white flowers
mountain plant with white bell-shaped flowers

Examples

In the spring, white heather covers the slopes with delicate blooms.
Hikers came across a rare patch of white heather during their trek.
Some gardeners prize white heather for its unique color and hardiness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heather - a low-growing shrub with small purple, pink, or white flowers
