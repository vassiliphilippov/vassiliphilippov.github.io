serpent fern

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɜrpənt fɜrn / (AmE), / ˈsɜːpənt fɜːn / (BrE)
a tropical American fern with brown scaly rhizomes and large, deeply lobed bluish-green fronds, sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant; sometimes classified in the genus Polypodium
a tropical fern with lobed bluish-green fronds

Examples

The serpent fern is prized for its elegant, deeply lobed fronds in tropical gardens.
After repotting, the serpent fern thrived on the shaded veranda.
You can recognize a serpent fern by its brown, scaly rhizomes and distinct bluish-green leaves.
