high wire

1. NOUN

/ haɪ ˈwaɪɚ / (AmE), / haɪ ˈwaɪə / (BrE)
a tightrope fixed high above the ground, especially for circus performances
tightrope high above the ground

Examples

The acrobat performed daring stunts on the high wire above the cheering crowd.
Spectators held their breath as the artist balanced on the high wire without a safety net.
Training for the high wire requires years of practice and incredible focus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tightrope - a rope or wire stretched tightly for walking across, especially in circus acts
