frat house

1. NOUN

/ fræt haʊs / (AmE), / fræt haʊs / (BrE)
A house used as the residence of a fraternity chapter, especially at a university or college.
house for a college fraternity
american

Examples

He spent his junior year living in the frat house on campus.
The frat house was known for throwing wild parties every weekend.
After the meeting, the brothers gathered at the frat house to discuss upcoming events.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fraternity - a social organization for male students in a college or university

Etymology

Shortened form of 'fraternity house', from 'fraternity' (Latin fraternitas: brotherhood).
