Brer Rabbit

1. PROPN

/ brɛr ˈræbɪt / (AmE), / brɛː ˈræbɪt / (BrE)
a fictional rabbit character who appears in stories told by Uncle Remus, known for outsmarting his enemies through cleverness and trickery
fictional trickster rabbit from Uncle Remus stories

Examples

Brer Rabbit always managed to outwit Brer Fox with his clever tricks.
As children, we loved listening to stories about Brer Rabbit and his adventures.
The tales of Brer Rabbit were passed down through generations in the American South.

Etymology

'Brer' is a dialectal form of 'brother,' from African American Vernacular English; the character dates to Joel Chandler Harris's Uncle Remus tales (1880).
