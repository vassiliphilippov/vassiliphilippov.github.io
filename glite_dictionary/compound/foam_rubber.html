foam rubber

1. NOUN

/ foʊm ˈrʌbɚ / (AmE), / fəʊm ˈrʌbə / (BrE)
A soft, spongy material made from rubber with air bubbles introduced during production, used especially for cushions or upholstery.
spongy cushioning material made from rubber

Examples

The mattress is made of foam rubber for extra comfort and support.
She replaced the old seat cushion with a new piece of foam rubber.
Many running shoes contain foam rubber insoles to absorb shock.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sponge rubber - rubber with a porous, spongy structure; similar to foam rubber
