Tocantins River

1. PROPN

/ toʊ.kænˈtiːnz ˈrɪv.ɚ / (AmE), / təʊ.kænˈtiːnz ˈrɪv.ə / (BrE)
a river in eastern Brazil that flows generally northward and joins the Pará River near the Atlantic Ocean
major river in eastern Brazil

Examples

The Tocantins River is crucial for transportation and hydroelectric power in eastern Brazil.
Many unique species of fish inhabit the waters of the Tocantins River.
Local farmers depend on the Tocantins River for irrigation during the dry season.

Etymology

From Portuguese 'Tocantins', from Tupi language meaning 'toucan’s beak'.
