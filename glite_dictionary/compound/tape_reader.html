tape reader

1. NOUN

/ teɪp ˈriːdər / (AmE), / teɪp ˈriːdə / (BrE)
a device that reads information encoded on punched tape, typically used in data processing or early computing
device for reading punched tape

Examples

The technician repaired the tape reader so the machine could process program instructions again.
Old computer systems often relied on a tape reader to input data efficiently.
He found a vintage tape reader at the museum's computer history exhibit.

Synset Examples

The tape reader used compressed air, which passed through the holes and was directed into certain mechanisms of the caster.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: punched tape - paper tape with holes used to store data
