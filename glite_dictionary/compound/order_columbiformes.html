order Columbiformes

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdɚ kəˌlʌmbəˈfɔrmiːz / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə kəˌlʌmbɪˈfɔːmiːz / (BrE)
a taxonomic order of birds that includes pigeons, doves, extinct dodos and solitaires, and sandgrouse.
order of birds (pigeons, doves, etc.)

Examples

The order Columbiformes comprises species such as pigeons, doves, and the extinct dodo.
Scientists debated the classification of sandgrouse within the order Columbiformes for years.
The order Columbiformes displays a remarkable diversity in both size and habitat among its members.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pigeon - a common bird found worldwide, especially in cities
  • Synonym: dove - a small bird related to the pigeon

Etymology

From Latin columba (dove) + -formes (having the form of).
