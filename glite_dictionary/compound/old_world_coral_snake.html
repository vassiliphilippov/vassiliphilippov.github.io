Old World coral snake

1. NOUN

/ oʊld wɝːld ˈkɔr.əl sneɪk / (AmE), / əʊld wɜːld ˈkɒr.əl sneɪk / (BrE)
Any of several venomous elapid snakes native to Asia, Africa, and Australia, typically with brightly colored bands.
venomous elapid snake of Asia, Africa, Australia

Examples

The Old World coral snake is found in parts of Asia and Africa.
Unlike their American relatives, Old World coral snakes display a range of vivid colors.
Despite their striking appearance, Old World coral snake bites are rare due to their reclusive behavior.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coral snake - venomous New World and Old World snakes with brightly colored bands
