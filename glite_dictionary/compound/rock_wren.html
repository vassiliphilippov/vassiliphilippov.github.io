rock wren

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɑk ˌrɛn / (AmE), / ˈrɒk ˌrɛn / (BrE)
a small, short-tailed songbird of arid regions of western North America, resembling a wren, especially noted for its preference for rocky habitats
small bird of arid, rocky areas in North America

Examples

The rock wren is often seen flitting among the boulders in the desert.
We spotted a rock wren building its nest in a crevice during our hike.
Unlike other birds, the rock wren prefers to sing from exposed rocky outcrops.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wren - small, active songbird with a short bill and tail

2. NOUN

/ rɑk rɛn / (AmE), / rɒk rɛn / (BrE)
a small wren (Salpinctes obsoletus) found in rocky and arid regions of the western United States and Mexico
small wren native to western U.S. and Mexico

Examples

We spotted a rock wren perched on a boulder during our hike.
The rock wren is well adapted to dry, rocky habitats.
Several rock wrens were singing early in the morning near the canyon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wren - small active songbird of the family Troglodytidae
