equiangular triangle

1. NOUN

/ ˌiːkwiˈæŋɡjələr ˈtraɪæŋɡəl / (AmE), / ˌiːkwɪˈæŋɡjʊlə ˈtraɪæŋɡl / (BrE)
A triangle in which all three interior angles are equal, each measuring 60 degrees.
triangle with all angles equal

Examples

In an equiangular triangle, each angle measures exactly 60 degrees.
The logo design used an equiangular triangle to symbolize balance and unity.
Mathematicians often use the equiangular triangle as an example of a regular polygon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: equilateral triangle - triangle with all sides equal

Etymology

From Latin 'aequus' (equal) + 'angulus' (angle).
