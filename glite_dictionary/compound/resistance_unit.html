resistance unit

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈzɪstəns ˈjunɪt / (AmE), / rɪˈzɪstəns ˈjuːnɪt / (BrE)
a unit used to measure electrical resistance, such as the ohm; the reciprocal of a unit of conductance
unit for measuring electrical resistance

Examples

The resistance unit most commonly used in science is the ohm.
You can convert conductance units to resistance units by taking the reciprocal.
Technical manuals often specify electrical values in a standard resistance unit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ohm - the SI unit of electrical resistance, symbol Ω
