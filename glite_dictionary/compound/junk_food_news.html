junk food news

1. NOUN

/ dʒʌŋk fuːd nuːz / (AmE), / dʒʌŋk fuːd njuːz / (BrE)
Sensational or trivial news often focused on unhealthy snacks and aspects of popular culture, considered lacking in substantive or serious content.
trivial news about snacks and pop culture

Examples

Some people prefer junk food news over serious journalism for easy entertainment.
Social media platforms are often flooded with junk food news that distracts from important issues.
Critics argue that junk food news contributes to a less informed society.

Synset Examples

She loves to read junk food news for the latest trends.
The latest junk food news has everyone talking about unhealthy snacks.
