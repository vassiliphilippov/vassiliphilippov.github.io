Ob River

1. PROPN

/ ɑb ˈrɪvər / (AmE), / ɒb ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
a major river in western Siberia, Russia, flowing generally northward and westward to the Gulf of Ob and the Kara Sea
major river in western Siberia, Russia

Examples

The Ob River is one of the longest rivers in Russia, stretching over 3,600 kilometers.
Many cities in western Siberia are situated along the banks of the Ob River.
During spring, the Ob River often floods due to the melting snow in its basin.

Etymology

From Russian Обь (Ob'), name of the river, possibly of Irtysh or local origin.
