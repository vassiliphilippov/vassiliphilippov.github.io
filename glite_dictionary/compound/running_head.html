running head

1. NOUN

/ ˈrʌnɪŋ hɛd / (AmE), / ˈrʌnɪŋ hɛd / (BrE)
A heading printed at the top of each page or every other page of a book or document, usually showing the title or chapter.
heading at top of each page

Examples

The running head on each page makes it easy to identify the document's title.
Many academic papers require a running head that includes an abbreviated title and page number.
She made sure the running head matched the chapter title throughout the book.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: header - a line of text serving as a heading, often at the top of a printed page
