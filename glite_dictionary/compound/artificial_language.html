artificial language

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːr.tɪˈfɪʃ.əl ˈlæŋɡ.wɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌɑː.tɪˈfɪʃ.əl ˈlæŋ.ɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
a language that has been deliberately invented rather than having developed naturally, typically for use in areas such as computer science, logic, or international communication
a deliberately invented language

Examples

Esperanto is an example of an artificial language designed for international communication.
Many computer programs are written using an artificial language known as a programming language.
Linguists study the structure of both natural and artificial languages to understand how communication works.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: natural language - a language that has developed naturally among people
