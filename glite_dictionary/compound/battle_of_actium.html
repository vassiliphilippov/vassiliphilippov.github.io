Battle of Actium

1. PROPN

/ ˈbæt̬əl əv ˈæktiəm / (AmE), / ˈbætəl əv ˈæktɪəm / (BrE)
the decisive naval battle fought in 31 BC in which the forces of Mark Antony and Cleopatra were defeated by Octavian's fleet, marking the end of the Roman Republic and the rise of the Roman Empire.
historic 31 BC naval battle between Antony/Cleopatra and Octavian

Examples

Historians often cite the Battle of Actium as the turning point that led to the Roman Empire.
Octavian’s victory at the Battle of Actium ensured his sole control over Rome.
Many ancient writers described the pivotal events of the Battle of Actium in great detail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Battle of Lepanto - major naval engagement in the Mediterranean (1571)
  • Synonym: Battle of Waterloo - decisive battle that ended Napoleon's rule (1815)

Etymology

From Actium, the ancient Greek name of the battle site in western Greece.
