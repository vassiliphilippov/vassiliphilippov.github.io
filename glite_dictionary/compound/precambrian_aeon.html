Precambrian aeon

1. NOUN

/ priˈkæm.bri.ən ˈiː.ɑn / (AmE), / priˈkæm.bri.ən ˈiː.ən / (BrE)
the period of Earth's history following the Hadean and preceding the Phanerozoic eon, spanning from about 3,800 million to 544 million years ago
geological eon before the Phanerozoic

Examples

Fossils from the Precambrian aeon are rare due to the simple life forms of that time.
Geologists believe the Earth's crust formed during the Precambrian aeon.
Many of the rocks studied in the Canadian Shield date back to the Precambrian aeon.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Phanerozoic eon - the eon following the Precambrian, characterized by abundant animal and plant life

Etymology

From Latin 'prae' (before) + Cambrian (from Cambria, the Latin name for Wales) + aeon (from Greek 'aiōn', age).
