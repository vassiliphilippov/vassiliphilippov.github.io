buzz bomb

1. NOUN

/ bʌz bɑm / (AmE), / bʌz bɒm / (BrE)
a small jet-propelled missile with wings, used especially by Germany in World War II to deliver bombs at a distance
jet-propelled missile used in WWII

Examples

During World War II, Londoners feared the distinctive sound of the buzz bomb overhead.
The museum has a restored buzz bomb on display in its aviation exhibit.
Hundreds of buzz bombs were launched at England, causing widespread destruction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: V-1 flying bomb - German jet-propelled missile used in WWII

Etymology

From the word 'buzz' describing the distinctive sound of its engine, and 'bomb', meaning an explosive weapon.
