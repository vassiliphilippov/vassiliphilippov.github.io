defense attorney

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈfɛns əˌtɝːni / (AmE), / dɪˈfɛns əˈtɜːni / (BrE)
a lawyer who represents the person accused of a crime in a court of law
lawyer representing the accused
legal american

Examples

The defense attorney argued that the evidence was insufficient to convict his client.
Her defense attorney cross-examined the witness thoroughly.
As a defense attorney, he always advocates for the rights of the accused.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: prosecutor - lawyer who represents the government in criminal cases
  • Synonym: defence counsel - British term for a lawyer representing the defendant
