iliac artery

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪliæk ˈɑːr.ti / (AmE), / ˈɪli.ak ˈɑː.tər.i / (BrE)
either of the two large arteries that supply blood to the pelvis and legs, branching from the aorta in the lower abdomen
major artery supplying pelvis and legs
medical

Examples

The surgeon located the iliac artery to restore blood flow to the patient's leg.
An aneurysm in the iliac artery can be life-threatening if not treated promptly.
Blockage of the iliac artery can result in severe pain and poor circulation to the lower limbs.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: femoral artery - major artery continuing from the iliac artery into the thigh

Etymology

From Latin 'iliacus' (pertaining to the ilium, part of the pelvis) and 'arteria' (artery).
