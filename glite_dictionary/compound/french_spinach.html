French spinach

1. NOUN

/ frɛntʃ ˈspɪnɪtʃ / (AmE), / frɛntʃ ˈspɪnɪtʃ / (BrE)
a common Eurasian herb (usually referring to Good King Henry, Chenopodium bonus-henricus) often grown as a vegetable or found as a weed, naturalized in North America
a Eurasian herb, also known as Good King Henry

Examples

Many gardeners grow French spinach for its tender leaves in the spring.
You can use French spinach in recipes just like regular spinach.
French spinach has become established in some gardens across the United States.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Good King Henry - another common name for the same Eurasian herb
  • Degree Lower: spinach - edible leafy green plant, distinct species
