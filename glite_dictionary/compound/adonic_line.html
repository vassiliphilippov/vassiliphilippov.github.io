Adonic line

1. NOUN

/ əˈdɑnɪk laɪn / (AmE), / əˈdɒnɪk laɪn / (BrE)
A line of verse consisting of a dactyl followed by a spondee or a trochee, traditionally associated with laments for Adonis in classical poetry.
verse line: dactyl + spondee/trochee

Examples

The poet chose an Adonic line to evoke a mournful tone in her elegy.
In ancient Greek poetry, the Adonic line was often associated with ritual lamentations.
Scholars debate the exact rhythm of the Adonic line in classical Latin verse.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dactyl - a metrical foot with one stressed and two unstressed syllables

Etymology

From Latin 'Adonicus,' relating to Adonis, a figure from Greek mythology.
