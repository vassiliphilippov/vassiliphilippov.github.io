Faux News

1. NOUN

/ foʊˈnuːz / (AmE), / fəʊˈnjuːz / (BrE)
False or misleading news, especially when perceived as intentionally biased or deceptive.
false or misleading news
informal

Examples

Some social media posts have been flagged for spreading Faux News during the election.
Critics argue that certain websites are major sources of Faux News.
He accused the network of broadcasting Faux News to sway public opinion.

Synset Examples

The website was criticized for spreading Faux News.
Many people are wary of Faux News these days.
He dismissed the claims as just more Faux News.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fake news - false information presented as news

Etymology

From French 'faux' meaning 'false' and English 'news'; also a pun on 'Fox News'.
