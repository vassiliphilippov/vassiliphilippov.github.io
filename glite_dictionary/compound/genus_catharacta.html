genus Catharacta

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs kæθ.əˈræk.tə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs kəˈθær.ək.tə / (BrE)
A former genus of large predatory seabirds known as skuas, now generally included in the genus Stercorarius.
group of predatory seabirds (skuas)
medical

Examples

The genus Catharacta was once considered distinct from other skuas but is now merged into Stercorarius.
Researchers studied the feeding habits of birds classified under genus Catharacta.
Conservationists monitor the populations of seabirds formerly known as genus Catharacta in sub-Antarctic regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skua - a predatory seabird of the family Stercorariidae

Etymology

From Greek katharaktēs (one who attacks violently), used as a genus name in zoological taxonomy.
