inner ear

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪnər ɪr / (AmE), / ˈɪnə ɪə / (BrE)
the innermost part of the ear, consisting of a complex system of cavities and tubes, involved in hearing and balance
the deepest part of the ear; aids hearing and balance
medical

Examples

Infection of the inner ear can result in dizziness and hearing loss.
The delicate structures of the inner ear are essential for maintaining balance.
Doctors studied how sound vibrations are processed in the inner ear.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: middle ear - the air-filled central cavity of the ear, behind the eardrum
