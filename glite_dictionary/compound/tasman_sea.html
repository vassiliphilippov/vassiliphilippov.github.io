Tasman Sea

1. NOUN

/ ˈtæzmən siː / (AmE), / ˈtæzmən siː / (BrE)
an area of the southern Pacific Ocean between southeastern Australia and New Zealand
sea between Australia and New Zealand

Examples

The Tasman Sea can be treacherous during storms, making navigation challenging for ships.
They flew over the Tasman Sea on their way from Sydney to Auckland.
The Tasman Sea is famous for its rich marine biodiversity and unpredictable weather.

Etymology

Named after the Dutch explorer Abel Tasman, who was the first European to sight New Zealand and parts of Australia.
