foot rot

1. NOUN

/ ˈfʊt ˌrɑt / (AmE), / ˈfʊt ˌrɒt / (BrE)
a contagious degenerative infection affecting the feet of hoofed animals, especially cattle and sheep, causing swelling, lameness, and sometimes severe tissue damage
contagious hoof infection in livestock
medical

Examples

The farmer treated several cows suffering from foot rot in the wet pastures.
If foot rot is left untreated, it can lead to severe lameness in sheep.
Regularly cleaning the barn can help prevent foot rot in livestock.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfʊt ˌrɑt / (AmE), / ˈfʊt ˌrɒt / (BrE)
A disease in plants in which the base of the stem or trunk decays or rots, often caused by fungi or bacteria.
disease causing rot at a plant's base

Examples

The vineyard suffered significant losses due to foot rot affecting many young vines.
Gardeners should check for signs of foot rot at the base of their plants after heavy rains.
Preventive treatments can reduce the risk of foot rot in susceptible crops.

Etymology

From 'foot' (base of a stem or trunk) + 'rot' (decay); analogy with livestock disease of the same name.
