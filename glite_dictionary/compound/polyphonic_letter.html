polyphonic letter

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑːliˈfɑːnɪk ˈlɛtər / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈfɒnɪk ˈlɛtə / (BrE)
a letter in an alphabet that has two or more distinct pronunciations, depending on the word or context
letter with multiple pronunciations

Examples

The letter 'c' is a polyphonic letter because it can sound like 'k' in 'cat' or 's' in 'cell'.
Many English learners find polyphonic letters confusing when trying to pronounce new words.
French also has several polyphonic letters that change pronunciation depending on their position in a word.

Synset Examples

‘c’ is a polyphone because it is pronounced like ‘k’ in ‘car’ but like ‘s’ in ‘cell’

Related Terms

  • Synonym: homograph - a word spelled the same as another but having a different meaning and possibly pronunciation

Etymology

From Greek polyphōnos (having many sounds) + letter.
