computational linguistics

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːmpjuˈteɪʃənəl lɪŋˈɡwɪstɪks / (AmE), / ˌkɒmpjʊˈteɪʃənəl lɪŋˈɡwɪstɪks / (BrE)
the scientific study of language using computational methods, including the development of algorithms and models for processing and analyzing linguistic data
the study of language using computers

Examples

Computational linguistics combines computer science and linguistics to analyze and understand human language.
She is pursuing a PhD in computational linguistics to develop better machine translation systems.
Many tech companies hire experts in computational linguistics to improve their virtual assistants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: natural language processing - the field concerned with interactions between computers and human language

Etymology

From 'computational' (relating to computation) and 'linguistics' (the scientific study of language), reflecting the interdisciplinary nature of the field.
