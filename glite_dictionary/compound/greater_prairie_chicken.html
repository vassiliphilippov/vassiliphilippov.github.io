greater prairie chicken

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡreɪtər ˈprɛri ˈtʃɪkən / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪtə ˈpreəri ˈtʃɪkɪn / (BrE)
a large North American grouse, Tympanuchus cupido, native to grasslands, noted for its elaborate courtship displays
large North American grouse species

Examples

The greater prairie chicken is known for its unique booming calls during mating season.
Conservationists are working to protect the habitat of the greater prairie chicken in the Midwest.
We saw a flock of greater prairie chickens displaying their colorful feathers in the grasslands.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: prairie chicken - any of several North American grouse of the genus Tympanuchus
