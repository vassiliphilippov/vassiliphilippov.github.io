doctor–patient relation

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɑktər ˈpeɪʃənt rɪˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈdɒktə ˈpeɪʃənt rɪˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the professional and ethical relationship between a doctor and a patient, in which the doctor seeks to act in the patient's best interests
professional relationship between doctor and patient
medical

Examples

Confidentiality is a vital element of the doctor–patient relation.
Modern healthcare places great emphasis on building trust in the doctor–patient relation.
Laws regarding consent are designed to protect the doctor–patient relation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: physician–patient relationship - the relationship between a physician and their patient, emphasizing care and trust
