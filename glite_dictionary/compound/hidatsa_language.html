Hidatsa language

1. NOUN

/ hɪˈdɑːtsə ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / hɪˈdɑːtsə ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Siouan language traditionally spoken by the Hidatsa people of North Dakota
the traditional language of the Hidatsa people

Examples

The Hidatsa language is considered critically endangered, with only a few native speakers remaining.
She is taking a course to learn the Hidatsa language and help preserve her cultural heritage.
Linguists have documented traditional stories told in the Hidatsa language.

Etymology

Hidatsa, the name of the Native American people who speak the language.
