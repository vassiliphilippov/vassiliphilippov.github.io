radar echo

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪˌdɑr ˈɛkoʊ / (AmE), / ˈreɪˌdɑː ˈɛkəʊ / (BrE)
an electronic signal reflected back to a radar antenna, revealing information about the position and distance of an object
signal reflected by radar from an object

Examples

The storm produced a strong radar echo that appeared on the weather map.
Air traffic controllers detected an unidentified radar echo approaching the airport.
Scientists use the radar echo to determine the distance of asteroids from Earth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: echo - sound or signal reflected back from a surface
