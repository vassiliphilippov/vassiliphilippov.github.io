Tuscarora language

1. NOUN

/ ˌtʌskəˈrɔrə ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌtʌskəˈrɔːrə ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Iroquoian language spoken by the Tuscarora people, primarily in the Northeastern United States and Canada
Iroquoian language of the Tuscarora people

Examples

The Tuscarora language is an important part of the tribe's cultural heritage.
Efforts are being made to revitalize the Tuscarora language among younger generations.
She learned phrases in the Tuscarora language for a school project about indigenous peoples.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Iroquoian - relating to the family of North American indigenous languages including Tuscarora

Etymology

From Tuscarora, the name of the people, originally from North Carolina, combined with 'language'.
