hub-and-spoke

1. NOUN

/ hʌb ənd spoʊk / (AmE), / hʌb ənd spəʊk / (BrE)
a system of transportation, especially in aviation, where local routes connect to a central hub airport from which long-distance flights operate
transport system with central hub and connected spokes

Examples

Many airlines have adopted a hub-and-spoke model to maximize the efficiency of their routes.
The hub-and-spoke system reduces the number of direct flights needed between smaller airports.
Shipping companies often use a hub-and-spoke network to centralize distribution.

Etymology

Formed by analogy to the structure of a wheel, with 'hub' (center) and 'spokes' (connecting rods).
