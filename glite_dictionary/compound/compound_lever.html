compound lever

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑm.paʊnd ˈliː.vɚ / (AmE), / ˈkɒm.paʊnd ˈliː.və / (BrE)
a mechanism consisting of two or more levers connected so that the force applied to one is transmitted and possibly multiplied by the others
mechanism of connected levers

Examples

The engineer designed a compound lever to increase the output force with minimal input.
A pair of scissors is an everyday example of a compound lever in action.
By adjusting the arrangement, the compound lever provided greater mechanical advantage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lever - a rigid bar used to exert a force by pivoting on a fulcrum
