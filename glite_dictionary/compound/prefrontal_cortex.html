prefrontal cortex

1. NOUN

/ priˈfrʌntəl ˈkɔrtɛks / (AmE), / priˈfrʌntəl ˈkɔːtɛks / (BrE)
the front part of the frontal lobe of the brain, involved in complex cognitive behavior and decision making
front part of the brain's frontal lobe
medical

Examples

Damage to the prefrontal cortex can affect decision-making and personality.
Researchers studied the prefrontal cortex to understand its role in memory and attention.
Development of the prefrontal cortex continues into early adulthood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frontal lobe - the part of the brain located at the front of each cerebral hemisphere

Etymology

From Latin 'prae-' (before) + 'frons' (forehead) and 'cortex' (bark, outer layer).
