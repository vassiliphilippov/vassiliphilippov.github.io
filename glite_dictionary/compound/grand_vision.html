grand vision

1. NOUN

/ ɡrænd ˈvɪʒən / (AmE), / ɡrɑːnd ˈvɪʒən / (BrE)
an ambitious and impressive idea or plan for the future, often on a large scale
ambitious, impressive plan or idea

Examples

The CEO outlined his grand vision for the company’s transformation during the annual meeting.
Many revolutionary inventions started with someone’s grand vision of what the world could be.
The architect was known for her grand vision that shaped the city’s skyline.

Synset Examples

The artist’s grand vision turned him into a god that failed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: master plan - a comprehensive or overarching plan
  • Antonym: pipe dream - an unrealistic or unattainable hope or plan
