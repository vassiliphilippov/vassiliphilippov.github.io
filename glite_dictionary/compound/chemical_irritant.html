chemical irritant

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɛmɪkəl ˈɪrɪtənt / (AmE), / ˌkɛmɪkəl ˈɪrɪtənt / (BrE)
a substance that causes irritation to the body, especially the skin, eyes, or respiratory system
substance causing irritation

Examples

Exposure to a chemical irritant can cause redness and swelling of the skin.
Police used a chemical irritant to disperse the crowd during the protest.
The cleaning product contains a chemical irritant that may affect sensitive individuals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: irritant - a substance that causes irritation
