lubber line

1. NOUN

/ ˈlʌbɚ laɪn / (AmE), / ˈlʌbə laɪn / (BrE)
A fixed line marked on a ship's compass, used to indicate the vessel's heading.
fixed steering line on a ship's compass

Examples

When steering, keep the lubber line aligned with your course.
The damaged compass made it hard to see the lubber line during the storm.
He checked the lubber line before adjusting the ship's direction.

Etymology

From 'lubber', an old term for a clumsy person, perhaps referencing straightforward navigation.
