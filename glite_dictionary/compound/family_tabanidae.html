family Tabanidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli tæˈbænɪˌdi / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli tæˈbænɪdi / (BrE)
A family of large biting flies, commonly known as horseflies, that are known for their painful bites and are found worldwide.
family of horseflies (biting flies)

Examples

The family Tabanidae includes many species of horseflies notorious for their painful bites.
Researchers studied the distribution of the family Tabanidae in tropical regions.
Members of the family Tabanidae are important in the transmission of certain animal diseases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horsefly - a large, fast-flying biting fly of the family Tabanidae
  • Synonym: Tabanus - the type genus of family Tabanidae

Etymology

From New Latin 'Tabanidae', based on the genus name 'Tabanus' (type genus of the family), ultimately from Latin 'tabanus' meaning horsefly.
