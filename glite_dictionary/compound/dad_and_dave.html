Dad and Dave

1. PROPN

/ dæd ənd deɪv / (AmE), / dæd ənd deɪv / (BrE)
a popular Australian comedic duo, originally featured in stories, stage plays, and an influential radio show, often representing rural Australian life
Australian comedic duo and radio show
british

Examples

Many Australians fondly remember listening to Dad and Dave on the radio each week.
The humor in Dad and Dave reflects rural Australian values and lifestyle.
A stage play based on Dad and Dave characters attracted large audiences.

Synset Examples

The adventures of Dad and Dave are legendary.
I grew up listening to Dad and Dave on the radio.

Etymology

From the names of the two main characters, 'Dad Rudd' and his son 'Dave', created by Steele Rudd; popularized by the radio show starting in the 1930s in Australia.
