mariposa tulip

1. NOUN

/ ˌmærɪˈpoʊsə ˈtuːlɪp / (AmE), / ˌmærɪˈpəʊsə ˈtjuːlɪp / (BrE)
A plant of the genus Calochortus, native to the southwestern United States and Mexico, with tulip-shaped flowers having three sepals and three petals.
a Calochortus plant with tulip-shaped flowers

Examples

The fields were dotted with blooming mariposa tulip in early spring.
A single mariposa tulip added a splash of color to the rocky hillside.
Researchers studied the pollination patterns of the mariposa tulip in northern Mexico.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Calochortus - a genus of plants including mariposa tulip

Etymology

From Spanish 'mariposa' meaning 'butterfly', referring to the shape of the flower, and English 'tulip'.
