family Asilidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli əˈsɪlɪˌdi / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli əˈsɪlɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of large predatory flies commonly known as robber flies, characterized by their strong bodies and bristly heads.
a family of predatory flies (robber flies)

Examples

Members of the family Asilidae are known for their aggressive hunting behavior towards other insects.
Biologists discovered a new species within the family Asilidae in South America.
The distinctive bristly face of the family Asilidae helps protect their eyes during prey capture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: robber fly - a predatory fly of the family Asilidae

Etymology

From New Latin Asilidae, from Asilus (a genus of flies) + -idae (taxonomic suffix for animal families).
