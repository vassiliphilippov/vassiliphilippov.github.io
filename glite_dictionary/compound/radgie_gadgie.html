radgie gadgie

1. NOUN

/ ˈrædʒi ˈɡædʒi / (AmE), / ˈradʒi ˈɡadʒi / (BrE)
(British, informal) A person who is aggressive or rowdy, especially in the context of Northern English slang.
aggressive or rowdy person (Northern England slang)
informal slang british

Examples

In the pub, everyone could tell Tom was a bit of a radgie gadgie after he started raising his voice.
The teachers warned students to avoid becoming radgie gadgies during the football match.
You don't want to mess with that radgie gadgie down the end of the road.

Synset Examples

He's known as the radgie gadgie of the neighborhood.

Etymology

‘Radgie’ is Tyneside slang from Romani origin meaning ‘rage’ or ‘ragey’; ‘gadgie’ is Northern British slang for ‘man’ or ‘guy’, from Romani ‘gadjo’.
