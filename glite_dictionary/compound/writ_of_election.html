writ of election

1. NOUN

/ rɪt əv ɪˈlɛkʃən / (AmE), / rɪt əv ɪˈlɛkʃən / (BrE)
an official legal document that orders an election to be held, usually to fill a political office or vacancy
official order for an election
legal

Examples

The government issued a writ of election after the parliamentary seat became vacant.
A writ of election must be served before the by-election can be arranged.
The court reviewed the legality of the writ of election issued by the governor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: writ - an official order issued by a court or legal authority

Etymology

From Middle English 'writ', from Old English 'writ' (something written), and Latin 'electio' (choice, election).
