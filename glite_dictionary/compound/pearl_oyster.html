pearl oyster

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɜrl ˌɔɪstɚ / (AmE), / ˈpɜːl ˌɔɪstə / (BrE)
a tropical marine bivalve mollusc, found chiefly off eastern Asia and the Pacific coasts of North and Central America, that produces pearls and is harvested commercially for this purpose
marine mollusc producing pearls

Examples

The pearl oyster is highly valued for the beautiful pearls it can produce.
Divers in the South Pacific collect pearl oysters from the seabed.
Most cultured pearls are grown inside the shell of a pearl oyster.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oyster - a marine bivalve mollusc with a rough irregular shell
