lyrate leaf

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪreɪt lif / (AmE), / ˈlaɪrət liːf / (BrE)
A simple leaf with a form or curvature suggestive of a lyre, often with a large rounded terminal lobe and smaller side lobes.
leaf shaped like a lyre

Examples

The mustard plant is easily recognized by its distinctive lyrate leaf shape.
Botanists often classify specimens based on features like a lyrate leaf or a lobed margin.
A lyrate leaf can be seen in some species of wild radish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pinnatifid leaf - a leaf with lobes cut almost to the midrib but not forming separate leaflets

Etymology

From Latin 'lyra' (lyre), referencing the similarity in shape to the musical instrument.
