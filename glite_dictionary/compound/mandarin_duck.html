mandarin duck

1. NOUN

/ ˈmændərɪn dʌk / (AmE), / ˈmændərɪn dʌk / (BrE)
A small, brightly colored Asiatic duck (Aix galericulata), known for its ornate plumage and often domesticated.
a brightly colored Asiatic duck

Examples

A pair of mandarin ducks glided gracefully across the pond, their vibrant feathers shimmering in the sunlight.
The zoo's new exhibit features the colorful mandarin duck, native to East Asia.
Many people admire the unique appearance of the male mandarin duck during the breeding season.

Etymology

Named after the Mandarin (Chinese) officials, due to the bird's elaborate plumage reminiscent of their robes.
