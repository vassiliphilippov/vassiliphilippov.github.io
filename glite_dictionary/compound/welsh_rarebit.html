Welsh rarebit

1. NOUN

/ wɛlʃ ˈrɛr.bɪt / (AmE), / wɛlʃ ˈrɛː.bɪt / (BrE)
A savory dish of melted cheese mixed with ale or beer, served hot over toasted bread.
cheese-and-beer sauce served on toast

Examples

She made Welsh rarebit for lunch, using sharp cheddar and a splash of ale.
The menu featured traditional British dishes like Welsh rarebit and shepherd's pie.
A steaming plate of Welsh rarebit is perfect for a cozy evening at home.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cheese toast - slice of toast topped with melted cheese

Etymology

Originally 'Welsh rabbit', a jocular use of 'rabbit' to refer to a cheese dish, altered to 'rarebit' by folk etymology.
