government note

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡʌvərnmənt noʊt / (AmE), / ˈɡʌvənmənt nəʊt / (BrE)
A piece of paper money issued by a government's central bank, used as official currency.
official paper currency issued by a government

Examples

The old shopkeeper refused to accept a torn government note.
She collected rare government notes from different countries as a hobby.
Most day-to-day transactions are carried out with government notes and coins.

Synset Examples

he peeled off five one-thousand-zloty notes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: banknote - a piece of paper money issued by a bank
  • Antonym: coin - a piece of metal money used as currency
