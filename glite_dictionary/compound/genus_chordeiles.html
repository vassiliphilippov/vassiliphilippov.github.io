genus Chordeiles

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs kɔːrˈdaɪliːz / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs kɔːˈdaɪliːz / (BrE)
A genus of birds in the family Caprimulgidae, comprising the nighthawks.
bird genus including nighthawks

Examples

Several species in the genus Chordeiles are found throughout North and South America.
The genus Chordeiles is best known for its members' distinctive, booming call at dusk.
Scientists conducted a study on the migratory patterns of birds within the genus Chordeiles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nighthawk - a bird in the genus Chordeiles, known for its nocturnal habits

Etymology

From Greek khordeilēs, a kind of bird mentioned by Aristotle, used as the scientific genus name.
