Bermuda onion

1. NOUN

/ bərˈmjuː.də ˈʌn.jən / (AmE), / bəˈmjuː.də ˈʌn.jən / (BrE)
a variety of large, mild, flat onion with a pale skin, originally grown in Bermuda and now cultivated in warm regions
a mild, flat onion variety

Examples

She topped her salad with thin rings of Bermuda onion for extra flavor.
The recipe calls for half a Bermuda onion, finely chopped.
Bermuda onions are prized for their mild taste and crisp texture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: onion - an edible bulb of the plant Allium cepa

Etymology

Named after Bermuda, where this type of onion was first commercially grown and exported.
