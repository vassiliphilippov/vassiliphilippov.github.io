chromosomal disorder

1. NOUN

/ kroʊˈmoʊsəməl dɪsˈɔrdər / (AmE), / krəˈməʊsəməl dɪsˈɔːdə / (BrE)
a medical condition caused by an abnormal number or structure of chromosomes, often resulting in physical or mental abnormalities
genetic condition with abnormal chromosomes
medical

Examples

Down syndrome is a well-known chromosomal disorder affecting both physical and intellectual development.
Doctors use genetic testing to diagnose chromosomal disorders in newborns and fetuses.
Research on chromosomal disorder treatment has led to significant advances in prenatal care.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: genetic disorder - a disease caused by abnormalities in genes or chromosomes

Etymology

From 'chromosome' (coined from Greek 'chroma' meaning 'color' and 'soma' meaning 'body') and 'disorder.'
