chaulmoogra oil

1. NOUN

/ tʃɔlˈmuɡrə ɔɪl / (AmE), / tʃɔːlˈmuːɡrə ɔɪl / (BrE)
an oil extracted from the seeds of chaulmoogra trees, used in the treatment of skin diseases and leprosy, especially prior to modern drugs
oil from chaulmoogra tree used medicinally
medical

Examples

Traditional healers in India once used chaulmoogra oil to treat a variety of skin disorders.
Before antibiotics, chaulmoogra oil was a primary remedy for leprosy.
Researchers eventually replaced chaulmoogra oil with more effective treatments for leprosy in the twentieth century.

Etymology

From Hindi 'chaulmoogra', the local name for the tree, combined with 'oil'.
