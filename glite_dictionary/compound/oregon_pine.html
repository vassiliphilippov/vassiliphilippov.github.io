Oregon pine

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrɪɡən paɪn / (AmE), / ˈɒrɪɡən paɪn / (BrE)
a large evergreen tree of northwestern North America, also known as Douglas fir, with short needles and egg-shaped cones
large evergreen tree (Douglas fir) of northwest North America

Examples

The Oregon pine is prized for its strong, lightweight timber used in construction.
We hiked through forests filled with Oregon pine towering above us.
Carpenters often source lumber from Oregon pine for making beams and rafters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Douglas fir - large evergreen conifer of western North America
