beta blocker

1. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪ.t̬ə ˌblɑː.kɚ / (AmE), / ˈbiː.tə ˌblɒk.ə / (BrE)
a drug that reduces blood pressure and heart rate by blocking the action of beta-adrenergic receptors in the heart, used especially to treat hypertension and arrhythmia
drug reducing heart rate and blood pressure
medical

Examples

After starting on a beta blocker, his blood pressure dropped to a healthier level.
Doctors often prescribe beta blockers to patients with certain heart conditions.
She experienced fewer migraines after her physician added a beta blocker to her treatment plan.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beta-adrenergic receptor - a receptor in the heart affected by beta blockers
  • Synonym: antihypertensive - a drug used to treat high blood pressure

Etymology

From 'beta' (referring to beta-adrenergic receptors) and 'blocker' (agent that blocks).
