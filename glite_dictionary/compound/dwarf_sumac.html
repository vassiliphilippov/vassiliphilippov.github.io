dwarf sumac

1. NOUN

/ dwɔrf ˈsʌmæk / (AmE), / dwɔːf ˈsʌmæk / (BrE)
a nonpoisonous shrub of eastern North America, with compound leaves, green paniculate flowers, and red berries
nonpoisonous North American shrub with red berries

Examples

The dwarf sumac brightens up the woodland edge with clusters of red berries in late summer.
Researchers studied how dwarf sumac provides food for local wildlife.
I planted a dwarf sumac in my garden because it's safe and attracts birds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sumac - any of various shrubs or small trees of the genus Rhus
