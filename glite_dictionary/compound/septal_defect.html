septal defect

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛptl dɪˈfɛkt / (AmE), / ˈsɛptl dɪˈfɛkt / (BrE)
a congenital abnormality in the wall (septum) separating the left and right sides of the heart
congenital heart abnormality
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that the baby's heart had a septal defect requiring surgery.
Treatment for a septal defect often depends on the size and location of the abnormality.
Many patients with a small septal defect may not experience any symptoms at all.

Etymology

From Latin 'septum' (partition) and Latin 'defectus' (a failing, deficiency).
