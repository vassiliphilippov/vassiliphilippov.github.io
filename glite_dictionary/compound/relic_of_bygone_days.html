relic of bygone days

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛlɪk əv ˈbaɪˌɡɑn deɪz / (AmE), / ˈrɛlɪk əv ˈbaɪɡɒn deɪz / (BrE)
an object remaining from a time long past, often seen as a reminder of that earlier era
object from a past era

Examples

The steam locomotive sitting in the park is a true relic of bygone days.
Some people see handwriting as a mere relic of bygone days in the digital age.
The cobblestone streets are a charming relic of bygone days that attract tourists.

Synset Examples

The old steam shovel was a relic of bygone days.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: relic - an object surviving from an earlier time
  • Synonym: antique - a collectible object from a previous period
  • Antonym: modern object - something recent or contemporary, opposite of relic
