aluminium oxide

1. NOUN

/ əˌluːmɪnəm ˈɑksaɪd / (AmE), / ˌaljʊˈmɪniəm ˈɒksaɪd / (BrE)
A chemical compound, Al2O3, occurring naturally as corundum and used in various industrial processes.
compound found in corundum, industrial uses

Examples

Industrial ceramics often contain aluminium oxide for its durability and resistance to heat.
Sapphire and ruby are coloured varieties of naturally occurring aluminium oxide.
The abrasive used on sandpaper is commonly made from aluminium oxide.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corundum - a hard mineral form of aluminium oxide
  • Synonym: alumina - another term for aluminium oxide

Etymology

From 'aluminium' (from Latin 'alumen') and 'oxide' (from Greek 'oxys' meaning 'sharp, acid').
