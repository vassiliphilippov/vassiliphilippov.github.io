recurrent fever

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈkɜrənt ˈfiːvər / (AmE), / rɪˈkʌrənt ˈfiːvə / (BrE)
a disease marked by repeated episodes of high fever, usually caused by infection transmitted by lice or ticks and accompanied by symptoms such as chills, headache, muscle pain, and nausea
disease with periodic high fevers
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with recurrent fever after exhibiting high fevers and chills every ten days.
Doctors often associate recurrent fever with infections spread by lice or ticks.
Symptoms of recurrent fever typically include headaches, nausea, and periodic high temperatures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: relapsing fever - an infectious disease with recurrent high fevers, usually caused by Borrelia bacteria
