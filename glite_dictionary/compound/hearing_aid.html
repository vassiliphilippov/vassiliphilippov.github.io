hearing aid

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɪrɪŋ eɪd / (AmE), / ˈhɪərɪŋ eɪd / (BrE)
a device, often conical in shape, formerly used to amplify and direct sound to assist people with hearing loss
device to help people hear better
medical

Examples

The antique hearing aid resembled a small trumpet and helped people with hearing loss.
Before the invention of electronic devices, many relied on a hearing aid to communicate better.
She donated her great-grandfather’s old hearing aid to the museum of medical history.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɪrɪŋ eɪd / (AmE), / ˈhɪərɪŋ eɪd / (BrE)
a small electronic device worn in or behind the ear to amplify sound for people with hearing loss
electronic device to help with hearing loss

Examples

After losing some of his hearing, my grandfather started wearing a hearing aid to help him follow conversations.
The new digital hearing aid is much smaller and more comfortable than older models.
Her hearing aid needed new batteries before she could use it again.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amplifier - a device that increases the strength of audio signals
  • Synonym: cochlear implant - a surgically implanted device for hearing
