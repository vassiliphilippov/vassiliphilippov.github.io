old squaw

1. NOUN

/ oʊld skwɑ / (AmE), / əʊld skwɔː / (BrE)
a long-tailed sea duck (Clangula hyemalis) found in the northern parts of North America, now more commonly called the long-tailed duck
long-tailed sea duck of North America
archaic offensive american

Examples

Birdwatchers occasionally spotted an old squaw mingling with local ducks along the bay.
The distinctive tail feathers of the old squaw make it easy to identify during migration.
Although now referred to as the long-tailed duck, old squaw was the traditional name for the species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: long-tailed duck - common name for Clangula hyemalis, a sea duck of northern regions

Etymology

The name refers to the duck's vocalizations and its aged appearance; now largely replaced by 'long-tailed duck' due to cultural sensitivity.
