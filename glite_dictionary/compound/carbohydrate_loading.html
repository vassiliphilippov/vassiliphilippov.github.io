carbohydrate loading

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːr.boʊˌhaɪ.dreɪt ˈloʊ.dɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌkɑː.bəʊˌhaɪ.dreɪt ˈləʊ.dɪŋ / (BrE)
the practice of consuming a diet high in carbohydrates before endurance exercise to increase the body's energy reserves
increasing carbohydrate intake before endurance events
medical

Examples

Many marathon runners practice carbohydrate loading during the week before a race.
Proper carbohydrate loading can improve endurance performance in long-distance cycling events.
Some athletes mistakenly think carbohydrate loading means overeating in general, rather than increasing carb intake.

Synset Examples

carbo loading is used by endurance athletes just before competing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carbo loading - abbreviation of carbohydrate loading
