broad bean

1. NOUN

/ brɔd bin / (AmE), / brɔːd biːn / (BrE)
an upright plant of the Old World, grown for its large, flat, edible seeds and also used as fodder
plant grown for large edible seeds

Examples

We grew broad beans in our garden last spring and enjoyed a delicious harvest.
The broad bean thrives in cooler climates and is a staple in Mediterranean cuisine.
Farmers often use broad beans as fodder for livestock as well as for human consumption.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fava bean - another name for the broad bean plant and its seeds

4. NOUN

/ brɔd bin / (AmE), / brɔːd biːn / (BrE)
a type of bean plant (Vicia faba) cultivated for its edible seeds and sometimes used as animal fodder
type of bean plant, Vicia faba

Examples

The farmer planted broad bean to help improve the soil quality in his fields.
In some regions, broad bean is grown mainly as animal fodder rather than for human consumption.
The livestock thrived on a diet supplemented with broad bean leaves and stems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fava bean - another common name for broad bean

Etymology

'Broad' refers to the large, flat shape of the seeds; 'bean' from Old English 'bēan'.
