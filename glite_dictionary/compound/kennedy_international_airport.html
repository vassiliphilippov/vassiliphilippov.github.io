Kennedy International Airport

1. PROPN

/ ˈkɛnədi ˌɪntərˈnæʃənl ˈɛrˌpɔrt / (AmE), / ˈkɛnədi ˌɪntəˈnæʃnəl ˈeəpɔːt / (BrE)
an international airport in the borough of Queens, New York City, serving as a major gateway to the United States
major airport in New York City

Examples

Flights from Europe often arrive at Kennedy International Airport early in the morning.
The renovation of Kennedy International Airport has improved the passenger experience significantly.
Many travelers prefer Kennedy International Airport for its wide range of international flights.

Etymology

Named after John F. Kennedy, the 35th President of the United States.
