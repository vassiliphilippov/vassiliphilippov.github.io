shadow banking system

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃædoʊ ˈbæŋkɪŋ ˌsɪstəm / (AmE), / ˈʃadəʊ ˈbaŋkɪŋ ˌsɪstəm / (BrE)
A network of financial intermediaries, such as investment funds or hedge funds, that carry out bank-like activities without being subject to typical banking regulation.
non-bank financial intermediaries network

Examples

Many economists argue that the shadow banking system played a significant role in the 2008 financial crisis.
Regulators are debating whether to impose stricter rules on the shadow banking system.
China’s shadow banking system has become an important part of its financial sector.

Synset Examples

Experts are concerned about the stability of the shadow banking system.
The shadow banking system grew rapidly during the financial crisis.
The shadow banking system operates outside regular banking regulations.
