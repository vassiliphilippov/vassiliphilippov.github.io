muckraker journalist

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌkˌreɪkər ˈdʒɜːrnəlɪst / (AmE), / ˈmʌkˌreɪkə ˈdʒɜːnəlɪst / (BrE)
A journalist who investigates and exposes corruption, scandal, or misconduct, often in politics or business.
journalist exposing corruption or scandal

Examples

The magazine hired a muckraker journalist to investigate the allegations of fraud in the organization.
As a muckraker journalist, he exposed political scandals that shocked the nation.
Her groundbreaking series marked her as a leading muckraker journalist of her time.

Synset Examples

She earned a reputation as a muckraker journalist.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: investigative journalist - a journalist who investigates deeply for news stories, especially related to corruption or wrongdoing
  • Antonym: yellow journalist - a journalist who sensationalizes news, often disregarding accuracy

Etymology

muckraker: From 'muckrake' (to search for and expose corruption), popularized in the early 20th century from US President Theodore Roosevelt's usage.
