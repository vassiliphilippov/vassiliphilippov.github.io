common heath

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmən hiθ / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən hiːθ / (BrE)
a small upright shrub native to Australia and Tasmania, noted for its fragrant ivory flowers
Australian shrub with fragrant flowers

Examples

The common heath blooms in early spring across Tasmania’s open woodlands.
Botanists admire the common heath for its clusters of fragrant ivory flowers.
Many gardens in southern Australia feature the common heath for its beauty and resilience.

Australian shrub with colorful flowers

Examples
