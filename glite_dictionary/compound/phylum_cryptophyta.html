phylum Cryptophyta

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪləm ˌkrɪptoʊˈfaɪtə / (AmE), / ˈfʌɪləm ˌkrɪptəˈfʌɪtə / (BrE)
A phylum of primarily single-celled aquatic organisms, formerly in the kingdom Protoctista, now usually classified as cryptophytes.
group of aquatic single-celled organisms (cryptophytes)

Examples

Scientists have discovered several new species in the phylum Cryptophyta inhabiting Arctic lakes.
Members of the phylum Cryptophyta are often studied for their unique pigments.
The phylum Cryptophyta includes organisms that are important to freshwater ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cryptophyte - An organism belonging to the phylum Cryptophyta

Etymology

From Greek 'kryptos' (hidden) and 'phyton' (plant).
