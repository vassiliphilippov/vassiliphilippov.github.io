European larch

1. NOUN

/ jʊˌroʊpiən ˈlɑrtʃ / (AmE), / jʊəˌrəʊpiən ˈlɑːtʃ / (BrE)
a tall deciduous tree of central Europe with slender conical crown, soft needlelike leaves, and cones with hairy scales
deciduous tree native to central Europe

Examples

The European larch is prized for its durable timber and rapid growth.
Botanists studied the needle drop of the European larch during the autumn months.
A row of European larch trees lines the entrance to the estate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: larch - any coniferous tree of the genus Larix with needle-shaped leaves
