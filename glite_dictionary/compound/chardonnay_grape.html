Chardonnay grape

1. NOUN

/ ʃɑr.dəˈneɪ ɡreɪp / (AmE), / ʃɑː.dəˈneɪ ɡreɪp / (BrE)
a variety of white grape widely grown for use in producing Chardonnay wine
white grape variety for Chardonnay wine

Examples

The Chardonnay grape thrives in a variety of climates, producing distinctly different wines.
Many winemakers prefer the Chardonnay grape for its versatility in both oaked and unoaked styles.
Harvesting the Chardonnay grape at different ripeness levels can affect the wine's acidity and flavor profile.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Chardonnay - a type of dry white wine made from the Chardonnay grape

Etymology

From French 'Chardonnay', named after the village of Chardonnay in Burgundy, France.
