family Phyllidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli fɪˈlɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli fɪˈlɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of insects known as leaf insects, notable for their remarkable resemblance to leaves as a form of camouflage.
family of leaf insects

Examples

The family Phyllidae includes some of the most extraordinary masters of camouflage in the insect world.
Scientists study the family Phyllidae to understand the evolution of mimicry.
Many species in the family Phyllidae are found in tropical forests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leaf insect - insect that closely resembles a leaf

Etymology

From Greek 'phyllon' (leaf), referring to their leaflike appearance.
