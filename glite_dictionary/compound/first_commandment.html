first commandment

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɜrst kəˈmændmənt / (AmE), / ˈfɜːst kəˈmɑːndmənt / (BrE)
An essential principle or rule regarded as most important or foundational in a particular context.
most important principle or rule

Examples

Honesty is often considered the first commandment in journalism.
In our company, customer satisfaction is the first commandment.
For the team, safety is the first commandment on any project.

Synset Examples

The first commandment teaches us to treat others kindly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cardinal rule - a fundamental and most important rule

Etymology

From 'First Commandment', referencing the initial commandment in the biblical Ten Commandments, used figuratively.
