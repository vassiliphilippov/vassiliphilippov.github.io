beta ray

1. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪtə reɪ / (AmE), / ˈbiːtə reɪ / (BrE)
Radiation consisting of streams of beta particles emitted during radioactive decay of certain nuclei.
radiation of beta particles

Examples

Exposure to beta rays can be hazardous and requires proper shielding.
The Geiger counter detected the presence of beta rays in the laboratory sample.
During radioactive decay, some isotopes emit beta rays along with other particles.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: gamma ray - Radiation of high-energy photons emitted by radioactive substances
  • Degree Lower: alpha ray - Radiation consisting of alpha particles emitted during radioactive decay

Etymology

From Greek 'beta', the second letter of the Greek alphabet, used in scientific naming, and 'ray', meaning a line or beam.
