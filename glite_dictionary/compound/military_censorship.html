military censorship

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪlɪˌtɛri ˈsɛnsɚˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈmɪlɪtəri ˈsɛnsəʃɪp / (BrE)
the control or suppression of information, communication, or media by military authorities, typically to maintain security during wartime or military operations
censorship by armed forces

Examples

During wartime, military censorship can restrict journalists from reporting sensitive information.
The leaked documents bypassed military censorship and quickly spread online.
Many letters sent home by soldiers were reviewed by military censorship before delivery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: censorship - the practice of suppressing or regulating information or expressions
