Arthurian legend

1. NOUN

/ ɑrˈθʊriən ˈlɛdʒənd / (AmE), / ɑːˈθjʊəriən ˈlɛdʒənd / (BrE)
The body of medieval stories and myths about King Arthur and the knights of his court at Camelot.
the stories and myths of King Arthur

Examples

Many medieval poets contributed to the Arthurian legend through their tales of knights and quests.
The Arthurian legend has inspired countless movies, books, and television series over the years.
Her dissertation focused on gender roles in the Arthurian legend.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: King Arthur - legendary king featured in the Arthurian legend

Etymology

From Arthurian, referring to King Arthur, a legendary British leader; legend from Latin legenda (things to be read).
