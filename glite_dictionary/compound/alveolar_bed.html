alveolar bed

1. NOUN

/ ælˈviː.ələr bɛd / (AmE), / alˈviː.lə bɛd / (BrE)
the region of lung tissue that is densely packed with alveoli, responsible for gas exchange
lung tissue with many alveoli
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that damage to the alveolar bed can seriously affect breathing efficiency.
Fibrosis can cause scarring within the alveolar bed, leading to reduced lung capacity.
Researchers are studying new ways to regenerate the alveolar bed in patients with pulmonary disease.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alveolus - a tiny air sac in the lung where gas exchange occurs

Etymology

From 'alveolar', relating to alveoli (air sacs in the lungs), from Latin 'alveolus' (small cavity), and 'bed', meaning a layer or area.
