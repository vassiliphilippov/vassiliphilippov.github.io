eastern woodrat

1. NOUN

/ ˈiːstərn ˈwʊdræt / (AmE), / ˈiːstən ˈwʊdræt / (BrE)
a large greyish-brown woodrat found in the southeastern United States
rodent of the southeastern US

Examples

The eastern woodrat builds large stick nests that can be seen at the base of trees.
The decline of the eastern woodrat population has raised concerns among conservationists.
Researchers are studying the diet of the eastern woodrat in different habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: woodrat - a rodent of the genus Neotoma, also known as pack rats
