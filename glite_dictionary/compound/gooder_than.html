gooder than

1. X

/ ˈɡʊdər ðæn / (AmE), / ˈɡʊdə ðan / (BrE)
(nonstandard, informal) Used humorously or by children to mean 'better than'. Not accepted in standard English.
nonstandard comparative of 'good' (playful)
informal

Examples

The kids insisted that their cake was gooder than anything the bakery could make.
He joked that his homemade pizza was gooder than any restaurant's.
Some students said the new playground was gooder than the old one.

Synset Examples

Their new restaurant is gooder than all the others in town.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: better than - the standard comparative of 'good'
