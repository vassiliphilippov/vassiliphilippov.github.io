plot armor

1. NOUN

/ plɑt ˈɑrmɚ / (AmE), / plɒt ˈɑːmə / (BrE)
a literary device in fiction where a character is protected from harm or death by the needs of the story, rather than by plausible in-universe reasons
protection of a character in fiction due to story requirements
informal

Examples

The main character's plot armor was so strong that even a building collapse couldn't hurt him.
Many viewers complained that the villain survived only because of plot armor.
Without plot armor, the hero would never have made it past the first film.

Synset Examples

The hero survived the explosion thanks to his plot armor.
Characters with plot armor often escape impossible situations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deus ex machina - an unexpected power or event saving a seemingly hopeless situation, especially as a contrived plot device in a play or novel
