brown noser

1. NOUN

/ ˈbraʊn ˌnoʊzər / (AmE), / ˈbraʊn ˌnəʊzə / (BrE)
an informal term for a person who flatters or ingratiates themselves with people in authority to gain favor or advantage
person who flatters for personal gain
informal

Examples

Everyone in the office knows Mark is a brown noser who agrees with everything the boss says.
She disliked being labeled a brown noser, but she often complimented her manager unnecessarily.
If you're seen as a brown noser, your coworkers might not respect your achievements.

Synset Examples

Being a brown noser won't earn you real respect.
She called him a brown noser for always agreeing with management.
Everyone knows he's a brown noser to the boss.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sycophant - someone who acts obsequiously toward someone important to gain advantage
  • Synonym: flatterer - a person who compliments others insincerely for personal gain
  • Synonym: toady - a person who behaves obsequiously to someone important

Etymology

From the phrase 'brown nose', originally US slang, referring figuratively to excessive flattery, with imagery implying one’s nose is 'browned' from being overly close to someone’s backside.
