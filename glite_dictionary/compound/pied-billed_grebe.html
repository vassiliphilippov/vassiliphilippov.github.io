pied-billed grebe

1. NOUN

/ paɪd bɪld ɡriːb / (AmE), / paɪd bɪld ɡriːb / (BrE)
A small North American water bird with a whitish bill marked by a black band, belonging to the grebe family.
North American water bird with a black-banded bill

Examples

The pied-billed grebe is commonly found in ponds and marshes across North America.
We spotted a pied-billed grebe diving for food near the lake shore.
During migration season, several pied-billed grebes stopped at our local wetlands.

Etymology

From 'pied' (having patches of two or more colors), 'billed' (having a bill), and 'grebe', from French 'grèbe' (a kind of water bird).
