to pack (one's) bags

1. VERB

/ pæk wʌnz bæɡz / (AmE), / pæk wʌnz bɑːɡz / (BrE)
to put belongings into suitcases or bags, typically in preparation for travel or to leave a place
prepare luggage for travel or departure

Examples

She told him to pack his bags and move out after their final argument.
We need to pack our bags tonight for the early morning flight.
The sudden job offer made him pack his bags and relocate within a week.

Synset Examples

I decided to pack my bags for a spontaneous trip.
After the argument, she was ready to pack her bags and leave.
Please pack your bags for the weekend trip.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pack - to put things into a suitcase or container
