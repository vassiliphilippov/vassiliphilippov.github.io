genus Malaclemys

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌmæ.ləˈklɛm.ɪs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌmæ.ləˈklɛm.ɪs / (BrE)
A genus of North American terrapins, notably including the diamondback terrapin, found in brackish coastal waters.
genus of American terrapins

Examples

The genus Malaclemys includes the well-known diamondback terrapin native to the eastern United States.
Researchers have studied the diet and habitat preferences of turtles in the genus Malaclemys.
Conservation efforts often focus on species within the genus Malaclemys due to their declining populations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diamondback terrapin - A species of terrapin belonging to genus Malaclemys

Etymology

From New Latin 'Malaclemys', the genus name given in taxonomy; likely formed from Greek roots 'malakos' (soft) and 'klemmys' (turtle).
