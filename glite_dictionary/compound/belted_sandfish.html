belted sandfish

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛltɪd ˈsændˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈbɛltɪd ˈsændˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
a small marine fish found in warm shallow waters of the western Atlantic, notable for the dark band around its midsection
a western Atlantic marine fish with a dark band

Examples

The belted sandfish is often seen darting around coral reefs in the Bahamas.
Researchers observed a belted sandfish feeding near the sandy ocean floor.
Local fishermen sometimes catch belted sandfish along the Florida coast.
