Generation X

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒɛnɚˈreɪʃən ˈɛks / (AmE), / ˌdʒɛnəˈreɪʃən ˈɛks / (BrE)
the generation of people born roughly between the early 1960s and late 1970s, following the baby boomers
generation born in 1960s–1970s

Examples

Many Generation X members grew up during times of economic uncertainty.
Authors often discuss how Generation X differs from older and younger generations.
Technology adoption among Generation X accelerated rapidly in the 1990s.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: baby boomer - someone born during the baby boom, especially between 1946 and 1964
  • Degree Higher: millennial - member of the generation born between the early 1980s and mid-1990s or early 2000s

Etymology

Popularized in the early 1990s, from 'generation' plus the letter X (to denote an unknown or variable).
