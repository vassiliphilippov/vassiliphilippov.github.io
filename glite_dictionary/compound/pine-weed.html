pine-weed

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪnˌwid / (AmE), / ˈpaɪnˌwiːd / (BrE)
An annual North American plant with wiry stems, minute scalelike leaves, and small yellow flowers.
a wiry-stemmed North American annual weed

Examples

The fields were covered in pine-weed, brightening the landscape with its tiny yellow blooms.
After pulling up the pine-weed, the farmer noticed the soil was much clearer.
Some gardeners find pine-weed difficult to control due to its persistent growth.
