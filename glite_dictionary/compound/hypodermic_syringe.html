hypodermic syringe

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪpəˈdɜrmɪk səˈrɪndʒ / (AmE), / ˌhɑɪpəˈdɜːmɪk səˈrɪndʒ / (BrE)
a syringe fitted with a hollow needle, used for injecting substances into or beneath the skin
a syringe with a hypodermic needle for injections
medical

Examples

The nurse filled the hypodermic syringe with a painkiller before administering the injection.
Doctors must always use a sterile hypodermic syringe for each patient to prevent infections.
He was trained to dispose of a hypodermic syringe safely after use.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: syringe - an instrument for drawing in or expelling liquids by means of a plunger

Etymology

From 'hypodermic', from Greek 'hypo' (under) and 'derma' (skin), and 'syringe', from Greek 'syrinx' (pipe, tube).
