conger eel

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːŋɡər iːl / (AmE), / ˈkɒŋɡə iːl / (BrE)
a large, dark-colored, scaleless marine eel found in temperate and tropical coastal waters; some species are used for food
large marine eel; some edible

Examples

Fishermen caught a massive conger eel off the coast last weekend.
The chef prepared a traditional Japanese dish using fresh conger eel.
While snorkeling, she spotted a conger eel hiding beneath the rocks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eel - a long, snake-like fish in the order Anguilliformes

Etymology

From Latin 'conger' (a kind of eel), from Greek 'gongros'.
