Eighteenth Amendment to the United States Constitution

1. PROPN

/ ˌeɪˈtinθ əˈmɛndmənt tə ðə juˌnaɪtɪd steɪts ˌkɑnstɪˈtuʃən / (AmE), / ˌeɪˈtiːnθ əˈmɛndmənt tuː ðə juːˈnaɪtɪd steɪts ˌkɒnstɪˈtjuːʃən / (BrE)
the amendment to the United States Constitution, adopted in 1920, which prohibited the manufacture, sale, and transport of alcoholic beverages; it was later repealed in 1933 by the Twenty-First Amendment
U.S. constitutional amendment banning alcohol (1920–1933)
legal american

Examples

The Eighteenth Amendment to the United States Constitution ushered in the era of Prohibition across the country.
Many people opposed the Eighteenth Amendment to the United States Constitution, leading to widespread illegal alcohol trade.
The passage and eventual repeal of the Eighteenth Amendment to the United States Constitution had lasting effects on American society.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Prohibition - the period during which the Eighteenth Amendment was in effect, banning alcohol
  • Antonym: Twenty-First Amendment to the United States Constitution - the amendment that repealed the Eighteenth Amendment
