Yorkshire pudding

1. NOUN

/ ˈjɔrkʃər ˈpʊdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈjɔːkʃə ˈpʊdɪŋ / (BrE)
A light, puffy baked batter dish, traditionally cooked in a pan with roast beef and served as part of a British meal.
puffy baked batter dish, British cuisine
british

Examples

Every Sunday, my grandmother makes Yorkshire pudding to go with our roast beef.
The Yorkshire pudding was golden and crisp, soaking up the gravy perfectly.
I've never tried homemade Yorkshire pudding until I visited England last year.

Etymology

Named after Yorkshire, a county in northern England, where the dish originated.
