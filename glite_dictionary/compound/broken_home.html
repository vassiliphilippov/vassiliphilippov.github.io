broken home

1. NOUN

/ ˈbroʊ.kən hoʊm / (AmE), / ˈbrəʊ.kən həʊm / (BrE)
a family in which the parents have separated or divorced, often leading to an unstable home environment for children
family with separated or divorced parents

Examples

Growing up in a broken home affected her outlook on relationships.
Teachers are often concerned about students from broken homes.
He tried to avoid repeating the mistakes of his broken home.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: intact family - a family where both parents are present and married

2. NOUN

/ ˈbroʊkən hoʊm / (AmE), / ˈbrəʊkən həʊm / (BrE)
a family in which the parents are divorced or separated, often resulting in hardship for the children
family with divorced or separated parents

Examples

Support groups can be helpful for teenagers adjusting to life after a broken home.
