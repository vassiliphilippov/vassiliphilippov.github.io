mathematical relation

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæθəməˈtɪkəl rɪˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌmæθəˈmatɪkəl rɪˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
a relationship between mathematical expressions, such as equality or inequality, indicating how two or more quantities or values are connected
relationship between mathematical expressions

Examples

An equation is a type of mathematical relation that shows two expressions are equal.
The concept of a mathematical relation is fundamental in set theory and algebra.
Inequalities are examples of mathematical relations that describe order or difference between values.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mathematical equation - a statement that two expressions are equal
  • Synonym: inequality - a mathematical relation showing one value is not equal to another
