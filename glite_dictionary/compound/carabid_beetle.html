carabid beetle

1. NOUN

/ ˈkær.ə.bɪd ˈbiː.təl / (AmE), / ˈkær.ə.bɪd ˈbiː.tl̩ / (BrE)
A predatory ground beetle typically black or metallic in color, known for destroying many harmful insects.
predatory ground beetle

Examples

The scientist collected several carabid beetles from beneath logs in the forest.
Farmers value carabid beetles for their role in controlling agricultural pests.
She observed a shiny black carabid beetle scurrying across the garden path.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ground beetle - a beetle of the family Carabidae, commonly found on the ground

Etymology

From New Latin Carabidae, the beetle family name, based on Greek 'karábos' (a kind of beetle).
