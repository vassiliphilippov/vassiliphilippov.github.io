middle ear

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪd.əl ɪr / (AmE), / ˈmɪd.l̩ ɪə / (BrE)
the air-filled cavity in the ear that lies between the eardrum and the inner ear, containing the ossicles that transmit sound vibrations.
cavity between eardrum and inner ear
medical

Examples

Infection of the middle ear often causes ear pain in children.
The three tiny bones in the middle ear amplify sound vibrations.
A ruptured eardrum can expose the middle ear to bacteria and lead to hearing loss.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: inner ear - the innermost part of the ear involved in hearing and balance
  • Degree Lower: outer ear - the external part of the ear, including the pinna and ear canal
