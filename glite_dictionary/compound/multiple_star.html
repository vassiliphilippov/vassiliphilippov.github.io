multiple star

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌltəpəl stɑr / (AmE), / ˈmʌltɪpl stɑː / (BrE)
a system of three or more stars gravitationally associated and orbiting a common center
system of three or more stars

Examples

Astronomers have discovered a new multiple star system in our galaxy.
The stability of orbits in a multiple star system can be quite complex.
A multiple star can often be mistaken for a single point of light when viewed from Earth.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: binary star - a system of two stars orbiting each other
  • Synonym: star system - a group of stars that are bound together by gravity
