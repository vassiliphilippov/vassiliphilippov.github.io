Tasmanian devil

1. NOUN

/ tæzˈmeɪniən ˈdɛvəl / (AmE), / tazˈmeɪniən ˈdɛvəl / (BrE)
a small, fierce carnivorous marsupial native to Tasmania, with a mostly black coat and a long tail
small carnivorous marsupial from Tasmania

Examples

The Tasmanian devil is known for its loud screeches and aggressive feeding behavior.
Scientists are studying the decline of the Tasmanian devil population due to a contagious cancer.
The Tasmanian devil is found only on the island of Tasmania, south of mainland Australia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marsupial - a mammal of an order whose members are born incompletely developed and are typically carried and suckled in a pouch

Etymology

Named after Tasmania, the island it inhabits, and 'devil' for its fierce nature.
