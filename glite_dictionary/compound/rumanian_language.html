Rumanian language

1. NOUN

/ ruˈmeɪniən ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ruːˈmeɪniən ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Romance language spoken in Romania and Moldova, an eastern Romance language that is the official language of Romania
the language spoken in Romania

Examples

Many students in Bucharest study English as well as their native Rumanian language.
The Rumanian language has Latin roots because it developed from the Roman Empire's expansion.
Her grandmother still prefers to read books written in the Rumanian language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Romanian language - the main contemporary variant name for the same language

Etymology

From Romanian 'română' (the language name), with the spelling 'Rumanian' being an older English variant of 'Romanian'.
