Eastern Orthodox

1. ADJ

/ ˌiːstɚn ˈɔːrθədɑks / (AmE), / ˌiːstən ˈɔːθədɒks / (BrE)
Relating to the branch of Christianity consisting of churches that follow the Byzantine rite and traditions, and originated from the Byzantine Church.
of the Byzantine-influenced branch of Christianity

Examples

She was raised in an Eastern Orthodox family and attends church every Sunday.
Many Eastern Orthodox traditions are rooted in ancient Byzantine practices.
The Eastern Orthodox calendar celebrates Easter at a different time than the Western churches.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Eastern Orthodox Church - The church consisting of Eastern Orthodox Christians
  • Antonym: Roman Catholic - Branch of Christianity under the authority of the Pope

Etymology

Eastern refers to the geographic origin in Eastern Europe and the Middle East; Orthodox from Greek *orthodoxos* meaning 'having the correct belief'.
