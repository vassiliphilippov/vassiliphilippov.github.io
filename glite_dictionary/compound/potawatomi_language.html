Potawatomi language

1. NOUN

/ ˌpoʊtəˈwɑːtəmi ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌpɒtəˈwɒtəmi ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Algonquian language traditionally spoken by the Potawatomi people, indigenous to the Great Lakes region of North America
Algonquian language of the Potawatomi people

Examples

Few people today are fluent speakers of the Potawatomi language.
A new dictionary project aims to preserve the Potawatomi language for younger generations.
Many traditional stories have been recorded in the Potawatomi language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ojibwe language - an Algonquian language closely related to Potawatomi

Etymology

'Potawatomi' from the Potawatomi autonym 'Bodéwadmi,' meaning 'keepers of the fire.'
