northern sea robin

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɔrðərn ˈsi ˈrɑbɪn / (AmE), / ˈnɔːðən ˈsiː ˈrɒbɪn / (BrE)
a large species of sea robin (Prionotus carolinus) found in the western Atlantic Ocean from Nova Scotia to Florida
a large Atlantic sea robin fish species

Examples

Several anglers in New England reported catching a northern sea robin during their fishing trips last summer.
The northern sea robin can often be seen gliding above sandy bottoms near the coast.
A northern sea robin uses its large pectoral fins to stir up the ocean floor while searching for food.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sea robin - a common name for fish of the Triglidae family known for their winglike pectoral fins
