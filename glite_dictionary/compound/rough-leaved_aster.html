rough-leaved aster

1. NOUN

/ ˈrʌf liːvd ˈæstər / (AmE), / ˈrʌf liːvd ˈastə / (BrE)
A North American perennial herb of the daisy family (Eurybia radulina), characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and clusters of purple or lavender flowers.
a North American plant with rough leaves and purple flowers

Examples

The rough-leaved aster blooms in late summer along forest edges in western North America.
Gardeners appreciate the rough-leaved aster for its hardiness and attractive flowers.
The hairy leaves help the rough-leaved aster retain moisture in dry habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aster - a type of flowering plant in the daisy family
