prussic acid

1. NOUN

/ ˈprʌsɪk ˌæsɪd / (AmE), / ˈprʌsɪk ˌasɪd / (BrE)
a solution of hydrogen cyanide in water, used in fumigation and in organic synthesis; highly poisonous
hydrogen cyanide solution (poisonous compound)

Examples

The laboratory stored prussic acid in tightly sealed containers due to its toxicity.
Exposure to even small amounts of prussic acid can be fatal if proper precautions are not taken.
Fumigators sometimes use dilute solutions of prussic acid to eradicate pests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hydrogen cyanide - a highly poisonous, volatile liquid or gas with chemical formula HCN

Etymology

From French 'acide prussique', named after its initial isolation from Prussian blue pigment.
