common soldier

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːmən ˈsoʊldʒɚ / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən ˈsəʊldʒə / (BrE)
an enlisted member of the military of the lowest rank, especially in the Army or Marines
low-ranking enlisted military member

Examples

The common soldier often faces the greatest risks on the battlefield.
Many veterans began their careers as a common soldier before advancing in rank.
The general praised the bravery of every common soldier under his command.

Synset Examples

our prisoner was just a private and knew nothing of value

Related Terms

  • Synonym: private - lowest rank of an enlisted person in the Army or Marines
