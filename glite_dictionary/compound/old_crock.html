old crock

1. NOUN

/ oʊld krɑk / (AmE), / əʊld krɒk / (BrE)
an old or worn-out object, especially a vehicle or machine, that is considered of little value
worn-out object or vehicle

Examples

My grandfather still drives his old crock to the market every morning, despite its age.
They finally decided to get rid of the old crock in the backyard.
The repairman laughed and called my bike an old crock when he saw it.

Synset Examples

That old crock in the garage is a family heirloom.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jalopy - an old, dilapidated car
  • Synonym: wreck - something badly damaged, especially a vehicle
