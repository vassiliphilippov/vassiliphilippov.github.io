depreciation charge

1. NOUN

/ dɪˌpriʃiˈeɪʃən tʃɑrdʒ / (AmE), / dɪˌpriːʃiˈeɪʃən tʃɑːdʒ / (BrE)
an amount of money periodically recorded as an expense to represent the decrease in value of property or equipment over time
amount expensed for asset depreciation

Examples

The company recorded a depreciation charge of $5,000 for its equipment this year.
Every year, a depreciation charge is included in the financial statements to reflect the machinery's wear and tear.
Ignoring the depreciation charge could significantly overstate the firm's net income.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: depreciation - a reduction in the value of an asset over time
