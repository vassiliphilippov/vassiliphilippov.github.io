wire grass

1. NOUN

/ ˈwaɪɚ ɡræs / (AmE), / ˈwaɪə ɡrɑːs / (BrE)
A hardy North American grass with stiff, slender stems and foliage that turns pale bronze in autumn.
North American hardy grass with bronze autumn foliage

Examples

Patches of wire grass spread across the meadow, their bronze color glowing in the autumn sun.
Many landscapers choose wire grass for its resilience and unique seasonal appearance.
The nature trail runs through areas thick with wire grass and wildflowers.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwaɪr ˌɡræs / (AmE), / ˈwaɪə ˌɡrɑːs / (BrE)
a coarse annual grass with fingerlike spikes of flowers, native to the Old World tropics and naturalized as a weed elsewhere
a coarse tropical grass with fingerlike flower spikes

Examples

The field was dominated by tough clumps of wire grass.
Many farmers struggle to control the spread of wire grass in their pastures.
The sharp edges of wire grass can irritate the skin when touched.
