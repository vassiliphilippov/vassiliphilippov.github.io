genus Hyla

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˈhaɪlə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈhɪlə / (BrE)
A genus of tree frogs in the family Hylidae, commonly called tree toads, found in various parts of the world.
a genus of tree frogs (family Hylidae)

Examples

The genus Hyla includes many species known for their distinctive calls and bright colors.
Researchers observed that members of the genus Hyla are highly adapted to arboreal life.
Some species within the genus Hyla are found in both temperate and tropical regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tree frog - any frog of the family Hylidae that lives in trees

Etymology

From New Latin, from Greek hūlē (wood or forest), referring to the arboreal habitat.
