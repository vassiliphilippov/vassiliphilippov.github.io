apache devil dance

1. NOUN

/ əˈpætʃi ˈdɛvəl dæns / (AmE), / əˈpatʃi ˈdɛvəl dɑːns / (BrE)
A ceremonial dance performed by the Apache people, usually as part of a ritual or religious ceremony.
Apache ceremonial dance

Examples

The apache devil dance was performed during the festival to honor ancestral spirits.
Anthropologists have studied the apache devil dance to understand its cultural significance among the Apache.
Spectators watched in awe as members reenacted the traditional apache devil dance at the heritage event.
