World War 1

1. PROPN

/ wɜrld wɔr wʌn / (AmE), / wɜːld wɔː wʌn / (BrE)
the global war fought mainly in Europe from 1914 to 1918, involving the Allies and the Central Powers
the First World War, 1914–1918

Examples

Many historians believe that the causes of World War 1 can be traced back to treaties made decades earlier.
Both of my great-grandfathers fought in World War 1.
The assassination of Archduke Franz Ferdinand was the spark that ignited World War 1.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: World War 2 - the global war fought from 1939 to 1945 between the Allies and the Axis powers

Etymology

Named in retrospect after the occurrence of the Second World War.
