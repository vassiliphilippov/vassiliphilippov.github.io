Maryland golden aster

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛrələnd ˈɡoʊldən ˈæstər / (AmE), / ˈmærɪlənd ˈɡəʊldən ˈæstə / (BrE)
A perennial wildflower native to the southeastern United States, known for its yellow, daisy-like flowers.
perennial wildflower of southeastern U.S.

Examples

In late summer, the Maryland golden aster brightens the fields with its vibrant yellow blooms.
Conservationists are working to protect the habitats of the rare Maryland golden aster.
We spotted a patch of Maryland golden aster along the roadside during our hike.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: golden aster - a plant of the genus Chrysopsis, with yellow flowers
