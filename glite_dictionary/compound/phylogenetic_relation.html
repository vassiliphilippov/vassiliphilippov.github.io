phylogenetic relation

1. NOUN

/ ˌfaɪloʊdʒəˈnɛtɪk rɪˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌfʌɪlədʒənˈɛtɪk rɪˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
(biology) the evolutionary relationship between organisms or groups that results in similarities in structure or structural parts.
evolutionary relationship between organisms
medical

Examples

The phylogenetic relation between birds and dinosaurs has been well established by fossil evidence.
Genetic studies are often used to determine the phylogenetic relation among closely related species.
Understanding the phylogenetic relation can help scientists track the evolutionary history of a group.

Synset Examples

in anatomical structure prehistoric man shows close affinity with modern humans

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phylogeny - the evolutionary development and history of a species or group

Etymology

From Greek 'phylon' (tribe, race) + 'genetikos' (origin, birth).
