scarlet bugler

1. NOUN

/ ˈskɑr.lət ˈbjuː.ɡlər / (AmE), / ˈskɑː.lɪt ˈbjuː.ɡlə / (BrE)
a plant with bright red tubular flowers in narrow clusters at the tips of upright stems, native to coastal ranges from central California southward
plant with bright red tubular flowers, native to California

Examples

The scarlet bugler is commonly found growing along the sunny slopes of central California.
Gardeners prize the scarlet bugler for its vibrant display of red flowers each spring.
Hummingbirds are attracted to the scarlet bugler because of its tubular blossoms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: penstemon - the genus of flowering plants to which scarlet bugler belongs
