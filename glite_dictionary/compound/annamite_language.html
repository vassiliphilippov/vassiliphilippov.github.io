Annamite language

1. NOUN

/ ˈænəmaɪt ˈlæŋgwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈænəmaɪt ˈlaŋgwɪdʒ / (BrE)
an earlier term for the Vietnamese language, spoken chiefly in Vietnam and belonging to the Mon-Khmer branch of the Austroasiatic language family
historical name for the Vietnamese language

Examples

French colonial records often referred to Vietnamese as the Annamite language.
Linguists classify the Annamite language within the Mon-Khmer family.
The use of 'Annamite language' has declined, with 'Vietnamese' now being the common term.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Vietnamese - the official language of Vietnam

Etymology

From Annam, a former name for central Vietnam, plus 'language'.
