contract of hazard

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑn.trækt əv ˈhæz.ərd / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.trækt əv ˈhæz.əd / (BrE)
a sale of a tract of land as a whole, without any warranty as to the actual acreage included
sale of land with no acreage warranty
legal

Examples

The buyer signed a contract of hazard, accepting the land without a guaranteed acreage.
In a contract of hazard, disputes over exact size are usually not entertained.
The lawyer explained that a contract of hazard carries risk for both the buyer and seller.
