exponential equation

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˌspoʊnɛnʃəl ɪˈkweɪʒən / (AmE), / ɪkˌspəʊnɛnʃəl ɪˈkweɪʒən / (BrE)
an equation in which the variable appears as an exponent or in an exponential function
mathematical equation involving exponents

Examples

To solve the exponential equation, you must isolate the exponent first.
Many growth and decay problems in science can be modeled with an exponential equation.
He found the final answer by substituting values into the exponential equation.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: linear equation - an equation whose highest power of the variable is one
  • Degree Lower: logarithmic equation - an equation involving logarithms of the variable

Etymology

From 'exponential', relating to an exponent, and 'equation', from Latin 'aequatio' (making equal).
