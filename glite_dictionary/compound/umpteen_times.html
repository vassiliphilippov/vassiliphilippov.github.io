umpteen times

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌmpˌtin taɪmz / (AmE), / ˈʌmpˌtiːn taɪmz / (BrE)
a large but indefinite number of times; very many times
many times (indefinitely)
informal

Examples

I've tried calling him umpteen times, but he never picks up.
She has explained the process umpteen times, yet people still get confused.
He watched his favorite movie umpteen times as a child.

Synset Examples

I've told you umpteen times to clean your room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: countless times - so many times that they cannot be counted
  • Antonym: once - one single time

Etymology

From 'umpteen' (early 20th century), modeled on numerals like 'thirteen' and originally US military slang.
