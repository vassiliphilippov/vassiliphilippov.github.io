Susian language

1. NOUN

/ ˈsuːziən ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈsuːziən ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
an extinct ancient language of unknown origin, once spoken by the Elamites in the region of ancient Elam (now southwestern Iran)
extinct language of the ancient Elamites

Examples

Archaeologists have discovered inscriptions written in the Susian language at the ancient site of Susa.
Very little is known about the grammar and vocabulary of the Susian language.
The Susian language is important for understanding the culture of the Elamites.

Etymology

From Susa, the name of the ancient city at the center of Elamite civilization.
