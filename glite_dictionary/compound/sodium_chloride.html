sodium chloride

1. NOUN

/ ˌsoʊdiəm ˈklɔr.aɪd / (AmE), / ˌsəʊdiəm ˈklɔː.raɪd / (BrE)
a white crystalline compound (NaCl) commonly known as table salt, used in food and industry
common salt, chemical compound NaCl

Examples

Doctors may recommend reducing your sodium chloride intake to lower blood pressure.
Many household cleaning products contain sodium chloride as an ingredient.
The chemist explained how sodium chloride forms crystals when water evaporates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: salt - a substance used for seasoning and preserving food, especially sodium chloride

Etymology

From sodium (a metallic element) and chloride (from chlorine).
