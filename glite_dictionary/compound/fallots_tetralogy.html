Fallot's tetralogy

1. NOUN

/ fəˈloʊz tɛˈtræləʤi / (AmE), / fəˈləʊz tɪˈtrælədʒi / (BrE)
A congenital heart defect that causes cyanosis, consisting of four distinct abnormalities of the heart's structure.
congenital heart defect with four abnormalities
medical

Examples

The cardiologist diagnosed the infant with Fallot's tetralogy after noticing symptoms of cyanosis.
Surgery is often required to correct Fallot's tetralogy in young children.
Advances in medical imaging have improved the detection of Fallot's tetralogy before birth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tetralogy of Fallot - alternate name for the same heart defect

Etymology

Named after French physician Étienne-Louis Fallot, who first described the condition in 1888.
