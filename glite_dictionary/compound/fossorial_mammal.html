fossorial mammal

1. NOUN

/ fəˈsɔriəl ˈmæməl / (AmE), / fəˈsɔːriəl ˈmaməl / (BrE)
A mammal that lives primarily underground and has limbs or body features adapted for digging.
mammal adapted for digging/burrowing

Examples

The mole is a classic example of a fossorial mammal found in many temperate regions.
Some rodents, such as pocket gophers, are highly specialized fossorial mammals with strong forelimbs for digging.
Researchers are studying the skeletal adaptations of fossorial mammals to understand their burrowing behavior.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burrowing mammal - a mammal that lives in burrows or underground

Etymology

From Latin 'fossorius' (of digging) + 'mammal'.
