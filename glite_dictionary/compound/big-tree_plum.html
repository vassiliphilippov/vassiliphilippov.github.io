big-tree plum

1. NOUN

/ bɪɡ tri plʌm / (AmE), / bɪɡ triː plʌm / (BrE)
a small tree native to the southwestern United States, known for its large leaves and purplish-red fruit, sometimes grown as an ornamental plant
small southwestern US tree with large leaves and red fruit

Examples

The big-tree plum is often planted for its attractive foliage and shade in gardens.
Birds are attracted to the purplish-red fruit of the big-tree plum in late summer.
Many nurseries offer big-tree plum saplings for home landscaping projects.
