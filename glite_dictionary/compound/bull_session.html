bull session

1. NOUN

/ bʊl ˈsɛʃən / (AmE), / bʊl ˈsɛʃən / (BrE)
an informal conversation, especially among a group of people, usually men, often about unimportant topics
informal group discussion
informal american

Examples

We spent hours in a bull session talking about life after graduation.
The team's late-night bull session turned into a serious planning meeting.
Every Friday, the friends would gather for a bull session at the local diner.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chat - an informal conversation
  • Synonym: discussion - the act of talking about something with others

Etymology

Originated in American English in the early 20th century, from 'bull' (nonsense/talk) and 'session' (meeting).

2. NOUN

/ bʊl ˈsɛʃən / (AmE), / bʊl ˈsɛʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
an informal discussion or conversation, often focused on exchanging ideas or brainstorming without a formal agenda
informal discussion or brainstorming
informal american

Examples

After dinner, we sat around the table for a lively bull session about our weekend plans.
The manager called a quick bull session to gather everyone's input before making a decision.
Late at night, college students often have a spontaneous bull session in the dorm lounge.

Synset Examples

The team enjoyed a casual bull session over lunch.
Let's schedule a bull session to brainstorm ideas.
We had a productive bull session about the project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brainstorming - a group technique for generating ideas and solutions
  • Degree Higher: meeting - an assembly of people, especially for a formal discussion
  • Synonym: conversation - an informal exchange of ideas by spoken words

Etymology

American English, early 20th century; 'bull' as slang for trivial or idle talk.
