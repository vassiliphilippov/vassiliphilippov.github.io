Easter Day

1. NOUN

/ ˈiːstər deɪ / (AmE), / ˈiːstə deɪ / (BrE)
The day, usually in March or April, on which the Christian festival of Easter is celebrated.
the Christian festival day of Easter

Examples

Many families attend church services on Easter Day to celebrate the resurrection of Christ.
On Easter Day, children often hunt for painted eggs hidden in the garden.
He always prepares a special meal for friends and relatives on Easter Day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Easter - the Christian festival celebrating the resurrection of Jesus

Etymology

From Old English ēastre (name of a festival) + day.
