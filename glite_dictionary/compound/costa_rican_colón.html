Costa Rican colón

1. NOUN

/ koʊˈlɔn / (AmE), / kɒˈlɒn / (BrE)
the basic unit of currency in Costa Rica, equal to 100 céntimos.
currency of Costa Rica

Examples

Tourists usually exchange their dollars for Costa Rican colón when traveling in Costa Rica.
The price of the meal was 3,500 Costa Rican colones at the local restaurant.
He compared the value of the Costa Rican colón to the US dollar before making his purchase.

Etymology

Named after Christopher Columbus (Cristóbal Colón in Spanish).
