scientific knowledge

1. NOUN

/ saɪˈɛntɪfɪk ˈnɑlɪdʒ / (AmE), / saɪˈɛntɪfɪk ˈnɒlɪdʒ / (BrE)
knowledge gained through systematic study, observation, and experimentation, organized by general principles or laws
knowledge from systematic scientific study

Examples

The course will introduce students to the foundations of scientific knowledge.
New discoveries can sometimes overturn established scientific knowledge.
Critical thinking is essential for the advancement of scientific knowledge.

Synset Examples

mathematics is the basis for much scientific knowledge

Related Terms

  • Synonym: science - the systematic study of the structure and behavior of the physical and natural world
  • Antonym: folk knowledge - traditional knowledge based on observation and experience, not formal scientific study
