Beaufort Sea

1. PROPN

/ ˈboʊfərt si / (AmE), / ˈbəʊfət siː / (BrE)
the part of the Arctic Ocean located to the northeast of Alaska and northwestern Canada
sea in the Arctic Ocean, north of Alaska and Canada

Examples

Researchers traveled to the Beaufort Sea to study the effects of climate change on polar ice.
The Beaufort Sea forms part of the northern boundary of Alaska and Canada.
Oil exploration in the Beaufort Sea has raised environmental concerns in recent years.

Etymology

Named after Sir Francis Beaufort, a British naval officer and hydrographer.
