order Mallophaga

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdər məˈlɒfəɡə / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə məˈlɒfəɡə / (BrE)
A former taxonomic order of insects comprising the biting lice, which are ectoparasites mainly of birds and some mammals.
former insect order; biting lice

Examples

The order Mallophaga includes many species that infest the feathers of birds.
Scientists reclassified members of the order Mallophaga based on genetic evidence.
Poultry farmers are concerned about infestations by lice from the order Mallophaga.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: biting louse - an insect of the group that feeds on skin debris and feathers of birds or mammals

Etymology

From Greek mallos (wool) + phagein (to eat), referring to their feeding habits.
