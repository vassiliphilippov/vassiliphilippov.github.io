genus Plumeria

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs pluːˈmɛriə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs pluːˈmɪəriə / (BrE)
A genus of deciduous shrubs and trees native to tropical America, known for candelabra-like branches and fragrant white or pink flowers.
genus of tropical American flowering shrubs/trees

Examples

The genus Plumeria is prized in tropical gardens for its beautiful and sweet-scented blossoms.
Many species within the genus Plumeria are cultivated as ornamental plants around the world.
Cuttings from the genus Plumeria root easily, making them popular among home gardeners.

Etymology

From Latin 'plumeria', named after Charles Plumier, a French botanist.
