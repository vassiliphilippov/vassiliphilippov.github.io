muscular dystrophy

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌs.kjələr ˈdɪstrəfi / (AmE), / ˈmʌs.kjʊ.lə ˈdɪstrəfi / (BrE)
Any of a group of hereditary diseases that cause progressive weakness and wasting of skeletal muscles.
hereditary muscle-wasting disease
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that muscular dystrophy is a genetic condition affecting muscle strength.
Children with muscular dystrophy may require physiotherapy to help maintain mobility.
Research into treatments for muscular dystrophy has advanced significantly in the past decade.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: muscle disease - disease affecting the muscles

Etymology

From Latin 'muscularis' (pertaining to muscle) and Greek 'dystrophia' (faulty nutrition).
