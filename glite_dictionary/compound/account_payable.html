account payable

1. NOUN

/ əˈkaʊnt ˈpeɪ.ə.bəl / (AmE), / əˈkaʊnt ˈpeɪ.ə.bəl / (BrE)
an amount of money a company owes for goods or services received but not yet paid for, usually recorded as a liability on the balance sheet
amount owed for goods/services (accounting)

Examples

At the end of the fiscal year, the company's accounts payable increased due to delayed payments.
The finance department is responsible for managing accounts payable and processing invoices.
Failure to pay accounts payable on time can damage business relationships.

Synset Examples

the problem was to match receivables and payables in the same currency

Related Terms

  • Antonym: accounts receivable - amounts owed to a company by its customers for goods or services delivered
