epistatic gene

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛp.ɪˈstæt.ɪk dʒin / (AmE), / ˌɛp.ɪˈstæt.ɪk dʒiːn / (BrE)
A gene that suppresses or masks the effect of another gene at a different locus, thereby determining whether a specific trait will be expressed.
gene that masks another gene's effect
medical

Examples

The gene responsible for albinism is an epistatic gene that overrides normal pigmentation genes.
In some plants, an epistatic gene can control whether color traits are visible or hidden.
Scientists identified the epistatic gene that determines coat color in certain breeds of dogs.

Synset Examples

The gene responsible for albinism is an epistatic gene.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hypostatic gene - gene whose effect is masked by an epistatic gene
  • Degree Lower: gene - basic unit of heredity in a living organism

Etymology

From Greek 'epi-' (upon) and 'statikos' (causing to stand), combined with 'gene.'
