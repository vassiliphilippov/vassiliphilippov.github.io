folk dance

1. NOUN

/ foʊk dæns / (AmE), / fəʊk dɑːns / (BrE)
A traditional style of dance that originates among the ordinary people of a region or country, typically reflecting local customs and stories.
traditional dance of ordinary people

Examples

The local festival featured a vibrant folk dance performed by children in colorful costumes.
She has been studying Indian folk dance since she was a teenager.
Many folk dances tell stories about the everyday lives of people in rural communities.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ballet - highly stylized dance form, often performed in theaters
  • Synonym: traditional dance - dances connected with longstanding customs

Etymology

folk: from Old English folc (people); dance: from Old French danser.
