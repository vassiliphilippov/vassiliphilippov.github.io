carbon atom

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑr.bən ˈæt.əm / (AmE), / ˈkɑː.bən ˈæt.əm / (BrE)
An atom of the chemical element carbon, consisting of six protons, six neutrons (typically), and six electrons.
an atom of the element carbon

Examples

A diamond is formed entirely from carbon atoms arranged in a crystal structure.
Every organic molecule contains at least one carbon atom in its composition.
In carbon dioxide, each oxygen atom is bonded to a central carbon atom.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: atom - the smallest unit of a chemical element

Etymology

From 'carbon,' from Latin carbo (coal, charcoal) + 'atom,' from Greek atomos (indivisible).
