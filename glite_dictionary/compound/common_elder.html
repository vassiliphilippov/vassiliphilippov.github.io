common elder

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmən ˈɛldɚ / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən ˈɛldə / (BrE)
A deciduous shrub or small tree (Sambucus nigra) native to Europe and Asia, with clusters of small white flowers and black berries often used in making wines and jellies.
European/Asian shrub with black berries

Examples

We harvested common elder berries in late summer to make jam.
The common elder is valued for its medicinal properties and culinary uses.
Clusters of black fruit hang from the branches of the common elder.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elderberry - the fruit of the elder tree

Etymology

From Latin sambucus (elder tree), with 'common' distinguishing this widespread species.
