lock-up option

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɑk ʌp ˈɑp.ʃən / (AmE), / ˈlɒk ʌp ˈɒp.ʃən / (BrE)
A contractual right, typically offered to a friendly investor (a white knight), giving the option to buy valuable assets ('crown jewels') of a company as a defense against a hostile takeover.
anti-takeover option for asset purchase

Examples

The company granted a lock-up option to the white knight as part of its defense strategy.
Activating the lock-up option allowed the investor to buy key assets before the hostile bidder could.
During merger negotiations, a lock-up option was included to make the offer more attractive to the friendly investor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: white knight - a friendly investor who rescues a company from a hostile takeover
  • Antonym: hostile takeover - an unwanted attempt to acquire a company
