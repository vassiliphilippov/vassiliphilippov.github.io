dozen gross

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʌz.ən ɡroʊs / (AmE), / ˈdʌz.ən ɡrəʊs / (BrE)
A cardinal number equal to 1,728 (12 times a gross or 12 × 144).
a cardinal number: 1,728 (12 × gross)

Examples

The warehouse received a shipment of dozen gross of screws for the new project.
If you order a dozen gross of pencils, you'll get 1,728 pencils altogether.
Historical records mention that the craftsman produced a dozen gross of buttons each month.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: gross - a quantity of 144; a dozen dozens
  • Degree Lower: dozen - a group of twelve

Etymology

From 'dozen' (from Old French 'douzaine') and 'gross' (from French 'grosse douzaine', large dozen).
