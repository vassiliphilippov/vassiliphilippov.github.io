Otaheite arrowroot starch

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊtəˌhiːt ˈæroʊˌruːt stɑrtʃ / (AmE), / ˌəʊtəˌhiːt ˈærərʊt stɑːtʃ / (BrE)
a starch obtained from the root of the pia (Tacca palmata), used as a thickener in cooking and sometimes in medicine
starch from the root of the pia plant

Examples

Some traditional desserts use Otaheite arrowroot starch to achieve a silky smooth texture.
The import of Otaheite arrowroot starch was valuable for the local baking industry in the 19th century.
Pharmacists once prescribed Otaheite arrowroot starch as a soothing remedy for digestive issues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arrowroot - starch obtained from the rhizomes of various tropical plants

Etymology

Otaheite is an older name for Tahiti, reflecting the plant’s origin; arrowroot refers to its similarity to true arrowroot starch.
