dovetail joint

1. NOUN

/ ˈdoʊvˌteɪl dʒɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˈdəʊvteɪl dʒɔɪnt / (BrE)
a woodworking joint made by interlocking wedge-shaped projections and corresponding slots, creating a strong, durable connection
interlocking woodworking joint

Examples

The cabinetmaker used a dovetail joint to securely connect the drawer's sides.
A well-made dovetail joint is both strong and visually attractive in fine furniture.
The instructions recommend practicing the dovetail joint on scrap wood before starting the main project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mortise and tenon joint - a woodworking joint consisting of a mortise hole and a tenon tongue
  • Degree Lower: butt joint - a simple wood joint where two pieces are joined end to end
  • Degree Lower: box joint - a woodworking joint with interlocking square projections and recesses

Etymology

From 'dovetail', referring to the joint's resemblance to a dove's tail.
