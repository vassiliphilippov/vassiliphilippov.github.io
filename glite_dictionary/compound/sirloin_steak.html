sirloin steak

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɜːr.lɔɪn steɪk / (AmE), / ˈsɜː.lɔɪn steɪk / (BrE)
a thick, tender cut of beef taken from the sirloin, typically used for grilling or frying
a cut of beef from the sirloin

Examples

He ordered a juicy sirloin steak with mashed potatoes for dinner.
The chef recommends grilling the sirloin steak to medium-rare for the best flavor.
Supermarkets often have sirloin steak on special during the summer barbecue season.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: ribeye steak - a cut of beef from the rib section
  • Degree Higher: tenderloin steak - a very tender cut of beef from the loin
  • Synonym: sirloin - the rear part of the loin of beef

Etymology

From Middle English 'surloine', ultimately from Old French 'surloigne' (above the loin).
