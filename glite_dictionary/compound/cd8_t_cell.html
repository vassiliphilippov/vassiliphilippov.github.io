CD8 T cell

1. NOUN

/ si di eɪt ti sɛl / (AmE), / siː diː eɪt tiː sɛl / (BrE)
a type of T cell with a CD8 receptor that recognizes and kills virus-infected cells by binding to antigens on their surface
T cell with CD8 receptor that kills infected cells
medical

Examples

When a CD8 T cell encounters a virus-infected cell, it can destroy it to prevent the spread of infection.
Researchers measured the activity of CD8 T cells in patients recovering from the disease.
The immune therapy aims to boost CD8 T cell response against cancer cells.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cytotoxic T cell - a T cell that kills infected or cancerous cells
  • Antonym: helper T cell - a T cell that assists other white blood cells in immune responses

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'cluster of differentiation 8' (CD8), a protein marker on the cell surface.
