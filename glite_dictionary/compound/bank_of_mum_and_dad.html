bank of mum and dad

1. NOUN

/ bæŋk əv mʌm ənd dæd / (AmE), / baŋk əv mʌm ənd dad / (BrE)
an informal term for parents who provide financial support, especially to their adult children for major expenses
informal: parental financial support
informal british

Examples

Many young couples rely on the bank of mum and dad to buy their first home.
His new business venture was funded by the bank of mum and dad.
Without the bank of mum and dad, she wouldn't have been able to afford university.

Synset Examples

Students often tap into the bank of mum and dad for tuition.
He bought his first car from the bank of mum and dad.
Young adults often rely on the bank of mum and dad.
