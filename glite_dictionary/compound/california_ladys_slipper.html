California lady's slipper

1. NOUN

/ kælɪˌfɔrnjə ˈleɪdiz ˌslɪpər / (AmE), / kælɪˌfɔːniə ˈleɪdiz ˌslɪpə / (BrE)
a species of wild orchid (Cypripedium californicum) native to southwestern Oregon and northern California, noted for its yellow-green flowers with distinctive white pouches
native orchid of the US West Coast

Examples

The California lady's slipper can be found along moist stream banks in northern California.
Botanists are working to preserve populations of the rare California lady's slipper from habitat loss.
We hiked through the forest to find the beautiful blooms of the California lady's slipper in early summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lady's slipper - any orchid of the genus Cypripedium with slipper-shaped flowers

Etymology

Named for its slipper-shaped pouch (lip) and its occurrence in California.
