ultraviolet radiation

1. NOUN

/ ˌʌltrəˈvaɪələt ˌreɪdiˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌʌltrəˈvaɪələt ˌreɪdiˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
electromagnetic radiation with wavelengths shorter than visible light but longer than X-rays, typically responsible for causing sunburns
electromagnetic radiation beyond visible light

Examples

Ultraviolet radiation from the sun can damage unprotected skin and cause sunburn.
Many insects can see ultraviolet radiation, which is invisible to the human eye.
The laboratory used special lamps emitting ultraviolet radiation to sterilize equipment.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: infrared radiation - electromagnetic radiation with wavelengths longer than visible light
  • Degree Lower: visible light - electromagnetic radiation visible to the human eye
  • Degree Higher: X-ray - electromagnetic radiation with wavelengths shorter than ultraviolet radiation

Etymology

From Latin 'ultra' (beyond) + 'violet', referring to radiation beyond the violet end of visible light.
