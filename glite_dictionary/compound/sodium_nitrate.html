sodium nitrate

1. NOUN

/ ˌsoʊdiəm ˈnaɪtreɪt / (AmE), / ˌsəʊdiəm ˈnaɪtreɪt / (BrE)
a white crystalline salt (NaNO3), used chiefly as a fertilizer and in explosives
a chemical used as fertilizer and explosive

Examples

Many farmers use sodium nitrate to enhance the growth of their crops.
Sodium nitrate is a key ingredient in some types of explosives.
Exposure to large amounts of sodium nitrate can be harmful to humans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: potassium nitrate - a similar chemical compound used in fertilizers and explosives
