Grand Teton

1. PROPN

/ ɡrænd ˈtiːˌtɑn / (AmE), / ɡrænd ˈtiːˌtɒn / (BrE)
the highest mountain peak in the Teton Range, located in northwestern Wyoming, USA, standing 13,766 feet (4,199 meters) high
mountain peak in Wyoming, USA

Examples

Many climbers travel from around the world to ascend Grand Teton each summer.
The view of Grand Teton reflected in the lake is breathtaking at sunrise.
We camped near Grand Teton while exploring the national park.

Etymology

French, meaning 'big nipple,' named by French trappers in the early 19th century.
