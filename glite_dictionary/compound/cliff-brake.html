cliff-brake

1. NOUN

/ ˈklɪf ˌbreɪk / (AmE), / ˈklɪf ˌbreɪk / (BrE)
Any of several small ferns that grow on rocks, found in tropical and warm temperate regions.
a small rock-dwelling fern

Examples

The cliff-brake thrives in crevices along the rocky hillsides.
We spotted a rare cliff-brake during our hike in the canyon.
Several species of cliff-brake can be found in this warm temperate region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fern - a non-flowering plant with feathery or leafy fronds

Etymology

From 'cliff' (a steep rock face) and 'brake' (an old word for fern).
