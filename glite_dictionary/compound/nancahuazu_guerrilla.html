Nancahuazu Guerrilla

1. NOUN

/ nɑːŋ.kɒˈwɑː.zuː gəˈriː.jə / (AmE), / nan.kəˈwɑː.zuː gəˈrɪl.ə / (BrE)
a Bolivian Marxist-Leninist guerrilla organization, originally led by Che Guevara in the 1960s and later serving as an umbrella for indigenous subversive groups.
Bolivian Marxist guerrilla organization

Examples

The Nancahuazu Guerrilla operated covertly in Bolivia during the 1960s under the leadership of Che Guevara.
Some historians analyze the impact of the Nancahuazu Guerrilla on subsequent revolutionary movements in South America.
Government forces spent years trying to suppress the activities of the Nancahuazu Guerrilla.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guerrilla - member of a small independent group fighting against larger regular forces

Etymology

Named after the Ñancahuazú region in Bolivia; 'guerrilla' from Spanish 'guerra' (war).
