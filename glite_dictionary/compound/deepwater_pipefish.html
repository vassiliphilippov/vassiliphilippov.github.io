deepwater pipefish

1. NOUN

/ ˈdipˌwɔtər ˈpaɪˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈdiːpˌwɔːtə ˈpaɪˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
a small marine fish about 8 inches long, found from eastern Florida to the western Caribbean, known for its elongated body and tubular snout
small marine fish of the western Atlantic

Examples

The deepwater pipefish is often seen swimming near seagrass beds in the Caribbean.
Researchers discovered a new breeding site for the deepwater pipefish off the coast of Florida.
The aquarium displays a variety of marine species, including the rare deepwater pipefish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pipefish - any fish of the family Syngnathidae with a long, slender body and tubular snout
