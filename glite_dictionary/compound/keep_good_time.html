to keep good time

1. VERB

/ kip ɡʊd taɪm / (AmE), / kiːp ɡʊd taɪm / (BrE)
(of a clock or watch) to show the correct time consistently; to be accurate in measuring time.
be accurate in timekeeping

Examples

Even after years of use, this old watch still keeps good time.
The school bell keeps good time, ringing exactly at the top of each hour.
She checked to make sure her new clock would keep good time before buying it.

Synset Examples

The train is known to keep good time on its route.
This clock will keep good time for years.
My watch is reliable and keeps good time.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: run fast - (of a clock or watch) to show a time ahead of the correct time
  • Antonym: run slow - (of a clock or watch) to show a time behind the correct time
