national bank

1. NOUN

/ ˈnæʃənəl ˌbæŋk / (AmE), / ˈnæʃ(ə)n(ə)l ˌbaŋk / (BrE)
A commercial bank that is chartered and regulated by the federal government, especially in the United States.
commercial bank chartered by federal government

Examples

The central government recently established a new national bank to expand credit access across the country.
Many businesses prefer working with a national bank because of its extensive network and regulatory oversight.
A national bank must comply with strict federal guidelines to maintain its charter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: commercial bank - bank that offers services to the general public and to companies
