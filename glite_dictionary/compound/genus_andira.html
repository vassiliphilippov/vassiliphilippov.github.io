genus Andira

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ænˈdaɪrə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs anˈdɪərə / (BrE)
A small genus of evergreen trees native to tropical America and western Africa, known for their hardwood and decorative value.
genus of tropical evergreen trees

Examples

The genus Andira contains several species prized for their durable timber.
Researchers discovered a new medicinal compound in the bark of trees from the genus Andira.
Many species in the genus Andira are important components of tropical forest ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Andira - the type genus of the family containing Andira species

Etymology

From Latin 'Andira', the scientific name for the genus.
