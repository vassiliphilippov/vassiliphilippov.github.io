Fighting Joe Hooker

1. PROPN

/ ˈfaɪtɪŋ dʒoʊ ˈhʊkər / (AmE), / ˈfaɪtɪŋ dʒəʊ ˈhʊkə / (BrE)
a United States general in the Union Army during the Civil War, best known for his defeat at the Battle of Chancellorsville (1814–1879)
Union general in the American Civil War

Examples

Fighting Joe Hooker is remembered for his leadership at Chancellorsville during the Civil War.
Many historians debate whether Fighting Joe Hooker's defeat was due to poor strategy or bad luck.
Despite his nickname, Fighting Joe Hooker struggled to inspire confidence among his troops.

Etymology

Nickname 'Fighting Joe' originated from a typographical error in newspaper reports, attached to General Joseph Hooker.
