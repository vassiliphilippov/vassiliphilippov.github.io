market capitalisation

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑrkɪt ˌkæpɪtələˌzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈmɑːkɪt ˌkæpɪt(ə)laɪˈzeɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
The total value of a company on the stock market, calculated by multiplying the number of shares outstanding by the current share price.
total stock market value of a company

Examples

Apple's market capitalisation exceeded one trillion dollars for the first time in 2018.
The company's market capitalisation dropped sharply after the disappointing earnings report.
Investors often compare market capitalisation when evaluating potential stocks to buy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: market value - the price at which an asset would trade in a competitive auction

Etymology

From 'market' (location or system for trading) and 'capitalisation' (from Latin 'capitalis', relating to the head or principal).
