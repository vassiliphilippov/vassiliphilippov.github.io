Florida bean

1. NOUN

/ ˈflɔrɪdə ˌbiːn / (AmE), / ˈflɒrɪdə ˌbiːn / (BrE)
an annual climbing plant of Asian origin, with long clusters of purplish flowers and hairy pods, cultivated in the southern United States chiefly for green manure and animal grazing
tropical vine grown for manure and grazing

Examples

The farmer planted Florida bean to improve the nitrogen content of his soil.
During the summer, the fields were lush with climbing Florida bean vines.
Livestock often graze on Florida bean because of its rich foliage.
