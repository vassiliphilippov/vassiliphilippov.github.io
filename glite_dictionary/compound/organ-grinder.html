organ-grinder

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrɡən ˌɡraɪndər / (AmE), / ˈɔːɡən ˌɡraɪndə / (BrE)
a street musician who plays a hand organ or hurdy-gurdy, often for tips
street musician with a hand organ

Examples

As they walked down the street, they heard an organ-grinder playing lively tunes for the crowd.
The organ-grinder was accompanied by a small monkey collecting coins from passersby.
In the old days, an organ-grinder was a common sight at fairs and in city squares.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: street musician - a performer who plays music in public places for money

Etymology

From 'organ' (a musical instrument) and 'grinder' (one who operates a machine); first used in the 19th century.
