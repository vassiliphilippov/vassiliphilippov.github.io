Bren gun

1. NOUN

/ brɛn ɡʌn / (AmE), / brɛn ɡʌn / (BrE)
a light machine gun operated by gas pressure, used especially by British forces during World War II
British World War II light machine gun

Examples

The infantry squad advanced cautiously, supported by the steady fire of the Bren gun.
During training, he learned how to strip and clean a Bren gun efficiently.
Collectors value a genuine Bren gun as an important piece of World War II history.

Etymology

Portmanteau of Brno (the Czechoslovakian city where it was designed) and Enfield (the British town of manufacture).
