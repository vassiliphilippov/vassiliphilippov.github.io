large intestine

1. NOUN

/ lɑrdʒ ɪnˈtɛstɪn / (AmE), / lɑːdʒ ɪnˈtɛstɪn / (BrE)
the part of the digestive system extending from the cecum to the rectum, responsible for absorbing water from food residues before they are excreted
part of the digestive tract after the small intestine

Examples

The large intestine absorbs water and electrolytes from indigestible food material.
After passing through the small intestine, waste enters the large intestine for further processing.
A healthy large intestine is essential for good digestive health.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: small intestine - part of the digestive tract between the stomach and the large intestine

Etymology

From Latin 'intestinum' (gut, intestine), with 'large' indicating its greater diameter compared to the small intestine.
