mistletoe family

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪsəlˌtoʊ ˈfæməli / (AmE), / ˈmɪsəlˌtəʊ ˈfæməli / (BrE)
a family of parasitic or hemiparasitic shrubs, including mistletoes, sometimes classified as a subfamily of Loranthaceae
a family of shrubs including mistletoes

Examples

The mistletoe family contains several species of parasitic plants found worldwide.
Some botanists classify the mistletoe family as a subfamily of Loranthaceae.
Species in the mistletoe family are often seen growing on the branches of trees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Loranthaceae - a family of flowering plants that sometimes includes the mistletoe family

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɪsəlˌtoʊ ˈfæməli / (AmE), / ˈmɪsəlˌtəʊ ˈfæməli / (BrE)
a family of parasitic or hemiparasitic shrubs or small trees, often including Viscaceae, found in tropical and temperate regions, that attach to host plants by haustoria
family of parasitic shrubs or trees (botany)

Examples

The mistletoe family includes several parasitic plants that attach themselves to host trees.
Ecologists are studying members of the mistletoe family for their unique way of extracting nutrients from other plants.
Some botanists now classify certain species separately from the traditional mistletoe family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Loranthaceae - family of mostly hemiparasitic plants often called mistletoes
  • Synonym: Viscaceae - family of mistletoes, sometimes included in mistletoe family
