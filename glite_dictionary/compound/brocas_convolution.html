Broca's convolution

1. NOUN

/ ˈbroʊ.kəz ˌkɑn.vəˈluː.ʃən / (AmE), / ˈbrəʊ.kəz ˌkɒn.vəˈluː.ʃən / (BrE)
the region of the brain in the left frontal lobe, often associated with the control of speech muscles and speech production
area of the brain involved in speech
medical

Examples

Damage to the Broca's convolution can result in difficulty speaking or forming words.
Brain scans showed increased activity in the Broca's convolution during language tasks.
Researchers are studying how the Broca's convolution interacts with other regions involved in communication.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Broca's area - region in the frontal lobe involved in speech production
  • Antonym: Wernicke's area - brain area related to language comprehension

Etymology

Named after French physician Paul Broca (1824–1880), who first identified its role in speech.
