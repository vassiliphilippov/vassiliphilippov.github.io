cloud grass

1. NOUN

/ klaʊd ɡræs / (AmE), / klaʊd ɡrɑːs / (BrE)
a Spanish grass (Agrostis nebulosa) with fine, feathery, cloud-like flower panicles, often grown for use in dried bouquets
a Spanish grass with feathery flower panicles

Examples

Cloud grass is often added to floral arrangements for its delicate, airy appearance.
Gardeners commonly dry cloud grass to use in winter bouquets.
The feathery heads of cloud grass create a soft, floating effect in landscapes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grass - a common type of plant with narrow leaves, typically growing in lawns and fields
