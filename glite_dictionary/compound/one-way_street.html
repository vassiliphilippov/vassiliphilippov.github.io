one-way street

1. NOUN

/ ˈwʌn weɪ strit / (AmE), / ˈwʌn weɪ striːt / (BrE)
A street on which vehicles are only allowed to travel in one direction.
street where traffic flows one way only

Examples

We got lost and accidentally turned down a one-way street, so we had to go around the block.
Parking is prohibited on this side of the one-way street during rush hour.
Drivers must pay attention to signs to avoid entering a one-way street from the wrong direction.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: two-way street - a street where vehicles can travel in both directions

2. NOUN

/ ˌwʌn ˈweɪ ˌstrit / (AmE), / ˌwʌn ˈweɪ ˌstriːt / (BrE)
a situation or relationship in which only one side acts or benefits, without reciprocity from the other side
unilateral situation or relationship

Examples

Good communication should not be a one-way street; both sides need to share their thoughts.
Trust is a one-way street in this company, as employees can't question management.
He realized too late their friendship had become a one-way street, with him always making the effort.

Synset Examples

cooperation cannot be a one-way street

Related Terms

  • Antonym: reciprocal - a situation in which both sides act or benefit equally
