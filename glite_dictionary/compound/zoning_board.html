zoning board

1. NOUN

/ ˈzoʊnɪŋ bɔrd / (AmE), / ˈzəʊnɪŋ bɔːd / (BrE)
A local government body that determines land use regulations by dividing an area into zones with different restrictions and permitted activities.
government body for land-use regulation

Examples

The zoning board approved plans for the new shopping center after a lengthy public hearing.
Residents voiced their concerns about traffic to the zoning board during the monthly meeting.
She appealed the zoning board's decision to deny her permit for building a second story.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: planning commission - governmental body overseeing land use and urban development
