Board of Trade unit

1. NOUN

/ ˌbɔrd əv ˈtreɪd ˌjuːnɪt / (AmE), / ˌbɔːd əv ˈtreɪd ˌjuːnɪt / (BrE)
a unit of energy equal to the work done by a power of 1,000 watts operating for one hour; equivalent to one kilowatt-hour, especially used in British contexts
unit of energy, equal to one kilowatt-hour
british

Examples

The electricity bill listed the total usage in Board of Trade units.
One Board of Trade unit is the same as one kilowatt-hour of energy.
Industrial consumption is often measured in thousands of Board of Trade units each month.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kilowatt-hour - a unit of energy equal to 1,000 watt-hours

Etymology

Named for the British Board of Trade, which standardised electricity measurement in the UK.
