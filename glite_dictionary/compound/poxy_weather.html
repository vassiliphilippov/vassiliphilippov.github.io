poxy weather

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑksi ˈwɛðɚ / (AmE), / ˈpɒksi ˈwɛðə / (BrE)
Unpleasant or miserable weather, typically wet, cold, or dreary.
miserable or unpleasant weather
informal british

Examples

The poxy weather ruined our plans for a picnic in the park.
After a week of poxy weather, everyone was desperate to see some sunshine.
I hate having to walk the dog in this poxy weather.

Synset Examples

The poxy weather kept everyone indoors all weekend.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bad weather - weather that is rainy, cloudy, or otherwise unpleasant

Etymology

"Poxy" is British slang, originally meaning affected with pox, later used for anything considered poor or worthless.
