Atlantic salmon

1. NOUN

/ ətˈlæntɪk ˈsæmən / (AmE), / ətˈlæntɪk ˈsæmən / (BrE)
the pink, fatty flesh of a large fish from the northern Atlantic, commonly eaten as food
flesh of a northern Atlantic fish, used as food

Examples

The chef prepared grilled Atlantic salmon with a lemon-dill sauce for dinner.
Fresh Atlantic salmon is prized for its delicate texture and rich flavor.
The restaurant offers both wild and farmed Atlantic salmon on its menu.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: salmon - an edible fish with pink flesh, often found in the Atlantic and Pacific oceans

2. NOUN

/ əˈtlæntɪk ˈsæmən / (AmE), / ətˈlæntɪk ˈsæmən / (BrE)
a large salmon (Salmo salar) found in the northern Atlantic Ocean and its tributaries, known for migrating upriver to spawn and surviving after spawning
species of salmon found in the North Atlantic

Examples

The Atlantic salmon population has declined due to overfishing and habitat loss.
Many chefs consider Atlantic salmon a delicacy because of its rich flavor.
Scientists are studying the migration patterns of Atlantic salmon to help conserve the species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: salmon - a large edible fish known for migrating to spawn
