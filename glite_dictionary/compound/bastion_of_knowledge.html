bastion of knowledge

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæs.tʃən əv ˈnɑ.lɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈbæs.tɪ.ən əv ˈnɒl.ɪdʒ / (BrE)
an institution, place, or person regarded as a strong supporter, defender, or repository of knowledge
strong defender or repository of knowledge

Examples

The local university is often considered a bastion of knowledge in the region.
She regarded her grandfather as a bastion of knowledge, always ready to answer any question.
During the war, the monastery served as a bastion of knowledge, safeguarding precious manuscripts.

Synset Examples

The library was a bastion of knowledge in the community.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: repository of knowledge - a place or person where knowledge is stored or maintained
  • Synonym: bastion - a stronghold or fortress; person or thing defending particular principles

Etymology

From 'bastion', meaning a fortified place (from French bastion), combined with 'of knowledge'.
