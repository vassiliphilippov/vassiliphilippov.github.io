false bittersweet

1. NOUN

/ fɔls ˈbɪtərˌswiːt / (AmE), / fɔːls ˈbɪtəˌswiːt / (BrE)
a North American climbing shrub with yellow capsules that enclose scarlet seeds
North American shrub with yellow capsules and scarlet seeds

Examples

The berries of false bittersweet are brightly colored and easily spotted in the autumn woods.
Gardeners are warned not to confuse false bittersweet with its toxic relatives.
False bittersweet climbs over fences and shrubs, adding splashes of yellow and red in the fall.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bittersweet - a climbing plant with orange-yellow capsules enclosing seeds in a scarlet aril

Etymology

Named for its resemblance to the true bittersweet (Celastrus genus) but being a different species.
