binge-eating syndrome

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɪndʒ ˌitɪŋ ˈsɪndroʊm / (AmE), / ˈbɪndʒ ˌiːtɪŋ ˈsɪndrəʊm / (BrE)
a psychological eating disorder characterized by recurrent episodes of eating large quantities of food, often accompanied by feelings of guilt, depression, and self-condemnation
psychological eating disorder involving recurrent binges
medical

Examples

Many patients with binge-eating syndrome struggle with feelings of shame after episodes of overeating.
Therapists recommend support groups as part of treatment for binge-eating syndrome.
Awareness about binge-eating syndrome has increased as more people seek help for the disorder.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bulimia nervosa - eating disorder involving binge eating followed by purging
  • Antonym: anorexia nervosa - eating disorder characterized by self-starvation and weight loss
