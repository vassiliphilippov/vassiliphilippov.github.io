February daphne

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɛb.juˌɛri ˈdæf.ni / (AmE), / ˈfɛb.ruː.əri ˈdæf.ni / (BrE)
a small European shrub (Daphne mezereum) with fragrant lilac flowers blooming in early spring and poisonous red berries
European shrub with fragrant lilac flowers

Examples

The February daphne is prized for its beautiful blossoms and early spring fragrance.
Many gardeners avoid planting February daphne due to the toxicity of its berries and twigs.
I saw a February daphne blooming in the woodland border before any other shrub this year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mezereon - another name for February daphne (Daphne mezereum)
