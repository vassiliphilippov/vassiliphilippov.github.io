clue-by-four

1. NOUN

/ kluː baɪ fɔr / (AmE), / kluː baɪ fɔː / (BrE)
A blunt or obvious hint or explanation delivered in such a way as to force understanding, often experienced as a sudden or painful realization.
blunt or forceful hint
informal slang

Examples

After missing the point several times, the teacher delivered a clue-by-four that finally made the concept clear.
Sometimes it takes a clue-by-four to get someone to realize their mistake.
His sarcastic comment was such a clue-by-four that everyone finally understood the situation.

Synset Examples

He used a clue-by-four to make his point clear.
Her explanation was so confusing it felt like a clue-by-four.
Sometimes you need a clue-by-four to get through to people.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hint - a small piece of advice or suggestion
  • Synonym: suggestion - an idea or plan put forward for consideration
  • Antonym: subtle hint - an understated or delicate suggestion

Etymology

From 'clue' and the humorous allusion to a 'two-by-four' (a wooden plank), implying a figurative blow to give someone a clue.
