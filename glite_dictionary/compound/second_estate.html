second estate

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛk.ənd ɪˈsteɪt / (AmE), / ˈsek.ənd ɪˈsteɪt / (BrE)
the class of nobility in France before the Revolution, and the peerage in Britain, considered second in rank after the clergy
the nobility class in France and Britain

Examples

During the Ancien Régime, the second estate held significant power and privileges over the common people.
Members of the second estate were exempt from many taxes, unlike those of the third estate.
The British peerage was known as the second estate in historical parliamentary sessions.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: first estate - the clergy as the first social class in France and Britain
  • Degree Higher: third estate - the commoners as the third social class in France and Britain

Etymology

From the division of European society into three 'estates' or social orders; 'second' indicating its position after the clergy ('first estate').
