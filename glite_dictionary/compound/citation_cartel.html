citation cartel

1. NOUN

/ saɪˈteɪʃən kɑːrˈtɛl / (AmE), / saɪˈteɪʃən kɑːˈtɛl / (BrE)
a group of individuals, journals, or institutions that manipulate or exploit academic citations to artificially increase impact or gain advantage.
group manipulating academic citations for advantage

Examples

The university investigated whether the journal was involved in a citation cartel.
Awareness of citation cartels has prompted reforms in academic publishing.
He warned junior researchers about the risks of joining a citation cartel.

Synset Examples

Researchers are wary of the citation cartel's influence.
The journal was criticized for being part of a citation cartel.
He refused to join the citation cartel that exploited academics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: citation - the act of citing or referencing academic sources
  • Synonym: cartel - an association formed to restrict competition or manipulate outcomes

Etymology

Formed from 'citation' (from Latin *citare*, to summon) and 'cartel' (from Italian *cartello*, a letter or placard), by analogy with commercial 'cartel'.
