electromotive series

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌlɛktroʊˈmoʊtɪv ˈsɪriz / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛktrəʊˈməʊtɪv ˈsɪəriːz / (BrE)
A series of metallic elements or ions arranged by their standard electrode potentials, indicating the tendency of one metal to reduce the ions of another.
series ranking metals by electrode potential

Examples

The electromotive series helps chemists predict the outcome of redox reactions.
According to the electromotive series, zinc can reduce copper ions to metallic copper.
The placement of lithium at the top of the electromotive series indicates its strong tendency to lose electrons.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: activity series - a similar ranking of metals by reactivity; often used interchangeably with electromotive series

Etymology

From electromotive (electricity-producing) + series, referring to the sequence of electrode potentials.
