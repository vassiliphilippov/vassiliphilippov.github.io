nitrite bacterium

1. NOUN

/ ˈnaɪtraɪt bəˈktɪriəm / (AmE), / ˈnaɪtraɪt bækˈtɪərɪəm / (BrE)
A bacterium that oxidizes ammonia to nitrites in the process of nitrification.
bacterium that converts ammonia to nitrites
medical

Examples

The soil contains nitrite bacterium that play a vital role in the nitrogen cycle.
A nitrite bacterium oxidizes ammonia, helping to maintain water quality in aquariums.
Research on the nitrite bacterium has advanced our understanding of nitrification in soil ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nitrifying bacterium - bacterium involved in the process of nitrification

Etymology

From 'nitrite' (a salt or ester of nitrous acid) and 'bacterium' (from Greek 'baktērion', meaning small staff or rod).
