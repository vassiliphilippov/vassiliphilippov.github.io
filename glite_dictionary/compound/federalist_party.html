Federalist Party

1. PROPN

/ ˈfɛdərəlɪst ˈpɑːrti / (AmE), / ˈfedərəlɪst ˈpɑːti / (BrE)
A major political party in the early United States, founded by Alexander Hamilton, which supported a strong central government.
early U.S. political party favoring strong central government

Examples

The Federalist Party played a key role in the early years of American government.
Alexander Hamilton was one of the founders of the Federalist Party.
The Federalist Party eventually declined as the Democratic-Republicans gained prominence.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Democratic-Republican Party - an early U.S. political party opposed to the Federalists

Etymology

From 'federalist' (supporter of federal government) and 'party', used in late 18th and early 19th-century United States.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɛdərəlɪst ˌpɑːrti / (AmE), / ˈfɛdərəlɪst ˌpɑːti / (BrE)
a major political party in the United States in the late 18th and early 19th centuries, founded by Alexander Hamilton, which supported a strong central government
early U.S. political party favoring strong central government
american

Examples

The Federalist Party played a key role in shaping the U.S. Constitution and advocating for a strong federal government.
John Adams was a prominent leader of the Federalist Party during his presidency.
The influence of the Federalist Party declined after the War of 1812.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Democratic-Republican Party - early U.S. political party opposing the Federalists

Etymology

From 'federalist', relating to support for a federal union, and 'party', group organized for political purposes.
