to strip-mine

1. VERB

/ ˈstrɪp ˌmaɪn / (AmE), / ˈstrɪp ˌmaɪn / (BrE)
to remove surface layers of soil or rock from an area in order to extract minerals or other resources near the surface, typically by using large machinery
remove surface layers for mining

Examples

Several companies strip-mine large areas of land to reach hidden coal deposits.
If we strip-mine this forest, it will take decades for the ecosystem to recover.
Strict regulations are needed wherever corporations strip-mine to ensure minimal environmental harm.

Synset Examples

They plan to strip-mine the area for valuable minerals.
The company is accused of strip-mining without proper regulations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to excavate - to dig out material from the ground
  • Synonym: quarry - to extract stone or other materials from a quarry

Etymology

Formed from 'strip' + 'mine'; refers to stripping away earth to access minerals.
