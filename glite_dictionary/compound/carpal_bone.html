carpal bone

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑr.pəl boʊn / (AmE), / ˈkɑː.pəl bəʊn / (BrE)
any of the eight small bones forming the wrist in primates, located between the forearm and the hand
one of the small wrist bones
medical

Examples

The human wrist contains eight carpal bones arranged in two rows.
A fracture of a carpal bone can significantly limit wrist movement.
Arthritis may develop in the carpal bones and cause pain or stiffness.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: metacarpal - any of the five bones between the wrist and the fingers
  • Degree Higher: phalanx - any of the bones in the fingers or toes

Etymology

From Latin 'carpus' (wrist), from Greek 'karpos'; 'bone' from Old English 'bān'.
