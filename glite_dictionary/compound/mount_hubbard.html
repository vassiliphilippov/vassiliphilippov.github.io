Mount Hubbard

1. PROPN

/ maʊnt ˈhʌbərd / (AmE), / maʊnt ˈhʌbəd / (BrE)
a mountain peak in southeastern Alaska, part of the Coast Range, rising to 14,950 feet (4,556 meters)
mountain peak in southeastern Alaska

Examples

The climbers spent weeks preparing for their ascent of Mount Hubbard in the harsh Alaskan wilderness.
A breathtaking view awaited them at the summit of Mount Hubbard, high above the clouds.
Mount Hubbard is one of the tallest peaks in the Coast Range, drawing adventurous mountaineers from around the world.
