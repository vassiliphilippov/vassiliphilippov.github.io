eve of the event

1. NOUN

/ iv ʌv ði ɪˈvɛnt / (AmE), / iːv əv ði ɪˈvɛnt / (BrE)
the day or period immediately before a particular event, often marked by anticipation or preparation
the time just before an event

Examples

On the eve of the event, the team stayed up late making final preparations.
Excitement built on the eve of the event as guests arrived from all over the country.
Nerves and tension were high on the eve of the event as everyone awaited the big announcement.

Synset Examples

The eve of the event was filled with anticipation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eve - the day or period before an event
  • Synonym: on the eve of - immediately before a specific event or occasion

Etymology

From 'eve' meaning 'the day or evening before,' historically from Old English 'ǣfen' (evening).
