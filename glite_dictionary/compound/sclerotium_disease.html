sclerotium disease

1. NOUN

/ skləˈroʊtiəm dɪˈziz / (AmE), / skləˈrəʊtiəm dɪˈziːz / (BrE)
A plant disease caused by fungi of the genus Sclerotium, characterized by the formation of sclerotia.
plant disease caused by Sclerotium fungi
medical

Examples

The wheat crop suffered extensive damage due to sclerotium disease during the rainy season.
Farmers use fungicides to prevent sclerotium disease from spreading in their vegetable fields.
Symptoms of sclerotium disease often include the appearance of hard, white fungal masses on plant stems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: root rot - a disease of plants in which the roots decay, often caused by fungi or bacteria

Etymology

From New Latin 'Sclerotium', from Greek 'sklēros' (hard), referring to the tough fungal structures formed.
