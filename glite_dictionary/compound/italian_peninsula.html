Italian Peninsula

1. PROPN

/ ɪˈtæliən pəˈnɪnsjələ / (AmE), / ɪˈtaliən pəˈnɪnsjələ / (BrE)
the peninsula in southern Europe shaped like a boot, extending into the Mediterranean Sea, forming the main part of Italy
boot-shaped peninsula in southern Europe, main part of Italy

Examples

The Italian Peninsula has played a crucial role in European history since ancient times.
Tourists are drawn to the Italian Peninsula for its beautiful coastline and rich cultural heritage.
Because of its boot-like shape, the Italian Peninsula is easy to spot on a map of Europe.
