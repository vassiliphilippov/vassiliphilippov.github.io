major fast day

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪdʒər fæst deɪ / (AmE), / ˈmeɪdʒə fɑːst deɪ / (BrE)
Either of two principal days of fasting observed in the Jewish calendar, namely Yom Kippur or Tisha B'Av.
Jewish principal day of fasting

Examples

Yom Kippur, a major fast day, requires total abstention from food and water.
Of all the Jewish observances, the major fast day of Tisha B'Av is one of the most solemn.
He explained the differences between a major fast day and a minor fast in the Jewish tradition.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: minor fast day - lesser fasts on the Jewish calendar
