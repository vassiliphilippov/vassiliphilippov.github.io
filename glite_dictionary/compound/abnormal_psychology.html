abnormal psychology

1. NOUN

/ æbˈnɔrməl saɪˈkɑlədʒi / (AmE), / æbˈnɔːməl saɪˈkɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the branch of psychology that studies unusual patterns of behavior, emotion, and thought, which may or may not be understood as precipitating a mental disorder
the study of abnormal behavior in psychology

Examples

She decided to specialize in abnormal psychology during her graduate studies.
Abnormal psychology examines behavior that deviates from societal norms.
His interest in abnormal psychology led him to research anxiety disorders in children.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clinical psychology - the branch of psychology concerned with assessing and treating mental illness and disability

Etymology

From 'abnormal' (Latin abnormis, irregular) and 'psychology' (Greek psukhē 'soul' + -logia 'study').
