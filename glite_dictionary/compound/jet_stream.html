jet stream

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɛt ˌstriːm / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛt ˌstriːm / (BrE)
a narrow band of strong, high-speed winds in the upper atmosphere, usually blowing from west to east around the Earth, which has a significant influence on weather patterns
fast upper-atmosphere wind current

Examples

Pilots sometimes alter their flight paths to take advantage of the jet stream for a faster journey.
A strong jet stream can influence the development of storms across the continent.
Changes in the position of the jet stream often lead to unusual weather patterns.

Etymology

Formed by combining 'jet' (as in stream of fluid) and 'stream'; first used in meteorology in the mid-20th century.
