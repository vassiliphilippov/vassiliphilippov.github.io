to feel no pain

1. VERB

/ fil noʊ peɪn / (AmE), / fiːl nəʊ peɪn / (BrE)
to experience no discomfort or suffering, often as a result of medication, alcohol, or other factors
to experience no discomfort or suffering

Examples

After the dentist gave her the injection, she began to feel no pain during the procedure.
Some people drink alcohol to feel no pain after a long, hard day.
With the strong painkillers, he claimed he could feel no pain at all.

Synset Examples

The medication helped him feel no pain during recovery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: be numb - to be unable to feel, physically or emotionally
  • Antonym: to suffer - to experience physical or mental pain
