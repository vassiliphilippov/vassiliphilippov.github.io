dwarf chinquapin oak

1. NOUN

/ ˈdwɔrf ˌtʃɪnkwəpɪn oʊk / (AmE), / ˈdwɔːf ˌtʃɪnkwəpɪn əʊk / (BrE)
A small, shrubby deciduous oak tree native to northeastern and central United States, known for its sweet edible nut and tendency to form dense thickets.
small shrubby American oak tree with edible nuts

Examples

The dwarf chinquapin oak produces small, sweet acorns that are prized by wildlife.
Gardeners often plant dwarf chinquapin oak to form natural hedges in large landscapes.
Dense thickets of dwarf chinquapin oak can be found in open woodlands across the Midwest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oak - a type of tree known for its hardwood and lobed leaves
  • Synonym: chinquapin oak - a species of oak tree with sweet edible nuts
