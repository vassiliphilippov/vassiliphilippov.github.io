surface tension

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɝː.fɪs ˌtɛn.ʃən / (AmE), / ˈsɜː.fɪs ˌtɛn.ʃən / (BrE)
The tendency of the surface of a liquid to contract to the smallest possible surface area, caused by intermolecular forces at the surface.
phenomenon of contracted liquid surfaces

Examples

The high surface tension of water allows insects to walk on its surface.
Droplets form because of surface tension pulling the molecules into a spherical shape.
Some detergents lower the surface tension of water, helping it spread and clean more effectively.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cohesion - the attraction between molecules of the same substance
