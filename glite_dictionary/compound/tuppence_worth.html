tuppence worth

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʌp.əns wɜrθ / (AmE), / ˈtʌp.ɪns wɜːθ / (BrE)
one's opinion or contribution to a discussion, typically regarded as minor or unsolicited
trivial or unsolicited opinion
british

Examples

Everyone was keen to add their tuppence worth during the heated discussion.
She always feels the need to give her tuppence worth, even if no one asks.
Before we move on, does anyone else want to add their tuppence worth?

Synset Examples

He offered his tuppence worth on the debate.
She shared her tuppence worth, though no one asked.
Everyone gave their tuppence worth on the new policy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: two cents - one's unsolicited or trivial opinion (American English)

Etymology

From British English 'tuppence' (two pence, a small amount of money) + 'worth' (value).
