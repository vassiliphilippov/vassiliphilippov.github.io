queen bee

1. NOUN

/ kwiːn biː / (AmE), / kwiːn biː / (BrE)
the fertile female bee in a colony, responsible for laying eggs and reproducing
fertile female bee in a colony

Examples

The queen bee is essential to the survival of the hive, as she lays all the eggs.
Each spring, the colony may produce a new queen bee to replace the old one.
In a beehive, only the queen bee is capable of laying fertilized eggs.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: worker bee - a sterile female bee that performs labor in the colony
  • Antonym: drone - a male bee whose primary role is to mate with the queen

2. NOUN

/ kwiːn biː / (AmE), / kwiːn biː / (BrE)
a woman who is the dominant or most influential member of a group, often dictating social dynamics and decisions
dominant woman in a group

Examples

In her high school, Jessica was always the queen bee, setting trends for everyone else.
The office queen bee quickly made it clear she was in charge of social events.
He didn't like her queen bee attitude, always bossing people around.

Synset Examples

Everyone listens to her; she's the queen bee in our group.
At the office, she's definitely the queen bee of our team.
He acts like the queen bee, always needing attention.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leader - a person who leads or commands a group or organization
  • Antonym: follower - a person who supports and is guided by another

Etymology

From the literal 'queen bee', the sole reproductive female in a bee colony, used figuratively since the mid-20th century.
