needle-wood

1. NOUN

/ ˈniːdəl wʊd / (AmE), / ˈniːdl wʊd / (BrE)
a large bushy shrub with sharp pointed leaves and creamy white flowers, native to central and eastern Australia
Australian shrub with sharp leaves and creamy flowers

Examples

The needle-wood is known for its striking creamy white flowers in the Australian bush.
Farmers in central Australia often plant needle-wood as windbreaks around their properties.
Botanists studied the adaptation of needle-wood to arid environments.
