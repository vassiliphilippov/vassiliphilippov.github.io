late night

1. NOUN

/ leɪt naɪt / (AmE), / leɪt naɪt / (BrE)
a time late in the evening or night, especially when someone stays at work or is awake until a late hour
period late in the evening or night

Examples

After a long late night at the office, he was too tired to cook dinner.
She tries to avoid late nights so she can wake up early for her workout.
The team pulled a late night to finish the project before the deadline.

Synset Examples

She announced no more late nights at the office.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: all-nighter - a period of remaining awake all night, often to study or work
