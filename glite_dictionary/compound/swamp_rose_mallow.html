swamp rose mallow

1. NOUN

/ swɑmp roʊz ˈmæloʊ / (AmE), / swɒmp rəʊz ˈmæləʊ / (BrE)
A tall shrub native to salt marshes of the eastern United States, known for its large rose-colored flowers.
a shrub with large rose flowers native to eastern U.S. marshes

Examples

The swamp rose mallow blooms in midsummer, adding bright color to wetland landscapes.
Gardeners value swamp rose mallow for its showy, tropical-looking flowers.
We spotted a cluster of swamp rose mallows along the edge of the salt marsh.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hibiscus - genus of flowering plants that includes swamp rose mallow
