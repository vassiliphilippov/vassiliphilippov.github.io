graduate school

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡrædʒuət skuːl / (AmE), / ˈɡrædʒuət skuːl / (BrE)
A division of a university or college that offers advanced academic degrees, such as master's or doctorates, beyond the bachelor's degree.
school for advanced degrees beyond bachelor's

Examples

After earning her bachelor's degree, Maria applied to several graduate schools to pursue a master's in biology.
Admission to graduate school often requires strong academic performance and letters of recommendation.
He completed his PhD in chemistry at a prestigious graduate school on the East Coast.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: postgraduate - A student engaged in study beyond a bachelor's degree
