absurdist humour

1. NOUN

/ əbˈsɝːdɪst ˈhjuːmɚ / (AmE), / əbˈsɜːdɪst ˈhjuːmə / (BrE)
A style of humour based on deliberately illogical actions or events that defy logical reasoning, often producing a sense of incongruity or nonsense.
humour based on illogical or nonsensical elements

Examples

The play's reliance on absurdist humour leaves the audience both amused and bewildered.
Absurdist humour often involves situations that completely defy common sense, such as people conversing in gibberish.
Many internet memes have embraced absurdist humour to create unpredictable and funny visual jokes.

Synset Examples

Surreal humour is also found frequently in avant-garde theatre such as Waiting for Godot and Rosencrantz & Guildenstern Are Dead.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: surreal humour - humour characterized by bizarre, dreamlike, or fantastical elements

Etymology

From 'absurdist', relating to absurdism, + 'humour'. 'Absurdist' derives from French 'absurde' and Latin 'absurdus' (out of tune, unreasonable).
