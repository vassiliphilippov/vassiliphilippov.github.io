Mercalli scale

1. NOUN

/ mərˈkɑːli skeɪl / (AmE), / mɜːˈkɑːli skeɪl / (BrE)
a scale used to measure the intensity of earthquakes based on observed effects and damage, with values ranging from I (not felt) to XII (total destruction)
scale for earthquake intensity

Examples

The Mercalli scale rates earthquake intensity based on visible effects rather than instrument readings.
In 1906, the San Francisco earthquake was estimated at level XI on the Mercalli scale.
Engineers studied the area to assess damage according to the Mercalli scale.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Richter scale - a logarithmic scale for measuring the magnitude of earthquakes

Etymology

Named after Giuseppe Mercalli (1850–1914), Italian volcanologist who developed the scale.
