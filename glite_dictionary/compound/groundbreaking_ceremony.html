groundbreaking ceremony

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡraʊndˌbreɪkɪŋ səˈrɛmoʊni / (AmE), / ˈɡraʊndˌbreɪkɪŋ səˈrɛməni / (BrE)
a formal event held to mark the start of construction work on a building or other project, typically involving the ceremonial turning of the first soil
formal event marking start of construction

Examples

The mayor attended the groundbreaking ceremony for the new hospital last Monday.
During the groundbreaking ceremony, officials wore hard hats and used gold-plated shovels to turn the first soil.
A band played at the groundbreaking ceremony, making the start of the project a festive occasion.
