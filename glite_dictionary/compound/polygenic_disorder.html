polygenic disorder

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑːliˈdʒɛnɪk dɪsˈɔrdər / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈdʒɛnɪk dɪsˈɔːdə / (BrE)
An inherited disease or condition that is caused by the combined effect of multiple genes rather than a single gene.
disease caused by several genes
medical

Examples

Type 2 diabetes is a common polygenic disorder influenced by several genetic and environmental factors.
Many cases of high blood pressure are considered to be a polygenic disorder.
Unlike single-gene conditions, a polygenic disorder results from the interaction of multiple genes.

Etymology

From 'polygenic' (from Greek 'poly' meaning 'many' and 'genos' meaning 'kind, birth') and 'disorder'.
