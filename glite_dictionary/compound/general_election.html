general election

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɛnɚəl ɪˈlɛkʃən / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛnərəl ɪˈlɛkʃən / (BrE)
an election in which all or most members of a political body are chosen, typically held simultaneously across different constituencies in a country or state.
nationwide or statewide election for government office

Examples

The country will hold its next general election in November.
Many citizens registered to vote ahead of the general election.
After the general election, the new prime minister was sworn into office.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: primary election - a preliminary election to select party nominees
