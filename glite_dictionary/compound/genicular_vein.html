genicular vein

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˈnɪkjələr veɪn / (AmE), / dʒɪˈnɪkjʊlə veɪn / (BrE)
any of several veins that drain blood from the structures around the knee and empty into the popliteal vein
vein near the knee that drains blood
medical

Examples

The surgeon carefully identified the genicular vein during the knee operation.
Damage to a genicular vein can lead to complications during knee replacement surgery.
Blood from the region around the knee flows into the genicular vein before reaching the popliteal vein.

Etymology

From Latin *geniculus* (knee joint), relating to the knee region.
