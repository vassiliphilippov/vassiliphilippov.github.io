conjure woman

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːn.dʒɚ ˌwʊ.mən / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.dʒə ˌwʊ.mən / (BrE)
a woman who practices conjury or folk magic, especially as a witch doctor or healer
female practitioner of conjury or folk magic

Examples

Local legends tell of a mysterious conjure woman who lived deep in the woods and healed the sick.
The villagers sought the conjure woman's advice when they suspected a curse had been placed on their crops.
As a respected conjure woman, she was known for her herbal remedies and spiritual guidance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conjurer - a person who practices conjury or performs magic
  • Synonym: witch doctor - a person who cures illness or dispels evil by magic, especially in traditional societies
  • Antonym: wizard - a man who practices magic or sorcery

Etymology

From 'conjure' (to perform magic or summon spirits) + 'woman'.
