giant sunflower

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒaɪənt ˈsʌnˌflaʊər / (AmE), / ˈdʒaɪənt ˈsʌnˌflaʊə / (BrE)
a very tall perennial sunflower of central and eastern North America, especially the United States and Canada, with edible tuberous roots
a tall North American sunflower with edible roots

Examples

Native Americans traditionally harvested the roots of the giant sunflower as a food source.
A giant sunflower can reach impressive heights in just one growing season.
The edible tubers of the giant sunflower are valued in some indigenous cuisines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sunflower - a tall plant with a large yellow flower head and edible seeds
