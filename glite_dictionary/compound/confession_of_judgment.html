confession of judgment

1. NOUN

/ kənˈfɛʃən əv ˈdʒʌdʒmənt / (AmE), / kənˈfɛʃən əv ˈdʒʌdʒm(ə)nt / (BrE)
a judgment entered by a court based on a written admission of liability by a debtor, without the need for a trial or ordinary legal proceedings
judgment based on debtor's written confession
legal

Examples

The lender required a confession of judgment in the loan agreement to protect its interests.
By signing a confession of judgment, the debtor allowed the creditor to obtain a court judgment without a lawsuit.
A confession of judgment can foreclose a debtor’s right to contest the creditor’s claim in court.
