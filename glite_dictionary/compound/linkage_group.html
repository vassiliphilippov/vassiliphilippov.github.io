linkage group

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪŋkɪdʒ ɡruːp / (AmE), / ˈlɪŋkɪdʒ ɡruːp / (BrE)
a group of genes on the same chromosome that tend to be inherited together during cell division
group of genes inherited together

Examples

In fruit flies, each linkage group corresponds to a single chromosome.
Researchers identified several linkage groups involved in the inheritance of eye color.
The genetic map shows distances between genes within the same linkage group.

Synset Examples

the genes of Drosophila fall into four linkage groups

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chromosome - thread-like structure of nucleic acids and protein carrying genetic information
  • Synonym: gene - unit of heredity; segment of DNA

Etymology

From 'linkage' + 'group', first used in early 20th-century genetics.
