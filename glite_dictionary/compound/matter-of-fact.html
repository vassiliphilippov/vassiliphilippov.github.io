matter-of-fact

1. ADJ

/ ˌmæt̬ɚ əv ˈfækt / (AmE), / ˌmæt.ər əv ˈfækt / (BrE)
Not showing emotion and dealing only with facts and practical details; straightforward and unemotional.
practical and unemotional; straightforward

Examples

Her matter-of-fact tone reassured everyone during the emergency.
He gave a matter-of-fact explanation of the incident, avoiding any drama.
Despite the shocking news, she remained matter-of-fact in her response.

Synset Examples

a matter-of-fact (or pragmatic) approach to the problem
a matter-of-fact account of the trip

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pragmatic - dealing with things sensibly and realistically
  • Antonym: emotional - showing strong feelings

Etymology

Formed from the phrase 'matter of fact,' meaning 'something that is a fact'.
