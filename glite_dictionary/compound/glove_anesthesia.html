glove anesthesia

1. NOUN

/ ɡlʌv ænəsˈθiʒə / (AmE), / ɡlʌv ænɪsˈθiːzɪə / (BrE)
a condition in which numbness or loss of sensation affects the hand and wrist in a pattern resembling a glove, typically due to psychological rather than physical causes
loss of hand sensation from psychological cause
medical

Examples

The neurologist suspected glove anesthesia when her complaints of numbness did not match any known nerve distribution.
Patients with conversion disorder may exhibit glove anesthesia during episodes of stress.
The sudden onset of glove anesthesia was found to be related to psychological trauma.

Synset Examples

since no combination of nerves serve this area, a glove anesthesia is clearly psychogenic in origin
