deep resonance

1. NOUN

/ dip ˈrɛzənəns / (AmE), / diːp ˈrɛzənəns / (BrE)
a strong emotional connection or impact that something has on a person or group
strong emotional impact or connection

Examples

Her music always has a deep resonance with listeners going through tough times.
The novel's themes found deep resonance among readers of all backgrounds.
His words carried a deep resonance that stayed with me long after the speech.

Synset Examples

The speaker's words had a deep resonance with the audience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resonance - quality of being deep, full, or reverberating, especially emotionally or acoustically
