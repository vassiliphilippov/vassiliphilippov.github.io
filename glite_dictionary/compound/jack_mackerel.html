jack mackerel

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒæk ˈmækɚəl / (AmE), / ˈdʒæk ˈmækərəl / (BrE)
a small marine fish of the Pacific coast of North America, commonly caught for food
Pacific food fish species

Examples

Fishermen often catch jack mackerel off the coast of California during the summer months.
The restaurant special includes grilled jack mackerel served with a lemon-butter sauce.
Canned jack mackerel is a popular ingredient in many Pacific coastal recipes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mackerel - a common name for various species of fast-swimming marine fish
