sinus cavernosus

1. NOUN

/ ˌsaɪnəs kæˈvɜrnəsəs / (AmE), / ˌsaɪnəs kæˈvɜːnəsəs / (BrE)
either of a pair of large venous channels located at the base of the brain, within the cranial cavity, through which blood drains from the brain
large venous sinus at base of brain
medical

Examples

The carotid artery passes through the sinus cavernosus in the cranial cavity.
An infection can sometimes spread to the sinus cavernosus, leading to serious complications.
The doctor explained the function of the sinus cavernosus during the anatomy lecture.

Etymology

From Latin 'sinus' (a curve, hollow) and 'cavernosus' (full of cavities).
