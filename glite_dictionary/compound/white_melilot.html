white melilot

1. NOUN

/ waɪt ˈmɛlɪˌlɑt / (AmE), / waɪt ˈmɛlɪˌlɒt / (BrE)
A tall biennial plant (Melilotus albus) bearing fragrant white flowers, valued as a honey plant and sometimes used as fodder.
a biennial plant with white flowers, honey source

Examples

Beekeepers often plant white melilot to provide nectar for their hives.
The meadow was filled with blooming white melilot swaying in the summer breeze.
Farmers sometimes use white melilot as green manure to enrich the soil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sweet clover - a common name for plants in the genus Melilotus, including white melilot

Etymology

Melilot from Latin *melilotos*, from Greek *meli* (honey) + *lotos* (clover, lotus).
