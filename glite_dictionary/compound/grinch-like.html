grinch-like

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡrɪntʃˌlaɪk / (AmE), / ˈɡrɪntʃˌlaɪk / (BrE)
Miserly or uncharitable, especially around celebrations; resembling the Grinch in attitude or behavior.
miserly or uncharitable, like the Grinch

Examples

He became increasingly grinch-like as the holiday season approached.
Her grinch-like refusal to participate dampened everyone's enthusiasm.
Don’t be so grinch-like; share some of your treats with us!

Synset Examples

Her grinch-like attitude spoiled the festive mood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: miserly - unwilling to spend money
  • Antonym: generous - willing to give and share unsparingly

Etymology

From 'Grinch', a character in Dr. Seuss's 1957 book 'How the Grinch Stole Christmas!', plus '-like'.
