domestic carp

1. NOUN

/ dəˈmɛstɪk kɑrp / (AmE), / dəˈmɛstɪk kɑːp / (BrE)
a large freshwater fish (Cyprinus carpio), originally from Asia, widely introduced to Europe and North America, often kept for food or ornamental purposes
large freshwater fish, often farmed for food

Examples

The domestic carp is commonly found in ponds and lakes throughout Europe.
Fishermen consider the domestic carp both a valuable catch and a nuisance, depending on the region.
Many ornamental varieties, such as koi, descend from the domestic carp.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: common carp - the same species of freshwater carp, often called by this name
