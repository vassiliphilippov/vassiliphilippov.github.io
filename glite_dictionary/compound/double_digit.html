double digit

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʌbl ˈdɪdʒɪt / (AmE), / ˈdʌbl ˈdɪdʒɪt / (BrE)
a number that has two digits, consisting of an integer from 10 to 99 inclusive
a two-digit number (10–99)

Examples

Sales rose into the double digits for the first time this quarter.
Children in school typically learn to add double digit numbers by the second grade.
The jersey number must be a double digit between 10 and 99.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: single digit - a number with only one digit (0–9)
  • Degree Higher: triple digit - a number with three digits (100–999)
