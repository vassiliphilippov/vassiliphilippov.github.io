gag law

1. NOUN

/ ɡæɡ lɔ / (AmE), / ɡæɡ lɔː / (BrE)
a law that restricts or limits the freedom of the press or the expression of opinions
law restricting freedom of the press
legal

Examples

The controversial gag law was heavily criticized by journalists and human rights organizations.
Many activists protest against any proposed gag law that would threaten press freedom.
Under the new gag law, publishing certain information has become a punishable offense.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: censorship - the suppression or prohibition of speech or writing considered harmful or sensitive
  • Antonym: free speech - the right to express opinions without censorship or restraint

2. NOUN

/ ɡæɡ lɔ / (AmE), / ɡæɡ lɔː / (BrE)
A rule or law that limits or ends debate or discussion in a legislative or deliberative body.
law restricting debate in a legislative body
legal

Examples

The parliament enacted a gag law to curb lengthy debates on controversial issues.
Opponents argue that the gag law restricts free speech and open discussion among lawmakers.
The senator criticized the introduction of a gag law as undermining democratic processes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: closure - ending a debate in a legislative assembly by calling for a vote
  • Antonym: filibuster - prolonged speech or series of speeches to delay action in a legislative assembly
