British West Africa

1. PROPN

/ ˈbrɪtɪʃ west æˈfrɪkə / (AmE), / ˈbrɪtɪʃ west ˈæfrɪkə / (BrE)
the former collective name for British territories in western Africa, including Nigeria, Cameroon, Gambia, Togo, Sierra Leone, and the Gold Coast
former collective name for British territories in western Africa

Examples

Many colonial records from British West Africa are preserved in London.
European powers drew arbitrary borders in British West Africa during the colonial period.
Post-independence leaders of British West Africa faced similar challenges transitioning to self-rule.
