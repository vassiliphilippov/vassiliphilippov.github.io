nucleolus organizer

1. NOUN

/ nuːˈkliː.ə.ləs ˈɔːr.ɡə.naɪ.zɚ / (AmE), / njuːˈkliː.ə.ləs ˈɔː.ɡə.naɪ.zə / (BrE)
the specific region of a chromosome that forms the nucleolus after cell division, containing genes for ribosomal RNA
chromosome region forming nucleolus
medical

Examples

The scientist studied the nucleolus organizer to understand its role in ribosome production.
After mitosis, the nucleolus organizer helps re-establish the nucleolus within the cell nucleus.
Mutations in the nucleolus organizer can disrupt normal cell function and growth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nucleolus organizer region - region on a chromosome responsible for nucleolus formation

Etymology

From 'nucleolus' (Latin, 'little nucleus') and 'organizer'; used in cytogenetics since early 20th century.

2. NOUN

/ nuːˈkliːoʊləs ˈɔːrɡənaɪzər / (AmE), / njuːˈkliːələs ˈɔːɡənaɪzə / (BrE)
the region of a chromosome that is associated with the formation of a nucleolus after nuclear division
chromosome region forming the nucleolus
medical

Examples

The nucleolus organizer is critical for ribosome production in the cell nucleus.
Researchers identified a mutation in the nucleolus organizer region of the chromosome.
After mitosis, the nucleolus organizer becomes active, forming a new nucleolus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nucleolus - a small dense spherical structure in the nucleus of a cell

Etymology

From 'nucleolus' (diminutive of nucleus) and 'organizer', indicating its organizing role in nucleolus formation.
