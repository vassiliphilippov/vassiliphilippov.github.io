neonatal mortality rate

1. NOUN

/ ˌnioʊˈneɪtəl mɔrˈtæl.ɪ.ti reɪt / (AmE), / ˌniːəʊˈneɪtəl mɔːˈtæl.ɪ.ti reɪt / (BrE)
the number of deaths of infants within the first 28 days of life per a specified number of live births, usually per 1,000.
infant death rate in first 28 days
medical

Examples

The neonatal mortality rate in that region has declined significantly over the past decade.
Public health officials monitor the neonatal mortality rate to assess the quality of perinatal care.
Improvements in maternal healthcare can lead to a lower neonatal mortality rate.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: infant mortality rate - the number of deaths of infants under one year per 1,000 live births
  • Synonym: maternal mortality rate - the rate of deaths of women due to pregnancy- or childbirth-related causes

Etymology

From 'neonatal' (relating to newborns, from Greek 'neos' new + 'natalis' of birth) and 'mortality rate'.
