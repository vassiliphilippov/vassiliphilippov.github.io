pneumococcal vaccine

1. NOUN

/ ˌnuːmoʊˈkɒkəl vækˈsiːn / (AmE), / ˌnjuːməˈkɒkəl vækˈsiːn / (BrE)
a vaccine used to protect against infections caused by Streptococcus pneumoniae, covering the most common strains of pneumococcus
vaccine protecting against pneumococcal infection
medical

Examples

The doctor recommended the pneumococcal vaccine for all adults over 65.
Getting a pneumococcal vaccine can help prevent serious lung infections.
Children with weakened immune systems may need additional doses of the pneumococcal vaccine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vaccine - a substance that stimulates immunity to a disease

Etymology

From 'pneumococcus' (the bacterium Streptococcus pneumoniae) and 'vaccine'.
