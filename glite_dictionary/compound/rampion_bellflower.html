rampion bellflower

1. NOUN

/ ˈræm.pi.ən ˈbɛlˌflaʊ.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈræm.pi.ən ˈbɛlˌflaʊ.ə / (BrE)
A Eurasian and North African bellflower with bluish flowers and an edible tuberous root, sometimes eaten with its leaves in salads.
bellflower with edible root and leaves

Examples

The rampion bellflower thrives in well-drained soil and blooms mid-summer with striking blue flowers.
Historically, people valued the rampion bellflower for its edible roots and medicinal properties.
She added young leaves of the rampion bellflower to her salad for a slightly sweet, earthy flavor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: campanula - genus of bellflowers, including rampion bellflower

Etymology

Rampion from Middle French 'rampon', from Italian 'raponzo'; 'bellflower' for its bell-shaped blossoms.
