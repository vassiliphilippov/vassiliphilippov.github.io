geographical zone

1. NOUN

/ dʒiˌɑɡrəˈfɪkəl zoʊn / (AmE), / dʒiˌɒɡrəˈfɪkəl zəʊn / (BrE)
any of the regions of the Earth's surface distinguished by latitude, longitude, or climatic conditions.
region of Earth's surface by latitude, longitude, or climate

Examples

The tropics are a geographical zone known for their warm weather and rich biodiversity.
Rainfall varies greatly depending on the geographical zone in which you live.
Scientists study how climate change affects each geographical zone differently.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: climatic zone - a region of the Earth distinguished by its climate
  • Synonym: time zone - a region of the Earth that has the same standard time
