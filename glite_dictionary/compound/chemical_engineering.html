chemical engineering

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɛmɪkəl ˌɛnʤəˈnɪrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkɛmɪkəl ˌɛnʤɪˈnɪərɪŋ / (BrE)
the branch of engineering concerned with the design, construction, and operation of industrial plants and machinery for chemical processes
engineering of industrial chemical processes

Examples

She decided to study chemical engineering at university because she was fascinated by industrial processes.
Advancements in chemical engineering have led to more efficient production of fuels and plastics.
Many companies hire graduates in chemical engineering to work on designing safer manufacturing plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chemical engineer - a person who works in the field of chemical engineering

Etymology

From 'chemical' (relating to chemistry) + 'engineering' (from Latin ingenium, meaning cleverness).

2. NOUN

/ ˌkɛmɪkəl ɛn.dʒəˈnɪr.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌkɛmɪkəl ˌɛn.dʒɪˈnɪə.rɪŋ / (BrE)
the branch of engineering concerned with the design and operation of industrial chemical processes and the application of chemistry to solve practical problems
branch of engineering applying chemistry

Examples

He decided to study chemical engineering at university because he was interested in both chemistry and industry.
Advances in chemical engineering have enabled the mass production of many important materials, such as plastics and fertilizers.
She works in chemical engineering, focusing on developing safer industrial processes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chemistry - the science of substances and their properties and reactions

Etymology

From chemical + engineering, coined in the late 19th century as the discipline emerged.
