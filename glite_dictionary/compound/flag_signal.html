flag signal

1. NOUN

/ flæɡ ˈsɪɡ.nəl / (AmE), / flæɡ ˈsɪɡ.nəl / (BrE)
a method of sending messages at a distance by displaying or moving flags in specific patterns, especially for communication between ships or in military contexts
method of signaling using flags

Examples

Before the use of radio, ships often relied on flag signals to convey instructions or warnings at sea.
The captain ordered a flag signal to warn the approaching vessel of danger ahead.
During ceremonies, scouts practiced their flag signal techniques to communicate across the campsite.

Synset Examples

Flag signals allowed communication at a distance before the invention of radio and are still used especially in connection with ships.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: semaphore - a system of signaling using visual signals with instruments, lights, or flags
