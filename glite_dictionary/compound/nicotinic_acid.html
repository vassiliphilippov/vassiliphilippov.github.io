nicotinic acid

1. NOUN

/ nɪkəˈtɪnɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / nɪkəˈtɪnɪk ˈasɪd / (BrE)
a form of vitamin B3; a B vitamin essential for normal nerve and digestive system function
a B vitamin (vitamin B3); essential nutrient
medical

Examples

Doctors sometimes prescribe nicotinic acid to help lower cholesterol levels in patients.
A deficiency of nicotinic acid can result in a disease called pellagra.
You can obtain nicotinic acid from foods like meat, fish, and whole grains.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: niacin - another name for nicotinic acid (vitamin B3)
  • Synonym: nicotinamide - another form of vitamin B3, often paired with nicotinic acid

Etymology

From 'nicotine', due to its derivation from nicotine, and 'acid'.
