Mount Adams

1. PROPN

/ maʊnt ˈædəmz / (AmE), / maʊnt ˈædəmz / (BrE)
a prominent mountain peak in southwestern Washington State, USA, in the Cascade Range, rising to 12,307 feet (3,751 meters)
mountain in Washington, USA

Examples

Climbers often attempt to summit Mount Adams during the summer months when the weather is more favorable.
The snow-covered slopes of Mount Adams are visible from many parts of the Pacific Northwest.
Several hiking trails around Mount Adams offer breathtaking views of the surrounding Cascade Range.

Etymology

Named after John Adams, the second President of the United States.
