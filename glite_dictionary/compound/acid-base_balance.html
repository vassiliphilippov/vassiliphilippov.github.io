acid-base balance

1. NOUN

/ ˈæsɪd ˌbeɪs ˈbæləns / (AmE), / ˈæsɪd ˌbeɪs ˈbæləns / (BrE)
the equilibrium between acids and bases in the body that keeps bodily fluids at the proper pH level
bodily equilibrium of acids and bases
medical

Examples

Maintaining a healthy acid-base balance is vital for proper cell function.
Certain diseases can disrupt your acid-base balance and make your blood too acidic or alkaline.
Doctors monitor the acid-base balance of patients in intensive care units.

Synset Examples

with a normal acid-base balance in the body the blood is slightly alkaline

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pH balance - the level of acidity or alkalinity in a substance, especially in the body

Etymology

From 'acid' (from Latin acidus, sour) and 'base' in the chemical sense, plus 'balance'.
