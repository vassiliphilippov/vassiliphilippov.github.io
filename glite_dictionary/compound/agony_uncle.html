agony uncle

1. NOUN

/ ˈæɡəni ˈʌŋkəl / (AmE), / ˈæɡəni ˈʌŋkəl / (BrE)
a man who writes a column in a newspaper or magazine giving advice to people about their personal problems
male advice columnist in newspapers
british

Examples

The newspaper recently hired a new agony uncle to help readers with their relationship problems.
As an agony uncle, he answers dozens of letters about family issues every week.
Many people find it easier to confide in an agony uncle than to talk to friends or relatives.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agony aunt - a woman who writes advice columns for personal problems

Etymology

Modeled after "agony aunt," with "uncle" denoting a male advice columnist; "agony column" originates from the late 19th century British press.
