church tower

1. NOUN

/ tʃɝtʃ ˈtaʊɚ / (AmE), / tʃɜːtʃ ˈtaʊə / (BrE)
the tall structure, often containing bells, that is part of a church building
tower of a church

Examples

The old church tower can be seen from miles away across the valley.
They climbed to the top of the church tower to enjoy the view of the town.
The bells in the church tower rang out to mark the start of the service.
