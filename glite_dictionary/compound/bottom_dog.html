bottom dog

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑːtəm dɔɡ / (AmE), / ˈbɒtəm dɒɡ / (BrE)
a person or group with low status, who is likely to lose in a struggle or competition
person of low status; underdog

Examples

As the bottom dog in the company, he often got the least desirable assignments.
Many people root for the bottom dog in a competition, hoping for an upset victory.
She knew what it felt like to be the bottom dog and worked hard to prove herself.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: underdog - a person expected to lose in a contest
  • Antonym: top dog - a person or group who is dominant or successful

Etymology

Formed by analogy with 'top dog,' referring to the loser or less dominant party in a contest.
