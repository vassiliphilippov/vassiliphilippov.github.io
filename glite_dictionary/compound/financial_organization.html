financial organization

1. NOUN

/ fəˈnænʃl ˌɔrɡənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / faɪˈnænʃl ˌɔːɡənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
an institution that collects funds and invests them in financial assets, such as banks, credit unions, or investment firms
institution managing money and investments

Examples

The bank is a type of financial organization that offers loans and savings accounts.
Many companies rely on support from a financial organization to fund their growth.
Regulations require every financial organization to report suspicious activities to the authorities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: financial institution - an establishment that focuses on financial transactions or services
