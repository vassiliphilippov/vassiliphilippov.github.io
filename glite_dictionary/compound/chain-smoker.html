chain-smoker

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃeɪn ˌsmoʊkər / (AmE), / ˈtʃeɪn ˌsməʊkə / (BrE)
A person who smokes cigarettes continuously, often lighting a new one from the end of the last.
person who smokes cigarettes one after another

Examples

My grandfather was a chain-smoker for most of his life.
The meeting room reeked because the manager is a notorious chain-smoker.
After quitting, she couldn't believe she used to be a chain-smoker.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heavy smoker - person who smokes a large number of cigarettes regularly

Etymology

Formed from 'chain' and 'smoker', implying a continuous sequence like links in a chain.
