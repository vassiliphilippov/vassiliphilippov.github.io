taxonomic category

1. NOUN

/ ˌtæksəˈnɑmɪk ˈkætəˌɡɔri / (AmE), / ˌtæksəˈnɒmɪk ˈkatɪɡəri / (BrE)
a rank or level in the biological classification system, such as species, genus, family, or order, indicating an organism's relationship to others
a biological classification rank

Examples

The species is the most basic taxonomic category in the classification of living things.
Each taxonomic category reflects evolutionary relationships among organisms.
Scientists debated whether to introduce a new taxonomic category above the kingdom level.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: taxonomic rank - level in the hierarchy of biological classification

Etymology

From 'taxonomy', from Greek 'taxis' (arrangement) + 'nomia' (method), and 'category', from Greek 'katēgoria' (accusation, predicate).
