Transvaal kafferboom

1. NOUN

/ trænzˈvɑl ˈkæfərˌbuːm / (AmE), / tranzˈvɑːl ˈkæfəˌbuːm / (BrE)
A small semi-evergreen tree of South Africa, known for dense clusters of bright scarlet flowers and red seeds.
South African tree with scarlet flowers

Examples

The Transvaal kafferboom adds a splash of red to South African gardens in the spring.
Botanists studied the Transvaal kafferboom for its strikingly colored seeds and nectar-rich flowers.
A row of Transvaal kafferbooms was planted along the avenue to attract sunbirds.

Etymology

Afrikaans: 'kafferboom' meaning 'kaffir tree', referring to Erythrina species; Transvaal is a region in South Africa. Note: 'kaffer' is now considered offensive in some contexts, though retained in some botanical names.
