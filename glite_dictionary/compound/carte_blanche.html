carte blanche

1. NOUN

/ kɑrt blɑnʃ / (AmE), / kɑːt blɑːnʃ / (BrE)
complete freedom or authority to act as one thinks best
complete freedom to act

Examples

Her boss gave her carte blanche to redesign the company's website.
The committee was granted carte blanche to choose any location for the event.
With carte blanche, the architect created a truly unique building.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: free rein - unrestricted freedom of action or decision

Etymology

From French, literally 'blank/white card', suggesting a signed blank paper to be filled in at will.
