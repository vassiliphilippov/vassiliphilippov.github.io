garden symphilid

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɑrdən ˈsɪmfɪlɪd / (AmE), / ˈɡɑːdn ˈsɪmfɪlɪd / (BrE)
a minute, soil-dwelling arthropod that infests the underground parts of garden and greenhouse crops, sometimes damaging their roots
tiny soil pest of crop roots

Examples

The roots of several vegetable plants were damaged by garden symphilids in the greenhouse.
A heavy infestation of garden symphilids can significantly reduce crop yields in commercial gardens.
To control garden symphilids, growers often practice crop rotation and improve soil drainage.

Etymology

From 'garden', referring to cultivated land, and 'symphilid', from modern Latin 'Symphyla' (a class of myriapods).
