blowing gas

1. NOUN

/ ˈbloʊɪŋ ɡæs / (AmE), / ˈbləʊɪŋ ɡas / (BrE)
gas released from a generator or furnace during the blowing process, especially in industrial or power-generating contexts.
gas leaving a generator during a blow period

Examples

Engineers monitor the composition of blowing gas during the steelmaking process.
After the blow period ended, the blowing gas was vented safely into the atmosphere.
The machinery includes filters to remove contaminants from the blowing gas before release.
