made-to-order

1. ADJ

/ ˌmeɪd tə ˈɔr.dɚ / (AmE), / ˌmeɪd tə ˈɔː.də / (BrE)
(of goods or services) produced or prepared specially according to a customer's individual requirements.
produced to a customer's specifications

Examples

She ordered a made-to-order suit tailored to her exact measurements.
The restaurant specializes in made-to-order pizzas with a wide range of toppings.
Each made-to-order piece of furniture is handcrafted by local artisans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: custom-made - specially made to order for a particular customer
  • Antonym: off-the-shelf - available ready-made, not custom-made

2. ADJ

/ ˌmeɪd tə ˈɔrdər / (AmE), / ˌmeɪd tʊ ˈɔːdə / (BrE)
(Of goods, especially clothing or food) made according to the customer's individual specifications or requirements; custom-made.
custom-made to customer specifications

Examples

The suit was made-to-order to fit his exact measurements and preferences.
They offer made-to-order pizzas with a wide choice of fresh ingredients.
Her made-to-order dress arrived just in time for the wedding.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ready-made - (Of goods) made in standard sizes or forms, not custom-made
  • Antonym: off-the-rack - (Of clothing) ready-made, not customized
  • Synonym: custom-made - made to the specifications or needs of an individual customer
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00675647-s
Synset POS: s
