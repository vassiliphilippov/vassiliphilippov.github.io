Order of Our Lady of Mount Carmel

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdər əv aʊər ˈleɪdi əv maʊnt kɑrˈmɛl / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə əv ˈaʊə ˈleɪdi əv maʊnt ˈkɑːməl / (BrE)
a Roman Catholic mendicant order founded in the 12th century, especially noted for its devotion to the Virgin Mary and contemplative practices
Roman Catholic mendicant order founded in the 12th century

Examples

The Order of Our Lady of Mount Carmel played a significant role in the spread of Carmelite spirituality.
Several historic churches in Europe were established by the Order of Our Lady of Mount Carmel.
Members of the Order of Our Lady of Mount Carmel are known for their distinctive habits and devotion to contemplation.

Etymology

From the Latin 'Ordo Dominae Nostrae de Monte Carmelo', referring to Mount Carmel in present-day Israel.
