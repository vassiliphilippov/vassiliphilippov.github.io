Bosin's disease

1. NOUN

/ ˈboʊzɪnz dɪˌziːz / (AmE), / ˈbəʊzɪnz dɪˌziːz / (BrE)
A rare, chronic, and progressive brain inflammation caused by the measles virus, usually affecting children and young adults, often leading to death within a few years.
rare chronic brain disease from measles virus
medical

Examples

Bosin's disease is characterized by a slow but relentless deterioration of the central nervous system.
Doctors struggle to treat patients diagnosed with Bosin's disease due to its rarity and severity.
Early measles infection has been linked as a cause of Bosin's disease in young children.
