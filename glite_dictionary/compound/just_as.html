just as

1. SCONJ

/ dʒʌst æz / (AmE), / dʒʌst æz / (BrE)
at the same time that something else happens; used to show two actions or events occur simultaneously.
at the same time as

Examples

The phone rang just as I was leaving the house.
She arrived just as they were closing the doors.
The lights went out just as the show was about to start.

Synset Examples

even as he lay dying they argued over his estate
the building collapsed just as he arrived

Related Terms

  • Synonym: while - during the time that something is happening
  • Synonym: as soon as - immediately at the moment that something happens
