genus Hydrobates

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs haɪˈdrɒbeɪtiːz / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs hʌɪˈdrəʊbiːtiːz / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Hydrobatidae, comprising a group of small seabirds known as storm-petrels.
type genus of Hydrobatidae (storm-petrels)

Examples

The genus Hydrobates includes various species of storm-petrels found in oceans worldwide.
Recent research has led to the reclassification of several birds within the genus Hydrobates.
Ornithologists often study the behaviors unique to the genus Hydrobates among seabirds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hydrobatidae - the family of small seabirds known as storm-petrels

Etymology

From Greek 'hydro-' (water) + 'bates' (one who treads), referring to the birds' habit of appearing to walk on water.
