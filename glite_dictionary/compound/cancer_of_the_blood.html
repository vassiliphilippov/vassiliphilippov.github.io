cancer of the blood

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæn.sɚ əv ðə blʌd / (AmE), / ˈkæn.sə əv ðə blʌd / (BrE)
A malignant disease affecting blood-forming tissues, resulting in the abnormal growth of white blood cells; commonly known as leukemia.
malignant blood disease (leukemia)
medical

Examples

After being diagnosed with cancer of the blood, she began an intensive course of chemotherapy.
Scientists are developing new treatments to fight cancer of the blood more effectively.
His family supported him through his battle with cancer of the blood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leukemia - a type of cancer affecting blood-forming tissues
