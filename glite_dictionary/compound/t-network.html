T-network

1. NOUN

/ ˈti ˌnɛt.wɜrk / (AmE), / ˈtiː ˌnɛt.wɜːk / (BrE)
an electronic circuit consisting of two series components connected by a shunt between them, forming a shape resembling the letter 'T'
circuit with T-shaped configuration

Examples

The engineer designed a T-network to match the impedance between the amplifier and the antenna.
A T-network filter is often used to control signal frequencies in radio circuits.
By adjusting the components of the T-network, the technician achieved the desired frequency response.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: pi-network - an electronic circuit arranged in the shape of the Greek letter pi (π)

Etymology

Named for the resemblance of its schematic diagram to the letter 'T'.
