cylindrical lining

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈlɪndrɪkəl ˈlaɪnɪŋ / (AmE), / sɪˈlɪndrɪkəl ˈlaɪnɪŋ / (BrE)
a cylindrical metal sleeve or layer used in machinery to reduce friction between moving parts
cylindrical metal sleeve reducing friction

Examples

The engineer replaced the worn cylindrical lining to restore the machine's smooth operation.
Regular maintenance includes checking the cylindrical lining for signs of wear or damage.
This cylindrical lining helps minimize heat generation by reducing metal-on-metal contact.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bushing - a removable cylindrical lining for an aperture to constrain, guide, or reduce friction
