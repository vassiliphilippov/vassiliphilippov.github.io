cobalt-60

1. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊˌbɔlt ˈsɪksti / (AmE), / ˈkəʊˌbɒlt ˈsɪksti / (BrE)
a radioactive isotope of cobalt with mass number 60, used as a source of intense gamma rays in radiation therapy and industry
radioactive isotope of cobalt used in therapy
medical

Examples

The hospital uses cobalt-60 to treat certain types of cancer with radiation therapy.
High-intensity gamma rays from cobalt-60 are instrumental in sterilizing medical equipment.
After the leak, authorities checked for cobalt-60 contamination around the facility.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cobalt - a hard, silvery-white, magnetic metal, chemical element with symbol Co
