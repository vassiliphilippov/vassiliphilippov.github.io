Solvay process

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɑːl.veɪ ˌprɑː.sɛs / (AmE), / ˈsɒl.veɪ ˌprəʊ.sɛs / (BrE)
an industrial method for producing sodium carbonate (soda ash) by reacting sodium chloride with ammonia and carbon dioxide
industrial method for making sodium carbonate

Examples

The Solvay process revolutionized the chemical industry by enabling large-scale production of soda ash.
Today, most of the world's sodium carbonate is made using the Solvay process.
Ammonia is recycled during the Solvay process, making it more economical and environmentally friendly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sodium carbonate - a chemical compound produced by the Solvay process

Etymology

Named after Belgian chemist Ernest Solvay (1838–1922), who invented the process.
