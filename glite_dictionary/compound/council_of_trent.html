Council of Trent

1. PROPN

/ ˈkaʊn.səl əv trɛnt / (AmE), / ˈkaʊn.səl əv trɛnt / (BrE)
the ecumenical council of the Roman Catholic Church held in Trento between 1545 and 1563, which aimed to address and condemn Protestant Reformation teachings, reform Church practices, and clarify Catholic doctrine
major Roman Catholic council, 1545–1563

Examples

The decisions made by the Council of Trent shaped the direction of the Catholic Church for centuries.
Many reforms proposed at the Council of Trent were aimed at addressing corruption within the clergy.
Catholic doctrine was clearly defined during the sessions of the Council of Trent.
