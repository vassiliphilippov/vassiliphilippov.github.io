mountain sheep

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaʊntən ʃiːp / (AmE), / ˈmaʊntɪn ʃiːp / (BrE)
any wild sheep that lives in mountainous regions, such as the bighorn sheep or mouflon.
wild sheep inhabiting mountains

Examples

The mountain sheep are well adapted to the steep slopes and rocky terrain of the highlands.
We spotted a herd of mountain sheep grazing near the alpine lake early in the morning.
Conservationists are studying the migration patterns of mountain sheep to protect their habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bighorn sheep - a North American wild sheep with large curved horns, commonly found in mountains
  • Synonym: mouflon - a wild sheep native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia
