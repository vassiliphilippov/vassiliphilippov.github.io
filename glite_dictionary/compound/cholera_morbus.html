cholera morbus

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːlərə ˈmɔːrbəs / (AmE), / ˈkɒlərə ˈmɔːbəs / (BrE)
an acute and severe type of gastroenteritis, characterized by abdominal pain, vomiting, and diarrhea, typically of unknown cause
acute severe gastroenteritis
medical archaic

Examples

In the 19th century, outbreaks of cholera morbus caused widespread fear in urban areas.
The patient was admitted with classic symptoms of cholera morbus, including severe vomiting and abdominal cramps.
Before modern medicine, cholera morbus was often mistaken for other deadly diseases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gastroenteritis - inflammation of the stomach and intestines causing vomiting and diarrhea

Etymology

From Latin 'cholera morbus', where 'cholera' means bile or disease and 'morbus' means disease.
