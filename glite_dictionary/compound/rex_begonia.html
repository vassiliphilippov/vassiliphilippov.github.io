rex begonia

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛks bəˈgoʊniə / (AmE), / ˈrɛks bɪˈɡəʊniə / (BrE)
a decorative hybrid begonia with rough-textured leaves, often patterned in silver, bronze, purple, and red-brown, grown mainly for its foliage
a decorative hybrid begonia plant

Examples

My grandmother grows a beautiful rex begonia on her windowsill, admired for its colorful foliage.
The leaves of a rex begonia are often marked with striking patterns of silver and purple.
Gardeners prize the rex begonia for its unusual leaf texture and decorative appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: begonia - a genus of flowering plants, including rex begonia

Etymology

From Latin 'rex' (king) and modern Latin 'Begonia', named after Michel Bégon, French administrator and plant collector.
