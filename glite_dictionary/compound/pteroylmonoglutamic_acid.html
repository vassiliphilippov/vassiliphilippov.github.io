pteroylmonoglutamic acid

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɛroʊˌɪlməˌnoʊˌɡluːˈtæmɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / ˌtɛrəʊˌɪlməˌnəʊˌɡluːˈtæmɪk ˈæsɪd / (BrE)
a form of vitamin B, also known as folic acid, essential for cell growth and reproduction
form of vitamin B (folic acid)
medical

Examples

Pregnant women are advised to take pteroylmonoglutamic acid supplements to prevent birth defects.
A deficiency in pteroylmonoglutamic acid can lead to anemia and other health issues.
Food fortification with pteroylmonoglutamic acid has helped reduce neural tube defects in newborns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: folic acid - common name for vitamin B9, essential for cell growth

Etymology

From Greek 'pteron' (wing) and 'glutamic acid', referring to its chemical structure.
