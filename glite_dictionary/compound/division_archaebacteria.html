division Archaebacteria

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈvɪʒən ˌɑːrkiˌbækˈtɪriə / (AmE), / dɪˈvɪʒən ˌɑːkiːˌbækˈtɪəriə / (BrE)
a taxonomic group in some classification systems, regarded as a kingdom, consisting of single-celled microorganisms distinct from bacteria and eukaryotes
group of single-celled microorganisms, considered a kingdom

Examples

Scientists study the unique adaptations of division Archaebacteria found in extreme environments.
The classification of division Archaebacteria has changed as new genetic data have become available.
Some textbooks treat division Archaebacteria as a separate kingdom from Bacteria and Eukarya.

Etymology

From Greek 'arkhaios' (ancient) and 'baktēria' (bacteria), naming the ancient, primitive form of bacteria.
