thrum of the bass

1. NOUN

/ θrʌm əv ðə beɪs / (AmE), / θrʌm ɒv ðə beɪs / (BrE)
a continuous, low vibrating sound, especially produced by bass instruments or speakers
low, continuous vibration from bass

Examples

The thrum of the bass vibrated through the floor during the concert.
She could feel the thrum of the bass even outside the nightclub.
The quiet room was disturbed by the distant thrum of the bass from a passing car.

Synset Examples

You could hear the thrum of the bass from the concert.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hum - a low, continuous sound
  • Synonym: throb - a regular, pulsing sound or sensation
