childhood mischief

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃaɪldˌhʊd ˈmɪstʃɪf / (AmE), / ˈtʃaɪldhʊd ˈmɪstʃɪf / (BrE)
Playful or mildly troublesome behavior typical of children, often regarded as innocent or harmless.
playful or troublesome behavior in childhood

Examples

He often got into childhood mischief with his cousins during the summer holidays.
Stories of childhood mischief brought a smile to the teacher's face.
Many friendships are formed through shared childhood mischief and adventures.

Synset Examples

Everyone laughed when Tio Tom recounted his childhood mischief.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mischief - playful misbehavior or troublemaking
