inflectional morphology

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˌflɛkʃənəl mɔːrˈfɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ɪnˌflɛkʃənəl mɔːˈfɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the branch of grammar concerned with the way word endings and forms change to express grammatical features such as tense, number, or case
study of word inflections in grammar

Examples

Linguists study inflectional morphology to understand how verbs change form to indicate tense.
A knowledge of inflectional morphology is essential for learning highly inflected languages such as Latin.
The textbook provides clear examples of inflectional morphology in English nouns and verbs.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: derivational morphology - the branch of morphology dealing with the formation of new words

Etymology

From Latin 'inflectere' (to bend) and Greek 'morphē' (form), via English 'morphology'.
