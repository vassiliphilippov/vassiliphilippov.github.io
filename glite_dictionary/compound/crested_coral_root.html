crested coral root

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɛstɪd ˈkɔːrəl ˌruːt / (AmE), / ˈkrɛstɪd ˈkɒrəl ˌruːt / (BrE)
a North American orchid (Corallorhiza wisteriana) with yellowish-brown flowers marked with dark veins, found from southeastern Arizona to the eastern United States
a North American orchid species

Examples

The crested coral root blooms in early spring across the woodlands of the southeastern United States.
We spotted a rare crested coral root during our hike in Arizona.
Botanists value the crested coral root for its unique yellowish-brown flowers streaked with dark veins.
