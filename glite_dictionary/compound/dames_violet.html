Dame's violet

1. NOUN

/ deɪmz ˈvaɪ.əl.ɪt / (AmE), / deɪmz ˈvaɪ.əl.ɪt / (BrE)
a perennial herb (Hesperis matronalis) with fragrant purple or white flowers, more strongly scented in the evening, cultivated and naturalized in many regions
fragrant-flowered perennial herb

Examples

The garden looked beautiful in spring with clusters of Dame's violet along the borders.
I could smell the sweet scent of Dame's violet as dusk approached.
Wild Dame's violet has spread through woodlands and roadsides in this region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sweet rocket - another common name for Hesperis matronalis

Etymology

From 'dame's' (a woman's title) and 'violet' (the flower), referencing its flower's resemblance to violets and historical association with ladies' gardens.
