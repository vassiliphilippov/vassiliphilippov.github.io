prescribed medication

1. NOUN

/ prɪˈskraɪbd ˌmɛdɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / prɪˈskraɪbd ˌmɛdɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
a medicine that is authorized and recommended for use by a doctor or other licensed healthcare professional
medicine given by a doctor
medical

Examples

The pharmacy will only dispense prescribed medication with a valid prescription from your doctor.
She must take her prescribed medication every morning to manage her condition.
Before travel, make sure you have enough prescribed medication for the entire trip.

Synset Examples

The doctor prescribed medication to treat her ulcer.
The doctor prescribed some medication for my allergy.
The doctor prescribed haldol to manage his anxiety.
The doctor prescribed methylpred to reduce her inflammation.
The doctor prescribed midaz for his anxiety before surgery.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: over-the-counter medication - medicine available without a prescription
