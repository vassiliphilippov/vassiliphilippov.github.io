judicial system

1. NOUN

/ dʒuˈdɪʃəl ˈsɪstəm / (AmE), / dʒuˈdɪʃəl ˈsɪstəm / (BrE)
the system of courts and judges responsible for interpreting laws and administering justice within a country or state
the court system for interpreting laws

Examples

The independence of the judicial system is essential to a functioning democracy.
He has spent his career studying the strengths and weaknesses of the American judicial system.
Recent reforms have aimed to make the judicial system more efficient and accessible.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: court system - the system of courts that interprets and applies the law
  • Synonym: legal system - the complete set of laws and institutions for governing a society
