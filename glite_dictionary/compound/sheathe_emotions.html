to sheathe emotions

1. VERB

/ ʃiːð ɪˈmoʊʃənz / (AmE), / ʃiːð ɪˈməʊʃənz / (BrE)
to deliberately hide or suppress one's feelings, especially to avoid displaying them outwardly
deliberately hide one's feelings

Examples

During the trial, she managed to sheathe her emotions and remain composed.
It's not healthy to always sheathe emotions instead of talking about what's bothering you.
He sheathed his emotions so well that no one knew he was upset.

Synset Examples

He decided to sheathe his emotions during the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hide one's feelings - to conceal one's emotional state
  • Antonym: express emotions - to show one's feelings openly

Etymology

From the verb 'sheathe', meaning to put a weapon into its covering, used here figuratively.
