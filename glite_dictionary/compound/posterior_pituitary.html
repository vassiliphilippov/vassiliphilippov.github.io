posterior pituitary

1. NOUN

/ pəˈstɪriər pɪˈtuːəˌtɛri / (AmE), / pɒˈstɪəriə pɪˈtjuːɪtəri / (BrE)
the back part of the pituitary gland that releases hormones such as oxytocin and vasopressin into the bloodstream
back lobe of the pituitary gland
medical

Examples

The posterior pituitary releases hormones that regulate water balance in the body.
Damage to the posterior pituitary can cause disorders like diabetes insipidus.
Researchers studied the effects of a drug on hormone secretion from the posterior pituitary.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: anterior pituitary - the front part of the pituitary gland

Etymology

From Latin 'posterior' (behind) + modern Latin 'pituitaria', relating to the pituitary gland.
