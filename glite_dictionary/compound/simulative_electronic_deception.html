simulative electronic deception

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈmjə.lə.tɪv ɪˌlɛkˈtrɑnɪk dɪˈsɛpʃən / (AmE), / sɪˈmjuː.lə.tɪv ɪˌlɛkˈtrɒnɪk dɪˈsɛpʃən / (BrE)
the use of electronic means to simulate friendly capabilities, with the purpose of misleading enemy forces about actual capabilities or intentions
simulating capabilities to mislead enemies electronically

Examples

The military employed simulative electronic deception to make the enemy believe their radar systems were more advanced than they actually were.
Advances in technology have increased the effectiveness of simulative electronic deception during training exercises.
Intelligence officers analyze reports of simulative electronic deception to adjust their defensive strategies.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: electronic deception - use of electronic means to mislead or confuse an enemy
