husk tomato

1. NOUN

/ hʌsk təˈmeɪɾoʊ / (AmE), / hʌsk təˈmɑːtəʊ / (BrE)
An edible berry of the genus Physalis, especially the tomatillo, enclosed in a papery husk.
edible berry of Physalis genus with papery husk

Examples

The garden's husk tomato plants thrived in the warm weather, producing dozens of paper-covered fruits.
She added chopped husk tomato to the salsa for a tangy flavor.
Farmers often grow husk tomato for both its unique appearance and its culinary uses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tomatillo - a small, round, edible green or purple fruit of Physalis philadelphica

2. NOUN

/ hʌsk təˈmeɪtoʊ / (AmE), / hʌsk təˈmɑːtəʊ / (BrE)
A small edible fruit similar to a tomato, typically yellow to purple, enclosed in a papery husk, belonging to the genus Physalis.
small edible fruit in a papery husk

Examples

The salsa was made with fresh husk tomatoes and chilies for a tangy flavor.
You can find husk tomatoes in many farmers' markets during the late summer season.
Each husk tomato is wrapped in a thin, papery covering that must be removed before eating.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tomatillo - a Mexican husk tomato with a tart flavor
  • Synonym: ground cherry - a plant of the genus Physalis with similar husked fruits
