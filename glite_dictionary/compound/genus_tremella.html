genus Tremella

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs trəˈmɛl.ə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs trɪˈmiː.lə / (BrE)
A genus of fungi characterized by yellowish, gelatinous fruiting bodies with convolutions resembling those of the brain.
genus of gelatinous fungi

Examples

The genus Tremella includes several jelly-like fungi often found on decaying wood.
Many members of the genus Tremella have a distinctive, brain-like appearance.
Researchers studied the reproductive cycle of fungi in the genus Tremella.

Etymology

From Latin 'tremella', meaning 'something trembling' or 'jelly', referring to the gelatinous texture.
