black hellebore

1. NOUN

/ blæk ˈhɛlɪˌbɔr / (AmE), / blæk ˈhɛlɪˌbɔː / (BrE)
a slightly hairy perennial plant with deep green, leathery leaves and purplish-green flowers, known scientifically as Helleborus niger
a perennial plant with leathery leaves and purplish-green flowers

Examples

The black hellebore is often grown in gardens for its striking winter blooms.
She identified the plant as a black hellebore by its characteristic leathery leaves.
Caution is advised, as all parts of the black hellebore are toxic if ingested.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Helleborus niger - the scientific name for black hellebore

Etymology

From Greek *helleboros*, the name for plants of this genus; 'black' refers to the color of its rootstock.

2. NOUN

/ blæk ˈhɛlɪˌbɔr / (AmE), / blæk ˈhɛlɪˌbɔː / (BrE)
A European evergreen plant (Helleborus niger) with white or purplish, rose-like flowers that bloom in winter.
European evergreen with winter flowers

Examples

The black hellebore is often among the first flowers to bloom in the winter garden.
Gardeners admire the striking appearance of black hellebore's blossoms against the snow.
Despite its beauty, black hellebore is toxic if ingested and should be handled with care.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hellebore - any plant of the genus Helleborus

Etymology

From Latin 'Helleborus niger', where 'hellebore' derives from Greek 'helleboros'.
