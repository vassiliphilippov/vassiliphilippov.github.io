gauffering iron

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɔfərɪŋ ˌaɪərn / (AmE), / ˈɡɔːfərɪŋ ˌaɪən / (BrE)
a heated metal tool used for pressing pleats and ridges, especially in fabric or lace
iron for pressing pleats and ridges

Examples

The antique gauffering iron was displayed alongside Victorian-era clothing.
She used a gauffering iron to shape the delicate pleats of the lace collar.
Craftsmen preserved traditional techniques, including the use of the gauffering iron.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pleat - a fold in fabric
  • Synonym: iron - a heated tool for pressing clothes

Etymology

From French 'gaufre' (waffle; decorated surface), referring to the textured effect produced.
