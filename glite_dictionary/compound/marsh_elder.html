marsh elder

1. NOUN

/ mɑrʃ ˈɛldər / (AmE), / mɑːʃ ˈɛldə / (BrE)
a coarse shrubby plant of the genus Iva, common in moist areas of eastern and central North America, with small greenish flowers
coarse shrubby North American plant of genus Iva

Examples

We spotted a marsh elder growing along the edge of the salt marsh.
The leaves of the marsh elder are often used in traditional remedies by local communities.
Many birds nest among the thick stands of marsh elders near the water.
