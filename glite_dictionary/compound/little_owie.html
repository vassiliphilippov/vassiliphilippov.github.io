little owie

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪt̬l ˈaʊ.i / (AmE), / ˈlɪtl ˈaʊ.i / (BrE)
A small injury or slight hurt, often used by or with children in informal contexts.
minor injury (child's term)
informal

Examples

My son fell and got a little owie on his knee, but he was brave about it.
Let me kiss your little owie and make it feel better.
Even a little owie can feel like a big deal to a child.

Synset Examples

That little 'owie' will heal in no time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: owie - a minor injury, especially as referred to by a child
  • Synonym: boo-boo - a minor injury, especially in children's speech

Etymology

From 'owie,' a child's term for a minor injury, probably imitative; 'little' added for emphasis.
