capital account

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæpɪtl əˈkaʊnt / (AmE), / ˈkapɪtəl əˈkaʊnt / (BrE)
(finance) an account that shows the net worth of a business at a specific date, typically recording the owner's equity or accumulated funds.
finance: account of business net worth

Examples

The company's capital account reflects all owner investments and withdrawals over the fiscal year.
At the end of each accounting period, the capital account indicates the net worth of the partnership.
The auditor reviewed the capital account to ensure all equity transactions were properly recorded.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: current account - account showing a firm's day-to-day financial transactions

Etymology

From 'capital' (wealth or assets) and 'account' (record in financial bookkeeping).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkæpɪtl əˌkaʊnt / (AmE), / ˈkapɪtəl əˌkaʊnt / (BrE)
(in economics) the part of a country's balance of payments that records the flow of financial assets, such as investments and loans, into and out of the country.
record of international financial flows (economics)

Examples

The government's capital account showed a significant surplus due to foreign investments.
Many developing countries struggle to keep their capital account balanced as money flows in and out.
Global economic changes can greatly affect a nation's capital account position.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: current account - part of the balance of payments recording trade in goods and services
