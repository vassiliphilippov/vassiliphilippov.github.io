whitetail jackrabbit

1. NOUN

/ ˈwaɪtˌteɪl ˈdʒækˌræbɪt / (AmE), / ˈwaɪtˌteɪl ˈdʒækˌræbɪt / (BrE)
a large North American hare found in the northern plains and western mountains of the United States, known for its seasonal coat color and distinctive white tail
large North American hare with white tail

Examples

The whitetail jackrabbit is well adapted to survive the harsh winters of the northern plains.
Farmers in the region often spot a whitetail jackrabbit bounding across the fields at dusk.
During the summer, the whitetail jackrabbit's coat turns a brownish-grey to blend in with the landscape.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jackrabbit - a large North American hare
  • Synonym: hare - a fast-running, long-eared mammal similar to a rabbit
