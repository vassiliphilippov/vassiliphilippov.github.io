higher criticism

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪər krɪˈtɪsɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈhaɪə krɪˈtɪsɪzəm / (BrE)
the academic study of biblical texts to establish their origins, authorship, and historical context
analysis of biblical texts' origins and context

Examples

She specialized in higher criticism during her theological studies.
The professor's lecture on higher criticism challenged traditional views of biblical authorship.
Debates on higher criticism often arise in academic discussions about the Bible.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: textual criticism - the study of manuscripts to determine the correct text
  • Degree Lower: lower criticism - the analysis of the physical text to recover the original wording of documents

Etymology

From 'higher', meaning 'more advanced', and 'criticism', from Greek 'kritikos' (able to discern).
