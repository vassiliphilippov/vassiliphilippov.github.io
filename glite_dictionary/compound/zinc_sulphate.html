zinc sulphate

1. NOUN

/ zɪŋk ˈsʌl.feɪt / (AmE), / zɪŋk ˈsʌl.feɪt / (BrE)
a colorless, water-soluble powder used as a mordant, wood preservative, or for the electrodeposition of zinc
a chemical compound used in various industrial processes

Examples

The solution contained zinc sulphate as an essential component for the experiment.
Tim used zinc sulphate to treat the wooden fence against decay.
Industrial processes sometimes require large quantities of zinc sulphate for electroplating.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zinc sulfate - US spelling of 'zinc sulphate'

Etymology

From 'zinc' and 'sulphate', indicating a sulphate salt of zinc; 'sulphate' from Latin 'sulphur'.
