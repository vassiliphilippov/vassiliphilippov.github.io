family Pleuronectidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli pluˌrɒnɛkˈtɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli plʊərɒnɛkˈtɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of flatfish known as righteye flounders, whose members have both eyes on the right side of the head in adulthood
a family of righteye flounders (fish)

Examples

The family Pleuronectidae includes many commercially important fish species such as the halibut and the plaice.
Marine biologists study the evolutionary adaptations of family Pleuronectidae to life on the ocean floor.
Several types of flatfish belonging to the family Pleuronectidae are known for their unique eye migration during development.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flounder - a common name for several species of flatfish, including members of Pleuronectidae
  • Degree Lower: sole - a different family of flatfish, sometimes confused with flounders

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Latin 'pleura' (side) and Greek 'nekton' (swimming), referring to the sideways-swimming nature of these fish.
