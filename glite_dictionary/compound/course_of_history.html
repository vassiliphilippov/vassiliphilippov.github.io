course of history

1. NOUN

/ kɔrs əv ˈhɪstəri / (AmE), / kɔːs əv ˈhɪstəri / (BrE)
the development and progression of important events and changes throughout history
the development of historical events

Examples

Historians debate which inventions most profoundly altered the course of history.
The discovery of penicillin changed the course of history in medicine.
Protests have sometimes shifted the course of history in unexpected ways.

Synset Examples

9/11 changed the course of history for many nations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: turning point - a decisive moment when something changes significantly
  • Antonym: status quo - the existing state of affairs, especially regarding social or political issues
