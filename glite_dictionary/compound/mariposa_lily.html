mariposa lily

1. NOUN

/ ˌmærəˈpoʊsə ˈlɪli / (AmE), / ˌmærɪˈpəʊsə ˈlɪli / (BrE)
A plant of the genus Calochortus, native to the southwestern United States and Mexico, with tulip-shaped flowers having three sepals and three petals.
species of Calochortus with tulip-like flowers

Examples

The hills were covered with blooming mariposa lilies in early spring.
A single mariposa lily stood out against the dry grassland.
Gardeners admire the mariposa lily for its delicate, butterfly-shaped blossoms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Calochortus - The genus to which mariposa lilies belong

Etymology

From American Spanish 'mariposa' meaning 'butterfly', referring to the flower's shape, and English 'lily'.
