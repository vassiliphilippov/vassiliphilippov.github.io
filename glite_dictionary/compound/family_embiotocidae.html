family Embiotocidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌfæməli ˌɛm.baɪ.əˈtɒ.sɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˌfæmɪli ˌɛm.baɪ.əˈtɒ.sɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of viviparous percoid fishes, known as surf fishes, found mainly along the Pacific coasts of North America and Asia.
a family of viviparous percoid fishes (surf fishes)

Examples

The family Embiotocidae consists of surf fishes commonly found along the Pacific coastline.
Researchers studied the reproductive strategies of the family Embiotocidae in California waters.
Members of the family Embiotocidae are known for giving birth to live young rather than laying eggs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: surfperch - common name for fish in the family Embiotocidae

Etymology

From modern Latin 'Embiotocidae', the family name based on Greek 'embios' (living in) and 'tokos' (birth).
