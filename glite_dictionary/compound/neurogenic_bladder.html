neurogenic bladder

1. NOUN

/ ˌnʊroʊˈdʒɛnɪk ˈblædər / (AmE), / ˌnjʊərəʊˈdʒɛnɪk ˈbladə / (BrE)
A disorder of the urinary bladder caused by disruption or malfunction of nerve signals that control bladder function, resulting in problems with urination.
urinary bladder disorder due to nerve problem
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed her with neurogenic bladder after she experienced frequent urinary retention.
People with spinal cord injuries are at increased risk of developing neurogenic bladder.
Treatment for neurogenic bladder often includes medication and specialized bladder training.

Etymology

From neuro- (relating to nerves) and bladder.
