storage battery

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɔɹɪdʒ ˈbæt̬əri / (AmE), / ˈstɔːrɪdʒ ˈbætəri / (BrE)
A rechargeable battery that stores electrical energy for later use, commonly used in applications such as cars and backup power systems.
rechargeable battery for storing electricity

Examples

The car's storage battery needs to be recharged after long periods of inactivity.
Solar panels can charge a storage battery to power your home at night.
They replaced the old storage battery in the backup generator.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: battery - a device that stores and produces electricity

Etymology

From 'storage' (the act of storing) + 'battery' (device producing electricity), denoting its function as a battery for storing electricity.
