yaupon holly

1. NOUN

/ ˈjoʊ.pɑn ˈhɑː.li / (AmE), / ˈjəʊ.pɒn ˈhɒl.i / (BrE)
an evergreen shrub (Ilex vomitoria) native to the southeastern United States, known for its small leaves and red berries
North American evergreen shrub (Ilex vomitoria)

Examples

The bright red berries of the yaupon holly attract birds in winter.
Many gardeners in the southern United States plant yaupon holly as an ornamental shrub.
Native Americans once brewed a caffeinated tea from the leaves of the yaupon holly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: holly - a type of shrub or small tree of the genus Ilex

Etymology

From Catawba and Creek (Muskogean) words for the plant, adopted into American English.
