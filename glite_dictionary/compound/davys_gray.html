Davy's gray

1. NOUN

/ ˈdeɪviz ɡreɪ / (AmE), / ˈdeɪvɪz ɡreɪ / (BrE)
a slightly purplish or bluish dark grey color, used especially in painting and design
a purplish or bluish dark grey color

Examples

The artist chose Davy's gray to create subtle shadows in his landscape painting.
This room looks elegant when the walls are painted in Davy's gray.
She mixed a small amount of blue into the Davy's gray to achieve the perfect tone.

Etymology

Named after Henry Davy (1793–1865), an English painter.
