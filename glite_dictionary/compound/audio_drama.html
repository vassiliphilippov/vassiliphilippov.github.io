audio drama

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔdiˌoʊ ˈdrɑmə / (AmE), / ˈɔːdiəʊ ˈdrɑːmə / (BrE)
A dramatized performance presented solely through sound, broadcast on radio or distributed via audio media such as tapes or CDs.
dramatized story performance using only sound

Examples

The BBC has produced many well-known audio dramas that captivated listeners across generations.
She enjoys listening to a science fiction audio drama on her commute every morning.
The podcast blends elements of mystery and suspense in a serialized audio drama format.

Synset Examples

An important turning point in radio drama came when Schenectady, New York's WGY, after a successful tryout on August 3, 1922, began weekly studio broadcasts of full-length stage plays in September 1922, using music, sound effects and a regular troupe of actors, The WGY Players.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: radio play - a play written specifically for broadcast on radio
  • Degree Lower: podcast - digital audio series, often episodic, distributed online

Etymology

Formed from 'audio' (relating to sound) and 'drama' (from Greek 'drama', meaning action).
