Japan Trench

1. PROPN

/ dʒəˈpæn trɛntʃ / (AmE), / dʒəˈpan trɛntʃ / (BrE)
a deep depression in the floor of the Pacific Ocean northeast of Japan, reaching depths of about 30,000 feet (9,000 meters)
deep oceanic trench northeast of Japan

Examples

The Japan Trench is known for some of the strongest earthquakes in history.
Oceanographers recently explored the extreme depths of the Japan Trench.
Tsunamis affecting Japan have often originated near the Japan Trench.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Mariana Trench - the deepest oceanic trench in the world, located in the western Pacific Ocean
