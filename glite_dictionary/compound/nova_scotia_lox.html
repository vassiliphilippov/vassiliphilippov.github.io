Nova Scotia lox

1. NOUN

/ ˌnoʊ.və ˈskoʊ.ʃə lɑks / (AmE), / ˌnəʊ.və ˈskəʊ.ʃə lɒks / (BrE)
A type of brine-cured salmon that is less salty than traditional lox; sometimes sugar is added during curing.
lightly brined salmon

Examples

The bagel was topped with Nova Scotia lox, cream cheese, and capers for an authentic taste.
She prefers Nova Scotia lox because it is less salty than other types of cured salmon.
Their deli offers a special platter featuring Nova Scotia lox alongside pickled onions and tomatoes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lox - brined salmon fillet, usually served sliced

Etymology

From Nova Scotia, Canada, where this style of lox originates; 'lox' from Yiddish 'laks' (salmon).
