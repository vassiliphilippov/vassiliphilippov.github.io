Scotch malt whiskey

1. NOUN

/ skɑtʃ mɔlt ˈwɪski / (AmE), / skɒtʃ mɔːlt ˈwɪski / (BrE)
Whiskey distilled in Scotland, especially one made from malted barley in a pot still.
whiskey made from malted barley in Scotland

Examples

The aging process gives Scotch malt whiskey its distinctive smoothness and rich flavor.
He prefers Scotch malt whiskey over blended varieties for its pure barley taste.
A fine Scotch malt whiskey can be enjoyed neat to savor its complexity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Scotch whisky - whisky produced in Scotland
  • Antonym: bourbon - American whiskey made primarily from corn

Etymology

Scotch, referring to Scotland, and whiskey, from Irish Gaelic 'uisce beatha' (water of life).
