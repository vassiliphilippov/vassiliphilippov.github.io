tetradecanoic acid

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɛtrədiˈkeɪniɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / ˌtɛtrədɪˈkeɪnɪk ˈasɪd / (BrE)
a saturated fatty acid found naturally in animal and plant fats, also known as myristic acid
a saturated fatty acid (myristic acid)
medical

Examples

Tetradecanoic acid is present in coconut oil and palm kernel oil.
The laboratory analysis detected traces of tetradecanoic acid in the sample.
Tetradecanoic acid is an important component in the synthesis of some soaps.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: myristic acid - the common name for tetradecanoic acid, a saturated fatty acid

Etymology

From Greek 'tetra' (four) + 'deka' (ten), indicating fourteen carbon atoms.
