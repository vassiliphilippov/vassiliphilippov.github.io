ransom money

1. NOUN

/ ˈrænsəm ˌmʌni / (AmE), / ˈrɑːnsəm ˌmʌni / (BrE)
money demanded or paid for the release of someone who has been kidnapped or captured
money demanded for a captive's release

Examples

The kidnappers demanded ransom money before agreeing to release their hostage.
Police tracked the serial numbers on the ransom money used in the exchange.
She delivered the ransom money to a designated location as instructed by the criminals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ransom - a sum of money demanded or paid for the release of a prisoner
