time sharing

1. NOUN

/ taɪm ˈʃɛrɪŋ / (AmE), / taɪm ˈʃeə.rɪŋ / (BrE)
A method in computer science that enables multiple users to access and use the resources of a central computer at the same time.
multi-user computer resource sharing

Examples

The concept of time sharing revolutionized computing in the 1960s.
Many universities adopted time sharing to maximize the use of expensive mainframe computers.
Modern cloud computing evolved from early time sharing systems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: multiprocessing - the use of two or more CPUs within a single computer system
