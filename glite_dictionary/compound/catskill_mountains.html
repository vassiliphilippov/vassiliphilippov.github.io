Catskill Mountains

1. PROPN

/ ˈkætskɪl ˈmaʊntənz / (AmE), / ˈkætskɪl ˈmaʊntənz / (BrE)
a mountain range in southeastern New York, west of the Hudson River, known for its scenic beauty and popular resort areas
mountain range in southeastern New York, USA

Examples

Every summer, tourists flock to the Catskill Mountains for hiking and relaxation.
The Catskill Mountains offer breathtaking views in autumn when the leaves change color.
Many artists have been inspired by the landscapes of the Catskill Mountains.

Etymology

Named after the Catskill region; 'Catskill' possibly derives from Dutch 'Kaaterskill' (Kaaters [pronounced as cats] creek/stream).
