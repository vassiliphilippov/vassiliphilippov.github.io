family Ulvaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ʌlˈveɪsiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli ʌlˈveɪsiː / (BrE)
A family of green algae that are typically thin, flat, or tubular in form, commonly found in marine and freshwater environments.
family of green algae

Examples

The family Ulvaceae includes many types of green seaweed, such as sea lettuce.
Marine biologists collected specimens from the family Ulvaceae to study their ecological role.
Some members of the family Ulvaceae are edible and used in salads and soups.

Etymology

From Latin 'Ulva', a type of sea lettuce, plus the standard botanical family suffix '-aceae'.
