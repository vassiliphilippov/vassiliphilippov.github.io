high table

1. NOUN

/ haɪ ˈteɪbəl / (AmE), / haɪ ˈteɪbl̩ / (BrE)
A dining table in a hall, especially in a college or similar institution, raised on a platform and reserved for distinguished guests or senior members.
table for distinguished persons in dining hall
british

Examples

Professors and their guests dined at the high table while students sat below.
During the formal dinner, only faculty were allowed to sit at the high table.
Many traditions are associated with the high table in British universities.
