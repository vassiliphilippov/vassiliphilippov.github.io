prickly ash

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɪkli æʃ / (AmE), / ˈprɪkli æʃ / (BrE)
An Australian tree with alternate simple leaves (pinnate and prickly-toothed when young) and slender spikes of white flowers.
Australian tree with prickly-toothed leaves and white flowers

Examples

The bark of the prickly ash is sometimes used in traditional remedies.
A prickly ash was planted in the botanical garden for educational purposes.
Young prickly ash trees have leaves with sharp, toothed margins.

2. NOUN

/ ˈprɪkli ˌæʃ / (AmE), / ˈprɪkli ˌaʃ / (BrE)
Any of several trees or shrubs of the genus Zanthoxylum, characterized by spiny branches, found in temperate and subtropical regions.
spiny tree/shrub (Zanthoxylum genus)

Examples

The bark of the prickly ash has long been used in traditional medicine.
Several species of prickly ash are cultivated for their distinctive aromatic qualities.
The spiny branches of prickly ash trees can make them difficult to handle without gloves.
