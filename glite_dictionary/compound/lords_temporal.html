Lords Temporal

1. NOUN

/ lɔrdz ˈtɛmpərəl / (AmE), / lɔːdz ˈtɛmpərəl / (BrE)
The secular members of the British House of Lords, as distinguished from the Lords Spiritual; historically, also secular nobility in France.
secular nobility in British and French peerages

Examples

The Lords Temporal debated the new legislation with passion and authority.
Unlike the bishops, the Lords Temporal inherit their seats or are appointed for life.
In the past, the Lords Temporal played a central role in British political life.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Lords Spiritual - the bishops who are members of the British House of Lords

Etymology

From Latin 'temporalis' (secular), contrasting with church or spiritual authority.
