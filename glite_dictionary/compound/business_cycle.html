business cycle

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɪznɪs ˌsaɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈbɪznɪs ˌsaɪkəl / (BrE)
A cycle of recurring periods of economic expansion and contraction in an economy.
recurring pattern of economic expansion and contraction

Examples

The government implements policies to try to smooth the business cycle and reduce unemployment.
Economists study the business cycle to predict periods of slowdown or growth.
During every business cycle, some industries perform better than others.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recession - a period of temporary economic decline
  • Degree Higher: boom - a period of rapid economic growth
