Rip van Winkle

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɪp væn ˈwɪŋkəl / (AmE), / ˌrɪp van ˈwɪŋkl̩ / (BrE)
a person who is oblivious to or unaware of social or political changes occurring around them, often for a long period
person oblivious to social changes

Examples

He was called a Rip van Winkle for his ignorance of the latest technology trends.
After years abroad, she returned home a Rip van Winkle in a changed society.
Don't be a Rip van Winkle; stay informed about what's happening in the world.

Etymology

From the name of the title character in Washington Irving’s 1819 story, who sleeps for 20 years and misses significant changes.

3. NOUN

/ ˌrɪp væn ˈwɪŋkəl / (AmE), / ˌrɪp van ˈwɪŋkl̩ / (BrE)
a person who sleeps a lot or appears to be oblivious to changes over a long period, referencing the fictional character who slept for twenty years
someone who sleeps a lot or misses change

Examples

Since college, he's become such a Rip van Winkle, sleeping until noon every weekend.
The new manager seemed like a Rip van Winkle, unaware of all the changes that had taken place.
After her long illness, she felt like a Rip van Winkle returning to a world that had moved on without her.

Etymology

From the character Rip Van Winkle in Washington Irving's 1819 short story, who slept for twenty years.
