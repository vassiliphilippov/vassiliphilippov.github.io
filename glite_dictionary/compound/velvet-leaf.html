velvet-leaf

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɛlvət ˌlif / (AmE), / ˈvɛlvɪt ˌliːf / (BrE)
a tall annual herb or subshrub (Abutilon theophrasti) with soft, velvety leaves and yellow flowers, yielding strong fiber; native to tropical Asia and widely naturalized elsewhere
herbaceous plant with velvety leaves

Examples

Farmers often consider velvet-leaf a troublesome weed because it spreads quickly among crops.
The large, soft leaves of the velvet-leaf plant feel almost like velvet to the touch.
In the late summer, fields bloom with the yellow flowers of velvet-leaf.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Abutilon theophrasti - scientific name for velvet-leaf

2. NOUN

/ ˈvɛlvət liːf / (AmE), / ˈvɛlvɪt liːf / (BrE)
A perennial shrub with palmately lobed, furry leaves and showy red-purple flowers, native to the southwestern United States.
perennial shrub with furry leaves and red-purple flowers

Examples

The garden's most striking plant was the velvet-leaf, with its large, soft foliage and vivid blossoms.
Botanists identified several velvet-leaf specimens growing along the arroyo in Arizona.
The velvet-leaf thrives in dry climates, making it well-suited to desert landscaping.
