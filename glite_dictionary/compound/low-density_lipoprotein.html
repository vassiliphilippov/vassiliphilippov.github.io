low-density lipoprotein

1. NOUN

/ loʊ ˈdɛnsəti ˌlaɪpəˈproʊtin / (AmE), / ləʊ ˈdɛnsəti ˌlɪpəˈprəʊtiːn / (BrE)
a type of lipoprotein in the blood that carries cholesterol, high levels of which are linked to increased risk of heart disease and atherosclerosis
lipoprotein that carries cholesterol in blood
medical

Examples

Doctors often check your cholesterol by measuring your low-density lipoprotein levels.
A diet high in saturated fats can increase low-density lipoprotein in your bloodstream.
Reducing low-density lipoprotein may help lower the risk of heart disease.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: LDL - abbreviation for low-density lipoprotein
  • Antonym: high-density lipoprotein - a lipoprotein that removes cholesterol from the arteries; considered 'good' cholesterol

Etymology

From 'low-density' (referring to mass per volume) and 'lipoprotein' (protein combined with fat/lipid).
