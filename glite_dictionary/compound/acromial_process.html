acromial process

1. NOUN

/ əˈkroʊmiəl ˈprɑːsɛs / (AmE), / əˈkrəʊmɪəl ˈprəʊsɛs / (BrE)
the outermost point of the spine of the shoulder blade (scapula), forming the tip of the shoulder
outermost point of the shoulder blade
medical

Examples

The surgeon made an incision near the acromial process to access the shoulder joint.
A fracture of the acromial process can cause severe pain and limit arm movement.
X-rays revealed a small bone spur forming on the acromial process.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acromion - the bony process on the scapula forming the highest point of the shoulder

Etymology

From Greek akrōmion ('highest point of the shoulder') + process (anatomical projection).
