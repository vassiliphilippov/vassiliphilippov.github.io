paternity leave

1. NOUN

/ pəˈtɝːnəti liːv / (AmE), / pəˈtɜːnəti liːv / (BrE)
time off work that a father is allowed to take following the birth or adoption of his child, usually with pay or partial pay
leave for fathers after child's birth or adoption

Examples

He decided to take paternity leave to help care for the newborn twins.
Many companies now offer paid paternity leave to support new fathers.
After his child was born, he applied for paternity leave to spend more time with his family.

Synset Examples

Don't worry Petunia, I'll take a paternity leave to help you take care of Dudley.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: maternity leave - leave granted to mothers after childbirth or adoption

Etymology

From 'paternity' (from Latin 'paternitas', fatherhood) + 'leave' (time away from work).

2. NOUN

/ pəˈtɝː.nə.t̬i liːv / (AmE), / pəˈtɜː.nə.ti liːv / (BrE)
a period of paid or unpaid absence from work that a father is allowed after the birth of his child
time off work for new fathers

Examples

After the birth of his daughter, Jacob took paternity leave to help at home.
Some companies offer paid paternity leave, while others provide only unpaid time off.
More men are choosing to take paternity leave to spend time with their newborns.

Synset Examples

Paternity leave allows fathers to bond with their newborns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maternity leave - leave from work for mothers after childbirth

Etymology

From 'paternity' (from Latin 'paternitas', fatherhood) and 'leave' (from Old English 'lǣf', permission).
