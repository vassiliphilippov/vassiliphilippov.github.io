striped coral root

1. NOUN

/ straɪpt ˈkɔːrəl ˌruːt / (AmE), / straɪpt ˈkɒrəl ˌruːt / (BrE)
a nearly leafless wildflower with reddish-purple stems and racemes of pale pinkish and brownish-striped flowers, found from western Canada to Mexico
wildflower with striped flowers and reddish-purple stems

Examples

The striped coral root blooms in shaded woodlands during late spring.
We photographed a cluster of striped coral root near the forest trail.
Botanists are studying how the striped coral root adapts to different soil conditions.
