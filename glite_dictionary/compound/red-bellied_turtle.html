red-bellied turtle

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɛd ˈbɛlid ˈtɝtəl / (AmE), / ˌrɛd ˈbɛlɪd ˈtɜːtl̩ / (BrE)
a freshwater turtle native to the Chesapeake Bay tributaries, distinguished by red markings on its lower shell
freshwater turtle with a red underside

Examples

The red-bellied turtle can often be seen basking on logs along the rivers of the Chesapeake Bay.
Local conservationists have worked to protect the habitat of the red-bellied turtle.
Children were excited to spot a red-bellied turtle during their field trip to the nature reserve.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: turtle - a reptile with a hard shell that lives in or near water
