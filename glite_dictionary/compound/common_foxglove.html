common foxglove

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmən ˈfɑksˌɡlʌv / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən ˈfɒksˌɡlʌv / (BrE)
A tall European plant (Digitalis purpurea) with clusters of large tubular pink-purple flowers, whose leaves are used to produce digitalis and can be poisonous to livestock.
European plant with pink-purple flowers; source of digitalis

Examples

The garden was full of common foxglove, its tall spires covered in vibrant purple blossoms.
Farmers are cautious about common foxglove because its leaves can be poisonous to sheep and cattle.
The leaves of the common foxglove are a vital source for the heart medication digitalis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: digitalis - Plant genus used in medicine; includes common foxglove
  • Degree Higher: deadly nightshade - Poisonous European plant also known for medicinal uses

Etymology

'Foxglove' from Old English 'foxes glofe' (the glove of a fox).
