spinal column

1. NOUN

/ ˈspaɪnəl ˈkɑləm / (AmE), / ˈspaɪnəl ˈkɒləm / (BrE)
the series of vertebrae extending from the skull to the lower back, forming the axis of the skeleton and enclosing the spinal cord
series of bones in the backbone
medical

Examples

Injuries to the spinal column can lead to serious complications, including paralysis.
The human spinal column consists of thirty-three vertebrae separated by intervertebral discs.
He wore a brace to support his spinal column during recovery from the car accident.

Synset Examples

the fall broke his back

Related Terms

  • Synonym: backbone - the main support structure of the skeleton running along the back
  • Synonym: vertebral column - the bony structure consisting of the vertebrae that encloses the spinal cord

Etymology

From Latin 'spina' (thorn, backbone) + 'column' (pillar, support).

2. NOUN

/ ˈspaɪ.nəl ˈkɑː.ləm / (AmE), / ˈspaɪ.nəl ˈkɒl.əm / (BrE)
the series of vertebrae extending from the skull to the lower back, enclosing the spinal cord; the backbone
the backbone; the vertebral column
medical

Examples

She was advised to rest after injuring her spinal column in the accident.
The surgeon explained the importance of protecting the spinal column during the procedure.
Damage to the spinal column can result in loss of mobility.

Synset Examples

She injured her spinal column while playing basketball.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: backbone - the column of bones supporting the body from the skull to the pelvis
  • Synonym: vertebral column - the bony structure forming the central axis of the skeleton

Etymology

From Latin 'spina' (thorn, backbone) and 'column(a)' (pillar).
