chicken provencale

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɪk.ɪn prɒˌvɑ̃ːˈsɑːl / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪk.ɪn prɒˌvɒnˈsɑːl / (BrE)
A dish consisting of chicken cooked in a sauce made with tomatoes, garlic, and olive oil, often with herbs typical of Provence.
French chicken dish with tomato, garlic, and olive oil sauce

Examples

For dinner, she prepared chicken provencale with fresh herbs from her garden.
The restaurant's signature dish is chicken provencale, served with rustic bread.
I decided to try making chicken provencale after watching a French cooking show.

Etymology

French Provençal, from Provence, a region in southeastern France known for its cuisine.
