cliff dwelling

1. NOUN

/ klɪf ˈdwɛlɪŋ / (AmE), / klɪf ˈdwɛlɪŋ / (BrE)
A type of shelter or house built into the sides of cliffs, often made of stone and adobe, typically on natural ledges and associated especially with ancient peoples of North America.
house built into the side of a cliff

Examples

The ancient Puebloans are famous for their impressive cliff dwellings in the American Southwest.
We visited a national park to see well-preserved cliff dwellings built centuries ago.
Archaeologists study cliff dwellings to learn about the daily lives of early Native American civilizations.

Synset Examples

the Anasazi built cliff dwellings in the southwestern United States
