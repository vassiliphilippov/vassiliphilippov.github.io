slagging comment

1. NOUN

/ ˈslæɡɪŋ ˈkɑmɛnt / (AmE), / ˈslæɡɪŋ ˈkɒmɛnt / (BrE)
a remark intended to criticize or insult someone
derogatory remark; insult
british

Examples

Everyone was tired of his slagging comments during the meeting.
She apologized for the slagging comment she made earlier.
Online discussions often attract unnecessary slagging comments from anonymous users.

Synset Examples

He was criticized for his slagging comments about the project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insult - a disrespectful or scornful remark
  • Antonym: compliment - a polite expression of praise or admiration

Etymology

'Slag' as a verb meaning 'to criticize' is chiefly British, from slang for casting off waste ('slag') in metalwork.
