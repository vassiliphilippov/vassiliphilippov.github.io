cutaneous senses

1. NOUN

/ kjuːˈteɪniəs ˈsɛnsɪz / (AmE), / kjuːˈteɪnɪəs ˈsɛnsɪz / (BrE)
the faculty by which outside objects or forces are perceived through contact with the skin, especially the hands; the sense of touch
sense by which touch is perceived
medical

Examples

The cutaneous senses allow us to feel pressure, pain, and temperature on our skin.
Damage to the nervous system can impair the cutaneous senses.
Infants rely heavily on their cutaneous senses to explore the world around them.

Synset Examples

only sight and touch enable us to locate objects in the space around us

Related Terms

  • Synonym: touch - the sense by which physical contact with objects is perceived

Etymology

From Latin cutaneus (of the skin) + senses, from Latin sensus (perception, feeling).
