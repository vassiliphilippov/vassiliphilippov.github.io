2 Pallas

1. PROPN

/ tuː ˈpæləs / (AmE), / tuː ˈpæləs / (BrE)
the second asteroid to be discovered, one of the largest in the asteroid belt, officially designated as '2 Pallas'
large asteroid, second discovered

Examples

The orbit of 2 Pallas is inclined compared to most main belt asteroids.
Astronomers first observed 2 Pallas in 1802, shortly after the discovery of Ceres.
With its irregular shape, 2 Pallas stands out among the largest asteroids.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: asteroid - a small rocky body orbiting the sun, mainly in the asteroid belt

Etymology

The name 'Pallas' comes from Greek mythology (an epithet for Athena); the '2' indicates its discovery order among asteroids.
