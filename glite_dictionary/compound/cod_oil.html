cod oil

1. NOUN

/ kɑd ɔɪl / (AmE), / kɒd ɔɪl / (BrE)
An inferior oil obtained from cod, especially used in leather manufacturing and other industrial applications.
oil from cod used industrially

Examples

The tannery preferred cod oil for treating leather goods due to its specific properties.
Industrial use of cod oil expanded as it became a more affordable alternative to other fish oils.
Unlike medicinal cod-liver oil, cod oil is not intended for human consumption.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cod-liver oil - an oil extracted from the livers of cod, often used as a dietary supplement
