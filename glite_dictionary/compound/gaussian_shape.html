Gaussian shape

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡaʊsiən ʃeɪp / (AmE), / ˈɡaʊsiən ʃeɪp / (BrE)
A symmetrical bell-shaped curve representing the normal distribution in mathematics and statistics.
bell-shaped curve of a normal distribution

Examples

The data points fit nicely to a Gaussian shape on the graph.
The scientist observed a clear Gaussian shape in the experimental results.
Any deviation from a Gaussian shape might suggest an abnormality in the distribution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bell curve - a graph with the shape of a bell, representing a normal distribution

Etymology

From Carl Friedrich Gauss, the German mathematician, whose work on the normal distribution inspired the term.
