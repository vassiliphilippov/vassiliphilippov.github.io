Mandarin Chinese

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæn.dəˈrɪn tʃaɪˈniːz / (AmE), / ˌmæn.dəˈrɪn tʃaɪˈniːz / (BrE)
the standard form of Chinese spoken in Beijing and used as the official language of China
the official standard Chinese language

Examples

She studied Mandarin Chinese for two years before traveling to Beijing.
Government documents in China are published in Mandarin Chinese.
His parents speak Cantonese, but he is fluent in Mandarin Chinese.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Putonghua - the Chinese word for standard Mandarin Chinese spoken in China
  • Antonym: Cantonese - a major Chinese dialect spoken in Guangdong and Hong Kong

Etymology

Mandarin from Portuguese 'mandarim', from Malay 'menteri' (counselor), and 'Chinese' referring to China.
