prairie dog

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɛri dɔɡ / (AmE), / ˈpreəri dɒɡ / (BrE)
a burrowing rodent native to the grasslands of North America, known for its complex colonies and barking call
burrowing North American rodent

Examples

On our trip to South Dakota, we watched a colony of prairie dogs darting in and out of their tunnels.
The park ranger explained how prairie dogs communicate with each other using high-pitched barks.
Farmers sometimes view prairie dogs as pests because their burrows can damage crops and equipment.

2. NOUN

/ ˈprɛri dɔɡ / (AmE), / ˈpreəri dɒɡ / (BrE)
a small North American rodent that lives in large colonies in burrows on grasslands
North American burrowing rodent

Examples

We spotted a prairie dog standing upright to keep watch for danger.
The grasslands are home to thousands of prairie dogs living in underground colonies.
A prairie dog barked sharply and darted back into its tunnel.

Synset Examples

The prairie dog emerged from its burrow, looking around.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: groundhog - a burrowing North American rodent, also known as a woodchuck
