mother wit

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌðɚ wɪt / (AmE), / ˈmʌðə wɪt / (BrE)
natural common sense or practical intelligence that one is born with, rather than acquired through education
native common sense; practical intelligence

Examples

She relied on her mother wit to get through difficult situations when formal education fell short.
Although he lacked formal schooling, his mother wit made him respected in the village.
In emergencies, mother wit often proves more valuable than years of training.

Synset Examples

Common sense is not so common
he hasn't got the sense God gave little green apples
fortunately she had the good sense to run away

Related Terms

  • Synonym: common sense - good sense and sound judgment in practical matters
  • Antonym: book learning - knowledge acquired through study, as opposed to practical experience

Etymology

From 'mother' (as origin or source) and 'wit' (Old English 'witan', to know); meaning natural, innate sense.
