Emperor of Rome

1. PROPN

/ ˈɛmpərɚ əv roʊm / (AmE), / ˈɛmpərə ɒv rəʊm / (BrE)
the sovereign ruler of the Roman Empire, especially during the period from Augustus to the fall of the Western Empire
sovereign ruler of the Roman Empire

Examples

Augustus was the first Emperor of Rome and established a long period of relative peace.
The Emperor of Rome held supreme authority over the Senate and the army.
Historians debate the exact role of the Emperor of Rome in daily life during the empire.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Roman Emperor - ruler of the Roman Empire
  • Synonym: emperor - a sovereign ruler of great power and rank, especially one ruling an empire
  • Antonym: king - the male ruler of an independent state, especially one who inherits the position by right of birth
