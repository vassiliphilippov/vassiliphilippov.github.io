inherent aptitude

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈhɛrənt ˈæptɪˌtjud / (AmE), / ɪnˈhɪərənt ˈæptɪˌtjuːd / (BrE)
An innate tendency or ability present from birth, especially one that prompts a particular response to certain stimuli.
innate ability or tendency

Examples

Even from a young age, birds exhibit an inherent aptitude for building complex nests.
Scientists believe that the desire to help others may stem from an inherent aptitude for altruistic behavior.
The salmon's ability to navigate vast distances to their birthplace is driven by an inherent aptitude for migration.

Synset Examples

the spawning instinct in salmon
altruistic instincts in social animals

Related Terms

  • Synonym: instinct - an innate, typically fixed pattern of behavior in animals in response to certain stimuli

Etymology

From Latin 'inhaerent-' (adhering to) and Latin 'aptitudo' (fitness, ability).
