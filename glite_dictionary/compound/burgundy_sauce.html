Burgundy sauce

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɜːrɡəndi sɔs / (AmE), / ˈbɜːɡəndi sɔːs / (BrE)
A rich sauce made from red wine (typically Burgundy), onions, parsley, thyme, and butter, often served with meats.
rich red wine sauce with herbs

Examples

The steak was served with a decadent Burgundy sauce that enhanced its flavor.
She prepared a classic Burgundy sauce to accompany the roasted chicken.
He reduced red wine to make a homemade Burgundy sauce for the beef.

Etymology

Named after Burgundy, a region in France known for its wines.
