granadilla wood

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡrænəˈdɪlə wʊd / (AmE), / ˌɡranəˈdɪlə wʊd / (BrE)
A dense, dark wood from the granadilla tree, valued for making musical instruments, especially clarinets.
wood used for musical instruments, esp. clarinets

Examples

The body of the clarinet is traditionally crafted from granadilla wood for its superior acoustic properties.
Some musicians prefer instruments made of granadilla wood because of its durability and tone.
Due to overharvesting, sourcing high-quality granadilla wood has become more challenging for instrument makers.

Etymology

From Spanish 'granadilla', diminutive of 'granada' (pomegranate), used for the tree's fruit and wood.

2. NOUN

/ ˌgrænəˈdɪlə wʊd / (AmE), / ˌgranəˈdɪlə wʊd / (BrE)
A dense, dark red hardwood, derived from certain tropical trees such as cocobolo, used especially in making musical instruments like clarinets.
dark hardwood used for musical instruments

Examples

The clarinet was crafted from granadilla wood for its rich, warm tone.
Some flutes and oboes are made using granadilla wood due to its durability and resonance.
Instrument makers prize granadilla wood for making high-quality woodwind instruments.

Etymology

From Spanish 'granadilla', diminutive of 'granada' (pomegranate), referring to several tropical trees and their fruits.
