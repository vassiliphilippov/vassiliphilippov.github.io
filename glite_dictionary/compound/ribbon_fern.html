ribbon fern

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɪb.ən fɝn / (AmE), / ˈrɪb.ən fɜːn / (BrE)
a fern found in North Africa, the Azores, and the Canary Islands, known for its long, slender, ribbon-like fronds
fern with ribbon-like fronds

Examples

The ribbon fern is often admired for its delicate, flowing leaves in shaded gardens.
A collection of ribbon ferns was planted in the conservatory for their unique appearance.
Because the ribbon fern prefers moist conditions, keep its soil damp but not waterlogged.

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɪb.ən fɝn / (AmE), / ˈrɪb.ən fɜːn / (BrE)
A species of epiphytic fern found in lowland forests of tropical America, known for its long, ribbon-like fronds.
a tropical epiphytic fern species

Examples

The ribbon fern thrives in the humid canopies of Central American rainforests.
Many collectors prize the delicate appearance of the ribbon fern for their indoor gardens.
Researchers discovered a new species of ribbon fern growing on the branches of ancient trees.

3. NOUN

/ ˈrɪb.ən fɝn / (AmE), / ˈrɪb.ən fɜːn / (BrE)
A tropical epiphytic fern with long, straplike, often twisted fronds, native to Asia, Polynesia, and America.
a tropical fern with narrow, twisted fronds

Examples

The ribbon fern thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often grown as a houseplant.
Botanists admire the way a ribbon fern's fronds twist and drape elegantly from tree branches.
She decorated her living room with a delicate ribbon fern to add a touch of greenery.
