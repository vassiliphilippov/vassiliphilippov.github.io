off year

1. NOUN

/ ɑf jɪr / (AmE), / ɒf jɪə / (BrE)
A year in which productivity, success, or activity is lower than average.
year of lower-than-usual productivity or activity

Examples

After last season's record harvest, this was an off year for the vineyard.
The company experienced an off year, with profits lower than expected.
It's common for fruit trees to alternate between a strong year and an off year.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: banner year - a year of exceptional success or growth

2. NOUN

/ ˈɔf ˌjɪr / (AmE), / ˈɒf ˌjɪə / (BrE)
A year in which no major political elections are held, especially between years of general or presidential elections.
year without major elections

Examples

Congress usually deals with less controversial legislation during an off year.
Voter turnout tends to be much lower in an off year compared to a presidential election year.
The mayor used the off year to focus on local projects and community outreach.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: election year - a year in which major political elections are held
