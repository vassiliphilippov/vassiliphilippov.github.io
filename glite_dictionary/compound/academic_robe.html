academic robe

1. NOUN

/ əˈkædəmɪk roʊb / (AmE), / əˈkadəmɪk rəʊb / (BrE)
a long, loose-fitting gown worn by academics or judges, typically on formal occasions such as graduation ceremonies or in court
formal gown worn by academics or judges

Examples

At the graduation ceremony, each professor wore an academic robe over their regular clothing.
Judges are required to don the traditional academic robe in court during important cases.
The university's oldest academic robe is displayed in the archival museum.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gown - a long robe worn on formal occasions
