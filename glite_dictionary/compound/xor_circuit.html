XOR circuit

1. NOUN

/ ɛks ɔr ˈsɝː.kɪt / (AmE), / ɛks ɔː ˈsɜː.kɪt / (BrE)
A type of digital logic circuit that outputs true only when an odd number of its inputs are true, especially one that implements the exclusive OR operation.
digital logic circuit for exclusive OR

Examples

The XOR circuit is essential for performing binary addition in many digital systems.
When troubleshooting the malfunctioning controller, the engineer suspected a faulty XOR circuit.
In this assignment, students will design an XOR circuit from basic logic gates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exclusive OR - a logical operation outputting true only when inputs differ
  • Antonym: AND gate - a logic circuit that outputs true only if all inputs are true

Etymology

Short for 'exclusive OR' circuit, from logic and computing terminology.
