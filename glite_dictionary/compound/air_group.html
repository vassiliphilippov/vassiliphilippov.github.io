air group

1. NOUN

/ ɛr ɡruːp / (AmE), / eə ɡruːp / (BrE)
a unit of the United States Air Force that is larger than a squadron and smaller than a wing
military unit in the Air Force (US)

Examples

The base commander was responsible for coordinating three different air groups during the exercise.
After the reorganization, the air group received additional fighter squadrons.
The policies outlined new roles for each air group within the wing's operational structure.
