Lancastrian line

1. PROPN

/ lænˈkæstriən laɪn / (AmE), / laŋˈkɑːstriən laɪn / (BrE)
the English royal house that reigned from 1399 to 1461, whose emblem was a red rose
English royal house (1399–1461)

Examples

Many historians studied the succession crisis that occurred after the fall of the Lancastrian line.
The red rose became a lasting symbol of the Lancastrian line during the Wars of the Roses.
Henry VI was the last monarch from the Lancastrian line before the Yorkists took the throne.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: House of Lancaster - another name for the Lancastrian line, a branch of the English royal family
  • Antonym: Yorkist line - the rival royal house to the Lancastrian line in the Wars of the Roses

Etymology

Lancastrian: relating to Lancaster, a city in England, and the noble House of Lancaster.
