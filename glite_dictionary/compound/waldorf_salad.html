Waldorf salad

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔl.dɔrf ˈsæl.əd / (AmE), / ˈwɔːl.dɔːf ˈsæl.əd / (BrE)
a salad made from diced apples and celery, typically with walnuts or raisins, dressed with mayonnaise
fruit and nut salad with mayonnaise

Examples

She ordered a Waldorf salad for lunch at the café.
The Waldorf salad is a classic dish often served at holiday gatherings.
Chopped walnuts add a delightful crunch to a traditional Waldorf salad.

Etymology

Named after the Waldorf Hotel in New York, where the salad was first created in the late 19th century.
