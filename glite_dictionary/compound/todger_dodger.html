todger dodger

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɑːdʒər ˈdɑːdʒər / (AmE), / ˈtɒdʒə ˈdɒdʒə / (BrE)
an informal term for a person who avoids responsibilities or commitments, especially by making excuses or evading tasks
person who avoids responsibilities
informal slang british

Examples

He’s become known as the office todger dodger, always finding ways to avoid extra work.
Nobody trusts a todger dodger to help when needed.
You can't be a todger dodger forever if you want to keep your job.

Synset Examples

He’s a notorious todger dodger, always avoiding responsibilities.
Being a todger dodger won't help you succeed.
She called him a todger dodger for skipping work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slacker - a person who avoids work or effort
  • Synonym: shirker - a person who avoids responsibilities or duties

Etymology

Formed from 'todger' (slang for male genitalia, also rarely 'person') and 'dodger' (one who dodges); informal British slang.
