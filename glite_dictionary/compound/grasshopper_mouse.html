grasshopper mouse

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡræsˌhɑpər maʊs / (AmE), / ˈɡrɑːsˌhɒpə maʊs / (BrE)
a small, carnivorous mouse of western North America that feeds on insects and is known for its aggressive behavior
insectivorous North American mouse

Examples

The grasshopper mouse is famous for its loud, wolf-like howl used to claim territory.
Unlike most rodents, the grasshopper mouse hunts insects and even scorpions at night.
Biologists study the grasshopper mouse to understand its resistance to venom from its prey.
