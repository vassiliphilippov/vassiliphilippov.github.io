elephant bird

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛlɪfənt bɝd / (AmE), / ˈɛlɪfənt bɜːd / (BrE)
a huge extinct flightless bird from Madagascar, known to have reached up to 9 feet in height
huge extinct flightless bird of Madagascar

Examples

Scientists believe the elephant bird disappeared from Madagascar several centuries ago.
The elephant bird is famed for laying the largest eggs of any known bird species.
Fossils of the elephant bird have helped researchers understand Madagascar's prehistoric fauna.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moa - large extinct flightless bird of New Zealand
  • Synonym: ostrich - large flightless bird native to Africa
