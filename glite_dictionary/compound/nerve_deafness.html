nerve deafness

1. NOUN

/ nɜrv ˈdɛfnəs / (AmE), / nɜːv ˈdɛfnəs / (BrE)
hearing loss resulting from damage or failure of the auditory nerve or inner ear
hearing loss from auditory nerve damage
medical

Examples

After years of exposure to loud music, he developed nerve deafness in his left ear.
Many cases of nerve deafness cannot be treated with conventional hearing aids.
Doctors explained that her nerve deafness was likely caused by an infection damaging her auditory nerve.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sensorineural hearing loss - hearing loss caused by damage to the inner ear or nerve pathways
  • Antonym: conductive deafness - hearing loss due to problems with the ear canal, eardrum, or middle ear
