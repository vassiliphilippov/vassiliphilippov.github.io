leprosy bacillus

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛprəsi bəˈsɪləs / (AmE), / ˈlɛprəsi bəˈsɪləs / (BrE)
the bacterium that causes leprosy, scientifically known as Mycobacterium leprae
bacterium causing leprosy
medical

Examples

Discovery of the leprosy bacillus was a major breakthrough in medical science.
The spread of the leprosy bacillus can be controlled with early detection and treatment.
Scientists are researching new drugs to combat the leprosy bacillus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mycobacterium leprae - scientific name of the leprosy bacillus

Etymology

From 'leprosy' (disease) and Latin 'bacillus' (little rod, referring to rod-shaped bacteria).
