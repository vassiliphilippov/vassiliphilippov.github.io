cheval glass

1. NOUN

/ ʃəˈvɑl ɡlæs / (AmE), / ʃəˈvɑːl ɡlɑːs / (BrE)
a tall, full-length mirror set in a frame allowing it to be tilted at different angles
tall tilting full-length mirror

Examples

She admired her reflection in the cheval glass before leaving for the event.
The antique cheval glass in the bedroom added an elegant touch to the decor.
He carefully adjusted the angle of the cheval glass to get a better view of his suit.

Etymology

From French 'cheval' (horse), because the mirror stands on four feet like a horse.
