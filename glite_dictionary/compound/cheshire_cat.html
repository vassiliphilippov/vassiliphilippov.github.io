Cheshire cat

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɛʃɚ kæt / (AmE), / ˈtʃɛʃə kæt / (BrE)
a fictional cat with a broad, fixed smile, famous for its mysterious and enigmatic behavior in 'Alice's Adventures in Wonderland' by Lewis Carroll
fictional cat with a broad smile in 'Alice in Wonderland'

Examples

The Cheshire cat appeared in the tree, grinning mysteriously at Alice.
She smiled with the wide grin of a Cheshire cat when she heard the good news.
The Cheshire cat is famous for its ability to disappear, leaving only its smile behind.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grin like a Cheshire cat - to smile broadly and persistently; idiomatic expression

Etymology

Named after Cheshire, a county in England, and popularized by Lewis Carroll's 'Alice's Adventures in Wonderland' (1865).
