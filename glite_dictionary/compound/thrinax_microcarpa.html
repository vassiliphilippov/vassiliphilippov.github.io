Thrinax microcarpa

1. NOUN

/ ˈθraɪ.næks ˌmaɪ.kroʊˈkɑːr.pə / (AmE), / ˈθraɪ.naks ˌmɪ.krəʊˈkɑː.pə / (BrE)
A small, stocky fan palm native to southern Florida and Cuba.
small fan palm of Florida and Cuba

Examples

The Thrinax microcarpa can often be found in the coastal habitats of southern Florida.
Botanists study the Thrinax microcarpa for its unique adaptation to sandy soils.
In her garden, she planted a Thrinax microcarpa for its decorative fan-shaped leaves.

Etymology

From genus Thrinax (from Greek θρῖναξ, meaning ‘trident’), and Latin microcarpa (small-fruited).
