observer's meridian

1. NOUN

/ əbˈzɝːvɚz məˈrɪdiən / (AmE), / əbˈzɜːvəz məˈrɪdiən / (BrE)
the meridian that passes through the zenith of a particular observer, used in astronomy and geography to denote the line of longitude aligning with the observer's position
meridian through an observer's zenith

Examples

The observer's meridian is crucial for determining the precise position of celestial objects in the sky.
At noon, the sun crosses the observer's meridian, marking local apparent noon.
Navigators use the observer's meridian as a reference for calculating latitude and longitude.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meridian - an imaginary line on Earth's surface from pole to pole
