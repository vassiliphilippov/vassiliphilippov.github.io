USS Constitution

1. PROPN

/ ˌju ɛs ɛs kənˈstɪtʃuˌʃən / (AmE), / ˌjuː ɛs ɛs kənˈstɪtjuːʃən / (BrE)
a historic United States Navy frigate launched in 1797, renowned for its victories in the War of 1812 and preserved as a museum ship in Boston.
historic U.S. Navy frigate (Old Ironsides)

Examples

The USS Constitution is permanently berthed in Boston as a museum ship open to the public.
During the War of 1812, the USS Constitution earned the nickname 'Old Ironsides' for her resilience in battle.
Many tourists visit the USS Constitution to learn about early American naval history.

Etymology

Named for the United States Constitution; 'USS' stands for 'United States Ship'.
