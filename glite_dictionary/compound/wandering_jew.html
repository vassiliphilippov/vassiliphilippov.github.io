Wandering Jew

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɑndərɪŋ dʒu / (AmE), / ˈwɒndərɪŋ dʒuː / (BrE)
A legendary figure in Christian folklore, said to be a Jew condemned to wander the Earth until the Second Coming for mocking Jesus on the way to the Crucifixion.
legendary figure doomed to wander

Examples

The story of the Wandering Jew has inspired countless works of art and literature across Europe.
Some scholars debate the origins of the myth of the Wandering Jew.
He referenced the legend of the Wandering Jew in his latest novel.

Etymology

From the legendary story originating in medieval European Christian tradition.
