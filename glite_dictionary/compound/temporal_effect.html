temporal effect

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛmpərəl ɪˈfɛkt / (AmE), / ˈtɛmpərəl ɪˈfɛkt / (BrE)
An effect or change that occurs over time or is related to the passage of time.
effect related to time

Examples

The study analyzed the temporal effects of stress on cognitive performance.
Scientists monitor the temporal effect of pollutants on water quality.
Understanding the temporal effect is crucial in assessing the drug's safety profile.

Synset Examples

She focused on the temporal effects of the medication.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: long-term effect - an effect that appears after a long period
  • Degree Lower: immediate effect - an effect that occurs right away
