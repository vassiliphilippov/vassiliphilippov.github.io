physiological psychology

1. NOUN

/ ˌfɪziəˌlɑdʒɪkəl saɪˈkɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ˌfɪziəˌlɒdʒɪkəl saɪˈkɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the branch of psychology that studies the relationship between physiological processes and behavior or mental functions
study of physiological bases of behavior
medical

Examples

She is conducting groundbreaking research in physiological psychology at the university.
Many discoveries in physiological psychology have deepened our understanding of how the brain regulates emotions.
Courses in physiological psychology often cover topics such as neural mechanisms of learning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: biopsychology - scientific study of the biological basis of behavior
  • Synonym: psychophysiology - study of the interaction between psychological and physiological processes

Etymology

From 'physiological' (relating to body functions) + 'psychology' (study of mind), both from Greek roots.
