family Geastraceae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli dʒiːˌæstəˈreɪsiː / (AmE), / ˈfæməli dʒiːˌɑːstəˈreɪsiː / (BrE)
A family of earthstar fungi in the order Lycoperdales, characterized by star-shaped fruiting bodies.
family of earthstar fungi
medical

Examples

The family Geastraceae includes fungi known as earthstars due to their unique star-shaped appearance.
Researchers discovered several new species within the family Geastraceae in the temperate forests.
Members of the family Geastraceae are often found growing on decaying wood in woodland areas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: earthstar - a type of fungus with a star-shaped fruiting body

Etymology

From New Latin 'Geastraceae', formed from 'Geastrum' (type genus) + '-aceae', a suffix used in botanical family names.
