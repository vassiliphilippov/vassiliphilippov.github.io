Clark standard cell

1. NOUN

/ klɑrk ˈstændɚd sɛl / (AmE), / klɑːk ˈstændəd sɛl / (BrE)
a type of voltaic cell once used as a standard reference for electromotive force measurements
historical standard voltaic cell

Examples

The Clark standard cell was historically important in calibrating laboratory equipment.
Before modern references, scientists relied on the Clark standard cell for consistent voltage.
A Clark standard cell typically used zinc and mercury sulfate electrodes in its construction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: standard cell - a cell used as a standard of electromotive force

Etymology

Named after Latimer Clark, its inventor.
