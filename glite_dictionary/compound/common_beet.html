common beet

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmən bit / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən biːt / (BrE)
a biennial Eurasian plant (Beta vulgaris), usually having a swollen edible root, widely grown as a food crop
a plant with an edible swollen root, grown as food

Examples

The common beet is often used in salads and soups.
Farmers in this region have cultivated common beet for generations.
She bought several fresh common beets at the market to make borscht.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beetroot - the edible root part of the beet plant
  • Degree Higher: sugar beet - a variety cultivated for its high sugar content
  • Degree Lower: mangel-wurzel - a cultivated beet used chiefly as animal feed

Etymology

From Latin beta (beet), with 'common' denoting the typical cultivated variety.
