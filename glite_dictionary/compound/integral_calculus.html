integral calculus

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪn.tə.ɡrəl ˈkæl.kjə.ləs / (AmE), / ˈɪn.tɪ.ɡrəl ˈkæl.kjʊ.ləs / (BrE)
The branch of mathematics that focuses on integration and its applications, such as finding areas, volumes, and solving differential equations.
branch of mathematics dealing with integration

Examples

Several engineering problems require the use of integral calculus to compute areas under curves.
My favorite topic in mathematics is integral calculus because of its wide range of applications.
Understanding integral calculus is essential for solving certain types of physics equations.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: differential calculus - branch of calculus concerned with derivatives and rates of change

Etymology

From 'integral' (from Latin integralis, making whole) and 'calculus' (from Latin calculus, small stone used for counting).
