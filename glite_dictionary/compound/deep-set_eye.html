deep-set eye

1. NOUN

/ ˌdipˈsɛt aɪ / (AmE), / ˌdiːpˈsɛt aɪ / (BrE)
An eye that is positioned further back in the skull than is typical, often creating the appearance of a prominent brow or shadowed sockets.
eye positioned further back in skull

Examples

His deep-set eyes made him look thoughtful and intense.
Artists often struggle to accurately paint the shadows around deep-set eyes.
She inherited her mother's striking deep-set eyes and high cheekbones.

Synset Examples

She has deep-set eyes that give her a mysterious look.
