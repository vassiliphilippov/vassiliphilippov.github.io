genus Conium

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs koʊˈnaɪ.əm / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs kəʊˈnʌɪ.əm / (BrE)
A small genus of highly poisonous biennial herbs, including the hemlock, native to Europe and Asia.
genus of highly toxic biennial herbs

Examples

The genus Conium is best known for including the deadly poison hemlock.
Many botanists study the genus Conium due to its historical significance in toxicology.
All members of the genus Conium contain potent neurotoxins dangerous to both humans and animals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hemlock - a highly poisonous plant in the parsley family, Conium maculatum

Etymology

From Latin 'Conium', derived from Greek 'kṓneion' (hemlock).
