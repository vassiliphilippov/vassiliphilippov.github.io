flying boat

1. NOUN

/ ˈflaɪɪŋ boʊt / (AmE), / ˈflaɪɪŋ bəʊt / (BrE)
a large seaplane with a hull that allows it to float directly on the water, rather than using separate pontoons
large seaplane with boat-like hull

Examples

The vintage flying boat touched down gracefully on the calm lake waters.
During the 1930s, air travel across the Atlantic was often done in a flying boat.
Engineers restored the flying boat for display at the aviation museum.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seaplane - an aircraft capable of taking off and landing on water

Etymology

Formed from 'flying' + 'boat', reflecting its combination of flight capability and boat-like hull
