cingulate gyrus

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪŋɡjələt ˈdʒaɪrəs / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋɡjʊlət ˈdʒaɪərəs / (BrE)
a long, curved fold on the inner surface of each cerebral hemisphere, forming part of the limbic system and involved in emotion and behavior regulation
curved brain fold in limbic system
medical

Examples

The cingulate gyrus plays a key role in processing emotions and regulating behavior.
Damage to the cingulate gyrus can affect attention and mood.
Researchers found increased activity in the cingulate gyrus during decision-making tasks.

Etymology

From Latin 'cingulatus' (girdled) and Greek 'gyros' (circle/turn), referring to its position around the corpus callosum.
