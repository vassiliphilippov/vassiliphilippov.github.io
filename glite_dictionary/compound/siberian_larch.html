Siberian larch

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈbɪriən lɑrtʃ / (AmE), / sɪˈbɪəriən lɑːtʃ / (BrE)
a medium-sized deciduous conifer native to northeastern Russia and Siberia, with a conical crown and soft, narrow bright-green leaves, valued for its timber and use in gardens
deciduous conifer tree of Siberia

Examples

The wood of the Siberian larch is highly durable and resistant to rot, making it valuable for outdoor construction.
Gardeners in colder climates often plant Siberian larch because it can tolerate harsh winters.
In spring, the soft bright-green leaves of the Siberian larch create a striking display against the landscape.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: larch - a type of deciduous coniferous tree
