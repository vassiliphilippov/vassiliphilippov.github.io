blue cat

1. NOUN

/ bluː kæt / (AmE), / bluː kæt / (BrE)
a large species of catfish native to the Mississippi valley, known for its bluish color and valued as a game fish
large catfish of the Mississippi valley

Examples

Fishermen along the river often hope to catch a blue cat because of its impressive size.
The blue cat can weigh over a hundred pounds in some cases.
Conservation efforts are underway to protect the habitat of the blue cat in the Mississippi valley.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catfish - a freshwater fish with whisker-like barbels around the mouth
