ground floor

1. NOUN

/ ɡraʊnd flɔɹ / (AmE), / ɡraʊnd flɔː / (BrE)
the floor of a building that is at or nearest to the level of the ground around the building
floor at ground level of a building

Examples

Our apartment is on the ground floor, so it's easy to access from the street.
The museum's ground floor houses the main exhibition hall and gift shop.
They moved their elderly mother to the ground floor for easier mobility.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: first floor - the floor above the ground floor (in British English) or the floor at ground level (in American English)

2. NOUN

/ ɡraʊnd flɔr / (AmE), / ɡraʊnd flɔː / (BrE)
the initial stage or starting point of a business or venture, usually seen as offering an advantage to those involved at this early stage
initial stage of an enterprise

Examples

She was lucky enough to get in on the ground floor of a successful tech startup.
Investors who joined at the ground floor reaped the biggest rewards when the company grew.
Being part of the ground floor team gave him influence over key decisions.

Synset Examples

he got in on the ground floor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beginning - the point in time or space at which something starts
  • Antonym: end - the final stage or conclusion of something
