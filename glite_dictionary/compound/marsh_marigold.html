marsh marigold

1. NOUN

/ mɑrʃ ˈmɛrɪˌɡoʊld / (AmE), / mɑːʃ ˈmærɪɡəʊld / (BrE)
A perennial swamp plant of Europe and North America, with bright yellow flowers resembling buttercups.
swamp plant with yellow flowers

Examples

The bright yellow blooms of the marsh marigold stood out along the riverbank in early spring.
Gardeners often plant marsh marigold in wet areas for its cheerful spring flowers.
We spotted several clusters of marsh marigold during our walk through the wetlands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cowslip - another common name for the marsh marigold
