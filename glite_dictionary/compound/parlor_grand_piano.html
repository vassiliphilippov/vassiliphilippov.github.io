parlor grand piano

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑrlɚ ˌɡrænd piˈænoʊ / (AmE), / ˈpɑːlə ˌɡrænd piˈænəʊ / (BrE)
a small grand piano, typically designed for use in homes or small venues, shorter in length than a concert grand piano
a small grand piano for homes or small spaces

Examples

The elegant parlor grand piano stood in the corner of the living room, gleaming in the sunlight.
Many music teachers recommend a parlor grand piano for students who want a rich sound but have limited space.
At the recital, she performed flawlessly on an antique parlor grand piano that once belonged to her grandmother.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: concert grand piano - a full-sized grand piano typically used in concert halls
  • Degree Lower: baby grand piano - an even smaller grand piano than a parlor grand, for limited spaces

Etymology

From 'parlor' (a sitting room) and 'grand piano'. 'Parlor' derives from Old French *parloir* (speaking place).
