fall cankerworm

1. NOUN

/ fɔl ˈkæŋkɚˌwɚm / (AmE), / fɔːl ˈkæŋkəwɜːm / (BrE)
A green or brown, white-striped caterpillar that is the larva of the moth Alsophila pometaria; it is known for damaging the leaves of trees in late autumn.
a caterpillar pest, larva of Alsophila pometaria

Examples

The fall cankerworm can cause serious defoliation in oak and elm trees each autumn.
Gardeners watched as fall cankerworms devoured large portions of the orchard's foliage.
To control fall cankerworm infestations, experts recommend banding tree trunks in late fall.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spring cankerworm - a similar caterpillar, larva of Paleacrita vernata, active in spring
