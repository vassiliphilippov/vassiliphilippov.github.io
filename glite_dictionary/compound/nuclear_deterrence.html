nuclear deterrence

1. NOUN

/ ˈnuːkliɚ dɪˈtɝːrəns / (AmE), / ˈnjuːkliə dɪˈtɛr(ə)ns / (BrE)
the military strategy by which a country prevents an enemy from using nuclear weapons by maintaining the capability to retaliate with its own nuclear force
military strategy preventing nuclear attack by threat of retaliation

Examples

During the Cold War, nuclear deterrence was a central element of both superpowers' defense policies.
Many experts debate whether nuclear deterrence genuinely reduces the risk of large-scale war.
The theory of nuclear deterrence relies on the credibility of a nation's ability to retaliate.

Synset Examples

when two nations both resort to nuclear deterrence the consequence could be mutual destruction

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deterrence - the act or process of discouraging an action or event through instilling doubt or fear of the consequences
