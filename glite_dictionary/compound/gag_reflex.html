gag reflex

1. NOUN

/ ɡæɡ rɪˈflɛks / (AmE), / ɡæɡ rɪˈflɛks / (BrE)
an automatic contraction of the throat muscles, often triggered by touching the back of the mouth, resulting in retching or the urge to vomit
automatic throat contraction response
medical

Examples

Some people have a sensitive gag reflex that makes dental visits uncomfortable.
The doctor tested his gag reflex by touching the back of his throat with a swab.
A strong gag reflex can be a challenge for patients undergoing certain medical procedures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: retch - to make efforts to vomit noisily
