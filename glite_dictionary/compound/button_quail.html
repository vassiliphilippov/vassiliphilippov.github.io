button quail

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌt.ən kweɪl / (AmE), / ˈbʌt.ən kweɪl / (BrE)
a small, quail-like terrestrial bird of southern Eurasia and northern Africa that lacks a hind toe and is classified with wading birds, but typically inhabits grassy plains
small, quail-like terrestrial bird

Examples

The button quail can be found in grassy plains across southern Eurasia.
Unlike true quails, the button quail lacks a hind toe.
Birdwatchers were excited to spot a rare button quail near the marsh.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quail - a small, ground-dwelling game bird

Etymology

Named for its small, rounded shape resembling a button.
