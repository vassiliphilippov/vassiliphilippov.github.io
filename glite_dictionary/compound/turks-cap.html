Turk's-cap

1. NOUN

/ tɝks kæp / (AmE), / tɜːks kap / (BrE)
a lily native to eastern North America, having orange to red, maroon-spotted flowers with petals that curve backward
North American lily with curved-back orange-red flowers

Examples

The bright orange flowers of the Turk's-cap add color to the woodland garden every summer.
At the botanical garden, we saw a beautiful Turk's-cap lily blooming among the ferns.
Gardeners often plant Turk's-cap to attract butterflies and hummingbirds to their yards.

Etymology

Named for the flower’s petals, which resemble a Turkish turban or cap.

2. NOUN

/ tɜrks kæp / (AmE), / tɜːks kap / (BrE)
a species of lily (Lilium martagon) with small dull purple flowers, native to northwestern Europe and northwestern Asia
a type of lily with dull purple flowers

Examples

The Turk's-cap grows well in woodland gardens with partial shade.
She admired the distinctive shape of the Turk's-cap flowers during her trip to Scandinavia.
Botanists often call the Turk's-cap the martagon lily due to its characteristic blossoms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: martagon lily - another name for Turk's-cap, a lily species with turban-shaped flowers

Etymology

Named for the flower's shape, which resembles a Turkish turban or cap.
