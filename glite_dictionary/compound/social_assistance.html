social assistance

1. NOUN

/ ˈsoʊʃəl əˈsɪstəns / (AmE), / ˈsəʊʃl əˈsɪstəns / (BrE)
government benefits paid to individuals or families to ensure their income meets minimum levels set by law
government welfare payments for minimum income

Examples

After losing her job, she relied on social assistance to pay her rent and buy groceries.
The government increased social assistance to help struggling families during the recession.
Eligibility for social assistance is determined by assessing an individual’s income and financial resources.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: welfare - government-provided support for those in need
