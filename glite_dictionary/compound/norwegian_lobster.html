Norwegian lobster

1. NOUN

/ nɔˈwi.dʒən ˈlɑb.stɚ / (AmE), / nɔˈwiː.dʒi.ən ˈlɒb.stə / (BrE)
a slender species of lobster (Nephrops norvegicus) found in European waters, valued as food
slender lobster species found in Europe

Examples

The chef prepared a delicious dish using fresh Norwegian lobster caught that morning.
Norwegian lobster is often served as scampi in European restaurants.
Marine biologists are studying the habitat of the Norwegian lobster along the Atlantic coast.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scampi - another name for the Norwegian lobster, especially as a dish
  • Degree Higher: lobster - large marine crustacean with a hard shell and pincers
