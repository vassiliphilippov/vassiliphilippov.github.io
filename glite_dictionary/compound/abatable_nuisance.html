abatable nuisance

1. NOUN

/ əˈbeɪ.tə.bəl ˈnuː.səns / (AmE), / əˈbeɪ.tə.bəl ˈnjuː.səns / (BrE)
A nuisance that can be stopped, removed, or made harmless, especially as recognized by law.
nuisance that can be remedied by action
legal

Examples

The court ordered the property owner to remove the abatable nuisance affecting the neighborhood.
An abatable nuisance like excess noise can be legally addressed by local authorities.
Legal action may be taken if an abatable nuisance is not corrected in a timely manner.

Synset Examples

the continued existence of an abatable nuisance is not authorized under the law

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nuisance - a thing or action causing annoyance or harm

Etymology

From 'abatable' (from Old French abatre, to beat down) and 'nuisance' (from Old French nuire, to harm).
