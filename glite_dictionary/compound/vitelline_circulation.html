vitelline circulation

1. NOUN

/ vɪˈtɛlɪn ˌsɜrkjuˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / vɪˈtɛlɪn ˌsɜːkjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the system of blood circulation between the embryo and the yolk sac during early development
embryonic blood flow between embryo and yolk sac
medical

Examples

The vitelline circulation is essential for transporting nutrients from the yolk sac to the developing embryo.
Researchers studied defects in the vitelline circulation to understand certain congenital conditions.
Abnormalities in vitelline circulation can lead to developmental issues in the embryo.

Etymology

From Latin 'vitellinus' (of egg yolk) + English 'circulation'.
