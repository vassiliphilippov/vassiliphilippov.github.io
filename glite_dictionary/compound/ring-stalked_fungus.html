ring-stalked fungus

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɪŋ stɔːkt ˈfʌŋɡəs / (AmE), / ˈrɪŋ stɔːkt ˈfʌŋɡəs / (BrE)
A genus of gill fungi producing brown spores, closely related to Agaricus, and placed in its own family, Strophariaceae.
genus of brown-spored gill fungi

Examples

Scientists discovered a new species of ring-stalked fungus in the old-growth forest.
The ring-stalked fungus is known for the distinctive ring around its stem.
Many ring-stalked fungi are studied for their role in forest ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Stropharia - A genus of ring-stalked fungi in the family Strophariaceae
  • Synonym: Agaricus - A related genus of gilled mushrooms, includes common button mushrooms

Etymology

From 'ring' (referring to a ring-like structure on the stalk) and 'fungus' (from Latin fungus, 'mushroom').
