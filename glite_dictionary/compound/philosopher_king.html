philosopher king

1. NOUN

/ fəˈlɑːsəfər kɪŋ / (AmE), / fəˈlɒsəfə kɪŋ / (BrE)
a ruler who possesses philosophical wisdom and governs with fairness and justice, especially as described by Plato
a wise and just ruler in philosophy

Examples

Many scholars debate whether a philosopher king could ever exist in modern society.
She admired the ancient ideal of the philosopher king, blending wisdom with leadership.
His policies reflected the influence of Plato's philosopher king model.

Synset Examples

Plato's idea of a philosopher king inspires many leaders.
The community praised her as a philosopher king for her thoughtful governance.
He aspired to be a philosopher king, ruling wisely.

Etymology

From the ancient Greek political philosophy of Plato, combining 'philosopher' (lover of wisdom) and 'king'.
