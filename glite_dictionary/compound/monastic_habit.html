monastic habit

1. NOUN

/ məˈnæstɪk ˈhæbɪt / (AmE), / məˈnastɪk ˈhabɪt / (BrE)
a long, loose garment traditionally worn by monks in a monastery as a sign of their religious order
long garment worn by monks

Examples

The novice carefully donned his monastic habit before morning prayers.
Centuries-old paintings often depict monks in traditional monastic habits.
The color and style of the monastic habit can indicate the monk's religious order.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cassock - a close-fitting ankle-length garment worn by some clergy
  • Synonym: robes - loose outer garments worn especially by clergy

Etymology

From Latin 'monasticus' (of a monk) and Old French 'habit' (clothing)
