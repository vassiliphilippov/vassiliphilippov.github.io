Battle of Blenheim

1. PROPN

/ ˈbæt̬.əl əv ˈblɛn.haɪm / (AmE), / ˈbæt.əl əv ˈblɛn.haɪm / (BrE)
a major battle fought in 1704 during the War of the Spanish Succession, in which the First Duke of Marlborough and Prince Eugene of Savoy defeated the French forces
1704 battle in the War of the Spanish Succession

Examples

The Battle of Blenheim marked a turning point in the War of the Spanish Succession.
Many historians regard the Battle of Blenheim as a major victory for the Allied forces.
Paintings depicting the Battle of Blenheim are displayed in several British museums.
