black hemlock

1. NOUN

/ blæk ˈhɛmlɑk / (AmE), / blæk ˈhɛmlɒk / (BrE)
A large evergreen tree native to the western United States, known for its much harder wood than the Canadian hemlock.
western US evergreen tree with hard wood

Examples

The black hemlock thrives in the mountainous regions of the Pacific Northwest.
Lumber from black hemlock is prized for its exceptional durability.
During our hike, we identified several black hemlocks by their dark, rough bark.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hemlock - a coniferous tree of the genus Tsuga
