fresh sanga

1. NOUN

/ frɛʃ ˈsæŋə / (AmE), / frɛʃ ˈsaŋə / (BrE)
An informal term, especially in Australian English, for a freshly made sandwich.
freshly made sandwich (informal)
informal

Examples

There's nothing better than a fresh sanga at the beach.
He packed a fresh sanga for his lunch break at work.
After the game, they all shared a fresh sanga in the park.

Synset Examples

I love a fresh sanga after a long hike.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sandwich - two slices of bread with filling between them

Etymology

From Australian slang 'sanga' (sandwich), likely a reduction of 'sandwich'.
