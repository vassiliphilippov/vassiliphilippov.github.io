red maids

1. NOUN

/ rɛd meɪdz / (AmE), / rɛd meɪdz / (BrE)
a succulent, low-growing plant with small, bright reddish-pink flowers, found in the southwestern United States
succulent plant with reddish-pink flowers

Examples

The bright blossoms of red maids add cheerful color to desert gardens in spring.
Botanists identified the plant as red maids during their field survey in Arizona.
After the winter rains, red maids often form a thick carpet over the sandy soil.
