family Thraupidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli θræˈjuːpɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli θræˈjuːpɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of small to medium-sized songbirds known as tanagers, found mainly in the Americas.
family of songbirds (tanagers)

Examples

The family Thraupidae includes more than 370 species of tanagers.
Birdwatchers admired the colorful members of the family Thraupidae during their trip to South America.
Recent studies have revised the taxonomy within the family Thraupidae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tanager - a brightly colored songbird of the family Thraupidae

Etymology

From New Latin 'Thraupidae', based on 'Thraupis', a genus of tanagers.
