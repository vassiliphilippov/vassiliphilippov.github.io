cliff dweller

1. NOUN

/ klɪf ˈdwɛlɚ / (AmE), / klɪf ˈdwɛlə / (BrE)
A member of a prehistoric Native American people, especially the Anasazi, who lived in rock or adobe dwellings built on the sides or ledges of cliffs in the southwestern United States.
prehistoric Native American living in cliff dwellings

Examples

The ancient cliff dwellers of the Southwest built elaborate homes in the canyons of Mesa Verde.
Archaeologists have uncovered pottery and tools left behind by cliff dwellers in Arizona.
The cliff dwellers adapted ingeniously to their harsh desert environment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pueblo - a member of a Native American people living in pueblos
