weather eye

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɛðɚ aɪ / (AmE), / ˈwɛðə aɪ / (BrE)
a state of vigilant watchfulness for potential trouble or changes, especially in one's surroundings or situation
vigilant watchfulness; alertness for changes

Examples

Ever since the incident, Tom has kept a weather eye on his finances.
Sailors are trained to maintain a weather eye for sudden changes at sea.
She always has a weather eye open for new opportunities.

Synset Examples

he keeps a weather eye open for trouble

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alertness - the quality of being alert or vigilant
  • Antonym: heedlessness - lack of care or attention

Etymology

Originally a nautical expression referring to a sailor’s constant lookout for changes in the weather.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɛðɚ aɪ / (AmE), / ˈwɛðə aɪ / (BrE)
a watchful or alert attitude; careful observation, especially for any changes or possible danger
an observant or cautious attitude

Examples

She kept a weather eye on the horizon, looking for signs of rain.
It's wise to have a weather eye during the stock market's unpredictable periods.
He maintained a weather eye on his competitors' movements throughout the season.

Synset Examples

Keep a weather eye open for any changes in the forecast.
As a sailor, he always keeps a weather eye open.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alert - quick to notice and react to potential danger or problems
  • Synonym: vigilant - being watchful for danger or difficulties

Etymology

Originally nautical, referring to sailors watching for changes in the weather.
