vacancy rate

1. NOUN

/ ˈveɪ.kən.si reɪt / (AmE), / ˈveɪ.kən.si reɪt / (BrE)
the percentage of available rental units (such as in hotels or apartments) that are unoccupied at a given time
percentage of unoccupied rental units

Examples

Due to the economic downturn, the vacancy rate for office buildings rose sharply last year.
The hotel manager monitors the vacancy rate to adjust prices and maximize revenue.
A high vacancy rate can indicate an oversupply of apartments in the neighborhood.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: occupancy rate - percentage of rental units that are occupied
