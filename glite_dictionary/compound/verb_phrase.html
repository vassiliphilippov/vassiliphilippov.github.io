verb phrase

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɝb ˌfreɪz / (AmE), / ˈvɜːb ˌfreɪz / (BrE)
A group of words consisting of a verb and its complements, objects, or modifiers, forming the predicate of a sentence.
group with verb as predicate

Examples

In the sentence 'She is reading a book,' the verb phrase is 'is reading a book.'
Students were asked to underline every verb phrase in the paragraph.
Identifying the verb phrase helps understand the sentence structure.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: noun phrase - a phrase with a noun as its main word
  • Synonym: predicate - the part of a sentence containing the verb and stating something about the subject
