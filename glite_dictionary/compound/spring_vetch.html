spring vetch

1. NOUN

/ sprɪŋ vɛtʃ / (AmE), / sprɪŋ vɛtʃ / (BrE)
a climbing herbaceous plant, valued as livestock fodder and for improving soil fertility
climbing plant used for fodder and soil-building

Examples

The farmer planted spring vetch to enrich the soil before sowing cereal crops.
Livestock graze on spring vetch during the early summer months.
Including spring vetch in crop rotations can improve soil health and reduce the need for fertilizers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vetch - any leguminous plant of the genus Vicia

2. NOUN

/ sprɪŋ vɛtʃ / (AmE), / sprɪŋ vɛtʃ / (BrE)
A bushy European perennial plant with nodding racemose violet-blue flowers, belonging to the vetch family.
European perennial plant with violet-blue flowers

Examples

The spring vetch adds a lovely touch of color to the meadow in early summer.
Gardeners often plant spring vetch to enrich the soil with nitrogen.
We identified several wildflowers, including spring vetch, along the hillside.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vetch - any of various climbing plants of the genus Vicia
