red-diaper family

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛd ˌdaɪpər ˈfæmli / (AmE), / ˈrɛd ˌdaɪpə ˈfæmli / (BrE)
A family in which the parents hold strong leftist or communist beliefs, often raising their children in that environment.
family with communist or radical leftist beliefs

Examples

He often spoke about being raised in a red-diaper family and its impact on his politics.
Growing up in a red-diaper family, she learned about activism from a young age.
Many members of the movement came from red-diaper families with a tradition of political engagement.

Synset Examples

He grew up in a red-diaper family with strong beliefs.
She often joked about her red-diaper upbringing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: red-diaper baby - a person raised by parents with communistic or socialist beliefs

Etymology

From 'red-diaper baby', American slang from 'red' (communist) and association with childhood upbringing.
