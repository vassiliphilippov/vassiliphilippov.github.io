charm campaign

1. NOUN

/ tʃɑrm kæmˈpeɪn / (AmE), / tʃɑːm kæmˈpeɪn / (BrE)
A planned effort by a person or organization to use flattery and friendliness to gain popularity or support.
effort to gain support through friendliness

Examples

After a series of scandals, the company launched a charm campaign to restore its image.
The prime minister's charm campaign included meeting with local residents and promising reforms.
Celebrities often begin a charm campaign when promoting a new film or product.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: public relations - the practice of managing the spread of information to the public
