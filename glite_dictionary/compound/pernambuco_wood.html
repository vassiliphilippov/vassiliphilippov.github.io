pernambuco wood

1. NOUN

/ pɚˌnæmˈbjuː koʊd / (AmE), / pəˌnæmˈbjuː kʊd / (BrE)
The hard, heavy red wood of a tropical Brazilian tree (Caesalpinia echinata), valued for its red dye and use in fine cabinetry and violin bows.
hard red wood from a Brazilian tree

Examples

The finest violin bows are often made from pernambuco wood due to its strength and flexibility.
Cabinetmakers prized pernambuco wood for its rich color and durability.
Historically, pernambuco wood was exported for its vibrant red dye.

Etymology

Named after Pernambuco, a state in Brazil where the tree is found.
