borderline schizophrenia

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɔrdərˌlaɪn ˌskɪtzəˈfriːniə / (AmE), / ˈbɔːdəˌlaɪn ˌskɪtsəˈfriːniə / (BrE)
A form of schizophrenia characterized by mild symptoms or by a tendency toward developing full schizophrenia.
mild or early form of schizophrenia
medical

Examples

Some psychiatrists use the term borderline schizophrenia when patients show only mild psychotic symptoms.
He was diagnosed with borderline schizophrenia after experiencing occasional paranoia and social withdrawal.
The early intervention program aims to support those with borderline schizophrenia before symptoms become severe.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: schizophrenia - a severe mental disorder characterized by loss of contact with reality
