Byzantine Church

1. NOUN

/ bɪˈzæntin tʃɝːtʃ / (AmE), / bɪˈzæntɪn tʃɜːtʃ / (BrE)
the Christian Church as it existed in the Byzantine Empire, characterized by its traditions, liturgy, and practices before the East-West Schism of 1054
the Christian Church in the Byzantine Empire

Examples

The influence of the Byzantine Church can still be seen in the liturgy of Eastern Orthodox Christians.
Many ancient mosaics in Istanbul were created during the height of the Byzantine Church.
The Byzantine Church played a significant role in preserving classical Greek and Roman knowledge.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Eastern Orthodox Church - the Christian church arising from the Byzantine Church after the East-West Schism

Etymology

Byzantine, from Byzantium (ancient name for Constantinople) + Church. 'Byzantine' comes from Latin 'Byzantinus,' after the city of Byzantium.
