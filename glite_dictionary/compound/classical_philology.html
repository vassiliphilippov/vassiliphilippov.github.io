classical philology

1. NOUN

/ ˌklæsɪkəl fɪˈlɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ˌklæsɪkəl fɪˈlɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the study and scholarly analysis of ancient languages and literatures, especially Classical Greek, Latin, and Sanskrit.
study of ancient classical languages and literature

Examples

Many universities offer courses in classical philology for students interested in ancient languages.
Her research in classical philology focused on ancient Roman poetry and its influence on European literature.
Advances in classical philology have shed light on the transmission of Greek philosophical texts through history.

Synset Examples

Classical philology was a major preoccupation of the 19th-century German education system, which became ‘the paradigm for higher education’ throughout Western culture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: philology - the study of language in written historical sources

Etymology

From Greek philo- (loving) + -logia (study), combined with 'classical' referring to the ancient classical civilizations.
