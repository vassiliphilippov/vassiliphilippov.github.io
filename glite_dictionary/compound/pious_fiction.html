pious fiction

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪəs ˈfɪkʃən / (AmE), / ˈpaɪəs ˈfɪkʃən / (BrE)
a literary work, especially a story or narrative, that is intended to promote religious or moral values rather than to reflect reality
fiction promoting religious or moral values

Examples

The author was known for weaving pious fiction into his children’s books to impart moral lessons.
Some critics dismissed the novel as mere pious fiction, lacking in genuine human conflict.
Her stories, earnestly written as pious fiction, found a devoted audience among religious readers.

Synset Examples

She criticized the movie for its pious fiction theme.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: edifying tale - a story intended to instruct or improve morally
  • Synonym: didactic novel - a novel primarily intended to teach a lesson

Etymology

From 'pious' (devoutly religious) and 'fiction' (narrative prose of imagined events); commonly used since the 19th century.
