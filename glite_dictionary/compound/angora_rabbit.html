Angora rabbit

1. NOUN

/ ænˈɡɔːrə ˈræbɪt / (AmE), / ænˈɡɔːrə ˈræbɪt / (BrE)
A domestic breed of rabbit known for its long, silky white hair, often bred for its wool.
rabbit breed with long silky hair

Examples

She raises Angora rabbits and spins their wool into soft yarn.
The Angora rabbit is prized for its extremely fine and silky fur.
Grooming an Angora rabbit requires regular brushing to prevent the wool from matting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Angora - a breed of animal (rabbit, goat) with long, silky hair or wool

Etymology

Named after Angora (now Ankara), Turkey, noted for animals with fine hair or wool.
