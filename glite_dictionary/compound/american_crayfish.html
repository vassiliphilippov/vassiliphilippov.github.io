American crayfish

1. NOUN

/ əˈmɛr.ɪ.kən ˈkreɪ.fɪʃ / (AmE), / əˈmɛr.ɪ.kən ˈkreɪ.fɪʃ / (BrE)
a large crayfish species native to eastern North America, commonly found in freshwater habitats
large crayfish of eastern North America

Examples

The American crayfish has become invasive in some European rivers.
Researchers studied the habitat preferences of the American crayfish in local streams.
Many anglers use traps to catch American crayfish for bait or food.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crayfish - a freshwater crustacean resembling a small lobster
