interplanetary space

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪntərˈplænəˌtɛri speɪs / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈplænɪtəri speɪs / (BrE)
the region of outer space that lies between the planets within a solar system, especially the area between the planets of our own solar system
space between planets within a solar system

Examples

The probe traveled through interplanetary space before reaching Mars.
Scientists study cosmic rays that travel across interplanetary space.
Dust particles in interplanetary space can affect spacecraft navigation.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: interstellar space - the region of space located between stars
  • Degree Higher: outer space - space beyond Earth's atmosphere
  • Degree Lower: planetary atmosphere - the gaseous envelope surrounding a planet

Etymology

From Latin 'inter-' (between) and 'planetarius' (of planets), referring to the space between planets.
