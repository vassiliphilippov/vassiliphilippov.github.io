planning frame

1. NOUN

/ ˈplænɪŋ freɪm / (AmE), / ˈplænɪŋ freɪm / (BrE)
A square wooden frame used by archaeologists to help record and draw archaeological features at a site.
square frame used in archaeology

Examples

The archaeologist set up a planning frame to accurately record the excavation area.
Students learned how to use a planning frame for drawing site features.
A planning frame helps ensure that maps of the site are drawn to scale.
