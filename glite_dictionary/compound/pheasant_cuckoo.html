pheasant cuckoo

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɛzənt ˈkʊku / (AmE), / ˈfiːzənt ˈkʊkuː / (BrE)
A large tropical American bird (Dromococcyx phasianellus) of the cuckoo family, known for its long tail resembling that of a pheasant.
a tropical American cuckoo with a long tail

Examples

The pheasant cuckoo is rarely seen due to its elusive nature in dense forests.
During our birdwatching trip, we spotted a pheasant cuckoo displaying its long tail.
Many ornithologists are fascinated by the pheasant cuckoo's unique calls and secretive behavior.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cuckoo - a bird of the Cuculidae family, known for laying eggs in other birds' nests
