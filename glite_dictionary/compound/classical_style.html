classical style

1. NOUN

/ ˈklæsɪkəl staɪl / (AmE), / ˈklæsɪkəl staɪl / (BrE)
an artistic style, especially of ancient Greek art, emphasizing proportion, harmony, and balance
ancient Greek artistic style emphasizing proportion and harmony

Examples

The museum's new exhibit showcases sculptures in the classical style with their idealized forms and balanced proportions.
Many Renaissance artists were inspired by the classical style of ancient Greece.
The architecture of that temple reflects the harmony and proportion characteristic of the classical style.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Hellenistic style - later Greek artistic style emphasizing drama and movement
  • Antonym: Gothic style - artistic style characterized by pointed arches and elaborate ornamentation, contrasting classical simplicity
  • Antonym: Romanesque style - rounded-arch European art before Gothic, less harmonious than classical

Etymology

From Latin 'classicus' (relating to the highest class of citizens or of the first rank) and 'style' from Latin 'stilus' (writing instrument, manner of expression).
