the great and the good

1. NOUN

/ ðə ˈɡreɪt ənd ðə ˈɡʊd / (AmE), / ðə ˈɡreɪt ənd ðə ˈɡʊd / (BrE)
prominent and respected people in society, especially those with influence or moral authority
influential and respected people

Examples

The mayor invited the great and the good to the opening ceremony.
Decisions were made after consulting the great and the good from various industries.
Whenever there is a big event, the great and the good are sure to be present.

Synset Examples

The report was discussed by the great and the good in academia.
She mingled with the great and the good at the exclusive event.
The charity gala was attended by the great and the good of society.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: notables - well-known or important people
  • Synonym: elite - the most powerful, rich, or talented people in a group
