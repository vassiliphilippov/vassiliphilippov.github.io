yellow-leaf sickle pine

1. NOUN

/ ˈjɛloʊ liːf ˈsɪkəl paɪn / (AmE), / ˈjɛləʊ liːf ˈsɪkl paɪn / (BrE)
a species of rain forest tree or shrub native to New Caledonia, known for its conical crown and pale green, sickle-shaped leaves; host to the rare parasite yew
rain forest tree or shrub from New Caledonia

Examples

The yellow-leaf sickle pine is unique to New Caledonian rain forests and prized for its unusual foliage.
Researchers discovered a rare parasite living on the yellow-leaf sickle pine.
Gardeners appreciate the yellow-leaf sickle pine for its attractive, sickle-shaped leaves.
