assault and battery

1. NOUN

/ əˈsɔlt ənd ˈbætəri / (AmE), / əˈsɔːlt ənd ˈbætəri / (BrE)
The criminal offense involving both the threat or attempt to cause physical harm (assault) and actual physical contact or violence (battery) against another person.
criminal offense: threat and physical attack
legal

Examples

The suspect was charged with assault and battery after the altercation in the bar.
Committing assault and battery can lead to serious legal consequences.
The victim filed a lawsuit against him for assault and battery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assault - attempt or threat to cause harm without actual contact
  • Synonym: battery - actual physical contact or violence against another

Etymology

Legal term combining 'assault' (from Latin 'assultus', attack) and 'battery' (from Old French 'baterie', beating).

2. NOUN

/ əˌsɔlt ənd ˈbætəri / (AmE), / əˌsɔːlt ənd ˈbætəri / (BrE)
the crime of physically attacking another person, especially in a way that causes harm or injury
physical attack; legal term
legal

Examples

The suspect was found guilty of assault and battery after the trial.
She pressed charges for assault and battery following the altercation.
The law treats assault and battery as a serious criminal offense.

Synset Examples

The case involved allegations of assault and battery.
She filed a report for assault and battery.
He was charged with assault and battery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assault - an act that threatens physical harm to another
  • Synonym: battery - the act of physically attacking someone

Etymology

From legal terminology; 'assault' from Old French 'asalt' (attack), 'battery' from Old French 'baterie' (beating).
