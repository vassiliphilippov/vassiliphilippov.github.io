epidural anaesthesia

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛpɪˈdʊrəl ænəsˈθiʒə / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈdjʊərəl ˌanəsˈθiːziə / (BrE)
a type of regional anaesthesia produced by injecting an anaesthetic into the epidural space of the spinal cord, leading to loss of sensation in the lower body, commonly used during childbirth and certain surgeries
regional anaesthesia injected near the spinal cord
medical british

Examples

The doctor recommended an epidural anaesthesia to help manage her pain during labor.
She remained awake and comfortable throughout the procedure thanks to the epidural anaesthesia.
Some women prefer epidural anaesthesia for childbirth to minimize discomfort.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spinal anaesthesia - regional anaesthesia injected into the spinal fluid
  • Degree Lower: local anaesthesia - anaesthesia affecting a small area of the body
  • Degree Higher: general anaesthesia - anaesthesia causing total unconsciousness

Etymology

From Greek 'epi-' (upon) + Latin 'durus' (hard, referring to dura mater) + 'anaesthesia' (from Greek, meaning insensibility).
