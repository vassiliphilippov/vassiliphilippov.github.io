Eastern Samoa

1. PROPN

/ ˈiːstərn səˈmoʊə / (AmE), / ˈiːstən səˈməʊə / (BrE)
the United States territory comprising the eastern part of the Samoan Islands, officially known as American Samoa
US territory in the eastern Samoan Islands

Examples

The capital of Eastern Samoa is Pago Pago, located on the island of Tutuila.
Many tourists visit Eastern Samoa to experience its unique blend of American and Polynesian culture.
Official documents concerning Eastern Samoa are managed by the U.S. Department of the Interior.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: American Samoa - an unincorporated US territory in the South Pacific comprising the eastern Samoan Islands
