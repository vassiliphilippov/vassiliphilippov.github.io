croaker fish

1. NOUN

/ ˈkroʊkər fɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈkrəʊkə fɪʃ / (BrE)
A fish of the family Sciaenidae, named for the croaking or drumming sounds it produces, commonly found in warm coastal waters.
a fish that makes croaking sounds

Examples

Fishermen often catch croaker fish near the river mouth in summer.
The distinctive sound made by the croaker fish helps researchers identify them underwater.
Many recipes in coastal regions feature croaker fish as the main ingredient.

Synset Examples

The croaker fish is known for its unique sound.

Etymology

From 'croak', imitative of the sound made, plus 'fish'.
