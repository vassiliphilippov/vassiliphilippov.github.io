Eaton-Lambert syndrome

1. NOUN

/ ˈitən ˈlæmbɚt sɪnˌdroʊm / (AmE), / ˈiːtən ˈlæmbət sɪnˌdrəʊm / (BrE)
A rare autoimmune disorder, often associated with lung cancer, characterized by weakness and fatigue of muscles in the hips and thighs, back pain, and caused by antibodies interfering with neuromuscular junctions.
rare muscle weakness disorder, often linked to lung cancer
medical

Examples

The neurologist suspected Eaton-Lambert syndrome after noticing persistent muscle weakness in the patient's thighs.
People with Eaton-Lambert syndrome often experience increased strength with repeated movements of their muscles.
Treatment for Eaton-Lambert syndrome typically involves managing both the neurological symptoms and the underlying cancer.

Etymology

Named after American neurologists Edward H. Lambert and Lee Eaton, who first described the syndrome in the 1950s.
