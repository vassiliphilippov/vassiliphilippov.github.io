isoclinic line

1. NOUN

/ ˌaɪsoʊˈklɪnɪk laɪn / (AmE), / ˌaɪsəˈklɪnɪk laɪn / (BrE)
A line on a map connecting points where the magnetic inclination is the same.
line of equal magnetic inclination

Examples

The map displayed an isoclinic line passing through several regions, indicating areas with the same magnetic inclination.
Navigators use the isoclinic line to determine how a compass needle tilts at different locations.
The scientists traced the isoclinic line to study changes in Earth's magnetic field over time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: isogram - a line connecting points of equal value
  • Synonym: isogon - a line connecting points of equal magnetic declination

Etymology

From Greek iso- (equal) + klinein (to lean), referring to equal angles of magnetic inclination.
