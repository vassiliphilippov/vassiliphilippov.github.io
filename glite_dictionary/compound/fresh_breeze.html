fresh breeze

1. NOUN

/ frɛʃ briːz / (AmE), / frɛʃ briːz / (BrE)
a wind with a speed of 19–24 knots (22–28 mph or 29–38 km/h), corresponding to Force 5 on the Beaufort scale
wind of 19–24 knots; Beaufort scale Force 5

Examples

Sailors welcomed the fresh breeze that carried them swiftly across the bay.
A fresh breeze made the flags stand out sharply at the harbor.
Forecasts predict a fresh breeze for most of the afternoon, ideal for windsurfing.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: gentle breeze - a wind with a speed of 8-12 knots; Beaufort scale Force 3
  • Degree Higher: strong breeze - a wind with a speed of 25-31 knots; Beaufort scale Force 6
