the t-word

1. NOUN

/ ði ˈtiː wɝːd / (AmE), / ði ˈtiː wɜːd / (BrE)
a euphemistic expression used to refer obliquely to a word or topic considered taboo or sensitive
euphemism for a taboo or sensitive word

Examples

The journalist carefully avoided the t-word during the interview to prevent controversy.
In academic circles, using the t-word can spark heated debates about language and sensitivity.
She knew that bringing up the t-word would make everyone uncomfortable.

Synset Examples

Avoiding the t-word helped keep the conversation friendly.
She cringed at the mention of the t-word.
He didn't want to use the t-word during the discussion.

Etymology

Patterned after the naming convention for taboo words, such as 'the n-word' and 'the f-word', where the initial letter of the word is used plus 'word'.
