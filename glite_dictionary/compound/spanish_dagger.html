Spanish dagger

1. NOUN

/ ˈspænɪʃ ˈdæɡər / (AmE), / ˈspænɪʃ ˈdaɡə / (BrE)
A yucca plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, with sword-shaped leaves and clusters of white flowers.
a yucca plant with sword-shaped leaves

Examples

The Spanish dagger in our garden bloomed this spring with beautiful white flowers.
Many animals use the dense leaves of the Spanish dagger for shelter in the desert.
We planted a Spanish dagger to add a touch of southwestern flair to the yard.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yucca - a genus of plants with sword-shaped leaves and white flowers

2. NOUN

/ ˈspænɪʃ ˈdæɡər / (AmE), / ˈspænɪʃ ˈdaɡə / (BrE)

a yucca plant with short trunk and smooth leaves

Examples

The Spanish dagger produces tall spikes of white flowers each spring.
Gardeners in the region often plant Spanish daggers as ornamental shrubs.
Beware of the sharp leaves on the Spanish dagger when trimming around it.
