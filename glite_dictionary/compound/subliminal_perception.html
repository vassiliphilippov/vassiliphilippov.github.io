subliminal perception

1. NOUN

/ sʌbˈlɪmɪnəl pərˈsɛpʃən / (AmE), / sʌbˈlɪmɪnəl pəˈsɛpʃən / (BrE)
the process of perceiving or reacting to stimuli without conscious awareness, typically below the threshold of conscious perception
perception without conscious awareness
medical

Examples

Advertising often tries to use subliminal perception to influence consumers without their direct awareness.
Some psychologists debate whether subliminal perception can truly affect a person's decisions.
Research has shown that subliminal perception can cause people to react to images they do not consciously see.

Synset Examples

Subliminal perception or cognition is a subset of unconscious cognition where the forms of unconscious cognition also include attending to one signal in a noisy environment while unconsciously keeping track of other signals (e.g one voice out of many in a crowded room) and tasks done automatically (e.g. driving a car).

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unconscious perception - perception occurring without conscious awareness

Etymology

Formed from 'subliminal,' from Latin 'sub' (below) + 'limen' (threshold), and 'perception' from Latin 'perceptio.'
