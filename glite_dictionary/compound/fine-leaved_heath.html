fine-leaved heath

1. NOUN

/ faɪn liːvd hiːθ / (AmE), / faɪn liːvd hiːθ / (BrE)
a low European shrub (Erica cinerea) with slender leaves and purple-red flowers, commonly found in heathlands.
low European shrub with purple-red flowers

Examples

The hillside was covered in fine-leaved heath, adding a splash of color to the landscape.
Gardeners often use fine-leaved heath to create natural-looking borders in rock gardens.
Fine-leaved heath thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and blooms vibrantly in late spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heather - a low, branching shrub with small purple flowers, similar in appearance to fine-leaved heath
