sulcus centralis

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌlkəs sɛnˈtreɪlɪs / (AmE), / ˈsʌlkəs sɛnˈtreɪlɪs / (BrE)
A deep groove on the lateral surface of each cerebral hemisphere that separates the frontal lobe from the parietal lobe; also known as the central sulcus.
central groove separating brain lobes
medical

Examples

Damage near the sulcus centralis can impair both movement and sensation.
The location of the sulcus centralis is critical for neurosurgeons planning brain operations.
In anatomy class, students learned to identify the sulcus centralis on brain diagrams.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: central sulcus - prominent groove separating frontal and parietal lobes of the brain

Etymology

From Latin 'sulcus' (furrow, groove) + 'centralis' (central).
