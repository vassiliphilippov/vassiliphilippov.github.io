common roundworm

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmən ˈraʊndˌwɜrm / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən ˈraʊndˌwɜːm / (BrE)
a type of intestinal parasitic worm (Ascaris lumbricoides) that infects humans and pigs, causing disease
intestinal parasitic worm

Examples

The common roundworm is transmitted through contaminated food and water.
A severe infection with the common roundworm can cause abdominal pain and malnutrition.
Farmers must take precautions to prevent common roundworm infections among their livestock.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ascaris - genus of large intestinal roundworms
  • Degree Lower: pinworm - a different type of small roundworm parasite
