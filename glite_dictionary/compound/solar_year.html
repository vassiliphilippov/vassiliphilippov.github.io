solar year

1. NOUN

/ ˈsoʊlər jɪr / (AmE), / ˈsəʊlə jɪə / (BrE)
the period of time it takes the Earth to complete one orbit around the Sun, usually measured between two successive vernal equinoxes, approximately 365.24 days
the time for Earth to orbit the Sun (c. 365.24 days)

Examples

A solar year is about eleven days longer than a lunar year.
Ancient civilizations often based their calendars on the solar year.
The extra quarter day in the solar year is why we have a leap year every four years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calendar year - a year as reckoned by the calendar, typically 365 or 366 days
  • Antonym: lunar year - a year based on the cycles of the moon, about 354 days

Etymology

From Latin 'solaris' (of the sun) and 'year'.
