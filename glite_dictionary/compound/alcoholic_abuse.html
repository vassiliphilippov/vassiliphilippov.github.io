alcoholic abuse

1. NOUN

/ ˌælkəˈhɑlɪk əˈbjus / (AmE), / ˌalkəˈhɒlɪk əˈbjuːs / (BrE)
the excessive or harmful consumption of alcohol or alcoholic drinks, often resulting in negative health, social, or legal consequences
excessive use of alcohol

Examples

Alcoholic abuse can lead to serious health problems such as liver disease and heart failure.
Families often suffer greatly as a result of prolonged alcoholic abuse.
Programs to prevent alcoholic abuse are essential in many communities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alcoholism - chronic, compulsive drinking resulting in dependency
