cyber-terrorism

1. NOUN

/ ˌsaɪbər ˈter.əˌrɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˌsaɪ.bə ˈter.əˌrɪz.əm / (BrE)
The politically or ideologically motivated use of computers and information technology to cause severe disruption or fear by attacking electronic communication networks.
use of technology for ideological network attacks

Examples

The government has implemented new measures to prevent cyber-terrorism against critical infrastructure.
Many experts believe that cyber-terrorism poses a growing threat in the digital age.
The attack on the banking system was suspected to be an act of cyber-terrorism by a foreign group.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cyberattack - an attack via computers or networks
  • Synonym: terrorism - the unlawful use of violence to achieve political aims

Etymology

Formed from cyber-, relating to computers or the internet, and terrorism.
