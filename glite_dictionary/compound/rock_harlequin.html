rock harlequin

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɑk ˌhɑr.lɪˈkwɪn / (AmE), / ˈrɒk ˌhɑː.lɪˈkwɪn / (BrE)
A glaucous herb native to northeastern United States and Canada, with loose racemes of yellow-tipped pink flowers; sometimes classified under genus Fumaria.
North American herb with pink, yellow-tipped flowers

Examples

We spotted a rock harlequin growing along the rocky outcrop during our hike.
The delicate yellow-tipped pink flowers make the rock harlequin a unique addition to wildflower gardens.
Botanists often debate whether the rock harlequin belongs in the genus Corydalis or Fumaria.
