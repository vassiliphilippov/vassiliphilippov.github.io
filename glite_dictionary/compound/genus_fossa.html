genus Fossa

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈfɒs.ə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈfɒs.ə / (BrE)
A monotypic genus of carnivorous mammals from Madagascar, closely related to palm civets, containing the fossa.
monotypic genus of Madagascar carnivores

Examples

The genus Fossa includes only one species, the fossa, native to Madagascar.
Scientists have studied the unique evolutionary adaptations of the genus Fossa in the forests of Madagascar.
Unlike other civets, the genus Fossa is limited to a single representative species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fossa - a carnivorous mammal native to Madagascar

Etymology

From Latin 'fossa' (ditch, trench), coined in zoological taxonomy.
