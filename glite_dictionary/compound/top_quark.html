top quark

1. NOUN

/ tɑp kwɑrk / (AmE), / tɒp kwɑːk / (BrE)
a type of elementary particle with a charge of +2/3 and a mass more than 100,000 times that of an electron, hypothesized and later confirmed in particle physics
elementary particle in physics

Examples

The discovery of the top quark in 1995 was a major milestone in particle physics.
Physicists study the properties of the top quark to better understand the fundamental forces of nature.
The top quark is much heavier than any other known quark.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: bottom quark - a type of elementary particle, third-generation quark paired with the top quark

Etymology

Named 'top' following the convention of naming the third-generation quarks as 'top' and 'bottom.'
