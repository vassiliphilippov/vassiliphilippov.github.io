middle name

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪd.əl neɪm / (AmE), / ˈmɪd.əl neɪm / (BrE)
A name given to a person between their first name and surname, often used to distinguish them from others with the same first and last names.
name between first name and surname

Examples

Her middle name is Marie, just like her grandmother.
Please write your middle name on the application form.
He doesn't usually use his middle name except on official documents.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɪd.əl neɪm / (AmE), / ˈmɪd.əl neɪm / (BrE)
a name given to a person that comes between their first name and surname, often used to distinguish them from others or for family/tradition reasons
a name between first name and surname

Examples

On official documents, you are often asked to write your middle name as well as your first and last names.
Emily only discovered her mother's middle name after finding her birth certificate.
He was named after his uncle, which is why his middle name is David.

Synset Examples

Her middle name is Rose, after her grandmother.
He never uses his middle name in introductions.

Related Terms
