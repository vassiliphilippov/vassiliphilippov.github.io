Hebrew script

1. NOUN

/ ˈhiːbruː skrɪpt / (AmE), / ˈhiːbruː skrɪpt / (BrE)
the alphabetic writing system used for the Hebrew language and, later, for languages such as Yiddish and Ladino.
alphabet used for Hebrew, Yiddish, and Ladino

Examples

The Torah is traditionally written in Hebrew script on parchment scrolls.
Many Jewish communities preserved their language using Hebrew script, even when speaking Yiddish or Ladino.
The museum displayed manuscripts in ancient Hebrew script dating back to the 10th century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alphabet - a set of letters or symbols in a fixed order used for writing a language

Etymology

From Hebrew *ketav* (writing) and English 'script', meaning writing system.
