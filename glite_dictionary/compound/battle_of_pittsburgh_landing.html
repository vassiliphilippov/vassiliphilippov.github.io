battle of Pittsburgh Landing

1. PROPN

/ ˈbæt̬l əv ˈpɪtsbɚɡ ˈlændɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbætl əv ˈpɪtsbɜːɡ ˈlændɪŋ / (BrE)
A major battle fought in 1862 during the American Civil War, also known as the Battle of Shiloh, marked by heavy casualties and ending with the withdrawal of Confederate troops without a decisive Union victory.
Civil War battle in 1862, also called Battle of Shiloh

Examples

The battle of Pittsburgh Landing was a turning point early in the Civil War.
Many soldiers lost their lives at the battle of Pittsburgh Landing in Tennessee.
Historians often debate the strategies used during the battle of Pittsburgh Landing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: battle of Shiloh - another name for the battle fought near Pittsburgh Landing in 1862
