brain drain

1. NOUN

/ ˈbreɪn ˌdreɪn / (AmE), / ˈbreɪn ˌdreɪn / (BrE)
the movement of highly educated or skilled people from their own country to another country where they can work in better conditions or earn more money, resulting in a loss of talent at home.
loss of skilled professionals from a country or region

Examples

Many developing countries face a significant brain drain as talented professionals move abroad for better opportunities.
The government is introducing incentives to address the brain drain problem in the science and technology sectors.
The persistent brain drain has left the nation's healthcare system understaffed and vulnerable.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: brain gain - increase of skilled or educated people entering a country

Etymology

Calque of British English, from 'brain' + 'drain', first used in the 1960s to describe emigration of scientists and technologists from the UK.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbreɪn ˌdreɪn / (AmE), / ˈbreɪn ˌdreɪn / (BrE)
the emigration of highly educated or skilled people from one country, organization, or region to another, typically in search of better opportunities
emigration of skilled people for better opportunities

Examples

The country is trying to reverse the brain drain by improving research facilities and salaries.
Many medical professionals are contributing to the brain drain by moving abroad for better opportunities.
The government has set up new programs to address the growing brain drain problem.

Synset Examples

Efforts are underway to combat the brain drain in the region.
High taxes are contributing to the brain drain we see today.
The city is experiencing a brain drain as young professionals leave.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: emigration - the act of leaving one's country to settle in another
  • Antonym: brain gain - an increase in the number of highly skilled or educated people arriving in a region

Etymology

Formed from "brain" (intelligence, skilled people) and "drain" (outflow or loss).
