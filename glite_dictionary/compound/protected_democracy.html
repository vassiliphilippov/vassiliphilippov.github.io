protected democracy

1. NOUN

/ prəˈtɛktɪd dɪˈmɑkrəsi / (AmE), / prəˈtɛktɪd dɪˈmɒkrəsi / (BrE)
a form of democracy that includes institutional safeguards to prevent threats to democratic processes and guarantees the protection of citizens' rights
democracy with safeguards and guaranteed rights

Examples

In a protected democracy, institutions are designed to resist authoritarian threats.
Many scholars debate whether constitutional courts are essential to a protected democracy.
Activists argued that media freedom is a cornerstone of any protected democracy.

Synset Examples

She advocated for reforms to strengthen the protected democracy.
A protected democracy ensures the rights of its citizens.
They fought hard to maintain their protected democracy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: liberal democracy - a democracy characterized by free elections, rule of law, and protected civil liberties
  • Antonym: illiberal democracy - a democracy with elections but weak protections for individual rights
