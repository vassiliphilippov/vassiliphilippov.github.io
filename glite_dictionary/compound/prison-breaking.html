prison-breaking

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɪzənˌbreɪkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈprɪzənˌbreɪkɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of escaping from prison or jail, especially by force or stealth
an escape from prison or jail

Examples

The gang spent months planning the prison-breaking that shocked the nation.
After the daring prison-breaking, the authorities launched a massive manhunt.
He was sentenced to five more years for his role in the prison-breaking.

Synset Examples

the breakout was carefully planned

Related Terms

  • Synonym: breakout - an escape from prison, especially by force
  • Degree Lower: prison - a building where people are kept as a punishment for a crime
