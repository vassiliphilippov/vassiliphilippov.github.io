executive branch

1. NOUN

/ ɪɡˈzɛkjətɪv bræntʃ / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzɛkjʊtɪv brɑːntʃ / (BrE)
the part of a government responsible for implementing and enforcing laws, typically headed by a president or prime minister
branch of government enforcing laws

Examples

The executive branch is responsible for enforcing the laws passed by Congress.
The president is the head of the executive branch in the United States.
Checks and balances ensure that the executive branch does not become too powerful.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: legislative branch - the branch of government that makes laws
  • Antonym: judicial branch - the branch of government that interprets laws
