insulin-dependent diabetes mellitus

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪn.sə.lɪn dɪˈpɛn.dənt daɪəˈbiː.t̬iːz məˈlaɪ.t̬əs / (AmE), / ˈɪn.sjʊ.lɪn dɪˈpɛn.dənt daɪəˈbiː.tiːz mɛˈlaɪ.təs / (BrE)
a form of diabetes mellitus that usually develops early in life, in which the body cannot produce enough insulin and regular insulin injections are needed to control blood sugar levels
diabetes needing insulin injections
medical

Examples

After being diagnosed with insulin-dependent diabetes mellitus, she began a strict regimen of insulin injections and diet control.
The main symptoms of insulin-dependent diabetes mellitus include excessive thirst and frequent urination.
Researchers are studying genetic factors that increase the risk of insulin-dependent diabetes mellitus in children.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: type 1 diabetes - another term for insulin-dependent diabetes mellitus
  • Antonym: non-insulin-dependent diabetes mellitus - a form of diabetes not requiring insulin injections, usually develops later in life
  • Antonym: type 2 diabetes - chronic condition affecting glucose metabolism, usually does not require insulin injections

Etymology

From 'insulin' (a hormone) + 'dependent' + Latin 'diabetes mellitus' (literally, 'sweet siphon').
