character assassination

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɛrɪktər əˌsæsəˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈkærɪktə əˌsæsɪˈneɪʃn / (BrE)
the act of deliberately damaging someone's reputation, especially through false or unfair accusations
deliberate attack on someone's reputation

Examples

The politician became a victim of character assassination during the heated election campaign.
Celebrity scandals often lead to intense media character assassination whether the claims are true or not.
She sued the magazine for character assassination after they published a damaging article about her.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: defamation - the act of damaging someone's good reputation through false statements
  • Synonym: libel - a published false statement damaging a person's reputation
  • Antonym: praise - expression of approval or admiration

Etymology

From 'character' (person's reputation) + 'assassination' (murder, destruction of a person).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkærɪktɚ əˌsæsəˈneɪʃ(ə)n / (AmE), / ˈkærɪktə əˌsæsɪˈneɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
The act of intentionally damaging someone's reputation by making false or misleading statements about them.
deliberate destruction of reputation

Examples

The politician was a victim of character assassination after false rumors spread about him.
Her career suffered greatly due to continuous character assassination by her rivals.
They accused the reporter of engaging in character assassination rather than objective journalism.

Synset Examples

His remarks were nothing but character assassination.
She faced character assassination after the scandal broke.
They resorted to character assassination to undermine her credibility.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: defamation - the act of damaging someone's good reputation; slander or libel
  • Synonym: libel - the act of making a false written statement that damages a person's reputation
  • Synonym: slander - the action or crime of making a false spoken statement damaging to a person's reputation
