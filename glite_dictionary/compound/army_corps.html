army corps

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːrmi kɔːr / (AmE), / ˈɑːmi kɔː / (BrE)
A large military unit or formation, typically consisting of two or more divisions with supporting units.
large military unit of several divisions

Examples

The general commanded the entire army corps during the campaign.
A new army corps was deployed to the region to reinforce the defenses.
She studied the structure of an army corps for her military history project.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: division - a military unit that forms part of an army corps and is made up of brigades or regiments
  • Degree Higher: army group - a larger military formation, consisting of several army corps
