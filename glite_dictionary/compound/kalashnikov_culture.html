Kalashnikov culture

1. NOUN

/ kəˈlæʃ.nɪ.kɑv ˈkʌl.tʃər / (AmE), / kəˈlæʃ.nɪ.kɒf ˈkʌl.tʃə / (BrE)
a social environment or set of attitudes in which political disputes are resolved through the use of firearms, especially automatic rifles like the Kalashnikov
social use of firearms to resolve disputes

Examples

Many analysts discuss the persistence of Kalashnikov culture in parts of the Middle East.
The film explores the dangerous legacy of Kalashnikov culture among local tribes.
Attempts to reduce Kalashnikov culture have faced strong resistance from armed militias.

Synset Examples

the Kalashnikov culture in Afghanistan

Etymology

Kalashnikov from the Russian designer Mikhail Kalashnikov, inventor of the AK-47 rifle, whose widespread use symbolizes armed culture.
