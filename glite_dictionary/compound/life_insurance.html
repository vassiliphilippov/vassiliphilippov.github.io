life insurance

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪf ɪnˌʃʊr.əns / (AmE), / ˈlaɪf ɪnˌʃɔː.rəns / (BrE)
A type of insurance policy that pays a sum of money to designated beneficiaries upon the death of the insured person.
insurance paying at death to beneficiaries

Examples

He bought life insurance to provide for his family in case something happened to him.
Many people compare different life insurance policies before choosing the right one.
Some employers offer life insurance as part of their benefits package.

Synset Examples

in England they call life insurance ‘life assurance’

Related Terms

  • Synonym: life assurance - insurance paying a sum on death (British term)
