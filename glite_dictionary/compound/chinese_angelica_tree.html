Chinese angelica tree

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃaɪˌniz ænˈdʒɛlɪkə tri / (AmE), / ˈtʃaɪˌniːz anˈdʒɛlɪkə triː / (BrE)
a small tree native to China, similar to the American angelica tree but with fewer prickles, cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.
tree species native to China

Examples

The Chinese angelica tree is often grown in gardens for its exotic appearance and medicinal uses.
Unlike its American cousin, the Chinese angelica tree has softer spines and smoother bark.
Researchers are studying extracts from the Chinese angelica tree for their potential health benefits.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: American angelica tree - a similar small tree native to North America with more prickles
