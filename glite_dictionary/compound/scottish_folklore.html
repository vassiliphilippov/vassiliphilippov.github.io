Scottish folklore

1. NOUN

/ ˈskɑːtɪʃ ˈfoʊk.lɔːr / (AmE), / ˈskɒtɪʃ ˈfəʊk.lɔː / (BrE)
the traditional stories, legends, and beliefs originating from Scotland, passed down through generations
traditional stories and legends from Scotland

Examples

Many creatures from Scottish folklore, such as kelpies and selkies, have inspired modern fantasy writers.
He has always been fascinated by the ghosts and fairies featured in Scottish folklore.
Scottish folklore is full of mysterious tales about Loch Ness and its legendary monster.

Synset Examples

Sawney is a popular character in Scottish folklore.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: folklore - the traditional beliefs, customs, and stories of a community
