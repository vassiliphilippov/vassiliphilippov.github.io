sterling bloc

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɝːlɪŋ blɑk / (AmE), / ˈstɜːlɪŋ blɒk / (BrE)
the group of countries whose currencies are linked to the British pound sterling, especially during the interwar and early postwar periods
group of countries linked to the British pound

Examples

After World War II, many former colonies remained part of the sterling bloc for economic stability.
The collapse of the sterling bloc in the 1970s marked a shift in global financial relations.
Britain coordinated exchange rates within the sterling bloc to maintain trade advantages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: currency bloc - group of countries with closely linked currencies

Etymology

From 'sterling' (British pound) and 'bloc' (French, 'block', a group united for a purpose).
