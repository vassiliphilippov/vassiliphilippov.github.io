white-tailed kite

1. NOUN

/ waɪt teɪld kaɪt / (AmE), / waɪt teɪld kaɪt / (BrE)
a grey-and-white bird of prey found in the Americas, especially in warm and tropical regions
American bird of prey with a white tail

Examples

We watched a white-tailed kite soaring gracefully over the grasslands at sunset.
The white-tailed kite hunts small mammals by hovering above open fields.
In California, the population of white-tailed kites has increased due to conservation efforts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kite - a medium-sized bird of prey with long wings and tail
