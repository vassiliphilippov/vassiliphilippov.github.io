avoirdupois system

1. NOUN

/ ˌævərdʊˈpɔɪz ˈsɪstəm / (AmE), / ˌavədjuːˈpwɑːz ˈsɪstəm / (BrE)
a system of weights based on a pound of 16 ounces, commonly used in the United States and other English-speaking countries for everyday goods
system of weights with 16-ounce pound

Examples

The United States still uses the avoirdupois system for most common weights.
Historically, the avoirdupois system was adopted for trade in English-speaking countries.
He converted the recipe measurements from the avoirdupois system to metric units.

Synset Examples

He weighed his groceries using the avoirdupois system.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imperial system - British system of weights and measures where pounds, ounces, and stones are used
  • Antonym: metric system - decimal-based system of measurement based on the kilogram, meter, and liter

Etymology

From Old French 'avoir de pois' (goods of weight), referring to merchandise sold by weight.
