fly bridge

1. NOUN

/ flaɪ brɪdʒ / (AmE), / flaɪ brɪdʒ / (BrE)
a small, often open, deck above the pilot house on a ship, used as the highest navigational bridge
highest, open deck above a ship's pilot house

Examples

The captain climbed up to the fly bridge for a better view as the yacht entered the harbor.
From the fly bridge, all the ship's navigational equipment was clearly visible and easy to operate.
During calm weather, passengers enjoyed relaxing on the fly bridge to watch the sunset.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: bridge - the main navigational platform on a ship
