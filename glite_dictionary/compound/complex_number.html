complex number

1. NOUN

/ kəmˈplɛks ˈnʌmbɚ / (AmE), / kəmˈplɛks ˈnʌmbə / (BrE)
a number in the form a + bi, where a and b are real numbers and i is the square root of -1
number with a real and imaginary part

Examples

The equation has no real solution but it can be solved using a complex number.
Electrical engineers often use complex numbers to analyze alternating currents.
Every real number can be thought of as a complex number with an imaginary part of zero.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: real number - a number that can represent a distance along a line, without an imaginary part
  • Degree Lower: imaginary number - a number that is a multiple of the square root of -1

Etymology

From Latin 'complexus' (entwined, entwined together) and English 'number'. In mathematics, used to describe numbers combining real and imaginary parts.
