direction finder

1. NOUN

/ dəˈrɛkʃən ˌfaɪndər / (AmE), / dɪˈrɛkʃən ˌfaɪndə / (BrE)
a device for determining the direction from which a radio signal is received, used in navigation and radio communication
device to locate radio signal direction

Examples

The pilot used a direction finder to determine the location of the distress signal.
Modern ships often rely on automatic direction finders for navigation at sea.
Radio enthusiasts sometimes build their own direction finder to track down signal sources.
