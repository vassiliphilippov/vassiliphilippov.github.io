awning deck

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔnɪŋ dɛk / (AmE), / ˈɔːnɪŋ dɛk / (BrE)
the upper deck of a passenger ship, typically covered or partially sheltered, often used for seating and recreation
upper deck of a passenger ship

Examples

Passengers often gathered on the awning deck to enjoy the sea breeze and the view.
The awning deck was crowded with travelers watching the distant shoreline fade away.
During sunny afternoons, deck chairs would quickly fill up on the awning deck.

Etymology

From 'awning' (a rooflike shelter) + 'deck' (floor of a ship); nautical term from the 19th century.
