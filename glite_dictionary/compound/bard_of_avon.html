Bard of Avon

1. NOUN

/ bɑrd əv ˈeɪvən / (AmE), / bɑːd əv ˈeɪvən / (BrE)
a poetic epithet for William Shakespeare, regarded as one of the greatest English writers (1564–1616)
epithet for William Shakespeare

Examples

Many literary scholars refer to Shakespeare as the Bard of Avon in their essays.
Tourists flock to Stratford-upon-Avon to celebrate the legacy of the Bard of Avon.
The Bard of Avon wrote plays and poems that have endured for centuries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: William Shakespeare - English playwright and poet, 1564–1616

Etymology

From 'bard' (poet) and 'Avon', the river in Stratford-upon-Avon, Shakespeare's birthplace.
