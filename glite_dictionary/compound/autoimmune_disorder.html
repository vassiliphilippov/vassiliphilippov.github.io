autoimmune disorder

1. NOUN

/ ˌɔtoʊɪˈmjun dɪˌsɔrdər / (AmE), / ˌɔːtəʊɪˈmjuːn dɪˌsɔːdə / (BrE)
A disease in which the body's immune system attacks its own healthy tissues by mistake.
disease where immune system attacks itself
medical

Examples

Rheumatoid arthritis is an autoimmune disorder that causes joint pain and swelling.
Doctors suspect she has developed an autoimmune disorder after noticing unusual antibodies in her blood.
Lupus is another serious autoimmune disorder that can affect multiple organs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: autoimmune disease - disease caused by an immune response against the body's own tissues

Etymology

From 'autoimmune', combining Greek 'auto-' (self) and 'immune', with 'disorder' meaning a disease or abnormal condition.
