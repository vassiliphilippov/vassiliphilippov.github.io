Pied Piper of Hamelin

1. PROPN

/ paɪd ˈpaɪpər əv ˈhæməlɪn / (AmE), / paɪd ˈpaɪpə əv ˈhæməlɪn / (BrE)
the title character in a German folk tale and Robert Browning's poem, known for leading away the children of Hamelin by playing his pipe
legendary figure who lured away Hamelin's children

Examples

The story of the Pied Piper of Hamelin has been retold in countless books and films.
Many view him as a modern Pied Piper of Hamelin, leading people with charismatic promises.
The legend of the Pied Piper of Hamelin serves as a warning against broken promises.

Etymology

Named after the town of Hamelin, Germany, and the character described in Robert Browning’s 1842 poem based on medieval legend.
