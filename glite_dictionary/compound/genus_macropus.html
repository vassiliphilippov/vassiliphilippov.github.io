genus Macropus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˌmækˈroʊpəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˌmækˈrəʊpəs / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Macropodidae, comprising the typical kangaroos and wallabies.
genus of kangaroos and wallabies

Examples

The genus Macropus includes the well-known red kangaroo and several species of wallabies.
Scientists have studied the evolutionary history of the genus Macropus in Australia.
All members of the genus Macropus are characterized by their powerful hind legs and long feet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kangaroo - a large Australian marsupial of the genus Macropus
  • Synonym: wallaby - a small or medium-sized macropod of Australia and New Guinea

Etymology

From Greek 'makros' (long) and 'pous' (foot), referring to the long feet of kangaroos and wallabies.
