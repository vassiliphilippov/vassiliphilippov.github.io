Fox language

1. NOUN

/ fɑks ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / fɒks ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Algonquian language spoken by the Fox (Meskwaki) people, native to the Midwest region of North America.
Algonquian language of the Fox people

Examples

The Fox language is still spoken by some members of the Meskwaki tribe today.
Linguists have documented the Fox language to help preserve it for future generations.
Anthropologists studied the grammar and oral traditions of the Fox language.

Etymology

Named after the Fox (Meskwaki) people, with 'Fox' as an English rendering of the tribal name.
