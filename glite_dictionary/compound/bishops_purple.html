bishop's purple

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɪʃəps ˈpɝpl̩ / (AmE), / ˈbɪʃəps ˈpɜːpl̩ / (BrE)
A moderate reddish-purple color, slightly bluer and lighter than heliotrope and duller than eupatorium purple.
a moderate reddish-purple color

Examples

The velvet drapes were dyed a deep bishop's purple for the cathedral's restoration.
She admired the bishop's purple in the artist's palette, finding it both rich and subtle.
The choir's new robes were a distinctive bishop's purple that stood out in the candlelight.

Synset Examples

He wrote that it turned ‘the colour of bishop's violet’ and that he would bear the signs of it for weeks.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: heliotrope - a pale purplish-pink color

Etymology

From the association with the traditional color of bishops' ceremonial robes.
