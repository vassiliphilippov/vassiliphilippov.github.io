Chilean firebush

1. NOUN

/ tʃɪˈliən ˈfaɪɚˌbʊʃ / (AmE), / tʃɪˈliːən ˈfaɪəˌbʊʃ / (BrE)
a South American shrub or small tree (Embothrium coccineum) with bright scarlet-crimson flowers, native to the Andes and often cultivated for its striking display
South American shrub/tree with scarlet flowers

Examples

The Chilean firebush is prized for its brilliant red blossoms, adding vivid color to many gardens.
In its native habitat, the Chilean firebush grows along the slopes of the Andes Mountains.
Gardeners admire the Chilean firebush not just for its beauty but also for its resilience to harsh weather.
