double Dutch

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʌb.əl ˈdʌtʃ / (AmE), / ˌdʌb.əl ˈdʌtʃ / (BrE)
A jump rope game in which two long ropes are turned in opposite directions and one or more players jump over them.
jump rope game with two ropes

Examples

Children at the playground loved playing double Dutch during recess.
She practiced double Dutch every afternoon to prepare for the city competition.
Learning how to jump into a fast-moving double Dutch game can be quite a challenge.

Etymology

Likely from the association of 'Dutch' with things considered foreign or complicated in English; usage attested in U.S. urban communities since the early 20th century.
