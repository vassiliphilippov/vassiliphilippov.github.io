Eastern Turki

1. NOUN

/ ˈiːstərn ˈtɝːki / (AmE), / ˈiːstən ˈtɜːki / (BrE)
a Turkic literary language used in medieval Central Asia, named after the eastern territories of the Mongol Empire
historic literary language of Central Asia

Examples

Scholars have found many manuscripts written in Eastern Turki in the region of Xinjiang.
The epic poetry of Eastern Turki greatly influenced later Central Asian literature.
He spent several years learning Eastern Turki to study ancient texts.

Etymology

Composed of 'Eastern' (direction) and 'Turki' (pertaining to Turkish/Turkic peoples).
