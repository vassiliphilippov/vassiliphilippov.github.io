solar time

1. NOUN

/ ˈsoʊlɚ taɪm / (AmE), / ˈsəʊlə taɪm / (BrE)
time measured by the position of the Sun in the sky, usually as the interval between two successive transits of the Sun over the same meridian
time measured by Sun's position

Examples

The ancient Egyptians developed their calendar using solar time rather than lunar cycles.
Adjustments to clocks are sometimes made to reconcile standard time with solar time.
Solar time varies during the year because of Earth's elliptical orbit and axial tilt.

Synset Examples

In September the Sun takes less time (as measured by an accurate clock) to make an apparent revolution than it does in December; 24 ‘hours’ of solar time can be 21 seconds less or 29 seconds more than 24 hours of clock time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mean solar time - time based on the average length of a solar day throughout the year
  • Antonym: sidereal time - time reckoned by the positions of the stars rather than the Sun

Etymology

From Latin sol (sun) + time; scientific usage since the 18th century.
