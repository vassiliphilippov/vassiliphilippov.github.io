cultural Marxist

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌl.tʃə.rəl ˈmɑːrks.ɪst / (AmE), / ˈkʌl.tʃə.rəl ˈmɑːks.ɪst / (BrE)
A person who advocates or is associated with cultural Marxism, a theory in sociology that critiques capitalist culture and ideology; often used pejoratively, especially in discussions of perceived influence in societal institutions.
proponent of cultural Marxism theory

Examples

He accused them of being cultural Marxists who want to change traditional values.
Some commentators warn about the influence of cultural Marxists in universities.
The article argued that cultural Marxists are reshaping societal norms through media and education.

Synset Examples

He accused them of being cultural Marxists in their approach.
Critics labeled the movement as driven by cultural Marxists.
The debate focused on the influence of cultural Marxists in education.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Marxist - a person who supports the theories of Karl Marx

Etymology

Formed from 'cultural' and 'Marxist', referring to the adaptation of Marxist theory to cultural analysis.
