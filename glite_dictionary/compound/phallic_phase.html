phallic phase

1. NOUN

/ ˈfælɪk feɪz / (AmE), / ˈfælɪk feɪz / (BrE)
(in psychoanalysis) the third stage of a child's psychosexual development, during which the genitals are the primary focus of pleasure and the child becomes aware of anatomical sex differences.
psychoanalytic stage of child development
medical

Examples

According to Freudian theory, the phallic phase occurs between the ages of three and six.
Psychologists debated the impact of experiences during the phallic phase on adult personality.
Some critics question the validity of the concept of the phallic phase in modern developmental psychology.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: latency period - the subsequent psychosexual stage following the phallic phase
  • Degree Lower: anal stage - the preceding psychosexual stage before the phallic phase

Etymology

From Greek phallos (phallus, penis) + English phase.
