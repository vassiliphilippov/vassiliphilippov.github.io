Egyptian paper reed

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈdʒɪpʃən ˈpeɪpɚ riːd / (AmE), / ɪˈdʒɪpʃən ˈpeɪpə riːd / (BrE)
A tall sedge native to the Nile valley, known for its fibrous stems historically used to make paper, mats, and other items.
tall Nile sedge used for papermaking

Examples

The ancient Egyptians used Egyptian paper reed to make scrolls for writing.
After visiting the wetlands, the botanist collected samples of Egyptian paper reed for study.
Egyptian paper reed grows abundantly along the banks of the river.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: papyrus - the plant or the paper made from it
