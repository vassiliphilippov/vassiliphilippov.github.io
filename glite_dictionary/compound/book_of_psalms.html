Book of Psalms

1. NOUN

/ bʊk əv ˈsɑːmz / (AmE), / bʊk əv ˈsɑːmz / (BrE)
the collection of religious songs, prayers, and poems included as a book in the Old Testament of the Bible, traditionally used in Jewish and Christian worship
a book of the Bible containing psalms

Examples

She found comfort reading passages from the Book of Psalms during difficult times.
Many hymns are adapted from verses in the Book of Psalms.
The priest quoted the Book of Psalms in his sermon on hope.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Psalms - the biblical book containing psalms

2. PROPN

/ bʊk əv ˈsɑːlmz / (AmE), / bʊk əv ˈsɑːmz / (BrE)
A book of the Old Testament consisting of a collection of 150 religious poems or songs, commonly called Psalms.
biblical book of 150 Psalms

Examples

The choir sang a hymn taken from the Book of Psalms during the service.
Many people find comfort in reading passages from the Book of Psalms.
Scholars often study the poetic language of the Book of Psalms to understand ancient Hebrew worship.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Psalms - The collective title for the Book of Psalms or the individual psalms within it

Etymology

From Greek 'Psalmoi' via Latin 'Psalmi', referring to sacred songs or poems.
