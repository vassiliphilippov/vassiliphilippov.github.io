acorn barnacle

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪkɔrn ˈbɑrnəkəl / (AmE), / ˈeɪkɔːn ˈbɑːnəkl / (BrE)
a small, cone-shaped barnacle that attaches to rocks, especially in intertidal zones
a type of cone-shaped barnacle

Examples

At low tide, the rocks were covered with acorn barnacles and seaweed.
The acorn barnacle is commonly found along rocky coasts worldwide.
Children collected shells and observed the acorn barnacles clinging tightly to stones.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barnacle - a marine crustacean that attaches itself permanently to various surfaces
