quandong nut

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwænˌdɑŋ nʌt / (AmE), / ˈkwæn.dɒŋ nʌt / (BrE)
the edible nutlike seed found inside the fruit of the quandong tree, native to Australia
edible seed of the quandong fruit

Examples

Many bushwalkers enjoy eating the quandong nut for its distinctive flavor and nutritional value.
The quandong nut can be used in baking and traditional Aboriginal dishes.
After cracking open the fruit, she collected several quandong nuts to roast over the fire.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quandong - Australian tree bearing the quandong fruit

Etymology

From Wiradjuri (Australian Aboriginal) word 'quandong' for the tree and its fruit.
