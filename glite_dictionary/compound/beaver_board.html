beaver board

1. NOUN

/ ˈbiːvər bɔrd / (AmE), / ˈbiːvə bɔːd / (BrE)
a lightweight wallboard made from compressed wood pulp, used especially for interior walls and ceilings
light wallboard of compressed wood pulp

Examples

The old house still had beaver board covering the living room walls.
During renovations, they discovered layers of beaver board behind the plaster.
Many early 20th-century homes were constructed using beaver board for ceilings and partitions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fiberboard - a building material made of compressed wood fibers or other plant fibers

Etymology

From the trade name 'Beaver Board', originally produced by Beaver Manufacturing Company, US, early 20th century.
