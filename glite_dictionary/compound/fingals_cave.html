Fingal's Cave

1. PROPN

/ ˈfɪŋɡəlz keɪv / (AmE), / ˈfɪŋɡəlz keɪv / (BrE)
a large sea cave with basalt columns on the island of Staffa, Scotland, known for its natural acoustics and striking geological formations
large basalt cave on Staffa, Scotland

Examples

Hundreds of tourists each year visit Fingal's Cave to admire its unusual basalt columns.
The legend of Fingal's Cave has inspired poets, artists, and musicians for generations.
Waves echoed hauntingly inside Fingal's Cave during our boat tour around Staffa.

Etymology

Named after Fingal, a hero of Scottish legend, with 'cave' from Old English 'caf' (cave, cavern).
