genus Lagostomus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs læˈɡɒstəməs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs laˈɡɒstəməs / (BrE)
A genus of South American rodents in the family Chinchillidae, comprising the viscachas.
a genus of South American rodents

Examples

The genus Lagostomus includes several species of burrowing rodents found in South America.
Scientists recently discovered a new species belonging to the genus Lagostomus.
The plains viscacha is a well-known member of the genus Lagostomus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: viscacha - a rodent of South America related to chinchillas

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'genus' (class, kind) and 'Lagostomus', from Greek 'lagos' (hare) and 'stoma' (mouth).
