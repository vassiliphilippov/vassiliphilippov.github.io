garlic chive

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɑrlɪk tʃaɪv / (AmE), / ˈɡɑːlɪk tʃaɪv / (BrE)
a perennial herb with flat leaves and a mild garlic flavor, used in cooking much like chives
herb with flat leaves and garlic flavor

Examples

She sprinkled fresh garlic chive over the stir-fry for added flavor.
The recipe calls for finely chopped garlic chive as a garnish.
Farmers at the market were selling bunches of garlic chive with their vegetables.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chive - an herb with slender, hollow leaves used in cooking

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡɑrlɪk tʃaɪv / (AmE), / ˈɡɑːlɪk tʃaɪv / (BrE)
A perennial herb of eastern Asia (Allium tuberosum) with flat leaves that have a mild garlic flavor, used in cooking and as a garnish.
Asian herb with mild garlic flavor

Examples

She sprinkled freshly chopped garlic chive over the stir-fry just before serving.
The garden's garlic chive plants bloom with delicate white flowers each summer.
Many Asian dishes use garlic chive to add a subtle onion and garlic flavor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chive - a small perennial herb (Allium schoenoprasum) used for its slender leaves
