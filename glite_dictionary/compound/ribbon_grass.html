ribbon grass

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɪb.ən ˌɡræs / (AmE), / ˈrɪb.ən ˌɡrɑːs / (BrE)
a perennial grass with broad, often striped leaves, commonly found in marshy meadows and ditches in Europe and North America
perennial grass with broad, striped leaves

Examples

Ribbon grass is popular in water gardens because of its attractive striped leaves.
The edges of the pond were lined with clumps of ribbon grass waving gently in the breeze.
He planted ribbon grass along the ditch to help control soil erosion.
