family Tropaeolaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌtroʊpiˌoʊləˈsiːi / (AmE), / ˌtrəʊpiːəʊliːˈsiːiː / (BrE)
A family of flowering plants known as Tropaeolaceae, consisting mainly of the genus Tropaeolum, including nasturtiums.
plant family including Tropaeolum (nasturtiums)
medical

Examples

The family Tropaeolaceae contains only one genus, Tropaeolum, best known for nasturtiums.
Botanists classify nasturtiums under the family Tropaeolaceae due to their distinct characteristics.
Members of the family Tropaeolaceae are widely cultivated for their bright and colorful flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Tropaeolum - the genus comprising nasturtium plants
  • Synonym: nasturtium - common name for several species of Tropaeolum

Etymology

From the genus name Tropaeolum, originally from Latin 'tropaeum' (trophy).
