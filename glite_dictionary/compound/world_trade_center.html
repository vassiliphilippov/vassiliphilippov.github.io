World Trade Center

1. PROPN

/ wɝrld treɪd ˈsɛn.tɚ / (AmE), / wɜːld treɪd ˈsɛn.tə / (BrE)
The former complex of twin skyscrapers in New York City, built in the early 1970s and destroyed in the terrorist attacks of September 11, 2001.
site of New York City's former twin skyscrapers, destroyed in 2001 attacks

Examples

Thousands of people worked in the World Trade Center before it was destroyed in 2001.
The memorial now stands where the World Trade Center towers once rose above Manhattan.
Many documentaries recount the events that occurred at the World Trade Center on September 11.
