oak-leaved goosefoot

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊk liːvd ˈɡuːsfʊt / (AmE), / ˈəʊk liːvd ˈɡuːsfʊt / (BrE)
an annual European plant (Chenopodium glaucum) with spikes of greenish flowers and leaves that are white and hairy on the underside, commonly found as a weed in North America
a European plant with white-hairy leaves, weed in North America

Examples

The fields were overrun with oak-leaved goosefoot by late summer.
Gardeners often struggle to remove oak-leaved goosefoot from vegetable patches.
Many people mistake oak-leaved goosefoot for similar weedy species at first glance.
