genus Sloanea

1. NOUN

/ ˈd͡ʒiː.nəs sloʊˈɑː.ni.ə / (AmE), / ˈd͡ʒiː.nəs sləʊˈɑː.nɪə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical hardwood trees native to regions in Central and South America, Asia, and the Pacific.
genus of tropical hardwood trees

Examples

The dense wood of genus Sloanea trees is often used in furniture making.
Researchers discovered a new species within the genus Sloanea in the Amazon rainforest.
Several members of the genus Sloanea are valued for their ecological and economic importance.

Etymology

From Latin 'genus' (kind, type) and the taxonomic name 'Sloanea', named after Hans Sloane, British naturalist.
