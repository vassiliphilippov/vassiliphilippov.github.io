climbing corydalis

1. NOUN

/ ˈklaɪmɪŋ kɔːrɪˈdælɪs / (AmE), / ˈklaɪmɪŋ kɒrɪˈdælɪs / (BrE)
An annual climbing vine with decompound leaves and racemes of yellow and pink flowers, native to parts of Europe and Asia.
annual vine with yellow and pink flowers

Examples

The garden looked beautiful with climbing corydalis draping over the old fence.
Botanists studied the unique flowers of the climbing corydalis during spring.
She planted some climbing corydalis to add color to her trellis.

Etymology

From 'corydalis,' derived from Greek 'korydalis' (crested lark), referring to the shape of the plant's flowers.
