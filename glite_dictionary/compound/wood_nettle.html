wood nettle

1. NOUN

/ wʊd ˈnɛtəl / (AmE), / wʊd ˈnɛtl̩ / (BrE)
A North American perennial herb with stinging hairs, found in rich woods and sometimes used for textile fibers.
North American stinging herb, textile fiber source

Examples

Be careful not to touch the wood nettle, as its hairs can cause a stinging sensation.
Indigenous peoples once used fibers from the wood nettle to make textiles and cordage.
We found a patch of wood nettle growing along the shady banks of the stream.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nettle - a plant with stinging hairs
