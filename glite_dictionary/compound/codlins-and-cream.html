codlins-and-cream

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑdlɪnz ənd ˈkriːm / (AmE), / ˈkɒdlɪnz ənd ˈkriːm / (BrE)
a plant of Europe and Asia with purplish-red flowers and hairy stems and leaves, commonly grown as an ornamental and known for its distinctive appearance; introduced into North America.
Eurasian plant with purplish-red flowers

Examples

The codlins-and-cream bloomed early in the cottage garden, adding splashes of purplish-red among the greenery.
In the meadow, we spotted a cluster of codlins-and-cream growing alongside the path.
Gardeners prize codlins-and-cream for its delicate flowers and unusual name.

Etymology

The name refers to the supposed resemblance of the flower's shape and color to 'codlings' (small apples) and cream.
