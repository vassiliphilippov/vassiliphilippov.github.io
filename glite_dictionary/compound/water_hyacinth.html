water hyacinth

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔtər ˈhaɪəsɪnθ / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə ˈhaɪəsɪnθ / (BrE)
a tropical aquatic plant with broad, thick leaves and large blue or violet flowers, often considered a troublesome weed because it clogs waterways
tropical aquatic plant with blue flowers

Examples

The rapid spread of water hyacinth has caused serious problems for local fishermen.
Boats often get stuck because water hyacinth completely covers the surface of the river.
Environmental groups are working to remove water hyacinth from the region's lakes.
