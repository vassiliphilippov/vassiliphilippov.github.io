afferent nerve

1. NOUN

/ ˈæfərənt nɜrv / (AmE), / ˈæfərənt nɜːv / (BrE)
A nerve that carries impulses from sensory receptors toward the central nervous system.
nerve carrying signals to the CNS
medical

Examples

The afferent nerve fibers transmit sensory information to the spinal cord.
Damage to an afferent nerve can result in loss of sensation in the affected area.
Researchers mapped the pathways of each afferent nerve involved in the reflex arc.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: efferent nerve - a nerve that carries impulses away from the central nervous system

Etymology

From Latin 'afferens', present participle of 'afferre' (to bring toward).
