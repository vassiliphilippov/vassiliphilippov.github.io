Northern United States

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɔrðərn ˌjuː.naɪ.t̬ɪd ˈsteɪts / (AmE), / ˈnɔːðən ˌjuː.naɪ.tɪd ˈsteɪts / (BrE)
the region of the United States located north of the Mason-Dixon line, traditionally considered the northern part of the country
region north of the Mason-Dixon line in the US

Examples

The climate in the Northern United States is generally colder than in the South.
Many industrial cities developed rapidly in the Northern United States during the 19th century.
Immigration patterns varied significantly between the Northern United States and other regions.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Southern United States - the region of the United States located south of the Mason-Dixon line
