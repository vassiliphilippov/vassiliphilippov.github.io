family Lucanidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli luːˈkænɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli ljuːˈkænɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of beetles known as stag beetles, characterized by their large mandibles and belonging to the order Coleoptera.
family of beetles (stag beetles)

Examples

The family Lucanidae includes some of the largest beetles in the world.
Entomologists often study family Lucanidae to understand the evolution of beetle mandibles.
Many members of the family Lucanidae are prized by insect collectors for their striking appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stag beetle - A beetle of the family Lucanidae, notable for the large mandibles of the male

Etymology

From the genus name Lucanus, Latin for a type of beetle.
