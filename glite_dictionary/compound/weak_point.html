weak point

1. NOUN

/ wiːk pɔɪnt / (AmE), / wiːk pɔɪnt / (BrE)
an aspect or feature that is inadequate, deficient, or vulnerable compared to the rest.
an area of weakness or vulnerability

Examples

Every athlete works to improve their weak point before a competition.
The report highlights supply chain security as the system’s weak point.
Her inability to delegate is often cited as her only weak point.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: strength - an attribute or quality of being strong or effective
  • Synonym: vulnerability - the quality or state of being exposed to the possibility of being attacked or harmed

2. NOUN

/ wik pɔɪnt / (AmE), / wiːk pɔɪnt / (BrE)
an aspect of a person, system, or thing that is especially vulnerable or lacking in strength; a flaw or shortcoming
vulnerability; flaw; area of weakness

Examples

Although she is a skilled designer, attention to detail remains her weak point.
Hackers exploited a weak point in the system’s security to gain access.
Public speaking was always considered his weak point during evaluations.
