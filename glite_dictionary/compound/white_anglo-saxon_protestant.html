white Anglo-Saxon Protestant

1. NOUN

/ waɪt ˌæŋɡloʊ ˈsæksən ˈprɑːtɛstənt / (AmE), / waɪt ˌæŋɡləʊ ˈsæksən ˈprɒtɪstənt / (BrE)
A white person of British (Anglo-Saxon) ancestry who is a member of a Protestant Christian denomination, often regarded as belonging to a socially and economically dominant group in the United States.
a white Protestant of Anglo-Saxon descent

Examples

Many of the founding families of the town were white Anglo-Saxon Protestants.
The term white Anglo-Saxon Protestant is often used to describe a particular segment of American society.
She was raised in a traditional white Anglo-Saxon Protestant household in New England.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: WASP - abbreviation for white Anglo-Saxon Protestant

Etymology

Formed from 'white', 'Anglo-Saxon' (from Latin 'Angli Saxones'), and 'Protestant' (from Latin 'protestantem', meaning 'one who protests').
