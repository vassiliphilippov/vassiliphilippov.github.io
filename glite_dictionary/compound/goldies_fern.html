Goldie's fern

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡoʊldiz fɜrn / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊldiz fɜːn / (BrE)
A large North American fern (Dryopteris goldiana) with a blackish, shiny stem, found in moist woodlands.
a North American fern with a shiny black stem

Examples

We spotted a beautiful Goldie's fern growing near the creek during our hike.
The dark, shiny stipe is a distinguishing feature of Goldie's fern.
Goldie's ferns thrive in damp, shaded forests across eastern North America.

Etymology

Named after Scottish botanist John Goldie (1793–1886).
