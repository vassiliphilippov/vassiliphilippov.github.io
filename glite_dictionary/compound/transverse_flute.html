transverse flute

1. NOUN

/ trænˈsvɜrs fluːt / (AmE), / trænzˈvɜːs fluːt / (BrE)
A side-blown woodwind instrument, typically made of metal or wood, held horizontally while played; commonly refers to the Western concert flute.
side-blown woodwind instrument (concert flute)

Examples

The transverse flute is a staple of classical orchestras and chamber ensembles.
She learned to play the transverse flute during her years at music school.
Unlike the recorder, the transverse flute is held horizontally when played.

Synset Examples

The dimensions and key system of the modern western concert flute and its close relatives are almost completely the work of the great flautist, composer, acoustician and silversmith, Theobald Boehm, who patented his system in 1847.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flute - A wind instrument producing sound from the flow of air across an opening.
  • Antonym: recorder - A woodwind instrument held vertically and played by blowing into an end.

Etymology

From Latin 'transversus' (turned across) and 'flute', referring to the instrument's horizontal playing position.

2. NOUN

/ trænzˌvɜrs ˈfluːt / (AmE), / trɑːnzˌvɜːs ˈfluːt / (BrE)
A woodwind instrument consisting of a slender tube closed at one end, played by blowing across a hole near the closed end and held horizontally.
side-blown woodwind instrument

Examples

The orchestra featured a solo transverse flute during the Baroque piece.
She learned to play the transverse flute instead of the recorder in music class.
Many composers write melodies specifically for the transverse flute because of its bright sound.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: recorder - a woodwind instrument played vertically with a whistle mouthpiece

Etymology

From Latin 'transversus' (turned across), referring to the way the instrument is held.
