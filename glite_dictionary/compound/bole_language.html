Bole language

1. NOUN

/ ˈboʊleɪ ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈbəʊleɪ ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
a Chadic language spoken in northern Nigeria, closely related to Hausa, with several dialects such as Bara and Fika
Chadic language of northern Nigeria

Examples

The Bole language belongs to the West Chadic branch of the Afro-Asiatic language family.
Linguists have been studying the dialects of the Bole language to better understand its relationship with Hausa.
There are ongoing efforts to preserve the Bole language due to its cultural and historical significance in northern Nigeria.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hausa - a major Chadic language spoken in West Africa
