Hottentot's bread vine

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑtəntɑts brɛd vaɪn / (AmE), / ˈhɒtɪntɒts brɛd vaɪn / (BrE)
a South African vine with a massive rootstock covered in deeply fissured bark, known for its large underground tuber
South African vine with large tuber

Examples

The Hottentot's bread vine produces a huge tuber that was once an important food source for local communities.
Botanists have studied the Hottentot's bread vine for its unique adaptations to arid climates.
We saw a Hottentot's bread vine while hiking in the semi-desert region of South Africa.

Etymology

The term 'Hottentot' is dated and was historically used to refer to the Khoikhoi people of southern Africa; the plant is named for its use as food by these people.
