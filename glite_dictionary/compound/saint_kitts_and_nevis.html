Saint Kitts and Nevis

1. PROPN

/ seɪnt kɪts ənd ˈniːvɪs / (AmE), / seɪnt kɪts ənd ˈniːvɪs / (BrE)
A country consisting of two islands in the West Indies, located in the Caribbean Sea to the east-southeast of Puerto Rico; achieved independence from the United Kingdom in 1983.
country in the Caribbean Sea

Examples

My friends traveled to Saint Kitts and Nevis for their honeymoon last year.
Citizens of Saint Kitts and Nevis celebrate their independence each September.
Exports from Saint Kitts and Nevis mainly include sugar and tourism services.

Etymology

Saint Kitts is short for Saint Christopher, after the patron saint of travelers. Nevis is from Spanish 'Nuestra Señora de las Nieves' (Our Lady of the Snows).
