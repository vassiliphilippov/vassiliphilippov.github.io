alcohol radical

1. NOUN

/ ˈælkəˌhɔl ˈrædɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈalkəhɒl ˈrædɪkəl / (BrE)
the univalent chemical group –OH, characteristic of alcohols, also called a hydroxyl group
the –OH group in chemistry
medical

Examples

The alcohol radical is responsible for the characteristic reactions of alcohols.
In organic chemistry, the alcohol radical is often represented as –OH.
The presence of an alcohol radical in the molecule significantly affects its solubility.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hydroxyl group - the –OH group in chemistry

Etymology

From 'alcohol' (Arabic al-kuḥl, a powdered antimony) and 'radical' (from Latin radix, root), used in chemistry to denote a functional group.
