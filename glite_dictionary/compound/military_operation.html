military operation

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪl.ɪˌtɛr.i ˌɑː.pəˈreɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˈmɪl.ɪ.təri ˌɒp.əˈreɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
an activity or series of activities carried out by an armed force, usually as part of a campaign or maneuver
action or campaign by armed forces

Examples

The general planned a military operation to regain control of the region.
During the night, a secret military operation was launched across the border.
International law governs the conduct of military operations during times of conflict.

Synset Examples

it was a joint operation of the navy and air force

Related Terms

  • Synonym: campaign - a series of organized military actions with a particular goal

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɪlɪˌtɛri ɑpəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈmɪlɪtəri ɒpəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
An organized and coordinated action carried out by armed forces to achieve specific objectives.
coordinated action by armed forces

Examples

The army launched a military operation near the border to secure the area.
Thousands of troops participated in the joint military operation with allied forces.
Planning for the military operation required weeks of careful preparation and intelligence gathering.

Synset Examples

They conducted a special military operation at dawn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: campaign - A series of military operations intended to achieve a particular objective
  • Synonym: mission - A specific operation or duty assigned to military personnel
