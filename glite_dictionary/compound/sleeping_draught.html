sleeping draught

1. NOUN

/ ˈsliːpɪŋ dræft / (AmE), / ˈsliːpɪŋ drɑːft / (BrE)
a medicinal liquid or preparation taken to induce sleep
medicine taken to induce sleep

Examples

The doctor prepared a sleeping draught to help the patient rest through the night.
In old detective stories, a sleeping draught is sometimes used to render someone unconscious.
After several sleepless nights, she reluctantly took a sleeping draught as prescribed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sedative - a drug promoting calm or inducing sleep
  • Antonym: stimulant - a drug increasing physiological activity

Etymology

From Middle English 'draught', meaning a drink, from Old English 'dræht' or 'dræġan' (to draw, pull).
