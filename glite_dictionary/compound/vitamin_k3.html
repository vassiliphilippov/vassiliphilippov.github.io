vitamin K3

1. NOUN

/ ˈvaɪtəmɪn ˌkeɪ ˈθriː / (AmE), / ˈvɪtəmɪn ˌkeɪ ˈθriː / (BrE)
a synthetic compound used as a form of vitamin K, known chemically as menadione, used in animal feed and certain medical applications
synthetic form of vitamin K (menadione)
medical

Examples

Some animal feeds are supplemented with vitamin K3 to prevent bleeding disorders.
Unlike vitamin K3, the natural forms of vitamin K occur in green leafy vegetables and fermented foods.
Excessive use of vitamin K3 can cause toxicity in some animals.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: vitamin K1 - natural form of vitamin K found in plants (phylloquinone)
  • Degree Lower: vitamin K2 - natural form of vitamin K found in animals and fermented foods (menaquinone)

Etymology

From 'vitamin' + 'K' (the vitamin group) + '3' (the compound's number designation).
