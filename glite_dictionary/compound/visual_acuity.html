visual acuity

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪʒuəl əˈkjuːɪti / (AmE), / ˈvɪʒuəl əˈkjuːɪti / (BrE)
the sharpness of vision, especially the ability to see fine details clearly, often measured by a standard test such as a Snellen chart
sharpness of vision; clarity of sight
medical

Examples

Children usually have excellent visual acuity in both eyes.
The eye test measures your visual acuity using rows of letters of decreasing size.
Wearing corrective lenses can help improve visual acuity in people with certain vision problems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eyesight - the ability to see; vision
  • Antonym: visual impairment - reduced ability to see; partial or total blindness

Etymology

From Latin visus (sight) + acuity, from Latin acuitas (sharpness).

2. NOUN

/ ˈvɪʒuəl əˈkjuːɪti / (AmE), / ˈvɪʒuəl əˈkjuːɪti / (BrE)
the sharpness or clarity of vision, especially as measured by the ability to distinguish details at a specified distance
sharpness or clarity of vision
medical

Examples

The doctor tested his visual acuity during the eye examination.
Wearing glasses improved her visual acuity significantly.
He has excellent visual acuity and can read very small print.

Synset Examples

Her visual acuity allows her to see details others miss.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eyesight - ability to see; vision
  • Antonym: visual impairment - reduced ability to see; diminished vision

Etymology

From Latin visus (sight) + acuity, from Latin acuitas (sharpness).
