calabar-bean vine

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæləˌbɑrˈbin vaɪn / (AmE), / ˌkaləˌbɑːˈbiːn vaɪn / (BrE)
a tropical African woody vine (Physostigma venenosum) known for producing calabar beans, which are used in medicine and historically as an ordeal poison
woody African vine yielding calabar beans

Examples

The seeds of the calabar-bean vine have been used in traditional African medicine for centuries.
Researchers are studying toxins produced by the calabar-bean vine for their potential medical applications.
She learned about the calabar-bean vine during a lecture on poisonous plants of Africa.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Physostigma venenosum - the botanical name for the calabar-bean vine

Etymology

Named after Calabar region of Nigeria, where the beans are native; 'bean vine' reflects its botanical form.
