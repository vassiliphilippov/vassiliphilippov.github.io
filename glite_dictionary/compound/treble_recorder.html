treble recorder

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrɛbəl rɪˈkoʊrdər / (AmE), / ˈtrɛbəl rɪˈkɔːdə / (BrE)
a medium-sized recorder, pitched higher than the tenor recorder and lower than the soprano, commonly used in music ensembles
medium-sized recorder instrument

Examples

She played the treble recorder in the school orchestra during the spring concert.
The music piece requires three types of recorders: descant, treble recorder, and bass.
After mastering the soprano, he decided to learn the treble recorder for more challenging repertoire.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: descant recorder - higher-pitched recorder, also known as soprano recorder
  • Degree Lower: tenor recorder - lower-pitched recorder

Etymology

From 'treble' (high-pitched) and 'recorder' (a type of woodwind instrument).
