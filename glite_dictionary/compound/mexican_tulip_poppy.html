Mexican tulip poppy

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛksɪkən ˈtuːlɪp ˈpɑpi / (AmE), / ˌmɛksɪkən ˈtjuːlɪp ˈpɒpi / (BrE)
A flowering plant native to the Mexican highlands, cultivated for its bright yellow flowers and blue-grey, finely divided foliage.
flowering plant with yellow flowers, native to Mexico

Examples

The Mexican tulip poppy adds a splash of yellow to summer gardens with its vibrant blooms.
Gardeners admire the blue-grey leaves of the Mexican tulip poppy, which contrast beautifully with its flowers.
In Mexico, the Mexican tulip poppy thrives in sunny, well-drained soil.
