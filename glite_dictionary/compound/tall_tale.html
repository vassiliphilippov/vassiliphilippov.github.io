tall tale

1. NOUN

/ tɔl teɪl / (AmE), / tɔːl teɪl / (BrE)
a story that is difficult to believe because it exaggerates events or details in a fanciful or improbable way
improbable or exaggerated story

Examples

He entertained the children with a tall tale about wrestling a giant fish.
Everyone knew the story was just a tall tale that Grandpa liked to tell.
The movie is based on a tall tale from American folklore.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fable - a short story conveying a moral, often with animals as characters
  • Synonym: myth - a traditional story, especially one explaining natural or social phenomena
  • Antonym: truth - something that is true or factual

2. NOUN

/ tɔl teɪl / (AmE), / tɔːl teɪl / (BrE)
an exaggerated story that is very difficult or impossible to believe, often told for amusement
exaggerated, improbable story

Examples

The fisherman entertained us with a tall tale about the one that got away.
She dismissed his story as just another tall tale and laughed.
Throughout the campfire, each child tried to outdo the last with a crazier tall tale.

Synset Examples
