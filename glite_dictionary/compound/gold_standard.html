gold standard

1. NOUN

/ ɡoʊld ˈstændɚd / (AmE), / ɡəʊld ˈstændəd / (BrE)
A thing regarded as the best or most reliable example of its kind; the highest standard of excellence.
best or most reliable example

Examples

The Nobel Prize is often considered the gold standard for scientific achievement.
This procedure is the gold standard in modern heart surgery.
Peer-reviewed academic journals are viewed as the gold standard in research publishing.

Synset Examples

academic education is the gold standard against which other educational activity is pejoratively judged

Related Terms

  • Synonym: benchmark - a standard or point of reference against which things are compared
  • Synonym: yardstick - a standard used for comparison

Etymology

Originally referred to the monetary system where currency value was based on gold.

2. NOUN

/ ɡoʊld ˈstændərd / (AmE), / ɡəʊld ˈstændəd / (BrE)
A monetary system in which the value of a country's currency is directly linked to a specified amount of gold.
monetary system linked to gold

Examples

Many countries abandoned the gold standard during the twentieth century.
The debate about returning to the gold standard resurfaces occasionally among economists.
Under the gold standard, each dollar could be exchanged for a fixed amount of gold.

3. NOUN

/ ɡoʊld ˈstændɚd / (AmE), / ɡəʊld ˈstændəd / (BrE)
the best or most reliable example against which other things are measured; a benchmark of excellence
the best standard or benchmark

Examples

This new smartphone has set the gold standard for design and performance.
Their customer service is the gold standard in the industry.
Many athletes see Olympic gold as the gold standard of achievement.

Synset Examples

This model is the gold standard in the industry.
Her hard work set the gold standard for others.
He believes honesty is the gold standard in relationships.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: benchmark - a standard or point of reference
  • Synonym: yardstick - a standard of comparison or evaluation
  • Synonym: paragon - a model of excellence

Etymology

Originally referred to a monetary system in which paper money and coins are backed by gold; now used figuratively for ultimate quality.
