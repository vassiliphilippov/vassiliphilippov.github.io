dyer's rocket

1. NOUN

/ ˈdaɪɚz ˌrɑkɪt / (AmE), / ˈdaɪəz ˌrɒkɪt / (BrE)
a European plant (Reseda luteola) cultivated for its yellow dye, now naturalized in North America
plant used for yellow dye

Examples

Traditionally, dyer's rocket was harvested to produce natural yellow dye for textiles.
Botanists have noted that dyer's rocket has become widely established in North America.
The vibrant color obtained from dyer's rocket is prized by artists and craftspeople.

Related Terms
