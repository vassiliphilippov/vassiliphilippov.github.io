myasthenia gravis

1. NOUN

/ ˌmaɪ.əsˌθi.ni.ə ˈɡreɪ.vɪs / (AmE), / ˌmaɪ.asˌθiː.ni.ə ˈɡræ.vɪs / (BrE)
A chronic autoimmune disease causing severe muscle weakness and fatigue, particularly in the face and neck, resulting from problems with nerve-muscle communication.
chronic disease causing muscle weakness
medical

Examples

The symptoms of myasthenia gravis can include drooping eyelids and difficulty swallowing.
Doctors confirmed the patient was suffering from myasthenia gravis after several muscle tests.
Treatments for myasthenia gravis have improved significantly in recent decades.

Etymology

From Greek 'mys' (muscle) + 'astheneia' (weakness) and Latin 'gravis' (serious).
