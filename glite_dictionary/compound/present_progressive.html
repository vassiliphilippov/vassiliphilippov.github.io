present progressive

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɛzənt prəˈɡrɛsɪv / (AmE), / ˈprɛzənt prəˈɡrɛsɪv / (BrE)
a tense used to describe actions or events that are currently happening or ongoing at the present time
verb tense for ongoing present action

Examples

He is studying for his exams right now, which is an example of the present progressive tense.
English learners often struggle when first using the present progressive correctly in conversation.
The present progressive is formed using 'am', 'is', or 'are' plus the verb ending in '-ing'.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: present continuous - an alternative term for the present progressive tense

Etymology

Formed from 'present' (from Latin 'praesent-', present) and 'progressive' (from Latin 'progressivus', relating to progress or advancement).
