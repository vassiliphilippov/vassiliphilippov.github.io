marrow squash

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæroʊ ˌskwɑʃ / (AmE), / ˈmarəʊ ˌskwɒʃ / (BrE)
A type of squash plant grown for its elongated fruit with smooth dark green skin and pale flesh, commonly used as a vegetable.
elongated squash with dark green skin

Examples

The recipe called for one large marrow squash sliced into thin rounds.
She grew several types of marrow squash in her garden every summer.
Stuffed marrow squash is a traditional dish in many European countries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zucchini - A similar summer squash, especially in American English
