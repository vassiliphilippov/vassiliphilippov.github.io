King's English

1. NOUN

/ kɪŋz ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ / (AmE), / kɪŋz ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ / (BrE)
the standard form of British English as spoken and written by educated people, typically associated with southern England and traditionally considered correct and proper
standard, educated British English
british

Examples

He spoke the King's English with impeccable clarity during the interview.
Many believe that speaking the King's English demonstrates one's education and refinement.
English teachers often encourage their students to use the King's English in formal writing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Queen's English - standard, educated British English as associated with a female monarch

Etymology

Named after the reigning king of England, originally from the phrase 'the King's (or Queen's) English' dating to the 15th century.
