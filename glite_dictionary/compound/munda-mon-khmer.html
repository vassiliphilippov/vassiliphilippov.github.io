Munda-Mon-Khmer

1. NOUN

/ ˌmʊndə mɒn kəˈmɛr / (AmE), / ˌmʊndə mɒn kəˈmɛː / (BrE)
a family of languages spoken in southern and southeastern Asia, including the Munda and Mon-Khmer branches
language family of southern and southeastern Asia

Examples

The Munda-Mon-Khmer language family includes numerous languages spoken across India and Southeast Asia.
Linguists often study the Munda-Mon-Khmer group to trace historical language development in the region.
Many indigenous people in central India communicate in tongues belonging to the Munda-Mon-Khmer family.

Etymology

From the names of the two principal branches, Munda and Mon-Khmer.
