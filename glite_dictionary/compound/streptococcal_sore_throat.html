streptococcal sore throat

1. NOUN

/ ˌstrɛptəˈkɑkəl sɔr θroʊt / (AmE), / ˌstrɛptəˈkɒkəl sɔː θrəʊt / (BrE)
A bacterial infection of the throat and tonsils caused by streptococcus bacteria, marked by a sore, red throat, and often fever.
bacterial throat infection from streptococcus
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed her with a streptococcal sore throat and prescribed antibiotics.
Symptoms of streptococcal sore throat include a sudden sore throat, fever, and swollen glands.
Children are especially susceptible to streptococcal sore throat during the winter months.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strep throat - common term for streptococcal sore throat

Etymology

From 'streptococcus', a genus of bacteria, and 'sore throat', a condition of throat pain.
