river basin

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɪvɚ ˈbeɪsən / (AmE), / ˈrɪvə ˈbeɪsən / (BrE)
the area of land drained by a river and its tributaries, where all runoff water flows to a common outlet
area drained by a river system

Examples

The Amazon river basin covers an immense area including parts of several countries.
Dams have been constructed throughout the Yangtze river basin for flood control and irrigation.
Deforestation in the river basin can increase the risk of soil erosion and downstream flooding.

Synset Examples

flood control in the Missouri basin

Related Terms

  • Synonym: watershed - an area of land that separates waters flowing to different rivers
  • Synonym: drainage basin - the land area where precipitation collects and drains off into a common outlet
