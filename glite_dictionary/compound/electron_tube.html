electron tube

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈlɛktrɑn tuːb / (AmE), / ɪˈlɛktrɒn tjuːb / (BrE)
An electronic device consisting of a system of electrodes in a vacuum-sealed glass or metal enclosure, used to control electric current.
vacuum-sealed electronic device for controlling current

Examples

The old radio was powered by several electron tubes that needed to warm up before use.
Early televisions relied on electron tube technology for displaying images.
Replacing an electron tube requires care due to its fragile glass structure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vacuum tube - an electron tube with a vacuum inside, often used synonymously
  • Antonym: transistor - a semiconductor device that replaced most electron tubes
