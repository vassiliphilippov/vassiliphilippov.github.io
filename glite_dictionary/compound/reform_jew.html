Reform Jew

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈfɔrm dʒu / (AmE), / rɪˈfɔːm dʒuː / (BrE)
A member of a liberal branch of Judaism that seeks to adapt religious practices and beliefs in accordance with modern values and circumstances.
member of liberal branch of Judaism

Examples

As a Reform Jew, she celebrates the holidays while also embracing modern values.
Many Reform Jews support gender equality within religious practices.
The synagogue welcomed both Orthodox and Reform Jew members.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Orthodox Jew - a member of the traditionalist branch of Judaism
  • Synonym: Conservative Jew - a member of a moderate branch of Judaism

Etymology

From 'Reform' (in the sense of religious reform movement) + 'Jew'; 'Reform Judaism' originated in 19th-century Germany.
