magna cum laude

1. ADV

/ ˌmæɡnə kʊm ˈlɔːdeɪ / (AmE), / ˌmaɪɡnə kʊm ˈlaʊdeɪ / (BrE)
With high honors; used to signify a level of distinction when graduating from a college or university.
with high academic honors

Examples

She graduated magna cum laude from Harvard University in 2010.
His impressive academic record earned him magna cum laude honors at graduation.
Obtaining a magna cum laude distinction requires both consistent effort and high grades.

Synset Examples

he graduated magna cum laude

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: summa cum laude - with highest academic honors
  • Degree Lower: cum laude - with praise; with honors (lowest level)

Etymology

From Latin, meaning 'with great praise.'
