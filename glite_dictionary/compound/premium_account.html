premium account

1. NOUN

/ ˈpriː.mi.əm əˈkaʊnt / (AmE), / ˈpriː.mi.əm əˈkaʊnt / (BrE)
a bank account that offers additional services and benefits compared to standard accounts, often for a fee or for customers meeting certain criteria.
bank account with added benefits

Examples

Many banks charge a monthly fee for their premium account services.
She upgraded to a premium account for benefits like travel insurance and airport lounge access.
To qualify for a premium account, customers must maintain a higher minimum balance.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: current account - a bank account from which money can be withdrawn without notice
  • Degree Lower: basic account - a simple bank account with limited features
