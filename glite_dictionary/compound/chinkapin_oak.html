chinkapin oak

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɪŋ.kə.pɪn oʊk / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪŋ.kə.pɪn əʊk / (BrE)
A medium-sized deciduous tree native to the eastern United States, known for its strong, durable wood.
species of deciduous oak tree (U.S.)

Examples

The chinkapin oak is prized for its durable wood, often used in furniture and flooring.
Birds and squirrels feed on the acorns produced by the chinkapin oak each autumn.
Several chinkapin oaks were planted along the park trail to provide shade and habitat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oak - any tree or shrub of the genus Quercus, known for strong wood and acorns
  • Synonym: chinquapin - small tree or shrub producing a nut similar to chestnut

Etymology

From 'chinkapin', an alternative spelling of 'chinquapin', from Algonquian (Virginia) *chechinquamin* (‘acorn’), combined with 'oak'.
