Battle of El Alamein

1. PROPN

/ ˈbæt̬l əv ɛl ˌæləˈmeɪn / (AmE), / ˈbætl əv ɛl ˌæləˈmeɪn / (BrE)
a major battle of World War II fought in 1942 in North Africa, resulting in a decisive Allied victory over German and Italian forces
World War II battle in North Africa, 1942

Examples

Many historians consider the Battle of El Alamein a turning point in the North African campaign.
The Battle of El Alamein halted the advance of Axis forces toward Egypt and the Suez Canal.
Field Marshal Montgomery led the Allied forces to victory at the Battle of El Alamein in 1942.
