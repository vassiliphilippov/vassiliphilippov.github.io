great-bellied

1. ADJ

/ ˌɡreɪtˈbɛlɪd / (AmE), / ˌɡreɪtˈbɛlɪd / (BrE)
having a large, prominent, or swollen belly; big-bellied.
having a large or prominent belly
archaic

Examples

The great-bellied man shuffled slowly down the street, attracting curious glances from passersby.
In the painting stood a great-bellied jug, brimming with red wine.
The poet described the river as great-bellied after the spring rains.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: potbellied - having a large, protruding belly
  • Antonym: flat-bellied - having a flat or slender abdomen

Etymology

Formed from 'great' (large) and 'bellied' (having a belly).
