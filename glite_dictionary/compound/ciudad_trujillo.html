Ciudad Trujillo

1. PROPN

/ siˈuðɑd truˈxiʝoʊ / (AmE), / siˈuːdæd truˈhiːjəʊ / (BrE)
the former name (1936–1961) of Santo Domingo, the capital city of the Dominican Republic, used during the regime of Rafael Trujillo
former name for Santo Domingo, Dominican Republic

Examples

During Trujillo's dictatorship, Santo Domingo was renamed Ciudad Trujillo.
Many older maps of the Caribbean still mark the city as Ciudad Trujillo.
The city's name reverted from Ciudad Trujillo back to Santo Domingo after 1961.

Synset Examples

Santo Domingo is the oldest continually inhabited European settlement in the Americas with the oldest cathedral and the oldest hospital and the oldest monastery in the Western Hemisphere

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Santo Domingo - the present name of the capital of the Dominican Republic

Etymology

From Spanish 'Ciudad Trujillo', meaning 'Trujillo City', named after dictator Rafael Trujillo.
