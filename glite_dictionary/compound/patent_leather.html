patent leather

1. NOUN

/ ˈpætənt ˌlɛðər / (AmE), / ˈpætənt ˌlɛðə / (BrE)
a type of leather with a hard, shiny, glossy surface, often used for shoes and accessories
leather with a hard, glossy surface

Examples

The child's shoes were made of patent leather and shone brightly in the sunlight.
She wore a black patent leather handbag to add a touch of elegance to her outfit.
His patent leather belt matched his polished dress shoes perfectly.

Etymology

From the practice of patenting the new process of finishing leather with a glossy coating, first popularized in the early 19th century.
