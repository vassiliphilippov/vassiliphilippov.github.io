scalenus syndrome

1. NOUN

/ skəˈliːnəs ˈsɪnˌdroʊm / (AmE), / skəˈliːnəs ˈsɪn.drəʊm / (BrE)
A medical condition characterized by discomfort, vascular symptoms, and loss of sensation in the shoulder and arm, caused by compression of the subclavian artery and brachial plexus by a scalene muscle.
compression disorder of shoulder and arm nerves/vessels
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with scalenus syndrome after reporting numbness and tingling in her right arm.
Physical therapy is often recommended for those suffering from scalenus syndrome.
Doctors may use imaging studies to confirm a diagnosis of scalenus syndrome when symptoms are unclear.

Etymology

From 'scalenus', the anatomical name for a group of neck muscles, and 'syndrome', from Greek sundromē (concurrence of symptoms).
