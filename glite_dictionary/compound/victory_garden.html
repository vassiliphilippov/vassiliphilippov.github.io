victory garden

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪktəri ˌɡɑrdn̩ / (AmE), / ˈvɪktəri ˌɡɑːdn̩ / (BrE)
a garden planted during wartime, especially in private homes or public parks, to help alleviate food shortages by growing vegetables, fruits, and herbs for personal use.
wartime kitchen garden for food production

Examples

During World War II, many families maintained a victory garden in their backyard to supplement their food supply.
My grandmother still plants a victory garden every spring, just as she learned during her childhood.
The community center organized a victory garden project to teach children about self-sufficiency and history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kitchen garden - a garden where vegetables, herbs, and fruit are grown for household use

Etymology

From 'victory', referencing patriotic effort, and 'garden'. Popularized during World War I and II.
