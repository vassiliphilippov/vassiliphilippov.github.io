rules of order

1. NOUN

/ ruːlz əv ˈɔrdər / (AmE), / ruːlz əv ˈɔːdə / (BrE)
a set of formal rules or procedures governing the conduct of meetings or assemblies
procedures governing meetings

Examples

According to the rules of order, only one person may speak at a time during the meeting.
The club president referred to the rules of order when a dispute arose among members.
Familiarity with basic rules of order helps meetings run more efficiently.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parliamentary procedure - the formal process and rules used to conduct meetings and make decisions in assemblies
