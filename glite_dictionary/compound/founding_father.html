Founding Father

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaʊndɪŋ ˈfɑːðɚ / (AmE), / ˈfaʊndɪŋ ˈfɑːðə / (BrE)
a member of the group that drafted the United States Constitution in 1787, often regarded as establishing the foundations of the U.S. government
early leader who helped create the U.S. Constitution
american

Examples

George Washington is often celebrated as a Founding Father of the United States.
Historians frequently debate the intentions of the Founding Fathers when interpreting the Constitution.
The Founding Fathers played a crucial role in shaping American democracy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: framers - members of the group that wrote the U.S. Constitution
  • Degree Lower: forefather - an ancestor, especially a founder of an institution or nation
  • Degree Higher: president - the elected head of a republic state, especially in the U.S context as some Founding Fathers later became president
