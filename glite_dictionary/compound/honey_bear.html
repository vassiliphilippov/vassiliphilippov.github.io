honey bear

1. NOUN

/ ˈhʌni ˌbɛr / (AmE), / ˈhʌni ˌbeə / (BrE)
An arboreal mammal of tropical America, known for eating fruit and having a long prehensile tail.
arboreal mammal of tropical America

Examples

The honey bear uses its long tail to grasp tree branches as it searches for fruit.
Children were excited to see a honey bear climbing high in the rainforest canopy.
Because the honey bear is nocturnal, it is most active at night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kinkajou - a nocturnal mammal of Central and South America with a prehensile tail (Potos flavus)
