clean break

1. NOUN

/ klin breɪk / (AmE), / kliːn breɪk / (BrE)
a complete and definite separation from a situation, relationship, or past, typically allowing for a fresh start
complete and definite separation

Examples

After years of tension, they finally agreed to a clean break and went their separate ways.
Leaving the city was a clean break that allowed her to start a new chapter.
He wanted a clean break from his old habits to focus on his health.

Synset Examples

After the divorce, she needed a clean break from the past.
They decided on a clean break after years of arguments.
He made a clean break from his old job to start fresh.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: break - an interruption or separation; a pause or division
  • Synonym: separation - the action or state of moving or being moved apart
