sandalwood family

1. NOUN

/ ˈsændl̩ˌwʊd ˈfæmli / (AmE), / ˈsændlwʊd ˈfamli / (BrE)
a family of chiefly tropical herbs, shrubs, or trees, many of which bear nuts or one-seeded fruit, including the sandalwood tree and its relatives
family of tropical plants including sandalwood

Examples

Several economically important species belong to the sandalwood family.
Botanists are studying the distribution of the sandalwood family in Southeast Asia.
Some trees in the sandalwood family are valued for their aromatic wood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Santalaceae - the botanical family name for the sandalwood family
