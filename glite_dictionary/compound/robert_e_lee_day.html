Robert E Lee Day

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɑbɚt i ˈli ˌdeɪ / (AmE), / ˈrɒbət iː ˈliː ˌdeɪ / (BrE)
a holiday observed on January 19 in some southern U.S. states to commemorate the birth of Confederate General Robert E. Lee
U.S. holiday honoring Robert E. Lee
american

Examples

Some states observe Robert E Lee Day alongside Martin Luther King Jr. Day in January.
Controversy surrounds the continued celebration of Robert E Lee Day in the modern era.
On Robert E Lee Day, some communities hold parades and commemorative events.
