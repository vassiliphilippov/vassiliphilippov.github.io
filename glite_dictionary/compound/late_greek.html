Late Greek

1. NOUN

/ leɪt ˈɡrik / (AmE), / leɪt ˈɡriːk / (BrE)
the historical stage of the Greek language spoken and written from the 3rd to the 8th centuries AD
Greek language, 3rd–8th centuries AD

Examples

Scholars study Late Greek texts to understand the transition between ancient and medieval Greek.
The New Testament is written in a form of Late Greek known as Koine.
Many Byzantine documents are preserved in Late Greek manuscripts.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Ancient Greek - the Greek language before approximately the 3rd century AD
  • Degree Higher: Medieval Greek - the stage following Late Greek, roughly from 9th to 15th centuries
