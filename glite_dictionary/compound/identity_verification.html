identity verification

1. NOUN

/ aɪˈdɛntəti ˌvɛrɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / aɪˈdɛntɪti ˌvɛrɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of confirming that a person is who they claim to be, typically by checking their physiological or behavioral characteristics or credentials
process of confirming someone's identity

Examples

Banks now require identity verification for all online transactions to prevent fraud.
The airport uses biometric scanners as part of its identity verification process.
Without proper identity verification, access to the secure facility is denied.

Synset Examples

negative identification can only be accomplished through biometric identification
if a pin or password is lost or forgotten, it can be changed and reissued but a biometric identification cannot

Related Terms

  • Synonym: authentication - the process of proving or checking that something is real or genuine, especially in computing
