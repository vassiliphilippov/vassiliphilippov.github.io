racemic acid

1. NOUN

/ rəˈsimɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / rəˈsiːmɪk ˈasɪd / (BrE)
the optically inactive form of tartaric acid, commonly found in grape juice and used in chemistry
optically inactive form of tartaric acid
medical

Examples

Many winemakers are aware that racemic acid can form in old barrels of grape juice.
The chemical properties of racemic acid differ from those of optically active tartaric acid.
Pasteur studied racemic acid to understand the phenomenon of optical isomerism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tartaric acid - a white crystalline organic acid present in grapes and other fruits

Etymology

From French 'racémique,' from 'racème' (a cluster of grapes), referencing its discovery in grape products.
