Alexandria senna

1. NOUN

/ əˌlɛkˈsændriə ˈsɛnə / (AmE), / əˌlɛkˈsændriə ˈsɛnə / (BrE)
A tall shrub (Senna alexandrina) with racemes of yellow flowers, whose dried leaves are used as a medicinal laxative.
shrub yielding medicinal laxative leaves
medical

Examples

The dried leaves of Alexandria senna have long been used as a natural remedy for constipation.
Herbalists often recommend Alexandria senna for its potent laxative effects.
Traditional medicine in Egypt has valued Alexandria senna for centuries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: senna - any of various plants of the genus Senna, known for laxative properties

Etymology

Named after Alexandria, Egypt, a region historically noted for exporting senna leaves; 'senna' from Arabic 'sana'.
