spring chicken

1. NOUN

/ sprɪŋ ˈtʃɪk.ən / (AmE), / sprɪŋ ˈtʃɪk.ɪn / (BrE)
A young chicken, especially one that is tender and suitable for roasting.
young, tender chicken

Examples

The chef recommended using a spring chicken for the recipe because of its tender meat.
For Sunday dinner, we roasted a spring chicken with fresh herbs from the garden.
At the market, the butcher claimed his spring chicken was the juiciest in town.

Etymology

Refers to chickens typically available in spring, when they are youngest and most tender.

2. NOUN

/ sprɪŋ ˈtʃɪk.ɪn / (AmE), / sprɪŋ ˈtʃɪk.ɪn / (BrE)
a young person, especially one who is unusually youthful or inexperienced; often used in the phrase 'no spring chicken' to indicate someone is not young anymore.
young person (often used negatively about age)

Examples

Although he still plays soccer every weekend, he's no spring chicken anymore.
She may be a spring chicken compared to her colleagues, but she's very talented.
Don't expect a spring chicken to have the same experience as a seasoned expert.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: youth - a young person, especially a young man
  • Antonym: elder - an older person, especially one with greater age or experience

Etymology

Originally referring to young chickens available in spring, later figuratively used for young people.
