calendar method

1. NOUN

/ ˈkælənˌdɚ ˈmɛθəd / (AmE), / ˈkælɪndə ˈmɛθəd / (BrE)
a natural family planning technique in which fertility is estimated by calculating ovulation as occurring 14 days before the onset of the next menstrual period
method of calculating fertile days for contraception
medical

Examples

Many couples use the calendar method as a natural form of birth control.
The effectiveness of the calendar method depends on the regularity of the menstrual cycle.
She tracks her period carefully to make the calendar method more reliable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rhythm method - another name for the calendar method in contraception
