Christ's Resurrection

1. NOUN

/ kraɪsts ˌrɛzəˈrɛkʃən / (AmE), / kraɪsts ˌrɛzəˈrɛkʃən / (BrE)
the event, according to Christian belief, in which Jesus Christ rose from the dead on the third day after his crucifixion.
Jesus rising from the dead after crucifixion

Examples

Easter celebrates Christ's Resurrection and the promise of eternal life.
The doctrine of Christ's Resurrection is central to the Christian faith.
Early Christians risked persecution for proclaiming Christ's Resurrection in the ancient world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Resurrection - the act of rising from the dead
