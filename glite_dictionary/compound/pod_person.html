pod person

1. NOUN

/ pɑd ˈpɝsən / (AmE), / pɒd ˈpɜːsən / (BrE)
A person who appears to lack individuality or emotion, often seeming alienated or detached from others.
emotionless, unindividual person

Examples

After the stressful week, she felt like a total pod person at work.
He acted like a pod person, barely responding to anything around him.
People joked that the Monday morning crowd looked like pod people.

Synset Examples

He seemed like a pod person after that long meeting.
She joked that her friend was a pod person today.
Feeling like a pod person, he just wanted to sleep.

Etymology

Popularized by the 1956 film "Invasion of the Body Snatchers," based on the idea of people replaced by emotionless duplicates grown in pods.
