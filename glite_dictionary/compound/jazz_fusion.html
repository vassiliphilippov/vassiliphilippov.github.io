jazz fusion

1. NOUN

/ dʒæz ˈfjuʒən / (AmE), / dʒæz ˈfjuːʒ(ə)n / (BrE)
A style of music that blends elements of jazz with rock and uses electronic instruments such as guitars and synthesizers, originating in the 1970s.
jazz-influenced rock music genre

Examples

Miles Davis is often credited as a pioneer of jazz fusion in the late 1960s and early 1970s.
Her favorite era of music is the 1970s, especially the rise of jazz fusion.
Many modern bands draw inspiration from the experimental nature of jazz fusion.

Synset Examples

Jazz Fusion incorporates elements of different genres while maintaining an element that is distinctly ‘jazz’.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fusion - a music genre that combines different styles, especially jazz and rock
