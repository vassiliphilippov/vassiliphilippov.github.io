judicial admission

1. NOUN

/ dʒuˈdɪʃəl ədˈmɪʃən / (AmE), / dʒuˈdɪʃəl ədˈmɪʃən / (BrE)
(law) a formal statement, concession, or agreement made by a party or their attorney in a court proceeding, which is accepted as evidence and is binding in the case; typically must be written or entered into the court record.
formal statement or concession in court
legal

Examples

The defendant's judicial admission of the facts made a lengthy trial unnecessary.
A lawyer may make a judicial admission to streamline the litigation process.
The court relied on the judicial admission when delivering its judgment.

Synset Examples

a stipulation of fact was made in order to avoid delay

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stipulation - a formal legal agreement or condition in a proceeding

Etymology

From 'judicial' (relating to a court of law) and 'admission' (a statement acknowledging a fact).
