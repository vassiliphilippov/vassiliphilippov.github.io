antispasmodic agent

1. NOUN

/ ˌæntiˌspæzˈmɑdɪk ˈeɪdʒənt / (AmE), / ˌæntiˌspæzˈmɒdɪk ˈeɪdʒənt / (BrE)
A drug used to relieve or prevent spasms, especially in the smooth muscles.
drug for relieving muscle spasms
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed an antispasmodic agent to ease the patient's abdominal cramps.
Certain antispasmodic agents are often used to treat irritable bowel syndrome.
Before surgery, an antispasmodic agent was administered to prevent muscle spasms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antispasmodic - A drug or substance that relieves spasms

Etymology

From Greek 'anti-' (against) + 'spasmos' (spasm) + Latin 'agens' (doing, driving).
