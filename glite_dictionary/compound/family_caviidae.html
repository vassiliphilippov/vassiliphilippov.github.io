family Caviidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli keɪˈvɪɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfamɪli kaɪˈvɪɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of rodents in the order Hystricomorpha, which includes guinea pigs and cavies.
rodent family including guinea pigs

Examples

The family Caviidae comprises various species, including the domesticated guinea pig.
Researchers are studying the evolutionary history of the family Caviidae.
Members of the family Caviidae are native to South America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cavia - the type genus of the family Caviidae, including guinea pigs

Etymology

From New Latin 'Caviidae', based on the type genus 'Cavia' (guinea pig).
