Common Era

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːmən ˈɪrə / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən ˈɪərə / (BrE)
the period of history beginning with year one of the Gregorian calendar, equivalent to Anno Domini (AD), used especially as a secular alternative
secular alternative to Anno Domini (AD)

Examples

The ancient manuscript was dated to 200 Common Era.
Many historians now use the designation Common Era instead of Anno Domini.
Artefacts from the early Common Era period reveal the spread of new religions.

Synset Examples

in 200 CE

Related Terms

  • Synonym: CE - abbreviation for Common Era
  • Synonym: Anno Domini - in the year of our Lord; the traditional calendar system used in the West
  • Antonym: Before Common Era - the period before the Common Era; secular equivalent of BC

Etymology

Formed in the 19th century as a secular alternative to "Christian Era."

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːmən ˈɪrə / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən ˈɪərə / (BrE)
the period of time beginning with the traditionally reckoned year of the birth of Christ, used as an alternative to Anno Domini (AD)
period from Christ's (supposed) birth onward

Examples

Most historical events after the first century are dated using the Common Era calendar.
The year 2023 in the Common Era corresponds to 5783 in the Hebrew calendar.
In scholarly writing, dates are often given as '1500 Common Era' or '1500 CE'.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Anno Domini - the period of time since the birth of Christ (AD)
  • Antonym: Before Common Era - the period of time before the Common Era (BCE)

Etymology

From the Latin 'aera communis' meaning 'common era', adopted as a secular alternative to Anno Domini.
