genus Croton

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˈkroʊtɑn / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈkrəʊtən / (BrE)
A group of tropical shrubs and herbs in the spurge family, Euphorbiaceae, some of which are sources of croton oil.
group of tropical plants in the Euphorbiaceae family

Examples

Many species in the genus Croton are cultivated for their ornamental foliage.
The toxic croton oil is extracted from certain members of the genus Croton.
Researchers are studying the medicinal properties of plants in the genus Croton.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Croton - A genus of tropical plants in the spurge family

Etymology

Croton: from Greek 'krotón' (tick), referring to the shape of the seed; used in scientific Latin.
