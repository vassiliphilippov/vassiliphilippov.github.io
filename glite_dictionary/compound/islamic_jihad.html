Islamic Jihad

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈslɑmɪk dʒɪˈhɑd / (AmE), / ɪˈslæmɪk dʒɪˈhɑːd / (BrE)
an Islamic extremist organization founded in the late 1970s in Egypt, aiming to overthrow the government and establish an Islamic state; known for operating in underground cells and for its involvement in political violence.
Egyptian Islamic extremist group

Examples

Islamic Jihad was implicated in the assassination of Egypt's President Anwar Sadat in 1981.
Egyptian authorities have targeted Islamic Jihad due to its efforts to destabilize the government.
Many underground cells associated with Islamic Jihad operated in secrecy during the 1980s.

Synset Examples

the original Jihad was responsible for the assassination of Anwar Sadat in 1981

Etymology

From Arabic: 'islāmī' (Islamic) + 'jihād' (struggle, holy war).

2. PROPN

/ ɪˈslɑːmɪk dʒɪˈhɑːd / (AmE), / ɪˈslæmɪk dʒɪˈhɑːd / (BrE)
a Shiite militant organization with strong ties to Iran that seeks to establish an Iranian-style Islamic state in Lebanon; known for the use of car bombs.
Shiite militant group allied with Iran

Examples

The Lebanese government blamed Islamic Jihad for several attacks in Beirut during the 1980s.
Authorities believe that Islamic Jihad orchestrated the bombing in an effort to destabilize the region.
Many countries have designated Islamic Jihad as a terrorist organization due to its violent activities.
