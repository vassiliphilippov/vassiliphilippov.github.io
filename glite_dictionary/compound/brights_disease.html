Bright's disease

1. NOUN

/ braɪts dɪˌziːz / (AmE), / braɪts dɪˌziːz / (BrE)
a historical term for various forms of kidney inflammation (nephritis), characterized by the presence of protein in the urine, swelling, and high blood pressure
historical term for kidney inflammation
medical archaic

Examples

In the nineteenth century, Bright's disease was a common diagnosis for kidney failure.
The patient exhibited symptoms characteristic of Bright's disease, including swelling and protein in the urine.
References to Bright's disease can be found in historical medical texts from the Victorian era.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nephritis - inflammation of the kidneys

Etymology

Named after English physician Richard Bright (1789–1858), who first described the disease.
