family Sphyrnidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli sfɪərˈnɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli sfɪəˈnɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of sharks including hammerhead sharks and bonnethead sharks, characterized by their flattened, hammer-shaped heads.
taxonomic family of hammerhead sharks

Examples

The family Sphyrnidae includes some of the most distinctive shark species, such as the great hammerhead.
Scientists are studying the evolutionary advantages of the head shape within the family Sphyrnidae.
Both hammerhead and bonnethead sharks belong to the family Sphyrnidae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hammerhead shark - a shark with a distinctive, flattened, hammer-shaped head; member of Sphyrnidae

Etymology

From New Latin 'Sphyrnidae', based on Greek 'sphyra' (hammer), referring to the head shape.
