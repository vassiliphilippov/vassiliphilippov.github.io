cap shroud

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæp ˌʃraʊd / (AmE), / ˈkap ˌʃraʊd / (BrE)
A shroud attached near the masthead of a sailing vessel, supporting the mast against forces from the side.
shroud supporting a ship's mast near its top

Examples

The crew tightened the cap shroud to keep the mast stable during strong winds.
A broken cap shroud can put the entire rig at risk on a sailing vessel.
The yacht's cap shrouds were inspected before the long journey across the Atlantic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shroud - a rope or wire that supports a ship's mast

Etymology

From 'cap' referring to the mast cap and 'shroud' meaning a supporting rope or wire in ship rigging.
