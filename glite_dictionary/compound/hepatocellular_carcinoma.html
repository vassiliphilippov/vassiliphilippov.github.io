hepatocellular carcinoma

1. NOUN

/ hɛˌpæt̬oʊˌsɛljələr kɑːrˈsɪnoʊmə / (AmE), / hɪˌpatəʊˌsɛljʊlə kɑːˈsɪnəmə / (BrE)
A malignant tumor that originates in the main type of liver cells (hepatocytes).
a type of liver cancer
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with hepatocellular carcinoma after a liver biopsy.
Treatment options for hepatocellular carcinoma include surgery, chemotherapy, and liver transplantation.
Early detection of hepatocellular carcinoma can improve the chances of successful treatment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: liver cancer - A general term for cancer that starts in the liver
  • Degree Lower: cholangiocarcinoma - A cancer that forms in the bile ducts of the liver

Etymology

From Greek 'hēpar' (liver) + Latin 'cellula' (small cell) + Greek 'karkinoma' (cancer).
