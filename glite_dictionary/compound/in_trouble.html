in trouble

1. ADJ

/ ɪn ˈtrʌbəl / (AmE), / ɪn ˈtrʌbəl / (BrE)
experiencing difficulty or likely to face negative consequences, especially due to wrongdoing, mistakes, or challenging circumstances
facing difficulties or consequences

Examples

If you don't hand in your assignment on time, you'll be in trouble with the teacher.
After the company lost all its data, it found itself in trouble financially.
He knew he was in trouble when he saw the angry email from his boss.

Synset Examples

She realized she was late and muttered, 'Mai tu liao, I'm in trouble!'
They found themselves in trouble after the storm.
She was in trouble for missing the deadline.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: in difficulty - facing a difficult or problematic situation
  • Antonym: out of trouble - free from difficulties or problems
