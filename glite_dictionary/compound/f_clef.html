F clef

1. NOUN

/ ɛf klɛf / (AmE), / ɛf klɛf / (BrE)
a clef in musical notation that designates the fourth line of the staff as the F below middle C, commonly used for bass voices and instruments
musical clef marking fourth line as F below middle C

Examples

The F clef is primarily used for writing music for lower-pitched instruments like the cello and bassoon.
He quickly identified the F clef at the beginning of the musical staff and adjusted his fingering accordingly.
Learning to read the F clef is essential for bass singers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bass clef - a type of F clef, specifically placing F on the fourth line
  • Antonym: G clef - a clef marking G above middle C, usually on the second line
  • Antonym: C clef - a clef marking C on lines other than the fourth, such as the alto and tenor clefs
