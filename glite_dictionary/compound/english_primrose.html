English primrose

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ ˈprɪmˌroʊz / (AmE), / ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ ˈprɪm.rəʊz / (BrE)
a plant native to western and southern Europe, widely cultivated for its pale yellow flowers; a variety of primrose (Primula vulgaris)
a European primrose with pale yellow flowers

Examples

My grandmother planted an English primrose in her garden for its delicate yellow blooms.
In the spring, the fields were dotted with wild English primrose flowers.
Gardeners often choose the English primrose for borders because of its cheerful appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: primrose - a flowering plant of the genus Primula
