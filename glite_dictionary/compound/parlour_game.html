parlour game

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑrlɚ ɡeɪm / (AmE), / ˈpɑːlə ɡeɪm / (BrE)
a game played indoors, typically at social gatherings, involving conversation, word play, or guessing
indoor social group game

Examples

Charades is a classic parlour game enjoyed at family gatherings.
During the party, they played several parlour games to break the ice.
He suggested a parlour game to entertain the guests on a rainy afternoon.

Etymology

From 'parlour', meaning a sitting room for receiving guests, and 'game'.
