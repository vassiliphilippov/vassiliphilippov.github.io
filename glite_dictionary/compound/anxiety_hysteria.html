anxiety hysteria

1. NOUN

/ æŋˈzaɪəti hɪˈstɪriə / (AmE), / aŋˈzaɪəti hɪˈstɪəriə / (BrE)
a psychiatric condition characterized by symptoms of both conversion disorder and anxiety neurosis
psychiatric disorder with conversion and anxiety traits
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with anxiety hysteria after displaying physical symptoms without a clear medical cause.
Early psychiatrists often categorized certain unexplained neurological symptoms as anxiety hysteria.
Modern classifications have largely replaced the term anxiety hysteria with more specific diagnostic labels.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conversion disorder - a mental condition involving neurological symptoms with no medical explanation
  • Synonym: anxiety neurosis - anxiety disorder with no apparent cause

Etymology

From 'anxiety', Latin *anxietas* (uneasiness) + 'hysteria', Greek *hystera* (uterus), historically associated with nervous disorders.
