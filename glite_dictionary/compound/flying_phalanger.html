flying phalanger

1. NOUN

/ ˈflaɪɪŋ fəˈlæŋdʒər / (AmE), / ˈflaɪɪŋ fəˈlæŋdʒə / (BrE)
A nocturnal Australian marsupial that glides between trees using parachute-like folds of skin along its body.
gliding Australian marsupial

Examples

The flying phalanger moved silently from tree to tree under the cover of night.
Children were fascinated to see a flying phalanger gliding across the exhibit at the zoo.
Researchers studied the flying phalangers to understand how their skin folds enable long glides.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sugar glider - a small nocturnal gliding marsupial native to Australia and New Guinea
  • Degree Higher: marsupial - a mammal of an order whose members are born incompletely developed and typically carried and suckled in a pouch

Etymology

From English 'flying' (capable of flight) and 'phalanger', from French phalanger, from Greek phalangion (spider's web, referring to the animal's webbed limbs).
