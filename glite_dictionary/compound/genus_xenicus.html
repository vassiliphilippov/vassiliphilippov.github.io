genus Xenicus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs zɛnˈiː.kəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs zɛnˈɪ.kəs / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Xenicidae, comprising small insectivorous passerine birds native to New Zealand.
type genus of the Xenicidae family (birds)

Examples

The genus Xenicus includes the well-known rifleman bird of New Zealand.
Conservation efforts have intensified to protect species within the genus Xenicus due to their declining populations.
Members of the genus Xenicus are notable for their small size and ground-foraging habits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Xenicidae - family of small insectivorous birds endemic to New Zealand

Etymology

From Neo-Latin genus name 'Xenicus', formed from Greek xenos (strange, foreign).
