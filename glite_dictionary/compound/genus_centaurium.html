genus Centaurium

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs sɛnˈtɔːriəm / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs sɛnˈtɔːriəm / (BrE)
A genus of low-growing herbs found mostly in the Northern Hemisphere, known for their flowers with protruding, spirally twisted anthers.
a genus of low-growing herbs with twisted anthers

Examples

The genus Centaurium includes several species commonly known as centaury.
Botanists classified the specimen under the genus Centaurium due to its distinctive spiral anthers.
Medicinal herbs of the genus Centaurium have been valued in traditional remedies for centuries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Centaurium - Any plant belonging to the genus Centaurium

Etymology

From Latin 'centaurium', named after the mythical centaur Chiron who was said to have discovered medicinal uses for these plants.
