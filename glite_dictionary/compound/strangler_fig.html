strangler fig

1. NOUN

/ ˈstræŋɡlər fɪɡ / (AmE), / ˈstræŋɡlə fɪɡ / (BrE)
A tropical fig tree that begins life as an epiphyte and develops thick aerial roots, often enveloping and eventually killing its host tree.
tropical fig tree with strangling habit

Examples

A strangler fig can eventually engulf its host tree, leaving only a hollow center.
Many rainforests are home to the impressive strangler fig.
The roots of the strangler fig spread widely, making it a dominant presence in its habitat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: epiphyte - a plant that grows on another plant for support

Etymology

Refers to its habit of enveloping and 'strangling' host trees.

2. NOUN

/ ˈstræŋɡlər fɪɡ / (AmE), / ˈstræŋɡlə fɪɡ / (BrE)
A tropical tree, especially of the genus Ficus, that begins life as an epiphyte and eventually envelops and often kills its host tree by growing around it.
tropical tree that envelops its host

Examples

The strangler fig can be seen wrapping itself tightly around its host tree in the rainforest.
As the strangler fig matures, it often outcompetes its host for light and nutrients.
Many tropical forests are home to the remarkable strangler fig, which starts as a small seed in the canopy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: banyan - a type of fig tree, often forming aerial roots
  • Synonym: epiphyte - a plant that grows on another plant but is not parasitic
