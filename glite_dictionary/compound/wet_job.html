wet job

1. NOUN

/ wɛt dʒɑb / (AmE), / wɛt dʒɒb / (BrE)
an illicit or morally dubious task, especially one involving violence or assassination, often for an organization or government agency
illicit or violent contract assignment

Examples

He was hired for a wet job overseas, and the risk terrified him.
The agency denied authorizing any wet job in the region.
Rumors spread about a wet job carried out by rogue operatives.

Synset Examples

She was nervous about the wet job they planned.
His latest assignment was a wet job for the agency.
The undercover operation turned out to be a wet job.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hit - an act of murder for hire, especially by a criminal organization
  • Synonym: assassination - the act of murdering an important person for political or ideological reasons

Etymology

From the Russian phrase 'mokroye delo' (мокрое дело), literally 'wet affair', referring to bloodshed.
