high school

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪ skuːl / (AmE), / ˈhaɪ skuːl / (BrE)
A school providing education for students in the grades before college, usually including grades 9 through 12.
secondary school for grades 9-12

Examples

After graduating from high school, she planned to attend university.
He made many lifelong friends during his years at high school.
The football team at our high school just won the state championship.

Synset Examples

he goes to the neighborhood highschool

Related Terms

  • Synonym: secondary school - A school for students between primary school and college

2. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪ ˌskuːl / (AmE), / ˈhaɪ ˌskuːl / (BrE)
A school that provides secondary education, usually including grades 9 through 12 in the United States.
secondary school for grades 9–12

Examples

She graduated from high school last year and is now attending college.
Most teenagers in the U.S. attend high school between the ages of 14 and 18.
His parents moved to a new town so he could go to a better high school.

Synset Examples

he goes to the neighborhood highschool

Related Terms

  • Synonym: secondary school - a school for students between elementary and higher education
