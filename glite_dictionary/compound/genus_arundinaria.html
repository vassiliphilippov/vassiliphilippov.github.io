genus Arundinaria

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs əˌrʌndəˈnɛriə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs əˌrʌndɪˈnɛːrɪə / (BrE)
A genus of bamboos native to North America, often called cane or switchcane.
a genus of North American bamboo

Examples

Botanists have identified several species within the genus Arundinaria in the southeastern United States.
The genus Arundinaria is commonly known as switchcane in the wild forests of North America.
Conservation efforts are underway to protect habitats of native bamboos in the genus Arundinaria.

Etymology

From Latin 'arundo', 'arundinis' (reed) and Neo-Latin taxonomic usage.
