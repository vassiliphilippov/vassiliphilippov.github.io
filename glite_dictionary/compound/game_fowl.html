game fowl

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡeɪm ˌfaʊl / (AmE), / ˈɡeɪm ˌfaʊl / (BrE)
any of several breeds of domestic fowl that are bred and reared for cockfighting or for their fighting abilities
breed of chicken bred for cockfighting

Examples

The farmer kept a few game fowl for breeding purposes on his property.
In some countries, it is illegal to organize fights involving game fowl.
Enthusiasts admire the strength and agility of game fowl in exhibitions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chicken - domesticated bird kept for eggs or meat
  • Synonym: cockfight - a fight between roosters for entertainment or gambling

Etymology

From 'game' meaning 'brave in contests' and 'fowl' referring to a bird; used since the 17th century.
