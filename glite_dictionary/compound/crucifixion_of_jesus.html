Crucifixion of Jesus

1. NOUN

/ ˌkrusəˈfɪkʃən əv ˈdʒiːzəs / (AmE), / ˌkruːsɪˈfɪkʃən əv ˈdʒiːzəs / (BrE)
the execution of Jesus Christ by being nailed to a cross, regarded as a central event in Christianity
the death of Jesus Christ by crucifixion

Examples

The Crucifixion of Jesus is commemorated by Christians around the world on Good Friday.
Many artists have depicted the Crucifixion of Jesus in paintings and sculptures through the centuries.
Historians debate the political and religious context surrounding the Crucifixion of Jesus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crucifixion - the act of executing someone by nailing or binding to a cross
