vanadium pentoxide

1. NOUN

/ vəˈneɪdiəm pɛnˈtɑksaɪd / (AmE), / vəˈneɪdɪəm pɛnˈtɒksaɪd / (BrE)
A yellowish-red solid compound, V₂O₅, used as a catalyst and in the production of other vanadium compounds.
a yellowish-red vanadium compound (V₂O₅)

Examples

The bright yellow color of vanadium pentoxide makes it easy to identify in the laboratory.
Industrial chemists use vanadium pentoxide as a catalyst in the production of sulfuric acid.
Exposure to dust from vanadium pentoxide can pose health risks to workers in certain industries.

Etymology

vanadium from Latin 'Vanadis' (the Scandinavian goddess Freyja), pentoxide from Greek 'pente' (five) and 'oxide' (a compound with oxygen).
