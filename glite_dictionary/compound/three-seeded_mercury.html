three-seeded mercury

1. NOUN

/ θriː ˈsiːdɪd ˈmɜːrkjəri / (AmE), / θriː ˈsiːdɪd ˈmɜːkjʊəri / (BrE)
A weedy herb native to eastern North America, commonly found in disturbed habitats.
weedy herb of eastern North America

Examples

The field was overgrown with three-seeded mercury after years of neglect.
Gardeners often remove three-seeded mercury to control weeds in native plant beds.
The broad leaves and small flowers help identify three-seeded mercury along woodland edges.
