family Soricidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌfæməli sɒˈrɪsɪdi / (AmE), / ˌfæmɪli sɒˈrɪsɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of small insectivorous mammals known as shrews, characterized by their pointed snouts and tiny eyes.
taxonomic family of shrews

Examples

The family Soricidae includes more than 300 species of shrews found worldwide.
Members of the family Soricidae are distinguished by their sharp, spike-like teeth.
Scientists study the family Soricidae to understand the evolution of small insectivorous mammals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shrew - a small, mouse-like mammal in the family Soricidae

Etymology

From New Latin, where 'Soricidae' is derived from Latin 'sorex' (shrew).
