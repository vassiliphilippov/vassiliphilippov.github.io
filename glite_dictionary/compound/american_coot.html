American coot

1. NOUN

/ əˈmɛrɪkən kuːt / (AmE), / əˈmɛrɪkən kuːt / (BrE)
a medium-sized water bird native to North America, with a slate-gray body, white bill, and lobed toes, often found in freshwater lakes and ponds
North American water bird species

Examples

We spotted an American coot swimming among the reeds at the edge of the lake.
The distinctive white bill of the American coot makes it easy to identify from a distance.
Every winter, flocks of American coot migrate south in search of milder climates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coot - a medium-sized water bird with lobed toes and a predominantly dark plumage
