trifid bur marigold

1. NOUN

/ ˈtraɪ.fɪd bɜr ˈmɛr.ɪ.ˌɡoʊld / (AmE), / ˈtrɪ.fɪd bɜː ˈmær.ɪ.ɡəʊld / (BrE)
A species of bur marigold native to temperate regions of Eurasia, known for its trifid (three-lobed) leaves and small yellow flowers.
Eurasian bur marigold species

Examples

The trifid bur marigold can often be found growing along riverbanks in eastern Europe.
Gardeners sometimes remove trifid bur marigold to prevent it from spreading among native plants.
In autumn, the seeds of the trifid bur marigold cling to animal fur, helping the plant disperse.

Etymology

From Latin 'trifidus' (split into three) and 'bur marigold' (a plant in the genus Bidens).
