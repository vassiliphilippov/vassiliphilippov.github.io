synthetic rubber

1. NOUN

/ sɪnˈθɛtɪk ˈrʌbər / (AmE), / sɪnˈθɛtɪk ˈrʌbə / (BrE)
an artificial elastic material produced by chemical synthesis, with properties similar to those of natural rubber
artificial elastic material similar to natural rubber

Examples

Most car tires today are made from synthetic rubber instead of natural rubber.
The invention of synthetic rubber was crucial during World War II.
Researchers are developing new types of synthetic rubber to improve durability.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: natural rubber - elastic material obtained from the latex of certain plants
