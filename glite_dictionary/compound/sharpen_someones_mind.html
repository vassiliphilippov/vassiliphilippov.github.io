to sharpen someone's mind

1. VERB

/ ˈʃɑːr.pən ˈsʌm.wʌnz maɪnd / (AmE), / ˈʃɑː.pən ˈsʌm.wʌnz maɪnd / (BrE)
to make someone able to think more clearly, quickly, or effectively; to improve mental acuity
improve mental acuity or quickness

Examples

Regular reading can sharpen your mind and keep you intellectually engaged.
Challenging discussions with colleagues always sharpen my mind.
Playing chess is said to sharpen someone's mind at any age.

Synset Examples

Math puzzles are a fun way to sharpen your mind.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to stimulate - encourage or increase activity, especially mental activity
  • Antonym: dull someone's mind - to reduce mental sharpness or alertness
