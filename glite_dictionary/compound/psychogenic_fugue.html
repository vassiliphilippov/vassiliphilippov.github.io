psychogenic fugue

1. NOUN

/ saɪkoʊˈdʒɛnɪk ˈfjuːɡ / (AmE), / saɪkəʊˈdʒɛnɪk ˈfjuːɡ / (BrE)
A rare dissociative disorder in which a person loses awareness of their identity and leaves home to start a new life, later having no memory of the fugue period.
dissociative disorder involving amnesia and travel
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with a psychogenic fugue after disappearing for several weeks and returning with no memory of his past.
Cases of psychogenic fugue are extremely rare and often linked to severe psychological stress.
After the psychogenic fugue episode ended, she could not recall any events that had occurred during that period.

Etymology

From Greek 'psychē' (soul, mind) and Latin 'fuga' (flight), indicating a flight from one's own identity.
