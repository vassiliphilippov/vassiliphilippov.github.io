rabbit burrow

1. NOUN

/ ˈræbɪt ˌbɝːroʊ / (AmE), / ˈræbɪt ˌbʌrəʊ / (BrE)
A hole or tunnel in the ground used by wild rabbits as a nest or home.
hole or tunnel made by wild rabbits

Examples

A fox was seen waiting silently outside the rabbit burrow at dawn.
The children peeked into a rabbit burrow hoping to spot some baby bunnies.
We found a deserted rabbit burrow in the middle of the field.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: warren - a network of interconnecting rabbit burrows
