bridal wreath

1. NOUN

/ ˈbraɪ.dəl ˌriːθ / (AmE), / ˈbraɪ.dl̩ ˌriːθ / (BrE)
a Chilean evergreen shrub (Escallonia virgata) with delicate spikes of small white flowers, often grown as an ornamental plant
evergreen shrub with white flower spikes

Examples

The bridal wreath bloomed beautifully in the corner of her garden each spring.
Gardeners prize the bridal wreath for its arching branches covered in white blossoms.
He planted a bridal wreath hedge along the walkway to add a touch of elegance.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbraɪdəl riːθ / (AmE), / ˈbraɪdəl riːθ / (BrE)
a deciduous shrub, especially Spiraea prunifolia, with arching branches and clusters of small white flowers that bloom in spring, often planted as an ornamental
ornamental shrub with white spring flowers

Examples

The bridal wreath in her garden bloomed beautifully each spring.
They planted a row of bridal wreath to create a natural hedge along the fence.
In early April, the bridal wreath was covered in delicate white blossoms.
