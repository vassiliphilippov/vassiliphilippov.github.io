great ragweed

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡreɪt ˈræɡˌwid / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪt ˈraɡwiːd / (BrE)
a tall annual plant, Ambrosia trifida, with coarse leaves that are deeply divided into three or five parts, commonly found in North America
a tall, coarse North American annual plant

Examples

Many people are allergic to the pollen produced by great ragweed in late summer.
The leaves of great ragweed are deeply divided and rough to the touch.
Farmers often struggle to control great ragweed in their fields due to its rapid growth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ragweed - a group of plants known for causing hay fever
