family Trypetidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli traɪˈpɛtɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli traɪˈpɛtɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of small flies known as fruit flies, some of which are leaf miners; members are notable for their patterned wings and their larvae's effect on fruit and plants.
a family of fruit flies (some leaf miners)

Examples

Several species in the family Trypetidae are significant agricultural pests due to the damage their larvae cause to fruit crops.
Entomologists have reclassified many members of the family Trypetidae under Tephritidae, but the older name is still widely used.
Some flies in the family Trypetidae are known for their brightly patterned wings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Tephritidae - another family of fruit flies, often considered to include Trypetidae
  • Synonym: fruit fly - common term for flies of the family Trypetidae or Tephritidae

Etymology

From New Latin Trypetidae, from genus Trypeta plus the zoological family suffix -idae.
