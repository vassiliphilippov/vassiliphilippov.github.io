War to End War

1. NOUN

/ ˌwɔr tu ɛnd ˈwɔr / (AmE), / ˌwɔː tuː end ˈwɔː / (BrE)
a term used to refer to World War I, in the belief that it would put an end to all further wars
World War I, believed to end all wars

Examples

Many believed that World War I would be the War to End War, but history proved otherwise.
Historians often refer to the First World War as the War to End War due to the hopes it inspired.
H. G. Wells described the conflict as the War to End War in his writings during 1914.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: World War I - the First World War, fought from 1914 to 1918

Etymology

Popularized by British author H. G. Wells in 1914, expressing hope that World War I would be the final war.
