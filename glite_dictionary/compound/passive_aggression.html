passive aggression

1. NOUN

/ ˈpæsɪv əˈɡrɛʃən / (AmE), / ˈpæsɪv əˈɡreʃən / (BrE)
a pattern of indirectly expressing negative feelings or hostility, often through procrastination, stubbornness, or deliberate inefficiency rather than direct confrontation
indirect expression of hostility or anger

Examples

His passive aggression made teamwork difficult, as he would agree to help but never follow through.
You could sense her passive aggression in the way she responded with silence and coldness rather than open argument.
Instead of voicing his complaints, he resorted to passive aggression by missing important deadlines.

Synset Examples

In psychology, passive-aggressive behavior is characterized by a habitual pattern of passive resistance to expected work requirements, opposition, stubbornness, and negative attitudes in response to requirements for normal performance levels expected of others.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: aggression - hostile or violent behavior or attitudes toward another
  • Synonym: hostility - hostile behavior; unfriendliness or opposition
  • Antonym: assertiveness - confident and direct self-expression
