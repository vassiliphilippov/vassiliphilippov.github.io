theory of organic evolution

1. NOUN

/ ˈθiəri əv ɔrˈɡænɪk ˌɛvəˈluʃən / (AmE), / ˈθɪəri əv ɔːˈɡænɪk ˌiːvəˈluːʃən / (BrE)
the scientific theory that explains the origin and development of species of plants and animals through evolutionary processes
scientific theory on evolution of species

Examples

Darwin is most famous for developing the theory of organic evolution, fundamentally changing how we view life on Earth.
The theory of organic evolution suggests that all species are connected through common ancestry.
Some textbooks present the theory of organic evolution alongside other explanations for the diversity of life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: evolution - biological process by which organisms change over generations
  • Antonym: creationism - the religious belief that life originated from specific acts of divine creation
