snake feeder

1. NOUN

/ sneɪk ˈfiːdɚ / (AmE), / sneɪk ˈfiːdə / (BrE)
A slender-bodied insect with iridescent wings that rests with wings outspread; adults and nymphs feed on mosquitoes and similar insects. Another name for a dragonfly, used chiefly in some regional dialects.
regional name for a dragonfly

Examples

Children often call a snake feeder a dragonfly because of its swift flight near ponds.
A bright blue snake feeder hovered just above the tall grass by the river.
In some places, people believe seeing a snake feeder means a snake is nearby.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dragonfly - a large, slender insect with two pairs of transparent wings
