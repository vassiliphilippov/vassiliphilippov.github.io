Horner's syndrome

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɔrnərz ˌsɪnˌdroʊm / (AmE), / ˈhɔːnəz ˌsɪn.drəʊm / (BrE)
A medical condition characterized by drooping eyelids, constricted pupils, and absence of sweating on the face, caused by damage to nerves in the cervical region of the spine.
neurological condition causing facial symptoms
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with Horner's syndrome after showing a drooping eyelid and a constricted pupil.
A tumor in the upper chest can sometimes lead to Horner's syndrome.
Partial facial paralysis and lack of sweating are classic symptoms associated with Horner's syndrome.

Etymology

Named after Johann Friedrich Horner, the Swiss ophthalmologist who first described it.
