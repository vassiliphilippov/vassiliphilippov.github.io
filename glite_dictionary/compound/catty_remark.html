catty remark

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæti rɪˈmɑrk / (AmE), / ˈkæti rɪˈmɑːk / (BrE)
a mean or spiteful comment, typically intended to hurt or criticize someone
mean or spiteful comment

Examples

She made a catty remark about her colleague's dress during the meeting.
His catty remark left everyone feeling uncomfortable at the party.
Instead of being supportive, he couldn't resist a catty remark about her new project.

Synset Examples

Her catty remarks made the meeting uncomfortable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cutting remark - a comment intended to hurt someone's feelings
  • Antonym: compliment - a polite expression of praise or admiration

Etymology

From 'catty' (like a cat, spiteful) + 'remark'.
