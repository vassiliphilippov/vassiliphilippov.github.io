mountain swamp gum

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaʊntən swɑmp ɡʌm / (AmE), / ˈmaʊntən swɒmp ɡʌm / (BrE)
a medium-sized eucalyptus tree (Eucalyptus camphora) found in swampy regions of New South Wales and Victoria, Australia
Australian eucalyptus tree

Examples

The mountain swamp gum is often found growing in the cool, wet highlands of Victoria.
Many conservation projects focus on preserving the habitat of the mountain swamp gum.
The pale bark of the mountain swamp gum makes it easy to recognize in the forest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swamp gum - another name for certain eucalyptus species found in wetlands
