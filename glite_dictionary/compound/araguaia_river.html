Araguaia River

1. PROPN

/ ˌɑːrəˈgwaɪ.ə ˈrɪvər / (AmE), / ˌɑːrəˈgwaɪ.ə ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
a major river in central Brazil that flows northward, joining the Tocantins River, known for its many falls and diverse ecosystems
major river in central Brazil

Examples

The Araguaia River is home to a wide variety of wildlife and stunning natural landscapes.
Tourists travel thousands of miles to witness the dramatic waterfalls of the Araguaia River.
The Araguaia River eventually meets the Tocantins River in northern Brazil.
