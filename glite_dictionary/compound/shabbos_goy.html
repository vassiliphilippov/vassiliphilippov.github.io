shabbos goy

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃɑːboʊs ˌɡɔɪ / (AmE), / ˈʃabəs ˌɡɔɪ / (BrE)
A non-Jewish person who performs tasks or work prohibited for Jews to do themselves on the Sabbath.
non-Jew helping Jews on the Sabbath

Examples

During winter, the family would ask their neighbor to act as the shabbos goy when the lights needed switching on.
Many communities in the past relied on a trusted shabbos goy to help with essential tasks on Saturdays.
The building superintendent served as a shabbos goy for several Jewish residents in the apartment block.

Synset Examples

Being a shabbos goy requires understanding cultural traditions.
He acted as a shabbos goy by helping with chores.
The neighbor offered to be our shabbos goy during the holidays.

Etymology

From Yiddish 'shabes' (Sabbath) + 'goy' (non-Jew), from Hebrew.
