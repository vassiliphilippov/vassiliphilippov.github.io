coding DNA

1. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊdɪŋ diːˌɛnˈeɪ / (AmE), / ˈkəʊdɪŋ diːˌɛnˈeɪ / (BrE)
the portion of a gene's DNA sequence that is transcribed and translated into protein structures
DNA sequence transcribed into protein
medical

Examples

Scientists study coding DNA to understand how genes direct the production of proteins.
Mutations in coding DNA can lead to changes in protein structure and function.
Only a small percentage of the human genome is made up of coding DNA.

Synset Examples

exons are interspersed with introns

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exon - region of a gene that codes for proteins
  • Antonym: non-coding DNA - DNA sequences that do not code for proteins

Etymology

From 'coding', referring to its role in specifying protein structure, and the abbreviation 'DNA' (deoxyribonucleic acid).
