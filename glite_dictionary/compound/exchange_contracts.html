to exchange contracts

1. VERB

/ ɪkˈstʃeɪndʒ ˈkɑnˌtrækt͡s / (AmE), / ɪksˈtʃeɪndʒ ˈkɒntrækts / (BrE)
to formally swap signed legal documents, especially in property transactions, making an agreement legally binding
formally swap legal documents in a transaction
legal

Examples

The buyer and seller will exchange contracts next week to finalize the deal.
Once both parties exchange contracts, the agreement becomes legally binding.
We can't move forward until the solicitors have exchanged contracts.

Synset Examples

They will exchange contracts after the final inspection.
The lawyers met to exchange contracts for the sale.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: complete the sale - to finalize the sale process, especially in property law
