low frequency

1. NOUN

/ loʊ ˈfriːkwənsi / (AmE), / ləʊ ˈfriːkwənsi / (BrE)
a frequency in the range of 30 to 300 kilohertz, often used in radio and electronic communication
frequency range (30–300 kHz) in radio/electronics

Examples

Radio waves in the low frequency range are used for submarine communication.
The engineer adjusted the equipment to detect low frequency signals.
Lightning can generate natural electromagnetic pulses in the low frequency band.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: very low frequency - 3 to 30 kilohertz
  • Degree Higher: medium frequency - 300 to 3000 kilohertz

2. NOUN

/ loʊ ˈfriː.kwən.si / (AmE), / ləʊ ˈfriː.kwən.si / (BrE)
a frequency that is lower in pitch compared to others, especially referring to the lower end of the audible spectrum or electromagnetic spectrum
frequency lower than others

Examples

The storm produced sounds at such a low frequency that they could barely be heard.
Elephants can communicate using low frequency rumbles that travel long distances.
Many subwoofers are designed to reproduce low frequency audio for home theaters.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: high frequency - a frequency that is greater in pitch or cycles per second than others
