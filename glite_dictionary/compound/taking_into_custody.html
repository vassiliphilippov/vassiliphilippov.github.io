taking into custody

1. NOUN

/ ˈteɪkɪŋ ˈɪntu ˈkʌstədi / (AmE), / ˈteɪkɪŋ ˈɪntu ˈkʌstədi / (BrE)
the act of detaining or arresting someone, especially by the police or legal authorities
act of arresting or detaining someone

Examples

The taking into custody of the suspect occurred without incident.
His taking into custody was reported widely in the news the following day.
Police announced the taking into custody of three people in connection to the robbery.
