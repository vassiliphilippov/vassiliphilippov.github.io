Egyptian lupine

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈdʒɪpʃən ˈluːpaɪn / (AmE), / ɪˈdʒɪpʃ(ə)n ˈluːpɪn / (BrE)
a white-flowered Eurasian herb, widely cultivated for animal forage and erosion control
a white-flowered Eurasian forage plant

Examples

Farmers often plant Egyptian lupine to enrich the soil with nitrogen.
The fields were covered in the white blossoms of Egyptian lupine each spring.
Egyptian lupine is recognized for its effectiveness in controlling soil erosion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lupine - a plant of the genus Lupinus, known for its compounded leaves and racemes of flowers
