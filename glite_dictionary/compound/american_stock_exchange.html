American Stock Exchange

1. NOUN

/ əˈmɛrɪkən stɑk ɪksˌtʃeɪndʒ / (AmE), / əˈmɛrɪkən stɒk ɪksˌtʃeɪndʒ / (BrE)
a stock exchange in New York City, formerly known as the New York Curb Exchange, handling stocks and securities not listed on the New York Stock Exchange.
New York stock exchange (the AMEX)

Examples

The American Stock Exchange was once known for its trading of small and mid-sized companies' shares.
Many technology firms initially got listed on the American Stock Exchange before moving to larger markets.
Financial news often reports changes in indexes from both the NYSE and the American Stock Exchange.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: New York Stock Exchange - main stock exchange in New York City (NYSE)
