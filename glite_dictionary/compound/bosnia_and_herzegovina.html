Bosnia and Herzegovina

1. PROPN

/ ˈboʊzniə ənd hɛrtsəgoʊˈviːnə / (AmE), / ˈbɒzniə ənd hɛːtsəɡəˈviːnə / (BrE)
A mountainous country in southeastern Europe on the Balkan Peninsula, formerly part of Yugoslavia, which declared independence in 1992.
country in southeastern Europe

Examples

During the 1990s, Bosnia and Herzegovina experienced a devastating civil war.
She traveled through Bosnia and Herzegovina to learn about its diverse culture.
The capital city of Bosnia and Herzegovina is Sarajevo.

Etymology

From Slavic roots: 'Bosnia' refers to the Bosna river; 'Herzegovina' from German 'Herzog' (duke), after the medieval duchy.
