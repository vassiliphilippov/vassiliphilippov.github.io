false mildew

1. NOUN

/ fɔls ˈmɪldu / (AmE), / fɔːls ˈmɪldjuː / (BrE)
a type of parasitic fungus from the family Peronosporaceae, affecting plants such as grapes, potatoes, and melons
parasitic fungus affecting plants

Examples

The vineyard suffered heavy losses due to false mildew spreading among the grapevines.
Farmers apply special fungicides to protect their crops from false mildew.
Potato plants are particularly vulnerable to infection by false mildew during wet seasons.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: downy mildew - a related group of parasitic fungi of the family Peronosporaceae
