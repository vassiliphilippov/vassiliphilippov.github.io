Council of Chalcedon

1. PROPN

/ ˈkaʊnsəl əv kælˈsiːdən / (AmE), / ˈkaʊnsəl əv kælˈsiːdən / (BrE)
the fourth ecumenical council of the Christian Church, held in 451 AD, which proclaimed the doctrine of the two natures, human and divine, in the person of Christ
early Christian ecumenical council (451 AD)

Examples

The decisions of the Council of Chalcedon have shaped Christian doctrine for centuries.
Scholars still debate why the Council of Chalcedon was convened in 451.
Many churches regard the Council of Chalcedon as a cornerstone of their beliefs.

Etymology

From the ancient city of Chalcedon (present-day Kadıköy, Istanbul), where the council was held.
