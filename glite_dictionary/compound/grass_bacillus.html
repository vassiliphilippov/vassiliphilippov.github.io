grass bacillus

1. NOUN

/ ɡræs bəˈsɪləs / (AmE), / ɡrɑːs bəˈsɪləs / (BrE)
a species of bacterium (Bacillus subtilis) commonly found in soil and decomposing organic matter, some strains of which produce antibiotics
species of soil-dwelling bacterium
medical

Examples

The grass bacillus can be isolated from soil samples in many environments.
Some antibiotics are commercially produced from strains of the grass bacillus.
Researchers studied how the grass bacillus interacts with plant roots to promote growth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Bacillus subtilis - species of bacterium also known as grass bacillus
