common front

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑm.ən frʌnt / (AmE), / ˈkɒm.ən frʌnt / (BrE)
a unified position or movement adopted by different individuals or groups, especially to achieve a shared goal or to present solidarity
a unified stance by a group or alliance

Examples

The environmental groups formed a common front to demand stricter pollution controls.
By creating a common front, local businesses were able to negotiate better terms with the city council.
The political parties put aside their differences to present a common front against the new legislation.

Synset Examples

the unions presented a common front at the bargaining table

Related Terms

  • Synonym: united front - a group acting together for a common purpose
