word-painting

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɝd ˌpeɪntɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwɜːd ˌpeɪntɪŋ / (BrE)
The act of describing something in vivid or graphic verbal detail, especially in writing or speech.
vivid or graphic verbal description

Examples

The novelist's use of word-painting brought the bustling city to life for the reader.
Through skillful word-painting, the poet evoked the serenity of a summer meadow.
Her word-painting of the storm made us feel as if we were right there in the midst of it.

Synset Examples

too often the narrative was interrupted by long word pictures
the author gives a depressing picture of life in Poland
the pamphlet contained brief characterizations of famous Vermonters

Related Terms

  • Synonym: word-picture - a vivid or graphic verbal description
  • Synonym: characterization - the creation or construction of a fictional character or a description of someone's qualities

Etymology

From 'word' + 'painting', by analogy to painting with words rather than with colors; attested since the 17th century.
