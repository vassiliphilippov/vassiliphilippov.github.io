suborder Anisoptera

1. NOUN

/ ˌsʌbˈɔrdər ˌænəˈsɒptərə / (AmE), / ˌsʌbˈɔːdə ˌænɪˈsɒptərə / (BrE)
A zoological suborder comprising the dragonflies, characterized by strong flight and wings held horizontally when at rest.
dragonfly suborder in zoology

Examples

The suborder Anisoptera includes all species commonly known as dragonflies.
Members of the suborder Anisoptera are powerful fliers often seen near ponds and streams.
Unlike damselflies, insects in the suborder Anisoptera hold their wings flat when at rest.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Odonata - the order of insects including dragonflies and damselflies
  • Synonym: damselfly - insect of suborder Zygoptera, similar to but distinct from dragonflies

Etymology

From Greek 'anisos' (unequal) + 'pteron' (wing), referring to the unequal size of dragonflies' wings.
