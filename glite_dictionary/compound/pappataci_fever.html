pappataci fever

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑːpəˈtɑːtʃi ˈfiːvər / (AmE), / ˌpapəˈtɑːtʃi ˈfiːvə / (BrE)
A mild viral disease transmitted by the bite of the sand fly Phlebotomus papatasii, typically causing fever and malaise.
mild viral disease from sand fly bite
medical

Examples

Travelers to Mediterranean regions should be aware of pappataci fever, which can be spread by sand flies.
Early symptoms of pappataci fever include high temperature and general discomfort.
Preventative measures against pappataci fever involve using insect repellent and sleeping under nets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sandfly fever - A synonym: another name for pappataci fever.

Etymology

From Italian 'pappataci' (the sand fly species name) and English 'fever'.
