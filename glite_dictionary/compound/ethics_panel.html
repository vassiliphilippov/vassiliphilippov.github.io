ethics panel

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛθɪks ˌpænəl / (AmE), / ˈɛθɪks ˌpanəl / (BrE)
a committee appointed to examine and make decisions on matters of ethical concern, especially within an organization or institution.
committee reviewing ethical issues

Examples

The university's ethics panel is reviewing the conduct of several researchers.
An ethics panel may be called upon to rule on patient confidentiality cases.
The company created an ethics panel to address emerging moral dilemmas in artificial intelligence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ethics committee - a committee responsible for ensuring ethical conduct
