paraffin scale

1. NOUN

/ ˈpærəˌfɪn skeɪl / (AmE), / ˈparəfɪn skeɪl / (BrE)
a partly refined paraffin wax, often obtained as a residue during petroleum refining and used in various industrial applications
partly refined paraffin wax

Examples

The laboratory tested samples of paraffin scale for purity before further processing.
Refineries produce paraffin scale as an intermediate step in making commercial wax products.
Blocks of paraffin scale are sometimes used in industrial lubrication applications.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paraffin wax - a white or colorless soft solid derived from petroleum, used in candles and lubricants
