sure enough

1. ADV

/ ʃʊr ɪˈnʌf / (AmE), / ʃɔː ɪˈnʌf / (BrE)
as expected or just as one supposed; used to confirm that something happened in the way anticipated.
as expected; just as supposed

Examples

He said it would rain, and sure enough, it started pouring within an hour.
I thought the keys might be in my jacket, and sure enough, there they were.
She warned him the cake would burn if left unattended, and sure enough, it did.

Synset Examples

sure enough, he asked her for money again

2. ADV

/ ʃʊr ɪˈnʌf / (AmE), / ʃɔː ɪˈnʌf / (BrE)
Used to say that something happened just as expected or predicted.
as expected or predicted

Examples

I thought it would rain today, and sure enough, the sky opened up this afternoon.
She said the movie would start late, and sure enough, the previews ran long.
You predicted trouble, and sure enough, the project ran into problems.

Synset Examples

Sure enough, he arrived late.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indeed - used to emphasize a statement or response confirming something already suggested
