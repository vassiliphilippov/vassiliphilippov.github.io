Cracker Jack box

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrækɚ ˌdʒæk ˌbɑks / (AmE), / ˈkrækə ˌdʒæk ˌbɒks / (BrE)
used to refer humorously to something that is surprising, unexpected, or seemingly obtained by luck rather than skill, alluding to the toy surprises found in Cracker Jack snack boxes.
something surprising or unexpectedly acquired
american

Examples

Her flair for painting seemed to come straight out of a Cracker Jack box.
Do you think he got that degree from a Cracker Jack box or what?
His sudden ability to play piano was like finding a prize in a Cracker Jack box.

Synset Examples

Her singing talent seems to come from a Cracker Jack box.

Etymology

Named after the U.S. snack brand 'Cracker Jack,' which included toy surprises in its boxes.
