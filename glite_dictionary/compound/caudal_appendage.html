caudal appendage

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔdəl əˈpɛndɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈkɔːdəl əˈpɛndɪdʒ / (BrE)
the tail, especially in mammals, located at the posterior end above the anus
tail or rear appendage (esp. in mammals)

Examples

The horse's caudal appendage swished as it tried to brush away flies.
Many vertebrates possess a caudal appendage that aids in balance and movement.
Veterinarians examined the injury at the base of the cat's caudal appendage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tail - the rear part or appendage of an animal, usually extending beyond the anus

Etymology

From Latin 'cauda' (tail) and English 'appendage' (something attached).
