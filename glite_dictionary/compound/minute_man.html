minute man

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪnɪt ˌmæn / (AmE), / ˈmɪnɪt ˌman / (BrE)
(US history) A member of the colonial militia during the American Revolution who could be ready to fight at a minute's notice; more generally, a person prepared to act quickly in urgent situations.
colonial militia ready for rapid action
american

Examples

The minute man of the Revolution was recognized for his swift response to alarms.
During the emergency, she proved herself to be a modern minute man, ready to help at once.
The museum features a statue of a minute man commemorating their historic role in the war.

Synset Examples

The minute man was prepared for any situation.
In emergencies, he acted like a minute man.
He was a minute man, always ready for action.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: militiaman - member of a militia, especially in colonial America

Etymology

From the idea that members could be ready in a minute; first used for US colonial militias in the 18th century.
