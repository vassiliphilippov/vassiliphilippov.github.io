old caboose

1. NOUN

/ oʊld kəˈbus / (AmE), / əʊld kəˈbuːs / (BrE)
the last car attached to a freight train, often used historically as a crew's quarters or office
last car of a freight train

Examples

The children explored the old caboose that had been restored at the railway museum.
Photographers often find inspiration in the weathered paint and rusty wheels of an old caboose.
The old caboose was converted into a cozy reading nook at the library.

Synset Examples

The kids loved playing near the old caboose at the park.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caboose - the last car of a freight train, used by crew

Etymology

From Spanish 'caboose' (cambuz), originally meaning a ship’s galley; later extended in English to the train car. 'Old' signifies something from the past, often unused or from an earlier era.
