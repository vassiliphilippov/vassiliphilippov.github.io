macedoine of fruit

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæsəˈdwæn əv ˈfruːt / (AmE), / ˌmæsɪˈdwɑːn əv ˈfruːt / (BrE)
a dish consisting of a mixture of diced fruits, often served as a dessert
dish of mixed diced fruits

Examples

For dessert, they prepared a colorful macedoine of fruit with a light syrup.
A chilled macedoine of fruit is perfect for hot summer days.
The menu included a macedoine of fruit alongside cakes and tarts.

Synset Examples

The chef served a refreshing macedoine of fruits for dessert.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fruit salad - a dish consisting of mixed pieces of various fruits

Etymology

From French 'macédoine', originally meaning a mixed cut salad, and referring to Macedonia, a region known for its diversity.
