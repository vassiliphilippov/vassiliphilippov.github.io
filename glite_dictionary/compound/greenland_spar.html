Greenland spar

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡrinˌlænd spɑr / (AmE), / ˈɡriːnlənd spɑː / (BrE)
A transparent or white mineral composed of fluorides of aluminum and sodium, used as a source of fluorine.
white mineral; source of fluorine

Examples

The aluminum was extracted from the Greenland spar in carefully controlled laboratory conditions.
Miners once sought Greenland spar for its value as a source of fluorine.
A rare specimen of Greenland spar is displayed in the museum's mineralogy section.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cryolite - A mineral consisting of sodium aluminum fluoride, principal source of fluorine

Etymology

Named for being found in Greenland, 'spar' is a general term for non-metallic minerals with a vitreous luster.
