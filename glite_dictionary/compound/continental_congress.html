Continental Congress

1. PROPN

/ ˌkɑːn.təˈnɛn.t̬əl ˈkɑːŋ.ɡrəs / (AmE), / ˌkɒn.tɪˈnen.tl ˈkɒŋ.ɡrəs / (BrE)
The assembly of delegates from the American colonies that met during and after the American Revolution to govern, declare independence, and frame the Articles of Confederation.
assembly of delegates in the American Revolution

Examples

The Continental Congress played a crucial role in uniting the colonies against British rule.
Delegates to the Continental Congress signed the Declaration of Independence in 1776.
After fierce debate, the Continental Congress adopted the Articles of Confederation as America's first constitution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Congress - the national legislative body of a country, especially the US

Etymology

From 'continental', referring to the American continent, and 'congress', from Latin 'congressus' (gathering, meeting).
