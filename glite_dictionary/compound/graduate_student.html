graduate student

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡrædʒuət ˌstuːdənt / (AmE), / ˈɡrædʒuət ˌstjuːdənt / (BrE)
a student who is studying for a master's or doctoral degree after completing a first degree
student pursuing postgraduate degree

Examples

After finishing her bachelor's degree, she became a graduate student at the university.
Many graduate students work as teaching assistants while completing their studies.
The conference was open to both faculty and graduate students.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: undergraduate - a student at a college or university who has not yet earned a first degree

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡrædʒuət ˌstuːdənt / (AmE), / ˈɡrædʒuət ˌstjuːdənt / (BrE)
A student who is studying for a degree beyond the bachelor's level, such as a master's or doctoral degree.
student pursuing advanced studies after a bachelor's degree

Examples

As a graduate student, she conducted original research in biology.
Many graduate students teach undergraduate courses as part of their degree requirements.
He was accepted as a graduate student at a prestigious university.

Synset Examples

Her graduate student descent made her a skilled researcher.
With her graduate student descent, she excelled in her studies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: postgraduate - a student engaged in advanced study after having earned a bachelor's degree
  • Antonym: undergraduate - a university or college student who has not yet earned a bachelor's degree
