downy wood mint

1. NOUN

/ ˈdaʊni wʊd mɪnt / (AmE), / ˈdaʊni wʊd mɪnt / (BrE)
a North American perennial herb with soft hairs and pink to purple flowers stacked in whorls, resembling a pagoda (Blephilia ciliata)
a soft-haired herb with purple flowers (Blephilia ciliata)

Examples

The shaded edges of the forest were dotted with blooming downy wood mint.
Bees are especially attracted to downy wood mint for its vibrant, fragrant flowers.
Gardeners value downy wood mint for its ability to attract pollinators and its unique foliage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wood mint - a herbaceous plant of the mint family found in woodlands
