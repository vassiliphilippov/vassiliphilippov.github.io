family Palmae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ˈpælmi / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli ˈpɑːlmiː / (BrE)
A large family of chiefly tropical trees, shrubs, and vines, usually with tall columnar trunks and a crown of very large leaves; also known as the palm family.
the palm family of plants

Examples

The family Palmae includes many well-known tropical plants, such as the coconut and date palm.
Botanists have long studied the distinctive leaf structure of members of the family Palmae.
In some regions, the family Palmae plays a crucial role in both ecology and agriculture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: palm - a tree of the family Palmae, with a tall trunk and large leaves

Etymology

From Latin 'palma' meaning 'palm (tree, hand)'.
