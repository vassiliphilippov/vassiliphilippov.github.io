superior general

1. NOUN

/ suːˈpɪriər ˈdʒɛnərəl / (AmE), / suːˈpɪəriə ˈdʒɛnərəl / (BrE)
the head of a religious order or congregation, especially in the Roman Catholic Church
head of a religious order

Examples

The newly elected superior general addressed the members of the order in Rome.
She reported directly to the superior general during her assignment abroad.
A superior general is responsible for the overall governance of the congregation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abbot - head of a monastery for monks
  • Synonym: prior - a religious superior in certain orders

Etymology

From Latin 'superior' (higher, elder) and 'general' (from Latin 'generalis', relating to all).
