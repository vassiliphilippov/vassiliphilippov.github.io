nose flute

1. NOUN

/ noʊz fluːt / (AmE), / nəʊz fluːt / (BrE)
A type of flute that is played by blowing air through the nostrils rather than the mouth.
flute played with nostrils

Examples

The Hawaiian musician captivated the crowd by playing the nose flute.
Traditional ceremonies in some cultures include performances on the nose flute.
He learned to play the nose flute during his travels in Southeast Asia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flute - a musical instrument played by blowing across a hole
