red fox

1. NOUN

/ rɛd fɑks / (AmE), / rɛd fɒks / (BrE)
a medium-sized, reddish-brown wild fox widespread across the Northern Hemisphere, known for its bushy tail and adaptability
a reddish-brown wild fox species

Examples

The red fox is known for its keen hunting skills and adaptability to various environments.
We spotted a beautiful red fox darting through the snowy field early in the morning.
Red foxes are common in both rural and suburban areas across North America and Europe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fox - a small-to-medium-sized, wild dog with a bushy tail and pointed snout

2. NOUN

/ rɛd fɑks / (AmE), / rɛd fɒks / (BrE)
a fox species (Vulpes vulpes) with reddish-brown fur, native to the Northern Hemisphere
a fox species with reddish fur

Examples

The red fox is known for its cunning and adaptability in various environments.
We spotted a beautiful red fox while hiking through the forest last spring.
Farmers sometimes complain when a red fox steals chickens from their coops.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fox - a small to medium-sized, carnivorous mammal with a pointed muzzle and bushy tail
