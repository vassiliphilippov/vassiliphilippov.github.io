station wagon

1. NOUN

/ ˈsteɪʃən ˌwæɡən / (AmE), / ˈsteɪʃən ˌwæɡən / (BrE)
a car with an extended rear cargo area and a rear door, providing extra space behind the rear seats
car with extended rear cargo area

Examples

They loaded the camping gear into the station wagon before leaving for the mountains.
Her family chose a station wagon for its spacious trunk and comfortable seating.
The old station wagon still runs well despite its age.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: estate car - British term for a car with an extended rear cargo area; same as 'station wagon'

Etymology

From 'station' (railway station) and 'wagon', originally referred to vehicles used to carry luggage for train passengers.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsteɪʃən ˌwæɡən / (AmE), / ˈsteɪʃən ˌwæɡən / (BrE)
A car with a long body, a rear door, and space behind the rear seat for carrying goods or luggage.
car with extended cargo area

Examples

They loaded the camping gear into the back of the station wagon before leaving for the mountains.
The family bought a station wagon because it offered plenty of space for their three kids and luggage.
Our old station wagon was reliable and could haul everything from groceries to bicycles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: estate car - British term for 'station wagon', meaning a car with an extended cargo area

Etymology

Originally used in the early 20th century to describe cars designed to carry passengers and luggage to and from train stations.
