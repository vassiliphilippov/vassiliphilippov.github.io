commercial law

1. NOUN

/ kəˈmɝːʃəl lɔ / (AmE), / kəˈmɜːʃəl lɔː / (BrE)
the branch of law that governs business and commercial transactions, especially those arising from the practices of merchants and traders.
law relating to business transactions
legal

Examples

She specializes in commercial law, advising companies on contracts and mergers.
Changes in commercial law have made international trade more straightforward.
During his studies, he developed a strong interest in commercial law and its impact on business.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: business law - the body of law dealing with business and commercial matters

Etymology

From 'commercial' (relating to commerce) + 'law'.
