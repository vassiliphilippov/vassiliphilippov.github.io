suborder Ophidia

1. NOUN

/ sʌbˌɔrdɚ oʊˈfɪdiə / (AmE), / sʌbˌɔːdə əʊˈfɪdɪə / (BrE)
A former taxonomic group consisting of snakes within the order Squamata.
former zoological group for snakes
medical

Examples

The extinct genus appears in fossil records as part of the suborder Ophidia.
Taxonomists once classified all snakes under the suborder Ophidia.
Modern classifications have largely replaced the use of suborder Ophidia in herpetology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snake - elongate, legless, carnivorous reptile

Etymology

From New Latin 'Ophidia', from Greek 'ophis' meaning 'snake'.
