case study

1. NOUN

/ ˈkeɪs ˌstʌdi / (AmE), / ˈkeɪs ˌstʌdi / (BrE)
a detailed study of a particular person, group, or situation, especially as an example of a class of things or to identify the causes of success or failure
detailed examination of an example

Examples

The marketing team presented a case study to illustrate how their new strategy had increased sales.
For her dissertation, she conducted a case study of a rural health clinic in India.
This book includes a case study that demonstrates the impact of leadership on organizational culture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: analysis - detailed examination of something, typically for purposes of explanation or interpretation

2. NOUN

/ ˈkeɪs ˌstʌdi / (AmE), / ˈkeɪs ˌstʌdi / (BrE)
A detailed analysis of a particular person, group, or situation, especially in social, psychological, or medical contexts, often used for research or teaching purposes.
detailed analysis of an individual case

Examples

The professor assigned a case study to help students apply theoretical concepts to real-world situations.
This case study explores the impact of early intervention on children with developmental disorders.
Our team published a case study in the journal to illustrate a rare medical condition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clinical case - a detailed account of a patient's medical history and symptoms for study
  • Degree Higher: survey - an examination of a group or situation but more general than a case study
  • Degree Lower: overview - a general outline or summary, less detailed than a case study
