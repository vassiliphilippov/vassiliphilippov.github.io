internal medicine

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtɝnəl ˈmɛdəsən / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɜːnəl ˈmɛdɪsɪn / (BrE)
The branch of medicine concerned with the diagnosis and nonsurgical treatment of diseases affecting internal organs, especially in adults.
medical specialty treating internal organs (adults)
medical

Examples

After completing his residency in internal medicine, he chose to specialize in cardiology.
Many adults with chronic illnesses are referred to internal medicine specialists for ongoing care.
Her interest in diagnosing complex cases led her to a career in internal medicine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: family medicine - medical specialty focusing on comprehensive care for all ages

Etymology

From Latin 'internus' (inward) and 'medicina' (the healing art).
