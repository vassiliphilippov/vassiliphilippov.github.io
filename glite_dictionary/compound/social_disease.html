social disease

1. NOUN

/ ˈsoʊ.ʃəl dɪˈziz / (AmE), / ˈsəʊ.ʃəl dɪˈziːz / (BrE)
a disease that is transmitted through sexual intercourse or genital contact; a sexually transmitted infection
sexually transmitted disease (historical term)

Examples

The rise in social disease rates during the early 20th century prompted new public health campaigns.
Doctors in the past often used the term social disease to refer to syphilis and gonorrhea.
Laws were enacted to suppress the spread of social disease in crowded urban centers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sexually transmitted disease - a disease passed through sexual contact
