pulmonary artery

1. NOUN

/ ˈpʊlməˌnɛri ˈɑːrtəri / (AmE), / ˈpʊlmənəri ˈɑːtəri / (BrE)
either of the two arteries that carry deoxygenated blood from the right side of the heart to the lungs for oxygenation
artery carrying blood from heart to lungs
medical

Examples

The pulmonary artery carries blood from the right ventricle of the heart to the lungs.
A blockage in the pulmonary artery can cause serious complications.
Doctors examined the patient's pulmonary artery to assess blood flow to the lungs.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: pulmonary vein - blood vessel that brings oxygenated blood from lungs to the heart
  • Antonym: aorta - main artery carrying blood from the heart to the body
  • Synonym: artery - a blood vessel that carries blood away from the heart

Etymology

From Latin *pulmonarius* (of the lungs) + English *artery*, from Greek *artēria* (windpipe, artery).
