primordial soup

1. NOUN

/ praɪˈmɔr.di.əl suːp / (AmE), / praɪˈmɔː.di.əl suːp / (BrE)
a solution of organic molecules thought to have existed in the early Earth's oceans, from which life is believed to have originated
early Earth's life-originating organic mixture

Examples

Many theories about the origin of life focus on the primordial soup theory.
Scientists have tried to recreate the conditions of primordial soup in laboratory experiments.
According to some researchers, amino acids first formed within the Earth's primordial soup billions of years ago.

Synset Examples

Scientists believe life began in the primordial soup of the oceans.
The concept of primordial soup explains early Earth's conditions.

Etymology

Primordial from Latin 'primordialis' (original, first), and 'soup' referring metaphorically to a liquid mixture.
