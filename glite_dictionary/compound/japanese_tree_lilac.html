Japanese tree lilac

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˈpænɪs ˈtri ˈlaɪlək / (AmE), / dʒəˈpænɪs ˈtri ˈlɪlək / (BrE)
a small tree native to Japan, with narrow pointed leaves and clusters of creamy-white flowers, often grown as an ornamental plant
ornamental tree from Japan with white flowers

Examples

The Japanese tree lilac blooms in early summer, filling the air with a sweet fragrance.
We planted a Japanese tree lilac in the front yard for its decorative flowers.
Bees are attracted to the creamy-white blossoms of the Japanese tree lilac every year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lilac - a shrub or small tree with fragrant purple, white, or pink flowers
