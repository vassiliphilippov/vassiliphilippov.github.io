cyanide process

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪəˌnaɪd ˌprɑː.ses / (AmE), / ˈsaɪənaɪd ˌprəʊ.ses / (BrE)
an industrial method of extracting gold and silver from ore by treating it with a sodium cyanide solution
industrial method of extracting gold and silver

Examples

The cyanide process revolutionized gold mining in the late nineteenth century.
Many modern mines still extract precious metals using the cyanide process.
Strict environmental regulations are now in place for operations that use the cyanide process.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cyanidation - a process for extracting gold or silver by treating ore with a cyanide solution

Etymology

From 'cyanide', after the chemical compounds used in the process.
