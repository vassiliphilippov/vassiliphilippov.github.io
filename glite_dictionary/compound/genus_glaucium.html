genus Glaucium

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˈɡlɔːsiəm / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈɡlɔːsɪəm / (BrE)
a genus of herbaceous plants known as horned poppies, native to Europe, North Africa, and Asia
genus of horned poppy plants

Examples

The genus Glaucium includes several species of horned poppies with distinctive seed pods.
Botanists studied the medicinal properties of plants within the genus Glaucium.
The yellow flowers of the genus Glaucium thrive in sandy coastal environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horned poppy - a common name for species in the genus Glaucium

Etymology

From Latin 'Glaucium', the botanical name for the horned poppy, ultimately from Greek 'glaukion' meaning 'juice of the poppy'.
