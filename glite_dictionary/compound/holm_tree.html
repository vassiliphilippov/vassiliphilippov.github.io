holm tree

1. NOUN

/ hoʊm tri / (AmE), / həʊm triː / (BrE)
an evergreen oak tree (Quercus ilex) of southern Europe with holly-like leaves and hard wood
evergreen southern European oak tree

Examples

The ancient holm tree stood at the edge of the Mediterranean forest, its dark leaves gleaming in the sun.
Carpenters valued wood from the holm tree for its exceptional hardness and durability.
She mistook the spiny leaves of the holm tree for those of a holly bush.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: holm oak - evergreen oak tree of southern Europe (Quercus ilex)

Etymology

From Old English *holm* (holly, evergreen tree).
