fan tracery

1. NOUN

/ fæn ˈtreɪsəri / (AmE), / fæn ˈtreɪsəri / (BrE)
the ornamental stonework forming patterns on the surface of fan vaulting, typical in Gothic architecture
ornamental stonework in fan vaulting

Examples

The fan tracery in the cathedral ceiling is admired for its intricate design.
Victorian architects revived fan tracery as a nod to medieval craftsmanship.
You can see beautiful examples of fan tracery in many English Gothic cathedrals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tracery - ornamental stone openwork, typically in the upper part of a Gothic window

Etymology

From 'fan' (due to the radiating pattern) and 'tracery' (from Old French 'tracerie').
