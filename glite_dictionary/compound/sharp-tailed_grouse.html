sharp-tailed grouse

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃɑːrp teɪld ɡraʊs / (AmE), / ˈʃɑːp teɪld ɡraʊs / (BrE)
a large species of grouse found in prairies and open forests of western North America, notable for its pointed tail feathers
North American prairie grouse

Examples

The sharp-tailed grouse is known for its elaborate courtship dances in the spring.
We spotted a flock of sharp-tailed grouse while hiking through the grasslands.
Conservation efforts are underway to protect the prairie habitat of the sharp-tailed grouse.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grouse - a bird of the pheasant family, found in temperate and subarctic regions
