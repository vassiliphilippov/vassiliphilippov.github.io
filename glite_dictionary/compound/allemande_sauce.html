allemande sauce

1. NOUN

/ ˌæləˈmɑnd sɔs / (AmE), / ˌaləˈmɑːnd sɔːs / (BrE)
A rich white sauce made from velouté thickened with egg yolks and cream, often flavored with lemon juice.
egg-thickened white sauce

Examples

The chef finished the dish by spooning a delicate allemande sauce over the grilled chicken.
Mastering allemande sauce is essential for students in French culinary schools.
They enriched the vegetables with a velvety allemande sauce flavored with lemon.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: velouté sauce - basic white sauce made from butter, flour, and stock
  • Synonym: béchamel sauce - classic white sauce made with butter, flour, and milk

Etymology

From French 'sauce allemande' meaning 'German sauce'.
