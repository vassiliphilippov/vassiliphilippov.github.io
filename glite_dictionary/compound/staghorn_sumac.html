staghorn sumac

1. NOUN

/ ˈstæɡˌhɔrn ˈsuːˌmæk / (AmE), / ˈstæɡˌhɔːn ˈsjuːmək / (BrE)
a North American deciduous shrub or small tree (Rhus typhina) with compound leaves that turn bright red in autumn, dense clusters of greenish-yellow flowers, and crimson, acidic berries
a North American shrub or tree with red autumn leaves and red berries

Examples

The brilliant red foliage of the staghorn sumac is a highlight of the fall landscape.
Birds are attracted to the bright red berries of the staghorn sumac during winter.
A row of staghorn sumac shrubs lines the edge of the park, providing vibrant color in autumn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sumac - any plant of the genus Rhus, especially those with red berries

Etymology

From the resemblance of its hairy branches to the antlers (horns) of a stag; 'sumac' from Old French, ultimately from Arabic 'summāq'.
