Arapahoe language

1. NOUN

/ ˌær.əˈhoʊ ˈlæŋ.ɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌær.əˈhəʊ ˈlaŋ.ɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Native American Algonquian language spoken by the Arapaho people, primarily in the United States.
Algonquian language of the Arapaho people

Examples

He is studying the Arapahoe language to help preserve his heritage.
Few people today are fluent speakers of the Arapahoe language.
The university offers a course in the Arapahoe language and culture.

Etymology

From the name 'Arapaho', a Native American tribe of the Plains, combined with English 'language'.
