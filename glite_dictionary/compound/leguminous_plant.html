leguminous plant

1. NOUN

/ lɪˈɡjuːmɪnəs plænt / (AmE), / lɪˈɡjuːmɪnəs plɑːnt / (BrE)
A plant belonging to the family Leguminosae (Fabaceae), such as beans, peas, or clover, which typically bear pods containing seeds.
plant of the legume family (beans, peas, etc.)

Examples

The farmer decided to rotate his crops by planting a leguminous plant to enrich the soil with nitrogen.
Clover is a common leguminous plant used to improve pasture lands.
Many leguminous plants have root nodules that fix atmospheric nitrogen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: legume - a plant or its fruit or seed in the family Leguminosae

Etymology

From Latin 'legumen' (pulse, bean) + '-ous' (having the quality of), forming 'leguminous'.
