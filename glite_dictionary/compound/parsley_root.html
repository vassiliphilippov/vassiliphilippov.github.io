parsley root

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑr.sli ˌruːt / (AmE), / ˈpɑː.sli ˌruːt / (BrE)
The edible root of a variety of parsley, used as a root vegetable in cooking, especially in Central and Eastern European dishes.
the edible root of a type of parsley

Examples

The chef added parsley root to the soup for extra flavor.
You can eat parsley root raw, much like a carrot.
In Eastern European cooking, parsley root is a common ingredient in stews and broths.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parsnip - root vegetable similar in appearance to parsley root
