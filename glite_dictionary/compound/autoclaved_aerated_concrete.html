autoclaved aerated concrete

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔtoʊˌkleɪvd ˈɛreɪtɪd kɑnˈkrit / (AmE), / ˈɔːtəˌkleɪvd ˈeəreɪtɪd ˈkɒnkriːt / (BrE)
a lightweight, precast building material made from concrete that is cured with steam under pressure, providing structural strength, thermal insulation, and resistance to fire and mold
lightweight, steam-cured concrete building material

Examples

The new housing project uses autoclaved aerated concrete blocks to improve energy efficiency and speed up construction.
Because autoclaved aerated concrete is lightweight, it reduces the load on the building's foundation.
Contractors often choose autoclaved aerated concrete for walls where both insulation and fire resistance are priorities.

Synset Examples

Better thermal efficiency of AAC makes it suitable for use in areas with extreme temperature as it eliminates need for separate materials for construction and insulation leading to faster construction and savings.

Etymology

From 'autoclave' (equipment for high-pressure steam treatment), 'aerated' (having air introduced), and 'concrete'.
