organic compound

1. NOUN

/ ɔːrˈɡænɪk kəmˈpaʊnd / (AmE), / ɔːˈɡanɪk kəmˈpaʊnd / (BrE)
a chemical compound containing carbon, typically bonded to hydrogen and may include other elements such as oxygen, nitrogen, or sulfur
chemical compound with carbon; often in living things
medical

Examples

Benzene is an example of an organic compound frequently used in industrial chemistry.
The study of organic compounds is the main focus of organic chemistry.
Many medicines are synthesized from organic compounds derived from plants.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inorganic compound - a compound that does not contain carbon-hydrogen bonds, opposite of organic compound

Etymology

From 'organic', relating to organisms, and 'compound', from Latin 'componere' (to put together).
