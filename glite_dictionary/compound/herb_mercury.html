herb mercury

1. NOUN

/ hɜrb ˈmɜrkəri / (AmE), / hɜːb ˈmɜːkjʊri / (BrE)
A Eurasian annual plant (Mercurialis annua), often considered a weed, formerly used as a purgative, diuretic, or to treat syphilis.
Eurasian weed, once medicinal

Examples

Early herbalists used herb mercury to prepare remedies for digestive problems.
Herb mercury, once valued for its medicinal properties, is now seen mainly as a garden weed.
The leaves of herb mercury were historically dried and administered as a diuretic.

Etymology

From Latin 'herba' (plant, herb) and 'Mercurialis' (the genus name, from Mercury, the Roman god).

2. NOUN

/ hɜrb ˈmɜrkjəri / (AmE), / hɜːb ˈmɜːkjʊəri / (BrE)
A small annual plant (Mercurialis annua), native to Europe and Africa, naturalized as a weed in America, formerly used in herbal medicine as a purgative, diuretic, or treatment for syphilis.
a weedy medicinal plant (Mercurialis annua)

Examples

Herb mercury is often found growing in cultivated fields and gardens as a weed.
In traditional medicine, herb mercury was used for its mild purgative effects.
Botanists observed that herb mercury spreads quickly in disturbed soils.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mercury - the genus of plants known as Mercurialis

Etymology

herb from Latin herba (grass, herb) + mercury, the plant's genus name (Mercurialis), said to be named for the god Mercury.
