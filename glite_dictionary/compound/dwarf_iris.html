dwarf iris

1. NOUN

/ dwɔrf ˈaɪɹɪs / (AmE), / dwɔːf ˈaɪrɪs / (BrE)
A low-growing species of iris native to North America, noted for its bright blue-lilac spring flowers.
a small iris with blue-lilac flowers

Examples

The dwarf iris adds a splash of color to the garden each spring.
Botanists admire the resilience of dwarf iris in rocky soil.
She planted dwarf iris near the front walkway for early-season blooms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: iris - a genus of flowering plants with showy flowers

2. NOUN

/ dwɔrf ˈaɪrɪs / (AmE), / dwɔːf ˈaɪrɪs / (BrE)
A low-growing summer-flowering iris (Iris verna) native to the northeastern United States, valued for its small size and colorful blooms.
low, summer-flowering iris of NE United States

Examples

The dwarf iris adds a splash of purple to shady garden corners in spring.
She planted several dwarf irises along the edge of the walkway for early color.
Unlike taller varieties, the dwarf iris thrives in rock gardens and borders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: iris - plant genus with colorful flowers
