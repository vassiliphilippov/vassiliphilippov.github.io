Bowman's capsule

1. NOUN

/ ˈboʊ.mənz ˈkæp.səl / (AmE), / ˈbəʊ.mənz ˈkæp.sjuːl / (BrE)
A thin, double-walled sac that surrounds the glomerulus of a nephron in the kidney and is involved in the filtration of blood to form urine.
structure in kidney nephron for filtration
medical

Examples

Damage to the Bowman's capsule can impair the kidney's ability to filter blood properly.
Under a microscope, you can clearly see the Bowman's capsule surrounding the glomerulus.
The initial step in urine formation takes place within the Bowman's capsule.

Etymology

Named after Sir William Bowman (1816–1892), English surgeon and anatomist, who first described it.
