water level

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔtər ˌlɛvəl / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə ˌlɛvəl / (BrE)
a line marking the height of the water against the hull of a vessel when it is afloat on an even keel, often painted to indicate loading limits
mark showing height of water on a vessel

Examples

The ship's water level was carefully checked before departure to ensure a safe load.
Overloading a vessel can cause the water level to rise dangerously above the painted mark.
Inspectors noted that the water level was exactly at the limit allowed for cargo.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɔtɚ ˌlɛvəl / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə ˌlɛvəl / (BrE)
the underground surface below which the ground is completely saturated with water; also known as the water table
level below ground fully saturated with water

Examples

Due to the heavy rainfall, the water level has risen significantly in recent weeks.
Farmers monitor the water level to predict potential flooding in crops.
Drought conditions have caused the water level to drop below normal.

Synset Examples

spring rains had raised the water table

Related Terms

  • Synonym: water table - the upper level of groundwater; underground surface fully saturated with water

3. NOUN

/ ˈwɔtɚ ˌlɛvəl / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə ˌlɛvəl / (BrE)
an instrument that indicates the surface level of water, usually by displaying it in a transparent tube or trough
device showing water surface level

Examples

The technician checked the reservoir's water level to ensure it was not falling below the safety mark.
A simple water level can be made at home using a clear tube and some water.
Using the water level, the builder confirmed the foundation was perfectly horizontal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spirit level - a tool for determining if a surface is horizontal or vertical

4. NOUN

/ ˈwɔtɚ ˌlɛvəl / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə ˌlɛvəl / (BrE)
the height of the surface of water in a lake, river, reservoir, or other body of water, usually relative to a fixed reference point
height of the surface of water

Examples

After the storm, the water level in the river rose dramatically and flooded nearby fields.
Engineers constantly monitor the water level in the reservoir to prevent shortages.
When the water level dropped, old tree stumps became visible along the lake shore.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sea level - the average height of the sea's surface, used as a standard for measuring land elevation and ocean depths
