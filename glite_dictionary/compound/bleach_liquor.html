bleach liquor

1. NOUN

/ bliːtʃ ˈlɪkər / (AmE), / bliːtʃ ˈlɪkə / (BrE)
a solution containing bleaching agents, used in the bleaching of textiles or paper pulp
solution used for bleaching

Examples

The cotton fabric was treated with bleach liquor to achieve a brighter white color.
Proper handling of bleach liquor is essential to ensure worker safety in the paper mill.
After soaking in bleach liquor, the textile fibers became much lighter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bleach - a chemical used to whiten or sterilize materials
