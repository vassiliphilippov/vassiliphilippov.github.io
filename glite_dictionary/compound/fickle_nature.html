fickle nature

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪkəl ˈneɪtʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈfɪkəl ˈneɪtʃə / (BrE)
an inconsistent or changeable character or disposition, especially in terms of loyalty, opinions, or feelings
inconstant or changeable character

Examples

Because of her fickle nature, she often changed her mind about important decisions.
The company's fickle nature made investors wary of committing their funds.
His fickle nature meant that friendships rarely lasted long.

Synset Examples

Her fickle nature makes it hard to trust her decisions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inconstancy - lack of steadiness or stability in behavior or feelings
  • Antonym: steadfastness - firmness and constancy in action or belief

Etymology

From Middle English *fikel* (deceitful) and Latin *natura* (nature, inherent quality).
