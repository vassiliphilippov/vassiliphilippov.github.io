League of Nations

1. PROPN

/ liɡ əv ˈneɪʃənz / (AmE), / liːɡ əv ˈneɪʃənz / (BrE)
an international organization established in 1920 to promote cooperation and peace among countries, dissolved in 1946 and replaced by the United Nations
international organization for world peace (1920–1946)

Examples

The League of Nations was the first international body created to prevent future wars after World War I.
Despite its efforts, the League of Nations failed to stop the aggression that led to World War II.
Membership in the League of Nations did not include the United States, which weakened its influence.
