acne rosacea

1. NOUN

/ ˈækni roʊˈzeɪʃə / (AmE), / ˈækni rəʊˈzeɪsɪə / (BrE)
a chronic skin disorder of the face, especially in adults, marked by redness, flushing, and sometimes pimples due to enlarged blood vessels
chronic facial skin disorder causing redness
medical

Examples

The dermatologist diagnosed her with acne rosacea after examining the persistent redness on her cheeks.
People with acne rosacea are often advised to avoid triggers like spicy foods or alcohol.
Effective treatments for acne rosacea may include topical creams and lifestyle changes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rosacea - chronic skin condition causing facial redness and pimples

Etymology

From Latin 'acne' (skin eruption) + 'rosacea', from Latin 'rosaceus', meaning 'rose-colored'.
