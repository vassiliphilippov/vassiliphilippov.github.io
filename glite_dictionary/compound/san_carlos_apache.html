San Carlos Apache

1. NOUN

/ sæn ˈkɑrloʊs əˈpætʃi / (AmE), / san ˈkɑːlɒs əˈpatʃi / (BrE)
A variety of the Apache language spoken in east-central Arizona, primarily on several Native American reservations.
Apache language spoken in Arizona

Examples

Linguists have documented the unique features of San Carlos Apache compared to other Apachean languages.
He grew up hearing San Carlos Apache spoken at home on the reservation.
Efforts are underway to preserve and teach San Carlos Apache to new generations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Western Apache - Another major Apachean language spoken in Arizona
  • Degree Higher: Apache - Group of related Native American peoples and their languages
  • Synonym: Chiricahua - A closely related Apache language

Etymology

San Carlos from Spanish (meaning 'Saint Charles'), Apache from Zuni (as an exonym).
