spherical polygon

1. NOUN

/ ˈsfɛrɪkəl pɑːˈliːɡɑːn / (AmE), / ˈsfɪərɪkəl ˈpɒlɪɡən / (BrE)
A figure on the surface of a sphere that is bounded by arcs of three or more great circles.
polygon on a sphere's surface

Examples

The path of the spacecraft described a spherical polygon on the surface of the planet.
In advanced geometry, students learn how to calculate the area of a spherical polygon.
The boundaries of the nations could be represented as spherical polygons on a globe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polygon - a flat shape with three or more straight sides

Etymology

Formed from 'spherical' (relating to a sphere) and 'polygon' (from Greek 'poly-' many + 'gōnia' angle).
