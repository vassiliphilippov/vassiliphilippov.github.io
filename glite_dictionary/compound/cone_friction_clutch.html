cone friction clutch

1. NOUN

/ koʊn ˈfrɪkʃən klʌtʃ / (AmE), / kəʊn ˈfrɪkʃən klʌtʃ / (BrE)
a type of friction clutch in which the mating frictional surfaces have a conical shape, used to transmit rotational motion between shafts
a friction clutch with cone-shaped surfaces

Examples

A cone friction clutch provides better torque transmission due to its larger friction surface area.
Maintenance of a cone friction clutch involves regular lubrication of moving parts.
Many early automobiles were equipped with a cone friction clutch rather than a plate clutch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: friction clutch - a clutch that transmits torque via friction between its surfaces
