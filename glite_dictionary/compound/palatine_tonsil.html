palatine tonsil

1. NOUN

/ ˈpæləˌtaɪn ˈtɑnsəl / (AmE), / ˈpæləˌtʌɪn ˈtɒnsɪl / (BrE)
either of two masses of lymphatic tissue located on each side of the oral pharynx, commonly referred to as the tonsils
tissue masses at sides of oral pharynx (tonsils)
medical

Examples

The doctor examined her palatine tonsil for signs of infection during the checkup.
Swelling of the palatine tonsil can cause difficulty in swallowing.
The surgeon removed the inflamed palatine tonsil to relieve the patient's symptoms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tonsil - either of a pair of oval masses of lymphoid tissue on either side of the throat

Etymology

From Latin 'palatinus' (of the palate) and 'tonsilla' (a tonsil).
