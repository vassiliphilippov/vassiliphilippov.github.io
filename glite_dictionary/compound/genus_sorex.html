genus Sorex

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈsɔː.rɛks / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈsɔː.rɛks / (BrE)
the type genus of the family Soricidae, comprising the common shrews
type genus of shrews (Soricidae)

Examples

The genus Sorex includes many species of small, insectivorous mammals called shrews.
Researchers discovered a new species belonging to the genus Sorex in the remote mountain region.
Members of the genus Sorex are often studied for their high metabolic rates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sorex - the genus of shrews in the family Soricidae

Etymology

From Latin 'genus' (kind) and 'Sorex' (shrew).
