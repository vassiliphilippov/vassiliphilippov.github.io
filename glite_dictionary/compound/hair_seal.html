hair seal

1. NOUN

/ hɛr sil / (AmE), / heə siːl / (BrE)
A type of seal that lacks external ear flaps, has a stiff hair-like coat, and hind limbs adapted as swimming flippers.
seal without external ear flaps

Examples

The hair seal is easily recognized by its sleek, stiff coat and lack of external ear flaps.
Scientists observed a group of hair seals basking on the icy shore.
Unlike the fur seal, the hair seal does not have visible ears.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: earless seal - seal lacking external ear flaps
  • Antonym: fur seal - seal with external ears and thick fur
