family Eschrichtiidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ɛˌskraɪktɪˌaɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli ˌɛskrɪktɪˈaɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of baleen whales consisting solely of the grey whale (Eschrichtius robustus)
family of whales including only the grey whale

Examples

The family Eschrichtiidae contains only one living species, the grey whale.
Scientists debated the evolutionary relationships of family Eschrichtiidae with other baleen whales.
Research on migration patterns focuses on the unique characteristics of family Eschrichtiidae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grey whale - a large baleen whale, the only living member of its family

Etymology

From New Latin Eschrichtiidae, named after Danish zoologist Daniel Eschricht, with the suffix -idae used for animal family names.
