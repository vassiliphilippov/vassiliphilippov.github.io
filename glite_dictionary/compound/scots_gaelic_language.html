Scots Gaelic language

1. NOUN

/ skɑts ˈɡeɪlɪk ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / skɒts ˈɡeɪlɪk ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Celtic language spoken in Scotland, closely related to Irish and Manx.
the Gaelic language of Scotland

Examples

Many people in the Highlands still speak Scots Gaelic language as their first language.
He took evening classes to learn the basics of the Scots Gaelic language.
The Scots Gaelic language is taught in some schools across Scotland.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Gaelic - the group of closely related Celtic languages of Ireland, Scotland, and the Isle of Man
  • Synonym: Irish Gaelic - the Gaelic language spoken in Ireland
  • Synonym: Manx - the Gaelic language of the Isle of Man

Etymology

Scots, referring to Scotland, and Gaelic, from Irish 'Gaeilge', meaning Irish language.
