long-burning log

1. NOUN

/ lɔŋ ˈbɝnɪŋ lɑɡ / (AmE), / lɒŋ ˈbɜːnɪŋ lɒɡ / (BrE)
a log designed or selected to burn for a prolonged period, often used to provide sustained heat in fireplaces or wood stoves
a log that burns for a long time

Examples

On cold winter nights, a long-burning log keeps the living room warm for hours.
We bought a long-burning log for the fireplace to last through the evening.
The camping store recommends bringing at least one long-burning log for overnight trips.

Synset Examples

He prefers long-burning logs for his fireplace.
