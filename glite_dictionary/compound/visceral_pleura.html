visceral pleura

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪsərəl pluˈrə / (AmE), / ˈvɪsərəl ˈplɪərə / (BrE)
the thin membrane that closely covers the surface of the lungs
membrane covering the lungs
medical

Examples

The visceral pleura is essential for protecting the delicate tissues of the lungs.
Damage to the visceral pleura can cause serious respiratory problems.
During surgery, the visceral pleura must be handled with care to prevent complications.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: parietal pleura - membrane lining the inner surface of the chest wall

Etymology

From Latin 'viscera' (internal organs) and 'pleura' (side, rib, membrane).
