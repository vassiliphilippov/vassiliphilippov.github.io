Magna Carta

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæɡ.nə ˈkɑr.tə / (AmE), / ˌmæɡ.nə ˈkɑː.tə / (BrE)
an important royal charter of liberties granted by King John of England in 1215, which limited the monarchy's power and established certain legal rights
historic English royal charter (1215)

Examples

The Magna Carta is often seen as the foundation of constitutional government in England.
Legal scholars frequently reference the Magna Carta when discussing individual rights and the rule of law.
During the ceremony, a replica of the Magna Carta was displayed to honor its historical significance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: charter - a formal document granting rights or privileges
  • Synonym: constitution - a set of fundamental principles or established precedents

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'Magna Carta' meaning 'Great Charter'.
