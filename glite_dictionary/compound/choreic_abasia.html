choreic abasia

1. NOUN

/ kɔˈriɪk əˈbeɪziə / (AmE), / kɒˈriːɪk əˈbeɪzɪə / (BrE)
A type of abasia characterized by an inability to walk due to involuntary, dance-like movements of the legs.
inability to walk from involuntary leg movements
medical

Examples

The neurologist diagnosed the patient with choreic abasia after observing her involuntary leg movements.
Symptoms of choreic abasia can severely impact a person's mobility and daily life.
Unlike other movement disorders, choreic abasia is specifically linked to choreiform leg movements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abasia - inability to walk due to impaired coordination

Etymology

From Greek 'choreia' (dance) and 'abasia' (inability to walk).
