sweet scabious

1. NOUN

/ swit skæbiəs / (AmE), / swiːt ˈskæbɪəs / (BrE)
an Old World annual plant (Scabiosa atropurpurea) with fragrant purple to deep crimson flower heads, often grown ornamentally and naturalized in parts of the United States
fragrant annual garden plant with purple or crimson flowers

Examples

The sweet scabious adds vibrant color and a lovely scent to summer flower beds.
Gardeners value sweet scabious for its long-lasting blooms and ease of cultivation.
I planted sweet scabious along the border to attract butterflies to my garden.

Etymology

From 'sweet' (pleasant-smelling) and 'scabious', from Latin scabiosus, a plant name.
