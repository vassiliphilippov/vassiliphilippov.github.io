spotted bonytongue

1. NOUN

/ ˈspɑtɪd ˈboʊniˌtʌŋ / (AmE), / ˈspɒtɪd ˈbəʊnɪˌtʌŋ / (BrE)
a large species of freshwater fish native to Australia, noted for the red or pink spot on each of its scales (Scleropages leichardti)
Australian freshwater fish with spotted scales

Examples

The spotted bonytongue is highly sought after by aquarium enthusiasts for its distinctive beauty.
Researchers discovered several young spotted bonytongues in a remote river in northern Australia.
A full-grown spotted bonytongue can reach more than 90 centimeters in length.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bonytongue - a family of primitive freshwater fish with bony tongues
