spherical cow

1. NOUN

/ ˈsfɪrɪkəl kaʊ / (AmE), / ˈsfɪərɪkəl kaʊ / (BrE)
an intentionally over-simplified model used, often humorously, to explain or analyze complex problems, especially in scientific contexts
humorous over-simplified scientific model

Examples

In introductory physics, the concept of a spherical cow is used to make calculations manageable.
The professor warned against relying on spherical cow assumptions in real-world engineering.
They joked that their business model was just a spherical cow version of reality.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

Etymology

Popularized among physicists as a joke about oversimplification of real-world problems.
