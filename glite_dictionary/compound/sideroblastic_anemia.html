sideroblastic anemia

1. NOUN

/ ˌsaɪdɚˈblæstɪk əˈniːmiə / (AmE), / ˌsʌɪdəˈblastɪk əˈniːmɪə / (BrE)
A rare type of anemia in which the bone marrow produces ringed sideroblasts instead of healthy red blood cells, leading to reduced oxygen transport in the blood.
anemia with ringed sideroblasts in the bone marrow
medical

Examples

A diagnosis of sideroblastic anemia was made after reviewing the bone marrow biopsy.
Patients with sideroblastic anemia often require regular blood transfusions to manage their condition.
The presence of ringed sideroblasts is a hallmark of sideroblastic anemia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anemia - a condition marked by a deficiency of red blood cells

Etymology

From Greek 'sideros' (iron) + 'blastos' (germ, sprout), referring to iron-laden cells; 'anemia' from Greek 'anaimia' (lack of blood).
