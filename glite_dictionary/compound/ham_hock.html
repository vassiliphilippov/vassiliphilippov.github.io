ham hock

1. NOUN

/ hæm hɑk / (AmE), / hæm hɒk / (BrE)
a cut of pork taken from the lower part of a pig's leg, just above the foot, usually used to flavor soups, stews, and other dishes
cut of pork from the lower leg

Examples

The soup was simmered for hours with a ham hock to add a smoky flavor.
She bought a ham hock at the butcher to use in her bean stew.
A hearty dish of greens is often cooked with a ham hock in Southern cuisine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ham shank - a similar cut from the upper leg of the pig
