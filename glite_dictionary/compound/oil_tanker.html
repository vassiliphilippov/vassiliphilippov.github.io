oil tanker

1. NOUN

/ ɔɪl ˈtæŋkɚ / (AmE), / ɔɪl ˈtaŋkə / (BrE)
A large ship designed for transporting crude oil or petroleum products in bulk.
ship for carrying crude oil

Examples

The massive oil tanker docked at the port to unload its cargo.
After the oil tanker accident, a large oil spill polluted the coastline.
An oil tanker can carry millions of barrels of crude oil across oceans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tanker - a ship designed to carry liquids in bulk
  • Antonym: container ship - a cargo ship designed to carry containerized cargo
