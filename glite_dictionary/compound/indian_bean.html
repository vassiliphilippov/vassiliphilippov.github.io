Indian bean

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪndiən ˌbin / (AmE), / ˈɪndiən ˌbiːn / (BrE)
a tree of the genus Catalpa with large heart-shaped leaves, white flowers, and long slender seed pods
tree (Catalpa) with long seed pods

Examples

The Indian bean blossoms in late spring with clusters of fragrant white flowers.
Gardeners appreciate the shade provided by the broad leaves of the Indian bean.
The long seed pods hanging from the Indian bean tree are a distinctive feature in autumn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catalpa - a genus of trees with large leaves and long seed pods

2. NOUN

/ ˈɪndiən biːn / (AmE), / ˈɪndiən biːn / (BrE)
a perennial climbing vine (Lablab purpureus) of the Old World tropics with purple flowers and maroon pods containing edible seeds, grown as an ornamental and food plant, especially in South Asia
climbing vine with edible beans, grown in South Asia

Examples

The Indian bean is often grown in gardens for its beautiful purple flowers and edible pods.
Farmers in India cultivate Indian beans for both local consumption and export.
She added fresh Indian beans to the curry, giving it a distinctive flavor and texture.
