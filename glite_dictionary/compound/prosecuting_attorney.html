prosecuting attorney

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɑsɪˌkjuːtɪŋ əˈtɝːni / (AmE), / ˈprɒsɪˌkjuːtɪŋ əˈtɜːni / (BrE)
An official who conducts criminal prosecutions on behalf of the government or the state.
official who prosecutes criminal cases for the government
legal american

Examples

The prosecuting attorney presented new evidence during the trial.
After reviewing the case file, the prosecuting attorney decided to pursue additional charges.
The prosecuting attorney questioned the witness about his involvement in the incident.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: district attorney - chief prosecuting officer of a local government area, especially in the US
  • Antonym: defense attorney - lawyer representing the accused in court
