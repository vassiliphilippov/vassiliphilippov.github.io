floating policy

1. NOUN

/ ˈfloʊ.tɪŋ ˈpɑl.ɪ.si / (AmE), / ˈfləʊ.tɪŋ ˈpɒl.ɪ.si / (BrE)
an insurance policy that covers loss or damage to movable property, such as jewelry, regardless of its location
insurance covering movable property anywhere

Examples

She took out a floating policy to insure her valuable antiques no matter where they were kept.
A floating policy is ideal for travelers who own expensive jewelry or equipment.
Unlike a standard fire insurance policy, a floating policy protects items wherever they are located.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: specific policy - insurance covering particular property at a designated location

Etymology

From 'floating', meaning not fixed in one place, and 'policy', in the sense of an insurance contract.
