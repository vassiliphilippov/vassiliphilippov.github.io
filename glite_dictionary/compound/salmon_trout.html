salmon trout

1. NOUN

/ ˈsæmən ˌtraʊt / (AmE), / ˈsæmən ˌtraʊt / (BrE)
a large fork-tailed trout found in lakes of Canada and the northern United States
a large North American lake trout

Examples

The salmon trout is a prized catch among anglers in the northern lakes.
We prepared a delicious dinner using a freshly caught salmon trout.
Many wildlife documentaries feature the migration of the salmon trout during spawning season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lake trout - a large freshwater trout found in lakes in northern North America

2. NOUN

/ ˈsæmən traʊt / (AmE), / ˈsamən traʊt / (BrE)
The flesh of a marine trout that migrates from saltwater to freshwater, valued as food.
flesh of migratory trout used as food

Examples

Grilled salmon trout pairs perfectly with lemon and dill for a light summer meal.
The chef recommended the smoked salmon trout as an appetizer.
We enjoyed fresh salmon trout caught from the river that morning.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: trout - flesh of various freshwater and anadromous fish of the family Salmonidae
  • Degree Higher: salmon - flesh of large anadromous fish valued as food

3. NOUN

/ ˈsælmən traʊt / (AmE), / ˈsælmən traʊt / (BrE)
A type of speckled trout native to European rivers, sometimes introduced to North America, known for its resemblance to both salmon and trout.
a speckled trout native to European rivers

Examples

The river is famous for its abundant salmon trout during the spring season.
Anglers travel from afar hoping to catch a prized salmon trout in these waters.
Salmon trout have been introduced to several lakes outside their native range.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trout - a common freshwater fish, typically with speckled body
  • Synonym: salmon - a large fish known for its migration upriver to spawn
