reserve fund

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈzɜrv fʌnd / (AmE), / rɪˈzɜːv fʌnd / (BrE)
an amount of money set aside out of profits or income to cover future expenses, liabilities, or emergencies
money set aside for future expenses

Examples

The building association maintains a reserve fund for major repairs and unexpected expenses.
Prudent companies regularly contribute to a reserve fund to ensure long-term financial stability.
The school used its reserve fund to cover the cost of emergency renovations after the storm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contingency fund - money reserved to cover possible future emergencies or unexpected costs
  • Antonym: operating fund - money used for daily expenses of an organization
