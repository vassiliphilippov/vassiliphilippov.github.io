trustee account

1. NOUN

/ trʌsˈti əˌkaʊnt / (AmE), / trʌsˈti əˌkaʊnt / (BrE)
A savings account managed by a person acting as trustee for a beneficiary, with control during the trustee's lifetime and the balance payable to the beneficiary upon the trustee's death.
savings account held by a trustee for a beneficiary
legal

Examples

Mary opened a trustee account for her grandson, planning to transfer the funds to him when he turned eighteen.
The trustee account gives parents control of the money until their child becomes an adult.
Upon the death of the account holder, the remaining balance in the trustee account was paid to the named beneficiary.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trust account - an account where funds are held by one party for the benefit of another
