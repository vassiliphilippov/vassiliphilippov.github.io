Spanish needles

1. NOUN

/ ˈspænɪʃ ˈniːdəlz / (AmE), / ˈspanɪʃ ˈniːdəlz / (BrE)
the barbed seeds of bur marigold plants, especially of the genus Bidens, known for attaching to clothing or animal fur
seed of bur marigolds

Examples

The Spanish needles stuck to my socks after our hike through the field.
Many animals help disperse Spanish needles by carrying them in their fur.
Gardeners often find Spanish needles clinging to their clothes after weeding.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bur marigold - a plant of the genus Bidens, producing barbed seeds

2. NOUN

/ ˈspænɪʃ ˈniːdəlz / (AmE), / ˈspænɪʃ ˈniːdlz / (BrE)
a common bur marigold of the eastern United States, known for its slender, needle-like seeds that attach to clothing and animals
common bur marigold (plant, eastern US)

Examples

In late summer, Spanish needles can cover fields with their bright yellow flowers.
Hikers often find Spanish needles stuck to their socks after a walk through the woods.
The meadow was thick with Spanish needles, attracting bees and butterflies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bur marigold - another name for plants of the genus Bidens

Etymology

The name refers to the slender seeds resembling needles; 'Spanish' is a traditional modifier with unclear origin in this context.
