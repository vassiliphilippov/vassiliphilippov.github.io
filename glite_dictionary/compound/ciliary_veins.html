ciliary veins

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪliˌɛri veɪnz / (AmE), / ˈsɪliəri veɪnz / (BrE)
the small veins that drain blood from the ciliary body of the eye
veins draining the ciliary body of the eye
medical

Examples

The ophthalmologist examined the ciliary veins to assess blood flow in the patient's eye.
Obstruction of the ciliary veins can lead to increased intraocular pressure.
During the surgery, careful attention was given to avoid damaging the ciliary veins.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ciliary body - part of the eye that includes the ciliary muscle and processes

Etymology

From 'ciliary', relating to the ciliary body of the eye, and 'vein', from Latin 'vena' (blood vessel).
