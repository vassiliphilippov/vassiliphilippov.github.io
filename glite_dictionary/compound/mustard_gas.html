mustard gas

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌstərd ˌɡæs / (AmE), / ˈmʌstəd ˌɡas / (BrE)
a toxic chemical warfare agent that causes severe blistering of the skin, eyes, and lungs, with no known antidote
toxic chemical warfare agent

Examples

During World War I, soldiers feared the effects of mustard gas on the battlefield.
Exposure to mustard gas can cause severe lung damage and painful skin blisters.
International treaties now ban the use of mustard gas in warfare due to its devastating consequences.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sulfur mustard - another name for mustard gas, highlighting its chemical composition

Etymology

Named for its faint mustard-like smell; 'gas' from its use as a chemical weapon, not its strict physical state.
