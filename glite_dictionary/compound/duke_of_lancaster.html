Duke of Lancaster

1. PROPN

/ duːk əv ˈlæŋkəstər / (AmE), / djuːk əv ˈlæŋkəstə / (BrE)
a title held by John of Gaunt (1340–1399), the fourth son of Edward III and influential ruler during the late reign of Edward III and the minority of Richard II; father of Henry Bolingbroke, later Henry IV
historical English noble title; John of Gaunt

Examples

The Duke of Lancaster played a crucial role in English politics during the 14th century.
Historians often debate the legacy of the Duke of Lancaster in shaping the future monarchy.
As Duke of Lancaster, John of Gaunt wielded immense power both at court and on the battlefield.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: John of Gaunt - fourth son of Edward III, known as Duke of Lancaster
  • Degree Higher: Henry Bolingbroke - son of John of Gaunt, later Henry IV of England

Etymology

Lancaster from the city in northwestern England; 'Duke' from Latin dux (leader).
