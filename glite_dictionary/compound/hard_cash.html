hard cash

1. NOUN

/ hɑrd kæʃ / (AmE), / hɑːd kæʃ / (BrE)
money in the form of physical bills and coins, rather than checks or electronic payments
physical money; bills and coins

Examples

Paying with hard cash often gets you a better deal at markets.
The charity is in urgent need of hard cash to continue its work.
Some businesses only accept hard cash and do not have card machines.

Synset Examples

there is a desperate shortage of hard cash

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cash - money in coins or notes, as distinct from checks, money orders, or credit
