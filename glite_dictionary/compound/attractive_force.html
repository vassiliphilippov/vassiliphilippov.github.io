attractive force

1. NOUN

/ əˈtræktɪv fɔrs / (AmE), / əˈtræktɪv fɔːs / (BrE)
a force that causes one object to be drawn toward another; the tendency of objects to move closer together due to physical interaction
force that causes attraction

Examples

Gravity is an attractive force that pulls objects toward the center of the Earth.
The attractive force between two magnets can be surprisingly strong.
Electrostatic attraction occurs when an attractive force acts between opposite charges.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: repulsive force - force that causes objects to move away from each other
