coronary failure

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔːrəˌneri ˈfeɪljɚ / (AmE), / ˈkɒr.ən.əri ˈfeɪljə / (BrE)
A condition in which the heart is unable to pump enough blood to maintain normal bodily functions.
heart unable to pump enough blood
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with coronary failure after showing symptoms of severe fatigue and fluid retention.
Advanced coronary failure may require medication, lifestyle changes, or surgical intervention.
Doctors monitor patients with coronary failure closely to prevent complications and improve their quality of life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heart failure - inability of the heart to pump sufficient blood

Etymology

From 'coronary', relating to the arteries supplying the heart, and 'failure' meaning a lack of proper function.
