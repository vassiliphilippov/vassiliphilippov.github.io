parliamentary law

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑːrləˈmɛn.tɛri lɔ / (AmE), / ˌpɑː.lɪˈmɛn.tᵻ.ri lɔː / (BrE)
the body of rules and procedures that govern the conduct of meetings and decision-making in assemblies or legislative bodies
rules governing conduct of assemblies or meetings

Examples

The committee relied on parliamentary law to resolve disputes during their meetings.
Understanding parliamentary law is essential for anyone who wants to chair an official assembly.
They amended their organization's rules to conform more closely to established parliamentary law.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parliamentary procedure - the set of rules for conducting meetings

Etymology

parliamentary from 'parliament' (from Anglo-Norman 'parlement', assembly) + law.
