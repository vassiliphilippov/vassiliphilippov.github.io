macadamia nut

1. NOUN

/ məkəˈdeɪmiə nʌt / (AmE), / məkəˈdeɪmɪə nʌt / (BrE)
The edible, round seed of the macadamia tree, with a sweet, crisp white interior, often used in cooking and baking.
edible seed of the macadamia tree

Examples

She sprinkled chopped macadamia nuts on top of the cookies for extra crunch.
The creamy texture of a macadamia nut makes it popular in desserts and salads.
We bought a bag of salted macadamia nuts at the gourmet store.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nut - a hard-shelled fruit or seed

Etymology

From genus name Macadamia, named after Scottish-Australian chemist John Macadam (1827–1865).

2. NOUN

/ məˌkædəˈmiːə nʌt / (AmE), / məˌkadəˈmiːə nʌt / (BrE)
The edible nut of the macadamia tree, native to Australia and widely cultivated for its rich, sweet flavor.
edible nut from the macadamia tree

Examples

She sprinkled chopped macadamia nuts on top of the cookies before baking.
Macadamia nuts are valued for their creamy texture and buttery flavor.
The bakery imports fresh macadamia nuts from Hawaii for their pastries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: macadamia - the macadamia tree or its nuts
  • Synonym: hazelnut - another common edible nut

Etymology

Named after John Macadam, a 19th-century Scottish-Australian chemist.
