genus Alopex

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs əˈloʊ.pɛks / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs əˈləʊ.pɛks / (BrE)
A genus of arctic foxes, formerly used to classify the arctic fox species.
former genus of arctic foxes

Examples

The genus Alopex was once used to classify arctic foxes before being merged with Vulpes.
Recent genetic studies have reclassified species from the genus Alopex into the genus Vulpes.
In older zoological texts, the arctic fox is often referred to as part of the genus Alopex.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arctic fox - a small fox species adapted to arctic regions

Etymology

From Latin 'genus' (kind, type) and Neo-Latin 'Alopex', from Greek 'alopex' (fox).
