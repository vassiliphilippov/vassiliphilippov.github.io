Solomon's seal

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɑːləmənz siːl / (AmE), / ˈsɒləmənz siːl / (BrE)
a six-pointed star formed by two overlapping equilateral triangles, used especially as a symbol of Judaism; also called the Star of David
six-pointed star symbolizing Judaism

Examples

The ancient manuscript was adorned with a Solomon's seal on its cover.
Many synagogues display the Solomon's seal as a prominent emblem above their entrance.
He wore a necklace featuring a gold Solomon's seal as a sign of his faith.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Star of David - a six-pointed star and symbol of Judaism

Etymology

Named after the biblical King Solomon, whose seal was legendary in medieval Jewish lore.
