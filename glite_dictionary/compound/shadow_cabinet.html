shadow cabinet

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃædoʊ ˈkæbɪnət / (AmE), / ˈʃadəʊ ˈkabɪnət / (BrE)
(in the UK and some other parliamentary systems) a group of senior opposition politicians who would take up ministerial positions if their party came to power.
senior opposition politicians ready to form a cabinet
british

Examples

The Labour shadow cabinet met to discuss their response to the proposed new legislation.
She was appointed to the shadow cabinet as shadow Secretary of State for Education.
The shadow cabinet criticized the government's approach during the economic crisis.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cabinet - a group of senior government ministers

Etymology

From 'shadow' (oppositional counterpart) and 'cabinet' (group of ministers).
