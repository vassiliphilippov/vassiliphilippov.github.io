large cranberry

1. NOUN

/ lɑrdʒ ˈkrænˌbɛri / (AmE), / lɑːdʒ ˈkrænˌbəri / (BrE)
A North American trailing plant (Vaccinium macrocarpon) with large, red, edible berries used especially for making cranberry sauce and juice.
a North American trailing plant with large edible red berries

Examples

The commercial cultivation of large cranberry has become a vital agricultural industry in certain U.S. states.
Many people add dried large cranberry to their baked goods for a tart flavor.
The bogs were thick with large cranberry plants trailing over the wet ground.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cranberry - a small, red, acid berry; the plant it grows on
