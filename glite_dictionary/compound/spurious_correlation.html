spurious correlation

1. NOUN

/ ˈspjʊriəs ˌkɔrəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈspjʊəriəs ˌkɒrəˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
a statistical relationship between two variables that appears to be causal but actually results from each variable's association with a third factor
false or misleading statistical correlation

Examples

The researchers discovered that the link between ice cream sales and crime rates was a spurious correlation.
It's important to distinguish a true causal relationship from a spurious correlation in scientific studies.
A spurious correlation can mislead people into thinking two variables are related when they are not.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: correlation - a mutual relationship or connection between two or more things
  • Antonym: causal relationship - a direct relationship where one variable causes a change in another

Etymology

From Latin 'spurius' (false) and 'correlation' (relationship or connection).
