bell tower

1. NOUN

/ bɛl ˈtaʊɚ / (AmE), / bɛl ˈtaʊə / (BrE)
A tall structure, often part of a church or public building, built to house and support one or more bells.
tower housing a bell or bells

Examples

The old church's bell tower could be seen from miles away across the fields.
Tourists climbed the bell tower for a spectacular view of the city.
A newly restored bell tower was unveiled during the town's anniversary celebration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: campanile - an Italian term for a freestanding bell tower, especially in Italy
