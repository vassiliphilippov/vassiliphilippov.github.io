rock and roll

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɑk ən ˈroʊl / (AmE), / ˌrɒk ən ˈrəʊl / (BrE)
A genre of popular music that originated in the 1950s, combining elements of rhythm and blues with country and western.
popular music genre from the 1950s

Examples

The birth of rock and roll changed American music forever.
Many classic rock and roll songs feature powerful guitar solos and energetic rhythms.
He is considered one of the pioneers of rock and roll in the 1950s.

Synset Examples

rock is a generic term for the range of styles that evolved out of rock'n'roll.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: rock music - A broader category including music styles evolved from rock and roll
  • Synonym: rhythm and blues - A musical genre combining jazz, gospel, and blues, influential in rock and roll

Etymology

From 'rock', describing rhythmic movement, and 'roll', both slang terms in African-American culture for dancing or sexual activity.
