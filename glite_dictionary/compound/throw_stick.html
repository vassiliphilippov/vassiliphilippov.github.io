throw stick

1. NOUN

/ ˈθroʊ ˌstɪk / (AmE), / ˈθrəʊ ˌstɪk / (BrE)
a curved stick designed so that when thrown correctly, it returns to the thrower; often used as a hunting tool or for sport
curved stick that returns when thrown

Examples

The Aboriginal hunter skillfully threw his throw stick to bring down the bird.
A throw stick requires practice to use effectively so that it returns to the thrower.
Many museums display a traditional throw stick alongside boomerangs and other ancient tools.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boomerang - a curved stick that returns to the thrower when thrown
