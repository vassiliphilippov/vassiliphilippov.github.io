weed-whacker

1. NOUN

/ ˈwid ˌwækər / (AmE), / ˈwiːd ˌwækə / (BrE)
A powered gardening tool with a rotating string or blade used for cutting weeds and grass, especially around obstacles.
powered tool for cutting weeds and grass

Examples

I used the weed-whacker to tidy the edges of the lawn before our guests arrived.
After ten minutes with the weed-whacker, the garden looked much neater.
The old weed-whacker finally broke down, so we'll need to buy a new one.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: string trimmer - a similar powered tool for cutting grass and weeds

Etymology

From 'weed' (unwanted plant) and 'whacker' (one who whacks or strikes repeatedly).
