Holy Sepulchre

1. PROPN

/ ˌhoʊli ˈsɛpəlkɚ / (AmE), / ˌhəʊli ˈsɛplkə / (BrE)
The tomb in which the body of Jesus Christ was laid between his burial and resurrection, especially referring to the site in Jerusalem.
tomb of Jesus Christ in Jerusalem

Examples

Pilgrims from around the world visit the Holy Sepulchre each year.
The Church of the Holy Sepulchre is regarded as one of the holiest sites in Christianity.
Many Christians believe the Holy Sepulchre marks the place of Jesus' resurrection.

Etymology

From Latin 'sepulcrum' (tomb), used in Christian tradition.
