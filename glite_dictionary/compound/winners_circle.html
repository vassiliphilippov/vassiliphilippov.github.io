winner's circle

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪnərz ˈsɝːkəl / (AmE), / ˈwɪnəz ˈsɜːkəl / (BrE)
a small area at a racecourse where the owners of winning horses are awarded prizes
area for awarding race winners

Examples

After her horse finished first, Sarah was invited to the winner's circle to receive the trophy.
The excitement in the winner's circle was palpable as the owners celebrated their victory.
Photographers gathered around the winner's circle to capture the moment the prize was awarded.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: winners' enclosure - enclosure at a racecourse for winning horses and owners, especially in British English
