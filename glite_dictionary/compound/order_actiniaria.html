order Actiniaria

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdər ˌæktɪˈnaɪəriə / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə ˌæktɪˈnaɪəriə / (BrE)
A taxonomic order of marine animals known as sea anemones, characterized by a cylindrical body and tentacles surrounding the mouth.
taxonomic order of sea anemones

Examples

The order Actiniaria includes a wide variety of sea anemones found in oceans around the world.
Researchers discovered a new species within the order Actiniaria near deep-sea hydrothermal vents.
Several members of the order Actiniaria form symbiotic relationships with fish such as clownfish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sea anemone - marine animal with a cylindrical body and tentacles, belonging to Actiniaria

Etymology

From Neo-Latin 'Actiniaria', from Greek 'aktīnē' (ray), referencing the radiating tentacles.
