Russian Soviet Federated Socialist Republic

1. PROPN

/ ˈrʌʃən ˈsoʊvi.ət ˌfɛdərˈeɪtɪd səˈʃælɪst rɪˈpʌblɪk / (AmE), / ˈrʌʃən ˈsəʊvi.ət ˌfɛdərˈeɪtɪd səˈʃælɪst rɪˈpʌblɪk / (BrE)
the largest and most populous constituent republic of the former USSR, occupying eastern Europe and northern Asia; existed from 1917 to 1991
former Soviet republic, largest in USSR

Examples

The Russian Soviet Federated Socialist Republic played a dominant role within the USSR throughout most of the twentieth century.
After the dissolution of the Soviet Union, the Russian Soviet Federated Socialist Republic became the Russian Federation.
Historians often study the governance and policies of the Russian Soviet Federated Socialist Republic to understand the broader context of Soviet history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Soviet Union - a former communist country in eastern Europe and northern Asia, known as the USSR
  • Degree Higher: Russian Federation - modern Russia, the current nation-state that succeeded the Russian SFSR
