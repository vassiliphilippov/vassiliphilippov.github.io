genus Nyssa

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈnɪs.ə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈnɪs.ə / (BrE)
A genus of deciduous trees known as tupelos, typically found in moist habitats such as swamps and alongside ponds.
genus of trees (tupelos) in moist habitats

Examples

The genus Nyssa includes several species of trees commonly known as tupelos.
Botanists often study the genus Nyssa to understand adaptations to wetland environments.
Some species in the genus Nyssa are important for wildlife habitats and honey production.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tupelo - deciduous tree native to wetland areas in North America, especially of the genus Nyssa

Etymology

From Greek Nýssa, a mythological water nymph; scientific Latin nomenclature.
