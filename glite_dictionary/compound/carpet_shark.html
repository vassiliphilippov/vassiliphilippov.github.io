carpet shark

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːr.pət ˌʃɑrk / (AmE), / ˈkɑː.pɪt ˌʃɑːk / (BrE)
a shark of the western Pacific with a flattened body and mottled skin, belonging to several species in the family Orectolobidae
shark with flattened, mottled body

Examples

The carpet shark is known for its remarkable camouflage abilities on the ocean floor.
Divers spotted a carpet shark resting motionless among the rocks.
Some species of carpet shark are popular attractions in public aquariums.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wobbegong - a type of carpet shark with a flattened body and distinctive skin flaps near its mouth

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːrpɪt ˌʃɑrk / (AmE), / ˈkɑːpɪt ˌʃɑːk / (BrE)
any of various sharks of the order Orectolobiformes, typically bottom-dwelling and often camouflaged, found near the ocean floor
a type of bottom-dwelling shark

Examples

The carpet shark often blends into the ocean floor, making it difficult for predators to spot.
Divers were excited to encounter a rare carpet shark resting under a ledge.
Unlike other species, the carpet shark prefers to stay close to rocky reefs and sandy bottoms.

Synset Examples

The carpet shark hid beneath the coral, waiting for prey.
He caught a glimpse of the carpet shark gliding through the water.

Etymology

So called because of their often mottled, carpet-like patterns on their skin.
