head voice

1. NOUN

/ hɛd vɔɪs / (AmE), / hɛd vɔɪs / (BrE)
a category of vocal register comprising the higher range of the voice, in which the vibrations are perceived as resonating in the head, used especially in singing or speaking
higher vocal register felt in the head

Examples

She effortlessly switched into her head voice to reach the highest notes of the aria.
Some singers struggle to blend their chest voice and head voice smoothly.
In the chorus, he uses his head voice to give a lighter, more ethereal sound.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: chest voice - lower register of the voice with resonance felt in the chest
