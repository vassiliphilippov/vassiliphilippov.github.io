salivary duct

1. NOUN

/ ˈsæləˌvɛri dʌkt / (AmE), / ˈsalɪvəri dʌkt / (BrE)
a duct in the mouth through which saliva flows from a salivary gland
duct carrying saliva to the mouth
medical

Examples

A blocked salivary duct can cause swelling and discomfort in the mouth.
The dentist carefully examined her salivary duct for signs of infection.
Stones may occasionally form inside a salivary duct, leading to pain and decreased saliva flow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: salivary gland - gland in the mouth that produces saliva

Etymology

From 'salivary' (relating to saliva) and 'duct' (from Latin 'ductus', meaning 'lead' or 'drawn').
