nicotinamide adenine dinucleotide

1. NOUN

/ nɪˌkoʊtɪnəˌmaɪd əˈdiːn ˌdaɪˌnuːkliəˈtaɪd / (AmE), / nɪˌkəʊtɪnəˌmaɪd əˈdiːn ˌdaɪˌnjuːklɪəˈtaɪd / (BrE)
a coenzyme found in most living cells, derived from vitamin B3, which functions as a reductant in various metabolic reactions
biological coenzyme involved in metabolism
medical

Examples

Nicotinamide adenine dinucleotide plays a key role in cellular respiration by transferring electrons during metabolic reactions.
Scientists studied changes in nicotinamide adenine dinucleotide levels to better understand energy production in cells.
Supplementing vitamin B3 can affect the amount of nicotinamide adenine dinucleotide in the body.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: NADH - the reduced form of nicotinamide adenine dinucleotide
  • Degree Higher: NAD+ - the oxidized form of nicotinamide adenine dinucleotide

Etymology

From components: 'nicotinamide' (from nicotinic acid, a form of vitamin B3) and 'adenine dinucleotide' indicating two nucleotides joined together.
