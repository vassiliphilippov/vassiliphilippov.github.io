carbon process

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑrbən ˈproʊsɛs / (AmE), / ˈkɑːbən ˈprəʊsɛs / (BrE)
a photographic printing method in which a paper coated with bichromated gelatin containing pigment is used to produce images
photographic printing process using bichromated gelatin

Examples

The carbon process was widely used in the 19th century for its permanence and rich image quality.
He learned to produce prints using the carbon process during his studies in historical photography techniques.
The museum's collection includes rare photographs made by the carbon process.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: photogravure - an intaglio printmaking or photographic process
