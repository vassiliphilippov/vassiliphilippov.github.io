golden parachute

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡoʊldən ˈpærəˌʃut / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊldən ˈpærəˌʃuːt / (BrE)
A financial arrangement that gives top executives generous benefits if they are dismissed following a company takeover.
lucrative severance for executives after takeover

Examples

The CEO received a golden parachute worth millions when the company was sold.
Shareholders criticized the board for approving extravagant golden parachute deals.
Many executives negotiate a golden parachute clause in case of unexpected mergers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: severance package - compensation paid to employees upon dismissal

Etymology

Modelled on the literal 'parachute,' symbolizing a soft and safe landing; 'golden' refers to substantial financial value.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡoʊldən ˈpærəˌʃut / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊldən ˈpærəʃuːt / (BrE)
an agreement to provide a large financial compensation package to a top executive if they are dismissed or forced to leave a company, especially after a takeover
large severance package for executives

Examples

The CEO left the company with a golden parachute worth ten million dollars.
Many investors criticized the board for approving such a generous golden parachute.
After the merger, several executives received golden parachutes to ease their departures.

Synset Examples

He secured a golden parachute after his controversial exit.
The executive's golden parachute was worth millions.
Companies often face backlash over extravagant golden parachutes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: severance package - compensation paid to an employee when leaving a company

Etymology

From 'golden' (valuable) + 'parachute' (device for a safe landing), metaphorically implying a soft financial landing for executives.
