family Tortricidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli tɔːrˈtrɪsɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli tɔːˈtrɪsɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A large family of moths, known as tortricid moths, which includes leaf rollers and codling moths, many of which are agricultural pests.
a family of moths including leaf rollers and codling moths

Examples

Several species in the family Tortricidae are notorious pests of apple orchards.
Entomologists study the family Tortricidae to understand the impact of leaf rollers on crops.
The family Tortricidae contains thousands of species distributed worldwide.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tortricid - a moth of the family Tortricidae

Etymology

From New Latin Tortricidae, based on the genus Tortrix, from Latin torquere (to twist).
