hydrogen chloride

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪdrədʒən klaɪˈraɪd / (AmE), / ˌhaɪdrədʒən klɒˈraɪd / (BrE)
a colorless, corrosive gas with the chemical formula HCl, used in industry and chemistry
a colorless, corrosive gas (HCl)
medical

Examples

Industrial processes often use hydrogen chloride to produce hydrochloric acid.
If hydrogen chloride escapes into the air, it forms white fumes by reacting with moisture.
Authorities evacuated the area after a hydrogen chloride leak was detected at the plant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hydrochloric acid - the aqueous solution form of hydrogen chloride

Etymology

hydrogen + chloride, indicating a compound of hydrogen and chlorine.
