dressing-down

1. NOUN

/ ˌdrɛsɪŋ ˈdaʊn / (AmE), / ˌdrɛsɪŋ ˈdaʊn / (BrE)
a severe reprimand or scolding, especially delivered to someone in authority
severe reprimand or scolding

Examples

The manager gave Lisa a dressing-down in front of her colleagues for missing the deadline.
He dreaded coming home late because he knew his parents would give him a dressing-down.
After that public dressing-down, he resolved to double-check all his work in the future.

Synset Examples

He received a dressing-down for being late again.
The boss gave him a dressing-down after the presentation.
After the dressing-down, she decided to improve her performance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reprimand - an official expression of disapproval
  • Synonym: scolding - an angry reprimand or rebuke

Etymology

Originally from the phrase "to dress down" meaning to reprimand, 19th century.
