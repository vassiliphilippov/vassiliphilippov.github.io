starch wheat

1. NOUN

/ stɑrtʃ wit / (AmE), / stɑːtʃ wiːt / (BrE)
a hard red wheat variety grown chiefly in Russia and Germany, used mainly for stock feed in the United States
type of hard red wheat used for feed

Examples

In Eastern Europe, starch wheat is often preferred for its hard kernel and high yield.
Most of the starch wheat grown in the Midwest is used as feed for livestock.
Researchers are studying ways to improve the quality of starch wheat for commercial use.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wheat - a cereal plant cultivated for its grain
