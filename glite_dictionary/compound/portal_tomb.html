portal tomb

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɔrtəl ˌtoʊm / (AmE), / ˈpɔːtəl ˌtəʊm / (BrE)
a prehistoric megalithic tomb consisting of two large upright stones supporting a capstone
megalithic tomb with upright stones and capstone

Examples

Archaeologists discovered a portal tomb dating back to the Neolithic period in the Irish countryside.
The massive stones of the portal tomb stood untouched for thousands of years.
We visited an ancient portal tomb during our history field trip.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dolmen - a type of megalithic tomb with upright stones and a capstone
