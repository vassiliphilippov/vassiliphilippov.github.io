Shenandoah National Park

1. PROPN

/ ʃəˈnændoʊ.ə ˌnæʃənəl ˈpɑrk / (AmE), / ʃəˈnændəʊ.ə ˌnæʃənəl ˈpɑːk / (BrE)
a national park in Virginia, United States, known for encompassing the scenic Blue Ridge Mountains and portions of the Appalachian Trail
national park in Virginia, USA

Examples

We hiked the Appalachian Trail through Shenandoah National Park last summer and enjoyed stunning mountain views.
Wildlife such as black bears and deer can often be seen in Shenandoah National Park.
The fall foliage in Shenandoah National Park attracts thousands of visitors each year.

Etymology

Named after the Shenandoah River and Valley; 'Shenandoah' is of Native American origin.
