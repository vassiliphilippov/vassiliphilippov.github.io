lac wax

1. NOUN

/ læk wæks / (AmE), / læk wæks / (BrE)
a hard, naturally occurring wax obtained as a by-product when shellac is treated with alcohol, separated due to its insolubility.
hard wax by-product of shellac processing

Examples

The coating was made from purified lac wax for a glossy finish.
Manufacturers extract lac wax from shellac during refinement processes.
Lac wax is valued in industry for its resistance to moisture and alcohol.

Etymology

From 'lac' (a resinous secretion of insects of the family Lacciferidae) and 'wax.'
