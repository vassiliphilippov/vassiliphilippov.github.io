Battle of the Aisne

1. PROPN

/ ˈbæt̬.əl əv ði ɛn / (AmE), / ˈbæt.əl əv ði ɛn / (BrE)
a major battle in World War I in May 1918, where German forces attempted an offensive against the Allies, resulting in tactical gains but ultimately a strategic failure
a World War I battle in May 1918 between Germans and Allies

Examples

The Battle of the Aisne marked a significant turning point in the final year of World War I.
Historians still debate the strategic consequences of the Battle of the Aisne for both sides.
Remnants from the Battle of the Aisne can still be seen in the fields of northern France.

Etymology

Named after the Aisne River in northern France, near where the battle took place.
