plompy shoe

1. NOUN

/ ˈplɑmpi ʃu / (AmE), / ˈplɒmpi ʃuː / (BrE)
A shoe that is noticeably bulky, heavy, or clumsy in appearance or design.
bulky or clumsy shoe
informal

Examples

She decided not to wear her plompy shoe to the interview for a more professional look.
Those plompy shoes didn't match her delicate dress at the wedding.
After spraining his ankle, he found even his plompy shoe uncomfortable to walk in.

Synset Examples

Her shoes were too plompy for the formal event.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clunky shoe - a shoe that is heavy and awkward
  • Antonym: elegant shoe - a shoe that is graceful and stylish, opposite of 'plompy shoe'

Etymology

Formed from the word 'plompy', likely an extension of 'plump' or 'plod', and 'shoe'.
