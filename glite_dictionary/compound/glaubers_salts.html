Glauber's salts

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡlɔːbərz sɔlts / (AmE), / ˈɡlaʊbəz sɔːlts / (BrE)
a colorless crystalline compound (sodium sulfate decahydrate, Na2SO4·10H2O) used chiefly as a laxative and in chemical industries
sodium sulfate decahydrate; used as a laxative
medical

Examples

Early scientists used Glauber's salts as a common laxative remedy.
Glauber's salts dissolve easily in water to form a clear solution.
The laboratory ordered a supply of Glauber's salts for chemical experiments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sodium sulfate - a sodium salt of sulfuric acid (Na2SO4)

Etymology

Named after German chemist Johann Rudolf Glauber (1604–1670), who discovered the compound.
