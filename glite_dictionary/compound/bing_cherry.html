Bing cherry

1. NOUN

/ bɪŋ ˈtʃɛri / (AmE), / bɪŋ ˈtʃɛri / (BrE)
A variety of sweet cherry with firm, dark red to almost black skin and juicy flesh, often eaten fresh.
a variety of sweet dark red cherry

Examples

She bought a bag of Bing cherries at the farmers' market for a summer dessert.
The Bing cherry tree in their yard produces fruit each June.
Bing cherries are known for their deep color and exceptionally sweet flavor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sweet cherry - a type of cherry with a sweet flavor, as opposed to sour varieties

Etymology

Named after Ah Bing, a Chinese foreman who helped develop the variety in Oregon, USA, in the late 19th century.
