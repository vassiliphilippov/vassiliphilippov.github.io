ad-hoc committee

1. NOUN

/ æd ˈhɑk kəˌmɪti / (AmE), / æd ˈhɒk kəˈmɪti / (BrE)
A committee formed temporarily for a specific purpose or task.
temporary committee for a specific purpose

Examples

The city council established an ad-hoc committee to address the sudden water shortage.
An ad-hoc committee was created to investigate the financial discrepancy within the company.
After the emergency, the ad-hoc committee was dissolved when its purpose had been fulfilled.

Synset Examples

The ad-hoc committee met to discuss urgent issues.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: standing committee - a permanent committee with ongoing responsibilities

Etymology

From Latin 'ad hoc' meaning 'for this', indicating a committee created for a particular purpose.
