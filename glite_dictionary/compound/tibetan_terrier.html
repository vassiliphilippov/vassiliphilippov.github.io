Tibetan terrier

1. NOUN

/ tɪˈbɛtən ˈtɛriər / (AmE), / tɪˈbɛtən ˈtɛriə / (BrE)
a breed of medium-sized dogs, originally from Tibet, known for their thick, long coat and fluffy curled tail
medium-sized dog breed from Tibet

Examples

My family adopted a Tibetan terrier because of its friendly and playful nature.
The Tibetan terrier has a distinctive long coat that needs regular grooming.
Although called a terrier, the Tibetan terrier is not a true terrier by classification.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Old English sheepdog - large British herding dog with a shaggy coat

Etymology

Named for its Tibetan origin and resemblance to terriers, though not a true terrier.
