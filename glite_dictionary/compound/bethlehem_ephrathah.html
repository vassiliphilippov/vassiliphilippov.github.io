Bethlehem Ephrathah

1. PROPN

/ ˌbɛθlɪˌhɛm ɛˈfræθə / (AmE), / ˈbɛθlɪˌhɛm ɛˈfrɑːθə / (BrE)
a small town near Jerusalem, traditionally identified as the birthplace of Jesus and the early home of David
ancient town near Jerusalem; Jesus' birthplace

Examples

Many pilgrims travel to Bethlehem Ephrathah to visit significant religious sites.
The prophet Micah foretold the importance of Bethlehem Ephrathah in biblical texts.
Archaeologists have studied Bethlehem Ephrathah for insights into ancient Judea.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Bethlehem - town in the West Bank, traditionally regarded as the birthplace of Jesus

Etymology

'Bethlehem' from Hebrew *Beit Lehem* (house of bread); 'Ephrathah' from Hebrew *Efratah*, an ancient name for the area.
