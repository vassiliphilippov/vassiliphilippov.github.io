Bahasa Indonesia

1. NOUN

/ bɑːˌhɑːsə ɪnˌdoʊˈniːʒə / (AmE), / bɑːˌhɑːsə ɪnˌdəʊˈniːzɪə / (BrE)
the standard form of Malay used as the official language of Indonesia
official language of Indonesia

Examples

Students in Jakarta learn Bahasa Indonesia from an early age.
The official documents are all written in Bahasa Indonesia.
She speaks both Javanese and Bahasa Indonesia fluently.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Malay - the language from which Bahasa Indonesia is derived

Etymology

From Indonesian, literally 'language of Indonesia': 'bahasa' meaning 'language' and 'Indonesia'.
