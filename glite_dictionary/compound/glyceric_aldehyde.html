glyceric aldehyde

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡlɪˈsɛrɪk ˈældəˌhaɪd / (AmE), / ˌɡlɪˈsɛrɪk ˈaldɪhaɪd / (BrE)
a sweet, crystalline compound formed during the breakdown of sugars, often encountered in biochemical processes
a sweet crystalline compound from sugar breakdown
medical

Examples

Researchers studied how glyceric aldehyde forms during the initial stages of sugar metabolism.
The presence of glyceric aldehyde was detected in the solution after the enzymatic reaction took place.
Understanding glyceric aldehyde is important in biochemistry courses dealing with carbohydrate breakdown.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aldehyde - class of organic compounds containing the -CHO group
  • Synonym: glyceraldehyde - an alternate name for glyceric aldehyde

Etymology

From 'glyceric', relating to glycerin, and 'aldehyde', from German 'Aldehyd'.
