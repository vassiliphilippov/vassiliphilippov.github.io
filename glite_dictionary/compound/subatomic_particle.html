subatomic particle

1. NOUN

/ sʌbəˈtɑmɪk ˈpɑrtɪkəl / (AmE), / sʌbətɒmɪk ˈpɑːtɪkl̩ / (BrE)
a particle smaller than an atom, such as a proton, neutron, or electron, with finite mass and internal structure
particle smaller than an atom

Examples

Physicists study the behavior of a subatomic particle to learn more about the nature of matter.
The discovery of the Higgs boson was a milestone in subatomic particle research.
Each subatomic particle has unique properties like charge and mass.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: atom - the basic unit of a chemical element
  • Synonym: proton - a positively charged subatomic particle
  • Synonym: neutron - a neutral subatomic particle
  • Synonym: electron - a negatively charged subatomic particle

Etymology

From 'sub-' (below) and 'atomic', meaning below the scale of atoms.
