neon tube

1. NOUN

/ ˈniːɑn tuːb / (AmE), / ˈniːɒn tjuːb / (BrE)
a glass tube filled with neon gas at low pressure that glows when an electric current is passed through it, used especially in signs and lighting
glass tube with glowing neon gas, used in signs

Examples

The old theater sign was restored with bright neon tubes outlining every letter.
She admired the vivid colors produced by the neon tube in his art installation.
Electricians carefully replaced the damaged neon tube in the store's window display.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neon lamp - a lamp using neon gas to produce light
  • Degree Lower: fluorescent tube - a glass tube filled with gas, produces light by fluorescence

Etymology

From 'neon', the chemical element, plus 'tube'.
