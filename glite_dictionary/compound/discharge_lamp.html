discharge lamp

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɪs.tʃɑrdʒ læmp / (AmE), / ˈdɪs.tʃɑːdʒ læmp / (BrE)
a lamp that produces light by creating an electric discharge between two electrodes in a gas
lamp with light from electric discharge in gas

Examples

Many streetlights use discharge lamps because they are energy efficient and long-lasting.
The color of the light from a discharge lamp depends on the type of gas inside it.
She replaced the old discharge lamp in the laboratory with a newer model for better illumination.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arc lamp - lamp producing light by an arc between two electrodes
  • Antonym: incandescent lamp - lamp producing light using a filament heated to incandescence
