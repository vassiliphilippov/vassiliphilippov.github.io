fag end

1. NOUN

/ fæɡ ɛnd / (AmE), / fæɡ end / (BrE)
the frayed or worn end of a length of cloth, rope, or similar material
the frayed end of cloth or rope

Examples

The old flag hung in tatters, its fag end fluttering in the breeze.
She tied the fag end of the rope to prevent further unraveling.
After years of use, only the fag end of the cord remained attached to the curtain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fray - an unravelled or worn part of fabric

Etymology

From 'fag' meaning the last part or frayed part, combined with 'end'.

2. NOUN

/ fæɡ ɛnd / (AmE), / fæɡ end / (BrE)
the final or last part of something, especially when regarded as less important or of lower quality than earlier parts
last or final part of something

Examples

We're now in the fag end of the negotiations, but there are still a few points left to discuss.
By the fag end of the party, only a few tired guests remained.
The company was founded in the fag end of the nineteenth century.

Synset Examples

the fag end of this crisis-ridden century
the tail of the storm

Etymology

Originally referred to the frayed end of a rope or a cigarette stub; 'fag' meaning loose end is of uncertain origin, possibly from dialect.
