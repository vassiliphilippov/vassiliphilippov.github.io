Bahama grass

1. NOUN

/ bəˈhɑːmə ɡræs / (AmE), / bəˈhɑːmə ɡrɑːs / (BrE)
A trailing grass native to Europe, now widely found in warm regions, often used for lawns and pastures, especially in the southern United States and India.
tropical grass for lawns and pastures

Examples

Drought-tolerant Bahama grass is often chosen for lawns in warm climates.
Farmers in southern India rely on Bahama grass to feed their livestock.
The golf course was carefully sown with Bahama grass for its durability.
