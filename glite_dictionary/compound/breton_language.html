Breton language

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrɛtən ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈbrɛtən ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Celtic language spoken in Brittany, a region in northwestern France
Celtic language of Brittany, France

Examples

The Breton language has experienced a revival in schools across Brittany.
He is fluent in both French and the Breton language.
Medieval texts written in the Breton language provide insights into Celtic culture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Welsh language - a Celtic language of Wales
  • Synonym: Cornish language - a Celtic language historically spoken in Cornwall

Etymology

From French 'breton' (of Brittany) and 'langue' (language); related to Latin 'Britto' (Briton).
