bottle tree

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑt̬l ˌtri / (AmE), / ˈbɒtl ˌtriː / (BrE)
an Australian tree of the genus Brachychiton, known for its swollen, bottle-shaped trunk used to store water
Australian tree with bottle-shaped trunk

Examples

The bottle tree stores water in its thick trunk, helping it survive Australia’s dry climate.
Several bottle trees dotted the landscape, their swollen trunks distinctive against the red soil.
Early settlers would tap the bottle tree for water during long journeys.
