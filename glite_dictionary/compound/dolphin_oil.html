dolphin oil

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɑlfɪn ɔɪl / (AmE), / ˈdɒlfɪn ɔɪl / (BrE)
an unsaturated oil obtained from dolphins, formerly used as a fine lubricant
oil from dolphins used as lubricant

Examples

Before synthetic alternatives became common, dolphin oil was highly valued for its lubricating properties.
The use of dolphin oil is now banned due to environmental and ethical concerns.
In the nineteenth century, watchmakers sometimes used dolphin oil to fine-tune gears and mechanisms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sperm oil - oil obtained from sperm whales, used as lubricant
