deathwatch beetle

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛθˌwɑtʃ ˌbiːt̬əl / (AmE), / ˈdɛθˌwɒtʃ ˌbiːtl / (BrE)
a wood-boring beetle that makes a ticking sound, traditionally believed to be an omen of death
wood-boring beetle thought to foretell death

Examples

The old house was infested with deathwatch beetles that bored into the ancient timbers.
Many people once believed the faint ticking of a deathwatch beetle signaled an impending death.
Carpenters inspected the beams for signs of deathwatch beetle activity after noticing tiny holes.

Etymology

The name refers to the beetle's ticking sound, thought to be associated with vigils (watches) beside the dying ('deathwatch').
