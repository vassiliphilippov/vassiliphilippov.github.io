spiritual wifery

1. NOUN

/ ˈspɪrɪtʃuəl ˈwaɪfəri / (AmE), / ˈspɪrɪtjʊəl ˈwɪfəri / (BrE)
an unconventional relationship model in which individuals form deep, emotionally intimate bonds with partners based on spiritual or religious principles, rather than traditional marriage.
non-traditional, spiritual relationship model

Examples

Many religious communities in the 19th century practiced spiritual wifery as part of their doctrine.
Her interest in spiritual wifery led her to join a group focused on deep emotional bonds.
Some people view spiritual wifery as an alternative to conventional marriage.

Etymology

Formed from 'spiritual' and 'wifery,' referencing 19th-century religious movements that advocated non-traditional relationships.
