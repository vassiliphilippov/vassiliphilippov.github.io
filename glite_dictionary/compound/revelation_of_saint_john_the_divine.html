Revelation of Saint John the Divine

1. PROPN

/ ˌrɛvəˈleɪʃən əv ˌseɪnt ˌdʒɑːn ðə dəˈvaɪn / (AmE), / ˌrɛvəˈleɪʃən əv ˌseɪnt ˌdʒɒn ðə dɪˈvaɪn / (BrE)
the last book of the New Testament, containing visionary descriptions of heaven, the final battle between good and evil, and the end of the world; also known as the Book of Revelation or Apocalypse.
last book of the New Testament (Book of Revelation)

Examples

Many scholars study the Revelation of Saint John the Divine to interpret symbolic visions of the end times.
The Revelation of Saint John the Divine is often cited in discussions about prophecy and apocalypse.
In his sermon, the priest quoted passages from the Revelation of Saint John the Divine to emphasize hope amid tribulation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Apocalypse - alternative name for the Book of Revelation
  • Synonym: Book of Revelation - another common name for the last book of the New Testament
