Texas chachalaca

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛksəs tʃəˈtʃæləkə / (AmE), / ˈtɛksəs tʃəˈtʃaləkə / (BrE)
a large, brownish-grey bird of the guan family (Ortalis vetula) native to southern Texas and Mexico, known for its loud calls
a bird species native to Texas and Mexico

Examples

The Texas chachalaca can often be heard making raucous calls at dawn in south Texas.
A group of Texas chachalacas wandered through the brush searching for berries.
Birdwatchers were excited to spot a Texas chachalaca during their visit to the nature reserve.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chachalaca - a bird of the genus Ortalis, similar in appearance and behavior

Etymology

From 'Texas,' the U.S. state, and Spanish 'chachalaca' (imitative of the bird's call).
