Folsom culture

1. NOUN

/ ˈfoʊl.səm ˈkʌl.tʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈfɒl.səm ˈkʌl.tʃə / (BrE)
a Paleo-American culture in Central and North America, known for its distinctive leaf-shaped flint projectile points
Paleo-American culture with unique flint tools

Examples

Archaeologists discovered Folsom culture artifacts at the ancient campsite.
The Folsom culture is noted for its finely crafted flint spear points.
Research into Folsom culture has shed light on early human migration in North America.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Clovis culture - an earlier Paleo-American culture known for different projectile point types

Etymology

Named after Folsom, New Mexico, where evidence of the culture was first discovered.
