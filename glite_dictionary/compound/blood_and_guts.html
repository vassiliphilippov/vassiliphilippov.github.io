blood and guts

1. NOUN

/ blʌd ənd ɡʌts / (AmE), / blʌd ənd ɡʌts / (BrE)
graphic violence, especially involving bloodshed and internal organs; also, intense or raw emotion or determination
graphic violence or intense emotion

Examples

The horror film was filled with blood and guts from start to finish.
After the accident, his account was all blood and guts, and everyone cringed.
That team showed real blood and guts in the final minutes of the game.

Synset Examples

His story about surviving the accident was all blood and guts.
The documentary featured blood and guts from the battlefield.
The movie was filled with blood and guts, not for the faint-hearted.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gore - bloody violence or graphic bloodshed
  • Degree Lower: bravery - courage or determination, especially in tough situations
