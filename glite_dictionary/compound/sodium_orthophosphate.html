sodium orthophosphate

1. NOUN

/ ˌsoʊdiəm ɔːrˌθoʊˈfɑs.feɪt / (AmE), / ˌsəʊdiəm ɔːˌθəʊˈfɒs.feɪt / (BrE)
a sodium salt of orthophosphoric acid, used as a laxative and in various industrial applications
a sodium salt of orthophosphoric acid
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed sodium orthophosphate as a laxative before the procedure.
Industrial cleaning agents often contain sodium orthophosphate for its effectiveness.
Excessive use of sodium orthophosphate can lead to an imbalance of electrolytes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trisodium phosphate - a common sodium phosphate salt used in cleaning and food processing

Etymology

From sodium (the element) and orthophosphate, which derives from Greek orthos (straight, correct) + phosphate.
