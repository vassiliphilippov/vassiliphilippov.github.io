law of segregation

1. NOUN

/ lɔː əv ˌsɛɡrəˈɡeɪʃən / (AmE), / lɔː əv ˌsɛɡrəˈɡeɪʃən / (BrE)
the principle in genetics stating that during the formation of gametes, the two members of a pair of homologous chromosomes separate so that each gamete receives only one member of the pair
genetic principle of chromosome separation

Examples

The law of segregation helps explain why offspring inherit one allele from each parent.
Mendel's experiments with peas were crucial in formulating the law of segregation.
Biology students must understand the law of segregation to grasp basic genetic inheritance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mendel's first law - another term for the law of segregation in genetics
