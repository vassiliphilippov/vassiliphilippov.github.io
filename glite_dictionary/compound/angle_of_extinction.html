angle of extinction

1. NOUN

/ ˈæŋɡəl əv ɪkˈstɪŋkʃən / (AmE), / ˈæŋɡl əv ɪkˈstɪŋkʃən / (BrE)
the angle through which a crystal must be rotated from its axis before it appears maximally dark when viewed in polarized light
rotation angle causing darkness in polarized light

Examples

The mineralogist measured the angle of extinction to help identify the crystal specimen.
Accurate determination of the angle of extinction is essential in optical mineralogy.
Each crystal has a characteristic angle of extinction when analyzed with polarized light.
