to raise suspicion

1. VERB

/ reɪz səˈspɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / reɪz səˈspɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
to cause people to feel doubt or mistrust about someone or something
cause doubt or mistrust

Examples

Her unexplained absences from work began to raise suspicion among her colleagues.
The locked door at the end of the hallway immediately raised suspicion.
He tried to act normal so as not to raise suspicion during the investigation.

Synset Examples

If you protest too much, it raises suspicion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arouse suspicion - to cause suspicion or mistrust
