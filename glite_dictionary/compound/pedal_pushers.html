pedal pushers

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛdəl ˌpʊʃɚz / (AmE), / ˈpɛdəl ˌpʊʃəz / (BrE)
Snug-fitting pants for women and girls that end at the mid-calf, typically worn in casual settings.
close-fitting calf-length pants

Examples

She wore her favorite pedal pushers and a striped T-shirt to the picnic.
In the 1950s, pedal pushers became a trendy item among young women.
The store sells pedal pushers in a variety of colors for summer.

Etymology

Named for their association with bicycling; the length allowed comfortable pedaling.
