hitching post

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɪtʃɪŋ poʊst / (AmE), / ˈhɪtʃɪŋ pəʊst / (BrE)
A fixed post, usually with a ring, to which a horse can be tied to prevent it from wandering away.
post for tying horses

Examples

The cowboy tied his horse to the hitching post outside the saloon.
Old photographs of the town show a hitching post in front of every shop.
She leaned against the hitching post while waiting for her friends.
