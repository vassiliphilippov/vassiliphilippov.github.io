Homo insipiens

1. NOUN

/ ˌhoʊmoʊ ɪnˈsɪpiˌɛnz / (AmE), / ˌhəʊməʊ ɪnˈsɪpiːənz / (BrE)
a satirical term for modern humans, emphasizing their perceived lack of wisdom or tendency toward foolish behavior
satirical term for unwise humans

Examples

Many environmentalists argue that Homo insipiens is driving countless species to extinction.
The documentary uses the term Homo insipiens to criticize humanity's reckless behavior.
She joked that as Homo insipiens we rarely learn from our mistakes.

Synset Examples

Scientists debate the impact of Homo insipiens on the environment.
The term Homo insipiens highlights our lack of wisdom.
Homo insipiens often makes shortsighted decisions.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Homo sapiens - the species name for modern humans, meaning 'wise man'

Etymology

From Latin 'homo' (man, human) + Latin 'insipiens' (unwise, foolish), modelled after 'Homo sapiens'.
