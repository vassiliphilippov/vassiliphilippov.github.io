Palace of Versailles

1. PROPN

/ pəˈlæs əv vɛrˈsaɪ / (AmE), / pəˈlæs əv vɛəˈsɑːj / (BrE)
the grand palace built in the 17th century for King Louis XIV, located southwest of Paris, famous for its architecture, gardens, and historical significance
grand royal palace near Paris, France

Examples

The Hall of Mirrors in the Palace of Versailles is renowned for its opulent beauty.
Tourists from around the world visit the Palace of Versailles to admire its magnificent gardens.
The Treaty of Versailles was signed in the Palace of Versailles at the end of World War I.

Etymology

French, from the city of Versailles where the palace is located; Versailles derives from Latin versare (to keep turning, to dwell).
