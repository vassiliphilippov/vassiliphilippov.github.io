red clay

1. NOUN

/ rɛd kleɪ / (AmE), / rɛd kleɪ / (BrE)
A type of clay that is red in color due to the presence of iron oxide.
iron-rich reddish clay

Examples

The pottery was made from local red clay found near the riverbanks.
Farmers often struggle to grow crops in red clay soil due to poor drainage.
After the rain, the tennis court’s red clay became sticky and difficult to walk on.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clay - a natural, earthy material that is plastic when moist and hard when fired
