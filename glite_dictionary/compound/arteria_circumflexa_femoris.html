arteria circumflexa femoris

1. NOUN

/ ɑrˈtɪəriə sɜrˌkʌmˈflɛksa ˈfɛməˌrɪs / (AmE), / ɑːˈtɪəriə sɜːˌkʌmˈflɛksa ˈfɛmərɪs / (BrE)
A blood vessel that supplies blood to the hip joint and thigh muscles.
artery supplying hip and thigh
medical

Examples

The arteria circumflexa femoris plays a crucial role in supplying blood to the upper leg.
Damage to the arteria circumflexa femoris can result in impaired blood flow to the hip region.
Surgeons carefully avoid the arteria circumflexa femoris during hip replacement procedures.

Etymology

From Latin: 'arteria' (artery), 'circumflexa' (bent around), 'femoris' (of the thigh).
