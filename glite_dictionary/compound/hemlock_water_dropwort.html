hemlock water dropwort

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛmˌlɑk ˈwɔtər ˈdrɑpˌwəːrt / (AmE), / ˈhɛmlɒk ˈwɔːtə ˈdrɒpwɜːt / (BrE)
a European poisonous plant with tuberous roots, celery-like leaves, yellowish juice that stains, and yellow flowers; extremely toxic in all parts
poisonous European plant resembling celery

Examples

Eating even a small amount of hemlock water dropwort can be fatal to humans and livestock.
The leaves of hemlock water dropwort are sometimes mistaken for parsley or celery by foragers.
Botanists warn that hemlock water dropwort grows near streams and should be handled with extreme caution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Oenanthe crocata - the botanical name for hemlock water dropwort
