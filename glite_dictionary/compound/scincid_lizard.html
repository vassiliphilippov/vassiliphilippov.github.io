scincid lizard

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪnsɪd ˈlɪzərd / (AmE), / ˈsɪnsɪd ˈlɪzəd / (BrE)
A lizard belonging to the family Scincidae, typically characterized by an elongated body, shiny scales, reduced limbs, and a preference for moist environments, found especially in tropical regions.
a lizard of the family Scincidae

Examples

The rainforest is home to several species of scincid lizard with brilliantly colored tails.
When disturbed, a scincid lizard may quickly burrow into loose soil to escape predators.
Researchers observed that the scincid lizard relies on moist habitats to regulate its body temperature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skink - a lizard of the family Scincidae

Etymology

From modern Latin 'Scincidae', the scientific family name, derived from Greek 'skinkos' (a kind of lizard).
