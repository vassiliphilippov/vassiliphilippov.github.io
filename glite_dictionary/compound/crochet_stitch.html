crochet stitch

1. NOUN

/ kroʊˈʃeɪ stɪtʃ / (AmE), / krəʊˈʃeɪ stɪtʃ / (BrE)
a type of stitch made by pulling a loop of yarn through another loop using a crochet hook
type of stitch in crocheting

Examples

She learned how to make a basic crochet stitch from her grandmother.
Each pattern requires a specific crochet stitch to create the desired texture.
After mastering the chain, she practiced the single crochet stitch for hours.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: knit stitch - a basic stitch in knitting
  • Degree Lower: slip stitch - a simple crochet stitch where the hook is inserted and yarn pulled directly through
  • Degree Higher: double crochet - a common and taller variety of crochet stitch

Etymology

From French *crochet* (small hook), referring to the tool used.
