Blackfoot language

1. NOUN

/ ˈblækˌfʊt ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈblækˌfʊt ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Algonquian language spoken by the Blackfoot people, indigenous to the northwestern Great Plains of North America
language of the Blackfoot people

Examples

He is fluent in the Blackfoot language and teaches it at the local university.
The Blackfoot language is an important part of cultural preservation efforts.
She learned traditional stories told in the Blackfoot language from her grandparents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Algonquian - a family of North American indigenous languages to which Blackfoot belongs

Etymology

Named after the Blackfoot people and the English word 'language'.
