wild bean

1. NOUN

/ waɪld ˈbiːn / (AmE), / waɪld ˈbiːn / (BrE)
a North American vine with fragrant blossoms and edible tubers, used as an important food crop by Native Americans
North American vine with edible tubers

Examples

The wild bean was once a staple food for many indigenous peoples in North America.
Botanists are studying the wild bean to understand its potential as a modern crop.
We found a patch of wild bean growing near the riverbank.

2. NOUN

/ waɪld ˈbin / (AmE), / waɪld ˈbiːn / (BrE)
a nutlike edible tuber, traditionally an important food for some Native American peoples
edible tuber, Native American food

Examples

The wild bean was highly valued by many Native American tribes as a source of nutrition.
For days, they survived on roasted wild beans and roots gathered from the prairie.
Today, botanists study the wild bean for its potential to improve modern crops.
