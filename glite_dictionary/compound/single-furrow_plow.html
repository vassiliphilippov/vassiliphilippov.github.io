single-furrow plow

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪŋɡəl ˈfɜroʊ plaʊ / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋɡəl ˈfʌrəʊ plaʊ / (BrE)
A plow designed with a single blade or moldboard for cutting one furrow at a time, typically guided by handles at the rear and sometimes supported by a front wheel.
plow with one furrow blade

Examples

A single-furrow plow is ideal for cultivating small gardens and narrow plots of land.
The farmer repaired his old single-furrow plow before the start of spring planting.
Many antique single-furrow plows are displayed in the local agricultural museum.

Synset Examples

A single draught horse can normally pull a single-furrow plough in clean light soil, but in heavier soils two horses are needed, one walking on the land and one in the furrow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plow - a farm tool used for cutting, lifting, and turning over soil
