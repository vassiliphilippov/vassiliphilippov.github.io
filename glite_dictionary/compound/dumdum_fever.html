dumdum fever

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʌm.dʌm ˈfiː.vər / (AmE), / ˈdʌm.dʌm ˈfiː.və / (BrE)
A severe infectious disease (visceral leishmaniasis) caused by protozoan parasites, affecting internal organs such as the liver and spleen.
visceral leishmaniasis; severe tropical disease
medical

Examples

The outbreak of dumdum fever affected hundreds of people in the region.
Early detection is crucial in the treatment of dumdum fever to prevent organ damage.
Researchers are studying the spread of dumdum fever in rural communities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leishmaniasis - infection caused by Leishmania parasites
  • Synonym: kala-azar - another term for visceral leishmaniasis

Etymology

Named after Dumdum, a suburb of Kolkata (Calcutta), India, where outbreaks were first described.
