rent-a-cop

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛnt ə ˌkɑp / (AmE), / ˈrɛnt ə ˌkɒp / (BrE)
(informal, derogatory) A security guard, especially one employed by a private firm or temporarily hired, and regarded as having little authority or competence.
(derogatory) private security guard with little authority
informal slang

Examples

The concert hired a rent-a-cop to patrol the entrance.
Some people don't take the authority of a rent-a-cop seriously.
After the incident, the mall replaced its rent-a-cops with professional security staff.

Synset Examples

Don't be fooled by the rent-a-cop; they have no real authority.
He joked about the rent-a-cop trying to enforce rules.
The mall's security is just a bunch of rent-a-cops.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: security guard - person employed to protect property or people

Etymology

Formed humorously from 'rent-a-' (indicating something for hire) + 'cop' (police officer, slang).
