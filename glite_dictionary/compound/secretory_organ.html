secretory organ

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈkriːtɔri ˈɔːrɡən / (AmE), / sɪˈkriːtəri ˈɔːɡən / (BrE)
an organ in the body that produces and releases substances, either through ducts or directly into the bloodstream
organ that releases substances

Examples

The pancreas is a secretory organ responsible for producing important digestive enzymes.
Damage to a secretory organ can disrupt the balance of hormones in the body.
The doctor explained how each secretory organ has a unique function in maintaining health.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gland - an organ in the body that secretes particular chemical substances

Etymology

From Latin 'secernere' (to separate) and Greek 'organon' (instrument, tool).
