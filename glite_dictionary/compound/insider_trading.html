insider trading

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪnˌsaɪdər ˈtreɪdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɪnsaɪdə ˈtreɪdɪŋ / (BrE)
the illegal practice of buying or selling stocks by someone who has access to confidential information not available to the public
illegal trading using confidential information
legal

Examples

The CEO was charged with insider trading after secretly selling shares before the company's poor earnings report was made public.
Strict laws are in place to detect and prevent insider trading in financial markets.
Allegations of insider trading damaged the firm's reputation and led to a lengthy legal investigation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: market manipulation - Intentional interference with free and fair operation of the market
  • Degree Lower: insider - A person within an organization with access to confidential information
  • Degree Higher: securities fraud - A broad category of illegal activities involving stocks and investments

Etymology

From 'insider', meaning someone within an organization, and 'trading', the act of buying and selling shares.
