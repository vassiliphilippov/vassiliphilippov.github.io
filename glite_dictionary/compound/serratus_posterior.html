serratus posterior

1. NOUN

/ səˈreɪɾəs pɑˈstɪriɚ / (AmE), / səˈreɪtəs pɒˈstɪərɪə / (BrE)
A skeletal muscle of the back that pulls the rib cage backward and downward.
muscle pulling rib cage back and down
medical

Examples

The serratus posterior assists in moving the ribs during respiration.
During deep breathing, the serratus posterior helps expand the thoracic cavity.
Injury to the serratus posterior can cause pain in the upper back.

Etymology

From Latin 'serratus' (saw-shaped) and 'posterior' (behind).
