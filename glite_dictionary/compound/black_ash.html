black ash

1. NOUN

/ blæk æʃ / (AmE), / blak aʃ / (BrE)
A vigorous North American tree (Fraxinus nigra) with dark brown heavy wood, whose leaves turn gold in autumn.
North American ash tree species

Examples

The furniture was crafted from solid black ash for durability and beauty.
Native Americans traditionally used black ash wood to make baskets.
In autumn, the leaves of the black ash tree turn a striking golden color.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ash - A type of deciduous tree in the genus Fraxinus
