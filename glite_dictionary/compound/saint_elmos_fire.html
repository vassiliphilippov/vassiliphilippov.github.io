Saint Elmo's fire

1. NOUN

/ seɪnt ˈɛlmoʊz ˈfaɪɚ / (AmE), / seɪnt ˈɛlməʊz ˈfaɪə / (BrE)
a weather phenomenon in which a visible blue or violet electrical discharge appears on pointed objects, caused by ionization of the surrounding air during thunderstorms.
weather phenomenon: blue electrical discharge

Examples

The sailors watched in awe as Saint Elmo's fire danced atop the ship's masts during the storm.
Pilots sometimes report sightings of Saint Elmo's fire on airplane wings while flying through thunderstorms.
The eerie glow of Saint Elmo's fire has inspired legends among those who travel at sea.

Etymology

Named after St. Elmo, the patron saint of sailors, who regarded the phenomenon as a sign of protection.
