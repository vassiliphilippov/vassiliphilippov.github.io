scrub beefwood

1. NOUN

/ skrʌb ˈbiːfˌwʊd / (AmE), / skrʌb ˈbiːfˌwʊd / (BrE)
a tree or tall shrub with shiny leaves and umbels of fragrant creamy-white flowers, yielding hard heavy reddish wood, commonly found in Australia
Australian tree with fragrant white flowers and reddish wood

Examples

The scrub beefwood is prized for its dense, reddish timber used in cabinetry.
Wildlife often seek shelter beneath the spreading branches of scrub beefwood in the Australian outback.
Fragrant flowers make scrub beefwood a notable feature in native plant gardens.
