hate mail

1. NOUN

/ heɪt meɪl / (AmE), / heɪt meɪl / (BrE)
letters or messages containing abusive, threatening, or hateful language, typically sent to upset or intimidate the recipient.
abusive or threatening messages sent to upset someone

Examples

The celebrity received a flood of hate mail after her controversial remarks.
He reported the hate mail he received to the police for further investigation.
After publishing her article, she was targeted with anonymous hate mail criticizing her views.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fan mail - supportive or admiring letters sent by fans
