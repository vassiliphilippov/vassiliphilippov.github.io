soda jerk

1. NOUN

/ ˈsoʊdə dʒɜrk / (AmE), / ˈsəʊdə dʒɜːk / (BrE)
a person, typically a young man, who serves drinks and ice cream at a soda fountain, especially in a drugstore or diner
person serving at a soda fountain
archaic american

Examples

My grandfather used to work as a soda jerk in a small-town drugstore during the 1950s.
The soda jerk wore a crisp white uniform and made the best milkshakes in the city.
Back in the day, every diner had a friendly soda jerk behind the counter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soda fountain - a counter where soft drinks and ice cream are served, especially in a drugstore

Etymology

From 'soda fountain' and the slang 'jerk', referring to the jerking motion of pulling the soda tap.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsoʊdə dʒɝk / (AmE), / ˈsəʊdə dʒɜːk / (BrE)
a person, typically in the past, who prepared and served soda drinks at a soda fountain or ice cream shop
person who serves soda at a soda fountain

Examples

In the 1950s, my grandfather worked as a soda jerk in a busy downtown drugstore.
The soda jerk expertly mixed root beer floats for the eager customers.
Young students often admired the soda jerk for his quick hands and friendliness.

Synset Examples

I used to chat with the soda jerk after school.

Etymology

From 'soda', referring to carbonated drinks, and 'jerk', from the jerking motion of operating the lever on a soda fountain.
