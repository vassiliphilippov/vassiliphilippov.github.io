first class

1. NOUN

/ ˌfɝst ˈklæs / (AmE), / ˌfɜːst ˈklɑːs / (BrE)
the most luxurious and expensive category of accommodations available on a ship, train, or aircraft
luxury travel accommodation category

Examples

He always travels first class on international flights to ensure comfort.
The tickets for first class on the train were much more expensive than standard class.
She was upgraded to first class as a reward for being a frequent flyer.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: business class - a travel class below first class but above economy
  • Degree Lower: economy class - the lowest-cost class of travel accommodation

/ fɝst klæs / (AmE), / fɜːst klɑːs / (BrE)
a category of mail service for letters, postcards, and sealed packages, usually given priority in delivery
priority category of mail service

Examples

You should send the documents by first class to make sure they arrive quickly.
Sending a birthday card in first class ensures prompt delivery within a few days.
Packages sent via first class mail are sealed and not open to inspection.

Related Terms
