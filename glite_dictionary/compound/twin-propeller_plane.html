twin-propeller plane

1. NOUN

/ ˌtwɪn prəˈpɛlɚ pleɪn / (AmE), / ˌtwɪn prəˈpɛlə pleɪn / (BrE)
an aircraft powered by two propellers, typically for enhanced stability and performance
aircraft with two propellers

Examples

The company purchased a new twin-propeller plane for regional flights between the islands.
Flying a twin-propeller plane requires additional training compared to a single-engine aircraft.
The storm made landing the twin-propeller plane particularly challenging.
