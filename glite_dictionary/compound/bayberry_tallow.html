bayberry tallow

1. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪˌbɛri ˈtæloʊ / (AmE), / ˈbeɪbəri ˈtæləʊ / (BrE)
a fragrant green wax obtained from the wax myrtle (bayberry) and used in making candles
green wax from bayberry for candles

Examples

Candlemakers often use bayberry tallow to create naturally scented candles.
Early settlers valued bayberry tallow for its pleasant aroma and clean-burning properties.
You can extract bayberry tallow from the berries of the wax myrtle shrub.
