stellar association

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɛlɚ əˌsoʊsiˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈstɛlə əˌsəʊsiˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
a large, loosely bound group of stars with similar spectral types and a common, relatively recent origin
loose group of similar stars with common origin

Examples

The newly discovered stellar association contains several massive, bright stars.
Astronomers study stellar associations to understand star formation and evolution.
A stellar association is less densely packed than an open cluster.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: star cluster - a grouping of stars bound by gravity

Etymology

From Latin 'stella' (star) and English 'association'.
