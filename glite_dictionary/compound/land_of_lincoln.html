Land of Lincoln

1. PROPN

/ ˈlænd əv ˈlɪŋkən / (AmE), / ˈlænd əv ˈlɪŋkən / (BrE)
a nickname for the U.S. state of Illinois, referring to Abraham Lincoln's association with the state
nickname for Illinois (U.S. state)

Examples

Many tourists visit the Land of Lincoln to explore sites related to Abraham Lincoln's life.
Illinois proudly bears the nickname Land of Lincoln on its license plates.
She grew up in the Land of Lincoln and often spoke about her Midwestern heritage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Illinois - a state in the midwestern United States

Etymology

Refers to Abraham Lincoln, who spent much of his life in Illinois, known as the 'Land of Lincoln.'
