occupation license

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːkjəˈpeɪʃən ˈlaɪsəns / (AmE), / ˌɒkjʊˈpeɪʃən ˈlaɪsəns / (BrE)
an official permission or document allowing a person to engage in a particular occupation or profession
license to pursue a profession
legal

Examples

In order to practice law, she had to obtain an occupation license from the regulatory body.
Many cities require an occupation license for small businesses to operate legally.
His occupation license was renewed after he completed the necessary training.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: license - official permission or permit to do something
