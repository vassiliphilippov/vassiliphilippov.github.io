velvet plant

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɛlvət plænt / (AmE), / ˈvɛlvɪt plɑːnt / (BrE)
any of various plants of the genus Verbascum that have large, usually woolly leaves and spikes of yellow, white, or purplish flowers
plant of the Verbascum genus with woolly leaves and spikes of flowers

Examples

The velvet plant is often grown in gardens for its striking flower spikes and soft leaves.
Many herbalists use parts of the velvet plant in traditional remedies.
We saw a field full of blooming velvet plants during our hike.

2. NOUN

/ ˈvɛlvət plænt / (AmE), / ˈvɛlvɪt plɑːnt / (BrE)
A tropical ornamental plant known for its attractive velvety leaves covered with violet-purple hairs, especially from Java.
ornamental plant with velvety purple-haired leaves

Examples

The velvet plant is popular among gardeners for its vividly colored, soft-textured leaves.
She placed a velvet plant on the windowsill to brighten up the room.
Botanists admire the unique purple hairs that cover the leaves of the velvet plant.
