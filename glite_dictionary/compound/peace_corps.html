Peace Corps

1. NOUN

/ pis kɔɹz / (AmE), / piːs kɔːz / (BrE)
an organization run by the United States government that sends volunteers to provide assistance and promote development in countries abroad
U.S. volunteer development agency
american

Examples

After graduating, she joined the Peace Corps and spent two years teaching English in rural Nepal.
The Peace Corps was established in 1961 to promote peace and friendship through volunteer work.
Many former Peace Corps volunteers say the experience changed their outlook on life.
