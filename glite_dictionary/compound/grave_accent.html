grave accent

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡreɪv ˌæk.sənt / (AmE), / ˈɡrɑːv ˌæk.sənt / (BrE)
a mark (`) placed over a vowel in some languages to indicate pronunciation, stress, or tone
a written mark (`) above a vowel

Examples

In French, the word 'père' contains a grave accent over the letter 'e'.
Use a grave accent on 'a' in the word 'à' to distinguish it from 'a'.
The grave accent is less common in English but appears in some borrowed words.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acute accent - a mark (´) used over a vowel to show a different pronunciation or stress
  • Synonym: circumflex - a mark (^) over a vowel, indicating a specific pronunciation or stress

Etymology

From French 'accent grave', meaning 'serious accent'.
