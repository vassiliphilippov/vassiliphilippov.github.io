tarnal tone

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɑrnəl toʊn / (AmE), / ˈtɑːnəl təʊn / (BrE)
An irritating or harsh tone of voice; a manner of speaking that annoys or grates on the listener.
irritating or harsh tone of voice
archaic

Examples

His tarnal tone grated on everyone's nerves during the meeting.
She couldn't stand his tarnal tone whenever they argued.
The tarnal tone in his complaint was impossible to ignore.

Synset Examples

He spoke in a tarnal tone that made everyone uncomfortable.

Etymology

From 'tarnal', a euphemism for 'eternal', historically used in exclamations, plus 'tone'.
