evolutionary trend

1. NOUN

/ ˌiːvəˈluːʃəˌnɛri trɛnd / (AmE), / ˌiːvəˈluːʃənəri trɛnd / (BrE)
a general direction of change observed in the evolution of a species or group over time
general direction of evolutionary change

Examples

The fossil record reveals an evolutionary trend toward larger body sizes in this lineage.
Scientists study evolutionary trends to understand how certain traits become more prevalent over time.
An evolutionary trend can sometimes be reversed when environmental conditions change.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: evolution - the process by which different kinds of living organisms develop and diversify from earlier forms
