tin foil

1. NOUN

/ tɪn ˈfɔɪl / (AmE), / tɪn ˈfɔɪl / (BrE)
a thin sheet of metal made chiefly from tin or an alloy of tin and lead, used especially for wrapping or covering items
thin sheet of tin used for wrapping

Examples

He carefully wrapped the leftovers in tin foil before putting them in the fridge.
In the past, chocolates were often packaged in tin foil to keep them fresh.
She shaped a piece of tin foil into a small ball for her craft project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aluminum foil - thin sheet of aluminum used for wrapping food etc.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɪn ˌfɔɪl / (AmE), / ˈtɪn ˌfɔɪl / (BrE)
a very thin sheet of metal, especially aluminum, used chiefly for wrapping food or cooking
thin sheet of metal for wrapping food

Examples

She wrapped the leftovers in tin foil before putting them in the fridge.
Cover the chicken with tin foil to keep it moist while baking.
He put a piece of tin foil over the dish to reheat it in the oven.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aluminum foil - thin sheet of aluminum used for wrapping food

Etymology

Originally referred to thin foil made from tin; now mostly aluminum but the old name persists.
