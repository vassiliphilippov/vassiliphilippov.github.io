out of court

1. ADV

/ aʊt əv kɔrt / (AmE), / aʊt əv kɔːt / (BrE)
(of a legal matter) settled or resolved without undergoing a trial or formal court proceedings.
settled without going to court
legal

Examples

The company decided to settle the dispute out of court to avoid lengthy proceedings.
They reached an agreement out of court and signed the necessary documents.
The case was resolved out of court, saving both parties considerable time and expense.

Synset Examples

He agreed to settle with the plaintiff out of court.
The paternity suit was settled out of court.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: in court - involving formal court proceedings or litigation
