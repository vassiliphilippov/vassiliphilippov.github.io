World Court

1. PROPN

/ wɜrld kɔrt / (AmE), / wɜːld kɔːt / (BrE)
The principal judicial body of the United Nations, established to resolve legal disputes between states and provide advisory opinions on international legal questions.
principal judicial body of the United Nations

Examples

The World Court ruled on the territorial dispute between the two countries.
Cases brought before the World Court often involve complex issues of international law.
Many nations recognize the authority of the World Court in resolving state conflicts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: International Court of Justice - official name for the World Court, the main judicial organ of the UN
