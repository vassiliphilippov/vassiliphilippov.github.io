Australian cockroach

1. NOUN

/ əˈstreɪliən ˈkɑːkˌroʊtʃ / (AmE), / ɒˈstreɪliən ˈkɒkˌrəʊtʃ / (BrE)
a large species of cockroach (Periplaneta australasiae), widely distributed in warm countries, recognized by its yellow-bordered thorax and wings
species of cockroach found in warm countries

Examples

We found an Australian cockroach under the kitchen sink last night.
The wings of the Australian cockroach have distinctive yellow edges.
Unlike other species, the Australian cockroach often lives outdoors in warm climates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cockroach - a nocturnal, fast-moving insect with a flattened body

Etymology

Named after Australia, where it was first described, plus 'cockroach.'
