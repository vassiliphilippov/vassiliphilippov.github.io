sea-coast

1. NOUN

/ ˈsiˌkoʊst / (AmE), / ˈsiːˌkəʊst / (BrE)
the land or shore along the edge of a sea or ocean
shore of a sea or ocean

Examples

We spent our summer vacation exploring the beautiful sea-coast of Cornwall.
Many fishing villages can be found along the Scottish sea-coast.
The storm damaged several homes situated near the sea-coast.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coast - the land beside the sea
  • Antonym: inland - situated away from the coast

Etymology

From 'sea' (Old English 'sæ') + 'coast' (from Old French 'coste', from Latin 'costa' meaning 'rib, side').
