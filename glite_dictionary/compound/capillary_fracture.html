capillary fracture

1. NOUN

/ kəˈpɪləri ˈfræk.tʃɚ / (AmE), / kəˈpɪl.əri ˈfræk.tʃə / (BrE)
a fracture in which the bone breaks along a very thin line with no separation of the fragments
very thin bone fracture without fragment separation
medical

Examples

The X-ray revealed a capillary fracture in her wrist after the minor fall.
Athletes are sometimes unaware of a capillary fracture because the pain can be very mild.
Doctors advised him to rest his foot due to a capillary fracture sustained during training.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hairline fracture - a very thin or narrow crack in the bone

Etymology

From 'capillary', referring to something very fine, and 'fracture', meaning a break.
