family Ciconiidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli sɪˌkoʊniˈaɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli sɪˌkəʊnɪˈiːdiː / (BrE)
A family of large wading birds known as storks, characterized by long legs and necks, found in warm regions worldwide.
the biological family of storks
medical

Examples

The family Ciconiidae includes several species of stork found across different continents.
Members of the family Ciconiidae are known for their impressive migrations.
Biologists study the family Ciconiidae to understand the diversity of storks and their habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stork - large wading bird with long legs and neck; member of the family Ciconiidae

Etymology

From scientific Latin 'Ciconiidae', from 'Ciconia', the type genus (stork), and '-idae', a suffix denoting animal families.
