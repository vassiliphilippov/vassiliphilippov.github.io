noble cane

1. NOUN

/ ˈnoʊbəl keɪn / (AmE), / ˈnəʊbəl keɪn / (BrE)
A type of sugarcane known for its highly developed qualities, characterized by large, juicy stalks, soft rinds, and high sugar content.
highly developed variety of sugarcane

Examples

The plantation specialized in growing noble cane to produce high-quality sugar.
Researchers have developed new hybrids based on the characteristics of noble cane.
Farmers prefer noble cane varieties for their increased yield and sweeter juice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sugarcane - a tall tropical plant whose juice is used to make sugar
