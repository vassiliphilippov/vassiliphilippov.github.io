tenor clef

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛnər klɛf / (AmE), / ˈtɛnə klɛf / (BrE)
a musical clef placing middle C on the fourth line of the staff, used mainly for cello, bassoon, or tenor horn music
musical clef with middle C on fourth line

Examples

The viola part was written in the tenor clef, making it a bit challenging for beginners.
Cellists often switch between bass and tenor clef in advanced orchestral music.
Learning to read tenor clef is essential for bassoon players as they progress.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alto clef - clef placing middle C on the third line of the staff
  • Antonym: bass clef - clef indicating F below middle C, used for lower-pitched instruments

Etymology

From 'tenor' (the voice part) and 'clef' (French for 'key'), as it was originally used for the tenor voice part.
