Podsnap moment

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑdˌsnæp ˈmoʊmənt / (AmE), / ˈpɒdˌsnæp ˈməʊmənt / (BrE)
A moment characterized by smug self-satisfaction or willful ignorance, often in reference to the attitudes of the character Podsnap from Charles Dickens' novel 'Our Mutual Friend'.
a moment of smug self-satisfaction or ignorance

Examples

During the interview, the politician had a real Podsnap moment, dismissing any criticism without a second thought.
Literary critics often refer to a Podsnap moment when discussing characters blinded by their own self-righteousness.
She recognized her own Podsnap moment only after her friends pointed out her lack of understanding.

Synset Examples

His genius was evident in every Podsnap moment.

Etymology

From the character Mr. Podsnap in Charles Dickens' 'Our Mutual Friend' (1865), famed for his self-satisfied and blinkered worldview.
