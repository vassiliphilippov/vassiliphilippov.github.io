periodic motion

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɪriˈɑdɪk ˈmoʊʃən / (AmE), / ˌpɪəriˈɒdɪk ˈməʊʃən / (BrE)
motion that repeats itself at regular intervals, with each cycle taking the same amount of time
motion repeating at regular intervals

Examples

A pendulum exhibits periodic motion as it swings back and forth.
The Earth’s rotation around its axis is an example of periodic motion.
Sound waves are created by the periodic motion of particles in a medium.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oscillation - regular movement back and forth, often in periodic motion
  • Antonym: random motion - motion with no regular or repeating pattern

Etymology

From 'periodic', ultimately from Greek 'periodos' (way around, cycle), and 'motion', from Latin 'motio'.
