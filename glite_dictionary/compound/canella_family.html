canella family

1. NOUN

/ kəˈnɛlə ˈfæməli / (AmE), / kəˈnɛlə ˈfamɪli / (BrE)
A family of aromatic tropical trees of the order Canellales, found in eastern Africa, Florida, and the West Indies, comprising the genus Canella.
family of tropical aromatic trees

Examples

The canella family includes several species valued for their fragrant bark.
Botanists have classified the spice tree under the canella family.
The canella family is known for its distinctive aromatic qualities.

Etymology

From New Latin Canellaceae, based on the genus Canella, from Latin 'canna' (reed).
