double reed

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʌbəl ˌrid / (AmE), / ˈdʌb(ə)l ˌriːd / (BrE)
a woodwind musical instrument, or the part it uses, that produces sound with two reeds joined together vibrating against each other
woodwind instrument or reed with two joined reeds

Examples

The oboe is a type of double reed instrument found in orchestras.
He learned how to make a double reed for his bassoon.
Musicians often debate the subtle differences in sound produced by each double reed.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: single reed - a woodwind instrument or mouthpiece using only one reed to produce sound

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʌb.əl ˈrid / (AmE), / ˈdʌb.əl ˈriːd / (BrE)
A pair of joined reeds that vibrate together to produce the sound in certain woodwind instruments, such as the oboe and bassoon.
type of reed in some woodwinds

Examples

The oboe is a double reed instrument with a distinct, penetrating sound.
Making a double reed by hand requires patience and skill.
Unlike the clarinet, the bassoon uses a double reed to produce its tone.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: single reed - a type of reed consisting of a single blade, used in instruments like the clarinet and saxophone
