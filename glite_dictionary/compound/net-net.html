net-net

1. ADV

/ nɛt nɛt / (AmE), / nɛt nɛt / (BrE)
used to indicate the overall outcome or conclusion after considering all aspects; on balance or in summary
overall result after all factors
informal

Examples

Net-net, this campaign increased our market share despite some setbacks.
He was late and over budget, but net-net the event was a hit.
We lost a client, but net-net, our overall profits grew this quarter.

Synset Examples

Net-net, the project was a success despite challenges.
The deal fell through, but net-net, we learned a lot.
Net-net, we saved time and resources on that project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: on balance - after considering all relevant factors
  • Synonym: all in all - taking everything into account

Etymology

From 'net' meaning the final amount after deductions, repeated for emphasis; originated in business jargon.
