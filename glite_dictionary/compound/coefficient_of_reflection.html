coefficient of reflection

1. NOUN

/ koʊˌɪfɪʃənt əv rɪˈflɛkʃən / (AmE), / kəʊˌɪfɪʃənt əv rɪˈflɛkʃən / (BrE)
the fraction of incident radiant energy that is reflected from a surface, often expressed as a decimal or percentage
fraction of energy reflected from a surface

Examples

The coefficient of reflection determines how much solar energy is bounced off the Earth's surface.
Metal surfaces usually have a high coefficient of reflection compared to darker materials.
Accurate measurements of the coefficient of reflection are essential in optical engineering.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: albedo - the proportion of the incident light or radiation that is reflected by a surface

Etymology

From Latin 'co-' (together), 'efficiens' (producing) and Latin 'reflectere' (to bend back).
