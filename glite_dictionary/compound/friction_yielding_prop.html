friction yielding prop

1. NOUN

/ ˈfrɪkʃən ˈjiːldɪŋ prɑp / (AmE), / ˈfrɪkʃən ˈjiːldɪŋ prɒp / (BrE)
a steel support column designed to yield under load by controlled friction between sliding surfaces or telescopic tubes, commonly used in mining or construction for controlled deformation under stress
a steel prop that yields via friction under load

Examples

The tunnel was supported by a friction yielding prop to absorb the pressure from the surrounding rock.
Engineers installed a new friction yielding prop in the mine shaft for improved safety.
A friction yielding prop can accommodate ground movement without sudden failure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yielding prop - a prop designed to deform to absorb stress
  • Antonym: rigid prop - a non-deformable supporting column
