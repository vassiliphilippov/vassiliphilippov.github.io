ethnic cleansing

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛθ.nɪk ˈklɛn.zɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɛθ.nɪk ˈklɛn.zɪŋ / (BrE)
The deliberate and systematic expulsion or killing of members of an ethnic or religious group from a particular area by another group.
mass expulsion or killing of an ethnic group

Examples

International courts have investigated several cases of ethnic cleansing in the region.
Reports confirmed that villages experienced systematic ethnic cleansing during the conflict.
The documentary examined the consequences of ethnic cleansing on displaced communities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: genocide - the deliberate killing of a large group of people, especially those of a particular nation or ethnic group

Etymology

Formed from 'ethnic' and 'cleansing'; first used in the late 20th century, notably during conflicts in the former Yugoslavia.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛθnɪk ˈklɛnzɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɛθnɪk ˈklɛnzɪŋ / (BrE)
The deliberate and systematic removal of an ethnic or religious group from a particular area by force or intimidation, often involving violence or persecution.
systematic expulsion of an ethnic group

Examples

The UN intervened to prevent further ethnic cleansing in the war-torn country.
Documentaries have exposed the horrors of ethnic cleansing committed during the conflict.
Leaders were held accountable for their roles in orchestrating ethnic cleansing.

Synset Examples

The report highlighted the ongoing issues of ethnic cleansing in the region.
The international community condemned the acts of ethnic cleansing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: genocide - deliberate killing of a large group, especially of a particular ethnicity or nation
  • Antonym: integration - the process of combining or incorporating different groups
