hole-in-the-wall

1. NOUN

/ ˈhoʊl ɪn ðə wɔl / (AmE), / ˈhəʊl ɪn ðə wɔːl / (BrE)
A small, modest, and often inconspicuous place, especially a restaurant or store, that may be out of the way or without any distinctive features.
small, inconspicuous place; modest shop or restaurant

Examples

My favorite cafe is a little hole-in-the-wall that serves amazing coffee.
We discovered a hole-in-the-wall bakery tucked away in a quiet alley.
Although it looks like a hole-in-the-wall, the food at that restaurant is delicious.

Synset Examples

his office was a hole-in-the-wall

2. NOUN

/ ˌhoʊl ɪn ðə ˈwɔl / (AmE), / ˌhəʊl ɪn ðə ˈwɔːl / (BrE)
A small, modest, and often inconspicuous establishment, especially a restaurant or cafe, that is typically known for its quality or uniqueness despite lacking external appeal.
small, inconspicuous restaurant or cafe

Examples

Last night we ate dinner at a hole-in-the-wall that served incredible tacos.
You'd never expect such amazing food from this little hole-in-the-wall near the train station.
They prefer dining at a cozy hole-in-the-wall rather than expensive restaurants.

Synset Examples

The hole-in-the-wall cafe serves the best coffee.
I love discovering new hole-in-the-wall eateries.
We found a great hole-in-the-wall restaurant downtown.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dive - an informal, unpretentious bar or restaurant
  • Synonym: eatery - a place where people can eat, especially a small, informal restaurant

Etymology

Originally referring to a literal small opening in a wall, the phrase evolved to describe modest establishments with an unremarkable exterior.
