ethnic group

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛθ.nɪk ɡruːp / (AmE), / ˈɛθ.nɪk ɡruːp / (BrE)
A community of people who share a common cultural background, ancestry, or heritage, often distinguished by language, religion, or traditions.
group sharing common culture or ancestry

Examples

The census collects information about each citizen's ethnic group to help support diverse communities.
In her research, she studied the traditions of an indigenous ethnic group in South America.
Members of the ethnic group celebrated their cultural heritage with a festival each year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: race - group of people identified as distinct based on physical or social qualities
  • Synonym: nationality - legal or emotional identification with a specific nation

Etymology

From 'ethnic', relating to a group with shared cultural traditions, combined with 'group'.
