family Gobiidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ɡoʊˈbaɪɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli ɡəʊˈbɪɪdiː / (BrE)
A large family of small, often bottom-dwelling fish known as gobies, found mainly in marine and brackish waters worldwide.
taxonomic family of goby fishes

Examples

The family Gobiidae includes over 2,000 species of goby fish worldwide.
Researchers discovered a new species within the family Gobiidae in Indonesian coastal waters.
Members of the family Gobiidae are known for their fused pelvic fins forming a suction cup.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: goby - a small fish belonging to the family Gobiidae

Etymology

From modern Latin 'Gobiidae', formed from the genus 'Gobius' + '-idae' (family suffix).
