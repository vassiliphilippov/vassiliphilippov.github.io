psychotropic agent

1. NOUN

/ saɪ.kəˈtroʊ.pɪk ˈeɪ.dʒənt / (AmE), / saɪ.kəˈtrɒ.pɪk ˈeɪ.dʒənt / (BrE)
a chemical substance that affects a person's mental state or consciousness
substance affecting the mind or consciousness
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that a psychotropic agent could help stabilize her mood.
Research on the long-term effects of each psychotropic agent is still ongoing.
Not all medications that act on the brain are classified as a psychotropic agent.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: psychoactive drug - a drug that affects the mind
  • Degree Lower: antipsychotic - drug used to treat psychosis; an example of psychotropic agent
  • Degree Higher: hallucinogen - drug that induces hallucinations; a type of psychotropic agent with a stronger effect

Etymology

From Greek psycho- (mind) + tropikos (turning) + agent (from Latin agent-, agere 'to do, act').
