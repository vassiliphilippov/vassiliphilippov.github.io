family Giraffidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli dʒɪˈræfəˌdi / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli dʒɪˈræfədiː / (BrE)
A family of large African mammals that includes giraffes and okapis.
biological family including giraffes and okapis

Examples

The family Giraffidae consists of just two living species: the giraffe and the okapi.
Fossils show that members of the family Giraffidae were once much more diverse than they are today.
Research on the family Giraffidae helps scientists understand the evolution of long necks in animals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Giraffidae - the family of giraffes and okapis

Etymology

From modern Latin 'Giraffidae', the taxonomic family name derived from 'Giraffa' (the genus name of giraffes) plus '-idae' (a suffix for animal families).
