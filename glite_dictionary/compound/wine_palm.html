wine palm

1. NOUN

/ waɪn pɑm / (AmE), / waɪn pɑːm / (BrE)
a tall fan palm native to Africa, India, and Malaysia, valued for its hard wood, sweet sap used for palm wine and sugar, and leaves used in thatching and weaving
palm tree yielding sap for palm wine and sugar

Examples

The wine palm is tapped by locals to produce a sweet sap that is fermented into palm wine.
Craftsmen wove mats and baskets using leaves from the wine palm.
The hard wood of the wine palm is prized for its strength and durability.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: palm wine - an alcoholic beverage made from the fermented sap of various palms
  • Synonym: fan palm - a grouping of palms with fan-shaped leaves

2. NOUN

/ waɪn pɑm / (AmE), / waɪn pɑːm / (BrE)
a type of palm tree, native to India and the Malay Peninsula, whose sap is used to make brown sugar (jaggery) and whose trunk pith yields sago
palm tree used for sap and sago

Examples

The sap from the wine palm is collected to produce sweet jaggery in local communities.
Farmers rely on the wine palm for both food products and building materials.
The wine palms along the river provide shade and important resources for the villagers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fishtail palm - palm tree of Asia, source of jaggery and sago
