arctic skua

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑrktɪk ˈskuːə / (AmE), / ˈɑːktɪk ˈskjuːə / (BrE)
a medium-sized seabird of the skua family (Stercorariidae), found in Arctic and subarctic regions, known for aggressive behavior towards other birds while feeding
a medium-sized Arctic seabird

Examples

The arctic skua often chases other seabirds to steal their catch.
We watched an arctic skua swoop low over the water in the early morning light.
Several arctic skuas were spotted nesting along the rugged coastline.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jaeger - another name for certain skuas, especially in North America

Etymology

From "Arctic" (region around the North Pole) + "skua", from Faroese *skúgvur* (skua).
