Filipino time

1. NOUN

/ fɪˈliːpinoʊ taɪm / (AmE), / fɪˈliːpiːnəʊ taɪm / (BrE)
the tendency, especially among Filipinos or in Filipino culture, to be late or have a relaxed attitude toward punctuality
tendency to be late; relaxed punctuality

Examples

The meeting didn't start until thirty minutes after the scheduled time, which was just Filipino time.
She told her guests to arrive on Filipino time so that no one would be too early.
Joking about Filipino time, he assured everyone there was no need to rush.

Synset Examples

He was late again, blaming it on Filipino time as usual.
She jokingly told her friends to come on Filipino time to avoid waiting.
The party started at 7, but typical Filipino time had everyone arriving by 8.

Etymology

From English 'Filipino' (relating to the Philippines) + 'time.' Stereotypically refers to Filipino cultural norms regarding punctuality.
