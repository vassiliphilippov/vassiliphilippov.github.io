saccharic acid

1. NOUN

/ səˈkærɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / səˈkærɪk ˈasɪd / (BrE)
a white, crystalline dicarboxylic acid obtained by the oxidation of sugars or starches, used in chemical analysis and industry
dicarboxylic acid from oxidized sugars
medical

Examples

The chemist synthesized saccharic acid from glucose during the experiment.
Industrially, saccharic acid is sometimes used for its chelating properties.
Researchers studied the reactions of saccharic acid with various metal ions.

Etymology

From Latin *saccharum* (sugar) and '-ic' (adjective suffix), referring to its origin from sugar.
