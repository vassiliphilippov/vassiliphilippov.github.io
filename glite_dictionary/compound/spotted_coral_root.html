spotted coral root

1. NOUN

/ ˈspɑt̬ɪd ˈkɔrəl ruːt / (AmE), / ˈspɒtɪd ˈkɒrəl ruːt / (BrE)
a North American orchid with leafless yellowish, reddish, or purplish-brown stems and loose clusters of flowers that have white, purple-spotted lips (Corallorhiza maculata)
a North American orchid species

Examples

The spotted coral root can be found in shady forests across much of North America.
Researchers studied the distribution patterns of spotted coral root in its natural habitat.
Because it lacks chlorophyll, the spotted coral root relies on fungi for nourishment.
