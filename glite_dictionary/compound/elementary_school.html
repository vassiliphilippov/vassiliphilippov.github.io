elementary school

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛləˈmɛn.tə.ri skul / (AmE), / ˌɛl.ɪˈmɛn.təri skuːl / (BrE)
A school for children between the ages of about five and eleven, typically covering the first six or eight grades.
school for young children (first grades)

Examples

My daughter will start elementary school next fall when she turns six.
The city is building a new elementary school to accommodate the growing population.
Children usually learn basic math and reading skills in elementary school.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: primary school - a school for children, especially in the first grades; mainly British
