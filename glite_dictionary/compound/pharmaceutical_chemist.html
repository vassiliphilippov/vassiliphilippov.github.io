pharmaceutical chemist

1. NOUN

/ fəˌrmasuːtɪkəl ˈkɛmɪst / (AmE), / fəˌmɑːsjuːtɪkəl ˈkɛmɪst / (BrE)
a person trained in the science of drugs, particularly their composition, uses, and effects
expert in drug composition and effects

Examples

The company hired a pharmaceutical chemist to develop safer painkillers.
As a pharmaceutical chemist, she researches new compounds for disease treatment.
You need years of education to become a successful pharmaceutical chemist.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pharmacist - a person licensed to prepare and dispense drugs

Etymology

From 'pharmaceutical' (relating to pharmacy) and 'chemist' (expert in chemistry).
