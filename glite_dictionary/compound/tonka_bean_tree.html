tonka bean tree

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɑŋ.kə ˌbiːn ˌtriː / (AmE), / ˈtɒŋ.kə ˌbiːn ˌtriː / (BrE)
a tall tropical South American tree (Dipteryx odorata) that produces pulpy egg-shaped pods containing fragrant black almond-shaped seeds used for flavoring
South American tree; source of tonka beans

Examples

The tonka bean tree thrives in the tropical rainforests of South America.
Many perfumers use seeds from the tonka bean tree for their distinctive sweet aroma.
Harvesting the tonka bean tree requires patience, as its pods are picked by hand.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tonka bean - the fragrant seed of the tonka bean tree used for flavoring

Etymology

From Galibi 'tonka', via French 'tonka'.
