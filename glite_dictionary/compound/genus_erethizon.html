genus Erethizon

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ɛˈrɛθ.ɪ.zɒn / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ɛˈrɛθ.ɪ.zɒn / (BrE)
A genus of large rodents in the family Erethizontidae, which includes the North American porcupine.
a rodent genus including the North American porcupine

Examples

The genus Erethizon contains only the North American porcupine as its living species.
Scientists classified the fossil as belonging to the genus Erethizon based on its skull structure.
Unlike other porcupines, members of the genus Erethizon are found in North America.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Erethizontidae - the family of New World porcupines
  • Synonym: porcupine - a rodent with sharp spines or quills

Etymology

From New Latin 'Erethizon', the type genus of the family Erethizontidae, based on Indigenous Algonquian roots.
