half-wit

1. NOUN

/ ˈhæfˌwɪt / (AmE), / ˈhɑːfˌwɪt / (BrE)
An offensive term for a person regarded as stupid or lacking intelligence.
stupid or unintelligent person (offensive)
offensive

Examples

Don't be such a half-wit and read the instructions before you start.
The movie portrayed the villain as a bumbling half-wit who couldn't get anything right.
Calling someone a half-wit is rude and likely to offend them.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: idiot - person of very low intelligence (offensive)
  • Antonym: genius - person of exceptional intelligence

Etymology

Formed from 'half' and 'wit', meaning half (not enough) mental capacity; wit meaning intelligence or mental sharpness.
