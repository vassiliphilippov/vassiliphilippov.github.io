superorder Ratitae

1. NOUN

/ ˌsuː.pərˈɔːr.dɚ ræˈtɪti / (AmE), / ˌsuː.pəˈɔː.də ræˈtɪti / (BrE)
a former taxonomic category that included all ratite bird orders, such as ostriches, emus, and kiwis
former taxonomic group of ratite birds

Examples

Until the late twentieth century, the superorder Ratitae grouped all flightless ratite birds together.
Recent genetic studies have challenged the classification of birds in the superorder Ratitae.
In older zoological texts, you may still encounter references to the superorder Ratitae.

Etymology

From Latin 'ratis' (raft or boat, referring to the flat breastbone), used in scientific naming.
