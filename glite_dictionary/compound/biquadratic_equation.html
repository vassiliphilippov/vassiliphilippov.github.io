biquadratic equation

1. NOUN

/ ˌbaɪkwɑːˈdrætɪk ɪˈkweɪʒən / (AmE), / ˌbaɪkwɒˈdrætɪk ɪˈkweɪʒən / (BrE)
An algebraic equation of the fourth degree, especially one in which the highest power of the unknown is four.
equation of degree four

Examples

The biquadratic equation can be solved using specialized algebraic methods.
She encountered a biquadratic equation in her advanced mathematics course.
Many physical phenomena can be modeled by a biquadratic equation when describing certain systems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quartic equation - an equation of the fourth degree, another term for biquadratic equation

Etymology

From biquadratic (from Latin 'bi-' meaning 'two' and 'quadratus' meaning 'squared', thus 'squared twice') + equation.
