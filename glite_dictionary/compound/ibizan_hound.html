Ibizan hound

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈbizən haʊnd / (AmE), / ɪˈbiːzən haʊnd / (BrE)
a breed of slender, agile, medium-sized hound, originally from the Balearic Islands, known for its graceful build and keen hunting abilities
breed of slender, agile hound from the Balearic Islands

Examples

The Ibizan hound is highly valued for its speed and agility in hunting small game.
Her family adopted an Ibizan hound from a rescue shelter last year.
With its distinctive ears and lean body, the Ibizan hound stands out at dog shows.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: greyhound - a slender, fast hunting dog breed

Etymology

Named after Ibiza, one of the Balearic Islands in Spain.
