Abu Nidal Organization

1. NOUN

/ əˌbu niˈdɑl ˌɔrɡənəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / əˌbuː nɪˈdɑːl ˌɔːɡənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
a Palestinian militant group that broke away from the PLO in 1974 and was responsible for numerous terrorist attacks internationally
Palestinian militant group, active 1970s–90s

Examples

The Abu Nidal Organization was responsible for a number of high-profile attacks across Europe in the 1980s.
Several countries designated the Abu Nidal Organization as a terrorist group due to its violent activities.
Intelligence agencies closely monitored the movements of the Abu Nidal Organization during its most active years.

Synset Examples

in the 1980s the Fatah-RC was considered the most dangerous and murderous Palestinian terror group

Etymology

Named after its founder, Sabri Khalil al-Banna, known as Abu Nidal.
