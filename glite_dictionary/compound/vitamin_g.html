vitamin G

1. NOUN

/ ˈvaɪt̬əmɪn dʒi / (AmE), / ˈvɪtəmɪn dʒi / (BrE)
an older name for riboflavin (vitamin B2), a vitamin that prevents skin lesions and weight loss
old term for riboflavin (vitamin B2)

Examples

Early studies showed that vitamin G deficiency in animals resulted in skin problems.
The food label still mentioned vitamin G, though it now lists riboflavin in parentheses.
Before vitamins were fully understood, scientists referred to riboflavin as vitamin G.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: riboflavin - a water-soluble B vitamin, also known as vitamin B2

Etymology

So named when vitamins were identified by letters before being chemically characterized; now known as riboflavin (vitamin B2).
