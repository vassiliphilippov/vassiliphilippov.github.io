Fibonacci sequence

1. NOUN

/ fɪˈbɑnətʃi ˈsiːkwəns / (AmE), / fɪˈbɒnətʃi ˈsiːkwəns / (BrE)
a sequence of numbers where each number is the sum of the two preceding numbers, starting with 0 and 1
mathematical sequence where each term is the sum of the previous two

Examples

The Fibonacci sequence is often observed in the arrangement of leaves and flowers in nature.
She programmed an algorithm to generate the first ten terms of the Fibonacci sequence.
The ratio of successive numbers in the Fibonacci sequence approximates the golden ratio.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: arithmetic progression - sequence of numbers each differing from the previous by a fixed amount

Etymology

Named after Leonardo Fibonacci (c. 1170–c. 1250), an Italian mathematician.
