lip synchronisation

1. NOUN

/ lɪp ˌsɪŋkrənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / lɪp ˌsɪŋkrənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of matching recorded speech with the lip movements of a speaker on screen, so that they appear to be perfectly in sync
matching speech to lip movements

Examples

With perfect lip synchronisation, the audience couldn't tell that the dialogue had been dubbed.
Animators pay close attention to lip synchronisation to make characters look more realistic.
Poor lip synchronisation in the movie was distracting, as the voices didn't match the actors' mouths.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lip sync - the process of matching a person's lips with spoken or sung words
  • Synonym: dub - to provide a new soundtrack for a film, especially in a different language

Etymology

From 'lip' + 'synchronisation', the action of synchronizing (from Greek 'syn-' together + 'khronos' time).
