white-man's foot

1. NOUN

/ ˈwaɪt mənz fʊt / (AmE), / ˈwaɪt mænz fʊt / (BrE)
A common European plantain (Plantago major) naturalized worldwide, often considered a troublesome weed.
a common weedy plantain (Plantago major)

Examples

The field was covered in white-man's foot, making it difficult for crops to grow.
Gardeners often try to remove white-man's foot because it spreads so quickly.
Native species sometimes struggle to compete with white-man's foot in disturbed soils.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plantain - a plant of the genus Plantago, including Plantago major and related species

Etymology

Refers to the widespread growth of the plant in places frequented or inhabited by European settlers, likened to their footsteps.
