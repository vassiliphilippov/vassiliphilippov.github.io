spinning frame

1. NOUN

/ ˈspɪnɪŋ freɪm / (AmE), / ˈspɪnɪŋ freɪm / (BrE)
a machine that draws out, twists, and winds fibers into yarn, used in textile manufacturing
machine for spinning yarn

Examples

The invention of the spinning frame revolutionized the textile industry in the eighteenth century.
Modern textile mills still use advanced versions of the spinning frame to produce fine yarn.
Richard Arkwright's spinning frame greatly increased yarn production efficiency.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spinning jenny - early spinning machine for textile fibers
  • Synonym: spinning mule - spinning machine combining features of earlier machines to spin yarn
