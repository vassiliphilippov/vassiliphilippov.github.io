circumbendibus approach

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɜrkəmˈbɛndɪbəs əˈproʊtʃ / (AmE), / ˌsɜːkəmˈbɛndɪbəs əˈprəʊtʃ / (BrE)
an indirect or roundabout way of doing something; a method that avoids a straightforward course
indirect or roundabout method
archaic

Examples

The politician used a circumbendibus approach to avoid giving a clear answer.
Her circumbendibus approach frustrated those who wanted a direct response.
Through a circumbendibus approach, the company sidestepped the regulation.

Synset Examples

Using a circumbendibus approach, he evaded the question completely.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: circumlocution - the use of many words when fewer would do, especially to be vague or evasive
  • Antonym: direct approach - a straightforward or honest method

Etymology

Circumbendibus from Latin 'circum' (around) + English 'bend', referring to going around rather than directly through.
