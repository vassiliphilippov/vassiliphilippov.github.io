Syrian bear

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪriən bɛr / (AmE), / ˈsɪəriən beə / (BrE)
A yellowish-grey subspecies of the brown bear (Ursus arctos syriacus) native to parts of the Middle East, including Syria, Turkey, and Iran.
subspecies of brown bear from the Middle East

Examples

The Syrian bear is now extremely rare in much of its original habitat due to hunting and habitat loss.
A Syrian bear at the zoo attracted attention for its pale, almost yellowish fur.
Researchers are studying the migration patterns of the Syrian bear in northern Iran.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brown bear - large bear species, Ursus arctos
