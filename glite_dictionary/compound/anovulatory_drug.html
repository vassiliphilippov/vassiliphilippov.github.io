anovulatory drug

1. NOUN

/ ˌænoʊˈvɑljəˌtɔri drʌɡ / (AmE), / ˌænəˈvɒljʊləˌtʌri drʌɡ / (BrE)
A contraceptive medication, typically in pill form, containing estrogen and progestin that inhibits ovulation to prevent pregnancy.
contraceptive pill inhibiting ovulation
medical

Examples

Many women use an anovulatory drug as their preferred method of birth control.
Your doctor may prescribe an anovulatory drug if you need hormonal contraception.
The anovulatory drug works by preventing the eggs from being released during your menstrual cycle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contraceptive pill - oral medication to prevent pregnancy
  • Antonym: ovulation - release of an egg from the ovary

Etymology

From Latin 'an-' (not) + 'ovulum' (small egg) + '-atory', referring to preventing egg release.
