troop movement

1. NOUN

/ truːp ˈmuːvmənt / (AmE), / truːp ˈmuːvmənt / (BrE)
the movement of military units or soldiers from one location to another, especially as part of a planned operation or strategy
movement of military units

Examples

The general planned a troop movement at dawn to surprise the enemy.
Satellite images revealed unusual troop movements near the border.
Successful troop movement depends on efficient communication and coordination.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deployment - the movement of troops or equipment to a place for military action
