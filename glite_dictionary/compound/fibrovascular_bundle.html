fibrovascular bundle

1. NOUN

/ ˌfaɪbroʊˈvæskjələr ˈbʌndəl / (AmE), / ˌfɪbrəʊˈvæskjʊlə ˈbʌndəl / (BrE)
a strand of tissue in the stems and leaves of higher plants, composed primarily of xylem and phloem, that conducts water, nutrients, and provides structural support
vascular tissue strand in plants

Examples

The fibrovascular bundle is easily visible in cross-sections of young plant stems.
Each leaf typically contains at least one fibrovascular bundle to transport water and nutrients.
Damage to the fibrovascular bundle can disrupt the plant's ability to move essential substances.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: xylem - the tissue in plants that conducts water
  • Synonym: phloem - the tissue in plants that transports nutrients

Etymology

From Latin 'fibra' (fiber) + 'vascularis' (relating to vessels).
