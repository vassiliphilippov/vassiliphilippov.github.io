to drive like a hoon

1. VERB

/ draɪv laɪk ə hun / (AmE), / draɪv laɪk ə huːn / (BrE)
to drive in a reckless, aggressive, or dangerous manner, especially in a way that endangers others
drive recklessly or aggressively
informal british

Examples

He was fined after he drove like a hoon through the quiet neighborhood.
Teenagers sometimes drive like a hoon when showing off to their friends.
After the party, she regretted driving like a hoon on the way home.

Synset Examples

He drove like a hoon through the streets.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: speed - to drive very fast, often excessively so

Etymology

From Australian/New Zealand slang ‘hoon’ (a loutish or reckless driver), probably of unknown origin.
