fence line

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɛns laɪn / (AmE), / ˈfɛns laɪn / (BrE)
A boundary marked by or along a fence, often marking property limits or divisions between areas of land.
boundary created by a fence

Examples

The cattle broke through the fence line and wandered onto the neighbor's property.
Wildflowers were growing all along the fence line at the edge of the field.
They spent the afternoon repairing the old fence line to keep the sheep contained.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boundary line - a line marking the limit of an area; border
