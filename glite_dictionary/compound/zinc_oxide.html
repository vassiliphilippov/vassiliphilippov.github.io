zinc oxide

1. NOUN

/ zɪŋk ˈɑksaɪd / (AmE), / zɪŋk ˈɒksaɪd / (BrE)
a white, powdery compound of zinc and oxygen, used as a pigment and in various products such as cosmetics and ointments
white powder used in cosmetics and ointments

Examples

She applied a cream containing zinc oxide to protect her skin from sunburn.
Many sunscreens list zinc oxide as a key active ingredient for UV protection.
The artist mixed zinc oxide into her paint to achieve a bright white color.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zinc - a bluish-white metallic element
  • Synonym: oxide - a compound of oxygen with another element

Etymology

From zinc, the metal, and oxide, denoting a compound of oxygen.
