trace italienne

1. NOUN

/ ˌtreɪs iˌtælˈjɛn / (AmE), / ˌtreɪs iˌtælˈjɛn / (BrE)
A style of fortification featuring many projecting triangular bastions, designed to provide overlapping fields of fire, first developed in Italy in the mid-15th century.
fortification style with triangular bastions

Examples

The fortress was rebuilt in the trace italienne style to withstand artillery attacks.
Architects adopted the trace italienne system throughout Europe during the Renaissance.
A well-preserved trace italienne can still be seen surrounding the old city.

Synset Examples

Star forts were employed by Michelangelo in the defensive earthworks of Florence, and refined in the sixteenth century by Baldassare Peruzzi and Scamozzi.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: star fort - A fort with a star-shaped plan, typically using trace italienne design principles

Etymology

From French, literally 'Italian outline,' referring to its Italian origin.
