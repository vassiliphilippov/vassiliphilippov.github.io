moon curser

1. NOUN

/ mun ˈkɜr.sɚ / (AmE), / muːn ˈkɜː.sə / (BrE)
a smuggler; a person who illegally imports or exports goods without paying duties on them
a smuggler who avoids paying duties
archaic

Examples

The moon curser made his way across the border under cover of darkness.
In old tales, a moon curser was feared more by customs agents than pirates.
They said the coast was haunted by a notorious moon curser known for his clever tricks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smuggler - someone who illegally moves goods to avoid taxes or bans

Etymology

From 'moon' and 'curser', suggesting a smuggler operating at night and cursing the moon for revealing them.
