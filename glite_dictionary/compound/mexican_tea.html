Mexican tea

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛk.sɪ.kən tiː / (AmE), / ˈmɛk.sɪ.kən tiː / (BrE)
A Eurasian aromatic plant (Chenopodium ambrosioides) with oak-shaped leaves and many yellow-green flowers, naturalized in North America.
aromatic Eurasian plant, Chenopodium ambrosioides

Examples

The herb known as Mexican tea is often used in traditional medicine.
Gardeners warn that Mexican tea can become invasive if left unchecked.
Chefs occasionally use Mexican tea leaves to flavor certain dishes.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɛksɪkən ti / (AmE), / ˈmɛksɪkən tiː / (BrE)
a strong-smelling tropical American plant (Chenopodium ambrosioides) of the amaranth family, used in traditional medicine and cooking
a pungent tropical American plant

Examples

Many traditional dishes use Mexican tea for its pungent flavor.
Gardeners often grow Mexican tea as a natural pest repellent.
Some herbal remedies feature Mexican tea for its medicinal properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: epazote - another common name for Chenopodium ambrosioides, especially in Mexican cuisine
