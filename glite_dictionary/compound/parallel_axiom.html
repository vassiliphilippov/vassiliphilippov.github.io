parallel axiom

1. NOUN

/ ˈpærəˌlɛl ˈæksiəm / (AmE), / ˈparəlɛl ˈaksɪəm / (BrE)
the axiom in geometry stating that, given any straight line and a point not on it, there is exactly one straight line through the point that does not intersect the original line (is parallel to it)
geometric axiom about parallels

Examples

The parallel axiom is fundamental in Euclidean geometry and distinguishes it from non-Euclidean systems.
Many attempts were made to prove the parallel axiom from other axioms, but all failed.
Lobachevsky developed a geometry that does not require the parallel axiom to hold.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Euclid's fifth postulate - another name for the parallel axiom in Euclidean geometry
