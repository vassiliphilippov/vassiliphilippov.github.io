gender identity

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɛndɚ aɪˈdɛntəti / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛndə aɪˈdɛntɪti / (BrE)
a person's internal sense and personal experience of their own gender, which may or may not correspond with the sex assigned at birth
a person's personal sense of gender

Examples

Many people find it difficult to discuss their gender identity with family members.
Schools are working to create environments that respect every student's gender identity.
Her gender identity does not necessarily match her biological sex.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sexual orientation - a person's sexual identity in relation to the gender to which they are attracted

Etymology

Formed from 'gender' (from Latin 'genus', kind or type) and 'identity' (from Latin 'identitas').

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɛndɚ aɪˈdɛntəti / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛndə aɪˈdɛntɪti / (BrE)
a person's internal sense of their own gender, which may or may not correspond with the sex assigned at birth
a person's internal sense of gender

Examples

Issues of gender identity are increasingly being discussed in schools and workplaces.
She began to explore her gender identity during her teenage years.
Some people feel their gender identity does not match their biological sex.

Synset Examples

Transmania has sparked conversations about gender identity.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sexual orientation - a person's emotional, romantic, or sexual attraction to others

Etymology

Formed from 'gender' (from Middle English, from Old French, from Latin 'genus') and 'identity' (from Latin 'identitas', from 'idem' meaning 'the same').
