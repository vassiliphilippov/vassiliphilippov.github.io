black cohosh

1. NOUN

/ blæk ˈkoʊ.hɑʃ / (AmE), / blæk ˈkəʊ.hɒʃ / (BrE)
a tall North American plant (Actaea racemosa) of the buttercup family, with small white flowers and used in herbal medicine
a North American plant used in herbal medicine

Examples

Many people use black cohosh supplements to relieve symptoms of menopause.
The forest was filled with wild black cohosh blooming in the late spring.
Researchers are studying the potential health benefits of black cohosh in modern medicine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bugbane - a related group of plants in the Actaea genus

Etymology

Cohosh from an Algonquian word meaning 'rough', likely describing the plant's root.
