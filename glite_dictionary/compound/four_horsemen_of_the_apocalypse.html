Four Horsemen of the Apocalypse

1. NOUN

/ fɔr ˈhɔrsmən əv ði əˈpɑkəlɪps / (AmE), / fɔː ˈhɔːsmən əv ði əˈpɒkəlɪps / (BrE)
the four figures described in the Book of Revelation in the Bible, symbolizing conquest, war, famine, and death, often representing impending doom or disaster
Biblical figures representing doom or disaster

Examples

The ominous arrival of the Four Horsemen of the Apocalypse is depicted in many famous paintings.
Many historians have drawn parallels between global crises and the Four Horsemen of the Apocalypse.
The film uses the imagery of the Four Horsemen of the Apocalypse to symbolize the end of an era.

Synset Examples

The artist depicted the Four Horsemen of the Apocalypse in his painting.
The Four Horsemen of the Apocalypse symbolize conquest.
War, famine, and plague are among the Four Horsemen of the Apocalypse.

Etymology

From the Book of Revelation in the Christian Bible.
