genus Cygnus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˈsɪɡnəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈsɪɡnəs / (BrE)
The genus of birds in the family Anatidae that includes all swans.
genus of birds including swans

Examples

The genus Cygnus includes all true swans found across the world.
Recent studies have revealed several new species within the genus Cygnus.
All members of the genus Cygnus are notable for their graceful appearance and large size.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swan - large waterbird with a long neck of the genus Cygnus

Etymology

From Latin 'Cygnus' (swan), used in scientific classification.
