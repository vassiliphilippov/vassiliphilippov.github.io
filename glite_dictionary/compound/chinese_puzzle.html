Chinese puzzle

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃaɪˌniz ˈpʌzəl / (AmE), / ˈtʃaɪniːz ˈpʌzl̩ / (BrE)
an intricate or ingenious puzzle, often consisting of boxes or compartments within one another, difficult to open or solve
complex, ingenious puzzle, often with hidden compartments

Examples

The old chest was a Chinese puzzle with secret drawers and hidden locks.
To solve the Chinese puzzle, she had to figure out the precise sequence of moves.
His explanation was as complicated as a Chinese puzzle, leaving everyone confused.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: puzzle box - a box that can only be opened by solving a complex mechanism
  • Synonym: brain-teaser - a problem or puzzle that requires thought to solve

Etymology

Named for intricate Chinese-made puzzles popular in the 19th century, noted for their complexity.
