five-star admiral

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪv stɑr ædˈmɪrəl / (AmE), / ˈfaɪv stɑː ˈædmɪrəl / (BrE)
an admiral of the highest rank in the navy, denoted by five stars, used especially in the United States Navy during wartime
admiral of the highest naval rank (five stars)

Examples

Fleet Admiral Chester Nimitz was promoted to the rank of five-star admiral during World War II.
Only a select few officers in history have ever attained the title of five-star admiral.
The position of five-star admiral was created to establish seniority over allied officers.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: admiral - a high-ranking naval officer

Etymology

Derived from the US military ranking system, where five stars denote the highest grade of admiral, first established during World War II.
