corn earworm

1. NOUN

/ kɔrn ˈɪrˌwɜrm / (AmE), / kɔːn ˈɪəˌwɜːm / (BrE)
A caterpillar of a noctuid moth (Helicoverpa zea) that is a highly destructive pest of corn, cotton, tomato, and other crops.
destructive caterpillar pest of crops

Examples

The farmer found several corn earworms damaging the newly formed ears of maize.
Pesticides are often used to control corn earworm infestations in commercial tomato fields.
Research into natural predators of the corn earworm could offer environmentally friendly pest control options.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bollworm - larva of various moth species, pest of cotton and other crops
