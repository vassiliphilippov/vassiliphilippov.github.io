genus Asparagus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs əˈspærəɡəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs əˈspærəɡəs / (BrE)
A large group of Old World perennial herbs, including asparagus, with erect, spreading, or climbing stems, small scalelike leaves, and inconspicuous flowers; sometimes classified in the family Asparagaceae.
a plant genus including asparagus

Examples

Botanists classify the edible plant under the genus Asparagus for its unique structural traits.
The genus Asparagus contains several wild species used in traditional medicine.
Members of the genus Asparagus are known for their slender, branched stems and feathery leaves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: asparagus - a widely cultivated edible shoot of the genus Asparagus

Etymology

From Latin 'asparagus', from Greek 'asparagos' (asparagus plant).
