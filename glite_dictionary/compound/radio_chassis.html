radio chassis

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪdioʊ ˈtʃæsi / (AmE), / ˈreɪdiəʊ ˈʃæsi / (BrE)
the supporting frame or base structure that holds the components of a radio receiver
frame or base for a radio receiver

Examples

The technician removed the radio chassis to repair a faulty circuit board inside.
Dust often accumulates on the radio chassis, affecting performance over time.
Classic car enthusiasts sometimes restore the original radio chassis to keep the vintage look.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chassis - the frame or base of a piece of equipment or vehicle
  • Synonym: radio receiver - device that receives and converts radio signals into sound

Etymology

Chassis from French 'châssis' (frame); combined with 'radio', from Latin 'radius'.
