minstrel show

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪnstrəl ˌʃoʊ / (AmE), / ˈmɪnstrəl ˌʃəʊ / (BrE)
a form of entertainment popular in the 19th and early 20th centuries in which a troupe of performers, often in blackface, presented comic sketches, songs, and dances, typically caricaturing African Americans
historical comedic stage show with blackface performers

Examples

The history class discussed the cultural impact of the minstrel show in shaping American entertainment.
He was shocked to learn that his grandfather had once performed in a minstrel show during the 1920s.
Many critics today condemn the minstrel show for its offensive stereotypes and use of blackface.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vaudeville - a type of variety show featuring diverse acts, popular in the late 19th and early 20th centuries

Etymology

From 'minstrel' (a medieval singer or musician) and 'show'; referencing 19th-century U.S. entertainment.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɪnstrəl ʃoʊ / (AmE), / ˈmɪnstrəl ʃəʊ / (BrE)
a type of theatrical variety entertainment popular in the 19th and early 20th centuries, featuring white performers in blackface portraying caricatured representations of Black people.
historical American variety show in blackface

Examples

The minstrel show was once a staple of American entertainment, despite its offensive caricatures.
Historians study the minstrel show to understand racial attitudes in 19th-century America.
The influence of the minstrel show can still be seen in some forms of popular culture today.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blackface - theatrical makeup to represent a caricature of a Black person

Etymology

From 'minstrel', referring to musicians or entertainers, and 'show'.
