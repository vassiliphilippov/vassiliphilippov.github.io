billy club

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɪli klʌb / (AmE), / ˈbɪli klʌb / (BrE)
A short, thick stick used as a weapon or for defense, especially by policemen.
policeman's short club

Examples

The police officer carried a billy club at his side during his patrol.
He swung the billy club to defend himself from the attacker.
In some cities, the use of a billy club by police is regulated strictly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: truncheon - A short police baton, especially in Britain
  • Synonym: nightstick - A police officer's baton, particularly in American English

Etymology

Probably from 'billy' as a generic man's name, used in 19th-century slang for various implements, and 'club' (stick or cudgel).
