line of reasoning

1. NOUN

/ laɪn əv ˈrizənɪŋ / (AmE), / laɪn əv ˈriːzənɪŋ / (BrE)
a sequence of logical steps or arguments used to reach a conclusion or support a claim
sequence of logical arguments

Examples

Her entire line of reasoning was based on a false assumption.
The lawyer’s line of reasoning was clear and persuasive to the jury.
I couldn’t understand the professor’s line of reasoning during the lecture.

Synset Examples

I can't follow your line of reasoning

Related Terms

  • Synonym: argument - a reason or set of reasons given in support of an idea
  • Synonym: logic - reasoning conducted according to strict principles of validity
