crown wart

1. NOUN

/ kraʊn wɔrt / (AmE), / kraʊn wɔːt / (BrE)
a fungal disease of alfalfa that causes white growths at the base of the stem
fungal disease of alfalfa

Examples

Crops infected with crown wart often display stunted growth and reduced yields.
The agricultural extension office provided brochures about preventing crown wart in alfalfa fields.
Researchers are developing new alfalfa varieties that are resistant to crown wart.
