Pythian Games

1. PROPN

/ ˈpɪθ.i.ən ˌɡeɪmz / (AmE), / ˈpɪθ.i.ən ˌɡeɪmz / (BrE)
an ancient Panhellenic festival held every four years at Delphi in honor of Apollo, featuring athletic and musical competitions
ancient Greek festival at Delphi honoring Apollo

Examples

The Pythian Games were considered second only to the Olympics in prestige among ancient Greek festivals.
Musicians and poets as well as athletes competed in the Pythian Games at Delphi.
The revival of the Pythian Games attracted scholars interested in ancient Greek culture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Olympic Games - ancient Panhellenic games held at Olympia

Etymology

From Pythian, relating to Pythia (the priestess of Apollo at Delphi), and the Games of ancient Greece.
