the wrong hands

1. NOUN

/ ðə rɔŋ hændz / (AmE), / ðə rɒŋ hændz / (BrE)
used to refer to people who are untrustworthy or may misuse something, especially if they come into possession of it
untrustworthy or harmful person's possession

Examples

Sensitive documents should never fall into the wrong hands.
There's a risk this technology could be misused if it ends up in the wrong hands.
We must secure the files so they don't get into the wrong hands.

Synset Examples

If that data gets into the wrong hands, it could be disastrous.
We must ensure this information doesn’t get into the wrong hands.
She feared her personal details might get into the wrong hands.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: the right hands - in the possession of someone trustworthy or suitable
