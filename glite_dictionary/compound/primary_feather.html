primary feather

1. NOUN

/ ˈpraɪˌmɛri ˈfɛðər / (AmE), / ˈpraɪməri ˈfɛðə / (BrE)
one of the main long feathers on the outer edge of a bird's wing, used for flight.
main flight feather on bird's wing

Examples

The eagle lost a primary feather during its flight, affecting its balance.
To identify the species, the biologist closely examined the shape of a primary feather.
A damaged primary feather can temporarily prevent a bird from flying properly.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: secondary feather - flight feather located closer to the body on a bird's wing
