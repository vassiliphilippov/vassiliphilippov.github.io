bangalore torpedo

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæŋɡəˌlɔr tɔrˈpiːdoʊ / (AmE), / ˈbæŋɡəlɔː tɔːˈpiːdəʊ / (BrE)
a metal tube packed with explosives, used by military forces to clear obstacles such as barbed wire or mines
explosive device for clearing obstacles

Examples

The soldiers used a bangalore torpedo to clear a path through the barbed wire barricade.
Engineers prepared a bangalore torpedo to detonate hidden land mines safely.
A bangalore torpedo can be quickly assembled and deployed under hostile conditions.

Etymology

Named after Bangalore, India, where the device was first developed.
