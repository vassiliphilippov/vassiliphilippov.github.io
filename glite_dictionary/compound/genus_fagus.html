genus Fagus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˈfeɪɡəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈfeɪɡəs / (BrE)
A genus of deciduous trees known as beeches, native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.
genus of beech trees

Examples

The genus Fagus includes several species of beech trees found across Europe, Asia, and North America.
Botanists studied the differences between European and American species within the genus Fagus.
The distinctive bark and leaves help to identify members of the genus Fagus in the wild.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beech - a deciduous tree of the genus Fagus

Etymology

From Latin *fagus* meaning 'beech'.
