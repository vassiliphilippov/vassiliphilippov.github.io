bunya bunya

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌnjə ˌbʌnjə / (AmE), / ˈbʌnjə ˌbʌnjə / (BrE)
an Australian coniferous tree (Araucaria bidwillii) bearing large edible seeds and significant in Aboriginal culture
Australian conifer tree with edible seeds

Examples

The bunya bunya is valued for its nutritious seeds, which resemble chestnuts in flavor.
Many Aboriginal groups celebrate the bunya bunya harvest through traditional gatherings and ceremonies.
The bunya bunya can grow to impressive heights, making it a striking feature of the Australian landscape.

Etymology

From an Aboriginal Australian language, likely Wakka Wakka or Kabi Kabi.
