Alabama River

1. PROPN

/ əˈlæbəmə ˈrɪvər / (AmE), / əˈlæbəmə ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
a river in Alabama, United States, formed by the joining of the Coosa and Tallapoosa Rivers near Montgomery, and flowing southwest to join the Mobile River.
river in Alabama, United States

Examples

We crossed the Alabama River just as the sun was setting behind the trees.
The city of Montgomery lies near the banks of the Alabama River.
The Alabama River provides important water resources for the region.

Etymology

Named after the state of Alabama, which derives from the Alabama people, a Native American tribe.
