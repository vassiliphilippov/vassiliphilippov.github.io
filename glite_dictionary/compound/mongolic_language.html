Mongolic language

1. NOUN

/ mɑŋˈɡɑlɪk ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / mɒŋˈɡɒlɪk ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
a family of languages spoken primarily in Mongolia and surrounding regions, often considered part of the Altaic group
language family of Mongolia region

Examples

The Mongolic language family includes Mongolian, Buryat, and several other languages.
Scholars debate whether the Mongolic language group is part of the larger Altaic family.
Khalkha is the most widely spoken Mongolic language today.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Altaic language - a proposed language family including Turkic, Mongolic, and Tungusic languages
  • Synonym: Mongolian - official language of Mongolia, principal member of the Mongolic family

Etymology

Derived from 'Mongol', relating to Mongolia, and the suffix '-ic'.
