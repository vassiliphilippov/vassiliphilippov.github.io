Medusa's head

1. NOUN

/ məˈdusəz hɛd / (AmE), / mɪˈdjuːsəz hɛd / (BrE)
a type of weedy rye grass with long, bristling awns resembling the snake-like hair of Medusa in Greek mythology
weedy rye grass with bristling awns

Examples

The farmer pulled up a clump of Medusa's head that was invading his field.
Experts warn that Medusa's head can spread rapidly and outcompete native grasses.
Grazing animals tend to avoid Medusa's head because of its tough, bristly texture.

Etymology

Named for Medusa, a figure in Greek mythology, whose hair was made of snakes, reflecting the plant's bristly appearance.
