medusa's head

1. NOUN

/ məˈdjusəz hɛd / (AmE), / mɪˈdjuːzəz hɛd / (BrE)
an African dwarf succulent perennial shrub with numerous slender, drooping branches, named for its resemblance to Medusa's hair
African dwarf succulent shrub

Examples

The medusa's head is prized by collectors for its unusual, snake-like branches.
Despite being rare, the medusa's head thrives in well-drained, sandy soil.
Gardeners often display the medusa's head in rock gardens because of its unique appearance.

Etymology

Named after the Greek mythological figure Medusa, whose hair was made of snakes, due to the plant's shape.
