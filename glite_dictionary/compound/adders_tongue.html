adder's tongue

1. NOUN

/ ˈædərz ˌtʌŋ / (AmE), / ˈædəz ˌtʌŋ / (BrE)
a small fern of the genus Ophioglossum, having a single leaf and a distinctive spore-bearing spike resembling a snake's tongue
small fern with a snake-like fertile spike

Examples

The distinctive spike of the adder's tongue makes it easy to recognize among other ferns.
We found several adder's tongue ferns growing in the damp meadow.
Botanists studied the reproduction of adder's tongue to better understand fern development.

Etymology

Named for the resemblance of its fertile spike to a snake's (adder's) tongue.
