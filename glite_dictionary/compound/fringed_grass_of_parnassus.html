fringed grass of Parnassus

1. NOUN

/ frɪndʒd ɡræs əv pɑrˈnæsəs / (AmE), / frɪndʒd ɡrɑːs ɒv pɑːˈnasəs / (BrE)
a bog plant native to western North America, with broadly heart-shaped basal leaves and cream or white saucer-shaped flowers with fringed petals
bog plant with fringed white flowers

Examples

The marsh was home to the delicate fringed grass of Parnassus, blooming in early summer.
Botanists observed the unique petals of the fringed grass of Parnassus during their field study in Alaska.
Hikers admired the fringed grass of Parnassus growing near the stream in the mountain meadow.

Etymology

Named after Mount Parnassus in Greece, where similar species were first described; 'fringed' refers to the petal shape.
