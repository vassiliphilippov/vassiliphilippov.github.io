stage fright

1. NOUN

/ steɪdʒ fraɪt / (AmE), / steɪdʒ fraɪt / (BrE)
Nervousness or fear experienced before or during appearing in front of an audience, especially when performing or speaking publicly.
fear of performing before an audience

Examples

Despite years of experience, she still suffers from stage fright before every show.
Many actors learn breathing techniques to manage their stage fright.
His stage fright vanished as soon as he stepped into the spotlight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: performance anxiety - nervousness before performing in front of others

Etymology

Formed in English from 'stage' (performance platform) and 'fright' (sudden fear); in use since the late 19th century.
