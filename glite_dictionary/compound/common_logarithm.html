common logarithm

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmən ˈlɑɡəˌrɪðəm / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən ˈlɒɡərɪðəm / (BrE)
a logarithm with base 10, often written as log(x)
logarithm with base 10

Examples

Many scientific calculators have a dedicated key for the common logarithm function.
The common logarithm of 1000 is 3 because 10 to the power of 3 is 1000.
To solve the equation efficiently, you might take the common logarithm of both sides.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: natural logarithm - a logarithm to the base e
