sea bream

1. NOUN

/ ˈsi ˌbrim / (AmE), / ˈsiː ˌbriːm / (BrE)
the flesh of any of various saltwater fish of the family Sparidae or the family Bramidae, commonly eaten as food
flesh of certain edible saltwater fish

Examples

The chef recommended the grilled sea bream for its delicate flavor and tender texture.
She bought fresh sea bream from the fish market to prepare for dinner.
Many Mediterranean dishes feature sea bream as a main ingredient.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bream - a species of freshwater or marine fish; also used for similar fishes

2. NOUN

/ si briːm / (AmE), / si briːm / (BrE)
a marine fish of the family Sparidae, having a deep, laterally compressed body and often valued as food
a type of edible marine fish

Examples

We grilled a fresh sea bream with lemon and herbs for dinner.
The waters off the coast are famous for their abundant sea bream population.
Fishermen often compete to catch the largest sea bream during the annual tournament.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bream - a freshwater or marine fish with a flat body
