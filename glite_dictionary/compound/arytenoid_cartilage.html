arytenoid cartilage

1. NOUN

/ ˌærɪˈtɛnɔɪd ˈkɑrtəlɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌærɪˈtiːnɔɪd ˈkɑːtɪlɪdʒ / (BrE)
either of two small cartilages located at the back of the larynx, to which the vocal folds are attached
cartilage at back of larynx
medical

Examples

Damage to the arytenoid cartilage can affect a person's ability to speak clearly.
The surgeon carefully examined the arytenoid cartilage during the operation on the larynx.
Inflammation of the arytenoid cartilage may result in hoarseness or voice changes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cartilage - firm, flexible connective tissue in the body

Etymology

From Greek arytenoeidēs (shaped like a ladle), from arytaina (ladle) + eidos (form, shape).
