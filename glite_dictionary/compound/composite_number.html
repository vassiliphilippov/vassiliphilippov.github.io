composite number

1. NOUN

/ kəmˈpɑːzɪt ˈnʌmbər / (AmE), / kəmˈpɒzɪt ˈnʌmbə / (BrE)
An integer that can be divided exactly by a positive integer other than itself and one; a number that is not prime.
an integer divisible by numbers other than itself and one

Examples

The number 6 is a composite number because it can be divided by 2 and 3.
Every composite number has at least three positive divisors.
Unlike a prime number, a composite number is not limited to just itself and one.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: prime number - an integer greater than one whose only positive divisors are itself and one

Etymology

From Latin compositus (put together) and number.
