quantum mechanics

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɑntəm məˈkænɪks / (AmE), / ˈkwɒntəm mɪˈkænɪks / (BrE)
The branch of physics that deals with the behavior of matter and energy at the atomic and subatomic levels, based on quantum theory.
branch of physics studying atomic and subatomic systems

Examples

Many technological advancements, such as semiconductors, rely on principles of quantum mechanics.
The uncertainty principle is a fundamental concept in quantum mechanics.
She decided to study quantum mechanics for her postgraduate research.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: classical mechanics - branch of physics dealing with macroscopic systems using Newtonian laws

Etymology

From Latin 'quantum' (how much) and 'mechanics', reflecting its focus on the quantization of physical properties.
