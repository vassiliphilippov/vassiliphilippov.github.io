family Stizidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ˈstɪzɪˌdeɪ / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli ˈstɪzɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of large, solitary wasps known as cicada killers, distinguished by their habit of hunting cicadas.
a family of cicada killer wasps

Examples

The family Stizidae includes several species that are commonly referred to as cicada killers.
Members of the family Stizidae are known for their ability to paralyze cicadas to feed their larvae.
Researchers observed nesting behaviors unique to the family Stizidae during the summer field study.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cicada killer - a large wasp that hunts cicadas, especially of genus Sphecius

Etymology

From New Latin 'Stizidae', based on the type genus 'Stizus', with the suffix '-idae' used for animal family names.
