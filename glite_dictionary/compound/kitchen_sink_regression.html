kitchen sink regression

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɪtʃən sɪŋk rɪˈɡrɛʃən / (AmE), / ˈkɪtʃɪn sɪŋk rɪˈɡrɛʃən / (BrE)
a software or statistical regression that affects many aspects or incorporates all possible variables, often leading to widespread problems or overcomplicated models
regression affecting many aspects or variables

Examples

The team struggled to track down the kitchen sink regression introduced in the new update.
Our QA process caught a kitchen sink regression that broke several modules.
Developers are working to fix a kitchen sink regression that affects the user interface and database.

Synset Examples

The latest update caused a kitchen sink regression, breaking several features.
We encountered a kitchen sink regression during the testing phase.
The developer warned us about potential kitchen sink regressions in the new build.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: regression - return to a previous state or a fault reintroduced in software development

Etymology

From the idiomatic phrase 'everything but the kitchen sink', meaning all-encompassing or including everything possible, combined with technical uses of 'regression'.
