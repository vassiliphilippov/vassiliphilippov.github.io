lack-latin

1. NOUN

/ læk ˈlætɪn / (AmE), / læk ˈlatɪn / (BrE)
meaningless jargon or language used to appear learned; pretentious or unintelligible speech
meaningless or pretentious jargon

Examples

When confronted with technical questions, he resorted to lack-latin to avoid admitting he didn't know the answer.
The manager's report was filled with so much lack-latin that nobody understood the conclusions.
Academe is sometimes accused of using lack-latin to mask the simplicity of its arguments.

Synset Examples

She tended to use lack-latin when unsure of the topic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gibberish - unintelligible or meaningless speech or writing

Etymology

From English 'lack' (absence) + 'Latin' (reference to the scholarly use of Latin), often used humorously.
