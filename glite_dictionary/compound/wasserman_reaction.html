Wasserman reaction

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɑs ər mən riˈæk ʃən / (AmE), / ˈwɒs ə mən riˈæk ʃən / (BrE)
a blood test formerly used to diagnose syphilis by detecting antibodies against the causative organism
blood test for syphilis (historical method)
medical

Examples

The Wasserman reaction was once the standard test for diagnosing syphilis in clinical laboratories.
A positive Wasserman reaction indicated the presence of syphilis antibodies in the patient's blood.
Although outdated, the Wasserman reaction marked an important advance in medical diagnostics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: VDRL test - modern blood test for syphilis
  • Synonym: syphilis - sexually transmitted infection caused by Treponema pallidum

Etymology

Named after August von Wassermann (1866–1925), German bacteriologist, who developed the test.
