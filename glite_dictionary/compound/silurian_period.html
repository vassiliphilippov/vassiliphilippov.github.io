Silurian period

1. NOUN

/ səˈljʊriən ˈpɪriəd / (AmE), / sɪˈljʊəriən ˈpɪəriəd / (BrE)
a period in the Paleozoic Era, occurring about 443 to 419 million years ago, marked by the appearance of the first air-breathing animals and widespread shallow seas
geological period in the Paleozoic Era

Examples

The discovery of fossils from the Silurian period has provided insights into early terrestrial life.
Geologists studied the rock layers to learn more about the climate during the Silurian period.
The transition from the Ordovician to the Silurian period marked significant evolutionary changes.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Devonian period - the geological period following the Silurian, marked by the rise of the first forests and amphibians
  • Degree Lower: Ordovician period - the geological period preceding the Silurian, marked by diverse marine life

Etymology

Named after the Silures, an ancient British tribe, due to rocks of this age found in their region; from Latin 'Silures'.
