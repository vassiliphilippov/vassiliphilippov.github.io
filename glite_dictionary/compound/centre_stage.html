centre stage

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛntɚ ˌsteɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈsɛntə ˌsteɪdʒ / (BrE)
the central area of a theater stage, considered the most prominent position for performers
central area of a theater stage
british

Examples

The actress moved to centre stage to deliver her final monologue.
A bright spotlight illuminated centre stage as the curtain rose.
He crossed from stage left and paused dramatically at centre stage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: center stage - the central area of a theater stage, American spelling

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɛn.tɚ steɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌsɛn.tə ˈsteɪdʒ / (BrE)
a position of prominence or importance; the most noticeable or important position or activity
most prominent position

Examples

After her breakthrough performance, she found herself at centre stage of the music industry.
Education reform took centre stage at the recent political debate.
Environmental issues have moved to centre stage in international discussions.
