fibrous dysplasia of bone

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪbrəs dɪsˈpleɪʒə əv boʊn / (AmE), / ˈfaɪbrəs dɪsˈpleɪzɪə əv bəʊn / (BrE)
a medical disorder in which normal bone is replaced with fibrous tissue, leading to weakened areas in the bone and sometimes skin lesions
bone disorder with fibrous tissue replacing bone
medical

Examples

The X-ray revealed fibrous dysplasia of bone in the patient's femur, causing pain and deformity.
Researchers are studying new treatments for fibrous dysplasia of bone to improve quality of life for affected individuals.
Children with fibrous dysplasia of bone may develop fractures more easily due to weakened bone structure.

Etymology

From Latin fibra (fiber) and Greek dys- (bad, difficult) + plasis (formation), combined with 'of bone'.
