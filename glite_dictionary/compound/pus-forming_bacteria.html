pus-forming bacteria

1. NOUN

/ ˈpʌs ˌfɔrmɪŋ bækˈtɪəriə / (AmE), / ˈpʌs ˌfɔːmɪŋ bækˈtɪəriə / (BrE)
bacteria that cause the formation of pus, typically by inducing infection and inflammation in tissues
bacteria that produce pus
medical

Examples

Staphylococcus aureus is one of the most common pus-forming bacteria in hospital infections.
The wound became infected with pus-forming bacteria and required antibiotic treatment.
Doctors must identify the type of pus-forming bacteria to choose the most effective therapy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pyogenic bacteria - bacteria that produce pus
