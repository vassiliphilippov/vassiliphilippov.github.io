Rust Belt

1. NOUN

/ ˈrʌst ˌbɛlt / (AmE), / ˈrʌst ˌbɛlt / (BrE)
A region in the northeastern and midwestern United States characterized by declining industry, aging factories, and a shrinking population.
region of industrial decline in the US
american

Examples

Many former manufacturing cities in the Rust Belt have struggled to attract new industries.
The economic downturn hit the Rust Belt especially hard, leading to widespread unemployment.
Urban revitalization projects are underway throughout the Rust Belt to spur growth and development.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Sun Belt - region in the US known for rapid population growth and a warm climate

Etymology

From 'rust' (corrosion of iron) plus 'belt' (region), reflecting the area's aging, decaying industries.
