superior ophthalmic vein

1. NOUN

/ suˈpɪriɚ ɑfˈθæl.mɪk veɪn / (AmE), / sjuːˈpɪər.i.ə ɒfˈθæl.mɪk veɪn / (BrE)
a vein that starts at the inner angle of the eye socket and passes through the superior orbital fissure to drain into the cavernous sinus
vein in the orbit draining into the cavernous sinus
medical

Examples

The superior ophthalmic vein is often visualized during orbital imaging studies.
A blockage in the superior ophthalmic vein can lead to serious complications affecting vision.
The surgeon carefully avoided the superior ophthalmic vein during the procedure to prevent bleeding.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inferior ophthalmic vein - a vein of the orbit that drains structures lower in the eye socket

Etymology

From Latin: 'superior' (higher), 'ophthalmicus' (of the eye), and 'vena' (vein).
