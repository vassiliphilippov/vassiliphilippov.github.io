administered price

1. NOUN

/ ədˈmɪnəstɚd ˌpraɪs / (AmE), / ədˈmɪnɪstəd ˌpraɪs / (BrE)
a price that is set by an authority, such as a government or organization, rather than determined by the free market
price set by authority, not the market

Examples

Several essential goods are sold at an administered price to ensure affordability for all citizens.
The government reviewed the existing administered price for fuel every quarter.
Unlike a market-determined rate, an administered price can remain stable during economic volatility.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: market price - the price determined by supply and demand in a free market
