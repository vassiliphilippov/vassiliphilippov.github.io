Cape kafferboom

1. NOUN

/ keɪp ˈkæfərˌbuːm / (AmE), / keɪp ˈkæfəˌbuːm / (BrE)
a small semi-evergreen tree (Erythrina caffra) of eastern South Africa, with broad-spreading branches, orange-scarlet flowers, and coral-red seeds; its light, soft wood is used for fence posts and shingles
South African tree with orange-scarlet flowers

Examples

The garden's Cape kafferboom was covered in brilliant orange-scarlet flowers during early spring.
Local farmers often use wood from the Cape kafferboom for making fence posts due to its softness and light weight.
Bright coral-red seeds collected from the Cape kafferboom are sometimes used in decorative crafts.

Etymology

From Afrikaans 'kafferboom', meaning 'Kaffir tree', a historical term for the tree based on its occurrence in regions inhabited by the Xhosa and Zulu peoples; 'Cape' refers to the Cape Province of South Africa.
