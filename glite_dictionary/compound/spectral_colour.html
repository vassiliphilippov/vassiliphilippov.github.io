spectral colour

1. NOUN

/ ˈspɛktrəl ˈkʌlər / (AmE), / ˈspɛktrəl ˈkʌlə / (BrE)
a colour produced by a single wavelength of visible light, as found in a spectrum (such as red, green, or blue); not a mixture of different hues.
colour visible in the spectrum
british

Examples

Each spectral colour corresponds to a different wavelength of visible light.
Scientists separated light into its spectral colours using a prism.
The rainbow displays all the spectral colours in their natural order.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spectral color - American spelling of 'spectral colour'
  • Synonym: hue - the attribute of colour that permits it to be classified as red, blue, etc.

Etymology

From 'spectral', relating to a spectrum, and 'colour'; 'spectrum' from Latin 'spectrum' (appearance, image).
