salt-cured

1. ADJ

/ sɔlt ˈkjʊrd / (AmE), / sɒlt ˈkjʊəd / (BrE)
(of food, especially meat) preserved by being treated with salt to prevent decay.
preserved in salt (esp. meat)

Examples

The chef used salt-cured pork in the traditional stew to enhance its flavor.
Many travelers packed salt-cured meats for their long journeys due to their extended shelf life.
He prefers salt-cured fish for its distinctive taste and texture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pickled - (of food) preserved in vinegar or brine
