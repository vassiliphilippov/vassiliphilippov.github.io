Dayton axe

1. NOUN

/ ˈdeɪtən æks / (AmE), / ˈdeɪtən æks / (BrE)
a type of ax with a long handle and a head featuring one cutting edge and one flat, blunt side, commonly used in forestry and logging
single-bit ax with one blunt side

Examples

The logger selected a Dayton axe for felling the tall oak trees in the forest.
Her grandfather’s old Dayton axe still hangs in the barn, well-worn but sharp.
Many professionals prefer the Dayton axe for its versatility and durable design.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ax - a tool with a heavy bladed head mounted across a handle, used for chopping

Etymology

Named after Dayton, Ohio, where this style of ax head was popularized.
