Columbus Day

1. PROPN

/ kəˈlʌmbəs deɪ / (AmE), / kəˈlʌmbəs deɪ / (BrE)
A public holiday in the United States commemorating the arrival of Christopher Columbus in the Americas, observed on the second Monday in October.
American public holiday honoring Columbus
american

Examples

Many schools and businesses close for Columbus Day every October.
Some cities now celebrate Indigenous Peoples' Day instead of Columbus Day.
The parade for Columbus Day attracts large crowds in New York.

Etymology

Named after Christopher Columbus, the Italian explorer credited with the European "discovery" of America in 1492.
