faint praise

1. NOUN

/ feɪnt preɪz / (AmE), / feɪnt preɪz / (BrE)
Praise that is so weak or unenthusiastic that it suggests a lack of real approval or may imply criticism.
barely positive or insincere praise

Examples

Her accomplishments were met with faint praise from her supervisor.
He offered only faint praise for my efforts, which felt more like a criticism.
The author's work received faint praise in the review, hinting at the critic's lack of enthusiasm.

Synset Examples

She knew he would damn her with faint praise at the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to damn with faint praise - to criticize someone indirectly by offering only weak praise
