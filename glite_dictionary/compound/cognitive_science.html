cognitive science

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑɡnəˌtɪv ˈsaɪəns / (AmE), / ˈkɒɡnətɪv ˈsaɪəns / (BrE)
the interdisciplinary scientific study of the mind and its processes, combining aspects of psychology, neuroscience, linguistics, computer science, and philosophy
the study of the mind and its processes

Examples

She decided to major in cognitive science because she was fascinated by how the mind works.
The conference brought together leading experts from every area of cognitive science.
Advances in artificial intelligence have been heavily influenced by research in cognitive science.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: psychology - the scientific study of the mind and behavior
  • Synonym: neuroscience - the scientific study of the nervous system, especially the brain
  • Synonym: philosophy of mind - the branch of philosophy studying the nature of the mind and mental events

Etymology

Formed from 'cognitive' (relating to cognition) and 'science'; popularized in the 1970s.
