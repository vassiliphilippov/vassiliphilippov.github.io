popularity contest

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑː.pjəˈlær.ə.t̬i ˈkɑn.tɛst / (AmE), / ˌpɒp.jʊˈlæ.rɪ.ti ˈkɒn.tɛst / (BrE)
a competition, election, or situation in which success depends more on being liked or attracting popular support than on merit or qualification
competition based on popular support

Examples

Winning the election turned into a popularity contest rather than a debate about policy.
Critics argue that award shows often become a popularity contest instead of recognizing true talent.
The student council race felt like a popularity contest, with candidates focusing more on their social lives than on issues.

2. NOUN

/ ˌpɑː.pjəˈlɛr.ə.ti ˌkɑn.tɛst / (AmE), / ˌpɒ.pjʊˈlær.ə.ti ˌkɒn.tɛst / (BrE)
a competition in which success is determined by how popular someone or something is, rather than by objective criteria or actual merit
competition judged by popularity, not merit

Examples

Winning the election often feels like a popularity contest instead of a fair judgment of skills.
Sometimes, high school can seem like a never-ending popularity contest.
This job interview should not be treated as a popularity contest.

Synset Examples
