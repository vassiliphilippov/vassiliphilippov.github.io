blue law

1. NOUN

/ blu lɔ / (AmE), / bluː lɔː / (BrE)
A law that restricts or prohibits certain activities, especially commercial ones, on Sundays or religious holidays.
law restricting Sunday activities
legal

Examples

Many states once had blue laws that prohibited shops from opening on Sundays.
The lifting of blue laws allowed more businesses to operate on weekends.
Historians debate the social effects of 19th-century blue law enforcement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sabbath law - a law enforcing Sunday as a day of rest
  • Antonym: civil law - law concerned with private affairs, not criminal or religious

Etymology

Possibly from the 18th-century term 'blue' meaning rigidly moral; exact origin unclear.
