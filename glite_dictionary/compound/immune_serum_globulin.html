immune serum globulin

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈmjun ˈsɪrəm ˈɡlɑbjəlɪn / (AmE), / ɪˈmjuːn ˈsɪərəm ˈɡlɒbjʊlɪn / (BrE)
a preparation of antibodies, derived from the blood serum of humans or animals, used to provide passive immunity against diseases
antibody preparation for passive immunity
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed immune serum globulin to help prevent infection after exposure to the virus.
Recipients of immune serum globulin gain immediate, short-term protection against certain diseases.
Before traveling to the affected region, she received an immune serum globulin injection as a precaution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: immunoglobulin - proteins functioning as antibodies in the immune response

Etymology

From 'immune', relating to immunity, and 'globulin', from Latin 'globulus' (small ball), referring to the globular shape of the protein.
