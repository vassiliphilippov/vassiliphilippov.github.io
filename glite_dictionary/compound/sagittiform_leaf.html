sagittiform leaf

1. NOUN

/ səˈdʒɪtəˌfɔrm liːf / (AmE), / səˈdʒɪtɪfɔːm liːf / (BrE)
A leaf shaped like an arrowhead, typically with two pointed lobes at the base.
arrowhead-shaped leaf
medical

Examples

The plant's sagittiform leaf helps distinguish it from similar species in the region.
Botanists identified the sample by its unique sagittiform leaf structure and sharp basal points.
Many aquatic plants, such as arrowhead, feature a prominent sagittiform leaf.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: hastate leaf - a leaf shaped like a spearhead, with basal lobes spreading outward

Etymology

From Latin sagittiformis, meaning 'arrow-shaped', from sagitta (arrow) + -form (shape).
