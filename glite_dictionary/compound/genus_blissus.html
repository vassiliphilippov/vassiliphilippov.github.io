genus Blissus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˈblɪsəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈblɪsəs / (BrE)
A genus of insects in the family Blissidae, commonly known as chinch bugs, which are pests of grasses and crops.
a genus of insects (chinch bugs)

Examples

Researchers studied the life cycle of the genus Blissus in local grasslands.
Damage to the crops was traced back to insects belonging to the genus Blissus.
Farmers are familiar with members of the genus Blissus due to their destructive impact on wheat fields.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chinch bug - a small insect that is a pest of cereals and grasses

Etymology

From Latin genus (kind) and Blissus, the type genus name in entomological taxonomy.
