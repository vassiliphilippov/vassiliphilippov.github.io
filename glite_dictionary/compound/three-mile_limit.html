three-mile limit

1. NOUN

/ ˈθriː ˌmaɪl ˈlɪmɪt / (AmE), / ˈθriː ˌmaɪl ˈlɪmɪt / (BrE)
the boundary extending three nautical miles from a nation's coastline, formerly recognized as the extent of its territorial waters
boundary of territorial waters (formerly three nautical miles)
legal

Examples

Until the 20th century, the three-mile limit was commonly accepted by many countries.
The fishing boats were seized for operating inside the three-mile limit of national waters.
Debates over extending the three-mile limit ultimately led to broader territorial claims.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: territorial waters - the waters under the jurisdiction of a state
  • Degree Higher: exclusive economic zone - area of coastal water where a state has special rights over resources
