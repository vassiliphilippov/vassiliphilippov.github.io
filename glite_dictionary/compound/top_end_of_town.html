top end of town

1. NOUN

/ tɑp ɛnd əv taʊn / (AmE), / tɒp end əv taʊn / (BrE)
the most expensive or desirable part of a community, typically where wealthy people live
the affluent area of a community

Examples

They could never afford to live at the top end of town on their salaries.
Shops at the top end of town tend to be upscale and exclusive.
He likes to invest in properties located in the top end of town.

Synset Examples

Real estate prices are rising in the top end of town.
They moved to the top end of town for better schools.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: suburb - an outlying district of a city, typically residential
  • Antonym: slums - a squalid and overcrowded urban street or district inhabited by very poor people
