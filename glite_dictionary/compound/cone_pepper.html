cone pepper

1. NOUN

/ koʊn ˈpɛpər / (AmE), / kəʊn ˈpɛpə / (BrE)
a variety of pepper plant bearing upright, pungent, conical fruits, typically red, yellow, or purple, sometimes cultivated as an ornamental
variety of pepper plant with upright, conical fruits

Examples

The gardener planted a row of cone peppers for their vibrant color and spicy flavor.
She decorated her kitchen window with pots of ornamental cone pepper plants.
The recipe called for a finely chopped cone pepper to add heat to the dish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chili pepper - a hot-tasting pod of a variety of capsicum, used in sauces, relishes, and spice powders
