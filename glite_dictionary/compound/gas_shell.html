gas shell

1. NOUN

/ ɡæs ʃɛl / (AmE), / ɡas ʃɛl / (BrE)
A military explosive projectile filled with a toxic gas, which is released upon detonation.
explosive military projectile with toxic gas

Examples

During World War I, soldiers dreaded the use of gas shells on the battlefield.
The army developed special masks to protect troops from gas shells.
After the attack, the area remained dangerous due to the lingering effects of the gas shell's contents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shell - an explosive projectile for use in warfare
