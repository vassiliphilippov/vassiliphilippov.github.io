pale rider

1. NOUN

/ peɪl ˈraɪdər / (AmE), / peɪl ˈraɪdə / (BrE)
a symbolic figure of death or doom, often depicted as a ghostly or spectral rider
symbolic figure of death or doom

Examples

In the dim light, the villagers believed they saw the pale rider approaching on his ghostly horse.
Folklore describes the pale rider as an omen of death in the quiet, remote valleys.
The old painting depicted a pale rider looming over a devastated battlefield.

Synset Examples

The pale rider appeared ominously in the distance.
The legend of the pale rider still haunts the village.
They dubbed him the pale rider after his ghostly appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grim reaper - personification of death, often depicted with a scythe and cloak

Etymology

Biblical allusion: from the Book of Revelation, where Death is described as a rider on a pale horse.
