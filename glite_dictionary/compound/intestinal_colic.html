intestinal colic

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtɛstənəl ˈkɑlɪk / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɛstɪnəl ˈkɒlɪk / (BrE)
acute abdominal pain, especially in infants, caused by spasm or obstruction of the intestines
acute abdominal pain in infants
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed the baby's excessive crying as intestinal colic.
Many parents struggle to soothe infants suffering from intestinal colic during the first months.
Persistent intestinal colic can be distressing for both the child and the caregivers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: colic - severe, often fluctuating pain in the abdomen caused by intestinal or other muscle spasms

Etymology

From Latin 'intestinum' (intestine) and Greek 'kolikos' (pertaining to the colon).
