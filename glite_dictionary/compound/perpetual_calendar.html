perpetual calendar

1. NOUN

/ pərˈpɛtʃuəl ˈkæləndər / (AmE), / pəˈpɛtʃuəl ˈkælɪndə / (BrE)
a chart, table, or mechanical device that shows the day of the week for any given date over a long period of years
device to find day of week for any date

Examples

My grandfather owns a perpetual calendar from the 19th century that still works perfectly.
A perpetual calendar can help you find out what day of the week you were born on.
Watchmakers often display luxury watches with a built-in perpetual calendar feature.
