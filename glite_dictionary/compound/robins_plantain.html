robin's plantain

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɑːbɪnz ˌplænˈteɪn / (AmE), / ˈrɒbɪnz ˌplænˈteɪn / (BrE)
A common perennial plant of eastern North America, having flowers with usually violet-purple ray petals.
North American perennial with violet-purple flowers

Examples

The meadow was dotted with robin's plantain in full bloom.
Botanists often study robin's plantain for its resilience in rocky soils.
We found a patch of robin's plantain near the edge of the forest.
