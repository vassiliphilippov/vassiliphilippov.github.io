water buffalo

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔtər ˌbʌfəˌloʊ / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə ˌbʌfələʊ / (BrE)
a large domesticated Asian buffalo, commonly used as a draft animal in agriculture
large Asian domesticated buffalo

Examples

Farmers in Southeast Asia often use water buffalo to plow their fields during the rainy season.
The water buffalo is known for its strength and endurance in hauling heavy loads.
Tourists were amazed to see a water buffalo bathing in the river to cool off.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɔt̬ɚ ˌbʌfəˌloʊ / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə ˌbʌf.ə.ləʊ / (BrE)
A large domesticated animal, native to Asia and parts of Africa, used in agriculture and known for its ability to work in wet and muddy conditions.
large domesticated bovine used in agriculture

Examples

The water buffalo is often seen cooling off in muddy ponds during the heat of the day.
Farmers in Southeast Asia rely on the water buffalo to help plow their rice fields.
The horns of a water buffalo can grow to impressive lengths, curving outward and upward.
