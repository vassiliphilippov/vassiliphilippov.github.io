Medellin cartel

1. PROPN

/ məˈdɛliːn kɑɹˈtɛl / (AmE), / məˈdɛliːn kɑːˈtɛl / (BrE)
a notorious Colombian drug cartel that controlled much of the cocaine trade from the 1970s until 1993, when its leader was killed
Colombian drug cartel (1970s–1993)

Examples

The Medellin cartel was once considered the most powerful criminal organization in the world.
Law enforcement agencies struggled for years to dismantle the Medellin cartel.
After the fall of the Medellin cartel, drug trafficking routes changed significantly throughout South America.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Cali cartel - a rival Colombian drug cartel operating mainly in the 1980s and 1990s

Etymology

Named after Medellín, a city in Colombia where the cartel was based, and 'cartel' from Italian cartello (a poster, placard, or agreement).
