unearned revenue

1. NOUN

/ ʌnˈɝnd ˈrɛvəˌnu / (AmE), / ʌnˈɜːnd ˈrɛvənjuː / (BrE)
personal income received without direct work, such as dividends, interest, or rent income
income received without direct work

Examples

Dividends from stocks are considered unearned revenue for tax purposes.
The accountant carefully recorded the company's unearned revenue in the financial statement.
Interest on savings accounts is a common form of unearned revenue for individuals.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: earned income - income received for direct work or services performed

2. NOUN

/ ʌnˈɝnd ˈrɛvənju / (AmE), / ʌnˈɜːnd ˈrɛvənjuː / (BrE)
(Accounting) income received in advance for goods or services that have not yet been delivered, and which is recorded as a current liability on a company's balance sheet.
advance income not yet earned (accounting)
legal

Examples

The company's balance sheet showed a significant amount of unearned revenue from advance customer payments.
Airlines often report unearned revenue for tickets sold but not yet used by passengers.
When the service is provided, the unearned revenue is recognized as actual income.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deferred income - income received before it is earned, synonymous in accounting
  • Antonym: accrued revenue - revenue that has been earned but not yet received
