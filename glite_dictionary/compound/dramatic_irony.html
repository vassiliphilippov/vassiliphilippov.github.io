dramatic irony

1. NOUN

/ drəˈmætɪk ˈaɪrəni / (AmE), / drəˈmatɪk ˈaɪrəni / (BrE)
A literary device in which the audience is aware of something important that the characters in a play or story do not know.
irony known to audience but not characters

Examples

The play's use of dramatic irony made the audience anxious as the hero walked into the trap.
Shakespeare often employed dramatic irony in his tragedies to heighten tension.
In the movie, dramatic irony occurs when viewers know the villain is hiding in the closet, but the protagonist does not.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: irony - the expression of one's meaning by using language that normally signifies the opposite; literary device involving contrast between expectation and reality
