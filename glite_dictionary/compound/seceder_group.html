Seceder group

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈsiːdər gruːp / (AmE), / sɪˈsiːdə gruːp / (BrE)
a group of people who advocate for separation from a larger organization or political entity, often seeking independence
group advocating separation or independence

Examples

The Seceder group called for autonomy from the national government.
After months of debate, a Seceder group formed within the organization.
Historical records show the influence of the Seceder group on the region's path to independence.

Synset Examples

The Seceder group argued for independence from the main party.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: secessionist - a person who advocates secession from a larger group, especially a political entity

Etymology

From 'secede', from Latin 'secedere' (to go apart), plus 'group'.
