military government

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪl.ɪ.ˌtɛr.i ˈɡʌv.ərn.mənt / (AmE), / ˈmɪl.ɪ.təri ˈɡʌv.ən.mənt / (BrE)
a government administered by military authorities, often after a coup or during martial law, where the armed forces control state power instead of civilians
government run by the military

Examples

After the coup, the country was ruled by a military government for several years.
The transition from military government to civilian rule was marked by protests and unrest.
Many citizens feared the strict policies imposed by the new military government.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: martial law - temporary rule by military authorities in place of a civilian government
  • Antonym: civil government - government administered by civilian officials
