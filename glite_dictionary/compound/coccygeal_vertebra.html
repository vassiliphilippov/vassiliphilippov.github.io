coccygeal vertebra

1. NOUN

/ kɑkˈsiːdʒiəl ˈvɜrtəbrə / (AmE), / kɒkˈsiːdʒɪəl ˈvɜːtɪbrə / (BrE)
any one of the small bones forming the coccyx (tailbone) at the base of the human spine
bone forming the coccyx (tailbone)
medical

Examples

An injury to a coccygeal vertebra can cause persistent pain while sitting.
During development, the coccygeal vertebrae fuse to form a single coccyx in adulthood.
The surgeon examined the alignment of each coccygeal vertebra before closing the incision.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coccyx - the small, triangular bone at the base of the spinal column
  • Synonym: vertebra - each of the series of small bones forming the backbone

Etymology

From Greek kokkux (cuckoo, referring to the shape of the bone) and Latin vertebra (joint of the spine).
