steak tartare

1. NOUN

/ steɪk tɑːrˈtɑr / (AmE), / steɪk tɑːˈtɑː / (BrE)
A dish of finely chopped or ground raw beef, typically mixed with seasonings and served with a raw egg yolk, onions, capers, and other flavorings.
dish of raw seasoned ground beef

Examples

He ordered the steak tartare as an appetizer at the French restaurant.
Some people are hesitant to try steak tartare because it is made from raw beef.
The chef prepares steak tartare tableside, mixing in capers and onions just before serving.

Etymology

French, from 'steak' (English) and 'tartare', after the Tatars (Tartars), with an association to raw meat.
