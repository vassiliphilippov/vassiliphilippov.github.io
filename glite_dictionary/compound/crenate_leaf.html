crenate leaf

1. NOUN

/ ˈkriːneɪt liːf / (AmE), / ˈkriːneɪt liːf / (BrE)
A leaf with a margin having rounded, scallop-like teeth or notches.
leaf with scalloped margin

Examples

The holly plant has a distinctive crenate leaf that helps identify the species.
Botanists note that the crenate leaf structure aids in water runoff.
You can recognize a crenate leaf by its gently scalloped edges.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crenate - having a scalloped or notched edge, especially of leaves

Etymology

From Latin 'crenatus' meaning 'notched' or 'scalloped'.
