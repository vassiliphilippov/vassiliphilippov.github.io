Evangelical and Reformed Church

1. PROPN

/ ɪˌvæn.dʒəˈlɪkəl ənd rɪˈfɔrmd tʃɝːtʃ / (AmE), / ɪˌvan.dʒɪˈlɪk.əl ənd rɪˈfɔːmd tʃɜːtʃ / (BrE)
A Protestant Christian denomination with Calvinist roots, formed in 1934 by the merger of the Evangelical Synod of North America and the Reformed Church in the United States.
Calvinist Protestant denomination

Examples

The Evangelical and Reformed Church played a significant role in shaping American Protestantism in the twentieth century.
She grew up attending services at an Evangelical and Reformed Church in Ohio.
Historical records show the Evangelical and Reformed Church emphasized education and social service.

Etymology

Named from the union of the Evangelical Synod of North America (of German descent) and the Reformed Church in the United States (of Swiss/German Reformed tradition) in 1934.
