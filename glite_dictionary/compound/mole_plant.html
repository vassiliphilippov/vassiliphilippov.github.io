mole plant

1. NOUN

/ ˈmoʊl plænt / (AmE), / ˈməʊl plɑːnt / (BrE)
a poisonous plant (Euphorbia lathyris), native to the Old World and introduced to America, whose seeds yield a purgative oil
a poisonous plant (Euphorbia lathyris)

Examples

The seeds of the mole plant are known to produce a strong purgative oil.
Gardeners sometimes grow the mole plant to deter burrowing animals from vegetable beds.
Because the mole plant is poisonous, it should be handled with care, especially around children and pets.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmoʊl plænt / (AmE), / ˈməʊl plɑːnt / (BrE)
a showy plant (Euphorbia lathyris), native from the southern United States to Peru, known for its upright stems and green leaves
a showy plant, Euphorbia lathyris

Examples

The mole plant can be seen growing in gardens across North America.
Gardeners often plant mole plants to deter moles and voles from their yards.
With its tall stalks and distinctive leaves, the mole plant adds interest to any landscape.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Euphorbia - a large plant genus including spurges and the mole plant
