tongue-lashing

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʌŋ ˌlæʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtʌŋ ˌlæʃɪŋ / (BrE)
a severe scolding or harsh verbal rebuke
severe verbal scolding

Examples

After forgetting their anniversary, he received a tongue-lashing from his wife.
The coach gave the players a tongue-lashing after their poor performance.
She dreaded the tongue-lashing she knew awaited her for missing the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scolding - angrily criticizing someone, especially a child, for their behavior
  • Synonym: reprimand - a formal expression of disapproval

Etymology

From 'tongue' (referring to speech) and 'lashing' (striking repeatedly), indicating striking someone with words.
