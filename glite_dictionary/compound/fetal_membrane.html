fetal membrane

1. NOUN

/ ˈfiːtəl məˈmɪnˌbreɪn / (AmE), / ˈfiːtəl mɛˈmːbreɪn / (BrE)
any of the membranes that surround and protect a developing fetus, aiding in its nourishment, respiration, or excretion
membrane surrounding a developing fetus
medical

Examples

The fetal membrane plays a crucial role in protecting the embryo during pregnancy.
Doctors examined the fetal membrane for any abnormalities after the premature rupture.
Damage to the fetal membrane can increase the risk of infection for the developing baby.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amniotic sac - a membranous sac that surrounds and protects the embryo in mammals
  • Synonym: placenta - organ in the uterus providing oxygen and nutrients to the developing fetus

Etymology

From Latin 'fetus' (offspring) and Middle English 'membrane', from Latin 'membrana' (parchment, membrane).
