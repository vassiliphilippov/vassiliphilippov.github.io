copper's nark

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑpərz nɑrk / (AmE), / ˈkɒpəz nɑːk / (BrE)
an informer or spy working for the police
police informer or spy
slang british

Examples

The thief suspected there was a copper's nark among his friends, so he kept his plans secret.
Back in Victorian London, being called a copper's nark was a serious accusation on the streets.
The gang was wary of a copper's nark infiltrating their organization.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grass - a police informer (British slang)
  • Synonym: snitch - a person who informs on others
  • Degree Higher: policeman - a member of the police force

Etymology

'Copper' is slang for police officer; 'nark' is British slang for informer, possibly from Romani 'nāk' (nose).
