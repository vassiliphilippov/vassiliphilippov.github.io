nominating speech

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɑː.məˌneɪ.tɪŋ spiːtʃ / (AmE), / ˈnɒ.mɪˌneɪ.tɪŋ spiːtʃ / (BrE)
an address delivered to formally propose a candidate's name, typically at a political convention, for consideration as an election nominee
address proposing a candidate for election

Examples

The senator delivered a powerful nominating speech at the party convention last night.
Her nominating speech highlighted the candidate’s achievements and vision for the country.
Each nominating speech must follow the guidelines set by the convention committee.

Synset Examples

the nomination was brief and to the point

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nomination - the act of proposing someone for a position or office
  • Degree Higher: acceptance speech - a speech given by a nominee after being formally chosen
