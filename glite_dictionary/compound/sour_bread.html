sour bread

1. NOUN

/ saʊər brɛd / (AmE), / saʊə brɛd / (BrE)
Bread made with a starter containing active fermentation, giving it a characteristic sour taste.
bread made with fermented starter

Examples

The bakery's sour bread is famous for its tangy, rustic flavor and chewy crust.
She prefers making sour bread at home using her own starter culture.
Many traditional European cuisines feature sour bread as a staple in their diet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sourdough - bread made from fermented dough starter
