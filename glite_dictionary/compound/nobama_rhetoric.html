Nobama rhetoric

1. NOUN

/ noʊˈbɑːmə ˈrɛtərɪk / (AmE), / nəʊˈbɑːmə ˈrɛtərɪk / (BrE)
political speech or language characterized by strong opposition to Barack Obama or his policies
discourse opposing Barack Obama

Examples

The senator's speech was filled with Nobama rhetoric that energized his supporters.
Media outlets criticized the campaign for relying too heavily on Nobama rhetoric rather than substantive policy.
Increasingly, talk shows featured hosts engaging in Nobama rhetoric during political debates.

Synset Examples

Critics labeled the policy as 'Nobama' rhetoric.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anti-Obama - opposed to Barack Obama or his policies

Etymology

Formed from 'No' and 'Obama', referring to opposition to Barack Obama, with 'rhetoric' denoting political speech.
