mother-hive

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌðər haɪv / (AmE), / ˈmʌðə haɪv / (BrE)
A place that is bustling with activity or acts as a central support system for growth and development.
central place of activity or growth

Examples

The old bookstore became a mother-hive for artists and writers in the city.
During festival season, the village square transforms into a mother-hive of excitement and celebration.
Their organization served as a mother-hive that nurtured many successful startups.

Synset Examples

In spring, the park becomes a mother-hive of activity.
Their community acts as a mother-hive for local businesses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hive of activity - a place full of people who are busy doing things

Etymology

Formed by analogy with 'hive' (a busy place), with 'mother' implying an origin or central source.
