calico aster

1. NOUN

/ ˈkælɪkoʊ ˈæstər / (AmE), / ˈkælɪkəʊ ˈɑːstə / (BrE)
a North American species of aster (Symphyotrichum lateriflorum) with small white flowers often tinged with pink or purple
a species of aster with small white flowers

Examples

The calico aster adds a delicate touch to late summer gardens with its clusters of tiny flowers.
Botanists observed a field covered in blooming calico asters along the woodland edge.
In the wild, the calico aster often grows in moist meadows and along streams.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aster - a plant of the genus Aster, related flowering species

Etymology

From 'calico', referring to its variegated flower colors, and 'aster' from Greek 'astron' (star).
