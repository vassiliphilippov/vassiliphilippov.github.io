cow dung

1. NOUN

/ kaʊ dʌŋ / (AmE), / kaʊ dʌŋ / (BrE)
the solid waste excreted by a cow, often used as fertilizer or fuel when dried
the feces of a cow, often dried for fertilizer

Examples

Many farmers collect cow dung to use as a natural fertilizer in their fields.
In some rural villages, people burn dried cow dung as a source of fuel for cooking.
The children were told to avoid stepping in cow dung while playing near the pasture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: manure - animal dung used for fertilizing land
