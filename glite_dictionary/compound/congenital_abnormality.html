congenital abnormality

1. NOUN

/ kənˈdʒɛnɪtəl æbˌnɔrmˈæləti / (AmE), / kənˈdʒɛnɪtəl æbˈnɔːməlɪti / (BrE)
A physical or functional defect present at birth.
birth defect; condition present at birth
medical

Examples

The baby was diagnosed with a congenital abnormality affecting her heart.
Doctors often screen for congenital abnormalities during pregnancy.
Certain medications can increase the risk of a congenital abnormality in newborns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: birth defect - A defect present at birth
  • Antonym: acquired disorder - A disorder not present at birth but developed later

Etymology

From Latin 'congenitus' (born together) + 'abnormality' from Latin 'abnormis' (irregular).
