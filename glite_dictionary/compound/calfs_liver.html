calf's liver

1. NOUN

/ kæfs ˈlɪvɚ / (AmE), / kɑːfs ˈlɪvə / (BrE)
The liver of a calf, used as food, considered tender and mild in flavor compared to mature beef liver.
the liver of a calf, used as food

Examples

The restaurant's specialty is sautéed calf's liver with onions.
My grandmother often served fried calf's liver for Sunday dinner.
Some people prefer calf's liver because it is more tender than regular beef liver.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: beef liver - the liver of a mature cow or steer, used as meat
