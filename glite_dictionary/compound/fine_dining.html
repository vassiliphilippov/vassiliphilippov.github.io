fine dining

1. NOUN

/ faɪn ˈdaɪnɪŋ / (AmE), / faɪn ˈdaɪnɪŋ / (BrE)
the experience of eating meals at an elegant or upscale restaurant, often characterized by high-quality food and service
elegant or upscale restaurant meals

Examples

She enjoys fine dining when celebrating special occasions.
The city's best fine dining establishments require a reservation weeks in advance.
He prefers fine dining over fast food when traveling abroad.

Synset Examples

He's such a snob about fine dining.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: casual dining - a more relaxed style of dining, usually less expensive and formal than fine dining
