atactic abasia

1. NOUN

/ əˈtæktɪk əˈbeɪziə / (AmE), / əˈtæktɪk əˈbeɪziə / (BrE)
An inability to walk due to lack of muscle coordination (ataxia) in the legs.
inability to walk from leg ataxia
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with atactic abasia following a series of neurological examinations.
Due to damage in her cerebellum, she developed severe atactic abasia and could no longer walk unaided.
Doctors explained that atactic abasia resulted from the progressive loss of coordination in his legs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abasia - inability to walk
  • Synonym: ataxia - lack of muscle coordination

Etymology

From Greek ataktos (uncoordinated) and abasia (inability to walk).
