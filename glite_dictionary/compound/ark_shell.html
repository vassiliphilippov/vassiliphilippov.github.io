ark shell

1. NOUN

/ ɑrk ʃɛl / (AmE), / ɑːk ʃɛl / (BrE)
a marine bivalve mollusk with a heavy, toothed shell and a deep, boat-shaped inner surface
marine bivalve mollusk with toothed shell

Examples

Several species of ark shell are harvested for food in some Asian countries.
The ark shell can be recognized by its sturdy, ridged shell.
On the sandy seabed, a diver found a large ark shell half-buried in the sediment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cockle - a small, edible, heart-shaped bivalve mollusk
