Palestine Liberation Front

1. PROPN

/ pæləsˌtaɪn ˌlɪbəˈreɪʃən frʌnt / (AmE), / ˌpælɪstiːn ˌlɪbəˈreɪʃən frʌnt / (BrE)
a Palestinian militant organization formed in 1977 following a split from the Popular Front for the Liberation of Palestine, known for attacks primarily targeting Israel
Palestinian militant group formed in 1977

Examples

The Palestine Liberation Front was involved in several attacks in the 1980s.
Many members of the Palestine Liberation Front were based in Lebanon during the organization's peak.
After breaking away from another group, the Palestine Liberation Front established its own operations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Popular Front for the Liberation of Palestine - another Palestinian militant group from which the PLF split
