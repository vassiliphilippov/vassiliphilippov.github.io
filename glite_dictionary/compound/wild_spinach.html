wild spinach

1. NOUN

/ waɪld ˈspɪnɪtʃ / (AmE), / waɪld ˈspɪnɪtʃ / (BrE)
Edible leafy greens foraged from the wild, often used as a substitute for cultivated spinach.
leafy greens used as spinach substitute

Examples

Many foragers collect wild spinach in the spring to use in salads and soups.
The chef substituted wild spinach in the recipe when she couldn't find fresh spinach at the market.
Some people prefer the stronger flavor of wild spinach over cultivated varieties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spinach - an edible green leafy plant commonly eaten as a vegetable

3. NOUN

/ waɪld ˈspɪnɪtʃ / (AmE), / waɪld ˈspɪnɪtʃ / (BrE)
An edible leafy European plant, often found in the wild and collected as a potherb, now naturalized in North America.
edible leafy plant, foraged as potherb

Examples

Foragers gathered wild spinach from the fields every spring for use in soups and salads.
She prefers wild spinach to cultivated varieties because of its stronger flavor.
The cookbook includes a recipe that calls for sautéed wild spinach as a side dish.

Related Terms

edible weedy plant, used like spinach

Examples
