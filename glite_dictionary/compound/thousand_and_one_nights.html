Thousand and One Nights

1. PROPN

/ ˌθaʊzənd ənd ˈwʌn ˈnaɪts / (AmE), / ˌθaʊzənd ənd ˈwʌn ˈnaɪts / (BrE)
A celebrated collection of Middle Eastern and South Asian folktales, originally in Arabic, dating from the 10th century.
famous collection of Arabic folktales

Examples

Many of the stories in the Thousand and One Nights have been adapted into films and novels.
He grew up enchanted by the magical tales from the Thousand and One Nights.
The themes of fate and adventure in the Thousand and One Nights have influenced writers for centuries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Arabian Nights - alternative title for the same collection of stories

Etymology

From Arabic 'Alf Layla wa-Layla' meaning 'One Thousand and One Nights'.
