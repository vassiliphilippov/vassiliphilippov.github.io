frontal cortex

1. NOUN

/ ˈfrʌntəl ˈkɔrtɛks / (AmE), / ˈfrʌnt(ə)l ˈkɔːtɛks / (BrE)
the part of the cerebral cortex located at the front of the brain, directly behind the forehead, involved in various cognitive functions such as decision making, problem solving, and voluntary movement control
front part of the brain's cortex
medical

Examples

Damage to the frontal cortex can lead to changes in personality and decision-making.
Scientists are studying how the frontal cortex is involved in attention and working memory.
Development of the frontal cortex continues into a person's early twenties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prefrontal cortex - the anterior part of the frontal cortex involved in complex cognitive behavior
  • Degree Lower: parietal cortex - the region of the cerebral cortex behind the frontal cortex
