family Saccharomycetaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌsækəroʊmaɪˌsiːtəˈsiː / (AmE), / ˌsakərəʊmaɪˌsiːtəˈsiː / (BrE)
a family of fungi that includes the typical yeasts, which reproduce by budding and ferment carbohydrates
family of yeasts (fungi)

Examples

The family Saccharomycetaceae contains species commonly used in bread and alcohol production.
Researchers study the genetic diversity within the family Saccharomycetaceae to understand yeast evolution.
Many industrial fermentation processes rely on members of the family Saccharomycetaceae for their efficiency.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yeast - a type of fungus that ferments carbohydrates

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'Saccharomyces' (yeast genus) and the suffix '-aceae' denoting a family.
