credit rating

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɛdɪt ˌreɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkrɛdɪt ˌreɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
A score or assessment that indicates a person's or organization's ability to repay debts based on their financial history.
assessment of ability to repay debts

Examples

The bank checked his credit rating before approving the loan.
A poor credit rating can make it harder to rent an apartment.
The agency lowered the country's credit rating after the financial crisis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: credit score - a numerical expression of a person's creditworthiness
  • Synonym: creditworthiness - the quality of being trusted to repay borrowed money
