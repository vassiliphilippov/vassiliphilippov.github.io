double quotes

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʌb.əl ˌkwoʊts / (AmE), / ˈdʌb.əl ˌkwəʊts / (BrE)
a pair of quotation marks (“ ”) used in writing to enclose direct speech, quotations, or titles
a pair of quotation marks (“ ”)

Examples

Please use double quotes around the spoken words in your essay.
The teacher explained the difference between double quotes and single quotes.
In American English, double quotes are typically used for direct speech.
