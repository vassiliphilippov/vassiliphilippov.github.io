mercury poisoning

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɜrkjəri ˌpɔɪzənɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmɜːkjʊri ˌpɔɪzənɪŋ / (BrE)
a medical condition caused by exposure to mercury, resulting in toxic effects on the body
toxic reaction to mercury exposure
medical

Examples

After years of working in the factory, he developed symptoms of mercury poisoning.
The doctor warned that eating too much fish could increase your risk of mercury poisoning.
Researchers are studying the effects of mercury poisoning on brain function.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lead poisoning - a condition caused by the accumulation of lead in the body
