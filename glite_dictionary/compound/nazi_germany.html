Nazi Germany

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɑzi ˈdʒɜrməni / (AmE), / ˈnɑːtsi ˈdʒɜːməni / (BrE)
the period of German history (1933–1945) when the country was under the dictatorship of Adolf Hitler and ruled by the Nazi Party
Germany under Nazi rule (1933–1945)

Examples

Nazi Germany invaded Poland in 1939, triggering the start of World War II.
Many fled their homes to escape persecution during Nazi Germany.
The rise and fall of Nazi Germany has been studied extensively by historians.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Third Reich - Nazi regime in Germany from 1933 to 1945

Etymology

From 'Nazi', short for Nationalsozialist (German for National Socialist), and 'Germany'.
