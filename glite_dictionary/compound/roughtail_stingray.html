roughtail stingray

1. NOUN

/ ˈraʊtˌteɪl ˈstɪŋˌreɪ / (AmE), / ˈraʊtˌteɪl ˈstɪŋˌreɪ / (BrE)
a large species of stingray (Dasyatis centroura) found in the western Atlantic, especially from Cape Cod to Cape Hatteras
a large stingray species of the western Atlantic

Examples

Divers spotted a massive roughtail stingray gliding near the reef off Cape Cod.
The roughtail stingray is easily recognized by its rough tail and large size.
Scientists tagged several roughtail stingrays to track their seasonal migration patterns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stingray - a type of large, flat sea fish with a long, thin tail that can sting
