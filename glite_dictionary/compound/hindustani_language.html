Hindustani language

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɪnduˈstɑni ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌhɪndʊˈstɑːni ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
A variety of Hindi, historically spoken around Delhi, which serves as a basis for both modern Hindi and Urdu.
variety of Hindi spoken around Delhi

Examples

The Hindustani language was widely used in North India during the Mughal period.
Many Bollywood films are made in Hindustani language to appeal to both Hindi and Urdu speakers.
He studied the Hindustani language to better understand the shared culture of India and Pakistan.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hindi - The official language of India, part of the Indo-Aryan language family
  • Synonym: Urdu - The national language of Pakistan, closely related to Hindi

Etymology

From Persian 'Hindūstānī' (of Hindustan, i.e., India).
