lysine intolerance

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪsin ɪnˈtɑlɚəns / (AmE), / ˈlaɪsiːn ɪnˈtɒlərəns / (BrE)
a rare disorder in which the body lacks enzymes necessary to digest the amino acid lysine, leading to metabolic problems
inability to digest lysine (medical disorder)
medical

Examples

People with lysine intolerance must avoid foods high in certain proteins to prevent complications.
Diagnosing lysine intolerance can be challenging due to its rarity and non-specific symptoms.
Lysine intolerance often requires dietary management under medical supervision.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lysine - an essential amino acid involved in protein synthesis

Etymology

From 'lysine,' an amino acid, and 'intolerance,' meaning inability to tolerate a substance.
