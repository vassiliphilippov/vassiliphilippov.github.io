leaf mold

1. NOUN

/ ˈliːf moʊld / (AmE), / ˈliːf məʊld / (BrE)
Soil consisting chiefly of decomposed leaves, used especially as a soil conditioner in gardening.
soil of decomposed leaves for gardening

Examples

Gardeners often add leaf mold to enrich the soil in their flower beds.
The forest floor was soft with years of accumulated leaf mold.
You can create your own leaf mold by allowing leaves to decompose naturally in a pile.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compost - decayed organic material used as fertilizer
