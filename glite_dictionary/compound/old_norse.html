Old Norse

1. NOUN

/ oʊld nɔrs / (AmE), / əʊld nɔːs / (BrE)
an extinct North Germanic language spoken in Scandinavia and Iceland from about 700 to 1350
medieval Scandinavian language

Examples

Many Viking sagas were originally written in Old Norse.
Scholars study Old Norse to better understand Norse mythology and medieval literature.
The influence of Old Norse can still be found in modern Scandinavian languages.

Etymology

From the English word 'old' and 'Norse', referring to the language of Norsemen.
