Annamese language

1. NOUN

/ ˌænəˈmiz ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌanəˈmiːz ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
an old or historical term for the Vietnamese language, a Mon-Khmer language spoken primarily in Vietnam
historical term for the Vietnamese language

Examples

The old explorer learned to speak Annamese language during his travels in French Indochina.
Some colonial-era documents were translated from Annamese language to French.
Linguists once classified Vietnamese as the Annamese language, reflecting historical terminology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Vietnamese - the national language of Vietnam

Etymology

From Annam, an old name for a region of Vietnam, combined with -ese (denoting language or people).
