graduated tax

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡrædʒuˌeɪtɪd tæks / (AmE), / ˈɡrædjʊeɪtɪd tæks / (BrE)
A tax system in which the tax rate increases as the amount subject to taxation increases.
tax with increasing rates by amount

Examples

Many economists argue that a graduated tax system is fairer to lower-income individuals.
The country implemented a graduated tax to address income inequality.
Under a graduated tax, higher earners pay a higher percentage of their income.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: progressive tax - a tax in which the rate increases as the taxable amount increases; synonymous concept
  • Antonym: regressive tax - a tax in which the rate decreases as the taxable amount increases; opposite concept
