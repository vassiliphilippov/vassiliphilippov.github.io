ship's officer

1. NOUN

/ ʃɪps ˈɑfəsər / (AmE), / ʃɪps ˈɒfɪsə / (BrE)
a person who holds a position of authority and responsibility on a vessel, such as a captain, mate, or engineer
person in authority on a ship

Examples

The ship's officer conducted a safety drill for all passengers and crew.
As a ship's officer, she was responsible for navigating through rough waters.
Each ship's officer must be fully trained in emergency procedures.

Synset Examples

he is the officer in charge of the ship's engines

Related Terms

  • Synonym: captain - the person in overall command of a ship
  • Degree Lower: crew member - a person who works on a ship, but without specific authority
