ohmic resistance

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊmɪk rɪˈzɪstəns / (AmE), / ˈəʊmɪk rɪˈzɪstəns / (BrE)
The resistance offered by a material to the flow of electric current that obeys Ohm's law, measured in ohms.
electrical resistance obeying Ohm's law
medical

Examples

The ohmic resistance of the wire increases as its length increases.
Accurate measurement of ohmic resistance is essential for electrical circuit design.
Copper has a low ohmic resistance compared to other metals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resistance - property of opposing the flow of electric current
  • Synonym: impedance - measure of opposition to alternating current, including resistance and reactance

Etymology

From 'ohmic', relating to Ohm's law, named after German physicist Georg Ohm.
