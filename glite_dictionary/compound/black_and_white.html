black and white

1. ADJ

/ ˌblæk ən ˈwaɪt / (AmE), / ˌblæk ən ˈwaɪt / (BrE)
(of images, film, television, etc.) showing only shades of black, white, and gray; not in color.
not showing or capable of showing color

Examples

The old photographs were all in black and white, giving them a timeless quality.
He prefers watching classic black and white movies from the 1940s.
The newspaper published a black and white image on the front page due to printing limitations.

Synset Examples

black-and-white film
a black-and-white TV
the movie was in black and white

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monochrome - an image or picture developed or executed in black and white or in varying tones of only one color
  • Antonym: color - having hues; not limited to black, white, or gray tones

2. NOUN

/ ˌblæk ən ˈwaɪt / (AmE), / ˌblæk ən ˈwaɪt / (BrE)
a photograph, film, or television image that is produced in shades of black, white, and gray, without color
photograph or film without color

Examples

The old family album was filled with black and white photographs from the 1940s.
He prefers to shoot movies in black and white for a more classic feel.
The exhibition features stunning black and white portraits of jazz musicians.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monochrome - an image in shades of a single color (typically black and white for photos)
  • Antonym: color photograph - a photograph in full color, as opposed to black and white
