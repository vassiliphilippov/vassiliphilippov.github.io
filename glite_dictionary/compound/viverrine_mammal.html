viverrine mammal

1. NOUN

/ vɪˈvɛraɪn ˈmæməl / (AmE), / vɪˈvɛrɪn ˈmaməl / (BrE)
a small, cat-like predatory mammal native to the warmer regions of the Old World, belonging to the family Viverridae, such as civets and genets.
cat-like predatory mammal of the Old World

Examples

The African civet is a well-known viverrine mammal found in various habitats across the continent.
Many viverrine mammals have long bodies and short legs, making them agile hunters at night.
Researchers are studying the diet of different viverrine mammals to understand their role in the ecosystem.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: civet - a small, cat-like mammal known for its musky scent
  • Synonym: genet - a slender, cat-like carnivorous mammal of the Viverridae family

Etymology

From Latin *Viverra* (genus name for civet) and English 'mammal'.
