primary colour for pigments

1. NOUN

/ ˈpraɪˌmɛri ˈkʌlər fər ˈpɪɡmənts / (AmE), / ˈpraɪməri ˈkʌlə fə ˈpɪɡmənts / (BrE)
any of the three basic pigments—red, blue, and yellow—from which all other colours can be produced by mixing.
basic pigment (red, blue, yellow) for colour mixing

Examples

Artists learn about primary colour for pigments to understand how to mix paints effectively.
Red, blue, and yellow are the primary colours for pigments used in traditional painting.
By blending two primary colours for pigments, you can create a wide array of other shades.

Synset Examples

the primary colors for pigments are red, blue, and yellow

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: secondary colour - a colour produced by mixing two primary colours
