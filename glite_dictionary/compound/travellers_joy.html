traveller's joy

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrævələrz ˌdʒɔɪ / (AmE), / ˈtræv(ə)ləz ˌdʒɔɪ / (BrE)
a vigorous deciduous climbing plant (Clematis vitalba) native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa, with fragrant greenish-white flowers borne in panicles during summer and autumn
a climbing plant with greenish-white flowers (Clematis vitalba)

Examples

The hedgerows were covered in traveller's joy blossoming in the late summer sun.
Gardeners sometimes plant traveller's joy for its attractive flowers and rapid growth.
In some regions, traveller's joy is considered invasive because it spreads quickly.
