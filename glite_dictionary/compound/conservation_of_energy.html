conservation of energy

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑnsərˈveɪʃən əv ˈɛnərdʒi / (AmE), / ˌkɒnsəˈveɪʃən əv ˈɛnədʒi / (BrE)
a fundamental principle of physics stating that the total energy in an isolated system remains constant and cannot be created or destroyed, only transformed
principle that total energy is constant

Examples

The conservation of energy explains why energy cannot be magically created or destroyed in physical processes.
Understanding the conservation of energy is crucial when studying thermodynamics.
Engineers use the principle of conservation of energy when designing more efficient machines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conservation of mass - principle that mass is neither created nor destroyed in a closed system
