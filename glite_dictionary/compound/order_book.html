order book

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdɚ bʊk / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə bʊk / (BrE)
a printed or written record showing the sequence of activities or items scheduled for a particular day, especially in formal organizations or meetings.
record of scheduled activities for a day

Examples

The secretary placed the order book on the chairman's desk before the meeting began.
According to the order book, the award ceremony is scheduled for noon.
She double-checked the order book to confirm the sequence of speakers.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdər bʊk / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə bʊk / (BrE)
a book used to record customers' orders, often producing duplicate copies for record-keeping or processing
book for recording customers' orders

Examples

The shopkeeper wrote my purchase details in the order book.
At the end of each day, she reviewed the order book to track sales.
Each carbon copy from the order book was sent to a different department for processing.
