passion fruit

1. NOUN

/ ˈpæʃən fruːt / (AmE), / ˈpæʃən fruːt / (BrE)
An egg-shaped tropical fruit with a sweet-tart flavor, produced by certain passionflower vines and used in desserts, drinks, and confectionery.
tropical fruit with sweet-tart flavor

Examples

The juice from a ripe passion fruit adds a refreshing zing to cocktails.
She decorated the cake with slices of passion fruit for a tropical touch.
Many sorbets use passion fruit to achieve a vibrant, tangy flavor.

Etymology

From 'passion' in reference to the passionflower genus (Passiflora), so named by 16th-century Spanish missionaries, and 'fruit'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpæʃən frut / (AmE), / ˈpæʃən fruːt / (BrE)
a small, round, tropical fruit with a tough rind and aromatic, seedy pulp, known for its tart flavor
tropical fruit with tart, seedy pulp

Examples

The cheesecake was topped with fresh passion fruit for extra flavor.
She bought a basket of ripe passion fruits at the exotic fruit market.
Some people find the taste of passion fruit juice too sour, but I enjoy it.

Synset Examples

I love the tart flavor of fresh passion fruit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: granadilla - another name for certain types of passion fruit, especially in South America

Etymology

The name comes from the passion flower, from which the fruit grows. 'Passion' refers to the Passion of Christ, as the flower's structure was seen by Spanish missionaries as symbolic of the crucifixion.
