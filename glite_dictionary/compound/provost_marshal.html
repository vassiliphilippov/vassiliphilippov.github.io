provost marshal

1. NOUN

/ ˈproʊvoʊst ˈmɑrʃəl / (AmE), / ˈprəʊvɒst ˈmɑːʃəl / (BrE)
an officer who supervises the military police in an army
military police supervisor (officer)

Examples

The provost marshal is responsible for maintaining discipline among soldiers.
After the riot, the provost marshal reviewed security procedures at the base.
Each provost marshal must coordinate closely with commanding officers during operations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: military police - the police organization within a military force

Etymology

From French *prévôt* (magistrate, overseer) and English *marshal* (military officer).
