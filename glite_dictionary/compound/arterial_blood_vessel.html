arterial blood vessel

1. NOUN

/ ɑːrˈtɪriəl blʌd ˈvɛsəl / (AmE), / ɑːˈtɪəriəl blʌd ˈvɛsəl / (BrE)
A blood vessel that carries blood away from the heart to the body.
blood vessel carrying blood from the heart
medical

Examples

An arterial blood vessel transports oxygen-rich blood to various tissues in the body.
A blockage in an arterial blood vessel can lead to a heart attack or stroke.
Surgeons must be careful not to damage an arterial blood vessel during operations near the heart.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: artery - a blood vessel that carries blood away from the heart
  • Antonym: vein - a blood vessel that carries blood towards the heart

Etymology

From 'arterial', relating to arteries, and 'blood vessel'. 'Artery' comes from Greek 'artēria' (windpipe, artery).
