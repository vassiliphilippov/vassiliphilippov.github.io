topical anaesthesia

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɑːpɪkəl ˌænəsˈθiːʒə / (AmE), / ˈtɒpɪkəl ˌanɪsˈθiːziə / (BrE)
loss of sensation in a specific area of the skin or mucous membrane, produced by applying a local anesthetic to the surface
surface loss of sensation from applied anesthetic
medical

Examples

The dentist applied topical anaesthesia to numb the area before giving the injection.
Topical anaesthesia is often used for minor surgical procedures on the skin.
Patients with a sore throat sometimes benefit from sprays that provide topical anaesthesia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: local anaesthesia - loss of sensation in a specific area by anesthetic injections or applications
  • Antonym: general anaesthesia - loss of sensation and consciousness throughout the whole body

Etymology

From topical, meaning 'applied to a particular place,' and anaesthesia, from Greek anaisthēsia (insensibility).
