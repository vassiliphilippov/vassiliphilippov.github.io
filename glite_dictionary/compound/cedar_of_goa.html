cedar of Goa

1. NOUN

/ ˈsiːdər əv ˈgoʊə / (AmE), / ˈsiːdə əv ˈgəʊə / (BrE)
a tall, spreading evergreen tree native to Mexico, with drooping branches, believed to have been introduced into Portugal from Goa
Mexican evergreen tree with drooping branches

Examples

The cedar of Goa is valued for its attractive drooping branches and evergreen foliage.
Botanists believe the cedar of Goa was introduced into Portugal from Goa centuries ago.
We admired the majestic shape of the cedar of Goa in the botanical gardens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cedar - a type of large evergreen coniferous tree
