pudding stone

1. NOUN

/ ˈpʊdɪŋ stoʊn / (AmE), / ˈpʊdɪŋ stəʊn / (BrE)
a type of conglomerate rock consisting of pebbles and particles of varying size cemented together in a finer-grained matrix.
a conglomerate rock of pebbles in a matrix

Examples

The geologist pointed out a large pudding stone on the riverbank, noting its embedded pebbles and rounded shape.
Collectors often seek out pudding stone specimens because of their distinctive appearance and colorful inclusions.
Some local buildings were constructed using pudding stone, giving them a unique and recognizable look.

Etymology

Named for its resemblance to traditional boiled puddings containing many pieces (such as raisins) within a matrix.
