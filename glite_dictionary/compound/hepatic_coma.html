hepatic coma

1. NOUN

/ hɪˈpætɪk ˈkoʊmə / (AmE), / hɪˈpætɪk ˈkəʊmə / (BrE)
A coma occurring in severe cases of liver disease, due to the accumulation of toxins that the liver cannot remove.
coma caused by severe liver disease
medical

Examples

The patient lapsed into a hepatic coma after suffering from chronic liver failure.
Early detection of symptoms can sometimes prevent progression to hepatic coma.
Doctors monitored her closely for signs of hepatic coma following her diagnosis of severe cirrhosis.

Etymology

From Greek *hēpatikos* (relating to the liver) and Latin *coma* (deep sleep, unconsciousness).
