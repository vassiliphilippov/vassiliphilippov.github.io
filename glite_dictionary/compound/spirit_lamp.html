spirit lamp

1. NOUN

/ ˈspɪrɪt læmp / (AmE), / ˈspɪrɪt lamp / (BrE)
a small lamp that burns alcohol or another volatile liquid as fuel, typically used for heating in laboratories or for demonstrations.
lamp burning liquid fuel for heating

Examples

The chemist used a spirit lamp to heat the solution gently.
In science class, we learned how to safely light a spirit lamp.
A small spirit lamp was placed under the beaker to boil the water.

Etymology

From 'spirit' (alcohol, especially ethanol) + 'lamp', referring to a lamp fueled by alcohol.
