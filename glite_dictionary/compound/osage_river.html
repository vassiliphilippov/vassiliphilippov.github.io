Osage River

1. PROPN

/ ˈoʊseɪdʒ ˈrɪvər / (AmE), / ˈəʊseɪdʒ ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
a river in Missouri which flows into the Missouri River
river in Missouri, USA

Examples

The Osage River winds through central Missouri before joining the Missouri River.
Many recreational activities take place along the Osage River during the summer months.
After heavy rainfall, the Osage River can rise quickly and cause flooding in nearby areas.

Etymology

Named after the Osage Nation, Native American people who lived in the region.
