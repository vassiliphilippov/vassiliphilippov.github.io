genus Trachipterus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌtræk.ɪˈfiː.tər.əs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌtræk.ɪˈfiː.tər.əs / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Trachipteridae, comprising certain ribbonfishes found in deep oceans.
type genus of ribbonfishes (Trachipteridae)

Examples

The genus Trachipterus includes several species of deep-sea ribbonfish.
Scientists recently discovered a new species within the genus Trachipterus.
The elongated bodies of fish in the genus Trachipterus are well adapted to open ocean life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Trachipteridae - Family of ribbonfishes containing the genus Trachipterus

Etymology

From Latin 'Trachipterus', from Greek 'trachys' (rough) + 'pteron' (fin, wing), used as a scientific name.
