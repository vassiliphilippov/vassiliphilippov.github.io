tartare sauce

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɑr.tɑr sɔs / (AmE), / ˈtɑː.tɑː sɔːs / (BrE)
A cold sauce made from mayonnaise and chopped pickles, often with capers, shallots, parsley, and sometimes hard-cooked egg, typically served with fish or seafood.
mayonnaise-based sauce for seafood

Examples

He ordered fish and chips with extra tartare sauce on the side.
A spoonful of tartare sauce adds a zesty flavor to fried calamari.
Many people dip their shrimp in tartare sauce instead of cocktail sauce.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mayonnaise - a thick, creamy dressing of egg yolks, oil, and vinegar or lemon juice

Etymology

From French 'sauce tartare', originally referring to the Tatar people but later applied to the sauce.
