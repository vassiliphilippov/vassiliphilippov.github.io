petty whin

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛti wɪn / (AmE), / ˈpɛti wɪn / (BrE)
A small, prickly shrub with yellow flowers, found on moors in New England and Europe.
prickly yellow-flowered moor shrub

Examples

The petty whin adds a splash of yellow to the moorland during summer.
Botanists surveyed patches of petty whin in the heathland reserve.
She carefully avoided the sharp spines of the petty whin as she walked.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gorse - a larger related shrub with yellow flowers and spines
  • Synonym: broom - another similar yellow-flowered shrub of the pea family
