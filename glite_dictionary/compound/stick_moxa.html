stick moxa

1. NOUN

/ stɪk ˈmɑksə / (AmE), / stɪk ˈmɒksə / (BrE)
A stick made from dried mugwort leaves, used in moxibustion for traditional Asian medicine.
mugwort stick for moxibustion

Examples

The practitioner lit a stick moxa and carefully held it near the patient's skin during the therapy session.
She purchased several stick moxa rolls for her traditional medicine practice.
To stimulate the acupuncture point, the therapist used a smoldering stick moxa.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moxibustion - a traditional Chinese medicine therapy involving burning moxa

Etymology

From Japanese 'moxa' (from Chinese 'mogusa', meaning mugwort); 'stick' refers to its cylindrical form.
