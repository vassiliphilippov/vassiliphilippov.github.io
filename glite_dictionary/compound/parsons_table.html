Parsons table

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑrsənz ˌteɪbəl / (AmE), / ˈpɑːsənz ˌteɪbəl / (BrE)
a sturdy rectangular table with legs at each corner, where the tabletop and legs are of equal width, creating a clean, modern appearance
rectangular table with legs and top of equal width

Examples

The living room featured a sleek Parsons table in the center, giving the space a minimalist look.
She placed her coffee mug on the Parsons table while reading the newspaper.
Modern designers often choose a Parsons table for its simple, versatile design.

Etymology

Named after the Parsons School of Design in New York, where the style was developed in the 1930s.
