audiovisual aid

1. NOUN

/ ˌɔːdioʊˈvɪʒuəl eɪd / (AmE), / ˌɔːdiəˈvɪʒuəl eɪd / (BrE)
a tool or material, such as a film, tape, or slide, that uses both sight and sound to present information, especially for teaching or training purposes
teaching material using sight and sound

Examples

The teacher used an audiovisual aid to explain the complex scientific process.
Modern classrooms often incorporate audiovisual aids such as videos and interactive whiteboards.
She found that students learned better when an audiovisual aid accompanied the lecture.

Synset Examples

language tapes and videocassettes and other audiovisuals

Related Terms

  • Synonym: teaching aid - an object or device used to improve the learning process

Etymology

From 'audio-' (Latin ‘audire’ to hear) and 'visual' (from Latin ‘videre’ to see), plus 'aid' (from Old French 'aide', help).
