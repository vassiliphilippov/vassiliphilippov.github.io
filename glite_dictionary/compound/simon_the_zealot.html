Simon the Zealot

1. PROPN

/ ˈsaɪmən ðə ˈzɛlət / (AmE), / ˈsaɪmən ðə ˈziːələt / (BrE)
one of the twelve Apostles of Jesus Christ, distinguished from others named Simon by his epithet 'the Zealot'.
one of the twelve Apostles of Jesus
american british

Examples

Simon the Zealot is sometimes also called Simon the Canaanite in the Gospels.
Many churches are named after Simon the Zealot in recognition of his role as an Apostle.
Little is known about the later life of Simon the Zealot, but he is venerated as a saint.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Judas Iscariot - another of the twelve Apostles, known for betraying Jesus
  • Synonym: Apostle Peter - one of the leading apostles, also called Simon Peter

Etymology

'Zealot' refers to zeal or fervor; possibly from Greek 'zēlōtēs', meaning 'zealous one'.
