Rydberg constant

1. NOUN

/ ˈraɪdˌbɜrɡ ˈkɑnstənt / (AmE), / ˈrɪdˌbɜːɡ ˈkɒnstənt / (BrE)
a physical constant representing the limiting value of the highest wavenumber of any photon emitted from the hydrogen atom, used in spectroscopy
spectroscopy constant for hydrogen atom

Examples

The Rydberg constant plays a crucial role in calculating the wavelengths of spectral lines for hydrogen.
To determine atomic transitions accurately, scientists rely on the Rydberg constant.
She referenced the Rydberg constant in her explanation of the element's emission spectra.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Planck constant - a physical constant important in quantum mechanics

Etymology

Named after the Swedish physicist Johannes Rydberg (1854–1919), who studied atomic spectra.
