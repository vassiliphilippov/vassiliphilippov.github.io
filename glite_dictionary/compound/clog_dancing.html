clog dancing

1. NOUN

/ klɑɡ ˈdænsɪŋ / (AmE), / klɒɡ ˈdɑːnsɪŋ / (BrE)
A traditional dance performed while wearing shoes with wooden soles, characterized by heavy stamping steps.
dance with wooden-soled shoes

Examples

She has been practicing clog dancing for an upcoming folk festival.
The group gave a lively clog dancing performance in traditional costume.
Many people find the sound of clog dancing to be both rhythmic and exciting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tap dancing - A dance involving rhythmical tapping of the toes and heels, often with metal plates attached to the shoes.

Etymology

Derived from 'clog' (a type of wooden-soled shoe) and 'dancing'.
