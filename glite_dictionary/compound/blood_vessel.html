blood vessel

1. NOUN

/ blʌd ˈvɛsəl / (AmE), / blʌd ˈvɛsəl / (BrE)
Any of the tubular structures, such as arteries, veins, or capillaries, through which blood circulates in the body.
tube for carrying blood in the body

Examples

A blocked blood vessel can lead to a heart attack or stroke.
The surgeon repaired the damaged blood vessel during the operation.
High blood pressure can weaken the walls of a blood vessel over time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: artery - a blood vessel that carries blood away from the heart
  • Synonym: vein - a blood vessel that returns blood to the heart
  • Synonym: capillary - a very small blood vessel connecting arteries and veins
