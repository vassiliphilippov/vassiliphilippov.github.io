preventive medicine

1. NOUN

/ prɪˈvɛntɪv ˈmɛdɪsən / (AmE), / prɪˈvɛntɪv ˈmɛdɪsɪn / (BrE)
the branch of medicine focused on preventing the occurrence of diseases rather than treating them after they arise
branch of medicine preventing disease
medical

Examples

Many diseases can be avoided with regular preventive medicine check-ups and a healthy lifestyle.
The government is investing more in preventive medicine to lower long-term healthcare costs.
She chose to specialize in preventive medicine to help stop illnesses before they start.

Synset Examples

the medical establishment doesn't profit from preventive medicine

Related Terms

  • Synonym: public health - the science and practice of protecting and improving the health of people and their communities

Etymology

From Latin 'praeventivus' (preventing) and 'medicina' (medicine).
