gooseberry eye

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡuːsˌbɛri aɪ / (AmE), / ˈɡʊzbəri aɪ / (BrE)
a look expressing suspicion, jealousy, disapproval, or anger
disapproving or suspicious look

Examples

She gave her coworker a gooseberry eye when he interrupted her presentation.
Whenever he heard gossip, he would glance over with a gooseberry eye.
I noticed the teacher throwing a gooseberry eye at the chattering students.

Synset Examples

I caught her giving me a gooseberry eye during the meeting.
She shot him a gooseberry eye for arriving late.
His gooseberry eye warned him to stay quiet.
