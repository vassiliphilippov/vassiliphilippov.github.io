chromatic aberration

1. NOUN

/ kroʊˈmætɪk əˌbɛrəˈʃən / (AmE), / krəʊˈmatɪk əˌbɛrəˈʃən / (BrE)
An optical fault in which a lens fails to focus all colors to the same point, resulting in colored fringes around images.
optical fault causing colored fringes

Examples

The photographer noticed chromatic aberration around the edges of the bright white clouds in her images.
Newer lenses are designed to minimize chromatic aberration, resulting in sharper and clearer photographs.
Chromatic aberration can often be corrected using advanced image editing software.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spherical aberration - a type of optical aberration caused by spherical surfaces of lenses or mirrors

Etymology

From Greek 'khrōmatikos' (relating to color) and Latin 'aberratio' (a wandering), used in optics since the 18th century.
