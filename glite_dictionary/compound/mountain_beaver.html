mountain beaver

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaʊntən ˈbiːvɚ / (AmE), / ˈmaʊntən ˈbiːvə / (BrE)
a bulky, nocturnal, burrowing rodent (Aplodontia rufa) found in the uplands along the Pacific coast of North America, considered the most primitive living rodent
nocturnal burrowing rodent of Pacific North America

Examples

The mountain beaver is rarely seen during the day because it is nocturnal.
Unlike the common beaver, the mountain beaver does not build dams.
Biologists consider the mountain beaver a living fossil due to its primitive features.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rodent - a mammal of the order Rodentia, such as a mouse or beaver
