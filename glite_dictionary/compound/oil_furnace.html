oil furnace

1. NOUN

/ ɔɪl ˈfɝːnɪs / (AmE), / ɔɪl ˈfɜːnɪs / (BrE)
a heating device that burns oil to produce heat, commonly used in buildings
a furnace that burns oil for heat

Examples

We had to replace our old oil furnace with a more efficient heating system.
The technician serviced the oil furnace before winter arrived.
Some rural homes still rely on an oil furnace for central heating.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oil heater - a device that heats by burning oil or using electric oil-filled elements
  • Antonym: gas furnace - a furnace that burns gas as fuel
