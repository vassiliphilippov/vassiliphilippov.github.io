cardiac plexus

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑr.di.æk ˈplɛk.səs / (AmE), / ˈkɑː.di.æk ˈplɛk.səs / (BrE)
a network of nerves that supplies the heart and nearby structures
network of nerves supplying the heart
medical

Examples

The cardiac plexus plays a crucial role in regulating heart rate and rhythm.
Doctors studied the patient's cardiac plexus to determine the source of his chest pain.
Damage to the cardiac plexus can affect normal heart function.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: solar plexus - a complex network of nerves in the abdomen

Etymology

From Latin 'cardiacus' (of the heart) + Latin 'plexus' (network).
