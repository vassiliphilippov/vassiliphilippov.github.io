lardizabala family

1. NOUN

/ lɑːrdɪˈzæbələ ˈfæməli / (AmE), / lɑːdɪˈzæbələ ˈfæmɪli / (BrE)
A family of flowering plants, including thick-stemmed climbing vines (lianas) and some shrubs, some of which produce edible fruit, scientifically known as the Lardizabalaceae.
a family of flowering plants (Lardizabalaceae)

Examples

The lardizabala family contains several species known for their unusual climbing habits and showy flowers.
Some fruits from the lardizabala family are considered delicacies in parts of South America.
Botanists have studied the lardizabala family to understand the evolution of lianas.

Etymology

Named after the genus Lardizabala, itself named in honor of the Spanish botanist Miguel de Lardizábal.
