harbor seal

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑrbɚ siːl / (AmE), / ˈhɑːbə siːl / (BrE)
a small, spotted seal (Phoca vitulina) found in coastal waters of the Northern Hemisphere, especially along North American and European coasts
a small spotted coastal seal

Examples

We spotted a harbor seal swimming near the rocky shoreline.
The harbor seal population has rebounded due to conservation efforts.
A young harbor seal basked in the sun on the sandbar.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seal - a fish-eating aquatic mammal with flippers
  • Degree Higher: grey seal - large seal of the North Atlantic, related species
