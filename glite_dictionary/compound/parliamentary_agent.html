parliamentary agent

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑːrləˈmɛntɛri ˈeɪdʒənt / (AmE), / ˌpɑːlɪəˈmɛntəri ˈeɪdʒənt / (BrE)
a person, especially in Britain, employed to represent the interests of businesses or other organizations in dealings with acts or procedures of Parliament
person representing organizations before Parliament
british legal

Examples

The company hired a parliamentary agent to navigate the complex legislation affecting their industry.
As a parliamentary agent, she regularly liaised with Members of Parliament on behalf of her clients.
Historically, parliamentary agents played a crucial role in promoting private bills in the UK Parliament.
