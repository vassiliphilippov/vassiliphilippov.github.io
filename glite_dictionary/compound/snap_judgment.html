snap judgment

1. NOUN

/ snæp ˈdʒʌdʒmənt / (AmE), / snæp ˈdʒʌdʒmənt / (BrE)
A decision made quickly and without careful thought, often leading to unfair or inaccurate results.
quick, often careless decision

Examples

Try not to make a snap judgment about someone you've just met.
Her snap judgment resulted in a missed opportunity.
It's wise to pause and reflect rather than issue a snap judgment.

Synset Examples

Avoid making a snap judgment without all the facts.
Making a snap judgment could lead to misunderstandings.
He regretted his snap judgment about her character.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hasty decision - a decision made without sufficient thought or information
  • Antonym: considered opinion - an opinion or judgment formed after careful thought
