back talk

1. NOUN

/ bæk tɑk / (AmE), / bak tɔːk / (BrE)
disrespectful or impudent remarks made in response to authority; rude replies or retorts
disrespectful reply; rude retort
informal

Examples

The teacher grew tired of the student's constant back talk during class.
When told to clean his room, he responded with some back talk that got him grounded.
She warned her children that back talk would not be tolerated in the house.

Synset Examples

don't give me any of your sass

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sass - impudent or disrespectful talk; cheeky response
  • Synonym: insolence - rude or disrespectful behavior, especially to someone in authority
  • Antonym: respect - polite or deferential behavior or talk, especially to authority
