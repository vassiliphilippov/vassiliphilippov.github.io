Scotch marigold

1. NOUN

/ skɑtʃ ˈmærɪˌɡoʊld / (AmE), / skɒtʃ ˈmærɪɡəʊld / (BrE)
a common European annual plant (Calendula officinalis) with bright yellow or orange flowers, widely cultivated for ornament and herbal uses
a European annual garden marigold

Examples

The vibrant blossoms of the Scotch marigold brighten gardens throughout the summer months.
Some herbal teas contain petals from the Scotch marigold for their supposed healing properties.
She planted rows of Scotch marigolds along the walkway for a colorful border.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pot marigold - another common name for Calendula officinalis
