slave trader

1. NOUN

/ sleɪv ˈtreɪ.dɚ / (AmE), / sleɪv ˈtreɪ.də / (BrE)
a person who buys and sells people as slaves or engages in the business of trading slaves
person who trades slaves

Examples

The museum includes exhibits about the role of the slave trader in colonial history.
He made his fortune as a notorious slave trader in the eighteenth century.
Public opinion eventually turned against those who profited as slave traders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slaver - a person engaged in slave trading
