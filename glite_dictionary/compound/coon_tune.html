coon tune

1. NOUN

/ kun tun / (AmE), / kuːn tjuːn / (BrE)
an old term for a style of song associated with African American performers, often containing racist stereotypes and lyrics; considered highly offensive and derogatory today
derogatory term for a type of song, now offensive
offensive archaic

Examples

The band refused to play a coon tune due to its racist history.
Historians study the role of the coon tune in early American music.
That minstrel show featured a coon tune that shocked the modern audience with its offensive lyrics.

Synset Examples

She hummed a coon tune while walking down the street.
That old coon tune always brings back memories.
He played a coon tune that lively filled the room.

Etymology

Derived from 'coon' (a racial slur for Black people) and 'tune' (song). The term was popularized in late 19th and early 20th century minstrel and vaudeville shows.
