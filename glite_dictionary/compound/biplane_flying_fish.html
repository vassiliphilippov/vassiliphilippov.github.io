biplane flying fish

1. NOUN

/ ˈbaɪˌpleɪn ˈflaɪɪŋ ˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈbʌɪˌpleɪn ˈflʌɪɪŋ ˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
A flying fish with both pectoral and pelvic fins enlarged, allowing it to glide long distances above water.
flying fish with enlarged pectoral and pelvic fins

Examples

The biplane flying fish is able to glide remarkably far thanks to its large pelvic and pectoral fins.
Scientists observed a biplane flying fish hovering just above the ocean waves.
Unlike other species, the biplane flying fish utilizes both sets of enlarged fins for extended flight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flying fish - a fish capable of gliding above water using enlarged fins

Etymology

Formed by analogy with 'biplane', describing two sets of fins similar to a biplane's wings.
