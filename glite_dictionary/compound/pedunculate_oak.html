pedunculate oak

1. NOUN

/ pɪˈdʌŋkjələt oʊk / (AmE), / pɪˈdʌŋkjələt əʊk / (BrE)
a medium to large deciduous European oak (Quercus robur) with smooth, rounded-lobed leaves, yielding hard, strong, light-colored wood
European oak tree species (Quercus robur)

Examples

The ancient pedunculate oak in the park is estimated to be over 400 years old.
Carpenters prized the wood of the pedunculate oak for its strength and durability.
In many European forests, the pedunculate oak is a dominant tree species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oak - a type of tree in the genus Quercus

Etymology

From 'pedunculate', meaning having a stalk, from Latin 'pedunculus' (a small foot or stalk), referring to the stalked acorns.
