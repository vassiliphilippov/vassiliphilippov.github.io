or else

1. SCONJ

/ ɔːr ɛls / (AmE), / ɔː ɛls / (BrE)
used to introduce the consequence if something does not happen or is not done; otherwise.
to indicate a consequence if not, otherwise

Examples

You must leave now, or else you will miss the train.
Lock the door, or else someone might get in.
Bring an umbrella, or else you could get soaked if it rains.

Synset Examples

You need to apologize, or else she won't forgive you.
Finish your homework now, or else you'll be grounded.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: otherwise - if not; or else
