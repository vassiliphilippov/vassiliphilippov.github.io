pitch blackness

1. NOUN

/ pɪtʃ ˈblæknəs / (AmE), / pɪtʃ ˈblæknɪs / (BrE)
A state or condition of complete darkness; total absence of light.
total darkness; absence of light

Examples

The cave was filled with pitch blackness, making it impossible to see anything.
They wandered through the forest, surrounded by pitch blackness after their flashlight died.
During the power outage, the house was left in complete pitch blackness.

Synset Examples

they fumbled around in total darkness
in the black of night

Related Terms

  • Synonym: darkness - absence of light
  • Degree Lower: gloom - partial or deep darkness; dimness
  • Degree Lower: twilight - faint light before sunrise or after sunset
  • Antonym: brightness - the quality of being full of light

Etymology

From 'pitch' (black, sticky substance) + 'blackness', indicating the extreme depth of darkness.
