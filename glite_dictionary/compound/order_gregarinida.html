order Gregarinida

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdər ɡrɛɡəˈraɪnɪdə / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə ɡrɛɡəˈraɪnɪdə / (BrE)
a taxonomic order of parasitic protozoa in the subclass Telosporidia, including the gregarines
an order of parasitic protozoa (gregarines)
medical

Examples

Scientists discovered a new species in the order Gregarinida while studying marine invertebrates.
The life cycle of protozoa in the order Gregarinida is complex and involves multiple hosts.
Many members of the order Gregarinida are known to parasitize the digestive tracts of invertebrates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gregarine - a parasitic protozoan of the group Gregarinida

Etymology

From modern Latin Gregarinida, from Gregarina (genus) + -ida (taxonomic suffix).
