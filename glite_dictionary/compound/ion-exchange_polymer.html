ion-exchange polymer

1. NOUN

/ ˌaɪ.ɑn ɪksˈtʃeɪndʒ ˈpɑl.i.mɚ / (AmE), / ˌaɪ.ɒn ɪksˈtʃeɪndʒ ˈpɒl.ɪ.mə / (BrE)
a synthetic polymer containing functional groups that can exchange ions with those in a surrounding solution, commonly used in water purification and chemical separation processes
polymer used for ion exchange in solutions
medical

Examples

Water treatment systems often use ion-exchange polymers to remove unwanted minerals.
Scientists developed a new ion-exchange polymer with improved capacity for heavy metal ions.
The effectiveness of the ion-exchange polymer depends on the nature of its functional groups.

Synset Examples

Sodium polystyrene sulfonate is a strongly acidic ion-exchange resin and is used to treat hyperkalemia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ion-exchange resin - an insoluble matrix that exchanges ions in a solution

Etymology

Formed from 'ion' (from Greek ion, meaning 'going') + 'exchange' (Latin ex- + Cambiare, to exchange) + 'polymer' (from Greek polus, many + meros, part).
