innate reflex

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈneɪt rɪˈflɛks / (AmE), / ɪˈneɪt rɪˈflɛks / (BrE)
an automatic, instinctive reaction to a stimulus that occurs without prior learning or experience
automatic, unlearned response to a stimulus

Examples

The sucking response in infants is an innate reflex that helps them feed.
Touching something hot triggers an innate reflex causing you to quickly withdraw your hand.
Doctors often check an innate reflex like the knee-jerk to assess nervous system health.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: conditioned reflex - a learned response to a previously neutral stimulus
  • Synonym: reflex - an automatic, involuntary response to a stimulus

Etymology

From Latin *innatus* (inborn) and *reflexus* (bent back, turned back).
