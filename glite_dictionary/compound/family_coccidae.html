family Coccidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ˌkɑːkɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli ˌkɒsɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of small insects known as scale insects, many of which are harmful plant pests.
a family of scale insects

Examples

Many tropical crops are vulnerable to infestation by family Coccidae members.
Several species in family Coccidae damage ornamental plants by sucking sap.
Entomologists often study family Coccidae to understand pest control in agriculture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scale insect - a small insect of the family Coccidae that feeds on plants

Etymology

From scientific Latin Coccidae, after 'coccus' meaning 'berry' or 'grain', referring to the insects' shape.
