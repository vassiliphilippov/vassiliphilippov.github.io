vinegar tree

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪnəɡər tri / (AmE), / ˈvɪnɪɡə triː / (BrE)
A North American deciduous shrub or small tree (Rhus typhina) with compound leaves that turn brilliant red in autumn and dense clusters of greenish-yellow flowers followed by crimson, acidic berries.
deciduous North American shrub with red autumn leaves and acidic berries

Examples

The vinegar tree is known for its brilliant red foliage in the fall.
Birds are often attracted to the crimson berries of the vinegar tree.
Native Americans used the berries of the vinegar tree to make a refreshing drink.

Etymology

Named for the acidic taste of its berries, which are used to make a tart drink similar to vinegar.

2. NOUN

/ ˈvɪnəɡɚ ˌtri / (AmE), / ˈvɪnɪɡə ˌtriː / (BrE)
a common North American shrub (Rhus typhina) with waxy compound leaves, green flowers in panicles, and clusters of red berries; also known as staghorn sumac
North American shrub with red berries (staghorn sumac)

Examples

The vinegar tree is often planted for its vibrant red berries and autumn foliage.
A cluster of vinegar tree shrubs provided shade near the old farmhouse.
Birds are attracted to the vinegar tree in the garden because of its bright fruit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: staghorn sumac - a North American shrub with hairy branches and red berries; another name for the vinegar tree
