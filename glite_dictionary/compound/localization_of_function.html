localization of function

1. NOUN

/ ˌloʊkəlaɪˈzeɪʃən əv ˈfʌŋkʃən / (AmE), / ˌləʊkəlaɪˈzeɪʃən əv ˈfʌŋkʃən / (BrE)
The principle in physiology that specific functions are associated with particular regions or organs in the body.
physiological principle linking function to body regions
medical

Examples

Modern neuroscience supports the localization of function in the human brain.
The localization of function was demonstrated when damage to Broca's area affected speech production.
Debates about the localization of function continue to shape research in cognitive science.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: functional specialization - The idea that different parts have specialized roles
