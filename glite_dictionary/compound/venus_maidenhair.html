Venus maidenhair

1. NOUN

/ ˈviːnəs ˈmeɪdənˌhɛr / (AmE), / ˈviːnəs ˈmeɪdənheə / (BrE)
a delicate maidenhair fern (Adiantum capillus-veneris) with slender, shiny black leaf stalks, found in various regions worldwide
delicate fern with black stalks

Examples

The Venus maidenhair is often prized by gardeners for its graceful, arching fronds.
We spotted several Venus maidenhair ferns growing near the shaded waterfall.
Botanists classify Venus maidenhair as Adiantum capillus-veneris, a cosmopolitan fern species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maidenhair fern - a type of fern with delicate fronds and black stems

Etymology

Named from Latin 'capillus Veneris', meaning 'hair of Venus', after the Roman goddess.
