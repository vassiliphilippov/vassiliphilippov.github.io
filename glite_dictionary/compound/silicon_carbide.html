silicon carbide

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɪlɪkən ˈkɑrˌbaɪd / (AmE), / ˌsɪlɪkən ˈkɑːˌbaɪd / (BrE)
an extremely hard blue-black crystalline compound (SiC) used as an abrasive, a heat-resistant material, and a semiconductor
hard blue-black crystalline compound (SiC) used in abrasives and electronics

Examples

Cutting tools made from silicon carbide are valued for their exceptional hardness and resistance to wear.
The engineer selected silicon carbide as the abrasive material for the grinding wheel.
Light-emitting diodes were first constructed using silicon carbide crystals as semiconductors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carbide - a compound composed of carbon and a less electronegative element

Etymology

From 'silicon' and 'carbide', reflecting its composition of silicon and carbon atoms.
