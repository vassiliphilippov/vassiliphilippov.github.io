Culver's physic

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌlvɚz ˈfɪzɪk / (AmE), / ˈkʌlvəz ˈfɪzɪk / (BrE)
a tall perennial herb native to eastern North America, with spikes of small white or purple flowers, also known as Veronicastrum virginicum
a North American perennial herb (Veronicastrum virginicum)

Examples

The garden featured a patch of Culver's physic blooming beside the pond.
Botanists identified the rare Culver's physic during their survey of the meadow.
Early settlers sometimes used Culver's physic as a herbal remedy.

Etymology

Physic means 'medicine'; named after Dr. Culver, an early American physician who promoted its medicinal uses.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkʌlvərz ˈfɪzɪk / (AmE), / ˈkʌlvəz ˈfɪzɪk / (BrE)
A tall perennial herb (Veronicastrum virginicum) native to eastern North America, bearing spikes of small white or purple flowers.
a North American perennial herb

Examples

The garden featured Culver's physic in the wildflower section alongside coneflowers and asters.
Early settlers used Culver's physic as a traditional remedy for various ailments.
You can identify Culver's physic by its tall spikes of white or purple flowers in midsummer.

Etymology

The name likely derives from 'Culver', an old term for 'dove,' and 'physic,' meaning a medicinal plant.
