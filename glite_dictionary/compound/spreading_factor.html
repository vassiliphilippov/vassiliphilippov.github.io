spreading factor

1. NOUN

/ ˈsprɛdɪŋ ˈfæk.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈsprɛdɪŋ ˈfæk.tə / (BrE)
an enzyme that breaks down hyaluronic acid, reducing tissue viscosity and increasing the permeability of connective tissue to promote fluid absorption
enzyme increasing tissue permeability
medical

Examples

The spreading factor in the injection helped the medication disperse rapidly through the tissue.
Doctors use spreading factor to enhance the absorption of certain drugs during treatment.
Researchers are investigating new uses for spreading factor in reducing tissue resistance in medical procedures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hyaluronidase - an enzyme that catalyzes the breakdown of hyaluronic acid
