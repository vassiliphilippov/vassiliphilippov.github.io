soap-rock

1. NOUN

/ ˈsoʊp ˌrɑk / (AmE), / ˈsəʊp ˌrɒk / (BrE)
A soft, heavy, compact variety of talc with a soapy feel, commonly used for making hearths, tabletops, and ornaments.
soft talc-like rock used for carvings

Examples

The artisan sculpted a beautiful vase from soap-rock.
Early settlers often used soap-rock to build fireplace hearths.
Museum displays include ancient ornaments carved out of soap-rock.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soapstone - soft metamorphic rock composed mainly of talc
